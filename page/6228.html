Why should we use DIV+CSS to replace TABLE-based layouts? Here's an illustrative metaphor: Using **TABLE** for layout is like building a house with fixed, pre-assembled modular furniture. Every piece of furniture is rigidly connected, and if you want to change the structure or design of the house, you have to dismantle and reassemble large sections. It’s inflexible and time-consuming. On the other hand, using **DIV+CSS** is like having a flexible interior design system where walls and decorations can be easily moved or adjusted without affecting the foundation of the house. You can rearrange elements, change colors, or modify styles dynamically, all while keeping the core structure intact. In short: - **TABLE**: Rigid, hard to modify, tightly coupled content and presentation. - **DIV+CSS**: Flexible, easy to maintain, separates content from styling. This flexibility makes DIV+CSS more efficient and adaptable for modern web development.
