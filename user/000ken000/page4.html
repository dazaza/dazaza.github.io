Below is an introduction to Amantadine: Amantadine, also known as adamantaneamine, is an antiviral medication that has been used for many years to prevent and treat certain types of influenza, including swine flu (H1N1). It works by interfering with the replication process of the influenza virus, specifically targeting the M2 protein found in some strains of the influenza A virus. Key points about Amantadine: 1. Mechanism of Action: Amantadine prevents the uncoating of the viral genome within the host cell, thereby stopping the virus from replicating. 2. Usage: It can be used both prophylactically to prevent infection and therapeutically to treat symptoms during the early stages of infection. 3. Limitations: Over time, many influenza viruses have developed resistance to Amantadine, reducing its effectiveness in some cases. 4. Side Effects: Possible side effects include nausea, dizziness, insomnia, and in rare cases, more serious neurological or psychological reactions.
