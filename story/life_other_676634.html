Ha Ge's Love

Little earthworm Ha Ge spent his eighteen years of youth in a dark and damp soil. He grew up, and his heart began to open to love. He started pursuing so-called love. He had his eyes on another earthworm, Ha Mei, from the neighboring soil. Ha Mei, who was just full of romantic fantasies, accepted Ha Ge's pursuit. They fell in love, with a bit of naivety and innocence between them as they cultivated this newfound love.

They had been together for three years, from acquaintance to love. As they were experiencing the so-called "three-year itch," Ha Ge was planning for his future marriage when something unexpected happened: Ha Mei proposed a breakup to him.

Ha Ge was very sad. He cried and asked Ha Mei through his tears, "If we have to break up, can you tell me why? What's wrong with me?"

"You're fine, it's just that I don't love you anymore. I used to love you because of my naivety, because I didn't know any better. Now that I'm more mature, I feel embarrassed being with you. To be honest, I've fallen for a snake. Look at how ugly you are—no head or tail visible, and so old-fashioned. The snakes, on the other hand, are handsome! It would be so great to be with him! We could walk down the street with our heads held high, so proud and confident! So many beautiful girls would envy me!"

Ha Ge was helpless, "What's the point of looking good if he has a bad temper and a serious tendency towards violence? He often gets into fights."

Ha Mei angrily retorted, "Do you even know what violence is? That's called having a manly demeanor. I like his handsomeness."

Feeling utterly helpless, Ha Ge prayed to heaven to turn him into a snake. Thus, Ha Ge and Ha Mei rekindled their love, which lasted for two years until Ha Mei once again proposed a breakup.

At this moment, Ha Ge was completely confused, his head ringing like a bell: "What? Didn't you like the handsomeness of a snake? Why do you want to break up now?"

"Why? Look at you—you always fight, lose your temper easily, and wander around all day. You're so handsome, and there are so many beautiful girls surrounding you. I have no sense of security. Besides, let's talk about your level of education. You have no culture; all you know is to rely on brute force to find a little food every day. You only eat one kind of food until you're sick of it, but you won't change your diet. Where's the ambition in this? Look at the parrots—they are highly educated, speak foreign languages, and effortlessly get fed. Whatever they want, they get. Can you do that? I've fallen for a parrot. He can give me the life I want. He's my prince charming."

"But look at how disgusting he dresses—colorful and garish, with red lips. He's not masculine or feminine. How can he be an object of affection?"

"Do you even know anything? That's called gender-neutral beauty, called fashion. You're such a bumpkin."

Under Ha Ge's desperate pleas, heaven transformed him into a parrot, and he regained Ha Mei's love. But who would have thought that shortly after falling in love again, Ha Mei got tired of him: "I don't want to continue this relationship. I want to break up with you."

"Why? I have knowledge and culture. Isn't that what you love about me? I live by my intellect."

"Living by intellect is true, but what's the use of it now? The streets are full of intellectuals. Moreover, you may have knowledge, but do you have a house? Look at the snails—at least they belong to the housed class! And your house—when it rains, it becomes unrecognizable. Besides, is this outdated house even worthy of being called a house? Not to mention, without a house, how can you talk about love?"

"But she lives miserably, submissive to those stronger than her, shrinking back all the time. Besides, her house weighs her down so much she can't breathe."

Ha Mei countered, "Do you even understand anything? She's showing gentlemanly grace, not quarreling with them. A gentleman doesn't fight with dogs."

Ha Ge felt a sense of helplessness. At this moment, he didn't know what kind of life Ha Mei wanted. However, he deeply loved Ha Mei, so he was willing to change for her. Thus, Ha Ge, carrying a heavy snail shell, began dating Ha Mei again.

But good times didn't last long. Ha Mei got tired of the current life. Having a house was true, but what about work? Crawling here and there every day without a stable job, floating aimlessly, how could this life go on? She broke up with Ha Ge again and fell in love with a spider because the spider had a stable job, earning money by simply surfing the internet in the office. How stable and comfortable!

Through his own efforts, Ha Ge became a spider, spending his days typing on his web with his furry legs. Ha Ge secretly smiled, thinking that finally, he would have happiness, and Ha Mei would finally be satisfied. But Ha Mei seemed to have lost her original joy, and she proposed a breakup again.

Ha Ge was furious: "Why? Why is this happening? I gave you everything you wanted—handsomeness, culture, a house, a job. Why do you still want to break up with me?"

"I admit, you gave me all these things, but look at your working environment—it's just a small broken village among the trees. We both came from the countryside. Do you think I'd ever go back to that place? I want to stay in the big city. I love turtles because they have jobs, and more importantly, they live in the big city, and even coastal cities. That kind of life is so happy, so warm. I love the blue sea, and I love the fireworks blooming under the night sky in the big city. That kind of life will always make me yearn. Since I've left the countryside, I'll never come back."

Ha Ge argued, "But turtles are pampered, effeminate, lacking any manly qualities, and retreat into their shells at the first sign of trouble. They only rely on others to solve problems."

"That's called humility."

Ha Ge endured it. To gain Ha Mei, he went to the sea and turned into a turtle, taking Ha Mei to watch sunrises on the beach. Perhaps due to the passage of time, Ha Mei frequently argued with Ha Ge. Ha Mei couldn't take it anymore: "Let's break up. I've wanted to break up with you for a long time. What's good about living in the big city? What do you have? All you do is swim around in the sea. Even though you're in the big city, isn't this life without status terrible? Look at the crabs—they also live in the big city, but what level are they at? They have pincers! Do you have pincers? Let me be honest with you—I've fallen for a crab. Look at him, with his pincers, how impressive! People with pincers walk differently; they always walk sideways. Do you have the courage to walk sideways? I want to date him. Dating him will be my happiness."

"Will dating him guarantee happiness? Your thoughts are too naive! Do pincers mean everything?"

"Don't call me naive, okay? I'm not stupid. I know my own happiness. I won't let myself suffer. Do you know how bitter life is without pincers? Sure, you have food, but just a little bit every day. What's that good for?"

Ha Ge pleaded repeatedly, "Now, I only have a little, but I believe that through my efforts, I will have pincers."

"Haha, are you joking? You'll never have pincers. That's someone else's fate. No matter how hard you try, you won't have pincers. Besides, even if you somehow manage to get pincers now, it'll be too late." After saying this, Ha Mei left Ha Ge and headed straight for the beach.

Ha Ge cried. He bitterly begged heaven, "Even if I have to make tremendous efforts, whatever Ha Mei likes, I hope you, Lord Heaven, can turn me into that." Touched by his sincerity, heaven agreed. Whatever Ha Mei liked, Ha Ge would become. Thus, Ha Ge became a small crab, using his pincers to create storms for Ha Mei every day. However, for some reason, facing all these things Ha Ge gave to Ha Mei, Ha Mei seemed to show no joy. She left when Ha Ge was asleep, going off to seek happiness.

Ha Ge slept for four years. When he woke up after four years, he realized Ha Mei was gone. His tears soaked the entire beach. He still firmly believed that he had given Ha Mei everything she wanted, and she hadn't left him. But when he found Ha Mei next to a kangaroo, he was utterly devastated. Ha Mei had already fallen in love with the kangaroo. Enraged and humiliated, he blamed heaven for breaking its promise: "Heaven, why did you deceive me? You said that if she liked something, you would turn me into that. Why did you break your word?"

"Ha Ge, I didn't break my word. I simply couldn't transform you. I am powerless because Ha Mei now likes foreigners, and you, a native species, no matter how you mutate, you can't become foreign. So, I truly regret that I cannot help. Ha Mei will never be satisfied, and you can never fully change. All you can do now is to let go."

All of Ha Ge's efforts turned out to be in vain. He despaired: "What is love? What is the love that belongs to me?" The more Ha Ge thought about it, the angrier he became. Helpless and overwhelmed with anger, he died from internal bleeding.

This story reflects the complexities and challenges of love and the pursuit of happiness, highlighting the importance of understanding and acceptance in relationships.
