Analysis Report on the Demand for Express Service Satisfaction

by a20110325 on 2011-04-01 11:17:50

In recent years, with the rapid rise of e-commerce, the express delivery industry has entered a period of high-speed development and achieved remarkable results. However, during this rapid development, issues such as random unloading of goods, contract traps, signing before inspection, and difficulties in compensation have been frequently exposed. Express delivery services have become one of the hotspots for consumer complaints. It is not difficult to see that the quality of service in the express delivery industry still falls far short of user expectations.

To objectively evaluate the role of China's express delivery industry in all aspects of society and promote the improvement of service levels among various express companies, CCTV.com’s logistics channel launched a "China Express Industry Service Satisfaction Survey" activity targeting netizens nationwide. According to the voting results, only 70% of netizens are satisfied with express delivery services, with the speed of collection and delivery, cargo safety, service attitude, and claims insurance becoming focal points of concern for netizens.

### I. Analysis of Express Delivery Service Satisfaction

During the survey process, we statistically summarized the factors that make netizens satisfied or dissatisfied with express delivery services. The specific situation is as follows:

#### Chart One: Satisfying Factors of Express Delivery Services

#### Chart Two: Unsatisfying Factors of Express Delivery Services

From the above two charts:

(1) In the collection phase, netizens are more satisfied with express delivery services that are fast and have skilled personnel; they are dissatisfied with companies where pickup times are delayed, delivery is slow, and there are delays or missed deadlines.

(2) Regarding transportation range, netizens are more satisfied with express companies that offer wide-ranging transportation and comprehensive network coverage. Many netizens suggested in their comments that “the scope of express delivery should be expanded” and “hope that the network can cover rural areas to facilitate online shopping.”

(3) In terms of cargo safety, netizens are more satisfied with express deliveries where the outer packaging is intact and there is no damage or loss. They are extremely dissatisfied with cases of private unpacking, damaged goods, or lost items. Netizens have suggested that express companies should eliminate rough handling and sorting errors, and “hope that express companies will no longer let goods ‘fly around.’”

(4) Regarding transportation prices, netizens show higher satisfaction with transparent and reasonable freight charges from express companies. They hope for standardized pricing among express companies, avoiding low-price malicious competition and instead improving services.

(5) In terms of service attitude, netizens are more satisfied with uniformed delivery personnel who are polite and allow inspection before signing. They also have high requirements for telephone voice services.

(6) After-sales service-wise, netizens are satisfied with online tracking and query services provided by express companies but dissatisfied with issues such as failure to honor insurance policies, difficulty in making complaints, and non-standardized compensation.

### II. Analysis of Attention on Express Delivery Services

So, what factors do netizens care about most when using express delivery services?

#### Chart Three: The Most Concerned Factors of Express Delivery Services Among Netizens

From the statistical results of the above chart, it is evident that netizens generally pay attention to the following aspects:

(1) Speed of sending and receiving packages. The speed of package receipt and dispatch is the factor netizens care about most when using express services, accounting for 21%. According to statistics from the 315 Consumer Electronics Complaints Network, delays remain the primary issue in the express delivery industry. In 2010, there were a total of 15,194 complaints related to this issue, accounting for 50.20% of all complaints, an increase of 6 percentage points compared to 2009. The demand for "speed" is paramount.

(2) Cargo safety. Cargo safety has long been a significant and prominent issue in the express delivery industry. 19% of netizens are most concerned about cargo safety (loss and damage rate), ranking second.

(3) Insurance and after-sales service. In addition to service attitude and transportation range, which continue to be focal points of concern for netizens, it is noteworthy that 17% of netizens chose "failure to honor insurance and difficulty in complaints" as reasons for dissatisfaction with express delivery services. As the express delivery industry develops, the issue of item insurance is becoming increasingly prominent.

(4) Express delivery price. Compared to speed and safety, the concern for express delivery prices is relatively lower at 14%. Some netizens frankly stated, "Speed and safety are the primary considerations, and only under these guarantees would price be considered as a comparison factor."

(5) Personalized value-added services. The demand for express value-added services is also increasing. Nearly 10% of netizens hope that express companies can provide better and more complete personalized services (such as online tracking).

### III. Expectations of Netizens for Express Delivery Companies

1. **Keywords**

Netizens' greatest expectations for express delivery companies include: professional competence of staff, industry regulation, entry threshold, distribution range, information services, price transparency, sending/receiving speed, cargo safety, customer service attitude, online inquiry systems, industry legislation, compensation system, and inspection before signing.

2. **Expectation Value**

We summarized 13 "netizen suggestions" keywords and their proportions from the comments and suggestions, as shown in Chart Four.

#### Chart Four: Netizen Suggestions

From the chart, it can be seen that apart from the most concerning factors mentioned above, netizens have many suggestions regarding the professional competence of staff, information services, industry regulatory mechanisms, entry thresholds for express delivery, price transparency, and even the service image of express delivery personnel.

(1) **Regulatory Strength and Compensation Mechanism**. It is worth noting that many netizens proposed demands and suggestions regarding the strength of industry regulation and the establishment of a compensation mechanism for lost or damaged goods. They also made requests to the government. Netizens suggested: "The government should further standardize the express delivery industry, establish a certain supervision mechanism, ensure cargo safety, and timely delivery," "Industry associations must fulfill their monitoring responsibilities, control malicious competition, and enhance social credibility," "Government supervision must be in place; it's always hard to file complaints, and express companies have many reasons to make you feel wronged," "Raise the entry threshold for the express delivery industry," etc.

(2) **Information Services**. Currently, with the rapid development of information networks, netizens are particularly concerned about information services. Good express tracking and inquiry services are urgently needed by netizens. Most netizens have proposed that the online inquiry system needs further upgrades.

(3) **Professional Competence and Service Image of Staff**. Many netizens mentioned that they hope express companies can comprehensively improve the professional competence of their staff and put forward certain requirements for uniform clothing. From our understanding, companies like EMS and larger private express companies such as SF Express, Yunda, HHTT, Shentong, and ZTO perform better in terms of uniform clothing and unified service images.

(4) **Price Transparency**. Price transparency is a term repeatedly mentioned by netizens. How to achieve price transparency is an area where express companies need to work harder. It is understood that EMS and SF Express perform well in this regard. On the websites of EMS and SF Express, one can directly check the price between two locations, offering high transparency.
