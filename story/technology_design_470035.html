Why did the "temp worker" urban management officer become a thug who beats people up?

by tuoqing1188 on 2011-08-03 13:26:22

Recently, four incidents of urban management officers beating people occurred in Zhengzhou. Reporters found that during the accountability process for all the incidents, there was always a group called "temporary workers" of the urban management department who were ambiguously involved, and the responsibilities for these four incidents of beating people all fell on so-called "temporary workers". (According to Beijing News on November 13)

In the end, the act of beating people was all placed on the "temporary workers". If this is not a coincidence, then there are three scenarios: First, the "persuasion by beating" of the urban management has been handed over to the "temporary workers", they don't do it themselves, just let them handle the difficult situations with a beating. Second, these temporary workers are "pretend-to-be-temporary", up until now, their true identities are unknown to the public. They could be "permanent employees", or even fabricated, who knows? Third, the urban management really didn't beat anyone, their qualities are much higher than those so-called "temporary workers".

According to current investigations, the possibility of these "temporary workers" existing is very high, but how can these "temporary workers" be more "skilled in martial arts" than the urban management themselves? If they are "temporary workers", which "heroes of the rivers and lakes" are they? What are their salary standards and who should set them? All these doubts eventually confirm one fact: this is a mysterious organization. If I could, I would also like to organize such a group of "temporary workers" to guard my home, and if any urban management dares to cause trouble, they can "spar" with them.

The controversy about urban management enforcement has always been a hot topic of attention. As city managers, urban management itself has certain law enforcement powers and is a special comprehensive law enforcement agency. However, apart from the regular urban management staff, there are also auxiliary management personnel and "outsiders", namely these "temporary workers". The power of urban management itself is limited, so actions such as beating people, confiscating property, and auctioning off sidewalks have caused great controversy; then where does the "courage" of the "temporary workers" under the urban management department come from?

In the final analysis, the reasons are no more than external weakness and internal "strength": loopholes in the supervision and management mechanism plus the self-expansion of power. Urban management supervises the city, but lacks supervision itself, creating a very convenient environment for "doing as they please"; and the lack of personal legal awareness or "knowingly violating the law" leading to self-indulgence gradually replaces the function of "managing and serving the city" with the self-satisfaction of "teaching citizens lessons". In addition, the salary of urban management is a big issue, its "fiscal revenue" does not have a good guarantee, so the misdeeds of confiscating vehicles from small vendors and imposing random fines to create income are inevitable. This is already a murky situation, and adding a group of "temporary workers" who muddy the waters makes it even more confusing. Not to mention the common people who tremble in fear in front of urban management, even bystanders are completely baffled.

Recently, many places have been promoting the slogan of "improving the image of urban management", with various forms such as "college student urban management", "people-friendly urban management", "urban management popularization education", etc., but the results are far from satisfactory. Even if the quality of urban management improves, another group of "temporary workers" who beat people appears, continuing in this way, seeking to change the image without changing the substance is like neglecting the root while pursuing trivialities, how can there be standard management? As the grassroots law enforcement agency of the city, urban management often forgets the basic purpose of better serving the city within its authority, which is the most disheartening.

The "temporary worker" urban management gives a false impression of urban management beating people, "substituting beams and pillars" but not deviating from the topic of urban management; if this is indulged excessively, it will only lead to greater misfortune for ordinary people. Therefore, standardizing the system of urban management itself is far more urgent than "image projects", do not let "temporary workers" become "temporary beating workers", otherwise, it will only get more chaotic.
