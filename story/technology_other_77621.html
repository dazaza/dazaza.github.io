Accompanying love, performing love, and marital love

by yosky98 on 2008-07-14 09:06:43

My young fellow villager Yan is getting married. Three months ago, she didn't even have a boyfriend.

Yan isn't rushing into marriage. Her fiancé is her former lover, her high school classmate Kai. Yan and Kai dated for more than two years after they graduated from university in 2002, but eventually broke up over an ice cream.

It was a summer night in 2004, about 9:30 pm. Yan said, "I want to eat ice cream." Kai replied, "The supermarket closes at 9 o'clock, where do you expect me to buy it?" Yan said, "I want to eat it. I know there's a store open 24 hours near the traffic light four stops away." Kai didn't go, so Yan went herself. After eating the ice cream, she never returned to Kai's side.

After their breakup, Yan and Kai followed their own paths, going on many blind dates and having one or two short-lived relationships, but none of them worked out.

In March this year, one of Yan's classmates from out of town came to Beijing, and the classmates in Beijing took the opportunity to gather once again. Yan attended and met Kai, only to find out that he was still single. When the gathering ended, Kai said, "Let me buy you an ice cream." Yan agreed. She ate one after another, consuming three in total before Kai felt sorry for her and said, "If you can't eat anymore, don't force yourself. If you want, I'll buy you ice cream every day." At this, Yan's tears began to flow.

When I saw Yan, there were no more tears in her eyes. She was holding onto Kai's arm, her face full of happiness. Knowing their story, I laughed at them. Yan giggled and then said that she felt love could be divided into three kinds.

Yan said, actually, the time when she wanted to eat ice cream at 9:30 pm was deliberately picking a fight. Prior to that, she had heard Kai's mother ask on the phone, "When are you two getting married?" After the call, both Kai and Yan's hearts became as restless and suffocated as the summer night in Beijing. They had never thought about marriage; they were just attracted to each other. So, when the word "marriage" appeared, the two of them used the excuse of ice cream to escape. This kind of love is actually like a military drill, just a feeling and experience of love, which Yan called "acting love".

Yan said that compared to two years ago, neither her nor Kai's work has changed, their personalities haven't changed, and their income hasn't changed much either. What has changed is their psychology. After graduating for five years and drifting in Beijing for five years, they always had a sense of being rootless. Now they wanted to start a family, and their mindset had changed. When she met Kai again, she was ready psychologically and the purpose of their relationship was clear: to get married. So this kind of love is called "marriage love".

After breaking up with Kai, Yan went on blind dates more than ten times, including some excellent men. In between, she also dated once, but when the other party proposed marriage, she still backed out. Although the other party was mentally prepared for marriage, Yan wasn't. So the other person was "accompanying love", accompanying Yan in her romance.

Now, putting myself in her shoes, I feel that "accompanying love" is like accompanying someone to grow up slowly, which must be very painful. Therefore, I have always felt apologetic towards that boy. Yan spoke seriously. She said, today, I saw several posts online soliciting "accompanying love" partners, so I wrote my experience behind them. Finally, I told the original poster not to recruit "accompanying love" because it often ends up hurting people in the end.
