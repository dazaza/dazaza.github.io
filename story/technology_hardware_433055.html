TV Series 'Black Cat Sheriff' Voice Cast Unveiled - Nationwide Release in Late April - China Pictures Network

by letaoeoe on 2011-05-18 20:11:46

"Black Cat Sheriff": A Mysterious and Captivating Experience with Star-Studded Voice Acting

"The Black Cat Sheriff" TV series is hailed as a classic that every child has watched. This time, the production team has added new elements to re-present it on the big screen. We have every reason to believe that the new cinema version of "Black Cat Sheriff" will attract audiences of all age groups to revisit this classic together in theaters.

Famous international artist Da Shan was also invited to passionately join the cast, voicing the African cat-eating mouse. This marks his debut in animated voice acting. Da Shan expressed that he feels exceptionally delighted and incredibly honored to be able to voice for this internationally renowned work from Shanghai Animation Film Studio, the birthplace of Chinese animation. In order to better portray this foreign mouse in a Chinese animated masterpiece, Da Shan sought advice from Li Yang on voice-acting techniques before recording. We believe that when the film is released, audiences will witness the excellent performances of these two mice in the theater. Additionally, in line with the film's themes of anti-terrorism, rule of law, environmental protection, and science, the producers specially invited Zhu Yanting, a female host from the Shanghai Golden Shield Art Troupe under the Ministry of Public Security, to voice Detective White Dove. She has hosted the Ministry of Public Security's Spring Festival Gala multiple times, and her transformation from a real-life police officer into the animated sheriff is one of the highlights of the film.

Looking back at the classics, the original "Black Cat Sheriff" once invited many famous actors from Shanghai Dubbing Studio to lend their voices, with numerous masters willingly playing supporting roles. Time flies, and today, when these distant voices sound again, they inevitably reopen sealed memories. Therefore, in this new cinema animation "Black Cat Sheriff," the basic characters continue to be voiced by top dubbing artists from Shanghai Dubbing Studio, who are considered paragons of Chinese dubbing art. Their vocal works have enhanced trendy Hollywood blockbusters like "Kung Fu Panda" and "Spider-Man." For example, Wu Lei, the voice actor for the male protagonist in the Chinese version of "Harry Potter," will voice the main character Black Cat Sheriff. His outstanding performance earned the film the Huabiao Award for Best Dubbed Film. Imagine how this clear male voice will blend and harmonize with Black Cat Sheriff. Moreover, Chief Monkey Eagle will be voiced by Liu Feng, who voiced Garfield in the Chinese version of "Garfield." He stated that he had previously competed for the role of Black Cat Sheriff, but after auditioning, the dubbing director felt he was more suited for the completely evil Chief Monkey Eagle. Will he add Garfield-style humor to this villainous character? We are also very much looking forward to it. We believe that this intergenerational competition between professional and non-professional voice actors will present a brand-new "Black Cat Sheriff."

Voice Cast of "Black Cat Sheriff"

When voice acting in animated films has become one of the key focuses for audiences, the new cinema animation "Black Cat Sheriff" brings fresh ideas to the dubbing process. The most memorable character, One Ear, will be voiced by the renowned dubbing expert and "Mickey Mouse" voice actor, Li Yang. Li Yang's distinctive voice has accompanied generations of Chinese audiences through their growth. This time, he transforms from a foreign-sounding rat to a native Chinese rat, offering plenty of auditory appeal. Although Li Yang's vocal prowess is well-known, voicing One Ear is another challenge in his artistic career, especially when handling intense sounds during fight scenes. Li Yang candidly admitted that he put all his effort into presenting a breakthrough version of the new One Ear.

According to China Film News, at the beginning of the New Year 2010, the media visitation event and star-studded voice cast unveiling press conference for the latest domestic cinema animation blockbuster "Black Cat Sheriff" was held in Shanghai on the afternoon of February 25th.

At the press conference site for the latest domestic cinema animation blockbuster "Black Cat Sheriff," two visually impactful and stylish promotional posters were unveiled, created by the main creative team of Shanghai Animation Film Studio and a hardcore fan of "Black Cat Sheriff" born in the 70s. The screening date for the latest domestic cinema animation blockbuster "Black Cat Sheriff" was officially announced at the press conference by Wang Tianyun, Vice President of Shanghai Film Group Corporation. The film will grandly premiere nationwide in major theaters in late April 2010.
