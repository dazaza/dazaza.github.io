by nbthwjjj627 on 2012-02-09 16:45:09

Therapists have been giving Yangyang rehabilitation training recently. When the reporter interviewed them the other day, Yangyang burst into laughter while being held in his mother's arms. Shenzhen Business Journal reporter Guo Guorui took the photo. Although Yangyang cannot speak, he can communicate with his mother through the heart. The child was diagnosed with serious cerebral palsy two months after birth. Since then, her husband disappeared and left her alone to take care of their son. She earned money while doing her best to rehabilitate her son. Five years passed, and she never gave up. Rehabilitation is hard work, but the effect is minimal. Faced with repeated setbacks, ordinary people would have given up long ago, but she has never been slack at all. She has always been optimistic, gentle, and tenacious. Her luck has brought her special encounters, realizing her rapid growth in life. Shenzhen Business Journal reporter Deng Xiaoqun If she did not have a child or had a healthy child, she would be living an unencumbered and even world-traveling abundant life; now that she has a severely disabled child, she feels that in the process of fighting together with her child, she no longer feels bored and plain, and understands respect for life more. "I am just a mother who loves her child very much." Facing others' admiration, her smile is always so composed and calm. Yangyang's mother lives in our city. A disabled baby is also an angel for his mother. Yangyang's mother still remembers the situation five years ago when she gave birth to her son at the age of 36: the little guy weighed 8 pounds 6 ounces, with black shiny hair, big hands and feet, and dark eyes. He cried with a loud voice. Those days, Yangyang's family was filled with happiness. His father, despite having very limited time, specially asked for leave and flew back from abroad to see his precious son. His 70-year-old grandmother, who had been longing for a grandson for many years, covered her toothless mouth firmly before laughing. However, something seemed wrong soon after. Two months later, Yangyang's mother, who had been a doctor, felt that there was indeed a problem with the child: one of his little hands was always reflexively raised upward, with opisthotonos, and his body could not maintain a straight line; his little hands were always tightly clenched into fists, and his limbs were often stiff, especially when he was throwing tantrums, making it impossible to pry them open, sometimes accompanied by spasms... Parents took Yangyang to the hospital for examination, and the doctors diagnosed him with "severe infantile cerebral palsy." Cerebral palsy is generally divided into three levels: mild, moderate, and severe. In Yangyang's case, the doctor gave the highest level of disability as "Level Four." Infantile cerebral palsy refers to brain substance damage in infants caused by various reasons (such as infections, bleeding, trauma, etc.), leading to movement, intellectual, and other impairments, which requires immense effort and lifelong rehabilitation. After leaving the hospital, Yangyang's father felt weak in the legs, unable to drive. Yangyang's mother drove home. Laughter disappeared from the house, and everyone knew the meaning of this diagnosis. Soon, her husband went abroad again and sent her an email: "Give up on this child. Treating him is harder than climbing to heaven." For the first week after the diagnosis, Yangyang's mother cried every day, crying until her head hurt, crying until everything in front of her turned dark, unable to think about anything, unable to do anything, even forgetting to feed Yangyang. She thought about choosing to give up like her husband, or even how to let Yangyang leave without suffering. But when she saw Yangyang's chubby little face, his innocent eyes, and his contented appearance while leaning against her chest, the child became her little liver, her little angel again. Yangyang's mother replied in an email to her husband: "Then I will climb to heaven and show you!" Her reply made Yangyang's father disappear forever, like a stone sinking into the sea. Hard rehabilitation is difficult like obtaining scriptures from the west. She became a de facto single mother. From then on, she would raise her cerebral-palsied child alone, bearing economic, time, and physical pressures independently. Prior to this, Yangyang's mother didn't need to worry about finances. Now, work became her top priority because it was her only source of income for raising and treating her child. To accompany her child in rehabilitation, she applied to work night shifts at her company. During the day, she would take her child to various hospitals and rehabilitation institutions, return home to perform home exercises with him, and then go to work the night shift after he fell asleep, accumulating only four or five hours of sleep a day. The child’s rehabilitation training was extraordinarily challenging. At the beginning, the attending doctor informed her: “Your child needs lifelong rehabilitation. His life journey is like going to the West to fetch scriptures, full of difficulties and tribulations along the way.” The training began. As an infant, Yangyang had to wear braces on his little hands, and his arms and legs were completely wrapped with straps. Then, a large adult body pressed down on his tiny frame, spreading his legs into a split position. Meanwhile, another adult stepped on his little hands, making him lie on an inclined surface, continuously training him to lift his head. After six months of high-intensity training, blisters formed on Yangyang's hands and shoulders, turning into thick calluses, with bruises all over. Sometimes, the child was so exhausted that he fell asleep during the training. They couldn’t let him sleep, so they had to wake him up forcefully and continue the training. Of course, the child resisted and cried loudly, drying his lips and hoarsening his throat from crying. No matter who watched such devilish training, they couldn't help but shed tears, but almost no one had ever seen Yangyang's mother cry. She referred to herself and her son as "comrades-in-arms" fighting together. Each time, she used her own unique methods to comfort her child, picking up the crying Yangyang and whispering: “Yangyang loves Mommy, and Mommy loves Yangyang.” At this moment, the child would miraculously calm down and continue the devilish training after a while. The training was so arduous, but the child's progress was extremely small. Lifting his head, occasionally holding the milk bottle with his hand—these actions that normal children naturally know how to do—Yangyang often needed one or two years of practice to achieve, truly harder than climbing to heaven. And whenever the child made slight progress, the mother would cry out of joy. In her eyes, this was a great gift from heaven, a major breakthrough achieved together with her "little comrade"! Years of daily four-hour sleep finally affected Yangyang's mother's health. For a period, she experienced intense headaches, dizziness, and projectile vomiting twice within half a month, especially the second time, which required emergency treatment by ambulance. After examination, there was nothing growing in her brain, just blood vessel spasm, but it gave her a warning. She decided to resume swimming, which she once loved most. Every day, no matter how busy she was, Yangyang's mother would make time to swim 1000 meters: "As long as I stay healthy, there will be a future for my child. I cannot leave him millions of assets, but I will strive to give him a healthy mother who can accompany him further down the road." There should be no lack of happiness. Upon arriving home every day, Yangyang's mother would first hug her child. Yangyang's mother said: "Although he still cannot talk now, he is particularly attached to me, and I cannot neglect him for a moment." Various vegetables, such as mustard greens, green beans, and cucumbers, are planted on the balcony of Yangyang's home. These are the organic vegetables that the mother grows for her son, basically meeting the child's needs, while also creating a beautiful garden. This home gives people a sense of warmth and happiness, without any trace of gloom. This is the carefully crafted world that Yangyang's mother created for her child. Yangyang's mother said that everything she does is for Yangyang's happiness. On each birthday, Yangyang's mother would take him to the photo studio to take birthday photos. During photography, others would curiously look at this disabled child. At this time, Yangyang's mother would always explain to people: the child has cerebral palsy, hoping everyone could understand and be patient when posing for him. Yangyang's mother does this with deep intentions: "On the one hand, it allows Yangyang more opportunities to interact with the outside world; more importantly, it gives myself a chance to exercise, allowing me to face others' gazes bravely. Actually, this is a psychological threshold that every parent of a disabled child must overcome. Only physically and mentally healthy parents can raise physically and mentally healthy children. Disabled children already lack sound bodies, and they should not lose sound minds either." Yangyang's mother often takes the child out to play with the nanny. Her broad-mindedness and the child's charming smile make everyone like Yangyang. Every time they go out for a walk, adults and children vie to hold him and tease him, making Yangyang reluctant to go home! Yangyang's happiness has infected the parents of many disabled children in nearby neighborhoods. Previously, they were worried that others might find out about their disabled children and rarely brought them out, but seeing Yangyang happily running around without hesitation, they gradually started bringing their own children out too. Nowadays, in the park near Yangyang's home, it is common to see many disabled children walking together, presenting a spectacular scene. Now, Yangyang not only has many normal playmates but also quite a few disabled friends. Yangyang is nearly five years old now, but in terms of motor skills, he can barely reach the level of a five-month-old child. What other children achieve in a month, Yangyang needs a whole year to accomplish. But Yangyang's mother says he is very happy, with a super charming smile on his face every day. His smile even makes the mothers of healthy children envious. "My only hope is that he can be happy" The rehabilitation of a fourth-degree severe cerebral palsy child is difficult, known as the "presidential level." Despite the continuous struggle of mother and son, Yangyang's progress has always been slow. While other cerebral palsy children make great strides forward, Yangyang often stays in place. Yangyang's mother once wrote these words: "In 2007, I dreamed that one day he would walk; in 2008, I hoped that he could learn to sit and use his hands; but today, I no longer have too many ideas. My only wish is for him to be happy. Some people may think I am too optimistic and lack a goal to fight for, but facing reality, facing heavy fourth-degree cerebral palsy and quadriplegia, I don't want to put too much pressure on him or myself. As long as we have paid, tried our best, the result is not important. What matters is maintaining a healthy mindset and living happily." Yangyang's mother's optimism and resilience brought her unexpected life experiences. Meeting the renowned traditional Chinese medicine practitioner Guo Tingying was one of them. Guo Lao comes from a family of traditional Chinese medicine practitioners, is extremely compassionate, and possesses remarkable medical skills. All nine members of his family are acupuncture physicians, and they have saved countless lives over half a century of medical practice. When Guo Lao learned about Yangyang and his mother's story, he voluntarily offered free acupuncture treatments for Yangyang, with very noticeable results. After long-term observation, Guo Lao found that Yangyang's mother faced difficulties calmly and resiliently, never complaining, possessing a kind disposition, and having a medical background. Therefore, he actively proposed to accept her as his last disciple. This decision changed the trajectory of Yangyang's mother's life. Having studied Western medicine, she entered the vast and profound field of traditional Chinese medicine. After more than two years of dedicated study, with her insight and diligence, under Guo Lao's careful guidance, Yangyang's mother has completed her apprenticeship and is now a well-known acupuncture "young master" who can treat many diseases. Due to the numerous people seeking medical help, with the help of friends, Yangyang's mother recently opened a clinic in Meilin. Yangyang's mother really likes her current profession. She said that every time she cures a patient, it accumulates merit for Yangyang, giving her a deeper sense of peace inside. Of course, it also increases her income, helping her save some capital for Yangyang's future high medical expenses. Grateful for the "hardships from heaven" During the interview, the reporter asked about her husband. She said she harbors no resentment or hatred towards him because she understands him; he is the type of man who would avoid difficulties. She mentioned that in the hospital where Yangyang received treatment when he was young, among dozens of similar cases involving small patients, more than half of the children's families ended in divorce: "This is a pit of fire. Whoever jumps in dies. Whoever escapes survives. I am the mother, and if the whole world runs away, I cannot escape." Back then, she even hoped that her relatives and friends would stay away, not wanting to drag anyone down, because understanding leads to kindness. She did not run away, not for a moment stopping Yangyang's rehabilitation, which many dual-parent families might not necessarily achieve. Motherly love has brought Yangyang's mother bountiful rewards, and whenever she talks about her life experiences over the past few years, she is always expressing gratitude. "At the beginning, it was because my mother could not accept the reality and cried all night. For her, I had to pretend to be strong; later, I pretended to be strong for the sake of my child because my emotions would affect him, so I also had to pretend to be strong. Over time, the pretense became real, and I genuinely became optimistic and cheerful." She said that in the process of Yangyang's rehabilitation, she originally focused on the child's progress but failed to notice her own rapid growth: "If it weren't for the child's illness, I wouldn't have realized that I have such enormous energy within me, capable not only of independently raising a cerebral palsy child without affecting my work but also learning acupuncture to enhance myself." Regarding the child's future, Yangyang's mother once seriously told her friends: "All the struggles and efforts that Yangyang needs to make throughout his life, I will help him achieve. He only needs to live a carefree and happy life. We will cooperate; I will work, and he will enjoy. I won't let him feel pressured or troubled."
