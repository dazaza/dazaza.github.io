The Magic of Financial Management: Saving Where It Matters

Good financial management is like magic, but the difference is that magic turns paper into money, while financial management turns money into more money.

Saving is like giving yourself the tools to perform magic (www.coko365.com).

There are two kinds of people in this world: those who spend money like water and those who save money for a rainy day. The first group's finances are like listening to rock music – they come and go quickly, leaving behind a deeper sense of loneliness and helplessness after the loud noise subsides. The second group's finances are like listening to Richard Clayderman’s piano music in a restroom – they just do what they need to do, with almost silent background sounds, maintaining a forever distant relationship. In modern financial management concepts, the relationship between people and money should be like a symphony, with both grand chapters and light adagios. Being a competent conductor, combining each note, creates truly beautiful music.

In this combination, the most important and seemingly easiest step is – how to save money. A wrong notion only tells people the first half of the sentence: saving money is not the ultimate way to get rich. The second half is what students who don't pay attention in class are most likely to forget – those who are good at saving money are the ones qualified and capable of starting to make money.

During the Meiji era in Japan, there was a famous shipping magnate named Kawamura Zuisho. Like all rags-to-riches stories, this ancient tycoon also had an empty wallet when he was young, even worse than many people today. Kawamura Zuisho didn't even have a job. To save up a sum of money for great endeavors became his initial wish. Unbelievably, the "small change" of this future shipping king came from discarded vegetables, which he then resold to poorer laborers. Through savings and diligence, Kawamura Zuisho made enough small money to invest in shipping, eventually rising to prominence.

Reality often shows its brilliance in the ordinary, and the simpler the goal, the harder it is to achieve. Almost every household has an account book, and apart from checking how shockingly over-budget it is at the end of the month, it seems to serve no other purpose. Therefore, saving money requires some thought – how can you have both fish and bear paws?

Page One of the Account Book – Establish Your Own Emergency Fund

This is a very practical issue; everyone will have unforeseen needs, such as illness, accidents, debts, sudden unemployment, and related medical, life, and social insurance. There must be no negligence in these matters.

Tip: An emergency fund generally should not be less than three months' salary, and part of it can be considered for higher-interest fixed-term deposits.

Page Two of the Account Book – See How Much Money Doesn’t Belong to You

Still a practical issue, everyone will have fixed expenses, such as housing, car loans, education, and support costs... Actually, they belong to someone else from the moment your paycheck is issued, just like your income tax. Put this portion of money into a fixed current account, ensuring you're always prepared and never caught off guard.

Tip: Having an income and expenditure timetable, or even a professionally designed household financial plan, is entirely necessary.

Page Three of the Account Book – Save 10% Wisely

If possible, take out 10% of your monthly salary for long-term investments such as stocks, funds, bonds, etc., instead of depositing it in a bank with pitifully low interest rates. This will be a wise choice. After forming a habit, one day you'll realize when you gradually became wealthy.

Tip: Always record your investments, even the simplest savings. Knowing where and when your money is located is a great feeling – the feeling of making money. When money is on the edge, reality can always be split open, and life can always sail through the waves.

Page Four of the Account Book – Detailed Recording of Expenditures, the Beginning of Good Savings

Mr. Wang, due to work relations, always gets some fixed small bonuses of around 200 yuan outside of his salary. For this part of his income, Mr. Wang always puts it directly into envelopes. When he runs out of loose change, he takes out an envelope to use, making daily expenses more relaxed. If you don't have this extra income like Mr. Wang, and your accounts at the end of the month mercilessly make you start losing hair, then it's time to slim down your daily expenses. So, the account book might as well be as detailed as possible to find leaks in your daily finances.

Tips:

1. Pay attention to controlling expenses for dining out, clothing, daily transportation, and taxis. Money easily escapes through these areas. Carefully calculate, and saving 50% won't be difficult. Give yourself a "stop-loss price" each month, why not?

2. Be cautious about economic embarrassment caused by impulsiveness and vanity. Always weigh whether you have the ability to consume, learn to say no to face-saving, and you'll ultimately have face.

3. Be careful with behaviors like chatting on mobile phones. Exceptions may be made during passionate romances, but it's not necessary to be a lifelong romantic saint.

4. Often ask yourself why you're spending money. A common method is to find three reasons, and don't forget to let your partner be the final judge. If it doesn't work, add three more reasons.

5. A good and regular lifestyle is also indispensable. Try to reduce the romance that only exists in bars. Being constantly trapped in a sentimental lifestyle is also one of the obstacles to saving money.
