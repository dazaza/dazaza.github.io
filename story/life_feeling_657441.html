See the poem "Butterflies in Love with Flowers" again.

by lili1 on 2012-01-08 22:27:31

Once again, I saw Su Shi's timeless masterpiece "Butterfly in Love with Flowers", and I was struck by lightning. What amazed and moved me was not Mr. Su's style of writing or his exquisite wording. But a month ago, I saw this "Butterfly in Love with Flowers" in a text message. I like Song lyrics, such as Liu Yong's "Gan Sheng Ba Zhou" and "Rain Bells", and Li Qingzhao's "Slow Sounds". The slow lyrics of the Song Dynasty are no less than today's rock and jazz music. Or it may be due to personality factors, I am extremely fond of this kind of light and lingering slow lyrics culture. Many times, a book called "I Wish to Live in the Song Dynasty" makes me forget a lot of sadness and helplessness.

This time, when I saw "Butterfly in Love with Flowers", what I want to express is absolutely not my obsession and preference for slow lyrics. My mind immediately thought of that girl whom I only met once.

Under the fluorescent lamp, my thoughts were countless. I stared blankly at the flashing cursor. I remembered a line from "Rain Bells": "Unable to speak, choked with emotion." It felt like there was an olive weighing thousands of pounds falling down my throat, as if it was going to suffocate and bury me. I wanted to suppress it, but had no way.

Standing on the third floor of the dormitory building, looking out at the twilight and this beautiful city, the noisy cries gradually became calm. The spots of neon in the twilight dotted the night about to fall silent. Occasionally, a few cuckoo calls passed through my ears and trickled into my heart. I like this quiet and peaceful twilight enveloping me. It wasn't loneliness, nor was it loss, it was like the resonance between my heart and the twilight, very peculiar, very yearning. At this moment, I liked to pour a glass of red wine by the window. The deep red sweet wine exuded an alluring aroma, the crystal highball glass, and a rock song by Wu Bai would take my heart back to those years and months as if it were yesterday. I like Wu Bai's music just as I like slow lyrics. Wu Bai's music has progress, excitement, loneliness, rebellion, and madness. Every time I listen to his songs, my soul often surges uncontrollably.

Tonight, a piece of "Butterfly in Love with Flowers" made me like this. How I wish I could sincerely ask her: Are you doing well?
