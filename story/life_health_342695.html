Every 2 minutes, a Chinese person dies from stomach cancer, who is responsible for this?

by alexsgxu on 2010-04-09 21:10:11

While you are reading this article, several Chinese people may have died of stomach cancer. Don't you feel sad about that? The saddest part is that all of this was caused by ourselves...

Some readers have asked me what the laws in the United States do to regulate the morality of individuals and companies, and what good effects they have had. In light of the example of stomach cancer, I would like to share a few small stories with everyone:

1) The McDonald's hot coffee incident: When McDonald's sold hot coffee, the high temperature of the coffee caused an elderly woman to be scalded, resulting in McDonald's compensating for the loss of nearly one million US dollars. This case drew the attention of many companies, so now in the United States, fast food companies do not serve coffee at very high temperatures when selling it. Although the taste of coffee may not be as good as when it is served hot, the health and safety of the public are ensured.

2) In the United States, whether it is tobacco companies, pharmaceutical companies, automobile companies, or real estate companies, they must provide high-quality and extremely safe products and services to the American public. Otherwise, once any problem occurs, the consequences will be severe. In my work in real estate development and construction in the United States, I need to provide customers with a ten-year product quality and safety guarantee. During these ten years, if there are any problems with the house, we need to repair and maintain it promptly. If the customer suffers any direct or indirect harm due to issues with the house, including air quality problems caused by dampness after the house gets wet, it can lead to legal action from the customer, and the company often has to pay a large amount of compensation. Therefore, in order to reduce losses in this area, the company dares not be careless in architectural design and quality management, let alone falsify information. Because such costs are something a company cannot bear.

3) Similar examples happen every day. In general, the United States gives great importance and protection to the interests of the general consumer. As long as the interests of the general consumer are violated, regardless of the situation, strict punishment will be meted out. Therefore, in the United States, the quality of life for the general public is better protected.

4) Surprisingly, in China, too many very serious incidents have occurred, including the toxic milk powder incident, the toxic chopsticks incident, and the gutter oil incident. Every time these incidents occur, it seems that handling them is very helpless. Most of the time, just a few typical cases are caught, while other companies slip away. Without strict laws, it is impossible to exclude these problems to the greatest extent possible.

5) Moreover, our general consumers also fail to fulfill their responsibilities in protecting their own interests. I remember some well-known dairy companies in China first imported low-cost milk powder, causing huge damage to China's dairy industry. However, now these companies are promoted as ecological and environmental food companies. What level has China's food hygiene and safety reached? Let us compare it with India. I believe many of you have seen reports in newspapers and heard from many friends that India's hygiene conditions are much worse than China's, but India's stomach cancer incidence rate is much lower than China's. Falsification has brought too much loss to our society and too much harm to the public. How can we calculate the value of so many lives? Can we legislate strictly and enforce the law strictly to prevent similar things from happening again?

6) I remember someone telling me, "Why make such a big fuss? It's just the use of toxic lunch boxes. What's the big deal?" If our attitude is like this, how can society eliminate falsification? If they don't poison you, who will they poison? Forgetting pain once the wound has healed is a common flaw of many people. When others harm you, if you remain so numb and cannot stand up to say "no," do we have any hope?

7) Why are the true culprits behind so many "toxic" incidents in China always hard to find? Too many relationships, too many interests, and too much local protectionism interfere, leaving our law enforcement officers at a loss where to start. Can so many lives not awaken our conscience? Therefore...

8) I appeal to the Chinese people and the general consumers to bravely stand up and actively protect their rights when their interests are harmed, and not stop until the problem is solved.

9) I appeal for China's Food Hygiene Law and Consumer Protection Law to become more perfect! Various regulatory departments should coordinate and unify their regulatory actions to avoid regulatory vacuums.

10) I appeal to our law enforcement departments to strengthen their efforts in enforcing the law. For behaviors that endanger the physical and safety of the people, thorough investigations must be carried out to the end, excluding local protectionism and all other interference, bringing these people to justice according to the law. I believe that severe punishment can prevent these people from having侥幸psychology to falsify and not allow them to gain momentum. Only in this way can we ensure our basic physical health and safety. I think this requirement is not excessive.

Even if we cannot achieve perfection in our work and satisfy all our guests, we absolutely will not falsify or deceive. If individual employees or companies violate this principle, we will never tolerate it!
