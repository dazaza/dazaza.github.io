What are the symptoms of cervical cancer?

by betty2012 on 2012-03-05 16:38:54

What are the symptoms of cervical cancer? Cervical cancer is one of the common malignant tumors in women. The cause of the disease is still unclear at present. Women who get married and give birth early, have many children and have disordered sex life have a higher incidence rate. For women, the harm of cervical cancer is very great. So, what are the symptoms of cervical cancer in women? Experts from Xi'an Gynecological Hospital will answer the question specifically. What are the symptoms of cervical cancer? Generally speaking, there are no symptoms in the early stage of cervical cancer. As the disease progresses, patients may have abnormal vaginal bleeding. Since young women are in the active sexual period, their estrogen level and frequency of sexual intercourse are relatively high, so they are more likely to have sexual intercourse bleeding as the first symptom. In addition, increased leucorrhea is also a common symptom of cervical cancer, about 80% of cervical cancer patients have this symptom. Clinical tracking observation shows that it takes about 10 years for general pre-cancerous lesions of the cervix to develop into cervical cancer. From this point of view, cervical cancer is not terrible. It is a preventable and curable disease. The key to prevention and treatment is to have regular gynecological examinations, find and treat pre-cancerous lesions of the cervix in time, and stop its development into cervical cancer. If preventive and therapeutic measures can be implemented, the cure rate of cervical cancer is very high. 1. There are usually no symptoms in the early stage, which is not significantly different from chronic cervicitis. Sometimes, even the smooth cervix can be seen, especially in elderly women with atrophic cervix. 2. Vaginal bleeding: Young patients often show contact bleeding, which occurs after sexual intercourse, gynecological examination and defecation. The amount of bleeding can be more or less, generally depending on the size of the lesion and the degree of invasion of blood vessels in the stroma. The amount of bleeding is small in the early stage and large in the late stage when the lesion is larger. Once larger blood vessels are eroded, fatal massive bleeding may occur. Young patients may also show prolonged menstruation, shortened cycle, and increased menstrual flow. Postmenopausal irregular vaginal bleeding is often complained by elderly patients. 3. Vaginal discharge: Patients often complain of increased vaginal discharge, white or bloody, thin like water or rice soup, with a fishy smell. In the late stage, due to cancer tissue rupture, tissue necrosis, secondary infection, etc., a large amount of purulent or rice soup-like foul-smelling leukorrhea is discharged. 4. Symptoms of advanced cancer: Secondary symptoms appear according to the range of lesion invasion. When the lesion involves pelvic connective tissue, pelvic wall, compresses ureter or rectum, sacral nerve, patients often complain of frequent urination, urgent urination, anal fullness, constipation, tenesmus, lower limb swelling and pain, etc. Serious cases can lead to ureteral obstruction, hydronephrosis, and finally cause uremia. In the end stage of the disease, patients may become emaciated, anemic, febrile, and suffer from systemic exhaustion. Experts pointed out that the diagnosis of cervical cancer mainly depends on medical history and clinical manifestations, especially those with contact bleeding, need detailed whole-body examination and three-in-one gynecological examination, and adopt cervical smear cytology examination, iodine test, nitrogen laser tumor intrinsic fluorescence diagnosis method, colposcopy examination, cervical and cervical canal live tissue examination, cervical conization, etc. After confirming the diagnosis of cervical cancer, chest X-ray photography, lymphography, cystoscopy, proctoscopy examination, etc. should be performed according to specific conditions to determine its clinical stage.
