【Qing Dynasty Culture】Experts Believe the Four Major Spirits of the Manchu People Are Worth Learning From

Experts believe that the Manchu people were once a small ethnic group, and their rapid rise in history is a historical phenomenon worth paying attention to. Experts have summarized this into "four major spirits," and believe that learning from these four spirits has very important practical significance for promoting the history and culture of the Manchu people, advancing cultural prosperity, and revitalizing old industrial bases.

Experts such as Guan Jialu, Director of the Institute of History at the Liaoning Academy of Social Sciences and Director of the Manchu Studies Research Center, mentioned that the formation and rise of the Manchu people did not take a long time. In this brief history, they left behind rich and precious historical legacies, namely the "four spirits" of the Manchu people: the spirit of hard work and entrepreneurship; the innovative spirit of continuous exploration; the spirit of learning and progress; and the spirit of inclusiveness and unity. The core of these four spirits is inclusiveness and innovation. Without the spirit of inclusiveness and innovation, there would be no Qing culture. Inclusiveness determines the richness and diversity of Qing culture; innovation determines the development and uniqueness of Qing culture.

Firstly, the spirit of hard work and entrepreneurship. During the formation of the Manchu people and the rise of the Qing Dynasty, the spirit of positive progress and courage to face challenges was always promoted. At that time, "all soldiers wanted to achieve merit, and upon hearing of battle, none were not pleased. Everyone strove for the front lines, fought bravely, with power like thunder and momentum like the wind." This became a true reflection of the Manchu spirit, and this spirit was also one of the important spiritual driving forces for the continuous development and progress of the Manchu people.

Secondly, the innovative spirit of continuous exploration. Experts believe that from the early stages of the formation of the Manchu people and the Later Jin regime and the rise of the Qing Dynasty, there was no advantage to speak of. To unite all possible forces under the historical conditions at the time and stand firm on China's political stage, continuous innovation was necessary. To meet the needs of the struggle at the time, Nurhachi reformed the Niu Lu system and created a new social, political, economic, and military organizational form - the Eight Banners - which gradually developed into a national government organization form. This played an immeasurable role in the formation of the Manchu people and the rise of the Later Jin regime and the Qing Dynasty.

Thirdly, the spirit of learning and progress. During the formation and development of the Manchu people, their social development level was still significantly behind that of other ethnic groups such as the Han people. However, due to their willingness and ability to learn, they absorbed the essence of Han culture in many aspects. Most of the Manchu leaders, led by Nurhachi, and Manchu intellectuals were either recipients or proficient learners of Han culture. By drawing on the strengths of various cultures, they gradually formed a "combination of kindness and authority" political strategy during the process of accepting Han culture. They achieved the most political resources at the least cost, quickly making the Manchu people and the Later Jin regime and the Qing Dynasty significant forces on the political stage of China at that time.

Fourthly, the spirit of inclusiveness and unity. The Manchu people embraced everything like the sea, with a broad-mindedness to include all under heaven, uniting all forces that could be united. Qing history experts said that every ethnicity in the world has its own narrowness, just to varying degrees. During the formation of the Manchu people, they were able to largely overcome their own narrowness, continuously uniting different members of the same ethnicity and members of different ethnicities to fight together, forming a broad united front and maximizing their strength. This was an important foundation for the rise of the Manchu people, and incorporating members of different ethnicities into the Eight Banners is clear evidence of this.
