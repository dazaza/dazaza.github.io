The Ministry of Railways has asked CNR Corporation to respond to the issue of luxury EMU trains.

by king3h0a on 2012-03-06 09:11:25

On the afternoon of March 5, Sheng Guangzu, Minister of Railways, responded to a question from China Youth Daily regarding the "luxurious bullet trains," indicating that "[We have already requested CNR Corporation to respond to the media about the luxurious bullet trains]."

At around 2:40 PM that afternoon, Sheng Guangzu left the delegation site of Tibet and joined the review of the government work report by the Tibetan delegation, during which he answered questions from China Youth Daily.

Previously, New Century Weekly reported that a CNR sales catalog showed that on some bullet trains, one automatic washbasin cost 7.2395 million yuan, one marble washbasin cost 260,000 yuan, one感应 water valve cost 128,000 yuan, one restroom tissue holder cost 1,125 yuan, with the final assembly price reaching up to three or four hundred thousand yuan for an overall restroom; there were also 15-inch LCD monitors costing over ten thousand yuan, single seats priced at 220,000 yuan each, and refrigerated display cabinets worth 680,000 yuan... These bullet trains were thus called "luxurious bullet trains."

Sheng Guangzu said that CNR Corporation is not a superior enterprise of the Ministry of Railways, suggesting "[Ask CNR]." However, he indicated that the Ministry of Railways has already requested CNR Corporation to respond to the media on this issue.

During the 2012 Spring Festival travel rush, the Ministry of Railways expanded the implementation scope of the real-name system, implementing real-name ticket purchasing and checking nationwide for the first time. Regarding the real-name system for train tickets, Sheng Guangzu said that it "[still has certain effects] in combating ticket scalping."

Question: "[Are there any areas worthy of improvement?]"

He replied very affirmatively: "[Yes. This year is the first year we fully implemented the real-name system, (the Ministry of Railways) is summarizing, striving to improve it better. Our focus is on making it convenient for the public to buy tickets.]"

In October 2011, a serious scandal was exposed regarding the Jilin Jingyu to Songjianghe railway. A farmer who had previously worked as a chef and knew nothing about bridge construction led dozens of farmers to build a major railway project's special bridge after signing a "construction contract." As a result, many bridge piers originally supposed to be entirely concrete-cast were filled with large amounts of gravel, sandstone, and other mixed materials, leaving significant safety hazards. The contractors of these projects were also accused of being fraudsters.

After investigation, the Ministry of Railways found that the issues reported by the media, including contract fraud, illegal subcontracting and transfer of projects, and engineering quality problems, were true. This was identified as a typical serious liability incident concerning engineering quality. The Ministry of Railways required complete rework and the handling of responsible individuals.

Sheng Guangzu said that regarding the problems with this railway, the Ministry of Railways has already communicated the investigation conclusions and handling opinions. Regarding the lessons learned from this incident for railway construction, he said: "[From the bidding process, strict bidding procedures must be followed to avoid irregularities. After winning the bid, construction companies are prohibited from illegal subcontracting, and we will adopt central platform bidding and social platform bidding.]"

Sheng Guangzu also stated that the Ministry of Railways is currently signing agreements with the center to achieve the task of transferring railway courts and procuratorates to the center. It is understood that in order to implement the central arrangements for judicial system reform, the task of transferring railway courts and procuratorates to the center will basically be completed in the first half of the year.
