Chinese smartphone makers make second attempt to enter oversea market: Lenovo plans to acquire OPPO, forms alliance with CM

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 20:49:36

After a few years of competition, domestic brands have gradually adapted to the rhythm of the smartphone market competition and have secured a significant share of the Chinese market. However, outside the Chinese market in the global market, Samsung and Apple remain the absolute focus, while domestic smartphones can only play a supporting role with little presence.

As representatives of the first batch of smartphones venturing overseas, ZTE and Huawei have gained numerous mid-to-low-end phone orders through their long-term good cooperation with major global operators, quickly growing into the top positions in the global smartphone market. However, this path has little relevance for other domestic smartphone manufacturers.

In short, especially in overseas markets such as Europe and America, operators dominate absolutely, but Chinese smartphone manufacturers find it difficult to win the favor of major overseas operators. In this situation, how should Chinese smartphone manufacturers other than Huawei and ZTE go global?

While most manufacturers maintain a wait-and-see attitude, Lenovo and OPPO have begun to seek ways to break into overseas markets from different angles. Although their specific approaches are entirely different, both companies have chosen to increase added value in their brands to seek breakthroughs.

Lenovo: Focusing on Special Products and Attempting to Acquire Well-Known Phone Brands

At the beginning-of-the-month media briefing for the Yoga tablet, Yang Yuanqing, Chairman of the Board and CEO of Lenovo Group, revealed that Lenovo smartphones will enter mature markets in 2015, reserving a year's time to prepare more. Lenovo smartphones' brand image still needs to be re-established among the younger generation.

In fact, the Yoga tablet itself is a rehearsal for Lenovo's entry into mature markets. This product, which differs in shape from conventional tablets on the market, is not a product of the Yoga notebook team but of Lenovo's mobile phone team. It is reported that this product, regarded by Lenovo as the vanguard of its tablet products, will be launched simultaneously in multiple countries, including the European and American markets Lenovo smartphones plan to enter in the future.

An industry insider told Tencent Technology that when Lenovo smartphones enter mature markets, it will be difficult to use the same 'ocean of models' strategy used in the Chinese market; they can only rely on single flagship products to break into the market. However, he also noted that Lenovo's recognition in mature markets cannot yet compare with top-tier brands, so even if a single product is excellent, it will be hard to create a market explosion.

For this issue, Lenovo clearly considered it early on. According to information from investment banking circles, Lenovo did indeed participate in the bidding for BlackBerry. Although acquiring BlackBerry today is no longer realistic, Lenovo does intend to solve the shortcomings in its smartphone brand through acquisitions. With the recent news of Lenovo raising $1.5 billion, the possibility of making acquisitions has significantly increased.

OPPO: Aligning with CyanogenMod to Rebuild Professional Image

For Steve Kondik, opinions are often polarized—some have never heard of him, while others have admired him for a long time. This founder of CyanogenMod has long been hailed as the father of Android customization within the industry. Including Xiaomi, third-party ROMs have referenced or used CM's code at various levels. Luo Yonghao also paid tribute to him prominently at the launch event for the Hammer ROM.

Few people know that Steve and CyanogenMod have become one of OPPO's most important overseas partners. Four months ago, the OPPO Find 5 became the first domestically produced phone officially compatible with CM, and subsequently, OPPO was invited as the only domestic manufacturer to attend the XDA Developers Forum hosted by CM. Future collaborations between OPPO and CM will also be announced.

According to Tencent Technology's understanding, OPPO's overseas business is divided into the directly managed European and American markets and regional markets controlled by agents, including Indonesia, Russia, Vietnam, Thailand, etc. Outside of the European and American markets, OPPO often prefers to promote its flagship products using fashion and celebrities. However, in the US and European markets, OPPO uses CyanogenMod to attract smartphone enthusiasts and tech-savvy individuals first.

Clearly, OPPO aims to build a professional and enthusiast-oriented brand image in the US and European markets, consistent with the good reputation OPPO gained in overseas markets through Blu-ray DVDs. In fact, this approach has already achieved some effect, as OPPO has gained considerable recognition within the overseas smartphone enthusiast community.

Brand Becomes the Biggest Shortcoming for Domestic Smartphones

Industry insiders revealed that sales of smartphones in the US and Europe still mainly depend on operator channels, and these channels tend to cooperate with long-term clients. To enter the local operator’s centralized procurement list, the prerequisite is to possess unique brand value and target audiences, which is why Lenovo and OPPO prioritize branding as their first step into mature markets.

Both Lenovo and OPPO have chosen to focus heavily on branding as a breakthrough into mature markets. This consistency is not surprising, as even ZTE, whose smartphone share in the US has entered the top four, recently strengthened its brand influence in the North American market through an exclusive sponsorship deal with the NBA Houston Rockets.

Apparently, brand issues might be the first problem or prerequisite that domestic smartphones need to address when going global. Following brand issues, there are other concerns like patents and local policies that need attention. Now, Lenovo and OPPO's attempts have just taken the first step, and whether this round of Chinese smartphones can successfully go global can only be proven by time.
