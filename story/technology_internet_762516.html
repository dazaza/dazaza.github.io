Taobao draws a national "insomnia map": Shanghai has the most sleepless people

by anonymous on 2013-07-24 15:50:25

"Every day I go to bed around 11, then browse videos and online shops on my phone. I don't feel sleepy until around 1 a.m." Mr. Ding, a 30-year-old white-collar worker in a commercial building on Moganshan Road in Hangzhou, complained. In fact, more and more people are starting to have trouble sleeping. "People who sleep well are all similar, but those who can't sleep have various reasons," "Dear, why can't you sleep?" Taobao shopkeepers take advantage of the situation to sell sleep aids.

Yesterday, a set of data published by Taobao was also quite interesting. People who can't sleep have very obvious regional characteristics. In the top 5 cities with the highest per capita purchase volume of eye masks and earplugs in the past 5 months, the four first-tier cities of Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, and Shenzhen all made the list collectively. Hangzhou is the only non-first-tier city in the top five, ranking third.

The most active "insomnia tribe":

23 million people shop online at midnight

"At this time, is there anyone still awake?" Nowadays, such midnight insomnia tweets are becoming increasingly common. The "insomnia tribe" instantly transforms into the "night shopping tribe", using their mobile phones to shop on Taobao while lying in bed. The passion for shopping does not diminish due to the quiet night; instead, late night becomes one of the high-frequency times for mobile online shopping.

"In such hot weather, I definitely won't go out shopping during the day. When I can't sleep at night, I use that time to shop." Ms. Li from Hangzhou is a typical night owl. She said she often doesn't feel sleepy after 12 o'clock, so she opens her phone and browses Taobao. "Yesterday, I bought 2 pairs of sandals and 1 dress within an hour." Finally, with the satisfaction of shopping, she finally fell asleep. However, the money flows out every day.

There are quite a few such "night shopping tribes". According to the data released by Taobao, in 2012, 22.832 million people browsed Taobao between 11 p.m. and 5 a.m. Among them, many men also stay up late browsing Taobao, accounting for 48% of the total, while women account for 52%.

The most insomnia-prone provinces:

Jiangsu, Guangdong, Zhejiang

What should you do if you really can't sleep? More and more people are beginning to rely on external tools like earplugs and eye masks to fall asleep. Smart online shopkeepers have obviously already spotted the business opportunity. I searched for earplugs on Taobao and found more than 200,000 items for sale, and nearly 90,000 eye masks.

The "sleep aid market" is quite promising. Just the product of eye masks alone comes in various types such as 3D, cartoon patterns, summer-appropriate ice packs, silk eye masks, bamboo charcoal eye masks, etc. Since summer began, ice pack eye masks have become highly popular. On one Taobao store, I saw that the monthly transaction volume of a single item exceeded ten thousand pieces.

Many sellers are also making significant efforts in pillows, offering lavender pillows, buckwheat pillows, and even foods like millet and jujubes labeled as promoting sleep.

The "insomnia map" shows that according to the trading data of Taobao in the past 5 months, from the proportion of people buying earplugs and eye masks to the total population of the city, the top five include all first-tier cities: Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, and Shenzhen. Hangzhou, which ranks third, is the only non-first-tier city among them.

Top 10 cities for purchasing earplugs: Shanghai, Beijing, Hangzhou, Guangzhou, Shenzhen, Nanjing, Suzhou, Wuhan, Chongqing, Ningbo.

Top 10 cities for purchasing eye masks: Shanghai, Beijing, Hangzhou, Shenzhen, Guangzhou, Chongqing, Nanjing, Suzhou, Wuhan, Chengdu.

Shanghai is considered the most severely insomnia-prone city. Jiangsu, Guangdong, and Zhejiang are the three provinces with the worst sleep quality.

"The vicious cycle of not being able to sleep at night and not being able to wake up in the morning." Cities where people have trouble sleeping at night are also the cities where people have the hardest time waking up in the morning. In the past 5 months, the top five cities with the largest number of people buying alarm clocks are also Shanghai, Beijing, Hangzhou, Shenzhen, and Guangzhou.

"I have to wear earplugs every night to fall asleep, but then I can't hear my phone's alarm in the morning. After setting my phone's alarm to vibrate, I'm worried it won't be powerful enough to wake me up, so I bought another vibrating alarm clock." Xiao Mao's "sleep aid" and "wake-up" equipment has become essential for many insomniacs.

The most helpless reason for buying earplugs:

Husband snores

So who are the people suffering from insomnia? Online shopkeepers know best. "The number of people who need earplugs to sleep increases every year, and there are more customers consulting. The age range is also getting wider. Basically, students and urban white-collar workers account for 90% of the total sales," said an online shopkeeper selling earplugs.

Indeed, the signature product in the store—a 19 yuan earplug—sold 19,000 units in just one month.

Why can't everyone sleep? There are actually many reasons. It is understood that students who buy eye masks and earplugs may live in school dormitories. Mobile devices are now very popular among students. After bedtime, some classmates may still be playing games, so to avoid disturbance, they might as well "seal themselves off."

White-collar workers, on the other hand, experience greater work pressure during the day, leading to sleep problems at night. Some buyers even humorously say they bought earplugs because "my husband snores too loudly."

According to Taobao data, most buyers of earplugs and eye masks are "post-90s" and "post-80s." Taking the eye mask sales in the past 5 months as an example, the "post-90s" are the largest demand group, with 36% of eye masks purchased by people aged 18-24. Additionally, people aged 25-29 account for more than 30% of the total.

From the purchase ratio of various products, men are more likely to suffer from insomnia than women, with about 20% more male insomniacs than female.
