Cherish it well.

by ruydyir02 on 2012-02-09 12:17:02

I wrote this article by myself to commemorate the third anniversary of the love that had passed away.

At that moment, they loved each other. There was no shocking story, no heart-stirring encounter on the shaded path, and no romantic fireworks lighting up the night sky. It was merely an unintentional collision of glances in the classroom; they lowered their heads like guilty thieves and then peeked at each other again. At that moment, a fiery heat warmed their lonely hearts.

He and she were classmates. The seniors all said that in high school, many perplexing flowers of false love blossomed. They also believed in this statement and didn't dare to expect love in this naive time, so they chose to remain silent with each other.

She liked to quietly listen to his passionate voice answering the questions posed by the teacher. His voice deeply attracted her. She felt that his voice was like a piece of music, sometimes like ten thousand galloping horses, sometimes like a gentle stream in the valley, and sometimes like a low, resonant cello. She knew he was a very strange boy. She tried to start a conversation with him by passing a note filled with hope, which read: Can we chat? However, the note returned without any writing, and she felt a bit disappointed.

That faint feeling was like the lingering incense from a censer, surrounding her for a long time. She herself couldn't explain why she didn't talk to him face-to-face about her feelings. Was it because she feared that her remaining fantasy would be shattered instantly? Apparently, a mental block made her feel lost. She thought about it. If one person kept silently paying for the person they admired, when the other person realized it, they might remember them for a lifetime.

Thus, she quietly recorded every word he said in each mood. She wanted to imitate a complete version of him and draw him in her beloved notebook. When graduation came, she would give it to him, making him surprisingly confused, then moved, and then she would leave gracefully, leaving him with a mysterious memory. Thinking about it, she smiled.

He liked looking at her quiet appearance. She always looked down, as quiet as a gentle lamb, a very strange girl, what was she writing all day? Every time he shouted loudly, he wished she would look over, but she never did. He patted his own stupid head and thought that everything seemed to depend on fate.

Last time, there was a note that surprised him, but it was blank. He couldn't understand its meaning. So he drew a big question mark on the blank paper and sent it back, but there was no response. He encouraged himself many times to be brave, but he couldn't do it. Beauty always stayed in a moment. He was really confused. He began to sit quietly, sadly gazing at the blue sky, sighing at the white clouds in the blue sky, like the cotton candy he liked in childhood, strand by strand, so delicate. He wondered if people at this age were particularly fond of humming tunes, moaning without illness. Thinking about it, he laughed.

One day, he suddenly turned around and told me that he was going to leave here. I asked, why? He scratched his head unhappily and said that the school treated him like a soccer ball. Then he looked at her sitting behind me and said meaningfully to me, take good care of her.

I understood the meaning in his words. Guiltily, I said, before you leave, I want to tell you the truth, my friend. He got up and left the classroom without listening to my explanation. He turned back, waved his hand, and said, I knew from the beginning, take care. Watching his departing figure, I felt very desolate, thinking about what I had done before that didn't make me happy, and had suppressed me for a long time.

She was also a secretly loving girl. The distance between us three was very subtle. He sat in front of me, and she sat behind me. When they decided to pass notes to express their feelings, a note full of love gently passed through me. At that moment, I made a blind decision. I tried to alter the content to cause some misunderstanding between them, but I overlooked the fact that true feelings don't need words to summarize. There was something called emotion intertwined between us, but now I think about it. In that love gamble without winners or losers, I was actually playing the role of a thief using small tricks, discarding the original sincerity.

On the evening of the graduation day, I looked at that girl, and my heart was especially calm. The moonlight was overflowing, and the pale moonlight filled her entire face. Was it happiness or reluctance? No one knew, maybe we all knew the answer.

I separated from a group of classmates who were hugging each other reluctantly, walked outside the classroom to the corridor, leaned on the railing, and watched the stars, enjoying the last bit of inner peace.

After a long time, I looked at her and said lightly, do you know, he has always liked you. The girl smiled, looked back at me, and then pointed at the star shining brightly in the night sky with her finger, smiling and said, look, it represents eternity.

Our story quietly came to an end with the night sky. That day, I wanted to say something but hesitated and didn't say it. Is everything the end? I thought about it and decided to let our once confused collision of hearts become eternal. Maybe we won't see each other again soon, each packing our luggage to go to a distant place for further studies. And in the midst of my excitement, I learned how to love.

In the evening, I had a strange dream. Pairs of men and women pointed at me and laughed, saying, that guy is so old-fashioned. Suddenly woke up, and I heard the words of the seniors: In high school, many perplexing flowers of false love bloom; that flower full of great kindness will never wither, swaying in our memory riverbed.

I exhaled deeply, tiredly closed my eyes again, and fell into a deep sleep...
