Scientific cancer prevention: Stomach cancer tends to "attack" which types of people

The peak age for stomach cancer onset is 50 to 80 years old, but Xu Chong'an introduced that there has been a trend towards younger ages in recent years. High-risk groups for stomach cancer include: those suffering from moderate to severe gastric ulcers, atrophic gastritis, intestinal metaplasia or dysplasia; those who have been infected with Helicobacter pylori; more than 10 years after stomach surgery; those who like to eat hard, pickled products and high-salt foods with gastric discomfort symptoms; those with pernicious anemia; those with a family history of stomach cancer and esophageal cancer; obese individuals, those who suffer mental stimulation and depression; and those who work long-term in dusty environments.

Never underestimate these early warning signs

(1) Upper abdominal discomfort and fullness Often feels like abdominal burning, noise, and fullness, especially after meals, and as the condition progresses, the symptoms worsen. These symptoms need to be differentiated from indigestion and chronic gastritis. Indigestion often has a history of improper diet and binge eating, while chronic gastritis often has a history of repeated episodes.

(2) Loss of appetite Early stomach cancer often suddenly manifests as loss of appetite and aversion to greasy foods. This needs to be differentiated from hepatitis. Hepatitis often has elevated transaminases and systemic symptoms such as fever, fatigue, dark yellow urine like strong tea, and jaundice.

(3) Nausea, belching, acid reflux, and vomiting When the stomach cancer lesion is located in the pylorus area of the stomach outlet, nausea is most obvious. If the stomach outlet is completely blocked, a sour or egg-smelling gas will be belched out, or vomiting will occur, with vomitus mostly being undigested food and gastric juice.

(4) Upper abdominal pain Early stomach cancer pain is irregular, or manifested as persistent dull pain, unlike gastric or duodenal ulcers which have obvious postprandial pain or preprandial pain characteristics. If the patient originally had gastric or duodenal ulcers, the regularity of the pain can suddenly change, and drugs that were effective for treating ulcers suddenly become ineffective or significantly less effective.

(5) Hematemesis and black stool If the tumor only destroys small blood vessels, it is often manifested as occult blood in the stool, where the stool appears normal but tests reveal blood cells. If larger blood vessels are invaded early on, it causes hematemesis, black stool, or tar-like stool. The occult blood and black stool in stomach cancer are persistent and stubborn, whereas bleeding caused by gastric or duodenal ulcers is often intermittent and can be stopped with timely treatment.

(6) Rapid weight loss and severe anemia Molecular cancer prevention experts remind that because cancer is a consuming disease, and stomach cancer can cause poor digestion and absorption and gastrointestinal bleeding in patients, thus aggravating weight loss and anemia. Not all six of these conditions may be present in one patient, nor do they necessarily coexist simultaneously. As long as one of them appears, especially in males over 40 years old, it should be taken seriously, and necessary examinations should be conducted at the hospital promptly. Expert cancer prevention: Preventing stomach cancer starts with preventing stomach diseases

Stomach cancer is one of the world's common malignant tumors. In our country, the mortality rate of stomach cancer accounts for 23% of all malignant tumor deaths, ranking first among various cancer deaths, and there are even records of children aged seven or eight suffering from stomach cancer, so the prevention and treatment of stomach cancer is urgent. Currently, medical research experts have preliminarily determined that Helicobacter pylori is an important risk factor and initiating factor for stomach cancer. Populations in high-incidence areas of stomach cancer have higher infection rates of Helicobacter pylori (abbreviated as Hp hereafter), and the age of infection is also earlier. Moreover, as age increases, both the infection rate of Hp and the incidence rate of stomach cancer increase synchronously, and the risk of developing stomach cancer for Hp-infected individuals is 4-6 times higher than for non-infected individuals. Therefore, just by preventing or curing Hp infection, it seizes an important link in preventing stomach cancer. It also indicates that preventing stomach cancer must start from childhood, such as performing timely endoscopic examination on children with stomach diseases and providing radical treatment if Hp infection is found.

Studies by molecular cancer prevention experts also reveal that those who are frequently exposed to stomach cancer factors from a young age are prone to develop stomach cancer. In addition to Hp being identified as the first-class stomach cancer carcinogen by the World Health Organization in 1994, i.e., a confirmed carcinogen, nitrosamines are also possible stomach cancer-causing substances. Nitrosamines are the products formed by the reduction of nitrates to nitrites and then combining with amines, and both nitrates and nitrites are widely present in pickled vegetables, salted vegetables, salted fish, salted meat, smoked foods, etc., so reducing the intake of these foods is beneficial for preventing stomach cancer. Other methods for adjusting diet to prevent stomach cancer include: low-salt diet, which can reduce the intake of nitrates and nitrites. Drinking more milk. Eating more fresh vegetables and fruits, where the abundant vitamin C inhibits the combination of nitrites and amines. Relevant surveys also confirm that populations in high-incidence areas of stomach cancer who take vitamin C long-term have fewer cases of stomach cancer. Advocating the consumption of refrigerated food. Japan is a high-incidence country for stomach cancer, and the incidence rate has decreased in recent years because Japan views stomach cancer as a "national disease" and has implemented various comprehensive preventive measures and popularized health knowledge for preventing stomach cancer, including the widespread use of refrigerators.

Medical research has discovered that the evolution process from normal stomach to stomach cancer often goes through a relatively long stage. Generally, the rapid type of stomach cancer development period requires 7-8 years, the ordinary type lasts up to 20-22 years, and the evolution from early-stage stomach cancer to mid-late stage stomach cancer still takes about 3-5 years. The progression from Helicobacter pylori infection to stomach cancer takes 10-20 years. Therefore, as long as attention is paid, there is ample time to prevent the occurrence of stomach cancer. The precancerous lesions of stomach cancer are very easy to discover and diagnose. Such as chronic atrophic gastritis, gastric ulcer, gastric polyps, remnant gastritis, verrucous gastritis, intestinal metaplasia, dysplasia, etc. Therefore, actively curing the above diseases is also an important measure to prevent stomach cancer. As long as doctors and patients closely cooperate, these diseases can be thoroughly cured. Mild intestinal metaplasia and dysplasia can also be reversed. Even moderate dysplasia, which is highly likely to transform into stomach cancer, or severe dysplasia that should be treated as stomach cancer, can block the occurrence of stomach cancer through surgery. There are also many Chinese herbs that have the effects of regulating and enhancing the body's immune function, treating precancerous lesions, and fighting cancer, such as monkey head mushroom tablets, Weifuchun tablets, etc. Therefore, patients with precancerous lesions of stomach cancer can appropriately choose these Chinese herbs.
