"Captain America" Aims to Break Global Box Office Records, Domestic Release Imminent

by tvbing21 on 2011-07-28 22:39:51

The full-length trailer of "Captain America" was released a few weeks ago at the Superhero Film Festival (Hero Complex Film Festival), featuring more previously unreleased, stunning action scenes. Notably, the abundant action sequences are perfectly blended with the historical atmosphere of 1942. Some audiences had previously thought that "Captain America's" retro style might seem outdated, but after watching this trailer, their opinions will likely change.

"Captain America" will be released nationwide in the U.S. on July 22nd in both 2D and 3D formats. For more information, please visit Qian Du Movie Website.

Like the previous trailer, this one also spends a great deal of time portraying how Steve Rogers, played by Chris Evans, transforms from a scrawny recruit rejected by the army (with special effects reminiscent of "The Curious Case of Benjamin Button") into a superhero. For non-comic fans, it helps them understand the backstory of the film and build an emotional connection with the characters. Later, the villainous Red Skull's (Hugo Weaving) formidable army, HYDRA, makes its first appearance.

The entire story revolves around a man who has no choice but to accept his fate, as he is destined to become the world's first Avenger... It is 1941, and amidst the chaos of war, the sights of injury and death are everywhere. As a spirited young man, Steve Rogers (Chris Evans) repeatedly volunteers for military service to join the Allies and fight alongside his compatriots on the front lines, only to be rejected each time due to his frail physique. Eventually, Steve, who never gave up, is recruited into a military experiment program, where he is transformed into a super soldier known as Captain America. With his new image, covered in rippling muscles, Captain America, with the help of his best friend Bucky Barnes (Sebastian Stan) and the confident Peggy Carter (Hayley Atwell), is directly commanded and led by Colonel Chester Phillips (Tommy Lee Jones). He soon launches a war against an evil organization - a deeply hidden Nazi scientific experimental division, led by the sinister Red Skull (Hugo Weaving).
