The liver is the largest solid organ in the body. In traditional Chinese medicine (TCM), it is metaphorically referred to as the general of the army, highlighting its importance. Relevant experts informed that when protecting the liver, using some TCM methods appropriately can yield better results.

Typically, there are five TCM methods for liver protection:

Firstly, TCM emphasizes emotional health. The five organs correspond to five emotions: anger, joy, thought, sorrow, and fear. Among these, the liver corresponds to anger, meaning that anger harms the liver. Therefore, one should pay attention to controlling their emotions. Long-term depression or sudden bursts of anger can disrupt the liver's blood and qi, affecting its function of promoting free flow. When feeling emotionally unsettled, one should find a way to vent negative emotions, such as confiding in a close friend, rather than suppressing them, which could harm the liver.

Secondly, TCM particularly stresses regular rest and routine living. According to TCM’s concept of Zi Wu Liu Zhu, the liver and gallbladder are most active between 11 PM and 3 AM, during which time all the internal organs' blood passes through the liver, reaching its peak detoxification function. Hence, people should follow this natural rhythm and ensure adequate rest at this time. Moreover, TCM believes that lying down allows blood to return to the liver. Thus, the best way to nourish the liver is through proper rest. Avoiding overwork and replenishing energy timely ensures the liver can regulate blood and qi, reducing feelings of fatigue.

Thirdly, TCM advocates dietary supplementation for liver protection. Traditional Chinese medicine has the theory of five-colored diets, where green relates to the liver, entering the liver meridian. Therefore, eating more green foods like spinach, kale, mustard greens, cucumber, winter melon, and mung beans can nourish yin, moisten dryness, soothe the liver, and nourish blood. Additionally, since the liver prefers sour flavors based on the principle of sour entering the liver, consuming vinegar regularly can benefit the liver and prevent colds. Some sour foods, like hawthorn, cornus fruit, and goji berries, have liver-protective effects. For liver care, one should avoid spicy, greasy foods and limit alcohol consumption.
