How to use your spare time to train your beloved dog

by wendj on 2009-11-17 19:49:47

How to use your spare time to train your Scottish Shepherd dog, many friends raise dogs in order to have more leisure time, but this should not affect your other hobbies and playtime. Today, Su Mu Zhi Jia will teach you how to achieve both.

Find excitement in playing

Play some beneficial games with your dog to promote its physical and mental development. Dogs are naturally alert, and lack of activity can lead to boredom syndrome, and a mentally exhausted dog can be dangerous. Even the simplest game can enhance the bond between you and your puppy, and through play, you can also strengthen your leadership authority.

How to make full use of toys

When you finish playing with your puppy, remember to put away the gaming equipment. This way, the puppy will soon understand that these things belong to the owner, and it must have your permission to play with these toys.

Balloon game

These dogs are jumping and chasing balloons enthusiastically. Although the balloons cannot actually be caught, it is still a good test to understand the intelligent reactions of the puppy.

Feelings about humans and dogs

Walking on the bustling streets of the city, you will find that people passing by are all in a hurry, expressionless; entering each office, you will see colleagues properly entertaining each other with colorful masks, while their inner feelings are completely hidden; living in a fast-paced rhythm, sometimes you really feel physically and mentally exhausted, feeling lonely inside; occasionally, you really want to find a close friend to vent your frustrations, open up your damp heart, but the coldness inherent in the city has made friends and sincerity become legends, you and I today are just lonely stars in the urban sky.

In my loneliness, I chose the Internet, choosing a corner of the forum. "Why didn't you think of raising a dog when you were lonely?" A friend online once asked me this question and talked about many benefits and experiences of raising a dog...

"Anyone who has raised a dog will have such an experience. After certain training, dogs can understand their owner's words. They are very empathetic and can feel the owner's happiness and sorrow from the subtle gestures and speech. Dogs that have undergone special training will have broader communication with humans, mastering many special skills, and their intelligence can reach the level of a six to seven-year-old child.

Dogs are so cute, why don't you take a puppy home? Your family will definitely spoil it like having another child. The atmosphere at home will be different. The former coldness will be replaced by warmth and joy, and everyone's face will be filled with smiles. The slightly strained relationships caused by daily family trivialities soften, and there is an unprecedented tacit understanding on the issue of the pet dog baby. Even the air smells warm and harmonious.

As the dog grows day by day, its relationship with the whole family will deepen day by day. You will increasingly understand its temperament, and it will always observe you, getting familiar with everyone's temperaments in the house. It will get along well with people and cleverly deal with each person.

It is absolutely loyal to you. Even if it gets scolded or beaten, it never holds grudges and remains affectionate with you. Even if you abuse it or harm it, it will not retaliate, only avoid and endure. Sometimes, seeing its silly loyalty, you may feel a pang of sadness. Isn't it just the kind that would count the money for the master even after being sold? More often, you will feel it is your most reliable friend, especially in today's world where human relations are as thin as paper, the state of affairs is unpredictable, and false trends are rampant. You will feel spiritual comfort because of its sincerity.

Over time, the dog will completely integrate into your life. For single children who are alone, the dog is the best companion. Children and dogs are like siblings, depending on each other, neither can live without the other. The child's nature will be released in the game with the dog, and the child's love will gradually grow in the company of the dog.

The dog's participation awareness is very strong. Since it is one of the family members, it needs to take care of one part and bear one responsibility. Family members go to school or work, and it sends them off one by one; after school or work, it warmly welcomes them back one by one. If someone hasn't returned when they should have, it will sit outside the door waiting until it hears familiar footsteps.

It doesn't like to see anyone quarreling in the house. Occasionally, if someone argues, it will run over and bark, demanding that they stop immediately. When the children fight, it will run over to mediate. If they don't listen, it will rudely drag the two apart by their clothes. If there are elderly widows or widowers in the house, raising a dog can bring a lot of fun and comfort. Having a lovely dog around is like having a faithful companion, and they will no longer feel lonely or isolated. Once you raise a dog, you will never let it go..."

I have never raised a dog. Hearing what my friend said, although I found it interesting, I felt a strange sense of sadness deep inside. Is it true that in today's rapidly developing society, people choose to raise dogs for entertainment rather than communicating with others when they are alone and lonely?
