Computer suddenly black screen, color distortion, blue screen?

by lk0cswu on 2009-08-18 14:01:50

Frequently encountered, how to prevent it? Seriously, after that, even the computer can't be turned on??

1. When the monitor shows a black screen without image display (although currently available monitors will display "no signal connection" when no signal is sent from the host), first check whether the power supply of the main unit is properly plugged in, whether the fan of the power supply is rotating? Is the power indicator light and hard disk indicator light on the main unit panel flashing? Because if the main unit's power supply does not work or the motherboard is not powered, the monitor will naturally not display an image when it does not receive a signal.

2. Then check if the power supply of the monitor is properly connected? If your monitor's power switch is a touch switch, you should hear a slight crackling sound when you power on the monitor, indicating that the switch power supply circuit of the monitor is good. Is the power switch of the monitor already turned on? Is the power indicator light of the monitor on? When you bring your hand close to the monitor screen and slowly move it, is there a hissing sound, and at the same time, do the hairs on your hand feel like they are being pulled up? This checks whether the high-voltage circuit of the monitor is working normally.

3. If you have confirmed that the monitor has been powered on and high voltage is generated, continue checking whether the data cable connector and the signal output interface of the graphics card are making good contact? Is there any looseness? Then unplug the connector and check again, whether there are bent or broken pins or a lot of dirt in the D-type interface. This is a problem that many users often encounter. When connecting the D-type port, force is applied unevenly, or the fixing screws of the interface are forgotten to be tightened, causing loose connections resulting in poor contact, or due to improper installation methods or excessive force, causing broken or bent needles inside the D-type port, leading to poor contact.

Note: The 15-pin data line plug of the monitor may have missing pins, such as pins 4, 9, and 11, which is normal. Do not artificially use other metal wires to supplement these missing pin positions to avoid causing other faults.

Let me add a bit more: Pins 1, 2, and 3 are for red, green, and blue gun signal inputs. If one of these pins has poor contact, the screen will lack the corresponding color.

If there is a problem with pins 12 and 15, the main unit cannot detect the corresponding monitor, but this does not affect usage.

If there is a problem with pins 13 and 14, the monitor will show a black screen, and the indicator light of the monitor will change from green to orange after turning on the machine for a while. This is one of the aforementioned black screens.

4. Open the case and check if the graphics card is installed correctly? Whether it makes good contact with the motherboard slot? Whether the graphics card or slot has accumulated too much dust due to long-term use, causing poor contact? Whether there are burnt or cracked traces on the chip of the graphics card. When the graphics card causes a black screen, there will be a short beep followed by four long beeps during the computer's power-on self-test. When installing the graphics card, hold the upper part of the graphics card, apply even force to insert it into the slot, making the screw holes of the graphics card match with those of the main unit. Do not forcibly fix it if it is not inserted correctly, which may cause the graphics card to twist. If you confirm that it is installed correctly, you can remove the graphics card and clean the gold fingers of the plug with alcohol swabs, or try installing it in another slot (only applicable to PCI graphics cards). If it still doesn't work, you can only try another good graphics card.

If it still doesn't work and the graphics card is confirmed to be intact, consider the compatibility between the graphics card and the motherboard. It's best to check relevant materials or ask online friends.

5. Check if other expansion cards (including sound cards, decompression cards, video capture cards) make good contact with the motherboard slots? Pay attention to whether the data lines and power lines of the hard drive are connected correctly? Change the slots of other expansion cards and clean the pins. Many people often overlook this point. Generally, it is thought that the computer's black screen is related to the monitor and graphics card, and has nothing to do with other devices. In fact, incorrect installation of sound cards and other devices can lead to system initialization difficulties, especially when the data lines and power lines of the hard drive are plugged incorrectly, easily causing no display faults.

6. Check if the memory stick makes good contact with the motherboard? Reinsert the memory stick or try another slot, or replace it with a new one. If there is a problem with the memory stick, the computer will emit continuous urgent beeps when starting. The warning sound may vary depending on the motherboard.

7. Check if the CPU makes good contact with the CPU socket on the motherboard? Due to moving or other factors, the CPU may have poor contact with the SLOT1 socket or SOCKE370 socket. It's best to press the CPU by hand or remove and reinstall the CPU.

8. Check if the jumper settings or CMOS settings for the CPU external frequency, multiplier, and memory frequency are correct. Refer to the motherboard manual and check each related jumper in sequence: CPU external frequency jumper - CPU multiplier jumper - memory frequency jumper.

9. Check if the CPU voltage is set appropriately. Be careful when setting the CPU voltage jumpers, they must correspond to the CPU operating voltage. Steps eight and nine should be carefully checked for some assembled machines or users who like overclocking when encountering a black screen.

10. Check if the CMOS parameter settings are correct. If your computer has two graphics cards, and you set the first initialization to the PCI graphics card in the CMOS, but your only monitor is connected to the AGP graphics card, then the monitor will not light up.

11. Check if the working environment required by the main unit and monitor is suitable? Is the working voltage normal, and is the ambient temperature too high? In addition to following the above steps for inspection, you can also quickly locate the issue based on the computer's working status. For example, after turning on the main unit's power, if you hear a beep indicating that the computer has completed its self-check, and if the hard disk indicator light keeps flashing at this time, then you should check steps two to four.

12. If the monitor displays content during the computer startup process, but shows a black screen after loading the WIN98 interface, then this is just a problem with the WIN98 system software.

The above inspection method is based on the assumption that the monitor itself has no electrical fault, i.e., after turning on the main unit's power, the power indicator light of the monitor changes from green to yellow but the monitor shows a black screen without image display. If the monitor still has no display after using the above steps, please consult professional personnel for repair.

There are several reasons, one being hardware issues with the graphics card itself, such as minor damage to the graphics card's video memory, poor craftsmanship or material quality of the graphics card, or poor compatibility, all of which can significantly reduce the electrical performance of the graphics card. If such a situation occurs, it's best to confirm using the substitution method. Since an AGP8x graphics card is used, the user can also try adjusting the graphics card's operating speed to AGP4x in the BIOS. Also, the graphics card should not be easily overclocked. Additionally, frequent crashes when playing 3D games may also be due to poor heat dissipation of the graphics card. Please carefully check if the cooler of the graphics card is functioning normally.

Secondly, apart from issues with the graphics card itself, it may also be related to other components, such as insufficient power supply to the motherboard's AGP slot. You can try appropriately increasing the working voltage of the AGP slot in the motherboard BIOS. Other components like memory and CPU having quality issues may also cause frequent crashes when playing 3D games.
