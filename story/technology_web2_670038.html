Hidden food safety hazards in bakery shops

by hdghu19056 on 2012-02-20 14:46:20

On July 28, Beijing announced its "12th Five-Year Plan" action plan for food safety, increasing the requirements for public disclosure of food information. Consumers can report food safety hazards at any time. However, recently, reporters visited several well-known bakeries in Beijing and found that common issues included insufficient bread weight, failure to disclose food additives, incomplete or incorrect price tag information.

Experts suggest strengthening supervision

Regarding the reasons why bakeries do not take "price tag information" seriously, Associate Professor Liang Jianfen from the College of Food Science and Nutritional Engineering at China Agricultural University analyzed that on one hand, consumers who shop at branded bakeries generally have stronger purchasing power and are more concerned about the taste and style of the bread, paying less attention to the weight; on the other hand, relevant regulations are not very strict on the weight requirements for ready-to-eat food, and the restrictions on whether dishes, snacks, bread, and pastries are sufficient are relatively loose. Therefore, some bakeries do not care much about this. It is hoped that relevant departments will formulate more detailed law enforcement criteria for such special industries.

After overnight, the bread became flat

Mr. Liu bought a few breads at noon the day before yesterday at a bakery located at the southeastern corner of SITONG Bridge. Due to his busy work schedule, he quickly chewed two of them and put the remaining meat floss bread into a bag, intending to bring it home to eat.

The next morning, Mr. Liu discovered that the two fist-sized meat floss breads had shrunk into a long thin strip shape. "I didn't press it, nor did I squeeze it, how did it turn out like this?" Mr. Liu was puzzled.

Ms. He encountered an issue with bread weight reduction. She went to a bakery near Princess Tomb Cuiwei Department Store to buy bread. She has been buying bread as breakfast for a long time. Recently, she noticed that the price of bread has risen significantly, but it doesn't last long when eaten. It only takes three or two bites to finish one, and it's hard to get full without spending 20 yuan on two or three pieces. Thus, she decided to check how heavy the bread actually was.

In front of the meat floss bread tray stood a price tag indicating a weight of 120 grams. Initially, Ms. He thought nothing of it, but then she suddenly noticed that the hot dog bread lying next to the meat floss bread also weighed 120 grams. The hot dog bread was considerably larger than the meat floss bread and had a sausage as "solid goods" to support it, yet both breads weighed the same.

Ms. He purchased this meat floss bread and asked the store clerk to weigh it, but the clerk stated, "Our store does not provide this service."

Fifteen minutes later, Ms. He arrived at her workplace canteen and asked the chefs to help weigh the meat floss bread. The electronic scale clearly showed "60 grams," which was half the weight indicated on the price tag.

Ms. He was furious. She called the bakery's hotline and demanded an explanation for "how the bread weight was measured." Initially, the staff replied that "the bread weight was uniformly measured upon leaving the factory." Subsequently, they returned the call informing her: "The dough weight of the meat floss bread is 50 grams, the meat floss weight is 12 grams, taking into account losses, the total weight is approximately 60 grams. The 120 grams indicated on the price tag was mistakenly printed, and they expressed willingness to compensate the consumer."

Labels do not specify additives

Are Mr. Liu and Ms. He encountering black stores? Over the past two days, reporters visited seven different branded bakeries in areas such as Princess Tomb Cuiwei Department Store, Shuangjing Bridge Fuli Plaza, Oriental Plaza, and Chongwenmen Xinyi World. Hardly any of these bakeries disclosed product information according to the relevant regulations.

At a bakery located at the southwest corner of Shuangjing Bridge, people lined up to buy bread continuously, and long queues were a common occurrence. However, on the price tags of the bread for sale here, apart from the name and price information, all other information, including weight, was not provided. When the reporter inquired with the store clerk, the clerk looked puzzled and asked the reporter, "What information needs to be labeled?"

At a bakery on the west side road of Cuiwei Department Store, seventy percent of the price tags indicated weight information, and most of the price tags indicated a weight of 120 grams.

In the aforementioned visited bakeries, none of the bakeries used various signs to clearly inform customers of the bread's food additive components. Experts told the reporter that during the bread processing process, "it is inevitable to add food additives."

Citizen Ms. Zhao believes that when bread is sold, consumers can only see ingredients such as bacon, ham, meat floss, and fruits placed on the surface of the bread with their naked eyes. Whether food additives have been added to the bread cannot be seen. Only by explicitly informing consumers can they purchase with peace of mind.
