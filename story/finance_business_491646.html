Various helplessness in life

by rawtaomgf on 2011-09-19 18:07:49

I look forward to the long river of time. I hope that some helplessness can slowly disappear. If so, I will not doubt the happiness that has just approached, and perhaps they really existed in life. I am waiting for time to seal the bitterness of the past. But when will those carefree days and those smiling people also walk away with time?

My grandmother is also getting old like this. Nowadays, she is over eighty years old and cannot tell the people and things around her clearly. Before, she would occasionally wake up for a while. When I went back to see her, she always asked repeatedly: whose child are you? When she realized that the person in front of her was her own granddaughter, she would be happy like a child for a while. She would hold my hand and ask me to accompany her, show me the newly bloomed flowers, and the orange tree by the door.

The last time I saw her, her memory seemed to have faded even more. She still held my arm, but she could no longer recognize me. She said, I miss my granddaughter very much. She hasn't come to see me for a long time, maybe she's too busy.

One night, I dreamed that I slept in my grandmother's room, just like when I was a child. I lay there extremely tired, she called me to take off my clothes, and gently covered me with a quilt. The grandmother in my dream still had faint eyebrows and a calm expression. I only wish that she is now sleeping and waking up calmly, and will no longer be troubled by those helpless worries.

That thing must have been on her mind for a long time alone. Every time she saw me, every time she remembered who I was, she would pull me aside with a serious expression and talk about her troubles. Once she realized that I didn't take it seriously, she would grab my arm tightly and say anxiously: you were never like this before, why are you so disobedient now? I hurt everyday, this matter in my heart can no longer wait.

Nowadays, they bend their waists under the hard work of their whole lives. Their hands can no longer stroke my head, they are inevitably getting old.

At that moment, a taste flooded my heart heavily. I still held her hand, but how could she know that the person she misses is right beside her, watching the flowers that haven't yet faded and the short fruitless tree together with her.

There are many helplessnesses in life, as well as so-called happy times, always following closely behind. Helplessness exists in everyone's life, more or less, just the content is different, and the symbols are not entirely the same. Therefore, we cannot deny that this is originally the misfortune of being human. We can avoid talking about our helpless experiences, but we are powerless to push away the bitter taste of facing it, unable to answer and express it. Life may be like this, everyone's life carries their own regrets that they cannot let go, it is they that make the emotions of life intertwine like day and night, joys and sorrows all along, with various tastes.

Just like I watch the dearest people grow old quickly, unable to retain them, helpless. Once upon a time, they were so energetic, I never thought they had a time of fatigue. They came home happily from the fields carrying hoes, bringing fresh fruits and vegetables along the way; they just took off their aprons and served hot meals, then turned around to plan for tomorrow's life; they looked far away anxiously in the wind, and what they loved in their eyes was such an ignorant and young me; once upon a time, did they lose to the life they supported with one hand?

Occasionally, she would pull me aside and complain: they were extremely mean to me, forcing me to eat those things! After asking my father, I learned that she was forced to drink a bowl of milk every day now. Her aggrieved appearance made me laugh and cry at the same time, where was the shadow of the fearless bride who once lifted her red veil and gambled on the mahjong table as she narrated herself?

Every time she muttered about it, I just slowly broke free from her hand and changed the topic, making her frown and pout in anger at my response. I always thought that time would make her forget such thoughts. The only compromise I don't want to make in my life, and the only obsession of an elderly person, met in this dilemma. What could I do? I think that the current mood should be the most helpless bitterness in my life.

When there was no one around, she would hold my hand and say to me very seriously: come on, I want to tell you something.
