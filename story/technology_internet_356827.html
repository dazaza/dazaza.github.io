The prosecutor handling the case said that Grandma Liu bought 31 apartments in Dagang Haiyue Maternal Residence, most of which were single-person apartments. She also bought two villas.

It was this "extravagant and fashionable" Grandma Liu who convinced many people to lend her money without hesitation. Even some residents from the same neighborhood lent her money.

Moreover, many of the properties under Grandma Liu's name were pre-sale units, which could not be sold promptly to repay debts. With no other choice, Grandma Liu had to take out high-interest loans, with monthly interest rates reaching 8%, beginning a life of borrowing money to repay debts.

However, the "extraordinary" Grandma Liu suddenly found no one knocking on her door to collect debts anymore.

"A very fashionable and capable old lady, always going out late at night and coming back early in the morning, not easily bumping into her," said a neighbor.

The prosecutor stated that later, even if Grandma Liu sold all her existing properties, it would still not be enough to repay her debts.

In the scorching heat of the afternoon, outside a residential area in Jiaochuan Street, Zhenhai, Ningbo, there was still some suffocating warmth. People inquired about Grandma Liu from an aunt passing by.

This "extravagant and trustworthy" old lady deceived many people.

By 2008, Grandma Liu borrowed over 10 million yuan from more than 40 victims, ranging from 10,000 yuan to 1.5 million yuan. The money Grandma Liu borrowed was used for 2 million yuan as down payments for houses, 2.6 million yuan for bank mortgages, and the rest for repaying high-interest loans.

Eventually, when Grandma Liu could no longer repay the loans, the creditors reported her to the police. On August 27, 2009, Grandma Liu was arrested by the police.

"Invested in 30-some apartments, but ended up investing herself into 'here'?" said a neighbor.

Grandma Liu lived in a small state-owned enterprise family building located in Jiaochuan Street, Zhenhai. The community was built in the 1980s, and after more than 20 years, it inevitably appeared somewhat shabby.

However, after a series of renovations, Grandma Liu's home stood out much more. The white wall tiles clearly distinguished it from the walls of her neighbors. Under the windows, three air conditioner outdoor units were neatly lined up. "I heard the interior decoration is also very luxurious," said a resident.

A resident in the community told the reporter that the last time they saw Grandma Liu was on a night in the second half of last year. "Around 7 or 8 PM, in the alley, her mother-in-law quietly told the debt collector 'If I have money, I will definitely pay you back.'"

Ningbo’s real estate agent Mr. Shen said that due to the impact of financial crises and housing loan policies, Ningbo's housing prices experienced dramatic fluctuations, like riding a roller coaster, in 2007 and 2008.

Grandma Liu, aged 58, was a retired worker from Zhenhai Refining & Chemical. In late 2005, Grandma Liu discovered the business opportunity in real estate, believing it could make big profits. However, due to insufficient capital, she began borrowing money from friends and acquaintances, with monthly interest rates reaching 3% to 5%.

In recent years, because she was able to repay the loans on time, people regarded her as very trustworthy and beneficial, so they lent her money. "Word of mouth spread, and even people from other neighborhoods who didn't know her lent her money," said an aunt in the neighborhood.

After the incident, many victims concealed the matter from their families, fearing reproach from their children. One grandmother even secretly lent 700,000 yuan to Grandma Liu.

According to Grandma Liu herself, in the first half of 2007, she was involved in a car accident and sustained facial injuries. After the news spread, some lenders started coming to her door to demand repayment.

What kind of old lady was she, who could easily deceive such large amounts of money?

In the eyes of the prosecutor handling the case, Grandma Liu was different from ordinary grandmothers. "She had quick thinking and a good memory, clearly remembering details such as the layout, area, house number, and down payment for the more than 30 houses she purchased."

The evaluations from her neighbors often mentioned "a bit extravagant, fashionable, articulate, and capable of causing trouble."

This Grandma Liu was a notable figure locally, though not necessarily well-known. Today, the elderly woman was prosecuted by the Zhenhai Procuratorate on suspicion of illegally absorbing public deposits.
