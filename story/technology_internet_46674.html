In 2007, Haier topped the list of China's most valuable brands, while Lenovo ranked second with a value of 65.8 billion yuan.

by zhaoyue0438 on 2008-02-02 14:30:29

The Haier brand is ranked first for six consecutive years with a value of 78.6 billion yuan, Lenovo is second with a value of 65.8 billion yuan, and Gome is third with a value of 49 billion yuan. They are followed by Wuliangye at 40.218 billion yuan, TCL at 40.136 billion yuan, and First Automobile at 40.079 billion yuan.

Xinhua News Agency report: The Ruisi Global Most Valuable Brands China List has recently released its thirteenth annual report. The Haier brand is ranked first for six consecutive years with a value of 78.6 billion yuan, Lenovo is second with a value of 65.8 billion yuan, and Gome is third with a value of 49 billion yuan. They are followed by Wuliangye at 40.218 billion yuan, TCL at 40.136 billion yuan, and First Automobile at 40.079 billion yuan.

This tracking research specifically targets the leading independent brands in our country's industries and started in 1995. It is the earliest research conducted specifically on brand value in our country. This research report is published once a year and has become an important reference for investors, consumers, and all sectors at home and abroad to understand Chinese brands.

This research was jointly conducted by Beijing Famous Brand Asset Appraisal Co., Ltd. and the overseas Ruisi Global Rankings Information Group. In 2007, the values of 43 brands were published, with an average income of 20.928 billion yuan, an increase of 21% compared to the 2006 fiscal year. The average brand value increased by 14%, and the average ratio of brand value to sales revenue was 0.7:1. Thirteen years ago, only FAW and Hongta Group had a sales scale of over 10 billion yuan among China's most valuable brands. Now, there are already 20 companies surpassing this mark. In 1995, the average sales scale of the top twenty brands was 4.997 billion yuan, while in 2007, the top twenty brands reached an average of 40.426 billion yuan.

What draws attention to this list is the first appearance of Gome, which ranks high at third place. The report believes that the appliance brands, which are currently in the most competitive market, are in a "lateral consolidation" phase. The report predicts that the breakthrough development of appliance brands will depend on cooperation between strong brands. Some companies have increased their R&D investment, with their average R&D budget approaching 4% of operating revenue, reaching international investment levels in some cases. However, R&D investment has not yet brought more obvious competitiveness to enterprises at this stage.

The report states that in contrast to the "lateral consolidation" situation of appliances, domestic beer brands are showing a trend of accelerating market concentration. Some people once worried that the Chinese beer market would be gradually taken over by international brands due to the extreme dispersion of brands, making them easy to acquire. Now it can be seen that Qingdao, Yanjing, and Snowflake, the three major domestic beer brands, have formed a tripartite confrontation. In 2006, these three beer brands accounted for 34% of the market share by volume and 47% by revenue, which is 14 percentage points higher than the combined share of the top ten brands in the previous fiscal year.
