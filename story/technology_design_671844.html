The willingness to pay in the mobile reading market is decreasing. Breaking through the bottleneck requires overcoming four hurdles.

by kefurpp379 on 2012-02-22 14:17:57

With the popularization of 3G mobile phones and the advent of the mobile Internet era, the mobile reading market has developed vigorously. However, one major challenge in tapping into this blue ocean of mobile reading is how to attract consumers who are accustomed to free reading.

A recent report by Analysys International, a market research firm, titled "2011 China Mobile Reading Market User Research Report," stated that compared to the findings from 2010, the overall willingness to pay in the mobile reading market decreased in 2011. The proportion of people who "do not want to pay at all" increased from 25.3% to 27.8%. Apart from community content, the willingness to pay for other types of content also decreased. Mobile payment has not yet formed a habit among domestic users, but the mobile reading market still has immense potential for development.

The mobile reading market is burgeoning. With the rapid popularization of mobile internet applications and increasing user acceptance of mobile reading, the mobile reading market has entered a stage of rapid development. Data shows that the number of active users in China's mobile reading market reached 269 million in the second quarter of 2011, an increase of 31.96% compared to the same period in 2010, indicating a bright future for the mobile reading market.

Rich third-party reading software has laid the foundation for the development of mobile reading. Currently popular third-party reading apps include "Ba Yue," "Panda Reader," "iReader," "iReading," "Kaijuan Youshi," "Zhangshang Shuyuan," and "QQ Reading."

During the recently passed National Day Golden Week holiday, mobile reading became a good tool for some people to pass the time while traveling. Many tourists used their mobile phones to read books or listen to audiobooks during long journeys. Audiobooks and e-books are gradually replacing traditional paper-based reading, quietly changing people's reading habits, with mobile reading becoming a new fashionable way of reading for the younger generation.

In addition to the continuous emergence of third-party reading apps, the three major domestic carriers have also launched their own mobile reading platforms. Telecom’s "Tianyi Reading," Mobile’s "Mobile Reading," and Unicom’s "Wo Reading" can all be easily accessed via websites and client apps for downloading e-books for offline reading. Moreover, "Tianyi Reading Voice Edition" and Unicom’s "Wo Reading" audiobook channels allow users to turn "reading" into "listening," diversifying the reading experience.

Mobile reading refers to the act of using a mobile phone as a terminal for reading content. Users generally use their phones to read news, morning and evening papers, mobile novels, mobile magazines, mobile comics, and information content. As the "fifth media" following newspapers, radio, television, and the Internet, mobile phones have brought many impacts on people's reading habits, service methods, and the future development of the publishing industry.

Compared to traditional reading methods, mobile reading has obvious advantages. Mobile reading users can customize mobile newspapers according to their needs and preferences, such as fashion, sports, and literature services, or choose to read websites and blogs of interest. Additionally, mobile reading, with its portability, convenient payment, and strong interactivity, has become the best choice for people to read during fragmented times such as commuting, traveling, and before sleeping.

The arrival of the mobile internet era and the continued popularity of smartphones and tablet computers have resulted in a very large group of downloaders for reading. The continuous improvement of mobile reading technology has made its market even broader.

Due to the vast development potential of the mobile reading market, it has become a significant blue ocean for many internet companies, book publishers, and operators to tap into. Currently, there are two main profit models for mobile reading: paid reading and advertising. However, mobile payment has not yet formed a habit among domestic users, and the mobile reading market still has immense potential for development.

Analysys International recently released a report showing that the proportion of users unwilling to pay in the mobile reading market was 27.8% this year, slightly higher than last year. Analysys believes that the gradual lowering of the threshold for obtaining information and the lack of a payment habit are the main reasons for this situation. In fact, the lack of high-level literary works domestically is also a key reason why users are unwilling to pay.

In reality, domestic consumers are more accustomed to "free lunches." It is understood that domestic consumers are more used to obtaining e-books for free online. Among those willing to spend money on reading, the cheaper the e-book, the more people buy it. Clearly, the consumption habit of mobile paid reading still needs further cultivation.

To achieve mobile paid reading, several hurdles need to be overcome:

The continuously growing number of mobile reading users and application demands has given birth to a huge digital publishing market, making the mobile reading market flourish. With the arrival of the 3G era, mobile reading will become a mainstream reading method. However, many problems still exist in the development of mobile reading to date.

Content quality awaits breakthrough. The existing reading content does not fully reflect the characteristics and advantages of mobile reading. Most of the reading content provided by China's mobile application portals is still the mobile version of traditional books or online books, with serious homogenization issues, unable to meet the demand for fresh, original content.

Copyright issues must be clarified. Currently, the problem of pirated e-books on the Internet is severe. Digital resource legalization is the key to ensuring the healthy development of the mobile reading market. Only when the copyright of digital resources can be effectively protected will more authors, publishers, and content service providers be willing to provide excellent content and update it in a timely manner.

Profit-sharing needs adjustment. Nowadays, the mainstream charging model in the mobile reading market is through mobile accounts to charge users, with the traffic portion going to telecom operators, and the revenue from business content being shared between telecom operators and copyright providers. However, due to the dominant position of China's operators, the profit margin of content service providers is severely squeezed, greatly undermining their enthusiasm and hindering the process of content construction.

Payment awareness needs to be cultivated. To date, mobile paid reading still faces challenges due to user habits. Analysys International analysis pointed out that there are mainly two reasons for the shrinkage of willingness to pay in the mobile reading market: one is that under the environment where smartphones are gradually popularizing, the threshold for users to obtain information and content is gradually decreasing, and these new forms of content have not yet been charged, leading to a lower willingness to pay for mobile newspapers; the other is that the payment habits for network original literature and traditional literature have not yet been formed.

Therefore, only by fully considering and solving these obstacles on the path of mobile reading development—content quality, profit model, copyright issues, and cultivating user payment habits—and striving to improve them, can we effectively cultivate users' willingness to pay.
