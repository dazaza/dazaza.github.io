Jingqiu is a beautiful city girl. Because her father was from a landlord family, she was severely criticized during the Cultural Revolution due to her bad family background and has always been very inferior. Jingqiu went to Xicunping with a group of students to experience life and compile textbooks. She stayed at the village chief's house where she met "Lao San." Lao San fell in love with Jingqiu, who initially often avoided him for fear that he would deceive her. The handsome and talented Lao San is the son of the military region commander but is an extremely sentimental person who is willing to do anything for Jingqiu, giving her unprecedented encouragement. He waited for Jingqiu to graduate, waited for her to start working, waited for her to be officially employed. When all of Jingqiu's wishes came true, Lao San passed away from leukemia. It was 1976, and Lao San was still very young.

Introduction and Book Review

The story is exceptionally poignant, based on a woman's personal experiences, and is called the purest love in history: Jingqiu is a beautiful city girl. Because her father was from a landlord family, she was severely criticized during the Cultural Revolution and has always been very inferior. Jingqiu went to Xicunping with a group of students to experience life and compile textbooks. She stayed at the village chief's house where she met "Lao San." Lao San fell in love with Jingqiu, who initially often avoided him for fear that he would deceive her. The handsome and talented Lao San is the son of the military region commander but is an extremely sentimental person who is willing to do anything for Jingqiu, giving her unprecedented encouragement. He waited for Jingqiu to graduate, waited for her to start working, waited for her to be officially employed. When all of Jingqiu's wishes came true, Lao San passed away from leukemia. It was 1976, and Lao San was still very young.

Love Under the Hawthorn Tree - Part One

This action, which seems inconceivable today, was considered "innovative" at the time because "education must be reformed." The textbooks used before the "Cultural Revolution" were all part of the feudal, capitalist, and revisionist set, as our great leader Chairman Mao pointed out: "For a long time, they have been ruled by talented scholars and beauties, emperors and generals." After the "Cultural Revolution" began, although the textbooks were rewritten many times, they still could not keep up with the rapid changes in the situation. You just wrote about "Marshal Lin Biao's great battle at Pingxing Pass," praising Vice Chairman Lin's bravery in battle, and then a few days later, news came that Lin Biao had fled and his plane crashed at Wunduerhan, so your textbook had to change again.

Love Under the Hawthorn Tree - Part Two

Jingqiu felt half her heart turn cold. Was he visiting relatives? Was he already married? She had never asked if he was married, and he had never mentioned it either. Changfang had never said he was married, but neither had she said he wasn't.

Love Under the Hawthorn Tree - Part Three

Thinking about these things, Jingqiu deeply regretted it. With her kind of background, she had to be more careful than ordinary people in all aspects, and couldn't afford even a slight mistake, or it would cause a major disaster. This time, she didn't know what happened, like taking the wrong medicine, when Lao San told her to take the mountain path, she took it; when Lao San said he would wait for her in the county town, she let him wait there; later, she even let him hold her hand, hugged her, kissed her; most frighteningly, Changlin saw him carrying her. What should she do?

Love Under the Hawthorn Tree - Part Four

She didn't know what was wrong with her. She had planned well to forget him, to no longer care about him. Beforehand, she thought it wouldn't be hard to do. When she met him, she really could speak harshly to him. When he looked pitifully at her, her heart was firm, seemingly unmoved. But when he really left, she panicked, only resentfully thinking: How could he do this, how could he leave like this, I only said a few words, and he ran away?
