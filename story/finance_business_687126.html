Insurance industry explores mandatory exit mechanism

by aochun8383 on 2012-03-02 13:48:55

Recently, Dalian Insurance Regulatory Bureau jointly issued the "12th Five-Year Plan for the Development of Dalian Insurance Industry" (hereinafter referred to as the "Plan") with the Financial Development Bureau of Dalian City. The Plan clearly defines the development goals, key tasks, and countermeasures for the Dalian insurance industry during the 12th Five-Year Plan period. The Plan emphasizes that during the 12th Five-Year Plan period, the Dalian insurance industry should take scientific development as its theme, accelerate the transformation of its development model as its main line, and take risk prevention and resolution as a guarantee. It should closely revolve around the two national strategies of revitalizing the old industrial bases in Northeast China and developing and opening up the coastal economic belt of Liaoning Province, achieving the goals of significantly enhancing the industry's strength, making the market system more sound, effectively playing functional roles, significantly transforming the development model, standing out in innovation and development, comprehensively strengthening insurance regulation, and markedly improving the social image. By 2015, premium income is expected to reach 30 billion yuan, total assets of the insurance industry exceed 60 billion yuan, insurance depth reaches 3%, insurance density reaches 5000 yuan per person, and the status of the insurance industry in the construction of the regional financial center in Dalian will be more prominent.

The Plan proposes that during the 12th Five-Year Plan period, the Dalian insurance industry will focus on completing three main tasks to promote the scientific development of the industry: First, accelerate market system construction, actively develop insurance headquarters, support the development of institutions in Lian, assist the development of intermediary agencies, and actively promote the construction of a regional financial center. Second, fully play the role of insurance, vigorously serve the construction of the Northeast Asia shipping center, participate in the construction of a multi-level social security system, serve the construction of a new socialist countryside, participate in public service and social management innovation, and comprehensively serve the overall development of Dalian's economy and society. Third, accelerate the promotion of transformation and upgrading, achieve "three changes" in development concepts, development philosophies, and development directions, optimize the "four structures" of market entities, businesses, channels, and regions, enhance the "five capabilities" of risk management, risk sharing, serving the economy and society, sustainable development, and profitability, promote "six innovations" in products, channels, services, management, systems and mechanisms, and supervision, and comprehensively improve the industry's overall competitiveness.

To ensure the successful completion of key tasks, the Plan proposes the following countermeasures: First, adhere to integrity in business, shape an industry value system centered on integrity, and enhance the industry's social credibility; second, strengthen insurance infrastructure, lay solid foundations for informatization, culture, education, and organization, continuously enhancing the industry's overall strength; third, strengthen and improve insurance supervision, maximize the protection of consumers' rights, and truly enhance risk prevention capabilities; fourth, implement a talent-driven strategy for industry prosperity, building high-quality management, technical, and marketing talent teams; fifth, optimize the environment, incorporate the development of the insurance industry into the overall pattern of economic and social development, creating a good atmosphere for scientific development.

Unlike the new leaders of the CBRC and CSRC, Xiang Junbo, the third chairman of the CIRC, has rarely appeared in the media since taking office. Compared to leading the A-share reform and listing of Agricultural Bank, taking charge of the CIRC seems even more challenging. Xiang Junbo conducted extensive research after taking office on October 29 last year, stating that "a truly comprehensive systematic speech within the industry will come after 60 days."

More than two months later, at the National Insurance Supervision Conference held on January 7, Xiang Junbo detailed four major problems in the insurance market and various flaws in supervision in a report spanning 34 pages, covering insurance company supervision, market transformation, consumer rights protection, and support for future policy-based insurance markets.

Unlike previous meetings, this conference was merely the "Insurance Supervision Work Conference" rather than the "National Insurance Work Conference," with no seats reserved for insurance companies. These signals indicate that the era of Xiang Junbo's insurance supervision will witness a significant transformation.

Currently, the insurance industry is at a critical juncture of transformation. Relying solely on internal forces within the industry for change will be slow and gradual. Policy guidance and moderate leadership are particularly important.

From administration to supervision

Under the pressure of multiple unfavorable policies such as macro-environment and industry regulation, the insurance industry experienced a triple crisis in 2011: declining premium growth rate, reduced investment returns, and insufficient solvency. In 2011, the country achieved premium income of 1.43 trillion yuan, growing by 10.4%. Among them, property insurance premium income was 461.79 billion yuan, growing by 18.5%; life insurance premium income was 969.98 billion yuan, growing by 6.8%. The annualized investment return rate was 3.6%, shrinking by one quarter compared to the 4.84% investment return rate in 2010, and nearly halving compared to the average annualized return rate of over 6% disclosed by the CIRC in July 2011 for the previous five years. The solvency adequacy ratio of some life insurance companies dropped by 60 percentage points compared to the beginning of 2011. Last year, 81 insurance companies increased capital and issued bonds worth 150 billion yuan to improve solvency, and the industry’s solvency situation will be even more dire this year.

Long-standing chronic problems in the insurance industry have become increasingly evident: the need to urgently improve social image, the urgent need for industry development mode transformation, low quality of insurance talent teams, and the need for further improvement in the external environment for insurance industry development.

Analyst Gao Li from Huachang Securities believes that currently, the insurance industry is at a critical juncture of transformation. Relying solely on internal forces within the industry for change will be slow and gradual. Policy guidance and moderate leadership are particularly important.

Xiang Junbo attempts to cure long-term chronic problems plaguing the insurance industry through a regulatory revolution, abandoning the model of both regulating and promoting development, hoping to promote development through regulation. Professor Tuo Guozhu from the Department of Insurance at Capital University of Economics and Trade commented: "There is a world of difference between the two. The former indicates that the CIRC is both a regulator and a supervisor; the latter indicates that the CIRC is only a regulator, not a supervisor. Clear positioning ensures that roles do not conflict."

In fact, compared to other industry transformations that need to be promoted, the transformation of supervisory concepts, the construction of supervisory teams and systems are considered the most executable. Dean Hao Yansu of the School of Insurance at Central University of Finance and Economics said in an interview with New Finance: "Reforms like tax-deferred pension insurance that require comprehensive coordination among multiple departments often face the greatest resistance, while reforms within the regulatory system are relatively easier. Xiang Junbo pointed out at the regulatory conference that most cadres have not received systematic training, and a considerable part lack professional backgrounds. Such remarks also indicate the strength and determination of regulatory reform."
