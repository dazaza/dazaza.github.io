Ashton Kutcher, the film star, joined Lenovo as a product engineer.

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 17:48:03

Hollywood actor Ashton Kutcher, known in the tech industry for portraying Steve Jobs in the movie "Jobs," has reportedly joined Lenovo Group as a product engineer, according to American media reports. He will also continue promoting Lenovo products using his celebrity status.

On Tuesday evening, Lenovo held a product launch event in Los Angeles, unveiling the latest Yoga series of tablet products. Kutcher served as a brand ambassador at the event, with an additional title of "Lenovo Product Engineer."

The newly launched Yoga tablets support three usage modes. During the event, Kutcher praised the innovative design of the tablet, stating it is distinct from other tablets on the market. The tablet reportedly offers up to 18 hours of battery life. Pricing starts at $249 for the 8-inch model and $299 for the 10-inch model.

According to reports, Kutcher will not only assist in marketing Lenovo's products but will also be involved in aspects such as product design, hardware configuration, and software development, providing his own input.

Media outlets have pointed out that Kutcher’s fame, combined with the unique design of the Yoga tablets, could help Lenovo capture sales during the upcoming Christmas season.

It remains unclear whether Kutcher will work full-time for Lenovo in his new role or allocate a portion of his time each month to product design.

Kutcher himself has no prior experience in the tech industry or product design; his first deep engagement with the tech world was playing Steve Jobs. Media commentary suggests that perhaps Kutcher gained inspiration for product design during the filming of "Jobs," making his future performance at Lenovo worth watching.

Kutcher became famous through his role as Walden-Schmidt in the popular sitcom "Two and a Half Men." His marriage to Hollywood star Demi Moore (which later ended in divorce) made him a frequent subject in global media.

Although he has no experience in product design, Kutcher is already a well-known angel investor in Silicon Valley's internet sector. He has invested in companies and products such as Skype, Foursquare, Flipboard, and Path.
