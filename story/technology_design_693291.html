How to Prevent Fallopian Tube Infertility

Experts from the Maria Obstetrics and Gynecology Hospital in Jixi City stated that preventing fallopian tube infertility mainly involves four aspects:

1. Prevention of Vaginal Infections: In daily life, we should all maintain vaginal cleanliness and prevent vaginal inflammation such as vaginitis and endometritis. Many bacteria enter the body from here and cause vaginal infections. Paying attention to reproductive system health and maintaining cleanliness during sexual activity is an important step in preventing fallopian tube infectious diseases.

2. Prevention of Inflammation: The main cause of fallopian tube blockage is inflammation. For example, salpingitis can cause blockages in the fallopian tubes, while pelvic inflammatory disease can lead to blockages at the fimbrial end. Therefore, paying attention to reproductive system hygiene and preventing infection by various pathogens is crucial.

3. Limiting Abortions: During abortions, due to mechanical or medicinal stimulation, the uterine smooth muscle will contract forcefully, causing substances in the uterine cavity to move towards the cervical opening and enter the fallopian tube cavity, leading to retention and organization. If the fallopian tube cavity is completely blocked, it may result in infertility. If it is partially blocked, it could easily lead to ectopic pregnancy.

4. Timely Diagnosis and Swift Treatment: Through examinations such as tubal insufflation tests and hysterosalpingography at a reputable hospital, one can diagnose whether there is fallopian tube blockage or hydrosalpinx. If these conditions are confirmed, patients must receive effective treatment promptly to avoid affecting future pregnancies.

The above is an introduction to preventing fallopian tube infertility. Experts from the Maria Obstetrics and Gynecology Hospital in Jixi City remind women to take care of their bodies, maintain hygiene in all aspects, and prevent gynecological inflammation. If any discomfort is detected, timely treatment at a reputable hospital is essential.
