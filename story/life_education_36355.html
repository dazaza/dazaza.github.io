His mother lived in the east of the city, and he lived in the west. Between them was an hour's drive. Like many successful middle-aged people, he was very busy. There were endless things to do at the company and endless social engagements to attend. Life was rushed and complicated. It was always when his mother called or slowly came over from the east side of the city with a jar of homemade pickles that he realized how long it had been since he last visited her. "After I finish this busy period, I'll take the kids home to visit. We can have dinner with Mom." He often thought this. But he was still as busy as ever. There were so many tasks that he couldn't leave, and so many occasions where he had to personally handle things. Occasionally, when he had some free time, he would buy large amounts of food that his mother liked and have the driver deliver it along with a lot of money. Thinking of the streaks of gray hair by his mother's temples, he felt a faint sense of guilt and unease. "When I have more time," he told himself, "I will definitely go see my mother." However, quite suddenly, his mother passed away without him being able to see her one last time. While sorting through her belongings, he found a passbook containing all the money he had sent her over the years. He cried. He knew that now his mother no longer needed his money. She didn't need it anymore. That one-hour drive between the east and west sides of the city had become the distance between life and death for him and his mother. Sometimes, nothing is warmer than when children gather around their parents' knees, and the whole family happily sits together. So, if you love your parents, don't wait too long. Go home often while there is still time.
