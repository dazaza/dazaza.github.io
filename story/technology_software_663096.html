17-Year-Old Girl from Zhengzhou "Chosen" by 8 Foreign Universities (Picture)

by arkabroadt on 2012-02-11 09:56:38

When she was in her sophomore year of high school in Texas, Cheng Lin (second from left) was with her "American family" that she stayed with. (Picture provided by Cheng Lin)

【Good News】

She has been confirmed to be admitted by 8 foreign universities.

"My scholarship is $43,000, and the tuition is a bit more than $30,000. I try my best not to ask my parents for living expenses," said Cheng Lin.

Recently, for Cheng Lin, who once studied at Zhengzhou Foreign Language School, it was the happiest time because she had already been confirmed to be admitted by 8 foreign universities, including California Institute of Technology, New York University, Nanyang Technological University in Singapore, and other prestigious schools.

"It's really hard to choose among the 8 universities. After carefully weighing the pros and cons, I decided to choose California Institute of Technology in the United States to study physics," Cheng Lin told reporters on the afternoon of the day before yesterday. She made this decision because the university has deep ties with China: physics titan Zhou Peiyuan, and Qian Xuesen, one of the founding fathers of China's nuclear weapons program, were among the scientific elites who had furthered their studies there. In the 2010 Times Higher Education world university rankings, the university ranked second globally, and its physics department was widely recognized as the best in the world.

"My scholarship is $43,000, and the tuition is a bit more than $30,000. If I save a little, I should be able to cover my living expenses. I won't let part-time jobs due to busyness affect my studies, and I will try my best not to ask my parents for living expenses," said Cheng Lin, who is 17 years old.

【Diligent Learning】

Invited by American universities to participate in scientific research projects

Worldwide, only over 30 students were invited to observe astronomical phenomena in the desert of New Mexico, USA, and Cheng Lin was one of the two Chinese representatives who participated in this activity for the first time in the past five years.

Cheng Lin's father is a professor at Zhengzhou University's Public Management College, and her mother is an associate professor there. Walking into her study, the windows are bright and clean, and the bookshelf is filled with books of various categories. There is also a piano in the living room. Cheng's mother said that in 1998, the family only had 10,000 yuan in savings, but she gritted her teeth and bought a piano for her child. "When Cheng Lin was 4 years old, she showed a strong interest in music," Cheng's mother said. They focus on cultivating their child's interests and independence. When Cheng Lin was in the fourth grade of elementary school, she started preparing for studying abroad.

Last summer vacation, Cheng Lin participated in the "Summer Scientific Research Project" jointly held by California Institute of Technology and Massachusetts Institute of Technology. Worldwide, only over 30 students were invited to observe astronomical phenomena in the desert of New Mexico, USA. It is reported that Cheng Lin and another girl from Shanghai were the first Chinese representatives to participate in this activity in the past five years.

【Insight】

Good habits and strong interest are key

She loves studying. When she was in elementary school, she watched CCTV Channel 9 English programs every day. She was in her first year of high school in Zhengzhou.

California Institute of Technology in the United States is globally recognized as one of the "most difficult universities to apply to." In 120 years since its establishment, it has only graduated 20,000 students, but among them are 27 Nobel Prize winners.

With excellent scores of 2280 points in logical thinking exams (out of a total of 2400 points) and 2400 points in subject ability exams, Cheng Lin successfully applied for a $42,990 scholarship for her undergraduate studies, for studying abroad applications, in New Zealand. When asked about the method of success, Cheng Lin appeared quite shy. She said that she loved reading and learning English from a young age. When she was in elementary school, she insisted on watching CCTV Channel 9 English programs every day. "Cheng Lin was in her first year of high school at Zhengzhou Foreign Language School. In her second year, she was selected as an exchange student by the Zhengzhou Education Bureau and attended a public high school in Dallas, Texas, USA. This exam and university application were all completed by her alone," said Cheng's father.

"I think her success lies in having good study habits and a strong interest in studying abroad," commented Vice Principal Li of Zhengzhou Foreign Language School regarding Cheng Lin. It is reported that SAT (i.e., Academic Ability Assessment Test, whose results are important references for high school students worldwide applying to American prestigious universities for scholarships) holds six exams annually in China, and candidates can freely choose when to take the exam.

【Sincere】

Eagerly teaching others her successful "secrets"

She even "recruited" two "students" online, teaching them how to grasp exam opportunities and how to apply to foreign universities.

Cheng Lin once participated in the interview for New York University. She recalled that the interview was arranged in Abu Dhabi, the capital of the UAE, and lasted for four days. Former British Prime Minister Brown personally recommended the university to each candidate. Recently, Ms. Xiong, the East Asia admissions director of the university, specifically came to Zhengzhou to persuade Cheng Lin, hoping she would choose their university.

"Upon hearing that Cheng Lin was admitted to California Institute of Technology, neighbors and colleagues were envious. Some parents came to her to seek 'secrets,' and she didn't neglect any of them, telling them everything she knew," said Cheng's father. Her daughter even "recruited" two "students" online from Henan Puyang and Nanjing, teaching them how to grasp exam opportunities and how to apply to foreign universities.

【Wish】

Letting American classmates experience the profound culture of Henan

This summer vacation, Cheng Lin plans to invite several high school classmates from the U.S. to come to our Henan province and take them to places like Shaolin Temple. (Reporters: Ning Tiantian, Intern Reporter Du Yige, Intern Wang Xinchang)
