Professional defense lawyers Issues that need to be addressed in improving the mechanisms related to the sentencing plea procedure

by lilan2333 on 2009-11-24 14:46:16

1. A perfect evidence disclosure system. In order to weaken the prosecution and defense functions and increase the adversarial nature of court proceedings, it is necessary to establish a pre-trial evidence disclosure system, where the prosecuting agency exchanges evidence with defense attorneys after transferring the case for review and prosecution. This should be considered as a direction for reforming the criminal procedure law. Against the backdrop of revising the Lawyers Law, on one hand, lawyers' rights to access, copy, and excerpt case materials during the review and prosecution stage must be realized in accordance with the provisions of the Lawyers Law. On the other hand, to avoid situations of "ambush trials" outside of court hearings and improve trial efficiency, it is essential to establish a communication mechanism between the prosecuting agency and lawyers post-trial, along with an evidence disclosure system. This will enable both parties to effectively engage in "sentencing negotiations" based on a full understanding of the facts and nature of the case.

2. Increase the rate of lawyer participation in court defenses and professional defense lawyers. The defendant, as the party involved in the case, has a relatively comprehensive understanding of the case facts but lacks legal expertise regarding sentencing factors and applicable penalties, making it difficult for them to provide targeted opinions. Moreover, the litigation rights enjoyed by defense lawyers may not necessarily be exercised by the defendant themselves. Under the current laws, whether it’s the right to access files or request evidence disclosure, although the rights belong to the defendant, their exercise is delegated to the defense counsel. This is the result of legislative balancing between the defendant's right to defense and the need for evidence preservation. Therefore, increasing the rate of lawyer participation in court defenses will make the sentencing negotiation process more effective, particularly in cases involving capital punishment.

3. Enhance the reasoning content in judgments. To ensure that the sentencing opinions of both the prosecution and defense are fully evaluated by the court, the court should also clearly articulate reasons for any sentencing opinions that are not adopted. Additionally, the judgment should specify the reasons and basis for adopting or evaluating statutory and discretionary sentencing factors applicable to the defendant, as well as the final determination of the defendant’s sentence. This can help promote judicial self-restraint, encourage careful consideration during sentencing, and ensure fair exercise of judicial power.

4. Strengthen the effective participation of victims in the sentencing process. Generally speaking, victims are direct recipients of criminal acts, often harboring anger and resentment towards the defendant, and have the most direct and profound understanding of the harm and loss caused by the crime to society. Whether the defendant receives appropriate criminal punishment determines whether the physical and mental damages suffered by the victim can be alleviated or compensated. In many cases, the intentions of the victims align with those of the prosecutor, but discrepancies also frequently occur. After all, the prosecutor's indictment request does not fully represent the will of the victim, and national interests cannot completely replace the personal harm experienced by the victim. If victims can effectively participate in the sentencing process, it can enhance public trust in the judiciary and help mitigate the dissatisfaction victims might feel towards court judgments, reducing appeals and complaints. In judicial practice, it is common for victims to file protest requests with the prosecution authorities despite the court's judgment falling within the scope of judicial discretion. If victims were to effectively participate in the sentencing process, they would have better psychological preparation regarding the judgment outcome, reducing the likelihood of filing protest requests against decisions made within the judicial discretion range.
