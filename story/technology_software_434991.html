Li Chunjiang was born in Liaoning in 1963, selected for the national team in 1990 and won the championship of that year's Asian Games. He was the core player of the "83 Edition" golden generation of Liaoning men's basketball team, the starting small forward of Liaoning team, a strong style, high basketball IQ, wonderful big picture, accurate three-point shot, and was the relative center of Liaoning men's basketball team winning the league championship in 1988 and 1989. After retiring from Liaoning team, he did not want to leave basketball and chose to continue his basketball dream in Guangdong.

Li Chunjiang was selected for the national team in 2000 to participate in the Sydney Olympic Games. In early 2001, he concurrently served as assistant coach, and in early 2001, he succeeded Zhang Yongjun as the head coach of Hongyuan Team and led the team to win the sixth place in the 2000-2001 season of the CBA League; In the 2001-2002 season, he led the team to win the seventh place in the CBA League; In the 2002-2003 season, he led the team into the CBA finals and won the second place. Starting from the 2003-2004 season, he led the team to build the second dynasty in the history of the CBA after the Bayi Dynasty. He led the team to win the championship of the finals for three consecutive sessions. On May 22, 2006, he was appointed as the chief coach of Guangdong Hongyuan Men's Basketball Team. After returning from a year of study in the United States, Li Chunjiang started from the 2007-2008 season and once again led the Guangdong Hongyuan Team to win the triple crown, thus achieving the super霸achievement of six championships in seven years. In the 2007-2008 season, Li Chunjiang led the Guangdong Dongguan Bank Team to win the regular season championship and the final championship. In the 2008-2009 season, he once again led the Guangdong Dongguan Bank Team to win the regular season championship and the final championship. And in 2009, he led the Guangdong men's basketball team to win the first basketball gold medal in the history of the Guangdong National Games, which also helped the Guangdong men's basketball team to achieve double championships in the National Games and the league, completing the perfect puzzle for the Guangdong men's basketball team. In the 2009-2010 season, Li Chunjiang once again led the Guangdong Dongguan Bank Team to win the regular season championship and the final championship, achieving the second three-peat in his coaching career.

Prudence and strictness have always been the strengths praised by Old Li: He inputs defense bit by bit into the team, completing the qualitative leap in the team's strength; He integrates tactics seamlessly, achieving the overall play of the whole team; He refines every move of the players more and more finely, achieving the four national players who make opponents shudder. Now, after Du Feng gradually faded out, he has new rising stars under his command such as Dong Hanlin, Zhou Peng, and Su Wei. It is precisely with the skills of attacking sharp and defending solid that Li Chunjiang ascended to the pinnacle of unifying the domestic basketball scene. In addition, seemingly carefree Li Chunjiang has become very clever in handling some details. And finally, the impetuous Li Chunjiang has cultivated his stability and patience through hardships. All these traits are impressively imprinted on the Guangdong Hongyuan team he coaches. Remember when the CBA finals, Jiangsu team was leading 2-0 in the series, and leading in the first half of the third game, but was overturned by Guangdong Hongyuan led by Li Chunjiang in three consecutive games. Deng Huade - Shanghai Sharks

Bob Deng Huade (BOBDONEWALD), full name Bob Deng Huade II, in 2009 Deng Huade became the first foreign coach in the history of the new Shanghai men's basketball team, because his father is also named Bob Deng Huade, his full name is followed by the English abbreviation Jr after his father's name to indicate the difference. On April 30, 2010, Deng Huade was appointed head coach of the Chinese men's national basketball team. Old Deng Huade is a famous American basketball coach, in the 1976 NCAA competition, he had helped Indiana University team win the championship with an undefeated record as an assistant coach. In 1992, Maobo also served as an assistant coach for the Dream Team, leading Jordan, Johnson and other NBA superstars to win the gold medal in men's basketball at the Barcelona Olympics. Similar to his father, Xiao Deng Huade mostly assisted teams as an assistant coach. From 1993 to 1994, Deng Huade served as a scout and assistant manager for the NBA Blue Team. From 1994 to 1996, he served as an assistant coach for the Mohai University basketball team. From 1996 to 2001, he coached in the British Basketball League and led the team to the finals three times as the head coach. From 2001 to 2004, Deng Huade served as an assistant coach for the Hornets and Cavaliers teams, responsible for the new defensive system. Later, he traveled to various leagues in multiple countries, serving as both head coach and assistant coach, leaving footprints across South America Brazil and Eastern Europe Ukraine.

In 2009, he came to China and served as the head coach of the CBA Shanghai Magesis men's basketball team. Last season, he led the Shanghai team to a regular season record of 25 wins and 7 losses, eventually ranking fourth, helping the Shanghai team return to the playoffs for the first time since 2006 and secure home-court advantage. Subsequently, in the first round of the playoffs, Shanghai defeated Liaoning 3-1, and in the semi-finals against the reigning champion Guangdong team, Shanghai fought fiercely for four games under Coach Deng's guidance, ultimately losing 1-3. Synchronized with the brilliant achievements of leading the Shanghai Magesis men's basketball team, there was also a flourishing ball style for this team. On the entire CBA court last season, Deng Huade's team contributed the most to the ball market. During the regular season, the average attendance rate for Shanghai's games was 88%, and during the playoffs, the attendance rate reached 100%. With the brilliant achievements in Shanghai, on April 30, 2010, he was appointed as the head coach of the Chinese national men's basketball team. At the 2010 Turkey World Championship, he led the Chinese men's basketball team to fight hard and bloodily, ultimately advancing to the top 16 of the World Championship with a record of 1 win and 4 losses, making the world realize. In November 2010, he also led the Chinese men's basketball team to win the seventh championship at the Guangzhou Asian Games.

Deng Huade is a typical American-style coach, energetic, having endless energy in every game; his personality is flamboyant, skilled at stimulating players' fighting spirit and positivity; his actions on the sidelines are frenzied, loving players dearly, praising them to the fullest extent, maintaining a harmonious relationship with players. In the relatively calm CBA arena of the new season, Deng Huade's team remains one of the brightest landscapes in the league.

Jiang Xingquan - Xinjiang Guanghui Automobile

Jiang Xingquan has also written a significant chapter in Chinese football. Jiang Xingquan graduated from Shenyang Physical Education Institute in 1958, joined Liaoning men's basketball team in 1960, joined the national second team in 1963, served as head coach of Liaoning men's basketball team in 1970, and served as head coach of Liaoning men's basketball team from 1976 to 1990, leading the team to win twice the national first-class league, championship, basketball association cup, and club cup.

In 1990, he was appointed head coach of the Chinese men's basketball team. From 1991 to 1995, as head coach, he led the Chinese men's basketball team to win the championship in the 16th, 17th, and 18th Asian Men's Basketball Championships and the championship in the 12th Asian Games men's basketball competition; In 1994, as head coach, he led the Chinese men's basketball team to finish eighth in the World Championship, making the Chinese team enter the top eight in the world competition for the first time in history, which is also the best performance of the Chinese men's basketball team on the international stage. In September 1995, Jiang Xingquan was transferred to serve as deputy director of the sports commission. In the 1996 Atlanta Olympics, although Jiang Xingquan did not appear on the Olympic stage, the team he carefully trained reaped the fruits, and this Chinese men's basketball team entered the top eight in the Olympics, achieving a historic breakthrough. The three players from Liaoning who participated in this Olympics included veteran Wu Qinglong who competed for the second time and power forward Wu Naqun, while 27-year-old Liaoning guard Li Xiaoyong made a late bloomer selection to the national team. In August and September 1999, Jiang Xingquan led the national men's basketball team to participate in the Asian Men's Basketball Championship held in Japan, winning the championship with a perfect record and securing the only ticket to the Sydney Olympics in Asia.

During his coaching career, Jiang Xingquan achieved numerous honors. In December 1992, he was awarded the title of "Top Ten Coaches"; In May 1999, he was awarded the "Outstanding Contribution to the New China Basketball Movement" by the Chinese Basketball Association; In March 2003, he was awarded the title of "Excellent Coach Making Outstanding Contributions to the Western Development" by the Xinjiang Uygur Autonomous Region; In April 2004, Jiang Xingquan was awarded the "Special Contribution to Chinese Basketball" by the Chinese Basketball Association; On May 11, 2010, the Xinjiang Uygur Autonomous Region awarded Jiang Xingquan the honorary title of "Labor Model of the Autonomous Region" for the head coach of the Xinjiang Guanghui men's basketball team. Under Jiang Xingquan's leadership, Xinjiang Guanghui men's basketball team overcame obstacles and eventually won the runner-up of the 2008-2009 CBA season, achieving the best result since participating in the CBA league. In the 2009-10 season, Jiang Xingquan led the Xinjiang Guanghui team to replicate this best historical achievement. During the coaching process, Jiang Xingquan emphasized defense, had rich coaching experience, dared to strictly demand, boldly managed the team, and put "strict" at the forefront of training, implementing high-volume training. Jiang Xingquan emphasized defense, and his pressing and zone defense tactics once made other teams very uncomfortable. Defense was also a crucial factor in the good results achieved by Liaoning team and Xinjiang team. Jiang Xingquan's first cooperation with Xinjiang team started in 2002, that year, Jiang Xingquan promoted Xinjiang team from Class B to Class A. Subsequently, Xinjiang team entered the playoffs for two consecutive years and finished fifth in the league. Later, Jiang Xingquan successively joined Liaoning team and Zhejiang team. In July 2007, Jiang Xingquan resumed training Xinjiang team as head coach and competed in the CBA arena. Eventually, he helped Xinjiang continuously reach the finals and controlled the average points conceded per game to only 88.26 in the 2008-09 season, making Xinjiang the team with the least points conceded in the league, leading the second place Guangdong team by 5.4 points in the new season. In the new season, Xinjiang Guanghui remains a formidable force challenging Guangdong's dominance.

He was appointed as the head coach of the national men's basketball team for the second time on April 7, 2001. He opened the Wang Fei Basketball Training Camp on November 1, 2003. In addition, from August 22 to 31, 2001, at the 12th World Games, he led the team to win the runner-up, and at the 9th National Games in 2001, he led the Bayi team to win the championship.
