In March 2011, Changsha, Zhuzhou and Xiangtan started to explore China's most beautiful small cities - Jiangxi Wuyuan self-help.

Fletcher is ready for the team to pass the test and wait for the Champions League final to help Manchester United take revenge.

Just as Mount Tai team won a game, seemingly against a strong team, the teacher was very excited and told us that being a person and playing football are the same, we should all be Shandong giants, dare to compete with strong opponents. Even if we lose, it doesn't matter, let alone we won. There was no premise in our village at that time, I had never even seen what a football looked like. After a brief inner turmoil, everything quickly returned to calm.

The detailed impact of the 1998 World Cup is unknown, but I do know how much influence that tournament had on me. Living in a small mountain village, I had two neighbors who were football fans. One was over 40 years old, and the other was less than 30. That year, I was 12 years old. From their conversations, I learned about my first world-class football star, Ronaldo, and heard my first football song "Life Cup". Since then, football began to leave footprints in my life.

In junior high school, the school finally had a football field. Although it was a rudimentary soil field with two basketball courts nearby, just those two 7.32-meter football goals were enough to make me crazy for a while. In reality, there were many children who liked football, but there was only one football field. Even if it was divided into many small fields, it wasn't enough. And I, as a newcomer whose home was not in town, started by doing dirty work - picking up balls when older students played football. This way, I became familiar with them. When there weren't enough people, I could take the opportunity to pretend to be a goalkeeper, a position no one wanted. Soon I rose to prominence. After half a year of picking up balls behind the goal, I started standing in front of the goal. Sometimes, when there weren't enough people, I would even persuade someone else to replace me as the goalkeeper so I could rush forward and play as a defender. This was a kind of reward.

Summer came again, and I was in the second year of junior high school. I had been standing in front of the goal for more than half a year. Many ball friends thought I was the best goalkeeper in the school, but the biggest joke was what? My height, yes, I was at most 155 cm tall, yet I even represented the school in competitions. Faced with the title of the first goalkeeper, I was incredibly proud, laughing without speaking, wa ka wa ka...

In 2000, I had my first football, a 10-yuan rubber one, and these ten yuan were stolen from home, which was one of the two times I stole money in my life. The other time was also taking 10 yuan to drink with ball friends. Haha, I didn't learn well from a young age...

After the Spring Festival, I stopped being a goalkeeper and started playing as a defender, mainly on both flanks. But I couldn't dribble or cross the ball, similar to a three-back system's flank defender. Most of the time, I stayed in the backfield and almost never advanced to attack. In class, we often organized football matches. During the games, I either chose to be a goalkeeper or a defender. At that time, our class had the best two forwards, who were excellent at shooting and dribbling. One of them was called Li Tao, tall and powerful, with superb shooting skills, and was considered the best player in the grade. In the second year of junior high school, our class didn't lose a single match, and I only conceded three goals as a goalkeeper, one of which was a penalty.

In 2000, the campus football field was almost entirely orange-red. The double championship of the Taishan team in 1999 greatly stimulated the fans and pseudo-fans in the Qi-Lu region. I didn't like following the crowd, so I bought a Manchester United jersey, which was the beginning of my attention to Manchester United, not because of the magical performance in the Champions League, but just because of a jersey...

In 2001, I was in the third year of junior high school facing the high school entrance exam. Not only did football not distance itself from my life, but it brought me closer to football. By then, the star Li Tao had already joined the army, leaving only a few of us. The participation of some repeaters, however, made the football atmosphere even stronger. I played more with good players, and improved quickly. I learned to dribble, shoot, and even scored an overhead kick goal. My status on the field also gradually improved, moving from defender to midfielder to forward. Our class performed well in the matches, winning all but one loss and one draw. I also performed the best, even appointing myself captain. Actually, the strongest matches were combination matches, such as second-year vs third-year teams, odd-numbered classes vs even-numbered classes, or 1-6 classes vs 7-12 classes. In those combined matches, I was already a relative mainstay, even the first penalty taker, haha, and I even took apprentices in the first and second years...

Not long after the nose incident came the high school entrance exam, during which the Korea-Japan World Cup was held, and the Chinese national team miraculously entered the finals. I watched little of that tournament, only seeing China's first appearance against Costa Rica. Seeing Sun Jihai injured and substituted, I knew tragedy had begun...

In our school, there was a funny tradition during the high school entrance exam: the graduates of the third year would play football matches against students from other middle schools at the county-level examination site. This tradition lasted for many years, and even students who broke bones and couldn't take exams continued to participate. Every year before the exam, the school would earnestly advise against it, but prohibiting it couldn't stop it, why? Because repeaters would come back and tell everyone about the matches, making everyone eager. Hearing they won against a certain middle school last year, we wanted to win again; hearing they lost to another school, we wanted to retaliate for our seniors. I don't remember how the repeaters conveyed the orders that year, but upon hearing that every year someone would return to repeat due to football-related accidents, I was afraid. I decided not to bring football shoes to the exam this time.

Our exam site was at Qi-Lu Bo-Yuan, where we arrived two days early. Although I didn't want to have any accidents due to football, I still went to visit the football field with classmates after settling the luggage. It was my habit to check out the football field whenever I visited a new school, like a scout. Bo-Yuan's football field was similar to ours, except the basketball court wasn't inside the football field. It was also a dirt field. I came to the goalpost and jumped like a professional goalkeeper to grab the crossbar, unfortunately, I fell off, hitting my right elbow on a stone on the ground, and blood came out. Seeing red meant success! It wasn't serious, it wouldn't affect the exam...

None of my classmates who were football friends brought footballs, but I heard that students from other classes did. My feet were itching, wanting to play. On the first day of the exam, I met a student from another class in the dormitory. He said some students from his class were playing against students from Bo-Yuan's own school and invited us to join quickly. At that moment, I couldn't resist anymore, borrowing a pair of football shoes from a classmate, which were a size too big, but it didn't matter. They had just started, I came and replaced a boy, forgot the score, but we won big. I didn't score, but assisted three or four times, it was a great game.

After the high school entrance exam, I often led the village children to go out and play football, challenging other villages. We always won big, and I scored the most, six goals once. Finally, the town's football friends found out and challenged us. The result was painful, we lost 11-21. But it wasn't embarrassing, we had two high school students plus me, a third-year graduate, against mostly junior high and even elementary school children, while their youngest was also a third-year graduate, and they had several high school students. Age and physique were factors, and I still scored six goals, but we ultimately lost. As punishment, I walked 11 miles home.

After that game, I didn't play in any matches for a long time. I would just take a ball and play alone in the yard, practicing dribbling moves, etc., because I felt my moves could be more elegant, so I focused on practicing the step-over move.

In 2002, I was in the first year of high school. There were hardly any football players in the class, let alone anyone who played well. I always played football with fourth-year students, who played much better. However, I found that my step-over move had little effect on them, so I reverted to a simpler style.

In the summer of 2003, after choosing my major, I ended up in a liberal arts class composed mainly of students from my original class. The head teacher changed, the new teacher nicknamed Lao Dai was about 180 cm tall, weighed over 220 pounds, with a big head wearing a huge tea-colored glasses. He had violent tendencies, enjoyed tormenting students, including those from other classes. Initially, there were around 120 students in our class, but eventually, only 90 remained. Some went to specialized classes, but most were expelled or transferred due to his violence. I often wondered if there was anyone among those 120 students who hadn't been beaten, scolded, or punished by Lao Dai? I doubt it, probably fewer than 10 people, and I was deeply affected...

When choosing majors, I met Sun Xiaohui, my desk mate. On the first self-study class, I heard Sun Xiaohui and his desk mate Gong Haowei talking about football, specifically European league matches. I listened for a while without interrupting. Later, during a class, Sun Xiaohui disappeared and only returned just before the next class started, drenched in sweat. I asked him where he had gone, and he said he had gone to play football. Haha, I gave him a look of disdain. Looking at Sun Xiaohui, although he was quite tall, he was pale and not robust, completely lacking the wildness of the children I usually played football with...

We were going to the branch school for the second year, so there was no chance to play football with Sun Xiaohui at No.1 Middle School. We agreed to meet at Bo-Yuan. A few days later, we moved to Bo-Yuan, and finally, we could play football during PE class. Remember, the football field at Bo-Yuan was full of weeds, and I fell once, staining my pants with grass color. After a few PE classes, everyone got familiar. About 20 people regularly played football, so during PE class, we split into groups to play. Eventually, we fixed the members in advance, and started playing as soon as we arrived at the field, and I and Sun Xiaohui were in the same group. That period of football was perhaps the purest football I've ever played, with no foul play, no dirty tricks, no greed, and no controversies. Right, Mr. Platini, Mr. Blatter, the purest football was permanently defined on the sandy soil field at Qi-Lu Bo-Yuan...

The most interesting part was Liu Zhen's technical statistics. After each game, he would record the goals and scores, and I found it amusing too, so I copied some from him. In the last semester, we played 25 games, and my group with Sun Xiaohui won 21, drew 3, and lost 1. I scored 28 goals, and Sun Xiaohui scored 33. Winning was easy, let me talk about the loss. That was the last good game, our class's last PE class. Our group of 8 played against the other side of 12. Early in the game, I received a pass from Sun Xiaohui and headed the ball to make it 1-0, then Sun Xiaohui scored another goal to make it 2-0. At that point, we had a penalty opportunity, and I easily scored. Suddenly, I realized that this was our last football game, and they had never beaten us, perhaps they would never have the chance again...

I signaled to the goalkeeper that the goal didn't count, and I deliberately missed the penalty. I retreated to the backfield, no longer attacking, and deliberately let them score. Finally, Ju Peigen's goal completed the comeback, 3-2, they won. Class ended, and when the game finished, looking at the smiles of Chen Guotao, Gong Haowei, Liu Zhen, Li Zhaoting, Ju Peigen, etc., I was also happy. I felt we didn't lose, we were winners too...

During school years, each academic year coincided with the European League season. That year, it was the 2003-04 season, and my beloved Manchester United was exactly at the time of a major overhaul. Keane was getting old, Giggs was playing at his lowest point, Beckham left, Silvestre was a giant SB, Howard occasionally went crazy, Ferdinand was suspended, Brown was barely competent, except for the frontline with Van Nistelrooy and the winter signing of Saha, there was nothing else to say...

At that time, I was obsessed with Manchester United, as if I and Manchester United were one. Watching Manchester United lose or even draw would cause me great pain. Finally, one day I caused trouble. That day, Manchester United lost again, and I was brainlessly listening to the radio in the classroom, lost in thought. At that moment, the cruel Lao Dai came, and I was caught off guard. To others, it might seem like a small thing, but Lao Dai was an extremely sinister person. He told me I must leave the class, which was actually just an excuse to extort benefits. Naively, I agreed to his unreasonable request and pleaded to return to the classroom for the last evening study session. I loved this class, these football friends, how could I bear to leave?

That night was like a farewell performance. Chen Guotao even hugged me in the hallway, crying uncontrollably. Many football friends and classmates wrote notes to comfort me. Writing here, I can't help but feel a surge of emotion, and I want to thank those classmates who encouraged and cared for me: Thank you, I will never forget you.

Sun Xiaohui, Chen Guotao, Gong Haowei, Liu Zhen, Mao Fangchao, Wang Jun Kai, Xiao Chenglong, Li Zhaoting...

This wasn't the first time Lao Dai messed with me. I had been tormented many times before. Sometimes for reading sports newspapers and magazines, sometimes even for listening to the radio, which were all considered acts of great disrespect according to Lao Dai. In his words: anything besides textbooks and tutoring materials were forbidden items, violators would be dealt with severely. I had endured many punishments, sometimes checking in dormitories, sometimes writing guarantees promising not to repeat similar mistakes, and if I did, I would voluntarily leave the class collective. With Lao Dai's words: I have done my utmost for you...

Haha... It's truly horrific... Are you asking us to thank your 180 ancestors?

Amen, Lord, Emmanuel, forgive me, Amitabha Buddha, give me a way out...

My brothers, let's say goodbye, maybe temporarily, or maybe forever. My brothers, I love you.

1 Realistic approach

The next day, I sat on the bus home. I don't remember what day it was, but according to the driver, it was a good day, evident from the many wedding cars I saw... But I was lost in thought on the bus. Was this my good day?? At that moment, I felt as if I had been abandoned by society, pale, powerless, wanting to hold onto something, but it was all empty and bleak. Reality was so cruel. That day wasn't the weekend, and I don't remember how I explained my reason for coming home to the neighbors, certainly not saying I was dropping out of school...

My psychological resilience was ordinary, but in difficult situations, I could remain calm and burst with stored energy. All the way, I didn't know how to explain the situation to my parents. Back home, I calmed down and simply explained the situation. My parents were farmers, with no money, no power, no square dancing, no connections, but they understood that this time, money was needed, and help was required...

Uncle-in-law and my uncle worked in the county, they knew a vice-principal of the school, and finally, with the accompaniment of two elders and the vice-principal, the beloved Mr. Dai was very accommodating. Over dinner, he promised that if I could restrain myself, I could immediately return to school...

It took exactly seven days from leaving the school to returning. What seemed like a normal seven days felt like seven centuries, every night I stayed up late but woke up early, sitting in the corner of the sofa, unable to eat, unwilling to talk, eyes glazed, not watching TV, not watching football matches, every time the sun set, I would ask my parents who came home after finishing farm work when I could return to school! Time was so slow, so suffocatingly slow, I missed the classroom, never missing it so much, I missed those football friends, even if I didn't play football anymore, just watching them play made me happy...

Missing is a disease... Missing is a breathing pain...

I don't remember how I returned to the classroom seven years ago. It seemed very calm that day, yes, very calm, but I was still called to the office, the place I hated the most in my life. Things were simple. While I was away, Mr. Dai rummaged through my desk and took away the notes exchanged between me and my brothers.
