Want to get on the App Store's recommendation page? Learn to think like Apple first.

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 19:28:04

　　English Title: How to get featured on the App Store

　　Our product has been fortunate enough to be featured several times on the iOS and Mac App Stores, and we are often asked by friends and developers for the secret behind it. In reality, the secret is... there is no secret!

　　Apple features apps to highlight their own products and for the benefit of consumers, not for the developers. Keep this in mind, and your chances will greatly improve.

　　That said, people often overlook small details that can increase the odds. Here are some tips I have:

　　1. Create an amazing app

　　Easier said than done, but this is truly the most important step. Apps made with the sole intention of making a quick buck won't last long. If you focus on creating something amazing that you're proud to show off as a testament to your hard work, then your product is closer to being featured on the homepage. Stay consistent, and your product will eventually be discovered, sooner or later.

　　From Apple's perspective, they value apps that demonstrate the superior performance of the Mac and iOS platforms. Apple doesn’t feature apps to advertise for developers; instead, it’s to showcase Apple itself, to tell consumers about the unique experiences they can have using Apple products.

　　2. Aim for "the latest and the greatest"

　　Want to learn about the latest APIs in the newly released OS and gain insight into Apple's future focus? Attending WWDC (Worldwide Developers Conference) is an excellent way. You will surely gain a lot of inspiration and incorporate it into your app.

　　One such API is iCloud. Apple has recently been pushing this product, and it is likely to become a long-term focus. If your app does not yet support iCloud, start now!

　　Remember, apps that highlight the latest OS and are compatible with new hardware are what Apple cares about the most.

　　3. It should be universal

　　Your app should work on Mac, iPhone, and iPad. This effectively increases its chances of being featured, for the following reasons:

　　Imagine this: among thousands of apps, would Apple choose one that showcases the unique experience of iOS, supports iCloud, and runs on Macs, or one that runs on both Android and Apple phones?

　　Put yourself in their shoes, and the answer becomes clear.

　　4. Invest in good UI and UX

　　The competition in the Apple Store is fierce, though bloodless. Your competitors are numerous, well-resourced, and possess world-class teams. What you need is an opportunity!

　　It’s difficult for a solo-developed app to stand out in the iOS App Store, so I recommend hiring the best UI/UX designers you can afford. If budget is limited and you can't hire full-time staff, check out Dribbble and Folyo. There are many talented individuals there who might collaborate with you to create something great.

　　5. Media coverage

　　This is hard. But it is also an indispensable part of achieving product success. Everyone thinks this is old news, and you may not believe it, but many app developers don’t even think about media coverage until the last moment, or even after the product has already launched.

　　My advice is to create a teaser page with product advertisements, at least including screenshots and a brief description of the app along with its release date. A teaser page without these key pieces of information is ineffective.

　　Don’t forget to include email registration and all common social buttons on the teaser page, which will help attract more interested people. Once the app is released, timely announcements will help boost its ranking on the App Store, increasing its visibility and awareness.

　　Promoting too early can backfire. Generally, a few weeks in advance is sufficient. Dragging out the campaign too long risks losing people's interest, and competitors might steal your ideas and launch similar products before you do.

　　There is another important point...

　　Keep in mind that Apple receives approximately 1,000 app submission requests every day, and your product could easily get lost in the sea of apps.

　　To ensure that you and your app are known to Apple, try reaching out to Apple employees. I know they sometimes seem like a walled garden, but if your app is good enough, they will want to know about it.

　　How do you reach out to Apple employees? My recommendation is to attend WWDC, chat with Apple developers after sessions, network with peers, and submit Radar Bug Reports. If you catch Apple's attention, someone will reach out to you.

　　There are millions of apps on the App Store, and the chance of getting featured is slim. But the good news is, even if you don’t get featured, you’ve still created an amazing app, and getting featured would just be the cherry on top!
