Thin people are more prone to osteoporosis than fat people.

by po13929357028 on 2012-03-03 17:32:08

In today's society, thinness is considered beautiful, and weight loss advertisements are everywhere. However, according to surveys, underweight individuals are more prone to osteoporosis. This is because overweight people, having to bear the weight of their bodies, give their bones a certain amount of exercise on a regular basis, whereas underweight individuals lack this exercise. If they also enjoy smoking and drinking, the likelihood of developing osteoporosis will increase significantly. With the arrival of an aging society, the incidence of osteoporosis is increasing. Currently, there are already 84 million osteoporosis patients in our country, accounting for 6.6% of the total population, with most patients being elderly people over 65 years old and postmenopausal women.

When people talk about osteoporosis, they may think of fractures and bone cracks. An orthopedic expert from Kunming Kunlu Hospital explains that fractures and bone cracks are not the main dangers of osteoporosis; fractures can also lead to a series of problems associated with aging. Fracture patients have to rest in bed, leading to a decline in physical resistance, making them more susceptible to disease; their daily activity levels decrease, reducing the elasticity of arterial vessels and making them more prone to damage. Eventually, the human immune system may be unable to resist some serious diseases and infections.

Osteoporosis differs from other diseases in that patients often do not know they have osteoporosis. It is a 'silent disease', often without symptoms, until the bones fracture from minor trauma. Some people fall on ice and break their arm, but they don't realize they have osteoporosis, thinking it was just an accident. But when you can break your arm by bumping into a doorframe, it shows that your bones are already very fragile, possibly indicating osteoporosis. Waiting until harm occurs before seeking treatment is often too late. Therefore, regularly undergoing bone density tests is a good way to find out early and prevent bone loss.

For dealing with osteoporosis, prevention is better than cure. This is because treatment cannot cure osteoporosis, it can only slow down the rate of bone density loss and assist in renewing bones. Effective stimulation at the right time is necessary for bone growth. In terms of diet, attention should be paid to calcium supplementation, drinking more milk, eating shrimp skin, soy products, small fish and other foods with higher calcium content; vitamin D supplementation should also be noted. There are three sources of vitamin D: sunlight, food, and vitamin supplements, so elderly people should go out more often to sunbathe and eat foods such as eggs and cod liver oil which are high in vitamin D. At the same time, good living habits should be cultivated, avoiding smoking, excessive drinking, and excessive consumption of coffee and strong tea.
