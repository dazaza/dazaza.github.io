In the Low-carbon Era, China's New Energy Aims for the World's Highest Point

by ditan360 on 2010-06-23 10:46:09

Low Carbon Network News: "The world is entering a major adjustment and transformation period dominated by low carbon. To address the contradiction between the high-carbon structure based on high-carbon energy and the demand for low-carbon development, China has proposed to vigorously develop low-carbon energy technology and accelerate the formation of pillar industries such as wind energy, solar energy, and advanced nuclear energy as green and alternative energy." Xie Kechang, Vice President of the Chinese Academy of Engineering, said in a keynote speech at the 'Science and Technology Innovation and Urban Future' thematic forum (hereinafter referred to as the 'Wuxi Forum') held in Shanghai Expo 2010 on the 21st.

Xie Kechang and other experts present pointed out that energy shortages and environmental problems are forcing the global community towards "low carbon," and in the global game of low-carbon economy, the new energy industry is gradually becoming a "must-contested area" for various countries. In the realistic pursuit of future energy structures, China is making great strides forward, seeking to achieve the world's highest point in the new energy industry.

The low-carbon economy is changing the international new energy industry pattern.

As global environmental and resource issues become increasingly prominent, new energy has become a focal point of attention for countries around the world. Since the financial crisis, whether developed or developing countries have placed greater emphasis on developing new energy and other low-carbon economic industries. With the deep-rooted concept of low-carbon economy, new energy is facing a new round of development opportunities.

"In Europe, 30 cities have been selected as pilot units to implement high-tech energy systems." Supachai Panitchpakdi, Secretary-General of the United Nations Conference on Trade and Development, said at the Wuxi Forum. Among them, Denmark clearly proposed in its latest energy plan that by 2030, the energy composition will be 50% wind energy, 15% solar energy, and 35% bioenergy and other renewable energies; Sweden regards "sustainable development of energy conservation and alternative energy" as a basic national policy, and simultaneously considers the sustainable development of alternative energy as the focus of its energy policy. At the same time, the European Commission has set a target of achieving 20% renewable energy and 12% solar power generation by 2020. It also announced on March 9 this year that the EU will invest 105 billion euros to support the "green economy" in the EU region before 2013.

As for the United States, after Obama was elected President of the United States, he made the investment in new energy involving hundreds of billions of dollars one of the most important stakes in saving the U.S. economy, which became the most radical public spending plan since Roosevelt's New Deal. More than half of the projects involve the energy industry, equivalent to the complete reconstruction of the entire U.S. energy system. Among them, the photovoltaic investment tax reduction policy has been extended, and the subsidy method more favorable to investment has been adjusted.

Supachai Panitchpakdi said, "Exploring the development of low-carbon, green, and renewable energy cities is not limited to developed countries. Among developing countries, China, Brazil, and India have taken the lead and have become exporters of solar, wind, and biofuel technologies."

It is understood that China currently holds a leading position in many energy fields: such as nuclear power and coal energy. China is the country with the largest investment in renewable energy sources, and China's wind power and solar power generation technology have greatly reduced the cost of wind and solar power generation worldwide.

"Developing new energy is the most important way to achieve a low-carbon economy, and the new energy industry is becoming a powerful tool for governments around the world to solve economic recessions." Many experts attending the Wuxi Forum expressed that in today's rapid urbanization, the low-carbon economy is influencing the global layout of the new energy industry, and the new energy industry represented by solar energy, wind energy, hydropower, and nuclear energy is showing an accelerating development trend.

China’s New Energy Industry Begins to Show International Competitiveness

The core of the low-carbon economy is new energy technology, including wind energy, solar energy, geothermal energy, tidal energy, biomass energy, hydropower, and nuclear energy. In recent years, China's new energy industry has developed rapidly. Taking the fastest-growing wind power as an example, it has achieved a doubling growth for three consecutive years; Jiangsu Province's photovoltaic production capacity accounts for half of the country and 20% globally; Zhejiang, Shanghai, Jiangxi, Hebei, Sichuan, Henan, Chongqing, Qinghai and other provinces and regions have quickly formed industrial division of labor and distinctive advantages. More than ten enterprises have formed the "Chinese Solar Overseas Listed Sector", occupying seven seats among the top 20 globally, and in 2007, Wuxi Suntech's module production volume ranked first in the world.

Data shows that China ranks among the world leaders in multiple areas related to the development of the new energy industry: As of the end of 2009, China's installed wind power and solar power capacities reached 17.58 million kilowatts and 230,000 kilowatts respectively, representing increases of 51 times and 7.7 times compared to 2000. Photovoltaic power generation has shown explosive growth, with an annual installation capacity exceeding 160 megawatts, surpassing the total installation capacity over several decades prior to 2009. Of this, grid-connected solar power accounted for 85%, reaching 135 megawatts. The nuclear power sector has introduced and constructed 9.1 million kilowatts, with a construction capacity of 20 million kilowatts, aiming to reach a target of 70 million kilowatts by 2020. Currently, China's solar manufacturing capacity and solar utilization area have reached the world's first place, with wind power doubling several years in a row. In 2009, China added more than 10 million kilowatts of wind power installations, ranking first in the world, followed by the United States and Germany. In terms of cumulative installed capacity, China ranks third after the United States and Germany.

As a concentrated practice and display of the new energy concept, during this Expo, solar photovoltaic power generation technology has been widely applied in the Expo Park, with a total installed capacity reaching 4.5 megawatts, making it the largest photovoltaic building-integrated grid-connected power generation system in the country and even in Asia. Meanwhile, the Expo Park has become the largest global LED demonstration zone, where more than 80% of the night landscape lighting uses LED light sources.

"Shanghai Expo presents us with a beautiful vision of the city's future because it adheres to the green and low-carbon concept, applying many green and low-carbon technologies and showcasing the best urban practices, presenting to the world the promising future prospects of cities and providing the best choice for realizing the city's beautiful future. For instance, solar power generation technology, semiconductor lighting, harmless treatment of garbage, reduction, and resource utilization technology." said Xie Kechang.

Another example is the transportation services within the Expo Park, which use a total of 1,080 new energy vehicles including fuel cells, pure power, and hybrid power, achieving "zero emissions" of carbon dioxide from passenger transport vehicles within the park and "low emissions" for surrounding traffic. Currently, buses running on the roads in Shanghai include those using natural gas, electricity, hybrid power, and other new energy sources. Shanghai has the world's first commercial diether natural gas refueling station, demonstrating excellent effects, and future new energy refueling stations will gradually form a network.

High Starting Point: China Advances Towards the World's Highest Point in New Energy

As the largest carbon emitter, China's new energy industry has received unprecedented attention. During the "12th Five-Year Plan" period, China's primary task is to cultivate and develop emerging energy industries, including nuclear power, hydropower, wind power, solar power, and bioenergy among renewable energy sources.

"In the next decade, 50% of the countries in the world will achieve parity between the cost of solar power generation and the general cost of power supply in the grid." Shi Zhengrong boldly predicted at the Wuxi Forum, "By the end of the 21st century, 70% of the world's electricity will be supplied by solar power."

Currently, nearly 20 provinces across the country have issued new energy promotion plans, and more than 100 cities have proposed to build new energy industry bases, including large and mega-cities like Shanghai, Shenzhen, Chengdu, and Suzhou.

The host city of this forum, Wuxi City, has already determined the photovoltaic industry as the leading dominant industry guiding the city's industrial upgrading and high-tech development, providing strong support. Statistics show that in 2008, Wuxi's new energy industry output value reached 37.2 billion yuan. Among them, the photovoltaic industry realized an output value of 30.2 billion yuan, increasing by 145.72% year-on-year, with the solar cell production capacity accounting for more than 50% of the national total scale. The total investment in wind energy industry construction has approached 10 billion yuan, with an output value of 7 billion yuan. Meanwhile, a number of backbone leading enterprises with independent intellectual property rights and brands have emerged, playing a driving and supporting role in the entire industry's development.

In addition, Wuxi City has specially formulated the "Wuxi New Energy Industry Adjustment and Enhancement Action Plan (2009-2011)." The plan sets clear goals for industry development: to achieve a sales revenue of 55 billion yuan in the new energy industry in 2009, and 85 billion yuan in 2010. By 2011, the city's new energy industry aims to achieve a sales revenue of 127 billion yuan.

Journalists learned at the Wuxi Forum that China's first large-scale offshore wind power demonstration project - the Donghai Bridge Offshore Wind Farm has been preliminarily built, which is also the first offshore wind power grid connection project outside Europe. Meanwhile, the Shanghai municipal government is also actively promoting the implementation of solar roof plans in government office premises and residential areas.

Zhou Haomin, Deputy Director of the Shanghai Municipal Commission of Economy and Information Technology, stated at a special news conference for the Expo that Shanghai will take advantage of hosting the Expo to comprehensively promote the development of the new energy industry.

According to the latest statistics from Bloomberg New Energy Finance, China's newly added financial investment in wind power in 2009 was $21.8 billion, an increase of 27% year-on-year; the solar energy investment was $1.9 billion, a significant increase of 97%, thus driving the total newly added financial investment in clean energy in the Asia-Pacific region to reach $37.3 billion. China has surpassed Germany and become the second-largest global investor in renewable energy, only behind the United States.

At the same time, alternative fuel oil-based bioenergy and technologically mature nuclear energy will present a rapid development trend in the long term and are favored in the capital market for long-term layout. It is not difficult to see that under the combined effect of wealth effect and policy stimulation, China's new energy industry is gradually advancing towards the global high ground step by step.
