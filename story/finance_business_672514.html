When the drowsy mood comes over me

by 8j6q24l1p on 2012-02-22 22:42:43

After entering spring, with the rise in temperature, the body's pores, sweat glands, and blood vessels begin to dilate, and skin blood circulation also becomes more vigorous. This way, the blood supply to the brain will relatively decrease. With the weather warming up, metabolism gradually becomes more vigorous, oxygen consumption continuously increases, and the oxygen supply to the brain is inevitably insufficient. Coupled with the benign stimulation of warm temperatures, the brain is subjected to a certain degree of inhibition. Therefore, people feel drowsy and always feel like they haven't slept enough. What should we do about spring fatigue? Let's take a look at the methods to overcome spring fatigue.

1. Go to bed early and get up early

With good rest and sleep in spring, the human body can be adjusted and replenished, further promoting the ability of functions to withstand tension, reducing daytime drowsiness. Sleeping too much cannot increase the blood supply to the brain; instead, it can cause laziness, making one sleepier and lazier. One thing to note is that in spring, you should try not to stay up late to avoid inducing or worsening spring fatigue.

2. Look into the distance

In spring, go outdoors and look into the distance, letting the beautiful natural scenery come into full view, giving yourself good visual stimulation, which helps eliminate spring fatigue.

3. Use your brain wisely

This means giving the left hemisphere of the brain appropriate rest. The brain has different left and right hemispheres. In daily work during spring, the left hemisphere mainly handles language, mathematics, and abstract thinking, while the right hemisphere is less utilized by mental laborers, making it relatively relaxed. Therefore, when your mind feels unclear or painful, put down your work, listen to music, appreciate flowers, or do some exercises, allowing the right hemisphere to move and be utilized, giving the left hemisphere appropriate rest. More importantly, exercise allows the brain, which consumes the most oxygen in the human body, to be replenished and supplied in time, which helps alleviate fatigue.

4. Brush teeth and wash face frequently

Brushing teeth and washing face is a relatively simple and effective way to eliminate spring fatigue. When fatigue strikes, use aromatic toothpaste to brush your teeth and rinse your mouth, and wash your face with cold water, which can refresh and invigorate you.

5. Jogging

Jogging helps cells and tissues get extra oxygen, waking up the brain.

6. Swimming

Swimming enhances the body's endurance without increasing the burden on the joints. It combats spring fatigue and eliminates tiredness.

7. Discover your own hobbies

Cultivating good interests and hobbies is the best method for mental supplementation. For example, growing flowers not only beautifies the environment but also purifies the air, benefiting physical and mental health. Experiments have proven: walking among flowers and gardens every day can increase endurance by 15%, reduce surface skin temperature by 1°C to 2°C, reduce pulse rate per minute by 4 to 8 times, shorten the time to clear fatigue by 80%, and improve smell, hearing, vision, and touch to their optimal states. No wonder someone said that flowers are our best "health doctors." Hobbies such as calligraphy, painting, writing, and raising birds are all good ways for mental supplementation. As long as you like them, they can refine your character and are not limited to any particular form. For example, practicing calligraphy requires high concentration, with the pen moving gracefully, using eyes, hands, and heart together. After reaching a certain level, internal energy can be communicated and directed to the tip of the pen, achieving the effect of practicing qigong.

8. Control and regulate emotions, balance your mindset

Skin nerves are also controlled by the brain. If you are in poor spirits or under prolonged tension, fear, or suppression, it can lead to poor skin blood circulation, insufficient nutrient supply, making the skin pale, wrinkled, and aging prematurely.
