Two confirmed absentees for Arsenal's clash with Liverpool as Wenger refuses Cruyff's call to bench Cesc Fabregas

by qinglai888 on 2009-12-16 12:32:33

Arsenal vs Liverpool: Two Confirmed Absences in Starting Lineup, Wenger Again Rejects Cruyff's Call for Fabregas

Sina Sports - This Wednesday's away match against Burnley is not expected to be too challenging for Arsenal, with the sole aim being to secure three points and reclaim the lead over Villa once again. However, Arsene Wenger's squad has suffered some new injuries, as confirmed by Arsenal's official website. In Sunday's match against Liverpool, starting player Denilson suffered a back injury, while Traore injured his groin. It has been confirmed that both players will not feature in the upcoming match against Burnley.

However, there is some good news. Eboue, who missed the Liverpool game due to a groin strain, has returned to the squad and can bolster the right-sided options. Wenger said: "There was too little recovery time after Sunday's game, so we have picked up a few more injuries. Denilson is definitely out of the question, he might be available for Hull on Saturday, but we must conduct thorough checks to ensure his back is fully recovered before he plays. However, Eboue has returned to the team."

After defeating Liverpool, Wenger was in high spirits as his team had completely shaken off the shadow of their heavy defeat to Chelsea. Discussing the championship race this season, Wenger stated that the Premier League title battle for the 2009-10 season is the most open (in terms of the number of teams involved) since he arrived at Arsenal. In an interview with the official website, Wenger said: "No team has been able to dominate early in the season. Instead, four teams are closely entangled, and I believe that overall, six, seven, or even eight teams can surprise us on any given weekend. Clearly, this is the most open championship race I've experienced in my 13 years here. I feel that many teams have a real chance of winning the league title."

The respect from other teams does not equate to pessimism about his own team. Wenger has always held special confidence in Arsenal, saying: "I have deeply believed from the start of the season that this will be our year, and I said the same after the Chelsea game. The reason I say this is because: look at how well we have improved as a whole, how strong our team spirit and quality is, and how thoroughly prepared we are for each match, like going into battle. From September to next May, all of this will continue to improve."

For Arsenal to maintain their championship aspirations, Cesc Fabregas' importance cannot be overstated. With Van Persie injured, Fabregas is almost the only guiding light for Arsenal. Wenger naturally wants to ensure that Fabregas can play as many key matches as possible. Some external requests that can be refused will be refused, such as the friendly match between Catalonia and Argentina scheduled for next month on the 22nd. Catalonia coach Johan Cruyff requested Fabregas to participate in this event at Camp Nou, but Wenger firmly rejected it.

Fabregas takes pride in his Catalan heritage and also admires Cruyff, but Wenger disregards these sentiments. He has the right to reject such requests because Catalonia is not recognized as an official FIFA national team. However, the 22-year-old Fabregas feels his physical condition is fine, and since it's not a week of double fixtures, he believes he has time to visit Spain. He said: "I'll play in the matches against Burnley on Wednesday and Hull three days later, but after that, there will be no Premier League games for nearly a week, so I can return to Spain on the 22nd to play for Catalonia."

In Wenger's mind, however, he can only focus on his own team. There is a crucial match against Villa the following weekend, where three of the big four have already lost to Villa, raising questions about whether Arsenal can perform well. Van Persie got injured during international duty for Holland, and Liverpool explicitly refused to allow Mascherano to represent Argentina in the friendly against Catalonia. Wenger, who has always disliked the complications of international matches, naturally would not agree to let Fabregas play in a game that doesn't count as an A-level match. Wenger said: "We must respect the concept of priority, and the priority is the club itself."

Besides this match, Spain has another highly anticipated friendly next week – a match between the La Liga All-Stars and the African All-Stars. Known as the second Africa Charity Match, it is jointly organized by UNICEF and the Kanouté Foundation and will take place at the Bernabeu. Originally, the African team's roster included five Arsenal players: Song, Nasri, Diaby, Sagna, and Eboue. Clearly, besides locally born players, those with African heritage were also counted, which Wenger would not approve of. Therefore, in the final roster, there isn't a single Arsenal player, and in fact, not even one active Premier League player. The closest connection to the Premier League, apart from the founder of the Kanouté Foundation, former West Ham and Tottenham forward and current Sevilla player, Mali's Frederic Kanouté, is probably former Manchester United striker Manucho, who moved to Valladolid this summer.
