A Tidal Wave of Android Games is Coming to the PC

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 14:40:06

In the past two years, with the popularization of smart mobile devices, game works for the Android system have been emerging in an endless stream. Besides most casual games, there are also quite a few notable masterpieces. And in the future, these excellent mobile game works will have the opportunity to be launched on PC and Mac platforms through professional game publishers.

Recently, the world-renowned game company BigFish announced a future plan, hoping to launch more Android games on PC and Mac platforms through BlueStacks technology. The BlueStacks technology mentioned here is originally an Android emulator, which many tech-savvy users should already know about.

BigFish has already sounded the rallying call to mobile game developers, stating that it owns the world's largest casual game development and distribution network. In addition to over 140 exclusive partners, it also has access to more than one billion PC and Mac users worldwide.

BigFish stated that developers only need to upload their games to the future digital distribution platform to allow PC and Mac players to experience their works, and they can also gain considerable profits from this process. Throughout the entire process, developers do not need to make any modifications or porting to the game source code; the BlueStacks technology will provide all necessary compatibility interfaces.

Some analysts believe that although BigFish has not announced the specific launch time of the new platform, it is foreseeable that this will greatly enrich the gaming experience for touch-screen PC users. At the same time, this is not good news for Microsoft's Windows Store.
