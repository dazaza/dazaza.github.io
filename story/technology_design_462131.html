Man sued for staying in hospital for three years without leaving, judge holds court in the ward.

by zzf000zxye0 on 2011-07-13 16:25:00

Screenshot of the newspaper article:

"I've been lying here for nearly three years. I want to go home, but the issue hasn't been resolved yet, so I can't be discharged!" Yang Zhongkuan, 41 years old, stretched out his shriveled hand that resembled an "eagle's claw" and pointed towards the door to tell the reporter.

Yesterday morning, Fangshan First Hospital took Yang Zhongkuan to court, demanding that he leave the hospital and pay over 160,000 yuan in overdue medical fees. The judges from Fangshan Court moved the courtroom into the ward for a special trial.

Pre-trial preparations

Early morning grooming with the help of a caregiver

At 6 o'clock yesterday morning, Yang Zhongkuan urged caregiver Old Chen to shave his face because there would be a court session in the ward today, "There will be a lot of people coming." Yang Zhongkuan said he liked having many people around as it made the ward livelier.

Having lain in bed for almost three years, apart from eating and drinking, all he did was watch TV. Whenever someone entered the ward, he always wanted to chat with them, but few people were willing to stop. "It's too stuffy here," he said.

"How many people do you think will come?" Yang Zhongkuan kept asking caregiver Old Chen, "Don't move, be careful not to pull." Seeing Yang Zhongkuan getting too excited, Old Chen stopped him. After shaving, Yang Zhongkuan extended his "eagle claw"-like hand to feel his face, "Is this part not clean?" Only after the caregiver in the next bed confirmed did Yang Zhongkuan stop muttering.

After breakfast, requested to get dressed

After breakfast, Yang Zhongkuan made his second request, asking Old Chen to dress him, "Since returning to the hospital on April 22nd, this is the first time I've worn clothes." Yang Zhongkuan said that due to lying down for too long, his limbs had atrophied, and his weight had dropped from 140 pounds to over 70 pounds, "Wearing clothes feels uncomfortable."

He said that he rarely wore clothes since he was always lying down and couldn't even sit in a wheelchair.

Lively ward, nervous in front of the camera

At 10 o'clock in the morning, Yang Zhongkuan's ward became lively as the judges from Fangshan Court entered the ward, and his brother also came to attend the trial.

To ensure the smooth running of the trial, the hospital specially moved another patient to another ward.

Staff brought in tables and placed the defendant and plaintiff signs, filling up the narrow ward completely.

When the media's camera was pointed at Yang Zhongkuan, he looked a bit nervous, lifting his hand and using his stiff fingers to adjust his collar while shouting, "Brother, am I dressed properly?" "Yes, you are," his brother replied.

Special trial

Brother dissatisfied with hospital treatment flaws

At 10:30, the gavel sounded, and the trial officially began.

After the plaintiff's agent from Fangshan First Hospital finished reading the complaint, Yang Zhongkuan's brother said he disagreed with the hospital's demands.

"My younger brother being like this is the hospital's fault because they didn't keep the perpetrator, who ran away. Therefore, the money shouldn't be demanded from us." As soon as his brother finished speaking, Yang Zhongkuan lying on the bed nodded vigorously.

"We have no obligation to watch the perpetrator for the patients, and we have no power to control them. The defendant is shirking responsibility," the plaintiff's agent said.

Yang Zhongkuan's brother also mentioned flaws in the hospital's treatment: "After October 20th, 2007, my brother has been bedridden. Due to inadequate care, he developed bedsores."

"And initially, when my brother was sent to the hospital, they said surgery was necessary. When I asked what would happen if we didn't do it, they said the worst-case scenario would be needing a wheelchair later. But after the surgery, he couldn't even get out of bed, and even the movable parts of his body have atrophied," Yang Zhongkuan's brother said.

Hospital emphasizes continuous treatment

"Our treatment has no flaws. Even though the defendant owes money, we have continued treatment, and we are still giving him intravenous fluids now. We disagree with the defendant's claims," the hospital's agent stated. They emphasized that debts must be repaid, which is self-evident, and now that the defendant has reached the standard for discharge, he should be discharged. Staying in the bed for too long is a waste of resources.

"I also want to go home, but until the problem is resolved, I will absolutely not leave the hospital," Yang Zhongkuan insisted on his position. Due to significant differences between both parties, the judge announced that mediation would not take place, and a verdict would be announced on another day.

Hospital's statement

Hoping for a good outcome for the defendant

"As a hospital, our treatment of the defendant has no issues whatsoever. It's not because of unpaid bills that we stopped treating him; the hospital has already done everything possible for the defendant's situation," the hospital's agent claimed.

"We know that Yang Zhongkuan cannot bear these costs, but the hospital still hopes to resolve this matter through legal procedures as soon as possible. We hope Yang Zhongkuan finds a good outcome," the hospital expressed after the trial.

Heartbreaking memories

Severe injuries from a car accident leading to high-level paralysis

Yang Zhongkuan is from Zhuanghutai Village, Xayunling Township. Due to intellectual disabilities, villagers called him "fool."

On September 8th, 2007, coworker Old Wen drove a farm tricycle carrying Yang Zhongkuan and four other coworkers out for dinner. "Around 10 PM, when returning to the construction site, it was raining, the speed was a bit fast, plus the mountain road was steep, and the car flipped during a turn," Yang Zhongkuan said that when he woke up, he was already in the hospital. Among everyone involved, Yang Zhongkuan was injured the most severely, with cervical fractures, eventually resulting in high-level paralysis.

Dissatisfaction with the hospital losing the perpetrator

Yang Zhongkuan's brother said that on the night of the incident, he received a notification and rushed to the hospital, where he saw the perpetrator, Old Wen. At the time, Old Wen admitted it was his mistake in the hospital and promised to take responsibility for the medical treatment.

Due to Yang Zhongkuan's mother suffering from cerebral thrombosis in another hospital, his brother attended to her after the surgery.

Unexpectedly, on October 20th, 2007, Old Wen left the hospital, and the 30,000 yuan he paid was quickly used up. The hospital then notified Yang Zhongkuan's brother to come and take care of him.

"My brother said the hospital should figure it out. It's their responsibility that the person ran away," the family refused to come, leaving Yang Zhongkuan abandoned in the hospital, lying there for nearly three years.

"The person ran away, and the hospital also stopped my medication," Yang Zhongkuan said that when he first came to the hospital, he could move his hands, and his legs could straighten. However, due to lying down for too long without any rehabilitation training, his hands and feet have now atrophied.

Temperamental changes, more than ten caregivers replaced

The reporter observed that Yang Zhongkuan's hands were deformed like eagle claws. "Now I can only lie on one side, unable to even turn over," Yang Zhongkuan said he couldn't be without a caregiver, "Lying down is very uncomfortable, it's suffocating, and I always want to chat with someone, but they don't want to talk to me."

Because of lying down for too long, Yang Zhongkuan's temper worsened, often scolding others, making it difficult for caregivers to endure. They couldn't last long, and now more than ten caregivers have been replaced.

"After midnight, he asks others to massage him, and if they don't, he scolds them," at the hospital, whenever mentioning taking care of Yang Zhongkuan, the caregivers all shook their heads unwillingly. It was understood that the shortest stint lasted only three days before the caregiver resigned.

Owing a huge sum, once sent home by the hospital

Lying in the hospital bed, daily eating, drinking, urinating, and defecating all required assistance. After Old Wen fled, the hospital spent money hiring caregivers to take care of Yang Zhongkuan while urging his family to pay.

However, Yang Zhongkuan's brother said he didn't have the ability to take care of him—Yang Zhongkuan himself lived on low-income benefits, his father in his 80s suffered from dementia, and his mother passed away after spending over 300,000 yuan on medical expenses.

Since his brother couldn't take care of Yang Zhongkuan, the hospital applied to the Civil Affairs Bureau for assistance but was denied. To date, Yang Zhongkuan has accumulated over 160,000 yuan in debt to the hospital, with just the caregiver fees amounting to over 50,000 yuan.

On April 21st this year, the hospital sent Yang Zhongkuan home by car, but not long after, he was sent back.

Small wish

To go outside the ward in a wheelchair and bask in the sun

"I can only sleep three or four hours a day, and I particularly fear the darkness." Yang Zhongkuan said he was only 41 years old, "My biggest wish is to be able to sit in a wheelchair and bask in the sun outside the ward."

"I really have nowhere else to go, I can only stay in the hospital," Yang Zhongkuan said he didn't hate his family because if the hospital hadn't let the person escape, he wouldn't have been left alone, "As long as the issue isn't resolved, I won't be discharged."
