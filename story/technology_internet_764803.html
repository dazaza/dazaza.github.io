WeChat Official Accounts: What’s Behind Them

by anonymous on 2013-08-06 18:48:21

WeChat Official Accounts are already beginning to replace the role of Apps in many areas, but will it be the future?

Li Zhifei, who once worked at Google's US headquarters, had a dream of creating a Chinese version of Google Now. To achieve this, he resigned and returned to China to start a business in Shanghai. However, the grandeur of his dream could not solve the problem of limited resources.

Li Zhifei is the founder of "Toutiao Search," a voice search service. In March this year, Toutiao Search launched its Android mobile application. One month later, the WeChat Official Account was launched. Now, Li Zhifei has temporarily abandoned the promotion of the Android app. "Because the effect of the WeChat Official Account is good, and as a small company, due to limited resources, we cannot promote both Android and WeChat simultaneously."

For Li Zhifei, leading a small team to undertake such a large-scale project as search requires learning how to leverage external forces. This time, he chose the largest platform in mobile internet - WeChat. After adding "Toutiao Search" on WeChat, users can simply speak to query weather, restaurants, movie tickets, trains, flights, banks, and more. For example, asking "What high-speed trains are there from Beijing to Shanghai tomorrow?" or "Where are some good Hunan cuisine restaurants nearby?" will yield the desired answers.

Just like Google's birth was inseparable from the vast amount of information on the internet, the open flow of information in the web era allowed this startup to become the world's largest search engine in just two years. In the mobile internet era, information is encapsulated within various apps. A startup cannot recreate content creation before doing search, but the unique account system of WeChat and the open APIs of many mainstream apps actually provide a possibility to connect the closed information within different apps.

The working principle of Toutiao Search is to first convert the user's voice input into text, then perform natural language analysis, search the backend data, and finally return matching results. Essentially, Toutiao Search is a search engine that does not maintain its own content. Most of the backend data comes from other companies' open API interfaces, such as flight and hotel information from Ctrip, group-buying information from Meituan. For those without open APIs, Toutiao Search collects data itself, but ultimately links back to the original website pages, such as Dianping. For information not yet covered by the internet, Toutiao Search prepares its own content, such as bank branches, mobile phone service centers, and public restrooms.

Value of WeChat Official Accounts

In the first month after launching the WeChat Official Account, Li Zhifei did little promotion, merely introducing the product to friends and WeChat groups. In the second month, media coverage of "Toutiao Search" began, and user numbers increased rapidly. When WeChat listed "Toutiao Search" as one of its officially recommended top ten applications, user numbers grew even faster. Currently, it has accumulated 100,000 users, surpassing the total number of Android users, and continues to grow several times per month.

"WeChat itself uses voice interaction, so users already have the habit of using voice. We also do voice search, which aligns well with WeChat's interaction method." Li Zhifei explained that voice search is a product with a simple front-end but requires strong backend technology, making it a perfect fit for WeChat. "Now we dedicate 80% of our efforts to the backend, while the front-end UI and other aspects don't require much attention since WeChat has already solved these problems."

Moreover, compared to individual apps, WeChat Official Accounts offer other irreplaceable advantages.

Li Zhifei still hasn't developed an iOS mobile app because he lacks iOS engineers, which is why he started with Android. For startups, developing an app is a significant investment. Additionally, Android and iOS are currently two separate worlds requiring entirely different front-end designs, development, and promotion strategies, which many startups cannot handle simultaneously. In contrast, WeChat is a cross-platform product with over 400 million users, offering small companies a better alternative to apps.

Besides reducing development costs, WeChat Official Accounts can iterate quickly. For apps, updates mean releasing new versions, submitting them to the App Store, and waiting for approval, consuming time. On the WeChat Official Account platform, changes made in the backend are immediately visible.

Previously, small companies would painstakingly develop an app only to find it unpopular after launch, having already incurred high costs. Li Zhifei suggests, "Now, small companies can try it out on WeChat first, accumulate their first batch of seed users, and understand what their needs really are. 'Toutiao Search' is exactly like that; on WeChat, users ask all sorts of questions, revealing many demands we hadn't thought of before." He gave the example of movie searches; they originally didn't plan to offer this feature, but many people asked about recent good movies, nearby movie group buys, and discounts, so "Toutiao Search" added the movie and group-buying information functions.

Although not a startup, Liu Jun deeply understands this point. He is the principal of the National Museum's Information Network Department Website Studio, responsible for the museum's publicity work on the internet. In September 2012, the WeChat team approached the National Museum, hoping they would launch an official account and provide voice-guided tours. After internal research, the museum found the cost reasonable and decided to give it a try. The National Museum's WeChat Official Account was launched on December 21, 2012, and the voice-guided tour function went live on April 25, 2013. It currently has 2.6 million users, gaining 800,000 followers in one day alone, prompting some in the industry to jokingly say, "One day's growth equals a 'small rumor site'." Behind all this, there wasn't even a dedicated person in charge.

"As a non-profit cultural institution, we don't have the funds to develop apps, and obtaining technical support is quite troublesome. But WeChat already has a great native platform, and we don't need much technical capability to implement it." Liu Jun introduced. Currently, besides voice-guided tours, the National Museum's WeChat Official Account can also query basic visiting information, push exhibition news, and introduce exhibits. Furthermore, it plans to implement free ticket reservations and location navigation services.

Similarly enthusiastic about embracing WeChat Official Accounts is the Credit Card Center of China Merchants Bank. Currently, the services available on China Merchants Bank's WeChat customer service platform include 79 items, accounting for 71% of the bank's total services, with over 90% of these handled through self-service by users. Compared to the China Merchants Bank credit card mobile app, the WeChat intelligent service platform has a chatbot that can provide many extended services, whereas the mobile app can only conduct fixed item queries.

Whether small startup teams or large companies, WeChat Official Accounts provide them with another choice beyond apps. They have lower technical thresholds, update more quickly, save human and time costs, and can realize certain functions that mobile apps cannot.

And for entities ranging from Toutiao Search to the National Museum to China Merchants Bank, the core reason they can harness the WeChat Official Platform to create enormous value is that they haven't simply treated WeChat as a chat tool, but rather as an I/O system, fundamentally relying on WeChat to address the relationship between information and people.

However, in Li Zhifei's view, not all services are suitable for presentation on WeChat.

He summarized several types of applications suitable for WeChat, including low-frequency needs such as checking hotels, flights, courier phone numbers, and public toilets. These are demands that occur every few weeks, months, or even longer. Downloading a specific app for such low-frequency needs is wasteful, while the advantage of WeChat accounts is that when not needed, they quietly remain hidden in your contact list without consuming any resources.

Another type may also be a high-frequency need, but with less complex user interactions, such as checking the weather. Functions requiring various sensors to achieve, such as utilizing gravity sensing or location information, are less suitable for WeChat.

"On WeChat, it's suitable for meeting fragmented needs, such as jokes, group buying, discounts, and movies," Li Zhifei has been expanding the features of "Toutiao Search" based on this point. According to him, the next addition will include recipe searching and listening to crosstalk comedy.

Counter-trend Apps

The reason these WeChat Official Accounts are so popular is, at the root, that they are easier to use than apps. Zhu Kun, Vice President of Express Hotel Butler, often recounts a personal experience to illustrate this point in various occasions.

Once, he took a high-speed train from Shanghai to Beijing and chatted with a seatmate on the train. The seatmate was also in Beijing on a business trip and had not yet booked a hotel. So Zhu introduced his own Express Hotel Butler. The seatmate searched for it in the App Store, intending to download and use it. However, the 3G signal on the high-speed train was poor, and after several attempts, it failed. At this point, Zhu remembered the WeChat Official Account. The seatmate searched for it, followed it immediately, completed the hotel reservation within it, and also shared it with his other colleagues.

Clearly, the acquisition cost of WeChat Official Accounts is lower, saving time, data, and hardware storage space. An app can be dozens of megabytes in size, and most people can only download it in Wi-Fi environments, but whether they remember to download it after reaching a Wi-Fi environment is uncertain.

Additionally, in WeChat, the usage scenarios and dissemination scenarios of applications overlap, making it easier for users to share and spread information among each other. Information originally confined within various apps can now flow freely in WeChat, breaking down the invisible walls of information barriers.

"Some people have asked me, if the information on 'Toutiao Search' is available elsewhere, why should they still use your product?" Li Zhifei believes that previously, to achieve each function required downloading corresponding apps, and each use necessitated opening the app. However, in WeChat, all these can be integrated into a single official account, providing a more convenient user experience.

Whether it's apps or WeChat Official Accounts, their essence is to provide services. For users, the form of the product doesn't matter as long as the service can be conveniently accessed. Therefore, things that previously required a dedicated app might no longer be necessary with the advent of WeChat Official Accounts.

For users, even if they have these apps, do they really have the time to open them? A person's time is limited. When people have already spent a lot of time on WeChat, the time left to open other apps decreases accordingly. Li Zhifei himself is a typical example, "I now spend 80% of my time on WeChat. In the past, I had all kinds of apps installed on my phone, but now 'Toutiao Search' meets most of my mobile needs."

In 2010, Chris Anderson, editor-in-chief of Wired magazine, published a famous article titled "The Web Is Dead, Long Live the Internet." He believed that although you're online, it's no longer through the web but via various apps. Indeed, since the birth of the App Store five years ago, it has changed the way we go online, seemingly unifying everything under apps.

However, looking back, from the perspective of information naturally seeking freedom of movement, apps have never and will never unify everything. Debates about the death of apps have never ceased since the day apps were born, and in fact, apps were a compromise and transitional phase from the day they were created.

In 2007, when Jobs released the first iPhone, it only had a few built-in native apps. At that time, Apple hoped developers would create web apps, but the technology back then wasn't sufficient to achieve good results, especially with the challenge of payment issues being hard to overcome. During this process, jailbreaking programs for the iPhone emerged, along with unauthorized native apps. Based on various considerations, Apple eventually released the iPhone software development kit (SDK) in the spring of 2008, leading to the subsequent App Store.

Today, with the maturity of HTML5 technology, various issues previously constrained by technology are expected to be resolved. Li Zhifei has always closely followed the development trends of web apps and native apps, believing that in the long run, the web will definitely become the mainstream. "The internet is attractive because it is an open platform that allows rapid updates and iterations. The current App Store is a counter-trend approach." And WeChat, in essence, is a web.

Why WeChat?

To understand how WeChat Official Accounts will evolve in the future, one must first understand what WeChat truly is.

In a sense, WeChat is essentially an I/O system. The WeChat Public Platform has openly stated that it is "not a marketing platform." The current public platform is not an App Store; it has no rankings, no recommendations, and no categories. Most of the official accounts on the platform belong to traditional enterprise accounts, whereas in the true App Store, products from internet companies, games, and applications dominate.

This determines the distinct application categories and gameplay on the WeChat Public Platform compared to the App Store. Banks, airlines, and lifestyle websites can use WeChat to build a lightweight customer service system. As a communication tool, WeChat's strongest attribute is the flow of information. Any mobile application that solves communication and information asymmetry can thrive on the WeChat Public Platform.

In a sense, today WeChat has become an alternative choice for businesses beyond apps, primarily because WeChat's real value lies in aggregating and connecting information. With its powerful ID system, it enables information to flow more effectively and forms a cycle, addressing the issue of information being isolated in independent apps under the App Store model, where information cannot flow smoothly.

In an interview with the Business Value magazine, WeChat's head Zhang Xiaolong said: "No matter how complex the world is, the core of communication is solving the relationship between the object and information." Abstractly speaking, the object corresponds to the ID. Information only flows around the ID rather than being encapsulated within apps to generate greater value.

This is also why the app model has been widely criticized in recent years. A user's phone may have dozens of apps installed, but these apps are separated by high walls. Sometimes, to make a decision, a user needs to open several apps at the same time. This predicament explains why IFTTT, which connects different apps, has become so popular.

From this perspective, WeChat Official Accounts are essentially an enhanced version of IFTTT. Their strength lies in the ID system. Around the ID, information transitions from isolation to free-flowing, and WeChat plays the role of a hub in this flowing river of information.

In fact, a crucial hallmark of Apple's ability to build an ecosystem is its ID system. However, Apple IDs are mostly used as tickets for downloading applications. When Apple's platform generates tens of thousands of applications, which in turn create billions of pieces of information, the information remains isolated within respective apps. Apple IDs do not play a significant role in facilitating the flow and aggregation of information.

This is precisely WeChat's opportunity. With its 400 million users, this giant has transformed from an app into a platform. The greatest value of the ID system lies in clearing the road amidst the complex and chaotic sea of information, removing obstacles, and enabling smooth information flow.

The ID system makes WeChat a cost-effective best choice for some enterprises during the transition from apps to web in the mobile internet era. Of course, beneath the iceberg, a larger iceberg is gradually emerging. This giant also possesses a powerful ID system and has the potential to become a new information transfer hub in the future web app era.

Over the past three years, while Apple has been at its peak, Google has been promoting the global普及and cross-platformization of Chrome, even launching Chrome OS laptops. Behind this is an ecosystem centered around web apps. Using a unified Google ID, future web-based freely flowing information can be efficiently integrated, which is also Google's mission. Only with the premise of fully efficient information flow can future mobile search be highly valuable.

We are no longer in the pioneering era of mobile internet information scarcity. When enough information is generated, the value of IDs that connect people and information and enable information to flow efficiently around people becomes increasingly significant. This is the core reason why Apple in the past, WeChat in the present, and Google in the future can stand at the pinnacle of the industry.

Without service to people, no amount of information has any value. Whether it is the current software-level ID-ification or the future device-level ID-ification, it corresponds to a huge opportunity behind — efficiently connecting people and information.
