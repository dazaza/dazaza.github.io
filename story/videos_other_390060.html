In essence, your product targets which customer segment? Do they prefer branded items? Are they price-sensitive? Do they favor fashionable and trendy items? Is there a specific age group? Simply put, the product positioning of the store involves pricing and style. Regardless of the type of product you sell, just search the major category on Taobao's homepage, and you'll find at least ten thousand stores selling similar items. In the same market, others are seasoned veterans, while you're a newcomer. To carve out a niche among so many competitors, your store must have its own unique features and style. Of course, there are other conditions, such as promotion, service, integrity, etc., which we won't discuss here. Today, we mainly focus on store positioning.

How to determine the store's positioning and style, please first examine the characteristics of your inventory source.

If your inventory source is directly from a factory, generally speaking, you shouldn't worry about price issues. Therefore, your positioning can primarily focus on factory goods, with the factory goods' style being your store's style.

If you source from a wholesale market, this depends on the overall level of the market. In some big cities, such as Guangzhou, Shanghai, Beijing, etc., the wholesale market offers a wide range of choices, and the goods are relatively new. If you have a good eye, you can position yourself toward fresh, fashion-forward, and trendy individuals. This group values the speed of product updates and how well the products align with fashion trends, allowing you to set slightly higher prices.

However, if the goods from the wholesale market lack distinct features, it's best to set your prices low and win by price advantage, targeting specific demographics. For instance, some stores specialize in elderly clothing, maternity wear, or student attire, forming stable markets.

There are many ways to define your store's style. In summary, can you summarize the common traits of your store's products with one or two keywords? If your current store can't achieve this, it indicates unclear positioning. In such cases, you must calm down and adjust accordingly.

Why should new stores have clear positioning?

For new stores, product positioning must be clear because typically, new store owners have limited funds. If the product positioning spans too widely, it reduces the selection for various consumer preferences. For example, if I prefer buying branded items, upon entering your store, I initially see over a hundred items and feel pleased. But after browsing two pages, I find only a third or fewer suit my needs. In this case, I won't waste more time choosing in your store. I'd rather go to stores that exclusively sell branded items to shorten my search time.

I deeply understand this point. Below, I share my personal experience to illustrate how crucial store positioning is. My store specializes in foreign trade women's clothing. In the first three months after opening, overall sales were better than expected. Generally, even during the worst times, sales occurred every two to three days, and each sale usually involved more than one item. Since my sourcing channels were relatively fixed, all foreign trade products with price advantages, the number of people collecting my store kept increasing. Within four months, I had risen to three hearts, which made me very happy. Simultaneously, since my products were mainly knitwear, I wasn't confident about summer sales, so I began actively searching for new sources to diversify the product range and attract more varied customers. Due to different sources, the product price positioning was higher than before, but due to limited funds, I diverted part of the original channel's funding. Consequently, my store's products ranged from 9 yuan to over 400 yuan, but the variety within each price range wasn't extensive. A significant change occurred. From the second half of April, the click-through rate remained unchanged, but sales suddenly disappeared. Later, the click-through rate also dropped, and transactions ceased entirely, and the number of people collecting my store basically stopped increasing. I was very puzzled and kept looking for reasons. Initially, I thought the pictures weren't professional enough, so I bought a professional photo studio and increased participation in forums, which helped improve the click-through rate to some extent, but sales didn't improve. Later, a few friends' words helped me identify the problem. They said my recent items seemed mixed, prompting me to recall the importance of product positioning. Carefully reviewing the situation when sales were good, I realized the product positioning was clear, mainly focusing on cost-effective foreign trade products priced mostly below 40 yuan. Later, it became disordered, and the decline in sales started precisely then. I immediately adjusted the product structure, reallocating most of the working capital back to the original channel. A week after the new products were listed, both click-through rates and sales showed signs of improvement, and the number of people collecting my store began to increase again. I sighed in relief!

Based on my experience, the major product categories for new stores should not exceed two types; more would create chaos. Meanwhile, the price range for each major category should ideally not exceed 150 yuan, maintaining clarity. Of course, once you develop well and stabilize sales, enriching the product range is entirely acceptable, ensuring steady sales.

For beginners, the growth of a store isn't merely a simple accumulation of credit and increased sales but requires continuous exploration, summarization, and adjustment. During slow business periods, beginner sellers should carefully analyze the reasons and seek solutions. As long as you are dedicated, success is certain to come.
