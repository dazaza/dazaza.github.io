China Network Beijing, December 20th - In the afternoon of December 20th, a media meeting regarding the Siemens refrigerator door incident was held at the Haidian Theatre in Beijing. Luo Yonghao, founder of Laolu English Training, fulfilled his previous promise by smashing Siemens refrigerators again to protect consumer rights. (Click on the picture to enter Luo Yonghao's Weibo account.)

Before the meeting, Luo Yonghao's team had already transported nearly 20 different models of Siemens refrigerators to the theater stage. After the audience confirmed that the refrigerator doors indeed could not be closed, reporters and netizen representatives immediately smashed them with hammers. It is reported that these included problematic refrigerators contributed by consumers such as Han Han, as well as Siemens refrigerators newly purchased by Luo Yonghao and sample units.

At the meeting, Luo Yonghao expressed dissatisfaction with Siemens' apology for acknowledging weakness but not admitting fault, hoping that Siemens would address three questions: which models of refrigerators have doors that cannot close, which refrigerators can install door closers, and how to inform users who do not use the internet.

In addition to continuing to focus on the refrigerator door incident, Luo Yonghao also stated that the next step would involve protecting rights regarding the issue of water accumulation in the refrigerator's cooling compartment.

News Recap:

Luo Yonghao smashes fridges to protect rights at Siemens Beijing headquarters

On November 20, Luo Yonghao, a former famous English trainer at New Oriental and founder of Laolu English Training, conducted a rights protection activity at Siemens headquarters in Beijing. He used an iron hammer to smash three fridges with quality issues or design defects, submitting a written application urging Siemens to immediately correct their refusal to acknowledge product problems, shirking responsibility, and ignoring consumer demands, and to recall defective fridges.

Luo Yonghao had already announced on Weibo on the 17th that he would hold the previously promised media communication meeting. At 2 PM yesterday, the black screen at the Haidian Theatre in Beijing only displayed the eleven words: "Exchange Meeting on the Siemens Refrigerator Door Incident." There were several dozen fridges, several dozen hammers, three cameras, dozens of journalists, and five to six hundred spectators. Luo Yonghao demonstrated opening and closing the fridge doors on-site to prove that the fridge doors without door closers could not be shut.

Afterwards, Luo Yonghao started smashing! With a swing of the hammer, about 20 Siemens fridges, including Han Han's gray fridge, were smashed. Luo Yonghao also told the onlookers that if they wished, they could take the hammers home as souvenirs. This would be our last time smashing fridges, though I will continue to pursue Siemens through various means.

At around 5 PM yesterday, Luo Yonghao updated his first Weibo post in four days, explaining why he hadn't sought help from the Consumer Association and Quality Supervision Department to resolve the issue of the Siemens fridge doors. He also attached a blog post link where his friend David Xu communicated with the Consumer Association and Quality Supervision Department as a consumer. The blog post included 19 audio recordings totaling 94 minutes. From the recordings, it appeared that David Xu communicated with multiple departments, including the Beijing Consumer Association and the Beijing Technical Supervision Bureau. The 19 audio recordings showed that finding the appropriate department to test the fridge was not easy. Tencent netizens commented extensively. One user named Ji Si Liu described it thusly: "For 90 minutes, 19 people kept passing the ball!"

Luo Yonghao said: "In my personal experience of rights protection, I've had two experiences dealing with the Consumer Association, industry and commerce, and quality supervision departments, but each time has ended in humiliation. So this time, I chose not to bother them or myself." This time, he responded to external criticism of his irrationality by showcasing these 19 audio recordings.

Yesterday, Luo Yonghao told reporters that he believed Siemens' apology lacked sincerity. Simultaneously, Luo Yonghao revealed that the next step would involve focusing on a new problem with Siemens fridges: water accumulation in the cooling compartment.
