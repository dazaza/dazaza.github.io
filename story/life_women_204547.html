Not all women can get pregnant easily.

by yunfu8 on 2009-07-17 16:50:46

Female friends who are preparing to get pregnant should first learn about the relevant knowledge and content related to pregnancy, study common health care knowledge for pregnant women, etc. The ideal age for women to conceive is 25-30 years old, with 25-26 years old being the best age for conception. Getting pregnant too early can easily lead to cervical cancer; getting pregnant too late increases the likelihood of congenital defects, malformations in the fetus, and also increases the rate of difficult labor. Are you ready to get pregnant? First, take a look at the essential pre-pregnancy guide: conception is best suited to certain seasons. It is generally recommended to conceive in spring, specifically during the months of March, April, and May. Conceiving during this time allows the fetus's brain cells to develop rapidly around the third month, which is when nutrition is most needed—this coincides with the summer months of June-August, when the market offers an abundant supply of vegetables, fruits, eggs, and meat, fully meeting the needs of pregnant women. Moreover, the climate from March-May is stable, making it less likely for pregnant women to catch colds or other illnesses.

If the woman preparing to get pregnant works closely with radiation, lead, mercury, gasoline, paint, carbon disulfide, organophosphorus pesticides, or anesthetics, she should be reassigned from these work positions or environments that are harmful to the fetus to avoid causing chromosomal mutations in sperm and eggs.

An important aspect to know when preparing for pregnancy is that women are more prone to dental complications during pregnancy, such as toothaches and gum bleeding. If we pay attention to this point and treat dental problems before pregnancy, we can avoid suffering from dental issues during pregnancy. Hormonal changes during pregnancy are more likely to induce "pregnancy gingivitis." Increased levels of progesterone and estrogen may cause the gums to react differently to bacterial stimulation in dental plaque, resulting in swelling, bleeding, and pain. Women who focus on dental health before pregnancy can effectively reduce the risk of developing dental problems during pregnancy. Therefore, it is recommended to undergo a comprehensive oral examination and necessary treatment at the dental clinic within six months before pregnancy. Teeth should not be extracted during the first three months and the last three months of pregnancy. Extracting teeth in the early stages of pregnancy may induce miscarriage, while extracting teeth in the later stages of pregnancy may lead to premature birth. Therefore, if your teeth are in poor condition or you have dental problems, it is advisable to cure them promptly before becoming pregnant to avoid trouble during pregnancy.

Pregnant women preparing for pregnancy should pay attention to diabetes. If a pregnant woman has diabetes, it can cause miscarriage, premature birth, and sometimes fetal death in utero. Additionally, the rates of giving birth to large babies and malformed babies will increase. Based on the severity of the disease in pregnant women preparing for pregnancy, consult a doctor to determine whether pregnancy is possible. Hypertension. Hypertensive patients are prone to preeclampsia, and it can become severe. If you are unclear about your blood pressure values but experience symptoms such as severe headaches, shoulder pain, insomnia, dizziness, and swelling, you should go to the hospital for a check-up. Liver diseases. During pregnancy, the liver burden increases; if there are liver diseases, it can worsen the condition of the liver. If the condition is severe, the pregnancy should be terminated; if the condition is not serious, the pregnancy can continue under the guidance of a doctor.
