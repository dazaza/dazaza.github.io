What if you're the one writing the letter???

by lk0cswp on 2009-08-18 06:33:56

When I was writing this letter to you, I have struggled in my mind for a long time, but I still want to tell you, otherwise I will feel sorry for your kindness to me. On the day you went to the hospital to pick me up, it was also the moment when I just learned that the doctor called me and told me the result of this morning's blood test, which showed that I had a sexually transmitted disease, syphilis. After hearing this, I was stunned. I asked the doctor if I didn't have pelvic inflammatory disease, how did it become a sexually transmitted disease? I asked the doctor if he made a mistake with the results. The doctor told me to check at another hospital. On the short walk back to the ward, I almost lost my mind. I couldn't believe it could be me. It must be a mistake, it must be. But then I thought, if I really have it, who passed it on to me? The first person I thought of was you. But after being with you for so long, your serious attitude and honesty made me immediately dismiss this thought. When I saw you, I realized that my face had already turned pale, but I didn't tell you because I was afraid to scare you. I also couldn't believe whether it was true or not. After returning home, I didn't dare to tell Mom, so I went to a certain hospital with my younger sister to get checked again, but the result was the same. I still doubted it. Was it because I said to check for sexually transmitted diseases when I went there, and they used only half an hour more to come out with the result? The next day, I went to an authoritative hospital that others recommended, saying that the result would come out the next day. During this day, I was very sad and anxious. When the doctor said that this disease has an incubation period, I thought of my ex-boyfriend, because I know clearly how many men I've been with without using protection. If I like to play around, I have no complaints, and I don't blame anyone. But at this point, I think I don't need to lie anymore. When I called him, I told him everything, but his attitude was very bad. He said it couldn't be him, even if he had the disease, it wouldn't be that serious. He said I might have caught it from someone else. I was so angry, he hung up before I finished speaking. Then I sent him a text message, asking him not for money, but just wanting to know if he is aware of his own physical condition. If he knew everything clearly, why did he harm me? But he didn't even reply to me. What makes me angry is that I ever followed such a person. Does that mean tacit consent? I will know the result in the afternoon. If it really is positive, if I really have it, then I think no matter how many times I say sorry to you, it won't be useful. The last thing I can do for you is to be honest. Concealing it can only bring selfish benefits to myself, but if you really pass it on, what was originally an easily treatable disease will become serious. I think the harm I caused you, even if I die, I can't make up for it. I can't forget your kindness to me, and I don't want to forget every beautiful moment we spent together. Writing all this is not to gain your sympathy, but because I clearly know that this will be my last chance to confess to you.
