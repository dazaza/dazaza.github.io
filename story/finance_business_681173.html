by xue94fwsh on 2012-02-28 11:48:32

The straw was filled with lifelike dough figures: there are traditional historical characters such as Jiang Taigong, and also modern cartoon characters like Mr. Grey Wolf, covering a wide range. The famous Xie Fusun powder shop! Yangzhou has produced beauties since ancient times, and Yangzhou has always enjoyed the reputation of "the best powder in the world is no comparison to Yangzhou". Known as the "first makeup of China", Xie Fusun's most classic product should be the duck egg powder first created in the Daoguang period of the Qing Dynasty. There is a saying that goes "Suzhou rouge and Yangzhou powder", these two treasures were taken back to the palace after Emperor Kangxi's southern tour, and soon became popular. In the literary masterpiece "Dream of the Red Chamber", Cao Xueqin also highly praised the Yangzhou powder! Maltose candy is delicious, it's many people's childhood memory. But now the art of "blowing sugar figurines" is rarely seen. In a food store on Dongguan Street, this folk artisan is selling his variously shaped works, but only those made with a slotted spoon can be seen, not those blown with a glass tube... Most adults and children passing by would stop to watch, even if they don't buy, just watching is quite satisfying.

According to the plan, we will leave Yangzhou for Mount Lushan in Jiangxi in the evening, and there is still plenty of time left in the afternoon. There are several very distinctive gardens in Yangzhou city, such as Ge Garden, He Garden, Wang's Small Courtyard, and Wu DaoTai Mansion etc. These gardens come from different backgrounds, some are official and some are salt merchants. The significant characteristic of Yangzhou-style gardens is their exquisite elegance, and their rockery landscapes are the best in the world! The entrance of Ge Garden is right on Dongguan Street, so choose it! Let's see what the difference is compared to Suzhou gardens...

Continuing forward... On Dongguan Street, there are several old-established shops of the three knives of Yangzhou. These three knives are kitchen knife, foot-care knife, and hairdressing knife, which have greatly promoted the social and cultural development of Yangzhou, and were once closely related to the lives of ordinary people. However, each old-established shop's name is called "Yangzhou Three Knives", is this a brand? Or a general term? This can't help but make one confused...

Dongguan Street in Yangzhou, has a simple background: historically, this place was the water and land transportation hub of Yangzhou City, and also the commercial and cultural center of Yangzhou; the canal culture and salt merchant culture converge here, radiating westward from the canal dock on the east side, making Dongguan Street the gateway to the "Southeast First Business Port" of Yangzhou...

A promotional poster for the "March Smoke International Economic and Trade Tourism Festival" on Dongguan Street finally made me understand why there are so many tourists in Yangzhou today... Why I couldn't book the Green Yang Hotel, which opened in the early Republic of China...

Steamed crab meat lion's head! Comparing this large, clearly textured meatball to a "lion's head" is indeed quite apt. Before it even reaches your mouth, your taste buds have already been conquered by the aroma of the meat, crab, and vegetables!

Faced with such dense crowds just now, I had already foreseen that today is not suitable for garden sightseeing. As expected: entering the Huang family garden, or the true meaning of Ge Garden, an unimaginable number of tourists has already ruined the beauty of the garden. Fortunately, tourists cannot step onto the artificial mountains and lake surfaces, otherwise, the garden would have completely fallen...

On April 18, 2009, Saturday, Yangzhou fried rice is a culinary calling card of Yangzhou, restaurants across the country love to use this name for their egg fried rice. The taste of the Yangzhou fried rice here is good, but it seems not to be the most authentic method, it appears that one should go to the Yechun Tea House where morning tea is served to eat fried rice...

There are too many beautiful and fun things on Dongguan Street, it was only after two o'clock that I realized I hadn't eaten yet. Just then, there happened to be a Yangzhou restaurant, went in to try Huaiyang cuisine! Although this is an ordinary restaurant, the interior decoration and tableware show that the owner follows the traditional route!

Yangzhou paper-cutting was also a popular skill during the Tang Dynasty, and now similarly faces the awkward situation of a lack of successors... The main material for paper-cutting is Anhui handmade xuan paper, and achieving the effect of "round as autumn moon, sharp as wheat awn, square as blue brick, jagged as saw teeth, line as beard" requires years of practice!

The stone stacking and mountain building skills of Ge Garden are indeed breathtaking. Starting from the central spring scenery, all the way to the summer, autumn, winter artificial mountain clusters at the back, a large amount of stones collected from all over have been used. Some are natural strange rocks, some have been artificially carved. The stacked rocks are varied! Water flows through the stone caves, ancient trees and pavilions echo each other on various peaks. Placed among the artificial mountains, one feels the undulating terrain and winding paths...

The long Qing brick walls in front of Chang Le Inn stretch over a hundred meters, and today they are holding an opening ceremony. The musicians who were playing music have just dispersed, but the flowers around the gate remain in full bloom. Chang Le Inn is a hotel themed around traditional Yangzhou residential culture, its large scale is eye-catching: several historical gardens of the old city of Yangzhou, Li Chang Le's former residence, Yi Pu, and Hua Family Garden, have all been incorporated into the territory of this "inn". The "boss" of the inn seems to have strong connections! And great ability!

Next to the "blowing sugar figurines" is "pinching dough figurines", another ancient folk art. The main ingredients for dough figurines are glutinous rice flour and tung oil, mixed well and repeatedly kneaded, the dough is not only soft but also highly malleable, allowing it to be pinched into various shapes of characters, decorated with colors and appropriate carving techniques to complete the work.

Stir-fried dry silk threads, a famous Huaiyang dish. Unlike the generally sweet and greasy dishes of Jiangsu and Zhejiang cuisines, this dish emphasizes meticulous preparation and timing. Dry silk threads, chicken silk, black fungus, shrimp, ham slices, broad bean slices, and bamboo shoots are cooked in chicken broth to produce a fragrant delicacy. The taste can be summed up in one word: fresh!

Among Yangzhou's local handicrafts, lacquer ware ranks as one of the best. The lacquer ware displayed in this shop has an elegant style and exquisite craftsmanship, with joints so tight that when you touch it, you almost don't want to put it down.

Exiting the north gate of Ge Garden leads straight to Yanfu West Street... diagonally opposite happens to be where we parked our car. Farewell Yangzhou, next stop Mount Lu...

Ge Garden was built by the Huang family, who were salt merchants in charge of the Two Huai Salt Industry during the Qing Dynasty. Named after the owner's love for bamboo, whose character "bamboo" resembles the shape of "ge", hence the name Ge Garden. Entering the main gate, first comes the house then the garden. Travel groups are everywhere, already filling every passage of the Huang residence, the inside of the house wasn't carefully examined, and was again pushed towards the garden entrance by waves of people.

China's lacquer ware craft dates back to the Xia Dynasty, and there are many types of lacquer ware. However, due to the extremely complicated materials and procedures, the number of excellent lacquer artisans is decreasing day by day... Nowadays, society can only rely on industrial mass production to meet the huge demand brought about by rapid population growth. How should such traditional Chinese handicrafts be inherited and developed? Where exactly lies the way out?

Different lighting angles cause the light to be bright and dim. The Taihu stones of the Summer Mountain, the Yellow Mountain stones of the Autumn Mountain, and the Anhui Xuan stones of the Winter Mountain reflect different colors. The changes in color further give visitors the feeling of changing seasons.

The visit to Ge Garden can be summarized as coming and going in a hurry, following the dense crowd to the northern exit. Along the way are antique shops, selling various souvenirs, none of which caught my interest. Instead, on this square stage, a wonderful Yangzhou opera is being performed. Looking around, the elderly sitting in the front row, attentively watching the performance, I think they are the most knowledgeable audience about the Yangzhou opera stage...

The status of Huaiyang cuisine in China far surpasses the neighboring Hangzhou and Suzhou cuisines... Not tasting the famous old-established Fumanlou in Yangzhou this time was a mistake. Therefore, in an ordinary restaurant, one must order the most famous Huaiyang dishes, the chef's repeated preparation should result in flavors that are more or less the same.
