On the 25th, the quotes for the three major components: memory prices fall while CPU prices continue to rise.

by anonymous on 2012-03-03 11:37:10

Yesterday, the EU conditionally approved Western Digital's acquisition of Hitachi's hard drive business, on the condition that WD sells its 3.5-inch hard drive factory. This might mark the end of Hitachi's hard drives, and there are rumors of another hard drive price increase. The good news is that memory prices have changed direction and are back in the declining price range.

Today in Beijing’s Zhongguancun spot market for Intel processors, Intel prices have shown slight fluctuations with a weakening upward trend. The entry-level G530 increased by 10 yuan, now quoted at 270 yuan; the i5 2310 increased by 20 yuan, now quoted at 1120 yuan. Additionally, three popular LGA775 processors saw small increases ranging from 5 to 20 yuan.

Today in Beijing’s Zhongguancun AMD processor spot market, AMD processor prices continued to rise. The old 45nm Athlon remains the main driver of price increases, after a brief period of price drops, prices are once again high. Today's increase ranges between 5-15 yuan, with most being 5-10 yuan.

Today in Beijing’s Zhongguancun desktop memory spot market, memory prices turned downward. Kingston led the price drop, which was unexpected. Among them, the 2GB/1333 memory had a significant decline, with prices inverted compared to Hong Kong's general distributor; the 4GB/1333 memory had a smaller decline. During the price increase period, Apacer/Adata 4GB memory did not follow the trend, indirectly forcing Kingston to lower its prices.

Today in Beijing’s Zhongguancun notebook memory spot market, notebook memory prices remained unchanged. Recent fluctuations in memory prices have not affected notebook memory prices. Kingston's 4GB/1333 notebook memory is quoted at 115 yuan. Adata/Apacer's 4GB/1333 notebook memory is quoted between 112-113 yuan.

Today in Beijing’s Zhongguancun desktop hard drive spot market, hard drive prices are facing changes, potentially leading to an increase, specifically related to the Western Digital acquisition case. With early stockpilers gradually selling off their hard drives, the week-long slight decline in hard drive prices has been curbed, with hard drive prices gradually aligning with Hong Kong's general distributor, returning pricing power to Hong Kong's distributor.

Today in Beijing’s Zhongguancun notebook hard drive spot market, Seagate's 500GB 5400rpm/7200rpm notebook hard drives fell by 10 yuan and 40 yuan respectively, now quoted at 520 yuan and 610 yuan. Hitachi notebook hard drive prices remained unchanged.

Today in Beijing’s Zhongguancun Intel processor spot market, Intel prices showed slight fluctuations with a weakening upward trend. The entry-level G530 increased by 10 yuan, now quoted at 270 yuan; the i5 2310 increased by 20 yuan, now quoted at 1120 yuan. Additionally, three popular LGA775 processors saw small increases ranging from 5 to 20 yuan.
