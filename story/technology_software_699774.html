**Spyker**

Country: Netherlands

Brand Establishment Year: 1875

Brand Style: A perfect blend of magnificent art and technology, luxurious and timeless, unconventional

Classic Model: C8 Spyder (starting at 48.8 million yuan)

Spyker, as a factory with over a hundred years of history, has always maintained its unique brand style. All their sports cars are handmade, featuring a unique lightweight body, using large-displacement mid-mounted engines, rear-wheel drive, and many specifications adhere to F1 standards, offering astonishing performance parameters. In 1898, the Spijker brothers Jacobus and Hendrik-Jan manufactured their first car in Amsterdam, powered by a Mercedes engine, and immediately gained acclaim for their outstanding craftsmanship. The same year, to celebrate the upcoming coronation of Queen Wilhelmina of the Netherlands, they created the famous Golden Coronation Carriage, which is still in use today. From that point on, the Spijker brothers entered a turning point in their careers: fully dedicating Spyker Company to automobile manufacturing. To better adapt to foreign markets, they changed the "ij" in Spijker to "y", thus renaming the company Spyker. As a renowned luxury sports car manufacturer, Spyker's noble sports cars are entirely custom-made according to customer needs. In 2005, Spyker increased production to around 100 cars per year. Not only do we produce for a limited number of esteemed owners, but each model is also an absolute limited edition collectible. Spyker launches a new car every year, and when global production reaches 500 units, production stops immediately. Spyker targets the highest-end users in the sports car market, with customers mainly concentrated in the United States, Western Europe, the Middle East, Greater China, and smaller affluent countries such as Switzerland and Monaco.

**Rolls-Royce**

Country: United Kingdom

Brand Establishment Year: 1904

Brand Style: Established in 1904

Classic Model: Phantom (starting at 50 million yuan)

Mr. Rolls was an aristocrat and a racer, sociable and widely connected, who always wanted to produce a truly British car. Mr. Royce was an outstanding engineer, multi-talented, and very interested in Rolls' plan, leading to their partnership in producing famous British cars. The first Rolls-Royce car was born in Manchester, marking the beginning of their illustrious career. In 1906, Charles Rolls and Henry Royce co-founded Rolls-Royce. Initially, Rolls-Royce engaged in aircraft engine manufacturing, with automobiles being a subsequent product; they continue to produce aircraft engines today. The sculptural logo of Rolls-Royce is a gleaming silver flying goddess statue, known as the "Flying Goddess," symbolizing the soul of speed. Its origin dates back to 1911 when, through the introduction of Sir Montagu, Rolls met Charles Sykes, a painter and sculptor for The Motor magazine. He commissioned Charles Sykes to design a sculptural logo for "Rolls-Royce." Sykes used Miss Thornton from the magazine office as the model to design the "Flying Goddess," representing the spirit of speed. On February 6, 1911, the "Flying Goddess" descended upon Rolls-Royce, immersing the entire world in fresh air and the beautiful music melody of fluttering wings. The inauguration ceremony for the "Flying Goddess" was as grand and enthusiastic as the launch of the first Rolls-Royce car. The emblem of Rolls-Royce features two overlapping "R"s, symbolizing mutual integration and reflecting the harmonious relationship between the two founders.

**Bugatti**

Country: France

Brand Establishment Year: 1909

Brand Style: A perfect combination of technology and art, luxurious comfort, easy to drive

Classic Models: Brescia, Royale, Atalantes, Pur Sang

Bugatti is a world-famous old sports car brand established in 1909 by Italian Ettore Bugatti in Germany, specializing in producing sports cars and high-end luxury sedans. Due to changes in the geographical location of the factory, it was located in Germany from 1909-1918, in France from 1919-1956, and returned to Italy after being re-established in 1991. The English letters in the Bugatti logo represent Bugatti, with EB being the abbreviation of Ettore Bugatti's name in English, surrounded by small dots symbolizing ball bearings, set against a red background. Bugatti cars are among the most preserved classic old cars, with various models visible in multiple famous car museums worldwide. They are superior in performance, feature novel and streamlined body designs, and have distinctive engine configurations.

**Maserati**

Country: Italy

Brand Establishment Year: 1914

Brand Style: Dignified elegance without ostentation, extreme sports enjoyment with comfortable driving pleasure

Classic Models: Quattroporte, GranTurismo (starting at 17.9 million yuan)

Maserati is a well-known Italian racing and sports car manufacturer founded in 1914 in Bologna. The company's headquarters is now located in Modena, and their emblem is a trident. Maserati was once part of Ferrari (a company majority-owned by Fiat), and it is now directly owned by the Italian automotive giant Fiat. The Maserati brothers—Alfieri Maserati, Bindo Maserati, Carlo Maserati, Ettore Maserati, Ernesto Maserati, and Mario Maserati—all became involved in automobiles in the early 20th century. Alfieri, Bindo, and Ernesto produced 2-liter Grand Prix race cars for Diatto. In 1926, when Diatto ceased production of race cars, it led to the creation of the first Maserati and the establishment of the Maserati model line. The first Maserati, driven by Alfieri, won the Targa Florio in 1926. Maserati began producing racing cars with 4, 6, 8, and 16 valves (essentially two straight-eight valves mounted along another). Mario, an artist, is credited with designing the company's emblem: a trident. Alfieri Maserati passed away in 1932, but his three brothers continued running the company, producing race-winning vehicles.

**Aston Martin**

Country: United Kingdom

Brand Establishment Year: 1914

Brand Style: Unique styling, exquisite craftsmanship, superior performance

Classic Model: DBS (starting at 41 million yuan)

Aston Martin was originally a British luxury sedan and sports car manufacturer established in 1913 by Lionel Martin and Robert Bamford. The company is located in Newport, renowned worldwide for producing convertible touring cars, racing cars, and limited-production sports cars. Participating in races is indeed an important means of developing car production, but due to excessive costs and poor management, it was acquired by Ford Motor Company in 1987. The Aston Martin logo features a soaring eagle with the English words Aston and Martin inscribed, symbolizing the company's speed and lofty ambitions like an eagle descending from the sky. The inclusion of both Aston and Martin signifies a "three-way alliance" car company. The prestigious reputation of Aston Martin Lagonda owes much to this eagle's good fortune. Mentioning Aston Martin brings to mind the legendary stories of James Bond, seemingly attributing its success to him.

**Bentley**

Country: United Kingdom

Brand Establishment Year: 1919

Brand Style: Outstanding driving experience, combining traditional handcrafted skills with powerful sports performance

Classic Models: Arnage 728 (starting at 99.8 million yuan), Arnage RL (starting at 54.8 million yuan), Continental GT (starting at 30.8 million yuan), Continental Flying (starting at 29.8 million yuan)

The Bentley company was founded by two brothers and was initially translated as "Ben Tian Li" before officially adopting "Bentley" when entering China in 2001. Walter Owen Bentley served as an apprentice in the railway industry before moving into the automotive sector. In 1912, he and his brother Horace Milner founded Bentley Motors in London, selling French DFP cars. He improved these cars by replacing the pistons with aluminum ones, enhancing their performance. During World War I, he worked for the Royal Naval Air Service Technical Committee, improving the French Clerget engine, resulting in the renowned BR1 and BR2 aviation engines. In 1919, W.O. Bentley resumed sales of the DFP car and launched the Bentley 3.0, named after himself. This 3-liter engine car, producing 85 horsepower and capable of speeds above 80 mph, dominated numerous races, winning five consecutive championships at Le Mans in France in 1924, 1927, 1928, 1929, and 1930. This marked Bentley's journey into professional design of high-end sports cars and racing cars. After leaving in 1935, he joined Lagonda Automobiles, where he worked for 11 years until his death in 1971. The Bentley car logo features the first letter of the company name, "B," adorned with wings, resembling a soaring eagle, a symbol that has been retained to this day. Previously, it featured a flying "B."

**Maybach**

Country: Germany

Brand Establishment Year: 1921

Brand Style: Noble elegance, ultimate personality, perfectionism

Classic Models: Maybach 57S (73.1 million yuan), 62S (83 million yuan)

Daimler-Chrysler Group announced in New York that the German ultra-luxury car brand Maybach, dormant for 60 years, had officially revived. Two models were introduced initially: the Maybach 57 and Maybach 62, produced by Mercedes-Benz, priced at 310,000 euros and 360,000 euros respectively. These prices are astonishing, equivalent to approximately 3.5 times the price of a Mercedes S600 or 2.5 times that of a Ferrari 360. Even the renowned Rolls-Royce and Bentley can only match the top-tier products of Maybach 62. Some define the resurrected Maybach as "Germany's Rolls-Royce." While this statement makes sense, it is not entirely accurate. Rolls-Royce, a pride of the British automotive industry, will soon become "thoroughly German" under the BMW Group. Another ultra-luxury brand, Bentley, has also joined the Volkswagen Group. Currently, BMW Group is busy updating Rolls-Royce and may introduce a new model in the coming years. Against this backdrop, Daimler's decision to launch Maybach cars appears to be a strategic move, positioning Maybach as a sharp weapon in the ultra-luxury car market to prevent BMW from monopolizing it. Compared to Rolls-Royce's "angel" logo, Maybach's double-M logo is much simpler. Initially, the double-M represented "Maybach cars," but now it signifies "Made by Maybach." The Maybach logo consists of two intersecting Ms encircling a spherical triangle, reviving memories of its legendary cars and era through brand representation: alongside Mercedes models, becoming an absolute classic among German automakers. Each car is independently crafted according to user specifications, with engines and chassis entirely handmade.

**Ferrari**

Country: Italy

Brand Establishment Year: 1947

Brand Style: Speed and passion, innovation and courage

Classic Models: F430 (27 million yuan), F430 Spider (32 million yuan), 599 GTB (43.5 million yuan), 612 Scaglietti (43.8 million yuan)

Ferrari is an Italian car manufacturer primarily producing Formula One racing cars, race cars, and high-performance sports cars, founded in 1929 by Enzo Ferrari. Initially, Ferrari sponsored drivers and produced racing cars, transitioning to independent car production in 1946, eventually growing to its current scale. It is now owned by Fiat Automobiles Group, headquartered in Maranello near Modena, Italy. Italian cars are referred to as "two kings and one queen": the two kings are "Ferrari" and "Lamborghini," while the queen is "Maserati." Enzo Ferrari, founder of Ferrari Automobiles, said his favorite racing car was one he hadn't yet built, and his greatest success was one he hadn't yet achieved. Known as the "Father of Racing," the Italian man's passion for cars boiled in his blood from a young age. When he was 13, he convinced his father to allow him to begin driving alone. The roar of engines on the racetrack, the excitement and thrill of competition, made him stronger with each battle. At the time, he raced Alfa Romeos, achieving numerous victories and earning the nickname "Knight" from his teammates. He progressed from participating in races to forming a racing club, ultimately founding his own car company. Today, Ferrari is known as the "Red Lightning." The shield-shaped Ferrari logo features a prancing black horse, with the background color matching the feathers of the Modena goldfinch. The prancing horse originates from Modena, the birthplace of Ferrari's founder Enzo Ferrari, and carries heroic legends. During World War I, Italian pilot Francesco Baracca first adopted this symbol as his personal emblem, painting it on his aircraft. After the war, Baracca's parents entrusted this prancing horse emblem to Enzo Ferrari, who placed it on a yellow shield as the team's symbol. Later, following Baracca's brave demise, the horse was changed to black, with the Italian flag added above it as a tribute.

**Porsche**

Country: Germany

Brand Establishment Year: 1948

Brand Style: Ultimate daily-use sports car

Classic Model: Porsche 911 Turbo (19.5 million yuan)

The founder of Porsche, Ferdinand Porsche, invented the electric wheel hub motor at the age of 24 (in 1899). At the Paris International Exhibition the following year, Porsche had already gained international fame. Porsche cars have distinct characteristics: a Beetle-like shape, rear-mounted engines, and excellent performance, quickly making them well-known. In 1963 at the Frankfurt International Motor Show, the Porsche 911 model was showcased, a design that remains popular even today. The body designer was Ferry Porsche's eldest son, Ferdinand Alexander Porsche. The Porsche 928 with rear transaxle chassis and V-8 engine, the new technology 955 model, and the 911 Carrera 4 with electronically adjustable four-wheel drive system and lift-free body are some of the most important developments in recent years. Porsche was formerly translated as "Porche," and its emblem uses Stuttgart's shield-shaped city emblem. The middle of the Porsche emblem features a galloping horse, representing a famous breed of horses prevalent in Stuttgart, with "Stuttgart" written above to indicate the company's headquarters in Stuttgart. The upper left and lower right sections feature deer antlers, indicating Stuttgart's past as a hunting ground. The upper right and lower left sections display yellow stripes representing ripe wheat, symbolizing bountiful harvests, with black representing fertile land and red representing people's wisdom and love for nature. Together, these elements form a beautiful pastoral landscape, symbolizing Porsche's glorious past and promising future.

**Lamborghini**

Country: Italy

Brand Establishment Year: 1963

Brand Style: Wildness and Passion

Classic Model: Lamborghini Murcielago (48.8 million yuan)

Lamborghini, also translated as "Lang Bo Ji Ni" or "Lin Bao Jian Ni," is officially known as Ferruccio Lamborghini Automobili S.P.A., the Italian Ferruccio Lamborghini Automobile Stock Company. Lamborghini's emblem is a bull full of strength, attacking its opponent, aligning with the characteristics of Lamborghini's high-horsepower, high-speed sports cars. It is said this emblem reflects the unyielding temperament of founder Ferruccio Lamborghini. Ferruccio Lamborghini, the founder of Lamborghini S.P.A. and its early sports cars and tractors, embodies the special ambition and determination of Italians, inspiring him to rise from a farmer's son to a respected industry leader through hard work. With uncompromising perseverance and almost fanatical enthusiasm, he tirelessly pursued the dream of creating the perfect sports car. Since then, the stubborn "bull" emblem has become a symbol of Lamborghini, interpreting all the characteristics of this unique car brand—challenging limits, proud and extraordinary, unrestrained and free-spirited.
