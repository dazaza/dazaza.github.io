[Qingdao Auto] On average, there is one car for every 9 people across Qingdao city.

by qssqwe215 on 2012-03-06 15:58:45

Yesterday, a reporter learned from the city's Vehicle Management Office that last month, the number of new car registrations in our city saw the first rebound after the holidays. Within one month, a total of 7,800 new cars were registered, with an average of about 270 new car registrations per day, representing an increase of approximately 18% compared to the same period. Calculated based on our city's permanent population of 8 million, as of now, there is one car for every seven people in our city and one private car for every nine people.

The total number of motor vehicles in the city is nearly 1.8 million.

A reporter learned from the city's Vehicle Management Office that in the just-concluded February, our city registered 7,800 new cars, averaging about 270 per day, marking the first rebound after the holidays. The reporter learned that compared to the same period last year, the number of new car registrations last month increased by approximately 18%.

According to the latest statistics from the Vehicle Management Office, our city currently has nearly 1.8 million motor vehicles, including 1.19 million cars and 920,000 private cars. Last year at the same time, our city had a total of 760,000 private cars, meaning that from February last year to February this year, our city added 160,000 new private cars within one year.

Calculated based on our city's permanent population of 8 million, as of now, there is one car for every seven people in our city and one private car for every nine people.

Last year, there was one private car for every ten people.

The reporter learned that since 2004 and 2005, our city has experienced a wave of car purchases. According to statistics, the number of cars in our city each year is as follows: 258,900 in 2003, 313,300 in 2004, 484,200 in 2005, 542,000 in 2006, 614,000 in 2007, 684,800 in 2008, 811,500 in 2009, 955,000 in 2010, and 1,145,300 in 2011. In just the first two months of this year, the number of cars in our city has increased to 1.19 million.

From the perspective of vehicle age composition, the [age] of cars in our city is generally young, with cars aged [seven years] or less accounting for about half of all cars. Last year, there was one private car for every ten people in our city, and now this figure has risen to one private car for every nine people, ranking first in the entire province. Calculated based on a [three-person household], there is one car for every three households, which is higher than the ratio of [one private car for five households] in Shanghai.

Eighty percent of new cars are [mid-to-high-end].

The reporter learned that in terms of car models, mid-to-high-end cars priced around 280,000 yuan account for about 80% of newly added cars. "Currently, the proportion of private car ownership is still increasing, which is related to the improvement in living standards of citizens. Among newly added motor vehicles, some are first-time purchases, while others are second-time replacements," said relevant personnel from the city's Vehicle Management Office. With the improvement in living standards and greater understanding of vehicles, citizens who have not yet purchased a car generally tend to prefer mid-to-high-end cars when making their purchase.

"Customers today generally pay attention to safety performance, such as whether it is sturdy enough and resistant to impact, followed by fuel efficiency. About one-third can afford prices between 100,000 to 200,000 yuan, another third may lean towards cars over 200,000 yuan, and the remaining third would prefer cars around 100,000 yuan," said a staff member of a 4S store on Chongqing South Road.
