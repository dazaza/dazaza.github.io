How to eat hot pot healthily in winter

by zhouzhou56 on 2011-12-23 12:13:37

As the weather gets colder day by day, the business of hot pot restaurants is getting more and more prosperous. There are even long queues outside the stores. Nowadays, eating hot pot is not only a dietary choice but also a fashionable lifestyle. However, the editor reminds everyone that hot pot should be eaten in moderation and not excessively. Moreover, some people with certain diseases should not eat hot pot...

How to Eat Hot Pot Healthily in Winter

Sichuan Hot Pot: Those who suffer from chronic pharyngitis, oral inflammation, gastritis, ulcers, skin diseases, hemorrhoids, anal fissures, frequent nosebleeds, gum bleeding, and those with "hot constitution" or pregnant women should avoid eating it.

Seafood Hot Pot: People with diabetes, hypertension, high blood pressure, hyperlipidemia, gout, and seafood allergies should avoid eating it.

Mutton Hot Pot: Those with "hot constitution", those who have phlegm-fire, at the early stage of a cold, taking laxatives, acute tonsillitis, acute pharyngitis, acute rhinitis, acute bronchitis, liver disease, and abscess patients should avoid eating it.

Mushroom Hot Pot: Those allergic to mushrooms, as well as those with gout and chronic gastritis, should avoid eating it.

How to Eat Hot Pot Healthily

Firstly, the hot pot should be thoroughly cleaned before use. Especially for copper hot pots, the copper rust must be removed to avoid copper poisoning, which mainly manifests as nausea, vomiting, dizziness, and shortness of breath.

Secondly, the food must be fully cooked in boiling broth. Eating hot pot often involves raw meat, raw fish, and raw vegetables being cooked while eating. These foods can easily be contaminated by pathogenic microorganisms and parasite eggs. Therefore, they must be fully cooked in boiling broth to achieve a "sterilization and disinfection" effect, thus preventing or reducing gastrointestinal inflammation and intestinal parasitic diseases.

Thirdly, the temperature of the food entering the mouth should not be too hot. Food taken out from the hot pot should be slightly cooled before eating to avoid scalding the oral cavity and esophageal mucosa, which may lead to ulcers in the oral cavity and esophagus or harm the gums and teeth, causing allergic toothache.

Fourthly, the time spent eating hot pot should not be too long. Sitting for a long time eating hot pot causes gastric juice, bile, pancreatic juice, and other digestive fluids to secrete continuously, depriving the glands of normal rest. This can easily lead to gastrointestinal dysfunction and cause abdominal pain and diarrhea.

Fifthly, the hot pot broth should be made and used immediately. The broth should not be reused, and leftover vegetable broth should not be left in the pot overnight for reuse. Otherwise, the nitrite content (a strong carcinogen) in the broth will increase after prolonged cooking, which is harmful to health when consumed.
