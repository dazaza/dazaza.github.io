Facebook's user base in developed regions has reached its limit, losing tens of millions of users in a year.

by geekzhang on 2013-05-03 21:00:56

Facebook UK traffic statisticsMay 1, Beijing time -- According to foreign media reports, data recently released by Nielsen, a well-known market research company, shows that in the past year, Facebook has lost 10 million users in the US region, and its monthly traffic in the UK has not seen any growth.The survey shows that the number of unique visitors accessing the Facebook website through smartphones, tablets, and traditional PCs peaked in August last year at 158 million people. However, this figure has now dropped from 153 million in March 2012 to 142 million in March this year.In its latest earnings report, Facebook revealed that it currently has over 1.11 billion monthly active users, an increase of 23% year-over-year; mobile monthly active users are 751 million, up 54% year-over-year. However, the growth in these monthly active users mainly comes from underdeveloped regions, and the advertising revenue these regions can bring to the company is usually lower.Currently, Facebook, which has been established for 9 years, is facing challenges from many competitors, including cross-platform messaging app WhatsApp. In response, Facebook has started to revamp its mobile applications and make efforts to accommodate users' preferred login methods in an attempt to regain its advantage. However, the growth in mobile access users does not seem sufficient to offset the rapid decline in Facebook's PC access volume.Data from Nielsen shows that the number of unique visitors to Facebook in the UK reached a peak on October 28 last year at 28 million people, but this figure had fallen back to 26 million by March this year. Moreover, the number of Facebook users in the UK has now returned to the level of the same period last year, indicating that the rapid growth phase of this social network in this region has come to an end.It is understood that Nielsen mainly counts the number of browsers accessing this site by setting tags on the server side with Facebook's consent, and this statistical data will then undergo cross-validation by user verification agencies in each country. Currently, Nielsen selects 40,000 users in the UK for sampling based on differences in geographic distribution, income, and age. Although the institution can accurately count the number of browsers accessing Facebook through various devices, it cannot provide precise data on the exact number of people using the Facebook application. However, Nielsen said that an average Facebook mobile user might only log into the web version of Facebook once a month.Notably, the data published by Nielsen aligns quite closely with the data provided by other research institutions. On Sunday local time, the UK's Guardian cited data from Socialbakers, a company specializing in analyzing social data, stating that both the US and European regions of Facebook have seen a decline in monthly user visits.However, Jan Rezab, CEO of SocialBakers, subsequently reminded everyone in his blog to be cautious when using SocialBakers' data as a reference because SocialBakers only made a rough estimate of Facebook's traffic, which may not reflect the actual situation. Now, Nielsen's survey data has drawn similar conclusions to SocialBakers.Facebook declined to comment on this matter.Future OpportunitiesFacebook previously stated that users in developed countries accessing Facebook via personal computers are decreasing, but mobile device access traffic is growing rapidly. During last Christmas, the number of monthly visitors accessing Facebook through mobile devices reached 680 million people, exceeding 50% of Facebook's total user base. Moreover, about 25% of advertising revenue comes from contributions from small-screen devices.Undoubtedly, for Facebook, mobile users already possess relatively greater user value. Mark Zuckerberg, Facebook's CEO, stated during the earnings call interpreting the financial report: Users who only visit Facebook's desktop product might only have a 40% chance of using Facebook on any given day, while the probability for mobile Facebook users is 70%. This situation might be somewhat unexpected for many people, but such news is very beneficial for our development in the mobile space.According to Facebook's first quarter financial report for the fiscal year 2013, ended March 31, Facebook's first-quarter revenue was $1.458 billion, up from $1.058 billion in the same period last year, representing a 38% increase year-over-year; net profit was $219 million, up from $205 million in the same period last year, representing a 7% increase year-over-year. Mobile advertising revenue accounted for 30% of the company's total advertising revenue, up 7 percentage points from 23% in the same period in 2012.
