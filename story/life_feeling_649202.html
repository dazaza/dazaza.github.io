If there is a next life, I will still be your woman.

by meimei234 on 2011-12-30 18:00:07

If there is a next life, I will still be your woman.

She was a distant relative of my friend, and by seniority, she should be called grandmother. I only saw her when she was seriously ill, which was when I was in college, accompanying my friend to the hospital where she was admitted. At that time, she seemed to have liver disease, with a yellowish complexion and swollen eyes. Even though she was very sick, one could still see from her eyes, eyebrows, and thick little lips that she must have been a rare beauty when she was young.

My friend told me that when she was young, she was one of the most beautiful women within dozens of miles. She had a tall figure, fair skin, and a head of black hair. Her big, bright eyes and red cherry lips made her stand out. Both of her parents passed away when she was very young, so she grew up in her uncle's house. Her uncle was a chef for weddings and funerals, so she started helping him when she was a teenager, and later on, she could work independently. In Liaodong, working is referred to as "doing jobs."

At the age of eighteen, she married a school teacher from her village. He was an only child, had attended private school for a while, could write and calculate, and was considered a cultured person locally. Their marriage of beauty and talent was envied by the villagers. On their wedding night, just as the groom lifted her red veil, he was pulled outside by someone. It turned out that the groom was an underground party member whose identity had been exposed. The Nationalist Party was coming to arrest him. The groom asked the messenger to wait for a moment while he went inside to explain the situation to his bride. Grandmother understood immediately and said, "You should run, don't stay here and get caught." She then took off her gold and silver jewelry and handed it to him, saying, "Better safe at home than rich on the road; it might come in handy."

The man cupped Grandmother's face in his hands and saw her tearful, bashful expression, her beauty almost intoxicating. He said, "You must wait for me, you are my woman..." and then kissed her small mouth, his tears falling onto her face... They parted hastily without even exchanging a few intimate words.

The man's parents were both sick, and upon hearing about their son's situation, they became bedridden with anger. On the second day after entering the groom's home, Grandmother put on her work clothes and started working as a chef to earn food to support the two elderly people.

She worked during the day and took care of her in-laws at night, living a very hard life. She never ate any good food given by others but brought it all back for them. For two years, there was no news from the man. Every night, she would bundle up his old clothes and hug them in her arms because they smelled like him, and holding them felt like hugging him...

In the fifth year, on a snowy afternoon, she was leaving a household when she was kidnapped by some bandits... Three days later, when she returned, there was a large bruise on her forehead, seemingly from a collision. No one knew what exactly happened during those three days, but she was covered in bruises. Her in-laws didn't dare to ask too many questions but quietly observed her. After a while, she discovered she was pregnant.

Since learning this news, her in-laws' attitudes towards her changed significantly. Although they continued to eat the food she cooked and drink the medicine she prepared, they never looked at her kindly. They thought she was dirty, disgracing their family name, and unworthy of their son. She knelt down and cried to the two elders, explaining that she had no choice, that she had to fight for her life to return. She said she didn't want to live anymore, but she couldn't abandon the elders, which was something her husband had entrusted her with before he left. Another reason she couldn't die now was that she hadn't yet been his woman. For these reasons, she was willing to live like a pig or a dog. She begged the elders not to tell anyone about her pregnancy.

Every day, she continued to work as a chef, but she had to tightly wrap her belly with wide white cloth strips to hide it from others. However, paper can't wrap fire forever, and two months before childbirth, her pregnancy was discovered by others. In people's eyes, she transformed instantly from a flower into a pile of foul-smelling cow dung...

No one was willing to greet her when they saw her, and no one wanted her to cook for them anymore. She became like Xiang Lin Sao in Lu Xun's "Blessing," an ominous object in everyone's eyes, a completely evil woman.

Every night, she hugged the man's clothes and asked him through tears, "Would you look at me this way too? This life, I am just your woman..."

On the night of labor, her in-laws could no longer bear her loud cries in the room because she was giving birth to another man's offspring, not her son's. The old couple hanged themselves with two ropes tied to the beam...

Amidst the pain of near death, she gave birth to a baby boy. Looking at the baby's pinkish-white little face, she knew he would grow up to be a very handsome son. She struggled to get up from the bed and wrapped the baby in a piece of red cloth. The baby kept crying. She kissed the baby's face repeatedly and then dressed herself, carrying the baby unsteadily towards the river...

When she was rescued, the baby was already gone. From then on, she lived in another village not far from her home. Her beauty still attracted many people. Many people tried to arrange marriages for her, advising her to remarry, but she refused them all. In her heart, she said, "I am only his woman in this life, and I will not marry anyone else." She barely got by doing short-term work for others, and such a life lasted for seventeen years...

Just a year before liberation, she learned from someone that her husband was still alive and had become a high official. Overjoyed, she hurriedly asked someone to write a letter and send it to him, telling him that she was still alive. After sending the letter, she eagerly waited for the messenger to return...

Finally, the messenger appeared before her, but did not bring the result she wanted. The letter said that he had learned about her situation and thanked her for everything she had done for his parents. But he could not forgive her for having another man's child behind his back. He advised her to remarry, as he already had a wife and children, and would not take her back.

She held the letter in her hands, dumbfounded, tears dropping onto the paper. This was the result of her twenty-two-year-long waiting, this was the reward she received for sacrificing her youth and life... She wanted to curse him... hate him... but she couldn't forget the joyous red veil of that year, the warmth of the hands that once caressed her; she couldn't forget the hurried kiss at parting, nor the deep gaze in his eyes as he left... Overnight, her jet-black hair turned white...

From then on, she lived alone in the countryside, receiving much help from the village. When night fell, she still had the habit of talking to him. She told him how much she missed him, how lonely she was, and how much she missed that wedding night... There were no more tears in her eyes; they had long been given to time; her heart no longer hurt, as suffering had numbed it...

According to my friend, she lived to be over seventy years old. Before she passed away, she hadn't eaten or drunk water for several days and couldn't speak. But in her final moment, she looked for someone with her eyes. After observing for a while, people finally understood. They brought the person who had delivered the letter to her. As soon as she saw him, two turbid tears streamed down her dry eyes, and her wrinkled lips moved slightly. The person quickly pressed his ear to her lips and heard her say brokenly, "You must tell him... In this life... I am only his woman... If there is a next life... I will still... be... his woman..."
