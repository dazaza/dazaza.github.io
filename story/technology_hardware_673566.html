Historical Introduction: Rice wine is one of the oldest types of alcohol in the world, originating in China, and is unique to China, ranking alongside beer and wine as one of the three ancient wines in the world. About three thousand years ago, during the Shang and Zhou dynasties, the Chinese uniquely invented the double fermentation method of wine yeast and began to produce large quantities of rice wine. The production area of rice wine is relatively wide, with many varieties. Famous ones include Zhejiang Jiafan wine (Flower Carving wine, etc.), Shaoxing Zhuangyuan Red, Suzhou's Shaizhou Youhuang, Wujiang's Wugong old wine, Baihuayang Taoyuan rice wine, Shanghai old wine, Fujian old wine, Jiangxi Jiuyang sealed jar wine, Jiangsu Baipu rice wine (Shuiminglou), Jiangsu Danyang sealed jar wine, Wuxi Huiquan wine, Guangdong Pearl Red wine, and Shandong Jimo old wine.
