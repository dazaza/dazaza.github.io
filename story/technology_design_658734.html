At the beginning of the new year, the well-known crosstalk performer Guo Degang and his team received a bad piece of news: three employees from the Deyun Society were administratively detained and fined by the police for assaulting people.

In early January this year, it was revealed that employees of Guo Degang's Deyun Society had assaulted a reporter. Guo Degang responded that the attackers were temporary workers at the Deyun Society and had been dismissed afterward. However, the police confirmed that the three individuals penalized, including Wang Hai, Wang Yuqin, and Wang Nan, were all employees of the Deyun Society.

Review:

Deyun Society Reporter Assault Incident – Guo Degang Claims They Were Temporary Workers

On the evening of January 9, 2012, photographer Huang Liang from the Fengxing Studio was taking pictures of Guo Degang and others returning to Beijing at Terminal 3 of Capital Airport when a physical altercation occurred with employees of the Deyun Society. Eight members of the Deyun Society surrounded the interview vehicle and demanded the photographer hand over his equipment, leading to an uncontrollable situation. Both the photographer and the driver sustained varying degrees of injury.

The following day, Guo Degang stated that he was not present during the assault and that the attackers were temporary workers responsible for carrying luggage and props at the Deyun Society, who had already been dismissed after the incident.

Progress:

Police Confirmation: The Attackers Were All Employees of the Deyun Society

The previous day, airport police informed me that three employees of the Deyun Society involved in the alleged assault at Capital Airport were given administrative detention and each fined 500 yuan. Currently, the fines have been paid, but since the three individuals applied for administrative reconsideration, the detention punishment has not yet been carried out. Airport police disclosed that the three individuals penalized included Wang Hai, Wang Yuqin, and Wang Nan. Wang Hai is Guo Degang's agent. The specific roles of the other two within the Deyun Society could not be determined by the police as master-disciple relationships are not legally binding. The only confirmation is that they are all employees of the Deyun Society. A relevant official from the airport police introduced that currently, the three individuals have returned to their respective residences awaiting the reconsideration investigation. If the reconsideration conclusion does not become the final decision and the parties involved are dissatisfied with the reconsideration decision, they can still file a lawsuit according to regulations. Whether the reconsideration result will change needs further investigation, as stated by the police.

Expansion:

"Penalty Decision Document" Appears Online; Netizens Worry About No Resolution

The previous day, a "Penalty Decision Document" issued by the Capital International Airport Public Security Bureau appeared online. According to the "Penalty Decision Document," around 11 p.m. on January 9, 2012, suspects Wang Hai, Wang Yuqin, and Wang Nan at Gate 12 on the fourth floor of Terminal 3 at Capital Airport got into an argument with Huang Liang and Liu Aimin. Subsequently, Wang Hai assaulted Liu Aimin and, together with Wang Yuqin and Wang Nan, assaulted Huang Liang. I learned from the Capital Airport Public Security Bureau that the bureau never published this "Penalty Decision Document" online, but the document was indeed issued by the airport bureau and its content is true. The police revealed that they had conducted prolonged civil mediation between both parties involved, but neither party accepted it. Finally, based on the results of the on-site investigation, the police decided that Wang Hai, Wang Yuqin, and Wang Nan, who carried out the assaults, would receive administrative detentions ranging from 10 to 12 days respectively, along with a fine of 500 yuan each.

Some netizens expressed concern that Wang Hai and others might delay through channels like administrative reconsideration to evade punishment. In response, a relevant official from the airport public security bureau said that according to the provisions of the Administrative Litigation Law, within 60 days of receiving the "Penalty Decision Document," Wang Hai and others may apply for administrative reconsideration to the higher-level public security organ issuing the decision document, and the higher-level public security organ will make a reconsideration decision within 60 days after accepting the reconsideration application. If Wang Hai and others still disagree with the reconsideration decision, they can legally file an administrative lawsuit with the court. Therefore, the matter of the assault will not end without resolution.
