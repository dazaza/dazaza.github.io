On-duty on March 15th

by rq204 on 2008-03-26 23:09:20

Today is March 15, the International Day for Protecting Consumers' Rights. While on duty at the company, I will briefly introduce the origin of March 15:

In order to expand the promotion of consumer rights protection and make it receive attention worldwide, promote cooperation and exchanges among national and regional consumer organizations, and better carry out the work of protecting consumer rights, the International Consumer Union Organization designated March 15th each year as the "International Day for Protecting Consumers' Rights" in 1983.

This date was chosen based on a Special Presidential Message on Protecting Consumer Interests delivered by former US President John Kennedy to the US Congress on March 15, 1962, where he first proposed the famous "four rights" of consumers: the right to safe consumption; the right to obtain consumer information; the right to freely choose goods; and the right to legal appeal.

Since 1983, every March 15th, consumer organizations around the world hold large-scale events, using various forms and media to focus on promoting the rights of consumers, the obligations of consumer organizations, and showcasing the strong power of consumers.

After China joined the International Consumer Union in September 1987, in 1988, it officially proposed to commemorate March 15 as the theme. Before this, there were events held on March 15 in 1986 and 1987, but they were not clearly defined as commemorative activities. In recent years, with the continuous enhancement of consumers' legal awareness, this day has truly emerged in China. For counterfeit agricultural supplies, our country has long taken severe measures. March 15th is the day to combat counterfeit and substandard products and protect consumer interests. However, the anti-counterfeiting efforts on March 15th can only be seen as a snapshot of the broader fight against counterfeits. The state has many corresponding policies regarding counterfeit agricultural supplies, and the most influential one is the "Red Shield Protects Agriculture" campaign formally named by the State Administration for Industry and Commerce in early 2004. In 2004, in response to a significant rise in agricultural supply prices and the prevalence of counterfeit agricultural supplies in the market, the state organized a nationwide special governance action to combat fake agricultural supplies and protect farmers, severely cracking down on illegal activities involving the production and sale of counterfeit agricultural supplies.

At the same time, the country's leaders have remained steadfast on the issue of combating counterfeit agricultural supplies. On April 1, 2004, CCTV exposed issues with the production and sale of substandard fertilizers in Hengyang City, Hunan Province. The next day, Premier Wen Jiabao made important instructions on the matter, requiring the General Administration of Quality Supervision, Inspection and Quarantine to directly investigate and publish the results, propose rectification requirements, and immediately deploy a comprehensive inspection of the national fertilizer market.

According to incomplete statistics, during the 2004 "Red Shield Protects Agriculture" campaign, all levels of industry and commerce administrative authorities across the country reorganized 38,200 types of agricultural supply markets; investigated and dealt with 31,389 cases of producing and selling counterfeit and substandard agricultural supply goods, with a total case value of 4.4 billion yuan, including 4,953 seed cases worth 53.3451 million yuan, 14,922 fertilizer cases worth 253.538 million yuan, and 7,022 pesticide cases worth 86.8922 million yuan. Through the "Red Shield Protects Agriculture" campaign, nearly 2.56 billion yuan in economic losses were recovered for farmers.

To combat counterfeit pesticides, fertilizers, and other agricultural supplies and fundamentally improve the order of the agricultural supply market, in 2005, the Ministry of Agriculture decided to continue organizing special governance actions to combat counterfeit agricultural supplies. These actions included: (1) convening a mobilization and commendation teleconference for the 2005 National Special Governance Action for Combating Counterfeit Agricultural Supplies and the Special Rectification Work for Strong Rat Poison; (2) organizing the "Trustworthy Agricultural Supplies Go to Villages Publicity Week" activity; (3) organizing agricultural supply quality inspection activities; (4) initiating pilot projects for "Trustworthy Agricultural Supplies Go to Villages"; (5) organizing agricultural supply knowledge competition activities; (6) organizing autumn joint inspections for combating counterfeit agricultural supplies.

The focus of the 2005 "Red Shield Protects Agriculture" action was to revoke the business licenses of a batch of seriously law-breaking operators to purify the agricultural supply market entities; support a batch of standardized, trustworthy, and law-abiding leading agricultural supply distribution enterprises to grow stronger and larger, initially standardizing the order of the agricultural supply market.
