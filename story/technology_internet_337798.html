People from Liuzhou or those who have driven a "Wuling" car all know: 1. It can protect against wind and rain. You won't be blown by the wind or drenched in the rain when going out. 2. It can increase your speed of travel. The speed is at least over 00 km/h. 3. It can seat more than 5 people at the same time, so everyone can be together when traveling.

People who have driven a "Mercedes-Benz" also know: 1. It can protect against wind and rain. You won't be blown by the wind or drenched in the rain when going out. 2. It can increase your speed of travel. The speed is at least over 80 km/h. 3. It can seat 5 people at the same time, so everyone can be together when traveling.

From the above three analyses, there seems to be almost no difference between "Wuling" and "Mercedes-Benz." But if these two types of cars pass in front of you at the same time, which one would leave a deeper impression on you? If you were asked to go on the highway or for outdoor leisure trips? If you were to conduct business activities with the owners of these two types of cars, which one would you choose?

The answer is definitely affirmative: Excluding personal factors, "Mercedes-Benz" obviously has an advantage over "Wuling." Do you know why this is?

1) "Mercedes-Benz" is rare: There aren't many people in China who can afford it.

2) "Mercedes-Benz" is dominant: Each costs millions, and good models need to be pre-ordered. The warranty is lifelong.

3) "Mercedes-Benz" is more individualistic: It's always spotless and always enters high-end places...

Similarly, in the vast and boundless network world, a website is like a company's "address," and the only way to distinguish this "address" is by remembering the website's domain name. Simply put, as long as you remember the domain name, you can easily understand and access the corresponding corporate entity.

Like the situation with the two types of cars mentioned above, originally there isn't much difference between domain names. All you need to do is remember them. But why are so many website owners willing to go to great lengths, even spending large sums of money on domain names?

1. Some domain names are easier to remember: "QQ" and "ACPFQQ" are both letter combinations, but "QQ" is clearly much easier to remember, and once remembered, it's hard to forget. The latter is not the case. This is one of the reasons for Tencent's success.

2. Some domain names are more appropriate and suitable: "www.net.cn" is the domain name of "China Wanwang." "Net" is one of the globally recognized domain suffixes. Isn't it obvious that "China Wanwang" is a professional internet website with a world-class domain suffix combined with China's unique domain suffix?

3. Some domain names sound more impressive: "www.OrientalHope.com." China is in the east of the world, "Oriental" means China. Life cannot exist without hope; hope is desire. "Com" is the first domain suffix of the global internet. Can a website with such a domain name be underestimated? Would a website with such a striking name worry about having no visitors?

4. Some domain names are more creative: "Regardless of thirty-seven or twenty-one" is a Chinese habitual saying. I think this might be the reason why many people habitually use the "3721" website!
