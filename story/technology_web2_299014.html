Barcelona's Ballon d'Or count surpasses Real Madrid, ranking third. La Liga leads the Premier League by a significant margin of 1002. Official Crown website.

by dghv69y8 on 2009-12-08 14:20:41

By reporter Tang Yangke: Messi's victory belongs to Barcelona.

This is the seventh Ballon d'Or trophy that Camp Nou has received, following Suarez (1960), Cruyff (1973, 1974), Stoichkov (1994), Rivaldo (1999), and Ronaldinho (2005). Barcelona surpasses Real Madrid (6 times) and stands alone in third place on the Ballon d'Or club rankings. Still leading the way are two Italian giants: Juventus and AC Milan, each with 8 Ballons d'Or. However, under the star-studded assault from Barcelona and Real Madrid, and amid Serie A's decline and talent drain, the first position held by Juventus and AC Milan is no longer unattainable.

In the past three years, Barcelona's achievements have been proportional to Messi's ranking: third in 2007, losing out to AC Milan and Kaká, as well as Manchester United and Cristiano Ronaldo; second in 2008, closely trailing Manchester United and Cristiano Ronaldo. In the 2009 Ballon d'Or club rankings, Barcelona set multiple records: 6 players among the top 30, 4 in the top 10 (the sixth time in history), 3 in the top five, and a total score of 852 points far ahead of Real Madrid in second place (299 points). Inter Milan ranked third with 84 points, followed by four English Premier League heavyweights behind the Italian champions: Manchester United and Liverpool both scored 54 points, Chelsea had 40 points, and Arsenal had 18 points.

Barcelona's victory belongs to La Liga.

With Barcelona's 7 trophies + Real Madrid's 6 trophies, this marks the 13th time in La Liga's history that it has won the Ballon d'Or, placing it second. Ahead of it is Serie A with 18 Ballon d'Or awards.

In 2008, despite Spain winning the European Championship, La Liga, with 603 points, lost out to the Premier League (700 points) due to the strong contingent from Manchester United. In 2009, Barcelona triumphed in the Champions League, and Real Madrid's summer splurge "bought" three Ballon d'Or candidates (Cristiano Ronaldo, Kaká, Benzema), tipping the scales completely towards the Iberian Peninsula. Perhaps no one could have predicted just how overwhelmingly powerful La Liga would be in 2009: 1168 points, making it the undisputed number one Ballon d'Or league today, surpassing the Premier League in second place by an incredible 1002 points! This is also the largest gap between the top two leagues since the Ballon d'Or's reform in 1995. Serie A ranked third with 87 points, while Germany (13 points) and France (6 points) placed fourth and fifth respectively.

The 2009 Ballon d'Or lineup (3-4-3): Casillas / Maicon, Terry, Vidic / Xavi, Iniesta, Gerrard, Kaká / Messi, Eto'o, C. Ronaldo.
