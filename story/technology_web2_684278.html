Second, the current situation and difficulties in managing subsistence allowances and special funds:

Urban residents’ minimum living guarantee, Star plans, and disaster relief policies are an important part of the social security system and an important way to protect the lives of the urban poor through the system, reflecting the superiority of the social system and serving the people wholeheartedly. However, as the civil affairs department specifically manages and operates these programs, the difficulties increase, especially in regions with lagging economies where the majority of the population faces difficulty, further increasing the challenges in various aspects of local matching funds not being guaranteed.

The specific performance of ensuring work includes more than five issues:

1. Applicants for subsistence allowances who are not satisfied criticize more.

2. Staff going door-to-door face hardships.

3. Thorough investigations of low-income households lead to mutual competition.

4. Staff criticized by both leadership and the masses.

5. First-line operators have grievances.

Four areas that are not in place:

1. Counties and streets, neighborhood committees first, staffing is not in place.

2. Head funding is not secured.

3. Infrastructure is lacking.

4. Wages are insufficient.

Three difficulties:

1. Low staff required to conduct weekly, monthly, and quarterly home visits with limited transportation, making it difficult to go on.

2. Interference from various relationships makes checks difficult.

3. Understanding the work of the masses is difficult.

Two kinds of relationships:

1. Human relationships from friends and relatives.

2. Greetings from leaders at all levels, approved article relationships.

One dissatisfaction:

A lot of work, often without the leadership's recognition and understanding of the masses.

Management strategies and measures:

The party group's great importance is the key. Resistance and difficulties in problems for the management of subsistence allowances and special funds come from all aspects. The civil affairs bureau party group has conducted five case studies and held several coordination hearings over the past two years, conducting dozens of in-depth research sessions in counties, cities, streets, and neighborhoods. Only in 2003, the household survey in Atushi City lasted more than 80 days, resulting in three files and eight communications to the District Office, Autonomous Prefecture party committee, National People’s Congress, government, and Ji Jianwei. Two thematic reports were submitted on guaranteeing working conditions; checks on low-income households amounted to 3755 households, totaling 11,202 checks.
