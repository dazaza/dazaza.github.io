Huawei Responds to US and European Security Accusations: No Evidence? Shut Up

by anonymous on 2013-07-29 12:07:16

Old problems, new controversies, have once again hit Huawei. The US and UK have repeatedly criticized Huawei over security issues, claiming that its telecommunications equipment poses security risks. In response, William Plummer, Huawei's Vice President of International Affairs, stated today in a declaration to The Verge: "Some people say they have evidence of threats. Alright, show it, or else keep quiet."

Earlier, Michael Hayden, the former CIA director (not to be confused with William Plummer), told The Australian Financial Review that he believes Huawei submits information to China. He is uncertain about how detailed this information might be, but he believes it involves at least some foreign communication systems.

Huawei has already made concessions; it no longer provides network equipment to the US. Due to similar concerns, the UK is currently investigating Huawei.

Plummer stated that such nonsense has been heard for many years now, and it amounts to racist corporate defamation.

Below is Plummer's response:

This nonsense has been going on for years. The reason it keeps coming up is because it's repackaged as a vulnerable, clear target to distract attention. There have been some real issues recently with global networks and information, and these statements are designed to shift focus.

Pointing fingers at Huawei and defaming Huawei may feel good to them because Huawei is a Chinese company. But there is no harm, no violation, right? Wrong. Huawei is a globally proven multinational enterprise spanning 150 markets. We provide livelihoods for countless people in the US, and we will continue to do so, indirectly, by purchasing $6 billion worth of products annually from American suppliers.

Some people say they have evidence of threats? Alright, show it, or else keep quiet. Without evidence, it’s purely for political purposes — it's racist corporate defamation, nothing more.
