Jack Ma Steps Down as CEO of Alibaba, Leaving successor Jonathan Lu Three Major Challenges

by geekzhang on 2013-05-10 15:22:04

On the day that coincides with Taobao's tenth anniversary, Jack Ma has chosen to officially end his 14-year tenure as CEO of Alibaba Group, passing the baton to Joe Tsai, with whom he has worked for 13 years.Jack Ma and Joe Tsai have vastly different personalities. If Jack Ma is the imaginative Martian, then Joe Tsai is the operation-oriented realist; Jack Ma is good at setting visions, while Joe Tsai excels in execution. To some extent, the two are complementary partners in terms of personality.Before stepping down as CEO, Jack Ma made final arrangements during his term for Ali: organizational restructuring, investment in Sina Weibo, layout of mobile internet entry points, and anti-counterfeiting strategies for Taobao’s transformation, among others.For Joe Tsai, this baton carries significant weight. His advantage lies in having served as a firefighter multiple times during Ali's development, from Alipay, Taobao, B2B to Ali Cloud OS, successfully completing tasks assigned by Jack Ma each time.This time, can Joe Tsai, who has undergone a role transformation, lead Ali to new heights?The Multi-faceted TemujinJoe Tsai, alias Temujin, known within Ali as Old Lu. A former subordinate described Joe Tsai like this: approachable, low-key, no airs; simple, straightforward, subordinates are willing to work hard for him.Righteous towards subordinates. The aforementioned former subordinate recounted such a story: when Joe Tsai was serving as the manager of the Shenzhen branch, Jack Ma visited Shenzhen and discussed values with a colleague. This colleague casually mentioned: values seem somewhat abstract, what we salespeople care about most is performance. After hearing this, Jack Ma was displeased and told Old Lu that this employee should be let go.However, Old Lu firmly defended this subordinate to Jack Ma, saying that this employee had excellent values and performance, and must be retained. Therefore, those who follow him are willing to work hard.In the eyes of ordinary employees, he is a star of hope. No matter how difficult the current business may be, when they hear that Old Lu is leading it, everyone gets excited. An Ali employee said.Pragmatic, strong in execution. Joe Tsai is one of the few people in Ali's sales management region who does not rotate positions. During his tenure as the highest responsible person in the South China region, Jack Ma liked going to Guangdong, partly because of the good sales performance there.In December 2004, Jack Ma transferred Joe Tsai from Shenzhen to Hangzhou to serve as President of Alipay. Under Joe Tsai's leadership, Alipay's market share reached over 50%. In January 2008, after Sun Tongyu went abroad for study, Joe Tsai took over as CEO of Taobao. Currently, Taobao holds a monopolistic position in China's C2C market. In February 2011, following the resignation of Jonathan Lu, CEO of Alibaba's B2B business, Joe Tsai took over. In September 2012, Ali Group announced that Cloud OS would operate independently from the Ali Cloud Business Group, and Joe Tsai concurrently served as President of this business. Additionally, in July 2012, Joe Tsai was appointed Chief Data Officer (ChiefDataOfficer) of Ali Group, responsible for advancing the strategy of making Ali Group a data-sharing platform.Joe Tsai has basically been through all subsidiaries, and most of them were either new businesses or emergency assignments. An internal employee of Ali Group stated.Simple in nature. Multiple insiders revealed that Joe Tsai does not cultivate inner-circle loyalists, does not gossip, dislikes forming cliques, and enjoys playing mahjong at most.Joe Tsai also has a tough side. Old Lu lacks guile, gets angry when unhappy, but once the anger is gone, everything is fine, focusing on issues rather than individuals. A person familiar with Joe Tsai said that he only loses his temper and scolds people when they are particularly close.A netizen on Zhihu evaluated Joe Tsai like this: very spontaneous, whether discussing work with colleagues in the office or talking on the phone about work, his voice is loud, sometimes laughing heartily, or getting extremely angry and scolding people.The Three Major Campaigns Facing Joe TsaiBefore Jack Ma stepped down, he had already laid out a plan, hoping to achieve goals in platforms, finance, and data.Ali's assets can be divided into two parts. One is Ali Group, which underwent structural adjustments at the beginning of this year, establishing 25 business units, with Joe Tsai serving as CEO. The second is the establishment of the Micro Financial Services Group, composed of Alipay and Ali Finance, appointing Peng Lei as CEO. Thus, a dual-CEO structure (note: not co-CEOs) was formed, with Joe Tsai and Peng Lei becoming Jack Ma's right-hand men.The development direction of various businesses under Ali Group has been largely determined. Taobao is transitioning to smaller, more beautiful models, and before stepping down, Jack Ma announced at a press conference that counterfeit goods must be eradicated. Layout of mobile internet entry points, increasing investment in Dianping, investing in Sina Weibo, rumors of investing in Momo, acquiring Umeng, Zhuo Master, increasing stake in UC, and investing in Kuaidi Dache. Accelerating the mining of big data, developing Ali Finance based on big data.With his role transformed, Joe Tsai will shift from an executor to a strategic initiator. How to better implement existing strategies and dynamically adjust them, improve organizational structures, smoothly lead the group to go public, and find new growth points will all become new tests.Firstly, establishing personal authority. Ali has both strengths and weaknesses in its past, namely its faith culture and authority culture, where there is a god-like figure, and following is enough. Now, how to transform Ali into a regular company while not losing efficiency and vitality is a contradiction. Internet veteran Xie Wen commented.Another evaluation by someone familiar with Ali: Ali is a one-man culture, many of the company's younger employees are fervent admirers of Jack Ma. Under the premise of personal worship and supreme personal authority, how should the successor position themselves?On one hand, Joe Tsai was personally selected by Jack Ma, familiar with the original culture, but now it is Joe Tsai managing, not Jack Ma. When the admired figure steps down, it is easy for the successor to lose their way, which is a great challenge. The above-mentioned individual said.After taking office, Joe Tsai needs to inherit Ali's core corporate culture and fine traditions, while transitioning from personal authority to institutionalized mechanisms. Such a transition cannot be completed in one or two years. Actually, not just Ali, Microsoft and Yahoo's successors all face similar challenges.Secondly, improving organizational structures. Xie Wen believes that Alibaba's hard structure is complex, from the Seven Swords of Ali to 25 business units, no company has a more complex structure than Ali, and it changes rapidly.For managers, managing 3-5 business units is the best management radius. Joe Tsai's management radius reaches 25 business units, how to coordinate the relationships between these 25 business units, and how they collaborate, is another major challenge.What kind of hard structure will eventually form is something the CEO needs to handle. The current structure is definitely unsustainable and must change. Xie Wen said.Thirdly, going public and finding the next growth point. Regarding going public, Joe Tsai plays the most important role. Joe Tsai needs to clearly explain Ali's story: why it is worth investing in, why its future prospects are imaginative.So far, the general direction can be vaguely seen, but from an investment perspective, the story still needs to be explained more clearly, which is the CEO's responsibility. Xie Wen commented.Founder of the Internet Lab, Fang Xingdong, believes that Joe Tsai's first task is to maintain the high-speed growth of Ali Group. If growth slows down, going public will face significant problems. Only high-speed growth allows for sufficient imagination.Can Ali Group find the next growth point at this stage?In the development of Ali Group, three major patterns have been seized. First, Alibaba B2B, solving the problem of information symmetry between Chinese enterprises and overseas buyers; second, with the popularization of the internet, seizing the e-commerce landscape; third, Alipay seizing the online transaction landscape.However, in the mobile end, Ali has not yet achieved much. WeChat has inserted itself into a major entry point of mobile internet, and Ali is currently acquiring to seize these entry points.Although no one can predict the next trend, Joe Tsai must face this issue: can Ali Group seize the next pattern?
