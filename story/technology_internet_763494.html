Apple's most important product isn't the watch. It's the new iPhone.

by anonymous on 2013-08-02 10:24:20

On July 31, according to foreign media reports, there are rumors in the industry that Apple is producing a TV, a wristwatch, and even its own cable TV service. However, in what has been one of Apple's most stable years, the most important product remains the iPhone, which has brought success to the company over the past five years.

Since its launch in 2007, iPhone sales have grown rapidly, becoming the product that accounts for the largest proportion of Apple’s sales. So far this year, the iPhone has generated 54 cents of revenue for every dollar of Apple's sales, and analysts say the iPhone will bring an even larger share of profits. However, the growth rate of iPhone sales has slowed down recently. In the last nine months, sales have only grown by 16%, compared to 71% in 2012 and around 90% previously.

One of Apple's problems is that it hasn't provided an update for this star product since the release of the iPhone 5 in September last year. Another issue is that to attract online shoppers, Apple launched the iPhone 4 in 2010.

Josh Spencer, manager of T. Rowe Price Global Technology Fund, said: "This company, driven by innovative products, hasn't introduced any new ones." By the end of June, Apple was the fund's largest holding.

The main reason for the sharp drop in Apple's stock price from its historical high of $705.07 in September last year to today's $450 is the slowing growth in Apple's sales and the subsequent decline in profits. The rush of new product launches at the end of last year clearly botched the marketing of some products. The iPhone 5 was launched in late September along with the updated fourth-generation iPad, the new iPad mini was released in early November, and the iMac desktop computer followed in late November.

Although sales were strong, the time before the holidays was too short. Tim Cook later admitted that the company couldn't produce enough iPhones, iPads, and iMacs to meet customer demand, leading to a drop in sales.

Another major issue is that most people in developed countries in Europe and America already own a smartphone, while sales are growing faster in developing countries. However, the price of the iPhone is too high. Investors hope that Apple will launch a cheaper version of the iPhone, perhaps with a plastic colored shell instead of the current metal casing.

Analysts say that a completely new phone could attract more customers and be designed with higher profit margins than the iPhone 4. A low-cost version of the iPhone with a colored plastic shell could significantly boost Apple's sales in developing countries. Apple will have to design a cheaper version of the iPhone, maintaining a relatively high profit margin without affecting its premium reputation among affluent customers. Some analysts worry that the low-cost iPhone may never generate enough value for the company.

While some analysts claim that producing new products like a TV could help Apple win back investors, most investors still believe that launching a new iPhone should be the top priority.
