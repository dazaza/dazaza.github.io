In the red dust of the world, I look at you across the field.

by zhouzhou56 on 2011-12-30 19:09:20

Among the red dust of countless paths, I look at you across the way.

How I miss the days of yore. Those days spent in the embrace of words, when the sky was always azure blue, and the sun shone brightly without end. Even the occasional rain seemed to carry a unique charm and romance.

Every time my fingers wandered, it felt as if the wind danced lightly in my heart. Every time I extended my wrist, there was an indistinct fragrance lingering around my sleeves. Even brushing my hair unintentionally would remind me of the joyous ease of flowing rivers.

A red leaf, a winding brook, a cluster of green bamboo, a falling snowflake, a gentle breeze, a smile, a glance, even the babble of a baby in its sleep—everything I saw turned into a poem, a verse, an essay, or a vivid ink painting.

I cherished those days spent with dear friends, exchanging simple greetings, heartfelt blessings, and warm reminders. They were like sunlight filtering through tree shadows, repetitive and fragmented, yet unspeakably warm and intimate.

But I wonder when all this changed, and how it did so. I don't know from which day onward—I never noticed a beginning—but when I realized it, such days had already passed silently for a long time.

Perhaps it was due to busyness, perhaps fatigue, or maybe these were just excuses. I only clearly knew that I had become lazy. I preferred to sit in the chair under the sun, closing my eyes and daydreaming, rather than write a single word or exchange thoughts with others through writing. For a long period, I didn't write, comment, or leave messages, staying almost in a state of seclusion.

I thought I was the only one like this, but when I looked closely, I found that many familiar friends had disappeared just like me.

There were no longer active figures in our updates, few comments on message boards, and hardly any short messages after logging in. The once lively groups were now often silent.

Seeing more and more unfamiliar faces on the familiar websites, feeling the increasingly cold environment, I often sadly wondered if we could ever return to the past. But within the next second, I gave myself a more sorrowful answer: I think we can never go back.

I just don’t know why, in just a year’s time, everything has truly changed so much that things are no longer what they used to be.

Where are the concerns across distant waters? Where are the watchful gazes deep in time? Where is the harmonious resonance between words and hearts? Have they all quietly drifted away with the river of time, never to return?

Some say the internet is inherently a cold world. Is this really true?

I don’t believe it.

When I heard it was raining in Shanghai, I wondered if Feixue remembered her umbrella. When I learned about floods in a certain area of Guizhou, I worried if it was Dongtian's hometown. Seeing the two characters for Jiangsu, I could faintly hear Fenglinxiyu's intimate whispers. Seeing big apples from Yantai, a light breeze would immediately pass through my mind. When the word Xinjiang appeared before my eyes, my beloved Baihe would warmly smile in my thoughts. Once, when bright red kapok flowers bloomed all over my farm, I instantly thought of Luli, Xiao Xi, and dear Lan who were far away in Guangdong... Each originally strange city, because of them, became inexplicably close and familiar to me.

And when Shuwu Brother left a message telling me he had been in my city on business recently, walking down the streets and wondering if he might turn around to see me; when Zesi Brother made meticulous plans and safe arrangements for us when he learned I was traveling to his city (even though I eventually couldn't accept his kindness, that sense of being cared for warmed me throughout the National Day holiday); when I received a gift from Xiaoyeyu Sister thousands of miles away on a radiant autumn day; and when I hadn't logged onto the website for over ten days, and my QQ continuously received "missing person notices" from Liu Jin, Fenger Sister, and Linxi Sister... I finally realized happily that we have always been in each other's hearts.

I also finally understood that some people and some events do not need to be deliberately remembered, because we have never truly been apart. Once feelings take root in the heart, they sprout, grow, flourish into trees, and bloom with abundant flowers. Even if we never meet again, never think of each other again, how could we forget, how could we not forget, and how could we bear to forget the beauty of those flowers, the days when the wind blew, the crystalline petals that fell with the wind, the delicate and graceful thoughts, the beautiful and peaceful times? All of this will forever grow in the softest, most moist parts of our hearts.

Mentioned gently, it is a lifetime, a journey across mountains and rivers.

I believe that whether we see or don’t see, whether we remember or don’t remember, we will always quietly sit on both sides of the passing years. Separated by the flow of time, crossing thousands of mountains and evening snows, our eyes clear and sincere, we earnestly gaze at each other among the red dust of life, watching the transformations of space and time, the clouds gathering and dispersing, and the flowers blooming and wilting on the other shore.

And among the countless paths of red dust, we often need only an unintentional glance back to see old acquaintances gracefully coming toward us from the depths of clear, distant times.

That lowering of the eyebrows, that faint smile, that gentle sigh, still carries boundless charm, just as it did in those years.
