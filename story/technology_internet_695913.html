Hurun Global Rich List released for the first time, Zong Qinghou becomes the richest man in mainland China

by abcj5hehe on 2012-03-07 17:42:53

Hurun Research Institute released the Hurun Global Rich List for the first time in Beijing on February 6. Mexican telecom tycoon Carlos Slim topped the list with a fortune of $55 billion to become the richest man in the world in 2012 according to Hurun. Among the five Chinese tycoons on the list, Zong Qinghou ranked 78th.

In this global rich list published by Hurun Research Institute for the first time, 83 billionaires made the list, with the entry threshold at $10 billion. Bill Gates ranked second with a fortune of $50 billion, while Warren Buffett ranked third with $48 billion.

Mark Zuckerberg, the 28-year-old founder of Facebook, entered the top ten of the Hurun Global Rich List with a fortune of $26 billion, becoming the youngest self-made billionaire in the world.

Among the five Chinese tycoons on the list, Li Ka-shing, aged 84, became the richest man in China in 2012 with a fortune of $24 billion, while Zong Qinghou, aged 67, became the richest man in mainland China in 2012 with a fortune of $10.5 billion.

Rupert Hoogewerf, founder and chief researcher of Hurun Report, said at the press conference that despite the impact of the European debt crisis, unrest in the Middle East, and a slowdown in economic growth in China and India, the club of billionaires with fortunes of $10 billion or more still had 83 members.

On average, they were 66 years old with an average fortune of $1.83 billion. Among them, there were ten women, all of whom inherited their wealth. Of the 83 members, 56 were self-made, accounting for two-thirds.

The list showed that American and European tycoons still accounted for the majority in this global billionaire club, with 27 and 17 people respectively; Russia, South America, China, and India followed closely behind. The total wealth of the 83 members reached $1.515 trillion, equivalent to the total national debt of the UK.

Hoogewerf said that this was the first time they had released the global rich list. Although currently only five Chinese tycoons had entered the global billionaire club, Chinese entrepreneurs were constantly growing and becoming more mature and confident.

Zong Qinghou became the richest man in mainland China with a fortune of $10.5 billion.

On this super-rich list, three were from Hong Kong, with Li Ka-shing as the richest, while Zong Qinghou became the only mainland tycoon on the list. According to statistics, the most tycoons came from the finance and investment industry, accounting for 18%; followed by the IT industry, accounting for 13%; retail and natural energy industries ranked third. In this global billionaire club, American and European tycoons still accounted for the majority. The total wealth of the 83 tycoons on the list reached a staggering $1.515 trillion, equivalent to the total national debt of the UK.

Notably, Liang Wengen, the chairman of Sany Heavy Industry and double richest man on last year's Forbes and Hurun rich lists, failed to make it onto this list due to the depreciation of his Sany shares. The position of the richest man in mainland China once again returned to Zong Qinghou. This Wahaha Group leader had become the richest man in mainland China in 2010 and this year once again topped the mainland list with a fortune of $10.5 billion. Although Li Ka-shing's fortune shrank slightly due to an 8% fall in the Hang Seng Index in Hong Kong, he still owned a fortune of $24 billion.

When analyzing the list, Hoogewerf said that one-third of the tycoons on the global rich list inherited their wealth, while only 1% of Chinese tycoons inherited their wealth, with the remaining 99% being self-made. This was a significant difference between the global list and the Chinese list.
