The XT502 is a mid-range model from Motorola, targeted at young consumers and focusing on the mid-to-low-end mobile phone market. It still uses the latest Android 2.1 operating system and is Motorola's first Android phone with a trackball.

Motorola XT502

The traditional black tone occupies 90% of the body area of the Motorola XT502, without much that catches the eye. However, it features a highly dynamic iridescent blue as the side frame of the phone, which adds a touch of brilliance to its design, making the Motorola XT502 very fashionable and a good choice for young people. With dimensions of 115.86 x 6013.7mm, the XT502 is relatively compact, equipped with a 3.2-inch capacitive screen with 16 million colors and a resolution of 480x320 pixels, providing average screen performance.

First use of a trackball design

The Motorola XT502 adopts a trackball design, making it Motorola's first smartphone with such a feature, adding a hint of BlackBerry style. The XT502 is equipped with a 5-megapixel lens that supports LED flash.

The XT502 is powered by a 550MHz CPU, has 512MB ROM and 256MB RAM, and runs on the same Android 2.1 platform as the Milestone, ensuring smooth system operation. In terms of network connectivity, the Motorola XT502 does not support 3G networks but can connect to the internet via WLAN. It also has a built-in GPS chip, allowing for geographic positioning and navigation when combined with Google Maps or third-party mapping software.
