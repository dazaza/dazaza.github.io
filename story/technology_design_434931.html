Ministry of Railways deploys key work, ensuring safety and stability of railways during the two sessions!

Liu Zhijun pointed out that after the festival, all levels of organizations and a large number of employees in the railway system continued their hard work, faced passenger peaks, carried out post-disaster reconstruction work, and achieved good results. The railway system successfully handled the post-festival passenger peak, with stable and orderly Spring Festival transport; continuously increased coal transportation organization, exceeding coal transportation targets; fully ensured disaster relief and holiday material transportation, effectively supporting post-disaster reconstruction in affected areas and market supply; post-disaster reconstruction of railways achieved significant results, with damaged equipment and facilities quickly restored. Liu Zhijun said that the achievements in post-festival Spring Festival transport, coal transportation, post-disaster reconstruction, etc., were the result of conscientious implementation of instructions from the central leadership of the Party and the State Council by all railway employees, who worked tirelessly, selflessly contributed, and worked hard. Heavy crackdown on ticket scalpers by the Shanghai Railway Station Public Security Bureau!

People's Network Shanghai, February 29th (Shen Wenmin, Zhang Yan) - To purify the ticket purchasing order during the Spring Festival transport period, the Shanghai Railway Station Public Security Bureau launched the "Blue Shield Operation" against ticket scalpers from January 1st this year. As of today, they have investigated 195 cases of scalping tickets, dismantled 23 ticket scalping dens, destroyed 9 ticket scalping gangs, arrested 232 ticket scalpers, and seized 1530 tickets, valued at over 230,000 yuan. At around 1:00 PM on February 28th, based on an anonymous tip from the public, the Shanghai Railway Station Public Security Bureau swiftly acted and caught a man near the Beijing East Road ticket office attempting to sell a ticket at a 10 yuan markup to a passenger. On the spot, they found 21 tickets with different times, train numbers, and destinations in his bag, totaling 5983 yuan. Upon investigation, suspect Xiao admitted to purchasing tickets on behalf of a certain media interaction company in Shanghai without any qualifications, and illegally marking up each ticket by 10 yuan. Xiao has been criminally detained by railway police. Railway police remind citizens that with the upcoming spring outings and tomb-sweeping season, be sure to purchase tickets only from official railway ticket outlets, and report any ticket scalping activities to the police immediately.
