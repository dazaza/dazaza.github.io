To find the most economical and convenient way to ensure the astronauts' healthy diet, researchers from Cornell University and the University of Hawaii at Manoa are currently recruiting six volunteers to try out the Martian astronaut diet continuously for four months.

Accommodation, meals, and travel expenses are all covered, and after completion, a $5000 (approximately 31,000 RMB) reward will be given. This research is part of a three-year project funded by the National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA). The data obtained from this simulation experiment will help formulate the astronauts' menu to provide them with as much variety as possible while finding the most convenient materials to transport.

"Ensuring that astronauts eat well is very important," said Jean Hunter, Professor of Biological and Environmental Engineering at Cornell University, "This relates to the safety of the astronauts and the success of the mission." Professor Hunter stated that the volunteers would live like astronauts in a simulated Martian base, wearing simulated space suits and trying some foods. These foods include fast food currently eaten by NASA astronauts and some packaged foods, such as flour, sugar, and dried meat.

Applications close on February 29th (11:59 PM Hawaii time). Researchers indicated that they would complete the selection of volunteers by the end of May.

Volunteers can choose different experimental plans with varying durations, such as 4 days, two weeks, or 120 days, with differing rewards accordingly.

If choosing the 4-day experiment, round-trip economy class airfare, accommodation, and meal expenses during the experiment are covered — equivalent to a free 4-day tour of the Martian base.

For the two-week experiment: all travel and accommodation expenses are covered, with a $500 (3150 RMB) reward or an opportunity to work at the local institute.

For the 120-day experiment: all travel and accommodation expenses are covered, with a daily subsidy of $25 (157 RMB). After completing the entire project, a $5000 reward or an opportunity to work at the institute is offered. Those choosing the latter also receive a daily subsidy, along with covered meals, accommodations, and travel expenses.

However, if a volunteer drops out midway, they won't receive any reward and must bear additional travel costs due to plan changes themselves. If leaving due to health or psychological reasons, they may receive a proportional reward and travel expense subsidy.

Application Method:

Download the application form at http://manoa.hawaii.edu/hi-seas/#selection, fill it out, and send it to binsted@hawaii.edu

Recruitment Conditions:

● High education: Must have a bachelor's degree in engineering, biology, physics, mathematics, or computer science; must have a driver's license.

● Work experience: More than three years of work experience after obtaining a bachelor's degree; priority given to those with construction, electronics, or property maintenance work experience; priority given to those with medical background.

● Good physical condition: Able to pass a second-level flight physical examination; no respiratory surgery history, applicants with rhinitis excluded; at least no smoking in the past two years; priority given to those who can lift 15 kg and run 100 meters within 40 seconds.

● Foodie: Willing to try various kinds of food, with normal sense of smell and taste.

● Strong interest: Have a strong interest in space exploration.

● Language proficiency: Fluent in English speaking, listening, reading, and writing.

● Age range: Applicants aged 21 to 65 are eligible.

● Others: Female applicants should not be pregnant or breastfeeding between 2012 and 2013. (Fang Hai)
