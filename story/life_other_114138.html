My 2008 Summary

The Year of the Ox is here, and in order to carry forward the spirit of relentless internet use, I aim to build on my strengths and address my weaknesses in the new year, achieving greater personal value online. Here's a summary:

1. A Year Filled with Hardships

a) Physical Health

- Gained 10 kilograms, mainly concentrated in the abdomen, resulting in an unsightly bulge of fat. The cause? Too much time sitting in front of the computer with insufficient exercise.

- Myopia increased by 200 degrees due to excessive screen time. I started experiencing dizziness and was diagnosed with mild neurasthenia by the doctor. That foolish doctor even suggested I reduce my internet time moving forward. Is that even possible? Ridiculous!

b) Family Life

- My child called me "Dad" approximately 1,000 fewer times this year, averaging about 3 times less per day. Reason: Seeing my intense interest in being online, they dared not interrupt for fear of getting scolded.

- My wife’s affection towards me has cooled down by 15 degrees, reaching a body temperature of 37 degrees Celsius, which is dangerously close to the freezing point of divorce at 0 degrees. We set a record low for family outings to the park—only once this year. I forgot tasks assigned by my wife 28 times, leading to her verbally assaulting me 18 times. I scorched the pot 15 times, rendering it unusable on 7 occasions.

c) Work Performance

- Caught surfing the web at work by my boss 51 times.

- Lost about 3,500 yuan in bonuses.

- Mistakenly wrote “MM” instead of “ladies” three times while writing self-criticisms, earning myself ridicule from colleagues 700 times.

d) Online Activities

- Posted 9 threads and replied to 1,500 posts on the Rudos Forum.

- Had 3 posts deleted and 11 forwarded.

- Removed as a friend 18 times.

- Plagiarism detected 5 times.

2. A Year Full of Joy and Confidence

a) Made a Large Group of Friends

- Joined 18 groups.

- Added 121 attractive female friends on QQ. Among them, 105 call me “Brother,” and 9 call me “Dear.”

b) Successfully Transitioned from Virtual Network to Real Life

- Met up with attractive women 100 times, among whom 11 met my beauty standards.

c) Enriched Emotions

- Engaged in online romances 56 times. Of these, 19 lasted no more than 10 days, 29 lasted one month, 6 lasted two months, and 2 lasted three months.

- Daily indulgence in terms like “dear,” “kiss,” “hug,” made me feel rejuvenated despite looking weary from long hours in front of the computer. Psychologically, I feel much younger.

d) Enhanced Communication Skills

- Chatted in chat rooms 258 times and via QQ 752 times.

- In contrast to real life, I now confidently approach women online without blushing or feeling nervous.

- Learned to wear a mask and hurl vicious insults at men I hate or envy.

- Realized the profound truth that a sweet call of “beautiful lady” can easily win a woman's heart.
