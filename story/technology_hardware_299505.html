Acute pelvic inflammatory disease (PID) can progress and cause diffuse peritonitis, sepsis, and infectious shock, which in severe cases can be life-threatening. Therefore, patients with acute PID should receive timely treatment. So, what are the treatment methods for acute PID available in Suzhou?

1. Drug Therapy for Acute PID in Suzhou

In recent years, with the continuous emergence of new antibiotics, advances in anaerobic bacterial culture technology, and the use of drug sensitivity tests, clinical drug usage has become more rational, considering both aerobic and anaerobic bacteria control, significantly improving the efficacy of treating acute PID. The vast majority of cases of acute PID can be completely cured with active treatment during the acute phase. Selection of antibiotics based on drug sensitivity tests is more reasonable; however, before receiving lab results, it's necessary to speculate on the type of pathogen based on medical history and clinical characteristics, taking into account any antibiotics used after the onset of the illness. Since acute PID often involves mixed infections of aerobic bacteria, anaerobic bacteria, and chlamydia, with aerobic and anaerobic bacteria further divided into Gram-negative and Gram-positive categories, antibiotics are usually administered in combination.

2. Supportive Therapy for Acute PID in Suzhou

Bed rest is recommended, with a semi-recumbent position helping to collect pus in the rectouterine pouch, thus limiting the spread of inflammation. A high-calorie, high-protein, high-vitamin liquid or semi-liquid diet should be provided, with fluid supplementation as needed. Attention should be paid to correcting electrolyte imbalances and acid-base disturbances, and if necessary, small amounts of blood transfusions may be given. Physical cooling can be used for high fever. Unnecessary gynecological examinations should be avoided to prevent the spread of infection. If abdominal distension occurs, gastrointestinal decompression should be performed.

4. Surgical Treatment for Acute PID in Suzhou

The following situations indicate surgical intervention:

(1) Hydrosalpinx or tubo-ovarian abscess: After drug treatment shows improvement, continue controlling inflammation for several days. If the swelling hasn't disappeared but has localized, surgery should be performed to avoid future acute episodes requiring another operation.

(2) Ruptured abscess: Sudden worsening of abdominal pain, accompanied by chills, high fever, nausea, vomiting, abdominal distension, and symptoms of toxic shock upon examination, all suggest a ruptured abscess, necessitating immediate laparotomy. Surgery can be chosen either through an open abdominal procedure or laparoscopic surgery. The extent of the surgery should consider factors like the lesion range, patient age, and overall condition. The principle is mainly focused on removing the diseased tissue. For young women, ovarian function should be preserved as much as possible, opting for conservative surgery; for older women or those with recurrent bilateral adnexal involvement or adnexal abscesses, total hysterectomy and bilateral salpingo-oophorectomy may be required. For extremely weak critical patients, the scope of surgery must be decided based on specific circumstances. If there is a pelvic abscess or pelvic connective tissue abscess (extraperitoneal abscess), drainage can be done via a vaginal or lower abdominal incision depending on the abscess location. If the abscess is low and protrudes toward the posterior fornix of the vagina, it can be drained vaginally while injecting antibiotics; if the abscess is higher and superficial, extraperitoneal incision and drainage can be performed above the inguinal ligament.

(3) Ineffective drug therapy: If pelvic abscess forms and persists despite 48-72 hours of drug treatment, with no reduction in temperature, worsening systemic toxicity, or enlargement of the mass, timely surgery should be performed to prevent abscess rupture.
