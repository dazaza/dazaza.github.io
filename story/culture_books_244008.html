Due to the circumstances of life, Arakawa Ben and Iiwashimaru dropped out of high school and went together to Tokyo. After much hardship, they joined the Uesugi family, one of the two major families in Tokyo, becoming security guards at a high-end nightclub. The Takeda family and the Uesugi family used every means possible to vie for dominance and expand their influence. In this process, the sins on Arakawa Ben and Iiwashimaru accumulated more and more, but they had no other choice but to continue down this path with no return until death. Was all of this predestined, or was it due to a single misstep?

When they thought that one day they might leave behind the person they deeply loved in this world, or even cause her death because of themselves, would there be a trace of regret in their hearts?

Ben became a war general of the family, and his capable advisor Momoi Senza suddenly discovered that the brakes were missing while racing, causing him to fall off an overpass and die; on that race car, the police found 600 grams of methamphetamine and a modified FN "tactical" pistol. What kind of conspiracy lies behind all this?

Tortured by hatred and despair, Iiwashimaru drove alone into the grounds of the Uesugi family's residence, determined to fight to the death with Uesugi Kyuho. As bodyguards swarmed him, can Iiwashimaru escape this peril?

At the critical moment, Ben's first love, Asai Aoi, called to make an appointment to meet. Although he harbored some suspicions, out of lingering affection, he still decided to show up. Is this a call of love or a fatal trap?

Japanese Gangs - Part One

As the taxi door closed, the hustle and bustle of Shinjuku street came to an abrupt halt. What couldn't calm down immediately was not just Arakawa Ben's breathing, but also his thoughts. His head was still buzzing, as if rapidly contracting and expanding, as if it were about to explode. Sweat trickled down his forehead, and his long bangs disheveledly covered his frantic eyes. All of this happened too suddenly, even though it was somewhat expected, it still felt too sudden...

Japanese Gangs - Part Two

In his second year of high school, Arakawa Ben's mother, Miyoko, lost her sight. Koshima Masao was honest, but he was a man without ability, and household expenses were insufficient. During such difficult times, Koshima Masao became addicted to gambling. He thought it was the fastest way to make money, but soon lost all his savings. Feeling frustrated, he began drinking heavily and would beat Miyoko when drunk. Remembering Koshima's kindness, and lacking the ability to resist, Miyoko could only endure silently. Ben was often not at home and unable to protect his mother.
