Is Alimama really the second wife that Ma Yun keeps?

by fenqu on 2007-08-16 11:19:11

Is Alimama really the "second wife" secretly maintained by Jack Ma?

The Internet world has always known that Jack Ma's moves are unpredictable. He doesn't act easily, but when he does, it hurts. Recently, there have been rumors online that Alimama (www.alimama.com) belongs to Jack Ma's company. I was skeptical at first, but later I became convinced that Alimama is indeed Jack Ma's "secret second wife." Today, after checking the DNS, I found out it was registered in 1999. I've also discussed this with several friends, and we all agree that Alimama truly belongs to Jack Ma.

Recently, my own website (www.coko365.com) has been looking for advertisers, so I've been searching for advertising trading platforms, but haven't found a suitable one yet. The emergence of Alimama undoubtedly gives me some hope, though I'm not sure if it will truly help us find advertisers.

Why does Alimama's promotion resemble that of a small company, like gossip, while the Alibaba Group hasn't officially announced it? Back in the day, when Jack Ma entered the C2C market, he would send his troops first, then quietly develop, slowly release insider information, and eventually achieve today's Taobao. Now, as we carefully observe Alimama, it seems similar to how Jack Ma played his C2C card in 2003.

Regardless of whether Alimama is Jack Ma's secret "second wife," we can affirm that an advertising trading platform like Alimama is a much-needed platform in the current network, even though it's not a blank market, it is still a large market. Recently, I learned that Microsoft spent over $6 billion acquiring an online advertising company, while Yahoo! and Google both heavily invested in acquiring online advertising companies, clearly demonstrating the promising future of the online advertising market. If Jack Ma doesn't make a move now, when will he?

Regardless of which chess piece Alimama is in Jack Ma's strategy, analyzing solely from the inherent advantages and market vitality of the Alimama platform, we can see that China's Internet has developed for more than ten years, and websites have become increasingly closely integrated with traditional industries. Many traditional industries have recognized the advantages and value of the Internet, and their investment in online advertising has been increasing. However, many traditional enterprises struggle with knowing how to precisely place online ads, while small websites in China suffer due to difficulties in finding relevant advertisers. The emergence of Alimama undoubtedly solves these problems for traditional enterprise owners and small websites. As long as it can meet customer needs, such a website will certainly gain market and customer recognition. We look forward to seeing how Alimama develops.

Previously, during my graduation research, I learned about a similar advertising trading platform called Gonghe Network and registered as a member. They received venture capital funding, but their website required payment upfront before use, so I stopped using it later.

Looking at the products under Jack Ma's control: there's B2B (Alibaba), C2C (Taobao), online payment (Alipay), Yahoo! China, Ali Software (Aliwangwang and online software), and an advertising trading platform (Alimama). From these simple product lines, we can understand that Alibaba, considering its own strategy, strengths, and long-term strategy, is visionary. Currently, Alibaba is the best in the B2B field, and both online payment and C2C have become leaders in their respective fields, cooperating closely with each other. Only Aliwangwang is struggling, as QQ is not an easy opponent. However, in the current field of Alimama, there aren't any strong competitors. With Alibaba's own resources and advantages, it can definitely become the leader in this field. The most important point is that Alibaba has launched online software, a domain that is just starting in the Chinese market, with not much competition yet, and no powerful market leader formed. With Alibaba's resources and advantages, it can certainly become the market leader.

Jack Ma's "secretly maintained second wife" is indeed not simple, capable of bringing significant development opportunities to Alibaba. Alibaba now has the full capability to acquire advertising trading platforms similar to Alimama, but instead chooses to do it themselves. While I wouldn't say Alimama is a very beautiful "second wife," she is definitely not simple. I believe that under Jack Ma's guidance, her current flaws can be transformed into becoming the "Xi Shi of Zhejiang" among second wives!
