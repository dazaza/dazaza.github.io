A 14-Year-Old Discovers That iPad Smart Cover Magnets Can Halt Pacemakers

by geekzhang on 2013-05-10 18:02:45

Apple has included magnets in every iPad to facilitate the use of the tablet with its accessory, the SmartCover. However, a 14-year-old boy has discovered that when users place their iPads on their chests, the magnets inside the iPad may inadvertently cause pacemakers to stop functioning, especially during sleep or while lying down when it's common to rest devices on the body.

The 14-year-old who made this discovery is named Gianna Chien. Although her finding did not win first place at a science exhibition, she presented this significant discovery to 8,000 doctors at a Heart Rhythm Society conference in Denver. It’s worth noting that the father of this high school student is also a doctor.

This discovery is very valuable for people who already have cardiac pacemakers or other electromechanical generators implanted. When an iPad and its SmartCover are placed on the chest during sleep, the magnets could unintentionally cause the pacemaker to stop functioning. The head of relevant medical institutions believes this is something that deserves serious attention.

Under normal circumstances, due to the small size of the magnets in the iPad, no abnormalities usually occur. However, when placed on the chest, this issue can arise more easily. Chien's research found that if an iPad is placed on the chest, about 30% of pacemaker users will be affected by the magnets.

On the other hand, Apple had already considered this issue in the product guidelines: Apple advises keeping the iPad at least 15 centimeters away from pacemakers when it is turned on. However, most people probably haven't paid attention to this guideline, so it's fortunate that this teenager has now alerted everyone.
