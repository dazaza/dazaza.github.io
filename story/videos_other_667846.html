British couple have a daughter 11 years after freezing their fertilized egg (picture)

by anonymous on 2012-02-17 10:45:37

Twins Megan and Bethany from the Sheffield family hold their 11-years-younger sister. Photos of Megan and Bethany were taken right after they were born. How is it possible that though being fertilized at the same time, there's an 11-year age gap? According to the UK's Daily Mail on December 26, a British couple gave birth to two girls through IVF technology in 1998 and had the remaining fertilized eggs frozen. Eleven years later, they successfully used the preserved fertilized egg to have another daughter.

Mrs. Sheffield and her husband got married in 1994. They originally planned to have children immediately after marriage, but doctors found out that Mrs. Sheffield suffered from endometriosis and polycystic ovary syndrome, making the probability of natural conception quite low.

In 1998, the couple came to the Midland Fertility Hospital in the UK, hoping to use IVF technology to help them realize their dream of becoming parents. The hospital's doctor extracted a total of 24 eggs from Mrs. Sheffield's ovaries, and through testing, 14 of them successfully combined with Mr. Sheffield's sperm. After consulting with the couple, the doctor implanted two fertilized eggs into Mrs. Sheffield's uterus and froze the other 12 for preservation.

The 37-year-old Mrs. Sheffield said in an interview: "A week after the implantation, I went to the hospital for a check-up, and the doctor told me I wasn't pregnant, which made me extremely disheartened. However, when I went back for a second check-up the following week, the result showed that I was indeed pregnant." This female sales manager maintained good health throughout the pregnancy and eventually gave birth via cesarean section to a pair of twin daughters, named Megan and Bethany. "Though they were six weeks premature, both of them were very healthy. Three days after giving birth, the doctor allowed us to be discharged," Mrs. Sheffield said.

When Megan and Bethany turned 10, the couple wanted to have another child. In December 2009, they returned to the Midland Fertility Hospital, requesting the doctor to perform another IVF procedure using Mrs. Sheffield's 11-year-old frozen fertilized eggs.

Mr. Sheffield, a 45-year-old mechanical engineer, described the scene at that time: "We didn't know if it would succeed this time. We only let the doctor implant one fertilized egg, and if it didn't work, we would give up."

The doctor performed surgery on Mrs. Sheffield, and she was incredibly lucky to become pregnant again. In November this year, lively and adorable Laila Sheffield was born, weighing approximately 3.5 kilograms. Doctors and experts all believe this is a miracle: the fertilized egg that had been "hibernating" for 11 years still maintained such a high success rate.

Mrs. Sheffield joyfully said: "Laila looks exactly like her two sisters did when they were just born. Megan and Bethany know that this adorable younger sister comes from the same batch of fertilized eggs as them, just 11 years later. It's such a wonderful feeling, as if time has gone back 11 years."
