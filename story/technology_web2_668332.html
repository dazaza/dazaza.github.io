Business license Trademark similarity judgment

by gddingtian7c on 2012-02-17 17:02:13

Common types: 1. Approximate judgment of one character 2. Approximate judgment of two characters 3. Approximate judgment of three characters 4. Approximate judgment of multiple characters

First, we need to judge: 1. Whether it has distinctiveness 2. Whether it has meaning

One character with another character:

1. Same character shape and pronunciation, no meaning: approximate. For example, 辉 and 晖.

2. Similar character shape but different pronunciation, no meaning: approximate. For example, 戊 and 戌.

3. Different character shapes but same pronunciation, no or some meaning: not approximate. For example, 易 and 仪.

4. Different character shapes and pronunciations, with meaning: not approximate.

☆ 1. Adding "氏", "族", "老" etc. after a surname is approximate. For example, 李 and 李老.

2. Repetitive characters are approximate. For example, 亮 and 亮亮亮亮.

Two characters with two characters:

1. Same sound and shape, no meaning: approximate. For example, 长青 and 长清.

2. Same sound and shape, with meaning: not approximate. For example, 好哥 and 好歌.

3. Same sound but different shape, no meaning: not approximate. For example, 亮亮 and 凉凉, 星越 and 兴悦.

4. Different sounds but same shape, no meaning: approximate. For example, 酷多少 and 酷儿.

5. Different sounds and shapes, no meaning: not approximate.

☆ 1. Normally specified or meaningful phrases will not be judged as similar trademarks;

2. If the earlier one has no meaning while the later one does, they are generally not similar. For example, 红琪 and 红旗;

3. Same meaning and pointing to the same thing, different pronunciation and character shape, generally not judged as similar. For example, 荷花 and 莲花.

4. Chinese characters and pinyin are generally not judged as similar trademarks. For example, 鼎田 and dingtian.

5. Chinese trademarks, if the Chinese characters are the same but in different order, are generally judged as similar. For example, 鼎田 and 田鼎, 波斯帝卡 and 斯波卡帝. However, if the order is different and the things referred to are different, each having specific meanings, then they are not judged as similar.

6. Independent pinyin trademarks, reviewed according to English standards, causing consumer confusion, are generally judged as similar. For example, XUANMEI and XUANMEN.

7. Fully including others' previously registered trademarks, judged as similar. For example, 月圆三千里 and 三千里.

8. Trademarks composed of two or more absolutely independent parts, if the significant part is similar, judged as similar trademarks. For example, 出色生活爱丽丝 and 爱丽丝.

9. Adding adjectives or function words like big, small, good, bad, new, old, red, green, and, si etc., judged as similar. For example, 富翁 and 大富翁, 太阳 and 红太阳, 金鳄 and 金鳄王.

10. But if it's specifically referred to, then it's not similar. For example, 玫瑰 and 红玫瑰, 太阳 and 蓝太阳.

11. If the meaning is changed and the things referred to are different, not similar. For example, 白云 and 白云山.

12. If adding the above words changes the trademark's meaning, not similar. For example, 王中 and 王中王.

13. Simultaneously comprehensively compare the Trademark Office's prior review cases.

Three-character approximate judgment

1. Distinct differences in meaning, not similar. For example, 幸运数 and 幸运树.

2. Significant differences in overall appearance, not similar. For example, 高太丝 and 高泰斯.

3. First character different, not similar. For example, 迷派斯 and 舒派斯.

4. Adding words without distinctiveness or weak distinctiveness such as "东方", "南方", "世家", "一族", "派", "风" etc., judged as similar. For example, 家园 and 东方家园; but if the overall meaning differs, not similar, for example, 东方雪 and 东方雪狼.

Multiple-character approximate judgment

Mainly consider its distinctive part, such as "一江春水向东流", "天才第一步，雀氏纸尿裤", "广东鼎田知识产权代理有限公司".
