1. Adjust the showerhead water flow When using a showerhead, it's important to avoid using its maximum water flow. The reason is simple: the showerhead is a foreign product designed according to the body types and physical characteristics of people in Europe and America. If you use the maximum water flow, you will be subjected to the same pressure that a tall and robust European man can withstand. You may feel comfortable after one or two uses, but over time, excessive water flow can cause your dermal cells to tear, accelerating the aging process. It can also put too much strain on your spine, shoulders, and waist, affecting bone growth and causing deformities. For Asian women, using two-thirds of the water flow supplied by the showerhead is sufficient.

2. Tissue paper isn't better when it's whiter What kind of tissue paper is considered good? It's absolutely not the case that the whiter it is, the better! The original color of paper isn't pure white. If the tissue paper is overly white, it means that a large amount of bleaching agent or other chemical substances were added during the manufacturing process, which can cause unpredictable harm to the body. From a sensory perspective, good tissue paper appears relatively uniform in color, clean, with some wrinkles but evenly fine without holes. When choosing, you can also touch it with your fingers. If the tissue paper sheds powder or even fibers, don't buy it.

5. Wallpaper colors should be warm Regarding the bedroom, the color of walls and wallpaper is particularly important. First, it's not recommended to choose too heavy colors like green or bright yellow. Bright colors can make the cerebral cortex nerve cells continuously active, possibly leading to insomnia and even altering your personality. Traditional white walls are the safest choice, but white is a distance color. If you and your partner live in the same room, too much or overly bright white can easily create a sense of estrangement between you, especially after a big argument. In the bedroom, the best color is beige mixed with a little light red or light pink.

6. Big clocks can cause insomnia Does the clock in your bedroom have a ticking sound? I repeatedly suggest not placing too large a clock in the bedroom, even if it's far from your bed. The ticking sound belongs to extremely low-frequency audio, but this frequency continuously broadcasts and still affects your sleep quality, possibly disrupting the secretion of melatonin during sleep. Many patients with sleep difficulties have been troubled by insomnia for a long time, later discovering that the solution to their insomnia was very simple and consistent: removing the clock from their bedroom.

7. Fresh flowers Most flowers absorb carbon dioxide and release oxygen through photosynthesis during the day, but at night it's exactly the opposite. Many people often complain to doctors that they feel short of breath while sleeping, which might be related to the fresh flowers on the windowsill competing for oxygen with you. Therefore, it's suggested to place some flowers in the bedroom during the day, but remember to move them away at night. Of course, Aloe Vera is an exception because it's the only plant that releases oxygen 24 hours a day. Additionally, for families with children, some flowers need extra attention. Orchids can keep children's brain nerves continuously excited, making it hard for them to fall asleep, while Paulownia flowers might trigger pediatric asthma.
