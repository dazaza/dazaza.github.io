Google's Amazing Return on Investment

In August 2004, Google conducted its initial public offering, selling 22.5 million shares at $85 per share. Many people believed they had a talent for investment; perhaps they bought real estate in Miami Beach, or purchased hot mining products like copper, or invested in other companies going public. Over the past three years, if you had bought property in Miami, you could have seen a 34% return on investment; if you had bought copper, you would have gained over a 178% return, which was undoubtedly a good result.

According to data published by Thomson Financial, since Google’s IPO, the three most successful IPO companies were the securities market operator Intercontinental Exchange (ICE), shoe manufacturer Crocs, and outdoor advertising network provider Focus Media, with returns on investment of 415%, 367%, and 358%, respectively. However, none of them could surpass Google in terms of investment return. Despite a lack of growth since mid-year, as of August 16th, each share of Google stock was still worth $491.52, representing a 478% increase from its IPO price.

Even Better Than the Best

Despite this, Google wasn't the stock with the highest return on investment over the past three years. By analyzing the Russell 1000 Index, BusinessWeek found 11 stocks whose returns over the past three years exceeded Google's. Of course, these stocks wouldn't always stay on the "fast track." In the week before this article was published, BusinessWeek discovered three more stocks whose returns surpassed Google's. However, since last Monday, the entire U.S. stock market has been sluggish. Due to their stock prices falling faster than Google's, SBA Communications, General Cable, and Guess Jeans were removed from the list.

Among the companies whose stock investment returns exceeded Google's, many are energy-related enterprises. Thanks to a significant rise in oil prices, the stock prices of these companies also increased correspondingly. For example, Frontier Oil directly benefited from the rise in oil prices, with a three-year return on investment of 611.5%; the metal giant Titanium Metals indirectly benefited, with a three-year return on investment of 1023.2%.

Surprisingly, among the companies whose stock investment returns exceeded Google's, there were two "century-old establishments." One of these companies is Foster Wheeler, an old construction company based in New Jersey. Due to an unfavorable real estate market, the company shifted its focus to energy, pharmaceuticals, and environmental industries. Over the past three years, Foster Wheeler's stock return on investment reached 557%. Another company is McDermott International in Houston, where the continuous growth in demand for subsea drilling equipment caused its stock to achieve a three-year return on investment of 827.6%.

Even within the technology sector, Google isn't the company with the highest return on investment. For instance, MEMC Electronic Materials, a silicon wafer manufacturer in Silicon Valley, had a three-year return on investment of 530.9%, while graphics chip manufacturer Nvidia achieved an impressive 627.7%. Moreover, thanks to the strong sales of products like iPod, iTunes, Mac, and the excitement generated by iPhone, Apple's stock price rose by 637.6% over the past three years.

The Biggest Winner Over the Past Three Years

Although the aforementioned companies were very successful, the biggest winner over the past three years was actually a relatively unknown small company — NutriSystem. This company primarily provides weight loss and health services. Thanks to restructuring measures launched in 2003 by Chairman Michael Hagan, the company's stock price surged nearly 3000% over the past three years, achieving a return on investment six times that of Google.
