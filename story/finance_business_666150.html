The theory about collagen in the skin: Collagen is the main component of the skin, accounting for 72% of the skin and 80% of the dermis. In the skin, collagen forms a dense elastic net that locks in moisture and supports the skin. The loss of collagen causes the elastic net supporting the skin to break, leading to atrophy and collapse of skin tissue, resulting in dryness, roughness, looseness, wrinkles, enlarged pores, dullness, spots and other signs of aging.

What is deionized water and what is its function?

Answer: To meet the high stability and good performance requirements of cosmetics, there are two requirements for the water used in cosmetic production, including the concentration of inorganic ions and microbial contamination. The inorganic ions in the water need to be removed to reach pure water standards, reducing the salt content to below 1mg/L, meaning the conductivity needs to be reduced to 1~6s/cm2. The production water should ideally be processed and used immediately without microbial contamination.

Hyaluronic acid (HA) is mainly extracted from human umbilical cords, rooster combs, and bovine vitreous bodies, and is widely used in medicine. It has unique moisturizing properties and is effective for skin rejuvenation, anti-aging, wrinkle reduction, and inflammation relief.

Natural Moisturizing Factor (NMF) refers to water-soluble substances with adsorption properties in the stratum corneum of the skin. These substances play a role in retaining moisture in the stratum corneum. The main source of moisture in the stratum corneum comes from sweat secreted by the sweat glands, and the sebum film on the skin surface prevents moisture evaporation, thus maintaining the moisture contained in the stratum corneum of the skin.
