Jeremy Lin teaches you "how to get into Harvard"

by tctdn9d4 on 2012-02-22 16:37:20

NBA rising star Jeremy Lin [http://www.tctd.net/index.php?action/tag/tagname/%C1%D6%CA%E9%BA%C0], not only excels at basketball, but also has outstanding academic results. He aspired to attend the University of California [http://www.tctd.net/index.php?action/tag/tagname/%B4%F3%D1%A7], but unfortunately chose Harvard [http://www.tctd.net/index.php?action/tag/tagname/%B9%FE%B7%F0].

Jeremy Lin [http://www.tctd.net/index.php?action/tag/tagname/%C1%D6%CA%E9%BA%C0] is currently one of the brightest new stars in American NBA. The most popular term in the NBA now is LINSANITY (Lin fever), derived from the English word INSANITY (crazy). At the age of 23, Jeremy Lin emerged like a thunderclap, rising from a player who only played during garbage time to become the MVP (Most Valuable Player) at the basketball mecca Madison Square Garden after just three games. Reports indicate that after becoming famous overnight, Jeremy Lin broke through traditional American [http://www.tctd.net/html/4/category-catid-4.html] perceptions: Harvard [http://www.tctd.net/index.php?action/tag/tagname/%B9%FE%B7%F0] isn't just a place for bookworms who prioritize academics; this time, Harvard has produced a [sports star].

Jeremy Lin graduated from Harvard University [http://www.tctd.net/index.php?action/tag/tagname/%B4%F3%D1%A7], majoring in economics and minoring in sociology. However, Harvard wasn't his first choice. In 2006, Jeremy Lin helped Palo Alto High School win the state championship in the California Interscholastic Federation Division II league, but he didn't receive any scholarships from Division I schools. When Lin was about to graduate from high school, he sent his resume and game highlights DVD to all Ivy League schools, including Stanford University, UC Berkeley, and his dream school, UCLA. However, these schools showed little interest in his background. Eventually, only Harvard and Brown offered Lin a formal position on their teams, but neither was willing to provide an athletic scholarship. Lin ultimately chose Harvard, making him one of the most academically accomplished players in the current NBA.

Although Harvard University is globally renowned, it hasn't achieved much in basketball. There have been only three students in its history who entered the NBA. During his college years, Jeremy Lin became a star player for the school's basketball team and was selected for the Ivy League First Team in his senior year. However, at a school like Harvard, you can't just rely on sports achievements to obtain a degree. Lin already earned his economics degree from Harvard University. During his time there, he also worked as an editor for the school newspaper and interned with a California [http://www.tctd.net/html/03/n-8203.html] senator.

Harvard does not recognize "athletic talents"

In high school, Lin had a GPA of 4.2, and his SAT (Scholastic Assessment Test) score was nearly perfect. By the 9th grade, he had completed the equivalent of advanced mathematics AP courses in China [http://www.tctd.net/index.php?action/tag/tagname/%D6%D0%B9%FA]. Since Ivy League schools in the United States [http://www.tctd.net/html/4/category-catid-4.html] do not offer special admissions for athletes, Lin was admitted to Harvard solely based on his excellent academic performance.

During last year's NBA lockout, Lin made a video and uploaded it to the internet, titled "How I Got Into Harvard." His explanation for this topic was as follows: "Many of you have asked how I got into Harvard. Now that I have no life [http://www.tctd.net/index.php?action/tag/tagname/%C9%FA%BB%EE] or job [http://www.tctd.net/index.php?action/tag/tagname/%B9%A4%D7%F7], I decided to create a short video explaining it in five basic steps: 1. Get glasses; 2. Learn to play an instrument, but since many people play the piano, try something unusual; 3. Improve test-taking skills; 4. Learn by doing and practice makes perfect; 5. Stay informed about current events."

As one of the top universities in the U.S., having excellent grades and standout personal abilities are crucial factors for successfully [http://www.tctd.net/index.php?action/tag/tagname/%B3%C9%B9%A6] applying [http://www.tctd.net/index.php?action/tag/tagname/%C9%EA%C7%EB] to prestigious institutions. American study abroad [http://www.tctd.net/liuxue.html] consultants stated that some of Lin's suggestions on how [http://www.tctd.net/index.php?action/tag/tagname/%C8%E7%BA%CE] to apply [http://www.tctd.net/index.php?action/tag/tagname/%C9%EA%C7%EB] to Harvard make sense. American study abroad [http://www.tctd.net/liuxue.html] consultants suggest reading more newspapers and going online to understand professional [http://www.tctd.net/index.php?action/tag/tagname/%D7%A8%D2%B6] knowledge. Having a comprehensive understanding of your discipline can significantly help your application and future studies.

How to prepare for a Harvard interview?

Additionally, students applying to Harvard typically need to go through an interview process. During the interview, it's important to focus on the following points and prepare thoroughly:

Have your own perspective. Harvard mentors will tend to understand your views on courses to see if you fit to study at Harvard. This process tests whether you've seriously studied your chosen field. Share your unique insights, such as what deficiencies exist in your chosen field within today's research scope or how your field impacts future societal development. Remember, no mentor likes generic answers.

Give yourself a mock interview. You can seek help from teachers and friends who are knowledgeable about your field but don't know you well. This helps you express yourself better in unfamiliar environments. After repeating several mock interviews, your fear and nervousness about the actual interview will decrease significantly.

Regarding attire, attending a Harvard interview isn't like going to a dance party, so avoid dressing too formally because mentors won't be dressed overly formally either. On the day of the interview, simply dress neatly and cleanly. (By Li Qiong)
