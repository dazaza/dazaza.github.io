English Education for Accounting Major in Higher Vocational Colleges - Thesis Publication Service

by hylw113k8l5r on 2011-06-30 09:14:02

Honesty

Abstract: With the internationalization of the economy and the deepening of globalization, domestic enterprises are gradually growing, which also puts forward higher requirements for English teaching in accounting majors. Based on the author's teaching practice over the past three years, five methods of professional English teaching in accounting are proposed from the aspects of teaching concept, teaching method, and areas that need improvement in teaching.

Keywords: Accounting, English teaching, teaching methods, internationalization of the economy, and deepening of overseas business of enterprises in globalization.

In this new situation, higher vocational colleges put forward higher requirements for accounting major students to become highly skilled and applied talents. They must master a certain amount of English knowledge and possess corresponding business processing abilities. The professional English course in accounting is precisely aimed at meeting this demand, with improving the overall quality of accounting major students as its starting point, improving the knowledge structure of students, and enhancing the goal of opening professional English courses and their professional skills. Since its establishment, the course has achieved certain results and accumulated some experience, but there are still some shortcomings. Based on my teaching practice over the past three years, I have seriously summarized the following teaching suggestions.

For a long time, the concept of English teaching in the accounting major has been updated, and English in the accounting major has never been given enough attention due to various reasons. I believe the key lies in insufficient emphasis on teaching concepts, especially the most prominent issue being fewer class hours and overly simple evaluation methods, which cannot guarantee the realization of teaching objectives. Nowadays, most institutions schedule only two hours per week, which is relatively insufficient for the rich content of English teaching in the accounting major. In terms of teacher shortages, schools can simply address it; evaluation methods often involve one test type, scoring based on written exams without strict requirements, leading many students to not take the course seriously, thus significantly affecting the teaching process. Therefore, to adapt to current needs and improve the English ability of accounting major students, we must fundamentally change our teaching concepts and look ahead from the perspective of global economic integration and future development. We must give sufficient importance to English classes in the accounting major, ensure sufficient class hours, and strengthen evaluation methods continuously.

To continuously improve the quality of English teachers in the accounting major, as teachers ourselves, we must continuously enhance our own qualities under the ever-changing economic conditions. On one hand, we should strengthen the study of theoretical knowledge, continuously expand our knowledge base, enrich and improve our knowledge, and make necessary adjustments to the curriculum. At the same time, teachers should emphasize practical experience because the accounting major is an operationally strong discipline. Different enterprises, industries, and countries have different practices in accounting. Therefore, teachers must value the accumulation of practical experience through methods such as practice and training, studying, and enriching their accounting practical handling capabilities.

The selection of English teaching materials for the accounting major is relatively simple, lacking interactivity and practicality [1]. I believe that the choice of Western accounting textbooks should be closer to the original and more fully meet international standards. Other materials should possess strong operability and applicability.

Through teaching practice, after comparison, I find that the "Higher Vocational Education Talent Training Materials" series published by Higher Education Press meets the needs of English teaching in the accounting major of junior colleges [2]. Carefully selected materials and finished versions introduce new knowledge and skills in today's international accounting development, emphasizing the connection between theory and practice. Based on case studies, the textbook allows students to repeatedly practice large amounts of accounting, highlighting the training of students' application skills. Although the preparation of textbooks, thinking, reasoning, narration methods, exercises, homework, and other links are very different from foreign original textbooks or others, these differences do not affect teaching and learning but instead stimulate students' curiosity and desire to learn, encouraging them to learn through means such as the internet and increase their interest in learning English and accounting.

Improving teaching methods and means, continuously enhancing teaching quality. In the past, the teaching methods lacked vitality in terms of quality policy, making it difficult to engage students' interest and provide sufficient information for each category, failing to meet teaching needs. If you use a large number of rich materials with traditional teaching methods and teaching tools, it is almost impossible to rely on them to accomplish tasks, resulting in poor teaching outcomes. Therefore, I made the following adjustments: Modern teaching equipment like multimedia has significant advantages, with modern teaching facilities providing a large amount of information, sound, graphics, and text-rich content, vivid and true-to-life, able to meet teaching needs.
