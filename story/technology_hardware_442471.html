Chapter Seven: Beidaihe Early Summer (Part 1)

The weather was getting hotter, which puzzled Li Qingyuan who grew up in Jiangnan. "Isn't Beijing in the north?" he wondered. Luckily, their dormitory was on the tenth floor, so mosquitoes couldn’t get that high.

It was another weekend. Yang Duo and Wang Xian had gone back to their parents' home, Wen Chunhua had just visited her uncle. Although final exams were coming up next week, Lan Ruoshui and Xiao Qing couldn't focus on their textbooks. Instead, they found themselves staring at the words as if they were reflecting their youthful beauty.

"Do you want to watch a movie tonight?" asked Lan Ruoshui.

"What movie?" Xiao Qing replied.

"Haidian Theatre is showing 'Youth Without Regret' directed by Zhou Xiaowen."

"Zhou Xiaowen? That's good," Xiao Qing remembered liking 'The Price of Madness,' also directed by Zhou Xiaowen from Xi Film Studio during her middle school days.

"In that case, I'll call our friendly dorm to see if they want to join us?"

Wei Yuan answered the phone and enthusiastically agreed. He then went back to inform the others.

"Sure," Cao Su especially liked actress Wu Yujuan from 'The Price of Madness.' He was curious about who the leading actress would be in Zhou Xiaowen's new film.

"I watched the premiere of 'Youth Without Regret' in the grand auditorium early spring. You missed it, which is really too bad. The leading actress Shi Lan was dazzlingly beautiful. Sigh, she’s still young and charming. But Shi Lan is too beautiful; today's female stars can only play her maidservant. Shi Lan also came to Peking University on the day of the premiere. I remember she wore jeans but still looked radiant," Li Qingyuan quickly said.

"Lan Ruoshui is dazzling too," Wei Yuan teased intentionally. Both girls ignored him.

This week, Cao Su was on duty. On his way down the building, he met Han Bei, a fellow student from Kunming, in the school's hot water room. Han Bei was all in white — an ivory-colored dress, pure white high heels, and a pale neck. Her usual lost look was like a dreamwalking woman in a white dress. Only the pink ribbon in her hair and her slightly red lips could wake someone from the dream.

"Is it your turn to fetch water?" Cao Su stammered.

"Can you help me?" Han Bei softly said, not daring to meet Cao Su's eyes.

"Sure." Cao Su took the kettle from her without hesitation.

"Thank you." Her voice was as soft as a mosquito’s. Han Bei didn’t know why, but recently she couldn’t raise her voice in front of him anymore, even during class when she used to sit beside him, though they were from the same hometown.

A few female classmates smiled at her while fetching water. Han Bei blushed and quickly followed Cao Su out of the small room. She instinctively leaned towards him, but Cao Su had both hands full with two kettles, making it impossible to get closer.

Cao Su chattered away, but she didn’t hear a word, lost in her own world.

"Will you join us to watch the movie later?" It was Cao Su’s words that brought her back to earth.

"Sure, I'll go with you," Han Bei agreed without asking what movie it was.

After calling Wei Yuan, the three rode westward. By the time they reached Haidian Theatre, Lan Ruoshui and Xiao Qing were already waiting at the entrance. Lan Ruoshui was dressed in a purple dress, while Xiao Qing wore a yellow dress with floral patterns. Han Bei stared at the two younger girls as Wei Yuan leisurely introduced them.

Just as the five found their seats in the theatre, the lights went out, and soon everyone was captivated by the plot except for Han Bei,

Sitting next to Cao Su, her mind was restless. Several times she wanted to move closer but didn't dare, especially in front of the other two younger girls.

Han Bei cursed them silently in her heart, but being able to watch the movie with him was satisfying enough. After all, he hadn’t forgotten her even when watching movies with other girls.

Wei Yuan sat next to Lan Ruoshui and Xiao Qing. Though the scent of their perfumes was very light, he still felt somewhat uneasy. What he didn't know was that at that moment, Lan Ruoshui's heart was racing, unable to settle.

After the movie, the group called for many snacks at the Zhongguancun Night Market near the main gate of Peking University. Seeing the stir-fried snails, Lan Ruoshui from Suzhou became excited, losing her usual composure. All five forgot about the upcoming final exams next week.

One evening a week later, Li Qingyuan, who had already finished his exams, was tidying up materials in the lab and planning for experiments after the summer vacation. He was alone on the second floor, the tranquility free from the oppressive heat of the summer night. Suddenly, the phone in the corridor rang, breaking the silence.

"Hello, who is this?" Li Qingyuan answered absentmindedly.

"Li Qingyuan? It's me, Yang Duo," came her familiar tone. The bird-like whisper made him feel uneasy but also provided some comfort like a mother's reassurance.

Yang Duo's voice accompanied Li Qingyuan ever since. Even if months or even a whole year passed without hearing her, her voice waves penetrated his body like a birthmark imprinted on his heart.

"Yang Duo, where are you?" Li Qingyuan raised his voice.

"I'm at school. I've just finished my last exam tonight. I want to celebrate. Will you come with me to Beidaihe?"

"To Beidaihe? Did you tell your family?"

"If you don't want to go, I'll find other classmates," Yang Duo said nonchalantly.

"Alright, let me think about it," Li Qingyuan cursed Yang Duo in his heart: "This girl is truly unruly."

"There's a train to Qinhuangdao at eleven-thirty tonight," Yang Duo insisted.

"Tonight?" Li Qingyuan shouted.

"There are less than three hours left. Come pick me up," Yang Duo glanced at her wristwatch and hung up immediately, her palms sweating.

When Li Qingyuan arrived at Yang Duo's dormitory, she had already packed her things and made a mischievous face at him. Li Qingyuan had no choice but to take her bag and head downstairs.

After buying the tickets at Beijing Station, there was still half an hour before departure. Li Qingyuan said, "Sit down, I'll buy some food."

"It's too late. The stores are closed."

"Should we starve?"

"Don't worry, I have some in my bag. You won't starve."

The night train car was quite empty. The two sat by the window. Though they had known each other for three months, this was the first time Li Qingyuan had stared at her so closely.

Yang Duo wore a pale pink dress with small flowers, a long skirt paired with a short-sleeved lace collar blouse. Her short ponytail was tied with a pale pink clip behind her round face and neat bangs, resembling a Republican-era student, only more vibrant in her pale pink attire.

Being stared at by him made Yang Duo feel nervous and shy. Quickly, she said, "Let's eat something," and stood up to grab her bag.

Though the blouse tightly hugged her chest, it couldn't conceal the youthful charm of an eighteen-year-old girl right in front of him. Li Qingyuan took a deep breath and stood up, saying, "Let me get the bag." Thus, the two stood back-to-back under the luggage rack, the baby-like faint fragrance making him flustered, as if time had frozen.

"Have some water first," Yang Duo's soft voice broke the suffocating silence between them.

"Thank you," Li Qingyuan finally woke up.

"Here's a novel for you to read," her small hand extended toward him.

"Thank you," the same two words again.

Li Qingyuan had just flipped through a few pages when Yang Duo's hand reached out again, "Put the book away, put the book away."

"But I've just started reading."

"A few lights in the carriage have been turned off. Your eyesight will worsen if you continue." Yang Duo added, "When I was a child, I used to distribute books to people in the carriage, and once I gave them all out and collected them back right after."

"When you were a child? How old were you then to read novels?"

"They were picture books, like 'The Story of Yue Fei.'"

"Oh, moral stories," Li Qingyuan, fearing she might feel embarrassed, continued, "The pictures were beautifully drawn."

That night, Yang Duo kept talking. First, she was free, completely detached from her parents, who didn't even know she wasn't at school. Second, she was happy to be with the lean and tall Li Qingyuan. Additionally, she was a bit scared because it was her first time traveling with a boy, and she concealed all her feelings by chatting.

Yang Duo was still young, and soon her eyes began to close, but her talkative mouth wouldn't stop. Li Qingyuan stood up, pulled out a single garment from her luggage, and draped it over her, feeling for the first time like he had a younger sister.

By morning, they had arrived in Qinhuangdao. Li Qingyuan, who hadn't slept all night, took Yang Duo to a city guesthouse not far from the train station. They booked two rooms. After entering her room, Yang Duo immediately closed the door, her heart fluttering like a little rabbit, both happy and vaguely worried about how she would explain to her mother when she returned to Beijing.

Li Qingyuan entered the opposite room and fell asleep as soon as he lay down on the bed.

Chapter Eight: Beidaihe Early Summer (Part 2)

Li Qingyuan woke up around noon. After washing up, he walked across to knock on Yang Duo's door. She responded but took a little time to open it.

"You woke up early. Why didn't you wake me up? Haven't you eaten breakfast yet?" Li Qingyuan spoke quickly.

"It's almost noon. I've just woken up too. You didn't sleep on the train, so how could I disturb you?"

Yang Duo sounded a bit resentful.

"Alright, thank you. Let's go, we'll eat breakfast and lunch together," Li Qingyuan coaxed.

The streets of Qinhuangdao were clean, with rows of poinsettias planted along the center of the road, looking quite impressive. Yang Duo changed into a dark red dress today, with irregular black fine lines scattered on the dress, giving a sense of imagination. Walking side by side, the elegant fragrance from her continuously wafted over to him, making him feel a bit constrained. Fortunately, the roadside snack shops relieved the tension.

They sat down at a small table and ordered dishes, naturally including small seafood. They started chatting casually. Soon, the first dish was ready, and Li Qingyuan, looking at the large plate in front of him, exclaimed, "So much!" The waitress serving the dish turned back in confusion. Yang Duo covered her mouth and giggled secretly, thinking how anyone would complain about too much food.

Although both had skipped breakfast, they couldn't finish all the dishes they had ordered. After returning to the hotel, they each grabbed their swimsuits and headed to Beidaihe located in the western part of the city center.

After passing many sanatoriums and hospitals, they arrived at Beidaihe Beach. The flat sandy beach, soft and level, was an excellent natural seawater bathing spot. Yang Duo quickly took off her white sandals, holding one sandal by its thin strap, joyfully running towards the sea, not forgetting to shout back, "Hurry up." Then she threw her shoes on the ground, lifted her skirt with one hand, and stepped barefoot into the water, revealing her smooth white calves. Li Qingyuan was even simpler, wearing only beige shorts and a bright yellow undershirt.

The two waded towards the large rocks near the beach where the crowd gathered. The rocks of Beidaihe were uniquely shaped, evoking thoughts. A few seabirds perched on the rocks added some vitality. The slope of the beach was gentle, but the deeper the water got as they neared the rocks, the higher Yang Duo had to lift her skirt. When it reached above her knees, she couldn't reach the rocks. Thinking it would be inappropriate to go further, she turned back and shyly told Li Qingyuan who was following closely behind, "We should change into our swimsuits."

"Alright, let me rent a life ring first," he replied. Then they went ashore, rented a life ring, and proceeded to the changing rooms. Li Qingyuan quickly changed into a blue swimming trunk, but Yang Duo took much longer to come out. Li Qingyuan aimlessly gazed at the vast sea.

"Did you wait long? I applied some sunscreen," came a gentle voice nearby.

"No problem, I just..." Li Qingyuan turned around while speaking, but froze mid-sentence. For sixteen years, the image of Duo in a bright yellow one-piece swimsuit, her innocent shyness, has lingered vividly in his mind. The ruffled top of the swimsuit could no longer hide her blossoming youth.

"Let's go," Yang Duo's face flushed red, after all, it was her first time wearing a swimsuit alone with a boy.

"Alright," Li Qingyuan turned around so she wouldn't notice his embarrassment. At that moment, Li Qingyuan realized she was no longer a little girl.

Although the waves were not big, Yang Duo couldn't swim and could only play in the shallow water with the life ring. Li Qingyuan had just learned to swim and swam a few laps near her.

Soon, they reached the rocks. Yang Duo was extremely delighted, climbing wetly onto the rock and sitting comfortably to enjoy the seaside atmosphere. She patted the space beside her invitingly, acting like a little girl.

Under the blazing sun, they stayed for a while. Yang Duo nudged Li Qingyuan and went back into the water. The rubber life ring was large, and lying on it with her body arched backward, she seemed carefree. Li Qingyuan pulled the rope attached to the life ring and swam further into the water. As people gradually decreased, Yang Duo talked softly to him while looking at the sky, occasionally cooling off in the seawater, then lying back on the life ring. Time flew by unnoticed.

The surface of the sea started to ripple with the wind, and the waves followed. Yang Duo was thrilled, while Li Qingyuan guided her back to where she could touch the bottom. As they neared the shore, the waves grew larger. Yang Duo was unexpectedly overturned by a wave and clung tightly to Li Qingyuan next to the life ring, as if holding onto a lifeline, although the water was only chest-deep. Li Qingyuan quickly reassured her, but Yang Duo was still frightened. After a long while, she calmed down. Just as she regained her senses, she realized she was still tightly hugging him and blushed, quickly letting go. Reflecting on it, she started sobbing lightly. Li Qingyuan anxiously asked, "What's wrong? Did you swallow water?"

Yang Duo remained silent, thinking how she, a grown-up girl, had just pressed her chest against his, though unintentionally, it was her first time, filled with annoyance and anger, yet also a hint of sweetness. Seeing Li Qingyuan's anxious expression, she shyly said, "No, I just can't swim."

Li Qingyuan was preoccupied with comforting her. Now, observing her wet, beautiful, and full figure, the softness and elasticity he had felt when she hugged him earlier made him momentarily dazed. He quickly took a few steps forward and suggested to Yang Duo, "Let's go ashore, sit for a while, and then go shopping, alright?"

"That would be great." Hearing the word "shopping," Yang Duo's spirits revived, and she forgot all about her earlier embarrassment. During that shopping trip, Yang Duo received her first gift from him: a shell carved with a butterfly.

Chapter Nine: Beidaihe Early Summer (Part 3)

After changing back into summer clothes in the locker room, the two headed towards the city center. Evening approached, and tourists were returning to the shore, crowding the roadside stalls selling small handicrafts. Shells, starfish, sea snails, corals, and more dazzled the eye. Li Qingyuan picked up a large sea snail and asked Yang Duo what she thought.

"Hmph, when I was a kid in my aunt's house in Qingdao, we used to collect sea snails ourselves on the beach."

"How old were you when you started collecting shells yourself?"

"Five years old." Yang Duo looked proud.

"Only five? Such a prodigy! I don't even remember anything from when I was five," Li Qingyuan teased her.

"My uncle took me. I lived in Qingdao for a year, speaking fluent Shandong dialect. My grandmother loved me so much."

"Then tomorrow at low tide, you can take me to collect shells."

In the blink of an eye, Yang Duo had checked out five or six shops. Li Qingyuan noticed a desk lamp made from small shells.

"If you look closely, it's not delicate enough and breaks easily," Yang Duo dismissed it cleanly.

Her response left Li Qingyuan speechless, and he simply followed her around.

After a while, Li Qingyuan's steps grew heavier, and he gently reminded her, "Are you hungry?"

"Thanks for your concern," her gentle and graceful tone left him speechless.

At the end of the market stalls, a crowd had gathered. Li Qingyuan's legs were starting to ache, but Yang Duo quickly squeezed through the crowd. Turning back, she said, "Come check out this shell." Before he could respond, she tugged his arm and pulled him inside. Li Qingyuan gave a wry smile to the tourists pushed aside. The brown-purple shell was indeed beautiful, polished and carved with a butterfly. Li Qingyuan couldn't take his eyes off it and quickly bought it.

Leaving the market, on the way back to the hotel, there were many food stalls. The seafood was fresh. They ordered some shrimp and clams, which the stall owner tossed into the boiling pot nearby for a quick boil. The freshness was delicious, and Li Qingyuan couldn't help but recall fishing for shrimp along the wooden rafts on his way home from school during his middle school days.

As dusk fell, tourists returned to the beach in groups. Yang Duo and Li Qingyuan joined the crowd, walking leisurely along the beach. Yang Duo was happy. She had known him for three months, and they had danced and visited parks together. But this was the first time she felt free from the constraints of their friendly dorm activities, as they were away from Beijing, and the air seemed fresher and more enchanting.
