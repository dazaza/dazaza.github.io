Citizens discover out-of-print 3-yuan Renminbi note, experts estimate its value to be over 20,000 (photo)

by zzf000zxys6 on 2011-06-17 14:32:01

3 Yuan Denomination Banknote

February 21st, Southeast Network - Strait Herald (Our reporter Zhu Huang / text Liang Zhanglei / photo) A 3 yuan denomination banknote? Probably many people will be half-believing and half-doubting after hearing about it. Ten years ago, Xiao Chen from Dingxu Village in Guankou discovered such a banknote while cleaning out his grandfather's cabinet, and was also greatly surprised.

Yesterday, the reporter of the Strait Herald saw this 3-yuan banknote collected by Xiao Chen. "It is a relic left by my grandfather, the elderly person never mentioned it, and I guess he stored it because of its unusual denomination." Xiao Chen said that he does not have any interest in collecting, and hopes to transfer it.

The banknote is light green in color, slightly larger than the current 1 yuan RMB in circulation. On the front side, there are the words "SAN YUAN" in traditional Chinese characters at both ends. Below, it indicates "1953" as the year, with "People's Bank of China" written above. In the middle, there is a picture of the Longyuan Kou Stone Bridge in Jinggang Mountain. The floral border around the stone bridge is dark green, and the central background pattern is yellow. On the back of the banknote, there are patterns of flowers and the national emblem, with texts in four languages: Han, Uyghur, Mongolian, and Tibetan.

"It is the only 3-yuan denomination RMB in the history of Chinese currency, belonging to the second set of RMB, and was personally nominated for issuance by Premier Zhou Enlai," introduced Mr. Ding Jiannan, President of the Xiamen Collectors Association. At that time, China's printing technology was still very backward, so the three denominations of 3 yuan, 5 yuan, and 10 yuan banknotes were printed on behalf of China by the Soviet Union.

After circulating in the market for several years, this 3-yuan RMB also witnessed a financial war: due to the deterioration of Sino-Soviet relations, the Soviet side used the printing plates in their possession to continue printing and released them into our territory through the Xinjiang and Northeast borders, disrupting the financial order in our country. The People's Bank of China was forced to announce the recall of the 3-yuan, 5-yuan, and 10-yuan RMB, requiring holders to exchange these three denominations for other denominations at the bank within one month, or they would become invalid. At that time, people's income was still very low, and after seeing the announcement, most immediately went to the bank for exchange. Few banknotes scattered in society have now become collectibles for coin collectors.

"The latest quote in the current collection market shows that this 3-yuan banknote can be sold for more than 20,000 yuan," said Ding Jiannan.
