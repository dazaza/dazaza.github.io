Yagor is looking forward to becoming a representative brand of menswear for the post-80s generation.

by zhenwu5566n on 2012-02-08 10:20:47

In 2008, the group sincerely invited Mr. Tanaka Saburo's team to take charge of brand planning and design, jointly creating the emerging brand GY. Afterwards, Mr. Tanaka led the W-GAME team in participating in the brand planning and product planning development of the main YOUNGOR brand. During more than three years of cooperation with the group, Mr. Tanaka and his team were deeply involved in all aspects of the YOUNGOR and GY studios, contributing much effort and wisdom to the growth of these two brands. As we bid farewell to the Year of the Rabbit and welcome the Year of the Dragon, we had the opportunity to interview Mr. Tanaka. His interview provided us with a new perspective on interpreting the changes in these two brands over the past year, and his suggestions and opinions on brand operations are worth our consideration and reference.

**Question (Q):** In terms of audience targeting, do you think there is a certain gap between the overall positioning of these two brands and their actual market operations? If so, in what areas is it mainly reflected?

**Answer (A):** Over the past two years, our collaboration with YOUNGOR has mainly been in brand planning, focusing on product planning development and fashion menswear design. We have not been heavily involved in terminal operations, so we don't have much say. However, we value store inspections. In 2011, we visited 15 cities and inspected 120 YOUNGOR stores. From my personal experience, the changes in YOUNGOR over the past two years are quite noticeable: In terms of branding, it has gradually transitioned from being production-oriented to brand-oriented; in products, it has shifted from focusing on individual item development to emphasizing the overall feeling of the entire series, paying more attention to product development strategies and early planning, demonstrating more originality and autonomy. These are all positive changes worth celebrating.

However, there is a slight difference from our expected level of anticipation: the cultural connotation or overall atmosphere of the brand is not yet fully embodied. The VMD (Visual Merchandising Design) in the stores has not effectively interpreted the products and increased sales. We look forward to the improvement of the terminals.

Additionally, what disappoints us is that some regional marketing companies lack the courage to try new products, tending to be conservative. This easily leads to the loss of sales opportunities and growth points. For example, the plaid velvet surface shirt launched in the autumn and winter of 2011 was very popular in actual sales, but many marketing companies ordered small quantities during the initial order phase, missing out on good sales opportunities.

Behind the conservative ordering at the marketing frontline lies the "diagnosis" of the consumption characteristics of YOUNGOR's existing target consumer groups. These groups have insufficient sensitivity to fashion trends, leaning towards classic and traditional menswear found on Taobao, so sometimes a product can sell for two to three years. However, with the rapid development of society, the ideological concepts and aesthetics of these groups are slowly changing. They are also beginning to be willing to try new things and accept new trend information. When they have the courage to embrace new changes, but we lack the courage to try, there will inevitably be a time lag between the two, leading to missed sales opportunities.
