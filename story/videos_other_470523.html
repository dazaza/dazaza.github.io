Wongtech may be out of the Nuclear High Base project, and its CEO has quietly resigned.

by tuoqing1188 on 2011-08-04 10:04:13

Sina Technology News, afternoon message on August 3rd: More than a month after several enterprises received the "special funds allocation letter for core projects", Yongzhong Software still hasn't made any progress in the core project. According to informed sources, Yongzhong Software will likely not receive the special funds.

At the end of June this year, Kingsoft Office Software,中标 Software, Dalmen Database, and Renmin University Golden Warehouse and other companies successively announced that they had received the approval letters and special funds allocation letters for the core projects. However, Yongzhong Software, which applied for office software-related topics at the same time as Kingsoft Office Software, has yet to receive approval.

Yongzhong Technology was registered and established on January 24, 2000, in Wuxi City's Administration of Industry and Commerce. It is a company specializing in the research and development of Office software. Due to financial difficulties in operations, in 2010, Yongzhong Technology invested 10,000 yuan together with Huasoft Investment Wuxi Partnership Enterprise, which invested 25 million yuan, to establish the new company Yongzhong Software. Yongzhong Technology granted the intellectual property rights it held for use by Yongzhong Software and eventually obtained 51% equity. In the same year, the application subject for the core projects was changed to Yongzhong Software.

In October last year, Cao Can, the founder of Yongzhong Technology (Weibo), criticized the illegal establishment process of Yongzhong Software and accused Yongzhong Software of applying for and misappropriating core project funds. However, in April this year, Fang Cunhao, chairman of Yongzhong Software, told the media, "Yongzhong Technology and Yongzhong Software completed the change of the application subject for the core project under the premise of complying with regulations."

A relevant person from Yongzhong Software, when asked by Sina Technology today, stated that by the end of July, the company had not received any related approvals, but the application work for the core project was still ongoing. The person said that there were "no major issues" with the company receiving government approval.

However, Cao Can refuted the above statement. Cao Can, when connected by Sina Technology, stated that the establishment of Yongzhong Software was illegal, and the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology (MIIT) had already indicated that it would not allocate special funds to it. Relevant staff members of MIIT refused to disclose related information.

While the progress of the core project was not smooth, Sina Technology also learned that Chen Yong, who joined Yongzhong Software last year as CEO, had quietly resigned recently, and Yongzhong Software's operations have now been taken over by Wang Yuanbing, acting president of Yongzhong Software. (Liang Luo)
