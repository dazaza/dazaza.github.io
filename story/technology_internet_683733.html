The division of responsibility for housing allocation is reflected in several aspects.

by sgba1jm1nf on 2012-02-29 15:40:26

The division of responsibilities for housing allocation is reflected in the following aspects:

(1) The principal responsibility of individuals in housing distribution. The key to housing reform is to transform the main body of housing responsibility in the reform of the housing distribution system. It is necessary to clarify that employees and their families bear the main responsibility for housing issues. Under the condition that employee wages include housing consumption, establishing employees as the primary responsible party for solving housing problems is conducive to changing perceptions, overcoming employees' dependency on the government and their units, motivating employees to actively solve housing issues, and increasing personal investment. It also helps adjust consumption structure, increase the proportion of housing consumption, expand the sources of investment funds for housing development, accelerate residential construction, encourage residents to buy houses, promote the development of the residential and real estate markets, [http://www.nsj6gjj.com/] thereby promoting economic growth, and ultimately improve residents' housing conditions and living standards.

(2) The duty of enterprises is to manage wage distribution that includes housing consumption. The reform of the housing distribution system aims to free enterprises and institutions from the heavy social burden of housing and withdraw them from the status of being the main responsible party for housing, clarifying their original responsibilities for wage distribution. Enterprises complete the distribution process as long as they pay out sufficient wages that include housing consumption.

(3) The role of the government is to conduct macro-control of the housing market and implement housing social security for low-income earners. Housing issues have both economic and social security characteristics. Governments around the world raise social funds, provide fiscal support, and offer affordable housing to low-income groups, which is known as the so-called subsidized rental housing policy. This is one of the social functions of the government. Additionally, certain home purchase preferential policies are adopted for middle and low-income groups. Currently, China develops affordable housing for middle and low-income groups.
