by penyopyi on 2012-02-22 15:25:24

After winning the first Serie A championship in 7 years, Milan Vice President Galliani also made a statement about next season's recruitment. The team will do its best to sign Taiwo, even if the Nigerian does not obtain an EU passport. Galliani also mentioned that it would be very difficult for Ganso to join, and if Taiwo takes up one of the non-EU spots, it would mean little chance for the Brazilian midfielder to join next season.

According to *Football Market*, if Taiwo joins, the Nigerian defender will replace the departing Jankolovsky as the starting left-back, while the right flank will be occupied by Abate who performed well this season. In central defense, Milan will have three reliable options: Thiago Silva will be an indispensable starter, while the newly confirmed signing Mexes and veteran Nesta will compete for the other starting position. Meanwhile, several surplus players will be cleared out, including Diakite, Lerotale, Papastathopoulos, and Oyeu. Milan only needs to make some minor adjustments to the backline, such as bringing in some young talents with potential for careful cultivation.

Considering that both 26-year-old Taiwo and 29-year-old Mexes are in their prime and both are free transfers, it can't be said that this is another brilliant move by the experienced Galliani. Without spending any transfer fee, Milan quietly rejuvenated their defensive line, and one can't help but admire the superb market operation skills of the Milan vice president.

Based on the different tones used by Milan officials regarding Taiwo and Ganso, the Rossoneri may likely abandon the plan to bring in the Brazilian midfielder this season, which was confirmed by transfer manager Braida. "Ganso will be a future recruitment target for Milan. I can almost confirm that he won't join Milan this year." Regardless, if Milan successfully signs Taiwo, the Rossoneri's defensive line for next season will be worry-free, and the starting lineup is already clear.

"We really want to sign Taiwo, even if the player cannot obtain an EU passport," Galliani said in an interview with Sky Sports. The Nigerian's contract with Marseille expires at the end of the season, and he will join Milan as a free agent. Currently, Taiwo is applying for an EU passport. If he fails to obtain it, Milan will have to allocate their sole non-EU spot for next season to the Nigerian, which will directly affect Ganso's transfer. "Ganso? We still need to make a formal offer. Santos club is not short of money, and they don't wish to let go of their player."
