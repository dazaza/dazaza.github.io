Death toll rises to 105 in India's toxic alcohol incident

by kaxiya on 2009-07-10 16:01:13

According to the "Central News Agency", in Gujarat province of India where prohibition was implemented, another tragedy of poisoning by fake wine happened recently. By 10 a.m. on the 10th day, the death toll from poisoning over the past four days had continued to increase to 105 people, including a fake wine manufacturer.

The spokesperson's office of Ahmedabad police said that more than 100 other poisoned people were still receiving emergency treatment in hospitals, and it could not be ruled out that the death toll might continue to rise.

The police also said that at least 1200 "illegal liquor dealers" were arrested on the 9th day.

This number declared by the police cannot be verified by other aspects, but Shaktisinh Gohil, the leader of the opposition party Congress Party in Gujarat Provincial Assembly accused on the 10th day that the public cooperatives controlled by the ruling party Bharatiya Janata Party were suspected of shielding the buying and selling of fake wine, which was the main reason why illegal liquor dealers were still rampant in Gujarat Province although prohibition had been implemented since 1960.

Gohil complained at the same time that he had requested to propose a discussion on the rampant problem of fake wine in the Parliament, but was rejected by the Speaker Bat of the Indian People's Party.

The fake wine poisoning incident started on the evening of the 5th day. In several villages near Ahmedabad, there were successively reports that villagers felt unwell after drinking wine. Then they were sent to the hospital for emergency treatment on the second day (the 6th day), but began to die one after another on the 7th day when the emergency treatment failed. According to local media, after the news of fake wine poisoning spread, people in other areas of Gujarat Province continued to buy illegal liquor.

Local residents blamed the government and the police for their failure in banning fake wine. For two consecutive days, crowds took to the streets, surrounded government agencies and police stations to protest, burned vehicles and the straw image of Shah, the Minister of Internal Affairs of Gujarat Province, and threw stones at the police, forcing the police to disperse the crowd with batons and tear gas.

After the death incident, the police have intensified the investigation into the source of fake wine, and six senior local police officers suspected of failing to ban illegal liquor effectively had been suspended. However, although the police said that 1200 illegal liquor dealers had been arrested, they still failed to find the source of fake wine, which has aroused doubts and condemnation from all sides.
