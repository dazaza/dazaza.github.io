How to Choose a Graphic Designer? Graphic design is an essential component of every marketing plan. It can significantly enhance the professional appearance of your business or express your brand's creative style. From the very first glance, it will leave an impression. Finding the right design company or freelance designer can be a bit overwhelming if you don't know what to look for. I am a freelance designer, and I could simply end here by saying you should use me. However, I would rather you choose me only after you are informed and know what you need to know.

Portfolio:

Every designer must have a portfolio, both printed and online. This is how they market themselves, by showcasing their work. The better the designer, the larger the portfolio will be. It may contain work they have created for themselves or just for fun, but it should primarily consist of work they have done for their clients. Review a designer's portfolio and see what they have done, and more importantly, see if the work they have done matches the type of work you are looking for. If you see wild designs for bands but need a corporate marketing package, you should probably keep searching.

Quality:

If you're looking for a graphic designer, it means you're looking to have something done correctly. Make sure that when reviewing a portfolio, you see professional-quality graphics. Graphics that effectively convey their intended message. If you see poorly assembled images or basic clip art graphics being used in a portfolio, then that is likely what you will receive as well.

Price:

Price is a significant concern for everyone. You could say "You get what you pay for" or you could say "You get more than you could have hoped for." Getting the lowest price does not always mean receiving the least amount of work. If you choose to work with a large company, expect to pay more. If you opt for a freelance designer, expect to pay less. The best way to determine if the price is right is to check the portfolio, then request a free quote. Compare the quote to the quality of work in the portfolio. If you see good work at a good price, you have found a good designer.

Background/References:

After you have reviewed the portfolio and are satisfied with the price and quality, investigate the background of the company or designer. If you intend to complete more than one project or are looking to start a long-term project, it is crucial to check how long the designer has been in business and whether they have any other long-term repeat clients. This way, you can ensure they will be available for the duration of your project and any future needs you may have.

Response Time:

It is important that a designer responds to your requests and inquiries as soon as possible so you know you are important to them. If they take days to respond to your email or voicemail, they are either too busy to work with you or are simply not very professional. Either way, they are not the right choice for you.

Efficiency & Clarity:

Make sure the designer is able to understand what you are looking for. Nothing is worse than having a mental picture of what you need, only to receive samples that are so different that you wonder what went wrong and need to explain it again. A good designer will read your needs, confirm everything with you, and ask questions or provide suggestions before any work begins.

These are all important points to consider when choosing the right graphic designer. It may take some time to find the right one, but if you do your research, you will get the right product for the right price from the right designer.
