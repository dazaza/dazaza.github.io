Man refuses to pay the bill because of the high price, and the shop owner was run over and killed when he asked for his money back

by lovei0c1 on 2012-03-06 14:01:46

Nanfang Daily News - Report by Liu Hui Long, intern Huang Zuo Yuan, and communication by Chang Gong Xuan: In order to recover over a thousand yuan for a meal, Yan Kanggen, the owner of a "Spicy Fish Restaurant" in Changping Town, was run over by a man who had eaten without paying. Yan later died from his severe injuries. This incident occurred at 5 AM on February 23rd on Tian鹅hu Road in Changping Town, Dongguan.

**Case Recap**

Refusal to Pay Due to Expensive Bill

Yan Kanggen, aged 59, from Chongqing, ran a spicy fish restaurant with his daughter Yan Lin Cui on Tian鹅hu Road in Changping. Around 2 AM on February 23rd, more than 20 people came to Yan Kanggen's restaurant for a late-night snack. They occupied two tables, among them were two police officers from the Changping Police Station. The group toasted and drank merrily until around 5 AM when they left, leaving two men behind to settle the bill. That night, the group consumed over a thousand yuan worth of food. Upon seeing the bill, the two men suddenly claimed, "It's too expensive, there must be a mistake," and then said in unison, "We'll pay tomorrow." Despite multiple requests from Yan Lin Cui, the two men refused to pay. Eventually, one of the men started a Toyota car with the license plate "粤S899B3" and prepared to leave.

Seeing this, Yan Lin Cui rushed forward to block the car, but the car started moving, causing her to fall heavily. Yan Kanggen, witnessing the scene, immediately climbed onto the hood to stop the man from leaving. What happened next shocked everyone. The man in the car, upon seeing Yan Kanggen on the hood, did not get out but instead stepped hard on the gas pedal, throwing Yan Kanggen off the hood to the ground. After an emergency brake, the man restarted the car and ran over Yan Kanggen again. Subsequently, the driver sped away, leaving Yan Kanggen lying in a pool of blood. Yan Lin Cui quickly sent her father to the hospital for emergency treatment, but Yan Kanggen eventually succumbed to his severe injuries at Changping Hospital.

**Latest Developments**

Suspect Arrested in Hunan

After the incident was reported by Nanfang Daily, due to the involvement of two police officers from the Changping Police Station and the suspects remaining at large, it caused strong public attention and was taken seriously by the local government.

On the afternoon of yesterday, the Changping police announced that under the guidance of the Municipal Public Security Bureau, after meticulous investigation, the special task force responsible for the "February 23rd" intentional injury case successfully arrested the fugitive suspect Zhou Guo (male, 30 years old, from Dao County, Hunan Province) at a rented house in Lutang Town, Beihu District, Chenzhou City, Hunan Province, with the assistance of the Chenzhou police. Thus, both suspects involved in the "February 23rd" intentional injury case have been apprehended, and the suspects confessed to their crimes.

**Suspects' Confession**

Two Officers Unrelated to Case

Regarding the heated discussion about the police officers dining with the suspects, the Changping police provided a clear response: After preliminary investigation, at around 3 AM on February 23rd, suspect Zhou Guo and suspect Pan Hong were having a late-night snack at the Exclusive Spicy Fish Restaurant located at No.1 Lijing New Garden on Tian鹅hu Road in Changping Town. During this time, Pan Hong met a friend named Zeng sitting at another table and went over to toast him. At around 5 AM, Zeng's table finished eating and went to the front desk to settle the bill. Pan Hong voluntarily approached Zeng and asked to take care of the bill, which Zeng repeatedly declined but eventually agreed to due to social etiquette. Subsequently, Zeng's group of six people left the Exclusive Spicy Fish Restaurant.

According to the confession of suspect Pan Hong, there were six people at Zeng's table that night at the Exclusive Spicy Fish Restaurant. Pan Hong only knew Zeng and did not recognize the other five people, nor did he know that there were two police officers among Zeng's group. When settling the bill, Pan Hong felt the price was too high and, being drunk, requested to settle the bill the next day after sobering up. The shop owner disagreed, leading to a dispute. Without settling the bill, Zhou Guo ignored the obstruction of the shop owner's father, Yan, forcibly drove away, resulting in Yan falling off the hood and sustaining injuries, after which he fled. After their arrest, suspects Pan Hong and Zhou Guo confessed that the case had no relation to Zeng and his friends (including the two police officers), and expressed deep apologies for any impact caused to the police officers.
