People's Daily Online, Beijing, December 4th (reporters Chang Hong and Zhang Haiyan) - To celebrate the 10th anniversary of Macao's return, a symposium titled "Commemorating the 10th Anniversary of the Implementation of the Basic Law of the Macao Special Administrative Region" was held this morning at the Great Hall of the People. Zhang Xiaoming, Deputy Director of the Hong Kong and Macao Affairs Office of the State Council, stated in his speech at the symposium that the Basic Law has played an important role in Macao's prosperity, stability, and development.

Zhang Xiaoming expressed that during the practice of "one country, two systems," "Macao people governing Macao," and high autonomy, the Basic Law has played an extremely important role in many aspects. The Basic Law legalizes and specifies a series of policies of the Chinese government towards Macao. It is crucial in guiding Macao's society to comprehensively, accurately, and systematically understand and implement the "one country, two systems" policy and successfully advance its practice. The Basic Law defines the relationship between the Central Government and the Macao Special Administrative Region, establishes the political system of the Macao Special Administrative Region as well as basic systems and policies in economic, social, cultural, and other areas. It provides the basis for correctly handling the relationships between the Central Government and the Special Administrative Region, various internal relationships within the Special Administrative Region, and external relations of the Special Administrative Region. Moreover, it lays down the track for the orderly operation of the Macao Special Administrative Region government. The Basic Law not only maintains national sovereignty but also stipulates Macao's high degree of autonomy, residents' basic rights and obligations, and ensures the protection of private property rights and foreign investment according to law. It also allows Macao to formulate tourism and entertainment industry policies based on local overall interests, providing a solid legal guarantee for maintaining Macao's long-term prosperity, stability, and development.

It is worth noting that many provisions of the Macao Basic Law are formulated under the guidance of the "one country, two systems" policy, taking into account Macao's actual situation. For instance, in areas such as land system, legal system, definition of permanent residents, civil service system, eligibility requirements for the Chief Executive, principal officials, members of the Executive Council, President of the Legislative Assembly, President of the Court of Final Appeal, Prosecutor-General, methods of producing the Chief Executive, composition and methods of generating the Legislative Assembly, judicial institution settings, tourism and entertainment industries, civil aviation, protection of Portuguese-descended residents, and respect for cultural customs, the provisions of the Macao Basic Law differ from those of the Hong Kong Basic Law, showcasing Macao characteristics.

The Macao Special Administrative Region government and all sectors of society strictly adhere to the provisions of the Macao Basic Law in practicing "one country, two systems," actively exploring development paths suitable for Macao's actual conditions, and forming some good experiences and practices. For example, the political system and its operations of the Macao Special Administrative Region well reflect the provisions of the Basic Law. As a local administrative region directly under the Central People's Government, the political system of the Macao Special Administrative Region differs from the colonial rule system where the governor had absolute power during the Portuguese administration period and also differs from the "parliamentary supremacy" or "separation of powers" in Western countries. Instead, it is an administrative-led system with the Chief Executive at its core, which is determined by the status and responsibilities of the Chief Executive representing the Special Administrative Region and being accountable to the Central People's Government as stipulated in the Basic Law. Under the leadership of the Chief Executive, the executive, legislative, and judicial organs divide responsibilities and work together. The executive and legislative organs both check and cooperate with each other while the judiciary exercises its judicial power independently. Correctly understanding and grasping the characteristics of this new political system and properly handling specific issues arising during actual operations strictly in accordance with the Basic Law is one of the key experiences for the good operation of the political system of the Macao Special Administrative Region.

Zhang Xiaoming summarized that the achievements made in the past ten years since Macao's return are inspiring, and the accumulated experiences are invaluable. Although some problems and shortcomings have emerged during the development process, they do not overshadow the achievements. We, along with the vast majority of Macao residents, are full of confidence in "one country, two systems" and the great motherland, and we are also full of confidence in Macao's future!
