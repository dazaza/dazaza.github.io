How credible is the claim that a director was reported for offending someone?

by piaoc7529 on 2012-02-08 22:47:09

The author does not deny Zhang Mo's youthful audacity and daring spirit, and can understand that rebellious teenage characters sometimes need more guidance. Looking back at Zhang Mo's previous resume, the eccentric Zhang Mo himself once admitted to offending quite a few people, most of whom were heavyweights in the industry. In one interview, Zhang Mo said he was very serious about his work, even for a bad play, he would still put in 100% effort. However, because of this personality, he offended many directors. One statement by Zhang Mo is very thought-provoking: "They won't say it to your face, but after shooting, they will tell others I am difficult to deal with, and in the future, they will not hire me for any plays. But I have no way to explain myself, so I have been feeling very frustrated these years, because everyone has already formed a fixed impression of Zhang Mo." Does this mean Zhang Mo also admits having conflicts with some directors? I once saw a video clip where Zhang Mo gave an exclusive interview and openly criticized "a certain director as terrible, who doesn't understand plays" in front of everyone. So, which director is Zhang Mo talking about? If there really is a conflict between them, and Zhang Mo is indirectly criticizing the director, how could the director not be angry? There are too many intrigues and unbelievable things in the entertainment circle. If Zhang Mo's drug use was maliciously reported, I still believe this theory. This is not to say that Zhang Mo's drug use is acceptable; of course, the sooner he quits drugs, the better. Finally, here is Zhang Mo's original statement: "You clearly know how bad that director's directing is, but you still have to say how good this play is. Isn't that deceitful? I can't force myself into an extremely unwilling state and act like some actors who get excited when they see reporters. I don't want to see the hypocritical faces of these people. Acting is acting, we shouldn't lower our standards or please others just because of activities and promotions. Your dignity will be lost." Therefore, Zhang Mo has a unique personality and his own way of thinking. As for drug use, as long as he quits in the future, it will be fine. Content.
