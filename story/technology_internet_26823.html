Why is Alibaba stepping into the online software field?

by fenqu on 2007-08-07 22:56:21

Why does Alibaba want to enter the online software field?

Author: Tian Keshan MSN: fenqu@hotmail.com

Regardless of what Alibaba will be like in the future, at least for now, Alibaba is doing the best in both C2C and B2B fields as well as online payment. However,慧聪网, 万国商业网, 买麦网 and other websites mainly focusing on B2B are far behind Alibaba. Also, QQ which entered the C2C field did not perform as well as Alibaba, whether it was Paipai.com or Tenpay. Recently, I saw that QQ entered the online software field, and I was very happy at that time because I am currently working on an online financial management software - Caikè Online (www.coko365.com). This information at least indicates that QQ or large websites or giant companies have realized the future development trend and market gap of online software. But Alibaba has taken a half-step ahead of us, establishing Alibaba Software Company in 2006 (www.alisoft.com), with the main task being the development of online software. Currently, Alibaba has developed online software targeting online shop owners, foreign trade companies, and domestic small and medium-sized enterprises based on its own business needs. However, similar B2B websites domestically or large portal sites or industry giants have not yet entered this field. Is it because they don't see potential in this field? No, it's because they haven't realized the advantages and huge market space of online software. In fact, Google has already entered this field, but its actions are not yet obvious. The domestic online software market is still in its infancy, starting in 2005, slowly developing in 2006, and rapidly developing in 2007. Currently, the more powerful online software belongs to Alibaba's software company?

So why does Alibaba want to enter the online software field?

Firstly: Does Alibaba want to indirectly compete with traditional single-machine software giants?

Currently, there are many software giants domestically and globally, all of them developing single-machine software, such as Microsoft, Kingdee, and Yonyou, etc. They are software giants in their respective fields, but they are still small players in the online software field. Therefore, Alibaba, with tens of millions of SME customers, this ready-made customer resource, if not utilized, would be wasted. If utilized, selling single-machine software alone wouldn't be competitive enough against Yonyou, whether from technology, software functionality, market promotion, or software influence and brand. However, Alibaba goes against the tide, grasping the future development trend of software by entering the online software field, indirectly competing with international and domestic software giants through online software. It avoids the hard injuries of competition. At the same time, it grasps the development trend of software, seizing a blank market in the future. Alibaba's strategy is extremely forward-looking.

Secondly: Is online software the future development trend?

Just comparing single-machine software and online software, we find that online software has longer-term development and benefits compared to single-machine software, having more competitive advantages. However, the domestic online software market is not developing well domestically, still in its early stages or even a blank stage. So first-mover advantage equals business opportunity. Alibaba chooses online software, seeing its market prospects and current market gaps, also recognizing the advantages of online software. It can be said that it truly grasps the future development trend of software. Whoever grasps the trend undoubtedly grasps the market initiative and profit source.

Finally: From the customer's perspective, online software can provide Alibaba customers with more comprehensive services?

After burning tens of millions of US dollars, Alibaba must start making profits. During the process of burning money, it accumulated brand, reputation, fame, etc., most importantly accumulating customer numbers and customer resources. So how to convert customer numbers into benefits? Undoubtedly, it requires additional services and products to increase profit sources, and software happens to be an important profit point. Why? Because 99% of Alibaba's customers are SMEs, and these enterprises will definitely need software products more and more. However, they cannot afford legitimate single-machine software, so they hope to use low-priced software. Online software just meets this demand, not only being low-priced but also being user-friendly, more convenient than single-machine software, and having features. It conforms to the actual needs of SMEs. Therefore, online software is an important strategic layout for Alibaba's future profit sources.

Alibaba's B2B, C2C, and online payments have all developed very maturely and have been well promoted and hyped, except for AliSoftware, which hasn't been heavily promoted or hyped. This is quite an interesting issue. Why? Because Alibaba's online software is still in its immature stage, still in its early development stage, so they dare not boldly tell others about what they are doing. On one hand, they fear more competitors entering, and on the other hand, they fear that the product is not mature enough. Excessive promotion bringing usage volume would more likely ruin Alibaba's brand in the short term. Regardless of how Alibaba does it, Alibaba's entry into the online software field definitely has its own reasons, at least indicating that online software is a big market in the future, the future development trend of software, and currently a blank market.
