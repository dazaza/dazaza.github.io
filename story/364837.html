Not  adidas boat  but rather insightful analysis from renowned teachers — After the college entrance examination concluded, well-known teachers within the province conducted a concentrated review of this year's test questions. In the eyes of these teachers, relying solely on "rote memorization" would be insufficient to handle this year's test questions; the key lies in understanding and application. Meanwhile, this year’s questions effectively bridged the new curriculum reform, benefiting future candidates in seeking some "Sichuan models."

Chinese Language: Innovation amidst the commonplace

Evaluator: Zhu Dan (Distinguished High School Chinese Teacher of Sichuan Province, Head of the Chinese Department at Sichuan Normal University Affiliated High School, Member of the Chengdu Senior High School Chinese Center Group, Committee Member of the Sichuan College Entrance Examination Guidance Committee)

Zhu Dan's first impression of this year's Chinese language test was "refreshing," reflecting the trend of aligning with the new curriculum standards during the transition period.

In terms of genres, this year's expository reading comprehension has expanded from scientific and technological expositions to social science expositions. Classical Chinese texts have reverted from last year's narrative-commentary essays back to biographies. Ancient poetry appreciation has shifted from continuous years of ancient poetry appreciation to Song Ci appreciation. Modern literary works reading comprehension has broken the long-standing genre selection model, expanding from foreign novels tested last year to foreign prose this year.

Creative Thinking: The essay prompts candidates to make associations from geometry's points, lines, and surfaces, self-proposing titles, choosing genres, and determining themes. It requires candidates to start from contemplating the properties of "points" in geometry, either linking to certain aspects of social reality or connecting to personal life experiences, where divergent thinking may lead to new insights. This reflects the integration of arts and sciences, guiding candidates to expand the use of Chinese into the natural sciences field.

Mathematics: No Biased or Strange Questions, Including the Final Question

Evaluator: Li Yong (Chengdu Shude High School, Distinguished Mathematics Teacher of Sichuan Province, Head of the Mathematics Department)

This year's mathematics test questions, for most candidates, were considered relatively difficult. However, from Li Yong's perspective, the difficulty was moderate. The exam did not include any biased or strange questions, and the solutions to even the final question utilized only conventional thinking and basic methods.

Some questions originated from textbooks or could find their prototypes in the textbooks, such as the 13 questions in the science section and the 16 questions in the liberal arts section, particularly noteworthy being that the prototype of question (12) in the science section is question 3 on page 31 of the second volume (upper) of high school mathematics.

However, the exam was not easy to score high marks on. Often, the initial questions of various types were easy, but the final questions were more difficult. Most solution questions were easy to start but completing them was not simple.

History: Not Testing Rote Knowledge, But Historical Thinking

Evaluator: Liu Songbai (Senior Teacher at Sichuan Normal University Affiliated High School, Head of the History Department, Part-time Researcher of Jinjiang District High School History, Member of the Chengdu History Center Group, Member of the Sichuan New Curriculum Reform Expert Committee)

This year's history paper broke away from the misconception of overemphasizing rote memorization in historical studies, focusing on cultivating students' historical thinking abilities.

For example, the fourth part of question 37 asks candidates to discuss their views on "Premier Wen Jiabao's statement that the government should allow people to live with more dignity," and to cite historical facts to support their arguments, emphasizing the ability to use historical facts to substantiate one's viewpoint. Additionally, questions 14, 19, 20, 21, the second and third parts of question 37, and the fourth part of question 39 significantly strengthened the examination of candidates' levels of historical thinking, processes, and methods.

Moreover, this year's questions also embodied a strong "Sichuan flavor." For instance, the "Ningjiang Machine Tool Factory Advertisement" question (question 18) examines candidates' understanding of the pioneering and innovative spirit of Sichuan during the early stages of economic reform through an advertisement placed by Ningjiang Machine Tool Factory in the People's Daily on June 25, 1979. This is a major characteristic of the Sichuan history paper in examining local history.

From recent years' history questions, Liu Songbai believes that Sichuan's history test questions have developed their own model and style.

Geography: Understanding the Social Value of Geography

Evaluator: Zhou Jieming (President of Sichuan Normal University, Professor, Member of the Ministry of Education's Higher Education Institutions Geography Teaching Guidance Committee)

This year's test questions clearly reflect the social value of geography. For example, "the latitude difference of sunrise time, seasonal distribution characteristics of temperature" reflect the spatiotemporal concept of geography; "the influence of urban heat circulation on suburban pollutant migration" reflects the concept of interconnectedness of geographical phenomena; "comparison of environmental characteristics between Northeast China Plain and Central Asia region" reflects the concept of overall and differential geographical environment; "comparison of ancient and modern functions of Hangzhou city" reflects the concept of development and change of geographical phenomena; "the role of wetlands in improving and protecting ecological environment" reflects the concept of sustainable development, etc. These are excellent guides for future candidates.
