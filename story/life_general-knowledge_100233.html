"Three Tricks" for Mobile Internet to Avoid High Expenses

by hexiaoyao on 2008-11-01 21:26:27

In today's society, the Internet and mobile phones are becoming increasingly closely linked to ordinary people, and mobile Internet access, which connects the two, has become popular among consumers, especially young people. While enjoying the convenience of mobile Internet access, consumers must also bear the corresponding costs. Although most mobile Internet access is charged according to data usage, operators have also launched some packages that include a certain amount of free Internet access. However, users must choose the specified access point to enjoy the package discount. The phenomenon of "high" Internet fees caused by selecting the wrong access point or excessive data usage occurs from time to time.

Internet packages are quite cost-effective

College students can be said to be the group that accepts new things the fastest. At the Mobile Business Hall on Nanlu Qianfeng Road, Xiao Wang, a student at Taiyuan University of Technology, told reporters that now many students use their mobile phones to log in to QQ and MSN for online chatting, and there are even some "wealthy" students who play games on their phones. In addition to college students, "white-collar workers" also frequently use their mobile phones to read news and check stock prices... It can be said that the powerful network brings convenience and speed to consumers through the mobile phone platform, but at the same time, the issue of fees cannot be avoided. As Xiao Wang said, more students dare not try "this convenience" because they are worried about the high fees for mobile Internet access. For this reason, operators have launched some Internet discount packages for consumers with Internet needs.

The reporter learned from several mobile business halls in Taiyuan that many mobile fee packages now include 5M (megabytes) of mobile Internet traffic per month. Customer service personnel also introduced that they can adjust the package services according to the personal needs of consumers, increasing or decreasing the traffic.

Choose the right Internet access point

For ordinary consumers with mobile Internet needs, choosing a package that includes a certain amount of traffic is indeed more cost-effective. But this does not mean that you can be worry-free when surfing the Internet, because different discount packages have different limits on Internet traffic, and any excess traffic will still be charged according to the original fee standards.

Mr. Du, a citizen, said that not long ago, when he was handling a free incoming call service, the customer service personnel recommended a mobile fee package with 5M of Internet traffic to him. Because "at the time, I didn't pay attention to listening, nor did I ask much." Later, Mr. Du tried using his mobile phone to surf the Internet several times, and soon received a text message informing him that he needed to pay for his phone as soon as possible. After checking at the business hall, Mr. Du was surprised to find out that he had chosen the wrong access point while surfing the Internet, resulting in the unused free traffic in the package, and instead added more than 30 yuan in regular Internet fees.

The reporter randomly interviewed several mobile Internet users at several mobile and Unicom business halls in Taiyuan and found that many people have experienced high Internet fees due to choosing the wrong access point. It is understood that when using a mobile phone to surf the Internet, users can choose access points such as cmnet and cmwap to connect to the network, and operators will mark the corresponding access points for the included free traffic in the package introduction. If the user chooses incorrectly, additional Internet fees will be charged separately.

Internet traffic can be controlled

Since mobile Internet is still a new thing, the reporter found during interviews that many consumers are not very clear about the concept of "traffic" and do not know how to view or control their own Internet traffic. In response, customer service personnel from operators introduced that consumers can use the built-in data traffic recording function on their mobile phones, or install specialized traffic recording software on their phones to control the traffic.
