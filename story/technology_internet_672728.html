On Tuesday morning, after breaking through the hourly chart resistance, gold reached as high as $1760 per ounce in the evening. On Wednesday morning, it consolidated around $1755. From the daily chart perspective, the single-day increase exceeded $20, forming a large bullish candle without upper shadows. After consolidating for over half a month previously, the price of gold finally saw a breakout, and volatility is expected to increase significantly in the coming days. Short-term trading opportunities will become more apparent. Gold Insight [http://www.octopus63.com] suggests investors pay close attention to the performance of gold prices.

After the European finance ministers' meeting reached an agreement on the second Greek bailout plan, the European stock markets did not respond with a significant rally. This was mainly because investors had already anticipated the positive news quite fully, and the final Greek debt write-down will inevitably result in losses for private investors.

However, apart from Greece, there are several other factors recently that are beneficial to gold: Firstly, abundant liquidity. The People's Bank of China will cut the reserve requirement ratio by 0.5 percentage points starting from last Friday, marking the first reduction this year. Although this unexpected cut highlights the central bank’s intention to promote credit and stabilize growth, and does not imply a comprehensive easing of policy, when combined with the recent shift from raising interest rates to cutting them by central banks in Brazil, India, and others since the second half of last year, even China's slight action has triggered optimistic expectations in the market. Moreover, considering situations like the European Central Bank's refinancing operations and the decline in Italian government bond yields [http://www.octopus63.com/plus/list.php?tid=12], we can see that global liquidity has been improving since the beginning of the year. This is also why gold prices have been steadily rising without any significant correction.

Secondly, the Iran issue has been overlooked by many investors. Iran has warned that it will suspend crude oil supplies to multiple European countries, leading to a steady rise in oil prices recently. Although some analysts claim that oil prices are already overvalued, before seeing a retreat in crude oil futures, this factor should still be seen as supportive for gold prices.

In addition, the world's largest gold exchange-traded fund (ETF) - SPDR Gold Trust increased its gold holdings slightly by 0.02% on Tuesday compared to last Friday, adding nearly 10,000 ounces.

Yesterday's gains in gold could be considered a short-term one-step completion. The $1762 area represents the high point of the previous rebound, and the gold price quickly rose to this position. One could say that the segment of the market from breaking out at $1734 to reaching $1760 was the easiest and safest part to profit from. However, whether it breaks through $1760 carries some uncertainty. Currently, we suggest observing the $1750-$1760 range. Investors who followed the advice to reduce positions on rallies yesterday may use the Bank of China [either/or] entrustment for light-position operations. Either wait for a pullback to around $1752 to moderately add positions, or follow up after breaking above $1763. Note to set stop-loss orders at about $3-$5 for each entry point.
