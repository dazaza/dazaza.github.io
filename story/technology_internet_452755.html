Abstract: Today, many colleges and universities offer broadcasting and hosting majors which can cultivate excellent talents, but there are few radio hosts. I believe that students in the field of broadcasting and art should possess a more comprehensive knowledge base. In the process of continuing voice performance and other aspects of language advantages, it is necessary to focus on journalism courses on the basis of hosting schools to cultivate more outstanding broadcasting talents.

Keywords: Journalism, Broadcasting and Hosting, Art School

China's development of radio and television has not been long. China's first radio station was built in 1923 by foreigners. The Chinese National Radio was established in December 1940, with its predecessor being the Yan'an Xinhua Radio Station. Beijing Television (on May 1, 1978, Beijing Television officially changed to CCTV) was China's first television station, marking the birth of China's television industry. At that time, Beijing was the only program broadcast four times a week, and Beijing only had dozens of TV sets to receive programs. However, by the end of 2002, with the rapid development of radio and television, there were 360 TV stations and 1,300 radio stations. There were 2,058 TV projects, and the television population coverage rate reached 94.54%. With the rapid development of radio and television, there is an increasing demand for radio and TV hosts, attracting many students to apply actively. With more candidates, many colleges and universities have started offering broadcasting and hosting majors. According to the numbers published on the Ministry of Education website, in 2001, the Ministry of Education approved seven universities to establish broadcasting and hosting majors. In 2002, eleven approvals were granted, nine in 2003, two in 2004, fourteen in 2005, twelve in 2006, sixteen in 2007, eighteen in 2008, and twelve in 2009. Only nine universities out of 101 were approved by the Ministry of Education to set up broadcasting and hosting majors, showing an astonishing expansion speed. Schools offering these majors include not only universities and art schools but also technical colleges and engineering colleges that are interested in the project. However, among these universities, very few have developed human resources for social radio hosts, presenting a strange and contradictory phenomenon. According to the requirements of the Ministry of Education, broadcasting and hosting is a professional discipline under Chinese Language and Literature, News and Communication, and Art. Key courses include voice broadcasting, creation of literary and artistic works, radio and TV host training, news theory, news editing, and radio and TV program production. Therefore, this major is based on language, literature, and news and communication. Thus, the specialization requires students not to be single-minded or one-sided but to have a more comprehensive quality. In terms of sound art and language performance, art universities have significant advantages, but in journalism, communication, and literature, comprehensive universities have stronger faculty teams. Currently, art institutions provide a larger proportion of this major.

Taking Sun Yusheng as an example - a well-known host who has hosted programs for both domestic and international TV stations, he has a background in journalism like Dan from CBS in the US. Similarly, Bai yansong and Chai Jing from CCTV have been engaged in news reporting and journalism for a long time, accumulating substantial journalistic literacy. They have high news sensitivity and a good grasp of objectivity and social responsibility. These qualities are essential for a successful host. From the above examples, we can see how crucial journalistic literacy is for students in broadcasting and art. When facing a wide audience, it inevitably involves relevant knowledge and skills in communication. Communication (communication), interaction between radio/TV hosts and their audiences, spreads to the audience. The relationship between radio hosts and listeners, or TV program hosts and viewers, is similar to interpersonal communication. All forms of communication in interpersonal relationships are at least limited, making it easier to achieve expected results. Therefore, it requires hosts to convey news, opinions, and reviews to the audience effectively. The host’s ability to influence the audience affects the reception level, impacting the audience's perspective, leading to choices that determine TV ratings and channel competition. Since broadcasting is linear and lacks the textual permanence of newspapers, it needs audio and visual depth to leave a deep impression on the audience. As McLuhan stated, entering and engaging with deep reports and repeated emphasis can move the audience. In scientific communication, people choose what to pay attention to selectively. Therefore, for the audience, their acceptance of information is optional. This is because people's attention span is very limited. Hence, government news departments should enable hosts to capture the audience's attention through voice, tone, and detailed explanations, thereby attracting the audience. Not all events are urgent or important, but broadcasters must make broadcasts attractive to TV audiences. Because life doesn't always happen otherwise; emergencies may occur. Therefore, before use, hosts should conduct in-depth background research, comprehensively familiarize themselves with materials, understand a large amount of studio activities, and handle unexpected interruptions during the process by introducing relevant backgrounds, interacting with guests to fill time rather than leaving the audience beside the TV hanging. For live broadcasts, the hosting script should be prepared in advance, providing an outline that allows for flexibility. The report can freely adjust according to time constraints, starting and compressing naturally. The information literacy required for these written reports equates to a journalist's capability. During the handover of real-time transmission in 1997, a person observed and improvised a report on the most touching scene, expressing it accurately. It shows that journalistic writing and improvisational abilities are crucial. Therefore, students in the field of radio hosting should strengthen their learning in writing and communication skills regularly, whether written or oral. They should actively participate in work practices and improve their skills through practice to ensure clarity, accuracy, brevity, and wit when speaking in front of cameras.
