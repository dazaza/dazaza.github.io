Online shopping experience (continuously updating)

by anonymous on 2011-04-01 13:38:22

Online shopping has become a fashion and trend among the young people nowadays. I, as someone born in the 70s, don't want to be left behind and also want to be fashionable once. To be honest, when I first had the idea of online shopping, I didn't have much desire for what to buy, I just wanted to try out what online shopping was all about.

I've heard that online shopping has risks, so at first, I wanted to buy cheap items, thinking that even if I got scammed, it would only be a small amount of money. I selected a book I liked on Taobao, but when I decided to pay, I found out that I needed to activate online banking. It was such a hassle, so I temporarily gave up on online shopping.

My husband wanted to buy a motorcycle. He searched locally for a long time and also asked people to look in Guangzhou and Shenzhen, but he couldn't find anything satisfactory. Later, we had no choice but to search online. Online, you can really find anything you want. My husband found a motorcycle from Shandong that both its production place and distributor were in Shandong. Never having shopped online before, and since it was a large item with an expensive price, there were concerns inevitably. For this reason, we consulted several relatives who ran online stores or frequently shopped online. They all said that if payment is made through Alipay, generally speaking, there's no problem. The only concern was the long distance and the possibility of damage during transportation. My husband's previous motorcycle was almost scrap, and it was necessary to buy a new one. After careful consideration, my husband finally decided to buy online. From activating online banking to learning how to operate online, it was quite a hassle. Once the motorcycle was placed on hold and the store owner shipped it, we finally breathed a sigh of relief. Since it was our first online purchase, we weren't very familiar with the logistics company. When picking up the goods later, we learned that we didn't need to personally go to the logistics company to pick up the motorcycle; they could deliver it directly to our home, but prior communication was necessary. Later, someone entrusted my husband to buy a motorcycle online. The logistics company not only delivered the motorcycle directly to the buyer's home, but the shipping fee was also less than what my husband paid at the time. Truly, every setback brings wisdom.

Having experienced my husband's online shopping experience, I couldn't wait to try it myself. I bought a piece of clothing online, haggled with the store owner, but the store owner wouldn't budge, saying it was a newly opened shop, and the clothes weren't making much profit, any further discount would mean doing business at a loss. Eager to wear new clothes, I placed the order. By the next evening, the system still hadn't shown that the store owner had shipped the goods. When I asked the store owner, he said the goods had been shipped, but because he was too busy, he hadn't entered the courier slip yet, so it wasn't showing. He said he would enter it immediately, and soon it would show. After waiting for a long time, it still didn't show, and the system prompted that if the goods weren't shipped within 24 hours, I could split the order. My husband, impatiently, split the order and said the store owner must be a scammer.

Then I went to another store to buy clothes and quickly placed orders for three pieces of clothing. The next noon, the system showed that the goods had been shipped. In the afternoon, the express delivery arrived, and I was thinking it was incredibly fast. But when I opened the package, I was dumbfounded—it was the piece of clothing from the split order. I took the initiative to contact the store owner and sent her a message: Are you trying to give me free clothes?

The store owner said she saw I was a newcomer and worried I might leave a bad review, which would affect their business since they were a newly opened shop, so they decided to refund the money. She didn't expect me to take the initiative to contact her and ask if I would pay her. Ha ha, if I encountered someone greedy for small benefits, this piece of clothing could truly be unpaid for. I told the store owner I would pay her and also wanted to give her a good review because the shipping speed was fast, and I was relatively satisfied with the clothes. It was just due to some misunderstandings that I applied for a refund. The store owner was moved and immediately said she had met a good person and offered me a discount. Unexpectedly, after all the trouble, the originally penny-pinching store owner actively reduced the price of the clothes. I transferred the money to their Alipay account. Since I had applied for a refund, I couldn't comment on the store owner anymore. The store owner regretted losing a good review.

Among the three pieces of clothing bought from another store, one didn't look as nice as I imagined. Considering that returning the goods might be troublesome, I hastily cut off the tag. Later, I noticed that the seam work on this piece of clothing had problems. I took pictures with a camera and sent them to the store owner, trying to communicate with him. The store owner said if it was a quality issue, they would replace it and cover the round-trip courier fees, but the requirement was that the tag must be intact. Unexpectedly, the store owner was so straightforward, and I regretted it deeply.

I didn't tell the store owner that the tag was cut off. I just said returning it was too much trouble, and I could fix the seam work problem myself.

The store owner immediately said that was fine and suggested they would return the profit of this piece of clothing to me.

This piece of clothing felt uncomfortable on the skin, so I gave the store owner a neutral review. After seeing it, the store owner hoped I could modify it, saying she didn't make much money selling me three pieces of clothing and received a neutral review, which would impact their business. I replied: How could I lie? The quality of the clothes isn't good, and wearing them is uncomfortable. I definitely won't wear them and still write a good review. The store owner said that when writing reviews, one can speak frankly, but a neutral review would have a certain impact on their store. Still, she hoped I could give her a good review. Doing business isn't easy. Considering the store owner's good service attitude and fast shipping, I modified my comments, gave them a good review, but didn't lie in the review.

The third time I shopped online was for a cotton coat. After the deal was done, the store owner shipped the goods the next day. According to past experience, I should have received the clothes on the third day. Unexpectedly, after a week, I still hadn't received them. The store owner chose EMS express delivery, and the query results showed: On the 16th, the land route in Hangzhou collapsed, flights were suspended, and all the goods were piled up in the aviation department waiting. My husband said to apply for a refund, but I didn't agree. I had finally found a style of clothing that I was relatively satisfied with. Moreover, applying for a refund for this reason seemed somewhat inhumane. Later, I negotiated with the store owner. Since the express delivery was slower than regular mail, to balance my psychology a little, could the store owner give a discount on the clothes? For a 190-yuan coat, the store owner agreed to charge me 10 yuan less. It was said that the express company encountered a warehouse burst and cargo pressure, especially severe in Guangdong. It wasn't until the 11th day that I received the clothes.

I couldn't wait to open the clothes. First, the color differed slightly from what was shown online, which the website had mentioned, so there was nothing to complain about. When I wore the clothes, I was dumbfounded. I clearly chose the size suitable for myself, but it was surprisingly large. What a disappointment! After waiting more than ten days, I got this result.

Since there was no quality issue with the clothes, the courier fee for returning the goods would have to be borne by myself. Fortunately, when I placed the order, I spent 60 cents to buy insurance, and the insurance company could refund 12 yuan. Since I chose the fastest SF Express, the store owner received the clothes the next day. However, the courier fee was higher than that of other courier companies, costing twenty yuan. Calculating the loss, it was only a few yuan, which was worth it to learn from this experience for future online shopping.

Online shopping has advantages and disadvantages. I will occasionally shop online for some treasures I like. I hope to have more happiness than disappointment in the future.

After searching online, I found several tips summarized by experienced online shoppers:

First, don't set your expectations too high. Online shopping is different from physical shopping. Since you can't see the actual product, you only see pictures, and pictures are generally prettier than the actual product and can mask some defects of the real product. When you receive the actual product and find it less pretty than the picture, you may feel disappointed. Having this mental preparation in advance will prevent you from being easily disappointed.

Second, browse more online and compare prices. For the same item, prices on the internet vary greatly. Pay attention to comparing the pricing of different sellers so you know what to bargain for.

Third, don't blindly pursue low prices. For online shopping, excessively low prices indicate high risks because merchants aren't fools and won't sell good products at low prices.

Fourth, don't just look at the seller's level. Choose the seller with the best sales for the same item. Before, I always trusted sellers with high levels, but later I found that high levels aren't necessarily safe. For the same item, the seller with the best sales indicates that the quality of the item is generally better.

Fifth, don't ignore the buyers' comments. Don't just look at the rate of good reviews. The comments from buyers, especially those from high-level buyers, are more important. When I lacked online shopping experience, I usually only looked at the good review rate, checking whether the seller had 100% good reviews. Later, I found this point unreliable because some sellers use fake accounts to brush up their good review rates. The text comments from buyers, especially those from high-level buyers, are generally the true feelings of buyers and are more indicative of the situation.

Sixth, if you're buying books, don't go to Taobao. The prices are higher and you might encounter pirated versions. Dangdang and Joyo are suitable websites for buying books, at least saving on postage.

Seventh, for inexperienced buyers, the safest way is to go to Taobao Mall. Although the prices are generally higher than other sellers and it's hard to bargain, the authenticity rate is higher.

Written on January 12, 2011

The old bathtub at home has too much capacity. Taking a cold bath in summer is fine, but taking a hot bath in winter is really too costly. Not to mention going to a hot spring. Thinking about the wooden bathtubs I've seen at hot springs, buying one to replace the old bathtub at home would be great. Nearby stores don't seem to sell them, so I directly went online to look. You can find everything you want online, not what you can't find.

Seeing the price of the wooden bathtub, I immediately backed out. Instead, the inflatable bathtub caught my attention, especially the color - a sky blue I love very much, and the price under 200 yuan was acceptable to me. Including the bathtub, the electric air pump, and the courier fee, the total was 183 yuan. Bargaining, the store owner only agreed to deduct the loose change of 3 yuan. When the store owner asked about courier companies here, I said SF Express was good. After the store owner learned the charging standard of SF Express, they refused to use this courier, saying it was too expensive. We later chose E-Youbao. Everything went smoothly along the way, and there were no issues with the goods.

Occasionally, when inspired, I do some indoor fitness exercises. During this season, not wearing shoes feels a bit cold on the ground. I want to buy a pair of shoes to wear while exercising indoors. I bought the cheapest dance shoes online, including the courier fee, totaling 17 yuan. This time, I didn't communicate with the store owner and placed the order directly. The store owner chose Shentong Express, and both the shipping speed and courier speed were good. However, there was some unpleasantness when delivering the goods to the door. They called a few times asking me to pick up the goods personally, saying they couldn't deliver it to the house. They really bullied the "country bumpkin."

Knowing that the vehicles of this courier company pass by my house every day, they shouldn't treat customers this way. Where's the logic? Later, the store owner enthusiastically called to inquire what happened. After the store owner communicated again with the courier company, they finally delivered the shoes to my home personally, but it delayed by one day.
