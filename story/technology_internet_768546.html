Sina's net profit in the third quarter was 25.4 million US dollars, an increase of 157% year-on-year.

by anonymous on 2013-11-17 19:25:00

November 13, 2013 - Shanghai, China / November 12, 2013 - Sina Corporation (NASDAQ GS: SINA), a leading online media company serving China and the global Chinese community, today announced its unaudited financial results for the third quarter ended September 30, 2013.

Third Quarter 2013 Key Highlights

Net revenues reached $184.6 million, representing a 21% increase from the same period last year. Non-GAAP net revenues were $179.9 million, up 22% from the same period last year, close to the upper end of the company's guidance range of $176 million to $180 million.

Advertising revenues amounted to $151.6 million, an increase of 26% compared to the same period last year, within the company’s expected range of $151 million to $153 million.

Non-advertising revenues were $33.1 million, an increase of 4% from the same period last year. Non-GAAP non-advertising revenues were $28.4 million, an increase of 5% from the same period last year, exceeding the company's guidance range of $25 million to $27 million, primarily due to value-added services provided by Weibo.

Sina's attributable net profit was $25.4 million, up 157% from the same period last year, equivalent to diluted net earnings per share of 37 cents. Non-GAAP Sina attributable net profit was $28.5 million, up 144% from the same period last year, equivalent to diluted net earnings per share of 42 cents.

Charles Chao, CEO and Chairman of Sina, stated: "We are very pleased with our significant growth in profitability in the third quarter, mainly driven by the strong momentum of Weibo's monetization efforts. We are leveraging user behavior to provide more engaging and relevant advertising and services. Our focus will be on diversifying Weibo's profitability while continuing to invest in and innovate Sina's products. Not only do we aim to accelerate revenue growth, but also continue to expand margins."

Third Quarter 2013 Financial Results

For the third quarter of 2013, Sina's net revenues were $184.6 million, compared to $152.4 million in the same period last year. Non-GAAP net revenues were $179.9 million, compared to $147.7 million in the same period last year.

For the third quarter of 2013, online advertising revenues were $151.6 million, compared to $120.6 million in the same period last year. Non-advertising revenues were $33.1 million in the third quarter of 2012, compared to $31.8 million in the same period last year. Weibo value-added service revenues (including web games and Weibo membership fees) were $9.7 million, up 121% year-over-year.

Gross margin for the third quarter of 2013 was 57%, higher than 52% in the same period last year. Non-GAAP gross margin for the advertising business in the third quarter of 2013 was 58%, compared to 62% in the same period last year, reflecting the company's efforts to expand its advertising business. Gross margin for the non-advertising business in the third quarter of 2013 was 52%, compared to 44% in the same period last year. This is due to a mix of product portfolio adjustments between mobile value-added services and Weibo value-added services.

Total operating expenses for the third quarter of 2013 were $94.9 million, compared to $79.2 million in the same period last year. Non-GAAP operating expenses for the third quarter of 2013 were $90.5 million, compared to $73.7 million in the same period last year. The increase in non-GAAP operating expenses was mainly due to higher personnel and marketing costs.

Operating profit for the third quarter of 2013 was $23.2 million, compared to $3.8 million in the same period last year. Non-GAAP operating profit for the third quarter of 2013 was $23.4 million, compared to operating profit of $5.4 million in the same period last year.

Non-operating profit for the third quarter of 2013 was $7.7 million, compared to $8.3 million in the same period last year. Non-operating income for the third quarter of 2013 was $3.2 million under non-GAAP measures, including equity investment gains accounted for using the equity method, which lags one quarter behind in reporting.

Sina's attributable net profit for the third quarter of 2013 was $25.4 million, compared to $9.9 billion in the same period last year. Sina's attributable diluted net earnings per share for the third quarter of 2013 were 37 cents, compared to 14 cents in the same period last year. Non-GAAP Sina attributable net profit for the third quarter of 2013 was $28.5 million, compared to $11.7 million in the same period last year. Non-GAAP Sina attributable diluted net earnings per share for the third quarter of 2013 were 42 cents, compared to 17 cents in the same period last year.

As of September 30, 2013, Sina's total cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments were $1.2179 billion, compared to $713.6 million as of December 31, 2012. The increase in cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments was mainly due to the net cash received from Alibaba's investment in Weibo in the previous quarter. Cash generated from operating activities in the third quarter of 2013 was $12.3 million, capital expenditures were $24.3 million, and depreciation expenses were $8.6 million.

Outlook

Sina expects its non-GAAP net revenues for the fourth quarter of 2013 to be between $190 million and $194 million, with advertising revenues between $160 million and $162 million. Non-GAAP non-advertising revenues are expected to be between $30 million and $32 million. Non-GAAP net revenues and non-GAAP non-advertising revenues exclude $4.7 million of deferred revenue amortization related to Sina's equity investment in CRIC/E-House.

About Sina

Sina Corporation (Nasdaq GS: SINA) is an online media company serving China and the global Chinese community. Through its digital media network comprising the portal Sina.com, mobile portal Sina.cn, and social networking service and microblogging service Weibo.com, Sina helps users obtain professional media content and user-generated multimedia content through the Internet and mobile devices and share interests with friends.

Sina.com provides distinctive professional content targeted at local users through several regional websites and offers a series of value-added services. Mobile Sina.cn provides customized information and entertainment content for WAP users sourced from Sina's portal. Weibo is a social networking service and microblogging platform based on an open platform architecture that hosts both native and third-party applications, offering microblogging and social networking services that help users connect and share information with anyone, anytime, anywhere.

Sina provides a wide range of services to users through its main businesses and other business lines, including mobile value-added services (MVAS), online video, music streaming, online games, photo albums, blogs, email, classified information, subscription services, e-commerce, and enterprise services. The majority of the company's revenue comes from online brand advertising, mobile value-added services, and subscription services.
