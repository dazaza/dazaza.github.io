The Story of Spring Festival Couplets

The steps of the New Year are getting closer. Although the weather has not been good these days with rain and snow swirling around, covering the outside world in a cold and gloomy mist, I am still exceptionally happy and excited amidst the joyous atmosphere of welcoming the new year.

All family members have started to get busy: buying new clothes, cleaning the house, purchasing New Year's goods... It's bustling chaos.

On the evening of the 22nd day of the last lunar month, while I was selecting a beautifully printed, gold-embossed couplet and a roll of 'full ground red' firecrackers at a stall selling fireworks and couplets at the south entrance of the urban farmers' market, just as I was about to take out my money from my pocket, I suddenly felt someone pat me from behind. I turned around instinctively. A man of medium build, balding with big eyes, wrapped in a semi-new military overcoat, around fifty years old, stood smiling behind me.

"You—" I found the person very familiar, but couldn't remember who he was right away.

"Yue Ming, it's your brother Cheng..."

"Ahh, Brother Yong Cheng..." I immediately recognized him and exclaimed aloud.

He is Brother Yong Cheng, from my hometown. According to village seniority, I should call him brother. He is honest and passionate. More importantly, he is a "village jack-of-all-trades," having once been a civilian teacher, and writes an excellent hand in brush calligraphy. In village weddings and funerals, he is always an indispensable main organizer.

Since I transferred to work in the county town, we haven't met for more than ten years.

"Ho ho, younger brother, you're still wearing glasses, still so thin. Your appearance hasn't changed, I recognized you from afar..."

I was a bit puzzled: "Brother Cheng, what are you doing here? Did you come to the city to buy New Year's goods? Why are your hands empty?"

"This stall belongs to your young nephew. I have nothing to do at home, so I'm helping out..." Brother Cheng called out to a flustered young man inside the stall, "Little Second, come meet Uncle Yue Ming who teaches in the city..."

The young man who had just handed me the couplets and firecrackers, while still helping others with their purchases, turned around and called out, "Uncle Yue Ming, hello..."

Standing in the misty rain and snow, Brother Cheng and I seemed to forget the cold and chatted...

Back at home, I told my mother about meeting Brother Yong Cheng.

"Brother Yong Cheng doesn't write couplets anymore, he sells them instead, what a pity..." Mother paused her work, looking quite disappointed, sighing, "Twenty years ago, in our hometown, all the couplets in the entire village were written by him..."

Listening to Mother, memories of the village traces and stories of celebrating the New Year seemed to vividly reappear before my eyes:

Under the old archway, surrounded by crowds of men, women, and children, several eight-immortal tables were lined up, covered with neatly cut red paper. Brother Yong Cheng first rubbed his hands together, then blew on them. Subsequently, his left hand held the edge of a large porcelain bowl filled with ink, while his right hand grabbed a thick brush pen, suspended in mid-air, dipped deeply into the ink, and swung forcefully like a dragon flying through the air, writing "As time passes, people grow older; as spring fills the universe, blessings fill the door..." One after another, red couplets exuding the fragrance of ink were passed out from Brother Yong Cheng's hands under the expectant gazes of onlookers, amid exclamations of "Not bad, not bad, really good, really good," like red ribbons fluttering down into the courtyards and gates of the village...

"You damn brat, why did you write my 'Attract Wealth and Treasure' this way?" Suddenly, the village's five-guarantee household Old Scarred rushed over with a square piece of red paper, panting hard. His goatee was dotted with water droplets - whether from snot or sweat, no one knew. "Hmph, do you think I can't read? There are clearly four characters, but how come there's only one?"

Everyone saw the red paper in Old Scarred's hand, some started making noise, some laughed loudly, some were confused.

"Uncle Scarred, this is a compound traditional character. I combined the four characters 'Attract Wealth and Treasure' into one character 'Attract Wealth and Treasure,' which is a character from the old almanac, the most popular one this year..." Brother Yong Cheng put down his brush, rubbing his hands as he explained with a smile.

"Ah, that's how it is. I said you wouldn't dare..." Old Scarred wiped the sweat off his forehead and grinned.

"Quickly go home, otherwise the glue on the door will freeze and won't stick..." Someone shouted from the side. Hearing this, Old Scarred stopped talking and swaggered off amidst everyone's laughter.

In my memory, celebrating the New Year always seemed to start with Brother Yong Cheng writing couplets for the whole village during the twelfth lunar month. When I was in primary school, as soon as winter vacation began, I would run alone with the other kids in the village to where Brother Yong Cheng wrote couplets - under the old archway in the village. We would either stand on tiptoe stretching our necks to watch him write, or each grab a branch to scribble aimlessly on the ground, comparing whose was better. Back then, everyone would shout loudly, "Master apprentice, three-year slave; practice writing this year, become a master next year..." Unconsciously, everyone considered themselves Brother Yong Cheng's little apprentice, hoping to receive his true teachings, even if just a little guidance. Unfortunately, Brother Yong Cheng was too busy to notice anyone trying to learn from him.

In my memory, when the curling smoke rose, when the sound of firecrackers deafeningly echoed throughout the entire village, when the entire village was immersed in the happiness of celebrating the New Year... The most eye-catching red-background black-character couplets from Brother Yong Cheng, as if descending from heaven, illuminated every household's door lintel, and also illuminated the smiles of every individual...

"Two years ago, when I returned to the hometown in spring, I heard from the villagers that your Brother Yong Cheng started doing business after he stopped being a civilian teacher. Whatever makes money, he does: selling clothes at rural fairs, growing vegetables in plastic greenhouses, running a small printing factory..." Mother recounted everything, starting to tell me stories about Brother Yong Cheng again.

As I listened, I couldn't help but feel myriad emotions. Brother Yong Cheng, an ordinary villager, a man who was once extremely fond of practicing calligraphy (it's said he used at least six large bowls of water daily), has been constantly enduring the washing and polishing of time and life. Time waits for no one, running errands for family life, striving for a better life, undoubtedly, Brother Yong Cheng has undergone tremendous changes. So, is it life that gradually changes him, or is it he who gradually adapts to real life?

I remembered when Brother Yong Cheng handed me a pair of couplets that were clearly handwritten, not printed, from the cargo box before we parted ways, saying, "Brother, this pair of couplets may not be as beautiful as the gilded ones, but they are written by my own hand, very durable and practical, take them. Nowadays, although few people want to buy hand-written couplets, I still like to write a few pairs..."

At that moment, my heart was warm, and memories seemed to instantly traverse the rust-streaked time and space, returning to the old archway under the village where New Year celebrations took place long ago; but more importantly, there was an inexplicable surge of emotion within me because, from that moment on, in that instant, I seemed to understand much, comprehend much...
