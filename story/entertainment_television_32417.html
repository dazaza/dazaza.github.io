Love does not distort itself to please others; nor does it distort others to please itself!

Be it love or family affection,

Love only brings joy, warmth, and sweetness.

If it causes pain and torment,

Then either you have loved the wrong person or used the wrong method!

Qiuxi, a pitiful woman, fled from her husband who was about to sell her into prostitution. She held her infant son in her arms and escaped under cover of night. Her sorrow moved the sympathy of a wine shop bodyguard who let her go and killed her heartless and unrighteous husband.

Not daring to linger, Qiuxi fled south. Along the way, she met Su Caiping, a noblewoman returning to Fujian to reunite with her husband. Caiping also had an infant son. With no place to go, Qiuxi accepted Caiping's help and traveled in the same carriage. Unexpectedly, they were ambushed by bandits, and the carriage overturned. On her deathbed, Caiping entrusted her son to Qiuxi, asking her to deliver him to the Chen family in Fujian. Upon arriving at the Chen residence, Qiuxi was awestruck by the carved beams and painted rafters, symbolizing wealth and prosperity. It was like heaven on earth! How blessed would one be to live such a life?

Therefore, when the head of the household, Mr. Chen Mushi, learned that his child had survived a great calamity and someone had returned him, he urgently asked which son it was. Qiuxi, seized by a sudden thought, handed over her own unfortunate son, hoping he would grow up wealthy and honored.

Qiuxi thought she would never see her son again, but the crying child needed milk, so she was hired as a wet nurse. Miraculously, her son grew up right under everyone's nose. Was this not the grace of heaven? Overjoyed, Qiuxi secretly resolved that even if mother and son could never recognize each other in this lifetime, she would be content.

Twenty years passed, and the Chen family's young master Tianxiong and the wet nurse's "son" Junshan both grew up. The two had grown up together and shared a deep bond. However, Tianxiong's temper often caused Junshan, who was simple and honest, much suffering. Watching this "original young master" Junshan, Qiuxi felt both pity and deep remorse. Especially when Tianxiong threw tantrums and showed disrespect even to this "birth mother," Qiuxi tasted the bitterest regret in the world, and this bitterness could not be confided in anyone...

Tianxiong fell for Zhao Yuhua, the daughter of the Zhao family, but Yuhua liked Junshan. She gave him a gold hairpin and instructed him to use it to propose marriage. Junshan, considering himself just a servant, dared not aspire to marry the Zhao family's daughter. Moreover, he already had a childhood sweetheart, Wen Yue, and could not accept Yuhua's feelings. Before leaving far away, he instructed his mother, Qiuxi, to return the hairpin.

Not only did Qiuxi not return the hairpin, but she also lied, claiming it was a token of affection between Yuhua and Tianxiong. This was all because of selfish maternal love! Qiuxi hoped a good daughter-in-law would guide the dissolute Tianxiong back onto the right path. Qiuxi had her reasons, but this misunderstanding of marriage affected the lives of four people forever...

Since discovering that his new wife liked Junshan, Tianxiong was filled with jealousy and hatred. Recalling how, since childhood, whether learning something new or behaving properly, Junshan always received more applause — including applause from his father, Lord Chen! Tianxiong believed his father valued Junshan more than him, which was the most painful point. How could a servant be better than the young master?!

Soon after marrying Lord Chen, Qiuxi was terrified when a person arrived at the Chen family silk shop. It was none other than her former husband, Lin Huaiyuan, whom she thought she had killed years ago. Without the divorce papers, she couldn't drive him away, and she lamented how fleeting her happiness had been...

Lin Huaiyuan always believed Junshan was his biological child and plotted ways to create the best opportunities for him... Just as he was about to kill Tianxiong, Qiuxi hurriedly stopped him and had to reveal the truth of the "switched baby" incident... After hearing this, Lin Huaiyuan burst out laughing, mocking and admiring Qiuxi's cunning methods from years ago! A single sentence made Qiuxi cry uncontrollably, saying she had lived her entire life in endless regret and pain, always looking for a chance to tell the truth...

When Lord Chen was doing business in the north, he detoured to visit Su Caiping's family home. An old nursemaid there described the birthmark on the young master's back... Lord Chen was shocked, unable to recall any birthmark on Tianxiong. When he discovered the birthmark on Junshan, his doubts could no longer be dispelled...

Lord Chen had doubts, but Junshan had regrets. His lifelong emotions were steadfast and pure, with only Wen Yue, his childhood friend and lover. However, due to two misunderstandings, he suffered innocently; the first time was being dragged into Tianxiong's and Yuhua's marriage, and the second time was because of Wen Yue's misunderstanding, leading to their breakup. Wen Yue married another man, Xinghui, who was ill...

After Xinghui's death, Wen Yue left with her child, choosing family over love. To give her child full and pure maternal love, Junshan was disappointed again. Although he understood that these two choices did not contradict each other and were even better for the child, "Love should let it be!" He didn't rush to explain and watched his beloved leave. Though he felt melancholy in his heart, he believed that one day Wen Yue would understand that no matter where she landed, his love would always be with her.

Wen Yue tried to escape her fate but still couldn't escape the tragic song of destiny; Junshan let go after putting forth effort; Tianxiong overcame his lack of confidence and fear; Yuhua adapted from luxury to frugality and rebuilt the family business from nothing to something; Musheng was wise and dignified; and the kind and ignorant Qiuxi sang a variation of maternal love, perhaps off-key or making one want to stomp their feet, but the maternal love was so vivid and made it hard to criticize severely...
