Third, fault symptoms: The dehumidifier shuts down soon after startup and takes a long time to restart at home, with poor dehumidification effect. Fault inspection: (1) The wind speed at the outlet is measured to be lower than the normal value; (2) Use a multimeter to measure the resistance value between each lead wire of the fan motor, and it is found to be much lower than the normal value. Repair method: Based on the inspection situation, it is determined that there is an inter-turn short circuit in the winding coil of the fan motor, causing the fan speed to drop and the air volume to decrease, resulting in this fault. Replace the fan motor with a new one, and the fault is eliminated. Cause analysis: The compressor of the dehumidifier is a rotary compressor. The heat-resistant function of the household dehumidifier is poor. When the fan motor speed decreases and the air volume decreases, that is, the air volume flowing through the condenser decreases, causing the exhaust pressure of the compressor to rise, leading to the overheating protection (shutdown) of the compressor. It will take a long time for the compressor to cool down to a normal temperature after overheating, so the compressor can only be restarted after a long cooling period. In fact, whether it is a household dehumidifier or an industrial dehumidifier, the principle is the same. Second, fault symptom: The dehumidifier runs normally, the relative humidity of the environment is 85%~90%, but the dehumidifier has very little dehumidification capacity. Fault check: Open the intake panel of the dehumidifier, let the dehumidifier run, and check the evaporator. It is found that only a few U-shaped tubes have condensed water, while the surfaces of other U-shaped tubes are dry; Further inspection reveals oil traces at a pipe joint. This is judged as leakage. Repair method: Release the refrigerant, weld the leaking joint, re-evacuate the system, inject the refrigerant, and the fault is eliminated. It belongs to professional moisture-proof equipment and is a professional humidity regulator. Since the dehumidifier is an electrical product, various faults may occur after using it for a period of time. How do we find out where the fault of the dehumidifier occurs?

First, fault symptom: After using the dehumidifier for more than a year, it was repaired once, and the test machine was normal at that time. After stopping use for a period of time and then using it again, it was found that the compressor and fan were normal, but the dehumidification effect was very poor, with little water solidifying in an environment with a relative humidity of 83% and a temperature of 12°C. Fault inspection: (1) The air filter and the surface of the evaporator condenser are very clean. (2) There are no oil leaks on the surface of the pipeline. (3) Frost appears near the capillary tube close to the evaporator, and it stops running shortly afterward. After some time, the frost melts away, and the compressor can run again. (4) Wrapping the joint of the capillary tube and the evaporator with a hot towel results in normal dehumidification. It is determined that there is ice blockage in the refrigeration system. Repair method: Belongs to ice blockage fault. All the refrigerant with excessive water content in the system is cleaned up, nitrogen gas at 0.3MPa pressure is injected into the system to blow it clean, then vacuumed for about 20 minutes, and the amount of refrigerant required by the original machine is added. Run the refrigeration for 30 minutes, stop the machine and evacuate the vacuum for 30 minutes or longer, check if the vacuum degree meets the requirements, then fill in the correct amount of refrigerant, start the machine and run, and the fault is eliminated. Cause analysis: During repair, water vapor may have entered the system or refrigerant with excessive water content was charged. Water monomer at the interface between the capillary tube and the evaporator, due to throttling and cooling of the refrigerant, causes the temperature to drop below 0°C, and water freezes into needle-like ice crystals here. As the ice crystals accumulate, they gradually block the capillary tube, preventing the flow of refrigerant, making it impossible to dehumidify (cooling), and the compressor stops due to protection. Heating the capillary tube with air makes the ice crystals melt, allowing the compressor to resume operation. Repeating this process leads to a significant deterioration in dehumidification effect.
