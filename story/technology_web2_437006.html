Perfectly crafted operation area in the kitchen

The imagined kitchen expert must be a perfectionist bordering on being overly critical. Of course, their high-quality pursuit of food is one of their characteristics, and without complete kitchen facilities, it simply won't do. The utensils on the preparation counter need to be complete, even small accessories like draining baskets cannot be overlooked...

DEB professional design adds functional areas

From preparation, washing to processing, each process before the birth of delicious food is completed in one area. If the cabinets lack functionality, convenience decreases significantly. Cabinet spaces vary in size, and there's no fixed layout for the operation counters as long as they are convenient to use and properly configured.

Functional Kitchen Operation Golden Triangle Area

According to research by ergonomics experts, having a reasonably designed cabinet operation golden triangle area can reduce 60% of the back-and-forth distance and 27% of the operation time in daily activities, saving up to 5 years over a lifetime. Of course, this rule isn't uniform; we need to derive different operation golden triangle rules based on various types of cabinets and different layouts.

This refers to: the cooking area, washing area, and storage area of the cabinets. Based on these three areas, different kitchen layout forms are designed and planned: linear kitchens, double-line kitchens, U-shaped kitchens, as well as L-shaped kitchens and island kitchens. It can be said that every design considers people's behavioral habits, practical applications, and operational convenience.

The workflow in the kitchen is entirely carried out along a straight line, inevitably causing interference between the three points, especially when multiple people operate simultaneously. Therefore, the scientific positioning of the three points becomes the guarantee for smooth kitchen operations. During layout, ensure that the distance between the meal preparation area and the cooking area is controlled between 2.4 to 3.6 meters.

If the distance in a linear layout is less than 2.4 meters, the cabinet's storage space and operation counter will be very narrow; if the distance is too long, it increases the back-and-forth distance in kitchen work, making people tired and reducing efficiency. Due to spatial limitations, a linear kitchen can only arrange furniture and equipment on one side wall, aligning the washing area and cooking area in a straight line. The greatest advantage of a linear kitchen lies in its concentrated workspace, greatly saving kitchen space and extending storage space upwards.

The complete function of the kitchen relies on every piece of equipment in the kitchen. A reasonable layout of cabinets, paired with functional components, organizes the entire value realization of the kitchen.
