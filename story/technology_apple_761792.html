Wired: Amazon's Business Model Is Eating Google's Search Business

by geekzhang on 2013-05-10 14:22:29

Introduction: Wired magazine published an article today stating that Amazon's business model has begun to threaten Google's core search business, and that Google should help users more conveniently discover and purchase products on its site.The following is the full text of the article:Google is considered a tool for searching information, while Amazon is an e-commerce service. These are two completely different companies, right? The answer, according to Jeff Jordan, partner at American venture capital firm Andreessen Horowitz and former president of PayPal, is no.Jordan recently wrote in Fortune magazine that shopping services are the key to Amazon's profitability, while search is the center of Google’s current business. He said: Amazon is a vertical search engine, focused on helping users find products. The main way to find things on Amazon is through the search box. Users enter keywords and then see matching results. The order of search results is determined by algorithms, which Amazon optimizes for both relevance of search results and commercialization.Jordan said that the biggest difference between Amazon and Google Shopping services is that Amazon offers a better user experience, including price, product selection, and logistics. He said: Shopping on Google takes an hour, while on Amazon it only takes a minute.So why should Google be concerned about this issue? After all, Google makes money from advertising sales, not e-commerce.This is because the advertisers who place ads in Google services are the sellers who sell products on Amazon. If buyers discover and purchase more products through Amazon, then Google's importance will decrease, and sellers will have no need to advertise on Google. In such a case, Amazon as a search engine will threaten Google's core business, especially considering that consumer goods and the retail industry are the sectors that place the most advertisements on Google. Although Google does not disclose advertising revenue broken down by advertiser industry, many marketing analysis firms are trying to determine this data.However, Amazon currently seems not to have attracted Google's advertisers. A study earlier this year by digital advertising management service Marin Software showed that after paying to display products in search results, advertisers increased spending on such ads by 600%, and the click-through rate of these ads more than doubled.Google is also strengthening its offline commerce services, such as launching same-day delivery courier services in the San Francisco Bay Area. However, for Google, building its own logistics, transportation, and warehouse network is not the most effective way to compete with Amazon. Google should rather find a way to help users more conveniently search for and purchase products on its website.At the same time, Google can use an ironic fact to convince itself and its shareholders: as the world's largest online retailer, Amazon may also be one of Google's largest advertising clients.
