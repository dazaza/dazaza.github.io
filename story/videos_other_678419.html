Wood Introduction:

Ash Wood

Latin Name: Fraxinus chinensis

National Standard Trade Name: Ash Wood

Ash wood is tough and elastic, with a light yellow to pale brown color. It has a wide range of uses and is suitable for making luxury furniture, flooring, decorative moldings, fine woodworking products, sports equipment, tool handles, etc.

Beijing Poplar

Latin Name: Fraxinus mandshurica

National Standard Trade Name: Beijing Poplar

One of the three valuable broadleaf tree species in the Northeast forest region, it is an important timber tree species for artificial afforestation and renewal in the forest areas of Northeast China. It grows best along riverbanks and valleys and is also a good tree species for afforestation and greening. The maturation period is over 50 years to several hundred years, commonly abbreviated as "Qu Liu". The wood is slightly hard, with beautiful patterns, good resistance to decay and water, easy to process, high resilience, good adhesion and painting properties, excellent coloring ability, and good decorative performance. It is one of the most commonly used types of wood in current decorative materials.

How to Identify:

There are countless varieties of flooring on the market, and the types of wood vary widely. However, many flooring manufacturers will play tricks with the wood, mixing different species to make huge profits. For example, some regular flooring manufacturers mix ash wood and Beijing poplar, confusing consumers. In fact, ash wood and Beijing poplar do not belong to the same species. But Beijing poplar is also called white ash wood. The difference in naming can cause a lot of confusion for consumers.

Ash wood is relatively white and mostly imported from Europe. On the other hand, Beijing poplar is abundant in Northeast China. Ash wood and Beijing poplar should not be confused; they belong to two different categories according to national standards. The general claim that ash wood is just regular Beijing poplar is inaccurate, as there are significant differences in the color and import channels of these two tree species.

Ash wood is tough and elastic, appearing creamy white or slightly pink. Its appearance resembles European beech wood, with the heartwood ranging in color from light brown to dark brown. The grain is distinct, coarse-textured. It is often used for luxury furniture, flooring, decorative moldings, fine woodworking products, sports equipment, and tool handles. One of the biggest features of ash wood boards is the presence of large, striking patterns, exuding a natural Russian romantic charm. Wax wood floors have a soft touch, feeling warm even in winter.

Ash wood has a light and elegant color, mainly creamy white and light pink. Therefore, it is very suitable for country-style decoration and modern minimalist style decoration. Currently, light-colored flooring is a popular trend, favored by the post-80s generation and other younger demographics who like to use this type of flooring for their home decor.
