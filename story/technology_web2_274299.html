Currently, bank wealth management products linked to gold in the market are generally bullish on gold prices, with the rise and fall designed at 15%-20% of the initial price.

International spot gold prices have broken records again.

On November 18, international spot gold opened at $1140.75 per ounce, surged to a record high of $1152.50 during the session, and closed at $1145.10.

Since the beginning of 2009, the price of gold has risen by nearly 30%, reaching 26.2%. In fact, compared to the spot gold price at the beginning of October, the price has risen by 10% so far.

In terms of news, there are more "bullish" messages for gold prices than "bearish" ones. In the US market, the annualized monthly rate of new housing starts in October fell by 10.6%, continuing to put pressure on the dollar. Meanwhile, oil prices have risen due to reports of insufficient inventory. The weakness of the dollar and the rise in oil prices have laid the foundation for further increases in gold prices.

In terms of gold investment products, commercial banks' gold wealth management products sold this year have achieved good returns. However, compared to the rapid rise in gold prices, the returns on wealth management products are relatively conservative, prompting many investors to turn their attention to paper gold and the newly launched individual gold deferred delivery products that can be operated independently.

Foreign banks raise gold price forecasts

Due to favorable news and inflation expectations, BNP Paribas recently raised its average gold price forecasts for this year and next year to $975 and $1050 per ounce, respectively.

The market expects that central banks in emerging markets, which currently hold a very low proportion of gold in their foreign exchange reserves, will continue to buy gold in the future, which will help support the upward trend in gold prices.

Besides banks like East Asia and Standard Chartered raising their gold price forecasts and further highlighting gold as an investment hotspot, the warming up of gold prices in the domestic retail market is the most direct response to the market.

Recently, people who go to the market to buy gold jewelry will find that the price of pure gold in Beijing has been adjusted to 288 yuan per gram, and some merchants' prices are even higher than this level. In Shanghai, the price of pure gold in major gold shops can reach 299 yuan per gram.

If you buy gold jewelry in stores like Tse Sui Luen or Chow Tai Fook, the price of each gram of gold will exceed 300 yuan. The reference price of platinum is 398 yuan per gram, and the reference retail price is 405 yuan per gram.

The return on Bank of China's gold wealth management products exceeds 7%

Few investors choose gold jewelry as part of their asset allocation. Commercial banks' gold wealth management products can be selected by investors as a form of stable asset allocation.

However, if investors think that because of the sharp rise in gold prices, the bank-issued gold-linked wealth management products will also make them rich overnight, then such thoughts need to be corrected immediately.

Currently, the majority of gold bank wealth management products in the market are principal-guaranteed structured products, mainly betting on rising gold prices, with the increase and decrease designed within 15%-20% of the initial price. If this price is exceeded, it is considered triggered, and investors receive a fixed return.

In June, Weekly Wealth Management had already counted that there were nine gold-linked bank wealth management products running in the first half of the year. One of the hottest products in the first half of the year was the No. 6 product from the East Asia Bank's Profitable Treasure series.

Chen Boxuan, General Manager of the Personal Banking Wealth Management Department of East Asia Bank, introduced to Weekly Wealth Management reporters that the linked benchmark is SPDR Gold Trust. If after nine months, the fund does not rise but falls instead, the product holder receives an annual yield of 0.36%, equivalent to the interest rate of a current account deposit; if the fund rises by more than 20% within nine months, a trigger event occurs, and the product holder receives an annual yield of 2.25%, equivalent to the interest rate of a time deposit; if after nine months, the fund rises between 0-20%, then the product holder's maturity yield is the fund's increase multiplied by a certain participation rate.

This product started trading on March 6th this year, with an expiration date of December 9th this year. The initial price of the fund was $92.29, and the closing price on October 30th, 2009, was $102.50. Compared to the starting price, the product rose by 11.06%, and currently, East Asia Bank has announced that no trigger event has occurred.

Among the gold-linked bank wealth management products that have matured this year, one product from the Bank of China achieved a return exceeding 7%.

The Bank of China's "Gathering Treasure 0903V Gold Plus Gold" product was established on March 25th this year and matured on September 25th this year. This product is a six-month principal-guaranteed wealth management product linked to Australian dollars and gold.

The "Gathering Treasure 0903V" is linked to the Bloomberg "GOLDLNPM Index" published gold fixing price per ounce. The initial price was $923.75 per ounce, and the final price was $1014.00 per ounce. At maturity, investors received an annualized investment return of 7.33%.

Gold T+D business volume expands

If you want to fully enjoy the profits brought by the rise in gold prices, the best method is the personal spot gold deferred delivery business (Gold T+D). Currently, Industrial Bank and Minsheng Bank are offering this service.

Minsheng Bank acts as an agent for the Shanghai Gold Exchange's personal gold trading business, utilizing its valid agency qualification as a financial member of the Shanghai Gold Exchange to provide channels for individual customers to trade on the exchange.

The products offered to individual investors include the Shanghai Gold Exchange's spot gold deferred delivery products: AU(T+D), AG(T+D), AU(T+N1), AU(T+N2). The transaction adopts a 10.5% margin system, allowing both long and short positions, with an AU(T+D) handling fee of 0.0015%.

The popularity of AU(T+D) transactions can be seen from the data of the Shanghai Gold Exchange. From November 9th to 13th, the trading volume of AU(T+D) increased by 5.99%, and the trading amount increased by 9.02%.

Since AU(T+D) is a margin transaction, both profits and losses are magnified. Therefore, it is not suitable for customers with little investment experience and weak risk tolerance. The most common channel for domestic gold investment is still paper gold and physical gold investment.

Some banks combine paper gold with physical gold, allowing customers to trade and also choose to withdraw physical gold. For example, Everbright Bank's "Point Gold Pass" business. It adopts a 100% transaction margin management model, allowing customers to choose to withdraw physical gold for long-term collection or to profit from buying low and selling high by following market trends.

Despite the market's optimistic view on gold, Mei Lin, a trader at the Guangdong branch of the Bank of China, believes that while gold prices keep setting new historical highs and the trend is strong, they also continuously accumulate short-term correction pressures.

Recently, Lu Tinglong, head of Investment Advisory Services at Hang Seng Bank, stated: "A rebound in the dollar could be a good opportunity to consider purchasing gold."

The consensus among gold analysts is that gold prices will continue to strengthen, but a short-term correction cannot be ruled out. Specifically, when operating, you can consider adding positions when prices drop and reducing positions when prices rise, actively paying attention to the trend and movement of the dollar.
