Introduction to the plot of Poor Mom Rich Mom:

Tian Fang, the mother of Liu Xiaodong, is a dedicated and ambitious woman who worked her way up from an ordinary textile factory worker to a factory leader through her own efforts, receiving countless honors. Her father, Liu Dawei, although resourceful, refused to work hard, preferring to drink and play with friends, thus remaining an ordinary worker all his life. The stark contrast between the couple foreshadowed the eventual breakdown of their marriage. Liu Xiaodong was raised by his grandmother, being highly intelligent but emotionally immature. Despite consistently excelling academically, he strongly resented his parents' education method that focused solely on grades while stifling individuality. He secretly wrote a novel to vent his frustrations, which was discovered and stopped by his mother. The only person he could confide in was Zhong Kemin, a neighboring girl who grew up with him. Zhong Kemin admired Liu Xiaodong's talent, and the two gradually developed a vague affection for each other.

After the planned economy system was dismantled, the textile factory's performance declined, and Liu Dawei was laid off. Against Tian Fang's objections, he started his own advertising company. Amidst the wave of enterprise restructuring, the textile factory was auctioned off by the municipal government to a coastal real estate company willing to acquire it. The owner of this company, Moli, worth hundreds of millions, remained elegant despite being over forty, and had been single since her divorce. Her daughter, Wang Qi, was under the guardianship of her ex-husband. Liu Dawei participated in the bidding for Moli's company's textile-to-real-estate project and was appreciated by Moli at the bidding conference.

Later, Tian Fang and Moli clashed over the resettlement of the textile factory employees, with Liu Dawei mediating. Out of admiration for Liu Dawei, Moli compromised with Tian Fang but demanded that Liu Dawei work for her. Liu Dawei, leveraging his exceptional interpersonal skills, helped Moli achieve substantial returns, advancing rapidly himself, while Tian Fang became unemployed. Their positions were reversed. At this point, Tian Fang pinned all her hopes on her son, putting immense pressure on Liu Xiaodong. Although his academic performance improved significantly, he became increasingly introverted and sensitive inside.

Moli and Liu Dawei grew closer and had a relationship, but Liu Dawei, out of love for his son, refused to divorce. Moli seized upon one of Liu Dawei's business mistakes, threatening him with imprisonment. Liu Dawei had no choice but to comply, divorcing Tian Fang and moving to City B with Moli. Tian Fang endured her pain, raising Liu Xiaodong alone and concealing the fact of their divorce. As the college entrance exam approached, Tian Fang became stricter with Liu Xiaodong, who could only confide in Zhong Kemin. Zhong Kemin openly expressed her feelings, and they began their first love. Upon discovering this, Tian Fang flew into a rage, causing Liu Xiaodong to resent her deeply, planting seeds of revenge in his heart.

Liu Xiaodong deliberately answered questions incorrectly in the college entrance exam, scoring abysmally as a form of revenge against his mother. Tian Fang noticed something was wrong with her son and sent him to the hospital for examination, where he was diagnosed with depression. Afterwards, Liu Xiaodong left home intending to commit suicide, stopped by Zhong Kemin, Tian Fang, and Liu Dawei. Liu Dawei believed Tian Fang was incompetent in raising their child and reclaimed custody of their son. Moli fully supported this decision. Liu Xiaodong moved to City B to live with his father and Moli. Moli used money to try to win Liu Xiaodong's affection and paid a high price for him to attend a pre-university course abroad, where Wang Qi was also enrolled. This was precisely the university Zhong Kemin had entered.

Wang Qi pursued Liu Xiaodong, and her extravagant lifestyle resonated with his inner desire for freedom and rebellion. Although he still loved Zhong Kemin, he found more common ground with Wang Qi. Zhong Kemin was deeply hurt upon discovering Liu Xiaodong's relationship with Wang Qi. Unwilling to lose her son's love, Tian Fang reflected and began earning her own living. After saving some money, she invested it all in her son. Compared to Moli's lavish spending, this was like an ant trying to shake a tree, further alienating Liu Xiaodong. Tian Fang contracted the cleaning services of Liu Xiaodong's school to be near him. However, Liu Xiaodong and Wang Qi were distracted, and their grades continuously dropped. Tian Fang warned Liu Dawei and Moli to control their children's expenses, causing Liu Xiaodong and Wang Qi to hate Tian Fang even more. Under the influence of rebelliousness, Liu Xiaodong and Wang Qi began to indulge in online games uncontrollably, and Tian Fang tried every means to stop them, only for them to escalate their behavior and start using drugs. They were eventually sent to a rehabilitation center after being caught.

Tian Fang did not give up on her son. Guided by a doctor, she touched her son with genuine emotion. The power of drugs was immense; Liu Xiaodong repeatedly detoxified and relapsed, tormenting Tian Fang both mentally and physically while also ruining her career. Still, Tian Fang persisted, and Zhong Kemin also held onto a sliver of hope to help Liu Xiaodong quit drugs. Under the influence of maternal love and Zhong Kemin, Liu Xiaodong finally overcame his addiction, miraculously restored his health, and published his long novel based on his experiences.
