Hillary defends Obama's apology over Koran burning

by king2z9o on 2012-02-27 11:44:06

Xinhua News Agency, Washington, February 26th (by Ai Jun Yi and Ran Wei) - US Secretary of State Hillary Clinton defended President Obama's apology for the burning of the Quran by allied soldiers in Afghanistan on the 26th. She also hopes that the Afghan side will make efforts to defuse the situation.

In an interview with the American Cable News Network during her visit to Morocco, Hillary Clinton said: "It was the right move for the president to officially state that this action was not intentional and we deeply regret it. I feel a bit uneasy about how our political actions have fueled the dangerous situation in Afghanistan."

She also expressed her hope that figures within Afghanistan, like President Karzai, would take a stand and work to stabilize the situation. She said: "The situation is now out of control and needs to be stopped."

Personnel stationed at Bagram Air Base in Afghanistan burned a large number of Islamic religious books, including the Quran, which caused a huge uproar in Afghanistan. Since the 21st, multiple cities across Afghanistan have erupted in protest demonstrations. So far, it has resulted in at least 30 deaths, over 200 injuries, and four American soldiers were killed in retaliatory attacks.

Obama wrote a letter to Karzai on the 23rd to express his apology and stated that those responsible would be held accountable. However, some Republican presidential candidates and American experts severely criticized Obama for this move.
