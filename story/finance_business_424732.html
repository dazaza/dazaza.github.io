Because I like to eat natto, I call these low-key and friendly people natto men.

Getting up at dawn, feeling tired from spring lethargy, sitting alone by the window, I picked up a small box as big as a mooncake, opened it, and found yellow sticky natto inside. I stuck a chopstick into it and started stirring while spacing out. After three minutes, I discovered that the branches of the poplar tree outside the window actually had fuzzy green buds. Looking up again at the natto in the box, I saw that it had pulled out thousands of threads. Although there was no wind, the fine threads danced in front of me according to my thoughts, as if there was something unspeakable.

A box of natto, a few minutes time, is also a nutritious breakfast, comfortable and leisurely. Tasting the spring with soy sauce and mustard, my body gradually wakes up, and a familiar sentence "diligently untie the knot of the clove flower, let the lush branches scatter and enjoy spring" comes to mind.

Although natto is a Japanese breakfast, compared to the 2 to 5 minute action time of urokinase, it also originated from China. In ancient Chinese Zen temples, natto was often used as a gift between temples at the end or beginning of the year, or as a gift to donors. Monks would also eat natto before meditation to help absorb water in the body and reduce the number of times they need to get up to go to the bathroom during meditation. Natto is said to have been brought to Japan by Master Jianzhen, so the Japanese also call it "Tang Natto".

After the Meiji Restoration, the Japanese wrapped cooked soybeans in straw to keep them at a certain temperature, then allowed the natto bacteria to multiply and ferment fully, resulting in the natto we see today, which is covered in mucus and has long threads when picked up with chopsticks. However, no matter how developed Japan becomes and how much the Japanese value innovation, natto still maintains its simple essence and Buddhist background, and the various schools that have been inherited are called Da Long Temple Natto, Da De Temple Natto, Ikkyu Natto, Da Fu Temple's Hamana Natto, and Wozhen Temple's Yaqiao Natto.

Natto is not beautiful, taking a "simple" character and a "clumsy" character, in this materialistic society that judges people by appearance, it does not have the delicate and attractive qualities of most Japanese food, it is just a silly lump. Natto is not sweet, chewing it even brings bitterness, it does not have the sour and sweet coquettishness of kaiseki cuisine. But when the Japanese are with their families, they cannot live without natto every day. Elderly people like to add a raw egg and stir to supplement more nutrition, while children treat natto breakfast as a game, enjoying standing on stools and pulling out long threads.

Some enthusiasts discuss why the Japanese live longer, holding up the habit of eating natto as a banner. They say that the digestion rate of natto is 80%, it has the effect of regulating the intestines and stomach and lowering blood pressure. It can alleviate the damage of alcohol to the human body, gradually reduce the substances in the blood that form blood clots, reduce the burden on the liver and protect the gastric mucosa; it can also effectively prevent constipation and prevent colon cancer and rectal cancer.
