Baidu Raises $1 Billion in Bonds to Fund 91 Wireless Buyout

by anonymous on 2013-08-02 10:13:00

Baidu will issue $1 billion in five-year bonds to finance the acquisition of 91 Wireless. This is also the largest overseas corporate bond issuance by a Chinese company in the past two months.

The bonds issued by Baidu this time are senior unsecured bonds with a coupon rate of 3.29%, which is 1.9 percentage points higher than the interest rate of similar-term US Treasury bonds. This is also the first time that the company has issued bonds overseas since it issued $1.5 billion in bonds in November last year.

According to data from JPMorgan Chase, the average borrowing cost for Chinese companies has risen by 1.26 percentage points this year, while the average borrowing cost in emerging markets has increased by 1.09 percentage points. The main reason is that investors are observing the impact of China's economic growth slowdown.

In a report on Tuesday, Moody's said that although Baidu has enough cash to complete the $1.9 billion acquisition of 91 Wireless and increase its expansion in the mobile market, the company still hopes to seek tax-efficient financing in the offshore market.

"The technology sector remains a growth sector and will not be too much affected by the economic cycle in China," said Michael Roche, an emerging market strategist at U.S. brokerage dealer Seaport Group. "Compared to metals or real estate, this sector is still growing. The other two industries will face greater funding difficulties due to the slowdown in China's economic growth."

Moody's rated Baidu's bonds A3, four levels above junk status. Eight months ago, the company also issued bonds for the first time, with the interest rate on its five-year bonds at only 2.27%, which was 1.6 percentage points higher than the interest rate of U.S. Treasury bonds.

On July 16, Baidu announced its plan to acquire 91 Wireless, a subsidiary of NetDragon, for a total price of $1.9 billion. If the transaction is completed, the amount of Baidu's acquisition of 91 Wireless will exceed Yahoo!'s $1 billion acquisition of Alibaba in 2005, making it the largest acquisition case in the history of China's Internet industry.

91 Wireless is one of the major mobile application download channels in China, mainly focusing on the development and operation of two leading smartphone application distribution platforms in the country: 91 Assistant and Android Market. In 2012 alone, the number of applications downloaded through 91 Wireless exceeded 10 billion times. The acquisition of 91 Wireless is Baidu's latest move to further strengthen its position in the wireless Internet field, aiming to enhance Baidu's role as an entry point for mobile application distribution.

Wireless Internet business has always been a focus of Baidu in recent years. Official data shows that as of April this year, Baidu Mobile Search's daily active users have exceeded 100 million. Meanwhile, the monthly active users of Baidu Search APP have also exceeded 100 million, with daily active users exceeding 20 million. In terms of entrance construction based on app stores, Baidu Mobile Assistant has covered both PC and Android ends, and the distribution volume of APPs has increased by more than 50% for three consecutive quarters.
