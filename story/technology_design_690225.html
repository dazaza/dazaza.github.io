A university professor died suddenly after a dispute with a neighbor over parking in the neighborhood.

by xue94fwsh on 2012-03-05 08:40:57

A resident said that Prof. Chen's black Lexus was at first parked between two cars, blocking others' way and then conflicts occurred. At about 7 p.m. yesterday, in No.122 Taiping North Road, Xuanwu District, Nanjing City, within the dormitory area of Southeast University, Prof. Chen from the School of Chemical Engineering had a verbal dispute with his neighbor over parking problems and then they had physical contact. Afterwards, Prof. Chen's family called the police. When they were mediated by the police station, Prof. Chen suddenly became unconscious and fainted in the police station. At about 8 p.m., Prof. Chen was sent to the nearby hospital for emergency rescue but he died for ineffective rescue. The professor, who is in his middle age, a doctoral supervisor, unfortunately died because of a parking dispute which made many residents sighed with regret and pain.

Our reporter Haiyong Li Written/Photographed

After the conflict

The professor suddenly became unconscious when making records

At about 10 p.m. yesterday, I went to the No. 81 Hospital in Nanjing where Prof. Chen was being rescued after receiving a tip-off from citizens. In the emergency room, a well-built middle-aged man has already lain on the emergency bed in the emergency room without any sign of life.

I found that there were still quite a lot of bloodstains on the corners of his mouth and nostrils and various kinds of data lines for monitoring vital signs were connected to his four limbs. Although the cardiac pacemaker was still working, it no longer played any role. According to Mr. Zhiping Chen, brother of this man, the middle-aged man under rescue is his younger brother named Zhiming Chen. Although he is only 48 years old, he is already a professor and doctoral supervisor of the School of Chemistry and Chemical Engineering of Southeast University. He was directly sent to the hospital for emergency rescue from the police station by an ambulance at more than 8 p.m.. But unfortunately this young doctoral supervisor finally died. At about 11 p.m. yesterday, the body of Mr. Zhiming Chen was sent to the Forensic Medicine Center of the Nanjing Public Security Bureau, waiting for the final autopsy results made by the police.

At this time, in another medical room of the No. 81 Hospital, Mrs. Chen, wife of Prof. Chen who was also injured in the conflict over parking problems, was receiving examination and treatment from doctors. She did not know the news that her husband had passed away and was anxiously waiting for driving back to her hometown in Anhui Province with her husband. Mrs. Zhiming Chen introduced that on the evening of the incident, she and her husband returned to their residence east of Sipailou in Nanjing. "Because my mother fell ill, we set off from here and wanted to drive back to my mother-in-law's home in Huainan, Anhui Province during the National Day holiday."

"At around 6:50 p.m., I was shocked by what I saw downstairs when I prepared to go upstairs." Mrs. Zhiming Chen said that when she went downstairs and prepared to get into the car, she saw her husband surrounded by an old and a young man beating him. "Because it was dark, I didn't see clearly what was in the hands of this father and son. They kept hitting my husband's head. My husband's head shook like a drum continuously hit, and they kept kicking his belly."

After the two conflicting parties were persuaded by neighbors, Mr. and Mrs. Zhiming Chen quickly reported the police. After the policemen of Meiyuan Xin Village Police Station of Xuanwu District arrived at the scene, they required both parties to go to the police station for investigation. "But on the way to the police station, my husband always felt dizzy. Not long after sitting down, before the record was finished, he fainted on the ground. It was the policeman who notified the emergency center to send people to rescue." Mrs. Zhiming Chen cried and complained.

Cause of the Event

One wanted to come out while the other refused to give way. The conflict was triggered by parking space occupation.

At 8 o'clock yesterday morning, I rushed to the neighborhood to learn the specific situation. Mr. Chen, boss of the shop at the entrance of the scene, ever witnessed the process of the incident. According to this Mr. Chen, at less than 7 p.m. on the night of the incident, after hearing noise outside the shop, he ran out of the shop and found that because private cars were parked on both sides of the originally not wide road, the road was blocked. Prof. Chen who was driving his own black Lexus was stuck at the crossing. He just wanted to wait for his wife to take things from Building No. 18 and then leave. At this time, another father and son who visited Prof. Chen's mother-in-law in Building No. 8 with their child just went downstairs and prepared to drive their white Dongfeng Nissan car home. But because the road to drive out was blocked by Prof. Chen's black Lexus, they had a quarrel.

"In fact, it is just a parking dispute. Unexpectedly, because the quarrel was upgraded to physical contact, Prof. Chen lost his life, which is really unnecessary. All of us are drivers. The neighborhood is an old one with narrow roads and many cars, so traffic jam is inevitable. If either party had made concessions at that time, such uncontrollable situation would not have happened," said Mr. Chen, boss of the small shop at the gate of Building No. 18 and No. 8 of the Southeast University family building.

During the interview, many residents living nearby were all very surprised and opened their mouths widely after hearing the news that 48-year-old Prof. Chen had a verbal dispute and fought with others over parking disputes and then died unexpectedly. Among them, Mr. Zhang, a teacher of the same school as Prof. Chen, repeatedly expressed regret: "He is not only the tallest person in our school, but also a rare talent in our school. He even won the second prize of national scientific and technological progress last year. How could he die just because of a small parking problem?"

Was he beaten to death or dead of anger?

The police still need to investigate

"How can a person die just because of physical contact?" Yesterday morning, this is the most incomprehensible and doubtful question I heard in the East Family Area of Southeast University in No. 122 Taiping North Road.

One of the teachers familiar with Prof. Chen said that Prof. Chen is over 1.8 meters tall and relatively fat, the tallest among the teachers. People call him "Big Guy". However, the residents nearby indicated that the father and son who had a parking dispute with Prof. Chen are not short, but none of them can be compared with Prof. Chen in height and weight. The child assisting in the physical contact is still a second-year junior high school student this year. "Could it be that Prof. Chen suffered internal injuries and then died of anger?" Some local residents doubtfully said.

Then how did Prof. Chen die? How does the police currently deal with this murder case? For this, yesterday afternoon, I learned from the Xuanwu District police that the reason for the argument and physical contact between the two parties was indeed caused by parking disputes in the community. Why did the tall and heavy Prof. Chen die unexpectedly in the conflict? The police still need to wait for the autopsy results from the forensic medicine center. And the father and son who had a conflict with Prof. Chen have been controlled by the police. The police are investigating the specific case.

Yesterday evening, I found through the website of Southeast University that Prof. Chen who finally died in hospital due to ineffective rescue after a parking dispute is the director of the Chemical Engineering Department, professor, and doctoral supervisor of the university. His personal experience is: In 1996, he obtained a doctorate degree in chemical engineering from Zhejiang University, was promoted to professor in 2003, and was hired as the leading talent of innovation in Zhenjiang City in 2009. His research direction is fine chemical engineering, material chemical engineering, biomass energy chemical engineering.
