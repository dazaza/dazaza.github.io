Keywords Computer equipment maintenance, reliability, maintainability, with the widespread application of computers, higher requirements have been put forward for the reliability of computer equipment, and further confirm the related importance of reliability and maintainability.

The research on the reliability of computer equipment essentially attempts to reduce the failure rate; the study of the maintainability of computer equipment is about how to restore the equipment to normal after a fault occurs, reducing the downtime and maintenance costs caused by faults. Therefore, in the design, installation, and use of computer equipment, relevant factors that affect the reliability of the equipment must be carefully studied so that the equipment can be repaired when necessary. 1) Reliability, maintainability, and the effective relationship between the reliability and consequences of computer equipment and its maintenance, the accuracy and timeliness of maintenance work directly affect the failure rate and service life of computer equipment. From the perspective of the performance (performance, effectiveness, credit generally) of the computer system or equipment, combining reliability and maintenance ensures the effectiveness and reliability of the system or equipment. Improving the reliability of the system or equipment directly helps extend its working time, and enhancing the maintainability of the system or equipment helps reduce non-working time. If the reliability and maintainability of the system and equipment are not combined, it cannot ensure the effectiveness of the system and equipment, which sometimes leads to serious consequences. For example, if the reliability of a department and equipment is not well researched, repeated or long-term failures that render them unusable will seriously impact the department's operations, even affecting the overall work.

Additionally, if the system and equipment maintenance is poor, and the equipment takes too long to repair or is difficult to fix, it will also affect the department's operations, even the overall work, while increasing the burden on the maintenance department and adding to maintenance costs, causing unnecessary waste. Moreover, from an economic perspective, considering the cost, reliability, and maintenance evaluation, it must go through the operation cycle of the development, production, and use of the system and equipment. At the beginning of the development stage, it is not just once for a system or equipment, but all funds used throughout its usage period are estimated to be greater than the capital for purchasing the equipment. Using effective design methods to ensure reliability and maintainability can reduce maintenance costs. The reliability and maintainability of equipment should be effectively combined. For users, it is not only required to have high reliability but also to be easily repairable. In other words, the effectiveness of the equipment must be fully studied and improved. Computer system or equipment maintenance: The general concept of computer systems or equipment and other electronic systems and equipment, in reliability research, is divided into repairable systems, non-repairable, or hard-to-repair systems. The former refers to analyzing and handling faults during maintenance, restoring the system to normal afterward. For this system, the key lies in the speed of maintenance and the time required to restore faults to normal. The latter refers to systems that are non-repairable or hard-to-repair after a fault occurs, such as cable systems, where you need system (equipment) reliability design so that no faults occur or the probability of failure is minimized. Computer systems, radar, communication, various military and industrial, transportation, meteorological, space (air), and other automation control systems are repairable systems. Asking them to work reliably continuously, occasional failures still require as short a downtime as possible. In summary, to ensure that the computer system or equipment effectively realizes its required functions, studying only the narrow definition of reliability is insufficient, and it is also insufficient to narrowly define the quality reliability of the computer system. From a broad perspective, besides the normal definition of reliability for computer systems or equipment, it should also include the ability to quickly return to normal after a system or equipment failure, i.e., the size of the mean repair time. Therefore, reducing the maintenance time of the system or equipment and improving its reliability should be considered in the initial design of the system or equipment.
