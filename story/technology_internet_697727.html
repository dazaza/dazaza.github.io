Pinterest becomes the third largest social networking site in the US, boosting small business sales

by bingo123 on 2012-03-08 17:20:12

Introduction: Last week, the American financial channel CNBC published an analysis article by guest contributor Bernadette Tansey titled "Pinterest Grows Rapidly to Become the Third Most Popular Social Networking Site in the US After Facebook and Twitter." The article highlights how many small businesses are leveraging Pinterest not only to enhance their brand awareness but also to increase product sales.

The following is the full text of the article:

Carl Christensen, a 47-year-old aesthetic photographer, has often been nagged by his wife to use social media to boost the sales of his artwork. Reluctantly, Christensen registered on Facebook and awkwardly started writing blogs.

"I really started engaging with social media," said Christensen, "but it felt like I was having hollow conversations about things that didn't have much intrinsic value." Christensen runs a gallery called Integrity Studio in the town of New Hope, Panama.

Boosting Sales

Recently, Christensen discovered a social media platform that not only suited his work style but also increased his revenue — Pinterest. Online sales now account for 50% of his total artwork sales, which he attributes to the rapid growth of Pinterest. On this platform, Christensen can let his works speak for themselves.

Pinterest invites visitors to create their own "pinboards" where they can pin images they like into categories ranging from vintage motorcycles to meatball recipes. The company, headquartered in Palo Alto, uses a very simple method that even those hesitant to post photos online can easily capture images from web magazines, retailer websites, blogs, or their own computer files. Each image added to the "pinboard" is called a "pin." After Christensen began uploading his works to the pinboards last year, other users could also pin these images onto their own boards on Pinterest.

The recent rapid growth of Pinterest has surprised marketing experts, and companies of all sizes hope to capitalize on its potential. According to statistics from market research firm Experian Hitwise, Pinterest had over 103 million visits in February. As of last week, Pinterest became the third most popular social networking site in the US after Facebook and Twitter, with users spending an average of 16 minutes per visit.

Early browser Netscape co-founder and venture capital firm Andreessen Horowitz founder Marc Andreessen said: "Pinterest is riding high; they are doing an excellent job. A service created by 20 people is now being used by hundreds of millions."

Pinterest allows users to comment on pictures selected by others and follow friends' "pinboards." Users can collect ideas for home decoration plans on one board and display their favorite books or charities on another. They can also "repin" images they like from other people's boards. This creates an electronic network of word-of-mouth, gaining snowballing attention for grilling techniques or manufacturers' products.

Secrets for Small Businesses Using Pinterest

Chobani, a Greek yogurt manufacturer located in Norwich, New York, found that other bloggers were using their products to make dishes, so they also established their own pinboard — Chobaniacs — to strengthen interaction with consumers. Chobani digital communication manager Emily Schildt said: "In terms of referral traffic, Pinterest is massive."

Pinterest suggests to businesses wanting to leverage its platform for increased visibility that the best way to build a fan network is by repinning other users' collections and highlighting aspects of brands that people wouldn't think of first, such as collaborations with charitable foundations. Pinterest advises businesses to fully utilize interactions between Pinterest and other social media platforms.

Pinterest cited the strategy of the Travel Channel: asking their Facebook users what kind of "pinboards" they would like to create, and Bergdorf Goodman's successful approach on Pinterest. The department store asked its Facebook fans to complete the sentence "When I get up in the morning, I never forget..." and then displayed various answers on their Pinterest "pinboards."

For Carl Christensen's small business, Integrity Studio, Pinterest has become a direct electronic showcase that promotes sales. Consumers here can also be directed to another online store, Etsy.com, where they can purchase photographs. Etsy is a sales platform for artisans and visual artists who recently added a "Pin It" button next to each product for sale, allowing consumers to share art pieces on Pinterest's "pinboards." Even if users don't buy, artists still benefit from the significant exposure their works receive on Pinterest.

Jason Hennessey, CEO of Atlanta-based digital marketing company Everspark Interactive, said that small businesses involved in e-commerce should explore the potential of Pinterest, especially when their products have strong visual appeal.

However, Hennessey also noted that businesses need to invest time to see returns. Small marketers must engage with the Pinterest community to generate interest in their products rather than simply promoting them. For example, business owners can create "pinboards" to highlight ideas from people with similar tastes, and custom fine woodworking furniture makers should showcase the best projects from top carpentry magazines.

Businesses must also ensure that Pinterest users clicking on images are directly linked to the original website, such as the company website or online store. Hennessey stated that Pinterest can add such web addresses, and if the website link isn't added, Pinterest will show the forwarding address of the image, which might be a blog URL. If a link doesn't help visitors find the product manufacturer, it's considered a "dead link." For instance, the recipe for the dish in the picture.

Christensen added tags to his photographic works and tracked each Pinterest response. Based on these feedbacks, he decided which images should be enlarged in the gallery window and which ones should be expanded to mural size, with these mural-sized works selling for over $3,000. Christensen's wife, Ina Christensen, operates a jewelry business using Etsy and Pinterest. Christensen mentioned that after using Pinterest to run his business, the proportion of online sales rose from 30% to 50%.

For Christensen's small business, exchanging images and ideas with Pinterest followers is a very suitable method. He often chats with gallery visitors about their works and other experiences. Through Pinterest, he can establish similar relationships with visitors, friends, and friends of friends. "It's just like life; the attraction of crowds is greater," Christensen said. (Xiaoming)
