The vast majority of people will also stop to admire in front of the Louis Vuitton flagship store. For 150 years, LV has been advocating a "travel philosophy" of beauty, quality, and comfort. Starting with travel trunks, Louis Vuitton has gained the admiration and devotion of many high-ranking nobles...

The first-generation founder of LV, Louis Vuitton, was born in 1821 in a French village into a carpentry family. At the age of 14, he walked 400 kilometers to Paris to apprentice and learn skills, and later became a professional technician who took care of luggage for royal nobles when they traveled. In 1854, Louis Vuitton opened his first store near the Paris Opera House, specializing in crafting and selling travel bags, and used his name as the brand for his products. LV bags are lightweight, durable, sturdy, waterproof, combined with outstanding design and exquisite craftsmanship, and their reputation quickly spread throughout Europe, with royalty becoming their main customers.

In 1885, LV opened its first overseas branch on Oxford Street in central London. Since then, this brand has never ceased its innovation and expansion. In 1892, Louis Vuitton passed away, and his son Georges Vuitton took over the business. In 1896, Georges Vuitton designed the still very popular monogram pattern today. The symbols on the leather goods' surface, such as the LV letters, four-petaled flowers, and diamond shapes, have become symbolic identifiers of the LV brand. Even in today's diversified product line, they still hold a unique and irreplaceable historical position.

In recent years, LV has launched several classic designs. The Trianon suitcase is the first leather piece introduced by LV for travel. It features a wooden rectangular frame, covered with LV's original fabric, elegant in appearance, light, and stable. LV's travel product designs are always very distinctive, and no matter how demanding the requirements are, they can always be met by LV's products. Early LV suitcases already had designated spaces for storing fragile items like cameras, and the sealed waterproof suitcases made from zinc and camphor wood could prevent damp and insect-infested weather. During the two World Wars, many European cars were equipped with waterproof and dustproof LV suitcases. Today, while inheriting traditional fine animal leather craftsmanship, LV continues to research and develop new products. Its extended range of leather accessories, scarves, pens, watches, and even clothing all embody LV's beautiful and comfortable travel philosophy.

In 1987, LV merged with another French luxury group Moët Hennessy to form the LVMH Group, which later owned other famous brands like Givenchy. To date, LVMH has become the largest luxury group globally, with direct branches in major cities worldwide.

In 1997, the 34-year-old New York designer Marc Jacobs joined LV as the group's creative director. His pioneering fashion series injected new vitality into the iconic Parisian luxury brand LV. Marc Jacobs proposed a minimalist philosophy of "starting from zero," combining the classical image of LV by imprinting the traditional monogram pattern on candy-colored patent leather goods, paired with a simple clothing collection, making LV's image trendier and livelier, earning unanimous praise from the global fashion industry.

Armani is a world-renowned fashion brand founded in 1975 by the fashion master Giorgio Armani in Milan. Giorgio Armani is the best-selling European designer brand in the United States, known for using innovative materials and excellent craftsmanship. Product categories include men's wear, women's wear, sportswear, sports equipment, jeans, leather goods, accessories, perfumes, home decor.

Founder Giorgio Armani was born in 1934 near Milan. In 1957, after completing his military service, he worked as a "window dresser" at the prestigious department store "La Rinascente." In 1961, he transitioned to work as a designer for Nino Cerruti.

In 1974, he partnered with his friend Sergio Galeotti to establish the men's wear brand Giorgio Armani. Upon debut, Giorgio Armani's first men's wear collection immediately caught the attention of fashion buyers and the media. The tailored jacket became his signature piece, elegantly cut and easy to wear. In 1975, a women's line was added. Notably, his sister Rosanna Armani, a top model in Italy, used her influence to make Giorgio Armani widely recognized.

As Giorgio Armani's fame grew, so did his business, naturally leading to the establishment of a second line. In 1981, Emporio Armani was officially founded, opening its first Emporio Armani boutique in Milan. "Emporio" means "department store" in Italian. From its name, one can imagine that Emporio Armani is like an Armani department store, offering a wide variety of products: men's and women's wear, shoes, perfumes, even eyewear and jewelry. It follows a youthful style, providing young people who love Armani but dislike the mature main line with an excellent alternative, a beloved lifestyle department store.

Emporio Armani, leveraging the prestige of Giorgio Armani, became immensely popular in the 1980s, with stores opening one after another, from Milan to the New Continent of Asia. In recent years, Emporio Armani Cafes have been opened in twelve different cities around the world, such as Paris and Osaka, blending music, fine dining, and interior design aesthetics to showcase the leisure lifestyle philosophy of a generation of Italian masters to the general public. As a well-known sub-brand under Armani, Armani Exchange targets the young and trendy crowd, with designs that are more avant-garde and daring. This season, Armani Exchange takes "the sensuality of the night" as its theme, predominantly using black tones, incorporating denim and knit fabrics to create a youthful and energetic alluring style. While exuding sensuality and allure, it continues the elegance and charm that Armani is known for, being sensual and wild without being vulgar.

Valentino is one of the most important designers and innovators recognized in fashion history. From his unforgettable debut fashion show in Piazza di Spagna in 1962 to receiving the Lifetime Achievement Award from the Council of Fashion Designers of America in 2000, Valentino's career as a creator and entrepreneur has become a significant part of Italian fashion. His name represents imagination and elegance, contemporary sensuality, and eternal beauty.

The story of this fashion genius began in his teenage years when he left his hometown of Voghera, Italy, to study fashion in Paris. It was during his time in the cultural and fashion capital of Europe that Valentino honed his discernment and innate talent while working with Jean Dessès and Guy Laroche.

In the early 1960s, he moved to Rome and opened his first studio. This was a golden era, with "La Dolce Vita" ("The Sweet Life") in vogue, and Hollywood stars filling the city, signaling the arrival of a classic period. Amid the turbulent yet vibrant times, new personalities emerged, and a talented fashion designer began to shine. This marked the beginning of Valentino's rise to prominence.

By the mid-1960s, Valentino had become an undisputed Italian fashion master. In 1967, he received the Neiman Marcus Award, akin to the Oscars of the fashion world at the time. By then, Begum Aga Khan, Farah Diba, Jaqueline Kennedy Onassis, Liz Taylor, Marella Angelli, and Princess Margaret were already regular clients and friends.

Valentino pioneered the use of letter combinations as decorative elements. Most notably, in 1968, the "White Color Series." His "V" logo began appearing on clothing and accessories, even on belt buckles. These pioneering ideas changed the course of fashion history. In the 1970s and 80s, Valentino became the first high-fashion designer to launch both men's and women's ready-to-wear collections. He continued to open boutiques in Rome and Milan, the US, and the Far East, subsequently signing international sales licenses for his creations. Valentino was a pioneer in using world-class photographers for advertising campaigns and promotions, earning him two of Italy's highest honors: the title of "Grande Ufficiale dell’Ordine al Merito della Repubblica Italiana" in 1985 and "Cavaliere del Lavoro" in 1996.
