Facebook Wants to Add 5 Billion Users? It May Take Another 27 Years

by anonymous on 2013-07-29 18:22:46

On July 28, Beijing time, according to foreign media reports, Facebook CEO Mark Zuckerberg revealed during the company's second quarter earnings call this year that the company plans to add another 5 billion users. If this grand goal is achieved, Facebook's total user count will reach over 6 billion, which would be close to the world's total population at that time. In response, industry analyst Jim Edwards believes that, based on the current development speed, Facebook may have to wait another 27 years to achieve this goal.

A few days ago, Facebook released its Q2 2013 financial report. The report showed a significant increase in revenue for the second quarter, growing by 53% year-over-year to $1.81 billion, a performance that greatly excited investors.

Zuckerberg has repeatedly addressed Facebook's revenue growth trends and where future new users will come from. Facebook has clearly stated that its user base is shifting from desktops to mobile services, which will become a major channel for user growth. However, some people are skeptical about this. They believe they will never click on the small blue ads in Facebook's news feed and assume others won't either, suggesting that Facebook's future will face serious challenges, especially as user growth slows down.

We all know that Facebook's user growth may already be over. Facebook currently has over 1 billion users, so where can new users come from?

However, Zuckerberg doesn't see it that way. During the recent Q2 earnings call, Zuckerberg detailed how Facebook could potentially add another 5 billion users. It sounds almost impossible and exaggerated. After all, 6 billion is nearly the entire global population.

Given that much of what Zuckerberg has previously told us has come true, perhaps we should listen to him again. Therefore, if Facebook were to add another 5 billion users, following the current trend, it might take another 27 years to achieve this goal. Of course, summarizing Zuckerberg's remarks during the Q2 earnings call, it is not difficult to find that he mentioned multiple times that achieving the goal of adding another 5 billion users will still require a long time.

Regarding user growth, one thing worth noting is that since the third quarter of 2011, Facebook has frequently seen an increase of 45 million to 50 million users per quarter. Of course, compared to Facebook's 1 billion users, this scale is still relatively small. However, if some innovative companies in Silicon Valley had such a growth rate, that company would certainly become one of the hottest in the U.S. But when it comes to Facebook, fewer people seem to notice.

Most of Facebook's current users are in the Americas, meaning there is still enormous growth potential in Asia, Africa, and other large regions. Since the launch of Facebook For Every Phone, the number of users has increased by 100 million. Most American users do not use this app because they access Facebook through smartphones. However, if you don’t have much money or are a foreign user using a cheap or even free phone, this app essentially becomes your Facebook.

Below are some of Zuckerberg's statements regarding long-term user growth:

I often get asked, what major changes will we help bring to the world in the next five or twelve years? Now that our user base has exceeded 1 billion, what are our grand goals moving forward? I want Facebook to achieve three things—connecting everyone, understanding the world, and helping to build a knowledge economy.

Connecting everyone means increasing Facebook's user base by another 5 billion. Our goal is to empower everyone to share information and make your world more open and interconnected. This applies to everyone, not just users in developed countries.

I think we're still in the early stages. One of the most interesting things in the past six months or so is that our user base has exceeded 1 billion. To me, because we've surpassed 1 billion users, we also see our company's goals becoming even grander. Exceeding 1 billion users, and having them registered with real names, is certainly something worth noting, as no one has ever created such a service before. However, this isn't the ultimate achievement; our true goal is to connect everyone in the world and help people map everything out.

In a sense, this might sound like empty talk. Many CEOs have talked about connecting everyone. But the difference is that many CEOs leading their companies haven't achieved quarterly revenue increases of $100 million to $350 million, nor have they consistently added 45 million users each quarter.

For those customers who don't want to be part of Facebook, another aspect of scale is making these users face network effects and switching costs. We’ve already brought a lot of friends (friends who refuse to use Facebook) onto the platform. Not using Facebook is somewhat akin to not watching TV. Of course, you can choose not to watch TV, but doing so won't help you stay informed about cultural trends.

Facebook is very similar to cable TV—a device that almost everyone owns, but almost no one is extremely passionate about. We always complain about it and consider whether it will disappear. However, Facebook’s direction is to become a device that connects the globe and allows us all to use it, even if it might sometimes feel annoying.

In summary, over the past nine years, Zuckerberg has garnered over 1 billion users, and the number of new users added each quarter remains around 45 million. Of course, skeptics still believe that adding another 5 billion users will be difficult to achieve. However, if it does happen, following the current trend, it might take Zuckerberg another 27 years to add another 5 billion users.
