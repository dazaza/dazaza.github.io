Ali Micro: Our financial services rely on the Internet and big data, not banks.

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 12:38:54

When Yu'e Bao was first introduced, it caused quite a stir on the internet. Although this product should be considered as a financial product from Alibaba Microloan, due to its low threshold and ease of operation, it has become what many people see as a "current deposit with fixed deposit returns".

With the internet background generated by cooperation with Alibaba Microloan (Alipay), the underlying Tianhong Fund has also come to the forefront. On the other hand, the three major domestic BAT giants have been making frequent moves in finance. Alibaba's Yu'e Bao has captured plenty of attention, Tencent is considering applying for a private bank, and today, Baidu announced that its "Baidu Finance Center - Wealth Management" platform will officially launch on October 28th. At the same time, Baidu Finance Center and China Asset Management jointly launched a wealth management plan called "Bai Fa," with an annual return rate of approximately 8%.

It can be seen that the three giants' focus has once again aligned, turning towards the financial sector, and both Alibaba and Baidu have cooperated with funds to develop internet finance projects. Speaking of this recently emerging term, Alibaba still holds the advantage. Alipay naturally possesses such attributes, and with money in hand, offering wealth management services is a natural progression.

Yu'e Bao, the vanguard of Alibaba's Internet finance

Here, let's first quote Zhou Xiaoming, Vice President of Tianhong Fund, to introduce the concept of Yu'e Bao:

"The three words 'Yu'e Bao' represent a wealth management user entry point that Alipay, as a third-party fund payment institution, has launched on its online platform for its users. Strictly speaking, Yu'e Bao is a service provided by Alipay to its customers, or an entry point on its website. The reason why it has garnered so much attention now is because behind the Yu'e Bao business, it is tied to a money market fund raised by Tianhong Fund, enabling the Yu'e Bao service to provide functions such as wealth management, payment, transfer, and shopping for Alipay customers. Therefore, what we usually refer to as the Yu'e Bao model actually means that Yu'e Bao and Alipay together constitute a one-stop wealth management + shopping payment solution for customers, and this is what we mean when we talk about Yu'e Bao."

As the securities market began to decline in 2008, the fund market also started to stagnate. Afterward, the fund business, which was basically in the state of being sold through banks, began seeking breakthroughs. With the rapid development of the internet, especially mobile internet, internet direct sales became an inevitable choice. Tianhong Fund began cooperating with Alibaba in September 2011. Realizing the internet finance potential of Alipay, and with the national regulatory authorities encouraging funds to explore new businesses via e-commerce platforms, under this backdrop, Yu'e Bao was born.

To a certain extent, as mentioned at the beginning of this article, the background and mechanism behind Yu'e Bao are not simple, but the project itself has no threshold, thus creating the impression of a "current deposit with fixed deposit returns." This overlaps with banking services, and with recent rumors about private banks, whether Alibaba will open a bank has become a topic of concern.

A bank on the left, Alibaba moves to the right

At the previous Alipay Sharing Day event, a journalist asked: Some media have clearly reported that Alibaba has submitted an application to the regulators to establish a network bank, and even revealed that the capital of Alibaba Bank is 1 billion yuan. Could you (President Hu Xiaoming of Alibaba Microloan Financial Services Division) share your thoughts on our Alibaba's views regarding applying for a bank or establishing a private bank?

In response to this hot issue, Hu Xiaoming replied:

"I personally believe that the Chinese government holds an open attitude towards private enterprises entering the financial industry and securities industry. But for China, it doesn't lack a bank, nor does it need another bank. Recently, I've been looking at how two spots were allocated in this province, ShenZhou Bank was approved, SuNing Bank was approved, I don't know if that's correct, but for Alibaba, regardless of whether there is a bank or not, we aim to provide credit platforms for small and micro-enterprises through such methods and internet platforms, and our determination to create credit hasn't changed."

If this response was still ambiguous, when pressed by other journalists, Hu Xiaoming denied it to a certain extent and explained Alibaba's approach in financial services:

"As Alibaba Group, as Alibaba Microloan Financial Group, we continuously pay attention to reforms. We maintain continuous communication with Wenzhou's financial office. We hope to provide services to Wenzhou enterprises through small loans, etc., which I have already answered. It's already very clear online, they seem to be the CBRC, but indeed, for Alibaba, we keep track of this matter, keep interested, but we also noticed that Tencent discussed Tencent, Tencent has already disclosed through his listed company the application for a bank, this information is being followed, but for Alibaba to set up a bank in Wenzhou with a registered capital of 1 billion yuan, I haven't heard of it at least. I meet with Ms. Peng every day, she hasn't told me either. So, this won't change our determination to provide financing services to one million small and micro-enterprises, so no matter what, we still hope to provide financial services to small and micro-enterprises through big data and the internet method."

From Hu Xiaoming's statement, at least for the current stage, Alibaba has no actions regarding applying for a private bank. The internet and big data are the main directions for Alibaba's development in financial business. Indeed, as an internet company, it is a wise strategy for Alibaba to operate financial business within its familiar field, while setting up a bank is not as simple as launching a product like Yu'e Bao.

According to Peng Lei, CEO of Alibaba Microloan, in the future, the large Alibaba Group will be divided into three sectors:

The Alibaba Group mainly focused on e-commerce, including the previous B2B, Taobao, Tmall, cloud computing all belong to Alibaba; Alibaba Microloan Financial Services Group, simply put, integrates all financial-related businesses of the Alibaba system, including the original Alipay, small loans, guarantees, and some recent investments related to insurance and the fund industry; the third is the logistics industry just getting started.

From the frequent acquisition and equity participation actions of BAT in the first half of the year to their steps into the financial industry in the second half, the competition and movements of these three companies remain the theme of China's internet landscape. The scope of competition among these three companies is also expanding, and we should think about where to place our money to make it more worthwhile.
