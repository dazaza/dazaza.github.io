◎Introduction:

Director Yann Arthus-Bertrand spent 15 years preparing and visited 50 countries for filming, from the Great Barrier Reef under the sea in Australia to Mount Kilimanjaro on the highlands of Kenya in Africa; from the Amazon rainforest to the Gobi Desert; from the endless cotton fields in Texas, USA to the industrial town of Shanghai, China. The picturesque beauty wakes up people to cherish optimistically the 50% of the rainforest we still have, rather than just focusing on the lost half. More importantly, the 6 billion humans on Earth should all wake up to our responsibilities...

This documentary is produced for the first time by Luc Besson and directed by renowned international photographer Yann Arthus-Bertrand. It objectively narrates the birth, evolution, and current issues facing the Earth today! With one stunning natural image after another, it takes the audience on a journey to discover the beautiful Earth and promotes the importance and urgency of environmental protection.

In the 200,000 years since humans appeared on Earth, the balance established through nearly four billion years of evolution is no longer orderly. We are paying too high a price, but now is not the time for pessimism - humans have ten years to reverse this trend, understand the full truth of how we have exploited the Earth's abundant resources, and change human consumption patterns.

Renowned international photographer Yann Arthus-Bertrand brings us unique aerial shots taken during his visits to over fifty countries, sharing his amazement and concerns. His work lays the foundation for a massive project that we must all rebuild together.
