The reorganization process is accelerating, and ST Shixian harbors significant investment opportunities. According to relevant data, ST Shixian's (600462) predecessor was the Shixian Paper Mill, founded in 1936, a national large-scale pulp and paper integrated enterprise, and an important pillar enterprise of the Yanbian economic construction. The company's main business includes the production and sales of newsprint, offset paper, commercial pulp, and by-products. Due to the impact of the global financial crisis, it caused a decline in the prosperity of the papermaking industry, resulting in reduced sales volume and price of the company's products, increased accounts receivable, digestion of high-priced raw material inventory from previous periods, and planned disposal of part of the assets. The company's operating performance for 2008 and the first half of 2009 showed continuous losses, and currently relying solely on the company's main business operations is almost impossible.

However, like most ST stocks that eventually achieve a splendid transformation from "crows to phoenixes," as a major industrial enterprise and listed company resource of the Yanbian Korean Autonomous Prefecture, the Yanbian prefectural government has given considerable attention and importance to ST Shixian. According to the annual report data of ST Shixian (600462), the largest shareholder of Jilin Shixian Paper Industry Co., Ltd. is the Yanbian State-owned Assets Operation Corporation, holding 60.89% of the shares and is also the actual controller of the listed company. What deserves high attention is that historically, the Yanbian State-owned Assets Operation Corporation had announced intentions to restructure the major shareholder Shixian Paper Industry. Although this intention has not been implemented so far due to various factors, the strong reorganization expectations of ST Shixian (600462) can be seen from this.

As the year-end approaches, driven by factors such as shell preservation, the reorganization process of ST Shixian (600462) has clearly accelerated. On September 18, 2009, the company held the tenth meeting of the fourth session of the board of directors, which reviewed and agreed to transfer all 51% equity holdings in the controlled subsidiary Mudanjiang Hongshi Chemical Co., Ltd. to Honglin Chemical. Clearly, the company's pace of clearing non-performing assets is accelerating. Moreover, at the end of September, ST Shixian received summonses and complaints from the Intermediate People's Court of the Yanbian Korean Autonomous Prefecture due to overdue debt payments. Under the increasing difficulty of obtaining loans again through normal channels, the asset reorganization of ST Shixian (600462) is expected to restart.
