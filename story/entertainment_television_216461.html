When the main actors attended the press conference, Shun Oguri expressed that he had played the role of young "Sakichi", another name for Ishida Mitsunari, in a Taiga drama in 1996. "I can't imagine that after 13 years, I would have the chance to play the adult Mitsunari, which is such a mysterious destiny. Back then I was still a kid, and now I'm playing the same character again, so it must feel very different." Kyoko Takae, who was making her first appearance in a Taiga drama, said that she was studying history hard and hoped to bring a different feeling to the fans of Taiga dramas.

There have been examples in past Taiga dramas where one actor has played two roles, like Toshiaki Karasawa who appeared in both the 2002 "Rikyu and Matsu" and the 2006 "Crossroads of Fame". However, there are no records at NHK's broadcasting department of a case like Shun Oguri's, where an actor plays both the child and adult version of a character. Therefore, when Shun Oguri revealed this fact at the press conference, the staff responsible for the Taiga drama were greatly surprised. Producer Shinji Naito said, "We never realized he had played the role before."

Moreover, according to Shun Oguri, he has had ties with Taiga dramas since elementary school. At the age of 13, he was selected from his children's theater group to play the young "Eighth Shogun Yoshimune". One year later, he played the young Mitsunari, whose adult version was portrayed by Hiroshi Shimizu at the time. Then in 2000 and 2005, he also played roles in Taiga dramas. This could be considered his fifth connection with Taiga dramas.

In fact, prior to "Tenchi Jin", actors such as Satoshi Tsumabuki, Takako Tokiwa, Masami Nagasawa, and Saki Amamiya had already been cast. With the addition of Shun Oguri and Kyoko Takae this time, it could be said that this was the highest proportion of idol actors ever seen in a Taiga drama. The currently popular Taiga drama "T笃姬" starring Aoi Miyazaki has maintained high ratings (over 20%) since its premiere, becoming a new legend in Taiga dramas. Therefore, it is clear that the TV station put a lot of thought into selecting the cast for the 2009 Taiga drama, hoping to continue the brilliance of Taiga dramas with these popular actors.
