What to do if clothes are burned

Usually when cooking or ironing clothes, or when ashes touch items, minor burning occurs, producing these gray or black burn marks.

It is difficult to clean such burn marks, and if the situation is severe, they cannot be removed. Be sure not to wash with soap, as this will set the mark.

Trick:

1. First, rinse under the tap with a large flow of cold water.

2. Dilute 1 tablespoon of borax in 1 liter of warm water, then soak the clothes in the borax water for 1 hour.

3. Put the clothes in the washing machine and wash them with the highest water temperature that the fabric can withstand.

4. If there are still marks on the clothes, you can scrape them off with a nail file.

Trick 2:

1. After doing a fading test, mix cold water and hydrogen peroxide in a ratio of 4:1, and use a sponge to dip the mixed solution to wipe the scorched area.

2. Take another piece of clean white cloth with water absorption property, cover it on the burnt mark, and then press a slightly warm iron on the white cloth. Be careful not to let the iron and the burnt mark come into direct contact.

3. Repeat the above steps of wetting and ironing until the burnt mark is cleaned as much as possible.

4. Spray some stain remover on the stain, and then put it in the washing machine to wash.

If there are small marks on woolen fabrics, the burnt marks can be ground off with the edge of a coin.

How to deal with burnt marks on carpets or sofas?

If it's a small area of burnt marks on the carpet, take a sharp pair of scissors to cut off the scorched fiber ends, then use the edge of a coin to grind off the remaining burnt marks.

If it's a high-end plush carpet or sofa fabric, use very fine steel wool to lightly brush in a circular motion at the stained area to remove the burnt marks.

However, except for very small burnt marks, it's better to have professionals handle the burnt marks on carpets or sofa fabrics.
