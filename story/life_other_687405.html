Fragments of Life

Only when we are able to possess some things do we realize what we have lost before. However, it is only when we lose something that we understand how beautiful the things we once possessed were.

-- Written for the woman I once loved deeply and who loved me more than life itself

I have never thought about revealing another side of myself, as I feel there is nothing particularly fascinating or touching about my experiences because they are so ordinary, just like everyone else's. A few days ago, while attending a friend's wedding in Beijing, I received a phone call from a woman who had been long forgotten, almost to the point where I thought I had completely erased her from my memory... She reminded me of the woman who once loved me deeply but was betrayed by me – perhaps also the woman who loved me most in this lifetime. In the fourth year of knowing her, I felt ashamed of how ruthlessly I had hurt her, unsure whether leaving her out of love was wrong. Reflecting on it at midnight, I truly don’t know if I chose the wrong direction back then...

In my memory, I still retain the image of her when we first met. Back then, she was so pure, like a blank sheet of paper, often making me feel as though she were still a child. It was a summer evening after the rain, the fresh air made the sky seem much higher. Dressed in white, she resembled a princess from a fairy tale, surrounded by a soft glow, exuding an unusual beauty - a kind of purity I hadn't encountered in a long time, giving me the illusion that time had stopped.

Her home was very close to where I worked, separated only by a not-so-wide road. When I wasn't busy at work, if she was on the other side, I would always gaze at her for a while. It seemed she could feel my eyes on her, often blushing and smiling before lowering her head, yet never walking away. Ha... That blush belonged to me!

Love slowly accumulated under prolonged gazes, like a seed sprouting until it grew into a tree of longing large enough to cover us both. The power of love is the most irresistible force in the world, capable of breaking through even the strongest barriers between heaven and earth, showcasing its indomitable vitality.

Despite our significant age difference, it did not hinder the spread of our love. After many days, I often wondered if we had inscribed each other's names on the Three Lifetimes Stone in a past life, leading to our meeting in this one.

In my memory, the first birthday I celebrated with her after we met was in early spring. Back then, I was still somewhat childish, romantic in a naive way. I went to the flower shop early to pick out eleven roses, hoping our love would last a lifetime... Carefully hiding those gorgeous roses under my clothes, just as I cherished her... The crimson roses set off her unique blush, which I believed contained gratitude, and it was beautiful!

Many people have criticized my promiscuity, and indeed, I am aware of my inability to resist temptation, which was the root cause of our only argument and eventually led to her losing my love. Her love was selfless, giving everything. Therefore, she expected equal returns, but I often failed to meet her expectations...

We broke up for the first time for three months. During that period, I wandered among many beauties, indulging in their affection. To outsiders, it seemed I was enjoying the blessings of heaven, but who knew my loneliness! Whenever I was alone, the image of her blush hidden beneath her white dress would inevitably come to mind. I knew I still loved her, but I just refused to admit it.

When we reunited again, it was already late autumn, the season of my birth. It was she who took the first step, and on that night, she became my woman... As my fingers traced her skin, feeling her unique tremors, I realized that her essence would forever be part of my life, and I could sense her pulse within my soul.

I once asked her why someone like me could make her love me so deeply - a man who only loved her six-tenths. Unexpectedly, her answer was so simple: because she loved me ten-tenths, thousand-tenths, million-tenths... Who could not love such a woman? Even if it was only six-tenths...

The days that followed were happy, I believe. Due to my work, I went to a remote place, reducing our meetings, but our feelings deepened. Every weekend, she would come to see me, releasing her long seven-century-like yearning. Occasionally, she would take the last bus at night to visit me, unable to suppress her thoughts of me. By then, the weather had turned cold. Holding her icy fingers, I placed them under my armpit. I didn't feel the cold, only the warmth of happiness and gratitude surging in my heart.

Her love for me was so steadfast and resolute that it often made me feel as though I were being conquered, and in fact, she was doing just that. I couldn't interact with any other woman for more than ten minutes, as it would make her unbearable. I knew she couldn't lose me, at least not then.

She often created shocking tests for me. She would suddenly roll off the bed in the middle of the night, moaning in pain, causing me to panic and carry her downstairs, only to find her "mischievous" smile with tears in her eyes; On starry nights, she would bury her face in my chest, insisting I swear never to love another woman in this life – at least not while she was alive; When she was in a good mood, she would chase me out of the kitchen and personally cook scrambled eggs for me to enjoy with wine – despite her only knowing how to cook that one dish.

Such a woman, such a little woman, how could anyone not love her!

Mountains may crumble, and the heavens may collapse, but I dare not sever ties with you! I hoped we could continue loving each other like this forever, never imagining that we would break up again. There's no need to mention the reason for our second breakup. All I remember is the freezing wind that night, mixing dust with tears streaming down my cheeks, merging with the earth. Letting go of her fiery eyes, I turned around, refusing to look at her desperate gaze, quickly walking away, fearing that one more glance would make me embrace her without hesitation, wiping away her tears, and reigniting the dimmed light in her eyes.

The last time I heard from her was three months later. A friend told me she had been sick for three months, and many people thought she might die until she was discharged from the hospital. Although I wanted to visit her, I didn't. My friend brought me a letter from her, actually a poem:

In the secluded courtyard, I hear only the swallows' chatter,

Flowers fall idly throughout the garden.

Thinking of the joys of old, my spring thoughts increase,

Unable to settle, my emotions churn.

At the curved railing, willow threads hang golden,

In the small window, the silver strings of the zither break.

A piece of unrelenting longing cannot be dismissed,

Enduring the long day, sorrow grows incessantly.

Who sees the solitary dream at sunset,

Waking to infinite sorrow.
