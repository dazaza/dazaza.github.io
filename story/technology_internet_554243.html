41-Year-Old College Student Who Took the Gaokao 13 Times Earns Several Nicknames

by wenkuon18 on 2011-11-14 10:50:22

In the later stages, Cao Xiangfan took the college entrance examination with the purpose of becoming a private tutor. As Cao Xiangfan is about to graduate, he will face new challenges in life.

Cao Xiangfan, 41 years old, is a third-year student at Hunan Public Security College. He has taken part in the college entrance examination 13 times and is known as the "College Entrance Examination King." Some people call him the "Contemporary Fan Jin" or the "College Entrance Examination Stubborn Holder."

Cao Xiangfan strongly dislikes these titles. He says that he is a "retailer selling knowledge of the college entrance examination," and his only similarity to Fan Jin is having a good father-in-law. He frankly admits that three years of university life have made him realize: one should not hang on to just one tree.

Written by our special correspondent Zeng Xiangrong

Cao Xiangfan was born at the end of the 1960s in Hanshou County, Changde City, Hunan Province. Currently, he rents a room alone in a village near Gaoqiao Large Market in Changsha City. This place is far from the bustling city center, and it takes a 20-minute walk to the nearest bus stop.

This is a small house less than 8 square meters, with only a bed and a desk. The kitchen and bathroom are combined, but fortunately, Cao Xiangfan never cooks there. Even during the daytime, Cao Xiangfan cannot prepare lessons without turning on the light. On the desk, there are scattered college entrance examination tutoring books.

This is Cao Xiangfan's 40th "base" in Changsha, with a monthly rent of 200 yuan. For ten years, he moved houses four times on average each year because he rented houses that were awaiting demolition. After the houses were demolished, he had to find a new place to live.

The Separated Family

Three generations living apart, wife goes south

Cao Xiangfan's hometown is 200 kilometers away from Changsha, where his parents over seventy and three underage children live. The children live separately with their grandparents and maternal grandparents, while his wife works in Shenzhen.

Cao Xiangfan rarely returns home; it was his former battlefield for the college entrance examination, where the concept of "knowledge changes destiny" originated.

Like many rural children of his generation, Cao Xiangfan wanted to leave the countryside and enter the city, and the college entrance examination was the only way.

Through the high school entrance examination, 16-year-old Cao Xiangfan entered the city, studying at Hanshou No. 2 Middle School. He fell in love with the county town because "the county town has cinemas and Xinhua Bookstore." Cao Xiangfan determined that he would live in the city in the future, and "jumping out of the agricultural gate" became his original motivation for attending university.

Although he loved the city with cinemas, after more than ten years in Changsha, he has never been inside a cinema.

Repeated Failures

"The cooked duck flew away"

In 1987, Cao Xiangfan took the college entrance examination for the first time, scoring 397 points, falling short of the lowest admission line by more than 60 points. He chose to retake the exam. In 1988 and 1989, Cao Xiangfan also failed in the college entrance examination.

Cao Xiangfan felt as if the sky was dark, hiding himself at home and even dared not to go outside, fearing to look into his parents' eyes. He felt that he was "not a real farmer, nor a real intellectual."

In the 1989 college entrance examination, due to immense pressure, Cao Xiangfan fell ill, and the results of the exam were predictable. His classmates at that time had already started getting married and having children, but this was not the life Cao Xiangfan desired.

A year later, "repeatedly failing yet fighting again," Cao Xiangfan appeared in Taiyuan, Shanxi, becoming an "exam migrant." That year, he was admitted, but "because I was an exam migrant, I wasn't accepted." He refused to give up, still wanting to change his fate through the college entrance examination and prove his worth. Before the 1991 college entrance examination, Cao Xiangfan used the 16 yuan given by his family to buy fertilizer to register for the exam in the county town.

That year, Cao Xiangfan passed the junior college score line, applying to the Taxation College in Hunan. "But the school did not admit me because I had a history of mental illness." Cao Xiangfan frankly admitted.

He sometimes thinks that if he had smoothly entered the taxation school at that time, his life trajectory would have been vastly different.

Working in Guangzhou

Worked on CITIC Construction Site

Going to Guangzhou to work was a side note in Cao Xiangfan's life. Due to age restrictions for taking the college entrance examination at the time, Cao Xiangfan, being older, lost his eligibility and joined the ranks of workers. "From 1992 to 1996, I worked on construction sites in Guangzhou, doing all kinds of jobs. When the tallest building in Guangzhou began construction, I mixed concrete on that site. I also participated in the interior decoration of Guangzhou's book center," said Cao Xiangfan.

However, whenever he had free time, he would visit Sun Yat-sen University, dragging a pair of worn-out slippers, with cement stains still on his body. He still remembers the motto of Sun Yat-sen University: "Broad Learning, Careful Inquiry, Prudent Thinking, Clear Discrimination, and Diligent Practice." Departments like the Philosophy Department of Sun Yat-sen University, the International Politics Department of Peking University, the Labor Personnel Department of Renmin University of China, and the Law Department of Southwest University of Political Science and Law... These were all universities and majors that Cao Xiangfan once longed for.

With the help of kind-hearted people, Cao Xiangfan finally took the 1997 college entrance examination. "I passed the junior college line and was admitted to Hunan Radio and Television University. The admission notice also arrived, but the tuition fee was 7,000 yuan per year." Due to financial difficulties, Cao Xiangfan did not take the offer seriously. At that time, Cao Xiangfan had been married for a year. Although he sent out invitations, only one of his high school classmates attended his wedding.

Emotional Crisis

Divorce, Remarriage, and Taking the Exam Again

Cao Xiangfan was not afraid of being seen as a "nonconformist" by his classmates; he decided to continue charging forward in the college entrance examination. In 1998, he took the national adult college entrance examination and was admitted to the International Politics Department of Peking University. "Due to family reasons," Cao Xiangfan applied to the school for a one-year postponement of enrollment and received approval. But the next year, Cao Xiangfan still did not enroll. "At that time, besides the issue of tuition fees, there was also an emotional crisis between us two," Cao Xiangfan told reporters. That period was particularly difficult.

In 2001, the age limit for participating in the college entrance examination was abolished, and Cao Xiangfan immediately returned to Hanshou to register. "I felt the opportunity had come." However, under the dual pressures of an emotional crisis and survival, Cao Xiangfan did not seize this opportunity.

After a tiring tug-of-war, in 2002, Cao Xiangfan finally divorced his ex-wife and then married a girl 14 years younger than him. "Within a month before the college entrance examination, I obtained three certificates: divorce certificate, marriage certificate, and admission ticket." That year's college entrance examination, the Chinese composition topic was "The Choice of the Heart." While writing, Cao Xiangfan poured out his true feelings, recounting the several years of sweet and bitter love with his ex-wife.

"This time, my Chinese score was good, over 110 points, and previously my Chinese scores were always failing. Mainly because the essay was written well," said Cao Xiangfan.

Life Shift

Taking the Exam to Tutor

In the following few years, Cao Xiangfan's purpose for taking the college entrance examination changed. "Taking the exam is for research, to gain more experience when tutoring students," said Cao Xiangfan, adding that his scores in recent years have exceeded the junior college line.

For the past decade, Cao Xiangfan could be described as having "students all over Changsha." While he himself participated in the college entrance examination, he also tutored many senior high school students. "My earliest student who I tutored got into the Finance Department of Hunan University and now serves as the branch manager of a bank."

Since starting to tutor for the college entrance examination in 1998, Cao Xiangfan has continued to teach tirelessly, continuing his struggle with the exam through his "students." "I am currently teaching more than 30 students, and at most, I've taught over 50." Cao Xiangfan calculated that he has tutored a total of 633 students.

Because the students he tutored showed significant improvement, Cao Xiangfan gradually gained a good reputation among parents of senior high school students.

Cao Xiangfan's current life revolves around tutoring for the college entrance examination. He either prepares lessons, travels on buses to students' homes, or is currently tutoring. "Actually, I am a retailer selling knowledge of the college entrance examination."

Old Cao has been asked many similar questions: "Your college entrance examination scores are not outstanding, so why do parents let you tutor their children?" He replied: "This is like the relationship between coaches and athletes. For example, Liu Xiang's coach definitely can't run as fast as Liu Xiang."

Tutoring Income

6000 yuan per month

Tutoring is Cao Xiangfan's main source of income. "6000 yuan per month, more than a regular white-collar worker." However, Cao Xiangfan needs to support three children and his parents, and after deducting his rent and daily expenses, there is not much left.

To this day, his wife works in Shenzhen, which Cao Xiangfan says was driven away by him. In 2007, Cao Xiangfan took the college entrance examination for the thirteenth time, once again passing the junior college line. This time, at the age of 39, Cao Xiangfan entered the Law Department of the Higher Vocational College of Public Security in Hunan, sitting in the same classroom as young people from the "post-90s" generation. Cao Xiangfan said: "At the time, the counselor called me and said that this school didn't have good employment prospects, but the academic atmosphere was very upright. I also wanted to experience university life myself." Cao Xiangfan said.

His wife did not support his actions and warned him that "graduating means unemployment." Cao Xiangfan said that before enrolling, he knew that graduation might lead to unemployment because "over the age of 35, he doesn't qualify to take the civil service exam; the public security, prosecution, and court units in the urban areas of Changsha wouldn't accept him based on his education level; the monthly salary for grassroots staff is only over 1000 yuan, which isn't enough to support a family."

Cao Xiangfan's New Realization

"One shouldn't hang on to just one tree"

During the three years of university life, Cao Xiangfan had to attend classes at school while continuing to tutor students to support his family. Fortunately, the annual scholarships issued by the school solved his tuition problems.

In the first half of this year, Cao Xiangfan is about to graduate, and he hasn't actively looked for a job, knowing that competing for opportunities with students more than 20 years younger than him gives him no advantage. Once, Cao Xiangfan asked a parent of a student working at the procuratorate whether they could arrange a job for him there, and the response was: "I trust you to teach our children; but if you want to work at the procuratorate, you don't even qualify to sweep the floor." This greatly shocked Cao Xiangfan.

Regarding his three years of university experience, Cao Xiangfan said he does not regret it because "life becomes more complete." "Being stubborn is somewhat wrong; one shouldn't hang on to just one tree, especially in a diversified society," Cao Xiangfan said. This realization came to him during his university years and is considered one of the major gains from attending university.

He said that if time could flow backward, he would make a different choice when he took the tax school exam in 1991. "The school didn't admit me because I had a history of mental illness. I could have sought help from society, which might have led to a different outcome."

After Cao Xiangfan's story was reported, it attracted widespread social attention, including from Zhang Chunxian, the Secretary of the Hunan Provincial Committee. Just before the 2006 college entrance examination, Zhang Chunxian sent him blessings, wishing him good luck. Not long after the start of the school term in September 2007, Zhang Chunxian had his secretary call to encourage him: "Young man, keep going!"

Cao Xiangfan said that he plans to write a letter to Zhang Chunxian, applying to "become a teacher at the worst school in Changsha." He feels that although he is over 40 years old, he is "in the prime age for achieving success."

Cao Xiangfan's Total Score in the 2007 College Entrance Examination: 499 Points

Chinese: 108 Points

Mathematics: 114 Points

English: 67 Points

Comprehensive (Liberal Arts): 210 Points
