1. Positioning

Do you want to do virtual goods as a business or use it to boost your credit?

1) Doing virtual goods as a business

It's said: An insider sees the subtleties, an outsider sees the spectacle. Without entering this field, you won't know how it works. Outsiders only see the surface and criticize based on limited knowledge. You can sit there and look down on me now, but when I succeed, I'll glance out the car window at you.

a) First, you must know that the profit margin in virtual goods is embarrassingly low. Earning 0.2 yuan per transaction, charging 1000 times, earns only 200 yuan. By then, replying to customer inquiries might make your hands tired, but how much credit does 1000 transactions bring? You can calculate it yourself. Therefore, when doing virtual goods, you need to clearly understand where the focus lies. It's better to lose a little money on each recharge to gain credit than to wait idly for a few cents. This is something an outsider might already know.

b) Also, you need to be familiar with the operational processes and have a comprehensive understanding of Taobao knowledge to better answer customer questions promptly.

c) Most importantly, after-sales service is crucial. After receiving payment and providing an activation code, most buyers are Taobao beginners who know very little about starting a store. Therefore, our focus should be on after-sales service. It's not just about teaching usage methods; it's about guiding new sellers step by step: three-day virtual repayment—listing items—store decoration—software usage—anti-fraud knowledge—promotion experience. Although these are included in gift packages, as a beginner, they might not know where to start and might consider the gifts useless, leading to complaints. Teaching a new seller takes one to three days, and you need to maintain a positive attitude throughout their sales process, answering all questions patiently. These are things a seller in this industry should do. If done well, customers will be satisfied. Is it still a pyramid scheme? It's just tuition! Outsiders definitely don't know this.

2) Using virtual goods to boost credit

Virtual recharges indeed boost credit quickly because virtual goods eliminate the time needed for packaging and logistics. If clothing, food, housing, and transportation are essential to life, and you have all these covered, what market is the largest now? Just think carefully—it's our telecommunications network. But don't just focus on boosting credit; while upgrading your credit, you need to learn more about physical goods. When you feel your credit is sufficient and you've gained enough experience, you can switch to physical goods. With both credit and experience, isn't it easier than starting with physical goods right away?

2. Credit

As mentioned earlier, virtual goods boost credit quickly, which is normal for sellers who intend to continue with virtual goods. For those transitioning to physical goods, some might say, "This is virtual credit, buyers will turn away," and switching to physical goods means losing the sales record of physical goods, essentially starting over. Indeed, these are concerns worth considering. But remember, most buyers are drawn to credit (at least when I shopped on Taobao, I didn't differentiate between virtual and physical goods). For example, a store with 1 diamond credit (boosted by virtual goods) and another with 1 heart credit, both with 100% positive reviews, selling the same items, which would buyers choose? 80% would choose the 1 diamond store, and virtual credit wouldn't significantly affect this choice. Guangzhou VIP Information Technology Co., Ltd. Every successful virtual transaction leaves a positive review, earned through honest service. Buyers will surely notice this. Additionally, during the recharge process, you can hint that you plan to transition, letting buyers know your integrity and service attitude, ensuring they remain your customers after the transition. Another critical point is that some customers purchasing your virtual items might also browse your physical items if your shop is well-decorated and your physical items are presented nicely. Wouldn't this increase traffic? How much promotion cost and time would be required to achieve this for physical goods? If you still can't weigh the pros and cons after reading this, I'm speechless! Moreover, doing virtual goods allows you to save time learning more, stabilizing your mood while boosting credit. On the other hand, some new sellers of physical goods who persevere often lose money to attract customers through flash sales, one-yuan auctions, price differences, and shipping fee-only auctions—all aimed at gaining credit, losing even more money and exhausting themselves. Yet, after such promotions, few people end up buying anything from the store.

3. Service

Physical goods require service, but virtual goods need it even more. As mentioned earlier, our industry emphasizes after-sales service, full tracking, and ensuring the buyer learns everything. Wholeheartedly teaching the next seller is the essence of proper after-sales service. Many people in this industry assist lower-level sellers with item listings at 2 AM, hastily eating meals to help decorate shops, always feeling there's not enough time in a day. I've tried ordering takeout to my dormitory and only remembered hours later that I hadn't eaten. Although I work full-time, it's extremely tiring. Unless engaging in very complex promotional activities, you need to continuously update your Taobao experience to better serve newcomers. Our responsibility is to teach everyone well, even if they can't make money, ensuring they feel the hundreds of yuan spent was worth it as tuition, allowing them to navigate Taobao smoothly. Therefore, I usually emphasize or even force new sellers to read anti-fraud guides since scammers are everywhere. Commissions are indeed high, sometimes astonishing, but they're earned through service and experience. It's not simply recruiting agents, making money, and walking away. That tarnishes the industry. We are merchants, but also their mentors, helping them avoid unnecessary detours. This is our responsibility and service, and long-term satisfactory after-sales service is our goal.

4. Benefits for New Sellers Doing Virtual Goods

1) Full after-sales service from the upper-level seller helps faster familiarity with the Taobao store-opening process.

2) Learn everything from three-day virtual repayments—item listing—store decoration—software usage—anti-fraud knowledge—promotion experience.

3) Virtual goods quickly boost credit, transforming you from a novice to a seasoned one.

4) More time to learn store management skills while selling virtual goods, understanding how to operate, manage, and develop the store.

5) Have someone to ask questions—your upper-level seller. Of course, finding a good upper-level seller is crucial.

6) Spending a few hundred yuan, even if not directly profitable, boosts credit and buys valuable store-opening experience. Isn't it worth it?
