2. From a technical analysis perspective, the SME Board Composite Index has reached a new high for the year. The closing price of the SME Board Index is at its highest since the rebound on November 28th, only less than 500 points away from the yearly high. It is predicted that the SME Board Index will reach a new high in the near future, meaning there will be at least a 10% increase. After the SME Board Index reaches a new high, the Shenzhen Component Index and the Shanghai Composite Index will also successively reach new highs for the year. On the daily line chart, the Shanghai Composite Index is consolidating above the moving average. It hasn't reached a new high yet, but the low point is lower than Wednesday's, briefly breaking through the 10-day and 20-day moving averages. We believe this short-term breakthrough is invalid and doesn't have practical significance. It just indicates that the adjustment period at this level may be extended. On Friday, the 10-day moving average of the Shanghai Composite Index will fall to the 20-day moving average, where the 20-day moving average at 3231 points has strong support. The 5-day moving average has already started to rise, and it is expected that the index will maintain a strong oscillation above the moving average. According to Shen Guang Qixing LEVEL-2, the index is running above the life line, indicating that the market remains strong. In the next three days, the key points of the life line will be at 3267, 3294, and 3173 points respectively. On Friday, the life line position will move up, and the index needs to continue to rise slightly; otherwise, the strength cannot be sustained.

Short-term outlook remains positive. Meanwhile, the heavyweight stocks represented by finance and real estate are all showing a slight adjustment trend.

The procedures for buying and selling stocks are as follows:

When buying or selling stocks, you must specify whether you are buying or selling, the name (or code) of the stock, the quantity, and the price. This order is only valid for the day it was placed. The order content includes the abbreviation (code) of the stock you want to buy or sell, the quantity, and the price of the stock. The abbreviation of the stock is usually three to four Chinese characters, and the stock code is a six-digit number. When placing an order, the stock code and abbreviation must be consistent. At the same time, there are certain regulations regarding the quantity of stocks bought or sold: the quantity of stocks ordered to buy must be a multiple of 100, but the quantity of stocks ordered to sell does not have to be a multiple of 100.

There are four ways to place an order: counter submission orders, telephone automatic orders, computer automatic orders, and remote terminal orders.
