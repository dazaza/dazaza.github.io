Yan Yan woke up slowly, opening her sleepy eyes. She has always been punctual with her biological clock. Turning to the side, she scrutinized the man lying next to her - thick eyebrows, a straight nose, and lips of moderate thickness; it was a handsome face. Qingqing often reminded her, "Yan Yan, your husband is rich and handsome, so you must be extra vigilant. Don't let those vixens target him, or you'll be left crying before you know it." Every time Yan Yan thought about this, she found it amusing. Only she knew the truth about her marriage—it was all for show. Although Jian Hao maintained his integrity, their marriage had its gaps, much like a cracked egg that would inevitably attract flies and mosquitoes. But Yan Yan didn't care or concern herself with these matters.

Jian Hao usually gets up early as well, exercising in the garden with his father-in-law in the morning, then going to the study to read the morning newspaper. Perhaps due to being more tired these past few days, he returned home slightly later than usual, only getting to bed after midnight. Despite this, Jian Hao tries his best to return home before dinner every day to have meals with his family. However, this doesn't necessarily mean they have a great relationship; he's simply very filial. His in-laws are older and not in the best health, which might explain why they yearn even more for the joy of having their children around them. Moreover, Jian Hao is the only son in the family, and without a doubt, he is indeed filial. As such, even his marriage can be considered a product of filial piety.
