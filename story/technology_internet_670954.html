It's okay not to have breakfast.

by uefbp54187 on 2012-02-21 14:03:17

Can you skip breakfast? No, you absolutely cannot. Breakfast is something that should never be rushed or postponed, and under no circumstances should it be skipped! The negative effects of regularly skipping breakfast are far more serious than you might imagine:

1. Imbalanced nutrition, low immunity: Skipping breakfast over a long period not only leads to insufficient energy and nutrient intake throughout the day but also often causes extreme hunger by around 9 or 10 in the morning. This excessive friction in the intestines can damage the intestinal mucosa, leading to digestive system diseases and malnutrition. As a result, overall immunity decreases significantly, reducing the body's resistance.

2. Increased risk of gallstones: If breakfast is skipped, the stomach continues to move even without food. The gastric acid secreted overnight then irritates the stomach lining, not only damaging the gastric mucosa but also causing bile in the gallbladder to remain unexcreted. Over time, this leads to excessive cholesterol precipitation and deposition, increasing the likelihood of developing gallstones. Studies abroad have shown that among women aged 20-35 with gallstone disease, 80% to 90% have the habit of skipping breakfast.

3. Higher risk of heart attacks: Cardiovascular experts in the U.S. have repeatedly warned those who skip breakfast: if there is no food in the stomach in the morning, the body produces more B-type thromboglobulin in the blood. This protein promotes blood clotting, making one more susceptible to heart attacks.

4. Increased likelihood of blood clots: People who skip breakfast tend to have more viscous and aggregated platelets compared to those who eat breakfast, making them particularly prone to forming blood clots.

5. Weight gain: Skipping breakfast often leads to overeating during lunch and dinner, especially at dinner when one goes to sleep shortly after eating. Over time, the body struggles to digest and absorb the food, leading to excess calories being stored as fat, which easily results in obesity. Therefore, eating breakfast is key to weight management!

6. Accelerated aging: When breakfast is skipped, the body has to rely on stored glycogen and proteins. Over time, this can lead to dry skin, wrinkles, anemia, and accelerated aging.

7. Impact on growth, development, and academic performance: "Brain function largely depends on what you eat in the morning." A survey in the U.S. showed that children who skip breakfast consume less total daily energy, protein, fat, carbohydrates, and certain minerals like calcium, iron, vitamin B2, vitamin B12, vitamin A, and folic acid compared to those who eat breakfast. The nutrients provided by breakfast are difficult to make up for during lunch or dinner. Research involving 1,000 third to sixth-grade students indicated that students who ate breakfast performed better academically than those who did not. Moreover, the quantity and content of breakfast also correlate with academic performance. Therefore, based on all the above, breakfast must definitely be eaten.
