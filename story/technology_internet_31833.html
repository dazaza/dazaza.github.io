E-commerce continues to heat up, boosting the development of Internet industry

by now on 2007-10-12 09:34:19

The latest statistics show that China now has 162 million Internet users, the second highest in the world. Moreover, the number of Chinese netizens is still growing at a rate of 100 new users per minute. Meanwhile, the booming e-commerce models such as B2B, B2C, and C2C are driving further development of the entire Internet industry, especially the prosperity of the Internet service sector. Data from the "China B2C Market Trend Forecast 2006-2010" indicates that by 2010, the scale of China's B2C market will reach 18.83 billion yuan. The vast market prospects have further expanded the Internet service market, particularly the development of the virtual hosting industry.

Many dealers and individuals are gradually recognizing the potential and advantages of business websites and are investing in building e-commerce websites. In general, large enterprises, with their substantial resources and considerations for their own extensive databases and security, prefer to set up their own servers as the best choice. On the other hand, small and medium-sized enterprises and individuals are more inclined to use virtual hosting for website construction because it saves them significant financial and human resources required for managing and maintaining servers. Statistics indicate that 90% of corporate websites choose virtual hosting as their web space, making virtual hosting the preferred method for many netizens to build websites.

The market is the driving force behind technological development, and the enormous economic benefits bring opportunities as well as challenges to service providers. There are numerous virtual hosting providers in China, with varying levels of quality. Only those with solid technology, capable of withstanding tests, will win the market. Therefore, solving the stability and speed issues of virtual hosting has become the focal point of competition among service providers. Currently in China, the intelligent multi-line data center and mirror acceleration engine launched by Now Networks (www.now.cn) is an effective solution to the bottleneck of intercommunication between the North and South regions.

The principle of the intelligent multi-line data center is to intelligently determine and identify whether the online user is using China Telecom or China Unicom, then process accordingly: returning a China Telecom IP address for users on China Telecom and a China Unicom IP address for users on China Unicom, automatically acquiring the fastest route to achieve interoperability between the North and South. Currently, Now Networks has data centers with China Telecom, China Unicom, and in Hong Kong, which also solves the problem of slow intercommunication between domestic and overseas networks. Another solution is the use of GCDN global network mirroring and acceleration engines to achieve cross-regional and cross-carrier network acceleration.

The rapid development of e-commerce has brought new growth momentum to the Internet, presenting new opportunities and challenges to Internet services. Regarding virtual hosting, the demand is rapidly increasing. Without technological innovation, customer needs cannot be met, and providers will inevitably be eliminated by the market. Experts believe that technological progress should keep pace with market trends. Market demand serves as a direction, constraining and guiding the development of technology. Only through coordination can both develop together.
