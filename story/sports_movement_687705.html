California Series Putter 2012

by joshua2011 on 2012-03-02 17:19:42

Scotty Cameron’s new line of California Series putters has been launched with a brand new Sea Mist finish to replace the honey dip finish on the previous line.

The new range features five models - Del Mar, Fastback, Monterey, Monterey 1.5, and Sonoma, characterized by blade and mid-mallet style heads with five different neck styles.

The California putters also feature deeper, more aggressive milling in the face of the putter for a softer sound at impact.

Precision milled from soft 303 stainless steel, the Cameron California putters are fitted with circular weights in the heel and toe that allow each model to be offered at 33”, 34” and 35” lengths, as well as 34”H and 35”H heavier weight configurations. Each putter features a draft sole design that keeps the putter square in the playing position for proper alignment and a high toe set-up that reduces the tendency for players to raise the toe and aim left of target.

Del Mar California Putter: A heel shafted mid mallet for maximum toe flow during the stroke. Designed with a single sightline on the back flange and a flare neck hosel with a ¾ shaft offset.

Fastback California Putter: A modified Newport-style mid mallet design featuring a more compact head, heel-to-toe, with a rounded back flange, three sight lines and a single bend shaft with a full shaft of offset. The Fastback is a near face-balanced putter with minimum toe flow. Formerly offered in the Studio Select line, the Fastback Putter replaces the Hollywood in the California line.

Monterey California Putter: A blade body with smooth double-step flange with a back pocket and single site line, the Monterey features a plumbing neck hosel with a full shaft of offset for medium toe flow.

Monterey: A blade body with smooth double-step flange with a back pocket and single site line, replaces the Coronado model in the California line.

Sonoma California Putter: A compact mid-mallet with a D-shaped back flange and sight line. The Sonoma California features a round flow neck with ¾ shaft of offset for medium toe flow.
