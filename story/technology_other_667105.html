The working process and principle of the rotary kiln clinker cooler

by hx515317 on 2012-02-16 14:29:30

The multi-cylinder or single-cylinder coolers used in the past, due to the cooling air being drawn in by the kiln tail exhaust fan through the rotary kiln and cooler, although the material is lifted by the lifting plates to increase the gas-solid heat exchange area, the relative flow velocity between the gas and solid is small, resulting in a small contact area. Simultaneously, the counter-current heat exchange temperature difference is also small; therefore, the heat exchange efficiency is low.

The working principle of the clinker cooler lies in achieving efficient and rapid gas-solid heat exchange between the clinker and the cooling air. The grate cooler has undergone three generations of technological innovation.

(1) The first generation of grate coolers used compartmental ventilation and thin bed operation. Due to reasons such as particle segregation and uneven feeding, phenomena like "short circuits," "blow-throughs," "red rivers," and "snowmen" frequently occurred, resulting in low thermal efficiency.

(2) The second generation of grate coolers adopted multi-stage grates, optimized the width of the grate bed, ensured uniform feeding, strengthened sealing, and focused on thick bed operation. However, the issues of "short circuits" and "red rivers" were not completely resolved.

(3) It wasn't until the third generation of controlled-flow grate coolers with air beams and resistance grates appeared that the original problems were better addressed. In the third-generation grate cooler, the use of "resistance grates" relatively reduced the impact of changes in the clinker bed resistance on clinker cooling; the use of "air beams" enabled each plate or small section of the hot end grate bed to adjust the cooling airflow based on changes in the resistance above the grate; at the same time, high-pressure fans were used to reduce the amount of cooling air, increase the relative velocity and contact area between gas and solid, thereby greatly improving the heat exchange efficiency.

(4) Due to the optimized design in structure and material of the resistance grates, their service life and operating rate have been improved. Although the "resistance grates" solved many problems caused by uneven distribution of the clinker bed, their high resistance led to high power consumption. Therefore, the new generation of grate coolers developed towards "controlled flow." After eliminating the "resistance grates," air beams are used for sectional or zonal blowing, automatically adjusting the cooling air pressure and volume based on the resistance of the material layer on the grate, achieving efficient and rapid gas-solid heat exchange. At the same time, due to the wear caused by the movement of the clinker by the movable grates, the new generation of grate coolers is also developing towards fixed beds such as rod-type and pendulum-type.

The continuous innovation of various types of new grate coolers not only significantly improves the heat exchange efficiency, reduces the cooling air volume, lowers the clinker outlet temperature from the grate cooler, and achieves rapid cooling of the clinker, but also further increases the secondary air temperature entering the kiln and the tertiary air temperature entering the furnace, optimizing the production process of the entire pre-decomposition kiln system.
