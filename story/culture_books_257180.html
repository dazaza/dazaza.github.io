The Secret Behind the Calm Lives of Middle-Class Couples in "The Hydrangea Diary"

by jqrxass on 2009-11-07 21:00:14

Shigeta Kawanishi and Shimako are a very typical middle-class couple in Japan. The husband runs a private hospital, while the wife stays at home to take care of the housework. Their seemingly peaceful life was completely disrupted when the husband accidentally discovered a diary in his wife's bedroom...

Chapter One of "Ajisai Diary"

He continued lying on the bed that still smelled of his wife, barely regaining his strength. It had been three months since he had touched this bed. In other words, it had been three months since he and his wife had been intimate.

No, the last time he had asked his wife for intimacy, but she refused, saying, "I'm too tired." In fact, it had been more than half a year since they had engaged in sexual activity.

Perhaps we have become one of those "sexless marriages" that people often talk about these days. However, among couples over the age of forty-five, such "sexless marriages" are no longer uncommon.

Chapter Two of "Ajisai Diary"

Upon reading this, Shigeta felt a chill run down his back.

This was nothing short of absolute terror, and it wasn't some fictional ghost story. This was a crisis in real life.

In short, if things were left to continue like this, this kind of wife could do anything. She might even check the hospital staff list to find that woman's address. He should have thought of a solution earlier to prevent such an event from happening.

Chapter Three of "Ajisai Diary"

The diary ended here. Shigeta sat on the sofa, tightly holding his head, his body completely still.

The diary he had just read was terrifying. No, it made him see things he had never imagined. Could there be wives in the world who criticized and viewed their husbands' sexual encounters so coldly?

"What is all this nonsense?"

He felt an urge to scold someone fiercely, but it was a diary; what could he do? He had secretly read something that was originally written with no intention of being shown to anyone, so he couldn't get angry.

Chapter Four of "Ajisai Diary"

If he wanted to get intimate with her, now was the only chance. Shigeta steeled himself, approached from behind, and suddenly threw his arms around his wife's shoulders.

For a moment, his wife screamed, "What are you doing..." and struggled, trying hard to shake off Shigeta's hands.

Of course, Shigeta intended to embrace her. Despite her resistance, he held on tightly. His wife fought back desperately, shrank her body, and slipped out from under Shigeta's arms. With a strong push, she banged into the sideboard and fell to the ground.

Chapter Five of "Ajisai Diary"

Thus, without much change, another day passed.

He had been constantly worried that something serious might happen, but nothing did. Days went by with alternating tension and relief. Shigeta thought, perhaps he should just go with the flow and leave it to fate.

Has his wife decided not to make a big deal out of this matter? Just as Shigeta began to think this way, another opportunity to read the diary came on a Sunday in late October.
