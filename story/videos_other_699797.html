**Spyker**

Country: Netherlands

Brand Establishment Year: 1875

Brand Style: A perfect combination of beautiful art and technology, luxurious and eternal, unconventional

Classic Model: C8 Spyder (starting at 48.8 million yuan)

Spyker, as a factory with over a hundred years of history, has always maintained its unique brand style. The sports cars they produce are all handmade, featuring a unique lightweight body, large-displacement mid-mounted engine, rear-wheel drive, with many indicators adopting F1 standards, possessing astonishing performance parameters. In 1898, the Spijker brothers Jacobus and Hendrik-Jan manufactured their first car using a Mercedes engine in Amsterdam, immediately earning applause for their excellent body manufacturing skills. That same year, to celebrate the upcoming coronation of Queen Wilhelmina of the Netherlands, Spijker created the famous Gold Coronation Carriage, which is still in use today. From that time on, the Spijker brothers entered a turning point in their careers: dedicating Spyker Company entirely to automobile manufacturing. To better adapt to foreign markets, they changed "ij" in Spijker to "y", thus renaming the company Spyker. As a well-known luxury sports car manufacturer, the noble sports cars made by Spyker are fully customized according to customer needs. In 2005, Spyker increased production to around 100 cars per year. Not only do we produce for limited esteemed owners, but each model is also an absolute collector's item. Spyker launches a new car every year, and when global production reaches 500 units, production immediately stops. Spyker's target market is the highest-end users in the sports car market, concentrated mainly in the United States, Western Europe, the Middle East, Greater China, and some smaller wealthy countries such as Switzerland and Monaco.

**Rolls-Royce**

Country: UK

Brand Establishment Year: 1904

Brand Style: Established in 1904

Classic Model: Phantom (starting at 50 million yuan)

Mr. Rolls was a nobleman and racer, sociable and widely connected, who had always wanted to produce a truly British car. Mr. Royce was an outstanding engineer, versatile, and very interested in Rolls' plan, leading to their partnership in producing famous British cars. The first Rolls-Royce car was born in Manchester, beginning a highly acclaimed career. In 1906, Charles Rolls and Henry Royce co-founded Rolls-Royce. Initially, Rolls-Royce engaged in aircraft engine manufacturing, with automobiles being a subsequent product; they continue to produce aircraft engines today. The sculptural trademark of Rolls-Royce is a shimmering silver flying goddess statue, known as the "Flying Goddess," symbolizing the spirit of speed. Its origin dates back to 1911 when, through the introduction of Sir Montagu, Rolls met Charles Sykes, an artist and sculptor for The Motor Magazine. He earnestly requested Charles Sykes to design a sculptural trademark for "Rolls-Royce." Sykes used Miss Eleanor Thornton from the magazine's editorial office as a model to design the "Flying Goddess," meaning the soul of speed. On February 6, 1911, the "Flying Goddess" descended upon Rolls-Royce, immersing the entire world in fresh air and the melodious music of wing vibrations. The inauguration ceremony for the "Flying Goddess" was as grand and enthusiastic as the launch of the first Rolls-Royce car. The logo pattern of Rolls-Royce uses two overlapping "R"s, symbolizing you in me and me in you, reflecting the harmonious relationship between the two founders.

**Bugatti**

Country: France

Brand Establishment Year: 1909

Brand Style: A perfect combination of technology and art, luxurious comfort, easy to drive

Classic Models: Brescia, Royale, Atalantes, Pur Sang

Bugatti is a world-famous old sports car brand established in 1909 by Italian Ettore Bugatti in Germany, specializing in the production of sports cars and high-end luxury sedans. Due to changes in national boundaries based on geographical location, Bugatti was located in Germany from 1909-1918, in France from 1919-1956, and then in Italy after re-establishing in 1991. The English letters in the Bugatti logo represent Bugatti, with EB at the top being the abbreviation of Ettore Bugatti's English name, surrounded by small dots symbolizing ball bearings, with a red background color. Bugatti cars are among the most preserved classic old cars, with models under the Bugatti brand visible in many famous car museums worldwide. They feature superior performance, novel and fluid body designs, and distinctive engine configurations.

**Maserati**

Country: Italy

Brand Establishment Year: 1914

Brand Style: Dignified elegance without ostentation, extreme speed while enjoying comfortable driving pleasure

Classic Models: Quattroporte, GranTurismo (starting at 17.9 million yuan)

Maserati is a renowned Italian racing and sports car producer founded in 1914 in Bologna. The company's headquarters is now located in Modena, and their emblem is a trident. Maserati was once part of Ferrari (a company where Fiat holds a majority stake) for several years before becoming directly owned by Fiat. The Maserati brothers—Alfieri Maserati, Bindo Maserati, Carlo Maserati, Ettore Maserati, Ernesto Maserati, and Mario Maserati—all became involved in the automotive industry in the early 20th century. Alfieri, Bindo, and Ernesto manufactured 2-liter Grand Prix racing cars for Diatto. In 1926, Diatto ceased production of racing cars, leading to the creation of the first Maserati masterpiece and the establishment of the Maserati model line. The first Maserati, driven by Alfieri, won the Targa Florio in 1926. Maserati began producing race cars with 4, 6, 8, and 16-valve engines (essentially two straight-eight valves mounted together). Mario, an artist, is credited with designing the company's emblem: a trident. After Alfieri Maserati passed away in 1932, his three brothers continued to run the company, producing race-winning vehicles.

**Aston Martin**

Country: UK

Brand Establishment Year: 1914

Brand Style: Unique styling, exquisite craftsmanship, superior performance

Classic Model: DBS (starting at 41 million yuan)

Aston Martin was originally a British luxury sedan and sports car manufacturer founded in 1913 by Lionel Martin and Robert Bamford. The company is located in Newport, known worldwide for producing convertible touring cars, racing cars, and limited-production sports cars. Participating in races is undoubtedly an important means of developing car production, but it is costly and poorly managed, leading to its acquisition by Ford USA in 1987. The Aston Martin logo features a soaring eagle with the words Aston and Martin inscribed, symbolizing the company’s great ambition and speed akin to a diving eagle. The words Aston and Martin indicate a "triple alliance" car company. The renowned Aston Martin Lagonda company, famous for producing convertible touring cars, racing cars, and limited-production sports cars, owes its fame partly to the luck brought by this eagle. Mentioning Aston Martin brings to mind the legendary stories of James Bond (007), seemingly attributing Aston Martin's success to him.

**Bentley**

Country: UK

Brand Establishment Year: 1919

Brand Style: Offers an exceptional driving experience, combining traditional handcrafted techniques with powerful sports performance

Classic Models: Arnage 728 (starting at 99.8 million yuan), Arnage RL (starting at 54.8 million yuan), Continental GT (starting at 30.8 million yuan), Continental Flying (starting at 29.8 million yuan)

The Bentley brothers founded Bentley Motors, previously translated as Bentleys, but officially renamed "Bentley" when it first entered China in 2001. Walter Owen Bentley served as an apprentice on the railways before moving into the automotive industry. In 1912, he and his brother Horace Milner founded Bentley Motors in London, selling French DFP cars. He improved these new cars by replacing their engines with aluminum pistons, enhancing their performance. During World War I, he worked for the British Royal Naval Air Service Technical Committee, improving the Clerget engine, which became famous as BR1 and BR2. In 1919, W.O. Bentley returned to selling DFP cars and introduced the Bentley 3.0 named after himself. This 3-liter engine produced 85 horsepower, offering speeds above 80 mph, dominating numerous races and winning five consecutive championships at Le Mans in 1924, 1927, 1928, 1929, and 1930. Bentley Motors embarked on a journey to professionally design high-end sports cars and racing cars. In 1935, Bentley left to join Lagonda, working there for 11 years until his death in 1971. The Bentley car logo features the first letter "B" of the company name with wings, resembling a soaring eagle, a symbol that has been used ever since. Previously, a flying "B" emblem was also used.

**Maybach**

Country: Germany

Brand Establishment Year: 1921

Brand Style: Distinguished elegance, ultimate personality, perfectionism

Classic Models: Maybach 57S (73.1 million yuan), 62S (83 million yuan)

Daimler-Chrysler Group announced in New York that the German ultra-luxury car brand Maybach, dormant for 60 years, was officially revived. Initially launched were the Maybach 57 and Maybach 62, produced by Mercedes-Benz, priced at 310,000 euros and 360,000 euros respectively. These prices are astonishing, approximately equivalent to 3.5 times the price of a Mercedes S600 or 2.5 times the price of a Ferrari 360. Even the prestigious Rolls-Royce and Bentley only match the high-end products of Maybach 62. Some have defined the resurrected Maybach as "Germany's Rolls-Royce." While this statement makes sense, it is not entirely accurate. Because Rolls-Royce, the pride of British automotive industry, will soon become a "true-blue" German product under BMW Group. Another ultra-luxury brand, Bentley, has already joined the Volkswagen Group. Currently, BMW Group is busy updating Rolls-Royce and may introduce a new model in the coming years. Under this context, Daimler Group's launch of Maybach cars is clearly a strategic consideration, positioning Maybach as a sharp blade in the ultra-luxury car market, not allowing BMW to monopolize this market alone. Compared to Rolls-Royce's "Angel" emblem, Maybach's double M emblem is much simpler. Initially, double M represented "Maybach cars," now it signifies "Made by Maybach." The Maybach emblem consists of two intersecting Ms encircled in a spherical triangle, reminding people of its legendary cars and era through brand promotion: alongside Mercedes models, they became absolute classics among German automakers at the time. Their exquisite technology, supreme quality, and unique style made them the focus of attention worldwide. Each car is independently crafted according to user specifications, with engines and chassis entirely handcrafted.

**Ferrari**

Country: Italy

Brand Establishment Year: 1947

Brand Style: Speed and passion, innovation and courage

Classic Models: F430 (27 million yuan), F430 Spider (32 million yuan), 599 GTB (43.5 million yuan), 612 Scaglietti (43.8 million yuan)

Ferrari is an Italian car manufacturer primarily focused on Formula One racing, racing cars, and high-performance sports cars, founded in 1929 by Enzo Ferrari. Early Ferrari sponsored racers and produced racing cars before independently manufacturing cars in 1946, eventually reaching its current scale. It is now owned by Fiat Group, headquartered near Modena, Maranello. Italian cars are referred to as "two kings and one queen"—the two kings being "Ferrari" and "Lamborghini," and the queen being "Maserati." Enzo Ferrari, the founder of Ferrari Automobiles, said that his favorite racecar was the one he hadn't yet built, and his greatest success was the one he hadn't yet achieved. This Italian man, whose blood boiled with a passion for cars from a young age, finally convinced his father at age 13 to begin his own independent driving history. The roar of engines on the racetrack, the excitement and thrill of competition, drove him to greater heights. He raced Alfa Romeos, achieving repeated victories and earning the nickname "Knight" from his teammates. From participating in races to forming a racing club, he eventually founded his own car company. Today's Ferrari is known as the "Red Lightning." The shield emblem of Ferrari features a black prancing horse against the color of Modena's golden canary feathers. The prancing horse originates from Enzo Ferrari's birthplace Modena and carries a legendary heroic aura. During World War I, the highly decorated Italian pilot Francesco Baracca first adopted this emblem as his personal badge, painting it on his airplane. After the war, Baracca's parents entrusted this prancing horse emblem to Enzo Ferrari, who placed it on a yellow shield as the team's symbol. Later, following the brave pilot's death, the horse turned black, with the Italian flag added above as a tribute.

**Porsche**

Country: Germany

Brand Establishment Year: 1948

Brand Style: Ultimate daily-use sports car

Classic Model: Porsche 911 Turbo (19.5 million yuan)

At the age of 24 (in 1899), Ferdinand Porsche invented the electric wheel hub motor, and at the next year's Paris International Exhibition, Porsche was already renowned worldwide. Porsche cars have distinct characteristics: beetle-shaped bodies, rear-mounted engines, and excellent performance, quickly making them well-known. In 1963, at the Frankfurt International Auto Show, Porsche unveiled the 911 model, a design that remains popular to this day. The designer of the body was Ferry Porsche's eldest son, Ferdinand Alexander Porsche. The Porsche 928 with rear transaxle chassis and V-8 engine, the new technology 955 model, and the 911 Carrera 4 with electronically adjustable four-wheel drive system and lift-free body are the most important developments in recent years. Porsche (PORSCHE) was formerly translated as "Porsche," and its emblem adopts the shield-shaped emblem of Stuttgart city. In the center of the Porsche emblem is a galloping horse, representing a famous breed of horses from Stuttgart, with "Stuttgart" written above to indicate the company's headquarters in Stuttgart. The upper left and lower right of the emblem feature antlers, indicating that Stuttgart was once a great place for hunting. The upper right and lower left yellow stripes represent mature wheat, symbolizing a bountiful harvest, while black represents fertile land, and red represents human wisdom and love for nature. Together, they form a beautiful pastoral landscape, symbolizing Porsche's glorious past and bright future.

**Lamborghini**

Country: Italy

Brand Establishment Year: 1963

Brand Style: Wildness and Passion

Classic Model: Lamborghini Murcielago (48.8 million yuan)

Lamborghini is also translated as Langboqini or Linbaojiani. Full name: Ferruccio Lamborghini Automobili S.P.A. (Italian Ferruccio Lamborghini Automobile Joint Stock Company). The Lamborghini emblem is a fierce bull charging forward, aligning with the characteristics of Lamborghini's high-horsepower, high-speed sports cars. It is said that this emblem also reflects the unyielding temperament of the founder, Ferruccio Lamborghini. Ferruccio Lamborghini, the founder of Lamborghini S.P.A., along with early-produced sports cars and tractors, embodies the special Italian passion and determination that inspired him to rise from a farmer's son to a respected industry leader through hard work. This Northern Italian, with an uncompromising spirit and almost crazy enthusiasm, tirelessly pursued the dream of creating the perfect sports car. Since then, the stubborn "bull" emblem has become the symbol of Lamborghini, interpreting all the characteristics of this unique car brand — challenging limits, proud and extraordinary, bold and unrestrained.
