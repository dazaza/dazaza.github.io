Tomorrow marks the end of the first month linking drunk driving with mandatory traffic insurance rates. The linkage is aimed at curbing drunk driving.

Summer Enters the "Sober Driver" Off-Peak Season

Mr. Wang, a manager of a well-known company, hosted a few distinguished guests in Wuhan recently. Naturally, he spared no effort to entertain them as a hospitable host.

After drinking heartily, Mr. Wang ensured that all guests were safely delivered home. With years of experience in business, Mr. Wang was very considerate and arranged in advance for five drivers from a Wuhan car service company to provide sober driving services for himself and his friends who brought their cars to the banquet.

Hu Bin was one of the drivers booked that night. Around 8 p.m., he and his colleagues arrived at the designated hotel on time and safely delivered all guests to their destinations.

Hu Bin is a department manager in the company. Sober driving isn't his full-time job; during the day, he mainly provides rescue, insurance, and annual inspection services. In the afternoon or evening, he offers sober driving services based on the company's unified scheduling.

Contrary to many people's expectations, Hu Bin explained that summer, when beer consumption peaks, is actually the off-season for the sober driving industry because there are fewer official banquets. Currently, he averages two to three orders per day and usually gets home around 10 p.m. "From my interactions with customers, those who hire sober drivers are generally private entrepreneurs, company bosses, and senior executives," he revealed. Most customers who hire sober drivers are people who value quality experiences and rarely hire just one driver; they often hire multiple drivers to serve several guests at the same table.

"Ninety percent of our clients are rich second-generation individuals— young 80s, 90s, and even 00s generations. Their views seem different from older generations. For them, spending a hundred yuan or so to hire a sober driver is worth it for peace of mind," another sober driver, Mr. Hu, introduced. Depending on the distance and time, sober driving service fees vary but generally fall around 100 yuan. There are no specialized sober driving companies in Wuhan yet. "Most people ask friends or relatives to drive after drinking, so only doing sober driving business would starve the company."

Deng Hui, General Manager of Beijing Dayicheng Sober Driving Service, introduced that about 50 car service companies of various sizes in Wuhan offer sober driving services. Calculating an average of five sober drivers per company per night, approximately 200-300 people in Wuhan hire sober drivers each night.

The Industry Has Gone Through Three Development Stages Over More Than a Year

In the revised Traffic Safety Law implemented in 2004, the highest punishment for drunk driving was raised to 15 days of detention, a four-month suspension of the driver's license, and a fine of 2000 yuan. A third offense results in a permanent revocation of the driver's license and a "five-year driving ban." The untimely death of famous comedian Niu Zhenhua also served as a warning bell for many alcohol-loving drivers. Incidents like the Hangzhou 70 km/h case provided fertile ground for the emergence of a new industry—"sober driving." In 2004, the first dedicated "sober driving" company appeared in Beijing.

According to introductions, the appearance of "sober driving" in Wuhan was relatively late, starting just over a year ago. During this period, the industry roughly went through three stages: before May 2009 was the first stage, during which sober driving mostly happened privately, and the industry developed spontaneously; from May 2009 to the Spring Festival of 2010 was the second stage, during which the state conducted a concentrated crackdown on drunk driving violations, greatly stimulating the development of the sober driving industry; after the Spring Festival of 2010 until now is the third stage, during which the industry has returned to calm growth after the concentrated crackdown ended.

For many insiders, the second stage was the "golden era." During this period, traffic management departments carried out a two-month concentrated crackdown on drunk driving violations. The strict enforcement by traffic authorities and intensive media coverage had a significant deterrent effect on "drunk drivers." As a result, there was a huge demand for sober driving services in the market. Under this background, various sober driving service companies sprang up like mushrooms after rain, and some drivers even advertised individually in newspapers and websites to compete for sober driving business.

This was the peak period for sober driving in Wuhan. Besides strict law enforcement by traffic police, another reason was the arrival of the business season. "That period was extremely busy. Not only was I busy at night, but I was rarely idle during lunchtime. Sometimes I handled seven or eight orders a day, which was two or three times my normal workload. The latest time I worked was until four in the morning," said driver Abin.

Annual Market Size Could Reach 60 Million Yuan

Every year, 110,000 people die in China due to traffic accidents, increasing at an average rate of 10% annually. Drunk driving, especially driving under severe intoxication, has become one of the major killers causing traffic accidents. "From this perspective, sober driving services can, to a certain extent, reduce the possibility of traffic accidents caused by drunk driving. Sober driving is beneficial in reducing drunk driving behavior," experts analyzed. Sober driving services are a rapidly developing emerging service industry adapting to new social living conditions. Sober driving services provide a relatively safe choice for car owners who drink alcohol and objectively promote the implementation of national road traffic safety laws, contributing to improved public safety order and family happiness.

It is understood that currently, there is a good realistic foundation for the development of post-drinking driving businesses, including: with the improvement of people's living standards, more and more people own cars; Chinese people have the tradition of "no banquet without wine," meaning there will inevitably be drinking whenever there is a gathering; inviting friends or relatives to drive after drinking often involves owing favors, whereas spending about a hundred yuan to hire a sober driver avoids such inconveniences; some corporate bosses or government officials, although equipped with drivers, may not want to bring them to certain occasions, creating potential demand for sober driving services, etc.

Industry insiders made a rough estimate: currently, Wuhan has nearly 1 million registered vehicles. Calculating an equal number of male and female drivers, there are approximately 500,000 male drivers. Assuming that one out of every ten male drivers drinks at a banquet, around 50,000 male drivers need sober driving services daily. If each person hires a sober driver once a month at 100 yuan per session, the market size for sober driving in Wuhan could reach 60 million yuan annually. "The future of the sober driving market is promising and holds great potential," Deng Hui said.

Integrity Is the Bottleneck of Industry Development

Although industry insiders generally have optimistic views about the market prospects of sober driving, reality is less than ideal. "When traffic police enforce strictly, the sober driving business thrives. Without concentrated crackdowns, many companies' businesses plummet significantly," said the head of a Wuhan car service company.

However, some companies have found new ways to break through the bottleneck by collaborating with banks to provide additional services to VIP bank customers. In these cases, the bank covers part of the cost while the customer only pays about 50 yuan for the round-trip transportation fee of the sober driver. "We don’t take on unfamiliar customers; we only serve VIP customers from banks and other organizations. VIP customers have verifiable records, making them trustworthy and easier to work with," said the head of a car service company in Hankou. Currently, the company has signed agreements with multiple banks, including Hua Xia and China Merchants Bank, with good operational results.
