The well-known delicacies of mountains and seas in ancient China also included fish maw, fish lip, scallop, prawn, wild chicken, duck breast, Chinese brown frog, monkey head mushroom, silver ear fungus, etc. As for the "dragon liver and phoenix marrow" in the imperial court's recipe, they were nothing more than extremely precious food for the emperor's diet. "Dragon liver" was substituted by the liver of a white horse, and phoenix marrow was represented by the bone marrow of a pheasant.

Mountain delicacies and seafood are prestigious and rare foods produced in the mountains and seas. They are the essence part of food. Bear's paw, bird's nest, shark fin, sea cucumber, etc., often played the leading role in the ancient people's recipes.

Bird's nest. The nest built by the golden swallow on the cliffs of coastal counties in Fujian and Guangdong is called bird's nest. It is made from the sticky liquid that the golden swallow spits out after swallowing fish, shrimp, and seaweed digested by its gastric juice, which is nutritious for human consumption.

Shark fin, sea cucumber. Shark fin is the dried and processed shark fin, and sea cucumber is a kind of echinoderm in the sea. In ancient grand celebration banquets, shark fin and sea cucumber were used as the main dishes, known as the fin banquet (shark fin banquet) and sea cucumber banquet.

Shad. Shad comes to market once a year during the transition between spring and summer. Shad lives in the ocean and spawns in the Yangtze River and Qiantang River in spring. The big ones can be three feet long, and it is one of the delicious fish in China.

Camel hump. Using camel humps as ingredients, they can be steamed or roasted. In Du Fu's poem "The Beauty Parade" of the Tang Dynasty: "The purple camel peak emerges from the green pot, and the crystal plate serves the silver scales", these two dishes, camel hump and silver scales, were examples to describe the exquisite diet life of Emperor Xuanzong of Tang and Consort Yang.

Deer tail, regarded as a delicacy by the ancients. During the Ming and Qing Dynasties, banquets in Beijing emphasized deer tails most. In the book "Dream of Red Mansions", Wu Jinxiao, the head of the manor, submitted a list of annual rent and gifts to the Jia family, with the first item being thirty whole deer. Deer tail, deer tongue, deer tendon, venison, deer blood, and deer penis are not only delicious but also great tonics.

Bear's paw. Mencius said: "Fish is what I desire, bear's paw is also what I desire. If I cannot have both, I will give up fish and take bear's paw." Cooked bear's paw is tender, smooth, and has its own charm worth savoring. Bear's paw stew is a famous dish in ancient recipes.
