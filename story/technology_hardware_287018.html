by tuoqing05 on 2009-12-01 01:52:34

Survey Activity Background

With the rapid development of the Internet, more and more people are paying attention to website construction and maintenance. Through setting up personal or corporate websites, individuals can start their own businesses, and companies can promote themselves. The group known as "webmasters" is also growing rapidly. According to statistics released by the MIIT's ICP and IP Filing Management Platform, by the end of 2008, the number of registered websites had exceeded 2.85 million.

Despite the explosive growth in the number of websites, significant security risks still lurk behind this phenomenon.

From security announcements issued by Microsoft and various domestic security software vendors, apart from kernel vulnerabilities in Win series server systems, websites frequently suffer from malware attacks, DDoS attacks, cross-site scripting (XSS) attacks, injection attacks, ARP attacks, and more. Data shows that in the first half of 2009, the number of web pages infected with malware exceeded 200 million, with millions of Internet users browsing these compromised sites daily. From personal websites to corporate networks, from government websites to popular software, none have been spared from the harm caused by these attacks. In some cases, attackers directly target DNS servers or DNS service providers, disrupting internet stability and causing large-scale internet outages in China, resulting in nationwide network disconnections. These signs indicate that the situation regarding network security is critical. Meanwhile, there are very few investigation reports on website security available online, and most website owners are unaware of the security information related to their own sites, which is one of the fundamental reasons for the occurrence of network security issues.

Behind the rapid growth of websites, server security and website security have been neglected due to various problems, failing to meet the security needs of websites. This has led to webmasters and enterprises suffering substantial economic and reputational losses due to security issues.

NSFOCUS, as a leading domestic cybersecurity company, facing the new situation of internet security threats, has begun to focus on small and medium-sized websites and internet security. They will soon launch a web security service platform to provide professional internet security services to small and medium-sized websites.

In January 2009, NSFOCUS conducted a "Personal Website Security Status Survey" at a meeting of individual webmasters in Shanghai. This time, in collaboration with ChinaZ, they are conducting the "2009 Small and Medium-Sized Website Security Status Survey," which is the first activity by domestic cybersecurity companies targeting small and medium-sized websites and personal websites. It is hoped that the entire internet industry will pay attention to the security challenges and hidden dangers faced by small and medium-sized websites, ultimately building a green, peaceful, and secure internet ecosystem comprehensively.
