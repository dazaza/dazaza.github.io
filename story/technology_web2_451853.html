II. In terms of personnel governance

by dmakest9 on 2011-06-25 17:15:17

It has been half a year since I graduated from university. After graduation, I didn't feel the difficulty of finding a job. Perhaps it was due to a stroke of luck; I smoothly found a job for myself. Now, I have already been working at the company for half a year and things are going pretty well. I believe that in my future work, I can do better, but currently, I still need more effort. As I continue to grow, I have developed a clearer understanding of my own work. I believe that in my future career, I will proceed more smoothly.

In July of this year, I obtained my first job at xxx Company through a recruitment fair, with passion for my career and feelings of naivety towards a new life, I embarked on my position in the administrative personnel department. As a newly graduated college student stepping out of school gates, with a major not entirely relevant and without any work experience, everything seemed strange yet fresh. In my work, I always remained humble in seeking guidance, diligently performed my duties, and strived to do my best in my responsibilities. Over the past year, though I haven’t achieved grand results, I have certainly gone through a period of testing and tempering. For anyone who seeks progress, an annual "inventory" of oneself is inevitable, serving as a form of self-motivation.

I. Administrative Work Aspect

1. Summarizing oil prices, organizing energy reporting information, conducting monthly and annual price trend analysis.

I have never treated this work purely as a business-related task outside the scope of administrative affairs because everyone has the opportunity to contribute wisdom and ability for the company's growth. Since administrative work ultimately serves the company’s growth, perhaps my ideas lack originality, my methods may be inappropriate, or my abilities might be minimal, and even when no one pays attention, this might just be a trivial matter. However, when the business needs a certain data point, it becomes timely and necessary - this is the value of accumulation. Perhaps doing a little bit every day will lead to a time when there will be rewards for such efforts.

2. Handling enterprise business license annual review and changes in industrial and commercial filing materials and other related matters.

Handling industrial and commercial administrative affairs related to the enterprise is also part of interacting with government agencies, similar to handling social security, housing funds, and talent-related personnel work. However, although the setting of the industrial and commercial administrative office hall is humanized, their work attitude and methods cannot be praised. This indeed caused me significant difficulties at the beginning. Thanks to the strong support of the leadership of xxx Company and the great help of other administrative colleagues, I had the opportunity to gradually mature through repeated exercises, improving my efficiency in handling affairs. Now, I can independently complete the entire process of enterprise change and actively cooperate with the finance department and the business department to solve administrative matters related to industry and commerce.

3. Handling visas for company leaders.

Leaders often need to handle visas for business purposes. From being completely clueless when I first took over this work to becoming a "consultant" for other administrative staff in the company handling visas for leaders, this has been made possible by the meticulous guidance of many colleagues and my own diligence (this article comes from Dianliang Network http://www.dianliang.com, please retain this mark if reprinted). Under their guidance, I handled nearly ten visa applications for leaders traveling to Singapore, the UK, the USA, etc. Filling out forms, translating original documents, preparing photos and fees, backing up materials, submitting materials, verifying fingerprints, interviews, collecting visas... Each country has different application conditions and policies, different application forms, and procedures; what is common is that completing this work requires excellent English skills and the ability to continuously update one's knowledge. In the process of handling visas for leaders, I learned a lot of related knowledge and realized the importance and necessity of studying English and reading a wider range of knowledge. Therefore, only by tirelessly improving oneself can one competently handle seemingly simple administrative tasks.

4. Other administrative work

Administrative work is intricate, ranging from copying, scanning, faxing, ordering meals, renting flowers, sending express packages, printing business cards, renewing websites, subscribing to newspapers and magazines, preserving vehicle files, simple computer maintenance, to larger tasks like settling accounts, booking rooms, purchasing air tickets, handling employee reimbursements, negotiating office furniture prices with decoration companies, assisting leaders in donating to Hope Projects, purchasing office supplies, and registering their usage... The completion of each task is a test of responsibility and work ability. How to simplify complexity while ensuring accuracy, how to achieve the highest efficiency with the smallest cost, these are no longer merely requirements for business personnel in modern enterprises, but also apply to administrative staff.

II. Human Resource Management Aspect

1. Recruiting new employees

Based on the actual needs of the business department, the HR department began targeted and reasonable employee recruitment work in August. Reflecting on my own job search experience six months ago, I feel it is my duty to respect every job seeker. Starting from the company's interests and needs, adhering to the principles of openness, fairness, and impartiality in selecting talents, I ensure the first gatekeeping role in recruiting top-notch talents for the company. I can carefully screen each applicant's resume and treat every candidate who has the opportunity to come for an interview with the utmost enthusiasm, laying a good foundation for the further selection of new employees by the company's leadership.

2. Formulating and implementing relevant HR management systems

Upon arriving at the company, coinciding with organizational restructuring, xxx formulated a series of HR management systems matching the company's growth and execution based on its own situation, and I was responsible for the company's attendance statistics work. During the implementation process, I could quickly adapt to the company's policy arrangements, striving to accurately record attendance, using statistical data as the basis for timely recharging of employee meal cards at the beginning of each month, and providing good logistical support for everyone.

3. Archiving personnel files

I know that timely updating the communication information of all sister companies and related partner units plays a crucial role in the company's business growth. Therefore, I have never treated the detailed and accurate archiving and sorting of personnel files as ordinary HR work. I can categorize backups both on paper and electronically, strictly review all employee files, fully supplement any incomplete ones, and promptly copy and distribute the latest information to all leaders and clerks of xxx Company for reference.

4. Paying social insurance premiums and housing fund contributions, managing talent, social security, and housing fund accounts

Since I am also newly employed, I can say that I am completely unfamiliar with the specific policies and execution procedures regarding social insurance, housing funds, and the management of talent files at the Personnel Bureau. Facing monthly reports and frequent personnel additions and reductions throughout the year, I know that only by asking frequently, learning well, thinking hard, and acting diligently can I be competent in this work. The success or failure of social insurance, housing funds, and talent file management directly affects the personal interests of every employee, the credibility of the enterprise, and the harmony of the relationship between the enterprise and employees. The leadership entrusting me with such an important task reflects trust and a test of my work ability. To not betray this trust, I boldly worked, asking whenever I didn't understand, and received guidance and assistance from many experienced HR and financial management professionals. Additionally, I myself often browsed official websites of government service departments or sought advice from government clerks on-site to learn and improve, enabling further development of work. Now, I can independently and proficiently perform the routine work of social insurance, housing funds, and talent files. This would not have been possible without the active cooperation and enthusiastic help of every colleague.

As an administrative personnel officer, I fully realize that I am both a manager and an executor. Just as "all difficult things in the world start with something easy, and all great things start with something small," to coordinate the smooth operation of a team amidst every trivial matter, first and foremost, one must adopt the right mindset, work diligently and tirelessly to accomplish tasks assigned by superiors; secondly, continuously improve oneself, seize every opportunity to learn and grow, strive to enhance professional levels, and during leisure time, be good at discovering new things and knowledge related to work; finally, take the initiative as an owner and play to your strengths, taking on more responsibilities beyond your capacity for the company.

I thank all the leaders for providing me with this job, giving me the opportunity to grow and advance together with everyone. I also thank every colleague for their enthusiastic assistance and careful care in my work over the past year. Although I still have many shortcomings in experience and capabilities, I believe that diligence can make up for inadequacies. As long as we communicate more and make unremitting efforts, I believe xxx Company will have a brighter future.

The path ahead will be one I bravely walk on my own. The company's work suits me well, and I truly believe I can do it. I still feel the potential of my future, and I believe I can do it well. Having a better future is very attractive to everyone, and I firmly believe that in my future work, I will proceed more smoothly and can succeed. Actually, many things now are unpredictable, and I don't know what my future holds, but I will continue to work hard on my path forward!
