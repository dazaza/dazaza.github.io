An article that children should read

by gfrw0738 on 2010-04-13 23:24:30

A touching article found under the wall of a nursing home: Child! When you were very young, I spent a lot of time learning to slowly use a spoon or chopsticks like you did. I learned how to untie your shoelaces, fasten buttons, slide down slides, take off clothes, comb hair, and wipe snot. Every moment spent with you was so unforgettable. Therefore, when I can't think clearly or express myself properly, please give me some time, wait for a while, let me think again... It's possible that I might forget what I wanted to say altogether.

Child! Do you remember how we practiced hundreds of times before you could sing your first nursery rhyme? Do you remember how every day I had to rack my brains to answer questions that came out of nowhere from you? So, when I keep repeating old stories and humming songs from my childhood, understand me. Let me continue indulging in these memories! I hope you can also accompany me in chatting about daily trivial matters!

Child, now I often forget how to fasten buttons and untie shoelaces. When eating, I will dirty my clothes, and my hands will tremble uncontrollably when combing my hair. Don't rush me, be more patient and gentle with me. As long as you are here, there will be a lot of warmth in my heart.

Child! Now, my legs are unstable, and I can't walk steadily. So, please hold my hand tightly, accompany me, and walk slowly. Just like when you were young, I led you step by step to grow up. If children don't know how to understand their parents, the parents can only spend the rest of their lives in pain and darkness... Love may be able to find again if it is lost, but one has only one child-parent relationship in life, cherish it, value it!
