The internal space of the Roewe 550 is quite ample, especially in the rear seats where the legroom fully justifies the 2705 mm wheelbase length.

In terms of power, the Roewe 550 1.8 T is equipped with the latest Kavachi 1.8T high-performance full-aluminum intercooled turbocharged engine. The Roewe 550 1.8 DVVT uses the most advanced intake and exhaust dual continuous variable valve timing technology available today. Efficiency, energy saving, and environmental protection are the most prominent features of this engine. Through data testing, it is known that the Roewe 550 1.8L DVVT can increase power by 10% and reduce fuel consumption by 5%, with a 90 km/h constant-speed fuel consumption of 5.6L/100KM, and emissions are reduced accordingly, reaching Euro IV standards. The engine used in the Roewe 550 1.8L is SAIC Group's first 1.8L naturally aspirated engine, featuring DVVT technology. The biggest difference between engines with DVVT technology and ordinary engines lies in their smoother operation, offering linear power output as well as lower noise and fuel consumption.

The most profound impression given is the smooth power output of the Roewe 550 1.8L, which feels more restrained and unhurried compared to the previous 1.8T model. Of course, this does not mean that without the turbocharger, the Roewe 550 becomes completely inert. Its high-speed performance is very satisfying; starting from 100km/h and stepping on the gas, it won't take long to accelerate smoothly up to 170km/h.
