Car Beauty: Tire Cooling Method

In the scorching summer, people fear the heat, and so do their beloved cars. How can we ensure our cars survive the hot summer without encountering mechanical failures? In terms of daily maintenance, the following points should be noted:

A. Prevent poor lubrication. Lubricating oil tends to thin out due to heat, leading to poor oxidation resistance and degradation, which could result in faults such as burnt bearings and seized axles. Therefore, switch to summer-specific lubricating oil and frequently check the quantity and quality of the lubricant, replacing it when necessary.

B. Prevent spontaneous combustion and explosion. High summer temperatures slow down the radiator's cooling process, causing water temperature to remain high, affecting engine power and potentially leading to spontaneous combustion or explosion of fuel mixtures. The heat also causes some car components to expand and deform, accelerating wear at best and damaging parts at worst. With road surface temperatures often exceeding 70°C in summer, car tires heat up, increasing internal air pressure, making long-distance travel more prone to blowouts.

C. Protect car paint from excessive sun exposure. Long-term exposure to sunlight can cause car paint to age and wrinkle. Regular washing and waxing may provide some protection but is not long-lasting. Car wax contains silicon, which rusts the paint under prolonged UV exposure, leaving black spots. The abrasive particles in the wax leave fine scratches on the glossy paint. Additionally, the wax itself evaporates quickly at high temperatures. Therefore, park your car in shaded areas whenever possible.

D. Prevent overly rich fuel-air mixture. Due to high temperatures, gasoline flows more easily, expansion of the jet hole increases fuel flow and evaporation, leading to an overly rich fuel-air mixture. Thus, adjust the jet hole size, modify the connection between the accelerator device and the throttle lever, appropriately lower the float chamber oil level height to reduce fuel supply.

E. Prevent evaporation. Oil and water evaporate faster at high temperatures, so ensure the gas cap is tightly closed to prevent oil leakage; regularly check the water tank water level, the oil level and height in the crankcase, the brake fluid level in the master brake cylinder, and the electrolyte density and liquid level height in the battery, adding and adjusting as necessary according to regulations.

F. Prevent vapor lock. In the hot summer, gasoline engines' fuel systems are prone to vapor lock due to rising temperatures, leading to insufficient fuel supply or even fuel cutoff. To prevent vapor lock, use asbestos pads to separate the gasoline pump from the exhaust pipe, or wrap the gasoline pump with wet cloth to cool it down.

G. Prevent engine overheating. During maintenance, make sure the fan belt does not get oily to avoid slipping, and maintain proper belt tension. Take breaks during long drives by parking in shady areas to let the engine hood ventilate and cool down. If tire pressure increases due to heat, stop to cool down instead of releasing air or splashing cold water to lower pressure and temperature.

More methods to cool down your car:

- **Sealing the body**: This helps retain cool air inside the car. If your car has average sealing or if it’s older, consider buying metal strips to seal around the doors. These strips not only beautify the car but also help retain the cool air inside, much like window seals at home that are easy to install.

- **Using sunshades**: Popular sunshade options include cartoon-patterned "quick-change sunshades" and "anti-sunburn" instant stickers. They're easy to use, attaching via static electricity or suction cups, requiring no effort to fix and can be easily removed when opening windows without leaving any marks on the glass. These sunshades are usually one-way transparent, not obstructing the view. Folding sunshades are recommended as they protect the dashboard and keep seats cooler.

- **Cool seat pads**: For a cooler seating experience, opt for bamboo or hemp pads, or temperature-sensitive ice pads. Avoid jade-like pads, as these stone cushions can become extremely cold or absorb heat, especially after long exposure to sunlight, making them very hot to touch. In fact, using pure cotton seat covers in the summer can be more comfortable, providing a cool feel with refreshing colors.
