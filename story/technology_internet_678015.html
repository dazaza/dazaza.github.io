Why should you invest in gold [http://blog.sina.com.cn/s/blog_9227cb0e0100udg0.html]? According to data from the National Bureau of Statistics, the consumer price index (CPI), which is the most important indicator of inflation, increased by 5.9% throughout 2008. Generally speaking, when the CPI increase exceeds 5%, it indicates severe inflation.

Inflation is the archenemy of wealth, especially for those who hold large amounts of bank deposits and bonds, as its erosive effect becomes more pronounced. Therefore, many people resort to investment to counteract inflationary pressures. However, most people only focus on the stock market, often neglecting the importance of "diversified investment," which has caused many investors to suffer heavy losses during events like the 1997 Asian financial crisis, the 2000 dot-com bubble burst, and the recent financial tsunami. The key to "diversified investment" lies in allocating funds across various assets with low correlations between their returns. This way, risks can be reduced, following the principle of "not putting all your eggs in one basket." Currently, the main categories of diversified investment include stocks, bonds, and money market instruments.

The king of currencies—gold—should be your best strategic investment choice.

The value of investing in gold:

The investment value of gold mainly manifests in two aspects: long-term preservation of value and asset diversification.

(1) Long-term preservation of value

The total global stockpile of gold is only over 150,000 tons, and it is increasing at a slow annual rate of 2%. However, the enthusiasm for gold investment is rapidly rising, with demand surging by 51% just in the second quarter of this year. Rarity increases value, and the tight supply-demand relationship in the gold market determines its long-term preservation of value against inflation. Over the past four centuries, compared to the significant devaluation of global currencies, the purchasing power of gold has only increased. The famous economist Keynes pointed out that gold, as the last line of defense and emergency reserve, has yet to be replaced by anything else. This highlights the ability of gold to effectively resist changes in currency value and rising prices brought about by inflation.

(2) Asset diversification

International gold prices are primarily determined by the dollar, oil prices, and international geopolitical factors, with the dollar's influence exceeding 50% and inversely related to gold prices. Under the U.S.'s continued policy of quantitative easing, which encourages expectations of dollar depreciation, more professional investors are buying gold from an asset allocation perspective to hedge against the risk of a falling dollar exchange rate. The great bull market for gold has arrived, and after breaking its previous historical high before the financial tsunami, it continues to rise strongly. This is the perfect time for you to choose gold products as a stable investment to optimize your financial portfolio.
