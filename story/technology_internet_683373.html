Diabetics should be aware that sometimes a small wound may lead to the occurrence of diabetic foot disease, which is extremely harmful. It can not only cause disability but also bring great mental suffering and a significant economic burden, and the family will also suffer as a result. Therefore, if diabetic foot is discovered, it must be treated in time. Experts have shared methods of treating diabetes with traditional Chinese medicine.

nbsp; nbsp; nbsp; One, Traditional Chinese Medicine treatment for diabetic foot, [http://www.tangzk.com/] Blood Pulse Wash Agent: This wash agent can be used to prevent diabetic foot.

nbsp; nbsp; nbsp; The method of treatment is: take an equal amount of Duhuo, Honghua, Bai Zhi, raw Fuzi, Da Huang, Chishaoyao, Mo Yao, Chuanxiong, Tougusao, Sichuan Pepper, Mugwort Leaf, Borneol, and place them in a cloth bag. Then soak this bag in 2500 milliliters of boiling water. When the temperature of the liquid drops to 40-50 degrees Celsius, use it to soak the affected foot for 10-20 minutes each time, twice a day. Once the socks are thoroughly infused with the herbs, wear these socks for more than 10 hours a day, and treat for 2 weeks as one course. This method has a certain therapeutic effect on protecting against diabetic foot.

nbsp; nbsp; nbsp; Two, Traditional Chinese Medicine treatment for diabetic foot, Diabetic Foot [http://www.tangzk.com/] Soaking Formula

nbsp; nbsp; nbsp; Clinical observations show that using Chinese herbal soaking treatments for diabetic foot has a certain therapeutic effect. The method of treatment is: grind an equal amount of Da Huang, Jin Yin Hua, Hong Hua, Ju Hua, Sichuan Pepper, Bai Zhi, processed Ru Xiang, and processed Mo Yao into coarse powder, add 5000 milliliters of water, boil and then simmer for 15 minutes. nbsp; nbsp; nbsp; Three, Traditional Chinese Medicine treatment for diabetic foot [http://www.tangzk.com/], Fu Yu San External Application Formula

nbsp; nbsp; nbsp; Take 50 grams each of Da Huang, Dang Gui, and Huang Lian, 30 grams each of Luo Shi Teng and Bai Zhi, 60 grams of Di Gu Pi, 20 grams of processed Lu Gan Shi, 10 grams of Pearl Powder, and 15 grams of Borneol. Dry and grind all the drugs together, sieve through an 80-mesh sieve, mix evenly, and disinfect with ultraviolet light before bottling and storing. If the exudate from the affected foot is abundant and yellowish-turbid, add 25 grams each of Huang Bai and Qing Dai; if the exudate is less and clear-dilute, add 15 grams of dried alum. For third-degree ulcers on the affected foot, add 20 grams each of Ru Xiang and Mo Yao. The usage of Fu Yu San: for first-degree ulcers on the affected foot, first rinse the wound with physiological saline, then apply insulin and Gentamicin respectively, sprinkle Fu Yu San evenly on the wound, cover with sterile gauze, and secure with a bandage, changing the dressing every other day. For second-degree ulcers on the affected foot, first remove the necrotic tissue from the wound, rinse the wound with Shuang Huang Lian solution (prepared by dissolving four 600 mg vials of Shuang Huang Lian powder injection in 250 ml of physiological saline), spray Gentamicin and insulin evenly onto the wound, then apply Fu Yu San externally, changing the dressing once daily. For third-degree ulcers on the affected foot, first remove the necrotic tissue from the wound, rinse the wound with Metronidazole injection solution, and use saline-soaked gauze strips for drainage, then apply Fu Yu San externally, changing the dressing twice daily. Fu Yu San has the effects of promoting blood circulation to remove blood stasis, reducing swelling, and removing decay to promote muscle growth, and it has good therapeutic effects on treating diabetic foot.
