The Jinsha Site Museum Unveils Its Curtain

The tourist attraction, the Jinsha Site Museum, opened on April 16th. Starting from noon at 12:00, it will officially welcome domestic and international eco-tourists for visits. The Jinsha Site Museum is a key project in our city's cultural construction and an important component of Chengdu's "three major cultural brands." As the largest single investment project in museum construction in our city since the founding of the People’s Republic of China, the museum has a total design investment of 389 million yuan and covers an area of approximately 300,000 square meters. It consists of the Relic Hall, Exhibition Hall, Cultural Relics Protection Center, Portuguese Garden Area, and Visitor Reception Center, with a total building area of 37,895 square meters and a green space area exceeding 138,000 square meters, achieving a green space ratio of 70.7%. It is a specialized museum built on the original site of the Jinsha Site.

This is another cultural feast following the confirmation of the "Sun God Bird" gold ornament pattern from the Jinsha Site as the symbol of China's cultural heritage and the surge in locally created musicals triggered by the musical "Jinsha."

The construction of the Jinsha Site Museum right at home has indeed whetted the appetite of the citizens. To uncover its mysteries, the reporter conducted an on-site investigation. Let us gradually unveil her mysterious veil. Entering the south gate of the museum, to the west of the main road is the Sun God Bird Plaza, where a commemorative sculpture of the "China Cultural Heritage Symbol" stands. Since August 16, 2006, when the "Sun God Bird" gold ornament pattern excavated from the Jinsha Site was confirmed as the symbol of China's cultural heritage, a permanent memorial sculpture has been set up here. Moving forward along the main road, the semi-circular building is the Relic Hall, which adopts a large-span steel structure inside, creating a column-free large space. Here, one can experience the frequency and grandeur of sacrificial activities in the ancient Shu Kingdom and watch the archaeological excavation process up close. Crossing the Relic Hall and the Modie River Bridge, a square building comes into view, which is the Exhibition Hall. It has one underground floor and three above-ground floors, concentrating on displaying the life and production of the ancient Shu people during the Jinsha period, showcasing their exquisitely beautiful, uniquely shaped, and finely crafted artifacts. There is also a systematic introduction to the historical knowledge of the occurrence, development, and evolution of the ancient Shu civilization, as well as interactive game programs for the audience. The Cultural Relics Protection Center located in the northwest corner of the museum is the area dedicated to protecting and researching the cultural relics unearthed from the Jinsha Site. Nearby, there is a small Jinsha Theater that regularly hosts cultural performances related to Jinsha culture, where the essence version of the musical "Jinsha" will be staged. The garden area of the museum features cultural landscapes such as the Blackwood Forest and the "Jade Road," allowing visitors to feel the environmental changes over thousands of years on the Chengdu Plain and the hardships endured by the ancient Shu people in jade crafting. The entire garden area is equipped with various service facilities, including parking lots, visitor reception centers, and rest areas, making tourism very convenient.

The grandeur of the entire museum's architecture, the ingenious conception of its internal layout, and the exquisite beauty of the exhibited cultural relics are beyond what the reporter can describe in detail with words. It's best left for everyone to personally savor.
