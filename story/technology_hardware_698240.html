Who owes who?

by anonymous on 2012-03-09 02:34:16

(I) Once again, it rained outside the window. Whose tears were flowing this time? You said that if one reveals their sorrow without reservation to those around them, then only those who care about you will feel heartache. However, the person causing my sorrow would have no sense of guilt. I listened and began secretly changing myself, trying to be a carefree and foolish girl in front of those who care about me.

Was it because time passed too quickly or was it because my steps were too slow? Was it because I didn't do enough or was it because you had already grown tired of me? You left, leaving nothing behind...

(II) It was still a starless night sky, and I gazed at it alone, without shedding any tears. You said that this world does not believe in tears. When faced with issues, one must learn to confront them, facing them with no fear. Crying is merely a sign of weakness; tactical knives. Tears solve nothing. On the contrary, your crying is a form of surrender.

I listened and stopped shedding tears easily. Even when I wanted to cry, I chose to smile in the end. Now, everything has changed, without warning, changing everything. Taking away all, leaving behind only sorrow.

(III) That familiar melody drifted by my window once more. I fell silent. You said that it was your favorite song. This song was very sorrowful, revealing deep sadness within you, needing warmth. I remained silent.

Quietly, I downloaded your favorite song onto my phone, listening to it constantly, wanting to explore the sorrow in your heart and give you all the warmth I could. I thought doing so would move you, thinking that we could quietly walk together. But, things went against our wishes! Originally, I had only moved myself.

(IV) You said that if we are destined in this life, we will hold hands and walk together. And you also said that if we meet in the next life, we will never leave each other. These ethereal dialogues easily stirred the heartstrings. Your words were deeply imprinted in my mind, never forgotten. Silently, I paid attention and waited in ways you did not know. Even though much time had passed and people had changed, I still waited where you left!

(V) You said that life is like a train, passing through many beautiful landscapes. No matter how reluctant, one must wave goodbye to those beautiful but unowned views. In this world, separations are inevitable, be it friendships or family ties. When it's time to say goodbye, one should learn to turn around gracefully and not stay stuck in the past.

Under the help of time and your teachings, I experienced much and slowly learned to let go. Despite all this, some separations still hurt deeply. Before I could fully step out of the shadow of separation, you disappeared.

After turning around, we became passersby, strangers from then on. You don't belong to me, and I don't belong to you!

(VI) You said that our friendship would last forever, disregarding the passage of years, making us happier than anyone else. You said that meeting me was the most beautiful encounter in your life. You said that my world was too sorrowful, and you felt heartache and cared for me.

I always couldn't believe that I would encounter such deep friendship, unseen before, yet having such profound bonds. I worried it might be a dream, fearing I couldn't bear losing again, and even more afraid that my sorrow would affect you.

I kept pushing away, full of contradictions, eventually deciding to make myself better quickly so as to give you happiness and joy. Gains and losses, reunions and separations, are too common, leaving me at a loss. This is also my blessing for you, leaving this place of sorrow.

(VII) If one day I die, please scatter my ashes into the sea where we promised to meet in the next life. If there is a next life, let me transform into a pink dandelion. In this life, I owe her too much, so in the next life, let me become a dandelion that forsakes everything for the love in my heart. This is my vow to her in the next life. (Vow: Love you for eternity.) In this life, you are my sister; in the next life, I will wait for you.

This life is destined to make me think of him forever, so let the next life make him think of me forever. It's what he owes me in this life.
