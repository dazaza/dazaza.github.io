But according to a survey conducted by LeadershipIQ in 2011 in the United States and China, despite Chinese companies surpassing American companies in 75% of talent management and development areas. 19% of American employees have high engagement, while only 6% of Chinese employees have high engagement, which is less than one third of that in the United States. Only 22%-31% of Chinese employees believe that their colleagues put 100% of their effort into work, the survey shows. And only 3%-4% of Chinese employees would recommend the organization they work for as a quality workplace to others. According to a survey by a Shanghai management consulting company, Chinese companies outperform American companies in 75% of best practices in talent management, such as building employee loyalty and support for new innovations, helping employees grow and develop in their careers, effectively motivating employees, etc. The key factor driving employee engagement is corporate culture. Research indicates that success is oriented towards results and contributions. Leaders in an enterprising culture tend to be driven by a spirit of adventure; as much as 52% of highly engaged employees in China come from an enterprising corporate culture. An enterprising corporate culture values meritocracy and emphasizes employee creativity. As much as 40% of disengaged employees in China come from a hierarchical corporate culture. A hierarchical corporate culture is built on conservatism, in contrast, and relies on formal structures and typical obedience to orders. Leaders in a hierarchical culture focus on power, command, and structure, closely managing and supervising employees under them. When LeadershipIQ conducted the survey, Mark Murphy, founder and CEO of LeadershipIQ, said: Originally, it was expected that the survey results would show that employees prefer working in a community-oriented culture because everyone gets along well there. However, this is not the case. Mr. Murphy mentioned that employees actually want to speak with strength and competitiveness, rather than just focusing on collaboration and harmony among employees. The implication obtained here is quite clear. For Chinese companies in China, compared with other types of corporate cultures, an enterprising corporate culture has higher employee engagement. Chinese employers should consider more about how to create an enterprising leadership atmosphere instead of merely thinking about how to ensure high employee compliance or guarantee that everyone makes good friends at work.
