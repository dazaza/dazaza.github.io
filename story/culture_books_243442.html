"Change? A Full Record of the 2008 U.S. Presidential Election" from a Different Perspective

by jqrxass on 2009-09-27 22:26:48

"Change? The Complete Record of the 2008 U.S. Election" starts from the intra-party primaries of the two major parties in the U.S. election, tracking and documenting this globally watched political event of the 2008 American election.

The author personally conducted in-depth research, using popular and humorous language in a diary format to instantly record the progress of the election, encompassing all important electoral events, providing us with a multi-layered picture of the election:

Black gold politics, advertising wars, negative attacks, fan frenzy, fundraising secrets, personal image showdowns, comparisons of candidates' political platforms, exciting televised debates, unreliable public opinion polls... Readers can not only feel the thrilling nature of the entire campaign process but also understand the American political ecosystem as well as different cultures and values. Several amusing anecdotes from the election are highly captivating.

Change? The Complete Record of the 2008 U.S. Election - Part One

Barack Obama, who has half African-American bloodlines, is a rising political star whose fame began at the Democratic National Convention four years ago. Although he was then just an Illinois state senator running for federal office, he was already seen as a party star of tomorrow, having the opportunity to speak at a convention filled with political luminaries and receiving warm applause. Some media had already considered him as a potential future president. After successfully being elected as a U.S. Senator in 2004, he soon set his sights on the White House. Due to his relatively short political resume, he was temporarily at a disadvantage compared to Hillary Clinton.

Change? The Complete Record of the 2008 U.S. Election - Part Two

Hillary Clinton's image building was not as deeply rooted as Barack Obama's. Obama's main slogan "Change, we can" (Change, we can) was simple yet inspiring, while Hillary's main slogan "Solutions for America" (America's solutions) was too plain. Elections are not academic research; many voters do not rationally analyze who is more reasonable or who has better propositions, but rather vote based on images filtered by public media. The more inspiring and spiritually motivating the candidate's image is shaped, the easier it is to ignite the enthusiasm of voters.

Change? The Complete Record of the 2008 U.S. Election - Part Three

Obama said: Stop attacking my wife. She is not a politician, she is just an ordinary person. Michelle Obama has recently been controversial. Earlier, her comment about "first time patriotism" was revisited, and other recent statements have also been brought up, such as "Our souls are broken, the problem lies within ourselves." There are also more accusations that she is arrogant, domineering, and talks too much.
