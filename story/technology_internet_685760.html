How to carry out community promotion and promotion for curtain fabric products

by bery201209 on 2012-03-01 16:47:32

The community is the battlefield closest to customers other than the storefront, concentrating the main consumer groups. For curtains and fabrics [http://www.beryhome.com/], new communities are key promotional targets, and the success or failure of community promotions directly affects the sales of the storefront. Therefore, it is necessary for curtain fabric businesses to conduct community promotions and sales. How should we carry out community promotions and sales? There are several common methods.

1. **Key Community Advertising Promotion**: Focus on concentrated advertising campaigns in target communities, such as placing large advertisements in prominent locations at the main entrances and exits, hanging banners, elevator advertisements, and distributing flyers. Key community advertising must be done ahead of competitors; this kind of direct impact on store sales volume cannot be delayed. However, when competitors have already taken the first step, it is essential to find a way to place advertisements within the community, otherwise, it will be difficult to achieve success.

2. **Group Buying in Key Communities**: Organize group-buying events in new communities during important festivals or when new keys are handed over. First, you can independently organize a group-buying event, conducting advance advertising and formulating a detailed plan. Second, you can collaborate with related industries to conduct group-buying activities, effectively reducing costs and enhancing the promotion's effectiveness, leading to a higher success rate. For example, a certain store of a curtain brand in Huizhou organized a successful group-buying event during the handover of new keys in a community, supported by the manufacturer. Not only did it effectively promote the brand's influence in the local community, but it also secured nearly 20 orders on the day of the group-buying event, something no other brand had previously done.

3. **Sample Room Collection in Key Communities**: Strive to install sample rooms in key communities, even if it means providing them for free. Because offering one set of free sample curtains can bring the store dozens of sets of business. Having sample rooms allows customers to better understand your brand and product features, facilitating further expansion in the community and supporting the store’s sales activities.

4. **Building a Storefront Within the Community**: Rent an empty room in a key community to display one or two sets of samples. Instead of spending money on community advertisements, consider renting a house that hasn't been renovated yet or a shop within the community as a temporary storefront. Building such a storefront has two benefits: first, it makes it easier for salespeople to enter the community for promotion; second, it allows quick transactions where homeowners don’t need to visit the store but can finalize purchases within the community, especially beneficial for busy customers who don’t have time to visit the store. In Urumqi, a P-brand curtain store operated in this manner. On the day I was on a business trip, our salesperson signed a deal with a customer who paid a 500-yuan deposit after seeing the samples in our community storefront—quick, convenient, and effective, saving the customer time.

There are two ways to build a storefront in the community: first, establish a storefront independently; second, collaborate with other brands such as home decoration companies, flooring, or tile brands to jointly establish a storefront. This method not only saves costs but also shares customer resources, maximizing the effect of community promotion. For example, the P-brand curtain in Urumqi collaborated with ceiling and tile brands to rent a house for sample displays and promotions; the H-brand in Zhuhai collaborated with a home decoration company to rent a house for promotion, achieving similarly excellent results.

5. **Follow-up Promotions and Services After Community Handover**: Many brands lose momentum in promotions after handing over the keys, thinking that community promotion only lasts during the period when homeowners move in. However, in many areas, after the keys are handed over, many homeowners do not rush to renovate or move in immediately. Some may wait years before renovating. Therefore, the homeowner information collected in the early stage cannot be discarded, and follow-up services should be conducted via SMS or visits to those who have not yet purchased products from your brand. Use a cyclical revisit approach to screen every potential customer without missing any.

Don’t worry if customers have already purchased products from other brands; you can still visit and inquire about their choices. Even if they have already made a purchase, you can still promote your brand and service, leaving a good impression on the customer. According to the [250 Customer Rule], when you serve one customer well, it brings 250 potential customers; when you fail to serve one customer well, you risk losing 250 customers.

Therefore, during the later stages of community promotion, it is crucial to enhance service quality. For those customers who still haven’t ordered curtains [http://www.beryhome.com/], through multiple rounds of promotion, they will think of your brand first when they need it.
