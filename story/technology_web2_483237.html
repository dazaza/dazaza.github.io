It is currently unclear whether there will be any aftereffects. Further observation and treatment are needed.

At around 2:40 AM the day before yesterday, Li Xinhe was riding his motorcycle home. When he reached the area near Zhushikou, he saw several men chasing another man. They were shouting "Catch the thief." Upon seeing this, Li Xinhe turned his motorcycle around and chased after them, stopping the thief over a hundred meters away on the side of the road.

In parks fighting against criminals, on streets catching thieves, and on buses apprehending pickpockets... In the newspapers shown by Li Xinhe's family, all the reports were about his acts of bravery.

Yesterday morning, at the Xuanwu Traditional Chinese Medicine Hospital's inpatient building, Li Xinhe had his left leg wrapped in bandages. Regarding the questions from reporters, he didn't want to say much, repeatedly claiming it was "a trivial matter not worth mentioning." Only under the persistent questioning of his family did he recount the details of the incident.

Our news report (by reporter Han Dapeng and intern reporter Yang Yunxiang): At around 2:00 AM the day before yesterday, 43-year-old Li Xinhe was riding his motorcycle near Zhushikou in Xicheng District when he noticed multiple people chasing a man while shouting "Thief!" Immediately, Li Xinhe turned his motorcycle around and began chasing. When Li Xinhe caught up with the thief, they struggled, and the thief pulled out a knife, stabbing him in the left leg.

Li Xinhe said that he grabbed the thief's arm but was shaken off. He chased after him again, tackling the thief to the ground without using any weapons. About half a minute later, the other men arrived, pulling handcuffs from their pockets and taking the thief away. They claimed to be plainclothes police officers. It was then that Li Xinhe suddenly felt pain in his left knee. Looking down, he saw that his light-colored long pants had been soaked in blood. Apparently, during the struggle with the thief, he had been stabbed with a knife, “I didn’t realize I was stabbed until then,” he said.

At noon yesterday, officers from the Tianqiao Police Station and relevant personnel from the neighborhood committee visited Li Xinhe in the hospital, bringing flowers and consolation money. The police stated that according to preliminary investigations, the thief had been discovered stealing in a nearby civilian house in the Zhushikou area. During his escape, he encountered the plainclothes police officers. With Li Xinhe’s assistance, the police were able to capture the thief. The thief was found to have a knife on him, and the case is still under further investigation.

"I just can't believe evil can overpower righteousness!" Li Xinhe said that he served in the military for four years and after being discharged, worked as a taxi driver. Occasionally, he would encounter thieves and criminals on the street, and he would always stop to intervene, "My character is such that I love to stand up for justice."

"I just love to stand up for justice"

Ms. Han, the wife of Li Xinhe, said that her husband's actions of bravery were commendable, but lacked consideration. For years, she often worried about him, and they even considered divorce because of this. "It's not that I'm afraid of trouble; I'm concerned about his safety and the safety of our daughter," Ms. Han said. Although her husband was strong and sturdy, he hadn't received professional training. If the thief retaliated, the consequences could be unimaginable. "He loves to stand up for justice, I only hope that he can live a peaceful life."
