Healthy Living: Overcoming 10 Psychological Barriers to Fitness

by qingchuyulan on 2010-04-18 22:45:01

Do not worry because there is no fully equipped fitness equipment at home. On the contrary, forget your shortcomings and try to make use of the existing conditions. You will find that so-called hardships are nothing more than psychological barriers you have set for yourself!

1. Not having enough time to exercise regularly

Many people are busy every day and seem unable to find time for regular exercise. In fact, you don't need to sweat it out to get a good workout; the key is to focus on零碎time each day. Here are the specific steps:

1) Do "short-term" exercises several times a day. The benefits gained are equivalent to one long workout, such as taking a 10-minute walk during work breaks. Accumulated over time, this equals one long-distance workout per day.

2) Wake up half an hour earlier in the morning to exercise. Start with twice a week, then increase gradually after forming a habit.

3) When parking, give convenience to others by choosing the farthest parking spot. This increases many opportunities for walking, which accumulates over time for good fitness results.

4) Spend weekends cycling with your children or friends to the suburbs for mountain climbing or swimming. In short, as long as you carefully study your life and work schedule, you can definitely find some extra time for fitness.

Healthy living: Overcome the ten psychological barriers to fitness

2. Exercise is a boring thing

It's natural to feel bored from repeating the same things day after day, especially when exercising alone. The following suggestions may be helpful to you:

1) Exercise with family and friends or colleagues. This method not only helps with fitness but also provides motivation and increases enthusiasm. Especially during critical stages of fitness, mutual encouragement can be beneficial.

2) Participate in fitness courses or club activities where you can learn new exercise techniques and make new friends while engaging in great sports.

3) Let your exercise vary daily. Cross-training allows you to rotate between different types of exercise (such as walking, swimming, cycling, etc.), keeping both your physical and mental state vibrant. It’s a great way to exercise various muscle groups while cultivating many sports skills. However, remember that exercise doesn't mean being boring; choose activities you enjoy and you'll find your interest growing with exercise.

3. Lack of support from family and friends

The best way to gain support from family and friends is to involve them in your exercise routine. Specific methods include:

1) Sign up for parent-child fitness classes and exercise together with your family for immense joy.

2) Suggest a new fitness attempt to your friends. For example, invite them to rock climbing training or sign up for beginner kayaking classes.

3) Bring your family and kids to the park for frisbee games or jump into the pool and splash around with your children instead of sitting on the chair watching.

4) Go shopping with your family to improve relationships and burn calories. Additionally, if your family does not support your fitness goals, explain your reasons to them. Tell your loved ones how important they are to you, and that you want to stay healthy to be with them longer. Even if they do not share your fitness aspirations, ask them to at least respect your fitness wishes.

4. Fear of getting injured during exercise

If you are worried about getting injured due to incorrect exercise methods, follow these suggestions:

1) Beginner exercise courses are specifically designed for fitness newcomers. Start by attending such training sessions where coaches introduce basic knowledge and demonstrate techniques to minimize injury risks.

2) Engage in one-on-one practice with a personal trainer. A certified fitness coach can provide customized fitness plans for you, guide your movements at any time, and point you in the right direction for fitness.

3) Start with walking. Walking is a convenient exercise method that you can do alone or with friends, children, spouses, or pets, without restrictions on location or time. Remember: Don’t rush into high-intensity exercise projects that you haven't tried before. Instead, start with the simplest fitness methods. As your confidence in fitness grows, gradually add new sports.

5. Feeling awkward during workouts

Most importantly, don't underestimate yourself. Focus on your own exercise and don't let the neighbors on the treadmill distract you. To boost your confidence in fitness:

1) Choose some fitness DVDs to study and learn at home. As your health status and exercise level improve, so will your confidence.

2) Purchase a stationary bike, treadmill, stepper, or other home fitness equipment for anytime self-exercise at home.

3) Enroll in beginner fitness classes, especially those starting from zero. Also, remember that no matter whether your movements are clumsy or agile during the fitness process, don't worry about them. Just focus on the fact that fitness makes you healthier, and what you're doing now is very beneficial for cardiovascular health.

6. Unwilling to move after a day's work

If you feel exhausted after work, it indicates that you need to engage in fitness exercises, preferably in the morning. Consider the following suggestions:

1) Place your sportswear beside your bed, fill the kettle with water and store it in the fridge. When you wake up, put on your clothes, take the kettle, and head out.

2) Take a walk outside after breakfast.

3) Do you eat lunch at your workplace? Prepare a pair of sneakers and walk around your workplace after meals. Over time, this will show excellent fitness results.
