Buddhist Selection

by sjpfeifeiz on 2011-09-14 16:37:32

■ It's not so much that others make you suffer, but rather that your own cultivation is insufficient.

■ Jealousy of others will not add any benefit to yourself. Jealousy of others also cannot reduce their achievements.

■ Cultivating in favorable conditions will never lead to enlightenment.

■ Don't say others are pitiful, for you are even more so. How is your own cultivation progressing? How much do you truly understand about life?

■ If you don't give yourself grief, others can never give you烦恼. Because within your own heart, you cannot let go.

■ Be aware of yourself, subdue yourself, change yourself, and only then can you change others. Today's attachment will cause tomorrow's regret.

■ All evil practices are inherently illusory; don't be too proud of yourself.

■ Practicing Buddhism is about answering to your own conscience, not for others to see.

■ This world is originally filled with suffering, without exception.

■ Only when the mind is at peace does it matter whether you are in the east or west. If someone has yet to be saved, don't escape yourself.

■ When you know you are confused, you are not pitiable. When you don't know you are confused, that is the most pitiable state.

■ A person who doesn't bathe, no matter how much perfume they use, will not smell good. Fame and nobility come from being knowledgeable. Virtue naturally brings fragrance.

■ You must always forgive all beings, no matter how bad they are, even if they have harmed you, you must let go in order to attain true happiness.

■ Don't constantly criticize others; you should examine yourself instead. Criticizing others only causes your own suffering.

■ Don't deliberately guess the intentions of others. Without wisdom and correct judgment, you will often be wrong.

■ When you advise others, if you don't consider their self-respect, even the best words will be useless.

■ Don't waste your life on things you will surely regret. The moment you let go is the moment you have no more烦恼.

■ To understand a person, just look if their starting point and goal are the same; this way, you can know if they are sincere.

■ Do not mix arrogance into your wisdom. Do not lack wisdom in your humility.

■ Properly discipline yourself, and don't meddle in others' affairs.

■ Cultivation is a process of gradual effort.

■ Arrogant people can be saved, but conceited people cannot.

■ Those who constantly distort others' kindness are beyond help.

■ Tell more jokes and approach things with a sense of humor; this way, life will be easier.

■ Live with an ordinary heart, treat others with a sense of guilt, handle matters with sincerity, and align your heart with the Buddha's heart.

■ The reason people suffer is because they pursue the wrong things.

■ When you are happy, remember that this happiness is not eternal. When you are in pain, remember that this pain is also not eternal.

■ Not forgiving or understanding others only causes your own suffering.

■ People with insufficient blessings often hear gossip; those with sufficient blessings never hear such things.

■ Always be grateful to those who bring you adversity. Accept your fate, for you are human.

■ Whenever you see the faults and shortcomings of others, you should quickly repent; this is part of cultivation.

■ Many people, when leaving this world, say the same thing: "This world is truly hopeless and sorrowful!"

■ The way to get along with others lies in infinite tolerance.

■ You may have love, but don't cling to it, because separation is inevitable.

■ The truth of life is hidden in the mundane and tasteless.

■ Having no discriminatory thoughts in your heart is true asceticism.

■ The first concept of practicing Buddhism is to never look at the faults of sentient beings. Looking at the faults of sentient beings will always contaminate yourself, making it impossible to cultivate.

■ Instead of rejecting what has already happened, accept it. This is called accepting your fate.

■ Every wound is a kind of maturity.

■ There are probably a hundred or two hundred such sayings in Buddhist teachings. Hopefully, everyone will like them. These basically enumerate the essence of Buddhism. Please read below:

■ There is absolutely no need to look back at who cursed you. If a mad dog bites you, would you crawl down and bite it back?

■ Bad children always require more attention from their parents. Therefore, for sentient beings with deeper sins, we should especially forgive and pity them, rather than distance ourselves and abandon them.

■ Never waste a single second thinking about anyone you don't like.

■ Buddhas and Bodhisattvas only protect those who are willing to help themselves.

■ People with deep karmic obstacles spend day and night looking at the faults and defects of others. Truly cultivating individuals never look at the mistakes or faults of others.
