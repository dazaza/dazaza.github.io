Largest virus ever discovered, scientists guess they originated from Mars

by anonymous on 2013-07-29 15:30:48

According to the UK's Daily Mail, scientists have discovered the largest virus on Earth to date, named "Pandoravirus," with a diameter of 1 micron, which is 10 times the diameter of an average virus. Only 6% of its genes resemble those of other organisms on Earth.

Scientists have discovered a new virus - Pandoravirus, which is the largest virus in the world so far. They live in underwater environments and do not pose a threat to humans.

Scientists believe that "Pandoravirus" may have originated from Mars, with only 6% of its genes resembling those of other organisms on Earth.

The French scientists responsible for this research believe that this mysterious virus may have come from ancient times, or even from another planet, such as Mars. This giant virus exists only in underwater environments and is not considered a serious threat to humans. This study was published in the journal Science, raising questions about the origins of life on Earth.

Dr. Jean-Michel Claverie of Aix-Marseille University discovered this virus and stated that they believe these newly discovered Pandoraviruses originate from an ancient bacteria that has long disappeared. The size of many ordinary viruses is approximately 10-500 nanometers, meaning that Pandoravirus is very large and can be observed under a microscope.

Dr. Claverie explained that because Pandoravirus is very large and does not have the typical structure of a virus, it was initially thought to be a smaller type of bifidobacterium. After his research team found traces of Pandoravirus in seawater, they began searching for and studying them.

They collected samples from the waters off Chile and Australian ponds, sent these samples to the lab, placed them in solutions containing antibiotics to try and kill any possible bacteria, and discovered a large number of newly hatched Pandoraviruses in the collected samples.

Claverie found that the genetic sequence of Pandoravirus is twice that of Megavirus, which was previously the largest known virus with a diameter of 440 nanometers. What surprised them even more was that Pandoravirus has only 6% of its genes similar to those of other known Earth organisms.

Claverie said: "We believe it may have originated from an ancient Pandoravirus that no longer exists today. We will further uncover its mysterious origin, perhaps it came from another planet, such as Mars."

Researchers do not know why this cellular structure eventually became a virus, speculating that this evolution might be a survival strategy. Currently, they hope to discover more unknown large viruses.
