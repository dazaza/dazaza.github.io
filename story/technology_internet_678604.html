### Causes and Classification

Most anal fistulas form after the rupture or incision and drainage of perianal abscesses. As the abscess gradually shrinks, intestinal contents continue to enter the abscess cavity. During the healing and shrinking process, tortuous cavities often form. Poor drainage makes it difficult for the wound to heal, and over time, much scar tissue forms around the cavity, leading to chronic infection of the channel. This channel runs near the internal and external sphincters. The external opening grows relatively fast, often resulting in false healing, causing repeated episodes. Infections in the channel are mostly purulent, but some can be tuberculous.

Based on the position, depth, height, and number of the fistula openings and channels, classifications include:

1. **External Fistula and Internal Fistula**: External fistulas have at least two openings - one on the perianal skin (usually 2-3 cm from the anus) called the external opening, and another inside the bowel cavity, usually at the dentate line in the anal sinus, called the internal opening. Some internal openings are above the mid-dentate line on the rectal wall. Internal fistulas have similar internal openings but no wounds; clinically, 90% are external fistulas.

2. **Low Fistula and High Fistula**: If the fistula channel is below the anorectal ring plane, it is considered a low fistula; if it is above this plane, it is considered a high fistula. The latter is relevant for choosing the treatment method.

### Types of Anal Fistulas

There are more than twenty classification methods for anal fistulas worldwide. According to the Hengshui Conference in China (1975), the unified standard classification of anal fistulas is based on the deep part of the external sphincter muscle. If the fistula passes through this line, it is classified as high; if it is below this line, it is classified as low. A simple anal fistula has only one internal opening, one fistula channel, and one external opening. A complex anal fistula has two or more internal openings, fistula channels, and external openings. This classification method is now widely used domestically.

1. **Low Simple Anal Fistula**: Only one fistula channel passing through the deep part of the external sphincter muscle, with the internal opening near the anal sinus.

2. **Low Complex Anal Fistula**: Fistula channels pass through the deep part of the external sphincter muscle, with two or more external openings and channels, and one or several internal openings near the anal sinus.

3. **High Simple Anal Fistula**: Only one fistula channel passing through the deep part of the external sphincter muscle, with the internal opening near the anal sinus.

4. **High Complex Anal Fistula**: Two or more external openings with branched channels.

5. **Simple Anal Fistula and Complex Anal Fistula**: The former has only one fistula channel, while the latter can have multiple openings and channels.
