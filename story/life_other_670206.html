Don't Always Think You Know Best

He had a secret crush on her. It was that summer, during their brief encounter after meeting, that he inexplicably fell for her, even before he had a clear look at her face. Drawn to her beautiful hair and enchanted by her demeanor and words, it felt like an electric shock — he had fallen secretly in love with her.

He always thought she was good to him and believed that she liked him too. His heart grew increasingly filled with her, yet he didn’t dare confess his feelings. Quietly, he took care of her, maintaining this silent affection for years. He thought she could feel his love, making a confession unnecessary.

After a night of drinking, the two found themselves alone together. Emboldened by alcohol, he smiled and said to her, “I always feel like a child who’s done something wrong in front of you, while you’re like a mentor. It’s so unfair.”

“Is that so? I haven’t noticed,” her calmness left him feeling somewhat disheartened.

Gradually, he realized that she was equally kind to everyone. In fact, he discovered that she cared about others more than she did about him. This realization brought him pain and made him feel childishly wronged.

Yet, occasionally, a small gesture of kindness from her would make him feel sweet for days. Caught between sweetness and pain, he became quiet and withdrawn. He realized that she didn’t love him; she merely regarded him as a common friend or perhaps just someone she knew.

He vowed to leave this city. Still, he continued to care for her secretly, worrying about her trivial matters. He even thought of himself as overly fussy.

He secured a spot for a border assignment. Amidst the farewell banquet filled with friends’ scolding and regret, he got drunk. Waking up alone late at night, he finally cried out loud while leaning on his packed luggage.

The next morning, he called her to meet. Never before had he felt so relaxed when inviting her. Previously, he always feared rejection.

For the first time, he wasn’t acting like a child in front of her; overnight, he had become a gentleman, speaking without restraint. However, her eyes were brimming with tears. She knew he was about to leave far away.

He told her, “Loving someone is painful, especially when you love someone who doesn’t love you back. Being loved can also be painful, especially when someone loves you but you don’t love them back.

To avoid causing you pain and for my own sake, I’m leaving. Take care of yourself.” Pretending to be casual, deep down, he hoped she would try to stop him, even if it meant losing his job. He was willing to give anything up.

She softly replied, “Go then. There will be better girls for you to choose from there. I wish you well.” She remained calm, though a few tears uncontrollably rolled down her cheeks.

In reality, she wanted to yell at him: "Coward! You always think you know best. When I had no feelings for you, you assumed I liked you; now that I'm starting to have feelings, you fail to notice and are running away." But he never looked at her face properly, always glancing towards the distance.

He left, carrying his simple bag. He told others he would leave the next day, but no one knew he had departed early. He only wanted to feel her care one last time, wanting her to see him off. She watched his retreating back, her vision blurred by tears.

Suddenly, he turned around, and she felt excited. However, he simply smiled at her, waving goodbye. She stomped her feet and shouted at him, “Coward! You always think you know best!” But he didn’t hear clearly what she said, mistaking her words for blessings. Acting nonchalant, he made a funny face, turned around, and disappeared into the crowd without looking back.

Don’t always think you know best. Sometimes, life’s twists and turns teach us otherwise.
