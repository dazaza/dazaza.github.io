Still, what's wrong?

by lovewo779 on 2012-02-23 14:22:39

Waking up in the morning, raindrops mock outside the window, giving a hint of coolness. Watching the people outside the window rushing by, none of them seem to know how to enjoy this wonderful moment. Picking up the cup, squeezing toothpaste, my eyes are still blinking constantly, and I brush my teeth and wash my face half awake and half asleep. After getting dressed, I push my bicycle out of the room. The corridor is dark, giving a feeling of emptiness. In this dark corridor, a beam of light shoots from ahead, that's the breath of light seeping in from the window, seemingly bringing a glimpse of hope.

Because of work, like many others, I shuttle on the same road every day. Sometimes there's a little joy, sometimes it carries worries. The scenery every day seems the same, with a few cars still parked by the side of the road. The breakfast stalls at the corner never stop shouting. A familiar yet strange figure always passes through there. But when I look carefully, the scenery changes every day. At least, the cars parked by the roadside today are different. There are more customers around the stalls, and the figure passing by has an umbrella today, looking a bit taller than before. And today, I didn't put in the earplugs to listen to those familiar songs.

I love listening to music on my way to work, forgetting the pedestrians and scenery around me, quietly indulging in the beautiful songs. From time to time, I hum a few lines, either happily or sorrowfully. Occasionally, I also compose some songs and softly sing them, casting away all happiness and troubles. Everything doesn't need to be thought about, allowing myself to quietly enjoy this moment of time, enjoying the taste left by the wind on my cheeks, enjoying every ray of dawn bringing happy molecules, enjoying the joy or sadness in intoxication.

Sometimes sitting by the desk, looking at the silent QQ, I can't help but feel melancholic. Friends of the past, happiness of the past, have all learned to be silent at this moment. Everything seems to be still around, but they have gradually drifted away. There are no more innocent smiles, no pure friendship, and no true hearts to find. At this moment, everyone has learned to be busy, to forget, to give up. Occasionally, a warm greeting is met with "I'm very busy," a smile brings "It's boring." Is this the arrangement of fate? Many friends have learned to go offline to avoid being disturbed; many friends have learned to decline because they have become very busy. Sometimes I can't tell if it's us who have changed, or something else. I think we all start to become numb, passively dealing with people and matters. The emotions between us also become passive, eventually becoming gradually estranged.

People who like to be sentimental will always get hurt, and those who like to reminisce will always leave themselves with a sense of despair. Sometimes I want to let go of all beautiful memories, but I still can't forget. I can't forget the taste of eating instant noodles together, the spicy flavor of the spicy strips, someone laughing while drinking, someone quietly eating rice alone, someone proudly boasting of having eaten twelve bowls of rice, nor can I forget the taste of not having food at the end of each month. Everything seems to be drifting further away from me, but it remains so vivid. I wonder if the dear friends and brothers are still busy now, or working in ease and joy, or standing on the three-foot platform pouring their passion for education, or running their own shops, making a leap from students to bosses, or like me, sweating for others.

We once had dreams, but now these dreams are rushing towards the distance, becoming fairy tales, everything appearing so illusory. Occasionally contacting a few friends, but what we talk about are all current grievances, dissatisfaction with life, dissatisfaction with careers, then we start to comfort each other, encourage each other. Sometimes, unintentionally hearing that friends are getting married or have already married, I am quite surprised. Because everything comes so fast, I always thought they were still kids, but unexpectedly, everyone's pace has quickened. It seems that overnight, in my world, they have grown up and are no longer those hateful children.

Looking at classmates one by one starting their families, suddenly fear arises in my heart. I also want a stable home, want to live happily, but until now, I don't have enough capital to manage it. The existing salary can't even support myself, let alone casually talking about taking care of the family, taking care of the children. I have been dreaming, hoping that tomorrow or next year I will have enough capital to open a shop I like, operating things I like. But the dream is still a bit far away, and I am still waiting, waiting for an opportunity to come.

In fact, there are already some friends around me, someone willing to confide in me, someone willing to share happiness and sadness with me, but I am still missing, missing the school of the past, missing the scenery of the past, missing you and me of the past. Maybe in no time, we will be like children again, forgetting our companions, forgetting the person sitting next to us, forgetting those beautiful memories.
