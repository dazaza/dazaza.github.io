Google Nexus 5 Rumors Roundup: Expected to Launch by the End of This Month

by anonymous on 2013-10-08 18:20:02

Following the release of flagship phones by Samsung and Apple, the most anticipated smartphone of the year now only remains the Google Nexus 5. As the launch date approaches, information about its design and hardware has already leaked. Below, we summarize recent rumors regarding the Google Nexus 5 for those interested in the device.

Design

The Nexus 5 is still designed and manufactured by LG. Early rumors suggested that the Nexus 5 would be very similar to the LG G2, but according to leaked images, this isn't the case. The large camera on the back certainly stands out, but it seems to lack some of the aesthetic design elements from its predecessor. In terms of size, the Nexus 5 doesn't appear to have grown excessively; it should be roughly the same as other 5-inch models currently available.

Hardware Specifications

According to a leaked document, the Google Nexus 5 will feature a 4.96-inch 1080P full HD screen (444ppi), powered by a Qualcomm Snapdragon 800 processor with 2GB of RAM. It will support LTE and NFC, come with wireless charging capabilities, and include a 2300mAh lithium battery.

System

Without a doubt, based on past experience, as Google's latest flagship, the Nexus 5 is likely to become the first phone to run on Android 4.4 KitKat. However, some details about Android 4.4 remain mysterious, so we hope the Nexus 5 brings us some surprises in terms of system applications.

Storage Capacity

The Nexus 4 offered two options: 8GB and 16GB, so the Nexus 5 might continue with this configuration. However, we hope for larger capacity versions or even support for micro SD card expansion.

Camera Technology

According to previous rumors, the Nexus 5 may adopt the camera setup from the LG G2, which features 13 million pixels and supports optical stabilization. However, the latest news suggests that the Nexus 5 could be equipped with a new MEMS camera body, enabling cooler shooting features.

From the Nexus 5's log files, it appears that the camera sensor is not BSI back-illuminated, measuring 1/3.2 inches (slightly smaller than other mainstream models), with an F2.4 lens aperture. Overall, the configuration looks somewhat unimpressive. The newly added MEMS technology might make up for the hardware shortcomings, offering extremely fast shooting speeds, and allowing users to focus after taking a picture, similar to the Lytro light field camera. Of course, this is just a rumor.

Price

We believe the price of the Nexus 5 is what everyone is most concerned about. Based on past experiences, Google is likely to offer a relatively low unlocked price, possibly lower than most competitors. If it continues to be priced like the Nexus 4 at $299 (approximately RMB 1829), it will become one of the most worthwhile Android flagship phones to purchase on the market.

Sales Model

Firstly, Google will still sell the unlocked version of the Nexus 5 through the Google Play store. Additionally, since this device supports almost all LTE and 3G bands from U.S. carriers, contract sales may not be limited to T-Mobile as they were with the previous model. However, whether it will be available domestically remains unknown.

Release Date

According to reports from the well-known IT website TechRadar, Google is highly likely to release the Nexus 5 in the last week of October 2013, which is at the end of this month. Let's look forward to it together.
