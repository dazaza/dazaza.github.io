Arsenal won a close victory, and the professor exploded for one person. A time bomb forced the Gunners to make another move.

by yinding999 on 2009-12-16 10:57:21

Arsenal's narrow victory prompts Professor Wenger's outburst for one man; ticking time bomb forces the Gunners to act again. By reporter Ke Han - At Anfield, Arsenal came from behind to win. Amidst the joy, there was also a sense of relief: without Andrei Arshavin, would Arsenal have endured another embarrassing defeat? Especially chilling is the fact that Liverpool, despite dominating the first half, only scored due to goalkeeper Manuel Almunia's mistake, which effectively handed the opponents a gift.

Before the match, Arsenal legend Ian Wright had expressed his concerns in a Sun column: "Almunia is a good second-choice goalkeeper, but lacks the quality of a number one keeper for a title-chasing team. Occasionally, he seems completely out of his depth. Arsenal needs a top-class goalkeeper, but the problem is that such goalkeepers are hard to attract to the Emirates."

Almunia's deputy, Wojciech Szczesny, who participated in last season's infamous 4-4 draw and has recently recovered from injury, said: "I hope to put as much pressure on Almunia as possible. What else can I do? Only train hard every time and show my ability when I get the chance. I also hope Almunia likes this competition."

However, Almunia insisted: "Recently, it has been tough for me (due to illness, injury, and the unexpected death of his mother-in-law in a car accident). I wasn't feeling great at the start of the season, and everything else made things even more difficult. Psychologically, I wasn't fully recovered, but now my confidence is back. The old Manuel is back!" Nevertheless, just nine minutes into the game, he sent a goal kick directly out of play, drawing boos from the crowd.

In the 12th minute, Almunia did indeed hold onto Fernando Torres' one-on-one shot firmly. This wasn't surprising, as Torres, having just returned from injury, clearly hadn't regained his match fitness. Upon receiving Steven Gerrard's precise pass, Torres didn't rush into the box to try and bypass his compatriot but instead attempted a long-range shot from outside the area, lacking power, making it easy for Almunia to save.

But Almunia's weakness lies in his tendency to make wrong decisions when the opponent exploits set pieces by lofting high balls into the box. In the first half alone, there were several instances: in the 23rd minute, Gerrard's corner kick entered the penalty area, leaving Almunia somewhat flustered; two minutes later, Gerrard swung another free kick into the box, and Almunia could only punch the ball clear; in the 32nd minute, Gerrard's diagonal cross found its way into the box, where Almunia managed to claim the ball, only to immediately hand it back to the opposition during the subsequent counterattack!

In the 41st minute, Aurelio's free kick posed no real threat, but Almunia's charge failed to secure the ball, instead hitting it directly to Dirk Kuyt on the edge of the box. The Dutchman wasted no time in thanking him with a powerful shot into the net! In a challenging away fixture, the key is not to concede first, yet Almunia's amateurish error once again brought shame upon the team.

Fairly speaking, Lucas may have been offside on this goal, and Arsène Wenger on the touchline showed his anger over it. But as the BBC website pointed out: "Almunia might have been barged by Lucas, but he should still have done better. This is a goal Almunia will want to forget about. Those fans still chanting 'Almunia for England' can take a rest."

At halftime, the professor erupted in the dressing room. Vermaelen later said: "I've never seen the boss so angry, so in the second half, we all started working harder." The thoughtful Belgian didn't specify whether Wenger's main target of anger was Almunia, but many believe the Spanish goalkeeper likely received the full force of Wenger's fury.

The second half saw a dramatic turnaround. Arsenal scored twice within ten minutes to level the score, and an unexpectedly lackluster Liverpool couldn't properly test Almunia again, allowing the ticking time bomb to temporarily go silent. The lesson from this match for Arsenal is that, with Arshavin in their ranks, they may not lack for forwards, but sooner or later, they'll need to buy a better goalkeeper than Almunia.
