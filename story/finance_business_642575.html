How to seek 'up up up' in a turbulent workplace -- Chinese workplace

by xstookqu3 on 2011-12-23 14:47:46

The workplace has never been a place of great upheaval; rather, it often conceals many hidden complexities. To remain standing in the turbulent life of a workplace, one must continuously seek personal growth opportunities.

### Growth Points

#### Academic Growth Point

Zhu Shengshan, 23 years old, works for an electronics development company in Zhuhai.

Two years ago, I graduated from Wuhan University's Computer Science department and came to Zhuhai. The company I work for mainly develops gaming software, and I am an ordinary programmer. The company is privately owned, with limited scale, maintaining steady growth. Of course, personnel changes are rare within the company. Everyone spends their days idly, playing online games or chatting in groups during work hours.

The salary at the company is decent, and I don’t want to rashly quit my current job and start over somewhere else. So, while my colleagues idle away their time at work, I began self-improvement. In terms of self-charging, I took two approaches: first, I registered for the self-study examination for an undergraduate degree in business management, and second, I started reading extensively.

Through continuous effort, I have already passed half of the subjects in the self-study exam, and I am happy to soon possess a second bachelor’s degree. When purchasing books or borrowing them from the city library, I deliberately chose books with different contents, ranging from popular biographies to pure literary novels, even some geographical professional books. This massive volume of reading allowed me to absorb nutrients, increasing my confidence in external exchanges, transforming me from a timid person into someone who speaks confidently and logically.

**Commentary**: Academic ability not only refers to actual academic credentials but also represents learning capacity. Keeping oneself in a state of learning and constantly charging in all aspects is also a form of personal capability increase, allowing one to walk more freely in future workplaces.

#### Authority Growth Point

Zhang Wenyong, 26 years old, Deputy Manager of the Business Department at a trading company in Changsha.

I have worked continuously for this trading company for five years. Due to my diligence and intelligence, I have brought substantial benefits to the company. Three years ago, the deputy manager was transferred to the Shanghai headquarters, and the boss promoted me to the position of deputy manager. For three years in that position, I maintained good relationships with my subordinates, and everyone's evaluation of me was quite positive.

Three months ago, one of my subordinates performed exceptionally well and successfully replaced the position of the business department manager. My subordinate suddenly became my superior, which made me very uncomfortable. The stagnation in authority gave me a wake-up call in my career path, prompting me to reflect on myself. I thought that the reason I hadn't continued to be selected for promotion might be due to a lack of outstanding performance, so much so that the boss didn't pay special attention to me.

I began observing the new manager's working methods and humbly exchanged business insights with him. Additionally, I revised my quarterly work objectives and changed some of my work habits, no longer being as lazy and scattered as before. After making these adjustments, I unintentionally discovered that due to my procrastination, many businesses had been neglected. Thus, I and my subordinates began actively following up, quickly seeing significant improvements in performance. The boss gradually began praising me in different settings and encouraged me privately, saying that promotion opportunities were waiting for me...

**Commentary**: The desire for authority is not a scary thing; having authority can allow one to contribute more energy to the company. Moreover, a promotion is also the most direct way to test one's job performance. As long as legitimate channels are used, "step-by-step promotions" are worthy of respect.

#### Salary Growth Point

Li Jianhong, 31 years old, accountant at a logistics company in Harbin.

When I joined this logistics company, I was thrilled—it attracted me with its high salary. All along, my high income has been envied by those around me. Soon, I demonstrated exceptional abilities in my position, and my work became increasingly stable.

The company manager made several small adjustments to my salary. Once my monthly salary reached 2000 yuan, there were no further increases. Of course, compared to some university classmates or neighbors, my current salary is still quite good. However, the "stagnation" of my salary made me somewhat uncomfortable, as if everything about me had stopped improving. I began to lose focus at work and even made mistakes several times. A net friend of mine, upon knowing this, suggested that while ensuring work quality, I should appropriately raise the issue of a salary increase with my superior.

Originally hesitant to bring up the topic of a raise, I, encouraged by my net friend, found an opportunity to discuss my thoughts with the manager. I spoke sincerely, not hiding my intentions. But before the manager responded, I was still uneasy, fearing that the manager would blame me for "unreasonable troublemaking." Unexpectedly, the manager said to me: "Although the company hasn't grown significantly, your request is reasonable. Your request for a raise also shows that you have the confidence to work harder, so I've decided to give you an additional 300 yuan per month."

**Commentary**: People die for wealth, birds die for food. Even if the company's performance is mediocre or declining, as long as you feel justified in your own performance, asking for a raise is not excessive. Courageously expressing your thoughts, believe that a truly talent-loving superior will certainly satisfy your desires.

#### Networking Growth Point

Tang Miao, 25 years old, works for an advertising production company in Changchun.

I personally like the city of Changchun, so I came here from my hometown of Wuhan, hoping to establish my own career here. Having studied journalism in university, I applied and joined this advertising production company.

Among our company's client list are some of the largest local conglomerates, with enormous annual advertising investments. These client resources are extremely valuable. In interacting with these high-end clients, I not only understand their advertising demands and some trends in advertising production but also establish good friendships with them. I actively participate in interactive activities organized by client companies, seizing every opportunity to strengthen our rapport.

Our company's CEO and some colleagues excel in advertising and will undoubtedly achieve greater success in the future. Therefore, while enhancing "external networking," I also attach great importance to communication with "internal personnel." Time reveals true character, and I believe that today's sincere efforts will yield lasting harmonious relationships.

In companies like ours, employee turnover is high, and I haven't planned to stay here permanently. However, "networking is also productivity," and excellent networking and a large social circle will provide strong support for future career development.

**Commentary**: Networking is also productivity, and this statement is absolutely correct. Today's efforts will reap abundant results tomorrow, and excellent networking relationships are also a manifestation of strength accumulation. Therefore, maintaining good relationships both internally and externally is a demonstration of personal charm and the best way to accumulate capital.
