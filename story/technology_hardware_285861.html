Course Background

Retail managers and supervisors have participated in numerous in-store sales trainings, but why does the final sales performance still look poor?

Famous trainers have been invited to provide training for store managers and staff, yet why are there almost no results after the training?

Considering industry factors, the quality of supervisors, store managers, and staff in the apparel industry is relatively low. What kind of training do they actually need?

When conducting internal training, store managers often don't listen. External trainers find it difficult to connect with the industry and the specific realities of their own stores...

What should store managers and staff learn? What factors influence customer purchases? Which has a greater impact, actions or language? What kind of movements should we learn? What should we say? "Six Pulse Divine Sword" – it teaches you all these things.

"Six Pulse Divine Sword" – A Gold Standard Practical Training Course for Sales Service Skills in Apparel Stores. Derived from real terminal experiences, used to enhance terminal training, and incorporating the essence of multiple corporate applications, it helps establish benchmark terminal training for apparel brands.

Second Section: Course Outline

First Unit: Six Pulse Divine Sword First Technique – Welcoming Guests

1. What should store staff do after welcoming customers into the store?

2. What are the shortcomings of current store welcoming phrases?

3. What are the correct welcoming words and actions?

4. Brand positioning and requirements for welcoming language and actions.

5. After training, how should welcoming practices continue when staff go back on duty?

Second Unit: Six Pulse Divine Sword Second Technique – Seeking Opportunities

1. What is seeking opportunities? Why seek opportunities?

2. How to seek opportunities? How to maintain an appropriate distance with customers?

3. How to observe customer behavior and identify interaction opportunities?

4. Common mistakes made by store staff currently.

5. What proactive service actions can be taken?

Third Unit: Six Pulse Divine Sword Third Technique – Opening the Conversation

1. What are the principles and objectives for initiating conversation?

2. What are some common mistakes in opening conversations? What causes them?

3. How to introduce new and old products during the opening?

4. What are the key points to note when initiating a quick sale?

5. How to praise customers effectively during the opening?

6. How to create a best-selling atmosphere for products during the opening?

7. How to emphasize the value of limited availability and opportunity during the opening?

8. Skills in using different types of opening lines effectively.

Fourth Unit: Six Pulse Divine Sword Fourth Technique – Trying On (Using, Wearing, Carrying, etc.)

1. How to encourage and effectively guide customers to try on (use, wear, carry)?

2. How to inquire about customer needs and recommend suitable products accordingly?

3. How to confidently ask questions to customers? What are the principles and secrets of asking questions?

4. How to effectively stimulate the desire to purchase (own) in customers?

5. How to upsell and achieve multiple-item sales?

6. Customers who criticize products are potential buyers.

7. What objections might customers typically raise? How to handle customer objections?

8. Familiarizing with and handling price issues: When customers say, "It's too expensive," "Can you give a discount?" etc., how should we respond?

Fifth Unit: Six Pulse Divine Sword Fifth Technique – Closing the Sale

1. Must we only close the sale when the customer explicitly asks for it?

2. What are the signs indicating a customer is ready to buy? How to successfully close the deal?

3. What are the closing phrases and key points to note?

Sixth Unit: Six Pulse Divine Sword Sixth Technique – Seeing Off Guests

1. Partner's farewell phrases and actions.

2. Correct farewell phrases and actions.

3. Farewelling is the beginning of welcoming guests next time.
