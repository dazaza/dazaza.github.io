Deal with Baidu and Google station closure

by liuji on 2008-10-13 02:18:52

Many SEO newbies are complaining that their websites have been banned, or a certain keyword has been banned, or they have been penalized. What should be done in such cases? Fortunately, there are now solutions available.

Google administrators never manually intervene to check or delete any website. All search engine indexing processes are entirely generated by machines following rules. However, if issues arise that machines cannot detect, or if legal problems occur, Baidu may have other ways to handle them.

Being banned by Baidu is quite troublesome. First of all, Baidu does not provide any tools to help you recover, and the possibility of manual intervention is high. Moreover, after manual intervention, the recovery period could be three years or even longer, which would be very inconvenient because one cannot wait so long for a website; time is money. Therefore, if you believe you have been subjected to manual intervention, you need to first check your own website for issues such as scraping content, webpage structure, and external links. It’s also possible that it’s due to a neighboring site on the same server causing trouble. However, the chances of these scenarios happening are small. Some netizens might ask: why was my legitimate website also banned? Don’t lose hope, there are still ways to solve this issue. Based on my personal experience of being penalized and then recovering over the years, you can shut down your website for a month, allowing Baidu's servers to delete all your data. Then, make large-scale adjustments to your website structure and find some high-quality sites to link with. It is worth noting that in the initial stage, writing a few original soft articles that get reprinted is also crucial. Additionally, do not submit your URL to Baidu or use any SEO software. Afterward, just patiently wait. This method has an 80% chance of working according to my experiments, so everyone might want to give it a try.

Next, let’s talk about Google. Since Google operates entirely through programs, the methods mentioned above can also be applied. However, there is another way: Google provides a webmaster function where you can resubmit your URL to Google for re-review and re-indexing of your site.

Log into the Google Webmaster Center, choose and register for the Google Webmaster Tools, and the Webmaster Tools console will open.

Click on Request a Review under the Tools section and follow the steps.

Finally, note that Google will not lift the ban just because you bought AdWords.

If both Baidu and Google ban your site, and your modifications gain Google's approval, Baidu will also index your site. Pay attention to the logs.
