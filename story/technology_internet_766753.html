Teardown of 64GB Gold iPhone 5s: Higher Repair Costs

by anonymous on 2013-10-01 20:51:23

The renowned foreign electronics teardown master, iFixit, brought us a teardown analysis of the gold version of the iPhone 5s as soon as it hit the market. We know that Apple added a new Apple ID fingerprint recognition sensor and an M7 processor to the iPhone 5s. So what surprises will this new product bring us in terms of hardware? Let's take a look at the internal structure of the iPhone 5s with iFixit.

This teardown is of the highly popular "Gold" version of the iPhone 5s.

Apple continues to use these plum blossom screws to prevent ordinary users from disassembling the device themselves. However, there are now many specialized disassembly tools available.

Use a suction cup to separate the screen from the back cover.

You cannot recklessly pry it open because there is a wire connected to the Lightning interface under the Home button, which connects to the Touch ID sensor. If you apply too much force, it can easily break, so when removing the screen, you need to use both a suction cup and a flat pry knife as assistance.

After opening the screen, we see that the internal structure of the iPhone 5s is not very different from the iPhone 5. The most noticeable change might be that there is no longer a tab on the battery for easy removal.

Use tools to remove the metal cover on the connector, which is fixed with screws, just unscrew them. Next, start dismantling the battery. The battery inside this 64GB gold iPhone 5s is supplied by Huizhou Desay Lithium Battery Technology Co., Ltd. Specifications: 3.8V - 5.92Wh - 1560mAh.

Comparison:

iPhone 5: 3.8V - 5.45Wh - 1440mAh, 8 hours of 3G talk time, standby for 225 hours;

Samsung Galaxy S4: 3.8V - 2600mAh - 9.88Wh, 7 hours talk time, standby for 300 hours;

Motorola Moto X: 3.8V - 2200mAh - 8.4Wh, 24 hours mixed use.

It seems that different models have different battery suppliers; the 16GB gray iPhone 5s uses a battery from Neweb Technologies Corporation.

After safely removing the battery, the next step is to dismantle the screen. To do this, you first need to remove the FaceTime camera assembly, the digitizer, and the LCD cable before the screen assembly can be fully liberated.

Now you see the disassembly of the Home button and Touch ID. The fingerprint recognition technology is provided by AuthenTec, a mobile security company acquired by Apple.

This is the iSight camera module, printed with DNL333 41GRF 4W61W. According to Chipworks' analysis, this DNL marking matches those found on the camera modules of the iPhone 4s and iPhone 5, both of which use Sony's IMX145 camera. Although the marking beside the iPhone 5s camera module is different, insiders tell us that the iPhone 5s still uses Sony's camera module. However, the pixel pitch of the iPhone 5s' iSight camera has been increased to 1.5 μm, so it is unlikely to still be the IMX145—it may be a newer model. Printed below the camera module is AW32 65BD 4511 b763.

Although the internal components of the iPhone 5s are not significantly different from those of the iPhone 5, the internal structure has been optimized, appearing more streamlined. The wires connecting the antennas have been removed, and if the antenna connectors could be moved from beneath the motherboard to above it, it would be even better.

The component we see here should be the Murata 339S0205 Broadcom BCM4334 Wi-Fi module from Murata. The power modules of the 16GB and 64GB versions are the same, and so are the motherboards, but the printed markings differ, indicating that Apple manufactures motherboards for different subcontractors.

When we began looking for the M7 coprocessor, we were unsuccessful, as it seems Apple has hidden it well and we couldn't find it.

Alright, it's time to take a look at the 64-bit A7.

iSight camera.

The speaker assembly is somewhat different from the previous generation; it integrates more components. After disassembling the speaker assembly, the headphone jack, microphone, and Lightning connector are also easy to remove. Similar to the previous generation, you must replace each part individually since they are not designed as a single module.

This is the flexible circuit of the speaker assembly and the back cover assembly.

After completing the teardown, let's take a look at the complete family portrait of the 64GB "Gold" iPhone 5s after the teardown.

In general, from the perspective of display replacement and battery replacement, the iPhone 5s has become simpler. Especially regarding the battery, the iPhone 5 adopted a pull-tab design and used more adhesives to secure the battery. Additionally, users must be particularly careful with the cable for the Apple ID fingerprint sensor during disassembly, as relying on past experience can easily lead to its being pulled out of the socket.

The specially designed screws used in the iPhone 5s are still not easy to remove. Overall, the repair cost of the iPhone 5s is higher than that of previous generations.
