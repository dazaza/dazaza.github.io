Since as far back as he can remember, Xiao Zhuang has always considered himself a handsome guy. This belief wasn't baseless. From childhood to adulthood, whether it's relatives, elders, or the auntie at the snack stall, the questionnaire-filling sisters, the ticket-scalping aunties, or classmates borrowing notes, everyone would sincerely call him "handsome," so naturally, he believed he was one...

The ones who woke Xiao Zhuang up from his dream of being a handsome guy were his two classmates and best friends, Si Si — a whimsical roommate full of humor, and as the vice president of the pigeon racing club, Si Si claims that "Old Wang the Pigeon" is his only confidant! And Kang — a self-proclaimed playboy and founder/president of the pigeon research club. Having been in a few relationships, Kang loves to act as Xiao Zhuang's love analyst, but what drives Xiao Zhuang crazy is that following Kang's advice never seems to work out!

Actually, Xiao Zhuang and Cindy's connection goes way back. Even before they entered university, they had an imperfect encounter. For Cindy, Xiao Zhuang even joined the food club. Driven by all his courage, Xiao Zhuang confessed his feelings to Cindy, only to hear her coolly respond with a simple "no." Feeling as if he'd been shot in the chest, Xiao Zhuang struggled to rise from the bloodstain, using his last bit of strength to ask why? Cindy looked at Xiao Zhuang and said, "Because you're too ugly." At that moment, Xiao Zhuang felt like he'd been shot again...

Cindy and Xiao Zhuang's complicated love story becomes even more tangled with the addition of the beautiful Qiao Ping into the triangle. With Si Si now dating the quirky female Pin Xuan and unable to help Xiao Zhuang, and Kang encountering the hardest love triangle of his life, unable to assist either, the three find themselves simultaneously stuck in a "love crisis." Thus, these three brothers-in-arms weave a super romantic campus comedy filled with laughter through tears and exhaustion.
