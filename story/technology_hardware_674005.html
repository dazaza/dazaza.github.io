She herself is sick.

by lpoihjkmkk on 2012-02-24 09:36:17

There was once a case where the "adoptive mother" of a rescued child argued with the police officers who came to her door, holding the child in her arms. The "adoptive mother" kept emphasizing, "I neither steal nor rob, so what law have I broken? I am adopting the child, not trafficking, how is it wrong to raise a child? If you say raising a child is a crime, then the whole world is committing a crime." After being persuaded and educated by the police, this "adopter" finally realized the absurdity of her actions and voluntarily handed over the child to the police the next day.

The husband was addicted to drugs, so she sold her own child

According to the confession of members of the baby trafficking gang, Yuan and Xue, the children they bought from Ji were mainly sold to relatives and friends in their hometown of Dingtao County, Heze City. Jiang and her husband had two sons, and they both came out to work in Sichuan several years ago. Their family situation had just improved when unfortunately, her husband developed a drug addiction habit which could not be corrected despite repeated attempts, leading to multiple arrests by the police. At the end of 2010, Jiang became pregnant again, and at this time, her husband was detained for compulsory rehabilitation in Zibo due to drug use, leaving them without a source of income. Just a few days after the birth of her newborn, Jiang actively contacted her fellow townsman Ji and sold the child for 33,000 yuan. However, according to the introduction, among the five rescued children, none were Jiang's.

The black hand of a cross-provincial special large-scale infant trafficking gang was cut off by the Chengyang police, and five infants in swaddling clothes were successfully rescued. It was understood that these infants all came from Sichuan, with the youngest being only a few days old. In the "profit chain," the gang members each played their roles clearly. On January 5th, at the Chengyang police station, Jiang, who personally sold her biological son to human traffickers, expressed that she really could not afford to support the child who had been sold and did not want to reclaim him anymore.

The special investigation team worked hard and actively sought the families of these pitiful children. According to Ji's confession and the efforts of the police officers, on the evening of November 16, 2011, the police successfully arrested Jiang (female, 37 years old), a Sichuanese woman living with two underage children in an apartment in Laizhou. After being captured, Jiang regretfully told her tragic story.

If the perpetrator does not aim to sell but instead buys and raises trafficked children, not only are the costs of buying and raising the child unrecoverable, but it also constitutes the crime of buying trafficked children. According to Article 241 of the Criminal Law, luring trafficked children can result in imprisonment, criminal detention, or control for up to three years. Buying trafficked children, illegally depriving or restricting their personal freedom, or committing crimes such as injury or insult will be punished according to relevant provisions of the Criminal Law; if there are other criminal acts against trafficked children, penalties will be imposed according to the principle of cumulative punishment; selling trafficked children will be punished as child trafficking; if no harm is done to trafficked children and they are not obstructed from rescue, criminal responsibility may not be pursued. Obstructing state functionaries from rescuing trafficked children by violence or threat will be punished for obstruction of official duties; key figures obstructing state functionaries from rescuing trafficked children will be punished for the crime of obstructing the rescue of trafficked children.

Six circumstances of child trafficking can lead to the death penalty

On the afternoon of January 5th, I interviewed lawyer Zhang Baoqing, the head of Shandong Chuanjia Law Firm, regarding legal issues related to infant trafficking. Lawyer Zhang introduced that the crime of child trafficking refers to the act of deceiving, kidnapping, enticing, trafficking, receiving, or transferring children for the purpose of selling them. Children refer to minors under the age of 14. Biological parents selling their own minor children under the age of 14 also constitute the crime of child trafficking.

On the afternoon of January 5th, I saw Jiang and her two children at the Chengyang police station. It was introduced that because the two children still needed care, the Chengyang police did not detain Jiang at the police station but instead arranged for the three of them to stay in a hotel under surveillance. Jiang said that she herself was sick, could not do heavy work, and did not earn much money. Taking care of the two children had already tired her out, and she really could not afford to support a third one. Regarding the child that was sold, Jiang stated that she truly could not afford to support him and did not want to reclaim him anymore.

"These suspects initially helped others during their first baby trafficking, but later, driven by profit, they turned child trafficking into a means of making money." The investigating police officer analyzed the behavior of these suspects.

Yuan and Ji from the baby trafficking gang initially also helped others. On the morning of January 5th, I saw Yuan and Ji from the baby trafficking gang at the Chengyang Detention Center. Both admitted their criminal acts of trafficking children without reservation. Yuan confessed that a relative in his hometown had a boy but wanted a girl too, influenced by the traditional concept of having both a son and a daughter. Yuan contacted Xue to help the relative "adopt" a girl. "I never thought the matter would actually succeed, and later many childless fellow villagers asked me to help them 'adopt' children." Yuan said that the first time was indeed helping someone, without any payment, but later he seized this "opportunity" to start profiting. "At first, I didn't know it was a crime, now I deeply regret it."

One of the "female protagonists" in the baby trafficking gang, Xue, has two grown-up children, one working locally and the other married and having children at home. She and her husband came to work in Laizhou. According to Xue's confession, the "baby trafficking" started with an act of "doing good." The illiterate Xue quickly met Ji, a migrant worker from Sichuan renting a house in Laizhou. Ji proposed to Xue to ask around if anyone wanted to adopt a child, and Xue remembered a relative back home who had been married for many years without having a child. As an intermediary, Xue helped this relative buy a girl for 25,000 yuan from Ji.

"Dingtao County is one of the poorer areas, and these families buying children mostly rely on farming for a living, with generally low levels of education and weak legal awareness." Investigating police officers introduced that during their visits, they found that these villagers buying children placed great importance on traditional concepts such as "having both a son and a daughter" and "raising sons to support old age." "People buying children fall into two categories: those who don't have boys in their families and those who have been married for many years without having children." Investigating police officers introduced that it was precisely these foolish ideas that provided the market for criminals to traffic children.

"Trafficking children easily leads to the separation of victims' families, and even family destruction, with extremely serious social harm, therefore, the crime of child trafficking has always been a focus of China's criminal law." Lawyer Zhang Baoqing said.

According to Article 240 of the Criminal Law, those who commit the crime of child trafficking shall be sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment of more than 5 years but less than 10 years, and shall also be fined; if any of the following situations occur, they shall be sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment of more than 10 years or life imprisonment, and shall also be fined or have their property confiscated; if the circumstances are especially serious, they shall be sentenced to death and their property confiscated: (1) the main members of the child trafficking group; (2) those who traffic three or more children; (3) those who use violence, coercion, or anesthesia to kidnap children for the purpose of selling them; (4) those who steal infants for the purpose of selling them; (5) those who cause serious injuries, deaths, or other serious consequences to trafficked children or their relatives; (6) those who sell children overseas.

From "doing good" to "money-making tool"

Later, Xue took it as a way to make money, abandoned her job, and couldn't stop herself. She made several trips to her hometown in Heze, actively looking for buyers. At the same time, Xue also actively mobilized relatives and friends near Heze Dingtao county and townships, and asked fellow workers outside to help find people with "adoption" needs. In less than a year, five Sichuan babies had been sold through Xue and Yuan, four to Heze and one to Qingdao.

Ji also said that at first, E held a child and came to him, hoping he could find an adoptive person for the child. "There happened to be a friend who wanted a child, so I connected them and sold the child to him. Later, seeing that others also wanted children, I began to consider it as a way to make money."
