Zheng Guoguang: China's air environmental observation data has been internationally recognized Weather Forecast News Center Sina

by psyzems5 on 2012-03-07 21:48:05

Regarding the new PM2.5 observation standard introduced earlier, Zheng Guoguang said that the meteorological department would make corresponding adjustments to the standards and methods of air quality forecasting. The new detection not only includes PM2.5 and 10-day weather forecasts but also sulfur dioxide, inhalable particulate matter, PM10, and other contents. In addition, ozone has been added so that China's monitoring and forecasting of environmental air quality becomes more reasonable.

In addition, China's meteorological department is also responsible for observing atmospheric components. There are a total of 22 global atmospheric baseline observation stations, seven of which are in China. The Waliuan station in Qinghai is responsible for researching global greenhouse gas concentrations, while the remaining six stations observe harmful components such as carbon monoxide, carbon dioxide, methane, and nitrogen oxides in the atmosphere. These observations are carried out according to international standards and are part of the global atmospheric composition observation system. Weather forecasts for Gansu River at Nanchang section are nearly three meters higher than the same period last year due to heavy snowfall causing traffic disruptions in multiple areas of Inner Mongolia, with the scope of strong rainfall in southern regions shrinking. Snowflakes are falling in Northeast China while tornadoes are hitting the Midwest of the United States, flattening a small town.

Zheng Guoguang mentioned that there may be variations in PM2.5 observation results in different places. Beijing currently has seven PM2.5 observation points, and the differences in their observation data are significant. Beijing is trying to comprehensively understand the overall situation of PM2.5 by adding new observation sites.

Currently, China’s meteorological department has 74 PM2.5 observation stations, some of which have been operating for over a decade. The meteorological department also conducts PM10 observations, with a total of 95 observation stations nationwide. Additionally, there are 73 more refined PM1 observation stations. These observation stations have formed a relatively complete observation system.

On March 5, Zheng Guoguang, a member of the National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference and Director of the China Meteorological Administration, stated that meteorological departments at all levels cooperate closely with environmental protection departments in environmental meteorological forecasting. They have done a lot of work in air quality observation and forecasting, and their observation data has gained international recognition.
