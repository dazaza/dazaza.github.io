How to draw nice eyeliner by yourself?

by silenfir on 2008-03-07 16:00:42

Drawing eyeliner is to beautify and highlight the eyes, while also being able to modify and change the shape of the eyes through eyeliner techniques. Drawing eyeliner can adjust the eye contour and the distance between the two eyes, enhance the sparkle of the eyes, and make the contrast between the black and white parts of the eyes stronger. For example, for an eyeliner of the same length, if the center part of the eyeliner is drawn thicker, it will create a feeling of shortened eye length and bigger eyes; if the eyeliner at the tail of the eye is extended, it will create a feeling of narrow and long eyes. This is the secret of making small eyes look bigger and big eyes look smaller. Through drawing eyeliner, one can also express a person's temperament. For example, if the emphasis is placed on the lower eyeliner, which is thicker than the upper eyeliner, the position of the eyes will be lowered, appearing innocent and lively; if the emphasis is placed on the upper eyeliner, the eye position will be elevated, appearing mature and steady.

The tool for drawing eyeliner is the eyeliner pencil. There are two types of eyeliner pencils: one is soft and the other is hard. Hard-colored eyeliner pencils are easier to handle. Choose a waterproof soft-core eyeliner pencil, sharpen the tip thin and fine, and draw along the base of the eyelashes. The upper eyeliner is thicker, and the lower eyeliner is slightly finer. Drawing with an eyeliner pencil appears soft and natural, suitable for daily makeup. Soft eyeliner pencils, also known as eyeliner liquids, choose waterproof eyeliner liquid and draw from the outer corner of the eye towards the inner corner. When drawing, your hand should be steady, and the stroke should be higher than the eye contour. The drawing of eyeliner liquid appears bright and eye-catching, suitable for heavy makeup.

For beginners learning to draw eyeliner, the first problem encountered is that the hand holding the pen tends to shake, causing uneven thickness and wavy lines, failing to achieve a beautifying effect. To avoid hand shaking, you can place the little finger holding the pen against the cheekbone, or support the elbow on the table...
