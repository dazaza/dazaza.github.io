On February 26, Beijing time (February 25 in Orlando), Kobe finally appeared before the media prior to the All-Star training session. The previous day, Kobe had missed the press conference due to illness but attended a sponsor event, which left the media quite dissatisfied. Today, a reporter specifically asked Kobe if he was feeling better, to which Kobe casually replied, "I'm fine now." Jeremy Lin has been the talk of the All-Star game, and when Kobe previously faced off against Lin, it became one of the hottest topics in the NBA. When speaking about Lin again today, Kobe continued to praise him, even stating that if he were the owner, he would fire any general manager who had let Lin go.

Kobe openly admitted that for him now, the All-Star game means relaxation and rest; whether or not he wins the MVP is not important at all. "I'll watch others dunk or perform difficult moves," he said. When a Sina reporter asked him what his best memory from his 14 All-Star experiences was, Kobe smiled and said, "Absolutely the first one. I call that the real golden age. Playing with Jordan, Barkley—it was definitely the best experience."

Reporter: Kobe, you were sick yesterday, are you feeling better today?

Kobe: I'm fine now.

Reporter: How does it feel to play alongside Andrew Bynum for the first time in the All-Star game?

Kobe: It feels great. He has a knee injury, so he can't play much today, but I know he's very excited, and I'm happy for him. He's improved a lot, which is why he has this opportunity now.

Reporter: Can you tell us your most beautiful and profound memory of the All-Star game?

Kobe: I will always say my first All-Star game, which I call the real golden era, with Jordan, Charles Barkley, Karl Malone—it was an incredibly fun experience.

Reporter: What do you think about how Lin was continuously dismissed by team general managers?

Kobe: If I were the owner, I might fire all the general managers who didn't hire him or fired him. I heard about his journey where no one wanted to listen to him.

Reporter: If the Clippers make it to the finals and you can only watch from home, how would you feel?

Kobe: Let me tell you, that's not much of a question.

Reporter: You've won championships, five rings, been named Most Valuable Player, received countless honors—what does the All-Star game mean to you now?

Kobe: Rest and relaxation. Let others do those high-difficulty dunks and performances; it's nice just to sit on the sidelines and watch.
