Yesterday at noon, a citizen of Liuzhou left his child who was only 28 days old at home alone. As a result, the baby was attacked by rats and bitten seriously.

At about 12 o'clock yesterday at noon, Ms. Yang who lived in a rented house near Kanghua neighborhood on Liuyong Road in Liuzhou went out to buy vegetables after leaving her daughter who had been born for only 28 days on the bed alone. After a while, when she returned, she saw a rat weighting about 0.5 kilograms biting her baby randomly on the body, causing the baby to cry and struggle from the pain. Ms. Yang drove the rat away quickly, but at this time, her baby's face had already been bitten bloody and the blanket covering her baby was full of blood.

Ms. Yang ran to her neighbor for help and the neighbor dialed the emergency call immediately. Soon, the baby was sent to the Worker Hospital of Liuzhou for emergency treatment. Upon examination, the baby's upper lip had been bitten off by the rat. The nose, ears, scalp, palms, and feet were all skinned by the rat and her face was full of rat tooth marks. "It was terrible! Many doctors couldn't bear to look!" According to Tang Liping, the director of the pediatrics department of the Workers Hospital, they had also received children who were bitten by rats before, but this was the first time they encountered one that was so severely injured.

The medical staff immediately disinfected the wounded baby, stopped the bleeding, injected tetanus needle and transfused blood. Currently, the baby has gotten out of danger.

At around 5 p.m. yesterday afternoon, when the baby's father Mr. Xiao heard the news and rushed to the hospital, he almost fainted when he saw the situation of his baby. Mr. Xiao said that the day before, he had found a rat weighing about 0.5 kilograms at home, but failed to drive it out at that time. He didn't expect it would cause such a great disaster.

Tang Liping introduced that the reason why the rat attacked the baby might be that the mother didn't clean the milk residue on the baby's lips with water after feeding her. The rat smelled the milk and mistook the baby for food.

During the interview, the reporter found that both parents of the wounded baby were disabled and their family life was very difficult. Ms. Yang said that they came to work in Liuzhou not long ago. Currently, her family mainly relied on the money she earned every day by carrying packages at the cargo yard. In order to give birth to this baby, they still owed the hospital 850 yuan. Now that such an incident occurred, she was at a loss and didn't know how to get through the days ahead.

According to relevant doctors, although the baby has got out of danger, the cost of later treatment such as skin grafting and the expenses required for future recovery would be probably too much for this family to afford.
