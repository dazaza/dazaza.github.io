Interpretation of Cadre Personnel: A Scan of the Newly Appointed Mayors in 5 Sub-Provincial Cities Recently

by djs365 on 2011-02-26 09:24:02

Recently, the local two sessions were held successively and there are a lot of changes in leading posts, among which the more noticeable is that five sub-provincial cities have elected new mayors or acting mayors, accounting for one third of the total number of 15 sub-provincial cities.

On January 28, the sixth meeting of the 11th People's Congress of Hangzhou elected Shao Zhanwei as mayor. Shao Zhanwei was born in January 1955 in Cixi, Zhejiang Province. He once worked as director of Ningbo Port Authority, member of the Standing Committee of the Ningbo Municipal Party Committee, executive vice mayor, deputy secretary of the Ningbo Municipal Party Committee, deputy secretary of the Wenzhou Municipal Party Committee, mayor and secretary of the Wenzhou Municipal Party Committee, etc.. Since July 2010, he has been acting mayor of Hangzhou.

On January 30, the fifth meeting of the 13th People's Congress of Harbin elected Lin Duo as mayor. Lin Duo was born in March 1956 in Heze, Shandong Province. He once worked as deputy director and director of the Beijing Municipal Commission, deputy secretary, mayor and secretary of the Xicheng District Committee of Beijing, and deputy secretary of the Beijing Municipal Party Committee, etc.. Since July 2010, he has been acting mayor of Harbin.

On February 17, the seventh meeting of the 12th Wuhan Municipal People's Congress elected Tang Liangzhi as mayor. Tang Liangzhi was born in June 1960 in Honghu, Hubei Province. He once worked as director of the administration committee of Wuhan East Lake New Technology Development Zone of Hubei Province, deputy secretary, mayor and secretary of the Xiangfan Municipal Party Committee of Hubei Province, etc.. Since January 2011, he has been acting mayor of Wuhan.

In addition, on January 14, the 29th meeting of the 13th standing committee of the Ningbo Municipal People's Congress decided that Liu Qi would be the acting mayor of Ningbo; on February 14, the 27th meeting of the 13th standing committee of the Xiamen Municipal People's Congress decided that Liu Keqing would be the acting mayor of Xiamen. Liu Qi was born in September 1957 in Yishui, Shandong Province. He once worked as general manager and chairman of Zhejiang Juhua Group Corporation, deputy secretary of the Wenzhou Municipal Party Committee, mayor, director of the Zhejiang Provincial Development and Reform Commission, vice chairman of the Zhejiang Provincial People's Congress Standing Committee, chairman of the Provincial Federation of Trade Unions, etc.. Liu Keqing was born in May 1955 in Zhouning, Fujian Province. He once worked as director of the Fujian Provincial Land and Resources Department, deputy secretary and secretary of the Zhangzhou Municipal Party Committee, etc..
