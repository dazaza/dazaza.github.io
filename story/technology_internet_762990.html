Russian gas giant spends $3.7 million on a tablet for its CEO that boots up in five seconds

by anonymous on 2013-07-29 12:05:25

According to a report by Bloomberg, Gazprom, the world's largest natural gas producer, recently spent 119.7 million rubles (approximately $3.7 million or about RMB 22.7 million) on a custom-made private tablet for its CEO, Alexey Miller, to help him manage company affairs more efficiently.

The Russian gas giant custom-made a $3.7 million tablet with a five-second boot time for its CEO.

Gazprom posted a tender document on its official website and announced that this exorbitantly priced tablet must be developed based on Apple's iOS system. It will not only provide the functions of a regular tablet but also allow real-time monitoring of all company operations.

It is reported that this incredible tablet has a boot time of no more than five seconds, supports 3G, GPS, and Wi-Fi connectivity. Moreover, it can monitor in real-time essential data for Gazprom's daily operations, such as natural gas production and export volumes. This specially customized tablet will assist CEO Alexey Miller in changing his previous method of managing the company's daily operations solely through a standard desktop computer, allowing him to oversee the entire company’s operations from anywhere at any time.

Gazprom is the world's largest natural gas producer, and its revenue in 2013 declined by 13% compared to the same period. The company has consistently opposed calls to reduce fuel demand and even broke the 40-year-old system where natural gas prices were always higher than those of ordinary oil to increase natural gas sales. Currently, Gazprom's consecutive losses have significantly impacted investor confidence, and the company faces significant challenges given the global market share, especially the reduction in natural gas supply from the U.S. and Australia.

Currently, Russian President Putin has issued new requirements regarding the excessive waste prevalent domestically. During the 20th-anniversary celebration of Gazprom held at the Kremlin in February this year, he clearly stated the need to eliminate all unnecessary expenditures within Gazprom and enhance spending transparency.

Even under these challenging circumstances, not everyone believes this move is entirely wasteful. Alexander Patsay, the software project development director of Parallel and the development lead for this expensive tablet, said: "The outside world needs to understand what constitutes waste. We are not talking about an iPad encrusted with diamonds here but rather a tablet that can fully automate the CEO's work. This is similar in nature to the high-priced software services provided by companies like SAP AG; although costly, it indeed handles complex situations."

Currently, Apple's spokesperson in Russia has not made any comments regarding this expensive iPad. According to Gazprom's tender documents, the company will announce the winning bid between July 22 and 31. The winner will provide Gazprom with a complete solution from July 31 to April 30, 2014.
