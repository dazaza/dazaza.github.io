Futures - the purchasing entity has to keep raising the purchase price.

by yanlabav on 2012-03-07 09:51:58

Recently, after touching a new high for the year, Dalian corn futures retreated in weakness. The main contract 1209 fell back from 2428 yuan to below 2400 yuan. In terms of factors affecting corn, the firmness of spot corn prices supports the futures price, but difficulties in selling corn in North China and weak purchasing intentions from feed companies have dragged down corn prices. Therefore, corn is likely to consolidate in the short term.

The strong spot price supports the corn futures price. After the Spring Festival, as domestic buyers began purchasing successively, domestic spot corn prices steadily rose, like gold futures investment. Taking Northeast port spot corn prices as an example, as of March 6th, the mainstream flat-bottomed barge quotation for 15% moisture content second-grade corn at the port was 2430 yuan/ton, with higher quotations reaching 2440 yuan/ton, up about 50 yuan/ton from the same period last month.

Currently, there are many players in the corn spot market with strong purchasing intentions, including state reserves, traders, and deep-processing enterprises. To acquire more quality grain sources, these buyers had to repeatedly raise their purchase prices. Taking deep-processing enterprises as an example, the current second-grade corn acquisition listed price for Changchun Dacheng and Jincheng companies is 2240 yuan/ton, up 140 yuan/ton from the same period last month. From this, it can be seen that the possibility of corn spot prices remaining strong later on is relatively high, which will provide strong support for futures prices.

An increase in the mold rate of corn has increased the difficulty of selling corn in North China. Shandong and Henan provinces are major domestic corn-producing areas, with the combined corn output of the two provinces accounting for approximately 39% of the national total. The sales situation of corn in this region significantly affects the trend of domestic corn spot and futures markets. Information obtained from the market shows that the mold rate of corn in some parts of the two provinces is relatively high, limiting the amount of local corn that can be used in bulk as feed ingredients. Local feed companies have had to turn to purchasing high-quality corn from Northeast China.

Although the impact of corn mold on deep-processing enterprises is relatively small, the demand for corn from feed companies exceeds that of deep-processing enterprises. A decrease in demand for local corn from feed companies increases the difficulty of selling corn. Currently, farmers in Shandong and Henan still hold a large amount of grain, and the supply pressure in the spot market will weigh on corn prices. Taking Zhoukou in Henan as an example, the local farmers' selling progress is currently around 50%-60%, with a relatively high proportion of remaining grain.

Weak demand from feed companies restrains the upward space for corn. After the Spring Festival, the prices of some domestic livestock products dropped significantly, while the prices of feed raw materials such as corn and soybean meal continued to rise. This reduced the profits of domestic farming enterprises, leading to a decline in feed demand. Taking pig farming as an example, according to monitoring by Xinhua News Agency's national agricultural products and production materials price index system, national pork prices have been declining for over a month, and the pace of decline remains relatively fast. Compared to January 26th, 2012, as of March 5th, the prices of pork loin and pork belly nationwide dropped by 8.2% and 8.3%, respectively.

In contrast, as of the week ending March 2nd, the average purchase price of corn by national livestock farmers was approximately 2.21 yuan/kg, up 8.33% year-on-year. The national pig-to-grain ratio fell from 7.05:1 last week to 6.94:1, down 4.84% year-on-year. It can be seen that the recovery of demand from feed companies still requires time, which will restrain the upward momentum of corn.

In summary, the current supply and demand fundamentals of corn are intertwined with multiple bullish and bearish factors. The supply pressure of corn in North China and weak feed demand drag down the upward momentum of corn futures, but the strong spot price limits the downward space for corn. The likelihood of short-term consolidation in corn futures is relatively high. (The author is an analyst at Green Futures.)
