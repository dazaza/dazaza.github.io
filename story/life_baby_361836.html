Johnson's Baby Shampoo Gently Nurtures Your Baby's Skin

As the scorching heat of July arrives, the high temperatures in the air accompanied by heat waves have led to a peak sales period for many heatstroke prevention and cooling products. Among these are various baby bathing and skin care products specifically designed for infants during the summer months. The author personally experienced the purchasing enthusiasm and intensity of mothers at the largest supermarket in the city, where there is a dedicated counter for baby bathing and skin care products. The author noticed that the Johnson's Baby Shampoo series has consistently ranked in the top three on the supermarket's single-item sales chart for four weeks. According to supermarket sales staff, the biggest advantage of this shampoo is that its pH level is closest to that of a baby's skin, making it gentle in nature. It also features the "no-tear formula" logo, which represents the highest standard of purity and gentleness for global infant bath products. Although other brands also have this logo, consumers still prefer the well-known brand Johnson's Baby. During the summer, this Johnson's Baby Shampoo often sells out, with some parents going straight to the counter and picking up two bottles without even asking.

Today’s parents are mostly highly educated, and when choosing skin care products for their babies, safety and quality are their primary considerations. When buying such products, they will certainly pay attention to whether the packaging has the red water-drop shaped "no-tear formula" (currently, many competing products also use the term "no-tear formula", but what sets JB apart from them is that only JB can use the no-tear formula water drop icon) symbolizing the highest standard of purity and gentleness in global infant bath products. This is what constitutes a healthy, safe, pH-neutral professional qualified baby bath and skin care product.

A baby's skin is delicate and sensitive, with an incomplete development of the stratum corneum and poor resistance. The "no-tear formula" has larger molecular volume, so it does not irritate a baby's eyes or skin, and it carries no charge, maintaining a neutral pH, being mild and non-irritating. In medical terms, 0-1 month old is considered a newborn, 1 month to 1 year is an infant, 1 to 3 years is a toddler, and 3 to 7 years is a preschooler. Among these, babies aged 0 to 7 years have thin stratum corneum, less surface oil, tender and sensitive skin, and weaker resistance to external factors, making their skin prone to infections and various skin problems.

In spring, with frequent winds and blooming flowers, pollen can easily cause allergic reactions on a baby's skin. In summer, the high temperature and strong ultraviolet rays can easily lead to sunburns. In autumn and winter, with low humidity and temperature, issues like chapped and dry skin can arise. Therefore, a baby's bath lotion should first and foremost not irritate their delicate skin. An infant's skin pH is close to neutral, which is the ideal skin environment. Hence, protection and cleaning are their main skin care needs. Therefore, baby skin care requires a gentle, pH-neutral baby bath lotion that can increase the thickness of the skin's oil membrane and better moisturize the baby's skin.

Currently, there are many baby skin care brands available on the market. For instance, the Johnson's Baby Shampoo series offers corresponding skin care products based on seasonal changes and different needs, such as those containing milk essence and vitamins A and E, those with natural honeysuckle and chamomile essence, and those with SPF15 sun protection functions. These allow mothers to choose suitable types for their babies, hoping that their babies will have healthy, delicate, and fair skin all year round.
