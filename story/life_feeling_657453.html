Summer Sorrow, For You I Am Injured

This summer's plum rains were shattered by a sudden thunderclap, scattering an unexpected sorrow that cast you into a gaze with no return.

Your heart is a magnificent seed in this world, and wherever it has touched, life sprouts. Every flower, every blade of grass, every tree, every cycle of growth and decay opens its eyes under your skillful pen, expressing joy, worry, or sorrow, quietly radiating the light of existence.

Within and without the mundane world, emotions run deep, yet we are separated by heaven and earth.

Reading you, reading myself, I find endless joy and sorrow, extracting wounds from wounds. Fresh blood stains your window like crimson frost. Tell me, where does the door open in which alley? On your oar's sound, can I land at the edge of your dream?

Back then, you told me you were cold, so cold, colder than any winter in childhood.

I thought it was just another of your tricks, meant to force upon me a sleepless night of rain.

I ignored you. How foolish I was!

I never realized that you were telling me you had encountered the severe winter of your own life, how bone-chillingly cold it must have been?! Even if I couldn't spin a warm spring filled with blossoms for you, couldn't I have given you a warm coat to comfort the cold? But I, didn't even light a single soothing candle for you.

I hate myself! I cannot forgive myself! How could I be so indifferent to life?!

I want to be injured, I must be injured! My hands, which did not reach out to you when you needed them most, must endure punishment!!

Blood, flowing from the rivers of my heart, streams across the wilderness of my palms.

Does it hurt? It seems a bit, but I'm not sure, there's no feeling. All I see is the blood pooling on the ground. I tell you, "My heart, red as clouds, lightly taps on your window. Do you hear it?"

On the operating table, watching the doctor's surgical forceps with their sharp metal hooks bite into my skin, I know she is fishing for the wound in my heart, thereby saving me.

Your wound is vast as the heavens, what are mine compared to yours? I only wish for my blood, carrying my heartbeat and warmth, to rush towards you, sending you a short note, asking the mountain mists and clear winds to read it for you - our bond is not yet finished, we shall meet again in the next life.

The doctor reduced four stitches to three, saying there would still be a scar.

I said, "I like it."

She was puzzled.

I said again, "I will like that scar."

How could she know, this isn't a scar, but a plum blossom you've plucked from paradise, and as soon as I reached out, it took root, blooming eternally in my life as a flower of - love.

So, to approach you, no ladder is needed. To read you, no eyes or lips are required, but rather a pair of fragrant hands to gather the perfumed sleeves that come from reading you. Thinking of you, I no longer use sorrow as my oar, because the road to your homeland is no longer as bumpy as I once imagined.

"Think about it, how beautiful it is there, with orange flowers blooming, like a dream."

These were the words you spoke to me when we first met, now I say them to you.

Think about it, how ancient and genuine it is, full of the original sounds of life, resonating like music. There is the soil you adore, copper-red in color, lotus-pink in warmth, roseate in romance, just like the tranquil sentiment on the ancient walls of Lijiang.

Imagine, you have gone to antiquity, so far beyond everything, yet near within everything, it is the soil you have longed for in your hometown.

You have returned home, and found happiness. I, will smile.

In a daze, I found you in the 'Along the River During the Qingming Festival' painting.

Look, your flowing robe billows as you wear a hat woven from pine needles, walking with the steps of Suzhou storytelling. Beside you, your divine crane gazes up at you with deep affection. Your passionate songs drift among the ancient rafters, waves of divinity rippling through the air. In the market, people stop to listen and watch you. The scene is just like when you were on the island in Suzhou, and villagers lined up to practice martial arts with you. Ha ha, I secretly rejoice, they'll be too busy, they have too much to be enchanted by you, and surely many hearts will be captured by you. You will certainly teach them how to freely write poetry with ingenious imagination, how to weave stories into popular novels, how to play in water like divine fish, how to make leek and pork dumplings, how to cook sticky and fragrant sour soup. Oh, wait, your tricks are always too many, people can't keep up with your pace. If you miss home, drink the earthenware bowl of wine until it becomes the path back to your hometown, here, friends and family welcome you grandly.

Tonight, it might rain. If you go out, remember to put on a straw cloak...
