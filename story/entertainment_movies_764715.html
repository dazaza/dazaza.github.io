Synopsis:

The story revolves around the assassination of U.S. Senator Robert F. Kennedy on June 6, 1968. The fatal bullet not only claimed Bobby's life but also changed the destinies of 22 people who were present at the Ambassador Hotel that day. More profoundly, this bullet eradicated the innocence of the entire United States.

In this film directed and starred in by Emilio Estevez, 22 individuals unknowingly became witnesses to a tragic and decisive moment of the 1960s. Set in early June 1968 in California, the presidential primary became a hot topic among many "Golden State" residents, as the competition between Robert F. Kennedy, Eugene McCarthy, and Hubert Humphrey was fierce. Kennedy's campaign headquarters were located at the Ambassador Hotel in Los Angeles. As the brother of the assassinated President John F. Kennedy moved toward a similar fate, the hotel staff and guests became witnesses to this event. Paul is the manager of the Ambassador Hotel, and his wife Miriam is a hairdresser at the hotel's beauty salon. Angela is the hotel switchboard operator, who has an affair with Paul behind Miriam's back. Timmons oversees the hotel’s restaurant and catering department, never hiding his disdain for his African American and Latino employees. Miguel and Jose are young Mexican Americans working in the kitchen; they despise Timmons, while Robinson, an elderly black man, often advises them against their radical emotions. Virginia Fallon is the singer at the hotel bar, heavily dependent on alcohol; her husband Tim, a supporter of Kennedy and her manager, is barely tolerating her troubles. William is a young man desperate to avoid military service, especially being sent to Vietnam, while Diane is a young attractive woman dating William's brother but agrees to marry William to help him evade the draft. However, it's clear that William is infatuated with her, and she assumes it will be a marriage in name only. John Casey is one of the owners of the Ambassador Hotel, and Nelson is an old friend working there. Jack is a wealthy man and one of the sponsors of Kennedy's campaign, married to Samantha, much younger and very charming. "Bobby" also features characters like Joshua Jackson, Nick Cannon, and Shia LaBeouf, all young volunteers in Kennedy's campaign, along with Ashton Kutcher, Joy Bryant, Kip Pardue, and Mary Elizabeth Winstead, all supporters of Kennedy.
