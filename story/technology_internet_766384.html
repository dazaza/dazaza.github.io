Sina Product Manager: Why does Sina Weibo create a "fan service platform"?

by anonymous on 2013-09-11 14:39:06

The exposure of Sina Weibo's internally tested "Fan Service Platform" has sparked attention. Regular users can subscribe to verified accounts (referred to as "Big Vs"), and these Big Vs can send mass private messages to their subscribers, as well as offer keyword-triggered automated replies. Some external observers have criticized this move as being overly commercialized, disruptive to users, and merely a way for Big Vs to boost their presence. This article is contributed by @Wen Xiangrui, a product manager at Sina Weibo, who explains from a product management perspective why Sina Weibo decided to launch such a platform.

Seeing how much fun @Lang Shu and others are having with the Fan Service Platform, I am genuinely delighted as someone involved in its development. Of course, while enjoying the excitement, many people have speculated about or even expressed concerns regarding this product: will it be used by Big Vs to inflate their presence? Is Weibo again trying to monetize private messaging? Following @Wang Taizhong’s earnest explanation last time, I also want to share my insights and thoughts based on my recent experiences in developing this product.

All Starts with User-initiated “Subscription” – No Harassment

This is a very important issue. Since we are still in the internal testing phase, only partial screenshots of the Fan Service Platform have been revealed, which might lead some friends to worry about potential spam-like push notifications, such as receiving messages from non-followed accounts or unwanted advertisements. Therefore, it is essential to first introduce what exactly the "Fan Service Platform" is. Currently, the platform mainly offers four key functions:

1) Mass Private Messaging: Selecting quality content and recommending it to desired fans;

2) Real-time Messaging: Viewing and quickly replying to private messages sent by users within the past five days;

3) Content Management: Pre-editing content to be pushed, including text, images, and multimedia messages that can be saved in a material library;

4) Advanced Features: Setting up specific automatic responses based on user behaviors, so when users meet designated rules, they receive an automated reply.

It is crucial to emphasize here that although the Fan Service Platform provides a mass private message function, it can only send information to already followed fans. In most cases, it can only send messages to subscribed fans. Only some official institutions possess special permissions to send urgent public information via private messages to all followers during emergencies like earthquakes.

Therefore, everyone should focus on the word "service" in the Fan Service Platform. Everything stems from the user's "active subscription." Subscription builds upon following; compared to following, subscription represents a deeper relationship. If users do not actively subscribe, all this would be meaningless!

Do Not Ignore the Platform’s “Service” Value

This section may answer many people's questions: Why create the "Fan Service Platform?" How does it differ from WeChat Official Accounts? These two products exist on different platforms—Weibo and WeChat—and the distinct positioning of each platform determines the characteristics of their derived products. However, any platform with hundreds of millions of users has the potential to become a service platform. Hence, WeChat promotes China Merchants Bank as a model, and the Fan Service Platform aims to enhance its service capabilities.

In the past, apart from its dissemination value, Weibo was also recognized for its marketing value, but its "service value" has been overlooked. From a user structure perspective, Weibo primarily serves individual users and institutional users, and simultaneously, institutional users and some individual users provide services to their followers. Correspondingly, both personal users and institutional users use Weibo's basic features for foundational services. Additionally, Weibo offers specialized modules and tools like corporate Weibo, government Weibo, and media Weibo to help these groups provide functional services to their followers. This year, Weibo is undergoing Page-based renovations, focusing on providing exclusive functional modules for different groups (including institutional and individually certified users) to achieve differentiated services.

Specifically, the "Fan Service Platform" is Weibo's latest product and tool designed around functional services, encouraging third parties to offer better services through this tool by providing them with an efficient channel to reach their audience. Considering Weibo's nature, the initial internal tests selected more personal accounts to familiarize regular users with the product through high-quality content suggestions. However, since it is called a service platform, it should eventually open permissions to governmental and corporate accounts, inviting them to offer richer personalized services to subscribing users through this product.

A simple example is @Lian Zhongguo, a senior Chinese teacher at Beijing No. 4 High School, who previously shared critical materials with tens of thousands of students nearing the middle school entrance exam via Weibo. With the Fan Service Platform, students could subscribe and use custom keywords to obtain desired materials, such as "reading comprehension" or "real test questions."

Likewise, you could privately message @Durex and customize subscriptions for specific product information like "ultra-thin" or "motion." Ultimately, the "Fan Service Platform" aims to provide more personalized and convenient "services."

Characteristics of the “Fan Service Platform”

Though it may seem inappropriate to openly praise my company's product, I personally have great confidence in it. Here are some advantages of Sina Weibo in creating this product, feel free to critique:

High Delivery Rate: Weibo is characterized by broad coverage and rapid dissemination. Amidst fast-updating information, some quality content might get lost in the flood of data, failing to effectively reach fans. Through the Fan Service Platform, however, pushed articles go directly to fans' private mailboxes, ensuring nothing gets missed.

Precise Content Push: The Fan Service Platform includes a keyword automatic response feature. When the content of a fan's private message triggers pre-set keywords, they receive corresponding articles. Users can categorize fans based on their keyword responses, offering them interested content and achieving precise content delivery.

Sense of Belonging: A fan's subscription behavior demonstrates their recognition of a particular account, helping find like-minded individuals.

Advice for Users

This was once a point of contention, but after multiple discussions, we decided against providing specific channels for rapidly accumulating subscribed fans. Instead, we encourage accounts wanting to use the Fan Service Platform to leverage their status creatively. The purpose is to allow fans to maintain control over their subscriptions, enabling users to gain truly valuable audiences. After all, this interest-driven active subscription behavior connects content with users, fostering more stable Weibo social relationships.

Of course, the product's emphasis is on service, so we advise users to consider the perspective of subscribed users when sending mass private messages. Focus on content quality, carefully set practical and interesting private message keywords, promptly respond to fans' private messages, and enhance interaction. If you have good experiences, please share them with everyone.

Finally, we look forward to seeing big names like @Durex and @Ping An Beijing collectively begging for fan subscriptions on Weibo. Being cute isn't shameful; serving is glorious!
