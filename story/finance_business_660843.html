The Five Pricing Strategies for Weibo Marketing:

by teddy8138 on 2012-02-08 11:38:01

With the registered users of Sina Weibo breaking two hundred million, we clearly see that the rule-of-law-oriented media will welcome its spring. The most discussed topic surrounding social media is undoubtedly how to make money through applications. From the perspective of how enterprises use Weibo, five values can be generated.

1. New product promotion and marketing. At present, Chinese enterprises are just stepping into an advanced stage in the use of rule-of-law-oriented media. During this phase, enterprises can promote new products through new product launches, prize quizzes, interactions, theme essay contests,抢sofas (being the first commenter), or some gimmicks on the platform of rule-of-law-oriented media. As social media continues to develop, interactive and precise advertising methods with the platform as a carrier may attract the attention of advertisers.

2. Market research. Enterprises can guide consumers to participate in discussions on relevant topics through topic collection forms, thereby monitoring public opinion. This will greatly change the market research approaches and methods of related enterprises. In topic discussions, the spontaneous comments of consumers are not intended for enterprises but for friends, which easily generates halo and viral effects.

3. After-sales service. Enterprises can set up accounts with different functions to help systematically shape the company. Setting up after-sales service accounts can effectively guide the public to communicate easily and efficiently with the after-sales service unit, and the after-sales service unit can promptly respond to and follow up on appeals, complaints, or even anger posted on rule-of-law-oriented media, showing sincerity to consumers. On the other hand, the opening of functional accounts will help consumers solve problems more quickly, improving their satisfaction and loyalty. Meanwhile, the process of solving problems can help enterprises form a knowledge base, accumulating experience and reducing maintenance costs in the future. Other consumers can also find knowledge from this knowledge base to help other consumers in need, especially those who have received assistance and problem-solving from the system. For example, Dell Computers has set up 65 accounts on Twitter in the United States, allowing each account to perform different functions and summarize by region. Dell has invested nearly 100 people in its social media accounts in the United States and generated revenue exceeding one million US dollars, all thanks to their emphasis on new media.

4. Community. Enterprises have realized this value but haven't done well enough or invested enough. Many enterprises have built similar communities, but they haven't operated them well. From the perspective of shaping, community construction must be a completely closed community. However, many enterprises have doubts about this, worrying about being attacked by consumers or internet trolls spreading all sorts of rumors to disrupt. Professor Sun Baohong of Cheung Kong Graduate School of Business said: "It is precisely because of the non-closure of enterprise communities that messages cannot collaborate, and false information drowns or hides real information." Professor Sun Baohong also mentioned: "I have observed that once someone maliciously attacks or fabricates false information, others will rise up to condemn it, and over time, genuine information is filtered out. Therefore, if enterprises do not provide a channel for venting opinions about their products and services, they will find their own channels. For example, Dave Carroll, a guitarist of the Maxwell band, who was traveling on United Airlines, witnessed his guitar being mishandled like ammunition during baggage handling at Chicago O'Hare International Airport and was refused compensation. He then created the MV 'United Breaks Guitars' and uploaded it to YouTube. Within ten days, it had over 4 million views, causing a drop in United Airlines' stock price." This should raise our concern. Of course, there are also companies that have done well, such as JetBlue Airways in the United States, which uses traditional scanning machines to track airline customers. Once they detect any complaints or praise from customers, their system responds immediately and replies accordingly.

5. Full participation in shaping. Apply rule-of-law-oriented media to internal communication and knowledge application, and increase training for employees so that everyone can conduct positive corporate communications through new media, enhancing the media literacy of employees. At the same time, improve the production line of various aspects of new media and provide better services to consumers through systematic operation.
