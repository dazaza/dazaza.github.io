Reviews on the Qingcheng Mountain and Dujiangyan Scenic Area Website

by jdwo on 2007-03-28 16:30:53

To explore and study the informatization construction of domestic 5A-level tourist attractions, the domestic tourism informatization research blog - Zhi Travel Power initiated a 5A review. It considers from multiple angles such as search engines, site operations, planning, and user experience. This period invited SEO professionals, tourism informatization researchers, and the heads of the online marketing department of scenic spots to conduct it in the form of online chat dialogues. The entire chat process was attended by dozens of tourism site operators. The first phase's review subject is the Dujiangyan Qingcheng Mountain Scenic Area, with the reviewed sites including www.qcs.cn and www.djyjg.com. The guests for this phase's review are Stone, Robin, Xiao Tian, and Da Zui. Below is a summary of the comments from the four guests in this 5A review.

SEO: The description language on the page should be consistent with user search habits.

Below is a summary of Stone's comments regarding SEO:

Through a simple understanding, the two sites given earlier about Qingcheng Mountain and the Dujiangyan site are implemented using the same set of programs and technology. Therefore, overall, most structural issues existing among several sites are the same. However, when it comes to specific problems, there are still many issues concerning search engines.

1. Domain redirection issues: Why does entering a domain always result in an immediate redirect? For example, entering www.qcs.cn redirects to: www.qcs.cn/qceip/. This issue exists across several sites.

2. Basic static processing has not been done well. Nowadays, good sites mostly implement static pages, which are more beneficial for search engines, enhance access efficiency, and reduce server consumption. These famous tourism sites we often visit, like 17u.com and 51766.com, serve as examples. Static page judgment is simple; Google says that URLs without a "?" can be considered static.

"Http://www.qcs.cn/qceip/show.jsp?type=news&id=402881e310c390020110d8d70a69000b" type of URL is disliked by both people and machines.

3. The third issue is that the webpage code is too lengthy. That long section of CSS and JavaScript code before the can all be moved to external files and then called upon. This way, it only takes a few lines to write clearly.

4. Additionally, images are used quite frequently. In cases where there are many images, image search needs to be considered. All alt attributes that should be added to images must be included.

(These) are some simple examples mentioned above. Below, I will discuss other issues from the perspective of search engines.

a. Some internet users' search demands have not been considered. For example, through Baidu, I know that many people actually pay attention to "Qingcheng Mountain Bai Suzhen", "Back Mountain of Qingcheng", and "Villas at Qingcheng Mountain". However, the corresponding related sites do not have content about these aspects. Personally, I think a legend about Bai Suzhen under Qingcheng Mountain could be introduced on one page. Many people from other places may not know Qingcheng Mountain, but they know about the White Snake story, which would yield good results.

b. Another point I need to mention is that the content description language on the page should be consistent with the search habits of users. For instance, we use "illustrative diagram" and "ticketing" on our site. However, the terms tourists search for most frequently are map and tickets. Therefore, many places (should) replace illustrative diagrams with tickets and change ticket details to tickets. Only then will it conform to the search habits of search users. Otherwise, how can someone searching for "Dujiangyan Map" find it?

User Experience: Place the information blocks that users care about most in important positions on the webpage.

Below is a summary of Robin's comments regarding user experience:

Hello everyone, next I (Robin) will talk to you all about the user experience issues of the two websites.

Firstly, having the main domain redirected to a website directory, and also having non-uniform URLs such as "http://211.95.165.211/pp/index.htm", will dilute users' brand impression of the main domain of the website. At the initial stage of website planning, centralized planning must be carried out, placing all information page addresses under the main domain or a second-level domain. An IP address should never be used again.

Secondly: Extremely slow website access speed. Excessive use of images and animations, along with possibly using dynamic programs, so data must be retrieved from the database. Generally speaking, if a webpage hasn't loaded within 8 seconds, users will close the page.

Thirdly: The website interface design is overly flashy, giving a dizzy feeling. This is the third problem. In terms of webpage color schemes, the entire site must be unified to create color recognition. The main tone should not exceed three colors.

Fourthly: Talking about webpage layout module arrangement issues. There should ideally be a sense of hierarchy.

a. We should not plan the information modules of the webpage based on our own ideas. The correct approach is to place the information blocks that users are most concerned about in important positions on the webpage. Currently, both websites mainly place the announcements and policies of the scenic area in prominent positions at the top.

b. Page error prompts, lack of rigorous code writing. The IE browser status bar at the bottom of this website (http://www.qcs517.com/qceip/) shows an error prompt. This will make users feel that the website operator works irresponsibly, thus greatly reducing users' trust in the website.

c. Do not use too many scrolling effects on the webpage. If necessary, it is suggested to use one scrolling effect.

d. Provide hints for related articles to increase user visit stickiness and improve PV. Information is overly independent, lacking related hints. (Some pages) have ranking reading recommendations, but the recommended content is not strongly related to the specific news.

The above is a preliminary analysis. Due to time constraints, more detailed content will be discussed privately. Everyone is welcome to offer guidance and criticism.

Website Content: Scenic area introduction should be the focus, content can be more detailed

Below is a summary of Da Zui's comments regarding the website content:

Next, I will exchange views with you two on the website content.

1. The introduction of the scenic area should be the focus. This is what tourists truly care about, and other functions and help are built on this basis. But ROBIN already said, the first screen focuses on policy-related content? This is very disappointing. Tourists don't care about them at all, and it might even cause resentment.

2. Regarding self-guided tours, I think this is a trend. A scenic area website is not a travel agency website, and the information provided must be accurate and timely, which is the advantage of the official website. If we revolve around the six elements of food, accommodation, transportation, sightseeing, shopping, and entertainment, how much content is there? To what extent can these contents be detailed? For example, "Gourmet Guide" (http://www.qcs517.com/qceip/lyfw/mtxcindex.jsp), where are the prices? Where are the recommended restaurants? Many traffic routes are presented in text, without maps. Which is more intuitive for them to see, text or maps?

3. Besides, on the homepage, there is a reservation column, but this reservation seems insignificant. I think it's just to meet the requirements of the 5A evaluation. Apart from the scenic area, there should be activities.

4. I think Dujiangyan's next version content is good. http://www.djyjq.com/fsjeip/

This is what tourists like to see, which is interaction with tourists.

5. There is another issue. Both scenic areas have opened forums, but is this forum necessary? It's troublesome! You have to register! Maybe visitors just want to ask small questions. Can we consider a structure that makes it easier for visitors to leave messages? I think the BLOG format is nice.

If there are no issues, please let Xiao Tian comment on the foreign language version afterward.

Foreign Language Version: What may attract foreigners is not just the beautiful scenery, but also Taoist culture

Below is a summary of Xiao Tian's comments on the foreign language version:

In the content of the official website, everyone can take a look at the site of Yellowstone National Park in the United States.

http://fanyi.cn.yahoo.com/translate_url?fr=bf-maxthon&lp=en_zh&trurl=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.nps.gov%2Fyell%

http://www.nps.gov/yell/

The two sites discussed today are the official sites of the scenic areas. As official sites, they must provide absolutely useful official information to visitors. For example, the ticket price on May 1st, what other websites say doesn't count, only what the official website says counts. Additionally, official websites should appropriately avoid excessive commercialization and focus on exploring the culture and history of the scenic area.

Let me share some thoughts on foreign language sites.

1. Foreigners are already accustomed to deciding their travel destinations and arranging routes through online methods. It can be said that analyzing the psychology of European and American tourists is not only beneficial for grasping inbound tourists but also helpful for understanding the increasingly developing domestic self-guided tourists.

2. Taking Qingcheng Mountain as an example, obviously, few foreigners know about Qingcheng Mountain. What attracts them most may not be the beautiful scenery, but Taoist culture. So the English site of Qingcheng Mountain can take Taoist culture as the main line: Dujiangyan is a world-renowned water conservancy project. Shouldn't considerations be made for architecture or hydraulics? Many foreigners are very interested in this and look for related ruins everywhere, let alone those that are still well-preserved and fully functional. Words like ancient hydraulic facilities should be easy to rank high.

3. Through Yahoo English search “Sichuan Travel”, the first result is a very powerful Chinese site introducing Sichuan tourism to foreigners. It mentions "Qingcheng Mountain" and "Dujiangyan", discussing Taoism and hydraulics. The official site of the scenic area should have sufficient materials in this aspect and reflect them through the Internet.

4. Let me talk about some details below.

a. Different language sites all have contact numbers, but I found that they are all the same number. Could it be that the scenic area has someone proficient in multiple languages?

b. On the English site of Online Travel, the pictures that come up can only show Chinese clearly. Travel Service should be very important information, but it only introduces a few famous Sichuan dishes. Such travel service information is difficult to guide inbound tourists.

c. According to my understanding, many foreigners are accustomed to directly consulting the scenic area by phone or email. The phone and email on the website should have corresponding processes and require dedicated personnel to handle.
