Is eczema contagious?

by shenqiang0 on 2012-02-23 09:01:57

Is eczema contagious? Many people believe that eczema is contagious, but in fact, it is not. Experts from Shanxi Huimin Hospital stated: The idea that eczema is contagious is a common misconception. In reality, eczema is an allergic skin condition and does not have any contagious properties. However, eczema often has genetic factors involved; if ancestors had eczema, the likelihood of parents or offspring having eczema is generally much higher.

Eczema is not contagious. Eczema is a common type of hypersensitive, allergic dermatitis that frequently occurs on the face, behind the ears, hands and feet, breasts, and scrotum. The affected areas may exude inflammatory fluid and are accompanied by intense itching symptoms, which tend to recur easily.

Clinical manifestations of eczema:

① The clinical symptoms of eczema vary widely. Based on the different skin lesions during the course of the disease, it can be divided into acute, subacute, and chronic types.

② Acute eczema presents with polymorphic damage, initially appearing as red spots with sensations of burning and itching.

③ After some time or following treatment, the acute inflammation subsides, the skin lesions dry out, crust over, and develop scales, entering the subacute phase.

④ Chronic eczema develops from repeated episodes of acute and subacute conditions that do not heal, or it may present as chronic inflammation from the start. It manifests as thickened skin, deepened skin lines, infiltration, and pigmentation. The main symptom is intense itching.
