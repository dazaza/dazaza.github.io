The Programmer's Journey: From Rookie Coder to Software Engineering Master

by anonymous on 2013-09-24 20:39:52

"This is a career that relies on youth; if one does not achieve success before the age of thirty, with the decline in memory and learning ability, they will inevitably be eliminated from this industry." This statement has been hailed as a true reflection of the real life of programmers in the online gaming category.

Most people's work experience begins in their early twenties, and to achieve a certain level of success by the age of thirty, there are about ten years during which having a clear plan or feeling lost can make a difference. In my opinion, new programmers might benefit from answering the following three questions first, as reflecting on these answers could help them choose their future career paths.

**Question 1:** Do you enjoy programming? Do you like this job?

This is the first question you should ask yourself: A programmer who finds joy in their daily work and feels immense satisfaction upon completing a project will have a vastly different professional life compared to someone who sees themselves as just a "code farmer," going to work every day merely to pass the time and earn a fixed salary. These two types of individuals will have completely different attitudes towards work, and their career opportunities and futures will also differ accordingly. Interest is always the best teacher. Before entering this field and becoming a programmer, everyone should consider this question: How deep is your desire for programming?

**Question 2:** Have you chosen the right company? How do you select a company?

Not all companies aggressively hiring programmers are suitable for you. This is a problem every programmer must recognize when entering the workforce or changing jobs. Currently, domestic online gaming companies are mainly divided into two types: R&D-driven enterprises and market-driven enterprises. Joining different types of companies not only directly determines your future career path but also affects your mindset and attitude toward your work.

Besides interest, making a career choice should also be based on self-positioning: If you believe you excel in interpersonal communication, have strong logical planning and execution skills, and have unique insights into the overall development of the gaming industry, then companies like Shanda or Sohu Changyou, which focus on game distribution and operation, may be recommended. However, if you are familiar with various types of domestic and international games and have reached a "masterful" level in programming skills, then companies like Duoyi, NetEase, or Tencent, which specialize in R&D, would be more suitable choices.

**Question 3:** Titles or skill enhancement? Which do you prioritize?

Here, I want to tell every person about to face a new interview: When starting your career, what truly matters is how the company will enhance your technical abilities, not the titles or positions promised to you. Therefore, if you're looking for a new job, find companies that focus on employee capability and technical training, with healthy and active team cultures. Don't overly concern yourself with the title they offer; instead, focus on whether you can grow most rapidly within the shortest possible time. The road ahead is long; don't expect to achieve everything at once.

As summarized by me: Many internet companies specializing in online gaming, such as Duoyi Network, which focuses on online games and internet products, have an average employee age of only 26. However, almost every research and development and operations staff member demonstrates a higher level of working ability than their peers. If the company you choose, like Duoyi Network, is itself in a stage of rapid upward development, then employees growing on this platform will naturally develop faster than those in other industries or companies.

From entering the workplace to becoming a successful person in your career, there are bound to be many ups and downs. If you've already decided to excel in this field and dream of transforming from a novice coder into a software engineering master, you can start by answering the above three questions, considering aspects such as personal interest, the chosen platform, and the cultivation of capabilities. Sharpening the axe doesn’t delay chopping wood; with thorough planning and persistent courage, you can ensure smoother progress along the path of a programmer’s career.
