Beijing's high temperature persists, low carbon is imperative

by ditan360 on 2010-07-07 11:40:26

Exclusive report by Low Carbon Network. Yesterday, the sun was blazing hot, and the clouds seemed to have melted away under the scorching sun, disappearing without a trace. Walking on the road, the wind felt like waves of heat rushing towards me, making it hard to breathe. The soles of my shoes felt like they were melting. Someone even cracked an egg onto the manhole cover on the road surface, and after three minutes, the egg was actually cooked! On the bus, I was so hot that I wanted to peel off my skin and let my bones breathe. Turning around, I saw the young men and women next to me all drenched in sweat. The annoying peak-hour traffic in Beijing caused one hour of gridlock. I have always been deeply impressed by the public service advertisement "handsome men and beautiful women green travel" from some time ago. Now, looking at the dense line of cars outside the car window, I can't help but sigh that Beijing is such a high-carbon city. If everyone gave up driving and chose public transportation, perhaps this crowded traffic and sweltering weather would be alleviated.

It seems that the work of energy conservation and emissions reduction is indeed imperative.

The road temperature was astonishing

According to monitoring, yesterday during the day, except for Yanqing area, the highest temperatures in other areas of Beijing generally exceeded 40°C, with the highest being 43.8°C. This was the first time since 1951 that such high temperatures occurred in the first ten days of July in Beijing. Moreover, the high-temperature trend remains unchanged today. Meteorological experts said that the urban area temperature in Beijing will still reach 40°C today. "Under the high-temperature baking, the ground temperature in our city has continued to set new records," said an expert from the Meteorological Bureau. According to the ground temperature measurement, whether in the urban or suburban areas, the ground temperature exceeded 60°C throughout the city, with Shijingshan area being the highest at 68.3°C.

The meteorological bureau issued an orange warning (image source: Low Carbon Network)

High temperature prone to induce disease

From July 3 to yesterday, 120 and 999 emergency services received a total of 59 cases of heatstroke, reaching the total of the entire previous month. The usage of water and electricity also reached a historical high. In addition, the high temperature increased the number of cardiovascular patients by 10-20%. Dr. Luo Ping, deputy director of the emergency center at Anzhen Hospital, introduced that the number of cardiovascular patients had increased by 10% to 20% in recent days. Cardiovascular patients originally have arrhythmia, and their blood pressure tends to fluctuate easily. When the temperature is too high, people tend to become irritable, increasing their heart rate, which often exacerbates the condition of cardiovascular patients.

A man at Beijing Station suspected of dying suddenly due to high temperature

At noon yesterday, the square in front of Beijing Station was white-hot, and you could clearly feel the heat rising from underfoot. Many passengers held umbrellas to shield themselves from the sun, walking quickly towards the waiting hall or ticket office. Many passengers complained about the heat, saying, "Walking in the sun for five minutes, the umbrella handle became scorching." Around 12 o'clock, in front of the No. 62 ticket window at the north side of the square, a man suddenly collapsed. Despite the efforts of multiple people to help him, the man ultimately died after unsuccessful resuscitation. An informed person introduced that the man's surname was Wu, from Zhangjiakou, Hebei Province. The man brought his mother and child to the train station to buy tickets, originally intending to see his mother off, but unexpectedly such an accident happened. Since the man had no known medical history, doctors from 120 suspected that the man might have died suddenly due to high temperature.

Bus self-ignites in high temperature

Around 8:30 am on July 6, 2010, a bus caught fire about 50 meters south of Huawe Bridge, fortunately without any casualties. A witness surnamed Li said that at 8:30, when he arrived at Huawe Bridge South, he found a bus burning near a bus stop in front of the United International Plaza. The combustion may have been caused by the high temperature of the engine, possibly related to the continuous high temperature.

How many degrees can cause a holiday?

Unable to endure the heat, some netizens frequently searched online and asked: how many degrees above can cause a holiday? However, there is no basis for the answer.

A national-level regulation, the "Interim Measures for Heatstroke Prevention and Cooling", has been "temporarily implemented" since 1960, enduring 50 years of changes, and is now on its last legs. Even so, it only applies to industries, transportation, construction sites, small factories and mines, and field operations, unable to reach the vast modern industrial system and service sector. Allowing more people to enjoy legal heatstroke prevention benefits in extremely hot summers is the progress we look forward to.

Beijing's "heat island effect"

Beijing has a flat terrain, low altitude, far from the ocean, under the control of continental high pressure, with sinking airflow, clear or partly cloudy weather facilitates the absorption of solar radiation. The prolonged high temperature weather has accumulated a certain amount of heat, leading to soaring temperatures. Additionally, the dense building clusters in the city, asphalt roads, and concrete surfaces have greater heat capacity and heat absorption rates compared to soil and vegetation, storing more heat in the urban area, causing rapid temperature rise under the intense sunlight.

Who is the culprit

During the Copenhagen Conference not long ago, the World Meteorological Organization reported that the past ten years were the hottest ten years on record. Accompanying the arguments at the Copenhagen World Climate Conference, the public's attention to global warming continued to "rise". The recent prolonged extreme heat naturally further proves that this is the result of climate change and the greenhouse effect.

Carbon dioxide gas has the ability to absorb and retain heat. Its increase in the atmosphere results in forming an invisible glass dome, preventing the heat radiated by the sun from dissipating into outer space, reflecting infrared rays, thereby causing the Earth's surface to warm up. Therefore, carbon dioxide is also called a greenhouse gas, as it is the main culprit behind rising temperatures. Global warming, glacier melting, rising sea levels, land submersion, rampant hurricanes, overflowing floods—many disasters faced by humanity are caused by excessive emissions of greenhouse gases like carbon dioxide.

The main reason for excessive greenhouse gas emissions is the large-scale use of fossil carbon fuels in human production and life.

Beijing has strong winds, little vegetation, insufficient greening, lack of water, the increase and overuse of high-power electrical appliances such as air conditioners and private vehicles consume a large amount of carbon fuel and emit excessive amounts of carbon dioxide. When the sun shines directly and the sun's activity is intense, the resulting geoelectric current warms the crust, combined with the urban heat island effect, leading to rising temperatures, making people feel extremely uncomfortable. In such an extremely uncomfortable environment, people excessively use high-energy-consuming cooling methods like air conditioning, causing the temperature to rise even higher, creating a vicious cycle.

Adjusting the use of carbon fuels and controlling carbon emissions appropriately is the solution most needed to be implemented now.
