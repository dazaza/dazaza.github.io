Is love sweet or bitter?

Not every day do we wake up in a good mood.

Lying in bed, staring at the ceiling, An Lu counted on her fingers and muttered to herself. It had been a week since Cao Wei had last contacted her. Thinking about this, An Lu felt a little disheartened. She sighed and closed her eyes. The books say that women should learn to be reserved, for this is what makes them interesting. Whenever An Lu read books, she would think, "I am such a woman; if I'm not now, I surely can become one."

The summer vacation had already begun nearly half a month ago. A week before, An Lu and Cao Wei went shopping. An Lu didn't like going to malls, finding branded clothes both expensive and ordinary. Instead, she preferred small roadside shops where she could slowly search for treasures.

As they were leaving home, Cao Wei said, "We're not short of money, you should buy some decent clothes, it can't always be like this."

"What? Does that mean what I wear now isn't decent enough? Seriously, but I like it!" An Lu pouted, "I want to wear something different everyday, I like cheap things. I'm not an aristocratic woman anyway."

An Lu always thought Cao Wei was becoming more and more critical lately. The singers she liked very much he found too vulgar; the food she loved eating he considered junk food; she liked staying up late surfing the net, while he believed early to bed and early to rise was healthy. "Why must everything oppose me? Does he not like me?" An Lu sometimes asked herself. But upon further reflection, she thought it was actually his concern for her.

In a small shop, An Lu spotted a long dress. Though Cao Wei didn't like it, she still tried it on. An Lu looked left and right in front of the mirror, unconsciously turning a few circles, thinking how beautiful it looked. The boss also praised her, saying An Lu looked lively and cute in it. "This style is very popular in Japan this year, your taste is really good. And with your sweet face, it suits you perfectly." An Lu smiled broadly, asking Cao Wei: "Say something, does it look good?"

Cao Wei was playing with his new phone, only looking up when An Lu asked him, then said: "It's so long it will drag on the ground, doesn't it make you look old?"

An Lu turned around and looked again, hands on her hips, tilting her head and counter-asking through the mirror: "Does it make me look old? Do you even have any sense of fashion!"

At this point, the boss chimed in: "Oh no, how could a young girl wearing it look old? And the color is so pink, it looks very youthful."

"Anyway, I don't like it, you decide," Cao Wei finished and continued to fiddle with his phone.

"Every time it's like this, always ruining my mood! No way, I must buy it." Saying this, An Lu angrily pushed open the dressing room door and changed back into her own clothes. She skillfully haggled with the boss and finally happily held the bag up to Cao Wei: "Hehe, am I not amazing, got it at half price!"

Cao Wei looked up, looked at An Lu, slowly stood up, and walked towards the door.

"Hey, wait for me!" An Lu reached out to grab Cao Wei's hand, "What's wrong with you? I really think it looks good, it's not like I'm asking you to wear it, why are you like this?"

"I've already said I don't like you wearing it like that."

"And haven't I said I like wearing it like that?"

"As you wish."

An Lu paused, lowering her head to ask, "Isn't it because you can't stop thinking about your ex-girlfriend?"

"You're talking nonsense."

"Am I wrong? She likes wearing tight clothes, super short skirts, right? But I'm not her!"

"There's absolutely no relation!"

"No relation? You're always criticizing me, always comparing me to her!"

"..."

"But even if there's comparison, shouldn't it be fair? Can you not always take her as the best standard? Can you switch to me once?" As she spoke, An Lu started crying.

"Don't say anymore, you're overthinking." Cao Wei tried to hug An Lu, but she took a step back and avoided him.

"Just think carefully, what exactly do you feel for me? If it's just because we've started and you're afraid to hurt me, then you should just tell me honestly." An Lu lowered her head, "When you've made up your mind, come and tell me." Saying this, she turned and ran away.

Many passers-by noticed this quarreling young couple. Such scenes, whether on the screen or in novels, are quite common, so after glancing once, people stopped paying attention.

An Lu ran a few steps, but kept listening for footsteps chasing her. "Bad guy, why aren't you chasing me?" An Lu scolded in her heart. Thus, she deliberately slowed down.

"I should know that I hope he catches up, right?" An Lu thought. However, after walking a few more steps, Cao Wei still didn't chase her. "Maybe I should give him a way out," An Lu thought, and stopped.

Cao Wei still didn't chase her. An Lu stood among the crowd of passers-by. Suddenly, a picture flashed in her mind. A tall handsome man standing several meters away, motionlessly gazing at her. An Lu softened her heart and slowly turned her head.

The picture shattered. Cao Wei had already walked far away, leaving behind a swaying back figure.

"So annoying! Too unmanly!" An Lu pouted her lips and stomped her foot. Was it because she disliked Cao Wei's lack of masculinity, or because she disliked Cao Wei not giving her face? An Lu couldn't tell.

That day, Cao Wei did not contact An Lu. Holding her phone, An Lu secretly vowed: "Hmph, let's see who has more patience." Although she thought this, An Lu still constantly checked QQ and emails, fearing not being the first to get Cao Wei's news.

The next day, her good friend Lan Xiaomin called to invite An Lu to her house to see the newly bought puppy.

Because their houses were relatively far apart, An Lu originally didn't want to go, but considering this could kill time, she wouldn't have to anxiously wait for the phone all day, so she agreed. Normally, she should take the subway, but An Lu waited by the gate of the residential area for the bus.

"The bus moves slower, covers more distance, maybe I'll meet him along the way," An Lu calculated.

Lan Xiaomin's newly bought puppy was a Teddy dog, coffee-colored curly hair, standing still it looked like a plush toy, especially cute, very popular among young people.

"Oh, so cute!" An Lu hugged the puppy lovingly, gently stroking it, telling Xiao Min, "Your mom is so nice, I envy you so much! My mom never allows me to keep a dog, she says I won't take care of it properly."

"Yeah, true. But indeed, after keeping it, I find it's really troublesome. Washing and brushing its hair can be sent to the store, but dogs are lonely animals, you need to play with them often."

"This is perfect, you have nothing to do anyway, it's mutual entertainment."

"It's tiring to always play with it, besides, after school starts and I return to school, it will be lonely."

"Why not get a kitten then? Whether you play with it or not should be fine, right?" After speaking, An Lu hummed SHE's "Persian Cat", "In a blink of an eye, it's gone."

For some reason, An Lu suddenly felt Cao Wei was somewhat similar to a cat.

After returning home in the evening, An Lu still opened the computer first thing to check if there was any message from Cao Wei, but the result was another disappointment.

"It's fine, this guy loves to act cool anyway, let's give him a few more days," An Lu thought.

Speaking of which, they had been together for almost ten months. Cao Wei and An Lu studied different majors at the same university. Cao Wei was in the math department, An Lu in the electronics department. In their sophomore year, many classmates had already started preparing for the national mathematical modeling competition. Cao Wei was an average student in his class, although he didn't care much about such competitions, he was still pulled by classmates to form a small team. An Lu was the vice-year leader of the electronics department, academically excellent, plus her inherent interest in various competitions, so she also gathered a few boys to sign up for the competition. Thus, these two originally unrelated individuals gradually became familiar with each other.

Among the students participating in the modeling competition, most were boys, and very few girls could endure such hardship. Staying up continuously in the lab for several days without rest was physically exhausting. An Lu herself was already quite pretty, and now being in this male-dominated group, she stood out even more, drawing much attention. Gradually, someone began calling her the "Flower of the Electronics Department," and others actively approached her to chat and ask for her phone number.

An Lu wasn't the shy type, but at such moments, she would still blush, despite her inner joy, she would refuse verbally with "Sorry, I'm not used to this."

Cao Wei wasn't particularly handsome, but due to his tall stature, he naturally scored some points in appearance, making him a rather memorable face.

That day, it was drizzling. An Lu didn't bring an umbrella, running towards the dormitory with her head down. Passing by the supermarket, she bumped into Cao Wei. Cao Wei was just coming out of the supermarket, holding a can of cola. Since he had just opened it, when An Lu bumped into him, his hand trembled, spilling the cola. An Lu was short, reaching roughly Cao Wei's shoulder. When she collided with Cao Wei's left arm, he quickly raised his hand, his left arm holding the cola can circled in the air and landed behind An Lu. At this moment, their posture appeared to bystanders as if a boy was hugging a girl, but neither of them realized it.

"Oops, sorry, I was running too fast, didn't notice," An Lu looked up at Cao Wei, water droplets hanging on her eyelashes. Due to the rain, An Lu blinked continuously, and thus her eyelashes flickered up and down in front of Cao Wei with droplets.

An Lu's eyelashes weren't thick or curled upwards, but they were indeed long. Cao Wei looked down at her, thinking how beautiful she was. "Oh, it's okay," Cao Wei stammered a bit, but after a few seconds, continued, "You're the 'Flower of the Electronics Department', right? Haha!"

At this moment, An Lu also recognized the boy as a classmate from the math department. After apologizing for bumping into him and hearing such words, An Lu was momentarily stunned, suddenly feeling even more embarrassed. She hurriedly lowered her head, bowing slightly and said, "No, sorry, really sorry, I was in a hurry."

"That's fine, don't apologize anymore."

"Uh... ..."

"You keep saying 'uh...', it's still raining, you'd better go back."

At this moment, a few of Cao Wei's friends came out of the supermarket after finishing their purchases, seeing this scene, they all made sounds like "Oh", "Eh", "Hmm".

An Lu quickly looked up, said "Bye" and hurriedly left, without even glancing at Cao Wei again.

Cao Wei's friends approached, patting him on the shoulder, teasing him with "Not bad" and "You're something, huh."

"Well, she just bumped into me when she was running, stop teasing me," Cao Wei pushed his buddies aside, laughing.

"We don't know if there's anything going on, but Cao Wei, you're a sophomore now, you should find a girlfriend. Don't always think about your 'ex'. That woman is so promiscuous, she probably dated several men after breaking up with you, you should forget her quickly," Wan He put his hand on Cao Wei's shoulder, looking serious. Wan He and Cao Wei had been close friends since junior high school, and until now, they were classmates, they could talk about anything.

Seeing Cao Wei not responding, Wan He continued, "Do you think that girl might fancy you? Look, she blushed when she spoke. Hmm? You can consider it, buddy, we support you."

"Thank you, thank you so much. But you should worry about yourself more, Mr. Wan!" Cao Wei elbowed Wan He's chest, showing an aggressive demeanor.
