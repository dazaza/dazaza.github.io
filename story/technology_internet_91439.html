Chinese domain names are popular, Google supports .CN synchronously without obstacles.

by now on 2008-09-12 13:58:10

Recently, a set of photos named "the most powerful tricycle in history" has become popular on major forums. The main subject of the photos is a two-wheeled bicycle with an iCooperClubman trunk attached to the rear. This tricycle was indeed produced by BMW and is the only one of its kind in the world. Soon after, domains such as "BMW Tricycle.CN" and "Luxury Tricycle.中国" appeared, attracting attention as soon as they were introduced, and similar domains also experienced a rush registration phenomenon.

The reputation of Chinese language domains on the internet continues to rise. Industry experts predict that this trend will persist. The latest news indicates that Google yesterday launched its "Google Chrome" browser globally in over 100 countries, providing a Chinese version right away and fully supporting ".cn" Chinese language domains without any obstacles. A person in charge of Times Network told us: "This phenomenon reflects the wave of native language internet access that has been sweeping across the world. The enormous appeal of the Chinese market has become one of the important factors influencing global internet giants' development courses to promote the use of '.中国' for internet access."

Experts point out: "China currently has the world's largest number of internet users, but the internet penetration rate is only 19.1%, lower than the global average of 21.1%. For the 1 billion people who have not yet accessed the internet, it is necessary to remove barriers to internet access caused by language differences. This is also why browsers that account for more than 99% of the market, such as Microsoft, OPERA, Firefox, SAFARI, etc., all support Chinese CN domains."

Therefore, domain registrars are also coming up with strategies for Chinese language domains, launching opportunities to snatch up these "hot buns." On the website of the top-tier domain registrar Times Network (www.now.cn), we see a variety of Chinese language domains available. Registering a Chinese .CN domain can give you both simplified and traditional versions, along with an additional 100M of space. "To let more people understand Chinese language domains, such promotions are essential," said relevant personnel.

In response to the wide application of Chinese language domains, the state has issued and improved regulations such as the "Administrative Measures for Domain Names of China Internet Network" to regulate domain name registration. Names of government institutions, geographical names, etc., are legally protected under special laws. For commercial brands, the China Internet Network Information Center will also provide certain protection for some well-known enterprises and organizations.
