A Chrome browser plugin that synchronously posts status updates to Google+ and Facebook (with G+ invite)

by xdash on 2011-08-21 12:43:57

Google's latest social service, Google+, which was recently launched with much fanfare, is said to have already reached 27 million users, a very promising start. However, some industry observers believe that "there are no shortcuts to building a community." Lacking sufficient practical innovation and humanistic care, the overnight success of Google+ may turn out to be "all bark and no bite," starting strong but not necessarily ending well.

At least for me, I haven't yet fully immersed myself in using it as a product, but rather more so for the experience of something new and staying connected with my geek friends. In the absence of enough female users and celebrity users, the first wave of users and those who stayed longer were mostly Google's tech-savvy fans. Perhaps as mentioned by a high-ranking official at Google in public statements, "the worst-case scenario is that Google+ becomes a geek community." Hopefully, this won't come true.

Returning to the main topic, let me introduce today's Google Chrome extension —— Google+ & Facebook Status Sync (seriously, developers, couldn't you have come up with a shorter and catchier name...).

This plugin can achieve two-way synchronization of status updates across four major social networks: Google+, Facebook, Twitter, and Plurk. After installation and binding, when you post a status update on any of these four platforms, you can check the boxes below the input field for other platforms to synchronize your posts (this feature of manually selecting sync targets is excellent, as choosing appropriate channels for suitable opinions is responsible both to oneself and readers). See the image below:

Some points to note:

After installation, there might be conflicts with some already installed plugins, leading to crashes. It took me two attempts to successfully install it.

After installation, by default, it will post an ad-like message such as "I am using XXX" to your G+ status. You can either delete it manually or keep it as a recommendation to your friends.

Supports designated circles in Google+. Does not support Facebook's Fans Pages.
