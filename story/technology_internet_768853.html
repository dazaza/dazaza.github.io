How to become a WeChat partner that attracts attention? Emphasize user usage scenarios.

by anonymous on 2013-11-25 11:35:22

According to the latest data from the "WeChat·Official Accounts" Partner Conference, the number of WeChat Official Accounts has now exceeded 2 million. In order to stand out among so many partners and gain both user approval and attention from WeChat officials, it is extremely important to prioritize user usage scenarios.

At today's "WeChat·Official Accounts" Partner Conference, Dianping, Meilan Airport, and Haier were interviewed by the media as representative partners.

When discussing how to become a representative partner on the WeChat platform, Jiang Yaping, Vice President of Products and Operations at Dianping, stated that with the opening of nine advanced interfaces, the resources developers need have gradually been met. However, if one wants to explore some more standout values, the prerequisite is to value user usage scenarios, which is also an aspect that the WeChat official team highly prioritizes.

"If you can tell a very good story through your collaboration with WeChat, especially when your story is not particularly abundant among other partners, it will be easier to gain support from their team."

Jiang Yaping’s suggestion about "valuing user usage scenarios" was also mentioned in some guest speeches at this conference. Taking mobile shopping as an example, some shopping needs are often triggered by various scenarios, such as seeing a friend talk about a great book in your Moments (WeChat's social feed), which might make you want to buy the book—this is a shopping demand based on a scenario.

In the future, users may be reminded by purchasing scenes anytime and anywhere. This entry point will become increasingly decentralized, integrating into various life scenarios—it could be triggered by a picture shared by a friend or being attracted by something else while consuming something.

Due to the characteristic of smartphones being constantly by our side, they can serve users in numerous scenarios. If one wants to uncover value on the WeChat platform and leverage the advantage of WeChat's large-scale active user base, partners need to think more about how to discover unmet user scenario demands.

During the interview, Deng Chao, the information technology director of Meilan Airport, introduced that the reason why their official account can provide the first domestic "WeChat check-in, one-document clearance" service is closely related to the characteristics of their airport and user demographics. Haikou is a tourist city, and a large proportion of the airport's services cater to tourists. Their age, occupation, and the scale of the airport itself play a significant role in enabling this service. If inland airports want to adopt this model, it might not be suitable.

This indicates that developers on the WeChat platform often need to consider the ways of providing services from the perspective of their own users and resources, uncovering features suitable for themselves, thus making it easier to become a service with unique value.

Xiong Gengchao, General Manager of Beijing Innovation Center for Haier Smart Home, mentioned when explaining why they chose WeChat as a partner that WeChat has a philosophy of being online 24 hours a day, which aligns with the value of home appliance services because users' needs for using home appliances are anytime and anywhere. Therefore, utilizing WeChat to achieve smart control over home appliances has a good correlation.

Regarding the value generated from collaborating with WeChat, Jiang Yaping introduced that Dianping and WeChat Pay once held an event called "Get ten yuan back for using WeChat Pay." During this event, WeChat Pay increased the total transaction amount of the event by seven times. Once users successfully bind a card via WeChat Pay, the payment success rate is very high, far exceeding other quick payment methods. Moreover, the reuse rate is significantly higher than other channels. These aspects are what satisfy the partners.
