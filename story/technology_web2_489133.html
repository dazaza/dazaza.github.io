One person's sky is very blue.

by blmhaihaig on 2011-09-14 14:17:59

(Old Matters Touching the Heart)

The sky of a person alone is very blue, so blue that it feels a bit melancholic. When alone, one feels very free, so free that it's tinged with loneliness; when alone, one feels light-hearted, so light-hearted that it's slightly boring. Missing friends brings happiness, yet this happiness can carry a tinge of sadness.

Life is like a dream, time shows no mercy, and suddenly looking back, one realizes that living is also a kind of mood. Whether poor or rich, whether gained or lost, everything is fleeting as clouds and mist. Reflecting on yesterday, today, and tomorrow, any day in which one can awaken is a good day. Regardless of family ties, friendship, or romance, what truly matters is cherishing them for eternity. What was once possessed should not be forgotten; what has been gained must be treasured; what has been lost can be recalled fondly. If you desire something, you must strive for it. There will always be windy mornings, sunny afternoons, magnificent evenings, and starry nights.

I like having simple desires in life, a kind of tranquility and slow growth. I love the colors washed by time, the unsung songs, and being lifelong friends with all those who know me. I don't know how long a lifetime lasts, nor can anyone measure fate. The twists and turns of the road are not important. Even if we cannot walk together to the ends of the earth, it doesn't matter. I will cherish every moment spent with you as friends.

I enjoy life filled with pure aspirations, favoring relationships that last through the ages. I value the feeling of being friends with people I know. Friendship does not lie in quantity but in the sincerity between each other. Therefore, I cherish every person I call a friend and everyone I've met because they are places where my wandering heart can rest.

Life itself is a wandering journey. Meeting anyone is a beautiful encounter. We have the fortune to know and gather together, which is an opportunity. To become friends is a bond. Let us continue to stand side by side, cherish this bond, and this friendship!

Many things in life, the more you want them, the further they may seem. Thus, one should not overly demand anything.

In this world, nothing goes perfectly smoothly. There will always be troubles and worries. When unfavorable events often surround us, how should we face them? By going with the flow, and thus letting go of our troubles. Going with the flow is a form of progress, a wise action of the intelligent, and an excuse for the foolish. What is "going with the flow"? It is not following blindly but allowing things to happen naturally without resentment, impatience, excess, or forcefulness. It is not being casual but seizing opportunities without pessimism, rigidity, confusion, or losing oneself. Going with the flow is a form of optimism, elegance, maturity in life, and a refined understanding of human nature.

What is "bond"? All things in the world have the possibility of meeting, accompanying, and enjoying each other. Possibility means bond, impossibility means no bond. Bonds exist everywhere and at all times. You, I, and he are all within the web of bonds. As the saying goes, "A thousand miles away, people come together due to their bond." And "Even facing each other, strangers remain unknown due to lack of bond." Even across vast distances, strangers smiling at each other signifies a bond. Some may admire each other for a long time but never meet. Bonds gather and disperse, beginning well and ending gracefully. Some lament, "There is no banquet in the world that does not eventually disband." Since it must disperse, why gather? A bond exists, it is a process.

If there is a bond, stay; if not, leave. Let the breeze send the white clouds away. Life has its pursuits, and obtaining what one seeks brings joy; failing to obtain it brings no worry. If such is the case, where would there be any烦恼 (troubles) in life? Happiness and sorrow follow the flow, gain and loss follow the flow. Cultivating with a worldly attitude and sowing seeds with a detached attitude is the highest realm of a life following the flow.

Going with the flow is often misunderstood by some as needing no action, leading others to use it as an excuse to avoid problems and difficulties. However, going with the flow is not giving up on pursuing but embracing life with an open mind. It is a wisdom that allows one to maintain calmness and clarity even in heated environments. It is a cultivation, a deep understanding of life’s vicissitudes, and a profound realization of life's essence. Going with the flow does not mean lacking principles or standards, nor does it mean being careless. Bonds require many conditions to form. Following causes and conditions without violating truth is called going with the flow.

In life, people often wonder: Why do some dislike me? Why do some not understand me? Why is it like this? From the perspective of going with the flow, liking or disliking requires no reason, understanding or misunderstanding requires no reason. Bonds are bonds, requiring no reason.

In this vast world filled with countless beings, it could be said that every event has its bond—such as joyous bonds, blessed bonds, human connections, wealth bonds, opportunities, good bonds, and bad bonds. Everything follows the flow, happening naturally. This is not only the attitude of Zen practitioners but also a spirit needed for a happy life. Going with the flow is a gentle way of living, a Zen-like state of existence. Remaining unperturbed by praise or insult, watching flowers bloom and fall in the courtyard; unconcerned by coming or going, observing clouds roll and stretch in the sky. Being able to let go of praise and insult leads to peace and freedom. Eating when eating, sleeping when sleeping. Not desiring beforehand or reminiscing afterward, calmly accepting life as it comes, one finds Zen in every situation. In this busy field of fame and profit, if one can occasionally relax and reflect, over time, one will realize one's original nature beyond all bonds: lively yet serene, pure and undisturbed Bodhi awareness. People do not gain bonds through struggle and creation but through instinctive wisdom to comprehend and decide.

Buddhists often speak of going with the flow, concepts like "following the flow while maintaining integrity," "maintaining integrity while following the flow," and "not climbing unnecessarily." Going with the flow is not acting carelessly or compromising too easily but adapting to current environmental conditions while doing good deeds. Maintaining integrity is not sticking rigidly to rules but choosing good actions with determination. Following the flow while maintaining integrity means not compromising principles or attitudes. Living in this world, one must be reasonable and flexible in dealing with people and situations, achieving harmony between principles and practices.

Following the flow while maintaining integrity means not violating truth. Zhuangzi, upon his wife's death, knew that life and death were like the four seasons' changes, unchangeable and uncontrollable. Hence, he accepted fate and sang while drumming on a pot. Lu Jia wrote in "New Discourses": "Do not violate natural laws, do not alter the nature of things." Understanding that the universe and life are combinations of causes and conditions, gathering forms and dispersing forms, enables one to find peace and adapt wherever they may be. In life, if one can adhere to principles while following the flow in small details, they can naturally live freely and righteously.

Going with the flow is a broad-mindedness, a maturity, and confidence in one's inner self. Those who understand going with the flow can remain composed and skilled in navigating life's storms and hardships. They can find direction in adversity and maintain a peaceful and joyful mindset. Going with the flow is a correct and clear recognition of reality, a spiritual freedom after comprehending life, a graceful acceptance of gatherings and partings, a transcendent attitude of singing when gaining and stopping when losing, and a composed demeanor of "letting the rain fall as it may." Having a heart that goes with the flow, one discovers that whether the sky is cloudy or sunny, whether the path is bumpy or smooth, one's heart remains peaceful and detached.
