Then the new year, this year is very interesting, I met three Shandong guys who are not much older than me. We had a lot of fun together. They are absolutely three little treasures. This year, I still worked as an electric welder. This was my second year and also the last year in this industry. This year was really a particularly interesting year. Let me first talk about my funny stories. Before going to work this year, I was at home one day and I was very hungry. Before that, I had never cooked, but on that day, I suddenly wanted to fry an egg, so I followed the method I saw others use before. I put oil in the pan and turned on the gas stove to heat it up. Then, the oil caught fire. The flame went straight through the roof. But I didn't panic. I calmly picked up the handle of the pot with both hands, gritted my teeth, endured the burning heat, and carried it to the faucet to pour water over it. As a result, I had to cut my hair short later. Anyway, the fire was eventually extinguished. I just kept pouring water on it, not knowing whether it was because the oil burned out or my persistence moved heaven. Then, my entire hand, from the thumb to the index finger, was severely burned. I was bandaged for a whole month. (Later, people told me that if the oil catches fire, you can simply cover the pot with a lid and the fire will go out. It's a small trick for everyone to learn to avoid getting hurt.) Okay, back to the main topic. At the beginning of this year, I started working at a petroleum transfer station in Datong. Although the place was very remote, it was really beautiful. All around were grasslands, and we worked right in the middle of the grasslands. About a mile away, there was a gas station which served as our resource supply point where we bought cigarettes and alcohol. When I arrived, construction had already begun, so I immediately got into the tedious work of electric welding. Since I had a burn injury from frying eggs, I initially just followed around collecting materials and wandering around. On the third day after I arrived, something interesting happened in the morning. I had just arrived at the site when I saw a commotion among the workers. As I wandered closer to see what was going on, it turned out that Xiao Shi, Xiao Song, and Xiao Shao were fighting with someone. Let me introduce them: Xiao Shi was 22 years old, Xiao Shao was 21, and Xiao Song was only 19. These three kids were fighting with a man in his thirties. Coincidentally, I knew this man in his thirties. A large part of the workers here were brought by my father, and this man was a welder brought by one of my father's friends. There was nothing I could do but step in to break up the fight. If you remember carefully, my hand was still bandaged at that time. In this fight, all the wounds reopened... Thus, this was how I first met these three. However, we didn't have much interaction after that until about a month later when the four of us, along with two elderly men from Jiangsu, a slick-talking, chatterbox laborer from Northeast China, and a man from Liaoning, were grouped together and transferred to the North Oil Depot. During this time, these three young men fought two more times... After arriving at the North Oil Depot, we naturally spent every day together and quickly became familiar with each other. Initially, we didn't know what kind of work we would be doing here, thinking it would be easy, so we happily came over. Upon arrival, we stayed in a warehouse of a nearby family that sold beer and wine. It was very damp, and it was mid-summer. The mosquitoes were everywhere... Unbearable. What was worse was that the roof leaked. If it rained outside, it definitely rained inside too. In the first few days after we arrived, we were quite carefree since the materials hadn't arrived yet, so we just waited for the materials to come before starting work. By this time, the four of us had become very close. The four of us were quite crazy together. Initially, we ate at the same table with the older people, but within a week, we split into two tables because with the four of us at one table, the older people basically couldn't get any dishes. It was true; they couldn't even get a bit of grease from the pot. The four of us would joke and eat crazily, snatching food like crazy. Within five minutes, a pot of dishes would be completely empty. The older people wanted to drink and chat, but by the time they realized it, there was only the pot left. It was summer, and since we were surrounded by farmland, there were melon fields nearby. At that time, the melons were ripe. The four of us took turns crawling into the melon fields at midnight. Within a few days, there were no melons left within at least fifty meters from the roadside. We really did take them by the bagful, regardless of whether they were ripe or not. With so many, we couldn't eat them all, so we gave them out as gifts. Some of the other groups, who were stationed at Factory Six, would take bags of melons back when they came to deliver oxygen and acetylene to us. Ha ha, we haven't even mentioned the pitiful sand pears at the North Oil Depot yet. About two weeks later, we officially started working. Inside the North Oil Depot, there were two 100,000 cubic meter tanks and two 50,000 cubic meter tanks, all full of oil. At the time, we really thought about setting them on fire... Ha ha, we were just fooling around then. After a few days, our energy began to dwindle. We originally thought that coming here would mean a little renovation, cutting a few pipes, welding, and then leaving... Result? The pipes that needed to be renovated were buried six meters underground and couldn't be dug up with an excavator because it might damage the oil pipelines. So, we had to dig manually. Tragically, every day we didn't bring any welding machines or oxyacetylene torches, just shovels. Every day, we marched grandly into the North Oil Depot and began a protracted digging operation lasting one and a half months. That was truly a grand spectacle, though unfortunately, I barely witnessed the later stages of the digging operation... On the first day, we picked sand pears while digging here and there. It was 35.6 degrees Celsius, and we weren't allowed to take off our work uniforms for fear of causing a fire. MD, I was fined 50 yuan because I wasn't wearing synthetic clothing. We brought a big barrel of drinking water, which was used up in the morning. In the afternoon, it was impossible to return since it would take thirty minutes by car. Therefore, after some negotiation, our four-person group decided to pick all the thousands of fruits from their fruit trees that they couldn't eat, taking some, giving some away, and throwing away the rest. We also frequently damaged their main buildings, but I won't go into detail about that. Anyway, it was quite crazy. After working for about three days, we mainly hadn't done this kind of soil excavation work before, and it was really difficult. I could only dig about 20 centimeters deep in a day. You can imagine how big the hole was, not even big enough to bury a larger rat. It was really hot, and we couldn't dig further. The surface was filled with backfilled soil, all gravel and sand particles. We had to dig two meters wide and six meters deep. The length, in our eyes at that time, seemed as long as the Great Wall. Thus, Xiao Shi left the battlefield resolutely in the manner of seeing someone in Shandong province and returned to Shandong to hide. The next day, he called us to notify him once the soil was dug up. Then, Xiao Shao, who often dreamt of his deceased uncle, became mentally depressed and honorably, sickly, couldn't get out of bed. Xiao Song saw the situation and prepared to ask for leave, but at this time, asking for leave wouldn't work anymore due to the severe shortage of manpower. As for me? Ha ha, it's not that I didn't want to continue holding my position; it's just that my body couldn't take it anymore. On a rainy day, since everyone else went to shelter from the rain and I remained steadfast at my post (mainly for the coolness), I ended up with tonsillitis. The next day, I couldn't even swallow a grain of rice. In the morning, I could still smoke, but by the afternoon, I couldn't even drink water. By the next day, I hadn't eaten for a whole day and night. My throat was almost closed, and it hurt even to drink water. Thus, I couldn't accompany Xiao Song to the end of this grand war. It's said that Xiao Song dug a hole that was two meters wide and one meter long throughout the battle, achieving significant results. Role model! Later, since this work was really unsuitable for amateur players like us, it was decided above to hire a batch of professional soil excavators. Thus, batch after batch of soil excavation warriors came. Finally, the soil was dug up, Xiao Shi returned after seeing his object of affection, Xiao Shao's illness was cured, Xiao Song had finally reached the end, and I returned as well. We began our official work. What did we do on the first day? We peeled off the anti-corrosion layer, which is the layer wrapped around the pipes to prevent rusting. To mention one of the materials, it was glass fiber. Without exaggeration, those days were filled with glass fibers flying around in the wind, and we plunged headfirst into removing them forcefully since we couldn't cut the pipelines otherwise. Since KartRider was extremely popular at the time, we had a good reason to go online, so at the only internet cafe in Hongwei Xing where logging in took five minutes, a very impressive tractor without a trailer, about four meters high, often parked outside at night. It was a covered yellow tractor, looking very handsome. This tractor was our exclusive vehicle for online gaming under the guise of taking a bath. Later, somehow the news leaked out. According to my father's phone call, his informants often reported that we always went online at night. Maybe the stylish tractor betrayed us, so I couldn't go online as often. Thus, we shifted our target to nearby vehicles for transporting kitchen waste, delivery vans, donkey carts, horse carts, bicycles, etc., borrowing them whenever possible, hopping on whenever possible, and drifting online. It was indeed a refreshing period. Once the anti-corrosion work was done, we started welding, and within a week, this bit of work was finished. Then, we were transferred back to Datong, but our gang had already formed. Unfortunately, after returning to Datong, we only stayed together for a few days before the three of them were transferred away. During this time, we occasionally stole some eggs from the canteen to roast on the grassland, planning to catch some frogs but never succeeded. Then, they all left, leaving us and two other people responsible for finishing up the work here. Actually, the so-called finishing up meant monitoring the operational process to see where there were leaks, which valves weren't tightened, which heating valves weren't functioning properly, meaning the three of us were just idling here since our contributions were minimal. Thus, our leaders forgot about us, and our living expenses were cut off. Soon, we ran out of cigarettes. Meals can be skipped, but without cigarettes, we couldn't survive. The three of us began shamelessly bumming cigarettes from everywhere. After only three days of bumming, we couldn't endure it anymore. Since bumming has its limits and you can't ask everyone for cigarettes, we decided to be self-reliant. That night, driven by the lack of cigarettes, the three of us each carried nearly 100 pounds of scrap iron and trekked a mile through the mud to the supermarket near the gas station and exchanged it for five packs of premium cigarettes. Ha ha, the Changbaishan cigarettes costing six yuan per pack were considered premium there. Generally, they were two-yuan cigarettes. Our smoking problem was solved, and autumn had arrived. The corn in the fields was ripe, and every night, we organized corn roasting parties around campfires. This went on for a long time, and our cigarettes were always the best. One evening, while I was sleeping, someone suddenly shouted my name. Groggily waking up, I was astonished to find Xiao Shi, Xiao Song, and Xiao Shao in our room. It turned out that these three had gotten into a fight again, beating up a crane operator from the department. Since they were from Shandong and the other person was local, fearing retaliation, they walked twenty miles overnight to seek refuge with me. I sent them back, telling them they definitely wouldn't get beaten since there were so many people just breaking up the fight, and besides, the guy was hospitalized, so paying some compensation would suffice. Thus, after bumming two packs of cigarettes and a roasted corn meal from me, they returned. (I wonder if they heard about our prosperity and found an excuse to come and bum cigarettes from me.) They brought some news. It turned out that the place they were at was even worse—just a vast grassland. First, bulldozers would level a piece of land, then prefabricated houses would be brought in to enclose a yard, and then the oil station construction would begin. There was nothing around, and the nearest place where you could take a vehicle was twenty miles away. Basically, it was wilderness. Water had to be transported using cranes and trucks with water tanks from far away. Not long after learning this news, we were also dispatched there. I won't talk about the hardships, but let me share something enjoyable. Within five miles of that place, there was actually a rural area, albeit with a small population. When I first arrived, the cigarettes I brought quickly ran out. On the second night, Xiao Song mysteriously came to find me. They were three people sharing one room, and they insisted on calling me to their room. As soon as I entered, Xiao Shao pulled out more than ten welding rods from under the bed. They had already found alternative means of livelihood. Since we were all old comrades-in-arms, there was nothing more to say. I went back, put on thick clothes, and we set off under the cover of darkness. It was a distance of more than five miles, and along the way, we laughed and talked, not feeling tired at all. Upon arriving, the enthusiasm of the small grocery store owner surpassed expectations, directly pulling us over to hotpot. Apparently, they were already familiar with the routine. At twenty-five yuan per pack price, they swapped for a lot of cigarettes, alcohol, candy, snacks, and the boss specially drove the broken minivan to send us back and told us on the dinner table that in the future, we could call and they would come to pick us up. Ha ha, but the next time was even more interesting because the next time we encountered a leader from the client side of our project... www.92971.com. He actually drove there. That was our material manager, so our welding rods were under his control. We were all confused when this guy showed up, which was quite embarrassing. Later, we learned that it was even more awkward for him since we only sold a few packs, but this guy brought a truckload of materials. We could only admire him. Later, this guy insisted on driving us back, even though he was drunk. This journey was literally life-and-death speed. It took this guy nearly five minutes just to insert the car key. The four of us obediently sat in the car and didn't dare to get out. This guy finally managed to start the car... Swerving left and right, we almost fell into the ditch several times, but he continued driving. We were drenched in cold sweat at the back, almost ready to exchange last words. When we got out of the car, he was fine, but the four of us were all drenched in cold sweat. After this, our project became tighter, and we didn't go out anymore. Thinking back then, it was indeed hard, but there was joy amidst the hardship, and endless fun. Many years have passed... I can't remember too much. The above is all I can recall. Remember the hardest times? We worked on heating during the day and continued working in the wild at night. In the dead of winter, with no trees around, if we didn't move for a minute, we'd almost freeze solid. We worked continuously for three days. One meal was delayed for a long time, and five of us ate more than forty steamed buns, a dish, and a soup. Still not full, we cooked several bowls of noodles afterward. Back then, every day outside, for fifteen hours, even if we didn't do anything, we could freeze to death. Welding one welding rod made your hands numb, then warming them by the fire made them incredibly painful. However, whenever I think back to that time, what comes to mind more often is the various joys. The special kind of joy that exists only in hardship. People who laugh together may forget, but those who suffer together, you will always remember. Ha ha, I didn't realize I wrote for half the night. Really, I started writing at eight o'clock, and now it's almost two o'clock. Originally, I just wanted to write down and summarize my friendship in words, but as I wrote, I lost track of the theme and forgot what I was writing about. Only fragments of memories kept flashing back, segment by segment. The closer the year, the more I could recall. For older years, because the fragments were too scattered and couldn't connect, I didn't write about them. Old friends, please don't blame me. But after writing for so long, I really enjoyed it, just like reminiscing with friends. Everyone, please don't blame me for the messy writing. At least, my friends who can read this must be very good friends and people who care about me deeply. I hope everyone can recall some past events and smile knowingly. Reflecting on them is like sipping a fine wine—delicious, smooth, and slightly intoxicating. Ha ha, I'm thoroughly confused now, mainly because I'm sleepy... And that's it for today. I will continue writing from the next year up to now... It can serve as a year-end summary. Good night.
