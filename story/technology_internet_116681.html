Forum Tieba promotion examples

by skyito on 2009-01-04 16:04:55

Many times, I see many experts boasting about getting ***** IPs in a day, and I can't help but feel envious. Looking at the daily visit statistics of these experts, and then looking at my own meager dozens of IPs that I'm ashamed to show, it's really embarrassing.

I firmly believe that the Internet is something with very strong replicability. After research, I found the following insights and attached examples.

There are dozens of forums and Tieba (Baidu's discussion board) with super high traffic. Therefore, many experts gather inside to promote themselves. These places have become a must-contest land for experts to outwit each other, and also a good place for rookies like us to learn.

Forums and Tieba have one characteristic: your post will only get super high display opportunities if it continuously gets replies. Otherwise, you can only try various methods to ask the moderator to sticky your post.

Many times, my material is quite good, but there are very few replies. Why? The same material, my click-through rate is dozens of times worse compared to the experts. What's going on?

After research, I discovered that it was because my "final touch" wasn't done well. In other words, my ability to trigger them to reply, to make them click the mouse, isn't good enough. Let's look at an example, and everything will be clear. This post was written by me imitating an expert. It got 50,000 clicks in 3 days.

Title: "Female driver was raped (after reading, please observe a minute of silence) and support this post"

The female driver was raped (after reading, please observe a minute of silence)

Phoenix TV's 12:00 Noon program "Talk Show" told a true story:

In a certain place in China, a female driver was driving a bus loaded with passengers on a winding mountain road. Three thugs on the bus actually targeted the pretty female driver, forcing the bus to stop and wanting to take the female driver off the bus to "play around". The female driver cried out for help, but all the passengers were silent like chilled crickets.

Only a middle-aged, thin man stood up, but was beaten and fell to the ground. The man became extremely angry and shouted loudly, calling on all the passengers to stop the violence, but no one responded. They let the female driver be dragged into the bushes. Half an hour later, the three thugs returned with the disheveled female driver. As the bus was about to go again, the female driver wanted the injured middle-aged man to get off the bus. The man refused and insisted on staying.

"Hey, you get off, my bus doesn't carry people like you!" The middle-aged man said angrily, "How unreasonable are you? Is it wrong for me to save you?"

"You saved me? What did you save me from?" The female driver denied outright, causing some passengers to snicker. The middle-aged man became extremely angry, regretting his lack of great martial arts skills! He didn't manage to save her, but he certainly shouldn't end up being driven off the bus like this. He refused to get off. "Besides, I bought a ticket, I have the right to ride!"

The female driver said coldly, "If you don't get off, I won't drive."

Unexpectedly, the passengers who had just turned a blind eye to the violence woke up as if from a dream, and unanimously persuaded the man to get off the bus: "You'd better get down, we have things to do, we can't afford the delay!" Some stronger passengers even tried to drag the middle-aged man off the bus, reminding one of the plot in Maupassant's "Boule de Suif".
