Home Environment and Health -- 18 Home Health Tips

by gege on 2012-02-27 20:06:24

1. Bathrooms that are too damp can easily lead to illness.

A damp bathroom can easily cause fungi to grow and multiply, leading to respiratory diseases. Therefore, wet mops should be dried before being placed in the bathroom; ensure the drainage system is unobstructed; frequently use the exhaust fan.

2. Clever ways to remove odors at home

Sprinkle some yam water at the drain outlet, add some fresh orange peel to the flower pots, place a packet of dried tea leaves in wardrobes and drawers, put a little vinegar in the wok and heat it until it evaporates - all these methods can help remove odors.

3. Too many plants in the room can tire the eyes

Plants placed indoors should be few but exquisite. Too many will destroy the overall sense of the environment, making it difficult to regulate one's mood and causing visual fatigue.

4. Choosing the right curtains for a good night's sleep

Velvet fabric curtains are relatively heavy, with good sound absorption and light-blocking effects; using curtains with red and black combinations can help induce sleep more quickly.

5. Shower rooms, fully tempered glass is the safest

Using semi-tempered or bent glass may cause them to shatter when using hot water in winter or cold water in summer, while fully tempered glass has higher safety.

6. Home tiles, too bright can hurt the eyes

White and metallic colored tiles have strong reflection, which might lead to myopia and cataracts, thus not suitable for large-area use. During decoration, it’s best to choose matte tiles.

7. Using the balcony as a kitchen is dangerous

Placing stoves on the balcony can easily cause pipelines to freeze and crack in winter, leading to safety accidents; during windy spring and summer seasons, the fire can be easily blown out, causing gas leakage.

8. Buying furniture, samples are the most environmentally friendly

Furniture samples have been placed in the air for a longer time, allowing formaldehyde, benzene, and other harmful substances to be released more thoroughly; samples often use genuine materials, and some manufacturers even carry out formaldehyde removal treatment.

9. Decorating rooms, use less oil paintings

Oil paints contain certain amounts of soluble antimony, arsenic, barium, lead, mercury, selenium, etc., if over-consumed by the human body, they can harm health.

10. Don't always sleep on one side of the mattress

Always sleeping on one side of the mattress can easily lead to spring deformation and mattress sagging. New mattresses should ideally have their sides and orientations changed every 2-3 months.

11. Toilet brushes, replace every half year

Toilet brushes used for a long time can have bristles fall off, easily harboring dirt and grime, so it's best to replace them every half year. If stored in a non-ventilated container, bacteria can also easily grow.

12. Pet hair, sweep with tea dregs

Drain the remaining tea dregs after drinking, sprinkle them on the ground, then sweep with a broom, which can easily pick up pet hair, but it's better to use tea that has been steeped multiple times.

13. Even with a range hood, open windows

Although the range hood can suck away cooking fumes, it cannot suck away the exhaust gases produced by gas combustion, which are more harmful to the human body.

14. Furniture infested with insects, apply some Sichuan pepper powder

Wooden furniture infested with insects can have chili peppers or Sichuan pepper crushed into powder, stuffed into insect holes, then coated with paraffin oil, continuously doing this for 10 days can eliminate insects.

15. Choose stainless steel floor drains

Cast iron and cast copper floor drains have rough surfaces, prone to rusting, with small drainage volume and slow flow rate; PVC floor drains have poor anti-aging properties, easily deforming when exposed to cold or heat.

16. Wallpapers, only stick on one wall

Wallpapers themselves and adhesives can release volatile organic compounds, so it's better to just use them to decorate focal points like TV walls or theme walls.

17. For excessive sweating, use bamboo charcoal mattress

The porous structure of bamboo charcoal allows the mattress to adsorb carbon dioxide, ammonia, and high humidity sweat excreted by the skin, keeping the body comfortable during sleep.

18. Room colors can also heal

Orange can stimulate appetite, indigo blue helps reduce pain after surgery for patients, purple can soothe nerves and eliminate tension.
