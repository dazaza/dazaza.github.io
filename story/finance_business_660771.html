Baosteel Accelerates Integration of Guangdong Steel Enterprises, Aims to Recreate Another "Baosteel" (1)

by qssakkgfex3 on 2012-02-08 10:51:18

Baosteel Accelerates Integration of Guangdong Steel Enterprises, Aiming to Recreate Another "Baosteel" - Corporate Dynamics - Industry News - Building Materials Network

Following the substantial integration of three listed companies under Hebei Iron and Steel Group through an absorption merger, Baosteel Group has also begun to take advantage of the steel industry's yet-to-be-fully-recovered opportunity to accelerate the restructuring of two steel enterprises in Guangdong: Guangzhou Iron and Steel Group (hereinafter referred to as "Guanggang") and Guangdong Shaoguan Iron and Steel Group (hereinafter referred to as "Shaogang").

Yesterday, this reporter exclusively learned that currently, intermediaries dispatched by Baosteel are conducting due diligence on the assets of Guanggang and Shaogang. Preliminary results have been obtained for the evaluation of the net assets and rights of these two companies. After the official release of the evaluation results, Baosteel will accelerate the integration of Guanggang and Shaogang, making them truly become subsidiaries under Baosteel.

The integration of Guangdong's steel industry by Baosteel originated from the Zhanjiang Steel Project that Baosteel had been planning for many years. In March last year, the National Development and Reform Commission preliminarily approved Baosteel to build a steel project in Zhanjiang, Guangdong, and required Baosteel Group to jointly establish a joint venture company with Shaogang and Guanggang to co-build the Zhanjiang project, with Baosteel holding controlling shares.

Three months later, the platform aimed at building the Zhanjiang project —— Guangdong Iron and Steel Group Co., Ltd. (hereinafter referred to as "Guangdong Iron and Steel Group") was officially established. At that time, Baosteel Group announced that it would contribute cash to hold 80% of the shares in Guangdong Iron and Steel Group; while the Guangdong Provincial SASAC and Guangzhou Municipal SASAC would contribute the state-owned net assets of Shaogang Group and Guanggang Group respectively, merging to hold 20% of the shares.

On the day Guangdong Iron and Steel Group was established, the new company's leadership team was elected. Two leaders from Baosteel Group were appointed as chairman and general manager of Guangdong Iron and Steel Group, while the leaders of Shaogang Group and Guanggang Group were appointed as deputy chairmen.

However, this reporter learned from multiple insiders at Baosteel Group and Guanggang Group that the establishment of Guangdong Iron and Steel Group did not mean that Baosteel could restructure Guanggang and Shaogang. Since the Zhanjiang project still needed final approval from the state, the new company did not operate substantially. Baosteel, Guanggang Group, and Shaogang Group continued to operate independently, and the assets of Guanggang Group and Shaogang Group were not transferred to Guangdong Iron and Steel Group.

"Since the second half of last year, the steel industry has fallen into a low point, and Baosteel also wants to take this opportunity to accelerate the reorganization of Guangdong's steel industry because the cost of asset evaluation is lower at this time," an insider from Baosteel told the reporter. After the asset evaluation is completed, Baosteel hopes to further acquire the remaining 20% of the shares in Guangdong Iron and Steel Group, which is equivalent to acquiring the assets of Guanggang and Shaogang, thereby making Guanggang and Shaogang truly become subsidiaries of Guangdong Iron and Steel Group and incorporate them under Baosteel. This would facilitate Baosteel's ability to output management teams and technology to the two companies for unified planning and management.

Relevant personnel from the board office of Guanggang Stock, the listed company of Guanggang Group, also revealed to the reporter yesterday that the current asset evaluation conducted by Baosteel has already produced preliminary results, which need final confirmation and feedback. Whether Baosteel can acquire the remaining 20% of the equity in Guangdong Iron and Steel Group will be mainly decided by the SASAC.

According to what the reporter learned, the Guangdong provincial government also supports Baosteel's integration of Guangdong's steel industry. An insider from Baosteel disclosed that the reason why Guangdong Iron and Steel Group, in which Baosteel participated as a controlling shareholder, could quickly hang its sign before Hebei Iron and Steel Group last June was mainly due to the strong support of the Guangdong provincial government.

Listed Companies Need Handling

Guanggang and Shaogang are the two largest steel enterprises in Guangdong. Guanggang produced 1.5 million tons of steel last year, while Shaogang produced over 4 million tons of steel. If Guanggang and Shaogang are integrated, Baosteel will thus obtain access to the South China market, which is the second-largest steel consumption market nationwide, and there will be hope to recreate another "Baosteel" using the Zhanjiang project.

Previously, Xu Lejiang, chairman of Baosteel Group, told the reporter that China's annual steel production capacity consumes 40% in East China and 30% in Guangdong. Guangdong needs to consume more than 40 million tons of steel annually, while Guangdong's own steel production is currently less than 10 million tons, hence presenting huge market opportunities.

It is worth noting that one premise when the National Development and Reform Commission approved the preliminary work of the Zhanjiang Steel Base in March last year was that Guangdong Province must eliminate 10 million tons of outdated production capacity. Among them, Shaogang should eliminate 1.5 million tons of steelmaking capacity, and Guanggang should eliminate 1.5 million tons of ironmaking capacity and 3.5 million tons of steelmaking capacity. This means that most of Guanggang's steel production capacity will be eliminated.

However, an insider from Guanggang told the reporter that Guanggang currently does not have plans to cut production capacity. "The market is starting to improve now, especially the rebars and wire rods produced by Guanggang. The price per ton rose by several hundred yuan today, and the steel plant also started to make profits in June and July."

If Baosteel integrates Guanggang and Shaogang, the listed companies Guanggang Stock and Shaogang Songshan currently face the issue of whether there exists competition among peers. According to what the reporter learned, both Guanggang and Shaogang currently produce construction steel, and their markets are mainly in Guangdong. Baosteel Corporation already sells plates to the Guangdong market, and Shaogang also produces some medium-thick plates and other plate products.

Regarding how to integrate the listed companies of Guanggang and Shaogang, Yu Ziquan, chairman of Shaogang Group, once told the reporter that he hoped to achieve "no competition among peers" with Baosteel Corporation. However, the relevant person from Guanggang Stock believed that after the reorganization, what needs to be considered is the issue between listed companies, whether it is delisting or adjusting product structure and market scope, there is currently no conclusion.
