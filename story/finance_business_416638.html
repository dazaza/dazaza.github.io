BEIJING, April 2 (Xinhua) -- Libya's rebels said on Friday that they would accept a ceasefire if the Libyan government withdrew its troops from besieged cities and allowed peaceful demonstrations across the country.

A doctor in Libya said that NATO airstrikes hit a truck carrying arms, but the explosion destroyed two houses, killing seven civilians and injuring 25 near the site. The victims were aged between 12 to 20 years old.

The rebels proposed a ceasefire agreement, as reported by British media, where Qaddafi’s envoy visited London to hold talks with British officials. Reports mentioned that this envoy named Mohammed - the aide of Qaddafi’s son Hannibal - was the top Islamic assistant. A spokesperson for the British Foreign Office told The Guardian that they conveyed a clear message: "Qaddafi must step down." The spokesperson refused to comment further on any contact with the Libyan leader.

NATO also warned the Libyan rebels that if they attacked civilians, they would also be targeted.

Previously, after Libya’s foreign minister defected to England, along with the prime minister, speaker, intelligence chief, oil minister, and 12 other senior figures whose whereabouts are unknown, it is suspected that they fled to Tunisia and prepared to go to Europe.

This suggested that within a few hours, the rebels would enhance their military equipment. In order to control the main Mediterranean oil port, the battle for Benghazi continues. On the western front, troops loyal to Libyan leader Moammar Gadhafi continue to bombard the rebel-controlled important western city of Misrata.

Libya's rebels mention conditional ceasefire.

Meanwhile, U.S. Defense Secretary Robert Gates again strongly opposed any U.S. troops entering Libya. He stated that as long as he was in charge, American troops would not set foot on Libyan soil. However, before his remarks, American media reported that the CIA had sent agents into Libya to gather information and make contact with the opposition.

Coalition forces killed Libyan civilians during operations.

A senior NATO spokesman said that the UN Security Council resolution authorizing the protection of Libyan civilians "fits the conflict." Regarding NATO's mission enforcing no-fly zones, an investigation has been opened concerning the issue.

In the past few days, due to the lack of multinational force air strikes, the opposition forces retreated about 200 kilometers, pulling the oil town of Janzur into the eastern Benghazi port area, finally deciding to rally support here and prepare for a counter-offensive.

White House officials say that the defection of Libyan Foreign Minister Moussa Koussa to England shows that the ruling group of Libyan leader Moammar Gadhafi is falling apart internally.
