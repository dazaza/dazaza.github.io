The scene of trade depicted in the "Ballad of Mulan"

by jilan0783 on 2011-08-29 17:14:53

It has become a thing of the past forever. With the rapid development of the Internet and the rise of online shopping, today, people can complete transactions within seconds by simply clicking a mouse without leaving their homes, enjoying the convenience of "a flick of the finger." This change is not only a revolution in consumer concepts and behaviors but also a "reshuffle" in trade operation models and trade ecosystems.

With the popularization of the Internet, as well as the development of online banking and modern logistics, online shopping has shown a trend of high-speed growth in recent years, becoming increasingly prominent in economic and social life. Even during the "severe winter" of the global financial crisis, it still exhibited a thriving momentum, becoming one of the industries least affected by the crisis and with the best development prospects. Online shopping behavior is gradually evolving from an internet fashion to a mass consumption model, permeating all levels of society and triggering changes in consumer behavior and consumption concepts among internet users.

China's online shopping, after experiencing phases of fever, silence, and adjustment, is now entering a new stage of rapid development, with its advantages increasingly recognized by internet users. Surveys show that among active internet users (those who shop online at least once a month), 45.7% of those surveyed shop online regularly, 37.6% shop 1-5 times per month, and 78.1% spend 60% of their total expenditure on gifts purchased online. (As shown in Figure 1) It can be seen that in some consumption areas, people's preference for online shopping has surpassed traditional physical stores.

While online shopping becomes increasingly common in developed regions, it is also quietly emerging in small and medium-sized cities and rural areas across China. Statistics show that currently, second and third-tier cities, central and western regions, and rural areas account for 73.20% of China's total online shopping volume.

A considerable number of early adopters, whose main purpose was to satisfy curiosity and novelty, have developed a dependence on the online shopping model, making it an indispensable way of consumption in their lives. This indicates that an era of habitual online shopping is beginning to take shape and will soon start a new round of penetration.

The influencing factors of online shopping highlight "human-centered" characteristics.

Online shopping is a product of the comprehensive development of the Internet, banks, and the logistics industry. However, with the continuous improvement of the macro environment and the increasing maturity of supporting industries, research on the influencing factors of online shopping has gradually shifted from environmental factors to individual factors of internet users. Demographic characteristics such as education level, income, and urban/rural residence level, as well as human network behavior habits and real-life states, known as "human-centered" factors, have had a significant impact on online shopping.

1. Women are more inclined towards online shopping than men but are also more concerned about its risks.

Surveys show that the prevalence and depth of online shopping among female internet users exceed those of male users. The proportion of female internet users participating in online shopping reaches 89.34%, while this proportion is only 62.86% among males. As the business model and product categories of online shopping become richer, people tend to buy more household goods rather than just books, audio equipment, digital electronics, which might explain why the proportion of women among online shoppers has increased rapidly. Female online shoppers mainly focus on clothing, shoes, and home crafts, while men are more interested in prepaid cards and electronic products. Interestingly, despite women's preference for online shopping, they express more concerns about its risks compared to men.

2. Young and middle-aged groups are the main body of online shopping.

In this survey, the average age of internet users participating in online shopping is 30.16 years old. Among them, the youngest is 12 years old, and the oldest is 66 years old. The proportion of internet users aged 20-29 who participate in online shopping reaches 91.67%. In the group of internet users under 20 years old, the participation rate in online shopping increases with age. In contrast, in the group of internet users over 29 years old, the older they are, the lower the participation rate in online shopping. (As shown in Figure 2)

3. Online shopping has become one of the main shopping methods for white-collar workers.

Regression analysis results show that highly educated and high-income urban white-collar workers are deep users of online shopping. The proportion of white-collar workers with a bachelor's degree or above is nearly 10 percentage points higher than the sample average. The average monthly income of white-collar workers is nearly 1000 yuan higher than the sample average. Nearly 60% of white-collar workers earn more than 4000 yuan per month, and one-quarter earn more than 6000 yuan per month, with 5.6% earning over 10,000 yuan per month.

The regression analysis predictions were confirmed in the survey results, where the proportion of white-collar workers participating in online shopping reached as high as 93.3%, with more than five online shopping instances per month on average, higher than other groups. With the acceleration of living and work rhythms, white-collar workers have less time to visit malls and supermarkets. The popularization of the Internet and the continuous improvement of related service industries have gradually shifted the consumption behavior of this large group toward the Internet. Currently, online shopping has become an indispensable way of shopping for a significant number of white-collar workers. The variety of items white-collar workers purchase online is expanding, and the amount spent is increasing year by year. Apart from product and information factors, the ability of online shopping to deliver directly to a specified location is also a significant advantage attracting white-collar workers.

Two features and trends emerge in white-collar online shopping: first, computers and electronic products replace books and audio equipment to become the new "champion"; second, purchasing high-end or luxury goods through group buying, Taobao men's outerwear, China Railway Express inquiry, SF Express outlet inquiry, and proxy purchases are very common.

4. The longer the internet usage time, the higher the trust in online information, and the deeper the participation in online shopping.

The popularization and functional perfection of the Internet are conditions for the development of online shopping, and the usage situation of internet users also becomes an important influencing factor in online shopping. Data analysis shows that there is a clear positive linear correlation between internet tenure, mobile internet usage time, internet dependency level, and trust in online information with the degree of participation in online shopping. The longer the internet tenure, the longer the online time, the higher the internet dependency, the deeper the participation in online shopping, and the higher the trust in online information, the more actively internet users participate in online shopping.

5. The higher the life satisfaction, the higher the enthusiasm for online shopping.

This survey used the life satisfaction scale developed by psychologist Edward Diener from the University of Illinois to test the life satisfaction of internet users, then conducted a correlation analysis with their participation in online shopping. The results showed a clear positive correlation between life satisfaction and participation in online shopping, i.e., the higher the life satisfaction, the greater the enthusiasm of internet users for online shopping. According to the life satisfaction scores, internet users were divided into four groups. Among them, the high satisfaction group (21-25 points) had an average participation in online shopping of 2.84, while the low satisfaction group (5-9 points) had an average participation in online shopping of only 2.15.

Shopping behavior highlights personalized needs and differentiated business opportunities.

Online shopping not only meets the personalized needs of consumers but also allows merchants to gain huge business opportunities through differentiated market positioning. The vast amount of information available on the Internet and the convenient ways to obtain information enable people to acquire the goods they need at a very low transaction cost. According to surveys, obtaining more product information and being able to purchase goods unavailable through traditional means are important motivations for online shopping.

1. Acquiring rare products or product information is the biggest advantage of online shopping.

The openness of the Internet not only provides consumers with abundant information and convenient access to information but also expands the geographical scope of transactions, covering areas that traditional sales channels find difficult to reach. China’s vast territory, uneven population distribution, and complex market hierarchy make the establishment and maintenance of physical sales channels costly, leading to many quality products failing to reach target consumers. For example, in terms of luxury consumption, numerous potential consumers exist in China’s second- and third-tier cities. However, these products' physical sales channels often fail to reach local consumers, making online shopping an essential method for such transactions.

The survey found that 62.5% of respondents agreed that “online shopping allows me to buy products not available locally,” and 59.6% agreed that “online shopping allows me to get more product information.” These two factors are the biggest advantages of online shopping compared to traditional shopping. Additionally, internet users generally believe that online shopping is cheaper and more convenient than traditional shopping, while the frequently mentioned disadvantages of online shopping, such as increased spending, are not widely acknowledged.

2. Spiritual and fashionable consumer goods are the most popular online shopping items.

In the survey, we listed 12 types of goods and scored them based on their frequency of online purchases using a five-point scale (1 = never, 2 = occasionally, 3 = sometimes, 4 = often, 5 = always). The results are shown in Figure 3: Clothing, shoes, socks, and jewelry have the highest online purchase proportions; followed by paying bills or buying phone cards, books, and newspapers; leisure and entertainment, and online learning have the same proportion of 2.4; home crafts, electronics, computers, and travel ticket purchases have lower proportions; food, medical health supplements, and housekeeping services have the lowest proportions. It can be seen that online shopping in China focuses more on spiritual and fashionable products and services.

Product Quality and After-Sales Service Expose Online Shopping Risks

The transaction process of online shopping is fragmented, making it difficult for consumers to contact actual products during the decision-making process, thus increasing shopping risks. Figure 4 reflects the level of concern among internet users regarding online shopping risks. Internet users expressed concern about all nine issues, indicating that these nine issues are matters of concern. The two most worrying issues are "difficulty in understanding product quality" and "difficulty in returning or exchanging." The risks of online shopping mainly come from the following aspects:

1. Lack of integrity mechanisms leads to difficulty in ensuring product quality.

Since China's credit system is not yet perfect, irregular behaviors occur in online transactions in the unregulated virtual space. Customers cannot inspect product quality solely based on websites, giving speculative online merchants opportunities to exploit loopholes. Inferior products not only harm the enthusiasm of internet users for shopping but also disrupt the operating order of regular, honest online merchants.

2. Weak self-protection awareness among consumers leaves room for online fraud.

Although third-party transaction systems like Alipay are already quite mature, many novice online shoppers do not know how to use these platforms and instead continue with bank transfers or postal payments, leaving room for speculative online merchants to exploit. According to the survey, more than half of online shopping scams result from "paying first and shipping later." Therefore, issues such as "difficulty in returning or exchanging," "credibility problems of online stores," and "damaged goods or no delivery" are particularly concerning risks of online shopping.

3. Payment security and privacy protection become important issues in online shopping.

In online shopping, consumers are greatly concerned about the security of bank account passwords and funds during transactions. Additionally, since a large amount of personal information is required when shopping online, these details often involve personal privacy. If leaked, it could damage the rights of consumers. Therefore, among the nine investigated risks of online shopping, the "security of bank cards" and concerns about "leaking personal privacy" scored 3.73 and 3.67 respectively, indicating that payment security and privacy protection in online transactions have indeed become important risks in online shopping. This phenomenon was confirmed in a survey by the China Internet Network Information Center: "Many people question the security of online payments."

Optimization of Online Business Models and Online Trade Environment

Based on the investigation and analysis of online shopping, the following suggestions are proposed, hoping to contribute to the further prosperity and development of online shopping.

1. Adjust competitive strategies and change the current over-reliance on price advantages.

For a long time, people generally believed that the Internet significantly reduced transaction costs, so price advantage was considered the greatest advantage of online shopping. In fact, this advantage has been overly exaggerated. During the early stages of online shopping development, major shopping websites tried to capture the market with low-price strategies. As the market evolved, the main competitors of online shopping gradually shifted from traditional merchants to peers within the online shopping industry, compressing the space for price competition. Relying solely on low-price strategies to win the market became less feasible. The survey found that the greatest advantage of online shopping recognized by internet users is no longer price but the provision of rare products and information. Therefore, strategic adjustments around "rarity" and "information," implementing differentiation strategies, have become essential for the development of online shopping. Differentiation can bring higher prices and profits to online enterprises. American scholar Baker's research found that online enterprises selling highly branded health products and beauty products can increase prices by 17% without causing adverse reactions.

The greatest advantage of achieving integration strategies in the online shopping industry lies in the diversity of Internet functions, serving as both an information release platform and integrating information interaction, information transactions, and information utilization. Thus, the online transaction process involves the collection and analysis of consumer and consumer behavior information, helping merchants understand market dynamics and providing favorable conditions for implementing vertical integration strategies. Meanwhile, potential consumer groups formed around product information with certain common traits facilitate horizontal integration.

2. Focus on segmented markets and enhance the professionalization of products and services.

As internet users deepen their participation in online shopping, the shortcomings of comprehensive shopping websites in providing specialized products and services become evident, presenting opportunities for market segmentation in online shopping.

The research results on the influencing factors of online shopping provide valuable references for understanding and developing segmented markets in the online shopping industry. Developing segmented markets in online shopping is driven by two forces: one is consumer demand force, and the other is internet function force. Consumer demand force refers to meeting specific professional or specialized demands of consumers by providing deeply relevant products. For instance, white-collar internet users are loyal consumers of online shopping. As the first and second generations of white-collar workers enter marriage and childbearing periods, the market for home and baby products offers more business opportunities. Internet function force involves creating new internet functions through technological innovation and converting them into goods or services. If consumer demand force passively satisfies consumer demands, internet function force proactively creates consumer demands.

3. Expand business models and promote diversification of online shopping methods.

The survey shows that C2C (consumer-to-consumer) represented by Taobao and B2C (business-to-consumer) represented by Dangdang are the mainstream models of China's online shopping industry. Both models have their pros and cons, but the common issue is placing consumers in a passive trading position. Therefore, consideration should be given to promoting the C2B (consumer-to-business) model in certain specific markets.

C2B refers to internet users posting information online based on their own needs, guiding businesses to compete for trading opportunities. This method suits industries with large seller markets and substantial transaction amounts, such as the home renovation industry, where internet users post demand information on websites, and renovation companies can send service plans and prices to internet users, allowing them to decide whether to purchase.

4. Improve complementary services for online shopping, strengthen logistics systems and payment system construction.

China's online shopping industrial chain is not yet mature, and the complementary services of logistics and payment lag behind, constraining the larger-scale development of the online shopping market. Shopping websites primarily focused on C2C business heavily rely on third-party logistics, limiting their development. In November 2009, three courier companies—Yunda, YTO, and ZTO—suddenly joined forces to raise prices, catching many internet users and online store owners off guard. Currently, China's logistics industry lacks unified standards, making quality and service difficult to control, increasing the shopping risks for internet users. Moreover, private courier companies have fewer outlets in western regions and below the municipal level. Although postal EMS and SF Express offer speed and coverage advantages, their high costs limit the penetration of online shopping in these emerging markets. On the other hand, the enormous profits brought by online shopping attract more logistics companies and delivery systems to join the industry, adding pressure to the transportation system and leading to a highly fragmented logistics industry. Overall improvements in the logistics industry require collaboration from the government, enterprises, and industry associations, appropriately raising entry barriers, avoiding duplicate infrastructure construction, and improving the service level and quality of the logistics industry through enhanced management and encouragement of competition.

Online shopping has changed the traditional "cash-on-delivery" consumption model, with electronic remittances, online banking, and third-party payments becoming the primary payment methods. However, technical defects and management loopholes prevent adequate protection of personal information and property safety, causing internet users to express significant concerns about the security of bank cards in online shopping. Therefore, continuously improving the payment system through technological innovation and strengthening management to protect the information and property safety of internet users is crucial.

5. Perfect supervision and management, establish a good market environment.

Compared to the booming development of online shopping, the development of regulatory mechanisms lags behind. Frequent occurrences of online fraud, online pyramid schemes, false advertising, and other behaviors infringing on consumer rights have become the main factors limiting internet users' participation in online shopping. Furthermore, tax evasion, especially customs duty avoidance, during online transactions causes significant losses to the state.

Traditional industrial and commercial supervision models face difficulties in determining supervisory responsibilities, collecting evidence, and clarifying responsible entities in regulating online transactions. It is suggested that while continuously improving relevant legislation, a web-monitoring approach be adopted, establishing an online industry and commerce bureau to break geographical and departmental responsibility restrictions and conduct daily supervision of online transactions. At the same time, network platform providers should be urged to effectively fulfill their supervisory responsibilities and leverage the role of consumers in reporting and gathering evidence.
