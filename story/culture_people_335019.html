The scam of quick filing, 1-minute filing that is popular on the Internet now.

by nilaima588 on 2010-03-25 14:16:34

Case 1: Get approval in just a few minutes and obtain the filing number.

This kind of fast filing is done by ISPs using their modification authority to specifically alter old filing numbers from before. Therefore, all the filings obtained are from 2005, 2006, and 2007.

They use this method to delete the domain names inside the old filing numbers and add your domain name. Thus, it only takes a few minutes. They won't give you the password; they'll only let you check.

When you query at the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology, the filing status shows as "already filed," but its true status is 12 (12 indicates re-review of the filing, meaning after your filing is successful and additional information is added, it needs to be reviewed again.) The re-review has a high possibility of not passing.

Identification method: (If any of the following points are met, it's a scam filing)

1. This kind of filing has a filing number that isn't from this year, and the name of the principal filer belongs to someone else.

2. With this kind of filing, they will never provide you with an account or password because if they did, you would see the words "re-review" in the filing information.

3. This kind of filing does not have a certificate download. Some scammers claim they can provide a certificate, but actually, they just send you a random bazs.cert file because this file cannot be opened to view the information, and the filenames are all the same, so they randomly send you one.

Case 2: One filing number for multiple domains (N webmasters share one filing number)

This kind of filing is similar to what was mentioned above, but with slightly less authority. It can only add domains. In this kind of filing, all the domains are under one filing number, so if one website encounters problems, it will affect all the websites involved.

Vulnerabilities abound:

Same as above: This kind of filing does not have an account or password, fake certificates, filing numbers aren't from this year, and the filing principal is always someone else's name.
