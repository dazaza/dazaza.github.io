Tragedy can never "transcend"

by ai9ewud2q on 2012-03-06 16:03:16

History can be traversed, but tragedies never can. A sixth-grade elementary school student, influenced by [time travel] themes, unable to bear the burden of study and mental pressure, finally dressed in [Tang costume] and time-traveled to the [Tang Dynasty]. This is the product of time travel, and this is the sorrow of education. A modern version of a time-travel drama has caused a sixth-grade elementary school student to enact it with his own life.

We all know the fact that today's children's schoolbags are getting heavier and heavier. One day they will crush our children. Schools consider the rate of advancement, teachers consider performance evaluations, and parents consider report cards. Everything is considered for you. Then, what else can children think about?

Apart from the essential content in class, the tasks after class will not be any lighter. Children have become completely numb; they no longer know why they are doing these things or what they themselves are doing.

In the morning, looking at the children's sleepy faces makes one heartache. In the evening, watching the children drag their heavy steps leaves one speechless. Wasn't the original intention of education supposed to make the children so painful? I don't understand.

A sixth-grade elementary school student should be at an age where they kind of understand but not fully. What spiritual nourishment has our society provided for them? Apart from online games, television has become the main form of entertainment for children. We do not know how today's online games have affected the hearts of children. Television, this popular and beloved public medium, most influences and infects our children.

[Time-travel dramas] objectively play a role in allowing children to traverse history. Escaping reality, not daring to face the current situation, leaving oneself in history. Entrusting one's ideals and future in something intangible. [Time-travel dramas] traverse history, and at the same time traverse the tender minds of children. I wonder what the creators of [time-travel dramas] were thinking?

Nowadays, in this society, not only adults but even children feel immense pressure. They need release, they need venting, yet they suffer from not finding a place to do so. Adults hustle for life and housing, while children endure torment for studies and extra classes. As cultural workers, we should provide more inspiring works for this society, offering enlightenment to both adults and children. Let them truly experience the hardship and joy of striving upward.

Some people question why relevant departments would give such green lights to these [time-travel dramas]? Whom does our television serve? Is the ultimate goal of TV dramas to make money or to educate? The content of time-travel dramas has already transcended history, yet it can never transcend the tragedy of a sixth-grade child committing suicide by jumping from a building. Fortunately, relevant authorities have issued notices to stop the production of [time-travel dramas]. Real society does not need time travel, and ordinary people's lives need it even less. Let time-travel dramas stay far away from you, me, and him.
