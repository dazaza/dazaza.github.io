During last weekend's FA Cup match between Chelsea and Birmingham, the song "Jose Mourinho" occasionally rang out in the Stamford Bridge stadium. It was clearly sung by fans to current head coach Andre Villas-Boas. The young Portuguese coach has become an unwelcome figure here, and the fans can't wait for him to leave. Worse still, even the players seem to no longer trust Boas now. ESPN captured the scene of Chelsea stars holding a "mini-meeting" during halftime, which was seen by the media as a harbinger of the players' "rebellion." Although Boas denied this incident, ESPN also issued an apology, it is not hard to see that the Portuguese man is having a tough time.

In the match against Birmingham, Chelsea performed very poorly in the first half, being scored against by their opponents who took control of the game as visiting team. Before the start of the second half, ESPN's broadcast captured scenes inside the player tunnel, showing Didier Drogba rallying the players after coming on as a substitute for Fernando Torres, apparently "issuing orders." This ESPN footage carried a clear implication, seemingly hinting that the players were sidelining Boas. Such interpretation enraged Boas, who said in an interview with the Daily Mail: "This is completely absurd and makes no sense at all. I even gave an exclusive interview to ESPN before, if I had known such things would happen, I would never have done it. The claim that Drogba held a players' meeting is total nonsense."

Despite facing a serious crisis of confidence, Boas will not allow his authority to be challenged in any way. Facing ESPN's report, his stance was extremely firm, forcing ESPN to later apologize to Boas personally for their baseless claims. Although he successfully silenced the media, Boas had no response to the fans' doubts. In the face of deafening boos from the fans during the match, Boas appeared quite helpless: "I never comment on the reactions of the fans."

Without support from the fans, and with lackluster performances from the players, Boas can only rely on the backing of owner Roman Abramovich now. Abramovich has always been impatient with his coaches, but towards Boas, whom he personally brought over from Porto, Abramovich has shown unusual tolerance. Previously, the public generally predicted that Abramovich’s patience has limits. If Boas couldn't seize the lifeline of Champions League success, he might get sacked at any time. However, Boas’ pre-match statement shocked the media again — even if they lose to their Champions League opponent Napoli, his position won't be in any danger. Boas said in an interview with the Daily Mail: "This game will have no impact on my future. It's not something I decide, you need to ask the right person about this question."

Despite facing various doubts, Boas remains confident in himself and the team. Their opponent in the Champions League round of 16, Napoli, is absolutely not to be underestimated. However, Boas believes that if they beat this strong rival, Chelsea definitely has a chance to win the Champions League title. Boas told the Sun: "The game against Napoli will be a huge challenge. Everyone knows that Napoli eliminated Manchester City from the group of death, but I think Chelsea has a chance to defeat them. If we can get through this hurdle and draw a favorable opponent in the next knockout stage, then we will have a great chance. The Champions League trophy is something everyone wants to win. The preparation and planning for Champions League matches are entirely different forms, and the style of play will be completely different too. We need to enhance our competitiveness and play brilliant games."
