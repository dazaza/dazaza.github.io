Last year, 90% of Chinese stock investors lost an average of 40,000 yuan each.

by aswee25 on 2012-03-09 16:55:05

Last year, nine out of ten Chinese stock investors lost an average of 40,000 yuan each, while institutional investment losses exceeded 40 billion yuan. This morning, the fifth session of the 11th National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference (CPPCC) held its first plenary speech. Feng Peien, standing committee member of the CPPCC National Committee and former vice chairman of the Central Committee of Jiusan Society, said that huge losses can easily provoke public resentment. He suggested linking the scale of financing and refinancing by listed companies, restructuring plans, operator compensation, and related preferential policies closely to cash dividend regulations, forcing listed companies to fulfill their dividend obligations. In the morning, a total of 16 CPPCC members gave speeches.

The income gap between urban and rural residents is widening.

Suggestion: Reduce the proportion of indirect taxes and increase the proportion of direct taxes.

In 2000, the ratio of per capita income between urban and rural residents in our country was 2.85:1, reaching 3.45:1 in 2010. The widening income gap among the national population has become an increasingly prominent structural imbalance issue in the distribution sector. For this, Liu Kugu, CPPCC National Committee member, former vice president of the China Development Bank, deputy director of the Academic Committee of the China International Economic Exchange Center, and chairman of the China Microfinance Institution Coalition, suggested increasing the proportion of direct taxes to narrow the income gap among residents.

He recommended first reducing the proportion of indirect taxes and increasing the share of the household sector. We should take advantage of the reform from business tax to value-added tax to lower the proportion of indirect taxes and reduce the tax burden on ordinary people.

Additionally, we can narrow the income gap among residents by increasing the proportion of direct taxes. We should expand the coverage of personal income tax based on quickly realizing deductions for basic living expenses at the family unit level. Increase the rate differential spacing, lower the tax rate for the middle-income group. Appropriately lower the stock stamp tax rate at the right time, while restoring the collection of income tax on short-term stock transfer income and allowing certain deductions. Play the role of property tax in adjusting the gap in residents' assets and asset-based income.

We should also implement information sharing. We need to soon realize an information-sharing system with conditions, constraints, and controllability among taxation, public security, industry and commerce, social security, housing, finance, and other departments, laying a technical foundation for fiscal and taxation reforms and improving government efficiency.

To compete for GDP, there is serious duplication of construction in local areas.

Suggestion: Do not use local GDP, investment, and other indicators as performance evaluation criteria for local governments.

A prominent feature of the previous stage of China's economic development was administrative dominance by the government. This operational method could concentrate various resources to boost GDP, but in the new development stage, this operational method will lead to an "inseparable" relationship between the government and the market, and a "chaotic" relationship between the central and local governments. Regarding this, Song Xiaowu, CPPCC National Committee member and former deputy director of the State Council's Northeast Revitalization Office, pointed out that we should quickly define the boundaries between the government and the market, and play the fundamental role of the market in allocating resources.

He said that the competition among local governments has achieved great success, but it has also caused problems such as duplicate construction, overcapacity, resource waste, and environmental pollution. More seriously, local governments have become the main players in resource allocation, blurring the boundaries between the government and the market, and providing a broad platform for money-power transactions.

Taking the GDP growth indicator in the "12th Five-Year Plan" as an example, the central government expects 7%, while the weighted average of provinces, cities, and regions reaches 10.5%, which is 50% higher than the central government's target. Such a situation is common, and the responsibility does not lie with the localities, but rather with the fact that the relevant mechanisms and systems do not meet the requirements for transitioning development methods. This also indicates that it is necessary to reform these mechanisms and systems.

He said that defining the boundaries between the government, including both the central and local governments, and the market involves many fields such as administrative systems, fiscal and taxation systems, foreign trade systems, land systems, distribution systems, price mechanisms, and social management. It is suggested to use the equalization of basic public services and the basic completion of public facilities as the main indicators to measure whether provinces, cities, and regions are developing in a coordinated manner, quickly cancel local GDP, investment, and other planning indicators, and no longer use these economic indicators as performance evaluation criteria for local governments, which will help ensure the uniformity of national orders and markets.

It is suggested to gradually concentrate the authority of basic public services appropriately and timely to the central government, where the central government will coordinate and balance the standards of basic public services in various places, and establish a unified national funding guarantee mechanism for basic public services.

Being the first in manufacturing does not necessarily mean being a strong industrial power.

Suggestion: To accelerate industrial transformation and upgrading, it is key to scientifically and reasonably determine the development speed.

In 2010, China became the world's largest manufacturing country. However, it is "big but not strong." Li Yizhong, CPPCC National Committee member, deputy director of the Economic Committee of the 11th CPPCC National Committee, and former minister of the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology, said that currently, R&D investment in China's industry is insufficient, with R&D funds for large-scale industrial enterprises only accounting for 0.69% of operating revenue, while foreign large companies average between 2% to 3%. Independent innovation is lacking, with many technologies, materials, components, and complete sets of equipment still relying on imports. Some industries have low industrial concentration, with about 20% of outdated production capacity and lack of well-known brands. Generally speaking, we are still in the mid-to-low end of the global value chain.

Due to inadequate transformation and upgrading, resource and environmental issues have become difficult to resolve. Li Yizhong said that in 2011, China's crude oil import dependence exceeded 56%, iron ore imports reached 680 million tons, accounting for more than 60% of the world trade volume. Primary energy consumption reached approximately 3.5 billion tons of standard coal, with a growth rate of 7%, which is still high. The energy consumption per unit of GDP is three to four times that of developed countries; sulfur dioxide, carbon dioxide emissions, and chemical oxygen demand rank among the highest in the world.

He said that to accelerate industrial transformation and upgrading, it is crucial to scientifically and reasonably determine the development speed. A low speed is detrimental to improving people's livelihoods and employment, affecting social stability; too high a speed makes it difficult for resources and the environment to sustain, which is not conducive to changing the development model. Moderately slowing down the growth rate allows more effort to be placed on eliminating outdated production capacities, conserving energy, reducing emissions, and developing strategic emerging industries.

It is necessary to optimize the industrial layout, control overcapacity, and strictly control "two-high" projects. Orderly develop strategic emerging industries and productive service industries, cultivating new growth points.

More human, material, and financial resources should be tilted towards technological renovation and the real economy.

Insufficient financial innovation may lead to risks.

Suggestion: Actively broaden the direct financing channels for enterprises.

Yang Kaisheng, CPPCC National Committee member, president, vice-chairman, and second secretary of the Party Committee of the Industrial and Commercial Bank of China, said that slowing down the pace of financial innovation due to the recent international financial crisis is like throwing out the baby with the bathwater. Because insufficient financial innovation may lead to risks.

In 2011, loans from all types of financial institutions in our country accounted for about 75% of the scale of social financing, while corporate bonds and stock financing accounted for only 14%. The imbalance between direct and indirect financing structures is very obvious.

"This situation where various enterprises mainly rely on bank loan support reflects that our financial innovation is still insufficient, the development level of the capital market is still low, and the number of enterprise financing tools is still too few," Yang Kaisheng said. If we do not accelerate innovation and do not try to introduce more financing and investment tools, it will cause a large amount of credit risk to continuously accumulate in the banking system, which is not conducive to the sound operation of banks and will affect the sustainable development of the entire economy.

He suggested actively broadening the direct financing channels for enterprises. Resolutely solve the problem of multi-departmental approval and regulation for enterprise bond issuance, expanding the scale of corporate bond issuance. Allow bank loans to be securitized, through securitization enabling bank loans to be transferred and traded, thereby preventing the total amount of bank assets from growing indefinitely and having sustainable credit issuance capabilities. As for some potential risks involved, they can be controlled through technical design.

Not less than a hundred entrepreneurs in Wenzhou have "run away."

Suggestion: Open up channels for private capital to enter the financial sector.

"A Wenzhou enterprise with over a thousand workers earns just one million yuan in profit after hard work and meticulous calculation in a year, while the wife of the enterprise owner buys 10 houses in Shanghai and effortlessly gains 30 million yuan in profits eight years later." The above example given by Hu Chengzhong, CPPCC National Committee member and standing member of the All-China Federation of Industry and Commerce, during his speech at the conference was heart-wrenching. He said that the development crisis faced by small and medium-sized enterprises has reached a critical stage of survival or death.

He said that since April last year, not less than a hundred Wenzhou entrepreneurs have "run away" due to broken capital chains. According to data published by the Zhejiang Provincial Bureau of Statistics, in the first half of last year, 14,447 small and medium-sized enterprises in Zhejiang Province went bankrupt and were deregistered. He suggested that financial institutions "release water to nurture fish," and advance and retreat together with enterprises. The interest spread of our country's banks exceeds 3%, much higher than the average level in Western countries, and the per capita net profit is more than 12 times that of industrial enterprises. Banks need to lower loan interest rates, control the floating space, not force the bundling of wealth management products and mandatory retention of deposit balances, optimize the loan structure, increase the proportion of medium- and long-term loans, and support the technical renovation and upgrading of the manufacturing industry.

He suggested breaking industry monopolies and opening up private banks. Private manufacturing enterprises have narrow financing channels and narrow investment channels, leading to a narrow survival and development space. Government departments need to quickly formulate detailed implementation rules, broaden the industrial development space for manufacturing enterprises, and open up channels for private capital to enter the financial sector.

"Currently, it is estimated that there are several trillion yuan of private capital nationwide that have not been well utilized for the development of the real economy, but are instead being used for real estate speculation, coal speculation, precious metals speculation, and art and antiques speculation." He said that we might as well develop private banks and specialized loan institutions, "reorganize" private capital, solve the financing difficulties of small and medium-sized private enterprises, and lower loan interest rates through competition, forming a virtuous interaction and common development between the development of the real economy and private capital.

Last year, the Shanghai and Shenzhen stock markets led the global stock market decline.

Suggestion: Compel listed companies to fulfill their dividend obligations.

Last year, the Shanghai and Shenzhen stock markets fell by 21% and 28% respectively, leading the global stock market decline. Out of 50 million Chinese stock investors, 87% suffered losses, with an average loss of 40,000 yuan per person, and institutional investment losses exceeded 40 billion yuan. Feng Peien, standing committee member of the CPPCC National Committee and former vice chairman of the Central Committee of Jiusan Society, said that huge losses can easily provoke public resentment, and measures to save the market must quickly address institutional defects through reform.

In 20 years, the total amount of cash dividends given to stock investors in the A-share market only accounts for 17% of the total financing, which is lower than the transaction costs of stock investors, so investors can only profit from the price fluctuations of stocks in the secondary market.

Feng Peien suggested revising relevant laws and regulations, requiring listed companies to clearly state return and cash dividend plans in their prospectuses, linking the scale of financing and refinancing by listed companies, restructuring plans, operator compensation, and related preferential policies closely to cash dividend regulations, compelling listed companies to fulfill their dividend obligations.

At the same time, implement a strict delisting system to optimize the allocation of stock market resources. The NASDAQ stock market reduced the number of listed companies by 13.08% over three years, while in 21 years, the proportion of delisted companies in China's stock market only accounts for 1.82% of the total number of listed companies.

In 2011, 1,264 executives resigned or left their positions in the A-share market, representing a 68% increase compared to the same period the previous year. Executive resignations and cashing out hinder steady stock market development. Feng Peien suggested formulating relevant laws stipulating that company directors, supervisors, and executives may not transfer more than 10% of the total shares they hold in their respective companies annually during their tenure; executives must disclose relevant information two months before selling their shares; prohibit company directors, supervisors, presidents, and chairmen from taking jobs in listed companies within three years after resigning and cashing out and from taking jobs in non-listed companies within one year; impose a high capital gains tax on executives of listed companies who sell their shares and cash out; directors, supervisors, and executives may not transfer shares in their respective companies within one year after leaving their positions.

Land sale revenue needs proper management.

Suggestion: Audit the use of land sale revenue over the past five or ten years.

In 2010, the total proceeds from land sales across the country amounted to 2.7 trillion yuan, with Beijing contributing 163.85 billion yuan and Shanghai 152.7 billion yuan. Over the five-year period from 2006 to 2010, the total land sale revenue across the country reached over 7 trillion yuan. Is it time to properly manage the domestic land market "feast"? This morning, Lu Budong, CPPCC National Committee member, member of the Central Committee of the China Democratic建国Party, and deputy head of the Zhejiang Provincial Committee, spoke directly about how to better utilize the enormous "land sale" revenue.

Lu Budong pointed out that due to the lack of effective supervision over the use of land sale revenue, it leads to arbitrary usage without necessary principles. For example, a municipal government agreed to prioritize the increment income from the development of two plots of land over the next ten years for repaying the capital of a certain state-owned enterprise debt.

Secondly, corruption cases are frequent. An audit by the National Audit Office revealed that among the 84.826 billion yuan of non-tax revenue that should have been but was not included in budget management in 10 provincial levels, 23 municipal levels, and 41 counties, land sale revenue reached 62.642 billion yuan.

More importantly, this can be detrimental to the sustainable development of cities, causing a "temporary prosperity at the expense of the future." Land is generally sold for 40 to 70 years, and these future incomes are used for current constructions. However, land will eventually run out, and when that day comes, with houses sold, factories sold, and land sold, what else can we sell?

He suggested auditing the use of land sale revenue over the past five or ten years. After more than 20 years of land sales, how much has the cumulative land sale revenue been? Where has it been spent? Is the usage reasonable? How much has been spent on the general public? What percentage has been used according to the notification issued by the General Office of the State Council? These cannot remain unclear accounts but should be clearly communicated to society and the public.
