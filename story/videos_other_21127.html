Don't expose your privacy.

by shooter on 2007-06-01 20:02:42

After going online, browsers always retain some traces of internet usage, such as historical links and form-filling content. These can expose personal privacy online, and may even allow ill-intentioned people to steal our email accounts and other personal information through this private data. Therefore, for those who go online, especially in public places, it is essential not to ignore deleting the historical links and form-filling content in the browser after using the internet.

1. Clearing Internet Traces with Browsers

1.1 IE - The Veteran

IE is a browser used by many users, although its security has always been questioned, its ability to clear internet privacy is still very strong. Click on the menu bar's "Tools → Internet Options → General" tab, then click on the "Clear History", "Delete Files", and "Delete Cookies" buttons. Deleting these contents is not enough because the browser also saves other internet usage information, such as form information left when filling out forms online. Click on the "Content" tab, then the "AutoComplete" button. Next, click on the "Clear Forms" and "Clear Passwords" buttons, and uncheck all checkboxes before "Web Address", "Forms", and "Usernames and Passwords on Forms". This way, there will be no trace of your internet activity.

1.2 Firefox - The Newcomer

Nowadays, more and more people are using FIREFOX. Compared to the veteran IE, it might have stronger security features. Specific functions won't be discussed much here; we believe friends using Firefox Browser already know them. Click on the "Tools → Clear Browsing Data" command in the menu bar, then select the items you want to clean up in the pop-up menu, and click the "OK" button. It's quite simple.

1.3 Maxthon - Powerful Functionality

Maxthon (formerly known as MyIE2) became famous due to its powerful performance and support for numerous plugins, winning the hearts of many users. By clicking on the "Tools → Clear Browsing History" command in the menu bar, you can easily clean up internet privacy. Moreover, Maxthon allows you to automatically clear internet privacy by checking all checkboxes under the "Options → Maxthon Options → General → Exit" tab every time you close Maxthon, making it very convenient.

1.4 GreenBrowser - Twin Brother of Maxthon

GreenBrowser attracts a large number of users due to its powerful functionality comparable to Maxthon. Its ability to handle internet privacy is also very significant. Click on the "Tools → Clear History Information" command in the menu bar, then select the options you want to clear, such as clearing forms or use the "Clear All" command to remove all visit records.

1.5 Tencent TT - Bundled with QQ

Unlike IE and Firefox, Tencent TT browser download has become the preferred browser for many friends because it is bundled with QQ. After several version updates, TT's privacy-clearing function has grown stronger. Click on the "Tools → Clear Browsing History" command in the menu bar, then select the items you want to clean up in the pop-up menu, and finally click the "OK" button.

2. Other Cleaning Techniques

2.1 Manual Deletion Method

Method One: Click on "Start → Run", type regedit to open the registry, and find [HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\Microsoft\Internet Explorer\TypedUrls]. This main key contains the URLs used in the browser address bar. Just delete the keys corresponding to the URLs you don't want others to see. The advantage of this method is that it allows selective deletion of URLs.

Every time we input keywords in the search box, they are recorded. As long as you double-click the search text box, you can see what we've searched for. The simplest way to delete these keywords is to select a row of keywords and press the Del key on the keyboard to delete them effortlessly.

Method Two: The form information left on web pages is saved differently depending on the browser used. Simply deleting the files where each browser saves this information will suffice. For example, Firefox browser download encrypts form data and saves it in the FormData.ini file under the Config folder in the root directory; Tencent TT also encrypts and saves form data in the FormData.ini file in its root directory; while GreenBrowser saves form data in the FormData.ini file in its root directory. However, worryingly, the forms are not encrypted, and passwords are directly displayed in public.

2.2 Using Other Tools for Clearing

SuperCleaner is an all-in-one tool for clearing useless junk files from the disk and has the ability to clear Internet privacy. Download SuperCleaner, install it, and launch it. Click on the "Internet Privacy" item, check the "Web browsing history", "Clear all entered URLs", "Clear AutoComplete forms", and "Clear AutoComplete passwords" checkboxes, and finally click the "Execute" button. Then open IE, and you'll find that the dropdown URLs, history, and forms are all empty. It is recommended to also check "Clear cache" and "Remove selected Cookies", which will comprehensively protect your personal privacy. This is a good choice for those who cannot use the registry in internet cafes.
