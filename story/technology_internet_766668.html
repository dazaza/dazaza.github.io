Google's health tech company Calico gets support from its "long-time rival" Apple

by anonymous on 2013-09-24 21:20:26

The two founders of Google, Brin and Page, have a strong personal interest in the medical and health field. On September 18th, the founders of Google turned their interest in health research into action by announcing the establishment of a company named Calico for conducting research. In this project, Google will also collaborate with its long-time rival Apple.

The head of Calico will be Art Levinson, the current chairman of Apple and the former CEO of biotech company Genentech.

Google will not interfere with the daily operations of the company. The main task of Calico is to study the mental and physical problems caused by aging in humans, as well as life-threatening diseases.

In a blog post on Wednesday, Page stated that the establishment of the Calico company is a "long-term bet." Google believes that with the right research team, they can achieve research results within a reasonable timeframe.

Google did not disclose more details about the company, such as its location, how many researchers it will hire, or what role Page plays in the company.

An informed source revealed that Google's investment in Calico is "very substantial," allowing the company to conduct research on multiple different projects simultaneously.

Since Page became the CEO of Google in 2011, Google has allocated more resources and effort to the research and development of non-mainstream projects, including driverless cars, hot air balloons providing internet services to underdeveloped areas, Google Glass and other wearable computing devices. In his blog post on Wednesday, Page also mentioned that the establishment of the health research company seems to deviate from the services Google currently provides.

Page said that he hopes people won't be surprised by Google's investments in some strange fields. He emphasized that compared to Google's main business, these non-mainstream investments are very small in scale.

It was reported that since Google's research on "strange projects" has not interfered with its primary businesses such as online search and internet advertising, Wall Street generally tolerates Google's behavior.

It has been known that the two founders of Google, Page and Brin, have always been interested in the health care field. In May, Page announced that he suffers from a rare neurological disease that hinders vocal cord movement, making it impossible for him to give speeches in public. Page also mentioned that he was diagnosed with Hashimoto's thyroiditis, but this condition has not yet affected his life.

The other founder, Brin, revealed that his probability of developing Parkinson's syndrome is higher than that of ordinary people. However, through strict dietary and exercise control, Brin believes that the chance of getting the disease can be reduced from 50% to 13%.

It was reported that Brin's wife founded a genetic technology company called 23andMe, where Brin discovered the aforementioned situation during a test. It is understood that Google also invested in this company.

Prior to this, Google had ventured into the health sector. Google once launched the "Google Health" service, which allowed netizens to store their medical records online. However, after three years of operation, the service was shut down in 2011. At the time, Page stated that the service failed to establish a connection with the general public.

Data shows that Art Levinson, the head of Calico, besides serving as the chairman of Apple, is also a director of the well-known pharmaceutical company Roche.

Apple CEO Tim Cook expressed his congratulations and expectations for the establishment of this health company in a statement, saying that for many friends and families, life is too short and the quality of life decreases. Levinson has an innovative spirit, and no one is more suitable to lead a health tech company than him. "I look forward to seeing the results of the research and development," he said.
