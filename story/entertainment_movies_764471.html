Even though it's just rehearsal footage, all of Michael Jackson's fans and audience can still appreciate the seriousness and professionalism of the King of Pop, get a glimpse behind the scenes of the entire London concert and its ingenious planning, and enjoy top-notch audio-visual experience from his polished singing, improved dance moves, and newly shot high-definition music videos.

This film is not boastful, but real; not ordinary, but extraordinary; not sad, but awe-inspiring. Why is he the King? Why does he have magical power? Why is he a spiritual genius? Why has he profoundly influenced the world? Why is he the "greatest entertainer of all time" in the eyes of Motown founder Berry Gordy? After watching the movie, you will find the answers.

Behind-the-scenes Production:

The Chronicler of the King's Last Journey

The most shocking news in the entertainment industry in the summer of 2009 was none other than the passing of Michael Jackson. For Kenny Ortega, the director of "This Is It", his feelings were quite complex. He was originally the overall director of Michael Jackson's farewell concert, but due to the unexpected death of the King, he reluctantly became the director of this documentary. Speaking about this documentary that records the last traces of the King's life, Kenny Ortega said sorrowfully: "This documentary should not be what it is now... During the preparation period for the concert, Michael Jackson had already planned to record the entire process of the concert step by step, because at that time Michael Jackson no longer intended to produce such a large-scale concert. At that time, he told me, 'I'm too tired, I still have many things to do, I think I've reached a stage in my music career, I need to focus on other things as wonderful as music.' So we started recording the production process of this concert with the intention of making a complete record of Michael Jackson's last grand performance on stage. And indeed, I did not expect that this documentary would eventually replace the concert."

For Michael Jackson's fans, being able to see their idols so completely on the big screen, and with such special significance, made all aspects of "This Is It" a focal point. Director Kenny Ortega naturally felt a heavy responsibility: "Michael Jackson spent several months rehearsing for the concert before his death, leaving behind hundreds of hours of material. Our first task was to select the most representative content from it and present it to everyone. Editing these contents was a very difficult task. I wanted to show every scene to everyone, but I knew that was impossible. I had to condense hundreds of hours into less than two hours. So we finally decided that the main thread of the film would be focused on Michael Jackson's unfinished concert before his death, concentrating on showing various preparations Michael Jackson made for this concert. I hope this will gain the understanding of the fans."

Anticipation from Fans Around the World

Since the announcement of the release of the film, it has become the focus of media attention. Kenny Ortega said: "The production period of this movie was very tight, because initially all our preparations were for Michael Jackson's concert. After the unfortunate incident happened, our work was completely disrupted. There were many plans at the time, and some companies even prepared to shoot a biopic of Michael Jackson, but in the end, we decided to focus on the concert and tell the story of how he prepared for the entire concert. Initially, they wanted to release the film on Jackson's birthday, August 29th. I said 'that's impossible', then they set it for the Halloween season, which was fine for me. It happens to be Michael's favorite holiday."

Regarding the transformation of the unfulfilled farewell performance into a documentary shown in cinemas, Randy Phillips, president of AEG Entertainment, the concert organizer, said it was still worth looking forward to: "Although for most people, they want to see Michael Jackson on the concert stage, it is indeed regrettable that this cannot happen now. But fortunately, we have preserved as much as possible the efforts he put into preparing for this concert, and these contents are equally worthy of attention from his fans. This concert has special significance for Michael Jackson, as it was his last effort to prepare such a grand concert. From another perspective, we can understand from the documentary 'This Is It' how Michael Jackson produced this concert, and we can also see the dances and songs he prepared for the concert in the documentary. All these demonstrate the precious value of 'This Is It'."

Restoring the Reputation of the King of Pop

Will "This Is It" satisfy Michael Jackson's fans? And will it be criticized by critics as purely a money-making fan service that fails to qualify as a proper documentary? In response to this question, director Kenny Ortega specifically stated: "Many people have expressed similar doubts to me, as they all care about what level 'This Is It' will achieve. For me, 'This Is It' is simple, it is a documentary showing how seriously and passionately Michael Jackson prepared for his own concert. My core idea during production was just this. But I know there are more viewers who are not Michael Jackson fans who will watch this documentary, so I must explain in the documentary why Michael Jackson is so captivating?"

"Many people have misconceptions about Michael Jackson," Kenny Ortega continued, "Over the years, people have seen many news stories about Michael Jackson, many of which are irresponsible media reports, and Michael Jackson has never seriously refuted them. 'This Is It' has a tough mission, it must restore Michael Jackson's reputation, he is a great person who deserves respect, not baseless criticism. Therefore, 'This Is It' includes some segments explaining Michael Jackson's daily life, and I believe viewers who watch this movie will have a fair judgment on him."
