Several earthquake sites across the country have applied for national A-class scenic spots

by king2z9o on 2012-02-24 11:38:29

The news that Wenchuan is striving to create a national 5A-level tourist attraction has sparked endless disputes from all sides. During the interview, it was discovered that Wenchuan is not the only place applying for an A-level scenic area on earthquake ruins; the Tangshan Earthquake Site Park also plans to apply for a national 4A-level scenic area. In response, tourism experts believe that if considering the local economy and public life, developing tourism is one of the methods. However, local departments should consider comprehensively and conduct specific research.

Wenchuan Epicenter Strives for 5A Scenic Area

According to reports from Sichuan media, Wenchuan County recently launched the task of creating the [Wenchuan Yingxiu 512 Earthquake Epicenter Memorial Site] as a national 5A-level tourist attraction. It is understood that after three years of reconstruction, Wenchuan has already established four national 4A-level tourist attractions. This time, the creation of the [Wenchuan Yingxiu 512 Earthquake Epicenter Memorial Site] as a national 5A-level tourist attraction combines four major sectors: Yingxiu exhibitions, Shimmo leisure, Sanjiang vacations, and Xuan Kou industrial displays, achieving a unity of economic, social, and ecological benefits.

Deputy Dean Feng Dongming of the Tourism College at Guangzhou Union University introduced that it would be unlikely for the [Wenchuan Yingxiu 512 Earthquake Epicenter Memorial Site] to directly apply for a 5A scenic area because according to the rules of the National Tourism Administration, 5A-level tourist attractions are selected from 4A-level ones. Only those announced as 4A-level tourist attractions for over a year can apply for 5A status.

This revelation has sparked heated discussions among netizens who largely oppose the establishment of a 5A scenic area in the epicenter. Some netizens commented, "Are we supposed to visit a 5A-level tomb with joy?" Others pointed out that without perfect tourism facilities, contingency plans, or strategies, heritage sites shouldn't be arbitrarily developed for tourism purposes.

In response to the doubts raised by netizens, Deputy Minister of Publicity of Wenchuan County, Xie Lushuang, stated in an interview with the media that after the earthquake, farmland in Wenchuan County was lost, and enterprises such as the county cement factory and Minjiang Silicon Industry were relocated to Maocounty and Jintang County. Without farmland and job opportunities, "developing tourism is the only way forward." She also indicated that the scenic area will not charge admission fees once completed.

Tangshan Earthquake Site Plans to Apply for 4A Status

Actually, Tangshan is not the only place applying for A-level scenic areas on earthquake ruins. It was learned yesterday that the Tangshan Great Earthquake Site Park has submitted relevant materials and plans to apply for national 4A-level scenic area status.

Construction of the Tangshan Earthquake Site Memorial Park began in April 2008 and was initially completed and opened to the public in July 2008. It is the world's first earthquake site park themed around "memorial." The park is divided into several zones including the earthquake site zone, memorial water zone, memorial forest zone, and memorial square zone. Inside the park, there is an earthquake science museum renovated from old factory buildings following traditional design principles, a 396-meter-long glass memorial wall serving as a gravestone for the 240,000 victims of the earthquake, inscribed with their names.

During the premiere of Feng Xiaogang's (Weibo) movie "Tangshan Earthquake," the creators paid their respects individually at the memorial wall, further enhancing the park's reputation. Relevant staff members of the site park introduced that the annual visitor volume this year is approximately 600,000 people, and the entire park operates free of charge.

Additionally, the Beichuan Old Town earthquake site has passed the evaluation conducted by the 4A-level scenic area assessment expert group of the National Tourism Administration. Similarly rated as a 4A-level scenic area is the Qingchuan Donghekou Earthquake Site Park in Guangyuan City. The post-disaster reconstruction of Mianzhu City in Deyang into a New Year painting village is also being developed according to 4A-level scenic area standards.

Expert Opinions

Benefits of Applying for A-Level Status on Earthquake Sites

Deputy Director Liu Deqian of the Chinese Academy of Sciences Tourism Research Center stated that a national 5A-level scenic area serves as a symbol of quality for a city or region, bringing more spiritual significance to local residents than economic benefits. Moreover, A-level scenic areas can also drive the development of other local industries, such as cultural and public service industries. Liu Deqian believes that such scenic areas have educational and scientific significance, which can cultivate emotions and love among the public in the region. As for whether financial allocations will follow successful applications, Liu Deqian indicated that it depends on the support level of the local government. If they value it, the local government will provide some economic support.

Need for Comprehensive and Detailed Planning

Deputy Dean Feng Dongming of the Tourism College at Guangzhou Union University believed that the reason why netizens question Wenchuan's application for a 5A scenic area is mainly due to the term "scenic area" easily causing misunderstandings when applied to earthquake ruins. "If using 'heritage park,' people might find it easier to accept. We can learn from the practices of the Tangshan Earthquake Heritage Park, carefully plan and design so that the public can well appreciate and mourn."

Liu Deqian believes that to apply for an A-level scenic area on earthquake ruins, comprehensive and detailed planning must be made from the perspective of the entire region, balancing local industrial development and livelihood sources, making specific plans based on specific situations.
