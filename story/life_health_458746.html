In fact, the discovery that psychogenic factors can cause disease is not new. Traditional Chinese Medicine had extremely rich records of this over two thousand years ago. The book "Huangdi Neijing" pointed out that changes in emotions can make people sick. "Emotions" refer to "seven feelings" and "five aspirations." Emotions are not isolated; they have a material basis. Ancients believed that humans have five zang-organs transforming into five qi, with joy, anger, sorrow, worry, and fear, known as the five aspirations. The seven feelings are joy, anger, worry, thoughtfulness, sorrow, fear, and shock. Changes in emotions are closely related to the internal organs, with specific emotions corresponding to specific organs: the heart governs joy, the liver governs anger, the spleen governs thoughtfulness, the lungs govern sorrow, and the kidneys govern fear. As the saying goes: "Intense anger harms the liver and causes fire to rise, lung diseases fear additional sorrow, excessive contemplation injures the spleen and stomach, fear harms the kidneys leading to involuntary urination, excessive joy disrupts the qi leaving the heart without control, severe pain can lead to death."

In fact, all these factors influence an individual's emotions, and over time, may lead to cancerous changes. Many studies have been conducted on the specific mechanisms of carcinogenesis. For instance, in the past few decades, a new research field called Psychoneuroimmunology (PNI) has developed rapidly. It consists of three parts: psychology, neurology, and immunology. This field studies the connection between a person's emotional state, the central nervous system, and the immune system. Although the term PNI does not directly mention the endocrine system, its research often involves endocrine hormones. Therefore, some scientists believe that this new research field should be more accurately named "Neuropsychiatric Endocrine Immunology." The basic theory of PNI is that the brain can regulate immune function, and the functions of the immune system can change due to neurological and physiological stress. When mental emotions experience persistent abnormal fluctuations, it stimulates the nervous system, prompting it to continuously release various substances such as neurotransmitters, neurohormones, and neuropeptides. These mediators can directly interact with receptors on lymphocytes, changing or affecting immune cells, thereby influencing the formation and development of tumors.
