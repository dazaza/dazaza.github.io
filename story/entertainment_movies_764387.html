Synopsis:

In the 18th century, in Louisiana. After losing his wife and daughter, plantation owner Louis is consumed by grief. He encounters the vampire Lestat, who transforms him into a vampire through magic. With a kind heart, Louis refuses to kill humans for blood and survives by drinking animal blood. Lestat, however, is determined to teach Louis to become a true vampire.

In New Orleans, Louis meets the young girl Claudia, who has lost her family to an epidemic. Unable to resist temptation, Louis finally drinks Claudia’s blood. Despite Louis’ objections, Lestat turns Claudia into a vampire as well. The three of them form an unusual family.

Time flies, decades pass. Claudia begins to resent Lestat for depriving her of the ability to grow up. To leave Lestat and be with Louis, she tricks Lestat into drinking the blood of a dead person, stabs him, and throws him into a swamp. When Louis and Claudia are about to head to Paris, the disfigured Lestat suddenly appears. In the ensuing fight, Louis grabs an oil lamp and throws it at Lestat, engulfing him in flames. Amidst the roaring fire that consumes half the city, Louis and Claudia board a ship bound for Paris.

In Paris, they live a life that resembles both father-daughter and lovers. They encounter others of their kind — a group of vampires led by Armand who reside beneath a theater. Armand gives Louis much guidance. However, in the world of vampires, killing one of their own is the only crime. This causes a rift between Louis and Claudia. At Claudia's request, he finds her a female companion to act as her mother. But Santiago, a powerful vampire, suddenly appears, capturing them. Louis is sealed within a wall, while Claudia and her companion are exposed to sunlight and executed.

Louis is rescued by Armand, and he vows revenge. Soon, while the vampires are asleep, he sets fire to their lair, killing Santiago and many other vampires. He declines Armand's offer to live together and leaves Paris alone. Stricken by the loss of Claudia, Louis wanders aimlessly, never able to heal from the pain. In 1988, Louis returns to New Orleans and finds Lestat, injured and weak, living in seclusion. Louis tells his story to a young journalist named Malloy, hoping to serve as a warning to future generations. Unexpectedly, after hearing the tale, Malloy is deeply fascinated and insists that Louis turn him into a vampire. Disgusted, Louis leaves him. Disappointed, Malloy drives home alone. Suddenly, Lestat appears on the backseat of the car. He drinks Malloy's blood, heals his wounds, and drives off with him into the distance. A new vampire story is about to begin...
