Talk about why some URL navigation sites can succeed

by kaxiya on 2009-07-02 15:02:37

Choosing a good domain name is a prerequisite for the success of a URL site. For example, domains like 114, hao123, 1616, and 265 are simple, easy to remember, and have a catchy sound.

A memorable domain name extends into a prominent brand effect, which is also an important factor in the success of a URL navigation site.

In my previous article titled "Discussing the number of indexed pages on Baidu for well-known domestic URL navigation sites," I briefly touched upon the traffic structure of URL navigation sites. Search traffic only accounts for a small portion and can almost be ignored. The majority of traffic comes from regular customers or those who are familiar with the brand, such as 114 (http://www.114.com.cn) or hao123. When a user needs a URL navigator or requires recommended URL navigation, the first thing they think of is 114 or hao123. With China's massive base of over 400 million internet users, this kind of traffic is quite formidable.

Next, let's talk about the second key element of a successful URL site: a stable source of traffic inflow. This is something that many grassroots webmasters who run URL sites either don't understand or know very little about, but it is actually one of the most important factors in the success of a URL site.

In today's URL site market, apart from hao123 not needing this kind of fresh blood injection, all other URL sites rely on traffic inflow. Only with a continuous influx of new users will the URL site remain evergreen. Without this, it is bound to fail.

In fact, a single homepage link from Baidu is enough to bring hao123 sufficient traffic. Renowned domestic URL sites each have their own successful methods of traffic injection. The official website of 114 (http://www.114.com.com) leverages the 114 brand to gain a large amount of free traffic. Cool123 relies on nationwide Internet cafe homepage promotions and high-traffic websites like Kupig to provide a steady stream of traffic. 1616 gains strong traffic support through deep system promotions. The newcomer 360 Navigation promotes its browser by binding it to the 360 homepage. The highly controversial 9991 uses unconventional means to tamper with and lock a large number of user homepages. And so on.

In summary, behind the success and powerful traffic accumulation of URL sites lies software promotion, Internet cafe promotion, website cluster guidance, and even virus injection methods that are not widely praised, providing a continuous flow of traffic. Building a strong brand is not as some grassroots webmasters think: simply downloading a set of programs, uploading them to a server, and then waiting for the traffic to roll in.
