Who can guarantee the safety of our funds?

by bfukaitongm on 2012-03-09 09:44:40

Mr. Lin from Beijing searched online for a loan financing company, originally intending to borrow 10,000 yuan, but instead of borrowing the money, he was scammed out of 300 yuan [one quarter's interest]. Wu Yue, a senior wealth management expert at Fuyidai, reminds everyone that there are many online lending websites, and it is essential to choose a platform with security guarantees. Many private lending companies [http://www.fydai.com] are illegal.

Most online lending platforms adopt an [intermediary and guarantee] model to ensure the safety of lenders' funds. This model has three main features: the intermediary platform verifies the qualifications of both parties involved in the loan; it only acts as a matchmaker for both parties without participating in transactions, and introduces third-party guarantees. In case of any loss, the third party compensates the lender. Additionally, some online lending platforms have innovated. Taking Fuyidai as an example, their approach is to harness the power of acquaintances. Fuyidai [http://www.fydai.com] moves users' real-life social networks onto this platform. On this platform, as long as you provide sufficient personal information and social information, you can conduct small-scale financing. At the same time, you gradually build up your credit. On Fuyidai, everyone can add others as [friends], and the more friends you have, the easier the transaction becomes. Recently, they have also launched risk-free high-yield (annualized) financial products to achieve the wealth appreciation desires of lenders. Furthermore, Fuyidai has established a blacklist system, promptly publishing detailed information about borrowers who fail to repay on time on the homepage.

Regardless, the lending industry itself is a risk-aggregating industry. Controlling risk is the key to the healthy development of this sector. Professor Li Jianjun from the School of Finance at Central University of Finance and Economics sees a promising future for online private lending platforms. He believes that the development of online private lending cannot entirely rely on the improvement of the social credit system; websites need to address the asymmetry of information between lenders and borrowers and the cost of designing basic credit systems through self-regulation.

The credit mechanism of Fuyidai plays a significant role in ensuring the safety of lenders' assets. Wu Yue, a senior wealth management expert at Fuyidai, introduced that [the bad debt rate of Fuyidai is 1%, lower than that of banks.]

The self-management and innovation capabilities of the online lending [http://www.fydai.com] industry give us a glimpse into the future of this industry.
