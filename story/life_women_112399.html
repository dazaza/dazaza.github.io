Fat grafting for breast augmentation

by 865512 on 2008-12-18 10:25:46

Among all parts of the female body, the breast receives the most attention because it is the most capable of displaying its own allure. Many women like to shape healthy breasts and restore their unique curves by "polishing" their chest. This has made breast augmentation surgery dominate in breast plastic surgery.

Breast enlargement methods can be divided into three types according to different materials: breast implant insertion, autologous fat transplantation, and liquid material injection. Among these three methods, autologous fat transplantation has become a new favorite in breast augmentation due to its advantages such as being sourced from one's own body and having no rejection issues.

The concept of autologous fat transplantation: It was proposed, accepted, and used long ago. It can almost be used to fill any part of the body. However, in aesthetic surgery, it is more representative in facial soft tissue filling and breast augmentation. So, what exactly is autologous fat transplantation for breast augmentation? Simply put, it involves extracting fat tissue from certain parts of the body (such as the abdomen, buttocks, thighs, etc.), processing it, and then injecting it into the breast to achieve the effect of breast enlargement.

What are the advantages of autologous fat transplantation for breast augmentation? First, the fat used in the transplant comes from the recipient themselves, so it can avoid the body's rejection of the implant and psychological discomfort, and there will be no scar from surgery; secondly, using one's own fat for breast augmentation won't cause problems such as poor touch sensation or capsule contracture that other methods may cause; additionally, the fat tissue used in the operation exists in the recipient's abdomen, buttocks, thighs, etc., making it easy to obtain and achieving local slimming effects at the same time.

The extracted autologous fat is washed with physiological saline, filtered, and purified fat particles are obtained. The injection amount on both sides is calculated, and a 2-3 mm diameter injection needle is used on the outer side of both breasts to evenly inject the extracted granular fat into the breast gland, behind the mammary gland, and under the pectoralis major muscle. Generally, the injection volume per side is 50-150 ml each time. Local massage is performed to make the fat distribute evenly, achieving the desired shaping effect. Injecting too much or concentrating too much can easily lead to fat liquefaction and hard lumps. For smaller chests, it often takes 2-3 sessions to achieve ideal results, with each session spaced 2-3 months apart. Mastering the injection layer, range, and volume is the key to the success of the surgery. Due to improved operating skills, complications such as breast infection and fat calcification that occurred in the past have now rarely happened in regular specialized plastic surgery hospitals.

Although the fat tissue extracted from the human body is washed, processed, and screened before being injected into the breast, due to the lack of blood circulation in the fat tissue, if the correct operational standards are not followed, excessive use of fat or overly concentrated injections can lead to fat necrosis, dissolution, absorption due to insufficient blood supply. This can easily cause infections, pain, cysts, fibrosis, calcification, breast deformity, fat necrosis, and other sequelae. Although the chance of cancer caused by this is low, the necrotic fat forming lumps and fibrosis can affect the results of breast X-ray photography, delaying the diagnosis of breast cancer or causing misdiagnosis.

The adverse consequences mentioned above are mainly due to the operator's lack of understanding of the survival mechanism of granular fat transplantation, violation of operational norms, and lack of experience. Plastic surgery experts believe that compared to early liposuction breast enlargement, the technology is now more mature and less likely to fail. The relevant basis is that performing multiple surgeries with small injections, widely and evenly injecting, increases the contact area between transplanted fat cells and surrounding tissues, significantly improving the survival rate of fat transplantation. Additionally, live fat can now be "washed" out through screening, and measures such as adding basic fibroblast growth factor (bFGF) can help solve the problem of fat survival.

Professor Tan Xindong, an expert in plastic surgery at Guangdong Armed Police Hospital, introduced that another key factor in the success or failure of liposuction breast enlargement lies in the purification of autologous fat. Since the purity of fat cannot be tested during the surgery, the main reliance is on the experience and technical skills of the attending doctor to ensure the purification effect.

Professor Tan Xindong, a plastic surgery expert at Guangdong Armed Police Hospital, particularly emphasized that autologous fat transplantation for breast augmentation has certain restrictions on its indications. It is not suitable for people with particularly flat chests, small skin tension, fewer autologous fat tissues, and those who are thin. The surgery should adopt multiple small-fat-transplantation to avoid harm to the breasts.
