Not hot enough, quick enough

by yutao4194 on 2010-04-06 12:35:24

It's a little cold today. In the afternoon around four o'clock, I was on the light rail to Chifeng Road Station to have dinner with friends when I saw a man in a suit sitting by the roadside, his head down motionless, with his bag placed beside him. His situation can be summarized in four words - extremely depressed. It was clear that he had been sitting there for a long time, and almost no one paid attention to him as everyone hurried by. The light rail I was on had eight stops. Out of habit, I took out my phone and opened the social network to check the diaries shared by classmates. I then read an evaluation about the incident involving four female students jumping from a building in Shanghai. Naturally, the school would not admit that there were problems with management. Not only did they fail to examine their own issues, but after replacing the expired fire extinguishing equipment overnight, they instead used it as a blood example for teaching, saying, "Classmates, never use 'quick heaters' or electric blankets in the dormitory! Otherwise, there will be accidents~ Most accidents are caused by aging circuits. If 'quick heaters' are indeed 'burn fast', why don't relevant departments strictly prohibit their production and sales? Well, let's just say the classmates who jumped were because they lost their way in panic, and using quick heaters is wrong. But couldn't the school teach us how to choose the right path in a panic beforehand? Couldn't the school provide clean hot water? As a student, whether inside or outside the campus, in case of unexpected incidents, due to some unscrupulous school management and media reports, we are always at a disadvantage, gradually making people who read the news form a fixed mindset. Whenever university students encounter accidents, after reading the reports, people comment, 'Why are today's college students like this?' Almost without needing media guidance, the metro arrived, and so did my friends. A synchronized "FengGG" after counting to three made me feel cold to my feet. With those learning Korean, we ate affordable Korean barbecue brought by a Korean, and the atmosphere was a bit cold. On the way back before 7 pm, my mood was still pretty good. I read some cold jokes on the social network on the light rail. I found that the man in the suit was still sitting there, and the passers-by were still going their separate ways, very indifferent. Perhaps he is still sitting there now; it must be very cold outside these days. These days, I've also gotten used to being treated coldly. That's all for the day's report~ According to the weather forecast, it will be even colder tomorrow~ This world is too chaotic, and many people are too heartless.
