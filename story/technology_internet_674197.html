Grave sold for 180,000 yuan, the average price per square meter is close to commercial housing

by anonymous on 2012-02-24 11:07:30

Birth, aging, illness, and death are realities that no one can avoid. The idea of finding a moderately priced grave after one hundred years is not an easy task with the help of video conferencing software. Currently, the cheapest grave in Xi'an has already exceeded ten thousand yuan. So how much does the most expensive one cost? On the price list of a cemetery provided by a staff member in Xi'an, the most expensive one is called the "Fulu No.2" grave, priced at 180,000 yuan. This means the average price per square meter of a grave reaches nearly 100,000 yuan. At this price, you could almost buy a two-bedroom, two-living room apartment in a medium or small city for an average price of over 2000 yuan per square meter. Isn't that absurd?

In fact, the fundamental reason for such exorbitantly priced graves is because the funeral industry is a special industry, always managed by the Civil Affairs Department, lacking market competition. Someone once said: "The memorial hall is more expensive than the conference hall, the grave prices exceed the central city housing prices." Since 2003, the funeral industry has continuously topped the list of the top ten highly profitable industries for over five years. For instance, Xiao Berhong from the Wuhan Municipal Committee of the Revolutionary Committee of the Kuomintang found in his investigation of the HanYang Bian Dan Shan Cemetery that the lowest-priced grave currently costs 10,800 yuan, and there are also "special needs graves" where prices are negotiable, starting at 200,000 yuan with no upper limit. These graves have larger areas, open views around them, and can be used as family graves. In Gansu Lanzhou's Wo Long Gang Cemetery, journalists discovered that graves are even more expensive than residential properties. The highest-priced graves have also reached several hundred thousand yuan, compared to Lanzhou's residential property price of 7000 yuan per square meter, exceeding it by more than ten times. Similarly, in the capital Beijing, a salesperson from a cemetery claimed, "The price of our graves here is 70,000 yuan for 0.8 square meters; this is the price for bluestone material. If it's granite, it will be 75,000 yuan, but there aren't any available now. You'll have to come back after May Day to purchase." In Yong An Cemetery in Yantai, Shandong, the prices are respectively 110,000 yuan, 380,000 yuan, and 580,000 yuan. (Supplied by Shaanxi TV Station's 'Urban Express')
