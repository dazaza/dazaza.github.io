### 翻译：

( )Cu₂S + ( )HNO₃ === ( )Cu(NO₃)₂ + ( )NO + ( )H₂SO₄ + ( )H₂O

If a total of 5.6 L of gas is produced under standard temperature and pressure (STP), what is the minimum amount (in mol) of nitric acid (HNO₃) that was not reduced?

---

#### **最好能讲讲配平思路，什么电子得失之类的，谢谢！**

Using the principle of conservation of electrons to balance the equation:

Steps are as follows:

( )Cu₂S + ( )HNO₃ === ( )Cu(NO₃)₂ + ( )NO + ( )H₂SO₄ + ( )H₂O

1. **Identify which elements gain or lose electrons in the reaction.**

In this reaction, sulfur (S) changes from -4 to +6, so it loses electrons (oxidation). Nitrogen (N) changes from +5 to +2, so it gains electrons (reduction).

2. **Determine the number of electrons lost and gained.**

From the reaction, we see that sulfur only undergoes one oxidation state change, and it is oxidized entirely. However, nitrogen does not fully reduce. (We'll temporarily set aside the exact reduction ratio for now.)

- Sulfur: The electron loss is calculated as \( 6 - (-4) = 10 \) electrons. So, \( \text{Loss} = 10e^- \).

- Nitrogen: The electron gain is calculated as \( 5 - 2 = 3 \) electrons. So, \( \text{Gain} = 3e^- \).

3. **Apply the principle of electron conservation.**

From the previous step, we see that the electron gain-to-loss ratio is \( 3:10 \). This does not appear balanced yet, so to satisfy the conservation of charge, we multiply by coefficients (usually using the least common multiple of the two numbers):

- Multiply the reduction half-reaction by 10 and the oxidation half-reaction by 3.

- Resulting in: \( 10 \times 3 : 3 \times 10 = 30:30 \).

### 配平后的方程式（Balanced Equation）:

\[ 3 \text{Cu}_2\text{S} + 16 \text{HNO}_3 \rightarrow 6 \text{Cu(NO}_3\text{)}_2 + 10 \text{NO} + 3 \text{H}_2\text{SO}_4 + 8 \text{H}_2\text{O} \]

---

### 气体生成量计算（Gas Volume Calculation）:

At STP, 1 mol of any gas occupies 22.4 L. If 5.6 L of gas is produced, then:

\[

\text{Moles of NO} = \frac{\text{Volume}}{\text{Molar Volume at STP}} = \frac{5.6}{22.4} = 0.25 \, \text{mol}

\]

From the balanced equation, 10 moles of NO are produced for every 16 moles of HNO₃ used. Therefore, the moles of HNO₃ required to produce 0.25 mol of NO can be calculated as:

\[

\text{Moles of HNO}_3 = \frac{16}{10} \times 0.25 = 0.4 \, \text{mol}

\]

---

### 未被还原的硝酸（Unreduced HNO₃）:

In the balanced equation, 16 moles of HNO₃ are consumed in total. Of these, 10 moles are reduced to NO, and the remaining 6 moles remain unreduced (forming Cu(NO₃)₂).

Thus, the fraction of HNO₃ that remains unreduced is:

\[

\text{Unreduced HNO}_3 = 6/16 \times 0.4 = 0.15 \, \text{mol}

\]

---

### Final Answer:

The minimum amount of HNO₃ that was not reduced is **0.15 mol**.
