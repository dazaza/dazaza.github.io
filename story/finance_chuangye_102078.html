Sixteen types of people suitable for starting a business and making big money

by huangxin27 on 2008-11-10 09:47:18

Editor's digest: Are you one of these 16 types of people?

1. Very smart people.

How well someone does in studying and how much money they make in business are two completely different things. Scholars are often seen as naive and rigid, while doing business requires a flexible and adaptable mind, where thoughts must be turned into actions immediately.

2. People with strong work ability and efficiency.

Once there is a profitable business opportunity, take steady steps to act on it. Be bold and decisive, only then can you seize the opportunity.

3. People who are not content with the status quo and keep striving.

People who are satisfied with "three meals a day, a wife, children, and a warm bed" and those who cling to a "safe job" will never have the chance to make money. The phrase "enough is enough, why do I need so much money?" is often used by those who don't know or cannot make money to console themselves.

4. People who have truly experienced poverty.

Just like men who are not conventionally attractive but sincerely pursue beautiful girls often succeed, people who have experienced poverty feel its pain deeply, which fuels their desire and spirit to make money.

5. People who have set wealth goals in life.

Someone who monotonously goes to work, deposits fixed salary in the bank year after year, will not accumulate much money. If you want to make big money, you should aim for commerce and set higher goals, such as 100,000, 200,000, or 1,000,000 yuan as your goal, and consider having ten million yuan as being wealthy.

6. People who discard vanity.

If you want to get rich, don't be afraid of embarrassment. When you sell products on the streets, don't fear being looked down upon.

7. Diligent and active people.

Watch more, think more, do more, go beyond the norm, and win by surprise. The more "tricks" you have, the more money you can make.

8. The most innovative people.

The first rule of doing business is having unique insights. Think about what others haven't thought of, stay ahead of others, and let them chase after you. For example, if you find a product that fits the local market well and judge that importing it will definitely be popular, this is the principle by which businessmen make money. By the time the market expands, others following the trend will be too late because you've already made money from that product and won customers.

9. People with great self-confidence.

Whatever you do, first have confidence, believe that you are the best and the greatest. In this way, you can unleash your maximum potential. "Belief" is the guiding light on the path to becoming wealthy through commerce.

10. Outgoing people.

Successful entrepreneurs or businessmen are all straightforward individuals, acting decisively and neatly without dragging their feet.

11. People who are good at taking risks and making decisions.

Be decisive, and success or failure depends on courage. The story of the narrow bridge: There is a narrow bridge, and across it lies a lush orchard with large and fine fruits. Brave people cross the narrow bridge quickly and pick many fruits. But timid people, out of fear, do not cross the bridge and thus fail to pick any fruit.

12. People who are good at learning and summarizing.

In daily business dealings, communicate more with others and listen to their opinions and ideas. Continuously summarize to enrich your business experience.

13. Hardworking and resilient people.

Businesspeople do not rest all 365 days of the year, braving wind and rain, living without regularity, and carrying goods is quite arduous. Believe in the truth that "heaven rewards diligence."

14. People who constantly strive forward.

Face setbacks with perseverance, get up wherever you fall. For example, if you fail once, twice, three times... even if you fail 99 times, succeeding on the 100th attempt makes you a successful person in life.

15. People who are good at seizing opportunities.

In people's lives, there are often many opportunities around them. However, due to neglect, these opportunities are missed or taken by others. Therefore, in daily life, observe more, think more, and act more on things around you, which greatly benefits discovering and creating opportunities.

16. People who cherish time.

Wasting time is equivalent to wasting wealth. For businessmen, "time is money" is absolutely correct. Making money relies on cherishing time and utilizing it. Sleeping early and rising late to be lazy will not lead to accumulating more wealth. Life is limited, especially during the youthful years when the mind is agile, create more wealth to enjoy later in life.
