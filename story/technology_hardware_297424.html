Chinese Super League Confirms Continuation Amid Anti-Gambling Crackdown: Wang Bo and Others Face Public Prosecution and Trial

The anti-gambling campaign in Chinese football is proceeding with great intensity. Many people have wondered about the impact of this action on Chinese football, speculating whether the Chinese Super League (CSL) might be suspended like Malaysia's league was in the past. However, based on recent information, it is confirmed that the CSL will definitely proceed next year, and the anti-gambling storm will continue until the start of the season.

### The CSL Will Definitely Not Be Suspended

Will the CSL be paused? This question was raised by many during the early stages of the anti-gambling crackdown, but now such concerns seem unwarranted. Previously, Vice President of the Football Association Yang Yimin stated in Wuhan: “Suspend for a year? I haven’t heard of that. The league just ended not long ago, and the schedule for next year is being planned and formulated. The exact start date has not been determined yet.”

The Football Association usually doesn't consider the schedule for the next year's league at this time, but when they do need to address it, they find it to be quite a challenging task. The number of teams participating in next year’s league is still uncertain. How to penalize Guangzhou Pharmaceutical, which is already confirmed to be involved in match-fixing, and how many teams will be excluded from the CSL under the entry system are still unclear.

Despite these uncertainties, it is an unchanging fact that the 2010 CSL will definitely kick off as scheduled. "The league will certainly not be suspended. Everyone now recognizes that the league is the foundation of a country's football. If the league stops, then Chinese football will have no way out. Besides, four teams have AFC Champions League tasks. What would they do if the league does not take place?" According to relevant insiders, the national team has heavy tasks next year, and the league is the best training ground for the national players to maintain their form.

After the CSL award ceremony on the 4th, people became even more certain of one fact: the CSL will definitely proceed as scheduled, and the purpose of the anti-gambling campaign is precisely to make the CSL healthier and more orderly.

### The Anti-Gambling Campaign Will Continue Before the New Season

The investigation related to the Guangyao and Shanxi Land Rover case has largely concluded. Next, the case will face the process of being investigated and transferred to the procuratorate for public prosecution. Wang Xin, who was the team leader of the Shanxi team, General Manager Wang Bo, Assistant Coach Ding Zhe, intermediaries You Kewei and Zhong Jianguo, and then-Vice President of Guangyao Yang Xu, jointly planned and participated in this match-fixing case. The six of them will face legal judgment. Next week, the public security department will also announce new developments in the case, and at that time, newly disclosed individuals involved in the case will also be transferred to the procuratorate for public prosecution.

This anti-gambling storm has lasted for over a year, especially in the last two months, with frequent climaxes. So, how much longer will this storm continue? It is well known that match-fixing is not the act of one person; if someone is implicated, they will likely implicate others. Like this time, Wang Xin's arrest initially came from his being wanted by Singapore, but subsequently, it led to the exposure of Wang Bo, Ding Zhe, and You Kewei, among others. “This is a long-term and arduous struggle. At least until the start of next year's league, this storm will continue.” However, after the league starts, there will be some technical difficulties in continuing the anti-gambling campaign. “If the league has started and someone is implicated, the relevant departments directly taking them away from the field would cause significant issues!” Nevertheless, everyone believes that due to this anti-gambling storm, next year's league will definitely be cleaner. “Whoever dares to take risks at this time will only be asking for trouble!” Moreover, the anti-gambling campaign will continue in the future, and the public security department will undoubtedly increase its supervision over Chinese football, ensuring that match-fixing and gambling are completely eradicated from Chinese football!
