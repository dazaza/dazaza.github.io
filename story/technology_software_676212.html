A 14-year-old boy has been causing trouble for the police for a long time. In the police station, Huang Nenbang Xiao Liang was tidying up his clothes and said that the child didn't want to have wounds, and no one would care about him. Xiao Liang finally turned around and started crying. Huang Zhi'an quietly wiped away his tears: "Child, don't be afraid, don't be afraid."

The Wuchang police arrested habitual thief Luo Mou, but unexpectedly learned that he had a 14-year-old son who was left unattended. After more than 11 hours of searching, the police found his son and brought him back to the police station. However, all of the child's relatives refused to take custody or supervision. Upon hearing this, the boss of the thievery company rushed to the police station. He indicated that the child was innocent and should not be made homeless due to his father's crimes, delaying the child's future. He willingly took responsibility for helping the child until his father was released from prison.

When the police asked Xiao Liang where his mother was, Xiao Liang silently took out a crumpled piece of paper and said he was willing to live with his mother. But when the educator from the theater troupe, Ji Jiangtao, personally called his mother, she claimed financial difficulties and refused to take care of the child. Then, the police took Luo Mou to find his brothers, but they also refused due to family hardships. The police then contacted the Children's Welfare Institute, but Xiao Liang did not meet the conditions for foster care and could not be placed there.

Subsequently, the police criminally detained Luo Mou on suspicion of theft. Before being sent to the detention center, Luo Mou said he had a 14-year-old son who was left in an unnamed hotel in Wu Tai Gate without anyone to take care of him. The police quickly began searching, knocking on doors and asking questions. Until 10 a.m. the next day, the police found the child, Xiao Liang (a pseudonym), sleeping in an unidentified inn. At the time, the child only had 190 yuan in his pocket. The police took him back to the police station.

The police introduced that when calling his mother, Xiao Liang was very disappointed, and his eyes even filled with tears. He told the reporter that he didn't love his mother, as she herself was struggling. When asked if he knew what his father had done, Xiao Liang quietly replied that he knew. Although his father's actions were not good, he treated him well, and when he got into trouble, his father was also happy. In Xiao Liang's memory, after 2009, the family conditions improved, and his father bought him many things, even though he was often not at home. However, after late 2009, the family's possessions were gradually sold by his father, who then lived with others for several months. Later, his father took him to live in hotels, where they would sleep well but then disappear without a trace.

Speaking of school, the mature-looking Xiao Liang seemed somewhat disheartened. He said that when he was with his father, they rarely communicated. His father only gave him money and rarely managed him. In the inn, they just slept. Due to the lack of communication, he felt lonely and spent his days online, sometimes up to 10 hours a day. It had been two months since he last bathed or changed clothes. All his belongings were just two paper bags.

Huang Zhiming sighed as he looked at the child. He repeatedly emphasized to the reporter that it was the father who had made the mistake, and the child was innocent. It was too pitiful if no one cared for the child, as it would ruin him. However, since the child lacked legal guardianship, he could not privately take the child home. Nevertheless, Old Huang promised that if the child's relatives were willing to accept him due to mediation, he would provide the child with 1000 yuan per month for living expenses or cover all of the child's educational costs until his father was released from prison.

It turns out that the habitual thief Luo Mou was caught stealing again on the spot. Investigations revealed that Luo Mou had stolen from the same company multiple times. This time, Luo Mou even rented a bread van and hired two "porters" to brazenly move things. According to the company boss, Huang Zhiming, they were actually neighbors with Luo Mou. Previously, Luo Mou had openly asked or stolen from their company multiple times. Huang gave him some valuables each time, but never expected that it would escalate to such greed, leading to multiple open robberies, even renting a car to steal goods.

It is reported that the 55-year-old suspect Luo Mou once had a glorious past. After serving his second sentence and being released from prison, he relied on scrap iron business and amassed a fortune of over a million yuan. However, when his child was eight months old, he divorced the child's mother and developed a gambling habit, quickly squandering all his wealth. Last September, he sold his house to pay off debts and even sold his son's notebook computer. In January this year, his son Xiao Liang, who was studying in the second year of junior high school in Wuchang, was forced to drop out of school. Luo Mou had no choice but to take his son and wander everywhere, as he had infected those around him with bad habits, and relatives and friends no longer associated with him.

On the evening of November 14, the Liyuan police station received an alarm: Outside the Jidong Tieji Industrial Manufacturing Park, in the severe Han Xin Xia Rubber Products Co., Ltd., four people were stealing things. The police quickly arrived at the scene, and a woman who was moving equipment under the vehicle was captured first by the police and security guards. On-site, they discovered production equipment such as small welding machines and chucks, valued at about 8000 yuan.

Despite the challenges, Huang Zhiming stepped forward: "The child is innocent, I can help him." He realized that the boy was actually a good seedling.

Xiao Liang is short and sturdy, but a bit slow in speech. Conversations revealed that his academic performance was relatively good, and he had won awards in painting competitions for middle and primary school students in Wuhan and nationwide. Since childhood, he dreamed of attending Wuhan University, and his prize-winning artwork depicted Wuhan University.

When the police were at a loss, the boss Huang Zhiming spoke up: "The child is innocent, I can help him."
