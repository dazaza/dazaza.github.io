QQ Zone shines with student site managers and prodigies, who make over a million yuan a year - in one word, "impressive"

by qzone9 on 2008-04-27 12:55:10

Author: 0o逆流的鱼o0

Title: QQ Zone Student Station Elite, Creating a Millionaire Genius in a Year, One Word "Admire"

Main Text:

Without mentioning it, you wouldn't know; once mentioned, it's quite shocking. If I told you that a high school sophomore earns a million yuan a year, would you believe it? Even I wouldn't believe it at first glance. But such a fact actually happens right around us.

Since December 2007, I've been obsessed with QQ Zone. Initially, I knew nothing about it. I originally thought QQ Zone was simple and should be the same as other blogs. However, my idea was wrong because QQ Zone has a process called decoration, and it's a relatively complicated one. Unfortunately, there’s nothing to do but listen to friends who say my space is ugly. So, I had to collect some materials online and carefully decorate it.

As usual, I opened Baidu, searched for "QQ Zone," and the first website that caught my eye was http://www.qqywf.com. The title was very long, and from the title alone, you could tell this site was well optimized. Then I opened the site, and the first impression was pretty good—rather fresh. Without thinking too much, I directly looked for materials. In the following half month, I kept paying attention to this site. The materials, pictures, and FLASH provided by the site were very user-friendly. It seemed that the webmaster understood the core concept of "what users need" when building a site. Of course, all these efforts have been recognized by major search engines.

In March, by chance, I saw an article on a well-known webmaster information website titled "QQ Sites are Very Popular and Powerful, Analysis of QQ Sites." What impressed me most was when the author said, "WWW.QQYWF.COM is reportedly made by a high school sophomore." At that time, I definitely wouldn't believe it, thinking the author was just spreading rumors.

Four months passed quickly, and my life remained the same as usual—plain and boring. I'm also a webmaster, managing a very small website. I feel like I've failed. For five years, my website has stayed the same, without any significant changes. Around mid-April, I heard that a well-known domestic website was going to host a series of webmaster conferences, with the first stop being my hometown—Chengdu. I was extremely excited and immediately registered to attend the conference. On April 26th, I arrived at the conference venue early in the morning, hoping that this conference would bring about some changes or breakthroughs for myself.

At around 1:40 PM, I arrived at the venue. After signing in, I received a "Hero Invitation" card and then entered the venue.

After entering, I found that many webmasters had already arrived, and more than half of the seats were filled. I randomly chose a seat in the back row. After waiting for about half an hour, it finally began. The opening ceremony started with a funny movie clip dubbing session, followed by a rather humorous host who gave a brief introduction. The impression left was that he said he would make everyone laugh three times and smile three times. This host was not bad.

Ten minutes later, a beautiful lady tapped my shoulder and introduced herself as a staff member of the company, asking if I had a business card. Sweat! Where would I get a business card? There was no way out, so I had to write down something. She handed me a piece of paper and a pen. This woman was quite attractive, and I didn't expect the company to have such beauties. I took the pen and filled in my proud website address and contact QQ number. I roughly looked at what the other webmasters had written and found a familiar address: WWW.QQYWF.COM.

I was surprised. Wow, I never expected this site to be created by someone from Sichuan (I used this site before when I was doing QQ Zone). I carefully examined it and got basic information: Wu Wei, QQ number 955837.

Looking at the seating arrangement, he was only a few seats ahead, but I had no idea who he was. Later, when I went to the restroom, I saw a student-like webmaster talking to a female staff member of the company, seemingly familiar with each other. I heard her say something like "Wu Wei," and I was stunned. Feeling ashamed, I saw the webmaster, carrying a red backpack, wearing white and gray clothes, probably just arriving after lunch break. He looked somewhat handsome, about 1.67 meters tall, and was likely born in 1991 or 1992.

I stood there for a long time, feeling extremely ashamed. I, someone who has been in the webmaster circle for nearly five years, still haven't achieved anything. And him, a young student, can... sigh, I don't even want to say it. I had heard before that the webmaster of this site was a high school sophomore, but I never confirmed it. Now seeing it with my own eyes, I couldn't help but admire him.

After returning home from the conference, I checked the Alexa ranking of his site: www.qqywf.com ranked 19,440th overall on Alexa; daily average IP visits [weekly average] ≈ 19,800; daily average PV views [weekly average] ≈ 128,700. With this traffic, he should be earning at least 100,000 yuan per month. Isn't that making a million yuan a year?

I don't know whether this is a blow or a motivation for me to strive harder. At this moment, all I have for that high school sophomore is endless admiration and guilt for myself. To be honest, I am jealous of him, jealous of his talent, and jealous of the wealth he currently owns. From another perspective, through this event, we can see a social phenomenon: the Internet is a big platform where everyone has the potential to dig up valuable wealth.

To be honest, I don't understand why a high school sophomore starting to build a site in high school can make it so good and so big. Does he have the technical skills? I think not. He's still a sophomore, and computer science majors aren't realistic either. Sophomores study subjects like Chinese, math, and foreign languages, not computer science. Is it not his site? I think this is even less realistic. Previously, I noticed that his site was updated once a week at the longest. ... Such a long time between updates, yet it still maintains this kind of traffic. Looking at Whois, it's clearly owned by Wu Wei. After checking with several similar websites, it's indeed his...

These days, I have been immersed in shame and self-reproach. I constantly reflect: Five years of website-building experience, and I still can't match a high school sophomore? Late at night, I hesitantly added his QQ, as expected—he wasn't online, and his QQ level was only a moon. Half the night, I couldn't sleep, always pondering, where does his success lie?

Finally, though I feel ashamed, I still want to share a few insights I've gained:

I believe that in doing anything, as long as we make up our minds and persistently work towards it, success will eventually come.

I believe that in doing anything, we should rely on wisdom, not blindly engage in it.

I believe that in doing anything, we should seize opportunities and be good at seizing chances.

I believe that in doing anything, there's nothing impossible. As long as we fight and work hard, everything is possible!

The Internet is everyone's stage. Let's all work together to create our own careers. Come on!
