Can Microsoft's Panos Panay get Surface back on track?

by anonymous on 2013-10-01 22:25:49

Yesterday, Microsoft officially launched the Surface 2 and Surface Pro 2 in New York. At first glance, the new tablets look extremely similar to the original Surface RT and Surface Pro, but Microsoft has done a lot of work behind the scenes: fully improving speed and battery life, allowing users to use them more comfortably on their laps. Despite many people still questioning the effort Microsoft has put into Surface, it is clear that Microsoft is very clear about its future direction as a "devices and services" company. Panos Panay, the head of Surface, spoke to us about the two new products and the future vision of Surface.

"They need to be faster, and their battery life needs to last longer."

The main purpose of Microsoft's presentation this time was to explain why consumers would want to buy a Surface and introduce the company's broader ecosystem. Panay, who focuses on innovation and productivity, emphasized on stage how Surface can transfer a 6K file from a RED camera and demonstrated the Surface Music Cover for DJs to mix music with. By bundling Skype, Office, and SkyDrive, Microsoft hopes to make the Surface Pro 2 and Surface 2 faster and more powerful. "They need to be faster, and their battery life needs to last longer," said Panay.

The devices themselves also have one major change: both new Surfaces will come with a two-stage adjustable stand, allowing the Surface to be propped up at two angles on a flat surface. Other than that, the design is basically the same as the previous generation.

Microsoft's new Touch, Type, and Power Covers also play an important role. They not only improve typing performance but also make the Surface 2 and Surface Pro 2 more durable and convenient to use on your lap. "I tested both Surfaces and used the new angle, and the effect is really much better than before. Although there are still some imperfections, at least we don't feel like it's going to slip off our knees anymore."

"We created the first Surface RT in the basement."

The two-in-one stand is indeed a significant change, and Panay admitted that Microsoft made other changes as well. "We created the first Surface RT in the basement," he said, "You only get real feedback after users have used and understood it." This feedback led the team to focus on improving the battery life of the Surface Pro 2. Although Microsoft did not disclose specific numbers, Panay said his personal test showed continuous video playback for 10 to 11 hours. "When it goes to sleep mode, it doesn't consume any power at all. You can put it away, take it out a month later, and it will still be fully charged."

Setting aside battery life and the two-in-one stand, Microsoft's innovations this time were not in the Surface 2 or Surface Pro 2 themselves. Clearly, the new Touch, Type, and Power Covers are the stars. Compared to the previous generation, the number of sensors in the Touch Cover 2 increased from 80 to 1092, effectively reducing user error rates. It supports switching between multiple customized interfaces at any time and dynamically generates more function buttons. Like the Type Cover 2, the Touch Cover 2 also supports backlighting.

The new Music Cover shows what a good Touch Cover should be. Equipped with sound playback buttons and pads for mixing, this is undoubtedly the best blueprint for future Surfaces. The cover becomes a second interactive tablet, a creative idea that many manufacturers have dreamed of but never achieved.

Will there be a 7-8 inch mini tablet in the future for Surface?

Panay said, "We're trying a lot of different things, but we won't use productivity as the only reference factor. They will come in different shapes and sizes." There are rumors that the Surface mini will soon be released, but Panay and Microsoft executives remain silent about the 7-8 inch model. It seems that the possibility of the Surface mini appearing is increasing, as it appears to be the most logical next step for Panay's team. Panay said, "There are too many amazing modes for the product that can make consumers fall in love with it. Size is one of them."
