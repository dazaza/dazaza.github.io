City managers forcibly collect yuanxiao from vendors to visit the elderly at nursing homes

by xue99zwsh on 2012-03-03 10:17:10

Pointing to the place where the yuanxiao (sweet dumplings) were stacked, Zhang Wei said: "What's the difference between this and stealing or robbing?" Under the bright daylight, the yuanxiao that were placed at the entrance had vanished. Upon inquiry, it was learned that they had been "requisitioned" by urban management law enforcement officers to be taken to a nursing home for慰问 (comfort visits). This was an incident encountered by some vendors in Shuangcheng City, Harbin on February 2. The explanation given by the staff of the Shuangcheng Urban Management Administrative Law Enforcement Bureau was that their unit is one that seizes items, and when instructed by their superiors to seize, they comply. On February 6, the bureau director admitted to a journalist from Weibo Lianbo that the law enforcement officers had deviated in their duties. "Without informing us, they just took them away. What's the difference between this and stealing or robbing?" On February 6, four days after the incident, Zhang Wei from Banfenli Groceries Store was still visibly angry while recounting the events to a Weibo Lianbo journalist. According to her introduction, before the Lantern Festival, she had stocked up on some sweet dumplings to sell. Unable to fit all of them in the fridge, she left over 100 bags outside the door on the right side to prevent them from melting indoors. "They were there when I opened the store at 7:30 on the morning of the 2nd, but by 9 o'clock, they were gone."

Zhang Wei said that she had intentionally placed these boxes close to the wall, fearing they would be lost, and even stacked other frozen goods around them. "There were noodles nearby, and fish underneath, but nothing else was taken, only the sweet dumplings. At first, I thought they had been stolen, but later, customers who came to buy vegetables told me that they had been confiscated by the urban management officers."

"Urban management officers drove three vehicles down the street confiscating vendors' goods that day." Guo Zhenyu from Haoxinren Fruit and Vegetable Wholesale Store also had 60 plus bags of sweet dumplings forcibly requisitioned. "We received no prior notice, and by the time we noticed, the urban management vehicles had already left."

That day, Zhang Wei and Guo Zhenyu went to the Shuangcheng Urban Management Administrative Law Enforcement Bureau, only to learn that the sweet dumplings had been sent to the nursing home for elderly comfort visits. "Some vendors didn't dare to come forward."

From a video recording made that day, it could be seen that the staff receiving them at the bureau stated that they were still on holiday and any issues should be addressed after the Lantern Festival. "This is a unit that seizes items. Even if you pay someone to get into this unit, your job will involve seizing things. That's how this unit operates, and the seizures are decided temporarily by the leadership."

"We can deliver items to the nursing home ourselves, there's no need for the law enforcement bureau to seize our items for the purpose of giving comfort." Guo Zhenyu said, "When we returned to the law enforcement bureau later, no one was willing to receive us."

Deputy Director Cui Lei of the Shuangcheng Town Nursing Home said in an interview with reporters that the Shuangcheng Urban Management Administrative Law Enforcement Bureau had delivered over 140 bags of sweet dumplings to the nursing home that day. There were more than 60 elderly residents in the facility, and the urban management bureau often brought seized food items to the elderly.

At the Shuangcheng Urban Management Bureau, the reporter only saw two on-duty personnel. Director Zhou Xudong said over the phone that he was aware of the incident where the law enforcement officers took sweet dumplings from stores on the 2nd. He acknowledged that the law enforcement officers had deviated in their duties and had already punished the relevant personnel accordingly. According to CCTV.
