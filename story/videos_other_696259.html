[Omega Seamaster Series] Words of wisdom for 2012, each sentence is profound.

by frttptk02903 on 2012-03-07 23:05:22

1. Don't be afraid of being used by others, it shows that you are still useful when someone takes advantage of you.

2. Eating is practical, gambling is hedging, and indulging in prostitution is wasting money.

3. Spend one cent and get the effect of ten cents; that's a spending expert.

4. Do things thoroughly like cutting tofu with a knife to make both sides smooth.

5. There are three bowls of noodles hardest to eat in life: reputation, situation, and favor.

6. One must be sincere towards others. Even if someone deceives me temporarily, I can always move him with sincerity and make him submit. My way of dealing with the world lies entirely in the word "sincerity". You need to understand this principle before you can talk about making friends.

7. Mr. Du once said to a cultured friend: You were originally a carp, after 500 years of cultivation, you jumped over the Dragon Gate and became a dragon. As for me, I was originally an eel, first cultivated for 1000 years to become a carp, then another 500 years to jump over the Dragon Gate. If we both fail, you will still be a carp, but I would revert to being an eel. How could I not be cautious in doing things?

8. The top class: skilled but without arrogance; Second class: skilled but arrogant; The lowest class: unskilled but full of temper.

9. Du Yuesheng often said: "Carefulness wins the world, carelessness loses Jingzhou."

10. A person survives on two things in the world: courage and wisdom.

11. Leave the nitpicking tasks for others, I only do things that help those in trouble.

12. Money can be spent, but feelings last forever. Love happens between the legs, but friendship endures. So while others save money, I save friendships. No matter how much money you save, it’s just a pile of gold and silver, but friendship is priceless!

13. The number of zeros on your bank statement doesn't count as wealth; what matters is how much money you can spend.

14. In the first half of the night, think about yourself; in the second half, think about others.

15. I don’t fear gentlemen, and I don’t provoke petty thieves!

16. Never do things too absolutely; always leave room for maneuver.
