A Large Number of Commercial Villas Appear in the Xiaolangdi Reservoir Area in Henan, Unregulated for Five Years

LuoYang, February 15th - According to China National Radio's "News纵横" report, the Xiaolangdi Water Conservancy Hub is a key project for the management and development of the Yellow River, listed as one of the national "Eighth Five-Year Plan" key projects. Since its completion at the end of 2001, it has attracted many tourists to visit. However, within this nationally renowned Xiaolangdi Water Conservancy Hub project, there are people who have been vigorously constructing private tourist areas and villas. How did this happen?

In the Xiaolangdi reservoir area located in Lujia Town, Xin'an County, Luoyang City, Henan Province, among dozens of picturesque islands, quite a few have been artificially developed. On an island not far from the shore, two five-story buildings stand on top of the mountain. On another island in the center of the reservoir, apart from the buildings, fishing rods and chairs can be seen by the shore for people to fish, with people coming and going.

A construction contractor responsible for the island development told the reporter that the reason why the county allowed them to contract the islands was mainly for fruit tree planting and vegetation restoration work. However, many contractors, driven by profit, built residences and even tourist attractions on the contracted islands. For years, no one had questioned these illegal constructions:

Reporter: What do people usually do when they contract the mountains?

Contractor: Plant trees.

Reporter: But I see houses being built on some mountains. What are they doing?

Contractor: They're building tourist areas. People paid 2 million yuan to buy them.

Reporter: Are these tourist areas privately owned?

Contractor: Privately bought.

With doubts, the reporter then went to the Xiaolangdi Scenic Area where the Xiaolangdi Dam is located. Unexpectedly, upon entering the gate, someone immediately came forward to collect fees and enthusiastically promoted the villas within the scenic area:

Fee collector: If you want to go in, I can let you in. The ticket is 40 yuan per person. For both of you, it will be 50 yuan.

Reporter: Can we buy villas here?

Fee collector: Yes, Chengda Villa. That's where they sell villas. Let me give you their phone number?

It turned out that there were private villas within the Xiaolangdi Scenic Area. Following the staff member, the reporter arrived at the Chengda Villa complex. Here, the reporter saw that the villa complex covered about 5 mu (about 0.33 hectares), with more than ten luxurious villas towering high. The three-story red-and-white Western-style houses shone brightly under the sun. Judging from their condition, these villas should have been built for several years but remained unoccupied. Less than a kilometer in front of the villas was the Xiaolangdi Water Conservancy Hub of the Yellow River.

Villas could exist so close to the dam? The reporter immediately contacted the villa sales office. According to the staff, these villas were privately developed. The land used to build these villas was also privately purchased and contracted.

Subsequently, the reporter contacted a person in charge of Chengda Villa under the pretense of wanting to buy a villa. This person warmly stated that since the land belonged to the Xiaolangdi Water Conservancy Project, the property rights of the houses would last much longer than ordinary housing, and all procedures were complete, so one could buy with peace of mind:

Reporter: Can we get a real estate certificate?

Person in charge: All can be processed.

Reporter: If we buy, will the land belong to us?

Person in charge: Yes. It's all private land, privately bought. Whoever wants it, they can have it, and the real estate certificate can be processed.

Reporter: Will anyone investigate this?

Person in charge: This has been approved by the city and the county. We don't fear investigations.

Why does this responsible person have such confidence? Is it safe to live in a villa less than a kilometer away from the dam? When the reporter consulted a staff member of Chengda Villa, his attitude was somewhat impatient. He told the reporter that there were more illegal constructions here in the past few years, but due to the flood discharge of the dam, many already built villas were submerged by the water:

Reporter: In the past, there were private scenic areas on these islands.

Staff: Yes.

Reporter: Are there still any now?

Staff: No, they were flooded when the water level rose.

Why are these dangerous illegal villas not regulated? Regarding these issues, the reporter first called the Henan Provincial Water Resources Department and received the following reply:

Water Resources Department: Xiaolangdi doesn't fall under our jurisdiction; it is directly managed by the Ministry. We have almost no interaction with them. We are not involved in Xiaolangdi matters.

Subsequently, the reporter connected with the Xiaolangdi Project Supervision Bureau, but the staff member still spoke vaguely:

Supervision Bureau: We are currently working together with local authorities to rectify Xiaolangdi, but we have a rule: if the water level line is higher than 275 meters, it falls under local jurisdiction; if lower than 275 meters, it is governed by us.

Building illegal structures near the Xiaolangdi Dam for profit, even disregarding the lives and property safety of the surrounding population, who should manage these issues? We look forward to relevant departments providing answers.
