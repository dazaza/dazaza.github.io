Owner denied meeting with honest finder posts letters of gratitude on the street

by iface002 on 2011-05-23 15:01:39

Just arrived at Huju Pass, the reporter saw a lot of thank-you letters on the walls along the street. From the thank-you letters, it can be seen that Mr. Lu, the owner of the lost item, lost a bag near Huju Pass on May 19th. The bag contained some personal items and an ID card. "This bag was picked up and returned by Mr. Zhu. I am deeply moved by Mr. Zhu's noble quality of not keeping found money for himself. For this reason, I once personally went to Huju Pass to express my thanks, but Mr. Zhu did not want any gratitude for doing good deeds, so I couldn't thank him in person. I can only express my gratitude in this way, hoping that Mr. Zhu will see this thank-you letter. Thank you, Mr. Zhu."

Because there is a thank-you letter on the wall every certain distance along the street, many passers-by stop to take a look. "This Mr. Zhu who does not keep found money for himself is pretty good," Ms. Zhang, a passer-by said. The owner of the lost item is also very nice, as he could not thank in person but still thought of expressing his feelings through such a method. However, what made the reporter feel a bit strange was that there was another paragraph at the end of the thank-you letter: "If Mr. Zhu can see this thank-you letter, I would also like to ask Mr. Zhu to contact me when it is convenient for you. I will be waiting for your call all day. If it is just to express gratitude, posting the thank-you letter should be enough to convey the sentiment, why must there be further contact with Mr. Zhu?"

"There are many thank-you letters posted here at Huju Pass." After receiving the tip from the reader yesterday, the reporter immediately rushed to Huju Pass to find out why the thank-you letter had to be posted on the walls along the street? Why was there an expectation for the old man who did not keep found money to call?

According to the telephone number left by Mr. Lu in the thank-you letter, the reporter subsequently contacted him, but after learning that the reporter wanted to conduct an interview, Mr. Lu refused. He said that the lost bag was received through mail, so he did not meet Mr. Zhu. "There are some twists and turns in this matter." Mr. Lu stated that he has been waiting for Mr. Zhu to contact him and will tell the story of the incident at that time.

Mao Chaolin, Xue Ling
