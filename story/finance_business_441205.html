Cabinet Selection Tips

by f34ttr4t5ye on 2011-06-03 16:26:24

A kitchen cabinet is a noble consumer product, with its high price accounting for a significant portion of the renovation cost. The quality of cabinets on the market varies greatly. How can you ensure that your money is well spent? Based on knowledge from various well-known cabinet brands, the following information and tips are listed:

1. Five Essentials for Choosing Kitchen Cabinets

1) The cabinet body board should be 18mm thick

The market offers boards of various thicknesses such as 16mm and 18mm, with costs differing significantly. Cabinets made from 18mm thick boards have a lifespan more than twice as long, ensuring the board does not deform and the countertop does not crack.

2) The cabinet body board should be warm white

Cabinet colors on the market come in many varieties, mainly including warm white, gray white, and cold white. Different colors have different costs, with warm white being the most expensive, costing 4% more than other decorative colors. Warm white decoration has a soft, full, and elegant appearance, making it cleaner and safer to use.

3) The back panel should be double-sealed

To save costs, some manufacturers cut corners by only single-sealing the back panel, leaving the unseen side exposed. Single-sealed back panels are prone to dampness and mold, and can easily release formaldehyde, causing pollution. Therefore, it must be double-sealed.

4) The sink cabinet aluminum foil should be pressed in one operation

One-time pressing provides better sealing performance, preventing water and moisture from penetrating and more effectively protecting the cabinet, extending the life of the kitchen cabinet.

5) The edge-banding adhesive should be environmentally friendly

Imported adhesives contain little or no formaldehyde, so they do not produce processing pollution. In terms of durability, they can ensure that the glue does not peel off for a longer time, preventing formaldehyde leakage. Well-known manufacturers use imported brand edge-banding adhesives such as Bovine Head and Adhesive King.

2. How to Identify Details

1) How to distinguish between genuine and fake Egger boards

The size of an Egger board is 2.8 meters by 2.07 meters, while imitation Egger boards generally only reach 1.22 meters by 2.44 meters. To identify if it's a real Egger board, you can visit the factory to check the width of the whole board and the anti-counterfeiting marks around the edges of the whole board. Alternatively, you can request to make a cabinet over 2.5 meters high to see if the manufacturer can use the whole board.

2) How to choose countertops

Materials suitable for kitchen countertops include fireproof boards, artificial stone, natural marble, granite, stainless steel, etc. Among them, artificial stone countertops offer the best cost-performance ratio. It's difficult to identify the composition of artificial stone. Cheap countertops have a high content of calcium carbonate and perform poorly in toughness, heat resistance, scratch resistance, and environmental protection. It's best to avoid selecting these. Currently, composite acrylic and pure acrylic are widely used in the market. The optimal proportion of acrylic in composite acrylic is about 20%.

3) Material origin issues

There is a significant price difference between imported and domestic materials. Many manufacturers claim that their products are imported or that certain components are imported. Imported products must have customs clearance certificates, which consumers can request from merchants or call the customs office to inquire.

4) Check the finished product inspection report

Kitchen cabinets are also furniture, and national regulations require issuing a finished product inspection report and clearly indicating formaldehyde content. Some manufacturers can only provide raw material inspection reports, but raw material environmental friendliness does not necessarily mean finished product environmental friendliness. When purchasing, you can request a finished product inspection report from the merchant or record the quality inspection report number provided by the merchant and call the quality inspection department to verify its authenticity.

5) Pay attention to the length of the warranty period

Don't just focus on the price and style of the product; whether or not excellent after-sales service can be provided is a demonstration of the manufacturer's strength. For example, warranty years vary among manufacturers—some offer one year, others two years, and still others five years. The longer the warranty period, the higher the requirements will be in terms of materials and manufacturing processes.
