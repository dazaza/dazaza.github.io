Poor students' romance

by qvyfeifeic on 2011-09-13 19:19:49

The Love of a Poor Student, a tear-jerking romance!~

Like Liangduo, I am an extremely poor student, the only college student from my village. The school is quite ordinary, but it's still a bachelor's degree program. Moreover, I ranked first in the county for the college entrance exam. My grandfather said, "This is a champion!" He insisted on throwing a banquet and inviting guests. Our family, so poor, finally saw hope; finally, someone was going to study in Beijing.

However, they didn't know how inferior I felt among my city classmates. They didn't know how much effort I put into learning Mandarin and practicing English. They even didn't know how I managed to pay the tuition year after year. Once, when I accidentally mentioned that I could earn 500 yuan a month as a tutor, my father got angry at me for the first time. He thought that since I earned so much money, I should know how to respect the elders and send money home for my younger brother's tuition. He thought I was too naive.

My life could be described as very bleak: always studying, attending classes, taking exams, and earning money. Classmates called me a miser. Whenever there was an opportunity for part-time work, they would come find me, half-jokingly saying, "Hey, I heard you'd do anything for money." I pretended not to care: "Yes, I would." The money I worked so hard to earn went half towards my tuition and the other half to support my family, providing for my younger siblings' education. In our hometown, people spoke of me with admiration: "What progress! Not only can he support himself, but also his younger siblings." My younger siblings wrote letters to me, always saying, "Brother, I want to go to university in Beijing too!" They didn't know my struggles, and I didn't want anyone to see them, except her.

She was a freshman in the class below mine. When we welcomed the new students, I helped her carry her luggage and find her bed. She insisted on treating me to dinner, so I ate. After the meal, I paid the bill and took her around campus, helping her get familiar with the surroundings.

Later she said, "That's when I started liking you. Tall and thin, especially quiet, it seemed like you had many worries. Everyone said I was a little beauty, but you didn't even give me a second glance." Indeed, at that time, I was thinking about how I would manage my food expenses for the week after paying the bill. I hadn't considered having a girlfriend, let alone one as pretty and urban as her. But she often sought me out, auditing classes in our classroom, asking others about my affairs. What particularly moved me was that on my birthday, she bought a cake and waited for me at the school gate. I was tutoring and didn't return until 10 PM. She waited for me for six hours in the cold winter wind. From childhood to adulthood, no one had ever been so good to me. When I took the cake from her hand, I held her hand in mine, warming it.

She said, "I know you have a lot of pressure, don't be afraid, let's share it together, okay?" She was truly innocent then, and so was I. People blinded by love can do anything, say anything.

We rented a small room near the school and lived together. I had already been accepted into graduate school, and I bought her a lot of books to prepare for her postgraduate entrance exam. We attended classes and lectures together, ate in the cafeteria, and at night, I went out to work while she waited for me at home. She bought discounted fruit, peeled them, cut them into pieces for me to eat. She also learned to cook using a coal stove... I knew she loved me deeply, and I loved her wholeheartedly.

They say love is expensive, but I didn't spend much. Instead, I sent an extra 300 yuan home. She said it was for my sister to buy new clothes, and this month we could eat the cheapest dishes.

She told her family about me, and her parents wanted to meet me. I was mentally prepared, but I was still shocked by their wealth. They lived in a luxurious duplex house with extravagant decoration. Her mother said because she was an only daughter, they hoped we would live together after marriage. Her father frowned continuously, looking at my worn-out jeans and old shirt. I couldn't help but feel that the contrast between our wealth was like something out of a movie or novel, almost impossible to accept.

I couldn't forget what her father said to me: "Our Nan has never suffered, has never been wronged. It's our duty as parents. Young man, can you do that?" I didn't answer, knowing I couldn't. At the same time, I realized how much she sacrificed to be with me: not living in a nice house or dormitory, squeezing into a small flat with me; not wearing fancy clothes, wearing sportswear all year round; eating boiled cabbage instead of dining at new restaurants where her father used to drive the whole family. She saved her living expenses to help me pay tuition. That was all I could offer her, all a poor lover could offer her. Yet, it required her endless sacrifices, both in time and material. She said, "Loving you doesn't feel like suffering." But it pained me, really hurt, like my whole being was being torn apart. My birth I cannot choose, but why did she choose me, choosing such a heavy burden?

Indeed, her family disapproved. Her mother even asked if our cohabitation was due to some sinister means on my part. I must say, her parents were well-intentioned. On the surface, they didn't interfere, but in reality, they kept encouraging her to study abroad. She happily told me, "Let's apply together, let's study abroad." I laughed and said, "Good." I didn't tell her that my younger brother failed the college entrance exam and needed to retake it for another year, and my sister was in her third year of high school. I found more jobs, persuaded her to move back to the dormitory, and intentionally distanced myself from her without letting her notice. Because of her cheerful and somewhat careless nature, she didn't realize that I had already made up my mind to separate.

I forced her to memorize words. Most of our time together was spent studying English. She said, "I feel like you don't care about me as much anymore." I replied, "No, making you study well is good for you. Aren't you planning to study abroad?" I waited until she finished her GRE, helped her send resumes, materials, and write applications, busier than she was herself. She began to hesitate and asked, "What about you? What are you going to do?" I said, "It's easy for me, I've done well as a substitute test-taker, what are you worried about?" When I said these words, I didn't look at her because my eyes would reveal the truth.

Finally, the offer came, and I sighed in relief. I called her mother: "Auntie, Nan can go abroad now, you can rest assured." Her mother hesitated and asked, "Won't you go with her?" I said, "I won't go. I have a family to take care of. I sincerely hope Nan will be happy for the rest of her life, but I can't provide that, so I absolutely won't drag her down." Her mother cried on the phone, saying, "You're a good child, you understand the hearts of parents." I said, "I understand, I don't blame you, really."

Poor boys shouldn't have love. Let's break up, I'm not worthy of you, it's not because I'm not good enough, I can't bear seeing you suffer with me. I have parents and grandparents above, and younger siblings below who need to study. I have to work at least ten more years harder to make sure my whole family lives a good life. I love you, so I shouldn't be with you. We were wrong from the start. I'm sorry, I hope you can forget me.

She cried, hitting me, biting me, kicking me. I didn't fight back, but I didn't try to console her either. A long pain is worse than a short one. Go abroad, my beloved girl. Better people and better love will come your way. I don't want you to miss out on enjoying life during your best years because of me. I'm poor, and what I can offer you is far less than what you deserve. I don't want us to become like those doctoral seniors whose families constantly argue over supporting each other's hometowns. Let's end it here. Believe me, I'm sadder than you. I have to hurt you, I have to leave you, my dearest love. (A touching story)
