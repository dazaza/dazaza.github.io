Australian miner Exco seeks Chinese investors

by shfukkgfes8 on 2012-01-06 09:47:04

Australian miner Exco seeks Chinese investors - Wang Jie

Wherever there are mineral deposits, there are the shadows of Chinese investors, either actively seeking opportunities or being passively invited.

On September 1, a source in the field of mining acquisitions informed this newspaper that Australian copper and gold miner Exco Resources Limited (ASX:EXS) has received significant investment interest regarding asset purchases and company operations, mainly from China but also including some companies from South Korea.

Exco's Managing Director, Michael Anderson, stated: "Chinese enterprises interested in Exco include both state-owned and private companies." According to information obtained by this newspaper from the aforementioned source, Tongling Nonferrous Metals Group [16.960.71%] and Jiangxi Copper [31.251.49%] may be interested in cooperating with Exco.

Exco's financing needs

Exco’s website shows that this copper and gold miner located in West Perth has a core asset known as the Cloncurry Copper project (CCP).

The resource volume of CCP includes 472,000 tons of copper and 394,000 ounces of gold. One of its important deposits, “E1 Camp,” located in northwest Queensland, contains about 75% of the total copper reserves of the entire project. The drilling activities and resource modeling of this mine are Exco's key tasks for this year. Currently, the inferred and indicated resources of E1 Camp total 34 million tons, containing 257,000 tons of copper.

"This miner is relatively small in Australia; they have also asked us if we are interested in cooperation," said a representative stationed in Australia from a large private mining company on September 1.

In mid-August, Exco announced that it had implemented an agreement to issue approximately 16.4 million ordinary shares at a final price of 35 cents per share to a subsidiary of Singapore-based private company Sin-Tang Development, raising 4.9 million Australian dollars.

These funds will be used for exploration and resource development activities at its Cloncurry copper and gold mine.

Regarding this, Exco's Managing Director Michael Anderson stated: "Issuing subscription shares is just the first step; both parties will make every effort over the next three months to promote strategic investment by this subsidiary in the Cloncurry copper mine."

In the second phase, negotiations between both parties will simultaneously include share subscriptions and providing debt financing for the project. If both parties reach a consensus on strategic investment, Exco may also provide terms for this subsidiary to further acquire equity in the Cloncurry copper mine, as well as up to an additional 3.29 million ordinary shares under the same terms as the subscription shares.

London securities broker Fox-Davies Capital believes that the phased approach of the financing agreement will enable Exco to maintain all its strategic options in the short term; Sin-Tang Development will be a strong support for the independent mining operations of the Cloncurry project—even if Exco's cooperation with Xstrata regarding the use of the Earnest Henry site fails.

However, Michael Anderson refused to comment on recent media reports that mining giant Xstrata (Strata) or Ivanhoe (Ivanhoe) might launch a hostile takeover bid worth 153.5 million Australian dollars (135.9 million US dollars) against Exco in the near future.

"I do not comment on market-sensitive information," he said.

Currently, Ivanhoe owns 23% of Exco's shares, while Exco's Cloncurry copper project is close to Xstrata's Ernest Henry mine.

In April this year, Exco sold 50% of its shale gas assets in the Appalachian region of the United States to UK-based BG Group for 950 million US dollars.

Rational considerations by Chinese parties

According to our understanding, although Exco has already signed an agreement with Singaporean investors, it still retains the ability to consider all strategic options, not limited to Sin-Tang Development but possibly Xstrata or any other third party.

Because the company's set goal is to select a clear development plan for the CCP project within the next few months, before completing a definitive feasibility study and obtaining relevant approvals by the end of the year.

On September 1, the above-mentioned source told this reporter: "This makes several domestic enterprises, including Tongling Nonferrous Metals and Jiangxi Copper, potentially interested. But this does not mean an intention to initiate a hostile takeover. Chinese companies may be more interested in establishing joint ventures or signing off-take contracts."

Because the self-sufficiency rate of copper concentrate directly correlates with the growth rate of corporate profits.

An analysis report pointed out that among domestic listed copper industry companies, in 2010, Jiangxi Copper (600362.SH) had the highest self-sufficiency rate of copper concentrate raw materials, estimated at around 23%, followed by Yunnan Copper [21.120.76%] (000878.SZ) at 17%, and Tongling Nonferrous Metals (000630.SZ) at 6%.

"Currently, good copper mines in Australia are indeed scarce, but Chinese enterprises may consider Exco's geographical advantages. After all, transportation distances from places like Canada and Peru are much farther."

In the layout of overseas copper mine resources, Chinese investments in large copper mines with annual production capacities exceeding 150,000 tons number at least six, primarily led by China Aluminum Corporation [9.92-1.78%], Jiangxi Copper Company, and China Nonferrous Metal [0.23-0.88%]. However, these investments are mostly concentrated in Canada, South America, and Africa, with fewer in Australia.

For example, Jiangxi Copper joined with MCC Engineering to invest 2.87 billion US dollars to acquire 100% equity in Afghanistan's Enak copper mine, and invested over 400 million US dollars to purchase 21% equity in Northern Peru Copper Mine; Tongling Nonferrous Metals teamed up with China Railway Construction [7.500.67%] to acquire 66.67% equity in Canadian Corriente Resources for 4.411 billion yuan; Zijin Mining [6.540.62%] acquired 79.9% controlling equity in Peru's Rio Blanco copper mine; China Aluminum Corporation secured the Toromocho copper mine project in Peru.

"From the perspective of the resources themselves, investing in Australia remains the preferred choice overall. There are very few resources where Australia is not among the world leaders."

The representative of the aforementioned private mining company stationed in Australia told the reporter that now, for better resource projects within Australia, local miners would choose to finance themselves. Smaller companies or projects desperately needing development funds would actively extend olive branches to Chinese investors.

"As for us, we're not very interested in Exco. However, each enterprise makes judgments based on itself when making overseas investments. For smaller enterprises, some people like them, some people resist them, and risks and opportunities always coexist." He said.
