Sina's Premier League Team of the Week: No players from Manchester United, Korean winger included.

by yinding999 on 2009-12-15 11:02:45

Sina's Premier League Round Best XI: No Manchester United Players, Korean Wing Selected

In a challenging away game against the offensively powerful Tottenham Hotspur, Wolves goalkeeper Rui Patricio made an impressive seven saves, ultimately securing a clean sheet. Notably, this was only the third league appearance of the season for the American goalkeeper. However, in these three matches, Wolves conceded only two goals, helping the team achieve an excellent record of two wins and one loss. Who would have thought that Patricio is already 37 years old!

Defenders: Branislav Ivanovic (Chelsea), Sol Campbell (Portsmouth), Jody Craddock (Wolves), Emilio Nsue (Wigan)

Chelsea were held to a draw by Everton at home, but Ancelotti did not leave the match empty-handed, as his right-back Branislav Ivanovic stood out. Ivanovic’s two assists helped Drogba and Anelka each score a goal. With 2 assists, 5 key passes, and 3 successful tackles, Ivanovic has performed satisfactorily on both offense and defense since the injury of first-choice right-back Bosinwa. In the 93rd minute, just as Portsmouth seemed destined for another away defeat, Campbell stepped up courageously. His follow-up shot secured a crucial point for Pompey's survival battle. Besides, Campbell, playing as a center-back, contributed with no fewer than 10 clearances during the game. Sky Sports awarded him the highest rating of 9 points. Unfortunately, due to removing his shirt in celebration, Campbell received a second yellow card and was sent off before the final whistle. The promoted Wolves managed to secure 3 points at White Hart Lane, and captain Jody Craddock was undoubtedly a major contributor. Experts and fans from Sky Sports rated Craddock with the highest score of the game. This season, Craddock has scored 4 goals in the league, making him both an offensive and defensive asset. A stunning 60-meter free-kick chip from Figueroa resulted in a world-class goal that helped his team earn a point in Stoke City's notorious home ground. Moreover, the 26-year-old Honduran left-back delivered one key pass in the game. In Sky Sports' scoring, Figueroa received the highest mark of 9 points.

Midfielders: Lee Chung-yong (Bolton), Kevin Foley (Birmingham), Ashley Young (Aston Villa)

Assisting once, contributing several brilliant passes, Lee Chung-yong made his debut in the Sky Sports weekly best XI after joining the Premier League. Sky experts gave him a high score of 8 points, while fans rated him even higher at 9.1 points. Lee Chung-yong's performance is critical for Bolton's potential to remain in the Premier League. Once again, it was Kevin Foley's last-minute winner that brought Birmingham four consecutive victories in the Premier League. Foley provided the decisive strike thrice during this period. Since Chinese owner Carson Yeung took over the club, Birmingham has achieved five wins and two draws in their last seven rounds, with the two draws coming against strong teams like Liverpool and Manchester City. Now, Birmingham has climbed to the eighth position in the Premier League standings, level with Liverpool. Aston Villa's first-ever away victory over Manchester United in 26 years was significantly owed to Ashley Young. His assist to Agbonlahor was named the best pass of the week by Sky Sports. After Steven occupies the main left-winger position for England, Ashley Young can compete for the role of England's backup left-winger.

Forwards: Carlos Tevez (Manchester City), Didier Drogba (Chelsea), Andrey Arshavin (Arsenal)

Following his previous round's free-kick winner against Chelsea, in this round's away game against Bolton, when the team was in a difficult situation, Carlos Tevez's double helped Manchester City twice equalize the score. These were Tevez's fourth goals in his last three competitive matches. Apart from the two goals, Tevez also delivered two key passes, made two tackles, and won two free kicks. The beast's form has been improving recently. Although Chelsea failed to secure all three points in the end, Drogba still managed to score twice. His world-class performance leaves even the most critical reviewers speechless. This season, Drogba has scored 13 goals in the Premier League and 18 across all competitions. We must now admit that Chelsea with or without Drogba is essentially two different teams. Despite being relatively inactive throughout the match, stars are stars. With just one exquisitely precise shot, Andrey Arshavin earned his place in our weekly best XI. At Anfield, the Tsar always delivers performances that leave everyone in awe. Remember the epic 4-4 clash in April? Arshavin scored four goals, silencing all doubters.
