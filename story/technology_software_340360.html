Abstract: The establishment of road signs aims to ensure smooth traffic flow and driving safety. It should integrate road alignment, traffic conditions, and facilities along the route, setting up signs according to different types of traffic signs for construction. This is conducive to providing accurate information to road users, guiding vehicles smoothly and quickly to their destinations through traffic signs.

Keywords: Road signs & roles and setting characteristics & issues to be considered in construction

0 Introduction

Traffic signs provide clear, intuitive, and easy-to-understand traffic information for participants, ensuring the safety and smooth travel of many travelers while maximizing the functionality of roads. Practical evidence shows that reasonably placed traffic signs can enhance road capacity, reduce traffic accidents, prevent traffic jams, save energy, lower pollution, and beautify the environment along and around the road.

1 Roles and Setting Characteristics of Traffic Signs and Signboards

Road signs play a significant role in modern road traffic. The principles and features of establishing traffic signs mainly include the following aspects:

1.1 Generally, signs are set on the right side of the road where drivers are urgently looking for them. For lane divisions like overtaking lanes, travel lanes, emergency parking strips, and road indication signs, if it's difficult to set them on the roadside, they usually use gantry supports, installing signboards above the road. To facilitate driver judgment and ensure traffic safety, near the highway terminal, some prohibitory or warning signs are also set within the central median.

1.2 The placement of traffic signs ensures that there is no mutual interference between signs and no obstructions from other structures blocking the view.

1.3 Near interchanges and service areas, sign placements follow the principle of not affecting the driver’s line of sight as vehicles approach intersections or diverging/merging points.

1.4 Sign placement ensures that drivers have enough reaction time to make necessary safety judgments. Therefore, to allow sign content to serve its intended purpose, signs should be set with an advance distance. Here, it's also important to note that the larger the signboard size and the simpler the sign content, the smaller this advance distance can be.

1.5 Roadside signs must maintain a 25cm clearance from the shoulder edge, and the bottom edge of the signboard must have a 2m clearance above the outer edge of the emergency parking strip. Signs set above the road must maintain a clearance of more than 5m between the bottom edge of the board and the road surface.

1.6 The front of the signboard faces the direction of incoming vehicles, arranged at a basic right angle to the road's direction, slightly tilted inward. Regarding the tilt issue of roadside signs, there are currently differing opinions. Some national standards suggest that only when the signboard is more than 9m away from the shoulder edge should it be tilted inward, whereas when the signboard is less than 9m away from the shoulder edge, it is instead tilted outward by 3° to prevent glare-induced dizziness.

2 Issues to Note in Traffic Sign Construction

The general construction sequence for traffic signs is: base positioning and layout → excavation of the foundation pit → pouring of foundation concrete → installation of signposts → installation of signboards.

2.1 Base Positioning and Layout

Traffic signs need to be set with a certain lead time to ensure drivers have enough reaction time to make accurate and safe judgments. From seeing the sign to performing the corresponding operation based on the information provided by the sign, drivers typically go through stages of perception, judgment, and operation. The time required for processing varies depending on the sign's content, generally taking 3 to 10 seconds. During this time, the vehicle has moved forward a certain distance. Therefore, to ensure the sign serves its full function, it should be set ahead of time, especially for warning signs, where the distance from the hazard point should be determined based on the calculated speed of travel and must not be less than the safe stopping sight distance.

Before construction, the approved construction organization design should be followed, using design drawings, control stakes provided during the site briefing, and actual terrain and object conditions to conduct the construction layout of the sign location. Using instruments such as a theodolite, steel tape, or total station, accurately determine the planar position of the traffic sign foundation and the center planar position of the traffic signpost. If facilities or high-voltage lines along the road obstruct the signboard, affecting the readability of the sign, adjust the sign's position and pay attention to controlling the foundation elevation. After the layout and positioning are completed, notify the supervising engineer before excavating the foundation pit for inspection or testing of the sign location.

2.2 Excavation of Foundation Pit

The location, geometric dimensions, and depth of the foundation pit should meet the requirements of the construction drawing design. The foundation pit should be excavated to the depth and size specified in the drawings. If the excavation face is disturbed, increase the excavation volume until it meets the design specifications. The base should be leveled and compacted while maintaining good elevation control. Double-column foundations cannot be constructed simultaneously. The excavated foundation pit should be protected from rainwater ingress. Only after the supervising engineer's approval can the next step of construction proceed.

2.3 Pouring of Foundation Concrete

The sign foundation should be poured on-site according to the design specifications, with quality control mainly reflected in the following aspects.

2.3.1 Concrete should be mixed according to the construction mix ratio.

2.3.2 The exposed parts of each base top should be formed according to the drawing requirements, conforming to construction technical standards.

2.3.3 The shape and dimensions of the foundation reinforcement arrangement should meet the drawing requirements. The intersection points of the reinforcements should be securely tied with iron wire of the specified diameter, ensuring no sliding or omissions occur.

2.3.4 The placement of the base flange should meet the drawing requirements. The base flange should be centered with the foundation, its top surface flush with the top of the concrete foundation, and the embedded anchor bolts should be vertically bound to the base flange, with the exposed length controlled between 10~20cm and properly protected.

2.3.5 Concrete should be poured tightly against the undisturbed excavation face. The strength of the poured concrete should meet the design requirements, and the pouring should be completed within 24 hours after excavation.

2.3.6 When pouring concrete, vibration must be performed. Whether manual or mechanical vibration is used, it should be carried out layer by layer in sequence. The tamping should be dense and uniform, without any instances of mold running or slurry leakage. Vibration should ensure the accurate positioning of the base flange and anchor bolts.

2.3.7 The top surface of the base should be smoothed, all exposed edges of the foundation neatly trimmed, and the concrete color consistent. The appearance of the concrete surface must be flat and glossy, with no honeycombing or pitting allowed.

2.3.8 After pouring is complete, avoid direct sunlight exposure and cure the concrete according to regulations.
