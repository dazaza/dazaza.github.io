Causing all contaminated patients to present with major pneumonia and lumbar disc herniation symptoms.

by cz10260ps7 on 2012-02-12 16:37:34

Symptoms of lumbar disc herniation. On the 27th, the Chinese University of Hong Kong stated that the medical school had previously analyzed the conditions of nearly 400 patients with H1N1 influenza A, and the results showed that 70% of the patients developed severe complications.

Researchers from the CUHK Medical School analyzed clinical records, treatment responses, and virus characteristics of 382 patients hospitalized due to H1N1 influenza A in Hong Kong between 2009 and 2010. The average age of these patients was 47 years old, with men accounting for 45% and women 55%. The results showed that 70% of the patients developed serious complications, among which 9% to 13% were critically ill, most being adults.

The researchers indicated that due to a large amount of virus replication in the lungs, patients experienced diffuse pneumonia and excessive inflammatory reactions. If only respiratory secretion tests were conducted on patients, it might lead to diagnostic errors. Moreover, traditional five-day antiviral treatment regimens are insufficient for treating severe symptoms.

The study also found that mutant viruses were detected during two peak periods of H1N1 influenza outbreaks: from July to October 2009 and from February to March 2010. These viruses could replicate efficiently in the lower respiratory tract, causing severe pneumonia in all infected patients, and the effects of antiviral treatments after patient infection were not ideal.

CUHK suggested conducting viral genome sequencing for severe patients during flu peaks to detect any possible mutant viruses; citizens should also receive annual flu vaccinations, as getting vaccinated between November and December each year can yield better immune results.
