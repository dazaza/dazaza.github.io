Very soon, the young couple indulged in forbidden fruit. However, not long after, they encountered "trouble" - Xiao Ying was pregnant. Later, Xiao Ying underwent an abortion with the accompaniment of Li Bin's mother.

Lawyer Li Huayang from Henan Huahao Law Firm believed that Xiao Ying's hope for a large compensation was slim. He said that the sexual activity between the two parties was consensual. Li Bin did not foresee the infertility resulting from the abortion, and there was no illegal harm or fault. Moreover, the abortion was agreed upon by both parties, so in principle, there was no liability for compensation.

He stated that in actual cases, courts would consider specific circumstances. If the female party indeed suffered serious physical damage, the male party might be given some compensation.

Breakup

Henan Business Daily (Weibo) reporter Wang Hongwei

In October 2008, Xiao Ying became pregnant again. However, in order for Li Bin to focus on his studies, Xiao Ying underwent another abortion. In early 2009, Xiao Ying became pregnant for the third time. The doctor said that frequent induced abortions could likely lead to infertility and advised them to keep the child.

However, Li Bin opposed it, leading to several big arguments. Ultimately, Xiao Ying underwent another abortion. This surgery caused significant damage, as her left fallopian tube was removed. The doctor said that she had basically lost her ability to bear children.

Xiao Ying, currently 21 years old, lives in Gongyi, Zhengzhou. In 2006, while she was in high school, she met her classmate Li Bin and fell in love. In 2007, academically excellent Li Bin was admitted to a university in Zhengzhou, while Xiao Ying moved to Zhengzhou to farm.

Afterward, Li Bin became increasingly cold toward Xiao Ying. Eventually, during an argument, Li Bin proposed breaking up. As a result, Xiao Ying chose litigation. She believed that due to Li Bin's irresponsibility, she lost her ability to bear children and demanded that Li Bin compensate her for personal injury, nutrition expenses, mental distress, and physical disability totaling over 300,000 yuan.

Very soon, a once loving couple ended up in court, with the girl suing the boy for over 300,000 yuan in compensation.

Yesterday, the People's Court of Gongyi City, Zhengzhou City, officially accepted the case. It is reported that this case is also the first health rights dispute case in Zhengzhou caused by induced abortions due to a romantic relationship leading to infertility.

Currently, the case is under trial.

Story

Upon hearing the doctor's words, 21-year-old Xiao Ying said she was devastated. Due to excessive abortions, she had basically lost her ability to bear children. The subsequent blow was even greater, as her boyfriend proposed breaking up.
