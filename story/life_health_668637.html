What precautions should women take during menstruation?

In summer, due to the hot weather, some women, being greedy for coolness and fearing heat, often neglect keeping warm and getting cold shocks during menstruation, which can easily lead to reproductive organ diseases and even systemic diseases, affecting physical and mental health. Therefore, it is very important for women to do a good job of keeping warm during menstruation, especially in summer. So, what should women avoid doing during their menstrual period?

1. Do not stay in air conditioning or in front of a fan for a long time

Women who stay in front of a fan or under air conditioning for a long time during menstruation can easily catch a cold, leading to common colds, reduced menstrual flow, irregular menstruation, and even amenorrhea.

2. Do not eat cold food

Eating too much cold food during menstruation can irritate the gastrointestinal mucosa, causing sudden spasms of the gastrointestinal blood vessels and reducing the secretion of digestive juices, directly affecting the digestive absorption function of the gastrointestinal tract, and even causing abdominal pain and diarrhea. At the same time, pelvic and genital areas are significantly congested during menstruation, and sudden cold stimulation can reflexively cause uterine and pelvic vascular spasm contractions, leading to dysmenorrhea, amenorrhea, and other gynecological problems.

3. Do not have sex

The cervix is open during menstruation, and the body's immunity decreases, so sexual intercourse should be avoided. If women have sex during this time, it can easily lead to external bacteria entering the body, causing infections such as vaginitis and cervical erosion.

4. Do not engage in strenuous exercise

Women are physically weaker during menstruation and should avoid participating in strenuous activities like high jump, long jump, throwing, 100-meter race, etc., and also avoid strength exercises that increase abdominal pressure, such as push-ups, to prevent excessive menstrual bleeding or endometrial displacement.

5. Do not sit on the floor

Some women are used to sitting on the floor after returning home. During menstruation, sitting on the floor can easily lead to cold exposure, poor blood circulation, and can cause menstrual scarcity, dysmenorrhea, and other gynecological problems.
