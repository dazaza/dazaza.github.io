1. Composition of the Golden Abacus Full-process E-commerce Platform

The Golden Abacus Full-process E-commerce Platform is a leading e-commerce solution for SMEs. It mainly consists of three parts: the Golden Abacus E-commerce Website (Golden Abacus YueXin Network www.iecgd.com), the Golden Abacus E-commerce Tools (Golden Abacus eTools), and the Golden Abacus E-commerce Supply Chain Management Software System (Golden Abacus 9i).

The characteristic of the Golden Abacus Full-process E-commerce Platform is its innovative integration of e-commerce and supply chain management, forming a unique new type of e-commerce model. This is mainly reflected in three aspects: First, it extends supply chain management from within the enterprise to external upstream suppliers and downstream customers, providing online applications such as eERP, eCRM, eSRM, and eOA (some functions support offline applications), forming a full-process supply chain management system. Second, it extends e-commerce from primarily publishing information and searching for business opportunities to electronic procurement, integrity certification, online payment, and collaborative work, forming a complete full-process e-commerce trading system. Third, it successfully integrates the Software as a Service (SaaS) model into the application fields of management software and e-commerce.

The Golden Abacus Full-process E-commerce Platform is a powerful tool for SMEs in the Internet era to share global resources, enhance brand awareness, strengthen corporate competitiveness, provide excellent services, win loyal customers, and achieve maximum value for enterprises and shareholders.

(a) Golden Abacus 9i

The Golden Abacus E-commerce Supply Chain Management Software System (Golden Abacus 9i) is the backend application system of the Golden Abacus Full-process E-commerce Platform. Through a series of software products such as 9i/eERP, 9i/eCRM, 9i/eSRM, and 9i/eOA, it constructs a broad and complete e-commerce supply chain management (eSCM) system kingdom.

i. 9i/eERP: Mainly manages the internal supply chain of the enterprise, including modules for planning, budgeting, purchasing, sales, inventory, finance, funds, assets, reports, etc.

ii. 9i/eCRM: Mainly manages downstream customers, including marketing, sales automation, and customer service.

iii. 9i/eSRM: Mainly manages upstream suppliers, including distribution, supplier relationships, partnerships, etc.

iv. 9i/eOA: Mainly manages electronic office work, collaborating with Golden Abacus BIM to create an efficient supply chain collaboration and online communication platform.

2. Provided to customers through the Software as a Service (SaaS) model

Golden Abacus Company provides full supply chain management software and full e-commerce services to customers through the innovative Software as a Service (SaaS) model.

i. What is the Software as a Service (SaaS) model

"Software as a Service" (SaaS) is an entirely innovative software application model that began to emerge in the 21st century with the development of Internet technology and the maturity of application software. Vendors deploy application software uniformly on their own servers, and customers can order the required application software services based on their actual needs via the Internet, paying fees according to the amount and duration of the ordered services, and obtaining services provided by vendors via the Internet. While providing Internet applications to customers, software vendors also offer offline operation and local data storage, allowing users to use their ordered software and services anytime, anywhere.

ii. SaaS Model Development Trend

The SaaS model is becoming an exciting development trend in the application software market. According to IDC research reports, in 2004, software released through the SaaS model achieved $4.2 billion in sales. In the next five years, this figure will continue to grow at an annual compound growth rate of 26%, reaching a total market size of $7.2 billion by 2008.

In developed regions like Europe and America, users have already given high recognition to the SaaS model, achieving good development. A survey report published by AMR Research in November 2005 targeting American users showed that more than 78% of companies across various vertical industries and different sizes currently use or consider using SaaS services. Only 18% of companies have no plans to use SaaS services temporarily.

Although Software as a Service (SaaS) is still a newly emerging phenomenon in China, due to the country's very favorable growth environment, it is currently receiving significant attention from the industry. Statistics show that there are approximately 12 million SMEs in our country, making up a very large group of potential consumers for Software as a Service (SaaS). Our SMEs face challenges such as limited IT budgets, lack of professional technical support personnel, and long decision-making times, resulting in low levels of IT popularization. On the other hand, the flexible and rapid development characteristics of SMEs urgently require professional IT systems and services to help improve work efficiency, enhance management quality, reduce operating costs, and strengthen core competitiveness. Software as a Service (SaaS) is the best way to resolve these contradictions, allowing users to order corresponding application software services based on their application needs from service providers, and adjust the content of the used services according to changes in the company's development, offering strong scalability and extensibility. At the same time, the professional maintenance and technical support required for these application services are handled by the service provider's professionals.

As customers gain huge benefits through Software as a Service (SaaS), for software vendors, it turns into a huge potential market. Users who previously couldn't afford software licensing fees or didn't have the ability to configure professional personnel now become potential customers. Meanwhile, the Software as a Service model can help vendors enhance differentiated competitive advantages, reduce development costs and maintenance costs, accelerate product or service entry into the market, effectively lower marketing costs, change their revenue model, and improve the relationship with customers.

Whether for customers or vendors, Software as a Service (SaaS) has strong appeal and will bring a win-win situation for both parties.

iv. Customer Value Brought by the SaaS Model

The benefits of obtaining application services from professional service providers through the SaaS method are obvious. Customers only need to use very simple PCs as terminal devices, eliminating the need to purchase, build, and maintain infrastructure and application programs like in the traditional software usage license model. At the same time, when using software, customers can purchase the required software services according to their application scope and usage time, providing more choices and higher flexibility for customers.

Since all application software is deployed and maintained by the service provider, users do not need to configure professional technical personnel themselves, and ordering services on demand can eliminate temporarily unnecessary services, greatly reducing the user's total cost of ownership and increasing the user's freedom of choice; meanwhile, the service provider will continuously update and improve the deployed application software, so compared to the traditional software provision method, the software services used by users will have faster product update cycles; all services provided through the SaaS method are used via the Internet, therefore, as long as there is Internet access, users can use their services without needing to be on a local network like traditional software. These features allow users to focus more on enhancing their core competitive capabilities.
