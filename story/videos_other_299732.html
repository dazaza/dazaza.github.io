Giants praise AC Milan's new No. 10 as being like Boban, revealing that Kaka also felt regret over his departure.

by kersfe3f23 on 2009-12-09 09:27:39

Giants praise AC Milan's new No. 10 as being like Boban, revealing that Kaka also regrets leaving.

This Monday morning, before heading to Zurich with the team, AC Milan vice president Galliani was interviewed by reporters at Malpensa Airport, briefly addressing some of the recent hot topics surrounding the team.

Galliani said: "Now we are not thinking about Serie A. The competition in these two leagues is different. We are facing both the UEFA Champions League and Serie A. Now all we can think about is the competition, the only thing on our minds. Serie A is important, and we will do our best, but the UEFA Champions League belongs to us. We will consider Serie A after we return from Zurich. Now we are only thinking about how to win in Zurich. We cannot just sit nervously waiting to hear if Marseille beats Real Madrid. Obviously, if Marseille doesn't win, then no matter what the result of this match is, it won't be a problem for advancing. However, we must strive for victory. After earning 4 points from Real Madrid and another 4 points from Marseille, it is strange that we still don't have a guaranteed spot in the next round. It's regrettable that the only 3 points Zurich got were from us at our home ground, and now we aim to take those 3 points back from their home field. I don't make predictions; no one knows before the game if they will win. So far, we've earned 4 points from Real Madrid, 4 points from Marseille, and nothing from Zurich."

Galliani continued: "AC Milan is playing very well right now. We have won a series of important matches and played exceptionally well. As a connoisseur, I can say that yesterday (last Sunday) I basically watched all the games, and I thought ours was simply extraordinary, almost like a different sport. I watched the game through Milan Channel, and I think AC Milan was absolutely brilliant in the first 30 minutes. Abate and Antonini? We have always had such players. Clearly, when we bring in world-class players for every position, some players will be loaned out to other Serie A teams to gain experience. And our youth team has always achieved remarkable results. Now we have slightly changed our policy. Now we let these young players be the starters, and they are performing excellently. I remember when Borrello first came to AC Milan, he was young. He went through many teams, but indeed, he was developed by our youth team. In fact, we also have Di Gennaro and other excellent youngsters, but regardless, seeing Abate and Antonini start tomorrow night's game brings immense joy."

Discussing key players, especially Gattuso, Galliani said: "My relationship with Gattuso is unshakable, and we hope his relationship with Milan remains unshakable. Seedorf is an exceptional player. In 1995, at just 19 years old, he already represented Ajax in the UEFA Champions League final against us in Vienna, where his Ajax team ultimately triumphed. When Rui Costa gradually left AC Milan, Seedorf replaced him to become the new attacking midfielder No. 10 for AC Milan. Seedorf is somewhat like Boban, and if he were to play as a winger, he would definitely perform outstandingly. He has a glorious record, and Seedorf's experiences are extraordinary. As an attacking midfielder, he is truly a superstar. After hearing Leonardo's instructions and watching Seedorf's performance on the field, I believe that whether we have possession or the opponent does, Seedorf has done well in fulfilling what Leonardo asked of him. Borrello is performing well now, but Inzaghi is eternal. Kaka? I regret that he left AC Milan, and it could be said that both of us feel regretful."
