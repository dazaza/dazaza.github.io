Alibaba's 30 Global Interns Graduate

by anonymous on 2013-09-08 19:23:58

Some people's daily homework is to write emails to their mentors reporting the most impressive things.

On August 6th, Sesame Open of Alibaba welcomed 30 global interns who enjoyed a four-week guidance from one-on-one executive mentors. Recently, these interns graduated happily and noisily. They wrote on the video they gave themselves: "Alibaba is just a station on my life journey. We came down to see the scenery, and then we will rush to the next journey."

This group of young people, the oldest born in 1986 and the youngest in 1993, are all very excellent. So what did they do and learn at this company, under the guidance of executive mentors?

Internship Reflection A

Don't say "not suitable" easily.

"It turns out I can also be a pretty good technician or technical supervisor." This is a reflection written by Shi Zhuojie in his internship diary. This third-year boy once appeared in the People's Daily for his entrepreneurship with new media at school. "I have a clear purpose coming to Alibaba. I just want to see how their operations are done, which will give me some inspiration for my future entrepreneurship."

However, after a month passed, the dream of this particularly rational boy changed a bit, "I originally planned to start a business after working for two or three years, but now I think I should work in a big company for at least ten years before starting a business, because organizational structure, management philosophy, etc., many things depend on whether your thinking is correct."

He said, "Although I study computer science, I am actually not interested in technology, and I don't think being a programmer is my ideal. But my mentor at Ali is a technical person. I learned from him that doing technology is not about individual programming. In a team of programmers, how to reduce communication costs within the team and increase everyone's enthusiasm are all knowledge. I used to think I wasn't interested in technology without studying it deeply, which was wrong."

He summarized: Many things are just due to lack of experience. Don't negate yourself first. Study the methods of doing things. There are only wrong methods, no unsuitable matters.

Internship Reflection B

What exactly is initiative?

Studying in the United States at the University of Washington, self-learning Italian and French, reducing her weight from 128 pounds to 96 pounds, becoming a signed model under one of the top three modeling companies globally—such a girl has enough material for an inspiring novel. Can you say she lacks initiative?

The 20-year-old Li Yanjin said, "In the first three days of the internship, my mentor asked me to look at forums, and I couldn't stand it. On the third day, when my mentor talked to me, I said first, 'Can I ask you a question? Why did you let me look at forums for three days?', then the mentor sighed, 'I once thought you were a very proactive person. Now I think you are waiting for others to give you tasks. I suggest you take the initiative to find tasks.'

After realizing this, Li Yanjin took the initiative to find Qu Yang, the class teacher of the interns, and requested to hold a 'discussion meeting' about herself, telling everyone how she became a model, and how she went from a model with not-so-good conditions to signing with such a prestigious modeling company. "I actually wanted to change everyone's perception of me. Everyone would say to me, 'Oh, you're that model,' but I'm not just a model, and I don't want to be just a model. I want to learn how to become someone influential in fashion through the internet."

Indeed, after one discussion meeting, her nickname changed from 'goddess' to 'god', because everyone thought she was not only a tough girl, but had a man's heart.

Internship Reflection C

Sink into your experiences, don't rush.

Xiaoshi, the intern of Mentor Xia Ji, is a girl with rich experiences: She once attended the UN Young Women Partners Conference and volunteered in Cambodia. From the very beginning of the internship, Xiaoshi set herself a goal, dividing the month into seven parts, doing something in each module managed by her mentor every three days.

"When I saw Xiaoshi, I felt that her personality doesn't match her experiences. Her experiences are very rich, but she looks like a student. Sometimes, I think she moves too fast and hasn't sunk into her own experiences properly." Therefore, Xia Ji arranged Xiaoshi's internship homework to write an email to him every day, including three aspects: what she did every day, the most impressive thing, and life insights.

In one email he sent back to Xiaoshi, he subtly enlightened her: "The people you met yesterday afternoon have worked on average for 12 years, and experienced on average 3 companies. The failures, blows, and pains they went through are important reasons why they are what they are today. Time is the most important factor in growth. Time gives us different experiences, different harvests, and different insights. That's how mature people come about."

Mentor Xia Ji's painstaking efforts naturally made Xiaoshi realize: "Teacher asked me to chat with people, to choose meetings I am interested in. At first, I didn't like sharing sessions, thinking they were somewhat impractical and distracted from doing things. But later, I found that sharing sessions were very interesting. Many knowledge cannot be obtained simply by doing things. Life shouldn't be just about achieving a result. Pain and confusion are another kind of fun in work."

Xiaoshi remembered a sentence Xia Ji wrote to her: "On the road of idealism, pain and failure far outnumber flowers and success. So, don't rush."

The most fun thing: Adventure game

The most sense of accomplishment: Being treated to dinner by Lu Zhaoxi

A month of internship, speaking of which, isn't long.

When reporters interviewed them for the first time, they were still unfamiliar partners, unsure whether they should shake hands or give each other a hug upon meeting; a month later, they became close partners, gaining full friendship.

Recalling this month of internship, the most fun thing everyone agreed on was the adventure activity divided into groups. The 30 interns were divided into three groups, each doing something they always wanted to do but dared not: For example, someone climbed Laoshan Mountain at 4 a.m., someone performed on the street, and someone scared people wearing a mask.

And as for the most sense of accomplishment, it should be the opportunity for someone to be treated to dinner by Alibaba's CEO Lu Zhaoxi.
