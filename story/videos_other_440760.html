American Deep Springs College admits Chinese students, requiring 20 hours of cattle herding and grass planting per week

by anonymous on 2011-06-01 17:42:40

The grassland is a classroom. Raising cattle and pigs are also required courses. Wan Xin from Nanjing Foreign Language School has been admitted by Deep Springs College.

To outsiders, this is an isolated university; it is located in the desert valley of eastern California, USA. It is also a mysterious university with a two-year academic system, admitting no more than 15 students each year, all male. These young men can receive full scholarships here but must work for 20 hours a week—raising cattle, planting grass, living a cowboy-style life; it is also a legendary university where many students have given up admission opportunities from Yale and Harvard to come here, and after graduation, they will be sought after by world-class prestigious universities... This is Deep Springs College, called the "Utopia" for outstanding students, and also a successful example of American higher education experiments. Wan Xin from Nanjing Foreign Language School will have such an extraordinary college experience—he has been admitted by Deep Springs College!

How amazing is Deep Springs College?

Deep Springs College is an unconventional college located in the desert of eastern California, USA. The college was founded in 1917 by electric power tycoon Lucien Lucius Nunn. Nunn believed that the material world is full of sins, and true great people should be able to listen to the "voice of the desert." The management of the college is exceptionally strict; students are not allowed to leave the campus without permission, alcohol is strictly prohibited, and watching TV is not encouraged. Phones and the internet often get interrupted due to bad weather, and newspapers are delivered through the post office, usually two days late. Currently, there are 26 students at the school, and including faculty members, less than 40 people.

"In order to understand Deep Springs, I did not choose a telephone interview for my interview questions, but rather went for an on-site interview. Thus, at the end of January, I completed a peculiar 'Deep Springs journey,'" Wan Xin told reporters. He flew from Shanghai to San Francisco for eleven hours, then transferred to Reno City (phonetic translation), and took a six-hour bus ride to near the school. Finally, he took a school bus over a high mountain and arrived at Deep Springs College deep in the desert, which took over 30 hours of travel. "I was very curious about Deep Springs, and from departure to arrival, I didn't sleep for 36 hours."

Students entering Deep Springs College are mostly academically excellent high school students. "As far as I know, among the current students, several were admitted by famous universities like Harvard and Yale during their application process, but ultimately chose Deep Springs over Ivy League universities. Some students transferred into Deep Springs after studying one or two years at Princeton University or Williams College," Wan Xin told reporters. The average SAT score of the school's students is very high, and nine out of ten students who are admitted choose to attend the school.

It is understood that the academic system of Deep Springs is two years long, and after two years, students transfer to other regular American universities to complete the remaining two years of study. However, due to the high quality of Deep Springs' student body and its unique style, combined with its academic reputation and the leadership qualities cultivated by a hardworking life, students have significant advantages when transferring. Nearly 80% of graduates go directly to Harvard, Yale, Brown, Columbia, Cornell, and Oxford University to continue their studies as third-year students after leaving Deep Springs, and more than half of the students eventually obtain doctorate degrees. In terms of career paths, students from Deep Springs rarely choose to make money solely for the sake of wealth. More than 40% become lawyers, doctors, or professors, while writers, journalists, and poets are also popular career choices.

Wan Xin introduced the distinctive features of Deep Springs' educational approach in detail to the reporters. First is academics; students at Deep Springs College do not spend more time on academics because each person works at least 20 hours a week on farm labor and spends a lot of time participating in political activities. Generally, the requirement per semester is 2-3 courses, and if you want to take more classes, you need to apply. The website explains: "Here, excessive workload is a privilege, and we do not distribute it easily." However, the academic reputation of the school is very good, with students winning national-level academic awards in the United States every year on average.

Second is labor; labor is an inseparable part of the school. The concept of the school is that labor and politics are part of education. The entire school is located on a private farm in the valley, forming a self-sufficient community, like the legendary Peach Blossom Spring. The operation of the entire farm depends on the labor of these students and teachers.

The third feature of the school is student governance. All school affairs, including teacher hiring and admissions, are handled by the students. "For example, every year there is a discussion about whether to admit female students, but so far it hasn't been decided."

What makes "Brother Deep Springs" special?

Wan Xin attended Lasa Road Primary School in Nanjing during elementary school, and the solid foundation laid during his primary school years made him handle learning with ease. It is understood that Wan Xin did not attend any training classes outside when he was young, but instead focused on "learning well in class." Although he did not engage in extra tutoring, Wan Xin still ranked at the top in various competitions, especially excelling in informatics, allowing him to smoothly enter Nanjing Foreign Language School for junior high.

"Wan Xin is a very special child; his IQ is high, but his EQ is even higher," said Ms. Deng Jia, the head teacher of Class 1, Grade 12 at Nanjing Foreign Language School. When she first took over the science class, upon entering the classroom with some trepidation, a very enthusiastic voice greeted her: "Hi, teacher!" Since then, this sunny big boy has impressed the head teacher immensely. "Wan Xin is in the science experimental class, so everyone here is an academic standout. But besides having a high IQ, Wan Xin has an even higher EQ." To illustrate this point, Ms. Deng gave a small example. "This child possesses rare responsibility, coordination skills, and leadership abilities. I remember once during a school debate competition, the atmosphere on the stage was tense with heated arguments. Our class had already gained a clear advantage, and the opposing debaters were under immense pressure. Just after the debate ended, Wan Xin, as one of the debaters, immediately grabbed the microphone and said to everyone: 'On the stage, we are opponents, but off the stage, we are still the best brotherly class.' His words immediately sparked applause from the entire audience."

He is a "sports star," already "famous" at Nanjing Foreign Language School.

Basketball, badminton, football... Wan Xin is a sports enthusiast and stands tall. "I was elected as the sports minister of the school in my sophomore year, so I think my physical strength should be good enough to cope with the heavy labor at Deep Springs," Wan Xin smiled and told reporters. "He matches the character of Deep Springs," Ms. Deng Jia said. Wan Xin was already well-known at school before becoming 'Brother Deep Springs,' because he has a strong sense of mission to serve society and humanity. For example, during the sophomore student council election, although Wan Xin had the ability to run for student council president, he strategically chose the role of sports minister, and eventually, three students from his class entered the student council. "Deep Springs cultivates leaders, and its graduates are not just workers but individuals with unique thinking abilities."

He won over "Deep Springs" with his "voice."

"Besides solid grades, the application essays are very important. In the first round, three essays were required, and in the second round, four essays were needed." Wan Xin told reporters that his favorite essay inspiration came from a class reunion. At the dinner table, one classmate complained about the noise from renovations upstairs, and everyone started discussing the sounds around their homes. Suddenly, I realized that regardless of what I write, there are always elements of various sounds in my writing. That's how I discovered my sensitivity to sound, leading me to write an essay centered around sound. This essay received praise from 'Deep Springs' because it expressed my genuine inner thoughts."

In addition, American universities place great emphasis on students' social activities, but continuity and depth are important. Wan Xin started volunteering in his sophomore year, working as a guide at the Nanjing Museum. By his senior year, his guiding scope expanded from one hall to four and a half halls.

The Deep Springs interview could be conducted via phone or on-campus, and Wan Xin chose to interview on-campus. The interview lasted only 15 minutes, but most of the interviewers were students, spending four whole days with Wan Xin. "I think I was chosen because we got along pretty well," Wan Xin said.

If admitted to Deep Springs College, it means you can save approximately $50,000 annually in tuition and living expenses, as the school provides a full scholarship to each student. However, the cost is 20 hours of manual labor per week, and students must work alongside staff raising cattle and farming at the ranch.

It is understood that in 2006, a Chinese boy from Suzhou entered Deep Springs to experience this unique education in the world.

However, regarding such an opportunity, Wan Xin's parents have more concerns. It is understood that Wan Xin has also been admitted to prestigious universities like the University of Pennsylvania, and therefore, pursuing a regular university is what his parents hope for him. "Perhaps my parents would prefer me to become an engineer, but I am now listening to different opinions, and I hope to ultimately do what I want to do." Wan Xin obviously already has plans for his future.

"The desert has a profound character. It has a voice. You must listen to hear it, not amidst the struggle and clamor for material gain. Gentlemen, why have you come to this wilderness? Not for traditional academic training, nor for pastoral life; not for success in business or personal interests in your careers. You have come to prepare yourselves to serve with your lives, understanding that exceptional abilities and noble beliefs are expected of you."

— Founder of Deep Springs College, electric power tycoon Lucien Lucius Nunn

By Wang Ying
