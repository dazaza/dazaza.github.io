Taobao's profit-driven evil starts, large-scale crackdown forces out small and medium sellers

by longyi on 2008-08-31 13:56:00

I am an average seller on Taobao. I have several N-Diamond stores and new stores with three or four hearts. Each transaction is a hard work, and I am seriously paying for it, responsible for myself and also for the buyers. I can say that I have no regrets. My stores have been affected by Taobao's crackdown on small and medium sellers in the past six months (these stores operate different products, there is no "redundant store opening"). In the QQ group and Wangwang group, most of the Taobao friends have been sealed several stores or punished by Taobao for various reasons. Taobao's purpose is now very clear: to wash us small and medium sellers! The following are what I and several Taobao friends summarized. They may not be all correct and comprehensive, but they guarantee to speak the truth, not fabricate or slander, not personal attack, and not full of foul language.

A year ago, or even earlier, meticulous store owners found that after entering keywords in the Taobao search box, the first thing presented to users was the "popular items" or directly the "all items" under the column, instead of the "window recommendation" column. "Window recommendation" is the product that the store owner considers will sell well or urgently needs to sell, and the order is arranged according to the remaining time. Now, the carefully set recommended products by the sellers every day have become useless. All protest posts on the Taobao forum were deleted. What are the standards for the front page in the "popular items"? Collection volume? Current sales? Total sales? Browsing volume? It seems none of them. Next, Taobao began to vigorously promote his so-called Yahoo Express, and some activities that only consumer protection sellers can join. Its intention has already been self-evident.

Next, Taobao's "review gate" incident, even if the buyer's review on the seller is malicious and untrue, as long as it does not belong to XXXX, XXXX situations, the complaint about malicious reviews will no longer be accepted. The hundred percent perfect credit pursued and cherished by the majority of sellers will be regarded as worthless from now on. And apart from the good review rate, more serious is that unscrupulous customers threaten to lower prices without success or disagreeing with words, then give the seller such retaliatory bad comments as "the clothes are like a mop" or "I dare to say it's one hundred percent fake and has been tested at XXXX" or "my face is rotten after using your cosmetics" or even "my wife and I were tested positive for AIDS after using your condoms". How can the seller do business?? During that period, there were still gun articles on the Taobao forum, earnestly advising everyone not to take the so-called 100% too seriously, which was really laughable and disgusting. Everyone can see that he is paving the way for his new mall evaluation system. The mall evaluation system only scores and has no good, medium, or poor evaluation. It feels too fake. This move has been opposed and criticized on the Taobao forum and other forums. At this time, it can be seen that Taobao no longer cares about the appeals of small and medium sellers. Taobao is not a non-profit organization. Small and medium sellers cannot bring any profit to it, and it turns its face as soon as it sees no profit. What conscience and sense of social responsibility are all nonsense.
