Waterproofing treatment method for construction joints in the capital's subway

by fangfuv09 on 2010-04-09 17:57:58

**Title: Waterproofing Treatment Methods for Construction Joints in Subway Projects**

**Abstract:** This paper provides a detailed description and comparison of waterproofing methods used for construction joints in subway structures, focusing on the construction and outcome aspects. It analyzes the advantages and disadvantages of various waterproofing treatment methods. Three specific methods are introduced: single-component water-swelling joint sealant combined with pre-embedded grouting pipes, steel-edged rubber waterstop, and slow-expansion bentonite rubber water-swelling waterstop strips. The material properties, installation methods, waterproofing principles, and applicable scopes of these three methods are discussed.

**Keywords:** Subway; Construction joints; Waterproofing; Construction

---

### **0 Introduction**

In the construction of Beijing's new subway lines (including Line 5, Line 4, Line 10, and the Olympic trunk line), most sections are underground tunnels located in the multi-layered clay, sand, gravel, and boulder interlayers of the Quaternary alluvial plain in the city core. These tunnels are significantly affected by groundwater, including upper perched water, phreatic water, and confined water. To ensure that the subway structure is free from leaks and meets operational safety and structural lifespan requirements, effective permanent waterproofing is crucial. In subway projects, it is well-known that "nine out of ten joints leak," especially construction joints. There are various methods to treat construction joints in subway projects, each with different requirements. However, due to unfamiliarity with these methods, many construction personnel may inadvertently affect the quality of the work. This paper introduces the current methods for treating subway construction joints to aid future construction efforts.

### **1 Project Details**

The first section of Beijing Subway Line 10 runs from Wanliu Station to Suzhou Street Station, with a starting point at K0+497 and an endpoint at K1+406.9, covering a total length of 909.9 meters. The line spacing is 5 meters. From Wanliu Station, the tunnel passes through the southern side of Bagou Village South Road, gas stations, transfer stations, and fish ponds of the Wanliu Fishing World before continuing eastward. It crosses under the pile foundations of the elevated bridge on Wanchuanhe Road using an "S"-shaped curve (R-400m) and then proceeds along the northern side of Haidian North Road to Suzhou Street Station. The K0+730~K1+406.9 section uses a twin-connected arch tunnel structure constructed using the mining method.

### **3 Waterproofing Construction Methods for Beijing Subway Construction Joints**

#### **3.1 Dual-purpose Water-swelling Joint Sealant and Pre-embedded Grouting Pipes**

##### **3.1.1 Material Requirements and Properties**

- The water-swelling joint sealant should possess rapid expansion characteristics. It is an amorphous product that solidifies after extrusion. Its thickness after curing is 15~20mm, and its height is 8~10mm.

- The inner diameter of the grouting pipe is typically 8~15mm. Grouting pipes can have either a spring skeleton or a resin skeleton, both of which meet grouting requirements.

- The skeleton must be securely attached to the outer woven fabric without any separation or loosening.

Specific performance indicators are shown in Table 2.

1. Grouting pipes made of sponge rubber + plastic skeletons must meet requirements 1, 2, 5, and 6 in the table.

2. Grouting pipes made of filter cloth and spring skeletons must meet requirements 1~4.

3. Grouting pipes must undergo laboratory testing to ensure that the grout can adequately fill concrete cracks (less than 0.2mm) under the recommended grouting pressure provided by the material supplier. The type of grout must be determined before testing to avoid changes affecting the grouting results.

##### **3.1.2 Waterproofing Principle**

The grouting pipe features multiple grouting holes covered by netting and swelling rubber strips, acting as check valves. Advantages include:

1. Easy on-site installation.

2. Ability to repair leaks via grouting. During grouting, the swelling rubber strip compresses, allowing the grout to penetrate into the construction joint gap, providing excellent waterproofing performance.

Grouting pipes are connected every 4~5 meters, and grouting is performed through these pipes to evenly distribute the grout and fill gaps between the joint sealants, achieving water-stopping effects.

##### **3.1.3 Construction Method and Requirements**

1. The surface of the construction joint where the joint sealant is applied must be roughened to remove loose particles, dust, and debris. The surface should be clean, dry, and slightly uneven.

2. The water-swelling joint sealant should have rapid expansion characteristics. After extrusion, its cured thickness is 15~20mm, and its height is 8~10mm. It is applied using a dedicated applicator and adhered to the construction joint surface, positioned 10cm from the structural centerline on both sides.

3. After applying the joint sealant, it should be protected from water exposure during rainy weather or construction to prevent premature swelling, which could reduce its waterproofing effectiveness.

4. Grouting pipes are fixed using specialized clips at the centerline of the construction joint, with a fixing distance of 40~50cm. They are installed continuously along the joint. Connections are made using butt joints, with effective overlap lengths not less than 2cm.

5. Grouting pipes must be sealed permanently at their openings to prevent blockages caused by debris during concrete pouring. Grouting materials typically include polyurethane or acrylic resin grouts. Grouting pressure is determined based on the material supplier's recommendations and site conditions but should not exceed levels that could damage the secondary lining concrete. Typical grouting pressures range from 0.3~2.0MPa. Grouting is usually performed after the overall structure is completed but before water ingress occurs.

##### **3.1.4 Applicable Scope**

This method is suitable for:

- Construction joints in the main structure of underground stations.

- Construction joints at station entrances/exits.

- Construction joints between stations and tunnels.

- Construction joints at ventilation shaft interfaces.

- Construction joints on both sides of the post-pour ring beams in shield tunnel portals.

- Construction joints at cross-sectional changes in tunnel segments.

- Construction joints at interfaces with existing structures (equivalent to post-pour strips).

#### **3.2 Steel-edged Rubber Waterstop**

##### **3.2.1 Material Requirements and Properties**

- HJGB-type rubber waterstop, width 350mm, made of natural rubber.

- Suitable for saline water seepage prevention projects, operating temperature range -40~60℃.

##### **3.2.2 Waterproofing Principle**

The steel-edged rubber waterstop has thick plates on both wings. When bonded to concrete, the steel plates create strong adhesion with the surrounding concrete structure and the rubber. During deformation, only the rubber portion stretches and thickens, preventing water leakage between the steel plates, concrete, and rubber. Thus, the steel-edged rubber waterstop enhances the waterproofing performance of the structure. Its primary function relies on the compression or stretching of the rubber segments between the concrete deformation joints to achieve sealing. Additionally, the steel plate ensures good adhesion with the concrete, enabling the waterstop to withstand minor tensile and torsional forces while remaining effective within the allowable deformation range of the structure.

##### **3.2.3 Installation Method and Requirements**

1. The steel-edged rubber waterstop is fixed to the structural reinforcement using wire ties at intervals of 40cm. The fixation must be secure to prevent the waterstop from collapsing during concrete pouring and vibration, ensuring proper embedding in the concrete.

2. Horizontal waterstops are installed using a basin-style method (except for inverted arch areas). The basin opening faces upward to allow air bubbles generated during concrete pouring to escape. See Figure 3 for details.

3. All connections except for butt joints (T-shaped, cross-shaped, etc.) must be factory-made and not performed on-site. Butt joints can be made using cold bonding. The process involves:

- Using U-shaped clamps supplied by the manufacturer to secure two steel-edged waterstops together, followed by riveting with a rivet gun.

- Wrapping the connection with a 14cm-wide, 2mm-thick butyl double-sided tape.

- Attaching a 20cm-wide, 3mm-thick rubber sheet to the inside of the connection using U-shaped clamps.

- For structures with thicknesses ≤50cm, the waterstop is placed at the centerline, with no more than 5cm difference in thickness on either side.

- For structures thicker than 50cm, the concrete thickness on the side closer to the construction personnel should be 25~30cm to facilitate later repairs.

4. During concrete pouring and vibration, care must be taken to support the waterstop manually to prevent excessive serpentine deformation or collapse.

5. Cleaning debris between the waterstop and retaining structures can be challenging. Therefore, the construction joint surface must be thoroughly cleaned and roughened.

6. Concrete around the waterstop must be fully vibrated to ensure a tight bond with the waterstop. Vibration rods must not contact the waterstop.

##### **3.2.4 Applicable Scope**

This method is suitable for:

- Construction joints in the water-facing surfaces of underground stations.

- Construction joints in mined tunnels.

- Construction joints in cut-and-cover tunnels.

##### **3.2.5 Advantages and Disadvantages**

Advantages:

- Easy installation.

- Provides reliable waterproofing performance.

Disadvantages:

- Limited opportunities for post-construction repairs.

- Difficult to ensure proper adhesion between the waterstop and concrete, especially in arch walls or top slabs.

- Connections between waterstops are prone to failure due to challenges in achieving perfect adhesion with butyl double-sided tape.

#### **3.3 Pre-manufactured (PZ) Water-swelling Rubber Waterstop Strips**

##### **3.3.1 Material Requirements and Properties**

Pre-manufactured water-swelling rubber waterstop strips, also known as PZ-type water-swelling waterstop strips, are products made by mixing rubber with water-soluble high-molecular materials. These strips combine the characteristics of regular rubber products with the ability to swell upon contact with water, thereby achieving waterproofing functionality. The swollen material retains rubber-like properties, making it more resistant to water, acids, and alkalis. The strips exhibit excellent elasticity, flexibility, and swelling capabilities, with expansion rates adjustable between 40%~250%. The swelling rate is unaffected by water quality.

##### **3.3.2 Waterproofing Principle**

When the material swells upon contact with water, it deforms by up to 2~5 times its original size, filling irregular surfaces, voids, and gaps completely. This generates significant contact pressure, effectively preventing water infiltration.

##### **3.3.3 Construction Method and Requirements**

The pre-manufactured water-swelling rubber waterstop strips can be directly adhered to the construction joint surface using their inherent stickiness. Upon contact with water, they expand to seal the joint and surrounding micro-cracks, achieving reliable waterproofing. Care must be taken during storage and transportation to avoid heavy pressure, which could deform the material.

1. Clean the construction joint surface, removing dust and ensuring it is dry.

2. Reserve a trapezoidal groove on the construction joint surface, with dimensions: top width 20mm, bottom width 30mm, depth 10mm. The groove centerline should be 20~25cm from the structural surface.

3. Place the adhesive waterstop strip into the groove, pressing it flat. Remove the isolation paper and apply a decorative or protective layer if necessary.

4. If reserving a groove is difficult, the waterstop strip can be directly fixed to the construction joint surface using a dedicated adhesive. Alternatively, cement nails can be used if the surface is too wet to adhere, with a maximum fixing interval of 20cm.

5. During concrete pouring, ensure adequate vibration to guarantee a tight bond between the concrete and the waterstop strip. Vibration rods must not contact the waterstop strip.

##### **3.3.4 Applicable Scope**

This method is suitable for:

- Permanent vertical shaft construction joints.

- Ventilation shaft construction joints.

- Station floor slab construction joints.

The cross-section dimensions of the waterstop strip are 10mm x 20mm. Refer to Figure 4 for installation details.

##### **3.3.5 Advantages and Disadvantages**

Advantages:

- Simple installation process.

Disadvantages:

- Requires reserving a groove in the concrete for later casting.

- Due to the rigidity of the rubber strips, even closely spaced fixing points may result in poor adhesion between the waterstop strip and the construction joint surface. This increases the risk of the strip being embedded in the subsequent concrete pour, rendering it ineffective.

- Limited opportunities for post-construction repairs.
