Follow the moon exploration: China Lunar Exploration Network

by cacard on 2007-08-17 09:32:34

"Chang'e-1" is expected to be launched at an opportune time during the second half of this year. After the satellite is launched, it will take approximately 10 to 12 days to enter the lunar orbit. This is China's first deep-space exploration mission. This morning, the expert group of the Lunar Exploration Project provided a detailed interpretation of the "moon-bound" roadmap on the official website of the Commission for Science, Technology and Industry for National Defense (COSTIND).

Hao Xifan, deputy director of the Moon Exploration Project Center of the Commission for Science, Technology and Industry for National Defense, revealed that the lunar exploration project is planned in three phases, abbreviated as "orbiting, landing, and returning." It is estimated that by around 2020, it will be possible to collect soil samples from the moon and return them to Earth.

The journey to the moon will take about 10 days.

Sun Zegzhou, deputy chief designer of the satellite system for the circumlunar detection project, stated that "Chang'e-1" will be launched from Earth and orbit the Earth 5-7 times before reaching the moon in 10-12 days. The orbit is elliptical. "Chang'e-1" is a census-type exploration satellite, and a 200-kilometer orbit can meet the mission requirements. Its task is to perform a circumlunar detection, after which it will not return to Earth.

During the flight of the satellite, there are multiple processes of acceleration and deceleration, so the flight speed of the satellite varies at different stages. The entry speed of the Earth-Moon transfer orbit is about 10.9 km/s.

To reach the moon, the spacecraft must undergo 9-10 orbit changes.

According to Sun Zegzhou, deputy chief designer of the satellite system for the circumlunar detection project, the satellite needs to undergo 9-10 orbit changes before entering its designated orbit. Among these, the most critical ones are the third perigee (entering the Earth-Moon transfer orbit) and the first perilune (achieving lunar capture). These two orbit changes have the characteristics of being brief and unique. If they are not controlled properly, there is a risk of either colliding with the moon or flying away from it. Regarding issues related to orbit changes, Sun Zegzhou stated that they have been resolved.

Hao Xifan indicated that China's lunar exploration project is the country's first attempt to leave Earth and explore extraterrestrial bodies. In terms of engineering technology, China's satellite technology originates from Earth satellites, but it still needs to be tested under lunar conditions. Previously, China's telemetry and control technology had only been used within a range of 40,000 kilometers, but the distance to the moon is 380,000 kilometers away.
