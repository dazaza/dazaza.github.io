Seventeen Benefits of Mainland People Registering a Hong Kong Company

You might not have imagined the numerous benefits that registering a Hong Kong company could bring you. An ordinary Chinese person can establish a company in Hong Kong simply with their ID card, without requiring capital or even leaving home, thereby becoming a genuine international company owner. In reality, Hong Kong is one of the freest commercial regions in the world. After China joined the WTO, Hong Kong has consistently served as the locomotive for China's leap forward. Seizing business opportunities and setting up a company in Hong Kong can significantly boost your business.

Hong Kong covers only 1,103 square kilometers, yet thousands of new companies are established there every day. What exactly is the attraction? Let’s take a closer look: First, Hong Kong serves as an important window and bridge for China's opening-up to the outside world. It is the channel for mainland external contacts and the gateway for mainland enterprises to go global. Secondly, Hong Kong is a highly developed international metropolis, known as one of the freest trading ports in the world. With its excellent infrastructure and sound legal system, it provides entrepreneurs and businessmen with unparalleled business environments. Therefore, more and more mainland businessmen are establishing their own companies in Hong Kong for various purposes, expanding their businesses through different channels, enhancing their international image, and improving their competitive capabilities.

(1) Free choice of company name: Company names can include regional and industry characteristics, such as "China," "Beijing," "Zhejiang," "Hong Kong," "United States," "France," "International," "Asia," "College," "Publishing House," "Association," "Group," "Holding," "Industry," "Development," "Investment," "Finance," "Fellowship," "Technology," "Medical," "Fund," "Promotion Association," etc.

(2) Minimal restrictions on business scope: Finance, medicine, shipping transportation, import-export trade, real estate, construction, decoration, information networks, tourism, colleges, cultural publishing, associations, research institutes, and other high-tech industries can all be part of our business.

(3) Favorable low-tax environment: Hong Kong has low tax rates and fewer tax categories. Many international organizations utilize Hong Kong's tax advantages for reasonable tax avoidance. When we establish a company in Hong Kong, we generally need to pay two types of taxes: one is a one-time stamp duty on registered capital, at a rate of 1/1000, which is calculated based on your actual registered capital; the other is profits tax, at a rate of 17.5%, which is calculated based on our actual profits (net profit). Companies that do not make a profit do not have to pay taxes. Except for tobacco and alcohol or special cases, Hong Kong does not impose import-export taxes.

(4) Low registration capital and no need for capital verification: The amount of registered capital often directly affects shareholders' decisions to establish a company. In Hong Kong, regardless of the amount of registered capital, there is no requirement to transfer funds to a Hong Kong bank. The Hong Kong government requires a minimum registered capital of HKD 10,000, and you can increase the registered capital according to your actual situation.

(5) Gateway to international markets and stepping stone into the mainland market: Due to Hong Kong's unique historical conditions and geographical location, it creates favorable conditions for mainland enterprises to develop outwardly. Therefore, establishing a company in Hong Kong can serve as a window for external dealings, making it easier to gain the trust and cooperation of foreign enterprises. On the other hand, because China has just opened up, overseas investors prefer to set up regional headquarters in Hong Kong, using it as a stepping stone for investment in China.

(6) Easy access to international credit and loans: As is well-known, Hong Kong is the economic and financial center of Asia, with almost every street having a bank. We can use the credit from Hong Kong banks, which is the foundation for developing international business. Once we obtain credit, we can use Hong Kong as a financial center for financing, or directly issue letters of credit overseas to conduct large-scale transactions with minimal capital. If necessary, we can also apply for SME assistance loans from the Hong Kong government and reinvest this capital into the market for profit.

(7) Free movement of people, goods, and capital: Hong Kong is one of the freest and most prosperous commercial ports in the world, with excellent infrastructure. Not only is the movement of people free, but so is the movement of goods and capital. The freedom of people movement is reflected in visa-free agreements with over 100 countries and the upcoming visa-free travel plan between Hong Kong and the Pearl River Delta. The freedom of goods movement mainly manifests in the absence of tariffs on imports and exports, with extremely fast handling speed for sea, land, and air logistics. The freedom of capital movement reflects in the absence of foreign exchange controls in Hong Kong, where various currencies can be freely exchanged and transferred without restriction. Businessmen like to use the convenience of Hong Kong banks to receive and send letters of credit.

(8) Utilize Hong Kong's reputation to create a Hong Kong brand: Due to Hong Kong's significant international status, emerging enterprises often like to use Hong Kong's reputation to package themselves. For example, Hong Kong's clothing, toys, cultural publishing, and other industries have certain competitive advantages internationally. Establishing such companies in Hong Kong allows them to immediately share the overall international image and status, thereby enhancing their own competitiveness to a certain extent.

(9) Strive for Hong Kong residency rights: If your company has contributed to Hong Kong, you can apply for multiple-entry business visas from the Hong Kong Immigration Department. After residing there for seven years, you can obtain permanent residency in Hong Kong. Within this year, the Hong Kong government will launch an investment immigration program. For more information on this aspect, please refer to the updates released by the Hong Kong government.

(10) Hong Kong companies allow no business operations and permit shell companies to exist. Establishing a Hong Kong shell company can become a powerful advertising tool.

(11) Joint ventures or partnerships between Hong Kong and mainland companies for processing factories can enjoy various policy preferences in the mainland.

(12) Control accounts via online banking services, including current, savings, foreign currency, and letter of credit accounts opened in Hong Kong banks.

(13) Utilize the convenience of Hong Kong's foreign exchange transactions to directly receive and issue letters of credit, avoiding exchange rate differences.

(14) The Hong Kong government offers substantial financial support to small and medium-sized enterprises. We can use the advantageous conditions of Hong Kong and mainland companies to finance overseas, using these funds to develop businesses in the mainland or invest in Hong Kong's property and stock markets for profit.

(15) As long as your company pays taxes in Hong Kong, setting up a branch in the U.S. becomes effortless. Having a company in the U.S. provides opportunities for immigration.

(16) Use the company's name to invest in property in Hong Kong, conducting mortgage sales and securities financial investments.

(17) Apply for trademarks and brands created in the domestic market under the name of a Hong Kong company, helping you quickly establish a well-known trademark and preemptively occupy the international market.
