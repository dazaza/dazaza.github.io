What are the classifications of fungal sinusitis? Many people might hear about this disease for the first time because it is rarely mentioned in daily life, but from the name, we can see that it is a type of rhinitis. Of course, fungal sinusitis is not a very simple disease, and it also has classifications. Therefore, the following surgical treatment experts will introduce the classification of fungal sinusitis:

What are the classifications of fungal sinusitis? Experts from Gospel ENT: Fungal ball sinusitis produces clusters of spores, known as fungal balls, commonly found in the maxillary sinus. The immune system function of the patient is normal, but the affected sinus is often previously injured. This type of fungal infection does not have significant inflammatory reactions, but the sinus feels somewhat uncomfortable. Treatment involves simply cleaning the infected sinus thoroughly without the need for antifungal drugs.

Allergic fungal sinusitis is an allergic reaction to fungi dispersed in the environmental air. This situation usually occurs in patients with allergic rhinitis and normal immune function. Fungal fragments and mucus can be seen in the sinus, typically requiring surgery to clear these allergens. Since recurrences are common, anti-inflammatory drugs and immunotherapy are required.

Chronic indolent sinusitis: This invasive fungal sinusitis occurs in individuals with normal immune function and progresses slowly over months to years. Symptoms include chronic headaches, gradually swelling face, and severe cases may even result in blindness.

Fulminant sinusitis: Typically occurs in patients with compromised immune function, eroding the sinuses and possibly invading the eyes and brain.
