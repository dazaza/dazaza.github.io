What is B2B? What is B2C?

B2B is a type of e-commerce model, which stands for Business-to-Business in English. It refers to commerce between businesses or business-to-business e-commerce, where enterprises exchange products, services, and information over the internet.

In B2B, both Bs represent "Business," while the "2" is a homophone of the English word "to." Therefore, we generally pronounce B2B as "B-to-B" according to its English pronunciation rather than pronouncing "2" in Chinese. However, many people have now gotten used to reading "B2B" with Chinese pronunciation, and it doesn't cause confusion when discussing e-commerce, so no matter how it's pronounced, it's usually understandable.

B2C (Business To Customer) is one type of e-commerce classified by transaction objects, indicating e-commerce from commercial institutions to consumers. This form of e-commerce mainly focuses on online retail, leveraging the Internet to conduct online sales activities. The B2C model was the first e-commerce model to emerge in China, marked by the official operation of the 8848 online mall. In B2C, companies provide consumers with a new shopping environment - an online store - through the Internet, where consumers can shop and pay online. Since this model saves time and space for both customers and enterprises, greatly improving transaction efficiency, it is especially convenient for busy office workers, saving them valuable time. However, the characteristics of goods sold online are also very distinct, being limited to some special items such as books, audio-visual products, digital products, flowers, toys, etc. These goods require less sensory experience from the purchaser, such as sight, hearing, touch, and smell. Goods like clothing, audio equipment, and perfumes that require specific sensory experiences from consumers are not suitable for online sales. Of course, there are exceptions where some consumers decide to buy a certain brand or model without needing an in-person experience, but such consumers are few, especially in China where the product quality management system is not yet perfected, and people prefer to rely on their own sensory experiences to decide whether to purchase. So far, successful enterprises in the B2C market, such as Dangdang and Joyo, sell special items. Currently, the payment method for B2C e-commerce combines cash-on-delivery with online payments, and most enterprises choose logistics outsourcing to save operating costs. With the changing consumer habits and the promotion of exemplary enterprises, the number of online shoppers has grown rapidly, reaching 5.4% of all internet users in 2004. By the end of 2004, China's B2C e-commerce market size reached 4.5 billion yuan, and this business model has basically matured in China.

The B2B e-commerce model includes two basic models: one is direct e-commerce between enterprises (such as manufacturers' online procurement and supply), and the other is business activities conducted through third-party e-commerce websites. For example, Alibaba (china.alibaba.com), a well-known domestic e-commerce website, is a B2B e-commerce platform where various enterprises can engage in business-to-business e-commerce (B2B), such as publishing and querying supply and demand information, and conducting online exchanges and business negotiations with potential customers/suppliers.

Other abbreviations related to e-commerce include B2C (business-to-consumer), C2C (consumer-to-consumer), and G2B (government-to-business).

Traditional inter-enterprise transactions often consume a large amount of resources and time, whether it's sales and distribution or procurement, occupying product costs. Through B2B transactions, both buyers and sellers can complete the entire business process online, from building initial impressions, comparing multiple options, negotiating prices, signing contracts, and delivery, to customer service.

B2B reduces many procedural workflows and administrative expenses in inter-enterprise transactions, lowering enterprise operating costs. The convenience and extensibility of the network expand the scope of enterprise activities, making it easier and cheaper for enterprises to develop across regions and countries.
