JD.com's Over $1 Billion Financing Emerges: Liu Qiangdong May Lose Control

by a20110325 on 2011-04-01 11:14:19

Speaking of his constantly diluted personal shares, Chairman of JD.com's Board of Directors Liu Qiangdong said that real control comes from the capabilities a founder builds for the company. IC data.

The largest financing deal in the history of China's Internet is about to be unveiled.

Recently, Chairman and CEO (Chief Executive Officer) of JD.com Liu Qiangdong revealed that the total amount of JD's Series C financing will exceed $1 billion, with $800 million already received. JD is expected to go public as early as 2013, with a preference for listing in Hong Kong or the United States.

It is reported that this financing deal is expected to be announced within two days, with investors including Russia's DST and Walmart.

Massive logistics construction over the next three years

Available data shows that since August 2007, JD has gone through three rounds of financing, raising $181 million, with investors including "Red Chip Father" Liang Bochao.

Regarding the upcoming over $1 billion financing plan, Liu Qiangdong said that currently JD's top priority is to quickly scale up and gain customer recognition, so most of the financing will be used for logistics system construction and R&D.

"In the next three years, after our large-scale logistics investment concludes, I believe we will have a very good financial analysis," Liu Qiangdong said.

At the end of last year, Liu Qiangdong stated that JD's sales in 2011 could reach 35 billion yuan, but due to an incomplete logistics system, the sales target had to be set at 26 billion yuan (sales in the previous year were 10.2 billion yuan).

According to data from Analysys International, in 2010, the scale of China's B2C (Business-to-Consumer e-commerce) online retail market exceeded 100 billion yuan for the first time, growing by 373%.

Notably, earlier this year, JD's main competitor—Taobao's parent company Alibaba—took the lead in logistics investments, announcing plans to invest hundreds of billions of yuan to build a modern logistics system tailored for e-commerce. Regarding why such a significant investment was made in the logistics sector, Alibaba Chairman Jack Ma said at the time, "All complaints and grievances come from logistics." (Editor's note: HNA Group's Grand New China Investment and Han鼎Asia Pacific Fund announced on the 30th that they would jointly establish a 5 billion yuan private equity fund, Guangzhou VIPshop Information Technology Co., Ltd., which will mainly invest in logistics, services, and consumer industries. Han鼎Asia Pacific's previous primary investment area was technology.)

At that time, Jack Ma also said, "Today's B2C bubble is no less than what it was in 1999 and 2000. The vast majority of B2C websites will not exist in ten years. The results of the bubble bursting can be seen within one or two years."

Jack Ma's prediction has partially come true. According to Reuters on the 30th, after learning that JD.com's latest round of financing exceeded $1 billion, an unnamed venture capitalist told Reuters, "We originally planned to invest in 2-3 e-commerce companies this year, but now it seems that the threshold for this market has been raised too high. Those companies that have been established for too short a time and whose monthly sales are less than 100 million yuan may be directly removed from our investment list."

Liu Qiangdong may lose control

Although the $1 billion financing amount set a new record for Chinese Internet financing, analysts believe that through the third round of financing, the share of JD held by venture capitalists has reached 40%-50%. To bring in new capital, Liu Qiangdong must give up more equity, possibly turning from a boss-founder into a salaried professional manager.

Liu Qiangdong did not disclose the details of the new round of financing, but he revealed that although all current investors' combined shareholding has exceeded his personal stake, no single shareholder holds more shares than him.

Liu Qiangdong also said that from the perspective of the company founder, it is best if investors' share does not exceed that of the founder, ensuring the founder's control. However, he also believes that company control and shareholding do not necessarily correlate. "I think real control definitely comes from the capabilities the founder builds for the enterprise. If you can continue to do well, your control will be maintained well," Liu Qiangdong said.

In addition, Liu Qiangdong stated that JD plans to launch an independent luxury B2C website (Toplife) next month, focusing on luxury goods online sales.

Liu Qiangdong said that Toplife targets only "the wealthiest people in China." Initially, when the site launches, there will be products from 30-50 luxury brands, with plans to expand to 200 by the end of the year. The procurement and warehousing for this online platform will be entirely independent, while delivery and payment will leverage JD.com's existing platform.

Liu Qiangdong expects that this year, the sales of daily necessities and food and beverages will reach 3-3.5 billion yuan, and book products will reach 500 million yuan. "The growth rate of books and daily necessities far exceeds 3C. It is estimated that in three years, non-3C product revenue will account for 40%."
