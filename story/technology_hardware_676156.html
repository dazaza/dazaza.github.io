**Spyker**

Country: Netherlands

Brand Establishment Year: 1875

Brand Style: A perfect combination of beautiful art and technology, luxurious and eternal, unique and independent

Classic Model: C8 Spyder (starting at 48.8 million yuan)

Spyker, as a factory with over a hundred years of history, has always maintained its unique brand style. All the sports cars they produce are handmade, featuring a unique lightweight body, using large-displacement mid-mounted engines, rear-wheel drive, and many indicators that meet F1 standards, possessing astonishing performance parameters. In 1898, the Spijker brothers, Jacobus and Hendrik-Jan, manufactured their first car in Amsterdam using a Mercedes engine. Their outstanding body manufacturing skills immediately won them praise. The same year, to celebrate the coronation of Queen Wilhelmina of the Netherlands, the Spijkers created the famous Golden Coronation Carriage, which is still in use today. From then on, the Spijker brothers entered a turning point in their careers: dedicating Spyker entirely to automobile manufacturing. To better adapt to foreign markets, they changed "ij" in Spijker to "y," renaming the company Spyker. As a well-known luxury sports car manufacturer, the noble sports cars made by Spyker are custom-made entirely according to customer needs. In 2005, Spyker increased production to about 100 cars per year. Not only do we produce for a limited number of prestigious owners, but each model is also an exclusive collector's item. Spyker launches a new car every year, and when global production reaches 500 units, production stops immediately. Spyker targets the highest-end users in the sports car market, mainly concentrated in the United States, Western Europe, the Middle East, Greater China, and some smaller wealthy countries like Switzerland and Monaco.

**Rolls-Royce**

Country: United Kingdom

Brand Establishment Year: 1904

Brand Style: Established in 1904

Classic Model: Phantom (starting at 50 million yuan)

Mr. Rolls was a nobleman and a racing driver who loved socializing and making friends; he always wanted to produce a truly British car. Mr. Royce was an outstanding engineer, versatile, and very interested in Rolls' plan, leading to their partnership in producing famous British cars. The first Rolls-Royce car was born in Manchester. Since then, it began its highly acclaimed career. In 1906, Charles Rolls and Henry Royce co-founded Rolls-Royce. Initially, Rolls-Royce focused on aircraft engine manufacturing, with cars being a later product; they continue to produce aircraft engines today. The sculpted trademark of Rolls-Royce is a gleaming flying goddess figure. The story behind the "Flying Goddess" statue goes as follows: In 1911, through the introduction of Sir Montagu, Rolls met Charles Sykes, a painter and sculptor for The Motor magazine. He asked Charles Sykes to design a sculptural trademark for "Rolls-Royce." Sykes used Miss Eleanor Thornton from the magazine's editorial staff as a model, designing the "Flying Goddess," symbolizing the soul of speed. On February 6, 1911, the "Flying Goddess" descended upon Rolls-Royce, immersing the entire world in fresh air and the wonderful melody of wing vibrations. The inauguration ceremony for the "Flying Goddess" was as grand and enthusiastic as the launch of the first Rolls-Royce car. The Rolls-Royce logo features two overlapping "R"s, symbolizing you in me and me in you, reflecting the harmonious relationship between the two founders.

**Bugatti**

Country: France

Brand Establishment Year: 1909

Brand Style: A perfect combination of technology and art, luxurious comfort, easy to drive

Classic Models: Brescia, Royale, Atalantes, Pur Sang

Bugatti is a world-famous old sports car brand. In 1909, Italian Ettore Bugatti founded Bugatti in Germany, specializing in producing sports cars and high-end luxury sedans. Due to changes in the geographical location and nationality of the factory, it was located in Germany from 1909-1918, in France from 1919-1956, and in Italy after being re-established in 1991. The English letters in the Bugatti logo represent Bugatti, with EB being the abbreviation of Ettore Bugatti's name in English. The circle of small dots around it symbolizes ball bearings, with a red background. The Bugatti car is one of the most preserved classic old cars, and vehicles under the Bugatti brand can be seen in many famous car museums worldwide. They have superior performance, novel and streamlined body designs, and distinctive engine configurations.

**Maserati**

Country: Italy

Brand Establishment Year: 1914

Brand Style: Elegant and refined without being ostentatious, enjoying high-speed driving while maintaining comfortable driving pleasure

Classic Models: Quattroporte, GranTurismo (starting at 17.9 million yuan)

Maserati is a well-known Italian racing and sports car producer established in Bologna in 1914. The company's headquarters is now located in Modena, and their emblem is a trident. Maserati was once part of Ferrari (a company with significant shares owned by Fiat), and is now directly owned by the Italian automotive giant Fiat. The Maserati brothers—Alfieri Maserati, Bindo Maserati, Carlo Maserati, Ettore Maserati, Ernesto Maserati, and Mario Maserati—all became involved in the automotive industry in the early 20th century. Alfieri, Bindo, and Ernesto produced 2-liter Grand Prix racing cars for Diatto. In 1926, when Diatto ceased production of racing cars, it led to the creation of the first Maserati and the establishment of the Maserati model. The first Maserati driven by Alfieri won the Targa Florio in 1926. Maserati began producing racing cars with 4, 6, 8, and 16 valves (essentially two straight-eight valves mounted along another). Mario, an artist, is credited with designing the company's emblem: a trident. Alfieri Maserati passed away in 1932, but his three brothers kept the company running, producing race-winning cars.

**Aston Martin**

Country: United Kingdom

Brand Establishment Year: 1914

Brand Style: Unique styling, meticulous craftsmanship, superior performance

Classic Model: DBS (starting at 41 million yuan)

Aston Martin was originally a British luxury sedan and sports car manufacturer founded in 1913 by Lionel Martin and Robert Bamford. The company is located in Newport, known worldwide for producing convertible touring cars, racing cars, and limited-production sports cars. Although participating in races is an important means of developing passenger car production, it is costly and poorly managed, leading to its acquisition by the American Ford Company in 1987. The Aston Martin logo features a soaring eagle, with the words Aston and Martin inscribed, symbolizing the company's ambition and speed akin to a descending eagle. The words Aston and Martin indicate a "three-way alliance" car company. The renowned Aston Martin Lagonda Company, famous for producing convertible touring cars, racing cars, and limited-production sports cars, owes much of its fame to this eagle-bringing good fortune. Mentioning Aston Martin brings to mind the legendary stories of 007 James Bond, seemingly attributing Aston Martin's success to him.

**Bentley**

Country: United Kingdom

Brand Establishment Year: 1919

Brand Style: Excellent driving experience, combining traditional handcrafted techniques and powerful sports performance

Classic Models: Arnage 728 (starting at 99.8 million yuan), Arnage RL (starting at 54.8 million yuan), Continental GT (starting at 30.8 million yuan), Continental Flying (starting at 29.8 million yuan)

The Bentley company was founded by two brothers. Bentley (BENTLEY) was previously translated as Bentli, but it officially adopted "Bentley" when it first entered China in 2001. The founder of Bentley is Walter Owen Bentley, who apprenticed in railways before transitioning to the automotive industry. In 1912, he and his brother Horace Milner founded the Bentley Motors company in London, selling DFP cars from France. He improved the new car by replacing the engine with aluminum pistons, enhancing its performance. During World War I, he worked for the Royal Naval Air Service Technical Committee, improving the French Clerget engine, with the improved aviation engines named BR1 and BR2. In 1919, W.O. Bentley resumed sales of the DFP car and launched the Bentley 3.0, powered by a 3-liter engine with an output of 85 horsepower, capable of speeds above 80 mph. This racing car achieved remarkable success in various competitions, winning five consecutive championships at the Le Mans circuit in France from 1924 to 1930. Bentley embarked on a journey of professional design of high-end sports cars and racing cars. In 1935, Bentley left to join Lagonda, where he worked for 11 years. W.O. Bentley passed away in 1971. The Bentley car logo features the first letter "B" of the company name, sprouting a pair of wings, resembling a soaring eagle, a symbol that has been used to this day. Previously, a flying "B" logo was also used.

**Maybach**

Country: Germany

Brand Establishment Year: 1921

Brand Style: Noble elegance, extreme personality, perfectionism

Classic Models: Maybach 57S (7.31 million yuan), 62S (8.3 million yuan)

Daimler-Chrysler Group announced in New York that the German ultra-luxury car brand Maybach, which had been dormant for 60 years, has officially been revived. Two models were initially launched: Maybach 57 and Maybach 62, produced by Mercedes-Benz, priced at 310,000 euros and 360,000 euros respectively. These prices are quite astonishing, equivalent to approximately 3.5 times the price of a Mercedes S600 or 2.5 times that of a Ferrari 360. Even the renowned Rolls-Royce and Bentley can only match the top-tier products of Maybach 62. Some define the resurrected Maybach as "Germany's Rolls-Royce." While this statement makes sense, it is not entirely accurate. Because Rolls-Royce, a proud symbol of British automaking, will soon belong to the German BMW group and become "authentic" German goods. Another ultra-luxury brand, Bentley, has also joined the German Volkswagen group. Currently, the BMW group is busy updating Rolls-Royce and may introduce a new model in the coming years. In this context, Daimler's decision to launch Maybach cars at this time appears to be a strategic consideration, positioning Maybach as a sharp weapon in the ultra-luxury car market, not allowing BMW to monopolize this market alone. Compared to Rolls-Royce's "angel" logo, Maybach's double M logo is much simpler. Initially, the double M represented "Maybach cars," and now it signifies "Made by Maybach." The Maybach logo consists of two intersecting Ms surrounded by a spherical triangle, reminding people of its legendary cars and era through branding: alongside Mercedes models, they became absolute classics among German automakers. Exquisite technology, supreme quality, and unique style make them the focus of attention worldwide. Each car is independently crafted according to user specifications, with engines and chassis all handmade.

**Ferrari**

Country: Italy

Brand Establishment Year: 1947

Brand Style: Speed and passion, innovation and courage

Classic Models: F430 (2.7 million yuan), F430 Spider (3.2 million yuan), 599 GTB (4.35 million yuan), 612 Scaglietti (4.38 million yuan)

Ferrari is an Italian car manufacturer primarily producing Formula One race cars, racing cars, and high-performance sports cars, founded in 1929 by Enzo Ferrari. Early Ferrari sponsored racers and produced race cars, becoming an independent car manufacturer in 1946. It has since grown to its current scale and is now owned by the Fiat Group, headquartered in Maranello near Modena, Italy. Italian cars are known as "two kings and one queen": the two kings are "Ferrari" and "Lamborghini," and the queen is "Maserati." The founder of Ferrari, Enzo Ferrari, said that his favorite racing car is one he hasn't built yet, and his greatest success is one he hasn't achieved yet. This Italian man, whose blood boiled with a passion for cars from a young age, convinced his father at the age of 13 to begin driving alone. The roar of engines on the racetrack, the excitement and thrill of competition, made him increasingly courageous. At the time, he drove Alfa Romeo cars, achieving repeated victories, earning the nickname "Knight" from his teammates. He transitioned from participating in races to founding a racing club, eventually creating his own car company. Ferrari is now known as the "Red Lightning." Ferrari's shield emblem features a prancing black horse, with the background color being the golden canary feather color of Modena. The prancing horse originates from Ferrari's founder Enzo Ferrari's birthplace, Modena, and carries heroic legend. In World War I, the highly honored Italian pilot Francesco Baracca first adopted this emblem as his personal insignia, painting it on his aircraft. After the war, Baracca's parents entrusted this prancing horse emblem to Enzo Ferrari, who placed it on a yellow shield background as a symbol of the team. Later, after the brave pilot's death, the horse turned black, with the Italian flag added above to honor his memory.

**Porsche**

Country: Germany

Brand Establishment Year: 1948

Brand Style: Ultimate sports car for daily use

Classic Model: Porsche 911 Turbo (1.95 million yuan)

The founder of Porsche, Ferdinand Porsche, invented the electric wheel hub motor at the age of 24 (in 1899). The following year, at the Paris International Exhibition, Porsche was already renowned worldwide. Porsche cars have distinct characteristics, beetle-shaped bodies, rear-mounted engines, and excellent performance, quickly making them famous. In 1963, at the Frankfurt International Auto Show, the Porsche 911 was displayed, a design that remains popular even today. Its body designer was Ferry Porsche's eldest son, Ferdinand Alexander Porsche. The Porsche 928 with a rear gearbox chassis and V-8 engine, the new technology 955, and the 911 Carrera 4 with electronically adjustable four-wheel drive system and lift-free body are the most important developments in recent years. Porsche (PORSCHE) was formerly translated as "Porsche." Its logo uses Stuttgart's shield-shaped city emblem. The middle of the Porsche logo is a galloping horse, representing a famous breed of horses produced in Stuttgart, with "Stuttgart" written above it to indicate the company's headquarters in Stuttgart. The deer antlers in the upper left and lower right of the logo represent Stuttgart's former status as a hunting ground. The yellow stripes in the upper right and lower left represent ripe wheat, symbolizing a bountiful harvest, while black represents fertile land and red symbolizes human wisdom and love for nature. Together, these elements form a beautiful pastoral landscape, symbolizing Porsche's glorious past and bright future.

**Lamborghini**

Country: Italy

Brand Establishment Year: 1963

Brand Style: Wildness and passion

Classic Model: Lamborghini Murcielago (48.8 million yuan)

Lamborghini, also known as "Lamborghini" or "Linbaojiani," is the full name of the company Ferruccio Lamborghini Automobili S.P.A., Italian Ferruccio Lamborghini Automobile Joint Stock Company. The Lamborghini logo is a charging bull, matching the characteristics of high-horsepower, high-speed sports cars produced by Lamborghini. It is said that this emblem also reflects the founder Ferruccio Lamborghini's bull-like tenacity. Ferruccio Lamborghini, the founder of Lamborghini S.P.A., and early Lamborghini sports cars and tractors. Ferruccio Lamborghini embodies the special passion and ambition characteristic of Italians, inspiring him to rise from a farmer's son to become an admired industry leader through hard work. This northern Italian, driven by an uncompromising spirit and almost crazy enthusiasm, tirelessly pursued the dream of manufacturing perfect sports cars. Since then, the stubborn "bull" emblem has become a symbol of Lamborghini, interpreting all the characteristics of this unique car brand - challenging limits, proud and extraordinary, bold and unrestrained.
