I. The Zhengde era of the Ming Dynasty was the reign title of Zhu Houzhao, the eleventh emperor of the Ming Dynasty. In the Gregorian calendar, it was the sixteenth century, just five hundred years ago. In the fourth year of Zhu Houzhao's reign, which was 1509 AD, he encountered a thorny issue.

What was this matter?

A minister submitted a secret report, bypassing the then red-hot eunuch Liu Jin who was close to the emperor, quietly handing it over to Zhu Houzhao. This so-called "secret report" accused censor Wang Xianchen of privately accepting beauties and gold from border officials.

This was a typical case of corruption. Holding the report in his hand, Zhu Houzhao pondered... He wondered how this secret report could bypass Liu Jin, who was effectively managing the court for him, and reach him so quickly. For reasons unknown, instead of tossing the report to the eunuchs as he usually did, he weighed it again and set it aside.

The news eventually reached Liu Jin. When the emperor was absent, Liu Jin went to examine the report on the table, trying to figure something out. He wanted to know where the beauties had gone and more importantly, Wang Xianchen, who didn't belong to any faction, neither Li Dongyang's nor Yang Tinghe's disciple. Did he accept the beauties for himself or to gift others? If gifted, who would be the recipient? That person must be his patron! At this moment, Liu Jin regretted not having properly evaluated Wang Xianchen before.

What troubled Liu Jin was, who impeached someone like Wang Xianchen? Why bypass him, Liu Jin? Was it because someone couldn't gauge Wang Xianchen's depth, fearing they might anger Liu Jin!

Liu Jin cast a wide net meticulously.

Zhu Houzhao pretended nothing happened; he believed there would be some drama to watch. He liked watching "drama"!

In Zhu Houzhao's mind, Wang Xianchen was a good official who had done many good deeds while inspecting various places. Officials from all regions praised Wang Xianchen in their reports. Moving such a high-ranking official in the supervision department would affect the entire court significantly, requiring caution. Everyone understood this. He didn't want to disrupt the team arranged by the previous dynasty too much, affecting his grand plans.

For Zhu Houzhao to think this far was already quite an effort on his part, worthy of Wang Xianchen.

Next, Zhu Houzhao summoned the eunuchs and sent them to find Wang Xianchen.

Liu Jin couldn't uncover much about Wang Xianchen's background.

This, Zhu Houzhao also noticed. Wanting to see some "drama," Zhu Houzhao felt somewhat disappointed and immediately decided to hand the matter over to Liu Jin.

Liu Jin, who claimed to understand the emperor's intentions, failed this time.

Liu Jin misinterpreted the emperor's expression as "this person is handed over to you for handling." What followed, he intended to handle according to his will. This time, Liu Jin didn't send a minor eunuch but went personally, conveying a certain meaning to Wang Xianchen. Wang Xianchen instantly understood: if you join my ranks now, everything will be forgiven!

Wang Xianchen directly told Liu Jin that his end was near. According to the rules established by the previous emperor, Liu Jin had no authority to inquire, and he wished to meet the emperor.

Liu Jin made another mistake here. He didn't act as usual. Normally, he wouldn't let the other party speak a word, first ordering the Eastern Depot to torture them before any dialogue. The reason he didn't do so this time was that the beauties Wang Xianchen brought back were already in the palace, and Wang Xianchen hadn't reported this to the emperor yet, making him hesitant to act rashly!

Liu Jin swiftly reported Wang Xianchen's words to Zhu Houzhao.

Zhu Houzhao said, "Good! Let's summon him now." Liu Jin was stunned, thinking his ears were deceiving him. Only when Zhu Houzhao raised his voice again, "Go and invite him quickly! Minister Wang from the Ministry of Personnel must be invited!" did he realize what was happening.

Liu Jin stood there for several minutes, finally understanding that the emperor had only been "playing with him," fortunately, he hadn't fallen for it! At this point, Liu Jin realized that Wang Xianchen must be eliminated immediately!

Wang Xianchen wasn't weak either. In front of Zhu Houzhao, he took out a memorial detailing many of Liu Jin's crimes. It also involved some officials in the court who had bought their positions through Liu Jin.

Standing there, Liu Jin was drenched in sweat, his legs trembling.

Zhu Houzhao glanced at Liu Jin and laughed, but quickly composed himself. He knew that the memorial presented by this censor was impeccable. With Liu Jin's level of skill, he couldn't handle this, and what kind of censor would he be otherwise?

Finally, Zhu Houzhao said to Wang Xianchen, "You may retire and return home."

Later generations believed this was Zhu Houzhao's greatest protection for Wang Xianchen.

Then, Zhu Houzhao instructed Liu Jin to have those who impeached Wang Xianchen leave the capital and serve elsewhere.

In today's terms, Zhu Houzhao was playing a game of punishing both sides equally!

This was an outcome Wang Xianchen hadn't anticipated. He sighed deeply, realizing today's result stemmed from his own lack of prudence. Who leaked the information about receiving gifts and bribery? Never mind.

Returning home meant returning home; this officialdom wasn't a pleasant place after all.

II. Thus, Wang Xianchen, carrying the substantial wealth accumulated over decades of service, rented a boat and quietly left the capital, traveling along the Grand Canal. Along the way, he dared not linger, let alone sightsee, fearing the emperor might suddenly change his mind and punish him again. After all, Zhu Di wasn't the only one known for being fickle. More importantly, he feared Liu Jin might send someone to ambush him somewhere and eliminate him! Such incidents were plentiful in history! Liu Jin was an expert at such deeds!

A retired official was like a drowning dog, survival being the primary concern.

After a low-profile rush, he finally returned to Suzhou, which he had been away from for decades.

Upon landing, Wang Xianchen faced north and sighed deeply. All past matters were truly over. Then, brushing his sleeve, he laughed loudly to his family, saying, "Let's stay at the inn first. We'll find a suitable house later." According to the rules at the time, retired officials returning home without ancestral homes could temporarily stay at the "inn." If there was no room at the inn, the local government would rent a house for them!

Suzhou at the time was the largest city south of the Yangtze River. Since Emperor Taizong of the Tang Dynasty established the "Jiangnan East Road" in Suzhou, this city named during the Sui Wendi era had gradually evolved into today's provincial-level administrative unit through construction across dynasties.

During the Ming Dynasty, Suzhou's jurisdiction expanded further, encompassing areas south of the Yangtze River under Nan Zhili and extending to Anqing Prefecture north of the river. The "Yingtian Inspectorate Office" was established, meaning that the area south of the Yangtze River, including today's Anqing City in Anhui Province and even Fujian Province, was then under Suzhou's jurisdiction.

After settling in, Wang Xianchen received formal greetings from the inn's officials, which naturally included a banquet. Conveying some "local news" to this official who had been away from home for many years at the banquet was entirely necessary.

Adapting to local customs, Wang Xianchen listened attentively. He was clear that among the servants attending him were families of old friends and colleagues who had once favored him. He decided to discreetly greet or assist them, avoiding meeting those he merely recognized. Especially, encountering the families of former colleagues with whom he often crossed paths in the past, it truly made him feel sorrow for their shared misfortune. At such moments, he also felt fortunate, truly needing to thank Zhu Houzhao. If Zhu Houzhao were like Zhu Di, it would have been disastrous. His family might not even qualify as servants; he would be flayed alive, his wife and young concubines sent to the camp to be raped to death...

Not only should Wang Xianchen be grateful to Zhu Houzhao, but we today should also thank Zhu Houzhao. Without his kindness back then, the Humble Administrator's Garden in Suzhou might never have existed. Based on the official position Wang Xianchen held at the time, it was impossible for him to acquire such an estate, leaving behind a difficult question: how much bribe money did Wang Xianchen receive to purchase this property?

The Humble Administrator's Garden differs from any private garden in the world. With its style and personality, it inevitably became a representative of unique Jiangnan private gardens in China! Simultaneously, it is also a product of personal experience changes and reflects the thoughts of a generation of officials.

III. After settling his family, Wang Xianchen immediately visited old friends. Having been away from home for many years, returning now was not for visiting relatives but for settling down permanently. His purpose was naturally to pave the way for "settling down." Wang Xianchen wanted to visit old friends and especially the descendants of benefactors and teachers who had helped him in the past. However, this return home was neither glorious nor honorable, let alone a triumphant return. He had to tread carefully; excessive ostentation, if it reached the capital, would backfire, inviting fatal consequences, which wouldn't be worth it. As a veteran censor, Wang Xianchen had learned to keep a low profile. Accompanied by his book boy, dressed casually, he set off. Following the familiar streets and alleys of his youth, he visited a few surviving friends and learned about some vacant properties due to earthquakes or family declines.

Two years prior, in September, an earthquake struck, causing many deaths. The earthquake occurred around three o'clock in the morning. Due to abnormal weather after autumn, high temperatures persisted, followed by a heavy rain that lasted from the morning of the previous day until midnight. Temperatures dropped sharply. Exhausted people finally got a chance to sleep well. However, the earthquake happened then, catching everyone unprepared, leaving countless families homeless. Many prestigious mansions were severely damaged due to this earthquake.

"Is it the vacant land between Lou Gate and Qi Gate?" asked the old Suzhou expert at the inn.

When confirmed, the Suzhou expert and accountant repeatedly said, "Good place, good place, surrounded by water, connected by rivers."

Wang Xianchen also thought it was a good place. The main reason he chose it was its location right between Lou Gate and Qi Gate within Suzhou. Although currently desolate, this geographical location was closest to the city center, making it an excellent residential site. It had been coveted throughout history, but...

The rest couldn't be said.

The Suzhou expert and accountant changed the topic, hinting, "This is a place steeped in ancient charm, perfect for producing great works. It can allow you to write a monumental masterpiece for eternity." He reminded, "Surely you know that outside Lou Gate was originally the county seat of Lou County, right?"

"You mean when Emperor Qin Shi Huang implemented the commandery-county system? Oh! Yes, I remember."

The Suzhou expert and accountant hinted again, "Do you know that the garden you intend to acquire once housed the great poet Bai Juyi?"

Wang Xianchen became excited, asking, "Would he live there?"

The Suzhou expert and accountant said, "How wouldn't he? There's a cloud wall dividing the central garden and west garden, right? Though broken, the old traces remain! And there's a fake mountain with a hexagonal pavilion atop it. That's the 'Yi Liang Pavilion.' You mustn't forget this scene and should improve it later. Back then, Bai Juyi and Yuan Zongjian lived as neighbors, with tall willow trees protruding from the walls, allowing both households to enjoy them together. Bai Juyi wrote poetry praising this: 'The moon shines well on the three-path night, green willows suit the two-family spring.'"

Wang Xianchen clapped his hands, exclaiming, "Marvelous! Marvelous!"

With the guidance of the Suzhou expert and accountant, Wang Xianchen increasingly felt this was a feng shui treasure: the governor's residence of Wu Commandery during the Three Kingdoms period was here, and the famous musician Dai Yong of the Southern Song Dynasty built his residence here. Later, to avoid serving as an official, he moved with his wife and daughter to the southern suburbs of Runzhou (today's Zhenjiang), specifically Beast Hollow Mountain, building a thatched hut to listen to birds and compose music, leading to the historical anecdote "Dai Yong enjoys two cups of wine and listens to orioles."

Among the impressions Wang Xianchen had, the most profound was Hu Jizyan, who served as the magistrate of Shanyin during the Northern Song Dynasty. Seeing five waist-thick willows here and hearing they were planted by Lu Guimeng, he built the "Five Willow Hall" beside the willows. By the time it passed to Hu Jizyan's son, the residence was renamed "Ru Village," perhaps because he saw the place as Du Fu described, "Residences resemble desolate villages." During the Dade period of the Yuan Dynasty, Buddhism was officially reinstated in China. Historically, during the Southern Song and Liang Dynasties, as well as the Tang and Song Dynasties, emperors personally offered themselves to Buddhism to promote its prevalence. The "Da Hong Temple" was built on the adjacent empty land of "Ru Village," creating the "East Study." At the end of the Yuan Dynasty, Zhang Shicheng rebelled, marching from Gaoyou to Jiangnan, occupying Suzhou. Zhang Shicheng used the East Study as a temporary residence and later gave it to his son-in-law Pan Yuanshao after establishing his capital in Suzhou. After Zhang Shicheng's failure, the Da Hong Temple disappeared into history alongside Zhang Shicheng.

A good place is indeed a good place. This account keeper, a true Shaoxing scholar, never fully plotted for others. He considered this a "place of lustful revelry, a thoroughfare for scattering gold." Such a fine place should only serve as a public "happy forest," never as a private residence. This statement almost slipped out several times, but seeing Wang Xianchen's face, he swallowed it back.

According to his thought, Wang Xianchen, knowledgeable in ancient laws and classics, would certainly deliberate thoroughly when using this land.

What he never expected was that Wang Xianchen didn't bother to review the literature.

IV. After returning to his hometown Suzhou, Wang Xianchen began interacting with Wen Zhengming.

This was precisely the most destitute period of Wen Zhengming's life at thirty-nine years old. One had seen through the darkness of the officialdom with unfulfilled ambitions, the other desired to spread his wings but lacked the strength to rise. The two "underappreciated talents" grew closer, naturally having much to discuss. Wang Xianchen wanted to express his dissatisfaction in politics through garden construction, perfectly aligning with Wen Zhengming's similar feelings of frustration in his political career!

Naturally, politically inclined Wang Xianchen understood the value of Wen Zhengming's "perseverance in adversity." His involvement in the garden construction would ensure the garden was built well, creating value, making people aware of Wang Xianchen's unutilized governance skills and administrative abilities! How ironic it was that these talents were not used by the emperor but channeled into building a garden! How exhausting Wang Xianchen's efforts were! Flipping through the annals of history, which dynasty didn't suppress talent? Which emperor didn't oppress capable individuals? Even in the so-called peaceful world, weren't there countless wrongful souls and aggrieved spirits? Why stir up trouble and show off your abilities, Wang Xianchen? The emperor letting you build the Humble Administrator's Garden was truly giving you considerable face! Imagine doing it under Emperor Qin Shi Huang! If you survived until the "Anti-Rightist Movement," try tasting that bitterness! If you still crave more, the Cultural Revolution awaits, ensuring your bones are shattered if you survive!
