        Behind-the-scenes

     In terms of plot, Disney changed the originally tragic ending into a happy one and added many vivid characters. Princess Ariel was shaped into Disney's first modern personality heroine; the staff even joked that if Walt Disney were alive, he would never allow a runaway girl like Ariel.

     As Disney's last hand-drawn animated film, this movie used thousands of different colors for eleven hundred background images, and more than a million pictures were drawn in total.         Behind-the-scenes

     In 1950, Disney released its first long animated feature after World War II - "Cinderella." To shape Cinderella's elegant image, Disney specially hired an 18-year-old model, reproducing her every smile to make Cinderella's grace particularly lifelike. Especially when seeing Cinderella take off her tattered clothes and put on a magnificent dress, even Walt Disney himself couldn't help but shed tears of excitement.

         After the film's release, it was warmly received, playing a crucial role in Disney's animation history. Thereafter, Disney animations entered their colorful and glorious golden age.
