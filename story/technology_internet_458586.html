What are the precautions for buttock liposuction?

1. Buttock liposuction surgery requires an understanding of the anatomical characteristics of the buttocks, which have an extremely thick layer of fat that can be divided into a superficial layer (honeycomb layer) and a deep layer (plate-like layer). Therefore, during liposuction, both the superficial and deep layers of fat must be aspirated, i.e., full-layer aspiration, to achieve better shaping results.

2. Buttock liposuction surgery should formulate corresponding plans according to the shape of the deformity and carry out planned step-by-step aspiration. The operator must be familiar with the anatomy of the buttocks, understand the mechanism of the formation of the gluteal groove, and ensure symmetry on both sides after the surgery. The amount of fat aspirated from equivalent positions on both sides should be equal.

3. During buttock liposuction surgery, attention should be paid to the direction of the liposuction needle. Generally, important structures are not easily damaged during buttock liposuction. A certain thickness of subcutaneous superficial fat should be retained to maintain a smooth surface. The aspiration site should be kept away from the puncture points of subcutaneous blood vessels. When aspirating, attention should be paid to the color and quality of the aspirate. Postoperative measures such as fluid replacement and compression bandaging should also be noted.
