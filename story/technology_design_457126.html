Feel the eternity

by uucbofdh on 2011-07-04 16:18:06

In the pocket in front of the chest was a towering Red Pagoda Mountain, which symbolized identity, position, and property at that time, evoking much admiration. This became an indelible memory for many people in the 80s and 90s. Memory is a special kind of emotion. Lighting a Red Pagoda Mountain cigarette seems to bring back half a century of storms, half a century of prosperity and decline, as if it all returns before our eyes once again.

Red Pagoda Mountain represents an era, from the distant and profound past, we experience success and feel eternity.

The brand story of Red Pagoda Mountain originates from 1959. That year marked the 10th anniversary of the founding of the People's Republic of China, and to express respect for Chairman Mao Zedong, workers at a fledgling cigarette company worked overtime to specially prepare a box of cigarettes just before National Day. On the box were the five big words "For Chairman Mao", which was sent to Zhongnanhai in Beijing.

This pack of cigarettes had a white background with red patterns all over it: a red pagoda, red mountains, and red clouds... reflecting the "red tide" of that era. Moreover, Chinese people traditionally associate the color red with celebration.

The company producing Red Pagoda Mountain cigarettes was Yuxi Cigarette Factory (the predecessor of Hongta Group) from Yunnan Province. At that time, Yuxi Cigarette Factory was not well-known, but Yuxi itself had the famous title of "Hometown of Yunyan." There is a famous anecdote: In 1953, the national tobacco authority held the first national tobacco conference in Zhengzhou. Tobacco experts from China, the former Soviet Union, Germany, France, and Japan evaluated tobacco samples from all over the country. The representative from Yuxi arrived late due to inconvenient transportation, missing the official deadline. However, when the experts saw the high-quality tobacco leaves brought by the Yuxi representative—bright golden yellow, oil-rich, full-bodied, and aromatic—they not only allowed the Yuxi tobacco to be evaluated but also gave it a score higher than the perfect 100 points: 108 points.

But what truly made Yuxi famous nationwide was "Red Pagoda Mountain."

In 1959, the state light industry department carried out structural adjustments. Thirty "Yongjin" and "New China" cigarette machines were transferred from a Shanghai private cigarette factory named "Magnificent" to the Yuxi Tobacco Leaf Re-drying Factory, established in 1956. Along with the machines came 36 Shanghai masters. To quickly produce cigarettes, many processes were done manually. After producing cigarettes like "People's Commune," the then enterprise leadership decided to create a cigarette brand named after a Yuan Dynasty building—a red-painted ancient tower from the "Great Leap Forward" era—thus naming it "Red Pagoda Mountain."

However, under the circumstances of that time, Red Pagoda Mountain was discontinued after a few years because it was considered an imitative brand. It wasn't until 1974 that "Red Pagoda Mountain" began production again. By the late 1970s, the number of cigarette machines at the Yuxi Cigarette Factory increased from 30 to 60, with an annual output reaching nearly 300,000 cases at its peak, and they started producing filter-tip cigarettes in the early 1980s.

From the early 1980s to the mid-1990s, the decision-makers of the Yuxi Cigarette Factory boldly introduced the most advanced equipment and technology internationally. This led the company to make significant strides in production scale, technical and process levels, and production environment. Through several large-scale technical renovations, the Yuxi Cigarette Factory became a giant in the tobacco industry in the East. Meanwhile, the factory increased its investment in the "first workshop"—the tobacco fields. These substantial investments not only benefited workers and farmers but, more importantly, ensured that the Yuxi Cigarette Factory obtained top-quality tobacco leaves. The quality and taste of Red Pagoda Mountain products gradually improved, leading to a dominant position in the market.

The Yuxi people prioritized quality, and the merchants from coastal regions like Guangdong were the first to discover the value of Red Pagoda Mountain. In the early 1980s, there were frequent instances of smuggled cigarettes along the coast of Guangdong, making "foreign cigarettes" highly sought after. Upon arriving in Yunnan, these shrewd businessmen were pleasantly surprised to find that Red Pagoda Mountain could match foreign cigarettes in terms of flavor, quality, and appearance. These astute businessmen quickly started doing business with Red Pagoda Mountain cigarettes...
