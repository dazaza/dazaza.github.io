Synopsis:

Do you remember news anchor Evan Baxter from the previous story? The one who was made a laughing stock on live television thanks to Bruce's "divine powers"? Well, he has now left the world of journalism and ventured into politics, successfully becoming a congressman. To celebrate his new identity, Evan moves with his wife Joan and their three sons Dylan, Jordan, and Ryan from Buffalo to Huntsville, a small town in Virginia, ready to embrace a brand-new life.

Evan especially chooses to settle his new home in a large villa surrounded by mountains and begins preparing for his first working day in Congress... This ambitious man, who already considers himself a politician at heart, prays to the Almighty God, wishing to change the world. At this point, Evan does not yet know that God has already planned a series of significant responsibilities for his future life, which are both crucial and somewhat absurd.

It must be that his prayer received a response from God because Evan is delighted to find himself favored by Ron, the most powerful congressman, invited to participate in a very important bill. With the support of a strong team behind him, including his assistant Rita, advisor Marty, and intern doctor Eugene, Evan feels no panic about being given such an important role so soon after joining. Even though Evan realizes that the increasingly heavy workload may further distance him from his family, whom he has neglected for too long, he always believes there will be time later.

For the current Evan, power seems more important than family. However, since God "manifests" before him, his self-centered life is completely overturned. Strange events begin when packages keep arriving at Evan’s doorstep, all containing ancient tools. Just as Evan starts to wonder if he might be going crazy, a tall stranger suddenly visits. He claims to be God and gives Evan a simple yet ridiculous command: use those tools to build an ark to prepare for the impending fierce flood.

With the help of his three children and a copy of "The Boat Building Guide for Dummies," Evan finally completes a rather modest wooden boat. But this boat is so small it doesn't even have a storage room, let alone enough space for the animals that come rushing in upon hearing the call. It turns out they are responding to God's summons, each species selecting one male and one female to follow Evan onto the ark... Having no other choice, Evan has to start over.

It’s not like Evan hasn’t had any doubts during this process. Whenever he has the slightest doubt, his beard and hair grow uncontrollably until his whole face is covered with hair, making it hard even for his wife to recognize him. Evan silently builds the ark in preparation for the impending flood, risking losing everything he once had. The onlookers cannot understand his actions and begin to doubt his words: Is this just a midlife crisis-induced delusion of a man, or is the disaster only recorded in the Bible really going to strike the Earth?
