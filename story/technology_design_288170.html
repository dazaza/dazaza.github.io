Several years have passed in the blink of an eye.

The passage seems to be a personal narrative about someone's experiences and reflections on their life journey. Below is an English translation of the Chinese text:

---

The reality of time's speed. In the blink of an eye, years have passed without achievement. Recalling the clear moments of the past, everything seems so vivid. I remember that summer when people chose to leave school. At that time, it felt so tense. Since I always yearned for freedom, I wanted to create such a life for myself. Later, I had great illusions about society. With this mindset, I, a naive young person, embarked on my own path. My first stop was Baotou. Baotou gave me the impression of being big, good, and prosperous. Through a friend’s introduction, I came to this grand hotel — Julong Gourmet Entertainment Plaza. This hotel was very famous in Baotou. As a country boy entering this environment, everything seemed unfamiliar and curious. I was assigned to the food delivery department. The requirements for being a food delivery person were high. Speed had to be fast, and during idle times, we would practice standing at military posture. I clearly remember that within less than half a month, all my toes were blistered. Every night when I went to sleep, my feet were sore. Unfortunately, I didn’t get along well with the team leader and got fired. For a moment, I was stunned, not knowing what to do. After only three months away from home, I felt too ashamed to return. Mentally restless, I didn't know where to go next. So, I appealed to the manager, who then arranged for me to work at another hotel — Taiyangcheng. This hotel wasn’t as good as the previous one, but it was more comfortable. I got along well with everyone there. In Baotou, there were many unforgettable events. After leaving Baotou (the following autumn), I decided never to work in hotels again. My next destination was Nanjing. There, I worked in landscaping in Chaoyang District, a place where 70% of the residents were Korean, 20% were other foreigners, and 10% were locals. The job involved watering lawns and other miscellaneous tasks. It was exhausting, and after three months, I returned home to help with household chores. Staying at home for half a year, I often daydreamed and felt unaccomplished. Therefore, I decided to set out again after the New Year. No matter what, I had to persevere and achieve something meaningful.

In the blink of an eye, the year had ended. Crying wouldn't change anything; I had to leave again. Seeing my parents' eyes filled with both concern and hope made my heart ache. I silently promised myself that I must make them proud. This time, I went to Tianjin, Haihe County, Xiaochun Village. There were many factories there. I entered a factory management position. We only had half an hour for lunch and dinner each day, working until late at night. Then we rested for one day. It was tiring, but I didn’t give up. After six months, my brother visited me and criticized my current job, saying it wasn't worth doing. So, I quit. I went to Tianjin City, to my brother's friend, Uncle Tao. There, I met Li Tan and Dan, who became good friends. During this period, I spent most days looking at recruitment ads, making phone calls, and visiting places in person. I remember one day when I walked tens of miles but couldn't find a job. From morning until afternoon, I wandered aimlessly, feeling embarrassed to return home and even thought about sleeping on the street. Eventually, I summoned the courage to return home, though I didn’t know how to face my parents. My brother found out and scolded me, but I couldn’t express my grievances. Finally, time didn’t fail me. I found a job — at the Yuguang Billiards Club. There, I worked part-time for over a year. The manager treated me well, and I am grateful to him. It was at Yuguang where I met Daxue. When I encountered him, I felt an instant connection. From him, I learned many things. We often discussed life and found much common ground. He was steady, broad-minded, and slightly introverted. He helped me a lot, and I am deeply thankful.

After leaving the club, I became lost again. I went back to Uncle Tao. He asked me what I planned to do next. I said I hadn’t figured it out yet. He suggested I learn hairdressing. I agreed because I had already considered it due to its promising prospects. Thus, Uncle Tao helped me find a barbershop called Beautiful Workshop. This shop had many branches in Nankai District and was reputable. I worked at the Longjing branch, where there were more than ten skilled barbers and around ten assistants. Initially, my duties included washing hair and sweeping the floor. I thought hairdressing would be easy, but upon starting, I realized it was challenging. After a week, the store manager instructed me to learn how to curl hair. At first, I struggled. I was anxious and felt clumsy. Eventually, through practice, I improved. The shop provided training for assistants, which enhanced my understanding of the hairdressing industry. After less than two months, I decided to leave. The reason was that one day, while observing an assistant cutting hair, I noticed their technique was clumsy and stiff. Suddenly, I had the idea to quit. Although that assistant had been learning for over a year and was skilled at curling hair, their progress was too slow. To become a barber faster, I began seeking advice from other assistants. Gradually, I mastered basic techniques. At that point, I firmly believed that I would become as skilled as the assistants or even surpass them. Thus, I left Beautiful Workshop. Few people understood my actions, but I was determined. I just wanted to become a barber faster. My steps confirmed that my choice was correct. Here, I thank Uncle Tao for changing my confused state and guiding me into this profession. I will continue on this path. First and foremost, I thank those who care about me. I believe I will strive to meet your expectations.
