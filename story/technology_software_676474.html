At 3:45 AM Beijing time on February 26 (8:45 PM local time in Italy on February 25), the 25th round of Serie A featured a top-of-the-table clash at the San Siro Stadium, as AC Milan hosted Juventus on their home ground.

Before the match, AC Milan led Juventus by one point but had played one more game. Kevin-Prince Boateng and Maxi López were absent due to injury, while Zlatan Ibrahimović's appeal was rejected, meaning he would serve his final match of suspension. Alexandre Pato returned to partner Robinho upfront. Central defender Philippe Mexès recovered to start, and Antonio Nocerino was preferred over Mesbah at left-back. Mark van Bommel replaced Ambrosini, and Sulley Muntari continued in the starting lineup after scoring in his debut last round.

Antonio Conte kept his back three unchanged, with Quagliarella and Borrello leading the attack. In midfield, there were three changes: Lichtsteiner returned to the starting lineup replacing Padoin, Vidal came back from suspension, and Estigarribia started instead of De Ceglie.

A crowd of 79,208 fans filled the San Siro, generating ticket revenue of 3.118 million euros.

AC Milan (4-3-1-2): 32-Abbiati / 20-Abate, 5-Mexes, 33-Thiago Silva, 77-Antonini / 22-Nocerino, 4-Van Bommel, 14-Montari / 28-Emmanuelson / 70-Robinho, 7-Pato.

Substitutes: 1-Amelia, 19-Zambrotta, 9-Inzaghi, 23-Ambrosini, 25-Bonera, 92-Sharaqi, 76-Yepez.

Juventus (3-5-2): 1-Buffon / 15-Barzagli, 19-Bonucci, 3-Chiellini / 26-Lichtsteiner, 22-Vidal, 21-Pirlo, 8-Marchisio, 28-Estigarribia / 18-Quagliarella, 23-Borrello.

Substitutes: 30-Storari, 14-Vucinic, 10-Piero, 7-Pepe, 4-Caceres, 32-Matri, 24-Jakelic.

Referee: Tagliavento.
