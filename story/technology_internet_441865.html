Rich parenting knowledge

A good father's rich and accurate knowledge not only plays a vital role in the baby's growth process, but also is very important for establishing his own prestige in the baby's heart.

Careful and serious about doing things

Even as an ordinary person, this is an advantage. A careful and serious father will make the baby feel comfortable and relaxed, and the baby can learn these advantages by osmosis.

Upright and sincere

This is the virtue that all good people should possess. The baby understands how to be a person with this, and the father's teaching by words and deeds is better than endless preaching.

Possess a healthy physique

Playing with the baby, taking the baby out, holding the sleeping or sick baby... These all depend on the strong physique and broad shoulders of the father, so a good father should take care of his body, exercise often, and always prepare to fulfill his duties as a father.

Strong sense of responsibility

Harmonious relationship with wife

Protecting the family and children is the inescapable duty of a good father, and the harmonious relationship with his wife is the foundation for the baby's healthy growth.

Try to spare time to accompany the child

The harmonious relationship between a good father and the baby is built through long-term contact. Fathers who have never played with their babies should be considered a typical "bad" father.

Have an open mind

The sea can accommodate hundreds of rivers, and greatness comes from tolerance. A good father's open and broad-mindedness, learning information, can not only broaden the baby's horizons, but also let the baby become more skillful and creative in doing things, and will not dare to act recklessly.

How to cultivate a happy child's personality

(1) Let the child choose themselves

Letting the child freely make some choices is an important factor in cultivating a happy personality. Of course, parents cannot be the ones to decide on most things, but it doesn't hurt to let the child make some decisions. For example, letting a two-year-old eat cucumbers instead of carrots, or letting a six-year-old choose one TV program from those the parents allow them to watch. Even at this level, children will learn how to choose to make themselves happy.

(2) Get along well with others

Getting along well with others is an important condition for happiness. The condition for children to get along well with others is that they have a good relationship with their parents. Parents can arrange for their children to play with other children as much as possible, such as joining a game group, or taking the children to the playground to play with children of the same age; if you can always welcome the children's friends to play at home, it would be even better.

(3) Don't spoil the child

"Giving too much to children will make them mistakenly think that pursuing material wealth is the source of happiness." Family psychologists say this. Someone found that after taking away most of the children's toys, they became more imaginative in playing, and therefore happier. Children who do not emphasize materialism will be satisfied even if they own less because they can use the things they have more flexibly.

This does not mean never giving anything to the child, just that you should not make the child mistakenly think that happiness is owning more than others.

(4) Cultivate broad interests

Happy people live a balanced life, so they can get happiness from many aspects. If only one thing makes you happy, then you are quite dangerous. For example, a child may be unhappy all night because he missed his favorite TV program; but another child with broad interests would read a book or play a game instead, and enjoy it equally.

(5) Teach the child humility

Parents should point out that there is always a turning point in any difficult situation, and teach the child this important skill to make himself happy. If the situation cannot be reversed despite efforts, parents should help the child find ways to comfort themselves. Teach the child to do activities that calm their mood, such as listening to music, reading books, riding bikes, or confiding in friends.

(6) Establish a happy and happy home

One of the best ways to help children find lasting happiness is for parents to live happily themselves, and explain to the children why they feel happy. This helps children recognize and experience happiness, and makes them often happy.

Many of our parents pay special attention to their children's study and health, but care little about whether the children are happy in life or whether they live happily. When parents meet each other, they often ask how their children are studying? Are they healthy? And rarely ask if the children are living happily. In fact, whether life is happy and enjoyable is an important aspect of measuring the quality of life. A healthy personality is an important aspect of feeling and discovering happiness. Studies by child psychologists have found that focusing on cultivating a happy personality in children is beneficial to their healthy growth. So, how to cultivate a happy personality in children? The following aspects can provide you with reference:

First, most of our children now are only children, which leads to excessive interaction with adults in their daily lives. This lifestyle suppresses the child's interests, deprives them of free and pleasant activities, and prohibits them. As a result, some children become "promising" and overly rational in life, lacking innocence. They seem to know a lot, but it is superficial. They can chat, but when faced with practical problems, they fall short. This is actually a false precocity. Close contact and emotional exchange among children plays an important role in cultivating a happy personality. Therefore, parents should encourage their children to play with peers and learn to have pleasant and harmonious interpersonal relationships through actual contact with peers.

Second, educate children to adjust their mental state in games. Children should understand that some people are happy all their lives, and the secret does not lie in getting how much or encountering what good things, but in having a very adaptable mental state, which allows them to quickly recover from disappointment. When the child encounters some setbacks, let him know that the future is always bright, and teach the child to pay attention to adjusting their mental state, prompting them to restore a happy mood on their own.

Third, the child's material possession desire must be appropriately restricted. Because giving too much to the child will make them have the illusion that "getting is the source of happiness," and also make the child easily ignore the existing happiness, forget happiness or take it for granted, and even deny its value. Let the child move actively, don't deprive the child of movement. Pay attention to let the child accumulate experience, rich experience is an important (necessary) nutrition for the child's growth. Therefore, they should be educated with examples that the happiness of life cannot only be equated with the possession of material wealth. Encourage children to create their own happiness, rather than the happiness given to them by their parents. Try to avoid the way of life where parents are always around and replace everything for the children. Appropriate loss, trouble, threat, and even unhappiness experiences for the child can make the child re-recognize the value of what they have gained and the happiness around them.

Fourth, cultivate the child's broad interests in games. Pay attention to the child's interests in daily life. Provide various interest choices for the child and give the child necessary guidance. The child's wide range of hobbies naturally makes it easy to have a happy personality.

Sixth, maintain a harmonious family life. Family harmony is also an important factor in cultivating a child's happy personality. Data shows that children who grow up in happy families are much more likely to lead happy lives as adults than those who grow up in unfortunate families.
