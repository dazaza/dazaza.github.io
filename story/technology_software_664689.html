Information Times News (Reporters Luo Yanghui, Tong Dan; Correspondents Hua Gongxuan, Gong Xuan) - At around midnight on January 29th, a family tragedy occurred in Huadong Town, Huadu District. Due to her dissatisfaction with her grandmother's nagging and refusal to give her pocket money, Wang, a 15-year-old junior high school girl, strangled her grandmother to death. To cover up her crime, she set fire to the house and fled. Currently, Wang has been arrested by the police and is under criminal detention. The case is still under further investigation.

"Mostly because of a quarrel over pocket money," according to the villagers. At the time, there was money scattered all over the kitchen floor—coins of fifty cents, one yuan, five yuan, and ten yuan were everywhere. "If they had given her the money then, this incident might not have happened."

The windows of the house where the incident occurred were completely destroyed, and the interior was in chaos. Photo by Information Times reporter Chen Wenjie.

Yesterday, the Huadu police announced that at 11:48 PM on January 28th, the police received a public report about a fire at a residence in Yongguang Village, Huadong Town. After receiving the report, the police officers and firefighters quickly responded to the situation. At 12:30 AM on January 29th, the fire was put out, and the police found the body of the female homeowner Hou inside the house. There were signs of arson at the scene. After thorough investigations, the police discovered that Wang (15 years old), Hou's granddaughter who lived with her, had disappeared and was considered a major suspect.

"Hou Grandma was killed by her granddaughter Xiao Yin, who then set fire to the house in an attempt to destroy evidence," said the villagers on-site. "(She) first killed her grandmother, then wrapped her in a mat before setting fire to the house. She slashed her grandmother's neck several times—it was brutal."

The body discovered at the fire scene was confirmed by the police to be that of the homeowner, Grandma Hou. There were clear signs of arson, and her granddaughter Wang, who lived with her, had vanished, making her a prime suspect.

According to Wang's confession: Her grandmother, Grandma Hou, was usually quite nagging and often caused conflicts with her. On the day of the incident, after being scolded by her grandmother, Wang got into an argument and physical altercation. She pushed Grandma Hou down and then strangled her until she died. To cover up her crime, Wang set fire to the house and fled. Currently, Wang has been placed under criminal detention by the police, and the case is still under further investigation.

The tragedy took place at No. 63, Yonglong North Second Alley, Yongguang Village, Huadong Town. Upon arriving at the scene, reporters saw a large pool of blood and drag marks near the entrance of the kitchen. In the room adjacent to the kitchen, clothing and furniture were charred, leaving everything pitch black. Local villagers said that shortly after midnight, multiple fire trucks arrived with sirens blaring, waking many of the villagers.
