The process of buying and selling stocks is as follows: When buying or selling stocks, you must specify whether you are buying or selling, the name (or code) of the stock, quantity, and price. Moreover, this order is only valid on the day it is placed. The content of the commission includes the abbreviation (code) of the stock you want to buy or sell, the quantity, and the price of buying or selling the stock. The abbreviation of the stock usually consists of three to four Chinese characters, and the stock code is six digits. When placing a commission, the stock code and abbreviation must be consistent. At the same time, there are certain regulations regarding the quantity of stocks bought or sold: That is, the quantity of stocks commissioned for purchase must be a whole multiple of 100, but the quantity of stocks commissioned for sale does not have to be a whole multiple of 100.

There are four ways to place a commission: Counter submission, telephone automatic commission, computer automatic commission, and remote terminal commission.
