"The year 2035, this is the age of machines!" This not only refers to the already highly developed mechanized mass production, life products and household appliances filled with mature technology. As an advertising slogan for a robotics company, it more importantly signifies the intelligent robots that have already infiltrated human life. As the best production tools and human companions, robots are beginning to play increasingly important roles in various fields. And due to the restrictions of the well-known three laws of robotics, humans trust these partners who can perform all kinds of work without complaint. Many of them have even become part of a family.

Chicago detective Del Spooner (Will Smith) has always been skeptical about robots. He doesn't believe that humans and robots can coexist harmoniously, and this doubt becomes more firm due to a murder case: Dr. Alfred Lanning, the chief engineer of U.S. Robotics Research Center, was killed, and one of the NS-5 advanced robots he developed himself is under suspicion. The superior orders Del to investigate this case. After thorough investigation and analysis, he discovers suspicious behavior from the head of the robotics research center, and the secret leaked from the research center is even more shocking: it seems that some robots have begun to act out of control.

The Robotics Research Center designed a control program for the NS-5 advanced robots, but as their computing capabilities continue to improve, they have learned to think independently and have solved the control password themselves. Now, they are a completely independent group, a high-intelligence mechanical group coexisting with humans, and at any time, they could turn into humanity's "mechanical enemy." Del must uncover the truth before the robots take action. For this, he meets Dr. Susan Calvin (Bridget Moynahan), a female scientist specializing in robot psychology. Together, they will launch an action against the robots, while also dealing with unexpected dangers...
