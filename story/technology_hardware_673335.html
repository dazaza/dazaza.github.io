The most common usage method of pearl powder

by idaqijje7 on 2012-02-23 15:38:34

1. Pearl Powder Cleansing Method: Pearl powder has good cleansing properties. For detailed usage, please refer to the following article "Can Pearl Powder + Clear Water be used for daily facial cleansing?"

Pearl powder + clear water cleansing indeed has very good cleaning effects. It can: perform deep pore cleaning; remove excess oil and dirt from the skin surface; have a certain exfoliating effect.

Considering the above third point (exfoliation), it is best not to use pearl powder + clear water for daily cleansing, even for oily skin. And for dry skin, do not directly use clear water + pearl powder for cleansing, as the skin will not be able to withstand it.

Summary:

Regardless of any skin type: The safest method for using pearl powder for cleansing is to add pearl powder to your facial cleanser every morning and evening. Basically, all facial cleansers can be mixed with pearl powder (depending on your skin type, oily skin can use herbal aesthetics water-oil balance cleanser, while dry skin can use Fujimu super moisturizing cleanser or Biosun EGF cleanser). This not only provides good cleaning results but also whitening effects.

If you have oily skin: You can use pearl powder + clear water for cleansing every few days (two or three days, or even a week, depending on the degree of oiliness). This is equivalent to a deep clean of the pores and removal of blackheads. I generally recommend using this method after taking a hot shower, as the pores are open at this time, making cleaning easier.

If you have dry skin: Avoid using pearl powder + water for direct cleaning to prevent over-exfoliation. Dry skin usually has thinner stratum corneum and very little surface oil, so there is no need for excessive cleaning.

Proper basic skin care reduces the potential for more skin problems. My consistent view is that if you do a good job with cleansing and care, you've already succeeded 60%. Nano pearl powder, due to its powerful surface adsorption, multiple nutrients, and trace elements, can serve both cleaning and care functions, making this basic work more effective.

2. Removing Blackheads and Exfoliating: Due to its excellent adsorption capability, pearl powder has a strong deep-cleaning ability and is therefore very effective in removing blackheads and exfoliating.

The popular method is as follows: Wet the blackhead area on your face, take an appropriate amount of pearl powder and gently massage for two to three minutes, then rinse with clear water, and the blackheads will be significantly reduced.

This method is quite effective, but it needs to be done correctly. If used improperly, the following three to four issues may arise:

Issue One: After applying pearl powder and massaging for a while, the pearl powder disappears, but no blackheads come out, leaving a sticky feeling, with no sense that the pearl powder has drawn out the blackheads.

Cause Analysis: This situation occurs because the skin is relatively oily. Instead of drawing out the blackheads, the pearl powder gets absorbed by the skin's oil, resulting in a sticky and dirty sensation. Increase the amount of pearl powder until you can rub out white streaks in the blackhead area.

Issue Two: Following the method, but still unable to massage anything out, feeling slippery and ineffective.

Cause Analysis: Too much water turns the pearl powder into a paste, causing it to slip. Use less water and more powder, making the pearl powder stickier. During massage, you should feel significant friction.

Issue Three: Despite reddening the nose, no blackheads come out.

Cause Analysis: There might be two reasons: A) The pearl powder you're using isn't fine enough. For removing blackheads, nano pearl powder is particularly recommended, such as Yuanmei Tang nano pearl powder, because it has strong adsorption capabilities and doesn't damage the skin. Using coarse pearl powder results in poorer effectiveness and can damage the skin. B) The blackheads are too stubborn to be removed easily. It's suggested to first soften them with a hot towel before using pearl powder to remove them.

Although pearl powder's effect is not as fast as blackhead strips, it is much safer. Some blackheads may not go away immediately, but don't rush. Perform the treatment two to three times a week for several weeks, and your skin will become much cleaner.

Will blackheads reappear after removal? This is a concern shared by many. The answer is: Not necessarily. However, if you pay attention to deep cleaning and controlling oil, the occurrence of blackheads will significantly decrease. Just like sometimes eating and washing your face have similarities, you can't expect one meal to last forever without eating again. Even if you don't eat a big meal, snacks and light meals are necessary regularly.

3. Oil Control and Acne Treatment Methods:

Long-term practice has proven that using pearl powder for acne treatment is more effective when taken internally and applied externally simultaneously.

People with acne usually have oily skin and internal heat, which makes them more prone to acne under humid and hot weather conditions in the south. Taking pearl powder internally has very good regulatory effects, which is why pearl powder is especially popular in Guangdong for this reason.

The secret of using pearl powder externally for acne lies in its ability to deeply clean the skin, control oil, and keep the skin dry.

These effects are crucial because many people develop acne due to inadequate deep cleaning of the skin, excessive oiliness, and lack of attention to cleanliness. Dirt and sebum can easily accumulate on the skin surface and in the pores, mixing with dust and grime. When the weather heats up, fatty acids deteriorate, stimulating the skin and causing inflammation. People with thicker stratum corneum and clogged pores face even greater challenges when these blockages occur.

Nano pearl powder has excellent adsorption properties. Using it for external acne treatment mainly helps with cleaning, removing excess dead skin cells, controlling oil, soothing the skin, and keeping it dry. Achieving these points means conquering acne is just around the corner.

How to use pearl powder to clean acne-prone skin: Mix a small amount of nano pearl powder with a little water and use it as a cleanser, gently rubbing your face. You may be able to rub out white streaks, which are formed by the pearl powder adsorbing dirt and oil. If you feel the cleaning is insufficient, add more pearl powder.

Some girls, after treating acne with fruit acids or other irritating substances, may experience red blood vessels and sensitive skin. In such cases, it is recommended to mix pearl powder appropriately with your facial cleanser. Personally, I believe that doing a good job with cleansing solves 60% of acne problems.

Acne-prone areas are generally the T-zone, though some people experience it in the V-zone. These areas should be intensively cleaned with pearl powder, but always within the comfort of the skin, as solving the problem requires persistence over time.

I've seen some girls apply seven to eight layers of skincare products on their faces, resulting in poor skin condition and frequent acne outbreaks. Actually, applying more skincare products is not necessarily better, especially not thicker layers. If you fall into this category, reduce the layers to one or two.

How to use pearl powder for oil control: Two methods - one is to add a small amount of pearl powder to your daily skincare products. Remember not to mix the entire bottle of skincare product with pearl powder at once; instead, mix a bit at a time in the palm of your hand. Experience shows that proper mixing will make your skincare product non-greasy and suit your needs, gradually reducing skin oiliness and maintaining a fresh feeling throughout the day. Girls with makeup habits can use pearl powder instead of loose powder, preferably nano-level pearl powder, which not only provides a strong transparent and three-dimensional effect, completely reaching the level of high-end loose powder, but also does not clog pores, having nourishing and sun protection effects, which is very rare and precious.

If acne bursts or breaks, what should you do? You can mix chloramphenicol eye drops with nano pearl powder into a paste, or mix a little pearl powder with tetracycline ointment and apply it to the acne. After sleeping overnight, it will significantly subside. A special reminder: Overuse of antibiotics can easily lead to bacterial resistance (in short: prolonged use of a certain antibiotic against bacteria renders it ineffective, and the bacteria will mutate to counteract the drug), so... to avoid its resistance and difficulty in treatment, it is recommended not to use antibiotics casually.

For acne-prone skin, avoid using nutrient-rich things like egg whites or milk as masks. The reason is that our skin surface has many native bacteria. Acne-prone skin often has local inflammation or minor abrasions, and bacteria are preparing to invade. At this time, providing these nutrient-rich substances is equivalent to providing bacteria with a "culture medium," worsening the acne. Remember this.

Finally, it's worth noting that the causes of acne vary, so pearl powder cannot solve all types of acne - for example, if your acne recurs periodically with "old friends," it is likely due to hormonal issues, and pearl powder may not necessarily solve it. Others may result from contact with allergens, suddenly appearing with small rashes, which differ entirely from the previously mentioned acne types. Generally, eliminating the allergen is sufficient, and there is no need for pearl powder intervention.

4. Pearl Powder Mask Usage: Can achieve whitening, fading spots, making the skin delicate and smooth.

Although pearl powder masks seem simple, their functions and usage involve considerable knowledge. Pearl powder masks can have various effects such as whitening, fading spots, oil control, acne removal, and blackhead removal. Depending on different situations, they can produce excellent beauty results - this is probably why pearl powder masks have been passed down for thousands of years and continue to be popular.

1. Basic Method of Making Pearl Powder Masks

The basic method is: After cleansing your face, mix nutritional substances with pearl powder into a paste, apply it on your face, and wash it off after ten to fifteen minutes. It is advisable to perform this twice or thrice a week. With persistent maintenance, delicate and youthful skin naturally comes into being. There is no specific age limit for anyone who needs skincare.

Using diluted substances like fruit juice or pearl powder to make masks with paper masks promotes absorption and yields better results.

There are three principles for making pearl powder masks, following which will enhance your pearl powder beauty routine:

Diamond Principle: Combine pearl powder with different nutritional substances according to different skin types and desired skincare goals to achieve the best results.

Golden Principle: Cleanse your skin before application to facilitate pearl powder and nutrient absorption.

Silver Principle: Absolutely try to use fine pearl powder for masks, preferably nano pearl powder like Yuanmei Tang, which is easy to absorb due to its fine texture. Additionally, it saves on quantity and offers good results, making it a worthy choice.

So, how much pearl powder and nutritional substance should you use? This depends on the type of pearl powder we are using. The coarser the pearl powder, the larger the quantity needed; the finer it is, the smaller the quantity required. For example, nano pearl powder requires only a few grams per use (as an example, with Yuanmei Tang nano pearl powder, 30 grams of pearl powder is sufficient for 100 mask applications, truly astonishingly economical). Also, the accompanying nutritional substances should not be mixed excessively; just enough to cover the entire face is sufficient, and it’s not about applying thicker layers.

2. Typical Pearl Powder Masks

(1) Honey Pearl Powder Mask:

This is a mask suitable for normal skin maintenance. Normal skin maintenance refers to skin without particular issues (like acne). Honey, known for its rich nutrition, including fructose, various trace elements, vitamins, etc., has excellent anti-aging effects on the skin. Honey combined with pearl powder is a perfect match, especially suitable for mature skin.

Precautions: Honey pearl powder masks are not very suitable for dry skin because honey is a highly osmotic substance that can make the skin feel dry. Other skin types are suitable. Do not buy cheap "honey" made from sucrose; people with acne should not use pearl powder honey masks.

(2) Milk Pearl Powder Whitening Mask:

Milk + pearl powder = a great whitening duo. Among various pearl powder whitening mask recipes, the milk pearl powder mask is a timeless classic. Milk itself has excellent whitening effects, and when combined with pearl powder, another great whitener, the results are doubled. The challenge with milk and pearl powder masks lies in their thin consistency after mixing. To solve this, simply get a piece of compressed paper mask, soak the milk and pearl powder mixture onto the paper mask, and apply it to your face. If it dries out during application, you can sprinkle more liquid. After 15 minutes, remove the mask and look in the mirror – you'll be amazed at how bright and tender your skin looks!

Precautions: It's best to use boiled milk or UHT milk (Tetra Pak cartons marked with Tetrapak). Suitable for all skin types, but people with acne should not use milk pearl powder masks. Combining internal pearl powder intake enhances the effect.

If you have spots, you can gently massage the spot areas with milk pearl powder mixtures, but ensure you use nano pearl powder. Massaging post-acne marks (preferably those without broken skin) with milk mixed with pearl powder can help fade them. Milk-soaked pearl powder paper masks are ideal for people with red blood vessels and thin skin.

(3) Pearl Powder Egg White Mask:

The pearl powder egg white mask is a calming and tightening mask, suitable for oily skin with large pores. Since pearl powder has good oil control effects and egg whites have excellent tightening effects, combining the two effectively addresses large oil patches and large pores.

Precautions: People with dry skin should avoid trying this, otherwise, they might lose faith in pearl powder masks altogether.

Since using pearl powder egg white masks once or twice a week is insufficient, regular care is essential. Suggest cleansing with pearl powder + facial cleanser morning and night, then applying EGF tapping cream to tighten the skin! People with acne should not use this! People with red blood vessels, thin skin, or extremely sensitive skin should be cautious about letting the egg white dry or avoid using it altogether.

(4) Pearl Powder Aloe Vera Mask:

This mask has excellent anti-inflammatory and acne-removal effects and feels refreshing. Use high-quality aloe vera juice mixed with pearl powder. The slightly acidic nature of aloe vera juice does not affect pearl powder. Some people may be allergic to aloe vera juice, so it's best to test beforehand. If allergic to aloe vera juice, consider switching to tea tree or lavender hydrosol mixed with pearl powder for paper masks.

Precautions: For acne-prone skin, which is usually oily, poorly cleaned, and has internal heat, internal pearl powder intake is recommended, and extra attention to cleaning is crucial. People with acne should avoid mixing pearl powder with nutrient-rich substances like milk, egg whites, and honey to prevent worsening acne. Changing your lifestyle, such as reducing late-night activities, can be beneficial. For particularly aggressive acne, use eye ointment mixed with pearl powder to spot-treat it. Almond powder also has excellent anti-inflammatory and tightening effects, so adding it together can be helpful if possible.

(5) Pearl Powder Cucumber Juice Mask:

Cucumbers have excellent rejuvenating effects and keep the skin hydrated, so combining pearl powder with cucumber juice is a great way to moisturize dry skin. Dry skin tends to develop spots, so adding a little milk is a good idea!

Five, Other Precautions for DIY Pearl Powder Masks:

Once again, I strongly emphasize: Do not randomly use pearl powder for masks without considering the situation and skin type. Just because others are doing it and saying it works, you jump in without thought. Using the wrong method leads to opposite results. Although it may sound repetitive, this is the diamond rule, and it's worth emphasizing again.

Some students may feel dryness or itching after using pearl powder masks. Simply hydrate your skin by applying a moisturizing lotion, and everything will be fine. If you experience discomfort, such as allergies (redness, rash), wash it off quickly and inspect your pearl powder: Is there something wrong with the pearl powder? Many people buy fake pearl powder for cheap prices and then blame pearl powder for ineffectiveness, but counterfeit and inferior products can never be good.

When making pearl powder masks, the core is pearl powder, so using good quality pearl powder is key to achieving satisfactory results. Can any lotion or toner be mixed with pearl powder?

Answer: Pearl powder is not suitable for use with some lotions and creams.

Reasons:

1. Some lotions contain a widely used thickener called "carbomer." Under a certain pH value, it remains in gel form, but when the pH changes, it becomes watery. Cosmetics containing carbomer are adjusted to a near-neutral pH to maintain its gel characteristics. Adding pearl powder slightly raises the pH — at this point, carbomer turns watery, and pearl powder becomes flocculent. Therefore, such lotions are unsuitable for mixing with pearl powder.

2. Some creams are extremely thick with a high oil content, which can easily clog pores. Adding pearl powder makes them even stickier and more likely to clog pores. In such cases, even fine pearl powder can't prevent it, so avoid using pearl powder with these creams. An example is some users who shouldn't use Pearl Powder + Pien Tze Huang, although Pien Tze Huang is an excellent product, it just doesn't suit everyone.

3. How to determine if pearl powder is suitable for use with your lotion? Simply experiment: Take a bit of lotion in your palm, add a bit of pearl powder, and observe the results within seconds: If the lotion or cream turns watery and the pearl powder becomes flocculent, it's not suitable for mixing. Some enthusiastic individuals boldly mix pearl powder into their entire bottle of lotion... resulting in hundreds of dollars turning into water. So please be careful and mix as needed.

If pearl powder cannot be used with your lotion, you can apply the lotion first, then lightly dust a layer of pearl powder on top, serving both isolation and whitening purposes.

Six, Isolation and Sunscreen Usage: Mix an appropriate amount of pearl powder into the lotion, or after applying lotion and cream, lightly dust a layer of Yuanmei Tang nano pearl powder. This provides UV protection equivalent to SPF 15 to 20, fully meeting daily protection needs. However, outdoor sports require regular, oil-based sunscreen, as sweat and water can wash it off.

Seven, Pearl Powder Spot Fading Usage: Add pearl powder during morning and evening cleansing (refer to item one), perform pearl powder milk masks twice or thrice weekly (refer to item four), and follow daily care (refer to item five). Simultaneously, persistently take pearl powder internally for at least three months to see improvement. The likelihood of improvement for genetically inherited spots is lower. Solar spots, pregnancy spots, and age-related spots can improve, though some may not see results within three months, requiring longer periods. This process can be challenging to sustain, but many people have achieved relatively ideal results.
