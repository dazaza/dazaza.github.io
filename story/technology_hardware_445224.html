Is it safe to have ptosis surgery on the upper eyelid? Ophthalmic plastic surgery experts pointed out that common upper eyelid ptosis includes paralytic ptosis, sympathetic ptosis, and myogenic ptosis. If these three types of upper eyelid ptosis are not corrected at the best time, the ptosis will become more severe, causing greater trouble in daily life later on.

Methods of Upper Eyelid Ptosis Surgery:

Currently, there are mainly three methods for correcting ptosis: levator muscle shortening surgery, frontalis muscle flap suspension, and fascia lata band suspension. These three surgical methods target different conditions. Due to the varying specific conditions of each patient with upper eyelid ptosis, it is necessary for a ptosis correction doctor to identify and diagnose the upper eyelid condition to choose the most suitable surgical method for the patient.

During the process of upper eyelid ptosis surgery, it is necessary to adjust the height of the palpebral fissure and appropriately fix the position of the tarsal plate (http://www.didi.com.cn/list-29.html). The correction surgery for upper eyelid ptosis must maintain symmetry on both sides. Experts point out that after correcting bilateral upper eyelid ptosis and achieving symmetry, double eyelid surgery can be performed to achieve the best results.
