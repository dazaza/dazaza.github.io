"Son, am I right?" Laughing Arhat Du Ba immediately ordered someone to bring a basin of silver coins, with eighteen shiny silver ingots placed on top, each weighing ten liang, totaling exactly one hundred eighty liang. He smiled and said, "Please, young master, count it!" "No need! Just put them all on the gambling table!" The servant placed the silver on the table where the simpleton was sitting. Laughing Arhat said, "Wish the young master a pleasant game!" Then he turned to the young maiden and said, "Sister Jiu, make sure you entertain the young master well and don't neglect him." Sister Jiu beamed and replied in a pleasant voice, "Eighth Uncle, I'll be careful." Laughing Arhat nodded in satisfaction, then left with a pleased smile, taking the pearl with him. Once that pearl was in his hands, no one else could hope to redeem it. Not only couldn't they redeem it, but they would also owe Laughing Arhat an unpayable debt to Yama, leaving them ruined. The thirty-li interest per hour wasn't a small amount; after one hour, two hundred liang would become two hundred sixty liang, and later with compound interest, half a day later, it would turn into one thousand twenty-nine liang four qian seven fen of silver. So gamblers dared not borrow from the money shop, preferring to walk a few more steps to the pawnshop to exchange clothes and valuables for silver. Even without money to redeem their items, at least they wouldn't owe debts—just lose their belongings. Although the pawnshops were also run by the Yin Sect, the value of pawned clothes and goods was limited. For amounts over a hundred liang, the pawnshops wouldn't transact, forcing them to borrow from the money shop instead.

Once Laughing Arhat left, the young maiden named Jiu gave the simpleton a flirtatious look: "Young master, now that you have money, you can place big bets!" The simpleton laughed, "Exactly! Exactly, now we can play again!" Xiao Qin wanted to gouge out her flirtatious eyes so she couldn’t toss them around anymore. Xiao Qin thought that these eyes had disturbed the simpleton's spirit, but she didn't know that Sister Jiu was an old hand at the casino, highly skilled at gambling. The simpleton lost several times because she secretly tampered with things. Others couldn't see it, nor could Xiao Qin, but the simpleton noticed and pretended not to know, letting this seductive Sister Jiu feel accomplished. He planned to surprise her unexpectedly, win back tens of thousands of liang of silver, and turn her smile into tears.

Sister Jiu shook the dice cup, her eyes fixed on the one hundred eighty liang of silver in front of the simpleton. After placing it down, she asked the simpleton, "Young master, please place your bet!" She truly wanted to take all one hundred eighty liang of silver in one go.

The simpleton’s eyes weren't looking at the 'big' or 'small' characters but at the various numbered squares between the 'big' and 'small' characters. There were six large squares representing three identical numbers, like three fours or three sixes. Below the six large squares, there was another row of rectangles representing different pai gow tiles such as Heaven, Earth, Man, Harmony, Plum, Long, Board, Axe, etc. The simpleton asked, "If I bet on these squares, how is the payout calculated?"

Sister Jiu giggled and said, "Young master, if you bet on three identical numbers, it pays ten to one."

"Oh? Ten liang pays one hundred liang?"

"Yes!"

"And what about betting on the rectangles below?"

"One to three."

"Why is the payout so low?"

"Young master, it's not little! Because with three dice showing different numbers, they can form three tile combinations. For example, if the dice show one, three, and six, one and three form the Three and Pair tile, three and six form the Nine Point tile, and one and six form the Big Copper Hammer tile. It has a higher chance of winning, so paying one to three is reasonable. Young master, are you not betting on big or small but on numbers?"

"Yes, yes, I always lose when betting on big or small. I want to bet on numbers!"

"Good! Young master, please place your bet. I'm about to reveal the dice!"

"Alright! I bet!" The simpleton placed twenty liang of silver on the Harmony tile rectangle.

Sister Jiu's melodious voice rang out: "Everyone, please finalize your bets! I'm revealing the dice!" A bell chimed. Sister Jiu revealed the dice and announced, "One, two, five, eight points, small. Eat big, pay small. Three points, seven points, and Little Copper Hammer win; everything else loses!"

The simpleton's twenty liang of silver was taken by Sister Jiu once again. She smiled at him, "Young master, wouldn't it have been better to bet on small this time? How did you end up betting on Harmony? Young master, don't worry, try again next time, you're bound to win."

Xiao Qin sighed, "Young master, stop gambling. Your luck isn't good."

The simpleton said, "Stop? How do I get my lost silver back?"

"My young sir, do you still hope to win silver? If you don't lose all one hundred eighty liang, it's already good."

"Phew! Phew! Can't you say something nice? Good doesn't work, bad works. If I really lose it all, I'll sell you to the boss of this casino."

"What? You're selling me?"

"No! No! I was just saying it. Don't take it seriously. Even if I sold myself, I wouldn't dare sell you."

Sister Jiu saw the situation and smiled at Xiao Qin, "Little brother, you're so smart and clever. How could your young master sell you?"

Afterward, the simpleton continued to place several more bets, losing whether betting on big, small, numbers, or tiles. One hundred eighty liang of silver was finally reduced to fifty liang, like throwing a cucumber at a dog, losing most of it. Xiao Qin pouted beside him, wanting to speak but afraid it would upset the simpleton. It seemed the simpleton came here not to cause trouble but to gamble. Alright! Let's see what you do when you lose it all. Could one pearl possibly be reclaimed?

Finally, in a fit of anger, the simpleton placed all fifty liang of silver on the three threes square, making even Xiao Qin widen her eyes: "Young master, what are you doing?" The simpleton pinched her hand, telling her not to speak out of turn.

Sister Jiu, however, chuckled with delight: "Young master, are you going all-in?"

"Exactly! All-in."

Sister Jiu seemed to care about him: "What will you do if you lose, young master? Are you stopping?"

"Play! Why should I stop? I still have two pearls."

"Right! I forgot the young master still has two pearls. Pawning them, you can play for a whole day and night."

"Quickly reveal the dice!" said the simpleton.

"Alright! I'll reveal the dice! Everyone, finalize your bets!" This time, Sister Jiu tampered with the dice even more, almost certain to take all fifty liang of the simpleton's silver. But when she revealed the dice and looked, she was instantly stunned—three dice, all showing three. The surrounding gamblers were also dumbfounded. The simpleton jumped up in joy: "Three threes, I won! I won! Xiao Qin, I won!"

Xiao Qin's previously pouted lips now bloomed into a flower-like smile. Her childish character still took winning and losing very seriously, being happy when winning and discouraged when losing. She happily asked, "Young master, how much did we win?"

"Ten to one, we won five hundred liang of silver."

"Really? Does that mean we've won back all the silver we lost?"

"And more! Xiao Qin, look, aren't you silent now, and I've won?"

"Alright! Young master, I won't talk anymore!"

Sister Jiu paused for a moment, then recovered her usual flirtatious demeanor, smiling and saying, "Young master has indeed won five hundred liang of silver. Congratulations, young master!" She instructed her assistant to deliver five hundred liang of silver to the simpleton.

Xiao Qin said, "Young master, how can I carry so much silver? It's so heavy."

"Don't carry it! Don't carry it! I'm still gambling."

Sister Jiu sent another flirtatious glance at the simpleton, smiling and saying, "I thought the young master wouldn't gamble anymore!"

"I just won once, why shouldn't I continue? I want to win a few more times!"

Sister Jiu smiled, "Hopefully, your wish comes true." She began shaking the dice cup again, placing it down and saying, "Everyone, please place your bets."

The simpleton immediately placed all five hundred fifty liang of silver on the three ones square, another all-in bet, paying ten to one. Sister Jiu knew what was happening, thinking: This time, I doubt you won't lose. Don't I know what dice I'm shaking? The numbers inside the cup are four, five, six—big! Even if you guess correctly, as long as I slightly move my palm when revealing the dice, it will change completely. She smiled and asked, "Young master, have you finalized your bet? If not, you can still move it to another square."

"No need! No need! I've decided, reveal the dice!"

"Alright! Reveal the dice!"

As soon as Sister Jiu revealed the cup with her jade-like hands, all the gamblers around stared intently, almost shouting in surprise together: "Three ones, three!"

Sister Jiu's flirtatious eyes glanced down and saw that all three dice indeed showed one, like three red devil eyes glaring at her. Sister Jiu was shocked, almost dropping the cup lid in her trembling hands. She simply couldn't believe it—clearly, the numbers were four, five, six, how did they all turn into one?

Xiao Qin cheered even louder, "Young master, we've won again! How much silver did we win this time?"

"Ten to one, a total of five thousand five hundred liang of silver!"

Five thousand five hundred liang of silver, for the VIP room gamblers, was still insignificant, as their wins and losses often exceeded ten thousand liang. But for the main hall gamblers, it was a staggering amount, causing a commotion throughout the hall. The news quickly reached Laughing Arhat Du Ba's ears, who surprisingly asked the informant, "Who won so much silver?"

"The wealthy young master who borrowed silver from Eighth Master using a pearl."

"What? Him?"

"Yes, Eighth Master."

"How did he win?"

"He used the one hundred eighty liang of silver borrowed from Eighth Master. At first, he lost several times, leaving only fifty liang. In the end, he went all-in with the fifty liang, betting on three threes, and won five hundred liang. Then he placed the five hundred fifty liang on three ones and hit again."

"Sister Jiu didn't tamper with it, letting him hit?"

"How could Sister Jiu not tamper? But it was as if the young master was aided by divine spirits, shocking Sister Jiu greatly."

"What divine spirits? I don't believe that nonsense. Could the young man be a gambling expert, tampering with the dice himself?"

"The subordinate didn't notice anything."

Just as they were speaking, another person rushed in anxiously. Laughing Arhat shouted, "What's wrong? Why are you so flustered?"

The person reported, "Report to Eighth Master, Sister Jiu lost again, losing sixty thousand five hundred liang of silver. She sent me to ask Eighth Master for more silver."

Laughing Arhat Du Ba jumped up from his chair: "What!? Sixty thousand five hundred liang? How did she lose?"

Sixty thousand plus liang wasn't a small amount—it was the first major incident since the casino opened. This sum alone weighed over three thousand seven hundred catties, requiring eight large chests to hold five hundred catties of silver each. The casino's daily average income, after deducting wages, taxes, food, and other miscellaneous expenses, was only a few thousand liang of silver. These sixty thousand plus liang amounted to nearly half a month's revenue for the casino. The ever-smiling Laughing Arhat now couldn't smile anymore.

The person stammered, "That...that young master also went all-in and hit."

Winning three consecutive all-ins was unprecedented in the casino's history. It must either be luck or the young man tampering with the dice. Du Ba didn't believe in luck. This young man dared to eat at the casino without knowing his life was at risk. Laughing Arhat Du Ba shouted, "Ask Seventh Sister to check what's going on, and don't let the young man leave with the money!"

"Yes!"

"Hold on! Invite the young man to the VIP room instead, and I'll meet him there with Seventh Sister."

"Yes, Eighth Master."

Seventh Sister was not only beautiful but also excelled in gambling skills, being the ace of the casino. Laughing Arhat trained seven or eight beautiful women, among whom Seventh Sister was the best and most divine gambler. Whenever wealthy princes or rich merchants came, she usually represented the casino. Even seasoned gamblers couldn't help but admire her skills and retreat gracefully after a few rounds. It could be said that Seventh Sister had never failed, earning her the title of "Invincible Princess." Even Laughing Arhat Du Ba respected her, calling her Seventh Sister instead of Seventh Younger Sister.

The simpleton and Xiao Qin carried six hundred sixty thousand silver notes and five hundred fifty liang of silver into the VIP room. Now, they had become the richest people in Changsha City, with a total of sixty-six thousand five hundred fifty liang of silver. Even if they spent only on food and clothing, it might last them a lifetime.

This VIP room was even more meticulously decorated and luxurious than the one in Anhua County, giving a sense of comfort and enjoyment. The interior was elegant without being vulgar, making guests feel as if they had entered a fairyland. When the simpleton and Xiao Qin entered, Laughing Arhat, Seventh Sister, and two elegant maidservants-cum-assistants greeted them with smiles.

Seventh Sister's demeanor was vastly different from Ninth Sister's. Ninth Sister was beautiful but vulgar, frivolous, and overly flirtatious, making proper individuals reluctant to approach her. Seventh Sister was graceful and dignified, exuding warmth. She resembled a noblewoman yet surpassed one, dignified without arrogance, smiling without frivolity. The simpleton and Xiao Qin couldn't help but glance at her, secretly amazed: How could such a young woman exist in a casino? Who was she?

Laughing Arhat Du Ba maintained his cheerful demeanor: "Young master, please sit!"

"Thank you!"

The simpleton sat down, with Xiao Qin standing behind him. After the maids served fragrant tea, the simpleton asked, "May I inquire what matter Du Boss wishes to discuss with me?"

"To teach, I dare not. I've heard that the young master has extraordinary luck and miraculous gambling skills, captivating our Seventh Sister. She wishes not only to witness the young master's grace but also to learn from the young master's divine skills."

"Not at all, I have no divine skills, merely lucky. To tell the truth, I should thank Du Boss. If Du Boss hadn't lent me the money, I wouldn't have had such luck."

After finishing, the simpleton took out two silver notes worth a thousand liang each and handed them over, "These are two thousand liang of silver notes, as a token of my gratitude to Du Boss. Please kindly accept them."

Laughing Arhat hesitated, "Young master! What does this mean? Do you look down on Du?"

"How could I have such thoughts? I only wish to express my gratitude, nothing else. I hope Du Boss will honor me by accepting this gesture, otherwise, I would be utterly ashamed."

Seventh Sister gently spoke, "Eighth Master! Since the young master is so sincere, Eighth Master should accept it, otherwise, it would cool the young master's heart."

Laughing Arhat laughed heartily, "Fine! Fine! Then Du will humbly accept! Someone!"

Outside the VIP room, someone immediately responded, "Yes! Eighth Master!"

A tough-looking man entered and asked, "Eighth Master! What is it?"

"Fetch the young master's pearl and promissory note!"

"Yes!"

Soon, the pearl and promissory note were brought. Laughing Arhat personally handed the pearl and promissory note to the simpleton, "Please verify."

The simpleton immediately recognized the pearl as his own and smiled, "Do I not trust Du Boss? No need to verify." He handed the pearl to Xiao Qin and instructed her to return the promissory note to Du Ba, along with another two hundred liang of silver.

Laughing Arhat became somewhat displeased, "Young master, you..."

The simpleton bowed and said, "I know Du Boss is a businessman. As the saying goes, men and gentlemen, brothers are brothers, friends are friends, and finances must be clear and distinct. This is business, not personal sentiment. If Du Boss wishes to grant me a favor in the future, please use another method. Regarding these two hundred liang of borrowed silver, I hope Du Boss accepts them to avoid breaking the rules of the money shop."

Laughing Arhat looked at the simpleton with a strange gaze and said, "Fine! Fine! Since the young master says so, I, Du, cannot break the rules of the money shop and must accept." He then told the tough man, "Take these two hundred liang to the accountant and erase today's account."

"Yes! Eighth Master." The tough man took the silver and left.

Laughing Arhat smiled again, "My Seventh Sister wishes to learn from the young master's divine skills! The young master cannot refuse, can you?"

This exploiter, although he received two thousand liang from the simpleton, felt no gratitude at all. Compared to the sixty thousand plus liang, two thousand was a mere trifle. Moreover, this silver belonged to the casino, and sooner or later, both principal and interest would be reclaimed. Returning the pearl to the simpleton was already considered a great favor. Otherwise, the simpleton wouldn't even have been able to retrieve the pearl.

The simpleton said, "Learning is too honorable. If I may accompany Seventh Sister in a game, I would be honored."

Seventh Sister smiled gracefully and chirped like a bird, "I thank the young master in advance for allowing me to witness the young master's divine skills."
