In Dongguan, Guangdong, more than a hundred people collect tea leaves worth over 100 million yuan.

by anonymous on 2012-02-20 10:58:45

Xinhua News Agency, Dongguan, August 17th (Li Yingmin, Liu Yuanzhong) - Guangdong Province is currently the largest Pu'er tea sales and distribution center in the country, while Dongguan City is one of China's largest Pu'er tea collection markets. In this city, there are more than a hundred collectors whose tea collections are worth over RMB 100 million.

Lu Shuxun, the Secretary-General of the Dongguan Tea Industry Association, told reporters today that Guangdong Province exports over 6,000 tons of Pu'er tea annually, far exceeding Yunnan Province, the "hometown" of Pu'er tea (which exported 1,889 tons in 2008), ranking first nationwide. Industry insiders conservatively estimate that Guangdong still has no less than 2 million tons of Pu'er tea in stock, with a market value of RMB 15 billion waiting to be sold.

According to data provided by the Dongguan Tea Industry Association, there are more than 5,000 large and small tea shops distributed across various towns in Dongguan, with over 60,000 Pu'er tea enthusiasts who purchase more than 20,000 tons of Pu'er tea each year for collection and investment purposes. The transaction volume exceeds RMB 3 billion.

"Dongguan is one of China's largest Pu'er tea collection markets. After several years of Pu'er collection and investment fever, the current amount of Pu'er tea accumulated in Dongguan is quite astonishing, making it the city with the largest inventory within Guangdong Province," said Lu Shuxun, the Secretary-General of the Dongguan Tea Association. He added that due to many investors directly sourcing their goods from Yunnan, the exact inventory is difficult to estimate. According to his disclosure, there are over a hundred collectors in Dongguan whose tea collections are worth more than RMB 100 million. For example, in Chang'an Town, one enthusiast has collected over 4,000 tons of Pu'er tea and Hunan dark tea. Additionally, in Changping, Shilong, and Dalang, many business owners have the habit of collecting tea.

Lu Shuxun believes that after the "Pu'er craze," Dongguan's tea market has shifted from an "investment-oriented" model to a "consumption-oriented" one. He introduced that many tea tasters in Dongguan are increasingly paying attention to Pu'er tea. Although the overall price trend of Pu'er tea has started to decline, some popular "consumer-grade" Pu'er teas have shown signs of "rebound." He explained that since the beginning of the year, many tea merchants have seen a significant reduction in wholesale volumes, but retail sales have increased somewhat. This indicates that an increasing number of consumers are joining the ranks of those who drink Pu'er tea. (End)
