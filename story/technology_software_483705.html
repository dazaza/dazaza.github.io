The midpoint of the constant temperature tube is consistent with the normal water level of the pot in height.

by qewy26uu23 on 2011-08-31 12:55:44

The main element of a single-impulse constant-temperature tube type automatic water feed regulator is an inclined metal tube (constant-temperature tube). The lower end of the constant-temperature tube is fixed and connected to the water space of the drum, while the upper end is connected to the valve stem of the water feed control valve via a lever transmission mechanism, and is also connected to the steam space of the drum through a steam pipe. The midpoint of the constant-temperature tube is at the same height as the normal water level in the drum. The steam pipe should be as short as possible and insulated so that the temperature of the steam section of the constant-temperature tube is close to the saturated steam temperature. Conversely, the water pipe should be sufficiently cooled so that the temperature of the water section of the constant-temperature tube approaches the boiler water temperature. In this way, when the water level in the drum drops, the length of the steam section increases and the water section shortens, causing the constant-temperature tube to extend and pull the transmission mechanism, thereby opening the water feed control valve wider. Conversely, the constant-temperature tube contracts, reducing the opening of the water feed control valve, thus achieving the purpose of automatic water feed regulation.

**What are the characteristics of the water level control and protection device in Viessmann wall-hung boilers?**

Source: Beijing Viessmann Wall-Hung Boiler Repair http://blog.tianya.cn/blogger/blog_main.asp?BlogID=3778656.

An electrode-type water level controller belongs to a two-position regulator. That is, when the water level in the drum rises or falls to a certain value (e.g., 25mm), the electrode connects, then controls the stop and start of the water feed pump via a transistor relay, keeping the water level within the specified range. This regulator can be used in conjunction with an alarm and a low-water fuel cutoff device, which issues a voice alarm. The principle of operation of the electrode-type water feed regulation device is the same as that of a float-type water level alarm. The electrodes move up and down with the water level inside the regulator. On the outside of the sleeve, there are three pairs of mercury switches. Through the closure of these mercury switch contacts, different operations can be achieved, such as stopping the water feed pump at high water levels, starting the water feed pump at low water levels, and issuing an alarm at extremely low water levels.

The above two types of regulators are intermittent regulators, generally used on small industrial boilers. For boilers with larger capacities, constant-temperature tube type (single impulse), dual impulse, and triple impulse water feed regulators should be used.
