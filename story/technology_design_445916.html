With Exmor R® CMOS Image Sensor devices, the use of new technology allows placement of the metal wiring layer over the photodiodes. This results in a doubling of the sensitivity of the imaging device, while also halving the image signal-to-noise ratio, significantly enhancing picture quality, especially in low-light environments such as night shooting.

The BIONZ image processor, paired with the Exmor R® CMOS Sensor, provides high-performance and high-speed transmission of large amounts of pixel data signals. The key features include:

- Synchronization recording function that captures 2.7 million pixel images only limited by the highest capacity storage available.

- Smooth slow-motion shot function capable of capturing subtle movements like golf swings or football plays.

- Face Detection Technology that quickly associates personal faces for video recovery.

- Backlight compensation that adjusts exposure based on environmental brightness to capture details in both light and dark areas.

- New Shock Crabs (New Dynamic Mode) that improves stability against general shocks and vibrations, providing a stabilizing effect even while walking and shooting video.

- Full-resolution 1920x1080 high-definition recording with real images up to fine detail levels, creating more realistic visual effects according to international color gamut standards.

- Independent 3.111 megapixel still image recording with perfect quality not requiring AVCHD FX mode.

- LCD display offering more than 1.66 times the traditional hybrid LCD display with less reflection and increased contrast, visible even in bright sunlight.
