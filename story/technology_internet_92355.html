Interview by Men's Wear:

It's very refreshing to talk with such a genuine girl who practices yoga on the downhill path, secretly thinks about her own happy things while appearing on TV shows, and even lounges on the sofa during an interview with "Men's Wear," directly demanding "start asking" in an unadorned tone. All words are like bullets instantly leaving the barrel, facing the bullseye without any curve.

(F = Men's Wear, Tang = Tang Yifei)

F: I've seen the set of pictures you completed.

Tang: How do they look?

F: They look beautiful, with an explosive feel. You know you have a great figure, right?

Tang: Haha, I definitely know my own body. I even check myself in the mirror while showering!

F: I heard your grandfather was into jazz music.

Tang: Yes, he's the only one in our family related to this field.

F: Intergenerational inheritance, when did you discover your talent in acting?

Tang: Hey, I'm not suitable for being an actress at all! The creative part of acting is okay. But socially, it's completely unsuitable. Entering the industry was coincidental; after graduating from junior high school, I was already prepared to work as a flight attendant at an airline company. Who would have thought that I would meet the principal of Wuhan Art School, who pulled me home, played a disc, made me dance, and then had me "sight-sing and ear-train." As you know, I grew up surrounded by music, so my pitch was good, and he decided right there to let me try out at their school.

F: Can you dance?

Tang: No, I'm a clumsy type.

F: Then what were you dancing at that time?

Tang: Forgot, just wildly twisting.

F: What was the old man thinking? What did you learn at school?

Tang: Liberation of nature. I used to be introverted, didn't like speaking up, and didn't like showing off. But through these four years at the art school, my nature was fully liberated, almost over-liberated.

F: How was it liberated?

Tang: The first lesson was to discard shame. Once, the teacher asked me to play a fool, and there was a particularly pretty girl in our class who was embarrassed to do it, so the teacher stopped us all. In a large dance classroom, she was made to stand on a stool alone and slap herself.

F: Your teacher envied that little Lolita face.

Tang: Later, the teacher made her twist her mouth, squint, drool, and strum strings... walk three circles around the classroom. Tears streamed down the little girl's face, it was terrible. But later, I understood the teacher's intention. When I entered the Central Academy of Drama, the first course was still this. As an actor, this is a required course.

F: That's correct, according to Stanislavski's theory...

Tang: Could you stop that?

F: We're all cultured people, let's discuss. I heard you were a trainee student of the China Children's Art Theatre at the Central Academy of Drama?

Tang: We were the second batch; the first was Cai Guoqing's group. But after graduation, everything was withdrawn. If neither tuition exemption nor contract signing was provided, who could bear playing trees and houses all day long? Someone said, go for it, it can solve Beijing household registration issues, but I'm like this person - I don't plan to pay anything for this thing, so I don't take it seriously. My classmates who went, are still contributing to our country's children's cause now.
