Amazon launches virtual currency "Coins" to attract more developers and boost consumption.

by geekzhang on 2013-05-14 08:55:16

Today, Amazon officially launched its own virtual currency, Coins, in the US market. The exchange rate between Coins and the US dollar is 100 to 1. This is the first time the e-commerce giant has ventured into this type of business. Amazon stated that users purchasing large amounts will receive a 10% discount, and the revenue-sharing ratio with developers will be 3 to 7.From QQ Coins to Facebook Credits and various points systems in games, using virtual currencies to retain users and stimulate consumption is a common tactic among companies. TechCrunch believes that Amazon's issuance of Coins has at least two purposes: to attract more developers to its platform and to boost consumption.On the launch day, Amazon generously gifted each Kindle Fire user with 500 Coins, equivalent to $5. According to official statements, this move cost tens of millions of dollars. This is indeed a substantial amount of money, but Amazon has ample reasons for doing so. First, after exchanging for virtual currency, users often lose focus on the amount they are spending (similar to chips in casinos); secondly, due to the exclusivity typically associated with virtual currencies, users and developers possessing a certain currency are more easily tied to a specific game or platform; finally, the exchange rate between virtual and real currencies can disrupt normal consumer perceptions, as in the case of Coins, where 100 Coins sounds more powerful than $1.Currently, Amazon allows users to use Coins to purchase new apps and games, as well as for in-app purchases. In the future, Coins will definitely be usable on other Amazon services. According to Mike George, Vice President of Amazon's Apps & Games department, we will add more ways to earn and spend Coins across other services and content. Personally, I believe that Amazon's virtual currency has more potential and appeal compared to those issued by its competitors. It would be very interesting if Amazon were to accept Coins on its online shopping platform, especially since Mike George mentioned that Coins can be earned.VIA: techcrunch.com
