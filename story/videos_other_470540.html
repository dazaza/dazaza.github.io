Growth of refrigerator market slows down, high-end products see poor sales

by tuoqing1188 on 2011-08-04 10:13:34

Summer should be the peak season for refrigerator sales, but yesterday, data from AVC Retail Monitoring in Shenyang showed that in June this year, the sales proportion of small and medium capacity products increased while the share of high-end products declined.

High-end product sales are not performing well

In June this year, the structure of popular refrigerator products is also changing. Last year, multi-door refrigerators were in high demand, but in June this year, the sales proportion of small and medium capacity products has risen, and the share of high-end products has fallen back. Except for the retail average price of side-by-side products which rose by 1.5% month-on-month, the retail average prices of three-door and multi-door refrigerators both fell to varying degrees. In particular, the retail average price of three-door refrigerator products this month was 3123 yuan, down 1.9% month-on-month.

From a capacity perspective, the retail volume share of small and medium capacity products in the refrigerator market has increased this month. Products below 200 liters rose by 4.7 percentage points month-on-month, with products below 160 liters showing the most significant increase in retail volume, rising by 3.1 percentage points month-on-month.

Geng Minghua, white goods analyst at AVC, said that competition among domestic home refrigerator brands has intensified this year. With the growth rate of home refrigerators slowing down, whether the market is entering an era of "predatory" competition needs to be seen from the performance of the second half of the year.

Market growth slows down

AVC retail monitoring data shows that in June, the year-on-year growth rate of refrigerator products is still lingering at a low level, with market retail volume increasing by 0.6% year-on-year; retail value increased by 1.9% year-on-year, both maintaining low levels.

Geng Minghua stated that negative macro-environmental factors and weak consumer demand have cast a shadow over the entire refrigerator industry. "From the perspective of supply and demand, affected by rising raw material costs upstream and increased corporate cost pressures, companies have reduced their market input compared to last year. At the same time, consumer demand is also trending towards saturation. The first and second-tier markets have already stabilized. Under the rural appliance program, refrigerators have always been the most popular product in rural areas, but now the demand in the rural market stimulated by the rural appliance policy has weakened, making it unlikely to achieve high growth," Geng Minghua indicated.

The director of the white goods market at AVC said, "The refrigerator market is now returning to normal. There are no particularly significant external favorable factors in the second half of the year, so there will be a noticeable decline from the high growth rates of the past two years." (Lin Qiling)
