Yanbu strategy: enhancing the quality of underwear

by renzhen5555i on 2012-02-08 17:30:36

At the end of the 1970s, an era of reviving industries, as the spring breeze of reform and opening-up blew, when Hong Kong's "King of Bras" Luo Jielun decided to move production to the mainland, he chose Yanbu as this treasure land and jointly built the "Daleis内衣Factory" with the Yanbu Urban Area Industry Company, initiating the relationship between Yanbu and fashionable lingerie. Because of the large demand for labor force, the Daleis underwear factory employed a large number of local youth. After professional training, they were assigned to various production lines, becoming "screws" in the dozens of procedures for manufacturing underwear. Seeing truckloads of underwear being pulled away from here, exchanged for piles of banknotes, the clever people of Yanbu began to plan their own path to wealth. Thus, some people who had mastered certain skills left Daleis to start their own underwear production, relying on small orders split from the big factory to survive. Gradually, more and more underwear workshops appeared, and the underwear produced here was worn by consumers all over. It wasn't until 1995, when He Baiqiang founded Jialishi underwear, that Yanbu Taobao underwear truly had its own brand. In the last decade of the last century, Yanbu saw the emergence of brands such as Jialishi, Diana, Ailinon, Meisi, and Yizhini. "Despite this, many people still do not know what kind of place Yanbu is, because these so-called brands are just names of trademarks, and the added value of the brands themselves is not high." Chen Weiqiang frankly admitted. The already scaled Yanbu underwear industry gained the attention of the government. In 2000, under the strong promotion of the then Yanbu Town Government, the Yanbu Underwear Association was established. This ended the situation where Yanbu underwear operated independently, and the model of group development had milestone significance for the regional brand to become famous nationwide. Also in 2000, in the era where "everyone turned red talking about underwear," Nanhai Yanbu surprisingly held the first China Underwear Model Competition, bringing the name of Yanbu underwear into every household. Facing crises, the brand route became the collective choice of Yanbu underwear. By 2002, among the 15 leading brands in China's underwear consumption, Yanbu took 7 of them, and Yanbu was rated as the "China Underwear Famous Town" by the China Textile Industry Association that year. In the following years, Yanbu underwear almost held large-scale events every year, and enterprises' understanding of brands deepened increasingly. After years of "branching out," by around 2007, Yanbu underwear enterprises had developed to nearly 300, with over 30,000 employees, producing more than 30 million sets of underwear annually. However, at the same time, the business environment of Yanbu and even the entire Pearl River Delta quietly changed. Rising land rents, increasing labor costs, rising raw material prices... multiple factors pushed up the costs of underwear enterprises higher and higher. After the financial crisis erupted, the international market sharply shrank, foreign customers drastically cut prices, and the shrinking profit space became the hidden concern lurking under the halo of this "China Underwear Famous Town." "After the crisis erupted, Yanbu underwear exports decreased by about 31%, and prices could not go up," according to Chen Weiqiang's introduction. Even in 2009 when the crisis was not so severe, the export price of the same style of underwear dropped by 10%~15% compared to before the crisis, resulting in some orders being directly abandoned. With prices unable to rise, it became difficult to increase workers' wages, causing another concern faced by Yanbu underwear enterprises: recruitment becoming increasingly difficult. Meanwhile, the rise of underwear clusters in Shantou, Shenzhen, Shanghai, etc., also posed multiple challenges to Yanbu underwear.
