Safety clothing is one of the types that you’ll need to have for any security guards that you hire. You can choose from official or everyday t-shirts, with or without consistent pants. Hospitality apparel for your concierges is also critical and will end up being included in the lodge clothing budget. Naturally, your housekeepers may each need a housekeeping uniform as well.

Safety Uniforms: Many resorts of a reasonable size will have some security detail to manage rowdy guests and for any other potential problems that may arise. Based on the safety needs of the location, you might want several guards patrolling the parking lot to keep the cars protected, as well as various parts of the hotel. In many cases, you will want your security staff to be quickly identifiable, so using work uniforms or men's suits that make them stand out as the ones to go to if there’s an issue is helpful. Security apparel comes in a variety of styles, from police-style uniforms with a logo patch, to bright safety jackets which are perfect for parking lot security. For more formal styles, dark uniform jeans and a blazer provide a wonderful, formal look suited for any high-end hotel. If the guards wear badges, ensure that you select function uniforms fashioned with a place for the badge, and for the blazer, avoid simple access via an internal pants pocket.

Other Hotel Uniforms: There are many additional food service uniforms that can be used in a hotel. Among the most common ones we've seen among resort uniforms are those used in housekeeping. The housekeeping uniform identifies the maids and lets visitors know who to approach when they need extra towels, etc. It also allows quick recognition whenever a housekeeper knocks on the door of a room. As with safety outfits, there’s a lot of options for your housekeeping uniform. If comfort is the top priority, consider men's polo shirts with the resort emblem embroidered on them, along with comfortable pants or jeans. Some hotels will prefer to be more formal with black dresses and shirts with a half apron, but there are plenty of other available choices too. Don't forget your front desk staff. The men and women who attend to guests should also adhere to a hotel standard. Usually, this will be a bit more formal since they will be there to impress your attendees. Standard pants for men, with dresses or slacks for women are great, and a dress shirt or blouse with the resort logo custom embroidered on the chest or collar can also add a special effect. A professional uniform, along with a genial and useful attitude, will help make a great impact, and you’ll find that guests may be more prepared to return and recommend your hotel to their friends and business associates.

If you want the hotel to be considered authentic, you should invest in work clothing for the various positions. Safety uniforms, consistent trousers, food service attire, and other forms of hotel apparel should be considered. To provide something extra, have the hotel’s logo stitched onto the cleaning uniform and the shirts used by the concierges. It is these small details that truly say something about the professionalism of the company, and you’ll benefit greatly from them. Wow your guests and give your employees a sense of pride by having the right work uniforms.
