Are you ready for the preparations before hospitalization?

by woai2dd on 2012-02-22 15:22:19

Check if the supplies required by the institute are fully prepared. Regardless of which hospital you go to, the necessary supplies are the same. However, some of the Shanghai surrogacy [http://www.daiyunbaobei.com] supplies are prepared by the hospital, while others need to be prepared by yourself. Different hospitals have different requirements. You need to recheck the items that must be brought with you.

These prepared supplies should be organized and packed into a travel bag, placed in a location where anyone can find them.

(1) Mark the route to the hospital. Write down the hospital's address, telephone number, available public transportation, time required, and route correctly on a piece of paper and place it in a conspicuous place like next to the phone. If possible, drawing a route map would be even more convenient.

If you cannot take a taxi or public bus, include the estimated time and route for driving yourself.

(2) Contact methods. Ensure that you can always contact your husband. Before and after delivery, your husband should avoid long business trips or vacations. To facilitate communication, try to inform your family of your whereabouts.

If unable to contact your husband, you may arrange for someone familiar to escort you. In case of emergency, entrust your mother’s family, neighbors, or friends for help. The contact methods with these people should be arranged in advance for easy connection when needed.

Prepare a list detailing the positions, telephone numbers, and addresses of the contacts and place it next to the phone.

Sometimes, after being hospitalized, you may still need to make calls. It is best to record the contact information in a small notebook and carry it with you at all times.

(3) Vehicle arrangements. Consider nearby taxi companies or private car services. Even if you own a car, there could be situations where your husband is not home or the car doesn't start immediately. In general,

time is crucial.

Check the contact methods with the hospital, including the hospital's night and holiday phone numbers, and the entrance for emergency patients. Also, understand the reception times outside regular outpatient hours, as well as the locations of the delivery room, labor room, and other relevant facilities.

(4) Hospitalization fees. After deciding to be hospitalized, procedures need to be completed. Most hospitals require filling out a hospitalization guarantee form and paying a deposit. When settling the bill upon discharge, the receipt for the deposit will be needed, so it should be kept properly.

(5) Delivery costs. These may vary depending on the hospital, region, and delivery situation. The cost for a single-person ward differs from that of a multi-person ward.
