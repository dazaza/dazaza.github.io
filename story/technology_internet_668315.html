Metal wrapping and anti-corrosion treatment

Geotextile membrane is formed by two layers of needle-punched long filament geotextile with a layer of high-density polyethylene (HDPE) film in the middle, creating a blanket-like composite fabric. Its main application areas include: artificial lakes, artificial streams or river dam slopes, anti-seepage projects for artificial fish ponds, etc.

Advantages:

Environmental product

The material itself is non-toxic, acid and alkali resistant, and can last up to 50 years without deterioration.

Reasonable process

Adopting flexible connections, horizontal displacement at connection points decomposes force effectively, ensuring the integrity of the leak-proof system. It can be arbitrarily cut, trimmed, and spliced.

No prior treatment required

No need for any preliminary treatment on the construction base surface; it can be directly installed on compacted soil layers (removing sharp stones, large rocks, tree roots, and other debris).

Simple construction

Low technical requirements for construction, fast construction speed.

High comprehensive benefits

From price, material performance, construction methods, environmental impact, technical requirements, and time-saving considerations, it performs better than other anti-seepage materials.

I. Process Flow

● Excavation of foundation ● Construction of lake bottom mortar protective layer (H20-30mm)

● Slope trimming, compaction and leveling ● Adhesion of composite geotextile membrane to vertical retaining walls

● Base leveling, cleaning, and process handover ● Sealing and waterproofing of underwater pipe openings

● Laying and welding of composite geotextile membranes ● Process handover

● Welding and repair quality inspection ● Final acceptance

II. Project Overview

1. Maximum design water level: about 0.6m.

2. Waterproofing construction total area approximately 800㎡.

3. Retaining wall constructed with lightweight aggregate bricks.

4. Underwater collapse pit set up.

III. Main Waterproofing Materials

1. Composite geotextile membrane rolls, specifications: 50*4m; 150g/㎡/0.5mm/150 g/㎡.

2. GB rubber waterstop strip.

IV. Foundation Conditions

1. After excavation and shaping of the foundation, the waterproof board requires slope trimming, leveling, and compaction or tamping treatment. Replacement and backfilling of disturbed soil should be done in layers with water sprinkling and compaction or tamping, each layer thickness ≯400mm.

2. Remove substances on the foundation surface that have potential puncture hazards, such as bricks, stones, tiles, glass, metal shavings, branches, plant roots, etc.

Clear obstacles within the site, providing necessary laying conditions.

3. Installation and reservation of surrounding retaining walls, pump pits, bridge foundations, and underwater pipe openings must be completed before the construction of the composite geotextile membrane.

V. Welding and Laying Technology

1. Welding Technology

① Weld seam overlap width: 80~100mm; natural wrinkles on flat and vertical surfaces respectively: 5%~8%; reserved expansion allowance: 3%~5%; edge material remaining amount: 2%~5%.

② Hot-melt welding working temperature 280~300℃; travel speed 2~3m/min; welding form double weld seams.

③ Repair method for damaged areas, cutting out materials of the same specification for hot-melt bonding and sealing with polyethylene glue.

④ Connection without imitation cloth at the weld seam uses mechanical stitching.

⑤ Sealing and waterproofing of underwater pipe openings uses GB rubber waterstop strips sealed with metal wrapping and corrosion protection treatment.

2. Laying Technology

① The direction of laying and unfolding of the composite geotextile membrane, from E to W or from W to E, including both sides of the retaining wall height once per length of each roll laid.

② Laying sequence, advancing from the channel axis to S and N sides.

③ Construction procedures, first make the mortar protective layer (20-30mm) for the lake bottom composite geotextile membrane, then adhere it to the vertical retaining wall.

④ Adhesion technology between composite geotextile membrane and retaining wall. Apply adhesive to the adhesion surfaces of the composite geotextile membrane and retaining wall, dry static placement time about 5 minutes, then proceed with adhesion, pressing, and flattening. The adhesive is made by mixing asphalt emulsion paint, cement, and water. After adhesion, anchor with nails above the waterline and perform waterproofing and corrosion protection treatment.

VI. Quality Acceptance

1. Material and construction of composite geotextile membrane follow national standards issued by the National Quality and Technical Supervision Bureau for polyethylene geotextile membranes and composite geotextile membranes.

2. Construction quality acceptance includes self-inspection by the construction unit and random checks by the contracting unit.

3. Welding quality inspection adopts the "air pressure method", visual inspection for repairs, air pressure detection index is 0.06Mpa.

4. The quality acceptance process includes procedural handovers and final acceptance.

5. Normal seepage rate for artificial lakes: 15mm/24h (including seasonal evaporation loss).

VII. Construction Progress

1. Daily efficiency, one team daily construction area is 2000~2500㎡.

2. Planned construction period is 3 days for composite geotextile membrane.

VIII. Suggestions

1. Strengthen the protection and management of finished composite geotextile membranes, enhance the waterproof awareness of relevant construction personnel, which is crucial for ensuring project quality and timely normal operation.

2. It is suggested that the contractor notify and assign dedicated personnel to supervise, and if damage to the composite geotextile membrane is found, promptly inform the waterproof construction personnel for repair.

3. Composite geotextile membranes must avoid oil contamination, otherwise it will reduce the bonding strength. Storage and protection see "Introduction to Composite Geomembrane Construction Methods".

4. This project should pay attention to the following aspects:

① After the composite geotextile membrane is welded into a finished product, a mortar protective layer must first be constructed at the bottom of the lake.

② For bridge foundation waterproofing, use the steel bar through membrane welding method, and use a mortar protective layer to prevent quality hazards caused by electric welding.

③ Prevent large-scale air bubbles when adhering the composite geotextile membrane to the wall.
