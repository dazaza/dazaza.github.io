Apple Unveils 12.9-Inch iPad: No Surprise At All

by anonymous on 2013-11-22 11:59:44

Recently, multiple media outlets have reported on the news of a larger-sized iPad. According to reports from South Korean media ETNews and Japanese tech media Macotakara, this tablet, codenamed iPad maxi, features a 12.9-inch screen with an impressive resolution of 2712×2048.

I. Will Apple launch a larger-sized iPad?

Two years ago, many people firmly believed that Apple would not release a smaller-sized iPad. However, at Apple's event in October 2012, it confirmed the previous tech media reports about the iPad mini. Despite pessimistic voices predicting poor sales after its initial release, the iPad mini’s explosive sales figures clearly demonstrated consumer demand for a smaller-sized iPad.

So what about a larger-sized iPad?

Let us shift our focus back to Apple's event in October this year. Apple named its fifth-generation 9.7-inch iPad as iPad Air, which might remind you of Apple's ultra-thin laptop, the MacBook Air. Based on Apple's naming conventions for laptops, this larger-sized iPad is highly likely to be called iPad Pro, featuring even more powerful performance than the iPad Air.

Now let’s look at the development trends in the notebook market — an increasing number of touchscreen Windows laptops are emerging. In the pre-Windows 8 era, the war between OS X and Windows had been ongoing for many years.

After the release of Windows 8, its excellent support for touchscreens prompted various PC manufacturers to introduce more touchscreen laptops — as thin as the MacBook Air, while supporting both touchscreen and keyboard input.

In contrast, Apple's product lineup sees the MacBook Air without touchscreen support, and the iPad Air's screen size is too small compared to touchscreen Win8 laptops. For Apple, there are two possible solutions: one is to launch a MacBook Air with a touchscreen, and the other is to release a larger-sized iPad. Among these two options, the latter is much more likely to be Apple's choice — iOS is more suited for touchscreens compared to OS X.

II. Is the technology mature enough?

For a larger-sized iPad, the first consideration is the power consumption of the display.

According to the media leaks of a 12.9-inch screen with a resolution of 2712×2048, the PPI reaches 264. Without considering power consumption, traditional a-Si technology can also produce displays of this PPI level and size — for example, the 15-inch MacBook Pro with Retina Display uses this type of TFT technology. However, since the iPad is a handheld device, if it were equipped with a power-hungry a-Si display, it would inevitably fall short in terms of battery life.

Fortunately, Sharp's IGZO technology can solve the power consumption problem of the display. As a representative of Oxide TFT technology, IGZO can be used to produce medium and large panels with high PPI and has been widely applied in digital products this year. Japanese manufacturers have already launched 14-inch notebook products with resolutions as high as 3200×1800, boasting a battery life of up to 11 hours, all thanks to the low power consumption of IGZO displays.

Another example is the significantly "lighter" iPad Air, which still offers slightly better battery life than its predecessor, again due to the contribution of IGZO. The maturity of IGZO technology in commercial applications makes a larger-sized yet slim iPad no longer a myth.

III. A larger-sized iPad is very likely to be a product aimed at the professional market

There are precedents for large-sized ultra-high-resolution tablets; Panasonic launched a 20-inch 4K resolution tablet this year — expensive and targeted at professional users.

Looking at Apple's notebook product line — compared to the MacBook Air, the MacBook Pro is larger in size, more powerful in performance, and also more expensive. In terms of positioning, the MacBook Pro is aimed at professionals, while the MacBook Air focuses on portability and entertainment.

Therefore, for this larger-sized tablet, which is likely to be named iPad Pro, we can boldly make a guess: it will feature stronger performance, a higher price, and will be aimed at the professional market.
