**How are Gold and Silver T+D transactions conducted?**

Historically, trading took place in the trading hall through verbal bids by traders. Currently, most T+D transactions are completed electronically. When investors trade silver T+D, they input buy or sell orders via the T+D computer system, which are then matched and executed by the exchange's matching system. When buying or selling a T+D contract, both parties must pay a small amount of money to the settlement house as a performance guarantee, known as a margin. The first purchase of a contract is called establishing a long position, while the first sale of a contract is called establishing a short position. Afterward, the contracts held must be settled daily.

After establishing buy or sell positions (known as opening a position) in silver T+D, one does not need to hold the position until expiration. At any time before the T+D contract expires, an opposite transaction can be made to offset the original position, which is called closing a position. For example, if you sell 10 lots of T+D contracts on the first day and buy back 10 lots on the second day, the first transaction would be opening a short position of 10 lots of T+D, and the second transaction would be closing a short position of 10 lots of T+D. If you buy another 20 lots of T+D contracts on the second day, it becomes opening a long position of 20 lots of T+D. Then, if you sell 10 lots, it is called closing a long position of 10 lots of T+D, leaving 10 lots of T+D long positions. At the end of the trading day, any contracts that have not been closed out are called open positions. In this example, after the first day's trading, the open position is a short position of 10 lots of T+D, and after the second day's trading, the open position is a long position of 10 lots of T+D.

Compared to stocks, silver T+D has several very distinct features, which are particularly important for stock investors:

(1) **Time**: T+D trades for 10 hours per day, while stocks trade for only 4 hours per day.

(2) **Margin Trading**: T+D contracts are traded on margin and require daily settlement. For silver T+D, generally only about 10% of the contract value needs to be paid to trade one contract. This increases profit potential but also brings risk, requiring daily settlement of gains and losses. With stocks, profits and losses remain unsettled until the stock is sold. However, with T+D, after each trade, daily settlement is done based on the settlement price. Profits can be withdrawn, but losses must be replenished (i.e., additional margin added) before the next trading day opens. Additionally, since T+D is margin trading, losses could even exceed your initial investment, unlike in stock trading.

(3) **Short Selling**: T+D allows easy short selling, where you sell first and buy back later when prices drop. Stock short selling (margin trading) is possible but more complex. Of course, if the price rises instead of falling after a short sale, the investor faces potential losses.

(4) **Liquidity**: Studies show that the liquidity of the T+D market is significantly higher than that of the stock spot market.

(5) **Flexibility**: T+D can be applied for withdrawal at any point during the trading session.
