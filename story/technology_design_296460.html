II. About Colors

1. The colors of children's clothing should be bright and elegant. Preschool children already have a sense of color and requirements. If interesting animal patterns or other colorful decorative patterns can be added to children's clothing, it will spark the child's interest in wearing them and bring infinite joy to the child.

2. Babies have a primitive sensitivity and unique preference for colors. Therefore, when selecting clothing for babies, judgments should be made based on the child's physique and skin tone. If the skin tone is darker, high brightness and high purity clothing should be the first choice, as bright colors stand out and look spirited. If the skin tone is lighter, then the range of adaptable colors is wider. For instance, wearing pink, yellow, or red makes one appear lively and radiant. Even wearing gray or black makes one appear refined and elegant, giving a feeling of "beauty in all forms."

While paying attention to the adaptation of colors to the child's skin tone in Wendy House kids' clothing, attention should also be paid to the matching of the child's physique with the color of the clothing. If the child is on the heavier side, cold or dark-colored clothing should be selected, such as gray, black, or blue, as cold and dark colors have a contracting effect, compensating for the child's physical imperfections. If the child is on the thinner side, warm-colored clothing can be selected, such as green, beige, or coffee, as these colors expand outward, giving people a feeling of warmth.

Of course, there is no fixed format for color matching in Wendy House kids' clothing. Excessive formalization would appear rigid and lifeless, but too many changes could seem chaotic. The only guideline is that the color matching should be beautiful, pleasant to look at, and comfortable for everyone.
