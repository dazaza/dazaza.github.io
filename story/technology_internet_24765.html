Xiamen's first casualty after implementing real-name system online: all station staff resign and open a new site

by 127702 on 2007-07-14 11:27:21

Real-name system "in full bloom" while "Lilac" withers

A well-known university BBS, HIT's "Lilac," now requires ID registration for real-name posting, prompting the resignation of all station staff who have opened a new site.

At the beginning of this month, Xiamen considered legislating to implement a strict form of online real-name system —— real-name registration and front-end real-name posting. In fact, as early as June, this kind of real-name system that few people could imagine before has already been implemented on a highly renowned university BBS —— Harbin Institute of Technology's "Lilac." Unlike other university BBS's back-end real-name system, the current Lilac is front-end real-name, requiring users to bring their ID to register in person, with posts displaying the user's name, department, and class.

However, after the implementation of the real-name system on the Lilac website, personal posts display the real name, whereas school departments post under the department's name.

The BBS station staff resigned and started a new "Lilac" forum, calling the original Lilac a "fake site."

1. Current Situation: No new posts for two or three days on the forum

For some time, Alei hasn't visited the real-name version of the Lilac BBS. He used to be a station administrator. Since the implementation of the real-name system, there haven't been any new posts on the forum for two or three days. He already knew that this was no longer the original Lilac, and many people believed that Lilac had died.

On June 21, HIT administration issued a notice to further implement national regulations regarding online real-name systems, requiring the Lilac BBS to enforce complete real-name management, including both real-name registration and real-name article posting.

According to this notice, old users need to reactivate their accounts, while new users must present valid student IDs (or work IDs) and ID cards at the university network center to fill out personal information such as ID numbers, student numbers, department units, and phone numbers. After the network center verifies the authenticity of the information, the account is jointly activated by the network center and the user before it becomes effective and can be used for posting on the BBS.

"The Lilac inside the campus is now in a semi-dead state," said Gaya, a ten-year user of the Lilac forum. Fewer people visit it now, and even fewer post when they do.

2. Inside Story: False messages led to forum closure

In the eyes of Lilac netizens, several recent incidents on the HIT Lilac BBS drew attention from the university administration.

A student posted an article claiming they had been robbed, and the HIT security personnel traced the student through the IP address, only to find out that the robbery was fabricated. Another student posted on the graduate school board claiming that their supervisor was handling 40 graduate students. Similarly, the administration traced the student through the IP, but the student denied making the claim, and indeed, the supervisor was not handling 40 graduate students.

On the morning of April 30, when netizens logged on, they found that all Lilac sections had zero articles, and each section couldn't accept new posts. Subsequently, the site was closed, and users couldn't connect to Lilac.

On June 21, the official HIT website "Today's HIT" published the notice titled "Notice on Implementing Real-Name Posting on the Lilac BBS."

As early as June 20, Zhou Nan, the station master, and other station staff resigned from their positions at the Lilac station.

After resigning from their Lilac station duties, they began creating the Lilac Community. Old users of the original Lilac BBS flocked to the new Lilac, referring to the campus-based Lilac as the "fake site."

3. Voices: "The least popular suggestion became reality"

Regarding the Lilac BBS incident, the most authoritative insider story comes from the widely circulated article "The Beginning and End of the Lilac BBS Shutdown" written by Gaya, a teacher at HIT and a long-time Lilac netizen. He told reporters that to save the potentially shut-down Lilac, he proposed the front-end real-name system suggestion, never expecting the administration to actually implement it.

"Unfortunately, the suggestion I made turned out to be the infamous 'front-end real-name system' or 'posting real-name system' that had already spread and become notorious... I didn't expect that my least popular suggestion during the May Day holiday would be turned into reality by the school. I still don't know whether the people present reported my suggestion to the administration or if someone from the school side independently came up with the same idea... Many people discussed various solutions, but those solutions were difficult and lacked operability, so I suggested the front-end real-name system."

Gaya admitted that his suggestion at the time lacked thorough consideration. He believed that the school had already decided to implement the real-name system.
