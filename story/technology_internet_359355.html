<p> At the beginning of this year, the Information Times reported on "Several millionaires each spending 20,888 yuan to attend a matchmaking party," which caused widespread controversy in society. Recently, a new round of "matrimonial parties" has once again begun the preliminary selection process, attracting over 1,500 young women from Guangzhou and surrounding cities to sign up, more than one-tenth of whom are female college students.</p><br />

<p> Men's "entry ticket" costs 36,800 yuan</p><br />

<p> According to the person in charge of a certain matrimonial dating website organizing the event, the basic condition for male registrants is to "prove that they are single millionaires with assets exceeding ten million yuan," and the price of an "entry ticket" has risen to 36,800 yuan. Women, on the other hand, need not pay a penny, but there are strict restrictions on appearance, figure, and educational background. Ultimately, only the top 40 beauties who stand out will have the chance to meet the male participants at a high society yacht gathering.</p><br />

<p> Yesterday at noon, the "preliminary selection" activity was held in a high-end office building on Dongfeng Middle Road. Starting from 1 PM, the girls who had already registered arrived one after another to fill out forms, take photos, and show their ID cards and original academic credentials to the staff for review. The entire "preliminary selection" site remained relatively calm. By browsing through the preliminary selection data sheets, it was seen that most of the registrants had education levels above bachelor's degrees, with monthly salaries around 5,000 yuan. Furthermore, many graduated from prestigious universities such as Sun Yat-sen University, Jinan University, and South China Normal University, and there were no shortage of those with good looks and figures. According to statistics from the organizers, more than one-tenth of the registrants were female college students.</p><br />

<p> In the form submission and photography phase, several staff members began to "visually assess" the images of the beauties. Only a few exceptionally attractive girls were taken by the staff into a small meeting room on the side to engage in face-to-face conversations with professional matrimonial consultants. Most of the girls, however, were simply informed to "wait for notice" after submitting their forms and taking photos, then left the venue.</p><br />

<p> Female: Partner must have 5 million yuan</p><br />

<p> Little Yu, a 21-year-old female university student, was one of the girls yesterday "fortunate enough" to enter the interview phase. During the conversation, she informed the matrimonial consultant that she came from a well-known university in Guangzhou, and her friendship goal was very clear: a successful man over 35 years old, with assets exceeding 5 million yuan, owning both a house and car. Whether he was divorced or not didn't matter, as long as he had no children.</p><br />

<p> Matrimonial consultant Zhou Xia told reporters that although Little Yu's various conditions were quite excellent, she did not plan to arrange for Little Yu to participate in the next round of matchmaking parties, citing the reason that Little Yu's "life experience was insufficient, her understanding of marriage was shallow, and she did not fully meet the requirements of the male participants."</p><br />

<p> Doubt · Response</p><br />

<p> 1 How to prove they are millionaires?</p><br />

<p> "Do they really have 10 million yuan if their information states so?" According to Cheng Yongsheng, the director of the South China Regional Activities Department of the website, the organizers will conduct an objective review of the conditions of the male registrants. Men must provide originals of documents such as property deeds, stock options certificates, company registration capital certificates, and driver's licenses. The organizers claim that the fixed assets of these ten successfully "passed" millionaires range from 50 million yuan to over 100 million yuan. From the copies of the documents provided by these millionaires, there were stock option certificates worth nearly 15 million yuan, villa values close to 30 million yuan, Porsche Cayenne cars worth over a million, and even personal companies with registered capital close to 80 million yuan.</p><br />

<p> 2 How to prove the millionaires aren't just looking for fun?</p><br />

<p> "Is the millionaire matchmaking about seeking marriage or just looking for fun?" Regarding this doubt, Cheng Yongsheng told reporters that after passing the asset review, the millionaires would also undergo psychological reviews regarding honesty and matrimonial motivation. Professional psychological consultants will judge through telephone communication and face-to-face interviews whether the registered millionaires have strong, sincere, and positive desires for marriage and love, whether their motives and attitudes towards receiving matrimonial headhunting services are correct, and whether there are any major psychological obstacles or mental illnesses, etc. Additionally, at the time of registration, the organizers had already signed relevant agreements with the millionaires. If any complaints are received from female members, the organizers have the right to terminate the service.</p><br />

<p> 3 Are there any successful examples of millionaire matchmaking?</p><br />

<p> At the beginning of this year, a similar millionaire matrimonial preliminary selection activity was also held in Guangzhou. Has anyone been successfully paired? Or is it just hype from the organizers? Cheng Yongsheng stated that at the beginning of this year, a total of 37 millionaires participated in the event, along with nearly a hundred young girls. According to the latest feedback information, currently only one pair has officially registered their marriage, ten pairs are still in a romantic relationship, while the remaining 26 millionaires have not found suitable partners. Among them, a university professor and a 31-year-old young millionaire will continue to participate in this matchmaking event. (Reported by Wu Xia, intern Wang Shuaishuai)</p><br />
