In recent years, provinces and cities across the country, based on the Forest Law of the People's Republic of China and relevant local regulations, have begun to comprehensively rectify the charcoal market and ban the use of wood as raw material in traditional charcoal kilns. This not only protects forest resources but also prevents soil erosion, adjusts ecological balance, and benefits future generations. Wuhan Chuan Island Heavy Industry Machinery Manufacturing Co., Ltd. is committed to this cause.

However, charcoal is still an indispensable raw material and additive in industries such as industry, agriculture, and metallurgy, and it is also an essential fuel for daily life. Therefore, while prohibiting the cutting of trees for charcoal burning, the state advocates the use of mechanical charcoal to replace natural charcoal. In this way, it not only alleviates the crisis caused by the shortage of charcoal due to the prohibition of logging for charcoal burning but also provides a livelihood solution for those who "live off charcoal production and sales." This lays a solid foundation for implementing national and local policies and laws protecting forest resources. It can be said that the red light is on for logging and charcoal burning, and the green light is on for using waste to burn charcoal.

The production of mechanical charcoal has received support from relevant national departments for the aforementioned reasons. More importantly, mechanical charcoal does not contain harmful substances such as sulfur, produces no smoke or odor during combustion, has high heat output, and burns for a long time, making it superior to original wood charcoal. Moreover, our charcoal production equipment uses an environmental protection type charcoal machine. During the process of burning charcoal, the exhaust gas passes through a condenser to separate out tar, thereby meeting emission requirements.

In summary, the emergence of Chuan Island Heavy Industry Charcoal Machine represents a destructive impact on primitive charcoal. The final result will be that the former has infinite vitality, while the latter will gradually exit the historical stage of the charcoal industry.
