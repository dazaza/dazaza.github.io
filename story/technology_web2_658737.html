Talent is equivalent to wealth.

by bhhgsyfk4143 on 2012-02-04 13:24:50

If managers are good at harnessing talent, their leadership effectiveness will be doubled. Training lecturer Tan Xiaofang believes that a manager's abilities don't have to surpass others in every aspect, but they must possess extraordinary talent management skills.

Managers should have the virtue of recommending talented individuals. A person unsuited for a position is an unstable factor. Even if they conform without showing any frustration about being underappreciated, people will consciously or unconsciously compare the talent with the manager, reducing the manager's authority.

One: Talent is for the use of enterprises.

Every employee is a treasure trove. Only when managers first realize employees are treasures can they treat them from the perspective of development. People usually don't develop garbage heaps. What methods can be used to develop this "human" treasure? Teacher Tan Xiaofang indicates that one can manage people through systems or suppress them with authority, but each manager must also consider how to turn human resources into talents and talents into assets.

Nine: Managers must have the courage to protect talent. Although there were no righteous wars in the Three Kingdoms era, from a fair perspective, the competition among the three kingdoms was a contest of strength, ultimately leading to a balance of power based on strength and talent. Looking at our business operations today, the average lifespan of small and medium-sized enterprises is only 2.9 years. The key reason for the slow development and difficulty in scaling up of private enterprises lies in the lack of talent. Teacher Tan Xiaofang believes that in contemporary Chinese society, whether in government or enterprises, there exist three tendencies in talent value orientations:

They all have unique abilities, not only contributing to the organizations they serve but also improving their own lives while earning income. They work for companies by choice and have the right to weigh their own talents against what they consider a reasonable income to decide their fate.

Eight: Managers must have the ability to harness talent.

Key talents are our core and representatives, our soul and backbone. Regardless of company size or ownership structure, having such core employees is essential. In market economy conditions, competition between enterprises ultimately boils down to competition for key talents. The quantity and quality of core talents determine a company's core competitiveness and its survival and development.

Two: There must be a strong desire for talent.

Ten: Talent is wealth.

In the past, Chinese companies had a department called "Personnel Department," which is now generally renamed as "Human Resources Department." But does changing the name change its meaning, or is it just following trends blindly? When it was called Personnel Department, the supervisor's position was Director, but after renaming it Human Resources Department, the supervisor's position became Manager. Is there any change?

Competition between enterprises ultimately comes down to talent competition. Talent is the first resource of enterprises, the most important resource and main driving force for technological progress and social economic development. Enterprises that attract and gather excellent talents gain the upper hand in competition and will remain invincible in fierce technological and economic competition. Teacher Tan Xiaofang believes that to attract, gather, and manage large numbers of outstanding talents to prosper our enterprises, our managers at all levels must have advanced talent management concepts.

Talent is a precious asset to enterprises, and managers at all levels must cherish talent and never envy or persecute it. Even if a manager themselves is an extraordinary talent, without several brilliant lieutenants and a large number of backbone members, it is difficult to achieve great success alone.

Using talent is not easy, and tolerating talent is even harder. Talents have strengths and weaknesses, and often the more prominent the strengths, the more pronounced the weaknesses. Arrogance due to talent is a common ailment. Great talents often disregard trivial matters, and exceptional talents may even have peculiar habits. Conflicts between talents are also common.

The courage to protect talent is both the "talent of talent" and the "virtue of talent" for managers. Managers must prevent both "beating down" and "overpraising" talents. No one is perfect, even talents make mistakes and may fail in their work, especially during the process of exploration and innovation.

Five: Managers must have the virtue of recommending talent.

Seven: Managers must have the discernment to identify talent.

Chinese private enterprises often view talent in this way. As long as someone works tirelessly for the enterprise, they are considered talent. In fact, this is also a stubborn thought from traditional feudal emperors and officials. People should sacrifice themselves and live for the happiness of others.

There is. The so-called "manager" refers to operation and management. The HR manager is responsible for managing the company's people.

Two: Talent is about having a good attitude and a spirit of dedication.

China has largely held this idea for a long time. Look at what Emperor Gaozu of Han, Liu Bang, once said when summarizing his successful experience in using people: "Planning strategies within the tent and winning battles thousands of miles away, I am not as good as Zhang Liang; governing the country, comforting the common people, providing provisions, and ensuring uninterrupted supply lines, I am not as good as Xiao He; commanding millions of troops, always winning in battles and always succeeding in attacks, I am not as good as Han Xin. These three are all outstanding individuals. I can use them, which is why I gained the empire!" The underlying message here is: talent is for my service.

Four: Managers must have the tolerance to accept talent.

To possess talent, one needs mechanisms to cultivate it. Top-tier companies like Haier, Lenovo, Huawei, and Mengniu produce many talents. Why them? Is it because of their systems? Every company has systems, yet why do other companies fail? Is it because of their leaders' exceptional talents? Leaders have many responsibilities; how much time can they spend cultivating talent, and how many talents can they nurture? Teacher Tan Xiaofang indicates that these outstanding leaders create an excellent corporate environment and foster a positive corporate atmosphere. This environment and atmosphere give birth to the company's talents.

Three: Cultivation and management of key talents.

One: There must be an appreciation for talent.

If managers lack discernment, talent may pass before their eyes and still be missed. Identifying talent requires looking at the essence. Observing words and actions, especially current actions, is the fundamental method to recognize the essence of talent. One must be adept at identifying different types of talent. Everyone has talent, merely differing in magnitude and direction.

Six: Establish mechanisms for talent cultivation.

Three: Everyone is talent.

Having a love for talent naturally leads to a thirst for it. From a group perspective, talent is rare. Being talent means having standout qualities, making it naturally scarce.
