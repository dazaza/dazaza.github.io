Bitter Cold Descending to Treat Hemorrhoids

by anonymous on 2012-03-07 13:15:39

1. What is the concept of "pungent-opening and bitter-descending"? According to the commentary in the "Great Dictionary of Traditional Chinese Medicine," "pungent-opening and bitter-descending" refers to "using pungent flavors to open up phlegm-dampness in the chest and using bitter flavors to treat damp-heat in the chest." It is represented by the formula Xiao Xian Xiong Tang. Any reasonable combination of pungent and bitter flavors constitutes the treatment method of pungent-opening and bitter-descending. In the *Treatise on Cold Damage Disorders* (*Shanghan Lun*), Fuzi Xie Xin Tang treats epigastric fullness caused by intermingled cold and heat, while Ban Xia Xie Xin Tang, Gan Cao Xie Xin Tang, and Sheng Jiang Xie Xin Tang treat deficient epigastric fullness due to intermingled cold and heat. Later formulas such as Xiang Lian Wan and Zuo Jin Wan also belong to the pungent-opening and bitter-descending treatment method. The pungent-opening and bitter-descending method can also be used for treating hemorrhoids.

2. Pathogenesis of hemorrhoids: Western medicine believes that hemorrhoids are caused by hypertrophy of the anal cushion and varicosity of veins under the anal mucosa. Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM), however, associates the occurrence of hemorrhoids with dysfunction of the internal organs, not just local changes in the anus. The saying "if there is something internally, it will manifest externally" means that diseases appearing in certain areas are related to specific meridians. For instance, nasal diseases are associated with the lung meridian because the nose is the orifice of the lungs. Therefore, treating rhinitis with methods that promote lung function and unblock nasal passages can yield good results. The anus is considered the orifice of the stomach. In *Ling Shu - Zhang Lun*, it states "the five orifices of the stomach are like the gateways of a neighborhood." The *Lei Jing* explains: "From the upper part of the stomach to the lower intestines, all belong to the stomach; hence they are called the gates of the neighborhood."

The throat and the anus both fall under the jurisdiction of stomach qi, making them one of the five orifices of the stomach. From these discussions, it is evident that the anus (also known as the魄door) is one of the five orifices of the stomach. The stomach, intestines, and anus are closely connected, mutually dependent in physiology, and mutually influential in pathology. The stomach's primary function is to receive and descend, acting as the sea of grains, while the spleen is responsible for transforming and transporting, ascending clear qi, and together they form an interdependent relationship. Hemorrhoids often form due to pre-existing damp-heat exacerbated by spicy and dry foods, leading to dysfunction in the spleen's ability to ascend clear qi, the stomach's ability to descend turbid qi, and the overall regulation of opening and closing. This disrupts the anus' function of excreting waste, causing diarrhea and constipation. Over time, this can lead to accumulation of dampness, fluids, phlegm, and stasis, eventually resulting in intermingled cold and heat, stagnation of qi and blood, blocked meridians, and the formation of hemorrhoids.

3. Treatment of hemorrhoids with the pungent-opening and bitter-descending method: This method originated from the *Shanghan Lun* and has continued to develop over time, particularly used by the Wenbing School during the Ming and Qing Dynasties for treating damp-heat syndromes. Dr. He Jianqing believed that this method should not be limited to its original scope but applied flexibly based on accurate differentiation, which can lead to significant therapeutic effects.

When the spleen's qi ascends properly, it remains healthy, and when the stomach's qi descends appropriately, harmony is maintained. Together, they regulate the middle burner's functions of ascending and descending. When the flow of qi becomes abnormal, the balance of blood and qi is disrupted, and intermingled cold and heat block the anus, making it difficult to resolve without using the pungent-opening and bitter-descending method combined with softening and dispersing hard masses. Pungent herbs such as dried ginger, pinellia, and tangerine peel are used, along with bitter herbs like scutellaria, coptis, and immature citrus fruit. Pungent-warm herbs can disperse qi, promote water metabolism, warm the center, and resolve dampness, while bitter-cold herbs can clear heat, dry dampness, purge fire, detoxify, and stop diarrhea. The combination of pungent-warm herbs facilitates bowel movements, relieves abdominal distension, reduces abdominal pressure, and balances yin and yang, cold and heat, ascending and descending, opening and releasing. This harmonizes the middle burner, regulates the flow of qi, and promotes smooth circulation without harming yin or hindering yang. By regulating the spleen and stomach's qi, the fundamental treatment of restoring normal organ functions is achieved. Pungent herbs can soften hardness and promote qi movement, ensuring smooth blood flow and resolving stasis, thereby improving anal blood circulation. Bitter-cold herbs clear heat and soften hardened hemorrhoidal nodules, addressing both the root cause and symptoms. If stasis-heat is more pronounced, herbs like polygonum cuspidatum, peach kernel, and dandelion can be added to enhance the clearing of stasis-heat. For long-standing, hard-to-reduce hemorrhoidal nodules, herbs like cicada slough, silkworm pupa, cat's claw grass, and whole centipede can assist in softening and dispersing hard masses. For excessive damp-phlegm, herbs like Zhejiang fritillary bulb, trichosanthes root, and arisaema tuber can be added to resolve dampness and phlegm.

From the perspective of modern medicine: pungent-warm herbs mainly adjust organ functions, while bitter-cold herbs mostly have antibacterial and antiseptic effects. The main chemical components of bitter herbs exhibit significant biological activity, with notable pharmacological effects. They can exert dual-directional regulatory effects on pathological hyperactivity or hypofunction of the body. Combining these two types of herbs for treating hemorrhoids can shorten the treatment course and improve clinical efficacy.
