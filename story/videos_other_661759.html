Users ultimately determine the value and potential of a website.

When starting a website, site owners often face the challenge of choosing what kind of site to build. Whether building a spam site or a content site, the audience that site owners need to face can be divided into two categories: search engines, which can be summarized as robots; and real people, i.e., tangible users. When facing these two groups, some site owners often take the wrong path, adopting methods that "flatter" search engines to quickly gain trust, such as extensively using pseudo-original content regardless of readability, or constructing large numbers of external links through paid services or software mass distribution. These methods can quickly gain the trust and favor of search engines but inevitably harm user interests. Site owners who choose this method convert "one-time" traffic into income without considering long-term development. Once penalized heavily by search engines, it will be a "disaster" for these sites.

For real users, only by truly improving the quality of the website's content and continuously satisfying user needs can users establish trust in the website, turning more users into loyal users, increasing revisit rates, and enhancing visits. Over time, these users can become a very substantial stable source for the website. As for search engines, optimizing the website structure based on this foundation, ensuring high-quality original content, while supplementing with reprinted content, can also make the website appealing to search engines, steadily improving technical indicators and maintaining sufficient stability. In this way, websites with stable customer sources gradually possess real commercial investment value, laying a solid foundation for future profitability. How to value users during website development

During the construction and development of a website, how to value users is a topic that every site owner should pay attention to and practice. Starting from the details and putting in enough effort is the first step in valuing users. First, in terms of website structure, it should be concise and easy to use, avoiding confusion due to complex content that makes it difficult for users to find what they need. Second, regarding the selection of website content, there should be some control. Not only should high-quality articles attract users, but at least the readability and usability of the articles must be guaranteed. This allows users to feel the website's dedication, generating a desire to stay, laying the first solid foundation for cultivating loyal users. Finally, in terms of interaction between the website and users, the website should continuously invite users to provide feedback and suggestions, adopting the best ones and implementing them effectively. By doing so, feedback providers and other users can feel the website's emphasis on users, establishing trust and goodwill that keeps users loyal to your website.
