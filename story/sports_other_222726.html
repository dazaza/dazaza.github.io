AsiaSat 5 launch successful, expanding our country's satellite communication capabilities

by weixing123 on 2009-08-14 15:20:26

AsiaSat 5 Sends Back First Signal

Asia Satellite Telecommunications Company Limited (AsiaSat) announced that at 3:47 AM Beijing time on August 12, 2009, its AsiaSat 5 satellite was launched into space by a Proton rocket from the Baikonur launch base in Kazakhstan. After 9 hours and 15 minutes of flight, the satellite successfully separated from the last stage of the carrier rocket.

Currently, the AsiaSat 5 is operating normally, and AsiaSat has received the first signal transmitted by AsiaSat 5. In the coming days, AsiaSat 5 will reach the geosynchronous orbit approximately 36,000 kilometers above the equator.

The newly launched AsiaSat 5 will replace the AsiaSat 2, which is currently operating at the 100.5 degrees East orbital position, to continue providing high-quality services to users. The AsiaSat 5 uses the Space Systems/Loral 1300 satellite platform, integrating advanced technologies that have been rigorously validated. It is equipped with 26 C-band and 14 Ku-band transponders, with a designed operational life span of 15 years. It will provide advanced satellite communication services such as television broadcasting, telecommunications, and broadband multimedia to the Asia-Pacific region.

CITIC Strengthens Satellite Communication Business

CITIC Group's communication business has not been well known to the public. However, AsiaSat, a subsidiary of CITIC Group, is China’s oldest commercial satellite company and currently the strongest in comprehensive strength in the Asia-Pacific region. CITIC Group is the founding shareholder and largest shareholder of AsiaSat, which has been established for 20 years.

AsiaSat is a regional satellite company in Asia. Currently, through its AsiaSat 2, AsiaSat 3S, and AsiaSat 4 satellites, it provides broadcasting and telecommunications services to two-thirds of the world's population. More than 300 digital TV and radio channels are relayed via AsiaSat, reaching nearly 96 million households in the Asia-Pacific region, with a total of 360 million viewers; it also provides public telephone networks, private dedicated networks, and broadband multimedia transmission services to many telecommunications customers.

To strengthen its domestic operations in China, CITIC Group established the Beijing Satellite Communications Branch of CITIC Network Co., Ltd. in June 2008. As a professional company under CITIC Group engaged in satellite communications business, it holds an operating license for renting and selling satellite transponders issued by the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology, as well as a VSAT business operating license. It fully undertakes AsiaSat's business, contracts, and related work in China, providing domestic users with resources from AsiaSat 3S, AsiaSat 4, and AsiaSat 5 satellites.

The launch of AsiaSat 5 is CITIC's move to enhance its communication service capabilities.

Strengthening China's Broadcasting and Television Transmission Capabilities

It is reported that the C-band coverage of AsiaSat 5 extends eastward to Japan, westward to the Middle East and parts of African countries, southward to New Zealand, and northward to Russia, covering more than 50 countries and regions in the Asia-Pacific area.
