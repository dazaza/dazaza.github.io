1. Relying on mountains will cause them to collapse, relying on people will cause them to run away; only oneself is the most reliable.

2. No one will accompany you for a lifetime, so you must adapt to loneliness; no one will help you forever, so you must strive for life.

3. It's better to fight today with sweat than regret it with tears.

4. When the tears are gone, what remains should be strength.

5. Life is like a cup of coffee without sugar; it tastes bitter when drunk, but leaves a lingering aftertaste when recalled.

6. There is a kind of bond that becomes scenery after letting go; there is a heart that reveals sincerity while persisting.

7. Choose what you love and cherish what you choose.

8. If you don't give up in this second, there will be hope in the next.

9. Remember what you once had, cherish what’s hard to get, don't give up on what belongs to you, and don't force what doesn't belong to you; leave what has been lost as a memory.

10. If you like something, treasure it; if you treasure it, don't let it go.

11. Life is a journey; it doesn't matter where you end up, but rather the scenery along the way and the mood with which you enjoy it.

12. Don't compare yourself with others, but strive to surpass yourself; if you cry, cry with passion; if you laugh, laugh with character.

13. Those who rejoice at your sorrow are enemies; those who rejoice at your happiness are friends; those who grieve at your sorrow are your confidants.

14. If you love, love deeply; if you don't, leave.

15. As long as there is hope in your heart, there is happiness.

The first sentence: "People are all forced into action." Everyone has potential, living in comfort leads to death, and facing pressure does not lead to anxiety. Perhaps this is just a small test from life. Believe in yourself; everything can be handled well. Pressure can turn an ordinary person into a hero; difficult times create heroes, and poverty spurs change. People only have motivation when they have pressure.

The second sentence: "If you live simply, the world will be simple towards you." Simple living leads to happy living. Be content with what you have, be tolerant, and don’t overcomplicate things. A heavy mental load can make you blame fate. Regularly delete unpleasant memories and discard negative people and events.

The third sentence: "Life has no rehearsals; every day is a live broadcast." Sometimes I wonder, if life were like a video game where you could restart if you messed up, how would life be? Since time flows irreversibly and each day cannot be retrieved, we must cherish every moment, respect our parents, care for our children, be considerate to our loved ones, and be kind to our friends.

The fourth sentence: "Talent is like pregnancy; eventually, people will notice." Don't be arrogant; the Earth will continue to spin regardless of anyone. Throughout history, those who have relied on their talent have met bad ends. Even if you're highly capable, always remain humble, focus on your own tasks, and remember that gold will shine eventually.

The fifth sentence: "In the past, meeting a true friend meant drinking countless cups of wine; now, even with countless cups of wine, true friends are rare." Though I’m not much of a drinker, I understand the value of friendship. A life without friends is lonely and incomplete. However, due to life's busyness, we often lose touch, and friendships grow distant. Spend some time reconnecting with old friends, sharing laughter, and strengthening bonds. When a friend faces difficulties, remember to stand by them; even if you can't offer help, your support through words means a lot.

The sixth sentence: "If life takes a wrong direction, stopping is progress." We often struggle to correct our flaws or recognize our mistakes. Sometimes, despite knowing we’re wrong, we continue making the same errors repeatedly. Sticking to the right path and holding onto principles is crucial. The world offers many temptations, but there’s no such thing as free lunches. Don’t sacrifice happiness for fleeting pleasures. If you realize you're wrong, stop immediately.

The seventh sentence: "Two tragedies of life: losing all hope or becoming overly proud." Modern people seem especially fragile, with frequent reports of celebrities suffering from depression. These individuals often swing between extremes. Believing oneself to be perfect or omnipotent can lead to extreme self-loathing upon encountering setbacks, even causing someone to lose the courage to live. Find an accurate place for yourself and enjoy life’s pleasures.

The eighth sentence: "Life and love are similar; missing out on love is like missing out on life." What is love? It makes one feel lost and entranced. Facing love, be brave and express your feelings boldly. If flowers are available to pick, do so without hesitation. People inevitably age and die, and however we live, it's just one life. Missing out on love means missing out on life's brilliance.

The ninth sentence: "Rich people eventually find each other." Modern society has become increasingly materialistic, and love seems less significant. Thus, I believe "rich people eventually find each other" accurately describes modern romance.

The tenth sentence: "To succeed, you need friends; to achieve great success, you need rivals." Competition drives development. Because of the presence of rivals and the determination not to lose, we work harder to excel in our endeavors. Enemies sometimes exert more influence than friends. There are no eternal enemies, but there are eternal friends. In some cases, enemies can become friends.
