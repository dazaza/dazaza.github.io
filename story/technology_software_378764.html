Step-by-step Guide to Synchronizing and Backing Up Your Phone Contacts

by xdash on 2011-01-18 21:06:24

(Author of this article: @Li Shuo, Original link: http://lishuo.me/archives/75)

As the lunar new year approaches, an increasing number of students have lost their phones recently. Losing a phone is not a big deal, but losing your contact list can be quite troublesome and may result in potential or even immeasurable losses. To prevent such issues, it's wise to spend a few minutes backing up your contact list while you still have your phone. This way, if your phone is lost, you can recover your original contact information in just three seconds, minimizing your losses.

Below, I will guide you step-by-step on how to back up your phone’s contact list using four methods:

### Method 1: Use QQ Sync Assistant for Backup

It seems like everyone has a QQ account, so the first recommended backup method is Tencent's QQ Sync Assistant. QQ Sync Assistant supports numerous types of phones including iPhone, Android, Nokia, etc. You only need to download one software application to easily back up your contacts. When you switch to a new phone, simply install QQ Sync Assistant on the new phone to download the contacts from your old phone.

QQ Sync Assistant Download Link: http://mobile.qq.com/pim/

After downloading and installing the Sync Assistant based on your phone model, select the backup option. You can back up to the cloud or to a storage card. It's recommended to perform dual backups as a precaution.

### Method 2: Synchronize Your Phone Contact List with Google Contacts via Google Sync

Here are some benefits of using Google Sync to back up your contact list: no need to download any software, no need for data cables; Google is currently and will continue to be the most stable and secure data storage service provider; Google Sync can be used across different phone models and is not limited to specific operating systems or brands.

Here's an example using the most common Nokia phones to demonstrate how to synchronize your contact list via Google Sync:

1. Open the tools-settings-connection functions-data transfer-sync options-new sync scenario mode on your phone.

2. Name the sync scenario mode "Google sync."

3. Configure connection settings.

- Server version: 1.2

- Server identifier: Google (the letter G must be capitalized)

- Data transmission method: Internet

- Access point: Default connection

- Host address: https://m.google.com/syncml (note that there is an 's' after http)

- Port: 443

- Username: Enter your Gmail address xxx@gmail.com

- Password: Enter your Gmail password

- Sync type: Two-way

4. Configure contact list synchronization.

- Remote contact database: contacts (currently, Google only supports contact list synchronization).

5. After creating the new sync scenario mode, save it and start syncing directly. Now, your Google contact list will contain your phone's contact information.

### Method 3: Use the Built-in Contact Synchronization Function of Your Phone Brand

Nokia phones come with PC suites, which allow you to connect your phone to a computer with the PC suite installed to export the contact list in vCard format and store it on the computer. This allows for quick restoration when switching to a new phone.

Android operating system phones come with built-in Google contacts, which automatically update in real-time—very easy!

BlackBerry and Meizu also have built-in contact synchronization functions, which won't be elaborated here.

### Method 4: Use Third-party Software

Currently, some good third-party contact list software includes:

- Hozom Contact List http://www.hozom.com

- Youlu Contact List http://www.youlu.com/

(@XDash: Adding a few more for reference: Youbei, Quanniu, Labei.)

You can download and install these applications based on your phone model. They are all completely user-friendly operations.

That concludes the tutorial. It's recommended that students use at least two or more methods to back up their contact lists, and avoid storing all your contacts with just one service provider.
