"Future Cops": Hong Kong cinema also "copies"

by hipposhen on 2010-04-06 21:56:32

Directed by Wong Jing and starring Andy Lau and Model-turned-actress Dasuan, the movie "Future Cops" had its first trial screening in Shanghai recently. The full-length feature gathers a variety of elements such as science fiction, humor, action, car chases, idols, and children. It can be said to be another standard product from the assembly line of movies made by Wong Jing for second and third tier cities. However, because the story still has obvious signs of being pieced together, it is hard to predict the "prospects of movie tickets (www.311piao.com) in first-tier cities where the audience's appreciation level is quite high."

This film that once withdrew from the New Year's film lineup and then got caught up in rumors of being a shoddy production was never looked upon favorably. However, during its first trial screening, it won many laughs, and the special effects scenes that people were worried about reached an acceptable level. Although they are not as exciting as Hollywood blockbusters, there are no obvious flaws that can be found. Like other Wong Jing films such as "Royal Tramp", this movie's storyline still has obvious traces of being pieced together. A main plotline similar to "The Terminator", where future police from the year 2080 go back to 2020 to protect a child who will change the world in the future. Character settings like those in "Iron Man" and "Spider-Man" will make the audience feel a bit of a knockoff vibe, but the children's play parts led by young starlets like Joey Yung surprisingly take up a large portion, almost making this movie able to be classified as a children's movie. The commercially savvy Wong Jing has started trying to make money off parents again.

As for the comedy part that caters to adult audiences, Andy Lau, who hasn't been seen for a long time, starts to act innocent and funny again. Although there is nothing new, the influence represented by the three characters of Andy Lau is still there. The joke with "Love You Ten Thousand Years" wins the biggest laughs from the whole audience. Dasuan and Blackman's performances have sufficient comedic effect. Turning Blackman into a "TV man" counts as a new gag, and Fan Shao Huang's villain also does his homework well. In summary, this "Future Cops" can completely be categorized as the most common fast-food movie from the high-production era of Hong Kong films. Various elements are hodgepodge collages, only seeking to entertain without too much depth.
