The biggest mistake in family education is - the method to change it is - a must-read for parents.

by edurqusr on 2012-03-04 17:56:00

Making decisions for children under the pretext of "it's all for the children's good"

From the moment a child is born, we begin to teach them what they "should do" or tell them "you're too young to understand many things" or "this is all for your own good," and so on. In fact, this is not right. From a child's daily behavior to major decisions like schooling, almost everything is arranged by parents. If we keep making decisions for our children, they will lose the ability to think independently, make independent judgments, and make their own decisions. The correct approach is not for parents to decide everything, but to provide children with decision-making references and help them make the right judgment. Sometimes, parents' decisions may not be entirely correct, but their absolute authority has already left the child without a voice.

As long as the child's starting point for making a decision is correct, let the child make the decision. Especially for trivial matters that don't have serious consequences, the child's decision should be respected even more. Only in this way can the child grow up healthily. Remember, what determines a child's future is not just their current exam scores, but their overall ability and quality. Independent thinking and autonomous decision-making are one of the main abilities and qualities needed for future success. Such abilities and qualities may not necessarily be cultivated by universities in our country. Parents should not overly rely on or overly believe in school education. If school education is about examination-oriented education, family education is about quality-oriented education. Letting your child grow up healthily and happily, and helping your child acquire the qualities and abilities needed for future happiness, is the core goal of family education. Lifetime happiness is everyone's pursuit, and learning aims at happiness at its core. Therefore, educating your child around the indicators and qualities that will allow them to gain happiness in the future is the essence of family education.
