New generation of Apple iPhone to be released in June - Sweet IT.Net

by xjyzhenai on 2009-02-11 09:18:02

According to Internet sources, Apple has been granted a series of patents related to the iPhone. An analysis of the patents suggests that the next iPhone is likely to support video calling.

According to related claims, Apple obtained patents showing the next generation of products will support a variety of applications. Applications such as telephony, video conferencing, email, IM, blogging, picture management, digital cameras, digital video recorders, Web browsing, and digital audio/video playback. Other sections of the patent give more specific instructions, such as optical sensors that will be placed on the front of the product to capture images of users during video conferencing. Or in a simple way, it will design a video lens on the front of the phone as most 3G mobile phones do, so as to achieve video calls and video conferencing functions.

In addition, with the launch of Apple's iPhone 3G in the United Arab Emirates and Saudi Arabia, it is rare for Apple to reveal the details of the next iPhone. According to several Apple officials, including Chief Marketing officer Phil Schiller, the iPhone will be replaced again in June. This update, as we've reported in the past, will be a sea change from hardware to software. For example, it will use a multi-core ARM processor and PowerVR graphics chip, and it will provide background running functions in software. Even Apple's iPhone software development team conspired to provide experimental background running features on the iPhone 3.0 firmware, so many people believe that the rumored June iPhone release is the true second generation iPhone.

image

What is more interesting is that the network has also appeared with the next generation of Apple iPhone related functional specifications data, which will continue to stir the trend of Apple's new mobile phone. According to some of the functions of the concept design of some future iphones released on the famous technology blog Gizmodo, the machine will use a titanium shell design, and the screen material will be changed to AOLED, in order to save electricity and obtain a better use time. In addition to supporting the aforementioned video calling and recording functions, the new iPhone will also increase the main camera's pixel size to 3.2 million pixels, and the phone's storage capacity will also be greatly increased to 32GB. In addition to continuing functions such as 3G networks and GPS satellite navigation, Apple's past chip supplier Infineon recently launched the world's first single-chip 4G module, which can support 4G mobile network LTE. So it's easy to imagine that Apple plans to upgrade the next generation of iphones directly to 4G networks. However, this is not only far off the mark but almost guarantees that the credibility of previous assumptions will be very low.
