A Handsome Guy's 106 Blind Dates - Emotional Story - Jinan Home - Jinan's Most Popular Forum!

by mishi_1990 on 2010-10-25 09:36:31

Interviewee: Zhang Xiao, Birth Year: 1978, Profession: Civil Servant, Residence: Beijing

I met Zhang Xiao's parents at a parents' matchmaking event in a park in Beijing. Listening to Mr. Zhang, I learned that they had been attending these events for nearly two years, introducing over a hundred girls to their son, but none of them worked out. As their son, who has a stable job and minor achievements in his career, approaches thirty, the matter of finding him a wife remains unresolved, causing much concern for his parents.

Handsome and Sunny Zhang Xiao

In the photo, Zhang Xiao appears as a handsome, tall, and robust young man. Such a good-looking guy still struggles to find a girlfriend, despite his parents already purchasing and decorating a house for his marriage and recently buying him a car after he had worked for only a few years. Zhang Xiao's parents, ordinary intellectuals, have almost gone all out for their child’s matrimonial affairs. Yet, even so, his side remains empty, without a fixed girlfriend. Is it because Zhang Xiao's standards are too high, or are there fewer good girls available?

Zhang Xiao's father, who has accompanied his son through many life stages, expressed his sighs. From his son's entrance into a key high school to university, from graduation to finding work, he never felt any great difficulties with his son. However, when his son reached adulthood and needed to establish a family, he found that finding a satisfactory daughter-in-law seemed incredibly challenging.

In Zhang Xiao's father's view, cultivating a child up to 28 years old, then allowing him to marry a good wife, establishing a happy marriage and having his own small family would mean fulfilling his parental responsibilities completely, leaving no regrets in life. But this last hurdle seems to be quite tough. For this reason, Mr. and Mrs. Zhang often sigh and worry about their son's situation. What could be the reason why Zhang Xiao's over a hundred matchmakings haven't resulted in success? On an autumn afternoon with a gentle breeze, I met 28-year-old Zhang Xiao.

Compared to the young man in the photo, the Zhang Xiao in front of me is even more handsome, sunny, and filled with a strong scholarly aura. Studying psychology, he narrates his experiences with detail and charm.

Average Requirements for Girls

So far, I've been on over a hundred blind dates, precisely around 106 times. Why do I remember so clearly? That's because I've been very dedicated to this aspect. On one hand, my parents are anxious about my marriage and have prepared everything for me. On the other hand, my career is relatively stable, and I really want to resolve my marital issues during this period. Therefore, over the past two years, whether introduced by friends or arranged by my parents, I've taken each encounter seriously and even tried developing relationships with some girls. Unfortunately, I truly can't evaluate the girls of today. Among the girls I've met, about 80% were discontinued by me, while the remaining 20% ended due to various reasons. In most cases, it's not that others don't like me, but rather that I can't accept them.

People who don't know me think my standards are too high and advise me to be more realistic. Actually, I believe my requirements are just average: acceptable appearance, similar academic qualifications, negligible family conditions, good character, polite, kind-hearted, simple, and not overly materialistic. To my dismay, among the over a hundred girls I've met, those who aren't materialistic or obsessed with wealth are extremely rare. This may be a significant reason why I can't develop deeper relationships with them.

The First Doctoral Girlfriend

My earliest girlfriend was a postgraduate student pursuing a doctorate at a Beijing university. Born in 1979, she was also a first-generation only child like me. Although we were introduced by someone else, we studied the same major in college, and she was only one year behind me. When I met her, she had just started her doctoral studies, so we had much to talk about professionally.

After dating for a while, my parents thought highly of this girl and even visited her parents. Since both her parents were laid-off workers, we didn't have much common language, but they were simple, warm-hearted, and treated me well. My parents thought this marriage proposal was decent and began considering our engagement. Perhaps because our relationship had become more solid, my doctoral girlfriend started showing her lack of upbringing.

When my parents mentioned wanting us to get engaged, she immediately made demands. First, she wanted me to give her control over my salary, then she set rules requiring me to note down the birthdays of all her family members in my notebook and send gifts and flowers. Once, after returning from a business trip, I found out it was someone's birthday in her family. She ordered me to buy gifts and deliver them to her home right away; otherwise, she would keep nagging me. Helplessly, I had to take a taxi, buy things, and rush to her place.

Perhaps because of her tough upbringing, I found her desire for material wealth uncontrollable. On Sundays, she always hoped I would accompany her shopping. Although she was a doctoral student, I rarely saw her studying except before exams. She knew all sorts of brands well and demanded that I buy her expensive cosmetics. If I didn't, she threatened to break up with me. Besides clothes and cosmetics, many of her living expenses were borne by me. When dining out with friends, she would bring her favorite dishes to her plate, ignoring others and eating greedily. I advised her to mind her table manners since we had friends around, but she replied rudely, "This is what I like to eat, and I enjoy eating this way. At my home, we fight for food, what's it to you?"

Seeing my USB drive was nice, she took it off my computer without asking and walked away with it. Since it contained confidential work data, I panicked and called her to return it. She disdainfully said, "It's just a lousy USB drive, nothing special, you're too stingy."

Later, I discovered that not only did my belongings frequently go missing, but she would also steal money from my wallet and use it to buy expensive gifts for others. This made me feel somewhat scared. Around this time, she accidentally saw my payslip. Perhaps the number was far below her expectations, she angrily threw a line at me, "With such a low salary, aren't you embarrassed?" Then, in front of my parents, she stormed off angrily.

Her reaction didn't surprise me at all because, in her view, money was the sole standard for measuring happiness. For a period, to urge me to buy a car, she came up with a plan to stimulate me using her friends who owned cars.

One of her classmates married a slightly wealthy man, and they planned a suburban self-drive tour. She insisted that I join her to hitch a ride with them. However, her classmate lived in Shijingshan and refused to pick us up from the city. We'd have to endure a three-hour public transport journey to meet them. I really didn't want to go, but she kept throwing tantrums and forced me to accompany her. On the way back, sitting on the bus, she criticized me, "If you bought a car, we wouldn't have to suffer like this. Without stimulating you, you won't understand the benefits of having a car or the pain of not having one. Now you understand my intentions, right?"

Facing such a girl, what could I say? The biggest lesson from this doctoral girlfriend was that one's quality cannot be measured by their education level. High academic qualifications don't necessarily mean high-quality individuals; instead, some highly educated people might be worse than those with lower educational backgrounds. This became more evident in the girls I met later. My doctoral girlfriend often quarreled with me due to the gap between my income and her expectations. Sometimes, she even scolded me loudly in front of my parents, slamming doors and pounding tables like a shrew. This left me deeply disappointed. I discussed the idea of breaking up with her with my father, who said, "This girl's temper is bad, but if you can tolerate her, so can your mother and I. If you're willing, we have no problem."

Hearing my father say this, I almost cried. What kind of thing was this? For such a shrewish girl, I actually let my parents suffer. They were particularly kind and well-mannered people; how could they possibly coexist with such a daughter-in-law in the future? I always believed that marriage wasn't just my personal matter; it was the guarantee of happiness for several families. Thus, I decisively decided to break up with my doctoral girlfriend.

Miss Liu, A Japanese Language Graduate

Not long after the breakup, my parents introduced me to Miss Liu at a park matchmaking event. She graduated with a degree in Japanese, was 26 years old, an only child, and worked for a foreign company. After meeting a few times, I discovered she was simultaneously dating several men. During our dates, she often received flirtatious calls or notifications for other dates. I felt uneasy and asked her about it. Her parents repeatedly emphasized that she didn't have a boyfriend, yet she openly admitted to dating multiple men and justified it by saying, "This is called casting a wide net, focusing on the catch, and targeted cultivation."

Since she studied Japanese, she greatly admired Japanese culture and often compared herself to the female protagonists in Japanese dramas. Her lifestyle was extremely materialistic, and our dates often took place in large malls. She frequently required me to buy her high-end clothes and gifts and refused to dine in small restaurants, insisting only on upscale ones.

Soon after, she quit her job, claiming she was going to further her studies and asked me for ten thousand yuan as tuition fees. Seeing my hesitation, she suggested, "You can hug me, you know, the kind of hug between men and women." Saying this, she moved her body toward mine. Out of politeness, I hugged her briefly, but it felt cold and awkward.

Frankly speaking, this was a very uncomfortable experience, feeling like an exchange. Seeing that I didn't immediately agree to her request, Miss Liu hinted that she could live with me without any responsibility from my side. This truly frightened me. Could such a girl be a wife?

'Surplus Goods' Xiao Hui

After ending the relationship with this girl, I met Xiao Hui, whose parents were both university professors. They appeared to be well-educated and left a good impression on my parents. Thus, I agreed to meet her. Xiao Hui, 25 years old, an only child, and a graduate of a vocational college, looked decent. However, during our first meeting, I noticed something was off. Our conversation was interrupted several times by phone calls. Each time she answered, she seemed nervous, glancing at me before stepping aside to whisper. I faintly heard her constantly explaining where she was and promising to return soon. I confirmed that she definitely had a boyfriend.

After discussing this at home, my mother became worried and immediately called Xiao Hui's mother to inquire. It was then that Xiao Hui's mother hesitantly revealed that her daughter was living with a boyfriend. However, this man was a 30-something married man with substantial wealth. Willingly, Xiao Hui became his mistress.

This situation troubled Xiao Hui's parents immensely. They explained to their daughter all the reasons why she should leave the man, but she refused, citing, "He's rich, and I want to live a good life without hardship or toil."

Xiao Hui's parents couldn't accept this either. Unable to persuade her, they turned to the parent matchmaking events to find a young, suitable boy to introduce to Xiao Hui, hoping to replace the wealthy man in her life. Perhaps unable to resist her parents' arrangements, Xiao Hui reluctantly met me but acted rebelliously from the start. Moreover, upon meeting, she bluntly asked me, "I'm the type of girl who only wants to live a good life without working hard. Can you satisfy me?"

This girl's story infuriated both my parents and me. We thought, how selfish her parents were to introduce such a daughter to others. Wasn't this harmful to us?

Unexpectedly, after our single meeting with no further contact, Xiao Hui bombarded me with text messages and calls half a year later, desperately wanting to talk to me again. Initially, I was indeed busy, but seeing her urgency, I felt obligated to meet her once more. Unexpectedly, as soon as she saw me, Xiao Hui burst into tears, shedding all her previous arrogance. After listening for a while, I understood that Xiao Hui had been dumped by the wealthy man who promised her a comfortable life. Filled with anger, she thought of me and wanted to rekindle our relationship, "to date properly." Looking at Xiao Hui's tear-streaked face, devoid of any innocence, I suddenly wanted to cry, "I'm a decent man, how could Xiao Hui think I would be interested in 'surplus goods'?"

Miss Sun, Who Likes Mazda 6

Miss Sun, 26 years old, currently serving in the military, her parents being army officers, with her father a senior general. After both sets of parents exchanged information, I chatted with Miss Sun online a few times, feeling okay, and agreed to meet her at her workplace.

Upon picking her up at her unit, Miss Sun was not pleased from the start. After a brief silence, she remarked, "Why do you drive such a car? Our family drives Hongqi. How come you don't buy a Mazda 6?" I must say my car is not bad among peers; it's a mid-range sedan. I wanted to explain but realized there was no need. We just met for the first time, and who knows where this will lead. Why should I buy a Mazda 6 just because you like it?

Furthermore, are you here to date a person or a car? Can you fall in love with a luxury car? Isn't it said in "No Thieves in the World," "Just because someone drives a good car, does that make them a good person?" Such a worldly girl is intolerable. Before I could react, Miss Sun continued, "How many rooms is the house you bought?" Seeing I didn't respond, she added, "We live in a general's villa, a standalone one. My dad even has a driver. By the way, how much do you earn? You must make a lot, right?"

Seeing I wasn't responding much, Miss Sun grew impatient, "I'm not just any girl. If your conditions aren't good enough, let's not waste time. I look down on average men."

Unexpectedly, the Miss Sun who could chat about other things online turned out to be this way in real life. I only felt sorry for her high-ranking parents. With just this one daughter, how did they raise her?

Bad-tempered Miss Qin

Miss Qin, 26 years old, an only child, a doctor, her parents being state employees, often dressed fashionably and stood out at the parents' matchmaking events. Following their advice, I dated Miss Qin for a while. Perhaps due to high work pressure or excessive expectations during her growth, despite graduating from a prestigious medical university, her temperament was quite poor. She was perpetually gloomy, uninterested in anything, and frequently argued over trivial matters, displaying a complete lack of manners. Later, we learned her mother was a store clerk. I guessed her bad temper might be related to her mother's upbringing.

It should be said that the girls I met through the parents' matchmaking events had good educational backgrounds and mostly came from families with middle-income or better. However, regardless of whether their parents were professors or officials, most of these girls shared similarities: materialistic, lacking proper upbringing, impolite, strongly self-centered, and prone to hurtful remarks. Sometimes this had little to do with their education levels. A doctoral or master's degree holder wasn't necessarily more well-mannered or polite than someone with just a vocational diploma.

Some girls talked about economic conditions as soon as we met. Others straightforwardly stated they sought a man who was a cook, driver, and wallet combined. Furthermore, many of the girls' parents would openly say, "Our daughter can't cook, doesn't do housework, and can't do anything." Some even seemed proud of this fact. Meeting such girls often left me disinterested.

Deceived Girl

At this point, a colleague introduced me to a girl, 25 years old, an only child, a vocational school graduate working at a newspaper, from a worker's family. With the previous experiences, I no longer wanted to meet someone casually. I agreed to communicate with her online for a while before deciding whether to meet in person.

Unexpectedly, before we had communicated much, her parents suddenly called me, saying their daughter had been missing for several days and wondered if she had come to me. I was shocked; this wasn't something to joke about lightly. Quickly, I helped her parents check her QQ records online. It turned out she had a passionate online boyfriend, and according to the message records, they had "eloped."

More than twenty days after the girl went missing, she suddenly sent a text message to her parents, saying she was with her boyfriend, everything was fine, but they needed some money, asking her parents to transfer it to her bank card quickly.

Her parents repeatedly messaged her, urging her to come home first before discussing anything. However, the girl firmly insisted on staying with her boyfriend and informed her parents that the man she met online was the illegitimate son of a national leader, extremely wealthy and kind to her. Recently, this man was about to inherit a fortune elsewhere and thus requested her parents to wire them travel expenses.

Unwilling to send money but fearing harm to their daughter, they endured hunger and followed her instructions, wiring her several ten-thousand-yuan amounts. Her workplace also sent her a text message, informing her that if she didn't return soon, she would be dismissed. However, the girl responded with a text, "If I get dismissed, so what? My boyfriend will soon receive a fortune. Do I still need to work?"

Surprisingly, during this period, the girl and her boyfriend returned home once. At home, her parents pleaded with her not to leave, but when the boyfriend gave her a choice between staying or leaving with him, the girl decisively chose to walk out the door with him without looking back, leaving her parents utterly hopeless. More than two months passed, and the girl still hadn't returned home, continuously demanding her parents to wire money to her account. Her parents, unable to bear it anymore, entrusted me to investigate the man's true identity. Resultantly, there was no record of this man in the police's household registration archives, confirming him undoubtedly as a scammer. The girl's parents reported the case.

Later, after some investigation by the police, following the girl's credit card consumption trail, they finally located them in Haidian District. Upon verification, the man claiming to be the illegitimate son of a national leader was an undeniable scammer. His only props were a stolen Mercedes-Benz and a relatively presentable appearance. His real identity was a farmer from the suburbs of Beijing. Strangely, even after the police revealed the scammer's true identity to the girl, she still refused to believe she had been deceived. Only when she returned home and her parents showed her the dismissal notice from her workplace did she seem to wake up, bursting into tears.

Afterward, I conversed with her online. I asked her why she trusted a man she met online so easily and even gave herself to him, risking conflict with her parents. She told me, "I really want to live a good life, to be rich, to live comfortably without effort. But my family has no money, and I don't have a high education. When will I ever live such a life? I fantasize about shortcuts, getting rich quickly, becoming wealthy instantly. So, when that man said he had a large inheritance, I was tempted. And he didn't seem like a bad person, quite like a high-ranking official's child. So, when he asked me to go with him, I decided to take the risk. I never imagined it was a scam."

Although I never truly interacted with this girl in real life, our online exchanges made me realize that this incident was certainly a blow to her. Whether it was gullibility that harmed her or the desire for effortless gain that led her astray, a 25-year-old girl lacked any judgment, which was undeniably pitiful.

Never Give Up

After this, I met many more, and matchmaking almost became my entire leisure life. Miss Bai, 26 years old, an only child, a master's graduate from the UK, a senior employee at a major American company. Perhaps due to the hardships of studying abroad, she had become a "tool" focused solely on work, making normal communication nearly impossible. Zhang Qian, a law graduate, 29 years old, an only child, refusing to meet in person, preferring online communication, sending photos that looked weathered and weary, earning a high salary in the hundreds of thousands, yet repeatedly saying she was unhappy.

Some friends ask me if I feel defeated after all these experiences. On the contrary, I believe I am gaining more confidence. The more people I meet, the better my judgment becomes. Although I am indeed quite disappointed with today's girls, especially some of the post-80s generation, who are even harder to accept, I always believe that as long as I don't give up, I will eventually find the girl who gives me the confidence to build a happy marriage. I will continue searching.
