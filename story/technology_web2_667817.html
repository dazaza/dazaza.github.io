Male student is often mistaken as female because of his delicate name (Picture)

by anonymous on 2012-02-17 10:32:04

On Hong Xiangxiang's certificate, the gender column is often altered or mistaken.

Our correspondent Guo Jianchun, reporter Zou Hongshan and Wang Danjing.

There is a boy named Hong Xiangxiang, who is in his third year of studying English at Zhejiang Wanli University. At 22 years old, he wears glasses, stands at 1.78 meters, and has distinct features. However, because his name sounds too feminine, he is frequently mistaken for a girl.

When dormitory rooms were assigned during his freshman year, his roommates were thrilled upon seeing his name - could it be a mixed-gender dorm?

In his junior year, when applying for part-time jobs, two companies mistakenly hired him as a female secretary.

Hong Xiangxiang was very frustrated. He told me yesterday that he really wanted to shout out loud in a crowd: "I'm a real man!"

He was called "Fragrant Princess" when he was little.

Originally, Hong Xiangxiang was named Hong Bangxiang. His father thought this name was too rustic, so he changed it to Hong Xiangxiang since his sister was named Hong Sisi. Of course, the name change also hoped that he would think more before acting impulsively.

However, this name brought him a lot of trouble.

"When I heard your name, it sounded so cute..." When he entered kindergarten, the children particularly liked his name, gradually calling him "Hong Xiangxiang", and eventually transforming it into "Fragrant Princess."

In college, four boys shared one dormitory room. Before he arrived, the three roommates were delighted upon seeing the name Hong Xiangxiang: "Is this a mixed-gender dormitory?"

A few days ago, when he was looking for part-time work and sent out many resumes, two calls came saying he had been hired as a secretary - a position clearly designated for women.

His gender on certificates was often written incorrectly.

For the public English level three and computer level one exams, classmates helped collect the certificates, but the gender section was marked as female. Even on his college entrance exam admission ticket, the gender was incorrectly marked as female.

The most exaggerated case was during the middle school computer bonus test, where the gender column first wrote "female", then crossed it out and wrote "male", only to cross it out again and write "female".

Due to gender issues, he frequently got into fights with others when he was young.

People constantly joked about his gender, making Hong Xiangxiang feel humiliated. As a child, to avoid being called "Fragrant Princess," he fought several times with other children.

This time, for the two companies that hired him as a female secretary, he went to both places, "just to let them see clearly that I am male."

"Next year, I will graduate. This name is too childish, so I am considering changing my name."
