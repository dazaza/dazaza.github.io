Safety Points in Mold Design_Quanzhou Computer School

by gotook2012 on 2012-02-25 09:54:02

Structurally, it should ensure the convenience of feeding, positioning, unloading, and clearing waste. For the processing of small parts, it is strictly forbidden for the operator's fingers, wrists or other parts of the body to enter the mold area; for the processing of large parts, if the operator must put their hand into the mold, the range of entering the mold should be minimized as far as possible, and the time for any part of the body to stay inside the mold should be shortened as much as possible. The dangerous areas of the mold should be clearly defined, and necessary protective measures and devices should be equipped.

All kinds of parts on the mold should have sufficient strength and rigidity to prevent damage and deformation during use. Fastening parts should have anti-loosening measures to avoid accidental injury to the operator.

It is not allowed for waste or workpieces to fly out during processing, which may distract the operator’s attention, or even injure the operator. Additionally, it is necessary to avoid cuts from burrs on the stamped parts. Operators should not have excessive motion amplitude while performing stamping operations to avoid unstable postures. Repetitive and precise motions should also be avoided during the operation. Strong noise and vibration during stamping processing should be avoided as much as possible. The mold design should indicate the weight of the mold on the general drawing to facilitate installation and ensure safety. For parts weighing more than 20 kilograms, lifting and moving measures should be taken to reduce labor intensity. When disassembling and assembling mold parts, it should be convenient and safe, avoiding the possibility of pinching or cutting hands. The mold should be easy to disassemble and store. In short, even minor issues in molds can affect safety, and only by analyzing specific problems in each operation can safety precautions in molds be proposed.
