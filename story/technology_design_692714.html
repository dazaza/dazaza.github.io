A veteran netizen's new perspective on the web!

by seo17516 on 2012-03-06 11:15:15

My name is Long Lin. I specialize in SEO optimization forum signature value analysis and have been an avid internet user for 13 years. I've had over 10 years of experience building web pages (sites). I remember when NetEase first launched, we used to go there to learn HTML tutorials. Back then, websites were basically independent entities; unless introduced by magazines or newspapers, you wouldn't even know about the existence of other websites. Now, websites mainly rely on search engines for traffic. Compared to back then, this is a huge change, almost unimaginable. Perhaps this is why Google is so respected. I believe the emergence of search engines can be as great as the appearance of Windows.

I studied Nuclear Geology as an undergraduate in university. Whether it was luck or misfortune, at the end of my second year, the state approved the establishment of a Software Engineering undergraduate major. Out of personal interest, I applied to "switch careers" to the first batch of Software Engineering undergraduate majors (approved by the department director). To this day, many friends still ask me why I suddenly changed majors so drastically. Maybe this is fate.

After graduation, my first job was with a subsidiary of the Chinese Academy of Sciences, dispatched to IBM, and I was responsible for their official website. Later, due to outstanding performance, I was transferred to become a regular employee of IBM. However, this wasn't the kind of work I dreamt of, at least not the type of work someone of my age right after university should be doing, so I decided to leave and took a management position at another company where I had more time to research things I liked.

In 2007, I spent over $2000 USD to buy www.conghua.com from overseas to make it the local portal for Conghua. This is when I started getting into SEO. Unfortunately, during operations, several times due to domestic filing issues and server problems, my site was shut down for half a month to two months, resulting in penalties that made me lose my top ranking for the keyword "Conghua". Now, I'm stuck on the second page, which has prompted me to study SEO knowledge in hopes of returning to the first page. During this period, I also engaged in the page design of another fairly large-scale website, but due to some unpleasant matters later, I don't want to mention the name of this website here. Because of these reasons, SEO optimization simplifies the optimization and promotion process. Through my studies, I learned that external links are very important for websites.

One day, while chatting casually with a friend, I found out that A5 was organizing a group-buying event for ChongChong (a marketing tool). After understanding the functions of ChongChong, I felt it would be quite suitable for my target operations, so I quickly paid and bought ChongChong. Of course, in the first two weeks after purchasing ChongChong, my mood went from being very excited before buying to feeling somewhat disappointed. This was mainly because of ChongChong's powerful functions, but also its complexity, making it unclear where to start. However, after two weeks of learning, I finally began to understand ChongChong, and this was when I started collecting resources intensively.

However, since most of the things I collected had already been gathered and used by other ChongChong users, I found that there were very few resources that could be successfully posted without being deleted. Moreover, I personally felt it was immoral to post large amounts of information on others' websites, so I considered using some of my own domains to operate a network of sites. I established seven websites, installed programs supported by ChongChong's publishing interface, set permissions so only my account could post, and prevented others from registering and posting information. In fact, although my new sites have only been online for over a month, there have already been many applications for new accounts, so when we build websites, we should also consider how to prevent others from bombarding our sites with information when we do the same to theirs.

During the operation of the network of sites, I paid attention to the inclusion status of the websites every day. Initially, within about a week, Baidu enthusiastically included hundreds of pages. However, less than half a month later, it suddenly dropped my inclusion to just over twenty articles. Even for common phenomena explaining increased Baidu weight, two sites lost all their pages. At this point, I thought it might be because I set ChongChong to publish too frequently, basically releasing thousands of articles at fixed points every day. So I considered adjusting, using ChongChong's random release time interval settings, setting each article's release time from 30 seconds to 10 minutes randomly. This should seem more like human operation to search engines. Whether this operation was correct or if Baidu was just playing a joke on me, now all seven websites' inclusions have recovered, and the inclusions are all in the hundreds. Due to the low weight of the websites, the inclusions are temporarily not ideal, but I think for new websites, this result is already good. I feel it will gradually improve. However, because the content is mostly collected, it varies in quality and isn't easy to categorize, so my next step will be to gradually adjust and make the content better serve the website I aim to create external links for.

Once these seven websites gain a certain amount of weight, I plan to provide full-site links to the website I want to create external links for. This way, I will have decent external link resources and won't interfere with others' websites. Currently, due to the urgency of launching the websites, I haven't planned a particularly good website structure, so I haven't installed Baidu Statistics either, because I heard it analyzes your website experience. My content is all collected, so the experience isn't good, and because I set up one account to post information, this further creates the situation where all the information comes from one user. But with my subsequent improvements, this should change. Once the website inclusion improves and some keywords rank well, I can conduct further SEM operations, redirecting traffic to my valuable websites or conducting targeted marketing to convert traffic into revenue.

Of course, ChongChong's portal blog function is also quite good. I copied another version of ChongChong specifically for sending portal blogs, updating these blogs daily. If I continue to persistently post these blogs, the future weight will be good. Because there are too many sites, I actually haven't monitored the inclusion status of these sites, but I just conducted a specific keyword search and found that many articles have been indexed by search engines, proving it is effective and worth the electricity costs.

In reality, I've only used less than 20% of ChongChong software's functions. Recently, I've started using ChongChong to properly optimize a structured website http://www.smf.cn, learning from previous shortcomings and effectively leveraging ChongChong software to serve my website. Therefore, I continuously study and share experiences interacted with friends in the group. If you know me, even though I am a user, I help answer others' questions in the group. This is thanks to the PMP course I previously studied. The technology or experience you master, if you only keep it to yourself, you will soon forget it in the future, but if you organize it and share it with others, you will remember more experiences. I forgot the exact theory, but I remember this experience.

For any software, I think utilizing 40% of its functions is already quite good. For example, Word, we may not even use 5% of its functions in daily life. So, using tools in the most suitable place for oneself is the right path. Of course, if you need to widely publish a large amount of content, I constantly collect resources through ChongChong's built-in functions. Resources aren't something that can be fully obtained in a day or two, neither Google nor Baidu became what they are today overnight. Initially, everyone knows, even if you manually enter verification codes until your hands hurt, the success rate is less than 1%. But as you gradually collect new resources and accumulate your own resources, you can increase this success rate. My current experience is to delete all places requiring verification codes. If a website has had login errors for a while, discard it as well, indicating that an administrator is watching you, and continuing to post will waste everyone's time. Gradually accumulate your resources, don't fear failure. Each time I collect hundreds of resources, only 2-3 dozen can ultimately be used to post resources, and I don't collect them daily. If I did, the accumulated resources would be astonishing.

After buying ChongChong, my favorite part is the customer service QQ group, where experts often share various experiences, weekly regular sharing sessions, not only about the software but also other special uses of ChongChong or other SEO-related experiences. This is extremely valuable, and I especially thank Le Xiaoyao and Chen Mo. In summary, it's about using the software in the places you need it most, the places most suitable for your actual situation.
