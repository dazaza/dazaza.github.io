**How to determine if you have bedsores?**

1. If you feel pain, inflammation, or localized bruising in areas of your body that are under pressure, and red spots appear on the skin, it is highly possible that bedsores are about to develop. This should raise your attention.

2. If someone is extremely weak, unable to move, and is about to be bedridden, after local pressure, the skin may appear pale white, grayish white, or red, with a deeper color at the center and clear boundaries. The condition progresses rapidly, and blisters of varying sizes may appear on the surface. After these blisters rupture, they form ulcers, which easily deteriorate once exposed. This indicates the beginning formation of bedsores. At this point, the person may need medical care, guided by professional healthcare personnel.

3. Ulcers may produce a series of secretions, emitting foul odors or containing pus. If the ulcer turns black, it indicates that the lesion has involved deep fascia or muscle, and the affected tissue has necrosed due to ischemia.
