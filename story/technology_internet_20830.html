The Great Wall may fail to make the New Seven Wonders--Friends, vote for our "Great Wall"!

by kemi on 2007-05-30 21:56:24

The New Seven Wonders of the World selection was jointly inspired by UNESCO and the Swiss International Cultural Heritage Organization - the New Seven Wonders Foundation. It took place globally with 77 candidate buildings, and the final results will be announced on July 7, 2007, in Lisbon, Portugal. The top ten sites have been revealed, which are the Acropolis of Athens in Greece, the Chichen Itza Ruins on the Yucatan Peninsula in Mexico, the Roman Colosseum, the Great Wall of China, the Eiffel Tower in Paris, the Inca Forest City Machu Picchu in Peru, the Petra Ancient City in Jordan, the Prehistoric Stonehenge in the UK, the Easter Island Statues in Chile, and the Taj Mahal in Agra, India.

However, at present, the Great Wall of China, represented by the Badaling section in Beijing, may once again miss out on the title of the New Seven Wonders of the World. Yesterday, the Chinese Great Wall Society was forced to launch a voting campaign at the Badaling Great Wall, encouraging more citizens and tourists to vote before the deadline (July 7, 2007, midnight). It was revealed that the current total votes for the Great Wall were outside the top seven, but the specific number of votes would not be disclosed. (Source: The Great Wall May Not Be Selected as One of the New Seven Wonders, and the Unpublished Vote Count is Questioned.)

Friends, let's take action and cast our valuable votes for the magnificent Great Wall of our motherland! Let us work together so the world knows - in the ancient East, the land of the dragon, there stands a miraculous Great Wall, The Great Wall!
