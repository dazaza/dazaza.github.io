Heavy snowstorms trigger a sharp increase in coal demand, leading to universal coal price increase due to transportation difficulties

by ldfgskkgfea2 on 2012-02-15 09:32:41

Heavy Snowstorms Trigger a Surge in Coal Demand – Transport Difficulties Lead to Across-the-board Price Increases – Construction Cement - Industry News - Building Materials Network

The heavy snowstorms in northern China have triggered a significant increase in winter coal demand for heating. However, due to the disruption of road transportation and insufficient railway transport capacity, the average inventory of coal for electricity in northern thermal power plants is generally at the critical point of safe storage levels. The demand for thermal coal is currently at its peak, leading to an overall rise in coal prices.

Recently, the northern regions of China have experienced rare snowstorms that have affected transportation in coal-producing areas such as Shaanxi, Hebei, and Shanxi provinces. In Shanxi, about 70% of the coal transported out-of-province is via railway, and 30% is via road transport. The heavy snow has directly impacted the transport capacity of roads and railways, obstructing the normal operation of the coal transportation arteries. Analysts estimate that coal production will decline, and coal prices will show an upward trend due to weather conditions affecting coal shipments after railway transport.

According to the latest statistics from the China Coal Market Network, from October to the first half of November, the market price of coal transactions at the Qinhuangdao Port has seen three consecutive increases. As of November 15, the transaction price of coal with a calorific value of 5500 kcal reached 650 yuan per ton, which is 40 yuan higher than the coal price on October 1. By last Friday, the coal stockpile at Qinhuangdao Port decreased from the recent peak of 8.5986 million tons on November 10 to 8.2317 million tons, breaking the previous one-sided upward trend of coal stockpiles at the Qinhuangdao Port.

Analyst Li Chaolin from the China Coal Market Network stated that with the arrival of the winter coal storage peak, coal market demand will continue to be optimistic. The Qinhuangdao Port inventory will decrease somewhat, and the market has already shown some signs of tension. The coal transaction price is expected to show a slight upward trend.

Currently, many cities in the north have started supplying heat earlier than usual, leading to a continuous rise in the consumption of electric coal and a substantial increase in national heating electricity usage. For example, Beijing began providing heat gradually from November 1, about 15 days earlier than the scheduled heating period.

Meanwhile, due to insufficient railway transport capacity, the volume of coal imports into major coal-importing provinces and large coal consumers has declined. Currently, the average inventory of coal for electricity at the main thermal power plants in Shandong Province has dropped to 15 days, reaching the critical point of safe storage levels. The daily coal consumption of the 349 direct-supply power plants across the country exceeds 2 million tons, with some power plants having less than 3 days of coal reserves.

Additionally, statistics show that domestic coal consumption levels are rapidly rising. The coal consumption level in November has reached the highest daily average within the year. In the first ten days of November, the coal consumption of domestic direct-supply power plants was 2.09 million tons per day. The domestic coal inventory level has started to show a gradual downward trend, reaching the lowest level of the year, with the coal inventory of direct-supply power plants dropping to 14 days. Meanwhile, the monthly average increase in coal demand for winter heating is expected to be between 8-10 million tons. Both coal for power generation and heating will increase simultaneously, pushing domestic thermal coal demand into its peak season.
