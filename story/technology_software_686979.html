**Reasons and Solutions for Common Issues in Sound Systems**

1. **Cause**: The sound system and lighting system share the same single-phase power supply. The thyristor in the lighting system generates a large current, which is an AC pulse. Through the power cord, it couples directly to the input end of the amplifier, causing significant AC noise.

**Solution**: Carefully inspect the shielding lines between devices and use welding where possible.

2. **Cause**: Insufficient mid-frequency energy in the microphone.

**Solution**: Boost the 800Hz frequency band.

3. **Cause**: Excessive mid-frequency energy.

**Solution**: Attenuate the 1K-1.25KHz frequency band.

4. **Action Required**: Thoroughly check the wiring of all channel devices, preferably re-soldering all connection points.

5. **High-pitched Harshness**:

**Cause**: Some audio equipment uses two-prong plugs, but some devices require three-prong plugs. Otherwise, AC induction may occur during connection, leading to AC noise.

**Solution**: Reverse the power plug of two-prong audio equipment when running the system to reduce AC noise significantly.

6. **Sound Too Close to Ears**:

**Cause**: Excessive low-frequency lower limit energy.

**Solution**: Increase the lower limit frequency of the bass.

7. **Dry Microphone Sound, Tiring to Sing**:

**Cause**: Insufficient mid-low frequency energy.

**Solution**: Boost the 250Hz frequency band.

8. **Cause**: Excessive mid-high frequency energy.

**Solution**: Attenuate the 2K-4KHz frequency band.

9. **Buzzing Sound**:

**Cause**: Poor contact or cold soldering of the shielding line in device connections.

**Solution**: If thorough inspection of all connection points still results in "clicking" sounds, it could be due to broken wires in the connection cables. Use a multimeter to check each wire and replace cables with internal breaks.

10. **Boomy Bass**:

**Cause**: Excessive energy at the central low-frequency range.

**Solution**: Attenuate the 60-80Hz frequency band.

11. **Unpleasant Sound**:

**Cause**: Excessive high-frequency energy.

**Solution**: Attenuate the 6KHz frequency band.

12. **Thin or Dry Sound**:

**Cause**: Devices with three-prong power plugs are connected to two-wire input sockets, and the live and neutral wires of the two-wire socket are reversed.

**Solution**: Check the power strip to ensure proper socket contact.

13. **Severe Humming Noise When System is Silent**:

**Cause**: One or more devices in the serial-connected audio equipment have their power plugs not properly inserted.

**Solution**: Ensure all power plugs are securely inserted.

14. **No Sound When Playing Music**:

**Cause**: Cold soldering in the connecting lines between devices causes intermittent audio signals when music signals are present.

**Solution**: Use separate single-phase power supplies for the sound and lighting systems.

15. **Isolated Power Supply for Sound System**:

**Solution**: Use an isolation transformer for the sound system's power supply. The isolation transformer's power should be greater than the power consumption of the sound system.

16. **Harsh Sound**:

**Cause**: Excessive ultra-high-frequency energy.

**Solution**: Attenuate the 12K-16KHz frequency band.

17. **Excessive Sub-bass Energy**:

**Cause**: Excessive sub-bass upper limit energy.

**Solution**: Attenuate the 125-160Hz frequency band.

18. **Irritating Sound**:

**Cause**: High-frequency tones lack smoothness.

**Solution**: Attenuate the 8K-10KHz frequency band.
