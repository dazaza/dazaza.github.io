Traditional Media Compete for Chinese Domain Names, Actively Expanding the Internet Market

by now on 2007-11-23 09:26:20

After the activation of Chinese domains by more than 5,000 national government agencies including National Development and Reform Commission, Ministry of Foreign Affairs, State Administration for Industry & Commerce as well as people’s governments at all levels, recently, in order to better serve Internet users and develop online market, traditional media industry also activated Chinese domains one after another. By entering Xinhua News Agency.CN, Central People's Broadcasting Station.CN, People’s Daily Online.CN, etc. in the browser, you can directly enter the news website of the media to conveniently and quickly check the latest information.

Due to the impact of new media, more and more readers and viewers of traditional media have turned to the Internet, making traditional media attach importance to the construction and improvement of Internet platforms. Based on Chinese, Chinese domain names rely on the advantages of mother tongue, are simple and easy to remember, and are deeply loved by Chinese Internet users, becoming one of the indispensable domain names for Chinese websites. Therefore, major media outlets have registered to protect their Chinese domain names. According to statistics, by the end of September this year, domestic traditional newspaper, radio and television, film and other media groups and private media institutions had registered and activated thousands of Chinese domain names for their news websites, among which the relevant Chinese.CN domain names related to traditional newspapers accounted for 95% of the total number of Chinese domain names for news websites.

With the full support of the new version of the browser system for Chinese domain names, the phenomenon of Chinese domain names being preemptively registered by others is also increasing. Compared with other industries, the phenomenon of network brands of traditional media being preemptively registered is also serious. It took two years for "China.org.cn" to fight for its legitimate rights and interests; it was a long and difficult process for Central People's Broadcasting Station to obtain "Metropolitan Voice.CN" and "Voice of China.CN"; Shangshang Media has strong protection awareness and has already activated "Shangshang.com", while Tian Sheng Media, which bought the broadcasting rights of the Premier League in mainland China from 2007-2010, could not escape the fate, and "Tian Sheng Media.com" was preemptively registered by others.

It is understood that there are many kinds of Chinese domain names. In addition to the commonly used Chinese.CN, there are also Chinese.com, Chinese.net, Chinese.sh, plus some unique Chinese.HK (Hong Kong regional domain), Chinese.TW, Chinese.TM of Now Telecom (www.now.cn), and there are as many as 14 kinds of suffixes that can be registered. Experts said that the value of Chinese domain names is increasingly recognized by people, the value of traditional.CN and.COM suffixes is very high, and other Chinese domain name suffixes also have very high market value. On the professional domain name trading platform - Name Time Space (e.now.cn), “Plan.Network” is priced at 999,999 yuan, and “Media.tv” is selling for 80,000 yuan online.

Industry insiders believe that compared with registered trademarks, domain names as network trademarks are often not taken seriously, which also gives some professional domain name investors opportunities, and the phenomenon of preemptive registration of domain names of well-known enterprises and brand products occurs frequently. Experts suggest that enterprises should protect their own network brands. The top priority is to register domain names related to their enterprise names, brands, and products as soon as possible, especially Chinese domain names.
