Parents helplessly take children to work to spend the June 1st Children's Day in the Crown Service Center

by hefeibai0r on 2010-05-31 00:57:21

Xiaomei Huan was very doubtful about whether the Children's Day activities were really so grand and whether they would delay the time of parents in two places. "Don't schools think that all parents are free? Can a vacation be taken just by asking for one? My work is still quite busy, and even if I ask for leave, it might not be approved. What if these parents are too busy to come or if the grandparents at home are unwilling to help? I wonder if other kindergartens do this as well?"

Thinking about how her child would carry such a heavy drum in costume under the scorching sun, Ms. Zhou frankly said she felt very uncomfortable. Xiao Bo also told the reporter that last year, the event was held in the same way. On the day, due to some early guidance, the activity which was supposed to start at 8:30 AM did not officially begin until 9 AM. The children had to stand quietly below the stage, unable to make noise or play around, continuing until noon at 12 PM. "We were all baked under the sun."

The reporter learned that some kindergartens and primary schools in Guangzhou have issued notices stating that "according to regulations from higher authorities, June 1st will be a holiday for kindergartens." However, what about those children without parents available to accompany them? The notice does not address this.

Before the "Children's Day" celebrations, there were endless rehearsals that made some parents regretful. "I really regret letting my child join the drum band. It's not even Children's Day yet, but the events have been going on for two or three consecutive days." Ms. Zhou works as a middle-level manager in a foreign-funded company, and her 11-year-old son, Xiao Bo (a pseudonym), attends a key elementary school in Guangzhou and was selected as a drummer in the drum band. In preparation for Children's Day, Xiao Bo's drum band started practicing nearly a month ago. "In the past few weeks, after the second class every afternoon, he has to gather with his teammates to practice. Even Saturday mornings were not spared for rest; they had to go to school for practice. Starting from last Friday, there have been a series of performances."

It wasn't just the day of Children's Day that Miss Huan worried about. She told the reporter that last Friday afternoon, the kindergarten held a parent meeting mainly discussing the Children's Day activities. This year, the theme was a cartoon costume parade, requiring parents to dress their children up as various cartoon characters. They even suggested buying costumes online from Taobao or renting them from photo studios! Last Monday night, parents had to bring their children to the kindergarten for the event! Last Tuesday, the kindergarten was closed for a day.

According to national regulations, June 1st is a holiday for schools.

The reporter learned that currently, some public kindergartens in Guangzhou are more considerate towards dual-income families, adopting more humane practices. For example, during events at the kindergarten or school, parents who have time are invited to participate, or parents who can take leave to accompany their children may send their children back to the kindergarten where special teachers organize activities.

For some dual-income parents whose children don’t have grandparents nearby, the situation is even more complicated. Mr. Liu Chengwei (a pseudonym) and his wife both work and live in Liwan Garden. With no one to look after their child on holidays, he approached his son’s kindergarten hoping they could keep his child for the day. However, the kindergarten stated that Children’s Day is an important family bonding time, and leaving the child alone at school might cause psychological harm. Given the potential negative impact, Mr. Liu decided to bring his child to work on Children’s Day.

Some parents expressed confusion to the reporter regarding the complete closure of kindergartens and schools for Children’s Day. They questioned, “Isn’t Children’s Day the children’s holiday, not Teachers’ Day?” They suggested that teachers should not take the day off and instead, schools should organize meaningful activities for children without parental supervision, allowing them to enjoy the festive atmosphere while relieving working parents of concerns.

A staff member at a government office along Jiangdao Road, Ms. Huang Yu, does not have children but encountered similar issues with her nephew. "I once tried bringing my 3-year-old nephew to work. He kept asking to go to the bathroom and then wanted milk. When I tried calling his father, he hung up abruptly." Ms. Huang lamented, "This Children’s Day is coming again, and I’m really worried that my boss won’t approve."

Design teacher Mr. Chi Bo, born in the late 1970s, recalled that during his childhood, on Children’s Day, children would be led by teachers to watch movies or visit parks, and in the evening, celebrate with their families. Both teachers and parents contributed to making the children’s holiday joyful.

As Children’s Day approaches, the Zhao couple living in Yuexiu District faces a dilemma: Should they take leave to accompany their child or reluctantly leave him with the grandparents? With this year’s Children’s Day falling on a Tuesday, a regular workday, and their 5-year-old son Xiaoyi's (a pseudonym) kindergarten being closed all day, the Zhao couple, working in a private enterprise, feels extremely conflicted.

Parents have raised questions: Since Children’s Day is not Teachers’ Day, could schools organize activities for children without parental supervision, ensuring they engage in meaningful and enjoyable activities?
