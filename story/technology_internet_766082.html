Microsoft's Next Acquisition Target: BlackBerry?

by anonymous on 2013-09-04 15:01:39

The news of Nokia being sold to Microsoft came as a real surprise, but Microsoft urgently needed an assistant that could implement its hardware blueprint, so upon closer inspection, this acquisition made sense. With Nokia now under its wing, would Microsoft acquire any other companies, such as BlackBerry?

According to analysis by the tech media "The Wall Street Journal", Microsoft is very likely to acquire BlackBerry after Nokia. BlackBerry is also a veteran mobile phone manufacturer with a large number of patents and technologies.

The analysis pointed out that BlackBerry announced last month it would suspend stock trading and open up options for sale, alliances, or joint ventures. Nokia's choice may very well point the way forward for BlackBerry.

In fact, there have been constant rumors in the industry about Microsoft acquiring BlackBerry. Even earlier, The Times provided several reasons why Microsoft should buy BlackBerry.

We know that Microsoft's core business is aimed at enterprise users, including Windows, Office, servers, and tools, and coincidentally, BlackBerry's core business is also enterprise-oriented, thus aligning with Microsoft's business focus.

Moreover, BlackBerry also possesses a large number of technologies and patents in areas such as mobile phone security. Therefore, acquiring BlackBerry would allow Microsoft to amass more patents to counter Google and Apple's "patent wars."

According to Ballmer's plan, Microsoft will reorganize into a device and service company. To transform Microsoft from software to hardware, it certainly needs hardware engineering resources. After Nokia, if Microsoft were to acquire BlackBerry next, it would gain a large number of mobile engineers and designers in the smartphone and other fields.

BlackBerry has long put itself on the market, and now Microsoft is eager to make a big push in the mobile phone market. One is willing to buy, and the other is willing to sell. It remains to be seen whether Microsoft and BlackBerry will come to an agreement?
