Film review:

The film is adapted from a real case in 2000: a 30-year-old married woman was imprisoned for having an "inverse aid exchange" affair with a high school student more than 10 years younger. This "old cow eating tender grass" case caused a sensation in South Korea. Steven Soderbergh found out about the incident on the internet and sent an email to Park Cheolsu asking why it wasn't made into a movie. Thus, the story behind the incident unfolded.

In the film, the director symbolizes the two protagonists, aiming to express a sincere love and desire. In the first half of the film, scenes of Wen Ji and Xuan making love and eating are everywhere. In the second half, the relationship between Wen Ji, Xuan, and Zheng Na is handled ambiguously. The director does not actually show the relationship between Zheng Na and Xuan, but leaves the thinking to the audience. This deepens the discussion beyond sibling romance, expanding the nature of desire...
