Young children with leukemia find matching bone marrow but have no money for treatment

by ming1980k on 2012-02-20 11:39:43

Hua Lihui is taking care of Chengcheng. Reporter Wang Haixin took the photo. Abandoned by his parents in the hospital, having undergone 17 rounds of chemotherapy and receiving three critical condition notices... Chengcheng, who is just over 3 years old, has endured too much hardship but still faces a great challenge - due to developing drug resistance from multiple rounds of chemotherapy, the healthy cells in Chengcheng's body are only at 30% of normal levels. Yesterday, Beijing Daopei Hospital announced that after an initial screening, a matching donor for Chengcheng was found in the Chinese Marrow Donor Program. However, Chengcheng's family cannot afford the exorbitant medical costs, so many caring mothers have stepped up to raise donations.

The toddler suffers from leukemia

Yesterday morning, inside the pediatric hematology ward at Daopei Hospital, Chengcheng, who is undergoing his 17th round of chemotherapy, looked sallow. Affected by the medication, he couldn't eat anything and kept crying for his mother to hold him, ignoring any attempts to comfort him. Hua Lihui, Chengcheng's mother, immediately held him, massaging and moving his leg muscles.

Chengcheng was born on October 2, 2008, bringing hope to this remarried family and temporarily dispelling the shadow of tens of thousands of yuan of debt. "That apple-like little face, I couldn't kiss it enough," Hua Lihui recalled, her face full of happiness. In August 2009, while the whole family was still immersed in the joy of adding a new member, Chengcheng's loss of appetite for several days made Hua Lihui uneasy. She took Chengcheng to the town health clinic, where he was treated for diarrhea and fever, but Chengcheng showed no signs of improvement.

Subsequently, Chengcheng developed multiple red spots on his body and had persistent high fever. Hua Lihui and her husband Hao Shuzhan rushed Chengcheng to Xuchang Central Hospital, where Chengcheng was diagnosed with suspected leukemia.

Abandoned in the hospital to seek survival

Later, Hua Lihui and Hao Shuzhan took Chengcheng to Zhengzhou Children's Hospital, where Chengcheng was finally confirmed to have leukemia. Upon hearing the diagnosis, Hua Lihui fainted. This already heavily indebted family didn't know where they could raise the 400,000-500,000 yuan needed to save his life.

After visiting several major hospitals, Hao Shuzhan saw that the more than 30,000 yuan collected through various means for treatment was almost gone, yet Chengcheng's condition hadn't improved. His belly swelled as high as a small hill, and he fell into a coma multiple times. In May 2010, Hua Lihui decided to send Chengcheng and the family's last remaining 5,000 yuan to the First Affiliated Hospital of Zhengzhou University, hoping kind-hearted doctors would save him.

"I really had no other options. Leaving him there, he might have a chance to live; if I didn't leave him, it would be a dead end." Hua Lihui dressed Chengcheng in clean clothes, placed his favorite toys beside him, and left the hospital without looking back.

In the following two months, missing Chengcheng, Hua Lihui cried every day.

Caring mothers search for the birth mother

At this time, the hospital staff's online plea for help caught the attention of many "caring mothers."

Upon learning that Chengcheng had been left in the hospital by his parents, several caring mothers inquired with multiple hospitals in Henan province, hoping to find Chengcheng's treatment information and then track down the registration details of Chengcheng's family during their previous visits.

About two months later, the caring mothers finally found Chengcheng's home address and located Hua Lihui.

Subsequently, Hua Lihui was brought to the hospital to visit Chengcheng. Seeing that Chengcheng's condition had improved, Hua Lihui decided she would never leave her child again. The caring mothers also worked tirelessly to raise funds for Chengcheng's rescue.

Matching donor found

Because Chengcheng's condition fluctuated, the hospital issued three critical condition notices. Hua Lihui decided to have a second child, using the umbilical cord blood of the second child to save Chengcheng. "The child's illness can't wait. If we don't transplant during the remission period, the chances of survival are slim."

In October last year, Chengcheng experienced another infection, with tumor cells reaching as high as 80%, putting his life in grave danger. Under the doctor's advice, the caring mothers helped transfer Chengcheng to Beijing Daopei Hospital for treatment. After two courses of treatment, his condition stabilized.

Dr. Sun Yuan, deputy chief physician of the pediatric hematology department at Daopei Hospital, said that Chengcheng had undergone many rounds of chemotherapy and had developed resistance to many anti-cancer drugs. He needs a bone marrow transplant as soon as possible. Currently, after preliminary matching, the hospital has found a basically matching donor in the Chinese Marrow Donor Program. If the match is successful, surgery could be performed as early as the end of February this year.

Ren, one of the caring mothers who has been closely monitoring Chengcheng's condition, said that the caring mothers have posted updates about Chengcheng's condition on Weibo, raising approximately 300,000 yuan in donations so far, but there is still a shortfall of around 200,000 yuan.

Although the cost of the bone marrow transplant is enormous, Hua Lihui doesn't want to give up anymore. Chengcheng also said: "I'm not afraid of injections, I'm not afraid of medicine, my illness can be cured..."
