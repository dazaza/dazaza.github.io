BlackBerry Q10 keyboard phone fails, casting a shadow over market share plans

by anonymous on 2013-08-29 16:58:32

Initially, BlackBerry CEO Thorsten Heins was asked why BlackBerry would continue selling smartphones with physical keyboards just a few months after launching a new touchscreen smartphone. Heins replied that it was mainly because BlackBerry had always held a dominant position in the keyboard phone market.

The Wall Street Journal reported that Heins' decision seems to have suffered a major setback, as data provided by carriers in the U.S. and Canada showed that BlackBerry's Q10 keyboard smartphone sales were very poor. This situation also cast a shadow over BlackBerry's hope of reclaiming lost ground from Apple, Samsung, and other smartphone giants.

Industry expert Chris Jourdan said that consumer enthusiasm for the Q10 keyboard phone was much lower than expected. Jeff Trachsel, Chief Marketing Officer of the second-hand electronics buyer NextWorth, also stated that from the perspective of discounted deals, BlackBerry’s expectations for both the Z10 and Q10 were not met.

Currently, a spokesperson for BlackBerry still refuses to comment on the specific sales figures of these products. Verizon, a carrier of BlackBerry phones, also refused to comment.

For more than a decade, BlackBerry had a significant presence in the smartphone market but was later overtaken by Apple and Samsung. In the past three years, BlackBerry has been struggling to catch up, thus launching two key products - a touchscreen smartphone and a newly designed keyboard smartphone. Both smartphones come equipped with the latest operating system. Additionally, at the beginning of this year, BlackBerry announced plans to invest heavily in promoting these two phones. However, these two phones were delayed significantly before finally being released. By the time they hit the market, BlackBerry's share of the smartphone market had severely shrunk.

BlackBerry started rolling out the Z10 phone in the Canadian and UK markets in February, followed by the U.S. market in March. BlackBerry's Q10 keyboard smartphone didn't make its debut in Canada and other international markets until May, and only reached the U.S. market in June. According to informed sources, currently in the U.S. market, carriers like Verizon, AT&T, and Sprint have begun offering discounted versions of the Z10 phone, and inventories of both the Z10 and Q10 phones are piling up among these carriers. Meanwhile, stores like Best Buy have started offering free Z10 phones with a two-year contract. Moreover, sales of the Q10 in its home market of Canada have also been lackluster.

An AT&T spokesperson refused to comment on related matters. A Sprint spokesperson, however, noted that business customers still have an interest in purchasing the Q10 phone; business customers may become BlackBerry's last chance for a breather.
