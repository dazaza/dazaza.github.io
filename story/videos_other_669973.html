Dietary Principles for Depression

Depression is generally manifested by symptoms such as headaches and insomnia. Currently, due to the fast pace of life, there is an increasing trend in the number of people suffering from depression; however, due to various concerns, many patients with depression in China are unwilling to admit that they have this illness. Depression can be likened to a "mental cold," and it is very likely that a person will experience several such "mental colds" in their lifetime. As long as dietary adjustments are made promptly and attention is paid to nutrient intake, most patients will experience relief or recovery.

The Link Between Depression and Nutritional Deficiency

Severe cases of depression may lead to suicide. French experts have recently pointed out that nutritional deficiencies are significantly associated with depression, a factor that has been largely overlooked in past treatments. If one does not perform a certain action, something unfortunate might happen. Monique Ferri, a nutritionist at the Valence Medical Teaching Center in France, explained that when people lack nutrients, the brain cannot obtain certain trace elements that are crucial for producing neurotransmitters. Existing studies have shown that a lack of neurotransmitters can easily lead to depression. On the other hand, poor appetite is one of the main symptoms of depression because prolonged loss of appetite leads to nutritional deficiencies, which further exacerbate the condition of depression.

Claude Gendreau, head of the Geriatric Specialty Hospital in France, suggests that it is necessary to conduct early physical examinations on people who may be at risk of developing depression, preventing them from entering a vicious cycle of nutritional deficiency leading to depression, which then leads to even greater nutritional deficiency and worsened depression. At the same time, doctors should pay attention to the role of nutrition when treating patients with depression.

Dietary Principles for Depression:

[1] High Protein, High Fiber, High Calorie: Long-term insomnia consumes a large amount of energy. Timely supplementation of nutrients is beneficial for the recovery of the disease. It is recommended to consume a diet rich in high protein, high fiber, and high-calorie foods while paying attention to eating foods that help keep the bowels moving smoothly.

[2] Adequate Water Intake: Maintain the normal needs of the internal organs, lubricate the intestines, promote bowel movements, and facilitate the excretion of harmful substances within the body.

[3] Avoid Excessive Spicy, Salted, and Smoked Foods: Avoid consuming excessive amounts of spicy, salty, smoked, and other stimulating foods, as there are various causes of insomnia. Therefore, patients should choose foods suitable for their constitution based on individual conditions.

Dietary Recommendations for Preventing Depression:

To prevent depression, it's important to maintain a balance of yin and yang within the body, regulate qi and blood, so that yin and yang are harmonized, and qi and blood flow smoothly. This removes the basis for the onset of the disease. Additionally, it's essential to cultivate personal skills to handle unexpected events and resolve adverse mental stimuli.

Effect: Tonifies the spleen and kidneys, calms the spirit.

3. Fried Pig Heart with Wolfberry Leaves

Ingredients: 1 pig heart, 150-200g of wolfberry leaves, an appropriate amount of peanut oil and salt.

Method: Clean the pig heart and cut it into cubes. Stir-fry with wolfberry leaves using peanut oil according to standard methods and serve as a side dish.

Effect: Tonifies qi and blood, benefits the heart and kidneys.

4. Pork and Bitter Melon Threads

Ingredients: 300g of bitter melon, 150g of lean pork, an appropriate amount of oil and salt.

Method: Cut the bitter melon into threads and boil quickly in water to discard the bitter taste. Slice the lean pork, stir-fry in oil, then add the bitter melon threads and stir-fry together. Season and serve.

Effect: Drains liver fire.

5. Lotus Seed and Jujube Soup

Ingredients: 3g of lotus seeds, 10 jujubes.

Method: Grind the lotus seeds into powder and decoct with jujubes. Take once daily after meals.

Effect: Tonifies qi and blood, calms the heart and soothes the spirit.
