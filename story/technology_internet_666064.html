The Impact and Countermeasures of Dewatering and Excavation of Foundation Pits on Nearby Group Piles

The unloading excavation of deep foundation pits will inevitably lead to ground surface settlement and stratum movement around the pit, affecting surrounding existing buildings and even causing damage. For existing pile foundations, the influence of horizontal groundwater displacement caused by dewatering and excavation of the foundation pit is often more pronounced, potentially resulting in excessive bending and moment within the piles, leading to the failure of the upper structure's functionality. Therefore, it is meaningful to discuss the impact patterns of dewatering and excavation of foundation pits on nearby pile foundations and seek feasible deformation control measures. Currently, scholars have conducted extensive research on this issue using experimental methods [1-3] and theoretical analysis [4-8]. However, when boundary conditions are complex, soil layers are diverse, or groundwater is abundant, neither experimental nor theoretical methods can fully address the problem. A more convenient and effective method is numerical simulation. However, current simulation work [9-10] focuses only on analyzing the impact patterns of foundation pit excavation on nearby pile foundations without considering the fluid-solid coupling effect of groundwater and soil during dewatering and excavation processes. Research on the deformation control effects and rules of various engineering reinforcement measures (such as isolation piles, active zone reinforcement, passive zone reinforcement, etc.) for nearby pile foundations is still extremely rare.

This article relies on the Xunlimen Station project of Wuhan Metro Line 2, utilizing a three-dimensional numerical simulation method that considers the coupling effect of groundwater and soil to study the impact patterns of deep foundation pit dewatering and excavation on nearby light rail pile foundations. Then, it analyzes the displacement control effects of various engineering reinforcement measures and elaborates on the influence rules of passive zone reinforcement parameters on light rail pile foundation displacements.

1　Project Overview and Numerical Model

1.1　Foundation Pit Project Overview

Xunlimen Station is surrounded by dense buildings. To the east lies the elevated bridge of Light Rail Line 1, with the minimum distance between the diaphragm wall and the light rail bridge pier foundation being less than 1.5 meters. Controlling the impact of construction on the pile foundation is the top priority. The standard section width of the station is 30.5 meters. The top slab of the main structure is covered with soil 2.4 to 5.3 meters deep, and the bottom slab is buried at a depth of 20.5 meters, with the pit bottom located on a fine sand layer. Near the light rail bridge pier, the top-down construction method is adopted. The excavation section is 23.5 meters wide, and the retaining structure consists of an 0.8-meter diaphragm wall + internal supports (a total of five internal supports are set, with the second one being a steel support of Φ609 with a wall thickness of 16 mm, while the others are reinforced concrete supports). The bottom of the diaphragm wall is buried at a depth of 51 meters, entering the strongly weathered mudstone by 1.5 meters. The distribution of the site's geological layers is shown in Figure 1 of reference [11]. There are two types of groundwater: upper stagnant water and confined water. The upper stagnant water is stored in mixed fill soil, with a burial depth of 0.5 to 2.0 meters. The confined water is stored in fine sand, forming a unified confined aquifer together with the overlying silty sand. The reinforcement plan and scope for the light rail bridge pier are detailed in the relevant references.
