Currently, the increasingly severe water resource shortage in China not only troubles the national economy and people's livelihood but has also become an important factor that restricts the sustainable development of society and economy. According to statistics from the Ministry of Water Resources, since the 1990s, the scope of water shortages in Chinese cities has been continuously expanding, with the severity of shortages constantly increasing. Among more than 600 cities nationwide, 400 suffer from varying degrees of water shortages, and 110 experience severe shortages. In normal years, the total urban water shortage across the country amounts to 6 billion cubic meters. Faced with such a water-scarce situation, conserving water has become one of China’s basic national policies, and water conservation in buildings faces a heavy responsibility.

### Potential for Water Waste

#### Huge Water Waste from Over-Pressure Outflow

The rated water flow of sanitary fixtures is the amount of water that flows out per unit time at the outlet of the water supply accessories of sanitary fixtures to meet usage requirements. The outflow head is the static water pressure before the valve required to ensure the rated water flow from the water supply accessories. If the water pressure before the valve exceeds the outflow head, the water flow will exceed the rated flow, which is known as the over-pressure outflow phenomenon. Over-pressure outflow in water supply accessories not only disrupts the normal distribution of water in the water supply system and negatively affects water usage conditions but also results in wasted water without any use benefits, making it ineffective water usage. Because this waste occurs during use and is not easily noticed or recognized by people, it falls under hidden water wastage and has yet to receive sufficient attention. However, this type of hidden water wastage exists to varying degrees in all kinds of buildings, and the amount of water wasted is quite significant. To address this issue, water-saving faucets have been widely adopted in water supply systems. However, although water-saving faucets can reduce pressure and save water, if the design of the water supply system is inappropriate, the problem of over-pressure outflow cannot be effectively resolved.

#### Significant Water Waste from Hot Water Main Pipe Circulation

Water wastage in China's hot water supply systems is relatively serious. The main manifestation is that after opening the water distribution device, hot water at the desired temperature cannot be obtained promptly. Often, a considerable amount of cold water must be drained before the hot water equipment can function normally. This lost cold water, which provides no use benefits, is considered ineffective cold water and represents wasted water. For example, consider a six-story inn with a timed hot water supply system that employs main pipe circulation and has 16 risers. Assuming each riser has an average diameter of 32mm and length of 18m, and hot water is supplied once daily, then approximately 105 cubic meters of ineffective cold water are lost annually. Therefore, the cumulative annual water wastage in similar buildings across the country would also be substantial. Clearly, main pipe circulation results in the most water wastage.

#### Water Wastage Caused by Sanitary Fixtures and Distribution Accessories

Some older types of sanitary fixtures and distribution accessories waste a large amount of water. For instance, old-style high-water tank squat toilets consume up to 12 liters per flush, leading to significant water resource wastage. Particularly in the selection of flushing systems for public restrooms and municipal public toilets, improper choices can cause tremendous water resource wastage.

### Measures for Water Conservation in Buildings

#### Control Over-Pressure Outflow

Firstly, following standards and reasonably zoning the building. The vertical zoning of the water supply system in high-rise buildings should be determined based on usage requirements, material equipment performance, maintenance management, building height, etc., while utilizing the water pressure from the outdoor water supply network. The static water pressure at the lowest sanitary fixture water outlet point should ideally be 300-350kpa for residences, hotels, and hospitals, and 350-450kpa for office buildings. Secondly, it is recommended to adopt the water tank water supply method, where the pressure fluctuations at each water outlet point are minimal, which is beneficial for saving water. Thirdly, install pressure-reducing devices. Except for bathtubs with an outflow head of 5-10m, the outflow head for other water-using accessories should not exceed 5m. An inlet pressure of 10m and a minimum pressure of 5m at the most disadvantageous point can satisfy the requirements of each household. Pressure exceeding 10m will result in over-pressure outflow. Pressure can be reduced by installing pressure adjustment plates or throttling plugs before the water meter on the inlet pipe, thereby reducing the over-pressure outflow volume.

#### Selection of Hot Water Circulation Method

The causes of this problem are multifaceted, including improper construction and management as well as insufficient design. Regarding design, the choice of hot water circulation method is one of the main factors affecting the amount of ineffective cold water. Currently, three types of hot water circulation methods are proposed in the standards: main pipe circulation, riser and main pipe circulation, and branch pipe, riser, and main pipe circulation. Among these, main pipe circulation results in the most water wastage. Changing this phenomenon has become an urgent requirement for users. The branch pipe, riser, and main pipe hot water circulation method is the most water-efficient, but economic conditions currently limit its adoption in all hot water supply systems. However, for multi-story buildings with timed hot water supply systems, adopting the riser and main pipe circulation method is both necessary and feasible. It is suggested that when designing centralized hot water supply systems, the hot water circulation in main pipes and risers should be ensured, and those with economic conditions should implement branch pipe circulation.

#### Selection of Water-Saving Sanitary Fixtures and Distribution Accessories

Under the premise of reasonable use, complete functions, and aesthetic pursuit, water-saving sanitary fixtures should be selected as much as possible, especially in public restrooms where the number of users cannot be predicted. The most water-saving method is to control the water consumption after each use. Advanced water-saving options include dual-flush toilets and pneumatic flush toilets. In terms of distribution accessory selection, water-saving measures for bathtubs and washbasins mainly depend on the performance changes of water supply accessories, such as aerated or foam outlets, foot-operated showerheads, water-saving delayed-closing self-closing flush valves, etc., all of which can conserve water to varying degrees. Aerated faucets save about 25% more water compared to ordinary faucets. The primary measure for saving water in bathtubs is to install flow controllers and automatic constant temperature regulators. Constant temperature regulators can mix cold and hot water to the predetermined temperature, and once adjusted, they can automatically adapt to changes in water temperature and pressure. Municipal public toilet flushing systems can abandon traditional timed flushing and adopt photoelectric numerical control, flushing according to the number of people to reduce water wastage.

As a pillar industry driving China's economic development, the construction industry is developing rapidly. With the improvement in people's quality of life, the demand for water quantity and quality is continuously increasing. At the same time, implementing sustainable water utilization and protection to prevent water resources from being damaged and entering a benign cycle of water quality and quantity regeneration poses many new requirements for building water supply and drainage design. The pace of advancing water supply technology urgently needs to accelerate. Simultaneously, people's habits of wasting water need to change, enhancing their awareness of water conservation. People should regard fresh water resources as precious resources, advocate their conservation, and call for nationwide water savings.
