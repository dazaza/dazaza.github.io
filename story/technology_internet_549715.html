Unicom Introduces Apple iPhone News Manufactured by Jobs

by szjqd510 on 2011-11-11 15:11:10

By Mao Qiying: Currently, the rumors of Apple's iPhone teaming up with Unicom are rampant in the industry. Reports suggest that Unicom has reached an agreement with Apple, and the iPhone could enter the domestic market as early as late August. To enter the domestic market, Apple might change its carrier revenue-sharing model used in the U.S., shifting to a more common domestic model where carriers bulk purchase the phones and provide subsidies to consumers. Is it because negotiations with China Mobile fell through? The "true feelings" between the iPhone and Unicom were debunked by a related Unicom official as "nonsense," shattering Apple's dream of entering China in August.

Previously, Jobs cited two reasons for Apple's confidence in achieving its goals: launching a 3G phone and offering a lower price. Apple hopes these two factors will boost sales not only in the American market but also in overseas markets where the iPhone is already available. Apple stated that a total of 70 countries will officially carry the iPhone, including new additions like Canada and Australia, and that the iPhone will eventually land in China.

This news was dismissed yesterday by a related Unicom official as "pure nonsense." The official said that since the restructuring of China Telecom in August has not been completed yet, the operator structure and the situation regarding the issuance of 3G licenses remain unclear. Therefore, Unicom cannot possibly be in contact with Apple at this time to attempt introducing the iPhone.

Previously, Li Zhengmao, the former vice president of Unicom, had expressed hope in negotiating with Apple to introduce the iPhone, but following the telecom restructuring, Li was transferred to China Mobile as vice president. To date, the main reason why the Apple iPhone has failed to enter China lies in China Mobile's refusal of Apple's demand for a revenue-sharing model, insisting that Apple follow the procurement rules set by China Mobile. As a result, Apple and China Mobile have been unable to reach an agreement.

Despite Apple revealing that since its launch in June last year, the iPhone has sold 6 million units worldwide, the Chinese agent channels do not seem optimistic about the Apple iPhone. "The current business model of the iPhone is clearly unsuitable for the Chinese market," according to a consensus among Chinese telecommunications experts.

Previously, there were rumors that Wang Jianzhu, Chairman and CEO of China Mobile, had mentioned ongoing negotiations with Apple to introduce the iPhone. Although China's major operators "look forward to" Apple, based on feedback from high-level executives at both Mobile and Unicom, neither has any substantial plans to introduce the Apple iPhone.

Currently, China's telecommunications industry is busy with restructuring, making the timing for negotiations immature.

At present, the primary way for the iPhone to enter the mainland Chinese market is through gray market channels, with prices reaching as high as 4000 yuan. Some individual agents specializing in the iPhone conduct jailbreaking operations. An operator insider analyzed, "For Apple, breaking into the Chinese market is absolutely no easy task." Furthermore, the successful landing of the iPhone in China in August was merely Jobs' wishful thinking.
