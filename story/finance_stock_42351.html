Now maybe the hottest one is gold futures.

by jiangushi on 2008-01-10 21:13:31

I don't know if you have noticed, but the hottest thing right now might be gold futures.

All seven contracts opened at the limit-up price, with a trading volume of 27.347 billion yuan on the first day, exceeding one-third of the trading volume of the Shenzhen Stock Exchange on the same day. This was the fiery performance of the gold futures listed on January 9th at the Shanghai Futures Exchange (hereinafter referred to as SHFE).

Among the seven contracts, the most actively traded was Au0806 (the contract expiring in June 2008). In the first 21 minutes after opening, about 21 tons of gold were traded, averaging 1 ton per minute. The opening price of this contract was 230.95 yuan per gram, which was 9.98% higher than the benchmark price of 209.99 yuan per gram. After opening, the contract price continued to rise to the highest point of the day at 230.99 yuan, then began to fall back. The lowest price of the day was 221.88 yuan, closing at 223.30 yuan, and settling at 224.74 yuan.

Affected by the news of the listing of China's gold futures, the international gold price surged nearly 30 dollars on January 8th, touching a historical high of 880 dollars per ounce during the session. On the 9th, the opening price of gold futures had exceeded 890 dollars per ounce, higher than the foreign market, creating an arbitrage opportunity between the international and Chinese markets.

Futures industry participants at the gold futures listing ceremony of the SHFE generally believed that the opening price was too high, mainly due to two reasons: one was the competition for the first order at opening; the other was the stimulation brought by yesterday's sharp increase in the international gold price. They expected that the price would gradually return to a reasonable level.

On the first day of the gold futures listing, all seven contracts together traded 121,500 lots, with open interest of 21,800 lots, and a trading amount of 27.347 billion yuan. Industry insiders stated that compared to other domestic trading varieties, gold futures could be considered very active, but still had a gap compared to major international exchanges.

Jinyuan Futures General Manager Chen Zhiquing believed that given the recent huge fluctuations in the gold price, there was no need to worry about the market's activity. He predicted that the trading volume of gold futures would surpass zinc futures, becoming the second-largest commodity futures trading variety in China, only behind copper futures.

Yide Futures analyst Qiu Hanyi believed that the trading scale on that day was around 120 tons, which should be considered very active for a newly listed variety. Compared to other domestic futures varieties, it showed that investors had a high enthusiasm for participating in gold futures. Currently, the number of investors in the spot gold market has exceeded one million. Even if only some of these investors switch to futures, there is still potential for the futures market. Moreover, a legitimate financial derivatives market is a necessary complement to the spot market, playing a positive role in protecting investor interests and combating underground gold trading.

However, the scale of China's gold futures market still lags behind foreign gold futures markets. According to Qiu Hanyi, comparing with the trading situation of Comex, the world's largest gold futures market, on January 8th, the trading volume of China's gold futures market on the 9th was close to one-third of Comex's trading volume on the 8th, and the open interest was close to one percent of Comex's. In addition, the trading volume of the Tokyo Stock Exchange, Asia's main gold futures market, surged on the 9th, with the total market trading volume approaching 170,000 lots, and the trading volume of the main contract increased by nearly 50% compared to the 8th.

Domestic gold production and processing enterprises welcomed the listing of gold futures. Shi Min, chairman of Shandong Gold Mining Co., Ltd., one of the first self-operated members of gold futures, said that the gold price was currently in a trend of fluctuating upward. When the gold price rises to 900 dollars per ounce, they would consider going short in the futures market. At the same time, they would also conduct cross-market arbitrage in the foreign market, the Shanghai Gold Exchange, and the SHFE.

Wang Chiwei, vice general manager of Jiangxi Copper Corporation (hereinafter referred to as JCC), said that JCC needs to import more than one million tons of copper-gold ore every year, which contains a large amount of gold. From the loading of copper-gold ore imported from South America to the production of finished gold products, the cycle is approximately four months, during which the gold price may undergo significant changes. However, the processing fee the company receives is only five dollars per ounce. If the market risks cannot be effectively hedged, the gold processing fee may not even cover the losses caused by the change in gold prices. JCC once applied to hedge gold in the foreign market but was not approved by the Ministry of Commerce. Wang Chiwei said that the listing of gold futures provided hedging tools for non-ferrous metal production and processing enterprises, thus enhancing the international competitiveness of Chinese enterprises.

To control risks, the SHFE increased the trading unit from 300 grams per lot in the draft of the "Shanghai Futures Exchange Gold Futures Standard Contract" to 1000 grams per lot. Under normal circumstances, the minimum trading margin is 7% of the contract value. On the first day of listing, the minimum margin was temporarily raised to 9% of the contract value. Most futures brokerage companies added three to five percentage points on top of the 9%, while some futures companies charged a margin at a standard of 15%. Therefore, the investment threshold of over 20,000 yuan per lot excluded some investors.

The SHFE explained that increasing the trading unit from 300 grams per lot to 1000 grams per lot aimed to discourage investors with insufficient risk-bearing capacity from participating in gold futures trading, but facilitate institutional investors' participation, and help align with the international and domestic markets. Currently, the majority of international main futures exchanges use 1000 grams per lot as the unit for gold futures contracts.

The SHFE also excluded individual investors from entering the delivery process of gold futures. According to the newly revised "Risk Control Management Measures of the Shanghai Futures Exchange," after entering the delivery month, natural person investors' positions in the gold futures contract should be 0 lots. The SHFE explained that the main functions of the futures market are price discovery and hedging, and physical delivery is not the primary purpose of futures trading. If natural person clients fail to adjust their positions to 0 lots according to regulations when entering the delivery month, the exchange will enforce forced liquidation. If natural person clients indeed need to purchase spot gold, they can realize it through channels such as banks or the spot market.

General Manager Ouyang Yong of Shandong Zhaojin Precious Metals Refining Co., Ltd., believed that although GJ still strictly controls gold, due to its dual nature as both a financial instrument and a metal, its attraction to individual investors cannot be underestimated. "China's folk tradition has always been to 'hide gold,' so it is inevitable that individual investors will eventually enter the delivery process," he said.
