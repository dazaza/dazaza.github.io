Landscape Gardening

In a certain area, using engineering technology and artistic means, through the transformation of terrain (or further mountain building, stone stacking, water management), tree planting, flower planting, building construction and garden road layout, etc., to create a beautiful natural environment and recreational realm, which is called a garden. Gardens include courtyards, residential gardens, small parks, gardens, parks, botanical gardens, zoos, etc. With the development of landscape disciplines, it also includes forest parks, scenic spots, nature reserves or national parks' tourist areas, as well as health resorts.

Name: In history, recreational realms have used different names due to their different content and forms. In the Yin and Zhou periods of China and in Assyria in Western Asia, the realm for raising animals for hunting and recreation was called "you" and "hunting park". During the Qin and Han dynasties in China, the realm for imperial recreation was called "yuan" or "palace garden"; those belonging to government offices or private individuals were called "garden", "garden pool", "residential garden", "villa", etc. The term "landscape gardening" appeared in poems and writings after the Western Jin Dynasty, such as in Zhang Han's "Miscellaneous Poems" from the Western Jin Dynasty, there is a line "In late spring, harmony responds, the white sun shines on the garden"; Yang Xuanzhi of the Northern Wei Dynasty commented on the residence of Zhang Lun, the Minister of Agriculture, saying, "The beauty of the mountains, pools, and gardens surpasses that of all the princes." After the Tang and Song Dynasties, the application of the term "landscape gardening" became more widespread, often used to generally refer to all kinds of recreational realms mentioned above.

Function: According to modern understanding, landscape gardening is not only for recreation but also has the function of protecting and improving the environment. Plants can absorb carbon dioxide, release oxygen, purify the air; they can absorb harmful gases and adsorb dust to a certain extent, reducing pollution; they can regulate the temperature and humidity of the air, improve the microclimate; they also have protective effects such as noise reduction, wind prevention, and fire prevention. More importantly, landscape gardening has beneficial psychological and spiritual effects. Recreational activities in beautifully scenic and quiet gardens help eliminate tension and fatigue brought about by long-term work, allowing mental and physical strength to be restored. Cultural, recreational, sports, and popular science education activities in gardens can enrich knowledge and充实spiritual life.

From the perspective of layout methods, gardens can be divided into three categories:

Formal style: Representatives are Western gardens, represented countries Italian palaces and Chinese royal gardens.

Natural style: Represented by China's private gardens, Suzhou gardens, Lingnan gardens Mixed style Modern architecture Formal and natural styles combined

Development method: From the perspective of development methods, gardens can be divided into two categories:

One type is to use the original natural scenery, remove the redundant and organize the chaos, repair and develop, open up paths, arrange garden buildings, and form natural gardens without artificial effort. For example, Wang Wei's Wangchuan Villa during the Tang Dynasty was built in a naturally picturesque valley with mountains, forests, and lakes, which can be called a mountain villa; places like Zhangjiajie in Dayong County, Hunan Province, Jiuzhaigou in Songpan County, Sichuan Province, large-scale natural areas with beautiful scenery, slightly developed and utilized, are called natural scenic areas; places like Mount Tai, Mount Huang, Wuyi Mountain, etc., with a long history of development, containing cultural relics, mythological legends, religious art, etc., are called scenic spots.

Another type is artificial gardens, i.e., environments created within a certain geographic range to improve ecology, beautify the environment, meet recreational and cultural life needs, such as small parks, gardens, public parks, etc.
