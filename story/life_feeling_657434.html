Things have changed, and love has been lost

by lili1 on 2012-01-08 22:21:34

Things have changed, and love has been lost.

When the memory that has passed away will not be replayed again, when the long river of years flows eastward, will the once profound pictures still remain? Things have changed, and love has been lost. Yesterday still appears pure and charming. If there is a lack of past laughter, will I spend my whole life enjoying this loneliness?

As the year draws to a close, only that unchanging melody lingers in the deep autumn season. It blooms with its unique desolation at midnight, dissipating the remaining warmth of the autumn cold, swaying in a melancholy dance, making this night even more beautiful. And so, slowly... one night turns into dawn. When I wake up, I find myself lying in an unmanned forest path. The morning dew drops onto my half-asleep, half-awake eyes. A drop of morning dew slides lightly over my eyes, the clear dew resembling my crystalline tears, always piercing my heart during this barren season. Perhaps I am tired, weary of the disputes of this world, yet I must continue moving forward with my exhausted body. Maybe death is the final destination.

How much I envy the innocent memories of my childhood! Those bygone smiles are still deeply hidden in my cold heart, always surfacing during lonely nights as I revisit memories buried in the passage of time. Is it the silent passage of time, or the heartless flow of years, leaving too many fragments for us to regret. The withered leaves carried by the wind are like a journey far away in our youth. When the dust settles, the years that have reached their end have completed their mission. The ending forgets all the sorrowful and joyful scenes of the past. We keep rewriting, rewriting our own roles. Some cry, some laugh, and some, in the end, experience just a fleeting moment of ease.

In the songs, whose notes stir up the beautiful melody? In the sadness, who accompanies the parting song with feelings after pain? The sorrowful parting song is truly beautiful, beautiful enough to make one drunk. My heart breaks alone, walking steadily towards the hidden memories, never allowing anyone to understand this implicit sadness. In the ocean of memories, I seem to see my footprints from the past, appearing so carefree and unrestrained. Flowers bloom on both banks, and the seasons cycle yearly. But now, the years weigh heavily on my weary body and mind, how can I forget? What was supposed to be a year's cycle seems to have changed all the fragrances, losing the dreams I desired.

Thus, thus; I walk under the midnight streetlights, my shadow slightly showing signs of desolation. Ahead lies my ceaseless progress. When everything reaches the end, I realize that yesterday's laughter has all been washed away by time on the return journey. After the years pass, they leave behind a yellowish hue, narrating my past joy, commemorating my subsequent沧桑(cangsang - weathered/careworn appearance).

If one person performs this monologue of the years, then I would also fully recount the vastness of my life!
