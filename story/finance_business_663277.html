Data Shooting Points Examination Debate Record On February 11, Beijing time, the Knicks faced the Los Angeles Lakers at Madison Square Garden. Jeremy Lin continued his scorching performance, with various break-throughs that left people in awe and dismantled the entire Lakers team. In the end, he scored 38 points alone, helping the Knicks win 92-85 at home.

In the first quarter, Lin's 10 points and 3 assists left a deep impression. Lin's first three-point attempt was impressive. After two unsuccessful attempts to break through, he passed the ball to Jeffries at the high position. Jeffries quickly returned the ball to Lin at the left corner. With just seconds left on the shot clock, Lin took a shot over Bynum's defense, scoring cleanly! Lin then played smoothly. Halfway through the quarter, Lin forced World Peace into a turnover during a small confrontation near mid-court. After getting the ball, Lin charged forward. Just as people thought he would finish with a dunk, he chose a light layup instead. This basket helped the Knicks unleash a 13-2 wave, and all these scores were related to Lin.

At the start of the second quarter, Lin began challenging the Lakers' towering interior. Although his first attempt was blocked by Bynum, he soon used his sharp breakthrough to successfully pass Blake on the right side, pinning him behind and using his excellent rhythm to break through under the basket. Facing Goodlock, he scored with a difficult fadeaway jump shot and drew a foul, completing a 2+1. Later in the quarter, the game seemed to enter a battle between Lin and veteran Fisher. Fisher's assist and mid-range jump shot once narrowed the Lakers' deficit to 38-45. However, with 3 minutes and 8 seconds left before halftime, Lin started playing around Fisher. After an unsuccessful breakthrough against Fisher, Lin had to stop the ball. Unexpectedly, Lin suddenly faked a move to the right while facing away from the basket, leaving Fisher momentarily flustered. Soon after, Lin executed a perfect left-turn fadeaway jump shot, the ball swishing cleanly through the net, drawing cheers from the entire arena. Less than half a minute later, Lin grabbed the defensive rebound and advanced to the frontcourt. More surprisingly, Lin almost immediately chose to challenge Fisher again. Ultimately, Lin used a large backspin to completely shake off Fisher. In desperation, Fisher even pulled Lin from behind. Facing Gasol, Lin completed a perfect layup — the lead widened to 9 points again, forcing Mike Brown to call another timeout due to Lin's two scores.

In the third quarter, Lin's breakthrough remained the biggest headache for the Lakers. Three minutes before the end of the quarter, as the Lakers gradually narrowed the gap, Lin chose to break through Blake from the baseline. Although he couldn't completely shake off Blake's defense, outside the reasonable contact zone, Lin used his good physical strength to push Blake aside and hit the board with his body about to lose balance.

In the critical fourth quarter, Lin was still the one haunting the Lakers. His three-pointer from the corner gave the Knicks a 13-point lead. Afterwards, his breakthrough left Barnes undecided. Entering the paint, he avoided Gasol's block inside and used a reverse layup to score, helping the Knicks maintain a 10-point lead. With 41.5 seconds remaining, his excellent positioning in the paint drew a charging foul on Barnes as he drove to the basket for a layup. (Sohu Sports Yi Chong)
