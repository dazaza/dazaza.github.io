Customer application is very critical, domestic software is a must.

by haochenruanjian on 2008-11-21 14:57:52

—— Record of the National Software Users Alliance Roadshow: Nearly Half of the Members Exceed Ten Thousand

The national software users alliance roadshow was grandly launched in Chengdu on September 18. After stops in Fuzhou, Guangzhou, Lanzhou, Xi'an, and Shenyang, it will soon continue fervently in Kunshan, Jinan, Nanjing, and other locations.

Looking back at the sites we've visited, we are astonished to find that over ten thousand users have applied to join the national software users alliance. What particularly moves us are their heartfelt blessings for domestic software and the national software users alliance. "Support domestic software, trust Chinese manufacturers," "You support users, users support you," "A wise populace leads to a wealthy nation; protect the intellectual property industry"... Each blessing, every word of support, fills us with emotion, moistens our eyes, and makes us feel the weight of our responsibilities. The users aren't just supporting us Haochen; they're supporting the entire domestic software industry. As one of the leaders in domestic CAD software, Haochen Software bears a heavy yet glorious historical responsibility for the development of the industry.

We know that there are many "alliances" domestically, but very few succeed, with most eventually becoming mere tools for publicity. So, what is the charm of the national software users alliance that has captured the hearts of Chinese users? This question isn't a secret to Liang Haixia, the executive vice president of Haochen Software: "Thought determines path." She said:

What is the core issue of the domestic software industry?

First, we need to understand what the core issue of the development of the domestic software industry is. The core issue isn't how to make customers buy domestic software, but rather how to make them willing and accustomed to using domestic software—application is the key! The dominance of foreign software and the rampant piracy have resulted in insufficient mature applications of domestic software. When we initiated the establishment of the national software users alliance earlier this year, there were no precedents within the industry. In the past, software companies formed alliances to sell more software, but we believe that the purpose of such alliances wasn't to focus on customer application but rather more of a sales strategy aimed at selling software. Moreover, this model has at least the following defects in promoting customer use of domestic software:

1. This method doesn't prioritize the customer, studying how to make customers adopt domestic software, but instead combines products from different software manufacturers into some kind of solution. Compatibility between solutions, synchronized upgrades of products from different manufacturers, and whether these meet the actual needs of customers are major challenges;

2. Software company alliances are inevitably exclusive, leaving customers without sufficient choice.

Additionally, successful industry enterprise alliances are usually formed by manufacturers that can approach monopoly status or become de facto industry standards. Given the current situation where foreign CAD software dominates the market, we believe that forming a software company alliance would be easy to operate initially but difficult for customers to accept because customer needs are diverse. Limiting choices to one option would restrict the development of the alliance, leading to an increasingly narrow path.

Therefore, we believe that to solve the core issue of domestic software application, we must focus on the customer, study the problems customers face when applying domestic software, establish a service-oriented body to gradually resolve these issues, and truly give users confidence and peace of mind regarding domestic software!

Supporting users to better apply domestic software

At the beginning of the year, due to the large number and good application of Haochen CAD users, the China Investigation and Design Association established the "Haochen Software User Liaison Meeting," which remains the only secondary institution set up by the China Investigation and Design Association so far. At the founding conference of the "China Investigation and Design Association Haochen User Liaison Meeting," many customers expressed earnest expectations for Haochen while also sharing their concerns: "It's not that we don't want to use domestic software, but we are used to foreign software. Is the transition easy? Can the copyright issues be resolved?" It was then that we realized clearly: since domestic software came later, besides continuous improvements in products and services by software providers, additional transition services are required. These transition services should be provided by a permanent organization that brings together users, relevant government departments, industry associations, experts, and legal professionals to jointly create a better policy environment for the application of domestic software, provide technical services, offer legal support for corporate copyright issues, and promote awareness of copyright policies and laws. We communicated this idea with representatives from the domestic software community and received strong resonance, exceeding our expectations. Outstanding domestic software representatives like Kingsoft and Yongzhong, well-known construction industry software manufacturers like Lisheng, and domestic software spokespersons like Academician Ni Guangnan all highly endorsed the principle of "supporting users to better apply domestic software." When we communicated this with user representatives such as Beijing Urban Construction Institute, Beijing Ketic Design Institute, Siemens, and Schneider Electric, we also received a warm welcome. These positive responses strengthened our confidence, making us firmly believe from the start that we were on the right path—a broadening highway.

When rivers are full, streams overflow; domestic software is a family

Some users expressed concern about the current competition among domestic CAD software companies: "I heard about another alliance. What’s the difference between the two? Will fierce competition benefit foreign manufacturers?" Liang Haixia stated: The purposes behind the formation of alliances differ; some aim at serving sales superficially, others focus on long-term customer application pragmatically. Haochen's goal is to enable customers to use software effectively, planning for long-term service to industry clients. Therefore, our alliance is open. As long as customers need it, any domestic software can join to serve customers. Domestic software enterprises encounter many similar issues during development. The strategic priority for the current domestic CAD industry is not competition between domestic softwares but how to enhance its own capabilities to compete with multinational corporations. Foreign giants are financially robust, having operated in China for over two decades, while domestic CAD software vendors remain relatively weak. Even if all domestic CAD software vendors unite, they might only achieve parity, let alone competing individually. The six states fell to Qin due to internal strife over minor gains, and Wu and Shu were swallowed by Wei due to fighting over Jing Province. We must identify the primary and secondary contradictions, formulate correct strategies, and avoid undermining the overall development of domestic software for short-term gains. Therefore, from the outset, the national software users alliance established principles of openness and equality, charging no fees, making no exclusivity or non-compete demands, uniting all possible forces (users, government, associations, manufacturers, experts) to revitalize domestic software. Wherever the roadshow went, it received strong support from local copyright offices, associations, and experts. Some secondary developers are launching versions compatible with Haochen CAD, and especially, the enthusiasm of users has further strengthened our confidence!"
