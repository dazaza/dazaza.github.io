by sxykmgy on 2012-02-18 10:14:32

What are the treatment drugs for allergic vasculitis? Allergic vasculitis is an immune system disease involving the skin, which usually occurs in young and middle-aged adults. Generally, symptoms such as fatigue, joint and muscle pain may occur, and in a few cases, there may be irregular fever, but these symptoms may also be absent. The damage to the skin caused by allergic vasculitis can take multiple forms, including macules, nodules, purpura, papules, necrosis, and ulcers.

Due to severe inflammatory reactions, bullae, necrosis, and ulcers may occur on purpura and papulopurpuric lesions, or painful ulcers may develop. These symptoms are more obvious in the afternoon, accompanied by weakness and soreness in the lower limbs.

The skin lesions of allergic vasculitis vary, but almost all include purpura or nodules. There may be spontaneous pain, itching, or burning sensation, while some patients have no spontaneous symptoms but experience tenderness upon pressure. After healing, the lesions leave pigmentation, and if there are ulcers, atrophic scars may form after healing. Lesions appear in batches, distributed widely, accompanied by edema in the lower legs, indicating a more severe condition. Chronic lesions may recur and last for months or even years.

Regarding the treatment principles and plans for allergic vasculitis, Shanxi Yikang Peripheral Vasculitis Hospital provides the following opinions and suggestions, including treatment plans, to help patients accurately understand their conditions and choose appropriate treatment options:

1. Since patients often experience fatigue, myalgia, arthralgia, and swelling in the lower limbs, rest is essential. Severe cases should involve bed rest with the affected limb elevated to reduce the impact of venous water pressure on the lesion.

2. Removing the cause to reduce antigen sources, eliminating infection foci such as viral or bacterial infections, stopping the use of allergenic drugs and heterologous proteins.

3. If there is an upper respiratory tract infection, antibiotics can be used for treatment.

4. To prevent antibody production, reduce the formation of immune complexes, and reduce inflammatory reactions, it is appropriate to take hormones.

5. Colchicine may inhibit leukocyte chemotaxis factors, inflammation, and stabilize lysosomal enzymes.

6. Indomethacin has an inhibitory effect on prostaglandin synthesis.

7. To improve vascular function, vitamin E and vitamin C can be taken three times daily.

8. Traditional Chinese medicine should promote blood circulation and remove blood stasis, clear heat, and detoxify. Compound Danshen tablets or Fuchun tablets can be used.
