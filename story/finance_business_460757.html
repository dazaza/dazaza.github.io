2. Paipai

Tencent Paipai is Tencent's e-commerce trading platform, which officially began operations in March 2006. Relying on Tencent QQ's massive user base and 350 million active users, Paipai already has a good development foundation. As an important part of Tencent's online life strategy, Paipai was positioned from its inception as a popularizer and innovator of Chinese e-commerce, aiming to promote the universal adoption and development of e-commerce in China. However, due to insufficient promotion and late start, it has never grown significantly, despite its large scale, but without a noticeable increase in market share.

Based on Tencent QQ and Tencent's other business advantages, Paipai has introduced many innovations in management concepts and product technologies, such as the industry-first developed simultaneous chatting and buying feature, and the separate credit system for buyers and sellers, which have been widely recognized and adopted in the industry.

Official Website: http://www.paipai.com

3. EachNet

EachNet was jointly established in December 2006 by eBay, the world's largest e-commerce company, and TOM Online, a leading domestic portal site and wireless internet company. It is the oldest surviving C2C website but also the least satisfactory in development due to cultural differences between China and foreign countries.

EachNet's mission is to help almost anyone conduct any transaction anywhere. It not only provides sellers with a stage for online entrepreneurship and self-actualization but also offers buyers a wide range of high-quality, affordable goods for a new shopping experience.

Official Website: http://www.eachnet.com
