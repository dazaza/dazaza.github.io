Understanding Hyperthyroidism Symptoms and Timely Treatment for Hyperthyroidism

Keywords: hyperthyroidism, hyperthyroidism treatment, hyperthyroidism symptoms, can hyperthyroidism be cured, what should not be eaten with hyperthyroidism, hyperthyroidism treatment, hypothyroidism symptoms

When it comes to hyperthyroidism, we are not unfamiliar with it. However, for the hyperthyroid crisis, most people are not very familiar with it or may have never even heard of it. In fact, a hyperthyroid crisis refers to a situation where hyperthyroidism remains untreated for a long time, leading to a series of complications. It is very important to treat hyperthyroidism in a timely manner to prevent the occurrence of a hyperthyroid crisis. This is because a hyperthyroid crisis is extremely dangerous and can threaten the patient's life. Below, let us understand the hyperthyroid crisis.

The exact mechanism of the occurrence of a hyperthyroid crisis is still not fully understood. It often occurs when hyperthyroidism has not been treated or has not yet been controlled after treatment, and certain stress factors lead to the occurrence of a hyperthyroid crisis. Under stressful conditions, the thyroid gland synthesizes large amounts of thyroid hormones and releases them into the bloodstream, causing the original hyperthyroid condition to worsen drastically. Stress can also significantly enhance the activity of catecholamines, further worsening the condition.

A hyperthyroid crisis represents a sharp deterioration of hyperthyroidism, leading to severe metabolic disorders throughout the body and serious dysfunction of the cardiovascular system, digestive system, and nervous system. It often endangers life, and if diagnosis and rescue measures are not timely, the mortality rate is extremely high. Even with timely diagnosis and treatment, about 5-15% of patients may not survive thyroiditis. Common clinical manifestations include "four major ones and one change": 1. High fever, often exceeding 39 degrees Celsius; 2. Profuse sweating followed by anhidrosis; 3. Frequent vomiting and diarrhea, often up to 8 times a day; 4. Increased heart rate, often exceeding 140 beats per minute, often accompanied by arrhythmia. "One change" refers to changes in the nervous system, including changes in consciousness, leading to coma. Severe cases can endanger life. These are common manifestations of a hyperthyroid crisis.

Receiving reasonable and effective treatment during the early stages of the disease can reduce the occurrence of hyperthyroid crises and effectively control the condition. Some patients, due to lack of timely treatment or ineffective control during treatment, may develop a hyperthyroid crisis, which is very unfavorable for treatment. If hyperthyroidism is detected and treated early, it can be cured. Patients should not worry excessively.
