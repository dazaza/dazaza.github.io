Lost Love

Fifteen years old, the season of flowers, we were in the same class and became classmates. Back then, you sat in the front row, and I sat in the back row, with no interaction at all. Once, when we were putting on a class program, it brought us together, and we got to know each other. Perhaps from that moment, we developed a liking for each other, which lasted throughout our three years of high school.

We promised to study hard together, and after finishing university, we would definitely be together, even if we couldn't attend the same university. In the end, I went to University A, and you repeated a year and went to University B. The long-distance relationship was tedious, but we were both very determined. Many times, you made me feel moved by your attention to small details, always letting me feel warmth and love; as for me, I didn't cherish it enough.

During the four years of our ivory tower life, we had our disagreements and even talked about breaking up, but in the end, either you sincerely admitted your mistakes or I shamelessly clung to you. Regardless of how things turned out, our feelings were always good.

Dear, please forgive me for describing our seven-year relationship in such a short passage, because I don't dare to recall it. Because I know that I still deeply remember everything, whether it's happiness, joy, sadness, or sorrow...I remember them all vividly.

After graduation, after working for a year, we separated. At first, I always blamed reality issues for causing conflicts in our lives, but now, I no longer complain. Dear, you are a good woman. Even though we said we loved each other back then, we were only eighteen years old, but you already made me realize that you weren't just a little girl; you gave me a sense of security. But I wasn't good enough to you, making you sad and cry time and again. Finally, you chose to leave. At that time, I wanted so much to keep you, but you were resolute, and later on, I truly understood what it means to value something only after losing it. Before parting, you blessed me, hoping that I would mature and treat my next girlfriend well. Do you know that three years later, thinking about these things makes me feel so upset? The one thing I regret is not treating you well.

It has been three years since we parted. Classmates told me that you went to Beijing. That day, upon hearing this news, I went drinking with friends after work. For a while, I used to drink every night with my colleagues, but for different reasons. He liked drinking, while I just wanted to get drunk enough to fall asleep, so we ended up together. I chose occasional decadence, but it was never about falling into despair or irresponsibility. I knew that you had told me that a man must have a sense of responsibility, and I understood that. So, sometimes I just wanted to indulge in decadence before starting anew. This way, I passed nearly three years: refusing blind dates introduced by family, not attending friends' weddings, and skipping class reunions... I somewhat enjoyed saying no and started to become addicted to solitude.

Now, I should say that I do have a girlfriend, who treats me quite well right now. I also understand that there can be no comparison, because in any aspect, she doesn't match up to you or even half of what you meant to me. But we are still together. You know, in the past three months, we've had minor arguments every half month and major ones every month. It's a common occurrence. I even told her outright that I don't feel loved at all. Do you know what she said? She admitted it was her fault, that she hadn't taken our relationship seriously and that she hadn't treated me well. What's the point of saying those things? I don't need those words, so I said she simply doesn't understand. But we're still together because now she seems to be changing a bit, so I think, regardless of anything, I should bravely try once more. After we broke up, I didn't even consider love anymore. I planned to just let someone be introduced when I got older and under family pressure. If it weren't for her appearance stirring some impulse in me to want to be together again, I would certainly still be indulging in the pleasure of watching horror movies.

Maybe we'll get married next year, who knows? My best friend said he can't understand why I would choose her because, in the eyes of friends and colleagues alike, I've chosen the worst one if I'm not aiming for the best. But now, I have indeed chosen her, and regardless of what others say, I will stick to my decision. I think I shouldn't dwell on the past because I've asked her about hers. I believe that mutual honesty about our pasts allows us to better face the future. However, after learning about her past, I felt great pain. She made me feel insecure, without any sense of safety, not even knowing why such a man deserved her sacrifices. Haha, maybe it's just my problem. I am a rather traditional man, or perhaps I'm just out of step with the rhythm of the times. At that moment, I retreated, feeling cold inside. She said I am the only man who has been sincere to her, yet now, I am treated this way by her. Sometimes, I think about it and find myself quite laughable.

In fact, from the three years after you left until now, my behavior has often been inexplicable in the eyes of my friends. Perhaps they see it as self-harm, although it hasn't reached the point of using knives or weapons. Ha ha...

Dear, saying that my feelings for you have never changed does not mean I am being irresponsible to the person I am with now. It's just that you remain in my heart, intertwined with my guilt towards you. Dear, I should be more mature now. Since then, I've even completely changed my style of dress. Colleagues say I dress like a thirty-year-old man, but I'm still a bit younger than that. I understand that all these changes are just superficial. True maturity comes from within.

On a cloudy day, listening to Karen Mok's "If There Were No You," reminiscing about the past - QJT, Base Camp, the mornings I woke up early to make breakfast for you during your repeat year, playing games together...so many memories immerse me, and I can still tear up. Thank you, dear, for spending the most beautiful years of your life with me, helping me through the darkest period of my life.
