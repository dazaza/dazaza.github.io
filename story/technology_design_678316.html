Science sometimes finds it hard to tell right from wrong... A withered tree bursts into new buds after a year.

by daoxiao1768 on 2012-02-27 13:31:49

The atemoya tree planted in the early spring of 2008... is the most beautifully shaped tree in the yard... when I bought it, it was probably about seven or eight years old... its main branches were clearly carefully pruned and selected... Unfortunately, after being tormented by dust and wastewater from renovation for several months, it lost all its leaves last summer... everyone said it could be cut down and used as firewood...

At the beginning of this year, I consulted a gardening expert... he said that the roots had been soaked and rotted... if not too serious, we could dig it up, dry it out, disinfect it, and replant it... if serious, we would have to drastically prune it, leaving only two or three main trunks (we can even give it an IV drip), waiting for the vitality of the tree to recover before sprouting new buds and slowly nurturing it again... But we are not gardening experts... cutting everything off, even if it regrows buds, it will take at least three to five years to restore to its current form... It's not that we don't have time... but we really don't have the skills to calculate life based on one's birth date...

The lazy person's decision was to transplant it to a cooler corner of the wall and observe it further... then "rooting water", "root power" and various other solutions were poured and irrigated bucket by bucket and can by can... Initially, I could persist for a few weeks... but gradually, I forgot and neglected it... only watering it routinely... also relying on pre-laid pipelines...

The other day, I found that the small guava tree in the front yard finally had completely dried branches... though reluctant, I cut it branch by branch, from the small ones to the big ones to the main trunk... indeed, from the bark to the core, it was completely dried and could be used as firewood... The small spot left vacant, I'll see if I can find a plum tree or something to fill in later... or just transplant the "fragrant olive" there...

After finishing cutting this one, I casually cut a dead branch from the perfectly shaped atemoya tree in the back yard... who knew that where the knife fell, the tree bark oozed water (not recent rainwater)... the bark and core still exuded the leaf oil scent of life... Quickly, I thoroughly cleaned all the smallest dead branches on the entire tree... those that were completely dry were broken off, and those with flexibility were carefully examined... Congratulations to myself... The neglected dead tree for almost a year has already shown tiny green buds on its fine branches... The vitality of plants is absolutely incomparable to animals... humans can also learn from plants' survival methods... not fearing adverse environments... although their roots are planted in dark soil... every green leaf proudly faces the light...

I look forward to these few green buds restarting the life mechanism of the whole plant... The water evaporation from the leaf surface will drive the long-absent water flow and absorption in the tree trunk and roots... The photosynthesis of the leaf surface will inject new energy into the entire plant... Maybe it won't flower or bear fruit soon... but life itself is a kind of meaning... to live proudly... to live happily...
