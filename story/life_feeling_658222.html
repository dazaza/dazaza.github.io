I hear the wind coming from the sea of humanity...

In the book of youth, we are on the same line. The quilt is the grave of youth. Regarding our teenage years, warmth and coldness are only known to oneself; the simplest life with the farthest dreams. All these will be abstracted into some mist-like dust under the arbitrary rewriting of memories, floating in the empty dark outside dreams, falling day and night, eventually settling down. In the depth of our hopes and desires, there hides a tacit understanding of youth. Like seeds quietly dreaming under the snow. So you need to know, I will return in greater silence. Youth is the warmest quilt of life, the most splendid skirt tail of existence, the wind blowing through emptiness. Time passes like this. And time will continue to pass like this. I don't know if stability hides disappointment, or if disappointment ultimately contains stability. But we can't find it. Looking at the distant mountains, I hear the wind coming from the sea of humanity...

Listening to sorrow at night, using moonlight to see longing!

I hear the wind coming from the sea of humanity... Walking on the street where the wind lives. I can't help but think of your face, your lively and innocent figure.

The sea of people is vast, the world is mundane. Beauty always meets, sadness always encounters. At the end of unfamiliar paths, scattering a handful of pale ash, how many indifferent human relationships can hold the regret of lavish upbringing and frugal burial? Under the lingering notes of mournful elegies, bowing to the lonely souls under these silent green tombstones, and between this birth and death, how many not yet cold spirits slip into emptiness, yet cannot be retrieved by any remembrance in the human world. Meeting you gave me a lifetime of emotional stirring, creating ripples in my heart. But I know that ripples will always become calm. Now, it's as if standing at an awkward crossroads between old and new, losing the straightforward confession of deceit, and not yet gaining the composure of wandering with a pen. Look, in this world full of love and being loved, hurting and being hurt, life is stingy with us because it always lets us down; but life is also so generous, always giving us salvation after disappointment. Because we so easily walk into the halo and shadow of others, foolishly making noise, still firmly believing that this is our advantage and value. Yet I calmly insist on using pale language to portray the hostility between ideals and reality, as well as the long-stored coldness and hope, decisiveness and compromise deep within my heart. Real, real, and more real. I grieve in longing...

It was the wind that woke me up from sleep, awakening the dream of yearning. My body in pain, my heart confused by yearning, is always so sadly filled with yesterday's pain. The wind no longer has its former gentleness.

The sea of people remains vast. If lost love could be regained, then, yearning comes from the sea of humanity. From the wind. In the long road of time, who can tell me how many mistakes I will make?

Today, where exactly is my final resting place? Once, you told me in the wind "to be together for this life", never to leave me. The entanglement woven into painful dreams, love and hate carved as lenses of happiness. Thus, everything was destined to end up as nothing. Can you hear the wind of yearning coming from the sea of humanity?
