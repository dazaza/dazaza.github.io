Missing you is the beauty of fallen flowers drifting onto my shoulder.

by zhouzhou56 on 2012-02-02 22:34:35

Missing you is the beauty of fallen flowers drifting onto my shoulders.

Walking alone in the twilight drizzle, lost in thought, thinking of you far away. The wind is gentle, the rain is light, and the distant longing intoxicates me in a chapter of scattered verses composed by the rain and wind. I always feel that something belongs to the memories of a previous life, for a heart-stirring encounter, I don't know how long I have prayed before the Buddha, asking for a segment of fate, for this, I would rather become the reed flower waiting for you by the waterside, planting my yearning into a fence in my foolish wait. The seasonal winds traverse the corridors of past lives, caressing a thousand-year-old guqin, attracting butterfly wings with beautiful aspirations.

Gathering my full bosom of yearning, it condenses into red beans, entrusted to the bright moon and pure breeze of the plain to deliver. When the moon is full again, you come, walking steadily and calmly, carrying an elegant demeanor like a gentleman orchid, crossing the water from afar, with a quietly nodding smile, knocking on my rarely visited heart.

The breeze rises from the end of the green萍, the mountains turn green again, the trees turn lush again, the flowers turn red again, and I, too, get drunk, drunk in your familiar eye waves from the previous life, drunk in every soft call of your voice.

Once obsessed with an ancient legend, I stubbornly believe that the ups and downs of destiny are the cycle of a flower and a butterfly. Therefore, I only want to pour all my tenderness into cherishing you, holding the butterflies that flew wrong in the previous life as the cluster of chrysanthemums by my hairpin in this life. From now on, accompanying you in the mortal world, I read you day and night, reading you into the warmth of a bright spring sun, reading you into the gracefulness of flowing moonlight, reading you into the charm of delicate Song lyrics, reading you into the profound antiquity of the Reed Poems. I am accustomed to lighting an exquisite scented lamp, listening to your heart's words every quiet night. I am accustomed to writing your name on white pages in every corner without people, painting countless smiling eyes, telling my endless yearning and joy. Standing under a tree full of blossoms, I can't help but think of you again. Thinking of you feels like my heart is light as a feather dipped in happiness, each tremble imbued with your breath. "Flowing gently with the wind, gracefully falling to dance in the mortal world."

As the wind rises and the trees sway, petals fall piece by piece. A fleeting dizziness, akin to celestial music playing gracefully, yet I cannot distinguish whether what dances before my eyes are transparent butterfly wings or fairy-like rainbow wings, nor can I tell if it is the fine rain like silk whispering thousands of years of love to the floating flowers, or if the light petals have already transformed into murmuring fine rain with beautiful vows.

At this moment, how I wish to hold your hand, let the clear wind fill our sleeves, play with flower fragrances covering our clothes. Focused after the wind passes, gazing at an unknowable distance. Gently stroking the traces of red still intoxicated on my shoulder.

Gently extending my fingers, collecting a portion of eternal beauty for you. Then smiling to myself, saying that blooming flowers are beautiful, and falling flowers are intoxicating, why be fixated on one outcome?

The fading red understands the meaning of yearning. So, let me pick up a few fallen flowers as tea, scoop my hands as a cup, warming the tenderness around my fingers into water, brewing a rich and mellow pot of tea for you, so that in all the years to come, the air will be filled with the fragrance of yearning. I don't know if we will truly meet in this life, if it's possible, I hope it's in March, the season when grass grows and orioles fly, flowers are thick and rains are moist. I will stand under the tree appointed in the previous life, softly smiling against apricot flowers and spring rain, saying that having you in this mortal world is wonderful! If one day, destiny is destined to fade like smoke, then I only pray: let me remember you again in the moonlight of five hundred cycles!

Missing you is the beauty of fallen flowers drifting onto my shoulders.
