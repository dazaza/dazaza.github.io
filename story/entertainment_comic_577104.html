Anime live-action adaptations: The third dimension destroys the world.

by mlwang on 2011-11-22 10:14:48

Money is something you can never earn enough of, just like how there are always more manuscripts to write T_T~ When the anime industry in Japan was booming, many companies saw the potential in this lucrative business and jumped on board. Various adaptations emerged—games, movies, TV series—all as methods to make money, which soon spiraled out of control. In short, every year now sees dozens of animes being adapted into games or live-action versions... If you've got the skills, why not try adapting "Little Tadpole Finds Mommy" into a live-action version?

Unfortunately, while there are many adaptations, very few actually meet expectations. Most seem to exist merely to scare people! They barely reach the level of "live-action tokusatsu shows." The sets and acting feel like they're meant to deceive children. Even if they can't reach Hollywood standards like "Superman" or "Men in Black," couldn't they at least aim for the high-quality soap operas like "My Lovely Samsoon" or "Boys Over Flowers"?

The live-action version of "Ranma 1/2" is brutal!

Let's start with the currently hot topic: the live-action version of "Ranma 1/2," which is said to air in Japan on December 9th. My first reaction upon hearing that Ranma would be adapted into a live-action version was ~ Yattaze~

Let's talk about the story of Ranma: the protagonist (sometimes the heroine) Ranma Saotome is a young Japanese martial artist who, during his training in the cursed springs of Jusenkyo in Qinghai, China, accidentally fell into the "Girl's Drowning Spring," thus acquiring the peculiar condition of turning into a girl when exposed to cold water and back into a boy with hot water. His father, Genma Saotome, turns into a panda when exposed to cold water and back into human form with hot water.

Thus, Ranma Saotome strives to overcome his transformation troubles and achieve higher levels of martial arts. Meanwhile, Akane Tendo, the female lead, constantly challenges Ranma due to their forced engagement by both sets of parents...

This is essentially a story filled with constant laughter and incredible plot twists. From what has been revealed so far, male and female versions of Ranma will be played by different actors. The highly popular Gakki New垣 will take on the role of Akane Tendo. Now, regardless of the actors' abilities, how exactly do they plan to transform from male to female or human to panda when doused with cold water? Is it going to be covered by holy light, pixelation, or smoke bombs?

Additionally, the live-action version of the panda looks too terrifying. Moreover, the TV drama version introduces an original antagonist - Mr. Komatsuda, Akane's high school principal. This...this...this character design is just too flamboyant.

The live-action version of "Sailor Moon" exposes panties~

When I first heard about this, I knew it was bound to be a disaster. My first thought was, have they really run out of ideas to the point of exploiting old properties like this?

Almost all forums mercilessly criticized this live-action adaptation. Personally, I think aside from the faithful reproduction of the original jumping forward combat style, except for Luna being a doll, the monsters and sailor senshi designs being too outrageous, Usagi looking too...well, let's not say, and the special effects being too poor, it's still somewhat acceptable (Hey! There's hardly anything left that's acceptable).

Although there are quite a few ridiculous aspects, overall, when not transformed, the sailor senshi are still quite cute. But once transformed, they look like strange plastic figures hopping around~

Also, since the skirts are basically copied from the animation, various scenes of underwear exposure occur when the girls flip around~ Interestingly, almost all the panties are white, which doesn't seem very "sailor-like," does it? The live-action version of "Hell Girl" is terrifying!

"Hell Girl" is a work by Eiichi Shimizu.

I haven't read the manga, but the animation effects are amazing, especially the kimono color schemes. The story goes: in the city, there is such a rumor: if you log into "Hell Communication" at midnight and write down the name of the person you hate, Hell Girl will appear if your hatred is strong enough, taking the person you hate to hell.

In reality, letters sent to Hell Communication are delivered to a girl named Ai Enma. When her feelings resonate with the hatred in the letter, she transforms into Hell Girl, presenting her followers-turned-straw dolls and explaining the contract terms and consequences: if the red string on the straw doll is untied, the contract is formed, Hell Girl will send the hated person to hell. However, after resolving the hatred, one must also pay the price: after death, one will also go to hell.

The entire story is shrouded in mystery, so when it was adapted into a live-action version, it completely turned into a horror movie!! Actually, I only watched the first episode of the live-action version because when Ai Enma floated outside the window and revealed her half-red, half-black eyes, it scared me to death! This is literally a ghost movie~

The live-action version of "Death Note" has a flawed plot

"Death Note" is banned in China because its theme is too dark and unharmonious. However, this does not detract from its excellence. The story begins with a bored shinigami dropping a "Death Note" into the human world, where it is picked up by Light Yagami, another bored genius. Light was originally a top student at a prestigious university, highly anticipated. However, he gradually realized the limitations of the law and began using the Death Note to punish criminals, building his ideal pure world.

Speaking of this movie, I can't help but criticize the casting. That chubby guy who looks like a typical good youth playing Light... Are you sure he didn't walk onto the wrong set? What's up with Misa Amane's hair color? Couldn't you at least use a wig? The only consolation is that the shinigami looks so real... Was the entire budget spent here?

Additionally, that scene at the end of the first movie, where Misa clings to the barbed wire fence, turns her head, and suddenly a notebook falls from the sky... Why does it make me laugh more and more? It feels like "a pie falling from the sky"~

The most tragic part is that the entire story is off. L writes his own death... Light dies the most depressingly, and Ryuk says he's too bad and writes him to death... Hey director, even with strong creative desires, you should consider the audience's feelings a little!

The live-action version of "Nodame Cantabile" is a masterpiece worth appreciating

Alright, finally, let's appreciate a masterpiece. "Nodame Cantabile" is a manga work by Tomoko Ninomiya. The story revolves around Nodame Wild田, an extraordinary young woman who lives in a garbage room, completely disheveled and lacking in appearance, yet possesses an extraordinary talent for music. She appears before Shinichi Chiaki, the son of a famous pianist and an excellent music college student, who has always wanted to study conducting in Europe but has been unable to leave the country due to his fear of flying.

I watched the live-action version of this show before checking out the anime. Maybe it's because I watched the live-action first, but Hiroshi Tamaki is much better-looking than his animated counterpart haha. And the settings and music are incredibly lavish.

One of my favorite scenes is when Chiaki loses his temper, grabs Nodame, and keeps tossing her up and down repeatedly—it’s absolutely fantastic and holds its own against the anime version.

Back then, both Tamaki Hiroshi and Juri Ueno were considered second-tier actors. This drama propelled them directly into the first tier. Moreover, the biggest impact of this series was sparking a symphony craze in Japan, making the usually niche symphony music suddenly very popular. It's said that people who never listened to symphonies before became fans after watching this!
