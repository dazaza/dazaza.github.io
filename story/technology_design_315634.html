Champions League draw conspiracy comes to light: Platini fooled the whole world

by qinglai888 on 2009-12-20 09:24:30

The shocking conspiracy behind the UEFA Champions League draw has come to light - Platini fooled the entire world. "This is my UEFA," Platini boasts. Kenyon: "This is all your doing, President." Platini: "I... ..."

According to a report by Sina Sports, last season in the round of 16 Champions League draw, Manchester United faced Inter Milan, Chelsea faced Juventus, Arsenal faced Roma; this season it's become Manchester United against AC Milan and Chelsea against Inter Milan. What changes are the opponents for Manchester United and Chelsea, but what remains constant is the main theme of Premier League vs Serie A in the knockout stages. Meanwhile, the three Spanish teams have drawn well. If drawing Lyon can be considered a neutral draw for Real Madrid, then Barcelona and Sevilla undoubtedly got the best possible draw. And this isn't the first time that Spanish teams have received good draws. Last season, while the Premier League and Serie A teams were duking it out one-on-one, Barcelona, Villarreal, and Atletico Madrid's opponents were Lyon, Panathinaikos, and Porto respectively. Apart from Atletico Madrid being eliminated by Porto, the advances of Barcelona and the Yellow Submarine were very easy. Only Real Madrid, who met Liverpool, was brutally slaughtered 5-0 by the Reds, which somewhat tarnished the face of La Liga.

Upon careful analysis, this is already the third consecutive season where Premier League and Serie A teams have clashed head-to-head in the Champions League, and also the third consecutive season where Spanish teams have received good draws.

In the 2007-08 season, during the round of 16 Champions League matches, Arsenal faced AC Milan, Liverpool faced Inter Milan, Manchester United encountered their old rival Lyon, and only Chelsea faced the relatively weaker Olympiacos; conversely, the Spanish teams' draw saw Real Madrid up against the slightly inferior Roma from Serie A, and Sevilla and Barcelona facing Fenerbahce and Celtic respectively, almost guaranteeing passage to the quarter-finals. In the quarter-final draw, UEFA had Arsenal and Liverpool eliminate each other, and had Manchester United face off against the Italian team Roma, who had eliminated Real Madrid. But Barcelona's opponent? Schalke 04!

In other words, in the past three seasons of the Champions League knockout stages, Premier League and Serie A teams have faced each other six times previously, with Premier League teams always coming out on top; this season, Chelsea vs Inter Milan and Milan vs Manchester United mark the seventh and eighth encounters between Premier League and Serie A teams over the past three seasons. Surprisingly, Spanish and Italian teams have only met once in the knockout stages of the Champions League over the past three seasons, with the Italian team Roma ultimately triumphing. In other words, over the past three seasons, Spanish teams have never defeated Italian teams in the Champions League knockout stages. And in the past three seasons, Premier League and Spanish teams have faced each other five times in the Champions League knockout stages, with Premier League teams prevailing three times, and Spanish teams twice, both victories achieved by Barcelona, who eliminated Chelsea in last season's Champions League semi-final and defeated Manchester United in the final.

Objectively speaking, it will be difficult for the Premier League this season to replicate the scenes of the past two seasons where they faced Italian teams six times, eliminating all of them. It will also be hard to repeat the glory of occupying three out of four Champions League semi-final spots for three consecutive seasons. After all, Liverpool has already been eliminated at the group stage, and Manchester United and Arsenal did not make significant summer signings, with their strength decreasing rather than increasing. The revamped Chelsea also did not receive substantial reinforcements.

After Cristiano Ronaldo left this summer, Manchester United now lacks a stable second scoring point. The £30.75 million purchase of Berbatov has proven ineffective. This season, Berbatov has yet to score a goal in the Champions League, and now, more alarmingly, even when facing weak opponents like Wolves Reserve Team, Berbatov has lost his desire to shoot, and a forward without shooting ability cannot score goals. With Berbatov failing to deliver, a hidden concern for Manchester United is that if their top scorer Wayne Rooney gets injured, United's attacking power will be extremely weak. Unless Sir Alex Ferguson can sign strong reinforcements in the winter transfer market, finding powerful strikers who haven't participated in the Champions League is no easy task in today's transfer market.

Just as Manchester United depends on Rooney, Chelsea heavily relies on Didier Drogba. Despite the diligent efforts of Kalou and Borini, objectively speaking, their abilities fall far short of the beast. If new signings cannot be brought in next month, it will be difficult for anyone to hold up Chelsea's frontline should Drogba get injured. The much-rumored Aguero, even if he joins, would lose the opportunity to play for Chelsea in this season's Champions League. Of course, Chelsea's greatest advantage is Ancelotti, the renowned Italian coach skilled in Champions League play, who has already stated that Chelsea's goal this season prioritizes the Champions League over the Premier League.

Arsenal is, relatively speaking, the least reliable among these three Premier League teams, and also the team whose strength has declined the most this summer. Nevertheless, the Gunners' difficulty in advancing to the quarter-finals is not great. To continue further will depend on the next draw opponent. Wenger's team has not faced any tests in this season's Champions League group stage. Their round of 16 opponent Porto, after losing key players Lisandro Lopez and Lucho Gonzalez, is no longer particularly tough. However, Arsenal's problem lies in losing Adebayor and Toure this summer, followed by losing their top scorer from last season, Van Persie, at the start of the season, significantly reducing their strength. If Arsenal hopes to progress further in the Champions League, Wenger must act in the January transfer market.

In fact, at the moment the Champions League group stage draw ended at 00:00 Beijing Time on August 28, while the British media were celebrating the draws of the four major teams, I rationally pointed out that this was probably another political game orchestrated by Platini. At that time, almost everyone's attention was captured by the hype of Inter Milan and Barcelona being drawn into the same group, Milan and Real Madrid in the same group, Kaka returning to San Siro, and Ibrahimovic returning to Meazza, neglecting the potential Premier League vs Serie A clashes among the top 16. It should be noted that this was merely a smokescreen by Platini et al.

For Premier League giants, even if they were placed in the same groups as Real Madrid and Barcelona, under normal circumstances, advancing from the group stage would still be highly possible. For these teams, advancing from the group stage shouldn't be the primary concern; what's important is the knockout stage opponents. And now, as the knockout stage opponents are revealed, Platini's masterpiece staged four months ago begins to unfold.

"If AC Milan and Inter Milan both finish second in their groups, there is a high possibility they will face Premier League teams in the round of 16 draw. Unless Platini swaps the group opponents of the Milan duo, meaning AC Milan faces Inter Milan's group opponent Barcelona in the round of 16; and Barcelona faces AC Milan's group opponent Real Madrid in the round of 16, which is almost impossible. As we all know, this season's Champions League final venue is Real Madrid's home ground Bernabeu, and UEFA is unlikely to allow Real Madrid to be eliminated too early. In other words, if they advance as group runners-up, it is almost certain that the Milan duo will face Premier League teams. Even if Fiorentina overtakes Liverpool to finish top of their group, the Viola cannot face the Milan duo in the round of 16 match, thus excluding Real Madrid, Barcelona, Juventus, and Fiorentina, the remaining four opponents that the Milan duo needs to face in the round of 16 could likely include three Premier League teams. In summary, assuming the Milan duo both finish second in their groups, it is almost certain they will face one of the four strong Premier League teams in the round of 16. Meanwhile, Real Madrid and Barcelona, who will hardly face any Premier League (Premier League teams mostly finish top of their groups) and Serie A teams, are highly likely to encounter non-powerhouse teams in the crucial round of 16 elimination matches, easily advancing to the quarter-finals."

The scene of Premier League vs Serie A clashes was already set the moment the group stage draw for this season's Champions League ended. After all, it is difficult for Inter Milan to snatch the group top spot from the powerful Barcelona; and which team has the absolute stronger strength between the newly reformed Milan and the second phase of the Galacticos who invested over €200 million this summer, every fan has their own yardstick. Now it seems that if you still insist that there is no conspiracy in the Champions League draws arranged by Platini, it really is naive.
