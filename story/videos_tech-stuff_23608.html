BMW Ad Hire Network Film Selection

by dkinglong on 2007-06-28 23:40:51

"BMW Advertisement 'Hire' Network Film Selection" gathers the advertising works of eight top film directors! Although the directors, themes, and styles vary, since these are BMW advertisements, car chase scenes naturally appear in all eight films. Of course, the main characters' cars are all BMWs. The professional drivers in the films, driving BMW luxury cars, are all played by British actor Clive Owen.

Breaking through traditional car advertisements, besides various high-speed driving scenes emphasizing performance, there are no shortage of scenes where BMW cars are pierced by bullets or collide. "We want these cars to look like very entertaining characters in the films," Jim Mcdowell, Vice President of BMW North America, once said, "We can't keep being restricted by the idea that these cars will never be hit by bullets, will never get dusty or muddy, or will never be dented."

Official website: www.bmwfilms.com

1. Hostage — Directed by John Woo, a tense story about rescuing hostages. Old Wu's control of the camera has reached a masterful level, filling my viewing room with tension within 30 seconds. Notably, Old Wu's ambition is clear; this 8-minute short film conveys far more than just an advertisement. You can already see the shape of a suspense crime film.

2. Ticker — Directed by Joe Carnahan, the director of Narc. It tells a story of rescue and escape, with very impressive action scenes. The tone resembles Swordfish, and the story is full of tension within the short 8 minutes, using flashbacks and interludes very skillfully.

3. Beat the Devil — A work by Tony Scott, the director of Crimson Tide, starring Gary Oldman. Additionally, it features a super funny appearance by Marilyn Manson. The editing of the piece is very powerful, filled with eerie atmosphere and wild strength.

4. Ambush — Directed by John Frankenheimer, also a story about "escape." The Jews in the film are interesting.

5. Chosen — Directed by Ang Lee, a story about rescuing a little lama. The pace is a bit slow, and Director Lee even made an ad for his Hulk in the program.

6. The Follow — A work by Wong Kar-wai, featuring the protagonist from Ghost Dog: The Way of the Samurai as a supporting character.

7. Star — Directed by Guy Ritchie, the funniest one in the entire short film collection. It's a story about teaching a highly annoying female star a lesson... Interestingly, this "annoying female star," whether in terms of appearance, material, or the actress herself, is the director's wife—Madonna!

8. Powder Keg — A work by Alejandro Gonzalez-Inarritu, the director of Amores Perros, the most heart-wrenching piece in the entire collection. It's a story about war correspondents.
