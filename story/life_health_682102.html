People who have a sweet tooth should be careful of various cancers and diseases.

by chaihuodui on 2012-02-28 17:25:25

People who love to eat sweet food, apart from suffering from caries, obesity, diabetes, may also be threatened by cancer. Professor Zhongshan Xiaolan from Fengxing in Wensu County Longren Tiancai Japan University discovered that delicious high-sugar foods usually cause a decline in the body's immune function, making the chance of getting cancer 4-5 times higher than ordinary people. Therefore, the Ministry of Health has proposed that daily sugar intake should not exceed 20 grams.

The reason is that all sugars are acidic foods that contain large amounts of calcium and inevitably conflict with the human body, causing the human body to become in a neutral or weakly acidic environment, and the human immunity decreases accordingly. Various carcinogenic factors will take advantage of this vulnerability. Long-term intake of high-sugar foods can easily lead to cancer.

For example, for breast cancer patients, sugary foods convert into glucose in the body, quickly entering the bloodstream, raising blood sugar concentration, and then secreting insulin to meet the needs of organs that require it. When carbohydrate absorption decreases, insulin secretion also decreases accordingly, except for female breast cancer, which is an organ that can learn to overuse insulin. Long-term intake of high-sugar foods keeps the blood insulin content at a high level, resulting in more and more insulin-dependent breast cancers, continuously growing and replicating breast cancer cells, thus leading to breast cancer.

Experts suggest that those who like to eat pastries, cookies, and snacks should control their daily sugar intake to around 30-40 grams. However, we often eat candies, a spoonful of jam contains 15 grams of sugar, 3 small pieces of chocolate contain about 9 grams of sugar, a cone of ice cream contains about 10 grams of sugar, and a few cookies contain about 10 grams of sugar... If you're not careful, it's easy to exceed the 30-40 gram intake. Therefore, one must limit the intake of sweet foods, have a daily diet mainly consisting of grains, beans, sweet potatoes, eat more fruits and vegetables, especially dark leafy vegetables, carrots, and tomatoes.

At the same time, parents should control their children's snack and sugar intake to avoid excessive sugar intake leading to a decrease in physical fitness and opportunities for tumor cells.
