As scheduled, Nokia launched 6 new products including tablets and mobile phones.

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 13:19:53

This was Nokia's first product launch announcement after announcing its acquisition, and it might also be the last "Nokia World." After enduring various doubts about the acquisition, Nokia regrouped and launched six devices at once, including the tablet Lumia 2520, phones Lumia 1520, Lumia 1320, as well as Asha 500, Asha 502, and Asha 503.

As with previous launches, besides regular hardware updates for products, many advancements in software ecosystems were introduced. Of course, the biggest difference lay in the first release of a Windows RT system tablet, and two phones both equipped with 6-inch screens.

**Nokia Lumia 2520**

The Lumia 2520 looks very similar to the other two phones, more like an enlarged version. It features a Unibody design and supports LTE networks. The screen glass is Gorilla Glass 2, with screen brightness surpassing all current tablets, ensuring good visibility outdoors. The glass reflection rate is only 6%. Its detailed specifications are as follows:

- 10-inch 1920 x 1080 resolution screen

- Qualcomm Snapdragon 800 processor, clocked at 2.2GHz

- Windows RT 8.1 system

- 2GB RAM, 32GB ROM, expandable up to 64GB

- Rear 670-megapixel camera with F/1.9 aperture, front 2-megapixel camera

Additionally, Nokia launched a keyboard accessory specifically for the Lumia 2520, which can extend battery life by 5 hours, adding to the original 11 hours, totaling up to 16 hours. The product also includes fast charging functionality, allowing half the battery to be charged within 40 minutes.

In terms of software, it also supports Nokia Camera, Storyteller (an app that integrates geographical locations and social networks into photo collections), Flipboard, DreamWorks' game "Dragon’s Adventure," video editing software Video Director, Nokia Music, etc.

This product will be available in the U.S. and UK by the end of the year, priced at $499, available in black, white, blue, and red. The keyboard price is $149.

**Nokia Lumia 1520**

- 6-inch 1920 x 1080 resolution screen

- Qualcomm Snapdragon 800 quad-core processor

- Windows Phone 8.1 system

- 20-megapixel PureView lens

- Four microphones

The Lumia 1520 is priced at $749 and will be launched first in Hong Kong, Singapore, the U.S., China, France, Germany, and the UK, available in black, white, orange, and yellow.

**Nokia Lumia 1320**

- 6-inch 720p screen

- Qualcomm Snapdragon dual-core processor

- 5-megapixel camera

The Lumia 1320 will be launched first in China, followed by Europe, Asia, and India, priced at $339.

It is worth noting that Vine and Instagram have come to the Windows Phone platform, enhancing the platform's ecosystem. In terms of other applications, the new Nokia Camera application combines intelligent photography, professional photography, and video, performing well under low light, close-up, and sports modes, and allows for focus movement. There is also a new photo social application called Storyteller.

Additionally, the Lumia Black system upgrade will begin early next year, adding custom ringtones, driving mode functions, etc.

**Asha 500, Asha 502, Asha 503**

The Asha brand is Nokia's main force in the low-end market, with substantial annual shipments. As a smartphone, it has also undergone functional upgrades. For example, emphasizing the "swap" operation, where swiping from the bottom in lock screen mode enters the camera interface, long pressing anywhere pops up the camera settings, swiping left enters recording function, swiping right enters the album.

Additionally, WhatsApp has landed on the Asha platform, further enriching the Asha platform ecosystem.

In terms of pricing, the Asha 500 comes with a 2-megapixel camera, priced at $69, the Asha 502 comes with a 5-megapixel camera, priced at $99, the Asha 503 also comes with a 5-megapixel camera and supports 3G functionality, priced at $99. The products will be launched first in Africa, Asia-Pacific, Middle East, Europe, and other regions.
