With a companion, street encounter

by ucqp3139 on 2011-08-24 16:41:06

Fellow soldiers: When I met a couple on the street, they looked at my olive green military uniform and the faded liberation shoes, and he asked: What time is it? Why don't you change into civilian clothes? Some relatives wrote to inquire about my allowance of dozens of yuan, and he asked: What era are we in now? Is it enough? A classmate mentioned my future and was surprised that even as an "official," I still have to stand guard, crawl around, and train hard. He asked: What kind of society are we in now? Is it worth it? In response to these continuous questions, I don't want to answer hastily. Haven't some people said that we are the "abandoned children" of the market economy, the backward ones of modern society? Then, facing the tide of the new century, what is the value orientation for us soldiers? Where should our contribution boat sail to? I really want to face Luding Bridge and Jinsha River, and ask the heroes of the Long March with their strong bones and heroic souls; I really want to go into Yuhuatai and Geleshan, and ask countless martyrs who sacrificed themselves for the country and rest in the netherworld; I really want to climb up to Hongqilafu and Ganbala in Tibet, and ask the border defense soldiers' spirits coexisting with the sky: You, me, all of us, what answers should we give?

I have a comrade named XX, who before joining the army was a big chicken farmer in XX city with more than 1 million yuan in assets. But he didn't come to carry a gun in a car, or wear a suit instead of a military uniform, or become a profit-seeking businessman instead of an obligated soldier carrying guns and cannons, which puzzled many people. However, at his first platoon meeting after joining the army, he told us his touching original intention. On a business trip, he encountered two fierce robbers. Just as the 50,000 yuan in his hand was about to be robbed, an old comrade who was on leave stood up suddenly, and the robbers fled. XX took out 1,000 yuan to give to this soldier but was firmly refused. He thought it was too little and asked, "How much do you want?" The old comrade replied decisively, "You've misunderstood me! If it's for money, then I've wasted my time wearing this military uniform!"

XX felt ashamed. The soldier hesitated for a moment and said, "If you really want to thank me, please help me introduce a girl. Our political instructor is an orphan who has served in the border defense outpost for XX years and is XX years old, but still unmarried. After returning from this leave, the comrades in the outpost pooled their savings penny by penny and gave me a special task - I must find a sister-in-law for us." XX promised readily, but soon after he returned home, the old comrade wrote a letter saying that the political instructor had died heroically during patrol duty. He made a bold request: if the "sister-in-law" could be found, please let that girl write a letter to the political instructor, even just a few words would do. We want to offer it as a tribute on the political instructor's grave to comfort his lonely soul in the netherworld. Faced with this simple request, XX was moved to tears. He felt that there was a great and mysterious power in these soldiers. A person cannot live without money, nor can they live without a comfortable life, but what is often lacking is this inspiring and soul-shaking force.

With excitement, confusion, and reflection, he came to a desolate desert in Hexi Corridor. The wind and sand whipped his face, the scorching sun burned his back, hardship blocked his steps, and loneliness tormented his soul. Through XX years and XX nights, the barracks tempered his will, enhanced his courage, and trained his character. He achieved excellent results and entered the military academy. During winter vacation at home, he was invited to a high school classmate's house. That classmate said to him, "Look, after so many years, I've brought my family into the modern age, while you've earned two battle merit badges. The battle merit badges are eye-catching, and the congratulatory notices are moving, but frankly speaking, in this market economy era, what we care about is practical benefits. How much are these intangible things worth? Even if we calculate with the minimum annual income of XX ten thousand yuan, you've lost over 300,000 yuan in four years! Don't you regret it?"

XX stood up and walked around the room, saying, "Have you ever calculated such a simple account? Assuming a person lives to be 70, excluding the 3 years we spend standing guard and making contributions, can't we contribute XX years for others? If there were no soldiers like us, foreign warships might still be rampaging on the Yangtze River, and the Japanese might still be burning and killing at the Marco Polo Bridge! Frankly speaking, your modernized residence might become a stable trampled by the invaders' horses! As contemporary soldiers, we face choices between fame and interest, beauty and ugliness, justice and evil, material wealth and spiritual ideals. However, dear comrades, when we see certain speculators rising rapidly and strutting proudly in luxurious cars surrounded by crowds, I say, we don't need to have the impulse to chase them; when we see those ostentatious so-called 'big names' and 'stars' boasting arrogantly, I say, we don't need to show envious expressions; when we see wealthy bosses with one hand holding elderly parents and the other tightly hugging a coquettish lady, I say, we don't need to cast admiring glances. Because we wear this military uniform, it means we must reduce trivialities and pursue more goals; reduce personal feelings and increase loyalty; reduce demands and increase contributions!

Truth inspires dreams, dreams generate faith, faith stimulates will, and will produces strength! Let the thunder of truth roar above our heads; let the lightning of dreams flash in our brains; let the fire of contribution burn in our hearts! This is the answer given by contemporary soldiers - the steel fortress stepping into the XX century - to Huangdi, the spirits of the Jiawu War, Sun Yat-sen, Mao Zedong, Dong Cunrui, Lei Feng, echoing across the vast land of China! Thank you all!
