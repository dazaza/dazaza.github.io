How to Correctly Select a Gas Turbine Flow Meter

With the advancement of the "West-to-East Gas Transmission" project, the use of natural gas has become increasingly widespread. In the process of using natural gas, the gas turbine flow meter has become an indispensable measuring instrument. Now, technical personnel from Jiangsu Aikete Instrument Co., Ltd. will introduce issues to consider when selecting, using, and maintaining gas turbine flow meters as follows:

1. Selection

The selection of a gas turbine flow meter should take into account the following aspects:

1.1 Accuracy Class: Generally, the reason for choosing a turbine flow meter is its high accuracy. However, the higher the accuracy of the flow meter, the more sensitive it becomes to changes in on-site usage conditions. Therefore, careful consideration should be given to the choice of instrument accuracy from an economic perspective. For large-diameter gas pipeline trade settlement instruments, it is cost-effective to invest more in the instrument. For occasions with smaller transmission volumes, medium-accuracy level instruments are sufficient.

1.2 Flow Range: The selection of the flow range of a turbine flow meter significantly affects its accuracy and service life. Each size of flow meter has a certain measurement range, and the selection of the flow meter size is determined by the flow range. The principle for selecting the flow range is: the minimum flow during use must not be lower than the minimum flow that the instrument allows to measure, and the maximum flow during use must not exceed the maximum flow that the instrument allows to measure. For intermittent working conditions where the actual daily operating time of the instrument does not exceed 8 hours, choose 1.3 times the actual maximum flow during use as the upper limit of the flow range; for continuous working conditions where the actual daily operating time of the instrument is no less than 8 hours, choose 1.4 times the actual maximum flow during use as the upper limit of the flow range. The lower limit flow of the instrument should be 0.8 times the actual minimum flow during use.

1.3 Gas Density: For gas turbine flow meters, the main influence of fluid properties is gas density, which has a significant impact on the instrument coefficient, especially in the low-flow region. If the gas density changes frequently, corrective measures need to be taken for the flow coefficient of the flow meter.

1.4 Pressure Loss: Preferably select a turbine flow meter with minimal pressure loss. Because the smaller the pressure loss when fluid passes through the turbine flow meter, the less energy consumed by the fluid from input to output pipelines, thus reducing the total power required, saving energy significantly, lowering transportation costs, and improving utilization efficiency. Someone once conducted an experiment showing that the main component affecting pressure loss is the pre-guide of the turbine flow meter. Choosing a semi-ellipsoidal pre-guide instead of a conical one can greatly reduce the pressure loss of the turbine flow meter.

1.5 Structural Form: (1) Internally, it is advisable to select a reverse-thrust type turbine flow meter. This structure allows the rotor to float within a certain flow range, eliminating axial contact points, avoiding end face friction and wear, and extending the service life of the bearing. (2) Select the type according to the pipe connection method. Turbine flow meters can be installed horizontally or vertically. Horizontal installation connects to the pipeline via flange, thread, or clamp connections. Medium-sized diameters should use flange connections; small diameters and high-pressure pipelines should use threaded connections; clamp connections are only suitable for low-pressure small-medium diameter pipes; vertical installations only have threaded connections. (3) Select the type based on environmental conditions, considering temperature and humidity effects. For natural gas measurement, intrinsically safe explosion-proof turbine flow meters should be chosen.

1.6 Bearings: The bearings of turbine flow meters generally come in three materials: tungsten carbide, polytetrafluoroethylene (PTFE), and carbon graphite. For natural gas measurement instruments, tungsten carbide material bearings should be selected.

These are the main aspects to consider during selection. Due to the wide variety of turbine flow meters and differences in product quality among different manufacturers, it is advisable to gather as much information as possible about the manufacturer and product technical standards, conduct thorough investigations and comparisons, and then make a decision.

2. Use and Maintenance

To ensure the long-term normal operation of the gas turbine flow meter, it is necessary to regularly check the operating status of the flow meter, perform maintenance work, and promptly resolve any issues that arise.

2.1 Before putting the turbine flow meter into operation, first set the instrument coefficient, carefully check, confirm that the flow meter wiring is correct and grounded properly before powering on.

2.2 Regularly clean, inspect, and recalibrate the flow meter. For flow meters equipped with lubricating oil or cleaning liquid injection ports, follow the instructions in the manual to regularly inject lubricating oil or cleaning liquid to maintain the good operation of the rotor.

2.3 Monitor the condition of the display instrument, evaluate the readings of the display instrument, and promptly check if there are any abnormalities.

2.4 Keep the filter unobstructed. Blockage of the filter by impurities can be judged by the increase in the pressure gauge reading difference at its inlet and outlet. Timely remove blockages when they occur; otherwise, it will severely reduce the flow.

2.5 For large-scale trade settlement measurements, to ensure the accuracy of the flow meter, the flow meter must be frequently calibrated. On-site online calibration equipment or portable calibration devices should be provided. Although the initial investment is high, it is worth it from a long-term economic perspective.
