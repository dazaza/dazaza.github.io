Intel Integrated Platform: Recommendations for Seven H61 and H67 Motherboards

by tuoqing1188 on 2011-08-04 10:22:04

For a long time, AMD's integrated platforms such as 780G and 880G have been the first choice for entry-level users. However, since Intel launched the H55/H57 platform, the situation has changed. Many users were attracted by the powerful performance of i3/i5 processors and their good power consumption when paired with the H55/H57 platform, thus choosing Intel's integrated platform.

This year, Intel once again strengthens its integrated platform by launching the new generation of i3/i5 processors with an integrated graphics core. Not only does the processor performance improve significantly, but also the performance of the core graphics card has made great progress compared to before. On the other hand, although AMD’s mainstream A6/A8 series APUs integrate a graphics core that supports DX11 and has strong performance comparable to entry-level independent graphics cards, the market currently generally lacks availability of these processors. Consumers actually don't have much choice. It can be said that now if you want to build an integrated platform, besides H61/H67, what else could you choose?

Recommended Product: ASUS P8H67

Reference Price: 1099 yuan

Main Selling Points: First-class brand, digital power supply, EPU, EFI interface, TurboV

The ASUS P8H67 is designed in ATX form factor, based on the Intel H67 chipset, supporting LGA1155 series Sandy Bridge i3/i5/i7 processors and DDR3 memory. It also supports four SATA2.0 and two SATA3.0 interfaces. The appearance of SATA3.0 interfaces greatly improves transmission speed and execution efficiency.

In terms of power supply, the ASUS P8H67 adopts a five-phase all-solid-state power supply system, supplemented by all-solid-state capacitors to provide stable and reliable power supply for the system, while reducing the heat generation of the power module, making overclocking more stable.

In terms of memory, this motherboard provides four mainstream DDR3 memory slots, supporting dual-channel DDR3 1333/1066MHz memory with a maximum support of 32GB memory capacity.

In terms of storage interfaces, the motherboard provides four SATA2.0 and two SATA3.0 interfaces, supporting up to twelve USB2.0 and two USB3.0 interfaces.

On the back panel I/O interface part, the ASUS P8H67 has a set of PS/2 keyboard/mouse device interfaces, one optical fiber interface, two USB3.0 device interfaces, six USB2.0 device interfaces, one network transmission interface, and a set of audio device interfaces, meeting the device interface needs of users.

Summary of Pros and Cons of ASUS P8H67:

Pros:

1. First-class brand, EFI interface

2. Digital power supply, supporting EPU energy saving

3. TurboV easy acceleration

Cons:

1. Higher price

Review: The ASUS P8H67 has excellent craftsmanship and materials, adopting a new generation of digital power supply design, supporting EPU energy-saving technology and TurboV overclocking acceleration, effectively balancing efficiency and performance. It also provides high-speed SATA3.0 and USB3.0 interfaces, suitable for building a high-performance integrated platform with great upgrade potential.
