Synopsis: This film brings the exotic and beautiful landscapes of Bali, along with its enchanting sounds, right into your home.

It allows you to experience the marvels of the magical water temples of Bali, where ancient pools adorned with intricate fountains, carvings, and statues see 10,000 gallons of sacred spring water flow through every minute.

You'll be taken on a journey to witness dragonflies, birds, butterflies, and frogs thriving amidst a sea of beautiful tropical flowers. High-definition close-ups reveal the lives of Bali's wildlife and the intricate beauty of nature.

You will also visit the Besakih Mother Temple and the Tanah Lot Sea Temple. Experience the sacred rituals that give Bali its vibrant energy, making it one of the most active places on Earth.

Bali (Bali) is an Indonesian island located at the western end of the Lesser Sunda Islands, roughly diamond-shaped with its main axis running east to west. It covers an area of about 5623 square kilometers with a population of approximately 2.47 million. The terrain is higher in the east and lower in the west, with mountains running across the island featuring over ten volcanic cones. The eastern Agung Volcano stands at an altitude of 3140 meters, making it the highest peak on the island. The climate is sunny with sufficient rainfall; most areas receive about 1500 millimeters of annual precipitation, with a dry season lasting around six months. Economically developed, Bali ranks second in population density after Java. Its residents, mainly Balinese, practice Hinduism and are renowned worldwide for their temple architecture, carvings, paintings, music, textiles, dances, and natural scenery. As a world-renowned tourist destination, more than 65% of the land is cultivated, producing rice, corn, cassava, coconuts, coffee, tobacco, peanuts, cabbage, onions, fruits, and palm oil. Cattle, coffee, and dried coconut are the main export products. The Lombok Strait on the eastern side of Bali serves as a boundary line between typical animals from Asia and Australia, holding special significance in biology.

Bali is also a famous tourist area in Indonesia, being one of the islands in the Lesser Sunda Islands east of Java, covering an area of more than 5560 square kilometers with a population of about 2.8 million. Located west of Jakarta, the capital, about 1000 kilometers away, it faces Java, where Jakarta is located, separated by the sea, only 1.6 kilometers apart. Due to its tropical location and oceanic influence, the climate is mild and rainy, with fertile soil, resulting in green mountains and waters all year round, vibrant flowers, towering trees. The Balinese people love flowers and decorate everywhere with them, earning the island the nickname "Island of Flowers" and the reputation of "Paradise in the South Sea" and "Island of the Gods." Most of the island consists of mountainous terrain, with several complete conical volcanoes scattered across the landscape. The terrain is higher in the east and lower in the west, with Mount Agung (Bali Peak) standing at an altitude of 3142 meters, the highest point on the island. Nearby is the active volcano Mount Batu, which erupted in 1963. Beaches such as Sanur, Nusa Dua, and Kuta offer some of the island's most scenic seaside bathing spots, characterized by fine sand, wide beaches, and clear blue seas. Every year, tourists from various countries flock here continuously.

The wooden carvings in the Hindu temples of Bali showcase the deep influence of Indian culture and religion throughout history, with most residents practicing Hinduism, making it the only place in Indonesia that follows this faith. However, this form of Hinduism differs from the version practiced in India, combining Hindu doctrines with local customs of Bali, known as Balinese Hinduism. Residents primarily worship the three major gods (Brahma, Vishnu, Shiva) and the Buddhist figure Siddhartha Gautama, while also offering prayers to the Sun God, Water Goddess, Fire God, Wind God, etc. Every household has a family shrine, communities formed by families have communal shrines, and villages have village temples. There are over 125,000 temples on the entire island, hence the island is also called the "Island of a Thousand Temples." Among these, the most famous is the thousand-year-old Besakih Temple, built on the slopes of Mount Agung, referred to as the "Navel of the World," dedicated specifically to the god of this intermittently erupting volcano. The tiered stone carvings of the temple resemble those of Angkor Wat in Cambodia.
