He almost represents a direction of Hong Kong films for two decades during the 80s and 90s. Even after the return of Hong Kong, the films he directed, such as "Dragon Fight" and "Black & White Forest," achieved over 2 billion HK dollars in local box office revenue. Adding the series of works he produced makes this number even more astonishing.

Wong Jing, originally named Wong Yat Cheung, was born in 1955. His father is the famous Hong Kong director Wong Tin Lam (who often makes cameo appearances as elder characters in recent Hong Kong gangster films). Under family influence, he watched many movies and memorized numerous plots, which laid the foundation for him to become a screenwriter and director later.
