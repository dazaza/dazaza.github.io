Can pelvic inflammatory disease cause a woman's period to be delayed?

by betty2012 on 2012-02-24 18:08:12

Can pelvic inflammatory disease (PID) cause delayed menstruation in women? The main reasons for delayed menstruation in women are two-fold: one is pregnancy, and the other is gynecological inflammation. Therefore, many women have this doubt: can PID cause delayed menstruation in women? What are the reasons for PID causing delayed menstruation in women? Experts from the Xi'an Gynecology Hospital provide specific answers based on these questions.

Experts at Xi'an Gynecology Hospital point out that the harm of PID is relatively significant. Thus, PID can affect women's menstruation. However, the impact of PID on women's menstruation mainly depends on the severity of PID. Different degrees of PID have different effects on menstruation. For example, when suffering from chronic endometritis, due to long-term inflammation stimulation, it affects the shedding, regeneration, and repair of the uterine lining, as well as the contraction of uterine vessels, leading to irregular menstrual cycles, excessive menstrual flow, prolonged menstrual bleeding, or irregular vaginal bleeding. Chronic pelvic inflammatory disease includes chronic endometritis, chronic salpingo-oophoritis, chronic pelvic peritonitis, and chronic pelvic connective tissue inflammation. Whether several types of inflammation coexist or exist independently, they can all lead to menstrual disorders.

The harm of PID is significant. Besides affecting menstrual irregularities in women, there are many other harms. Chronic PID often involves bilateral salpingitis, which over time can cause adhesion and blockage of the fallopian tubes, narrowing or even closing of the lumen, making it easy for fertilized eggs to fail to implant in the uterine cavity and form an ectopic pregnancy. Sometimes, the occurrence of PID can affect both fallopian tubes, causing lumen adhesion or even complete obstruction, leading to obstacles in the passage of ova, sperm, or fertilized eggs, resulting in infertility. PID can also lead to negative emotions such as anxiety, irritability, and depression in women, and even cause sexual indifference or aversion, severely affecting marital life and disrupting marital harmony. What are the harms caused by PID in women?

If a woman experiences delayed menstruation and it only occurs occasionally, this is a normal phenomenon, and women do not need to worry. However, if it happens frequently, it is necessary to go to the hospital for a check-up as soon as possible. Delayed menstruation sometimes leads to pelvic congestion during the menstrual period, causing soreness and heaviness in the waist and sacrum. About half of young women experience dysmenorrhea during menstruation, and usually, it is accompanied by symptoms such as headaches, low blood pressure, pallor, cold sweats, and infertility. Therefore, women should go to the hospital for a check-up as soon as possible to determine whether delayed menstruation is caused by PID.
