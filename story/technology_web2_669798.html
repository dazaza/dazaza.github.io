Australian man becomes genderless person after sex change, authorities change his gender to indeterminate

by anonymous on 2012-02-20 11:15:36

Nori Mae Velarde's passport. Typically, the gender field on everyone's ID is either male or female, with no other options. However, there really are people in this world who have a third choice. According to British media reports on the 15th, Nori Mae Velarde, 48 years old, was born in Scotland as a male, and at the age of seven immigrated to Australia. In 1990, she underwent gender reassignment surgery to become a woman. However, after stopping hormone injections, Nori discovered that she neither wanted to be a man nor a woman, and decided to become "genderless."

After two doctors confirmed their inability to determine whether Nori should be classified as male or female physiologically and psychologically, Australian authorities changed the gender field on Nori's birth certificate, passport, and other identity documents to "unspecified." In Nori's view, the concepts of male or female do not suit her, so "the simplest solution is to have no gender determination." Nori still remembers her experience when she went to the bank to change her identity information: "That woman's eyes widened when she saw my ID, and she said it was a good choice." Although she became the first genderless person, Nori believes there should be many others who share her thoughts.

The Australian judicial department confirmed that this is the country's first officially recognized case of a genderless individual. Even infants who are born with both male and female reproductive organs have not had such an exceptional case.

A member of an Australian transgender organization, Tracy Ochif, referred to this case as a gender revolution, especially for teenagers who begin doubting their gender from a young age. In many cases, neither doctors nor parents can determine their gender, often resulting in them undergoing surgery.

Some people believe that an increasing number of people oppose the practice of arbitrarily assigning a gender to infants born with both male and female reproductive organs, which means that there may be more "genderless" individuals in the future. (According to Information Times)
