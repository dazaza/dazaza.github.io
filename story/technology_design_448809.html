Students should learn to protect themselves

Dr. Wang Dawei, a Ph.D. in Educational Jurisprudence from the People's Public Security University of China, provided relevant analysis and preventive measures for young women to avoid sexual assaults: As long as the other party has the motive to sexually assault a woman, it can be considered sexual assault. For instance, telling dirty jokes also counts as sexual assault. Women aged 15 to 25 are the most vulnerable group. Offices and schools, where people aged 15 to 25 gather, are therefore "dangerous places." In an unequal relationship, when a superior forces an employee or a teacher forces a student, many people adopt silence and tolerance, which undoubtedly encourages the occurrence of sexual assaults in offices and schools.

According to incomplete statistics, at least 80% of women have experienced sexual harassment in their lifetime. Girls who suffer sexual assaults aren't necessarily "wild kids" or "sexy ladies" in our traditional concept; rather, the more "good girls" are likely to suffer sexual assaults. "Good girls" have always listened to their parents, thus believing "what adults say is right," making them easily deceived by adults with bad intentions. Moreover, "good girls" often have timid personalities, and when bullied, they usually dare not speak up, giving attackers opportunities. Therefore, we teach girls a phrase: Do not stay alone with a man for more than 30 minutes. I also give them a vigilance formula: Vigilance is inversely proportional to trust and familiarity. That is, the more familiar you are with someone or an environment, the lower your vigilance becomes.

Japanese surveys show that 60% of sexual assault cases occur between acquaintances. I want to emphasize again that we should be more vigilant with acquaintances. Those special warm closed environments are high-risk times and spaces for sexual assaults and harassment.
