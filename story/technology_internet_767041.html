Lei Jun's Untold Secrets: Stories Behind the Chip and Xiaomi TV

by anonymous on 2013-10-02 17:23:20

If Lei Jun were to be given an award, it might well be for "CEO who most supports his own products." Take a look: whether Qualcomm or Nvidia, as long as their technology is used in Xiaomi phones, they are described with terms like "industry leading." The video recording of the September 5th launch event is still pinned at the top of Lei Jun's Weibo page.

Just the other day, someone questioned whether the Xiaomi TV uses a V13 screen instead of a V14 one. Protective of his reputation, Lei Jun had to once again step forward to advocate for his product. He said that the V13 has better backlighting and performance metrics than the V14.

The actual difference between these two screens may be something that ordinary consumers don't even know about. But under the guidance of "Master Lei," they have come to believe — newer generations are definitely better than old ones, and Xiaomi always uses the latest and best. So when Lei Jun did not choose the "larger number" version for the Xiaomi TV screen, it became a big question.

In fact, besides this game of numbers, there are too many secrets that Lei Jun didn't reveal during this launch event.

A phone, a TV — on September 5th, Lei Jun took the stage to release these two products, and the fans below went wild as usual. After the frenzy, some fans asked around, "What is Nvidia? What is Qualcomm?" Such questions are a great irony to Xiaomi, which claims to have a "performance culture." The reason is simple: Lei Jun's audience has changed, from enthusiasts to fans.

Using Nvidia to balance Qualcomm

Actually, Lei Jun himself isn't a geek; he's just a businessman. Finding reasons, creating news, cultivating fans, enhancing interaction — using the method of idol creation, Lei Jun and his team made Xiaomi. Faced with the madness below, all Lei Jun needs to do is mention a few high-end component manufacturers and use some technical jargon mixed with English acronyms, and it’s enough to make the fans below shout repeatedly. However, no one thought about how important the things Lei Jun didn't say actually were. Because knowing the reason would only dim the halo of the idol.

Starting from Xiaomi 1, Lei Jun began to shape the grand image of Xiaomi, and as partners, Qualcomm seemed to have early established itself as the spokesperson for Xiaomi's performance. Lei Jun's promotion of Qualcomm let many Chinese consumers learn about this overseas chip company for the first time, and their cooperation was once extremely intimate.

However, at the launch of Xiaomi 3, when Lei Jun talked about Qualcomm chips, he only mentioned Jeff Lorbeck, Qualcomm's global VP being present, but the latter did not take the stage. If following the previous procedures of Xiaomi launches, this was the time when Wang Xiang or Shen Jin would surely stand up to state Qualcomm's advantages.

This scene was interpreted as Lei Jun beginning to adjust his relationship with Qualcomm, where Redmi was the earliest attempt. On Redmi, Lei Jun hoped to use Nvidia's chips, but ultimately switched to MediaTek due to the difficulty of compatibility. However, regardless of which chip Redmi ended up using, it wasn't important. What was important was that Qualcomm was no longer Xiaomi's sole choice. Xiaomi had passed the stage where it needed the industry giant to drive publicity and accumulate market presence.

On the Xiaomi 3 TD version, Lei Jun continued to abandon Qualcomm and chose Nvidia Tegra 4, describing it as "famous worldwide" and elevating it to be on par with Qualcomm — "the two strongest platforms in history."

Here, I must gossip a bit: Mr. Jeff once boasted to the Chinese media that Qualcomm never worried about its Chinese customers choosing other chip manufacturers to replace them, but Lei Jun really did it. Therefore, the claim that Lei Jun uses Nvidia to balance Qualcomm now doesn't seem far-fetched.

On the other hand, Latte learned that Xiaomi is in contact with domestic chip manufacturers. They previously wanted to invest in a domestic private enterprise founded in 2004, dedicated to broadband wireless communication and intelligent processor IC core technology research and development. But later, the latter rejected the investment request. Clearly, Lei Jun has his own expectations for chips, and his attitude towards Qualcomm will not remain in a prolonged honeymoon period. Lei Jun wants to control the supply chain himself to build his own Lei Jun Empire.

Baseband chip actually didn't use Qualcomm

Although unable to confirm with Lei Jun himself, Latte believes that Lei Jun didn't understand Qualcomm's advantage from the start.

From Xiaomi 1 to now, Xiaomi has launched seven phones, but Lei Jun only emphasized the powerful performance of Xiaomi's processor chip in each launch event, ignoring the baseband chip. As a result, Lei Jun made a joke about so-called "three-network phones" (official website telecom version) and telecom contract phones when doing Xiaomi 1 Telecom Edition.

At this Xiaomi 3 launch event, Lei Jun still didn't tell you that Xiaomi 3's baseband chip used Spreadtrum's sc8803G. Although this baseband chip is used by some mainstream models, such as Samsung Galaxy series, Note series, HTC ONE, etc., it is still necessary to emphasize here that separating the processor chip and the baseband chip is what Latte thinks Lei Jun doesn't understand about Qualcomm.

In fact, Qualcomm's advantage lies in chip integration. In other words, integrating the processor chip and the baseband chip into one solution, rather than combining two modules. For example, in other Xiaomi phone products, you'll see Xiaomi using Qualcomm APQ chips, not MSM. Simply put, the former is CPU+GPU, while the latter is the essence of Qualcomm's products.

Facing the upcoming 4G, maybe one day Lei Jun will realize that Qualcomm's strength lies in its technical reserves in these communication patents, not just a foreign brand that can enhance its own value. Just look at China Mobile's recent 4G terminal bidding, except for Huawei Hisilicon, all other winners used Qualcomm chips. Although Qualcomm never had production lines, it's just a design company, but patents are often more important to it than actual products. Actually, just look at Apple and Samsung, you'll understand that although Apple and Samsung also make processors, when making LTE devices, they still need Qualcomm's baseband chip.

Xiaomi TV is just a box of secrets

Regarding Xiaomi TV, Lei Jun talked even less. We can still use Qualcomm to introduce this topic, because whether Qualcomm is suitable as a TV chip is currently still one of the focal points of debate in the industry.

In the eyes of some traditional practitioners, many modules need to be added if Qualcomm's chip is to be used in TVs, which will increase the design complexity of the entire system. But Qualcomm started emphasizing its entry into smart TVs last year at CES, and claimed that its chips would become the preferred choice of manufacturers. Letv did just that, using Snapdragon S4 Pro in its 60-inch TV.

Putting aside these arguments where everyone has their own opinion, Lei Jun mentioned in the launch event that Xiaomi TV's screen is the top configuration from Samsung and LG, but why there are screens from two manufacturers, Lei Jun did not explain.

Samsung and LG differ in that Samsung uses SVA screens, while LG uses IPS screens. Technically speaking, these two screens achieve wide viewing angles differently, but their resolution, viewing angle, and brightness are the same. For example, when you touch the screen with your hand, if you clearly feel a light circle like touching water, it's SVA, also known as "soft screen" (IPS is commonly referred to as "hard screen").

This caused a journalist from a domestic portal digital news outlet to embarrass themselves a few days ago. Since the journalist personally received a soft-screen Xiaomi TV, but did not understand the detail that Lei Jun had to choose two types of screens due to supply chain control issues, the journalist questioned in their review article, "Why isn't it 'hard'?"

Of course, we can't blame this journalist. Usually, ordinary TV manufacturers wouldn't tell consumers which screen their purchased TV uses either, and the decision on what screen to use for a batch of products depends on the supply situation at the time. Xiaomi TV also considered this and chose two solutions, since capacity issues might affect early sales. Therefore, choosing another screen that can achieve the same effect as a backup plan indeed solves some problems, it's just that Lei Jun habitually kept quiet about it.

Back to the beginning of the article, regarding the issue of V13 and V14, if we use professional terms, this refers to different "generations" of screens. The term "generation" simply represents the size difference during cutting, but it has little impact on the cut screens. If we want to put it in simpler terms, it's like a sheet of paper can be cut into A4 or B5, it's just that their cutting methods are different, but it doesn't affect the quality of the paper. So, asking some TV manufacturer friends with Lei Jun's perspective, they would think Lei Jun is just using this as an excuse for hype.

In fact, this is also related to what kind of screens Lei Jun can get. In the TV supply chain, TV manufacturers usually invest or hold shares, or even raise funds to build upstream panel factories themselves, ensuring that they can definitely obtain large-scale supplies to guarantee their production and sales. But Xiaomi lacks such capabilities, and currently, Lei Jun's procurement ability is still too weak. Even some insiders revealed that the reason Lei Jun abandoned the 48-inch and chose the 47-inch screen this time is also due to supply chain considerations, not because it is the most suitable for users as he said.

"Optional" means you might not be able to use it

In addition, Lei Jun also mentioned a "mini HD wired set-top box" produced by Sony's contract factory Chuan, which also caused a roar of applause. But don't forget, before that, Lei Jun mentioned "optional accessories."

If the product is really so good, why is it "optional"? This is because not all users may be able to use such a product. Simply put, the wired TV set-top box can access the network and display relevant content through the decryption system inside the set-top box. This system can decrypt the encrypted wired TV network signals locally and then broadcast them. However, the encryption methods and rules for wired digital TV vary across the country, meaning that no single wired set-top box product can be used nationwide. This is also the difficulty facing Xiaomi's wired set-top box.

The solution provider for this box is Beijing Jia Shi Interactive, a company jointly invested by Yongxin Shiboh and Intel. Although Yongxin Shiboh basically covers more than two-thirds of the country, there are still some areas not covered, so Jia Shi Interactive cannot leverage the advantageous resources of its shareholder Yongxin Shiboh in these regions.

For example, Beijing Gehua uses the encryption system of Swiss NAGRA (Nagra Kudelski Group), so Xiaomi Box cannot leverage the advantageous resources of Yongxin Shiboh in Beijing and needs Jia Shi Interactive to integrate and adapt the encryption system with NAGRA Company. Similarly, this problem exists in more than ten provinces and cities including Chongqing, Guizhou, Shanghai, Tianjin, Guangxi, Shaanxi, Guangdong, Jilin, Liaoning, Xinjiang, and Inner Mongolia. Therefore, Lei Jun could only call this product an optional accessory.

Throughout the entire launch event, Lei Jun had too many secrets he didn't reveal, and these secrets more or less affected the halo Xiaomi built. If this layer of halo fades and the idol collapses, how many people will suddenly realize, and how much surprise will it cause? Everything is unknown. All Latte sees is that Xiaomi's valuation has just broken through $10 billion, successfully entering the forefront of China's internet companies, and Lei Jun's followers are no longer enthusiasts, but fanatical fans shouting "(phone) throw it down."
