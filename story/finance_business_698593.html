Si Haijian_How to retain sales guides

by stonepx918 on 2012-03-09 10:16:40

Headaches over this question happen everyday, and the answer seems obvious to terminal operators or store managers who are involved in retail business. Every time a practical training camp for franchisees/store managers/terminal supervisors is held, how to retain employees is always an unavoidable important topic! It's understandable why, by 2008, everyone understands the sentiment. Many bosses or their spouses serve as store managers, or even sales guides selling goods themselves. Of course, it's even more understandable why so many bosses/bosses' wives personally attend the "Store Manager Management Panoramic Simulation Practical Training Camp" and the "Sales Guide Sales Panoramic Simulation Practical Training Camp". The reason is simply that there are currently no capable store managers or sales guides worth cultivating in the store, or that the turnover rate of sales guides is really high, fearing that they will quit and join another company after learning, or even worse, open a store and become a boss, becoming one's competitor! After researching some companies, Shidun Beijing Management Consulting found the root cause: all these seem to be related to the difficulty in recruiting and retaining sales guides!

The sales guides in the store can never be retained, and whenever someone asks Mr. Liu this question. When mentioning "high turnover", I always counter-question: Excuse me, are you unable to retain good people or bad people? At this point, the terminal operators or store managers who ask this question always pause momentarily!

Don't immediately get nervous upon seeing high turnover among sales guides and start explaining. There is no inherent good or bad in the turnover itself. Sometimes, appropriate turnover can actually be something to celebrate. The key lies in whether the situation is "all good people leave and all bad people stay" or "all bad people leave and all good people stay". Undoubtedly, the former type of turnover must be highly vigilant about, as it can be very harmful to your store. It's essential to find the root cause. Please refer to the related thematic articles: Are sales guides really hard to recruit? First, figure out why "new sales guides" come to work for two days and then never return. In the latter case of turnover, you can go home and secretly rejoice in bed.

In the premium course "Seven Steel Knives for Building Strong Retail Teams at Terminals" by Xin Ying Xiao Company, optimizing the terminal retail team on a regular basis is indeed an important part of the sixth steel knife. The problem is that in the many retail terminals we have coached or interviewed, the loss of the terminal retail team is often an active behavior (the other party proposes not to continue working) rather than an active behavior (actively optimizing the team).

After explaining this principle, terminal operators or store managers often feel enlightened because those who are daily perplexed by the construction of the terminal retail team have never thought about this issue!

Then, I would counter-question again: Are the sales guides happy? Based on more than 10 years of personal experience dealing with terminal retail teams every day, the reason for asking this question is that within the entire terminal retail team in China, phenomena such as poor sense of belonging, getting by, low loyalty, high turnover, repetitive work, natural slackness, garbage emotions, maximizing benefits, lack of mission, and inability to effectively combine individual employee interests with overall store interests, are all very common.
