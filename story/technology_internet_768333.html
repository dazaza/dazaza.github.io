Nexus 5 Teardown: Easy to Disassemble and Repair, Except for the Screen

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 20:39:04

After much anticipation, Google has finally released the Nexus 5 alongside KitKat. The phone's appearance has significantly changed compared to its predecessor, the Nexus 4. However, with its still reasonable price, some models have even sold out in a short period of time.

New phone owners usually take great care of their devices. Except for iFixit, many phones or tablets generally cannot be restored to their original state after being dismantled by them, with extreme examples like the HTC One. However, this time the situation with the Nexus 5 is better; it is relatively easy to disassemble and repair.

Skipping over the specifications, let’s go straight to the teardown. Although designed with a non-removable back cover, in reality, the Nexus 5's back cover is quite easy to remove. After removing the back cover, the back of the Nexus 5 looks like the image above, clearly showing that the battery is also produced by LG.

After removing the protective shell below the battery, what is revealed is an Oompa-Loompa-colored daughterboard. The image shows both sides of the daughterboard, both of which are welded with electronic components. The most recognizable item within the yellow box in the middle of the upper image is the Micro-USB port; the blue boxes on either side indicate antenna contact points; the cyan box above the yellow box indicates speaker contact points. In the lower image, the microphone is within the dark red box, while the RGB LED indicator light is within the orange box that matches the color of the daughterboard.

The main circuit board is similarly used on both sides. The most prominent and largest area within the red box in the upper image is Hynix H9CKNNNBPTMRLR-NTM 2 GB LPDDR3-1600 RAM. Below the RAM is a layer we cannot see: the Qualcomm Snapdragon 800 2.26 GHz quad-core processor. Within the yellow box to the left of the RAM is the Analogix ANX7808 SlimPort transmitter. Within the small blue box above and to the right of the RAM is Texas Instruments' BQ24192 I2C 4.5 A USB/power adapter charger. Within the orange box below and to the left of the RAM is Qualcomm's WCD9320 audio decoder, and finally, within the magenta box is Avago's ACPM-7600 front-end module.

On the other side of the main circuit board, the red box remains the most prominent, indicating SanDisk SDIN8DE4 16 GB flash memory. Within the orange box to the right of the flash memory is Qualcomm's WTR1605L LTE/HSPA+/CDMA2K/TDSCDMA/EDGE/GPS transceiver. Within the small blue box below the transceiver is Avago's RFI335 chip module. Within the yellow box at the farthest edge on the left side is Broadcom BCM4339 5G Wi-Fi and low-noise amplifier integrated chip. In the magenta box at the top-left corner is InvenSense MPU-6515 motion capture chip (gyroscope and accelerometer), and within the adjacent black box is Asahi Kasei AK8963 three-axis electronic compass. Above the flash memory, within the cyan box, is Qualcomm PM8841 power management chip.

This is the prominent camera on the back of the Nexus 5. The optical stabilization of the Nexus 5 is achieved through the InvenSense IDG-2020 dual-axis gyroscope within the red box.

After taking everything apart, only the screen component remains. Unfortunately, this part cannot be removed separately, meaning that the screen and the body are highly integrated. If the screen breaks, it will be difficult to repair, and even if it can be repaired, the cost will be higher than usual.

As per tradition, here's a final family portrait.

In conclusion, iFixit gave the Nexus 5 a high score of 8 points for repairability due to its extensive modular design, making some component replacements very easy. The use of irreversible methods such as glue during assembly is also minimal, allowing the device to be reassembled after disassembly. The screws used for fixing are standard crosshead screws, so no specialized tools are needed for disassembly and assembly. The biggest deduction was for the panel glass and LCD screen, so users should take good care of their Nexus 5s going forward.
