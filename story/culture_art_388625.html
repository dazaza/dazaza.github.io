The Call for New Curriculum Reform: Abandon Old Concepts, Create Efficient Classrooms

by dianxin8 on 2011-03-24 22:04:23

A truly effective classroom must undergo four transformations: the essence of teaching, the essence of learning, the essence of evaluation, and the essence of growth. We must complete such a metamorphosis to achieve success like a butterfly. Educational reform is not just about changing classrooms; it's about transforming deeper aspects within the classroom. China has undergone eight educational reforms, with the first seven failing without proper summaries. Experts from ks5u College Entrance Examination Resources Network have concluded that in China, any attempt at improvement has only one fate - failure. Today, there are still moral criticisms against limiting teachers' instructional time in an efficient classroom. Some even argue that education is simply about letting children speak in class. Educational reform must move away from excessive lecturing. A true classroom isn't defined by how much you lecture but by the way you lecture. As long as there is direct instruction, it remains a traditional classroom. The distinction between a traditional and an efficient classroom lies in whether teaching occurs. If teacher and student roles remain unchanged, can we claim it’s an efficient classroom? The best way to keep students engaged in class is for the teacher to stop lecturing. Statistics from ks5u show that if a teacher speaks 8,000 words in class and you speak 7,000, students will only engage with 1,000. Only by speaking less can students possibly speak more.

Some ask if a moderate approach is possible, questioning the extremity of certain rules. Eventually, we need to let students take their first independent step in life. A good mother lets her child get up after falling. One cannot learn to fly by lying in bed. Therefore, reform isn't about improvement or merely upgrading a carriage's engine. We should not focus on superficial changes. Even with my explanations, some teachers still can't adapt. Changing someone's mindset is one of the hardest tasks, akin to stopping people from eating peppers in Hunan or onions in Shandong. Despite the difficulty, it must be done.

Efficient education shouldn’t solely focus on utility. I believe a truly effective classroom is one dedicated to the nation. I summarize this as 'a supermarket of knowledge, a carnival of life.' Seeing these ten words should excite you; if not, it indicates a lack of spiritual richness. Education needs to return to rationality beyond mere poetic romanticism. From an educational perspective, an effective classroom must have three characteristics: initiative (critical due to the passivity of traditional classrooms), vitality (focusing on students' mental states, unlike traditional classrooms which suppress students emotionally), and creativity (true creation happens naturally when life is inspired and ignited, unlike the rigid preset plans of traditional classrooms).

In traditional classrooms, teachers often use planned economies, unable to cater to each student’s capacity, leading to overfeeding or underfeeding. Doctors and teachers differ in that doctors tailor treatments based on symptoms, whereas educators often apply a one-size-fits-all solution for forty years without adaptation. First, can we classify students better? Blackboards play a crucial role in this regard. Surrounding the classroom with blackboards transforms it into a supermarket where different boards address various needs, enhancing individualized learning.

Spiritual jubilation manifests in learning, living, and growing states. Imagine a classroom where both teacher and students enter devoid of emotion, resembling a funeral parlor. If attending funerals saddens us for days while weddings bring joy, why subject students to eight daily funerals? I propose that if a school experiences student suicides, its principal should be held accountable.

Stories in the 22 Principles of Efficient Classrooms by ks5u experts are all real. Teachers must find ways to turn around students contemplating suicide. Current education focuses too much on time consumption. Students completing homework until 11 PM nightly highlights inefficiency necessitating extra effort. Efficient classrooms require zero homework commitments from schools. Efficiency doesn’t mean sweating more but working smarter.

Modern education creates non-readers. UNESCO reports that China's per capita reading is merely 1.6 books annually. Traditional education distorts ideals, making retirement a common aspiration among students. Our education sacrifices lifelong development for short-term exam success, which is not worth it. Elementary teachers enjoy seeing every child as a genius, yet middle school sees them as apes, reflecting a failed educational system pushing the nation to the brink.

Teachers today suffer greatly. To rejuvenate both teachers and students, reducing workloads and stress is essential. Instead of focusing on attendance, improving teachers' working conditions matters more. Would teachers welcome eliminating lesson preparation or grading? These are possibilities through reform. By rotating responsibilities in creating study guides, we aim to make female teachers more radiant, signifying hope in education. Currently, our system turns beautiful young teachers into weary ones within three years. We must prevent teachers from becoming martyrs who destroy themselves alongside achieving goals. Education involves imperfect individuals guiding others toward perfection. Unfortunately, 52.38% of teachers exhibit signs of mental disorders. In Wuhe County, all teachers are considered mentally ill, meaning they share strong spiritual beliefs. However, some revert to old habits post-reform, lacking insight and hope.

UNESCO reports that Chinese children rank fifth from the bottom globally in creativity. This number deeply concerns me. Despite claims of innate Chinese creativity, educated individuals seem less creative than those uneducated. Observing society, well-off individuals often skipped formal education, and unmarried women over thirty-five are likely highly educated. Advanced degrees seem useless in practical terms, even hindering personal lives.

Chinese educational theories that fail to implement are like decorative shotguns that cannot hunt rabbits. When parents ask experts how to motivate students, responses like "not my research area" reflect disconnection.

The root problem in Chinese education is classroom syndrome—boring classes. Reform is urgent, akin to rescuing children from boiling oil. Twenty-one percent of middle school students suffer depression. Both teachers and students exhibit mental health issues. Effective classrooms address these problems directly.

After diagnosing the issue, appropriate solutions become clear. For example, if your partner is an alcoholic, forcing them to drink low-grade liquor instead of their preference might help reduce dependency. Similarly, classrooms should prioritize student comfort. Training basic behaviors early helps create conducive environments. Internet cafes satisfy three needs: freedom, exploration, and achievement. If classrooms meet these needs, they could be equally engaging. To combat internet addiction, allow controlled online access with specific games following set guidelines.

Experts from ks5u emphasize that classrooms must make students comfortable. Elementary teachers understand preparing students physically and mentally is crucial. Proper classroom management satisfies natural curiosities and achievements, making learning enjoyable rather than burdensome.
