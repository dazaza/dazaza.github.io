2. Why Make a Forum

Forums, as everyone knows, don't collect advertising fees as well as regular webpages. They also don't make as much money as webpage-based sites. However, I think forums are lively, which is why I like them. Because they gather a circle of people. It's easy to retain users. Interaction is strong. But this isn't absolute; not all sites should be turned into forums. This article only provides forum operation experience. Those who don't like making forums don't need to read this.

3. What Kind of Forum to Make

I suggest everyone choose based on your own preferences and resources. Forums in any industry can succeed. Personally, I find excellent examples like Tianya, or industry-specific ones like Houxin and Xiake Webmaster Forum, which are very well done.

4. What Kind of Space Do You Need for a Forum?

If your forum attachments are small or few, you need less space. Forum programs are usually only a few MBs, but the database will gradually increase. This specific issue needs specific analysis.

5. How Many "Hardworking Moderators" Do You Need?

When making a forum, it's best not to rely on one person. Recruit moderators as soon as possible. At the beginning, you can use measures like Qb rewards. Moderators need to work hard at the start. At least, the content updates need to come up. I don't recommend scraping content for forums; scraped forums tend to be garbage (personal opinion). Attachments should be localized as much as possible because some resources become outdated. Besides updating content, moderators need to set section rules, register some small accounts, post replies to create an atmosphere of having many repliers.

6. New Forums Need Some "Tricks"

Because new forums are obscure, few people like to stay. Thus, your promotion and retention won't be effective. It's suggested to appropriately employ some "tricks." Nowadays, most forums have virtual user number systems, and you can also register a batch of members yourself. Don't have too many; they should be proportional to the content and reply volume. For example, at the beginning, you can have 20 members online. And it's essential to display the detailed online list (of course, once the forum grows big, this isn't necessary).

7. Forums Need Reward Systems

Besides moderators, you need to retain members. The content must attract these members. To create an atmosphere, new forums can adopt hidden posts that require replies to view. However, images and attachments must be readable by guests. The reward system should be clear. For active members, upgrade permissions, promote positions, issue forum coin rewards, QB, physical rewards. These are all good measures.

8. You Need to Establish a "Consumption System"

Forum virtual coins are the motivation for users to post and also a symbol of identity. Many members like to be the "rich people" of the forum. Therefore, downloading a post attachment might cost a certain amount of coins, while posting content adds a certain amount of coins. This needs to be adjusted according to your own forum.
