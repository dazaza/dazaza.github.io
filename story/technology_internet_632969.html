Schneider Electric Boosts China's Shipbuilding Industry

by juyaqufa004 on 2011-12-19 10:37:11

On November 23, 2011, Hu Zhen, Director of the Distribution Terminal User Industry for Schneider Electric's Power Distribution and Energy Division, gave an exclusive interview to the *International Ships* magazine. The topics covered Schneider Electric's expertise and latest achievements in the maritime sector, global and Chinese maritime development trends, as well as opportunities and challenges.

**Schneider Electric in China**

Hu Zhen first introduced Schneider Electric as a global expert in energy efficiency management and solutions, established in France in 1836. With a history of 175 years, Schneider provides comprehensive solutions for over 100 countries across sectors such as energy and infrastructure, industry, data centers and networks, buildings, and residential markets. Since 1987, Schneider Electric has been rooted in China for more than 20 years, offering advanced products and comprehensive technical services to Chinese customers. Its products are widely used in various aspects of China’s economic construction, promoting the industrialization process.

Currently, China is Schneider Electric's second-largest market globally. In China, Schneider has set up 77 offices, 26 factories, 6 logistics centers, 1 training institute, 3 R&D centers, 1 laboratory, collaborations with 500 distributors, and a nationwide sales network. Schneider Electric China currently employs nearly 22,000 people.

Schneider Electric not only excels in the aforementioned markets but also shows remarkable performance and achievements in vertical fields such as maritime industries. As a leading provider of ship automation and power distribution solutions, Schneider Electric boasts over 90 years of deep experience and professional expertise in the maritime field. It offers comprehensive solutions for commercial ships, offshore engineering, and naval vessels, including full-ship power distribution systems, full-ship automation systems, ship video security systems, ship energy management systems, and remote monitoring. Its global service system provides services throughout the entire lifecycle of ships, including 24/7 online technical support, onboard services, and preventive maintenance activities based on service history records.

**"Made in China, Benefiting the World"**

China's shipbuilding industry has become one of the most outward-oriented industries in China. Currently, about 80% of the completed ships are exported to 112 countries and regions. China's total shipbuilding output has exceeded Japan's for many consecutive years and competes with South Korea for the top spot globally.

With the continuous increase in demand for oil, offshore oil development has become one of the key focuses of the national energy strategy. After the start of the 12th Five-Year Plan, offshore oil development will enter a high-speed development phase, and the construction of offshore engineering equipment will see a peak.

As the world's factory, China's demand for maritime trade of energy, iron ore, raw materials, and export products will remain at a high level for a considerable period in the future, driving sustained strong demand for ship products. To further enhance international competitiveness, China's shipbuilding industry and offshore engineering equipment industry must focus on improving technical capabilities and design innovation. With the intensification of competition between domestic and international enterprises in the maritime industry, there is no doubt that the competitive pressure faced by China's shipbuilding enterprises and related supporting industries will greatly increase. Only through advancements in technical strength and design innovation can they maintain a competitive edge.

Hu Zhen believes that the development of shipbuilding and shipping cannot be separated from the global economic environment. Regardless of how the shipbuilding and shipping markets change, there will always be demand. What Schneider Electric focuses on is how to use foresight within the limited market space, providing professional support from the initial stage of ship design to the operation of the vessel, maximizing customer benefits. Hu Zhen said, "There is no best product, only the most suitable," emphasizing the importance of tailoring products and solutions to meet customer needs. Future shipbuilding will develop towards larger and more integrated designs, with luxury cruise ships entering the Chinese market. Schneider Electric aims to assist China's shipbuilding industry in reaching global markets.

**Professional Energy Efficiency Management Experience and Global Service System**

Schneider Electric has over 100 energy-saving and efficiency-enhancing solutions, along with more than 300 energy-saving and efficiency-enhancing products. Its maritime solutions have unique technical advantages, helping clients achieve comprehensive safety, reliability, security, scalability, efficiency, and cost-effectiveness, reducing production costs and improving operational levels.

Schneider Electric closely monitors the development trends of the global and Chinese maritime industries, evolving from an excellent product and equipment supplier to an overall solutions provider. As an energy efficiency management expert, Schneider integrates its expertise in building, IT, security, power, and industrial processes and equipment into a unified framework, offering customers an open, transparent, energy-saving, efficient, and standardized EcoStruxure energy efficiency management platform, saving up to 30% of investment and operating costs.

A year ago, Schneider Electric signed a strategic cooperation agreement with Shanghai Maritime University to jointly build a maritime energy efficiency management platform based on energy-saving and efficiency-enhancing solutions. This collaboration aims to create the world's first research case focused on ship energy analysis for maritime energy efficiency management, providing valuable references and decision-making support for future studies and applications in maritime energy efficiency planning.

Schneider Electric consistently prioritizes innovation as a development focus. Schneider Electric China invests 5% of its annual sales revenue in R&D, dedicated to providing higher quality, lower cost, and more user-friendly products and solutions. Currently, Schneider Electric has established three R&D centers in China, employing 440 professional engineers. Additionally, Schneider Electric has a dedicated maritime R&D center in Shanghai, providing R&D support for both the Chinese and global markets. Brands under Schneider Electric such as TE Electrical and Merlin Gerin are industry leaders with over 80 years of practical application experience, making them key players in the maritime field.

Schneider Electric has a comprehensive global maritime service system with service institutions spread worldwide. Six global hotlines are available, including a Chinese hotline for the Chinese region, ensuring close alignment with customer needs and guaranteeing excellent service quality. Strong support from Schneider Electric is available at major ports in Europe, North America, South Africa, the Middle East, and Asia. Schneider Electric can resolve various issues encountered anywhere in the world at the fastest speed possible. Schneider Electric is committed to helping customers "Use Effectively, Enjoy Fully."
