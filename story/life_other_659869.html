I'm sorry, I love you...

No reason to give any excuse or explanation for my words, and I dare not recall the memories that have piled up bit by bit. Neither can I find an escape route for myself. It's my own mistake, choosing this way, I cannot cover up my mistakes and commit another one. Because of the mistakes, I will be punished, and I am willing to accept it. Punishment cannot make up for the harm caused; it will only torment my body and soul. The pain I've caused, I'm sorry, I can't help you erase it. I can only accept the punishment and endure the pain, a pain that's even more painful than yours, so that we both experience the feeling of pain, because we share in each other's suffering.

Suddenly, I gave myself a heartfelt smile. It turns out that I had kept you stored away in a box, quietly placed with my toys in the drawer. After some time, I would take it out and look at it with a smile, laughing and saying how foolish I was back then. Those memories were beautiful and pure, not caring about right or wrong, not concerned about the weather tomorrow. On rainy days, we could hold umbrellas, and on sunny days, we could lie on the grass. Occasionally, those ten minutes might be your most "tragic" ten minutes - girls don't like boys being late. Back then, we had our Kongming lanterns, our songs, our flowers, our laughter, and our dreams. Back then, we felt time flew quickly, and there was always more to say. A class, a day, a week, a month, a semester passed by, leaving us with a little regret on our way home. Back then, we were envisioning our dreams and striving for them. I knew my dream had no chance of coming true, but has your dream awakened? Gently closing the drawer, I took a deep breath, feeling as if my grandmother smiled slightly when she saw my niece making strange movements. Getting up and leaving, walking out of the house, there is a scene I want to relive. Walking between the flower beds, I quietly admired the clusters of blooming chrysanthemums. Year after year, the flowers are similar, but people are different.

In fact, there's a kind of fear that has always accompanied me, but I have hidden this fear. I once strived hard to pursue what I wanted, but the results were always so tragic. No matter how much I put in, there was no return. I felt tired. Suddenly, if all I desired was placed before me, my heart trembled, and I dared not reach out to touch it. I couldn't explain why I was afraid.

At the end of a year, I casually wrote down these notes. I like this place, yet I hate this city. What kind of departure is it? Painful, yet always unavoidable. Saying, waking up the sleeping pedestrians, go, it's time to part ways.
