An 80-Year-Old Woman Drank About 3 Tons of Baijiu in 30 Years (Picture Included)

by gogbjijang on 2011-11-17 15:14:06

Grandma Tang Xiuying

Grandma Tang Xiuying, who lives at No. 26 Xifang Alley in the Baima District, leads a truly carefree life! At 85 years old, she drinks half a catty (about 250ml) of alcohol every day and enjoys tasting tea. Even more enviable is that she only drinks and doesn't buy anything—these items are all gifts from others, like a video conferencing system. Neither an official nor a businessman, why do people keep giving this elderly woman presents?

Drinking since her youth, almost ruining her wedding prospects

Yesterday at noon, I visited Grandma Tang by reputation, and she was drinking alone. Her only dish to accompany her drink was mushroom-stewed tofu, yet the elderly woman savored it with great relish. "I've been drinking since I was a girl, for over 70 years!" Grandma Tang's words shocked me. Seeing my surprised expression, she chuckled and revealed the reason—her father would drink a few cups of wine after selling his flatbreads every day to relieve fatigue. During her childhood, Grandma Tang often stole sips of her father's wine when he wasn't paying attention, and unexpectedly became addicted. As she grew up, she no longer hid her habit and began drinking openly with her father. Grandma Tang laughed as she said that her future mother-in-law was very dissatisfied with this habit, and their marriage almost fell through because of it. Fortunately, her husband was determined to marry her. After their marriage, her husband would often personally cook a couple of dishes to accompany her drinks.

At the age of 44, Grandma Tang's husband passed away from liver cancer, leaving behind four children. Grandma Tang said that during the toughest times of her life, she never left alcohol for even a day. She said, "If I don't drink, I'll get sick."

For the past 30 years, Grandma Tang has been drinking an average of more than half a catty of strong liquor every day. A rough calculation shows that she has consumed three tons of liquor.

Great interpersonal relationships; tobacco, alcohol, and tea are all gifts from old neighbors

Grandma Tang also likes to taste tea, and smoking is another indispensable part of her life.

Grandma Tang is not financially well-off, with only 1300 yuan of retirement pension each month. Besides covering her daily meals, she also subsidizes her unemployed son. Whenever there's an opportunity, she will give some money to her grandson. Drinking half a catty of alcohol a day, smoking a pack of cigarettes, and enjoying several cups of tea... these expenses are not small. How can Grandma Tang afford them? Grandma Tang said, "They're all gifts from others!"

Why do people compete to give Grandma Tang gifts? The director of Longwangmiao Community, Ms. Xu Shuilan, hit the nail on the head: Grandma Tang has great interpersonal relationships, and everyone gives her gifts during festivals. Originally, Grandma Tang has a straightforward personality. Whenever others are in difficulty, she will definitely lend a helping hand. When her monthly salary was only 30 yuan, if a neighbor borrowed 10 yuan from her, she handed it over without hesitation. Whenever there were conflicts between mothers-in-law and daughters-in-law, she would always intervene voluntarily, determined to mediate until the conflict was resolved.

Her sense of justice and enthusiasm have made her a large group of good sisters and friends. Not long before the Mid-Autumn Festival, Auntie Zhu sent two bottles of wine, a pack of cigarettes, and a box of tea. Auntie Zhu introduced that her children received frequent care from Grandma Tang when they were young, so she has been giving festival gifts to Grandma Tang for over twenty years.

There are dozens of people like Auntie Zhu who give gifts to Grandma Tang, including shoemakers and urban management officers near her home who give her cigarettes. "She's like our own grandmother," sighed a city management officer surnamed Chen from the Hongwu Road Subdistrict. Grandma Tang moves her stools outside every day for people to rest. People don't know how to thank Grandma Tang, so after learning about her three major hobbies, they all bought gifts according to her preferences.
