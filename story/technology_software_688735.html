Zhang Yali was pulling her good friend to test-ride a self-made "giant" bicycle in the plant area. Photo by reporter Wang Qiang. According to the New Culture Herald (reporter Yang Jin Hao), at about 10 o'clock on December 25th, inside the yard of a fertilizer factory on Xian Road in Chuan Ying District, Jilin City, Zhang Yali was riding her self-made "giant" bicycle and prepared to go around the courtyard. Because the bicycle was too big, it was very strenuous to ride. She was out of breath before she finished half a lap. "This bike needs further debugging before it can be ridden on the road," said Zhang Yali.

Zhang Yali is 49 years old. Three months ago, she suddenly had an idea: to make an ultra-large bicycle for her son. "The raw materials for making the bicycle were all purchased from the dismantling market and the second-hand market," said Zhang Yali. After having the idea, she started drawing the design of the bicycle on paper. Once the design was completed, due to insufficient space at home, she began the manufacturing process in her friend's fertilizer factory.

"In order to build this bike, my husband quit his job," said Zhang Yali's wife Mei Jianhua. After having the idea of making a bicycle, her husband quit his temporary job and devoted himself entirely to making the bicycle. During the three months of making the bicycle, her husband was busier than when he was working. Every day at 8 o'clock, he would arrive at the fertilizer factory to start making the bicycle and wouldn't return home until it was dark.

"To highlight the size of the bicycle, I specifically bought two wheels from a bulldozer to use as the front and rear wheels of the bicycle. The frame was welded piece by piece using steel plates. The power system is transformed from the pedals, chains, and gears of ordinary bicycles, and the seat is made from old sofas," said Zhang Yali. The principle of power for this bicycle is the same as that of a regular bicycle; it can be ridden by one person or three people together, and four or five people can sit on the backseat. The height of the bike is 3.2 meters, length is 5.5 meters, the diameter of the wheel is 1.5 meters, the total weight of the whole vehicle is about 1 ton, it took more than 3 months to finish, and cost over 20,000 yuan.

"I have always liked making things since I was a child. After working, I became a machine repair worker, and the desire to make things became even stronger," said Zhang Yali. Fifteen years ago, she made a multi-functional bicycle that could exercise, which had several ways to ride, just not as large as this one.

"Our family has never been very wealthy. My husband stopped working and instead spent money making a bicycle. At first, I didn't agree with him and we argued. But my husband made the car for our son, and later I saw his seriousness and passion for making it, so I gradually agreed," said Mei Jianhua.

"Cartoons themselves are a form of exaggerated art, and I thought about making a particularly huge entity to give to my son who works far away," said Zhang Yali. Although the bicycle has already been made now, there are still some details that need adjustment. Once it's ready, she plans to ride the bike to Shenzhen and personally present it to her son to express her care for him.

"I made this bicycle for my son," said Zhang Yali. Her son has always been interested in drawing comics since he was a child. He even created a comic book when he graduated, and his current work is also related to comics. Because her son has been working far away for a long time, she and her family miss him very much. In order to enhance her son's creative inspiration and encourage him to create better works, she made this bicycle.

"I plan to ride this bike to Shenzhen next summer and personally deliver it to my son," said Zhang Yali. Since the bicycle is too large in volume, if there are steep slopes, someone will have to push it. It's impossible for one person to ride all the way to Shenzhen, so she wants to invite some comic lovers and bicycle tourism enthusiasts to go to Shenzhen together. She initially plans to invite six or seven people to take turns riding this bike.
