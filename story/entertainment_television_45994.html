Still Determined: Episode-by-Episode Plot Introduction

Episode 6:

Zhou Lingyun, through Feng Xiaobing who had been transferred to work at the Municipal Commerce Bureau, found Academician Gu Shixing of the Engineering Academy. Initially arriving in Beijing, Zhou Lingyun brought one million yuan in cash to purchase the secret formula for Xinshuyin. He Mingcong arrived with Chen Zhijiang at Gu Shixing's house to also acquire the secret formula and unexpectedly met Zhou Lingyun. The two confronted each other without yielding. Unexpectedly, Gu Shixing neither accepted Zhou Lingyun’s one million yuan nor entertained Chen Zhijiang, dismissing both.

After returning to Nanjiang, Zhou Lingyun believed that Gu Shixing didn’t want to give him the secret formula because he was unwilling to hand it over to a joint venture, fearing it would fall into foreign hands. Zhou Lingyun quickly registered a new sole proprietorship factory and planned to use a non-joint venture entity to again approach Gu Shixing for the secret formula. In contrast, Chen Zhijiang had no money, let alone any factory or equipment. Chen Zhijiang and He Ningsheng hoped He Mingcong could learn from his mentor Gu Shixing what the bottom price for the secret formula was. Unexpectedly, Gu Shixing set an astronomical price, leaving everyone disappointed.

Despite returning empty-handed, Fuji believed obtaining the secret formula from Gu Shixing still held hope and gave Zhou Lingyun many promises. Zhang Chengli had been waiting for Zhou Lingyun to return from Beijing and introduced him to advertising planner Hu Yue. Under Hu Yue's planning, Zhou Lingyun launched a powerful publicity campaign, hoping to convince Gu Shixing through media coverage that he was the most suitable person to develop Xinshuyin. Both Chen Zhijiang and He Ningsheng knew that Gu Shixing's secret formula was a priceless national treasure, and trying to obtain it without spending a penny was akin to a fairy tale.

While worrying about finances, Chen Zhijiang's childhood female classmate Ajiao proactively offered to lend him money, but Chen Zhijiang refused. He Ningsheng couldn't understand why, mistakenly thinking there was a special relationship between Chen Zhijiang and Ajiao. In reality, Chen Zhijiang clearly understood that the astronomical price set by Gu Shixing was unattainable. Chen Zhijiang decided to visit Gu Shixing in Beijing once more, making a final effort. Meanwhile, the promotional campaign orchestrated by Zhang Chengli and Hu Yue also influenced Gu Shixing in Beijing, who, after reading numerous reports about Zhou Lingyun's achievements in newspapers, developed a favorable impression of him.

Episode 7:

He Mingcong further introduced Zhou Lingyun's character to Gu Shixing, prompting Gu's desire to meet Zhou Lingyun again. Seeing extensive reports about himself in the newspaper, Zhou Lingyun became somewhat complacent, while Feng Xiaobing reminded him that this approach might be excessive. Zhang Chengli and Hu Yue visited Zhou Lingyun, informing him that another round of promotion was coming, assuring him that this would surely impress Gu Shixing and secure the ownership of the Xinshuyin secret formula for Zhou Lingyun. Zhou Lingyun was confident this attempt would succeed.

Chen Zhijiang visited Gu Shixing again, only to be rejected once more. Even his return train ticket was paid for by He Mingcong, but Chen Zhijiang was not willing to leave empty-handed. He secretly returned the ticket, prepared overnight, drew up a blueprint for the new pharmaceutical factory, and rushed to the alley where Gu Shixing lived early the next morning. While having breakfast, he encountered Zhou Lingyun, who told him he had come with three million yuan this time. Confident in his victory, Zhou Lingyun seemed like a bucket of cold water poured on Chen Zhijiang, yet Chen Zhijiang still clung to his last hope as he entered Gu Shixing's home.

Leaders from the Traditional Chinese Medicine University heard that Gu Laoshi was going to donate the secret formula and came to congratulate him. Surprised, Gu Shixing asked how they knew about it. The university leaders handed him the day's newspaper, where Gu Shixing saw the promotional advertisements made by Hu Yue. Unexpectedly, the exaggerated publicity did more harm than good to Zhou Lingyun, as Gu Laoshi was very dissatisfied with the inaccurate reporting. In front of the college leaders, Gu Shixing took out two pieces of paper, each written with the words "gain" and "loss." He asked Chen Zhijiang and Zhou Lingyun to draw lots, seeing whose luck was better. Zhou Lingyun drew the "loss" paper and was disappointed, while Chen Zhijiang was confident, knowing that Gu Shixing was helping him succeed.

After Zhou Lingyun left disappointedly, Gu Shixing explained why he wanted to give the Xinshuyin secret formula to Chen Zhijiang. Gu Shixing signed a contract with Chen Zhijiang for the usage rights of the secret formula, stipulating that within six months, the development of the new drug must commence. If the trial production of the new drug failed within the deadline, Gu Shixing retained the right to reclaim the usage rights of the secret formula.

Episode 8:

Zhou Lingyun ultimately returned empty-handed, feeling extremely frustrated. He Mingcong was also surprised that Gu Shixing entrusted the secret formula to Chen Zhijiang for development. Gu Shixing shared his inner thoughts with He Mingcong. Although Chen Zhijiang obtained the secret formula, developing it into a traditional Chinese medicine posed even greater difficulties—no money, no equipment, no factory, and no laboratory.

Although Zhou Lingyun did not secure the usage rights to the Xinshuyin secret formula, he did not give up. At an old comrades gathering, Zhou Lingyun proposed funding a factory for Zhi Jiang Pharmaceutical Company, with the new drug research and development rights belonging to Hongxing Factory. This was immediately rejected by Chen Zhijiang, as he sought complete intellectual property rights for the new drug and refused to transfer core technology to others, causing dissatisfaction among the old comrades.

He Ningsheng criticized Chen Zhijiang for showing no consideration for old comrades, feeling that his actions were overly harsh. Chen Zhijiang also felt he might have gone too far but wasn't sure where he went wrong. He Ningsheng believed that lacking funds and refusing collaboration would benefit no one. Once the deadline passed, if Gu Laoshi reclaimed the Xinshuyin secret formula, all their efforts would be wasted. Chen Zhijiang realized the severity of the situation but wasted no time, actively seeking solutions.

Hongxing Factory under Zhou Lingyun was increasingly struggling, with many employees resigning. Disheartened, Feng Xiaobing returned home, where her daughter, Chen Yuanfan, asked why she was upset with her father. Feng Xiaobing said that Chen Zhijiang was indifferent to his plight, leading Chen Yuanfan to suspect emotional issues in her mother's support for Zhou Lingyun. Feng Xiaobing evasively attributed it to camaraderie among comrades.

He Ningsheng also thought Chen Zhijiang's refusal to collaborate with Zhou Lingyun was excessive, suggesting they should cooperate. However, Chen Zhijiang had his own considerations, believing that in commercial competition, one cannot solely consider comrade sentiment. Market economy follows its own rules that cannot be replaced by comrade emotions. To build a new factory, He Ningsheng went to the town government for land but faced rejection.

With limited joint-venture interests in China, Fuji decided to withdraw investments, plunging Zhou Lingyun's Hongxing Factory into dire straits. As Fuji prepared to leave, he stopped by Chen Zhijiang's dumpling stall to bid farewell. Though Chen Zhijiang showed no warmth, Chen Yuanfan, overhearing Fuji's plans to go to the U.S., expressed interest in his help for her study-abroad aspirations. Fuji agreed to assist her.

In Japan, after resigning following the withdrawal of Japanese headquarters' investments, Fuji left Nangjiang. Chen Yuanfan appeared at the airport, hoping Fuji would help her achieve her dream of studying in the U.S., which Fuji promised. Zhang Chengli informed Zhou Lingyun that Chen Zhijiang was facing loan rejections everywhere. Increasing media hype could force Chen Zhijiang into transferring the secret formula.

Episode 9:

Chen Zhijiang urgently needed funds to build a new factory. An old comrade suggested applying for a bank loan, which Chen Zhijiang decided to try. Before leaving, Fuji stopped by Chen Zhijiang's dumpling stall, surprising Chen Yuanfan with news of his trip to the U.S., sparking her interest. Chen Zhijiang struggled to raise funds for drug development, being rejected by multiple banks. Proposing the secret formula as collateral, the bank deemed it worthless. Meanwhile, Gu Shixing in Beijing anxiously awaited updates on the new drug's progress, urging He Mingcong to call.

After Fuji resigned and left Nangjiang, Chen Yuanfan appeared at the airport, asking Fuji to help her realize her dream of studying in the U.S., which Fuji readily agreed to. Zhang Chengli told Zhou Lingyun that Chen Zhijiang was struggling to secure loans, suggesting increased media pressure might force him to transfer the secret formula.

Chen Zhijiang and He Ningsheng were at a loss, receiving urgent calls from He Mingcong in Beijing regarding the development progress. Reports in various newspapers highlighted Chen Zhijiang's inability to develop Xinshuyin, alarming He Ningsheng. Believing this to be Zhou Lingyun's doing, he confronted him, who instead expressed sympathy and proposed a joint venture. He Ningsheng thought this a good idea, confident he could persuade Chen Zhijiang.

Chen Zhijiang carefully analyzed recent newspaper promotions, viewing them not as a problem but as an opportunity to leverage Zhou Lingyun's publicity to enhance Xinshuyin's reputation, aiding future loans. He Ningsheng mentioned Zhou Lingyun's willingness to provide factories and equipment for cooperation, but Chen Zhijiang viewed it as a defensive tactic. Instead, he decided to auction off the Xinshuyin secret formula to increase its value, much to He Ningsheng's opposition. Gu Shixing in Beijing, hearing about the planned auction, felt uneasy and intended to visit Nangjiang but was dissuaded by He Mingcong.

Episode 10:

Feng Xiaobing believed it inappropriate for Chen Zhijiang to auction off the Xinshuyin secret formula, doubting he would do so. Confirming her doubts via phone, Zhou Lingyun saw this as an opportunity to fairly compete for Xinshuyin during the public auction. He Ningsheng urged Chen Zhijiang to collaborate with Zhou Lingyun rather than auctioning, warning that the auction proceeds might be less than Zhou Lingyun's offer.

Hearing her son planned to auction the secret formula, Chen Zhijiang's mother considered selling their old house. Upon learning of the auction, He Mingcong rushed from Beijing to Nangjiang to stop it but was convinced otherwise by Chen Zhijiang. Around this time, Zhang Chengli's Hongtu Pharmaceutical Factory collapsed. After selling the factory and equipment, the bureau approved four million yuan for new projects. Zhang Chengli encouraged Zhou Lingyun to participate in the Xinshuyin auction, financing his bid.

The Xinshuyin secret formula auction commenced, with Zhou Lingyun personally participating. Bidding reached seven million yuan, which Zhou Lingyun believed secured the formula until realizing it fell short of the reserve price. Originally, Chen Zhijiang set the minimum acceptable price at ten million yuan. With no successful sale, Chen Zhijiang planned a second auction, requiring public disclosure of the reserve price. Disappointed, Zhou Lingyun received a call from Fuji, who had returned to Nangjiang.

Fuji aimed to support Zhou Lingyun in the second auction, promising twenty million yuan from Japanese investors. If successful, they would pursue a second collaboration. With Fuji's backing, Zhou Lingyun confidently prepared for the second auction. Despite Feng Xiaobing's warnings against Fuji's profit-driven nature, Zhou Lingyun remained determined to compete with Chen Zhijiang and seized this opportunity.
