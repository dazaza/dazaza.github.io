Manchester United's flying wing is more stylish than 'Showboat', becoming the Devil's second brother; Real Madrid's 20 million offer hard to get him.

by qinglai888 on 2009-12-16 13:10:39

Manchester United's flying wing is more dashing than "Saber" and has become the Devil's second-in-command; Real Madrid's 20 million can hardly buy him. Since November, a new source of firepower has emerged for Manchester United - Antonio Valencia. The Ecuadorian has scored 3 goals in 6 league matches, averaging one goal every two games. The situation where he only provided assists without scoring goals has been rectified. Currently, he has played in 16 league matches for Manchester United, scoring 4 goals. Although 4 goals in 16 matches might not seem significant, considering his recent performances, Valencia has become Manchester United's second-largest attacking threat after Rooney.

It's embarrassing to note that despite having 14 players scoring goals this season in both league and cup competitions, Rooney stands far above with 13 goals. The second-highest scorer, Owen, has only managed 7 goals, with only 2 coming in the crucial league matches. Berbatov's 4 league goals have come at longer intervals. Only Valencia has continuously scored recently, with each goal being more beautiful than the last. Although he ranks second alongside Berbatov on the league scorers' list for the team, it's clear that fans recognize Valencia's recent form as far superior to Berbatov's.

Today's goal clearly demonstrated how strong teams, while adhering to high-speed and efficient counterattacks, emphasize the combination of individual creativity and opportunity-grasping abilities. Counterattacks are easy to execute, but if they're just fast without proper passes or accurate final shots, any miscalculation will make fast attacks ineffective. In this play, Scholes' precise lofted pass, Berbatov's flick-on followed by an overhead kick cross (a perfect blend of technique and imagination), and finally Berbatov's right-footed volley from a well-adjusted position all echo classic finishing moves reminiscent of Teddy Sheringham and Ole Gunnar Solskjær. These elements were fully replicated in Valencia’s performance today.

After the match, Ferguson lavishly praised Valencia: "That finish was excellent. Antonio can definitely increase his goal tally because we know during training that he's actually a very good striker. Today's goal is a great example. Antonio has made tremendous progress. His expansive play in the game was particularly impressive. He keeps improving himself, and we're very satisfied." What does "expansive" mean? It refers to Valencia's playing style—his activity in the midfield and forward areas has expanded significantly. He now moves and switches positions with ease, unlike when he first joined and was more of a dedicated right-winger. His numerous one-touch passes throughout the game had a high success rate. Executing one-touch passes requires a high level of understanding between the player and teammates running along different routes, especially when maintaining overall high-speed progression. Valencia's willingness to perform such plays shows that he has fully integrated into Manchester United.

Manchester United should feel fortunate to have secured Valencia. This summer, Bayern Munich offered 18 million pounds, and Real Madrid went up to 20 million, both higher than what Manchester United paid. Fortunately, Wigan Athletic didn't care about the difference of a few million and emphasized that Valencia's destination would depend solely on himself. Already accustomed to playing in the Premier League, Valencia ultimately chose Manchester United. Looking back after half a year, this deal has started to yield significant returns for Manchester United. Although Valencia hasn't yet reached Cristiano Ronaldo's level of destructiveness, his performance of 4 goals and 3 assists in 16 matches, especially his stellar displays since November, makes people believe that although his position is somewhat fixed, the 24-year-old Valencia is advancing towards becoming an absolute right-wing mainstay who could leave an important mark in Manchester United's illustrious history.
