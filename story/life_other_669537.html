Fate does not allow us to be together!

by lili1 on 2012-02-19 22:48:39

Love you, but fate does not allow!

It has been a long time since I last said goodbye, and I never knew who to talk to!

I liked a boy in junior high school, and I've kept this feeling for three years, but I have never told him. I didn't dare to tell him.

We were in the same class in the second year of junior high school. He was held back a grade due to an injury to his leg, and that's how I got to know him. We sat at front and back desks, and I used to look at him every day. He always read novels, didn't listen to the teacher, and talked with classmates around him. Actually, he really liked math and physics, maybe most boys like these subjects.

I am not very pretty, like an ugly duckling. Sometimes classmates would make fun of me, but he always protected me and spoke up for me. I was really grateful to him, and my affection for him grew more and more.

One time, the teacher made a large-scale change of seats in the class. Because he was tall, he was moved to a side seat. I sat in the middle, and my classmate next to me was an average student. So I persuaded him several times to switch seats, and finally succeeded. We became deskmates. We played together in class, sometimes he read novels, and sometimes we played cards together.

But later, I found out that he had a crush on a girl, though they had already broken up. Later, they met again, and all I could do was to bless him! But I knew there would be no result between them.

Later, we were in the same class again in the third year of junior high school.

This year, he changed. He stopped reading novels, stopped playing around, and his grades improved rapidly. While I stayed in the same place. I knew his progress was the result of his efforts! He said he wanted to get into No.1 High School, and I silently thought in my heart that I also wanted to get into that high school...

But it seemed that fate always liked to play tricks on me. On the 16th day of the first lunar month, I went out to play and broke my leg, so I dropped out of school. His wish came true.

Later, I repeated the third year of junior high school. I told myself that I must get into No.1 High School, and I must confess to him. I got into a key high school, but I wasn't assigned to his school. I went to another school.

Now he is very excellent, and very good at studying. I think our connection has come to an end...
