File: Ms. Feng's US marriage announcement claims 300,000 Chinese people want to marry her, rejects Asians

by mlwang on 2011-11-16 14:51:39

Luo Yufeng, who gained online popularity due to a series of outrageous statements, has started seeking a partner once again! According to a Weibo post by netizens, Sister Feng is now looking for a spouse in the United States and has simultaneously released an all-English "marriage proposal". Sister Feng refers to herself as China's most popular celebrity, and her marriage requirements remain extremely high, changing according to the national situation. Tsinghua and Peking University are no longer on her radar; now, Sister Feng demands Harvard and Oxford. One condition in the "proposal" that caught everyone's attention is: no Asians.

Besides proposing her own standards, the flyer also includes Sister Feng's personal resume at the end. Sister Feng claims, "I am China's most famous celebrity. There are over 300,000 men in China who want to marry me, but none of them meet these conditions." Some netizens tried calling the phone number on the marriage proposal but were unable to get through. Others attempted to check if the owner of the number was indeed Sister Feng, but they could not find any information. Therefore, whether this "marriage proposal" is genuine or not remains uncertain. Wherever Sister Feng appears, ordinary people stay away.

Sister Feng's extreme behavior has also attracted the attention of American media. Recently, the American magazine People published an article titled "Getting Close to China's Most Hated Reality Star," pointing out that Sister Feng has begun a new life in the United States, currently working as a nail technician. However, back in China, Luo Yufeng has already become "infamous."

From being ridiculed online to suddenly obtaining a U.S. green card, Sister Feng's transformation has sparked astonishment among many netizens, who believe it is an organized publicity stunt. However, yesterday, Sister Feng claimed on Weibo that she does not have a team: "From distributing flyers in 2009 until I left China in 2010, there was no company or team behind me. All the hype and marketing events were planned, operated, and marketed entirely by myself. I've been exceptionally smart since childhood, known as a genius. Planning and hyping these things are a piece of cake for me."

Sister Feng's partner selection criteria in the "marriage proposal":

1. Age 25-31 years old, unmarried with no children

2. Height 5.74-6.11 feet (approximately equal to 1.74-1.86 meters)

3. Must graduate from a prestigious university

4. Must have a master's degree or higher

5. Preferably owns a house and car

6. Cannot be Asian

Sister Feng's personal resume in the "marriage proposal":

1. From a poor family in Chongqing

2. Height 4.82 feet (approximately equal to 1.46 meters), often wears high heels, appearing to be 5.08 feet (1.54 meters) tall

3. Associate degree

4. Reads a lot, skilled at writing poetry and prose

5. Strictly adheres to family planning policies, only wants two children

6. Became China's most famous celebrity in 2010, with 300,000 Chinese men wanting to marry me, but none meeting the conditions
