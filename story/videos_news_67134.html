Shanghai Bus 842 Explosion Video

by diglog2008 on 2008-05-06 15:08:38

At around 9 o'clock yesterday morning, a fire broke out on a No.842 bus near the Huangxing Road close to Guoquan Road in Yangpu District of Shanghai. The public security department has confirmed that three people were killed and twelve others injured.

At about 6 p.m. yesterday, the Shanghai Public Security Bureau issued another official statement indicating that according to preliminary investigations, the fire was caused by a passenger carrying flammable materials onto the bus. The police are continuing their investigation into the matter.

The fire inside the bus

engulfed the entire bus in less than a minute

The incident occurred near the intersection of Huangxing Road and Guoquan Road during the morning rush hour. As such, there were many passengers on the burning bus, many of whom worked near Wujiaochang. Wujiaochang is one of the emerging four major commercial sub-centers of Shanghai.

Mr. Xiong, an eyewitness, clearly witnessed the entire event and took some pictures with his mobile phone. First, a No.842 bus pulled over as usual, the rear door opened, and everything seemed normal. Suddenly, many people rushed towards the rear door at the same time, with some being pushed down, yet the people behind still urgently moved forward. Mr. Xiong felt something was wrong. At this moment, he saw sparks appear in the middle of the bus. Meanwhile, the remaining passengers continued to rush out, and some even smashed the windows to escape. However, the fire spread quickly, with thick smoke billowing. In less than a minute, the flames had completely engulfed the No.842 bus, leaving no way for the people inside to escape. About six minutes later, the windows exploded. The bus continued to burn, with the flames rising higher and higher, damaging the wires approximately 8 meters above the ground.

The fire burned for ten whole minutes, completely destroying the iron roof and body of the bus. The shape of the windows was severely distorted, and the seats were left only as metal frames.

Only the rear door was open

Some people escaped by breaking the window glass

"It only took a minute for the people to be gone. Seeing all this, my heart went cold," Mr. Xiong said. He witnessed the trapped passengers struggling on the bus but was unable to help them.

According to multiple eyewitnesses, the burning bus only had its rear door open, with the front door closed. Almost all the escaping people ran towards the rear door, causing chaos inside the bus. Some passengers at the back of the bus did not have time to escape.

Some people escaped by smashing the window glass. One witness saw two people successively climb out from the rear window. The first person was stuck in the window due to their size, unable to get down. The second person then gave him a push to speed up, allowing both of them to successfully escape. At the time, there was also a young man following them who wanted to crawl out but failed. He eventually died inside the bus.

Due to concerns about explosion, most people who escaped from the bus, if their injuries were not severe, ran further away, stopping only after running more than 10 meters. Someone saw an old grandmother holding her grandson run out, while an old grandfather was burned to death at the door, almost having escaped.

According to eyewitnesses, most of the passengers who couldn't escape were at the back of the bus. When the firefighters pulled them out, they were unrecognizable, and their genders could not be distinguished.

There was a faint strange smell on the bus

The phenomenon of lost emergency hammers is common

Grandma Jiang, living in Zhongyuan Road, held her 2.5-year-old granddaughter and escaped from the fiery sea. She recalled that when the fire started, she smelled a faint strange odor, followed by noticing balls of fire rising beside the middle seats of the bus, accompanied by thick smoke.

According to reports from Shanghai media, since air-conditioned buses are relatively sealed, many buses are equipped with emergency hammers. Passengers can use these hammers to break the windows and escape in emergencies. However, many bus companies have reported that some of the onboard emergency hammers have been stolen by passengers, leading to a very common phenomenon of loss. This means that if a fire breaks out on a bus, passengers will find it difficult to locate tools to break the window glass, making escape more challenging.
