The US military will recommend the government give up the ability to win two wars simultaneously.

by lovea6o4 on 2012-02-24 13:27:20

&Nbsp;

A US official revealed the other day that due to the issue of defense budget cuts, the US which has always emphasized its global combat capability is currently facing great military adjustments. The Pentagon will advise the government to give up its ability to win two wars at the same time.

According to a January 3 report by the British Reuters, the US Pentagon announced on that day that US Secretary of Defense Panetta would publish the results of the discussion regarding US strategic interests with the Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff Martin Dempsey on the 5th. It was reported that the related discussions had lasted for about eight months.

An official from the US government revealed that the contents advised by the Ministry of National Defense included reducing the deployment of US troops in Europe and only retaining two strike teams. Another official said that each team would include approximately 3,000 to 4,000 soldiers depending on their structure. An anonymous US official also commented that this move had extremely high practical feasibility, it was not a new arrangement, and it had been brewing for several years.

The first official introduced that the Ministry of National Defense also advised the government to give up the national policy of participating in and winning two wars at the same time. Instead, the US would only need to participate in and win one major war while maintaining the ability to disrupt any aggressive plans of a second enemy country.

The report pointed out that the above-mentioned suggestions regarding the consideration of US strategic interests were initiated by the former US Secretary of Defense Gates. The purpose was to adapt to US President Obama's plan to cut 400 billion military expenses over the next 12 years while not affecting the US's strategic interests.

Currently, both the US military and the White House have refused to comment on the above content, but the White House spokesman Carney stated that Obama "deeply participated" in the military's discussion process and met with Panetta, Dempsey, and others. Carney emphasized that after experiencing ten years of war, the US is currently at a major turning point. New challenges and opportunities require the US to reshape its national defense policies.

It was learned that some domestic media in the US have already shown dissatisfaction with the Ministry of National Defense's plan. The Christian Science Monitor commented that the Pentagon seemed to be abandoning the strategic principle that the US had long adhered to, namely winning two wars at the same time. The US Fox News Network also linked this with the current tense relationship between the US and Iran, claiming that the Pentagon's suggestion "sent wrong signals" to Iran.
