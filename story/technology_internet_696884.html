[Qingdao Cuisine] Creative Delicious Ribs [Fruit Jam Sweet and Sour Ribs]

by qssqwe215 on 2012-03-08 11:17:48

A few days ago, I received a box of New Zealand kiwifruit. I used two of them to make kiwi jam. But since there was no bread at home, I didn't know how to eat it. I also wanted to try the new Lee Kum Kee Double Tea Dark Soy Sauce for stewed meat to see if it's effective. It's also a good time to improve my husband's diet. So, I combined the kiwi jam with the double tea dark soy sauce. Considering that kiwifruit is nutritionally rich and there is some sugar in the jam, I decided to make spareribs with jam. The result was amazing, highly recommended!

The biggest feature of this sweet and sour spareribs is that no sugar was added, but the taste is absolutely sweet and sour. This method can be applied in various ways. You can use leftover jam as a sugar substitute when stewing meat. It can not only color the dish but also add fruit fragrance and enhance the complex aroma of the stewed meat, achieving multiple benefits. The recipe for homemade kiwi jam will be updated in a later blog post.

This time, I used spare ribs with cartilage instead of pure ribs. The cartilage in the ribs can effectively supplement calcium, allowing you to enjoy the meat while getting your calcium. Some friends have asked me about the two types of tea in the new Lee Kum Kee Double Tea Dark Soy Sauce. Let me clarify: Lee Kum Kee Double Tea Dark Soy Sauce contains Pu'er tea and green tea, mainly for its fresh aroma and double de-greasing function.

Ingredients: appropriate amount of ribs, bay leaves, cloves, fennel, star anise, Sichuan peppercorns, cinnamon bark, scallions, ginger slices.

Seasonings: Lee Kum Kee Double Tea Dark Soy Sauce, cornstarch, salt, cooking wine, white vinegar, kiwi jam.

Preparation:

1. Place the ribs in cold water, add a little Sichuan peppercorn, star anise, and cooking wine, bring to a boil and blanch.

2. Once boiling, immediately remove the ribs, drain, and set aside.

3. Drizzle 2 ceramic spoonfuls of Lee Kum Kee Double Tea Dark Soy Sauce over the ribs.

4. Add an appropriate amount of cornstarch, mix well, and marinate for about 10 minutes.

5. Pour an appropriate amount of oil into the pan, heat to about 70%, then add the ribs.

6. Quickly stir-fry the ribs. When the oil temperature reaches around 90% hot (smoking), stir evenly, remove, and drain.

7. In another pot, add an appropriate amount of water, along with half a block of concentrated chicken soup bouillon. If using more ribs, you can add one whole block.

8. Add 2 ceramic spoonfuls of Lee Kum Kee Double Tea Dark Soy Sauce, a little white vinegar to adjust the broth, and an appropriate amount of salt for seasoning.

9. Add about half a small bottle of kiwi jam.

10. Other flavors of jam can be substituted, and the amount of jam can be appropriately increased or decreased.

11. Put the ribs into the seasoned broth, add star anise, cinnamon bark, cloves, fennel, and bay leaves.

12. Bring to a boil over high heat, then simmer for about 40 minutes to an hour. Finally, reduce the sauce over high heat.
