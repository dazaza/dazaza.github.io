Three men interfered with the weighbridge at the oil depot to steal oil and made more than 1 million yuan

by zsh0827595 on 2012-03-08 09:05:16

Tongchuan News (Reported by Du Junling) - A tanker truck that was clearly underweight passed the weighbridge without any discrepancy. It turned out that the weighbridge had been fitted with an interference device, and three men had profited from it, reselling 176 tons of diesel in just over ten days.

The diesel inventory has been short by several tons for days on end.

On March 25, the Wangjiahe Police Station of the Wangyi Branch of the Tongchuan Public Security Bureau received a report that there was a problem with the oil quantity at a certain unit's oil depot, and the police immediately began their investigation. The affected unit is located in Wangjiahe and is an oil storage depot where a Chinese petrochemical company recently wanted to store 10,000 tons of diesel.

It is understood that petroleum transportation has strict regulations: when the oil is transported out from the enterprise, it must be weighed, and it must also be weighed upon arrival at the oil storage depot. On March 18, during the oil depot's inspection, it was discovered that a significant amount of grade zero diesel was missing upon entry into the warehouse. "They thought they had made a calculation error and looked for reasons themselves," said the officer. In the following few days, the shortage of oil frequently occurred. On March 25, several tons of diesel were again found missing, leading them to realize that something was amiss, prompting them to file a police report.

During the investigation, the police found that one tanker truck was a major suspect. The record showed that this truck carried 40 tons of oil when it left a petrochemical enterprise, but at a toll station en route, it only showed 32 tons, yet when it arrived at the oil storage depot, it once again showed 40 tons. After questioning the driver, the police learned that he was employed by Wang Mou, a person from northern Shaanxi. Upon learning that the matter had been exposed, Wang fled to Xi'an overnight and discarded the remote control of the interference device along the way.

At around 3 a.m. yesterday morning, the police captured Wang in Xi'an and brought him back to Tongchuan for questioning.

A remote control interference device ensured the oil appeared "sufficient."

Wang Mou, aged 37, hails from Zizhou and engages in coal and oil businesses. He met Liu Mou and Mao Mou, who are involved in transportation, during his business dealings. In early March, Wang heard about a type of electronic interference device that could interfere with the display on a weighbridge; even if the weight was insufficient, it would still show as exactly correct.

"They discussed together and decided to sell the oil halfway and then use the interference device to interfere with the scale at the oil storage depot, believing no one would discover them," the officer explained. Wang immediately spent more than 4,000 yuan to purchase the interference device and his accomplices busied themselves contacting oil transporters.

In mid-March, the three learned that the Wangjiahe oil depot needed oil deliveries. Wang took advantage of the management personnel's lack of attention, installed the receiving device of the interference device on the weighbridge of the oil depot, and set the receiving weight. As long as the confirmation button on the remote control was pressed within a certain range, the interference device would start working, and the weighbridge would only display the preset weight. "After filling up with oil from the enterprise, they sold part of the diesel along the way," the officer said. Before the vehicle passed the weighbridge at the oil storage depot, they activated the electronic interference device, allowing the oil to be stored normally. Since mid-month, Wang, Liu, and Mao have committed over ten offenses, reselling 176 tons of diesel and making profits exceeding 1.2 million yuan. After the incident, the police have already arrested Liu and Mao.

Currently, due to suspicion of fraud, the three suspects have been criminally detained. (Editor: SN005)
