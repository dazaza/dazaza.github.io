16-Year-Old Boy Tries to Set Himself on Fire with Gas Cylinder After Argument with Father

by dehua900 on 2011-05-08 09:41:00

After arguing with his father, a young man resorted to extreme measures.

The cab was connected to the lower deck by a ladder. When Mr. Zhou reported the incident, the young man had already carried a gas cylinder from the cabin to the cab.

Mr. Zhou is a ship captain from Shiji town in Xiangshan County. Recently, he bought a large iron fishing boat which was moored at the shore and not yet in use. Although the ship was not in use and no one was on board, Mr. Zhou would check the ship every day.

At 10:25 AM, the young man's emotions became unstable again as he started clicking a lighter. The police station chief immediately ordered an emergency rescue.

At 9:45 AM, nine officers from the Shijia Border Defense Police Station arrived at the scene. They saw a teenager sitting in the cab holding a kitchen knife in one hand and hugging a gas cylinder in the other.

From 9:45 to 10:27 AM - A 42-minute life-and-death rescue operation took place.

At 9:57 AM, fire trucks and ambulances successively arrived at the dock.

Yesterday, Zhao, a 16-year-old boy who was successfully rescued by border defense police, was criminally detained for endangering public safety. The previous morning, he held a knife and occupied a fishing boat, opening the gas cylinder in his arms, attempting to go down with the ship. After rescuing Zhao, the police investigation revealed that Zhao had become addicted to the internet and stayed out all night, leading to an argument with his father who was trying to persuade him to return home, causing him to take extreme actions.

At 9:30 AM, the ship captain discovered a knife-wielding teenager hugging a gas cylinder.

Police investigations found out that the teenager surnamed Zhao was only 16 years old, having just celebrated his birthday on January 20th this year. Zhao moved with his family from Bazhong, Sichuan to Shijia several years ago. He dropped out of school after completing the first year of junior high school. While his family worked, he often went to internet cafes to play. According to his father, Zhao wasn't addicted to online games but liked watching movies in the internet cafe.

Mr. Zhou felt strange because he didn't know this person, nor had he offended anyone recently, so why would someone be targeting him? He asked the young man to leave the ship. Unexpectedly, when the young man moved, a kitchen knife appeared from behind his back. Shocked, Mr. Zhou quickly retreated from the cab and called the police.

At 9:30 AM on January 23rd, Mr. Zhou boarded the ship and approached the cab door where he saw a young man inside. Initially, Mr. Zhou thought it was a visiting crew member. As soon as he reached the door, the young man became agitated and shouted, "Don't come closer!"

On the night of January 22nd, Zhao stayed in the internet cafe until midnight without returning home. His father then went to the internet cafe to call him home, but Zhao refused. His father scolded him, and they argued. Unable to bring his son back, the father returned home.

When the situation stabilized, to ensure the young man's life safety, the soldiers on-site immediately carried him out to the shore and sent him to the hospital by ambulance.

The cabin was only two square meters in size, almost completely unventilated. If timely measures were not taken, the young man might have taken even more drastic actions; if delayed, he could also die from poisoning. The atmosphere at the scene was very tense.

The police quickly used a previously prepared axe to break down the cabin door. Soldiers immediately sprayed water into the room with a high-pressure hose. The police station chief led the first team of rescuers rushing into the cabin.

Currently, after medical examination, Zhao’s vital signs are normal. However, Zhao is under investigation by the Shijia Border Defense Police Station for allegedly endangering public safety through dangerous means.
