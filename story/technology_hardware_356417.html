And very openly express various opinions on men - no sincere intention, no disturbance

by wuraowo98 on 2010-05-27 21:31:24

Last weekend, I was idle at home and watched "If You Are the One". I didn't pay attention to this entertainment program before, but as more people were talking about it, I also followed the trend. I increasingly felt that there was no highlight left for entertainment, and it was purely for promoting a certain website. It seemed that they spent a lot of money on it. Even the so-called male guest's understanding level of the female guest had to be searched on Baidu first! It was simply too much!

Looking at the history of television programs, there have been many similar matchmaking shows, but none of them has had as much influence as "If You Are the One"! A friend said the reason was due to lack of conflict points and contradictions. Traditional methods are relatively mild, mostly adopting point-to-point, self-introduction matching and then exchanging contact information. Chinese people seem to be accustomed to this traditional matchmaking method. The entertainment program "If You Are the One" is face-to-face, with 24 beauties facing one man. They can freely express their views, state their own standards for choosing a partner, and frankly express their opinions on the man. Their specific requirements in terms of material wealth, appearance, and looks have overturned the traditional concept of choosing a partner; regarding the issues of second-generation rich men and ugly men, whether on stage or offstage, there are quite intense verbal battles. And it is precisely this antagonism that makes the program more interesting. It also verifies another saying: there must be resistance, contradiction, and conflict, and only with conflicts will more people participate. Therefore, the director of this entertainment program must rack their brains to select people from all walks of life to participate. In summary, pilots, train drivers, steelworkers, sanitary napkin sellers, in short, one principle: everything is possible! Everything you could possibly imagine!

Chairman Mao once said: Dating without the intention of marriage is like being a hooligan. If there is no result, then it can only mean one thing: "If You Are the One" is also a place for fame and fortune, a place for people to become famous through hype. Facts have proven this point: Ma Yimeng became popular, Ma Nuo became popular, Yan Fengjiao became popular, and also the second number Zhu Zhenfang who voluntarily withdrew after saying "twenty thousand yuan just to touch a hand". It's just an entertainment program, there's no need to make a fuss over trivial matters and follow the wave of public opinion to comment on who is right or wrong. When more people watch, naturally more people will express their opinions! Still, it's better to watch it with a tolerant and relaxed attitude, don't let any view in it affect your real life, it's just an entertainment program!

However, for this entertainment program, I would previously support it during my boring time to help boost its ratings, but that was when it still had some appeal! Programs entertain themselves, they are tools for passing time and entertainment! It's a win-win situation! But now, in my eyes, this program has lost its appeal. Reasons: one, fake and not genuine. Two, a substitute for advertising promotion. Three, viewers become one of its roles, and the purpose of the program becomes increasingly obvious! Don't we watch entertainment programs for entertainment, or do entertainment programs use us to make profits? Is the focus ultimately "If You Are the One"? Or the audience? I hope the content of the program lives up to its name and is as true and reliable as its name suggests!
