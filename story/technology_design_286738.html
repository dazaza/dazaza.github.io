Society is very much into the halo of white-collar and gold-collar workers. Many people categorize white-collar and gold-collar workers as high-income groups, which are considered to be above the white-collar level. According to relevant surveys in Beijing, those who earned more than 5000 yuan before were considered white-collar workers. Little did they know that in Beijing, earning over 5000 yuan has become quite common due to the high cost of living in the city. Without 5000 yuan, it's impossible to cover daily living expenses. Therefore, defining a specific income level for white-collar workers might not be entirely appropriate.

So, how much income should white-collar workers have to be considered as such? In fact, there is indeed a close relationship between white-collar status and income, but it cannot be completely defined that one must earn above a certain amount to be considered white-collar. The lifestyle of white-collar workers is somewhat bourgeois, which is mainly because of their good income that allows them to have such consumption awareness, like having breakfast and dinner mostly outside or participating in gatherings.

White-collar workers generally possess a certain level of professional work ability, and this point must first be acknowledged. The working posture of white-collar workers is often independent and capable, with unique insights when handling problems. Of course, those who possess these abilities naturally have higher salaries. The standard definition of white-collar seems to align with the salary standard. However, regional differences and urban disparities can also cause variations in white-collar income.

Take Beijing as an example; a salary of 5000 yuan only covers daily expenses and falls short of the white-collar lifestyle. There are also people who own houses and cars but earn less than 5000 yuan, yet they are still part of the white-collar class. White-collar definition cannot be determined solely by salary standards.

What exactly is a white-collar worker? People may just know this term without knowing who exactly qualifies as a white-collar worker. White-collar is actually a borrowed term, referring to those who generally work in offices, engage in mental labor, usually in welfare-rich, well-treated enterprises and institutions, earn high wages, dress neatly, always have a confident smile, handle problems efficiently, and live a fast-paced life.

That’s why we say the white-collar lifestyle is somewhat bourgeois. Because their appearance differs from ordinary people, they not only value their looks but also place greater emphasis on inner and spiritual enjoyment. Although their work is stable, the intense competition brings significant pressure, leading to many white-collar workers suffering from sub-health conditions. Their lifestyle makes many people envious and admiring, so white-collar professions have always been surrounded by a halo.

White-collar workers value material aspects, requiring higher wages to satisfy their consumption needs. They also value spiritual enjoyment, thus needing a fusion of Eastern and Western cultures. The white-collar lifestyle is simple yet fashionable. Although they experience some pressure from the workplace, they are evidently still happy. As middle-level backbone members of enterprises and institutions, they are confident that they are not necessarily the highest-income group, but rather look at the way of life and urban differences.
