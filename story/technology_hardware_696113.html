Presidents of the five major commercial banks solve the problem of difficult financing for small and medium-sized enterprises

by b4w9f6x8f3 on 2012-03-07 20:43:32

By reporter Ren Xiao, Beijing - At the international forum hosted by CCTV's Economic Channel on June 6th titled "How to Solve the Financing Difficulties of Small and Medium Enterprises (SMEs)", the presidents of the five major banks expressed that SME businesses are "profitable", and banks need to use business innovation to develop this sector.

The president of Industrial and Commercial Bank of China (ICBC), Yang Kaisheng, stated that the development of SMEs not only relates to the overall economic development but also concerns the bank's own operation and management. Among ICBC's corporate loan clients, 88% are SMEs, and 50% of the loans are allocated to SMEs.

"Unless you don't want these 88% of clients or 50% of the loans, there is no reason for us not to pay attention to this issue or improve our work in this aspect," he said.

Yang Kaisheng mentioned that from January to May this year, ICBC's total outstanding loans to SMEs amounted to 2.22 trillion yuan, with an additional 325.4 billion yuan in new SME loans, a year-on-year increase of 17.16%, surpassing the overall loan growth rate of 14.5%. In terms of loan allocation, 61% of the funds were directed towards SMEs, increasing the proportion of SME loans from 47.16% at the end of last year to 49%.

President of Bank of China (BOC), Li Lihui, indicated that SME business is a very important area, "It could be said that it is also our business 'blue ocean,' and we hope to do better in this aspect."

Li Lihui introduced that from January to May this year, BOC's SME loans increased by 44%, and the number of SME clients grew by 22%. By the end of May, BOC provided various forms of credit totaling 1.1 trillion yuan to SMEs, with over 30,000 SME clients. "Of course, compared to the more than 9 million SMEs, this is a small number, but we have taken an important step forward."

Deputy President of Construction Bank (CCB), Zhu Xiaohuang, stated that SME business is "profitable." He introduced that CCB's SME loan disbursement reached 1.2 trillion yuan, accounting for 42% of its total credit balance. Among these, loans to smaller enterprises reached over 500 billion yuan, making up 18% of the total loan balance.

He pointed out that CCB's SME business yields better comprehensive returns compared to large enterprise business, reaching 2.8 times that of large clients. "From a strategic perspective, positioning SME business as a key strategic development focus is very reasonable."

Promoting Business Innovation

Li Lihui emphasized that to solve the financing problems of SMEs, banks need to use innovative thinking to plan the operation and management of SME businesses, finding good balance points in aspects such as risk and profitability.

Li Lihui introduced that BOC has established a number of credit factories, handling SME credit business through streamlined and efficient processes. Through this arrangement, the approval process for SME credit has been shortened from two to three months in the past to just two to three weeks now.

Moreover, in terms of credit policies, BOC has formulated specialized customer evaluation standards and admission criteria; in product services, new products have been developed according to SME needs, such as mutual guarantees among upstream and downstream companies; and incentive mechanisms for SME innovation have been set up.

President of Agricultural Bank of China (ABC), Zhang Yun, stated that ABC has separate credit access conditions, separate credit approval procedures, credit approval authorization, a separate resource allocation system, separate performance evaluation systems, and a separate risk management system for SME businesses, providing services to SMEs through dedicated management.

President of Bank of Communications (BoComm), Li Jun, stated that BoComm has explored two development models: one is the cooperative marketing model, mainly through collaboration with government departments, chambers of commerce, high-tech industrial parks, and industry banks, implementing cluster marketing to reduce information costs and lower the cost of SME loans. The second is developing SME business through supply chain financing, mainly utilizing the resources of large enterprise clients and intervening through supply chain financing to provide services to SMEs located at both ends of the supply chains of large enterprises.

Zhu Xiaohuang stated that CCB has launched new products and created new practices in some areas. For example, in Shenzhen, the enterprise joint-loan and joint-guarantee model was introduced, and in Zhenjiang, the credit factory model was piloted. Additionally, cooperation with Alibaba in Hangzhou to establish an online bank is currently under implementation.
