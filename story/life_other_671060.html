"Jostling with fire trucks" is the coldness of human nature 18671079399

by xfxybk on 2012-02-21 15:11:22

Recently, a video of a social vehicle overtaking a fire rescue vehicle has become popular on the Internet, sparking heated discussions. The video was filmed during the recent fire at a rice noodle shop in Beijing's Gui Street, capturing the moment when the fire truck was cut off while rushing to the scene.

In the face of such scenes, I seem to have lost much of my anger, because similar backward civil phenomena and indifferent hearts have become all too common, such as food safety issues, frequent traffic accidents, coal mine production accidents... The lack of responsibility and legal awareness, the coldness towards others' lives and health in some people has left people speechless with tears, leaving only the burning anger in their hearts...

The anger stems from indifference. With the development of human civilization today, the citizens who should carry forward the excellent Chinese civilization of five thousand years, often show an incredible regression: ignoring children involved in car accidents, eighteen passersby showing indifference and refusing to help, drivers not yielding to approaching fire trucks or even competing for lanes with them, doctors who should save lives but instead exploit patients for profit, knowing that food is essential for survival yet still disregarding safety standards in food production, ignoring the right to life and health of ordinary people just for profit, knowing there is no safety guarantee in production but still insisting on illegal production leading to accidents...

Perhaps some would say, these are isolated cases, no need to make a fuss. However, behind these individual cases, without exception, there lies inevitability, which no one can deny. There is necessity within chance, and chance within necessity, this is dialectics.

The power of criticism lies in drawing attention, making people suddenly realize. The sharper the criticism, the more it should achieve such effects. Pointing out existing problems and flaws requires great wisdom, insight, and courage. I am not a hero, much less a great man, just a rational citizen. Perhaps many others are busy with affairs and have no time to care about these trivial matters, but I feel that by speaking out, I have done what I should.

I remember not long ago a father passed by an injured person and ignored him, only later realizing the injured person was his own son. Later, the father regretted it deeply, but it was too late. Indifference to others ultimately leads to indifference to oneself, this cause-and-effect relationship is also dialectics.

Fire trucks going to extinguish fires is the job of firefighters, but this work needs the joint help and support of the entire society, such as pedestrians and vehicles giving way on the road. The self-centered behavior of pedestrians and vehicles, ignoring the fire truck or even competing for lanes with it, cannot be justified under any circumstances, it can only indicate that the regression of civilization and the coldness of hearts have reached an alarming level.

Of course, not everyone is like this, but this incident is very worthy of our deep reflection. It goes against the requirements of a civilized society, seriously violating the ethical moral bottom line of civilization, despised by people, and intolerable in a civilized society, and should be punished severely by law.

With the progress of economic and social development, material civilization has developed significantly, higher spiritual requirements must keep pace with the times, especially ideological and moral construction should not lag behind the development of material civilization. Inner indifference is a diseased state of civilizational development, a regression that urgently needs drastic treatment. Most importantly, we need to awaken the kindness deep within human nature, learn to care, learn to care, love, and help others, establish the concept of "all for me, and me for all." Otherwise, if everyone gets stuck in their selfish little worlds, then building a harmonious society will forever remain a dream.
