Introduction to the plot of "Red Dresses Are Popular on the Street":

In the early 1980s at the Dafeng Cotton Mill in Shanghai. Axiang, a female worker from the countryside, heard that the girls in Shanghai have a habit of competing to wear beautiful clothes, called 'chopping clothes' and 'chopping skirts'. So she asked an individual clothing seller, 'Little Suzuki', to buy her a beautiful red silk skirt, which she wore to resist being mocked as a country bumpkin by the other female workers. Model worker Tao Xing'er liked this red skirt very much and secretly tried it on herself. Axiang found that Tao Xing'er looked extremely beautiful in the red skirt, so she invited her to go 'chopping skirts' in the park together. The shift leader thought exposing shoulders and arms in red skirts was very inappropriate, so Tao Xing'er secretly added a white collar and white patch to the red skirt. But it was quickly torn off by her companions. Encouraged by her friends, Tao Xing'er finally went to the park with the other female workers. She mustered up the courage to walk alone through the crowd, boldly comparing her beauty with various colorful skirts and dresses, leaving all the beautiful clothes behind. Through 'chopping skirts', Tao Xing'er felt her spirit lifted and began to realize the constraints of old ideas on herself. One day, Tao Xing'er and her good friend Ge Jia missed a defect in the fabric during the shift change, and the responsibility was unclear. However, the leadership attributed it to Ge Jia to preserve Tao Xing'er's reputation as a factory model worker, and Ge Jia also took the blame out of friendship. The female workers were quietly discussing the matter, and when Tao Xing'er found out, she felt very guilty. She worked overtime desperately; when she learned that Ge Jia also wanted to go to university, she requested the opportunity for Ge Jia to take the exam from the shift leader and gave Ge Jia the chance to go to university during the exam. Unknown to Tao Xing'er, Ge Jia discovered her wish while checking the exam paper. With her straightforward and open nature, Ge Jia refused to accept Tao Xing'er's hidden favor and insisted on not going to school. Dong Xiaqin, the machine operator, was Tao Xing'er's lover during her time in the countryside. Five years ago, when Dong Xiaqin was transferred back to Shanghai, he abandoned Tao Xing'er due to various reasons, but he still loved her. For five years, they worked in the same workshop, facing each other every day, yet Tao Xing'er never forgave Dong Xiaqin. After Dong Xiaqin confessed multiple times, he painfully transferred to work at the Public Security Bureau. When Tao Xing'er finally realized that everyone, including herself, has flaws and mistakes, she forgave Dong Xiaqin. In order to elevate her status, Axiang falsely claimed that her brother was in Hong Kong and bought clothes, skirts, and socks from 'Little Suzuki', claiming they were 'Hong Kong goods' and sold them at a low price to the female workers. Axiang couldn't cover the loss from buying high and selling low with her salary, so she had to ask 'Little Suzuki' to sell her own clothes. The shift leader, representing the organization, came to question Axiang, and Axiang burst into tears, revealing that she only wanted to spend money to buy equality and couldn't bear being mocked as a 'country bumpkin'. Tao Xing'er redeemed Axiang's clothes, and Ge Jia also scolded the female workers who mocked Axiang. During the team meeting to help educate Axiang not to look down on herself, Tao Xing'er unexpectedly examined her own weakness of not daring to admit her guilt publicly. She finally understood that young people in the 1980s should live more boldly and sincerely.

The film combines people's pursuit of inner beauty and behavioral beauty with the pursuit of fashion beauty, reflecting textile female workers' aspirations for life, love, and beautiful things. It depicts the contradiction between old and new ideas in the process of the times and the gradual perfection of the spiritual world of the youth.
