Debit: Administrative expenses - relevant detailed accounts (department special items) Credit: Cash/Bank deposit/Other receivables

Vouchers involving cash are passed to the cashier position, vouchers not involving cash are passed to the supervisor position for review.

Note:

(1) Non-salary expense expenditures must obtain invoices or receipts supervised by the Tax Bureau, filled out in a standardized manner, with consistent capital and lowercase amounts, no alterations, and VAT invoices must strictly adhere to filling standards.

(2) Ensure that the upper left corner of vouchers and attachments is neat, fold attachments to the size of bookkeeping vouchers, and do not use staples.

(3) Expense reviews are based on the "2001 Expense Control Method", "Travel Expense Expenditure Scope and Standards", "Communication Fee Management Method", etc., key points include: planned expense amounts within limits must be approved by department heads, divisional leaders, and financial ministers; unplanned expenses must have reports approved by the general manager; municipal transportation fees and communication fees must be registered by the General Office; recruitment expenses must be reviewed by the Human Resources Minister; travel expenses must be accompanied by an approved itinerary form, and hospitality expenses must be accompanied by an approved hospitality expense detail form.

(4) Accurately use detailed accounts (see account table), correctly select special items.

(5) After the preparation of vouchers for cash withdrawals is completed, if the cashier has no cash, the bookkeeping voucher should be temporarily retained until the cashier retrieves the cash and notifies the recipient.

(6) If the claimant has outstanding debts from previous periods, all reimbursement expenses must first offset these debts, with repayment receipts issued by the administrative expenses position.

(II) Fund disbursement

1. Review monthly fund plan

By the 28th of each month, according to the annual expense plan, related accounts receivable/payable, and contracts → Review the next month's fund usage plan for the management department → Summarize the fund plan → Report to the financial minister for approval.

Note: Research and Development Center contract execution fees, Administrative Affairs Department repair and purchase fees, Human Resources Department large-scale training fees, Financial Department tax payments and interest expenses, Party Masses Department group activity fees, etc., which are predictive special expenses, need to be reported in the monthly fund plan.

2. Review payments and accounting

(1) Based on the monthly fund plan, verify payment items → Review whether the approval procedures for the "Payment Approval Form" are complete → Register the fund plan → Payment by the cashier position.

Note: For payment amounts under 100,000 yuan within the plan, directly pass to the cashier position for payment; amounts over 100,000 yuan within the plan or outside the plan require approval from the finance minister or financial director.

(2) Sign receipt of the "Payment Approval Form" and bank payment vouchers transmitted by the cashier position → Receive invoices submitted by relevant personnel of the management department → Review whether the approval procedures on the invoice are complete → Review whether there are signatures of collectors or recipients on the stubs of bank instruments → Prepare bookkeeping vouchers

Debit: Administrative expenses - relevant detailed accounts

Credit: Bank deposit

→ Pass to the supervisor position for review.

Note: (1) Payments except for prepayments or temporary purchases must be made with invoices.

(2) After the payment is made, urge the check to be accounted for within one week and the draft within two weeks.

3. Cleanup and collection:

(1) Deduct from the borrower's reimbursement expenses and promptly notify other positions of the debtor list.

(2) On the second-to-last day of the month, clean up the borrowing situation of personnel in various departments → Prepare a "Detailed Table of Department Borrowing Situations" (listing borrowers, borrowing amounts, whether overdue) → Send to various departments to remind borrowers to repay → As of the 5th day, if there are still unreimbursed amounts → Prepare a "Deduction Detail Table" (listing persons to be deducted, this month's deduction amount) → Notify the salary welfare position to deduct from the borrower's salary (if it involves salesperson deductions, pass to the sales accountant for deduction).

Note: For overdue unpaid loans, all reimbursement expenses must first offset the debt.

(VII) Managerial work

1. By the 10th of each month, issue expense notifications based on the department expense plan quota, send to department heads, and request attention to overruns or potential overruns.

2. Within 15 days after the end of each quarter, issue expense analysis reports that can propose reasonable suggestions for company expense management control.

3. Participate in formulating and improving the company's expense control methods.

4. Participate in formulating the annual expense control plan.

5. Timely clean up and collect other receivables.

6. Participate in formulating and improving the company's other receivables management methods.

7. Regularly and irregularly review office supplies warehouse and engineering material warehouse ledgers to maintain consistency between records and reality.

(VIII) Work requirements

1. Be familiar with the company's expense management methods, financial systems, and fund usage methods and other relevant systems.

2. Understand the work content of each position in the finance department and do a good job of connecting with each position.

3. Have clear work objectives, strong sense of responsibility, and establish a good department image.

Four, Fixed Asset Calculation Position Work Process

(I) Fixed Assets

1. Purchase

Review payment → Urge reporting → Review invoices and fixed asset transfer orders → Query paid situations → Prepare vouchers

Debit: Fixed assets

Credit: Bank deposit/Prepaid account

Note: (1) Payments should strictly adhere to the "Fund Disbursement Management Measures."

(2) Promptly urge relevant departments to handle reporting procedures based on contracts and payment situations.

(3) After purchasing fixed assets, invoices and acceptance forms must be used to process fixed asset transfers. Specifically, production-use fixed assets are handled by the production department, and non-production-use fixed assets are handled by the administrative affairs department.

(4) When entering fixed assets into accounts, the abstract column of the bookkeeping voucher must specify the name, model, and using department of the fixed asset.

(5) Transfers of fixed assets between workshops and departments should undergo accounting treatment to strengthen fixed asset management and accurately calculate depreciation.

2. Depreciation Calculation

Based on the fixed asset subsidiary ledger, query last month's new or reduced fixed assets → Calculate the cumulative depreciation changes corresponding to the original value of fixed assets and the company's depreciation policy → Prepare the depreciation calculation table → Prepare bookkeeping vouchers

Debit: Administrative expenses/Manufacturing expenses/Selling expenses - Accumulated depreciation

Credit: Accumulated depreciation - relevant detailed accounts

→ Pass to the supervisor position for review

Note: (1) At the beginning of the year, calculate the monthly depreciation amount for each department and type of asset based on the fixed asset subsidiary ledger. Adjust timely based on changes in fixed assets and prepare the depreciation calculation table.

(2) Fixed asset depreciation is calculated individually per asset, summarized by department and category.

(3) When the accumulated monthly depreciation amount changes, the basis for calculating depreciation should be communicated to workshop accountants so they can verify the existence of fixed assets and the correctness of depreciation calculations based on the fixed asset list.

(4) At the end of the year, calculate the full-year depreciation based on the fixed asset subsidiary ledger, reverse excessive depreciation due to net values lower than the full-year required depreciation for individual assets, and determine the base for next year's depreciation.

Withdraw cash daily in the morning according to the cash plan (or via a construction bank passbook) → Safely store and accurately pay out cash → Timely count cash → Deposit in the bank by 3:30 PM if necessary.

4. Manage the cash journal, ensuring daily clearing and monthly closing, and timely reconcile balances with computer accounts.
