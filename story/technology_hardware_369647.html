"Seven Highlights of the Week" - Apple's Air Takes the Stage, Adamo Bows Out

Introduction: If we were to talk about the most eye-catching event in the notebook industry this week, it would undoubtedly be the release of Apple's MacBook Air. Apple's products always bring surprises, and sometimes revolutionary changes. The ultra-thin MacBook Air is no exception.

With the birth of new products, there must be the withdrawal of older models. However, today, I want to tell you that although the new product is dazzling, the discontinued product was indeed a classic in its time... Let's enter the 44th issue of "Seven Highlights of the Week."

"The Seven Highlights of the Week" - The Fastest Boot Time

According to foreign media reports, some netizens have already received the latest version of the MacBook Air and briefly tried it out. If we were to talk about the performance of this product, perhaps our most direct impression should start with the boot time. Therefore, this netizen compared several Apple products. This includes:

1. MacBook Pro (15 inches, 5400rpm/HDD)

2. MacBook Pro (15 inches, RealSSD C300)

3. MacBook Air (13 inches, 4200rpm/HDD/1.8 inches)

4. Latest MacBook Air (11.6 inches, SSD)

The latest MacBook Air naturally came out on top after a simple test, with a boot time of 15 seconds. The 15-inch HDD version of the MacBook Pro had a boot time of 42 seconds, while the SSD version took 20 seconds. The old HDD version of the MacBook Air had an astonishing boot time of 64 seconds. Interested netizens can search for boot test videos on video websites.

The next piece of news still comes from the latest version of the MacBook Air. Hit the next page!

Page 2: The Smallest System Drive

"The Seven Highlights of the Week" - The Smallest System Drive After the media entered the experience area at Apple's "Back to the Mac" event, they eagerly experienced the new MacBook Air. Of course, the new laptop deserves praise, but what caught the media's attention even more was a USB drive the size of a fingernail.

USB drive? Yes. The MacBook Air does not come with an optical drive, so the older MacBook Air required an external optical drive to complete system installation. However, future MacBook Airs may no longer need an optical drive. A system USB drive is enough to handle the installation of the entire system and applications.

Apple's MacBook Air system drive

This USB drive contains Mac software and iLife '11 software. Installation is similar to installing from a CD; just press C at startup to enter the installation interface.

The next piece of news comes from a vice president who made a bold statement... Page 3: The Department Least Afraid of Independence

"The Seven Highlights of the Week" - The Department Least Afraid of Independence On October 21, 2010, Apple held a product launch event titled "Back to the Mac" in the United States. During the event, Phil Schiller, Apple's senior vice president, revealed that even if the Mac business division became an independent company, it could rank 110th in the Fortune Global 500.

Phil Schiller stated that the number of Apple-owned stores worldwide is increasing day by day. The stores in China have been very successful, with large foot traffic.

The number of developers has reached 600,000 in Shanghai Pudong store

Phil Schiller disclosed that the number of Mac program developers has reached 600,000. AutoDesk also released the Mac version of the software AutoCAD. Microsoft also launched new products such as Office on the Mac platform. With these powerful developers joining Apple's camp, the Apple platform has become more user-friendly and useful.

Since we've mentioned Mac, we can't help but mention Windows... Continue on the next page. Page 4: The Most Tragic Operating System

"The Seven Highlights of the Week" - The Most Tragic Operating System According to foreign media reports, as of last Friday, Microsoft has stopped supporting pre-installed Windows XP operating systems for all PC manufacturers.

From the time Microsoft launched the Windows Vista operating system, it had plans to stop pre-installing XP. However, due to Vista's high requirements for computer performance, it had to continue supporting the pre-installation of the XP system. "We will extend the final deadline for supplying the Windows XP Starter Edition to June 30, 2010," Microsoft said at the time.

Farewell, Windows XP

In fact, Microsoft did not decisively end support for the XP operating system on June 30 this year. To allow PC manufacturers to smoothly transition to Windows 7, Microsoft extended support for XP until last Friday.

However, if you insist on using XP, that's fine too. You can use the activation code from the genuine Windows Vista or Windows 7 pre-installed on your computer to activate a downgrade to Windows XP. Microsoft has not yet clarified when this service will end. Next, the U.S. President makes an appearance... Page 5: The Most Technological Signature

"The Seven Highlights of the Week" - The Most Technological Signature According to foreign media reports, at a rally at the University of Washington in Seattle, U.S. President Obama's appearance drew many supporters. Some people kept taking pictures, while others asked for autographs, and these autographs were quite creative.

Reports say that when a supporter named Kahn pulled out an iPad to ask for a signature, Obama did not immediately agree. But he eventually smiled and signed his name on the screen with his finger.

Fan gets Obama's signature Mr. President, please sign on our iPad

Considering Obama's previous statement that he would not use an iPad and criticized such electronic products as being too entertaining and distracting, his attitude has clearly changed this time. However, considering Obama's meeting with Jobs a few days ago, signing on Apple's iPad makes sense, after all, he has to give Jobs some face.

Next, a brand-new netbook brand appeared, and it's also very cute... Page 6: The Cutest Netbook

"The Seven Highlights of the Week" - The Cutest Netbook If you have a powerful operating system, would you launch corresponding hardware products? Of course, Jolicloud did just that.

Recently, Tariq Krim, CEO of Jolicloud, posted several photos of their upcoming "mini-laptop" on Twitter, which is actually a netbook!

Jolicloud netbook Jolicloud netbook

Tariq Krim revealed that this netbook will use the Jolicloud 1.1 system. In terms of hardware, the product will feature a 10.1-inch screen design and can smoothly play 720p videos. Additionally, VGA and USB interfaces are, of course, indispensable. Jolicloud netbook

Additionally, from the photos, we can confirm that this netbook will have a very fashionable lid.

In terms of price, Krim confirmed it will be below $500. As for the launch time, it cannot be determined yet.

Some say that the birth of a new product means the fall of another... Is that really true? Continue on the next page. Page 7: The Most Tragic Classic Notebook

"The Seven Highlights of the Week" - The Most Tragic Classic Notebook

According to foreign media reports, Dell recently adjusted its consumer computer product line. Currently, Dell's product line is divided into three series: Inspiron, XPS, and Alienware. This means that the ultra-lightweight series Adamo has been abandoned.

Of course, Adamo will not completely disappear from our sight. After all, the ultra-lightweight and luxurious elements of Adamo are still cherished by many people. Therefore, industry insiders analyzed that in the future, Adamo's elements will be integrated into other product series.

Dell's three product lines

Industry insiders analyzed that reducing a sub-brand will be more conducive to Dell's control over marketing costs. Previously, when Adamo was launched, Dell spent hundreds of millions of dollars on advertising expenses.

Final Thoughts:

A new generation replaces the old, and new products replace the old ones. The classic Windows XP and the luxurious Dell Adamo have left our world. And surprisingly, the products that will soon fill this void are all from Apple, including the MacBook Air and macOS Lion. Here, I can't help but admire the ambition of Apple CEO Jobs, and at the same time, I have some concerns about the traditional PC industry.
