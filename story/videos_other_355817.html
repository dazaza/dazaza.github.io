Heilongjiang Greater Khingan Range reduces timber production by 2.38 million cubic meters__Rosewood N2

by ww0997348 on 2010-05-26 08:10:27

Ten years ago, the official implementation of the Natural Forest Resource Protection Project began in Heilongjiang's Daxing'anling forest area. Within the national approved logging quota, they voluntarily reduced the amount of forest felling. The latest statistics show that during the first four months of this year, this forest area has reduced timber production by 350,000 cubic meters. Up to now, since 2007, the reduction in timber output in this forest area has exceeded 2.38 million cubic meters.

According to the核定 (approved) standard of the National Natural Forest Resource Protection Project, Heilongjiang's Daxing'anling forest area could produce 2.144 million cubic meters of timber annually from 2003 to 2010. Producing this amount of timber would consume 3.7 million cubic meters of forest stockpile.

In 2006, the Daxing'anling Forestry Group Company analyzed the status of the forest stockpile and found that in the limited cutting areas and commercial forests of Heilongjiang's Daxing'anling forest area, there were only 76.85 million cubic meters of exploitable forest stockpile.

"We did a calculation. According to the annual consumption of 3.7 million cubic meters of forest stockpile as approved by the Natural Forest Protection Project, these forests can only be harvested for 21 years," said Zheng Xuehui, Director of the Planning and Statistics Department and the Natural Forest Resource Protection Project Office of the Daxing'anling Forestry Group Company. "Moreover, constrained by natural conditions, it is easy to damage the forest resources of Daxing'anling but hard to restore them. After decades of overcutting, the quality of the forest has been continuously declining. If we continue to harvest timber, it will have more adverse effects on the environment."

Considering the protection of ecological resources and reducing the production of low-quality logs, Heilongjiang's Daxing'anling forest area began to voluntarily reduce the amount of timber felling, with an annual reduction in timber production accounting for about one-third of the approved annual production.

It is understood that Heilongjiang's Daxing'anling forest area reduced timber production by 640,000 cubic meters in 2007, 730,000 cubic meters in 2008, and 660,000 cubic meters in 2009. According to the reduction plan, there will still be a reduction scale of 650,000 cubic meters this year, and so far, 350,000 cubic meters have been completed according to schedule.

Up to now, from 2007 to the present, Heilongjiang's Daxing'anling forest area has cumulatively reduced timber production by 2.38 million cubic meters, reducing sales revenue by approximately 1.9 billion yuan.

The sharp decline in income has created immense pressure on the lives of local forestry workers in Daxing'anling. This region is developing industries such as forest mineral development, green food, and tourism to compensate for the loss of income due to the reduction in forest cutting.

The Natural Forest Resource Protection Project covers 17 provinces in China and is a decision made by China in response to the continuous deterioration of the ecological environment. It started piloting in 1998 and was officially approved and implemented by the State Council in 2000. After the implementation of this project, key state-owned forest areas in Northeast and Inner Mongolia implemented a timber limitation policy, resulting in a significant decrease in timber harvesting.

Heilongjiang's Daxing'anling forest area, located at the northernmost border of China, is the largest natural forest area in the country, and also one of the key state-owned forest areas and main distribution areas of natural forests.
