Nokia's Outburst, Microsoft Benefits

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 13:20:03

Windows Phone has finally caught up with Android on the hardware front.

At this most special Nokia World conference held in Abu Dhabi, before being taken over by Microsoft, Nokia left its boldest mark yet — unveiling six devices and a series of accessories all at once. This refreshed numerous firsts for the Windows Phone lineup: The Lumia 1520 is not only the first Windows Phone to feature a 6-inch screen, but also the first to sport a 1080p display, and simultaneously the first to be powered by the Snapdragon 800 processor.

Since April this year, Nokia has been very active in product releases. Within a month, four new models were launched: First, the full keyboard Asha 210 was introduced via the official blog, followed by the minor upgrade of the Lumia 920 called Lumia 928, then the Asha 501 was released in India. Subsequently, the Lumia 925, Lumia 1020... Nokia refreshed the flagship models of Windows Phone at an unimaginably high frequency.

This year is undoubtedly the most crucial year for the rise of Windows Phone, and Nokia has essentially led this trend single-handedly. Let's take a look back at Nokia's product layout: In September last year, the Lumia 920 and 820 brought Nokia back into the smartphone battlefield, occupying the high ground through hardware advantages. The Lumia 620 was then released at the end of the year. Following that, at this year’s MWC, mid-to-low-end models Lumia 720 and Lumia 520 were unveiled, completing the basic layout of the Lumia product line. In the following months, Nokia began to empower the Lumia series with technological breakthroughs, differentiating the camera imaging technology at the software level with the Lumia 925, while the Lumia 1020 officially applied the oversampling technology used earlier in the Nokia 808 to Windows Phone.

It must be said that in terms of product release rhythm, Nokia has regained the same vigor as in the Symbian era, and the Lumia model numbers have become countless.

Even though Lumia smartphones running the Windows Phone operating system still lag far behind Apple and Samsung products in sales, Nokia continues to prove that Windows Phone holds a significant place in the smartphone market.

An earlier report from The Wall Street Journal stated that Nokia Lumia smartphone sales set a new record for the fourth consecutive quarter. The company sold at least 8 million Lumia phones in the third quarter from June to September, nearly three times the 2.9 million units sold during the same period last year, and much higher than the 7.4 million units sold in the second quarter of this year. Additionally, in the five major European markets (UK, Germany, France, Spain, Italy), from May to July this year, Windows Phone accounted for 8.2% of shipments.

A statistical report from AdDuplex, a Windows Phone advertising agency, showed that Nokia accounts for 85% of the entire Windows Phone market share. It would not be an exaggeration to say that compared to HTC, Huawei, and other brands' tentative steps into the Windows Phone camp, Nokia almost single-handedly supports half of the Windows Phone ecosystem, and the Lumia brand has clearly risen above Windows Phone itself.

As Nokia's mobile phone business gradually falls into Microsoft's arms, the vast Finnish public, who hold deep sentiments for Nokia, may feel conflicted. On one hand, they see the explosive growth of Nokia; on the other hand, they see Microsoft reaping the benefits.
