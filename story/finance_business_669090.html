Yesterday, he rejected her.

All along, she thought that he would always be following her, no matter when she needed him. Because he had said, he would wait for her all his life. But, he broke his promise.

She and he were classmates in university. She entered university at 16, two years younger than him. In school, she was always the little sister, getting along well with many boys. Of course, she was especially close to him.

She indeed didn't want any romantic relationship; maybe it was because she was still young. Until that day, he suddenly blushed and handed her a movie ticket, stammering that it was a love story. Truly outdated, annoyingly so. But she still straightforwardly refused him. She said he wasn't her type, that she wouldn't choose him, but he didn't back off.

In the following time, he was always around her, caring for her, protecting her. Whatever happened, he had already taken care of it before she could act. But she told him, he wasn't her type, and he should stop pursuing her. He said, he would keep waiting until the day she agreed.

After graduating from university, both of them were admitted to the graduate school of their alma mater, though not in the same major. All the experimental difficulties during the postgraduate period were basically handled by him alone. Even her supervisor joked with his supervisor: "Where is my prospective son-in-law? Why haven't I seen him recently?"

Five years of graduate studies passed quickly, and she reached the age of 25. She indeed considered choosing him. But she never opened her mouth. Why... Maybe it was still felt imperfect. He always looked sloppy, with messy hair, disheveled. Also, he was too thin, not too short even among southerners, yet weighing less than 100 pounds. She preferred athletic boys. She frankly told him the reasons why she wouldn't choose him, and the usually talkative he fell silent for a long time. At that moment, she actually hoped he would leave after hearing those words, but he continued saying, "I am the best for you, forever."

After obtaining his Ph.D., she stayed as a teacher at the university, while he chose to study abroad. When their classmates saw him off, she felt no sadness but rather a sense of relief. The person who had been around her for seven years finally left, and she no longer had to repeatedly say, "I don't like your type, give up" such things.

Soon she started dating a boyfriend, six years older than her, from a wealthy family, working in his father's company as a financial manager, earning over ten thousand a month, with a house and car.

But as their relationship deepened, she discovered the gap. What she said, he always responded with "hmm hmm hmm," half-understanding or not understanding at all. Her meaning, he could never grasp. This relationship lasted on and off for one and a half years, and finally, they parted ways. It was then that she remembered him, the one who always smiled behind her, taking care of everything without her needing to worry.

Since childhood, she had always been an excellent student, entering one of the best universities. As an undergraduate, she was directly recommended for a Ph.D., and as a doctoral candidate, she graduated as an outstanding doctorate. So she thought everyone around her should be like that, understanding without her needing to explain, but she was wrong.

In her melancholy, she welcomed her 28th birthday. A 28-year-old woman seemed to give people a different feeling. Although looking in the mirror, she didn't feel proud or old, her parents and friends began to worry about her. Thus, she started a round of blind dates.

Those blind date candidates, either rich and crude, or awkward and sensitive, or pompous and nonsensical. One after another, they only made her think more of him at the horizon. That wise, lazy, caring, humorous, and eloquent him.

At her associate professor review presentation, she suddenly saw him. Sitting below, his eyes were still as clear, and he was still as thin. His hair looked dirty, holding a pen, sitting in the front row smiling at her. The only change seemed to be that he had aged a bit, no longer like the young man who liked to debate endlessly.

It turned out he had returned to the school as a talent import, not in her department, but in the same college. While she was still applying for associate professorship, the school had already granted him a full professor position. So he had the qualification to enjoy a modest welfare housing in this land-scarce city, which was quite good.

When he was renovating his house, she often went to his place to give advice, seemingly returning to the days four years ago, where he served her water, peeled fruits, and answered all sorts of work-related questions for her.

That day, after his house was renovated, he invited her for dinner. Only the two of them, the evening light was beautiful. She knew what he was going to say, somewhat looking forward to it, yet somewhat nervous. Sure enough, he said, "I have a house now, missing a bride."

She pretended not to understand, actually hoping he would continue being as straightforward as four years ago, decisively saying: "I will make you know I am the best for you all my life." But he didn't. He just sighed, saying nothing.

When he sent her downstairs, he actually held her hand tightly, so tight it hurt a bit. Fortunately, he soon let go and walked back without even saying goodbye.

The days afterward became suddenly awkward. When he met her, he was just indifferent, nodding briefly. On the school bus, he no longer sat with her. When waiting for the bus, he no longer talked to her. When her master's students asked him to be a committee member, he declined.

Finally, she saw him with another girl, laughing and talking. Her heart felt sour, inexplicably angry. Her best friend persuaded her, saying that since he started pursuing her in his third year of university, what was wrong with her turning around and pursuing him once? She was almost thirty.

But, when she mustered up the greatest courage to tell him, he calmly said, "I already have a girlfriend!"

She also heard that he had a girlfriend, but how could it be so fast? After so many years of feelings, from when she was nineteen to now, could he forget so easily? Didn't he say he would wait for her all his life?

Last night, she cried all night. For so many years, she had never cried like that. Could men really shift their feelings so easily? Really?

Cherish the ones you love.
