Preface

In a person's life, there are always some people who leave an indelible mark. They go from strangers to acquaintances, through the journey of mutual understanding, and reach the sweet stage of love. But in the end, they part ways, embarking on separate journeys of love, returning to being strangers once more.

Some say they once loved each other, while others claim they caused each other harm. Thus, the phrase "love until it hurts" came about. Some believe that distance is no issue as long as two hearts beat as one, whereas others argue that distance disrupts the flow of their love. Hence, the saying "love stranded by distance" was born.

We belong to the post-90s generation, and we too have our own perceptions of love. Yet, in the face of certain situations, we sometimes act against our own wishes, doing things methodically. I wonder if we're being confused by something, numbing our nerves. We handle matters with such caution, pretending not to care at all. Perhaps, in everyone's eyes, there's a bit of confusion, not knowing why.

Our phase is bound to have its little problems. Regarding love, we all have unique perspectives. When advising others, we speak so deeply and reasonably, but when these matters happen to us, we feel like the world is spinning, even getting stuck in dead ends. The nonchalant attitude expressed by phrases like "passing through myriad flowers without a single leaf clinging to me" is now long gone. What was once "not hanging oneself on one tree" has become a complicated excuse.

Facing our own emotions, we are so irrational, loving each other only to end up burying our hearts. At the moment of turning away, we suddenly realize, regretting our former naivety and mischief, repenting for pretending not to care. By hurting the other party, we've harmed not just a heart but a love that meant more than life itself. Perhaps, we just wanted to irritate the other party, making them care, but repeated provocations lose all meaning.

After reading countless novels and watching numerous TV dramas about love, we finally come to understand it quickly. However, even if we possess love, we may not necessarily know how to nurture it. Loving someone is simple; mutual love isn't difficult either. The challenge lies in continuing together, free from arguments and misunderstandings, each day filled with warmth and happiness. I think that's hard—until the other party grows old, still holding each other dear. That's what love is.

When we were still naive, the movie "Those Years, Together We Chased Girls" became wildly popular, drawing in massive attention. The reason was its authentic story, reenacting the youthful details of Ke Jing-Teng and Shen Jia-Yi's adolescence—a kind of love that many admire.

Each of us has our own story, a nameless and untellable memory. It's like a little-known secret that can't be spoken. We keep masking the gloom within, our sunny faces tinged with a slight blush, yet we still bury that emotion deep inside. Perhaps because we lack the charm of authors like Ji Baodao, we don't get the chance to adapt our memories into fascinating stories. But I think that's fine; at least both parties remember, having the capital to recount how things were back then.

But, that Shen Jia-Yi hidden deep in our hearts, the girl who makes us dream endlessly, with big eyes and a pouting mouth, do you still clearly remember her? Not everyone is as lucky as Ke Jing-Teng, able to play and frolic with her in the snow and release sky lanterns together. Even though he might have thought of her night and day, in the end, Ke Jing-Teng couldn't hold her hand and accompany her till the end.

But those people, those memories, those stories—we must remember, because during our blossoming years, they walked alongside us. That alone is enough to make the other party reminisce.

When we made grand vows about love, we were struck down heavily by "Beijing Love Story." We always thought love was like that, but at this point, we unwillingly changed our optimistic views on love. Sometimes, love can become utterly worthless. These are things that are hard to foresee. Undoubtedly, those seemingly illusory loves hide a small secret: sometimes love is so fragile that it leaves no warm memories behind.

I really don't know how to comment on their complex love. In real life, even simple love causes headaches, let alone intricate chains of love. Actually, reality exposes our false views on love, but when it comes to our beloved ones, can we measure up? I believe this isn't just their story; it's also the story each of us will face in the future.

As we grow up, different people keep entering our lives, wearing various masks. How should we respond? Friendship, romance—how do we handle them? We are growing up, becoming more mature, but regarding love, we seem overly sophisticated.

Some people like reminiscing, recording their beautiful pasts in diaries. Even if that fleeting love has scorched their eyes and shattered their weary hearts, those moments, those tender instances, linger forever. This is love—a deeply etched experience, full of laughter and joy, yet ultimately escaping not a tragic ending.

In a lifetime, intertwined with love, there is inevitably pain, but also warmth and happiness. And the reason for the pain lies in pursuing the wrong things.

Love is like a tide, leaving many unforgettable moments in its rise and fall.

Love is simple. As long as the beloved one is happy, that's enough.

Summary

Nowadays, in the real world, we each perform our own brilliant acts, squandering our youth. We even think that among all living beings, under heaven, most emotions are pessimistic. Even if we waste them, those feelings seem unchanged.

It feels like just a few short years ago, we saw "Snail House" in days without worries, only to have our moods hit rock bottom. Just as we escaped those pessimistic and world-weary emotions, thinking love was warm and waiting for "The Pursuit of Happyness," "Beijing Love Story" dealt us another heavy blow. We should have been bearing the happiness destined for the post-90s generation, but instead, we silently struggled in the realm of emotions.

Facing this complex world, Jimmy, a child who wrestles with emotions daily, harbors some animosity towards everything. His loneliness can only be expressed through the tip of a pen, revealing a murky soul like a stream beneath his weak exterior. He wears a smile drenched in happiness every day, but it's so fake, so childish, that when the smile tugs painfully at his upturned corners, he finally calms down to face his silent world. He likes writing poetry but often asks himself, "Who is my warm reader?"

Life always brings prosperity unexpectedly. Regardless of whether it's "thirty years east of the river, thirty years west," even a year or a month can bring unexpected surprises. A girl with a perpetual smile entered Jimmy's world. Her name was Janie. She came from the beautiful city of sunlight—Lhasa. Janie stayed close to Jimmy, since he happened to be her seatmate. Eating together, studying together, Janie learned many secrets Jimmy didn't want to share with others. Maybe over time, Jimmy slowly started liking Janie. But he didn't dare tell her, fearing their friendship would lose all value due to a single word.

Days passed, and Jimmy wrote poems for Janie unlike ever before, while Janie pretended not to understand their meanings. In truth, no one knew that Janie also liked writing poetry. Familiar with Nalan Rongruo, she understood Jimmy perfectly well. But she didn't dare take a step further. Both feared their friendship might collapse.

Perhaps fate liked playing tricks on Jimmy. One day, Janie received news that she would attend a high school in Beijing. Faced with this situation, both cried. Unlike before, this time Jimmy held back his tears, not wanting Janie to worry; while the once strong Janie couldn't contain her inner sorrow, sobbing loudly as if facing a life-or-death farewell.

On the night Janie was about to leave, sitting quietly on the cool bench by the lotus pond where they had spent time together, they spoke occasionally about "long futures ahead." But Jimmy knew that perhaps after this parting, he would never see Janie again. Or if he didn't confess tonight, what if Janie found a boyfriend in Beijing?

Thinking more and more sorrowfully, Jimmy shouted out loud, "Talented scholars drift away with the wind, leaving you to listen to whom?" Janie smiled, hugging Jimmy's arm for the first time and said calmly, "Dummy, we'll meet again. Don't you have more words to say to me?"

That night, they chatted late into the night. The stars shone brightly, and moonlight scattered across the ground. The mottled shadows concealed their uneasy hearts about the impending separation. Then, Janie handed Jimmy a beautifully crafted box, forbidding him from opening it immediately. So, they chatted until they were extremely tired and returned to the dormitory to rest.

Back in the dormitory, Jimmy couldn't resist curiosity and rashly opened the box. But inside, he found a note telling him not to read it now. Jimmy put the box aside carefully. Perhaps Janie anticipated Jimmy's inability to resist curiosity and wrote accordingly.

The next morning, Jimmy got up early, carrying Janie's large and small bags, seeing her off onto the bus. Janie didn't look back, fearing meeting Jimmy's eyes longing to keep her. She simply waved and said, "Zaxidele." The car disappeared in the blurry view.

After seeing Janie off, Jimmy quickly returned to the dormitory and opened the box Janie left behind. Instantly, Jimmy was stunned. Originally, in Janie's heart, Jimmy was very important. Unfortunately, they missed the intimate time.

In the days without seeing each other, Jimmy and Janie still kept in touch, everything remained peaceful and good. They expressed their sweet love through letters. They didn't care about the distance, innocently believing that as long as they cherished each other, the great distance wouldn't stop their yearning steps.

Let me ask you, do you believe in the statement that distance is no issue? You might nod, but distance can widen the emotional gap between two people. In the loneliest times, it's easiest to be swayed by others. Dreams are fleeting, leaving behind tears. When Jimmy was feeling low, another considerate girl appeared in front of him. Jimmy confided his love for Janie to Anna and even showed her the poems he had written for Janie. Gradually, Anna understood the plump but emotionally complex Jimmy in front of her. She treated Jimmy sincerely.

However, spending more time together, Jimmy inadvertently fell for Anna, but although Anna was nice to Jimmy, she didn't like him. She just felt Jimmy was a good person suitable to be a lifelong friend.

Jimmy lost Janie but couldn't gain Anna, and his heart froze instantly. In these dark days, he frequently quarreled with the adorable Anna, and remembering Janie, Jimmy always felt guilty. Jimmy struggled bitterly in the whirlpool of emotions but couldn't find the way out. Seeing Jimmy's worried face every day, Janie felt terrible, but what could she do? She herself didn't know. Perhaps neither Jimmy nor Janie was clear about what they truly desired.

Finally, Janie chose to leave, going to a strange city, escaping the world's catharsis. But she couldn't forget those beautiful memories, a fragile sentiment lasting for ages. So, she quietly wrote "Goodbye, Jimmy" in her notebook, and the story began here.

One Quiet Classroom

"Dinglingling..."

The bell rang like a funeral toll. Reluctantly, the students returned to their seats, preparing for the much-dreaded Chinese class. Everyone has their favorite subject, but Chinese has become the universally disliked course in the class, winning the title of "Most Boring Subject" in the boring subjects election.

Jimmy, a short boy with slightly dark skin, was cautious in everything he did due to a lack of love in his childhood. Lacking listeners, he used a pen to record the details of his life. He enjoyed reading and writing, always quietly staying in the corner of the front row, immersed in his favorite novels, silently melancholic.

As usual, Jimmy took out a novel, opened it, laid it flat under the textbook, and read cautiously but monotonously. The classroom was chaotic as usual, and the Chinese teacher continued to scribble on the blackboard.

"Dongdongdong...", a hurried knock disrupted the previously quiet classroom, causing whispers among the students about what would happen next. None of this affected Jimmy, who was absorbed in his novel.

"Mr. Sun, this is Janie, transferred from the Ethnic Middle School, coming here to study Mandarin. Please arrange a seat for her," said Principal Yang, followed by a girl with a ponytail, slightly dark but handsome, like a finely carved jade piece, drawing wide-eyed stares from the classmates. Surprisingly, this girl walked straight to the empty seat beside Jimmy, which had been vacant for several months.

Janie gently placed her bag, sat quietly, and didn't disturb the seemingly calm boy beside her. At this moment, Jimmy still hadn't looked up to observe what was happening. Janie quietly took out her textbook and silently read.

At this point, Mr. Sun approached them, asking Janie some questions. When he noticed Jimmy flipping through a magazine under his textbook, he didn't say anything, merely coughing lightly. Jimmy seemed to sense something, skillfully putting the magazine into his drawer. Looking up, he discovered a surprisingly charming girl beside him. But he acted indifferent.

"Dinglingling..." The eagerly awaited recess bell finally rang. Some mischievous children rushed out of the classroom, either sneaking into other classes to spy on their crushes or hiding in the restroom to smoke secretly. Of course, curious students discussed matters related to Janie.

"You, hello, my name is Janie, nice to meet you," Janie introduced herself in broken Mandarin, extending her hand. The long-silent Jimmy was at a loss, his face blushing furiously.

"My name is Jimmy, hello," he lightly touched Janie's hand, recoiling as if electrocuted. Meanwhile, Janie secretly chuckled at the bashful boy in front of her.

"Jimmy, no wonder you prefer your solitary seat, expecting such a beautiful girl to accompany you!" Cai Ni teased maliciously. Instantly, the entire classroom erupted in laughter, while Jimmy silently returned to his seat, reading his favorite magazine.

This recess was exceptionally unrestrained. The teachers had all gone to meetings, assigning only minimal homework symbolically. Teachers knew these assignments were negligible but felt compelled to assign them for appearances. Such was life.

Despite efforts by the class representative and monitor to maintain order, the classroom descended into chaos. The noise was deafening, stirring everyone's hearts but delighting the students after prolonged suppression.

Suddenly, Jimmy felt a nudge on his back. Turning around to investigate, the student behind handed him a note from Cai Ni. Knowing that someone like Cai Ni, a wolf in sheep's clothing, wouldn't utter anything meaningful, Jimmy decided not to bother reading it. Crumpling it, he prepared to discard it.

Janie swiftly grabbed the note, waving it teasingly in front of Jimmy. Boldly, she unfolded the note that made Jimmy blush. Pointing at the words "Dare to kiss her, and I'll treat you to dinner," Janie asked Jimmy what it meant. Jimmy's face turned beet red, while many behind laughed even louder. At this moment, Janie seemed to anticipate something and stopped questioning.

Time flies when one is happy, just as students perceive teacher-less classes passing quickly. Before anyone realized, the recess bell rang. Even diligent students felt the lesson yielded nothing.

It was lunchtime. Unfamiliar with the surroundings, Janie planned to follow Jimmy to the cafeteria. Jimmy happily agreed, finding solace in companionship at least in the dining hall.

Walking behind Jimmy, Janie was lost in thought, nearly bumping into an older student. Her thoughts were unclear, just random musings along the way.

Strangely, Jimmy usually found the walk from the classroom to the cafeteria long, but today it felt brief. Perhaps he realized the difference with someone, especially a girl like Janie, by his side.

Sitting at the cafeteria table, they exchanged a few casual words. After finishing their meal quickly, Jimmy hurriedly returned to the classroom to continue reading his favorite novel. Meanwhile, Janie paid attention to this seemingly cold boy. She guessed Jimmy had secrets he wasn't willing to share and perhaps she could help him.

Entering the classroom, Janie found it empty as all the students had returned to the dormitories for a nap. The vast classroom felt desolate. Janie quietly observed Jimmy's focused demeanor and couldn't help but laugh aloud. Realizing his awkwardness, Jimmy wiped his tearful eyes, stammering, "This little story is too touching."

Janie seemed to understand.

"Actually, everyone has one or two touching stories about themselves; we just aren't adept at uncovering them."

Jimmy looked at the slightly dark-skinned Janie, his heart softening instantly, feeling as if he'd found a lifeline, elated and excited. He thought: Perhaps this girl named Janie could be his warm reader.

During this quiet noon, while others slept, the two talked about their dreams, the future, and even many secrets. What Jimmy shared was beyond Janie's expectations. Slowly, she understood Jimmy's fragility and offered her sincere sisterly love, gradually influencing Jimmy's lonely heart.

An hour or two passed quickly. Students returned to the classroom. During the noon, Janie didn't have enough time to fully understand Jimmy, only realizing that the upcoming days might require her to treat Jimmy well. Using her warmth, she aimed to heal Jimmy's inner wounds. Perhaps only this way could he become lively and appreciate himself. Janie thought silently.

Thus, Jimmy poured out all his sufferings to Janie, telling her how his fragile heart often shattered. Sharing everything with her, he believed Janie wouldn't mock his stories. He even felt that Janie was someone he could fully trust.

The afternoon schedule brought excitement. Why? Except for music and PE, the remaining periods were self-study. Although the homeroom teacher might visit a few times, it was far less boring than regular classes. Finally, Jimmy's face lit up with a rare smile. He thought: Now, he could finally read his favorite novels freely and confidently.

During music class, the teacher suggested letting students entertain themselves by singing. Instantly, the class became lively. They likely preferred this way to relax their mood or exchange learning experiences.

"Teacher, according to Jimmy, his seatmate sings beautifully," Cai Ni grinned mischievously, pulling a funny face at Jimmy.

"Is that so? Then, Janie, could you sing a few songs for everyone?" The teacher actually didn't know Janie but had heard from other teachers that she was a diligent and progressive student. He assumed Janie wouldn't refuse.

Jimmy couldn't contain his excitement, shouting for the first time in class, "Whoever wants to sing can sing, but don't drag others into it." The classroom fell silent momentarily.

Janie instinctively tugged at Jimmy's arm. Standing naturally from her seat, she cleared her throat and sang "We Will Love Well." The class was astonished. Even Cai Ni fell silent, listening intently to the girl's melodious voice, feeling somewhat unfamiliar with her.

After the song ended, the classroom erupted in applause, affirming and encouraging Janie. No one expected her to have such a beautiful voice, including Jimmy, who learned it only now.

Amidst everyone's admiration, Janie sat quietly, smiling at the class. Looking up, she saw Jimmy staring curiously at her, making her blush slightly. She smiled at Jimmy, sending ripples through his heart. He didn't know what this feeling was.

Joyful classes always end quickly, as perceived by the students.

As soon as the bell rang, marking the end of music class, several classmates mysteriously approached Jimmy and Janie, praising Janie's singing skills and envying Jimmy for having such a talented seatmate. This made Jimmy feel a faint pride, albeit lacking confidence. Perhaps it was the first time after a long period of arrogance that he felt unashamed.

"Just average! That's my destiny!" Jimmy feigned helplessness, sighing dramatically. Despite appearing troubled, he couldn't conceal his inner joy. People are often like this—enjoying praise but pretending to be humble. Jimmy was no exception.

"Ouch, sour grapes! Got praised and still acting modest!" Cai Ni joked, enjoying teasing Jimmy.

"How about we switch seats?" Cai Ni asked Jimmy next, seemingly sincere. But Jimmy found it amusing, wondering why a "three-color dog" (pervert) would say such nonsense. Out of politeness, Jimmy shook his head and casually said, "No way." Cai Ni left gleefully, leaving Janie quietly laughing at Jimmy's slightly petulant expression earlier.

Next was PE, but the PE teacher didn't show up. This outdoor activity period was temporarily converted into self-study. The students were somewhat reluctant. Someone shouted.

"Why don't we let Jimmy's 'that someone' sing a song?"

"Who is Jimmy's 'that someone'?"

"Are you dumb? Who else? Janie, of course!"

Everyone chimed in, making Janie blush. But Jimmy felt a peculiar comfort. The feeling was unprecedentedly pleasant, and something in his heart seemed to subtly change. However, Janie didn't know.

PE and self-study required silence; otherwise, inviting school leaders would result in unfavorable consequences. Thus, the noisy classroom quickly quieted down, though reluctantly.

Unable to focus on his novel, Jimmy secretly wrote on a piece of paper, "Janie, your voice is incredibly moving! Hehe..." He then gently pushed it to Janie, who was doing her homework.
