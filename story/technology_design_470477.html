NetQin's net profit in the second quarter was 1.1 million US dollars, an increase of 978% year-on-year.

by tuoqing1188 on 2011-08-04 09:36:30

Sina Technology News, August 4th, early morning Beijing time. NQ Mobile (NYSE:NQ) announced its unaudited financial results for the second quarter of 2011 ended June 30. The report showed that NQ Mobile's net revenue for the second quarter was $8.9 million, an increase of 147.4% compared to $3.6 million in the same period last year; net profit was $1.1 million, an increase of 977.8% compared to $0.1 million in the same period last year.

Key Highlights:

- NQ Mobile's net revenue for the second quarter was $8.9 million, an increase of 147.4% compared to $3.6 million in the same period last year, surpassing the high end of the company’s prior guidance.

- NQ Mobile's operating profit for the second quarter was $0.5 million, an increase of 241.7% compared to $0.14 million in the same period last year. Excluding share-based compensation expenses (non-GAAP), NQ Mobile's operating profit was $3.7 million, an increase of 727.4% compared to $0.45 million in the same period last year.

- NQ Mobile's net profit for the second quarter was $1.1 million, an increase of 977.8% compared to $0.1 million in the same period last year. Excluding share-based compensation expenses (non-GAAP), NQ Mobile's net profit was $4.3 million, an increase of 954.7% compared to $0.4 million in the same period last year.

- NQ Mobile's net cash flow from operating activities for the second quarter was $3.0 million, compared to a net cash outflow of $1.7 million in the same period last year. As of the end of the second quarter of 2011, NQ Mobile held total cash and cash equivalents of $98.4 million.

- Deferred revenue as of the end of the second quarter was $4.6 million, an increase of 35.6% compared to $3.4 million at the end of the first quarter of 2011.

Operational Metrics:

- As of June 30, 2011, NQ Mobile had a cumulative total of 102.7 million registered user accounts, compared to 50.2 million as of June 30, 2010, and 86.0 million as of March 31, 2011.

- Average monthly active user accounts during the quarter ended June 30, 2011 were 36.4 million, compared to 17.0 million in the quarter ended June 30, 2010, and 30.3 million in the quarter ended March 31, 2011.

- Average monthly paying user accounts during the quarter ended June 30, 2011 were 4.2 million, compared to 1.9 million in the quarter ended June 30, 2010, and 3.7 million in the quarter ended March 31, 2011.

Financial Analysis:

Revenue:

NQ Mobile's net revenue for the second quarter was $8.9 million, an increase of 147.4% compared to $3.6 million in the same period last year, and an increase of 16.4% compared to $7.6 million in the previous quarter. The significant year-over-year and sequential growth in NQ Mobile's net revenue was primarily due to strong growth in revenue from premium mobile internet services and other services.

NQ Mobile's net revenue from premium mobile internet services for the second quarter was $7.7 million, an increase of 147.9% compared to the same period last year, and an increase of 17.2% compared to the previous quarter. The growth in NQ Mobile's premium mobile internet service revenue was mainly driven by robust and steady growth in paying user accounts. The increase in paying user accounts reflected the growth in registered users and active user accounts, as well as increased usage of the company's premium services, particularly the increase in overseas paying user accounts. Generally, overseas paying users pay higher membership fees for the company's premium services. The proportion of revenue from overseas paying users in total net revenue from premium mobile internet services was 49.5% in the second quarter, up from 42.3% in the same period last year and 45.4% in the previous quarter.

NQ Mobile's net revenue from other services for the second quarter was $1.18 million, an increase of 144.0% compared to the same period last year, and an increase of 11.8% compared to the previous quarter. The year-over-year and sequential growth in NQ Mobile's other service revenue was mainly due to growth in revenue from secure downloads and delivery services for third-party produced mobile applications. In the past five quarters, the proportion of net revenue from other services in total net revenue has remained relatively stable at 12% to 14%.

Cost of Revenue:

NQ Mobile's cost of revenue for the second quarter was $1.8 million, an increase of 71.1% compared to the same period last year, and an increase of 20.4% compared to the previous quarter. The year-over-year and sequential growth in NQ Mobile's cost of revenue was mainly due to growth in user acquisition costs and employee salaries and headcount, which drove up employee costs.

Gross Profit and Gross Margin:

NQ Mobile's gross profit for the second quarter was $7.1 million, an increase of 179.3% compared to $2.5 million in the same period last year, and an increase of 15.4% compared to $6.1 million in the previous quarter. NQ Mobile's gross margin for the second quarter was 79.6%, up from 70.5% in the same period last year but down from 80.3% in the previous quarter.

Operating Expenses:

NQ Mobile's operating expenses for the second quarter were $6.6 million, an increase of 175.6% compared to the same period last year, and an increase of 44.0% compared to the previous quarter. Excluding share-based compensation expenses (non-GAAP), NQ Mobile's operating expenses for the second quarter were $3.4 million, an increase of 62.5% compared to $2.1 million in the same period last year, and an increase of 7.8% compared to $3.1 million in the previous quarter.

Sales and Marketing Expenses:

NQ Mobile's sales and marketing expenses for the second quarter were $1.2 million, an increase of 20.5% compared to the same period last year, and a decrease of 20.4% compared to the previous quarter. The year-over-year growth in NQ Mobile's sales and marketing expenses was mainly due to the granting of new stock options and restricted shares to eligible employees, as well as increases in employee salaries and headcount, partially offset by decreases in office-related expenses, travel and entertainment expenses, and marketing and advertising expenses. The sequential decrease in NQ Mobile's sales and marketing expenses was mainly due to decreases in employee costs resulting from reduced headcount, as well as decreases in office-related expenses and travel and entertainment expenses. Excluding share-based compensation expenses (non-GAAP), NQ Mobile's sales and marketing expenses for the second quarter were $0.9 million, a decrease of 5.0% compared to $0.95 million in the same period last year, and a decrease of 35.0% compared to $1.4 million in the previous quarter. Excluding share-based compensation expenses (non-GAAP), the year-over-year decrease in NQ Mobile's sales and marketing expenses was mainly due to decreases in office-related expenses, travel and entertainment expenses, and marketing and advertising expenses, partially offset by increases in employee costs. Excluding share-based compensation expenses (non-GAAP), the sequential decrease in NQ Mobile's sales and marketing expenses was mainly due to decreases in employee costs resulting from reduced headcount, as well as decreases in office-related expenses and travel and entertainment expenses.

General and Administrative Expenses:

NQ Mobile's general and administrative expenses for the second quarter were $4.3 million, an increase of 437.3% compared to the same period last year, and an increase of 101.9% compared to the previous quarter. The year-over-year and sequential growth in NQ Mobile's general and administrative expenses was mainly due to the granting of new stock options and restricted shares to eligible employees, which drove up employee costs, as well as increases in legal and professional fees. Excluding share-based compensation expenses (non-GAAP), NQ Mobile's general and administrative expenses for the second quarter were $1.5 million, an increase of 188.4% compared to $0.5 million in the same period last year, and an increase of 73.0% compared to $0.9 million in the previous quarter. Excluding share-based compensation expenses (non-GAAP), the year-over-year growth in NQ Mobile's general and administrative expenses was mainly due to increases in employee salaries and headcount, which drove up employee costs, as well as increases in legal and professional fees. Excluding share-based compensation expenses (non-GAAP), the sequential growth in NQ Mobile's general and administrative expenses was mainly due to increases in legal and professional fees.

Research and Development Expenses:

NQ Mobile's research and development expenses for the second quarter were $1.1 million, an increase of 80.7% compared to the same period last year, and an increase of 14.5% compared to the previous quarter. The year-over-year and sequential growth in NQ Mobile's research and development expenses was mainly due to increases in employee costs resulting from salary increases and the granting of new equity awards. Excluding share-based compensation expenses (non-GAAP), NQ Mobile's research and development expenses for the second quarter were $1.0 million, an increase of 58.7% compared to $0.6 million in the same period last year, and an increase of 10.4% compared to $0.9 million in the previous quarter. Excluding share-based compensation expenses (non-GAAP), the year-over-year and sequential growth in NQ Mobile's research and development expenses was mainly due to increases in employee costs resulting from salary increases, as well as increases in office-related expenses.

As a percentage of net revenue, NQ Mobile's total operating expenses for the second quarter were 74.2%, compared to 66.6% in the same period last year and 60.0% in the previous quarter. Excluding share-based compensation expenses (non-GAAP), NQ Mobile's total operating expenses as a percentage of net revenue for the second quarter were 38.2%, compared to 58.2% in the same period last year and 41.2% in the previous quarter.

Operating Profit and Operating Margin:

NQ Mobile's operating profit for the second quarter was $0.5 million, an increase of 241.7% compared to $0.14 million in the same period last year, and a decrease of 69.2% compared to $1.5 million in the previous quarter. NQ Mobile's operating margin for the second quarter was 5.4%, compared to 3.9% in the same period last year and 20.2% in the previous quarter. The sequential decrease in NQ Mobile's operating profit and operating margin was mainly due to the impact of share-based compensation expenses.

Excluding share-based compensation expenses (non-GAAP), NQ Mobile's operating profit for the second quarter was $3.7 million, an increase of 727.4% compared to $0.45 million in the same period last year, and an increase of 23.7% compared to $3.0 million in the previous quarter. Excluding share-based compensation expenses (non-GAAP), NQ Mobile's operating margin for the second quarter was 41.6%, compared to 12.4% in the same period last year and 39.1% in the previous quarter.

Foreign Exchange Gains and Interest Income:

NQ Mobile's foreign exchange gains for the second quarter were $0.4 million, compared to foreign exchange losses of $0.04 million in the same period last year and foreign exchange gains of $0.1 million in the previous quarter.

Income Tax Expense:

NQ Mobile's income tax income for the second quarter was $0.02 million, with an effective tax rate close to zero; NQ Mobile's income tax expense was $0.03 million in the same period last year, and income tax income was $0.01 million in the previous quarter.

Net Profit:

NQ Mobile's net profit for the second quarter was $1.1 million, compared to $0.1 million in the same period last year and $1.7 million in the previous quarter. Excluding share-based compensation expenses (non-GAAP), NQ Mobile's net profit for the second quarter was $4.3 million, compared to $0.4 million in the same period last year and $3.1 million in the previous quarter.

Cash Flow and Deferred Revenue:

NQ Mobile's net cash flow from operating activities for the second quarter was $3.0 million, compared to a net cash outflow of $1.7 million in the same period last year and net cash flow of $2.0 million in the previous quarter. As of June 30, 2011, NQ Mobile held total cash and cash equivalents of $98.4 million, with deferred revenue of $4.6 million.

Outlook:

NQ Mobile expects net revenue for the third quarter of 2011 to be between $10.1 million and $10.3 million, representing year-over-year growth of 87% to 90% and sequential growth of 14% to 16%. NQ Mobile also expects net revenue for the full year 2011 to be between $38.3 million and $38.8 million, representing year-over-year growth of 116% to 119%, higher than the prior guidance range of $37.5 million to $38.5 million.

The above outlook reflects NQ Mobile's current and preliminary views, which may be subject to adjustment in the future.
