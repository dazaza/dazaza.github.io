Lamborghini's online store opening results in over 5,000 orders, but no actual payments yet.

by hj1718004 on 2011-10-23 18:50:53

Lamborghini's online store opening resulted in hardcore netizens placing 5000 orders in three days, but not a single person made a payment. This included famous screenwriter Ning Caisun and a 21-year-old man who wanted to jump into a river because he felt disrespected by his parents for being unemployed.

All online purchases of Lamborghinis turned out to be "bounced checks". After the official Taobao store of Lamborghini opened, netizens placed over 5000 orders, but no one actually paid. Many netizens said that although they didn't have enough money, after making the decision to buy on the shop page, it felt great. This included the well-known screenwriter Ning Caisun.

On May 20th, the supercar Lamborghini dealer opened a store in Taobao Mall, selling 17 luxury sports cars with prices ranging from 500,000 to 6,480,000 yuan.

However, it was found that the sales records for each sports car on the official store were all displayed as "0".

It is understood that after the opening of this online store, more than 5000 orders have been placed, but no one has actually paid for their purchase so far, and many orders have already been automatically canceled.

"Personally, I clicked 'buy now', even though there wasn't enough money in Alipay, it still felt really exciting," recently, the renowned screenwriter Ning Caisun admitted on his real-name registered Weibo account that he had "flirted" with the Lamborghini online store.

Many netizens claimed they also joined in the "flirting", saying "it feels great, like grabbing parking spaces."

But some netizens criticized this behavior, saying there's no need to disrupt people's normal business just because the price is too high. "They're neither deceiving nor cheating us, besides, there are plenty of luxury stores in the city, it's not worth causing trouble."

Additionally, some netizens posted a screenshot of a chat record where they asked the customer service staff: "Is shipping free? What courier do you use?" The response was filled with profanity and reminded the "poor guy" to respect himself. Netizens questioned whether Lamborghini and Taobao were using this method to stir up publicity.

● Response

The online store will not complain about netizens who only bid without buying

It was learned that the physical store of this online store is located in Shanghai, operated by Shanghai Jingpinhong Auto Sales Company.

This morning, the marketing department stated that they did not expect such high attention on the Lamborghini online store and clarified that the "customer service swearing" incident was not a publicity stunt. This time, Lamborghini going online could sell sports cars while also providing an "experience" opportunity for fans of Lamborghini.

When orders are not paid within 3 days, the system will automatically cancel them. Therefore, the online store will not file complaints against netizens who only bid without buying.

Regarding the "customer service swearing" incident, Taobao confirmed that this information was untrue and denied the allegations of a publicity stunt.

In the statement released on the home page of the Lamborghini online store, it says, "the chat record screenshots circulating online are fabricated," and another version of the chat record screenshot was published, replacing the original "swearing" part. Customer service personnel explained, "if you want to buy, we can deliver to your door."

Mr. Zhao from Taobao's PR department introduced that Lamborghini was not the first car brand to open a store on Taobao, plus its own brand influence means it doesn't need such stunts to increase its fame.

● News extension

It was learned that currently Audi, Geely, Hyundai, Lamborghini and other 7 brands have opened Taobao online stores. According to Taobao's statistics in May this year, car sales have exceeded 10 million yuan. In May, a netizen purchased a full-price Audi A8 priced at 1.11 million yuan in Audi's official online store.

Taobao staff expressed optimism about the model of selling cars online. According to a luxury consumption report published in 2010, last year our country's luxury consumption reached over 3 billion yuan, mostly through overseas proxy purchases. More than half of consumers expressed willingness to buy online and have made psychological preparations.
