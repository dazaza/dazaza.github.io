Essential Skills for Understanding and Using the Android Operating System (Part 1)

by dream2012 on 2012-02-20 12:52:56

The Android operating system might seem complicated and confusing to many novice users who are just getting started. Here, we have compiled some essential tips for understanding and using the Android operating system, hoping they will be helpful. Please note that these tips mainly target the most mainstream Android 2.1 system, but most of them also apply to older or newer Android systems.

1. Use the Android Power Management Widget

Starting from Android 2.1, a very powerful built-in energy management widget is available. Through this widget, you can quickly turn on or off Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, GPS, and synchronization, which are major power consumers. You can also conveniently set the brightness of your phone screen. To enable it, long press on the phone screen, then choose from the widgets directory.

2. Android Phone Dialing Screen

If you have special requirements for the functionality of your phone's dialing screen, Android will definitely meet your needs. Click on any user in the contact list, press the phone menu > options, and there will be detailed settings for individual contacts.

3. Customize Ringtones for Each Contact

You can set separate ringtones for your girlfriend or family members so that you know who's calling when you hear the ringtone. The setup method is to click on any user in the contact list, then you can set the ringtone.

4. Do Not Include the Phone Alarm Clock in the Process Kill List

This is a classic mistake. Many users like to use process management programs to automatically kill some programs to save power or free up phone memory. However, if you accidentally kill the alarm clock process, the consequence will be waking up at 10:30 AM the next morning and rushing to work in panic.

5. Organize Content with Folders

Although it took Steve Jobs a long time to decide to allow iOS users to organize content through folders, in fact, Android has supported this function since its inception. Android folders not only support placing app icons but can also store contacts, even Facebook contacts and other information. Long press on the phone screen to create a folder on the desktop.

6. Rename Folders

After creating a folder, you may need to change its name. Just open the folder and long press on the name at the top to bring up the folder renaming window.

7. Check System Date

New Android users may find checking the system date a bit troublesome. In fact, just hold the notification bar at the top left corner of the screen, and today's date will be displayed. Try it now.

8. Mount Your SD Card

Since Android is based on the Linux kernel, external disks must be mounted before they can be used normally. When connecting your phone to a computer via USB, the phone will prompt you to mount the SD card. You must click to complete the mounting before you can copy or delete data on the SD card.

9. Set Keyboard Shortcuts

Another important reason why many users are fond of physical full keyboards is that Android supports a large number of keyboard shortcuts. Through shortcuts, you can quickly invoke related programs. You can set them in system settings > applications > quick launch.

10. Download More Android Live Wallpapers

Another feature supported by Android starting from version 2.1 is live wallpapers. Although live wallpapers may increase your phone's power consumption, they make your phone look cooler. If you like being cool, search for "live wallpaper" in the Android Market, and there will be many dynamic wallpapers waiting for you to choose from.
