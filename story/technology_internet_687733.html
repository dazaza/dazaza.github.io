The case of the disfigured teenage girl in Hefei has entered the judicial process, and the heaviest punishment could be death with reprieve.

by erng6q4b on 2012-03-02 17:44:51

Yesterday, the Hefei Public Security Bureau released a notice regarding the case of the intentional harm to female student Zhou. The notice stated that the case has entered the judicial process, the suspect has been detained in the detention center, and the recent login to the [Renren Network] account [Tao] was an impersonation. Relevant departments are investigating.

[Notice]

The destruction of a young girl by lighter fluid in a Sprite bottle

On the evening of September 17, 2011, the suspect Tao (male, born April 1, 1995, a student at a certain college in Hefei) failed in his pursuit of the victim Zhou (female, a former middle school classmate of Tao), which led to feelings of revenge. He went to Zhou's home, poured lighter fluid he had prepared beforehand in a Sprite bottle onto Zhou's body, and set her on fire, causing burns to her face, neck, and other areas. After hearing the news, Zhou's family came and extinguished the fire. Subsequently, Zhou was sent to the Burn Unit of the First Affiliated Hospital of Anhui Medical University for treatment.

On the morning of September 18, 2011, relatives of the victim Zhou reported the incident to the Yaohai Branch of the Hefei Public Security Bureau. The Yaohai Public Security Bureau quickly launched an investigation and arrested the suspect Tao. That evening, they legally placed him under criminal detention. On September 22, the Criminal Science and Technology Institute of the Hefei Public Security Bureau issued a "Preliminary Review Opinion on the Degree of Bodily Injury" based on the photos and medical records provided by the investigating unit. On September 26, the prosecution approved the execution of the arrest of Tao based on this document.

Injury assessment not currently appropriate

The injury assessment of the victim is an important piece of evidence in this case and also a crucial basis for determining the guilt and sentencing of the suspect as well as civil compensation. In response, the public security authorities have always attached great importance to it. During the hospitalization of the victim Zhou, she was treated in the intensive care unit due to severe injuries, making it difficult to conduct an injury inspection inside the ward. However, forensic doctors and police officers from the public security bureau visited the hospital eight times to inquire about the condition and treatment progress from the attending doctor or the victim's relatives. After the victim was discharged, her injured areas were wrapped with bandages, preventing further damage inspections. Police officers held three interviews with the victim's relatives to understand the recovery of her injuries.

On February 20, 2012, two forensic doctors and police officers from the bureau specially visited the victim Zhou's home to inspect her injuries and invited Zhou and her relatives to come to the forensic office of the criminal investigation team for further inspection and evaluation on another day. On the afternoon of February 24, accompanied by her family and the investigating police officers, Zhou arrived at the forensic office where the forensic doctors conducted a detailed inspection and photographed her injured areas. According to the treatment records of the First Affiliated Hospital of Anhui Medical University, her neck injuries may require a second surgery. Based on forensic practices, an injury assessment can only be conducted after the treatment concludes (involving functional disorders requiring three to six months of rehabilitation). Therefore, it is currently inappropriate to conduct an injury assessment on the degree of neck damage of the victim.

The next step for the public security authorities will be to strictly follow relevant regulations and promptly make a comprehensive injury assessment based on the treatment and recovery of the victim Zhou. For damages that do not yet meet the conditions for assessment, supplementary evaluations will be conducted during the litigation phase of the case.

Someone impersonated the suspect online

Since September 18 last year, the suspect Tao has been detained in the Hefei No. 1 Detention Center without internet access or possibility.

Preliminary investigations revealed that the recent logins to the [Renren Network] account [Tao] were clearly impersonations. Relevant departments are investigating.

[Visit]

In the eyes of the head teacher, Tao Rukun prefers leniency over severity

Yesterday, the reporter visited Zhou Yan's home where her mother was changing her bandages. Zhou Yan was in unbearable pain. Her mother said that Zhou Yan's mental state had been very poor recently, and she was most worried about people seeing her current appearance. In the eyes of her family and teachers and classmates, Zhou Yan was sensible and obedient; regarding her relationship with Tao Rukun, the Zhou family said that perhaps they dated before, but after discovering his issues, she no longer wanted to continue. In the eyes of the head teacher, Tao Rukun was highly rebellious but hadn't committed any major offenses.

Zhou Family

Parents busy with work sometimes neglected Zhou Yan

On the evening of September 17 last year, Saturday. Zhou Yan's aunt Li Yun said that she was the only one at Zhou Yan's house at the time, and she opened the door when Zhou Yan returned. Not long after, screams came from Zhou Yan's room, and when she rushed there, the fire had already started burning, with Tao Rukun standing beside her holding a lighter.

[Tao Rukun followed Zhou Yan home, had prepared the oil beforehand, and had planned it.] Li Yun said that the incident didn't occur at the entrance but inside Zhou Yan's room. Zhou's home was approximately 80-90 square meters, with three bedrooms, and Zhou Yan's bedroom was at the farthest end. Regarding why Zhou Yan allowed Tao Rukun into her room, the Zhou family did not respond.

Li Yun said that Zhou Yan suffered immense psychological trauma. Before being discharged from the hospital, Zhou Yan kept saying that she didn't want to go home, she didn't dare to return to her own room, she feared being seen by others, and she lacked a sense of security. To address this, the family redecorated Zhou Yan's bedroom. The room was newly wallpapered with floral patterns, new beds, cabinets, and computer desks were added, and the family said they wanted to provide Zhou Yan with a new environment.

In Zhou Yan's room, there were photographs of her from childhood to the present, but now, a quarter of her body had been burned. The diagnosis certificate from the First Affiliated Hospital of Anhui Medical University read: 28% burn area, multiple parts of the body with second-degree to third-degree burns, respiratory tract burns.

Yesterday, the reporter saw words written by Zhou Yan on the back of a photo taken when she was around six or seven years old: [I hope you never forget me, left by Zhou Yan]. Li Yun said that these words were unknown to whom they were addressed.

Both of Zhou Yan's parents worked as workshop workers at Unilever. Born on Mid-Autumn Festival in 1995, Zhou Yan lived in a flat with her family at the time. Around 2000, Zhou Yan's parents bought their current apartment. At the time, housing prices were about 2,000 yuan per square meter, and the family relied on the limited salaries of her parents to maintain their livelihood.

Sometimes working day shifts and sometimes night shifts, Zhou Yan's parents often didn't have time for her, and Aunt Li Yun was the person who spent the most time with Zhou Yan.

Yesterday, at Zhou Yan's home, Zhou's father spoke little, constantly cleaning the scar patches that Zhou Yan had changed. These scar patches cost about 200 yuan each, and Zhou's father cleaned nearly a hundred daily.

Zhou's mother was continuously applying ointment to Zhou Yan, who occasionally cried out in pain, gritting her teeth to hold back tears. When asked if Zhou's mother was usually busy and sometimes neglected her child, Zhou's mother nodded and couldn't help but shed tears. Li Yun said that Zhou Yan liked playing the guzheng but might not be able to play it anymore.

In the eyes of Zhou Yan's father, mother, and aunt, Zhou Yan was very obedient, had good academic performance, and even received several awards. Li Yun said that after Tao Rukun entered Zhou Yan's life, her life trajectory changed, and her smiles diminished. She transferred to Feidong County Cuozhen Middle School in high school and then took a year off.

[From Shouchun Middle School to Feidong Cuozhen Middle School? Unless someone has water in their brain. Shouchun Middle School is a place everyone wants to get into.] A taxi driver in Hefei who was unaware of the details expressed incomprehension.

School

Zhou Yan diligent, Tao Rukun strong-willed and rebellious

Shouchun Middle School is the alma mater of both Zhou Yan and Tao Rukun. Speaking about their relationship, the vice-principal responsible for student affairs revealed that they were in the same grade but different classes, and often went home together after school. There had been rumors about their friendship.

[The middle school period is when one forms habits and corrects them. Although both graduated in June 2010, the school bears responsibility for such an incident.] This vice-principal said that early romance among teenagers during adolescence is normal, and excessive criticism should be avoided.

It was introduced that Zhou Yan was in Class 28, serving as the discipline committee member, diligent in study, and had above-average academic performance. Tao Rukun's homeroom teacher, Ms. Wang, told reporters that Tao visited her on Teachers' Day last year, claiming he was studying at Hefei No. 1 High School, but Hefei No. 1 High School stated there was no such person.

Ms. Wang said that Tao Rukun performed poorly academically, would daydream, doze off, and talk back to teachers, but hadn't committed any major offenses. Online reports of Tao Rukun getting into gang fights, assaulting teachers, and robbing people did not happen during his junior high school years.

[Tao Rukun is a student who prefers leniency over severity, with a strong rebellious nature.] Ms. Wang said that some teachers used overly harsh methods to educate him, which proved counterproductive.

In Tao Rukun's Weibo posts, last September, while commenting on a video titled [Son Complains About His Father's Lack of Money and Swears at Him], he said: [Damn, I really want to hit him!]

Audit Bureau

Tao's father advanced from a clerk to a section-level position

At Tao Ru's father Tao Wen's workplace, the Hefei Audit Bureau, Deputy Secretary Zhu revealed that on the afternoon of February 26, Tao Wen took leave, saying he would return to work after handling matters at home.

According to Deputy Secretary Zhu, Tao Wen passed the civil service examination in 1989 and entered the audit bureau. Initially just a small clerk, he worked diligently for more than 20 years and eventually became the director of the office. Tao Wen is not from Hefei but from another district or county within Anhui Province. [Tao Wen is a section-level cadre, merely a functionary, earning a few thousand yuan monthly. Netizens calling his son 'second generation official' somewhat confuse right and wrong.]

Will this matter affect Tao Wen's work? Deputy Secretary Zhu said that if the event receives a reasonable trial, it won't affect his work since it is a private matter; however, if unjust situations arise due to his position, it will inevitably impact his work, and they will handle it strictly.

Tao Ru's home is in the Tianwan Lake neighborhood in the western development zone of Hefei City, one of Hefei's upscale neighborhoods. The property management said the average price of houses in the neighborhood was around 8,000 to 9,000 yuan, with all house areas exceeding 100 square meters. Yesterday, the reporter visited Tao Ru's home, where no one was present. Those in the know said they haven't seen them return since the 26th.

A woman in the neighborhood who claimed to know Tao Ru said that Tao Wen and his wife were busy with work, so Tao Ru was left in the care of his grandparents. Busy with work, they educated him insufficiently, leading to his becoming somewhat reclusive and radical.

Lawyer

Tao's family refused interviews

Tao Ru's defense lawyer, Mr. Shen, visited Zhou's home yesterday. Mr. Shen said he came to apologize on behalf of Tao's parents and stated that Tao's family would actively cooperate with Zhou's subsequent treatment.

Mr. Shen revealed that he had visited Tao Ru in the detention center, [He was in a room with more than ten people. When I went, he complained to me. In my view, the boy's mental state isn't good either].

The reporter expressed interest in interviewing Tao Wen, but after consulting with Tao Wen, Mr. Shen said that Tao Wen's mental state was very poor, under immense pressure, and not ready to accept media interviews.

Clarification

Whether they were in a romantic relationship does not affect the verdict

Would the relationship between Zhou Yan and Tao Ru affect the verdict? Lawyer Zheng Jinen from Jianghuai Law Firm said it wouldn't, [There's no need to argue about this issue, it doesn't help with the verdict. Based on Zhou Yan's injuries, considering Tao Ru's age of 16, the sentence would be at least 10 years or more, possibly even life imprisonment with a suspended death sentence].
