A Preview of the 2010 Shanghai World Expo

by longersing on 2010-01-31 16:28:18

The 2010 World Expo is different from ordinary corporate conferences and exhibitions, and it is also different from ordinary diplomacy between countries. More appropriately speaking: The Expo is a stage for the exhibition of the cultural and technological essence of various countries, a super stage that you cannot miss. Guilin Conference Tourism Network will provide you with full coverage of the unprecedented grandeur of the Shanghai Expo.

China Pavilion: A Hundred-Meter Wall Featuring the "Qingming Scroll"

In the planning map of the Expo, the total construction area of the China Pavilion is 160,100 square meters, standing at 62 meters high, equivalent to a 20-story building. The pavilion will consist of three parts: the China National Pavilion, the Regional Pavilion, and the Hong Kong, Macao, and Taiwan Pavilion. The appearance of the China Pavilion is conceptualized as the "Crown of the East," comparing the towering national pavilion to a magnificent crown and heaven, and the regional pavilion that gathers people to the four directions and earth, symbolizing "the granary of the world, prosperity in all directions." On the core and highest floor of the pavilion, at the 49-meter level exhibition area on the north side, a hundred-meter-long wall will be covered by the Song Dynasty masterpiece "Qingming Scroll" magnified hundreds of times, and the original piece of this national treasure will also appear in the China Pavilion during the Expo.

Finland Pavilion: Sauna Room Inside the Expo Venue

Inspired Finns will build the only sauna room in the Shanghai Expo venue within their Finland Pavilion, named "Ice Pot." The design inspiration for "Ice Pot" comes from the pot holes naturally formed by glacial erosion of rocks. Entering the pavilion feels like walking into a courtyard with flowing water and rocks, and the interior wall screens will showcase Finland's unique islands and other scenic beauties.

France Pavilion: French Mayor Presides Over Weddings

Renowned as the "Capital of Art" and the "City of Romance," France closely adheres to these two themes. During the Expo, they plan to transport seven top artistic treasures from the Musée d'Orsay, including works by French painter Millet's "The Angelus," Manet's "The Balcony," Van Gogh's "The Dance Hall in Arles," Cézanne's "Woman with a Coffee Pot," Bonnard's "The Dressing Room," Gauguin's "Meal (or Bananas)," and Rodin's sculpture "The Age of Bronze." The inherently romantic French will also bring the "French Romantic Wedding Ceremony" to the France Pavilion. During the Expo, newlyweds can apply to hold a wedding celebration, presided over by Jean Germain, the Mayor of Tours, who will issue a legally binding French marriage certificate and present a sumptuous French meal.

UK Pavilion: 60,000 Seeds Create an Impression

The UK National Pavilion, considered the "most creative" among all pavilions, has a main structure resembling a "glowing box." The six-story cubic structure is covered with 60,000 acrylic fiber "tentacles" that emit different colors depending on the light. More intriguingly, each "tentacle" contains seeds of different kinds and shapes at its base. They could be a pine cone, a coffee bean, or a seed whose name you might not know. The UK National Pavilion also boasts an open-air space the size of a soccer field, ingeniously designed as "an unwrapped gift package," symbolizing the "Seed Cathedral" presented as a gift to the Shanghai Expo.

Spain Pavilion: "Woven Basket" Displays Spanish Charm

Saying that the Spain National Pavilion is hand-woven is no exaggeration. The steel framework supports the streamlined shape of the entire Spain Pavilion, making disassembly convenient after the event; the woven wicker panels cover the entire venue without blocking natural light. The streamlined appearance resembles undulating waves, full of dynamism. Most anticipated is that 8,524 wicker panels of different textures and colors will use calligraphy art to form ancient Chinese characters, creating a Chinese poem on the exterior wall.

Turkey Pavilion: Ancient Maps Display Elegance

A red perforated exterior wall, beige walls, animal carvings depicting hunting scenes... The creative design inspiration for the Turkey Pavilion comes from one of the world's oldest villages, Catalhoyuk, with a history of 8,500 years. There is a mural on the wall of this village, regarded as the world's oldest map or planning diagram. Entering the Turkey Pavilion, you will have the opportunity to see the "oldest map."

Switzerland Pavilion: Cable Cars Ascend and Descend

What does Switzerland have? Many people would think of snow-capped mountains. This time, the Swiss have transported cable cars commonly found on snowy mountains intact to the Expo Park. During the Expo, 20 cable cars will operate within the Switzerland Pavilion, allowing visitors to ride the sightseeing cable cars up and through the natural space on the roof, completing a wonderful journey from Swiss cities to the countryside.

Poland Pavilion: A Hundred Pianos Play Chopin's Masterpieces

2010 marks the 200th anniversary of the birth of Poland's famous composer and pianist, Chopin. During the Shanghai Expo, the core cultural activity at the Poland Pavilion will be commemorating Chopin. At that time, daily Chopin piano concerts will be held in the central square and concert hall of the Poland Pavilion. Even more interestingly, the Poland Pavilion will host a unique concert during the Expo, inviting 100 musicians to simultaneously play Chopin's works on 100 pianos, setting a new Guinness World Record.
