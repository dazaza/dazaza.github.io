This is an ordinary place, so mundane that throughout history, not a single somewhat famous person has emerged from here.

In the earliest period after my birth, I didn't leave much of an impression, only remembering that we all started by staying in a pile of cardboard boxes. There were two people nearby who later gradually gave us the concept of "parents."

After an unknown length of time, one day, father forcibly pulled me away from the pile of cardboard boxes and took me to a place outside the city.

When I first arrived here, I noticed that mother was no longer with father as she had been before. For a long time, father never mentioned anything about mother, but instead taught me how to hunt, including catching mice hiding in holes and fish in a nearby lake. As a beginner, I frequently tripped over narrow mouse holes or choked on water because I couldn't swim. The worst part wasn't the pain; it was that every day, father made me repeatedly practice these monotonous actions over and over again.

Tired of forced repetition, I finally asked father after another painful training session, "Why do I have to do these boring movements every day?"

"Can you still remember where the mice and fish you ate when you were little came from? They are living beings too, they don't just sit still, and they certainly won't jump into your mouth!" Father said this coldly without any expression.

"Isn't there an easier way then?"

"We aren't like humans, with brains that can infinitely expand and diversify," father continued without expression, pointing at some stones far away that looked like they reached the sky. "Do you see those human settlements built out of stone, towering heights beyond our imagination? And those various machines made of metal that move... All these are things they create using natural resources. They can use any method to achieve their survival goals, and not just survival. They can use these things to realize all sorts of wishes. But for us, we only have the most primitive methods: using our own strength to capture the food we need. There are no shortcuts. Once, when your mother and I were picking up leftover fish meat from humans, a human saw us. We had no enmity with him, yet he poured scalding hot water towards us. Luckily, we ran back in time and weren't injured."

Mother... yes, where is mother now after all this time?

"Dad, why haven't you mentioned mother since we came here? Where is she now?"

"..."

"Dad, please tell me!"

"..." Father's face remained expressionless, but his facial muscles twitched slightly. "It seems you're not so small anymore. Fine, you'll find out sooner or later, so I might as well tell you everything. Do you know why we left the paper box place instead of staying there? That day, it was just dawn, and I had just woken up when I suddenly felt the surrounding piles of trash violently shaking. I quickly went out to check and found that human machines were clearing this area of garbage. I didn't have time to think much, so I dragged you, who was still half-asleep, out of there. However, when I returned, the cardboard boxes we were in had already been buried by trash. Not long after, they built another human settlement on that land. Humans are greedy like that. We don't have great power, so we can't take revenge. We can only survive by hunting food every day."

I felt like crying, but in the end, I didn't show it.

We continued our repetitive life of hunting for food. My skills improved gradually, and the probability of getting hurt decreased to almost negligible levels. Every day, under the warm and glaring sunlight, we felt exhausted. We had no reason not to choose to lie lazily on the grass and nap. The bright objects around us seemed unclear. When the sun set and the moon decorated the earth in another beautiful form, it was when we felt most energetic. With proficient techniques, we hunted the prey we discovered. This kind of life, though dull, finally freed us from worrying about survival.

Any form of life prolonged enough will make everyone seek novelty, even the most comfortable life. Father said that if I stayed here forever, I would never grow up.

Gradually, we finally set out to search for a new place to live. But this time, we weren't so lucky. We didn't know how many days we walked, but we still hadn't found an ideal location—mainly due to the lack of water sources. We wanted to go back, but we had long forgotten which direction we came from, so how could we return? There was no other option but to keep moving forward, either finding water or dying of thirst.

After two more days and nights, just when we were about to give up hope, a city dominated the entire horizon ahead of us. Below that line of the horizon, reflecting the sunlight with a sparkling light—a river! Oh, let's drink! We had no reason to suppress our joy. Suddenly, a voice came from beside us: "You... are you new here? I don't seem to have seen you before."

Only then did we realize that there was another cat drinking water next to us. Unlike our red-and-black striped appearance, she had silver-white fur—this was the first time I had seen such a cat. Still, it was father who spoke first: "Yes, our hometown is gone, so we've come here to explore."

This was a good excuse for me... I thought, but of course, I didn't say it aloud.

"Oh, then why don't you join our society afterward? We have many members here. Maybe it's related to the large number of humans in this massive human settlement, which I once heard called 'Chongqing' something—I don't have the special ability to understand human language. We have many members, so it's convenient to handle affairs. We can also contact some compatriots from other nearby human settlements and hold regular social gatherings. By the way, shouldn't your place be a human settlement too? Why don't you have a society?"

"Well, maybe the economy in our place isn't developed enough yet. We agree, so we'll register at your community later."

"Alright, then meet at a garbage pile closer to the center of this human city later. There are a few trash bins nearby that can serve as reference points. I'm going back now."

"Okay, see you."

Looking at her retreating figure, my heart was still unsettled.

"Dad, are we really going there?"

"Otherwise, we can only continue wandering. Living in this world for even a day is not easy. Having one more companion to support each other gives us more courage to survive."

"Fine, but it looks like we need to ask for directions..."

"We can't ask humans; our languages are different. As for our compatriots, it's unlikely we'll encounter them easily."

"Troublesome, if only human language was the same as ours."

"If what you said were true, the world would be in chaos. Alright, let's set off. At worst, we'll just take a few detours."

We were like refugees avoiding war, except worse—we couldn't even compare to refugees. Refugees in such a large human gathering place would still have opportunities to ask for directions, but we could only rely on our instincts to slowly advance toward the city center—we knew the consequences of being hit by those fast-moving human machines.

"We must find the target location before nightfall," father said, "otherwise, we will definitely starve to death."

Easier said than done. This human city was like a maze. After turning left several times, when we saw the trash bins she mentioned—we had forgotten to ask her name—we immediately realized we had arrived.

But we couldn't connect what we saw with her description—it looked like there was not a single fellow creature, no vitality, how could this possibly be a community? Just as we were puzzled, suddenly hundreds of cats emerged from among various pieces of trash: "Haha, welcome new members! Surprise is our consistent way of greeting newcomers."

"As far as I know, we didn't have the habit of living in large groups before. Why are you all living together?" Father was still confused.

No one answered. Only an elder, who appeared to be the leader, full of old age marks. "We originally didn't live here," he coughed a few times, "we moved here from the nearby cities around this human city. In those cities, humans kept reducing the size of waste disposal areas to continuously expand construction sites, causing our homes to suffer. Many of our clan members were captured and killed by them, so we had no choice but to move here. We heard that the mayor of this city doesn't promote such policies, so it should be safer here."

I don't know what father was thinking, but I currently felt secretly pleased—I could finally feed myself properly, and perhaps even thrive. Haha.

As expected, the following days were undoubtedly the happiest of my life—instead of just the two of us catching mice and fish, the leader allocated tasks, and we all went out in groups. The spoils were distributed according to effort, and the meals were several times better than what I used to have. As for entertainment, the regular gatherings she mentioned gathered all kinds of talented singers and dancers from all directions. Various colored troupes competed brilliantly, making it difficult even for professional judges to distinguish between them. Oh, wait, her name... during a toast, the leader said to her—yes, I'm sure it was her—"Fuyuan, bring some more fish soup." Yes, I remembered the name well, haha.

Good times always pass quickly. One day, while we were still immersed in the joy of the gathering, Fuyuan suddenly raised her voice and said, "Uh-oh, I hear a sound, very similar to the human machine that came to clean our home before." As soon as she finished speaking, a wall next to our community was instantly pushed down! Everyone barely had time to prepare or gather belongings! We could only run in all directions! Father originally called out for me to run with him, but at that moment, a trash bin fell...

I have no memory of what happened next. After an unknown amount of time, I felt the hard object pressing on me suddenly being moved, followed by something soft and light slowly covering me. I struggled to open my eyes and found that father and Fuyuan, who had been with me earlier, were nowhere to be seen. Instead, there was... a human girl standing in front of me? She opened her mouth, but I paid no attention to listening to her. Father had once said that each species has its unique language, which we fundamentally cannot understand. However, what followed made me doubt it. "Are you okay?" I stared wide-eyed—I clearly understood what she was saying! I pinched myself—it wasn't a hallucination. "I'm fine," was still a simple sentence, but she was also surprised. I continued, "It seems you can understand our language." "Too flattering, how could I have the ability of the little boy in the movie 'The Atmosphere Disappears'?" "But the fact is that you can understand. Also, what is 'Atmosphere Disappears'?" "I'll tell you when there's a chance. Right now, your body is very weak. Let me take you home to recuperate." "Wait, where's my father? And my friends?" "My father is the mayor, and he originally didn't plan to do this. But the developers convinced him with profits, and then they started cleaning this place. She sighed, "Let me take you home. Staying here now is no different from waiting to die. Whether my parents will allow it or not, they usually indulge me, so it should be fine. Even if not, I can hide you and secretly take care of you."

She put me in her schoolbag, leaving a bit of the zipper open, "Move around a bit. I'll wake you up when we get home."

In a completely unfamiliar environment, I had no choice but to comply.

After an unknown amount of time, I was awakened by her voice. "We're here. My parents are temporarily not at home. Great, I'll go to the fridge and get some milk for you to drink."

I didn't refuse or try to decline—even resisting here would be meaningless.

A beverage I had never tasted before always tastes good. Feeling energized, I couldn't help but lick my lips a few times.

Do girls naturally possess keen observational skills? Regardless of whether it's Fuyuan or this human girl who is still relatively unfamiliar to me, they both give me the same feeling. "My name is Dai Wei Ni, what about you?" "...Father never gave me a name." "...Ah, that makes sense. The animals in 'The Atmosphere Disappears' didn't have names either." "By the way, what exactly is 'Atmosphere Disappears'?" "It's a story movie made by humans. The basic plot revolves around a train hijacking incident, resulting in the leakage of three sections of yellow tank cars filled with highly toxic substances, burning through the ozone layer above a certain region, putting global life in peril. But that's not the main point. The protagonist is a little boy who can also understand the language of a stuffed cat that often stays in his backpack, which is quite similar to our current situation. As for the name, I'll give you one." She drank a glass of water and continued, "To be honest, my family background in this city should be considered pretty good. However, living on such high floors, I rarely had opportunities to play games with peers growing up. All day, I could only follow the adults' requests to study this and that, with no interest. Even if they could buy me delicious food and toys, it wouldn't change my feeling of boredom. There's a lot of homework, and even after finishing it, I could only stare blankly at the blurry buildings outside the window. By the way, I'll name you 'Mirage'... wait, that's too complicated. Simplified, I'll call you 'Reflection'."

Reflection... Reflection... I silently contemplated this new name she gave me, still not quite accustomed to it.

"Of course, that movie is only similar in this aspect to our current situation. I'm not like the main character, bearing the responsibility of saving the Earth, and even if I were alone, I couldn't possibly bear it. Anyway, from now on, you'll sleep in the upper layer of my backpack. I'm not sure about the adults' attitudes for now."

Since then, Wei Ni took me with her to school every day. She told me that I should sleep well while she's in class, and she'll let me out to play after class. Human schools were strange places to me, but despite that, I didn't like the feeling of being watched by many pairs of eyes simultaneously.

This went on for about a year without any major incidents. However, I might have forgotten to ask her about the adults' attitude towards me. One day, as usual, she was doing her homework and playing with me. A rhythmic and urgent knocking sound came. Wei Ni hid me and went to open the door. She didn't return to the room until evening, then she brought out a plate of fish and called me out, signaling me to eat slowly. "A classmate wants to borrow you for a few days. I refused, and then he told my father. My father doesn't want you to stay here," her tone was still gentle, but her teary eyes betrayed her emotions. "Previously, the order to clear the garbage area was given by my father. Developers told my father that cats and dogs spread various diseases, and he believed them." "Don't worry, I know how to take care of myself." "Ha ha. Don't fool me. This is the city center, not undeveloped suburbs. There's basically no source of food for you to find, and your body hasn't recovered much yet. You won't even make it to the suburbs." "If I have the will, I can walk there." "Even if you have the will, what can you do without the strength? I don't know what you're thinking, but I have a fiancé now. Yet, strangely, for you, I feel a kind of love at first sight—a feeling that should only exist between the same species. Love is inexplicable, but the fact is, it exists here. Even if you're not human, just a cat, as someone with feelings, how could I bear to let you go? Moreover, how would you survive outside..." She sniffled. "Eat slowly. They only allowed me to keep you for one night. Tomorrow, I'll have to send you away." I felt like the fish in front of me was as tough as a shell that I couldn't chew. It seemed like I would never finish eating it in a lifetime. Of course, that was just an illusion. The lights went out, and I lay quietly, the smell in her room probably came from some artificial perfume—but that didn't affect my memorization.

The next night eventually came. I slowly opened—or rather forced open—one of my eyes. Wei Ni stood at the entrance of their house, with bloodshot eyes, clearly having not slept well. I walked out emotionlessly, then slowly descended the stairs along the railing. Her gaze remained fixed on me the whole time, and mine on her. When our gazes lost their targets, I heard a sudden slam of the door, mixed with five parts anger and five parts helplessness. And me, the railing ahead seemed to lead to an endless abyss.

Eventually, I reached the bottom floor of this human residential building. If walking to a garbage pile near the outskirts of Chongqing city center almost starved me to death before, now being in the true center of the city, walking to the suburbs wouldn't be any different from what Wei Ni said—a certain death?

But where could I go now? There was no place in this human city where I could find food, and father and the others were not around to help. I could only gamble once with death—either reach the suburbs before collapsing, or perish. All other pursuits and goals were discarded; now, there was only one thought in my mind: survival.

The roads were still filled with various speeding machines, and I still had to take long detours. What Wei Ni said was correct—at least theoretically. I felt like I had just crossed the cleared area from my previous visit, and I was already exhausted. But I kept reminding myself not to lie down, otherwise, I would never have the chance to stand up again. My legs felt like they were tied with heavy fish, and at times, I could only drag them slowly.

Until my eyes could barely stay open, I finally saw a hint of blue—yes, it was the river where my father and I first drank water when we arrived here.
