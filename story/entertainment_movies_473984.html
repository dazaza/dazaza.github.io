◎ English Title: Transsiberian

◎ Year: 2008

◎ Genre: Thriller/Crime/Drama

◎ Director: Brad Anderson

◎ Cast: Emily Mortimer as Jessie, Woody Harrelson as Roy, Ben Kingsley as Grinko, Eduardo Noriega as Carlos

◎ Synopsis:

The story takes place on the Trans-Siberian Railway that spans Russia. Jessie (Emily Mortimer) and her husband Roy (Woody Harrelson) take the train along the Trans-Siberian Railway to Moscow. Among the colorful Russian passengers, this American couple appears particularly awkward, conservative, and ignorant. Another couple boards midway: Carlos (Eduardo Noriega) and his girlfriend Abby (Kate Mara). A subtle relationship develops between these two couples. Jessie and Carlos exchange glances, hinting at growing feelings. The train makes a brief stop at a remote Russian town, where everyone gets off to rest. Mysteriously, Roy disappears. The other passengers reboard, and the journey continues. Jessie was promiscuous before marriage, and with Roy missing, she quickly becomes intimate with Carlos. This strange Siberian journey involves not only passion but also murder and crime. Officer Grinko boards to investigate a drug case, accidentally uncovering secrets about the main characters.

Roy and Jessie are a middle-aged American couple. Roy is the owner of a hardware store, warm-hearted, and passionate about spreading the gospel through church aid. Jessie is a photographer, sharp-tongued, and slightly critical. Before marriage, Jessie was promiscuous and carefree. Although she settled down after marrying Roy, a good man, the restless factor still flows in her blood, making their marriage more in appearance than in spirit. They served as volunteers in China for two weeks with a church group and then decided to take the famous Trans-Siberian train to Moscow to enjoy the exotic scenery along the way.

During the journey, the Roy couple meets a young couple, Carlos and Abby. The four chat happily. The train makes a short stop at a remote Russian town, and the passengers disembark to rest. However, when the train starts again, Jessie realizes that her husband Roy has not reboarded. From here, the plot takes a dramatic turn. The previous ease and joy disappear, replaced by a heavy and suspenseful atmosphere. Other passengers enthusiastically help search for the suddenly missing Roy, but Jessie soon discovers that their motives are not pure. Lonely, Jessie succumbs to temptation and commits adultery with Carlos. The originally warm and harmonious carriage gradually transforms into hell, filled with murder, deception, lies, and sin.
