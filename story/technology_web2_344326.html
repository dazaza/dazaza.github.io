Don't let imperfections affect your whole life.

by qingchuyulan on 2010-04-12 22:13:52

His wife was just a poor paint farmer, barely scraping together the funds to support his studies abroad. That year, he had the fortune of being accepted into a prestigious university in the U.S. — Yale University. However, he faced the risk of having to drop out due to an inability to pay the high tuition fees. As such, he decided to use his vacation time to work as a painter like his wife in order to earn enough money for the tuition. He took on various jobs and eventually secured a painting job for a small house. Despite the homeowner being very picky, the offered price was not low. It was not only enough to cover that semester's tuition but even left some room for living expenses.

One day, nearing the completion of the job, he was about to give the final coat of paint on the cupboard door panel that he had taken down. Once painted, it would be put back to dry. But at that moment, the doorbell suddenly rang, and in his haste to answer it, he tripped over a broom. The falling broom knocked over one of the cupboard door panels, which then fell onto a freshly painted white wall, leaving a clear mark. He immediately used a scraper to remove the mark and filled it with some paint. After everything dried thoroughly, he looked left and right, feeling that the newly patched paint did not match the original wall color. Considering the picky homeowner, and for the sake of the soon-to-be-lost compensation, he thought he should repaint the entire wall.

Finally, after working tirelessly, he finished. However, upon entering the next day, he noticed that the wall he repainted the previous day had a noticeable color difference compared to the adjacent walls, becoming more apparent the closer he looked. In the end, he decided to repaint all the walls...

Ultimately, even the picky homeowner was satisfied with his work and paid him the agreed amount. But for him, after deducting the cost of the paint, there was hardly anything left, far from enough to pay the tuition.

The homeowner's daughter somehow learned the truth of the matter and informed her father. After knowing this, she was deeply moved and, upon her daughter's request, agreed to sponsor him through college. After graduating, this young man not only married the homeowner’s daughter, but also entered the company owned by this person. More than ten years later, he became the chairman of this company. He is none other than the current billionaire owner of the world-renowned Walmart supermarket - Sam Walton.

A minor mistake can lead to a flaw, a flaw can ruin the perfection of a wall, one wall can affect all the walls, and all the walls can influence a person's life... The consequences of flaws do not lie in the flaws themselves, but rather in people's attitudes towards these flaws.
