World-famous performer: Mèng Xiǎodōng

Before Chen Kaige's movie "Méi Lánfāng" was released, few people knew about her. Her name occasionally appeared in biographies about "Méi Lánfāng" or "Dù Yuèshēng." But now, through Chen Kaige's movie, people across China, and even around the world, have begun to notice this name shining with peculiar brilliance.

Her unparalleled singing voice, her touching love affairs with Méi Lánfāng and Dù Yuèshēng, her genuine personality, and her stunning beauty made her destined to capture the world's attention. She was the "Winter Emperor" of the opera world, a famous beauty, and she was the world-famous performer—Mèng Xiǎodōng!

The Queen of Social Circles in Old Shanghai: Táng Yīng

Sixty years ago, Shanghai’s Paramount Dance Hall was known as the "First Music House of the Far East." A graceful woman often danced here, enjoying herself and spending her youth. She was Táng Yīng. At that time, she was the most popular figure in social circles and was mentioned alongside Lù Xiǎomàn as "Southern Táng and Northern Lù." However, now Lù Xiǎomàn is well-known because of the poet Xú Zhìmó, while Táng Yīng's image has gradually faded into obscurity. One important reason for this result is that although Táng Yīng was beautiful, she did not have a highly sensational marriage history.

Lonely Splendor: Lù Xiǎomàn

If "Southern Táng and Northern Lù" refers to Táng Yīng being the red mole on men's hearts, then Lù Xiǎomàn is the bright moonlight before the bed. She is not fireworks, yet she is lonelier than fireworks by three degrees; she is not a rose, yet she is more beautiful and alluring than roses; she is a vast blue sea, clear and transparent yet deep and unfathomable. She could create waves in any era, and she could ignite all the hidden passion and desire in men.

Lù Xiǎomàn became known due to Xú Zhìmó, and Xú Zhìmó also burned his radiant light throughout his life for Lù Xiǎomàn's fragile youth, but at the same time, he overshadowed Lù Xiǎomàn's extraordinary talent.

A Remarkable Woman in Literature: Líng Shūhuá

Nowadays, not many people know the name and person of Líng Shūhuá, but she was one of the "Three Talented Women of the Literary World" in the 1920s, alongside Bīngxīn and Lín Huīyīn. Líng Shūhuá was rich in learning, excelling in both writing and painting. In writing, painting, and dealing with people, Líng Shūhuá was renowned for her calmness, gentleness, and elegance. She viewed the world with the unique breadth and warmth of a woman, and treated those around her with the same mindset.

Líng Shūhuá's talent and artistic practice have added significant chapters to the history of modern Chinese literature. Getting closer to this artist who was once overlooked by literary history, we can experience a thought-provoking historical flavor.

She is the April Sky: Lín Huīyīn

Lín Huīyīn wrote poetry for Xú Zhìmó, calling him the April sky on earth. In fact, in the hearts of the men of her time, she was their April sky. Her beauty, her talent, and her endearing little temperaments all made hearts flutter. Lín Huīyīn was a woman of remarkable talent but also ordinary temperament, and three outstanding and excellent men completed her beauty.

Now, everything about Lín Huīyīn has become an endless topic of conversation. People admire her beauty, intelligence, and talent, and lament even more that she possessed the most beautiful love in the world. But, can a person's life really be lived so perfectly?
