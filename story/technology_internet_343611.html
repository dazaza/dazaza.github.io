Exclusive Interview with the Chairman of the Global IPv6 Forum: China's Internet Hits the Ceiling

by ymaxi68k on 2010-04-11 20:07:53

April 8th noon news, Latif Ladid, Chairman of the Global IPv6 Forum, said in an exclusive interview with Sina Technology today that China's Internet is developing rapidly, but IP address resources, especially secure and usable IP address resources, can no longer meet the demand. "The booming Chinese Internet is encountering a ceiling."

Latif introduced that there are currently about 340 million remaining available IPv4 addresses globally, divided into 22 blocks. However, among these, 3 billion addresses belong to 'Dirty Addresses,' which should not be allocated but are still being used by some people, making them vulnerable to hackers and posing significant security risks. Based on this calculation, there are only 40 million secure 'Clean Addresses' available for use globally, while the global annual demand for IPv4 addresses is 200 million.

According to statistics, China currently owns 230 million IPv4 address resources, second only to the United States. "China's Internet is developing rapidly. In 2000, China had only 80 million IP addresses, and China's current annual new demand for IP addresses is around 40 million," Latif said, "but China has no Clean Address left for use."

It is reported that the Asia-Pacific Network Information Center (APNIC) recently allocated 16 million new IP addresses to China, known as 'Block 1.' All these addresses fall under 'Dirty Addresses,' which are "unsafe, and it is recommended that China does not use them. The high-speed development of China's Internet may come to an end." Latif said, "ISP service providers and government agencies also recognize this issue but deliberately ignore it and do not actively participate in IP address applications. Last year, at the allocation meeting held by APNIC in Kuala Lumpur, China did not attend."

"Looking forward, the development of the Internet of Things, smart grids, and cloud computing will require more and safer IP address resources. The construction of IPv6 networks is imminent," Latif suggested that the Chinese government should enforce mandatory IPv6 Ready certification for network equipment providers, as the US government has already done so.

Encouragingly, China Telecom has become the world's first ISP to pass the IPv6 Enabled ISP certification. Latif highly praised China Telecom's commercial planning for IPv6 networks, especially since this plan has received government support.

He introduced that currently, out of the global total of 220,000 ISPs, one-tenth are using IPv6 technology. It is estimated that China could apply for approximately 13 to 20 IPv6 address blocks.
