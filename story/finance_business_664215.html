Android 2.2 Usage Tips (Part Four)

1. Exchange Email Synchronization Feature

Many users have already found that the Gmail account pushmail feature provided by Android phones is very convenient, and email notifications can be faster than on computers. Starting with Android 2.2, Exchange email users can also enjoy this fast and convenient pushmail service. Simply input your account and password, and the Android system will automatically identify other mail settings. From there, you can use your phone to send and receive work emails. This brings Android one step closer to being the perfect business phone.

2. Enable Automatic Updates for Android Applications

Another milestone improvement in Android 2.2 is support for automatic application updates. By default, applications still require manual updates. However, you can now open the Android Market, select an installed application, and at the top of the "About" section, you'll see an "Allow Auto-Update" option. By checking this box, your phone will automatically connect to the network, download updates, and install new versions as soon as they appear in the Android Market. The "Update All" and "Auto Update" features in the Android 2.2 Market greatly simplify the management of your applications. Be mindful, however, of exceeding your data plan when using the auto-update feature.

3. Disable Application Update Notifications

If you're the type who never turns off your phone, even while sleeping, you've probably been woken up in the middle of the night because your phone notified you about an application update. To avoid such interruptions, simply open the Android Market, go to the "Downloaded" section, press the Menu key, select "Notifications," and check "Do Not Notify Me." This will permanently disable application update reminders.

4. System Prevents Updates for Suspicious Programs

Automatic application updates can be a double-edged sword. Imagine if a new version of an app engaged in malicious behavior, such as accessing private user data, while you had auto-updates enabled. Don't worry; Android 2.2 has considered this. If an application changes its original permission settings during an update, the system will block the automatic update. Therefore, unless you manually confirm the installation, a wallpaper app won't gain access to your location or web browsing history.

5. Chrome to Phone Feature

At the Google I/O 2010 conference, Google introduced the Android Cloud to Device Messaging (C2DM) service. Android 2.2 already supports last month's release of the Chrome to Phone service, allowing Froyo users to send URLs from their PC directly to their Android device to open. The Chrome to Phone service also supports sending Google Maps addresses and YouTube videos to your phone, making it one of the coolest features currently available for Android devices. Froyo users can now install the Chrome to Phone app on their phone along with a browser extension to experience one of Android's most advanced cloud computing features.
