In the 21st century, advertisements are everywhere... Looking at every corner of life, televisions, movies, books, supermarkets... they are all filled with those "exciting" advertisements. And among these advertisements, more than 90% of them are endorsed by celebrities. Without even considering the effect of the advertisement, just looking at the celebrity endorsing it gives a 100% conviction rate. Perhaps, many advertisers have this mindset when placing ads, and our consumers also view the advertisements with the same mindset. Unknowingly, a win-win goal is achieved. But who could have thought about how many dark secrets lie behind the scenes? Please read the article "The Inside Story of Hou Yaohua's Endorsement: I Was Hit By Money".

Recently, a mysterious person revealed the inside story of Hou Yaohua's endorsement for a prostate treatment advertisement, claiming that he would say whatever was asked as long as he was paid enough money. Although Hou Yaohua has apologized regarding this incident, the "shadow" left in people's minds cannot be easily removed. Not only did it harm his own image, but it also had an impact on consumers.

According to developers, they choose celebrities to endorse their advertisements mainly for three reasons: good recruitment, good promotion, and adding to the appearance. Some celebrities, for the sake of money, do not carefully select what they endorse. This gives unscrupulous developers a chance to exploit the situation. In the case of Hou Yaohua's endorsement, according to the mysterious person, he simply asked if the product was effective before proceeding with the recording. His concern was only about how much money he would receive.

Although the state has already issued relevant laws, such incidents continue to occur despite being prohibited. Here, we are not suggesting that celebrities should be banned from endorsing everything. We are merely appealing that if each celebrity would think twice and ask more questions before endorsing, then the interests of consumers would not be unjustly harmed, and incidents like the inside story of Hou Yaohua's endorsement would not happen again.
