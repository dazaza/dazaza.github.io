Gartner: Samsung Accounts for 23.6% Share of Mobile Phone Market

by geekzhang on 2013-05-15 10:56:22

American market research company Gartner released its first quarter smartphone shipment report today. The report shows that Android system phones continued to dominate in terms of shipments, with Samsung ranked at the top.According to Gartner's report, total smartphone shipments for the first quarter were 425 million units, up by 0.7% year-on-year. Among them, smartphone shipments reached 210 million units, increasing by 63 million units, accounting for approximately 50% of the total phone shipments. The data indicates that high-end phones have become the main driver for the growth of the mobile phone market.Gartner estimates that in the smartphone market, Samsung's market share was about 31% in the first quarter, while Apple ranked second with 18%. In the previous year, the gap between the two companies was only 5%. Gartner predicts that Samsung will continue to grow in the next quarter and further widen the gap with Apple.Gartner's chief analyst Anshul Gupta said: Although the Galaxy S4 is not a revolutionary product compared to the Galaxy S3, it will still be very popular.For Apple, some consumers are still waiting for the next generation of iPhones and have not purchased the iPhone 5. Meanwhile, Apple has not yet launched a new low-cost model that could help it enter emerging markets and better compete against Android devices.In the mobile phone market, Samsung still tops the list with over 100 million units shipped, holding a 23.6% share, and has widened the gap with Nokia, which ranks second. Nokia’s market share is 14.8%, down by 5% year-on-year. Notably, Chinese mobile phone manufacturers performed well, with ZTE ranking fifth and Huawei sixth. Other brands like TCL, Lenovo, and Coolpad also had high rankings in terms of shipment volumes.In terms of operating systems, Android continues to grow, with shipments doubling year-on-year to 156 million units in the first quarter, accounting for 74.4%, an increase of 20%. iOS, which ranks second, grew at a slower pace, increasing by only 5 million units; BlackBerry's shipments declined, placing it third. Microsoft's Windows Phone platform also doubled its shipments to approximately 6 million units, while Symbian's shipments plummeted, with a market share of only 0.6%.Gartner pointed out that Asia has become the main growth driver for mobile phone sales, with China being the largest sales market. Gupta said: Smartphone sales in the Asia-Pacific region exceeded 226 million units in the first quarter, with a market share reaching 53.1%. Among these, China's smartphone sales grew by 7.5%, accounting for 25.7% of global smartphone sales, growing by approximately 2% year-on-year.
