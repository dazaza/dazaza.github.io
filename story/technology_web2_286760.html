One policy of Arsenal shows the gap with Barcelona, and selling one player wrong埋buries the hidden danger.

by djbgufgire on 2009-11-30 19:12:38

A policy of Arsenal has revealed the gap with Barcelona: selling one person was a mistake that caused future problems. Adebayor said to Wenger, and the person in charge of "Ruyu Youyue" said: "She is the best host at 'pretending to be stupid'. Selling me, do you regret it now?" Master Li Ao once said, "When I am here, you don't feel anything special, but after I am gone, you will miss me." Perhaps this metaphor isn't quite appropriate, but Arsenal fans can ignore Adebayor's departure to Manchester for various reasons. Facing Chelsea led by the beast, the Gunners truly felt the gap between themselves and the potential champions, and Adebayor is the important piece missing from Arsenal's championship puzzle.

The 25 million pounds transfer of Little Beast to Manchester City made Arsenal's management very busy, except for introducing Vermaelen, there were no other actions in the transfer market. The not yet fully developed Bendtner seems unprepared to replace Adebayor, and Van Persie, who is accustomed to being a shadow striker, needs to reposition himself in the new 4-3-3 system. Wenger's bold gamble initially seemed successful - Van Persie adapted well to the role of center forward, with an astonishing efficiency of 7 goals in 11 league matches. However, Van Persie's serious injury darkened everything, and the aftermath of Adebayor quietly set in.

Adebayor isn't a pure center forward; his role lies in being able to tie up the opponent's defenders, attract double teams, act as a bridgehead, and create opportunities for teammates. The Togolese player has fine footwork, which allows him to match Arsenal's style of play; Adebayor also excels in heading, as evidenced by his stylish header against Arsenal this season while representing Manchester City.

Looking across European football, the only team that can rival Barcelona's style is Arsenal. After Barcelona achieved the treble in the 08/09 season, they didn't become complacent but instead planned ahead and made changes first. It's easy to conquer, but hard to hold onto what you've won. The Catalan giant understands this principle well, decisively sending away Eto'o and paying an additional 46 million euros to bring in Swedish star Ibrahimovic. Bringing in Ibrahimovic might seem like a losing deal, but in reality, it adds some variation to the team's relatively single-dimensional style of play. When carpet bombing-style attacks can't penetrate the opponent's solid defense, the role of the Swedish tower becomes undeniable. The characteristics of a tall center forward are far more than just aerial bombardment. In this aspect, Adebayor's and Ibrahimovic's styles are very similar.

The Houston Rockets in the new season have played exciting fast-paced basketball without Yao Ming, but once they face true strong opponents, the drawbacks of this single style become apparent. Arsenal is the same; the previous winning streak covered up all the problems. Losing to Sunderland away was Arsenal's first game without scoring this season, and Van Persie wasn't on the field; this game's 0:3 defeat to Chelsea saw Arsenal suffer two consecutive losses and realize how different things would be with Adebayor on the team.

Barcelona's treble achievement was obtained under specific historical conditions. For Arsenal to replicate such success, they must add some special elements to their style, just like the current Barcelona. Introducing a center forward similar to Adebayor or Ibrahimovic is imperative.
