29-Year-Old Dies in Test Vehicle After Scoring Full Marks in Driving Exam

by murxkh179 on 2009-12-08 21:06:34

Related thematic articles: "No pulse! No heartbeat, no breathing." At around 9:20 am yesterday, near the starting point of the subject two examination site of the Fuzhou Municipal Traffic Police Detachment's Driver Management Office, a team leader from a certain driving school in Fuzhou almost fainted after feeling trainee Lin Bin's pulse, carotid artery and checking his breathing by reaching into the examination vehicle.

Just a few minutes earlier, Lin Bin had just completed the last project of the subject two exam, seeing that his score was a perfect 100 points, he was preparing to drive the car back to the test site starting point about 100 meters away, but the car lost control, crashed into a guardrail, and only stopped after stalling.

At around 11:00 am, when the reporter arrived at the scene, the police had already cordoned off the driving test site. All trainees taking the driving test were urgently asked to leave the test site. The candidates were arranged by the police to rest and wait for re-examination notice inside the driver management office building.

According to a Mr. Lin, a candidate from Gulou district, at around 9:00 am he was still waiting in the driving test hall when suddenly a security guard came over and announced an emergency event had occurred, and the test site needed to be "cleared" temporarily. The candidates began to buzz with chatter, some said a candidate mistook the accelerator for the brake, hit the nearby guardrail, got into a car accident and died, others claimed it was because the excitement of passing the subject two exam with a perfect score caused heart attack.

What everyone could confirm is that after this unexpected incident occurred, someone immediately called 120, but the specific rescue situation on-site and the handling of the event could not be known as the Director of the Fuzhou Driver Management Office, Zhang Mian, refused to give an interview. The security at the gate of the driver management office stated that one needed the director or other leaders to come down to escort them into the building.

By around 12:10 pm, the candidates were notified they were allowed to enter the site again to continue their exams.

Director Zhang Mian of the driver management office stated that the case has been taken over by the Chengmen Police Station under the Cangshan Public Security Bureau, all information needs to be obtained from the police station.

The candidate failed once during the reverse parking test and then passed after retaking it, scoring full marks in the subsequent tests.

According to informed sources, Lin Bin was from Tingjiang Town, Mawei District, Fuzhou City, had just returned from abroad, and his child was only a few days old. During the morning's exam, Lin Bin made a mistake in the reversing route during the initial attempt, failing the first time, but passed after retaking it; "In the following single-bridge crossing and hill start projects, he must pass all of them, otherwise, he would have to take the exam again next time," said the team leader who led Lin Bin to the test. In the subsequent exams, Lin Bin did not make any mistakes, achieving a perfect score, which made him very excited. However, Lin Bin later died in the car, making it hard for the team leader to accept.

At around 1:00 pm, Lin Bin's father went to Chengmen Police Station to record a statement. Lin Bin's body was sent to the morgue at the Pulmonary Hospital, and currently, the autopsy report has not yet come out, so the exact cause of Lin Bin's death remains unknown. His family said they had not found any signs of heart disease in Lin Bin.

Medical experts: This young man might have suffered sudden death, possibly due to excessive excitement causing cerebral hemorrhage or myocardial infarction, but fatigue cannot be ruled out either.

Zhang Min, director of the rehabilitation department at the second inpatient department of the General Hospital of Fujian Province, who has rich experience in cerebrovascular diseases and hypertension, said that Lin Bin's death within a short period of time requires an autopsy to determine the exact cause, but from some phenomena, it can be preliminarily judged as sudden death. There are many reasons for sudden death, such as those with heart disease, who may suffer from myocardial infarction leading to shock, coma, and even death; hypertensive patients may also experience cerebral hemorrhage due to excessive excitement, with accumulated blood inside the cranium pressing on brain tissue causing sudden death. About 80% of sudden deaths are directly related to the heart. Some sudden deaths are associated with cerebrovascular diseases, such as sudden rupture of arteries, etc., while sudden deaths caused by other organs are relatively fewer.

"Of course, there are also some young people who die suddenly, which is related to their living habits, such as heavy smoking, working very hard, often staying up late, irregular schedules, great mental pressure and work stress, etc." Director Zhang Min indicated that even for these people with bad living habits, most of them will eventually be found to have problems with their hearts or cardiovascular system if they experience sudden death.

Question: If Lin Bin had heart disease, how did he pass the physical examination?

"He might have died from a sudden heart attack due to being too excited." Many candidates who witnessed the event drew this conclusion, but since the relevant authorities have not yet released the autopsy results for Lin Bin, this conclusion lacks scientific basis.

But if Lin Bin indeed had heart disease, could he participate in the driver's license exam? Outside the driver management office examination site, the candidates interviewed by the reporter said that filling out a physical examination form is required when applying for a driver's license, but this is basically just a formality, as they didn't undergo physical examinations before coming to take the test.

According to Article 12 of the revised "Regulations on the Application and Use of Motor Vehicle Driving Licenses" implemented from April 1, 2007, those with organic heart disease, epilepsy, mental illness, dementia, and neurological diseases affecting limb movement that hinder safe driving are not allowed to apply for a motor vehicle driver's license.

"We don't know if Lin Bin had heart disease. The physical examination form is issued uniformly by the driver management office, and the physical examination is conducted by the students themselves. As long as the form has the seal of a district-level hospital and the doctor's signature, the driver management office will review and approve it," said the person in charge of Lin Bin's driving school. Physical examinations mainly check the student's vision, five senses, and limbs, along with blood pressure checks, but do not include internal organ checks. Moreover, in their driving school, physical examinations are also conducted by the students themselves finding hospitals for inspection. As long as they can get the seal of a district-level hospital and the doctor's signature, the driver management office will basically approve the application.

Additionally, according to the person in charge of this driving school, more than 90% of those who pass the subject two exam can pass the subject three exam and successfully obtain a driver's license.

This means that Lin Bin, aged 28, had more than a 90% chance of obtaining a driver's license and appearing on roads filled with vehicles. It might be that the perfect score of 100 made him too excited, and his last journey ended when the out-of-control car slowly collided with the guardrail and stopped.
