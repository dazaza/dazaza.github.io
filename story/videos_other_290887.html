The upward movement of the stock market encountered pressure, with securities firms and steel sectors taking turns to support the market＿Stock Knowledge

by dx0966632 on 2009-12-03 22:54:32

The Shanghai Composite Index opened lower this morning at 3,268.00 points. It then underwent several rounds of bottom testing due to the drag from PetroChina and Sinopec as well as the banking sector, hitting an intra-day low of 3,243.75 points in the morning session. Subsequently, the steel and brokerage sectors jointly bolstered the market, causing the index to continuously rise amidst fluctuations and touching an intra-day high of 3,270.37 points. However, under the pressure of yesterday's closing price, the index again fell back amidst fluctuations. In terms of support and resistance levels, the index found potential support at the 10-day and 20-day moving averages, and potential resistance at yesterday's closing price. From the perspective of gaps on the chart, there were still two short-term gaps at 2,635.31 points and 2,803.86 points that needed to be filled below the market, and the lowest gap at 1,762 points below the index remained unfilled, suggesting a long-term technical need for the Shanghai Composite to adjust downwards. The next long-term target above the index should be the gap left by the sharp low opening on January 22 last year, ranging from 4,818.00 points to 4,891.28 points. Therefore, the outcome of the confrontation between bulls and bears will determine the directional choice of the index. As of the noon break today, the Shanghai Composite was reported at 3,255.61 points, down 14.14 points or -0.43% from the previous trading day's closing price, with a turnover of 97.529 billion yuan.

Similarly, the Shenzhen Component Index also opened lower this morning at 13,789.08 points. The subsequent weakness in the real estate, brewing, and electrical appliance sectors dragged the Shenzhen Composite into a narrow decline, soon touching an intra-day low of 13,675.07 points in the morning session. After continuous oscillation and shakeout, the steel and brokerage sectors significantly strengthened, driving the Shenzhen Composite to turn red in the intra-day session and touch an intra-day high of 13,822.91 points. From the perspective of support and resistance levels, the area around yesterday's closing price provided significant pressure for the Shenzhen Composite this morning, while the 10-day moving average offered potential support for the index. From the perspective of gaps on the chart, the Shenzhen Composite previously had gaps at 11,874.90 points and 11,300.03 points that needed to be filled below the index, and the earlier gap at 7,266.41 points still suggested the necessity of a long-term adjustment for the index. As of the noon break today, the Shenzhen Component Index was reported at 13,734.68 points, down 65.82 points or -0.48% from the previous trading day's closing price, with a turnover of 66.191 billion yuan.

In terms of sectors, the initial determination of Chinese shareholders for the Shanghai Disneyland has been made, with Lujiazui holding 60% of the shares. Lujiazui opened with a limit-up, and the strong opening of Lujiazui stimulated the Disney concept stocks to perform relatively strongly after the opening bell, with individual stocks such as Jielong Industry, S.M.I.C., and Pudong Golden Bridge currently leading in gains. Song Liping, General Manager of the Shenzhen Stock Exchange, stated that margin trading and short selling have been prepared, and funds will become both financing targets for margin trading and financial targets for hedging risks associated with stock index futures. Influenced by this news, the stock index futures concept has returned with a vengeance, with individual stocks showing a surge. Stimulated by rumors of margin trading, the brokerage sector also performed strongly overall in the early session. On November 1st, the Ministry of Finance issued a notice stating that after the new corn crop in Northeast China is available in 2009, the central government will provide a one-time fixed-amount subsidy to southern feed-consuming provinces that purchase the new corn crop from Northeast China and transport it to their local areas. This message stimulated agricultural stocks to become the biggest hotspot in the current intra-day market. Additionally, the transportation and logistics sector indices also showed good growth. With the global economy warming up, the recent increase in demand for raw materials has been relatively noticeable, and the recent coal demand peak season has also led to some small price increases in freight enterprises. However, most stocks in the Growth Enterprise Market (GEM) fell, but Huayi Brothers and Dayu Water Conservation, which closed with limit-ups yesterday, rose more than 4%. In summary, the computer application services, trust, GEM, agriculture, telecommunications equipment, and securities sectors are currently leading in gains, while the banking, aerospace, insurance, nonferrous metals, and real estate sectors are performing relatively poorly.

From the news front, we have: 1. 68 central state-owned enterprises involved in financial derivatives business incurred floating losses of 11.4 billion yuan; 2. High-level officials reiterated curbing speculation in the property market; 3. The State Council will strengthen the incentive-constraint mechanism for eliminating outdated production capacity; 4. The Chinese company for the Shanghai Disneyland has been established, with Lujiazui holding 60% of the shares; 5. WTO ministerial meetings failed to make significant progress on the Doha Round; 6. Excess inventory caused New York oil prices to fall by 2.3%; 7. New York gold prices closed above $1,200, setting a new high; 8. Financial and energy stocks declined, resulting in mixed U.S. stock market closes: Dow Jones fell 18 points, Nasdaq rose 9 points.

As of the close today, across the Shanghai and Shenzhen markets, a total of 711 stocks rose, with 16 stocks reaching the daily limit-up, including 6 S, ST, and *ST category stocks. The three newly listed stocks, Santai Electronics, Rihai Communications, and Yaze Shares, all saw opening gains exceeding 80%, and as of the noon close, the gains were: Santai Electronics up 109.16%, Rihai Communications up 99.96%, and Yaze Shares up 78.12%. A total of 850 stocks fell across the two markets, with the two stocks in the SEG group - SEG Samsung and Shen SEG - hitting the daily limit-down. Among the top 50 weighted stocks in the Shanghai market today, except for SAIC Motor which was suspended from trading, 11 blue-chip stocks rose, 34 weighted stocks fell, and 4 large-cap stocks remained unchanged. It can be seen that the short side clearly held the advantage among the weighted stocks in the morning session, and taking advantage of the low point to position for a rebound, adjusting positions and forward-deploying could allow one to enjoy the rally in individual stocks.

The process of buying and selling stocks is as follows: when buying or selling stocks, you must specify whether you are buying or selling, the name (or code) of the stock, the quantity, and the price. Moreover, this order is only valid for the day it is placed. The content of the order includes the abbreviation (code) of the stock you wish to buy or sell, the quantity, and the price at which you intend to buy or sell the stock. The abbreviation of the stock usually consists of three to four Chinese characters, and the code is six digits. When placing an order, the code and abbreviation of the stock must match. At the same time, there are certain regulations regarding the quantity of stocks being traded: the quantity of stocks being bought must be a whole multiple of 100, but the quantity of stocks being sold does not have to be a whole multiple of 100.

There are four methods of placing orders: counter submission orders, telephone automatic orders, computer automatic orders, and remote terminal orders.
