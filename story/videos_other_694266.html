A 64-year-old elder spends 100,000 yuan of his own money every year to run a Lei Feng information center

by hdj4vjfbd3 on 2012-03-07 00:38:36

He Zhaohai is carefully reading the Lei Feng newspaper. A 64-year-old man, He Zhaohai has been self-funding his museum for 35 years to promote the spirit of Lei Feng ――

Lei Feng's teachings according to Uncle He:

● If everyone strives to be a good person, there will be no shortage of good people in the world; if everyone does good deeds, there will be no insurmountable difficulties.

● The spirit of Lei Feng can be simply summarized in 16 words: kindness to others, respect for the elderly and care for the young, dedication to one's job, and contribution to society.

Today is March 5th, and many citizens want to visit the Lei Feng Archive Museum. However, due to financial strain, the museum closed its doors more than a year ago and it is difficult for it to reopen to the public. "Before March 10th, we will try to exhibit some of the archived materials inside the part of the museum that hasn't been demolished!" The 64-year-old curator, He Zhaohai, stated to the press. For 35 years of his life, he has run the museum and published newspapers, devoting his entire life to just one thing ―― promoting the spirit of Lei Feng.

The cost of running the archive museum is 100,000 yuan per year.

In a simple room less than 8 square meters in size on the west side of Laotan Teahouse at Qianmen, I met He Zhaohai, whose temples were already streaked with gray. Despite his age, his memory was particularly sharp, "By August 15th this year, it will have been 50 years since Lei Feng's passing." Mr. He said that as March 5th approaches, he has recently received over 20 calls from organizations and individuals wishing to visit, but the exhibition hall was dismantled two years ago for renovation and has not yet resumed operations. "Before March 10th, we will try to exhibit some of the archived materials inside the part of the museum that hasn't been demolished."

The Lei Feng Archive Museum was originally located in a narrow alley on the east side of Nanxiaojie Street in Donggao, Fengtai District. This seemingly unremarkable courtyard was the only Lei Feng Memorial Hall in the Beijing area, purchased by He Zhaohai himself with funds raised from selling land for 100,000 yuan, and has been open to the public for free for 35 years. Inside are displayed various materials he painstakingly collected: the oil lamp Lei Feng used when alive, the Mao Zedong Selected Works he read, the wooden gun and passbook used during training, photographs, etc. Many people came especially to visit, with a peak day once receiving over 4,000 visitors.

"Maintaining the normal operation of the museum costs at least 100,000 yuan per year," said Mr. He. The museum receives no funding or subsidies, and for 35 years, it has relied solely on personal and staff contributions, "Some employees work outside to earn money to subsidize the museum's needs." He mentioned that he is currently exploring ways to build a new museum. The government had previously offered assistance, but he politely declined, "I want to use the spirit of Lei Feng to run the Lei Feng Museum."

Publishing the Lei Feng Newspaper is entirely free.

Running the museum is a major event in He Zhaohai's life. To specifically celebrate the spirit of Lei Feng, he also planned and established the Lei Feng Newspaper. In front of his desk, Mr. He carefully unfolded a newspaper, "It was first published in March 1983, and now it has been 29 years, with a total of 496 issues." His speech revealed a hint of pride.

I noticed that the China Lei Feng Newspaper is a bi-weekly publication with four pages, rich in content. The title "Voice for the People, Free Gift" is prominently visible. "The average print run of the newspaper can reach around 20,000 copies." Mr. He calculated for me, the printing cost is 50 cents per copy, envelopes cost 20 cents each, and stamps cost 80 cents each, so sending out each Lei Feng newspaper costs 1.5 yuan. "Since it is given away for free and lacks a fixed source of funds, the development of the Lei Feng newspaper has been very difficult, fortunately, there has been help from enthusiastic individuals in all sectors of society." He said that about 70 organizations have provided financial support to the newspaper, mainly to cover printing costs. "Recently, we received another sponsorship, and the Lei Feng newspaper will soon be changed to a weekly publication." At this point, Mr. He's joy was evident.

According to teacher Su from the office, in order to properly run the Lei Feng newspaper, Mr. He became a regular visitor to nearby libraries and bookstores. Whenever he had time, he would extract articles from books and newspapers, along with submissions, then arrange them himself. After the newspaper was printed, Mr. He sent copies to various government agencies, schools, and sponsoring organizations, sometimes distributing them at bookstores or park entrances.

His perseverance is entirely due to the spirit of Lei Feng.

During our conversation, I learned that Mr. He was born in Quanjiao County, Anhui Province, but he preferred to call himself a native of Shaxian County, Fujian Province, "I was adopted by the Shaxian County People's Government as a child, they gave me an urban household registration and sent me to school." With gratitude in his heart, the young him was determined to continue promoting the spirit of Lei Feng.

Before the renovation of the exhibition hall, Mr. He lived in the gallery itself. Due to inability to pay the installation fee, the exhibition room had no electricity; in winter, there was no heating, and the water pipes froze, leaving no water. "I dug a hole in the ground, then piled bricks to make a stove, and cooked my own meals." Mr. He said that although living in poverty did not make him feel bitter, he saved where he could, spending money on what truly mattered. Running the museum and publishing the newspaper faced severe financial shortages, and the monthly salary for the staff was only around 200 yuan, leading many to leave the newspaper over the years. The team of 22 people now only has 12 remaining.

"The hardest times, we all ate steamed buns together." Mr. He said that his daily life was spent in this office, and many friends, upon learning of his situation, often brought him steamed buns, oatmeal, baked flatbreads, etc., which he paired with hot water for meals. He pointed to the blue-gray shirt he was wearing and said, "This piece of clothing was also given to me by someone else, I truly eat from a hundred families and wear clothes from a hundred families, ha ha..." Mr. He's cheerful laughter echoed through the narrow office.
