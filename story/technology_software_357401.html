Cathodic electrophoretic coating has many advantages.

by ddhpeakp83 on 2010-06-02 13:10:37

If the coating film is uniform and has strong adhesion, it can simplify or cancel the conventional electroplating process, reduce production costs, shorten working time, improve productivity, and enable automation of mass production; good swimming penetration, good corrosion resistance of the coating film; bright coating color, by using different pigments and ratios, various colors of coatings can be obtained; good appearance of the coating film, with better leveling and extensibility during drying; adopting closed-loop ultrafiltration device and ultrafiltration liquid purifier, the application rate of coatings can reach more than 98%; especially, there are no toxic and heavy metal ions in the paint solution, and the discharged water almost does not need treatment, which is beneficial to environmental protection and is one of the latest low-pollution coating methods.

Given the above advantages, cathodic electrophoretic coating has developed rapidly in the field of metal surface treatment at home and abroad. Domestic small and medium-sized manual cathodic electrophoretic paint production lines have been constantly put into operation. However, some manufacturers cannot form continuous, large-scale, and stable production due to lack of technical management experience. The author has summarized his/her work experience over nearly six years for readers' reference.

2 Equipment

2.1 Hanging tools

Currently, the products processed by manual cathodic electrophoretic paint production lines are mainly fishing gear, bicycles, motorcycles, and various daily small hardware accessories, etc. The product characteristics are: a large number of workpieces and small surface area. Practice shows that the design and manufacture of hanging tools are crucial in the electrophoretic coating process, directly affecting production efficiency. When designing, making, and using electrophoretic coating hanging tools, the following points should be noted.

2.1.1 Electrical contact between hanging tools and workpieces

After the hanging tool undergoes electrophoretic painting treatment, its hook becomes insulated due to being covered with electrophoretic paint. Cathodic electrophoresis uses high voltage and low current. Practice shows that as long as there is a "needle tip" size contact point between the workpiece and the hook, it can meet the electrical conductivity requirement. Currently, some factories directly hang the workpieces on the hanging tools. During production, the workpieces swing back and forth easily causing collisions, and after each electrophoresis, the paint needs to be removed from the hanging tool, increasing cost and reducing production efficiency. Research determined that the hook part should be made of spring steel wire, and the shape of the hook should be flexibly chosen according to the type of product. Generally, a three-point or four-point contact form is adopted. The tip of the hook should ideally maintain a 45° angle when cut. When hanging the piece, turn the workpiece back and forth so that the electrophoretic paint layer on the tip of the hook is broken, avoiding the need for paint removal from the hanging tool, ensuring sufficient electrical conductivity of the workpiece, reducing cost, and improving production efficiency.

2.l.2 Fixing the hook

Currently, some factories use copper welding or tin welding methods to assemble hanging tools, which have the following defects: ① The melting point of copper is 1083℃. When copper welding is used, the spring hook anneals and loses elasticity, failing to meet production requirements; ② The drying temperature of cathodic electrophoretic paint is generally above 160℃. Tin heated to 160℃ turns brittle and breaks easily. For hanging tools assembled with tin welding, the hooks will fall off after a period of work.

Our factory adopts mechanical assembly to assemble hanging tools, which have been continuously used for more than three years without any damage, reducing production costs and improving production efficiency.

2.l.3 Cleaning of hanging tools

For complex-shaped workpieces and those with blind holes, in order to thoroughly clean and recover the solution inside the blind holes, both ends of the hanging tool are equipped with handles for cleaning twice in reverse order. This not only thoroughly cleans the workpieces but also removes floating paint adhered to the surface of the paint film, preventing the phenomenon of blotchy paint film.

2.2 Ultrafiltration Device

2.2.l Principle and Composition

Ultrafiltration is a pressure-driven membrane separation process using specific porous membranes. Under applied pressure, organic solvents, water, inorganic ions, and relatively low molecular weight solutes pass through the ultrafiltration membrane, while suspended pigments and high molecular resins in the bath cannot pass through the ultrafiltration membrane and flow entirely back into the electrophoresis tank.

An ultrafiltration device generally consists of a pre-filter, an ultrafilter, a magnetic drive pump, and a cleaning storage tank.

2.2.2 Function

The ultrafiltration device is one of the main devices in electrophoretic coating. Its performance and usage directly affect the quality of electrophoretic coating and production costs. Its functions are as follows:

① Stabilize the bath and improve the quality of the paint film.

The existence of impurity ions severely affects the electro-deposition characteristics, stability, and quality of the paint bath. However, during continuous production, impurity ions inevitably enter the paint bath. Therefore, their content must be strictly controlled within a certain range. Impurity ions, organic solvents, and small molecule resins in the electrophoresis bath can be controlled through the discharge of ultrafiltration liquid, ensuring stable and continuous production.

② Recycle electrophoretic paint and save a large amount of rinse water.

Using an ultrafiltration device, the "closed-loop" water washing method after electrophoresis can be realized. Using ultrafiltration liquid to thoroughly wash away the electrophoretic paint adhering to the coated object, combined with the use of ultrafiltration liquid purifiers, the recovery and reuse rate of paint can reach more than 98%, saving electrophoretic paint, reducing sewage treatment volume and costs, benefiting environmental protection. Without using an ultrafiltration device, the utilization efficiency of electrophoretic paint is only 70%～80%.

2.2.3 Precautions for the design, manufacture, and use of ultrafiltration devices

① Design separate ultrafiltration systems, circulation systems, and backflushing systems, and ensure all three systems can operate independently, allowing for continuous large-scale production.

② Pipeline manufacturing must be sealed tightly, completely eliminating any leakage issues.

③ Strictly follow the operating instructions to avoid excessive pressure causing the ultrafiltration membrane to burst and become ineffective, or damaging the ultrafiltration membrane due to too high concentration of backflushing liquid during backflushing. Timely perform system sterilization treatment when not in use.

2.2.4 Current Usage Status

Currently, some small and medium-sized factories consider equipment investment costs and omit ultrafiltration devices in production. At the beginning, they can still maintain production, but as impurity ions accumulate in the solution, the stability of the electrophoretic paint bath is destroyed, causing various paint film defects, rendering the paint bath unusable and leading to scrapping, affecting production and causing significant waste.

I once visited several factories and found they all had the aforementioned problems. Based on the actual situation of each factory, we made rectifications to the cathodic electrophoretic paint production line, with noticeable results, improving the quality of electrophoretic painting, reducing production costs, recovering the cost of a set of ultrafiltration equipment in less than half a year, and receiving consistent recognition and praise from the factories.

2.3 Drying Equipment

Drying equipment is one of the main devices in the electrophoretic coating process. Its quality directly affects the appearance and various properties of the coating. Fuel or gas drying equipment has lower energy consumption, but the equipment investment cost is high, so few small and medium-sized factories adopt it, mostly using electric heating drying equipment. When designing, manufacturing, and using electric heating drying equipment, the following four issues should be noted:

① To make the temperature inside the drying chamber uniform and ensure the quality of the coating, it is best to use a convection-type drying device to improve the effect of heat conduction.

② Due to the easy generation of hydrogen chloride, sulfur dioxide, carbon dioxide, trichloroethylene, water vapor, and co-solvents as exhaust gases during the baking process of the paint film, these gases act as catalysts when they momentarily contact the surface of the paint film, promoting the hardening of the paint film's surface. Subsequently, the interior of the paint film appears compressed, resulting in pinholes or poor appearance defects. These exhaust gases may also condense above the inlet and outlet of the drying chamber, dripping onto the coated object and causing paint film defects. Therefore, when designing, a waste gas emission pipe, butterfly valve, and stop valve should be installed at the top of the drying equipment, or it should be designed as a self-extraction chimney to always discharge the exhaust gases generated during the drying process outside the drying chamber.

③ To reduce heat loss when entering and exiting workpieces, the drying equipment can be designed with two-sided opening doors. Tracks should be laid inside and outside the furnace, equipped with two alternating drying carts. The bottom of the cart should be laid with perforated steel plates to prevent workpieces from falling into the drying chamber and becoming difficult to retrieve. Heating elements should use electric heating tubes rather than resistors. Electric heating tubes should be laid at the bottom and side walls of the oven to make the temperature inside the furnace uniform.

④ To prevent rust on the inner wall of the oven and dust from outside entering the oven to contaminate the paint film, it is recommended to use stainless steel plates for the inner wall of the oven. To ensure the drying quality of the paint film, when measuring temperature with a thermocouple, the thermocouple should be placed at the position of the workpiece. If necessary, directly measure with a thermometer.

2.4 Electrophoretic Coating Power Supply

Electrophoretic coating power supply is one of the important devices in the electrophoretic coating process. Its characteristic is high voltage and low current. Whether the power supply is appropriate directly determines the performance of the coating and the continuity of production.

Currently, most small and medium-sized cathodic electrophoretic paint manufacturers use manual silicon rectifiers as the power supply for electrophoretic coating, which has the following drawbacks:

① Manual voltage regulation, without a soft start device, due to the large impact current when the workpiece is electrified, rough paint film appearance, orange peel defects are likely to occur.

② Low stability accuracy, large ripple coefficient, large waveform fluctuation, defects such as patterns and unevenness are likely to appear on the paint film.

③ Cannot automatically complete various preset electrophoretic process curves, unstable quality.

④ Incomplete protective function, prone to damage fuses, diodes, and carbon brushes, unable to achieve continuous mass production.

In response to the above shortcomings, our factory selected the KGY series thyristor air-cooled rectification equipment, which has been in continuous production for more than two years without any component damage, improving production efficiency and reducing production costs. This equipment has the following features:

① Has a soft start function that can be arbitrarily set from 0~180 seconds, completely eliminating the rough appearance defects caused by large impact currents.

② Uses microcomputer digital trigger control technology to achieve constant voltage and constant current automatic control of the load without conversion, with a stabilization accuracy of ±3%, ripple coefficient <5%, low probability of pore formation in the paint film, good quality.

③ Uses a dedicated thyristor multifunctional controller that can automatically complete various preset electrophoretic process curves, stable quality, capable of meeting continuous large production requirements.

④ This equipment has comprehensive protection functions, including short circuit, phase loss, overheating, overcurrent, overvoltage, and overload protection functions, and saves electricity, low cost.

2.5 Circulation Device

2.5.l Composition

To ensure obtaining excellent electrophoretic coating appearance quality and maintaining the stability of the bath, the electrophoretic bath should be continuously circulated and stirred after initial preparation. The circulation device is composed of a closed loop consisting of circulation stirring, filtration, and heat exchange devices.

2.5.2 Function

① Keeps the components of the bath uniformly mixed, preventing pigment sedimentation at the bottom of the tank, thereby reducing the solid content of the bath.

② Prevents pigments from settling on the workpiece, affecting its surface quality.

③ Removes impurity particles and precipitated aggregated paint particles and oil contamination from the bath through the filter.

④ Maintains uniform and constant bath temperature.

⑤ Eliminates bubbles generated during the electrophoresis process, ensuring the paint film has a good appearance.

Electrophoretic coating equipment also includes auxiliary equipment such as testing equipment, electrophoresis tank, water washing tank, degreasing tank, and pure water machine, which will not be explained in detail here.

2.6 Process Protection

To ensure the normal progress of electrophoretic coating and obtain stable coating quality, strict process management is essential.

2.6.1 Testing the content of co-solvent

Co-solvents can improve the water solubility of the resin and the appearance of the paint film, simultaneously adjusting the viscosity of the paint solution and enhancing its stability. The content of co-solvent must be appropriate. Excessive content leads to overly thick paint films, reduced swimming penetration and breakdown voltage, severe re-dissolution phenomena; insufficient content causes poor stability of the bath and a shriveled paint film.

Due to the high price of detection equipment such as gas chromatographs, small and medium-sized factories generally do not have this equipment, so they usually do not test the content of co-solvent, relying solely on experience to add it, making it difficult to keep the content of co-solvent within a certain process range, leading to unstable product quality. After extensive experiments, I have summarized a method to determine the content of co-solvent, which has already been applied in production.

2.6.1.1 Sample

Three stainless steel plates of size 20mm x 60mm.

2.6.1.2 Preparation of the paint film

Take one sample plate, use modified epoxy cathodic electrophoretic paint, under the conditions of a bath temperature of 23°C, workpiece voltage of 150V, and time of 1.5 minutes for electrophoretic coating. Since the co-solvent is volatile, the sample should be tested immediately after electrophoretic cleaning.

2.6.1.3 Analysis Method

Use your right thumb and index finger to pinch the electrophoretic paint film slightly forcefully. When you feel sticky and leave fingerprints that cannot be wiped off, the co-solvent is within the process range; if there are fingerprints with stringy phenomena and a small amount of paint film sticks to your fingers, it indicates that the co-solvent content is too high; if there is no stickiness and no fingerprints after pinching, it means the solvent content is too low.

2.6.2 Wiping of Electrodes

Since small and medium-sized factories generally do not have anode membrane devices, reaction products of organic acids and other impurities continuously form in the anode area during cathodic electrophoresis, depositing on the anode plate and forming yellow harmful substances, affecting the electro-deposition performance and product quality. These plates should be promptly cleaned. Some factories directly use cloth dipped in sulfuric acid or formic acid to wipe the electrode plates. This method not only harms workers' health but also easily brings residual acid into the bath, affecting its stability. It is recommended to use cloth dipped in co-solvent to clean the electrode plates. This method not only easily cleans the yellow harmful substances on the surface of the electrode plates, reduces harm to workers, but also does not affect the stability of the bath.

2.6.3 Water Washing

Water washing mainly includes pre-treatment water washing and post-treatment water washing.

The quality of water washing at each stage of pre-treatment will have a serious impact on the entire pre-treatment and paint film quality. After degreasing and acid neutralization, thorough water washing is required to prevent acidic and alkaline residues from being carried into the electrophoretic bath and reacting to form particles attached to the surface of the paint film, affecting the appearance quality of the paint film. It is recommended to use ultrasonic cleaning. The last three water washes of pre-treatment must use deionized water to prevent impurity ions in tap water from entering the bath and affecting the electro-deposition characteristics, stability, and performance of the paint film. The conductivity of the last deionized water wash should be controlled at ≤20μs/cm, and the conductivity of the deionized water used in the electrophoretic bath should be controlled at ≤5μs/cm. Deionized water replacement should use the tank inversion method. The purpose of post-treatment water washing is to remove the floating paint adhering to the surface of the deposited paint film during the electrophoretic coating process to prevent defects such as "streaking", sticking paint, and re-dissolution of the electrophoretic paint film. To thoroughly wash off the floating paint on the surface of the paint film, two positive and negative washes can be used, allowing the paint to stay in the air for 20-30 seconds to burst automatically. Since the acidity, alkalinity, and hardness of water will directly affect the quality of the paint film, the last water wash after electrophoresis must use deionized water or add 0.5%-3.0% surfactant to tap water, which is beneficial for improving the appearance of the electro-deposited paint film, increasing smoothness, and overcoming "water marks". During the water washing process, mutual collisions are not allowed because the paint film is very soft and sticky at this time, making it easy to damage the surface.

2.6.4 Zinc Alloy Die-Casting Parts Electrophoretic Coating Technology

Zinc alloy die-casting allows complex-shaped parts to be formed in one step, featuring high production efficiency, low cost, and high machining precision. Zinc alloy die-casting parts are lightweight, have a certain strength and brightness, and are widely used in daily hardware, light industry, home appliances, machinery, electronics, automotive industries, and craft manufacturing. However, during processing, the plating qualification rate of zinc alloy die-casting parts is low, and plating costs are high, limiting its use. The author, based on production practice, directly performs electrophoretic coating treatment on zinc alloy die-casting parts after appropriate pre-treatment, significantly improving product qualification rates, reducing costs, and overcoming material inherent defects. This has been promoted and applied in production.

2.6.4.l Process Flow

Zinc alloy die-casting parts → Sand rolling → Water washing → Hanging → Tap water cleaning (2 steps) → Activation → Tap water washing (2 steps) → Pure water washing (3 steps) → Electrophoresis → Water washing → Drying

2.6.4.l.l Sand Rolling

Use sea sand or river sand for rolling to remove burrs, parting lines, flash, and oil stains on the surface of the blank. Use hexagonal cleaning rollers, controlling the speed at 20-60r/min. Load the workpieces first, then add the sea sand, flush with tap water, and add an appropriate amount of OP emulsion and trisodium phosphate. Control the rolling time at 30-50 minutes.

2.6.4.1.2 Activation

The purpose of activation is to remove the extremely thin oxide film formed on the surface of the parts, exposing the metallographic structure to enhance the bonding force between the substrate and the coating. After numerous experiments, the following formula was finally determined:

Hydrofluoric acid (AR grade) 3-5ml/L

Concentrated sulfuric acid (concentration 98%, CP grade) 8-10ml/L

t 5-20s

T Room temperature

It is best to prepare the activation solution with deionized water. The activation time is optimal when uniform small bubbles emerge on the surface of the workpiece and it slightly changes color. At this point, the workpiece should be immediately removed from the bath, cleaned thoroughly, and transferred to the next process.

2.6.4.2 Removal Process of Unqualified Zinc Alloy Die-Casting Parts Electrophoretic Paint Layer

Concentrated sulfuric acid (industrial grade) 100%

T Room temperature

Stirring Compressed air stirring (air should contain no water)

t 2-10min

Note: The surface of the workpiece should be dry and free of water before entering the bath.

Practice shows that when the bath conductivity is high, low voltage can be selected, along with reduced electrophoresis time and lower bath temperature to prevent coarse and thick electrophoretic paint films, producing pinholes, orange peels, and other undesirable phenomena. When the conductivity is low, high voltage can be selected, extending the electrophoresis time and increasing the bath temperature to enhance the thickness, dispersion ability, and brightness of the electrophoretic paint layer.

2.7 Conclusion

Although cathodic electrophoretic coating has only been applied in industrial production for more than 20 years, due to its own characteristics, it has not only been widely used in the anti-corrosion and decoration aspects of metal surface treatment but also plays an important role in functional electrophoretic coating and environmental protection. With China's accession to the WTO and the continuous efforts of electrophoretic coating workers, it will exhibit even more vigorous vitality.
