Aquarium shark attacked and has accidental C-section, 8 baby sharks survive

by anonymous on 2012-02-23 10:41:42

China Daily website Global Online News: According to a comprehensive report by foreign media, on November 11th, in Kelly Tarleton's Underwater World in New Zealand, a female shark was suddenly attacked fiercely by another shark. The belly of the female shark was bitten open with a large wound. Subsequently, the female shark smoothly delivered 8 adorable baby sharks from this wound. Visitors who were present at the time witnessed this rare and extraordinary scene with amazement.

The "Cesarean" female shark

A spokesperson for Kelly Tarleton's Underwater World introduced during an interview that when visitors saw the female shark being attacked, they all felt very surprised. However, what was even more unexpected was that after the female shark was bitten, there were suddenly 4 baby sharks around her. Therefore, they quickly called the staff of the underwater world.

The baby sharks were "delivered" from this wound

"The wound on the belly of the female shark was long and deep, and such a situation is indeed very rare. 4 baby sharks were delivered from that wound," said the spokesperson.

Since they were worried that the baby sharks might be eaten by stingrays or other sharks in the same tank, the staff of the underwater world rushed to take the baby sharks out of this tank and put them into another one.

When the staff prepared to scoop out the female shark to sew up her wound, they found there were actually another 4 baby sharks inside her belly. Thus, with the help of the staff, the female shark delivered another 4 baby sharks.

The lucky baby sharks

The spokesperson then said: "Now, the physical condition of the female shark is good, and we are closely monitoring her. After all, there is no precedent for such an event, so we are not quite sure if the female shark can recover later."

The shark keeper Davis of the underwater world said: "Whether in aquariums or in the sea, it is common for sharks to attack each other. However, I have never heard of a shark giving birth through this kind of cesarean section!"

Davis also said that if the female shark had not been suddenly attacked, those 8 baby sharks would likely have been born on the evening of the 11th. Since no one would be present at that time, the baby sharks might have been eaten by stingrays or other sharks. Generally speaking, sharks become completely "independent" from the moment they are born, and mother sharks do not protect their baby sharks.

Related Link: Sharks Can Reproduce Asexually

In May 2007, a joint research team from the United States and the United Kingdom discovered that female sharks can reproduce the next generation without mating with male sharks.

After analyzing the DNA of a dead baby shark, the researchers found that the baby shark was produced through asexual reproduction. It only had a mother, but no father. That baby shark was born in 2001 in a zoo in Omaha, Nebraska, USA. At that time, there were only three female sharks there, and they had not had any intimate contact with male sharks for 3 years prior. Unfortunately, the baby shark was soon killed by a stingray in the fish pool.

Before this, people generally believed that all sharks could only reproduce through sexual reproduction between males and females. The researchers initially also doubted their own discovery. However, after repeated tests, they failed to find any chromosomes of male sharks in the DNA of that baby shark.
