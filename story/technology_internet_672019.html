The out-of-touch question "whether willing to marry a rich second generation" was questioned by the candidates.

by jintaimqr on 2012-02-22 15:13:39

Interviews ask whether candidates would marry the rich second generation, strange questions in university enrollment are questioned. Since the independent enrollment examination of various universities has started, it has been constantly paid attention to by the media and the public. However, there have been frequent reports about weird interview questions, which has caused a stir among netizens.

Questions like "[Who is greater, Buddha or Emperor of Heaven?]" shocked the netizens, then it was reported that Guizhou University's independent enrollment interview asked candidates "[Would you marry the rich second generation?]". Such irrelevant interview questions have raised doubts among candidates and parents. Some experts have also joined the questioning ranks, worrying that too tricky questions may eliminate good students.

Let's take a look at the detailed report from Northeast Network:

[Would you marry the rich second generation?] Strange questions in independent recruitment interviews are questioned, experts remind--- be careful not to eliminate good students with [tricky] interviews.

[Who is greater, Emperor of Heaven or Buddha?][Would you marry the rich second generation?][If both your birth parents and step-parents attend the wedding, how should the seating arrangement be...?] Since the start of the independent recruitment interview season, all kinds of [unusual] interview questions from universities have caught people's eyes, drawing social attention and also raising doubts. Do university independent recruitment interviews really need unusual questions to select excellent students? Journalist Xu Yuqin

[Strange questions keep coming]

No thunderous question is more shocking than the next one

[Who is greater, Emperor of Heaven or Buddha?] This is a question from Fudan University's first [Wangdao Plan] independent recruitment interview. After this interview question was uploaded to Weibo, it immediately sparked heated debates among netizens. Some even initiated voting. The interviewer from Fudan University explained that the interview is not an exam, there is no standard answer itself.

On February 16, Guizhou University's independent recruitment interview asked candidates [Would you marry the rich second generation?], sparking heated discussions again. On February 19, Zhongnan University of Economics and Law's interview questions stunned all candidates: if both birth parents and step-parents attend the wedding banquet, how should the seating arrangement be? Such interview questions made all candidates exclaim dizziness, even parents outside with years of social experience couldn't help but shake their heads continuously.

Interviews are not better for being more [unusual]

The better the independent recruitment interview questions are [unusual]? Professor Chen Xue Ming, doctoral supervisor of Fudan University, expressed that he "really can't agree." [I am a teacher at Fudan, but I really can't agree with Fudan's independent recruitment method. Can this way really recruit excellent students? Is this way fair to high school students?]

[Root cause]

Lack of observation skills in interviewers

For the current phenomenon of some universities' independent recruitment interview questions being [characteristic] for the sake of [characteristics], Xiong Bingqi, deputy director of the 21st Century Education Research Institute and professor at Shanghai Jiaotong University, believes that universities have "deviated." [From these questions, it is completely impossible to see what they have to do with the school's talent cultivation characteristics.] said Xiong Bingqi.

Xiong Bingqi said that abilities do not need to be demonstrated through strange questions, but mainly through accumulation in daily study and life, and through dialogue to show. [Interviewers want to evaluate students through strange questions, but they fail to realize that the stranger the questions, the less favorable they are for students to showcase themselves.]

Xiong Bingqi believes that the increasing [unusual] nature of university independent recruitment interview questions reflects problems with interviewers, which is [lack of ability to observe students in interviewers]. He said that many interview experts at universities are professors, but although these professors are experts in their research fields, they may not necessarily be experts in independent recruitment interviews. [When professors interview their own doctoral students, they examine professional knowledge, but in independent recruitment interviews, they need to examine students' potential.] Xiong Bingqi said, [Independent recruitment interviews require experienced experts.]
