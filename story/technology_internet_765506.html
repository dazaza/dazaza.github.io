Sina's Q3 Financial Report in Charts: Operating Loss of $18.2 Million, Operating Margin -12%

by anonymous on 2013-08-13 15:02:32

Sina has released its financial report today. According to the report, Sina's net revenue in the second quarter was $157.5 million, representing a 20% increase compared to the same period last year; Sina reported a net loss of $11.5 million in the second quarter, compared to a net profit of $33.2 million during the same period last year.

Sina's operating loss for the second quarter of 2013 was $18.2 million, with an operating margin of -12%, compared to an operating profit of $200,000 during the same period last year.

Sina CEO Charles Chao stated: "Despite uncertainties in China's macroeconomic environment, we have been actively expanding our advertiser base beyond large brand clients and driving social and mobile advertising as well as value-added services on the basis of sustained growth in Weibo traffic. These strategic deployments enable Sina to achieve high-profit revenue growth while making significant investments for the future."

Sina Q2 revenue reached $157.5 million, up 20% year-on-year.

Sina's net revenue for the second quarter of 2013 was $157.5 million, an increase of 20% compared to $131.6 million during the same period last year. Non-GAAP net revenue was $152.8 million, compared to $126.9 million last year.

Sina Q2 advertising revenue was $120.6 million, up 17% year-on-year.

Sina's online advertising revenue for the second quarter of 2013 was $120.6 million, up 17% from $103.1 million during the same period last year. Sina's Weibo advertising revenue grew by 209% year-on-year to $30 million in the second quarter of 2013.

Sina Q2 non-advertising revenue was $32.2 million, up 13% year-on-year.

Sina's non-GAAP non-advertising revenue for the second quarter of 2013 was $32.2 million, up 13% from $23.8 million during the same period last year.

Sina's mobile value-added service revenue for the second quarter of 2013 was $19.9 million, up 13% year-on-year; Weibo value-added service revenue was $7.7 million, up 186% year-on-year, which includes revenue sharing from online games and Weibo membership fees.

Sina Q2 cost was $73.14 million, up 19% year-on-year.

Sina's cost for the second quarter of 2013 was $73.14 million, up 19% from $61.36 million in the previous quarter and up 19% from $61.62 million during the same period last year.

Sina Q2 gross profit was $84.34 million with a gross margin of 54%.

Sina's gross profit for the second quarter was $84.34 million, with a gross margin of 54% in the second quarter of 2013, surpassing the 53% from the same period last year.

Selling costs included $3.9 million in stock-based compensation expenses related to the Alibaba transaction. The non-GAAP advertising business gross margin was 57%, surpassing the 54% from the same period last year, benefiting from improved profitability of the company's advertising business.

The non-advertising business gross margin was 55%, surpassing the 54% from the same period last year. The non-GAAP non-advertising business gross margin for the second quarter of 2013 was 49%, surpassing the 45% from the same period last year, driven by increased revenue contribution from higher-margin Weibo value-added services, partially offset by a decline in the profit margin of mobile value-added services.

Sina Q2 expenses were $102 million, up 47% year-on-year.

Sina's total operating expenses for the second quarter of 2013 were $102.5 million, compared to $69.8 million during the same period last year. Excluding $23.2 million in stock-based compensation related to the Alibaba transaction, the non-GAAP operating expenses for the second quarter of 2013 were $75.4 million, compared to $66.8 million during the same period last year.

The increase in non-GAAP operating expenses mainly came from increased labor costs and relatively smaller bad debt expenses, partially offset by a decrease in marketing expenses.

Sina Q2 operating loss was $18.2 million with an operating margin of -12%.

Sina's operating loss for the second quarter of 2013 was $18.2 million, compared to an operating profit of $200,000 during the same period last year. The non-GAAP operating profit for the second quarter of 2013 was $8.8 million, compared to an operating loss of $800,000 during the same period last year.

Sina's non-operating profit for the second quarter of 2013 was $8.2 million, compared to $34.2 million during the same period last year. The non-operating profit for the second quarter of 2013 included $1.3 million in equity investment income and $4.2 million in non-GAAP gains, calculated according to the equity accounting method, which is disclosed one quarter later.

Sina's non-operating profit for the second quarter of 2012 included a net gain of $32.8 million, primarily due to investment gains resulting from the merger of China Real Estate Information Corporation (CRIC) and E-House (China) Holdings Limited.

Sina Q2 net loss was $11.5 million, turning to a loss year-on-year.

Sina's attributable net loss for the second quarter of 2013 was $11.5 million, compared to a net profit of $33.2 million during the same period last year. Sina's attributable diluted net loss per share for the second quarter of 2013 was 17 cents, compared to a diluted net profit per share of 49 cents during the same period last year.

Non-GAAP Sina attributable net profit for the second quarter of 2013 was $14.2 million, compared to $3.7 million during the same period last year. Non-GAAP Sina attributable diluted net income per share for the second quarter of 2013 was 21 cents, compared to 5 cents during the same period last year.

As of June 30, 2013, Sina's cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments totaled $1.237 billion, compared to $713.6 million as of December 31, 2012. The increase in cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments was mainly due to the net cash received from Alibaba related to its investment in Weibo. Cash provided by operating activities for the second quarter of 2013 was $43.6 million, capital expenditures were $10.9 million, and depreciation expenses were $8.7 million.

In April 2013, Alibaba invested $586 million through its wholly-owned subsidiary to purchase preferred shares and common shares of Sina Weibo Corporation, representing approximately 18% of Weibo's fully diluted shares after dilution. Additionally, Sina granted Alibaba an option to increase its fully diluted stake in Weibo to 30% at a pre-agreed pricing method. Affected by this "transaction", an investor option liability of $49.7 million was recognized to reflect the fair market value of the option as of June 30, 2013, along with a one-time stock-based compensation expense of $27.1 million.

Sina expects its non-GAAP net revenue for the third quarter of 2013 to be between $176 million and $180 million, with advertising revenue between $151 million and $153 million, and non-GAAP non-advertising revenue between $25 million and $27 million.
