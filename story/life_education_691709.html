What does professional consulting for outdoor training include?

by boe868 on 2012-03-05 17:20:11

The implementation process of the training is divided into three phases: planning, implementation, and evaluation.

Description of the Training Process

The purpose of the training process is to cultivate the skills and knowledge of individuals so that they can effectively and efficiently perform their duties. It first identifies the training needs at the organizational, project, and individual levels, then proceeds or seeks to obtain training to meet the identified needs. The selection of training topics must be able to satisfy the real needs of system application personnel; a good training organization and evaluation process are necessary guarantees for effective training results.

Training Process Explanation

● The training administration department is responsible for hosting and managing the advancement of the training program.

● Training Needs Research

Optional tools for training needs research include written surveys, group discussion methods, and interview methods. Before conducting the needs research, it is necessary to have a detailed understanding of the true needs of each type of personnel (which may already exist in job descriptions) and to clarify the development direction and goals of the organization and individuals based on these needs.

● Training Needs Analysis

Based on the original feedback data from the needs research, statistical analysis is conducted to determine overall and individual needs, and summarizing conclusions and feasible recommendations are provided for the upcoming training plan design.

● Training Plan Design

After the "Training Needs Analysis" has been discussed in a group setting, the training plan is designed. The training plan should be detailed down to the possible individuals involved, including the scheduling of training times, content, instructors, etc. This information must be communicated to relevant parties and confirmed before execution. Relevant parties include trainees, trainers, project managers, etc.

● Training Budget

To achieve the goal of controlling training costs and effectively utilizing resources, the training budget must strictly follow the predetermined training plan design. This includes internal personnel fees, material costs, external instructor hiring fees, and various service charges.

● Resource Preparation

The resources required for organizing training vary depending on different training methods. Some resources come from within the company, while others are supported by clients.

● Training Implementation

The training process is an interactive one between instructors and students. Records kept during the training will provide necessary references for training evaluations and future training plan designs and implementations.

● Training Evaluation

Post-training evaluations consist of three parts: trainees assessing the rationality of the training procedures, trainers evaluating the quality of learning of the trainees, and trainees assessing the trainers.

● Information Feedback

This is a continuous cyclical process. The end of information feedback becomes the beginning of the next stage of training work. Information feedback follows the principle of whoever organizes the training submits the feedback.

Characteristics of the Training Process

This training process emphasizes the integrity of the training implementation process, stressing the consideration of specific analytical design issues under the premise of overall optimization, i.e., a top-down approach. Another emphasized point is the strict distinction between implementation stages, stressing step-by-step strict analysis, design, and implementation. Each step can be summarized promptly, with problems being fed back and corrected in a timely manner. Work in the subsequent stage must be based on the results of the previous stage, thus providing reliable grounds for each phase's work and greatly enhancing the effectiveness of training implementation.

Guarantee for Training Process Improvement

It is undeniable that strictly adhering to the aforementioned process for any project is impossible. Therefore, to ensure the flexibility of the training implementation process, for each specific training project, we will make modifications according to need, reducing or adding certain parts as needed. Any issues that arise during the training process will be addressed through the following methods:

● Using course feedback forms to timely identify problems related to instructors, course settings, and services during the training process, and formulating detailed and feasible improvement plans;

● Collaborating with clients to jointly develop solutions;

● Holding periodic training process improvement discussion meetings with student representatives;

● Providing instant solutions to various small problems encountered by students during training, documenting them post facto, and notifying relevant personnel.
