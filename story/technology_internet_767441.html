Alibaba plans to invest $16 billion in logistics development before 2020

by anonymous on 2013-10-15 21:29:14

Alibaba Group plans to invest 16 billion US dollars before 2020 for logistics and related support construction, aiming at a comprehensive innovation of China's retail industry. This will open up China's huge domestic market and help it obtain hundreds of millions of potential new users.

Alibaba may possibly get an additional financing of 15 billion US dollars from IPO (Initial Public Offering). Alibaba and its partners such as express companies and insurance companies will use these funds to inject capital into the fragile supply chain in the Chinese market and comprehensively transform large data centers responsible for processing a large amount of consumer information.

While Alibaba sees itself as the catalyst for the innovation of China's e-commerce, its investment plan also sets the tone for further development of competitors' businesses. By encouraging retailers to be more Internet-savvy and building a national network, Alibaba is showing traditional physical stores' competitors how to develop online business.

Companies like Gome Electrical Appliances, Haier Electrical Appliances, and Chow Tai Fook Jewellery have already started to venture into the e-commerce market, establishing their own specialty stores on Alibaba's website, enhancing their brands and gaining returns.

Alibaba CEO Jonathan Lu said that by 2016, Alibaba's online market transaction scale is expected to grow about twice to 3 trillion yuan (about 490 billion US dollars), surpassing Walmart to become the world's largest retail network.

The signal conveyed by Alibaba to retailers is actually very simple: adapt or die.

"Old enterprises unwilling to change are likely to be eliminated by market competition." Alibaba's Chief Strategy Officer Zeng Ming said, "Most traditional retailers have now realized that if they do not develop online business, their survival time is limited."

Analysts predict that within five years, e-commerce will account for one-fifth of China's total retail market, while last year it was only 6%.

Boaz Rottenberg, head of Maverick China based in Beijing, said: "The Chinese market has great potential, most of the retail industry will maintain growth, and the fastest-growing will be e-commerce. If you look at consumers' spending, most of it is online business. And no other country can compare with it."

Next stage

As China's economy slows down from double-digit growth over the years, Alibaba aims to extend its services to remote rural areas and further narrow regional economic development imbalances.

By evaluating supply and demand through data, Alibaba hopes to accurately locate investment locations, such as where to build warehouses, and how to better turn over goods on its Taobao and Tmall websites. Among the 5.69 billion packages in the Chinese market last year, 3 billion came from Taobao and Tmall.

By increasing logistics and data construction, Alibaba hopes to establish a faster logistics speed than other competitors. Alibaba also plans to create a network financial service model through which buyers can pay online, and companies and individuals can apply for loans in this service.

"Alibaba wants to make the e-commerce market as big as possible. By establishing logistics and its surrounding support system, it will innovate the entire retail industry and bring it into the next stage of development," Gartner analyst Praveen Sengar said.

Founded in 1999, Alibaba is the only e-commerce site that can achieve this. Alibaba once defeated foreign competitors and became the dominant force in China's e-commerce market, currently controlling more than three-quarters of the domestic market. According to estimates by market research firm Bain & Co., before 2015, the growth rate of China's e-commerce market size will be 32% per year. And the number of Chinese Internet users is less than half of the total population, so the growth potential is still huge.

Alibaba Vice President Li Junling said, "We have created a real nationwide, cross-sector single market for the first time. We have liberated the consumption ability of Internet users."

Risk

Other retailers have also discovered opportunities.

In the first half of this year, Haier's e-commerce revenue grew nearly six times to 6.33 billion yuan (about 1.034 billion US dollars), accounting for 2% of its total revenue. Suning Commerce Group's e-commerce revenue doubled to 10.6 billion yuan, and Gome's e-commerce revenue in the first half of the year was 27 billion yuan, accounting for 5%-6% of its total revenue.

Forrester Research Asia-Pacific Vice President Andrew Stockwell said, "Consumers are beginning to need a better user experience, and shopping malls and brand websites must achieve differentiation in competition. Brands, especially some luxury and high-profit product brands, are more willing to let users transact through their own websites."

Enterprises bypassing Alibaba's services will gain more profits, obtain user data, and control the entire user experience.

Upon learning of Alibaba's logistics plan, Gome company spokesperson Helen Song said, "For retail, the most important thing is the supply chain. It takes many years to build the supply chain and logistics, and we have been doing it for 20 years. Gome's pressure does not come from e-commerce, in fact, we have not done our own things well enough."

Alibaba is not the only e-commerce company investing in logistics and data. It is reported that JD.com holds nearly one-fifth of the B2C market in China. In major cities in China, JD.com's express service can deliver high-value products within 24 hours, which is its advantage compared to Alibaba.

Forrester analyst Bryan Wang said, "(Alibaba's plan) is good, eye-catching, and bold, but JD.com has already achieved door-to-door delivery within 24 hours in many cities. Do they really need to deliver goods to those remote places within 24 hours? With the competitive pressure from JD.com and other competitors, Alibaba's plan may be urgently needed to improve the user experience."

Big Data

Alibaba has realized that expanding its e-commerce business involves more than just e-commerce itself.

Through launching a series of products, customers can pay online, deposit money into Alipay Investment Fund; enterprises can obtain loans, enterprises and local governments can store data in Alibaba's cloud computing service. In addition, it has an online shopping search engine, mobile operating system, Internet set-top box, digital map service, and it also owns 18% of the equity of China's most popular microblogging platform - Sina Weibo.

Therefore, the data obtained from enterprises is crucial for Alibaba. Alibaba has three data centers in China, which can process more than 1PB of data per day - equivalent to three times the total DNA data of the entire U.S. population.

"These data are extremely valuable," Alibaba's Chief Strategy Officer Zeng Ming said, "How to use the power of technology to truly promote the transformation of China's economy, we have a unique understanding."
