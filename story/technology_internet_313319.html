30th Birthday + 300 Premier League Matches = A Forlorn Owen, Capello Can No Longer Tolerate Sir Alex

Sina Sports - Two days ago, Owen celebrated his 30th birthday, and the former Golden Boy has now entered his thirties this week; today, Owen also reached his 300th career Premier League match, despite Sir Alex only giving him 15 minutes of playtime.

In the 71st minute, Obertan was substituted off for Welbeck, and the person who most often passed to Owen had thus left the field. Welbeck, who came on, preferred to work alone. In the 76th minute, Owen finally came on, replacing Rooney, who had scored the opening goal for Manchester United in this match. As Rooney walked off looking sullen, there was no interaction between him and Owen. However, Owen still took the initiative to give Rooney a high-five, even though Rooney never once looked directly at his former national team golden partner.

In the 82nd minute, Evra crossed from the left flank, and Owen tried to head the ball over the advancing Wolves goalkeeper Hahnemann but missed the target. This was Owen's only attempt at goal during the match. However, in that fleeting moment, we could still feel Owen's sharp sense of smell around the goal area. In the 85th minute, Valencia crossed from the right flank, and Owen received the ball at the edge of the penalty box before passing it immediately to Welbeck on the left side. He then ran into the heart of the penalty area to prepare for an interception, but Welbeck chose to shoot directly instead. His weak shot was easily gathered by Hahnemann. Berbatov waved in dissatisfaction with Welbeck’s decision, while Owen silently walked away with his head down. In the 87th minute, Valencia curled a pass with his right foot, and after controlling the ball with his chest inside the penalty area, Owen couldn’t turn due to close marking by the opposing defender and was eventually forced out of the box by the central defender... Owen played 14 minutes in total, and his stats were very bleak: seven passes all accurate, five accurate forward passes, one shot which went wide.

Without Obertan's assists, without starting, without scoring, and without hearing "Happy Birthday" sung by the crowd of 73,709, Owen ended his 300th Premier League appearance. It seems that he is getting further and further away from boarding the plane to South Africa. However, it appears that the real obstacle preventing Owen from flying to South Africa is Sir Alex Ferguson, not Capello. Capello even subtly expressed his dissatisfaction with Ferguson's arrangement, as Owen had only started three times in the first 17 league games this season.

Capello hasn't forgotten about Owen. On the contrary, the Italian has been quietly observing him. This can be easily understood from Capello's pre-match comments: "Owen is one of the players I am watching. The last time I saw him play was on TV, in the Champions League where he scored three goals, but in the next game, he didn’t start. For this reason, I often ask myself why? Why does Rooney always start while Owen doesn't? He is one of England's most important players."

English football legend Gary Lineker also strongly recommended that Capello take Owen to South Africa: "In my view, the logic is simple: if Owen can play, he will play for Manchester United. If he can play for Manchester United, he can score. Owen's movement and talent for finding space near the penalty spot are better than any of his competitors, although Defoe has improved significantly in that area this season. When English players see Owen in the dressing room – the winner of the European Footballer of the Year award, a star on the World Cup stage, and Germany's nightmare – every single one of them will become fearless."

However, within Manchester United, Owen is not the only one worrying about his World Cup dream. Similar to Owen's fate is Hargreaves, who has been absent for 14 months due to a knee injury. In this match, Hargreaves sat in the stands with O'Shea, and the camera focused on his face several times, yet Hargreaves showed no expression. The Daily Mail analyzed that the reason Hargreaves has not returned to action is entirely psychological. Nevertheless, when Capello mentioned his situation, he gave him ample hope.

"Two months ago, I spoke with him. I met him at Manchester United Club, where he was just preparing to start training, but he still cannot play. Why not (consider including him)? He still has enough time to recover. Hargreaves must play games; those who don't play are not within my consideration. He has six months to regain his best form, but he needs to play. I will select players who are fit to play all matches by next May."
