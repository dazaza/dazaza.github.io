On the 9th, South Korea's Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Trade revealed its stance regarding Japan's request to cancel a music concert planned by South Korean lawmakers on Dokdo (Takeshima Islands). The ministry stated that it saw "no value in responding" to Japan's request. Multiple South Korean media outlets reported on this matter.

South Korean media quoted Park Sung-yong, a member of the Liberty Forward Party, saying, "The music concert was planned to promote Dokdo as South Korean territory through the universal language of music." The classical concert, scheduled for 2 PM on November 11, was organized by a nonpartisan group of South Korean lawmakers known as the "National Assembly Members Group for the Protection of Dokdo." Approximately 100 people, including eight lawmakers, are expected to attend. This comes just after the Japanese government requested the South Korean government to halt plans for constructing a management office on Takeshima/Dokdo islands managed by South Korean local authorities.

For the music concert, there are no restrictions on whether the pieces are original or covers, nor on genre.
