The more failures, perhaps the closer to success.

by hunanshida on 2012-02-20 14:05:37

The teachers of Hunan Normal University's self-study examination want parents to know that they should not only let their children be unafraid of failure, but also encourage them to try and experience failure.

A little girl was just learning to skate. She was very cautious and nervous, but still fell down on her back. The girl sat on the ground sadly, tears in her eyes as she watched others glide gracefully.

At this moment, a handsome boy skated up to the little girl, helped her up, and kindly said to her: "Little sister, skating requires not being afraid of falling. This is a sport where you achieve success through falling. Starting now, you need to prepare to fall fifty times, then you'll be able to skate."

The little girl asked, "Really?"

The boy nodded affirmatively.

Then the little girl stood up resolutely and took a step forward.

One fall, two falls... with each fall, the little girl's eyes would shine a little brighter because she knew that the more failures she had, the closer it meant she was to success.

When she reached 21 falls, the little girl didn't need to count anymore.

The teachers of Hunan Normal University's self-study examination think it's really ingenious - scheduling failure, accepting and enjoying failure means that children won't be tripped up by failure. They can see success through failure and light through darkness. Any effort requires a kind of motivation, a guiding light towards a bright goal.

The teachers from Hunan Normal University's self-study examination website believe that during childhood, if parents continuously instill this concept in their children, they will gradually adjust their attitude towards failure.

Scheduling failure takes it a step further than merely allowing failure. Allowing failure is like getting a vaccine, while scheduling failure has already entered into treatment.
