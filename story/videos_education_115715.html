Chinese secretary award-winning essay contest

by zzang1 on 2008-12-30 10:46:14

I. Purpose of the Event

Over a turbulent 30 years of great change, 30 years of remarkable progress, and 30 years of brilliant achievements, to commemorate the 30th anniversary of the reform and opening up, we look back and summarize the development of China's secretarial industry during this extraordinary historical period. For every secretary, in these 30 years of development, each person will have an unforgettable memory, even more so, touching stories and emotions that are hard to let go. China Secretaries is specially holding a "Secretary Memories" essay contest for the 30th anniversary of the reform and opening up. Through the stories told by secretarial workers or people related to the secretarial profession.

II. Organizing Institutions:

Host Organization: Evaluation Committee of the 30th Anniversary of Reform and Opening Up "Secretary Memories" Contest

Co-organizer: China Secretaries

Lishui Vocational and Technical College, Zhejiang Province

Special Planning: Wenzhou Municipal Point Secretary Affairs Office

Sponsor: Wenzhou Renrui Investment Guarantee Co., Ltd.

Wenzhou Municipal Point Market Research and Consulting Center

III. Submission Requirements:

(1) Administrative secretarial staff, secretarial vocational educators, and secretarial enthusiasts from all provinces and cities can participate in this event.

(2) Works reflecting changes in the history of China’s secretarial industry over the past 30 years since the reform and opening up, whether in text or pictures, are acceptable, with no restrictions on genre or submission quantity. Submissions should focus on showcasing and genuinely expressing the author's inner experiences, and the author must be the copyright owner of the work. The word count of submissions should not exceed 5,000 words in principle, and electronic versions are preferred. A personal profile should also be attached.

(3) Photographs submitted for the competition must be original (not processed through PS or other technical treatments), accompanied by accurate and concise textual descriptions, along with the author's contact address and contact information.

(4) Authors are responsible for their own works. Submitted manuscripts should not have been published domestically or internationally (including on the internet); they should not involve legal disputes as defined by the "Copyright Law of the People's Republic of China," nor violate national policies and regulations. Plagiarism or fabrication will result in disqualification and public announcement on the internet.

(5) The host organization has the right of first refusal to publish and adapt the submitted works into other artistic formats.

(6) The interpretation rights of this submission activity belong to the Evaluation Committee of the 30th Anniversary of Reform and Opening Up "Secretary Memories" Contest.

(7) Submission Period: December 20, 2008 – April 30, 2009

IV. Award Descriptions

(1) Best Work Award: 1 winner, prize money 500 yuan

(2) Best Conception Work Award: 1 winner, prize money 300 yuan

(3) Best Reader Award: 1 winner, prize money 300 yuan

(4) Excellent Work Awards: 7 winners, each receiving a 100-yuan membership recharge card for China Secretaries

(5) Finalist Awards: 20 winners, each receiving a 50-yuan membership recharge card for China Secretaries

V. Activity Details:

(1) All submissions will be selected periodically in forms such as single pieces, photo sets, or special editions. Outstanding works will be displayed on the China Secretaries website for one week (7 days) based on online voting. The highest-voted works will be recommended to the Evaluation Committee of the 30th Anniversary of Reform and Opening Up "Secretary Memories" Contest for evaluation. Then, experts from the committee will select the best works, excellent works, best conception works, and best reader awards. Winning works will be edited and produced, published on the large-scale China Secretaries website, and awarded with certificates of honor and prize money. Meanwhile, all participating works will be collected by China Secretaries and promoted to various media outlets over the long term. If published, additional royalties will be paid.

(2) The results of the award evaluations will be announced in mid-May 2009, followed by an award ceremony.

(3) Winning works will be publicly displayed for 5 days. If there are no complaints or objections, the winners and their works will be confirmed.
