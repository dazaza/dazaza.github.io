"Dreams and Madness" - The Moral and Greed Game Under the Financial Crisis

by jqrxass on 2009-10-03 16:49:49

"Dreams and Madness" is a model work of perfect integration of critical realism and idealism. In a moment when the capital era is roaring in and changing people's lives and fates with its overwhelming power.

The brilliant appearance of three typical characters: Sun Heping, Yang Liu, and Liu Bidinger, brings this thrilling and bizarre long novel into direct contact with a series of sensitive issues in China's reform: the game between national development and social justice, the game among various social forces (energies) at the levels of interests and spirit, the integration and game between industrial capital and financial capital, and the game between wealth desire and moral adherence, presenting readers with scene after scene that are both strange and familiar, both intriguing and magnificent depth reality scenes.

Part One of "Dreams and Madness"

When the iron gate of the prison slammed shut, Sun Heping had an unusual feeling, as if he wasn't there to visit Liu Bidinger in jail but had also come in like Liu Bidinger. His entry was pretty high-level, with the warden personally accompanying him. The screws produced by the prison were expecting their company's orders, so the warden couldn't help but accompany them. However, Sun Heping felt that the accompaniment was more like an escort. Passing by row after row of cell doors, he inexplicably thought, which one here belongs to him? Subconsciously, it seemed as if he was always ready to hear a shout of "here we are." The diminutive warden never shouted, only smilingly introducing the production status of various screws and even suggesting he examine things on the side. Sun Heping responded vaguely, because of his sense of guilt, and didn't clearly hear what the warden said. It wasn't until he entered the workshop full of prisoners that he suddenly realized that his inspection had already begun.

Part Two of "Dreams and Madness"

Yang Liu judged that the conspiracy came from Sun Heping. Things should have been like this: Sun Heping learned from Liu Bidinger's mouth about Wang Xiaofei's fact of competing by raising the price for the acquisition of Hope Automobile's equity. Regardless of his threats, Sun Heping raised the bid again. He might have actually reached a share transfer agreement with Liu Bidinger, or even formed a certain interest alliance against him and Beizhong Group. Judging from the abnormal trend of Hope Automobile in the past two days, such a possibility completely existed. Sun Heping manipulated some institutions to raise the stock price, making the arbitrary price increase somewhat reasonable. If the group really pursued responsibility, he might take the opportunity to stir up trouble and publicly declare independence. In addition, raising the stock price has another benefit: providing justification for Liu Bidinger's reneging. Do you Northern Heavy Industries want this equity? Fine, continue raising the price!

Part Three of "Dreams and Madness"

This was an offensive, an unprecedented public opinion offensive that neither Beizhong Group nor Northern Heavy Industries had ever encountered. Everything was carefully planned, from the open letter of the small shareholder who claimed to be "ant," to the commentator article in "People's Securities." The three super-large-sized black bold words "I am angry" were shocking, and the content of the open letter was even more daring and cutting, cursing the major shareholder Beizhong Group of Northern Heavy Industries, the state-owned assets department, and the management department. The commentator article expressed comprehensive support for the views of this ant shareholder, calling on all small and medium shareholders across the country to read this open letter well, actively participate in the share reform game, and stand up straight like this ant shareholder. They issued warnings to these state-owned major shareholders under the guise of reminders, demanding that they face history and reality squarely and return fairness to the market.

Part Four of "Dreams and Madness"

Sun Heping believed that Tang Jiahe had multiple suspicions of accepting bribes and selling off state-owned assets at low prices. Selling off piecemeal was more profitable than overall transfer. Each sale could accept a bribe, and it could even appear as if he was breaking his heart to solve the enterprise's difficulties. It was estimated that Ren Yan'an didn't see it, or saw it but didn't find Tang Jiahe's bribery clues and evidence and dared not say it, only recognizing Tang Jiahe as a big fool. Sun Heping believed that Tang Jiahe was a big fool in the management of state-owned assets, but definitely not a fool in making money and getting rich. That person was incredibly astute. Today, this astute person has fallen. Knowing the clues of Liu Bidinger's bribery, if Tang Jiahe dare to mess around and act arrogantly, he would really go to Beijing to find the Central Commission for Discipline Inspection and let the Central Commission for Discipline Inspection investigate thoroughly based on his report as a nationally famous entrepreneur...
