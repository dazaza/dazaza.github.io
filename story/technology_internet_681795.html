New Material Industry Attracts Attention, Technology Transfer Rate Awaits Improvement

by keyizhang1 on 2012-02-28 15:36:34

The Ministry of Industry and Information Technology (MIIT) has recently issued the "12th Five-Year Development Plan for the New Materials Industry." The plan proposes that during the 12th Five-Year Plan period, China's new materials industry is expected to achieve a total output value of 2 trillion yuan, with an average annual growth rate exceeding 25%. It aims to promote 30 key varieties of new materials and implement several demonstration and application promotion projects.

### Important Status of the New Materials Industry

New materials refer to those emerging or under development, possessing superior properties and special functions not found in traditional materials; or materials produced using new technologies (processes, equipment), which significantly enhance or create new functions in traditional materials. Generally, key materials required for high-tech industries are also considered part of new materials.

The materials industry mainly includes: textiles; petroleum processing and coking; chemical raw materials and chemical products manufacturing; chemical fiber manufacturing; rubber products; plastic products; non-metallic mineral products; ferrous metal smelting and rolling processing; non-ferrous metal smelting and rolling processing; metal products; medical materials and medical supplies; electrical appliances and electronic components manufacturing, etc.

The new materials industry is widely recognized as one of the most promising high-tech industries in the 21st century, with significant influence on future development. Not only is it listed by various countries as a strategic emerging industry, but it is also considered the foundation for the development of all strategic emerging industries. It is the main force in enhancing corporate competitiveness and an important measure of a country's economic, social, scientific and technological progress, and defense strength.

Currently, China's economy is entering a critical phase of transformation and upgrading, with the proportion of traditional industries gradually declining, while emerging industries face rapid development. China has proposed developing strategic emerging industries, and since new materials are widely used in emerging industries, they play a crucial role as the foundation of manufacturing. The development of new materials plays a significant role in promoting industrial upgrades and transformations. If the new materials industry develops well, it can drive the upgrade of other industries; if it does not develop well, it will constrain the development of other industries.

### Development of China's New Materials Industry

Currently, the global market scale of new materials exceeds $400 billion annually, and the market for new products and technologies brought about by new materials is even broader, with annual turnover exceeding $2 trillion. It is understood that although China's new materials output accounts for a small proportion of its GDP, its growth rate is relatively fast.

The plan points out that since the beginning of the new century, China's new materials industry has developed rapidly. In 2010, the scale of China's new materials industry exceeded 650 billion yuan, with an average annual growth rate of about 20% compared to 2005. Among these, rare earth functional materials, advanced energy storage materials, photovoltaic materials, organosilicon, superhard materials, special stainless steel, glass fibers, and their composite materials have production capacities leading the world.

Some key technologies have achieved significant breakthroughs. China's independently developed tantalum niobium beryllium alloys, amorphous alloys, high magnetic induction oriented silicon steel, diphenylmethane diisocyanate (MDI), superhard materials, meta-aramid, and superconducting materials have reached or approached international levels. The variety of new materials continues to increase, with the supply capacity of high-end metal structural materials, new inorganic non-metallic materials, and high-performance composite materials significantly enhanced. The self-sufficiency level of advanced polymer materials and special metal functional materials is gradually improving.

However, China's overall development level of the new materials industry still lags behind developed countries, and the industry faces some pressing issues. These include weak independent development capabilities for new materials, insufficient innovation motivation among large material enterprises, inadequate supply of key new materials; disconnection between research, education, and application, short industrial chains, difficulties in promoting and applying new materials, and incomplete industrial development models; lack of unified planning and policy guidance for the new materials industry, insufficient and scattered R&D investment, and relatively weak basic management work.

### Opportunities for the Development of the New Materials Industry

The plan indicates that the 12th Five-Year Plan is a critical period for comprehensively building a moderately prosperous society and a tough period for accelerating the transformation of economic development methods. Strategic adjustments in the economic structure provide important opportunities for the development of the new materials industry. On one hand, accelerating the cultivation and development of energy-saving and environmental protection, next-generation information technology, high-end equipment manufacturing, new energy, and new energy vehicles, implementing major national and defense construction projects, requires support and guarantees from the new materials industry, providing broad market space for the development of the new materials industry. On the other hand, China's raw materials industry is on a large scale, with excess capacity in some industries, and resource, energy, and environmental constraints are becoming increasingly stringent. There is an urgent need to vigorously develop the new materials industry, accelerate the transformation and upgrading of the materials industry, and cultivate new growth points.

The downstream industries of the new materials industry are extensive, including automobiles, aviation equipment, communication equipment, home appliances, IT industries, as well as real estate, transportation, urban construction, and many other fields. These areas in China are currently in a period of rapid development, with huge demand for new materials.

The plan shows that during the 12th Five-Year Plan period, China's new materials industry will focus on developing special metal functional materials, high-end metal structural materials, advanced polymer materials, new inorganic non-metallic materials, high-performance composite materials, and frontier new materials.

Key projects during the 12th Five-Year Plan period include the rare earth and rare metal functional materials special project, the carbon fiber low-cost and high-end innovation demonstration project, the high-strength lightweight alloy materials special project, the high-performance steel materials special project, the high-performance membrane materials special project, the advanced battery materials special project, the new energy-saving and environmentally friendly building materials demonstration application special project, the electronic information functional materials special project, the biological medical materials special project, and the new materials innovation capacity construction special project.

### Need to Enhance Innovation Capacity in the New Materials Industry

As one of the three key technologies of the 21st century, new materials are the foundation and pioneer of high-tech development, an important material basis for developing information, aerospace, energy, biology, and other high-tech industries, and have become a powerful driving force for the rapid global economic growth and the focus for countries to enhance their core competitiveness.

Although China is a major material country, it has not yet become a strong material country. Some high-quality, high-tech content, high-value-added new products still mainly rely on imports. Compared with developed countries, China still has a significant gap in the field of new materials. Mainly reflected in: insufficient original innovation capabilities, few products with independent intellectual property rights; lagging behind in the transformation of scientific and technological achievements and industrialization development, low technical content and added value of enterprise products, and due to weak scientific research and innovation capabilities in new materials, as well as immature production and manufacturing technologies, resulting in insufficient quality stability and reliability of new materials products; a large amount of high-grade new materials products in China still depend on imports; low industrial concentration, small enterprise scale, and lack of international competitiveness.
