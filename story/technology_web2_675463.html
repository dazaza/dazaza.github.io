Blogger Chow Jialiang: An Exclusive Interview by Wang Xingdong for the International Weekly

Interviewee: Canadian-Chinese blogger Chow Jialiang

Netizens familiar with making money through blogging mostly know the name John Chow, whose Chinese name is Zhou Jialiang, a Canadian-Chinese national. Zhou Jialiang is a professional blogger who earns over $30,000 per month from his blog, making it a must-read for those interested in monetizing blogs. His experiences in earning money are documented in his free eBook "Make Money Online with JohnChow dotCom."

However, people may wonder how writing a blog can yield such high returns. In an interview with our publication, Zhou Jialiang stated that focusing on content and persistence are key to attracting readers consistently. He also suggested that novice bloggers could use professional online advertising software to sell ads, rather than distracting themselves with ad promotion.

Regarding next year's Shanghai Expo, Zhou Jialiang expressed great anticipation, stating he would definitely come and write about his experience on his blog.

Publishing Income to Attract Attention

Reporter: You've recently been publishing your monthly blogging income on your blog. Is this practice itself aimed at attracting attention? Has the disclosure of your income significantly increased blog traffic?

Zhou: You're correct. Publishing income figures effectively attracts readers, and as my monthly income increases, so does the blog traffic, including video conferencing. On the internet, traffic equals money, good meeting video conferencing makes the training industry soar higher. That's how it works. Increased traffic is just one key aspect of earning money through a blog; more importantly, it's about how you utilize the traffic. Most blogs perform averagely in this regard. With some adjustments, many blogs could earn three to four times their current income. For example, I promote my blog through affiliate marketing to maximize gains.

Content Still Reigns Supreme to Make Money

Reporter: We know your blog income is mainly divided into three parts: advertisements, promotions, and sponsored links. However, for ordinary bloggers, attracting advertisers can be challenging. Do you have any suggestions in this area?

Zhou: My advice is not to worry about finding advertisers but to focus on writing quality content. Advertisers will naturally come to you. I've never actively sought out advertisers; they always find me. What I do is concentrate on producing the best content and becoming an expert in my field to attract both advertisers and readers. For new bloggers, the most important thing is to focus on the content and let third parties handle the advertisements, like Google, who can manage ad-related matters for you. Of course, the simplest method is to install automatic ad sales software, such as OIO Publisher (OIOPublisher).

As a blogger, you should write your blog without thinking about selling ads. If you try to do both, it will分散your energy, likely leading to a decline in content quality. Besides myself, many other bloggers make money because we've become brands among the multitude of blogs, treating blogging as a business rather than a personal hobby.

Regular Updates and Long-term Persistence

Reporter: In China, celebrity blogs were once popular with high traffic, but now people's attention has waned, and there aren't many reports of anyone earning substantial amounts from blogging monthly. How can one continuously attract readers' attention instead of being a fleeting phenomenon?

Zhou: The key lies in consistently updating the blog. Since December 2005 when I started blogging, I haven't missed a day of writing. I write at least one post every day. It's precisely because of the daily fresh content that readers return to my blog.

Reporter: You mentioned writing at least one blog post daily, but I noticed other writers on your blog too. Is writing one post per day the optimal model?

Zhou: Over the past few years, I've averaged two posts per day. If you don't want to write that much, there's no need to force it. The critical point isn't how many posts you write daily but maintaining consistency. Your frequency must be consistent. If you write two or three posts per week, stick to that schedule. Regarding the issue of others writing on behalf of you, yes, there are guest writers on my blog, but they only write comments related to sponsored content. The majority of the content is still written by me.

Actively Utilize New Technologies

Reporter: Nowadays, new technologies emerge every few years or even annually, while older ones decline. For instance, Twitter is currently very popular, whereas MySpace has lost much of its popularity. How can blogging keep up with the trends of technological advancements?

Zhou: It's simple; as soon as a new technology appears, immediately register and start using it. Usually, I'm not the first person to use the latest technology. For example, when I registered on Twitter, I found someone had already registered under my name. But once I start using a new technology, I ensure I understand it thoroughly, including how it operates and how I can apply it to my blog. I wasn't the first person to use Twitter, but since I started using it, I've gained 50,000 followers on Twitter.

Stick to the Same Theme

Reporter: I've read your blog, and some content is quite personal, while most teaches people how to make money online. Which type of content is more beneficial for earning money?

Zhou: I enjoy both types of content you mentioned. The key lies in sticking to the same theme, though occasionally mixing in other topics is fine too. This is a good way to engage with readers on a personal level.

Much of the content on my blog relates to making money online, which is the main theme of my blog. There are also some personal stories from my life. These help reinforce my blog's brand. When readers see content about my travels around the world, they realize my lifestyle is what they aspire to, providing them motivation to write their own blogs.

Reporter: Your blog is essentially your personal website. There are also specialized blog sites like huffingtonpost.com, which mainly features political blogs. Compared to these specialized blog sites, what advantages do you have?

Zhou: The main advantage is that my blog has very low operational costs. I can work from home or anywhere with internet access. My readers are also more passionate about my blog because they can interact with me on a personal level.

Next Year's Visit to Shanghai for the Expo Blog

Reporter: You've visited China before and written some blogs about your visits. How was the traffic for those blogs? Next year, the Shanghai Expo will take place. Do you plan to visit Shanghai then and write about your experience at the Expo?

Zhou: The blog content about China was very popular and received high traffic. I will definitely attend the Expo next year and write some blogs about it. I am looking forward to it.
