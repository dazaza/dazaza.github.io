Two Arsenal starters confirmed out against Liverpool; Wenger rejects Cruyff's call for Fabregas

by qinglai888 on 2009-12-18 08:18:09

Arsenal vs Liverpool: Two Confirmed Absentees for the Starting Lineup, Wenger Rejects Cruyff's Call for Cesc Fàbregas Again

Sina Sports - This Wednesday's away match against Burnley is not expected to be too challenging for Arsenal, with securing all three points and reclaiming their position above Villa being the sole objective. However, Arsène Wenger's squad has encountered some new injuries, as reported on Arsenal’s official website. In Sunday's game against Liverpool, Denilson suffered a back injury, and Traoré sustained a groin injury. It was confirmed that both players will not feature in the upcoming match against Burnley.

However, there is some good news. Eboué, who missed the Liverpool game due to a groin strain, has returned to the squad and can bolster the right-sided options. Wenger stated: "There wasn't enough recovery time after Sunday's game, so we've picked up a few more injuries. Denilson is definitely out; he might be available for Saturday's match against Hull City, but we must ensure his back is fully recovered before letting him play. However, Eboué is back in the team."

After defeating Liverpool, Wenger was in high spirits as his team had completely shaken off the shadow of their heavy defeat against Chelsea. When discussing the championship prospects for this season, Wenger indicated that the Premier League title race for the 2009-10 season is the most open since he arrived at Arsenal (meaning the most teams are involved). Wenger told the club's official website: "No team has been able to dominate early in the season. Instead, four teams have been closely competing, and I believe there could be six, seven, or even eight teams capable of surprising anyone on any given weekend. Clearly, this is the most open title race I've experienced in my 13 years here. I feel that many teams have a genuine chance of winning the league."

Respect from other teams does not equate to pessimism about his own team. Wenger remains particularly confident about Arsenal. He said: "I have deeply believed from the start of the season that this will be our year. I said the same after the Chelsea game. The reason I say this is because: look at how well we've progressed as a whole, how strong our team spirit and quality are, and how thoroughly prepared we are for each match, like going into battle. From September to May next year, everything will continue to improve."

For Arsenal to maintain their championship aspirations, Cesc Fàbregas' importance is undeniable. With Robin van Persie injured, Fàbregas is almost the sole guiding light for Arsenal. Wenger naturally wants to ensure that Fàbregas plays as well as possible in every key match, so he rejects some external requests when he can. For instance, the friendly match between Catalonia and Argentina scheduled for the 22nd of next month. Catalonia coach Johan Cruyf requested Fàbregas to play in this game at Camp Nou, but Wenger firmly rejected it.

Fàbregas takes pride in his Catalan heritage and admires Cruyff, but Wenger doesn't care about these sentiments. He has the right to reject such requests because Catalonia is not an officially recognized FIFA national team. However, the 22-year-old Fàbregas feels his physical condition is fine, and since there isn't a double-header next week, he believes he has time to go to Spain. He said: "I'll play in the matches against Burnley on Wednesday and Hull City three days later, but after that, there won't be any Premier League games for nearly a week, so I can return to Spain on the 22nd to play for Catalonia."

In Wenger's mind, only his team matters. There is a crucial match against Villa the following weekend, and three of the four big clubs have lost to Villa. Whether Arsenal can perform well against Villa is worth questioning. Van Persie got injured while playing for Holland, and Liverpool clearly refused to let Mascherano represent Argentina in the match against Catalonia. Wenger, who has always disliked the hassle of international games, certainly wouldn't agree to let Fàbregas play in a match that isn't even considered an A-level international fixture. Wenger said: "We must respect the concept of priority, and the priority is the club itself."

Besides this match, Spain also has another noteworthy friendly next week — a match between the La Liga All-Stars and the African All-Stars. This game is known as the second African Charity Match, co-organized by UNICEF and the Kanouté Foundation, and will take place at the Bernabéu. Originally, Arsenal's five players — Song, Nasri, Diaby, Sagna, and Eboué — were included in the African team's list. Clearly, the African team composition includes not only locally-born players but also those with African heritage. Wenger would not agree with such arrangements. Therefore, in the final list, there isn't a single Arsenal player. In fact, there isn't even one active Premier League player. The closest connection to the Premier League is probably former West Ham and Tottenham forward, current Sevilla player, and founder of the Kanouté Foundation, Frederic Kanouté, along with former Manchester United striker Manucho, who recently joined Valladolid.
