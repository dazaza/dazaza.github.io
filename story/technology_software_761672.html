Nostalgia for Windows 98

by geekzhang on 2013-05-06 12:09:28

In just a few years, computers have transitioned from being exclusive to elite institutions to becoming a staple in ordinary households. In China, compared to the overly academic term "computer," people prefer the more popular term "PC." Over the past decade, various forms of computer products have appeared in common consumers' homes, ranging from laptops and desktops to later mini-desktops. Now, tablets optimized for touch operations and running on Windows 8 operating systems are also starting to appear in consumers' sights.However, in the fast-paced content consumption era, revisiting the initial Windows 98 operating system, its UI may not have been flashy, and its functions may not have been fully rich, but it was a landmark product that transformed many mass consumers from computer novices into regular users.From Windows 95, stronger than Windows 95On June 25, 1998, Microsoft released the English version of Windows 98, codenamed Memphis, as scheduled amidst much fanfare. When the first development version was announced on December 15, 1996, the new operating system was once called Windows 97 or Windows 9x. On the night of August 31, 1998, the square in front of Haidian Theater in Zhongguancun was bustling with people. After midnight, the Chinese version of Windows 98 set sail from here, beginning to serve millions of computer users in the Chinese market until late October 2001 when Windows XP gradually entered the mass electronics consumer market. It wasn't until then that Windows 98 started to phase out of the market and retreat into history. According to statistics, on the day of the launch of the Chinese version of Windows 98, all five launch points across the country sold a total of 337 copies within 98 minutes, worth around 600,000 yuan.Windows 98 can be considered the first significant version released in the field of operating systems after Windows 95. The two operating systems share a lineage, with no obvious changes in the user interface; the boot screen was still the typical flag design with trailing dots, and the desktop layout featured a green background with spread-out operating system icons. At the bottom was the iconic taskbar that continues to this day, with the Start menu on the far left and the notification area on the far right. To this day, features like the notification area with volume and date/time and the taskbar have remained, but the classic Start menu button was removed in the touch-optimized reform of Windows 8.Although it still looked similar to the early familiar Windows 95, Windows 98 included internal support for 32-bit device drivers, surpassing its predecessor in performance, and similarly in functionality, including support for plug-and-play devices, context-sensitive menus optimized for mouse usage, and an Explorer designed entirely according to Internet standards, among others.Computer life during the Windows 98 eraIn the mid-to-late development period of the Windows 98 operating system, content consumption both locally and online continued to enrich. At the time, for playing media files, the most commonly used players were the built-in Windows Media Player and RealPlayer. In terms of storage media, 3.5-inch floppy disks were the mainstream product at the time, although the concept of USB drives began to emerge during this period, their portability was far inferior to today's USB drives. The USB drives back then were like portable flashlights the size of pagers, but surprisingly, a year or two later, smaller MP3 players became popular. Due to their storage capabilities, many 512MB, and even 256MB, MP3 players hanging from people's necks contained not only music but also some documents and small application software.Alongside the development of the Windows 98 operating system were some popular local single-player games and online games at the time. Due to the low普及rate of home computers, people often saw groups playing Red Alert, Diablo, StarCraft, and Counter-Strike (CS) in internet cafes. Later, games like Stone Age, Fantasy Westward Journey, and Legends entered the public eye, shifting people's content consumption from local to online. This was roughly how users' PC content consumption lives looked back then, and most of these games were powered by the Windows 98 operating system. However, at the time, many people may not have had as clear a concept of operating systems as they do today.New generation shift, Windows XP becomes the new favoriteMicrosoft has consistently maintained a steady pace in developing its Windows operating systems, allowing users to gradually experience newer versions with enhanced performance and richer functionalities. On October 25, 2001, Windows XP officially launched. The new operating system introduced new shadow styles and embossed display effects based on traditional layouts, winning over more users. Similarly, Windows Vista followed suit. From this point onward, Windows 98 gradually faded from users' views, becoming part of the classic memories of the PC era for mass consumers.During the development of the Windows operating system, besides the gradual discovery of new features, the beautification and optimization of the operating system’s performance is also an interesting phenomenon. At the time, many third-party beautification software programs emerged to meet user needs. Taking Windows XP as an example, popular ones included the Windows Optimization Master and Super Rabbit. These softwares initially provided simple personalization customization for the operating system, but later, many beautification plugins could even customize the operating system's user interface to look completely different from Windows XP.The interplay of software and hardware led to the retirement of Windows 98Even to this day, there remains a very popular discussion topic: whether software adapts to hardware or hardware determines software. In reality, answers to this question vary depending on the context. However, in most cases, the characteristic where hardware determines software development is more evident. Taking Windows 8 as an example, due to the popularity of touch devices, Microsoft began introducing touch support in the new operating system and innovated the operating system's user interface for touch devices, such as the refreshing Metro UI. From this perspective, Windows 98 fading from public view stems from the rapid development of today's hardware industry or, more broadly, the rapid development of productivity levels. Regardless, Windows 98 remains the threshold through which many users first encountered computers, leaving behind classic memories and imaginations of computers and the internet for ordinary consumers.
