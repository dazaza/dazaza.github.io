How to Better Prevent Moth Damage in Pure Wool Carpets?

Author: Royal Elizabeth Carpet [http://www.elidt.com/] Website: www.elidt.com [http://www.elidt.com/]

Summary: Generally speaking, the middle part of a carpet [http://www.elidt.com/] is less prone to insect infestation, while the edges and areas within 0.3-1 meters around are more susceptible, because these areas are not frequently stepped on, leaving space between the carpet and the floor where dust and air can accumulate, providing a living environment for carpet pests.

Generally, the central part of the carpet is less likely to be infested with insects, whereas the corners and surrounding areas within 0.3-1 meters are more prone to infestation. This is because these parts are not often trodden upon, and there is some space between the carpet and the floor, storing dust and air that allows carpet insects to survive. However, all parts of tufted or woven carpets may potentially harbor insects due to the gaps between warp and weft threads at the back of such carpets, which allow air to flow even after contacting the ground. Additionally, pure wool carpets [http://www.elidt.com/] laid on wooden floors are more prone to insect infestations compared to those placed on cement, tile, or terrazzo floors. The reason lies in the fact that wooden floors easily absorb moisture, thereby providing conditions for insect growth. Parts of the carpet pressed under furniture are also prone to insect infestation.

Experts from Royal Elizabeth Carpet remind everyone that to prevent carpet infestation, the following methods should be adopted:

1. Keep the carpet clean and dry. After using the carpet for a year, it should be taken outdoors, dusted, and aired out but not exposed to direct sunlight. When re-laying the carpet, the floor should be swept clean.

2. After using the carpet for two to three years, it should be cleaned with chemical agents once. After washing the carpet, if it continues to be used, the floor should be wiped clean before laying the carpet again.

3. Wooden floors should preferably be waxed, and after a few days, a layer of naphthalene powder should be scattered on the floor before laying the carpet.

4. When storing the carpet, sprinkle some naphthalene powder evenly on the carpet, then wrap it tightly and store it in a ventilated place. It is best stored at a height of half a meter to one meter above the ground.
