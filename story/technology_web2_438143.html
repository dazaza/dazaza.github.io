Food lovers who want to experience local customs will definitely be interested in the farmers' market like me. As a coastal island county, Yuhuan is sure to have seafood, but with economic development, most locals are either involved in politics or business. The traditional profession of going out to sea for fishing seems to have become distant. Plus, factors such as environmental pollution and rising oil prices, this island has surprisingly shown a peculiar phenomenon: the high cost of living ranks fifth nationwide, even higher than big cities like Wenzhou.

Back to the original topic, let's "visit" the farmers' market!

The first stop is the Old Street. As the name implies, it is an old street where my father grew up. Nowadays, he can still meet one or two childhood friends on the street, which is amazing. This old street used to be the center of people's activities, but now it has become a small corner of the county town. People occasionally come here to buy some traditional snacks and can also find cheap vegetables here.

Sweet potato balls are made by mixing fresh sweet potatoes with glutinous rice flour to make a sweet skin, inside...

This year's Spring Festival was very special, our whole family spent it in a familiar yet strange place called Yuhuan, located in Taizhou City, Zhejiang Province, across the bay from Wenzhou City and Yandang Mountain. It is just a small county town, not a tourist destination, so many people have never heard of it. I have never been there, but I have been listening to Yuhuan dialect since I was a child, hearing stories about this island from my grandparents, tasting seafood sent by my uncle from Yuhuan, and filled with imagination about this small island county. My parents said they would take me back to our hometown, but this wish wasn't fulfilled until more than twenty years later.

This year when I returned to China, I caught the trend and took the bullet train. Whether at the station or on the train, the environment was great and very comfortable. Recalling the past experiences of squeezing into hard seats during Spring Festival, it truly feels like heaven and earth.

Not being religious, we don't celebrate Christmas. While in China, we only celebrated as an excuse to have fun; those few years we played with classmates at Tianfu Square with inflatable hammers. However, these blind gatherings have been banned, and I've heard that many classmates got drunk on the streets this Christmas... After coming to America, Christmas actually gives us a holiday, but we no longer love to join in the festivities. Due to different beliefs, we mostly treat it as a long vacation, gathering with friends.

That said, Christmas still has many adorable aspects, such as crazy discounts in stores, Christmas music playing everywhere, and most attractively for me, the string lights all over the streets. Every Christmas eve, many families hang various string lights. No need to go to lantern festivals, you can enjoy all kinds of lights in residential areas. This year, I specially found a Seattle neighborhood known for its Christmas lights; it really felt like a small park.

Yesterday was Winter Solstice, and Seattle has already entered the cold rainy season, making the nights seem exceptionally long. I couldn't help but miss the warm sunshine, especially the orange sunset at dusk. Speaking of sunsets, I must mention the German town of Eichstatt.

That day we had just received our car, and PS couldn't wait to experience driving on Germany's unlimited speed autobahn, so we headed north.

It was late autumn, and the urban scenery along the way was even more charming under the red leaves, occasionally spotting one or two European-style villas on the hillside.

Germany's automotive industry is highly developed, with several well-known car companies originating from Germany, among them BMW and Audi both coming from Munich. So if we're in Munich, we must visit the Audi headquarters.

Audi headquarters is located 80 kilometers from downtown Munich in Ingolstadt. We arrived in less than half an hour on the unlimited speed highway Auto Bahn in an Audi A8 driven by an Audi employee. I remember him exceeding 200 km/h several times, zooming through!

Upon arriving at Audi headquarters, we first enjoyed an Audi-exclusive cappuccino, marked with the four rings!

Munich Oktoberfest, also known as the October Festival, on the last day of October, I must record it!

My initial understanding of the German beer festival came from a "German Beer Festival" held on Wangfujing Street in Beijing. Jenny and I bought a glass of black beer together, and at the time, we found it quite unpalatable, throwing away more than half of it.

Nowadays, many countries around the world emulate Germany's beer festival, having seen several, none can compare with this authentic Munich Beer Festival. Munich's population is less than 1.5 million, yet during the beer festival, over 6 million people gather here.

At 11 a.m. on October 2, the plane landed at Munich Airport, officially starting our 14-day European free trip.

As soon as the plane landed, we hurriedly collected our luggage, dropped it off at the hotel, rushed to the train station, boarded the S8 train, heading straight to Marienplatz, Mary Square.

Arriving at the square, amidst drizzling rain, we searched everywhere for a sign "Free Walking Tour", finally meeting the guided tour starting at exactly 1 p.m. We rushed all the way to catch this famous sightseeing activity. All the guides here are young people from all over the world. They came here for various reasons, fell in love with the place, and stayed to guide tours, making more people fall in love with it. These guides receive no salary; all their income comes from tourists' tips, so they are all extremely dedicated and provide excellent introductions. We met an Irish guide with dual degrees in anthropology and history and was still a graduate student. With his strong Irish accent, he thoroughly introduced the history of Munich and Bavaria to us.

The first stop, Frauenkirche Cathedral, the largest Gothic cathedral in southern Germany, commonly known as the onion dome church. Unfortunately, one of the onions was under repair at the time, plus the bad weather, preventing us from taking exterior photos, so we could only go inside.

This church was completed in just 20 years.

Occasionally came across a summary of Chengdu dialect from a friend, it should have been reprinted many times, I don't know the original source. Although it may not be entirely comprehensive, it is still very representative, so I couldn't resist sharing it. Now, opportunities to speak Chengdu dialect are becoming fewer and fewer. Even when calling my parents, it's hard to use the "flavorful" old Chengdu dialect. Every time I return to Chengdu, relatives and friends laugh at how my Chengdu dialect has changed. I can only keep a summary article to constantly remind myself. Indeed, upon careful examination, it is full of hometown flavor~ haha

Walking = shaking legs, knees = knee heads, elbows = elbow hooks, fighting = slapping, cold = freezing, guessing games = finger guessing, crabs = plate sea, dragonflies = little cats, hide and seek = catching strong cats, wife = old lady, husband = old man, inside = throat head, lying = pulling legs / talking nonsense, parrots = en en, slap = ear wax, frogs = cut cats, arguing = pulling tendons, looking = qio, carrying = dia, talking = opening mouth, dressing up = gorgeous, kissing = biting rabbit heads, finished = returning liquid, stopping = killing cuts, restless = Li pulling fire, tires = rollers, often = ring victory, intentional = sharp edge, oh no = oh hop, winning mahjong = cutting, nearly winning = down call, yawning = big open child, straight walking turning = reaching and turning, acting silly = pretending to be crazy, describing something soft = slippery, fat person = mute lump.
