Inter Milan's secret exam of Sneijder's recommended midfield is exposed; the bottom price for the sudden purchase of a star striker exceeds 15 million Euros.

by hlz2012h on 2012-02-29 14:12:25

Inter Milan's secret summer transfer targets exposed: Snider recommends midfielders with price tags exceeding 15 million Euros. "Obviously, Ranieri's position is still stable as he will lead the team against Catania from the bench. Inter Milan has also already set its sights on the summer transfer market for the long term," said All Market in summarizing the news about Inter Milan yesterday. Indeed, given the current situation, Inter Milan is 15 points behind the Serie A leader and 9 points behind third-placed Udinese. They are now even 4 points away from the Europa League zone. Regardless of whether Ranieri will be fired or not, Inter Milan has little hope this season, and the more important task will be to prepare for next season and a new era.

However, compared to Guardiola's potential 20 million Euro annual salary to coach and sign rising stars like Lucas, Schurrle, and Damiao, Inter Milan has made greater progress in pursuing more practical and less attention-grabbing players. Among them is Bolonia midfielder Ramirez, who was rumored to be a target last summer. The young Uruguayan has performed exceptionally well in Serie A over the past two years. His left-wing breakthrough assist led to Di Vaio's first goal against Inter Milan. All Market stated that Inter Milan continues to monitor Ramirez's performance closely. For Moratti, acquiring a young player who excels in Serie A, akin to last year's pursuit of Sanchez, remains a priority. This time, Inter Milan does not want to miss out on their target again.

All Market mentioned another player on Inter Milan's list: Van Wolfswinkel, who currently plays for Sporting Lisbon. Born in 1989, he is one year older than Ramirez. He has scored 7 goals in 16 appearances this season, attracting interest from Real Madrid, Liverpool, and Zenit among others. According to Portuguese media, Wolfswinkel's price tag is not low. Inter Milan is expected to pay at least 15-16 million Euros for him, and if there is competition, the cost could rise to 20 million Euros.

Sky Sports' chief transfer expert Di Marzio mentioned another Dutch name: PSV Eindhoven midfielder Strootman. The 1990-born midfielder is already a member of the Dutch national team and has received high praise from Sneijder. Di Marzio revealed that Inter Milan scouts will travel to England to evaluate Strootman's performance during the Netherlands' match against England at Wembley Stadium. Additionally, Inter Milan officials will assess Sneijder's form in the Dutch national team. Moreover, Di Marzio indicated that "In this context, Inter Milan will also look into De Jong's situation since his contract with Manchester City expires in 2013, giving Inter Milan an opportunity to acquire him this summer." According to British media, De Jong demands a new contract with a weekly salary exceeding 100,000 pounds, but the club disagrees, causing negotiations to stall.

Franco Cosio, who briefly played for Inter Milan, recommended two promising talents from Lecce: Muriel and Guajardo. Both performed well against Inter Milan earlier. Cosio said, "I have high expectations for Guajardo and Muriel." For the current Inter Milan, the transfer strategy has shifted towards the future, but reality remains harsh, necessitating changes across all aspects.
