Zhangjiang Independent Innovation: Building a World-class Science and Technology Park by 2020

by anonymous on 2013-07-24 15:49:14

The Zhangjiang National New Area, which has incorporated 80% of the knowledge economy-intensive areas in Shanghai, has once again become a focus at the planning level after receiving power delegation from the Shanghai Municipality.

Recently, the Ministry of Science and Technology issued the "Development Plan Outline for the Shanghai Zhangjiang National Independent Innovation Demonstration Zone (2013-2020)" (hereinafter referred to as the "Zhangjiang Plan"), clearly specifying that the plan will revolve around building a world-class science park. As a result, Beijing Zhongguancun, Wuhan Donghu, and Shanghai Zhangjiang, the three national independent innovation demonstration zones, have all released their planning outlines with a planning horizon up to 2020. However, relevant individuals from Zhangjiang believe that the relationship among these three places in many industrial layouts is one of cooperation rather than competition.

Cooperation outweighs competition

According to the outline, Zhangjiang will focus on developing five leading industries: next-generation information technology, high-end equipment manufacturing, biomedicine, energy conservation and environmental protection, new materials, as well as two pioneering industries: new energy and new energy vehicles. After reviewing the planning outlines of the three major independent innovation demonstration zones, it was found that electronic information, biomedicine, and energy conservation and environmental protection industries are all included in the industrial layout of the three demonstration zones, and both Beijing's and Wuhan's plans were formulated earlier than Zhangjiang’s.

In terms of regional strategic positioning, Zhangjiang is "an important engine driving the innovative development of Shanghai, the Yangtze River Delta region, and even the entire eastern region," while Donghu will become "a growth pole for the rise of central China," and Zhongguancun will "radiate across the country."

Therefore, there are significant differences in the scale of development by 2020 among these three independent innovation demonstration zones. Zhangjiang's published development goal states that by 2020, total corporate revenue will reach approximately 6 trillion yuan, while Wuhan Donghu aims for 3 trillion yuan, and Zhongguancun targets 10 trillion yuan.

Ma Wengang, Director of the Comprehensive Coordination Department of the Zhangjiang High-Tech Zone Management Committee, told our reporter that compared with other independent innovation zones, Zhangjiang also has its own characteristics at the planning level. The Wuhan Donghu Demonstration Zone is a complete urban development model, which is quite different from Zhangjiang.

Compared with Zhongguancun, the lack of large-scale research institutes is considered a "weakness" in Zhangjiang's exploration of independent innovation. Ma Wengang said that Zhangjiang's 18 parks and more than 90 points are laid out according to Shanghai's urban development axis, thus integrating into the overall layout of Shanghai and radiating across the city through point-to-surface coverage.

Regarding the overlap in industrial layout, Ma Wengang said that the three places have a cooperative rather than competitive relationship in terms of industry. "For example, Huawei has R&D institutions nationwide, utilizing the different resources of each region to integrate them and participate in international competition."

Promoting Shanghai's transformation

The planning of Zhangjiang High-Tech Zone plays a crucial role in shouldering the responsibility of Shanghai's innovation ecosystem. In terms of spatial layout, the area of Zhangjiang High-Tech Zone has increased from 63 square kilometers to 294 square kilometers, and then to more than 470 square kilometers. A notable feature of Zhangjiang High-Tech Zone's planning is that its various blocks are distributed in a dot-like manner across multiple regions of the city.

"The scientific and technological innovation resources gathered in each region not only have strong radiation and driving effects locally, but also promote the overall transformation and development of Shanghai through multi-point cooperation," said Hou Jin, Deputy Director of the Shanghai Zhangjiang New High-Tech Industrial Development Zone Management Committee.

In addition to spatial-level promotion, the pilot testing of institutional levels is more important for Shanghai. "On some issues that may not be completely resolved or broken through overall, or where mature experiences have not yet been achieved, Zhangjiang can play a leading and exemplary role, providing us with very good experience and reference for subsequent follow-up actions," said Hou Jin.

To this end, the Shanghai Municipal Government requires Zhangjiang Demonstration Zone to make breakthroughs in five aspects: equity incentives, science and technology finance, talent experimentation zone, fiscal and tax support, and institutional mechanism innovation.

Taking institutional mechanism innovation as an example, one month before the release of the plan, the Shanghai Municipal Government piloted the delegation of municipal administrative approval powers in Zhangjiang. Currently, the pilot work in the park is ongoing, and the ten offices of the Zhangjiang Management Committee are formulating detailed implementation rules, expected to be released by the end of August.

"Each park will have the authority to receive and approve applications and establish service centers. Once the detailed implementation rules are released, the two can be connected," Ma Wengang revealed to our reporter.
