Memories Soaked by the Snowy Night

This is an online submission.

It's a wonderful winter night, because of the snow.

Under the dim yellow light, the snowflakes are swirling and falling gracefully. Occasionally, cars pass by with their lights on, and the beams cut through the snowy night like sharp knives, then continue cutting into the distant darkness. Under such a curtain of night, I stand alone in front of the window, pulling aside the curtain. A damp cold clings to my entire body, soaking my mood. My fatigue and laziness melt away like ice and snow, and vague feelings begin to rise in my mind.

The process of the snow falling seems like a fragment of memory, clear, bright, and moist. Just like more memories from the past, they flow endlessly towards the horizon in a river beyond memory. These days, over the years, following the routine of life, the weather seems not very important anymore. But nights like this, with snow, always bring back some memories. Many things, many people, and many experiences that were once on the edge of memory gradually become vivid and clear. Some of these episodes have already clearly outlined my fingerprints and heart tracks. This feeling is rarely experienced in daily life.

Downstairs, people walking in the snow make a crunching sound under their shoes. It’s a penetrating sound, one that comes just before breaking. Suddenly, I recall the winter of 2004. In fact, it has been seven years now. When time calms down, I was about to forget those times. If I hadn't occasionally remembered, I think I would have continued to forget in this peaceful and tranquil state of existence, until many things, many people, and many experiences were no longer remembered, or even if remembered, not mentioned again, but slowly integrated into the mundane life, gradually disappearing and decomposing as life grows old and withers.

I often feel compassion for myself and others. When getting food at the cafeteria, I suddenly saw the person who held great power seven years ago and tried to destroy me. He looked old and haggard, his spirit listless, his white hair prominent, his body curled up like an overly worn-out item standing there. To be honest, seeing him evoked some indescribable feelings inside me. Seven years ago, during that winter, he dealt me the severest blow at a critical moment in my life when I was powerless to resist. I could only watch as it came surging at me like a flood; he made me miss out on a lot and lose a lot, making me taste many "realities" of life and depriving me of many harvests and achievements that should have come at this age. He also caused my heart to bleed and get injured, forcing me to accept many absurd social rules in a state of physical and mental exhaustion. For many years after that, I was trapped in endless shadows, unable to extricate myself. I had to compress my thoughts into bundles, never easily showing them to others. It took me exactly seven years to heal from those wounds and build a little confidence in this space. He might be a bit blind and didn't recognize me standing opposite him. He just called out to a child behind him, probably his grandson. At that moment, the child was playing in the dining hall, his expression filled with innocence and curiosity, his eyes full of kindness. I hope I can walk forward and affectionately touch the child's head. Before he was born, there was a huge conflict between me and his grandfather that he knows nothing about, nor will anyone mention these past events to him. Of course, I will also grow old like his grandfather, my flesh will wither, my bones will decay, and many things will be forgotten, and many people will become strangers. In the dining hall, hundreds of people mix together, some faces, some irrelevant greetings, along with the secret heat and smell emitted from the kitchen, are all collected in my memory, chaotic yet exceptionally clear.

In fact, seven years compared to several decades of life is merely a short period, not even worth calling a phase. Therefore, I will never forget some people and things. I am a farmer's son, traditional family education instilled too many simple concepts in me, so at that time, I didn't understand whether there was hatred or contempt in others' eyes, let alone intrigue and deception. I only felt the sincerity among colleagues. When someone called me brother, I actually felt proud. Many years later, I realized how they carefully exploited, harmed, and belittled me back then, but I remained completely unaware, still treating them with heartfelt sincerity. Looking back after seven years, those who were righteous, those who threw stones into the well, those who were mysterious, those who were hypocritical... thinking about it now, they are all like old film reels, slowly panning over, then slowly panning away. The roles certain people played in certain periods seem as clear as yesterday. In these more than 2000 days, I have become estranged from some people repeatedly, and even when we face each other, there is silence. Eventually, even smiles became stiff. I know that the friends and companions I thought would last a lifetime, the friendships I thought would endure forever, the rare bonds I thought would remain hidden deep within, will all dissipate in the winds of time, becoming fragments drifting far away.

Some people say: Life is fluid, never having a fixed scene. There are only difficult or surprising beginnings and unremarkable processes; the ending can even be ignored. Years have passed, countless people and things have faded away, as if they never existed. Now, I am middle-aged. Regarding fame and fortune, gains and losses, honor and decline, life and death in this world, I can face them all calmly. So, when reminiscing about the past today, I always feel somewhat disoriented, somewhat unreal, as if watching a play, where the gongs sounded urgently, the drums thundered loudly, the strings were plucked skillfully, and there were good looks and fine singing voices, shining brightly in the present moment. But even so, it remains a strange self to me, slightly tinged with yellow, with a hoarse voice line, flickering in and out of sight in the passage of time, sometimes present, sometimes absent. In reality, for me, the burden of life has never lessened. Turning around, everything still needs to be faced by myself.

As the snowflakes fly, a new year has arrived again. All the past is gone like water, cool and desolate. People and events of the past seem to be covered by a vast expanse of white snow, and I still look forward to it snowing harder, hoping that in the snowy days, time can rewind: the past, the present, and the future...
