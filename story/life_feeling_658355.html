Expired Promises

In the years of youthful dreams, I met you. In the vast sea of people, even brushing shoulders is a kind of fate. A two-year period of passing by each other was destined to be —— the pain of my entire life. Facing you was like facing a crystal dewdrop, and even with the slightest breeze, I feared losing you.

Even though this love was as fleeting as the bloom of an evening flower, in my heart it seemed like a springtime where a hundred flowers bloomed, making me happy, letting me remember, and allowing me to be grateful. How I wished to keep this love deeply buried in my heart, forever preventing it from reincarnating.

You once said that I never confessed any feelings to you. I don't know if you didn't see it or if it's because you've forgotten, but it seems like I wrote you two lines of poetry in your textbook, clearly expressing my longing and admiration for you between the words. At the time, you seemed deeply moved, saying that someone had actually written poetry for you in this lifetime. Even though this feeling of being moved was short-lived, I still remember it vividly.

You once said that I didn't understand true human emotions, yet I covered the shaded paths with carpets of love; I filled the beams on the walls with calls for you; I cherished your faint fragrance in the "Ghost City." One sheet after another of notes, one blessing after another, continue to this day... In my heart, I can't forget the night before we parted when you secretly slipped out of campus under the moonlit night, repeatedly telling and crying in the quiet little forest... Yes, I gave you an expired promise, which I have yet to fulfill...

As graduation approached, I didn't leave. I was waiting, waiting for you to slowly go away. The empty dormitory, lonely me, carrying that fantasy, packed my belongings and set off on a cold journey home... At that moment, I looked back every few steps, how painful and conflicted my heart was, how reluctant I was to part... And just like that, I gradually transformed from a fiery body into icy pearls, completely losing my temperature. I knew I was powerless. I smiled coldly, sobbed, and slightly lifted the corners of my mouth, as if after a century of hibernation, the savior of forgetting had arrived! Forgotten? No, how could I forget! That once constant, restless attachment always resembles seeds under a large rock, stubbornly sprouting and growing in the softest place of my heart...

More than ten years have passed in the blink of an eye, and I haven't received a single piece of news from you. Back then, there were no mobile phones, no computers. Traversing through cycles of happiness and pain! Perhaps this is also the taste of love! I endured the happiness of love while bearing its pain!

The office was devoid of colleagues; the only sound in the empty office was the hum of the computer host, though light, it annoyingly entered my earphones and pierced through my eardrums. That expired promise was like mosquitoes in the summer, making me restless and uneasy. I struggled to suppress the unnamed agitation within me, angrily striking the keyboard in numbness... Thoughts without dreams are so pale! Such thoughts, without dreams, are quickly packed up and sealed after sadness, leaving behind only the noisy and drowsy journey. In my busyness, I tried to make myself more oppressed, striving to let the work akin to a hermit monk temporarily forget the yearning, despite the pain! Because I know that the future is full of too many thorns, and on this winding upward path, I climb with great difficulty. Perhaps, that affection has become my motivation!
