Apple Samsung patent battle to be retried next Tuesday, damages could be reduced

by anonymous on 2013-11-17 18:35:40

On November 10, Beijing time, according to foreign media reports, last year's "patent war of the century" between Apple and Samsung made a big splash, with many media outlets reporting that the jury had halved the $1 billion in damages that Samsung was ordered to pay to Apple. However, this is not the case; the trial is not over and will be retried next Tuesday.

In March of this year, Judge Lucy Koh, who was responsible for this litigation, reduced the compensation paid by Samsung to Apple by $400 million. In response to Samsung's appeal, Judge Lucy Koh ruled that the damages calculated by the jury for Apple's "losses" were inaccurate and needed to be retried.

Many legal experts have said that the amount of compensation proposed by the new jury may be lower than the previous amount. Of course, the compensation amount may also increase.

One of the problems encountered during the first trial was that the jury had to fill out a 20-page legal and mathematical form. Florian Mueller of FOSS Patents stated that during the second retrial, Samsung's lawyers criticized these forms as being too complicated.

Samsung's representative lawyer said: "Samsung wants to provide the jury with a table that clearly calculates the specific losses for each product. Samsung hopes that the court will require the jury to write down the loss amounts, while also detailing the types of losses."

Apple's lawyers have learned from these lessons. They provided a one-page form that Judge Lucy Koh has already agreed to use.

The third trial of this case will also involve other products released by Samsung in the two and a half years after the case was filed, such as the GS3. It is expected that the third trial will begin in March 2014. (kirk)
