Micro-charity is becoming increasingly popular, bringing us closer to charity.

by edivista on 2011-12-14 09:08:48

Click to enter Sina Weibo

Micro-charity is a new trend that has emerged in the field of charity after the widespread use of microblogs. How to maximize benefits and minimize harm, and use the new technology well to improve the current ecological environment of China's charity sector, are issues worth paying attention to after the emergence of micro-charity.

Looking back at last week's warm currents, in Guyuan, Ningxia, teaching support teachers raised winter clothes for poor children through microblogging and have now received more than 3,000 items; in Shenyang, Liaoning, Liu Yang, a 12-year-old boy suffering from bone cancer, realized his dream of playing chess with a master through microblogging; the benevolent microblog posted by netizen Ai Yao Yaotou, which cared for cleaners and asked to wrap sharp objects in garbage with several layers, was still spreading geometrically last week and was forwarded more than 160,000 times. With a simple forward, love is relayed, and mutual assistance becomes possible. As Internet new technologies develop, micro-charity gradually wins people's hearts.

Micro-charity is a new trend that has emerged in the field of charity after the widespread use of microblogging. It is a form of interactive charity based on the microblogging platform. It gathers small acts of kindness into a warm stream, and with its unique advantages, participates in information release, fundraising, and helping those in need in the charity process.

As a new emerging Internet technology, microblogging has developed extremely rapidly in China. Microblogging also provides another platform for the charity sector with its low threshold, fast dissemination, and open and transparent information. Micro-charity shortens the distance between charity work and people, and social love finds a more convenient way to gather. When people see the microblog about a student in Xinjiang suffering from leukemia who needs help, they can contribute money or effort; when people see the benevolent microblog caring for cleaners, they only need to take a little effort to forward the kindness and popularize common knowledge.

In fact, as early as May last year, the Free Lunch Program of the China Social Welfare Foundation promoted through microblogging forwarding has already raised more than 25 million yuan and implemented free lunch projects in 110 schools in 13 provinces. Currently, micro-charity is no longer limited to charity activities initiated by charity organizations but presents more of an all-people-participating "all-people charity" situation. When people have difficulties, they post microblogs for help. The changes brought by micro-charity to the charity sector are not only reflected in the speed and quantity of annual fundraising but also in the transformation of the public's charitable concept: charity begins to change from being unfamiliar to familiar, from distant to within reach.

The micro-charity with massive aggregation and massive transmission indeed provides opportunities for people in need to seek help. But currently, due to the freedom and randomness of the microblogging platform, there are some problems with micro-charity. While many regions implement love banks and public welfare feedback to ensure volunteer enthusiasm, some false requests for help mixed in micro-charity not only hurt the love of reposters but also damage the enthusiasm of volunteers. Another key link, fundraising, has not yet formed a standard, and relying solely on the self-discipline of organizers may not completely win the trust of socially loving individuals.

How to maximize benefits and minimize harm, amplify the positive power of micro-charity, requires not only calling on a large number of microblog users to adhere to the moral bottom line of integrity but also relevant departments to pay attention to micro-charity as a new charity platform, improve its supervision mechanism for donations and fundraising, and cultivate a good atmosphere for civilian charity. Good use of new technology to improve the current ecological environment of China's charity sector is an issue worth paying attention to after the emergence of micro-charity. (Shen Xiaogen)

Yang Kun

Although his grades were not very good, he is still a good student in our eyes. No one would have thought that such a shy boy could act so manfully in a critical moment. On December 4th, in a car accident in Gaochun, Jiangsu, sophomore Yang Kun not only promptly helped the elderly injured in the car accident but also stopped the hit-and-run offender. The school plans to give Yang Kun an award to encourage his good intentions.

Dialogue Between Kneeling Life and Standing Death

On December 5th, in Zhoushan, Zhejiang, a man on the top of a tall building attempting suicide made everyone watching feel terrified. At the scene, a male beggar with a leg disability kept shouting to the man on the building, persuading him not to commit suicide. This dialogue between kneeling life and standing death moved many netizens, and he thus became the beggar negotiator expert in the hearts of netizens.

Volunteer Incentive Mechanism

Good deeds should also be rewarded. To encourage love, many places have begun to explore long-term incentive mechanisms for volunteer services: Henan has opened a Love Bank where volunteers can store their love bit by bit and withdraw help when needed; Beijing's Shijingshan District pilots public welfare feedback, and community five-star volunteers aged 70 and above can receive a free home service project once a month before turning 80.
