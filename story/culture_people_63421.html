First Question

******************

I look in the mirror, who is the person in the mirror?

******************

I walk in front of someone's eyes, who is in that person’s eyes? When I say "I," who am I referring to?

What I see with my eyes, what I hear with my ears, and what I speak with my mouth, which one is truly me?

None of these are me, so why should I care about them?

The mirror needs me, people's eyes need me, and my own mouth needs me, so I fulfill these needs and preserve an intact version of myself.

But if I merely exist in the mirror, barely survive in others' eyes, and satisfy only what comes from my own mouth, then I truly lose myself.

When I search for myself, do I go into the mirror, into other people's eyes, or into my own words to find it?

My ideals, my needs, my friends, my crying, my laughter, my anger, my scolding—

Are they really

my ideals, my needs, my friends, my crying, my laughter, my anger, my scolding?

I look in the mirror, who is the person in the mirror?

Second Question

*****************

Who am I in my heart?

*****************

Is the 'me' I think of in my heart truly me?

Is the 'me' in my heart like the 'me' in the mirror, in others' eyes, or in my own words?

Is the 'me' in my heart formed by countless mirrors, each creating an image, and through these images, locating this 'me'?

If this is indeed the case, then the 'me' in my heart does not truly exist.

At any time or place, under any circumstances, I am merely the reflection of things; without those things, there would be no me.

Third Question

***************************

Does forgetting both self and objects mean reaching a state where there is neither self nor objects?

***************************

How can one forget both self and objects?

Outer = Inner

That must surely be a long process.

Forgetting both self and objects...

Do such people really exist?

In the world of mirrors. Every morning when you open your eyes, the entire world is made of mirrors—mirrors you can see, mirrors you can hear, mirrors you can touch, mirrors you can taste...

Buddhism says, holding onto one thought eliminates all other thoughts. To let go of even that one thought requires another thought—how can one let go?

If neither self nor objects are forgotten, but rather reflect each other harmoniously?

Fourth Question

***************

Am I just a mere illusion?

***************

Birth is the transformation from non-self to small self.

Until now, as I type here, I seem to be a relatively complete large self.

I am not just one illusion.

I am millions of illusions, accumulated day after day, layered upon layers, forming a colorful, complex, vast yet insignificant, beautiful yet ugly, smart yet clumsy... collection of illusions.

Self and objects, the current line of thinking has become much clearer.

A simple correspondence relationship.

Objects are fundamental; without objects, there is no self.

I am the collection of illusions of objects, integrating various illusions to form a seemingly complete world, known as a worldview.

And does human existence merely serve to carry or large or small worldviews?

Or perhaps standing at the height of humanity, integrating the worldviews of hundreds of millions of people, ultimately will this worldview reach beyond the universe?

If, like in The Matrix, outside of the outside, there is still more outside...

It is truly difficult to find a reason for the existence of "me."

I look in the mirror, who is the person in the mirror?

Fifth Question

*******************************************

How do objects and self combine within a physical body?

*******************************************

Longing, eternity, attachment, loss

These have already transcended simple illusions; calling them human instincts...

Where do instincts come from?

People are born with instincts—they cry, they eat.

Do these instincts belong to illusions, or are they objects, or something else entirely?

I must be a mirror.

Instincts existed before I was born; are these instincts mirrors? I think so. They are illusions based on heredity. Ancestors, ancestors of ancestors...inherited from there.

There is no choice in the inherited illusions, and individual differences arise from this.

Illusions acquired later in life can be chosen, but it is difficult to shake the foundation of innate illusions. Perhaps there is a way to change them, but the result might be a lifetime of madness.

Still, there are only objects and self, nothing else.

It is commonly believed that inheritance belongs to the realm of genes, a change in objects.

If "me" can be inherited...

Genetic knowledge is too complex, but I imagine the process of inheriting that "me" is perhaps like how raising a cup changes its position—it changes due to the needs of "me."

It is the interaction between "me" and objects.
