A flower is a world, a moment is eternity. A full-length novel is strategically pointing at the rivers and mountains; a mid-length novel is tactically arranging troops for battle; a short story is combat-like close-quarter skirmish; while an extremely brief micro-narrative is like encountering an ambush in a narrow alley, or being attacked in the restroom or kitchen - a close-range fight with bare-handed grappling. Writing micro-fiction is akin to the thrusting finger technique of Wing Chun Kung Fu: the shorter the distance, the stronger the force; it's also like ancient poetry, where words are few but emotions run deep. In the fast-paced rhythm of modern life, Wen Rui'an attempts to use the least amount of words to recapture the most exhilarating feelings, amidst the bustling red dust of worldly affairs, listening to sounds with a flick of the finger, seizing fleeting moments of brilliance.
