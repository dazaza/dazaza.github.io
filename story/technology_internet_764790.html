Google Invites American Film Students to Make Movies with Google Glass

by anonymous on 2013-08-06 18:46:10

Google is exploring the potential uses of Google Glass. Recently, Google sent out invitations to five film-related colleges in the U.S., inviting students to shoot films using Google Glass.

These five universities are the American Film Institute, California Institute of the Arts, Rhode Island School of Design, University of California Los Angeles, and University of Southern California. As part of this initiative, Google will loan three pairs of Google Glass to each university.

Google stated that after the summer break ends and school resumes, they will report to the public on the results achieved by these film students using Google Glass to shoot films.

Google mentioned that the invited universities and film students will explore shooting documentaries with Google Glass, character development, location-based storytelling, and attempt methods not yet seen in traditional film industries.

Professor Norman Hollyn from the School of Cinematic Arts at USC said that students would be encouraged to shoot films from a first-person perspective. Hollyn introduced that the students would use a technique similar to Mike Figgis's "Timecode," which used four cameras to simultaneously capture images of four characters to drive the story forward.

Hollyn added that students could also use the data overlay function of Google Glass to display important information in the movie. He said that students from USC would complete at least two short films using Google Glass.

It was reported that Google Glass can record 720P HD video, and the recording can be initiated through voice commands or touch gestures.

Previously, Google had already showcased the filming and movie-making capabilities of Google Glass, such as a physics teacher visiting the Large Hadron Collider at CERN with Google Glass, and tennis star Bethanie Mattek-Sands documenting her preparation for Wimbledon using Google Glass.
