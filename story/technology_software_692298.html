Dreams are not related to auspiciousness, inauspiciousness, misfortune or fortune. They are often caused by people's memories during the day. It is like "dreaming at night of what you think about during the day". Hehe, dreams are formed by the unconscious brain linking information within the brain in a disorderly way. Some of this information may have been long forgotten and sits on the edge of your memory but can still be recalled. It's quite amazing. In reality, however, the vast majority of dreams cannot predict reality. If we say that dreams can foresee facts, that this premonition can be interpreted, and that such gifted individuals actually exist, I can tell you that 99.99999% of people will never encounter such an extraordinary person even once in their lifetime.

Many people have this kind of trouble: after falling asleep, they often have nightmares, and sometimes even wake up startled. Due to poor sleep, they feel dizzy and disoriented the next day, which affects their work and study. Some even worry that nightmares will bring them bad luck, causing psychological fear and unease. In fact, dreaming is a normal psychological phenomenon that occurs while a person is sleeping. Under normal circumstances, when a person sleeps, the neurons in the brain are all in a suppressed state. This suppression process is sometimes complete, and sometimes incomplete. If it is not entirely in a suppressed state, there are still some areas of the cerebral cortex where neurons are active, and this is when dreams appear. Because the activity of these few cells loses the control and regulation of the entire cerebral cortex that exists in the state of wakefulness, certain fragments of memory become lively without restriction, manifesting as strange and bizarre dreams different from normal psychological activities. At this time, if the neurons associated with language and movement are also in an excited state, then not only will dreams appear, but muttering in one's sleep or sleepwalking may also occur.

So how can we reduce nightmares? To reduce negative stimuli, we should watch more healthy, beneficial, light-hearted, and joyful movies, TV shows, or novels, and watch or read less or try to avoid films or novels that easily form nightmare scenarios, avoiding storing harmful stimuli in our memory. Before sleeping, it's best not to overuse your brain, so as to prevent excessive excitement of the cerebral cortex which could trigger dreams.

Pay attention to your sleeping posture. Generally, it is better to sleep on your right side. When sleeping on your back, your hands and feet should naturally hang vertically, and your pillow should not be too high. You should correct the bad habit of sleeping face down. I hope this can help you and wish you sweet dreams!
