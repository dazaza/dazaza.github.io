Plot Introduction of It turns out that I am not handsome:

Since he can remember, "Zhuang" has always thought of himself as a handsome guy, and this belief is not entirely unfounded. From childhood to adulthood, whether relatives, elders, food stall owners, questionnaire girls, ticket scalpers, or classmates borrowing notes, everyone sincerely calls him "handsome guy." So, naturally, he believes he is indeed handsome... The person who awakens Zhuang from this handsome dream is none other than his two classmates and best friends, "Si Si," a quirky roommate full of humor and odd ideas, and the vice president of the Pigeon Club whose only confidant is an old pigeon named Wang Lao. "A Kang," a self-proclaimed suave womanizer and also the founder and president of the Pigeon Research Society, loves to be Zhuang's love analyst. What drives Zhuang crazy is that following his advice never seems to work right! In fact, Zhuang's connection with "Cindy" started early, even before they entered university. They once had an imperfect encounter. For Cindy, Zhuang even joined the Foodie Club. Then Zhuang gathered all his courage to confess his feelings to Cindy, only to hear her coldly reject him with a simple "no." Feeling as if he had been shot in the chest, Zhuang struggled to ask why. Cindy looked at him and said, "Because you are too ugly." In that moment, it felt like another bullet hit him.

With the addition of the beautiful "Qiao Ping" into the triangular equation of Cindy and Zhuang's complicated relationship, Si Si, already in a relationship with the eccentric "Pin Xuan," no longer has time to care about Zhuang. Meanwhile, A Kang encounters the hardest love triangle of his life, unable to help Zhuang due to his own issues with the clingy "Man Man." All three find themselves caught in emotional dilemmas, weaving a super romantic campus love comedy filled with laughter through tears and exhaustion.
