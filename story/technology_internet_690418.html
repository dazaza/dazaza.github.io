61 kg of flour hard to reclaim: On the afternoon of the 22nd, through the Hutun Town government, the reporter contacted the Hutun Finance Office. A staff member who claimed to have worked there for over ten years stated that they had never heard of this matter and did not understand it.

On the morning of the 21st, www.keda888.com, the reporter went to the home of villager Sun Teng (a pseudonym) in Hutun Town, Taian Feicheng. He boldly took out a yellowed passbook from a plastic bag. The reporter saw that on the cover of the passbook was written "Hutun Grain Depot, Feicheng City Grain Bureau" and "Reserve Grain Passbook," with an issuance date of "May 7, 1995." "A few days ago, while tidying up the household accounts, I found this passbook. I don't know if the grain can still be retrieved, nor do I know where to go to collect it," said Sun Teng.

In the afternoon of the 21st, the reporter went to the original location of the Hutun Grain Depot. To the west of the square in front of the Hutun Town government, several newly built residential buildings were particularly noticeable.

Sun Teng said that he took the grain passbook to the depot to inquire and found that the original site had been transformed into a residential building, and the depot had been reformed into an oil and grain company, leaving him somewhat confused.

The warehouse turned into a residential building

On the first page of the passbook, in the upper left corner, it says "special grade two flour." The data shows that Sun Teng deposited "336 kg" on May 7, 1995.

The finance office is unaware of this matter.

The former director of the Hutun Grain Depot, Director Zhai, told the reporter that in those years, the Feicheng municipal government decided that the grain depot would undergo enterprise reform. The transfer payment for the farmer-stored grain would be allocated by the local finance office to the grain depot, which would then pay the farmers. However, the Hutun finance office never paid this money. "Not just the Hutun town grain depot, but all towns have similar situations, which are historical legacy issues," said Director Zhai. He has reported this many times to the Hutun Town government and the finance office but has not received results.

On the 21st, Sun Teng (pseudonym) showed his old passbook to the reporter.

Text/photo by reporter Cao Jian

The staff of the Feicheng City Grain Bureau said that the villagers' passbooks are still valid, but the grain depot does not have the ability to pay and needs to wait for the finance office to allocate funds.

The grain depot said to wait for the allocation.

The reporter saw that after May 7, 1995, approximately every month, 25 kg was withdrawn. Each column recorded the remaining amount and had the signature stamp of the handler. Until October 6, 1997, after withdrawing 25 kg, there were no other records. At this point, there were still 61 kg of flour remaining in the passbook. Sun Teng said that each time the flour was eaten, he would go back to withdraw more. As for why there are still 61 kg of flour remaining, Sun Teng forgot what happened.

An informed villager told the reporter that the original Hutun Grain Depot has now become an oil and grain company nearby. The reporter immediately went to this oil and grain company, where the full name was written at the entrance: Feicheng Jinhai Oil and Grain Trade Co., Ltd. The gatekeeper elder told the reporter that this is the reformed Hutun Grain Depot, with the same responsible person. In the courtyard, the reporter saw that the company only had four or five offices, all locked. The elder said that these offices are usually idle, mainly used when someone comes to the town for meetings or when someone comes to study and uses them as a temporary stopover.

The grain depot reform became a company.

In the afternoon of the 21st, at the Feicheng City Grain Bureau, a staff member surnamed Du told the reporter, www.binglong.net, that in 2005, the Hutun Grain Depot underwent reform. Due to a deficit, the grain depot did not have the ability to repay the villagers' stored grain, but the Feicheng municipal government decided to convert the owed portion of grain into money based on the market price of that year, to be paid by the finance bureau.

An old passbook was found at home.

In Taian Feicheng Hutun Town, a villager's home has a reserve grain passbook that has been kept for 16 years, with 61 kg of flour remaining unclaimed. Can this flour still be retrieved? On the 21st, the reporter learned from the Feicheng Grain Bureau that this passbook is still valid. The Hutun Grain Depot underwent reform in 2005, and the stored grain could be converted into money based on the price of that year, but due to the fiscal allocation not yet arriving, it cannot be paid out.
