Mini electric vehicle invented by citizen can be folded into the size of a laptop (Photo)

by zzf0zxyh00 on 2011-06-22 10:43:12

Wang Demin's electric bike is very easy to ride (the small picture shows the folded electric bike) ■ Pictures in this article were taken by reporter Wang Ye of our newspaper.

The dimensions of the folded electric bike are 48cm x 37cm x 10cm, comparable to a laptop computer, weighing 27 jin (approximately 13.5 kg), and is convenient to carry.

Hua Shang Morning Post News (Reporter Yang Wei) - Can you believe that an electric bike can be folded into the size of a laptop using just seven hinges?

"Although it's much thicker than a laptop, it can absolutely fit into a small leather suitcase without any problems," said Wang Demin, a 60-year-old resident of Shenyang who invented the foldable electric bike, excitedly yesterday.

He hopes his invention can change and improve people’s lives: "If it can go into mass production one day, I would prefer it to be produced in our own factories in Shenyang."

Tired of having his bikes stolen

This time he made one that couldn’t be stolen

Wang Demin is an ordinary resident of Shenhe District in Shenyang. He, like his neighbors, has faced the same trouble—bicycles and electric bikes left outside always get stolen.

"Counting them up, I’ve lost six bicycles and one electric bike. Honestly, I'm scared of losing bikes now," said Wang in May 2009 when his niece's bike was also stolen. This time, Wang was determined: "Uncle will make you one that definitely won't get stolen."

Just after Labor Day, Wang began preparing materials, studying how to make an environmentally friendly and theft-proof electric bike. He believed that only keeping it close to himself would ensure its safety, so he decided to create a foldable and portable electric bike.

To reduce weight, he replaced the regular battery with a lithium battery.

For compactness and lightness, he modified old car rims and bought 20-centimeter diameter small tires, assembling two wheels himself.

To enable the bike to fold, he designed several plans. Steel plates were cut and welded repeatedly, costing over 1000 yuan in welding fees alone, until he finally came up with the idea of using hinges as folding pivots.

He conducted folding experiments on the handlebars, seat bracket, fork support bracket, pedals, and other parts. Eventually, he managed to fold the electric bike using only seven hinges. "I experimented with the footrests for over a month, repeatedly verifying how to install them to ensure the rider's safety," he said.

After folding, the electric bike weighs only 27 jin, measuring 48 cm long, 10 cm wide, and 37 cm high. Because current regulations require electric bikes to not exceed 20 km/h, Wang's foldable electric bike has a maximum speed of no more than 20 km/h. However, a single charge still allows it to travel 50 km.

Test rider weighed 200 jin

Electric bike moved smoothly

In October 2009, Wang Demin's foldable electric bike was finally completed. However, when he first rode it, he was flipped off. "Honestly, it was quite a blow. After half a year of research, I ended up flipping the bike and getting crushed under it," he said.

But Wang did not lose heart. He realized the problem was due to the rear-wheel drive, so he modified the bike to have front-wheel drive.

By mid-December 2009, the improved bike was once again carried downstairs by Wang, and this time it felt stable when ridden.

He also conducted tests, inviting a neighbor weighing approximately 200 jin to sit on the bike, which could still move smoothly.

Wang Demin said: "Not only can it fold, but I also installed a belt on the main frame, acting as a handle. This way, as long as the bike is folded, it can be easily picked up and carried."

When Wang lifted the bike out, it looked similar to a laptop in shape, though slightly thicker than a regular laptop.

It took Wang less than five seconds to unfold the bike. He said this vehicle produces no exhaust while driving, meeting energy-saving and emission-reduction requirements.

Wang is very satisfied with his invention and applied for a patent with the Intellectual Property Office of the People's Republic of China on December 24, 2009. He has already received the patent acceptance notice.

Feasibility Discussion

High price of lithium batteries may limit sales

Qu Peng, the manager of a professional electric bike manufacturing and sales company in Shenyang, believes that if mass-produced, there might be issues limiting the consumer base. The market for lithium batteries isn't particularly good because they are relatively expensive, costing about 1500 yuan per unit, and generally need replacement every two to three years. Therefore, the overall selling price tends to be higher. After the opening of Shenyang Metro, foldable bicycles or such foldable electric bikes may have a better market.

Would consider buying if the price is reasonable

Yesterday, reporters randomly interviewed 20 citizens on the street, all of whom showed interest in Wang Demin's electric bike upon seeing it.

Citizen Ms. Li said: "If the price is similar to the electric bikes currently available on the market, I would consider buying one. It's convenient! Opening and folding it isn't difficult at all, charging is easy, and it suits people riding to and from work." Reporter Yang Wei
