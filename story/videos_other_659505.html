To retain talent, career prospects are more important than high salaries.

by bhhgsyfk4143 on 2012-02-06 12:51:45

It is definitely impossible to prohibit headhunting companies from contacting executives, as these headhunters often "leave no stone unturned." The best way for a company is to keep its core employees closely united. There are two essential conditions for retaining talents: one is high salary, and the other is a good working environment.

In my personal opinion, the simplest and most effective method is to give these talents a raise, because salary is the most direct reflection of an individual's value. Our company's salaries are often higher than those in the industry. Moreover, the annual salaries of executives in our company are confidential; if competitors knew them, they might tempt talents to switch jobs with even higher salaries.

The second factor is the working environment. A harmonious atmosphere is essential for retaining talents. Therefore, we also do some warm-hearted things, such as buying toys for employees' children on Children's Day, giving small gifts on birthdays, etc. Our senior executives at the president level regularly hold discussions with their subordinates to understand their recent thoughts. Ultimately, we want them to feel that their boss and superiors are paying attention to them.

If one of my key personnel wants to leave, we will naturally try to retain him, and even the chairman may step in to persuade him to stay. But the most important thing is to establish a complete mechanism so that when a talent leaves, a new person can immediately take over his work, ensuring the normal operation of the company. Opposite the factory, there are two rows of townhouses, each worth more than 2 million yuan. High-end talents in our company can be allocated one set. At the same time, we also provide cars for these high-end talents, all priced above 500,000 yuan. Salary is also very important. Last year, Guo Hong proposed a plan for an annual salary of 1 million yuan. High-end talents could receive a basic annual salary of 1 million yuan at Guo Hong and could also be allocated a certain amount of shares based on their contribution rates.

In addition to material guarantees, there are also "warm services" like birthday greetings to enhance employees' sense of belonging. High-end talents even have their own kitchen. For example, some executives are from the north and are not used to eating spicy food, so we prepare special meals for them according to their preferences.

To prevent job-hopping, it is crucial to respect talents and create a relaxed environment. Some executives jump ship for higher salaries, while others seek a bigger stage. For business owners, how to prevent executives from leaving? What thoughts do you have on this topic that you can share with everyone?

Many bosses believe that raising salaries can retain talents, which might work for a small portion of people. However, for executives earning more than 300,000 yuan annually, a raise may not necessarily be effective.

Nowadays, many entrepreneurs have studied MBA or EMBA courses. They know that talents are a scarce resource, crucial for the development of the enterprise, and they should treat talents with respect. However, since most of these bosses started their careers in sales, they excel in business but lack experience interacting with professional managers, making it difficult for them to truly show respect for talents in details.

For instance, a company hires a professional manager at a high salary, but since the boss is more familiar with his subordinates, he directly asks them about matters instead of going through the professional manager. This shows disrespect to the professional manager, who may think that since the boss bypasses him to ask subordinates directly, his position in the company becomes dispensable. This easily leads to doubts. Additionally, many bosses know how to give raises, provide cars, and send gifts to talents, but they don't support their work. For example, a professional manager submits many proposals to the boss hoping to address current issues in the company, but they end up ignored. Professional managers have ambitions that cannot be realized, and they often leave when headhunting companies come knocking.

There was once such a case where a company generated over 1 billion yuan in annual revenue, involving industries like mining and real estate. At that time, we wanted to recruit the director of the human resources department of that company. During the exchange, the director said that when the boss hired him, he placed great importance on his work and hoped he would bring new ideas and vitality to the company. However, a few weeks later during a senior management meeting, the boss said, "Based on my years of experience, a strong marketing department is the most important. What does the human resources department matter?" Although it was an unintentional comment by the boss, for subordinates, it represents non-recognition of their work. Over time, these talents naturally develop thoughts of job-hopping.

I believe the best way to prevent executives from leaving or being poached is to make the company stronger. If someone reaches the top level, salary is not the first priority for him; what he thinks about the most is a bigger stage.

In our company, many high-end talents who join us are unwilling to leave, mainly because Guo Hong can offer them a platform to showcase their abilities. Therefore, the most important thing is still to enhance the capabilities of the enterprise and use the cause to retain people.

Support and tolerance are the keys to retaining talents. High-end talents joining a company usually don't just do it for a living; more importantly, they value whether the company can offer them more opportunities and a larger stage.

As a boss, first, you should support their actions in the company and give them a relaxed working environment. Second, you need to be tolerant. When these talents encounter failure or make mistakes, entrepreneurs need to tolerate them. For example, if a R&D staff designs a product that isn't popular in the market, the boss shouldn't scold him but rather help him find the root cause together and encourage him to keep trying.

From outside perspectives, Li Zhongbin, a councilor of the Chinese Human Resources Development Research Association, believes that material rewards such as cars and houses might impress talents temporarily, but it's hard to retain them for long. Because for corporate executives, what they probably don't lack is money. Today, you can give him a 300-square-meter villa, but tomorrow when the competitor offers him a 500-square-meter villa, what then? Furthermore, when a person's salary and material conditions reach a certain level, his pursuits extend beyond material needs, focusing more on his status in the industry and the sense of accomplishment gained from work. "For these employees, instead of giving gifts or raises, bosses should support them in pursuing their careers and let them feel that you can offer them a stage to showcase their values. And this stage is what the opponent truly cannot imitate or surpass," Li Zhongbin said.
