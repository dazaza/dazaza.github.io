The practice of silent reading has different requirements at different stages: In the first and second grades, students are required to learn silent reading, not to read aloud or point while reading; in the third and fourth grades, they initially learn silent reading. Learn reading methods and cultivate reading ability. Cultivate students' interest in reading and develop good reading habits. Primary school students study language mainly through the perception and accumulation of language, rather than rational analysis. This is determined by the age and psychological characteristics of primary school students. Primary school students are good at memorization and image thinking. Let them have extensive contact with standardized language and literature works, forming a rich perceptual understanding of their mother tongue. This is suitable for the psychological characteristics of primary school students studying Chinese. Many excellent articles and excerpts may not be fully understood by primary school students, and they may not have the ability to appreciate the unique aspects of word choice and structure in the text. However, it is not difficult for them to read and recite proficiently. If they can read and recite proficiently, the language of the text will become their own language, becoming their reserve and wealth. Once used, it will naturally come to mind, greatly improving their ability to use language. Reading aloud is an important way to stimulate students' interest in reading, promote understanding, cultivate linguistic intuition, and improve aesthetic ability. Heidegger said: "Language is human life activity." It can be seen that only through reading aloud can the humanistic imagination of the work be reproduced, the humanistic connotation of the work be tasted, and the humanistic quality of students be improved. Only through repeated reading aloud can the emotions of the text penetrate into the hearts of students, improving their sensitivity to language. In teaching, we should consciously guide students to accumulate words and sentences through reading aloud. In the first semester of the first grade, students just enter the stage of primary school learning. They recognize few characters and have a small vocabulary. When studying texts, we consciously accumulate some distinctive words. For example, in the article "Little Creek", there are "slippery" and "shiny". I guide students to accumulate through repeated reading by creating scenarios. The teacher says: "Winter has come, and the little creek has turned into a skating rink. What would you do if you came to the mountains at this time?" The student answers: "I would go skating." The teacher says: "Yes, children can skate now, how happy! Come on, read these two words well." By the second grade, students have a certain amount of character recognition. According to the learning requirements of second-grade students, accumulating commonly used words is the main focus. However, when teaching texts, if we encounter some relatively beautiful written language, we might as well let the students accumulate it. For example, in the article "The Fairyland of Jiuzhaigou", there are many good words like "colorful", "gorgeous", "cute", "towering ancient trees", "rare animals", "poetic and picturesque". During teaching, we can guide students to understand through colorful pictures and various forms of reading (such as competitive reading, game reading), helping students establish a preliminary concept of words. Then let the students use the words they understand most clearly to make a sentence. From reading aloud to application, the accumulation of students can be considered solid and in place. The article "Pomegranate" makes a detailed description of the shape of the pomegranate. Although pomegranates are fruits familiar to students in their daily lives, it's not easy for them to express it accurately with language. Therefore, I let the students underline the sentences describing the pomegranate in class, read and discuss them. After class, they can also memorize and remember them. This way, when students see pomegranates in the future, they will introduce them using the sentences accumulated in class, which also lays a foundation for future writing.
