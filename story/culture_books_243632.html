When life is everything you can give to the one you love, how can you not give your life for them? For Bella, after falling irrevocably in love with Edward, life becomes both a sweet fantasy and a deep, terrible nightmare. No matter what choice she makes, it will affect the fate of two races. From the moment they first held hands, Bella and Edward understood that they were each other's other half, their love warming their souls like sunlight after rain. Happiness feels warm, yet also makes one feel as if walking on thin ice. The forbidden love between these two people from different worlds torments their fragile hearts like a frenzied storm. Once Bella bravely makes her decision, a series of unprecedented astonishing events unfold. In this turbulent year, temptation and conflict will lead her to the final critical moment. Where will Bella's swirling heart finally settle? What will be the ultimate fate of her, Edward, and Jacob? Like the dawn breaking after a long night, this breathtaking, twisting romance finally comes to a surprising, breath-taking conclusion.

Part 1: Bella

Everything I've endured goes far beyond those near-death experiences, and this isn't something people can get used to.

Death came again, and strangely enough, this time it seemed inevitable. It was as if I really was a synonym for disaster. I had escaped the clutches of death many times before, but death kept coming back for me.

However, this time was completely different from before.

You can run away from the person you fear, you can fight to the death with the person you hate. All my reactions were merely adaptations to dealing with various kinds of murderers—those beasts, those enemies.

But when you deeply love the person who is preparing to take your life, you have no choice left. When doing so would only hurt the person you love, how can you escape? How can you resist? If life is everything you can give to the person you love, how could you possibly hold back?

What if he is the person you truly love?

Part 2: Jacob

I suddenly lunged forward.

The moment my fist made contact with him, his nose gave a satisfying crack. He tried to grab me, but before he could, I quickly hopped away, the crumpled bag of Doritos already in my left hand.

"You broke my nose, you idiot."

"Just between us two, right, Paul?"

I went over to pick up the chips. When I turned around, Paul was adjusting the position of his nose so it wouldn’t become deformed. The bleeding had stopped; at first, blood had flowed down his lips and dripped off his chin as if it had no source. His face twitched involuntarily as he cursed under his breath while pinching the cartilage of his nose.

"You're such a pain, Jacob. I swear, I'd rather play with Riley."
