Once the Most Beautiful

On a sunny afternoon in April, willow catkins floated into the classroom and landed on the randomly opened pages of my book. I suddenly thought of your QQ signature. Just like you once said, one day after waking up from a nap, you sat by the window peeling an orange, and in the faint sound of the orange peel tearing apart, you suddenly thought of me.

This sudden longing proves that we cannot forget each other. However, we also failed to support each other through life. Like two clumps of flying willow catkins, we were once entangled together, then separated in the wind, continuing to drift alone.

You are in the south, and I am in the north. This is our fifth year of being apart.

Five years ago, you told me that the four years of university would be the most glorious four years of our long lives. After that, with the increase in age, we would inevitably fall into mediocrity and decay. At that time, I laughed, with an attitude of disdain —— my usual disdain for your pessimism, which I later realized concealed a permissive sentiment.

I believe the best is always at the end, and we will leave this world in the brightest glory. So I always regarded your pessimism as a little girl wearing her mother's high heels, self-pityingly playing the role of a graceful maturity.

However, the best time you think is about to end.

You always laugh at me, laughing at my arrogance. You say, fate is never within our control. In every journey, what scenery we pass and who we meet are all pushed by an invisible hand in the unknown. I used to be like you, letting go in this sense of helplessness, and deeply self-pitying for my own insignificance and the intangible future.

But now, I believe that I can have the power to counteract that invisible hand to some extent. My close relationship with him is proof.

At first, he came in from the back door of a 200-person lecture hall, his tall figure caught my eye, then he quietly sat among the crowd.

During the break, he talked and laughed naturally with the surrounding classmates, exuding confidence in his every move, as if everything was under his control; in the evening in the dormitory corridor, he passed my dormitory door while dribbling a basketball, leaving behind a string of hearty and cheerful laughter.

I looked up at his sun-like brilliance in the shadows, not quite sure whether I admired the shining him or the imagined version of myself. I only knew that I desired to get closer to him.

I signed up for his elective course and joined the club activities he participated in, so naturally, among other strangers, we could have more exchanges because we were in the same department. I tempted him to discuss deeper topics with me than ordinary classmates, such as love, attitudes towards life, and buried memories from adolescence.

This was a strategy I had planned for a long time because I knew that when someone confides in you their deepest secrets, you occupy an irreplaceable place in their heart.

And just like that, he fell into the trap I designed, and became my intimate good brother.

Look, isn't this the harvest I gained with my sincere scheming beyond fate? And precisely because of this, I cherish this rare relationship even more and carefully maintain it.

I hope his sunshine will illuminate me and lead me out of the dark corner.

Alright, you've complained multiple times that I've told you too much about my admiration and affection for him, so I'll stop here. Because you'll soon meet him.

During the May Day holiday, I'll go back to his hometown in the south, and the train on the way back will pass through the city where you are. Then I'll take him to see you, and you'll understand why I talk about him to you so incessantly.
