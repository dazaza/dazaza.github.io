Of course, what is more disappointing is that investment institutions are pessimistic about the prospects of the group buying industry.

by beetgk2958 on 2012-02-29 11:58:53

The rise of a thing is vigorous, and the decline is sudden. No one can escape the law of nature, and the group-buying industry is no exception. Today, the website of TuangouBao is experiencing a vicious circle: the company has no money, so it delays paying employees and merchants, which leads to employee departure, branch closure, and merchants' loss of trust in the group-buying platform. As a result, merchants no longer accept consumers' consumption, and thus fewer consumers come to TuangouBao, which makes TuangouBao even more short of money. This cycle repeats.

Thinking about the current situation of TuangouBao delaying employees' wages and merchants' payments, the CEO Ren Chunlei's claim in 2011 that he would spend 550 million yuan on advertising seems extremely crazy. Now, he owes nearly 1 million yuan in goods payments to registered merchants alone.

The group-buying industry only focuses on crazily investing in advertisements and burning money, forgetting such consequences: too many celebrity endorsements and bombarding advertisements actually dilute the brand effect of the advertisements to some extent.

Recent data released by the shopping search engine Yitao shows that as of the end of November 2011, more than 35% of domestic group-buying websites have not updated their products for 30 days. The number of group-buying websites that stopped updating, underwent website redesign, or closed down in that month approached 2000. Yitao predicts that in the three months before and after the Spring Festival, especially during the Spring Festival, the wave of group-buying website closures will further intensify, with at least 70% of group-buying websites shutting down or being forced to transform.

It can be said that the bursting of the group-buying bubble is another lesson from a game or a dream.

On February 7, Ren Chunlei, who promised to repay the money on Weibo, ultimately did not appear in the company, which disappointed many employees and merchants who came to chase overdue salaries and payments. Of course, what is even more disappointing is the investment institutions' dim view of the prospects of the group-buying industry.

In the spring of the Year of the Rabbit, it was warm; in the Year of the Dragon, it was extremely cold. In just one year, the group-buying industry went from the hottest industry on the Internet to an industry being watched and sighed at, creating the shortest-lived bubble in Internet history. In terms of the speed of its burst, it surpassed blogs.

I do not know whether Ren Chunlei and others now regret the huge advertising investment last year, but one thing is certain: even if they still have such funds in hand now, the grandeur of last year cannot be repeated. Because currently, the growth of China's group-buying consumer market has stalled, and the difficulty of financing has further increased. "Burn money" cannot continue, and most front-line group-buying websites face more capital-related challenges.

"When God wants to destroy someone, He first drives them mad."

From the perspective of overall transaction volume, the transaction amount of the group-buying market decreased by 11% month-on-month in October last year, and the total transaction amount of physical goods and life service groups was only on par with June.

People with good memory should still retain beautiful memories of the prosperity of group-buying at the beginning of last year. At that time, the number of China's group-buying websites exceeded 5000, becoming the largest group-buying army in the world. During the same period, group-buying websites surpassed video websites to become the richest Internet industry in China. Various group-buying websites spent large sums of money to occupy outdoor billboards, bus bodies, elevators in high-end office buildings in major cities, mainstream websites, and television media. For a time, group-buying website advertisements were everywhere. The known group-buying website advertising investment plans exceeded 1 billion yuan RMB. Among them, Meituan indicated it had prepared 130 million yuan; Nuomi claimed it would invest 200 million yuan in advertisements on CCTV and local satellite TV channels; TuangouBao announced it would invest 550 million yuan in advertisements before signing up a celebrity endorsement for millions of yuan in Beijing. At that time, Gaopeng, which had not officially opened yet, also spent heavily to purchase prime positions on navigation websites.

Now commenting on the drawbacks of the group-buying industry is meaningless because domestic wise men have long predicted that China's "Battle of Thousands of Groups" would end in the "Death of Thousands of Groups." Li Kaifu of Innovation Works once bluntly stated, "99% of group-buying websites will die out, and only four or five will survive to dominate the market," while more industry insiders believe that the group-buying industry is about to enter the era of "the survivors are kings."

Therefore, when Li Wei, the domestic business director of TuangouBao, reassured everyone by saying, "TuangouBao has found financing and obtained life-saving money," the employees of TuangouBao all expressed, "We don't believe there are such foolish venture capitalists willing to invest." People in the venture capital industry also expressed doubts, "Investments may not necessarily exist." Investors value the operational team of a company. Given the current situation where most employees of TuangouBao have left, the possibility of continued capital support is slim. This scene is very similar to what happened in the video industry two years ago!

Using advertisements to gain more consumer recognition of the website, bringing in the core sales revenue, and thereby opening up the market—this business idea is not wrong, but doing anything requires considering comprehensive factors.

Actually, there was nothing wrong with placing advertisements in the market at the beginning of the industry's prosperity. The mistake lies in ignoring cost-effectiveness.

All practitioners in various industries are actually engaged in a race—a race between the speed of their own fund consumption and continuous income. Clearly, group-buying companies lost this race, and TuangouBao also lost. Their fund consumption speed far exceeded their income. A team with stronger execution capability achieves greater results with less money.

In the world of the Internet, many phenomena "look good," but they may not be. Transferring real-world consumption behavior to the network and expecting new wealth returns is a beautiful wish of e-commerce operators. However, due to the change in method, online operations and physical operations cannot be completely equivalent.
