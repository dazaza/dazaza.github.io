Detailed process of car rental

by wodingche on 2012-02-24 17:31:16

Many people currently do not yet understand the detailed process of renting a car, so I will introduce it to everyone here.

Step 1: Pre-rental procedures

Before renting a car, you should first be familiar with the rental procedures. Usually, renters with a local hukou (household registration) at the rental location have relatively simple rental procedures. You just need to provide your ID card, driver's license, credit card, sign a lease contract, and pay the specified deposit.

Step 2: Choose the right car

When choosing a car, you should start from the purpose of renting. For travel, it is best to choose a model with spacious and comfortable space. If the road conditions for your trip are poor or you are going on an adventure, consider choosing a vehicle with off-road capabilities. If it is a business event, you should choose a more luxurious car. If you are only using the car within the city or nearby areas, then two-door cars like Otti, Freer, or Fukang are good choices.

Step 3: Sign the car rental contract

After selecting the desired model, you need to sign a complete car rental contract. There are some important points to remember when signing the contract. You should understand the daily mileage limit and the fee standards for exceeding this limit. If the lease period expires without timely returning the vehicle, a penalty will be charged per hour based on the average price of 12 hours' daily rent. For trips exceeding the mileage limit, the rent fee will be added according to the average price per kilometer. If both time and mileage limits are exceeded, the higher-priced option will apply.

Step 4: Vehicle inspection

After signing the contract, the next step is to inspect the vehicle and fill out the inspection form, which is the process where both parties jointly confirm the condition of the vehicle. This is crucial to avoid disputes when returning the car. The inspection should prioritize safety, carefully checking items that directly affect driving safety such as brakes, steering, and lights. First, check the exterior for scratches, intact headlights, and normal functioning locks. Inside the cabin, check the fuel gauge, brakes, and air conditioning operation. Then open the hood to check the status of major components like fuel, coolant, lubricating oil, brake fluid, battery, and transmission system.

Step 5: Return

Most car rental companies require the fuel situation to be "full-to-full," meaning that the car should be returned with the same amount of fuel in the tank as when it was rented. However, many renters do not know how much fuel they need to add to match the original amount. Hefei Car Rental offers a tip: based on the hundred-kilometer fuel consumption of the rented vehicle and the mileage driven, you can roughly estimate how much fuel you used. When returning the car, compare it against the "Vehicle Transfer List" filled out during the inspection. If the vehicle has not encountered any accidents, returning the car is quite simple.
