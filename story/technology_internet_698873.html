"The 'no authenticity guarantee in antique auctions' is actually a misinterpretation of the law."

by abcdfdhehe on 2012-03-09 11:36:30

The notion that "antiques and artworks are sold at auction without authenticity guarantee" is actually a misinterpretation of the law [http://law.hexun.com/]. Song Xinchao, deputy director of the National Cultural Heritage Administration, expressed this yesterday during a joint discussion session with cultural and artistic circle CPPCC members.

"The Auction Law is a specialized law. It must be consistent with the spirit of higher laws such as the General Principles of Civil Law. Fair trade is a basic principle. 'No authenticity guarantee' does not mean you can deceive people. This is a matter of principle. Therefore, saying 'auctions do not guarantee authenticity' is a misinterpretation of the law," said Song Xinchao. "Of course, antiques and artworks being auctioned may have defects, but this is different from 'no authenticity guarantee.' " Song Xinchao pointed out that "no authenticity guarantee" is not a legal issue, but rather one of standardized operation.
