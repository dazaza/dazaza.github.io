Lawyer who killed his wife was arrested and released with a psychiatric certificate (Photo)

by 6itt959o8d on 2009-12-07 15:21:40

Core tip: Zhengzhou lawyer Dong killed his wife Gao because he suspected her of having an affair. Subsequently, the court issued an appraisal that Dong was a mental patient and the police released him. After Gao's family raised objections, experts re-examined and believed that Dong had criminal responsibility capacity, by which time Dong had already fled. Currently, the body of the deceased Gao is still frozen.

On December 6, Dahe Network reported that on July 31, 2007, a homicide occurred in Building 17 of the mechanical engineering factory in Zhongyuan District, where the female owner Gao was beaten to death. The Power Plant Road Police Station in Zhongyuan District confirmed that the perpetrator was the woman's husband Dong. Subsequently, the No. 8 Hospital of the city issued an appraisal stating that Dong was a mental patient, and the police released him on September 29 of the same year. After Gao's family raised objections, the Zhongyuan Public Security Bureau hired psychiatric experts to review Dong's medical records and determined that Dong had criminal responsibility capacity. By this time, Dong had already fled. Currently, the body of the deceased Gao is still frozen. The suspect has been wanted online for a long time, but there is still no sign of him.

The homicide that occurred at home

Young and beautiful Gao met and married Dong, who ran a law firm, ten years ago. Soon after, they had a son. But Dong always suspected that his wife was having an affair. Less than three days after Gao married into Dong's family, Dong started beating her. Due to the family's not-so-good financial situation, Gao worked as a salesperson at a real estate company. As many homeowners viewed properties at night, Gao sometimes returned home late, which made Dong even more suspicious.

On the morning of July 31, 2007, Dong and Gao were at home when Gao's phone rang. Extremely sensitive, Dong leaned in to listen if it was a man or a woman, and Gao immediately hung up the phone. Subsequently, Dong started hitting Gao. "I gradually hit harder, hitting her indiscriminately. Afterwards, I saw her fall down and stop moving, and I thought she was pretending to be dead. I continued hitting her for a while, and then realized she was really not moving anymore, so I called 120." The emergency medical personnel arrived and confirmed that Gao had died. Dong then called 110 to surrender himself.

The body has not been buried for two and a half years

Dong was criminally detained by the Power Plant Road Police Station of the Zhongyuan Public Security Bureau on suspicion of intentional homicide.

When Gao's father Gao Dexin and his wife wanted to see their daughter, they were persuaded away by the police. Due to the severity of the injuries, the deceased was sent to the Fifth Hospital of the city (now Zhengzhou People's Hospital) for plastic surgery, and then sent to the funeral home for cremation. Gao's family felt that their daughter had died unjustly and insisted on waiting until Dong was brought to justice before burying their daughter.

What Gao's family did not expect was that Dong's family used the excuse that Dong might have a mental illness to get him appraised at the No. 8 Hospital of the city. The No. 8 Hospital issued a certificate stating that Dong had a mental illness, and the police released Dong without charge on September 29 of the same year.

Yesterday morning, Gao Dexin held the relevant materials from the time and tearfully told our reporter that after that, his daughter's body was left untouched for two and a half years.

Experts reconfirm Dong's criminal responsibility capacity

The day after Dong was released, he disappeared from Zhengzhou. On April 1, 2008, the Zhongyuan Public Security Bureau re-examined the case and determined that Dong should be wanted online. Yesterday morning, on the detailed information website of the police's wanted persons, our reporter saw the nationwide arrest content issued by the police on April 1, 2008, for Dong's online pursuit. In the brief case details, it was introduced that: "Suspect Dong suspected his wife of having an affair, beat her to death, and was criminally detained on July 31, 2007. He was released on September 29 of the same year after being appraised as a mental patient. Our bureau hired psychiatric experts to review his medical records and criminal files, and the experts believed that he had criminal responsibility capacity. After the decision of the enforcement committee, he was re-criminally detained and put online for pursuit."

On the registration form for handling petition matters by the director, a leader of the Municipal Public Security Bureau once gave instructions: "Please quickly capture the suspect, Zhongyuan (Public Security Bureau)."

After the police put Dong online for pursuit, Mr. Gao went everywhere looking for Dong. "I know he has a relative in the suburbs of Xinyang, so I secretly rushed there and waited for half a month. Now, my wife and I don't have high salaries, but for our daughter, we spent all of our retirement pensions on the travel expenses to find Dong. I've been to two-thirds of the counties in the whole province." Saying this, Mr. Gao cried. (Zhengzhou Evening News)
