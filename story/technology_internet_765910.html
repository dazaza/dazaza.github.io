Apple acquires Swedish mobile media and data compression company AlgoTrim

by anonymous on 2013-08-29 16:37:52

On the evening of August 28, Beijing time, Swedish news website Rapidus reported that Apple Inc. had acquired AlgoTrim, a Swedish startup. This company develops encoding/decoding and design solutions to maximize the performance of data, mobile imaging, video, and computer graphics while minimizing memory requirements.

In March 2012, AlgoTrim announced it would provide technology for KDDI's Photo Album app in Japan. For this purpose, AlgoTrim developed specialized JPEG processing technology that was six times faster than the standard LibJPEG processing technology on Android devices. AlgoTrim was founded in 2005, and its flagship product, the Code Compression Library, was applied to mobile devices in 2006.

AlgoTrim is involved in various data compression technologies, and its image solutions enable mobile devices to achieve "modern computational photography." Computational photography uses sensors, computational techniques, actuators, intelligent light sources, and other devices to transcend the limitations of digital photography, which is based on traditional film photography. The basic concept of computational photography is to provide more accurate images that reflect more complex lighting conditions and significantly improve rendering effects, breaking away from pixel-based two-dimensional image display methods.

AlgoTrim’s achievements in digital imaging are useful for Apple, helping to elevate the camera capabilities of mobile devices to a new level. The company's other technologies can also be helpful in improving the overall performance of mobile devices. In short, AlgoTrim focuses on unleashing more powerful performance from mobile processors with lower power consumption.

Rapidus reported that Anders Holtsberg, co-founder, CEO, and head of software development at AlgoTrim, recently visited Silicon Valley. Neither AlgoTrim nor its incubator Minc would comment on the news regarding Apple's acquisition, a common statement after smaller companies are acquired by Apple. Apple also did not comment on the matter.

The acquisition of AlgoTrim aligns with Apple's acquisition strategy. According to media reports, AlgoTrim has acquired six companies so far this year, including several related to maps and location services, as well as a semiconductor company. Typically, Apple’s acquisitions are low-profile and not officially announced. Apple usually targets companies with strong expertise that can help achieve its goals, rather than large, already successful companies like Facebook's acquisition of Instagram. (Qiuyue)
