Shopping Precautions on Taobao (II)

by anonymous on 2011-04-01 12:17:26

First, the biggest taboo of online shopping - being greedy for small bargains!

I don't know how many reasons my fellow sisters have for choosing to shop online. For myself, there are three points: 1. In-season counter genuine products are usually much cheaper than in malls (but when the mall has a big promotion, we need to compare which is more cost-effective), and some off-season heavily discounted items (of course, all referring to counter genuine products); 2. Some things cannot be bought in your own city; 3. It can save you from the fatigue of going shopping.

Among these, price remains the most important factor. However, everyone needs to be cautious about "Korean style" items without physical photos. My first failure in online shopping was a coat that was supposedly "from Korea". That was three years ago, and I only bought it because it was cheap (only over 100 yuan). It turned out to be poorly made, with bad fabric, poor design, bad craftsmanship, and color differences. It also didn't fit well. When I looked at those super pretty pictures, I felt like I had been scammed! I considered it as a lesson learned by spending money, and since then I no longer buy "Korean style" clothes.

Nowadays, there are also many beautifully pictured items on Taobao with very low prices, but they may not be actual items (this will be discussed later). Just think, how could we possibly spend 100 yuan to buy something worth 1000 yuan?

I once spent 20 yuan to buy a knitted shawl, intending just to play around with it. So I didn't nitpick, as long as it didn't affect wearing.

Second, online purchases should be selective.

Not everything is suitable for online shopping. For example, tea. If you don't physically touch it, you can't judge its quality. There are also some inexpensive small items themselves, such as small accessories that women often buy. These items might be cheaper than shipping costs. If something goes wrong or problems occur, it's not worth arguing with the seller over just a few dollars. Both parties will find it troublesome.

Personally, I think buying counter products online is quite cost-effective.

Buying counter items through online purchasing agents is generally fine. They are exactly what we would buy in the mall, just that we can't get such a low discount. Some of my clothes and shoes were bought online, and the transactions were pleasant. For example, I couldn't find size 34 shoes in the counters in Northeast China, but I found them from Southern sellers online, and they were much cheaper, making it a great deal. Also, if you don't want to wait for seasonal discounts (and fear they might sell out before the discount), browsing online might bring surprises. Although counter items purchased online won't have fake goods, buyers shouldn't be too picky. For instance, if the shoes you buy have already been tried on in the counter, that's normal.

I don't recommend online shopping for electronics and digital products. Selecting these items is complex, and shipping costs are high.

Also, try not to buy fragile items online. We all know unexpected issues can be troublesome.

Third, definitely check the seller's credit! Look at the reviews for the item you're planning to buy!!

It's not about checking how many diamonds or crowns the seller has, but specifically looking at the positive review rate (be cautious if the positive review rate is low). If there are medium or negative reviews, click each one to see why the buyer didn't give a positive review. Some buyers can be picky. For example, I've seen buyers who gave medium reviews because they didn't like the taste of herbal tea, or even buyers who gave medium reviews because the delivery was slow. The seller provided reasonable explanations, and this situation is understandable. However, if medium or negative reviews are caused by real quality issues or the seller's attitude or methods in handling problems, then you need to consider carefully.

If the item you plan to buy has already been purchased by others and they have given reviews, you should definitely read what previous buyers have said. You can even choose to communicate with them. I once wanted to buy a piece of clothing. Although it was a counter product, I hadn't seen it in the mall, and I cared a lot about whether the blue color had any color difference. So, I asked a sister who bought it earlier. She kindly replied and explained in detail, allowing me to realize that the piece wasn't suitable for me, so I didn't waste money unnecessarily!

At this point, sisters can be sweet and use more emojis. Even though the internet makes us unable to see each other's faces, your smile can still be sensed!

Fourth, whether it's a real photo or if there are detailed pictures is very important.

If you're buying an item you've already seen and tried in the counter, you don't need a real picture. Remembering the item number is the most important. If you're directly shopping online, then you must pay attention to real photos. Many items have their "official website," like "Korean style" clothing and many online-sold foreign trade original orders or follow-up orders. Official websites' pictures are undoubtedly beautiful, but seeing real items allows us to get closer to reality. You can also ask for detailed pictures to learn more. Ladies who care about color differences should ask the store owner more questions.

Online shopping has the disadvantage of not being able to physically touch items. Therefore, before purchasing, you should do more research, don't be afraid of trouble. If the seller becomes impatient, you can completely abandon the purchase. A person without patience makes it hard to imagine what kind of after-sales experience we might have if something goes wrong.

Fifth, clearly understand the size and fabric.

This includes both your own size and the item you're planning to buy.

If you're buying clothing, you must clearly know your bust-waist-hip measurements. This way, comparing with the clothing's size will be more accurate. If unsure, you can measure a similar piece you already own for reference. At first, I bought oversized clothing, but later, after carefully measuring my bust-waist-hip measurements and measuring a bunch of my own clothes, I finally got an accurate grasp of sizing. Personally, I believe buying pants is the easiest to have problems with, because the fit of pants is crucial, and the requirement for suitability far exceeds that of tops. Even a slight inappropriateness in pants can ruin your entire appearance. Therefore, ladies buying pants should be careful + extra careful!

If you're buying a bag, find a bag you already own to measure against, and you'll know roughly. Don't rely on your memory, as it's unreliable!

Additionally, fabric issues may cause size discrepancies. For example, silk has no elasticity at all, so perhaps you can wear an 80 cm chest T-shirt but not an 82 cm silk blouse. This is something to note.

Sixth, approach with understanding and tolerance, and communicate more if there are issues.

Upon receiving the item, it's best to inspect it in person. If there's an error, reject the delivery. If you discover a problem after receiving it, determine if the "problem" is unforgivable. If the clothing has loose threads or some dust, being dissatisfied might be too picky. You can't guarantee getting a perfect item in a mall either, so there's no need to be overly critical.

If it's indeed a quality issue or the wrong item was sent, the first thing to do is negotiate with the seller rather than immediately giving a medium or negative review. We buy items to get what we like, not to evaluate others. So, focus on finding a solution.

I once returned an item due to quality issues. The seller initially refused to return it, offering to refund part of the money as compensation or asking me to send it back without covering the shipping fee. After I disagreed, he even said, "Then I'll refund your money and you don't need to send the clothes back, does that satisfy you?" I patiently told him that I wasn't haggling for a bargain but that the quality issue was serious and he didn't mention it beforehand (even though I had asked comprehensive questions). Returning it would allow him to relist it, as long as he clarified this point, potential buyers who understood would naturally still make the purchase. Ultimately, the seller agreed to the return, and the transaction ended without unpleasantness.

Another time, the package I received was mixed up by the seller. After contacting the seller, we decided to exchange between the buyers. Due to the remote address, I deliberately chose a cheaper courier company, and the issue was resolved smoothly. Although we received our items late, it still helped the seller. On two other occasions, I received incorrect items. One time, the clothing size was wrong, so I sent it back via the cheapest courier. Another time, I received a scarf of the wrong color. Considering the seller was on maternity leave, I accepted the mistake, thinking that helping others is also helping oneself.

In fact, many after-sales issues can be easily resolved if we communicate more and understand each other. Think about it, sisters, you only need to face one seller, but the seller has to face countless buyers. They dislike trouble too, so rarely intentionally make things difficult for us. Mutual understanding is key. And there's no need to be unreasonable even if you're right. Although "the customer is always right," has God ever acted arrogantly? Empathy and tolerance are important, and one act of generosity might bring future happiness.

Seventh, this is the last point, remember to manage your finances well.

When shopping on Taobao, we usually use Alipay. Although the payment is made upon receipt of goods, if you choose regular mail or encounter issues during transit, you must ask the seller to extend the confirmation period. Otherwise, the system will automatically make the payment after a certain number of days. To prevent deceptive behavior, be cautious!

In summary, online shopping has its drawbacks, but it also has some tangible benefits. As long as we put in more effort, we can have a pleasant online shopping experience.
