1. What is Email Marketing?

Email marketing is a form of online marketing that delivers valuable information to target users via email, with the user's prior consent. Email marketing has three basic elements: user permission, email delivery of information, and value of the information to the user.

1) Characteristics of Email Marketing: Low cost, fast speed, high efficiency, and wide coverage.

2) Functions of Email Marketing: Brand image promotion, product sales promotion, old customer maintenance, and market research.

3) Core aspects of Email Marketing: Email list, email content, sending time, and frequency.

3. Sending of Emails

Emails can be sent either independently or by entrusting professional email marketing service providers. Regardless of the method, the following rules need to be clarified:

a) Mass Sending Techniques: It’s best to use a group-send-single-display method for sending. When customers see your email, they should perceive it as a personalized email specifically sent to them, which will naturally draw their attention. On the other hand, if it looks like it was sent to dozens of people, the customer's attention will likely decrease.

b) Sending Time

c) Sending Frequency

The sending frequency shouldn't be too frequent; too many emails may cause customers to get annoyed and add you to their blacklist. Emails should ideally be sent regularly. Regular quality content pushes can help customers, while also building brand awareness. Generally, once a week or every half month is best. This way, email subscribers won’t forget that they subscribed to this email list due to a long time without receiving any emails, nor will they find the emails annoying and choose to unsubscribe or add you to the email blacklist because of too many emails within a short period. If it's a promotional email, the sending demand should be decided based on the analysis of tracking results according to the company's marketing needs.

5. Conclusion

To make email marketing achieve the best results, under the premise of ensuring that the email subject conforms to basic principles as much as possible, it's still necessary to conduct certain tests on the email subject, especially for important emails. It's more necessary to confirm whether the email subject is optimal through testing. If possible, draft several different email subjects and solicit opinions from a portion of users respectively, choosing the best one.
