Dalong Qiu

The attractions of Yandang Mountain are scattered, stretching eastward from Yangjiao Cave to Judaban Ridge in the west; southward from Jinzhu Creek to Liuping Mountain in the north. Within this scenic area covering 450 square kilometers, there are strange peaks and caves, towering rocks and overlapping cliffs, cascading waterfalls and springs, lush forests and secluded valleys. There are over 550 places of interest. The scenic spots are divided into eight major areas: Lingfeng, Lingyan, Dalong Qiu, Xianshengmen, Xianqiao, Yangjiao Cave, Jingming Three-Fold Waterfall, and Yan Lake, with the southeastern part being the most picturesque. Among these, the "two Lings and one Long" — Lingfeng, Lingyan, and Dalong Qiu — are known as the "Three Wonders of Yandang." Among the three wonders, Dalong Qiu is considered the best. Southern Song poet Lou Yue wrote: "To the north lies Taihang Mountain, and to the east lies Mount Yu's cave, but the most spectacular sight is at Yandang Mountain. There is no waterfall in the world like Dalong Qiu, praised by all with one voice." (From *Gonggui Ji - Dalong Qiu*), showing that Dalong Qiu was already renowned worldwide during the Song Dynasty.

Introduction to Major Attractions of Dalong Qiu Scenic Area

The Dalong Qiu Scenic Area extends eastward from Malan Ridge to Dongling in the west, and from Jinzhu Stream Mouth in the south to Lingyun Peak in the north. It spans 28 kilometers east-west and 7.5 kilometers north-south, covering an area of 28 square kilometers. Within this area, there are 15 peaks, 13 rocks, 7 stones, 5 cliffs, 6 caves, 1 peak, 3 gates, 2 arches, 8 ridges, 5 waterfalls, 2 pools, 8 ponds, 1 spring, 3 streams, 2 gullies, 1 canyon, 4 temples, 7 pavilions, 1 courtyard, 1 pot, 1 study, 2 bridges, totaling 105 attractions. This area, located in the middle-western part of Yandang Mountain, was formerly called "Xinai Gu". It is renowned for its extraordinary peaks, massive cliffs, and magnificent waterfalls. Notable landmarks include the sky-piercing Furong Peak, the endlessly changing Jiandao Peak, the grand Yunlian Cliff, the misty Jinxing Gorge, the deep and serene Jinzhu Gully, all of which are spectacular sights. The Dalong Qiu waterfall, hailed as the "World's Greatest Waterfall", presents a myriad of postures, forming a magnificent spectacle that leaves visitors awestruck. Together with Lingfeng and Lingyan, it is known as one of the "Three Wonders" of Yandang Mountain. Among the three wonders, Dalong Qiu is considered the finest.

Scenic Features

In reality, the Dalong Qiu Scenic Area is a valley, but the entrance is relatively spacious, with the Jinxu stream nearby. However, due to prolonged drought, the stream has dried up. Walking along the left side of the stream, you will see a thousand-foot precipice forming a barrier ahead, reaching high into the clouds. On the cliff face, various peaks overlap like countless Buddhas appearing, this is known as Qianfo Rock. Slightly lower down, there is a large flat rock standing upright, resembling a stone stele, known as Shibei Peak.

Tracing upstream along the stream, there is a bamboo bridge spanning the stream, with a pot of tea flowers placed on it, red blossoms shining brightly. In front of the mountain peaks, one stands alone. The giant rock of the mountain peak is sharply pointed, with a crack running from top to bottom, as if split open by a huge axe, forming two pieces. One piece is thicker, the other finer, like the double blades of scissors, supporting the clouds above, this is the famous Jiandao Peak. Wang Sirren described it as "splitting thousands of feet apart, wanting to cut through the blue sky." At the peak, the rock slightly bends, resembling the closed but not yet opened pincers of a crab, hence Ming dynasty Zhang Su referred to it as "Giant Claw."

Behind the Jiandao Peak, another peak rises high, with a sharp triangular tip, this is Tianle Peak. Due to the weather being gloomy, the peak is shrouded in dark clouds, smoke rising into the air. The greenish-gray rock walls are barren, while lush trees cluster at the base, making the peak appear even taller. As you move, the shape of the mountain changes. What previously looked like a crab claw on the upper left side of the mountain now resembles a woodpecker extending its beak to peck at the trees when viewed from here. Moving forward, it transforms into the shape of a bear, cutely raising its head to climb. After passing by and looking back, there is no longer any resemblance to scissors, instead, it becomes a pillar holding up the sky, thus earning the names "Jade Pestle Peak" and "Heavenly Pillar Peak." A signboard by the roadside indicates "Sail Peak," clearly a newer name. Although less intriguing than "Jiandao Peak," it still aptly describes the scene.

Further ahead, there is a sign indicating "Yifan Peak." Looking back, the pillar holding up the sky has transformed into a sail at sea. This truly exemplifies the saying "viewed horizontally, it becomes a ridge; viewed from the side, it becomes a peak." As Dai Mingshi said: "Generally speaking, the peaks of Yandang cleverly connect with nature, changing shapes with every step. Many names are derived from their appearance. Yet, many wondrous sights remain undiscovered amidst the thickets and wild grass."

Why isn't the longest waterfall in China called "Da Long Fall"? Because Dalong Qiu is not as gentle as other waterfalls. But why is it called "Dalong Qiu"? Because "Qiu" means "pool," and calling it "Da Long Pool" would sound strange.

Standing by the edge of Dalong Qiu, one feels an indescribable coolness. The air here is particularly fresh, and those who live here enjoy great comfort, staying warm in winter and cool in summer.

The water of Dalong Qiu comes from a natural water tower above. Due to excessive accumulation, it flows down. Dalong Qiu changes with the weather, sometimes resembling a silver dragon, sometimes a rainbow.

Dalong Qiu is "extraordinarily unique"

Its uniqueness is manifested in its form, changing according to the time and sequence. After a thunderstorm in the height of summer, Dalong Qiu is a restless white dragon emerging from halfway up the sky, twisting and turning in the air, thundering and spraying snow, roaring to the heavens, its sound deafening from a hundred meters away. Tourists dozens of meters away can still feel the spray of its water droplets. In the cold and dry winter, Dalong Qiu retracts its white silk ribbon, scattering only a handful of pearls from the Dragon Palace.

scattered about, when the wind blows, this handful of pearls drifts with the wind, floating up and down. If it encounters a sunny day, this necklace-like waterfall turns into a colorful rainbow, shimmering and flickering, appearing and disappearing.

During a winter with little rain, even after Start of Spring, there is still little rain. Dalong Qiu lacks the wild state of summer, the leisurely state of winter, and only retains the lethargic state of spring. Like a beautiful woman waking up in the morning, delicate and weak, sleeping like a crabapple blossom in spring sleep. The water flowing down from the peak of Yunlian Cliff should be silk rather than cloth, willow branches in the spring breeze rather than pines under the scorching sun, or long hair flowing freely rather than angry hair standing on end. That kind of unhurried, swaying motion is indeed difficult to describe, no wonder the ancients said: "It's hard to put pen to paper when writing about Long Qiu."

The cultural landscape of Dalong Qiu can rival its natural beauty.

Legend has it that in the early Tang Dynasty, Indian monk Nuojulu came to the mountain to build a pagoda and a temple. Because he loved the beauty of Long Qiu, he often came to watch the waterfall, eventually sitting in meditation until his death. A Song Dynasty poem tells this story: "Nuojulu never tires of watching, sitting quietly in the rain." Shen Kuo also quoted this in *Dream Brook Essays*: "Walking through Yandang, the clouds are desolate; sitting quietly at Long Qiu, the rain is misty." To commemorate the Indian monk, later generations built the "Waterfall Pavilion" here, with couplets on the pillars written by Sha Menghai and Shang Xiangqian. On the right side of the Long Qiu pool, there is a "Forget-to-Return Pavilion," taking its meaning from the line: "Under the Forget-to-Return Pavilion, guests forget to return, playing with the trickling water, sitting on green moss." The couplet on the pillars takes the majestic momentum of Summer Dalong Qiu: "Six dragons roll up the sea to the heavens, ten thousand horses neighing against the wind descend from the snowy domain." Standing in a three-legged formation with the aforementioned two pavilions is the Long He轩 on the high slope to the west. The couplet at the entrance was written by Kang Youwei: "A peak rises abruptly from the ground, water comes from the heavens," reminding one of the timeless verse "The Yellow River's waters come from the heavens." Frankly speaking, Li Bai's poetry blends reality with imagination, giving a feeling of overwhelming momentum that cannot be stopped. In my view, Kang Youwei's line "Water comes from the heavens" about Long Qiu always gives me a feeling of trying to imitate a tiger but ending up like a dog. Perhaps during Kang Youwei's visit to Yandang, it was midsummer, and after the rain, the water of Long Qiu could have been surging like the Yellow River. Alas, take the Yellow River as an example, who can now see the momentum of "flowing to the sea without returning?" It's often reported on the news that the Yellow River has run dry, which is truly depressing.

Dalong Qiu is the scenic spot with the most cultural relics in the entire Yandang Mountain scenic area. On its cliff walls, there are more than twenty inscriptions such as "The World's Greatest Waterfall," complementing the extraordinarily unique natural scenery, making people linger and forget to leave.

Dalong Qiu Waterfall

The Dalong Qiu Waterfall in Yandang Mountain is a famous scenic spot in Zhejiang Province. Along with the Huangguoshu Waterfall in Guizhou, the Hukou Waterfall on the Yellow River, and the Diaoshuilou Waterfall in Heilongjiang, it is known as one of China's four great waterfalls. Dalong Qiu uniquely stands out with its drop of over 190 meters, making it the tallest waterfall in China, earning the title of "The World's Greatest Waterfall."

The waterfall originates from Baitangjian, flows through Longqiubei, and plunges down from the Yunlian Peak, like spilling from the Milky Way, presenting a very grand spectacle. The most extraordinary feature of Dalong Qiu is the varied and fascinating scenes presented due to seasonal and weather changes. In the height of summer, after a thunderstorm, Dalong Qiu resembles an angry silver dragon fiercely descending from the sky, roaring like thunder, shaking heaven and earth, with a magnificent presence. On clear winter days, the waterfall cascades down from mid-air, and when illuminated by sunlight, it presents the marvel of a colorful five-colored rainbow, with especially enchanting scenery. In March, due to scarce rainfall, the waterfall hangs like a string of pearls, transforming into mist and clouds within just a few zhang, making Dalong Qiu incredibly changeable and unpredictable.

At the top of Dalong Qiu, there is another scene altogether. As recorded in *Zaijing Tang Ji*, "The source and background of Dalong Qiu are extremely vast and majestic, with towering and perilous terrain rarely reached by travelers. Only seeing the concave where the waterfall falls does not fully reveal the path it follows, which is indeed a regrettable matter." Poet Chen Zhisi wrote in *Ascending Longqiubei*: "For thirty years I've remembered the river falling from half the sky in Yandang, today I fulfill my wish to ascend the clouds and reach the water's source. Who split the thousand peaks surrounding us? Who displayed the ten thousand ravines' springs competing to flow? The bright and dark changes do not follow the time, the cold and heat alternations continuously present new forms. Rain combines with clouds from afar, the sun rises only illuminating the western screen. Reaching the stream, one laments the brilliant sunlight, the wind roars and thunder strikes. The grass and forest after the rain are cleansed and refreshed, the great mountains are naturally magnificent teachers. The flowers and birds of spring and summer lightly resemble famous paintings, the rocks and woods of autumn and winter resemble poems from the Jian'an era. Farmers speak willingly, adding new interest for travelers. With the tea of Long Qiu, I compose wonderful verses along the stream. Cutting through thorns and rocks, listening to the rippling water and tinkling sounds. Suddenly, the rumbling sound from ten miles away is heard, voices rise chaotically from beneath the ground. The position at the mouth of the cliff is difficult to approach, standing unsteadily on the vine-shaded ground. Only then do you see the wall of the dragon pool, with ten thousand catties of swirling water falling like snow."

There are many waterfalls in Yandang Mountain, eighteen of which have names. The waterfalls of Yandang Mountain differ greatly in style from the Huangguoshu Grand Waterfall in Guizhou. They don't win by size but captivate tourists with their graceful posture. Besides the Dalong Qiu Waterfall mentioned earlier, there are also famous waterfalls like Xiaolong Qiu and Sanzhe Qiu. Sanzhe Qiu consists of three waterfalls formed by the same stream cascading over three cliffs. Among them, the Zhongzhe Qiu is the most captivating. The surrounding cliffs resemble a semi-circular cave, with water cascading down from the cave's roof, gracefully drifting, very beautiful.

Famous Scenic Spots

Malan Ridge

Also known as Shicheng Ridge, the ridge back is shaped like the letter "凹", resembling a saddle, serving as the boundary between East Inner Valley and West Inner Valley, and also the passage from Lingyan to Dalong Qiu. Climbing the ridge offers panoramic views of red peaks and lush greenery, extremely grandiose. In 1990, a tunnel over 300 meters long was excavated below the ridge, connecting the road between Lingyan and Dalong Qiu. While providing convenience for tourists, it also often deprives them of the opportunity to climb the ridge and enjoy the scenery.

Furong Peak

Located on the north side of Dongling at the boundary between West Inner Valley and West Outer Valley. Ming dynasty poet He Bai wrote the line "Seeing Furong a hundred miles away on the river," precisely because this peak towers into the clouds, drawing special attention. As early as the Southern and Northern Dynasties, in *Xie Kang Le Ji*, there is a record: "On the Fuzhu Island, there stands a stone like a newly bloomed lotus, all in green and white." In the first nationwide local gazetteer compiled during the Northern Song Dynasty, *Taiping Huanyu Ji*, there is also a record: "Furong Mountain... standing tall like a lotus flower, red and white interspersed, hence its name."
