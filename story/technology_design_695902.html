Unknown huge bird with 3-meter wingspan and fierce eyes discovered in Mount Songshan, Henan (picture)

by gghot76hh1 on 2012-03-07 17:41:41

The avian fauna in the Songshan region consists of 14 families and 43 species, but people are unable to match the "Bird King" with any of them. Recently, something unusual happened in Wangzhuang Village at the foot of Shaoshi Mountain: a large bird, nearly as tall as half an adult, has been spotted almost every day on the hill behind the village, accompanied by a flock of birds like a group of fans. This scene has become the most stunning view of early spring in Songshan, amazing tourists visiting the Sanhuangzhai area of Shaoshi Mountain.

Reported by Evening News reporter Jianlong Yuan and correspondent Zhaohui Zhang with text and photos:

From afar it looked like a bird's nest, up close it was a big bird.

Yesterday, upon hearing the news, I went to Wangzhuang Village. Villager Chunyou Wang said that on the morning of March 14th, he and some friends were planting trees on the slope of Shaoshi Mountain when they noticed many small birds circling around a locust tree about ten meters away. There was a black lump on the tree which, upon closer inspection, moved. Curious, Chunyou Wang pulled his friend over to investigate and found a huge bird perched on the tree.

What surprised Chunyou Wang and others even more was that the big bird wasn't afraid of humans. When the smaller birds saw people coming, they flew off in all directions, but this big bird simply glanced down at the crowd below and then turned its gaze elsewhere, showing no intention of flying away.

Its head was bigger than a bowl, its eyes fierce—it scared off the old sheep herder.

"This thing's head is almost as big as a bowl, its claws larger than an adult's hand, its gaze very fierce, just looking at it scares you," said Xinjun Guo, a 60-year-old sheep herder from Wangzhuang Village, still somewhat shaken by his encounter with the strange bird.

On the afternoon of March 15th, while grazing his sheep on the hillside, Guo accidentally noticed a "man in black" sitting on a large rock not far from the sheep herd. Thinking it was a tourist visiting the Sanhuangzhai area of Shaoshi Mountain, Guo enthusiastically greeted the "man in black," but received no response.

Feeling somewhat awkward, Guo thought the guest might have encountered some trouble and went over to check. As he approached, he realized it wasn't a tourist at all, but a massive strange bird! The bird's eyes were fixed on the sheep herd. "I've lived for 60 years and have never seen such a big bird; it seemed like it wanted to attack my sheep."

Guo carefully observed and noticed that under the bird's wings was a patch of red. Its wings hung down, creating a striking contrast of red and black. Standing there, it resembled an ancient general draped in a cloak, majestic and commanding. Guo cautiously drove his sheep herd away from the strange bird, but unexpectedly, the bird calmly jumped down from the rock and leisurely followed the sheep herd for a stretch. Guo was uneasy, both frightened and nervous, breaking out in cold sweat.

"Hundreds of Birds Paying Homage to the Phoenix" - "Bird King" Unrecognized

Chunyou Wang said that when the big bird stood, it was taller than half a person, majestic and commanding, its eyes filled with ferocity, exuding an aura of menace, just like a general observing the enemy and deploying troops; when it walked through the grass, it did so gracefully and confidently. What was most astonishing was that whenever this big bird appeared, there were always flocks of crows, magpies, sparrows, and other small birds around it. Some would circle around it on the nearby grassland, others would perch on branches chirping loudly, creating a lively scene akin to a group of fans surrounding their beloved star. The big bird seemed accustomed to such attention, remaining calm and composed, enjoying the adoration. Occasionally, when it became impatient, it would suddenly spread its wings and soar high into the sky, with a flock of black crows and countless magpies following closely behind in alarm.

Chunyou Wang said that when the bird spread its wings, they were nearly three meters long, resembling the great eagle from "The Legend of the Condor Heroes," looking every bit like the legendary "Bird King"!

After several days of observation, Chunyou Wang discovered that the "Bird King" usually roosted at night on a large paulownia tree about 50 meters away from his pig farm. So Wang placed some pig offal under the tree. "For these past few days, I've been placing food there daily, and it's been eating it."

Wang led me to the place where the "Bird King" dined. "This is its droppings," Wang pointed to white substances on the ground that resembled lime water. He said that Shaoshi Mountain had many wild pheasants, and the variety of birds was also plentiful, but this kind of big bird was something he had never encountered before at nearly 50 years of age. "In recent years, the vegetation on Shaoshi Mountain has grown lush, and the ecological environment has greatly improved compared to before, which may be why this kind of big bird has appeared." Wang said that neither he nor his fellow villagers knew the name of this big bird.

The Appearance of the "Bird King" Attracts Poaching "Black Hands"

On the morning of yesterday, during the interview, I found six traps made of fine steel wire near the paulownia tree where the "Bird King" rested. Locals also called them "chuzi."

An angry Chunyou Wang said, "It seems someone is trying to harm the 'Bird King.'"

Upon hearing the news, Officer Lili Zhao from the Shaolin police station rushed to the scene, carefully examined the situation, and dismantled the six traps one by one. "Even though we currently don't know the origin of this bird or its name, protecting wildlife is the responsibility of each of us," said Zhao. She reminded Chunyou Wang and the villagers of Wangzhuang that if anyone was found harming the "Bird King," they should promptly intervene and report it to the authorities.

According to historical records, in ancient times, Dengfeng and the Songshan region were sparsely populated, with dense forests and abundant vegetation. The population and diversity of wildlife were numerous, including 14 families and 43 species of birds. Among them were geese, crows, katydids, bulbuls, owls, mountain eagles, golden eagles, woodpeckers, cuckoos, orioles, and others. However, people have been unable to identify the "Bird King" among them.
