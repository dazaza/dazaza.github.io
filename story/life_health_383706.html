Uterine diseases: Is uterine erosion serious? Zhengzhou Gynecology. Is uterine erosion serious? The gynecology department of the Zhengzhou Anti-aircraft Hospital explains that uterine erosion, also known as cervical erosion, is a common disease among women. It is more commonly seen in married women and those who are physically weak. The cause is mostly due to damage to the cervix during sexual activity or childbirth, allowing bacteria to invade and cause infection. It can also occur due to physical weakness, bacterial infections during menstruation, leading to the condition. Experts from the Zhengzhou Anti-aircraft Hospital explain that the normal surface of the cervix is covered by a layer of squamous epithelium, smooth and pink. Chronic inflammation in the deep tissues of the cervix leads to nutritional disorders of the epithelium, causing it to shed. This denuded surface is gradually covered by columnar epithelium from the cervical canal. Since the columnar epithelium is thin, it allows the underlying blood vessels and red stroma to be visible, making the surface appear red, which is referred to as cervical erosion.

Main Symptoms of Cervical Erosion:

1. Mild cervical erosion: The eroded area accounts for less than 1/3 of the total cervical area. Patients may have mild symptoms or none at all. Generally, there will be slightly increased yellowish discharge without significant discomfort, often detected during routine gynecological examinations.

2. Moderate cervical erosion: The eroded area accounts for between 1/3 to 2/3 of the total cervical area. Symptoms are more pronounced compared to mild cases, with yellow or yellow-green discharge, increased volume, and viscosity. There may also be pain during intercourse, lower back and abdominal pain, or even post-coital bleeding, irregular vaginal bleeding outside of menstruation, or blood-tinged discharge.
