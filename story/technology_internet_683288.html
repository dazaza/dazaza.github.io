The wife with liver cirrhosis has been taking care of her paralyzed husband for more than ten years without abandoning him.

by ff5cg0chhy on 2012-02-29 13:48:24

Tu Chuanqing, 58 years old this year, is from Bai Zhu Village, Duan Tan Township, Fengcheng City. Due to suffering from rheumatoid arthritis for 30 years, his four limbs have severely deformed and lost the ability to work. His family relies entirely on his wife Tian Xiuzhen to plant a few acres of rice and do odd jobs to maintain their livelihood. Tian Xiuzhen is 34 years old this year, has ascites due to liver cirrhosis, and her abdomen is swollen. With the main pillar of the family collapsed, Tu Chuanqing has parents in their eighties above him, and three children below him who are still minors. The whole family has fallen into difficulties. Yesterday, our reporter drove more than 70 kilometers to rush to Tu Chuanqing's home, experiencing the hardships of his family's life. The weather is unpredictable. In 2010, Tian Xiuzhen felt uncomfortable in her stomach and went to the First Affiliated Hospital of Nanchang University for a checkup. The result showed that she had liver cirrhosis with ascites symptoms.

In Tian Xiuzhen's home, the reporter saw many coal balls piled up in a room next door. These coal balls were all moved in by Tian Xiuzhen and her children. It might take the reporter half a day to move these coal balls, but it would probably take Tian Xiuzhen several days. This woman uses her frail shoulders to bear all the physical labor of the whole family.

Tu Chuanqing and Tian Xiuzhen are now both bedridden. After friends and relatives pooled together more than 20,000 yuan, they were no longer able to help. To not drag down the family, Tu Chuanqing once thought about committing suicide. "Once, my husband asked my son to give him pesticide for vegetables, but my son didn't give it to him," Tian Xiuzhen said helplessly.

"Doctor says there are two ways to alleviate the condition. One is liver transplantation, which costs 400,000 yuan, and requires taking 20,000 yuan worth of medicine each year for maintenance. The second option is to return home and slowly take medicine for recuperation." Mr. Xiong, Tu Chuanqing's nephew said. Tu Chuanqing spends 20,000 yuan each year on medication, accumulating up to 600,000 yuan so far. The house is already destitute, and all relatives and friends have been borrowed from. "Tian Xiuzhen can only choose to return home and slowly take medicine for recuperation," Mr. Xiong said helplessly.

Currently, there are five people in Tu Chuanqing's family without any source of income to sustain their livelihood. If there are any reader friends willing to provide help to Tu Chuanqing's family, please call the number, and our reporter will convey your kindness.

For the couple, the most valuable thing in the family are the children's awards. Despite her serious illness, Tian Xiuzhen takes on all the household chores. Our reporter carried Tu Chuanqing outside to bask in the sun.

All the houses in Tu Chuanqing's family were built in the 1980s as wooden plank tile houses. The middle one is the living room, and the right one is the bedroom. Their house is the most dilapidated in the village, with three wooden tile rooms and uneven ground inside. Tu Chuanqing and Tian Xiuzhen's eldest son is Tu Yuancun, 16 years old, studying in the third year of junior high school, with excellent academic performance, ranking among the top ten in the school. The second child is named Tu Furong, studying in the sixth grade of elementary school, and the youngest is named Tu Yuanwei, 12 years old. All three children have good academic performance, and the walls of the living room are covered with awards.

The reporter carried Tu Chuanqing, and this middle-aged man was like a bundle of dry firewood on the reporter's back, very light. Tian Xiuzhen said that every day she and her father-in-law have to carry Tu Chuanqing out of the bedroom together.

Expecting love to get through difficulties

Husband's illness makes walking difficult

"In 2000, when my youngest son was half a year old, my husband's rheumatoid arthritis suddenly worsened. His feet could not move at all, and when he was in pain, he rolled around on the bed. Sometimes he even had gastric bleeding and broke out in cold sweats," Tu Chuanqing's wife Tian Xiuzhen told the reporter about her husband's condition. Tu Chuanqing started taking Chinese medicine to treat his condition since 1981. In 2000, his condition flared up, making him unable to walk and almost paralyzed, with swollen feet like steamed buns. Thus, Tu Chuanqing has been sitting in chairs and beds at home for years, using a long bench to move around.

But now, both of their medication fees are gone, and they have stopped taking medicine. Meanwhile, there are three children in the family attending school.

Wife suddenly suffers from liver cirrhosis

Due to his illness, Tu Chuanqing cannot stand on his feet and can only sit in the chair in the bedroom at home. The reporter sees before him a very thin man whose hands and feet are constantly trembling. Tu Chuanqing lives in Xia Tu Village Group, Bai Zhu Village, Duan Tan Township, Fengcheng City. His family relies on planting a few acres of rice to make a living. Tu Chuanqing has suffered from internal rheumatism for more than 30 years and has been relying on medication to survive.

The wife shoulders the heavy burden of the entire family.

"I go out to work every day, sometimes leaving my husband alone at home. Sometimes he defecates and urinates on himself, and I have to clean him up when I come home," Tian Xiuzhen carefully takes care of her husband who cannot walk, never leaving or abandoning him. After Tu Chuanqing fell ill, his fingers also became large and immobile. "I feed him mouthful by mouthful, every meal is like this," Tian Xiuzhen said.

In 1995, Tu Chuanqing married Tian Xiuzhen from Sichuan, and their eldest son Tu Yuancun was born the following year. Apart from farming at home, Tu Chuanqing also worked outside to earn some pocket money to subsidize household expenses.

After Tu Chuanqing fell ill, his wife Tian Xiuzhen took on the heavy responsibility of supporting the family. Tian Xiuzhen has elderly in-laws in their eighties to take care of, as well as three underage children. Every morning, Tian Xiuzhen goes to the paddy field to water the rice plants. Afterwards, she returns home to cook breakfast for Tu Chuanqing and the children. To save money, Tian Xiuzhen grows her own vegetables, some for her own consumption and others sold at nearby markets. Additionally, Tian Xiuzhen works part-time at a nearby clothing factory to supplement the household income.
