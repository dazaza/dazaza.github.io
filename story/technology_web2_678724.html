An overview of Kyungpook National University:

The former President of South Korea, Park Chung-hee, graduated from KNU. The university's motto is "Truth, Humility, and Service." KNU is one of the two national universities that have entered the top 100 globally. KNU offers over 100 majors, among which engineering and business are particularly prominent in South Korea. The most focused major areas include IT, BT (Biotechnology), and NT (Nanotechnology). To obtain a Korean student visa, students must go through specific procedures. Daegu is located in the southeastern part of the Korean Peninsula, 327 kilometers southeast of Seoul. It is the political, economic, social, and artistic hub of the Yeongnam inland region. Covering an area of 885.54 square kilometers with a population of 2.5 million, Daegu has inherited the brilliant cultures of ancient Silla and Gaya. It is a historic large city with a highly developed specialized industry in fibers, machinery, eyewear frames, wood crafts, umbrellas, bicycles, etc. Synthetic fiber products account for 40% of global exports. Daegu has 27 universities, producing more than 40,000 highly skilled technicians annually. KNU’s Language Institute is equipped with advanced facilities and a team of highly qualified teachers to meet all foreign language teaching needs. Recently, the institute added a full-time Korean language program to provide a good learning environment for foreigners who wish to study Korean language and culture.

What are the characteristics of Kyungpook National University?

The expert on studying in Korea explains it from seven aspects:

1. As a national university, its ranking is second only to Seoul National University, placing it within the top 10-15 universities in South Korea.

What does BT mainly cover?

BT stands for School of Life Science and Bio-technology. Biotechnology is considered crucial for solving today's environmental and health issues. It has developed rapidly through various applications such as modern recombinant DNA technology, based on techniques, animal reproduction studies, and genetically modified organisms.

This major's strength lies in its course diversity, enabling students to conduct high-level research and adapt to productivity, preparing them for their career plans.
