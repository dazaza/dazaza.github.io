When raising a baby, we may face some minor illnesses, such as ear infections, sore throat, stomach pain, motion sickness, etc. Sometimes it's not even necessary to go to the hospital, but we can't ignore them. What should we do? There are some clever tricks to deal with these issues. These small remedies are authoritative because they were collected from pediatricians by the American magazine "PARENTS". Be sure to save these tips! Medicine is truly amazing; often only it can help our sick children. However, besides waiting for the doctor's prescription, we can also have some simple methods to effectively relieve our children's pain and discomfort. The PARENTS magazine interviewed many pediatricians and collected home remedies for common childhood diseases, which we call small remedies. This article presents some of the classic solutions among them. We are not telling you that you don't need to go to the hospital; as responsible mothers, you can always seek help from doctors if you have any doubts. We just hope that these small methods can help you and your child get through a cough-filled night and the anxious waiting time before the medicine takes effect more easily. 1. Ear Infection Doctors usually recommend placing a warm cloth on your child's ear to relieve pain. The warmth generally has a soothing effect and can distract attention; this is most suitable for a child who is restless due to pain. Meanwhile, heat can accelerate blood circulation, thus delivering more white blood cells that fight bacteria to the infected area. Additionally, Dr. Gordon, the pediatric supervisor from Los Angeles, has an even better method: heat a potato in the microwave for five minutes, wrap it in a wet towel after taking it out, and then use it to apply to the ear. The advantage of this method is that the potato allows the heat to last longer and be evenly distributed. (However, you must be very careful to control the temperature of the potato so as not to burn the child.) Since most ear infections are caused by bacteria, you still need to consult a doctor about whether your child should take antibiotics. 2. Burns/Scalds For more serious second or third-degree burns (where the skin has developed blisters), you must immediately take your child to the doctor. However, for common first-degree burns (including mild sunburn), you can place a cloth soaked in cold water on the affected area. After 24 hours of the burn, you can gently apply some aloe vera oil on the wound. You can use pure aloe vera oil or extract fresh aloe leaf juice. Besides having antibacterial properties, aloe vera oil can also accelerate cell growth, thereby helping the wound heal. Why wait 24 hours to use it? Doctors explain that it is to allow the swelling to subside before using aloe vera to reduce irritation to the skin. It's important to note that certain aloe gels should be avoided, as these products often lack real ingredients and can affect treatment outcomes.
