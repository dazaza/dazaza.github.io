critically ill patient in need of "panda blood" www.qx778.com

by qq832046 on 2012-02-05 08:57:02

When he saw the news forwarded by Renren Network, Zhang, a sophomore at the Ling Tong College of Nanjing University of Posts and Telecommunications, was just about to turn off his phone and go to sleep in order to get enough rest for the final exam on the 11th. After seeing the rescue information, he hesitated, thinking that he might be RH-negative type B blood. To confirm this, he immediately made a call to his parents who were local. The parents definitely informed their son that he was indeed this blood type. And when they heard that their son intended to donate blood for an emergency patient, they strongly supported it and urged Zhang to go to the hospital quickly.

Doctor Ye immediately contacted the Jiangsu Blood Station and the blood banks of various hospitals in Nanjing to look for "panda blood". Around 11 PM, a blood bank responded that they had found several hundred milliliters of RH-negative type B blood. At midnight yesterday, the precious plasma was delivered to the operating room outside the Provincial Chinese Medicine Hospital.

The students from Nanjing University of Posts and Telecommunications came overnight to donate blood.

At this point, those anxiously waiting outside the operating room couldn't stay idle either. They all picked up their phones and called everyone who might have a way, hoping to use their social networks to find people with RH-negative type B blood. In the midst of this hustle and bustle, a friend surnamed Sun suddenly realized that the power of the Internet should not be underestimated. Many people have sought help online and eventually received assistance. Therefore, he made a call to his son, asking him to try to post a cry for help online.

In the dormitory, the two roommates who hadn't yet rested also took action upon hearing this news. One of them even specifically arranged a car. The three young men then drove overnight from Xianlin to the Provincial Chinese Medicine Hospital.

Netizens widely forwarded the rescue information.

From this moment on, a race against time began to search for the blood.

At around 9:30 PM on January 10th, Mr. Xie, who suffered a sudden cerebral hemorrhage, was rushed into the emergency room of the Jiangsu Provincial Chinese Medicine Hospital by an ambulance. By then, he had already shown signs of dilated pupils and difficulty in autonomous breathing. Seeing this, Doctor Ye did not hesitate and decided to operate immediately. However, as soon as the blood test results came out, the doctor became anxious. Mr. Xie turned out to be RH-negative type B blood—commonly known as "panda blood".

At this time, Mr. Xie's relatives and friends also arrived at the Provincial Chinese Medicine Hospital. The doctor informed them that given Mr. Xie's current condition, an immediate craniotomy was necessary. Although there was only so much blood, they had to take a risk and attempt the surgery, otherwise the consequences would be unimaginable. After obtaining the consent of the patient's family, the surgery began.

Although Zhang's blood wasn't used, Mr. Xie's relatives still expressed immense gratitude towards him. "Coming in the middle of the night just to help others, such an action moved and inspired all of us," Mr. Sun said. However, Zhang felt it was nothing. "Drawing a tube of blood has no effect on me, but for others, it might mean a life. If I didn't do this today, I would probably feel guilty for my entire life."

When Zhang and his group arrived at the hospital, it was already past 2 AM, and the surgery was halfway done. As soon as he got to the operating room, Zhang immediately went to have his blood type tested. The test results showed that Zhang's blood type was indeed the RH-negative type B blood that Mr. Xie needed. That is to say, if the surgery required it, Zhang's blood could be immediately transfused into Mr. Xie's body. Meanwhile, the doctors performing the surgery were doing everything possible to control the patient's blood loss. Thanks to their efforts, the patient did not experience massive bleeding, and the plasma temporarily transferred from other blood banks was sufficient.

Zhang dialed the mobile number left online. Mr. Sun, who answered the phone, was overjoyed. He never expected that someone would actually step forward and respond to the rescue information online. He immediately told Zhang all the details and informed him of the hospital and ward where the patient was located.

Mr. Xie is a businessman in his 50s. On the evening of the day before yesterday, while walking alone on the street, he suddenly suffered a cerebral hemorrhage. A passerby noticed him and called an emergency number to send him to the hospital urgently. Doctor Ye, who was on duty that night, stated that by the time Mr. Xie arrived at the hospital, he had already shown signs of dilated pupils and difficulty in autonomous breathing. He immediately decided to perform surgery. However, after conducting various routine tests, Doctor Ye discovered that this patient was extremely rare RH-negative type B blood. Searching through the blood bank of the Provincial Chinese Medicine Hospital, there was no such rare blood available.

Outside the emergency room, more than ten relatives and friends were anxiously waiting. The hospital informed them that due to the extreme rarity of RH-negative blood, their hospital's blood bank had no stock. However, given Mr. Xie's condition, if surgery was not performed immediately, the situation would become even more critical.

However, because craniotomy itself involves large blood loss, plus concerns about the possibility of an aneurysm rupturing during the surgery causing massive bleeding, the several hundred milliliters of blood sent over were insufficient for the surgery needs.

At around 3 AM yesterday, Zhang and his classmates returned to school and quickly fell into a deep sleep because they still had to face the afternoon final exams that day.

Doctor Ye introduced that currently the hematoma in Mr. Xie's skull had been cleared, and his condition had temporarily stabilized. The cause of the bleeding would need further examination once his condition completely stabilizes after more than half a month. However, in future treatments, plasma will certainly be indispensable. Zhang also indicated that if the patient needed it, he was willing to provide the life-saving blood whenever necessary.

As soon as Mr. Sun found the school, the counselor Li teacher of the Communication Engineering major learned that his student had done a good deed, which made him feel very proud. "Not for fame or profit, it's really hard to come by." Li teacher introduced that Zhang has always been a cheerful and enthusiastic boy, currently serving as the class's Youth League Secretary. Li teacher stated that during the off-campus education class meeting, he would tell the whole class about this incident and publicly commend Zhang to encourage the students' enthusiasm for helping others.
