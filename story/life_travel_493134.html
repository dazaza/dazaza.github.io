Tibet travel by private car guide

by zhayandehudie on 2011-09-22 14:24:38

I have been engaged in accommodation, outdoor off-road activities, and tourism chartered vehicles in Tibet for a long time. With the increase of tourists in Tibet in recent years, many misunderstandings and prejudices have been bothering me. I have also given well-intentioned suggestions to some tourists for their wrong behaviors. Most friends can correctly understand and express their gratitude, and thus I have made quite a few good friends. But there are still some people who remain vigilant and indifferent. Personally, I can understand it, but I really feel regretful from the bottom of my heart. In order to let more friends truly understand the characteristics of Tibetan tourism and travel methods, avoid detours, and achieve the pursuit of spending less money to play with quality which is advocated by real travelers, I can't help but express myself. I will try my best to introduce and help everyone understand the related issues of Tibetan tourism! This article will introduce the relevant issues of renting off-road vehicles in Tibet.

Due to the unique tourism situation in the Tibetan region, most tourist attractions still require chartered vehicles. Regarding some common problems and misunderstandings about chartered vehicles, I will summarize them for friends who come to Tibet for tourism based on my own experience and insights, hoping to be helpful to everyone!

There are two common ways of traveling in Tibetan tourism: One is to travel with a tour group, the advantage is that you don't have to worry about anything, and the cost is relatively low (relatively speaking). The disadvantage is that the route is rigid, many small scenic spots along the way cannot be freely stopped for appreciation, the stopping time in the scenic area is short, and there are many shopping arrangements. It generally suits those friends who just want to visit. However, it is not suitable for in-depth tourism.

The second way of traveling is the common way for most backpackers. Generally, they freely gather companions and choose to travel by renting off-road vehicles or small minivans. Except for some scenic spots such as Namtso and Nyingchi regions, other major routes require off-road vehicles. Usually, four people share the rent of an off-road vehicle, which is the most common way of traveling in areas where the journey is far and the road conditions are poor. The advantage is that you can freely choose the route and scenic spots. As long as it doesn't delay the daily schedule, you can stop and take pictures at any time according to your preferences. Additionally, you can go to many branch small scenic spots and wild areas where other vehicles cannot reach. It's flexible and convenient, no shopping arrangements, and allows you to deeply experience the unique scenery of the plateau. It's the best way to travel in Tibet. The disadvantage is that the cost is slightly higher (relatively speaking), and disputes are more likely to occur.

Below, I will focus on how to rent an off-road vehicle and avoid disputes, as well as some techniques and misunderstandings to make renting an off-road vehicle more worry-free and enjoyable. As long as you grasp some basic knowledge, the problems you are worried about will basically not be problems.

One: About the choice of off-road vehicles:

In Tibet, the most common off-road vehicles with a good reputation are imported Toyota and Mitsubishi, and domestic vehicles are Leopard. Choosing a model is a relatively complex issue, and it's also the most common misunderstanding. Imported Toyota and Mitsubishi vehicles are popular among tourists because of their sturdy construction and stable quality. However, there are also many issues. Most friends who have been to Tibet know that Toyota 4500 is the most popular here, followed by the original Mitsubishi Pajero. Since there are more Toyota 4500 vehicles, its popularity is the highest, while there are fewer original Mitsubishi vehicles, and fewer people know about Mitsubishi cars. Due to the good reputation in Tibet, these two models have the most counterfeit modified vehicles. From the comparison of these two models, we can see that the relative comfort of both is similar, but Toyota is slightly wider than Mitsubishi. In terms of off-road performance, there is not much difference between the two models. In the four-wheel drive mode, except for the one hundred type of Toyota 4500 (which is rarely used for tourism), Mitsubishi Pajero's four-wheel drive mode is more advanced, featuring full-time four-wheel drive, part-time four-wheel drive, low-speed power-added four-wheel drive mode, etc., all four-wheel drive modes. Moreover, without getting out of the car, you can freely switch the four-wheel drive mode during high-speed driving according to the road conditions. On the contrary, the four-wheel drive mode of old-style Toyota cars is relatively simple, only having one four-wheel drive mode of locking differential. Unless the car is stuck, the four-wheel drive cannot be used on paved roads. Additionally, switching the four-wheel drive mode requires stopping the car and operating on the front wheels. Relatively, this is outdated. However, the spaciousness, sturdiness, and easy maintenance of Toyota cars are also one of its advantages.

It's unnecessary to exaggerate specific models; the condition of the vehicle is the most important. Choosing a well-conditioned domestic vehicle is better than choosing a shabby old imported vehicle. This is one of the common mistakes made by backpackers when renting vehicles for tourism in Tibet.

Two: Choosing the driver:

Personally, I think that when choosing and renting a chartered vehicle, especially an off-road vehicle, the importance of the driver outweighs the condition of the vehicle. This is the key that backpackers tend to overlook easily. Indeed, even if backpackers consider this point, it is difficult to judge the character of the driver, and this is also the reason why disputes often occur. Therefore, I give you a few suggestions. You can rent a car through a regular travel agency because the driver can only get the full payment after completing the tour without complaints. First, try to consult friends who have rented cars in Tibet to see if they have the phone number of a suitable driver. If you haven't found a suitable driver yet, after arriving in Tibet, you can first choose a shorter route for short-term rental of the vehicle. A one or two-day trip is enough to understand a driver, then consider long-distance rental of the vehicle. For example, a ten-day trip to Ali or Sichuan-Tibet line, without a suitable driver and vehicle, your journey will certainly be less enjoyable, leaving many regrets.

Most backpackers who come to Tibet for tourism have done sufficient preparation in advance, which is very necessary. However, everything has a limit. Many people overdo it, easily going astray. To protect their own interests, they formulate a detailed and strict tourism contract. I once saw a contract prepared by tourists that was so detailed that it even specified how much the driver should be fined if the vehicle repair exceeds a certain amount of time. Frankly speaking, people are reciprocal. Empathize with each other. Drivers also work hard for their money. No driver would willingly break down halfway. If it happens, mutual understanding is needed, and it's better to provide help to the driver. The key lies in how you treat the driver. Do you treat the driver as a friend or as an employee? This is particularly important. For long-distance trips, drivers also value passengers. If you treat the driver as a friend, the driver will certainly treat you kindly. Otherwise, the driver will purely regard it as an employment relationship. Alright, I'll go where I'm supposed to go, and won't go where I'm not supposed to go. Sorry, it's not included in the agreement. If you want to go, you need to pay extra. Such a journey will become a battle of wits, and it will be impossible to enjoy it. You must have known the answer.

To sum up, I personally believe that if the driver's character and other aspects are good and you recognize him, then the contract becomes irrelevant. Don't think that a contract guarantees peace of mind. Contracts are dead documents, but people are alive. If the relationship with the driver is not good, the contract is just a piece of waste paper. The driver can fully comply with the contract but still make you unhappy without finding fault. Of course, unless the tourists are too extreme, the driver generally wouldn't do that. It still depends on how you get along with the driver.

Most tourists are very sensitive to price when renting a car. Remember, price and tourism quality are completely proportional. Price is certainly important, but if you solely decide based on price, it's a big mistake. Generally, a good driver will quote a psychological price that ensures the quality of the journey under normal circumstances, which will not differ much from the market price. This is a reasonable price. Some unscrupulous drivers always attract customers with low prices to save expenses and reduce travel costs, inevitably leading to tricks on passengers, such as arranging shopping, taking kickbacks from scenic spots, compressing or reducing scenic spots and travel time, driving to dangerous sections and intimidating tourists with danger, tourists being afraid and unwilling to go, good-looking scenic spots along the way, tourists not mentioning and not stopping, or deliberately delaying reactions, sliding far away, most tourists are reluctant to return, and so on. For tourists, it's like picking up sesame seeds and losing watermelons, not worth it. Therefore, tourists can choose several drivers for comprehensive consideration. Prices should not be too high, but ultra-low prices should not be considered either. The average market price is reasonable.
