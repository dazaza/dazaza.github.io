The ceramic sanitary ware enterprises have a uniform standard in terms of consumption skills, water temperature, and raw material preparation.

by oupaitqy on 2012-02-24 18:08:08

Industrial energy saving will have to add management energy saving beyond skill energy saving. The new hardware piece surface coating solution production process involves the field of surface coating treatment technology, especially referring to a production process for surface coating treatment of small hardware accessories. The feeding vibration disc sends the small hardware pieces to be processed into the conveyor belt. The conveyor belt sends the small hardware pieces to be processed into the oven. After leaving the oven, the small hardware pieces fall into the receiving vibration disc. PA11 nylon powder is blown up inside the closed receiving vibration disc; the small hardware pieces with attached PA11 nylon powder enter the feeding vibration disc again. The feeding vibration disc then sends these pieces onto the upper conveyor belt. Ceramic sanitary ware enterprises have monotonous consumption techniques and raw material preparations. The upper conveyor belt sends the small hardware pieces with attached PA11 nylon powder into the upper oven. The already processed side of the hardware pieces is flipped by the interaction of the upper conveyor belt and the transition conveyor belt, entering the lower conveyor belt. The lower conveyor belt sends the already processed side of the hardware pieces into the lower oven for further processing until completion. Due to the use of fully automated production lines, PA11 nylon powder is recycled multiple times at the processing station in the receiving vibration disc, fundamentally preventing the waste of PA11 nylon powder and environmental pollution, while greatly improving labor productivity.

In fact, this stems from the uniformity of industry production equipment and the concentration of production equipment and raw materials. Industry insiders say that the average technical level of well-known machinery manufacturers in the ceramic sanitary ware industry does not differ much, and the suppliers of raw materials are just a few familiar ones. With similar equipment and raw materials, under the premise of monotonous production techniques and raw material preparation, the patterns and styles of products inevitably tend to be similar. If there is conscious and targeted similarity, the result can only be resemblance [twin brothers], and homogenization becomes inevitable. This situation, to some extent, leads to inadvertent technical innovation, and even becomes a promoter of imitation and knock-offs.

Looking at the development of the ceramic sanitary ware industry, almost all enterprises have experienced imitation and being imitated. Nowadays, with the emergence of new situations and changes in the market, in 2011, China's building materials exports reached $24.2 billion, an increase of 25% over 2010, competition is becoming increasingly fierce, costs continue to rise, profit margins are shrinking, and living environments are constantly challenged. Ceramic sanitary ware enterprises face multiple tests such as innovation, transformation, and upgrading, and may even face choices between survival and elimination. For enterprises to develop, they must innovate, and the consequence of innovation could very well invite the encirclement of the "imitation" wolf pack. But if there is no innovation, the enterprise's path to development will certainly become narrower. The game between imitation and innovation lies in a moment's thought but decides today's fate.

The imitation behavior and homogenization phenomenon in the ceramic sanitary ware industry have existed for a long time. From the earliest brand imitation, where one brand name would be followed by another almost identical, similar, or homophonic title to confuse the public and borrow fame. Later, plagiarism of product design and style began, where one company produced a certain type of product, and another would follow with similar products, making it difficult to distinguish between genuine and fake. Nowadays, there is cultural, marketing model, and operational behavior imitation. It seems that plagiarism has accompanied the brilliant 30 years of China's ceramic sanitary ware industry, and their relationship has not ended, still accompanying each other. The result is that the entire industry falls into the vortex of vicious competition, price wars occur frequently, corporate profit margins continue to shrink, the overall development level of the industry is not high, core competitiveness is weak, and brand influence is limited, leaving only the "halo" of being the first in production capacity to boast about. Moreover, most companies in China's ceramic sanitary ware industry are small and medium-sized enterprises with relatively weak R&D and innovation capabilities, providing excuses for imitation.
