A Letter to My Future Self

by wentfclove on 2011-09-17 20:55:33

Today in 2011, the university life will soon come to an end, and my student life will also come to a close. But whether it is completely over or not depends on this year. Looking back, the fifteen years of my student life seem to have more regrets than satisfaction. I did everything I shouldn't have done, and as for studying...

This letter written to my future self may not be very organized from beginning to end, but as long as I understand it, recognize it, and possibly grasp its meaning, that's enough!

At the same time, this letter written to myself is also dedicated to my friends. I hope we can encourage each other. For our own future, for all that belongs to us, let's cheer up and work hard!

So the you back then has learned to be content because the person who loved you the most is gone. Thus, self-reliance is your future. Actually, this is something you should have faced a long time ago, but you just didn't want to face it. Because family won't be with you until the end, parents will grow old one day, and one day you will have your own family. You cannot rely on others every day, you cannot have a permanent host. You need to grow up yourself, you need to live by yourself.

Now you can tell the heavens: I am still happy, because even though I feel empty, I remain cheerful, but I am no longer absurd. I sincerely try to feel this world because the world will not always live in darkness; it will eventually see the bright sun.

Finally, after 2011, I firmly believe that things will get better, we will work harder, everyone, let's cheer up together!

But now you have grown up. In fact, sometimes people grow up very quickly because it only takes one night. When your life was in a greenhouse, you wouldn't have this feeling. A sudden change in the family made you lose what was originally a perfect home, and also made you learn to grow up. This change might be heaven's punishment for being too happy and not knowing how to cherish it. Sometimes people should not be too greedy because happiness comes from being content!

In twelve years, you've taken too many detours. This should be the reason why your university life is spent every day taking exams. When others were studying hard, you were reading novels or sneaking out to watch NBA games. This doesn't seem like something a girl would do, but you did it anyway. And you could arbitrarily ruin others' results because someone was there to clean up your mess. The world is so beautiful, yet people trample on it like this, which isn't good at all. Actually, you know, but sometimes the heart is not controlled by the brain. Because you always think you are still a child, and that others' care is natural.

Your favorite sentence is "The sun always rises where there is vitality, and the moon always becomes hazy where there is hope. With dreams, there is hope; with dreams, there is a direction." This fills me with hope and faith. No matter how the road ahead is, you must persevere because you need to believe in yourself and give yourself hope. Even if you are walking on the edge of a cliff, you must take every step carefully because the society you will face in the future is like a cliff, and you cannot be careless. In the last year of study, don't think about unnecessary things. If love is lost, you can find it again, but time, once lost, is lost forever. You can warm up again when the light of hope lights up! Because you know, no matter how the road ahead is, you have no way back, only perseverance forward!

Your own dreams, your own efforts, the sweat you once shed, the tears you couldn't hold back, at this moment, have all been buried deep in your heart, covered by an increasingly strong character. Maybe, people become indifferent, or they move further away, but it cannot be denied that you are becoming more mature, full of passion and challenges towards life and existence.
