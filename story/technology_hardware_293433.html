Harbin, December 4th news (reporter Song Yang) - According to the "CNR News" at 13:50, in recent times, vegetable prices in Harbin have risen sharply, and the prices of rice and flour have remained high.

According to the monitoring data from Heilongjiang Province's price department: by the end of November, the average price of thirteen types of vegetables in Harbin was 2.67 yuan per catty, an increase of 50.85% compared to the beginning of November. Among them, the prices of celery, green beans, and tomatoes increased by more than 70%, and the price of leeks doubled. In addition, the prices of rice and flour in Harbin have also continued to rise. Yesterday, reporters visited some grain and oil shops and supermarkets in Harbin and found that the prices of rice and white flour were about 30% higher than last year. At the Hada Grain and Oil Wholesale Market, a brand of rice from Wuchang rose from 100 yuan/bag last year to 170 yuan/bag; a brand of flour rose from 60 yuan/bag to 72 yuan/bag; as a result, the price of noodles also increased.

It is understood that the main reason for the price increase is due to several snowfalls recently, which caused road passage difficulties, and even some highways were closed, resulting in delays in the transportation of out-of-town vegetables and rising vegetable prices. Additionally, the drop in winter temperatures has extended the growth period of vegetables, leading to a temporary shortage of vegetables, which has caused prices to rise. The rise in the prices of rice and flour is mainly because the weather affected this year's rice production, reducing yields. Moreover, rice manufacturers in the market now operate on a larger scale with higher quality, naturally leading to relatively higher prices. Furthermore, due to droughts in the wheat-growing regions, the harvest has decreased, causing the price of white flour to rise this year accordingly.
