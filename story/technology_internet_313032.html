Manchester United's acknowledged weakest link turns into a nightmare for opponents: A zero-transfer-fee defensive talent rises to fame in one match.

Sina Sports News - With the high-density schedule of playing twice a week and an injury-plagued defense, Sir Alex Ferguson had no choice but to use some less experienced players to temporarily fill the gaps. The Belgian player Rikard De Laet has thus become an indispensable figure on Manchester United's defense line. Just over 21 years old, De Laet's career development up until now could be said to have been rather bumpy. However, under Ferguson, as俗a saying goes: De Laet has found his rebirth.

Speaking of this season, De Laet's first appearance was in the League Cup against Wolves; today he faces Wolves again, this time in the higher-tier Premier League and starting from the beginning. Recent injuries to central defenders in a domino effect manner have indeed created opportunities for De Laet. But this is just on the surface. Ferguson first used him as a right-back, then moved him to center-back, daring to test such a player with only two games of league experience in the league itself, which clearly indicates Ferguson's strong confidence in his abilities.

In this match, De Laet played with considerable authority. The Belgian player is not afraid of tough confrontations, and against the Wolves team that planned to suppress Manchester United through aggressive tackling, you must be even tougher. De Laet made six tackles throughout the game, ranking first among both teams, successfully three times, and also made four successful clearances, ranking first within Manchester United. This shows that even if the opposing side deliberately targeted his position, the Wolves team did not gain any opportunities, even ones for mere consolation.

Passing the defensive tests is just one aspect. Being a right-back, De Laet must push forward to assist in attacks. Perhaps in this area, the Belgian player does not have many standout moments, but in the first half, he made a successful run down the right flank, sending the ball directly towards the goalmouth, where it was cleared by the opposing defender amidst the chaos. For De Laet, who was playing his first league game for Manchester United this season, you cannot expect too much. Additionally, when he switched to center-back at the 60-minute mark, he displayed a commanding presence, dealing with the ball decisively and cleanly. Standing at 1.86 meters tall, he handled the opposition's players over 1.9 meters effortlessly. After the game, Ferguson specifically praised De Laet's performance as a center-back, saying: "De Laet performed very well when moved to center-back, showing great potential. He is a natural center-back."

Including today's match, De Laet has played a total of five games for Manchester United, three in the League Cup and two in the league, with the last game of the previous season against Hull City being his first league appearance. Although five games might not seem like much, De Laet has already played left-back, right-back, and both sides of center-back, making him a standard versatile defender in the backfield. Looking to the future, De Laet has every chance to become a legendary figure for Manchester United, or at least someone like O'Shea, indispensable and capable of playing multiple positions. When he joined Manchester United in January 2009, he didn't cost the club anything, as Manchester United agreed to pay his former club Stoke City based on his performances. Clearly misjudging his potential, Stoke agreed at the time. Despite only a year passing, and Stoke possibly making a small profit off him, Manchester United is undoubtedly the sole beneficiary. Given Stoke's limited capabilities, they let go of a player who could have been a significant addition to their squad.

It's hard to imagine what would have happened if De Laet had set settling for Stoke City as his highest professional ambition and if Manchester United scouts hadn't noticed him. Transferring from Antwerp to Stoke City in 2007 was seen by many Belgians as a good opportunity, but being loaned out to Wrexham further dampened his professional aspirations. It might have been better for him to stay in Antwerp and play in the Belgian Second Division. First, the sharp eyes of Manchester United scouts identified him, and later, Ferguson's keen judgment gave Rikard De Laet the true opportunity for development.

One must marvel at Ferguson's ability to recognize young talents. The use of Macheda, the Da Silva brothers, and Obertan has already achieved initial success, with the key being that the costs of bringing them in were incredibly cheap. Obertan was the most expensive, costing only 3 million pounds, and De Laet represents the latest success. In August, the 16-year-old Paul Pogba was brought in from Le Havre, potentially the next surprise. Recently, he helped Manchester United's U18 team achieve a four-match winning streak, scoring three goals in four games, including one against Liverpool's U18 team. Just a few days ago, he scored within the first minute of a game, helping Manchester United's U18 team advance smoothly to the fourth round of the Youth FA Cup. On the 14th, in his first appearance for Manchester United's reserve team against Bolton, he not only scored the first goal but also provided an assist. Born in 1993, this player's growth rate is astonishingly fast. At this pace, a call-up to the first team could come at any moment. Ferguson's emphasis on young players, especially his precise and unique eye for talent selection, has laid a very solid foundation for Manchester United's future orderly development.
