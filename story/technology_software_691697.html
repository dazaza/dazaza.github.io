Court Ruling: Insufficient Evidence of "Cold Violence"

"How could the doctor-patient conflict accumulate to the point where the patient jumps off a building and loses their life in just over ten minutes?" said the hospital administrator. Wang Yun's family felt that the hospital did not meet the intensive care unit standards, failing to provide one-on-one care for the patient, resulting in serious negligence. They requested 4.6 million yuan in compensation. Wang Yun's daughter tearfully recounted that in the ICU, her father questioned the repeated inspections by asking, "Why are we still on IV drips?" The medical staff responded, "If I tell you to take it, you take it; otherwise, wait to die."

Wang Yun's daughter also described how her father entered the ICU wearing thick clothes. When the elderly man complained about being hot, the family opened the window for ventilation. The medical staff was described as having a rude demeanor, calling her father a 'child.' Due to the severity of his condition, the hospital informed the family to handle admission procedures.

Plaintiff: Cold Violence Provoked Elderly Man to Jump

A man over seventy years old admitted to the hospital's intensive care unit ended up jumping to his death. His family sued the hospital, demanding over 4.6 million yuan in compensation. The defendant hospital denied responsibility for Wang Yun's death, and the court dismissed the family's claims. Yesterday, Fengtai Court ruled to dismiss the plaintiff's lawsuit.

The hospital administrator described the situation: at the time, there were several patients in the ward, and it was impossible for the medical staff to monitor Wang Yun 24 hours a day. While the doctors were handing over shifts in the office, they suddenly heard a "clang" from the ward. When the medical staff rushed into the room, they found that Wang Yun had already jumped out of the window.

Hospital: Patient's Suicide Impossible to Prevent

"If they had a little more sense of responsibility, such a tragedy wouldn't have happened," Wang Yun's daughter rebutted. "My dad is a retired radio station official with a pension, his medical expenses covered by his company, and he has filial children. Why would he jump to his death?"

The administrator believed that Wang Yun, as a fully competent individual, should bear the responsibility for his suicide. The hospital couldn't prevent such actions and claimed no fault in the treatment process, thus denying any liability. On March 2nd of the same year, the elderly man's condition worsened, and he was sent to the ICU for treatment.

Additionally, the plaintiffs argued that the "cold violence" from the hospital's medical staff led to Wang Yun's suicide. However, apart from their own statements, there was no other evidence to support this claim, making it difficult for the court to accept.

"My father suffered from the 'cold violence' of the medical staff." Regarding the so-called "cold violence," the hospital administrator stated that in the ICU, medical staff might speak briefly due to the urgency of treatment but would not verbally provoke patients or engage in "cold violence."

The court determined that Wang Yun, as a fully competent individual under civil law, the hospital's duty of supervision towards him referred to medical monitoring, specifically observing vital signs like breathing, heartbeat, body temperature, and blood pressure, rather than supervising or caring for his personal life or property. In other words, the hospital had no legal obligation to supervise Wang Yun's voluntary termination of his own life.

Furthermore, Wang Yun's son-in-law stated that the "cold violence" from the hospital's medical staff greatly affected the elderly man, causing him mental harm, which ultimately led to his fatal fall. During the trial, the deceased Wang Yun's (a pseudonym) family recounted that on the evening of February 28th of the current year, the elderly man was admitted to the Dongfang Hospital of Beijing University of Chinese Medicine due to severe pulmonary infection via ambulance.
