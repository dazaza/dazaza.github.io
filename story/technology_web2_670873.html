Like strangers

by ruydyir02 on 2012-02-21 12:20:35

Junior high school, now that I think about it, seems like a long time ago.

In those years, we passed notes in class together; in those years, we bullied certain teachers together; in those years, we were arrogant and crazy; in those years, we intentionally blew out the candles when the school had a power outage; in those years, we watched "Smile PASETA" together during extra classes; in those years, we gave our all for the victories and defeats of the sports meet; in those years, we won first prize for singing about the eight honors and eight disgraces; in those years, we laughed when we wanted to laugh, cried when we wanted to cry, and confessed our feelings to people we liked, without any pretense or flattery; in those years, friendship was the most precious, and love was just a seasoning; in those years, although our grades were not good, we secretly swore to make the teachers look at us differently; in those years, we made the head teacher so angry that they wanted to quit; in those years, we begged the teacher to let us go out and have snowball fights; in those years, there were many joys and some sorrows, but when we look back, it's all sweet memories; in those years, we were passionate and immature; in those years, we were still in the same classroom, laughing and crying together.

I can no longer remember all the names of my classmates. We are all growing up, becoming beautiful or handsome. Everyone has their own life. Maybe one day if we meet on the street, we won't necessarily feel overly happy, just nodding and passing by quickly, or perhaps, like strangers. That's fine too, to avoid recalling too much and mocking the current distance between us.

Is it because I'm too lazy, or is it that time and distance are truly cold and cruel? Friendships will always gradually fade.

Just started university, this is the first winter vacation lasting nearly one and a half months. Thinking that my junior high school best friends have all gone home, I asked a good friend on QQ: "Let's hang out tomorrow." Reply: "Yeah, but I don't think I'll have much time tomorrow." "Oh, then let's wait until later when we have time." "Yeah." That's how it went. I kept thinking, what does having time mean? By the time we have time, will we already be old? When we don't see each other, we talk on the phone, as if there's an endless amount to say, constantly reminiscing about the past, saying that once we're home we'll play together well. But when we actually get home and meet, the words exchanged are trivial. Perhaps, this is growth. I tentatively view it as a kind of growth, so maybe it won't feel so sad.

Yes, I am an emotional person. I want to remember everyone who has appeared in my life, whether they were good or bad to me. Unfortunately, my circle is only so big. Some people come in, and others must go out, as if under an unbreakable curse. No matter what, I still believe that the friendships are there; I'm just too sensitive.

Because, friendship forever, right? Hehe.
