[Focus Points & Market Opportunities]

After a continuous rebound over three trading days, the Shenzhen and Shanghai stock markets adjusted on Thursday due to divergent individual stock trends. The Shenzhen and Shanghai indices fell slightly by 0.29% and 0.16%, respectively, both closing with bearish cross stars that have relatively long lower shadows. Despite the index correction, the overall market operation remained relatively stable, and the filling of the previous day's gap helped resolve the market's long-short divergence. After a short-term consolidation, it is not impossible for the market to once again gather momentum to challenge the previous high resistance.

From Thursday's market hotspots, two significant highlights are worth noting. First, the recently active Growth Enterprise Market (GEM) continued its collective strength. In fact, the recent strength of the GEM has played a significant role in maintaining market sentiment. The reason why the GEM is favored by the market lies first in its alignment with the current market enthusiasm for small- and medium-sized market value varieties. Among the currently listed 28 GEM companies, the majority of their circulating market values are below 1 billion yuan, with only a few exceeding this threshold, including JinYa Technology (300028, 1.028 billion), Huayi Brothers (300027, 2.332 billion), JiFeng Agricultural Machinery (300022, 1.611 billion), Lepu Medical (300003, 2.053 billion), Hongri Pharmaceuticals (300026, 1.121 billion), Robot (300024, 1.040 billion), Aier Eye Hospital (300015, 1.597 billion), Teradyne (300001, 1.348 billion), and ShenZhou TaiYue (300002, 3.433 billion).

Secondly, GEM companies generally possess high growth characteristics. Based on past experience, at the end of the year, there is usually a practice of mining annual report topics in the market, and stocks with high dividend and high bonus distribution themes often receive close attention from major funds. Most GEM companies have such expectations.

Thirdly, GEM companies have a certain scarcity characteristic. As a new phenomenon domestically, the GEM lacks good valuation references. In the current market environment where small-cap stocks are favored, they will inevitably become a focus for major funds, especially speculative capital. According to statistics, the average dynamic P/E ratio of the 28 GEM companies is 92.57 times. Although the market previously generally worried about the high issue price of the GEM already having a high premium on its high-growth features, the performance of these 28 GEM companies in the first three quarters of this year has seen significant increases. Therefore, their current high P/E ratios should be viewed dialectically. Additionally, 23 companies from the second batch of GEM companies have gone public. Of these, Haerbin Jiuzhou Electric, Hubei Huitian Adhesive, Beijing Meten Communication Technology, Shenzhen Xinzhoubang Technology, Guangzhou Yangpu Medical Technology, Beijing SuperMap Software, Hunan Zhongke Electric, Jiangsu Huarui Tianlong Photoelectric, Beijing Steel Research High-Na Technology, Jilong Micro-Electro-Mechanical Systems, Zhejiang Hexin Tonghuashun, Wuxi Baotong Belt Industry, and Juli Sling Tools were approved for their initial public offerings. The market expects the second batch of GEM companies to start recruitment this month, which has drawn attention to the GEM.

It is worth noting that after the sustained rise of the GEM in the recent stage, the short-term cumulative increase has been substantial. Moreover, the high growth of the GEM also hides high risks, and investors need to avoid blindly chasing highs and being overly heavily positioned during operations.

In addition to the GEM, the agricultural sector became another highlight of the market on Thursday, with XinNong Development, DunHuang Seed, DengHai Seed, and FengLe Seed all strongly closing at their upper limits. I believe that the strength of the agricultural sector is mainly due to the following three factors: first, the agricultural sector has been significantly underperforming in this year's market, so there is a demand for catch-up gains in the future. Second, against the backdrop of gradually rising inflation, future agricultural product prices will likely have further room for increase. Third, the impact of earlier disasters in the north led to reduced seed production and inventory declines, improving supply-demand relationships while some quality varieties' prices began to rise. Therefore, in terms of operations, I suggest investors pay close attention to two types of stocks in the agricultural sector: one is sub-sector listed companies with price elasticity and sensitivity, such as NanNing Sugar Industry, GuiTang Shares, XinNong Development, and XinSai Shares; the other is seed companies indirectly benefiting from the rise in agricultural product prices, such as DengHai Seed, DunHuang Seed, and LongPing Hi-Tech.

It is important to remind that regarding market trends, during the process of the market gathering momentum to break through, investors should closely monitor the direction of market hotspots, such as whether the low-carbon sector can successfully transition and orderly rotate, as well as the activity of blue-chip sectors.

[Risk Warning and Strategy]

According to foreign media reports, Vice Governor of the People's Bank of China Hu Xiaolian said on Wednesday that the current gold price is already at a historical high, and one must be cautious about the price bubble in gold assets. Currently, US gold futures prices have touched historical highs, and market analysts pointed out that apart from the weakening of the dollar, reasons for the sharp rise in gold prices also include rumors that China will increase its gold reserves again and large ETF purchases. Data shows that due to China's sustained trade surplus and the government's foreign exchange control system, the Chinese government has accumulated huge foreign exchange reserves. By the end of September 2009, China's foreign exchange reserve balance had reached $2.27 trillion, approximately 70%, or $1.6 trillion, of which were dollar reserves.

[Today's News Highlights]

On Thursday, the following information deserves investor attention:

1. Vice Chairman of the China Securities Regulatory Commission Yao Gang said on December 2nd that currently, with the economy stabilizing, the stock market has gradually recovered, and investor confidence has increased. Institutional investors currently hold about RMB 2.25 trillion in A-share market value, accounting for 17.2% of total circulating market value, an increase from before. Institutional investors are facing a good opportunity for further development. New technological revolutions are expected to bring greater development space for the capital market, and the capital market will also play a more active role in "structural adjustment."

2. A report released by the National Information Center on December 3rd suggested that next year, China's GDP growth rate could reach around 8.5%, roughly equal to 2009, achieving "double stability" in economic growth and prices. The possibility of obvious inflation in the short term is not significant, but future expansionary policies should not be further intensified. The report recommended that if the global economy continues to recover slowly, next year's macroeconomic policy orientation should remain basically unchanged, continuing to implement proactive fiscal policies and moderately loose monetary policies. The proportion of fiscal deficits to GDP should be kept around 3%, and the annual growth rate of broad money supply (M2) should be gradually controlled to around 18%. The scale of new loans should be controlled within RMB 8 trillion, and enterprises should raise more funds from the securities market than in 2009, ensuring relatively loose societal funding.

3. According to announcements on Wednesday, to maintain steady growth in base money and basic stability in money market interest rates, the central bank will issue the 63rd central bank bill of 2009 on December 3rd (Thursday). This central bank bill has a term of three months, with a scale of RMB 70 billion. The amount of matured funds in the open market this week is RMB 1.35 billion, and RMB 12 billion worth of one-year bills and RMB 45 billion worth of 28-day positive repurchase agreements were issued on Tuesday. It is expected that the open market will continue to see net withdrawals for eight consecutive weeks.

[Today's Market Situation]

After continuous rebounds over three trading days, the Shenzhen and Shanghai stock markets adjusted on Thursday due to divergent individual stock trends. The Shenzhen and Shanghai indices fell slightly by 0.29% and 0.16%, respectively, both closing with bearish cross stars that have relatively long lower shadows, with a combined turnover of about RMB 319.301 billion. From the full-day index movement trend, the Shanghai Composite Index opened at 3268.00 points and then declined due to the drag of gold, non-ferrous metals, banking insurance, etc., reaching a low of 3227.77 points near the 20-day moving average intraday. It filled the upward gap from the previous day, slightly recovering at the close, and finally closed at 3264.63 points. The Shenzhen Component Index accordingly carried out minor adjustments, but overall operation remained relatively stable.

From the market situation, there was a clear divergence in the performance of individual stocks between the two markets, with the Shenzhen market showing a ratio of 518:348 for rises and falls, and the Shanghai market showing a ratio of 508:377. In the top gainers list, 24 stocks, including Rainbow Shares, Sinoma Automobile, Chongqing Road & Bridge, Wuhan Water Supply, FiberHome Communications, XinNong Development, HaiMa Shares, and Lamei Pharmaceutical, hit their upper limits. In terms of sector styles, the GEM continued its collective strength, with an overall increase of 5.99%, where JinYa Technology, Dayu Water Saving, BeiLu Pharmaceuticals, Huace Testing, Lamei Pharmaceutical, and XinNing Logistics all hit their upper limits. Secondly, agriculture, automobiles, and chemical fibers also performed well.

On Thursday, the Shanghai Composite Index opened at 3268.00 points, with a high of 3270.37 points and a low of 3227.77 points, closing at 3264.63 points, down 5.12 points, or 0.16%, with a turnover of RMB 191.423 billion; the Shenzhen Component Index opened at 13789.08 points, with a high of 13822.91 points and a low of 13604.15 points, closing at 13759.83 points, down 40.67 points, or 0.29%, with a turnover of approximately RMB 127.828 billion.
