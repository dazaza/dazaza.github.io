The real cause of death of international pop diva Whitney Houston?

by anonymous on 2012-02-13 03:09:02

Whitney Houston

On February 12, a foreign report stated that Kristen Foster, the spokesperson for Whitney Houston, had just announced the news of Whitney Houston's passing on February 11, 2012. Instantly, the American music industry was shocked by the sudden loss of the legendary Black singing queen, Whitney Houston. On February 11, Whitney Houston (Whitney Houston) was found dead at the Hilton Hotel in Beverly Hills, Los Angeles, at the young age of 48. However, the cause of Whitney Houston's death is still under investigation by relevant U.S. authorities. According to reports, on Saturday evening local time in the U.S., Whitney Houston's press spokesperson, Kristen Foster, publicly announced the news of her passing. Subsequently, after confirmation by the media, it was revealed that Whitney Houston had died suddenly at the Hilton Hotel in Beverly Hills, Los Angeles. Renowned male and female stars in the global music scene, music writers, critics, entertainment tycoons, billionaires, NBA stars all paid tribute to the absolute elder sister of the music world and top diva, Whitney Houston (Whitney Houston).

Rumors circulated that Whitney Houston had used drugs in 2002. As the holder of the record for total album sales by a female singer globally, Whitney Houston, after battling drug addiction for many years, announced her return to the music scene last year. So, was Whitney Houston's death related to drug use?

Whitney Houston (English name: Whitney Houston) was an American R&B singer, actress, songwriter, film producer, and former model who won Grammy Awards. She was known worldwide for her powerful voice, multi-note expression, and wide vocal range, becoming a pop icon. Whitney has sold over 180 million albums worldwide. According to the Guinness World Records, Whitney is the most awarded female singer (having won 415 awards out of 562 nominations).

Whitney Elizabeth Houston was an American R&B singer, actress, film producer, and former model who won Grammy Awards. She was known worldwide for her powerful voice, multi-note expression, and wide vocal range, becoming an international music queen.

In the 1980s, Whitney was one of the few Black artists to receive substantial airplay on MTV during the male-dominated rock era. AOL Black Voices once said, "Although her success was like a lightning rod, it was always proven by her beautiful and powerful voice." This title of "The Voice" made her famous. She is the only female singer in the U.S. to have two best-selling albums in the top 35, and she is also the only singer to hold the record of seven consecutive weeks as the Billboard chart-topping single.

Whitney has sold over 170 million albums worldwide. According to Ebony magazine, Whitney was considered one of the top 100 most charming Black women in the world in the 20th century.
