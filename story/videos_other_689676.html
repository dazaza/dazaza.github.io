May 25th big three components quotation: memory price falls while CPU continues to rise

by anonymous on 2012-03-04 13:54:07

Yesterday, the EU conditionally approved Western Digital's acquisition of Hitachi's hard drive business, on the condition that WD sells its 3.5-inch hard drive factory. The Hitachi hard drive business may come to an end, and there are rumors of another hard drive price increase. The good news is that memory prices are showing a downward trend again, returning to the declining price range.

Today in Beijing's Zhongguancun spot market for Intel processors, Intel prices have experienced slight fluctuations with a weakening upward trend. The entry-level G530 increased by 10 yuan, now quoted at 270 yuan; the i5 2310 rose by 20 yuan, quoted at 1120 yuan. Additionally, three popular LGA775 processors saw small increases ranging from 5-20 yuan.

Today in Beijing's Zhongguancun AMD processor spot market, AMD processor prices continue to rise. The old 45nm Athlon remains the main force behind the price hikes, which had seen a temporary price drop but are now high again. Today's increase ranges between 5-15 yuan, mostly around 5-10 yuan.

Today in Beijing's Zhongguancun desktop memory spot market, memory prices turned towards a decline, led by Kingston, which has surprised many. Among them, the 2GB/1333 memory saw a significant drop, with prices inverted compared to Hong Kong's general distributor; the 4GB/1333 memory had a smaller decline. During the price hike period, Apacer/Adata's 4GB memory did not follow the trend, indirectly forcing Kingston to lower prices.

Today in Beijing's Zhongguancun laptop memory spot market, notebook memory prices remained stable, despite recent fluctuations. However, these changes have not affected the current prices. Kingston's 4GB/1333 notebook memory is quoted at 115 yuan. Adata and Apacer's 4GB/1333 notebook memory is quoted between 112-113 yuan.

Today in Beijing's Zhongguancun desktop hard drive spot market, hard drive prices face potential changes and may rise, specifically related to the Western Digital acquisition case. With early stockpilers gradually cooling off their sales, the sustained week-long decline in hard drive prices has been curbed, gradually aligning with Hong Kong's general distributor pricing, with pricing power reverting back to Hong Kong's distributors.

Today in Beijing's Zhongguancun laptop hard drive spot market, Seagate's 500GB 5400rpm/7200rpm two notebook drives fell by 10 yuan and 40 yuan respectively, now quoted at 520 yuan and 610 yuan. Hitachi notebook drive prices remain unchanged.

Intel prices saw slight fluctuations today in Beijing's Zhongguancun spot market, with weaker upward trends. The entry-level G530 rose slightly by 10 yuan, now quoted at 270 yuan; the i5 2310 increased by 20 yuan, now quoted at 1120 yuan. Additionally, three popular LGA775 processors saw small increases ranging from 5-20 yuan. (Note: All prices below were obtained by editors through on-site inspections at Zhongguancun counters, provided by certified agents, and are subject to change at any time, for reference only.)
