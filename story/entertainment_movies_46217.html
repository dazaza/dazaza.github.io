**Plot Introduction of Hitman 47**

The genetically engineered human "47" (played by Timothy Olyphant) is a product of advanced gene replication technology created by scientists to represent a new generation of humans. Due to their superior abilities far beyond those of ordinary people, these clones are trained as highly skilled professional assassins. "The Agency," a secretive organization specializing in various covert assassination missions, employs these genetically modified individuals. The man marked with the number "47" as a tattoo or barcode is one of their latest and most advanced creations.

After completing a series of successful assassination missions, Agent 47 becomes the most capable ace assassin for The Agency. Gradually, he begins to take on some of the most critical killing assignments. However, after his latest mission, he becomes entangled in a mysterious political conspiracy. Especially in Eastern Europe, where power struggles abound, he faces double pursuit from Interpol and the Russian military. From the Middle East to Moscow, from South Africa to London, 47 flees while also investigating the true culprit behind the conspiracy. Along the way, he encounters a special woman (Olga Kurylenko). The cold-blooded killer gradually changes under her influence, but his genetically enhanced life is also threatened with fatal danger...

**Behind-the-Scenes Production**

After years of ups and downs, adapting video games into movies has become an important means for Hollywood filmmakers to make money. Although sometimes they suffer setbacks like *Doom*, the lure of financial gain and the enthusiasm of countless game fans remain irresistible to many Hollywood producers.

Not long after the famous game adaptation *Resident Evil 3* stabilized at the top of the North American box office, another globally best-selling computer game, *Hitman 47*, was promptly adapted by Fox for the big screen. The production company, 20th Century Fox, boldly invested $70 million in this globally popular game and invited renowned French writer, director, and producer Luc Besson, known for works such as *The Fifth Element* and *Arthur and the Invisibles*, to serve as the film's producer, showing their determination to recreate the miracle of game-to-film adaptations.

The strong participation of the French team injected fresh vitality into the film—introducing young director Xavier Gens. At only 32 years old, the young French filmmaker Xavier Gens had previously apprenticed on several low-budget French films and served as an assistant director or second unit director on a few projects. After starting his independent directing career in 2000, Gens quickly demonstrated his talent, winning the Cognac Short Film Award for his thriller short *Morning*. Luc Besson then supported him with $3 million to direct his first feature-length film, *Frontiers*. Now, Gens has once again been promoted by his mentor, entrusted with a large budget to emulate his predecessors and make waves in Hollywood.

Interestingly, Xavier Gens himself is a passionate gamer who knows the original game inside and out. Cleverly recognizing this, Luc Besson allowed the gaming enthusiast to embark on his own "director's game." Gens first set the final tone for the film—maximizing the inclusion of violent elements from the original game, which is what loyal gamers would most want to see. To preserve the essence of the original work, the production team went to great lengths. They invited the game's music composer and original creators to ensure the movie stayed true to the source material. To align with the game's storyline, filming took place across multiple countries, spanning Europe, Asia, and Africa—from Cape Town to Istanbul, from St. Petersburg to London. The main plot unfolds in Sofia, Bulgaria. Clearly, Xavier Gens aims to faithfully recreate all the original game scenes on the big screen...
