"The Buried Sword" - Father and Son Special Forces in the Vietnamese Jungle War

by jqrxass on 2009-08-26 20:13:47

In 1995, China's retired old general Zhang Guodong and America's retired old general Carl Anderson arrived in Vietnam at the same time. They were strangers to each other but came for the same purpose: to find the remains of their sons who had gone missing 26 years ago during the Vietnam War. Zhang Guodong's son was named Zhang Jianhua and had been a special forces instructor; Carl Anderson's son was named Carl Tom and was also a member of the special forces. Zhang Guodong was accompanied by his grandson Zhang Chongwu, who was proficient in English, while Anderson was accompanied by his granddaughter Alice. Based on the clues they possessed, the two old generals began their investigation with the cooperation of relevant Vietnamese authorities. Unexpectedly meeting each other, they both had a strong feeling that their sons might have been opponents back then. Thus, they decided to share information and intelligence resources, and conduct a joint investigation.

It is the most tragic and sorrowful thing for parents to bury their children. Holding the pocket watch, Zhang Guodong told Carl Anderson that their sons had died at the same moment. There is an ancient Chinese saying: "We don't ask to be born on the same day, but we wish to die on the same day." This saying originally referred to sworn brothers of different surnames, but it turned out to apply to these two enemies.

"The Buried Sword" is a long novel describing the jungle warfare between Chinese, Vietnamese, and American special forces during the Vietnam War era. It showcases the love and hatred of two generations of soldiers and artistically recreates the image of heroes. A war legend sealed away for forty years, a journey to find family after the smoke of battle has cleared; a grand war canvas, a stirring battlefield sentiment! A literary masterpiece that delves into the cultural foundations of China and the United States, restoring the true history, and an artistic masterpiece displaying the love and hatred of two generations of soldiers, recreating the image of heroes.

The search process for Zhang Guodong and Carl Anderson was extremely difficult. Every time they reached a place, the clues would always break off, but each time there would be unexpected twists. As they investigated, scenes of brutal battles that took place in the jungle back then kept reappearing. Ultimately, they found the remains of their sons. Zhang Jianhua and Carl Tom had perished together. Carl Anderson found an identity tag on his son's remains; Zhang Guodong found a rusted pocket watch under his son's remains. This pocket watch was a war trophy he had captured during the Korean War.
