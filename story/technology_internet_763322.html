Today, Li Bin, the head of Suning.com, has become extremely angry! He fiercely criticized JD.com for employing a large number of internet trolls to smear Suning.com on Weibo.

"Over these two days, dozens of major accounts have coincidentally reposted an old article titled 'The Secret of Cash Flow'. Is this purely coincidental? Last year, they established a 'Beat Suning Command Center', and this year, they've transformed into a 'Smear Suning Command Center'? What exactly do you intend to do?"

Clearly, Li Bin's remarks were directly aimed at JD.com.

Hunter Cloud contacted Li Bin, but as of Hunter Cloud's publication time, Li Bin had not commented on his Weibo post. JD.com also did not make any public response.

Industry insiders stated that JD.com and Suning have "developed grudges" during their price wars in e-commerce. The future open and covert struggles between the two parties are expected to intensify. "Healthy competition benefits the industry's development, but if it escalates to smearing, it is truly regrettable."

Subsequently, Xu Lei, the marketing director of JD.com, publicly denied it: "The suit e-commerce is just imagining things again, really TMD bored out of their minds. My PR colleagues are busy with the pop conference, otherwise even I would be dragged in if I weren't on vacation."

In Li Bin's Weibo post, he mentioned an article titled 'The Secret of Cash Flow', which Hunter Cloud published below for the readers' reference:

"I believe, through my vivid analysis, you will gain profound insights into Suning, Gome, and JD.com! Not to boast, but my article is worth at least 1000 yuan because I have done business and understand the critical aspects, unlike those who only read news and pretend to be knowledgeable! No one can explain such important issues clearly with such vivid language!"

"Cash flow, do you know how crucial it is for doing business?"

"In my previous article, I already mentioned that Gome and Suning (hereafter referred to as Meisu) don't actually make money from selling home appliances, or only earn minimal profits. Someone might ask, 'Then why do they open so many stores? Why do they sell at such low prices?' At this point, journalists and pretentious people would tell you: They aim to expand their scale, crush competitors, and profit from their scale advantage. It doesn't matter if they aren't making money now; once they defeat their rivals, they can make big profits later, right?"

"Let me tell you: That's pure nonsense! Their purpose is to obtain cash flow!"

"Is cash flow that important? Yes, because Meisu rely on cash flow for capital operations to generate profits."

"Here's an example: Suppose I give you 10,000 yuan every day (note: every single day), and require you to return 10,000 yuan after three months (note: also daily). Your annual cash flow would be 3.65 million yuan. Are you making money? No. But the smart ones among you have probably realized that you always have three months' worth of money, which is 900,000 yuan. Theoretically, this 900,000 yuan stays in your hands forever, right? Can't you deposit it in the bank and earn interest? If you deposit 900,000 yuan long-term, you could earn tens of thousands of yuan annually, right? So, as long as it's managed properly, cash flow itself can generate profits!"

"The principle is quite simple, isn't it?"

"For Meisu, assuming an annual revenue of 10 billion yuan, delaying payment by three months gives them 2.5 billion yuan in cash flow! Delaying payment by six months gives them 5 billion yuan! If they aggressively expand their store network to increase cash flow, reaching an annual revenue of 20 billion yuan, delaying payment by three months gives them 5 billion yuan, and by six months, 10 billion yuan!"

"Would Meisu deposit this money in the bank? Hehe, before 2007, during China's rapid economic growth and inflation, and in the era of skyrocketing real estate prices, wouldn't it be foolish not to invest in real estate or engage in capital operations with all this money? Buy a piece of land, build a building, and in a few years, the property value triples—how much money would you make? Moreover, once Meisu earn money through real estate and capital operations, banks would even beg them to take loans. With cheap bank funds, they can make even more money!"

"So, selling home appliances is merely a means for Meisu to obtain cash flow! Selling home appliances only makes a little bit of money, and even if they lose some, it's fine because they can earn much more elsewhere! But the premise is: There must be sufficient cash flow!"

"This is the fundamental reason why Meisu aggressively open new branches!"

"Therefore, analyzing the stock price of Suning (and companies with huge cash flows) absolutely cannot be based solely on the sale of home appliances themselves! Some institutions and pretentious idiots still analyze Suning's sales and gross margins and confidently predict its growth prospects, causing retail investors to also look forward to Suning's growth potential. "What about the current situation of Meisu?"

"We need to start with cash flow: Gree requires advance payment before delivery, leaving no room for Suning to benefit, hence Suning is reluctant to sell Gree products. "From hundreds of billions suddenly reduced to 4.6 billion yuan, how many hidden projects, how many loss-making projects urgently needing cash, would be exposed due to the drastic reduction in cash flow? "Gome and Suning both have numerous real estate projects whose values have significantly depreciated. In 2007, the overall Chinese economy was still good, allowing Meisu to continue operating with accumulated reserves and bank loans. However, over the past few years, China's economic situation has not improved, and banks have tightened control. Consequently, Meisu will increasingly face cash shortages. Now you understand the severity of their situation, right?"

"Alright, let's analyze the current business environment of Meisu and see what their prospects are:"

"Meisu can no longer adopt the past business model of aggressively occupying cash, rapidly expanding stores to enlarge cash flow, and then using the cash flow for extensive capital operations. Third, Meisu's limited cash flow cannot fund as many good projects as before because China's overall economic slowdown and the severe commercial real estate market mean fewer opportunities for Meisu. However, closing a store is like cutting off a piece of flesh, as it involves losses in various areas!"

"In summary: Tightened cash flow, significantly reduced profits or even losses, this is the current situation facing Meisu. Relying on e-commerce? It can only temporarily provide cash flow, and in the medium to short term, it is impossible to make a profit!"

"Therefore, the good old days of Meisu's rough-and-ready management have completely passed. Their situation is quite difficult. "You understand now, right? Only someone like me, who has done business and understands the importance of cash flow, and has the leisure to write about it, can vividly explain these principles! Anyone who has done business understands what I mean, and what I am saying is not profound. It's simply that I have done it, while those who only pretend haven’t, so they cover their ignorance by pretending."

"In theory, as long as there is cash flow, losing money doesn't matter! As long as the cash keeps flowing, slightly delaying repayment by a few days or months, who knows if the company is making or losing money? Now you understand how important cash flow is, right?
