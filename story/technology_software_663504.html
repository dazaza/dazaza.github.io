Every detail is crucial in online marketing.

by sspj8031 on 2012-02-11 20:57:42

Compared with the traditional sales model, online marketing is a way to expand emerging markets. Due to its relatively low cost and numerous successful cases, it has attracted widespread attention from small and medium-sized enterprises. However, many people have aspirations but lack understanding of what exactly the concept of online marketing is. Some people think that online marketing simply means building a website; others believe that the essence of online marketing is selling things on the Internet, or more straightforwardly, making money online. As a result, many companies rush into promotions without having a complete website, only to abandon them hastily due to poor results. Moreover, the transformation of some traditional enterprises into e-commerce is not smooth sailing, with more failures than successes. Therefore, doubts inevitably arise: Is online marketing, which originates from the virtual world, trustworthy?

The answer is unequivocal. E-commerce online marketing is undoubtedly an effective supplement to corporate marketing models and will inevitably evolve into the mainstream marketing model. "If you don't engage in e-commerce now, you will have no business in five years," we may not delve deeply into whether this statement was made by Bill Gates or borrowed by Jack Ma, but online marketing is absolutely a trend. There is an example to support this. Five or six years ago, perhaps few of us knew about online shopping, but now? From Taobao to JD.com, from home appliances to clothing, food, housing, and transportation, online shopping is everywhere. But why do some companies fail frequently when trying out online marketing? Shanghai Website Construction Company Yinhai Technology believes that the lack of details causes many small and medium-sized enterprises to lose at the starting line. Without discussing e-commerce, online marketing is a systematic project, and every detail of the three most basic steps is crucial.

Step One: Website construction is very important, and analysis and planning are indispensable.

Before constructing a website, more important than the actual construction is planning, which many small and medium-sized enterprises neglect when building a website. What kind of website a company should build, what domain name to use, how to layout the website pages, and what sections should be set up—these fine details cannot be overlooked. Many companies now follow the common practice of finding one or several network companies to propose a few plans, then comparing prices and starting production. What kind of effect will such websites bring? If only they realize that a website is an organic part of online marketing, they might not be so rash and rough. In my opinion, a more reasonable approach would be for companies needing a website and those constructing the website to carefully analyze the website's positioning and browsing audience together, defining the website's functions and page design based on demand, www.taobao.com.

Website construction is also extremely critical because the website is the execution result of the preliminary planning of online marketing and the carrier of website marketing performance. For example, when we promote through advertisements on search engines, users naturally click on the ads and are directed to the website for conversion. If the website design is not effective, it cannot retain users, let alone convert traffic later on. Under the guidance of planning and website construction solutions, how does the importance of website construction manifest? I believe it lies in the details. It can be affirmed that two websites with the same functions and sections will offer different user experiences depending on the works of different website construction companies. There are many details related to website construction, such as page loading speed, some humanized color matching or text enhancement, etc.

Step Two: Optimize the entire site from the perspective of users and search engines.

In many people's minds, when they mention optimization, the first thing that comes to mind is the search engine. This thought is understandable because more and more people are using search engines to look for the information or services they need. A company with a website hoping to conduct online marketing cannot ignore the importance of search engines. Almost everyone engaged in website construction or online marketing knows that search engines measure website weight through two factors: content and links. This is why some people summarize the essence of search engine optimization as "content is king, link is emperor." However, website optimization is far more than just writing original articles and posting external links. If it were that simple, almost every website could achieve ideal rankings. Where exactly does the secret of website ranking lie? Perhaps only the search engine itself knows. Nowadays, there are many teams specializing in website optimization diagnosis, such as the SEO Optimization Team of A5 Station Master Network. With the help of professional teams for diagnosis and guidance, one might avoid taking some detours in website ranking.

Besides search engine rankings, website optimization has another layer of meaning, which is user experience. And I believe that the main focus of website optimization should be on enhancing the user experience of the website. Because no matter what promotion method is used, the goal is to make users see the website. If the website design is ineffective, all marketing efforts will be in vain. There are many ways to enhance user experience, such as improving website access speed and enriching and perfecting website content from the user's perspective. However, enhancing user experience must avoid going too far. For example, some websites expect to leave a deep impression on users with videos, animations, and constant pop-up windows recommending products or services, resulting in users becoming annoyed and closing the website. I wrote an article a few days ago titled "Don't Fall Into the Subjective Trap, User Experience in Website Construction is Important" which outlines six excessive behaviors in online marketing that are worth considering when building a website.

Step Three: Choose appropriate methods for promotion based on the user.

With the in-depth development of the Internet, there are now many ways to promote online. However, online promotion must choose methods suitable for oneself, and analyzing user behavior is crucial. Let's take the website construction industry as an example. Promotion methods include posting on forums, writing tweets, publishing soft articles on major portals, placing ads on industry portals, and even conducting EDM email marketing. Among all these promotion methods, which one yields the best results? Search engine promotion is undoubtedly the most effective promotion method due to the nature of the industry. For instance, if a company selling infant products wants to conduct online promotion, search engine marketing might not be the best choice if targeting end-users. Promoting on Taobao or some baby-related forums and portals might yield better results. Similar examples abound. In short, the core principle is to carefully analyze user consumption habits before engaging in online promotion; otherwise, it will be time-consuming, labor-intensive, and counterproductive.

I believe many people receive countless advertising SMS messages every day, which is also a form of promotion. The purpose of mentioning advertising SMS here is to remind online marketers that when promoting online, one must consider not only user habits but also user feelings. Taking our website construction website as an example, using the EDM email marketing method for promotion, under the targeted mass mailing of tens of thousands of emails, it will definitely have some effect. However, if not well controlled, the more emails sent, the worse the effect might be. Often, I see many blog comments and website article reviews that are blatant advertisements. What's the point of doing this? Not only does website construction require user experience, but website promotion also needs to value user experience. Marketing details are omnipresent, and so is user experience, like prenatal music.

Every detail of website construction and online marketing is crucial to success. A simple, memorable domain name, a secure, stable, and fast host, a good website, and a network promotion method that fully considers user experience, etc. Shanghai Website Construction Company Yinhai Technology believes that there is no such thing as accidental success, and online marketing is no exception. Only by truly putting oneself in the user's shoes and integrating user experience into every detail of online marketing can online marketing proceed smoothly. Before writing this article, I was preparing to shop online for an exhaust fan. I originally thought that the online shopping sites of traditional appliance chain enterprises like Suning and Gome would be more powerful (after all, they are professional appliance companies), but I didn't expect that after searching for products for a few minutes, I couldn't open the product page. However, YiXun and JD.com websites opened instantly and provided quick search results. Observing a small aspect reveals the whole picture. Perhaps some companies still have advantages now, but if they don't realize the importance of details and user experience, the e-commerce transformation path of traditional enterprises will become increasingly difficult.
