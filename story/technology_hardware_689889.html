The south still has a lot of overcast and rainy days, and measures need to be taken to ensure the spring sowing weather forecast news center sina.com

by psyzems3 on 2012-03-04 17:01:56

The Central Meteorological Observatory predicts that in the next ten days, in the 30-day weather forecast for Song County in Henan Province, most areas in the south will experience more overcast and rainy weather. The total precipitation will generally be 30-60 millimeters. In some areas of Jiangnan and northern Guangxi, there will be 80-180 millimeters of rain. The main precipitation periods will occur from March 3rd to 7th, during which heavy rain to storm will occur in some parts of Jiangnan and northern Guangxi. Additionally, as we enter March, cold and warm air frequently converge in southern China, leading to an increase in strong convective weather. Timely measures must be taken to guard against adverse effects brought by thunderstorms, hail, and heavy rains.

According to China Weather Network, there is no sign of improvement in the recent prolonged low temperature, overcast rainy (snowy), and insufficient sunlight weather conditions in the southern regions. It is expected that in the next ten days, the southern region will still have more overcast and rainy weather, and precautions need to be taken.

Entering March, Jiangnan, Guangxi, and southwestern regions should ensure the smooth progress of spring planting. They should seize the favorable conditions of rising temperatures and sow early rice and single-season rice in time. Measures such as plastic film seedling cultivation should be adopted to ensure the smooth emergence of seeds and normal growth of seedlings. Southern agricultural areas need to strengthen water and fertilizer management for summer harvest oilseed and grain crops to promote their growth and development. Sichuan's southern part and central-northern Yunnan should carry out rational water use and scientific drought resistance work to ensure the smooth progress of spring planting. Prolonged low temperature and overcast rainy weather have led to continuous excessive moisture in the soil in most parts of Jiangnan and Guangxi, causing waterlogging in some farmland. The growth of some late-sown live-seeded rapeseed, open-field vegetables, and facility crops has slowed down. Vegetables have shown yellowing seedlings, dead seedlings, rotten leaves, and roots, resulting in reduced yield and quality, significantly delayed market availability, and a decrease in the vegetable re-cropping index. The opening period of spring tea in Jiangnan and the flowering period of rapeseed have also been postponed. The lower temperature caused the plum blossoms in Nanjing to bloom about 7 days later than usual.

Chen Zhenlin, director of the Disaster Prevention and Mitigation Department of the China Meteorological Administration, introduced at a routine press conference that since February, the number of rainy days in Jiangnan, Guangxi, and Guizhou has generally been 10-15 days. In most parts of Jiangnan, eastern Guangxi, and most parts of Guizhou, the number of rainy days exceeded 15 days. The number of rainy days in these areas was generally 3-9 days more than the same period in previous years, with some areas in Guizhou having more than 9 extra days. Temperatures in Jiangnan, western Guangxi, and Guizhou were 1-2°C lower than the same period in previous years, with some areas in Guizhou, Guangxi, Hunan, and Jiangxi being 2-4°C lower.
