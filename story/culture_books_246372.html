"How to Become the Big Brother: Management Lessons from Jin Yong's Martial Arts Novels"

by jqrxass on 2009-10-11 18:26:56

"How to Become the Big Brother" draws material from Mr. Jin Yong's fourteen martial arts novels, analyzing and elaborating on content that spans many areas of leadership and management. These include strategic planning, team building, employee motivation, goal management, performance evaluation, organizational change, crisis response, talent development and utilization, successor selection, recruitment interviews, and corporate image management. The language style of this book is both serious and humorous, appreciated by both the refined and the common, allowing readers to appreciate the management wisdom of the martial arts master and thereby understand how to become an outstanding leader akin to a "Big Brother."

Completely different from many management books on the market that fabricate stories based on fictional characters to illustrate their points, this book stays true to representative characters and plots from Jin Yong's original martial arts works. It uses the perspective of leadership and management studies to analyze and distill many valuable and beneficial viewpoints, hoping to assist leaders in their management practices.

The book is ingeniously conceived with rigorous logic, being the pioneering work in Jin Yong's martial arts management studies. While providing great insight for fans, it also aims to spark interest from the grandmaster Jin Yong himself, hoping he might return to write about management theory, engaging in intellectual exchanges on "Management in the midst of swordplay and shadows" with the author, which would be a great joy for all fans.

How to Become the Big Brother - Part One

Xuan Ci made a major decision-making error. Such a Xuan Ci should have been placed among the leaders to be despised. However, more than thirty years after the dissolution of the temporary organization he led, when he had no coercive power over its members, the few remaining members willingly and spontaneously sacrificed their own and even their families' lives and reputations to protect his interests and dignity.

How to Become the Big Brother - Part Two

Xiao Feng's first experience with a crisis occurred during his time as the leader of the Beggar Sect. At that time, Deputy Leader Ma Dachun was killed by someone using his own signature move, "Throat-Locking Grappling Technique." Given that Murong family from Gusu was known for "Using your methods against you," the entire sect suspected the Murong clan and launched a large-scale southern expedition to hold them accountable.

But all of this was just part of a conspiracy.

How to Become the Big Brother - Part Three

The "Falling Petals Flowing Water" team, with the Southern Four Heroes at its core, was a very powerful team. As the evil character Blood Knife Elder said: "One-on-one, I am not afraid. If they fight together, I will lose more often than win, and I must flee. If three of them fight me, I will be utterly defeated, likely unable to escape. If all four attack me, Blood Knife Elder will die without a trace..." Yet perplexingly, the powerful "Falling Petals Flowing Water" team, along with a group of two or three hundred people, was completely routed by Blood Knife Elder who was alone and carrying a burden (Dai Yun with broken legs), resulting in three deaths and one surrender. What exactly caused this?

How to Become the Big Brother - Part Four

As the saying goes, "The revenge for a father’s murder cannot be shared under the same sky." Driven by a strong motive to avenge his unjustly killed father, Yuan Chengzhi infiltrated Huang Taiji's "Chongzheng Hall" alone, intending to assassinate him.

But during Yuan Chengzhi's brief moment of eavesdropping outside the hall, Huang Taiji and his Han Chinese ministers discussed state affairs and military strategies, words that deeply moved Yuan Chengzhi, making him forget his original purpose of assassinating Huang Taiji. Instead, he secretly hoped to listen for longer.
