Why does my boyfriend chat with others? I found out, but he insists that I am wrong and says that I don't trust him. Are men all like this, telling lies with eyes wide open?

by lk0zswa on 2009-08-21 07:01:47

My boyfriend and I live in different cities, so we don't get to see each other often. Every day, besides talking on the phone, we also log into QQ during work hours and chat while working.

For me, after getting to know him, QQ became our dedicated communication channel, and I no longer chatted with others. As for him, he used to know a lot of female netizens. Due to the rampant cheap emotions and casual encounters on QQ nowadays, I asked him not to chat with others, and he agreed.

However, from time to time, I would feel that his typing speed slowed down, and my woman's intuition told me that he might be chatting with someone else. So sometimes I would jokingly ask him if he was chatting with some other girl, or I would directly tell him not to chat with others. In most cases, he would confidently tell me that he wasn't doing anything like that and sometimes even send me screenshots of his QQ online status.

Sometimes, I also felt that it was not good to be suspicious all the time, and I decided to trust him completely.

However, for some reason, I always couldn't fully trust him and tended to make trouble out of nothing according to what he said.

I analyzed that there were several reasons why I didn't trust him: First, he used to like hanging out and joking around with girls, but he has become more restrained since being with me; Second, before me, he had a relationship with a netizen, which made me think he lacked immunity; Third, once when I checked his chat records, I found that he called a girl "baby" and they chatted frequently, and also texted and called each other (at that time, we had a big argument because of this, and he insisted that they were just ordinary friends), I believed what he said, but sometimes when I thought about it, I still couldn't let go; Fourth, due to my personal reasons, I am an extremely insecure person, prone to suspicion, and sometimes too extreme about things.

I know my own problems, but I also blame him in my heart for not considering my feelings. However, as these issues were discussed repeatedly, he became unhappy, and so did I. It seemed like there was no way to resolve them, and we fell into a recurring vicious cycle.

Later, because I knew his QQ password, I looked up the QQ numbers of a few of his close female netizens. Then, I registered a new QQ account and added his friends as my friends, so I could clearly know when they were online.

When I chat with him, I usually only open one QQ account, but when I intuitively sense that something is wrong, I immediately open another one. If I find that his friend isn't online, I will blame myself for being too suspicious. But sometimes when I see them online, I will casually ask him if his friend is online. I hope he will honestly tell me, but he says no. At that moment, I feel very disappointed. He clearly promised me that he wouldn't chat with others, but he can't keep his word.

Recently, one evening at 10:00, while we were chatting, I realized something was off again, so I opened the other QQ account and saw that one of his hottest netizens was online. I indirectly asked him if his friend was online, and he firmly said no.

I asked again, "Is that so?" Unexpectedly, he immediately got angry and asked me what I wanted to do, saying that I didn't trust him at all and always made trouble out of nothing. He even said that if we couldn't trust each other, we might as well break up. His attitude was as if I had wronged him, but in fact, he was lying, and they were indeed chatting. Why does he act so confidently after doing something wrong and even bite back?

This isn't the first or second time he's done this. Clearly, he is the one lying, but he always blames me for not trusting him. I know it's wrong of me to investigate him, but if I don't, should I just let him deceive me?

My worldview and values tell me that if I do something wrong, I would feel guilty inside and wouldn't talk back so strongly, but why is he different?

Is this related to character?

Every time he gets angry, I don't know what to say. I dare not tell him that I know his secrets through such means.

However, I really don't understand, are men all like this? Do they act confidently even after doing something wrong?

I hope male friends can help me analyze his psychology from a man's perspective.

Also: Usually, he treats me well, and I know his feelings for me. I also know that he has nothing serious with those online friends. But for some reason, maybe due to the reasons mentioned above, I am very uneasy about this aspect. It feels like there's a "knot" in my heart, and I often have wild thoughts. What should I do?

I think that if this problem isn't solved and my mind isn't set at ease, we will eventually break up due to endless suspicion, deception, suspicion, and deception.
