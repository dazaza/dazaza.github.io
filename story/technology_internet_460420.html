Manual DIY Snack One: Green Tea

Today, we ran out of green tea drinks at home. I was originally planning to buy another box, but the weather is too hot and I'm lazy to go out. Then I suddenly noticed that the ingredient list on the green tea bottle shows a very simple formula. So, I got an idea and used tea leaves, water, white sugar, and honey to mix it up and put it in the fridge to chill. When I tried it just now, it tasted just like the one sold in supermarkets!

The simplest method is to use black tea bags steeped in water + rock sugar/honey/white sugar (just make it sweet!) + a slice of dried lemon (available in most supermarkets).

Manual DIY Snack Four: Sweet Taro Heart~~~ I also like it~~

But it's so troublesome~~

You'll need taro, glutinous rice flour, starch, white sugar... And milk if you want it to taste better

Steam the taro until cooked, mash it into a paste~ Mix it with glutinous rice flour and milk, adjust the softness... Just enough to form shapes, which gives it a crispy outside and soft inside feeling~~ Haha

Manual DIY Snack Five: Mashed Potatoes (This one is easier than Sweet Taro Heart~~ Haha)

First, let's talk about mashed potatoes, then chicken juice

1. Steam the potatoes until cooked, mash them into a paste, add milk, salt~ Don't add too much milk, it won't taste good if it's too runny!

2. Chicken juice, use chicken broth (like Simmered Chicken Soup), add black pepper (this is very important! I think the chicken juice relies entirely on the pepper for flavor), heat it up, thicken with water and cornstarch, don't add too much cornstarch, otherwise it will be too thick@@~

Then pour it over the mashed potatoes~~ Bingo

Here's another slightly more complicated method for making mashed potatoes, mainly different in seasoning.

Mash the cooked potatoes into a paste, add sliced cheese, milk, yogurt, salt, black pepper, adjust the ratio according to your taste, just don't make it too runny. Add the cheese while the potato paste is still hot, or heat these two ingredients together in the microwave, mainly to fully melt the cheese.

Cut cucumbers, pickles, carrots (blanched), corn kernels, ham, etc., into small pieces. Choose your preferred ingredients, you can omit what you don't like.

Mix everything evenly, although it's a bit more trouble, it's absolutely healthy and delicious.

Manual DIY Snack Six: Honey-preserved Lemon

Slice the lemons and place them in a jar (the jar must be thoroughly washed and scalded with hot water)

Pour honey over the slices so each piece is coated with honey (some people use white sugar, but honey is healthier)

Seal with cling film and refrigerate, leave it for three to five days

Sour, sweet, fragrant, super delicious, beautifying, detoxifying, weight loss

Manual DIY Snack Seven: Let's talk about New Orleans wings~~ (It feels like revealing KFC's secrets@@~ haha)

These are delicious but expensive@@~ So I roast them at home, each time roasting over 20, it's really satisfying!

Homemade KFC New Orleans Wings (Simple Version)

Ingredients: Chicken wings (wingettes or drumettes are both fine), New Orleans BBQ sauce (sold at Metro, a big bag costs over 20 RMB, I've been using it for over a year)

Method: 1. Marinate the chicken wings in the sauce for 2 hours, don't use too much sauce, just coat the surface of the wings evenly

2. Preheat the oven to 200 degrees for 10 minutes, then bake the wings at 180 degrees for half an hour

Ready to eat!! The taste doesn't lose to grandpa's!

Homemade KFC New Orleans Wings (Complex Version)

Ingredients: 10 chicken wings, honey, soy sauce, salt, cooking wine (red wine also works), ginger, red dry chili peppers, five-spice powder, black pepper.

Method:

1. If using frozen chicken wings, thaw them first. Place 10 fresh chicken wings in a microwave-safe dish.

2. Add a little soy sauce, two tablespoons should suffice.

3. Add cooking wine or red wine, approximately two tablespoons, mainly to remove the fishy smell of the chicken wings.

4. Add salt, a little ginger shreds, a few chili seeds, a little five-spice powder (generally available in supermarkets), a little black pepper.

5. Mix the chicken wings and seasonings evenly, ensuring they are fully coated.

6. Cover the dish and marinate the chicken wings in the refrigerator's chilling compartment, preferably overnight (so preparation must be done in advance, cannot be done on the spot). If really urgent, marinate for 2-3 hours to ensure the flavor penetrates.

7. Take out the marinated chicken wings, brush with honey (this ensures the correct color and taste! Adjust the amount of honey brushed based on preference).

8. Place the honey-brushed chicken wings in the microwave, medium-high heat (usually the setting with three flames) for 11 minutes.

This was actually unintentional mischief.

But

Let's assume the VC content in beverages is genuine, since I haven't conducted experiments to test it. However, by using appropriate methods, like directly using VC tablets plus sucrose and flavoring, making a beverage that indeed contains VC, the cost... A bottle of beverage sells for 3-4 RMB.

Manual DIY Snack Thirteen: Let me teach you how to fry French fries

Someone above said the fried ones were a bit soft, this method won't make them soft

Cut the potatoes into strips, wash off the starch and drain the water, then put them in the fridge to freeze. After freezing for a few hours until they're hard, fry them in the oil until golden brown

And you can cut a bunch of potatoes and freeze them all, take out some to fry when you want to eat

If you cut the potatoes into thin slices, freeze them before frying, you get potato chips. Add seasonings, they're almost the same as the store-bought ones, just more challenging for knife skills

One more tip, if you want to fry French fries with less oil

Boil the fries in a pot for two minutes before freezing, drain the water, cool down, then freeze

This is from a home trick book, all other steps are the same, hahaha

Here's another one~ Buy more lychees when they're in season. Wash them at home, peel them. Use a toothpick to remove the seeds. Try not to damage the flesh. It's better to dig out the seeds from the top. Then fill the space left by the seeds with red bean paste (sweet, bagged, should be available in most supermarkets??) Fill it up. Then freeze it~~~ Once frozen, you can eat it as popsicles, they taste great~

Manual DIY Snack Fourteen: Another great method for fruit ice cream

Ingredients: Whatever fruits you like

Whipping cream (boxed whipping cream sold in supermarkets)

Lemon juice, corresponding fruit juice (add more for stronger flavor)

Tools: Blender

Method: Wash the fruits and cut them into small pieces (remove seeds if necessary, peel if necessary), place them in the freezer for over three hours until they're solid. Take them out and place an appropriate amount in the blender, add a little whipping cream (lemon juice and fruit juice optional, can skip), start blending. If too thick, add more cream, if too thin, add more fruit (pay attention to the "degree"!). The best procedure is: able to scoop out balls with an ice cream scoop and it's ready!

Here's a simplified version - steamed milk custard

Manual DIY Snack Nineteen: Spicy Spare Ribs with Shrimp (Just tested, a whole pot...)

Ingredients: Shrimp, spare ribs (small ribs are best), potatoes, sweet potatoes (yams), winter melon, mushrooms, bamboo shoots, lotus root, black fungus (basically add whatever you like...)

Seasonings: Scallions, ginger, garlic, bay leaves, star anise, tangerine peel, Sichuan peppercorns, dried red chili peppers, lantern peppers (I love spiciness, classmates who don't eat spicy can add less.), white sesame seeds, roasted peanuts. Dry pot seasoning (I couldn't find dry pot seasoning, so I used hot pot seasoning instead)

1. First, blanch the spare ribs (can add ginger, cooking wine to remove the fishy smell), discard the blanching water, replace with clean water and cook until tender, I set it to cook for about half an hour, zzf000zxyb4_Baidu Space, this way you also have spare rib soup, no waste. After cooking, take out and set aside for later use.

2. While cooking the spare ribs, you can trim the shrimp legs and whiskers, make a cut down the back, actually scissors work quickly, the deeper the better for flavor absorption.

3. Cut the potatoes, yams, winter melon into strips, slice the mushrooms, cut the lotus root into rounds.. etc., prepare the vegetables.

4. Fry the spare ribs and shrimp in oil until the surface is crispy yellow (basically look at the color), then fry the potato strips and yam strips until golden yellow. Fry the peanuts briefly.

5. Use the oil in the pot to sauté scallions, ginger, garlic, dried red chili peppers, lantern peppers, Sichuan peppercorns, add the hot pot seasoning, I used about half a package, stir-fry until fragrant, then add the spare ribs and shrimp and stir-fry for a bit, take out the spare ribs and shrimp, then add the mushrooms, lotus root, black fungus, winter melon strips, bamboo shoots, etc., stir-fry, then add some spare rib soup or water and simmer until the sauce reduces, then add the potato strips and yam strips, stir-fry, add chicken essence and sugar to taste, serve and place at the bottom of the pot, then place the spare ribs and shrimp on top, sprinkle with cilantro and white sesame seeds, and it's ready. You can eat first then add more dishes like in restaurants.. We didn't add more dishes.. Eating dry food stuffed us full..

Manual DIY Snack Twenty: Let's talk about Pineapple Buns with Ice and Fire

Buy a pack of small pineapple buns, cut them in half
