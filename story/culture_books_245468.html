"Lazy Person's Health Book by the Pillow" - Health is actually very simple.

by jqrxass on 2009-10-08 00:07:13

Nowadays, health has become a common "stock" for billions of people to achieve wealth and dreams. How should we manage our health? Should we entrust it to the experts called "doctors," allowing them to use advanced tools and technologies to help us recover when we lose our health? Or should we confidently believe that "we are our own best doctors," tending to our health like gardeners pruning branches as we see fit? Perhaps for health, choosing one option isn't ideal; instead, a combination of methods or even multiple approaches is what provides comprehensive care.

The fast-paced life in urban areas often lets career take precedence over health. Health needs to be nurtured in every little detail. This book focuses on practical living, without delving into complex pathological or pharmacological knowledge. It's almost like common sense, yet it cares for your health meticulously like a partner who is emotionally rich and mentally delicate.

**Lazy Person's Health Bedside Book - Volume 1: Good Habits, Good Health**

Society is a big vat of dye, influencing both thoughts and health alike. The body carrying our health has always been the battleground between good and bad habits. And with the development of society, this battle has become more intense. Tomatoes from your own garden might be wiped clean and eaten, while some greenhouse tomatoes may need brief soaking or even professional detergent and scientific procedures to meet edible standards. Since we can't change the "origin" of tomatoes, let's change our habits instead, allowing good habits to defend our physical health.

**Lazy Person's Health Bedside Book - Volume 2: Refreshing Health Concepts: Regulating Body Immunity**

In simple terms, immunity is the ability of people to resist diseases. In fact, it is a dynamic developmental process, going from weak to strong, and then from strong to weak again. A sound immune system mainly has three functions: (1) Defense function: This function primarily helps the body eliminate invading bacteria and viruses, avoiding disease occurrence, and protecting the body from harm; (2) Surveillance function: This function mainly prevents tumors and cancerous changes, timely identifying and clearing aging and dying cells; (3) Housekeeping function: Continuously clearing aging and dying cells, maintaining cleanliness and renewal within the body.

Every December 15th is "World Strengthening Immunity Day." For this health commemorative day, it has far fewer fans than Valentine's Day. A survey aimed at ordinary readers showed that in the eyes of the general public, "immunity" is still achieved through various vaccinations. In fact, directly injecting vaccines into the human body to produce specific antibodies and defensive capabilities against specific pathogens merely supplements the barrier of the human immune system. Therefore, people need to engage in a protracted battle to enhance their own immunity.

**Lazy Person's Health Bedside Book - Volume 3: Sexual Happiness Equals Happiness**

Happiness, as society progresses today, is no longer merely a material issue of whether there is "bread" to eat. To a large extent, it has transformed into spiritual issues such as whether there is love, whether there is joy, etc. Moreover, the standard of health, based on the body, increasingly focuses on the integration of mind and body. Thus, a high-speed road to happiness—sexual happiness—is specially opened.

**Lazy Person's Health Bedside Book - Volume 4: Emotional Blues**

Blues (in English: Blues, blue, bluish) also translates as low mood, sadness, depression. Nowadays, insomnia often accompanies us unintentionally, and even things we consider trivial may cause an unexpected burst of anger. Neither we nor others know why. Emotions mysteriously invade our health. Recognizing ourselves, controlling ourselves, let's defeat the "Blues" and say "Bye-bye" to depressive moods.

**Lazy Person's Health Bedside Book - Volume 5: Health Always "Moves" You**

Exercise is the archenemy of disease. Generally speaking, prolonged medication leads to the body developing antibodies, and most supplements come with pros and cons. However, exercise, especially sustained exercise, is akin to building a Great Wall of health for oneself. Of course, exercise is not simply kicking your legs or stretching your waist; it requires science and multi-dimensionality.

**Lazy Person's Health Bedside Book - Volume 6: Lazy Person's Health Decoding**

Lazy people, haha, don't sneer at them just because they're lazy. Due to laziness about walking, people have used their wisdom to invent cars; due to the fatigue of climbing stairs, people have invented elevators that go straight up and down... In a certain sense, because of many reasons of laziness, people's brains have been developed, and to a great extent, the idea of "not moving if you can avoid it" has been realized. In fact, regarding health, lazy people are still commendable. They are too lazy to go to the hospital, too lazy to run on fitness fields, too lazy to practice yoga which they think is unnecessary hardship for themselves, etc. Upholding the greater cause of health, they defend and save their own health through simple living.

**Lazy Person's Health Bedside Book - Volume 7: New Advocacy for Health**

What is known as a new advocacy for health is not merely for attracting attention or creating visual impact but rather using a scientific perspective to examine our own lives, reviewing how behaviors and choices affect health. Thus, we choose the good ones to follow and clear out the blind spots of health.
