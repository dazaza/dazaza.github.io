Taxis dare not refuse passengers; breakdown occurs over Nanguanling bridge tunnel_3

by zhang7532 on 2012-02-24 19:20:13

Violation inquiry http://www.biyaode.com www.biyaode.com "The passenger insisted on passing through this bridge cave. If I don't let her get in, she will accuse me of refusing to carry passengers. If I don't go this way, she will say that I am taking a detour. What should I do?" Taxi driver Master Liu, with a worried look on his face, was scooping water out of the car while shaking his head and sighing. Yesterday afternoon, under the old Nan Guan Ling Bridge which had already suffered from flooding, a taxi that stalled halfway and got flooded had to pay 100 yuan to be "dragged" out of danger. However, in recent days, those who were eager to "drag" their vehicles out of danger were not only Master Liu.

Afraid of refusing passengers, a taxi forced its way through a large water pit and stalled. Around 14:30 yesterday, when the reporter arrived near the old Nan Guan Ling Bridge, he saw a taxi with all four doors wide open. The floors under the front and back seats were full of dirty water, and the driver's seat was stained brown by mud. "This engine needs to be disassembled and cleaned. In the blink of an eye, more than 2,000 yuan is gone." With a worried look on his face, Master Liu was scooping water out of the car while shaking his head and sighing. According to Master Liu, at noon, a female passenger stopped his car and wanted to go to Quan Yuan. "This deep water pit, my car definitely can't pass through, but she (the passenger) saw other big vehicles passing one after another and insisted that I follow them." Master Liu told the reporter that because he was afraid of being complained about for refusing to carry passengers, he also suggested turning around and taking a detour, but it was refused.

Unable to persuade the passenger, Master Liu had no choice but to drive into danger. But just as he drove to the middle of the water pit, the vehicle stalled and broke down in the water. "At that time, I deliberately chose the shallow water areas to drive through, but even so, I couldn't avoid being flooded." Looking at the car that needed major repairs, Master Liu's face remained tense.

When the reporter asked how the car submerged in water was "dragged" out of danger, Master Liu looked even more helpless, saying that it cost 100 yuan to tow the car, but this amount could be ignored compared to the total loss.

Towing fees: 20 yuan for hooking and 100 yuan for towing, charged separately

During the reporter's interview with Master Liu, a silver-gray minivan failed to learn from the previous mistake and stalled immediately upon entering the water pit. At this moment, a man wearing water boots quickly approached the trapped vehicle. After some negotiation with the driver, he called a bulldozer for help.

Standing at a high point of the old bridge cave, the reporter witnessed the entire process of towing the vehicle. First, the yellow bulldozer slowly entered the muddy water, allowing the man in water boots to stand on the bulldozer's bucket, then approached the trapped vehicle. The man in water boots tied a rope to the trapped vehicle, and then the bulldozer pulled the trapped vehicle out. The whole process was smooth and efficient, showing that the bulldozer and the man in water boots were very experienced and cooperated seamlessly.

At that time, a citizen standing next to the reporter said that in recent days, he had seen many vehicles that had stalled due to water and were "dragged" out of danger by these people. "It costs 120 yuan for each trip! Towing and hooking are charged separately. If you've already paid 100 yuan for towing, who would still get out of the car and wade through the water to hook it themselves?" This citizen said that these people were making a fortune, "As long as the Nan Guan Ling bridge cave doesn't improve, these people will continue to profit!"
