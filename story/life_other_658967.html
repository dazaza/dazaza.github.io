Waiting for the Dawn to Spread Its Wings

In the silent night, the sun has already fallen into slumber, and the stars drag their weary eyes, blinking lazily. I sit by a dim yellow light, listening to the wheels of cars speeding past on the road, rolling over my withered heart again and again, leaving piles of shattered fragments weeping in the middle of the road.

Outside the window, the flying fallen leaves tell tales of the helplessness and desolation of parting from the branches, their destiny of returning to the soil dulling the once vibrant green. Tears of longing for you have turned into endless flying catkins, dancing with butterflies in the night sky where you reside. Under this boundless starlit sky, I trace your unsteady footsteps, paint your innocent childishness, and read your clever heart words. You are a book I will never tire of reading, you are a painting I will never finish. Your radiant smile, like flowers blooming in May, brightens the northern sky; your clear voice, like wind chimes in March, beautifies the fields of Jiangnan. With you in the daytime, happiness abounds; with you at night, loneliness no longer follows.

The swirling past settles down into pebbles of true feelings, floating up bubbles of illusion. I pick up memories of you, piecing them together into an oasis of love, embedding them in my dreamlike paintings, decorating my monotonous dreams. Missing you, let my kisses, accompanied by my murmurs, send you into a sweet dreamland.

Who has stolen the bag of time, turning that warmth into coldness? My continuous calls cannot open the tightly locked door of your heart. Your faded memories can no longer reflect the appearance of childhood; your fermented thoughts cannot nurture the warmth of youth. Has the warmth you've become indifferent to still been stuck in your blurred past? Has your boat of memory already run aground on the shoal of love? The wheels of time crush my hopes, tearing me into strips of fragments, while you hide in the corner and chuckle secretly.

The backbone of time bears the heavy burden of love, and I warm myself with the heart that longs for you, letting the chariot of time linger on my icy forehead. Guarding your memories, I wait for the dawn to spread its wings.
