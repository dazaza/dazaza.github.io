Raising public welfare posts to guarantee employment for students in difficulty

by mcly98312 on 2010-04-15 10:12:26

  College graduates from disadvantaged families can solve the livelihood of an entire family through employment. The "Opinions on Promoting Entrepreneurship and Employment for Ordinary College Graduates" recently issued by the Municipal Party Committee and Municipal Government specifically stipulates: to carry out "one-to-one" employment assistance services for college graduates from disadvantaged families, ensuring that all college graduates from disadvantaged families who have the willingness to be employed achieve employment in the same year.

  Policy: To provide "one-to-one" employment support services for college graduates from disadvantaged families enjoying urban and rural minimum living allowances, "zero employment" families, and those severely affected by earthquakes, ensuring that all college graduates from disadvantaged families who are willing to be employed achieve employment in the same year.

  Interpretation: The beneficiaries of this policy include college graduates from recognized urban and rural low-income families, "zero employment" families, disabled individuals' families; recognized disabled college graduates; college graduates whose female or male parents have died (disappeared) or lost their ability to work, and whose houses were completely destroyed due to the earthquake; and college graduates recognized by relevant departments as meeting assistance conditions.

  College graduates from disadvantaged families who have not yet been employed can apply to the local government's personnel department where their household registration is located, and submit the "Application Form for Employment Support for Chengdu Disadvantaged Family College Graduates", proof of academic qualifications and identity, "Reemployment Preferential Certificate" of themselves or immediate family members, "Urban Resident Minimum Living Security Allowance Payment Certificate", "Rural Resident Minimum Living Security Allowance Payment Certificate", "Disability Certificate", etc.

  The personnel departments of each district (city) county will review and verify the materials, and display the verified information publicly for 5 working days. After review and public display without objection, the local government's personnel department where their household registration is located will issue the "Employment Assistance Card" and request relevant employment assistance services.

  Support measures include: First, prioritizing job recommendations to society. Special sections and columns will be opened in news media and student employment websites to publish basic information about disadvantaged family college graduates and recommend them for employment to society. Publicly collecting job positions from society, and employment service agencies will conduct "one-to-one" focused recommendations for employment.

  Second, preferential recruitment by government and public institutions. For disadvantaged family college graduates participating in civil servant and public institution employee recruitment exams, all fees and examination costs will be exempted, and they will be given priority under equal conditions.

  Third, targeted recruitment for urban and rural grassroots service programs for college graduates. Programs such as the "One Village (Community Involving Industry) Two University Students Plan", "Special Teacher Position Program in Rural Areas", "Medical Recruitment Plan for University Students in Township Health Centers", and "University Students Service Community Employment and Social Security Program" will reserve a certain proportion of positions for targeted recruitment of disadvantaged family college graduates.

  Fourth, guaranteeing employment through public welfare positions. Governments at all levels will specially collect public welfare positions for the placement of disadvantaged family college graduates.
