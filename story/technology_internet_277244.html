Top ten search sites in the world

by mennei on 2009-11-25 20:00:38

There are thousands of websites around the world known as "search engines." In reality, only about ten of these websites are truly suitable for overseas promotion. The most famous ones include Google, Baidu, Yahoo, Alltheweb, AltaVista, and Inktomi, among others. The search results from other websites either come from these search engines or they share search results with each other. To better help you promote your website, we will introduce them one by one below.

Baidu.com is the largest Chinese search engine and doesn't need much further introduction.

Google.com is the largest global machine-based search engine. Google handles 200 million queries daily, accounting for 29.2% of the global search engine query market share, making it undoubtedly the world's first. By organizing over 8 billion web pages, Google provides users worldwide with appropriate search results in typically less than half a second.

Yahoo.com is one of the most recognized and valuable internet brands globally, and also the largest portal site. It has versions in more than ten languages including English, Chinese, Japanese, Korean, French, and German. Ranked 38th in global consumer brand rankings, it is the largest search engine and portal site globally. With 294 million valid registered users, it receives 1.2 billion visits per day, covering 61% of global internet users; globally, 18.4 million business procurement decision-makers visit Yahoo!

MSN.com belongs to Microsoft Corporation. According to statistics published by Comscore, which tracks internet browsing volume in the U.S., Microsoft's MSN website has become the second most visited American portal site after AOL, closely approaching Yahoo.

AOL.com, also known as America Online, is one of the earliest portal sites in the U.S. and the world. Eighty percent of its users are from the U.S., and all of its search results come from Google. This means that good rankings on Google also lead to good rankings on AOL.

Lycos.com is one of the earliest search engines in the world. According to media surveys, Lycos ranks fifth as the most frequently visited website with 37 million unique monthly visits. Currently, Lycos' main search results come from Alltheweb.

Ask.com is a smaller but distinctive search engine. Ask is the parent company of DirectHit and acquired the Teoma search engine in 2001, fully adopting Teoma's search results.

Overture.com was the first pay-per-click search engine. Its search results were adopted by Yahoo, MSN, and others. After acquiring Inktomi, a rival of Google, Overture itself was later acquired by Yahoo. It launched the "world's largest" (Overture) search index service containing approximately 3.2 billion documents and is also a company providing industry-standard keyword traffic.

Hengsou.com is a well-known meta-search engine. Meta-search engines accept user queries and simultaneously search multiple other engines, returning the results to the user.

Netscape.com is a search engine developed by Netscape Communications Corporation, the renowned browser company. All of Netscape's search results come from Google. Additionally, DMOZ, the world's largest open directory, belongs to Netscape.

AltaVista.com is one of the oldest search engines in the world and also one of the most feature-complete and accurate full-text search engines. As of June 2002, AltaVista claimed to have stored 1.1 billion web files in its database. After an upgrade, its search accuracy reached an industry-leading level. This search engine was acquired by Yahoo in 2003.

Inktomi.com only provides search results to other search engines. It was acquired by Overture in 2003. Before Yahoo developed its own new search engine technology in 2004, Inktomi was the second-largest search engine globally, with its search results being used by Hotbot, MSN, and other famous websites.

HotBot.com is a relatively active search engine with faster data updates compared to other engines. It has a web page library capacity of 110 million and is known for its unique search interface. This engine was acquired by Lycos and became part of the Terra Lycos Network.

AllTheWeb.com is currently the fastest-growing search engine, supporting searches in 225 file formats. Its database contains 2.1 billion web files in 49 languages, and it is widely recognized for its fast update speed and high search accuracy, considered a strong competitor to Google.

LookSmart.com is a classified directory search engine comparable to Yahoo, Open Directory Project (Dmoz), etc., providing directory searches to MSN, AltaVista, Excite, and others. It has a significant scale of commercial clients globally and shares resources through cooperation with heavyweights such as MSN, AltaVista, Infospace, making LookSmart an important channel for domestic export enterprises to conduct overseas search engine registration and promotion.

Infoseek.com is one of the world's most famous search engines, offering full-text retrieval functions and detailed classification directories. Its webpage inclusion is extremely rich, mainly in Western languages.

CNN.com, the American Cable News Network (CNN), is a news media website under Time Warner. In 2001, it merged with AOL as part of the Time Warner Group. CNN covers six continents and has become the first truly global television network in history.
