10 Tricks to Help Desperate People Out of the Quagmire

by lxdhr2012 on 2011-08-19 15:54:35

1. Tempering Willpower

It means giving oneself a strong will. Life often brings about unpleasant events, and when the goals we strive for are not met, a sense of frustration arises. Especially at some turning points in life, if one does not achieve what they expect, a great sense of loss and depression may follow. Those with insufficient willpower might even think of taking their own lives. In reality, this is an act of avoidance. The correct choice is to bravely accept challenges. Once started, one must persist until the goal is reached. A crisis signifies coexistence of danger and opportunity!

An ancient saying goes: "When Heaven bestows a great mission on a person, it first tests their will, strains their muscles and bones, starves their body, empties their resources, and disrupts their actions, so as to stir their heart, toughen their nature, and enhance their capabilities." There's also a lyric that goes like this: "The sun always shines after the rain." Greater harvests mean greater efforts are required.

2. Humorous Resolution

It means skillfully using humor. Sometimes being too focused on one thought, especially something yet to be successful, can easily lead one into a mental dead-end from which they cannot escape. At such times, a good way out is to use a bit of humor. Everything has two sides, but the negative side is often more noticeable. One should look more towards the positive side, and for the negative side, try to transform it into something meaningful and beneficial for personal growth. Whether alone or with others, laugh off one’s weaknesses.

3. Transferring Emotions Elsewhere

It means staying rational. Persist in what should be persisted, and abandon what should be abandoned. Often, the latter requires even greater courage and wisdom. If a goal is unreachable for oneself, instead of continuing to torment oneself, it would be better to choose another path. Sometimes, when setting initial goals, one’s plans for the future may not have been clear, and one’s self-awareness may not have been objective enough, leading to unnecessary effort. If one can timely identify previous mistakes, it doesn't signify failure; rather, it shows maturity! Investing the same amount of effort into a new goal may bring unexpected rewards.

4. Spiritual Exchange with Ancients

It means being objective. Now, with highly developed technology, some things may seem different on the surface compared to before, but in essence, they haven't changed. Consulting predecessors will definitely be beneficial, even if experiences aren't entirely the same, similar ones are still sufficient for later generations to learn from. More importantly, the success of our predecessors gives us hope. Since someone has succeeded, why can't we? Everyone admires certain people, and we generally aspire to become like them. They have achieved it, proving that prospects are indeed realistic.

5. Actively Seeking Help

It means putting aside one's pride. In fact, many individual troubles now may be caused by social factors. With the passage of time and societal progress, traditional cultural values are constantly challenged, and the ways people communicate and interact have changed. This sometimes makes us question many things, including the meaning of life. Therefore, when one feels lost, don’t pretend to be strong; confide in others and let them help you. As the saying goes, those involved are confused, while bystanders see clearly!

6. Relying on Family

It means caring about one's family. As our closest relations, family members are most likely to lend a helping hand during critical moments. Don't give up the chance to seek help because of fear that family members may get tired of you or that it may cause pressure. In fact, family members are always ready to help us, and their assistance often reflects their best expression of self-worth. On the contrary, if one never communicates with family, and when they find out how much you've suffered without mentioning it, they may feel hurt due to the assumption that you don't trust them.

7. Seeking Help from Friends

It means showing concern for your friends. If it's hard to talk about inner thoughts with family, then confide in friends. They may share similar life experiences and current situations with us, and relatively speaking, they might understand us better.

8. Seeking Psychological Counseling

It means seeking help from professionals. Data shows that half of those who commit suicide suffer from depression. This highlights the importance of seeking professional help to adjust personal cognition in preventing suicide. Rest assured, psychologists do not criticize those who need help. Instead, they establish friendly relationships with clients, understanding and respecting them.

9. Seeking Medical Treatment

It means taking medication according to a doctor's requirements. If suicidal tendencies arise due to mental illness, taking antipsychotic and antidepressant drugs can stabilize the condition well and eliminate negative thoughts. Of course, one should not take medicine arbitrarily and must strictly follow medical advice.

10. Seeking Spiritual Comfort

It means loving oneself. There's a saying: "The reason the Chinese character 'busy' is written this way is because its meaning implies: when people are overly busy, they tend to ignore their inner thoughts, eventually leading to spiritual death." Modern people are all very busy, easily losing themselves and forgetting why they hustle. Spare a little time to reflect on one’s original intentions and goals, and it will reward you abundantly. Strive hard, but avoid becoming a 'spiritually dead' 'busy person'.
