It's been a long time since I last wrote a diary entry myself.

by xjnp2615 on 2011-05-13 16:41:56

Maybe I'm lazy. Yesterday it rained, so I didn't go to work today. Let me tell you a silly thing I did... On the evening of March 29, 2011... I have something to do now, I will continue to write tonight!!!

Finally, it's evening. I am really tired after a day's work. But I still forced myself to finish this diary. On the night of March 29, 2011, I was adding QQ numbers randomly for fun. I don't know if it was intentional or unintentional, but I added a number that was the same as my phone number. The owner of that number is from Daqing. After chatting with him for a few sentences, I felt he was a nice person. Then I negotiated with him to buy his number since it's the same as my own phone number. I like it quite a bit. So I told him I wanted to buy his number. We finally agreed on 100 yuan. I thought it would be troublesome and time-consuming to transfer money to his bank card. Then I told him I would buy him a 100-yuan recharge card instead. A few days later, on April 1st, maybe because it was April Fool's Day, I made a mistake. I bought the recharge card for him, called him and gave him the password, etc. Finally, after confirming that he had successfully recharged, I asked him for the QQ password and security questions. He said he didn't know what the security question was. A certain someone wrote a poem for me. There was only one password. With no other options, I just used the password to log into QQ. At that time, I was at work, thinking it would be safer to change the password. If he regrets it now, there's nothing I can do. Finally, I called a friend for help. After my friend logged in, he said, "Sorry buddy, your number cannot change the password because there are security questions." My mind went blank. He told me there was no security question, only one password. Then I called him to question him. He was still being stubborn, saying things like, "I don't know. Let me ask my friend. It was given to me by a friend." Then I hung up the phone. Ten minutes later, I called him again. Guess what he said? That damn fool said his friend scolded him for selling such a good number for only 100 yuan. His friend demanded the money for the number. If he didn't give the money, his friend wouldn't give him the security question. In the end, he shamelessly said, "I've already given the money to my friend, and I've lost my number. What did I gain? You should give another 100 yuan." I got angry when I heard that. Do you think I would give him more money? It's not about the money. People must keep their word. I think I did well. I gave him the recharge card without seeing anything. In the end, he pulled this trick on me. I was upset but couldn't do anything. I didn't dare to say anything too harsh to him because I still wanted that number. Even if there was just a little hope, I frequently logged into this QQ account on my computer and tried to recover it through an account appeal. After repeated failures, I lost confidence. Five days passed in frustration. Then I let my close friend log into this account. Two days later, I finally succeeded in appealing. At that moment, I couldn't describe my feelings. It wasn't because the QQ finally belonged to me, but because I felt like I had defeated a scammer and prevented his conspiracy from succeeding. Thus, a heavy burden was finally lifted off my shoulders. Next, I repeatedly logged into this QQ account using my phone. Suddenly, on the afternoon of May 6th at 1 o'clock, my phone showed that my number was logged in elsewhere. Feeling something was wrong, I tried logging in on my computer but it failed due to incorrect password. I was puzzled. I had set a security question and bound it to my phone. Who could have stolen my number? Guess what? When I checked, the number was now bound to a very familiar phone number: 13936854052. Damn it, he got it back again! For your sake, I've wasted away... - Preface. I was so angry, I immediately called him and questioned him. He was still pretending to be confused, saying things like, "I don't know what the security question is. I didn't change it. I was sleeping." Damn it, how could he live like this? Without his phone sending a verification code, how could his phone be bound? He was still pretending to be cool, saying he didn't know and was sleeping. I almost wanted to kill him. He's truly a piece of trash. Finally, I recovered the QQ through an account appeal this time without much effort. Alas, I'm still worried he might get it back again. Well, let fate decide. Someone once said to me, "What's yours will always be yours, and what's not yours will disappear no matter how hard you try to get it." Hopefully, I can keep this QQ that makes me anxious and gives me headaches forever. That's all. It's not early anymore. I've been rambling for half a day. I guess everyone is already annoyed. I'm also tired. I need to sleep because I have to work tomorrow. Goodnight!!!
