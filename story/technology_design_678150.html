"Vanity"

The church bells rang at 7 PM.

Johanna looked out the kitchen window at the dilapidated scene outside. The sound of the church bells reminded her of the song "Christmas Bells." She could almost hear someone singing in her ear: "I hear the Christmas bells... peace on earth, goodwill to all men."

Johanna pressed her head against the cold window glass. It was so ironic - world peace? Since the Germans invaded Holland four years ago, there has been no peace here. There are German soldiers guarding every street corner in Rotterdam. She sees one right now. That soldier is putting a piece of candy into his mouth. Johanna has seen him many times, but they have never spoken. Every time she sees him, he's eating candy. It seems he really likes candy.

"Johanna, where are Dad's shoes?" Mom was frantically packing Dad's luggage. Her brother Derek was helping too. Tonight - on Christmas Eve - Dad was leaving home for the train station to take a train to the labor camp to work. All adult men in Rotterdam had to go.

"Merry Christmas, Johanna!" Dad smiled as Johanna handed him his shoes.

"Merry Christmas?" Johanna cried, "Merry Christmas? How can you say that? This is the worst Christmas ever!"

Dad bent down and tied three knots in the shoelaces. He always does this when he has to leave town. It's a 30 minute walk to the train station. "Johanna," he said, "sometimes things don't turn out the way we hope, but we must still strive for good results." He opened his doctor's ID card. "With this, I won't be treated too badly. Do you understand? This will be a happy Christmas."

Dad put down the ID, hugged Johanna and said, "From now on, you're Mom's good helper, right?" Johanna couldn't speak, she just nodded continuously.

Dad put on his coat, wore his hat, picked up his suitcase, smiled, waved goodbye to the family, and walked out the door.

Johanna felt a lump in her chest. The hardest part was that after 8 PM, there would be curfew throughout Rotterdam and they couldn't go to the train station to see him off. The guards had orders to shoot anyone found on the streets after 8 PM.

Johanna leaned against the kitchen wall again, staring blankly at Dad's usual seat by the dining table. Suddenly, her blurry vision caught something. Oh no!

"ID!" she shouted. She picked up the ID on the ground and exclaimed, "Dad left his doctor's certificate!"

The whole family panicked. Mom took the ID from Johanna's hand and bit her lips tightly. Derek grabbed the ID and said, "I'll deliver it to Dad."

Johanna snatched the ID from her brother's hand. "I run faster than you!" Before Mom and Derek could react, she ran out the door and onto the street.

At that moment, the church bells struck half past seven. If I keep running nonstop, I can definitely make it back. Johanna thought. Although she felt like her lungs were about to explode, she didn't allow herself to stop. Finally, she stumbled onto the train platform. Hundreds of men were crowded on the platform. She anxiously searched for Dad among the crowd. It felt like hours had passed. Finally, she spotted a pair of shoes - shoes with laces tied in three knots.

That pair of shoes was about to board the train. Johanna pushed through the crowd toward them. As the shoes stepped onto the train, Johanna shouted with all her might, "Dad!" while raising the ID high above her head.

Johanna felt Dad's hand reaching out, moving closer to her. Finally, he touched her hand and took the ID. But before he could say another word, he disappeared into the crowd.

Johanna dared not linger any longer and turned to push through the crowd, rushing out of the train station. Running down the dark streets, she kept telling herself over and over, "I have to go back! I must go back!" She felt like she was flying. The wind whistled past her ears.

"Ding..." The bells struck eight o'clock.

There were still a few streets to go before reaching home. Johanna gritted her teeth and sped up even more. They can't catch me, they can't catch me! She prayed as she ran.

Suddenly, a German voice came out of the darkness, "What are you doing?"

Johanna felt her heart almost jump out of her chest. She panted and turned around. It was the candy-loving soldier.

"What are you doing?" he asked again.

Tears streamed down Johanna's face. "My dad needs his doctor's certificate. I ran to the train station to give it to him... I just came back from the train station. I've run as fast as I could. Sorry!"

The German soldier looked at Johanna sobbing. "Where do you live?"

"Right next to the school."

"Hah, right next to my bunker," he said, "I'll escort you home."

Johanna's mouth dropped open. She stared at the German soldier, hardly believing her ears. He walked very slowly, seemingly intentionally letting Johanna catch her breath. A few minutes later, Johanna arrived home. The German soldier accompanied her all the way to her doorstep.

"Don't violate the curfew again," the soldier said.

Johanna nodded.

The soldier said in German, "Good evening! And... Merry Christmas!"

In the distance, the chimes played music: "Peace on earth, goodwill to all men."

"Merry Christmas!" Johanna responded with a smile. The soldier waved to her and walked away.

[Excerpted from "Satire and Humor", December 24, 2010]

In Berlin [US] O'Rear

A train slowly left Berlin. The carriages were full of women and children, with hardly a healthy man to be seen. In one carriage, there was a gray-haired veteran of the wartime reserve sitting next to a frail and sickly old woman. Clearly lost in thought, the passengers heard her counting "one, two, three," her voice drowning out the "clatter-clatter" of the wheels. After a pause, she repeated it from time to time. Two young girls saw this strange behavior, gesturing and laughing without thinking. An old man glared fiercely at them, and then the carriage returned to calmness.

"One, two, three." The mentally unstable old woman repeated the counting. The two young girls burst out laughing again. At this point, the gray-haired wartime reserve veteran straightened up and spoke:

"Young ladies," he said, "when I tell you that this poor lady is my wife, you probably won't laugh anymore. We have just lost our three sons; they died in the war. Now it's my turn to go to the front myself. Before I leave, I must send their mother to the insane asylum."

There was a terrible silence in the carriage.

[Attention Please] Xu Xing

"Tell me, why do you stand at attention every time I mention Chiang Kai-shek? Is it..."

My words were not yet finished when the captured Nationalist army captain snapped to attention again with a "Bang" because he heard me mention Chiang Kai-shek.

This made me extremely angry, and if it weren't for the discipline of the People's Liberation Army, I would have slapped him long ago.

"You are truly reactionaries to the end!"

"Comrade, I want to change, but I can't help it. Whenever that person is mentioned, I involuntarily do this..."

"I see, you will die for him!" I said harshly.

"No, Comrade, I want to reform my thoughts, I want to start anew!" The captured captain sincerely said.

"With your superstitious attitude towards Chiang Kai-shek, how can you..."

Before I finished my sentence, mentioning Chiang Kai-shek made him snap to attention again with a "Bang".

This time I finally couldn't hold back and shoved him, making him stumble. I sternly said,

"If you stand at attention again, I'll break your legs!"

"Comrade, hit me! In the past, I was beaten into doing this. When I was still a platoon leader, just because I didn't stand at attention when that person's name was mentioned, the regiment political training director found out and gave me a severe beating. After the beating, he gave me individual training. He would say that person's name, and I would immediately stand at attention. If I was slow, I would get beaten. Sometimes, he would unexpectedly mention that person's name when I wasn't paying attention, and if I didn't react in time, I would get severely beaten... Ever since then, I've had this problem, regardless of time or place, whenever that person or that person's name is mentioned, I stand at attention, acting like a lunatic... but I was commended, saying this was loyalty to the leader... Comrade, hit me! You hitting me hard might fix it. Comrade, hit me, hit me!" The captured captain said painfully while crying and earnestly asking me to hit him.

It was really strange! But it was clear to hear. He avoided even mentioning the three characters of Chiang Kai-shek, fearing it would trigger his conditioned reflex. There was no doubt about the sincerity of his words.

This made me somewhat dumbfounded, not knowing what to do!

In 1948, when I was managing prisoners from the Nationalist army, I encountered such an incident. At the time, the prisoner battalion consisted of officers below the rank of lieutenant from the Nationalist army, intending to reform them for use, but unexpectedly encountered such a guy.

"Political Commissar, let's beat him up! Maybe it will cure him," I requested from the battalion Political Commissar.

"We cannot act recklessly. Can we use the methods of the Nationalist army? Do you think beating him only affects him?"

Wow, the Political Commissar raised the issue to a high level.

"So?" I asked.

"Let the military doctor examine him."

At the time, medical standards were limited, and naturally, no definitive diagnosis was made, nor was there any treatment method available. After the intensive training ended, other prisoners were assigned positions, but due to this unresolved issue, he was sent back home.

Thirty years later, after the Cultural Revolution, I visited a county in Hebei and unexpectedly met him on the street. He sat in a wheelchair and recognized me from afar.

"Instructor, Instructor!" He called out to me with deep emotion.

His hair was gray, his face haggard, looking very aged, and his legs were already disabled. I asked him how his legs got disabled, and he said it was because the problem wasn't resolved, and he was beaten by the Red Guards. If it weren't for a doctor confined in the "cowshed" who spoke up for him, he would have almost lost his life!

I was horrified, life was indeed like this!... His legs being broken meant he couldn't stand at attention anymore, which was a thorough way of reform. So, I couldn't help but say,

"All your life, you've been ruined by Chiang Kai-shek!"

Heaven! I noticed painfully that when I mentioned Chiang Kai-shek, his upper body in the wheelchair still instinctively straightened into an attention posture.

(Excerpted from "Xu Xing Selected Works")

[Vanity]

[Germany] Max Grün translated by Lao Wen

Renate quarreled with her lover Erich. The reason was simple: she thought he was an insignificant lathe operator with a meager income.

When Erich left in shame and anger, Renate shouted at his retreating figure: "You'll never come to see me again unless you amount to something!"

But what she hadn't anticipated was that this harmless remark made in anger would cause these childhood sweethearts to drift apart forever.

Five years later, on a hot summer morning, at Platform Three of Cologne Central Station.

Renate was about to board a train bound for Amsterdam via Aachen at this platform, while Erich had just disembarked from a train arriving from Hamburg.

Unexpectedly meeting each other, they were overwhelmed with mixed feelings and excitement, hastily pushing through the bustling passengers to embrace each other passionately.

Reunion brought tears of joy, and they gazed deeply into each other's eyes for a long time.

After leaving the station, they entered a café together.

"Oh, tell me quickly, Renate, how are you? Tell me, how have the last five years been? Are you well? Are you married?" Erich eagerly bombarded her with questions, desperate to learn about her current situation.

"Fine, fine, everything's fine, still single and carefree. As you know, for us women, to achieve anything in our careers requires diligent effort and hard work; there's no time for moonlight romance with men." She paused briefly before continuing, "I am now the manager of a textile company in Cologne. Due to excessive workload and immense stress, I'm heading to Holland this afternoon for a few weeks' vacation to relax my nerves."

Impressive achievements in such a short time! Erich secretly applauded her success while simultaneously feeling a pang of regret: if she hadn't risen so rapidly, perhaps he would have asked her right now whether she wanted to reconcile and make amends for their past grievances. But if he rashly revealed his thoughts, would he risk being mocked again as he was five years ago when they parted ways?! He couldn't bear her sarcasm. Besides, even if he confessed his love again, it would be futile since she was now a successful and influential lady.
