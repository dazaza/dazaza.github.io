A Brief Talk on the Causes of Erectile Dysfunction from Placenta Extract

Causes of Erectile Dysfunction: Most cases of erectile dysfunction (ED) are functional, while a few are organic. The common causes include the following aspects:

1. Psychoneurotic factors: such as sexual psychological trauma during childhood, lack of sexual knowledge in newlyweds, tension and anxiety, or disharmony between couples, strained family relationships; or bad habits, such as excessive masturbation leading to reduced sensitivity of the penis, mental stress, excessive mental burden, etc., can lead to ED; excessive physical or mental exertion, or adverse mental stimulation, such as excessive depression, sadness, fear, etc., or unrestrained indulgence in sex, excessive sexual activity, etc., can all cause dysfunction of the cerebral cortex leading to ED.

2. Nervous system lesions: tumors in the hypothalamus-pituitary or other areas, localized brain damage such as focal epilepsy, encephalitis, intracerebral hemorrhage compression, etc., spinal cord injury or tumor, chronic alcohol poisoning, multiple sclerosis, pelvic surgery damaging peripheral autonomic nerves, etc., can lead to ED.

3. Endocrine lesions: such as diabetes, pituitary insufficiency, testicular damage or low function, or hypothyroidism and hyperthyroidism, adrenal insufficiency, etc., can all lead to ED.

4. Urogenital organ lesions: such as prostatitis, prostate hyperplasia, epididymitis, varicocele, etc., often lead to ED. Some middle-aged and elderly patients develop ED due to prostatitis and prostate hyperplasia.

5. Drug effects: Many drugs have inhibitory effects on sexual function in clinical practice, such as reserpine, guanethidine, digoxin, diazepam, furosemide, metoclopramide, etc., which can all cause ED.

6. Chronic fatigue: Fatigue can lead to ED because it is caused by excessive muscle fatigue or mental exhaustion, depression, unease, tension, etc., resulting in psychogenic fatigue that interferes with the arousal of sexual desire. This includes reduced brain function that suppresses sexual interest, decreased excitability of the limbic system emotional center, and reduced secretion of gonadotropins by the pituitary and androgens by the testes, thereby reducing sexual excitement.
