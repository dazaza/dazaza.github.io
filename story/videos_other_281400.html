Good Samaritans report "emergency" on the highway, but the traffic police discover a "car quake"

by yyusk on 2009-11-28 15:19:43

A car stopped at the side of a highway, its body shaking violently. This scared the wits out of a passing driver. "Could an accident have happened?" he thought, and immediately called the police to report it. When the traffic police arrived at the scene, they realized that this was not an emergency but a case of 'car sex' on the highway.

The two protagonists were a 48-year-old female boss and a 34-year-old male driver. Both had families, yet due to their unconventional relationship, they engaged in inappropriate behavior on the highway. At the Quanzhou South exit of the Quanzhang Expressway, facing verbal warnings from the traffic police, both buried their heads in shame.

### Suspected Incident Prompts Alarm

Around 12:40 AM, on the Quanzhang Expressway, a passing driver reported seeing a black sedan parked by the roadside with its body shaking violently. Suspecting an emergency, he called for help.

Upon receiving instructions from the command center, Officer Lin from the First Detachment of the Second Brigade of the Quanzhou Expressway Traffic Police quickly rushed to the scene. On a vacant lot about 10 kilometers away from Yongchun towards Jinjiang, the officers indeed spotted a black sedan parked there.

The car had stopped shaking, but the hazard lights (double flashers) were still on. The officers hurried toward the car, banging on the window and shouting, "Is anyone inside?" After a long pause, a crack appeared in the left rear window, and a man's voice came through saying, "Nothing's wrong."

If nothing's wrong, why are the hazard lights on? The officers, unconvinced, circled around to the front windshield to take a closer look. They immediately noticed a naked man and woman in the back seat. The officer turned his back and said loudly, "Get dressed and step out for questioning." Later, the officers recalled thinking that some kind of illegal activity might have been happening.

### The Boss and Her Driver

After a brief inquiry, the officers brought both individuals along with the car to the Quanzhang Expressway Quanzhou South Exit and notified the Jinnong Police Station in Licheng District to assist in the investigation.

This pair cooperated quite well. The woman introduced herself as Zheng Hong (a pseudonym), the owner of a building materials trading company in Xiamen. She stated that the man was named Xu Jing (also a pseudonym), her driver. Despite both having families, they frequently spent time together, developed feelings over time, and had been engaging in extramarital affairs for a while.

Zheng Hong explained that after finishing business in Sanming and heading back to Xiamen, the two became passionate during the journey. In the heat of the moment, they pulled over to an emergency parking area and began their intimate encounter. Concerned about potential accidents, they even turned on the hazard lights to warn other vehicles passing by.

### No Prostitution, Case Closed

After hearing the explanation from the couple, the officers were still cautious and worried that they might not be who they claimed to be. Thus, they requested identification. Zheng Hong produced her business license and ID badge, while Xu Jing also showed his ID badge, confirming their stated relationship.

The traffic police also noted that since Zheng Hong’s car was parked in a designated emergency parking area, it did not constitute illegal use of the parking lane. Therefore, they decided to issue a verbal warning and education before letting them go.

At 1:30 AM, Zheng Hong and Xu Jing left the Quanzhou South Exit and drove towards Xiamen. The officers commented that such incidents fall within the realm of moral issues and hoped they would not occur again on the expressway.
