Car Maintenance Knowledge: Easily Solve 7 Common Car Problems

Issues such as inexplicable increases in fuel consumption, the engine failing to start suddenly, and unsatisfactory performance of the windshield wipers are not serious problems, but they can be very troubling for car owners. How do we solve these issues?

We have listed seven common car problems, and interviewed experienced drivers and technicians with years of experience in car maintenance and modifications. They stated that many car owners currently do not pay enough attention to regular vehicle maintenance. When problems arise, they take their cars to repair shops or 4S dealerships, which increases the cost of car ownership while also consuming a lot of time and effort. In fact, many of these minor issues can be avoided through regular maintenance.

1. Engine Fails to Start

Cause: The engine starts through the battery's current igniting the spark plug. There may be two reasons why the engine suddenly fails to start. First, it could be due to battery corrosion or battery leakage. Second, it could be caused by moisture on the spark plugs during rainy weather.

Maintenance Method: Check the battery fluid level every one or two months. If the liquid level is between the upper and lower lines, it is considered appropriate. You should also check the difference in liquid levels among the cells. If the liquid is insufficient, you can unscrew the cap on top of the battery and add distilled water to replenish the liquid level. Then, check the battery terminals and use a wire brush to clean off any dirt. If the clamps are severely dirty, you can use sandpaper to rub along the inner wall and remove the dirt. If the spark plugs are damp, you can remove them and use a lighter to dry them to quickly evaporate the moisture.

2. Increased Exhaust Noise and Excessive Emissions

Cause: This may be due to the oxidation of engine exhaust at high temperatures, causing leaks in the exhaust system.

Maintenance Method: Regularly check the pipes and joints of the exhaust system (especially the muffler) for corrosion by exhaust gases and whether the gaskets at the joints are damaged. If an exhaust system leak is found, promptly visit the 4S dealership for repairs or replacement of leaking components. An annual check not only ensures the normal operation of the exhaust system but also reduces harmful substances in the exhaust from polluting the environment.

3. Unexplained Increase in Fuel Consumption

Cause: There are many reasons for increased fuel consumption. However, the most common and easily overlooked reason is checking the tire pressure. Insufficient tire pressure not only increases driving resistance, leading to higher fuel consumption, but also affects the lifespan of the tires.

Maintenance Method: While driving, pay more attention to whether each tire has sufficient air and regularly check the tire pressure. Of course, remember not to forget checking the spare tire, so it is ready when needed. Tire pressure tends to be higher in hot weather, so measuring it in cold weather or in the shade is more accurate. It is recommended to swap the positions of the tires every 10,000 kilometers to avoid excessive wear differences between front and rear tires. If heavily worn tires need to be replaced, it is best to replace two or all four together. Tires with similar tread patterns can be swapped diagonally front-to-back. Additionally, tires with straight-line treads help save fuel.

4. Significant Decrease in Engine Performance Accompanied by Increased Fuel Consumption

Cause: This is likely due to long-term neglect of replacing motor oil, oil filters, air filters, and gasoline filters, resulting in incomplete fuel combustion and increased engine carbon deposits.

Maintenance Method: Motor oil and oil filters should be replaced every 5,000 kilometers driven. Air filters and gasoline filters need to be replaced every 10,000 kilometers. Otherwise, impurities in the air, fuel, and motor oil can cause parts to wear out and block the fuel line, affecting the normal operation of the engine.

5. Damaged Fuel Pump

Cause: Improper refueling behavior may damage the fuel pump.

Maintenance Method: When refueling, try not to wait until the fuel warning light comes on before refueling. Doing so will shorten the life of the fuel pump, and too little stored fuel or running out of fuel may burn out the fuel pump. If you often drive in urban areas where refueling is convenient, there’s no need to fill up completely each time, as this might cause the fuel float and sensor to malfunction, distort the fuel gauge reading, and increase fuel consumption. Therefore, it is recommended to refuel when the fuel is nearly consumed.

6. Unstable Battery

Cause: Abnormal changes in electrolyte can affect the performance and reliability of the battery.

Maintenance Method: During summer driving, overcharging of the battery can occur, leading to rapid evaporation of the electrolyte and easy damage to the plates. Therefore, it is necessary to frequently check the liquid level of the battery and the specific gravity of the electrolyte (the specific gravity in winter should be slightly less). Frequently add distilled water to the battery; in winter, try to avoid complete discharge of the battery and pay attention to whether the electrolyte freezes.

7. Poor Windshield Wiper Performance

Cause: Damaged wiper blades can result in poor cleaning performance.

Maintenance Method: First, car owners need to correct the bad habit of "dry wiping." It is best to spray some water first before using the wipers, which helps protect the wiper blades. Secondly, sometimes the wipers make a clicking sound. If the wiper blades are intact, it is best for the owner to go to the 4S dealership for calibration of the wiper angle. Finally, if the vehicle is parked for a long time, the owner can place small objects like foam strips or wooden strips under the wipers to separate the wiper blades from the windshield, extending the life of the wipers.
