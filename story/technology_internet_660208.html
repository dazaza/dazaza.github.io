In all of twentieth century China, Shanghai has always been a city that represents fashion, from the glittering and bustling metropolis of the thirties and forties of last century, to the Nanjing East Road wafting with fragrant winds in the fifties, and then to the capital of Chinese popular culture after the reform and opening up. Shanghai is not only a leader of trends but also a creator of fashion, industrial humidifiers.

The charm of Hefei doesn't just lie in its water; its cuisine is also a great attraction. As one of the eight major cuisines of China, Huizhou cuisine emphasizes oil, color, and cooking techniques. Hefei is also known as the "Capital of Chinese Freshwater Lobster." More notably, Hefei has successfully held the Daxu Grape Festival for eight consecutive sessions, from "Who gets the sweetest grape" and "Grape Festival Evening Party," to "Tasting grape wine" and "Golden Grape Planting Competition," making "Go to Daxu to pick grapes" a trend in leisure tourism.

Hefei is everywhere connected with water. Just its name has such a reason - according to the ancient Chinese dictionary "Erya," the explanation of "Fei" is "different streams converging into one is called Fei." Two rivers converge into one source, separate into two, hence the name Hefei.

Water, the witness of history. The ancient town of Sanhe, with over 2500 years of history, still preserves the traditional style of a waterside town, with bridges, flowing streams, and homes, and a simple folk custom. Hefei is both a famous city of the Three Kingdoms era and the hometown of Bao Gong and Li Hongzhang. To this day, there are touching legends like Hutbing Pond, Cangzhou Bay, Zhengdi Bay. Walking through these places, we can imagine how historical figures wielded their power, how they were impartial and selfless, holding up a sky without injustice for the common people; how they retired from military life and established the Huai Army capable of combating the Taiping Heavenly Kingdom, and how they were shaped by the times into powerful ministers of the Huai clique who influenced the diplomacy, military, and economy of the Qing court. Traveling in Hefei is like detaching oneself from the fast-paced modern life, wandering through history and culture, and revisiting traditional spirit and values.

Nanjing Road is one of the most famous and prosperous shopping streets in the world and was one of the first commercial streets established after Shanghai's opening, hailed as the "First Street of Chinese Commerce." However, locals in Shanghai do not shop on Nanjing Road; they may instead view it as a museum of shop evolution. Not only does Nanjing Road have the century-old stores Yong'an, Sincere, Sun Sun, and Dajin, four major department stores, but it also stands alongside modern large malls such as Shanghai No.1 Department Store, Xinhua Department Store, Shanghai Huilian Commercial Building, Shanghai Costume Company, and Shanghai No.1 Food Store.

Water, the purifier of the soul. Hefei is close to Chaohu Lake, one of the five largest freshwater lakes in China. The Binhu New Area of Hefei lies south of Chaohu Lake. In the area with the distinctive Huizhou-style "water street" characteristics of earthen buildings, there will soon be a series of expansions designed around water culture, allowing visitors to cruise in black canopies and painted boats, viewing characteristic local residences along the pond. Moreover, Hefei has the Daishan Lake Scenic Area, where various water activities can be enjoyed: fishing, camping, hunting. Whichever lakeside tourist area you choose, it will make your heart pure and clear. Truly, "Here lies the best scenery, far from the noise."

Water, the guardian of ecology. Rivers, lakes, and other water bodies are referred to as the "kidneys of the city," being important ecosystems of cities. The Binhu New Area of Hefei integrates water and greenery, maintaining the benign cycle of the natural ecosystem, regulating urban climate, and beautifying urban landscapes. Strolling leisurely in such a pleasant city, isn't it truly delightful and enjoyable?
