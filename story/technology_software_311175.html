Joel Expresses Desire for CSL through Australian Media; Beijing Guoan Holds the Initiative

Since last week, when Newcastle Jets reduced Joel's transfer fee from Beijing Guoan from $1 million to $350,000, the initiative for bringing in Joel has been in the hands of Beijing Guoan. Previously, Guoan had not been in contact with the Australian side due to dissatisfaction with their offer. Now that the biggest obstacle to the transfer has been removed, Guoan still hasn't taken action.

The substantial price reduction was entirely due to Joel's own urgent desire. However, it appears that the reaction from Guoan has been lukewarm, and it seems that the permanent transfer of Australian striker Joel Griffiths to Beijing Guoan is still at an impasse.

Yesterday, John, the executive officer of Newcastle Jets, told local media: "We have sent our $350,000 offer for Joel to Beijing Guoan, but we haven't received a response yet. We hope to finalize this as soon as possible, but the initiative for the transfer lies with Guoan."

Currently, Newcastle Jets' roster is also incomplete, with Italian forward Vigianaroli suffering from a knee injury. Originally, after finishing his loan spell with Guoan, Joel could have become an important member of the team. However, the club's CEO stated: "He's an excellent player, but if his heart isn't here, there's no need for us to force him to stay."

Joel himself is also very eager for this transfer. In an interview with the Australian media, he said: "We hope to resolve this matter in the next few days. The sooner this issue is resolved, the better, so I can prepare to leave. The ideal outcome would be for me to return to Beijing. This would be best for me personally, and my family and future are tied to whether or not I participate in the World Cup. I believe transferring to Guoan is a wise choice."

Initially, Newcastle Jets intended for Joel to play out the rest of the season before heading to China. However, this possibility now seems slim. "I don't think Guoan would agree to such a proposal," Joel said. "If they spend money to bring me in, they naturally want me fully committed to the AFC Champions League starting in February."

In the previous season, both Joel and his younger brother Ryan scored eight goals each, becoming the team's top scorers, which has proven his true ability. From this perspective, Joel would be a good choice for Guoan. However, one point cannot be overlooked: towards the end of last season, Joel was banned from playing seven matches in Chinese football due to the "middle finger incident." Currently, three of those bans remain unfulfilled. For any team, introducing a foreign player who cannot immediately participate in games would naturally be somewhat regrettable.

Currently, Guoan has indeed started its recruitment drive, but with a wide range of choices, the coaching staff hopes to compare options thoroughly before bringing in a forward who suits the team's tactical style. If a more outstanding forward cannot be found in the near term, Guoan may re-engage with Joel at the last moment.
