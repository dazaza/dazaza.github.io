Educational software for autistic children

by phonics6168 on 2012-02-10 02:11:52

As technology becomes increasingly important and beneficial every day, a variety of educational software programs are now available for children with autism. Many mainstream educational software products can also be tailored specifically for autistic children. Since the sensory outputs from a computer (such as adjustable volume levels or screen brightness) can be easily modified, children with autism often find this type of technology easier to use compared to other technologies where they might fail. Here are some software programs introduced to help your child in their education:

For younger children, schemes that focus on sound have been successfully used for many autistic children. These schemes teach phonics through songs and games. Some of the most highly regarded software programs include:

- **Sound Foundations (1 and 2)**: This includes specific headphones for each child's auditory needs, allowing them to interact verbally with the computer.

- **Myself**: It incorporates many graphics alongside text and sound, also offering teaching videos that avoid unnecessary changes and include Makaton signs.

- **Music Maker Jamboree**: This teaches both non-verbal and verbal usage of music.

These programs within the preschool age range help children begin learning skills like the alphabet, counting, simple math, and spatial relationships. Titles like Kidspiration, Mima, Touch It, and Thinkin' Things 1 are worth checking out.

As your child grows and matures, introduce more age-appropriate software into their learning routine. School-age autistic children benefit greatly from programs that focus on emotions, using videos and visual recognition to identify feelings. Another excellent title for elementary school children focuses on problem-solving. It is designed for parents of autistic children and is specifically targeted towards this learning disability. Engaging in problem-solving helps children work on W-questions (who, what, when, where, and why), auditory processing, comprehension, and conversational skills. Other age-appropriate titles you might consider for your elementary-aged autistic child include:

- **School Rules and Routines**: This teaches a child the necessary interactive visual skills required for learning in school.

- **All About Me**: This helps autistic children work on language and visual skills while learning about institutions.

- **Options, Choices**: This includes many scenarios that encourage autistic children to develop social skills and awareness.

Older children may also benefit from some computer programs. As your child matures, he or she might benefit more from using the same computer software that typical students use, but some special programs are still useful for developing key skills such as social and verbal communication. At this stage, it truly depends on your child's abilities, but middle and high school students with high-functioning autism might benefit from the following programs:

- **The Crystal Rainforest 2000**: This helps many autistic children enjoy computer design and programming by developing math and basic programming skills.

- **My Community**: This is best if introduced at a younger age but is suitable for students up to 15 years old, focusing on learning social interaction skills.

- **GrammarTrainer 2**: Specifically designed to help children with learning disabilities learn sentence construction and understand parts of speech.

Remember, there are numerous products available in the market for your autistic child to use on the computer. In this era, computers play a very important role in our daily lives, and learning to use technology is a key skill for all children. By introducing these programs and others to autistic children, he or she can become computer-literate while learning other essential skills.
