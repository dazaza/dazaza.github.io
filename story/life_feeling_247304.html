After work, the night is hazy. I get out of the building, open the car door, start the engine, and look at the distant neon lights. I sit dumbly in the car. Every day, we wear masks to get through the day. We all have something that we can't tell others.

The Internet gives us a space to vent. When we exit this space, we are just strangers who pass by. Because there is no intersection, we can open our hearts. In this sense, we are all lonely, lonely enough to seek comfort on the Internet.

Everyone has their own troubles: love, marriage, interpersonal relationships, life... even vague thoughts! But where do these knots come from? And how can they be untied? We often don't have answers.

Sometimes, when I read other people's sad words, my heart is tinged with sorrow, so I write down a few words. But when I look back at my own words, they are also filled with sadness! Only when writing these words, it is unconscious and involuntary.

In real life, I am like sunshine, so sunny that people feel no shadow. But the me under the sun is just the side I'm willing to show. In fact, most of the time, others only want to see the side they want to see.

We all live alone wearing masks. You may sneer at this, but if you really think about it, it's not true. Sometimes, some things, you can only solve them yourself, think them over yourself. Colleagues cannot be fully trusted, classmates have no time, parents cannot say everything, lovers don't want to tell everything, so you naturally choose silence.

We often say that home is our safest and strongest shelter. But when you face too many setbacks and injuries outside, will you call your parents or lover to complain? No, definitely not. What we Chinese are best at is reporting good news and not bad news. Sometimes, even when you go home, facing the concern of your parents and lover, you still just say "Today is fine" or "I came back to take a look". And when your parents and lover happily prepare a table of dishes you like, they would never think that you are tired, hurt, and come back to lick your wounds.

Our lives can only be borne by ourselves. But who am I? Who can understand myself? Which one in the crowd is the real you?

In front of others, you wear too many masks. A moment ago, when you were alone, you were sad; when you turn around and meet an acquaintance, you smile, and smile so brilliantly, so unconsciously. When that person walks away, your smile freezes on your face.

We are only truly ourselves when we face ourselves. Some things, you clearly know that you handled them improperly, but in front of others, you absolutely won't talk about it. Sometimes, you clearly hate someone, but in front of him, you said a lot of flattering words. Sometimes, you clearly know that others made mistakes, but you are afraid that they misunderstand your kindness, so you say "Good, good." Sometimes, you clearly are unhappy, but you still try to tell jokes to entertain others.

Only at night, when the lights dim and you face yourself, do we realize how tired, forced, and helpless we are. In front of others, we wear too many masks. When we take off those masks and face the real self, we understand our loneliness and sadness. Thus, many sad and melancholy texts are produced. The readers are still those deeply lonely people. Only lonely people can feel the faint sorrow and helplessness between the lines.

Life has too many human factors, and we are too selfish, so we choose loneliness. On the contrary, it is loneliness that makes us. Let us learn to reflect, change, and improve in the long nights.

Loneliness should not exist, but being alone is not scary. With a faint bitter smile on the corner of the mouth, we each live alone wearing masks.
