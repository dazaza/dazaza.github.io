CCTV Exposes Samsung Phones 'Font Library Gate': Design Defect Causes 30 Crashes Per Day

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 12:40:36

From "sudden crashes" to "unable to turn on" and then high-cost repairs, this has become a common issue for many owners of Samsung's NOTE series and S series smartphones. The reporter from the 'Economic Half Hour' interviewed experts who pointed out: This is because there are inherent design flaws in Samsung products, it is very likely that there is a problem with the erase command of the storage chip, which is equivalent to a computer's hard drive. And yet, consumers are made to pay for such hardware design issues, with a single repair costing no less than 800 yuan, and up to 2000 yuan...

The reason why smart phones frequently become "bricks" remains a mystery, with inflated repair costs causing consumer distress.

In May 2012, Samsung Group held a new product launch event in London, UK. At this event, Samsung's third generation GALAXY S series smartphone, the S3, was grandly introduced.

This smartphone, praised by Samsung Co-CEO J.K. Shin as the "most outstanding," performed exceptionally well in the global market, achieving sales of millions of units within just 50 days. In the third quarter of 2012, this phone even surpassed its competitors with 18 million units sold, becoming the world's most popular phone.

The widely popular Samsung smartphone

It was in September 2012, during this period of product popularity, that Ms. Jin from Beijing became one of the users of this phone.

Consumer Ms. Jin: I bought it from JD.com in September.

Reporter from 'Economic Half Hour': How much did you spend at the time?

Consumer Ms. Jin: More than 4180 yuan.

This phone of Ms. Jin's was a contract phone jointly launched by Samsung and a domestic telecommunications company. For her, who had just graduated from university, spending over 4000 yuan wasn't a small amount. At the time, Ms. Jin also considered buying cheaper gray-market goods online, but for better product assurance, she spent more money to purchase this genuine Samsung phone through official channels.

Consumer Ms. Jin: I also chose Samsung because it's a big brand; I thought it would be reliable both before and after sales.

Ms. Jin said she took good care of this phone, using it carefully and never having it re-flashed. But despite this, in June of this year, problems began to appear with the phone, which had been in use for less than 9 months.

Consumer Ms. Jin: It should have been around the end of May to early June, then it started crashing frequently. Sometimes when it was in my bag, others could call me, the call actually went through, but I didn't know. When I tried to restart it, there was no missed call notification, so I missed a lot of calls.

Out of desperation, Ms. Jin sent her phone to Samsung's service center for repair. A staff member received her, checked it thoroughly, and informed her that the issue with her phone was due to software conflicts, which could be resolved by simply re-flashing the system. However, after the after-sales service center re-flashed the phone's system, Ms. Jin's phone continued to crash frequently. The problem persisted.

Consumer Ms. Jin: It crashed again within about half a month. This situation lasted for about half a year, with the phone constantly crashing,

Reporter from 'Economic Half Hour': To what extent did it crash frequently?

Consumer Ms. Jin: About twenty or thirty times a day. I could only force-shut down or pull the battery out.

Reporter from 'Economic Half Hour': Twenty or thirty times a day.

Consumer Ms. Jin: Sometimes, especially during the peak periods, it would crash a few minutes after turning on, making it completely unusable.

Ms. Jin visited Samsung's service center twice more, but each time she received the same repair advice. Even after three system flashes, the frequent crashing issue remained unresolved. With doubts in mind, Ms. Jin decided to consult other phone repair shops. At this private repair shop, the technician quickly provided an answer upon hearing about the S3 phone crash problem.

Technician: Its character library is damaged.

What exactly is the character library of a phone?

Technician: It's a chip on the motherboard that's broken, specifically the storage chip.

This technician explained to Ms. Jin that simply put, the character library refers to a chip on the Samsung S3 phone's motherboard. He believed that some design flaws in this chip were causing the frequent phone crashes. To solve this issue, the chip needed to be replaced with an upgraded version.

The "character library" causing frequent Samsung phone crashes

Ms. Jin: Will everything be fine after replacement?

Technician: Yes, once replaced, it will work fine. The original factory character libraries all have this issue, and it gets fixed after replacement. Do you understand?

This technician said that the character library problem in Samsung phones is not uncommon, and currently, he himself was repairing three units.

Ms. Jin: Which models are most prone to issues?

Technician: Samsung 7100, isn't there another one that needs replacing too?

Ms. Jin: Yes, this one.

Technician: Hmm.

Ms. Jin: That Note2.

After hearing such explanations in the market, Ms. Jin immediately searched online. The search results showed that entering the keyword "Samsung character library gate" on search engines yielded over 1.03 million related results. Searching "Samsung character library" on Weibo produced 817 results. Additionally, there were no fewer than six QQ groups established by consumers affected by the Samsung character library issue, with the largest group having over 400 members. These phones affected by the character library issue mainly belonged to the NOTE series and S series, with the common manifestation being that their Samsung phones suddenly wouldn't turn on, humorously referred to by netizens as "turning into bricks." Such news made Ms. Jin start worrying about her Samsung phone, leading her to visit Samsung's service center again. However, the staff here firmly denied that her phone's issue was caused by the character library.

Ms. Jin: Is it a problem with the character library?

Samsung staff: There's no issue with the character library. In cases like this, it's always software-related, nothing else.

Although Ms. Jin requested repairs, the method used by Samsung's repair staff this time was still re-flashing the system. This was already the fourth time Ms. Jin's phone had been re-flashed within five months, leaving her very disappointed. She believed that Samsung's refusal to admit that the phone's issue was due to the character library was essentially unwillingness to bear the cost of replacing the character library. So, does Samsung offer warranty services for phones identified as having character library issues?

"Character Library Gate" may hide larger design flaws, Samsung's official explanation questioned by experts

From "sudden crashes" to "unable to turn on" and then "character library damage", high-cost repairs have become a common experience for many Samsung phone owners. What is the exact cause of the Samsung phone's character library damage? The 'Economic Half Hour' reporter conducted interviews with questions.

Zhang Xiaolong: This storage chip is equivalent to your computer's hard drive. The hardware storage chip no longer accepts new instructions, stops operating, and dies on its own.

In Beijing, the 'Economic Half Hour' reporter met with Zhang Xiaolong, a former senior engineer at Motorola, regarding consumer concerns about the Samsung phone's character library issue. He told the reporter that terms like "character library damage" or "character library detachment" are actually inaccurate. Because now, in the era of smartphones, the concept of "character library" no longer exists; it is a concept from the feature phone era.

Zhang Xiaolong: During the feature phone era, many programs and phone information were stored in a dedicated chip, and some after-sales and repair personnel habitually referred to this storage chip as the character library chip. In the smartphone era, the function of this storage chip has far exceeded storing character libraries.

In Zhang Xiaolong's view, in the smartphone era, the functions of the storage chip go far beyond what "character library" can summarize. A more accurate description should be EMMC storage chip damage. Currently, many consumers online refer to Samsung phone storage chip failures as "character library gate," which, in a sense, underestimates the severity of the issue. Zhang Xiaolong's viewpoint was also endorsed by Li Yi, the secretary-general of the Mobile Internet Industry Alliance.

Li Yi: It is actually a system-level control chip. When this memory chip malfunctions, the entire system cannot load or operate. Its role is extremely important.

Zhang Xiaolong: (The term "character library") can easily mislead others and consumers, making them think it's a minor issue. In reality, this storage chip is very crucial.

During the interview, Zhang Xiaolong and Li Yi both agreed that labeling the phone's "brick transformation" as caused by "character library damage" might dilute the seriousness of the issue. Chip damage is actually a more severe problem. However, industry observer and president of Feixiang Network, Xiang Ligang, expressed differing views.

Xiang Ligang: It's not about downplaying it. If it boils down to the "character library gate" or a problem with the character library, then there must be issues within the character library itself.

Xiang Ligang believes that the possible triggers for the phone turning into a brick are complex and cannot be simply attributed to chip problems. A slight error in the software's logic instructions could also lead to crashes. Without specific research, it's difficult to make a judgment. However, Li Yi thinks the problem is clearly due to defects in Samsung's product design. Li Yi told the 'Economic Half Hour' reporter that the design flaw in Samsung phones primarily lies in the unreasonable design of Samsung's motherboard. Zhang Xiaolong's view, however, suggests that the problem with Samsung phones might lie in defects in the erase commands of the storage chips.

Zhang Xiaolong: When Samsung upgraded to Android 4.0, the core code inside Android 4.0 would invoke this logic, which inherently contains flaws. There's a certain probability that erasing a specific area of the storage chip will result in hardware damage to the storage chip.

Zhang Xiaolong told the 'Economic Half Hour' reporter that in the mobile phone manufacturing industry, the occurrence of such quality issues in Samsung phones is usually very rare. Typically, a phone undergoes rigorous testing before release. If there are design flaws, technical personnel in the company would easily detect these faults during testing. Following standard production processes, major companies' products almost never exhibit such obvious defects. However, the occurrence of storage chip failures in multiple Samsung phone models puzzled this insider about what went wrong in Samsung's production inspection process.

In the interview, some insiders also had other viewpoints. They believed that the "character library gate" incident of Samsung could be attributed to the stability issues of Samsung products. They think that the increasingly fierce competition in the industry might have led to a rush to bring products to market to capture market share, shortening research and development time and introducing potential instability in product quality.

Xie Yufen: Therefore, they didn't allow enough time for corresponding tests, such as stability tests or aging tests. Or, during the production process, insufficient testing was given to bulk shipments.

Xie Yufen, CEO of ChinaBBB International Procurement Platform, believes that accelerating product advancement speed might have caused manufacturers to overlook product details. However, according to Zhang Xu, the director of mobile security technology at Qihoo 360, all these conclusions still require further data validation.

Zhang Xu from Qihoo 360: We speculate there might be a few reasons. First, the chip itself is damaged, or there might be issues during the chip welding process, resulting in poor contact or short circuits. Another possibility could be due to user misuse.

Zhang Xu told the 'Economic Half Hour' reporter that following last December's complaints about the Samsung Galaxy S3 encountering "character library gate," in April-May this year, another Samsung phone, the Galaxy Note II, also frequently reported becoming a brick due to "character library" damage. However, Samsung's official response has been non-existent, and this secretive attitude makes it difficult for even industry professionals like them to accurately determine the cause of the "character library gate" based on disclosed information.

So, is it due to consumer misuse or manufacturer design flaws? Facing various debates and doubts surrounding the "character library gate," what is Samsung's official stance? The 'Economic Half Hour' reporter has consistently attempted to interview Samsung. After several days of communication, on the evening of October 18, 2013, Samsung (China) Investment Co., Ltd. only provided a brief written response to the questions posed by the reporter. The content of the written response is as follows:

Samsung's official written response to the 'Economic Half Hour' reporter's interview

First, the 'Economic Half Hour' reporter asked: From "sudden crashes" to "unable to turn on" and then "character library damage," how does Samsung view the "character library gate" issue? What is the main cause of the "character library gate"?

Samsung's response is: We have always been committed to enhancing product quality and user experience for a wide range of consumers. The aforementioned issues stem from firmware problems in the initial production of some GALAXY S3 and GALAXY NOTE2 models. This issue has now been resolved. To address this problem, we recommend customers upgrade the firmware themselves or visit nearby Samsung authorized repair stations.

Second: What measures has Samsung taken in response to consumer dissatisfaction?

Samsung's response is: To resolve this issue, we began providing FOTA (Firmware On The Air) firmware upgrades from November 2012, allowing customers to easily perform upgrades. For customers experiencing this issue and visiting Samsung authorized repair stations, we have provided free repair services.

Third: Which Samsung models are involved in the "character library gate" issue? What is the repair ratio? Are there any statistics?

Samsung's response is: This issue occurred in some initially produced GALAXY S3 and GALAXY NOTE2 models, with an extremely low defect rate. From the perspective of continuing to enhance customer service, we maintain communication with customers to ensure that any issues that arise are properly resolved.

Can this first official response from Samsung adequately explain the cause of the "character library gate" and address public concerns? The 'Economic Half Hour' reporter also contacted several industry insiders, who all expressed disappointment with this response.

Zhang Xiaolong: I feel that this response is too official and too cunning. In fact, there is no formal acknowledgment, at least indicating an issue with their attitude.

Zhang Xiaolong told the 'Economic Half Hour' reporter that if the cause of the sudden phone crashes and bricking, as Samsung claims, is not hardware but software, then all products using this firmware would encounter issues, implying an extremely high defect rate, contradicting the statement in the third point.

Zhang Xiaolong: He says the failure rate is low, but if it's a firmware fault caused by design issues, then it means all phones have this problem.

Regardless of whether it's a hardware or software issue, Zhang Xiaolong believes that both should be considered product defects of Samsung, and Samsung should take responsibility for them. However, Samsung has never officially acknowledged this fact, nor has it taken any corresponding measures.

Zhang Xiaolong: Normally, if a design flaw causes this fault, you should proactively inform users or initiate a recall. Since Samsung hasn't officially admitted to this issue, it definitely hasn't issued any announcements regarding it, so the second point's response is a false one.

Mobile Internet Industry Alliance Secretary-General Li Yi directly accused Samsung of switching concepts. If, as Samsung claims, the phone crashes and becomes a brick due to firmware issues, they could be resolved through software upgrades without needing hardware replacements or incurring costs.

Li Yi: Why do I think this is a logical switch? If understood this way, if Samsung understands it this way, then consumers shouldn't have to spend so much money to replace the so-called character library chip. If this were the case, consumers would be happy. Either they could handle it themselves at home by upgrading the FOTA (Firmware On The Air), or they could bring their retail unit for free service since FOTA (Firmware On The Air) is just a simple flash fix. So why are there consumer complaints requiring so much money? I think this is self-contradictory.

And Zhang Xu, Director of Mobile Security Technology at Qihoo 360, believes that whether it's a software or hardware issue, Samsung's response still lacks sufficient information to clarify doubts for both industry insiders and ordinary consumers.

Zhang Xu: To verify this issue, there's actually a simple method. If Samsung tells us which version of the S3 or Note2 phone has the problem, we can find a problematic phone and reproduce the crash issue according to Samsung's solution. Then, after upgrading the phone to the new version via Samsung's FTOA update, if the phone no longer crashes using the same method, we can conclude that it's a software issue and can be resolved through software upgrades.

Efforts from all sides yield only a paper statement, lawyers urge protection of consumer rights

From the consumer's consumption experience, to the on-site investigation of Samsung's service centers in Beijing, to the enthusiastic discussions among multiple industry insiders, until finally receiving a one-page explanation from Samsung regarding the "character library gate" issue, throughout the entire investigation process spanning over ten days, we haven't seen any sign of respect for consumer rights. The "character library gate" issue with some Samsung phone models, whether it's a character library problem or a firmware problem, is not significant for ordinary consumers. What matters is why consumers who purchased defective phones are unable to enjoy the legally mandated consumer rights. Samsung's one-page explanation cannot resolve the issue of damaged consumer rights. The 'Economic Half Hour' specially consulted the head of the Consumer Rights Protection Professional Committee of the Beijing Lawyers Association to understand how Samsung's actions in the "character library gate" incident have infringed upon the rights of phone users under legal frameworks.
