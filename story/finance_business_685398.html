Mother Guo Aiyun: If the kidney transplant is successful after the Spring Festival, just like giving her son his first life 21 years ago, she will use her own organs to give her son a second life. Maternal love is a circle, without a beginning or an end. After learning that her blood type and kidney function matched her son's, Anqing Wangjiang County farmer Guo Aiyun decided to donate her kidney to save her son. She said, "To be able to continue my son's life in this way, no matter how bitter or painful, it's worth it!"

A diagnosis report shattered their peaceful life. Before the kidney transplant, the mother and son often encouraged each other.

At 45 years old, Guo Aiyun is an ordinary rural woman from Baishe Village, Huayang Town, Wangjiang County. The area is a cotton-producing region, and Guo Aiyun and her husband Zu Changyou lived a tranquil life of working with the sunrise and resting with the sunset.

After their eldest son Zu Qing got married, Mr. and Mrs. Zu Changyou focused all their energy on raising their younger son, Zu Yun. Farming life was arduous, but their younger son Zu Yun was studious and ambitious. In September 2009, he was admitted to the Chemistry Engineering and Technology program at Anqing Normal University with excellent scores, which greatly comforted the barely literate couple.

Currently a third-year student, Zu Yun has consistently been at the top of his class. Last autumn, he decided to take the postgraduate entrance exam and prepared accordingly. Besides studying for the exam, Zu Yun also worked as a tutor on weekends to alleviate his parents' financial burden. However, starting from November 2011, Zu Yun noticed some swelling on his face. Initially, he thought he had gained weight and didn't pay much attention.

But on December 8th, while in class, Zu Yun suddenly felt abdominal pain and went to a hospital in Anqing for a check-up. The result showed suspected renal failure. Shocked, Zu Yun called his parents to inform them of the situation.

Upon hearing the news, Mother Guo Aiyun immediately came to Anqing and decided to take her son to Nanjing for a re-examination. Unfortunately, the re-examination confirmed that Zu Yun suffered from severe renal failure, a very serious condition. Learning that her son had uremia, Guo Aiyun almost collapsed.

The loving mother decides to donate her kidney to save her sick child.

In order to ensure timely treatment for her son, Guo Aiyun immediately took him back to Anqing for hospitalization and hemodialysis treatment. Lying in bed, Zu Yun cooperated with the doctors' treatment while still asking classmates to help him catch up on missed lessons. In late December 2011, during the time when Guo Aiyun was raising money at home, eight classmates including Lin Zheng not only helped Zu Yun with his lessons but also took turns staying overnight in the ward to care for him.

When Zu Yun learned that in just one month, the family had borrowed over 20,000 yuan for his treatment, he felt extremely uneasy. One evening, Zu Yun discussed with his mother and decided to abandon treatment. Hearing her son's words, Guo Aiyun stopped him sternly and cried, "As long as it can save you, I'm willing to do anything!"

"Blood dialysis costs too much, it would be better to do a kidney transplant early. Not only is there hope for a complete cure, but in the long run, it would also save money." One day, the doctor's advice gave Guo Aiyun hope. Without enough money to buy a kidney and no time to wait for a donor, Guo Aiyun immediately decided to donate her kidney to her son.

Seeing the whole family tirelessly running around raising money and thinking about her father whose back was already bent like a shrimp, Zu Yun relented and decided to accept his mother's great love, saying, "If I don't stay strong, my family will collapse!"

After making the decision to donate her kidney, Guo Aiyun brought her son to the Provincial Hospital for blood type and kidney function matching tests.

On January 9th this year, the test results from the Provincial Hospital showed that the kidney source between Guo Aiyun and her son Zu Yun was a very good match.

Mother and son together, they fear nothing.

Yesterday afternoon, when the reporter visited Zu Yun's ward, he was lying in bed studying English while Guo Aiyun was preparing food for her son beside the bed. Guo Aiyun said that besides letting her son recuperate peacefully, she also had to encourage him not to become dispirited.

Guo Aiyun told the reporter that although the matching test report had come out, formal kidney transplant surgery still required various physical examinations, which could take two to three months. "Before the surgery, Zu Yun needs to undergo dialysis treatment three times a week."

Guo Aiyun said that apart from instilling confidence in her son to overcome the disease, she herself also needed to remain optimistic. If the kidney transplant is successful after the Spring Festival, just like giving her son his first life 21 years ago, she will use her own organs to give her son a second life.

"The days waiting for the kidney transplant are like being in a war, every day filled with fear and anxiety, afraid of any unexpected changes." Zu Yun said that tears and laughter go hand in hand, but as long as he thinks of standing shoulder to shoulder with his mother, there is nothing that can crush them.
