Which directions will Xiaomi develop next?

by anonymous on 2013-10-22 19:22:20

For a start-up to achieve such good results and influence, it draws much attention from many professionals and the media. Xiaomi's model has become the success method that people in the business world talk about with admiration. The "fast, focus, extreme, reputation" advocated by Lei Jun, the founder of Xiaomi, has become an operating concept that Internet industry or even the traditional industry want to adopt. Indeed, just a few days ago, there was a report on the more than 60 hours of exchange between Chen Nian and Lei Jun from June to August regarding Vancl, as well as guidance provided to Vancl executives, which is enough to show the charm of Xiaomi's model.

By leveraging social media and promotion by Mi fans, Xiaomi spent three years creating a dark horse in the technology industry. Why not say the Internet industry? Why not say the mobile phone industry? It is not because Xiaomi is called Xiaomi Technology Co., Ltd., but because the Internet cannot encompass Xiaomi's current business scope, and mobile phones are fast becoming the next PC industry. However, technology is always eternal and will never change, even after ten or twenty years. I think this is also the difference between Xiaomi and other peers. The emphasis on technology and science has far exceeded people's imagination, and that is why we have today's Xiaomi.

Although Xiaomi has achieved a certain degree of success today, at least for a start-up, there is no company in the world that can be compared with Xiaomi. But behind Xiaomi's certain degree of success, some very thorny problems are also being faced.

For any company, globalization is hoped for, and ultimately to become a globally renowned brand. Xiaomi's growth environment is in China, where the commercial environment and consumers differ greatly from those abroad. Moreover, the Chinese market has given Xiaomi such an opportunity to rise. At the same time, Xiaomi's team has been in the Chinese market for many years and fully understands the Chinese market, but their understanding of foreign markets is obviously insufficient. Attracting international talent is Xiaomi's current problem. After three years of establishment, Xiaomi only had Hugo, who has international market experience, join in October this year. There are not many truly global brands in China now, such as Lenovo, Huawei, Haier and other giants in their respective fields. They have very experienced international teams abroad, and these three companies' internationalization capabilities are what Xiaomi needs to learn from.

Secondly, Xiaomi is a newly established company, and its technical accumulation is not as good as Apple and Samsung, and even the already declining BlackBerry and Nokia. This is most evident in the quality of Xiaomi phones. Although Xiaomi phones have high configurations and cost-performance ratios, the probability of Xiaomi phones having problems is higher than that of Apple, Samsung, BlackBerry, and Nokia. This is also one of the reasons why many Xiaomi phone users are very dissatisfied with Xiaomi. If quality cannot be guaranteed, it is the biggest harm to users. Even if your configuration is high and your price is low, it cannot restore consumer loyalty to the brand. This is also the biggest problem that Vancl has encountered in recent years. On the one hand, it is the accumulation of patent numbers and the quality of patents, and on the other hand, it is the quality and quality of Xiaomi products after they are produced. This is another big problem that Xiaomi faces. I think if there is a suitable opportunity, Xiaomi will acquire other companies' patents to enrich its own technical accumulation.

Thirdly, Xiaomi's brand image is gradually moving away from the enthusiast mobile phone. Initially, Xiaomi's customer positioning was on mobile phone enthusiasts, that is, the group of people who love to play with mobile phones. But with the expansion of Xiaomi's scale, customers have changed from mobile phone enthusiasts to pseudo-enthusiasts, and now ordinary users. In the beginning, people who used Xiaomi were naturally labeled as mobile phone enthusiasts, meaning they were very familiar with mobile phones, which held a high image in the hearts of Mi fans. But with the expansion of Xiaomi's user base, this image has disappeared today. Today's Xiaomi has become synonymous with low-priced and not high-end. High-end has always been the market competition strategy of Apple and Samsung, which is why so many people like to use Samsung and Apple phones. Therefore, the difficulties that Xiaomi encounters in terms of brand image are also obstacles to Xiaomi's further expansion. If Xiaomi wants to maintain its high-end image, I think Xiaomi still needs to reorganize product design and promotional orientation, namely the establishment of a brand image. If Xiaomi's products do not have a high-end image, it will be difficult for them to compete with Apple and Samsung products, let alone the international market which is already the main field of Apple and Samsung. Of course, the current price positioning must be the foundation for Xiaomi to continue to move forward smoothly.

Finally, how Xiaomi deals with public opinion pressure. Not long ago, the founder of Xiaomi published an article on the Xiaomi blog titled "There Are Always Things That Make People Helpless". Huxiu website excerpted the title of this article more realistically: "When Did Criticizing Xiaomi Become A 'Politically Correct' Thing?" Any company will be criticized, even if you are acquired by others, even if you are sold out, such as Nokia being acquired by Microsoft and still being criticized by people. Even if you are a very successful company, such as Apple, since the death of Jobs, the media, peers, and consumers have all criticized Apple, saying that Apple's innovation is gone, and Apple's magic is gone. To a large extent, people's doubts about Apple come from Apple's inability to control public opinion well. In fact, no company can make anyone satisfied. There is an ancient Chinese saying: "How can everything be completely as one wishes, but seek no shame in heart!" I think this sentence fits here best. No matter how good or excellent a company is, it is impossible to satisfy everyone. For example, Apple phones are indeed good, but they are too expensive; for example, Haidilao's service has reached the extreme, but it is not so satisfactory in the United States. So, what Xiaomi needs to do is to focus on its own products and strive to make people who use Xiaomi products valuable. As for these public opinions, let them say what they want, but explanations must be made when necessary. Public opinion should have space for expression, and public opinion should be controlled within a controllable range.

For a company that has achieved a certain degree of success or success, people pay attention to several aspects: how it came through, where it is now, and where it will go in the future? The past is already over, you can't go back no matter what. The present is ongoing, and the difficulties faced need to be solved. As for the future, it is something that many people should look forward to but has been ignored.

For Xiaomi, today it has achieved a certain degree of success, there are still some problems now, but more importantly, where will it go in the future?

Xiaomi's attention to the hardware entrepreneurship field is the strongest among all domestic technology companies of a certain scale. It has sponsored many maker competitions. Although hardware entrepreneurship is just beginning in China and even around the world, just like the Internet and smartphones once were, it will surely spark another wave of technological enthusiasm in the future. Wanqiang Li of Xiaomi once said that Xiaomi's future development direction is to become a company like Muji. I think a more appropriate statement would be to be like Muji in the consumer electronics field. All this is related to the Internet and intelligence.

Nowadays, smart homes are being paid attention to by many people, but I always feel that calling it a smart home at this stage is not quite suitable. At least now it is impossible to turn beds into smart ones, impossible to turn wardrobes into smart ones, and impossible to turn chairs and tables into smart ones. Calling it smart appliances at this stage is more appropriate, such as TVs and lamps. Anything that can be combined with the Internet now is possible. Household appliances should be the best entry point, so Xiaomi now has Xiaomi Box and Xiaomi TV. Therefore, the most likely business aspect for Xiaomi in the future is the smart appliance field, products that can deeply integrate with the Internet, such as lamps, temperature and air control, home security products, and possibly music-related, family KTVs, etc. These are all completely possible. Just the specific product form and integration methods may evolve with the development of products and people's needs.

Duokan is a company acquired by Xiaomi. Xiaomi Box and Xiaomi TV are also handled by the Duokan team, but Duokan still has a part of the business that is slowly developing, which is Duokan Reading. Now Duokan Reading is one of the few in China with a very good user experience, but the number of books is still a big problem. After all, the domestic publishing industry's attention to digital publishing is still insufficient, and the environment for digital publishing in China is completely different from that in foreign countries, with numerous obstacles to development. However, with Kindle entering China, it has promoted the development of digital publishing in China to a certain extent. Now China is about to enter the reform process again, which will inevitably promote the development of digital publishing in China. Developing an e-reader to compete with Kindle is not entirely impossible, and under the increasingly strict digital copyright protection, making the reader free or absolutely low-cost is highly possible, relying on content to make money has always been Xiaomi's strategy, and Amazon is also taking this path, but Kindle's price in China is too high. With a rich collection of books and superior user experience, Xiaomi's e-book reader has great potential to recreate the miracle of Xiaomi phones, and it may even spark a national reading craze.

Under the impetus of the consumer electronics trend, and with hardware entrepreneurship likely to become mainstream in the future, perhaps Xiaomi will acquire many start-ups in the hardware entrepreneurship field in the future to achieve a complete layout in the consumer electronics field. Although Xiaomi Watch has been exposed, the development and competition of wearable devices have just begun, and the consumer electronics field will also merge with the Internet to become intelligent. For example, many products are currently heading in this direction, such as Ji Lu You: Smart Router, NEST: Smart Thermostat Controller, etc. In Xiaomi's future map, hardware will definitely occupy an important part, and the integration of these products with Xiaomi phones will be the core competitiveness.

As a company, whether you are a start-up or a company with decades of history, you need to constantly think about what you have experienced in the past. Lenovo calls it "review", you must also clearly understand your current position, the difficulties and obstacles on this position, and you must also understand where you will go in the future, because the size of a goal, that is, the short-sightedness or foresight of leaders, determines what kind of company this company will eventually become. I particularly like a quote from Steve Jobs, which is a line from the Think Different advertisement, "Only those crazy enough to think they can change the world are able to truly change the world!" Maybe others will say you are daydreaming, maybe others will say you are impractical, maybe others will hit you hard in various ways, but only having great goals and beliefs can help you overcome all difficulties on the road to success, otherwise small difficulties are enough to knock you down.
