The nature of babies is to love playing, and games are voluntary activities conducted for the purpose of seeking happiness. Spending a little time every day accompanying your child in play, you will soon find that the relationship between you and your baby becomes increasingly close, and your baby also becomes happier and smarter in different games. Games are the magic that make both the baby and yourself happy. Frequently playing games with your baby, not only to amuse the baby, but also has more important roles! Emotional communication: Communicate emotionally with the baby through the process of the game, stimulating the baby's curiosity and satisfaction. In contact with dad and mom, social emotions are also developed. Sensory stimulation: Games are usually accompanied by language. Talking to the baby while playing games makes the baby familiar with the parents' voices and appearances, stimulating the baby's hearing, which is beneficial for auditory and language development. Motion development: Babies at each stage will have new motion developments, such as turning over, sitting, crawling, etc. Conducting some games targeting the baby's motion development at different stages is very beneficial for motion development. Happy parent-child games for babies in the supine period. For four-month-old babies, it is the period to develop actions like turning over and lifting the head. More games that can train these actions should be done. Before four months, the baby's cervical vertebrae have not yet fully developed, so there is not enough support to support the head. Therefore, the games should also be gentle movements mainly. Dad's movements need to be relatively gentle. Passive exercises for babies within three months old: Babies within three months old do not even have well-developed necks, so intense games are not suitable. Passive exercises can be tried. Section one: Upper limb expansion exercise: Grasp the baby's two small hands, spread the baby's two arms flat, then cross them in front of the chest, then raise the baby's two arms up, then lower them. Section two: Up and down arm movement: Grasp the baby's two small hands, raise the two arms up, then lower them. Section three: Waist movement: Place the four fingers together, place the hands under the baby's waist, lift, then lower. Section four: Shoulder joint movement: Grasp the baby's left arm, rotate it from the chest upward and then downward, then do the same with the right hand. Section five: Lower limb movement: Grasp the baby's feet, raise the baby's legs up, or alternately raise the legs. Seesaw: Sit on the bed, bend your legs, let the baby lie face-to-face on your knees, support the baby with your hands, then lean forward and backward like riding a seesaw. Peek-a-boo: Cover your own face with your hand, then take it away, talk to the baby while doing this, just like playing peek-a-boo with the baby, which will make the baby feel interesting and happy. Happy parent-child games for babies in the crawling period starting from six months old: Starting from six months old, the whole body flip, sitting, and crawling actions of the baby can be gradually trained. To promote the baby to better and faster learn these actions, targeted game exercises can be done. The spine and soles of the baby at this stage have not yet fully developed, long-term sitting and jumping may affect development. Therefore, when doing games and exercises, attention should be paid to gradual progress. Tunnel crawl: Use your arms and legs flexibly to transform into a tunnel. Parents call the baby's name while enticing the baby to crawl through the tunnel. Kneeling on daddy's stomach: After lying flat, let the baby kneel on your stomach while playing. At this time, hold the baby's hands tightly to support the baby. Benefit: This action can train the baby's kneeling action, exercise the knee joints, and prepare for the baby to start crawling. Everyone flips over together: Lie flat with the baby, gently call the baby's name, then make the action of flipping over to attract the baby to flip over together. Tickling: Gently stimulate various parts of the baby's body, such as the belly, feet, and head. You can gently scratch the baby's belly, or let the baby lie flat, use your mouth to blow air tightly against the baby's abdomen. When the baby feels these slight stimuli, they will be very happy.
