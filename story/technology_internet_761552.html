Yahoo Extends Paid Maternity Leave to 8 Weeks as It Adds to Employee Perks

by geekzhang on 2013-05-03 19:00:21

Yahoo announced an extension of the paid maternity leave for employees to 8 weeks. Female employees can take an additional 8 weeks off after giving birth, enjoying a total of 16 weeks of paid maternity leave.

The extension of paid maternity leave is the latest move by Yahoo CEO Lisa Mayer (Marissa Mayer). Mayer became the CEO of Yahoo in July last year and gave birth to her first child just two months later. The 37-year-old Mayer has been at the center of debates over gender equality in the workplace since she took over Yahoo.

At the beginning of this year, Mayer announced that Yahoo employees would no longer be allowed to work from home, causing a huge stir in the US. Some people believe this will inconvenience dual-income families with children. It was also reported that Mayer had set up a nursery near her office for her newborn child, which caused even more controversy.

Under Yahoo's new parental leave policy, all Yahoo employees, regardless of gender, are entitled to 8 weeks of paid parental leave. Female employees can take an additional 8 weeks off after giving birth, enjoying a total of 16 weeks of paid maternity leave. In addition, maternity benefits include $500 for infant care and laundry expenses.

Previously, male employees at Yahoo did not have paid paternity leave, and the paid maternity leave policy for female employees varied depending on state regulations, but generally lasted about 6 weeks.

This extension of paid maternity leave makes Yahoo's employee benefits more attractive and more competitive among major network companies in the San Francisco Bay Area. Since Mayer took over as CEO of Yahoo, the company has started offering free meals and new, high-end smartphones to its employees, aligning itself with Google and Facebook.

Although Yahoo's new paid maternity leave policy is better than most companies in the US, it still falls short of its biggest rivals. For example, both male and female employees at Facebook enjoy 16 weeks of paid parental leave and $4000 for infant care, while Google offers paid parental leave lasting from 18 to 22 weeks.
