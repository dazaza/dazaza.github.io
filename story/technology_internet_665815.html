Nasopharyngeal cancer is the most common malignant tumor in the ear, nose, and throat department. It often occurs at the top of the posterior wall of the nasopharynx, followed by the side wall. The majority of cases occur in middle-aged people, but there are also cases among teenagers. Nasopharyngeal cancer is mainly caused by viruses, smoke inhalation, irregular diet, and nasopharyngeal diseases. For this reason, the following points should be noted: be careful of weather changes, prevent colds; try to avoid harmful smoke inhalation and actively quit smoking and drinking alcohol; seek medical treatment early for nasopharyngeal diseases. According to scientific analysis, nitrosamines contained in spoiled salted fish are direct carcinogens, especially prone to cause nasopharyngeal cancer.
