"Three Kingdoms" sold for 1 billion yuan, gaining popularity before airing and breaking the "Tuanzhang" record.

by kaxiya on 2009-07-15 14:46:31

The 2009 hit TV series "My Commander, My Team" set a good precedent for TV drama marketing. The collaboration of four satellite channels resulted in the show earning over 40 million yuan in its first round of broadcasts, with each episode selling for over 1 million yuan, setting a benchmark in the history of TV drama sales. However, this record was soon broken. The TV version of "Romance of the Three Kingdoms," directed by Gao Xixi and currently in post-production, was sold in its first round to four satellite channels for a total price exceeding 100 million yuan, with each episode selling for over 1.2 million yuan.

Production Cost of 100 Million Yuan

Currently, "Romance of the Three Kingdoms" is undergoing intensive post-production work, planning to complete editing, special effects, music composition, synthesis, and submission for review within six months. It is expected to be aired after next year's Spring Festival. Gao Xixi's version of "Romance of the Three Kingdoms" features numerous war scenes, a grand production scale, and exceptionally fine sets, costumes, and props, resulting in enormous costs and setting a new high in the history of Chinese TV dramas. According to information from the production crew, nearly 30 million yuan was spent on just the art direction and props departments alone, which would be enough to shoot several regular TV dramas. Adding actor fees and other shooting costs, the production cost of the show amounts to 100 million yuan, with a per-episode production cost of 1.2 million yuan. Therefore, effective marketing is essential to recoup the costs.

First Broadcast on Four Satellite Channels

According to the reporter's investigation, in the current TV drama sales market, apart from some powerful satellite channels that purchase exclusive broadcasting rights at high prices, TV dramas generally follow a 4+4 model: first sold to four local channels, followed by a second round on four satellite channels. This joint sales model ensures revenue. Yesterday, the reporter learned from Jiangsu Satellite TV that they paid over 40 million yuan for "Romance of the Three Kingdoms," but it wasn't for exclusive broadcasting rights. The first round will be broadcast on four satellite channels including Chongqing, Anhui, and Tianjin. Regarding the purchasing prices of other satellite channels, a staff member of Jiangsu Satellite TV said, "Prices vary depending on the platform." However, the reporter learned that Jiangsu Satellite TV has one of the better broadcasting platforms among the four satellite channels, so their 40 million yuan price was the highest, and the prices of other satellite channels wouldn't differ much. Calculating based on this price, "Romance of the Three Kingdoms" was sold for a "100 million yuan" sky-high price. According to director Gao Xixi, even before filming began, the show was pre-sold to Japan's NHK at a high price of 700,000 RMB per episode, ensuring that "Romance of the Three Kingdoms" recovered its costs even before its release.

Yesterday, the reporter interviewed Ms. Yu from the publicity department of Little Horse Gallops, the production company of "Romance of the Three Kingdoms." She stated that the overall marketing situation of the TV drama hasn't been released yet. "I don't know how this news got out. Until the contract is signed, anything can change. I can only say that the sales have been very ideal so far. Once there's definite news, I'll provide further clarification."
