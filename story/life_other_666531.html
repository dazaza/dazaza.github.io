No More Foolish Unrequited Love

Someone said that love makes people blind, but I think unrequited love numbs the five senses and enhances the sixth sense.

When you focus on him/her, and care about him/her becomes a habit, you will keep others at bay. Because, in your eyes and ears, it's all about him/her, and you even wish that all of your five senses were connected to him/her. At this time, no matter how others persuade you, you won't listen, because you firmly believe that one day a miracle will happen, or you hold the attitude of "he/she is good, I am good". At this time, where is your position? Where is your self-respect? Where are your dreams and pursuits? You have been numb, and the only remaining little bit of sensation lies in your sixth sense.

Why is the sixth sense so clear? It's because of the underlying desires in your heart. You hope or yearn to be with him/her in some way, which leads to your abnormal sensitivity and great concern for his/her every move. When he/she gets a little closer to someone else, your heart immediately tightens, and you are filled with jealousy. But what can you do? A word or a smile from him/her is enough to make you find ten thousand reasons to defend him/her in your heart, thinking that the person he/she cares about the most is actually you.

Whether you are introverted or extroverted, you cannot escape the state of coexistence of sweetness and pain in unrequited love. For extroverted people, it's more terrible. Originally good at socializing and enjoying making friends, because of him/her, everything seems not so important anymore. But, how about the result? Have you received the love you longed for? What do your friends think, and do you know their feelings?

That "you"---is me, is you, is many infatuated children and little fools. Maybe we really enjoy it, but does the pain of not getting it affect only you? The feelings brought to you by your "him/her" are directly poured onto the people around you without any filtering. Is it fair to them? Moreover, some of them are people who really care about us. How good is the "him/her" we like, and how worthy is he/she for us to do everything不顾一切，even break our own established principles?

That beautiful emotion is like the moon in the mirror and the flower in the water, which can be encountered but not sought after. Rather than cherishing the person before your eyes, why not happily live every day?

Fool, your life is not in his/her hands, but in your own control, ok? Please, stop being foolishly unrequited...
