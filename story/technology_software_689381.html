Shanghai Women's Volleyball Team Suffers Reversal at www.ra3377.com Real Person Entertainment

by dlhxybh8 on 2012-03-04 03:21:55

The volleyball team of Evergrande wept with delight after their victory, whereas the players of Shanghai volleyball team were all dejected at their loss. Yang Jie, the main attacker was in tears. Her eyes were swollen and glittering with tears as she walked down from the podium. On the afternoon of March 3, the second round of the final of Group A of the Women's Volleyball League ended with Evergrande defeating Shanghai 3-2. Yang Jie performed well at the beginning. She helped Shanghai take the lead in the first set with several counterattack hits. In the second set, her offensive invisibility became the stumbling block for Shanghai's winning. In the third set, her service caused the rival to be stuck in a long rally. With an advantage of 2-1 entering the fourth set, Shanghai received a heavy blow at the start. Zhou Yuan's serve made Yang Jie directly pad fly the ball on the serve reception. Under the pressure of being chased to receive the serve continuously, Yang Jie failed to adjust her mentality timely, which not only caused the collapse of serve reception but also got her offense under control. Coach Zhang Liming had no choice but to replace Yang Jie with Chen Yina. However, Yang Jie who started again in the fifth set failed to score any point. Just passed her 18th birthday, Yang Jie didn't expect to receive such a delayed "birthday present". With red eyes, she said chokingly: "I made too many mistakes in the fourth set, which affected the whole team's performance. I desired so much when facing the opportunity that I fluctuated greatly in the game. I feel like the opportunity has slipped away from my hand...." Two years ago, Yang Jie ever participated in the finals of the league with her team. She was selected into the national team due to her excellent performance in that season. With the experience of participating in the finals, she admitted that she wasn't overly nervous mentally. She said: "Because we lost the first game, we wanted to win more when leading in the second game. The great fluctuations in the game are because I am immature in mentality and psychology." Having contended with Evergrande volleyball team twice in such an important game of the finals, No. 12 (Costa Granda) on the opposite side has quietly become Yang Jie's role model. She said: "I should learn more from No. 12 on the opposite side, whether it is attack or back row defense. There are many things worthy of learning." When will Yang Jie overcome this mental hurdle depends on her own efforts. It is not a bad thing for the young Yang Jie to encounter such setbacks in the league, because discovering problems in time and solving them through efforts and improvements in domestic league can help reduce the bitterness in international competitions. Yang Jie who was selected into the national team again will return to the main attacker position. Although she may not appear as the main player in the London Olympic Games, the Rio four years later will be the peak year for Yang Jie.
