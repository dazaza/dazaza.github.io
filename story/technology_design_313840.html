Mourinho's bizarre changes confuse Italian media; the masterstroke may be beyond the reach of Leonardo and Ferrara for life.

Sina Sports - Before the Inter vs Livorno Coppa Italia match, Mourinho’s list of 19 players puzzled the Italian media. With only four defenders (Mc孔, Donati, Lucio, Chivu), what kind of formation would he deploy?

The "Sport Mail" predicted: "Cambiaso will partner with Lucio, with Chivu and Mc孔 on the left and right respectively." The "All Sport" had a slightly different idea: "Donati starts, Cambiaso partners with the sole central defender Lucio, and Chivu is on the left." If the media could easily guess Mourinho's thoughts, then perhaps Mourinho wouldn't be Mourinho. Lucio as the center-back was correct, but his partner was Chivu, and in Chivu's original position stood Montari...

"The boss told me to play as a left-back, so I did as he asked," Montari said calmly, moving from the midfield to the flank, from a midfielder to a defender, seemingly without discomfort because "I played left-back when I was very young."

After the game, on Inter Milan's official website fan ratings, Montari scored 7 points, which was an average score for the defense, indicating that in most fans' eyes, the Ghanaian's performance was competent. At the 14th minute, Pieri crossed to Lucarelli, Montari preemptively moved ahead of him and headed the ball out of play. However, his usual rashness didn’t diminish due to the positional change. In the 22nd minute, Montari committed a foul against Dani Levićus outside the box, giving Livorno a direct free-kick opportunity. Montari isn't short of defensive physicality, just lacking a bit of calmness. Like in the Champions League against Barcelona, he was repeatedly targeted by Alves on the left wing in the first 45 minutes, lacking adaptability and thought for improvement, often leading to repeated mistakes. But as mentioned earlier, Montari possesses the fighting spirit and tenacity that Mourinho desires. In the Champions League away game against Kiev, it was his long-range shot from the left wing that led to subsequent goals by Milito and Sneijder.

Mourinho loves to contemplate changes in player positions. When Sneijder was injured, he even considered letting Montari play as an attacking midfielder. As for other players, Cambiaso has been used as a center-back, Balotelli has been placed on the wings, Santon has switched from the right to the left. Regarding Sane蒂's positional changes, there's no need for further elaboration.

During the game, Rai's cameras captured details of Mourinho's tactical board, showing four players' names, with a horizontal line separating the first two. Almost every game, Mourinho can be seen writing and sketching on a small notebook. Such actions were once considered a way for Mourinho to relieve stress, akin to Ferguson chewing gum or Ancelotti smoking. But that might not be the case. Inter Milan's ever-changing tactics undoubtedly illustrate that Mourinho is constantly thinking, and meaningfully at that. From trying out four formations in the last 18 months at Inter Milan, to continuously adjusting player positions, Mourinho is actively and proactively building his team.

In fact, this is Inter Milan's advantage over AC Milan and Juventus, an absolute advantage of Mourinho over Leonardo and Ferrara. Such thinking, without long-term refinement in Spain, Portugal, England, Italy, and the Champions League stage, might be something you'd never understand in your lifetime.

Juventus lost 2-3 at home to Napoli, where Mazzarri's substitution of D'Alessandro in the second half proved decisive. Ferrara had no reaction to this move at all, watching the 2-0 lead slip away. Ferrara does make changes, but when trailing, he simply substitutes De Ceglie and pushes Grosso forward, nothing more. Leonardo's situation is slightly better due to AC Milan's results, but the hesitation during the Milan Derby when substitutingGattuso should be etched in his memory. (Giotto)
