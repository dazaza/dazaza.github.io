I. Application

The GZY electromagnetic vibrating feeder is a new type of feeding equipment, and the closed guide trough is the replacement product of the DZ series electromagnetic vibrating feeder (the DZ series was listed as an obsolete product by the state in 1992). It saves 60% energy compared to the DZ series electric vibrator. In mining, metallurgy, coal, chemical, building materials, power, machinery manufacturing, as well as grain and light industry enterprises, it has been widely used. It evenly and continuously or quantitatively feeds block and powder materials from storage bins or hoppers into receiving devices, such as belt conveyors, bucket elevators, screening equipment, crushers, pulverizers, etc., and can also be used for automatic, proportioning, and quantitative packaging. Moreover, it can be equipped with electronic scales or microcomputer control equipment for automatic process control to achieve production automation.

II. Characteristics

Compared with mechanical feeders (such as disc feeders, pendulum feeders, plate feeders, etc.), the GZY electromagnetic vibrating feeder has the following characteristics:

1. No rotating parts, no bearings or reducer mechanism, no need for lubrication or oiling.

2. Simple structure, small size, light weight, easy maintenance.

3. Low driving power, low electricity consumption. The machine does not have a large starting current when starting, and produces low noise.

4. During the feeding process, the material is continuously thrown up and moves forward in a parabolic trajectory, so the wear on the feeder trough is small, making it suitable for conveying highly abrasive materials at higher temperatures (below 300℃).

5. Convenient adjustment of conveying capacity or feeding quantity. If equipped with electronic scales or microcomputer control equipment, automatic control can be achieved.

6. After the exciter starts, its amplitude can quickly reach the stable working value. Similarly, when stopping, its amplitude can instantly disappear, which improves the accuracy of automatic control. Moreover, the feeder can be directly started under full load.

Our factory produces the GZY series electromagnetic vibrating feeder. Due to the use of spiral spring exciters, its performance is better than that of plate spring exciters in electric vibrators, being about 40% lighter than similar specification equipment, with slightly increased amplitude.

2. Working Principle

Figure 2 is a structural diagram of the electromagnetic vibrating feeder. It is made up of a trough body, base, armature, core, coil, counterweight iron, etc., forming two mass points m1 and m2. These two mass points are connected by spiral springs, forming a double-mass directional vibration system.

The current of the exciter's electromagnetic coil is rectified by single-phase half-wave rectification. When the coil is energized, there is a current passing through during the positive half-cycle, generating a pulse electromagnetic force between the armature and the core, attracting each other. At this time, the trough body moves backward, and the main spring of the exciter deforms and stores potential energy. During the negative half-cycle, there is no current passing through the coil, and the electromagnetic force disappears. The main spring releases energy, causing the armature and core to move in the opposite direction toward the trough body. Thus, the electromagnetic feeder vibrates reciprocally at a frequency of 3000 times per minute according to the AC power supply frequency. Since the bottom plane of the trough body forms a certain angle with the line of action of the exciting force, the material in the trough moves continuously forward along a parabolic trajectory.

By adjusting the level of the rectified voltage, the feeding amount of the electromagnetic vibrating feeder can be controlled. The feeder uses silicon-controlled rectifier (SCR) rectified power supply. By changing the conduction angle of the SCR, the output voltage level can be controlled. According to usage conditions, different signals can be selected to control the size of the SCR conduction angle to achieve the purpose of automatic quantitative feeding.

There are three types of feeding troughs: ordinary open type, which is a common form; closed type mainly used in operations with dust prevention requirements, but with a 20% reduction in productivity; and top-vibration type mainly used in situations where there is insufficient space below the feeder.
