Abstract: Theme, form, environment, and urban sculpture are three basic factors. Properly handling the relationship among these three is a condition for urban sculpture.

Keywords: Urban sculpture, theme, language, environment

Renowned sculptor Mr. Liu Kaiqu once pointed out: "Urban sculpture is an important component of urban culture, as well as a symbol of cultural level. It can beautify the urban landscape, offering a unique function that is hard to be replaced by other art forms." [1] Renowned planning expert Ms. Chen Xiaoli noted: "In shaping the city's spirit and image, urban sculpture reflects the city's vitality; promotes traditional culture in urban cultural construction; beautifies the urban environment, strengthens the position and role of public space in public art, and has become an indispensable factor in the memory of citizens from one city. Urban sculpture is considered an artistic work representing the city in open spaces, reflecting specific understandings of urban, social, and human elements. Sculpture creation reflects geographical and cultural information about the city, releasing strong emotions towards the city, helping people better understand and love their own city. This has significant social and cultural implications in guiding urban culture and spirit. The durability of sculptural materials makes them more valuable over time, creating intangible benefits today that will become future urban heritage. A great urban sculpture, if properly maintained, can coexist with the city forever. Good urban sculptures will become witnesses of history and urban culture, even symbols of a city or a nation, giving people a sense of identity and regional markers."

"As an art form in public open spaces, urban sculpture should aesthetically serve the majority of the public. Art has a unique function where outdoor urban sculptures display roles that people enjoy passively, which requires sculptors to have more social responsibility, gain public support, and subtly enhance the value of the city by creating high-quality artistic atmospheres, contributing to improving citizens' cultural literacy. We can see that theme, form, environment, and urban sculpture are three fundamental factors. These factors are interdependent and also influence each other in a complementary way. In this sense, their relationships are an important issue faced by landscape designers. One could say that among these three, the environment is the most critical factor in urban sculpture because the environment determines the theme, and the theme selects the language to express its content in form."

"Hegel said: 'Statues or clay figures, especially when created, must consider their arrangement and location. Artists should not completely focus on perfecting the first sculpture and then think about its placement later but rather connect it with some external world ideas, considering its spatial form and local region. This sculpture should always be associated with space.' Here, what is mainly referred to is that the environment of urban sculpture includes several aspects: First, the social environment, including age structure, awareness state, history, culture, tradition, customs, celebrities, stories, now, future, and environment. Second, the natural environment, including mountains, water, vegetation, terrain, climate, etc. Third, the psychological environment: human emotions, including joy, anger, sadness, happiness, emotional aspirations, nostalgia, relaxation, dignity; spiritual aspirations, pursuit, pride, humility, even feelings, etc. Fourth, the architectural environment: including building era, architectural style and nature, functional construction; architectural space opening, closing, and changing; coarse and fine textures of building materials, and the warmth or coolness of architectural colors."

"Of course, environmental factors in urban sculpture go beyond these, but these are truly the most important and fundamental factors. Designers' comprehensive understanding of these factors and ensuring accurate analysis can only make the environment project suitable. For example, New York, although the subject and environment of the two forms may differ, they share a common feature of being able to handle the relationship between sculpture and the environment well. The natural environment where sculptures are located in New York is the open sea surrounding the island. Psychological factors include the hope of navigation, sailors coming from all over the world experiencing the ups and downs of life at sea, needing sufficient land indicators after landing, satisfying their psychological needs, providing spiritual comfort. When they see this lighthouse-like sculpture, their joy is self-evident."
