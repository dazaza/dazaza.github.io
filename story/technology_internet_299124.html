To facilitate travel for the public and better manage traffic, the Traffic Management Bureau has set up signposts at intersections and sections of roads that allow two-way traffic. They have also reactivated eight electronic police devices on this section of road to remind drivers to drive courteously and legally. According to Article 35 of the "Road Traffic Safety Law of the People's Republic of China", vehicles driving in the wrong direction will be fined 200 yuan. Additionally, according to the Ministry of Public Security's "Regulations on the Issuance and Use of Motor Vehicle Driving Licenses", drivers will also receive a penalty of 3 demerit points.

At the intersection of Yuetuo Road and Meiji Street, pedestrians are seen crossing the road arbitrarily despite the presence of an overpass.

Yesterday afternoon, at the intersection of Yu Hua Road and Weiliang Street, three traffic police officers from the Chang'an District were conducting routine checks on a car driver for possible drunk driving when they were attacked by two drunk men from the car. A patrol officer from the Chang'an Special Police Team who happened to be nearby tried to intervene but was also assaulted.

The Chaoyang Police Station later learned that the offending driver had been taken to the hospital for blood alcohol testing. One young woman was standing against a chair in the police station, clearly drunk. Another older woman was detained at the Qingyuan Street Police Station. The leadership of the Chadian Police Station stated that according to higher-level instructions, they would thoroughly investigate and handle the matter seriously. Since all three parties involved were drunk and unable to provide clear statements, they would wait until they sobered up before taking statements.

In another incident, a middle-aged man refused to park his car at a roadside car wash shop. After getting out of the car, he began assaulting Jiang Yu, tearing off his police insignia and damaging his coat. At this moment, Officer Wang Xiangbin and his colleagues from the Chang'an Special Police Team happened to be patrolling nearby and quickly intervened, only to be violently attacked themselves. Four officers immediately reported the situation to their team while requesting 110 emergency support. Soon, a Chang'an 110 police vehicle arrived on the scene to control the situation. The three individuals, including the driver, were separately taken to the Chadian Police Station and Qingyuan Street Police Station.

Yesterday afternoon, at the intersection of Fei City Yucai Street and Fangnan Road, three traffic police officers from the Chang'an Traffic Police Brigade were conducting routine checks on a car driver for possible drunk driving when they were attacked by two drunk men from the car. A patrolling officer from the Chang'an Special Police Team who happened to be nearby tried to intervene but was also assaulted.

The driver involved in the incident was fined 500 yuan, had 6 points deducted from his license, and had his driver's license suspended for 3 months. The two men who assaulted the police will be further dealt with by the authorities.

After the incident, Wang Hongming used his radio to call for interception at the next intersection. When the truck reached the intersection of Peace Road and Xiuhua Alley, it was stopped by Officer Xue Feng. The driver then reversed recklessly to avoid inspection, colliding with a large truck in front. Despite narrowly avoiding being dragged by the reversing vehicle, Officer Xue Feng was grazed by the side mirror. Eventually, the vehicle was captured after turning into a small alley near the intersection of Peace Road and Minyi River. Blood tests revealed that the driver, surnamed Huo, had a blood alcohol content of 104.74 mg, confirming drunk driving.

Yesterday at 8:30 PM, the Chang'an Traffic Police Brigade informed us that the driver of the vehicle with the license plate number Ji A6310M, surnamed Xia, is an employee of the Bank of Communications in Shijiazhuang. His blood alcohol content was measured at 71.96 mg/100ml, indicating suspected drunk driving. Based on these results, the traffic management department will impose a fine of 500 yuan, deduct 6 points, and suspend his driver's license for 3 months. Regarding the assault on the police by the two men, the Chang'an police will conduct further investigations. This case will continue to be followed by our newspaper.
