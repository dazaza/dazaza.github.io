[Quanzhou Geometry Design Training Institution] is a comprehensive training organization that offers advanced application training in mold design (CAD, UG, and ProE), CAM programming (UG, CIMATRON, PowerMill, and MasterCAM), as well as providing solutions for companies in mold design, product styling, and CNC programming.

The world's manufacturing center is in China, and the core of manufacturing is molds, which are referred to as the "Mother of Industrial Products."

How is the Mold Industry?

Due to the diversity, complexity, and wide applicability of mold manufacturing, the mold industry is called the "Emperor Industry." In the process of designing and manufacturing molds, mold fitters are required to use both their hands and brains — according to the usage requirements of the product (part), they must design the mold structure, draw blueprints, and then skilled workers follow the blueprint requirements to process each part of the mold using various machines (such as lathes, shapers, milling machines, grinders, electric discharge machines, and wire-cut machines). Afterward, the parts are assembled, debugged, and adjusted until the mold can produce qualified products. Therefore, mold workers need to possess comprehensive knowledge and skills. Well-made molds result in high-quality products, reasonable mold structures lead to higher production efficiency, and the work nature is relatively relaxed and flexible, making mold workers stand out as top performers among "blue-collar" workers.

The mold industry is one of the main sectors of mechanical manufacturing and is also one of the industries encouraged by the state for foreign investment. With the transfer of the manufacturing center to China after joining the WTO, the mold industry is expected to grow by 30%. Compared to the IT industry, the value of professionals in the mold industry increases with years of experience and accumulated expertise, making it a truly "never-declining industry."
