Secrets of Kitchen and Bathroom Decoration: Creating Practical Kitchens and Bathrooms

by longersing on 2009-10-29 16:55:31

There are many secrets to kitchen and bathroom decoration. Some teach you how to create a stunning kitchen, while others guide you in purchasing advanced bathroom decorations. However, there are very few practical options suitable for the average household. Below, we will teach you from multiple aspects how to create a functional kitchen and bathroom that every family can afford.

1. Buy enough tiles at once to avoid color differences

Tiles are essential materials for kitchens and bathrooms. Decorative tiles on the market can be divided into floor tiles, wall tiles, and waistline tiles according to their functions. Floor tiles come in a wide variety of colors and patterns, offering plenty of choices. They can be categorized by material into glazed tiles, anti-slip tiles, porcelain tiles, etc. When selecting tiles, you can differentiate their quality by checking the test reports and certification certificates provided by the seller. Tiles that have passed the 3C certification are generally more reliable. It's best to purchase a few extra tiles than your actual budget requires to avoid color differences between batches or delays caused by restocking.

2. Pay attention to the drainage method when choosing a toilet

When purchasing a toilet, pay attention to whether the sewage pipe is rear-drainage or bottom-drainage. For bottom-drainage toilets, the pipe distance can only be less than or equal to, but not greater than, the distance from the center of the sewage outlet to the wall. For rear-drainage toilets, the pipe hole height must match the sewage pipe hole height (referring to the distance from the ground).

3. Three square meters is the minimum limit for a bathroom

An ideal bathroom should be between 5-8 square meters, preferably with separate bathing and toilet areas. Three square meters is the minimum area for a bathroom, just enough to accommodate a sink, a toilet, and shower equipment. When choosing sanitary ware for a 3-square-meter bathroom, it is necessary to consider leaving some activity space. The sink and toilet should ideally be small-sized; the shower should be set against the wall corner, and the showerhead can be a linear shower panel or a simple showerhead. Additionally, using a bathroom mirror can create an illusion of a larger space.

4. Suggest choosing integrated furniture

As kitchen and bathroom decoration becomes increasingly important, integrated furniture has also become mainstream. For most families, it is still recommended to choose integrated cabinets or bathroom cabinets, as these pieces of furniture are designed more reasonably, and branded products have stricter control over environmental protection and safety.

5. Choose advanced kitchen appliances

Ovens, water purifiers, dishwashers, wine cabinets, etc., are all kitchen appliances that can change lifestyles. Although they haven't yet been popularized in every household, they can serve as marker appliances for improving kitchen quality. They not only solve many tedious housework tasks but also make kitchen work increasingly enjoyable.

6. Ordinary lighting is best with ceiling lights

The requirements for bathroom lights are similar to those for kitchen lights, both requiring fog-proof and easy-to-clean features. Therefore, ceiling lights with covers are the best choice. Bathroom lights should be bright rather than too dim. Mirror lights are best with incandescent or natural white fluorescent tubes; otherwise, under snow-white light, people may look pale when looking in the mirror.

7. Attention to faucet selection

Although all are faucets, each has its own taboos. For example, it is suggested to use high faucets in the kitchen for convenience in placing the sink; whereas for the basin in the bathroom, one must pay attention to the size of the faucet to ensure convenient use after installation on the basin. Too long or too short will cause trouble later.

8. Outlets need to be planned in advance

Whether in the kitchen or bathroom, more and more small appliances are becoming daily necessities. For electrical safety and spatial tidiness, it is best to plan the number of outlets required in advance. Each appliance should have its own independent outlet, and attention should be paid to the type of plug. For outlets in damp environments, they must have waterproof covers to avoid potential safety hazards.

9. Waterproofing is the key

Waterproofing is very important for bathrooms and kitchens. It is recommended to implement waterproof treatment simultaneously for both the bathroom and kitchen. The height of the waterproof layer: it is suggested that the bathroom walls reach the ceiling, and the floor is fully covered. Kitchen walls should be 1 meter high, ideally reaching the ceiling; the floor should be fully covered.

10. Use vertical cabinets to expand space

Vertical cabinets are generally large in volume, so their storage space is relatively ample. Generally, they can be used as storage cabinets, where items that are not frequently used can be stored. This not only saves space but also makes the kitchen and bathroom appear neat and tidy.
