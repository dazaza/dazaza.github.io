Nine Powerful Potentials Newborns Possess

by lmxbbwudi23 on 2012-02-27 17:36:55

I. Imitation Ability

Within 8 hours of birth, he could imitate his mother sticking out her tongue.

II. Sound Orientation

In an awakened state, a small plastic box with a few corn kernels or yellow beans inside can be gently shaken 10-15cm beside the newborn's ear, emitting a soft clattering sound. He will turn his eyes or head towards the direction of the sound. Newborns like to hear high-pitched sounds and enjoy listening to people talk. When the mother calls him, he will turn his head. If he listens to books like "Infant Picture Newspaper" every day after birth, the baby will develop a reading interest and habit that will influence him for life. His focus and language development will surprisingly surpass expectations.

III. Gaze and Pursuit Vision

Newborns like to look at things, especially books, colorful objects such as red balls, black-and-white distinct target patterns, bar charts, Chinese characters, etc. He particularly likes looking at human faces, especially the loving smile of his mother; if you wear glasses, it will attract him even more. When the red ball moves, the infant's gaze or even his head will track the ball moving 20cm in front of his eyes.

IV. Touch, Taste, and Smell

Newborns like lying snugly in their mother's arms. When the baby is crying, placing your hand on the baby's abdomen and gently pressing on both upper arms will stop the baby from crying. Newborns can finely distinguish tastes and will have unpleasant expressions for salty, bitter, and sour tastes. Upon smelling an odor, there will be reactions such as an accelerated heart rate and changes in activity levels. He can distinguish the smell of his mother's nursing pad. In good condition, he will also stare and smile at you, suck his own hand, or grab the hand of the person talking.

V. Motor Skills

Newborns possess many innate motor skills. For example: the crawling reflex - letting the newborn lie prone on the bed and using your hand to push against his two feet, the infant can move forward by crawling.

VI. Walking Reflex

Supporting the baby's bare feet upright on the bed, he will take steps forward [catwalk], and children who walk well will do so as if they are strolling.

VII. Startle Reflex

Sudden noise stimulation or being abruptly placed on the bed causes the newborn to immediately stretch his arms, spread his fingers, arch his back, throw his head back, and straighten his legs.

VIII. Swimming Reflex

Babies born through water delivery can swim around in the water without inhaling water.

IX. Grasping Reflex

Placing your index finger in the palm of a newborn, you can immediately feel the baby's grip tighten (grasping reflex). The examiner can use this to lift the baby into the air and hold him there for a few seconds, just as the ancestors of humans would hang from tree branches in the forest, etc.

These abilities present at birth, if not exercised in time, will naturally disappear after a few months. If trained promptly, these innate reflexes will become acquired skills.

The discovery of newborn abilities is considered one of the important events in the scientific history of the 20th century, marking a milestone-like leap in humanity's understanding of itself. It provides us with evidence to scientifically develop the potential of new lives from the moment of birth. No wonder some people compare the discovery of newborn potential to Columbus' discovery of the New World. However, the development of newborn potential must be carried out by personnel who have undergone strict professional training.
