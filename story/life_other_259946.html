Dealing with social engagements is truly a headache. Especially the Chinese custom of no banquet being complete without alcohol causes even more worry. How can one drink to build relationships without harming their health? Please follow our daily health section in the newspaper, where we will provide you with tips every day.

Before the Spring Festival, there is one gathering after another, especially for many business professionals who cannot avoid year-end social obligations. Even if you really don't want to drink, you still have to tough it out and have a few glasses. How can you avoid getting drunk? Let Senior Nutritionist Wang Xiaochun from the Ministry of Health teach you a few tricks.

1. Before drinking, have a glass of milk or eat a few slices of bread. Do not drink on an empty stomach to avoid irritating the gastric mucosa;

2. If you estimate that you will be drinking a lot, take Vitamin B supplements beforehand until your social obligations decrease to protect your liver. You can also consciously eat more foods rich in Vitamin B, such as animal livers, beef and mutton, egg yolks, vegetables, oats, and other whole grains, to increase the level of Vitamin B in your body;

3. When drinking spirits, drink more plain water to help flush alcohol out of your system through urine; when drinking beer, go to the bathroom frequently; when drinking strong liquor, it’s best to add ice cubes;

4. Do not drink too fast or aggressively. Drink slowly so your body has time to break down the ethanol in your system. Drinking multiple penalty shots or chugging drinks quickly at the dinner table can easily lead to intoxication;

5. Eat more animal livers like pork liver while drinking to enhance your body's ability to detoxify ethanol;

6. Eat more leafy green vegetables, which contain antioxidants and vitamins that can protect the liver;

7. Eat more soy products, as the lecithin they contain can help protect the liver;

8. It's better not to drink when you're tired;

9. Do not drink after taking medication, especially after taking sleeping pills, sedatives, or cold medicine;

10. Do not drink carbonated beverages like cola or soda while drinking alcohol, as this can speed up the absorption of alcohol by the body.

Common Misconceptions about Sobering Up

1. Strong tea sobers you up

The popular belief of drinking strong tea to sober up lacks scientific basis. Although the tea polyphenols in tea have some liver-protective effects, the caffeine in strong tea can cause blood vessels to constrict and blood pressure to rise, which may worsen headaches. Therefore, after getting drunk, you can drink some light tea but should avoid strong tea.

2. Showering after drinking

Taking a hot shower after drinking can accelerate blood circulation, causing increased energy consumption within the body, leading to hypoglycemia. For people with high blood pressure or cardiovascular diseases, showering after drinking can easily lead to stroke. It's advisable to rest a bit first.

Tips for Sobering Up

1. Eating fruit

If someone drinks too much involuntarily, they can eat some fruit or drink some fruit juice afterward, as the acidic components in fruit and fruit juice can neutralize alcohol.

2. Eating steamed buns

Many people do not eat after drinking, which is even more harmful. They should eat some easily digestible starchy foods, such as steamed buns or noodles. Steamed buns can convert into glucose, helping to supply blood to the human body and increase energy. Because they undergo fermentation, they also have a neutralizing effect on stomach acid. After eating them, the body will feel better immediately.
