Case: OEM Private Labeling

1. [Apparel Industry] Ms. Lin from Hangzhou: Engaged in wholesale clothing business in Hangzhou's Sijiqing and Beijing's Dahongmen markets, she had no brand of her own and always purchased goods in bulk from other factories. In 2001, through Ruifeng Accounting Firm, she registered the "[XX International Apparel Co., Ltd.]" in Hong Kong [http://www.rf.hk/company/hongkong/] and simultaneously registered trademarks in both Hong Kong and mainland China. Under the name of the Hong Kong company [http://www.rf.hk/company/hongkong/], she commissioned relevant factories for private label processing. The packaging and labels of the clothing were marked with the name of the international apparel company, giving the clothing its own brand and developing towards a higher-end direction. Now, Ms. Lin’s family has invested in Hangzhou, introducing advanced equipment to establish a large-scale garment factory with production lines.

2. [Furniture Industry] Mr. Wang from Guangdong: Founded a furniture production factory in Shunde, Guangdong Province, investing a considerable amount of funds in advanced production equipment. Although his product craftsmanship was good, in the fierce competition of the home furnishings market, consumers favored international brands. After some thought, he learned that Ruifeng Accounting Firm [http://www.rf.hk/] could help customers register an Italian company [http://www.rf.hk/company/italiana/]. He specially came to the Ruifeng headquarters in Hong Kong to consult and entrust them with handling related matters. Our business advisor at Ruifeng explained the situation to Mr. Wang: registering a company in Italy would be costly, and future maintenance procedures would be complicated. We suggested registering an Italian company [http://www.rf.hk/company/italiana/] locally in Hong Kong and simultaneously registering trademarks in both Italy and mainland China [http://www.rf.hk/trademark/]. The instructions and labels on the furniture were marked with the name of the Italian company [http://www.rf.hk/company/offshore/ltaly.html] and the trademark registration TM [http://www.rf.hk/trademark/]. Now, Mr. Wang's home products have truly become popular in the Chinese market.

Case: International Trade

1. [Tax Optimization] Mr. Wang from Fujian: Mainly engaged in project engineering investment bidding, due to business expansion needs, he wanted to establish a reputable group company in mainland China. However, tens of millions of registered capital could not be in place, and the company name he wanted was already taken by others. Later, through a friend's introduction, he established the group company in the Cayman Islands [http://www.rf.hk/company/cayman/introduction.html] and also founded holding subsidiaries in Hong Kong, Samoa [http://www.rf.hk/company/samoa/13235.html], the Bahamas, and mainland China, becoming a true multinational group. After careful planning by accountants, the company's tax was reasonably arranged.

2. [Import-Export] Miss Du from Hefei: Long-term engagement in international import trade. Once, a French chemical raw material supplier refused to sign a contract with her unless she entrusted an overseas intermediary company to sign the contract and issue a letter of credit [http://www.rf.hk/exp/xinyongzheng/], citing the high credibility and reliable payment of the overseas intermediary company. Since this was a long-term business relationship, Miss Du had no choice but to establish a company in Delaware, USA [http://www.rf.hk/company/usa/], opening an offshore account for the Delaware company at a foreign bank in Shanghai (the account has all settlement functions in international trade). This way, she established a long-term good cooperative relationship with the client.

3. [Trade Settlement] Mr. Jin from Tianjin: Engaged in international textile product export trade, he always had to export through middle agents, but was concerned about business secrets and settlement security. For long-term stability and to avoid exchange rate differential losses, he wanted to register an overseas offshore company [http://www.rf.hk/company/offshore/], independently export, and directly settle payments. However, the premise was that the offshore company did not require complicated financial reporting procedures. With the help of Ruifeng professionals, Mr. Jin registered a BVI offshore company in the British Virgin Islands [http://www.rf.hk/company/bvi/bvijr.html] (this company does not need to provide annual financial reports to the government), while also opening an offshore account at a foreign bank in Beijing, completely meeting Mr. Jin's offshore trade planning requirements and allowing for greater international development.

4. [Export Rebate [http://www.rf.hk/exp/ckts/]] Mr. Lin from Xiamen: Long-term engagement in stone import-export trade. Since Xiamen had already canceled the stone export rebate, trade profits plummeted. Through online research, he learned about Ruifeng Accounting Firm and successfully registered a "[XXXX Stone Import-Export Co., Ltd.]". Not only did it eliminate the export rebate issue, but it also allowed him to retain the maximum profit, and the speed of receiving overseas payments was faster than before.

5. [Payment Settlement] Mr. Chen from Shenzhen: Has been supplying electronic components to Hong Kong manufacturers. These Hong Kong manufacturers mostly have factories in Shenzhen, Dongguan, Zhuhai, etc., and sometimes pay with Hong Kong bank checks [http://www.rf.hk/bank/hongkongbank.html]. Mr. Chen always had difficulty safely and directly collecting payments. Later, after registering a company in Hong Kong and opening a bank account there, he not only solved the long-standing problem of difficult collections but also gained the trust of long-term cooperation from clients.
