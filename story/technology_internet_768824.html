Xiaomi's People's War: What it sells is not mobile phones, but a sense of participation

by anonymous on 2013-11-25 11:22:26

　　Xiaomi, a three-year-old smartphone brand, has been very successful: as of September this year, the Xiaomi 2 (including the Xiaomi 2A and 2S), which was officially launched 11 months ago, has sold over 10 million units. In the first half of 2013, Xiaomi sold 7.03 million units. MIUI users have surpassed 20 million, and it is estimated that Xiaomi smartphones will sell approximately 18 million units in total this year, generating sales of about 30 billion yuan.

　　Lei Jun places more emphasis on two other data points: first, the revenue from mobile phone products, with 30 billion yuan surpassing Lenovo and Huawei's mobile phone product revenues, making Xiaomi the top domestic mobile phone revenue earner; second, client activity levels are higher than other domestic phones, ranking alongside Samsung in the charts.

　　The former represents scale, while the latter represents user activity levels. Why has Xiaomi succeeded?

　　Internally and externally, many lessons have been drawn from Xiaomi’s success. Generally speaking, these include two aspects: one is an extreme user experience, achieved through what Lei Jun calls the "triathlon," or software + hardware + internet; the second is internet-driven, including internet-based marketing, channel internetization, and supply chain management internetization. Lei Jun summarizes this as "focus, extreme, reputation, fast."

　　What is discussed most by outsiders is the marketing aspect, with some industry competitors even believing that Xiaomi's success is solely due to its marketing strategies. Vice President and co-founder of Xiaomi, Li Wanqiang, told the "21st Century Business Herald" that Xiaomi's success is not just due to marketing, and Xiaomi's marketing is not what outsiders perceive it to be—such as simply posting tweets or sharing WeChat messages—but rather is driven by a comprehensive set of internet logic.

　　What is Xiaomi's secret? Li Wanqiang's answer is: participation, participation, and more participation. From product development, marketing, to service, users are fully involved throughout.

　　Independent analyst Jin Cuodao said: in a sense, what Xiaomi sells isn't phones, but participation.

　　Mi Fans Economics

　　In 2009, Lei Jun founded Xiaomi for his second entrepreneurial venture, and the first product was the MIUI operating system.

　　Li Wanqiang was responsible for MIUI operations at the time. Lei Jun asked him: "Can we get 1 million users without spending any money?" How do you attract users without spending money? The only way is to build word-of-mouth in forums. Under heavy pressure from Lei Jun, Li Wanqiang began leading his team to frequent forums, post comments, advertise, and seek out experienced users.

　　Li Wanqiang selected 100 people from the initial 1,000 to become superusers, participating in MIUI's design, development, and feedback. These 100 people became the "sparks" of the MIUI operating system, and also the first Mi Fans.

　　Later, when Xiaomi started making phones, they followed the same approach. On a "zero budget," Li Wanqiang established the Xiaomi forum, which became the hub for Mi Fans.

　　The difference is that when MIUI started, it had no resources. By 2010, when the decision was made to produce Xiaomi phones, the MIUI forum already had over 1 million registered users spread across dozens of countries around the world, who became the first fans of Xiaomi phones. Another difference was that by 2010, Weibo had begun to gain popularity, and naturally, the fan base expanded from forums to Weibo.

　　Lei Jun later summarized that the rise of Weibo helped Xiaomi, and Xiaomi capitalized on this trend.

　　Currently, on the Xiaomi forum, there are several core technical sections: resource downloads, beginner guides, Xiaomi Academy, and later lifestyle sections such as Cool Play, Snapshots, and Popcorn were added. Currently, Xiaomi forum registrations have exceeded 10 million, with daily posts exceeding 100,000.

　　On the Xiaomi forum, Mi Fans participate in surveys, product development, testing, dissemination, marketing, public relations, and multiple other aspects. In addition to online activities, there is also a powerful offline platform called the "Local Meetup."

　　Currently, Mi Fan "Local Meetups" cover 31 provinces and cities, where each local group organizes various events independently. Xiaomi officially holds "Xiaomi Local Meetups" every two weeks in different cities, deciding the order based on the number of users analyzed from the backend. Announcements are posted on the forum, and users sign up to attend. Each event invites 30-50 users to meet face-to-face with engineers.

　　Li Wanqiang says that Mi Fan culture is somewhat similar to car enthusiast clubs. Car clubs gather because of a shared interest in cars and then organize various forms of offline activities. Mi Fans gather because of Xiaomi phones, discussing them online and organizing offline activities, even engaging in charitable causes.

　　When there are no Mi Fan activities, or in areas where no activities are held, local Mi Fans spontaneously organize various forms of local gatherings. Across the country, there are three to four hundred events annually.

　　Additionally, Xiaomi has established a "Mi Fan Festival," a party to celebrate with users. This is a festival for Mi Fans, where during each annual Mi Fan Festival event, Lei Jun shares new products with Mi Fans and builds emotional connections.

　　Mi Fans are Xiaomi's most loyal users, with 42% of Mi Fans purchasing 2-4 phones.

　　Social Marketing Miracle

　　Forums and Weibo are not all of Xiaomi's social marketing efforts; there are also WeChat, QQ Zone, and others.

　　Li Wanqiang says Xiaomi has clear divisions for different community channels, simply put: "Weibo attracts new users, forums retain users, and WeChat serves as customer service." Weibo's strong dissemination capability is suitable for quickly infecting and spreading information within large populations to acquire new users; forums are suitable for retaining content operations and maintaining the activity of existing users; and WeChat serves as a super customer service platform.

　　Currently, Xiaomi Forum has nearly 10 million registered users, with 1 million users discussing daily, and over 200,000 daily posts. Xiaomi's Weibo account has over 2 million followers; the WeChat account subscription is 2.56 million, with over 30,000 daily user interactions.

　　Xiaomi recently entered QQ Zone, where Xiaomi's certified account has over 10 million fans, and Xiaomi easily generates tens of thousands of forwards during activities on QQ Zone.

　　On Tmall's Double 11 day, 50,000 Xiaomi Boxes were sold out in 13 minutes, and 110,000 Xiaomi 3 phones were sold out in 2 minutes. The single-store sales reached 553 million yuan, creating records for "the fastest store to break 1 billion yuan in sales, the highest single-store sales in the phone category, and the highest attention to a phone brand."

　　Through what forms does user participation occur? Xiaomi typically uses two methods: topics and activities.

　　Li Wanqiang lists a few activities, one being "150 grams of youth." After the topic was released, everyone was discussing: "If the human soul weighs 21 grams, why is it 150 grams?" When the youth version of the phone was released, the answer was revealed: 150 grams is the weight of the youth version of the Xiaomi phone.

　　On the release day of Xiaomi's Youth Edition phone, the "150 grams of youth" Weibo post set a record for the highest number of retweets on Weibo in 2012. The activity had over 2 million retweets and over 1 million comments. Li Wanqiang was proud to say: "This was achieved without spending a single cent."

　　Another activity was "I am a Phone Addict." In August 2011, Xiaomi launched this activity on Weibo, where Lei Jun led the way by showing off his "collection." Users' nostalgia and desire to show off were triggered, and within a short time, 1 million users participated, again without spending a penny.

　　Xiaomi Community held a "Wisdom and Courage Challenge" activity, where participants competed to see who knew more about Xiaomi phone parameters. Winners received rewards like badges and points from Xiaomi Community. The activity saw 18 million participant visits, with 1 million users participating. What was triggered again was people's desire to show off.

　　When Redmi was launched, Xiaomi collaborated with QQ Zone to jointly launch an activity, asking users to guess what the product would be. 6.5 million people participated in this activity, with 7.5 million users making reservations. The first batch of 100,000 Redmi phones sold out within 90 seconds.

　　This is the miracle Xiaomi created in social marketing. Li Wanqiang interprets the psychological basis behind this miracle: it is not just exploiting human vanity, but satisfying one of the most basic needs of self-awareness and self-expression. Vanity and a sense of existence are the most visible group consciousness characteristics in the transitional period between the post-industrial era and the digital age on the Internet.

　　Parallel Mode, Full Product Cycle Participation

　　MIUI's development edition is released every Friday, which Xiaomi defines as "Orange Friday": Xiaomi's brand tone color is orange, and every Friday at 5 PM, MIUI is officially upgraded.

　　On the Xiaomi forum, users can decide on the direction of product innovation or the addition/removal of functions. Xiaomi has established the "Popcorn Award" for this purpose: on the following Tuesday, Xiaomi selects the best project based on user votes for new features, rewarding employees with the "Popcorn Award."

　　Many Mi Fans participate in discussions about product functions, aiming to make improvements in the next version. This complete system that directly links employee rewards and punishments to user experiences and feedback ensures that all employee motivation is not based on large project teams or personal preferences of bosses, but on user feedback.

　　This activity has continued for more than 3 years and continues to deeply influence and guide the design and improvement of Xiaomi products.

　　Li Wanqiang said: "Every Friday when the development edition is released, the click rate on the MIUI community is hundreds of thousands to millions. In the previous week or two weeks, users discuss with my product managers and teams on the forum, exploring what features I want and whether the function is good enough."

　　Throughout the entire product development process, without Xiaomi actively guiding, many core users clearly know which engineer is responsible for the phone call function, who did a certain SMS feature, praising them when done well and criticizing them when not.

　　Recently, Zhang Ruimin, Chairman of Haier Group, privately exchanged views with the "21st Century Business Herald": future production and manufacturing will be parallel, meaning developers, consumers, supply chain partners, sales channels, after-sales services, etc., participate in product design from the start, involving themselves in development, product design, production, sales, and services throughout the entire product cycle. In contrast, past production and manufacturing were serial, where developers, consumers, supply chains, channels, and after-sales services were fragmented, only participating in specific stages.

　　"Parallel mode, full product cycle participation" is precisely Xiaomi's secret: Xiaomi Company, Mi Fans, Xiaomi suppliers, Xiaomi e-commerce (xiaomi.com), and Xiaomi after-sales services participate in all aspects of "Xiaomi Phones," with high-frequency interaction and deep involvement from all parties involved.

　　The role of social marketing in this is to involve more people in Xiaomi's initiated "People's War" through interaction and word-of-mouth models.

　　It is precisely in this sense that Xiaomi doesn't sell phones; it sells participation.
