Four Major Techniques for Guidance in Filling out College Entrance Examination Voluntary Forms for Adults

I. Selection of Learning Format

The teaching methods for the adult college entrance examination are divided into three formats: full-time, part-time, and correspondence courses. Candidates should choose a suitable learning format based on their own actual situation.

Part-time: Part-time teaching generally recruits students at the school's location, with classes mostly held at night or on weekends. This is suitable for employed candidates located near the school.

Full-time: Younger candidates or those who wish to experience university life can choose the full-time format. Full-time study involves studying on campus in a full-time manner, similar to regular universities. Students have normal, relatively fixed classrooms and management requirements, as well as stable winter and summer vacation schedules. (Note: Starting from 2008, regular colleges and universities stopped recruiting full-time adult education classes.)

Correspondence: This learning format is also suitable for working professionals with limited spare time. Correspondence teaching mainly involves self-study with planned, organized, and guided instruction, along with systematic centralized face-to-face teaching. The main components of correspondence teaching include tutoring, answering questions, assignments, experiments, internships, exams, course design, graduation design, and defense. Each academic year includes about three centralized face-to-face teaching sessions lasting 10 days or half a month. For schools that recruit teachers, face-to-face teaching time is generally during winter and summer vacations.

II. How to Choose a School

Choosing a school for the adult college entrance examination differs from the regular college entrance examination. Candidates should fully consider the following factors when selecting a school:

1. If you are an employed candidate looking to use your free time for study, it’s best to choose a school that offers part-time classes. To avoid affecting your work, it’s preferable to choose a school close to home or your workplace. Of course, if you choose correspondence, this factor doesn’t need to be considered.

2. In recent years, the value of adult college entrance examination diplomas has been questioned. However, if you attend prestigious universities like Peking University or Tsinghua University through adult education, the value of your diploma won't be worse than that of a regular university. Therefore, besides considering distance, you should also consider the reputation of the school.

3. For younger candidates or those hoping to experience university life, you can choose full-time study. However, many adult education colleges of universities do not offer classes on the main campus. Therefore, the environment of the school and its surroundings should be your main consideration.

III. How to Choose a Major

In general adult college entrance examination enrollment, science majors are concentrated in communications, electronics, and computer-related fields, while liberal arts majors are concentrated in foreign languages, accounting, economics, and law. Adult colleges do not categorize schools, and all score lines are the same. Schools that are particularly popular may adjust their recruitment plans based on the popularity of different majors. For key institutions and popular majors, candidates who barely meet the score line should carefully consider before choosing. When registering, focus on practicality and reality. It's important to learn useful knowledge rather than follow trends blindly. Candidates for upgrading from a junior college degree to a bachelor's degree should preferably choose a major related to their original one.

For high school graduates applying for a bachelor's degree or associate degree, choose your major based on your interests and actual foundation. Among many students who hope to receive higher education through adult education, most hope to enter prestigious universities. Taking well-known universities in Beijing as an example, their adult education admission quota distribution has the following characteristics: all evening school admission quotas are in Beijing. All correspondence student admission quotas are outside the province. Most full-time class (full-time) admission quotas are in Beijing. IV. How to Fill Out Application Forms

The adult college entrance examination adopts the method of filling out application forms first and then taking the exam. When filling out the application form, liberal arts and science majors starting from high school cannot be applied for simultaneously; high school starting point for a bachelor's degree can apply for both a bachelor's degree and an associate degree (vocational high school) major, and the principle of combining is that the cultural examination subjects are the same majors; police, medical, and traditional Chinese medicine majors cannot be applied for simultaneously; police majors can apply for liberal arts associate degrees; traditional Chinese medicine and Western medicine majors can apply for science associate degrees; art and sports majors must be filled in the [art, sports volunteer column]; applying for art majors can apply for liberal arts majors; applying for sports majors can apply for science majors; professional upgrading to a bachelor's degree can only apply for combined majors within the unified examination subjects.

High starting point: In the art and sports majors (first batch of admissions), two volunteer schools can be selected, and one major can be selected for each volunteer school; in the non-art and sports majors for high school starting point for a bachelor's degree (second batch of admissions), two volunteer schools can be selected, and one major can be selected for each volunteer; in the non-art and sports majors for vocational high school (associate degree) (third batch of admissions), two volunteer schools can be selected, and two majors can be selected for each volunteer school.

Professional upgrading to a bachelor's degree: Two volunteer schools can be selected, and two majors can be selected for each volunteer school. In the registration volunteer information, whether to agree to adjustment must be filled in. If agreed to adjustment, nearby schools, similar majors, and similar learning formats can be selected. Applying for vocational high school (associate degree) and professional upgrading to a bachelor's degree can only be combined within the unified examination subjects. High starting point for a bachelor's degree can be combined with vocational high school (associate degree). Applying for art majors can combine with liberal arts majors; applying for sports majors can combine with science majors.
