The most critical aspect of winter skincare is hydration, and using certain ingredients can effectively provide moisture to the skin. Today, let's take a look at how mung beans and coix seeds can hydrate your skin! First, prepare the ingredients: mung beans, coix seeds, and yam. These ingredients not only can be used for porridge but also for skincare. Coix seeds are known as the "Queen of Beauty," and many cosmetics contain coix seed components. Crush these ingredients.

After mixing and crushing the ingredients, we will prepare some cucumbers and loofahs. Everyone knows that cucumbers have moisturizing effects, and in recent years, loofah water has become popular. Therefore, these two can be blended into juice and used together with the crushed powder. The cucumber juice and loofah juice can be mixed separately with the crushed powder. Cucumber water, besides having moisturizing effects, also has excellent cleaning properties, so it can be used as a cleansing and moisturizing mask. Loofah can be used purely for hydration.

Before use, you can appropriately add some dry ingredient powder to ensure that the blended mask adheres evenly to the face. Apply the blended cucumber or loofah mask on your face, wait for 15 minutes. In winter, water loss accelerates, so don't leave it on for too long. If you're worried about drying out, you can add another layer of a water mask to enhance hydration.

After 15 minutes, wash off the mask from your face with warm water, and you'll have a well-hydrated, tender face. However, don't forget to continue with your skincare routine afterward.
