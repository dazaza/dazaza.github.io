How to Properly Plan Your Life

by qingchuyulan on 2010-04-12 22:15:56

After the age of 20, a small part of people are similar; they go to school for further education or choose not to... building their foundation. In terms of relationships with the opposite sex, societal values, and through trial and error, they complete their foundational education. Choosing to study should be done without interruption. When you have not yet entered an industry, take as much advantage of studying as possible because no matter when you enter an industry, you will still be considered a newcomer to society. However, once you have work experience and decide to return to school, there will naturally be more obstacles and struggles. This is because you may question whether, at your current age, conditions, qualifications... it is worth making such an investment in returning to school. If you consider yourself an older person who must work, I believe that academic credentials are important to you. Otherwise, time is precious and does not allow you to make mistakes. Between the ages of 20 and 25, you need to understand, control, and plan your future. Once decided, it should be a path without regret. You have just been legally granted various rights, and correspondingly, you must fulfill your duties and responsibilities regarding tasks and learning. At this time, you are battling with worries, conflicts, and pain. Worry comes from beginning to lose some autonomy, conflict arises from the inability to sever the umbilical cord with parents, and pain stems from starting to experience mistakes. You need to begin planning for your future, such as further education, employment, relationships... taking back control of your life rather than being constantly influenced by others and allowing them to sway your future. [Learn interpersonal relationships, make more positive friends, and within ten years, these friends will become key figures in the industry.] Between the ages of 25 and 30, you are like a sponge, eager to absorb knowledge and willing to be pushed, all for personal growth. At this stage, you should focus on career orientation, salary treatment, promotions, and job transfers. These should be carefully considered because only through hard work can you actively pursue opportunities. As a newcomer to society, your motivation should drive you to carve out your own place. Due to lack of experience, you don't know how to handle setbacks. With limited capital, you rely on fate and relationships. At this point: earning someone else's salary, learning from their experience, giving your youth, and building your future. PS: By the age of 30, pass the English Level 6 exam, able to directly read English literature and listen to English broadcasts. Complete MBA courses. Have a certain understanding of Zhuangzi philosophy and Confucian philosophy, maintain good health, and accumulate a certain level of interpersonal relationships. [Learn to accumulate experience, seek opportunities, and let good mentors help elevate your growth.] Between the ages of 30 and 35, you need to learn how to identify opportunities and seize them without room for making mistakes anymore. At this stage, you should focus on career direction and family orientation. Work should shift from physical effort to mental effort. You should look towards the future, not the present, facing a broad life rather than limiting yourself. Early marriage is a significant decision many people face. Facing early marriage, many believe it marks the end of a responsibility, but in reality, it is the beginning of learning. Just like newly listed companies that think they've achieved their goals, forgetting their corporate responsibilities and neglecting their core business, leading to a nightmare start. Isn't managing one's family akin to running a business? Making money aims to provide a better life for your family, but this should never mean neglecting your family. A person who poorly manages their family, even if they earn worldwide wealth, gains only superficial applause and has a permanent void in their life. Home should be your smallest spiritual pillar, source of strength, and strong backing! PS: Establish life goals, create detailed life plans, and fully follow those plans. Do not give up, do not abandon. [Time management, mental flexibility; work smarter, not harder.] Between the ages of 35 and 40, you should enjoy giving hope and live a life of quality and style. At this point, you should be enterprise-oriented, farming merely a form of leisure, and even more so, a responsibility to others. If you are dedicated to research, you should not just exhaust your life's efforts. Working tirelessly day and night, you should aim to establish a research institute and lead a team in further development. If you are a corporate manager, you should not remain stuck in trivial matters. Instead, you should have the ability to lead employees and families towards a better life. The larger your vision, the greater your achievements. Be an influential person, not one easily influenced. PS: Be someone who influences others, bringing happiness and joy. Bring hope to more people. [Regardless of how glorious your future might be, can you still envision yourself ten years ago?] Put your heart into it! Isn't all our hard work now for our children, partners, elders...? Farming shouldn't equate to life, nor should it be about managing another person's affairs. Try asking yourself: health, wealth, self-growth, interpersonal relationships, and time freedom—what drives you to work hard? Few would willingly give up any aspect. These are precisely what propel young people forward. Ten years from now, will you have achieved it ahead of schedule or delayed its abandonment? [Rather be busy with dreams than lose dreams due to busyness.] I see too many excellent friends around me, superior to me in many ways, unfortunately spending their days in endless toil, investing more time, energy, and resources, yet not receiving the deserved value. The reasons vary, but striving in the right direction, seizing opportunities, and rejecting inappropriate ones are crucial. Jordan playing basketball all his life wouldn't find his stage in baseball. Don't let busyness blind your vision until you look back and ask: Am I still capable like Lian Po in my old age? [Where your time goes, so do your results.] When you succeed alone, you only experience the joy of one person! Understand sharing and giving. True happiness comes from seeing your friends improve due to your growth, both spiritually and materially. True success comes from your contributions improving the lives of those around you, giving them hope and a better quality of life. We may not be doing charity work or have the capacity to help everyone, but we can use our individual power to help close friends and strangers we encounter. Extend your hand when they need it! Too many people wait for benefactors in life. Wise as you are, why not start helping others instead? "Some people in their 40s and 50s still proudly mention their alma mater. I find it strange that they consider their school years the peak of their life, whereas in reality, their journey uphill begins after graduation." PS: Learn to help others, manage time, and plan your life...
