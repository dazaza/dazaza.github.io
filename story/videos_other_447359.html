SNK is a Japanese game development and sales company. Originally named Shin Nihon Kikaku, the name SNK originates from the Romanization of the Japanese phrase "Shin Nihon Kikaku." The company has produced famous games such as "The King of Fighters," "Samurai Spirits," and "Metal Slug." On October 30, 2001, the company declared bankruptcy and merged with Playmore to form the current SNK Playmore company.
