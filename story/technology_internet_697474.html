According to data from the National Bureau of Statistics, in 2008, the most important indicator of inflation, the Consumer Price Index (CPI), rose by 5.9% for the whole year. Generally speaking, when the CPI increase exceeds 5%, it indicates serious inflation.

Inflation is the archenemy of wealth, especially for those who have large bank deposits and bonds, as its erosive effect is more pronounced. Therefore, many people resort to investment to counteract inflationary pressures, but most only focus on the stock market, often neglecting the importance of "diversified investment," which has caused many investors to suffer heavy losses during events such as the 1997 Asian financial crisis, the 2000 dot-com bubble burst, and the recent financial tsunami. The key to "diversified investment" is allocating funds across various assets with low correlations between their rates of return. This way, risks can be reduced, following the principle of "not putting all your eggs in one basket." Currently, the main categories of assets for diversified investment include stocks, bonds, and money market instruments.

The King of Currencies - Gold should be your best choice for strategic investment.

Investment value of gold:

The investment value of gold is mainly reflected in two aspects: long-term preservation of value and asset diversification.

(1) Long-term preservation of value

There are only over 150,000 tons of gold globally, and this figure increases at an annual rate of only 2%. However, the enthusiasm for gold investment is rapidly rising, with demand surging by 51% alone in the second quarter of this year. Rarity makes things precious, and the tight supply-demand relationship in the gold market determines its ability to preserve value against inflation in the long term. In the past four centuries, compared to the significant devaluation of global currencies, the purchasing power of gold has only increased. The famous economist Keynes pointed out: Gold, as the last guardian and reserve fund for emergencies, has yet to be replaced by anything else. This highlights the effectiveness of gold in resisting changes in currency value and price increases brought about by inflation.

(2) Asset diversification

International gold prices are mainly determined by the US dollar, crude oil prices, and international geopolitical factors, with the dollar's influence exceeding 50%, moving inversely to gold prices. Under the U.S.'s persistent policy of quantitative easing, allowing expectations of dollar depreciation, more professional investors buy gold from the perspective of asset allocation to hedge against the risk of falling dollar exchange rates. The great bull market in gold has arrived, and after breaking the historical high before the financial tsunami, international gold prices continue to rise strongly. Now is the perfect time for you to choose gold products as a stable investment to optimize your financial portfolio.
