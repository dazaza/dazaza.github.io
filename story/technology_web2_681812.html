Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is formulated in accordance with the Constitution to regulate social insurance relations, safeguard citizens' lawful rights and interests to participate in social insurance and enjoy social insurance benefits, enable citizens to share in the fruits of development, and promote social harmony and stability.

Article 2 The State establishes social insurance systems such as basic old-age insurance, basic medical insurance, work-related injury insurance, unemployment insurance, and maternity insurance to ensure that citizens have the right to obtain material assistance from the State and society under circumstances such as old age, illness, work-related injuries, unemployment, and childbirth.

Article 3 The social insurance system adheres to the principles of broad coverage, ensuring basic needs, multiple levels, and sustainability. The level of social insurance should be commensurate with the level of economic and social development.

Article 4 Employers and individuals within the territory of the People's Republic of China shall pay social insurance contributions in accordance with the law. They have the right to inquire about contribution records and personal entitlement records and request social insurance administration institutions to provide related services such as social insurance consultation. Individuals enjoy social insurance benefits in accordance with the law and have the right to supervise their employers' payment on their behalf.

Article 5 County-level or higher people's governments shall incorporate the social insurance cause into national economic and social development plans. The State raises social insurance funds through multiple channels. County-level or higher people's governments shall provide necessary financial support for the social insurance cause. The State supports the social insurance cause through tax preferential policies.

Article 6 The State implements strict supervision over social insurance funds. The State Council and the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government establish and improve systems for supervising and managing social insurance funds to ensure their safe and effective operation. County-level or higher people's governments take measures to encourage and support all sectors of society to participate in the supervision of social insurance funds.

Article 7 The social insurance administrative department of the State Council is responsible for social insurance management nationwide. Other relevant departments of the State Council are responsible for relevant social insurance work within the scope of their respective duties. The social insurance administrative department of a local people's government at or above the county level is responsible for social insurance management within its administrative region. Other relevant departments of a local people's government at or above the county level are responsible for relevant social insurance work within the scope of their respective duties.

Article 8 Social insurance administration institutions provide social insurance services and are responsible for social insurance registration, recording of personal entitlements, payment of social insurance benefits, etc., PICC.

Article 9 Trade unions legally uphold the legitimate rights and interests of employees, have the right to participate in research on major matters of social insurance, join social insurance supervisory committees, and supervise matters related to employees' social insurance entitlements.

Chapter II Basic Old-age Insurance

Article 10 Employees shall participate in basic old-age insurance, which is jointly paid by employers and employees. Self-employed individuals without employees, part-time workers not participating in basic old-age insurance through an employer, and other flexible employment personnel may participate in basic old-age insurance and pay contributions individually. The method for civil servants and staff managed in accordance with the Civil Servants Law shall be prescribed by the State Council.

Article 11 Basic old-age insurance implements a combination of social pooling and individual accounts, automobile insurance claims inquiry. The basic old-age insurance fund consists of employer and individual contributions as well as government subsidies, etc.

Article 12 Employers shall pay basic old-age insurance contributions according to the proportion of the total wages of their employees as stipulated by the State, which will be credited to the basic old-age insurance pooled fund. Employees shall pay basic old-age insurance contributions according to the proportion of their own wages as stipulated by the State, which will be credited to individual accounts. Self-employed individuals without employees, part-time workers not participating in basic old-age insurance through an employer, and other flexible employment personnel who participate in basic old-age insurance shall pay basic old-age insurance contributions in accordance with national regulations, which will be separately credited to the basic old-age insurance pooled fund and individual accounts.

Article 13 Before employees of state-owned enterprises and public institutions participate in basic old-age insurance, the basic old-age insurance contributions for the deemed contribution period shall be borne by the government. When the basic old-age insurance fund experiences insufficient payments, the government shall provide subsidies.

Article 14 Individual accounts cannot be withdrawn in advance. The bookkeeping interest rate shall not be lower than the interest rate of bank term deposits, and no interest tax shall be levied. In case of personal death, the balance of the individual account can be inherited.

Article 15 Basic pensions consist of pooled pensions and individual account pensions. Basic pensions are determined based on factors such as accumulated contribution years, contribution wages, average wages of local employees, individual account amounts, and average life expectancy of urban populations.

Article 16 Individuals who participate in basic old-age insurance and have cumulatively contributed for fifteen years upon reaching the statutory retirement age receive monthly basic pensions. Individuals who participate in basic old-age insurance and have cumulatively contributed for less than fifteen years upon reaching the statutory retirement age can continue contributing until they reach fifteen years and receive monthly basic pensions; they can also transfer to new rural social old-age insurance or urban resident social old-age insurance and enjoy corresponding old-age insurance benefits according to State Council regulations.

Article 17 Dependents of individuals participating in basic old-age insurance who die due to illness or non-work-related causes can receive funeral allowances and pensions; those who become completely disabled due to illness or non-work-related causes before reaching the statutory retirement age can receive disability allowances. The required funds are paid from the basic old-age insurance fund.

Article 18 The State establishes a normal adjustment mechanism for basic pensions. Based on increases in average employee wages and price hikes, the State appropriately raises the level of basic old-age insurance benefits.

Article 19 For individuals employed across pooled regions, their basic old-age insurance relationship transfers with them, and contribution years are cumulatively calculated. Upon reaching the statutory retirement age, basic pensions are calculated in segments and paid uniformly. Specific methods are prescribed by the State Council.

Article 20 The State establishes and improves the new rural social old-age insurance system. New rural social old-age insurance implements a combination of individual contributions, collective subsidies, and government subsidies.
