"Hourly husbands" become popular in Moscow

by extra7 on 2008-09-22 16:37:18

On September 19, Russia's Novye Izvestia published an article titled "Hourly Male Workers Provided, But No Sexual Services," with the following summary:

Pavel was once a successful builder who recently opened a company in Moscow called "Hourly Husband." The name may sound ambiguous, but the employees do not provide sexual services. Instead, they offer household services such as fixing faucets and replacing sockets, and the business is thriving. The secretary receives so many calls that her hands are constantly busy.

Pavel said, "I changed careers thanks to my wife's inspiration. Although she is a capable housewife, she often needs my help with tasks like putting up shelves on walls, repairing benches, and cleaning balconies. My construction company was very busy, and I couldn't spare the time, so I suddenly realized that perhaps Moscow really lacks people who can and are willing to give housewives a hand. Not all carpenters would readily agree to go install a set of curtain rods because the pay is low, and it takes too much time."

Housekeeping service companies only appeared in Russia in 2003, which is absolutely a new concept. However, according to a market research company, its annual demand growth is as high as 25% to 30%. Many housewives are alone at home, and when they encounter difficult problems, they used to seek help from neighbors or property management. Unfortunately, the quality of property management services is not satisfactory: long waiting times, lack of effort, not taking off shoes when entering homes, and inability to handle many tasks. Now, housewives no longer need to worry about these issues.

Do not assume that anyone who knows how to work can be hired by a housekeeping company. Pavel's company has quite strict employment conditions: at least a secondary education, with preference given to university graduates; versatile workers are most favored because a single visit usually involves doing several different types of jobs, such as plumbing repairs, painting, and assembling furniture. Of course, the company also regularly provides professional skills training for employees.

The most common service items include installing chandeliers, laying wires, and replacing cables, but there are also larger tasks such as changing wallpapers, loosening the soil in vegetable gardens of villas, and repairing houses. In addition, female customers often call to ask hourly workers to take their beloved pets for a walk, go shopping with them, help select appropriate appliances, or even just chat over tea.

The company has two pricing methods: clearly marked prices based on specific content and service duration. The first method is very convenient: lock installation costs 250-600 rubles (about 25 rubles per dollar), socket replacement costs 150 rubles, which is straightforward. If there is a lot of work that is hard to break down into details, it is usually priced by time, about 500 rubles per hour, with a 10% discount if it exceeds four hours. For major house repairs, customers may hire hourly workers for an entire day or even a week, enjoying more discounts. The full-day fee is approximately 4500 rubles, and for a week, it is around 15,000-20,000 rubles.

Perhaps due to the suggestive nature of the term "hourly husband," the company inevitably received phone calls from lonely women hoping for someone to provide special services when it first opened. In such cases, the receptionist always politely apologizes and patiently explains that the company offers housekeeping services, not sexual services.

Pavel still vividly remembers one incident. Once, a young girl starting her career cried on the phone: her group was hosting a grand costume ball requiring male escorts, but she had none, so she tearfully dialed the number. Pavel quickly picked the most handsome and suave hourly worker, dressed him in a suit, leather shoes, and tie generously lent by a colleague, and sent him with flowers to attend the event gracefully, making the girl laugh through her tears.
