Modified plastics, which are based on general plastics and engineering plastics

by fh67ff4sfhj on 2012-03-03 09:58:41

After being processed by methods such as filling, blending, and reinforcement, the performance of plastic products in terms of flame retardancy, strength, impact resistance, toughness, etc., has been improved. Through modified plastic components, not only can they reach the strength properties of some steel materials, but also have a series of advantages such as being lightweight, colorful, and easy to form. Therefore, currently, the trend of "replacing steel with plastics" is emerging in many industries, and at this stage, it is almost impossible to find a material that can replace plastic products on a large scale.

Modified plastics are a plastic industry field involving a wide range of areas, high technology content, and capable of creating enormous economic benefits. And plastic modification technology—filling, blending, and reinforcement modification—is deeply involved in almost all raw materials and molding processes of plastic products. From the production of raw resin to various specifications and varieties of modified plastic masterbatches, in order to reduce the cost of plastic products and improve their functionality, plastic modification technology is indispensable.

Ordinary plastics often have their own characteristics and defects. Modified plastics change the nature of plastics, and the basic technologies include:

1. Reinforcement: Using glass fiber to alter the strength of plastics.

2. Filling: Adding some minerals to ordinary plastics to change the properties of plastics.

3. Toughening: Adding toughening agents to ordinary plastics so that these plastics can be very tough. The modified product: railway track pads.

4. Flame Retardancy: Adding flame retardants to ordinary plastic resins can give plastics flame-retardant properties. Generally, flame retardants include bromine + antimony system, P system, N system, and inorganic flame retardant systems.

5. Cold Resistance: Generally refers to the cold resistance ability of plastics at low temperatures. Due to the inherent low-temperature brittleness of plastics, plastics become brittle at low temperatures. For example, plastic parts inside cars generally require cold resistance.

Function Examples

Overcoming limitations Overcoming defects such as poor processing performance and insufficient strength

Granting new functions Granting new functions such as flame retardancy, weather resistance, high impact resistance, antibacterial, anti-static, etc.

Reducing costs For example, mineral reinforcement and toughening modification of PP, engineering plastic modification, the effect of reducing costs is more obvious

Modified plastics, with superior cost-performance ratios, are increasingly applied in downstream fields. It can be said that modified plastics have become a consumption trend, and behind this trend lie five factors:

High Performance: Modified plastics not only possess the advantages of traditional plastics, such as low density and corrosion resistance, but their physical and mechanical properties are also greatly improved, such as high strength, high toughness, high impact resistance, wear resistance, shock resistance, etc. Moreover, the improvement in overall plastic performance provides a foundation for its extensive application in downstream fields.

Low Cost: Compared to other materials, plastics benefit from high production efficiency and low density, resulting in lower costs. The cost of plastic per unit volume is roughly one-tenth that of metal.

Government Policies: China's implemented "3C" mandatory certification system strictly regulates the safety performance of products within the directory, thereby promoting the widespread use of flame-retardant plastics in home appliances, IT, telecommunications, and other fields.

Consumption Upgrade: With the improvement in living standards, people are beginning to pursue more outstanding product performance, demanding that home appliances and other products become more aesthetically pleasing, safe, and durable. This places higher requirements on the upstream plastic industry, requiring better processability, mechanical properties, durability, and safety.

Technical Factors: Currently, more than 1000 polymers have been discovered worldwide, but only dozens have practical application value. Developing new polymers involves significant investment and unclear application prospects; conversely, modification technology can not only enhance the performance of existing polymers to meet industrial needs but also reduce the cost of some expensive engineering plastics, becoming an effective way to develop the plastic industry.

The main types of modified plastic products include flame-retardant resins, reinforced toughened resins, plastic alloys, functional color masterbatches, etc.

Flame-retardant resin class mainly includes flame-retardant high-impact polystyrene resin, flame-retardant polypropylene resin, flame-retardant ABS resin, etc. These products are divided into different flame-retardant specifications according to the flame-retardant grade, such as UL94 V0 level, UL94 V1 level, UL94 V2 level, and UL94 HB level. Compared with ordinary plastics, flame-retardant plastic products can significantly reduce the risk of fires caused by short circuits, overloads, water immersion, etc.

The main consumer groups of flame-retardant resin products include television manufacturing enterprises, computer manufacturing enterprises, office electrical appliance (printer, copier, fax machine, etc.) enterprises, lighting enterprises, electrician enterprises, audio factories, etc. They are mainly used to manufacture various product shells, internal parts, peripheral equipment (connectors, distribution boards, plugs), etc.

1) Weather-resistant toughened PP special material. Weather-resistant toughened PP special material is a new type of polypropylene material with engineering plastic characteristics, featuring good low-temperature toughness, small forming shrinkage rate, high rigidity, strong weather resistance, etc., mainly used in outdoor environments that need to withstand climate and ultraviolet rays. Its main consumer groups include home appliance enterprises and automobile parts enterprises, etc.

2) Glass fiber reinforced thermoplastic class. Products of glass fiber reinforced thermoplastics mainly include glass fiber reinforced AS/ABS, glass fiber reinforced PP, glass fiber reinforced nylon, glass fiber reinforced PBT/PET, glass fiber reinforced PC, glass fiber reinforced PPE/PPS, etc. Its main consumer groups include computer accessories enterprises, mechanical parts enterprises, power tool enterprises, lighting enterprises, etc.

Plastic alloy class mainly includes PC alloy products, PVC alloy products, polyester alloy products, etc.

PC alloy products have features such as high impact strength, anti-creep, heat resistance, low water absorption rate, non-toxicity, excellent dielectric properties, etc. They are mainly applied in car instrument panels, computers, and office automation equipment, power tool shells, mobile phones, etc. The main consumer groups of PC alloys include electrician enterprises, computer manufacturing enterprises, office electrical appliance (printer, copier, fax machine, etc.) enterprises, automobile accessory factories, etc.

PVC/ABS alloy is produced using PVC and ABS as the base material, adding toughening agents, lubricants, stabilizers, flame retardants, and other modifiers, possessing excellent mechanical properties, weather resistance, and process rheological properties. The surface gloss of the product is good, and the injection molding and extrusion effects are excellent, making it an extremely cost-effective alloy material. PVC/ABS alloy can replace flame-retardant weather-resistant ABS, PC, etc., widely used in home appliance shells, electrical switches, electricity meter shells, lighting materials, communication networks, building materials, etc.

Polyester alloy possesses excellent mechanical properties (fatigue resistance), dimensional stability, chemical reagent resistance, environmental stress crack resistance, mainly applied in the automotive, home appliance, and power tool fields.

Functional color masterbatch mainly refers to high-impact polystyrene toughened flame-retardant color masterbatch. Mixing this masterbatch with HIPS (high-impact polystyrene resin) at a certain ratio (1:2~1:30) can satisfy the flame-retardant requirements of electrical and electronic products under standards like UL94, IEC-65, and GB8898, while improving the toughness, processing flowability, and demoldability of HIPS resin, giving HIPS resin color, both reducing production costs and enhancing product quality.

The main consumer groups of high-impact polystyrene toughened flame-retardant color masterbatch include television manufacturing enterprises, audio factories, etc., mainly used for manufacturing electronic and electrical product shells.

Fiber reinforcement is an important method for plastic modification. Magnesium salt whiskers and glass fibers can effectively improve the comprehensive performance of polypropylene. Glass fiber-reinforced polypropylene has the advantages of low density, low price, and recyclability, and is gradually replacing engineering plastics and metals in applications such as car dashboards, car bodies, and chassis parts. Compared to glass fibers, magnesium salt whiskers offer higher precision, dimensional stability, and surface finish, suitable for producing various complex-shaped components, light-weight high-strength flame-retardant components, and electronic/electrical components. As a modifier, magnesium salt whiskers can significantly enhance the strength, stiffness, impact resistance, and flame-retardant properties of polypropylene. Therefore, the application of magnesium salt whiskers and glass fibers in polypropylene modification is receiving increasing attention.

Mineral reinforcement and toughening is one of the most common modification paths. The minerals typically added to polypropylene raw materials include calcium carbonate, talc powder, wollastonite, glass beads, mica powder, etc. These minerals not only improve the mechanical properties and impact toughness of polypropylene materials to a certain extent, reduce the forming shrinkage rate of polypropylene materials to enhance their dimensional stability, but also due to the huge cost difference between minerals and polypropylene matrix, can significantly reduce the cost of polypropylene materials.

Mineral-enhanced and toughened polypropylene is the most widely used type of modified polypropylene material in household appliances. Currently, the inner drums of both pulsator washing machines and drum washing machines generally use mineral-enhanced and toughened polypropylene materials to replace early stainless steel inner drums. After mineral enhancement and toughening, polypropylene materials can overcome their original problems of insufficient strength, poor luster, and excessive shrinkage. Currently, this modified polypropylene is not only used to make washing machine inner drums but also used to make agitators and laundry outlets, etc. Haier Group alone consumes about 1700 tons annually (each washing machine inner drum weighs approximately 2kg). The mineral content of this material can reach up to 40%, with tensile strength reaching 33Mpa, elongation at break reaching over 90%, and notched impact strength around 10KJ/m².

Many components of microwave ovens are also made of mineral-enhanced and toughened polypropylene materials. Due to the addition of minerals, the heat resistance of polypropylene materials can be further improved based on their already high heat resistance, adapting to the high-temperature requirements of microwave ovens. For instance, the sealing strips of microwave oven doors, speaker horns, horn brackets, etc., all use this modified polypropylene material. The shelves in refrigerators now basically use mineral-enhanced and toughened polypropylene materials, which, when combined with glass panels for integral injection molding, effectively solve the problem of panel water infiltration originally present in ABS materials.

A new high-filling glass fiber-modified plastic can overcome the defects of conventional glass fiber-reinforced thermoplastics. The matrix material of this material is high-temperature thermoplastics such as liquid crystal polymers, polyether sulfone, polyetherimide, and polyphenylene sulfide. When the glass fiber filling amount reaches 80%, the modified material still maintains good processability. Components made from the new material possess excellent wear and temperature resistance characteristics. This new material can bond with plastics and metals, making it suitable for surface mold processing. Potential applications include automotive and fuel system components, bearings, electronic components, scratch-resistant shells, etc. The additional benefits of glass fiber reinforcement include good flame retardancy, recyclability, high heat resistance, and dimensional stability.

Plastic blending modification refers to a modification method where one or more other resins (including plastics and rubbers) are mixed into a resin to change its original properties. Fluoropolymer alloys use domestically available ultra-high molecular weight ferrocene (FER) as the primary raw material, directly blended with tetrafluoroethylene filler using physical methods, and the material properties exceed those of the globally recognized "king of plastics," polytetrafluoroethylene.

In the current polymer flame retardant technology, brominated flame retardants are primarily used, commonly including decabromodiphenyl ether, octabromodiphenyl ether, tetrabromobisphenol A, hexabromocyclododecane, among which decabromodiphenyl ether has the largest usage. Brominated epoxy resins, due to their excellent melt flow rates, high flame retardant efficiency, superior thermal stability, and photochemical stability, can provide good physical and mechanical properties to flame-retarded materials without blooming, and thus are widely used in PBT, PET, ABS, nylon 66, and other engineering plastics, especially in the flame retardant treatment of PC/ABS plastic alloys.

Other varieties in the flame retardant family include phosphorus-based, triazine-based, silicon-based, intumescent, inorganic-based, etc., each playing unique flame retardant roles in various application fields. In phosphorus-based flame retardants, organic phosphorus compounds are mostly oil-like liquids, making them difficult to add during polymer processing, usually used in polyurethane foams, transformer oils, cellulose resins, natural and synthetic rubbers. Among inorganic phosphorus compounds, red phosphorus is a pure flame-retardant element with good flame retardant performance, but its bright color restricts its application. The application of red phosphorus should focus on micronization and surface coating, which allows it to disperse well in polymers, have good compatibility, not migrate easily, and maintain long-term flame retardant properties.

Researchers found that when particles reach the nanoscale, a strange phenomenon occurs where the periodic boundaries are disrupted, causing significant differences in their acoustic, optical, electrical, magnetic, and thermodynamic properties compared to traditional materials. Based on the structural characteristics of nanomaterials, different materials can be synthesized and combined at the nanoscale to form various nano-composites, such as nano-functional plastics.

Commonly used plastic types include PP (polypropylene), PE (polyethylene), PVC (polyvinyl chloride), ABS (acrylonitrile-butadiene-styrene), PA (polyamide), PC (polycarbonate), PS (polystyrene), and others. To meet special demands in some industries, traditional plastics' characteristics are altered using nanotechnology, presenting excellent physical properties, high strength, heat resistance, and lighter weight. As the use of plastics in automobiles increases, nano-plastics may become widely used in cars. These nano-functional plastics that attract the attention of automotive professionals include flame-retardant plastics, reinforcing plastics, UV-resistant aging plastics, and antibacterial plastics.

Reinforcing plastics involve filling plastics with nanoscale inorganic materials such as montmorillonite, CaCO3, SiO2, etc., which have a pronounced converging effect on the molecular crystallization of polypropylene, allowing for increased tensile strength, impact toughness, and elastic modulus of polypropylene and other plastics, significantly improving the physical properties of plastics. Enhanced and toughened plastics can replace metal materials, as they are light in weight, thus widely used in automobiles to significantly reduce vehicle weight and achieve fuel savings. These nano-modified enhanced and toughened plastics can be used for bumpers, seats, fenders, sunroofs, doors, engine covers, luggage compartment covers, and even transmission housings and gear transmission devices in cars.

Thermoplastic elastomers, abbreviated as TPE/TPR, use SEBS and SBS as the base material and are a class of high molecular alloy materials with general plastic processing properties but similar properties to rubber. In multi-material molding, there are four basic types of thermoplastic elastomers: styrene block copolymers (SBC), thermoplastic vulcanizates (TPV), thermoplastic polyurethanes (TPU), and copolyester elastomers (COPE).

Thermoplastic polyurethane elastomers were the first elastomers that could be processed using thermoplastic techniques. There are two types: polyester and polyether. Polyester types have higher mechanical properties, while polyether types have better hydrolytic stability and low-temperature toughness than polyester types. Polyurethane rubber has good wear resistance, and additives can improve weather resistance, dimensional stability, and heat resistance, reduce friction, or increase flame retardancy. They have a wide range of applications across various hardness grades, involving automotive seals and gaskets, stabilizer bushings, medical catheters, pacemakers, and artificial heart devices, mobile phone antenna gears, pulleys, chain wheels, slide liners, textile machinery components, casters, gaskets, diaphragms, couplings, and vibration damping components.

Copolyester elastomers have good dynamic properties, high modulus, high elongation, and tear strength, and exhibit good flex fatigue resistance at both high and low temperatures. By combining UV stabilizers or carbon black, weather resistance can be improved. They perform well or excellently with non-oxidizing acids, some aliphatic hydrocarbons, aromatic fuel oils, alkaline solutions, hydraulic fluids; however, polar materials such as strong inorganic acids and bases, chlorinated solvents, phenols, and cresols degrade polyesters. Copolyester elastomers are generally more expensive than thermoplastic elastomers and are used in elastic couplings, diaphragms, gears, bellows, gasket rings, protective sleeves, seals, athletic shoe soles, electrical connectors, fasteners, knobs, and bushings.

In 2007, global consumption of thermoplastic elastomers (TPE) exceeded 2.3 million tons, with a total value exceeding $11 billion. Between 2001 and 2007, world consumption maintained an average annual growth rate of 6.5%. North America had an average annual growth rate of 5.7%, Europe 4.4%, Latin America grew rapidly at a double-digit rate, and the Asia-Pacific region had an average annual growth rate greater than 8%. Rapid growth will drive the use of TPEs across various industries, with automobiles and daily consumer goods driving the growth in TPE consumption. Different types of thermoplastic elastomers have different growth rates. Currently, the application of thermoplastic polyurethane is growing at an average annual rate of 6.3%, mainly in the automotive industry. It is expected that future applications of thermoplastic polyurethane in daily consumer goods and sports goods will see breakthroughs.
