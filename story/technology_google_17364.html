Google Overtakes Microsoft to Become the Most Valuable Brand; China Mobile Ranks Fifth

by diglog on 2007-04-24 12:24:33

Beijing, April 24 - According to foreign media reports, based on the rankings published by the market research company Millward Brown for the top 100 most valuable global brands in 2007, Google has replaced Microsoft as the world's most valuable brand. China Mobile ranks fifth. Millward Brown is a division of WPP, the world's second-largest advertising and media group.

The ranking is based on brand value, with Google's current brand value reaching $66.43 billion, far surpassing other brands; General Electric is ranked second with a brand value of $61.88 billion; Microsoft ranks third with a brand value of $54.95 billion; Coca-Cola is fourth with a brand value of $44.13 billion; China Mobile ranks fifth with a brand value of $41.21 billion, the highest among non-American companies. The total brand value of the top 100 global brands in 2007 is $1.6 trillion, an increase of 10.6% from $1.44 trillion in 2006.

Google was ranked seventh last year but this year it has replaced Microsoft as the world's most valuable brand. In fact, Google hasn't invested much money in marketing but mainly relies on "word-of-mouth" promotion, which shows the immense power of the Internet. In contrast, despite the launch of its new operating system Vista, Microsoft's ranking dropped from first place last year to third place. Other major technology companies listed include IBM, Nokia, HP, Apple, Cisco, and Intel. (Otto)
