China National Tobacco Corporation made a net profit of 117.7 billion yuan in 2010, averaging 3.2 billion yuan per day

by king3h0a on 2012-03-07 10:36:27

Industrial Bank (13.91, 0.03, 0.22%) disclosed its targeted additional share issuance plan, revealing that China National Tobacco Corporation's net profit in 2010 was 117.7 billion yuan, surpassing Sinopec and China Mobile.

In a report by Liu Xia, Industrial Bank disclosed a targeted additional share issuance plan yesterday, making public the performance of China National Tobacco Corporation. The data shows that China National Tobacco Corporation's net profit in 2010 was 117.7 billion yuan, earning approximately 3.2 billion yuan per day.

The fundraising ability surpasses Bank of China

According to the announcement from Industrial Bank, its board has agreed to issue 2.0723 billion shares to four specific entities, including PICC Asset Management Co., Ltd. and China National Tobacco Corporation, raising a total of 26.38 billion yuan to supplement Industrial Bank's core capital. Among these, China National Tobacco Corporation invested 5.2 billion yuan to subscribe for 409 million shares.

With this announcement, China National Tobacco Corporation's performance in 2010 was also disclosed. According to the data, in 2010, China National Tobacco Corporation achieved revenue of 770.4 billion yuan and net profit of 117.7 billion yuan. On average, it earned about 3.2 billion yuan per day. According to China Tobacco Online, in 2003, China National Tobacco Corporation achieved industrial and commercial profits of 26.944 billion yuan.

Based on the annual reports of listed companies announced in 2010, China National Tobacco Corporation's total net profit that year surpassed Bank of China (3.01, -0.01, -0.33%), slightly behind Industrial and Commercial Bank of China (4.32, -0.02, -0.46%), PetroChina (10.30, -0.02, -0.19%), and China Construction Bank (4.79, -0.02, -0.42%). If it were listed, its annual profitability would rank fourth.

Accused of [separation of government and enterprise]

It is understood that China National Tobacco Corporation was established in 1982. Currently, its registered capital is 57 billion yuan, total assets are 969.9 billion yuan, and shareholder rights are 803.2 billion yuan. The business scope of China National Tobacco Corporation includes the production, operation, import and export trade of tobacco monopoly products, as well as the operation and management of state-owned assets.

Yesterday, upon checking the list of central enterprises on the official website of the State-owned Assets Supervision and Administration Commission, it was found that China National Tobacco Corporation is not among the central enterprises. It is understood that China National Tobacco Corporation is guided by the State Tobacco Monopoly Administration, which was established in 1984. The current general manager of China National Tobacco Corporation and the director of the State Tobacco Monopoly Administration and party secretary is Jiang Chengkang.

Previously, the tobacco industry was accused of [separation of government and enterprise]. National People's Congress representative Ma Li pointed out that the tobacco monopoly bureau and China National Tobacco Corporation are one set of people with two different names. [On the one hand, they need to increase production, increase profits, and increase taxes; on the other hand, they need to increase sales. These are two different and contradictory goals.]

The tobacco industry in our country belongs to the [high-tax industry]. In 2011, the national tobacco industry completed tax and profit of 752.956 billion yuan, an increase of 22.5% year-on-year. It is reported that in the [Top 100 Taxpaying Enterprises List] announced in Guangzhou, Guangzhou Tobacco Group ranked first.

■ Magnifying Glass

Profitable "China" prefixed enterprises

Not just China National Tobacco, more "China" prefixed enterprises attract market attention due to their strong [profitability]. Based on the State-owned Assets Supervision and Administration Commission's announcement of the "Central Enterprises' 2010 Annual Individual State-owned Capital Operation Status Table", in the 2010 "scorecard" of central enterprises, only six enterprises made a loss. Among them, PetroChina, with a net profit of 124.18 billion yuan, became the "most profitable central enterprise".

PetroChina, at the top, achieved operating revenue of 1.4654 trillion yuan in 2010, an increase of 43.8% year-on-year; net profit attributable to shareholders of the company was 139.99 billion yuan, an increase of 35.4% year-on-year. This is also the third highest record in PetroChina's history since its listing, ranking only below 2007 and 2006.

Following closely are several major giants: China Mobile (Weibo) 97.27 billion yuan (net profit, hereinafter the same), CNOOC 74.23 billion yuan, Sinopec 72.12 billion yuan, and Shenhua Group 46.86 billion yuan.

If the above net profits are averaged daily, these five can be said to make money every day. PetroChina, China Mobile, CNOOC, Sinopec, and Shenhua Group can earn net profits of 340 million yuan, 266 million yuan, 203 million yuan, 198 million yuan, and 128 million yuan per day respectively.
