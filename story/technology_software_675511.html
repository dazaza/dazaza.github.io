The openness and upgradability of systems are widely concerned by customers. Starting from this year, the openness and upgradability of systems have started to attract the attention of a wide range of customers. Can more devices be connected to achieve desired functions? Can it be compatible with every product in the market? Will upcoming electronic products be recognized by smart home systems? These are questions that customers often ask. Additionally, all customers do not want their costly smart home systems to be replaced every year like mobile phones. Therefore, the openness and upgradability of systems have become another focus for all suppliers in 2012. It takes certain strength to make their smart home system possess the same integration capability as large brands to integrate tens of thousands of products. Upgradability is a major challenge for bus systems realized through comprehensive wiring. Wireless solutions have inherent advantages in upgradability, as they can directly add corresponding devices without destroying the decoration, easily realizing extended functions. Moreover, remote automatic upgrades of system software like Wulian's is also a future trend, where the system and equipment can upgrade themselves to the latest functions without customer and dealer management. The convenience of use after system crashes has been included in the design. Everyone should pay attention to an issue: what if our smart home system breaks down or crashes? Many comprehensive wiring solutions route all the strong electric wires of the lamps to the integrated electric box and control them through relays. The switch panels actually control the weak electric wires that control the relays. Once the relay fails or there is a problem with the line, the lights cannot be turned on, and the customer can only spend a night in the dark or take a flashlight to the electrical room to try their luck. This problem will be completely solved in 2012. A new solution has already emerged, and the impact caused by such system failures will be minimized. For example, most of the switches of smart home systems currently available in the market can operate independently without the host. Even if the system crashes, they can still function as regular switches!
