360's search share reaches 20%, and Baidu's "monopoly" has become a thing of the past.

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 20:38:33

Last November, when talking about the expected market share of 360 Search, Zhou Hongyi said that:

"Overall, the result we hope to achieve is: in the PC market, we hope to gain a reasonable share based on our traffic and browser coverage... around 15-20%. Once this share is reached, the monopoly will be broken. The monopolist may also face improvement."

According to Sina Tech's report, Yu Guangdong, vice president of 360 responsible for search business, revealed to the public yesterday that:

"According to CNZZ data, the market share of 360 Search had already surpassed the 20% mark in October this year, reaching as high as 22.85% at its peak, achieving the goal ahead of schedule. Therefore, the company has raised the target share before the end of the year to 25%."

Based on Zhou Hongyi's criteria, Baidu's monopolistic position has already been broken.

360 Search was launched on August 16 last year. It only took 6 days to capture 10% of China’s search market. By June this year, its market share had risen to 16.58%. And by August 16 this year, the first anniversary of 360 Search's birth, its market share further increased to 17.6%, now breaking through the 20% mark.

On the other hand, Baidu's market share has fallen from 67% in April this year to around 60% currently. Meanwhile, Sogou's share has remained largely unchanged, so it can be concluded that 360 Search has largely taken away Baidu's market share.

Although 360 Search still has a gap compared to Baidu in terms of strength, the former's competitive strategy is very clear, could be described as "every move targeting the opponent, every hit effective."

Since its launch, 360 Search has consistently used "safety" as its selling point to attract users. When launching image search in December last year, Qi Xiangdong, President of 360, said: "Using 360 Image Search to find images has no ad information interference, and the search results have blocked a large number of Trojan websites that lure netizens to visit using images."

Subsequently, 360 Search announced full access to the drug query data of the National Food and Drug Administration and participated in the operation of Jike Search's online exposure platform and food safety section. This move directly targeted the widely criticized false medical advertisements of competitors.

In addition to having a clear and effective competitive strategy, 360 Search also fully utilized its own channel resources such as browsers and security products, and also attempted to occupy the user's desktop entry point —— in August, it launched the "Ctrl+Ctrl" function to bring up the search box, shortening the search path. Although this was a feature Google once introduced, after exiting China, Google's influence in this market has significantly weakened.

Of course, the difficulties faced by 360 Search cannot be ignored. The long-term cooperative relationship between Baidu and hundreds of thousands of advertising agents remains a major barrier to the commercialization of 360 Search.

Yu Guangdong admitted frankly that the ARPU value (average revenue per user) of 360 Search is only one-tenth of Baidu's. Moreover, competitors require partners and even advertising agents to choose between them. If someone cooperates with 360, the competitor immediately stops cooperating with them. This has caused significant difficulties for 360 in expanding provincial and municipal agents.

From 360's product strategy, we can always see the company's precise grasp of competitors' weaknesses and deep understanding of ordinary consumers' needs, thereby achieving success against greater odds or even surpassing later.

Recently, 360 launched its first wearable device - the "360 Children's Guardian" bracelet. This bracelet has functions such as positioning, warning, remote monitoring, and recording. Regardless of the actual performance and final sales volume of the 360 Children's Guardian, given the frequent occurrence of child safety issues today, such products undoubtedly hit the pain points of many parents.

Qi Xiangdong, President of 360, once stated that the target market share of 360 Search in 2014 is 30%. So how will Baidu counter?

For the frequently contentious, "gossip"-laden, and noisy Chinese Internet industry, I prefer to see the rise of multiple players.
