After rain, problems such as engine not starting, easy to stall when passing through water-logged areas, wheel slippage, uncomfortable damp seats, and malfunctioning audio systems may occur... Here are the solutions:

First: Chassis care

The chassis is the part closest to the ground and is particularly affected by road conditions. This part is usually the most prone to rust, and there might even be loose or perforated wheel wells. Therefore, it is essential to pay attention to the cleaning and rust prevention treatment of the car's chassis.

Chassis rust prevention treatment should be done at a professional car beauty center. First, thoroughly clean the chassis; computerized car washing equipment has a chassis cleaning function where high-pressure water jets can wash away mud and sand from the chassis. If there is too much oil residue, clean it again with a degreaser to expose the original color of the chassis.

Then, lift the car onto a lift for chassis drying and wiping, followed by rust prevention treatment by professionals. During the care process, ensure an even spray coating layer to form a long-lasting uniform rust prevention coating. After the entire process is completed, let it air dry naturally for 5-10 minutes. The chassis rust prevention treatment is then considered complete. The whole process takes about 30-40 minutes depending on the condition of the car. One chassis rust prevention treatment can last for up to 3 months. After the rust prevention treatment, avoid using alkaline cleaning agents (such as laundry powder, dishwashing liquid) to wash the body and chassis, as this will affect the rust prevention effect and shorten the rust prevention time.

Second: Timely cleaning

When encountering heavy rain, the car should be cleaned promptly after returning. Otherwise, mud can accumulate in the lower gaps of the vehicle, which becomes a place where moisture hides and is the easiest place for rust to occur.

Third: Paint surface beautification

The acidic components in rain have a very strong corrosive effect on the car's paint surface, and over time, they can cause damage to the car's paint surface. Therefore, during seasons with more rain, it's best to give your car a paint surface beautification treatment. The simplest method is waxing, while a more long-lasting and effective one is glazing beautification. No matter which method is used, it gives your car an invisible protective outer layer, preventing paint fading and aging, keeping its beautiful appearance always with you.

Fourth: Ignition start

On rainy days, car owners often encounter the problem of the engine being hard to start. Sometimes, even if it starts勉强starts reluctantly, it still feels weak. The biggest possibility for this issue is leakage caused by dampness in the ignition system. Additionally, when the weather cools down during rain, the hot air sealed inside the distributor could condense into water droplets, causing a small flood inside the distributor and thus losing its normal ignition function.

If it is found that the dampness in the ignition system causes poor ignition and reduced engine performance, it’s best to first use dry paper towels or dry cloths to wipe the inside and outside of the distributor and wires dry. Then use a dedicated drying agent spray can to spray the drying agent on the distributor cap, battery terminals, spark plugs, high-voltage wires, and ignition coils. After waiting for a while, the engine can be started.
