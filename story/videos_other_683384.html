Doctor holding gauze chases elderly man who was injured after drinking in the downtown area like running a race

by anonymous on 2012-02-29 14:07:35

Our reporter (Zhou Wenjie) - In front, there was an old man with continuously bleeding from the back of his head, running hard; behind, there was a doctor in a white coat holding up white gauze, repeatedly shouting "Stop quickly, stop". Yesterday, onlookers on Yangjiaping Pedestrian Street looked at these two people chasing each other and were puzzled: "What's going on? What exactly happened?"

It turns out that the old man had drunk too much and fell, breaking the back of his head. A kind-hearted citizen called 120 to get a doctor to help bandage his wound, but instead, he started racing the doctor on the pedestrian street.

At around 1 PM, there was a sudden "thud" sound at the Unity Intersection of Yangjiaping Pedestrian Street. An old man fell straight to the ground, his whole body convulsing for a moment before he silently got up again. Blood was streaming from the back of his head.

"What happened, Grandpa? Do you need help?" someone asked as they approached. The old man brushed off the dust on his clothes, glanced around with blurred eyes, and replied drunkenly, "No, no," then tried to leave. The owner of a nearby beverage stall was worried and dialed 120 while muttering to himself, "He was already staggering when he was walking earlier. Now that he has fallen and hurt his head, he might not even be able to find his way home."

A few minutes later, the ambulance from Jiulongpo First People's Hospital arrived. Upon seeing this, the old man started walking faster. The doctor holding the gauze quickly chased after him, only to see the old man covering his wound and start running. The doctor followed closely behind, shouting repeatedly, "Stop quickly." For a moment, more and more onlookers gathered together, asking, "Why are these two like they're in a race?"

The reporter on the scene saw that after running about 100 meters, the old man stopped under everyone's persuasion. The doctor quickly picked up the gauze and helped him with a simple bandage. Throughout the process, the old man refused to talk. After the wound was cleaned and bandaged, he left the scene alone.
