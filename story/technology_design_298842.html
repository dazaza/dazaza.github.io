Index futures stimulate blue-chip market conditions

by 51hicsb on 2009-12-08 12:00:12

The recent discussions about stock index futures and margin trading are not groundless. These two reforms were included in the securities industry reform plan in 2009. After the successful opening of the Growth Enterprise Market (GEM), it naturally leads people to associate with the stock index futures and margin trading that were mentioned alongside it at the time. The stock market always predicts new trends and new reforms ahead of time, especially under the current market conditions, the efforts to prepare for stock index futures may ignite a wave of blue chip stock rally.

The stock index futures product that has been tested for over three years is the CSI 300 Index, which almost includes all the blue chip stocks listed on the Shanghai and Shenzhen stock markets. Therefore, it is reasonable to speculate on blue chip stocks using the concept of stock index futures. Moreover, recently, thematic stocks and conceptual stocks have already gone through a round of speculation, and investors who have profited considerably are now looking for new "value lowlands". Thus, the relatively low valuation of blue chip stocks easily attracts funds. At this point, whether it is the reform of stock index futures or margin trading, both may become the rallying call for a blue chip stock rally.

The blue chip stock rally also has its own value-driven force within the market, one could say it's ready to burst forth. Under the premise that the macro policy tone remains unchanged in 2010, three major investment themes may lead the value investment of next year: accelerated urbanization, asset bubble formation, and population aging. In addition, with the confirmation of the economic recovery trend, the performance growth of listed companies will accelerate, and it is expected that there will be an overall performance growth of 25%-30% in 2010. If we take the core fluctuation range of the Shanghai Composite Index in the second half of 2009, 2700-3300 points, as the benchmark, an increase of 25% would result in a range of 3375-4125 points. This is the fundamental support for a blue chip stock rally. In other words, even without the leading role of the introduction of stock index futures, the blue chip stock rally is already in the upward trend of the index. From the perspective of the three major investment themes in 2010, under the trend of accelerated urbanization, industries that directly benefit include financial real estate and mass consumption; under the support of loose monetary policy, industries that directly benefit include financial securities, real estate, coal, and other resource-related industries; under the trend of the arrival of the aging era, industries that directly benefit include insurance, pharmaceuticals, and related industries. By mapping these concepts onto the listed companies in the Shanghai and Shenzhen stock markets, the industries that directly benefit cover 60%-70% of the total market capitalization, and almost all the weight-bearing stocks in the Shanghai and Shenzhen stock markets are in the aforementioned beneficiary industries. Therefore, a blue chip stock rally is imminent, either bursting forth under the impetus of reform or slowly forming in the rotation of sectors.

The question lies in: after the financial tsunami has proven the catastrophic consequences brought by high-leverage derivative financial products, should China still approve the stock index futures and margin trading business? Perhaps some people are glad about the lag in China's financial reform, which relatively reduced the loss of financial wealth evaporation. However, the financial tsunami not only proved the power of financial innovation products to the whole world but also showed that under effective risk management, innovative financial tools give us more choices, thereby allowing the capital market to reach higher levels and providing investors with richer financial wealth management products. If the leverage rate of bank finance is considered conventional weapons, then the high-leverage tools of securities finance are heavy weapons, capable of equipping modern mobile forces for unconventional warfare. In the military industry, people do not abandon because planes and missiles have suffered failures in experiments; similarly, in the financial industry, we will not abandon the innovation of financial tools due to the rampant harm caused by the financial tsunami.

Why should stock index futures and margin trading be launched in 2010? It was once said that 2009 was the first year of innovation for China's stock market, completing two major tasks: one was the basic completion of share structure reform, and the other was the successful launch of the highly anticipated GEM market. After the formation of a fully tradable securities market, the further reform direction of China's securities market is multi-level, from the main board to the SME board, and then to the GEM, which is a multi-level sequence, position risk assessment; from spot trading to margin trading, and then to stock index futures, this is another multi-level sequence. Before the completion of the share structure reform, initiating margin trading and stock index futures transactions in China's stock market would always carry some special uncertainties, but in a fully tradable securities market, such concerns disappear. We can learn from the experiences and lessons of many years of practice by our predecessors and steadily move towards a multi-functional and multi-level securities market.

In 2010, if China's stock market can successfully stand at the height of 4000 points on the Shanghai Composite Index, the total free-floating market capitalization will exceed 30 trillion RMB, approaching or exceeding 4.5 trillion US dollars, which means that China will genuinely enter the top five global securities markets. The expansion of the market calls for deeper reforms, and the economic recovery supports the market expansion. Facing this historic opportunity, reforms should not stop. The market supports reforms, and if preparing for stock index futures can ignite this round of blue chip stock rally, China's stock market will hopefully challenge the height of 4000 points on the Shanghai Composite Index in the first half of next year.
