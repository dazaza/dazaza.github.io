"Stars Change" - Car owner sues two departments of Guangdong for approving Zhuhai's over-due collection of bridge and road tolls.

by sznstejx3q3 on 2012-03-07 09:16:52

The reason is that they approved the collection of bridge and road tolls in Zhuhai "beyond the deadline".

In Zhuhai, recently Zhou Runfan and Sun Nong, two car owners, filed a lawsuit against the provincial Department of Communications and the Provincial Price Bureau. The reason is that all 11 items currently included in Zhuhai's annual ticket levy have exceeded the 15-year charging limit stipulated by relevant national laws. It is reported that this case has been accepted by the Guangzhou Intermediate People's Court. Yesterday, the person in charge of the Zhuhai Bridge and Road Management Office said that the Zhuhai annual ticket is a comprehensive fee. Although the previous 11 items have expired, there have been new projects built in recent years. Zhuhai does not charge for a specific project, so there is no issue of overcharging.

Zhuhai lawyers believe that the approval document infringes on the interests of car owners. According to reports, as early as January 22, 2010, Sun Nong, a lawyer from Zhuhai, sent a letter to the Zhuhai Bridge and Road Management Office, requesting the disclosure of government-related information on the tolls for roads, bridges, and tunnels in Zhuhai. On March 4 of the same year, the Zhuhai Bridge and Road Management Office issued him a "Reply Letter Regarding the Application for Disclosure of Relevant Information on Zhuhai Bridge and Road Toll Fees" (referred to as the reply). Sun Nong said that according to the content of the reply, he learned that the charges for the 11 road, bridge, and tunnel projects included in Zhuhai's annual ticket system started in 1994 and had reached the 15-year limit by 2009.

On December 31, 2011, the Provincial Department of Communications and the Provincial Price Bureau approved the "Yuejia Letter [2011] No. 1365", allowing Zhuhai to continue charging fees, agreeing to continue the trial implementation of the vehicle toll annual ticket system until December 31, 2012. It is understood that the reply still includes the bridge and road projects that Sun Nong believed had already exceeded the 15-year limit.

Sun Nong then coordinated with his friend, Zhuhai car owner Zhou Runfan, to file an administrative lawsuit with the Guangzhou Intermediate People's Court, accusing the Guangdong Provincial Price Bureau and the Department of Communications of approving the continued collection of bridge and road tolls from car owners in Zhuhai in 2012. According to Sun Nong's introduction, Zhuhai began trial collection of bridge and road tolls in 1994, including a total of 11 projects under the Zhuhai annual ticket system (of which the Banzhang Mountain Tunnel abolished charges in 2011).

According to relevant legal provisions, the maximum charging period for government repayment highways shall not exceed 15 years. Therefore, Zhuhai's aforementioned charging projects should have ended by the end of 2009. However, based on the joint reply from the Provincial Department of Communications and the Provincial Price Bureau, Zhuhai has continued to collect bridge and road tolls according to the annual ticket system from 2010 to the present. Sun Nong believes that these project charges have exceeded the 15-year term stipulated by the state, which is both unreasonable and illegal.

Sun Nong believes that the documents from the two departments have become the basis for the Zhuhai Bridge and Road Management Office to illegally collect vehicle tolls for the year 2012, seriously infringing on the vital interests of about 300,000 car owners in Zhuhai, including the plaintiffs.
