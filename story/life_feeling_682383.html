Love in the Coffee Shop

A friend once told her that coffee is the companion of loneliness.

She shook her head with a smile and said, "My coffee is related to longing, not loneliness."

When a story begins, it also sows the seeds of its ending. But from start to finish, it might be a long and wonderful journey, or perhaps a brief and painful farewell.

Pushing open the door of the "Mood" coffee shop, she saw him sitting by the window. His eyes held an indescribable look, one that was hard to fathom. Seeing her, he smiled and waved. She walked over and sat across from him. There was already a cup of coffee on her seat. Across from her, there were two cups. A sense of unease began to spread within her.

Sitting face-to-face, she looked at him intently and steadfastly. He avoided her gaze, glancing around nervously, as if seeking help. Finally, he smiled and met her eyes. In her eyes, he saw a selfish version of himself; in his eyes, she saw a questioning version of herself. In just a few minutes, his change left her confused. A girl sat beside him, and with her presence, he seemed more resolute. Eventually, she let down all her defenses and listened to his stories that had nothing to do with either her or him.

...

This was a clichéd plot twist straight out of a TV drama, and she was merely a side note, accompanying slightly melancholic music as she passed through his life, only to become a passerby.

The girl named Qi, the long-haired girl, the one who walked out with him, would begin a new journey with him. And she, finally, became just a passerby in his world. But even that wasn't allowed. He would leave, taking everything with him, except for the memories they shared. He handed her a box. It was the birthday gift she had given him, a puppet playing the violin. When he first received this gift, he was so excited, but today he returned it with such firmness. He said, "Since I've chosen to start with Qi, I must also choose to end with you." Without giving her a chance to react, he took the girl named Qi and left the coffee shop without looking back.

Seated in the coffee shop, she helplessly gazed out the glass window, feeling lonelier than ever. Finally, she mustered the courage to unwrap the gift she had once given him, only to unexpectedly find a note inside — "Our beginning was a mistake. By the time we both see clearly, it will be our end. Without my protection, you must walk your own path. Remember, you must forget the past." She thought to herself, "Why isn't even leaving as decisive as it should be, yet earlier he was so resolute? Why, this time, seeing your back evokes a feeling of reluctance?"

For countless days, she couldn't forget; for countless nights, she woke up startled from dreams. His shadow couldn't be erased, his traces couldn't be wiped away.

In the time machine of her mind, she kept searching for his silhouette, but could never grasp those marks that belonged to them both.

Thus, life played another joke on her. She saw Qi walking down the street with a man's arm, hoping to catch a glimpse of that familiar figure, hoping to see those bold eyebrows again. But it wasn't as beautiful as she imagined. Instead, she saw a stranger's face.

Qi told her that he was merely her cousin. After seeing her, he went to the U.S. for surgery. If successful, he would return; if not, he wouldn't want her to feel any regret.

The world crumbled right then. That single sentence was enough to devastate her. So she left silently, without a word.

Thus, she lived her life, forgetting the past because that's what he wanted her to do.

She got into the habit of stopping by "Mood" after work for a cup of coffee, but no matter how many she tried, none tasted like the one he had ordered that day. The coffee descriptions on the menu spoke of abandonment, of forgetting, of memory... Yet every time she saw the word "longing," her heart ached. Having tasted them all, she finally mustered the courage to order a cup of longing...

As she waited for the server to bring her coffee, she looked outside through the glass window, seemingly spotting a familiar figure. The server interrupted her distant gaze, placing the coffee gently before her. A sense of familiarity washed over her as she took a small sip, bringing back the feelings from that day...

The door of "Mood" opened, and she turned her head, stunned. Smiling, she sipped her coffee, knowing now that this was the taste of longing.
