What are the symptoms of breast lobular hyperplasia?

by jydw2010 on 2012-02-02 16:45:40

Lobular hyperplasia of the breast is the most common non-tumorous and non-inflammatory proliferative lesion among breast hyperplasia diseases. It accounts for about 70% of all breast diseases, and can occur in women of any age after puberty. So, what are the symptoms of lobular hyperplasia of the breast? To answer this question, we invited Dr. Liu, an expert from the Breast Department of Sanqin Hospital in Xi'an.

[The general symptoms of lobular hyperplasia of the breast are as follows:]

1. Breast distension: Common in women in their twenties, with breast distension being the main symptom, which becomes more obvious before menstruation, and often occurs on the upper outer side of both breasts.

2. Breast nodules: Common in women around 30 years old, with sometimes multiple nodules present in the breast, feeling like touching mung beans at the bottom of a bowl during examination. Sometimes the hyperplasia is significant, especially before menstruation, often showing localized thickening, making it difficult to distinguish from tumors.

3. Cystic masses: Often accompanied by distension, dull pain, or burning sensation. Experts from Kunru Mary Obstetrics and Gynecology Hospital in Qingdao stated that the masses of lobular hyperplasia of the breast are usually clearly defined cystic masses upon palpation, sometimes with multiple cysts clustered together, making it hard to differentiate from cancerous lumps during examination.

[What causes lobular hyperplasia of the breast?]

There are many causes of lobular hyperplasia of the breast, but they are mainly closely related to endocrine disorders or emotional states. The breast of women in childbearing age is controlled by ovarian endocrine. Once ovarian function is affected by certain factors, such as unstable emotions, mental discomfort, excessive fatigue, disharmonious sexual life, changes in living environment, overconsumption of hormone-containing health supplements, or long-term use of hormone-containing cosmetics, etc., these can all lead to imbalance or irregular secretion of estrogen and progesterone in the body, causing breast tissue hyperplasia.
