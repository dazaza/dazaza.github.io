Leadership Is Not About Borrowed Power

Some people often equate their positions, or the power bestowed upon them by a company or society, with leadership. They wield great influence while in these roles, believing themselves to be all-powerful. However, once they step down from their position, they find themselves suddenly lacking followers, left in isolation. One must approach their position and power with clarity, never mistaking this authority and status for personal charm or leadership ability. Leadership is the capacity and influence to still have followers even when you have nothing but belief.

To gain followers, one needs to earn the trust of others, which isn't as difficult as it seems. To do so requires demonstrating capability, especially the ability to guide or mentor others. You must care about your team members and be able to supervise them effectively, ensuring that your subordinates can gain knowledge under your leadership, develop healthily, see their talents put to use, and feel their value recognized. Only such leaders can become respected and beloved figures.

Otherwise, even if someone holds a position today, if they fail to make good use of this platform to develop themselves, they will ultimately remain mere puppets.

I hope our leaders can become true leaders with real leadership qualities, not just individuals wielding borrowed power. Let's strive for this together!

Xu Shuguang, GreenTree Hospitality Group
