Bayern Munich, which won at home against Schalke last weekend, seems to have recovered from the fluctuations in form after the winter break. Even Huntelaar, Schalke's main striker, had to admit helplessly, "The Bundesliga has returned to the situation of Bayern and Dortmund competing for dominance." At this critical time for the championship race, Bayern Munich received some good news: team vice-captain Bastian Schweinsteiger could return to the training ground as early as next week to participate in team training and matches.

After about half a month of rest, Schweinsteiger's injury recovery went well. According to Dr. Wolfaart, Bayern's team doctor, the vice-captain could return to the training field as early as next week. In an interview, Wolfaart said: "Schweinsteiger's recovery is going well. We believe he can return to the team next week. Currently, from a medical perspective, he has recovered. All the rehabilitation plans we have developed for him have been completed."

Schweinsteiger's return to the training field next week means a lot for Bayern. Although the vice-captain will continue to be absent for this weekend's away match against Leverkusen, if Schweinsteiger can return next week, it will be good news for the team, which is at a disadvantage in the Champions League. Two weeks later, Bayern will face Basel at home. Although the team lost 0-1 in the first leg, with the assistance of the vice-captain, Bayern is sure to find a way to advance.

(Sohu Sports Han Tan Leng Yue)

On February 9, during Bayern Munich's away game against Stuttgart in the German Cup, Schweinsteiger fell while competing with opposing defender Niedermeier, injuring his right ankle and leaving the field early. On the bus returning to Munich after the game, Schweinsteiger's ankle was already in a thick cast. Later, according to Bayern's team doctor's examination, the vice-captain's outer side of the right ankle was injured, but Bayern could not provide an exact recovery time at that time.
