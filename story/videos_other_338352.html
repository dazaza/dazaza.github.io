The city of Changzhou was built along the river and thrived because of the water. The canal water flows through the city, winding around it. After more than two thousand years of沉淀, the ancient canal of Changzhou has formed unique characteristics and cultural features of the city: white-washed walls and black-tiled roofs, arched bridges over flowing streams, scholarly families, and riverside households. With the inclusion of Changzhou's Grand Canal cultural experience journey in the World Expo, the tourism department of Changzhou has just launched a route for ancient canal cultural tours, including attractions such as Tianning Temple, Hongmei Park, Canal No. 5, Ge Xiaoxing Cigarette Label Museum, Dongpo Park, ancient canal boat tours, and Changzhou Comb Factory. The month of March, with its blooming flowers, is the best time to take a boat tour of the ancient canal. As spring warms up and flowers bloom, why not stroll hand-in-hand with your family along the canal and enjoy the bright spring scenery on both sides.

The ancient canal boat tour starts west at Biji Alley and ends east at Dongpo Park. Along the way, you can visit attractions such as the Big Wharf, Piling Post, Biji Alley, Wenheng Bridge, Xiyinli, Ming Dynasty City Wall, Qingguo Lane, Front and Back Beian, Tianning Pagoda, Tianning Temple, Hongmei Park, Dongpo Park, and others. From Guanhe Mouth through Xizhi Street to Haid Bridge, the old houses and buildings along the canal are relatively well-preserved, with white-washed walls, black-tiled roofs, and stone docks. East of Haid Bridge lies the famous Biji Alley. Within the alley are attractions such as the Big Wharf, Piling Post Stele, Comb Tower, "Biliang Lanterns", Ouyuan, the grand archway, Near Water Pavilion, Moon Piercing Tower, Wenheng Bridge, and other historical sites. "Biliang Lanterns" was one of the eight famous scenes of the western suburbs of Changzhou in ancient times. Back then, the glittering reflections of lanterns were mirrored in the canal water, blending with the lights on the boats by the shore, resembling golden dragons. Now, you can still vaguely see the four large characters "Biliang Lanterns" on the double-eaved archway. Beyond Biji Alley, the canal splits into the inner and outer southern branches. The inner branch runs alongside the ancient cultural area of Xiyinli, past Qingguo Lane to the east, merging with the northern inner canal at Yanling Road, and finally joining the outer branch at Dongpo Park. Visitors can enjoy the beautiful scenery and historical sites of Changzhou while listening to the soft Wu dialect, appreciating Jiangnan silk and bamboo music, tasting Longcheng delicacies, and enjoying the historical appearance and urban landscape of the "famous capital of eight counties" on the ancient canal with its green willows hanging from the banks, experiencing the fusion of history and reality.

As early as the 1980s, Changzhou, together with Wuxi and Suzhou, launched ancient canal tours that attracted countless domestic and foreign tourists, with multiple tour groups being received each day. At that time, the canal journey was called the "Emperor's Journey."

With the opening of the new canal, the main function of the ancient canal has shifted from "freight transportation" to "tourism and sightseeing." The launch of the ancient canal boat tour fully presents the beautiful scenery of the ancient canal to the citizens.

Changzhou's ancient canal boat tour is different from those in other cities. The ancient canal of Changzhou is one of the few canals in the country that passes through the city center. While many cities' canal tours emphasize the lighting and greenery on both banks, Changzhou highlights the culture on both sides of the canal.

The ancient canal of Changzhou forms the "Turtle City Dragon Shape". In the past, the rise of merchants, grain transport, wood industry, rice industry, bean industry, fruit industry, tea industry, and ethnic commerce along the canal contributed to the brilliance of Changzhou's economy. The unique cultural phenomena of Changzhou arose, developed, and distributed along the canal banks. Changzhou is known as the "Dragon City," and the canal water encircling the city forms the structure of the "Turtle City Dragon Shape." Attractions like "Dragon City Academy," "Six Dragons Entering the County City," industrial economy "Six Dragons," "Chinese Dinosaur Park," and roads, bridges, rivers, alleys, ports, and ships named after dragons give "Dragon City" deeper meaning. Tianning Temple, built on the edge of the canal during the Tang Zhen Guan period, has survived thousands of years of destruction and rebuilding to become the "First Forest of Jiangnan," forming the unique religious culture of Changzhou where "a pure land exists within the bustling city."
