Arsene Wenger's Rage Sparks Gunners' Comeback: Wenger: "Now we're not short of forwards"

by yinding999 on 2009-12-15 18:35:16

Arsenal's Gentleman Coach Explodes, Spurs Gunners' Comeback - Wenger: Now We Don't Lack Forwards

By reporter Chuan Liu in London - After securing their first win at Anfield in six years, Arsenal captain Cesc Fabregas revealed the key to the team's second-half turnaround, with two goals against Liverpool, was an outburst by manager Arsène Wenger: "How did we manage the comeback? To be honest, it was because the boss woke us up in the dressing room at halftime. He was like a hairdryer, and we've never seen him that angry before. He was very disappointed with our first-half performance and said we didn’t deserve to wear the Arsenal jersey. He was right; we played terribly in the first half. Luckily, we quickly got back on track in the second."

Discussing the incident of his outburst, Wenger stated: "Yes, I was very dissatisfied with the first half. Liverpool controlled the game situation for the first 45 minutes, and being one goal behind should have been considered lucky because they could have led by two goals at halftime, which would have ended the match early. So, we needed to perform at our normal level in the second half. The result was that the first half belonged to Liverpool, while the second half belonged to us. Fortunately, we scored one more goal than them. Want to know how angry I was during halftime in the dressing room? I don't want to talk about it now because it's an internal secret of our dressing room. The reason for our poor performance in the first half wasn't due to a lack of desire to win among the players but rather the young players were burdened with the common fear of losing in big matches. Thankfully, we managed to overcome it in the second half."

Regarding media inquiries about purchasing a striker during the winter transfer window, the professor said, "You can count the total number of goals we've scored this season (40 goals in 15 Premier League games, 17 goals in 8 Champions League games, 4 goals in 3 EFL Cup games, 61 goals in 26 games across all competitions), and Arshavin could have scored two or three goals today. Therefore, I think we don't lack forwards."

After securing their first victory in the top-four mini-league this season against Liverpool, Arsenal has reduced the gap with league leaders Chelsea to just six points. If they win their postponed second-round home game against Bolton, they will only trail Chelsea by three points and be level with Manchester United, which would greatly benefit their chances of reversing the title race. However, regarding the renewed hope, Fabregas reacted calmly, "It's still too early to talk about the league championship. The competition for the league title has never been as open as this season. You can beat any team, and you can also lose to any team at any time. I actually like this feeling." Talking about Arshavin's goal, Fabregas said, "Arshavin's goal was unbelievable, but we weren't too surprised because he does this every day on the training ground."
