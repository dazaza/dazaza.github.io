Twitter CFO Meets with Investment Banks, Assembles Professional Team for IPO Preparation

by anonymous on 2013-09-04 15:52:30

Insiders revealed on Tuesday that Mike Gupta, who has been the Chief Financial Officer of microblogging service Twitter for almost a year, has begun to reach out to investment banks to discuss the company's initial public offering (IPO). This signals that the most anticipated IPO since Facebook's is on the horizon, with Gupta set to play a key role.

Insiders disclosed that Twitter has started preliminary discussions with investment banks regarding its IPO. The company will decide whether to file an IPO prospectus this year or next based on its financial data from the third quarter. Gupta joined Twitter last year and will participate in the final IPO roadshow alongside the company's CEO Dick Costolo, explaining the business to potential investors.

Peter Fenton, an investor at Benchmark Capital, stated that as Yahoo!'s former finance chief, Gupta will demonstrate his ability to communicate with investors, articulating how Twitter can achieve $1 billion in online advertising revenue next year. Fenton said, "Many people are trying to understand 'how Twitter's ads make money and if they work.' Gupta has the ability to explain this issue clearly in a simple rather than overly complex way."

Transaction Record

After graduating from the University of Chicago Graduate School of Business, Gupta spent over a decade in the tech industry and became one of the trusted executives of Yahoo!'s co-founders David Filo and Jerry Yang. When Microsoft CEO Steve Ballmer offered to buy Yahoo! for $44.6 billion in 2008, Gupta advised Filo and Yang, ultimately leading to Ballmer's unsuccessful attempt. Subsequently, Gupta helped Yahoo! reach a search advertising cooperation agreement with Microsoft. Previously, he had assisted Yahoo! in reaching an investment agreement with Alibaba Group.

Gupta also helped social game developer Zynga conduct its IPO in 2011. Through an 11-hour negotiation, Zynga secured a $1 billion credit line from Morgan Stanley, Goldman Sachs, and other investment banks. John Schappert, Zynga's former COO, said, "Gupta played a supporting role in our roadshow and earnings calls."

Of course, not all of Gupta's transactions have ended well. After rejecting Microsoft's acquisition offer, Yahoo!'s stock price plummeted, and the two companies' search advertising sales did not meet the previously set expectations last year. Since Zynga's IPO, the company's stock price has fallen by more than 70% cumulatively.

High Valuation

However, Yahoo!'s investment in Alibaba Group brought substantial returns. This deal also allowed Yahoo! CEO Marissa Mayer to sell part of her stake in Alibaba Group and use the cash for acquisitions and shareholder dividends.

Gupta must convince investors and analysts that Twitter deserves a high valuation. Last month, GSV Capital, one of the company's investors, valued Twitter at $10.5 billion, a 5% increase from May this year. Market research firm eMarketer expects Twitter's ad revenue to reach $950 million in 2014, up 63% from $582.8 million this year.

Greencrest analyst Santosh Rao pointed out, "Twitter's revenue figures are unclear. People want to know when the company will start its roadshow and what the future drivers of revenue growth will be."

Secret Meetings

To help prepare for the IPO, Gupta has assembled a team of financial professionals. In May, Gupta recruited Cynthia Gaylor, a former executive with 17 years of experience from Morgan Stanley. A recent job posting on LinkedIn by Twitter indicated that the company is seeking a financial reporting manager responsible for preparing the company's financial reports "after going public."

Gupta was born and raised in New York City, starting his investment banking career at Merrill Lynch in the 1990s. He joined Yahoo! in 2003 to handle finance and corporate development and quickly gained the trust of the executive team.

In a secret meeting held in April 2008, Gupta was one of only three Yahoo! executives, along with Yang and Filo, involved in discussing the sale to Microsoft. It is reported that the trio met with Microsoft CEO Ballmer and his assistant Charlie Songhurst.

Credit Line

Regarding the company's IPO issues, Twitter spokesperson Karen Wickre declined to comment. As of now, Microsoft spokesperson Peter Wootton also declined to comment.

Despite Yahoo!'s subsequent rejection of Microsoft's acquisition offer, Gupta, assisting then-CEO Carol Bartz, still managed to sign a search advertising cooperation agreement between the two companies. Gupta also accompanied Yang on visits to China to ensure Yahoo!'s investment in Alibaba Group. With Alibaba Group's IPO approaching, the previous investment has already yielded substantial returns for Yahoo!.

David Wehner, Zynga's former CFO who has since moved to Facebook, said that after joining Zynga in June 2011, Gupta took charge of contacting investment banks and drafting the company's IPO prospectus. Wehner mentioned that Gupta used the banks' desire to participate in the company's IPO for huge fees to secure a $1 billion credit line through transactions. He said, "Banks can earn substantial fees from the company's listing, so they are also willing to provide credit lines to the company."

Lessons from Facebook

For Twitter's upcoming IPO, Gupta must learn from Facebook's lessons. After conducting its IPO in May 2012, Facebook's stock price halved within six months due to investor concerns about the lack of development in mobile advertising. Clark Fredricksen, Vice President of eMarketer, pointed out that considering a significant portion of Twitter's revenue next year will come from mobile device advertising, the company will avoid such situations. He said, "Twitter has long been prepared for a mobile future."
