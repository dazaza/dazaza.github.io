2. Milk Mask Combats Wrinkles and Whitens

Wash your face with frozen milk, then cover your entire face with cotton pads soaked in milk, or use a thin towel dipped in milk to cover the burning affected areas. If your whole body feels painful, consider taking a milk bath. This will soothe the skin damaged by sunlight, reduce pain, and prevent inflammation.

Milk contains abundant milk fat, vitamins, and minerals, providing natural moisturizing effects. It's easily absorbed by the skin, preventing dryness and repairing fine lines. Its beauty effects are excellent, so those who prefer natural beauty methods should try it.

DIY Milk Strawberry Mask:

Method: 100 ml of milk, 50 g of strawberries, mash them into a paste, mix until creamy, apply on the face, keep for 20 minutes, then wash off. This method prevents skin from drying out and aging, making the skin smooth, moist, and delicate.

3. Moisturizing Anti-Wrinkle Mask

1. Milk + Flour

Method: Mix 3 tablespoons of milk with 3 tablespoons of flour until creamy, apply on the face. After the mask dries, wash carefully with warm water following the steps of washing your face.

This mask should be used no more than twice a week; excessive use may harm the skin.

2. Milk + Olive Oil + Flour

Method: Take 1 tablespoon of milk, add a few drops of olive oil and a small amount of flour, mix evenly, apply on a clean face, wait until it dries, then wash with warm water.

This facial mask reduces wrinkles and increases skin elasticity.

4. Whitening and Spot-Removing Mask

1. Milk + Hydrogen Peroxide + Flour + Water

Method: Mix 1 spoon of milk, 2 spoons of hydrogen peroxide, 3 spoons of flour, and a little water thoroughly, then use a soft brush to apply evenly on the face. Once the mask dries completely, wash off with warm water.

The water added to the mask should preferably be distilled water without impurities. Avoid touching eyebrows and eyes when applying.

2. Milk Facial Adds Luster

The benefit of a mask is deep cleansing. However, if you don't have time to make a mask, regularly applying milk directly to your face once or twice a week can also make your skin lustrous and smooth.

Method: Pour milk into a clean basin, then cover your face with a thin towel. Once the milk on your face dries completely, wait a few more minutes before rinsing with water.

5. Eliminate Eye Swelling

Milk also has skin-tightening effects. If you wake up with puffy eyelids, you can mix an appropriate amount of milk and vinegar with hot water, then gently press on the eyelids for 3 to 5 minutes, followed by covering with a hot towel for a short while. The eyelid swelling will disappear instantly.

For a simpler method, soak two pieces of cotton pads in cold milk, then apply them on the swollen eyelids for about 10 minutes, then rinse with clear water.

6. Milk Bath Treats Insomnia

Drinking milk promotes sound sleep, and taking a milk bath can also treat insomnia! Since soaking in a milk bath relaxes both mind and body, and the milk fragrance calms the autonomic nervous system, thus promoting deep sleep. Additionally, regularly taking milk baths reportedly improves excessive sweating effectively!

Method: Adjust the bathwater temperature to 35 to 40 degrees Celsius, then pour in 2 to 3 liters of milk (based on a standard bathtub), stir until semi-transparent, soak for 20 to 30 minutes. It's truly a supreme enjoyment! After bathing, promptly rinse off the water and clean the bathtub to avoid leaving a milk smell that not everyone likes.

7. Remove Garlic Odor

After drinking milk without rinsing your mouth, you usually get bad breath, but holding milk in your mouth can remove garlic odor. Hold the milk in your mouth for a while, then rinse with hot or cold water, and the garlic odor will be removed.

8. Remove Hand Grease

After kitchen work leaves your hands oily and greasy, you can use leftover milk to wash your hands. This not only removes grease but also nourishes your hand skin.
