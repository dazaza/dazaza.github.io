Factors related to the survival rate of breast augmentation with autologous fat transplantation:

1. Selection of donor area

Research has found that the activity of lipoprotein lipase (Lipoprotion Lipase LPL) in fat tissue varies in different parts of the human body. It was found that the activity of lipoprotein lipase in thigh fat tissue is higher than in other areas. High LPL activity is beneficial for the regeneration and survival of transplanted fat cells. The LPL activity in the thighs and buttocks is the highest, followed by the lower abdomen and upper abdomen. Based on this research and the distribution of deep fat in these areas, the lower half of the trunk is preferred as the donor area for fat transplantation.

2. Suction method

The method of suctioning fat tissue during surgery directly affects the result of the surgery. Inevitably, some cells are damaged and destroyed during surgery. The greater the degree of injury, the fewer living cells there are, and the lower the survival rate. Most scholars advocate using hollow needles for liposuction, with negative pressure not exceeding 0.05MPa, which can reduce cell damage. If other non-invasive techniques are used to suck fat, the cannula should preferably be less than 4mm fine tube.

3. Purification treatment of fat particles

Collected fat particles should be washed and filtered with 4-degree physiological saline, washing away already damaged fat cells, lipid droplets, and blood. The purification process should pay special attention to aseptic operation; the storage time of fat particles outside the body should be minimized as much as possible to improve the survival rate of fat cells.

4. Injection method

Its principle is to evenly distribute in the recipient area tissue, multiple points dispersed injection, the injection layer should be subcutaneous and mammary posterior space. The amount injected per point should not exceed 1ml, avoiding concentrating large amounts of fat in one area, massaging and smoothing after injection. This method, due to small fat volume, scattered location, easy establishment of blood supply for the implant, is beneficial for the survival of fat particles.
