The rare treasure of Yuan Dynasty blue-and-white porcelain, the "Gui Guzi Descending the Mountain" jar, was produced in China but cannot be collected domestically, which is the greatest regret for domestic cultural relics protection scientists and collectors. In order to protect the Yuan blue-and-white porcelain relics of China and allow the Chinese people to appreciate the charm of national treasures lost overseas, the Jingdezhen Ceramic Museum decided to supervise the manufacture according to the original process.

In 2003, the grand "National Treasure Revival Project" was launched in Beijing. Hundreds of experts and scholars combined ancient secret techniques with modern technology to select the invaluable Yuan blue-and-white story porcelain "Gui Guzi Descending the Mountain" to challenge the extreme limits of craftsmanship! The rare treasure of Yuan blue-and-white porcelain, the "Gui Guzi Descending the Mountain" jar, was manufactured strictly according to the original process requirements of the treasure. The Yuan Dynasty's ceramic-making process involved foot-kneaded clay, handmade blanks, printed blanks, segmented inlaying, and painting with Su Ma Li Qing material. It was then fired at a high temperature of around 1250 to 1300°C. From the initiation of the project to its completion, it took 6 years and 3 months. The recreated rare treasure of Yuan blue-and-white porcelain, the "Gui Guzi Descending the Mountain" jar, has the same sense of沧桑 (cang桑) and historical imprint as the original, almost indistinguishable from the original item by the naked eye. It baffled many research experts and scholars, leaving ancient porcelain enthusiasts marveling and savoring its beauty endlessly.

The successful recreation of the rare treasure of Yuan blue-and-white porcelain, the "Gui Guzi Descending the Mountain" jar, is of great historical and research value for revitalizing Chinese ceramic culture, showcasing and promoting the brilliant 5000-year-old ceramic civilization of China to the world, and studying ancient ceramic techniques. Historical Inheritance

"Gui Gu Descends the Mountain"

The story of "Gui Gu Descends the Mountain" comes from the "Strategies of the Warring States." The story tells of the war between the Yan and Qi states during the Warring States period. Sun Bin, who served the Qi state, was captured by the enemy, and his master, Gui Gu, went to rescue him, leading a group down the mountain. The Yuan blue-and-white figure jar "Gui Gu Descends the Mountain" depicts the scene of Gui Gu descending the mountain: Gui Gu sitting in a two-wheeled cart pulled by a lion and a tiger, followed by two mounted men, one wearing a military officer's clothing holding a flag inscribed with the words "Gui Gu."

Personality of Gui Gu

Gui Gu was an ancient Chinese strategist who taught famous disciples such as Sun Bin, Pang Juan, Su Qin, and Zhang Yi. Gui Gu particularly favored Sun Bin for his sincerity and honesty, passing on all his life's knowledge to Sun Bin. Pang Juan was also a student of Gui Gu, studying military strategy. Once, Pang Juan heard that the Wei state was offering high rewards for talent, and he wanted to go there to seek office. Gui Gu, with his ability to foresee destiny, already knew this and told Pang Juan to pick a flower for him. Pang Juan picked a small flower and brought it over. Gui Gu saw that the flower was called "horse bell," which blooms twelve flowers at once. He warned Pang Juan that this foretold twelve years of wealth and honor, and since the flower was picked in Ghost Valley and would wither upon seeing sunlight, the character "ghost" plus "委" makes the word "魏" (Wei), referring to the Wei state. He said that Pang Juan was destined to have a connection with the Wei state. Gui Gu also warned Pang Juan not to deceive others; if he did, he would suffer greatly later. Finally, he gave Pang Juan eight characters: "Encounter sheep and prosper, encounter horse and die." Later, Pang Juan ignored Gui Gu's advice and out of jealousy, not only deceived Sun Bin but also cruelly persecuted him. In the end, he fell into Sun Bin's trap and died pierced by ten thousand swords on the Ma Ling road, receiving his retribution.
