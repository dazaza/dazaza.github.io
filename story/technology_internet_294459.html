Tourism: Signs of industry recovery are evident, focus on individual stock trends, recommend 7 stocks＿Stock Knowledge

In a high-growth market, the dependence of tourism demand on the macroeconomic environment is very strong. When the economy grows, residents' disposable income generally increases as well, and a considerable portion of this disposable income is spent on tourism expenses. This situation is particularly prominent in emerging economies. Overall, the growth rate of international tourist numbers significantly exceeds the GDP growth rate. Between 1975 and 2000, the global GDP growth rate was approximately 3.5%, while the growth rate of international tourist numbers was about 4.6%, making the growth rate of international tourist numbers 1.3 times that of GDP.

Currently, China's tourism industry is developing rapidly. In 2007, the total revenue of China's tourism industry reached 1,095.7 million yuan, breaking through the 1 billion yuan mark for the first time, an increase of 22.6% over the previous year. Tourism consumption is becoming the most dynamic part of China's consumption structure, driven by the following factors: (1) The consumption structure of Chinese residents is facing an upgrade; (2) The improvement of infrastructure makes tourism activities more convenient and comfortable; (3) Government policies strongly support the development of the tourism industry.

China's tourism industry still has great growth potential. The National Tourism Administration believes that the mid-term development speed (2006-2010) of China's tourism industry will be an annual growth of 10%. According to past experience, the prediction data of the National Tourism Administration tends to be conservative. Currently, the tourism industry has become a highlight in China's national economic life, with its total output value accounting for 3.8% of GDP, while the proportion of the world tourism industry's total output value in the global GDP is around 10%. Compared with the development level of the world tourism industry, China's tourism industry still has great potential. Moreover, the World Tourism Organization predicts that by 2015, China will become the world's top-ranked tourism destination country and also the world's fourth-largest source country.

Signs of recovery in the tourism industry are evident, with investment focusing on individual stock performance. In the first half of 2009, China's tourism market showed a "total volume growth, two up one down" development trend. Tourism listed companies mainly focused on the domestic tourism consumption market enjoyed significant benefits from the recovery of industry prosperity. In the first half of 2009, the tourism sector achieved a total operating revenue of 6.835 billion yuan, an increase of 2.46% compared to the same period last year. For the tourism sector, the signs of moving out of the shadow of the second half of 2008 have already become very clear. Due to the characteristics of the tourism industry such as low entry barriers, intense competition, and low average social profit rates, not all tourism enterprises can share in the "feast" of industry growth. Therefore, investment in the tourism sector should focus on individual stock performance, investing in companies that can share in the industry's growth. We believe that the following stocks deserve investors' attention, and we believe these enterprises can share in the growth of the tourism industry. They are: Guilin Tourism, Jinjiang Shares, CYTS, Shou旅 Shares, Mount Emei, Huangshan Tourism, Lijiang Tourism.

The process of buying and selling stocks is as follows: When buying or selling stocks, you must specify whether you want to buy or sell, the name (or code) of the stock, quantity, and price. And this order is only valid for the day it is placed. The content of the commission includes the abbreviation (code) of the stock you want to buy or sell, the quantity, and the price of the stock you want to buy or sell. The abbreviation of the stock usually consists of three to four Chinese characters, and the code of the stock is six digits. When entrusting the purchase and sale, the code and abbreviation of the stock must be consistent. At the same time, there are certain regulations regarding the quantity of stocks bought and sold: that is, the quantity of stocks commissioned for purchase must be a whole multiple of 100, but the quantity of stocks commissioned for sale does not have to be a whole multiple of 100.

There are four types of commissions: counter submission commission, telephone automatic commission, computer automatic commission, and remote terminal commission.
