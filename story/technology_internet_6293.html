What kind of website is Mangba exactly?

Mangba is an "online notepad": A. Individuals can use it as a "personal notepad"; B. Project collaborators can use it as a "project collaboration notepad".

What use is an "online notepad" to me?

The pace of life is fast, and there are many things to keep track of. Therefore, many people need a tool to help them organize their thoughts and clarify their ideas. You may have noticed that many older colleagues have a small notebook where they record their work plans, insights, and random notes. We call this a "notepad".

Why should my "notepad" be online?

A. To avoid forgetting where you've recorded something. B. Being online allows you to access it anytime and share or collaborate with others. C. Our servers are more reliable than your personal computer, with regular backups, no downtime, and no viruses. Mangba has implemented many security measures to protect your privacy. For example, you cannot find another user's email unless they add you as a friend. Of course, for "super secrets", nothing is completely safe, and they should remain locked in your heart.

Is Mangba similar to Google Calendar?

No. Mangba is not a "calendar" program. For daily chores like "going to the supermarket after work to buy milk," a portable sticky note or a mobile phone might be more suitable. Although Mangba also has a time reminder function, its task, project, and note-taking features are better suited for more complex tasks that require thought or collaboration among multiple people.

Why would someone make their tasks/notes public?

Most people keep most of their tasks/notes private. However, some people may want to make certain parts of their content public so that others can see it... This question is similar to why people write blogs. :-) There is no standard answer. What's important is that whether what you write is public or private is entirely up to you.
