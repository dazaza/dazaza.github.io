The PC market is shrinking more and more. Including the shipment figures for the third quarter of this year, the global PC shipments have declined for six consecutive quarters. The shipments in the third quarter of this year also hit a new low since the 2008 student back-to-school purchasing spree.

This conclusion comes from Gartner's recently released Global PC Shipment Forecast Report. In the third quarter of this year, global PC shipments are estimated to reach 80.28 million units, down 8.6% year-on-year. Traditional PC manufacturers Lenovo, HP, Dell, Acer, and Asus rank in the top five by shipment volume. Lenovo, HP, and Dell showed slight growth, but "the two A's of Taiwan" both experienced declines in shipments. Acer’s PC shipments dropped by nearly 2 million units in the third quarter, while Asus dropped by over 1.4 million units. This is related to the transformation of these two companies. Asus has shifted its focus from PCs to tablets, and its tablet shipments in the third quarter were almost on par with mobile PCs.

In the U.S. market, PC shipments in the third quarter were 16.12 million units, up 3.5% year-on-year. HP ranked first in the U.S. PC market with a 26.9% share, followed by Dell, Apple (Gartner includes the Mac series in the PC market), Lenovo, and Toshiba.

Gartner analyst Mikako Kitagawa attributed the continuous contraction of the PC market to the squeeze from tablets: “The tools people use to consume content are gradually transitioning to tablets, which is the reason why PC shipments are encountering cold reception in both mature and emerging markets.” “Affordable Android tablets have attracted the first-time buyers in emerging markets with their wider usability, while high-end tablets are increasingly being accepted by users in mature markets.”

Gartner's long-time rival IDC also recently released its 2013 Q3 Global PC Shipment Assessment Outlook, estimating that global PC shipments in the third quarter of this year will reach 81.61 million units. Lenovo ranked first in the PC market with a 17.3% share, followed by HP, Dell, Acer, and Asus.
