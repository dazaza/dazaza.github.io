Nanshan Football Club Constitution.

Chapter One: General Provisions

Article 1: The nature of the club: The football club is a self-organized group by football enthusiasts that can enrich students' spare time and benefit their physical and mental health.

Article 2: The purpose of the club: Enrich students' extracurricular life, implement the principle of "making friends through football", create a sports fitness environment for students, promote the development of football activities in our institute, stimulate the training enthusiasm of football enthusiasts, and improve the overall level of football in our institute.

Article 3: The slogan of the club: Extensive exchange, active training, sincere cooperation, team awareness.

Chapter Two: Management and Requirements

Article 4: All members must arm themselves with Marxism-Leninism, Mao Zedong Thought, Deng Xiaoping Theory, seriously implement the relevant spirit of the Institute Party Committee and the Physical Education Research Department, closely revolve around the purpose of serving all football enthusiasts wholeheartedly to carry out activities.

Article 5: In study and training, all members must obey organizational arrangements, follow the instructions of the club leaders, be full of confidence and passion in learning and training. Be responsible for club work, acknowledge and abide by the club constitution. Always maintain a positive and enterprising attitude.

Article 6: Actively participate in the activities organized by the club, overcome difficulties. Exercise physical strength, psychology, and ability from regular study and training.

Article 7: Strictly abide by the club's discipline. Members must attend meetings and activities on time. If absent, they must ask for leave. Those who are absent without cause three times will be considered to have automatically withdrawn from the club.

Article 8: Focus mainly on amateur training. Club activities mainly take place during spare time. Balance various tasks and properly handle the relationship between the club and daily study.

Article 9: Pay attention to personal image. All members should have confidence and ambition, not mock new members. Protect the honor of this club, enhance collective cohesion and appeal.

Chapter Three: Organizational Structure

Article 10: The association has one guidance teacher (hired from our institute's professional physical education teachers), one president, two vice presidents. There are also departments of organization, publicity, planning, and external liaison (finance). Each department has two ministers (main and deputy), and several members.

Article 11: The president is responsible for conveying the work instructions and spirit of superiors, grasping the overall direction of work, comprehensively guiding the work of the club, ensuring the smooth development of work, presiding over the formulation of the overall activity plan of the club, urging each department and all members to seriously implement it, striving to improve the efficiency and quality of work, coordinating relations with other clubs and members. Regularly report and consult work with the Physical Education Research Department or guidance teacher.

Article 12: The External Liaison Department promotes the good image of our club. Pay attention to absorbing advanced management experience from other school clubs. Coordinate well with other school clubs for communication and exchange.

Article 13: The Organization Department is responsible for the organizational work of the club, carefully completing tasks assigned by superiors. And responsible for arranging various activities of this club, try to coordinate internal activities so they do not conflict with external factors, making the internal activities orderly.

Article 14: The Planning Department continuously proposes new club activity plans and actively coordinates with other departments within the club to ensure the implementation of the proposed activities.

Article 15: The Publicity Department is fully responsible for the publicity work of the club, showcasing the good image of our club.

Article 16: Each department head is responsible for the normal operation of their own department's work, regularly or irregularly reporting work conditions to the president and vice presidents.

Chapter Four: Member Admission Standards

Article 17: Admission Standards

(1) Love football.

(2) Solid basic skills, certain football consciousness, and match experience.

(3) Have a tenacious will, not blindly proud, not easily give up.

(4) Obey management, attend club activities on time, not late, not early departure.

(5) Have a sense of responsibility and can unite other team members.

Chapter Five: Rights and Obligations of Members

Article 18: Rights of Members

(1) Right to supervise and be supervised.

(2) The right to participate in all collective activities held by this club.

(3) The right to participate in the general meeting of this club and comment and vote.

(4) The right to make suggestions and criticisms to the club and request answers or solutions.

(5) The right to receive help and guidance from the club.

(6) The right to obtain corresponding extracurricular sports credits and enjoy corresponding policies from the college.

(7) The right to freely withdraw from the club. Anyone wishing to withdraw must submit a written application, which must be approved by the club leader before withdrawal. Generally, re-entry after withdrawal is not approved.

Article 19: Obligations of Members

(1) Members must abide by the rules and regulations of the school.

(2) Follow the club constitution, obey organizational arrangements and management, and attend various meetings and activities on time.

(3) Actively participate in learning and training, with enthusiasm, confidence, and perseverance.

(4) Members have the obligation to strive for the promotion of spiritual civilization in our institute.

(5) Members have the obligation to protect the honor of this club.

Chapter Six: Funding

Article 20: After an activity resolution, the club leader may apply to the College Youth League Committee or the Club Federation for a certain amount of activity funding.

Article 21: Composed of legitimate income such as sponsorships obtained by the External Liaison Department, mainly used for organizing various sports activities.

Article 22: The application for allocated activity funds is made by the activity leader to the president, approved and filed by the president, then distributed.

Article 23: All aspects of finance follow the principle of fairness and transparency, subject to the supervision of all members. Everyone should be responsible for the growth of the club!

Chapter Seven: Supplementary Provisions

Article 24: The interpretation rights of this constitution belong to the leadership team of the Nanshan Football Club.

Article 25: Amendments to this constitution require the agreement of more than half of the leadership team members.

Article 26: The Nanshan Football Club Constitution shall be implemented from the date of its announcement.

Nanshan Football Club

March 22, 2010
