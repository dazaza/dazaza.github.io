Facebook Makes the Fortune 500 for the First Time in 2013

by geekzhang on 2013-05-06 21:17:55

On May 6, 2013, Fortune officially released the latest ranking of the US 500. Walmart reclaimed the top spot with a revenue of $469.16 billion, while oil giant ExxonMobil ranked second with $449.88 billion in revenue. Facebook, which went public last year, made its debut on the US 500 list this year at rank 482. The veteran internet company Yahoo fell into the bottom ranks at position 494.

Apple, whose products continue to sell well globally, climbed steadily up the rankings from 17th to 6th place. It reported revenues of $156.5 billion, an increase of 44.6% over the previous year. Its profit surged by 61%, reaching $41.73 billion.

In general, however, the U.S. economy entered a prolonged phase of low growth in 2012. The total revenue of the 500 companies on the list reached $12.06 trillion, an increase of only 2.7% over the previous year. Employment increased by 2.3% to reach 26.4 million people. However, overall profits declined by 0.5%, totaling $820.3 billion.

Notable changes occurred in the top ten rankings this year. Chevron, another oil giant, retained its third-place position. Phillips 66, one of the largest independent refineries in the U.S., which split from ConocoPhillips last year, made its debut in fourth place. Another oil company, Valero Energy Corporation, squeezed into the top ten.

Meanwhile, Berkshire Hathaway, under the guidance of Warren Buffett's precise investment strategy, rose to fifth place. The rankings of General Motors and Ford Motor Company dropped slightly, placing them at seventh and tenth respectively. General Electric fell two places to eighth.

Last year's eighth-ranked Fannie Mae was pushed out of the top ten this year. However, benefiting from the revival of the U.S. real estate market, Fannie Mae, which suffered the biggest loss among the Fortune 500 last year, turned a profit this year, achieving a profit of $17.2 billion. Similarly pushed out of the top ten was HP, the struggling PC giant, which replaced Fannie Mae as the company suffering the largest loss on the list, with total losses amounting to $12.65 billion.

From a profitability perspective, among all the companies on the list, the most profitable remains ExxonMobil, with profits of $44.88 billion leading the pack. Apple closely followed behind.

Compared to last year, there were 24 new companies on the list this year.

The top hundred names of the 2013 Fortune U.S. 500 are listed below with their respective revenues and profits:

1. Walmart (Wal-Mart Stores): Revenue $469,162 million, Profit $16,999 million

2. ExxonMobil: Revenue $449,886 million, Profit $44,880 million

3. Chevron: Revenue $233,899 million, Profit $26,179 million

4. Phillips 66: Revenue $169,551 million, Profit $4,124 million

5. Berkshire Hathaway: Revenue $162,463 million, Profit $14,824 million

6. Apple: Revenue $156,508 million, Profit $41,733 million

7. General Motors: Revenue $152,256 million, Profit $6,188 million

8. General Electric: Revenue $146,874 million, Profit $13,641 million

9. Valero Energy: Revenue $138,286 million, Profit $2,083 million

10. Ford Motor: Revenue $134,252 million, Profit $5,665 million

...and so on until the 100th company listed.
