The pilot counties (cities, districts) can further raise the premium subsidy criteria

Taihai Network reported on June 12th (reporter from the Strait Metropolis Daily, Xue Wei, intern Huang Shan) that rural residents paying social endowment insurance can choose different tiers, and the more they pay, the more incentive subsidies the government provides. The information was revealed in the "Notice on Establishing an Incentive Mechanism for Personal Contributions to New Rural Social Endowment Insurance" recently issued by the General Office of the Provincial Government. According to relevant personnel from the provincial government, this incentive mechanism aims to motivate farmers' enthusiasm for participating in insurance contributions. On the basis of the original personal contribution government subsidy of no less than 30 yuan per person per year, the government will appropriately increase the contribution subsidy for farmers who choose higher contribution tiers. For those choosing a 100-yuan contribution, the government subsidy standard is 30 yuan per person per year. For each tier increase in contribution, the government subsidy standard increases by 5 yuan. When the contribution tier reaches 500 yuan or more, the government subsidy standard is 50 yuan per person per year.

When the new rural pension system is implemented, farmers aged 46 to 59 years old who participate in insurance should pay annually. Before reaching the age of 60 and handling pension collection procedures, they are allowed to make a one-time supplementary payment for any shortfall within 15 years, with the corresponding subsidy provided by all levels of finance.

The funds required for the government subsidy for participation and contribution will still be granted according to the original regulations by the provincial finance, based on the different financial conditions of various places, at respective rates of 80%, 60%, 40%, and 20% for the pilot counties (cities, districts). The remaining part will be shared by the prefecture-level cities and the pilot counties (cities, districts), with specific ratios determined by the prefecture-level city governments. Pilot counties (cities, districts) may further increase the contribution subsidy standards, with the required funds borne by the local finance.

The provincial government requires that all places formulate and issue methods for participation subsidies that reward higher contributions, in accordance with the "Notice", combined with local realities, actively guiding farmers to participate in insurance contributions and promoting the smooth implementation of the new rural pension pilot work.
