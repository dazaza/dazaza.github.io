Some of my experiences in treating psoriasis - the miracle brought by sunlight.

by mengmengyu on 2009-07-20 15:01:32

I am 34 years old this year, and I got sick in early 2003. At the beginning, there was a small lump on my head. At first, I didn't pay much attention to it, but later, there were dozens of red spots on my head, each about the size of a green bean. At this point, I went to Huashan Hospital in Shanghai and the Shanghai Dermatology Hospital for treatment, where I was prescribed some topical medication. The effect was average; the condition was basically under control, but it didn't fully heal.

This situation persisted for about a year before the psoriasis began to develop on my lower limbs. I remember very clearly that one winter evening, I had dinner with my family at "Little Fatty Sheep" hot pot. After we finished eating and went home, I took a shower. While lying in bed, I felt an intense itch on my legs and scratched them. A few days later, the rash appeared. Within weeks, it reached its peak severity. There were twenty patches of psoriasis on my lower body, each about the size of an egg. I immediately went back to the hospital and was prescribed stronger topical medications containing more hormones. Unfortunately, despite applying the medication almost daily, there was no effect.

At this point, I thought that perhaps only sunlight would be effective. In the summer of 2004, I almost always wore shorts to play soccer every week, each session lasting four hours. Miraculously, after being exposed to strong sunlight, the psoriasis on my legs noticeably flattened. I realized this method worked. Later, I didn't pay too much special attention to it. By the end of that summer, the psoriasis on my legs was completely gone. During this period, I did not use any medication.

I mentioned this therapeutic experience during a visit to the Shanghai Dermatology Hospital. One doctor was quite surprised and said, "This is actually good!" Another doctor warned, "Be mindful of the duration and intensity of sun exposure to prevent recurrence." However, two years have passed since then, and even though I haven't played soccer, the psoriasis on my legs has hardly recurred. There's just one patch left, which I don't worry too much about. With summer approaching again, I can just go out and get some more sun, and it will be fine. Ha ha.

Also, this winter, I went to Sanya. After only two days of sun exposure, the condition on my head improved significantly. Now, there is still one relatively stubborn patch on my head. The ointment helps it clear up quickly, but it also recurs quickly. The key issue is that most ointments contain hormones, and long-term use is not advisable. There is also a patch on my thigh. These two patches are the original sites of the disease when it first started. Overall, the condition is well-controlled. This summer, I plan to play some soccer or go swimming, which should resolve the issue. Even if it recurs locally in the future, I'm not afraid because I know how to handle it.

To summarize my experience treating psoriasis: 1. Try to avoid using soap for washing your hair or bathing, as it can be too drying. Use shower gel instead! 2. Sunlight and ultraviolet light therapy should be effective for most cases of psoriasis. Pay attention to finding the methods that work best for you. 3. Essentially, this condition, which has yet to be fully conquered, is a minor ailment. Don't worry too much about it. A doctor from Huashan Hospital said that some people live their entire lives dealing with psoriasis, and they are still fine.

Let me add two more points: First, scalp psoriasis is very sensitive to polluted air, such as cigarette smoke and kitchen fumes, so it's important to ensure good air ventilation. Second, phototherapy effects vary from person to person. According to doctors, inappropriate exposure to sunlight may worsen the condition for some patients.
