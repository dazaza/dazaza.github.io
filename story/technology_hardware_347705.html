Below are some foods with high nutritional value, but many people don't like to eat them:

1. Cucumber Seeds: Cucumber seeds contain a large amount of magnesium. Studies have found that people with high levels of magnesium in their blood have a 40% lower chance of premature death.

2. Beets: This red vegetable contains a large amount of folic acid and betaine. These two chemicals work together to reduce homocysteine levels, an inflammatory mixture that can damage arteries and increase the risk of heart disease. Moreover, the natural pigment in beets - β-cyanin - has been proven through experiments to prevent cancer.

3. Pomegranates: Pomegranates contain high concentrations of lycopene, which better prevents prostate cancer compared to tomatoes and watermelons. Additionally, a cup of pomegranate juice contains 688 milligrams of potassium, which is 63% more than bananas. Pomegranates also contain a lot of fiber, with each cup containing 9 grams.

4. Lettuce: Half a cup of cooked lettuce contains a large amount of lutein and zeaxanthin, with each cup containing 10 milligrams. These carotenoids can prevent retinal aging because they absorb light harmful to the eyes.

5. Cinnamon: Cinnamon helps control blood sugar and reduces the risk of heart disease. In fact, studies have found that type 2 diabetes patients who consume 1 gram of cinnamon daily for six weeks will significantly reduce their blood sugar, lipids, and bad cholesterol.

6. Purslane: Purslane has the highest omega-3 content, and studies have found that the melatonin (an antioxidant that stops cancer cells from producing) in purslane is ten times that of any other fruit or vegetable.

7. Pomegranate Juice: Research shows that drinking 2 ounces (56 grams) of pomegranate juice daily for a year can reduce systolic blood pressure by 21%, and significantly improve blood flow to the heart.

8. Goji Berries: Among all fruits, goji berries have the highest antioxidant capacity index. Studies have also found that the sugar in goji berries can lower insulin resistance, thereby preventing diabetes.

9. Prunes: Prunes contain a large amount of neochlorogenic and chlorogenic acid, two chemicals that can resist free radicals. These free radicals can cause structural cell damage, leading to cancer.

10. Chinese Cabbage: A cup of chopped Chinese cabbage contains only 22 calories but is rich in nutrients. Chinese cabbage contains sulforaphane, which can increase the amount of enzymes in the body (enzymes can stop the production of free radicals that destroy cells and prevent cancer). Studies have found that among all plant chemicals, sulforaphane has the highest ability to promote enzyme production.
