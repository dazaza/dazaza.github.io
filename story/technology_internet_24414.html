Continuous online will be forced to disconnect

by 127702 on 2007-07-10 17:21:41

Recently, Beijing Telecom began to implement a "protection mechanism" for ADSL users - forcing users to disconnect once every 48 hours of continuous online activity. Some users have questioned this measure, while others believe the time limit should be shortened to 24 hours.

Users Suddenly Encounter Disconnection

Today, while Mr. Wen was sending and receiving emails, he suddenly found that his ADSL had disconnected. "A similar incident also happened a few days ago when I was trading stocks online, almost causing a major issue," said Mr. Wen.

Afterwards, he learned that it was due to Beijing Telecom's mandatory disconnection policy.

Beijing Telecom Claims Many Customers Requested This Feature

This morning, according to information from Beijing Telecom, they started implementing this "protection mechanism" last week.

Beijing Telecom stated: "This can prevent users from accidental losses, such as forgetting to disconnect after long-term internet use resulting in high fees, or automatically installed routers with auto-dialing functions leading to prolonged internet usage costs. After disconnection, users can reconnect through dial-up software."

Relevant personnel from Beijing Telecom mentioned that this service was initiated based on the requirements of many users. It is understood that for timed monthly subscription package users, the cost exceeding the package is 0.05 yuan per minute, equivalent to 3 yuan per hour, which is significantly more expensive than the package price.

Consumer Attitudes

From yesterday to today, the reporter randomly interviewed 50 ADSL users, including 8 timed monthly subscription users.

Among them, 34 unlimited monthly subscription users indicated that this service has little impact on them.

On the other hand, 10 users believed that the forced disconnection might affect normal internet use. Mr. Wen said, "What if you're about to buy a stock and get forcibly disconnected? Who will be responsible for the loss?" He highly questions this "protection" measure.

Additionally, 21 users believe that the forced disconnection time should be changed to 24 hours for better results.

Beijing Telecom's Explanation

In response to user inquiries, Beijing Telecom stated that this matter involves legal, business, and other departments, making the situation complex, and they are temporarily unable to provide answers.

Customer service personnel at Beijing Telecom's hotline 5700 explained that this service is aimed at ensuring user consumption safety. They currently haven't received any complaints and claimed, "After disconnection, users can reconnect immediately, and the short duration will not affect internet usage." (Reporter: Liu Wei)
