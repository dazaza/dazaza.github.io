The concept of low-carbon stock market is popular and the investment enthusiasm for heat pumps is high.

by ditan360 on 2010-07-08 09:48:13

Exclusive report of Low Carbon Network: With the low-carbon economy rapidly coming into people's view, the development and utilization of new energy has brought new opportunities to the heat pump industry. As a natural phenomenon, adopting heat pump technology can save a large amount of high-quality energy, and it has been proven to be an effective technology for reducing greenhouse gas emissions. Therefore, industry insiders expressed that the domestic heat pump market will maintain high-speed growth.

Low-carbon and Energy-saving Heat Pump Technology

Using heat pump technology, heat pump water heaters avoid the drawbacks of high energy consumption and serious pollution associated with traditional fuel and gas. Theoretically, its heat transfer efficiency can reach as high as 400%, and at the same time, the operation of the equipment is not constrained by climate or weather conditions. Air-source heat pump air conditioners use the atmospheric environment as a heat source, adopting the heat pump principle to achieve the transfer of low-grade thermal energy to high-grade thermal energy through a small amount of electrical energy output. Currently, heat pump air conditioners have gained widespread application. The world average level is 70%, Japan reaches nearly 99%, and China is on par with the world average.

Building energy consumption currently accounts for about 27% of the total societal energy consumption, where the energy consumption of building heating and cooling accounts for roughly 60% of the entire building energy consumption. Ground-source heat pumps, as a new technology for building heating and cooling, can achieve the purpose by consuming a small amount of electricity or thermal energy.

Considering installation costs, ground-source heat pump systems are only 10% more expensive than air-cooled systems. Air-cooled heat pump systems recover investment costs in less than four years, while ordinary water-cooled units recover costs in five years. Considering that ground-source heat pumps typically have a lifespan of over 20 years, they are very economical. With the introduction of new equipment and rising energy prices, costs will further decrease, making economic benefits even more apparent.

Currently, given the global energy shortage, the safety, low-carbon, and environmentally friendly features of air-source heat pump water heaters will become the best solution for home heating and hot water. The currently booming heat pump water heater market sees Europe as the largest market. Domestically, the industry is just starting, with nearly 400 manufacturers joining. Currently, Midea holds a larger market share, accounting for 31%.

The State Encourages the Development of Heat Pump Technology

The state has issued a series of policies and regulations to support ground-source heat pumps. The Ministry of Finance and the Ministry of Housing and Urban-Rural Development have released plans such as "Opinions on Promoting the Application of Renewable Energy in Buildings" and "Interim Measures for the Administration of Special Funds for Renewable Energy Building Applications," clearly pointing out the need to strengthen research on the application of mixed energy technologies and heat pump technologies. During the "Eleventh Five-Year Plan" period, the proportion of renewable energy applications in newly constructed building areas should exceed 25%.

Investment Potential

In recent years, low-carbon new products with obvious energy-saving effects, such as ground-source heat pumps, have gained preliminary market recognition. Moreover, the government has provided certain subsidies for ground-source heat pump products. Industry insiders claim that heat pump technology will be the direction of development for some time, perhaps entering a period of rapid development. The market overall presents a scene of multiple brands flourishing, without forming a monopolistic market structure dominated by one or a few companies. Currently, water-ground source heat pump enterprises are mainly small and medium-sized enterprises with relatively small market shares. In 2009, there were over 2000 research institutions, product manufacturers, and engineering service providers for domestic water-ground source heat pump central air conditioning systems, but mainstream production enterprises numbered only around forty to fifty. Companies like Clemence and Mei Yi might be considered dark horses. Under the market environment of 2010, as highly targeted personalized products, it's easy to see dark horses emerge in specific regions or for specific products. However, the complex and varied market environment is the greatest test of each company’s courage and vision, and we look forward to the emergence of new technologies and industry dark horses.
