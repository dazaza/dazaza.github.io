Great Kung Fu Panda movie review

Just like baozi, good stuffing is not in the fold. Kung Fu Panda's full understanding of Chinese culture is more than a slick panda can cover, let alone be deceived by the illusion of Hollywood themes. In fact, Kung Fu Panda is one of the most understanding films ever made about China, wrapped in gorgeous animation, and at its core is the most profound metaphor about China's political history, not just the personal history of a panda.

The story takes place in a highly condensed place: the Valley of Peace. There are ordinary people, there are superior and revered rulers, and in a forgotten corner, there is a symbol of state power of the violent prisons-prisons, the military force of at least 2,000 people.

In this country, the ruling class consists of people who have mastered the art of kung fu, who live high up in emerald temples on hilltops, and who even have minions to drive them around - ducks of all colors. And the supreme ruler, that is, the highest martial arts person - dragon warrior. Of course, dragon Warrior is only a formal title, and in the mouth of his subjects, Dragon Warrior is referred to as "master". The word can be interpreted as master, as well as overlord or master.

Dragon warriors wield supreme power here, and all subjects must salute their master. If we have a little memory of Chinese culture, it is not difficult to understand that the dragon warrior is the only ruler - the emperor.

Now the emperor of the Valley of Peace is the old Turtle Immortal, the creator of kung Fu and the supreme authority in the Valley of Peace. But he knew his time was coming and he had to find an heir for himself. Turtle immortal will be the dragon scroll high collection in the mouth of the dragon, just like the Zhengguang plaque behind the standing legacy. In other words, Po, the remnant Leopard and the five Warriors are fighting for the right to inherit the throne, not a martial arts handbook.

Despite the fact that the turtle Immortals created kung fu, despite the fact that all the people and even all kung fu practitioners think that kung fu is the highest, they are entitled to become dragon warriors. However, the six competitors most familiar to the public, the Leopard and the Fantastic Five, were not in the turtle fairy's eye, and the Leopard even paid the price of 20 years in prison.

Why?

There is only one reason: they are all disciples of Master Raccoon.

Who's Master Raccoon? He is a disciple of the Turtle Fairy, the most famous kung fu instructor, and he is even sincerely called master by the people. But he's not a dragon warrior, not a supreme ruler. He is below one man and above ten thousand. The contradiction between the turtle fairy and the raccoon is the contradiction between the monarchy and the facies.

Although Master Raccoon was always in the public eye, although his disciples were famous throughout the world, and although Master Raccoon would be on the front line to command when disaster struck, he never grasped the most central authority. Do you remember how the warden treated Shifu? "Go back and tell your coon master..." Yes, this is Master Raccoon of the Five, not Master Raccoon of the dungeon. It goes without saying whose territory this is.

But the raccoon wasn't willing to do that. Despite the outward appearance of absolute submission to the turtle Sensei, behind the scenes Master Raccoon is betting on the next heir. When he "picked it up" (anyone believe that?) When a martial arts wizard, Master Raccoon placed infinite love and hope on the disabled leopard. Master Raccoon's only personal disciple, in such a capacity to inherit the great unification how can get the approval of the turtle fairy, the turtle fairy refused to recognize the broken leopard, the official reason is the dark side of the heart.
