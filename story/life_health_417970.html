What diseases may cause anal pain _ Zhengzhou Anorectal Disease Hospital

by anonymous on 2011-04-18 09:40:40

Anal pain is another common symptom of anorectal diseases. Among patients seen in the outpatient department of anorectal surgery, apart from those with hematochezia forming the majority, anal pain ranks second. About 30% of patients visit the clinic primarily due to anal pain. Anal pain is a subjective sensation experienced by patients and lacks objective measurement criteria. Based on the nature and degree of pain, it can be categorized into severe pain, dragging pain, and dull pain. The main cause of anal pain stems from diseases of the anal canal itself, such as anal fissures, which manifest as localized anal pain. Pain caused by perianal and rectal diseases, like perianal abscesses, presents as pain slightly further away from the anus. Anal pain may be the primary symptom of a certain disease but not the sole symptom; it could also co-occur with hematochezia or other symptoms. For example, a patient with an anal fissure might seek treatment mainly due to anal pain. Dull pain refers to a general mild ache that is tolerable. Causes of anal dull pain are mostly inflammatory diseases around the anal canal and anus, and can also be observed in neurotic conditions.

1) Ano-sinusitis: Commonly seen in the outpatient department of anorectal surgery. Typical symptoms include anal dull pain and discomfort, gradually developing into brief anal pain during defecation. If the sphincter muscle is stimulated by inflammation, it can also cause spastic anal pain, with prolonged duration. There is often secretion overflow from the anus, causing itching or eczema on the surrounding skin due to irritation, and local skin remains moist and unclean, frequently soiling underwear. At the start of defecation, there may be mucus-like substances in the stool, which are inflammatory secretions from the anal sinus. Occasionally, blood streaks may be present within the mucus. If untreated for a long time, it may develop into chronic ano-sinusitis.

2) Anal fistula: Commonly seen in the outpatient department of anorectal surgery. Typical symptoms include repeated rupture and pus discharge from the skin around the anus, accompanied by anal dull pain during acute infection episodes. Patients have a history of acute perianal abscess, which forms a chronic anal fistula after the abscess ruptures or is drained surgically. Due to long-term chronic inflammation and infection stimulation around the anus, the fistula opening and its deeper parts often experience dull pain. Repeated rupture and pus discharge can lead to skin itching due to pus irritation. The fistula opening may heal falsely, stopping the pus discharge temporarily, but after some time, the area swells again with pain, followed by rupture and pus discharge, repeating this cycle.

3) Anorectal neurosis: Similar to other neuroses, the self-perceived symptoms are severe while physical examinations reveal no positive signs. Symptoms may include anal dull pain, which worsens when concentrating on the sensation and lessens otherwise. Other symptoms could include anal itching, unusual sensations, crawling sensations, anal fullness, burning sensation, and difficulty in defecation. Emotional fluctuations may exacerbate symptoms, while appropriate suggestive therapy can alleviate or relieve them.

4) Rectitis: In cases of rectitis, common symptoms include anal dull pain, along with diarrhea. Some patients present with bloody stools as the primary symptom. It's important to maintain good sleep habits, avoid staying up late or spending too much time online, engage in moderate exercise, follow a light diet, and avoid spicy and greasy foods. Consuming bananas, fiber drinks, fruits, vegetables, bamboo shoots, oatmeal, and other high-fiber foods can promote bowel movements. It is recommended to visit a specialized anorectal hospital for examination through an anorectal endoscope. Treatment should be targeted based on the examination results. Untimely treatment can lead to significant harm that shouldn't be underestimated.
