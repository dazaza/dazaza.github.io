SIHH 2009: A Watch Appointment in Advance

by biao156mq42 on 2012-02-07 15:48:29

Ralph Lauren Watch

Without the clamor of the 2,000 brands at Baselworld, the brands involved in SIHH have consistently been the 11 members of the Richemont Group and 4 peripheral brands. However, this year will see a new member take the stage - Ralph Lauren Watch and Jewelry Co., a joint venture between Richemont Group and Polo Ralph Lauren, which will be moving into SIHH to showcase its new watch series. This is Ralph Lauren's first step into the world of watches; positioned as a fashion favorite, it still promises top-notch craftsmanship, making it highly anticipated. Additionally, the Federation of the Swiss Watch Industry will also make its debut at SIHH, hosting an all-new exhibition titled "Think Time, Think Swiss Excellence."

The 19th Salon International de la Haute Horlogerie (SIHH) will be held from January 19th to January 23rd, 2009. At that time, the "feast of timepieces" for 2009 will premiere amidst snowy landscapes.

The arrangement of the exhibition hall is very simple, with the main color being creamy white. The exhibition halls of over a dozen brands are connected sequentially, spread out on both sides, while the adjacent area is set up as a permanent reception zone. Waiters weave through the space providing meticulous service. Champagne, red wine, juice, coffee... various drinks are available at your request. During lunchtime, cold platters made from caviar, white asparagus, foie gras, salmon, and other ingredients offer a particularly rich and delicious variety. Of course, these are all provided free of charge and without limit, allowing guests to casually sit together to eat, chat, or rest. This is the high-end salon atmosphere that the Geneva Watch Fair aims to create. Furthermore, if I'm not mistaken, following the side of the Piaget exhibition hall...

JeanRichard (尚维沙)

While admiring the watches, don't forget to visit the exclusive discount area at SIHH. For attendees, there are opportunities to purchase non-watch items from some brands within the Richemont Group at 30-50% off, such as jewelry, accessories, writing instruments, and various leather goods. My personal favorite is Montblanc's discount area, where I always find something worthwhile each year. However, styles and quantities are limited, so early birds get the best deals.

After walking down the corridor and turning left and right a few times, you'll discover a hidden gem behind the exhibition: a modernly designed restaurant. With black walls, carpets, and ceilings, black half-human-sized flower beds serve as decorative partitions between tables, filled with white branches, creating the feeling of walking into a winter night forest covered in snow. Dining here offers none of the noise and crowding of the outside exhibition halls. From appetizers to dessert, you can enjoy a very formal and delicious main meal.

A.Lange & SHNE (朗格)

GENEVA PALEXPO, as the venue for SIHH, has a prime geographical location, extremely close to the airport, greatly facilitating dealers and media coming from all over the globe. Due to the exceptionally strict identity verification procedures for entering the exhibition hall, a mysterious veil is cast over this top-tier watch fair. All invited visitors, including dealers and guests, must reserve their entry spots months in advance. On the first day of entering the exhibition hall, they must take photos to obtain credentials, often leaving watch enthusiasts who come uninvited disappointed and forced to leave.

Introduction: Every April, I travel to Switzerland to attend the watch fair, spending two weeks rushing around from Basel to Geneva. Amidst the joy, there is fatigue, and despite the stunning scenery along the way, there is no time to appreciate it. However, this year is different, as the 19th Geneva High End Watch Salon (Salon International de la Haute Horlogerie, SIHH) is being held for the first time in January.

If you have time to cross the Mont Blanc Bridge and reach Geneva’s old town, please don’t miss the Saint Peter’s Cathedral, Parc La Grange, Patek Philippe Museum, Place Neuve, and the Monument de la Refomation.

During previous exhibitions, when night fell, some brands would hold grand parties inviting celebrities from around the world and gentlemen of all kinds, creating a spectacular scene. Different brands had their own unique party styles, and if the timing conflicted, it was often regrettable not to be able to experience them all. However, this year's SIHH night carries a hint of the chill of "winter." The financial crisis has tightened the wallets of watchmakers, forcing brands to cancel their lavish parties in favor of smaller appreciation dinners. Don't be disheartened—this gives you more time for yourself. You can relax and enjoy a peaceful Geneva evening.
