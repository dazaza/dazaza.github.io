Yixin was born, can it unseat WeChat?

by anonymous on 2013-08-20 15:47:32

WeChat is no longer "micro". After the 5.0 version, this software that can basically be called a national application has found it difficult to make breakthroughs in human-to-human communication and has started to focus on commercial development. Some people like this transformation while others do not. Therefore, some people want to return to a pure communication application.

YiXin, the instant messaging service application jointly launched by NetEase and China Telecom, is precisely such a simple communication application, at least from its advertised functions. Whether from its name or functions, the target opponent of this application is WeChat.

Subsequently, I downloaded this newly launched application to take a quick look at its interface.

From the main interface, you can see that the functions of this software appear thin (pure), with only three main options: YiXin, Moments, and Settings. The menu hierarchy is also shallower than WeChat. However, there is not much difference in communication functions compared to WeChat.

Notably, during the process of registering for YiXin, I did not see any step to confirm the password, and there was no prompt after successful registration. Compared to WeChat, YiXin's breakthrough in communication functions is free SMS and voicemail, and these are all free. Moreover, the mobile phones or landlines receiving SMS and voicemails do not need to be YiXin users.

However, during my trial, the free SMS kept failing to send, and the voicemail function could not be used normally. Then I checked the comments about YiXin news on other websites and found that the problems I faced were not isolated cases. Of course, since this instant messaging software just launched today, let's be more inclusive and see if it can improve later.

Regarding the three highlights that YiXin emphasizes - "barrier-free, high-quality, and emotional" - I think only the first one has some significance. More advanced audio compression technology and free emoticons can only be considered as slightly better than nothing. Breaking down the basic communication barriers between instant messaging applications and mobile/landline phones is truly commendable, of course, which is possible only because China Telecom supports it behind the scenes.

The name "Moments" is even directly the same as WeChat's Moments, with almost no difference in functionality. However, according to users, replies in Moments are visible to all friends, not just mutual friends like in WeChat. Users will be able to choose between the two modes in the future.

From the current trial, YiXin is relatively complete in communication functions and very refreshing to use. However, the services are currently lacking. Not only was I unable to send free SMS and leave phone messages earlier, but now I can't even connect to the server.

Now let's look at NetEase and China Telecom. Compared to Tencent promoting WeChat, NetEase promoting YiXin has an obvious disadvantage in importing users. Tencent can rely on QQ numbers to import users to WeChat, while YiXin, which currently relies solely on phone numbers, is struggling. Nevertheless, YiXin still set the ambitious goal of developing 100 million users in six months. Additionally, due to the existence of mobile contact lists and QQ friends, users can easily rebuild a new social network on WeChat, while YiXin has a natural disadvantage in this aspect. Perhaps NetEase's well-performing email service might help in the future.

China Telecom has the fewest users among the three major operators in China, and competition between the three operators is fierce. YiXin breaks down the barriers between fixed-line phones, mobile phones, and applications, meaning that this connection transcends the operator level. So, how will Mobile and Unicom react?

If YiXin wants to go against WeChat's large platform route and instead pursue a small, precise, and free route, where will its profit point be? NetEase's main profit point is still games. Will YiXin's development direction be closely linked to NetEase games? If so, how will the telecommunications side view it?

According to NetEase reports, the cooperation framework between NetEase and China Telecom is as follows: Joint venture establishment, with China Telecom investing traffic resources and ground marketing, and NetEase investing funds; YiXin provides mobile internet services equally to mobile and fixed-line users of China Telecom, China Unicom, and China Mobile; Product research and development, operation, managed by the joint venture, fully led by Ding Lei; IDC costs required by YiXin, as well as inter-network settlements with Unicom and Mobile, are borne by the joint venture, with clear and straightforward relationships.

As a private enterprise, NetEase collaborates with China Telecom, a state-owned enterprise. Will there be any friction between these two companies with different characteristics in their joint venture, Zhejiang YiXin Technology Co., Ltd.?

Mobile's previous "FeiXin" has already faded from the stage, and Telecom's own YiLiao hasn't fared well either. Talking about reaching 100 million users in six months is no easy task. It took WeChat, which is popular today, a full 433 days to reach 100 million users.

YiXin has always positioned WeChat as its challenge target in its promotion, but I think YiXin's initial goal should rather be focused on how to develop users and improve services. Without a healthy social network and stable, fast instant messaging services, it's hard to talk about knocking down WeChat, let alone standing on its own feet.

It can be said that the YiXin presented before us today is not yet a complete YiXin, and its model and direction are still unclear. For this public-private partnership application, why not give it more time to prove itself?
