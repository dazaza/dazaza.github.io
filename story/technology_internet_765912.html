Sina Weibo tests the waters for short video sharing as its Miaopai video client goes online

by anonymous on 2013-08-29 16:38:12

Sina Weibo's MiaoPai video client goes online

On August 28th, it was reported that recently, Sina Weibo had released version 4.0 for both iPhone and Android. This version emphasizes the multimedia content of Weibo more strongly, providing users with more sharing functions. Notably, a fixed entry point for the video sharing application "MiaoPai" has been built into Weibo.

In the new version of Sina Weibo's publishing page, clicking on the plus sign at the bottom right corner will reveal an icon named "MiaoPai". Clicking this icon will guide the user to download the application from the app store. After downloading and installing, clicking the icon again will automatically open MiaoPai's video shooting function in Weibo. By simply holding down the screen, a 10-second video can be recorded, and users can also choose video borders and set background music, making it very interesting. After completing a video, click publish, and the video will immediately appear on the user's Weibo. From the operational process, once the user installs the MiaoPai mobile end, publishing self-shot videos on Weibo becomes exceptionally simple.

According to the product manager of MiaoPai, after uploading a video to traditional websites, it needs to go through transcoding and CDN distribution processes before it can be successfully published. Often, after uploading a video, one has to wait a long time to see it. However, Weibo users have extremely high requirements for the immediacy of information publication, and the old technical methods are no longer able to meet the needs of Weibo mobile-end video applications. The MiaoPai product uses an innovative technology that synchronizes shooting, transcoding, and uploading, minimizing the user's waiting time as much as possible. Even under 3G conditions, when the user clicks publish, the video can be posted to Weibo and successfully played on all terminals. In terms of data consumption, due to MiaoPai's targeted optimization for mobile video, a 10-second video approximately only consumes 600K of data, even saving more data than posting a picture tweet.

Globally, Twitter's Vine and Facebook's Instagram already support short video uploads, which have been widely popular among users and achieved success. The launch of short video uploads by Sina Weibo is clearly inspired by foreign counterparts. Domestically, it is generally believed that due to network environment influences, video-sharing applications are still difficult to develop. However, MiaoPai seems to have solved the data threshold facing users from a technical perspective. Just like how Weibo text only allows users to input 140 characters, length restrictions instead fostered a significantly influential social media platform. Similarly, MiaoPai only allows users to shoot 10-second short videos, which might spark another wave of selfies and creativity among users.

It is understood that the mobile version of MiaoPai has already been released in Apple's App Store, and the Android version has also been launched on various channels such as Android Market, Anzhi Market, 360 Mobile Assistant, 91 Mobile Assistant, and豌豆荚 (Wandoujia). Of course, the most convenient installation method is still to directly click the MiaoPai icon from the Weibo client's publishing box.
