Anxi Zhentian Pavilion Tieguanyin Tea Factory is located in Anxi, the hometown of China's Tieguanyin tea. With the corporate spirit of "honesty, progress, development, and innovation" as well as the business philosophy of "excellent value for money," we sincerely welcome inquiries and visits from friends from all walks of life! We offer direct sales from the origin, mainly dealing in Minnan Oolong teas including Tieguanyin, Huangjinguì, Benshan, Maoxie, and various other famous teas. We also operate a side business in exquisite tea sets and tea packaging machinery. Our factory was designated in December 2007 as a quality tracking unit for Fujian Province's industrial enterprises by the Fujian Industry and Commerce Times. We are also a promotion brand selected by one hundred tea cooperatives nationwide.

The Zhentian Tea Cooperative, established in 2004, is located in Changkeng Township, Anxi County. In Zhentian Village, 116 tea-farming households and 14 tea merchants have joined the cooperative, accounting for 91% of the total number of households. Among the members of the Zhentian Tea Cooperative, 88 people have obtained national vocational skills qualification certificates, including 2 senior technicians, 8 technicians, 50 senior workers, 12 intermediates, and 16 beginners. The total cultivated land area in Zhentian Village is 392 mu (approx. 26 hectares), with mountainous land covering 3709 mu (approx. 247 hectares) and tea garden areas covering 1008 mu (approx. 67 hectares). In 2006, the per capita income of farmers was 5154 yuan, of which more than 3100 yuan came from the tea industry.

Since its establishment, the Zhentian Tea Cooperative has achieved significant benefits through hiring expert professors as advisors; founding the first national peasant scientific research institution - "Anxi County Zhentian Peasant Tea Research Institute" to serve tea farmers; organizing technical training and exchanges, implementing the "five unifications" method for tea garden management; and establishing 10 direct-sales websites for tea farmers to conduct online transactions. First, the safety and quality level of tea has significantly improved, with all tea produced by members meeting the national tea health and quality safety standards. Second, the cost of tea garden management has clearly decreased, saving 100,500 yuan in 2006. Third, tea production techniques have significantly improved, increasing the average selling price of tea across the entire cooperative by 30 yuan per kilogram in 2006, representing a 25% increase compared to the same period in 2005. Fourth, the cooperative members' sense of collaboration has increased, with the mechanism of shared interests and shared risks fundamentally changing the overall awareness of all cooperative members. Fifth, 10 cooperative tea product direct-sales counters have been established in major and medium-sized cities such as Beijing, Shanghai, Chongqing, Xi'an, Hainan, Fuzhou, and Quanzhou, with annual sales reaching 33 million yuan.

On November 27, 2007, the online transaction initiation ceremony for tea farmers of Anxi County's Zhentian Tea Cooperative and the 2007 autumn Tieguanyin preliminary processing technology evaluation conference were held in Zhentian Village, Changkeng Township, Anxi County. Relevant personnel from the Fujian Agricultural Research Institute Dong Diwei, Quanzhou City Agriculture Bureau He Changlong, and Anxi County Tea Association Li Wentong attended the initiation ceremony.

On March 2, 2006, the Anxi County Zhentian Tea Cooperative was founded in Zhentian Village, Changkeng Township, Anxi County, marking the establishment of the first tea cooperative in Quanzhou City. The tea cooperative is voluntarily combined by tea farmers, tea merchants, and tea enterprises. It is a multi-benefit model that solves the problems of scattered tea production and operation, promotes advanced technology, expands the sales market, and maintains the interests of tea farmers.

In March 2006, Anxi Zhentian Tea Cooperative signed a supply and safe use contract with the "Minnan Oolong Tea Harmless Tea Garden Production Materials Distribution Center." The supplier provided pesticides and fertilizers uniformly to the cooperative at wholesale prices, guiding tea farmers scientifically in their application. The cooperative registered each of its 101 member households' tea garden areas and recorded pesticide usage, achieving unified supply of pesticides and fertilizers, ensuring the "source" of tea production passed inspection, and providing reliable guarantees for tea quality.

On April 30, 2006, the "2006 Spring Anxi Tieguanyin Preliminary Processing Technology Competition" was held at the Anxi County Zhentian Tea Cooperative.

In October 2006, the Anxi Zhentian Tea Cooperative held an autumn tea-making technology training exchange meeting, inviting senior agronomist Lin Huoliang, tea-making technology expert Chen Guorong, and Chen Lianban to teach autumn tea-making technology courses.

On March 2, 2007, the Anxi County Zhentian Peasant Tea Research Institute was established.

From October 12 to 16, 2007, the fifth China International Agricultural Products Fair, themed "Green Agriculture, Harmonious Rural Areas," was held at the Shandong Jinan International Exhibition Center. The Anxi County Zhentian Tea Cooperative participated in the exhibition of the Fujian Province One Village One Product Professional Cooperative Zone.

On April 20, 2008, Anxi Zhentian Pavilion Tieguanyin Tea Factory was officially established, marking the opening of Anxi Zhentian Tea Cooperative's external sales network, beginning a completely new marketing system, realizing the foundation of controlling pesticide residues, reducing costs, and improving quality for tea farmers, offering more affordable tea to a wide range of tea dealers and tea enthusiasts, making everyone buy with confidence and drink happily.

On August 6, 2008, Anxi Zhentian Pavilion Tieguanyin Tea Factory officially passed the national food quality and safety certification - QS quality certification.
