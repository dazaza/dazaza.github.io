The body of HTC Desire S (specifications, price, pictures, forum, software) in blue edition has been introduced to everyone before. The HTC Desire S, which is very popular among Internet users, also enjoys the reputation of "G12" and has a high level of attention. Today, the silver version has finally arrived. In terms of appearance, this upgraded version of HTC Desire removes the touchpad and makes great upgrades in CPU and RAM. The system has also been upgraded, and the overall quality has improved significantly, making it a perfect upgrade of "G7". The silver version of HTC Desire S is back again. Below, let's take a look at this popular phone with the editor.

The appearance of the silver HTC Desire S is very metallic.

The 3.7-inch screen makes the whole machine look very elegant.

HTC Desire S, like HTC Incredible S and HTC Wildfire S, belongs to the upgraded product. The main upgrade is the system to the latest Android 2.3 version, making it more fun to play with. The dimensions of the body are 115x59.8x11.63mm, which is moderate overall. The weight of 130g is mainstream. In terms of hardware, it has been upgraded to an ultra-large RAM of 768MB, using the second-generation Qualcomm 1GHz processor. The power consumption is significantly reduced, and both graphics and processor performance have increased geometrically, meeting the stringent requirements of users.

HTC Desire S is more fashionable based on HTC Desire.

The back design of HTC Desire S is classic and rounded.

The screen of HTC Desire S is a 3.7-inch SLCD capacitive screen, and the resolution remains at the 480*800 pixel level, providing good display results. It can be said to be a very classic screen configuration. There is a long strip speaker above the screen, which is the mainstream design of current HTC phones. On the right, there is a front camera that supports selfies and video calls. On the left side, there are two sensors that can automatically adjust the screen brightness and turn off the screen during a call.

The front panel of the body has a front camera.

Optical sensors and proximity sensors are very practical.

Below the device are still four Android system touch buttons. The awkward touchpad has finally been removed, making the whole machine look simpler. This improvement of HTC Desire S is consistent with HTC Wildfire S, moving towards the mainstream. It can be seen that the tail of HTC Desire S still tilts up, looking very fashionable. Under the smooth metal brushed body, it looks quite textured, and the metallic feel of the silver version is even stronger.

Four Android system capacitive touch keys.

The bottom still has a sexy upward tilt design inherited from all sides.

HTC Desire S has HTC's consistent design style, being relatively round overall. The design of each side of the body also continues HTC's tradition. The power button and 3.5mm headphone jack are located at the top of the body, while the right side of the body is still empty, lacking a camera shortcut key. However, this does conform to HTC's consistent minimalist style.

The top of the body has a power button and headphone jack.

The right side of the body is very simple.

On the left side of the body, the volume keys and micro USB interface are concentrated. Currently, the keys of HTC phones are all developing towards flatness. Of course, this feature is also reflected in the design of the earpiece, looking very techy. The key area is also invisibly expanded, providing a good feel. The micro USB interface has always been exposed, and this is no exception for HTC Desire S. Charging and data transfer are all realized through this interface.

The left side of the body integrates the volume keys and micro USB interface.

The tilted tail feels good with a 5-megapixel camera and flash added.

The entire back cover is relatively round and made of one-piece metal casting, providing a good feel. It can be seen that the top and bottom of the body are made of plastic material, but only the bottom part can be removed, which is somewhat similar to the design of HTC Flyer. HTC Desire S is equipped with a 5-megapixel rear camera, supporting 720P video recording and having an LED flash. Although it is slightly inferior compared to HTC Incredible S, it is still a commendable phone.

Rear 5-megapixel camera, supports flash.

HTC Desire S uses a 1450mAh lithium battery, which can meet the standby time for one day, with good overall performance. It must be admitted that removing the SIM card slot and micro SD card of HTC Desire S is a bit troublesome, and the plastic lock raises some concerns about its lifespan.
