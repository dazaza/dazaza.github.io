The implant material used in breast augmentation surgery is placed in the space created by stripping behind the breast. Therefore, the implant does not directly contact the breast but is instead encapsulated by a layer of fibrous tissue automatically formed around the breast. Consequently, if any changes are needed, the implant can be quickly and directly removed.

Moreover, the simplicity of the breast augmentation surgery is another significant feature. After the patient receives anesthesia from the anesthesiologist, the chief surgeon makes incisions in discreet or inconspicuous locations, accessing the natural space between the mammary gland or pectoralis major muscle. They then create a space large enough to accommodate the implant and insert it. This resolves all issues in one operation, with the surgery typically taking one to one and a half hours to complete.

The results of breast augmentation surgery are safe and reliable, with side effects being extremely rare. The outcome is immediate, offering a realistic feel and aesthetically pleasing breast appearance that can last long-term.
