Depressed scar: two self-developed plastic surgery technologies of Shanghai Tian Da have received national patent certification

by gf98bf2y on 2012-02-17 14:33:38

Recipient of the national patent technology for "mandibular angle reshaping" and "rhinoplasty"; China's renowned facial shaping expert, known as the Golden Finger.

On August 13th, an exhilarating piece of news reached Shanghai — Tian Da Medical Aesthetic Hospital received national patent certification for "mandibular angle reshaping" and "rhinoplasty." These two patents are new technologies specifically developed for "mandibular bone reshaping" and "rhinoplasty" surgeries. They have advanced these procedures to a level of precision measured in micrometers, turning personalized design and custom beauty solutions into reality.

"Mandibular angle reshaping" and "rhinoplasty" are among the most common facial cosmetic surgeries. From the early days of simple nose or chin augmentations to today, the demand for beauty and technical expertise has become increasingly detailed and stringent. In the past, China's medical aesthetic surgeries heavily relied on doctors' clinical experience. The question of "how can we make facial plastic surgery more precise and evidence-based?" has long troubled many Chinese plastic surgeons and was also one of the key reasons why Korean-style plastic surgery gained such prominence.

**Jiang Chunyi**

**Personal Profile**: Recipient of the national patent for mandibular angle and rhinoplasty surgeries, renowned in the field of plastic surgery; internationally acclaimed plastic surgery expert; Vice President of the Asia-Pacific Microplastic Surgery Association; Director of the International Department at Shanghai Tian Da Medical Aesthetic Hospital, and a member of the Chinese Physicians Association's Aesthetic and Plastic Surgery Branch. His surgical style emphasizes precision, detail, and accuracy, striving for harmonious aesthetics between features like the nose and the overall facial contour. He studied under Professor Ha Dong-ho, a renowned South Korean plastic surgeon, gaining a solid foundation in surgery as well as advanced plastic surgery techniques. He has applied endoscopic technology to aesthetic surgeries, developing highly popular minimally invasive beauty techniques. He maintains long-term academic exchanges with authoritative experts in plastic surgery from countries such as Korea, the United States, and France, and is frequently invited to attend the Asia-Pacific Microplastic Surgery Summit. He excels at combining the technical strengths of Chinese and Korean plastic surgeons, making significant contributions in clinical applications.

**Expert Insight**: According to senior experts from the national patent technology review department, plastic surgery reflects people’s pursuit of external appearance and inner spirit as life becomes increasingly enriched. After progressing through stages of experiential and rational development, China's plastic surgery techniques are beginning to mature. Combining the golden ratio of aesthetics with personalized design represents a key direction in modern plastic surgery technology. The promotion of these two patented technologies will turn personalized plastic surgery plans designed according to the golden ratio of aesthetics into reality, marking a very important breakthrough.

The "mandibular angle reshaping" and "rhinoplasty" patents are based on bionic principles, having undergone repeated scientific experiments and validations. When applied clinically, these technologies result in minimal damage, reduced bleeding, faster recovery, and their greatest advantage lies in their ability to perform surgeries with extremely high precision according to pre-surgical designs. This truly achieves personalized design, resulting in fine, lasting, harmonious, and natural surgical outcomes that can rival Korean plastic surgery techniques. These two technologies have now been exclusively licensed to Shanghai Tian Da Medical Aesthetic Hospital and are widely used in subsequent facial plastic surgeries.
