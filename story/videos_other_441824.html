1. Diet mainly consists of cereal foods with diverse combinations.

Many common psoriasis patients tend to consume more animal-based foods. The energy and fat provided by animal-based foods are too high, which is not conducive to the prevention of psoriasis. Therefore, psoriasis patients should follow the dietary principle of mainly consuming cereal foods with diverse combinations.

In addition, mental health, psoriasis patients should pay attention to the combination of coarse and fine grains, the health and beauty of sub-healthy populations through sweat steaming, and frequently eat some coarse grains and mixed grains. Otherwise, the vitamins, minerals, and other nutrients contained in the outer layer of grains, as well as dietary fiber, will be lost, reducing their nutritional value.

2. Eat more vegetables, fruits, and tuberous foods.

The incidence of heart disease induced by common psoriasis is 20% higher than that of the general population. Therefore, eating more vegetables, fruits, and tuberous foods plays a very important role in maintaining the cardiovascular health of psoriasis patients, enhancing their resistance, and reducing the occurrence of heart disease.

There are many types of vegetables with high nutritional value. They contain abundant carotene, vitamin B2, vitamin C, folic acid, minerals, dietary fiber, and natural antioxidants.

Fruits contain rich glucose, fruit acids, citric acid, malic acid, pectin. Red and yellow fruits are also rich in vitamin C and carotene, such as fresh jujubes, citrus fruits, persimmons, and apricots.

Tuberous foods contain abundant starch, dietary fiber, and various vitamins and minerals. However, in recent years, psoriasis patients have consumed fewer tuberous foods. They should eat more tuberous foods to supplement this aspect of nutrition.

3. Limit alcohol intake: Excessive drinking by psoriasis patients can reduce appetite, decrease food intake, and even lead to deficiencies in various nutrients. In severe cases, it can cause alcoholic cirrhosis and alcohol poisoning. Moderate drinking increases the risk of hypertension, stroke, and other diseases, and drinking can also induce the occurrence of psoriasis. Therefore, psoriasis patients should drink alcohol in moderation and try to avoid drinking altogether.

4. Regularly eat beans or their products and dairy products.

Bean foods contain large amounts of high-quality protein, unsaturated fatty acids, calcium, and vitamins B2, B2, and niacin. Psoriasis patients generally lack protein, and regularly eating soybeans and their products can not only replenish the above nutrients but also avoid the adverse effects of consuming excessive meat on the body.

Dairy products are an excellent natural source of calcium. In addition to containing abundant high-quality proteins and vitamins, they also have a high calcium content. The calcium content in the diet of psoriasis patients is generally low, reaching only about half of the recommended supply. Therefore, the proportion of dairy products in the diet should be increased.
