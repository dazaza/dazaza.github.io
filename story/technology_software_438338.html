Butto's Assassination Shocks the World, Kang Hui Live Broadcasts Without Panic

Author: Haoge

At 22:30 on the evening of December 27th, CCTV News Channel interrupted regular programming to launch a live broadcast titled "Breaking News," with Kang Hui hosting an almost two-hour special on Benazir Bhutto's assassination. This was a classic case of CCTV's emergency mechanism being activated in response to a sudden event, participating in news competition through large-scale live specials. It reflects the maturity of the news channel and is an effective live broadcasting effort to align the channel with international standards amidst intense international news competition.

Live rolling broadcasts are a common practice for the news channel, and responding to breaking events with emergency live broadcasts demonstrates the overall strength of the station. We found that CCTV News Channel also faces fierce competition for news resources, with Phoenix TV and other Chinese channels as strong competitors. The live coverage of the September 11 attacks in New York by Phoenix TV gave a strong stimulus to the Chinese television news industry. Under the backdrop of heated news competition and diversified media formats, how television news channels respond has become an urgent issue. After launching a national news channel aligned with international standards, live broadcasting capabilities have become a key point of comparison. Activating the emergency mechanism to interrupt regular programming and launch live specials is an effective way to strengthen authority. The timely, meticulously planned, and swift response in launching the Bhutto assassination special reflects the mature operations of the news channel.

The live broadcast of breaking news on the news channel has unique advantages in integrating news resources and can present its exclusive voice. The "Breaking News" Bhutto special faced significant challenges due to a lack of footage and highlighted the shortage of news commentators. Over the course of two hours, the weight of the host and guest contributions became quite significant given the limited relevant on-site footage. How to effectively integrate news resources? CCTV mobilized and utilized its own correspondents, Xinhua News Agency correspondents, China International Radio correspondents, and concentrated on Beijing's international affairs experts, which is a major advantage unique to a national-level station compared to Phoenix TV. However, we still appear lacking in terms of popular, well-branded current affairs commentators like Phoenix TV’s "three-horse chariot." The use of international affairs experts in the "Breaking News" Bhutto special effectively compensated for this shortfall. With detailed analysis from South Asia experts, comprehensive and authoritative interpretations helped viewers eliminate confusion and satisfy their curiosity.

Live breaking news broadcasts test the host's abilities; experience, knowledge, and psychological resilience are essential prerequisites for ensuring smooth live broadcasts. The host for the "Breaking News" Bhutto special was Kang Hui, the new anchor of "News Broadcast." The three live connections in the first segment posed a great challenge to Kang Hui. His questioning and time management displayed maturity and expertise. After Bhutto's assassination, due to unclear news information and a lack of authoritative sources, even stationed reporters were investigating and verifying the truth from multiple angles. To ensure the live broadcast continued, Kang Hui had to keep asking effective questions continuously. Whenever the stationed reporter seemed to run out of things to say, Kang Hui could always come up with new questions to keep the reporter talking. This was a strict test of the host's adaptability and knowledge. Kang Hui performed flawlessly in handling the live connections, making the entire segment flow smoothly without any fatal cold moments. Liang Jianzeng once revealed in a business lecture that Bai Yansong was chosen for important live broadcasts because of his strong ability to improvise and comment in real-time, avoiding cold moments, making such live broadcasts more reassuring. He also highly praised Shui Junyi's extensive international knowledge. Compared to these two, although Kang Hui started as a broadcaster, his rapid progress is evident. From Kang Hui's performance, it is clear that he excels in live adaptability, and his accumulated international knowledge ensures smooth questioning and dialogue. His strong hosting skills compensate for any gaps in knowledge.

Kang Hui's superior performance in the live broadcast makes the concept of an anchor clearer. Although Kang Hui hosts live broadcasts in "News Broadcast," it differs somewhat from the internationally recognized anchor concept. An anchor must play a leading role in the program, able to decide the process and content. In the Bhutto live special, Kang Hui became an indispensable component of the entire program. His organization, linking, and mobilization, along with his questioning, interviews, reporting, and commentary, were all essential parts of the program process. Without such an anchor, the program would not be able to proceed. The level of the anchor's performance is the fundamental guarantee for the success of the live broadcast.

Behind the successful launch of the "Breaking News" Bhutto special, we see the increasingly mature operations of the news channel, a product of updated television news concepts and a reflection of enhanced competitive strength. We look forward to more rich live content being launched to meet the public's most direct and authoritative news consumption needs.
