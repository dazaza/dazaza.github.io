The fat pig that fell off the truck caused more than ten people to fight with weapons in a struggle

by anonymous on 2012-02-25 10:46:33

Our Baoding correspondent reports (by reporter ZhiKun Guo and correspondent ZiHan Yang) that a pig fell from a truck on the highway during transportation. The attempt to retrieve the pig led to a brawl involving more than ten people armed with weapons, causing a near two-kilometer traffic jam on the highway. Fortunately, the highway police intervened promptly, eventually calming the situation down.

Yesterday afternoon, a driver from Wangdu bought over 20 pigs near Shijiazhuang and planned to sell them back in Wangdu. He was driving a medium-sized truck with license plate "Ji FR6999" northward when due to insufficient protection, a pig weighing over 150 kilograms fell off the truck at the Dingzhou section of the Jingzhu Expressway. Unaware of this, the driver continued north. The pig landed on the highway and was noticed by two Northeastern truck drivers who were passing by. Several people jumped out of their vehicles and quickly loaded the pig into the trailer of a truck with the license plate "Tianjin AC3938". This process was witnessed by another passing car driver who then drove up to the Wangdu-licensed truck ahead, informing the driver that someone had picked up his pig.

Upon hearing this, the Wangdu driver became extremely anxious since each pig is worth about 2000 yuan. If he couldn't retrieve it, not only would his efforts be wasted, but he would also suffer financial losses. He immediately slowed down, waiting for the truck that had taken his pig. A few minutes later, the Wangdu driver finally spotted the "Tianjin AC3938" OuMann truck. He immediately waved at the driver, asking him to pull over, but instead of stopping, the other driver accelerated further north. In desperation, the Wangdu driver sped up, overtook the OuMann truck, and forcibly stopped it at kilometer 197 of the Jingzhu Expressway. After stopping, the Wangdu driver grabbed an iron rod and rushed to the OuMann truck. Without a word, he poked through the window towards the driver, injuring the driver's arm. Seeing their comrade hurt, several men from another following truck jumped out with iron rods and began beating the Wangdu driver. Outnumbered, the Wangdu driver jumped over the highway railing and ran into nearby farmland, shouting insults at the other side while calling his family for support.

Due to the large size of both trucks, they blocked the highway lanes almost entirely, forcing subsequent vehicles to stop. Within a short time, nearly two kilometers of the highway were congested. Observing the fight on the highway and the resulting traffic jam, a passer-by driver immediately reported the incident to the highway police.

When the call came in, Deputy Team Leader Fang Pinghe and Officer Liu Guojun of the Dingzhou team had just finished handling a traffic accident near the Wangdu station exit. Five minutes later, they arrived at the scene, first ordering the Wangdu and Northeastern drivers to move their vehicles to the parking bay to restore smooth traffic flow. They then mediated between the parties involved. During this time, three small cars with Wangdu plates suddenly arrived at the scene, with over ten men armed with sticks getting out. It turned out that these men had come to support the Wangdu driver, creating a highly tense situation. Under the criticism and mediation of the officers, both sides eventually agreed to reconcile - the Northeastern driver returned the pig, and the Wangdu driver compensated the injured Northeastern driver with some medical expenses.
