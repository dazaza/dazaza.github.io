Introduction:

The heavy metal band AC/DC was formed in Sydney, Australia in 1974. They successfully made their way from Australia to conquer the American rock scene. The band members wore school uniform shorts while performing heavy metal rock, establishing a unique position in fans' hearts. With their explosive energy, they fused blues, rock, and contemporary pop music into hard rock, becoming one of its representative bands. This Blu-ray captures AC/DC's live concert in Madrid in 1996.

【Band Introduction】

AC/DC: Perhaps what distinguishes AC/DC from all other hard rock bands is that they care about nothing but playing exciting and stimulating rock 'n' roll. Hard rock is indeed a strange industry where a band that has produced the heaviest THRASH METAL refuses to follow conventions but suddenly releases an album full of short, sweet, and polished songs ready for radio play. Greed and arrogance often drive musicians into this peculiar industry. Many bands exist primarily to showcase the personal talents of some guitar virtuoso, vocal powerhouse, or master songwriter. Of course, there are also bands that attempt to express intelligence, knowledge, or even philosophical thoughts through their songs.

However, AC/DC stands as an exception outside all these norms; they don't care about any of those things. They simply play the most straightforward rock 'n' roll. Their songs leave deep impressions with the purest form of rock imaginable, which you can't do without at any party or sports event. Even though they limit themselves to pure drum, bass, and guitar rock, their music still exudes a peculiar charm with solid rhythms, cool lead guitars, and the ordinary melodies sung by the lead singer with his chainsaw-like voice.

Although AC/DC's songs are often covered by amateur bands, no band can match their live performance feel or learn their uncompromising attitude. The band's first lead singer, the legendary Bon Scott, once proudly said a famous line: "Don't talk about the bands around us... none of them matters." In fact, AC/DC has a truly unique look and vibe, especially Angus Young, who marks himself with short-legged school uniforms and crazy one-legged jumps, bringing his skills to their fullest potential on stage with noisy riffs and ear-splitting solos—things high school teachers wouldn't want students to hear.

In the mid-1970s, after the formation of the band by the Angus and Malcolm brothers, it was Bon Scott who started turning up the volume and invented that unprecedentedly harsh sound in rock history. Unfortunately, Scott passed away due to alcohol poisoning in 1980, leaving the band facing an existential crisis. After recruiting the new lead singer Brian Johnson, their first album "Back in Black" featured a simple all-black cover in memory of Scott. Unexpectedly, this album became extremely successful and ultimately ranked among the best classic albums in rock history. It appears in any list of the top 100 greatest rock albums ever made. Brian Johnson carried on Scott's mission, continuing to make simple and direct rock music with the Young brothers into the late 1990s.

The 1997 release "Bonfire" was undoubtedly a feast for the loyal fans of AC/DC who have followed them for over two decades. The album includes unpublished songs, B-sides, and even early live recordings from their time in Sydney. These sounds are exactly what fans wanted to hear and serve as the band's best tribute to Scott's unforgettable performances.

For a band with countless classic tracks like "Back in Black," "You Shook Me All Night Long," "Big Balls," "For Those About to Rock (We Salute You)," "Money Talks," and nearly 20 excellent albums such as "High Voltage," "Back in Black," "Powerage," and "Who Made Who," AC/DC provides the best proof that a good band doesn't need to change itself or conform to anything to withstand the test of trends. Screw the record industry.
