Regarding postpartum hair loss

by anonymous on 2011-06-16 15:36:27

After the anticipation of ten months of pregnancy, accompanied by the heavenly cry of a newborn, a new mother begins her great new journey. However, amidst the joy, she suddenly finds more hair left on her comb and hands than usual, her front hairline starts to recede, her hair begins to thin out, and the烦恼of hair loss comes unexpectedly... Why does having a baby make it so easy for new mothers to lose their hair?

This is what we commonly refer to as "postpartum hair loss." The rate at which women's hair renews is related to the level of estrogen in their bodies; when estrogen levels are high, the renewal rate slows down; when estrogen levels are low, the renewal rate speeds up. "Postpartum hair loss" is very common, with about one-third of women experiencing hair loss four months after giving birth. The reasons for this phenomenon are roughly related to the following four factors.

Changes in hormone levels: During pregnancy, the amount of estrogen secreted by pregnant women increases compared to normal times, slowing down the rate of hair loss and thus extending the lifespan of hair. After delivery, the body's estrogen levels return to normal, causing the "overdue" hair to fall out en masse, while new hair hasn't grown in yet, leading to a situation where there's not enough new growth to replace the lost hair, resulting in postpartum hair loss.

Stress from psychological factors: From a psychological perspective, after becoming pregnant, especially for the first time, women often experience significant mental stress, such as worrying about whether childbirth will be painful, if they can return to their previous state, whether they'll have a boy or girl, if the child will have any issues, etc. After giving birth, new mothers transition from an excited state to fatigue, and their emotions shift from high to relatively low. Some new mothers may become emotionally fragile and anxious, leading to dysfunction of the cerebral cortex, autonomic nervous system disorders, and even dysregulation of the nerves controlling blood vessels in the scalp, reducing blood supply to the scalp and causing hair to fall out due to malnutrition. Additionally, breastfeeding and caring for the baby can affect sleep, all of which contribute to hair loss. Worse still, when hair falls out, new mothers may feel worried and restless, creating a new source of mental stress that exacerbates the cycle, making hair loss increasingly severe.

Nutritional imbalance: Pregnancy and childbirth are extremely taxing processes for women. Postpartum recovery and breastfeeding require substantial nutritional supplementation. Some women eat a monotonous diet during pregnancy, failing to meet the nutritional needs of both the mother and fetus. During the postpartum breastfeeding period, if the new mother has poor digestion and absorption or eats a monotonous, selective diet, or even diets to maintain a slim figure postpartum, it becomes easy to suffer from nutritional deficiencies or imbalances, leading to insufficient supplies of protein, vitamins, or minerals within the body, affecting hair growth and metabolism.

Improper hair care: Influenced by traditional concepts, some new mothers dare not wash or comb their hair during the confinement period. This allows sebum secretions and dirt to accumulate on the scalp, affecting blood supply to the head and easily causing folliculitis or scalp infections, thereby increasing the likelihood of hair loss.

How should one deal with postpartum hair loss? Postpartum hair loss mostly belongs to physiological phenomena and doesn't need excessive worry. Precious Chinese herbs like Polygonum multiflorum (He Shou Wu) and Angelica sinensis (Dang Gui) not only replenish blood and nourish yin but also enrich essence and marrow, greatly improving postpartum hair loss.

In daily life, attention should be paid to the following points:

Firstly, relax your mind. It is essential to maintain a cheerful mood during pregnancy and lactation. Recognize that postpartum hair loss is a temporary process. Have confidence that the hair loss will stop, and beautiful hair will grow back quickly. Avoid being overly tense, as anxiety can only worsen the degree of hair loss.

Secondly, pay attention to balanced nutrition and strengthen your diet. Don't be picky about food, avoid favoring certain foods, or abstain from eating certain types of food. Eat more fresh vegetables, fruits, animal proteins, seafood, legumes, eggs, etc., to meet the nutritional needs of your hair and body.

Thirdly, choose mild, suitable shampoo products and wash your hair regularly. Frequently comb your hair with a wooden comb or massage and stimulate your scalp rhythmically with your fingers to promote scalp circulation and facilitate hair metabolism.
