by wtahtterfu on 2012-03-01 03:23:51

The match against Spain was originally scheduled to take place on the 11th because it was expected to be a nice day for a walk. However, due to Barcelona (Spain), which has many players from the Spanish national team, playing in the King's Cup on the 9th, the RFEF (Royal Spanish Football Federation) requested to shift the schedule by one day. Since Franco Baldini, the right-hand man of Coach Capello, permitted this change, Pierfilippo, his youngest son, lost the opportunity to attend the sunny stage on the same day. On the 10th, there will be an official practice session at the match venue. After seeing photos taken by the staff in advance, Okazaki (Stuttgart) described the rough pitch as "almost like dirt, with patches of grass here and there." Due to snowfall, the condition of the pitch may worsen further. It is also necessary to quickly adapt to these conditions.

The Japanese national soccer team arrived in Dushanbe, the capital of Tajikistan, on the evening of the 9th, where they will play their fourth match in the World Cup Asian third qualifying round against Tajikistan on the 11th. In Dushanbe, snow usually begins to accumulate around December, but this year it came about a month earlier, surprising even local Tajik men who remarked, "It's quite unusually cold." The forecast for the 11th predicts a high temperature of around 15 degrees Celsius. There will be significant temperature differences, making health management crucial. Captain Makoto Hasebe (Wolfsburg) stated that "we want to prepare our minds and bodies for the completely different climate and environment," aiming to reach the match in perfect condition.

According to Reuters, Coach Fabio Capello will miss his son’s wedding, as he has a friendly international match against Spain on the 12th. Doha, where the pre-match camp was held four days prior, experienced daytime temperatures exceeding 30 degrees Celsius. After a chartered flight lasting over three hours, upon landing in Dushanbe, the team found themselves in a silver-white world. Although the players had been informed about the cold, they couldn't adjust in time, as the approximate temperature of 3 degrees Celsius was colder than expected. They could see their breath turning white as they repeatedly mentioned how cold it was while boarding the bus from the airport to their lodging. At the airport, approximately 20 Japanese people, including staff from the Japanese Embassy in Tajikistan, welcomed them.

Pierfilippo commented on the situation regarding the transportation industry's development and the increasing number of free services in recent years to British media: "First and foremost, my father is a soccer coach. That's been the case since I was a child, and occasionally, this profession involves unreasonable demands that must be accepted." He showed understanding for his father's career.

Other newspapers have reported similar information using the same sources.
