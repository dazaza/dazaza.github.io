Today, I was in a QQ group where I saw several friends talking about how their websites sold advertisements through the Ali Mama platform. A few of their small sites sold ad spaces for 9.9 yuan, one IT site sold for 500 yuan. Looking at their excitement, I don't know whether to be happy for them or feel sorrow. I currently run an online accounting and financial management software website (www.coko365.com). I once considered selling ads through Ali Mama because the CaiKe Online site I operate has several thousand unique IPs daily. However, after careful consideration, I abandoned this idea.

The media, internet professionals, and many kind-hearted people have only seen Ma Yun's successful side but haven't seriously analyzed the negative aspects. I believe that "Ma Yun is a killer! He is疯狂ly slaughtering China's small and medium-sized enterprises and small and medium-sized websites!" If you don't believe me, carefully read my article and then reflect on it.

First: Alibaba is the killer of China's small and medium-sized enterprises.

Why do I say this? Perhaps those loyal members of Alibaba will curse me or even try to harm me, but I believe that in the near future, they will resent Alibaba. In its early stages, Alibaba seized the opportunity when many businesses were struggling to increase their operations, so Alibaba succeeded. However, as more and more companies joined, and with some policies implemented by Alibaba, many businesses on Alibaba have suffered heavy blows. Why? Because companies on Alibaba, in order to secure business via the internet, engage in fierce competition and price wars. Alibaba's introduction of online procurement, inventory procurement, and TrustPass services has led to many companies' product prices being fully exposed on Alibaba, making both competitors and customers very well-informed. Many traditional companies complain that profits are decreasing and customers are becoming more savvy. Actually, Alibaba's TrustPass and bidding rankings have caused many companies to engage in vicious price wars. Some traditional company bosses I've met all blame Alibaba, blaming the many companies on Alibaba for not adhering to industry rules, engaging in vicious competition just to make money, and disrupting industries and markets.

Second: Ali Mama is a malicious killer of China's small and medium-sized networks.

I belong to the small site category in the network, so I see Ali Mama as a malicious killer of small and medium-sized websites. Although Ali Mama took advantage of the hard work of many small site owners who wanted to make some money, Ali Mama launched such a platform to allow clients and websites to conduct online transactions. Naturally, Ali Mama also introduced a set of equations for calculating advertising fees, but if small site owners think carefully, how unreasonable these equations are. Everyone puts in different costs and efforts into building their sites, and each site targets different customer groups. For example, targeting a general audience (meaning men, women, old, and young can all use it) is much harder than creating a specialized online accounting site for those who like bookkeeping and finance. So, the costs and efforts are different, but when calculating advertising fees, it's the same. How laughable and unreasonable it is for Ali Mama to introduce such an advertising fee calculation equation. On the Ali Mama platform, many small sites are auctioning off ad spaces every day, priced according to the calculation method introduced by Ali Mama. Many sites are also engaged in price wars, which is a painful thing for us site owners. Prices are fully exposed, leaving us powerless. Our hard work goes towards merely creating value for Ali Mama, and we gain very little from it. We end up just doing work for others.

Although on the surface, Ma Yun has brought blessings to China's small and medium-sized enterprises, helping many SMEs develop, and Ma Yun has given hope for profit to many small and medium-sized websites in China, all of this is built on the premise of our websites making huge sacrifices, and it is not fair. The market is inherently unfair. The winner takes all, and those left standing also take what they can. We ultimately have no choice but to accept it.
