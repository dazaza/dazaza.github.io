Synopsis:

That summer, with two eccentric old men.

The story takes place in the early 1960s in Texas. Fourteen-year-old Walter (Haley Joel Osment) has two extraordinary uncles who live in the countryside of Texas, which is probably the last place Walter wants to go. Unexpectedly, as soon as school was out for summer break, his mother Molly (Kyra Sedgwick) kicked him out of the house. Always single, Molly wanted to enjoy a sweet two-person life with her new boyfriend, so she sent her son Walter to the distant countryside. At a young age, Walter had no choice but to pack his simple luggage and spend what he expected to be a happy summer vacation with two crazy old men. He would have to wait until his mother came to pick him up before Walter could be free.

The quirky and rough-tempered Harve (Robert Duvall) and Garth Mckin (Michael Caine) are Walter's two uncles on the country farm. It is rumored that they were once bank robbers who were hidden by the Mafia after committing crimes, or even participated in wars when they were young.

These hard-to-distinguish rumors are confusing. One thing for sure is that Harve and Garth McKin always seem to obtain the cash they need from mysterious channels. Once, Walter found a photo of a young Garth McKin with a beautiful woman in a wooden box and curiously asked his uncle who the pretty woman in the photo was.

Gradually, the uncles began to tell Walter stories about their youth when Texas was still in a chaotic era. These stories deeply attracted young Walter, who fantasized about the colorful scenes in those stories. Men rode horses, defending their survival with swords in their hands, winning the hearts of beautiful young women. His two uncles were heroes of that time, experiencing adventurous days that people could only dream of.

Unintentionally, the past of the uncles made young Walter discover another way to see the world, a new world full of true adventures. The rich life experiences of the uncles deeply influenced young Walter, showing him a different world where people respect and admire bravery more than money and power. Similarly, by telling their own youthful stories to their nephew, Harve and Garth began to view their own lives from a new perspective and rediscovered the joy of old age...

[About the Film]

Childhood Memories

Like other screenwriters, when Tim McCanles finished writing the story of "Secondhand Lions," he started considering who would play the three main characters: 14-year-old Walter and the two quirky rural old men. "When I was a child, I spent many summers with my grandfather, who also had a very rough character, just like the 'uncles' in the movie," Tim McCanles explained his idea behind making this film. "However, when my grandfather told stories about the past, his tone was very gentle, as if it came from deep underground. Those stories made me understand as a child what it means to be a real man. What I want to express in 'Secondhand Lions' is how older men tell their stories to children and influence each other's lives."

When considering who should play these two special "old men" roles in the film, Tim first thought of Robert Duvall and Michael Caine, both actors full of stories. For the role of the child, Haley Joel Osment was undoubtedly the most suitable choice. As a new generation of talented child stars in Hollywood, Haley has an unusually mature demeanor.

Tim McCanles, who comes from a screenwriting background, values the storytelling aspect of films greatly. Both "The Iron Giant" (The Iron Giant), which he wrote, and "Dancer Texas" (Daner Texas), which he directed, are excellent films. Tim usually works as a screenwriter and rarely directs personally. "Secondhand Lions" is actually Tim's second feature-length drama as a director. Interestingly, another Tim (Tim Burton) also directed a film this year telling a man's past story, "Big Fish" (Big Fish), which will be released at the end of this year.

New Line Cinema originally planned to release the film in November this year. Perhaps because of the consideration of the summer theme of the plot, the release date was changed to September 26th, then moved forward a week to September 19th, opening in 3013 theaters across North America. A plot-driven film being released in so many theaters shows how much New Line Pictures values this film.

"Little Forrest" and Two Oscar Winners

This is a film about men, and the three male actors playing the main roles—"Little Forrest" Haley Joel Osment and the two elders (Michael Caine and Robert Duvall)—have become the biggest highlights of this film due to their brilliant achievements in acting. Among these three, Michael Caine has won the Oscar for Best Actor twice, Robert Duvall has won one Oscar statuette, and Haley Joel Osment was nominated for an Oscar at the age of 12. Each of them can shoulder the heavy responsibility alone, and their collaboration in "Secondhand Lions" is bound to create an impressive impact.

Since playing the role of "little Forrest" in "Forrest Gump" in 1994 at the age of six, Haley Joel Osment has displayed remarkable acting talent and been admired by many filmmakers. In just a few short years, he starred in 26 films and TV series, becoming one of Hollywood's hottest child stars. Later, his stunning performance in "The Sixth Sense" became a key factor in the success of this suspenseful film, earning him a nomination for Best Supporting Actor at the 2000 Academy Awards, making him the youngest performer ever nominated for an acting award. In 2001, Haley starred in "A.I. Artificial Intelligence," where his flawless performance once again earned him praise as a prodigy of Hollywood, captivating countless teenage girls.

In this comedy film with a nostalgic style, "Secondhand Lions," Haley fits perfectly into the role of the precocious boy "Walter." Innocent and curious, Walter is captivated by the stories told by his uncles and grows from them, learning respect and courage. Haley's acting talent shines through in the film, and he says this talent comes from his actor father, Eugene Osment. "I think all my acting instincts come from my father's genetics," Haley said. "My father is a great actor who performed on stage for 12 years. When I was very young, I discovered my passion for acting, all thanks to my father."

[Review]

The Joyful Lives of the Old Guys

Two brothers who once galloped like cowboys in the chaotic era now guard a rural farm in their old age. The money they robbed in their youth is casually thrown into a dark little room. They till the land in front of their house, catch fish in the river with long guns, and bask in the sun on the porch of the wooden house—this is the joyful life of the two "uncles" in the film. They maintain their manly spirit even in old age, defending their own lives with dignity and courage. Michael Caine and Robert Duvall seem to truly embody the two quirky old men in the film. As actors, they both played many roles of galloping horsemen wielding knives and guns in their youth. In their old age, they returned to tranquility, and their life experiences coincidentally align with the "uncles" in "Secondhand Lions." Therefore, you can't help but like these two quirky old men and respect them as you would your own grandfather.

Haley, who has grown into a teenager, is no longer as cute as before, and some of his childish qualities are disappearing. However, instead of lamenting, we should look forward to the surprises he might bring. At least in this film, we can still see many of Haley's innocent shadows from before.

The two old men living comfortably in the countryside in "Secondhand Lions" easily remind us of "Heaven and Earth" released last month. That film also tells the story of brotherhood between two old men, and one of the main characters is also Robert Duvall. However, the feelings conveyed by the two films are completely different. On the surface, "Heaven and Earth" is restless and filled with adrenaline, but inside, it reflects the loneliness of heroes. In contrast, "Secondhand Lions" is peaceful and comfortable, and inside lies the courage and dignity of heroes, reflecting the pleasant sentiments of two old men enjoying their twilight years amidst changing times.
