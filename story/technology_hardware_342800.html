According to a report quoted by the US World Night Post on the 14th night from Hong Kong media, China will include the research and production of its fourth-generation aircraft into the '12th Five-Year Plan (2011-2015)'. A foreign aviation expert expressed "great confidence" in the fourth-generation aircraft, stating that "many technologies should surpass Russia's fifth-generation fighter - the T-50". He said that China's fourth-generation aircraft are not targeted against Russia's fifth-generation aircraft but rather not primarily aimed at competing with America’s F-35 and F-22 fighters.

The report mentioned that China's fourth-generation fighter, known as the 'Ribbon', is one of the most anticipated weapons currently in development. Presently, the United States is the only country that possesses fourth-generation fighters, with the main representatives being the American F-22 'Raptor' and the F-35 'Lightning' fighter jets. Russia completed the test flight of its fifth-generation T-50 fighter in 2010. The F-22A owned by the United States was not only the first to enter service but also the most expensive aircraft, costing $140 million per unit.

The report cited an unnamed authoritative aviation expert who stated that due to the many connections between China's aviation industry and Russia, 'China's fourth-generation aircraft will be relatively similar to the T-50'. This expert indicated that compared to Russia's T-50, China's fourth-generation aircraft in terms of electronic equipment and onboard systems would be 'absolutely no less than Russia's', while other aspects, including the aircraft's shape, would be 'mutually advantageous'.

The expert stated that the current biggest technical challenge for China's fourth-generation aircraft is the stealth issue, 'and resolving this issue, we can say with great confidence, it will be done better than the T-50'. He added that the primary competitors for China's fourth-generation aircraft should be America's F-22 and F-35.

The report also quoted Zhang Jigao, the deputy chief designer of China's J-10 fighter, saying that given China's economic strength and research capabilities, the ultimate goal of research will reach or even exceed the level of the F-22. However, this needs to be verified through flight tests and further proven through actual combat.

Worldwide commentary suggests that China has become one of the top three countries entering the development phase of fourth-generation fighters. In terms of ground force deployment and indigenous fighter technology, China will slightly surpass countries like the UK, France, and other Eastern European nations. Although there is still a gap compared to the U.S. and Russia, this gap is gradually narrowing and becoming increasingly smaller.
