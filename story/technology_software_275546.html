"Room prices can easily reach close to ten thousand yuan, making it difficult for small characters striving in big cities to find a place to stay." Some people lamented. A netizen named "Walking in the World" posted on the forum calling himself a member of the "McDull Tribe". He expressed his own views regarding the complaints online: "I am a student from a rural background who has been struggling in Beijing for many years. I understand that I can only rely on myself." He believed that as long as one stays positive and perseveres, the "McDull Tribe" can also lead a happy life.

Many netizens of the "McDull Tribe" believe that the lyrics of "Ordinary People's Song" - "When did you see this world change for people..." - best reflect their state of mind. "Being without support in a big city is indeed somewhat helpless, but complaining only adds to the troubles. The world will not change for an individual; only by continuously striving oneself can happiness be achieved."

The three "nos": "No waiting", "No asking", "No relying" are the primary standards for judging the "McDull Tribe". The post-80s "McDull Tribe" completely relies on their down-to-earth efforts to secure a place in big cities. They do not have the connections of their elders to rely on, nor do they ask for material help from their families after starting work. Many members of the "McDull Tribe" regularly send money home to support their parents.

In the animated series, McDull is an ordinary little pig. It does not come from a wealthy family or possess extraordinary intelligence, and is so ordinary that it goes unnoticed. But McDull does not let this bother him. It works hard with both feet firmly on the ground, is a complete optimist, and leads a simple yet happy life. In reality, there is a group of people similar to McDull. Born after the 80s, they come from humble backgrounds, sweat it out in big cities, and silently strive to realize their dreams step by step. They are the objects referred to by the new term on the internet - the "McDull Tribe", specifically referring to the post-80s strivers in big cities.

Dreaming of owning a house: The "McDull Tribe" hopes to carve out a piece of sky for themselves, and rooting in big cities is their greatest dream. Some take on multiple jobs to save money for a down payment, while others cut back on expenses to pay off their mortgages, dedicating all their efforts towards this goal.

Positive and optimistic: Although the immense pressure of life often makes them feel suffocated, the "McDull Tribe" always maintains the optimism seen in the animated McDull: not succumbing to reality, difficulties and setbacks cannot extinguish their simple and beautiful dreams. "Surviving calmly amidst fierce competition, finding one's own path in life."

After the term "McDull Tribe" was born, it quickly became popular on the internet, with netizens summarizing several characteristics of this group.

The so-called "McDull Tribe" refers to a group of ordinary little people born in the 80s. Unlike the "rich second generation," they do not come from prominent families. They struggle hard in cities, working for their livelihood, and are typical grassroots classes. Everything must be done personally, and the dreams of buying a house and a car can only be realized through their own bit-by-bit efforts.
