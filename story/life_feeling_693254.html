The Love Affair of the Rose

In the second year of junior high school, there was a very flirtatious boy who fell in love with a girl. The boy decided to be devoted and sincere to the girl from then on. But the girl did not accept his love. Her good friend quietly told the boy that the girl had actually liked him since the first year of junior high school.

But his actions had hurt the girl deeply. The girl was happy to know he liked her but was afraid it was just a game for him. After hearing this, the boy's eyes turned red. He deeply regretted his past behavior. That night, after returning home (where no one else was), he took a fruit knife, knelt on the balcony, looked up at the starry sky, and said: He had made up his mind to sincerely love the girl and would never be flirtatious again, never changing for the rest of his life. Blood dripped from the knife onto the ground, and the boy seemed to see the girl nodding in the blood. The boy smiled.

After that day, the boy changed. He no longer hung out with a large group of girls or gave rides home to other girls on his bicycle. The boy became obedient.

He always silently stayed by the girl's side, helping her with homework and escorting her home. When it rained and the girl didn't have an umbrella, the boy immediately handed her his umbrella and rushed into the rain with his head down... Eventually, the girl slowly became moved and believed in the boy's love.

One day, it was the boy's birthday. The girl and their friends went to celebrate the boy's birthday. The park was a small island, and the riverside at night was especially beautiful and peaceful. They stood on the bridge, watching the distant rainbow lights reflected in the water causing ripples, basking in the humid wind, and listening to the dripping snow melting off the small bridge. Suddenly, the girl realized that the boy had unknowingly taken her hand. Her face turned completely red, and she instinctively pulled her hand back.

But the boy held on tightly, his grip firm and warm even in the cold winter... "Let's go play somewhere," the boy broke the silence. "Alright! Let's go to the haunted house!" the friends cheered. In fact, they had all secretly conspired to leave the boy and the girl behind to see what would happen. As expected, the haunted house was terrifying, with dim lighting, reflective bloody words, and monsters jumping out unexpectedly. The girl was scared stiff, lying behind the boy screaming. The boy patted the girl's head and laughed, saying, "Little fool, as long as I'm here, no one will harm you. Don't be afraid!"

After leaving the haunted house, their friends had disappeared without a trace. It was just as well. They strolled along the forest path, where the girl stopped the boy, saying she wanted to give him a present. The girl tied a scarf around the boy's neck. The boy naturally embraced the girl, but the girl shyly pulled away. The boy suddenly produced a bouquet of roses, which were radiant under the bright moonlight. "I like you."

The boy confessed, and the girl panicked and cried. The boy gently wiped away her tears... Their friends suddenly appeared from somewhere unknown, cheering and scattering rose petals into the air. Under the bright moonlight, illuminated by the stars, the fresh red roses shone against the snow. Was that love? Perhaps. It was truly beautiful...

(Chapter Two) The girl leaned against the boy's chest and said that now he was only 15 years old, and she was only 14. They were still too young, and the girl didn't want anything to happen too early...

The girl asked the boy to wait for her for ten years. If after ten years, the boy still loved her, she would definitely marry him. But if the boy fell in love with another girl, please tell her so she could break up with him. She said she didn't want to be dumped by others...

The boy quickly covered the girl's mouth. He would definitely wait for the girl for ten years and even said he would be willing to wait a lifetime... The girl shook her head and sighed, saying she didn't believe in forever. Later, the boy and the girl both passed the entrance exam to the same high school in another city. The boy still loved the girl, but he found that the girl was becoming increasingly cold towards him. He was very worried and discovered that the girl was getting close to another boy. He was very troubled, not knowing what to do. Maybe the girl had changed her heart, maybe that boy was really excellent, maybe...

Friends came to comfort him, saying that boy wasn't better than him in any way, and the girl wouldn't like that boy. The girl really just considered that boy as a friend.

But he couldn't help but doubt. Coincidentally, he and that boy had birthdays on the same day. The boy invited the girl to meet at the KFC door at 2:00 PM to play together, saying they wouldn't part until they met. The girl agreed. At 1:50 PM, the boy hurried to the KFC door waiting for the girl. Tick-tock, tick-tock, the needle circled round and round, and the sky gradually darkened. It was 7:00 PM, and the girl still hadn't come...

The boy lost hope. He seemed to see the girl playing around with that boy. Lightning flashed, and rain began to fall. The boy remained unmoved. "Never part! Haha! Never part!" Tears mixed with rain slid down his face as the boy screamed to the heavens: "Tell me why? Who can answer him? Who will answer him?" The boy wandered the streets and was accidentally hit by an ambulance. In his blurred consciousness, he thought he saw the girl.

(Chapter Three) The boy was hospitalized, fortunately not too severely injured. Classmates came to visit the boy, but no matter how he looked around, he still couldn't find the girl's figure... After recovering, the boy left quietly, and no one knew. The boy said, leaving this sorrowful city might make him stop being sad...

During the day, the boy filled his time with endless homework, trying to numb himself and forget about the girl, but he failed. Every night when it was quiet, the boy would lean against the window sill, looking at the moon, touching the shoulder where the girl had once leaned, wanting to cry but having no more tears. The boy was really outstanding, tall, handsome, academically excellent, and otherwise exceptional. Many girls wanted to pursue him, but the boy said, although his heart has four chambers, it could only hold one person.

Time flew by, countless days and nights passed. Nine years later, the boy was about to have another birthday. He still remembered the promise the girl made ten years ago, but where could he cash this blank check of vows? The boy returned to that city, the city full of happiness, searching for their footprints. Fortunately, the city was basically unchanged compared to ten years ago. Unconsciously, the boy came to that park again. The scenes from ten years ago reappeared: the iron suspension bridge where they held hands, the forest paths they walked, and the place where he once kissed the girl... Hmm?

The boy discovered a grave, with a wordless tombstone surrounded by blooming roses. The boy was curious. At this moment, an elderly man sweeping the grounds approached. Curious, the boy asked about the origin of the grave. The old man stopped, sighing deeply and said, nine years ago, in another city, this girl waited for someone outside a KFC. She waited for four hours, and the person still hadn't arrived. Just as the girl was about to leave, she was mistakenly injured by two bank robbers. More unfortunately, while being sent to the hospital, the ambulance hit a boy, wasting quite a bit of time.

When she finally reached the hospital, the girl died from excessive blood loss. Before dying, the girl said she must be buried here because she believed that a boy she deeply loved would come to propose to her. And at that time, she would agree. "It was him who made me believe in forever..." Alas, if the ambulance hadn't hit that boy, the girl might have been saved. After hearing the old man's words, the boy was stunned. He suddenly remembered that city and that KFC at that time, but the boy hadn't specified which KFC. Coincidentally, the KFC he went to was a newly opened store that had only been in business for a few days.

The boy understood...

Ah!

The sky is raining again,

Drops falling into my heart,

Reflecting your beauty,

So unforgettable,

Ah!

The clouds are crying again,

Those are tears shed just for you,

Together,

Anything,

I am willing...

(The Final Chapter) The next morning, the park management discovered a male corpse. The man lay peacefully on that grave, leaning quietly against the flower bed behind the wordless tombstone. In his hands, he still held 99 fresh red roses...

(On a night equally beautiful as ten years ago, with the same deep love for her as ten years ago, under the same bright moonlight as ten years ago, with the same 99 roses as ten years ago.)
