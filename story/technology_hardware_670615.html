Ten sentences in the workplace can make events more perfect. (Transfer)

by ustooktc5 on 2012-02-21 09:07:38

The following is excerpted from Goiee Space article. As the saying goes: A good doer is not as good as a good speaker. If you think that you can be skillful and successful in your career just by relying on pure skills and hard work, it's somewhat naive. Although talent plus diligence is very important, being a good communicator can make your work easier and help you get a raise or promotion.

1. In response to tasks assigned by your superior: I'll take care of it immediately.

Responding calmly and quickly will let your superior feel that you are an efficient and decisive subordinate who follows instructions. Hesitation will only make your superior unhappy and leave an impression of indecision. The next important opportunity may not be for you.

2. Delivering bad news: We seem to have encountered some situations...

If there is trouble with a deal or a market crisis, rushing into your superior's office to report the bad news will make your superior doubt your ability to handle crises, and might even lead to scolding and becoming a scapegoat.

The correct method is to calmly say: "We seem to have encountered some situations..." Don't panic and make your superior feel that the situation is not beyond remedy, and that you will stand side by side with him to solve the problem.

3. Demonstrating team spirit: XX's idea is really great!

If Xiao Ma's creativity or design is appreciated by your superior, even if you are sad about your unsuccessful design or even jealous, you still need to praise Xiao Ma within the hearing range of your superior: "Xiao Ma's proposal is really great." In the competitive workplace, appreciating others will make your superior think you are kind-hearted and full of team spirit, thus giving you more trust.

4. If you don't know something: Let me think carefully about it. Can I answer you before 2 PM?

If your superior asks you a question related to business and you don't know how to answer, never say "I don't know." Instead, saying "Let me think carefully about it. Can I answer you before 2 PM?" not only temporarily relieves you but also makes your superior believe that you don't act rashly and are a thoughtful person. Of course, remember to give the answer on time.

5. Asking colleagues for help: This plan can't go without you!

If you can't complete a plan alone and need someone more experienced to help, how should you ask? You can sincerely say: "This plan can't go without you!" Colleagues usually won't refuse to maintain their image as experts. Of course, remember to thank them afterward.

6. Refusing dirty jokes: Such talk seems inappropriate in the office!

Men sometimes like telling dirty jokes and don't pay much attention to the setting. If a male colleague tells you a "dirty joke" that you cannot tolerate, this sentence can make them stop appropriately.

7. Reducing workload: I know this matter is important. Let's first arrange our current tasks and prioritize them.

First, emphasize that you understand the importance of this task, then request your superior's guidance to prioritize this task with other tasks. Subtly let your superior know that your workload is actually quite large. If it's not something only you can do, some things can be handed over to others or postponed.

8. Admitting mistakes: It was my momentary oversight, but fortunately...

Making mistakes is inevitable, so admitting your own mistakes is important. Shifting responsibility will only make you more wrong. However, admitting mistakes has its tricks; don't let all the mistakes fall on you. This sentence can shift others' attention and reduce the severity of your mistake.

9. Breaking the ice: I would like to know your opinion on this matter.

When you are with your superior, sometimes you have to find topics to break the silence. However, this is also a good opportunity to win your superior's favor. The most appropriate topic is something related to the company that your superior cares about and is familiar with. When your superior speaks at length, he will appreciate you as a humble listener.

10. Facing criticism: Thank you for telling me, I will carefully consider your advice.

Facing criticism or blame, regardless of whether you have done anything wrong, don't show dissatisfaction on your face, but let the other party know that you have received his message. Being neither humble nor arrogant makes you look confident and cautious, and more worthy of respect.
