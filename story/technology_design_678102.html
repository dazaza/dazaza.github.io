Well-known anti-trafficking volunteer persuades 33 fugitives to surrender online

by xue94fwsh on 2012-02-27 11:42:54

Yesterday morning, "Mr. Shangguan" waited for Zeng's arrival at the appointed location around 10 a.m.. At this time, Weibo also saw the first post about persuading the criminal suspect to surrender themselves, which immediately drew the attention of netizens.

The fugitive sought help through Weibo. It turns out that the fugitive named Zeng is a Guangdong native who was previously suspected of participating in fraud in Henan Province. The fraud gang defrauded more than 2 million yuan, and Zeng received over 200,000 yuan. After the case broke out, no matter how long it had been, Zeng still remembered and fled abroad, not daring to return home. According to Zeng's friends, Zeng said that this life of escape had already pushed his mental state to the brink of collapse, and moreover, Zeng was ill, which worsened due to his escape. Zeng once told this friend that he was escaping just to gather 200,000 yuan to hand over to the government in order to receive lenient treatment. "To help Zeng gather these 200,000 yuan, Zeng's family borrowed a lot of money, reportedly having already gathered enough," "Mr. Shangguan" said.

Southern Daily reported (by Zhao Yang, correspondent Wu Qiong): "This is the 33rd fugitive I have successfully persuaded to surrender since the beginning of this year's 'Clear Net Action'." A well-known anti-human trafficking volunteer "Mr. Shangguan" told Southern Daily reporters. Yesterday, he successfully persuaded the Shenzhen-based criminal suspect Zeng to surrender to the Chigang police station in Haizhu District, Guangzhou.

"I told him that if he voluntarily surrenders, he can obtain an opportunity for lenient treatment under the premise of legal regulations," "Mr. Shangguan" said.

"Mr. Shangguan" urges prodigals to turn back early

"Three days ago, this netizen contacted me again, hoping I could help persuade his friend to surrender. We agreed on a meeting point in Shenzhen to meet with the relatives of this fugitive, hoping to dispel their concerns so as to jointly promote this fugitive to step forward," "Mr. Shangguan" recalled.

Zeng said he fled to gather 200,000 yuan. Weibo recommended | Today's Weibo hotspot

"Mr. Shangguan" told Southern Daily reporters that he had been invited by Zeng's relatives to go to their location in Shenzhen, but Zeng did not appear that time. "If this meeting was still scheduled in Shenzhen, Zeng would very likely not dare to come out."

At around 11 a.m. yesterday morning, Zeng finally arrived in Guangzhou. Upon meeting, Zeng's first sentence was: "I am sorry to society, sorry to my family, I wanted to surrender long ago..." "Mr. Shangguan" and others accompanied Zeng to the Chigang police station of the Haizhu District Public Security Bureau in Guangzhou. Around 11:40 a.m., the police confirmed, and Zeng entered the detention center.

At 16:43, @Shangguan Justice - Zi Zi sent another Weibo post: "After several days of hard work and patient persuasion, it was not in vain. He surrendered! The 33rd; I hope the law can be lenient towards him, society can be inclusive and forgiving, giving him a chance to reform! I hope prodigals outside can return early, the last two days of the Clear Net Action, grasp the time, it's grasping your own life!"

On December 11th, "Mr. Shangguan" came to Shenzhen. According to his disclosure, the family of this fugitive was still unclear about what lenient treatment the fugitive could receive during the "Clear Net Action". After "Mr. Shangguan"'s introduction and advice, the fugitive's family finally dispelled their doubts and successfully convinced the fugitive. That night at 9:03 p.m., @Shangguan Justice - Zi Zi sent a Weibo post: "Communicated all day, the 33rd, finally persuaded. Departed at 9 p.m. to return to Guangzhou, will surrender the day after tomorrow."

Also on the same day, Gao, a 24-year-old suspect from Taojiang, Hunan, came to Guangzhou overnight accompanied by his family and surrendered to the Haizhu police. Gao fled after robbing in Shangchong Village, Haizhu District, on December 28th, 2006. Under the strong arrest and sincere persuasion of the public security organs, Gao eventually chose to surrender to the public security organs. So far, the "Clear Net" action of the Haizhu police has captured 414 fugitives, with those who surrendered accounting for six-tenths.

"Mr. Shangguan" told reporters that he promotes the "Clear Net Action" on Weibo and tries to persuade fugitives to surrender. More than 20 days ago, a netizen followed him and sent a private message to his Weibo @Shangguan Justice - Zi Zi, asking about the national policy towards fugitives and other issues. After a while, this netizen said that there was a fugitive among his friends.

According to the Haizhu District Public Security Bureau of Guangzhou, Zeng, aged 49, was initially investigated for suspicion of fraud and was placed on the online wanted list by the Henan police in July this year.
