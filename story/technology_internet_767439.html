Baidu sues 360 over blocking add-on case which opened in court yesterday, the latter claimed it was unrelated to the case

by anonymous on 2013-10-15 21:28:54

Baidu Online Network Technology (Beijing) Co., Ltd. (hereinafter referred to as "Baidu") sued Beijing Qihoo Technology Co., Ltd. (hereinafter referred to as "Qihoo Technology") and Qihoo 360 Software (Beijing) Co., Ltd. (hereinafter referred to as "Qihoo 360") on the grounds that the "Blocking Baidu Ads" plug-in provided by the 360 browser constituted unfair competition, claiming 600,000 yuan in compensation. Yesterday, Dongcheng Court held a trial for this case.

Baidu claimed normal search was affected

During the trial, both plaintiff and defendant's representatives appeared in court. The plaintiff, Baidu, claimed that the defendant applied the plug-in in obvious positions on websites and browsers, affecting its reputation, and some of Baidu's normal non-paid search results were also impacted. This behavior constitutes unfair competition against Baidu, causing economic losses. Now, it requests the court to order both defendants to immediately cease their unfair competition against Baidu and publish an apology statement continuously for 30 days on a prominent position of the www.360.cn homepage, as well as in a prominent position in a certain daily newspaper to eliminate the influence. In addition, it requests to order the second defendant, Qihoo 360, to compensate Baidu for economic losses amounting to RMB 500,000 and reasonable expenses incurred in stopping the infringement of RMB 100,000.

Qihoo Technology insisted there was no fault

The representative of the first defendant, Qihoo Technology, stated that the plaintiff, Baidu's claim of unfair competition has no factual or legal basis. He claimed that Qihoo Technology provides platform services for storage space, and the involved plug-in "Blocking Baidu Ads" was not made or uploaded by Qihoo Technology. "Qihoo Technology merely provided the upload platform service and did not participate in the creation and upload process of the involved plug-in, thus having no fault. Moreover, installing the 'Blocking Baidu Ads' plug-in is entirely at the discretion of internet users who have the right to freely choose. The plug-in itself has rationality and does not possess any illegality." The representative of Qihoo Technology argued that the plaintiff, Baidu, had no evidence proving its loss of interest.

Qihoo 360 claims no connection to the case

The representative of the second defendant, Qihoo 360, expressed agreement with the opinions of the first defendant, Qihoo Technology, and claimed that the operator of the 360 Security Browser and 360 Extreme Speed Browser is the first defendant, Qihoo Technology. However, Qihoo 360 is neither the rights holder nor the responsible entity for the 360 Security Browser and 360 Extreme Speed Browser, and does not operate the www.360.cn website or other URL platforms with 360.cn as the central domain, so the second defendant, Qihoo 360, has no connection to this case. "Even if it can be inferred that the second defendant, Qihoo 360, is the responsible entity for the 360 Security Browser and 360 Extreme Speed Browser, the involved 'Blocking Baidu Ads' plug-in exists on the www.360.cn platform. Before being installed by users, it has no association with the 360 Extreme Speed Browser or the 360 Security Browser. Therefore, regardless of who the subject of the 360 browser is, the platform involving this Blocking Baidu Ads plug-in is www.360.cn, and the operating entity of this platform www.360.cn is only the first defendant, Beijing Qihoo Technology Co., Ltd., which has no connection to the second defendant." Qihoo 360 earnestly requested the court to dismiss all the plaintiff's claims against the second defendant. "Moreover, the plug-in was made and uploaded by an internet user named 'Hun Hun Ge,' and has no relation to either defendant," the two defendants argued.

It is understood that the involved plug-in has been deleted. The court did not announce a verdict in the session and will reconsider the case on another day.
