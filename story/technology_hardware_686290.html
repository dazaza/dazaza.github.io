Unique Welsh Cheese_Welsh Cuisine 2065

by qianljfsx on 2012-03-02 06:31:33

Cheese making has a long history tradition in Wales. Early cheese is similar to the now famous Caerphilly, and it was immersed in brine. Under the Hywel Dda law, cheese was regarded as an item for resolving divorce. The cheese soaked in brine would be sent to the wife, while the hanging cheese would be sent to the husband. In the 17th and 18th centuries, cow's milk replaced goat's milk and sheep's milk. Existing records show that people drank goat's milk in the twentieth century. This goat's milk was mixed with skimmed milk used to make butter, thereby improving the quality of the cheese. Nowadays, with the advancement of technology, there are various types of cheese in Wales that can meet different taste needs, which will be briefly introduced below:

Fresh soft cheese: Soft cheese is not compressed. Compared with many hard cheeses, they have a lighter texture and less fat content. Soft cheese can be spread on biscuits or eaten with salad, and can also be used in cooking.

Tips: This type of cheese has a high water content and a short shelf life.

Hard and semi-hard cheese: Cheddar is the most popular and most produced hard cheese. Caerphilly is a slightly softer cheese and is sometimes classified as semi-hard cheese because it is only slightly compressed compared to Cheddar.

Washed-rind cheese: This cheese can be semi-soft or hard cheese. Its uniqueness lies in its orange skin. During the maturation period, this cheese needs to be washed with a cloth containing Brivibacterium linens bacteria or other selected bacteria in concentrated salt water. Most of this cheese has a soft texture and can even be described as smooth and delicate. Its aroma is mild. Many people find the taste too strong and even difficult to accept. Therefore, they are the gourmet choices of experts, and once you try them, you will feel their incredible deliciousness.

Gouda cheese and smoked cheese: Gouda cheese is made by removing part of the whey and replacing it with water, so it is also called washed-curd cheese.

Smoked cheese: Smoking food is considered a method of preservation, and in many cases, it still retains this function. Similarly, smoking several types of cheese is also a traditional way. However, today's smoked cheese provides not a method of preserving cheese but another type of cheese.

White mold cheese and blue mold cheese: People have some doubts about white mold cheese. Mold grows at unwanted times and places, and in such cases, discarding these cheeses is correct. However, the mold here is the good kind, including blue mold and white mold. They not only promote the fermentation process of cheese but also add another texture and flavor.

Tips: Many blue mold cheeses are only made at the most appropriate time for milk, so they are not available all year round.
