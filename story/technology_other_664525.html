A Comprehensive Analysis of the Detailed Knowledge of Vibrating Screens

by hx515317 on 2012-02-13 15:55:23

Vibrating screens are indispensable equipment in mineral processing and sandstone production lines, and thus they also affect the overall efficiency of the production line. Choosing a reliable, stable, and efficient vibrating screen is crucial. Users should have a detailed and comprehensive understanding of the vibrating screen when configuring the entire production line.

I. Principle and Introduction of Vibrating Screens:

Vibrating screens feature stability, reliability, low consumption, low noise, long life, steady vibration patterns, and high screening efficiency. Their simple structure makes them easy to install, which is why they are widely used in mining, coal, smelting, building materials, refractory materials, light industry, chemical, pharmaceuticals, and cement industries. When working, vibrating screens use two motors rotating synchronously in opposite directions to generate counter-rotational excitation forces, forcing the screen body to drive the screen plate for longitudinal motion. The material on it is periodically thrown forward by an excitation force, thereby completing material grading, de-sludging, dewatering, and de-medium screening operations.

III. Common Types of Vibrating Screens:

1. Fixed Screen: The working part is fixed and does not move, relying on the material sliding along the working surface to achieve screening. Fixed grizzly screens are commonly used in ore dressing plants, generally for pre-screening before coarse or medium crushing. They have a simple structure and are easy to manufacture, do not consume power, and can directly unload ore onto the screen surface. The main disadvantage is low productivity and screening efficiency, usually only 50-60%.

2. Roller Screen: The working surface consists of horizontally arranged rolling shafts, with discs on the shafts. Fine-grained materials pass through the gaps between the rollers or discs, while large blocks are moved by the rollers to one end and discharged from the end. These screens are rarely used in ore dressing plants.

3. Drum Screen: The working part is cylindrical, and the entire screen rotates around the cylinder axis, which is generally installed at a slight incline. Material is fed into one end of the cylinder; fine-grade material passes through the sieve holes on the cylindrical working surface, while coarse material is discharged from the other end of the cylinder. Drum screens have low speed, stable operation, and good dynamic balance. However, their sieve holes tend to clog easily, leading to low screening efficiency, small working area, and low productivity. Ore dressing plants rarely use them as screening equipment.

4. Planar Motion Screen: The body vibrates or swings within a plane. According to its planar motion trajectory, it can be divided into linear, circular, elliptical, and complex motions. Shaking screens and vibrating screens belong to this category.

5. Linear Vibrating Screen: A highly efficient new type of screening equipment, widely used in mining, coal, smelting, building materials, refractory materials, light industry, and chemical industries. Linear vibrating screens offer stability, reliability, low consumption, low noise, long life, steady vibration patterns, and high screening efficiency.

IV. Detailed Explanation of the Transmission System of Vibrating Screens:

Linear vibrating screens use double eccentric block, dual motor drives, with self-synchronization of the motors. The excitation of the screen machine is produced by the centrifugal force of the eccentric block.

2. Transmission System

For round screens, the motor drives the exciter via a V-belt (adjustable pulley speed). For linear screens, the motor directly drives the exciter, both connected by a petal-shaped flexible coupling.

3. Amplitude adjustment is achieved by increasing or decreasing the counterweight blocks.

4. The three-claw flexible disc connecting the intermediate transmission shaft to the exciter is made of special rubber plates, offering high torque capacity and flexibility. Universal joints can also be used.

V. Safety Technology for Vibrating Screens

When using screening machines, safety regulations must be followed. For example, no repair work should be done while the screening machine is running; damaged screening machines should not be used.

1. Drive belts and pulleys must have protective covers. During maintenance, the cover can be removed, but it must be reinstalled before starting the screening machine.

2. To create normal labor hygiene conditions, necessary dust prevention facilities should be present in the factory where the screening machine is set up. If the screened material easily produces dust, the screen should have a sealed hood.

3. Lubrication oil for bearings must be added when the screening machine is stopped. Similarly, cleaning of the screen mesh should only occur after the machine has stopped.

4. When inspecting the screening machine, particular attention must be paid to ensure that the counterweights on the flywheel are securely fixed. If not firmly fixed, during operation, the counterweights could detach from the flywheel and cause accidents.

5. If any hazard to personal or equipment safety is discovered, the machine should be immediately stopped and reported to the dispatcher.

VI. Common Faults in Vibrating Screens

There are many reasons for abnormal operation of vibrating screens. Generally speaking, the main situations include:

(1) Abnormal sound at the exciter, excessive surface heat;

(2) Screen plate damage, looseness, or broken screen strips causing repeated vibrations or mixed materials;

(3) Frequent breakage of shock-absorbing springs;

(4) Single motor operation or dual motors not in parallel or although in parallel but not rotating in reverse;

(5) Resonance occurs due to the operating frequency of the screening machine matching a fixed frequency;

(6) Incorrect installation of the screen body, uneven foundation;

(7) Excessive deviation between the center distance of the exciter shaft and the drive shaft;

(8) Excitation force deviating from the center of gravity of the screen body;

(9) Incorrect assembly of the exciter or vibration motor, inconsistent angles between two sets of counterweight blocks;

(10) Uneven feeding leading to overload, poor discharge of raw materials;

VII. Operation and Maintenance

1. Operators should be familiar with the performance of the screen machine and master the operating methods.

2. Starting the screen machine should follow the order of the process system to ensure empty start-up.

3. Stopping the machine should also follow the order of the process system. Do not stop with material inside or continue feeding after stopping.

4. Check the bearing temperature during or at the end of each shift.

5. Regularly check if all fastening bolts are loose and if the belt tension is appropriate.

6. Regularly check the flexible couplings and flexible discs for tears or damage, and replace them promptly if damaged.

7. Conduct a major overhaul of the vibrator once a year during annual inspections. Completely disassemble the vibrator for cleaning and oil replacement. Replace bearings if there are flaky corrosion points, color changes, deformation of rollers, or loose retainers.
