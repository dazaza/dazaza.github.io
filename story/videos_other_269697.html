Buddha Yunlai's incense culture has a long history, wide coverage, and universality. In China, the Zhou people burned wood to create smoke for sacrifices to heaven, called "Yin" or "Yin Sacrifice." By the time of Emperor Wu of Han, the concept of passing down incense generation after generation took on the meaning of continuous life and also expressed the spirit of respecting heaven and ancestors. After the Sui and Tang dynasties, incense culture became more popular and secularized. Song Dynasty poet Chen Yuyi wrote in his poem "Incense Burning": "Imbuing infinite meaning into this incense," reflecting how human emotions and philosophical inquiries found expression through incense, which has now been completely internalized into people's spirits. Interestingly, not only Chinese people burn incense, many other nations and countries around the world also have traditions of burning incense, such as the ancient Egyptians, Babylonians, Indians, Greeks, Romans, and Israelites...
