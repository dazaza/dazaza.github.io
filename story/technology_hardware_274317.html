AC Milan defeated Cagliari 4-3, but before the game, worrying news came out of Milanello. It was rumored that during the final training session on Saturday before the match, goalkeeper Christian Abbiati, who had just recovered from injury and rejoined the team, had an intense argument with head coach Leonardo. Originally, the media predicted that Abbiati, who had been training for a long time, would make it into the squad for this round. He was already in the squad for the game against Lazio, but after this quarrel, he was excluded from the squad for the Cagliari match. There were also reports that because Leonardo was preparing to let recently recovered Stefano Storari replace Abbiati in the squad, Abbiati was extremely dissatisfied, leading to the argument.

Currently, no specific details about this incident have leaked yet, but it's not hard to imagine that this is closely related to Dida, who regained his chance to play and made contributions after both Abbiati and Storari were injured. Before his injury, Abbiati had been the undisputed starting goalkeeper for the Rossoneri, and Storari had also pushed Dida down to the third-choice goalkeeper position with his performances. However, after seizing the opportunity fate threw at him, Dida suddenly burst onto the scene, not only improving his form but also leaping two levels to reclaim his position as AC Milan's No.1 goalkeeper. Now, with the successive recoveries of Abbiati and Storari, the goalkeeper competition at AC Milan has become fierce. Italian media reported that perhaps only time will tell who will ultimately win in this hierarchy struggle in front of the goal.

Abbiati has indeed been back in training for some time now; previously, Galliani had said: "Abbiati will be ready after the international break," but now that the national team games are over, Abbiati still isn't getting a chance. In response, Leonardo claimed at a press conference: "I've already talked with Abbiati. After these months of recovery from injury, he's slowly getting back to his old self, and next, we need to help him regain his match fitness, so he'll continue training to return to the pitch in top condition. We'll find suitable opportunities for him in future games to help him regain his feel." The implication being that Abbiati is still not ready to play. This might irritate Abbiati somewhat, but in fact, Leonardo's choice is entirely understandable because Dida has been performing well—what reason is there to suddenly bench him?

Dida really seized his chance tightly. Today, although he conceded three goals, his performance was still outstanding. At the 17th minute, Matri's powerful volley, at the 19th minute, Yeda's near-post shot, and at the 66th minute, De Silvestri's shot bound for the corner were all successfully deflected by Dida. If Dida continues to maintain his excellent form, his status as the first-choice goalkeeper will also continue, and the dispute between Leonardo and Abbiati will persist...

Regarding the current intense situation at AC Milan's goalkeeper position, former Lazio goalkeeper and current football TV commentator Angelo Marchigiani, in an interview with La Gazzetta dello Sport, stated: "If Leonardo believes that Abbiati has recovered, then given that he was considered the starting goalkeeper at the beginning of the season, he should be playing now. And if he needs another week or two to regain his form, then the existing balance within the team shouldn't be disrupted, and Dida should still start. But if Abbiati needs a longer period to recover, then Storari should also get the chance to compete."
