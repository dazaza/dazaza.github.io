Review of the war between 360 and Tencent QQ: Users' computers should not be used as a battlefield ---------------- www.seo-tribe.com ---------------- [Time: November 4, 2010, 13:39:19 | Author: Ouyang Chunfeng | Source: | Views: 3 times] Written by Ouyang Chunfeng (SEO Tribe), "Review of the war between 360 and Tencent QQ: Users' computers should not be used as a battlefield". From the verbal disputes of many days ago, to the criticism and accusation on each other's homepages, and then to the battle on users' computers. The dispute between 360 and Tencent QQ has finally erupted into a war in an atmosphere filled with gunpowder. From disputes to war, it increasingly resembles a fight between two children.

Before moving the battlefield to users' desktops, 360 and Tencent accused each other of various behaviors of stealing and peeping at user data on their respective homepages. Then they both gave examples to prove the shamelessness and vulgarity of the other side. Finally, they began to accuse each other through pop-up windows on user terminals. On the evening of November 3rd, the fighting began. QQ started scanning users' systems, and computers with 360 software installed would be unable to use QQ software. Meanwhile, on computers with QQ software installed, 360 popped up a large box calling for everyone to use other chat tools. It's quite interesting. Isn't this just like a war between two kids? No matter what, neither side should use users' computers as a battlefield. This is like two families who originally spoke for their children, but ended up pushing their children onto the battlefield, you push me, I push you. The ones who suffer the most are still the children themselves. After years of effort, 360 has indeed become a golden brand in the computers of thousands of households, making great contributions to protecting computers. Today, we have to uninstall 360 software to use QQ, leaving our computers unprotected. Who suffers the most? We, the users ourselves. To be honest, over all these years, the only antivirus software that I have never complained about is 360. It is really inappropriate to joke about the safety of the public for the sake of war, which is indeed not the behavior a world-class large enterprise should have. QQ is an indispensable part of our lives, we can say he has become a member of our family. Chatting with friends and relatives far away only requires such a small window, video, voice, how convenient. Naturally, QQ has also become the software with the largest number of domestic users. 360 calls on us users to uninstall QQ software and use other chat tools, isn't this asking us to stay away from the large group? This action is undoubtedly childish. As netizens, we only hope that this incident will end as soon as possible, don't use our computers as a battlefield. Don't let your battles end up with our operating systems crashing, ultimately making us the sufferers.
