Fireworks are easily cold, people are easily parted.

by xiaoxiao67 on 2012-02-03 20:45:52

Fireworks are easily cold, people are easily separated.

The heart trembles and dyes that red dust.

Can't forget how many mornings with the glow of dawn, can't count how many quiet nights. Sitting in front of the desk, that cup of fragrant wine, silent, waiting.

A touch of tender pity for the cold fireworks, a cup of thin wine ends the prosperity.

The slow tune spreads from the tip of the night's eyebrows. My piano keys fall silent, like the ripples of autumn waves, circling and drawing the corners of where the horizon rests. In my memory, there is you who shakes my soul. Fate takes root, with broken sunsets, deep calls in tears, but cannot awaken the meetings of this life.

Since stepping away with the sunset, a salty word is embedded in the eyes. Was it destined long ago, to wait for a thousand years like the desert poplar?

The same stars flash cold light alone, the same long nights are mottled like the misty red dust. Are the same old people still standing and waiting? Where have you, the beloved one, gone? Do you know, the fluttering leaves dance up like snow, the leaves holding autumn's hand have come. My shadow counts thousands of falling leaves at the door. Where is your whisper, if you become the wind, if you become the moon.

Rain falls profusely, the grasses and trees grow deep in the old hometown.

How many clouds linger in past years, how many flower moons flow through the branches of seasons, how many poems play the sentiments of joy and sorrow, only a gentle line of writing whispers quietly, desolate and unsupported.

Cold snow breaks the bridge, butterflies are silent, broken branches and flowers die as rain turns to ash.

Tender thoughts tangle in my palm, yet why can't the hand that pierces through the night touch your beating heart. Longing for eternity, the tragic love falls into whose river, and drifts on which fractured silver bridge. The street where we held hands is still bustling with city life, but the lonely figure has already faded.

Snow, quietly comes to an end. Thus, I no longer watch the snow by the window, no longer break the plum blossoms to recite poetry. For you said, when the snow falls, you would return hugging each crystal-clear flake. But looking out over the pure snow, there isn't a trace of you.

Fireworks are easily cold, people are easily separated. Can't understand what love is, love is the incomplete chapter of my life.
