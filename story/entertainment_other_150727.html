The Little Story of Post-80s Being Deceived by Ten Lies from Parents

by longersing on 2009-05-18 23:34:17

Post-80s - what an epoch-making generation of children! They are very special, and the generation whose personalities differ most from the post-90s and pre-70s. We were born in this crucial period, bringing fresh blood to the development of New China. However, we also often blindly believed in the lies told by our parents when we were young. Why is that? Let's take a look at these lies; they are quite amusing when we look back:

We, the post-80s generation, have now gradually become the main force of today's society. The pressure on us is getting heavier and heavier, and our responsibilities are getting bigger and bigger. We are often pressed for breath by workplace exams. Therefore, sometimes we can't help but think about our childhood days, relaxing our minds. Back then, we were always so innocent, so carefree, especially many of us wanted to be good children in our parents' hearts, so we always blindly believed in their words. Thus, many well-intentioned lies became truths in our hearts, worthy of being remembered. Now, as we recall those naive years, those words, though laughable, are beautiful memories worth cherishing forever, and interesting news worth savoring...

1: Children always liked to open umbrellas inside the house. At such times, adults would say: "Opening an umbrella indoors will make you stop growing taller." After hearing this, most children would instantly close their umbrellas to avoid becoming dwarfs and being laughed at by others...

2: Whenever there was a full moon, seeing the moon in the sky always aroused great curiosity. Often, we would point at the moon with our fingers and ask questions. When adults got tired of answering, they would say something frightening: "If you point at the moon, your ears will be cut off," and immediately we would withdraw our fingers and remain silent...

3: When eating fruit, some fruits had seeds. If we accidentally swallowed them, the timid ones would ask the adults if anything bad would happen. At such times, instead of comforting us properly, the adults would tell a frightening lie: "If you swallow it, a big tree will grow out of your stomach," making us even more afraid...

4: As children, we were always curious about where we came from and loved to pester our parents with questions. Our parents would always say: "You were picked up from somewhere." Looking back now, it's really funny and hard on our parents...

5: In our innocent childhood, occasionally we wanted to secretly look at other children's bottoms. When discovered by adults, they would say: "If you peek at others' bottoms, you'll get pinkeye," and from then on, we would never dare to do it again...

6: As children, we liked to play house and some kids liked to play kiss-kiss. Playing kiss-kiss was not allowed by our parents, who would say something we didn't quite understand at the time: "Kissing will result in having a baby," which really shows the power of their imagination...

7: As children, we loved candy. Before it was time to lose our baby teeth, we would accidentally lose one and worry whether it would grow back. We would anxiously ask our parents, and at such times, they would comfort us by saying: "Throw the upper tooth under the eaves and the lower tooth on the roof, and it will grow back." Upon hearing this, we would happily follow their advice right away...

8: For children who liked to leave food uneaten, parents had a clever way to deal with it. They would scare us by saying: "If you don't eat all the rice in your bowl, you will grow big freckles on your face." Upon hearing this, no matter how unpalatable the leftover food was, we would instantly finish every grain...

9: Sometimes, if we wanted something and our parents didn't comply, some children would cry incessantly. At such times, the adults, who were already exhausted, would mostly say: "If you keep crying, the police officer will take you away," and many of us would stop crying...

10: On the first day of kindergarten, most children would cling to their mothers. Once the mother left, the children would cry and make a fuss. Every time this happened, the mother would comfort us by saying: "No matter where you are, I will always see you." Although it was a lie meant to deceive us, looking back now, it felt really warm at the time...
