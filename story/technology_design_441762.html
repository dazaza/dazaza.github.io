Part One: Getting Started, Q Coins

Get 10 cars in the fastest time - 60 hours.

Prerequisites: One main QQ account and three sub QQ accounts.

0-12 Hours:

The start is the most crucial part. If you run at full speed and efficiency, within 2 days (48 hours), you can acquire 6 cars. At the beginning, either use a sub-account or park in someone else's car lot to earn money. Initially, the system gives one second-hand Auto + 1000 coins. Open 3 sub-accounts, place their cars in your car lot, and park your second-hand Auto in the sub-accounts' lots. With 4 cars, each earning 5 coins per minute, after 12 hours (4 cars x 12 hours = 14400 coins) plus the initial 1000 coins gifted by the system, you will have 15400 coins. After a few more minutes of parking, you'll reach 16000 coins and can immediately buy another second-hand Auto.

12-24 Hours:

At this point, you'll have 5 cars (2 from the main account + 3 from sub-accounts). After 12 hours, you'll have: 5 cars x 3600 coins = 18000 coins. Use this to buy the third car for the main account, leaving you with 2000 coins.

24-36 Hours:

6 cars x 3600 coins = 21600 coins + leftover 2000 coins = 22600 coins. Buy the fourth car, leaving you with 6600 coins.

36-48 Hours:

7 cars x 3600 coins = 25200 coins + leftover 6600 coins = 31800 coins. Use this to buy the fifth and sixth cars.

48-60 Hours:

9 cars x 3600 coins = 32400 coins. Use this to buy the tenth car.

Part Two: Upgrading Cars

(After buying 10 second-hand Autos, you only upgrade them without buying new ones. When upgrading, the original car price can be deducted, and you only need to pay the difference.)

After acquiring 10 second-hand Autos, as soon as you have extra money, upgrade them one by one to Chery QQ worth 36000 coins each. This car has a parking fee of 7 coins per minute and recoups its cost in 86 minutes. It’s the fastest recoup time besides second-hand Autos (53 hours) and Spark Letchi (85 hours). Why not directly upgrade to Spark Letchi? Because Spark Letchi is expensive. The goal is to maximize funds quickly. First, upgrade to Chery QQ as fast as possible to make money faster and then upgrade to Spark Letchi. After all 10 cars are upgraded to Chery QQ, the next step is to upgrade to Spark Letchi. What’s next after having 10 Spark Letchis? Refer to the attached "QQ Parking Space Car Cost-Effectiveness Table" for the sequence of upgrades based on the shortest recoup time. Consider carefully.

Part Three: The Fun of Playing (Where is the playability in parking spaces?)

What attracts me to this game is that it follows all real-world financial operation rules, namely the "80/80 Rule." The rich get richer, and the poor get poorer. It also aligns with the development laws of China's socialist economy: "Let some people get rich first" and "Development is the hard truth."

What is the 80/80 Rule? Also known as the "Pareto Principle."

In 1897, Italian economist Vilfredo Pareto discovered through statistical analysis of wealth and income distribution among different social classes in 19th-century Britain: 80% of societal wealth was concentrated in the hands of 20% of the population, while 80% of the population owned only 20% of societal wealth. This is the "Pareto Principle." The "Pareto Principle" reflects an imbalance but is omnipresent in society, economics, and life. It highlights a typical imbalance between cause and effect, input and output, effort and reward: 80% of results come from 20% of efforts; 80% of products may be produced by 20% of companies; 20% of customers may bring 80% of profits to businesses.

Similarly, in the parking space game, the rich get richer, and the poor get poorer. It also aligns with the development laws of China's socialist economy: "Let some people get rich first" and "Development is the hard truth." 20% of players who understand the game's hidden rules use these rules to make their 10 cars earn money every minute. Meanwhile, 80% of regular players, for the sake of appearances, want better cars. They work tirelessly to earn enough money to upgrade their single car, but they can't keep up with those who make money quickly. They also worry about where to park without penalties, whether to post tickets on friends' walls for money, and if others will park in their lots. Some even try to profit by reporting others, not realizing that out of 10 reported vehicles, only 2 may succeed. During this time, others' 10 cars continue to earn money every minute, while they earn only 200 yuan in fines and face criticism from friends. Others earn hundreds of thousands and admiration.

Part Five: Summary of Related Questions

(Here includes questions raised by all group members, as well as personal issues I've encountered and thought of but haven't faced yet.)

1. How do new users activate the game if no one invites them?

Answer: Use a sub-account to enter your own space - Personal Core - Friend Space Dynamics - There will be a display saying "Your friend has added QQ Parking Space," click "I also add" to activate.

2. If the main account invitation limit is full and sub-accounts cannot activate, what should be done?

Answer: Same method as above: Use a sub-account to enter your own space - Personal Center - Friend Space Dynamics - There will be a display saying "Your friend has added QQ Parking Space," click "I also add" to activate.

3. If there are no other available friends' parking lots in the sub-account's friend list except the main account, what should be done?

Answer: Enter the Road Condition Hall and look for players who have already activated the game. Add them as friends.

(Method to check strangers' QQ numbers in the Road Condition Hall is detailed in the next question.)

4. If there are too few friends who have activated the game and not enough parking spaces, how to add strangers? How to check strangers' QQ numbers in the Road Condition Hall?

Answer: In the road condition information, find the person you want to add, enter their parking lot, click the red small car icon in the upper left corner to view their vehicle information. In the dropdown menu of vehicle information, click the owner's avatar in the upper left corner to enter their space. The QQ space address contains the QQ number.

5. Where to park safely besides your sub-accounts?

Answer: How to find safe parking lots? Look in the Road Condition Hall. Find parking lots where many people frequently park and never get fined. The Road Condition Hall often shows messages like "Zhang San parked for 12 hours in Xiao A's parking lot and earned 3600 coins" or "Li Si parked for 24 hours in Xiao A's parking lot and earned 3600 coins, the car was automatically moved." Clearly, Xiao A has activated the game but doesn't play. Are these places definitely safe? No, Xiao A could be Zhang San or Li Si's sub-account. Their account used for brushing Q coins. If you occupy their spot, they might fine you. Identification methods are detailed in the next question.

6. How to distinguish between safe parking lots and others' sub-accounts?

Go to their parking lot and check their vehicle information. If they only have one initial second-hand Auto and cash is 1000, it's safe. Otherwise, it's someone else's sub-account. Or enter their QQ space, men require women to be pure, or check their QQ profile. The moment the starting whistle blows, it's hard to tell if it's a sub-account or a frequently used account. The QQ number checking method has been mentioned before.

7. How to report others?

You can only report QQ friends' cars found in someone else's parking lot. Next to the friend's car, there will be a "Report" button you can click.

You cannot find where your friend's car is parked unless it just left your parking lot, and your parking lot information will show: "Zhang San parked for 60 minutes in my parking lot, earned 300 coins, and drove to Li Si's parking lot." Otherwise, you have to search yourself. It's almost like looking for a needle in the sea.

Out of 100 reports, the police may not go to 20. Even if they go to 20, the car may not still be there in 10. Out of 10, it's uncertain if others haven't reported it before you. The success rate of reporting is almost 0. And even if you succeed once, it's 100. But in the time it takes to find 10 cars out of 1000, you could earn more by using sub-accounts.

8. After a sub-account parks in the main account's parking lot once, it cannot park again in the same spot. What should be done?

Method 1: Park for 15 minutes at someone else's place and then move back.

If you don't even want to wait 5 minutes, see Method 2.

Method 2: Have Sub-account A park at Sub-account B's place. Then immediately use Sub-account B to fine Sub-account A's car and kick it out. Sub-account A can then park in the previous spot again.

9. After a sub-account activates itself, there is no main account information in the parking lot or it shows as unactivated. What should be done?

Find them in the Road Condition Hall and then enter.
