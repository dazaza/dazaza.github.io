The news about WeChat 5.1 rolling out a membership feature is becoming increasingly credible.

by anonymous on 2013-09-04 15:00:49

Simultaneously with the news of WeChat Wo Card version upgrade, there is another piece of information: WeChat official further tightens the large group privilege. Multiple sources confirmed that users with the permission to open WeChat large groups have recently received an official message indicating that in the future, these privileged users will only be provided with four hundred-person large groups, whereas before, users with large group permissions could create an unlimited number of WeChat large groups.

As far as I know, Tencent has previously stopped reviewing the opening of WeChat large group permissions. Counting this action, WeChat officially completely cuts off the circulation of free large group privileges. If users want to upgrade their WeChat groups to hundred-person groups, they can only do so by purchasing a WeChat Wo Card. This also means that the news about WeChat 5.1 launching a membership feature becomes increasingly reliable.

In addition to WeChat large group permissions, there are multiple signs indicating that WeChat will launch a membership function in version 5.1:

1. Testing the membership service through WeChat Wo Card

Before selling the WeChat Wo Card, WeChat official conducted a marketing activity via YiXun, providing users with nine options to choose from, including: 1. Free gaming; 2. Priority to be found by nearby people; 3. Free HD movie viewing; 4. Free download of the latest singles; 5. Mutual transmission of HD images; 6. Mobile payment discounts; 7. Permission to build a 60-person WeChat large group; 8. More free emoticons; 9. More free data traffic.

Although it was proven that the user's choice among these nine privileges was meaningless, the interesting part lies in the concept of 'privilege': the definition of a privileged user is very close to QQ membership. It is evident that WeChat is testing the WeChat membership service through its cooperation with China Unicom.

2. WeChat Wo Card users become the guinea pigs for membership functions

Generally speaking, collaborations between operators and internet companies are one-off deals, where once the deal is done, that’s it. However, this time, the WeChat Wo Card privilege was upgraded from 1.0 to 1.1, granting the permission to open hundred-person large groups while simultaneously reducing the privileges of existing privileged users. These actions prove that WeChat is tightening up on the free privileges given out earlier, and WeChat Wo Card users have become the guinea pigs for WeChat membership functions. It can be affirmed that in the future, WeChat will continuously adjust the permissions for WeChat Wo Card users until the best form of the membership service is found, and WeChat Wo Card users will naturally become the first batch of WeChat members.

3. Tencent urgently needs WeChat to generate mobile-end value-added income

From Tencent's Q2 financial report, it is clear that the value-added income from IM software has significantly shrunk. Tencent President Liu Cheping publicly admitted that the mobility of IM has impacted the value-added income of QQ on the PC end, and Tencent will make up for this shortfall in mobile-end value-added income in the future.

Subsequently, we saw that Mobile QQ launched Super VIP (SVIP) and an emoticon store specifically for Mobile QQ, while although WeChat also launched an emoticon center, it has yet to achieve any significant progress in the most profitable membership area.

During Tencent's analyst meeting, there were continuous questions about Tencent's mobile-end value-added income. Tencent urgently needs WeChat to launch a membership function to produce value-added income. Therefore, even if Guangzhou Research Institute is extremely reluctant, the tasks assigned by the group must still be completed, making the news of the WeChat membership being launched in version 5.1 increasingly credible.
