Longyuan Construction is reported to have a per capita salary of 980,000 yuan

by zjsr2010 on 2010-06-29 12:01:46

Yesterday, a message titled "Longyuan Construction's per capita salary is 9.8 million yuan" becoming the "salary king" of the A-share market made Longyuan Construction an instant star in the A-share market. Are construction enterprises in Xiangshan county of our province really so rich that they are dripping with wealth? Yesterday, the reporter contacted Zhu Zhanjun, the board secretary of Longyuan Construction, and received the following response: "Our employees' average salary is around 70,000-80,000 yuan, how could it be so high?"

Migrant workers' total wage exceeds 1 billion yuan

Longyuan Construction is the largest private construction company in the Yangtze River Delta building market. In 2009, the company's operating revenue reached 6.56 billion yuan, and its operating profit was 620 million yuan. The company's total share capital is 474 million shares. Last year, the earnings per share were 0.41 yuan; this year, the earnings per share in the first quarter were 0.09 yuan, showing a slight increase compared to the same period last year.

Although the company's registered address is in Xiangshan, nearly 40% of its main business last year was in Shanghai, while the business volume in Zhejiang Province accounted for 18%. Other provinces (cities) where business volume exceeded 10% include Liaoning and Guangdong. In addition, overseas region income accounted for 4.16%.

According to available data, the company had 1,328 employees last year, and paid out various types of compensation (including wages, bonuses, allowances, and various benefits) totaling 1.3 billion yuan. If one only looks at these figures, it would be easy to conclude that the average annual salary is 980,000 yuan and the average revenue generated per employee is 49.5 million yuan, which is far from the figure of around 70,000-80,000 yuan given by Zhu Zhanjun.

What’s the mystery behind this? “We are a construction company, and the number of migrant workers employed on construction sites is at least 30,000-40,000 people, sometimes even 50,000-60,000 people. Migrant workers have strong mobility, different jobs, and personnel adjustments are often made based on the progress of construction projects. Some workers leave after working for a few months, so it is impossible to include them in the official employee count,” said Zhu Zhanjun.

It is understood that Longyuan Construction currently employs about 40,000 migrant workers. Generally, a migrant worker earns more than 100 yuan per day. Last year alone, Longyuan Construction paid over 1 billion yuan in wages to migrant workers.

Chairman's income last year was 7.6 million yuan

According to available data, the annual salary of the company's controlling shareholder and chairman, Lai Zhenyuan, is 420,000 yuan; his shareholding is 1.59536 billion yuan, and according to the dividend plan of 0.45 yuan per 10 shares after tax in 2009, his dividend income was 7.17912 million yuan, making his total annual income nearly 7.6 million yuan.

The general manager and vice chairman, Lai Zhaohui, had an annual salary of 300,000 yuan last year. His shareholding is 13.8298 million shares, and according to the 2009 dividend plan, his dividend income was 620,000 yuan, making his total annual income 920,000 yuan. In addition, the annual salaries of six senior executives in the company are all 132,000 yuan, which is not high among listed company executives. Employee supervisors earn an annual salary of 76,000 yuan.

However, in the past year, senior executives Wang Dehua, Qian Shuijiang, Meng Qingfu, and supervisor Qu Ying have been heavily reducing their stock holdings on the secondary market, with no senior executive increasing their stock holdings. After these executives reduced their stock holdings, the stock price often plummeted sharply.

For example, the most recent time Vice General Manager Wang Dehua reduced his stock holdings was on May 26th, although he only reduced 600 shares, the company's stock price fell by more than 17% in the next four trading days. Another Vice General Manager, Meng Qingfu, reduced his holdings by 2,854 shares on January 4th this year, with an average transaction price of 17.62 yuan, which was almost the highest price since the beginning of the year. Not long after, the company's stock price experienced continuous sharp declines, falling by more than 20% in January.

The most aggressive reducer was Director and Executive Vice President Qian Shuijiang, who reduced his holdings five times in September and October last year, totaling 40,000 shares, with the lowest average transaction price being 17.15 yuan and the highest being 19.51 yuan. At that time, the company's stock price was almost at its highest point for the entire year. It is understood that the company has punished some senior executives for their violations of regulations regarding stock reductions according to relevant rules.
