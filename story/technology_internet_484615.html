Kiosk role check-in, creating excellent employees_7320

by hmakekz9 on 2011-09-02 11:11:30

By Shu Hualu

Chief Expert of the Leadership Network for the Implementation of Standardized Management

A corporate organization is made up of independent individuals, brought together by the vision and goals of the company's growth. Regardless of how far this aggregation develops, it cannot eliminate the independence of the natural individuals that make up the group. At any time, they exist between a specific corporate organization and other social groups. They gather and disperse, continuously searching for and approaching social organizations that attract them, fleeing from those that do not recognize them or are not recognized by them. They might even join other socio-economic organizations that are directly opposed to the specific corporate organization they were once part of.

Therefore, individuals who enter a corporate organization to play a certain role are not necessarily guaranteed to become organizational cells of the enterprise. In fact, they could even become a thorn in the side of the enterprise, endangering its existence and growth. To incorporate an entirely independent natural individual into a specific role within a corporate organization — which is far from being a unified life organism — external management must be used to coordinate and integrate the relationships between these roles. This means transforming the relationships between these independent individuals, who are far from being cells, into the kind of cell-to-cell relationships found in a living organism. If this coordination and integration cannot ultimately be achieved, the corporate organization, as a fictional life organism, will not truly possess the characteristics of a living organism, and the organization will lack sufficient vitality and life force.

This kind of external management is known as role management.

For an entirely independent natural individual to give up some of their independence and exist as a component cell of a corporate organization, fully performing their duties within the role assigned by the organization — comprehensively doing all the work defined by the performance standards — there are conditions that need to be met. These conditions mainly include four aspects:

1. Competency and Qualification Conditions. They possess the ability to fully perform their duties and excel in their work — this is the basic condition for a role individual to become an organizational cell. No matter how hard one tries, everyone can only achieve what they are capable of. A company is not a welfare institute; it can only absorb people who can effectively contribute to achieving its value objectives. This limitation cannot be broken.

2. Willpower and Volition Conditions. They possess the willpower and volition to fully perform their duties and excel in their work — this is the willpower requirement for a role individual to potentially become an organizational cell. Not only do they wish to become a corresponding role in the corporate organization, but they are also willing to expend effort to do well in the tasks assigned according to the role responsibilities.

3. Enthusiasm and Patience Conditions. They possess the enthusiasm and patience to fully perform their duties and excel in their work — this is the stable willpower and volition required for a role individual to function as an organizational cell. Playing a specific role requires continuous functioning as a particular cell of the corporate organization. A momentary impulse cannot guarantee the full performance of duties. There must be a specific passion that continually strengthens their willpower, enabling them to withstand setbacks and difficulties without retreating.

4. Passion and Sacrifice Spirit. They possess the passion and drive to fully perform their duties and do their work well — this is the spirit of self-sacrifice that a role individual must have to function as an organizational cell. Like white blood cells in the human body, which are always ready to fight invading microorganisms to the death, without such a spirit, one cannot fully perform their duties due to excessive concern for gains and losses.

These four conditions are also prerequisites for a specific role to become an outstanding employee of the enterprise. An individual entering the corporate organization and assuming certain responsibilities will inevitably become an excellent employee of the company if they meet these four conditions.

An individual entering a corporate organization to play a specific role cannot automatically ensure that their abilities meet the requirements of their role or that they have the willpower, enthusiasm, patience, and spirit of sacrifice needed to fully perform their duties and excel in their work. Therefore, it is necessary to rely on role management to provide support and assurance. The content of role management directly corresponds to the four conditions required for a role to fully perform their duties and excel in their work: competency management, willpower management, emotion management, and sentiment management.
