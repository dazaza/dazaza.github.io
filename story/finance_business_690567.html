Hertz becomes the fastest-growing car rental brand in China

by anonymous on 2012-03-05 10:53:04

As a global leading car rental company, Hertz Rent A Car provides car rental services in 146 countries around the world, with over 8,500 rental outlets. Hertz processes more than 40 million bookings annually. Among these, North America, Europe, Australasia, and the major countries in Asia are the largest and most mature car rental markets, while China is increasingly becoming the fastest-growing emerging car rental market. Hertz has long-term cooperation projects with over 60 international airlines, hotels, and banks, continuously offering customers benefits and surprises through mileage exchange point programs and seasonal discount promotions. As early as 2002, the international car rental giant Hertz entered the Chinese market.

The car rental industry entered the Chinese market relatively late. In its early stages, China's car rental industry mainly served corporate users such as foreign enterprises and large state-owned enterprises. It is only in recent years that there has been a significant push into personal business. With the rapid development of China's automotive industry and social economy, people's consumption levels and travel demands have also increased accordingly. This has created fundamental conditions for the scaled development of the car rental industry.

The car rental industry is capital-intensive. Once companies break through financial bottlenecks, they will surely experience rapid growth. The increasing scale of operations, which brings economies of scale, is the trend in the global car rental industry. In China, car rental services are concentrated in first-tier cities, with Shanghai, Beijing, Guangzhou, and Shenzhen accounting for more than half of the market share. In 2010, the car rental penetration rate in Shanghai and Shenzhen reached 1.2%, approaching the U.S.'s average of 1.3% during the same period, while the penetration rate in third-tier cities was only 0.04%. Despite this, the vast potential for development in China's car rental market is undeniable. Large chain car rental companies like CAR Inc. have accelerated their expansion in recent years, opening new stores and gradually forming a network extension trend led by first-tier cities, extending to second- and third-tier cities.

**About Hertz**

Hertz Global was founded in 1918 in the United States and is the world's largest car rental brand, with over 8,500 rental locations in 146 countries worldwide. In 2002, Hertz entered the Chinese market and established a wholly-owned subsidiary in 2009, providing a full range of car rental services including domestic rentals, outbound rentals for Chinese citizens, and inbound rentals for foreigners. Additionally, Hertz offers heavy equipment, construction machinery, and industrial equipment rentals globally, serving shipyards, automobile industries, power sectors, industrial enterprises, and infrastructure industries. Hertz is the only provider of comprehensive mobility solutions for vehicles and equipment across five continents. Hertz is committed to becoming the preferred mobility solutions provider for both business and individual customers in every market around the globe.
