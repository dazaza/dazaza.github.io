The issue of the RMB exchange rate - What should China and the US do?

by alexsgxu on 2010-04-09 21:09:21

The issue of the RMB exchange rate has now been politicized! Last week, when I was discussing her speech topic with my daughter, I suggested she could consider talking about the issue of the RMB exchange rate. However, she told me that it was a political issue. I was quite surprised at the time, but then I realized that the issue of the RMB exchange rate had indeed been politicized. And once any issue is politicized, it will lead to extreme solutions and methods, and may even bring about revolution.

Who determines the exchange rate? How was the initial RMB exchange rate established, and according to what common reference point? These have become history. However, one can imagine that the US dollar was the hardest currency in the world at the time, and all countries inevitably referred to the US dollar to set their currency exchange rates. This was understandable. For the United States, if other countries can refer to the US dollar to set their exchange rates, does the US government have the right to refer to other countries' currencies to set the conversion ratio of the US dollar, especially when a country's currency value reaches a certain level? This is a big issue.

As an American, I believe that the problems facing the United States now cannot be solved simply by changing the RMB exchange rate, just as the appreciation of the Japanese yen cannot solve the economic problems of the United States. The general problem now for the governments of the United States and some Western countries is that politicians are weak, selfish, dishonest, and willing to use any means necessary to gain votes. Eligible voters mostly care about their own interests and focus on short-term benefits. As a result, these special interest groups, including government employees, do not care how poor the country is or whether it has the ability to pay, regardless of how much debt is incurred, as long as their own interests are not affected.

This is the biggest flaw in the current electoral system! No one is willing to sacrifice their political interests. Most politicians are unwilling to take risks and dare not fight against these special interest groups. Everyone wants to be the good guy, buy votes, and eventually deplete the nation's wealth... After these problems arise, some politicians will try to divert these contradictions elsewhere, including using the depreciation of the US dollar to solve these issues. Perhaps this might help the United States a little in the short term, but from a long-term perspective, it can only make the social problems in our American society more severe.

I hope that the US government and voters can use this opportunity to rise up, improve our consumption habits, reduce unnecessary waste, learn frugality, learn to save, learn to make good budgets, and learn to balance earning and spending appropriately.

Some people can consume in advance, but others must pay in advance. If an entire country is consuming in advance, then other countries must pay for this advanced consumption. But these debts will eventually need to be repaid. I don't want my children to bear so much debt in the future...

As a Chinese person, I believe that looking at the internal situation in China, whether the RMB exchange rate appreciates or depreciates, it cannot truly solve domestic problems in China. Exports can alleviate some internal problems and contradictions in China, but they cannot solve the potential development issues and crises in China.

Many decisions made by the central government are very reasonable, but without enforcement, they are empty words. Only when the central government and local governments work together, combat corruption, deal with bureaucracy, reduce waste by local governments, improve the administrative efficiency of all levels of government, and focus everyone's eyes on long-term benefits and the overall interests of China, can we ensure the proper adjustment of investment structures, accelerate the development of central and western regions, boost domestic demand, and improve the living standards of the people. This is the only way to address the huge potential crises in China's development.

At the same time, for local governments and local courts that ignore the central government and national laws, the central government should have the ability to seriously handle these issues. Only by creating a fair legal, administrative, and business environment nationwide can we attract more investment companies to develop in China and inland areas, create job opportunities and taxes for central and western regions. Especially some officials in central and western regions, including local judges, are highly corrupt, ignoring the fairness of the law, taking advantage of every opportunity to collude with local evil forces, finding various excuses to harm coastal companies and foreign companies investing in the central and western regions, becoming stumbling blocks to the development of these regions. Both the central and local governments should be alert and govern in a timely manner so that the local economy can develop healthily and steadily, improving the lives of the people. Fortunately, we see many excellent government officials and judges who uphold the law, so we have great confidence in China's development. I just hope that this development can be faster and healthier, improving the quality of life for ordinary people, and ensuring that events like gutter oil, toxic chopsticks, lunch boxes, melamine-tainted milk, and other embarrassing incidents for the Chinese do not happen again on Chinese soil, providing a fair and healthy living environment for the majority of hardworking people.
