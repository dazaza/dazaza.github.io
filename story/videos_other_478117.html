What does Nestle use to ensure food hygiene? How can a major brand be without style?

by jdhgsa078 on 2011-08-16 15:46:22

May 2011: According to the Peninsula Metropolis Daily, Ms. Li, a citizen, spent 10.8 yuan to buy a pack of Nestlé blackcurrant-flavored fructooligosaccharide powder at a supermarket in Shinan District. After dissolving it, she found some silver metallic residue at the bottom of the bottle. Ms. Li took the fructooligosaccharide powder to the supermarket for an explanation. The staff of the supermarket said that they did not know what this was and needed the manufacturer to conduct a test, and would compensate Ms. Li ten times according to the Food Safety Law. The reporter then called the Beijing Nestlé Consumer Service Hotline, and the staff said that someone would be sent to the scene tomorrow for inspection and then reply to the reporter.

Nestlé infant cereal refused on-site identification, consumers frankly say no sincerity

December 12, 2010: According to People's Daily Online, Mr. Chen, a consumer in Guangzhou, found dry matter in the Nestlé infant cereal he fed his baby, and after several communications with Nestlé about this issue, it still hasn't been resolved. Moreover, under the condition that Nestlé failed to obtain the dry matter, the consumer has not received any contact from Nestlé staff since then. Mr. Chen said: They don't take it seriously at all, there is absolutely no sincerity.

More than a month has passed, and Mr. Chen's problem remains unresolved. Mr. Chen introduced to the editor that the Nestlé staff claimed the sample was lost, and after failing to obtain another part of the sample in his hand, they have never contacted him again. With their current attitude, I now most want everyone to know that Nestlé is real foreign garbage, said Mr. Chen.

Metallic substances found in Nestlé soft candies, child nearly swallowed them

May 2010: According to the Beijing Morning Post, two packs of Nestlé soft candies were bought from the supermarket, and two pieces of metal were found inside. Consumers requested the supermarket and the factory to identify them and demanded compensation. The factory and the supermarket managers stated that they would give 10 times compensation according to relevant regulations.

This morning, the reporter met Mr. Ye and his mother, and saw the two pieces of metal found in the soft candy. According to Mr. Ye's introduction, yesterday morning, he and his mother went shopping at a supermarket in the urban area, spending more than 20 yuan to buy two packs of Nestlé brand soft candies. After returning home, his mother opened a pack and ate one piece, suddenly biting something very hard. At first, his mother thought it was rock sugar, but later felt increasingly uncomfortable, spitting it out and finding it was a small piece of metal. Subsequently, a larger piece of metal was also found.
