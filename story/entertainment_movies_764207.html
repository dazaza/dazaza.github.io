Synopsis:

In the spring of 1861, just before the outbreak of the Civil War, Scarlett O'Hara, a beautiful Southern belle from Tara plantation, is in love with Ashley Wilkes, the son of another plantation owner. However, Ashley chooses to marry Scarlett's cousin, the gentle and kind Melanie Hamilton. Out of jealousy, Scarlett hastily marries Melanie's younger brother, Charles Hamilton. Soon after, the American Civil War breaks out, and both Ashley and Charles are called to the front lines. Charles dies early in the war, leaving Scarlett a widow but still deeply in love with Ashley.

One day at a charity ball held to raise funds for the Confederate cause, Scarlett meets Rhett Butler, a dashing and wealthy merchant who begins courting her. Despite his advances, Scarlett remains focused on winning Ashley's affections, which ultimately leads to rejection.

As the war progresses, the Southern forces suffer defeat, and Atlanta becomes overcrowded with wounded soldiers. Scarlett and Melanie volunteer as nurses to care for the injured. When news spreads that Union troops are approaching, many families flee the city. Unfortunately, Melanie goes into labor, forcing Scarlett to stay behind to assist her.

With Union forces closing in, Scarlett pleads with Rhett to help her and Melanie, who has just given birth, return to Tara. Rhett reveals his intention to join the Confederate army in their final stand and bids farewell to Scarlett with an embrace and a kiss. Left alone, Scarlett bravely drives them back to Tara, only to find it ransacked by Union soldiers and her mother deceased from shock.

After the war ends, life remains difficult. The Northern rulers demand heavy taxes from the plantation owners. In desperation, Scarlett travels to Atlanta to ask Rhett for money, only to learn he has been imprisoned. On her way back, she encounters Frank Kennedy, a wealthy man originally intended to marry her sister. To save her family from financial ruin, Scarlett tricks him into marrying her.

Scarlett illegally employs convicts in Frank's lumber mill and conducts profitable business with Northern merchants. Meanwhile, Rhett regains his freedom through bribery and resumes his complicated relationship with Scarlett.

Frank and Ashley, having joined a secret anti-government organization, are ambushed by Union soldiers during a meeting. Frank is killed, and Ashley escapes wounded, eventually returning to Melanie under Rhett's assistance. Now widowed again, Scarlett agrees to marry Rhett, who had made a fortune smuggling arms and supplies during the war. They settle into a luxurious mansion in Atlanta. A year later, their daughter Bonnie Blue Butler is born, and Rhett lavishes all his affection on her.

When Rhett discovers Scarlett looking at Ashley's photograph, it leads to a rift between them. Later, Scarlett's passionate embrace of Ashley on the eve of his birthday causes scandalous rumors, though Melanie refuses to believe any impropriety exists between them. Rhett, however, isn't so forgiving.

Upon learning she is pregnant again, Scarlett angrily tells Rhett, who doubts the child's paternity. In her fury, Scarlett attempts to strike him but accidentally falls down the stairs, causing a miscarriage. Feeling remorseful, Rhett resolves to reconcile with Scarlett, but their conversation is interrupted by the tragic news of their daughter Bonnie falling from a horse and dying. Simultaneously, Melanie falls ill from exhaustion. Before passing away, she entrusts her husband Ashley and son to Scarlett's care, urging her to keep this arrangement secret. Overwhelmed by grief, Scarlett realizes her idealized love for Ashley was a fantasy and that she truly loves Rhett.

Rushing home to confess her feelings to Rhett, she finds him unwilling to believe her anymore. Determined to leave her, Rhett decides to return to his hometown in search of better days. Standing in the misty yard, Scarlett recalls her father's words: "Land is the only thing in the world worth working for...worth fighting for...worth dying for." Resolved to hold onto her land and rebuild her life, Scarlett looks forward to a brighter tomorrow.
