Taobao Mall attack incident escalates, Jack Ma's microblog suggests fatigue and doubt in compromise

by edivista on 2011-10-14 08:39:28

Click to enter Jack Ma's Weibo

The online protest action that began on the evening of November 11th was the largest scale protest in Taobao Mall's history. From the large sellers in Taobao Mall, to the merchants with popular keywords in Taobao Express, and then to Taobao Juhuasuan, none were spared.

On one side, tens of thousands of Taobao sellers gathered on the YY voice chat platform were still passionately accusing at this moment. On the other side, Alibaba's leader Jack Ma expressed his feelings through Weibo: "I'm heartbroken, I'm really tired, I really want to give up."

On one hand, there is the mall that insists on building a city of quality, and on the other hand, there are small and medium-sized sellers who are unwilling to leave the mall. How difficult a choice does the price increase of Taobao Mall bring to them? (Click to enter Jack Ma's Weibo)

Jack Ma posted three consecutive Weibo posts

The disputes caused by Taobao Mall announcing an upgrade to its merchant management system have entered the fourth day. The main actions taken by Taobao sellers remain attacks on large sellers, placing orders for goods and then applying for refunds. Affected large sellers reduce their losses by taking down products.

As the situation escalates, Duowan YY, chosen by small and medium sellers as a communication platform, issued a statement, urging both sides to remain rational and communicate, jointly maintaining the healthy development of the internet society, and hoping they (the organizers of the protest activities) establish communication channels with Taobao within the legal limits using appropriate methods.

Alibaba Chairman and CEO Jack Ma continuously posted 3 Weibo messages, emphasizing that making money is not Taobao's goal, and they will continue to adhere to their choices, while also expressing the feeling of being "heartbroken, really tired, and really wanting to give up." Meanwhile, the director of Taobao Juhuasuan revealed in a Weibo post that Taobao employees had received threatening calls.

This incident originated from Taobao Mall announcing an upgrade to its merchant management system, requiring merchants to pay a breach of contract deposit of over ten thousand yuan, and increasing the annual technical service fee from 6000 yuan to two tiers of 30,000 and 60,000 yuan. Although Taobao Mall emphasized conditional returns based on business performance, the new regulations sparked two different voices.

A seller named CiKe SunClot, claiming to be a grassroots merchant, proclaimed on their store homepage in the mall: "Clearing out early means liberation early."

Merchants such as Handu Yishe and Qigege, who have been attacked since the evening of November 11th, continued to call for small and medium sellers to stay calm. Qigege Louise stated: "If we knock down Taobao or Taobao Mall, where in the entire internet can we find a place that will never reform or change?" She urged everyone to stay calm, not be swayed, and not be exploited!

Why do small and medium sellers not want to leave the mall?

Taobao Mall was established in 2008, and in June of this year, Taobao announced a split into three parts, officially making Taobao Mall independent. According to data released by EnfoDesk of Analysys Mason in the "2011 Second Quarter China Online Retail Market Quarterly Monitoring" report, Taobao Mall ranks first, holding a 32.8% share of the domestic B2C market. Taobao Mall's data shows that it currently has more than 70,000 brands and over 50,000 merchants.

Previously, Zeng Ming, Chief Strategist of Alibaba Group, said in an interview with Nanfang Daily: "We hope Taobao.com, Taobao Mall, and Etao increasingly differentiate themselves. As for which platform sellers and buyers choose, that is the result of free market competition." Behind the adjustment of the renewal rules for the mall, Taobao Mall also provided merchants with the option to develop on Taobao Market, but small and medium sellers seem uninterested in this option.

An unnamed Taobao Mall seller told Nanfang Daily: "We wouldn't consider leaving Taobao Market or Taobao Mall either; similar situations would arise on other B2C platforms. We don't want to move to Taobao Market because the customers and traffic accumulated over years of operation cannot be transferred overnight."

In response, Taobao Mall told reporters: "In the new rules of Taobao Mall, merchants who do not meet the mall requirements or refuse to renew can transfer to the Taobao Market C2C platform to continue operations, and their historical transactions, performance, and cumulative credit will be converted to Taobao stores."

In addition, operating costs are another major factor for merchants to consider. The aforementioned seller stated that the current charging model of Taobao Mall is the transaction amount (excluding shipping fees) multiplied by the corresponding technical service rate + annual technical service fee, with an annual technical service fee of 6000 yuan, and the technical service rate varying depending on the category. For the clothing category they belong to, it is 5%.

However, Taobao Market does not require any basic fees. Merchants can selectively promote their products, such as through直通车promotion, which incurs charges.

Currently, when operating in the mall, we do not need to spend too much on promotion fees. But moving to the market, due to its large size, the promotion fees might even exceed the basic fees charged after the mall upgrade, presenting a difficult choice for small and medium sellers.

Chen Shousong, analyst at Analysys International, told Nanfang Daily that raising the charge standards of Taobao Mall is both about raising the threshold and shaping the image of a quality city, while also providing merchants with more valuable services to enhance their competitiveness and increase their revenue. For small and medium sellers, the increase from six thousand to thirty thousand is significant, but if sales levels improve, profits rise, and the proportion of basic fees in operating costs will decrease, reducing pressure.

Correspondingly, Taobao Mall proposed an annual fee return system, meaning as long as merchants' dynamic service scores reach 4.6 points and transaction volumes reach a certain level, part or all of the fees will be returned. For example, clothing merchants who achieve a service score of 4.6 points and an annual transaction volume of 1.2 million yuan, averaging around 3000 yuan per day, will receive full return of the annual technical service fee. Zhang Yong, President of Taobao Mall, said: "Most of the current merchants in Taobao Mall meet the annual fee return standard."

Two months to consider

Opinions among Taobao merchants regarding this network attack remain divided.

Yu Guofu, member of the Policy and Legal Committee of the China Electronic Commerce Association, stated that Taobao Mall is a company, whether to sign a contract with a particular merchant, how to sign, and at what price, is an autonomous business decision. The behavior of network attackers is illegal and should bear legal responsibility. Wang Ran, CEO of Easy Capital, expressed via Weibo: "We hope Taobao Mall restores order soon. Regardless of who, no one should infringe upon innocent others just to protect their own interests."

In the announcement, Taobao Mall stated: "This attack is deliberately manipulated by a group of people claiming to be small shop owners in Taobao Mall, attempting to force Taobao Mall to change the newly announced 2012 merchant recruitment renewal and rule adjustments." There are indications that organizations previously severely cracked down on by Taobao for 'brushing diamonds' and malicious negative reviews also participated.

Taobao Mall told Nanfang Daily yesterday: "Among those currently gathered on YY, so-called 'management layers' have formed, but until now, these so-called 'management layers' have never communicated with us."

According to regulations, Taobao Mall's entry contracts are signed according to the calendar year. That is, regardless of when the contract is signed during the year, its validity period ends on December 31st of that year. All new and old sellers next year will operate under the new rules. Under mutual selection, sellers and Taobao Mall still have two months to consider whether to continue cooperation and jointly build this city of quality.
