Tianjin self-driving tour

by iuleiitb on 2010-04-16 13:51:04

On the afternoon of the third day of the first lunar month in 2010 (February 16), we drove at a speed of 110 km/h onto the Jingfu Expressway from the Tianqiao entrance in North Jinan. After a journey of 200 kilometers, we arrived at the Nanshi Food Street in Tianjin around 6 p.m., had dinner there, and then checked into the Haihe Holiday Hotel next to the Tianjin Eye (the largest Ferris wheel in Asia) for 408 yuan per luxury twin room without breakfast during the off-season. We then took a ride on the Tianjin Eye (ticket price: 50 yuan per person) to enjoy the beautiful night view of Tianjin. The Ferris wheel was built on a highway bridge spanning the Haihe River.

On the fourth day of the first lunar month (February 17), we woke up naturally, settled the bill, found a place for breakfast, and visited Nankai University, the alma mater of Premier Zhou Enlai. Since our child would be entering junior high school in the summer, we provided some educational guidance. After the visit, we followed the navigation instructions and arrived at the aircraft carrier Kiev in Hangu after about 1.5 hours. It was impressive but not as good as active service carriers, hehe. The admission fee was 130 yuan per person. (Here's a reminder: the ticket includes a 4D movie shown next to the entrance. Each screening lasts 15 minutes with a 45-minute interval before the next one, meaning one hour per session with only 100 people allowed per session. If the timing is right, you can watch the movie before visiting the ship.) Follow the arrow signs on the deck for the tour, and finally visit the upper deck (aircraft takeoff and landing platform) and control room. There is only a sailor restaurant on the ship for meals, providing bowl-packed instant noodles and grilled sausages. Military compressed biscuits are very expensive: 30 yuan per pack (one pack contains 8 small pieces), with average taste, hehe, they are military products. In the afternoon, after the visit, we drove directly to Beijing via the Jing-Tian Expressway. (Note: now obtaining an entry permit does not provide on-site exhaust testing. Friends driving into Beijing must obtain an emission certificate in their respective vehicle registration locations. It costs 70 yuan for two in Jinan. Place the green certificate in the upper right corner of the front windshield, keep the white small certificate with the driving license, valid for one year). Due to the off-season, we stayed at the Peking University Boya International Conference Hotel, where a luxury twin room cost 400 yuan per night without breakfast, usually over 4,000 yuan, haha.

On the fifth day of the first lunar month (February 18), we woke up naturally, had a simple breakfast, and headed straight to the Palace Museum (best to take the subway, even though it's the off-season, parking is very difficult). Tickets can be purchased by card (adults 40 yuan/person, students 20 yuan/person, junior high students free with student ID). Renting an electronic guide for 10 yuan (with a 100-yuan deposit) was a good personal guide. The Treasure Gallery and Clock Gallery charge separately (10 yuan/person). The visit to the Palace Museum takes about 3 hours. For lunch, we ate Jindong meat pies, which tasted good but had too little meat filling. In the afternoon, we went to Daguanyuan. Originally, we had reserved electronic tickets (a gift from the Beijing Tourism Bureau), but they couldn't be found at the ticket office (but were usable at the Summer Palace three days later???) I think there might have been a temple fair inside, making the ticket much more expensive (normally 10 yuan/person, 25 yuan/person during the temple fair). We had no choice but to buy tickets to enter (25 yuan/person). The temple fair inside was similar to those across the country, with Peking Opera performances in the grand theater. We took a quick look around. For dinner, we had old Beijing fried sauce noodles, liver stew, and other local flavors, then casually visited the famous IKEA Home Furnishings before returning to the hotel to rest.

On the sixth day of the first lunar month (February 19), we got up early and took the Line 4 subway (from the East Gate Station of Peking University) to the Fuguo Underwater World located south of the Beijing Workers' Stadium (admission fee 85 yuan/person, tickets can be purchased by card). The scale and facilities were somewhat outdated, not as good as the aquarium at the zoo (which we had already visited multiple times at 100 yuan/person), giving us a feeling of being scammed. After lunch, we went to the new China Science and Technology Museum in the Olympic Park. The museum name was inscribed by Deng Xiaoping. By the time we arrived, it was already past 2 p.m. We watched the giant screen 3D movie "Underwater World," which was stunning, dynamic, and extremely stereoscopic. Truly worthy of being a national science museum, we also visited the main exhibition hall (admission fee 30 yuan/person). Staying close to Tsinghua and Peking Universities, we then went to Tsinghua University, entering through the west gate. The campus was so large that we rented two bicycles (single-person bikes for 10 yuan each, double-person bikes for 15 yuan each) and rode around to visit scenic spots such as Qinghuayuan, the old teaching buildings of the hydroelectric civil engineering department, Lotus Pond under the Moonlight, and Shuimu Tsinghua. We informed our child that this was one of the top universities in the country, hoping he could realize his dream. Hehe!

In the evening, we had the legendary roast duck at Quanjude in Qianmen. It felt like fast food (100 yuan per person including a small plate of duck meat (supposedly half a duck), a small plate of sauce and green onion threads, 5 small pancakes, and a small paper cup of duck soup). We had been staying at the Peking University Boya International Conference Hotel for two days and hadn't yet soaked in the hot spring inside the hotel. According to the hotel, the water comes from 3,000 meters underground. Just the three of us, it was a great way to relax and feel refreshed.

On the seventh day of the first lunar month (February 20), we woke up naturally, had breakfast, and then went to Peking University behind the hotel. Entering from the east gate, we passed by the Boya Tower, Weiming Lake, Red Building, and other scenic spots, arriving at the famous west gate of Peking University to take commemorative photos. We smoothly exchanged the pre-booked electronic tickets at the Summer Palace ticket office, saving 50 yuan. We continued to rent electronic guides, finding the royal garden grand and magnificent. However, the lake area has shrunk somewhat, leaving a slight regret. Initially, we planned to return to Jinan in the afternoon, but since Yuanmingyuan was next to the Summer Palace, we thought it was a good opportunity for patriotic education, so we postponed our return until the evening. After purchasing tickets (adults 15 yuan, students 10 yuan) and entering the park, we passed through the bustling temple fair and arrived at the ruins of the Grand Waterworks. Another ticket was required (here’s my personal opinion: Chinese people, especially children receiving patriotic education, should not forget national humiliation, strive hard, and revitalize China. There shouldn’t be another garden within the park). Throughout the entire visit, we only saw one foreign student touring with Chinese classmates, unlike the numerous foreigners at places like the Summer Palace, possibly because they don't want to face the crimes committed by their ancestors!

Around 6 p.m., we boarded the Jing-Jin-Tang Expressway at the Minzufosi Bridge on the Third Ring Road. The journey went smoothly, and we safely returned to Jinan at 11 p.m., concluding our trip.

Summary: A self-driving journey of over a thousand kilometers, with fuel costs exceeding 600 yuan and bridge/toll fees over 700 yuan. Accommodation costs were 1,600 yuan, scenic spot tickets over 500 yuan, and shopping/dining over 2,000 yuan. Total: 5,000 yuan. Shared travel insights with friends, apologies for any unclear details!
