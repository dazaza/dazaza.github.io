Who will be the champion, watch Panda Burning Incense PK Grey Pigeon Trojan

by eeyye on 2008-04-13 23:56:17

At the end of 2006, during what could be called the "Oscars" of the cybersecurity world, all major security companies unanimously awarded the title of Best Main Character to the Panda Burning Incense virus. At the start of 2007, an older Trojan—Gray Pigeon—made a comeback with significant force, threatening to reclaim the throne as the best main character. Following claims that "Gray Pigeon is ten times more powerful than Panda Burning Incense," news of online banking thefts resurfaced, adding a mysterious veil to Gray Pigeon. Today, let’s put Panda Burning Incense and Gray Pigeon through a comprehensive head-to-head comparison to see if Gray Pigeon is truly more harmful than Panda Burning Incense.

The samples of the two viruses used in this head-to-head comparison come from security vendors. The comparison is divided into three categories: typical behavior, virus activity, and transmission methods. Who will emerge victorious in the world of viruses? Let's find out.

### First Category: Typical Behavior

**Chinese Name:** Panda Burning Incense Virus (also known as Wuhan Boy)

**English Name:** Worm.WhBoy (the number of variants discovered so far has exceeded 400)

**Typical Behavior of Panda Burning Incense:**

After infection, users will notice that many .EXE file icons turn into a panda burning incense, which is where the name "Panda Burning Incense" originates. However, some recent variants no longer use this well-known icon. Some variants can directly update themselves over the internet, while others can infect files in web formats such as htm, html, asp, php, jsp, and aspx. Once a web server is infected, it means that all computers browsing these web pages may automatically download and become infected with the Panda Burning Incense virus.

This series of variants releases the following typical files:

- In the root directory of the partition: setup.exe, autorun.inf, %System%Fuckjacks.exe, %System%Driversspoclsv.exe.

- In a local area network environment: GameSetup.exe.

**Typical Behavior of Gray Pigeon:**

**Chinese Name:** Gray Pigeon Trojan (with over 60,000 variants)

**English Name:** win32.hack.huigezi

Gray Pigeon became famous nearly five years before Panda Burning Incense, making it a "senior predecessor" to the latter. As early as 2001, Gray Pigeon earned the reputation of being a "high-risk Trojan." Thanks to its author's persistent efforts, by this year, the number of Gray Pigeon variants had already surpassed 60,000. Almost everyone knows about Panda Burning Incense because of its cute and distinctive icon. However, after Gray Pigeon Trojan infiltrates a system, only very experienced computer users might detect any anomalies. Most users remain completely unaware, as if there were an invisible thief residing in their home.

According to information provided by a technical staff member at a security vendor, Gray Pigeon can be installed in any location, determined by the attacker. The file name of the Gray Pigeon Trojan can be customized by the attacker, and it can inject itself into the processes of normal programs to hide itself. Windows Task Manager cannot detect the presence of the virus, requiring third-party tools to check.

### Summary of the First Round:

Panda Burning Incense destroys files of different formats on the computer and causes a large number of processes to terminate, greatly inconveniencing users. Initially, it seems that Gray Pigeon does not affect the use of the computer. Therefore, in this category, Panda Burning Incense wins.

---

### Second Category: Virus Behavior

**Panda Burning Incense's Behavior:**

As mentioned earlier, Panda Burning Incense corrupts various types of files and disrupts multiple processes, causing significant inconvenience to users.

**Gray Pigeon's Behavior:**

Once inside a system, Gray Pigeon remains hidden and allows attackers to remotely control the infected machine. It can steal sensitive information, including passwords, bank details, and other personal data, without the user's knowledge. This makes it particularly dangerous for users who conduct online transactions or store sensitive information on their computers.

### Summary of the Second Round:

Although Panda Burning Incense causes immediate disruption, Gray Pigeon operates stealthily and can lead to more severe consequences, such as financial loss or identity theft. In terms of noticeable impact, Panda Burning Incense wins this round.

---

### Third Category: Transmission Methods

**Panda Burning Incense Transmission Methods:**

One of the key characteristics of Panda Burning Incense is its ability to spread via websites. USB drives, local networks, and web pages are just some of the ways it propagates. Essentially, it can use almost any conceivable method to spread.

**Gray Pigeon Transmission Methods:**

Unlike Panda Burning Incense, Gray Pigeon itself cannot replicate. Its primary transmission methods include:

1. **Web-based Transmission:** The virus creator embeds the Gray Pigeon Trojan into web pages, and users get infected upon visiting.

2. **Email-based Transmission:** Gray Pigeon is bundled in email attachments and spreads when opened.

3. **IM Chat Tool Transmission:** Links or files carrying Gray Pigeon are shared through instant messaging platforms.

4. **Illegal Software Transmission:** The virus creator bundles Gray Pigeon into illegal software, and users become infected upon downloading, extracting, and installing the software.

### Summary of the Third Round:

Both viruses have unique strengths in their transmission methods. While Panda Burning Incense uses vulnerabilities to spread, Gray Pigeon relies on bundling within malicious software. Overall, both methods are equally effective, resulting in a tie.

---

### Final Summary:

Despite the results of the three rounds showing that Panda Burning Incense and Gray Pigeon are evenly matched overall, closer examination of Gray Pigeon's behavior and consequences reveals that the Gray Pigeon Trojan is actually much more harmful than Panda Burning Incense. Both viruses cause damage to users to varying degrees, but it is heartening to see that security vendors are working tirelessly to combat both threats. Whether it is extinguishing the incense in Panda's hand or cutting off the wings of Gray Pigeon, the goal is to create a cleaner and safer cyberspace for all internet users.
