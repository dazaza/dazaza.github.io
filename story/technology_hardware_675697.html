Methods for repairing a winder

by malayo on 2012-02-25 11:39:55

I. Spare Parts Replacement Method

In the repair of winding machines, good spare parts are used to replace suspected faulty components. After the maintenance personnel basically determine the cause of the fault, this method can quickly diagnose the fault range and allow the winding machine to return to normal operation swiftly. The faulty component is then sent back for repair. This is currently the most commonly used method for troubleshooting.

II. Trial Operation Program Compilation Method

Compiling a reasonable program and running it successfully serves as the basis for determining whether the entire system function is intact. Some incorrect settings of winding parameters may lead to system failures or certain functions becoming invalid. Sometimes, faults that cause shutdowns are due to errors in user programs. In such cases, trial operation programs can be compiled to check and correct the errors in the user program, ensuring its normal operation.

III. Effective Use of Adjustable Components

Adjusting various adjustable components, such as winding tension, screen voltage, and starting position of the wire-laying rack, is an easy but effective method during repairs. By adjusting these components, some minor faults can be corrected. For example, during the repair of a winding machine that had been in use for many years in a certain company, the display on the system screen was dim. After adjusting the screen power supply voltage, it returned to normal.

IV. Maintenance Information Tracking Method

Based on the actual operation of the winding machine and past fault records, occasional faults caused by design flaws or process defects in the winding machine can be identified. These issues can be resolved through continuous modifications and improvements to the system software or hardware. These modifications are provided in the form of maintenance information to the maintenance personnel of Shanghai Longben Winding Equipment Co., Ltd. Through customer feedback, our company can use this as the basis for troubleshooting, correctly and thoroughly eliminating faults. Reasonable customer feedback is also a powerful driving force for our continuous improvement. In short, the first step in repairing a winding machine is to determine the true cause and location of the fault based on the fault phenomenon. There are no strict boundaries between these fault handling methods when applied in practice. Sometimes, one method alone can eliminate the fault, while other times multiple methods must be used simultaneously. The effectiveness mainly depends on the experience of the maintenance technician and their depth of understanding and mastery of the system's principles and structure. This is both a necessary quality for maintenance personnel and a high requirement placed on them. It requires that numerical control winding machine maintenance personnel be familiar with the structure and design philosophy of the winding machine, as well as the performance of numerical control winding machines. Only in this way can they quickly find the cause of the fault and determine its location. Additionally, during maintenance, experienced technicians should have some ability to draw engineering diagrams in order to conduct on-site measurements of certain circuits and components.

V. Whole Machine Reset Method

For general winding machine faults caused by instantaneous issues, hardware reset or sequentially turning off and on the system power can clear the fault. If the system's working storage area becomes disordered due to unstable voltage, unplugging circuit boards, or insufficient voltage from the rectifier power supply, then the system must undergo initialization to clear the issue. Before clearing, it is important to back up the current data. If the fault cannot be eliminated after reset and initialization, then hardware replacement diagnostics should be performed.

VI. Environmental Quality Improvement Method

For some very strange faults that cannot be traced using exclusion and substitution methods, it is often necessary to start from the surrounding environment. The environment generally falls into two categories: power supply and space. Power supply improvements can be achieved by using stabilized isolated power supplies to reduce fluctuations from the power source. High-frequency interference from the power supply can be mitigated using capacitor filtering methods. These preventive measures can reduce faults caused by the power supply. Checking and improving grounding is also essential. There are various reasons for spatial interference, such as dust, gases, floating or vibrating foundations, spatial radiation interference, etc. A thorough analysis and resolution may require experienced maintenance technicians and specialized equipment for testing and maintenance.
