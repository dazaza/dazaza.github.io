Leading the whole body calisthenics can effectively prevent and treat mouse hand.

by sunkang147 on 2010-07-15 14:29:29

Introduction: After working in front of a computer and feeling tired, office workers like to shake their wrists or swing their hands, thinking that this can get rid of the seemingly insignificant "mouse hand". In fact, to prevent and treat "mouse hand", relying solely on local exercise is not very effective. Only by exercising the whole body and increasing the overall blood circulation can the function of the body's local areas be improved.

“Mouse hand” is medically known as carpal tunnel syndrome, which refers to symptoms caused by the compression of the median nerve and blood vessels entering the hand at the wrist. The main manifestations are stiffness, pain, and numbness in the index and middle fingers, along with weakness in the thumb muscles. In the past, people generally only focused on local exercises to improve the function of a certain part of the body. The latest view suggests that to change or improve the function of a certain part of the body, one should first engage in full-body exercises. Therefore, if you want to prevent and treat "mouse hand," simply shaking your hands is not enough.

The following is a set of full-body exercises designed to effectively prevent and treat "mouse hand." Just spend a few minutes each day, and it will surely help you effectively prevent and treat "mouse hand."

First section: Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and swing your arms back forcefully 20 times.

Shenzhen Physical Examination Second section: Walk with a cross-step while adding movements for the hands, especially the wrists, similar to dancing with music, which enhances the effect. Each session lasts 2-3 minutes.

Third section: Cross and flip your fingers, trying to lift them from the chest over the head, then stretch them backward toward the back 20 times.

Fourth section: Stretch both arms and hands after expanding the chest, extending them horizontally backward 20 times.

Fifth section: Place your palms down, extend your arms horizontally to the sides, and then lower them, mimicking the motion of flying geese, repeating this 20 times.

Sixth section: Place your right hand behind your back and use your left hand to hook your right hand over your shoulder; repeat with the opposite hand. Hold each position for 30 seconds. Then bring your hands together at the shoulders and try to stretch them upward and spread them wide, repeating this 20 times.

It is important to note that when doing all these exercises, it is recommended to start with good posture—standing up straight, pulling in your abdomen, lifting your buttocks—and adjust your breathing to deep abdominal breathing. You should slowly understand the key points of each movement and progress gradually without rushing.
