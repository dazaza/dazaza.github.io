American netizens on Amazon review "Chinese luxury" Laoganma

by anonymous on 2013-11-17 20:17:12

Not long ago, "Lao Gan Ma" caused a big stir on Weibo. The reason is that in the United States, "Lao Gan Ma" is absolutely considered as a "luxury imported from China". The price of a bottle of "Lao Gan Ma" in the US is several times higher than that in China. Do foreigners like "Lao Gan Ma"? What are their comments? Below, we have compiled some user reviews from the American Amazon website for our readers. Some of these reviews are laughably crying...

Kae Winters:

The crispy and delicious chili explodes in your mouth, it feels so wonderful! My friend recommended Lao Gan Ma to me, I will keep eating it. This stuff really tastes great, and there are many crunchy granules. The peanuts taste fantastic! Wonderful Lao Gan Ma, wonderful fresh spicy flavor!

Austin Baldwin:

I think Lao Gan Ma is the best-tasting food sold by China to the US. It has a smoky taste, crispy, spicy, oily, and every aspect is too perfect. But Amazon sells it so expensively. Just find a local Chinese-owned retail store; they almost all sell Lao Gan Ma, and a can costs only 3-4 dollars. Only fools would buy Lao Gan Ma online.

Hot Sauce Addict:

I have more than 20 different kinds of hot sauce at home, but I think this one is the best. Others told me that Lao Gan Ma has a complex taste, and I must say I agree with that point. There's spicy oil, chili flakes, and fermented broad beans (you can hardly taste the broad beans), and the combination with peanuts is perfect! And this hot sauce doesn't burn your mouth like Mexican habanero peppers; instead, it gently spreads in your mouth.

Lauxydad:

My wife loves it, but when I received it, the lid was already gone, and the chili oil had leaked all over the box... I applied for a refund, but no one responded to me. I will never use Amazon again unless you resolve this issue for me.

Erik Zenhausern:

I love my "Angry Lady Hot Sauce"! I don't know if it's from China, but it definitely tastes very "Chinese." I just can't stop, I even scoop it directly out of the bottle. It's really delicious!

G. Day:

I am obsessed with hot sauce, and I've been eating it for over 50 years. During this time, I've tried many different hot sauces, but none of them compare to Lao Gan Ma. My favorite way to eat it is dipping potstickers in Lao Gan Ma and white vinegar. The peanuts inside are interesting, adding another layer of tempting flavor, ah~ the spiciness is just right. Compared to habanero hot sauce, Lao Gan Ma stands on its own. Although I can buy a can of Lao Gan Ma for $2.39 at the Chinese store near my house, I prefer to spend much more money on Amazon. Lao Gan Ma is a masterpiece among hot sauces! I spend my money on it, and I'm willing!

MzZavala:

This stuff can be addictive! Be careful! I warned you! Once, while browsing an Asian supermarket, I was looking for a hot sauce, but the results were disappointing. I was looking for a sauce similar to what I ate in Sichuan cuisine restaurants. Finally, I found it on Amazon. The reviews looked good, so I bought two cans. I made the right decision! This hot sauce has a lot of oil, crisp chili, and peanuts, which are delicious! The spiciness is nice but not overwhelming, and the salty taste is good. Brilliant seasoning!

Joanna:

It's hard to find authentic Chinese hot sauce in the US. So I always stick to the ones I'm used to. I've liked this hot sauce for many years. The Lao Gan Ma series has many varieties, but my favorite is the chili oil. There's a sesame flavor, and it tastes different from other hot sauces. I also recommend it to my American friends around me. I tell them: this isn't the Mexican hot sauce you usually eat! LOL...

David Dalton:

This is a must-have condiment in your kitchen. I like spicy food, and this chili sauce is between medium and mild... To be precise, it should be a little spicier than mild, but not quite medium.

A.G.Smith:

A Chinese friend gave me this, and I like it, so I bought a lot more.

Rjm1604:

Fuchsia Dunlop's "Every Grain of Rice" recommends "Angry Lady Hot Sauce," and I thought this was the "Angry Lady Sauce" (Lao Gan Ma) I was buying. It turned out not to be. This hot sauce is a cooking seasoning, mainly consisting of black beans and chili. This thing should be paired with plain rice, vegetables, or dumplings. I tried it, it's okay. A bit crispy, not very spicy, and not the numbing flavor of Sichuan cuisine I wanted. I can finish two bowls of brown rice with just 1/5 of the bottle of chili. However, $8.59 per bottle is ridiculously expensive. The Chinese supermarket near my house only sells it for $2.79. This company seems to produce other chili sauces, so I guess I need to try them all. The ingredients of Lao Gan Ma with black beans include: soybeans, chili, vegetable oil, MSG (maybe?), sugar, sesame, Sichuan peppercorns. Delicious.

Ren:

I am Chinese, and I like Lao Gan Ma. My American friends also like it when I give it to them. However, this isn't the Lao Gan Ma sold in China. The one sold in China contains fermented broad beans, but there aren't any in this bottle.

Zhang Guoyu:

It's okay, but after opening the lid, I don't like the chili oil.

Catherine Brady:

I ordered 9 bottles of Lao Gan Ma, and I was so excited when I received the package! Unfortunately, the items were packed in a garbage bag, and 5 cans were broken, still dripping oil! Can your Amazon delivery get any worse? As someone in the catering business, I originally planned to purchase from Amazon, but you broke more than half? I'll switch to another company.

Carry.A.Lane:

My son loves it! When I received it, the lid was open, and some had spilled out, so I worried about spoilage and threw it away. But my son picked it up and solemnly swore to me that it was the best hot sauce he'd ever tasted. I bought another can, and I'll comment once I've eaten it.

ForeX Technology Company:

Don't let the name of the sauce fool you, you might think this is a liquid like water. I thought there would be a bit of oil, but there wasn't a drop. It's just leaves and chili mixed together. It's spicy, but not stimulating at all.
