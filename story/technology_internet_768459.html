The world's first 3D printed gun made of metal has been unveiled with a range of 27 meters.

by anonymous on 2013-11-17 18:34:29

The World's First Metal 3D Printed Gun

Until now, 3D printed guns have not been considered rare or novel. But what about a 3D printed gun made from metal? Recently, according to foreign media reports, Solid Concepts, an SLS service provider based in Texas, USA, has successfully manufactured the world's first metal 3D printed gun capable of accurately hitting targets over 30 yards (approximately 27 meters) away.

According to a blog post on Solid Concepts' official website, the model for this metal 3D printed gun is the classic 1911 pistol, featuring over 30 3D printed components made from 17-4 stainless steel and Inconel 625 materials, ensuring sufficient hardness. This marks the first time Solid Concepts has utilized this new technology to create a 3D printed gun, and it is also the first metal 3D printed gun in the industry.

Ken Firestone, Vice President of Solid Concepts’ Additive Manufacturing department, expressed great excitement: "It has been proven that it is possible to manufacture a 3D printed gun using metal, and we have initially mastered this manufacturing technology. Although there are some areas that need further refinement and improvement, at least we have started off well. Moreover, everything we do is entirely legal."

Firestone is correct; Solid Concepts has indeed obtained the Federal Firearms License (FFL), allowing them to legally produce and sell 3D printed guns.

It is understood that producing a metal 3D printed gun requires mastery of metal laser sintering technology, a highly precise manufacturing process that only professional companies like Solid Concepts can achieve. Currently, very few companies possess this capability. Compared to traditional investment casting processes, 3D printing offers better air-tightness and is more complex. When firing, the barrel of a metal 3D printed gun must withstand a pressure of 20,000 psi.

It is understood that Solid Concepts chose the classic M1911 model primarily because the M1911 pistol does not raise issues of patent infringement.
