A Grand Review of Nintendo 3DS Launch Games

by gadgetoflife on 2011-02-28 15:03:24

The Nintendo 3DS was officially released in Japan today. Let's take a look at what games are coming out with the 3DS so potential buyers can decide which game to purchase.

**nintendogs+cats**

There are three versions of nintendogs+cats: Nintendogs+Cats: French Bulldog New Friends, Nintendogs+cats Golden Retriever New Friends, and Nintendogs+cats Toy Poodle New Friends. The main difference between these versions is the initial dog breed available for selection. As the game progresses, players will unlock other breeds, eventually being able to choose from all 27 breeds freely.

Here’s the list of dog breeds across the three versions:

- Nintendogs+Cats: French Bulldog New Friends

- Nintendogs+cats: Golden Retriever Friends

- Nintendogs+Cats: Toy Poodle New Friends

Breeds include: French Bulldog, Golden Retriever, Toy Poodle, Shetland Sheepdog, Shiba Inu, Pembroke Welsh Corgi, Cavalier King Charles Spaniel, Beagle, Miniature Schnauzer, Chihuahua, Miniature Pinscher, Boxer, Dalmatian, Miniature Dachshund, Labrador Retriever, Yorkshire Terrier, Maltese, Shih Tzu, German Shepherd, Great Dane, Jack Russell Terrier, Siberian Husky, Pug, Bull Terrier, Basset Hound, Cocker Spaniel, Pomeranian.

All three versions initially offer Tabby, Siamese, and Persian cats as options. As a sequel to the popular NDS game Nintendogs and the only Nintendo-developed game in the 3DS launch lineup, Nintendogs+Cats is certainly not to be overlooked. The enhanced functions of the 3DS and its 3D display will surely make the various cats and dogs appear more lifelike.

Suitability for families: 5/5

Game excitement level: 2/5

3DS feature showcase level: 3/5

**Samurai Warriors Chronicles (战国无双：编年史)**

The Samurai Warriors series makes its debut on the Nintendo handheld consoles. Unlike other Samurai Warriors games, this one allows players to develop their own characters or choose famous warriors. Additionally, players can select four warriors and switch between them freely during gameplay.

The Musou series has always been known for its fast-paced action with a large number of enemies on screen, testing the capabilities of the 3DS.

Suitability for families: 2/5

Game excitement level: 4/5

3DS feature showcase level: 5/5

**Super Street Fighter IV 3D Edition (超级街头霸王4 3D版)**

The famous fighting game Street Fighter is now available on the 3DS, allowing you to "battle" anytime, anywhere. Watching the 3D Hadouken come flying at you will surely be thrilling.

In addition to regular wireless battles, there's also a "pass-by connection" feature where, even when the console is in sleep mode, encountering another player with this game will automatically trigger a battle. The winner gets a figure.

Suitability for families: 2/5

Game excitement level: 4/5

3DS feature showcase level: 4/5

**Ridge Racer 3D (山脊赛车3D版)**

The Ridge Racer series has never aimed for realism, so if you're looking for a realistic racing experience, this isn't the game for you. However, if you enjoy speed and exhilaration, this game is worth trying. All vehicles in the Ridge Racer series are original designs, offering a fresh experience.

As the only racing game in the launch lineup, Ridge Racer 3D will surely test whether the 3D screen causes dizziness due to quick camera changes. Fans of speed won't want to miss it.

Suitability for families: 3/5

Game excitement level: 4/5

3DS feature showcase level: 4/5

**Winning Eleven 3D Soccer**

As a popular cross-platform football game, Winning Eleven has faced stiff competition from the FIFA series in recent years but still has a strong following in Hong Kong.

With the 3D functionality, players can enjoy an enhanced sense of presence and spatial awareness, allowing for more accurate passing and shooting decisions. Football fans should definitely give this game a try.

Suitability for families: 4/5

Game excitement level: 3/5

3DS feature showcase level: 4/5

**Professor Layton and the Miracle Mask (レイトン教授と奇迹の仮面)**

The Professor Layton series has consistently performed well in Japan and overseas. This move to the 3DS promises a unique experience.

Known for its puzzles, this version of Professor Layton introduces 3D puzzles in addition to traditional 2D ones. It will be interesting to see if the difficulty increases.

Suitability for families: 4/5

Game excitement level: 2/5

3DS feature showcase level: 3/5

**Puzzle Bobble 3D (泡泡龙3D)**

The classic puzzle game Bubble Bobble is back with a new edition! This time, it features 3D visuals, though the game remains easy to pick up, making it ideal for children.

Although this version includes 3D effects, it does not support online multiplayer, which may be disappointing for some.

Suitability for families: 5/5

Game excitement level: 1/5

3DS feature showcase level: 2/5

**Combat of Giants: Dinosaurs 3D**

Part of Ubisoft's animal fighting series, Combat of Giants has previously been released on Wii, NDS, and DSi. In this game, players can raise their own dinosaurs and gain experience by battling other dinosaurs.

While this game may not have the same recognition as others in the launch lineup, fans of raising and fighting games should take note.

Suitability for families: 2/5

Game excitement level: 3/5

3DS feature showcase level: 3/5

All the games launching alongside the 3DS have been introduced. We hope you find one that suits your taste.
