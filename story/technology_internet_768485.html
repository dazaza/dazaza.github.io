Third-party Android 4.4 upgrade plan announced, Galaxy Nexus favored

by anonymous on 2013-11-17 18:54:19

One week after the official release of the Android 4.4 source code, third-party development teams have also successively announced their future development plans. These teams cover the main Android development groups, including well-known ones like CyanogenMod, AOKP, Paranoid Android, and the newly established Omni ROM. Let's take a look at their arrangements.

A representative from CyanogenMod stated in a blog post that the team is currently working hard to transplant multiple features of CM into the new system. Devices currently being adapted include the HTC One, Sony Xperia T, Samsung Galaxy S4 mini, and the entire Nexus series, including the Galaxy Nexus. The initial version of the ROM is expected to be released at the end of November.

Another development group, AOKP, mentioned that the team’s work is currently divided into two parts: one part focuses on refining ROMs based on Android 4.3, while the other part starts developing Android 4.4 from scratch. They said that building Android 4.4 ROMs from scratch means that the functionality of the first version will be very limited, and the team plans to gradually add all features through continuous version iterations. AOKP did not mention which devices the new ROM will be compatible with, but it is estimated that the Nexus series and common popular devices will be covered.

Paranoid Android is the first team to launch a fully functional Android 4.4 ROM. According to their announcement on Google+, Paranoid Android will continue to focus on the Nexus series of devices. Non-Nexus devices that were previously supported will continue to receive support, but no new non-Nexus devices will be supported in the future.

Omni ROM, which focuses on multi-window UI, stated that the upcoming ROMs based on Android 4.4 will add multiple features, including a more universal app installer, OTA updates, and richer user customization options. ROMs based on Android 4.4 will be compatible with the Nexus series of devices as well as OPPO Find 5, LG Optimus G, Samsung Galaxy S II, Sony Xperia T, and other phones and tablets.

Last week, when Google announced its Android 4.4 upgrade plan, it stated that the Galaxy Nexus, released in 2011, would not receive the Android 4.4 system due to the expiration of its service term. Now, finally, there are third-party developers willing to develop ROMs based on Android 4.4 for the Galaxy Nexus, allowing this device, released two years ago, to continue serving for some time, which is the most comforting aspect.
