About choice: It is recommended that friends who want to start a business on Taobao take a look.

by anonymous on 2013-10-02 10:40:27

Recently, many friends have been asking me questions like: "I'm planning to start a business on Taobao, what category should I choose? Should it be part-time or full-time? Is Taobao suitable for entrepreneurship?" Today, I will talk about these issues that everyone cares about. These opinions may not be entirely correct, but they are my real experiences, and I hope they can help you.

First, let me tell you about my situation. I was the one who quit a high-paying job to do Taobao. At that time, I did it with two other friends; one was responsible for graphic design, the other for procurement (initially part-time), and I was in charge of operations. When we were discussing what category to choose, we thought a lot about it, but we didn't know which category would be good because none of us had any product experience before. So for us, every product was the same. Based on our circumstances, we finally chose the mobile phone case category.

There were several reasons:

First, we had a friend's cousin who ran a mobile phone case factory. After communicating with him, we could get better sources than the market price.

Second, we didn't have much capital, so we chose to do mobile phone cases as the investment was relatively low.

Third, considering the low profit margin of drop shipping, we initially stocked more than 10,000 items.

Fourth, the gross profit margin of mobile phone cases was quite high, at least 50%.

Fifth, mobile phone cases were still popular and easy to enter. As long as there were mobile phones, there would be a need for cases, just in different styles.

Sixth, taking pictures of mobile phone cases wasn't difficult. None of us had experience or enough money to hire professional photographers.

Seventh, mobile phone cases were just our entry point. We wanted to first familiarize ourselves with some rules and tricks on Taobao. Once we got used to it or made some capital, we planned to move on to better categories.

Eighth, Taobao was just our survival channel. Our main future sales channels were AliExpress and DHgate.

Based on all these considerations, we ultimately chose the mobile phone case category as our entry point into e-commerce.

However, things didn't go as planned. There was always a gap between imagination and reality, and after starting, we found it wasn't so simple.

First, we discovered that the mobile phone case market was a red ocean, with intense competition. Many sellers used low-price strategies, such as offering free shipping for 9.9 yuan when the cost was already around 9 yuan.

Second, although the gross margin was high, the average transaction value was low, and the daily volume wasn't significant, so we earned very little. For example, selling a mobile phone case for 35 yuan meant a profit of only around 10 yuan. Selling ten a day would make only over 100 yuan. On the other hand, selling high-value items, even with a gross margin of only 30%, could yield over 100 yuan per item, and selling just one a day would suffice.

Third, we made mistakes in our initial predictions by choosing products based on our preferences rather than customer demand, leading to some items being unsold.

Fourth, due to the variety of mobile phone case designs, we often had to restock. Each restocking couldn't be too small because of freight costs, so our inventory gradually increased. For instance, we initially sold a lot of 7100 and Xiaomi models, so we stocked up on them. However, later, these models didn't sell well, resulting in excess inventory. Meanwhile, the i9100 model, which wasn't selling well earlier, became a bestseller after special pricing promotions.

Fifth, Taobao turned out to be much more complicated than we imagined. The hardest part was getting people to find your store. Traffic was the biggest challenge in running a shop. Without funds, you'd feel powerless. Even if you used直通车 (Taobao Direct), without knowing how to operate it or understanding its rules, it would just burn money.

Sixth, we also tried posting products on AliExpress and DHgate, but no one paid attention. It might have been due to our product or price issues, but mainly because our English wasn't good, making the operation process extremely difficult. So, we eventually gave up.

In this way, months passed without improvement. Our team started to change, our funds dwindled, and our confidence waned. In the end, only I was left fighting alone. After saying all this, the main purpose is to remind everyone that when starting a business, you must consider all possible problems and have the ability to solve them. Through my few months of practice, I'll give you a brief answer.

One, How to Choose a Category

Choosing a category is always a headache, especially for those of us without product experience, not manufacturers, with limited funds, and no resources. We tend to be blind and don't know where to start. Here are some general suggestions:

1. Choose a less competitive category: How do you know if it's less competitive? For example, if you find a product, search it on Taobao, and see how many sellers there are and how many listings exist. If there are fewer sellers and not many listings but decent sales, or if it has potential for future development, such as health food or new gadgets, then it's a good category with less competition.

2. Do a niche category: Don’t aim for broad and complete coverage; instead, focus on something small but strong. This is definitely the future trend of the internet or e-commerce—increasing specialization and product specificity. Only by specializing can you reach excellence. Brands like Afu, Three Squirrels, and Xiaodog Vacuum Cleaners all specialize in smaller subcategories within larger ones. Good positioning leads to good development.

3. High-value goods: I suggest not doing low-value products. Even if your gross margin is high, the final gross profit isn't much, and profitability is hard. On Taobao, you need to do promotions, pay for manpower and logistics, leaving very little profit. Moreover, with lower prices, you cannot provide better pre- and post-sales services to customers. Therefore, I recommend high-value goods. This way, you have higher average transaction values, higher gross profits, higher profits, and more earnings. As for what specific goods, I won't elaborate here. You can check the market or Taobao yourself. Making 50 yuan per sale and selling a few orders a day can cover living expenses. Selling mobile phone cases is really tough.

Two, Part-time or Full-time on Taobao?

This question depends on individual perspectives and situations. Here's a small suggestion for you:

1. If you're already very familiar with Taobao, have some experience and capital, and everything feels okay, you can go full-time and fully commit. However, be sure to plan ahead, prepare well, and choose a good category.

2. If you lack resources and face life pressures such as family and living expenses, and your current job is stable, I suggest starting part-time until you feel confident, then consider going full-time. This way, the risks will be much lower. Nowadays, it's not suitable for a small seller to fully invest. Myself and my friends around me are living examples. Don't jump into trouble.

3. If you're young, have no pressure, don't need to support your family, and life is manageable, yet have an entrepreneurial impulse, now is the time to take a shot. Failure doesn't matter because you're still young.

In summary, if you're not very familiar with Taobao, inexperienced, lacking resources and capital, and facing life pressures, I suggest doing it part-time. If none of these are issues, then you can proceed confidently without too many concerns. Now is not the era where just taking photos and listing products will sell.

Three, Drop Shipping or Self-Sourcing?

Both drop shipping and self-sourcing have their own advantages and disadvantages. If you want to scale up, you must stock your own inventory, which is necessary for growth. However, in the early stages, I still suggest you avoid self-sourcing and try drop shipping first. Once you've explored for a while and become familiar with Taobao's rules, and earned some capital, you can stock up on the hot-selling items. This way, the risks are lower, and the profits are higher. Stocking up may seem profitable, but in the end, you might find that you've only accumulated inventory. To make money through stocking up, you must ensure high turnover.

Four, Is Taobao Suitable for Entrepreneurship?

This question is actually not suitable for me to answer, but since many friends ask, I'll reluctantly share my thoughts. Whether it's right or wrong, you decide. These are just my personal opinions.

The future will undoubtedly belong to the era of e-commerce and online shopping—a big trend. Taobao is the primary platform for e-commerce, and I estimate its status will remain unchanged for at least five years, possibly even a decade. Undoubtedly, many sellers grow and earn money on Taobao. More large sellers will emerge, and some will disappear. This is the market economy. Whether something is right or wrong depends on the timing, the person, meaning the opportunity point, the team, and the capital and resources. If all these factors are favorable, the success rate will be much higher.

Summary:

Today, I mainly discussed several choices to make before starting a business on Taobao. Recently, many friends have been discussing this topic extensively. Everyone feels that Taobao is becoming harder to manage now compared to a few years ago when it might have been easier. Now, it requires high standards for capital, products, and teams—it's no longer the world of small sellers. When doing anything, you’ll have this feeling, like joining a company and thinking you came too late, wishing you joined earlier to become a director or get a share. Or you think you missed the boat for entrepreneurship. In fact, I believe it’s because you haven’t built up your internal strength. If this opportunity isn’t right, it means our timing isn’t suitable. Of course, starting anything is hard, but as long as you persist, hopefully with determination, you will succeed.

Entrepreneurship lies in your momentary decision, with both success and failure depending on how you perceive it. I’m merely sharing my thoughts and views for reference. You should still base your decisions on your actual situation. Regardless of what you choose, I wish you all the best!
