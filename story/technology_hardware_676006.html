Introduction

When the mobile phone integrates the functions of a radio, MP3, camera, camcorder, TV, PDA, etc., into one device, it has long surpassed its original simple definition as a communication tool and transformed into an important part of people's daily lives. The rapid advancement of communication technology has further accelerated the arrival of the 3G era. Compared to the previous 2G, 3G moves the Internet onto the mobile phone, allowing users to not only handle images and music on their phones but also browse web pages, hold teleconferences, and engage in e-commerce. On the other hand, mobile phone manufacturers seize the huge business opportunities brought by 3G, continuously increasing R&D investment, and large-screen, full-keyboard, WIFI and WAPI-supported smartphones keep coming to the market. At the same time, the appearance of counterfeit phones has significantly reduced the average selling price of smartphones, greatly lowering the user threshold for high-performance phones, leading to a sharp increase in the number of smartphones, providing a complete hardware foundation for users to use mobile Internet services.

According to data from the International Telecommunication Union, the total number of global minicomputers was around 10 million in 1980, over 200 million terminals were connected to the Internet in 2000, the number of global Internet users exceeded 2 billion by the end of 2010, and the number of mobile phone registrations exceeded 5 billion; the rapid growth of users in the mobile Internet era is an inevitable trend. The rapid increase in the number of users implies enormous opportunities and will create huge commercial value.

In the mobile Internet era, terminals become one of the key focuses of mobile Internet development. Around the demand for mobile Internet development, the development of terminals in the mobile Internet era shows an obvious trend: closely focusing on user needs, providing users with comprehensive services and experiences, i.e., user experience is king. Today, when the mobile Internet has just begun, user experience has gradually become the ultimate pursuit of terminal development. Apple's iPhone has elevated user experience to a new level, winning the favor of global mobile phone users, and the application experience of applications developed based on Android (Google mobile operating system) has reached the best level.

Based on 3G networks and smartphone platforms, various demands of Internet users based on PCs are slowly shifting to mobile terminals. The trend of 3G includes the integration of desktop Internet and WAP content, and the development of 3G in China is making mobile phones surpass PCs. According to DCCI's "2010-2011 China Internet Market Data Report", the growth rate of mobile Internet in China is significantly faster than PC Internet. It is expected that by 2013, the scale of mobile Internet users in China will reach 721 million, exceeding the scale of 716 million PC Internet users, marking China's true entry into the mobile Internet era. Facing a promising market, mobile Internet quickly gathers a group of entrepreneurs to begin carving out territories.
