Hermes New Store Opens in Nanjing: President Raymond Lei Optimistic About Nanjing Market

On September 15, 2010, Hermes, the renowned handcraft brand from Paris, France with over 170 years of history, celebrated the grand opening of its boutique at the Deji Plaza in Nanjing. As the 19th boutique in China, Hermes' iconic orange carriage made its first stop in Nanjing, adding a touch of French elegance and sophistication to this historically rich city.

Located on the first floor of Nanjing's Deji Plaza, the Hermes boutique spans an area of 206 square meters, designed by RDAI, the architectural firm responsible for designing all Hermes boutiques worldwide. The most eye-catching feature of the boutique is undoubtedly its facade, where champagne-colored metal strips form a classic and elegant metallic mesh that envelops the boutique like a second skin, making it vibrant and dazzling.

Upon entering, the front section of the store features a square area adorned with a traditional mosaic pattern in red, gray, and white, representing 24 Faubourg Saint-Honoré. Around this central piece stand several vertical partitions extending from floor to ceiling, painted in light gray or decorated with striped mirrors, ingeniously dividing the space into different product zones. Various artisanal products are displayed here, harmonizing with the serene and coordinated atmosphere of the boutique. The perfect blend of architectural innovation and elegant tradition faithfully embodies the spirit of the Hermes brand.

(From left to right) Florian CRAEN, Managing Director of Hermes North Asia; Mr. Chen Gang, Vice Mayor of Nanjing; Ms. Tseng Wen-Wen, General Manager of Deji Plaza; Patrick THOMAS, CEO of Hermes Global; and Raymond Lei, President of Hermes China, raised their glasses together with other guests to celebrate the grand opening of the Hermes boutique at Nanjing Deji Plaza.

After over a century of historical accumulation and six generations of unwavering inheritance, Hermes has evolved from a small workshop in Paris producing saddles and harnesses in the early 19th century into an international top-tier handcrafted luxury brand seamlessly blending elegance and practicality. The current 16 categories of premium products, including bags, silk items, men's and women's clothing, perfumes, and tableware, perfectly interpret Hermes' relentless pursuit of select materials and exquisite craftsmanship.

As a brand that started with saddle-making and horse equipment, Hermes shares deep roots with horses. This heritage spanning over 170 years was vividly showcased during the opening event: bartenders wearing horse masks served life-like chocolate horses to guests, while attendees held delicate golden horse-shaped balloons, sharing the joy of the new store opening. During the climax of the opening ceremony, Hermes Global CEO Patrick THOMAS and Mr. Chen Gang, Vice Mayor of Nanjing, used their artistic brushes to bring life to two horse envoys, symbolizing the first customers of Hermes. Accompanied by these majestic horse envoys, they unveiled a lifelike horse lantern, marking the official opening of the Nanjing boutique as the ribbon was cut by Patrick THOMAS, Florian CRAEN, Raymond Lei, Vice Mayor Chen Gang, and Ms. Tseng Wen-Wen. Cheers erupted, and champagne flowed, celebrating this joyful moment. The magical world of Hermes gradually opened its doors, offering every guest present an elegant experience filled with surprises.
