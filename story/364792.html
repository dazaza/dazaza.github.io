[Must-read for success] The hardships of real entrepreneurship

by t1990com on 2010-09-17 06:38:43

Nowadays, many college students are facing the problems of difficulty in employment and entrepreneurship. College graduates today are no longer as sought-after as they used to be. Nowadays, companies focus on ability rather than academic qualifications. Academic qualifications are not very useful now, as many people buy them with money, which proves that the value of current academic qualifications is not as high as before. This is exactly why companies today do not emphasize diplomas but focus more on abilities!

College graduates fresh out of school have no experience other than textbook knowledge; they can't do anything, let alone have any special skills. When college students encounter difficulties in employment, they think about starting their own business, but is it really that easy to start a business? The answer is no!

Currently, relevant departments analyze that the following four aspects are the main obstacles for college graduates starting businesses:

One: Lack of funds

Funding shortages are the main bottleneck restricting college students from starting businesses. Relevant surveys have found that 75% of college students can afford less than 80,000 yuan for startup capital! And this is under the premise of family support. College students from poorer families have startup funds of less than 10,000 yuan, and 70% of graduating college students believe that "lack of funds" is the biggest obstacle to starting a business! For 50% of students, their dreams of entrepreneurship have been shattered due to lack of funding. Funding shortages have become the biggest barrier for college student entrepreneurs.

Two: Difficulty in financing

After securing funding for entrepreneurship, a company can be established. However, after the company is registered and established, it will take some time to develop before there are profits! During this period, entrepreneurial college students need to find various opportunities to attract investors in order to survive! But finding investors is not an easy task! Additionally, due to the lack of risk assessment mechanisms in banks and the lack of property collateral guarantees by entrepreneurial college students, financing becomes difficult.

Three: Lack of experience

College students nowadays don't study well in school. They spend their days online, sleeping, dating, or traveling, without paying attention to society at all, so they lack certain social experience! Often, they cannot pinpoint projects accurately, and their companies lack complete management systems. At the same time, their abilities in analyzing, judging, and handling the market are relatively lacking. Many college student entrepreneurs don't even know the process of registering a company, so how can they talk about entrepreneurship? Therefore, entrepreneurial knowledge and experience are also major challenges for college student entrepreneurs!

Four: Poor risk resistance capability

Entrepreneurial college students have weak market awareness, lack social resources, and especially poor risk resistance capabilities, making it easy for them to fail right at the start. "There are few college students who dare to start businesses, and even fewer who succeed in entrepreneurship."

From the above four aspects, it's not hard to see how difficult it is for college students to start businesses. With these four points, a large number of college students who want to start businesses may lose confidence!
