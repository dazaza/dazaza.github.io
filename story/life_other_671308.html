What is happiness....

The girl and the boy met on the internet. They became friends because of a game, and the boy fell in love with the girl. He liked her understanding nature, her gentleness and elegance, her beauty, and her quiet character. Even though he knew it was unrealistic, the boy still decided to pursue the girl. The boy had feelings for the girl, and every day when they chatted in the group, he would joke around with her. Everyone could tell that the boy liked the girl, except the girl herself.

When someone told the girl that the boy liked her, she was very surprised. She thought it was just a game and agreed to the boy's advances. As time went on, the boy realized he couldn't let go, and he was determined to bring their relationship into reality. He wanted to protect the girl and be with her forever. The boy knew that once he fell in love with the girl, their future together would be difficult, but he was still determined to continue.

Finally, the girl was moved by the boy's sincerity. She decided to wait for him to come and take her. To wait for the day he would propose to her. Although the girl knew the road ahead would be tough, she still decided to give the boy a chance.

The boy's parents and the girl's parents were against them being together. The boy's parents forbade him from seeing the girl or having any contact with her. The girl was disappointed; she knew they couldn't be together. But she still waited for the boy, rejecting the advances of other men. The girl didn't know what the future held, but the boy continued to work silently.

On this Valentine's Day, the girl walked down the street, watching couples holding hands, seeing roses everywhere, and reading signs that said "Happy Valentine's Day" and "May all lovers be united." Suddenly, the girl felt like crying. She missed the boy. She wished he was by her side. She didn't need the kind of romance many girls longed for; she only wanted a simple happiness - just to have the boy by her side forever. Lowering her head, tears streamed down her cheeks. She didn't want others to see her cry, so she kept her head down and wept silently.

Suddenly, a warm, large hand appeared before her, wiping away her tears. It was the boy. Seeing the boy who suddenly appeared, the girl cried even harder. The boy gently pulled the girl into his arms. His broad chest made the girl feel so warm, as if she could depend on him for a lifetime. This was her greatest happiness.

Finally, the boy spoke the words the girl had been waiting to hear: "I'm sorry for making you wait so long. Today, I've come to fulfill my promise."

The girl looked at the shiny, happy ring the boy suddenly took out. Tears flowed down her face again.

The boy's parents finally gave in to his persistence. They agreed to let him find the girl and be with her. Their parents finally decided to bless this pair of lovebirds.

The boy and the girl got married.

But not long after the wedding, the boy seemed to change. He stopped coming home, going out with his friends to eat, drink, and play. It was as if he had forgotten all the promises he once made to the girl. Every day, the girl was alone in an empty house, hearing only her own voice. The boy returned home very late every night, and when the girl woke up in the morning, the spot beside her was already empty.

Is it true that once you have something, it's no longer good? Once you have it, do you stop cherishing it? Don't you know how to cherish?

The girl's life was exhausting... She could no longer capture the warmth of the chest she once knew so well. The girl could only cry alone in sadness every day.

Tomorrow was the anniversary of the boy and girl's marriage. The girl wanted the boy to spend the whole day with her tomorrow, then she would leave on her own, giving the boy his freedom. The girl waited for the boy until very late, around 4 a.m., when he finally came back drunk. The girl asked, "Can you stay with me tomorrow?"

The boy immediately refused, saying, "I have things to do tomorrow. Maybe another day."

The girl cried, "Why are you so different after we got married? I never see your shadow. Is it true that once you get something, you no longer cherish it? Have you forgotten all the promises you once made to me?"

The boy shouted angrily at the girl, "I really have things to do tomorrow. Can't you stop being so selfish?"

The girl's tears flowed like a flood. Hearing the boy call her selfish broke her heart. "Tomorrow is our wedding anniversary. Can't you just spend one day with me? After tomorrow, I won't stop you from doing whatever you want."

For some reason, the boy's heart hurt, but he didn't pay attention. Finally, he softened and promised to go nowhere tomorrow and stay with the girl all day.

The sun rose, and the girl was both happy and sad. She was happy because she would finally get to spend a whole day with the boy. But she was sad because after today, she would leave the boy, leaving the man she loved most.

The girl got up early to prepare breakfast for the boy. The breakfast was ready for a long time, but the boy still hadn't woken up. The girl didn't want to disturb him, so she didn't wake him up. The boy woke up very late. Seeing that the food was cold, the girl said, "You're awake? The food is cold now, let me reheat it for you." Looking at the weary and somewhat melancholy back of the girl, the boy felt a pang of pain. He decided to spend the whole day with the girl.

As the boy and the girl walked down the familiar street corner where they used to go, everything seemed to have changed. People were no longer the same, and neither were they. The girl's heart ached again.

Suddenly, the girl had a premonition of impending doom. She turned around and saw a man holding a knife with a hateful look walking towards them, already behind the boy. Without thinking, the girl hugged the boy from behind, blocking the knife for him.

The girl only felt a cool sensation in her body, as if something warm was soaking the clothes she had dressed up specially for the boy today. Slowly, she began to fall backwards. Fortunately, at the last moment, the girl didn't fall onto the icy road. The boy caught her, muttering something incoherent. The girl couldn't hear anything, and the words she wanted to say but couldn't utter stuck in her throat. She could only use her last ounce of strength to reach out and touch the boy's face. The girl seemed to see the familiar face that once cherished her with all his life, and a tear fell from that familiar face onto hers. That tear was so warm, so hot. It was the only temperature the girl could feel at this moment. The girl smiled contentedly. Her hand fell limp, and the blurry figures around her gradually turned black and indistinct...

At this moment, the boy called out to the girl with all his might. How he wished the girl hadn't blocked the knife for him! Now he wished more than anything that it was his blood flowing instead!

He hated himself for not cherishing the girl enough, for not knowing how to treasure her. He hated everything about himself!

Holding the girl tightly, the boy cried loudly without restraint, oblivious to anyone else around him. His heart ached so much that he wanted to bash his head against a wall. Why hadn't he protected the girl?

The sound of the ambulance blared loudly beside the boy, but he heard nothing. All he hoped was that the girl would open her eyes and go home with him. Some people in white coats pulled the boy away, forcibly taking the girl away. The boy was almost breaking down. Those white clothes, so white they were scary, made the boy fear the color white for the first time in his life. Kneeling on the ground, he didn't know who had dragged him onto the constantly wailing ambulance. He stared at the pale face of the girl, tears streaming down his face like a flood.

Looking at the red light above the operating room that remained lit, the boy wanted to slap himself. After an unknown length of time, the light finally went out. The boy hoped to hear that the girl had woken up, but he was afraid to approach the green-clad doctor. Until the doctor stood in front of him and patted his shoulder. That pat felt like a thousand pounds to the boy, as if a hammer had struck his shoulder.

"Don't worry anymore. Luckily, the knife wasn't too deep. We managed to save her life. But..."

The boy was so relieved to hear that the girl was safe that it was like the sprouting of flowers she liked, such joy. But the doctor still said "But..." and the boy felt like he had fallen into despair again.

"But the child in her womb couldn't be saved. I'm sorry, we did our best. Now you need to take good care of her, her body is still weak."

Child? She was carrying our child? And I didn't even know. How am I supposed to be a husband? She was carrying our child, and I wasn't there to take care of her, I made her angry, and I even made her block the knife for me? Wife, why didn't you tell me you were carrying our baby? Is it because I was too bad? Is it because I'm an irresponsible husband? Am I unworthy to be a father? Wife, I'm sorry, it's all my fault, it will never happen again, I'm sorry...

The girl slowly opened her eyes... So tired...

It felt like she had slept for a long time, yet why was she still so exhausted? When she opened her eyes, she saw a strange white room. Suddenly, she remembered blocking the knife for the boy. Turning her head, she finally saw that familiar face. The girl was comforted to see that familiar face again. She recalled the tear that fell on her face before she lost consciousness, and unknowingly, tears streamed down her cheeks.

The girl wanted to reach out and touch that familiar face, but halfway through, a sudden pain hit her. Not only was there pain on her back, but also on her stomach, which seemed to tell her something. The girl understood - the baby she and the boy had conceived was gone. She touched her stomach, and tears flowed again. "I'm sorry, I failed to protect you and couldn't let you come into this world. I'm sorry..."

The boy woke up screaming the girl's name from a nightmare. He was so afraid of losing her. After realizing it was just a dream, he immediately looked for her. Seeing her hand on her stomach, her pale face covered with tears.

The boy grabbed the girl's hand. "I'm sorry, it's all my fault, I'm sorry for putting you through so much. It's all my fault. I promise it will never happen again. Please forgive me..."

The girl placed her hand on the boy's cheek. Her tears were warm, filled with happiness and relief... The girl felt it was worth it, to win back the boy's heart, even dying for him would be worth it.

A year later, the girl was pregnant again with the boy's baby.

"Wife, you've worked hard. It's a girl, as beautiful as you. You taught me what it means to cherish, I must remember this lesson, so I want to name our baby Mingxi (meaning 'cherish'), okay?"

The girl nodded, her face full of happiness...
