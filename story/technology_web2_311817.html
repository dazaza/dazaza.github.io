Sichuan-Chongqing battle shrouded in doubt as goalkeeper Lan Yi avoids the issue; teammates support: How could he be that stupid?

by yinding999 on 2009-12-17 09:11:29

Sichuan-Chongqing match shrouded in doubt, goalkeeper Lan Yi avoids the issue; teammates stand by him: "Could he be that stupid?" http://ccrr22.biz

There has been much speculation about the match on October 5th where Chengdu Sheffied United lost at home to the Chongqing team, but within the Sheffied United camp, there is no such secrecy surrounding that game.

"It doesn't seem likely. If that game was a fixed match, it would have been bizarre," one player told a reporter. A few days ago, CCTV broadcast some speculations about that match, which clearly put immense pressure on Chengdu Sheffied United's goalkeeper Lan Yi, who made low-level mistakes during that game.

On the morning of December 15th, while other team members were testing their YOYO on the field, several goalkeepers, under the guidance of their coach, were training on another field. Lan Yi participated in the training normally, but when the reporter came to take photos, Lan Yi intentionally or unintentionally avoided the reporter's camera. At this time, he did not want his exposure to be too high.

According to the reporter's understanding, before the Chengdu team's home game against the Chongqing team, Chengdu placed great importance on the match. The Sichuan-Chongqing derby has always been intense, and it was rumored that Xu Hongtao had individually reminded the players in the locker room that they were shouldering the responsibility of the entire city and must try their best. If they didn't play well, everyone in the club would bear the responsibility. But they still lost the match.

After the game, the focus was all on Lan Yi. Several leaders in the club called Lan Yi for serious talks, asking him to explain the situation. However, his teammates did not think there was any moral issue with Lan Yi: "Lan Yi might have been too nervous that day, causing him to lose his composure. Think about it, in such a situation, with the bosses reminding them beforehand, could he be that stupid? Everyone who has been a goalkeeper knows that if you wanted to throw the game, there would be no need to do it so obviously. Just slowing down your actions by a fraction of a second would let the ball in without needing such exaggerated moves. We feel that there might really be nothing wrong with that game. Can't the Chongqing team win us once? This is football after all."

Ma Mingyu stated that Chengdu Sheffield United's attitude towards all matters is positive and open: "We will fully cooperate with this anti-fraud and anti-corruption campaign. However, regarding the game against the Chongqing team, I still hope that evidence can be provided."

On December 13th, CCTV News Channel aired a news segment titled “The ‘Sichuan-Chongqing Match’ Suspected of Being Fixed by Fans.” In this news piece, several Chengdu fans expressed that the 2-3 loss of Chengdu Sheffield United at home to Chongqing Lifan on October 5th was a fixed match because of the three low-level mistakes made by goalkeeper Lan Yi, which seemed too amateurish. One female fan said: "At the time, many fans pulled out hundred-yuan bills and shouted 'fixed match,' with some even saying, 'If you want money, we can give it to you, but playing like this is an insult to the fans!'"

Currently, there has been no news confirming that relevant departments have started an investigation into this match.

In this year's Chinese Super League, there have been many matches that are somewhat confusing.

2009.9.12 - The 4-0 victory of Shandong Luneng over Tianjin at home also raised questions. Recently, "China News Weekly" disclosed that a bookmaker knew beforehand that Tianjin would lose by four goals.

2009.9.27 - Chongqing Lifan defeated Jiangsu Sainty 2-0 at home. In the second half, the visiting team suddenly substituted key forward Tan Si and others. The host team scored two goals in the dying moments, with the first being a penalty caused by an unnecessary foul inside the box by the Jiangsu player who replaced Tan Si.

2009.10.24 - Chengdu Sheffield United lost 0-3 at home to Shenzhen. After the game, the Sheffield United players threw footballs to the fans as thanks, but the fans shouted "fixed match" and threw the balls back.
