25 Websites You Can't Live Without

After selecting the 50 coldest websites in 07, Time Magazine has recently released a new list: 25 websites you can't live without. Of course, these are all the favorites of Americans. I won't say much, let's start with the list:

1 Amazon.com

This one doesn't need much introduction, it's the company that defined the concept of e-commerce. Since its operation began in 95, it has offered an extensive selection of books. The newly launched AmazonConnect allows you to share your favorite authors and works with a large group of book lovers while buying books.

2 Apple Movie Trailers

The ultimate collection of the latest and coolest movie trailers.

3 Blogger

A free personal blog "producer" with light and easy-to-use tools.

4 www.coko365.com

A completely free online accounting and financial management website that helps people easily manage their finances online.

5 Craigslist

The hottest classified ad website serving more than 300 cities in the U.S., now also expanding into the global real estate information service field. The densely packed text marks various life information, which may look a bit dull, but here, you can find everything from renting apartments, selling cars, mowing lawns, to even finding a wife.

6 Drudge Report

The domain of the scoop king Matt Drudge. This man once made headlines by revealing all of Clinton's scandals, and he still keeps his personal website thriving, receiving over 3.5 billion clicks last year.

7 Ebay

The world's largest C2C e-commerce website, now also offering abundant services. Users can write blogs and add entries to the Wiki encyclopedia. How about doing business at home using SKYPE?

8 ESPN

A well-known sports website providing instant sports updates, videos, and column reports. Now it also offers a function to replay live broadcasts, of course, for baseball and American football, which are Americans' favorites.

9 Factcheck.org

The Factcheck.org website run by the University of Pennsylvania, gathering news, TV ads, and public speeches by politicians.

10 Flickr

The ultimate showcase for personal photos and also one of the fastest-growing online communities.

11 Google

A powerful search engine now also offering various free services. Spreadsheets shared tables (stealing Excel's lunch?), calendar, image management Picasa, and the famous Gmail.

11 HowStuffWorks

A website introducing how various things work, such as how to launder money, how hybrid cars work, or how kidneys function.

12 The Internet Movie Database (IMDB)

An encyclopedia of the entertainment industry. It gathers 800,000 movies, TV shows, and games, and most of the movie ratings you see are the average scores given by netizens here.

13 Lifehacker

Introduces tips and experiences on using computer technology, saving you a lot of time. Its sister blog Gizmodo lets you be the first to touch the latest and strangest gadgets.

14 The Museum of Modern Art

Provides unique video services, allowing you to appreciate exhibits as if you were there in person. It also provides perfect audio tours for children and visually impaired people.

15 Netflix

The world's largest online movie rental service provider with over 60,000 DVDs available every day.

16 National Public Radio

The U.S. National Public Radio, with popular programs like NPR podcasts, NPR music, NPR This I Believe, and NPR Stories.

17 The Onion

A famous website specializing in fake news.

18 Rotten Tomatoes

An entertainment website under IDN Entertainment, focusing on movie news.

19 Shopzilla

A new website providing search services and comparison shopping services for online shoppers. Its strong search capabilities and comprehensive product rating services are the highlights of the site.

20 Technorati

A famous blog search engine. By April 2006, Technorati had indexed over 30 million blog sites. The search service is becoming faster and more accurate.

21 Television Without Pity

Provides sharp and interesting TV show reviews.

22 The Smoking Gun

A specialized website for rumors and interesting stories, but sometimes big news also appears.

23 Wikipedia

A true internet miracle. Almost entirely written, edited, and maintained by volunteers, it is a vast online encyclopedia. It currently has 1.3 million English entries and millions of entries in another 8 languages.

24 Yahoo!

To reclaim lost ground from Google, it has recently received good reviews from various Web 2.0 services. Del.icio.us for sharing website bookmarks, Maps.yahoo.com/traffic for virtual urban road navigation, Yahoo Photos for sharing photos, and tech news supported by CNET. This summer, the official fifaworldcup.yahoo.com for the World Cup also brought it many clicks.

25 Zappos.com

A simple online shoe store that has gained a large number of customers through excellent service and free shipping.
