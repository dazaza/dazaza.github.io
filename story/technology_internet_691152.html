1. Tissue therapy. For example, placental tissue fluid and placental globulin can be administered through intramuscular injection.

2. Physical therapy. Physical therapy uses mild heat stimulation, which can promote blood circulation in the pelvic cavity, improve the nutritional state of local tissues, and facilitate the absorption and subsidence of inflammation.

3. What methods can treat adnexal inflammation? Antibiotic treatment. Antibiotics can kill residual pathogenic bacteria and prevent acute attacks. Therefore, antibiotics should be used for patients with obvious symptoms.

4. Surgical treatment. For large tubal hydrosalpinx or tubo-ovarian cysts caused by inflammation, surgical treatment can be performed. For infertility caused by tubal obstruction, tubal reconstructive surgery can be carried out.

How to treat adnexal inflammation?

Chronic adnexal inflammation does not respond well to antibiotic drugs alone; it requires long-term supportive therapy, which is to increase nutrition and improve the body's immune function. The most important way to enhance immunity is exercise, which can both improve immunity and make you feel happy. A bad mood can lead to various diseases, and chronic adnexal inflammation is largely a psychosomatic disease.

The most important aspect of treating acute adnexal inflammation is timely and sufficient use of antibiotic treatment. During this period, the antibiotics used should preferably be broad-spectrum because the appendix is inside the abdomen, making it difficult to determine what kind of bacterial infection it is. Therefore, the broader spectrum the antibiotics are, the easier it is to cover the pathogens. It is essential to complete the full course of treatment, usually 7 days, followed by a period of consolidation treatment.

What are the hazards of adnexal inflammation?

1. Causes premature aging in women, turning them into "yellow-faced women": When women are infected with adnexal inflammation, it will cause the ovaries to fail to function properly. If the ovaries cannot ovulate, it will not only lead to female infertility but also gradually weaken and disappear secondary sexual characteristics, directly causing endocrine disorders, leading to skin aging, and making women turn into "yellow-faced women" earlier.

2. Can cause female infertility or ectopic pregnancy: When women are infected with adnexal inflammation, clinically, adnexal inflammation often appears together with pelvic inflammatory disease. The inflammatory stimulation of adnexal inflammation can cause tubal edema, thinning of the mucosa, which is unfavorable for the combination of sperm and eggs. If left untreated in time, the condition worsens, and the fallopian tube may become blocked due to the necrosis of mucosal epithelial cells, affecting the passage of sperm. Even if combined with the egg, it may not reach the uterus in time, possibly leading to ectopic pregnancy, threatening women's health.
