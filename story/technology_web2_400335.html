Another Version: Investigation Report on College Students' Online Shopping Situation

According to the 2007 Internet report, the proportion of Chinese Internet users who shop online is 22.1%, with the number of shoppers reaching 46.4 million. According to the content of the 2009 Internet report, in countries like Europe, America, and South Korea, two out of every three Internet users are online shoppers, while in China, about one out of every four Internet users is an online shopper.

The wave of the Internet has made the use of computers very common, and new electronic media disseminated through the network continue to emerge. Taobao, Alipay, and Wangwang are common terms among college students. Discussions about how to buy things online and where to find cost-effective items can be heard daily. Looking at the bustling scenes of courier companies conducting sales meetings, with vehicles from express services like Shentong and YTO parked in dormitory areas waiting for students to pick up their packages, it's quite lively. Conversely, many students remain uninterested or unwilling to try online shopping. What are the motivations, characteristics of purchased items, and reasons for not attempting online shopping among students who frequently shop on Taobao? What are the concerns that prevent students from trying online shopping? What differences exist between male and female college students in terms of online shopping?

I. Survey Purpose

For many consumers, online shopping has gradually shifted from a novelty to a part of daily life, gaining popularity due to its unique advantages. Additionally, the Internet is impacting traditional consumption habits, thinking patterns, and lifestyles. More and more people are accustomed to searching for information online, becoming potential customers when they search for relevant products and company information to meet certain needs. When the information satisfies their needs, they become customers. College students represent the new generation and are representative of Internet users, with a higher proportion of online shoppers compared to the general Chinese Internet population. Therefore, I took advantage of this trend and conducted a small survey on the online shopping situation of college students out of curiosity.

II. Survey Methods and Scope

Based on the above objectives, I placed a survey questionnaire on a questionnaire website and randomly distributed it via QQ groups to current students at my school. Finally, I screened out 100 valid questionnaires and used the analysis provided by the website to directly obtain our survey data. This primarily focuses on current college students, who generally have a higher level of education, are proficient in using the Internet, have more demand for products that satisfy spiritual needs and are suitable for online sales, and have relatively higher expected incomes. Thus, they have a greater likelihood of dominating future online consumption.

III. Survey Results

(A) General Situation of College Students' Online Shopping

The preferred websites available for online shopping are divided into three main categories: B2C websites primarily engaged in online retail, auction websites, and portal sites. In particular, auction websites like Taobao, EachNet, and Joyo, as well as B2C websites like Dangdang, are the top choices for students.

Through this survey, it was found that about 45% of college students have experience with online shopping. Among those without online shopping experience, only 20% have never browsed shopping websites. It is evident that the market potential for online shopping among college students is enormous!

In today’s era where online consumption coexists with traditional consumption, the majority of college students, about 67%, choose online shopping because it is convenient, cheaper, and saves time. A considerable portion, about 15%, also choose online shopping because of the wide range of products available. Curiosity, fashion trends, and convenience are the main consumption motives for college students when shopping online. There are significant differences in consumption motives between males and females. Males value convenience more, while females place more emphasis on price. Among those surveyed who shop online, the two most popular payment methods are online banking payments and cash-on-delivery, accounting for 77%. Bank transfers and postal money orders serve as supplementary methods.

Among those who shop online, the primary types of goods purchased are those that satisfy spiritual needs. The top five categories of goods purchased include books, clothing and footwear, sports and leisure products, computers and related products, and audio-visual products.

There is a temporal and spatial difference between online shopping and traditional shopping. About 40% believe that the goods purchased online are cost-effective, which aligns with the claim that genuine and reasonably priced goods make up a smaller portion, around 20%. Some have been deceived by online sellers. After purchasing goods, apart from the product itself, payment methods, merchant credibility, and delivery satisfaction also significantly influence overall satisfaction.

(B) The Impact of Online Shopping Behavior on College Students

College students without online shopping experience perceive higher risks associated with online shopping. Among all respondents, the main concern is the security of online transactions (credit card information, personal information, etc.). Concerns about after-sales service guarantees, product quality, and shipping speed are minor issues. The main psychological barriers preventing college students from engaging in online shopping include aspects such as brand, price, quality, reliability, and shelf life of products, as well as the richness, filterability, and comparability of similar products on websites. These factors must meet the expectations of buyers. Additionally, the security and convenience of online transactions are influencing factors. Males tend to doubt the reliability of website information, while females are more skeptical about the quality of products purchased online.

Regarding the development prospects of online shopping, 60% of respondents agree that online shopping is increasingly being accepted and has great growth potential. This indicates that online shopping will develop further and become one of the main forms of shopping for college students. On the other hand, 23% still believe that there won't be significant development in the short term, which includes the opinions of many respondents without online shopping experience.

IV. Brief Summary and Analysis

Through analyzing both students who have engaged in online shopping and those who haven't, the following conclusions can be drawn:

1. 45% of all students engage in online shopping, with a growing trend. This proportion is much higher than the shopping proportion of Chinese Internet users, indicating that college students are more likely to accept this novel and fast-paced way of shopping.

2. Students who have shopped online generally recognize the advantages of saving time and costs, which will attract more college students to shop online.

3. There are many factors influencing students' online shopping behavior, both subjective and objective, but the main concern remains the issue of security and credit.

4. Although college students have limited purchasing power, this consumer group with new ideas has a high shopping ratio, is willing to try online shopping, and has a high level of acceptance.

5. Overcoming the challenges faced by students who have already shopped online will accelerate the development of online shopping.

6. Online shopping has a promising outlook and a bright future.

The foundational environment of the online shopping market is becoming increasingly stable. Websites like Taobao, EachNet, Dangdang, and Joyo have become the primary venues for online shopping and are topics of discussion and "spaces" for shopping among college students. With the continuous rapid growth of China's economy, improved living standards, expansion of the telecommunications network, increased computer penetration rates, and the application and development of e-commerce, the online shopping market is becoming increasingly vibrant and will gradually develop into one of the main forms of shopping. Online shopping will surely become the preferred method of shopping for college students in the future.
