Ball valve pinyin: qiu fa

Pronunciation:

English: ball valve

Definition: According to GB/T21465-2008 "Valve Terminology", it is defined as: The opening and closing part (ball) is driven by the valve stem and rotates around the axis of the valve stem.

The ball valve was introduced in the 1950s. With the rapid development of science and technology, improvements in production processes and product structures have led to its rapid development into a major type of valve in just 40 years. In Western industrialized countries, the use of ball valves has been increasing annually. In China, ball valves are widely used in petroleum refining, long-distance pipelines, chemical, papermaking, pharmaceuticals, water conservancy, power, municipal, steel industries, and occupy an important position in the national economy.

Ball valves are mainly used to cut off or connect media in pipelines and can also be used for fluid regulation and control. Among them, hard-sealed V-type ball valves have strong cutting force between their V-shaped ball core and metal seats with welded hard alloys, making them particularly suitable for media containing fibers, small solid particles, etc. Multi-way ball valves can not only flexibly control the merging, splitting, and switching of media in pipelines but can also close any channel while connecting the other two channels. This type of valve should generally be installed horizontally in pipelines.

[Editing this section] Working principle

The ball valve has a rotating 90-degree action, with the plug body being spherical and having a circular passage or channel through its axis. In pipelines, ball valves are mainly used to cut off, distribute, and change the direction of the medium's flow. It only requires a 90-degree rotation operation and a small torque to achieve tight closure. Ball valves are most suitable for use as on/off valves, but recent developments have designed ball valves for throttling and controlling flow, such as V-type ball valves.

The main characteristics of ball valves are compact structure, reliable sealing, simple structure, easy maintenance, and the seal face and ball surface are often in a closed state, making them less prone to erosion by the medium. They are easy to operate and maintain, suitable for general working media such as water, solvents, acids, and natural gas, as well as harsh conditions like oxygen, hydrogen peroxide, methane, and ethylene. They are widely used across various industries. The valve body of the ball valve can be integral or combined.

[Editing this section] Advantages

1. Low fluid resistance; full-bore ball valves have almost no flow resistance.

2. Simple structure, small size, light weight.

3. Reliable sealing. It has two sealing faces, and currently, the sealing material of ball valves widely uses various plastics, providing good sealing and achieving complete sealing. They are also widely used in vacuum systems.

4. Easy operation, quick opening and closing; from fully open to fully closed, it only needs to rotate 90 degrees, facilitating remote control.

5. Easy maintenance; the ball valve structure is simple, and the sealing ring is usually movable, making disassembly and replacement more convenient.

6. When fully open or fully closed, the ball and valve seat sealing face are isolated from the medium, preventing erosion of the valve sealing face when the medium passes through.

7. Wide range of applications; diameters range from a few millimeters to several meters, applicable from high vacuum to high pressure.

8. Due to wiping during opening and closing, ball valves can be used in media containing suspended solid particles.

[Editing this section] Classification and Features

Ball valves are divided into: floating ball ball valves, fixed ball ball valves, track ball ball valves, V-type ball ball valves, three-way ball ball valves, stainless steel ball ball valves, forged steel ball ball valves, ash discharge ball ball valves, sulfur-resistant ball ball valves, three-way ball ball valves, pneumatic ball ball valves, electric ball ball valves, compression fitting ball ball valves, welding ball ball valves.

According to the shell/body material classification, ball valves can be divided into:

1. Metal material valves: such as carbon steel valves, alloy steel valves, stainless steel valves, cast iron valves, titanium alloy valves, Monel valves, copper alloy valves, aluminum alloy valves, lead alloy valves, etc.

2. Metal-bodied lined valves: such as rubber-lined valves, fluorine-lined valves, lead-lined valves, plastic-lined valves, enamel-lined valves.

3. Non-metallic material valves: such as ceramic valves, glass valves, plastic valves.

There are many domestic manufacturers of ball valves, and connection dimensions are mostly not unified. They are mainly divided into the following categories:

(1) General-purpose types based on JB/T2203-1999 "Ball Valve Structural Length". Most domestic ball valve manufacturers design and produce according to this standard. But this standard is not perfect, with incomplete specifications. The largest nominal diameter for floating ball valves is DN1200, and the largest nominal diameter for fixed ball valves is DN2000. Based on the valve specifications produced by factories and available data, the smallest nominal diameter for floating ball valves is DN6, and the smallest nominal diameter for fixed ball valves is DN50. After investigation, connection dimensions among manufacturers are not entirely uniform. To have a unified standard, so that users can select and install valves of the same specification interchangeably, it is recommended that the National General Machinery Research Institute revise JB/T2203-1999 "Ball Valve Structural Length". It is suggested that design institutes and users select according to the standard, and ball valve manufacturers design and manufacture according to the standard.

1. Floating Ball Ball Valve

The ball of the ball valve is floating and, under the action of medium pressure, the ball can produce a certain displacement and tightly press against the sealing face at the outlet end, ensuring the sealing of the outlet end.

Floating ball ball valves have a simple structure and good sealing, but the load of the working medium on the ball is entirely transmitted to the outlet sealing ring. Therefore, the material of the sealing ring must be considered to withstand the working load of the ball medium. Under higher pressure impact, the ball may shift. This structure is generally used for medium and low-pressure ball valves.

2. Fixed Ball Ball Valve

The ball of the ball valve is fixed and does not move when subjected to pressure. Fixed ball ball valves all have floating seats. After being subjected to medium pressure, the seat moves, pressing the sealing ring tightly against the ball to ensure sealing. Bearings are usually installed on the upper and lower shafts of the ball, resulting in small operating torque, making them suitable for high-pressure and large-diameter valves.

To reduce the operating torque of the ball valve and increase the reliability of the seal, oil-sealed ball valves have recently appeared. A specially formulated lubricant is injected between the sealing surfaces to form an oil film, enhancing sealing and reducing operating torque, making them more suitable for high-pressure large-diameter ball valves.

3. Elastic Ball Ball Valve (i.e., Track Ball Valve)

The ball of the ball valve is elastic. Both the ball and the valve seat sealing ring are made of metal materials, with a very high sealing pressure. Relying solely on the pressure of the medium itself cannot meet the sealing requirements, requiring external force. This type of valve is suitable for high-temperature and high-pressure media.

An elastic groove is opened at the bottom of the inner wall of the elastic ball to obtain elasticity. When closing the channel, the ball is expanded by the wedge head of the valve stem, pressing it tightly against the valve seat to achieve sealing. Before rotating the ball, the wedge head is first loosened, allowing the ball to return to its original shape, creating a small gap between the ball and the valve seat, reducing friction on the sealing face and operating torque.

4. V-Type Ball Valve

This ball valve belongs to the fixed ball valve category and is also a single-seat sealed ball valve. Its regulating performance is the best among ball valves, with flow characteristics being equal percentage, and a turndown ratio reaching 100:1. Its V-shaped notch and metal valve seat have a shearing effect, making it particularly suitable for media containing fibers, small solid particles, and slurries.

5. Three-Way Ball Valve

Three-way ball valves come in T-type and L-type. The T-type allows interconnection of three orthogonal pipes and cuts off the third pipe, serving both diversion and confluence functions. The L-type can only connect two mutually orthogonal pipes and cannot simultaneously maintain the mutual connection of the third pipe, serving only distribution functions.

Product Features

1. The three-way ball valve adopts an integrated structure, with four-sided valve seat sealing, fewer flange connections, high reliability, and lightweight design.

2. The three-way ball core is divided into T-type and L-type, with a long service life, large flow capacity, and low resistance.

3. Ball valves are divided into single-acting and double-acting types according to their mode of operation. Single-acting types have the characteristic that if the power source fails, the ball valve will remain in the state required by the control system.

[Editing this section] Temperature and Pressure Limitations

The nameplate shows the maximum and minimum operating temperatures allowed for the ball valve and its maximum operating pressure.

When using PTFE or RTFE material for the valve seat and seal, the operating temperature of other types of valve seats and seals should be checked by the KI factory.

The nominal pressure grade (PN) of the valve indicates the maximum working pressure of the valve under normal temperature conditions. For example: PN4.0 indicates that the maximum working pressure at an operating temperature of -29°C to 38°C is 40 Bar (4.0 MPa).

For precautions regarding electric or pneumatic actuators, refer to the corresponding instruction manual.

[Editing this section] Installation

1) Remove the protective covers from both ends of the flange, and clean and flush the valve in a fully open state.

2) Perform a full-system test according to the specified signal (electric or pneumatic) before installation (to prevent vibration during transportation affecting performance), and proceed with online installation after passing the test (wiring according to the electric actuator wiring diagram).

3) Prepare to connect to the pipeline, flushing and cleaning the pipeline of residual impurities (these substances may damage the valve seat and ball).

4) During installation, do not use the valve's actuator part as a lifting point to avoid damaging the actuator and accessories.

5) This type of valve should be installed in the horizontal or vertical direction of the pipeline.

6) Avoid sagging or external force on the pipeline near the installation point. Use pipeline supports or supports to eliminate pipeline deviation.

7) After connecting to the pipeline, tighten the flange connection bolts symmetrically with the specified torque.

[Editing this section] Operation and Use

1) Confirm that the pipeline and valve have been flushed before operation.

2) The valve operates by rotating the valve stem according to the size of the input signal from the actuator: rotating forward 1/4 turn (90°) closes the valve, and rotating backward 1/4 turn (90°) opens the valve.

3) When the directional indicator arrow of the actuator is parallel to the pipeline, the valve is in the open state; when the arrow is perpendicular to the pipeline, the valve is in the closed state.

[Editing this section] Maintenance

A longer service life and maintenance-free period depend on several factors: normal working conditions, maintaining a harmonious temperature/pressure ratio, and reasonable corrosion data.

Note:

In the closed state, the ball valve still contains pressurized fluid inside the valve body.

Before maintenance, relieve line pressure and place the valve in the open position.

Before maintenance, disconnect the power or air supply.

Before maintenance, detach the actuator from the bracket.

Ensure that the upstream and downstream pipelines of the ball valve have been depressurized before performing disassembly and assembly operations.

When disassembling and reassembling, take care to avoid damaging the sealing surfaces of parts, especially non-metallic parts. When removing O-rings, it is advisable to use specialized tools.

When assembling, tighten the bolts on the flanges symmetrically, gradually, and evenly.

The cleaning agent should be compatible with the rubber components, plastic components, metal components, and working medium (such as natural gas) in the ball valve. When cleaning metal parts with natural gas as the working medium, gasoline (GB484-89) can be used. Non-metallic parts should be cleaned with distilled water or alcohol.

Individual parts can be cleaned by immersion washing. Metal parts with remaining non-metallic components can be wiped with a clean silk cloth soaked in cleaning agent (to avoid fiber shedding adhering to the parts). Clean all grease, dirt, adhesive, and dust adhered to the walls during cleaning.

Non-metallic parts should be immediately removed from the cleaning agent after cleaning and should not be soaked for a long time.

After cleaning, allow the washed surfaces to dry (can be wiped with a silk cloth not immersed in the cleaning agent) before assembly, but do not leave for too long to avoid rusting or contamination by dust.

New parts should also be cleaned before assembly.

Use lubricating grease for lubrication. The lubricating grease should be compatible with the metal material, rubber components, plastic components, and working medium of the ball valve. When the working medium is natural gas, lubricants such as TE221 can be used. Apply a thin layer of lubricant on the sealing surface of the sealing component installation groove, a thin layer on the rubber sealing component, and a thin layer on the sealing surface and friction surface of the valve stem.

During assembly, avoid contamination, adhesion, or entry of metal shavings, fibers, grease (except where specified), dust, and other impurities or foreign objects onto the part surfaces or into the interior chamber.

1) Re-tightening the packing If there is slight leakage at the packing, re-tighten the valve stem nut.

Note: Do not over-tighten. Usually, re-tightening 1/4 to 1 circle will stop the leakage.

2) Replacement of Seats and Sealing Components

A) Disassembly

Place the valve in a semi-open position, flush, and remove any hazardous substances that may exist inside and outside the valve body.

Close the ball valve, remove the connecting bolts and nuts from both flanges, then completely remove the valve from the pipeline.

Disassemble sequentially: drive device - actuator, connecting bracket, anti-loosening washer, valve stem nut, butterfly spring, knuckle, wear-resistant plate, valve stem packing.

Remove the body cover connecting bolts and nuts, separate the valve cover from the valve body, and remove the valve cover gasket.

Confirm that the ball is in the "shut-off" position, which makes it easier to remove it from the valve body, followed by removing the seat.

Gently push the valve stem downward through the central hole of the valve body until it is completely removed, then remove the O-ring and the lower packing of the valve stem.

Note: Handle with care to avoid scratching the valve stem surface and the sealing area of the valve body packing.

B) Reassembly

Clean and inspect the removed parts. Strongly recommend replacing the seat and valve cover gasket and other sealing components with spare parts.

Assemble in the reverse order of disassembly.

Tighten the flange connection bolts crosswise with the specified torque.

Tighten the valve stem nut with the specified torque.

After installing the actuator, input the corresponding signal to rotate the valve stem and drive the valve core to rotate, placing the valve in the open and closed positions.

If possible, conduct pressure sealing tests and performance tests on the valve according to relevant standards before reinstalling it into the pipeline.

1. Principle

The opening and closing component of the ball valve is a sphere, which rotates around the centerline of the valve body to achieve opening and closing.

2. Applicable Places

Used on low-pressure, small-diameter pipelines to cut off water flow and change the distribution of water flow or require quick opening and closing.

3. Features

1) Low flow resistance;

2) Simple structure;

3) Small size, light weight;

4) Currently, the sealing surface material of ball valves selects plastic, providing good sealing;

5) Easy operation, quick opening and closing, convenient for remote control;

6) Easy maintenance, the sealing ring is generally movable, making disassembly and replacement convenient;

7) When fully open or fully closed, the sealing face between the ball and the valve seat is isolated from the medium, preventing erosion of the valve sealing face when the medium passes through.

4. Classification

1) Common ball valves are classified by their working principle into floating ball valves, fixed ball valves, rising stem ball valves, etc.

2) Ball valves are classified by their channel position into straight-through, three-way, and right-angle types. The latter two types of ball valves are used for distributing media and changing the direction of the media.

5. Construction and Installation Points

1) The installation position, height, import/export direction must comply with design requirements, and the connection must be firm and tight.

2) Various manually operated valves installed on insulated pipelines must not have handles pointing downward.

3) Before valve installation, appearance inspection must be carried out. The valve nameplate should conform to the current national standard "General Valves Marking" GB 12220. For valves with a working pressure greater than 1.0 MPa and those acting as shut-off valves on main pipelines, strength and tightness performance tests must be conducted before installation, and only qualified valves can be used. During the strength test, the test pressure is 1.5 times the nominal pressure, lasting no less than 5 minutes, with no leakage from the valve shell or packing indicating qualification. During the tightness test, the test pressure is 1.1 times the nominal pressure; the duration of the test must comply with GB 50243 standards, with no leakage from the valve disc sealing face indicating qualification.

4) Install a sealing pad between the valve flange and the pipeline flange according to the pipeline design requirements.

5) Ball valves with transmission mechanisms should be installed according to the product user manual.
