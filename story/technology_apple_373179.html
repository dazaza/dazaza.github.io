Apple has released the most important software upgrade for iPad. iOS4.2 brings a large number of new features to iPad, including many features that are interesting to business people and IT departments, which have been deployed or are being deployed on iPad.

This update can be downloaded for free in iTunes, and it is also the first time to upgrade iPad to iOS4. This spring, iPhone was upgraded to iOS4, but iPad remained at iOS3.2 until now. After upgrading to iOS4.2, these two devices will now be unified on the same operating system platform.

Here are the top 10 upgrades of iOS4.2 for iPad business users listed by the author, allowing you to quickly browse its new features.

1. Multitasking

The lack of multitasking has been one of the most important factors hindering the development of iPad. With the update of iOS4.2, multitasking has finally arrived. It works the same way as multitasking on iPhone. It should be noted that iOS4.2 actually functions more like quick switching between applications while saving the state of open applications. But at least it changes the experience of only being able to open one application at a time on iPad before. This is important for users who need copy-paste functionality between applications. To access multitasking, you just need to double-click the Home button.

2. AirPlay

AirPlay is the most dazzling new feature of iOS4.2. It allows you to quickly stream media through iPad, iPhone, or iPod Touch, even Apple TV. The function is still in its infancy, and it sounds like it's not intended for commercial use. Today, when you open a video or picture, there are only two video streams on your big screen. In addition, you can almost instantly switch different presentations. In the future, imagine connecting an Apple TV - only $99, slightly larger than a smartphone - to a projector or a 60-inch LCD TV in a meeting room, and being able to quickly and simply directly share charts, graphics, media clips, and reports from your iPad. A large number of programs are trying to take advantage of on-demand benefits.

3. Wireless App Configuration

With iOS4.2, enterprises can roll out custom business apps to enterprise iPad users via WiFi or 3G. These apps do not need to go through the Apple App Store, and users do not need to sync with iTunes or a computer to obtain enterprise apps.

4. AirPrint

Another much-discussed feature about 4.2 is AirPrint, which allows you to print wirelessly from your iPad. This device is quite nice. The print option has been added to the menu, allowing you to print documents, pictures, and web pages from your iPad, which can automatically identify nearby compatible printers.

However, the types of compatible printers currently available are very limited, requiring software that adds this feature to enable printing capabilities in more places for iPad.

5. Find My iPad

This was previously part of Apple's paid service MobileMe, but it is now free in iOS4.2. Once you activate it on your iPad, you can find your lost iPad on a map on the Apple website, send messages to your iPad, display your message on your iPad screen ("My iPad is lost, please call 987654321"), set passwords remotely, lock snooping, and even wipe the drive and all data remotely.

6. Folders

You can now create folders on your iPad to better manage your apps, group your apps, hide infrequently used apps, and easily find the apps you want without flipping through countless screens.

7. Unified Inbox

This mail app offers an improved interface, reminding me of a great mail feature on Palm Pre. It allows you to quickly jump to the inbox (new mail) of each email account, or jump to the folder list of the email account, or jump to your unified inbox, where you can view all your latest emails arranged by time. After upgrading to iOS4.2, it also supports multiple Microsoft Exchange mailboxes.

8. Remote Device Management

Another development in enterprise applications is: after upgrading to iOS4.2, mobile device management APIs are provided, allowing enterprises to make configuration changes wirelessly, monitor policy enforcement, and allow IT management departments to control locking or erasing content on iPads. These functions are designed to facilitate direct deployment of teams composed of employees using iPads within enterprises.

9. Text Search on Same Page in Browser

The web browser on the iPad has a search box, and Apple has added a new feature to this search box, allowing you to search for text on the current webpage. This feature is useful when you perform a web search, log into a webpage, but cannot find the reference keywords you want to search for.

10. New Quick Console

Double-click the Home button to access multitasking; tap the upper left corner of the screen, and you'll find a new iPod quick console. This quick console has "Play/Pause" buttons and "Forward/Backward" buttons, a volume control slider, a brightness slider, and a new virtual orientation lock setting. The old physical orientation lock button (just above the volume button) has now been changed to a mute button.
