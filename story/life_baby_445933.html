It's amazing that babies seem to be naturally fond of playing with sand. Even those shy babies can have a great time in the sandbox. Sand is a gift from nature to babies, and this natural material is by no means inferior to expensive toys. Perhaps this is the most intimate contact with nature, which can make babies so fascinated and reluctant to leave. Sand play game - Little Movers:

I. Digging Sand

Provide the child with a small plastic shovel and let him play with digging sand. Babies can start with basic movements, using the little shovel to scoop up the sand and pour it into a bucket; then dump the sand out of the bucket. These actions can exercise fine motor skills and muscle strength, forming a preliminary understanding of the relationship between the shovel, the bucket, and the sand. The child can also understand the concept of depth and observe that as the depth changes, the color of the sand also changes.

II. Digging Tunnels

As long as water is added, the ways to play with sand become endlessly fascinating. For hygiene reasons, parents can use measuring cups filled with cool boiled water and add it to the sand with the child. Make two small hills and create little tunnels on them. Then try to pass fingers through the tunnels and hook each other's fingers. Parents can extend this play, such as passing a pen through the tunnel or making animals go through the tunnel, creating an adventure story.

III. Transporting

Use toy trucks with dump beds to transport sand from one place to another. This helps improve the child's balance and enhances their sense of direction.

IV. Building Roads

Dig the sand to form a small road and teach the baby to drive toy cars along this path. Digging and building roads can help children understand the knowledge of depth changes.

V. Making Water Channels

This method is somewhat similar to drawing in the sand. First, spread a thin layer of sand on the lid of a box, then dig out a Z-shaped or S-shaped small channel. Children can place boats in the channel and create their own water adventure stories.

Sand is a material that can be shaped, with many variations and creativity. Different methods of playing with sand will produce different effects. Parents might as well let the child process, build, and play with sand according to their wishes. During the sand play process, the baby has no constraints, no pressure, and can freely imagine and create repeatedly.
