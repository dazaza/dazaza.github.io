Amazon CEO's past: His biological father only knew about Bezos last year

by anonymous on 2013-10-11 19:48:57

Jeff Bezos

Phoenix Technology News, October 11th, according to the BusinessInsider website report, recently, Brad Stone's new book "The Everything Store: Jeff Bezos and the Age of Amazon" is about to be published. The Bloomberg Businessweek magazine has issued a long serial story, telling the story of Amazon and its founder Jeff Bezos, and more mysterious stories from the early years of Bezos' family have been uncovered.

At the age of three, young Amazon CEO Jeff Bezos saw his biological father for the last time. His parents got married when they were in high school and had a child, but less than a year and a half later, they divorced. Bezos' mother remarried, and his biological father lost contact completely, even forgetting Bezos' new name. Last year, when writer Brad Stone visited, this careless father was surprised to learn that his son was still alive and had become one of the world's richest men.

When Bezos' biological father Ted Jorgensen was 18 and Jacqueline Gise was 17, they had an out-of-wedlock affair and got married due to pregnancy, but their marriage only lasted 17 months.

Gise could not tolerate Jorgensen staying out all night and drinking excessively, so she soon moved back to her parents' house. Stone revealed that the young Jorgensen was never a responsible father or husband. Later, Jorgensen agreed to the divorce, and when he had money, he occasionally gave Gise a small amount of child support, but other times he paid nothing at all.

Three years after the divorce, Bezos' mother Gise remarried a Cuban immigrant named Miguel Bezos, and young Jeff took his stepfather's surname. Jorgensen had to sign the name change agreement. Jorgensen only remembered Bezos' name for a short period of time, but after a few years, he completely lost contact with his son and ex-wife.

In a 1999 interview with Wired Magazine, Bezos mentioned "I've never seen him," but in fact, during the first year of his life, young Jeff lived under the same roof as Jorgensen. "In my eyes, he is just my biological father, and the only time I thought of him was when the doctor asked me to fill out a form."

Jorgensen himself also had a similar attitude towards Bezos. However, Stone managed to find him. In his old age, Jorgensen had his own bike shop in Arizona. After Stone explained his purpose, Jorgensen had no idea what this person was talking about. He didn't know who Jeff Bezos was and even denied being his biological father. Stone slowly guided Jorgensen's memories and found details related to Bezos and his ex-wife in his fragile items. This elderly man's expression changed, suddenly remembering something, "Is he still alive?"

Bezos' biological father lost complete contact with him, even forgetting his name, not knowing that his biological son is one of the richest people in the world, and even thought he was already dead.

Jorgensen admitted, "I wasn't a good father or a good husband," but later he quit drinking, focused on opening a bicycle shop, and remarried, but there were no other children. Stone's visit plunged the elderly Jorgensen into sadness and regret. He wanted to get in touch with Bezos, but he didn't know if it would come true.

Stone's book about Amazon will soon be available, and the story of Bezos' biological father is written on page seven.
