Monthly high temperature steaming

by gege on 2012-02-27 20:05:42

Many people change their towels every six months to a year, and some only replace them when they are too worn out to be used. In fact, personal towels should ideally be replaced every 30 days, and at most should not exceed 40 days.

Monthly high-temperature steaming

According to home textile experts, personal towels should be replaced after about 30 days of use, and at most should not exceed 40 days. Otherwise, they need to be disinfected and softened by high-temperature steaming. This is because, in addition to becoming hard and uncomfortable with prolonged use, the most important issue is that they accumulate large amounts of bacteria, which can be harmful to health.

Towels are made from pure cotton yarn, and cotton fibers have a tubular structure that can store water. The main chemical component, cellulose molecules, contains a large number of hydrophilic groups that can absorb water. The moisture-absorbing and water-storing properties of cotton yarn make the towel feel comfortable and enhance its cleaning ability, making it suitable for washing the face. However, these same properties also provide convenient conditions for bacterial growth. Bacteria thrive in warm, damp environments. When towels remain in a warm, humid state for a long time, they become breeding grounds for bacteria after a certain period. Additionally, oils from the skin, dust, impurities in the water, and bacteria from the air accumulate on the towel. Using such a towel to clean the skin does not only fail to clean effectively but can also contaminate the skin and block pores. Women who wear makeup and children with fast metabolism have more oily skin, making towels more prone to bacterial contamination, posing even greater risks.

When washing your face or taking a bath, it is advisable to rinse thoroughly with shower gel and water first, then dry with a dry towel. This reduces the attachment of dirt to the towel. After using the towel, it should be cleaned promptly, boiled in hot water for 10 minutes each week to disinfect, and hung in a ventilated area. Ideally, it should be dried or sun-dried promptly.

Each person should own multiple towels

Home textile experts say: improper use of towels can affect health. Especially in summer, it is recommended that each person owns multiple towels. Although towels are small, they accompany a person throughout their life. If a towel carries a large amount of bacteria and viruses, they can penetrate into the body through the sensory organs and skin, affecting health. Scientific evidence shows that coins, bedding, and towel products are the three major indirect transmission media for diseases. Due to the influence of traditional living habits and consumer concepts, many consumers overlook the role of towels in maintaining health and continue to use unscientific methods: such as sharing one towel among multiple people, using one towel for multiple purposes, replacing only when broken, reusing without regard for hygiene, and ignoring the harm of inferior products.

Choosing towels

When purchasing towels, try to buy from large malls, supermarkets, or specialty stores, choosing products from reputable manufacturers. Qualified towel products should have standard labels indicating the manufacturer, origin, phone number, trademark, execution standards, and washing instructions.

In terms of texture, choose towels that are soft and fluffy. Such towels feel elastic when held and give a soft, comfortable sensation against the face. Towels should be avoided if they feel dry and hard to prevent skin damage. Generally speaking, high-quality towels have softer textures and are fluffy with good elasticity.

Additionally, families can perform quality checks on towels. For example, if a drop of water placed on a new towel is quickly absorbed, it indicates good water absorption. High-quality towels have elasticity and friction during use and do not discolor in water. Inferior towels have poor water absorption, lack elasticity, feel slippery, and discolor significantly in water, causing greater irritation and damage to the skin and eyes.
