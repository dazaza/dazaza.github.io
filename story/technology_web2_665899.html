Steel prices show signs of a temporary "rebound"

by sffgskkgfel9 on 2012-02-15 11:16:14

Steel prices show signs of a brief "rebound" - Steel Information - Industry News - Building Materials Network. After three consecutive months of decline, steel prices experienced a short-lived报复性上涨 (retaliatory increase) last week before quickly retreating this week, showing signs of another adjustment.

Yesterday, several steel company sales managers informed reporters that steel prices rebounded from the 19th to the 23rd, with several construction steel varieties increasing their factory prices by an average of about 200 yuan/ton. However, this was merely a brief effect of price anticipation, and steel prices began to retreat again starting Monday this week, with an average decrease of 20-30 yuan/ton.

"The rebound in steel prices last week was a short-term behavior; the upward trend is not strong, and the overall steel market outlook for the second half of the year remains pessimistic," said industry insiders yesterday. They added that future steel prices will continue a trend of weak oscillation, and it will be very difficult for steel prices to surpass previous high points.

After a brief rebound, prices fell again

Following three months of continuous declines, steel prices saw a robust rebound last week, with several major steel product prices rising, particularly construction steel.

Data shows that national wire rod and rebar prices surged significantly last week, with mainstream increases ranging from 150-200 yuan/ton, and some markets seeing rises exceeding 300 yuan/ton. By last Thursday, the national average price for 3.0mm hot-rolled coil was 4219 yuan/ton, up 172 yuan/ton compared to the previous week.

However, this was just a fleeting moment, as steel prices started to adjust again on the 26th of this week, with an average decrease of 20-30 yuan/ton.

Yesterday, reporters learned from several major steel companies within the province that after last week's brief rise, prices for several main steel products have all decreased.

"People's anticipation for price increases and the state's renewed stimulation of the market caused a temporary rise in steel prices, which is a normal reaction to market psychology expectations," stated Manager Zhang from the Sales Department of Changzhi Iron and Steel Group in an interview with the *Shanxi Youth Daily*. However, due to no significant growth in actual market demand, steel prices will eventually start to fall again. According to this person, there is a downward trend in national steel inventories, and some small steel mills in Shanxi are also reducing production.

This statement was confirmed by Niu Huimin, the sales manager of Hai Xin Steel's North China region. After the average price increase of 200 yuan/ton for several main steel products last week, steel prices began to retreat on the 26th, with an average decrease of 30 yuan/ton. The factory price for wire rods was 4000 yuan/ton.

The future trend will remain volatile but weak

"The prices of several main steel products have stabilized since last Friday's adjustments, with only slight adjustments to individual varieties and little change overall," said relevant personnel from the Taiyuan Steel Chamber of Commerce. Following this round of rapid rebounds, analysts remain pessimistic about the future trends of steel prices.

"The steel price rebound last week was a short-term behavior with little upward trend," industry insiders said. Issues within the steel industry have not been well resolved, and inventory digestion is not obvious, remaining at relatively high levels, resulting in a situation where supply exceeds demand in the market.

Additionally, this person believes that since last year, market liquidity has been released quite aggressively, and there is a possibility that the state will withdraw liquidity in the future, putting pressure on steel prices. Combined with the unfavorable domestic and international situations, the pressure on foreign trade exports is significant, with much uncertainty. At the same time, domestic automobile consumption has declined for three consecutive months, and the growth potential of shipbuilding, home appliances, and machinery is not obvious. There are few factors supporting steel prices in the second half of the year.

"It will be very difficult for steel prices to set new highs in the second half of the year," the above-mentioned person said. Future steel prices will continue to show a weak oscillating trend. They also reminded steel traders to pay attention to policy trends in the future, as these will impact the movement of steel prices. (Youth Online)
