75-Year-Old Elder Has Adhered to Recording News Everyday for 40 Years (Picture)

by xuwbi718 on 2011-05-31 15:15:43

Family members do not understand Old Huang's habit. Yesterday, his 64-year-old wife Wei Zhulan complained: "You've wasted countless ink all your life! What use is there for us farmers to record those things?" Because of this, the family sold part of Old Huang's "news notes" as waste paper. However, Old Huang smiled and said: "Not watching the news is like being a headless fly, bumping around aimlessly. Besides, we love studying national policies and analyzing development trends. This habit cannot be changed!"

Whenever he has free time, Huang Quan takes out his news notebook to read.

Correspondent Shi Xiaorong took the photo.

Neighbors love to chat with him.

From agricultural policies to presidential elections, 75-year-old Huang Quan from Apu Village, Beiyuan Township, Baishui County, developed a habit in the late 1980s: every day he watches TV news. Even when farm work is busy, he still makes time to finish watching the news and organizes it into thick notebooks.

Huang Quan responded to the call in 1969 and moved from Xi'an to the countryside for training. He worked in various departments such as enterprises, reservoirs, and township governments for most of his life. Since 1970, he has liked recording big and small events happening domestically and internationally. At that time, although he only earned 9 yuan per month, he would still subscribe to newspapers and buy magazines. According to his preferences and the news value, he organized what he saw into notes.

Except for special reasons like power outages, Old Huang almost records news every day. "This is like an iron chain; one link dare not break." To watch the news and take notes on time, Old Huang often adjusts his schedule. When others rest at home during hot weather, he works in the fields. Sometimes, when he has only plowed halfway through the field and sees that the news time is approaching, he quickly drives the cattle back home.

"On October 1993, half of the domestic trains changed from bright white to red air-conditioned cars"; "In October 1998, the train was significantly upgraded again, half turned into blue carriages traveling at 160 km/h"; "The 14th Party Congress of Central Government Agencies was held in Beijing, Zhu Rongji made important instructions"... On Old Huang's news notebook, almost everything significant in politics, economics, society, and other areas over these decades is recorded.

Two years ago, Old Huang suffered from cataracts and his vision became blurry, but his daily habit of recording news did not change. During the interview, while his wife complained, she also served him tea and water. Old Huang laughed broadly: "A person lives old, learns old." In 40 years, he has almost recorded all major events both domestically and internationally. Reporter Qing Rongbo, Correspondent Shi Xiaorong.

In Apu Village, almost everyone knows about Old Huang. Villagers say: "Old Huang is a celebrity within ten miles. Not only can he speak well and write, but he also understands music, loves sports, and knows a lot about some national policies. Neighbors, whether they have something or not, love to chat with him."

Even if he has only plowed halfway through the field, he will go home to watch the news.

Zhang Renchuang, the secretary of Apu Village, Beiyuan Township, said: "Old Huang has had this habit for several decades." Due to his excellent chef skills, even when helping with weddings and funerals, once the news program starts, he uses any available material like cigarette packs or scrap paper to record. "This kind of spirit is indeed hard for ordinary people to achieve." Old Huang can write beautifully. After returning from working in the township, he has always been the village custodian and labor recorder. Although he is not a party member, he holds himself to higher standards than party members. While serving as a publicity officer in the village branch, besides publishing blackboard newspapers, he frequently explained good policies the party introduced for farmers to the masses.

Persistently watching news and organizing notes every day.

In 1986, Apu Village got electricity, and the village bought a television for Old Huang to keep. Every afternoon, he would carry the TV out for the public to watch programs, then carry it back late at night. Since then, he has always placed the TV early. "Every evening at 7 o'clock, when the news broadcast begins, I take my notebook and record the daily news content. After going home, I neatly organize it into notes according to its news value."
