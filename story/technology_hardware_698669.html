The conception of G2 not only causes contradictions among countries that are striving to enhance their international status in order not to be left out of the international decision-making circle, but also no one in the U.S. government, Congress, and think tanks plans or imagines foreign strategies based on it. China has explicitly expressed its disagreement. It is currently not accepted by most countries. However, if we do not presuppose political conditions and instead understand it as a certain joint support for the overall situation between China and the United States, then it can be said that it is a conception in line with future political realities.

People have witnessed the world dominated by the United States. What the future world will look like, a widely popular view holds that this largely depends on the future of Sino-U.S. relations. Recently, Brzezinski again mentioned G2 in an interview. G2 is the most eye-catching conception in Sino-U.S. relations. Although it has been widely criticized, as an idea from academia, both sides need to pay further attention to and discuss it.

A rupture in Sino-U.S. relations might lead to catastrophic economic consequences. The formation of "Chimerica" in the economy is neither the pursuit of governments nor the will of the public. It is the result of an unintentional dependence formed in state relations. The directional meaning implied in "Chimerica" lies in the fact that the success of national economies cannot be achieved apart from the international environment. Joint actions by China and the United States have become a major factor in global economic governance. The world cannot possibly have two economic systems and two economic forces as it did during the Cold War era. If the first and second largest economies cannot work together, they will not be able to pursue prosperity, and the world will not be able to enter a new era.

The unification of Europe has gone through many stops and starts, and the path of G2 will be even more bumpy. When Sino-U.S. relations fall into difficulties or achieve substantial improvement, or when humanity is at a loss in the face of common challenges, it will trigger continuous associations about G2. For the whole world, the positive role generated by the cooperation between China and the United States is extremely significant, and the destructive power generated by the split between the two sides is equally enormous. This is the reason why the academic circles of China and the United States need to rethink G2. (President of Lianyungang Development Research Institute, Jiangsu)

If antagonistic factors in Sino-U.S. relations overwhelm the pursuit of cooperation, it will probably be impossible to solve any of the problems in global governance. The almost instantaneous end of the unipolar era shows that "going it alone" is no longer feasible.

Currently, almost all Asian countries are staunch believers in realism: forming alliances with strong countries, purchasing advanced fighter jets, establishing military bases, testing long-range missiles, enhancing naval strength, and conducting military preparations while developing economically. The situation where Asian politics has been dominated externally since modern times has not changed, and this region seems to have no possible shared future.

At some of the sharpest stages of the Cold War, both the U.S. and Soviet Union's nuclear-armed strategic bombers were on 24-hour duty flights every day, and the combat readiness of their troops was always maintained at the level of "mutual assured destruction." If Sino-U.S. competition does not want to develop to such a situation, cooperation must be placed at the forefront. The establishment of a broad cooperative relationship between the two sides is a guarantee for world peace, which is the basis for understanding G2 strategically.
