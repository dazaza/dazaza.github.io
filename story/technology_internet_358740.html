Riding Diary: Cool breeze blows three times, walking through the green wilderness of湟中 (Liangzhong)

by ditan360 on 2010-06-22 14:44:23

Low Carbon Network exclusive manuscript. The poet Chang Yao wrote a long poem - "Harakuto" in the ancient city of Dangar (Lahore).

Castle, an eternal and unchanging theme of fate and sentiment,

The glorious mask has sunk with the warriors' shouts,

Like beeswax, dazzling but eventually softening, dissipating like dust.

Whether it be a sword, copper arrows, or the bone flutes of our ancestors,

None can resist the magic of the sun's radiation.

Here lies the memories of the poet's lifetime migration, yet I fell asleep early in the castle he described. Two decades have passed, and we quietly left our footprints.

At 5 in the morning, my companion woke me up. We hurriedly washed up, ate some bread, and drank milk. Forty minutes later, we drove to the next destination, Haiyan.

The temperature difference between morning and evening in Qinghai is quite large. Despite wearing a coat, the cool breeze still brings a strong chill, causing involuntary shivers. There was a bit of white light in the dark sky above, penetrating through narrow cracks to drive away the darkness. For safety reasons, we decided to proceed with the spotlight on. The road conditions were smooth, with almost no steep slopes.

Passing by the clear Yinxin滩, there were early-rising herders driving their cattle and sheep out to pasture. Naturally, Wang Luobin's "In That Distant Place" came to mind, its melody continuously echoing in my brain. So, we stopped the car, looked quietly at the grassland for a while, then left. The altitude here has already reached 2970 meters, making high-altitude cycling somewhat strenuous. Fortunately, the beautiful scenery along the way made the mood incredibly pleasant.

There were some minor accidents on the road; my companion punctured a tire. I quickly replaced it with a new one. At 7:30, we arrived at Haiyan. By this time, half the sky was already bright, but the temperature was still very low. We hardly paused and continued moving forward. Riding against the wind became unbearable, our faces frozen blue, and hunger followed suit. I drank a little cold water, resulting in being chilled thoroughly from the outside in.

At 9, we finally reached Xihai Town, with an altitude of 3070 meters.

Xihai Town was built in 1958, where China's first atomic bomb and hydrogen bomb were both born, hence also called "Atom City." Walking into it feels like returning to revolutionary times. The old buildings are neatly arranged, and the vegetation is very lush. The entire town gives a feeling of great peace, and the streets are clean.

We had three bowls of stewed noodles at a breakfast shop, small bowls with thin soup, really not commendable. However, the boss was very热情. I asked them what attractions were worth visiting here, and he said there was a museum to visit. After drinking some hot soup, we felt better and decided to ride around, looking to buy some convenient food. The memory of riding while cold and hungry was too painful.

After visiting the museum, I began to feel unwell, drenched in cold sweat. As the temperature rose, I felt my hands and feet icy cold, and frequent coughing shook my ribs painfully. Probably because of the fast speed in the morning, the bone-chilling plateau wind froze my body. Seeing my condition, my companion took me to a nearby pharmacy for a simple check-up and got some medicine.

After taking the medicine, my muscles started to ache. To avoid the sun seems to have been somewhat counterproductive. Our journey had to stop due to me. We found the youth hostel marked in the previous plan. Unfortunately, there were no vacancies left. Most tourists coming here make reservations in advance. We helplessly returned to the vast prairie, set up our tent, and I wrapped myself in a sleeping bag, feeling dizzy. Being sick always makes one abnormally vulnerable, and I was deeply moved by the hot coffee my companion went out to buy.

I was vaguely awakened by someone calling my name, unsure how long I had slept. My companion said, let's go, we've found a place to stay. It turned out that a local police officer driving by saw our tent. Knowing our situation, he kindly invited us to sleep at the police station. I looked at my watch, it was nearly 7. After returning to the police station, he cooked some noodles for us. Although far from home, encountering such hospitality warmed my heart again. The indoor temperature was only 16 degrees, but my heart felt warm.
