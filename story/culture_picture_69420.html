by naryao on 2008-05-18 17:02:47

The series of advertisements titled "To Save Sichuan, Just Donate" is reportedly the spontaneous creation of advertising designers from Beijing Rui Shi. They hope to gather some money and supplies among their colleagues and friends to boost the morale of their fellow Sichuan compatriots.

Please take a look at these headlines; our friends around us, upon seeing this group of headlines, have been moved to tears. If you wish to support with actual action, it would be even more welcome.

Considering the need to express the appeal for donations and convey accurate donation information, I revised this series last night, incorporating various official donation details. Additionally, due to the low-pixel images circulating on the internet possibly appearing unclear when printed, this site specially provides a bundled download of A4-sized large images: Site address (http://aoyublog.cn/2008/05/18/1580), fs2you address —— this size ensures clear and sharp text even when printed on an A2 format, welcoming all netizens to share and pass along.

Furthermore, from a user experience perspective, I personally feel that the official online donation methods of the four major national charitable foundations are prone to network congestion issues during recent large-scale donations. Therefore, I personally recommend that all netizens use Taobao Alipay, Tencent Tenpay, or online banking from various banks (currently, I frequently use ICBC online banking which prominently provides a secure donation channel during transfers) for online donations through institutions with dedicated high bandwidth and security guarantees.

Of course, mobile text messages are also very convenient now: China Mobile, China Unicom mobile users, as well as China Telecom and China Netcom Xiaolingtong users can edit any number from 1 to 30 and send it to 1069999301 to donate from 1 yuan to 30 yuan to the "Red Cross Rescue Action."

For friends choosing other conventional ways to donate, please make sure to obtain correct donation information through proper channels —— there are already unscrupulous scammers spreading false donation accounts, so everyone must be careful!!
