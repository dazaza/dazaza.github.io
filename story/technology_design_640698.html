Choice of formula milk

by wqnaifen on 2011-12-22 15:19:04

Formula milk, also known as breast milk substitute, is a dairy product that has been specially formulated to meet the nutritional needs of infants. It removes components from cow's milk that are not easily absorbed by babies and even improves some deficiencies in breast milk such as low iron content. Therefore, adding formula milk is a widely adopted practice worldwide for ensuring healthy growth in infants. However, no formula milk can match the quality of breast milk.

Protein: 27% of the protein in breast milk is β-lactoglobulin, while only 4% of the protein in cow's milk is β-lactoglobulin. α-lactalbumin provides an amino acid combination closest to breast milk, enhancing the biological utilization of proteins, reducing the total amount of protein, and effectively alleviating the burden on the kidneys.

DHA and AA: DHA specifically refers to docosahexaenoic acid, also known as "brain gold," while AA refers to arachidonic acid. Both DHA and AA are polyunsaturated fatty acids that play a crucial role in the development of an infant's brain and vision.

Vitamins A and D, iron, zinc, and a calcium-to-phosphorus ratio of 2:1, along with added vitamin D, promote calcium absorption. However, more added elements in formula milk do not necessarily mean better.

Nucleotides: Infants who consume breast milk have better resistance due to the presence of nucleotides, an important substance found in breast milk.

Linoleic Acid and Alpha-Linolenic Acid: Babies who drink breast milk are smart and lively because they synthesize enough DHA/AA. If a baby's formula contains sufficient linoleic acid and alpha-linolenic acid, DHA and AA can be naturally synthesized according to the baby's needs, promoting better brain development.

No Palm Oil: Generally, babies who consume breast milk have softer stools that are not very hard. Recent scientific research has discovered that the reason for poor calcium absorption and dry stools in babies is due to the combination of palm oil in formula milk with calcium, forming calcium soap. This proves that formula milk without palm oil promotes better calcium absorption and stronger bones in babies.
