On March 5, 2012, the press conference for the Xinjiang sub-division of the Red Bull City Badminton Tournament [Feather Grove Struggle] was held in Urumqi.

The Red Bull City Badminton Tournament [Feather Grove Struggle] has been officially approved by the Table Tennis and Badminton Sports Center of the General Administration of Sport of China and is sponsored by Red Bull Vitamin Beverage Co., Ltd. Since its inception in 2010, it has successfully been held twice, covering 129 prefecture-level cities or above across 28 provinces nationwide. In 2012, this tournament makes its first appearance in Xinjiang, with events taking place in Urumqi, Changji, and Kuitun.

The 2012 Red Bull City Badminton Tournament will officially kick off on April 7. The Xinjiang division competition will start on April 14, while the inter-city tournament for the Urumqi region will begin on May 26. The tournament format is a five-person mixed team event including men's singles, men's doubles, and mixed doubles. The schedule consists of four stages: city-level tournaments, inter-city tournaments, regional tournaments for northern and southern divisions, and the final. The finals are scheduled to take place on August 6, during which the two championship teams from the north and south regions will compete for the grand title during the Olympic Games period.

Deputy Director Liu Zhong of the Urumqi Municipal Bureau of Sports said: "The 2012 'Feather Grove Struggle' Red Bull City Badminton Tournament is a national-scale event, with broad coverage, a large number of participants, and a scale that has not been seen before. This event being hosted in three cities in Xinjiang will be beneficial for the popularization and improvement of badminton in Xinjiang. Through this badminton tournament, we aim to promote the nationwide fitness activities in Xinjiang, and hope for active participation from all citizens."

According to Chairman Yang Jun of the Urumqi Badminton Association, after several years of promotion, the amateur level of badminton in Xinjiang has significantly improved. There are some badminton clubs in Dushanzi, Karamay, Shihezi, and Urumqi. Nowadays, more and more people are using badminton as a means of physical exercise.

It is reported that registration for the Xinjiang division tournament has already begun and will end on April 16. The tournament offers generous prize money, with the city-level champion receiving 2000 yuan, the inter-city champion 6000 yuan, and the grand final champion 20000 yuan.
