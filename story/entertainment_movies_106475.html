"Love Left Right" is the sequel to "Love on Call" from 2006. This time, the sequel breaks away from the style of one man with twelve women in the original work, and changes to a plot structure of one woman dealing with twelve men. The film describes how an intellectual beauty gets involved in the emotional troubles of different urban men, begins to become confused about her own feelings and role at work, and after a series of emotional collisions and searching through the vast sea of people, finally realizes the true meaning of love: FIT IS BEST! What suits you is the best.

As women approach thirty, every choice for the other half feels like being at a three-way crossroads, should she go left or right? The men in front of her can never satisfy Nie Bing. Perhaps around the next corner, the next man she meets is worth staying for. So she keeps turning and moving forward. Reality often does not go as wished, has Nie Bing never met a suitable man, or does she herself simply not want to stay? As the title of this movie suggests, signaling left but turning right.

Nie Bing, a single female host of a well-known emotional program, wakes up in shock from nightmares of loneliness and aging every day. The black cat in her dreams becomes a nightmare in her reality. After breaking up with her ex-boyfriend, after her twenty-ninth birthday, Nie Bing remains alone.

In her emotional program, when the studio spotlights are turned on, Nie Bing becomes an emotional expert in front of the audience, helping many couples with emotional problems achieve happy marriages. In real life, Nie Bing can only hide alone in her spacious apartment, crying by herself in the corner of the sofa.

After getting drunk at the bar, a man claiming to be an angel gives her a magical car and tells her that driving this car will give Nie Bing ten chances, and each turn will allow her to meet a type of man. Thus, Nie Bing begins this magical journey of seeking love.
