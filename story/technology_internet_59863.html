by inspiron on 2008-04-04 16:35:41

The long-standing rumors about the resignation of Tang Jun have finally come true this time.

The Morning Post published a report on Tuesday (April 1) titled "Tang Jun, President of Shanda, will announce his resignation this week." As expected, yesterday Shanda Interactive Entertainment Limited formally announced that due to personal development needs, Tang Jun will resign from his position as company president and instead become a consultant to the CEO while continuing to serve as a company director. In addition, Shanda CTO Tan Qunzhao will take over as president.

"During the four years Mr. Tang Jun served as president of Shanda, he successfully completed the responsibilities and missions assigned to him by the company, setting an example for professional managers and making important contributions during a critical period of Shanda's growth and development. We thank Mr. Tang Jun for his work and wish him greater achievements in his new field." Chairman and CEO of Shanda, Chen Tianqiao, expressed his gratitude as he bid farewell to Tang Jun.

As Shanda officially announced this personnel change, Tang Jun also sent a text message to reporters yesterday, indicating that he has officially stepped down from his position as president of Shanda and plans to reveal his future plans at a later date.

Tang Jun told reporters that he currently faces many choices but will absolutely not act as an investor and will not join the social networking site Facebook as rumored.

In addition, Tang Jun confirmed to reporters that his future work will be unrelated to the internet and gaming industries and revealed that his workplace will still be in Shanghai.

In February 2004, Tang Jun surprisingly announced his early resignation as President of Microsoft China and joined Shanda as president. Thereafter, during his more than four years at Shanda, Tang Jun demonstrated his importance to the company through his actions. Besides successfully leading Shanda to its NASDAQ IPO, during a crucial moment when Shanda was losing favor with investors, Tang Jun once again traveled to Wall Street to explain Shanda's online game model to overseas investors, achieving great success.

While announcing Tang Jun's resignation yesterday, Shanda also quickly unveiled its new round of executive appointments.

Yesterday, Shanda announced the appointment of CTO Tan Qunzhao to replace Tang Jun as company president, retaining his role as CTO; the appointment of senior vice president Chen Dahan as the company's Chief Operating Officer (COO); the appointment of Vice President Li Yu as the CEO of the Shanda Games Division (SDG); and the appointment of Senior Vice President Wang Jingying as the CEO of the Shanda Online Division (SDO).

"Shanda has carefully considered these appointments and adjustments. As founders of the company, Tan Qunzhao and Chen Dahan, along with professional managers Li Yu and Wang Jingying, who have been cultivated and grown within Shanda for many years, already possess the necessary capabilities and qualities to handle the tasks that will be assigned to them," said Chen Tianqiao.

It is understood that Tan Qunzhao graduated from East China University of Science and Technology with a bachelor's degree in chemical engineering and is one of the founders of Shanda. He has held multiple positions within Shanda and before this appointment, served as senior vice president and president of the Shanda Games Division (SDG). Prior to joining Shanda, from July 1996 to November 1999, Tan Qunzhao worked as an assistant at the Clean Coal Technology Research Institute of East China University of Science and Technology.

Chen Dahan is also one of the founders of Shanda, having held multiple positions within the company. Before this appointment, he served as senior vice president and president of the Shanda Online Division (SDO). Although Li Yu joined Shanda in 2005, he previously held management positions at Expedia and Microsoft. Before this appointment, he served as vice president and vice president of the Shanda Games Division (SDG). Wang Jingying joined Shanda in 2002 and previously held management positions at Waterman Drinks and Mary Kay Cosmetics. Before this appointment, she served as senior vice president and vice president of the Shanda Online Division (SDO).

Shanda's stock price slightly increased by 0.3% yesterday, closing at $29.98 per share.
