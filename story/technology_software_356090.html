Housing prices remain high, sales of commercial housing in the main urban area of Wuhan drop by more than half_ Shanghai Second-hand Housing Network G1

by ww0938788 on 2010-05-26 20:57:08

The impact of the new round of property market regulation is gradually showing in Wuhan. Yesterday, according to the data from Wuhan Real Estate Market Information Network, the daily average sales volume of commercial housing in the main urban area has dropped to less than 120 units, a decrease of more than 50% compared to mid-April before the regulation. Overall, due to the low-cost housing in suburban areas being relatively unaffected, the city's daily sales volume has decreased by more than 30% compared to April. Statistics from Wuhan Efang Research Center show that from May 6th to 12th, there were 335 on-sale residential projects in the main urban area, with an average transaction price of 6640.66 yuan per square meter, selling a total of 790 units, averaging 113 units sold per day. Compared to the same period last year, this represents a decrease of 51.7%. This is the fourth consecutive week of declining transaction volumes since the implementation of the new property policies. In four weeks' time, the sales volume in the main urban area was 1889 units, 1685 units, 1023 units, and 790 units respectively, showing a one-sided downward trend. Compared to mid-April, the sales volume in the main urban area has dropped by more than 50%.

Looking at the entire city, in the first 13 days of May, a total of 4400 commercial housing units were sold, with a daily average sales volume of nearly 340 units. There were 5 days during which the daily sales volume was below 300 units. Compared to the city's daily average sales volume of 530 units in April, the daily average sales volume for the first 13 days of May decreased by 190 units, a drop of 36%. Analysts pointed out that the decline in sales volume in the main urban area "outperformed" the city's average level because the housing prices in the urban area are too high, leading to higher expectations for price reductions among homebuyers. Buyers are not satisfied with the small discounts currently offered by developers, causing a faster decline in transactions. On the contrary, housing prices in suburban areas are already low, so buyers have lower expectations for price reductions and still need to purchase homes.

From the data, housing prices reached their peak at the end of April, and the upward momentum has been curbed, remaining stable at a high point over the past two weeks. In the recent three weeks, the average transaction prices in the main urban area were 6644 yuan/sq m, 6619 yuan/sq m, and 6641 yuan/sq m respectively, with little change. Chen Long, a researcher from Wuhan Efang, analyzed that currently most housing developments only offer small discount incentives, leading buyers to choose to wait and observe, resulting in sluggish real estate transactions. This state will likely continue for some time. Chen Haifeng, head of Central China Real Estate, said that given the significant reduction in investment-driven and improvement-oriented homebuyers, and the severe decline in sales volume, many housing developments are now focusing on first-time homebuyers but with little effect. "As long as the sales volume does not increase, price cuts are inevitable," he stated.
