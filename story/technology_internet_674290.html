China and Russia refuse to attend the Friends of Syria conference

by king2z9o on 2012-02-24 11:37:24

On the 23rd, Syria engaged in a war of words with the West over the killing of two Western journalists that occurred within its borders the previous day; meanwhile, the Friends of Syria conference, led by Western and some Arab countries, was planned to take place on the 24th. Countries like Russia have already indicated they will not participate.

The Syrian Foreign Ministry issued a statement on the 23rd calling on foreign journalists reporting in Syria to abide by local regulations and avoid traveling to areas of unrest without permission. The statement said that the Syrian government would provide convenience and protection for foreign journalists who comply with Syrian press regulations. In the past two months, the Syrian Ministry of Information has issued permits to reporters from more than 200 media outlets [http://www.tingfeier.com]. The statement also refuted claims that the Syrian government should be held responsible for the deaths of the journalists.

On the 23rd, Syrian government forces clashed with anti-government armed groups in Homs, Idlib, and Hama. Security forces in Homs suffered a roadside bomb attack, resulting in one death and three injuries. Previously, according to Syrian human rights organizations, two Western journalists were killed in an attack on the 22nd. Governments and public opinion in the West criticized the "unacceptable" actions of the Syrian government.

In the capital Damascus, where demonstrations were rarely seen in the past, protests erupted multiple times in the last week. Demonstrators clashed with security forces. According to informed sources, demonstrators had reached an agreement with security departments [http://www.baode.cc] not to engage in radical behavior during marches, but some deliberately displayed anti-government flags during gatherings and attacked security personnel.

The Syrian government is making intensive preparations for the upcoming referendum on the new constitution draft, which will begin on the 26th of this month. Many volunteers dressed in clothing matching the colors of the Syrian flag appeared in the center of Damascus, distributing booklets of the new constitution draft to passersby, urging them to actively participate in the referendum. The Syrian Interior Ministry also introduced a series of measures to ensure the safe and smooth conduct of the referendum.

On the evening of the 21st, the domestic opposition party Front for Change and Liberation held a sit-in protest in front of the People's Parliament, protesting the clause adjustment in the new constitution draft stating that "[the president must be a Muslim]." Analysts pointed out that many of the reform measures taken by the Syrian government are substantial, but most opposition factions do not recognize reforms led by Bashar, making the prospect of "national dialogue" less than optimistic.

The Friends of Syria meeting will be held in Tunisia on the 24th, and the Syrian official media prominently published news that Russia and Lebanon will not attend. SANA quoted Palestinian political scholar Adel Samara as saying that the Friends of Syria meeting will further clarify the funding and arming of the Syrian opposition, but it will serve no purpose because they have already done everything they can. Tunisian official Ahmed Menei, who participated in the Arab League observer mission to Syria, stated that the so-called Friends of Syria meeting is entirely organized by Syria's enemies, aiming to further support the armed opposition and undermine Syria's security and stability.

Russian Deputy Foreign Minister Gatiylov stated at a press conference on the 22nd that Russia opposes discussing solutions to the Syrian crisis without involving the Syrian authorities. He said that, according to Russian understanding, the Syrian government was not invited to the Friends of Syria meeting, and before the meeting on the 24th, some Western and Arab countries will hold a small-scale meeting to "set the tone." Russia considers such practices unacceptable. He also demanded that external forces stop providing weapons to the Syrian opposition.

Qatar's Al Bayan newspaper reported on the 23rd that the Friends of Syria meeting aims to awaken the Arab initiative and find a political solution to the Syrian issue. However, Jordan's Al-Dustour newspaper's editorial article on the 23rd emphasized that the Friends of Syria meeting is unlikely to achieve concrete results due to severe contradictions and differences among various parties, especially since neither Russia nor China will attend.

The Middle East Online website believes that the Friends of Syria meeting will "set a certain tone" for the next steps in dealing with the Syrian issue. Since more than 70 countries and international organizations are participating, mostly Western and Arab countries, discussions on supporting the Syrian opposition, or even whether to arm the opposition, will be an important agenda item. Some media analysis suggests that this meeting will "sound the rallying call" for military intervention in the Syrian issue, although it may seem exaggerated, it is not completely baseless.

Our reporter in Beijing on February 23rd: Spokesman Hong Lei of the Foreign Ministry pointed out during a press conference on the 23rd that China needs to further understand and study the objectives, role, and mechanism of the Friends of Syria meeting to be held in Tunisia on the 24th. Furthermore, China does not know about the preparation status of the meeting, making it difficult to attend this time.

Hong Lei said that China is a friend of both Syria and the Arab people, and has long supported the just causes of the Arab people. China supports all efforts conducive to peacefully and appropriately resolving the Syrian issue, and wishes to play a positive and constructive role with relevant parties. At the same time, China believes that the actions of the international community regarding the Syrian issue should help ease tensions in Syria, promote political dialogue, resolve differences, and maintain peace and stability in the Middle East region.
