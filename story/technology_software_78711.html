9 Signs Your Computer Might Be Infected! Beware!

by mylovehisa on 2008-07-21 17:16:44

Do cybersecurity with the least effort: The best way is to prevent trouble before it happens! When a computer is reinstalled or restored via Ghost, it must be clean. So how did it get infected later? Today, I will recreate the scenario for everyone: Assume there is a novice user, an amateur actor named Xiao A, and assume I am the director. Whenever Xiao A's daily operations lead to infection, I will shout "CUT" and then explain the reasons and lessons.

### Chapter 1: Reinstalling the System

Recreation: Xiao A was tormented by viruses and had no choice but to reinstall the system again. Xiao A thought, "Let me download QQ first." While downloading, CUT.

Reason: After reinstalling the system, there are many vulnerabilities. When you download QQ, various viruses and hackers on the network have already breached the system's weak defenses.

Lesson: Remember, after installing the system, the first thing to do is to download and install Kingsoft Clear Expert to patch system vulnerabilities. Some friends may ask, if downloading QQ can infect the system, won't downloading Kingsoft Clear Expert also cause infection? The answer is yes, but Kingsoft Clear Expert is smaller, so the probability of infection is much lower. Of course, if possible, it's best to back up the patches made by Kingsoft Clear Expert, unplug the network cable (or better yet, don't plug it in at all) immediately after installing the system, and apply the patches first.

### Chapter 2: Downloading Software

Recreation: Xiao A finally patched the system, opened Baidu, and wanted to download some commonly used software. He saw a website that seemed reliable, clicked on it, CUT.

Reason: Nowadays, many rogue download sites don't even provide any good software downloads. They just use SEO to get a relatively high ranking on Baidu. When users click "download," they're actually downloading malware! Additionally, some legitimate download sites sell ad spaces to malware distributors, and these ads look like "download buttons." It's easy to accidentally click on them due to carelessness. I highly recommend this website: http://www.setuppc.cn, which is simple and easy to use. I strongly suggest writing it down (to be honest, I always thought my computer habits were good enough that I wouldn't get infected, so I didn't use antivirus or firewalls. Yesterday, I downloaded a virus-infected file from Huajun).
