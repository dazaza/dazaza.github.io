Submitting your site (Register Web Page) Submitting a place to any search engine is fairly simple. Find the "Add Your Site" or "Register URL" link on the home page of the search engine. Just follow the instructions to add your website. Some search engines will let you submit all pages that you want indexed, such as Altavista. Some require you to submit the URL of just one page of your entire site; all the remaining pages will be searched. This means that the search engine will automatically add the remaining pages. Google is an example of this. Submitting to directories like Yahoo! can be somewhat tedious. Because they keep pages in separate categories (directories), you need to find the best category under which your site will fit. Remember, these are handled by humans. If you submit under irrelevant categories, it may be considered spam and your site might be ignored. Getting Indexed Now you know that submitting your site is not a big deal, but how sure are you that your site will be indexed? Indexing simply means getting a place for your site in the database of the search engine. How do you find out if your site has been added to a search engine? It's easy. If you search with your URL or domain name as the keyword and the search results show it, then you are indexed by the search engine. Each search engine has its own policy (secret!) for accepting submitted URLs. Some search engines will take less time than others to list you. Be patient. Directories take more time because they require human intervention. It is a good practice to check after 30 or 40 days whether the site has been listed here.
