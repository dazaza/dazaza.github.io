Synopsis:

Main Cast: Riori Yamashita / Masaki Okada / Hanakusa Kimino / Tetsushi Tanaka

This is a world where magicians live. Everything except magic is just like our daily life. Magicians need to be recognized by the state and once the official procedures are completed, they can obtain the qualification to use magic.

Sixteen-year-old Suzuki Aoi (played by Riori Yamashita) dreams of becoming a magician to bring joy and hope to people. She leaves her hometown in Hokkaido and comes to Tokyo to enter the "Magician Training Program". Here, she meets friends who share the same dream and senior magicians, as well as Gota (played by Masaki Okada), who always fails to properly cast spells. Gota originally only wanted to become a professional surfer but was led onto the path of a magician by fate.

After meeting Aoi, Gota gradually changes. In the instant when Gota successfully casts his first spell, he falls in love with Aoi. However, their destiny is tragic...

Highlights:

Whether in comics, novels, or animation fields, "The Important Thing for Magicians" has been extremely popular. In this real-life version of the film's world, magicians are quite common and ordinary. As long as the official procedures are followed, anyone can become a magician with magical powers. The protagonist Aoi travels from Hokkaido to Tokyo to undergo training to become an official magician. The film depicts various stories between Aoi, her partners, and seniors as she strives to obtain the official qualification as a magician.

Director Shun Nakahara has received honors such as the "Yokohama Film Festival New Director Award" and the "Japanese Cultural Affairs Bureau Excellent Film Award." He started his career in the 1980s with pink films and created several works that were highly regarded by public opinion in the early 1990s, such as "Sakura no Niwa." In the new millennium, he produced the classic erotic film "Falling into Ruin." This time, Shun Nakahara's adaptation of "The Important Thing for Magicians," with its dreamlike tale of youthful romance, mesmerizes audiences.

Riori Yamashita, who plays the female lead, was the 12th generation heroine in the "Rehouse" promotional video by Mitsubishi Estate and had acted in episodic dramas like "Love on Sundays" and the movie adaptation of Osamu Tezuka's comic "MW." This film marks her first attempt at starring in a live-action movie. Despite lacking acting experience, Riori Yamashita happily expressed: "At first, I didn't understand anything and caused a lot of trouble, but gradually I adapted, and the filming process was very enjoyable." Regarding the male lead Masaki Okada, she admiringly said: "He often talks to me, like a gentle older brother."

Masaki Okada has recently gained immense popularity and was hailed last year as the "Most Promising Handsome Man of 2007" by the media. His recent Japanese drama "The Classroom of the Sun and the Sea" has also been well-received, and two new films will be released in 2009. Regarding his role in this film, he stated: "After finishing this movie, I also fell in love with fantasy things; it was really great." Additionally, regarding the challenging love scenes with Riori Yamashita, he laughed and said: "I worked hard. Since Riori seemed very nervous, I thought I couldn't be nervous, but in the end, I was incredibly nervous." Director Shun Nakahara also mentioned: "I tried my best to shoot with the most natural feeling. Their freshness and innocence are the most important elements of this work."

Besides the handsome Masaki Okada and beautiful Riori Yamashita, the film also features a star-studded cast including Tetsushi Tanaka, Hanakusa Kimino, Tomoe Midori, Taiga, Hiromi Nagazawa, and many other well-known artists. This luxurious lineup makes the audience eagerly anticipate this fantasy-infused movie.

Other Information:

The original work of "The Important Thing for Magicians" is by Norie Yamada, with the manga drawn by Hisanori Yoshifuji. It has also been adapted into an animated series. The story is set against the backdrop of the real world, with the addition of characters who can use magic. The supervision and use of magic are strictly regulated in the story, requiring one to pass formal exams to gain the qualification to use magic, managed by the "Magic Bureau."

Actually, even before the production of the animated version of "The Important Thing for Magicians," Norie Yamada approached film companies with her own movie screenplay, exploring the possibility of shooting a live-action film. However, at the time, the distributors believed that the work had more potential to be made into a TV animation, so these movie screenplays were used as blueprints for the animated version, which aired from January to March 2003 (as a side note, Kyoko Miyazaki voiced the character Yume Kikuchi). After the broadcast of the animation, over the past five years, "The Important Thing for Magicians" has maintained its charm, with many loyal supporters remembering the status of this work. The airing of "The Important Thing for Magicians: Summer Sky" TVA in July this year excited Yume Kikuchi's fans even more. Now, the release of the live-action movie of "The Important Thing for Magicians" will further expand the influence of Norie Yamada's works.
