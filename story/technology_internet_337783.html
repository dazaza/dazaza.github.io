The Principle of Cycloidal Pinwheel Transmission and Parameters of Cycloidal Pinwheel Reducer

Transmission Principle

(1) Rotary Arm: The rotary arm is composed of the input shaft and an eccentric sleeve. The two eccentrics on the eccentric sleeve are 180 degrees apart. To reduce friction losses, two roller bearings are installed between the eccentric sleeve and the cycloidal wheel.

(2) Cycloidal Wheel: The tooth profile of the cycloidal wheel is a short amplitude external cycloidal equidistant curve. According to motion requirements, only one cycloidal wheel is needed for motion transmission. However, in order to achieve static balance on the input shaft and enhance the load-carrying capacity of the transmission, two identical odd-toothed cycloidal wheels are used and mounted on the eccentric sleeve. The two cycloidal wheels are positioned 180 degrees apart.

(3) Pin Wheel: The pin wheel is composed of a pin gear housing and an even number of pin gear pins installed in the equally divided holes of the pin gear housing. A pin gear sleeve can be installed on the pin gear pins to reduce meshing friction losses.

(4) W Mechanism: The mechanism formed by the output shaft, pin axis, and cycloidal wheel is typically referred to as the W mechanism. The connection between the cycloidal wheel and the output shaft is realized through the pin axis. One end of the pin axis is fixed on the flange of the output shaft, while the other end is inserted into the equally divided holes of the two cycloidal wheels. To reduce friction losses, a pin bushing is installed on the cantilever of the pin axis.

Introduction to the Series of Cycloidal Pinwheel Reducers:

All the transmission devices of the cycloidal pinwheel reducer can be divided into three parts: input part, reduction part, and output part. An eccentric sleeve with a 180° offset is installed on the input shaft. Two roller bearings called rotary arms are installed on the eccentric sleeve, forming an H mechanism. The center holes of the two cycloidal wheels serve as the raceways for the rotary arm bearings on the eccentric sleeve, and they mesh with a set of circularly arranged pin teeth on the needle gear to form an internal meshing reduction mechanism with a tooth difference of one tooth. (In reducers with small speed ratios, the pin teeth are equipped with pin tooth sleeves to reduce friction.)

When the input shaft rotates one revolution with the eccentric sleeve, due to the characteristics of the tooth profile curve of the cycloidal wheel and its restriction by the pin teeth on the needle gear, the movement of the cycloidal wheel becomes a planar motion that includes both orbital and self-rotation. When the input shaft completes one forward rotation, the eccentric sleeve also rotates one revolution, and the cycloidal wheel rotates one tooth in the opposite direction, thereby achieving deceleration. With the help of the W output mechanism, the low-speed self-rotation motion of the cycloidal wheel is transmitted to the output shaft via the pin axis, thus obtaining a lower output speed.
