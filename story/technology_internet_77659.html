What is SEO?

by taosky998 on 2008-07-14 09:37:12

SEO stands for Search Engine Optimization. In English, it can be described as using techniques to place your website at the top positions in a search engine when someone uses the search engine to find something. In Chinese, this is called "search engine optimization," which can also be simply referred to as search optimization. Related search knowledge includes Search Engine Positioning (搜索引擎定位) and Search Engine Ranking (搜索引擎排名).

The main work of SEO involves understanding how various search engines crawl internet pages, index them, and determine the rankings for specific keywords, thereby optimizing web pages accordingly. This increases their search engine ranking, boosting website traffic and ultimately enhancing the site's sales or promotional capabilities.

Search engine optimization is a technique that follows the scientific and comprehensive theoretical mechanisms of search engines. It involves reasonably planning and deploying website structures, webpage text languages, and inter-site diplomatic strategies to maximize a website’s potential, providing strong natural ranking competitive advantages in search engines, thus promoting online sales and strengthening online brand presence for enterprises.

Simply put, SEO is a form of online marketing that allows a website to achieve good rankings on search engines like Baidu, Google, and Yahoo, thereby attracting more potential customers. It is also a type of SEM (Search Engine Marketing).

### International Development of SEO

In foreign countries, SEO started earlier. Those who specialize in SEO are called "Search Engine Optimizers" by Google, abbreviated as SEOs. Since Google is currently the world's largest search engine provider, it has become the primary research subject for global SEOs. For this reason, Google's official website has a dedicated page introducing SEO and stating its stance on SEO.

SEO can be divided into off-page SEO and on-page SEO, which we will discuss separately.

#### Off-Page SEO

Off-page SEO, also known as external SEO, refers to the influence of external sites on a website's search engine ranking. These external factors are beyond the control of the website. The most useful and powerful external factor is backlinks, or what we call external links. Undoubtedly, external links play an important role in getting a site indexed in search engine results pages.

So, how do you generate high-quality backlinks?

1. **High-Quality Content**

The best way to produce high-quality external links is to write high-quality content that makes readers want to read your site. You can exchange links with other websites, register for automatic link generation programs, or buy links from other sites.

2. **Email Relevant Websites**

I don't advocate sending mass emails to other websites for link exchanges, but I suggest that if you've written a quality article on a certain topic and believe it would interest other websites, sending a short, polite email to inform them about your article can be valuable. Even if they don't link back, there's no need to feel awkward. You'll find that if they click, it generates direct traffic, improving your site's score in search engines.

3. **Directory Submissions**

Another method to generate backlinks is to submit your URL to directories. Many webmasters are confident in the effectiveness of this method. When starting a new site, their first step is often working around directory submissions, choosing appropriate keywords and submitting them to relevant pages for linking. #### On-Page SEO

**Enrich Website Keywords**

Adding new keywords to your articles helps search engine "spiders" index them, improving website quality. However, avoid overloading keywords; instead, consider "What keywords might people use in search engines to find this article?"

These keywords should be frequently mentioned in your article. Follow these methods:

- Keywords should appear in the webpage title tag.

- Include keywords in the URL, such as directory names and filenames.

- Incorporate keywords in the anchor text of outbound links.

- Highlight keywords in bold (at least try it once).

- Mention keywords in tags (there is debate about using head tags, but consensus is that h1 tags have better effects than h2, h3, h4, though some pages without head tags still have high PR values).

- Place keywords in image ALT tags.

- Include keywords throughout the article, preferably in the first sentence of the first paragraph.

- Insert keywords in meta tags.

- Suggested keyword density is between 5-20%.

**Theme Websites**

If your website focuses on a single theme, it may gain better rankings. For example, a themed website will rank higher than those covering multiple themes. Building a site with over 200 pages, all on the same theme, will continuously improve rankings because your site is considered authoritative in that theme.

**Site Design**

Search engines prefer friendly web structures, error-free code, and clear navigation. Ensure all your pages are valid and visible in mainstream browsers. Search engines dislike too much Flash, iFrames, and JavaScript scripts, so keeping your site clean and tidy facilitates faster and more accurate crawling by search engine "spiders."

**Internal Linking**

Search engines work by "spider" programs that crawl web pages, track content, and follow links to find pages and extract hyperlink addresses. Many SEO experts recommend providing a sitemap, with one to two deep links between each page on your site. The first step for a website is to ensure that the navigation includes directory pages, and ensure each subpage has links back to the main page and other important pages.

**Regular Updates**

The more frequently a site updates, the more frequently search engine spiders crawl it. This means new articles can appear in indexes within days or even hours, rather than weeks. This is the best way for a site to benefit.

**Outbound Links**

Outbound links can improve a site's ranking in search engines. Linking to other related sites in articles is useful for readers, supported by anecdotal evidence. Too many outbound links can negatively affect your site, so moderation is key.

**Wise Domain Selection**

Choosing a domain requires extensive knowledge, with an important point being selecting a domain that includes keywords. Additionally, check if the domain was previously registered. If high-quality sites had done reverse linking to it, you benefit; however, if the reverse links were from low-quality sites, your site might face unfriendly treatment from search engines for some time.

**Article Theme**

The more focused a page's theme, the better its search engine ranking. Sometimes, you might find long articles covering different topics with low relevance, resulting in poor rankings. If you care about search engine rankings, it's best to split such articles into several more closely themed ones.

**Write Suitable Length Articles**

Short articles cannot achieve high rankings; generally, each article should have at least 300 words. On the other hand, overly long articles are detrimental to maintaining keyword density and appear less compact. Studies show that excessively long articles drastically reduce the number of readers, who choose to close the article upon first glance.

**Avoid Duplicate Content**

Search engines severely warn against duplicate content across multiple pages, regardless of whether these pages belong to you or others. A series of spam sites constantly replicate web content (and steal content from others). There is debate about what constitutes duplicate content, but it depends on whether the content is useful for selection.

**Number of Directories**

When I have too many directories, I get into trouble myself. I think larger sites have higher rankings than smaller ones, though some small sites also have high rankings, which isn't a standard. The more directories, the more comprehensive the search engine searches. If you have too many pages, you need to organize them to facilitate search engine crawling.

**Submit to Search Engines**

If you've done everything for on-page SEO but your site hasn't appeared in search engines, it's because the search engines haven't started indexing yet. Each search engine allows users to submit unindexed sites, usually requiring a wait of 3-5 days.

SEO relies on technical expertise and experience accumulated through long-term observation, utilizing search engine rules to optimize the overall structure, layout, keyword distribution, and density of a website, making it friendly to search engine crawlers, thereby achieving improved rankings. Since search engine ranking rules and algorithms are constantly changing and are commercial secrets, it's impossible to guarantee a specific ranking position. Currently, many people claim under the guise of SEO that they can rank your site in specific positions on Baidu or Google, using cheating methods to temporarily boost rankings. However, search engines can identify these methods and impose penalties. Severely penalized sites may completely disappear from major search engines and not be re-indexed. Therefore, please carefully discern and avoid short-term gains that could make your site invisible forever.

**SEO = More Page Indexing + Friendly Text Arrangement + Conversion Rate**

SEO is divided into White Hat SEO and Black Hat SEO. White Hat SEO refers to legitimate and reasonable operations, while Black Hat SEO involves cheating methods. Below are some common cheating methods that you should avoid:

**Bridge Pages (Doorway Pages)**

Typically, software automatically generates numerous keyword-rich pages, then redirects from these pages to the homepage. The purpose is to get these bridge pages, targeting different keywords, to rank well in search engines. When users click on search results, they are automatically redirected to the homepage. Sometimes, a link to the homepage is placed on the bridge page without automatic redirection. In most cases, these bridge pages are software-generated. You can imagine the generated text is disorganized and lacks logic. If the text were written by humans and truly contained keywords, it wouldn't be a bridge page.

**Keyword Stuffing**

Excessively stuffing keywords into web pages to increase keyword density and enhance the relevance of the page to targeted keywords. Keyword stuffing can occur in many places, such as visible text, Title Tags, keyword tags, and description tags. As search engine algorithms improve, keyword density is no longer a significant factor.

**Hidden Text**

Hidden text involves placing keyword-rich text in the HTML file of a webpage, but the text is invisible to users and only visible to search engines. Forms include extremely small font sizes, text matching the background color, text in comment tags, text in table input tags, and text placed in invisible layers via style sheets. The goal is to increase the relevance of the webpage. Sometimes, irrelevant but popular keywords are placed in these areas to improve rankings and traffic.

**Hidden Links**

Similar to hidden text, but instead, keywords are placed in links that users cannot see.

**Cloaked Pages**

Some webpages use programs or scripts to detect whether the visitor is a search engine or a regular user. If it's a search engine, the page returns an optimized version. If it's a regular visitor, another version is returned. This cheating method is typically undetectable by users because once your browser views the page, whether on the page itself or in the HTML source file, you receive a version different from what the search engine sees. The detection method involves viewing the webpage snapshot.

**Tiny Text**

Many SEO professionals understand that hidden text might lead to penalties, so they expose the hidden text in tiny fonts. Tiny text involves writing sentences with keywords in very small fonts in inconspicuous parts of the webpage. Usually, this text is placed at the top or bottom of the webpage. Although the text color is not the same as the background, it often appears in a very similar color.

In the eyes of search engines, declarative texts like "copyright" are typically displayed in mini-fonts. These tiny texts are often ignored by browsers but may raise suspicion due to misuse.

**Black Hat SEO Deception**

Deception refers to cloaking in Chinese, meaning disguising webpages to display different versions depending on whether the visitor is a regular user or a search engine spider. This black hat SEO method is a typical deception of search engines. The search engine sees a heavily optimized piece of content, while the browser sees ordinary content.

**Webpage Hijacking**

Webpage hijacking, also known as Page jacking, involves copying another website's content or the entire site and passing it off as one's own. This black hat SEO method attracts website owners with scarce content. However, this practice is highly risky and frowned upon. Search engine patent technologies can judge from multiple factors that the copied webpage or site is not original and refuse to index it.

**Buying Links**

While buying ads on other websites is normal, if the purchased ad links are purely for increasing page ranks or manipulating search engine rankings, it is considered cheating. There is a vague line between normal advertising and cheating. However, those involved know clearly. If your site is deemed to be cheating through link buying, there's no arguing with search engines since their decision is final. More seriously, if your site is selling links, the trouble will be even greater.

**Paid SEO Results**

Pay-per-call advertisements: Only charge when a client calls. This effectively resolves the relationship between SEO and results. However, this method is hard to implement in reality.

**What Does SEO Require?**

Understanding what SEO is might spark your interest in SEO. You might ask what you need to master to learn SEO. In fact, SEO isn't as mystical as you think. Simply put, you need to adjust your webpage to communicate well with search engines, allowing them to appropriately recognize your webpage. If search engines can't find your site, there's no question of ranking. Since every search engine finds your site through certain methods, your webpage must follow certain rules, otherwise, it's like the Chinese saying "strangers remain strangers." The guidelines provided by search engines to webmasters serve as excellent website construction guides.

Of course, just understanding this isn't enough. You need to master the following points:

1. **Understand How Search Engines Crawl and Index Web Pages**

You need to know some basic principles of search engine operations, differences between various search engines, how search robots (SE robots or web crawlers) work, and how search engines sort search results.

2. **Meta Tag Optimization**

Includes mainly the theme (Title), website description (Description), and keywords (Keywords). Some other hidden texts include Author (author), Category (directory), Language (encoding language), etc.

3. **How to Select Keywords and Place Them in Web Pages**

Searching requires the use of keywords. Keyword analysis and selection are among the most important tasks in SEO. First, determine the main keywords for your site (usually around 5), then optimize for these keywords, including keyword density (Density), relevance (Relavancy), prominence (Prominency), etc.

4. **Understand Major Search Engines**

Although there are many search engines, only a few significantly impact website traffic. For example, in English, the main ones are Google, Inktomi, Altavista, etc.; in Chinese, Baidu and Sohu, etc. Different search engines have different rules for page crawling and indexing, sorting. Also, understand the relationships between search portals and search engines, such as Yahoo and AOL using Google's search technology, MSN using Looksmart and Open Directory technology.

5. **Major Internet Directories**

Yahoo itself is not a search engine but a large website directory. Open Directory is also not a search engine. Their main difference from search engines lies in the method of collecting website content. Directories are manually edited, primarily including homepages; search engines automatically collect content, including both homepages and a large number of content pages.

6. **Pay-per-click Search Engines**

Search engines also need to survive. As internet commerce matures, paid search engines have become prevalent. Typical examples include Overture and Baidu, including Google's advertising project Google Adwords. Increasingly, people use search engine click advertisements to position commercial websites, where optimization and ranking science come into play. You need to learn how to get the most clicks with the least advertising investment.

7. **Search Engine Submission**

After building your website, don't just sit there waiting for visitors to fall from the sky. To let others find you, the simplest method is to submit (submit) your website to search engines. If it's a commercial website, major search engines and directories will require payment for inclusion (for example, Yahoo charges $299), but the good news is (at least up to now) the largest search engine Google is still free and dominates over 60% of the search market.
