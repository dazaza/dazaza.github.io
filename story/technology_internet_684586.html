17-Year-Old High School Student Robs Taxi Driver to Buy Girlfriend a Present

by uelu6c6b on 2012-03-01 09:02:07

**Location: Shenyang**

"A 17-year-old high school student wielding a knife robbed a taxi driver. With the help of his fellow drivers and bystanders, Master Liu managed to subdue the assailant."

The chilling words, "Give me the money, or I'll stab you!" came from a 17-year-old high school sophomore. Just to buy gifts for his girlfriend, this second-year high school student committed an act that was both hateful and heartbreaking.

At 1:10 PM yesterday afternoon, taxi driver Master Liu was driving around looking for fares when a handsome young man got into his car near the Wanda Plaza in Hunnan District of Shenyang. "Driver, take me to Meidi Manor near Taoxian Airport," the young man said.

During the ride, the young man remained mostly silent, barely speaking a word, with an unfocused gaze. This made Master Liu uneasy. He remembered hearing about a taxi driver being robbed in the area near "Meidi Manor." "Could it be possible that this fashionable and good-looking young man is the one who will rob me?" Master Liu wondered.

Soon, they arrived at the destination. Before Master Liu could even ask for payment, the young man spoke first. "Give me the money, or I'll stab you!" A shiny blade suddenly appeared in front of him. "Brother, all you want is money, right? Just don't hurt me," Master Liu responded calmly, having prepared himself mentally. He employed a delaying tactic, pretending to reach for his wallet. When he noticed the young man relaxing slightly, Master Liu quickly grabbed the knife from his hand. In the ensuing struggle, the knife fell inside the car. Master Liu promptly pushed open the car door and shouted out the window, "Someone's robbing me! Call the police!"

At this point, the young man panicked. He broke free from Master Liu, jumped out of the car, and attempted to run across the road. However, a guardrail blocked his way, forcing him to turn back. In the process, he scraped against Master Liu’s car, fell to the ground, and started bleeding from the head. Master Liu immediately pinned him down.

Bystanders quickly dialed the emergency number, and nearby taxi drivers, alerted by their radios, rushed to the scene to support Master Liu. About ten taxi drivers arrived to assist. The police also reached the scene within seven minutes of receiving the call. It was only then that Master Liu realized he had injured his left thumb during the struggle, with blood streaming from the wound.

Upon investigation, the police found that the young man was indeed 17 years old, a sophomore in high school, and already a repeat offender, having previously robbed taxi drivers. When questioned about his motive, he admitted, "I did it to buy gifts for my girlfriend."

Master Liu said that when he realized he had subdued the criminal, he felt a sense of relief. At the time of the incident, he wasn't afraid. "He must not harm anyone else; we have to stop him!" When he learned that the "criminal" was only 17 years old, he felt deeply sorry. "I hope this young man can repent and never do such things again," Master Liu said.

**Reported by journalist Zhu Bailin**
