Bring your animations to life with color and sound.

by lk0pcdd on 2009-07-22 16:52:28

3DS is a professional-grade 3D animation production software. The current version of 3DS MAX has greatly improved in terms of performance, operation, and software interface compared to previous versions. During the process of creating animations with 3DS MAX, I added background music and off-screen narration to the animation, which enhanced its appeal significantly. If your computer has a sound card installed, you can add any .wav file for playback. Even without a sound card, you can use the Metronome to play rhythmic background sounds through the PC speaker, ensuring that the animation has sound in any setting. Below, I will explain the methods to implement these two types of sound:

1. Using the Metronome

(1) In the main menu's EDIT option, select the Track View item to display the trajectory window.

(2) Click the "＋" sign next to the Sound item in the left window, opening the Sound trajectory window represented by black dots forming a Metronome trajectory.

(3) Move the cursor to the top of the scrollbar on the right side of the Sound trajectory window; the cursor will turn into a vertical double arrow. Drag the mouse downward, dividing the trajectory window into two parts. These two windows are independent of each other, and operations on one will not affect the other.

(4) Click the Object item in the left window and select the name of the object in the animation that requires dubbing, displaying the object’s “Position” and “Scale.”

(5) Thus, in the two windows divided earlier, one shows the sound trajectory while the other shows the object trajectory.

(6) Right-click on the sound trajectory window, choose Properties from the popup menu, and a “Sound Option” dialog box will appear. The “Beats Per Minute” represents the number of beats per minute; adjusting this parameter will change the spacing of the black dots in the trajectory window. “Beats Per Measure” refers to the frequency of each beat. After setting these two parameters, check the “Active” option on the right and click OK to activate the metronome.

(7) Click the play animation button at the bottom of the screen, and you will hear rhythmic sounds accompanying the animation coming from the computer's speaker.
