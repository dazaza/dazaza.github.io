Guo Shuang Wins Another Gold for Chinese Team in Beijing Station of Track Cycling World Cup - Sohu Sports

by b4d332z4 on 2012-02-11 11:05:45

In the final, Guo Shuang fully exploited her strong point of powerful sprinting ability. In the two rounds of competition, she made efforts in the sprint phase of the last dozens of meters to surpass opponents and touch the line first. She ultimately defeated Simona Krupeckaite from Lithuania 2:0 and stood on the champion podium. Lin Junhong of China Jiante Team won the bronze medal as she defeated Lee Wai Sze from Hong Kong, China 2:0 in the third place battle. Women's sprint event has always been Guo Shuang's strong item. However, due to injury, she failed to participate in last year's Beijing leg. This year, Guo Shuang returned strongly. She proved herself again with two gold medals as the leading figure in China's track cycling events. After the match, Guo Shuang expressed that she was very satisfied with her performance in this competition. She said, "Next, I will go to London for the next station. I will do my best in each game and adjust my condition to the best before the (London) Olympic Games." In the other three items held on the same day, rider P. Lasem from New Zealand won the men's individual pursuit championship; G. O'Shea from Australia won the men's all-around championship; and French rider F. Peris won the men's Keirin championship. The International Cycling Union Track Cycling World Cup Series started in 2003. There are four legs of competition in the 2011/2012 season. Beijing leg is the third one. The last leg will be held in London, UK at the end of February. This event attracted more than 500 riders from 44 countries and regions since it started on January 13th and will conclude on January 15th.
