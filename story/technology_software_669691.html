I. Overview of the Solution for Network Video Surveillance in Chain Stores

With the continuous development of networks, people's increasing demand for security, and the growing market needs for surveillance safety, traditional monitoring issues have gradually led to network video surveillance becoming more prevalent in our lives. Network video surveillance refers to equipment that can transmit images in real-time through a network to a centralized monitoring center. It is convenient to install, has remote monitoring capabilities, and is not restricted by region or country. Its application fields are expanding continuously, such as coal mine video surveillance, chain store surveillance system management, commercial-residential building surveillance, baby online monitoring, scenic spot display and surveillance.

Firstly, what is a network camera? A web camera (or IP camera) is a new generation of cameras that combines traditional video surveillance cameras with current network technology, allowing video images to be transmitted via the internet to any place on Earth. Viewers do not need to install or use specialized software; they only need standard network browsers (such as "IE", "Firefox") to watch and monitor the video images. In short, network cameras are indispensable members of future security systems. Their products have matured and are widely used across various industries, including coal mine monitoring, chain store surveillance systems, commercial-residential building video surveillance, baby care online, and scenic spot display and surveillance.

A brief comparison between traditional video surveillance and network video surveillance:

Traditional surveillance generally consists of DVR + analog cameras, transmitting through coaxial cables using analog video signals to meet the needs of local area monitoring. It is simple to install, easy to implement, relatively mature and complete hardware, widely used in the market, transmits analog video signals, provides clear and smooth images, and has a low cost. However, its disadvantages include being suitable only for local areas and small-scale monitoring needs, where video signals can easily attenuate and cannot be transmitted over long distances. As people's demands for surveillance increase and broadband networks continue to improve, network surveillance has gradually come into view. Network surveillance fully utilizes network resources based on traditional analog video surveillance, connecting surveillance devices across China and even the world. Through computers, it achieves the purpose of centralized monitoring in one area with high video compression formats and unlimited transmission distance. Currently, it is applied in various regions and fields, including the setup of some chain store surveillance systems and baby care.

II. Implementation Plan for Network Video Surveillance in Apparel Chain Store Monitoring Systems

The chain store monitoring system mainly focuses on three aspects inside the chain store: 1. Cashier counter 2. Shelves 3. Passageways.

Company managers only need to sit in the office, connect to the network via computer or laptop, and access the monitoring footage of each chain store. Through video surveillance images, they can see the personnel activities, sales conditions, and customer flow within each store.

a. Supervision situation: Not only can monitoring occur under supervision with recording, but also when no one is supervising, it can monitor the store and internal conditions of the chain store. When no one is monitoring, motion detection can be activated to detect moving objects, which can compensate for security loopholes in chain stores and save on labor costs. For cashier counters, real-time supervision can be conducted, managing friction-prone areas such as long customer wait times, unpaid items, or cash discrepancies effectively.

b. Employee and chain store management: Employees' work attitudes directly affect the company's business conditions. Upper-level managers can monitor many branch stores simultaneously through network surveillance and evaluate employees remotely.

c. Shop personnel flow supervision: During peak hours, detailed image functions can record detailed information, and the system's recording function can document footage, providing data for relevant departments to analyze.

III. Equipment Recommendations for Chain Store Monitoring Systems

Chain store monitoring systems primarily focus on three areas inside the chain store: 1. Cashier counter 2. Shelves 3. Passageways.

When selecting equipment for chain store monitoring systems, it is necessary to consider the stability, security, practicality, brand recognition, and market influence of the equipment. Therefore, from the functional needs and product selection, choices can be made selectively for the cashier counter, shelves, passageways, and remote management software.

For ordinary shops, generally between 50 to 100 square meters, they are divided into three main parts: cashier counter, shelves, and passageways.

a. For the cashier counter, high-definition bullet surveillance cameras can be chosen. Considering cost, the 338 network camera can be selected. The 338 network camera can not only replace lenses according to customer needs and adjust angles but is also an integrated network camera, making installation convenient, professional-looking, and cost-effective. It is an ideal choice for small chain store surveillance.

b. In the middle of the store, where the viewing range is wide and the angle requirement is large, two to three dome cameras with pan-tilt functionality like the CM601 would be appropriate. The CM601 offers 360 degrees horizontally and 90 degrees vertically, providing a full view of the store. Clients can adjust angles and rotate views in real time, making the store situation clear at a glance.

c. After selecting the hardware 338 and CM601, to better utilize the equipment, remote monitoring software such as MultiMonitor can be chosen. MultiMonitor can manage up to 16 network cameras' surveillance images simultaneously. By setting parameters, it can perform motion detection and send event emails. Thus, installing this software is essential for the entire company's chain store monitoring.

IV. Analysis of Three Network Implementation Solutions

CM601 uses current network technology to transmit images over the network, providing domain name resolution for dynamic IP users and PPPoE dial-up service functionality for XDSL dial-up internet users.

Considering the two main internet access methods in China: WEB authentication and PPPoE dial-up authentication.

1. Through shared routers and xDSL modems, ID01 transmits images over the network.

This is currently the most common network access method. Wherever there is router sharing, the router's RJ45 port can be directly connected to ID01, allowing internet access similar to a regular PC.

2. CM601 connects to HUB and xDSL.

Here, users need to set up PPPoE dial-up on the CM601, inputting the user name and password provided by the ISP to allow CM601 to dial into the internet.

3. CM601 directly connects to the modem.

Similar to the second option, PPPoE dial-up settings are also required.
