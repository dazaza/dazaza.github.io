Bezos' wife unhappy with new Amazon biography, gives it one star review

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 20:08:43

Amazon CEO Jeff Bezos' wife expressed dissatisfaction with a recently published book detailing the company's developmental history: on the book's product page, she gave it a one-star review. On Monday, MacKenzie Bezos, who is not only Jeff Bezos' wife but also a best-selling author, posted a comment titled "I Had Hoped to Boost This Book." In her comment, she questioned the accuracy of the new book written by Brad Stone, a journalist for Bloomberg Businessweek.

MacKenzie cited an example in the book regarding the timing of when Jeff Bezos read the novel *The Remains of the Day* — which inspired him and led to many new business ideas — as being inaccurate.

MacKenzie also pointed out that two people reviewed this book about Amazon's journey: one current Amazon executive and one former Amazon executive. Both reviewers noted inaccuracies in some details but still rated the book four stars and three stars respectively.

"If this were the only error, it would be fine, but it is not," MacKenzie wrote in her review. "Whenever I check facts based on my own experiences, I find numerous inaccuracies, which make me question the content of each chapter in the book. Like the other two reviewers, Jonathan Leblang and Rick Dalzell, I personally experienced many of the events mentioned in this book."

In addition, she was dissatisfied with another aspect of the book: it portrayed her husband as a goal-driven person, relentless and sometimes even cruel.

Jeff has not yet publicly commented on the book. Stone mentioned during the promotion of the book that although Jeff refused to be interviewed for the book, he agreed to let Stone interview his friends and family.
