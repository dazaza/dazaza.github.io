"Born in the 70s" - A post-70s woman's "reverse pursuit" leading to a fragile marriage

by jqrxass on 2009-08-23 13:04:27

Zhao Lanlan, born in the late 1970s, is an outgoing and cheerful girl. She has a younger brother Zhao Lei, who was born in the 1980s. Their family lives harmoniously and happily. However, at the age of 25, still unmarried, she becomes increasingly annoyed by her mother's persistent arrangements for blind dates. Determined to take charge of her own life, she decides to seek independence and carve out her own path.

At this moment, she unexpectedly encounters Xu Cheng, her senior from university whom she had a crush on. She launches a rather convoluted and comical "reverse pursuit" campaign towards him but faces repeated setbacks. After many twists and turns, Lanlan and Xu Cheng eventually tie the knot, much to everyone's delight.

Soon after their marriage, they welcome their beloved daughter, Xu Yuanyuan, into their family. Life seems to be going smoothly, appearing unshakable. However, an unexpected car accident involving her father completely disrupts the seemingly stable family dynamics. Additionally, Zhang Jin, a new female colleague in Xu Cheng's department, develops subtle feelings for him, quietly setting the stage for an impending upheaval...

After the turmoil subsides, Xu Cheng leaves for a distant place to pursue his ideals while Lanlan temporarily resigns from her job to return to the family.

The monotonous and uneventful life as a housewife begins to unsettle Lanlan. Thus, she plans to re-enter the workforce, simultaneously ensuring that she can still care for her family. Setting rather strict and special requirements for her work, she joins Wanfeng Company with the help of a classmate. There, she meets her unruly and extravagant boss, Chen Dong, leading to intense confrontations...

"Born in the 1970s" explores the stories of several couples from the 70s and 80s navigating love and marriage and gaining insights along the way. The characters may not be perfect and might even seem annoying at times, yet life often offers us only so much, rarely meeting our expectations.

We strive hard for happiness, discovering in the process that attaining happiness may require not just effort but also compromise and tolerance.

"Born in the 1970s" Part One: Maidens' Years (Upper)

Not the first time going on a blind date, 25-year-old Zhao Lanlan could be considered well-experienced in this "industry." Just after her 24th birthday, she was hurriedly pushed by her anxious mother Yun Xian to attend various matchmaking gatherings, reasoning that a daughter of one of her colleagues, the same age as Lanlan, was already a mother.

Rich or poor, officials or commoners... As the number of meetings increased, Zhao Lanlan joked with her close friend Dong Xiaoyun that the men she met could easily fill a painting like "Along the River During the Qingming Festival."

"Born in the 1970s" Part Two: Maidens' Years (Middle)

Before Lanlan could open her mouth, her phone rang. Seeing the name Xu Cheng displayed on the screen, her heart rate accelerated significantly. She silently reminded herself: stay calm, stay calm!

Taking a deep breath, she slowly answered, speaking very solemnly with a "Hello."

Cao Yuxiang, sitting nearby, became curious: whose call was the little girl receiving, acting so pretentious?

"Born in the 1970s" Part Three: Maidens' Years (Lower)

Liu Yansha noticed that ever since Zhao Lanlan returned from Singapore, she seemed like a different person, suddenly becoming quiet and no longer loud and boisterous. Moreover, she was always daydreaming, her thoughts wandering, as if half her soul had been taken away.

"Hey, are you done writing the training summary? You need to present it at the meeting on Friday," Liu Yansha reminded her by tapping on Lanlan's desk.

Lanlan's gaze finally refocused on the computer screen, lazily replying, "I'm writing it right now."

"Born in the 1970s" Part Four: Kitchen and Living Room (Upper)

Xu Cheng felt helpless about her complaints, "Of course, I know it's our second wedding anniversary, and I want to get there quickly too! But I'm stuck here. Sigh, just because I left ten minutes late, it's jammed like this, really! Or should I leave the car here and walk over to find you?"

Lanlan glared angrily, "You dare!" The car, worth over 100,000 yuan, had been driven for less than a year. Scratching it would surely make her upset again!

Even though he couldn't see it, Xu Cheng could vividly imagine Lanlan's flustered and angry expression. Whenever she twisted his arm with unreasonable demands, all he needed to do was mention money, and she would immediately give in.

"Born in the 1970s" Part Five: Kitchen and Living Room (Middle)

Since the birth of Yuan Yuan, her happiness has become extraordinarily simple; Yuan Yuan's health and joy bring her good mood, while Yuan Yuan's illness makes her lose focus on everything.

Driving silently, Xu Cheng had another thought.

He wondered, if he and Lanlan hadn't had a child, or hadn't had a child so soon, would their life have been more blissful?

Lanlan recently often feels restless. Some say that women's sixth sense is the most sensitive, and she can truly feel her sixth sense working subtly, as if warning her of some upcoming issues, but she cannot pinpoint exactly what it is.
