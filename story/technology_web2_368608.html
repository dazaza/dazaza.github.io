What is Push Mail?

image Push Mail. Image push mail. The Push Mail service refers to the mail push service for the Internet mailbox and enterprise mailbox system, that is, the newly arrived mail server is pushed to the user's mobile terminal on time. Customers can receive emails from personal and corporate mailboxes, read, reply, forward and compose emails on mobile terminals.

Push Mail refers to the use of push technology to send email directly to the mobile terminal. Simply put, the system directly sends the mail just received in the email box to the user's mobile phone immediately, unlike the traditional mobile email system, which must rely on the mobile terminal to receive/receive emails on a regular basis or the user actively checks the mailbox, so the client terminal can always maintain synchronous data with the specified mailbox, just like sending and receiving SMS.

Customers can receive emails from personal and corporate mailboxes, read, reply, forward and compose emails on mobile terminals. Based on the user's requirements for email security, enterprise mailboxes. Secure communication can be used. PushMail combines email system and mobile terminal, and realizes mobile email value-added service by using U-IMAP/SMS/WAP and other related technologies through the existing wireless network of mobile operators. Through this service, mobile users can not only use email services through mobile terminals anytime and anywhere, but also when new emails arrive, the system can proactively notify mobile users in time through SMS Push and WAP Push, and trigger the terminal to receive new emails in time through mail transfer protocol (such as U-IMAP protocol). Read and manipulate new messages.

Seven provides Push Mail services for mobile devices on a variety of platforms, primarily for enterprise mobile applications, but also for individual mobile users.

Mobile platforms supported by Seven include: Windows Mobile, Symbian, Android, Palm and more. It offers four versions of its mobile client software, all with support for mainstream email services and POP/IMAP protocols:

* Consumer Edition

Yahoo, Gmail, AOL, Hotmail/MSN or other POP/IMAP email services

* Out of Office™

Corporate email on Microsoft Exchange (Exchange 2000 and 2003), where Outlook Web Access is enabled.

* Workgroup Edition

Corporate email on Microsoft Exchange (Exchange 2000, 2003, and 2007), Lotus Domino (versions 6, 6.5, and 7), or POP servers behind the firewall, for up to 25 users on a single installation.

* Enterprise Edition

Corporate email on Microsoft Exchange (Exchange 2000, 2003, and 2007), Lotus Domino (versions 6, 6.5, and 7), or POP servers behind the firewall, for up to 1500 users per connector installation.
