Chungbuk National University, located in Cheongju, North Chungcheong Province, consists of 12 colleges and 9 graduate schools. There are 18,000 students enrolled, including about 400 international students, of which over 130 are from China. It ranks among the top ten most popular universities in the comprehensive evaluation by the Korean Ministry of Education.

Cheongju, where Chungbuk University is located, is only 1 hour and 30 minutes away from Seoul by car. It is the provincial government seat of North Chungcheong Province, with a population of 570,000, accounting for 50% of the entire province's population. Known as the city of education and culture, especially due to its high proportion of student population, it provides a relatively stable study and living environment for international students.

Korean language instructors hold master's degrees or higher, have received Korean language teaching training, and possess rich teaching experience and effective teaching methods, greatly helping students learn and master the Korean language and Korean culture.

Course offerings at Chungbuk National University include the College of Humanities, College of Social Sciences, College of Natural Sciences, College of Business Administration, College of Engineering, College of Agriculture, College of Law, College of Education, College of Home Economics, College of Veterinary Medicine, College of Pharmacy, College of Medicine, and the College of Information and Communication Engineering, totaling 74 majors. Among these, the school enjoys a high reputation in Korea in the fields of information and communication engineering, life engineering, healthcare, advanced horticulture, agriculture, forestry, and fisheries.

Major Recommendations:

The university stands out in automobile manufacturing, information and communication, health care, life engineering, business management, marketing, agriculture, forestry, and fisheries. In particular, it has been designated as a national key support university. From 1994 to 1998, it received a total of 25 billion KRW in national funding and was designated as a main university under the BK21 program for national talent cultivation in 1999. The electronics and computer science departments received a total of 24.7 billion KRW in national support funds over a seven-year period, demonstrating significant development potential.

Application Information for Chungbuk National University:

Regular courses start in March and September each year. They consist of beginner (Levels 1 and 2) and intermediate (Levels 1 and 2) levels. Each semester lasts 9 weeks (180 hours). Classes are held 5 days a week (Monday to Friday).

Each day includes 4 hours of classes, systematically cultivating reading, writing, speaking, and listening abilities. Additionally, the average class size is controlled between 12 to 15 students.

Besides Korean language classes, there are also sports activities and cultural education courses, enhancing students' understanding of Korean culture and motivation.
