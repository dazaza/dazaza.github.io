Ticket scalpers stockpiling goods disrupt group buying: sell tickets at a higher price after the end of the event

by tuoqing1188 on 2011-08-04 09:33:22

Buying electronic group-buying vouchers online, but when it comes to consumption, it is found that the voucher has been used before. Recently, Ms. Guo, a citizen of Hangzhou, encountered such a strange incident. After investigation by the reporter, this happens because "group-buying scalpers" send the same electronic voucher to multiple consumers. Although the emergence of "group-buying scalpers" has received support from some netizens, industry insiders remind that transactions with them carry significant risks.

Ms. Guo had previously purchased two self-service hotpot group-buying vouchers from the 24quan website on Taobao. On the evening of July 25th, when she went to consume, the merchant informed her that the group-buying voucher had already been used.

"Ah? This is my first time here!" Afterwards, Ms. Guo contacted the seller who was far away in Guangdong. "After reflecting the situation, the seller frankly said 'sent the wrong one' and then sent two new verification codes over. Although the issue was resolved, it took nearly two hours back and forth, ruining the meal." Ms. Guo felt a bit upset.

Ms. Guo stated that she usually pays close attention to group-buying. "Initially, I saw that the seller's online store had group-buying vouchers from the 24quan website that were already off-shelf, and the price was cheaper, so I bought them. I didn't expect such an episode to occur."

Group-buying scalpers hoard goods to earn price differences.

Industry insiders mentioned that since group-buying has a certain time limit, once the group-buying period ends, consumers are unable to make purchases anymore. Therefore, many online store sellers stockpile large quantities during the group-buying period and then resell them at a higher price after the group-buying ends, giving rise to a batch of "group-buying scalpers." Ms. Guo purchased the group-buying vouchers from a "group-buying scalper."

The reporter subsequently contacted multiple Taobao sellers for investigation, confirming this statement. "I pay attention to newly listed group-buyings every day. If there's something suitable, I quickly grab it, stockpile it in my online store, and then sell it after the group-buying period ends. Sales are quite good." An online store seller revealed that stocking goods also requires skills, not everything is stocked. "Those group-buyings from well-known and reputable merchants are the most sought-after, and those with great discounts sell quickly."

Another seller disclosed that many group-buying websites attract consumers by adopting strategies where inviting friends to buy earns money returns. That is, successfully inviting one friend to buy will return money to the user's account, with returns ranging from 5 to 10 yuan. "So, I register multiple accounts, invite each other to buy, and earn returns. This way, even if I sell at a lower price than the group-buying price, there's still profit." However, the reporter noticed that now group-buying websites have become aware of this issue, and many websites have canceled the return money activities.

Unable to verify authenticity, purchase carries risks.

Ms. Guo was lucky, but some buyers, when facing such awkward situations and unable to contact the seller temporarily, can only helplessly swallow the loss. "If the other party doesn't admit it and refuses to send me a new voucher, I really don't know what to do," Ms. Guo said. Although this time she successfully contacted the seller and avoided losses, upon reflection, she was still somewhat frightened.

Industry insiders reminded that although the emergence of "group-buying scalpers" satisfies some consumers' demand for group-buying, it's evident that there are certain risks involved. "The electronic vouchers in the hands of scalpers can be sold simultaneously to multiple buyers, and unlike regular goods, they cannot be immediately verified for authenticity upon receipt. These electronic vouchers only reveal their authenticity during consumption, so the risk is very high. Consumers are advised to purchase cautiously."
