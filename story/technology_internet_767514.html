Reading Alibaba through the financial statements: Not China's Amazon

by anonymous on 2013-10-20 18:32:40

Introduction: A foreign media outlet published an article on Wednesday titled "Alibaba Isn't the Amazon of China," which argues that although referred to as "China's Amazon," Alibaba's business model differs from Amazon, eBay, or any American e-commerce company. In some ways, Alibaba is more similar to Google.

The following is a summary of the article:

Comparing Alibaba’s latest financial report results with those of American Internet giants can provide a more accurate understanding of Alibaba. Calling Alibaba "the Amazon of China" is largely a misconception because Alibaba's business model differs from Amazon, eBay, or any American e-commerce company.

In some respects, Alibaba resembles Google as an advertising platform for many businesses that heavily rely on Alibaba for traffic to their online retail operations.

According to a document provided by Yahoo!, in the second quarter ending June 30, Alibaba reported a net profit of $707 million, nearly double the $273 million from the same period last year. Yahoo! owns 24% of Alibaba.

Alibaba's revenue for the second quarter was $1.74 billion, up 61% from $1.08 billion in the same period last year. Operating profit was $856 million, more than double the $372 million from the same period last year.

Alibaba's revenue is far lower than Amazon's because Alibaba does not sell products itself. Alibaba does not operate its own online store but runs several websites where millions of merchants sell goods directly to individual and business customers.

Amazon's second-quarter sales were $15.7 billion, up 22% from $12.8 billion in the same period last year, with a net loss of $7 million. Operating profit was $79 million, resulting in an operating margin of only 0.5%, sharply contrasting with Alibaba's 49%.

In the meantime, eBay's second-quarter revenue was $3.88 billion, up 14% year-over-year. Net profit fell to $640 million from $692 million in the same period last year, a decline of 7.5%.

Like eBay, Alibaba's online shopping site also functions as a marketplace platform where many merchants sell directly to consumers. However, Taobao, Alibaba's largest shopping site, does not charge commission on transactions. Taobao has over 7 million sellers offering more than 800 million items.

Taobao generates revenue through advertisements placed by merchants on the site. Many of these ads are linked to Taobao's own search engine, so when consumers enter keywords to search for products, the page displays both search results and product ads related to the keywords. Founded in 2003, Taobao has experienced explosive growth over the past decade, with revenues increasing rapidly as merchants increase their advertising spending.

In contrast, American consumers typically search for products on Google, which differs from Chinese consumers who search directly on Taobao rather than Baidu. For millions of Chinese entrepreneurs hoping to start an online retail business, Taobao is the best platform, attracting consumers while keeping startup costs low.

It should be noted that based on feedback from Taobao sellers, while merchants can open stores on Taobao for free, they will not achieve high sales without spending money on advertisements.

Besides Taobao, Alibaba also operates another shopping site called Tmall. Tmall mainly serves large enterprises and brands such as Nike and Gap, where sellers need to pay a deposit and a commission per transaction.

Data provided by Alibaba shows that the total merchandise sales on Taobao and Tmall last year exceeded 1 trillion RMB (approximately $160 billion). According to RetailNet Group, a research firm in Massachusetts, Amazon's sales last year were $86 billion, and eBay's were $67.8 billion.

In the U.S., Alibaba is known for its trading website Alibaba.com, which focuses on providing opportunities for Chinese manufacturers to collaborate with domestic and international merchants. This website was Alibaba's first business when it was founded in 1999, but now it constitutes only a small part of the company's operations. Last year, most of Alibaba's revenue came from Taobao and Tmall. (By Ming Li)
