Dear readers, hello everyone.

by wenfqplove on 2011-09-13 19:25:43

Dear readers, hello everyone. I am a well-educated person from the 80s generation. There is a secret buried deep in my heart that I still cannot decide whether to uncover or not. I don't know how to make this choice and hope that all of you can help this helpless person.

Everyone hopes that the people closest to them are the ones who accompany them as they grow up. However, this simplest desire was shattered for me when I first understood the world around me. The first time I got into a fight as a child was because a neighboring kid said to me: "You were adopted from the riverbank." Later, after my adoptive parents found out, they scolded and hit me. I replied: "Mom, am I your biological child?" My mother's silence deeply hurt my young heart. From then on, I, who used to be lively, became somewhat quiet. Because in the countryside, rumors could be devastating, and with so many people saying it, I gradually learned to accept this identity. I remember that at that time, I hated my birth parents the most, hating why they had the heart to abandon me! And I loved my adoptive parents the most because they provided for me, gave me food and clothing, and sent me to school. Therefore, no matter how hard or tiring the work was, I silently bore it. When other children were playing, I knew how to mop the floor, watch the store, and restock supplies at the age of six or seven. I often rode a bicycle taller than myself to restock goods, struggling to move the heavy items I couldn’t lift, tears rolling down my cheeks frequently. Whenever I felt wronged and during quiet nights, I would often imagine what my birth parents looked like. At that time, imagination was the greatest comfort to my soul! As the years went by, I gradually adapted to life at home. My adoptive parents had no children of their own, and they selflessly gave me all their love. Looking back at my childhood, perhaps they created an environment of independence for me!

As I grew older and gained more experiences, the childhood dreams in my heart became increasingly strong. Reflecting on life suddenly made me break out in cold sweat. Should I just live my life without knowing where I came from and then grow old in ignorance? Even leaves return to their roots, let alone living human beings. Moreover, now I have a family and a very adorable son. How can I let my descendants be like me, unable to find their ancestors! But my biggest concern now is my current parents. I have never mentioned anything about my origins in front of them since I was a child. I am afraid they will be hurt, and I cannot bear to see them sad. If I knew nothing about my origins, it wouldn't matter. But now I clearly know the city and even the hospital where I was born, which makes me extremely conflicted! The mental torment has made it impossible for me to focus on work, and sometimes my day and night get mixed up, becoming very irregular!

I don't know what to do!

(Heart-touching story)
