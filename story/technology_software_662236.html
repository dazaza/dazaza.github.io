Reading comprehension

by phonics6168 on 2012-02-10 02:10:25

In today's schools, so many children have problems learning how to read. Many teachers and parents need to be assured that reading problems can be resolved within a given time frame. Reading comprehension requires mental work that keeps the mind engaged, motivation, concentration, and good study strategies. Comprehension is a main component of learning how to read. Teaching reading comprehension strategies at all levels is complex. Teachers must have a strong understanding of text and methods, and must have good knowledge. The National Reading Panel has conducted research on reading comprehension skills and picked out three major issues concerning the development of reading comprehension skills for investigation. Reading comprehension is a complex cognitive process. It cannot be analyzed without clearly explaining the role of vocabulary development and vocabulary instruction in understanding texts. Moreover, understanding is an active process, therefore requiring thoughtful interaction between the reader and the text. Additionally, teacher preparation aimed at developing reading comprehension strategies connects to student performance. There has been relatively little research on the methods and strategies for developing comprehension. Initially, investigations into this issue should pay special attention to one of the teaching strategies. Later, it was proven that some teaching strategies were effective in research. Nonetheless, it is not easy as it requires teachers to be adept and able to respond to help students when they are reading. Researchers have found ways to successfully teach children how to read. According to the National Reading Panel’s report, results from text analysis indicate that reading instruction needs to incorporate five areas. They are respectively: phonemic awareness, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and text comprehension. Each area determines specific skills, which through research data, introduces certain strategies for teaching reading skills and addresses common problems. The best method for teaching comprehension strategies is systematic, including: direct explanation by the teacher, modeling by the teacher, guided practice led by the teacher, and application with the help of the teacher and students. Research has discovered some effective comprehension strategies. They include:

- Teach children to point out parts of the text that are difficult to understand and then look for solutions while re-reading sections for clues.

- Use graphic organizers - maps, charts, and diagrams - to help students focus on important points the author is trying to convey.

- Answer questions that help students focus on the main points of the text.

- Ask compensatory questions.

- Recognize story structure, such as plot, characters, and other content categories.

- By recognizing story structure, students can more easily analyze the plot, characters, and other content categories of the text and remember the story better. They also need to recognize the function of words in fictional and non-fictional texts.

- Summarizing encourages students to focus their attention on the most important factors in the text. Comprehension is the ability to create meaning in the mind. Nonetheless, not everyone knows that comprehension can be taught. Teachers can improve a child's ability to comprehend texts using specific comprehension strategies. In developing children's reading abilities, reading comprehension is very important. Therefore, it is important for them to receive education in these capabilities.
