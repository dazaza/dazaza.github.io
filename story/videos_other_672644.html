Tips on Customized Wedding Dresses - Get Whatever you Want, Specifically! The best wedding dress— it’s probably the most crucial element for any bride preparing her wedding ceremony, and perhaps the most stressful and overwhelming. Customized wedding dresses take away some of the stress and pressure from trying to find that one gown which has all the components needed for a dress you'd deem perfect. However, there are still several things you need to do to ensure it is exactly what you desire. The process of having customized wedding dresses made is certainly less stressful and overwhelming than searching for an off-the-rack wedding dress detail by detail, but there are still a few things that can be done to make sure everything goes as smoothly as possible. Here are a few tips to ensure the process is as painless as possible—perhaps even enjoyable!

Know What You Want

If the standard wedding gowns you see in stores and catalogues don't seem to match your style, don't worry about it—that's what custom wedding dresses are for. Remember that there are as many unique styles of dresses available as there are different types of brides. Help out your designer by coming with an idea of what you want, and if this is difficult for you (it generally is for many people), consider bringing pictures of what you like, your wedding concept, the type of hairstyle you plan to wear with your dress, and a few dresses that make you look and feel better. (Tip: they don’t necessarily have to be your favorites—just those that you receive the most compliments on.) Your designer/couturier/tailor can take it from there and provide a few recommendations of their own.

Get A Head Start

Custom wedding dresses typically need to be commissioned at least six months (nine is even better) before your wedding. If, like most brides, you plan to slim down before the wedding itself, make sure you have several fittings scheduled before the wedding day. Many designers schedule at least three fittings—with the final fitting as close to the big day as possible, but with enough time left for changes to be made to the dress if necessary.

Consider Practicality And Comfort

Practicality remains an issue. The most attractive custom wedding dresses are useless if they require industrial-strength foundation undergarments just to make you look good in them—this will only make you appear tentative, fidgety, and uncomfortable. Remember that nothing detracts from a bride’s beauty more than obvious discomfort! Also, consider the suitability of the style to the theme and venue. For example, if your wedding is somewhere with sand or dewy grass, trains and floor-sweeping hems are not practical—it might be better to choose cocktail or tea-length dresses instead.

Always remember, your wedding day is a special occasion—possibly one of the most distinctive in a woman’s life. Custom wedding dresses help ensure it stays this way. It’s not hard to get one made that makes you look great, is comfortable, and feels like you—so relax, you’ve got a wedding to plan!
