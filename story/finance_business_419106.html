The Legend of Pisces: The Romance of Fish

Fish also have tears, it's just that they weep in the water and we cannot see them.

Even the love god Cupid has his own romance. Only that, it was a very distant old story, no one remembers, and no one mentions it.

The story begins with the goddess of beauty Venus. When Venus was still young, she fell in love with David, the most handsome man in ancient Roman legend. David was perfect, while Venus was incomplete, she was a beautiful woman with broken arms. However, her imperfection appeared so complete in David's eyes. Their union was the greatest love in the realm of gods, thus heaven rewarded them with the most symbolic gift of love, which was their child Cupid.

Cupid was an adorable boy with wings, he had a small divine bow, and anyone hit by his arrow would fall in love and be forever happy. But, Cupid who equally desired love could not bring himself happiness because he couldn't shoot himself with his own arrow.

At a banquet of the gods, Venus took her beloved son Cupid to attend. A girl with peculiar expression intruded into Cupid's heart. This girl was beautiful but looked extremely sorrowful. Cupid approached her to ask the reason. Originally, this girl was the daughter of the prophet Solomon. Solomon once predicted that this would be a disastrous banquet, and she, Bloodstone, would become the sacrifice of this disaster. After hearing this, Cupid was very sad, because he not only sympathized with the girl's fate, but also had fallen in love with her unknowingly.

Just at that moment, the terrible monster with a hundred eyes appeared! It summoned wind and rain, turning the banquet into chaos. The monster was specifically against all gods, it was very powerful, and none of the gods could handle it, they could only flee for their lives. Bloodstone said: "This can't go on, we must eliminate this demon." She seemed to forget her father's prophecy, bravely rushing towards the monster. And under the great concern for Bloodstone, Cupid hastily shot an arrow at the monster. He only wanted to repel it, but forgot what his arrows were for. Unfortunately, the arrow not only hit the monster, but also hit Bloodstone running towards the monster! Meanwhile, Venus found her dear son, pulled him into the river, and they turned into two fish to escape danger. Cupid couldn't break free from his mother's hand, he tearfully looked back, watching Bloodstone and the monster disappear together in the vast universe...

Later, there was a constellation in the sky called Pisces, but Cupid was not on it. He sat alone on Jupiter, sometimes shooting an arrow towards Earth. Thus, romantic Pisces girls would dance with strangers at the end of the world, fall in love with them, and then emigrate to another planet to get married and have children...

The Legend of Aries: The Jealousy of Ram

In a distant and ancient country, the king and queen divorced due to incompatible personalities, and the king remarried a beautiful queen. Unfortunately, this new queen was naturally jealous. She was infuriated by how much the king cared for the son and daughter left by his ex-wife. As time went on, she decided to get rid of the prince and princess to regain the king's love.

When spring came, the new queen distributed all the wheat seeds given to the commoners after roasting them, making it impossible for farmers to grow wheat no matter how much water or fertilizer they used. At this point, the new queen started spreading lies, saying the crop failure was due to a curse on the country, and the curse was entirely because of the evil thoughts of the prince and princess! Because of the evil prince and princess, all the people in the country would fall into poverty and hunger, this was such a terrible thing! The kind and simple commoners easily believed the queen's words. Soon, regardless of gender or age, everyone across the country unanimously demanded that the king must execute the prince and princess to lift the curse, appease the heavens, allow the hard work of the people to bear fruit, and restore the country's former peace and abundance. The king, unable to defy the public anger, though reluctant, still ordered the execution of the prince and princess.

This news reached the ears of the prince and princess's birth mother, she then sought help from Zeus, praying daily. Zeus quickly learned about this matter, and on the day of the execution, he sent a ram with golden fleece to rescue the prince and princess. The prince never felt afraid because he was inherently optimistic; while the princess, playful and careless, accidentally fell off the ram's back and died while crossing the sea. Zeus rewarded the ram by hanging it high in the sky, which is today's familiar Aries constellation, and the prince's optimism and the princess's carelessness are the biggest characteristics of Aries people.

The Legend of Taurus: The Temptation of the Bull

In ancient Greece long ago, when Europe did not yet have its name, there was a kingdom called Phoenicia, with its capital Tyre and Sidon being rich places. King Agenor had a beautiful daughter named Europa.

Europa often dreamed of a strange woman telling her: "Let me take you to meet Zeus, for the goddess of fate has designated you as his lover." At that time, Zeus had only Hera as his wife, and Zeus did not love his wife, he was always in a state of melancholy. The goddess of fate Clotho felt the need to help Zeus find happiness. She knew that the fire god had a long-sleeved robe, a delicate purple gauze embroidered with many scenes of the lives of deities using gold and silver threads, invaluable and dazzling. Clotho obtained this robe and let Zeus give it to Europa. Initially, Zeus wasn't very interested, but when he saw Europa, he was deeply attracted by her beauty, falling irrevocably in love with this princess of Europe. He approached her in the guise of a neighboring prince to propose marriage and gave her the divine robe. However, Europa did not consent; she kept thinking about the promise made by the goddess of fate.

One morning, Europa, as usual, went to the seaside grassland with her companions to play. While they happily picked flowers and wove garlands, a group of strong cattle arrived on the grassland, and Europa immediately noticed the noble and splendid golden bull among them. The bull had broad horns and a sturdy waist, like a crudely made artifact, shining brightly, with a crescent-shaped silver birthmark on its forehead. Its fur was golden yellow, and its blue eyes burned with passion. That intangible allure was irresistible to Europa, she joyfully jumped onto the bull's back and beckoned her friends to join her, but none of them dared to ride the bull like Europa. At this moment, the golden bull gently leapt up and swiftly flew into the sky. Her companions panicked, shouting Europa's name, Europa was at a loss as the bull galloped across the beach, over the sea, and finally landed on a deserted island. At this time, the bull transformed into a handsome man like a god, telling her that he was the master of Crete Island, if Europa agreed to marry him, he could protect her. However, Europa did not consent; she kept thinking about the promise made by the goddess of fate.

As the red sun slowly rose, Europa was left alone on the deserted island, she shouted towards the sun: "Poor Europa, would you rather marry the king of beasts as a concubine? Goddess of vengeance, why don't you bring that golden bull back before my eyes, so I can break its horns!" Suddenly, laughter came from behind her, Europa turned around to see the strange woman from her dream. The beautiful woman stood before her and said: "Beautiful maiden, stop being angry, the golden bull whose horns you cursed will soon bring them to you to break. I am the goddess of beauty Venus, my son Cupid has pierced both your and Zeus's hearts, the one who brought you here is Zeus himself. You have now become a goddess on earth, your name will last forever, henceforth, this land shall be called Europe." Only then did Europa understand clearly, finally believing in the arrangement of the goddess of fate. And the constellation Taurus in the zodiac was named accordingly, becoming a symbol of love and beauty.

The Legend of Gemini: The Friendship of Twins

In the distant ancient Greek country, there was a beautiful and touching legend. The gentle and virtuous Queen Leda had a pair of very lovable sons, they were not twins, but looked completely different, and the bond between the two brothers was particularly deep, making Queen Leda feel very happy.

But one day, Greece was attacked by a huge wild boar, the princes gathered many warriors to hunt and kill this wild boar. In the process, the brave elder brother killed the wild boar, but was also injured. Upon returning triumphantly, the whole country celebrated, Queen Leda secretly revealed the truth to her wounded elder son. Originally, the elder brother was not the son of the king and queen, but the son of the queen and the god Zeus. So, he was a god, possessing eternal life, invincible to anyone. After knowing this, the elder brother repeatedly promised not to tell anyone this secret, not even his dearest younger brother.

However, unfortunately, the warriors quarreled over the credit and formed two factions, each looking down upon the other. Later, they started fighting, the situation getting completely out of control. The two princes immediately rushed to stop them, but no one was willing to stop first. In the midst of the chaos, someone threw a spear at the elder brother, the younger brother rushed forward to protect him, standing in front of his elder brother. As a result, the younger brother was killed, causing the elder brother immense grief. Indeed, the elder brother had eternal life, how could he be killed? It was only because the uninformed younger brother loved his elder brother too much.

For this reason, the elder brother returned to heaven to beg Zeus to bring his younger brother back to life. Zeus frowned and said: "The only way is to share half of your vitality with him, then he will come back to life, but you will become a mortal, dying at any time." However, the elder brother agreed without hesitation. He said, the younger brother was willing to die for him, why couldn't he die for his younger brother? Zeus was deeply moved and created a constellation in their names, naming it Gemini.

The Legend of Cancer: The Curse of Crab

A long, long time ago, during the battle between Hercules and the snake monster, a giant crab emerged from the sea to assist the snake monster by biting Hercules' ankle, later this crab was killed by Hercules and fell on a small island in the Aegean Sea. The crab failed to complete the task assigned by the goddess Hera, thus it was cursed, and this curse affected the Athenian queen. When Princess Melo was born, a prophet predicted that the day the princess got married would be the day the queen died. Due to this prophecy, the queen never allowed the princess to marry.

Until Melo was twenty years old, a prince named Sosa came to Athens. Sosa came because of Melo's reputation, he wanted to marry Melo, and Melo fell deeply in love with him at first sight. However, the curse was terrifying, the princess did not want to sacrifice her mother's life for her own happiness. So she tried every means to prevent Sosa and her own desires. She set nine trials, similar to nine impossible tasks, unless Sosa accomplished all of them, he could not marry Melo. However, the incredibly brave Sosa managed to accomplish them all! The princess was caught in a dilemma. The great mother, for her daughter's happiness, decisively decided to marry Melo to Sosa.

On the day of Melo and Sosa's wedding, the queen was not present, she did not want any unexpected events at the banquet to ruin the atmosphere. The queen quietly walked towards the sea, embracing the waves of the Aegean Sea, drowning herself. When people could not find the queen anywhere, they discovered a giant crab at sea, its arms crossed in front of its chest, seemingly lacking a sense of security, or like a protective mother.

Hera, knowing about this incident, regretted it somewhat, thus letting that gentle and sensitive mother become a constellation in the sky, its image being that of a crab.

The Legend of Leo: The Courage of Lion

Nemea was the son of the giant Typhon and the snake monster Echidna. When humans and demons fell in love, Nemea fell from the moon, a beautiful treasure given by heaven to this couple, and everyone called him Ani.

Ani was actually a half-man, half-demon monster. During the day, he was a fierce lion, his fur shimmering with the color of the sun; at night, he turned into human form, a blond, blue-eyed youth.

Ani's sister Hydra was a nine-headed serpent monster, her upper body was human-like and very beautiful; her lower body was a snake, moonlight silver.

Ani had loved Hydra deeply since childhood. Although they had the same parents, Ani fell from the sky, while Hydra was naturally produced by their mother Echidna. Hydra always thought Ani was a star in the sky, destined to return to heaven, and Ani said that before returning to heaven, he was willing to do anything for Hydra, including dying. Thus, they fell in love.

However, their happy days were soon shattered by misfortune. Hero Hercules, according to the oracle's revelation, accepted ten tasks from the king, two of which were to kill Ani and Hydra. Ani did not understand why the disputes of the gods involved them, and why Zeus's mistake should be borne by them. Ani originally did not wish to fight against Hercules, but to protect his beloved Hydra, he decided to block Hercules outside the Nemean forest. Hydra wanted to stop him from going, but Ani consoled her, saying: "Apart from you, no one can kill me! Don't worry, I will definitely defeat this son of Zeus and a mortal." After saying that, he went alone to confront Hercules.

Hydra loved Ani dearly, she would not let Ani go to his death, deciding to defeat Hercules before Ani, even if it meant mutual destruction. Hydra came to the Amymone spring to confront Hercules. However, despite her ability to create multiple heads creating an intimidating presence, Hercules was indeed a great hero, bravely and decisively killing the serpent monster Hydra and soaking his arrows in the highly toxic snake blood.

In the evening, Ani finally found Hercules; he was now a blood-soaked lion, fiercely pouncing on Hercules. Hercules drew his sword and fought with the lion, but the lion's fur seemed impenetrable by any weapon, making it impossible for Hercules to kill him. As the weather gradually darkened, Hercules remembered the poisoned arrows, aiming at the lion and shooting. One, two missed, the third arrow struck the lion's heart. The arrow soaked with Hydra's toxic blood instantly pierced Ani's shattered heart. The lion fell to the ground and turned into a human. Hercules stared in shock at Ani, who died without uttering a word.

Later, Zeus let Ani return to the sky as a star, which became the brilliant Leo constellation. People belonging to Leo were also endowed with the personality of sacrificing for love.

The Legend of Virgo: The Winter of Virginity

Persephone was a pure goddess.

She was the only daughter of Demeter, the goddess of the earth and grain, and the goddess of the bright spring. Wherever she lightly stepped, vibrant flowers bloomed.

One day, Persephone and her companions were picking flowers on the grassland in the valley, she surprisingly discovered a silver narcissus, radiating beauty. Gradually moving away from her companions, she stretched out her hand to pick the narcissus. The moment she plucked it, the narcissus turned into a purple mist, a faint fragrance of the underworld spread. As the mist gradually dissipated, a remarkable man dressed in black with purple eyes appeared before her.

Persephone stepped back in surprise. The man revealed a terrifying smile and said: "Goddess, you broke the spell and saved me, so fulfill my vow and marry me!" Persephone hadn't fully understood what was happening, when suddenly a crack appeared on the ground, and a powerful force swept her inside.

Persephone's cries echoed through the valley, Demeter dropped the grains she was holding and raced over thousands of mountains and rivers to find her daughter. Without the mother of the earth, seeds stopped sprouting, fertile lands yielded no bountiful harvests, humanity faced a great calamity. This soon reached Zeus's ears, he knew that Hades, the king of the underworld, had abducted Persephone, so he ordered another curse on him. Hades ultimately couldn't withstand Zeus's power, but he truly loved Persephone. Knowing he was about to fall into a long sleep again, he told Persephone: "The fragrance on me belongs to mankind, please take it with you!" After saying that, Hades closed his eyes and could no longer see the beloved spring goddess Persephone.

When Persephone returned from the underworld to the earthly world, it was already spring. She scattered the fragrance of hundreds of flowers on the earth and brought radiant sunlight to everyone. However, she couldn't forget Hades lying asleep in the underworld, those purple eyes lingered in the goddess's heart. In summer, the goddess tiredly yearned; in autumn, the goddess heavily yearned. By winter, the goddess finally couldn't resist and visited Hades in the underworld. At this time, Hades would miraculously wake up, and when spring came and Persephone left, he would fall asleep again. Year after year, this pure and beautiful virgin found herself truly in love with the gloomy shadow of the underworld.
