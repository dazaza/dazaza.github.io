Centipedes have been used as traditional animal-based Chinese herbal medicine for thousands of years. The government has long called for vigorous capture of centipedes every year, and in some main producing areas of centipedes, the original seasonal capture and seasonal purchase have gradually evolved into cross-seasonal capture and four-season purchases. In major Chinese herbal medicine markets, the purchase price per centipede has soared from a few cents to over 1.5 yuan, with even higher export prices and an ever-increasing demand. However, due to deforestation and pollution from pesticides and fertilizers that damage the ecological environment, wild resources are becoming increasingly scarce, leading to a severe shortage of centipede resources and rising prices. Therefore, a once-in-a-century opportunity has presented itself: breeding centipedes in Luzhou. In the coming years, the centipede farming industry is bound to become a shining highlight in the special farming sector, offering significant economic and social benefits.
