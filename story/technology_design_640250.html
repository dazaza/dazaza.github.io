"Eight Stars for Good Luck" - Yang Mi plays a rich girl, showing off her swimsuit in a deadly temptation

According to Tencent Entertainment, the New Year comedy "Eight Stars for Good Luck", jointly produced by Letv Pictures and Tianma Films and supervised by Zhang Zhao and Wong Bai-Nam, officially announced its strong entry into this year's Spring Festival box office race yesterday. The film features action star Donnie Yen, actor Louis Koo, comedian Wong Bai-Nam, actor Du Wenze, actress Wu Junru, singer Kelly Chen, actress Yang Mi, and model Xiong Da-Lin as the leading "Four Kings and Four Queens." In the first set of costume photos released by the production company yesterday, Yang Mi made a stunning appearance as a new member of the "Happy Family" with a water lily look.

The production company revealed that this is just one of Yang Mi's many versatile costumes in the movie. Wong Bai-Nam said, "There is no strongest, only stronger in the 'Happy' series. 'Eight Stars for Good Luck' has eight box office stars bringing laughter, making it the strongest star-studded cast, largest production scale, and the only pure New Year comedy in this season. As the King of New Year comedies in 2012, the four elements of sexiness, laughter, unpredictability, and romance will all be upgraded in 'Eight Stars for Good Luck.'"

The film will also be exclusively broadcast on Letv.com and Letv TV Cloud Video Super Clear Player. www.gzhl78.com "Eight Stars for Good Luck" sees Yang Mi playing a rich girl alongside Wong Bai-Nam in a story reminiscent of "If You Are the One." The movie tells the story of several women who, motivated by the promise of a sincere hug as payment, come to the aid of men experiencing career lows or emotional wounds. Among them are lawyer Wong Bai-Nam, whose family has failed, and the spoilt rich girl Yang Mi; never-successful pop singer Donnie Yen and washed-up idol Wu Junru who has been kicked out; construction supervisor Goo Ti-Leong, who moonlights as a model, and female photographer Kelly Chen, who seeks male charm; and romance novelist Du Wenze, who doesn't believe in love, and blind woman Xiong Da-Lin, who has never been in love. These four pairs of vastly different men and women go through unexpected and hilarious situations in their "hug-help-hug" process.

As a new member of the Happy series, mainland actress Yang Mi plays a "quasi" heiress expected to inherit a fortune of 6 billion yuan in "Eight Stars for Good Luck." To obtain this huge wealth, this "fake princess" teams up with her cheap father Wong Bai-Nam, found on the "hug happiness" website, using unreliable methods to seek a reliable "true person."

In a set of costume photos revealed yesterday, Yang Mi once again challenges her most daring level of sensuality since "Island of Fear," showcasing her acting desires on set, where she has already made me laugh uncontrollably, and audiences will surely burst out laughing after watching."

The "Four Kings and Four Queens" cleverly form a "hug group," with "Eight Stars for Good Luck" dominating as the "King of New Year Comedies." Continuing the big-name route of the Wong-style "Happy" series, Donnie Yen, Goo Ti-Leong, and others make up the most commercially successful star lineup in today's Chinese-language film industry. However, Wong Bai-Nam stated, "We're not simply stacking famous names. Choosing these eight is because we see an interesting chemical reaction between them. Unimaginable pairings will surprise and amuse the audience. This time, each superstar undergoes a significant character transformation, making it hard to imagine their astonishing performances outside of the movie."

Director Chan Ching-Ka gave high praise to the performances of the "Four Kings and Four Queens," saying, "Their acting skills have reached 100% of what the story requires. More often than not, their comedic talents add many unexpected surprises to the film, all of which are their on-site improvisations, with particularly great results!"
