Counting the Various Experts on the 3D Printing Chessboard

by anonymous on 2013-08-20 14:48:38

3D printing, this rapid prototyping technology, is like a magical key that opens the door from the digital world to the physical world. Unlike traditional subtractive manufacturing techniques, this technology builds three-dimensional objects by adding materials layer by layer through continuous physical stacking, hence it is also known as Additive Manufacturing (AM). This new field of 3D printing integrates cutting-edge technologies and knowledge from various areas such as digital modeling, mechatronics control, information technology, jetting technology, materials science, and chemistry. Its commercial applications are just beginning, yet the game of 3D printing has already started with quite a bit of buzz. Let's count the players.

3D Printing Equipment: The core of 3D printing. The international 3D printer manufacturing industry is currently undergoing rapid mergers and consolidations.

3D Systems

An American developer and manufacturer of 3D printing equipment. After acquiring Z Corporation, the earliest inventor and initial patent holder of 3D printing technology in November 2011, 3D Systems established its leading position in the 3D printing field.

Technical advantages and features: Resolution up to 600dpi, larger build size, capable of printing oversized models in one piece, full-color 3D printing technology, high-resolution selective layer-by-layer jetting of fused materials, higher process economy and convenience.

Stratasys

An American developer and manufacturer of 3D printing equipment. In 2010, Stratasys signed an OEM agreement with HP, a giant in the traditional printing industry, to produce HP-branded 3D printers. In May 2011, it acquired Solidscape, and in April 2012, it acquired Objet, an Israeli startup in 3D printing equipment for $1.4 billion. In June 2013, it acquired MakerBot for $403 million, thus becoming a digital manufacturing company covering both industrial-grade and desktop consumer-grade 3D printing markets.

Technical advantages and features: FDM (Fused Deposition Modeling) technology for layer-by-layer jetting, high precision of light-sensitive curing technology, capable of creating smooth surfaces, fine features, and complex shapes, and able to jet a second material to create supports for desired structures.

For developed countries, some business models have initially formed in the areas of 3D printing for scanners, modeling design, and personalized customization of 3D printed products.

Fuel3D Scanner

Establishment time: 2013

Kickstarter funding goal: $75,000

Amount raised so far: $256,907

A handheld high-resolution scanner that captures 3D models within seconds, combining software and hardware. Similar to a regular camera, you simply focus and press the shutter, and Fuel3D will capture your 3D model within seconds. Once scanned, the raw data from the Fuel3D scanner is converted into high-fidelity 3D color geometry data by the software. The generated 3D color data allows users to view and edit from all angles and can output 3D file formats that support 3D printing or other screen operations.

Volumental

Establishment time: 2012

Kickstarter funding goal: $20,000

Amount raised so far: $13,962

A browser-based fast and easy-to-use 3D scanner. The basic system includes a browser-based scanning application and a depth camera. Volumental can capture full-color 3D models of real objects. After connecting the web application with the scanner, the calculations and model creation occur in the cloud. It supports sharing, downloading, editing, embedding on web pages, and 3D printing. Volumental provides customized 3D scanning solutions for businesses.

Cubify Draw

Launch time: 2013

Financing situation: Unknown

An iOS 3D modeling application produced by 3D Systems. Users draw any shape by hand, combined with some typical 3D modeling functions like thickening, filling, and connecting, which can turn a flat graphic into a 3D model. It also supports importing existing images for tracing. Finally, by clicking "Make it 3D," a 3D model appears. Users can upload the generated 3D model to Cubify.com to share with others, or email it to their inbox for 3D printing.

Mixamo

Launch time: July 2008

Total financing: $4 million

A web-based 3D character and animation generation platform operating under the SaaS model, generating revenue by renting services to game studios. It helps developers easily create 3D characters and animations. Users can directly upload original 3D characters or use those provided by Mixamo for creation, and any 3D action in Mixamo can be automatically applied to the user-uploaded character. Mixamo can seamlessly integrate with all mainstream 3D software and game engines. It currently offers an animation creation suite, including thousands of ready-to-use animations and an automated skeleton service. Compared to standard animation keyframes and motion capture technologies, this technology can reduce animation production costs by 70% to 80%.

Sketchfab

Launch time: May 2012

Total financing: $500,000

A 3D model aggregation and sharing website. Sketchfab supports 27 types of 3D file formats and can embed interactive 3D models in web pages without any plugins (just add a universal code), using WebGL and HTML5 technology for real-time rendering in browsers.

Its business model can be summarized as SaaS, providing tiered services with basic services being free. Freelancers can use Sketchfab tools to create 3D portfolios, allowing people who need 3D design services to find them. Small businesses can use Sketchfab to privately share 3D design plans with clients online. Large brands or e-commerce websites can use Sketchfab to showcase products.

Domestically, Lao 3D, which went online in March 2013, adopted a similar model.

Platforms for personalized customization of 3D products have begun to show vertical segmentation trends.

Shapeways

Launch time: March 2007

Total financing: $47.3 million

Shapeways is a startup helping users DIY products using 3D printing technology. Users simply upload their own designed 3D models, choose materials, pay Shapeways, and the latter will print the product with a 3D printer and mail it to the user.

More than 10,000 stores have moved into Shapeways, where 3D designers can sell physical items printed on demand by Shapeways' equipment through these stores. Currently, there are over 1 million products stored on Shapeways, with 60,000 new designs added each month.

eBay Exact

Launch time: 2013

Financing situation: Unknown

A personalized customization platform for 3D printed products. Users can directly place orders with manufacturers on eBay Exact and pay with PayPal, after which they will receive their custom-made products from the manufacturer within 7 to 14 working days. The main partners are MakerBot located in Brooklyn, USA, Sculpteo in France, and Hot Pop Factory in Toronto, Canada.

MakieLab

Launch time: 2012

Total financing: $1.55 million

A British personalized 3D toy customization service provider. There are currently web-based platforms and iOS applications. MakieLab aims to build a business model combining real goods with virtual goods: users can design toys themselves, then choose to purchase 3D printed products in the real world or buy decorative accessories directly in the virtual world (similar to buying costumes for characters in many online games).

Its Makies Doll Factory iOS app allows children to design hairstyles, faces, and clothing for dolls, and MakieLab will use 3D printing technology to produce the designed toys and mail them to consumers.

Let's Creat! Pottery

Launch time: 2013

Financing situation: Unknown

A mobile app for personalized customization of 3D printed pottery. It allows users to create and submit exquisite pottery models according to their preferences on their phones, and Let's Creat! Pottery will mail the 3D printed works to the users. The cooperating 3D printing service provider is Sculpteo.

MakeXYZ

Launch time: January 2013

Financing situation: Unknown

An online platform connecting 3D printing needs with 3D printers. Owners of 3D printers can list specifications, unit printing prices, and other related information on MakeXYZ. Users with printing needs can filter out nearby printing service providers based on conditions, upload 3D printing drawings for printing, and complete the delivery of the works via courier or self-collection. MakeXYZ takes a 5% commission and has already achieved profitability.

CowFab, which started later, has a similar model.
