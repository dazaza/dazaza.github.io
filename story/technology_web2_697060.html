Braised Bream (Step-by-step pictures): 1. Bought an eight-liang bream (felt it was too small, when I was young, if I caught this size of fish, I would release it. Unfortunately, it is rare to see those big breams weighing 2-4 jin from my hometown in the market now. Actually, if there were triangular breams from the Yangtze River, that would be best. I guess all the good stuff from my childhood has almost disappeared) necessary ginger, green onions, garlic. 2. Pay attention to cleaning the fish's abdominal cavity thoroughly. The water bodies now are all contaminated to different extents, so there is black membrane in the fish's abdominal cavity! 3. Cut flower knives on both sides of the fish - this way it can easily absorb flavor. 4. Since the fish is small, there is no need to make a cross flower cut, three diagonal cuts will do - note that it should be diagonally cut into the flesh, not straight! 5. Evenly sprinkle some salt on both sides of the fish, even inside the abdominal cavity can have a little, slightly pre-season! I don't like the method of coating with flour much, as I feel it ruins the texture of the fish skin! 6. Place the well-seasoned fish in a plate, add ginger threads, flattened garlic, dried chili threads, then added some green onions! Several colors combined look quite nice! 7. Into the pot, when the oil starts smoking blue, add the seasonings and stir-fry until fragrant, fire cannot be too high, mainly to stir out the fragrance. 8. When the green onions and garlic turn yellow, you can scoop out the seasonings for later use, be especially careful not to burn the red chilies black, that wouldn't look good. 10. Use the remaining oil to pan-fry the fish! Oil temperature at six-tenths is fine. 11. Both sides lightly golden brown is enough, can also slightly overcook!! Be careful when flipping the fish, if it breaks, it's ruined. 12. Then you can add a small bowl of water (no rich broth at home) start cooking - restaurants have big fires and plenty of oil, they even cook entirely in oil, families just use their own methods! After the water boils, add the most important rice wine + soy sauce, this is a step to remove fishy smell, season taste and color! 13. Add the previous seasonings and cook together, add a little salt in the soup, consider the amount oh, because soy sauce is also salty, there will be more sauce added later, including the fish itself which has already been seasoned! Continuously pour the sauce over the fish to ensure even heating! 14. Add a large spoonful of broad bean paste, this is something I really like～～basically homemade red pepper paste in my hometown! 15. Cook for a short while, the fish is done, prolonged time easily damages the shape! 16. Now serve the fish on a plate, sprinkle green onion leaves - do you think it's done? 17. There is still some soup in the pot! The essence lies here! Add a little sugar and vinegar, monosodium glutamate, prepare a bowl of water starch, put it in the pot, boil on high heat, quickly stir evenly, when it becomes slightly sticky, take it off the heat! 18. Final process - pour the sauce! The amount of sauce you control yourself, too little is too dry, too much is tasteless! 19. Done! Looks great!

Mapo Tofu Recipe (with pictures): 1. Prepare materials: tofu cut into cubes (medium hardness), minced beef, broad bean paste, salt, alcohol, crushed dry red chili, green onions, minced ginger, Sichuan peppercorn powder, water starch, soy sauce, a little sugar. 2. Add a little rapeseed oil in the pot, heat on high fire, after the oil is hot, sequentially add broad bean paste, salt, dry red chili, green onions, minced ginger, Sichuan peppercorn powder, minced beef, or marinate the minced beef with the above seasonings and add them all together. Stir-fry until fragrant. 3. Add the tofu cut into small pieces. Change to low heat, bring to a boil. 4. After the tofu is cooked, change to high heat, add the prepared thickening juice made from water starch, sugar, alcohol, monosodium glutamate, soy sauce. After the thickening juice evenly coats, turn off the heat, take out of the pot. 5. Take out of the pot, sprinkle with Sichuan peppercorn powder, fragrant and spicy Mapo Tofu with full Sichuan flavor is ready!

Homemade Potato Pancake (with pictures): 1. Wash the potatoes, steam in a steamer pot (I secretly think using a pressure cooker is also good, and very convenient! However, I suggest those who haven't used a pressure cooker before not to take risks!) 2. Beat the eggs evenly, add milk, then add a little salt, granulated sugar, monosodium glutamate (chicken essence would be better!) 3. Peel the steamed potatoes, mash them. 4. Pour in the prepared milk egg mixture, add an appropriate amount of flour, stir hard! 5. Put the shaped potato pancake into a frying pan, remember to use low heat, otherwise it will affect the color of the potato pancake. 6. A delicious and delicate potato pancake is ready!

How to Make Red-Braised Pork Belly (step-by-step pictures): 1. Clean the wok, heat it up, add two tablespoons of oil, put three to four tablespoons of white sugar, switch to low heat. Reading 'The Eight Immortals', Duan Yu could smell the aroma of soy sauce and caramelized pork from a street away from Songhe Tower, always thought caramel was brown sugar, but after reading this article, I finally understand. Preliminary preparation: one piece of pork belly, cut into one-centimeter square strips. 2. Keep stirring with the ladle, making the white sugar dissolve, turning into reddish-brown sugar syrup, this is called caramelizing sugar. 3. Pour in the prepared pork belly strips, stir fry evenly, making sure each piece of pork is coated with the caramelized sugar. 4. Add soy sauce, cooking wine, ginger slices, rock sugar, a little salt, boil, then simmer for two to three minutes. When the sauce has evaporated enough, increase the heat to thicken the sauce. 5. This is what it looks like when it's done.

Winter Warming Winter Melon and Meatball Soup (pictures included): Ingredients: pork mince (a mix of lean and fatty), winter melon, starch, light soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, chopped green onions, sliced garlic, sliced ginger, salt, a little chicken essence. Mix the pork mince with starch, light soy sauce, salt, and Shaoxing wine evenly. Heat a little oil in the pan, when hot, sauté sliced garlic and ginger until fragrant. Add an appropriate amount of water, bring to a boil, then pinch the pork mince into small round meatballs and drop them into the pot. The meatballs look like this. Forgot to take a picture of what the meatballs look like in the pot. Simmer for 10 minutes on medium heat, then add the sliced winter melon. When the winter melon softens a bit, add chicken essence and an appropriate amount of salt. Once the winter melon is fully softened, turn off the heat and serve. Sprinkle with some chopped green onions, the taste is really good! Everyone take a look!

Winter Nourishing Lamb Shank Stew (step-by-step instructions): Today went to the market and saw some fresh lamb shanks, so I bought two pounds and decided to make a lamb shank stew. Used ingredients like water chestnuts, carrots, ginger slices. First, heat the pot, put the lamb in and stir-fry until dry, removing moisture. Start a new pot, add ginger slices, fermented bean curd, and stir-fry until fragrant. Then add the pre-stir-fried lamb, keep stirring, add a little wine, soy sauce, rock sugar, salt, and an appropriate amount of water to cook for 15 minutes. Then add water chestnuts and carrots and stew. After stewing for an hour and a half, the lamb shank stew is ready, no gamey smell at all.

Pickled Chili Beef Strips (step-by-step instructions): The sour and spicy pickled chilies are my first choice for being appetizing, with bright red color, just looking at them makes me want to eat. Slice the beef coarsely and season, slice the pickled chilies, and julienne the celery. Fry the beef in warm oil and remove. Stir-fry the pickled chili threads until fragrant, add the celery threads and stir-fry. Add the beef threads and stir-fry evenly. Beef is low in fat, celery is high in fiber, and chili can help burn fat, this is a weight-loss dish.

Sweet and Sour Ribbon Fish (step-by-step instructions): Ingredients: Two frozen fresh ribbon fish. Accompaniments: ginger, garlic, pickled chili peppers, Sichuan peppercorns, green onions, sugar, vinegar, soy sauce, etc. 1. Clean and chop into pieces, mix with a little cornstarch or flour, deep-fry until golden brown. 2. Remove and drain. 3. Fry the Sichuan peppercorns until fragrant, pour in the accompaniments (reserve the green onions), pour in soy sauce, vinegar, sugar, and a little cooking wine. 4. Pour in the deep-fried ribbon fish pieces, stir evenly, let sit for a few minutes to infuse flavors. 5. Add a little chicken essence for seasoning, thicken the sauce with cornstarch slurry, garnish with green onions, ready to serve. Smell that? It's ready!

Beijing-Style Oil-Braised Large Shrimp (step-by-step instructions): Oil-braised shrimp is a classic Beijing dish. In my childhood, due to scarce material resources and people striving towards a moderately prosperous life, such delicacies only appeared on the table during Spring Festival to satisfy my cravings. Now life is better, eating shrimp has become an everyday affair, but I still often recall the oil-braised shrimp made by dad when I was young... Main ingredient: shrimp (not necessarily the bigger the better, moderate size is fine for flavor absorption). Accompaniments: scallions, ginger threads, white sugar, cooking wine, salt, rice vinegar, rich broth, monosodium glutamate as needed. 1. First, clean the shrimp by removing whiskers, legs, sandbags, and glands. The shrimp I bought wasn't too big, looked pretty clean, so I skipped removing the sandbags, hehe. 2. Marinate the cleaned shrimp with a little salt and drain the water. Scallion and ginger threads. Still the accompaniments, two indispensable items: cooking wine and rice vinegar. 3. Heat the wok with oil until about five-tenths hot (place your hand above the oil, feel slightly warm), put in the shrimp and fry until both sides are firm. 4. Remove the fried shrimp for later use. 5. If there is still some oil left in the wok, heat it up and stir-fry scallion and ginger threads until fragrant; if there is no oil left, need to add more oil. 6. Pour in the fried shrimp and fry again until reddish in color. 7. Add cooking wine, vinegar, rich broth, salt, white sugar, monosodium glutamate, bring to a boil, then gently stir-fry for about 3 minutes on low heat, thicken the sauce on medium heat, then arrange neatly on a plate, drizzle the remaining sauce over the shrimp, and serve.

Spicy and Fragrant Fish Slices (step-by-step instructions): Mildly spicy, mainly fragrant. Tired of eating steamed fish all the time, bought a fish, took the meat off, used the head and bones to make soup, two ways to eat one fish, not bad. One live fish, gutted and cleaned, take the meat from both sides, slice into butterfly pieces, marinate for 30 minutes with seasonings. Fry until cooked in hot oil. Leave a little oil in the pan, stir-fry garlic slices until fragrant. Then stir-fry dried chili peppers and ginger powder until fragrant, add a little water, soy sauce, sugar, bring to a boil. Add fish slices to coat with the sauce, drizzle with vinegar, thicken with a thin slurry, sprinkle with green onion segments, toss and serve. A mildly spicy, crispy, slightly sweet and sour fish slice is ready!

Super Simple and Easy Omelette: From start to finish, less than half an hour, quick and easy~~~~~ Ingredients: 150g of flour, 2 eggs, appropriate amount of salt, appropriate amount of water (about 300ml), 20g of oil, appropriate amount of sesame seeds. Mix eggs, salt, oil, and water together until the salt dissolves, gradually add flour and stir, press any lumps with a small spoon, finally add sesame seeds and stir until there are no lumps. Let it rest for a few minutes. Turn on medium heat, use a non-stick frying pan, pour a little oil, pour the batter from the middle, if the poured batter spreads irregularly around, it means the batter is too thick, add more water to the batter. After adjusting the consistency, the batter will spread evenly when poured, and the batter flows quickly when the pan is shaken. When the surface of the batter changes color and the batter doesn't move when shaken, it's time to flip and fry the other side (best to use the sesame seed method to flip, one won't get burned, two exercises arm strength). After flipping, you can cover the pan, or leave it uncovered. Done... enjoy! Pair with a cup of black coffee!

Candy-Coated Bananas (step-by-step instructions): Extremely simple ingredients: 4 bananas, 50g of flour, 75g of cornstarch, 1 egg, water, 250g of sugar, oil. Cut the bananas into chunks, mix the powders, egg, and an appropriate amount of water. Add lots of oil to the pot, deep-fry the bananas. Fry until the surface of the bananas is crispy and fragrant. I fry a few pieces at a time, nonsense, finally reheat all the bananas in the pot to ensure all surfaces are crispy. Add a little oil to the pot, add sugar, stir continuously over medium-low heat, you will see the sugar gradually melt from granules, then quickly change from white to beige to dark yellow to light brown. At this point, you can pour in the bananas, stir to coat all the bananas with sugar. The caramelized exterior of the candy-coated bananas is crispy, the inside is soft and chewy, very tasty. The only downside is that the flour coating around the bananas gets a bit soft, need to adjust the batter recipe next time.
