As the weather gets cooler, electric heaters are becoming more popular. Here's a guide to help you choose the right electric heater.

by sa4563 on 2009-11-04 11:36:57

As the weather begins to cool, various heating products in supermarkets and stores enter their peak sales season. However, with so many types of heating products available, people often feel at a loss as to where to start — even common electric heaters come in multiple varieties. To help everyone choose a suitable heating device, it's necessary to introduce in detail the features and differences of several heating products on the market.

Currently, there are mainly five common types of electric heaters on the market: reflective far-infrared electric heaters, warm air blowers, oil-filled radiators, European fast-heating stoves, and European electric heat panels. Among them, the "little sun" style far-infrared electric heater has the advantages of instant heating upon plugging in and being inexpensive, but its disadvantage is that it can only provide localized heating. Warm air blowers have the advantage of instant heating and can provide overall heating, but their downside is that they can only heat a small area and are generally used in bathrooms. Oil-filled radiators have the advantage of providing continuous heating, but their drawback is that they heat up slowly and are typically used in rooms of 10-20 square meters. European fast-heating stoves have the advantage of quick heating through convection, but their downside is poor heat retention and are generally used in rooms of about 25 square meters.

Electric Heat Panel Heater

Also known as an oil-filled electric heater. This type of heater is filled with a new type of heat-conducting oil. When powered on, the heat-conducting oil around the heating element heats up and then dissipates heat along the heat pipes or radiator fins. When the oil temperature reaches 85°C, its temperature control element automatically cuts off the power. The radiator fins come in 7, 9, 10, or 12 pieces, and the power can be adjusted by selecting the number of radiator fins, with a usage power of around 1200 watts.

Advantages: Compared to the numerous fashionable styles of heaters on the market, oil-filled heaters may seem somewhat "outdated," but their benefit lies in evenly distributed heat, which makes people feel more comfortable. Moreover, using them in bedrooms poses no problems. Some models also come equipped with drying racks on top, allowing users to dry clothes on them. Additionally, the heat-conducting oil in oil-filled heaters does not need to be replaced, offering a long service life, making them suitable for use in living rooms, bedrooms, hallways, and families with elderly people and children. They have the advantages of being safe, hygienic, dust-free, and odorless.

Disadvantages: Longer preheating time and relatively high power consumption. Families with small electricity meter capacities or low voltage cannot use them.

PTC Warm Air Blower

PTC is the abbreviation for a ceramic heating element. It uses a fan to force air to flow over the PTC heating element, using this as the main method of heat exchange. It has a built-in thermostat that automatically cuts off the power when the outlet is blocked by the fan. Some models also have an automatic power cut-off function when tilted or tipped over. PTC warm air blowers have an output power of 800-1200 watts and allow for adjustable temperatures.

Advantages: Provides gentle airflow when working, heats up quickly, and has an automatic constant temperature function. Since PTC elements generally have waterproof functions, they are suitable for use in bathrooms. Prices range from $300 to $500, making them currently ideal portable household electric heaters.

Disadvantages: Can only be used in small areas. Long-term blowing on the same spot can cause discomfort to the skin. If not cleaned in time after prolonged use, bacteria on the warm air blower can be accelerated into the air, posing a health risk. Additionally, the power of warm air blowers is usually smaller, and the noise at startup can be a headache.

Convection Electric Heater

The shell of this type of heater has an air outlet on top and an air inlet at the bottom. After being powered on, the air around the heating element heats up and rises, flowing out of the air outlet, while cold air from the surroundings enters through the air inlet to replenish. This repeated cycle increases the indoor temperature. When the air inlet and outlet are blocked or the environmental temperature becomes too high, the temperature control element will automatically cut off the power to the heating element. This heater has a usage power of around 800 watts and allows adjustment of power by increasing or decreasing the number of connected heating elements.

Advantages: Higher safety performance, quiet operation.

Disadvantages: Slow heating.

Electric Heating Film Heater

This heater uses fully transparent high-temperature electric heating film as the heating material, with advanced technology globally. It adopts a hot air duct structure, with heat transfer achieved through enhanced convection. The thermal start-up speed is fast, and the air outlet temperature can reach above 100°C within 3 minutes.

Advantages: The electric heating film does not oxidize during heating, giving it a lifespan of over 100,000 hours. It also features compact size and aesthetic design, making it a replacement product in the family of electric heaters.

Disadvantages: Rapid cooling after power disconnection.

Bathroom Heater

Consumers who renovate their homes often install a bathroom heater above the shower column or bathtub for convenience during bathing. The bathroom heater is a recent product introduced from Australia, combining specially designed waterproof infrared lamps and exhaust fans to integrate multiple functions such as bathroom heating, ventilation, and infrared therapy. Due to the use of infrared radiation technology, it produces greater heat, higher efficiency, and is more energy-saving.

Moreover, infrared light has excellent health benefits for the human body, activating cells, delaying aging, and effectively treating conditions like shoulder periarthritis, cervical spondylosis, bone hyperplasia, arthritis, and slow wound healing.

Advantages: The biggest advantage of bathroom heaters is their safety and convenience. Upon turning on the heating lamp switch, the bathing area's temperature can quickly rise above 25°C. Some bathroom heaters come with waterproof remote controls, allowing free adjustment of temperature according to needs.

Disadvantages: Compared to oil-filled radiators, the heat efficiency of bathroom heaters is less uniform. After prolonged use, one might feel very hot on the head but colder on the lower body.

Other Heating Products

Siphon Tube Heat Pipe Warm Air Blower. This is a newly emerged type of electric heater, using "two-phase closed siphon tubes" as the heat source. It heats up quickly and has high thermal efficiency. During operation, it does not emit light, has no open flame, is resistant to water splashes and steam corrosion, and is suitable for ordinary rooms and bathrooms.

High-Temperature Superconducting Heat Master. It generates heat by heating superconducting oil and transfers heat using a fan, suitable for use in guest rooms and bathrooms. It is priced relatively high, generally between $500-$600.

Besides these traditional types, manufacturers have recently launched a new type of electric heater — the European electric heat panel. This heater combines the advantages of oil-filled radiators and European fast-heating stoves, heating up quickly and maintaining good insulation. It also comes with a humidifier, supports remote control and timer settings, and has an automatic power cut-off function when tipped over, though its price is relatively high.

[Tip]

When turning on the heater, there is sometimes a strange smell, which could be due to accumulated dust on the heater or the paint on the heater volatilizing due to temperature changes. Be cautious when using it, maintain a certain distance first, and use it after opening it for a period of time.
