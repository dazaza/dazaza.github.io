[Repost] Liu Yimiao's Quotations

by liuyimiaotqm on 2012-02-28 22:20:52

Original Address: Liu Yimiao's Anthology Author: Coach Bian What does wisdom mean? No one in human history has ever explained this word clearly. How to obtain wisdom? It is to enter irrelevance and be an outsider. Irrelevance generates intelligence, and being outside the situation generates wisdom. How can you enter irrelevance and become an outsider? The key is to find joy and take it as pleasure. Those who find joy enjoy everything. For those who don't, everything is a burden. The only way is to be obsessed with what you do. You must feel it to achieve success. Many of your confusions come from living from a human perspective. To learn from me, you need a mindset that frees you from humanity so that you won't be influenced by your emotions and desires, thus avoiding judgment errors. Ordinary people live by conscience, while those who achieve great things act on instinct.

Life can be categorized into four types. The fourth type questions whether something is right and studies "rightness" daily; the third type is busy doing things; the second type focuses on capturing the market; the first type starts thinking about problems from a macro perspective. Sun Tzu’s thirteen chapters illustrate the importance of "momentum" from thirteen different angles.

The distance between you and becoming an entrepreneur is both far and near. Once you touch the essence, you'll enter that category. The essence of decision-making for bosses is not to limit oneself. Problems often arise from our interactions with others. All bosses operate within an industry which essentially revolves around people. Industries and products are merely media used to bring these people together. Small bosses manage tasks, while big bosses manage people.

What potential energy do leaders use to stimulate motivation and dissolve resistance? Once this issue is resolved, all else will dissipate. For bosses, regardless of changes, the core remains unchanged. Even amidst constant change, they maintain control because they have touched the essence of things.

Why do we walk this path? Because we have touched and experienced something significant. China and the world are in a global village undergoing unprecedented transformation. In fixed times, bosses could learn a set pattern. However, in changing times, bosses must learn adaptability rather than sticking to fixed patterns. Your confusion stems from failing to break old thought systems and entering a new era, thus feeling lost.

Bosses have three fatal flaws: First, living with feudal thoughts in a democratic age; Second, living with home-bound thoughts in a globalized age; Third, living with agricultural thoughts in a market-driven age.

If your company's system and mechanisms remain unchanged, all learning becomes futile. Remember, reform always involves answering 'for whom' and 'how to distribute'. When people come together, it's about money and interests. Therefore, having a broad mind means sharing fame and profit with others.

The realm of managing people is using a group to influence another, rather than influencing a group yourself. This is a common lifelong misconception many cannot escape. Without a clear guideline, everything lacks a core, and without a core, nothing can grow. The guideline is why we establish companies, for whom we advocate, and for whom we exist! Understanding this allows us to transcend. Without understanding, transcendence is impossible.

Classes are meant to elevate a boss's realm, not teach how to make money. If I teach you mechanisms but your mindset hasn't changed, and you haven't genuinely wanted to improve life with your employees, nothing will be solved. Once you transcend and elevate your realm, everything becomes easier.

Managing a business is about managing impressions. Customers buy based on impressions. In business, the impression you give represents your entirety. The primary product is the boss themselves. The more people you can sell yourself to, the larger your team will become. The culture of a boss determines their strength and the kind of person they become. Specifically, the firmness of your belief, the determination of your boss, the size of your boss's dream, and the height your life reaches are directly related. Your aspirations determine your wisdom. A boss's culture defines who they are, and thus, what their company becomes. Culture itself is the essence of a company's existence. Corporate culture cannot be learned; it must be experienced by the boss and leadership or even all employees.

Why do we stagnate? Because we aim merely to escape poverty. Once escaped, momentum diminishes, and the enterprise loses steam. The force driving our development is limited, it satisfies and eventually depletes. When depleted, we become stagnant. Looking at great enterprises worldwide, none became great solely by pursuing maximum profit. All top enterprises started without considering profit and ended up achieving greatness. Regardless of who you are, your knowledge will never surpass your aspirations. Without increasing your aspirations, how can you increase your knowledge? If your aspiration is to help thousands live better lives, you will dream of ways to manage them successfully. If your scope is limited to thirty or three hundred, you will never master managing thousands, and this cannot be taught.

Why does a person constantly grow when starting a business? Because they genuinely want to solve problems. Making life better with our products is the soul of a business. Once this goal is firmly established, you won't disappear easily. Even if profits decrease temporarily, they will rebound strongly and even multiply. For Chinese private enterprises to develop sustainably, they must address commercial culture. In other words, when society appreciates and recognizes us, our businesses will grow.

Why do many companies fail to grow? Like growing trees, many bosses focus solely on upward growth, neglecting root development. Resultantly, they become like floating weeds. Today, I teach you to focus on deep roots. Once rooted deeply, upward growth happens naturally. People progress slowly due to seeking externally. Those progressing quickly seek internally. When you seek inwardly, you discover a boundless world of continuous improvement.

All masters follow a path to mastery, and later generations follow their lead. Hence, the difference between teachers and masters arises. Teachers bring students into their world, making them confused. Masters, however, enter the student's world, identifying their deficiencies and addressing them accordingly. Teachers aim for comprehension, whereas masters aim for awakening. Teachers fill your mind with knowledge, while masters foster growth within your mind. The essence of the Tao Te Ching is learning how Laozi wrote it. Can you turn his philosophy into a guide for business?

Any historical story or classic summarizes the context of its time. We should use their summarized thinking to deepen our current wisdom. Great cultures make people shine brilliantly in their time, motivating countless others to strive for excellence. This creates contemporary pulling power. Respecting past masters is important, but surpassing them is the true path of learning. Thus, today's heroes are the ultimate heroes. Treat figures like Li Ka-shing, Jack Ma, Yao Ming, Zhang Ziyi, or your company's top performers as modern sages. Any superior individual in any field can be your teacher. Embracing this mindset will propel you infinitely forward.

Willpower is the spiritual force of self-guidance. A self-guided person signifies maturity and the ability to generate internal motivation. Such individuals can progress rapidly and dramatically. The greatest teacher is always oneself. No matter how profound a classic, the wisdom and subtleties of everyday life surpass them. Historical classics merely summarize the mundane realities of their times. Thus, any classic or master serves as a reference. The core point is to learn from real-life experiences. Why do you prefer reading books or attending classes? Because they seem simpler, allowing direct absorption of others' summaries. Directly digesting the vast and complex reality is daunting. Thus, instead of self-reflecting, you absorb pre-digested knowledge. True wisdom comes from engaging directly with life's complexities.

Learning sparks new ideas and directions, prompting deeper personal reflection. Modern people suffer because they cling to conclusions. Experiencing life firsthand surpasses any conclusion. Engaging fully with the world reveals everything you seek daily. All your confusions will find ready answers. Yesterday's solutions are readily available if you immerse yourself. Standing on the sidelines leads to confusion. To succeed, you must commit fully. Your dedication to your cause determines your achievements.

Religion elevates belief into faith and then into conviction. Thus, bosses managing people start with belief. The hardest thing in the world is making someone truly believe. An employee believing in the product speaks convincingly, and a boss believing in the industry feels inspired. Without such belief, you're merely working physically or mentally, just surviving. To truly enter a career, one must transcend the mundane and enter the spiritual world. Employees work hard after eating and drinking, clearly for spiritual reasons. Thus, bosses should form spiritual communities with top-level staff, honor-based communities with middle-level staff, and interest-based communities with lower-level staff. Great bosses collaborate with employees on monumental tasks. Spirit endures over millennia, not material goods. Valuing material over spirit is tragic. National treasures should be the spirit of the nation, the most precious national treasure. A spirited person acts without needing proof, return, or explanation. Their actions flow purely from within.

Remember, I may not inspire you, but I might help you rediscover what everyone originally had. Over time, you've buried life's most valuable aspects and focused on less meaningful pursuits, leading to financial struggles. Bosses destroy themselves by losing their inner wildness. Once gone, everything fades. As a boss or entrepreneur, use your enterprise to serve the country and care for the people through business. Many fail by mistaking coincidence for inevitability. Starting from scratch, you succeeded despite lacking sophisticated methods or management theories. Learning wisdom means returning to that original state of creating from nothing.

The essence of heaven lies in two principles: First, being alive. So anyone who lives freely aligns with heaven. Second, continuity. Heaven operates continuously for billions of years without stopping. Humans, however, think and act intermittently, preventing alignment with heaven. When you act continuously, you align with heaven. Worshiping heaven means emulating its perpetual existence. Successful individuals burn brightly because they pursue authentic existence. Achieving great things simply means manifesting a true life state. The key isn't refining life but where life manifests. Great achievers ignite life at specific points. All capabilities are innate; no one adds anything. Only the original motivations hidden within each person need to be revealed. Everyone has their own path, leading them to their destination, not mine.

Isn't it scary how humans resist pain? When feeling down, instead of embracing the emotion, they try to avoid it. Your greatest suffering comes from wanting a pain-free life, which is impossible. When someone cries over a loved one's death, let them cry. Fully experience the sorrow; it's a form of life. Why control it? Observing people who live freely, you see they accept life as it is, treating every moment as ordinary. Be a god when needed, and a regular person otherwise. Mastery means appearing divine when necessary and disappearing seamlessly into life otherwise.

A boss can spiritually soar freely. Without limits, this state naturally manifests. Returning to one's original state unleashes infinite power. When I'm truly present, others feel the same authenticity. On stage, speaking truthfully makes me relaxed and impactful. Conversely, falsehoods tire me and lack impact. Thus, all fatigue stems from resisting truth. Observing relaxed individuals reveals their mastery. Among peers, those calm and composed likely possess high abilities. They exist independently, unaffected by surroundings, like independent planets. One must cultivate such independence to find happiness.

Interacting with students, I don't impose or add anything. I merely help them reveal their true selves. All great individuals simply live authentically, naturally. The realm a boss should enter is natural, simple living—often overlooked. Bring people back to this simplicity.

Humans live for hope. Without it, they perish. When managing something, instill eternal hope. A simple example: seeing a sprout on garlic in your kitchen, nurturing it instead of discarding it reflects love for life. Connect with nature, travel the world, and cultivate plants. Experiencing life ignites passion. Passion stems from connecting with life; its absence indicates disconnection. All demise begins with the loss of sensation. When you feel, acting or deciding carries immense power. Acting without feeling yields little. Buddhism says a second equals eternity because connecting with the external world brings beauty. Aligning with all brings a sense of existence. Living freely in the world absorbs infinite energy, enabling immediate effectiveness. The realm of a boss is waking up refreshed daily, free from yesterday's negativity. Don’t worry about becoming something; just exist and release fully. Results emerge naturally. Irrelevance generates wisdom, and detachment fosters insight. My heart is everything; everything is my heart. Truth requires no words; everything demands full commitment!
