British retired teacher claims to have photographed a UFO tracking an Air Force plane (picture included)

by anonymous on 2012-02-15 09:54:55

A retired teacher claims to have captured photos of an unidentified flying object "tracking" an RAF military aircraft. On August 4, according to the UK's Daily Mail, a retired teacher recently took photos of an unidentified flying object "tracking" an RAF Hercules military aircraft, reigniting the "UFO" mystery.

The report states that at 3:45 PM on July 22, Powell, aged 56, was working in his garden in Westbury when he accidentally noticed a mysterious spherical flying object in the sky following an RAF Hercules military aircraft preparing to land. Quickly reacting, he rushed into the house, grabbed his camera, and ran back to the yard, capturing the scene promptly.

It is reported that the unidentified flying object hovering above the military base did not have any flashing lights visible. However, under sunlight, the disc-like object shone brightly and continued to follow the military aircraft preparing to fly into the RAF station in Wiltshire.

Powell claimed that he has never believed in extraterrestrial objects, but he is certain that the object he saw was "tracking" the military aircraft. "My neighbor was also working in the yard at the time, and I wanted to call them over to look, but I didn't want to say 'there's a UFO in the sky,' so in the end, I said nothing."

He added that this sight was so rare, and he didn't expect the photos to turn out so well. "I am very curious to know exactly what this thing is." However, Powell's discovery remains a mystery to this day.

It is understood that the RAF station is a British military base where the bodies of deceased British soldiers are transported back via the Hercules military aircraft. However, there were no soldier transportation activities on the day Powell discovered the unidentified flying object.

Regarding this matter, a spokesperson for the UK Ministry of Defence stated that since they do not consider the discovery to pose a "potential threat," they will not conduct an investigation.

On the other hand, Russ Kellet (44), the vice-chairman of the UK UFO Association, provided an explanation for the unidentified object seen by Powell, seemingly lifting the veil on the "UFO" mystery.

This UFO researcher explained that cloud formations known as "lenticular clouds" are often mistaken for UFOs because these clouds form at high altitudes and resemble smooth lenses or flying saucers.

Nick Pope, another person who previously conducted UFO investigations for the Ministry of Defence, stated that the images captured by Powell are intriguing, but due to the blurry photos, no conclusions can be drawn.

However, he emphasized that regardless of whether it is a UFO or not, any object flying close to a military aircraft raises concerns, as it involves airspace security issues, and the Ministry of Defence must conduct an investigation.
