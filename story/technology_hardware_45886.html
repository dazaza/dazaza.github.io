Sony mylo2 begins selling

by yinyin on 2008-01-30 11:37:00

Today I found that Sony's Mylo2 appeared on its official website, and it has started to be released. The Mylo 2 device is equivalent to a small pocket PC, which can access the Internet through Wi-Fi hotspots for chatting and surfing online. It can also call free computers on Skype or chat via IM software including Google Talk, Yahoo Messenger, MSN and other chat software.

The improved web browsing performance, combined with the support of Adobe FlashLite 3, allows viewing YouTube videos, posting comments and messages on social networking sites, or running Flash games.

The Mylo 2 comes with a 3.5-inch LCD screen and supports 802.11b/g Wi-Fi. The Mylo 2 has 1GB of built-in storage space, and capacity can be expanded through Memory Stick Duo/PRO Duo slots, with a maximum support of 8GB.

The Mylo 2 device can also view emails from AOL, Google, Yahoo, etc., supporting formats such as MP3, ATRAC, WMA, AAC, MPEG4 AVC, JPEG, PNG, BMP, compatible with music purchased online, and capable of multitasking operations, allowing listening to music while surfing the web, supporting Windows Media DRM 10.

The Mylo 2 is equipped with a 1200mAh lithium-ion battery, providing 20 hours of music playback or 7 hours of video playback, with internet browsing lasting up to 6 hours and internet phone calls lasting up to 6 hours. It has a built-in microphone, a slidable QWERTY keyboard, and a 3.5mm headphone jack. It also has a built-in 1.3-megapixel camera, with dimensions of (130.8 x 20.7 x 64.6 mm) and a weight of 193g.

The above introduction is from ZOL.COM.CN.
