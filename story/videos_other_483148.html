Seeing Sun Xi and Shi Meng's painstaking efforts for the class graduation yearbook all along. As the monitor, I feel a bit ashamed, not only because I failed to call on the children of the journalism 2 class to finish this common memory that belongs to everyone as soon as possible, but also because I put it aside for a long time. Here, I apologize to everyone.

Today, it is about one year and two months away from graduation. Perhaps for me, it will be a little longer in terms of time. Suddenly, there is one less person staying in Beijing, Sun Xi went to Guangzhou for postgraduate study. Best wishes to Sun Xi, hope you and Old Wu are doing well in the south. And those children who are still drifting in Beijing, I hope you can adhere and persist.

Since last May when I left campus, I missed the opportunity to organize the last dinner gathering of the class. Whenever I mentioned this matter, I always felt guilty inside. Here, I thank Sister Feng and other classmates. Thank you for your sincere efforts, everyone has worked hard!

The "official" farewell dinner for graduation was on April 26th. I remember that day when we had reserved the table. (On August 1st, 2011, when I informed everyone that the gathering would be a "farewell dinner", many classmates didn't take it seriously.) However, for me, that gathering really became the "last time."

Everyone's last gathering was on June 26th. During the dinner that night, Ning Xiang called me, saying that when I return in early July, we will gather again. Apparently, everyone still thought I would come back. However, even after many classmates have left campus, I still broke my promise.

During this year's New Year's Day, I was fortunate enough to go to Beijing again. Due to the tight schedule, I didn't want to disturb several classmates in Beijing. Even for a small-scale gathering, it didn't happen.

Repeatedly breaking promises, I feel very ashamed.

If this passage appears in everyone's graduation yearbook, I think I must mention Journalism Class 2 of Grade 06 - this collective that gave me many memories. In 2006, I came to Beijing and entered Minzu University. When I first entered campus, my weak foundation in liberal arts made me cautious about many things, feeling inferior, timid, and at a loss, which initially made me hesitate about many things. After the dormitory allocation, I, the president, Old Wu, Grandpa Chuan, Grandpa Cong, and Little Xiang were assigned to the same dormitory. Except for me and Old Wu from the north, the other four were from the south. These four people from different places, with their distinct personalities, quickly became friends.

Later, during the first class meeting, I am still embarrassed by my broken Mandarin at that time. The life in the science class in high school made me accustomed to sitting among a group of men, and suddenly seeing so many beautiful girls made me nervous. As a result, I couldn't speak clearly during the self-introduction. Looking back now, it makes me laugh.

In the first-year classroom, exaggerating a bit, it was like a nightmare for me. Feeling lost and clueless about everything, I saw everyone actively participating in class, while I seemed to understand nothing about what the teacher said. I always hid in the corner of the last row, not speaking a word, quietly. No wonder Grandpa Cong later said, "I found that you could pretend back then!" Thus, the first year passed quietly. That year, I was particularly polite to everyone, without extra jokes or too many words. Many people said that in my first year, "I was especially cold, even a bit introverted."

In the second year, perhaps it was the year I started getting closer to everyone. Everything began with the class officer election that year. To be honest, although I had been a class monitor before university, I lacked both the strength and courage, and I never thought about being a university class officer. Unexpectedly, that day, many people pushed me up against my will. Just like that, I humorously became a class officer. And starting from that year, I got closer to every member of Journalism Class 2. From that year onwards, I also gained a unique nickname exclusive to my university years: "Uncle." Frankly speaking, I really liked this nickname.

In the third and fourth years, life seemed like a repetition of the first and second years, but I never felt that this collective was far from me.

In four short years, although everyone was busy with their own affairs during these four years, I always believed that Journalism Class 2 remained a highly cohesive collective from start to finish. Every collective activity, every class decision, I saw everyone's dedication, and each person wanted to make this collective better. We have excellent individuals, each of us has our own shining points, and we also have the confidence that other classes envy. These things are also the capital I have always been proud of for this collective.

I often think, where will we be in 10 years, 20 years, or perhaps even longer? What will we be like at that time?

In 10 years, 20 years, or perhaps even longer, whenever I think of that brief four-year period, every child of Journalism Class 2 (I like to call everyone that way) will always leap into my mind. I hope each of you will be doing well.

Finally, let me talk about myself now. Since leaving Tibet, last August, I returned to my hometown of Yulin, Shaanxi, and I am currently working as a journalist at the "Yulin Evening News". Compared to many classmates in broader media platforms, my work seems insignificant. But for me now, I really like this job, after all, it is close to my life. Regarding my future plans, if conditions permit, I still want to study for a few more years, and the location should be in Beijing.

I still want to use that sentence that I have used for a long time, a sentence that I believe the people belonging to this collective will never forget for life, to bless and express gratitude to this collective we once stayed together, this home that left me with beautiful memories: "Brilliant and brave, ruling over mountains and rivers. Historically the strongest, Journalism Class 2."

I love you all! Every student of Journalism Class 2, and those teachers who have always cared about and taught us knowledge and how to be good people. Wishing you and your families good health and all the best. Believe that your tomorrow will be better!
