HZ1 Both indoor and outdoor units cannot be turned on.

by uoj93my40 on 2011-11-24 22:30:37

Source: Beijing McQuay Air Conditioning Repair http://blog.163.com/mcquay_kongt/.

Analysis and Inspection: The primary and secondary voltages of the power transformer were both normal. It was found that the 102℃ 1A thermal protector connected in series in the secondary circuit had become open-circuited. After replacing this component, there was still no display. At this point, the voltage of TR1 (12V) was measured to be normal, while the voltage at pin 3 of IC2 was approximately 4.3V (it should normally be 5V), and the integrated block was noticeably overheating. When the load at pin 3 of IC2 was disconnected, a multimeter was used to measure the resistance between pin 64 of IC1 (MB88515B) and ground, which was only 1.5KΩ (it should normally be greater than 3.5KΩ), indicating a fault in the load. Upon inspection of IC6 (A2003), the resistance between its pin 12 and pin 9 was 2Ω, whereas it should normally be greater than 200Ω, suggesting internal damage to this IC. After replacement, when the machine was powered on for testing, the voltage at pin 64 of IC1 was 5V, which was normal, but the A2003 was still overheating upon touch. Further inspection revealed an abnormal voltage at the 12V input end of the solid-state relay SSR1 from pin 12 of IC6. When 12V voltage was directly externally applied to the input end of the relay, the contact status was measured and it was found that SSR1 could not conduct even with direct external power supply, indicating that the failure was due to SSR1 being ineffective. Since SSR is difficult to purchase, the Beijing McQuay Air Conditioning Repair Center replaced it with an MR301-12H relay, and the fault was resolved.
