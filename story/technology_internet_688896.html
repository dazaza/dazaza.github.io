In daily life, hemorrhoids, as a common and frequent disease, directly affect people's health. Experts from the Yangzhou Armed Police Hospital Anorectal Center pointed out: The harm of hemorrhoids can be big or small. If detected early and treated in time, it will not have much impact on health. However, if endured for a long time, the consequences may be unexpected. Experts in traditional Chinese and Western medicine from the Yangzhou Armed Police Hospital Anorectal Center recommend: Minimally invasive surgery is the best treatment for hemorrhoids.

Minimally invasive surgery has become the best method to treat hemorrhoids and is more favored by female patients. Experts from the Yangzhou Armed Police Hospital Anorectal Center introduced the main reasons as follows:

1. Small incisions, anal beauty preserved

Since PPH painless minimally invasive surgery for hemorrhoids is performed above the hemorrhoid, the anal cushion is completely preserved, ensuring normal defecation and gas discharge functions are unaffected. It also avoids complications such as anal stenosis, making it more consistent with human anatomy and physiology, preserving anal aesthetics, and saving patients the trouble of pursuing anal reshaping.

2. Minimal pain, small incisions, quick recovery

Female patients usually fear pain and have low tolerance. The anal area is the end of the digestive tract, where nerve endings below the dentate line are very abundant and sensitive. When stimulated by inflammation, ulcers, or surgery, severe pain can occur. Non-minimally invasive treatments result in large wounds and significant pain. The biggest advantage of minimally invasive surgery, and the reason it is most popular among female patients, is that the treatment process involves minimal pain, less bleeding, smaller wounds, mild pain during recovery, and no postoperative swelling or bleeding. Therefore, the recovery period is much shorter than with regular surgery.

3. No hospitalization required, outpatient treatment

Due to its short duration (within 30 minutes), minimal blood loss, and small incisions, patients generally do not need to be hospitalized for minimally invasive treatment and can complete the treatment on an outpatient basis. (If necessary, they may stay in the hospital for observation for one day.) They only need to return to the hospital regularly for dressing changes. This perfectly matches the psychology of women who prefer to spend the least amount of time solving the most embarrassing problems. However, regular dressing changes after minimally invasive hemorrhoid surgery are crucial. Only regular dressing changes can ensure that the wound does not become infected and heals on schedule. Compared to traditional surgery, this reduces the difficulty of nursing care.

4. Humanized treatment with minimally invasive technology

Compared to traditional methods, minimally invasive surgery has many advantages such as small incisions, less bleeding, and faster procedures. Hemorrhoid treatment has now entered the era of "minimally invasive treatment." Although the "four major minimally invasive techniques" for hemorrhoids represented by PPH originated abroad, the clinical application of minimally invasive techniques for hemorrhoids in our country has reached a relatively mature stage and is proven to be the safest treatment with the fewest side effects. Due to the conservative mentality unique to Eastern women, minimally invasive surgery is widely welcomed by female patients.

5. One-time treatment with no recurrence

For office workers, life and work pressures are already high. Without a healthy body, it can seriously affect work efficiency and income. In fact, any hemorrhoid surgery can only alleviate or eliminate symptoms but cannot prevent vascular congestion and dilation in the anus and rectum, which could lead to hemorrhoids. Minimally invasive treatment uses advanced technological instruments, and after one-time treatment, as long as proper care is taken, recurrence is generally avoided. This reduces the pain and increases the quality of life.

6. Can avoid [four major side effects]

The anus, though small, is an important and sensitive organ. Any misstep can cause various unforeseen problems. Hemorrhoid surgeries done in small clinics or non-specialist hospitals, due to insufficient equipment and doctor experience, can easily lead to serious bleeding, urinary retention, tissue necrosis, anal stenosis, and anal incontinence. Anal incontinence, although rare, once it happens, it brings irreparable losses to patients.

7. [Overall cost] is more economical

Because disposable imported equipment is used, the treatment costs for minimally invasive PPH surgery and hemorrhoid gun COOK are relatively higher. However, minimally invasive surgery does not require hospitalization, so non-medical insurance patients can save on hospitalization expenses; secondly, it saves over 20 days of hospitalization time. In this fast-paced society, these 20 days can create a lot of wealth. Overall, choosing minimally invasive treatment doesn't actually cost more money.
