What does it mean if there is a lump in the breast? Many female friends accidentally discover lumps in their breasts during daily life, and they often have such doubts. The appearance of breast lumps makes many women feel uneasy, suspecting whether they have breast cancer. In fact, experts from Xi'an Breast Disease Hospital believe that women must go to the hospital to find out the cause of breast lumps, only then can it be determined whether it is caused by breast cancer. So, what should you do if a lump is found in the breast? You will understand after reading the following content.

1. Fibrocystic changes: the most common benign change in the breast, also known as cysts, and its incidence increases with age.

2. Fibroadenoma: a benign tumor, more common in younger women; less common after menopause.

3. Breast cancer: the older you get, the more likely you are to develop it. Therefore, most breast cancer patients are women over 50 years old. In addition, factors that increase the risk of breast cancer in women include having a direct relative (mother/sister) who has had breast cancer, early onset of menstruation/late menopause, and never giving birth.

4. Inflammation: bacterial mastitis related to breastfeeding.

[What should you do if a lump is found in the breast? Clarify the treatment principle for breast lumps]

1. Fibrocystic changes: since tenderness is a common symptom, patients should avoid wearing tight clothes or bras; those with severe symptoms may require traditional Chinese medicine or even surgical treatment.

2. Fibroadenoma: the ideal treatment is surgical removal, and fibroadenomas do not increase the patient's risk of breast cancer.

3. Breast cancer: common treatments include surgery, chemotherapy, radiotherapy (radiation therapy), and endocrine therapy.

4. Breast inflammation: for simple inflammation, antibiotic treatment should effectively control it; more severe breast abscesses (pus in the breast) may require surgical drainage.
