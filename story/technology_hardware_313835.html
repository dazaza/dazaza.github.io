Inter Milan's Terrifying Star-Making Efficiency Faces First Obstacle in 3 Years; Mourinho's Obsession with One Aspect May Stifle Many Talents

by yinding999 on 2009-12-18 12:43:30

Inter Milan's frightening star-making rate encounters resistance for the first time in 3 years; Mourinho's obsessive preference for one type of player may stifle many gifted talents.

Sina Sports - "He is not a phenomenon, not the next Pele or the next Maradona chosen by God, but God clearly said that this child is a footballer."

Donati, the "child" mentioned in Mourinho's statement above, made his debut for Inter Milan's first team in last night's 1-0 victory over Livorno in the Coppa Italia round of 16. Mourinho said: "I have more faith in Donati. I've spoken with him because he has a passion for football, and it's not just verbal passion, as anyone can say they love football. Passion is shown through training and how much football occupies in life. He loves to train with the first team, even if it's just for a minute. With such passion, he can absorb any knowledge gained."

Regardless of how things were in England, at least since coming to Italy, Mourinho's criteria for judging young players always starts with whether they are low-key and pragmatic. This is the reason he strictly manages Balotelli and Santon, and also why he favors Klisinas and Donati. Therefore, it was no surprise to see a diligent and hardworking Donati trying his best not to make mistakes at Meazza last night.

It wasn't until the 29th minute when Donati delivered a cross from the flank for Suazo, who missed his scissor kick, marking his first true assist of the game. For most of the opening period, Donati rarely crossed the halfway line. Sneijder typically chose to combine with Balotelli in front of the penalty area or looked for Montari on the left. The right flank of Inter Milan often appeared vacant, an unusual occurrence. Of course, Donati doesn't need to be anyone's clone, nor does he need to imitate Maicon. However, during this season's league match where Inter Milan won 2-0 away against Livorno, Mourinho also started Klisinas, another player he liked for being "diligent," who only defended and didn't assist, and was substituted at halftime. Fortunately, Livorno did not put too much pressure on Inter Milan's defense today, otherwise, Donati might not have been able to play until the 82nd minute.

After winning four consecutive league titles, the Coppa Italia is no longer as important to Inter Milan as it was in the 2004-05 season. However, interestingly, in the past two seasons of the Coppa Italia, two young players launched by Inter Milan garnered significant attention. In the 2007-08 season, Balotelli made his first start for Inter Milan in a 4-1 Coppa Italia win over Reggina, scoring twice. In the 2008-09 season, Santon started in Inter Milan's Coppa Italia match against Roma, thus beginning to show promise. Now, Donati becomes the third young player developed by Inter Milan in three years of the Coppa Italia.

"This is a wonderful Christmas gift for him and all the young players," Mourinho did not admit that Donati performed averagely, "This is a message to all the kids. If they are honest, professional, and give their maximum effort, then they can participate in top-level matches."

Unlike Mancini, Mourinho, when nurturing young players, does not simply send out a full substitute lineup whenever there's a Coppa Italia match. He prefers to make opportunities precious to motivate the young players. "Honesty, professionalism, giving maximum effort," these are the standards he set for the Inter Milan youth team players, deliberately excluding "talent," which happens to be what Balotelli and Santon were recognized for. Mourinho's decision to promote young players in this way may bury many uniquely talented individuals in the future. Defending diligently in the back is definitely easier than making bold moves in the front. A good kid without a desire to perform or lacking sufficient confidence will likely end up closer to being a craftsman than a master. There's no need to cite many examples; there are two right beside Donati - Materazzi and Lucio.

In the 82nd minute, Mourinho replaced Donati with Maicon. The 19-year-old boy left the field smiling, with sporadic applause in the stadium. He should feel satisfied. Having joined Inter Milan from Luccese in the 2007-08 season, he had the opportunity to play for the first team after just 18 months. "Excited? Yes, but I was excited when I found out I was included in the squad. When I stepped onto the pitch at Meazza, I realized it was a completely different feeling, which made me incredibly excited." (Giotto)
