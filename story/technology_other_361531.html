Enterprises have serious environmental protection and safety hazards, and the accident enterprises have been rectified

by ditan360 on 2010-08-03 20:44:36

Carbon Finance - Around 10 o'clock in the morning on July 28, an explosion occurred at the former Plastic Plant Four of Nanjing city and triggered a big fire. The explosive power was huge, surrounding bungalows were flattened, houses within a 100-meter radius collapsed, windows of six or seven tour buses around the scene were all broken, many cars caught fire. Almost all glasses of a furniture mall located 200 meters away from the explosion site were shattered by the tremor. Two months ago, similarly in the region of Maigaoqiao, a propylene pipeline leaked and nearly caused a big explosion, and at that time thousands of residents nearby had been evacuated urgently.

Likewise, on July 28, floods flushed batches of raw material drums containing trimethylchlorosilane from Xinyaqiang Chemical Plant of Jilin Province into the Songhua River, and pungent smoke spread everywhere. Twelve days ago, an explosion occurred at Dalian's oil transmission pipeline, which triggered crude oil leakage. Fifteen days ago, Fujian Zijin Mining suffered a copper sulphuric acid leakage accident, which severely polluted some areas of the Tingjiang watercourse.

Are these sudden accidents happening in chemical petrochemical enterprises recently just coincidences or inevitable?

Reporters discovered after investigation that almost all these enterprises having accidents have been ordered to make rectifications.

As early as October 17, 2006, Jilin Xinyaqiang Biochemical Co., Ltd. was fined for environmental pollution hidden dangers and excessive sewage discharge without approval and being put into production arbitrarily. It was subjected to suspension for treatment and required to make up environmental evaluation procedures. On March 17, 2009, the company again experienced a big fire, with burning objects being toxic chemical substances such as hexamethyldisilazane, sulfuric acid, benzene, etc. After the accident, it was ordered to suspend production for rectification.

The location of the Dalian oil pipe explosion incident was also named during the environmental risk check in 06. Opinions were then given by the Environmental Protection Bureau. Moreover, there is a thick environmental assessment report on the storage tanks catching fire in this explosion incident.

Zijin Mining has the same problems. Being pointed out repeatedly but still not making any changes, eventually resulted in large-scale pollution, and had to bear serious consequences.

Facts prove that the occurrence of these accidents are largely due to human factors and not coincidental. If at the time when those warning accidents happened, local government environmental protection departments and safety supervision departments did not treat the environmental assessment results, calls for attention, and people's voices as over-worrying and ignore them because they did not cause serious consequences, but could take them seriously enough and strengthen treatment and rectification, would it be possible to avoid the reoccurrence of tragedy?

As early as 2006, Vice Minister Pan Yue of the State Environmental Protection Administration issued a warning: "Our country has entered a period of high incidence of environmental pollution accidents. With the continued rapid growth of our country's economy, in the relatively long period of time to come, layout-related environmental hazards and structural environmental risks will replace individual pollutions to become the No.1 threat to our country's environmental security".

However, four years have passed, and the environmental risk predictions by environmental protection departments on the chemical petrochemical industry have become footnotes of today's actual situation, while solutions to strengthen prior prevention seem to still need to be implemented.

Currently, environmental supervision in our country is weak, and enterprise illegal costs are very low. Enterprises violating regulations simply pay fines and settle things. Now there is no clear requirement for enterprise information disclosure regarding environmental protection, and it is hard for the public to supervise enterprises, which makes enterprise environmental protection safety management bury deep hidden dangers.

In foreign countries, enterprises discharging toxic and harmful substances into the environment need to regularly disclose emission data to society. Environmental assessment is not only a technical process, but also a process of balancing various interests. All groups whose interests may be affected will participate in the hearing discussion. Without this public participation procedure, the environmental assessment result will be invalid.

Due to the rapid urbanization process in recent years in our country, population density has sharply increased, and urban areas continue to expand. Even chemical zones far away from cities in the past will soon encounter extended urban areas. Conflicts will inevitably occur in limited resource space, chemical plants will get closer and closer to densely populated areas, sometimes even the minimum protective distance of 200 meters cannot be guaranteed, thereby greatly increasing public safety risks. Once something happens, it causes great casualties.

Human society is a risky society, and with the rapid development of society, risks are already everywhere. Now major environmental risks are further accumulating, and dangers are like "time bombs" existing. For controllable risks like chemical plant relocation, we still hope to play human roles, discard short-sighted thinking prioritizing private profits, and implement "environmental awareness" and "safety awareness" from words into planning.

Painful lessons have proven that if warnings about environmental risk assessments are taken seriously, many disasters can be avoided. Strengthening management and supervision is urgent. Enterprises must not treat environmental assessment as something they can ignore.
