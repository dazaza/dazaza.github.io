Butterfly's CangHai

Don't know if your mulberry fields are planted with yearning tea? I occasionally picked a leaf, and my calm heart was filled with deep longing. Thus, on that enchanting spring day, in that fragrant afternoon, I had bright eyes and graceful fingers. Your name appeared in my poems, deeply and shallowly, deeply and shallowly.

It seems like an appointment from a thousand years ago, countless days and nights of waiting, countless glances over shoulders, all for this encounter across mountains and waters!

I am like the pear blossom in April, one branch in the rain blooms as red as peach blossoms. On a night with flickering candlelight, I sit by the window under the moon. Who? The sound breaks my heart and enters my strange dreamland? A pillow of gentle water removes my makeup? Thinking we met like old friends in a distant place, henceforth I look forward to you. Who knew late autumn would mock my thin silk dress. The clear autumn water carries long dreams, facing the setting sun locks empty expectations! Wild geese fly south, wild geese fly south, you think and rethink! Expecting returning swallows, expecting returning swallows, I break my heart again and again! Peach blossoms fade the spring red, pear blossoms make the sunset slim. Two pools of spring sorrow fall into the dream lake, two pupils reflect broken hearts.

I am also like the fish in your lotus pond, weaving through your umbrella-like green shade unwilling to leave, swimming through four seasons of lingering lotus fragrance, and I still stand gracefully in the center of the water, waiting for you, waiting for you, waiting for you! Only water knows how my tears have dyed the lotus' attire green, even when my tears dry up, I refuse to leave. I don't want to leave, I don't want to leave, just to be with you and guard the bright moonlight together, watching dragonflies dance for us under the moon...

Not knowing how the lanterns illuminate your world, not knowing how many dreams stars have shaken off. You still rush about, letting my tears mix with your pain, scorching you a thousand miles away, like fallen leaves, drifting and drifting.

Often heartbroken after a heavy rain, watching how the paulownia leaves fall, scattering the ground with emotional sorrow. No matter how I hum "Dong Feng Po," it never comes out right, and the person singing yellow flowers on the chrysanthemum platform is nowhere to be found? I know, I know, you are like the most poisonous charm in Miao territory, gnawing at my heart and devouring my bones. I am still willing to be like an evening flower, blooming for you with instant brilliance.

My bright eyes are charming because of you, my heart is bitter because of you. I still pour my autumn thoughts onto my heart for you. I know, I am like the butterfly that can't cross the CangHai, having lost everything for you...

"Leaves gather then scatter, cold crows settle then startle, when will our longing and meeting be?" Heaven knows, heaven knows, a pure heart shared with you!

When it's quiet at night, sitting alone at the dressing table, I seem to still be the clear and ethereal woman who first met you, gazing every night in anticipation, waiting for you to take my hand. When my longing turns the night into a touch of green, do you know, do you know? The sky waits for misty rain, and I wait for you?

Your hurried steps hurt my heart; your distant dreams lengthen my gentle waters; your dense sorrows cool my clear autumn...

Do you know, do you know, do you know? I am your heart butterfly, and you are my CangHai...
