Gubaiyou Guilin Rice Noodles

by longersing on 2009-05-22 02:23:56

Guilin has three treasures: chili sauce, fermented tofu, and three flowers wine. But do you know that there is a brand representative of Guilind that makes Guilin people proud? That's right, it's Guilin rice noodles.

In the streets and alleys of Guilind, there are rice noodle shops everywhere. Unlike rice noodle shops in other places, some Guilin rice noodle shops require you to buy tickets first and then get the noodles, while others require you to pay when you take the noodles. The price is calculated by liang (a unit of weight). Generally, one liang of rice noodles costs 2 yuan, two liang costs 2.5 yuan, and three liang costs 3 yuan, and so on. If it's plain rice noodles, the price is reduced by 0.5 yuan accordingly. When taking the noodles, tell the worker whether you want braised noodles or soup noodles. Most people choose these two types, but occasionally someone will buy beef brisket noodles, lettuce noodles, and horse meat rice noodles.

Through the history of Guilin rice noodles and their production techniques and ingredients, you can see that they can cool you down in summer and warm you up in winter. They are truly a healthy food made from the best grains.

When buying rice noodles, the worker will put the noodle ball into a perforated ladle, then immerse it in hot water, and stir it with chopsticks. Once the rice noodles are cooked, they are immediately poured into an enamel bowl marked "Guilin Tableware Disinfection Center". Another person is responsible for adding the braising liquid, beef, etc. After this process is completed, the customer can take away the rice noodles. On the side, there is a table with green onions, chili peppers, and other condiments where guests can add the condiments and soup (usually made from pork bones), take the chopsticks, and find a seat in the shop to eat.

Braised dish noodles

Braised dish noodles are the most commonly eaten by Guilin people, but the most distinctive ones are horse meat rice noodles. Horse meat rice noodles are only available in winter because horse meat spoils easily in hot weather. Usually, one liang of rice noodles is served in five specially made extremely small and exquisite bowls, accompanied by horse meat soup and fresh horse meat. Some people may have already eaten more than ten bowls, and the small bowls have been stacked into a pile, which is really quite interesting.

Memories of Guilin Rice Noodles

When I was a child, my grandfather often picked me up from kindergarten to eat dumplings, but every time we passed by a rice noodle shop, I wanted to eat a bowl of Guilin rice noodles, but I couldn't. Because there was chili, Guilin rice noodles have chili powder, very spicy, very fragrant, and very delicious. Every time I passed by, I drooled. I hope I grow up quickly, grow up to one day when I can eat spicy food, and go to eat that sweet and delicious Guilin rice noodles.

My cousin's family business: "A Niu Sao"

Rice noodles are the most important part of Guilin people's lives. You will know when you come to Guilin, every street, every road, has Guilin rice noodle shops of different sizes, operating from morning till 10 p.m., and many even operate 24 hours a day. Guilin rice noodle shops outnumber fast-food restaurants, which shows the status of Guilin rice noodles in the hearts of Guilin people.
