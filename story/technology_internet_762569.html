"The Inevitable 'Death of Yougou': Belle's E-commerce Has Many Fatal Flaws"

by anonymous on 2013-07-24 16:08:13

Recently, Yougou.com, which has been severely affected by the departure of senior executives, suffered another blow. On July 17th, it was confirmed by an insider from Yougou that COO Zhang Xiaojun will be leaving and is currently in the process of completing his resignation procedures. As one of the founding members of Yougou, Zhang Xiaojun's departure is more significant, indicating that Yougou is indeed facing a serious crisis.

Let’s review the e-commerce journey of Belle International, to which Yougou belongs:

Belle quietly tested the waters of e-commerce several years ago, developing steadily through three channels: Taobao distribution, Tmall, and B2C distribution (B2C distribution was completed through Taoxiu.com). Led by veteran group member Xie Yunli, this not-so-luxurious e-commerce team started by selling products under Belle's own brands, then gradually launched exclusive online models, followed by new online brands. This development path was considered a successful case of traditional brand enterprises entering e-commerce before 2011, surpassing Li-Ning.

With the rise of Letao and Haolemai in 2010, which received over $100 million in financing and developed rapidly, Belle Group became restless. On one hand, they were worried about being controlled by the channel again; on the other hand, after evaluation, they found themselves had great development advantages in the e-commerce field, including:

One, numerous brands: In addition to Belle's own several women's shoe brands, the agency's international well-known sports brands - Adidas, Nike, etc., are growing at an astonishing rate in online sales;

Two, easier category expansion: For Belle Group, which mainly focuses on women's shoes and agency well-known sports brands, how to achieve absolute market advantage and status through multi-brands and multi-categories has been the most thought-provoking issue in recent years. The way of expanding brands and categories by opening physical stores is obviously too slow, while the ongoing e-commerce business can easily expand brands but cannot quickly expand categories;

Three, financial advantage: During cooperation with e-commerce enterprises, Belle found that e-commerce is a capital-intensive business. With its financial advantage, Belle can develop without relying on capital, thus defeating more vertical e-commerce companies that must rely on capital, becoming the "survivor as king";

Four, talent advantage: Through years of e-commerce practice, Belle already has a highly professional and execution-focused e-commerce team. The cultivation of talents can cope with the scale development of e-commerce business or even higher;

Five, supply chain advantage: This made Belle exceptionally excited internally and is also the core reason for attracting high-level executives from brand e-commerce or platform e-commerce. After all, the supply chain always fascinates people in e-commerce because it seems to determine life and death apart from funds. The supply chain includes a series of "business" contents such as product research and development, production, warehouse management, delivery, and after-sales service. In traditional enterprises that value "business" more than "electricity", this is considered the most competitive advantage over e-commerce enterprises.

Based on these advantages, Belle e-commerce decided to make a big move, first purchasing the double-character domain name yougou.com,

abandoning the slightly counterfeit "topshoes.cn" Taoshop directly for redirection, stopping supply to all distribution websites, while retaining direct third-party online shops like Taobao and Tmall Mall but reducing investment and attention. High salaries and high profiles were used to hire JD VP and Vancl VP as CMO and COO respectively, along with the dispatch of group veterans as CEO, forming a golden triangle. They attempted to build a full-category vertical B2C mall, implementing a multi-brand strategy on one hand, and expanding into multiple categories like clothing on the other, realizing the dream of a department store empire impossible offline!

Everything sounded perfect, but every dream needs to face the hard reality. The actual situation is:

One, higher technical requirements for independent B2C: Being based in Shenzhen headquarters, Belle couldn't recruit more excellent technical talents, and technical outsourcing services companies are scarce in Shenzhen. However, technology determines user experience, back-end management efficiency, and more reliable data. Independent B2C belongs to dynamic technology, different from relatively static technologies like ERP, continuous changes remain a hurdle many traditional enterprises can't overcome;

Two, increased management difficulty due to category expansion: Someone once said doing well in women's shoes doesn't mean doing well in men's shoes, something Belle should deeply feel when expanding into men's shoes. Expanding into women's clothing and accessories within a year can imagine what kind of difficulties the backend will encounter. For traditional brand enterprises accustomed to meticulous planning, this rapid approach was effective during Vancl's special period, understood within Vancl and JD's systems, but whether Belle, although having Vancl's COO and JD's CMO, can be understood and executed with "exaggerated costs" remains questionable;

Three, system: Many people who haven't crossed over between e-commerce and traditional enterprises find it hard to understand, bluntly speaking, the content including department settings (organizational structure), approval, finance and budget, reporting methods, corporate culture, communication methods, execution methods, risk control, processes and systems constitute an enterprise's so-called "mechanism". The result of this conflict is that e-commerce tries to persuade and accept the "system" of e-commerce, while the traditional team tries to understand but can never fully grasp the "system" of e-commerce, leading to low efficiency and instability of the execution team. Ultimately, the chairman or board might say: "E-commerce and traditional enterprises each have their strengths and weaknesses, the future must be integrated, so in these aspects, you must integrate..." If this so-called "fusion without harmony" appears, it will be called management innovation, and thus the "CEO rotation system" actually appeared in a company like Belle!

It was originally thought that Yougou, Belle's e-commerce arm, was far ahead, and the senior executives were waiting for a spin-off IPO to enjoy success and wealth, becoming a glorious example of many traditional enterprises succeeding in the e-commerce era, instead of being caught up in these issues.

After all, whether it's the veterans who grew up with Belle or the e-commerce professionals poached, it can be seen that they didn't leave due to lack of ability. When the VPs from JD return to JD, they can continue to perform outstandingly, and when the VPs from Vancl have the courage to start businesses. So are these hard injuries the three points mentioned above? Or are these not only Belle's hard injuries but also the hard injuries of many traditional enterprises attempting to enter e-commerce?
