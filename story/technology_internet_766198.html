Apple and Sony smartphones change their reserved attitude and show goodwill to the Chinese market.

by anonymous on 2013-09-08 19:43:57

Welcome to China!

Many foreign mobile phone manufacturers hope to hear this sentence more than ever. As China becomes the largest smartphone market in the world, companies that originally did not pay much attention to the Chinese market have begun to change their attitudes ——

Recently, Apple specially sent out invitations to Chinese media and will hold a new product launch event alone in China on September 11th. This is the first time for Apple to hold such an event in China —— almost synchronized with the new product launch event held at its headquarters in the United States on September 10th, excluding time difference factors.

Yesterday, just hours after Sony launched its new flagship phone Xpeira Z1 in Berlin, Germany, it also held a launch event for the phone in Beijing and announced that China would be the first market for the phone's global release.

With Sony and Apple successively starting to hold new product launches in China, many people can't help but wonder: why is this happening?

Poor market performance

According to Apple's second quarter financial report, Apple's revenue in the China region was $4.6 billion in the second quarter, down 14% year-on-year and down 43% quarter-on-quarter. Although Apple phones still maintain a high-end image in China, they only hold a 5% share of the Chinese smartphone market, far behind their 14.2% global market share, and even further behind their competitor Samsung.

Sony phones have also successfully made the list of the five major players who have failed in the Chinese market, gradually losing vitality in the Chinese market.

Meanwhile, according to the latest data from market research company Canalys, the shipment volume of smartphones in mainland China in the second quarter was 88.1 million units, exceeding one-third of the global total shipment volume, growing by 108% year-on-year, making it the region with the highest growth rate and the largest sales volume in the world. By 2017, the scale of China's smartphone market is expected to soar to $117 billion.

On one hand, there are unsatisfactory results, and on the other hand, there is such a huge pie. These two major mobile phone manufacturers are indeed worried and have no choice but to focus on the Chinese market.

Changing attitudes and valuing China

Currently, Apple has added features like the nine-grid input method, flashlight, control center, Tencent Weibo, etc., in iOS7 beta4. Earlier versions of iOS had already integrated local applications like Sina Weibo and AutoNavi Maps. Apple CEO Tim Cook made his third visit to China in August this year, and Apple also plans to recruit over 200 employees in the China region... These phenomena indicate Apple's increasing emphasis on the Chinese market. It is understood that Apple may release the most "Chinese characteristic" TD-LTE version at the launch event on September 11th.

Based on the information we have gathered, Sony has also started the process of reshaping its sales channels in China: 1) Accelerate the construction of open sales channels, aiming to cover all Sony Store official direct sales stores within the country, as well as important mobile phone sales stores; 2) Channel penetration, cooperate with regional chain channels to enhance coverage in cities below the third and fourth tiers; 3) Strengthen cooperation with the three major operators, gradually entering the direct sales stores of the three major operators; 4) Implement the One Sony corporate strategy, strengthen integration with other Sony products and channels domestically; 5) Fully strengthen cooperation with major domestic e-commerce websites, and e-commerce will become an important sales channel for Sony phones in China in the future.

At the same time, to resist the impact of domestic mobile phone manufacturers like Lenovo, Huawei, Xiaomi, ViVO, etc., Sony also stated that it will customize products specifically for the Chinese market, aiming to cover the three different price segments of high, medium, and low in China.

If the above enumeration cannot give you a more intuitive feeling, then at the recently concluded Sony Xpeira Z1 Beijing launch event, Huang Guoqiang, the newly appointed president of Sony Mobile China, made his debut —— he is the first Chinese president of Sony Mobile China.

Other manufacturers are also unwilling to fall behind. It is understood that Samsung phones, which already occupy more than 30% of the Chinese market share, have confirmed that they will customize 4G phones for China. LG phones, ranked just behind Apple and Samsung globally in the smartphone rankings but described by the media as "winning the world but losing China," have announced their partnership with JD.com to accelerate their entry into the Chinese market.

In the latest domestic smartphone market share report, domestic brands almost occupy half of the market. This achievement is inseparable from the efforts of domestic mobile phone manufacturers themselves, but we must also see another reason: many international mobile phone brands did not give enough attention to the mainland mobile phone market in the past.

Attitude may not decide everything, but attitude is the beginning of all efforts. When companies like Apple and Sony realize some truths, this market will become more interesting.
