The causes of abnormal uterine bleeding are introduced by the gynecological experts of Zhengzhou Anti-aircraft Hospital. Abnormal uterine bleeding refers to abnormal blood loss and menstrual cycle disorders. Many women don't understand why sometimes their menstrual flow is light, while other times it's heavy, with irregular monthly periods. Therefore, this issue is highly regarded by women. Below, our hospital experts will provide a detailed explanation:

Clinical manifestations of abnormal uterine bleeding include: heavy menstruation, irregular bleeding, continuous bleeding that doesn’t stop, bleeding before or after periods or between periods, post-coital bleeding, bleeding after menopause, postmenopausal bleeding, and increased vaginal discharge accompanied by bleeding. About 20% of adults experience abnormal uterine bleeding, and approximately 50% of women have experienced such abnormalities at some point. Therefore, abnormal uterine bleeding is a common gynecological disease. Long-term blood loss can lead to anemia, weakness, decreased resistance, making one more susceptible to diseases, thus affecting women's physical and mental health.

There are many reasons for uterine bleeding, such as uterine lesions like fibroids, cervical diseases, malignant uterine tumors, etc. If the uterus is normal, irregular shedding of the endometrium may also cause bleeding due to hormonal imbalances caused by excessive stress, fatigue, environmental changes, metabolic diseases, malnutrition, etc. Long-term use of anticoagulant drugs and hormone replacement therapy after menopause may also lead to abnormal uterine bleeding.

Causes of uterine bleeding in reproductive age

Uterine bleeding during reproductive age differs from that during puberty. During the reproductive years, ovarian function is already well-developed, and pregnancy, childbirth, and contraception processes need to be experienced. Abnormal uterine bleeding can be caused by various factors, such as miscarriage or contraception, which can lead to bleeding. It is important to seek medical attention promptly for examination and medication adjustment, which often yields good results. The general reproductive age ranges from 18-48 years old.

Causes of uterine bleeding after menopause

After one year of amenorrhea, the menopausal phase begins. Any bleeding at this stage is considered abnormal uterine bleeding. Common causes include atrophic vaginitis, benign uterine diseases, hormone replacement therapy, and malignant tumors. Menopause lasts about 30 years, and if any bleeding occurs during this time, it is necessary to seek medical treatment promptly.
