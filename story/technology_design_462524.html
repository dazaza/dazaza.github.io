Traffic police were attacked by people claiming to be from the office, injured and hospitalized, and had their uniforms stripped off.

by zxyasdk60 on 2011-07-14 13:15:35

Traffic police were attacked and sent to hospital while checking drunk driving.

By Chief Reporter Shen Zichong / Text by Reporter Shen Xiang

At around 13:00 yesterday, a dispute occurred 700 meters east of the intersection of Zhengzhou's Chemical Road and West Fourth Ring Road. The identities of both sides involved in the argument were quite special. One side was traffic police on duty checking for drunk driving, while the other side claimed to be from the "Gouzhao Office!" Both sides had people injured during the scuffle.

● Traffic Police Attacked While Checking Vehicles

At around 14:00 yesterday, I saw Traffic Police Officer Huang Ming from the Ninth Brigade lying on a hospital bed in the emergency room of the PLA 153 Hospital in Xushui Town. There was a diagnosis report by his bedside, which stated that he suffered from palpitations due to external injuries.

According to Officer Fang, Huang Ming's teammate, they were driving through Chemical Road when at 13:30, they saw two officers from the Second Brigade conducting checks on the road. During the inspection of a Songhuajiang minivan, they were surrounded by five or six men. "Upon seeing our colleagues under siege, we went forward to inquire and mediate, but they refused to cooperate. We requested the minivan driver to undergo an alcohol test, which was refused. Our attempt to legally seize the vehicle was also resisted. They kept shoving us, and Huang Ming was pushed down flat on his back, hitting the back of his head on the ground. He couldn't stand up immediately. It wasn't until the backup police arrived that the situation was brought under control. Both Huang Ming and Officer Li Liang from the Second Brigade were injured. Li Liang's hand was badly scratched," Officer Fang said.

Officer Fang also mentioned that when the other party attacked the police officers, they did not retaliate. He even used his mobile phone to record the scene.

Jiang Liude, an officer from the Second Brigade who was on duty with Li Liang, said that their random checks on drunk driving were routine. At that time, this minivan was speeding very fast, and they signaled it to stop. After the car stopped, the driver, who smelled strongly of alcohol, refused to get out of the car and did not show any identification.

Jiang Liude said that the driver was making phone calls to call for help, and since they couldn't control the situation, they requested reinforcements via radio. Luckily, colleagues from the Ninth Brigade arrived just in time.

● "Driver" Denies Driving

In the hospital emergency room, I met Mr. Yu, who was sitting in the minivan at the time. The police identified him as the driver of the minivan involved in the incident. "I wasn't the one driving then," Mr. Yu said that he is a staff member of the Gouzhao Office in Zhengzhou's High-tech Development Zone. He was very tired after doing demolition work in the morning and had lunch at a restaurant on Chemical Road. "I drank alcohol, and my colleagues all advised me not to drive. My colleague Liu Tao was driving."

Mr. Yu pointed to a small blood wound on his hand and said that after the car was stopped by the police, "The police insisted that I was the driver. I was afraid that the police would seize and tow the car, so I pulled out the car key. Then four people suddenly rushed at me to grab the key, including one in civilian clothes. The police grabbed me and twisted me down to the ground, swelling my wrist."

At around 15:00, I arrived at Shifo Town Police Station where a Wang surnamed man was being investigated by the police.

Deputy Chief Chen of the police station introduced that Wang was suspected of pushing down the police officer and was still sobering up. "He drank a lot, so we asked him to wash his face with cold water, but he was still very agitated and couldn't take the statement." It was understood that Wang confessed that Mr. Yu was driving at the time of the incident, not Liu Tao. The Second Brigade of the Traffic Police has already seized the involved vehicle and is further handling the matter.

● Police Humiliated in the Hospital

I contacted the staff of the Gouzhao Office and learned that Mr. Yu is indeed a staff member of the office, but the vehicle belongs to him personally, and his actions were personal behavior.

At nearly 19:00 yesterday, I contacted Huang Ming by phone and learned that he was still lying in the hospital bed. Huang Ming said, "They are still causing trouble. There are many of them, and the police station can't do anything. They obstructed my treatment. When I was alone in the emergency room just now, they came over to humiliate me. They pulled off my blanket, unbuttoned my police uniform, cursed at me, took photos of me, and said they would post them online to make me lose face." Source: Oriental Today
