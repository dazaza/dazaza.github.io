29 Things You Shouldn't Care About Before Age 30

1. Wandering - Wandering is not pitiful, but a kind of experience. While you don't have family to support and while you are still healthy, if not now, then when will you wander?

2. Stability - Caring about stability before age 30 only has two possibilities: either you won the lottery or you're prematurely aging.

3. Evaluation - The last thing we should do is change ourselves because of someone else's evaluation. Because those who point fingers at you don't even know what rules they follow themselves. Don't just follow rules to do things, rules are still being created. Make decisions based on your own judgment for every matter, although it might be more troublesome.

4. Maturity - Don't be afraid that people say you're naive, this just proves that you're still young and full of energy. "Maturity" is a scary word, also a harmful word. Maturity and naivety are the most inappropriate, irresponsible, and useless summaries of a person. Those mediocre people, no one would say they're naive. When one day you're pressed by life and become old, people will definitely say you've matured, and you'll understand what "mature" really means.

5. Adaptation - In a rock song, there's a line: "This city changed me, this city doesn't need me." Don't blindly adapt to your environment, because it's very likely that this environment itself is no longer suitable for social development.

6. Failure - My teacher once told me, a person must at least go through heartbreak in love, failure in career, and mistakes in decision-making to grow up. Don't say failure is the mother of success, the earlier failure comes, the better. If failure happens after age 30, 40, some things might never happen again.

7. Superficiality - If you cry every time you watch "Titanic" and laugh uncontrollably every time you watch "A Chinese Odyssey," some people might call you superficial. Actually, it only shows that your nerves are still very sensitive, responding quickly to even subtle stimuli. When your senses become dull, people will say you're profound.

8. Celebrities - Not that you shouldn't care, but you can't care too much. Celebrities in a commodity society are consumer goods. After spending money, listening to songs, watching movies, no matter how good celebrities perform, it's just getting your money's worth, not worthy of admiration. It's like buying a dress for fifty dollars from a street stall, and everyone guesses it costs eight hundred dollars. Would you admire this dress then?

9. Cost - Not ignoring cost, but understanding that doing anything requires a cost. For us at this age, this is not an empty talk. Otherwise, by age 30, you'll realize how much you've paid without knowing why, and unclear about how much you've gained and what you've gained.

10. Loneliness - This is the price for freedom.

11. Mistakes - This is the patent of youth.

12. Failure - Including emotional, career-related, or perhaps just spending regretful money today and not buying something satisfying, or friends having a lively conversation while you can't join in. Being overly concerned with feelings of failure isn't mocking oneself with bad luck, nor punishing oneself with others' mistakes.

13. Flaws - Maybe you're short, maybe you're not good-looking, maybe your voice sounds like Donald Duck... Then your advantage is that you won't be distracted by shallow highlights on the surface, spend less time, take fewer detours, directly discover your inner advantages, and directly tap into your deeper potential.

14. Misunderstanding - If out of malice, then explaining is useless; if out of goodwill, then no explanation is needed. Specifically mentioning "misunderstanding" is not because one gets misunderstood more often before age 30, but because people at this age worry more.

15. Lies - This is a contagious disease, silence is the best vaccine. Unless you can find the source of infection, explaining just becomes the ideal condition for virus transmission.

16. Madness - This is the best psychological adjustment for young people, indicating you're energetic and physically healthy. Saying you're "crazy" is a malicious evaluation from some middle-aged people who are suppressed by life and exhausted mentally. They're like long-out-of-repair machines, needing adjustment the most, but can only make minor adjustments. One major repair could make them break down.

17. Pressure - How much pressure middle-aged people can bear tests their resilience; how much pressure young people can bear unleashes their potential.

18. Studying abroad - It might be an opportunity, or it might be a trap. Unless since the moment you took the college entrance exam, you had this goal, otherwise, your attitude towards studying abroad should be like towards love: strive for it as much as possible, but let success or failure be up to fate.

19. Salary - Just working for someone else, no matter how high the salary is, it can't be too high. So before age 30, opportunities are far more important than money, career is far more important than money, and future is far more important than money. For most people, the main purpose of starting a business before age 30 is not to make money, but to create a future.

20. Savings - This isn't necessarily because we have little money. Young people nowadays all know that money has life. With so many opportunities and such good conditions, you can use money to get a mortgage, do today's work, spend tomorrow's money; you can also use money to invest, and use money to "recharge". Money is only money during its circulation process, otherwise, it's just a pile of the world's highest-quality waste paper.

21. Housing - Unless you buy a house for appreciation, or you're getting married. I have a classmate whose home is local. After graduating from university, his unit didn't have dormitories, so his family bought him a house. He once had the chance to work in Beijing, but he thought it was unreasonable to leave the city right after buying a house, so he gave up. Now, his work is stable, but he's achieved nothing. His only achievement is getting married and having a child, because he felt the house shouldn't be empty forever, so the house turned into a home. Houses are metaphors for urban life, this metaphor shouldn't be related to us too early.

22. Age - Girls start hiding their age after 25, but actually, there's no need. Nowadays, youth extends to 45 years old, so what's 25?

23. Caring - This is a state of mind where one can neither pick up nor put down. Its opposite is not giving up, but being unrestrained and free-spirited, always maintaining a revolutionary optimistic spirit.

24. Unemployment - Tasting unemployment before age 30 is certainly an unfortunate thing, but it's not necessarily a bad thing. Fixing on a career too early might be the biggest misfortune. Unemployment might remind you of a dream buried for a long time, might awaken potentials you yourself didn't even know, or might force you to dream if you originally had no dreams.

25. Fashion - Don't chase fashion. Youth should be the most fashionable, but independent thinking and individual life are more important. In this material society, the pursuit of fashion has already become the pursuit of money. Today, fashion is synonymous with greed and worldly desires.

26. Style - This is a petit-bourgeois thing, "petit-bourgeois" this word is popular again today. Pursuing style is their exclusive right. Petit-bourgeois say that having style needs four elements: wisdom, cultivation, confidence, and money. Style is understanding "nobility" as dressing, temperament, preference taste, and interior decoration. That is, rough people only show vulgar speech, "petit-bourgeois" already have the ability to vulgarize their souls. Mainstream concepts aren't necessarily alternative, alternative has become the mainstream concept of young people today. Today, being old-fashioned seems alternative. The key is that contemporary society is an era of creating concepts, not an era of attacking outdated concepts.

27. Giving up - The opposite of grasping is giving up. Choosing one opportunity equals giving up all other possibilities. When new opportunities come, dare to give up everything you've already obtained. This isn't failing at the last hurdle, nor halfway abandonment, but seeking greater development space. Or doing nothing, just because you like it this way. Because youth is the greatest opportunity. People only have this courage, capital, and qualification before age 30.

28. Breakup - Not that you don't care, but you can't afford to care. What young people fear losing most before age 30 isn't what they already have, but dreams. If love is just a process, then it's exactly what should be experienced at this age. If you need to bear the results, after age 30, you might have more ability and qualifications. Actually, there are many things we need to do before age 30. Drowning too long in a dried-up riverbed of love is inconsistent with the rhythm of life at this age.

29. Divorce - Not that you don't care, but everything is still in time. A 38-year-old female friend has been married to her husband for 15 years, fought cold wars for 13 years, and finally separated. She said: "If later unwillingness to divorce was for the children, then my refusal to agree to his first proposal of divorce, looking back now, I really don't know why." If we had broken up earlier, my life wouldn't be like this today. Starting anew now, I always feel everything is too late.
