Disney will drive hotel reshuffle in Shanghai Pudong

by alexsgxu on 2009-11-12 01:09:58

Disney to shake up hotel sector in Shanghai's Pudong

By Xu Shuguang, Chairman of the Board of Directors of Green Tree Inn and 519.com Wine Direct Sales Network

Yesterday morning, the Shanghai Municipal People's Government News Office announced that the report on the application for the Shanghai Disney project has been approved by relevant national departments. The decade-long "romance" of the Shanghai Disneyland project has finally come to an end, leading to a marriage celebrated with jubilation across all sectors.

It is reported that the initial investment for the Shanghai Disneyland project will exceed 10 billion RMB, and it is expected to stimulate related industries worth hundreds of billions of RMB. Industries such as construction, hotels, transportation, exhibitions, and creativity will directly benefit from this project. At the same time, this project will form an "east-west resonance" with the 2010 Shanghai Expo Park, directly boosting the rapid development of modern services in Shanghai. Without a doubt, the world's largest Disney theme park, once completed, will directly attract and serve a vast Chinese tourism consumption population.

Orlando, USA, was originally an obscure, closed-off, and backward agricultural town, but with the construction of Disney World, it became a global star-level tourist city virtually overnight, at its peak receiving an average of 68,000 visitors per day. Disney not only owns theme parks and animation products; every Disney location also has more than one hotel. These hotels are filled with Disney cartoon elements, offering guests fairy-tale weddings and allowing them to experience different styles of taste—whether fashionable, nostalgic, or romantic.

The latest statistics show that in 2008, the total number of tourist hotels in Shanghai's Pudong New Area was 868, including 162 star-rated hotels and 706 non-star-rated small hotels. They provide 48,634 rooms and 79,045 beds. Despite the overall quantity of existing hotels and beds being considerable, many analysts and hotel investors are still flocking in, hoping to grab a share of the massive "Disney" pie.

We believe that within the next four to five years, with the completion of the Disney Park, the hotel industry in Pudong will inevitably undergo a major reshuffle. I think this reshuffle will significantly reduce the survival space of socialized small hotels, which currently account for more than 80% of the hotel market, replaced by the further expansion of chain brands and the rise of fashionable boutique hotels. Green Tree Inn places great importance on its development in the Pudong New Area of Shanghai. We have already built a hotel inside the Shanghai Expo Garden, and recently several hotels have opened consecutively in Pudong. In the future, we do not rule out building a hotel near the Disney Park to meet market demand. With the convening of the Expo and the launch of the Disney project, the development potential of the Pudong area in Shanghai is very strong. For this reason, Green Tree Inn will increase its development efforts in the Pudong area in the future to adapt to market changes and work together with other hotel operators and relevant departments to promote the development of the hotel industry in Pudong towards greater maturity and standardization. We aim to elevate the service level of the entire Shanghai service industry to a new level through advanced hotel management concepts and service awareness, promoting Shanghai's transformation into an international "urban economy" from a software perspective.

However, this highly optimistic tone regarding the hotel industry cannot help but make me worry: many projects that should have greatly promoted regional economies and industries often end up having the opposite effect due to blind following trends and investments by the locals. We hope that all current hotel industry investors do not become blindly optimistic but carefully analyze the market based on their own situations and development goals before deciding whether and how to participate in this project. Rushing in without careful consideration will result in no benefits for anyone.
