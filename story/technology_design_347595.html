1. China believes that a country's overall mid-term emission reduction of 25% to 40% should be an international obligation. The EU negotiator said at a press conference in the Copenhagen Climate Conference the other day that China's recently proposed goal of reducing carbon dioxide emissions per unit of GDP by 2020 is an internal target, and so is the EU's 20% emission reduction target, which should be compared. In response to this, Su Wei, deputy head of the Chinese delegation attending the conference, pointed out that developed countries' overall mid-term emission reduction of 25% to 40% should be an international obligation rather than an internal target, and it was not decent to compare China with the EU. An international research report published yesterday also showed that by 2020, the EU should reduce emissions by at least 40% based on the level of 1990 in order to repay the "climate debt" accumulated over many years, which is affordable for the EU. According to another report, Zhao Baige, Vice Minister of the National Population and Family Planning Commission of China attending the United Nations Climate Change Conference, said yesterday that China's current per capita carbon dioxide emissions are only one fifth of those in the United States and half of those in the UK.

2. 130,000 college student soldiers enter the barracks, which can be recorded in the history of the army. As the year 2009 is coming to an end, batches of new recruits, carrying green dreams, bearing the expectations of their families, and brimming with youthful aspirations, leave their hometowns for the barracks. Carrying pen and joining the army as true men, they have a sincere heart to serve the country. Among the new recruits, university students have become a particularly eye-catching bright landscape. This winter, our military service collection system has seen significant changes: vigorously promoting adjustments to the main objects of collection, focusing mainly on graduates from all levels and types of schools, and prioritizing the recruitment of high-educated youth.

Including vision status in the hard indicators of student quality; incorporating student physical health status including vision status into the standard of measuring comprehensive student quality, schools should monitor students' vision status twice each semester. This is what I learned from the national network video conference on preventing myopia among primary and secondary school students held on December 10th. Deputy Minister of Education Chen Xiaoya pointed out at the meeting that we should establish and improve the eye exercise system, and include the two daily sessions of eye exercises in the class schedule. We should establish a regular vision monitoring system, do a good job in analyzing various indicators such as the detection rate of poor vision and the incidence of new cases among students, manage students with declining vision trends and mild myopia in different categories, and implement relevant measures to prevent nearsightedness.

4. Five scholars from Peking University Law School submitted "Suggestions for Reviewing the 'Regulations on the Demolition of Urban Housing'" to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress. The People's Daily reported that recently, five scholars from Peking University Law School submitted "Suggestions for Reviewing the 'Regulations on the Demolition of Urban Housing'" to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress. This is after the "Sun Zhigang Case" in 2003 when legal scholars submitted petitions, scholars once again petitioned the highest legislative body of the state regarding administrative regulations. People expect that this time it could be like six years ago, where tragic individual cases ultimately promote institutional progress. The article states that the biggest defect of the current "Demolition Regulations" is the lack of distinction between public welfare and commercial demolition, and its operational model is cooperation between public power and developers, where government departments act as both "athletes" and "referees." The article suggests that in terms of demolition models, we should address and separately govern based on accurately defining public interests. In government-led demolitions, judicial channels for "citizens suing officials" should be strengthened, and in commercial demolitions, the current practice of government adjudication should be changed, and disputes should be referred to courts for final adjudication, and all forced demolitions must be subject to judicial review before implementation.

5. Peking University: Real-name recommendation system for middle school principals. Recently, the "Real-name Recommendation System for Middle School Principals" launched by Peking University has been under the media spotlight and in front of the public microphone. Judging from the latest list, the selected students are without exception the top one or two in recent exams, and what was supposed to be a recommendation has apparently turned into a big competition of exam scores. An autonomous enrollment reform that balances efficiency and individuality has just started but seems to be merging with the existing "recommended students" and "self-recommended students" systems, showing a tendency of walking the old path with new shoes. I believe this is not the original intention of Peking University, which originally wanted to explore and supplement the current admission system, but ended up becoming a "new gimmick" to compete for high-quality students, preemptively capturing the "top candidates" from key provinces and cities.

6. Indian companies "reverse innovation". A phone for $20, a water purifier for $43, a refrigerator for $70... These are not broken second-hand goods, but new low-cost products developed by some well-known Indian companies targeting the poor market. To tap into the potential consumption power of India's poor market, some Indian companies focus on the actual needs of low-income consumer groups, conduct major technological innovations, reduce product costs, and attract poor buyers living in rural areas and slums with ultra-low prices. This trend of product reform focusing on low-end consumer groups in Indian enterprises began when India's local automaker Tata Motors launched the ultra-low-priced car NANO in July this year. This car, which attracted global media attention, has a minimum selling price of just over $2,000. Moreover, it is low-priced but not low-quality, fully functional and diverse in style, deeply favored by India's poor. Indian companies were thus inspired, and a "reverse innovation" campaign serving the poor quickly unfolded on a large scale in India.

7. Four dishes and one soup at state banquets, how about a few meat dishes and a few vegetables in local places? President Hu Jintao held a grand banquet on the evening of November 17 to welcome US President Obama visiting China. The menu combined Chinese and Western cuisine, with the main course including one cold dish (18 yuan), one soup, and three hot dishes: Cui juice chicken bean flower soup (28 yuan), Chinese-style steak (38 yuan), stir-fried water bamboo shoots and asparagus (yuan), and roasted red star grouper (480 yuan). Desserts included one pastry and one fruit ice cream (total 56 yuan). The accompanying red and white wines at the banquet were the Great Wall dry red and Great Wall dry white produced in Hebei, China in 2002 (576 yuan), totaling approximately 1214 yuan.

The president entertaining the president is a state banquet, and if following the specifications of a head of state, it is very appropriate and normal to have a sumptuous feast. Now, seeing President Hu's menu for entertaining Obama, one would be greatly surprised, and might even think "the state banquet is no big deal." Except for the "roasted red star grouper," there is nothing particularly enviable. Meanwhile, in some places, there is excessive drinking and eating, even "a table of wine and food equivalent to a whole cow," how many "four dishes and one soup" does that equate to? It was previously reported that the national annual public-funded eating and drinking costs exceed 200 billion yuan, which is normal, equivalent to consuming an entire Three Gorges Project. Four dishes and one soup, this was the standard set by Premier Zhou back then, and has continued until today.

8. Wu Jiagang: Designing evening gowns for Mrs. Obama. If there were no person named Obama in the world, there would be no Mrs. Obama, and if there were no Mrs. Obama, there would be no event of this young man surnamed Wu designing her evening gown, and thus everything would not have happened. But everything did happen, when Mrs. Obama appeared in a white single-shoulder evening gown at the presidential inauguration ceremony, the fate of the unknown young man Wu Jiagang took a turn. CNN called him for live commentary, and nearly 100 emails appeared in his inbox within minutes, his personal website received four million clicks, the number of people wanting to attend his New York Fashion Show increased significantly, and PR personnel were busy non-stop. Prior to this, he was just a frail Chinese-American designer who had just started in the fashion industry, running a small company with only six employees, and had to use every means possible to persuade his works to be sold in ten retail stores in the United States. However, thanks to Mrs. Obama's choice, Mr. Wu, struggling through the financial crisis, turned his life around and became a new favorite in the global fashion world. Now his designs are available in forty retail stores worldwide, and everything happened so fast that Jason Wu is even listed as a luxury brand, earning four million dollars in a year. Later, Wu Jiagang said: "I am indeed luckier than many people, but before luck comes, what I can do is work hard and seize opportunities."
