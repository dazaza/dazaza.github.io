Mars colonization project closes applications: over 200,000 applicants

by anonymous on 2013-09-11 13:48:33

The Mars One Mars colonization plan, which was announced earlier this year, has caught the attention of astronomy enthusiasts around the world. Despite doubts about it being a private scam, the first round of volunteer sign-ups has recently come to a successful close. Next, the organizers of Mars One will select suitable candidates for the next round of selection.

According to foreign media Space Industry News, the Mars One Mars colonization project has successfully completed its first round of volunteer recruitment and the relevant registration activities have been closed. In the end, the organizers received 202,586 applications from all over the world.

From preliminary statistics, Americans showed the highest enthusiasm for Mars One, with nearly a quarter of the applications coming from the United States. India and China ranked second and third in terms of the number of applicants, accounting for 10% and 6% of the total number of applicants respectively.

The organizers of Mars One stated that although they received more than 200,000 applications, only 40 people will be selected to participate in the final Mars colonization plan. The second round of selection will begin in early 2014, and the results will be notified via email.

According to the plan, the final list of 40 people will be announced in 2015. They will be divided into 4 groups and undergo seven years of professional training. The most outstanding group will take on the historical task of being the "first Mars colonists" in 2023 and spend the rest of their lives on Mars.
