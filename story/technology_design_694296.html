Feelings on Watching "Hachi: A Dog's Tale"

by chongwuyin5r on 2012-03-07 06:15:38

I've known the story of Hachiko, the loyal dog, for a long time. Many friends have told me that they were deeply moved after watching it and even couldn't help shedding tears. I spent nearly two hours carefully watching the story of Hachi, the story of Hachi and the professor, and the story of the professor's family. In the end, the professor's grandson said like this: "Hachi let me know what loyalty is. It is my hero forever." I didn't shed tears, but I had the same feeling as the child. We are people in the pet circle, frequently in contact with pets and pet owners, and have seen countless pet owners taking meticulous care of their pets. I saw how the professor took good care of Hachi just like a family member. Its life was free from pain, only experiencing the longing for its owner and endless waiting. Finally, it left in the dream of missing its owner, deeply telling us pet keepers that if we really want to be responsible for our pets at home, we should try to understand them, read them, and know what they need and shouldn't have. I really want to tell my friends that dogs communicate with you in many languages, maybe barking or maybe body language. Why don't we use our hearts to understand and carefully analyze and read our most loyal friends, our most benevolent friends? When it barks at you, it may not be an attack, but just excitement, just wanting to play with you, or perhaps it has discovered something abnormal. On the day the professor passed away, Hachi felt it. It didn't want the professor to go out. When the professor arrived at the station, Hachi begged the professor to stay and play with it, holding a toy ball it never wanted to play with before. That kind of foreknowledge might only be understood by those who have experienced it. When I saw the professor lying down in the classroom, I was completely stunned. I didn't know how to express my feelings at that moment. I hope that the professor knew that Hachi could stay. Even if he missed that day, perhaps the professor could accompany Hachi for a while more. This is not sympathy for Hachi, but a kind of inexplicable moving sympathy. They could be happy together, treating each other loyally. How nice it would be if that moment could last longer, unfortunately there is no "if".

From Hachi, I thought of last summer when a golden retriever owner cried her heart out when her pet left. Remembering how she pleaded with the doctor to save it, that look of helplessness. Her heart broke. Her boyfriend told her that the doctor had done his best, but she said, "It's impossible, it's impossible, it can still be saved. Doctor, please, save it." I shed tears standing next to her. My expression couldn't hide my tears. I really saw that love. Without saying goodbye to them, I left. I didn't want to see anymore and didn't know how to comfort them. I sincerely wished that they could have another beloved pet and hoped that a little cutie could walk into their lives and follow them, which would surely make them very happy.

In 2005, when I first entered the circle, I left my workplace with my master. At that time, I had a dog named Jingang. When I said goodbye to it, I held a bag of sausage and told it my troubles. It ate while looking at me. To this day, I still think it didn't understand my farewell, but I knew it was trying hard to understand what I was saying. I sat beside it in the dog house and cried because I was leaving my brother. I couldn't take it with me. I did everything I could and entrusted Jingang to a colleague who liked Jingang very much, just hoping it could live well. I also hope that it is still healthy now.

In the industry, I am a professional dog trader. Of course, I am also a breeder and any role in this industry. Actually, when selling dogs, I hope every puppy that goes out of my hands can get the best care from its new owner, and of course, the most appropriate care. Because reasonable feeding is the greatest protection they receive in their most vulnerable stage. So, dear friends, I hope you don't spoil these children taken away by us, otherwise they will get rich man's disease. If you encounter any problems, don't rush to handle them. Consulting is very important. Of course, I hope that you learn about how to feed and manage before deciding to raise a pet. When you take away our little friends, you should be responsible for them. This is a responsibility to life. They may start as your children, then become your friends, and then your family members. Always regard them as friends, not pets. We all have emotions like joy, anger, sorrow, and happiness. Carefully reading them is a process. When you read them and they read you, I believe you will become the happiest person in the world.

Finally, I wish all parents and your children, friends, and family members a pleasant relationship. If you have any questions, you can consult us. Please share your happiness with me. They are all Hachi, all Lassie, all Wolf Tooth and Princess. As long as you give, they will repay you for a lifetime...
