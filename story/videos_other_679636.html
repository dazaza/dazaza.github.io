Chapter Three: Feasibility Analysis of the Board on the Use of Proceeds from This Offering

1. Plan for the use of proceeds from this offering

The total amount of proceeds from this non-public offering is RMB 529.8 million. The net proceeds from this non-public offering (after deducting issuance expenses) are proposed to be entirely used to supplement working capital.

2. Necessity of this offering

(1) Steel trading business develops smoothly, and the expansion of future business scale urgently needs to increase working capital.

During the reporting period, the company has been in a state of negative net assets and high asset-liability ratio, and the shortage of operating funds has become the main bottleneck restricting the development of the company. In November 2010, the third extraordinary shareholders' meeting reviewed and approved the proposal that the company may borrow up to RMB 2 billion from Ju Shenghua Industrial through bank loans as appropriate. Since December 2010, the company has started to engage in trade business, initially focusing on steel trading. In 2011, the revenue from steel trading was RMB 350.0992 million, accounting for 53.31% of the company's total revenue.

Currently, the bottleneck of insufficient working capital is becoming increasingly prominent with the increase in steel trading volume, seriously restricting the scale of the company's steel trading business and other trading businesses. It is reasonable to raise funds through a non-public offering method to supplement working capital, which is conducive to the expansion of the scale of the company's steel trading business and the conduct of other trading businesses.

(2) Reduce the company's asset-liability ratio, optimize the capital structure, and improve the company's risk resistance capability.

From 2009 to 2011, the company's asset-liability ratio was higher than 100%, specifically 109.27%, 114.79%, and 106.48%. The asset-liability structure was extremely unreasonable, and the company's financial leverage utilization rate was extremely high. To some extent, the above financial structure reduced the company's risk resistance capability, limited the company's financing ability, and made the company face higher financial risks.

From the perspective of debt structure, the company's liabilities are mainly current liabilities, and the proportion of current liabilities to total liabilities has been over 90% in the past three years, indicating greater short-term debt pressure.

Through this non-public offering, the net assets of the listed company will turn from negative to positive, and the dilemma of negative asset operation will be completely reversed. The asset-liability ratio will drop significantly, and the debt-paying ability will improve. This is beneficial for improving the capital structure and enhancing the quality of assets of the listed company.

(3) Reduce financial expenses and improve the company's profitability level.

After the proceeds from this non-public offering supplement working capital, the company's operational funding pressure will be alleviated to a certain extent, equivalent to reducing equal short-term debt financing, thereby reducing financial expenses.

Chapter Four: Discussion and Analysis by the Board on the Impact of This Non-Public Offering on the Company

1. Changes in the company's business, articles of association, shareholder structure, senior management structure, and business income structure after this offering

(1) Business changes after this offering

The proceeds from this non-public offering of shares will be used to supplement working capital, so it will not change the company's business direction. The implementation of this non-public offering of shares will improve the company's asset-liability structure, reduce the company's financial risks, help the company expand its existing business scale, and enhance the company's profitability and sustainable development capabilities.

(2) Changes in the articles of association after this offering

After the completion of this non-public offering of shares, the company's share capital will expand accordingly. Therefore, the company will modify the articles of association according to the changes in share capital and other situations after completing this non-public offering of shares, and register the changes with the industrial and commercial authorities.

(3) Changes in the company's shareholder structure after this offering

After the completion of this non-public offering of shares, the company's shareholder structure will change, adding 60 million shares of restricted tradable shares. After the completion of this non-public offering, the company's controlling shareholder will still be Ju Shenghua Industrial, and the actual controller will still be Yao Zhenhua. This non-public offering will not lead to changes in the company's controlling shareholder and actual controller.

(4) Changes in the company's senior management structure after this offering

After the completion of this non-public offering of shares, the company will not adjust the senior management personnel due to this issuance, and the senior management structure will not change.

(5) Changes in the company's business income structure after this offering

After the implementation of this non-public offering of shares plan, the supplementary working capital will mainly be used for the development of the steel trading business, and it is expected that the proportion of steel trading income in the main business income will further increase.

2. Changes in the company's financial status, profitability, and cash flow after this offering

This issuance will have a positive impact on the company's financial status. If the required funds for this issuance can be fully raised as planned, both the company's total assets and net assets will increase accordingly, the company's asset-liability ratio will decrease accordingly, the company's financial structure and cash flow situation will further improve, and the core competitiveness will be enhanced. The company's long-term continuous profitability will be further improved.

3. Changes in the business relationship, management relationship, related party transactions, and competition in the same industry between the company and enterprises controlled by the actual controller, controlling shareholder, and their related parties after this issuance

After the completion of this issuance, there will be no major changes in the business relationship and management relationship between the company and its controlling shareholder and related parties, nor will this issuance generate competition in the same industry and other new related party transactions.

4. Whether the company will experience the occupation of funds and assets by the actual controller, controlling shareholder, and their related parties or provide guarantees for the actual controller, controlling shareholder, and their related parties after the completion of this non-public offering of shares

After the completion of this non-public offering, the capital transactions between the company and its controlling shareholder and related parties belong to normal business transactions. The company will not have the situation where funds and assets are occupied by the controlling shareholder and related parties due to this issuance. After the completion of this non-public offering of shares, the company will not provide guarantees for the actual controller, controlling shareholder, and their related parties due to this issuance.

5. The impact of this non-public offering of shares on the company's liability situation

As of December 31, 2011, the company's total liabilities were RMB 367.2307 million, and the total assets were RMB 344.8919 million. Without considering other influencing factors, calculated based on the estimated total amount of funds raised this time being RMB 529.8 million, after the completion of this non-public offering of shares, the company's asset-liability ratio is expected to decrease from 106.48% to 41.98%, and the company's financial risks and capital pressure will be reduced. Therefore, this issuance will lower the company's asset-liability ratio, www.zhuifa.com, reduce the company's financial expenses, and make the company's financial structure more reasonable.

6. Risk statements related to this stock issuance

In evaluating the company's non-public offering of shares this time, apart from the other materials provided in the prospectus, investors should carefully consider the following risk factors:

(1) Business Risks

To achieve better returns and realize the goal of company profits, the company is expanding its operations by engaging in steel trading and other forms of commodity trading business to increase profits. However, the company's trading business is in its early stages, and fluctuations in steel prices and adjustments in the macroeconomy may impact the steel trading business. The expansion of the company's business scale and sustained profitability remain uncertain.

(2) Debt Repayment Risk

As of December 31, 2011, the company's total assets were RMB 344.8919 million, total liabilities were RMB 367.2307 million, net assets were -RMB 22.3387 million, and the asset-liability ratio was 106.48%. After the funds raised from this issuance are in place, the liquidity of the company's funds will improve to some extent, and the asset structure will also improve to some extent. However, with the further expansion of the business scale in the future, if the debt level cannot be kept within a reasonable range, the company will still face certain debt repayment risks.

(3) Approval Risk

This non-public offering of shares still requires the approval of the company's shareholders' meeting, and there is a possibility that this plan will not pass the voting of the company's shareholders' meeting. This non-public offering of shares still needs the approval of the China Securities Regulatory Commission, and whether the relevant competent authorities' approvals can be obtained, as well as the final time of obtaining the approvals and approvals, are uncertain.

(2) Subscription Quantity, Subscription Price, Subscription Method, Payment Method, Lock-up Period

1. Subscription Quantity:

The number of shares issued in this non-public offering is 60 million shares, among which, Shenzhen Ju Shenghua Industrial Development Co., Ltd. subscribes for 35 million shares.

If there are any changes in the number of shares of Baicheng Investment Co., Ltd. due to dividends, bonus shares, capitalization, or other reasons before this issuance, the total issuance quantity and the subscription quantity of Shenzhen Ju Shenghua Industrial Development Co., Ltd. will be adjusted proportionally according to the changes in the total share capital.

2. Subscription Price:

After consultation among the parties involved in this non-public offering of shares, the subscription price for the non-publicly offered shares by Shenzhen Ju Shenghua Industrial Development Co., Ltd. is RMB 8.83 per share. If the stock of Baicheng Investment Co., Ltd. undergoes dividend distribution, bonus shares, capital reserve conversion into share capital, or other rights adjustment matters from the pricing benchmark date to the issuance date, the issuance price of this offering will be adjusted accordingly.

The pricing benchmark date for this non-public offering of shares is the announcement date of the resolution of the seventh meeting of the eighth board of directors. The subscription price of Shenzhen Ju Shenghua Industrial Development Co., Ltd. shall not be less than 90% of the average price of the stock over the previous 20 trading days before the pricing benchmark date (i.e., RMB 8.83 per share, the average price of the stock over the previous 20 trading days before the pricing benchmark date = the total trading value of the stock over the previous 20 trading days before the pricing benchmark date / the total trading volume of the stock over the previous 20 trading days before the pricing benchmark date).

3. Subscription Method:

Shenzhen Ju Shenghua Industrial Development Co., Ltd. subscribes in cash.

4. Payment Method:

After this non-public offering is formally approved by the China Securities Regulatory Commission, during the issuance process, Shenzhen Ju Shenghua Industrial Development Co., Ltd. should transfer the subscription funds in one lump sum to the special account opened by the sponsor (lead underwriter) for this non-public offering according to the requirements of the sponsor (lead underwriter). After the certified public accountants complete the capital verification and deduct the relevant fees, the funds will then be transferred to the issuer's special fundraising account.

5. Lock-up Period:

The shares subscribed by Shenzhen Ju Shenghua Industrial Development Co., Ltd. and other specific objects this time shall not be transferred within 36 months from the end of this non-public offering.
