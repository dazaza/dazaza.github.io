2009 Online Marketing Trends

by cherrer on 2008-12-18 08:56:21

For many companies, 2009 could be a huge challenge as the slowdown in economic growth casts a heavy shadow over business development. However, digital marketing appears to be unaffected, and with the continuous reduction and increasing constraints on marketing budgets, the advantages of digital marketing are increasingly being revealed.

Here are the 16 major trends in online marketing we have observed for 2009. We have analyzed these trends and proposed appropriate strategies to deal with them.

1. Keyword price increases will continue

Advertisers are demanding more control over ad effectiveness. With more advertising budgets shifting into digital marketing, competition in this market will become fiercer, and rising demand is driving up the cost of paid search. The increase in keyword prices, or pay-per-click ad costs, rose by 20% in 2008 (source: Jupiter Research), and we believe that even though the economy is slowing down, keyword ad prices will continue to rise in 2009.

Strange View: As the cost of click-through ads rises, it will directly lead to an increase in the cost of sales-based ads. Advertisers need to improve conversion rates to cope with this.

2. Conversion rate optimization becomes critical

If keyword prices continue to rise at the current pace, maintaining constant sales-based ad costs will require higher conversion rates. Advertisers face enormous pressure and urgently need to optimize search ads, display ads, integrated marketing, and email marketing to find ways to improve return on investment.

Strange View: Controlled testing is the best method. A/B testing and multivariate testing (evaluating the impact of changing various ad attributes simultaneously) along with tracking clicks are essential tools for improving your website. Eye-tracking tests also become necessary.

3. Consumer frugality will change their online behavior

Due to the impact of the economic crisis, consumers will increasingly use online shopping, especially through auction sites and classified ad websites. Craigslist, Gumtree, eBay, and many similar sites will see a surge in traffic.

Strange View: During an economic downturn, maintaining market share is crucial. Marketers need to be more proactive. Online retail sites should maintain sufficient inventory (including certain luxury items) and strive to bring consumers back to their site. All online businesses need to make incentives, supply, and orders standard operating procedures. Encouraging consumers to return to the site is key to increasing sales and achieving cross-selling. Placing discounted items in prominent positions can effectively shape consumer expectations and encourage repeat visits.

4. More innovation is needed

This may seem obvious, but it still needs further emphasis: Digital marketing increasingly requires creativity in implementing marketing strategies and achieving marketing goals. Creativity can make your display ads perform better, make your landing pages friendlier, and make your website more credible. Creativity can help you convey your message and enhance brand appeal. Although the economy is declining, the demand for spiritual fulfillment has not hit rock bottom, and creativity can influence all online marketing activities, thereby impacting return on investment.

Strange View: Creativity is the "X factor" guiding you through tough times because only the right design, copy, and message delivery can stimulate consumers. This is why we call what we're doing "creative digital marketing" rather than just "digital marketing."

5. IE8 will impact online advertising

The latest release of Microsoft's web browser, IE8, is important because it is the default browser in Windows systems. Although IE is not as flexible as Google Chrome or Firefox, most users are too lazy to switch and continue using it. If the private browsing feature in IE8 is widely adopted, it will significantly impact online advertising because this function blocks cookies, which are the core of online advertising and traffic monitoring.

Strange View: Standards created by Microsoft are now being blocked by Microsoft itself. The privacy browsing feature in IE8 blocks third-party cookies, which will cause headaches for ad servers.

6. Pay-per-sale ads will gain wider adoption

The pay-per-sale digital ad model will gain wider adoption. In these difficult times, large online advertisers have already allocated half of their budgets to pay-per-sale media because this model drives more actual sales rather than just brand benefits. There will be more of this type of advertising in the future.

Strange View: Pay-per-sale ads are great for advertisers because the ad costs they spend will directly generate sales revenue according to a certain conversion rate ratio. As the economic situation declines, advertisers will focus more on return on investment and place more orders for pay-per-sale ads. We believe that neither the cost-per-impression nor the cost-per-click ad models will disappear; these ads will be used more for building brand awareness, but the pay-per-sale ad model will become a major component of online marketing.

7. Data privacy and security remain top priorities

Data security and privacy issues will continue to be the top priority in online marketing in 2009. Whether it’s Phorm, communication authorities monitoring our behaviors, or government agencies and companies losing laptops, software, discs containing unencrypted data, these issues will draw attention.

Strange View: Data security protection is not just a safety issue; making mistakes here could negatively affect a company's public relations and brand image. Previously, we mentioned that if the private browsing features in Google Chrome and IE8 are widely adopted, it will have a significant impact on online advertising. Companies need to review their data processing and security measures to ensure everything is flawless. If a company loses or leaks data, it could very likely face class-action lawsuits from consumers.

8. Qualitative analysis will be strengthened

It's great to know what users on your website are doing, but it's even more important to understand why they do it. We predict that qualitative analysis will be extensively used in the analysis and decision-making process in 2009. User surveys, email questionnaires, internal search analysis, user complaints, consumer service forms, FAQ feedback, A/B testing and multivariate testing, eye-tracking analysis, and click density analysis will all become necessary tools for qualitative analysis and will help you improve return on investment.

Strange View: The key is extracting the correct information from your analysis — then clearly summarizing it — so you can make reasonable decisions. Qualitative analysis of a website can make your adjustments more effective. Additionally, in qualitative clickstream analysis, pay attention to Yahoo! and Google Analytics — these are all free indexing tools.

9. Mobile advertising will become popular

As Google starts releasing mobile hardware (G1) and software (Android) and heavily invests in the wireless sector, it's clear that these guys from Mountain View are beginning to tap the potential of this market. Smartphones like the iPhone, Blackberry Storm, and Google G1 offer standard web browsing functions. Google is seizing the opportunity to dominate the mobile advertising market by developing and controlling the platforms and operations principles we use when surfing the web on smartphones.

Strange View: If you haven't considered your mobile web strategy yet, now is the time. Brands that enter this market early will benefit greatly as the market explodes.

10. Local search/local services will grow

Search engines, browsers, and hardware manufacturers will continue to invest heavily in localizing search. Why? There are three main reasons: 1) Higher relevance in search results; 2) More precise advertising; 3) Localized search will bring more local sales to search ad networks instead of regional or national sales.

We say 2009 will be a big year for localized search because the Gears project (an open-source initiative aimed at embedding more web applications in browsers) provides a geographic location application programming interface (API). Mozilla's Geode and Yahoo!'s Fire Eagle provide similar functionality. Additionally, more laptops, GPS-enabled smartphones, and Palm devices supporting high-speed downlink packet access (HSDPA) technology will enable this UK-based search engine to obtain more geographic location information beyond existing internet services.

Strange View: Advertisers need to thoroughly understand the potential applications of localized search and prepare in advance to meet user needs.

11. RSS news feeds will go mainstream

RSS is an ideal platform for online marketing and communication as it provides marketers with powerful information dissemination channels and a large number of precise target audiences. After a tough early promotion phase, RSS will experience explosive growth in 2009, becoming a mainstream information dissemination channel, thanks in part to Microsoft embedding RSS functionality in the IE8 browser.

Strange View: For both users and marketers, RSS has three main advantages: 1) Users can subscribe only to the information they want, avoiding any spam; 2) Users gradually filter out news sources, receiving only specific information; 3) Users can read RSS feeds via various methods — through their web browsers, email clients, desktops, online aggregators, or mobile and handheld devices. This freedom to choose how to receive information means users have control, making RSS feed audiences more precise targets for email marketing.

12. Google's algorithm will include more user behavior metrics

We predict that Google will incorporate more user behavior metrics into its search engine algorithm. In fact, Google discussed this algorithm issue openly in a patent named "Information retrieval based on historical data" starting in 2003.

We have already seen some elements, such as domain search history and backlinks appearing in Google's algorithm, but commentators believe user behavior metrics are more likely. This would mean that metrics like click-through rates and bounce rates will be used to evaluate the value of search results.

Strange View: Over time, poorly performing websites will gradually drop in search rankings as the importance of data such as user usage and time spent on a site increases. For online marketers, this means relevance, quality, and user-friendliness will become key factors in online business, affecting not only conversion rates but also the volume of natural search traffic.

13. Netbooks will influence website design

Netbooks, which are smaller, cheaper, and designed entirely for web browsing, have become the fastest-growing product category in the notebook market. In 2009, netbooks will account for 10% of notebook sales and 8% of total computer market sales. New-style netbooks will include high-speed downlink packet access (HSDPA, 3G) technology, making them ideal tools for business people.

For website developers, the most important thing to consider is the screen resolution of netbooks. Most developers design based on screens 768 pixels high. However, netbook screens are typically only 600 pixels high, meaning the actual browsing area decreases by 22%.

Strange View: Developers need to design more compact websites, using the actual browsing space economically, focusing key functions and user behaviors within the 600-pixel height range.

14. User-generated content (UGC) is king

A report released by Universal McCann in 2008 pointed out that only 14% of users trust advertisements, while 78% trust recommendations from other consumers. This proves what many online marketers have just realized: user-generated content is highly beneficial.

Strange View: User-generated content, such as blog comments, product reviews, video reviews, ideas, photo albums, and other content, has extremely high value: 1) Brand Awareness: Open, honest, approachable; 2) Brand Identification: Users can contribute and delve deeper into brand culture; 3) Brand Dialogue: Users feel important and listened to; 4) Brand Trust: Your product/service must be excellent since it has been tested by users; 5) Added Value: User-created photo albums, product reviews, and video reviews make users more loyal and focused; 6) Free Content: Your users help improve your product/service for free.

15. Social media will create numerous opportunities

According to research by Universal McCann, 73% of users frequently read blogs, 36% believe companies with blogs are more trustworthy. Additionally, 83% of users have watched video clips (a side proof of increased sales), 49% have downloaded podcasts, 38% have subscribed to RSS feeds, and 57% have joined social networks. We could list more data, but these figures sufficiently demonstrate the immense influence of social media. If you don't take advantage of this trend, you'll miss out on many opportunities.

Strange View: Create a blog, tell people who you are, what you do, and why your products/services are good. Provide users with information and resources to enhance their experience and enrich their lives. Appropriately leverage the advantages of social networks, bookmarks, RSS, online and offline videos, audio and video resources. You can use these tools to engage users more openly, turning them into brand advocates and fostering brand loyalty. Additionally, you can convert user-generated content into various web aggregation applications, making your site one of users' preferred destinations.

16. Application Programming Interfaces (APIs) and web aggregation applications will become mainstream trends

We cannot predict who will be the next Facebook or Last.fm, but creating web aggregation applications based on these sites is a good idea. Through APIs, desktop or mobile applications can directly utilize data from these networks, greatly enhancing the functionalities of services like iTunes, RSS readers, desktop weather plugins, Xbox Live, and PlayStation Network.

Through these web aggregation applications, data as an information source can present many interactive forms unimaginable before. Web aggregation applications develop rapidly, and good ones have strong synergistic effects, providing users with better experiences and deeper information.

Strange View: Yahoo! Pipes is an excellent way to manipulate data, and Google Gears and Maps are also powerful tools. We need a bit of patience, waiting for more amazing new services to appear online, but we are confident that in 2009, more developers will open their websites and programs, attracting public attention and transforming cyberspace.
