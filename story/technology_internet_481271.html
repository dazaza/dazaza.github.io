Oven lamp not lit, turntable not rotating, fan not rotating B4

by mbs34uc1 on 2011-08-24 18:51:04

**Fault Phenomenon**: The oven light does not turn on, the turntable does not rotate, and the heating stops automatically after a few minutes with power off.

**Cause of Fault**: The main relay on the computer control board is damaged.

**Analysis and Maintenance**: The working procedure of a computer-controlled microwave oven is as follows:

(1) **Opening the Door**: The primary door interlock switch disconnects the power supply circuits for the synchronous motor, fan motor, and microwave system. The secondary door interlock switch also disconnects, notifying the computer control board to command the control relay to close, thus connecting the oven light circuit and lighting up the lamp for convenience in placing or removing food inside the cavity. The door monitoring switch closes, which ensures that if the microwave system accidentally receives 220V power, it will short-circuit the 220V power supply, blowing the fuse and stopping the microwave from operating, thereby preventing harm caused by microwave radiation when the door is open.

(2) **Closing the Door**: The primary and secondary door interlock switches connect while the door monitoring switch disconnects. When the secondary door interlock switch connects, it notifies the computer control board to ensure all relay contacts are disconnected and begins receiving and processing start instructions.

(3) **Placing Food and Pressing Start Button**: The computer control board executes the microwave cooking program, and the display starts counting down. The control relay closes, connecting the circuits for the oven light, synchronous motor, and fan motor. At the same time, the power relay switch closes, connecting the power supply circuit for the microwave system, and the microwave oven begins to operate and heat. In this case, although the unit can heat, the oven light and turntable do not work. The cause of the fault is that the main control relay cannot close. After opening the outer shell of the microwave oven and checking the main control relay on the computer control board, it was found that the voltage across the coil terminals was above 8V, but the contacts could not close. After replacement, the fault was resolved. The damage to the main control relay simultaneously disconnects the circuits for the oven light, synchronous motor, and fan motor, causing the oven light not to illuminate, the turntable not to rotate, and the fan not to spin. If the fan does not spin, it loses its cooling function for the magnetron, causing the high-power magnetron to rapidly overheat. When the temperature rises to 145°C, the thermally controlled switch mounted on the surface of the magnetron will disconnect, cutting off the entire machine's 220V power supply circuit, resulting in an automatic shutdown.

**Warm Reminder**: The power consumption of the microwave oven’s light, turntable, and fan motor is relatively small, so the combined current during their operation is also low. Therefore, the control relay on the computer control board used to control these three components is relatively small in size. By contrast, since the microwave system has a much higher power rating, the relay switch on the computer control board used to control the microwave system is significantly larger.
