1. Conceptualization.

The first step in opening a store is not online, but in your mind. You need to think about what kind of store you want to open. In this aspect, opening an online store is no different from a traditional one. Finding a good market and ensuring your products are competitive are the foundations for success.

2. Register as a user on the website.

You need to choose a website that provides a personal store platform and register as a user. Some websites require registration with your real name and valid ID. When choosing a website, high traffic and relatively low fees are important factors.

3. Apply to open a store on the website.

You need to fill out detailed information about the categories of products your store will provide. For example, if you sell fashion watches, they should be classified under "Jewelry, Watches, Glasses" -> "Watches" so your target customers can easily find you. Then, give your store a catchy name as clicks often depend on how attractive the name is. Personal information may be displayed and should be filled out truthfully to build trust.

4. Procure goods.

5. Log in.

You need to fill in each product's name, origin, nature, appearance, quantity, transaction method, and deadline on the website. It’s best to include pictures. The name should be as comprehensive as possible, highlighting its advantages since only the name will appear in search lists. To increase appeal, the quality of the picture should be as good as possible, and descriptions should be detailed. If mailing is required, it’s best to state who is responsible for postage.

When logging in, setting the price is very important. Websites usually offer options like starting price, reserve price, and fixed price set by the seller. Online bidding differs from traditional bidding as sellers set a starting price and buyers bid upwards from there.

A lower starting price attracts more buyers; some sellers start bids at 1 yuan to draw attention. However, too low a starting price risks too low a final sale price, so sellers should also set a reserve price. Another downside of a low starting price is it may suggest you're willing to sell at a very low price, keeping bids low. If sellers don’t want to wait for the auction to end, they can set a fixed price, allowing immediate sale if a buyer agrees, though this prevents price escalation among multiple interested buyers. Sellers should adjust these settings based on their specific situations.

6. Advertising.

To boost your store's popularity, especially in the early stages, appropriate advertising investment is necessary, but only online. For instance, buy ad space on high-traffic pages or make product names bold in category lists and add images to attract attention. Free advertising methods like exchanging links with other stores or websites can also be utilized.

7. Mid-sale services.

Customers may need additional information not provided initially. They may ask questions online, which you should respond to promptly and patiently. Many websites prevent private contact information exchange to avoid bypassing transaction fees, penalizing violators.

8. Transactions.

After a successful deal, the website provides both parties' contact details. Transactions can be conducted face-to-face or via remittance and mail, preferably quickly to avoid credit doubts. Post-sales services depend on prior agreements.

9. Evaluation or complaints.

Credit is crucial in online transactions. To help build a trustworthy environment, it's best to give positive reviews after satisfactory transactions and earn them through good service. If a transaction fails, give negative feedback or file a complaint to reduce losses and warn others. Handle complaints promptly to maintain your credit.

10. Fees.

Websites usually charge fees, including for recommended web positions, listing fees, transaction fees, and store fees, which should be paid on time. These can be reasonably avoided, such as guiding repeat customers to offline transactions. When calculating costs, consider such fees along with shipping, transportation, phone, and internet fees.

In fact, opening an online store isn't as complex as imagined. Many people list numerous steps like sourcing goods, finding suppliers, making pictures, writing introductions, etc., which seem daunting, but these aren't the main issues. Opening a physical store involves even more considerations like business registration, taxation, utilities, etc. So, avoid unnecessary complaints about things you can save on.

Advice on sourcing: Don't overthink it, choose what you're familiar with and interested in.

Based on my years of studying online stores, preparing for a week is enough. Choose goods you like and are interested in. I often see people seeking help on forums, which is useful, but I believe smart store owners select goods rather than just find them. Opportunities and sources are discovered by clever people. If a store owner can't secure sources, they likely lack potential. Don't over-reference when choosing sources; go with what you like. Alternatively, help customers save money through cash-back shopping sites (like Taokè).

Focus on promotional services: Spend less time researching appearances and more on learning promotion!

Regardless of how much graphic design or templates you use, it doesn't matter much. Customers care more about the service than the aesthetics. Spend less time on looks and more on promotions. Promoting an online store is challenging, but earning money requires hard work. Reasons for not earning money are simple: laziness. Spend less time studying the store and more on learning promotion.

Build good credit through after-sales service, sacrifice small profits to retain customers:

Once business picks up and orders come in, thinking everything is fine is wrong. Otherwise, you'll lose repeat customers. I once wanted to buy a programming book only available at one Taobao store. Despite negotiations, the owner wouldn't lower the excessive price, leading me to discover that 80% of reviews criticized the owner as stingy and profit-driven. This reflects poor management skills. Small favors, even keychains, can retain long-term customers.

1. User Registration

Log into Taobao and click “Free Registration” at the top of the page. Input username, password, email (which can be obtained for free from Yahoo, Sina, Sohu, NetEase, etc.), then click “Agree to the following service terms and submit registration information.” A confirmation email will be sent to your registered email. Click the link in the email to confirm and complete the registration.

Security Tip: To ensure transaction security, avoid overly simple passwords. Use combinations of letters, numbers, and symbols.

2. Identity Verification

Taobao requires real-name verification to sell items and open a store. After registering, proceed with the necessary verifications (personal real-name and Alipay verification). Follow these steps:

Step 1: Log into Taobao, click “My Taobao,” and then click “Please click here” next to “Sell items, first complete Alipay verification.”

Step 2: On the next page, activate your Alipay account by clicking “Activate Alipay account here.” Enter your real name, ID type and number, Alipay password, and click “Save and immediately activate Alipay account.”

Step 3: After successfully activating your Alipay account, refresh the page using F5 and click “Apply for Alipay personal real-name verification.” Read the terms and click “I have read” to continue.

Step 4: Fill out personal information as prompted, click “Next,” choose ID verification method (upload online or mail copy), click “Next,” input bank card information including bank, card number, province, city, etc., and wait for Alipay to transfer funds within a day.

Friendly Reminder: If uploading copies online, control file size to under 200KB. For IC IDs, include the back side.

Step 5: After a day, reopen “My Taobao,” click the corresponding link in the certification area to open the “Alipay Certification” page. In the “Bank Account Verification” section, click “Confirm Transfer Amount,” input the amount transferred by Alipay to your bank account, and click “Confirm.”

Common Questions:

1. Are you a manufacturer? (Would manufacturers sell to small sellers? A few hundred pieces per model is already minimal, and bulk prices would be higher than wholesale markets.)

2. Is it original order? (Understandable, but the following questions show it's not original.)

3. Are all sizes available? (Original order goods might have all sizes, customs seizures or whole order cancellations, but these generally don't cater to small orders.)

4. Are all tags present? (Brands usually control accessories strictly; 90% tag presence is considered good for leftovers.)

5. Any defects? (Foreign trade original orders are sometimes called second-grade goods. We can get low prices by taking both good and defective goods; otherwise, only good goods would cost more than factory prices. For example, a child's spandex base layer with a 2-square-centimeter rubber print costs $3.5 at the factory, while a New Balance 8020 color-woven flip collar T-shirt costs nearly $5, yet I wholesale them for 6-8 yuan with profit. I guarantee a defect rate of 5% or less, refundable above 5%. Don't look for 100% genuine products; find pricier alternatives.)

If you're not in a big city with nearby wholesale markets, consider traveling long distances to other cities for goods. Always prioritize cost awareness: How much goods will you bring back? How much will travel expenses be? Spending 1000 yuan to bring back 100 clothes adds 10 yuan to the cost per piece! Maybe the clothes aren't worth 10 yuan! Retailers typically double the profit margin, meaning a 10-yuan cost increase equals a 20-yuan retail price hike, noticeable to shoppers.

Storeowners with steady traffic and returning customers should adjust their procurement strategies. Some prefer mixed batches, believing it minimizes costs for maximum goods, but online stores struggle with mixed batches. Single-item batches are hardest to sell online; cheap buys unsold are useless. Mixed batches suit physical stores, where premium items sell for over 100 yuan, and lower-quality ones act as discount draws outside the store.

Months ago, I noticed many unemployed individuals starting businesses, finding sources for street stalls or small shops. I gathered complete orders, averaged prices, added slight profit margins, and sold them as mixed batches, surprisingly well-received and in high demand. However, this isn't suitable for online stores. Once established, focus on fewer styles with lower prices. Refined judgment allows purchasing more of a single style. Taobao's ranking system considers popularity; longer-selling, higher-volume items rank higher, bringing obvious benefits. Higher sales volume means lower procurement costs. Study Taobao's top sellers, who may have been reported for manipulation but consistently move goods quickly, even faster than wholesalers.
