Summer Health Tips for Everyday Life

by lili1 on 2012-02-25 17:37:20

Summer Living Health Tips: In summer, stored grains are prone to insect infestations. You can use two handfuls of Sichuan peppercorns and fennel seeds, wrap them in gauze, and place them in the rice jar. Cover the jar tightly, and the stored grains will be less likely to attract insects.

In summer, raw meat that has been purchased can be wrapped in a vinegar-soaked damp cloth to stay fresh for up to 24 hours.

In summer, soy sauce is prone to spoilage. Peeling a few cloves of garlic and placing them in the bottle prevents the soy sauce from molding and eliminates any strange odors.

In summer, when rendering lard, first cut two to three slices of raw radish and place them in the container before adding the oil. This will eliminate any strange odors.

In summer, food placed in porcelain bowls and then steamed in a pressure cooker can remain fresh for 3-4 days without changing flavor.

In summer, if tofu becomes sour after purchase, soak it in 50% hot alkaline water for 20-30 minutes, then rinse thoroughly with clean water to remove the sour taste.

In summer, vegetables that have already become infested with insects can be soaked in salt water for a while, then rinsed with clean water to wash away all the insects.

In summer, when cooking rice, add 2-3 milliliters of vinegar for every 1.5 kilograms of rice. The cooked rice will not easily spoil and will have no vinegar taste.

In summer, if tea becomes moldy, avoid drying it in the sun. Instead, dry roast it in a clean pot for about 10 minutes, and its flavor will be restored. However, the fire should not be too strong.

In summer, white clothes tend to turn yellow after washing. Take a basin of clear water, add 2-3 drops of blue ink, and soak the washed clothes for 15 minutes without wringing them out. Then hang them in the sun, and they will remain white and clean.

In summer, newly purchased vests and shirts should be scalded with boiling water before wearing. This makes them more durable and less likely to fade. However, synthetic fabrics should not be exposed to high temperatures.

In summer, new foam sandals can be soaked in salt water for half a day, dried, and then worn. This makes them less prone to cracking and more durable. Using loofah as a shoe insole can help remove moisture from rubber-soled shoes.

In summer, placing a few pickled garlic bulbs under the bed can repel bedbugs.

The summer climate is hot, bacteria reproduce and grow quickly, and there are many factors that affect physical health. Therefore, to safely get through the summer, the following content may be helpful to you:

1. Best seasoning - Vinegar

In the sweltering heat, sweat production increases. Eating more vinegar can increase stomach acid concentration, helping digestion and absorption, stimulating appetite. Vinegar also has a strong ability to inhibit bacteria, killing staphylococci and other bacteria within a short period. It also helps prevent intestinal infectious diseases like typhoid and dysentery. People often feel fatigued and lethargic in summer; eating more vinegar can quickly relieve fatigue and maintain energy levels.

2. Best vegetable - Bitter-tasting vegetables

In the hot and humid summer, people often feel listless, tired, chest tightness, dizziness, loss of appetite, and weight loss. At this time, eating some bitter-tasting vegetables can be very beneficial. Traditional Chinese medicine believes that people feel unwell in summer due to excessive summer heat and humidity, which damages kidney qi and affects spleen function. Bitter-tasting foods can replenish qi, strengthen kidneys, invigorate spleen, and dry dampness, balancing body functions. Modern scientific research also proves that bitter-tasting vegetables contain rich amounts of biologically active alkaloids, amino acids, bitter substances, vitamins, and minerals that have cooling, heat-clearing, irritability-relieving, energizing, and stomach-strengthening functions. Examples include bitter melon, bitter greens, lettuce, celery, dandelion, lotus seeds, and lily bulbs.

3. Best soup dish - Tomato soup

Drinking more tomato soup in summer provides both nourishment and hydration. Tomato soup (best consumed after cooling) contains lycopene, which has anti-prostate cancer and heart protection effects, making it especially suitable for men.

4. Best meat - Duck meat

Do not think that summer only allows light foods. Summer is also a good time for nourishing the body, provided you choose the right supplements. Here we recommend duck meat. Duck meat is rich in protein and other nutrients needed in summer and can also prevent and treat diseases. The reason lies in the fact that ducks are aquatic birds with a cool nature. According to traditional Chinese medical principles, "treat heat with cold," it is particularly suitable for people with internal heat or those who experience low-grade fever, weakness, poor appetite, dry stools, and edema, which are common in summer. Duck stewed with ham and sea cucumber produces a broth that nourishes the yin of the five organs. Duck cooked with glutinous rice makes a porridge that nourishes the stomach, replenishes blood, and generates fluids, greatly benefiting those recovering from illness. Duck stewed with kelp softens blood vessels and lowers blood pressure, preventing arteriosclerosis, hypertension, and heart disease. Duck stewed with bamboo shoots can treat hemorrhoidal bleeding...

5. Best beverage - Hot tea

During summer, beverages are indispensable, but the best choice is not various cold drinks, beer, or coffee, but rather ordinary hot tea. Tea is rich in potassium (on average, 100 grams of green tea contains 10.7 mg of potassium, and black tea contains 24.1 mg), which quenches thirst and relieves fatigue. British experts' experiments show that hot tea's cooling effect far exceeds that of cold drinks, making it one of the best summer refreshments.

6. Best nutrient - Vitamin E

Vitamins can help you safely navigate through the summer, and the title of "best" goes to vitamin E. German scientists emphasize that people face three major dangers in summer: intense sunlight, ozone, and fatigue. Vitamin E can significantly reduce these risks. Vitamin E is abundant in foods such as malt, bran bread, walnut paste, dairy products, etc. You can consume more of these during the summer. If necessary, you can take vitamin E tablets, with a daily dose of 15-60 mg being sufficient.

7. Best exercise - Swimming

The best summer exercise is swimming. Swimming not only exercises arms, legs, waist, and abdomen but also benefits internal organs such as the heart, brain, lungs, and liver, especially the blood vessels, earning it the nickname "vascular gymnastics." Additionally, the calories burned in water are significantly higher than on land, so swimming can also help reduce excess weight and achieve a fitness goal.

8. Best clothing color - Red

Many people believe that wearing white clothes is the best option for summer, but wearing red is actually better. The reason lies in the fact that red visible light has the longest wavelength, absorbing a large amount of ultraviolet rays, protecting the skin from damage, preventing skin aging, and even cancer. Other colors (including white) have weaker effects in this regard. As for fabric, mixed-fiber T-shirts are optimal, with the ideal blend being 33% cotton and 67% polyester.

9. Best cooling device - Fan

From a fitness perspective, the best cooling device is the fan. Although fans are considered "antiques," their fitness benefits surpass any modern cooling equipment. Shaking a fan is a form of exercise that can train limbs (if you consciously switch to using your left hand, it can activate the right brain, develop right-brain potential, and prevent strokes). Simultaneously, the wind generated by the fan feels most pleasant.

10. Best health measure - Regular sleep schedule

In summer, the days are longer, with earlier sunrises and later sunsets. Therefore, people's living and sleeping schedules should be adjusted accordingly, with late bedtime and early wake-up being optimal. Moreover, it is crucial to adhere to a fixed sleep schedule as much as possible. "Fixed timing" aligns with the body's biological clock. If you can maintain a regular schedule year-round, it creates a "dynamic pattern," allowing the body to adapt best and predictably. In summer, it is even more important to set a strict sleep schedule and not violate it unnecessarily, even on weekends. The best bedtime in summer is between 22:00 and 23:00, and the best wake-up time is between 5:30 and 6:30. The hot weather in summer can easily affect sleep, but once you establish a routine of regular sleep, it becomes easier to exclude climatic interference with sleep. You will naturally and comfortably wake up in the morning, and your body's biological clock will not become disordered.
