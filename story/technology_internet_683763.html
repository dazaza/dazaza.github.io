Market segmentation requires sufficient market analysis

Market segmentation is the process by which a company divides the entire market into different groups of consumers based on their different needs. Its objective basis is the heterogeneity of consumer demand. The main basis for market segmentation is a group of customers with consistent demands in a heterogeneous market, which essentially means finding homogeneity in a heterogeneous market. The goal of market segmentation is to aggregate, that is, to bring together consumers with similar needs in a market with different demands.

Market segmentation is an extremely complex process. The premise of market segmentation is to conduct extensive market research and analysis, choose the right product direction, and develop a promotion plan before the product can be launched into the market. Even so, it may not necessarily receive a good response from the market in the short term. The key to segmentation is to meet the different demands of consumer groups with mature products, truly highlighting the advantages of product segmentation, in order to win the market. Otherwise, it may result in huge costs with little effect.

Market segmentation cannot just play with concepts

In the process of home decoration, consumers pay more attention to the health and environmental performance of indoor curtains. Therefore, curtain companies cater to consumer preferences, segment curtain products, and hype concepts such as N-in-one, antibacterial, negative ions, odor-free, aldehyde removal, etc., filling the eyes of consumers. Conversely, the segmentation of China's architectural curtain market is clearly messy, with one company launching multiple products simultaneously into the market without focus. Nowadays, many curtain companies raise the banner of environmental protection and launch various types of eco-friendly products, secretly performing the trick of [changing the soup but not the medicine]. Products that seem segmented have not actually segmented the market; changing the packaging and product name makes it a new product, which has not achieved true [segmentation], directly leading to vicious competition among curtain companies for market share.
