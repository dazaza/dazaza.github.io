Why did Baidu invest in 100% Mobile?

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 20:20:55

Baidu's investment department has been very active this year. Even in the face of Alibaba's aggressive acquisitions, it has not shown any weakness. After acquiring 91 Wireless for $1.9 billion, this time Baidu has set its investment sights on hardware. Yes, a mobile phone company. Baidu is going to make phones again.

The mobile phone company invested by Baidu is named Shenzhen Bai Fen Bai Digital Technology Co., Ltd., and its founder is Xu Guoxiang, who recently challenged Lei Jun and Xiaomi's Redmi phone.

In fact, it was predictable that Baidu would invest in hardware. Not long ago, they released products like Little Du Portable WiFi and Media Stick. Investing in mobile phones is reasonable. Moreover, companies like Google, Microsoft, Amazon, and Alibaba are all involved in hardware. It would be hard for Baidu not to get involved in hardware. Even Qihoo 360, after reflecting on last year's underperforming 360 Special Edition phone, Zhou Hongyi is also actively planning. It is rumored that 360's 5G router will soon be launched, and not long ago, 360 also had contact with Meizu.

The key question is, why invest in Bai Fen Bai? It's definitely not because both companies have the word "bai" (hundred) in their names.

Before investing in Bai Fen Bai, Baidu and the company were already closely connected. In June this year, its phone brand 100+ collaborated with Water Wood as a joint brand, using Baidu Cloud ROM. Recently, Bai Fen Bai launched a phone priced at 798 yuan, also equipped with Baidu Cloud ROM. It is said that this phone was sold out within ten minutes each time it was launched on JD.com for three times.

In short, Bai Fen Bai's phones have remarkable sales, directly boosting Baidu Cloud's market share. This is the direct driving force behind Baidu's investment in Bai Fen Bai.

Xu Guoxiang of Bai Fen Bai is relatively well-known in the mobile phone circle. Known as "Little Beard," he has a Huawei background: he joined Huawei in 1995, established Huawei's Data Communications Product Department in 1998, serving as director and chief engineer. In 2002, Xu Guoxiang joined Yulong Coolpad as vice president and general manager of the mobile phone division; in 2004, he returned to Huawei as global marketing director of Huawei Terminal Company.

Xu Guoxiang can be considered a veteran in the mobile phone industry, personally involved in the operations of Huawei's first smartphone and Coolpad's first 3G phone. Both Huawei and Coolpad systems share the common feature of having deep operator resources. And Shenzhen-based mobile phone companies, due to the洗礼of the counterfeit trend in the pre-smartphone era, are all down-to-earth entities.

In June last year, Xu Guoxiang joined the internet phone craze - launching the 100+ personalized custom phone, promoting it as a joint brand with Water Wood and a C2B internet phone. Xu Guoxiang said, this is the first one, so it is relatively cautious, only releasing 100,000 units online. This year, they plan to increase the scale, and with Baidu's internet entry point resources, it is believed that sales will be even hotter.

Of course, after Baidu's investment, there will be a natural tilt towards entry point resources. However, what Baidu values is not just Bai Fen Bai's C2B phone model, but more importantly, the offline channel that Bai Fen Bai has spent 7 years building - integrating nearly 70,000 mobile phone shops across the country and supplying them directly with phones.

Now, these 70,000 stores will do more than just sell phones; they will become the landing points for mobile internet in third- and fourth-tier markets.

A responsible person from Bai Fen Bai said, "Previously, these stores had to source goods from provincial, municipal, county, and township-level mobile phone agents, being marked up layer by layer. However, Bai Fen Bai supplies directly, making prices at least 20% lower." Now, many mobile phone manufacturers and operators are supplying these 70,000 family-run stores through Bai Fen Bai's B2B small-scale direct supply platform, with an estimated turnover of 20 billion yuan in 2013.

Internet companies have advantages online, but offline, they also hold half of the market. The most direct reflection is that even Taobao and Alipay are painting wall advertisements in rural areas this year, promoting "To live well, you must go to Taobao." Moreover, various internet companies' promotional efforts involve pre-installing apps on phones.

Baidu's cooperation with Bai Fen Bai can be seen as a complementary integration of offline and online channels. Or, to put it more appropriately, Baidu's investment in Bai Fen Bai is actually a plan for door-to-door promotion of Baidu Cloud products.

Bai Fen Bai covers 70,000 retail stores, mostly in third- and fourth-tier markets, or even deeper channels. Facing the mobile internet, these stores have their own transformation needs. Bai Fen Bai hopes to transform them into "mobile internet experience stores" - unified UI, selling not just phones but also services. For example, helping users flash their phones, download movies, install apps, help operators release numbers, and earn some money on the side.

This idea from Bai Fen Bai is somewhat like car 4S stores for after-sales maintenance. Currently, more than 10,000 stores are covered in Guangdong. Xu Guoxiang said that the company will provide them with one-stop procurement, lower prices, training, and resources from telecommunications and internet companies.

This "down-to-earth" systematic channel is something no internet company can refuse.

Baidu's online entry point advantage combined with Bai Fen Bai's offline channel, despite the undisclosed amount of this transaction, at least sounds good.
