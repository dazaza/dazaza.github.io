Zhou Hongyi Exposes Himself as the Spoiler in Baidu's 91 Transaction Case: Participated in Driving Up the Price

by anonymous on 2013-07-24 18:09:34

July 18 news: Qihoo 360 Chairman and CEO Zhou Hongyi was recently invited by PingWest to give a semi-formal speech at Stanford University, during which he revealed that he had attempted to disrupt Baidu's $1.9 billion acquisition of 91 Wireless. He also claimed that if it weren't for the presence of 360, a certain company (note: implying Baidu) wouldn't have felt such immense pressure to offer this price.

Zhou Hongyi stated that 360 was also one of the early investors in 91 Wireless. In the early stages, he had advised the people at 91 Wireless not to sell their company, or at least try not to sell unless absolutely necessary. Later, when offers started coming in, Zhou Hongyi still suggested they avoid selling and strive for independent development unless an offer too good to refuse came along.

"They asked me what would be an offer too good to refuse? I said it should be at least $2 billion," Zhou Hongyi mentioned, thinking at the time that no one would offer such a high price for 91 Wireless. "Unexpectedly, after some haggling, they managed to close the deal at $1.9 billion."

360 and 91 Wireless indeed share a deep connection, but their relationship might not be as good as Zhou Hongyi portrayed it. Two years ago, before 360 ventured into the mobile assistant field, their strategy was to collaborate with NetDragon’s 91 Mobile Assistant to deeply penetrate the mobile internet sector. 360 partnered with NetDragon to establish a research and development center, sending R&D personnel to assist in developing the new version of 91 Mobile Assistant.

Now, 91 Wireless' 91 Mobile Assistant and 360 Mobile Assistant have become each other's biggest competitors, leaving NetDragon with a sense of nurturing a tiger to its own detriment. It is unlikely that Zhou Hongyi and the senior management of 91 Wireless can remain on good terms. However, 360 was indeed a disruptor in the sale of 91 Wireless. Tech Tencent learned that just before the finalization of Baidu's acquisition of 91 Wireless, Zhou Hongyi flew to Fujian to discuss with Liu Dejian, the founder of NetDragon, attempting to prevent the transaction from being completed.

In addition, Zhou Hongyi had another motive for trying to stop Baidu's acquisition of 91 Wireless: After Baidu acquired 91 Wireless, the combination of Baidu Mobile Assistant, 91 Mobile Assistant, and Android Market could quickly dominate the wireless distribution field, posing a threat to 360 Mobile Assistant. This acquisition would also serve as an important counterbalance to 360's offensive moves against Baidu's search domain.
