Nutritional Value and Health Benefits of Fresh Jujubes

by gddxp97717 on 2012-03-07 15:04:00

Fresh jujubes are sweet, crisp, and refreshing, with a unique flavor and especially rich nutrition. They not only contain abundant nutrients and mineral elements but also have rich medicinal substances. According to analysis by food science research units, every 100g of fresh LuBei winter jujube fruit pulp in the crispy mature stage contains 23.2g of carbohydrates, 430.54kj of calories, 1.46g of protein, 0.2g of fat, 25.76g of total sugar, 0.34g of total acid, 1.6g of crude fiber, all of which are indispensable for the human body. The fresh winter jujube fruit pulp also contains moderate amounts of mineral and trace elements. Every 100g of fresh jujube fruit pulp contains 12mg of calcium, 402.26mg of potassium, 18mg of phosphorus, 20mg of magnesium, 0.32mg of iron, 0.265mg of zinc, 0.12mg of copper, 0.214mg of manganese, and 0.08mg of selenium. The appropriate intake of these elements is very beneficial to the human body. Among them, potassium can enhance muscle excitability, participate in protein, carbohydrate synthesis, and heat energy metabolism; calcium and phosphorus are the main components of human bones and teeth; magnesium has the function of inhibiting nerve excitability, participating in protein synthesis within the body, muscle contraction, and regulating body temperature; selenium has the function of improving human intelligence, etc.

Fresh jujubes also contain abundant vitamins. In every 100g of fresh jujube fruit pulp, there is 300-600mg of Vitamin C, higher than apples, pears, grapes, peaches, hawthorns, oranges, lemons, kiwis, and other fruits, making it the "champion among hundreds of fruits." Additionally, jujube fruits also contain Vitamins A, B, P, E, etc., earning the reputation of "living vitamin pills." For the human body, Vitamin C is an important substance, and a deficiency of Vitamin C in the diet can lead to scurvy. Medical studies show that Vitamin C can soften blood vessels and is an ideal drug for preventing hypertension, coronary heart disease, and arteriosclerosis. At the same time, Vitamin C easily combines with carcinogenic nitrosamine, having a cancer-preventing effect. Although dried jujubes also contain Vitamin C, most of it is destroyed during the drying process, with losses exceeding 97%. Generally, the Vitamin C content in dried jujubes is only 7-12mg/100g. Therefore, from the perspective of eating fresh jujubes and nutritional health care, the development and utilization of fresh jujubes have extremely significant importance.

Jujubes possess high medical value and health functions. The fruits, seeds, leaves, wood core, and branch bark can all be used as medicine. Both the "Shennong's Classic of Materia Medica" and the "Compendium of Materia Medica" record their medical value, believing that jujubes have the effects of strengthening the spleen and nourishing the stomach, enriching the blood and invigorating the spirit. Modern medical studies show that jujubes are effective for conditions such as insufficient qi and blood, anemia, weak lungs causing coughs, neurasthenia, insomnia, hypertension, septicemia, and allergic purpura, and they have preventive and therapeutic effects on cardiovascular diseases like coronary heart disease and myocardial infarction. There is a long-standing saying in Chinese folk culture: "Grains plus small jujubes surpass lingzhi grass," and "Eating three jujubes daily leads to longevity." Thus, jujubes have become highly valued nutritional supplements for both domestic and international therapeutic health care.

Moreover, fresh jujubes contain abundant amino acids. According to measurements by the Food Science Department of China Agricultural University, every 100g of fresh LuBei winter jujube fruit pulp contains a total of 1.38mg of amino acids, and the composition of these amino acids is good. It contains eight essential amino acids that the human body cannot synthesize, such as isoleucine, leucine, lysine, methionine, phenylalanine, threonine, tryptophan, and valine. These eight amino acids are present in moderate amounts, suitable for absorption by the human body. It also contains ten non-essential amino acids, such as glutamine, aspartic acid, leucine, glutamic acid, glycine, alanine, serine, histidine, arginine, and proline, all of which have relatively high contents. These can synthesize various antibodies, hormones, and other substances that regulate physiological functions within the body. Methionine and cysteine are sulfur-containing amino acids, which enhance the immune system of the human body. Modern science has also discovered that fresh jujubes contain high levels of cyclic adenosine monophosphate (cAMP) and cyclic guanosine monophosphate (cGMP), physiological active substances that participate in multiple physiological activities within the human body. They can regulate the immune system, enhance cardiac muscle contraction, protect coronary arteries, lower cholesterol, and inhibit the proliferation of cancer cells. The cAMP content in fresh jujubes remains relatively high at around 37.45-47.25 pmol/g?FW during low-temperature storage.
