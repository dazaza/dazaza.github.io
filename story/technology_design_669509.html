2. Method for Making Fern Corn Cake

A method for making fern corn cake, realized by the following steps: weigh out 15 grams of ferns and chop finely; prepare 50 grams of cornmeal, stir evenly with water, mix the fern shreds into the well-stirred cornmeal, and fry in a flat-bottomed pan until cooked. The beneficial effect of this invention is: each component has its own advantages, collectively resulting in a nutritionally rich and balanced product with a unique taste, making it especially suitable for those with special taste requirements.

3. Method for Making Fresh Tender Corn Cake

A method for making fresh tender corn cakes, characterized by: ① Harvesting fresh corn, peeling, and removing the silk; ② Washing and grinding into a pulp-like consistency; ③ Mixing the corn pulp from step ② with flour, stirring evenly, where the weight percentage of flour is 20-30%; ④ Placing the mixed corn pulp from step ③ into a container, mixing with water, stirring evenly to form a batter, where the added water's weight is 10-30% of the mixed corn pulp's weight; ⑤ Adding yeast for fermentation, time 30-50 minutes, temperature 20-25℃; ⑥ Placing into molds and baking for 5-8 minutes, baking temperature 160-170℃. This method differs from traditional production processes, with reasonable and simple operating procedures, producing soft and good-tasting corn cakes.

4. Method for Making Fried Corn Cake

A method for making fried corn cakes, consisting of the steps of boiling, soaking, de-husking, wet grinding, shaping, and frying. Using the above technical solution, the shape of the fried corn cake can be diversified, no food additives are used, it is a green healthy food, rich in various nutrients, while retaining the inherent flavor of corn. Since both the boiling liquid and soaking liquid are alkaline, there is also a small amount of alkali contained in the final product, giving the fried corn cake characteristics of intense fragrance and crisp texture, suitable for modern tastes.

5. Equipment for Making Corn Cakes from Puffed Corn Dough

This invention relates to equipment for continuously and accurately rolling puffed raw corn dough into thin sheets in a determined manner, characterized by containing a conventional roller press with an output mesh conveyor, at the end of which a high-speed mesh conveyor is placed, driven by a motion generation and transmission system. This system includes a motor, a reducer, and a drive on the roller shaft of the high-speed conveyor. On the sides of the high-speed conveyor, three rectangular support structures with central slits on their upper parts and two shells are vertically fixed. On this structure, three rods are supported in a detachable manner transverse to the high-speed conveyor; two of them suspend separate metal pre-rolling nets at both ends like pendulums, these pre-rolling nets having smaller contact and traction surfaces with the high-speed conveyor net; on the third rod, a group of metal final rolling nets for these small corn cakes are hung like curtains at one end, these final rolling nets having larger contact and traction surfaces with the high-speed conveyor net.

6. Processing Method for Green Corn Cakes

A processing method for green corn cakes, involving refrigerating green corn cobs, peeling, removing the silk, de-kerneling, washing, then crushing to 510 mesh size, adding 10-15% of their weight in water and appropriate ingredients, stirring evenly, placing in cake molds, steaming until cooked, vacuum packaging in preservation bags. Products produced by this method preserve natural nutritional components and the original tissue substance and freshness of green corn, allowing people to eat fresh green corn cakes year-round without time restrictions, enriching dietary structure, while also opening new industrial pathways for developing new corn foods.

7. Method and Apparatus for Stacking Corn Cake Pieces

A method and apparatus for stacking curved triangular snack pieces such as corn cake pieces (10) for packaging in cylindrical containers. The pieces (10) are fed into a first U-shaped vibrating trough (30), which automatically extends the top corner (A) of each piece (10) over the opposite bottom edge (B) of adjacent pieces (10) and automatically orients the pieces so that the line (20) around which they are bent is parallel to the longitudinal axis (21) of the U-shaped trough (30). The pieces (10) are then fed into a W-shaped trough (40), where oppositely directed pieces automatically fall into opposite trough sections (44, 46) of the W-shaped trough (40). The pieces (10) are then rotated 120 degrees, thus being re-oriented automatically so that their upturned top corners (A) point in the direction of movement, and as the pieces slide upward in an overlapping manner, they are automatically stacked together.
