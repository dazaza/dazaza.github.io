Essential materials for bedroom decoration

by longersing on 2009-09-16 15:42:56

The bedroom, in the home space, is a very practical and private place. To put it more mysteriously, the bedroom is simply the owner's self-portrait. For many people, one third of life is spent in the bedroom. In this completely private space, you rest and relax thoroughly.

Therefore, an ideal bedroom always makes people feel pleased both physically and mentally - satisfying both emotional and physiological needs, eventually becoming an oasis in life.

The position of a bed within a room reflects the owner's living habits and lifestyle - how do they start and end each day? Chinese Feng Shui even links the position of the bed to the health and happiness of the entire family, believing that an improperly placed bed can lead to sleepless nights.

Size of the bed

So, if space permits, the bed should be as large as possible. The ideal size for a double bed is 160 cm x 200 cm. (The standard size for a double bed is 135 cm x 195 cm, which actually means each person has less space on the bed than a baby sleeping in a small bed).

Position of the bed

Regarding the position of the bed, I've interviewed many designers, and they all believe that the bed should face the room. There should be a bedside cabinet and lamp on either side of the bed; it’s best if the bed does not touch the wall, because it’s difficult to make the bed, and over time it will leave marks on the wall.

Choosing the right bed

The third question about beds is, what kind of bed should we choose? The beds used by modern people actually consist of two parts: the bed frame and the mattress. Most bed frames on the market have similar styles, so you can buy one that suits your appearance and material preferences based on your economic situation. When choosing a bed frame, primarily consider its aesthetics, while also considering whether it harmonizes with the bedroom style you expect.

Material of the bed

Mid-to-high-end bed frames on the market are mainly divided into wood and metal types. Wood types mostly include walnut, cherry, and beech. Metals include steel pipes, copper, forged, and aluminum alloys. Additionally, some Southeast Asian-style wicker products can also be seen in some home markets. Mattress is important

To sleep comfortably, the mattress is key. Experience tells us that a good mattress should be sturdy enough to support your body in all areas, and when you lie down on it, the state of your spine should resemble the state when standing. Of course, it shouldn't be too hard, causing your hips and shoulders to exceed their normal curvature, nor should it be too soft, making it difficult to move your body during sleep.

History of Beds

Evolution of Bed Design

From an interior effect perspective, the bed is the visual focus and center of the bedroom, often dominating the balance of other furnishings in the room. Functionally speaking, the bed interprets our most primitive aspirations for life. Life enjoyed in bed is the most vivid and natural. In a sense, the history of beds is the history of bedrooms. The design evolution of beds and bedrooms includes the development history of science, art, and philosophy.

In ancient times, people slept on sturdy and beautifully designed beds. They laid animal skins and soft fabrics on wooden, stone, or metal bed frames to enhance comfort. The height and decoration of the bed were also symbols of wealth and privilege. Wealthy people decorated their beds with metal, ivory, wood carvings, and even jewels to display their privileged status. At the time, people firmly believed that the higher the bed was from the ground, the more successful and prosperous the family would be, and this prosperity would continue. Similarly, the specifications of the bed were also a symbol of personal and family status. The larger the bed, the more it displayed the owner's wealth. Luxurious beds were quite popular in the 14th-15th centuries and later in many countries. Over 30 types of fabrics were draped on the beds, making the wooden parts of the beds almost invisible. The voluminous pleated bed curtains, feather decorations, and fringed bed edge hangings added color to the already luxurious beds. However, after such luxury persisted for several centuries, beds became lighter, more elegant, and simpler in the 18th century. The iron-framed beds and copper pipe beds from that period remain the basic models for many modern bed designs today.
