Covering foreign media reports, Gasgoo learned that as Opel, the Europe branch of GM has been losing money for years, there came a news that GM would close two factories of Opel in Europe and drastically lay off employees. However, the union of Opel denied the news about closing factories and laying off employees. The Wall Street Journal quoted unnamed senior GM executives as saying that considering Opel's long-term losses, GM decided to further reduce its operating cost, which would include shutting down Opel's factories in Bochum, Germany and Ellesmere Port, UK and carrying out layoffs. There are 3,100 and 2,100 employees respectively in the factories in Bochum and Ellesmere Port. Later, the Detroit News reported the response from the senior Opel union. The Opel union said that it had not received any notice regarding the news that GM would violate the agreement by closing factories and laying off employees. In order to respond to rumors, it decided to specify relevant plans from 2012 to 2014. According to the original agreement, GM should not close any factory or lay off any employee in Europe before the end of 2014. An Opel spokesperson disclosed that the agreement Opel previously signed covered the period from 2011 to 2014, but only the content of the part from 2010 to 2011 was given. Therefore, Opel needed to specify the content of the plan after 2012.
