The pattern of market fluctuation will continue. e9

by ww0963800 on 2010-04-15 22:43:46

I. Market Summary: On Thursday, the market continued with a volatile consolidation trend. The Shanghai Composite Index closed at 3,164.97 points, down 1.21 points or 0.04%; the Shenzhen Component Index closed at 12,513.29 points, down 44.41 points or 0.35%. The total trading volume of the two markets was 250.806 billion yuan, basically the same as the previous trading day. After opening flat in the morning, the stock index fluctuated and fell. Subsequently, favorable economic data for the first quarter stimulated a rapid rise in the market. However, the pullback of heavyweight stocks, along with another sharp decline in small and medium-sized cap thematic stocks and Growth Enterprise Market (GEM) stocks, led to the stock index fluctuating downward. In the afternoon, the stock index received support near the 120-day moving average line and rebounded. By the close, both markets ended with a negative cross star. On the board, ceramics, coal, finance, and oil industries showed leading gains.

II. Market Analysis: Today's market maintained a volatile consolidation pattern. The National Bureau of Statistics announced the main economic data for the first quarter today: GDP growth for the first quarter was 11.9%, and CPI growth was 2.2%. The data indicated a good momentum of economic recovery with inflation slightly lower than market expectations. Influenced by this positive news, heavyweight stocks once strengthened and pushed the stock index to a rapid rise. However, small and medium-sized cap thematic stocks and GEM stocks fell sharply again, dragging the market to fluctuate downward. In the afternoon, the outflow of panic selling triggered a volume surge and a plunge in the market. But then, heavyweight stocks such as finance, coal, and the Petrochemical Dual Giants exerted force to protect the market. After the Shanghai Index probed the 120-day moving average line, it received support and rebounded. From a technical perspective, the Shanghai market formed a negative cross star with a long lower shadow, reconfirming the support strength around the 120-day moving average line after a pullback. Meanwhile, the recent horizontal consolidation trend of the market indicates that investors are relatively cautious in the face of uncertainties such as the possible introduction of stricter real estate regulation policies by the government and the potential change in market operation patterns after the launch of stock index futures. Overall, under the current market environment, high valuation varieties like small and medium-sized caps and the GEM still face pressure for further pullbacks. However, heavyweight stocks, due to their low valuation levels and stimulation from the upcoming launch of stock index futures, have some upward momentum. Under the game between bulls and bears, it is expected that the short-term market will continue with a volatile operating pattern.

III. Operation Strategy: High valuation varieties such as small and medium-sized caps and the GEM still face pressure for further pullbacks. However, sectors and individual stocks with substantial themes and good performance support still offer certain transactional opportunities. It is recommended that prudent investors continue to layout quality blue-chip stocks in sectors such as finance, coal, non-ferrous metals, building materials, and machinery while controlling their positions. Short-term investors can continue to pay attention to high-quality varieties with performance support in themes such as the Internet of Things, triple network convergence, 3G communication, smart grid, and Expo while avoiding buying at high prices.
