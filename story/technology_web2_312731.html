30th Birthday + 300 Premier League Appearances = A Forlorn Owen; Capello Can No Longer Tolerate Sir Alex

Sina Sports - The day before yesterday, Owen celebrated his 30th birthday, marking the start of his thirties in this week. Today, Owen reached another milestone in his career—his 300th Premier League appearance. However, Sir Alex Ferguson only gave him 15 minutes to showcase his skills.

In the 71st minute, Obertan was substituted by维尔贝克 (Welbeck), and thus the player who most frequently passed to Owen left the field for rest. Welbeck preferred to work alone. In the 76th minute, Owen finally came on, replacing Wayne Rooney who had scored the first goal for Manchester United in this match. Rooney was visibly unhappy as he walked off the pitch without any interaction with Owen. Nevertheless, Owen actively approached Rooney for a high-five despite Rooney never looking at his former national team golden partner.

In the 82nd minute, Patrice Evra crossed from the left flank, and Owen tried a header over the advancing Wolves goalkeeper Haneemann but missed the target. This was Owen's sole attempt at goal in the match. Nonetheless, in that moment, we still sensed Owen's sharp instincts near the goal. In the 85th minute, Valencia crossed from the right flank, and Owen took possession at the edge of the box, quickly passing it to Welbeck on the left side. Owen then moved into the penalty area to prepare for an overlapping run, but Welbeck decided to shoot directly instead. His weak shot was easily saved by Haneemann. Berbatov gestured in dissatisfaction with Welbeck’s decision, while Owen silently walked away with his head down. In the 87th minute, Valencia curled a pass with his right foot, and Owen controlled the ball with his chest inside the box but was unable to turn due to the tight marking of the opposing defender, eventually being forced out of the penalty area by the central defender.

Owen played for 14 minutes in total, and his stats were rather unimpressive: seven passes all accurate, five successful forward passes, one shot which went wide. Without Obertan's assists, no starting position, no goals, and no "Happy Birthday" sung by the crowd of 73,709, Owen concluded his 300th Premier League appearance in such a manner. It seems that his chances of boarding a flight to South Africa are fading. However, it appears that the real obstacle preventing Owen from going to South Africa is Sir Alex Ferguson, not Fabio Capello. Capello even subtly expressed his dissatisfaction with Ferguson's arrangement, given that Owen had started only three times in the first 17 league games this season.

Capello has not forgotten about Owen; instead, the Italian has been quietly keeping tabs on him. This can be easily understood from Capello's pre-match comments: “Owen is one of the players I am monitoring. The last time I watched him play was on TV during the Champions League where he scored three goals, but in the following game, he didn't start. For this reason, I often ask myself why? Why does Rooney always start while Owen doesn't? He is one of England's most important players.”

Legendary English striker Gary Lineker also strongly recommended that Capello take Owen to South Africa: “In my view, the logic is simple: if Owen can play, he will play for Manchester United. If he can play for Manchester United, he can score goals. Owen's movement and ability to find space around the penalty spot are better than any of his competitors, although Defoe has made significant progress in that area this season. When English players see Owen in the dressing room—the winner of the European Footballer of the Year award, a star on the World Cup stage, and Germany's nightmare—they will all become fearless.”

However, within Manchester United, Owen is not the only one worrying about his World Cup dream. Similar to Owen's situation is Darren Hargreaves. The Manchester United midfielder has been out for 14 months due to a knee injury. During this match, Hargreaves sat on the bench alongside John O'Shea, and the camera caught several shots of his expressionless face. The Daily Mail analyzed that Hargreaves' delay in returning was entirely due to psychological reasons. Nonetheless, when Capello mentioned his condition, he offered him ample hope.

“Two months ago, I spoke with him. I met him at the Manchester United club when he was just preparing to start training, but he still cannot play now. Why not (consider including him)? He still has enough time to recover. Hargreaves needs to play matches; those who don’t play aren’t within my consideration. He has six months to regain his best form, but he needs to play. I will select players who are fit to play in May next year, those who can play all the games.”
