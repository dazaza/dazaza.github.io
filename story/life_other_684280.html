Love, that beautiful and melancholy sentiment

I laugh at you for wearing plastic leather shoes

In the first year of junior high school, you came to school wearing plastic leather shoes. Back then, you didn't care about this kind of attire at all.

It was when we went to a meeting together, and while climbing the stairs with you walking in front of me, I saw your plastic leather shoes. The shoes were made of plastic but looked like leather, coffee-colored, and already quite old. At the time, I had a sense of looking down on you; I thought you were outdated. Ironically, back then, I was also very unsophisticated, but I just hadn't noticed it myself.

Later, I told my desk mate about this matter, and the two of us laughed heartily. Back then, I was the best student in the class, arrogant and not putting anyone else in my mind. But who would have thought later that I would actually fall for the boy who wore plastic leather shoes? And even more tragically, perhaps he never knew about my feelings, and I was always alone on this glamorous yet sorrowful adventure, without the real you by my side...

You took off your plastic leather shoes and became the top student in the class

Finally one day, you found out about my ridicule towards you. You said nothing, didn’t take off the shoes right away, and continued to wear them for some time. Later, I saw you coming to school in white canvas shoes, so I asked you why you weren't wearing those shoes anymore. You replied indifferently, "They're broken."

From that point on, I never laughed at you again.

But it was during the spring of that year that I completely lost to you! My failure was utterly devastating. Indeed, your math scores were higher than mine by several dozen points, and I was far behind you. Everyone saw your success and my failure! Thus, my status plummeted, while you transformed into the top student in the class.

Since then, I've been unable to recover, but I fell for you.

That single failure dealt a severe blow to my confidence, and from then on, I couldn't recover. You, however, kept climbing step by step, entering the top ten students in the grade.

Yet, I didn't feel a hint of jealousy towards you; instead, I thought you were amazing.

As I experienced these ordinary stories, I still didn't realize that I was just an ordinary girl, nothing special. I viewed myself through the arrogance created by my past glories, thinking there was nothing wrong with me. Many years later, when I finally realized my own ordinariness, I understood why you didn't like me. This understanding, however, felt so powerless and empty!

Your excellence made everyone look at you differently, including me. Gradually, I started to think you were excellent, handsome, and good. Love, I suppose, starts from these little thoughts and slowly develops.

So, I began to think I liked you.

Actually, everything was quite absurd. My thoughts were based on my feelings, not yours. Even more tragically, I thought you liked me too. My illusion further plunged me into this glamorous yet sorrowful adventure…

You wrote your first love letter, but the recipient wasn't me

In the summer of the second year of junior high school, I learned about my one-sided affection. Before that, I had always looked at you with shining eyes, copied your homework, because you sat behind me, because you were the best student in the class—everything seemed so natural.

However, I was immersed in my own fantasy world, ignoring the fact that your attention extended far beyond just me. At the time, I didn't know, nor could I have imagined, that you actually liked her.

My arrogance, my illusions, and your indifference and misunderstanding did not align in any way.

If we were rays, I extended toward you, but your direction pointed elsewhere. No matter how much I tried to extend toward you, your direction and gaze never turned back to discover my existence…

Why did you have to like her...my best friend...I didn't even have the slightest intention to compete with her...

Disappointment, confusion, heartache, self-mockery.

Afterward, I no longer smiled at you, no longer borrowed your homework, but pretended to be indifferent in front of her. My coldness couldn't match your fondness for her. You could stay up late to tutor her in physics, you could give her things without caring what others might say. My coldness was incomprehensible to everyone, and it was meaningless against you. Just like my liking you was meaningless because you didn't understand.

You wrote your first love letter, giving it to the first girl you liked, but the recipient wasn't me...

You gave her flowers with the same name as yours, and I left disheartened while you two took photos

When I visited your house once, I asked if there were flowers with the same name as yours. I thought your name sounded nice, and I really liked it. You said yes, and I was overjoyed, asking for one. She said she wanted one too, and suddenly your eyes lit up as you happily agreed.

But when I saw your change, I made an excuse saying I didn't want any.

How could I bear to plant flowers with the same name as yours after knowing the truth? How could I bear to plant the same flowers you gave her, each carrying different meanings?

Years later, when I saw those flowers, they appeared as vivid red sadness.

Later, when they suggested taking photos, seeing the two of you, I had no mood at all and silently left.

Later, when I saw the photo of the two of you together, my heart felt something indescribable. Pain? Suddenly, I remembered that before this, there was a group photo taken in the literature club with you, her, and me, but I never saw that picture… The only photo I ever took with you was our middle school graduation photo.

Thinking about it, this unrequited love should come to an end, shouldn't it?

I thought I would never see you again, but fate arranged it this way

After you became the top student in the class and my complete failure, I lost all confidence to rise again. In the third year of junior high school, I played around a lot and didn't focus on studying.

I knew everything about you and her. She told me she wouldn't be with you.

One afternoon, in the dormitory, she asked me if I liked you. It must have been a question she had wanted to ask for a long time. I nodded, looking at the mountains outside the door covered with bamboo shells glistening in the bright blue sky, and said, "It's all in the past, it's all old news."

I never thought I could enter the No. 1 Middle School for the high school entrance exam.

Indeed, I couldn't have made it, but you did. However, the teacher approached me, saying that I was only a few points short, and if I paid some money, I could get in. My mother loved me and decided to let me go.

In the campus of No. 1 Middle School, I saw you.

And you actually greeted me!

Who would have known! You never used to greet me!

I thought we would be in different schools, and I would never see you again, but fate had other plans.

You two didn't end up together, and I ended up going home with you

She told me that you held her hand during the National Day holiday.

At that moment, her tone was somewhat hesitant, fearing she might hurt me, and I knew it. I just asked, "What happened after that?" She said nothing significant happened afterward...

At that time, I believed what she said, but now I don't. There must have been something else that happened, and she was just afraid of hurting me.

But my heart was still heavy.

In the end, you two didn't end up together, and I don't know why... But somehow, something feels missing... What it is, I don't know...

After the college entrance exam, I walked alone, carrying a box of books, which was heavy. Moving alone, with a lot of luggage, I felt helpless and wanted to cry.

Throughout high school, I couldn't forget you, but it was also a kind of abandonment that I couldn't forget.

While waiting for the bus outside, I unexpectedly saw you!

You dragged your luggage towards me, greeted me, waited for the bus, and silence ensued.

But my heart was still excited.

On the bus, you helped watch my luggage, and I still thought you were good.

When you reached your destination, got off the bus, and waved goodbye to me, I watched your retreating figure with reluctance, allowing myself to let go.

The belated courage and confession

Because of you, I started writing what are called novels. Those fictional stories clearly bore traces of you, your story, my story, the story between you and her, our story... Now I realize how deeply you touched me! You left such a profound mark in my youthful years, unforgettable, but it wasn't you; rather, it was my liking, abandoning, and not forgetting someone in such a beautiful era, along with all the joys and sorrows.

Such a story, should it come to an end?

But during that trip home, I saw her. She gave me your number and seemed to hint at something.

When I was wounded all over, when I was trying hard to forget some things, I impulsively confessed my feelings for you.

I didn't expect anything, yes, I've never held much hope.

My phone received no messages, one day, two days, three days...

The result I knew was exactly as expected.

I finally realized that I never intersected with any memories in your mind. I might not even be a regular character in your story. From beginning to end, it was always just me, foolishly walking down that road, relying on imagination and sorrow to complete my journey. Tragically, you were never there, and I was never in your course or vision...

Deleting your number, I truly let go.

But I cannot forget that in those beautiful years, there was that beautiful and melancholic sentiment.
