March 2nd was the transfer deadline day for the 2012 Chinese Super League (Weibo) and China League One leagues. Just a few minutes before the transfer window closed, Changchun Yatai (Weibo) completed their final signing of the season with AEL Limassol from Cyprus, securing a two-year contract for Brazilian central defender Cassino. With this, Changchun Yatai signed four foreign players at the rate of one per day: Lubinkovic, Wilton, Mosquera, and Cassino. Together with Ismaelov retained from last season, they form the five "foreign guns" under head coach Sablic.

When money is not an issue, whether big-name foreign players can adapt to the Chinese Super League becomes a technical challenge. Thus, Shanghai Shenhua (Weibo) waved their cash to bring in Anelka, which led to a rise in the value of foreign players in the CSL, as well as an improvement in overall quality. However, for Changchun Yatai, head coach Sablic seems not to be overly impressed by big-name players. Even though there were options of high-priced Premier League and Portuguese Liga players, he still adhered to the principle of "using the familiar rather than the new," choosing four players that he had been tracking for a long time.

As an Eastern European coach, Sablic has a preference for players who play in Eastern European clubs. Of the newly signed four foreign players, only Mosquera comes from German 2. Bundesliga side Union Berlin; the remaining three all played in Eastern European clubs. Wilton comes from Romanian top-tier club CFR Cluj, Lubinkovic from Serbian SuperLiga side Vojvodina, and Cassino from Cypriot first division team AEL Limassol.

Although these four new signings are not familiar to Chinese fans, they are tailor-made for Sablic's tactical ideas. Both Lubinkovic and Cassino are versatile players; the former can play as both an attacking midfielder and a midfielder, while the latter can serve as both a central defender and a defensive midfielder. Wilton and Mosquera, the two forwards, come from Brazil and Colombia respectively, and are technically skilled forwards. If paired with domestic midfielders like Du Zhenyu (Weibo), Wang Dong (Weibo), Jiang Pengxiang (Weibo), Pei Shuai, Zhang Wenzhao, and Liu Weidong, Sablic's tactical combinations will become even richer.

However, replacing Arechaga just hours before the transfer window closed was somewhat perplexing. After all, this forward, who joined the team mid-season last year, demonstrated a very high goal-scoring efficiency, scoring six goals in fourteen matches, making him the team's top scorer. However, when Changchun Yatai stopped playing a counter-attack style, Arechaga struggled to fit into Sablic's tactical system without support from long balls. During the Kunming winter training and the Cyprus training camp, Arechaga scored only one goal in thirteen friendly matches, far from his impressive performance last season.

Replacing Arechaga might be risky, but it was a reluctant decision made by the Changchun Yatai coaching staff after prolonged observation. After all, Arechaga still had two years left on his contract with Changchun Yatai. However, just as Changchun Yatai decided to loan out Arechaga, Hohhot Dongjin followed up and completed the deal. To ensure Arechaga's interests, Yatai included a clause in the loan agreement ensuring that his salary and benefits would remain unchanged.
