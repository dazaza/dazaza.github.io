[Original] 007: Skyfall ending exposed in stills, recommended by Wang Yidai

by annilalskk888 on 2012-02-23 14:27:32

Bond is officially back in Shanghai!

According to the official website of the 23rd Bond film, "Skyfall," which is currently in production, the first official still was released at noon (Beijing time) on their official site (click to visit). Even more exciting for fans is that according to the official description, Daniel Craig, who continues to play 007, will make his official return at a launch event held in Shanghai! It’s also reported that the film has a good chance of being released in mainland China. Regarding all the excitement around this new 007, Craig calmly stated, "[The filming process has been very interesting. We have an excellent script, a great director, and a strong creative team.]"

Compared to the behind-the-scenes photos leaked just before the Spring Festival, this new image shows Bond gripping his gun against the backdrop of Shanghai's night scenery, highlighting his stern side—a stark contrast to the relaxed, shirtless version of him sitting by the pool in swim trunks.

According to the official plot summary previously released, M's past comes to light, and Bond's loyalty to his own agency is tested. When MI6 faces peril, Bond must go to any lengths to eliminate threats, protect his colleagues, and ensure the safety of the organization.

"Skyfall" is directed by Sam Mendes. In addition to Craig, the two "Bond girls" have also been confirmed: French model Bérénice Marlohe plays the "alluring and mysterious" Sévérine, while British actress Naomie Harris portrays the "rookie-level" field agent Eve. Veteran actors Javier Bardem will take on the role of the main villain, while Ralph Fiennes remains tight-lipped about his character. However, based on previous insider reports, he will replace "veteran" Judi Dench as the head of MI6, M.

Compared to Dench, who will soon step away from the role of M, David Arnold, who has composed music for five consecutive "007" films, took one step further—he simply couldn’t spare the time to compose for the film due to his role as the Music Director for the 2012 London Olympics. Fortunately, his successor was quickly locked in as Hollywood's golden composer Thomas Newman, a long-time collaborator with director Mendes, whose work is highly anticipated.

The budget for "Skyfall" ($150 million) is $50 million less than that of "Quantum of Solace." The film is set to premiere in the UK and Ireland on October 26, with a North American release date two weeks later on November 9.
