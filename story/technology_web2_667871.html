Obtaining the qualification certificate of a first-class construction engineer and being employed by a construction project exploration, design, construction, supervision, bidding agency, cost consulting unit, etc., this means that registration in the construction department is not mandatory. For those who do not want to take risks, they can register with non-construction and supervision units, which can increase income while avoiding the risks brought about by problems in construction. Here, there is one point to be understood:

1. Regarding the reasonable pricing issue of first-class construction engineer affiliation

The "Regulations on the Administration of Registered Construction Engineers" was passed at the 112th executive meeting of the Ministry of Construction on December 11, 2006, and is now announced, coming into effect from March 1, 2007 (Ministry of Construction Order No. 153). For specific content, please refer to relevant documents. Article Seven of the regulations states: Obtaining the qualification certificate of a first-class construction engineer and being employed by a construction project exploration, design, construction, supervision, bidding agency, cost consulting unit, etc., which means that registration in the construction department is not mandatory. Those who do not want to take risks can register with non-construction and supervision units, which can increase income while avoiding the risks brought about by problems in construction. One point to clarify here is that it is registration, not affiliation. The contracts related to the registration unit will be discussed later. Registration to a unit is legal, whereas affiliation is illegal.

2. Regarding the supply and demand issue of first-class construction engineers

Currently, except for super-grade enterprises requiring 50 first-class construction engineers, the new qualification requirements for other grade enterprises have not yet been released. First-grade enterprises certainly need them. According to the current number of first-class construction engineers who have passed and the number required by first-grade and above construction enterprises, there is an oversupply. However, because the regulations stipulate that registration can only occur in one unit, a considerable portion of construction engineers have already registered due to other professional qualifications, thereby creating a situation where demand exceeds supply. Also, some first-class construction engineers work in second-grade or below construction enterprises, reducing the number available for first-grade and above construction enterprises. Furthermore, according to the new engineering management measures, starting from 2008, all large-scale projects must be managed by a first-class construction engineer as the project manager. According to the classification of projects, except for landscaping, general contracting projects worth over 30 million RMB are considered large-scale projects. Refer to the notice regarding the issuance of the "Trial Standards for the Scope of Practice of Registered Construction Engineers," which contains detailed classifications. Second-grade enterprises have a minimum registered capital of 20 million RMB and can undertake projects five times their registered capital, meaning second-grade enterprises can also undertake projects worth over 30 million RMB, which requires the use of first-class construction engineers. Therefore, second-grade enterprises also require a certain number of first-class construction engineers. From the perspective of qualification management, the calculation of the number of first-class construction engineers has not yet included the analysis of the number of ongoing or planned construction projects in the national construction industry and the number of first-class construction engineers. According to statistics from relevant articles, the number of first-class construction engineers required for engineering projects far exceeds the current number of first-class construction engineers who have passed. Due to this reason, temporary construction engineers have emerged. It is evident that first-class construction engineers are in short supply in the near future.

3. Regarding the remuneration issue of first-class construction engineers

Risk and profit go hand in hand; in economic matters, they always exist. Focusing on the issue of first-class construction engineers, we need to change our mindset and transform traditional concepts into modern ones. Whether it is affiliation or borrowing, both are illegal and lack legal protection. Therefore, we need to change our mindset. Since policies allow us to register with a unit, we should focus on registration. Because the "Regulations" not only permit first-class construction engineers to serve as project managers but also hold other positions. Specifically, see Article Twenty-One of the "Regulations": Registered construction engineers can engage in the overall management or construction management of construction projects, construction project management services, technical and economic consulting for construction projects, as well as other businesses prescribed by laws, administrative regulations, and the State Council's construction authorities. From the perspective of risk, serving as the person in charge of a construction project carries significant responsibility since the quality of current projects is a lifelong system. Therefore, the remuneration for being hired as the person in charge of a construction project is relatively high, while other management or consulting services have relatively lower remuneration. Remuneration varies based on market supply and demand and the city of employment. If you do not wish to take on too much risk, find a unit where you can register and only serve as a consultant or advisor, which is particularly suitable for those who do not need fixed working hours. If you desire higher remuneration, find a unit where you can serve as the person in charge of a construction project. If you are working full-time, you should have confidence in the quality of the project. If not full-time, a warning is necessary: always keep your registration certificate and practice seal yourself, especially the practice seal. Do not sell out your entire life for a few thousand dollars. Based on preliminary investigations, in first-tier cities or more developed cities, serving as a consultant or advisor, the annual salary is approximately 15,000 to 20,000 RMB for part-timers, about 20,000 to 100,000 RMB depending on the scale of the engineering project and time spent on-site. Full-timers earn over 100,000 RMB annually. Salaries in second-tier cities are relatively lower, varying by location.

4. Regarding the contract issues with the registration unit

I have reviewed some popular templates online, and they are not entirely appropriate. Many clauses are unlawful and invalid. Contracts are not effective just because both parties sign them. If the content violates relevant regulations or laws, even if signed, they are considered invalid contracts or contain invalid clauses. Therefore, when signing contracts, careful consideration is necessary. Pay particular attention to the "Regulations." Avoid using the term "affiliation" and refrain from drafting affiliation contracts or agreements. Regardless of which unit you register with, even if you do not perform actual work, you still generate benefits for them. Many people mistakenly believe that registering with a company without participating in construction project management and receiving remuneration is affiliation, which is incorrect. First, registering with them enhances their strength and usable resources. Even if you visit the company once a year, providing only advisory or consulting services still constitutes technical support. Second, initial registration may not involve construction project management, but that does not mean future involvement is excluded. Jobs are dynamic; perhaps someday you become a full-time construction manager. For those unwilling to work full-time at the company, consider signing an irregular work schedule contract for greater flexibility. Ideally, further details should be specified. For those with limited spare time or their own work, hiring as a consultant or advisor is recommended. For those with more spare time but unable to work full-time, part-time work can be considered. Besides basic remuneration, additional travel expenses or other subsidies can be provided based on the size of the managed project or the number of business trips, or calculated based on the number of days spent on-site.
