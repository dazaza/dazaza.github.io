A gentle breeze is blowing.

by xmrqggs on 2012-03-08 22:36:32

At this moment, I'm waiting for my dear one, he's on the phone with mom... Lying in bed browsing through Folded Net [http://www.fanzhewang.com], I opened a window, and gentle breezes are blowing in. The leaves rustle, and my mood is great~

I'll soon drift off to sleep with this good mood.

The landlord's house is a bit pricey, but it can't stop me from loving the cross breeze; I'm very satisfied.

The bedroom back home used to be just as airy and cool when I was little. Back then, Beibei and I would sit on the floor, drawing huge cartoons on the back of wall calendars. Cartoon books were scattered all over the floor, and we'd stay up until 1 or 2 AM before sending her home.

During summer vacation, my parents would sleep in the next room and not really bother us much.

I don't quite remember if we had deep conversations, but the scenes of us quietly reading our comic books individually stick out vividly in Folded Net [http://www.fanzhewang.com].

She read super fast, and I always tried to catch up with the ones she had finished reading. Then I would return them downstairs and borrow more.

Saying that our childhood and teenage years were filled with anime is no exaggeration at all.

Recently, I've been habitually reminiscing, signs of aging.

I turned on the Japanese channel of Douban Radio, and couldn't stop.

Listening through entire nights, recalling amidst studying.

Yesterday, I left a comment on the Innkeeper's blog, hehe,

It's been 5 years since I watched "Unnamed" in 2006.

This phone call has been going on for quite a while.

During college, whenever it was the weekend and he went home, I would always feel upset.

Now, with months-long business trips Folded Net [http://www.fanzhewang.com], it seems like I'm almost getting used to it.

Is it because memories are beautiful that I often reminisce? I want to shout out my loneliness.

And now I am indeed shouting it out.
