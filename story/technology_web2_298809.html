Vanke "puts new wine into old bottles", M&A + cooperation to solve the problem of land shortage

by 6zns086q7e on 2009-12-08 11:23:37

Expanding business through mergers and acquisitions, as well as cooperation, has been Vanke's usual strategy in recent years to reduce land costs. Especially during the period when "land kings" frequently emerged, Vanke performed the "buddy-buddy" drama with great vigor and success.

As of the publication of Vanke's October report, in the first ten months of 2009, Vanke had acquired 34 plots of land in 19 cities, among which 14 were obtained through cooperation or acquisition. There were also two plots where Vanke explicitly stated it would seek partners. In reference to previous examples, in 2007, 41% of the newly added planned area acquired by Vanke was obtained through "mergers + cooperation", and this figure rose to 84.5% in 2008.

Behind the complex moves, Vanke is experiencing the predicament of regional land "shortage" due to difficulties in acquiring land through public bidding.

The mechanism of perception triggers mergers and acquisitions. At the 2008 annual results meeting held in Hong Kong, Wang Shi explained Vanke's M&A strategy: "One is to enter regions where there was no business before through M&A, and the other is to supplement the missing product line."

"The old bottle is being filled with new wine." In November, Guangzhou Vanke announced that it had acquired the residential land use rights and all interests of the Huadu Yinhuwan project through equity acquisition. A relevant person from Guangzhou Vanke also revealed to the China Business Journal that the sellable project "Tianhe Yipin" of Xin Hengji Real Estate in Guangzhou would also be transferred to Vanke, and an announcement would be disclosed soon.

"It is very clear that the targets of these two M&A actions by Vanke are directly aimed at sellable projects," a latest report from an authoritative third-party institution that has been tracking Vanke for a long time pointed out. In 2008, Vanke achieved a sales area of 1.82 million square meters in the Pearl River Delta region, while in the first three quarters of 2009, the Pearl River Delta market had already realized a sales area of 1.367 million square meters. "We believe that due to the deepest adjustment in the Pearl River Delta region in 2008, Vanke cautiously slowed down its land reserve and project development in this region, which is the main reason for the current insufficient supply in the Guangzhou market."

I observed that due to the two-time leading price reduction in 2008-2009, Vanke's sales in Guangzhou and Shenzhen have remained rapid, but Guangzhou also faces the dilemma of land "shortage."

In fact, Vanke headquarters has long noticed the situation of land shortage and insufficient supply in Guangzhou. The perception mechanism originates from the "monthly meeting" held every month by Vanke, where the institute publishes the monthly statistics of the "sales-to-approval ratio" of 14 key cities, comparing this number with the company's internal sales and inventory situation in the region to judge the coldness and heat of the regional market and the company's sales speed. According to the company's internal control system, once the comparison of these two sets of numbers issues a warning, the company must adjust its strategy and respond promptly.

"The 'sales-to-approval ratio' refers to the proportion of market sales to approved presale areas over a certain period, greater than 1 means the market supply cannot keep up with the sales speed." Vanke explained so. At the shareholders' meeting in mid-September, Xiao Li, executive vice president of Vanke, revealed that when the policy turned in September 2007, the sales-to-approval ratio of the 14 major cities closely monitored by Vanke had already dropped below 1, indicating oversupply in the market; and the data in July 2009 when the policy was slightly adjusted was 1.78.

"Vanke has never been strong in the Guangzhou land market," real estate expert Han Shitong (blog) believed. To compete in a place full of big players obviously goes against Vanke's principle of "not becoming a land king."

On the other hand, acquiring finished projects or mature land can save time costs for Vanke. "The current M&A actions have a longer negotiation period, indicating that the 'shortage' Vanke was preparing in advance," Han Shitong pointed out.

For the land market where high-premium "land kings" often appear, Vanke always keeps a distance. The corresponding countermeasure is that while entering potential second and third-tier cities in large strides, Vanke seeks opportunities to cooperate with "landlords."

On September 28, Vanke officially signed a strategic cooperation agreement with Nanjing Transportation Group, which is the largest state-owned capital platform in Nanjing, owning rich local resource advantages, including the 2007 Nanjing land king Sanzhahe plot that will cooperate with Vanke.

On November 18, Yunnan Urban Investment Board announced that Yunnan Urban Investment would jointly invest 10 million yuan with Vanke to establish a joint venture, engaging in real estate development and operation, property management, and related businesses in Yunnan.

"This is different from Vanke's previous tricks of seeking land in the secondary market, it counts as a 'new trick'," Han Shitong believed. "Binding" state-owned background "landlords" through cooperation agreements to acquire land at lower prices is the secret of this "new trick" to reduce costs.

In Bai Wangang's view, the president of Huacai Consulting Group, Vanke's highly automated processes and internal controls are the internal support for Vanke to continue expanding cooperation. Directly using the regional headquarters to promote its standardization strategy makes it easier to seek land that conforms to "standardization," and Vanke's comprehensive expertise in the entire product line, from primary development, urban village renovation, small, medium, and large unit types, to villas, gives Vanke broader "options" for cooperation.

In fact, compared to the current active strategy of "clinging to tycoons," Vanke's previous land cooperation was more about "buddy-buddy" with peers, jointly acquiring land and developing projects.

Taking Vanke and COFCO Property's cooperation as an example. In 2006, Vanke and COFCO jointly acquired the Guangzhou Jinsandao B3725D01 plot for 1.75 billion yuan, the project was limited housing type, the floor land price was 4033 yuan per square meter, while the surrounding building prices were only 4000~6000 yuan per square meter at the time, making this plot a typical case of "flour being more expensive than bread."

Leveraging to enter the market is also another consideration for Vanke when seeking cooperation. On September 7, Vanke cooperated with COFCO for the first time to acquire land in Beijing, with a transaction price of 2.2 billion yuan, achieving a premium rate as high as 263%.

"When acquiring land through cooperation, Vanke does not pay too much attention to the high premium rate of the project," said Li Wenjiang, chief market analyst of He Fu Glory. "Strong cooperation helps to explore the maximum value of the land, with brand effect, capital, and design capabilities, how could the project fail?"

Of course, Vanke's cooperation model is not without risks. Developing land with others is obviously not as easy as doing it alone, and the brand reputation, financial status, and strategy changes of the partner may all become uncertain factors for the project.
