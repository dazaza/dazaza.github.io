"Aerobic Information" Communication Skills for Interpersonal Interaction

by jqrxass on 2009-10-04 21:01:12

Aerobic Information is about how to choose useful information platforms, express one's thoughts, ideas, creativity, and talents, so that more people can know about oneself.

Using "clever" ways to promote oneself does not necessarily mean "taking advantage of opportunities." Communication with others requires skills, and Aerobic Information aims to tell readers how to be "clever." It informs readers on how actions can influence others, affect the most important people in their lives, and achieve success.

Preface to "Aerobic Information"

The 21st century is often referred to by us as the Information Age. Economic information, political information, and cultural information flood towards us in overwhelming amounts. Taking book publishing as an example, in 2007, China published a total of 240,000 types of books. This means that on average, every three minutes, a new book is published. By the time you finish reading this page, it is estimated that another new book has been published in China.

First Chapter of "Aerobic Information" - Lifting the Veil on "Aerobic Information"

Without solid food, a person can survive for about 30 days;

Without water, a person can survive for about 7 days;

Without oxygen, a person can only survive for a few minutes.

Indeed, there is nothing in this world more important to humans than oxygen. Let's play a very simple game: while you read this text, hold your breath, pinch your nose with your hand, and tightly close your mouth to ensure no air enters your respiratory system. Look at the watch, phone, or alarm clock next to you and measure how long you can persist in an oxygen-free environment.

The vast majority of living organisms in this world rely on oxygen to survive. Through the chemical reactions caused by oxygen within living organisms, we acquire biological energy, which allows us to survive.

Second Chapter of "Aerobic Information" - How Information Magic is Created

As human beings, we are constantly transmitting information: speaking is transmitting information, writing is transmitting information, smiling is transmitting information, gesturing is transmitting information, and even remaining silent is also transmitting information...

Correspondingly, we are also constantly receiving information: when we listen to others speak, we are receiving information; when we read books, we are receiving information; when we watch movies, we are receiving information; when we browse the internet, we are receiving information; and even when we do nothing and daydream alone, we still receive information to some extent – information from within ourselves.
