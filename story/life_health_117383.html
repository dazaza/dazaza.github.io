Bone Grinding, Bone Cutting Facial Plastic Surgery

Bone grinding/bone cutting facial plastic surgery

A beautiful facial contour is the primary condition and most significant hallmark of a person's physical beauty. With increasing demands for overall physical aesthetics, facial bone restructuring has become increasingly popular. Easterners consider small faces with pointed chins to be beautiful, leading to a mainstream aesthetic preference for petite faces. As a result, there has been a growing trend towards slimming the face, with more people opting for facial bone grinding and bone cutting surgeries.

Facial bone grinding/bone cutting surgery mainly includes:

1) Mandible Angle Plastic Surgery: MANDIBLE REDUCTION SCULPTURE

Mandible angle plastic surgery is currently the most common type of face shape modification surgery. The normal mandibular angle is 120°, which gives the appearance of a smooth, rounded look without sharp edges. An enlarged mandibular angle can present in two ways: one where the angle is too small, resulting in a square-shaped "national" face, and another where the mandible protrudes outward and thickens, giving the face a trapezoidal shape that does not meet aesthetic standards.

Currently, as surgical techniques, results, and safety have improved, not only those with significantly enlarged mandibular angles who have square faces and severely affected appearances undergo mandibular angle plastic surgery, but more women with slightly enlarged mandibular angles are also opting for the procedure, becoming "Korean-style" petite-faced beauties.

Mandibular angle enlargement surgery can be performed via external approach, internal approach, or combined approach. Since the external approach leaves noticeable scars, it has now been abandoned. Bone removal methods include mandibular angle arc osteotomy, lateral plate splitting, and mandibular angle bone grinding. The lateral plate splitting method provides better narrowing effects on the face but generally has limited effects on lifting the mandibular angle. Mandibular angle bone grinding removes the lateral plate, which can lift the mandibular angle, causing less damage to the patient and ensuring higher safety. Mandibular angle arc osteotomy offers better lifting effects on the mandibular angle and obvious narrowing effects on the face, making it the mainstream international surgery for face shape modification.

The mandibular angle osteotomy involves making a 6-8 cm incision along the anterior margin of the mandibular ramus inside the mouth, extending downward to the vestibular groove anterior to the second molar, reaching the periosteum. The masseter muscle and inner periosteum are completely separated from the lateral plate of the mandibular ramus using a periosteal elevator. The medial pterygoid muscle at the inner side of the mandibular angle is then stripped using a periosteal elevator. The Linderf retractor is placed at the posterior border and angle of the mandibular ramus to fully expose the mandibular angle. A reciprocating saw or oscillating saw is used while flushing with water to cut out the required bone segment. The types of osteotomies include vertical sagittal osteotomy, oblique osteotomy, inverted L-shaped osteotomy, and T-shaped osteotomy.

2) High Cheekbone Plastic Surgery: ZYGOMATIC REDUCTION SCULPTURE

Westerners consider high cheekbones to be beautiful, and many Western beauties possess facial features characterized by high cheekbones and prominent contours. Easterners also have relatively high cheekbones, but in Eastern culture, overly high cheekbones forming a triangular face, with a wide and protruding mid-face, give an impression of coldness and appear gaunt and aged.

Sino-Canadian Kexiang Plastic Surgery uses American-imported specialized bone grinding instruments equipped with automatic protection devices and cold light source systems, allowing the surgery to be performed through a small 3cm incision inside the mouth to grind down the bones. Due to clear visibility and good exposure of the bones with minimal bleeding, meticulous shaping can be done without damaging the facial nerve and facial artery. Additionally, since the excess bone is ground away, the bone surface remains very smooth, resulting in a natural post-surgical face shape with no external scars.
