"What is trash, what is love" - the emotional labyrinth of urban youth.

by jqrxass on 2009-10-02 21:51:49

"What Is Trash, What Is Love" is a unique style of love novel, fresh, restrained, and thought-provoking. "Little Ding" is a hallmark character in Zhu Wen's novels, ever-present like a shadow. After this novel, the author never wrote about Little Ding's stories again. Therefore, we can consider "What Is Trash, What Is Love" as a sort of farewell performance for "Little Ding." Like a dreamlike journey, the author leads us through four seasons with varying landscapes, traversing the inner labyrinth of confusion of a contemporary youth. Zhu Wen transitions from reality into fiction in a Zhu-Wen-style caress and then incorporates fiction back into reality with extraordinary control.

**Part One of "What Is Trash, What Is Love"**

When Little Ding returned home, he found Little Chu hurriedly putting on makeup in front of the bookshelf in the living room. A palm-sized mirror was placed on the top layer of the bamboo bookshelf. Little Ding, covering his bruised face with his left hand, walked straight into the bathroom. He bent over and strained to rinse the blood off his face and hands under the faucet above the bathtub. "Why didn't you go to work today?" Little Ding asked inside the bathroom, his tone sharp. Little Chu quickly explained, "I'm leaving right now, I'm going to work. How could I not go? A client invited me to dinner tonight at Chaozhou City nearby, so I stopped by on my way." They had cohabited in this apartment for a year. To avoid arguments, Little Chu moved back to the company dormitory but would visit on weekends or Wednesdays. Indeed, their rapidly deteriorating relationship stabilized somewhat after this arrangement.

**Part Two of "What Is Trash, What Is Love"**

Little Ding leaned his head back against the chair, unable to recall why he had come here tonight. He no longer wanted to stay any longer. He stood up and headed towards the door. Liu Meilin immediately called out timidly behind him, "Hey, hey, aren't we playing anymore?" Little Ding gave a bitter smile, "Playing what? I'll leave first." Liu Meilin said more urgently, "The card game, are we not playing cards? Cheng Jun should be here soon, right?" She turned her face toward Hu Jie. Little Ding remembered; he had been invited to play cards tonight, gambling a little money. Hu Jie also looked at him with an expression that seemed expectant. Little Ding couldn't understand how they could still have the interest to continue the game tonight? Hesitating for a moment, Little Ding said, "Forget it, Cheng Jun is unreliable, I don't think he's coming today." Liu Meilin said, "How could that be? Why don't we give his house a call and remind him again?" As she spoke, Liu Meilin moved towards the phone. Suddenly, he stopped and looked pleadingly at Little Ding, "You make the call."

**Part Three of "What Is Trash, What Is Love"**

Little Ding tossed and turned in bed for half the night but still couldn't fall asleep. He suddenly realized that he himself couldn't understand what he was anxious about. It wasn't just some ordinary illness that he was dealing with, not just a problem with his body that caused no pain. Little Ding thought again that perhaps going out for a walk to distract himself wasn't such a bad idea. He got out of bed, preparing to give Cheng Jun another call to ask if he had changed his plans. After dialing the number, the ringtone sounded once before Little Ding hastily hung up. He had forgotten again - the number he remembered was wrong. Little Ding didn't know where this number came from or how he remembered it, only knowing that through this number, he could find a woman who had nothing to do with his life in the world. What kind of situation was this?
