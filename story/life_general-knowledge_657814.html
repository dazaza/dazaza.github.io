City of Sorrow - Jingxuan

Without your warm embrace, I always feel cold. Without your tender words, I always feel sad. Without your deep call, I always feel lost.

For the first time in a small Chinese test, I accidentally ranked second in the class. When the teacher read out my name and score, I secretly rejoiced sitting behind you.

You turned back and looked at me in surprise: "Is that you? Your name is Jingxuan?"

"Yeah, it's me." You are Xiaoran.

From then on, a disaster was destined to happen.

Xiaoran, the famous piano prince of the school, the teacher's favorite, the model of classmates, and the pride of parents. And I gave him the title of love strategist. Because he was good at writing poems, boys in the class who chased after their desired girls would ask him to write poems. All those who asked him for poems successfully got the girl they wanted. So the title of love strategist definitely suited him.

Even in the key class, there were some students who were weak in certain subjects, and I was one of them. I was naturally weak in math while Xiaoran had already mastered the XY axis. I was completely confused. The teacher worriedly found me, and I indicated that I needed help from classmates. As I wished, Xiaoran was assigned to help me. I liked the way Xiaoran analyzed things and explained appropriately. He was an extremely handsome boy, with not-so-long eyelashes that slightly curled upwards. His distinct contours revealed the handsomeness of a seventeen or eighteen-year-old boy.

"Hey, what are you thinking about? Do you understand?" Xiaoran noticed that I was distracted.

I glared at him, "Thinking about you, and I do understand!"

Xiaoran's face immediately turned red. Seeing my serious expression, Xiaoran thought I was joking.

Even though I concealed my admiration for Xiaoran very well, someone still noticed and spread it as gossip throughout the school. Xiaoran ignored it, and I didn't stop it either. I focused all my attention on studying.

During a class meeting where I hosted, each student prepared a performance. Xiaoran played a piano piece, which sounded great. The classmates cheered and asked him to compose a poem on the spot. Outnumbered, he improvised one and dedicated it to me.

"The bright moonlit night fills the building with paintings, holding hands only for you to stay, butterflies flying together in harmony, Xiaoran is crazily infatuated with Jingxuan."

After the words, many classmates started teasing me, leaving me in a difficult situation. Perhaps it was because I didn't understand poetry, or perhaps it was because of my previous teasing of you as a revenge.

Not long after, we unknowingly started dating.

The pressure of high school was immense, and we needed love to balance it. Thus, we became the ideal couple in our classmates' eyes.

Our homeroom teacher called us for a private talk. I waited outside the door, and only heard you say, "Understood, teacher. We will be mindful and won't let it affect our studies." Then the teacher called me in, and I simply told her, "We will turn our love into motivation for study!" Only then did the teacher let me go.

My diagnostic exam results were disappointing. On the night the scores came out, I didn't go to self-study. You found me on the grass next to the soccer field, sitting down gently beside me, "How did you end up here?"

"Sometimes I want to get away from people and be closer to plants. They are clean and safe and won't hurt anyone," I said dejectedly.

"You're so strong, one bad exam result doesn't mean anything. Don't be like this," you said lightly, patting my shoulder and pulling me into your arms. In the dim light, our shadows stretched long and close.

Getting into a prestigious university in Beijing was our unspoken agreement, urging us to give our all.

During the summer vacation, we went home together. There was still a distance between your house and mine. You got off the car, waving goodbye to me for the next semester, but I never saw you again.

The summer without you was particularly boring. I called you, but no one answered. Just when I was feeling sad, the phone rang. The caller ID showed your name. I picked it up.

The other side talked for a long time, and I listened silently without saying a word. After hanging up, I screamed desperately and collapsed on the bed powerless.

This call was from your mother. She said: Xiaoran passed by the reservoir and saw a group of children swimming. Coincidentally, the reservoir gate opened, and the powerful suction pulled them towards the drainage outlet. Below the water gate was a deep valley, and being sucked down meant certain death. Xiaoran saved them, but due to excessive physical exhaustion, he was caught in the powerful suction, dragged into the gate, and flowed into the valley with the water.

When people found him, he was already gone.

I couldn't accept the fact that you had left. On the day of your funeral, your family called me asking me to attend, but I made excuses to avoid it because I couldn't face the reality of losing you. I locked myself at home, quietly recounting our past. Xiaoran, does this mean you have betrayed me? Or is it fate's trick?

When school reopened, I hesitated to go, fearing to face life without you. Teachers and classmates avoided mentioning you in front of me, giving me encouragement so that I wouldn't become too sorrowful. Your seat in the classroom was empty, and I could no longer hear familiar voices. How could I bear such pain? I avoided places familiar to us for fear of reliving painful memories. I immersed myself in studying to numb myself, reducing the chances of missing you. Since we shared the same dream, I worked hard carrying both our dreams, achieving our goal as I was admitted to our ideal university.

Before leaving, I visited you, talking to you for a long time. I love you, Xiaoran! It was you who held my hand and led me out of the darkness. Until now, it might continue for a long time.

But I will try to move forward.

Sorry!

Revisiting our love was just a fleeting moment. I waved goodbye to you and turned around, grieving in ways you'll never know.
