Suo Chuanping: I approached every leader I could find, but they all passed the buck. Some said there was a new leader, so I should look elsewhere; others said I didn't have a contract... Time was wasted, and I also spent about 1500 yuan on round-trip travel expenses. When I finally received my 5000+ yuan salary, I had to pay over 300 yuan in taxes out of my own pocket.

In 1990, Luo Conghu passed away from cancer. Since then, the Luo family no longer pursued the wages, leaving Suo Chuanping to fight alone. In 1991, Suo Chuanping moved to Yining City, making it even less convenient to claim his salary. "For many years, I've been trying to get my money back. The money owed to me should be paid, as it is a matter of integrity. I don't care how much it costs," Suo Chuanping said.

Hope for reclaiming the unpaid salary emerged

Reporter: Have you ever calculated the cost over these many years?

Interest doesn't matter

In recent years, Suo Chuanping has worked odd jobs at construction sites, earning about 1500 yuan per month. "Whether it was back then or now, this sum of over 5000 yuan means a lot to me, so I must reclaim it," he said. "I did the work, and they should pay me. If they don't have the money, I can wait, but they cannot refuse to pay."

In September this year, Suo Chuanping read a news report about Cha County Party Secretary Wang Yiwen hosting a "People's Livelihood Revisit" event. The report mentioned that Wang Yiwen helped someone reclaim wages that had been owed for over a decade.

More than twenty years have passed, and Suo Chuanping's temples have begun to gray. His family advised him not to pursue the unpaid salary any longer.

Reporter: Now that you've finally reclaimed your money, what do you plan to do with it?

In 1990, local leadership was about to change. "With the change in leadership, it became even harder to reclaim the salary. The two of us found Shuang Cheng, the former mayor of Chabucha'er Town who had participated in the leadership project, and asked him to write a certificate stating we had worked at the county's first primary school." However, relevant departments still delayed payment citing financial difficulties.

From the age of 30 to nearly 60, after 27 years of struggle, Suo Chuanping, a migrant worker from Chabucha'er Autonomous County in the Ili Prefecture of Xinjiang, finally reclaimed his overdue salary of 5393.4 yuan with the help of the county party secretary. "My former partner passed away from cancer and never saw this day," Suo Chuanping said.

The distance from Yining to Chabucha'er Autonomous County is about 20 kilometers. Suo Chuanping said that back then, a round trip between the two places would cost seven or eight yuan in transportation fees, and over the years, he spent hundreds of yuan on these trips.

If I don't have money, I can wait

Suo Chuanping: What can 5000 yuan do now? It's not worth much, not enough even for one hospital stay.

Even though he successfully reclaimed his salary, Suo Chuanping's tone remained calm during a phone conversation on the evening of November 23rd. He lives separately with his wife, renting out a two-story house on a bustling street in Yining City. All three of his children have good jobs. Receiving over 5000 yuan in salary, "finally gave me some satisfaction."

Because he didn't know how to use the internet, Suo Chuanping asked his friend Li Minghui for help to send an email to the secretary. To Li Minghui's surprise, a week later, Suo Chuanping received a call from the relevant department of the county government, "The secretary saw the situation you reported and has ordered the relevant department to handle it within 15 days. Prepare the relevant certificates." Suo Chuanping hurriedly went to the county education bureau, but because the original mayor of Chabucha'er Town, Shuang Cheng, who wrote the certificate for Suo Chuanping, wasn't present, Suo Chuanping's attempt to reclaim his salary failed again.

In mid-October, Suo Chuanping once again asked someone to send an email to the county party secretary. At the same time, he also found Shuang Cheng. Shuang Cheng expressed his willingness to testify in person. Shuang Cheng said, "To persist for so long requires real perseverance."

Suo Chuanping said that on November 11th, with Shuang Cheng's testimony, he finally received the overdue salary of 27 years, totaling 5393.4 yuan.

Suo Chuanping: Doing this job was such a loss. Regardless, I've received the salary, achieved my goal, and gained some satisfaction. Comprehensive

The family of Suo Chuanping said that apart from the salary, interest for 27 years should also be reclaimed, but Suo Chuanping said: "Being able to reclaim the salary already makes me very satisfied, completing one of my major tasks. I don't want to calculate too much."

Dialogue

Reporter: Over the years, what methods have you used to reclaim the money? Have you ever calculated how much it cost to reclaim this money?

It's mine, so it should be given to me

At 58 years old, Suo Chuanping is from Chabucha'er Town in Chabucha'er Autonomous County. In his early years, he worked as a welder at the county labor company. In the late Spring Festival of 1984, at the age of 31, Suo Chuanping partnered with his friend Luo Conghu to build an iron fence for the county's first primary school. The project was contracted on a turnkey basis, and Suo Chuanping reached a verbal agreement with the county education bureau and construction bureau to pay the salary after the project was completed.

Suo Chuanping said that in the 1980s, the county was very poor, and funds were tight, which they could understand. But not paying the salary for such a long time raised questions of integrity.

Within half a month, the project was successfully completed, and Suo Chuanping was entitled to a salary of 5393.4 yuan, while Luo Conghu was entitled to over 6000 yuan. However, due to the tight budget at the time, neither the education bureau nor the construction bureau had sufficient funds, and thus the over 10,000 yuan in salaries were put on hold.
