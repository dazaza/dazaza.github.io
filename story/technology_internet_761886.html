As the Internet has entered an oligarchic era, what shall the entrepreneurs do?

by geekzhang on 2013-05-14 15:15:29

China's Internet is entering an era of oligopolistic competition from free competition.The recent acquisition and equity participation behaviors of the three giants - Alibaba, Tencent, and Baidu - are evolving into a situation where medium and small-sized companies are swallowed through capital operations, and the Internet power map is redrawn. Data shows that the total market value of China's listed Internet companies is about 80 billion yuan RMB, while Tencent and Baidu alone account for 56 billion yuan. As for the unlisted Alibaba, even with the most conservative calculation, its valuation has already exceeded 45 billion yuan RMB.On the other hand, with the closing of overseas listing windows, dollar venture capital (VC) lacks an effective exit mechanism, leading to a low point in the domestic venture capital market. Start-up companies face extraordinary difficulties in financing, and the alliances and consolidations among giants make the already strained survival environment for start-up companies even worse.Products being imitated or plagiarized by giants, user channels monopolized by giants, and capital oppression from giants – how can those internet companies still in their start-up phase, without any self-sustaining ability, break this predicament? With the arrival of the giant monopoly era, what should future entrepreneurs do?Xing Shanhu, the entrepreneur who successfully created "I'm MT" under the siege of giants, advised his peers: entrepreneurship is like rolling dice; as long as you keep going and don't quit, there will be a day when you roll triples.In fact, over the past few years, although the giants have monopolized the majority of resources on the Internet, cases where they were beaten by start-up companies still frequently occurred. In the video field, Sohu helplessly watched as start-up companies Youku and Tudou rose and went public one after another. After the integration of Youku and Tudou in 2012, their market share became something Sohu Video could never reach.In 2011, Meilishuo was the star start-up company. This company, which had only been founded for two years, had completed three rounds of financing and single-handedly heated up social commerce. The success of Meilishuo made Tencent, which had been eyeing the e-commerce market for a long time, see a glimmer of hope. Tencent’s initial choice was not to invest in Meilishuo but to reorganize its fashion channel after thorough research, hoping to use Tencent's vast resources to compete with and replace Meilishuo. However, this project failed within just a few months. In the end, Tencent's investment department, after failing to imitate, had no choice but to invest in Meilishuo to seize the traffic entry point of e-commerce.Other examples include: Although Baidu dominated the traffic entry points on PC, it achieved nothing in mobile Internet, whereas UCweb, a start-up company, emerged as a major player and became an important traffic entry point in mobile Internet; the strong alliance between Tencent and Groupon showed no advantage in the group-buying market and was instead wiped out by start-up companies such as Meituan and Lashou; the mobile Internet department of Tencent, which was once highly anticipated, did not produce any disruptive products over several years, while the Guangzhou Research Institute, relatively isolated, obtained a ticket for Tencent to enter the mobile Internet.We analyzed these cases of how start-up companies survived in the gaps between giants and summarized four advantages of start-up companies when facing giants, hoping to inspire entrepreneurs in the era of giant monopoly.Advantage One of Start-up Companies: Efficiency FirstFor a giant with thousands or even tens of thousands of employees, managing such a large organization mainly relies on KPIs and institutionalization to ensure execution. On the other hand, institutionalized management also brings problems such as lack of innovation and low efficiency. Ding Xiuhong, who left NetEase to start his own business, frankly stated that Dahule (a brand he co-founded) prioritized efficiency in its first two years. He, who previously served as deputy editor-in-chief at NetEase, had a firsthand experience of the "big company disease".Commentary: For giants, every decision needs repeated deliberation. While the giants are holding meetings for discussion, start-up companies have already started working hard. The trial-and-error cost for start-up companies is lower, allowing them to try more directions more flexibly than giants.Advantage Two of Start-up Companies: You Have Nothing LeftAfter Tencent failed to imitate Meilishuo, it still had the option to acquire a stake in Meilishuo through capital operations. However, for Meilishuo, if it were successfully imitated by Tencent, it would lose everything. Similar situations occurred in the case of Sohu Video vs. Youku and Baidu vs. UC. Backed by the parent company, Sohu Video could rely on the group's continuous financial support to tolerate slow development. Even if Sohu Video failed, the worst outcome would be losing a core business. Baidu, even if it achieved nothing in mobile Internet, could still survive relying on its nearly monopolistic market position on PCs. For Youku or UCweb at the time, they could not afford to lose any territory because they had no retreat route.Commentary: For giants, new businesses are just for expansion needs and often lack the determination to burn all bridges. For entrepreneurs who will lose everything if they fail, they are more likely to go all out.Advantage Three of Start-up Companies: Rely on Intuition Rather Than Report DataWhen recalling his entrepreneurial career, CEO of YY Inc., Li Xueling, regarded the launch of YY Voice as the turning point of the company. NetEase once cut down the IM team due to the short-term need to burn a lot of money affecting financial report data, and the proposal to embed voice business into games was opposed by the gaming business. The laid-off employees of NetEase's IM department became a treasure for Li Xueling, who was eager to develop voice business at the time. Later developments proved that the joining of NetEase's laid-off employees built the basic technical architecture of YY Voice, and YY Inc. successfully listed on NASDAQ at the end of 2012 thanks to YY Voice as its core business.Commentary: From NetEase's perspective, giving up IM business seemed reasonable at the time. A business that couldn't see short-term prospects and needed to continue burning money would definitely affect NetEase's financial report data. Compared to its own business, voice was a non-core business within NetEase, while gaming was NetEase's lifeline, supporting the interests of core departments was also correct. Compared to the giants who constantly look at report data, the natural sharp intuition of entrepreneurs will become the trump card against giants.Advantage Four of Start-up Companies: No Resources, Only Go Outside to FightCEO of Fengxing Online, Luo Jiangchun, once said that as long as the same mistakes aren’t made, the company allows employees to try and make mistakes, only through constant trial-and-error will they know what is right. Another entrepreneur who worked in a multinational company for many years said that in big companies, it's about following the right people, while in start-up companies, it's about doing the right things. The difference lies in that in big companies, as long as you follow the right person, doing wrong things might still be considered right, and if you follow the wrong person, doing the right thing might still be considered wrong. In contrast, start-up companies always face life-or-death situations and have too few resources to indulge in internal power struggles.Commentary: Start-up companies have too few resources and need to fight outside for resources. They also don't need to deal with more complex people or matters. If the company dies, everyone goes hungry together. Giants have too many resources, leading to the prevalent concept of resources + investment = performance. However, the reverse side of the coin is that there are countless cases in the Internet industry where simply throwing resources leads to failure. Sensitivity to the market and the correct way of doing things are more important than blind resource input.
