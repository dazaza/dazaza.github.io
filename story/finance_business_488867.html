Once, in order to wait for you

by poixiaoyang on 2011-09-13 21:08:44

Once, I changed my name to "This Shore Flower" just to wait for you, allowing the silent passage of time to erode my appearance. Even though you never returned, I still counted my silver strands during storms, saying, "If you don't come, I dare not grow old."

But one day, I hastily abandoned my waiting and began to peacefully enjoy the summer breeze from the south in my little nest, savoring the fragrance of lilies, looking at delightful recipes, and walking quietly on shaded paths, as if returning from another world.

I stood by the window, watching the bustling crowd downstairs, listening to someone on the phone say: "Girl, I feel a heatwave, you must not go out." But on that day in Kunming, the temperature was far from reaching 30 degrees; or, in the dead of night, receiving text messages urging me to sleep; or again, in the early morning, receiving calls ordering me to eat breakfast.

All of this, you have never given me.

What you gave me was endless, despair-filled waiting.

I knew your number, I saw your lit-up QQ, but when I occasionally mustered the courage to disturb you once, you never responded to me at all. Having grown accustomed to your indifference, I could only remain silent thereafter.

Last night, you called me. That specially set ringtone for you was distant and strange. I hadn’t expected it to ring.

I didn’t feel the joy and excitement of before, because even if you call, there are only two possibilities: either you're drunk and want to find me, or you're simply asking. In the past, both situations would drive me mad— the former, I'd use any means necessary to go out and see you, then listen to your drunken ramblings, cry my eyes out, watch you leave, and then mourn helplessly, never knowing the time, place, or people of our next meeting. The latter, I'd go from excitement to deep despair, seizing every opportunity to cry, showing how much I cared, and then be lost and sleepless night after night.

Unsurprisingly, came your intoxicated voice, still uttering that phrase only said after drinking—"I miss you." This time, you added, "I'm homeless."

I lay on my spacious bed, with my computer resting on my knees, speaking heartfelt words to my close friend. Because of your "homeless" remark, I became restless. But in the end, what I said was, "I'm sorry, it's inconvenient to reveal my address."

You hung up the phone sadly, and I wasn't doing much better. Dear, for me to do this, how much courage and determination did it require? I know you've had too much to drink, I know you still won't remember what I said, I know my tears will ultimately not keep you, and I know that after you leave, I will still silently sob, silently endure, and wait with disappointment.

So I willingly let my hands and heart freeze over this night.

Tossing and turning all night, worrying about your shelter and my future.

In the past, I fantasized countless times about being with you, turning my longing into wounds intertwined with affection, waiting for the day you would accompany me to watch the gentle flow of water. But you, besides giving me waiting and despair, offered nothing else.

I hope to travel to Yangzhou with fireworks in March with the person I love most. I hope the person who cares about me the most can take me on business trips. All of this, you've never said, nor done. I hope there's someone who, whenever I think of them, can express their feelings freely, receive responses, warm my heart, and comfort the loneliness inside me with soft and warm hands. Not like smelling a sweet flower scent and then asking why the flower is so red?

That once profound and hopeless waiting ended in some small, warm gestures. I started closing my heart, waiting for someone exclusively mine, as real and loyal as I once waited for you. If the flowers on the other shore haven't bloomed, then let them quietly wither away.

In the past, you said you'd come at three o'clock, and I began to feel happy at two; I asked more than once where you were while I crossed the street; I said I'd wait for your care until I closed my heart; we were strangers at first, and eventually remained unacknowledged.

So, dear, this time, even if you don't come, I will grow old.

(Heartfelt Story)
