How to be a good real estate agent

by nbkfpnz8m11 on 2012-03-02 13:48:55

If you want to be a qualified real estate broker, you not only need to master professional knowledge of real estate, but also skillfully control legal knowledge, financial common sense and negotiation skills, in order to build bridges between real estate developers and consumers, buyers and sellers, and to ensure your invincibility in the intermediary industry.

Real Estate Professional Knowledge (including real estate information): Real estate brokerage refers to providing real estate information and intermediary agency services for clients. Simply put, it means acting as an intermediary. Intermediaries connect two parties: the seller and the buyer, and play a role in communication, promotion, and stability between them. For a real estate broker, mastering a large amount of market information is a prerequisite for doing this job well. This information should include: laws, regulations, and related policies regarding real estate, changes in purchasing objects, etc. To a large extent, brokers make profits by utilizing information, which is their most important capital. If intermediaries emphasize information, then agencies must have professional knowledge. Agency represents one party in finding another party, covering the entire process of transactions and leasing, and runs through all stages of real estate development. Therefore, brokers need to be proficient in real estate development and operation, architecture, real estate transactions, real estate price evaluation, and other professional knowledge based on familiarity with relevant real estate laws, regulations, and ordinances. Specifically, one needs to master real estate development investment, as well as related market research, development procedures, project feasibility studies, engineering construction and completion acceptance, financing, market promotion. Secondly, one should be familiar with basic construction knowledge and understand the entire process of construction projects. Thirdly, one needs to be familiar with conditions, procedures, and specific regulations for transfer, mortgage, and lease, as well as some basic knowledge about property rights. Fourthly, one should master the theory and practice of real estate price evaluation.

Marketing Knowledge: The current state of real estate development is no longer just about single product competition as it was a few years ago. For real estate developers, what they need is not just simple intermediary services, but intermediaries that can provide comprehensive marketing plans and sophisticated market planning solutions to open up markets. The separation characteristic of production and consumption in the real estate market determines the importance of real estate marketing. The separations in time, quantity, type, housing model, floor ownership, information, and valuation must rely on marketing to fill the gaps. Reasonable theories, market predictions and research methods, even product strategies, pricing strategies, distribution and promotion strategies are indispensable components of the core of real estate marketing. To master marketing knowledge, one must master market research, target market selection, product development, product pricing, channel selection, product promotion, after-sales service, and other knowledge and skills.

Legal Knowledge: Although real estate brokers are not lawyers and do not necessarily need to reach professional levels, they must be proficient in laws and regulations related to real estate. The development of China's real estate industry benefits from the adjustment and perfection of real estate laws and regulations, meaning that every aspect of real estate business is inseparable from law and specific regulations. The three major laws of real estate - "Urban Real Estate Management Law", "Land Management Law", and "Urban Planning Law" - along with relevant rules and local regulations almost cover all real estate matters. As brokers, not only must they be proficient in real estate-related laws, regulations, and government policies, but they must also be familiar with civil law, economic law, administrative law, administrative litigation law, and civil litigation law. A simple example is signing contracts, which involves economic contract law and general principles of civil law. For lease agency, one must be familiar with "Urban House Lease Management Measures" and "Urban Private House Management Conditions", understanding which houses can be rented, which cannot, and how lease contracts take effect. For sales agency, one must be familiar with the conditions, transaction procedures, property rights knowledge, and registration procedures specified in "Urban House Transfer Management Regulations" and "Urban Property Rights Registration Management Methods". If representing developers, one needs to know even more legal knowledge. The popular real estate lawyer witnessing business in recent years sufficiently illustrates the importance of legal knowledge. Real estate brokers must learn the law, understand the law, use the law, conduct affairs according to the law, and protect the legitimate rights and interests of their clients using the law.

Financial Knowledge: Real estate finance, as an important part of the financial industry, is closely linked to real estate development, operation, and management. It is the product of the marriage between real estate and finance. The development of the real estate industry requires the financial industry as its backing, while the financial industry's expansion primarily focuses on the real estate industry. Especially after the implementation of the new housing reform system and the cancellation of physical housing allocation, residents purchase housing in the market, making financial knowledge crucial for brokers. Whether it's full-process agency for developers, mortgage loans, or even real estate consulting services, financial knowledge is indispensable. Generally speaking, brokers should be familiar with international financial conventions, current national financial policies, financing forms, bank interest rates, cost calculations, loan types and procedures, repayment methods, and housing provident funds.

Negotiation Skills: Negotiation is a technique, more so an art and science. It involves communicating viewpoints and emotions among negotiating parties to reach an agreement that satisfies both sides fundamentally. Real estate brokers' negotiations are no exception, achieving the tasks assigned by the client, obtaining commissions while satisfying market demand. Common negotiation strategies like opportunity utilization, benefit compromise, and winning by sincerity are essential for real estate brokers to reference. Specifically, in intermediary or agency businesses, at least three or more schemes should be proposed—upper, middle, and lower strategies. For real estate brokers, there is also a very practical issue: how to make both parties transact. This requires brokers to "seize the moment," employing some tactics and techniques to prompt the other party to make decisions early. However, fraudulent means must not be used.

Being a real estate broker is a profession that appears glamorous on the surface but is actually hard work. Only those who personally engage in this career can truly understand its essence. Engaging in this industry is extremely arduous, involving long hours and almost 24/7 availability, followed by incessant phone calls to others. It is a job that best showcases personal ability, charm, and value. Since each transaction involves two completely different clients (homeowners and buyers), every deal becomes a process of "strategizing" against many people, making every day exciting. Almost every seasoned broker has a lot of grievances. They often complain, "It's not that we don't want to be transparent; it's just that everyone lacks trust." Those in the intermediary industry admire Hong Kong's model, where every transaction of each house is crystal clear, the responsibilities of lawyers are defined, and trust in brokers is relatively high. In general, society is quite fair. The lack of integrity in the industry leads to a lack of trust in practitioners, thus only continuous "transparency" can gradually build trust. Who doesn't want to perform well? Who doesn't want smooth monthly transactions and good commission earnings? But in reality, the sales process is filled with more failures, lost deals, and unrewarded efforts. Returning home each day exhausted, the next morning brings even greater performance pressure. The average transaction cycle for brokers is 20 days to 1 month, sometimes tracking customers for a year, requiring service throughout the entire period until the customer signs the property handover form. The workload and psychological pressure are immense, challenging both physical and mental health.

How to become a successful and outstanding real estate broker?

I believe the following conditions should be met:

1. Stress tolerance: Real estate brokers shoulder huge sales targets. Newcomers face difficulties in closing deals, watching others successfully land deals and receive rich rewards. Local brokers face life pressures, with managers urging them repeatedly, yet facing client indifference and rejection. Faced with difficulties, many feel lost and discouraged, either giving up or working passively, moving further away from success. Therefore, positioning oneself correctly, maintaining a positive attitude, facing pressure, and accepting challenges are abilities that every real estate broker, especially young people stepping into the workforce, should possess. Where in the world does something fall from the sky? Which industry’s success comes easily? If every deal went smoothly without any rejections, wouldn’t everyone be a real estate broker? One can often imagine excellent performance, past happy events, family and friends’ expectations. I am a real estate broker, and I provide services with my expertise. Those who reject me might lose a great opportunity. What they lose is actually more. One can also imagine worse situations or tragic experiences of acquaintances, realizing comparatively that one is still successful. Gradually establish confidence.

2. Analytical skills: Real estate brokers need to possess sensitive analytical capabilities for market opportunities, learning to discover market opportunities, such as under the impact of the Six National Policies, when market transactions begin to shrink. At this time, one can explore new markets, such as commercial real estate. In the competitive battle, analyze market needs and the success factors of top brokers. What should I learn from and analyze continuously? Find market gaps or new performance growth points to keep oneself unbeatable.

3. Communication skills: A successful real estate broker must be a good communicator. The work content of real estate agents mainly involves dealing with people. How to achieve good communication with clients, homeowners, and colleagues is the key to success. Promoting one's views, beliefs, plans, and methods to superiors, subordinates, and clients/homeowners is one of the most important abilities of a real estate broker. Good communication skills are the best way to win others' support. In practice, many sales problems arise due to poor communication. Communication mainly involves conveying one's ideas to others while listening to theirs. Everyone has the desire to be respected. You should value others, carefully listen to every word they say, clearly express your own thoughts, and understand others' ideas and inner feelings. Smile, be enthusiastic, sincere, and let others have the desire to confide in you.

Learning: Never be satisfied with already achieved results, continuously learn new knowledge, absorb nutrients, learn from top performers, adopt their good qualities, and apply them in actual work to ensure continuous success for real estate brokers. For real estate brokers, sales life is like a war, an uninterrupted, relentless pursuit. Mixed within successes are many failures, joy, anticipation, pride, happiness, fear, refusal, and disappointment. Despite facing numerous obstacles and pressures, excellent real estate brokers always possess excellent learning abilities, maximally grasping social and client developments, using knowledge as a foundation, fully preparing to move forward steadily, persevering, and ultimately reaching the pinnacle of success.

Many real estate brokers suffer from a lack of marketing knowledge and low marketing skills, with blank spots in areas such as planning and design, market research methods, marketing promotion techniques, contract formulation, laws and regulations, real estate professional knowledge, finance, and architecture. Operations are messy, and some even cannot perform these tasks, relying solely on gifts, banquets, reducing commissions, and covert operations to offer "kickbacks" for sales, showcasing a startling lack of professional knowledge. Some real estate brokers, although capable of calculating purchase, sales, gross profit, departmental variable cost prices, lack basic understanding of expense levels, capital turnover, sales costs, capital profit-tax ratio, investment value analysis, and even cannot read ordinary financial statements. Without effort to learn, how can such brokers follow market economy rules to make marketing decisions? And it would be impossible to adjust sales behavior through accounting.

The professional knowledge of brokers mainly manifests in four aspects: having a comprehensive understanding of the company. Understanding the processes and quality management of real estate buying and selling, the contents of after-sales service, and the direction of company development. Mastering real estate industries and common terms. Brokers should understand the local real estate development trends, accurately grasp local real estate dynamics, and the strengths and weaknesses of competitors and reliable selling points. Additionally, they must master real estate marketing knowledge, bank mortgage knowledge, property management knowledge, engineering construction knowledge, real estate legal knowledge, and some professional terms such as green space ratio, building density, usable area, etc. Grasping customers' purchasing psychology and characteristics. Understand the practical, innovative, aesthetic, famous, profitable psychological preferences, self-respect, imitation, secrecy, doubt, safety, etc., during the purchasing process. Understanding marketing-related content. Brokers should learn product strategies, pricing strategies, channel strategies, promotional combination strategies, etc., to understand real estate marketing knowledge. Psychological requirements include strong adaptability, being sincere and confident, optimistic and generous, having perseverance, able to withstand various hardships, having a strong sense of responsibility, and strong self-control.

Knowledge: The main task of a broker is sales. Without sales, there is no hope for performance, and no hope for the enterprise. At the same time, the work of sales representatives must expand the market. Only focusing on sales offers no hope because what you sell is houses and services, while continuously expanding the market with integrity can establish a long-term market position, gain a long-term market share, build important intangible assets for the company's sales channels, and secure stable performance for oneself. Excellent real estate brokers not only show clients around houses, talk about prices, sign contracts, and collect commissions but should master more real estate-related knowledge: finance, insurance, investment management, law, property management, decoration, etc., providing clients with comprehensive investment management knowledge and becoming trusted financial experts for clients.

Details: From the client's perspective, most clients cannot decide immediately. If you can provide more options at once, especially in bad weather, they will chat endlessly with you and thank you profusely. After all, you consider their next step. Sometimes, when I know the situation of the next property they plan to visit, I directly suggest whether they should go or not. Because some properties have defects or do not meet their requirements, going would waste their time. By telling them directly, you save their trouble, advancing your service invisibly. Out of a hundred clients looking at houses, how many could give a deal on the spot?

Yes, sometimes it happens, but in most cases, it is impossible. If clients do not know the broker beforehand, there is no trust relationship, and they will hesitate and think twice. But as a broker, if out of a hundred clients asking you to show them houses, none give you a deal, would you feel disheartened or neglect them? If so, you should abandon this industry as soon as possible. Someone said: "Your mindset is really good, never annoyed. If it were me, I would have ignored them long ago. Who has that patience!" Actually, if you think carefully, you will realize: taking a hundred clients as an example, if you take good care of them, at some point in the future, any one of them could become your loyal client. If you neglect them now because they haven't given you any deals, you will gain nothing. On the contrary, if you treat them differently, you might win not only these hundred clients but also their friends, relatives, friends of friends, and relatives of relatives...
