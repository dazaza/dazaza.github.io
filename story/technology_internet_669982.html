What are the causes of childhood tic disorder? Parents should know: what are the causes of childhood tic disorder? When children get tic disorders, it takes away the smiles from many parents' faces, and how to cure their children's diseases becomes their main concern. In addition, many parents have such questions: how is tic disorder formed? Why do children become like this without any apparent reason, and how can we prevent it? I think this is a problem that troubles many people. For this reason, we invited relevant experts from the No. 705 Hospital of the PLA to introduce here in detail how tic disorder is formed.

Regarding the question of how tic disorder is formed, the experts from the No. 705 Hospital of the PLA said that there are six main factors leading to tic disorder in children summarized clinically:

1. Genetic Factors: The incidence rate of tic disorder and Tourette syndrome in the families of patients with Tourette syndrome is 10%-66%.

2. Neurobiochemical Factors: Due to the dysfunction of central nervous system neurotransmitters.

3. Organic Factors: Research suggests that Tourette syndrome belongs to organic diseases. About 50%-60% of children with Tourette syndrome have non-specific abnormalities in electroencephalogram (EEG).

4. Socio-Psychological Factors: The onset of Tourette syndrome may be related to stress factors, such as being shocked by a car accident, being beaten or scolded, or experiencing high academic pressure.

5. Other Factors: Conjunctivitis causes eye discomfort leading to blinking, pharyngitis causes throat pain and itching leading to dry coughing. Long-term blinking and dry coughing can make daily prevention and treatment more targeted. After conjunctivitis and pharyngitis heal, habitual tics, blinking, and dry coughing may occur. Recent studies have found that Tourette syndrome is associated with hemolytic streptococcus infection. Clinically, it has also been found that infections and fever can exacerbate Tourette syndrome and obsessive-compulsive disorder. After using penicillin, tic symptoms reduce, at which point appropriate diet becomes extremely important.

6. Pharmacogenic Factors: Long-term use of central nervous system stimulants, such as methylphenidate and Ritalin used by children with ADHD, leads to approximately 25% developing heart disease, which can cause tic disorders or worsen tic symptoms.

Parents should now clearly understand how childhood tic disorder is formed. Experts from the No. 705 Hospital of the PLA remind patients and their parents that tic disorder seriously affects the physical and mental health development of children. Parents should seek timely treatment for their children. Moreover, the quality of the treatment institution chosen directly impacts the effectiveness. Therefore, parents should choose professional hospitals and not believe in quacks!
