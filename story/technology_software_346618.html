Can't hear the teacher clearly

On March 31, at Class One, Grade One of Weisan Road Primary School in Jinan, 102-year-old Ma Xiuxian was attentively listening to the lecture. Reporter Zuo Qing and intern Liu Tianlin took the photos. The elderly woman was fully equipped with school supplies, including a magnifying glass. Reporter Zuo Qing and intern Liu Tianlin took the photos.

On March 31, Ma Xiuxian, a 102-year-old from Jinan, finally realized her decades-long dream of attending school as she sat in a classroom at Weisan Road Primary School in Jinan. Although her first class lasted only 23 minutes, it marked the first time in her 102 years that she entered a classroom...

At 8:20 AM on March 31, in Class One, Grade One of Weisan Road Primary School, 36 students quietly sat in the classroom waiting for their 37th classmate - 102-year-old Ma Xiuxian. This was a Chinese language class, and teacher Xu Yan wrote "Welcome Grandma Ma" on the blackboard.

Following the agreed-upon time with Weisan Road Primary School, at 8:30 AM, Ma Xiuxian, with white hair, carrying her schoolbag, walked into the classroom supported by her youngest son, Yin Fengxin. The children welcomed their oldest classmate with整齐applause, and Ma Xiuxian was seated in the third row next to seven-year-old Zhang Ruizhe.

"Hello classmates! Hello teacher!" Ma Xiuxian greeted the students with this opening line, her voice clear and loud, earning another round of applause.

The class began, and Ma Xiuxian placed her hands flat on her knees, eyes wide open. With her hearing aid, she listened attentively and respectfully with a smile. On her desk were the new textbooks, exercise books, and sharpened pencils given by the school. Unlike other primary school students, her desk also featured a magnifying glass.

Due to her age, Ma Xiuxian seemed unable to hear clearly what the teacher was saying or what the classmates were reading. She wrote her name, "Ma Xiuxian," on the exercise book, with the character "Ma" written in traditional characters. Then, she laboriously wrote "Class One, Grade One."

With two minutes left before the end of the class, considering safety, Ma Xiuxian had to leave the classroom early. At the request of her classmates, she said a few words: "Thank you, teacher! Thank you, everyone! Study hard and become successors to the nation!" Her voice slightly trembled but remained clear.

After the class, the Peking Opera Club of Weisan Road Primary School sang a segment of "Farewell My Concubine" for her, and Ma Xiuxian responded with "The Sky in the Liberated Areas is Bright."

"In the old society, there was no such happiness! Although my hearing has declined a bit, my eyesight is still good, and my mind is clear. I don't forget things easily." Ma Xiuxian said that being able to fulfill her lifelong dream of attending school today made her very happy.

The elder most envied "educated people."

Why did a 102-year-old pick up her schoolbag to enter the classroom, and how did she come to be there?

□ By reporter Xu Jie

Sold dowry to support children's education

Born in 1908 in Hancang, eastern Jinan, Ma Xiuxian became a child worker in a textile factory at 13 and married at 18, thereafter managing household affairs. Throughout her life, she raised nine children, seven of whom attended university, while the other two graduated from high school. Now, her eldest daughter, 82, attended the prestigious Qi Lu University Medical College in the 1940s and became a doctor at Beijing Tiantan Hospital; her second son graduated from Shanghai University of Finance and Economics and worked in central government agencies; her third daughter, after graduating from university, long engaged in railway construction design work in Northwest China...

Yin Fengxin, 58, is the youngest son of Ma Xiuxian. He said that his mother always took pride in her children's achievements. Their father worked at Jinan Boiler Factory, and with only one income in the family, they were not wealthy. After the third sister graduated from high school, she planned to find a job, but did not receive her mother's permission. Under her mother's insistence, she went to university. Yin Fengxin said that his mother always admired "educated people." In the hardest times, she sold her wedding jewelry to fund her children's education. As long as her children wanted to study seriously, no matter how difficult life was, she would fully support them.

The community helped the elder realize her dream

Yin Fengxin told reporters that his mother worked hard all her life. After her sons and daughters established their own families, she helped raise grandchildren. Having only attended a literacy class for a few months, she could write a few characters. At home, she was absolutely "diligent and eager to learn," often "consulting" her children about the pronunciation and meaning of certain characters. Not long ago, during an interview with a media outlet for centenarians, the elder expressed her wish to attend school. The Jing'er Road Community Committee and Daguan Garden Street Office quickly learned of her wish.

Li Jun of the Daguan Garden Street Office said that Ma Xiuxian was a well-known person in the community. Upon learning of her wish, everyone was deeply moved. They, together with the Jing'er Road Community, contacted the nearest Weisan Road Primary School, which also strongly supported her attendance. On March 31, Ma Xiuxian became the oldest student in the school's 51-year history.

Scene close-up: The elder smiled as she entered and exited the classroom

"Afterwards, I will read textbooks, I really envy today's children..." On March 31, after returning home from Weisan Road Primary School, Ma Xiuxian happily recounted her class experience to visiting neighbors, unable to stop smiling. Yin Fengxin said that this was the sentence his mother repeated most after returning home from school.

Facing classmates nearly a century younger, Ma Xiuxian smiled as she entered the classroom, clearly excited. Leaving the classroom, she smiled and said, "I am very happy today! In the past, with so many children, we couldn't even eat enough. There was no such condition. Today's children have much better conditions."

During Ma Xiuxian's class, there were many journalists present, and cameras clicked incessantly. Several video cameras filmed between the gaps, making the scene quite noisy. Often, the elder could not see the blackboard or hear the teacher clearly, which also affected the children's listening. Therefore, some parents questioned whether allowing a centenarian into the classroom was just for show. Relevant personnel from the Daguan Garden Street Office stated, "The elder was indeed happy and content today. Our purpose has been achieved."

Chongqing: A sixty-year-old elder wants to attend middle school

It was reported that in the first-grade classroom of Qianqiubang Primary School in Fenggao Town, Rongchang County, Chongqing, 64-year-old Liu Baofu held his textbook and concentrated on recognizing characters.

Liu Baofu is a villager from Qianqiubang Village, Fenggao Town, Rongchang County. He cannot read, and wanting to recognize more characters has been his dream.
