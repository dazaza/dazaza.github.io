Five people were involved in the sale of three million catties of water-injected pork, with sales amounting to over 15 million yuan.

by uelu6c6b on 2012-03-02 09:05:05

In order to increase the meat yield, a long iron hook was used to hang the pig up. Amidst its cries of agony, a mixture of brown sugar, salt, alum, corn starch, soy sauce, and water was forcibly pumped into the pig's stomach through its mouth using a water pump and rubber hose. From early 2009 to May 19, 2011, over a period of more than two years, Ma Fajun and four others at a pig farm in Tongshan District, Xuzhou City, produced and sold 12,800 pigs injected with this mixture. Calculating by each pig weighing over 200 pounds, the prosecution's indictment determined that the suspects had produced and sold three million pounds of "water-injected meat," amounting to sales revenue exceeding 15 million yuan.

Upon inspection, it was found that the "water-injected meat" contained levels of volatile basic nitrogen and moisture exceeding national standards, as well as significant amounts of aluminum harmful to human health. After the case became clear, the procuratorate filed a public prosecution against the defendants for the crime of producing and selling substandard products. The Tongshan District People's Court of Xuzhou originally planned to hold a hearing on this case yesterday morning, but due to unforeseen circumstances, the trial has been postponed, with no specific date set yet.

The Squealing Noise Triggered the Report

In May 2011, at the pig farm run by Ma Fajun and others in Dapeng Town, Tongshan District, Xuzhou City, the pigs were squealing day and night, severely affecting the normal rest of nearby residents. The locals were extremely dissatisfied and, unable to bear it any longer, sought help by reporting the issue to the police. After the police arrived on the scene, they immediately caught Ma Fajun and the other five people in the act of injecting water into the pigs, thereby uncovering the black curtain of the "water-injected meat."

According to investigations, Ma Fajun and the other five individuals used homemade tools to hook the pigs' nostrils, forcing their mouths wide open, then used water pumps and rubber hoses to forcibly inject the mixture into the pigs' abdomens. After being injected with the mixture, the pigs suffered from severe gastric relaxation, losing their ability to contract, causing them to wail continuously throughout the day. Ma Fajun and the others admitted that when they initially started the pig farm, they did not possess the technical knowledge to prepare the mixture or inject it into the pigs. However, after hearing about how injecting the mixture could improve the appearance of pork and increase the meat yield, they hired an experienced technician named Old Fan from Nantong specifically to handle the water injection at the pig farm. Later, after Old Fan left, Ma Fajun and his team began experimenting with the water injection technique themselves. Once proficient, they began mass-producing problematic pork.

Massive Quantity of "Water-Injected Meat" Unveiled

This case drew high attention from relevant departments in Tongshan District. As the investigation deepened, the truth gradually came to light. According to the confessions provided by Ma Fajun and others during police interrogations, injecting the mixture into the pigs could increase the meat yield by 2%-3% (some sources claim an additional weight gain of 10 to 30 pounds), making the meat appear fresher and tenderer, with an appealing look and a taste similar to lamb upon consumption.

Test results showed that the problematic pork failed to meet multiple national standards and contained large amounts of aluminum harmful to human health. Calculating by each pig weighing over 200 pounds, the five defendants manufactured and sold three million pounds of problematic pork over a period of more than two years, allowing it to flow into the market and end up in the hands of consumers, posing significant harm to food safety and seriously impacting public health. Based on the prosecution's charges, between early 2009 and May 19, 2011, defendant Ma Fajun conspired with defendants Ma Fujian, Ma Fusun, and others, hiring defendants Xie Yufeng, Yang Hongmin, and others to inject water into the pigs at the farm before selling them after slaughter at the Qiaojiahu Slaughterhouse in Yunlong District.

The prosecution charged that the five defendants collectively produced and sold 12,800 pigs injected with the mixture, generating total sales exceeding 15 million yuan. Upon inspection, the "water-injected meat" failed to meet multiple national standards, leading the procuratorate to file a public prosecution against the five defendants for the crime of producing and selling substandard products.

It is worth noting that the Qiaojiahu Slaughterhouse, where the suspects slaughtered the water-injected pigs, is one of the three designated pig slaughter enterprises in Xuzhou City. These harmful "water-injected meats" entered various markets through this slaughterhouse, eventually ending up on the dining tables of ordinary citizens. The enormous quantity of problematic pork involved in this case, along with the exceptionally high amount of money involved, is rarely seen in recent years. The "water-injected meat" incident not only exposed the greed of some vendors but also pointed to the ineffective supervision by several regulatory agencies regarding how such problematic pork could pass through various stages and enter the market and onto dining tables.

Suspects Likely to Face Severe Sentencing

According to Article 140 of the Criminal Law, if the production and sale of substandard products result in sales exceeding 2 million yuan, the punishment is a fixed-term imprisonment of fifteen years or life imprisonment, with a fine ranging from 50% to twice the sales amount or confiscation of property.

The public prosecution agency believed that the five defendants constituted a joint crime, with Ma Fajun and Ma Fujian being the principal offenders. Defendants Ma Fusun, Xie Yufeng, and Yang Hongmin were considered secondary offenders and should be given lighter, mitigated, or exempted penalties.

The case was originally scheduled to be heard yesterday morning at the Tongshan District People's Court, but it was learned yesterday that the trial was postponed for unknown reasons, with no specific date set yet.

Regarding the sentencing of this case, lawyer Liu Maotong, head of Jiangsu Maotong Law Firm, analyzed that in the past, cases involving water-injected pork mostly resulted in administrative penalties rather than criminal judgments, and even if there were criminal judgments, they often resulted in suspended sentences. However, in this particular case, the duration of the offense spanned over two years, with an extraordinarily large quantity of production and sales and a substantial amount of money involved. If the court confirms the related criminal facts after the hearing, the verdict will likely be quite severe.

Harm Caused by "Water-Injected Meat"

After injecting large amounts of water into the gastrointestinal tract of pigs, the digestive system loses its contraction capability, slowing down intestinal peristalsis. Food inside the gastrointestinal tract decomposes, producing toxic substances such as ammonia, amines, cresol, and hydrogen sulfide. These toxic substances are reabsorbed and spread throughout the pig's muscles, eventually entering the human body when consumed. Excessive water injection causes respiratory difficulties in pigs, putting their bodies in a semi-asphyxiated and self-poisoned state. Bacteria from the gastrointestinal tract can enter the muscles via the bloodstream, which is detrimental to human health upon consumption.

How to Identify "Water-Injected Meat"

By Observation – After water injection, the surface of the pork appears shiny and watery, with loose muscle tissue and a paler color.

By Touch – Due to the dilution of bodily fluids, "water-injected meat" lacks stickiness. When cut with a knife, the elasticity is poor, and the cut surface shows obvious traces like swelling.

By Paper Test – Place cigarette paper on the lean meat. After a while, remove and ignite it; if there’s a flame, it indicates oil presence on the paper, meaning the meat hasn't been injected with water. Conversely, if there’s no flame, the meat has been injected with water. Attach sanitary paper to the freshly cut surface; non-injected meat generally shows no significant wetting or slight wetting, whereas water-injected meat exhibits significant wetting. Attach thin paper to the meat; normal meat has stickiness, making the paper hard to peel off, whereas "water-injected meat" lacks stickiness and peels off easily.
