@What on earth is it

by nwaeoy634 on 2012-02-10 10:56:31

In traditional Chinese culture, "Heaven" is a very important concept. Regarding "Heaven," in Chinese folklore, the common people usually refer to it with reverence and familiarity as "Old Heaven Lord." Scholars respectfully call it "Lord of Heaven." Throughout history, emperors frequently held rituals to worship Heaven. In the event of major natural disasters or human misfortunes, the emperor often believed it was due to his own moral failure and would sincerely reflect on his mistakes, praying devoutly to Heaven. Sometimes, to demonstrate sincerity and determination, ancient people had the custom of making vows to Heaven.

So, what exactly is Heaven? The Heaven in traditional culture is not merely the cosmic void opposite to Earth. In fact, everything outside of humanity in traditional culture is categorized under Heaven. Anything beyond human intelligence, artificiality, and human effort is referred to as natural, divine craftsmanship, or divinely formed. Modern people call it nature. Terms like "naturally unadorned," "ingeniously surpassing divine craftsmanship," and "holistically divinely formed" allude to this concept. What happens naturally is attributed to the divine. All creations and transformations within nature and the universe are considered natural, divine craftsmanship, or divinely formed—what Heaven makes so, the power of Heaven, what Heaven creates. Nature is the most appropriate, divine craftsmanship the most ingenious, and divine creation so seamless that it goes unnoticed.

However, the power of Heaven is boundless, its creative wonders immeasurable, beyond any human capability, and no human force can resist it. Therefore, Heaven is worthy of respect and worship. Nature does not exist independently. What factors lie hidden behind nature and the natural? Who manifests these transformations invisibly?

Taoism speaks of "a deity three feet above one's head." Buddhism mentions celestial beings and heavenly realms. Siddhartha Gautama spoke of Buddhas in the heavens as numerous as the sands of the Ganges River. Gods and Buddhas can create humans and objects and shape the world. Chinese Taoism has the legend of the goddess Nuwa creating humans from clay. There is also the myth of the god Pangu opening up the heavens and earth to create the world. All gods and Buddhas possess abilities, wisdom, and realms far surpassing those of humans, naturally capable of doing what humans cannot, dominating and altering the living environment of humans, determining the fate of individuals and human society. The will and intention of gods and Buddhas are known as "Heaven's Will." Each god or Buddha is a "Heavenly Lord." Each god or Buddha has their own "Heavenly Realm." Each god or Buddha is the "Lord of Heaven" for the beings they have created. Each person's fate is arranged by the deities governing destiny, which is called "Heaven's Mandate." Heaven's Mandate cannot be defied; Heaven's Mandate cannot be altered. "The ways of Heaven are constant; they do not exist for Yao nor perish for Jie." The actions of gods and Buddhas also follow universal laws and principles, which are called "Heaven's Dao." "Heaven does not change, nor does the Dao." Heaven's Dao cannot be resisted; "To follow Heaven leads to prosperity, to defy Heaven leads to ruin." Gods and Buddhas control everything in the human realm, hence the saying "The net of Heaven is vast but lets nothing slip through." "Plans are made by man, success is determined by Heaven." People should act according to Heaven's Will, follow Heaven's Dao, and align with Heavenly Timing. "Heaven's Timing, Earth's Advantage, Human Harmony"—only then can things be accomplished. Therefore, people should "do their best and leave the rest to Heaven's Mandate." How could human intelligence rival Heaven's plans? "Human calculations are inferior to Heaven's calculations." "People have small schemes, but Heaven has grand plans." "People may calculate a thousand times, but Heaven needs only one calculation." Yet, "Heaven's intentions are high and hard to question," and Heaven's secrets should not be lightly divulged. "When people act, Heaven watches." "Whispered words among humans sound like thunder to Heaven; even in dark rooms where hearts are corrupted, the eyes of the divine shine like lightning." "A single thought in the human heart is known throughout heaven and earth." Can anything about humans remain hidden from Heaven? "Good deeds bring good rewards, evil deeds bring evil rewards. It's not that there is no retribution, just that the time has not come. When the time comes, all will be repaid." "Kind people may be deceived by others, but never by Heaven." "If people wish to harm others, Heaven won't allow it; if Heaven wishes to harm someone, how difficult is it?" Everything is balanced by Heavenly Reason. Those who violate Heavenly Reason will inevitably face punishment, known as "Heaven's Condemnation." How could the feeble efforts of humans possibly contend with Heaven? The idea of battling Heaven and Earth is merely delusional rambling, inviting ridicule!

Heaven is something humans need to revere and submit to. If humans, overestimating their strength and intelligence, insist on resisting and struggling against it, tragic fates will surely befall them. Thousands of years of human history have proven this point, and future human history will continue to prove it.
