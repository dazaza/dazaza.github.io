President Zhujing Shi of South University of Science and Technology: All of the university's first batch of over 40 students have been snapped up

by king3h0a on 2012-03-06 15:47:24

Zhu Qingxi, from Chengdu in Sichuan Province, member of the National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference (CPPCC), president of North University of Science and Technology, former president of the University of Science and Technology of China, academician of the Chinese Academy of Sciences, and chemist.

Zhu Qingxi, CPPCC National Committee member and president of North University of Science and Technology, is a popular figure at the two sessions. He was the former president of the University of Science and Technology of China, and has gained much attention in recent years due to his role as president of North University of Science and Technology and his practice of higher education reform.

Last Sunday morning, Huaxi Metropolis Daily interviewed this fellow Sichuan native at the Great Hall of the People.

Regarding school enrollment: It is still uncertain whether formal enrollment can take place

Huaxi Metropolis Daily: Will South University of Science and Technology be able to enroll students formally this year?

Zhu Qingxi: We are preparing for enrollment, but everything will have to wait until the Ministry of Education officially approves it before we proceed.

Huaxi Metropolis Daily: If the Ministry of Education does not approve this year, will South University of Science and Technology still enroll students?

Zhu Qingxi: I believe that this issue will be resolved soon, and I hope that North University of Science and Technology will enroll students this year. Both the Ministry of Education and leaders at all levels want South University of Science and Technology, as an experimental field for educational reform, to move forward quickly because it is a focal point of societal attention. I believe they will respond soon.

Regarding student employment: The first batch of over 40 students were fully recruited

Huaxi Metropolis Daily: Do you have confidence in the employment prospects of South University of Science and Technology students?

Zhu Qingxi: Absolutely confident. Our current students, the first group of over forty boys and girls, have already been snapped up by many large companies.

Huaxi Metropolis Daily: They haven't graduated yet.

Zhu Qingxi: They said that once these students graduate and your school recommends them, we'll take them all.

Huaxi Metropolis Daily: These are the first few batches, can you guarantee that it will always be like this?

Zhu Qingxi: That depends on our level of education. If we truly let the students learn real skills, then it will be like this.

Regarding tenure: After finishing this term, I won't continue

Huaxi Metropolis Daily: Shenzhen has selected a vice president with administrative position for South University of Science and Technology. How do you and the vice president selected by Shenzhen specifically divide responsibilities?

Zhu Qingxi: Shenzhen selected a vice president for logistics, and we also have a vice president for teaching.

Huaxi Metropolis Daily: After this term ends, will you run for re-election as the next term president?

Zhu Qingxi: I've never competed for election.

Huaxi Metropolis Daily: If the board wants you to stay on?

Zhu Qingxi: I'm too old, lack energy, and my health isn't good. I hope the board can find someone younger and more energetic to take over as president.

Huaxi Metropolis Daily: If recommending a president, do you have anyone in mind?

Zhu Qingxi: I have candidates, but these top candidates are still in the United States. Whether they can come back and are willing to come back is still uncertain.
