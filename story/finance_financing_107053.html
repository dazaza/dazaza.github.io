The Logic Shift of Throwing 2.4 Billion vs. Grabbing 2 Billion

by rodari on 2008-11-28 18:20:01

Not long ago, Premier Wen Jiabao of the State Council presided over a standing meeting of the State Council to study and deploy measures to further expand domestic demand and promote steady and relatively fast economic growth, changing the macro-economic control target from "one guarantee and one control" to "priority for guarantees". The meeting changed "guarantee" to "promotion" and explicitly put forward 10 specific measures. It is planned to invest a total of 4 trillion yuan by 2010 to further expand domestic demand and promote economic growth.

After this news came out, all sorts of funds acted accordingly. The Shanghai Composite Index kept rising. Starting from November 10, in the five trading days of the week, the Shanghai Composite Index rose by 13.66%. From individual stocks, there were 725 with more than 20% increase that week, 125 with more than 30%, 25 with more than 40%, and 5 with more than 50%.

Funds first wildly dumped then wildly absorbed

Since they still lacked firm enthusiasm for bullish market prospects, many funds regarded the major benefit of the 4 trillion yuan economic stimulus plan as a good opportunity to dump their holdings.

In the three trading days from November 10 to 12, funds chose to sell heavily. According to topview data, on November 10, funds bought 4.052 billion yuan and sold 6 billion yuan, net selling 1.948 billion yuan; on November 11, funds bought 4.591 billion yuan and sold 4.773 billion yuan, net selling 182 million yuan; on November 12, funds bought 2.81 billion yuan and sold 3.132 billion yuan, net selling 322 million yuan. In the three trading days, funds dumped 2.451 billion yuan.

However, in the two trading days of November 13 and 14, funds turned from heavy selling in the previous few trading days to heavy buying. On November 13, funds bought 6.976 billion yuan and sold 6.03 billion yuan, net buying 945 million yuan; on November 14, funds continued to strengthen their buying power. On November 14, funds bought 7.647 billion yuan and sold 6.539 billion yuan, net buying 1.108 billion yuan. In the two trading days, funds absorbed 2.053 billion yuan.

Why did the operation style of funds change so much in just a few days?

"The current economic situation is indeed very bad, and it is still hard to say how much effect the 4 trillion yuan economic stimulus plan will have in reversing the economic situation. Many details of this plan have not been determined yet. For the market's recent judgment, funds should only be positioned for rebound rather than reversal, which led to the massive dumping at the beginning of this wave of market activity."

Songbai Bao, researcher of Century Securities, said to Financial Weekly reporter, "As for the sharp turn to heavy buying in the following few days, it was probably because funds thought that the point position at that time had not reached the height of this rebound, and there was still some room for rise."

Jiewen Liu, head of the Research Department of China Europe Fund, also confirmed the above person's view. He said to Financial Weekly reporter: "We are relatively optimistic about the role of this 4 trillion yuan economic stimulus plan in ensuring economic growth. The economic situation in the next two years will be much better than previously expected by the market. However, regarding the stock market rise caused by this, we still think it is just a rebound rather than a reversal." When the reporter asked how high this rebound would be, Wenhua Liu said it was difficult to judge, but it might exceed market expectations.

Limited increase in positions

The position calculation results of the De Sheng Fund Research Center for the week of November 10-14 showed that increasing positions became the mainstream for funds that week, but the intention to increase positions of front-line fund companies was not too obvious.
