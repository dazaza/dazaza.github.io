Nanning Sugar Industry: Rising sugar prices boost company performance_ Stock Forum

by fj0953210 on 2009-12-05 21:56:31

Nanning Sugar Industry (000911) (enter the stock bar, new version of market conditions) surged strongly in the early session today. However, its gains narrowed under the drag of a falling broader market. By the close, it had risen 6.49%, with a full-day turnover rate of 7.12%. The company is the largest state-owned listed enterprise in China's sugar manufacturing industry. The sugarcane industry to which the company belongs is a sector supported by national policies and also a pillar industry for Guangxi and Nanning. For a long time, it has enjoyed multiple preferential policies from the state. The comprehensive earnings per share forecasts for the years 2009-2011 are 0.38 yuan, 0.76 yuan, and 0.64 yuan respectively, corresponding to dynamic price-to-earnings ratios of 63, 32, and 37 times respectively. Currently, there are 24 analysts tracking the company, with 2 giving a "strong buy" rating, 18 giving a "buy" rating, and 4 giving a "hold" rating. The overall rating coefficient is 2.08.

The supply of sugar in the 2009-2010 season will still be relatively tight, and domestic sugar prices will remain in an upward cycle. It is expected that sugar production for the next two years will be around 12.5-13 million tons, roughly the same as the previous crushing season. In the third quarter of 2009, the company achieved operating revenue of 695 million yuan, up 25.57% year-on-year; operating profit of 43 million yuan, up 6852.34% year-on-year; net profit attributable to the parent company owner of 37 million yuan, achieving earnings per share of 0.13 yuan. The gross margin for the first three quarters was 18.81%, up 0.16 percentage points year-on-year, and up 2.1 percentage points compared to the first half of the year. The company's profitability continues to recover.

According to quarterly reports disclosed, currently, old sugar inventory for the 08/09 crushing season nationwide is only over 3 million tons, not much pressure compared to normal years. If the start time of the 09/10 crushing season is delayed, there may be a slightly tight supply-demand situation, and sugar prices in the fourth quarter will continue to slowly rise. The planting area in Guangxi region for the 09/10 crushing season remains stable, while other regions across the country continue to reduce output. Therefore, the variable for new season output lies in Guangxi's yield per unit area. Based on current growth trends, limited by planting area, it is almost impossible for national sugar production in the new crushing season to increase significantly year-on-year. The supply-demand gap for the 08/09 crushing season exceeds 1 million tons, and the gap for the new crushing season will still be close to this number.

For the 09/10 crushing season, the company's sugarcane planting area basically remains unchanged at 1.08 million mu. Therefore, the production of refined sugar in the new crushing season mainly depends on the yield per unit area of sugarcane and the sugar content. According to reports from various professional websites, despite the persistent drought in southern provinces since July this year, the impact on the Nanning area is relatively small, and the growth of sugarcane is slightly better than last year, with the sugar content likely higher than last year. Shanxi Securities estimates that the company’s sugar production for the 09/10 crushing season could reach 600,000 tons, and sugar sales volume for 2009-2011 could remain at the level of 600,000 tons.

Huatai Securities believes that sugar can be called the "colored" variety in agricultural products, but demand is more rigid than colored metals. Since a reduction in production for the next crushing season in China and globally has become inevitable, both supply and demand data, as well as inventory data, are relatively transparent. Growth in the sugar manufacturing industry is certain. Given that the planting area in Guangxi may be lower than market expectations, the supply-demand relationship next year will be even more favorable, maintaining a "recommend" rating.

Risk factors: (1) Risk of a sharp fall in international market sugar prices; (2) Risk of reduced production due to natural disasters.
