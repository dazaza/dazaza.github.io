Gathering Momentum to Challenge Previous Highs: Two Sectors Gain Favor — Stock Market Basics

by xl0958870 on 2009-12-05 02:41:31

[Hotspot Focus and Market Opportunities] After three consecutive trading days of rebound, the Shenzen and Shanghai stock markets adjusted in a volatile manner on Thursday due to the divergence in individual stock performance. The Shenzen and Shanghai indices fell slightly by 0.29% and 0.16%, respectively, closing with negative cross stars accompanied by long lower shadows. Although the indices experienced a pullback, the overall market operation remained relatively stable, and the filling of the previous day's gap helped to resolve the differences between bullish and bearish forces. After a short-term consolidation, it is not impossible for the market to accumulate strength again and challenge the previous high resistance.

From the perspective of Thursday's market hotspots, two major highlights are worth noting. First, the ChiNext (Growth Enterprise Market) continued its collective strength after recent continuous activity. In fact, the recent strength of the ChiNext has played a significant role in maintaining market sentiment. The reason why the ChiNext is favored by the market lies first in its alignment with the current market enthusiasm for small and medium-sized capitalization stocks. Among the currently listed 28 ChiNext companies, the circulating market value is mostly below 1 billion yuan, with only Jinya Technology (300028, 1.028 billion), Huayi Brothers (300027, 2.332 billion), Jifeng Agricultural Machinery (300022, 1.611 billion), Lepu Medical (300003, 2.053 billion), Hongri Pharmaceutical (300026, 1.121 billion), Robot (300024, 1.040 billion), Aier Ophthalmology (300015, 1.597 billion), Teradyne (300001, 1.348 billion), and Shenzhou Taiyue (300002, 3.433 billion) exceeding 1 billion yuan.

Secondly, ChiNext companies generally possess high growth characteristics. According to past experience, there is a common practice at the end of the year to mine annual report topics. Varieties with high dividend and high bonus issues often receive significant attention from main force funds, and most ChiNext companies have such expectations.

Thirdly, ChiNext companies have a certain degree of scarcity. As a new phenomenon domestically, the ChiNext lacks good valuation references. In the current market environment that is enthusiastic about small-cap stock speculation, it will inevitably become a focus for main force speculation, especially becoming a key target for speculative funds. According to statistics, the average dynamic P/E ratio of the 28 ChiNext companies is 92.57 times. Despite the initial market concern over the high issue price of ChiNext companies, which already reflects a high premium on their growth characteristics, the performance of these companies in the first three quarters of this year has shown significant increases. Therefore, the current high P/E ratio should be viewed dialectically. Additionally, 23 companies are scheduled for the second batch of ChiNext listings. Among them, Harbin Jiuzhou Electric, Hubei Huitian Adhesive, Beijing Meten Communication Technology, Shenzhen New宙邦Technology, Guangzhou Yangpu Medical Technology, Beijing SuperMap Software, Hunan Zhongke Electric, Jiangsu Huarui Tianlong Photoelectric, Beijing Steel Research High-Na Technology, Jilong Electronics, Zhejiang Hexin Tonghuashun, Wuxi Baotong Belt Industry, Julis Sling Tools, etc., have passed the initial public offering review. The market expects that the second batch of ChiNext companies will start listing this month, which has drawn market attention to the ChiNext.

However, it is noteworthy that after the sustained rise of the ChiNext in the recent stage, the short-term cumulative increase is relatively large. Moreover, the high growth of the ChiNext also hides high risks. Investors need to avoid blindly chasing highs and being overly heavily positioned during the operation process.

In addition to the ChiNext, the agricultural sector also became a highlight in the Thursday market, with Xinong Development, Dunhuang Seed, Denghai Seed, and Fengle Seed strongly closing at the limit up. I believe that the strength of the agricultural sector mainly lies in the following three factors: First, the agricultural sector has been a significantly lagging sector in this year's market, and there is a demand for catch-up gains in the future. Second, under the gradually rising inflation background, there will be further room for the rise in agricultural product prices. Third, the impact of disasters in the north has led to reduced seed production and inventory decline, improving the supply-demand relationship while some quality varieties' prices begin to rise. Therefore, in terms of operations, investors are advised to focus on two types of individual stocks in the agricultural sector: one is sub-sector listed companies with price elasticity and sensitivity, such as Nanning Sugar Industry and Gui Sugar Industry in the sugar-making industry, Xinong Development and Xin Sai Co., Ltd. in cotton planting; the other is seed companies indirectly benefiting from the rise in agricultural product prices, such as Denghai Seed, Dunhuang Seed, and Longping Hi-Tech.

It is necessary to remind that regarding market trends, during the process of the market accumulating strength to break through resistance, investors should closely monitor the direction of market hotspots, such as whether the low-carbon sector can successfully transition and orderly rotate, and the activity of blue-chip sectors.

[Risk Warning and Strategy]

According to foreign media reports, Deputy Governor of the People's Bank of China Hu Xiaolian said on Wednesday that the current gold price is at a historical high, and attention must be paid to the price bubble of gold assets. Currently, the U.S. gold futures price has touched a historical high. Market analysts pointed out that apart from the weak dollar, reasons for the sharp rise in gold prices include rumors that China will increase its gold reserves again and substantial ETF buying interest. Data shows that due to China's continuous trade surplus and the government's foreign exchange control system, the Chinese government has accumulated huge foreign exchange reserves. By the end of September 2009, China's foreign exchange reserve balance had reached $2.27 trillion, of which approximately 70%, totaling $1.6 trillion, were dollar reserves.

[Today's Information]

On Thursday, the following information is worthy of investor attention:

1. Vice Chairman of the China Securities Regulatory Commission Yao Gang stated on December 2 that with the steady recovery of the economy, the current domestic stock market has gradually rebounded, and investor confidence has increased. Institutional professional investors currently hold about RMB 2.25 trillion worth of A-shares, accounting for 17.2% of the total circulating market value, indicating that institutional investors are facing good opportunities for further development. The new technological revolution is expected to bring greater development space for the capital market, and the capital market will play a more active role in "structural adjustment."

2. The report released by the National Information Center on December 3 believed that next year, China's GDP growth rate is expected to reach around 8.5%, roughly equal to that of 2009, achieving "dual stability" in economic growth and prices. The possibility of obvious inflation in the short term is not large, but it is inappropriate to further intensify expansionary policies in the future. The report suggested that if the global economy continues to recover slowly, the macroeconomic policy orientation of China next year should remain basically unchanged, continuing to implement an active fiscal policy and moderately loose monetary policy. The proportion of fiscal deficit to GDP should be maintained at around 3%, the annual growth rate of broad money supply (M2) balance should be gradually controlled within 18%, the scale of new loans should be controlled within RMB 8 trillion, and the scale of enterprise financing from the securities market should be larger than in 2009, keeping the society's overall funding relatively loose.

3. Announcement on Wednesday showed that in order to maintain steady growth of base money and basic stability of money market interest rates, the People's Bank of China will issue the 63rd Central Bank Bill of 2009 on December 3, 2009 (Thursday). This central bank bill has a term of 3 months and a scale of RMB 70 billion. The volume of funds expiring in the open market this week is RMB 135 billion. On Tuesday, RMB 12 billion worth of 1-year bills and RMB 45 billion worth of 28-day positive repurchase agreements were already issued, and it is expected that the open market will still welcome continuous net withdrawal of funds for the eighth week.

The procedure for buying and selling stocks is as follows: When buying or selling stocks, you must specify whether you are buying or selling, the name (or code) of the stock, the quantity, and the price. And this entrustment is only valid for the day it is placed. The content of the entrustment includes the abbreviation (code) of the stock you want to buy or sell, the quantity, and the price of the stock. The abbreviation of the stock is usually three to four Chinese characters, and the code of the stock is six digits. The code and abbreviation of the stock must be consistent when entrusting to buy or sell. At the same time, there are certain regulations regarding the quantity of stocks bought or sold: the quantity of stocks entrusted to buy must be a whole multiple of 100, but the quantity of stocks entrusted to sell does not have to be a whole multiple of 100. There are four ways to entrust: counter submission, telephone automatic entrustment, computer automatic entrustment, and remote terminal entrustment.
