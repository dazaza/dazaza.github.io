by wendrwlove on 2011-09-12 13:12:56

By An Xier

I have now become aware of the habit of reminiscing about your smile in the darkness of night. The warmth and indelible marks it left on my heart have become a part of me. I've grown accustomed to the deep quietness of the night, far from the hustle and bustle of daytime. In the silent night, only the crescent moon hangs high in the sky. A streak of melancholy moonlight gently falls by my window, seeping through the curtain gaps and illuminating my sleepless night. Quietly walking to the windowsill, I pull back the curtains. The faint light of the crescent moon scatters over my slightly long hair. In the distance, there are still a few lamps glowing with an orange hue, lonely and evoking endless thoughts of you.

Do you know that there is someone like me who sings sorrowful songs for you out of longing? Supporting my chin with my hand, I lean entirely on the windowsill, watching fireflies fly past in front of me, dyeing the entire blackness with melancholic thoughts. I don't know how many nights I've spent quietly thinking about your kindness. In the dim night, it seems as if your face is imprinted with white memories of time. Year after year, flowers may look similar, but people change. Beauty ultimately cannot withstand the flow of time, yet your laughter continues to echo in my ears.

I always recall our past, those moments like fireworks blooming, simple love like ballads, which are now emaciated in the mirror of life and buried in the dust of yesteryears. And now, the dust has settled, leaving behind the fading youth and a bright sadness. My eyes no longer carry the hesitation they once did, framed under glasses that make me appear too haggard. Lingering over your visage, I remain entranced in the incomplete dream you gave me. Please don't take my sorrow away with you.

When loneliness becomes a form of liberation, when morning light filters through the windowsill, can you see the crystal tear on the flower's face on the windowsill? That is my yearning, traveling thousands of miles to leave a teardrop by your side. Watching myself walk in the misty forest, my fragile shoulders unable to bear the promises I made to you. When you decided to escape my world, my powerless attempts at retention turned into mere desires. My lost youth carries a trace of sorrow caused by you, heavy sighs, stifled sobs, resentment towards the encounters of flowers and butterflies in love songs. A lone guitar broke its last string for you, your pale outline scripted into the sad tunes I write.

Time swiftly flows through the depths of my weathered soul. The naive years of youth, silence, and beautiful adolescence have faded. The illusions of the world shattered, leaving lifelong entanglements. Observing the solitary footprints lingering in the rings of time, firmly imprinted in the depths of my heart, on the tender wings, I am like a kite soaring in the sky, controlled by the string in your hand, manipulated in my direction. One day, when you grow tired, you cut the string and discard me carelessly.

In my helpless gaze, those threads of compassion still cannot stop your determined steps to leave. I watch your silhouette disappear from my sight in mid-air. I lack the strength to break free from the shackles of fate, falling heavily from the sky, disintegrating. Ultimately, I rest amidst the swirling dust, finally comprehending a love as tragic as falling flowers, cruelly destroyed by the harsh realities of life. When loneliness strikes, I silently count the wounds you planted in me in the corners of time.

Your love has gone, my heart is broken, and you have thoroughly hurt me. In the dark damp space, I ache to the bone. Fragmented sentences express my endless longing for you. The flowers of solitude have dyed the whole of heaven and earth red. Those tender memories crushed under my fingertips slowly shield me from the evening breeze. Escaping from the city walls you built, you are like the gravity of the earth pulling me deeply into your trap, unknowingly falling in love with you. But now, your merciless departure has left me immersed in the haze of night.

My melancholy stems from being drunk in your world, unwilling to let go of your hand. The sorrow remaining in that season bursts forth in the most brilliant moment. The final ending left in the play turns out to be just a dream. Today, the railing is wet with my tears; tonight, sleep eludes me, covered in frost. In the blink of an eye, the gray space hides beneath your unchanged plain face. My damaged wings have been severed by your sword. My tears have drowned your smiles. The bright sadness you gave me will be the only cherished memory of my life. My youth is a book of perfect scars that you can never finish reading.

With my last tears and fallen spirits, I want to use all the happiness of my life to bless you with the utmost joy. But love is like windblown sand, gathering in my eyes to form a tear I cannot let fall. Your fragrance is countless sorrows for me. I can't rid myself of your tenderness. Even if my tears turn into rain, I wouldn't dare to drench you, because my love is as deep as a butterfly's infatuation with flowers.

The pain in my life transforms into a large black butterfly, gracefully fluttering alone in the deepest part of your season. The fallen wings beautifully drift in your dreams, painting them with their final shades of black. You have always been a blood-red rose in my heart, forever blossoming with the most stunning colors, adorning my cold space with dazzling flowers. Now, these memories have turned into some displaced pure white recollections, hastily deviating from my path, drifting away from my mind. You are no longer the lighthouse on my route or the coordinates of my dreams.

Time, bit by bit, quietly slips away; thoughts, wave after wave, rise and fall. Shaking off the dust covering me, sunlight particles splash around, revealing another universe within the house, gradually fading away. I continue to hold a lost heart, silently gazing at time. I always feel that time is the cruelest sword, severing you from my side, creating an indescribable barrier. The love that disappeared in the core of time conceals a rotating black hole, draining my soul and exiling my thoughts completely. Even my last determination fades with the loneliness of the night.

Flickering candle flames sparsely shine in the night sky. I am weaker than I imagined. The nights aren't long enough. The wind chimes sway, listening to a kind of sorrow, releasing long-stored heartaches back to faith. Through the glass storefront, the dawn's glow disperses March's parting song, blending April's drizzling cold rain into my heart. Why have you abandoned me in this chilling season? All I can do is silently weep for you in my heart. Although you couldn't stay with me till the end, and although I'm no longer your refuge, I still thank you for giving me sunlit companionship during that period.

Gentle winds and falling petals settle down. Time leaves light footprints, rolling up the beauty of yesterday and gracefully departing. Following the scent of memory, I mistakenly enter the cracks of dreams, seeing traces of the past. Memory's power feebly lifts my heavy head, revealing unseen sorrows from the past. Recollections swirl in the music box, flowing backward with countless injuries. Since your quiet departure, my heart lake ripples like a stone thrown into it, losing the way home.

The moon walks, but the heart stays. The moon casts its shadow on the lily window, flowers wither - for whom do they mourn? You once appeared in my landscape with an oiled paper umbrella, becoming my affection. Now, riding away on horseback, you fade into the twilight of my street. Youth is a hurried book filled with your smiles and sorrows. Holding a handful of dust, I bury myself.
