Top 10 Tycoon Phones Review: Gold and Diamond Apple for Sale at 100 Million Yuan

by anonymous on 2013-09-18 21:52:24

Apple's iPhone has traditionally been available in black and white versions every year. However, this tradition was officially broken during the recent autumn product launch event, as a champagne-colored version of the iPhone 5s was introduced alongside the standard models. This flashy body color has been humorously referred to by many netizens as the "Tuhao Gold" version.

In fact, we have already seen various gold versions of previous generations of iPhones. Unlike the iPhone 5s, these special editions were not just about changing the phone's color; instead, they were specially customized for top billionaires from different countries, which is the biggest difference between them and the regular versions of the iPhone.

Of course, the tastes of these wealthy individuals vary. Tech-savvy billionaire geeks may choose to customize products like the iPhone, while others who are less interested in phone functions might prefer VERTU, a brand known only for its high price and luxurious materials with little else to offer. Perhaps only such phones can demonstrate what they consider to be true taste.

For ordinary consumers like us, it’s unlikely we will ever use such phones, but at least we can still take a look. Therefore, the editor has specifically compiled a list of the world's top ten most extravagant luxury phones so far. Just looking at their prices makes one shiver. Without further ado, let's take a look together.

Price: HKD 90,000 (approximately RMB 70,000)

In the luxury mobile phone sector, Vertu is undoubtedly a well-known name, with many wealthy individuals and celebrities being loyal fans. Recently, two new limited-edition colors, Sunset Red and Midnight Blue, were added to the Vertu Ti lineup. Together, there are only 1,000 units of these limited-edition Vertu Ti phones, each priced at HKD 90,000 (approximately RMB 70,000).

The limited-edition Vertu Ti still features a straight touch-screen design, with a rugged and individualistic appearance that continues the consistent design style of Vertu phones. However, this limited edition is available in only two colors: Sunset Red and Midnight Blue. In terms of materials, the phone's body is made from titanium, making it lighter and more durable compared to other phones. To ensure user comfort, the back of the phone is lined with calf leather.

The material of the Vertu Ti body is Grade 5 titanium, which is stronger than ordinary smartphones but weighs less. It features a 4.7-inch display screen, with sapphire crystal glass on the front. Inside, it is equipped with a 1.7GHz processor and 64GB of storage space.

Limited Edition Vertu Ti

Reference Price: HKD 90,000 (approximately RMB 70,000)

Price: USD 16,500 (approximately RMB 100,000)

VERTU is the first global luxury mobile phone company established by Nokia, designed in a high-end manner by world-renowned mobile phone designer Frank Nuovo. In 2012, Nokia sold 90% of the VERTU brand equity to the Swedish investment company EQT. The models of this brand are absolutely royal in terms of appearance, materials, and functions, with an average selling price around RMB 100,000 per unit. The Vertu Constellation T is priced at USD 16,500 (approximately RMB 100,000).

This phone uses high-performance materials in its design, including a flawless 3.5-inch HD sapphire crystal screen, multi-touch support, an 8-megapixel camera, and a beautifully decorated ruby around the shutter button. It also features a polished ceramic earpiece, brushed and polished surgical-grade stainless steel, a selected leather back panel, and Vertu's unique high-fidelity sound system.

In terms of hardware configuration, Vertu, which emphasizes luxury, has always been notorious for its extremely low cost-to-performance ratio, running on the Symbian system with a 680MHz ARM11 structure processor. In terms of network support, the Constellation is a truly global product, using quad-band GSM, five-band WCDMA, and HSDPA. Combined with high-speed WLAN, Constellation users can smoothly access the network wherever they are. The phone has 32GB of fixed user memory, allowing for the storage of large files and images.

Vertu Constellation T

Reference Price: USD 16,500 (approximately RMB 100,000)

Price: USD 51,800 (approximately RMB 320,000)

Speaking of classic models from the past, Motorola would occupy a significant portion, with the V220 being one of them. What? Can the V220 also enter the ranks of luxury phones? Yes! The one you see today is the special edition of the Motorola V220 created by Austrian Peter Aloisson, priced at USD 51,800 (approximately RMB 320,000).

Although the Motorola V220 is an old model, this special edition has significantly increased its value through the addition of gold and silver decorations. This luxurious phone crafted by Austrian Peter Aloisson Company has 1,200 natural diamonds embedded on its surface and its keyboard re-decorated with 18K gold, creating a dazzling and luxurious appearance that catches everyone's attention.

Of course, the merchants' painstaking efforts of "gold plating and diamond inlaying" are aimed at fetching a good price. If your money reaches the point where you can't spend it all, then ordering one for collection purposes could make you feel proud when showing it off to your wealthy friends. Its expensive price of USD 51,800 (approximately RMB 320,000) will surely impress.

Motorola V220 Special Edition

Reference Price: USD 51,800 (approximately RMB 320,000)

Price: USD 88,000 (approximately RMB 540,000)

The world's top luxury mobile phone brand, Vertu, has never disappointed. The Vertu Diamond draws inspiration from high-end custom jewelry and precision watches. During its production process, it inherits the excellent craftsmanship of the past and integrates the traditions of high-precision watchmaking. Every part is independently designed by Vertu. Each phone includes 388 components and has applied for 74 patents.

Since the design and manufacturing process of the Vertu Diamond are intricate and complex, it is almost impossible to replicate. Even ensuring the perfect tactile sensation of the keyboard required four years of work from eight designers. Fully hand-assembled, the entire process is extremely complex and precise, requiring three years of training for professional technicians to independently assemble a single Vertu Diamond.

To emphasize its unique and prestigious feel, the Vertu Diamond is crafted entirely from 18K yellow gold and 18K white gold, fully set with diamonds. It features 729 white diamonds and 205 rare yellow diamonds, intricately set by skilled Swiss artisans. With a global limit of 20 units, it naturally commands an astonishing price of USD 88,000 (approximately RMB 540,000).

Vertu Diamond

Reference Price: USD 88,000 (approximately RMB 540,000)

Price: USD 129,000 (approximately RMB 790,000)

Ulysse Nardin is one of Switzerland's top ten famous watch brands. Leveraging its high reputation and exquisite craftsmanship, the brand has launched a luxury phone called the Ulysse Nardin Chairman. This phone, known as a "hybrid smartphone," even charges via a mechanical system commonly found in watches, very creative though its effectiveness remains to be tested.

The Ulysse Nardin Chairman phone case is embedded with 3,000 genuine high-quality diamonds, totaling 17 carats in weight, making it extremely precious. The phone features a straight-body design, with a 3.2-inch touchscreen on the front. Below the screen is a T9 keyboard for easy operation. The circular design on the back is inspired by mechanical watch movements, accompanied by an 8-megapixel camera lens, capable of handling daily photography needs.

Unlike other high-end luxury phones, this one runs on the Android smartphone system, offering better expandability and usability. Notably, the back of the phone features the "1846" serial number design, representing the brand's founding year in 1846 and possibly indicating that only 1846 units of this phone will be produced. Each phone will have a unique serial number, making this valuable product priced at USD 129,000 (approximately RMB 790,000), and hard to come by.

Ulysse Nardin Chairman Diamond Edition

Reference Price: USD 129,000 (approximately RMB 790,000)

Price: USD 300,000 (approximately RMB 1.83 million)

The Black Diamond smartphone was originally a conceptual design personally handled by renowned Singaporean designer Jaren Goh, meant just to give us a visual feast. However, the Swiss mobile manufacturer VIPN went to great lengths to bring it to life, though the cost of realizing this dream seems rather steep, with a staggering price tag of USD 300,000 (approximately RMB 1.83 million). And even if you have the money, availability is limited, with only 5 units globally.

Despite this, the Black Diamond lives up to its name, showcasing ultimate simplicity and nobility. The almost completely black panel "shines with black brilliance." The body, made from polycarbonate + titanium alloy + platinum processed using seamless technology, along with the bezel-less screen under sapphire glass, exudes extreme luxury.

More interestingly, it is a smartphone running on the Windows Mobile 5.0 operating system, featuring a 400MHz XScale PXA225 processor and a 4-megapixel camera, supporting GSM networks and Wi-Fi wireless internet access.

VIPN Black Diamond

Reference Price: USD 300,000 (approximately RMB 1.83 million)

Price: USD 310,000 (approximately RMB 1.89 million)

The Signature Cobra Limited Edition released by Vertu two years ago reached an astonishing price of USD 310,000 (approximately RMB 1.89 million), leaving people speechless. However, it's difficult for me to comment on its appearance.

Notably, this luxurious phone jointly launched by Vertu and French jeweler Boucheron comes in two models: Signature Cobra and Signature Python. The brand claims to perfectly marry jewelry and watchmaking skills with mobile communication. Among them, the Signature Cobra Limited Edition features 439 rubies embedded in the snake body and two emeralds for the eyes, with only 8 units issued globally. The latter features 48 diamonds, with the pure gold paved model containing 943 round-cut diamonds, with only 26 units issued globally.

From a production perspective, these luxury phones are impeccable. Each component is designed by Vertu, with each phone including 388 parts and applying for 74 patents. Moreover, the phones are fully hand-assembled, with the jewel-encrusted keyboards taking four years of work from eight designers to complete.

Vertu Signature Cobra

Price: USD 1.3 million (approximately RMB 7.95 million)

Peter Aloisson can be considered the founder of high-end mobile phone customization and one of the most influential figures in today's luxury mobile phone market. As one of his representative works, the Diamond Crypto still attracts the eyes of the rich with its dazzling and brilliant diamonds.

This phone is embedded with 50 diamonds, 8 of which are rare blue fancy diamonds, and its buttons are made of 18K rose gold. Perhaps to add some substance, this phone runs on the Windows CE operating system and provides communication encryption functions to ensure the security of the wealthy's calls. The Diamond Crypto, which excels both inside and out, reaches an astronomical price of USD 1.3 million (approximately RMB 7.95 million), amazing ordinary people but absolutely able to showcase the owner's wealth and power.

Diamond Crypto

Reference Price: USD 1.3 million (approximately RMB 7.95 million)

Price: EUR 1 million (approximately RMB 8.14 million)

If the price of Vertu shocks you, then Goldvish would leave you awestruck. As a company dedicated to producing luxury communication tools, many phones under the Goldvish brand are basically made of gold, encrusted with diamonds, and designed by the famous designer Emmanuel Gueit. Plus, the iconic scimitar shape makes them stand out uniquely.

The Le Million phone was the diamond-encrusted phone launched by the company in 2006. Under the platinum casing of the entire phone, it features 120 carats of WS-1 grade crushed diamonds and a crocodile skin back. Globally limited to 100 units, its EUR 1 million (approximately RMB 8.14 million) price once made it the most expensive phone in the world.

Compared to its appearance, the functions of the GoldVish Le Million are quite mundane. According to the designer, this phone features Bluetooth transmission, 8x zoom function, MP3 playback, FM radio, 2GB memory, EDGE network, and more.

GoldVish Le Million

Reference Price: EUR 1 million (approximately RMB 8.14 million)

Price: USD 15.3 million (approximately RMB 100 million)

True luxury always comes from customization. For example, an otherwise common iPhone 5 can fetch a sky-high price of millions of pounds. According to foreign media reports, jewelry designer Stuart Hughes was commissioned by a Chinese businessman to create the most expensive iPhone 5 ever, priced at a staggering USD 15.3 million (approximately RMB 100 million), far surpassing the ordinary retail gold iPhone 5s on the market.

According to foreign media reports, a Liverpool-based jewelry designer created the most expensive iPhone 5 ever. This jewelry designer named Stuart Hughes had previously customized diamond-edition iPhone 4, platinum MacBook Air, and gold iPad 2 for clients. Now, the iPhone 5 became his next project.

Stuart Hughes used a large amount of gold and diamonds on this most expensive iPhone 5, all handmade, which took him nine weeks to complete. From the photos, the metal parts of the iPhone 5's shell have been replaced with 24K gold, and a 26-carat black diamond is embedded in the Home button. Additionally, this iPhone 5 features over 600 diamonds of various sizes, with 53 alone on the Apple logo on the back. The designer revealed that the owner of this iPhone 5 is a Chinese businessman who personally brought a 26-carat black diamond to Liverpool to collaborate with him, hoping he could create the world's most expensive phone.

iPhone 5 (Gold and Diamond Custom Edition)

Reference Price: USD 15.3 million (approximately RMB 100 million)

Why spend 100 million yuan on something other than a phone? Perhaps this is the gap between the rich and the poor. Actually, for ordinary users like us, the commercially available version of the iPhone is already a pretty high-end product. Besides, phones are inherently consumables. After prolonged use, even the most expensive phones will eventually be phased out. Therefore, we suggest that when choosing a phone, users should combine their own needs and select a device that truly suits them.
