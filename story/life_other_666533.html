The Story of Time

Another online submission

Year's end. Cold snap.

The prosperity and noise of the streets. My mood is a little low, but it has nothing to do with the weather. Every time I see snowflakes flying, I miss home. Fortunately, I haven't seen snow this year. That's a unique sentiment for wanderers. People without this experience can't imagine such a kind of sorrow. I miss home on nights like this, and I have no way to vent my emotions.

It seems that I have always been so stubborn, difficult to approach, and not very likable. I've gradually drifted apart from my friends, not for any reason. This is a somewhat cold world, and many people have their own indescribable loneliness and sadness. I like being alone, even if it means loneliness, I still enjoy it immensely. The smell of cigarettes and the dependence on coffee are all things one cannot let go of. Even if you're in the midst of noise, you're still this lonely.

A song plays over and over again, laziness at its peak. I turned off my phone, unwilling to wait in vain for that call. In fact, something that should have been settled long ago, I finally made up my mind, only to find it a bit cruel. Counting the years I've spent in this city, it's almost been close to ten years, what a distant age! My youthful and vibrant days were yesterday's coffee, already cooled by the cold wind, losing its flavor.

Love songs. Years.

What a night to reminisce about! Those bygone years, those people who appeared in my life, their faces are no longer as clear, but they are unforgettable. I think about the happiness and sadness they brought me, the twists and turns of love, excitement, indifference, passion, pain. I think about the encouragement they gave me, the thousand nuances of friendship, persistence, waiting, warmth, care. What precious years! Those people, those stories, have drifted far away in the wind, but there will always be those shadows in my heart that I can never forget.

That young boy, with a sunny smile, with eyes full of hope, walking through the city, intoxicated by the spring breeze, refreshed by the summer rain, free in the autumn wind, poetic in the winter snow. Time flows like water, carrying away how much time, our no longer young bodies. Our no longer simple minds, our unreachable dreams, is this life? Maybe it is, maybe it isn't, more likely, there is no answer.

In time, there are so many stories worth savoring. Perhaps there is still a surging passion within me, but I can no longer find it. I lost it, lost it in time, lost it in the years.

Memories are like keys, unlocking the door to memory, but not finding the desired scene. I am no longer myself, they are no longer the same as before. We are changing, changing each other, until we go from familiar to strangers. Eventually becoming like passersby, this is the sorrow of life, from which no one can escape.
