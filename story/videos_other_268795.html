Reprint - Online game player team sues NetDragon for compensation after being disbanded

by mimima07 on 2009-11-21 09:26:55

On the night of August 26, a case concerning Qi Bangwei, who suffered from the disbandment of his gaming team due to a system glitch in an online game, was heard in Linzhou Court. This gamer, Song Jianghua from Linzhou, as the leader of the team he had built within the game, was elected by other gamers to represent them. He sued Fujian NetDragon Computer Network Technology Co., Ltd. (referred to as NetDragon Company), demanding the restoration of his gaming team and compensation of 50 yuan for long-distance phone fees incurred during the process of dealing with NetDragon.

□ Ancient Newspaper Reporter Chi Bo, Correspondent Li Xia

● Gamer: Who Disbanded My Legion?

Yesterday, Song Jianghua, aged 35, said that he lived in Lingyin Town, Linzhou City. In May 2007, out of interest in a small online game called "Magic Domain," he registered a game account named "Falling Flowers Intentionally." In less than three years, Song Jianghua invested thousands of hours and spent over 50,000 yuan on upgrades and equipment purchases, eventually becoming one of the highest-level players in the game's server.

During this period, Song Jianghua established a legion named "Righteousness Has Traces" within the game and upgraded it to the highest level in the server. Song Jianghua became the highest-ranking member of the legion—the "Legion Leader." Song Jianghua mentioned that during its peak, the "legion" had nearly 1800 members.

Song Jianghua recounted that on the morning of June 21st this year, he suddenly received calls from several gamer friends asking why he disbanded the legion. At that moment, Song Jianghua was completely confused.

Song Jianghua explained that during the game, the upgrading and daily maintenance of the "legion" required contributions from many players. From this perspective, his "legion" had become a collective benefit for a group of gamers. Therefore, Song Jianghua insisted that he could not have disbanded his own "legion."

Song Jianghua then immediately contacted the operator of "Magic Domain"—NetDragon Company. Song Jianghua stated that on the afternoon of June 24th, NetDragon Company replied that there were no computer records of any legion being disbanded between 8 PM on June 20th and the early morning of the next day. "After multiple communications, they refused to restore the disbanded legion." Song Jianghua lamented, "I spent tens of thousands of yuan and worked hard to build this legion, which was now inexplicably disbanded." Complaints from friends and online players made him feel very wronged. With the support of many players, he came to Linzhou Court to sue the network operator of the "Magic Domain" game, NetDragon Company.

● Operator: Cannot Rule Out Account Being Misused

During the trial on August 26th, Song Jianghua requested that the defendant, NetDragon Company, quickly restore the legion and compensate symbolically for the 50 yuan in long-distance phone fees incurred during the interaction with the defendant.

The defense lawyer of the defendant NetDragon Company raised three objections in court: First, when registering the "Falling Flowers Intentionally" game account demonstrated in court, the ID number entered did not belong to Song Jianghua. Therefore, the defendant was not the player of this account and lacked the legal standing to sue in this case.

Second, according to the server computer records of NetDragon Company, at 0:07 AM on June 21st, someone logged into the "Falling Flowers Intentionally" account in Anyang region and disbanded the "Righteousness Has Traces" legion.

Third, based on the IP address used to log into the game, the "Falling Flowers Intentionally" account had been logged in multiple times in both Anyang City and Linzhou City within a short time frame. It cannot be ruled out that the account was shared with friends or misused by others leading to the disbandment of the legion.

● Plaintiff: NetDragon Company Fabricated Evidence

In response, the plaintiff side refuted each point: First, when registering the "Falling Flowers Intentionally" account, Song Jianghua used a friend's ID card, but an ID card alone cannot serve as the sole evidence of account ownership. He himself has consistently been the user of this account, knowing the password and security questions, and the account is linked to his mobile phone. Thus, it can be determined that he is the actual owner of the account.

Second, regarding the computer records presented by NetDragon Company, the plaintiff side argued that since this evidence was extracted and printed from the company's own computers, there is a possibility of data alteration.

Third, the "Falling Flowers Intentionally" account has always been used solely by Song Jianghua, without being lent to others or lost. The frequent changes in IP addresses are due to the operation services provided by the local telecommunications company in Linzhou.

Fourth, Song Jianghua stated that customer service personnel of the defendant had mentioned over the phone that between 8 PM on June 20th and the early morning of the next day, there were no records of any legion being disbanded. He recorded this conversation, which contradicts the computer records provided by the defendant’s lawyer in court, clearly indicating fabrication of evidence by the defendant.

● Judge: Lack of Legal Framework Becomes Bottleneck

Due to significant differences in opinions, the case ultimately failed to reach a settlement agreement.

Judge Ding, who handled the case, stated that according to their understanding, this should be the first nationwide lawsuit caused by the unexpected disbandment of a gaming team formed through heavy investment in an online game.

Judge Ding pointed out that with the increasing popularity of online games, disputes arising from them are also on the rise, mainly focusing on the nature and handling of virtual properties within the game. However, the country currently lacks relevant supporting laws, and online game companies do not have specific regulatory bodies to supervise and constrain their operations. When such cases are brought to court, judges generally lack specialized technical knowledge related to these cases, making it difficult to make accurate judgments during the trial process.

Judge Ding expressed that the court would consult with experts and choose another date for further hearings or directly announce the verdict.
