The Arrival of Luneng's New Coach Was Extremely Mysterious: Reporters Exclaimed He Played Hide-and-Seek Right Away

Report by Zhang Yuan in Jinan - Ivan Kovacic has not yet arrived, but news about him has already been circulating nonstop. This Croatian football coach, known for his tough style, may not necessarily be labeled as "Terrifying Ivan" in the future, but from the start of his arrival at Luneng, he has fully demonstrated an air of mystery.

The change of head coaches, even in world-renowned clubs like Real Madrid and Barcelona, is an ordinary matter. However, in the case of Luneng, it unfolded like a suspenseful detective novel. Despite endless speculation and guessing by fans and media, the club maintained strict confidentiality. Speculations arose one after another, with rumors flying everywhere. But the identity of the new coach remained undisclosed by the club. Eventually, after a long list of coaches including Matthaus, Klinsmann, Fink, and Oguntala were repeatedly speculated upon, Ivan Kovacic gradually surfaced and became increasingly close to becoming the new head coach of Luneng.

On the 15th, it was reported that Luneng had already signed a contract with Ivan Kovacic, with an annual salary not exceeding $500,000. Moreover, Luneng Club would hold a press conference on the 16th to officially announce the Croatian's appointment. Subsequently, insiders revealed that Ivan had already arrived in China, and senior club officials would all be present at the airport to welcome him that evening. The only flight from Beijing to Jinan that night was SC1158, departing from Beijing Capital International Airport at 7 PM and arriving at Yaoqiang Airport in Jinan at 7:50 PM.

That evening, the Guiyou Grand Hotel had already reserved rooms for Ivan's group and prepared welcoming flowers. "The club booked six rooms in total. I heard the new coach is coming," the front desk receptionist told reporters. She also mentioned that Tu Bao used to stay on the 11th floor of Guiyou, but due to recent room shortages, Ivan's group wasn't assigned to the same floor.

Flight SC1158 landed in Jinan on time, but there was no sign of Ivan at the regular passenger exit. The reporter quickly rushed to the VIP area, where the receptionist pointed out, "There was only one foreigner who just walked out. Is that him?" Judging from the slim figure of the foreigner, it was clearly not Ivan. The reporter then searched every corner of the VIP lounge but found nothing.

To confirm whether Ivan had arrived in Jinan, the reporter called a friend at the airport and checked the passenger information of Flight SC1158 through internal systems. Yao Jie's name, the translator, was clearly listed, meaning that both of them were likely on this flight.

If their names were on the passenger list, why couldn't they be seen? An airport staff member reminded the reporter, "Luneng's personnel might have driven the car directly onto the tarmac to pick up the guests." During the recent National Games, the organizing committee also used this high-spec reception method when welcoming Liu Xiang at the airport. If Luneng really picked up Ivan and his team from the tarmac, they were probably already on their way back. The reporter immediately drove off in pursuit but never spotted the "welcoming" convoy. Upon arriving at the hotel, there were still ten minutes left before Ivan's scheduled arrival time. The doorman said, "Ivan hasn't come yet." Looking around, there were no welcoming banners, only a bouquet of flowers quietly placed on a table near the entrance.

More and more reporters gathered at the hotel, enthusiastically discussing the scene of Ivan's appearance. No one doubted that they would soon meet the new Luneng coach. However, as time passed, the reporters grew disappointed. A manager-like person approached and said to everyone, "Please go back. We've just received notice that Ivan won't be coming today." After waiting for so long, the veteran reporters helplessly looked at each other: This Ivan, just arrived in China, and already started playing hide-and-seek. He learned his first lesson fast enough!
