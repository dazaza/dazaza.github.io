If there is a next life, I will still be your woman

She and my friend are somewhat related by kinship. According to the generation, she should be called Grandmother. I only saw her when she was seriously ill, which was when I was in college, accompanying my friend to the hospital where she was hospitalized. At that time, it seemed she had liver disease, her face was yellowish, and her eyes were swollen. Although she was very sick, one could still see from the corners of her eyes, eyebrows, and thick little lips that she must have been a rare beauty in her youth.

Friends said that when she was young, she was among the best-looking women for dozens of miles around. Tall stature, fair skin, a head of black hair. A pair of bright big eyes, red cherry-like small mouth. Her parents both passed away when she was very young, so she grew up at her uncle's house. Her uncle was a chef for weddings and funerals, so she helped him as an apprentice when she was a teenager, and later she could work independently. In Liaodong, working is referred to as "doing jobs."

At the age of eighteen, she married a teacher from her village. He was an only son, had attended private school for a few days, could write and calculate, and was considered a cultured person locally. Their marriage was envied by the villagers due to their matching talents and appearances. On the wedding night, just as the groom lifted her red veil, he was pulled outside by someone who burst in. It turned out the groom was an underground party member whose identity had been exposed. The Nationalist Party people were coming to arrest him. The groom asked the person to wait a moment while he went inside to explain everything to his bride. Grandma understood immediately and told him to run fast and not to wait here to be caught. She then took off her gold and silver jewelry and handed them to him, saying that even if the family was poor, the road might need some help.

That man cupped grandma's face with his hand, seeing her beautiful face filled with tears, making him dizzy with beauty. He said, "You must wait for me, you are my woman..." and then kissed her little mouth, tears falling onto her face... They parted hastily, without even having time for a few intimate words.

The man's parents were both sick, and upon hearing about this situation, they fell ill and bedridden. Grandma, on the second day after entering the home, put on her working clothes and went out to work as a chef, earning grain to support the two elderly people.

She worked outside during the day and came back at night to take care of her in-laws, living a hard life. Whenever she got something good to eat from others, she never ate any of it herself but brought it all back for them. This kind of life continued for two years, with no news from the man. Every night, she bundled up the man's old clothes and hugged them in her bed. Because those clothes smelled like him, hugging them felt like being with him...

In the fifth year, on a snowy afternoon, she was coming out from someone's house when she was kidnapped by a few bandits on the way... Three days later when she returned, there was a large bruise on her forehead, apparently from a collision. No one knew what exactly happened during these three days, but everyone could see that she was covered in injuries. Her in-laws didn't dare to ask too much, but observed her secretly. After a while, she discovered that she was pregnant.

Since knowing this news, the attitude of her in-laws changed greatly compared to before. Despite eating the food she cooked and drinking the medicine she prepared every day, they never looked at her kindly. They thought she was too dirty, disgracing the family name, and unworthy of their son. She knelt down and cried to the two elders, explaining that she had no choice, and finally resorted to threatening her life to be able to return. She said she didn't want to live anymore, but she couldn't abandon the elders, which was something her husband entrusted her with before leaving. Another reason she couldn't die now was because she hadn't actually been with that man yet. For these two reasons, she would live like a pig or a dog. She begged the elders not to tell anyone about her pregnancy.

Every day she still went out to work as a chef, but she had to tightly wrap her belly with wide white cloth strips to hide it from others. However, eventually, two months before delivery, people found out about her pregnancy. In people's eyes, she transformed instantly from a flower into a pile of cow dung stinking to high heavens...

No one was willing to greet her when they met, and no one wanted her to cook anymore. She became like Xiang Linshao in Lu Xun's "Blessing," regarded as an ominous object and an unforgivably bad woman by everyone.

Every night, she held the man's clothes in her arms and asked him through tears, "Would you look at me this way too? This life, I am just your woman..."

On the night of labor, her in-laws could no longer bear her loud cries in the room because she was giving birth to another man's child, not her son's. The old couple hung themselves with two ropes tied to the beams...

Amidst the pain of near-death, she gave birth to a male infant. Looking at the baby's pink-white little face, she knew he would grow up to be a very handsome son. She struggled to get up from the kang (traditional bed) and wrapped the baby in a piece of red cloth. The baby kept crying. She kissed the baby's little face again and again, then dressed herself and stumbled towards the river with the baby in her arms...

When she was rescued, the baby was already nowhere to be seen. From then on, she settled down in another village not far from home. Her beauty still attracted many people. Many people tried to arrange marriages for her, persuading her to remarry, but she refused them all. In her heart, she said that in this life, she was the woman of only one man and would never marry anyone else. She barely made a living by doing short-term work for others, and such a life continued for seventeen years...

Just a year before liberation, she learned from others that her husband was still alive and had become a high-ranking official. Overjoyed, she hurriedly asked someone to write a letter for her and send it to him, telling him that she was still alive. After sending the letter, she eagerly waited for the messenger to return...

Finally, the messenger appeared before her eyes, but did not bring the result she wanted. The letter said that he already knew her situation and thanked her for what she had done for his parents. But he could not forgive her for having another man's child behind his back. He advised her to remarry, as he already had a large family and would not take her back.

She dumbly held the letter in her hands, tears dropping onto the paper one by one. This was the result of her twenty-two years of waiting, this was the reward she received in exchange for her youth and life... She wanted to scold him... hate him... but she couldn't forget the festive red veil of those years, the warm hands that once touched her; she couldn't forget the hasty kiss at parting, and more importantly, the deep gaze in his eyes as he left... Overnight, her full head of black hair turned white...

From then on, she lived alone in the countryside. The village gave her a lot of help. Every time the night fell, she still had the habit of talking to him. She told him how much she missed him, how lonely she was, and how much she missed that wedding night... Her eyes no longer had tears, all the tears had been given to time; her heart no longer grieved, the pain had numbed her feelings...

According to my friend, she lived to be over seventy years old. Before her death, she hadn't eaten or drunk water for several days and couldn't speak. But at the last moment, she looked for someone with her eyes. Everyone looked for a long time and finally understood. People found the person who once delivered her letters. As soon as she saw him, two turbid tears flowed from her dry eyes, and her wrinkled lips moved lightly. That person quickly pressed his ear against her mouth and only heard her say intermittently, "You must tell him... In this life... I am the woman of only one person... If there is a next life... I will still... be... his woman..."
