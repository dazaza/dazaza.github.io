Zhang Zi Yi: The issue of money isn't necessarily solved by money!

by hipposhen on 2010-04-06 22:00:06

After dodging for two months, Zhang Ziyi finally made a direct response to the "donation scandal". A message from 311 Ticketing Network (www.311piao.com) claimed that on the morning of July 17th, the American law firm hired by Zhang Ziyi released a statement regarding the memorandum of the Zhang Ziyi Foundation. The statement confirmed the legal and financial status of the Zhang Ziyi Foundation, particularly answering questions such as "how much money has the foundation raised so far and in what form? Where is the money currently?" which were of concern to netizens. The "imperial petition" filed by the netizens for several months has finally received a response. Moreover, this statement also has the nature of cross-border notarization and certification, but the netizens still refuse to accept it. Some netizens who are addicted to criticizing Zhang have insisted on accusing her, believing that Miss Zhang's "clarification is unclear", and raised "four major doubts", deciding to "continue criticizing Zhang Ziyi".

Zhang Ziyi, as the most accomplished Chinese film star, was once the darling of the film industry and a guarantee for box office success. However, the former谋女郎who once made movie tickets (www.311piao.com) hard to come by has now become a rat crossing the street.

Focus One: Who was counting the money at the donation site?

The lawyer's statement pointed out that "about 15 to 20 media outlets and two or three dozen other guests attended the fundraising event in Cannes on May 21, 2008. At the fundraising event, each cash donation was placed into two transparent boxes, which were passed around the venue. Subsequently, cash donations in different currencies were placed into an envelope. These cash donations were counted and recorded on the outside of the envelope by two event volunteers. According to informed netizens, the staff has already confirmed the above facts in published interviews."

Ms. Zheng, the leader of the Cannes interview team, said that at the donation site, "it wasn't entirely us; there was also a staff member from Zhang Ziyi's side who helped count the money with us, seemingly some kind of assistant." Hai Xia, the text journalist of the Cannes interview team, stated, "the money was counted after the donation event by Ji Lingling and her assistant in a relatively independent VIP area. There were also our other staff members nearby, including Ms. Zheng." Therefore, it seems that Zhang Ziyi's side also sent someone to count the money, not just "two event volunteers."

Focus Two: Why was $1,300+ not deposited into the foundation?

The lawyer's statement pointed out that the fund collected a total of £145, €860, and HK$60 in cash donations at the scene, totaling $1,392.36.

At the same time, the lawyer's statement disclosed that from May 30, 2008, to August 4, 2008, a total of nine deposits were made into the Zhang Ziyi Foundation, amounting to $39,715.50, but did not include the aforementioned $1,392.36. The lawyer's statement did not address why this $1,392.36 was not deposited into the foundation.

Regarding this $1,392.36, netizens questioned, "even if there was only over $1,300 in cash, where was it used and which account did it enter? It should be clarified."

Focus Three: Can the promised donation of $450,000 be recovered?

The lawyer's statement pointed out that there were eight promised donations in total, among which $15,050 had already been received, while the promised $450,000 from another five donors had not yet been obtained. After contacting these promisees, one promised donor indicated that they would fulfill their promise and fully donate $50,000; another person who promised to donate $100,000 said that due to recent economic conditions, they could not make a full donation; the two others who each promised to donate $100,000 said they had already donated to another charity set up for Sichuan earthquake relief, with one indicating that they might still consider donating to the Zhang Ziyi Foundation, while the other had not decided yet.

Wendi Deng is one of the last two "promised donors," and she has already donated $100,000 after Zhang Ziyi's Cannes fundraising, as recorded in her blog. However, according to this lawyer's statement, the amount that Zhang Ziyi can confirm to recover should only be $50,000.

Focus Four: Where is the €2,700?

At the beginning of the "donation scandal," the report by Sohu on May 22, 2008, which was used as "evidence" by many netizens, wrote: "these RMB 3.5 million destined for disaster area reconstruction consist of a check for €2,700, and cash donations of US$465,050, £145, €860, and HK$60." Among these, according to the lawyer's statement, the US$465,050 was a promised donation, "which was also counted and recorded by two event volunteers from Sohu on the same envelope (together with cash donations). But the volunteer made a mistake when recording, writing the promised donation card as 'check'."

Now, the US$465,050 promised donation has been clarified, but where is the "check for €2,700"? Thus, some netizens questioned, "who is lying? Is it Zhang Ziyi or Sohu?" Some netizens lamented, "regarding the issue of money, it will never be clear no matter how much is said."

Additionally, the lawyer's statement only disclosed the situation of the Cannes fundraising. Some netizens found the donation advertisement placed by Zhang Ziyi in The Hollywood Reporter, and how much money was raised through that advertisement is also of interest to netizens. In response to this, Zhang Ziyi mentioned in an exclusive interview with China Daily that "unfortunately, we did not receive any donations." It seems that even celebrities face difficulties when raising funds.

After going through this ordeal, we don't know when we will see Miss Zhang's movie (www.311piao.com) works again, nor do we know how many people will still be willing to buy tickets for her movies.
