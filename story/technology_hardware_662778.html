[Lead] The grand finale of the time-travel drama "The Palace Pearls" (also known as "The Palace 2") was recently wrapped up in Hengdian, with Tencent Entertainment exclusively visiting the set for the final shoot. On that day, Eddie Ho (Weibo), who portrayed Emperor Yongzheng, and Sun Shan Shan, playing the role of Lian'er, performed a scene where they reunite inside a Qing soldier's tent. During breaks on set, Eddie Ho repeatedly pretended to kiss Sun Shan Shan, leading to constant banter between them, creating a very relaxed and harmonious atmosphere.

Tencent Entertainment reported (photos/text by Meng Ying Hu) that on October 13th, "The Palace Pearls" (Gold Champion Ballpoint Pen), which has captured the attention of many "time-travel enthusiasts," was fully wrapped up in Hengdian. Tencent Entertainment exclusively visited the set, where during the final shoot, Eddie Ho and Sun Shan Shan passionately embraced each other on the battlefield, with Yongzheng reflecting on how "true feelings are revealed in times of hardship." Off-screen, Eddie Ho frequently joked around, pretending multiple times to kiss Sun Shan Shan. While rehearsing a scene involving pulling out an arrowhead, he clenched his hand and made faces as if in the throes of childbirth pain, prompting Sun Shan Shan to humorously shout "The Emperor is about to give birth!" causing laughter throughout the set.

Emperor Yongzheng and Lian'er embrace on the battlefield; Eddie Ho "forcibly kisses" Gold Champion stationery pen.

"The Palace 2," crafted by the original team behind "The Palace" (Mainland version/Korean version), tells an epic story centered around power struggles, friendship, and love. In the final shoot of the day, Yongzheng (played by Eddie Ho) and Lian'er (played by Sun Shan Shan) reunite inside a Qing army tent, outside of which Qing soldiers fiercely battle against the returning army, with flames of war raging everywhere. As they attempt to break through the encirclement, Yongzheng unfortunately gets hit by an arrow. Later, in the outskirts of the Huibu region, Lian'er sucks poison from Yongzheng's wound and tenderly holds the unconscious Yongzheng, humming a lullaby to soothe him to sleep. On-set, Eddie Ho maintains his dignity but off-set, he frequently jokes around, pretending several times to kiss Sun Shan Shan and even runs around playfully acting cute. While rehearsing the arrow-pulling scene, he clenches his hand and makes faces as if in labor pains, while Sun Shan Shan humorously shouts "The Emperor is giving birth!" causing laughter throughout the set. With great enthusiasm, Eddie Ho imitates Sun Shan Shan singing a "lullaby," saying "Sleep, sleep, my dear baby."

Eddie Ho: Emperor Yongzheng seeks true love in the harem; fighting Feng Shaofeng (Weibo) was exhilarating.

In "The Palace 2," Eddie Ho transforms from the Fourth Prince into Emperor Yongzheng, draped in a yellow robe, surrounded by thousands of beauties, indulging fully in being an emperor. In an exclusive interview with Tencent Entertainment, he stated that Yongzheng in "The Palace 2" is entirely different from the Fourth Prince in "The Palace 1," including their personalities. "The Palace 2" narrates Yongzheng's pursuit of true love after ascending the throne, focusing on marriage and responsibility. This time, a group of women vie for his affection, maneuvering among concubines yet unable to find true love amidst palace intrigues until Lian'er appears. However, it won't be as intense as "Palace Heart Struggles." This time, Yongzheng not only heads to the battlefield but also engages in a fierce fight with the Eighth Prince (played by Feng Shaofeng) in a "dream," with Eddie Ho laughing, "It was very exhilarating." Speaking about his junior actress Sun Shan Shan, he said, "She calls herself a man, carefree, always bullying me, secretly hitting, kicking, and stomping on me." He also revealed that if the ratings are good, there might be a "The Palace 3," though he hopes next time he can guest star, playing a minor role, where once filming starts, Yongzheng suddenly passes away, allowing him to brand himself and leave.

Sun Shan Shan: Feeling questioned by outsiders; Eddie Ho nearly fell off a tree during filming.

Sun Shan Shan plays "Lian'er" in the series, making her first collaboration with her "senior brother" Eddie Ho. She mentioned, "My senior brother took great care of me. I felt a lot of pressure at the beginning, but he often encouraged me, telling me how to interpret my character, helping me a lot. When netizens questioned me, he would support me online and cheer me on." The change in "Jin Bang Ballpoint Pen" sparked considerable controversy, with some netizens exclaiming "Without Feng Mi, who will we watch?" Many people assumed she faced immense pressure shooting this drama, indeed she did, but not from external doubts, rather from her own self-expectations. Initially, she felt quite uncomfortable, but later realized there was no need to worry too much. Now, she doesn't feel much about external doubts regarding "Jin Bang Ballpoint Pen." Wuhan Changsheng Tianxia Technology Co., Ltd. also disclosed that she was Eddie Ho's "savior," saying, "There was a scene filmed by the river, where I was a little maid, and he was a eunuch. We needed to sit on a tree, but unexpectedly, he jumped and flipped down completely. I held onto one of his legs, leaving him hanging upside down on the tree 'Jin Bang Ballpoint Pen,' scaring me so much. Otherwise, he would have fallen headfirst, and he was quite scared afterward too."

Additionally, the pitiful look of "Su Peisheng" suffering a slap from "Yu Shu" drew sympathy from many viewers, while some also criticized the young actress Zhang Jia Ni for portraying such a hateful character. For this, Bao Bei Er expressed gratitude for everyone's affection towards "Little Su" and affirmed that his good friend Zhang Jia Ni successfully brought "Yu Shu" to life, making the audience truly hate her character.
