What does "autumn flood" mean?

Flood Season

The flood season is a hydraulic term, referring to the period in a year when river water levels rise significantly and regularly. "Flood" means the appearance of abundant water, and "flood season" refers to the time when rivers are full of water. The flood season does not equate to a flood disaster, but floods usually occur during the flood season.

"Flood" means the appearance of abundant water, and "flood season" refers to the time when rivers are full of water. The flood season does not equate to a flood disaster, but floods usually occur during the flood season. The changes in river runoff mainly depend on the replenishment conditions of the river. The replenishment sources of China's rivers include rainwater, snow and ice meltwater, groundwater, etc., with rainwater replenishment being the main source. Some rivers can form spring floods due to snow and ice meltwater, but they generally do not cause disasters.

Due to the differences in rainfall times in various places, the flood season is not consistent. Rivers south of the Yangtze River can cause floods in early summer.

In central and eastern regions, concentrated rainfall in July and August easily triggers floods.

"Ice Flood" is a phenomenon that occurs in northern rivers during spring thaw under special geographical conditions. Some northern rivers flow from south to north in certain sections, such as the Gansu Ningxia to Inner Mongolia section of the Yellow River and some sections of the Heilongjiang River. During the spring thaw, the upstream thaws first, and floating ice flows downstream while the downstream has not yet thawed, causing blockages by floating ice and leading to rising water levels. Moreover, floating ice can cut into the embankment, making it more likely to breach and cause flooding. If this situation occurs, explosive methods are often used to eliminate the danger.

In China's northern rivers, the Yellow River and the Heilongjiang River are mainly threatened by ice floods. The ice flood of the Yellow River occurs in March, while the Heilongjiang River occurs later.

From May 1st to October 20th each year is the flood season. In the Shanghai area, rainfall during the flood season is significantly higher than in other months, so the water level of rivers is higher than in winter, but the water level of rivers in the Shanghai area is greatly affected by tides. From May to October each year, due to the large tidal force of the sun and moon, the high tide level is high, so we conventionally refer to May to October as the flood season (in the Shanghai area, heavy rain may occur as early as late March, and heavy rain disappears by late October).

Autumn Flood Season

The rise in water level caused mainly by autumn storms (or prolonged heavy rain) is called the autumn flood season.

The start and end time of the flood season (mainly referring to the major floods in summer and autumn) is generally divided according to the frequency of floods during this period. Floods exceeding the average value of the annual maximum flood peak flow over many years are called "major floods." The determination of the flood season is to ensure that more than 90% of the "major floods" occur within the designated period; the main flood season is determined by controlling more than 80% of the "major floods" to determine the time period. For example: In southern China, April to September is the flood season, and May to June is the main flood season; the Pearl River has a flood season from mid-April to September, with April to June as the pre-flood season and July to September as the post-flood season, and May to June is the main flood season; the Yangtze River has a flood season from May to mid-October, and July to August is the main flood season; the Huai River has a flood season from June to September, and July to August is the main flood season; the Yellow River has a flood season from July to October, and July to August is the main flood season; the Hai River has a flood season from July to August, with late July to early August as the main flood season; the Songhua River has a flood season from July to September, with late August to early September as the main flood season. Analysis shows that the start time of the flood season in various parts of China gradually delays from south to north following the movement of the rain belt, while the length of the flood season gradually shortens from south to north. The Zhujiang River, Qiantang River, Oujiang River, Yellow River, Han River, and Jialing River have obvious double flood seasons, with the former divided into pre-flood and post-flood seasons, and the latter divided into summer flood and autumn flood seasons. July to August is the time when national floods occur most frequently.
