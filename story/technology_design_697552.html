What does "Da Xi Qian" mean in Zha Jin Hua? QQ Zha Jin Hua

by d6g6j0l9n2 on 2012-03-08 16:16:27

Questioner: I am not really a fan of playing games. During gatherings with friends, I often hear them talk about playing Zha Jin Hua and something about "Xi" money. I don't understand what this means. Could you explain?

Passerby A: At first, I also didn't know what "Xi" money meant in the context of Zha Jin Hua. I've always played Zha Jin Hua without ever involving "Xi". However, after doing a Baidu search, I found out that typically, when you have a "Leopard" (three of a kind) or a "Straight Flush" (also called a "Qing Long"), there will be "Xi", which translates to money. The amount depends on the stakes of your game. Generally, if you reveal a Leopard, each player gives you an additional 20 units of currency, and for a Straight Flush, each gives 10 units. The amount of "Xi" money should be agreed upon beforehand.
