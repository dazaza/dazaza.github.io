Movie Shanghai Film Festival opening segment settled He Ping's "McGrath" tops the list - China Movie Network

by letaoeoe on 2011-06-01 00:24:01

In fact, as early as the beginning of this year, the Shanghai Film Festival has discussed with the director and production team about the possibility of "The Wheat Field" being the opening film. Although both parties were in agreement, due to the slow progress of the film's post-production time and Director He Ping's unwillingness to lower the artistic standards and industrial norms of the film, a final decision was not made. After nearly half a year of intensive post-production work, the film is finally ready to be shown at the opening ceremony of the film festival for its world premiere.

"The Wheat Field" is the latest masterpiece created by director He Ping after his 2003 film "Hero". Since filming began, it has maintained a low profile, but because He Ping is an unconventional figure among the fifth generation directors, and due to the stunning appearance of the movie posters and trailers domestically and internationally, the film has been unanimously regarded by the film industry as one of the most anticipated works of 2009.

From the information released earlier, the lineup of competing and screening films for this year's film festival is unprecedentedly strong, leaving people amazed and envious. Now that "The Wheat Field" is set to open the festival, it indeed significantly raises the expectations for the festival.

As one of the most anticipated films of the year, "The Wheat Field", directed by renowned Chinese director He Ping, who once served as the chairman of the jury for the Asian New Talent Award at the Shanghai International Film Festival, and co-produced by Classic Media and Bona Film Group, has officially confirmed that it will have its global premiere at the opening ceremony of the 12th Shanghai International Film Festival.

The film features several top actors from today's Chinese film industry, including Fan Bingbing, Huang Jue, Wang Xueqi, Wang Zhiwen, and Wang Ji. At the same time, Director He Ping has always been known for discovering and nurturing new talent, and this time "The Wheat Field" features newcomers Du Jiayi and Wang Jiajia in major roles. All these elements have made filmmakers and film audiences eagerly anticipate the release of the film.

"I strive to make this film a good combination of art, entertainment, and modern film craftsmanship, which is my consistent pursuit in making movies," said Director He Ping in an interview. For a film with perfect visuals, this statement is the best promise to the audience. A loyal fan of He Ping online commented: "Director He makes films very slowly; now after six years of hard work on this one, it's absolutely worth looking forward to."

In the film, there are both vast and splendid visual beauty and rich detail descriptions, solid story content, and narrative-poetic visual language, showcasing a cultural quality rarely seen in recent Chinese entertainment blockbusters. A well-known filmmaker commented on "The Wheat Field": "This is an entertainment blockbuster for people with some knowledge." It can be seen that "The Wheat Field" is definitely one of the most surprising works in Chinese cinema this year, and it will change the aesthetic experience of watching Chinese costume blockbusters in recent years.

On June 12, Director He Ping will hold a press conference in Shanghai with all the main creative staff. On that day, the curtain of the film festival will officially rise.
