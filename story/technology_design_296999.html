From the test results, unqualified products have emerged in three aspects of shoe quality. First, the application of the sole does not conform to standards; the bending stiffness of the sole is unqualified, causing deformation and instability when wearing, which can easily lead people to fall down the stairs and hurt their feet. Second, some parts of imported shoes have cracked or become unglued. Such problems often occur in high-end imported shoes but have also appeared in other products. In addition, 45.5% of the shoes had issues with part of the label or tagging being unqualified.

As the chairman of Aokang Group, Wang Zhentao expressed his concern regarding the impact of EU anti-dumping measures on brand influence and production transfer. According to Wang Zhentao, Chinese enterprises will not only lose more business opportunities due to anti-dumping but will gradually lose sales channels. Nowadays, due to the influence of anti-dumping, some international brand names are transferring their production bases to countries in Asia and South Asia.

In December 2006, four well-known international brands—Toshiba, Fujitsu, NEC, and HP—had five types of notebook computers in Zhejiang province that were found to be non-compliant by the Industrial and Commercial Bureau. On December 12, 2005, the Zhejiang Industrial and Commercial Bureau exposed quality issues with Sony digital cameras, ordering a halt to the sale of problematic Sony digital cameras. Especially on December 29, the Zhejiang Industry and Commerce Department publicly disclosed the burning of Louis Vuitton, including its pleasures, Walundinuo, and more than 10 top world brands such as BOSS of double famous leather shoes in Europe.

According to the Industrial and Commercial Bureau, out of 46 batches of sampled leather shoes in Zhejiang province, 35 batches were unqualified, resulting in a pass rate of only 24%. Although some Chinese footwear enterprises have appealed to the WTO according to relevant regulations against EU anti-dumping, the case will last for at least two years, making it unlikely that the 16.5% anti-dumping duties will change in the short term. "This will not affect China’s vast footwear enterprises, but it will cause tremendous influence on shoes exported to the EU," said Wang Zhentao.

Due to China's export trade growing in recent years, there has been an increasing number of trade disputes worldwide. One manifestation of this contradiction is the pressure on our footwear industry, especially after October 5, when the European Council approved the formal anti-dumping measures on Chinese leather shoes. Since October 7 of last year, Chinese leather uppers have been subject to anti-dumping duties.

On December 29, 2006, in the suburbs of Hangzhou landfill, many people were shocked to see LV, BOSS, and other internationally famous brand shoes piled up with the labeled slogan "import inferior leather shoes" before being doused with gasoline and set on fire. This batch of inferior imports was destroyed under the supervision of the Zhejiang Industrial and Commercial Bureau, described by its director Zheng YuMin as tearing off the mask of "brand."

In fact, many domestic consumers are still blindly infatuated with imported goods, including automobile fittings, medical equipment, and clothing. Numerous imported goods have experienced quality issues in recent years. The reporter learned from relevant channels in 2007 that starting from March 15, known as Consumer Rights Day, the AQSIQ (General Administration of Quality Supervision, Inspection and Quarantine) and the State Administration of Industry and Commerce will conduct quality supervision inspections on imported goods on the market and address quality problems.

Production shift and brand quality: The transfer of production bases will increase product costs. Additionally, due to changes in origin, it may also affect product quality. Because new manufacturers producing new products need a period of adjustment, whether feedback from the market and producers takes some time, so during this period, endogenous output quality products may encounter certain problems.

Zhejiang QC slams inferior import shoes: With the advantages of Zhejiang province, it has been one of our major export provinces. According to the statistical data of Zhejiang province in 2006, the total import and export in Zhejiang amounted to 139.15 billion dollars, up 29.6% year-on-year. The total export list ranked ahead of Guangdong, Jiangsu, and Shanghai in growth, including exports of 100.9 billion US dollars, up $38.25 billion in fiber optic ductoscopy, and imports rose 25.1%.

At the same time, as the biggest trading partner, Zhejiang province's bilateral trade volume with the EU in 2006 reached $29.32 billion, up 24.6% year-on-year. Based on this background, the Zhejiang Commerce Department plays a strong role in quality control, particularly exposing domestic well-known brands and revealing regulatory issues that others dare not touch in multinational companies. They bravely expose product quality problems and maintain a firm determination to never let unqualified commodities pass.
