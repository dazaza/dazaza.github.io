Function Introduction:

The "Telecommunication Construction Project Estimate and Budget Computer-Aided Compilation System" (SF-GYS 2008) software is based on the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology's communication industry standards (such as "Methods for Compiling Estimates and Budgets of Telecommunication Construction Projects and Fee Quotas", etc.) and combined with the current state of industry development. In short, this system is more intuitive, more flexible, fully integrating the flexibility of spreadsheets with the speed of specialized software!

This system mainly consists of three parts: the estimate and budget compilation module, the database maintenance module, and the summary and export module. Through this system, compilers can conveniently create estimate, budget, settlement, and final account documents for telecommunication construction projects and automatically transfer relevant equipment and materials to the "Fixed Asset Report", thus improving the efficiency of the compilers. This system has the characteristics of complete quotas, full functions, intuitive human-computer interface, convenient operation, simple usability, and flexible fee calculation. It is widely applicable to the preparation work of new construction, expansion, and renovation projects in telecommunication line engineering, telecommunication equipment installation engineering, telecommunication pipeline engineering, etc. This system is a good helper for all units and individuals engaged in telecommunication construction project design, construction, construction, supervision, etc., to carry out estimate, budget, settlement, and final account preparation work. Its main functions and features are as follows:

1. Includes complete telecommunication construction project budget quotas and telecommunication construction project survey and design charge standards.

The software already includes relevant budget quotas and fee quotas involved in telecommunication construction projects, referring to the following specific related standards:

1. Methods for compiling estimates and budgets of telecommunication construction projects, fee quotas for telecommunication construction projects, and fee quotas for shifts of machinery and instruments used in telecommunication construction projects;

2. Budget quotas for telecommunication construction projects, Volume I: Telecommunication power supply equipment installation engineering;

3. Budget quotas for telecommunication construction projects, Volume II: Wired communication equipment installation engineering;

4. Budget quotas for telecommunication construction projects, Volume III: Wireless communication equipment installation engineering;

5. Budget quotas for telecommunication construction projects, Volume IV: Telecommunication line engineering;

6. Budget quotas for telecommunication construction projects, Volume V: Telecommunication pipeline engineering;

7. Compilation of supporting legal documents for the preparation of estimates and budgets for telecommunication construction projects;

8. Engineering survey and design charge standards [Revised Edition 2002].

2. Simple to use, flexible functionality

The interface design of SF-GYS 2008 is simple and clear, with convenient and easy-to-use operations. Users can master its basic operational requirements within tens of minutes. Simple operation does not mean simple functionality; in fact, SF-GYS 2008 has very powerful and flexible functions such as report customization, self-definition of fee names, formula-based customization of fee calculations, multi-level subdivision of materials, database maintenance and management, etc. Users can easily compile estimate, budget, settlement, and final account documents through it.

3. Flexible main material generation function

1. When users input sub-item quotas, the main materials associated with the sub-item are automatically linked.

2. Users can use the "Generate Main Materials, Machinery, Instruments" button to generate the main materials associated with the sub-item quotas into the main material table, the main material table supplied by Party A, the recovered material table, and the reused material table.

4. Powerful rate modification function

Adjustments to various related rates are intuitive, simple, and easy to operate. Rates in all tables can be quickly adjusted within the tables.

5. Data synchronization

After any modifications are made in any table, manual refreshment will recalculate the data in the associated tables, keeping the data synchronized across all tables.

6. Shielding of tables without data

Tables that are not needed during compilation can be shielded to improve compilation efficiency. This system has the function of shielding tables without data. Compilers can quickly shield unused tables according to their needs, making the main interface clearer and easier for compilers to grasp the status of the used tables.

7. Powerful import and export

It can conveniently convert estimate and budget files and summary files into Excel files. It can also import and export files, project templates, and even databases, greatly facilitating users' personalized work requirements.

8. File and database protection

After setting the current estimate and budget file as a read-only file, others can only view but cannot modify it. To make modifications, the correct password must be entered. Database modifications follow the same principle; only after entering the correct password can modifications be made, thereby protecting the rights of the compiler.

9. Project Summary

This function can consolidate several unit projects into a single item project or several single item projects into a construction project. After consolidation, the consolidated results can be edited, previewed, printed directly, and the results can be converted into Excel format files.

10. Database Maintenance

The database of this system is both secure and unified, yet relatively independent and personalized. It also has great convenience and simplicity in data maintenance. Theoretically, users can establish countless custom libraries and store them anywhere. It not only helps enterprises prepare project estimates and budgets but also assists construction enterprises in creating their own construction quotas.

11. More Humanized Considerations

Considering the diversity of actual applications in the market economy, this system provides many personalized functions, thereby enhancing user value. For example, the material library can be customized for printing to help users compare and reference. Users will certainly discover more humanized content during use.
