Greenpeace sues Wal - Mart for selling illegal genetically modified rice

by ditan360 on 2010-06-30 08:57:28

Low Carbon Network exclusive - Today, the international environmental organization Greenpeace went to Luohu District People's Court in Shenzhen to sue the China headquarters of Wal-Mart, the world's largest chain retailer, for "breach of contract compensation disputes" and other reasons. Greenpeace purchased illegal genetically modified loose rice at Wal-Mart supermarket that had not yet obtained commercial circulation qualifications, which not only violated relevant Chinese laws but also infringed on the legitimate rights and interests of consumers. Greenpeace demanded that Wal-Mart immediately destroy all genetically modified rice currently being sold and in inventory, establish a related control system to prevent similar problems from happening again, and issue a formal apology to the public; at the same time, the organization also requested that relevant government departments conduct a thorough investigation from seed source to rice products to ensure that the entire food chain is completely free from illegal genetically modified rice contamination.

"Although the Ministry of Agriculture has repeatedly emphasized that there is no commercial production or sale of genetically modified rice in China, Wal-Mart has been found multiple times selling illegal genetically modified rice, disregarding China's laws and the health of consumers. Therefore, Greenpeace has decided this time to initiate legal proceedings and require Wal-Mart to assume corresponding legal responsibilities," said Wang Weikang, Food and Project Director of Greenpeace.

In May-June 2010, Greenpeace conducted sampling of loose rice in four cities: Wuhan, Shenzhen, Dongguan, and Foshan, at Wal-Mart, CR Vanguard, AEON (Jizhida), and ParknShop supermarkets respectively, and commissioned qualified third-party laboratories to test these samples for genetically modified components. The latest test results showed that the sample named 'Domestic Fragrant Rice' sampled from the Xudong Street branch of Wal-Mart Deep International Investment Co., Ltd. in Wuhan was positive for genetically modified organisms. According to relevant laws, commercial production and sale of genetically modified rice in China are illegal.

Greenpeace revealed in a survey result published on this year's "March 15 International Consumer Rights Day" that illegal genetically modified rice was also sold at Wal-Mart's Huangxingnan Road branch in Changsha, Hunan. For this reason, Greenpeace has already reported the issue to relevant Wal-Mart departments through various means, but no active measures have been seen from Wal-Mart to resolve the problem so far.

Wang Weikang said: "As the world's largest chain retailer, Wal-Mart fully understands the long-term impact of genetically modified foods on human health and the environment. As early as 2005, Wal-Mart made a commitment to British consumers that it would not sell any genetically modified products. However, Wal-Mart not only implemented double standards for Chinese consumers but also adopted an attitude of indifference towards Chinese law."

Greenpeace demands that Wal-Mart establish a strict control system and issue a solemn apology to the public. At the same time, Greenpeace hopes that this lawsuit will warn other supermarkets, promote the entire industry to respect and effectively protect the legitimate rights and interests of consumers, and seriously take on the social responsibility of ensuring public health and protecting the environment.

Greenpeace also requests that relevant Chinese government departments undertake their corresponding regulatory responsibilities and make up for regulatory loopholes. Wang Weikang said: "Since genetically modified violations have appeared in various links such as seeds, rice, and rice products, our country urgently needs to establish a team including different departments such as agriculture, industry and commerce, environmental protection, health, and well-being to conduct comprehensive investigations and supervision."

Greenpeace is a global environmental organization committed to promoting positive change through concrete actions and protecting the earth's environment and world peace. We have branches in more than 40 countries and regions around the world, with over 2.8 million supporters.
