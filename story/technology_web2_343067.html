by agklh8dd on 2010-04-10 15:24:28

Scene of the incident

□ Chief Reporter Yu Chao of Today's Newspaper

Yesterday, a post titled "Bank Manager Sexually Harasses and Brutally Beats Woman After Drinking, Deputy County Magistrate Looks On" in the People's Daily Online "People's Appeals" section caught the particular attention of netizens.

The poster, identified as "Netizen Ms. Liu," wrote that at around 10 p.m. on March 25 this year, an enterprise boss from Gushi County, Xinyang City hosted a dinner for Min Mou, the manager of the Gushi branch of Bank of China, and Zhang Mou, the deputy county magistrate of Gushi County. Inside a teahouse in the county, after drinking, Min Mou "suddenly had wicked thoughts" and under the guise of toasting, he groped three women who were present. When his behavior was strongly opposed, Min Mou "became enraged with shame, grabbed Chen (one of the women) and slammed her to the ground, roughly grabbing her chest and private parts, and pulled her hair, slamming her head against the floor multiple times." The post reflected that when this happened, Deputy County Magistrate Zhang Mou was present but did not intervene, instead "leaving indifferently."

Beaten woman kneels and pleads for the deputy magistrate's help to uphold justice

In the People's Daily Online "People's Appeals" section, the poster named Ms. Liu claimed that at around 10 p.m. on March 25, 2010, an enterprise boss from Gushi County, Xinyang City hosted a dinner for Zhang Mou, the deputy county magistrate in charge of agriculture in the county, and Min Mou, the manager of the Gushi branch of Bank of China, among others.

After having their fill of food and drink, at the request of the two distinguished guests, the boss invited four women—Xu Mou, Chen Mou, and others from the Guobin Grand Hotel in Gushi County—to the "Yantianxue Green Tea House" in Chengguan Town for tea. Since the four women were marketing staff at the Guobin Grand Hotel, in order to maintain and develop customer relationships, and because they were invited by friends, and knowing beforehand that a deputy county magistrate would be present, they thought nothing untoward would happen, so they happily accepted the invitation and came to the tea house.

During tea time, under the influence of alcohol, Manager Min suddenly had wicked thoughts and under the guise of toasting, he touched three women including Chen Mou. Xu Mou tried to stop him but was also grabbed and molested by Manager Min. Min's vulgar behavior was strongly opposed by the four women, who then decided to leave.

Min Mou was not satisfied and took the opportunity while Chen Mou went to the restroom to follow her and molest her again. When his lewd actions were rejected once more, Manager Min became enraged with shame, grabbed Chen Mou and slammed her to the ground, roughly grabbing her chest and private parts, and pulled her hair, slamming her head against the floor multiple times. He then dragged Chen Mou from the second floor of the tea house to the first floor. When Xu Mou and the other three women tried to rescue her, they were also beaten by Min Mou in the tea house parking lot (later determined by forensic doctors that Chen Mou and Xu Mou suffered minor injuries). After beating them, Min Mou left indifferently.

Throughout the entire incident, Deputy County Magistrate Zhang Mou did not offer any persuasion. Strangely, when Chen Mou knelt down and pleaded with Deputy County Magistrate Zhang Mou to uphold justice, Zhang coldly said to her, "Young lady, don't make a fuss, Manager Min has connections." Then he left indifferently. Later, after the concerned onlookers at the scene called the police, the four women were sent to the hospital.

Several days have passed since the incident, and not only has Manager Min not actively apologized, but he has continuously used his connections to threaten and intimidate the parties involved, causing Chen Mou, Xu Mou, and the other four women to live in fear and remain indoors. Facing "Manager Min with connections," even the respected deputy county magistrate dare not uphold justice, and is even helping him evade responsibility. Who else will help them? Who else can uphold justice for them?

Tea house manager: All because of alcohol

The "Yantianxue Green Tea House" mentioned in the post is quite famous in Gushi County.

When asked about what happened on the evening of March 25, the duty manager of the tea house said that indeed, an enterprise boss from Gushi County hosted a dinner for Manager Min Zhita of the Gushi branch of Bank of China and Deputy County Magistrate Zhang Jiancheng in charge of agriculture in the county. "They drank wine outside at a hotel, and when they came to the tea house, they started singing in the multi-functional hall of the tea house, drinking red wine while singing. Several men got drunk, and for some reason, they started quarreling with several women. Manager Min hit two of the women, and it got quite messy. One of the women surnamed Chen had her head broken." The duty manager of the tea house said, "It's all because of alcohol. Actually, Manager Min is usually a nice person."

Ms. Xu: No one has come forward to apologize

After much effort, the reporter contacted one of the women present that evening, Ms. Xu, who is a customer director at the Guobin Grand Hotel in Gushi County. When asked about the events of that night, she seemed very cautious but still admitted that the events of that night occurred. "Currently, no one has come forward to apologize, the matter is still being handled, as for what the outcome will be, it's hard to say."

Ms. Chen: Too much pressure, resting at home

Yesterday afternoon, the reporter also contacted Ms. Chen mentioned in the post, who was chased and beaten by Manager Min. However, she on the phone "did not want to give an interview, did not want to talk about this matter." Ms. Chen said, "I am currently under too much pressure, don't ask me about this matter, I am still resting at home."

The reporter asked where the pressure came from? "It has greatly affected my family, and there are pressures from various aspects. The perpetrator has not yet come forward to apologize, but I believe there should be a result eventually."

Taxi driver: The scene was chaotic, couldn't bear to watch

On the evening of March 25, a taxi driver witnessed the scene in front of the tea house: "At that time, I was driving by, I saw a man kicking and punching a woman, the woman was knocked to the ground, crying loudly. There were several other women trying to stop him, the scene was chaotic. A man was hitting a woman, I really couldn't bear to watch, so I made the emergency call."

Police station official: Manager harassed and was refused, then beat people

The officers who responded to the call that evening were from the Xiguan Police Station of the Gushi County Public Security Bureau. The reporter learned that the case was handled by a political instructor surnamed He and a police officer surnamed Chai from the station.

An official of the station revealed to the reporter that the person who hosted the dinner for Manager Min and Deputy County Magistrate Zhang that evening was Zhao Mou, the factory manager of a printing plant in Gushi County. The several women were all customer managers at the Guobin Grand Hotel, called over to accompany the drinking. "Min Zhita was very drunk, went up to harass the women, and after being refused, he hit people. The girl surnamed Chen was the most seriously injured, and the other three women were also injured to varying degrees. The case has been transferred to the Gushi County Public Security Bureau."

It is understood that the printing plant is called "Quanyi Color Printing".

Parties involved

Manager claims "at the police station", Deputy County Magistrate plays "hide and seek"

Regarding the situation reflected in the post, as the main party involved, what does Min Zhita, the manager of the Gushi County branch of the Bank of China, have to say about the matter? At 5:45 PM yesterday, the reporter called Min Zhita and asked about the situation that evening. He just said: "The public security organs are currently investigating." The reporter repeatedly asked about the situation during the incident, and he emphasized twice to the reporter: "I am currently at the police station, it's not convenient to talk."

Another party mentioned in the post—Deputy County Magistrate Zhang Jiancheng of Gushi County—was he really standing idly by as Netizen Ms. Liu claimed? Starting from 5:20 PM yesterday, the reporter began calling Deputy County Magistrate Zhang's phone, and after more than ten calls, no one answered. At 6 PM, Zhang Deputy County Magistrate's mobile phone showed a call status, a few minutes later the phone was connected again, but still no one answered. As of the reporter's deadline, Zhang Deputy County Magistrate's mobile phone remained unanswered.

■ Information capture

Yesterday, the reporter saw such a message on the Gushi County government website: The Bank of China grants a credit line of 150 million yuan to enterprises in Gushi County. The message finally stated: The Gushi County branch of the Bank of China signed credit intention contracts totaling 1.5 billion yuan with four enterprises including Quanyi Color Printing and Xinhe Real Estate, and promoted its major financial products.
