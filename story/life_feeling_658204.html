Strangers passing by, cherry blossoms falling

The road of life is long. During the slow journey, we walk alone, follow our own path, sing our own songs, write words that only we can understand, and savor the loneliness of just one person. As time repeatedly swallows us up in various groups, we disperse and gather, parting ways, and only then do we realize that we have always been alone. The only thing that stands by us forever is the dark shadow.

It's late spring again. Peach blossoms have faded, and it must be the time for cherry blossoms to fall as well. "A sky filled with falling petals" - this must be the season for that. Today, my younger sister told me she saw cherry blossoms, and they were very beautiful. It's said that the tree behind our fifth teaching building is a cherry blossom tree. From afar, it seems similar to peach blossoms, not much different, except it's taller. Still pale pink flowers, high up, those bony branches like dry hands reaching out to the sky, praying for something.

Cherry blossoms are Japan's national flower. Personally, I think the song about cherry blossoms from their country is really beautiful. However, since I don't know its name, I haven't listened to it many times; otherwise, I would have listened hundreds of times. One should not easily miss out on good things.

We always encounter people who pass through the center of our lives like meteors. They leave quickly, leaving behind only a moment of beauty. As time goes by, this memory gradually fades. When enough time has passed, it disappears entirely. We call these people passers-by.

"Your heart is like a small, lonely city," tightly shut. Thus, we cannot enter each other's world because your world is already closed. Others cannot enter, and you cannot escape. Soon, you become isolated, whether passively or actively. Strangers brush past each other hurriedly, leaving no more than a bewildered face, three parts numbness, seven parts helplessness. From being strangers, to becoming familiar, then strangers again, after entering each other’s lives, we find ourselves escaping back to our own paths, realizing that we still prefer to walk alone.

You tell me that we are just strangers, not knowing each other, and over time, memories will fade. But do you know why? It's because you never truly considered me a friend. True friendship withstands the test of time and distance, and won't be destroyed by changes in time or geography. On the contrary, appropriate distance makes true friendship even stronger.

Who knows how many strangers we'll meet in life? Brushing past each other, leaving nothing behind. Yet sometimes, the feelings stirred are so real that they feel like stories. What does it matter if someone is a passer-by? Recalling past events, the nose still tingles, and the heart still aches. Though these feelings will fade, it is an extremely long process.

The cherry blossoms flying all over the sky seem to be telling us something. In the sound of hooves, we listen carefully and only hear: "I am not a returnee, but a passer-by."

Strangers continue walking on the path, gathering and dispersing, smiling while leaving, brushing past each other, remembering and passing on those emotions. Perhaps, the love on our shoulders is the strongest.

In the long journey of life, we only hope to encounter the falling of cherry blossoms one by one.

The most beautiful thing in the world isn't the brilliant and wondrous fireworks, but the falling of cherry blossoms after brushing past each other.
