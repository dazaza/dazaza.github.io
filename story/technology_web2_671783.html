**What is Collagen Tripeptide?**

Third-generation collagen is commonly referred to as collagen tripeptide or tripeptide collagen. A typical collagen molecule is made up of 3,000 amino acids (with a molecular weight of 3,000), making it difficult for the body to absorb and utilize, and even less so for direct skin absorption.

Collagen tripeptide involves cutting these 3,000 amino acids into segments that are 1/1000th their original size, forming the smallest molecular structure of collagen — a combination of just three amino acids.

Collagen tripeptide is obtained through globally patented composite enzyme gradient enzymatic hydrolysis technology and polypeptide separation technology, resulting in high-purity small-molecule peptides. After purification by this technology, collagen tripeptide has an even lower molecular weight and higher absorption efficiency.
