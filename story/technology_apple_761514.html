As Apple and Samsung are in fierce battle, smartphone "arms merchants" are the ones who profit.

by geekzhang on 2013-05-03 17:00:34

According to foreign media reports on April 29th Beijing time, as the competition in the smartphone market intensifies, Apple's stock has lost its luster. However, during this turbulent period in the tech sector, companies that provide communication chips for phones have found their own blessings, and their stock performances have been quite remarkable.Not long ago, Apple's newly announced quarterly earnings report still failed to restore investor confidence. People are still waiting for this American tech giant to find a way to compete with Samsung in the global smartphone market. Despite an increase in dividends and a large-scale stock repurchase, it was difficult to reverse the downward trend of the stock price; Apple had already fallen by 23% this year.JMP Securities analyst Alex Gauna said that such a level of dividends is very tempting, but the erosion of fundamentals remains quite terrifying.In an interview with CNBC, Gauna pointed out: The smartphone market is not saturated, but Apple shows signs of decline in the competition with Android products; with more diverse and more affordable products, competitors have made Apple unable to cope. He rated Apple's stock as "Market Perform".UBS analyst Steve Milunovich, on the other hand, appeared more optimistic. Although his future expectations were also turbulent, he still placed Apple stock at the "Buy" rating. Think about it carefully, it’s astonishing that Apple indicated it would repurchase 15% of its shares while the stock price was falling. This tells you how worried those investors are about the arrival of new products and the decline in gross margins.Many analysts and investors have been calling for the launch of a low-priced iPhone, using larger screens and more affordable prices to compete with Samsung in emerging markets like China.Milunovich wrote in a report that it is premature to conclude that Apple's innovation is dead now. And the answer to this question will ultimately determine the trend of the stock.Benefit to arms dealersAlthough the fierce battle between Apple and Samsung has filled the global market with smoke, the penetration rate of smartphones has remained unscathed. Such a situation will also benefit companies that provide chips.Communication chip company Qualcomm expects the shipment of 3G/4G devices this year to rise by 12%, reaching 1.05 billion units, with growth in emerging markets reaching 18%, and 6% in developed markets.This means that Qualcomm and Broadcom, these "arms dealers", can gain substantial benefits from it.Broadcom CEO Scott McGregor pointed out after the earnings call that Broadcom's products are almost present in all upcoming smartphones. We provide connectivity, touch, near-field communication (NFC) components, and many other technologies. He added, we hope to offer one-stop service to our customers.It is worth noting that Broadcom's customers include not only Apple, but also its archrival Samsung. According to the annual report, orders from these two companies accounted for 17% of Broadcom's total sales in 2012. Moreover, it also serves Chinese customers, including Huawei and ZTE.McGregor frankly admitted that for us, China is a huge market with rapid growth. Broadcom has always been an important technology provider in China's infrastructure construction.Analysts are also very bullish on this company's stock. On average, analysts maintain a "Buy" rating, and the median target stock price reaches $40 per share, indicating a 14% growth potential.In a report, analysts from William Blair & Company wrote that we believe Broadcom is a well-positioned large-cap quality stock. This company aligns with the long-term trends in consumer, enterprise, and service technology development, and the stability of the global macroeconomic situation is also a great advantage.Another beneficiary, Qualcomm, however, raises some concerns due to the entry of competitors Nvidia and MediaTek; cheap phones in emerging markets have also reduced Qualcomm's profit margin.However, Morningstar analyst Brian Colello wrote in a report that due to the further increase in global smartphone penetration, Qualcomm's revenue growth remains strong. We believe that Qualcomm's design has won on Samsung's Galaxy S4 smartphone, which will be reflected in the March quarter and bring solid performance to the June quarter.It is reported that Qualcomm's customers include Apple, Samsung, and Nokia.Colello also stated that Qualcomm's full-year performance guidance suggests that Qualcomm will perform quite well in the highly competitive wireless chip field.
