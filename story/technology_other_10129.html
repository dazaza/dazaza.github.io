Writer Zhang Yiyi created for Li Xiang the "Greatest Love Letter of the 21st Century"

by yinyin on 2006-12-06 21:19:13

Zhang Yiyi recently made a public proposal to Li Xiang, citing six reasons for the proposal, which caused quite a stir. On November 30th, Li Xiang formally replied to Zhang Yiyi via text message, stating that she was "already taken" and found Zhang Yiyi "too young," making their marriage inappropriate. This text message did not seem to deter Zhang Yiyi, who is controlled by love. If the "most romantic love letter" fails to move Li Xiang, Zhang Yiyi revealed that he would plan further proposals.

The event of an "80s writer proposing to Li Xiang" has once again sparked waves. After Li Xiang rejected Zhang Yiyi's proposal via text message, Zhang Yiyi told reporters that he is currently working day and night on writing a "love letter that rivals Xu Zhimo's 'To Lu Xiaoman,' Liang Shiqiu's letters to Han Jingqing, Pushkin's 'To Natalia,' and Beethoven's 'To the Immortal Beloved' - the 'Greatest Love Letter of the 21st Century'" for Li Xiang. It will be completed within a day or two, attempting to capture the heart of anchorwoman Xiang. When asked by the reporter what he would do if Li Xiang remains unmoved, Zhang Yiyi firmly told the reporter, "If Li Xiang still does not agree to marry me, I will streak through 31 major cities across the country where she has been, using my naked love to commemorate the already lost 31 years of beauty and youth of Xiang Xiang."
