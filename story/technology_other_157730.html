On the first day of the Fujian College Entrance Examination, more than 5,000 people were absent, and no malicious cheating incidents were found | Fujian Bar

by addintime on 2009-06-09 00:19:48

Yesterday, the first graduates of Fujian Province's popular high school new curriculum reform participated in the "new college entrance examination". More than 300,000 candidates across the province took exams at 12,550 test centers. According to information from the Higher Education Enrollment Office, as of 5 PM yesterday afternoon, order was maintained at all test sites. Apart from a few cases of rule violations and cheating, there were no organized or premeditated large-scale malicious cheating incidents.

According to Mr. Lin Qitian, director of the Provincial Higher Education Enrollment Office, during the Chinese exam held yesterday morning, 5,652 candidates were absent. During the mathematics exam held yesterday afternoon, 5,837 candidates were absent. The number of absentees per session is similar to previous years, all due to personal reasons of the candidates. There were no absences caused by work errors or lack of public conditions (meaning that there were no absences due to disasters or other reasons). The majority of absent candidates were either recommended students or athletes who had been admitted early through special admissions. In addition, very few candidates voluntarily withdrew from the exam.
