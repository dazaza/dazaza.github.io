Rainy day

by epttaodro on 2011-09-15 14:10:10

(Old Feelings)

On a rainy day, let me be honest, it's not a good weather. A rainy day, as the name implies, is a day with rain. Some people like the rain, others don't. Generally speaking, those full of poetic sentiment tend to love the rain because they can then write many beautiful chapters about a rainy day. There are countless exquisite sentences describing the rain, so I would like to recommend a few passages for my friends to admire:

1. Thousands of fine threads sway in the air, forming a misty light veil that covers the fertile fields. The fine rain descends from the sky like silk, and the raindrops are so small, the rain curtain so dense, that it drapes the mountains in a gauzy white veil as delicate as cicada wings. The rain threads are very fine, very soft, just like the floating willow catkins in spring. The rain, like a mist, wraps everything continuously. In an instant, the raindrops connect into lines, and with a splash, the heavy rain pours down from the sky as if the heavens had collapsed.

2. Outside the window, there is a drizzle of fine rain. Each drop seems to accompany a little dance tune, and I cannot help but be captivated by the world outside the window. The beautiful flowers in the rain, dressed as if in crystal garments, become even more enchanting. The raindrops, glittering under the neon lights, resemble luminous pearls, dazzling and radiant, perhaps even more beautiful than gemstones or diamond rings. These little pearls seem to be dancing ballet around me, moving up and down, or perhaps the willows are accompanying the water droplets in their dance.

3. Between heaven and earth, it's as if an incredibly vast curtain of pearls has been hung, creating a vast expanse of haze. The rain falls on the roof opposite, splashing up droplets of water that envelop the roof. The rainwater flows down the eaves, at first like beads falling one by one, then gradually forming a continuous line. The water on the ground increases more and more, converging into streams. The wet road shines like a shimmering river. The umbrellas held by those who must go out look like petals floating on the water's surface; occasionally passing vehicles resemble boats weaving through the waves. 4. After being baptized by the spring rain, the air becomes fresh and moist, the leaves emerald green and dripping, the spring flowers blooming invitingly, and the grass vibrant with life. Playful drizzles begin to fall gently, flying straight into the faces of passersby radiating heat, giving a refreshing sensation. Drizzling, drizzling. The sound is soft and delicate, like the bursting of grain seeds, like the growth of grass leaves, like the pushing of bamboo shoots through the soil... That is obviously the spring rain, does it not symbolize the gentle nature of spring? Fine threads of rain weave a huge curtain of rain, connecting heaven and earth, dyeing the landscape with a hazy charm.

The passages above that I recommended all describe the rain beautifully, right? After reading them, do you start to like the feeling of a rainy day? Actually, whether you like it or not, that's something you can't force. For most of us, we don't really like rainy days. Those who like the rain are few, usually people with poetic sentiment. People like us generally still don't like it. In fact, everyone dislikes the rain. Everyone finds their own real reason.

Some people say, "I hate the rain." Because it's not fun to go out and play when it rains.

Some people say, "I hate the rain." Because it always feels like a child peeing endlessly.

Some people say, "I hate the rain." Because when it rains, they feel increasingly lonely, like they want to cry.

Some people say, "I hate the rain." Because it makes the world feel covered in black gauze, becoming somewhat somber.

Some people say, "I hate the rain." Because they hate carrying an umbrella when it rains.

... ... ... ... ... ... ... ... ... ... In short, people who dislike the rain always have their reasons. Although each reason differs, in my view, you're not really disliking the rain, but rather the loneliness it brings. The rain makes everything inconvenient, leaving you idle for long periods, which leads to feelings of loneliness and irritability towards the rain, thus causing a dislike for rainy days.

Honestly, I don't particularly like rainy days either, for the reasons I've mentioned. But there are exceptions. Sometimes, the rain can be a wonderful thing, creating memories worth preserving. Meeting someone you like on a rainy day, even though it's raining, can still be sweet, making the rain feel romantic and intimate. Even if you can't stroll along country paths or hold hands by the riverside, it doesn't matter. Being with someone you like, doing nothing, can still make you feel happy and content. Just sitting together, listening to the sound of raindrops hitting the ground, creates a melody that sounds so pleasant. At that moment, you feel truly happy. Therefore, the rain isn't all bad; there are moments you cherish deeply. So although I don't like the rain, I don't completely dislike it either. Rain, fall as you may, I'll regard you as a melodious piece of music, accompanying me into sweet dreams.
