Breeding Tools: Centipede housing can be done indoors using three-dimensional boxes larger than 1.2 square meters, or underground pools with wooden or cement structures, walls over 0.5 meters high, smooth plastic film or glass around the inner walls, and at least 10 cm of loose soil (or sand) at the bottom. Place three rows of tiles upside down on top of the soil, then another layer of tiles upside down, repeating this process three or four times. For outdoor breeding, the surrounding walls should be similar to indoor ones but require a canopy to protect against sun, rain, and pests. Feeding tools include large porcelain plates or clean rectangular plastic trays placed in the breeding box, ensuring each centipede has access to food. Small saucers for drinking water should be placed in various locations within the box, with at least five per box. These tools should be cleaned daily and disinfected weekly. Additionally, thermometers, hygrometers, spray bottles, exhaust fans, small brushes, and capture chopsticks are necessary. To maintain good ventilation and fresh air, feces should be cleared promptly, and exhaust fans should be installed above windows or doors.

Feeding Methods: Timely and measured feeding, evenly distributed. Their diet requires attention: less fish and shrimp, more mealworms, no fruits, vegetables, insects, rotten meat, or leftover meats. Primarily choose mealworms as feed, or feed them cooked, mashed fish every 15-20 days (a thin layer spread on the plate). Cooked mudfish is even better. Young centipedes over 40 days old can eat cooked egg yolks, which must be fresh daily. After 50 days, young centipedes can be separated from their mothers, removing the larger ones while keeping the smaller ones for easier breeding.

Breeding Techniques: During the activity period of Chinese red-headed giant centipedes, food should be provided between 5-6 PM daily (about 1.5 kilograms for every 800 centipedes over 30 days). First, clean and dry feeding and drinking utensils without water droplets, place them evenly in the centipede pool so each centipede can access food. Feed yellow mealworms (or fish) evenly, add cool boiled water to the drinking dish, and avoid spraying water indoors before feeding. Food should be fed at fixed times daily, with appropriate amounts to prevent overfeeding and food poisoning. Drinking water should be shallow and flat dishes (approximately 1-1.5 cm deep), refreshed daily with cool boiled water. Maintain indoor hygiene and prevent flies. Keep room temperature at 20-25°C, sprinkle water outside the centipede housing if the temperature is too high, avoiding electric fans but allowing air conditioners if necessary. In winter, when early breeding is required, non-hibernation breeding techniques with added warmth should be used. Observe the centipedes regularly for any abnormal behavior. If they run back and forth continuously, it may be due to attacks from other centipedes, slight injuries to the body or head, which can be treated with red mercury, centipede wine, or alcohol applied all over the body. If bloated and slow-moving, isolate them and feed them mother yeast, tetracycline, and antiflatulent tablets (with limited food supply). If antennae point backward, feed them tetracycline, mother yeast, chloramphenicol, and wipe their bodies with alcohol or centipede wine.

Disease and Pest Control: Black Spot Disease: Black spots of varying sizes found under the body and head of red-headed giant centipedes. This can be treated by feeding 0.6 grams of mother yeast, 0.25 grams of tetracycline, and 0.25 grams of chloramphenicol, ground finely and mixed with feed insects for seven consecutive days. Severe cases may require separation and individual feeding. Alternatively, nystatin, amphotericin B, actinomycin D, and kanamycin emulsifiers can be used. Gastroenteritis: Treat with 0.5 grams of sulfonamide mixed with feed insects, or 0.25 grams of chloramphenicol mixed with 300 grams of feed insects. Molting Disease: Red-headed giant centipedes grow quickly, but overly dry conditions during molting make it difficult, while overly humid conditions lead to mites and fungal parasites. Continuously feed them 0.5 grams of tetracycline, 0.6 grams of mother yeast, and one calcium tablet every ten days, grinding them finely and mixing with 400 feed insects for consecutive feeding.
