Untitled

by a20110325 on 2011-04-01 11:17:07

I was stunned as I watched my beloved C510 fall into the deep, bottomless hole of the women's restroom on the 12th floor of Development Building 12. I wanted to retrieve it, just for one last glance, but how could I? It was simply impossible. I stood dumbly outside the restroom, looking at a girl washing her hands with a pitiful gaze, asking her what to do. Her response was: "This toilet has swallowed many phones." She asked me what kind of phone it was. I remained silent. To her, she only cared about the value of the phone, but to me, losing my beloved phone was nothing but sorrow and despair!

It was time to put away the earbuds I carried in my bag every day and unplug the charger that needed charging almost daily. It was time to let go of this habit that had lasted over two years! But I wasn't used to not having them, I wasn't used to it at all! The phone may not have been very valuable, but it was the most useful and suitable phone for me! It held so many memories; it stored all my numbers from the past two years, carefully selected pictures, classic videos I had filmed, now all gone! The phone’s standout feature was its camera function. I took so many photos with it. Now, opening my computer and looking at the photos in each folder, seeing the bitterness and sweetness it recorded, I felt an inexplicable sadness! It was like losing a good friend! This feeling had already surfaced six months ago when it went to the "hospital." At that time, I told my friends that if it couldn't be used anymore, I would buy an identical one! Unfortunately, this model was not available domestically, only in Hong Kong! Yan reminded me not to buy a phone online, which made sense. So, I decided to choose a Nokia with a good cost-performance ratio.

Friends all say that the old must go for the new to come, but I am someone who easily forms attachments to things. Whether they are people or objects, as long as they've accompanied me, as long as we've understood each other, as long as the time has been long enough, the moment of farewell becomes a period of time, and I need time to heal. I like to anthropomorphize objects: actually, it did give me subtle hints that it was going to leave. A few days ago, I told someone that I seemed to rarely use it to take photos recently. Perhaps it was my fault; I was too busy during that period and neglected it. So, it thought I didn't value it anymore, so it chose to leave. Hmm, leaving might not necessarily be bad. It seems like it should be the full stop of a small phase in my life. The numbers are all gone. Some can be easily retrieved, some may never be regained—well, it's also a way to re-screen my contact list, choosing to keep those who should stay and discard those who should leave! Can I understand it this way: everything has its comings and goings, people are like this, and things are the same? Without it, life couldn't continue normally, so soon another white phone replaced it (of course, I always believed that some things were irreplaceable). Alright, I don't need to be attached anymore; even if I am, it has already said goodbye to me. Goodbye, C510, thank you for accompanying me for over two years and leaving me with so many memories... Your owner's normal life continues.
