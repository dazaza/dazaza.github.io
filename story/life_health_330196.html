7 Unhealthy Habits That Are Actually Good for You

by 60888888 on 2010-02-25 10:25:39

Everything has two sides, even some bad habits widely recognized in daily life have a positive effect on physical health. Below are several "bad habits" we've listed for you.

1. Reasonable anger is beneficial to blood pressure

That's right, being angry can raise one's blood pressure. However, research from Carnegie Mellon University in the U.S. found that responding appropriately with anger under repressed conditions not only maintains normal blood pressure levels but also reduces the secretion of cortisol, the hormone responsible for stress.

Psychological studies show that anger gives people a more positive mindset and a sense of control; holding back when it's necessary to act, being timid and suppressing inner emotions, instead causes a sharp rise in stress hormones. In the long run, heart disease will "target you" (note: patients with high blood pressure should avoid getting angry).

2. Video games help you exercise

Who says playing video games makes you fat? Scientists at the University of Miami in the U.S. discovered that when people play video games, their heart rate increases and they breathe faster, causing the body to consume more energy. Dr. Aletta Perry believes that if you cannot participate in real physical exercise, playing games can help you lose weight, at least better than sitting idly on the couch eating chips while watching TV, provided that the gaming time is not too long.

3. Swearing relieves pain

Swearing is a bad habit frowned upon by others, but it helps relieve pain. Psychologist Dr. Richard Stephens believes that swearing is related to the regulatory function of adrenaline, which enhances aggressive tendencies.

Studies show that the more someone wants to infringe on others, the less sensitive they are to pain. Our ancestors, when undergoing surgery without anesthesia, would bite down on a piece of wood, but they could just as well have cursed loudly to reduce the pain.

4. Being lazy occasionally helps you live longer

Public health expert Peter Yiakoust said that those who wake up early every day and rush around may prematurely "end up in the grave." Taking small breaks occasionally not only reduces work pressure but is also key to longevity. Studies show that taking a brief nap at noon is more beneficial for longevity than playing tennis. Elderly people who always run for exercise might deplete the energy meant for cell regeneration or disease resistance. Psychological experts say that even lying at home daydreaming allows the brain to process important information, making your thinking more active.

5. Short-term stress enhances memory

Long-term life stress, such as divorce, can destroy the immune system, making one prone to infections. But research from the University at Buffalo found that short-term urgent events can improve the brain's learning ability and memory.

This is because stress hormones affect certain areas of the brain responsible for emotion and learning capabilities. Increased stress accelerates the transmission speed of glutamate, the substance responsible for transmitting information, thereby enhancing memory.

6. Avoiding housework prevents allergies

Research suggests that outbreaks of allergic diseases and autoimmune diseases are due to modern society being too clean. But the culprit isn't just dust. Investigations by the universities of Bristol and Brunel showed that pregnant women or new mothers frequently using cleaning equipment increase the risk of children developing asthma before the age of seven by 41%. This is because some indoor cleaning agents release chemicals that severely damage children's respiratory tracts.

7. Loud music stimulates brainpower

Attending rock concerts and turning up the volume of music both help stimulate the brain's vitality. Research from the University of Manchester in the UK found that the saccule in the human inner ear is only sensitive to sounds above 90 decibels. The saccule is connected to the area of the brain responsible for processing sex, happiness, and hunger. If these desires are satisfied through high-decibel music stimulation, we feel very calm and happy inside. When you're unhappy, high-decibel music can stimulate your "happiness hormone."
