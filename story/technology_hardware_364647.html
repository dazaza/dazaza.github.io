SONY showcases flexible electronic paper

by geekzhang on 2010-09-16 13:48:18

According to Sony Insider, SONY recently held a dealer convention in Tokyo, Japan, where it showcased its latest products and some prototype devices. In addition to the current popular 3D applications, a flexible electronic paper display unveiled at the same time is also quite concerned, and it is said that it may be installed in the future SONY reader.

At present, mature electronic paper is basically the use of glass as a substrate, but SONY shows this display prototype to plastic substrate, compared with the former has the advantage of lighter weight, the more significant feature is that it supports bending, and even can be rolled up like real paper, and it is not as fragile as the glass substrate electronic paper, the compression resistance and fall performance can be improved.

More parameters of this flexible electronic paper prototype have not been announced, from the scene pictures, it is still a monochrome gray screen, of course, and the current popular E Ink electronic ink screen similar, also has a paper-like display effect. Sony has not said when it will be used in its reader products, but according to Sony Insider, it may be commercially available in one to two years.
