Who owes who happiness?

by mimi456 on 2011-12-22 16:12:44

Many years ago, Zhou Xun said: I will not marry unless it is Da Qi.

Liu Ye also said: I will not marry unless it is Xie Na.

Yao Chen once said: The person most suitable for me is Ling Xiaosu.

Not long after, Zhou Xun was single again.

Liu Ye also married someone else.

Yao Chen also got divorced.

And the myth of Liang Luoshi and Li Zekai also shattered.

Dou Wei and Wang Fei got divorced.

The age-crossing love between Nicholas Tse and Wang Fei came to an end.

Tse married Cecilia Cheung.

Wang Fei also married Li Yapeng.

After the sex photo scandal broke out, Tse defended Cheung's image, but now their marriage has also hit a red light.

I want to ask: In this world, where are those people who once said they would not marry unless it was you?

Back in the day when Anita Mui and Andy Lau were together, she once said: "If after many years you haven't married and I haven't married, then we will get married." But now one is in heaven and the other on earth. Some vows are destined to be unfulfilled, at least in this life they cannot be fulfilled. Life is full of such regrets.

When Leslie Cheung jumped from the building, it was rumored that he committed suicide for love. The brilliant star eventually fell. What about the promises of love and vows? They all ended up being washed away by the tide.

In this life, whose happiness does anyone owe? If there had been no promises made in the first place, then there would be no question of owing or not owing. If there had been no liking in the first place, then there would be no question of loving or not loving. If there had been no real love in the first place, then how can there be any talk of owing or not owing?

Every day in this world, people are playing the game of love, whether they love or don't love, and many people are lost in it. Ask the world what love is, that makes people pledge their lives to each other? Did Zhuo Wenjun, who eloped with Sima Xiangru at night, ever receive true love? Maybe she did, maybe she didn't? Was Hongfu, who went to knock on Li Jing's inn door in the middle of the night, ultimately happy?

How many couples who once vowed to endure hardships together have eventually forgotten each other in the world? Many people want to "hold your hand and grow old with you," but when it comes down to it, either they retreat or find excuses to run away. Happiness sometimes seems like flowers in water or the moon in a mirror, visible but untouchable.

Eternal love is sometimes just something casually said, solemn vows ultimately turn into empty checks. There are too many exchanges and transactions, too much use and being used in the pursuit of fame and fortune. So-called happiness is wrapped in too much materialism, eroded by too much self-interest. Who can still give whom happiness?

In college, someone who loves you might give you a rose, in society, someone who loves you might give you a villa or a car. Every time there is love or being loved, material things are used as exchange. Without material things, love seems hollow, and marriage is even more based on material foundations. Without money, you owe the promise, and you owe the other person's happiness.

There is a person, maybe he once silently loved you, but because he couldn't provide for your material needs, he chose to leave. After several years, maybe you will meet again, you want to know if he is doing well, but he won't tell you anymore. Because he once loved you, he doesn't want you to know whether he is doing well or not. It's not that he doesn't want you to know, but because he knows that even if you know, it doesn't mean anything, because leaving at that time was to make things right. If anyone owes anyone's happiness, you are destined to owe his happiness.

Everyone knows the fable of the monkey carrying corn, everyone also knows the story of Buddha asking the unenlightened commoner to find the largest wheat ear in the field. We always think that the best will come later, gradually forgetting to cherish what we have now. When the person who has always stayed by your side quietly leaves, you suddenly realize that the person you have always loved is the one who has stayed by your side. At this point, if you say he owes you happiness, then who owes him happiness?

We can come and go from this world alone, but we cannot hold hands or let go casually. Once you hold hands, do not let go. This is the law of all love. If you want to give the person you love happiness, then never say the two words "break up." If you say them, you are destined to owe them happiness.

Many people only regret when they lose something. Unfortunately, this world does not sell regret pills, and the only "Moonlight Box" has been sealed for a long time, wanting to get it but unable to find it. Some people, some things, like a cloud floating away. Only when they disappear without a trace do we realize that we didn't cherish them before.

You can forget each other in the world, but you cannot hide from each other in the mountains. Too many principles, too much self-interest, too much argument over merits and demerits, have led many lovers to part ways. Not for any other reason, just because their interests are different, their paths are different, different ways lead to different alliances. Those past loves gradually fade away, and finally end up like withered lotus flowers, stinking and unpleasant.

After loving, hating, hurting, and being hurt, it seems that many people understand that happiness is something that can be encountered but not sought after. Even if the person you love is standing in front of you, you may not have the courage to take his hand, let alone bring him home. The human heart is a complex thing, how can love be? After being hurt, all love turns into hatred, and in the end, it either leads to mutual destruction or self-destruction. Ask: At this point, who owes who's happiness?

In this world, who owes who happiness? In a realistic society filled with principles and rules, love needs to be cultivated within certain principles, just like birds in a cage. If they fly out recklessly, they will surely die. Love that does not follow principles will be judged by secular eyes because it does not conform to the rules.

I have my own complexion, and I don't care about your mood. This is a proud and arrogant era, many people stick multiple masks on their faces, pulling off one layer reveals another. The real face will never be exposed. Saying you will give someone happiness, but there is no action; saying you love someone like a flower, but hating them like grass. Give someone happiness, and they will wait for it. Pity those passionate people, who ultimately end up with lifelong regrets.

Who owes who happiness?

Too many masks are worn by benefactors

Too much kindness is deceived

Too much unreality blinds the eyes

You say you will give me happiness,

But where are you?

I say I will give you happiness,

But how can I fulfill it?

When in love, you are my treasure,

When out of love, you become garbage

Is it because I am too stubborn, or are you too heartless?

We missed the season of enduring hardships together, missed the era of forgetting each other in the world, missed the vow of holding hands and growing old together. Are we going to keep making mistakes until the sea turns into mulberry fields?

I want to say: If you really love, please don't miss me who is by your side.

Before you marry,

Before I marry,

Let's be together, so you don't owe me happiness, and I don't owe you promises.

Forever, never part!
