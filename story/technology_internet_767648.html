Twitter to Shut Down #Music App, Reorganize Music Business

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 11:51:34

Twitter #Music Failure

Beijing time, early morning of October 20th, according to the U.S. tech blog AllThingsD citing sources, Twitter is considering abandoning the Twitter #Music app, which was launched only six months ago.

It's unclear when Twitter will stop supporting this app as the company is currently reorganizing its music business. However, sources say that the future fate of the app has been almost determined.

When it was released in April, Twitter #Music ranked very high on Apple's App Store, reaching number 6 among free apps. However, over the following months, the ranking of the app quickly dropped. Sources said that after the initial popularity, the app performed poorly in terms of downloads and user engagement on the App Store.

Data from app analytics company Onavo showed that by the end of August, Twitter #Music ranked 1672nd among all apps. As of October 19th, another app analytics service, AppAnnie, ranked the app at 264th.

The failure of Twitter #Music reflects a strategic misstep by Twitter in its music business. Over the past year, there hasn't been a clear direction for the development of this business. Within Twitter, the development of this app was relatively independent. The person in charge of this project was Twitter's former head of business development, Kevin Thau, and the development team was the team behind "We Are Hunted," which was acquired by Twitter. Sources said that Twitter #Music was never integrated into the overall product development of Twitter.

Not long after the launch of Twitter #Music, Thau left Twitter to become the Chief Operating Officer of startup Jelly, a new company founded by Twitter co-founder Biz Stone. This meant that the still immature Twitter #Music faced significant uncertainty.

While abandoning Twitter #Music, Twitter plans to further strengthen its cross-platform strategy and collaborate with other companies and brands on music-related businesses. Earlier this year, Twitter partnered with Apple to develop trending playlists for the iTunes Radio streaming music service. Twitter also launched its own playlist within another streaming music service, Rdio.

Currently, Twitter is restructuring its music team. After Ttiana Simonian, who was responsible for artist relations, left in August to join Nielsen, the company hired Bob Moczydlowsky, former Senior Vice President of Topspin Media. Moczydlowsky is seen as a product master in the music industry, rather than someone focused on relationships with record labels and artists, which may suggest his role at Twitter. Twitter declined to comment on this news. (Li Li)
