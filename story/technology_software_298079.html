Las Vegas in the United States, Rome in Italy, and Barcelona in Spain are the favorite destinations of Chinese tourists traveling abroad. However, for Chinese tourists with little experience traveling overseas and who are accustomed to carrying large sums of cash, pickpockets and scammers in these three places are hard to guard against. For this reason, ABC News specially dispatched reporters to secretly film the tricks of thieves and scammers in these three locations, which should be helpful to tourists visiting these places.

It can take just a minute to turn your trip into a nightmare. While you're captivated by the city's charming scenery, you won't notice that you've already become someone else's prey —— the greedy eyes of pickpockets are fixed on you. Every year, tens of thousands of people fall victim to pickpockets and scammers, leading to an irreversible situation where they "arrive in high spirits but return in disappointment."

To ensure that tourists can enjoy themselves without being stolen from, reporters from ABC News' 20/20 column carried secret cameras while touring famous tourist cities like Barcelona, Rome, and Las Vegas. The purpose was to secretly film the theft process of pickpockets, thereby raising tourists' awareness and informing them of the common tricks used by thieves.

**Barcelona: The Unwinnable "Shell Game"**

According to the reporter's continuous observations, at the tourist hotspot La Rambla in Barcelona, street scammers are most adept at the "Shell Game." Tourists, either unable to resist the scammer's sweet talk or driven by their own curiosity, will inevitably fall for the scammer's trap, betting $60 or more to guess which shell hides the ball. But no matter how confident they are in their judgment or how much money they bet, the result is always the same: whatever amount they put in, they lose it all.

Floyd personally experienced such a game to uncover the truth. She was quite confident in her judgment, sure that the ball was under the shell she guessed. But repeated failures proved her wrong; she never guessed correctly once. Expert Yarno, who handles crimes against tourists, said, "No one wins in this 'Shell Game.'" To prove his point, he demonstrated the trickery involved!

**Rome: Crowded Places Are Not "Safe"**

In Rome, the reporter came to another conclusion: the more crowded a place is, the less safe it is. In Vatican City, a very crowded bus is often the best spot for thieves to operate. In such situations, remember: don't keep your bag where you can't see it, such as on your side or back. Because pickpockets may use a large sweatshirt as cover, opening your backpack and stealing your wallet when you're not paying attention. This happens in an instant, and you won't even notice.

In Rome's crowded subway, pickpockets usually work in pairs. The crowded crowd and swaying subway car are exactly what these pickpockets dream of. A suspicious person walked behind the 20/20 reporter, quietly pulling the wallet out of the reporter's back pocket, with a sly smile on his face, unaware that his actions had been captured on camera. Just to film the theft process, the reporters sacrificed at least one wallet per day. Yarno said, "Tourists should remain vigilant at all times, keeping their bags in front of them and always putting their wallets in the front pocket. Everyone traveling should heed this warning."

**Las Vegas: Pickpockets Target Drunken People**

When the reporter arrived in Las Vegas, they found that stealing money there was as easy as spinning the roulette wheel. Tim, head of the Tourism Safety Department at the Las Vegas Police Department, said, "Although surveillance cameras are installed in all major entertainment venues, they haven't been effective."

Most pickpockets prefer to target those who are drunk because they have impaired consciousness, making it easier to succeed in their crimes.

The 20/20 reporter entered a bar and happened to see a man become the target of a seductive female pickpocket. Here's what happened: the man was drinking alone and seemed slightly tipsy. At this moment, a voluptuous woman sat next to him and placed her hand on the man's shoulder. While giving him a flirtatious look, she leaned her nearly naked body toward him. As the man was distracted by her provocative moves, the woman stealthily extended her "claws" toward the man's pocket. Unfortunately for her, the man suddenly noticed her intent and quickly retrieved his wallet. The woman immediately turned pale and fled in haste.

When you're fully focused on an ATM or a vending machine, your wallet is openly inviting pickpockets.

An expert familiar with thieves' tactics advised gambling tourists to always place their valuables on their laps. Some find it troublesome and place their valuables near their feet instead. What happens then? They become "prey" in the language of "crawlers."
