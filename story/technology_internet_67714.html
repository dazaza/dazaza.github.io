Experts pointed out that: SPF technology should become an important means for enterprise mailrooms to fight against spam.

by now on 2008-05-09 14:44:03

May 3rd marks the 30th anniversary of the world's first spam email. Over these 30 years, global spam has become increasingly rampant, significantly impacting "official" online communication, especially for businesses using corporate email systems for office work, management, and business operations. Experts are calling on internet infrastructure service providers to take the initiative in promoting the application of anti-spam technologies, particularly the promotion of SPF technology.

According to a responsible person from Times Internet, corporate email systems generally use Simple Mail Transfer Protocol (SMTP), whose biggest weakness is that email recipients cannot determine if the sender is impersonating someone else. This inherent shortcoming has caused significant headaches for many internet service providers.

With the emergence of SPF technology, spam emails can no longer hide. SPF stands for Sender Policy Framework, a technology that authenticates the identity of email senders using IP (Internet Protocol) addresses. It is an extremely effective solution for spam emails, specifically addressing the issue of sender impersonation in spam emails. When a user defines their domain's SPF record, the receiving end will verify whether the connecting IP address is included in the SPF record. If it is, the email is considered legitimate; otherwise, it is deemed forged. Thus, spam emails with forged domains can no longer hide under the keen eye of SPF, allowing corporate email systems to effectively avoid such spam emails.

According to reports, major internet infrastructure service providers are also troubled by spam emails. Customers often receive phishing emails pretending to be notifications from these service providers regarding renewal fees or payments, causing many customers significant losses. Now, as long as the service provider discloses their IP addresses to their customers, the customer’s corporate email system will authenticate the identity of all incoming emails. Fraudulent emails will be automatically intercepted before they even enter the inbox. It is reported that some domestic internet infrastructure service providers have begun actively applying SPF anti-spam technology, with Times Internet (www.now.cn) being one of the earliest companies to adopt this technology. A responsible person from the company stated that SPF is a technology related to DNS, with its content written in DNS txt records. To facilitate faster SPF record parsing for users, Times Internet has upgraded its VDNS Domain Management System Global Edition to version 7.9.

Experts indicate that for corporate email customers, applying SPF technology will also effectively assist them in improving work efficiency. Not only can it significantly reduce the number of spam emails, lighten the workload, and prevent financial fraud, but it also allows businesses to disclose their IP addresses to all their clients, protecting trade secrets and preventing financial fraud.

Industry professionals point out that since SPF's verification mechanism requires the sending end to set up SPF records for reverse checks, this means the more people use SPF technology, the more its effectiveness is demonstrated. International large-scale internet service providers like Microsoft and AOL are vigorously promoting the application of SPF anti-spam technology. Domestic internet infrastructure service providers should also actively engage, becoming important drivers in the promotion and application of this technology!
