Moving Feixin, stylishly "on the way"

by zhuzhongyi on 2008-01-20 23:19:57

The remarkable year 2007 has become history. In that year, a dynamic instant messaging service left a deep impression on people: Feixin (Fly message). As a new instant messaging tool, the mobile Feixin service launched by Beijing Mobile on June 5th saw its user base soar to over 20 million in just six months, quickly becoming a rising star among China Mobile's many services and earning widespread acclaim. It can be said that achieving such success was due not only to its inherent quality but also to Beijing Mobile's relentless marketing efforts over the past few months. Below, let us take a look at how Beijing Mobile's Feixin service stayed "on the road" in 2007.

From an overall perspective, Beijing Mobile's market promotion of the Feixin service went through three main stages: the "Communication Never Offline, Excitement Never Interrupted" Feixin Campus Tour from May 1st to June 30th; the "Chat All Summer" event from July 1st to September 31st; and the "End-of-Year Gifts" campaign from October 1st to December 31st.

Starting with the Feixin Campus Tour, Beijing Mobile mainly conducted offline promotions such as roadshows and door-to-door canvassing at major universities in Beijing, supplemented by online internet and wireless promotions, along with a comprehensive and systematic points reward plan (applicable to both mobile and non-mobile clients). This covered 50 key universities in Beijing and numerous well-known websites like mop.com, kaixin001.com, UUME, and zebra.cn, reaching a total of 1 million college students. By fully utilizing the trendsetting nature of young people, this effort enhanced the overall activity level of Feixin and established a positive image, laying a solid foundation for the upcoming events and raising Feixin's profile.

With Feixin's reputation established, the focus shifted to increasing users' utilization of Feixin services and enhancing peer-to-peer autonomous dissemination, which became key to maintaining continuous growth in the Feixin user base. To address this, Beijing Mobile launched the "Chat All Summer" campaign spanning an entire season. Through internet and network platform resources, combined with campus and internet cafe channels, multi-channel integrated promotions were carried out. Whether it was online ad placements or face-to-face teaching experiences in campuses and internet cafes, these activities effectively stimulated user activity levels, significantly boosting the frequency, duration, and speed of Feixin usage within the user base.

To ensure sustained growth in Feixin's user base, building sufficient user loyalty to the Feixin service was essential. The "End-of-Year Gifts" campaign conducted from October 1st to December 31st used a "refer friends for points" format to guide and promote the use of the PC client, helping users establish extensive friend networks while complementing a complete points reward plan. This extended the time users spent on the service, ultimately fostering user loyalty to the Feixin service. Coupled with the Feixin GPRS discount package plan starting on September 10th, this further accelerated the growth rate of registered Feixin users, enabling the scale of mobile Feixin users to achieve an impressive "three-stage jump" within half a year.

In addition to the three main activities outlined above that spanned Feixin's market promotion, Beijing Mobile seized opportunities to tie Feixin to many popular entertainment elements through bundled campaigns. For example, "Say 'Secrets That Cannot Be Said' on Feixin" turned Jay Chou's movie into a platform for Feixin ambassadors to connect with users; the "Jay Chou Concert Ticket Grab" and "Ticket Grab for 'Fate Calls'" events leveraged the popularity of the King of Mandopop and end-of-year blockbuster films to expand Feixin's influence; and the "Flying My Belief Connects Wonders" Feixin game incorporated leisure and entertainment, greatly expanding Feixin's connotations, making communication richer and more emotional, and showcasing Feixin's diversified and comprehensive development prospects.

These interwoven promotional activities linked Beijing Mobile's market promotion of the Feixin service into a powerful whole, guiding users' attitudes toward Feixin through a progressive transformation from awareness to application and finally to loyalty, playing an indispensable role in Feixin's rapid rise.

Users are the foundation of instant messaging. Like QQ, MSN, and Yahoo Messenger, developing users is the critical task in the early stages. The more users there are, the faster and better the scale effect can push Feixin onto the fast track of development. As an important component of China Mobile, Beijing Mobile is well aware of Feixin's strategic value. Through the annual activity review presented above, we can say that Beijing Mobile's proactive and effective market promotion has achieved gratifying results in developing users and establishing product images. We can also observe that as an instant messaging tool, Feixin has actually broken through the original IM concept, tightening the connections between PC-to-mobile, mobile-to-mobile instant messaging. The unification of phone numbers and Feixin accounts is gradually becoming the direction for future mobile instant communication development. Therefore, we have every reason to believe that as we step into the brand-new year 2008, more and more people will transform from mobile users to Feixin users, becoming a solid support for China Mobile in the fierce competition of the 3G era. Meanwhile, the deepening construction and diversified development of the mobile Feixin client platform will create a fashionable, practical, and convenient mobile communication tool integrating voice, data, and images, making users' lives in the 3G era even more colorful.
