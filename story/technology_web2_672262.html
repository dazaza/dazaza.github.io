"19-year-old"

by lpoihjkmkk on 2012-02-22 16:43:13

When Zhou Changjiang first entered this new family, Zhou Jinmin's son did not understand at the beginning, but was eventually moved by Xiao Changjiang calling him "brother, brother", and quickly accepted this younger brother.

Adopting a girl and then a boy, in many people's eyes, 61-year-old Zhou Jinmin can absolutely be considered a living Lei Feng. Now, her adopted daughter has already married, and her adopted son has grown up. She could have enjoyed some happiness, but instead she spends her days in tears: her adopted son Zhou Changjiang was hospitalized due to a serious illness, and if not treated in time, his life would be in danger at any moment.

In July, Zhou Jinmin took Zhou Changjiang to Ziyun County People's Hospital, where the hospital suggested transferring to a better-equipped hospital in Guiyang. On July 13, after sending Zhou Changjiang to Guizhou Medical University (GYMU), and after a full-body examination, the diagnosis results were as follows: left tibial anterior skin soft tissue infection with necrosis, adrenal tumor, secondary hypertension, and other metabolic diseases.

Due to lack of money for treatment, on July 16, Zhou Jinmin had no choice but to take Zhou Changjiang back to Ziyun, where the County People's Hospital issued a "Critical Condition Notice." Despite this, Zhou Jinmin refused to give up, selling all her valuable items like rings and earrings, and through various means, bought herbal medicine to treat Zhou Changjiang.

At that time, Zhou Jinmin, who had to support her son and daughter as well as take care of her elderly mother, had a heavy family burden, but she still agreed to adopt this 3-year-old boy and renamed him Zhou Changjiang.

Now, Zhou Jinmin's entire family is extremely worried about Zhou Changjiang's medical expenses. Her biological son takes out his monthly salary to help treat his younger brother, and the whole family has already accumulated debts of 70,000-80,000 yuan, but Zhou Jinmin remains determined to cure her adopted son Zhou Changjiang. "No matter what, I want to cure him."

Zhou Jinmin's deeds moved and evoked sympathy from the people in the ward; they recharged her phone, bought her food, and one cleaning aunt even specifically bought eggs for her. Zhou Jinmin said that a doctor surnamed Chen voluntarily paid 2000 yuan for her family’s hospitalization costs.

However, Zhou Changjiang has always been in poor health, easily falling ill when he was very young, often having high fevers, and frequently complaining of "back pain." Later, she found out that when Zhou Changjiang was very young and lived in the village, he had broken his lumbar vertebrae.

Zhou Jinmin lives in Songshan Town, Ziyun Miao and Buyi Autonomous County, and is a retired后勤employee from Ziyun No. 2 Middle School.

"If I hadn't adopted him, in that village, he definitely wouldn't have survived."

After adopting her daughter and registering her household registration, in 1986, Zhou Jinmin was transferred to Ziyun No. 2 Middle School to work in logistics. Her kindness often involved giving students meals and buying them notebooks, and soon her reputation as a "kind person" and "living Lei Feng" spread.

On October 1, 1995, after returning from a distant family gathering, Zhou Jinmin saw a dirty child lying on her bed, "covered in lice." Accompanying the child was a couple, from whom Zhou Jinmin learned that the child was 3 years old, from Landa Group, Qingfeng Village, Songshan Town. His father was an orphan who fell from a tree and died a few months ago, and his mentally unstable mother had disappeared without a trace. As a result, the 3-year-old child was left unattended and had to be entrusted to the kind-hearted Zhou Jinmin by the villagers.

In 1984, while passing through Baishiyan with a colleague, Zhou Jinmin heard a baby crying. Following the cries, she found a female infant covered in ants and mud in the grass. Unable to bear it, she immediately took the child back to the county hospital for disinfection and treatment, and decided to adopt the baby.

Yesterday, on the 17th floor, bed 5 of the surgical building at GYMU, the reporter saw Zhou Jinmin and her adopted son Zhou Changjiang. Due to the non-healing wound on his left leg, it was very difficult for Zhou Changjiang to get out of bed and walk, and relatives of fellow patients sympathetically said, "A 19-year-old walking like a 70-year-old."

On June 19, 2011, the already 19-year-old Zhou Changjiang was playing with his sister's 5-year-old child when he accidentally fell down the stairs. Zhou Jinmin recalled that at the time, the injured left leg had "fat flowing out." This fall resulted in 28 stitches at the Songshan Town Health Center in Ziyun County. The reason for the fat flowing out was explained by the hospital as being "caused by endocrine issues."

Hope for kind-hearted people to help

On October 6, unwilling to give up, Zhou Jinmin sent Zhou Changjiang back to GYMU for hospitalization. Her over 90-year-old mother, worried about her grandson, also came along. After an MRI examination, the hospital believed that Zhou Changjiang's tumor was benign, and surgery still held hope.

Bad news: Adopted son suffers from serious illness. Zhou Jinmin, eating sweet potatoes, will still save her adopted son.

At Zhou Changjiang's bedside, the reporter saw a basin of unfinished boiled sweet potatoes. Fellow patients said that was the food the mother ate daily to save money for her child's treatment.

"When I could carry 30 jin (about 15 kg) of rice without any problem, he found it particularly hard." This year during the Spring Festival, Zhou Jinmin noticed that Zhou Changjiang was "getting more and more abnormal," so she took him to Ziyun County People's Hospital, where he was diagnosed with high blood pressure.
