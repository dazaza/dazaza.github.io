Excerpted from "Liu Jixing Reads History" Lin Biao was appointed commander of the First Brigade of the main force of the Fourth Red Army at the age of 22, along with Peng Dehuai, Wu Zhonghao, and Huang Gonglue, they were called the "Four Brave Generals" under Mao Zedong's command, deeply trusted by Mao Zedong. How did the young Lin Biao stand out? The earliest discoverer of Lin Biao's military talent was Zhu De. Lin Biao was born in Hubei Huanggang in 1907. In June 1923, he joined the Chinese Socialist Youth League. In 1925, he entered the fourth phase of the Whampoa Military Academy and joined the Chinese * Party the same year. After graduating in October 1926, he served as a platoon leader in Ye Ting's independent regiment of the National Revolutionary Army and participated in the Northern Expedition. In August 1927, he participated in the Nanchang Uprising. After the failure of the uprising army in the Chaoshan region of Guangdong, Lin Biao followed Zhu De and Chen Yi to fight in the borders of Fujian, Guangdong, Jiangxi, and Hunan. In January 1928, he participated in the Xiangnan Uprising led by Zhu De, serving as a little-known company commander. On February 29, 1928, Lin Biao led a company guarding logistics supplies from Yongxing to Leiyang. When they arrived at Xiaoshui Pu in the southeast of Leiyang at night, it was pitch dark. The drizzle kept falling, the mountain road was rugged, dark, and slippery. Suddenly, gunshots erupted, hundreds of militia members attacked from the dark, cutting the logistics forces into several segments, and many people fell to the ground due to gunfire. Lin Biao ordered the troops to contract and fight to the death, barely managing to repel the enemy. Counting the numbers, there were over 30 casualties, and all the military supplies they were transporting were looted. With empty hands, Lin Biao's unit dejectedly arrived in Leiyang City, where Zhu De was extremely angry, questioning Lin Biao: "Where are the supplies you escorted? Where is the unit you led? Where are the skills you learned at Whampoa Military Academy?" Lin Biao, who was not good at speaking, was even more ashamed after losing the battle, lowering his head and remaining silent. After a while, Lin Biao clenched his fists and vowed: "I have found out that the attackers were the militia of Leiyang County led by Tan Zisheng, I will make him pay blood for blood." Then he detailed his plan. After listening, Zhu De's eyes lit up, reevaluating this non-surrendering young man, approving his plan. On the morning of the third day, a White Army unit under the banner of the "19th National Revolutionary Army" marched towards Xiaoshui Pu, with the leading Nationalist officer being Lin Biao in disguise. The stationed Tan Zisheng had already heard that the 19th Army would come to Leiyang to "suppress" the bandits, immediately leading his team to welcome them. He proudly reported how he had exterminated the logistics forces of the uprising army. Lin Biao closed his eyes and listened to the report, greatly praising it, and wanted to hold a celebration banquet, rewarding the battalion commander in advance. At 3 PM, the celebration banquet was held in the temple, with dozens of tables inside and outside the temple. Tan Zisheng and his subordinates entered the hall in sequence and took their seats. After three rounds of wine, Tan Zisheng respectfully asked the battalion commander to give a speech. Lin Biao walked to the center of the hall without showing any emotion, threw the wine glass in his hand, and the more than ten "officers" sitting in the hall pulled out their pistols and fired simultaneously, turning Tan Zisheng and his subordinates into bloody sieves. After this battle, Lin Biao not only retrieved all the looted supplies but also captured hundreds of militiamen. Zhu De highly praised Lin Biao's performance in this battle. Several events that followed made Zhu De look at Lin Biao with new eyes. On March 9, Li Yixuan led a Nationalist army division to drive the uprising army out of Leiyang City. In the evening, Zhu De's chief of staff Wang Erzuo commanded the main force to launch a counterattack from the west gate, encountering resistance below the strong city walls. After more than an hour of fierce battle, there was no progress. Li Yixuan, hearing from the dense gunfire that the revolutionary army only had a few light machine guns, decisively ordered the west gate to be opened and initiated a counterattack against the revolutionary army. For a moment, the momentum was unstoppable. Seeing the situation was not favorable, Wang Erzuo ordered the uprising army to retreat from the position. That night, the military headquarters discussed countermeasures in Zaoshi Street, and everyone advocated avoiding the enemy's sharp edge, except Zhu De who did not comment. A knock on the door was heard outside, Lin Biao came to request combat, saying he was willing to sign a military order, needing only one company to defeat Li's division and reclaim Leiyang. Lin Biao explained his idea, and everyone thought it made sense. Thus, Zhu De personally commanded Lin Biao's seventh company and two platoons of the sixth company to set off from the western Ma Bu Ling, sneaking to the west gate, suddenly initiating an attack, breaking into the city. After Li Yixuan's victory, he suddenly encountered a pincer attack, getting hit so hard that he lost direction and had no heart for further fighting, leading his remaining forces to escape. Without catching his breath, Lin Biao found Zhu De again, proposing that our army should press on while the iron is hot, sweeping through like a storm, completely annihilating the enemy forces within Leiyang. Zhu De once again adopted Lin Biao's suggestion, ordering Wang Erzuo to lead three companies, dividing into three routes, directly attacking the New Market Street in Leiyang City where the standing team of Leiyang County was located and the headquarters of the Leiyang Militia General Bureau in Daboshi. After a day of fighting, the revolutionary army cleared all the remaining forces of Li Yixuan in the city. Through these three battles, Zhu De discovered that beneath Lin Biao's quiet appearance lay extraordinary talent: this person was exceptionally intelligent, fearless in crisis, calm in chaos, composed and steady, not an ordinary person. More importantly, Lin Biao was alert and perceptive, good at thinking about problems, often having unexpected actions. If given time, he would grow into an eagle that roars and soars into the sky. On March 12, 1928, Zhu De convened a meeting of officers above the rank of company commander and cadres above the rank of committee member of the Leiyang County Committee in Wujia Ancestral Hall, promoting Lin Biao to battalion commander of the Second Battalion. This was a crucial step in Lin Biao's life. In mid-April 1928, Zhu De and Chen Yi led the remaining part of the Nanchang Uprising forces and the peasant armed forces of the Xiangnan Uprising, after fighting their way through, reached Jinggang Mountain, meeting with the Workers' and Peasants' Revolutionary Army led by Mao Zedong. The forces were reorganized into the Fourth Chinese Workers' and Peasants' Revolutionary Army, later renamed the Fourth Red Army, with Zhu De as the commander, Mao Zedong as the party representative, and Chen Yi as the director of the political department. At this time, Lin Biao, a camp commander just over 20 years old, lacked seniority and reputation, and due to his introverted nature, was shy and reserved, and did not attract attention during the meeting process. After the merger, Li Qizhong, the former camp commander of the Twentieth Regiment, was transferred to another post, and Lin Biao was reassigned as the camp commander of the First Camp. After the Jinggang Mountain merger, one day, Mao Zedong saw a soldier-like child giving a speech to the troops: "In fact, whether it's a bandit or a warlord, as long as they have guns, they own a piece of the world. We also have guns, and we can rule the world!" Mao Zedong learned that this person was Lin Biao, the camp commander who had commanded the victories at Ao Mountain Temple and Leiyang City, and thus remarked: Most camp commanders only know how to lead troops in battle, lacking political insight, but this child camp commander was full of "red separatism" principles, completely in line with his own views, capable of handling important responsibilities in the future. Later, Mao Zedong always favored Lin Biao, recognizing talent and utilizing it well, making Lin Biao always the youngest among military leaders of the same rank. This constant support and guidance became an important factor in Lin Biao becoming a brilliant military commander active across China. In the anti-"encirclement" struggle on Jinggang Mountain, especially in the three attacks on Yongxin personally commanded by Mao Zedong and the fierce battle at Longyuankou, Lin Biao's intelligent and flexible tactics, adept at using feints, won Mao Zedong's appreciation. Mao Zedong's approving gaze turned towards this 21-year-old camp commander. Those familiar with history know that in the summer of 1928, the Hunan-Jiangxi border suffered from the famous "August Failure". The "August Failure" caused considerable losses to the early revolutionary forces of our party, but it brought opportunity to Lin Biao, enabling him to enter the ranks of the important military leaders of the Fourth Red Army from the lower-ranking officers. In late July of that year, the Hunan Provincial Committee sent Du Xiujing as a "special envoy" to Jinggang Mountain to convey the Provincial Committee's instructions, intending to dispatch the troops southward to Xiangnan for combat. Du Xiujing and others, taking advantage of the homesickness of the soldiers from Xiangnan in the Twenty-ninth Regiment while Mao Zedong was absent,擅自 decided to send the main force of the Jinggang Mountain Red Army southward. During the decision-making meeting for troop movements, Red Fourth Army Chief of Staff and Commander of the Twenty-eighth Regiment Wang Erluo and Camp Commander Lin Biao, among others, raised objections, but they were not accepted by the meeting. While the troops were advancing south, Mao Zedong sent a long letter requesting Du Xiujing, Zhu De, and Chen Yi to reconsider the pros and cons of the decision to send the main force south, suggesting to withdraw the main force back to the border area. Du Xiujing ignored the advice and insisted on sending the main force south to attack Chenzhou. On July 24, the troops approached Chenzhou City. The initial attack by the Twenty-ninth Regiment failed, retreating, Wang Erluo then led the Twenty-eighth Regiment to attack again. At 9 AM, the First Regiment led by Lin Biao broke through the pass and hoisted the flag, being the first to ascend the city wall. The enemies inside the city hastily retreated to the mountains under the north suburb of Chenzhou City. After the entire Red Fourth Army entered the city, the Second Regiment of the Twenty-eighth Regiment, led by Battalion Commander Yuan Chongquan, was responsible for vigilance duties, while the rest of the troops rested on site. Unexpectedly, at noon, the enemies on the northern mountains took advantage of the Second Regiment's negligence and relaxation of vigilance, suddenly launching a fierce counterattack. The Red Army's main force resting inside the city was caught off guard, unable to organize effective defense in time, and retreated outside the city. The entire Twenty-ninth Regiment was wiped out, and the Twenty-eighth Regiment was scattered and disorganized, hastily retreating. Seeing the situation, Zhu De dared not linger in battle, ordering the troops to immediately retreat to Jinggang Mountain. As the saying goes: "Good fortune does not come in pairs, misfortune never comes alone." While the troops were retreating to the border area, Battalion Commander Yuan Chongquan feared being held accountable for the failure and rebelled with his troops. Under his influence, four companies of troops were dragged away. The situation was now very severe. Revolutionary forces must not suffer such losses! Zhu De acted decisively, sending Lin Biao with the First Regiment to track and search, and Wang Erluo volunteered to chase Yuan Chongquan alone to persuade him. Lin Biao quickly pursued and soon caught up with the Second Regiment, surrounding their base at Enshunwei. Yuan Chongquan ordered a counter-encirclement. Just as a bloody battle was about to break out, Wang Erluo also arrived at Enshunwei, shouting loudly, persuading the soldiers of the Second Regiment not to be deceived, causing pain to loved ones and joy to enemies, the Red Army does not fight the Red Army. The coerced and deceived soldiers of the Second Regiment, upon hearing the shouts of the army's chief of staff, put down their weapons one after another. Upon realizing the plot was exposed, Yuan Chongquan, enraged, shot a burst of bullets at Wang Erluo and fled to join the enemy. Wang Erluo was killed on the spot. Who would take over his work? After careful consideration by Mao Zedong and Zhu De, it was decided that Lin Biao would take over Wang Erluo's position, assuming the heavy responsibility of commander of the Twenty-eighth Regiment.
