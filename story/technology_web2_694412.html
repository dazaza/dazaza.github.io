The opportunity of innovative pattern is significant - the integration of the southern Sichuan region is an irreversible trend

by xyxcandy8 on 2012-03-07 09:35:09

Innovation in the pattern brings significant opportunities; the integration of the southern Sichuan region is an inevitable trend. ●Clarifying the relationship of division and positioning in regional economic development, integrating and optimizing resource allocation, and innovating a new pattern of regional cooperation are important issues currently facing the southern Sichuan area. ●With urgent logistics demand, having a logistics advantage has become an important condition for attracting foreign investment, and the southern Sichuan economic zone faces major development opportunities serving the Chengdu economic zone.

As the construction of the Chengdu-Chongqing economic zone advances, how can the southern Sichuan economic zone, as one of the five major sectors of the entire province, seize development opportunities under the new situation, explore and create comparative advantages in the region, clarify the relationship of division and positioning in regional economic development, integrate and optimize resource allocation, and innovate a new pattern of regional cooperation? This is an important issue currently facing the southern Sichuan area.

Regarding the future development trends of the southern Sichuan economy, Mei Yong, assistant director of the Investment Promotion Bureau of Luzhou City, is very optimistic. In his view, the southern Sichuan urban cluster will form a "golden fan handle," whose essence is the expansionary development of channel economy with port economy as the platform. The new round of large-scale development in western China will provide sustained momentum for the development of ports and port cities throughout the province.

Status and Advantages

The southern Sichuan economic zone is one of the five economic sectors in our province's 12th Five-Year Plan. It is the junction of Sichuan, Yunnan, Guizhou, and Chongqing provinces/cities and has always been an important passage connecting these four provinces/cities. Therefore, the broad southern Sichuan region also includes western Chongqing, northern Guizhou, northeastern Yunnan, and western Panzhihua areas. The five cities of southern Sichuan cover nearly 50,000 square kilometers, with a population of 21 million accounting for more than two-tenths of the entire province, and its total economic output accounts for about two-tenths of the entire province (statistics from 2010). The broad southern Sichuan area covers over 300,000 square kilometers, encompassing 60 million people. Southern Sichuan is a region in our province with relatively high population density, strong economic strength, fast industrialization process, and high level of urbanization. Located on the south wing of the Chengdu-Chongqing economic zone, it is at the intersection of the second horizontal and third vertical lines in China’s urbanization pattern. Given the existing industrial foundation, location advantages, and resource endowments of this region, as long as we seize the opportunity, the southern Sichuan region can achieve a breakout and leapfrog development in building a highland for economic development in western China, thereby becoming a new growth pole for the economic development of the entire province.

Problems and Challenges

Mei Yong believes: Currently, the development of the five major economic zones in Sichuan is unbalanced. Due to the huge suction effect of the Chengdu economic zone and the boost from post-disaster reconstruction, the contribution share of the southern Sichuan economic zone in the entire province has relatively decreased from three-tenths at the beginning of the "Eleventh Five-Year Plan" period to two-tenths, further widening the gap, which does not correspond to the development situation of the Chengdu-Chongqing economic zone. The integration of the southern Sichuan region is an inevitable trend, but currently, there are widespread problems in the southern Sichuan region such as lagging infrastructure construction, repeated investment, homogenization of industrial structure, low level of cooperation, lack of external synergy, and ecological environment levels below the provincial average.

As an overall economic zone, there is no clear characteristic, advantage, or common platform for the economic zone. The division and positioning of each part have yet to be clarified. Currently, competition outweighs cooperation. According to the principle of coordinated regional economic development, unity benefits while contention harms. Therefore, under the guidance of the "Twelfth Five-Year Plan," it is necessary to conduct further in-depth research on the development positioning of the southern Sichuan economic zone, systematically integrate regional resources, and develop together to grasp the direction of development at a higher level.

Development and Opportunities

Under the background of the new round of large-scale development in western China, coastal industries are gradually transferring to western regions. With the development of new industrialization, our province is entering the accelerated climbing stage of accepting industrial transfers. The industrial structure is rapidly transforming and upgrading, and the quality of economic development is continuously improving. The mode of development is quickly transitioning and enhancing, and the "economic highland in western China" is becoming increasingly prominent. With the rise and formation of three trillion-yuan industries (automobiles, IT, chemicals), the accompanying logistics demand is urgent. The supporting logistics demand for the economic highland in western China is significant. Having a logistics advantage has become an important basic condition for attracting foreign investment. The southern Sichuan economic zone faces major development opportunities serving the Chengdu economic zone. The southern Sichuan, northern Guizhou, and eastern Yunnan regions are also a golden triangle of resource energy. The broad southern Sichuan region is in the middle stage of industrialization. The upgrade and transformation of economic development, the acceleration of factor flow, configuration, and reorganization in this region itself urgently needs large-scale logistics services. High logistics costs and long turnover cycles are important factors constraining the economic development of our province, and logistics industry is an important value-added segment in the smile curve. To convert resource advantages into value advantages, it is possible to use the golden triangle of Luzhou, Yibin, and Leshan ports to integrate the golden triangle of liquor, tourism, and energy resources.

Expert Insights

Finding complementary projects for Chengdu and Chongqing

Professor Zhao Xiaohong (Dean of the School of Economics and Management, Neijiang Normal University, Director of the Western County Economy Research Center)

Using distinctive tertiary industries to drive "two modernizations"

Wang Shaonong (Professor at Neijiang Vocational and Technical College)

Wang Shaonong believes that urbanization is an inevitable trend for the development of Neijiang, and industrialization is an important way to achieve urbanization. Previously, Neijiang's urbanization was realized through administrative means, resulting in a relatively high urbanization rate. However, industrialization is still in the gradual development stage, so urbanization and industrialization are not well-coordinated. To let industrialization drive urbanization, the most direct way is to rely on leading industries.

Currently, the levels of industrialization in Chengdu and Chongqing are very high. Neijiang can take a distinctive path by vigorously developing Zhang Dachian culture and creating a unique tertiary industry belt along the river that integrates catering, calligraphy and painting art, sightseeing tourism, etc., to build Neijiang's leading industry. Through the tertiary industry, Neijiang's consumption can be driven, thereby promoting agricultural product deep processing, construction industry, etc. With consumer demand, Neijiang's investment attraction can be driven, attracting more high-tech industries and labor-intensive industries to settle in Neijiang. In the past, urban planning lacked foresight, and infrastructure was backward. Now, grand plans can be made according to the development of megacities, making the slogan of turning Neijiang into a leisure city known, truly making Neijiang a city where "visitors are attached and residents are proud."

Public Voices

University graduate Liu Chang hopes for more entrepreneurship preferential policies

Liu Chang, who lives in Gulin County, Luzhou, just graduated from university this year. He has witnessed the development changes in Gulin County in recent years. In his view, the process of urbanization has promoted the development of his hometown. "Urbanization has further improved the infrastructure of this small county of Gulin, including transportation, communication, finance, electricity, etc. This provides a basis for economic development."

Liu Chang studied marketing in university. He told reporters that during the first semester of his senior year, the class specifically organized learning about knowledge related to the Chengdu-Chongqing economic zone. Regarding the impact of the Chengdu-Chongqing economic zone on Luzhou, he also has his own views. "With Chengdu and Chongqing as the leaders, increase their attractiveness nationwide, attract industrial transfers, increase employment around, and complement the advantages of cities within the economic zone. Besides, the establishment of the Chengdu-Chongqing economic zone has given a shot in the arm to the Golden Triangle of Liquor."

But as a newly graduated university student, what Liu Chang cares most about are the benefits that the Chengdu-Chongqing economic zone will bring to employment. Currently, Liu Chang has successfully entered Langjiu Sales Co., Ltd., becoming a business representative.

He clearly recognizes that under the new market atmosphere, it can promote his learning and progress, improve his business skills, but challenges also exist. "Competition is fierce, and the requirements of the market and manufacturers on us are getting higher and higher, facing survival of the fittest." Liu Chang also plans to start his own business. With the process of industrialization and urbanization, the promotion of the Chengdu-Chongqing economic zone will inevitably bring about more entrepreneurial opportunities, but Liu Chang frankly said: "I hope the government can provide more preferential policies and safeguard measures, such as tax incentives, conducting entrepreneurship guidance, and more financial convenience."
