Don't let happiness wait.

by wangquan1986 on 2012-02-24 17:55:56

Don't let happiness wait. Autumn quietly descends, bringing with it seeds and wishes. I like the colors of autumn, but I also fear this kind of weather because the temperature difference between morning and evening is particularly large, and it has become much cooler. I have always been troubled by headaches, which occur from time to time. When the pain strikes, I feel so helpless. Even a slight bodily fluctuation can exacerbate the pain. It feels as if my blood vessels are about to burst, and the back of my neck and cervical spine feel dull and painful, making me feel nauseous but unable to vomit. People in pain most desire health; health is truly the greatest treasure in life.

In the past, when I had a headache, I once saw a doctor who diagnosed it as neuralgic headache. Aside from painkillers and injections, there seemed to be no other effective treatment, so I stopped trusting doctors. Every time I have a headache, I think about going to the hospital for a CT scan to check if there's anything wrong with my brain or cervical spine, but the thought of the long queues at the hospital makes me hesitate. What disappoints me even more now is that some doctors lack medical ethics, so I've kept delaying going to the hospital for an examination.

In the morning, as I was leaving home, I saw a huge pool of blood at the intersection. It was said that someone had been hit, and their survival was uncertain, but the car that hit them had vanished without a trace. Seeing that fresh red blood made my heart ache slightly. Life is originally so fragile and unpredictable. Perhaps, regardless of sorrow or joy, we should cherish and enjoy every moment of being alive. We don’t know what the next second will bring.

I no longer piece together scattered memories with time. With constant work, my heart seems to have lost some anticipation. It’s not that I'm not persistent, nor do I want to miss out, but I just don't want to be swayed by emotions anymore. I just want to return my heart to its original place. Like a flower, blooming quietly and opening gently.

Finally, I have calmed down and am no longer experiencing great ups and downs. Some things cannot be forced, so I slowly wait and let things take their natural course. I tightly wrap my already wounded heart, allowing pride to return to myself. I no longer lose myself for love, nor will I abandon my self-respect for love. It's not that I don't love anymore, but I'm not as passionate as before. It's not that I'm not persistent, but I've changed my approach to avoid hurting myself. Just like now, waiting...

Waiting is a kind of pain, but also a kind of hope. Waiting greatly tests one's patience and faith. It keeps people constantly reminiscing and looking forward, and this profound feeling is not something everyone can understand. Perhaps life is just such a continuous repetition. The same road must be walked several times.

Emotional people, in the space of reminiscence, hope, and waiting, try to preserve each present moment, only hoping to weave a beautiful future. What else can accompany you forever? Aren't all things like clouds that have once come and then disappeared? Such as pain and joy, love and hate, life and death, there is always a word "destiny" involved. Some people and events, no matter which path in the world you take, you cannot get closer to them, just like the moon and me, never too close or too far, having countless meetings and farewells, only a faint glow maintains the connection. They are external things, yet they often linger in my heart.

I think this is enough. I really feel old now, the marks of time are very evident, impossible to ignore or comfort oneself. Fortunately, I am not panicked, nor at a loss. I accept the fact of aging calmly. Since it cannot be changed, I can only accept it peacefully. I often wonder, what will I be like when I am forty, fifty, sixty, seventy years old? Will I turn into an unpleasant old lady? Will there be an old man who likes me?

In the past, I thought the world was vast, so vast that I could never finish exploring it in my lifetime. Now, I find the world small, with just family, a few close friends, and this simple life, nothing else matters.

Those distant places, those landscapes, those beauties, seem irrelevant to me. My days are as plain as water: working, eating, sleeping, reading, watching TV, occasionally walking around, drinking tea, these have become the themes of my life. Because it is inevitable, I also find beauty in it. Life continues, and I only wish that I and my family can safely navigate the river of time, and that is enough!

Gazing at the sky, the clouds, happiness nestled against the mountains; birds, happily chasing each other; falling leaves, dancing gracefully. Beautiful scenery, a joyful world quietly continues. What am I still waiting for? Stride confidently and pursue future happiness. Don't let happiness wait.
