1. [How to Make "Drool-Worthy Fish"]

Ingredients: One grass carp, two large tablespoons of starch, about half a liang (Chinese unit) of fried peanuts, two large tablespoons of roasted white sesame seeds, a small piece of old ginger, one garlic clove, five green onions, two tablespoons of fermented black beans, three tablespoons of cooking wine, two tablespoons of soy sauce, one tablespoon of vinegar, one teaspoon of sugar, one liang of red oil chili, two large tablespoons of Sichuan peppercorn oil, appropriate amounts of salt and monosodium glutamate (MSG), one large tablespoon of sesame oil.

1. Clean the fish and slice it into pieces. Mix with salt and cooking wine evenly and marinate for a few minutes. Then mix with water and starch evenly to code flavor. Crush fried peanuts into granules. Chop fermented black beans, green onions, garlic, and ginger finely.

2. Heat oil in the pan until six-tenths hot, then pour in fermented black beans, ginger, and garlic to stir-fry until fragrant.

3. In a large bowl, add salt, MSG, sugar, soy sauce, vinegar, stir-fried ginger, garlic, fermented black beans, red oil chili, Sichuan peppercorn oil, sesame oil, crushed fried peanuts, and mix evenly to form seasoning juice.

4. Place the pot on high heat, heat oil until four-tenths hot, then put in fish slices to stir-fry until cooked through.

5. Scoop out and drain the oil, then place in a bowl.

6. Pour the seasoning juice over it, sprinkle roasted sesame seeds and chopped green onions on top.

7. Mix evenly and serve.

2. [How to Make Water-Cooked Fish]

(I)

1. Buy a carp and slice it into fish pieces.

2. Marinate the fish pieces (salt, MSG, green onion, ginger, alcohol, pepper, or if you like, you can add five-spice powder or other flavors that you prefer).

3. Bring a little water to a boil, add the fish pieces to blanch until they are eight-tenths cooked, immediately scoop them out. Place them in a serving pot, discard the water.

4. Heat a lot of oil, then add dry red chili peppers and some Sichuan peppercorns (depending on personal taste). When the chili color turns slightly black, pour the oil over the fish pieces.

5. In restaurants, water-cooked fish often has a lot of mung bean sprouts underneath the fish slices. The fish slices are just laid on top of the bean sprouts as a layer. At home, you can skip the bean sprouts.

6. When the oil is first poured, be careful not to let the hot oil splash and burn yourself, so the container used for water-cooked fish must be large enough.

(II)

Bean sprouts or your favorite vegetables, ginger (a large piece, loosen and cut into slices), garlic (a head, slightly press but no need to chop), green onions in appropriate amount, doubanjiang (or diced chili), Sichuan peppercorn, dried red chili pepper, chili powder, salt, MSG, white pepper powder, cooking wine, soy sauce, a little vinegar, edible oil. Also needed: cornstarch, cooking wine, a little salt, one egg white.

1. Clean the fish well after killing it, chop off the head and tail, slice the fish into pieces, and chop the remaining fish bones into several chunks. Marinate the fish slices with a little salt, cooking wine, cornstarch, and one egg white, mix evenly and marinate for 15 minutes. Place the head, tail, and fish bones separately in a plate, and marinate using the same method.

2. Boil a small pot of water, wash the bean sprouts, and blanch them briefly in boiling water. Scoop them into a large basin, sprinkle a little salt according to personal taste, and set aside.

3. Add three times the normal amount of oil used for stir-frying in a clean wok. After the oil heats up, add three large tablespoons of doubanjiang (or diced chili) and stir-fry until fragrant. Add ginger, garlic, green onion, Sichuan peppercorn, chili powder, and dried red chili pepper, and stir-fry over medium-low heat until fragrant. After the aroma comes out, add the fish head, tail, and fish bones, switch to high heat, stir evenly, add cooking wine and soy sauce, an appropriate amount of white pepper powder and sugar, continue to stir-fry for a moment, then add some hot water, and season with salt and MSG (taste the saltiness). Once the water boils, maintain high heat, add the fish slices one by one, use chopsticks to spread them apart, cook for 3~5 minutes, then turn off the heat. Pour the cooked fish and all the soup into the large basin that holds the bean sprouts.

4. Take another clean pot, pour in half a catty of oil (the exact amount depends on the size of the prepared container, aiming to submerge the fish and bean sprouts when poured into the large basin, which can be visually estimated). After the oil heats up, turn off the heat and cool it down a bit. Then add a lot of Sichuan peppercorns and dried chili peppers (depending on individual preference for spiciness and numbness), and slowly stir-fry over low heat to bring out the fragrance of Sichuan peppercorns and chili peppers. Note that the fire should not be too high to avoid burning.
