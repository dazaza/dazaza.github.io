Manchester United's acknowledged weakest link has left opponents in despair, as a versatile defender with zero transfer fee gains fame in one match.

by qinglai888 on 2009-12-17 10:03:36

Manchester United's acknowledged weakest link yet leaves opponents in despair - a versatile defender with zero transfer cost becomes an overnight sensation.

According to Sina Sports, the high-density twice-weekly matches, coupled with an injury-plagued defensive line, forced Ferguson to use some less experienced players as temporary fill-ins. The Belgian Riki Dierckx has thus become an indispensable figure on Manchester United's defense line. Just past 21 years old, Dierckx's career development could be said to have been rather bumpy until now, but under Ferguson, to quote a cliché: Dierckx has been reborn.

Interestingly, Dierckx's first appearance this season was in the League Cup against Wolves; today again against Wolves, and this time in the higher-tiered league, starting from the beginning. The recent domino-style injuries among central defenders have indeed created today's opportunity for Dierckx. But this is only on the surface. Ferguson used him first as a right-back and then as a central defender today, daring to test a player with only two games of league experience in the league itself, which already indicates Ferguson's high regard for his abilities.

Dierckx performed admirably in this match, showing no fear of confrontation. The Wolves team that intended to suppress Manchester United through strong tackles had to face someone even harder. Dierckx made six interceptions in total, ranking first among both teams, successfully three times, and also had four successful clearances, ranking first in Manchester United, indicating that the opposing side deliberately targeted his position but failed to gain any consolatory opportunities.

Passing the defensive test was just one aspect. As a right-back, Dierckx had to push forward for support. Perhaps there weren't many standout moments in this area for the Belgian, but in the first half, he had a successful run down the right flank, forcing a scramble in front of the goal where the opposing defender cleared the ball first. For Dierckx, who played his first league game for Manchester United this season, you can't ask for too much. Additionally, when he switched to central defense at the 60-minute mark, it showed more of a commanding presence. His decisive and clean ball handling, and his ease in dealing with several opponents over 1.9 meters tall despite being 1.86 meters himself, earned him specific praise from Ferguson after the match. He said: "Dierckx performed very well when moved to center-back, showing great potential; he is a natural-born central defender."

Including today's match, Dierckx has played a total of five games for Manchester United, three in the League Cup and two in the league, with his first league appearance being against Hull City in the last game of the previous season. Although five games might not seem like much, Dierckx has already covered left-back, right-back, and both sides of central defense, making him a standard versatile backfield player. Considering the future, Dierckx has every chance to become a legendary figure at Manchester United, or at least a versatile and indispensable player like O'Shea, capable of playing any position. When he joined Manchester United in January 2009, it didn't cost them anything because Manchester United agreed to pay his former club Stoke City based on his performance. Clearly misjudging his talent, Stoke accepted at the time. Despite only passing a year, and although Stoke could make a small profit from him, Manchester United is clearly the sole beneficiary. With their current level, Stoke let go of a player who could have been an important addition to their squad.

It's hard to imagine if Dierckx's highest professional aspiration was merely joining Stoke City, and if Manchester United scouts hadn't noticed him, when he would have risen to prominence. Transferring from Antwerp to Stoke City in 2007 was seen by many Belgians as a good opportunity, but being loaned out to Wrexham further dampened his professional ambitions. It would have been better for him to stay in Antwerp and play in the Belgian Second Division. Thanks to the keen eyes of Manchester United's scouts initially, followed by Ferguson's ability to recognize talent, Riki Dierckx has gained a truly developmental opportunity.

One must admire Ferguson's recognition of young players' capabilities. The use of players like Macheda, Da Silva brothers, and Obertan has already achieved initial success, with the key being how cheaply they were acquired. Obertan was the most expensive at just £3 million, and Dierckx represents the latest success. And Paul Pogba, brought in from Le Havre in August at the age of 16, might be the next surprise. Recently, he helped Manchester United's U18 team achieve a four-match winning streak, scoring 3 goals in 4 games, including one against Liverpool's U18 team. A few days ago, he scored within the first minute of a match, helping Manchester United's U18 team advance smoothly to the fourth round of the Youth FA Cup. In his debut for Manchester United's reserve team against Bolton on the 14th, he scored a goal and provided an assist. Born in 1993, this player's growth rate is simply astonishing, and it won't be long before he receives a call-up to the first team. Ferguson's emphasis on young players, especially his unique and precise eye for talent selection, has laid a very solid foundation for Manchester United's future orderly development.
