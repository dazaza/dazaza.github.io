A complete advertising planning cycle consists of several different stages, with varying targets, contents, and objectives in each stage. Understanding these differences helps to grasp the key points, highlight priorities, clarify the particularities of various aspects, ensuring that the planning work proceeds in an orderly manner. Generally speaking, a standard advertising planning process can be divided into five stages: organizational preparation, market research, strategic planning, plan formulation, and implementation & summary.

1. Organizational Preparation Stage

(1) Establish an advertising planning group. The group should consist of personnel from client management, creative planning, copywriting, design production, photography, videography, market research, and media PR. Establishing a planning group is an effective way to utilize collective wisdom for advertising planning work, representing a transition from experiential to standardized and scientific advertising planning activities.

(2) Define work responsibilities and schedule timelines.

2. Market Research Stage

Market research is the premise and foundation of advertising planning.

(1) Investigate and collect market information and related materials. This includes brand and product research, brand image research, consumer status research, competitor status research, etc. For instance, brand and product research requires a detailed understanding of the specific content of all brand components, as well as the appearance, structure, function, principle, material, technology, quality, price, manufacturing process, usage method, and preservation, maintenance, and repair methods of the product.

(2) Analyze and study relevant data. Deduce, summarize, analyze, and study all collected materials and data, requiring the ability to describe the current situation and reveal trends, providing reference for the next step of strategy formulation.

3. Strategic Planning Stage

Strategic planning is the core and main body of the entire advertisement.

(1) Make decisive and strategic choices based on the results of previous analysis and research.

(2) Conduct strategic planning. Centered around creative planning personnel, combined with relevant personnel, analyze the advertising objectives. Based on target market strategies, determine the positioning and appeal strategies for advertisements, thereby developing advertising creativity and presentation strategies. Propose reasonable media combination strategies and promotional strategies according to the characteristics of the product, market, and advertising.

(3) This stage's planning also involves selecting advertising opportunities, formulating advertising plans, and writing about advertising budgets and planning reports.

4. Plan Formulation Stage

(1) Standardize strategic planning in a specific systematic form. Determine the time and spatial scope of advertising operations, and make restrictions on the selection and use of media, including how reasonable media combinations are, the frequency of advertisements, and how much budget is needed to support such frequency.

(2) Compile the advertising planning text, i.e., the planning book. The planning book is both a concentrated representation of the planning results and a textual basis for planners to explain and secure advertising business from clients, thus it must be carefully revised and verified before completion.

(3) Further communicate with the client and explain the planning, finally reaching a consensus on the advertising planning plan.

5. Implementation and Summary Stage

(1) Implementation and monitoring of the plan. The advertising planning group works together according to the planned requirements, creating and designing advertisements, publishing them through media, and monitoring and adjusting the entire process.

(2) Evaluation and summary.
