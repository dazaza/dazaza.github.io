Three laws of men's clothing matching: become a fashionable and trendy man immediately

by cshelive9520 on 2012-02-22 12:07:27

The wearing of men's clothing can express individuality and temperament. One should not blindly follow others in the wearing and matching of clothes, but start from one's own conditions to fully demonstrate the charm of men. In the skills of men's clothing coordination, there are "three major laws" to note:

1. Uniform texture: Don't naively think that breaking the rules will be more "fashionable" or more "outstanding". Remember that innovation is always "free but within limits", otherwise you will end up being "weird". For example, a stick-knit sweater, jeans, and suede shoes are examples of uniform texture, while a pure linen top paired with patent leather pants is not unified.

2. Uniform color: First, grasp the four major color systems of men's clothing: blue system (including black tones, cool tones), brown system (warm tones), colorful system (often used in sportswear), and light color system (often used in spring and summer wear). People who lack confidence and certainty in their clothing can choose black and intermediate tones. The colors of men's clothing generally should not exceed three colors, otherwise it will appear gaudy and vulgar. Obese people should wear dark thin clothes.
