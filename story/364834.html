Shen Shuo: Choosing "naked donation" is a personal deep thought

by ideas on 2010-09-17 10:12:36

　　Chen Guangbiao, 42 years old, from Si Hong of Jiangsu province and originally from Anhui province. He is the chairman of Jiangsu Huangpu Regenerative Resources Utilization Co., Ltd., vice president of China Council for the Promotion of Public Welfare, standing director of the Chinese Red Cross Society, vice president of the All-China Federation of Charity Organizations, among over 300 other roles. Due to his outstanding contributions in economic construction and charity public welfare, Chen Guangbiao has consecutively won the "China Charity Award" for four years and was once honored with titles such as "National Hero Model for Earthquake Relief" and "National May Day Labor Medal". In 2007, he was crowned as the "China's First Philanthropist" at the youngest age among Chinese philanthropists.

　　The Beginning

　　On September 5th, an open letter published online made the Chinese tycoons a topic of discussion again. The recipients were the world-famous Bill Gates and Warren Buffett, who are scheduled to host a charity dinner in Beijing at the end of this month; the sender was none other than Chen Guangbiao, known as "China's First Philanthropist." In the title of the letter, he wrote: "When I leave this world, I will donate all my assets," and in the text, he also said, "As a rich man, I will never be a miser."

　　The donation frequency increased from annually, quarterly to monthly, and the donation amount grew from 20~30% of corporate profits to 50~60%, and finally to 95%—ultimately, Chen Guangbiao, chairman of Jiangsu Huangpu Regenerative Resources Utilization Co., Ltd., announced a "complete donation."

　　What sparked public discussion wasn't just the seemingly extreme term "complete donation." After the exposure of Gates and Buffett's dinner plan, the domestic public opinion and reactions from the tycoons were negative and awkward. Some media even disclosed that some tycoons refused to attend the charity dinner due to concerns about being "persuaded to donate" or even "forced to donate." At this moment, by announcing a "complete donation" so loudly, Chen Guangbiao undoubtedly placed himself at the center of controversy.

　　Chen Guangbiao’s high-profile actions have drawn complex evaluations in this transitional society. Some praised it as the beginning for the wealthy to find a noble destination for their wealth, while more voices expressed typical distrust towards the rich.

　　On the 13th, Chen Guangbiao revealed on his Weibo account that nearly a hundred enterprises had responded to his call for "complete donation" through various means. Regarding the various doubts from the outside world, he stated that he would demonstrate his actions to everyone and hoped to receive encouragement and tolerance.

　　Dialogue

　　"Good deeds must be told to others to influence more people"

　　Chen Guangbiao's charitable acts always seem somewhat high-profile. On the official website of his company, there is little information about business operations, but most pages are filled with news about charity and public welfare activities. Among the most prominent content are his photos participating in rescue efforts during the Wenchuan earthquake. Some say that Chen Guangbiao is actually "showboating," while others call him a "madman."

　　Reporter (hereafter referred to as Rep): Why did you respond to Gates and Buffett's dinner invitation in such a high-profile form through an open letter? Aren't you worried about being criticized as "showboating" again?

　　Chen Guangbiao (hereafter referred to as Chen): I have been doing charity work for 10 years while running my business. To date, I have cumulatively donated goods and money worth 1.34 billion yuan to society, directly benefiting over 700,000 people. In that letter, I said that wealth is like water—it's external property. If there's a cup of water, one person can drink it; if there's a bucket of water, it can be stored at home; if there's a river, it should be shared with others. In the future, I will continue to uphold this belief.

　　I am high-profile in my charity work because I hope to influence more people to join me in doing charity work, making China become the most loving charity powerhouse in the world. I hope to be the locomotive, leading the way. I always believe that good deeds must be told to others, only then can they spread, influence more people, and tell the world that Chinese people are kind and full of love.

　　Rep: Facing this charity dinner, while some tycoons choose to avoid or reject it, you took a completely opposite stance. Aren't you worried about offending other tycoons or inviting more criticism?

　　Chen: Choosing "complete donation" is the result of my deep consideration, and I believe it will gain understanding and support. When I first started being high-profile in charity work, some people didn't understand or even doubted me; but now, more and more people understand, support me, and hope that more tycoons will follow my example. Over these many years, I believe I have indeed influenced and led many entrepreneurs to give back to society through charity.

　　This time, I wrote to Bill Gates and Warren Buffett as a response, support, and interaction to their actions and initiatives. At the same time, I also want to express to the world—represented by Chen Guangbiao, a group of Chinese rich people have broad minds, and one day, they will return all their wealth to the world, allowing more unfortunate and impoverished brothers and sisters to share, leaving this world cleanly and honestly.
