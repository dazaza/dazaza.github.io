The choice of trade-offs

by chandadada on 2012-03-06 18:29:11

When faced with the same problem, we have many ways to solve it. Often, we may not find a better way, but there will be a more suitable way. During Beijing's outdoor training activities, team members often struggle in choosing the right method, hesitating and losing the best opportunity, thus failing to complete the task. Like doing multiple-choice questions, our lives are full of choices. A thought forms, and possibly it is discarded instantly. This won't work, that can't either, we keep choosing in our own minds. What influences our thoughts? Why do the things we want to do change three times a day? We start with firm determination, but later it breaks easily like a vase. What makes us lose our own ideological territory?

People always use "daydreaming" to mock those who have unrealistic fantasies. However, sometimes precisely because of having dreams, one might achieve something. The experience of Little Stone stealing is the best proof.

Little Stone grew up following his father, who made a living by thievery. After entering junior high school, Stone was ordered by his father to drop out and learn stealing. Perhaps due to attending school for a few days, Stone knew stealing would harm others. He had also secretly followed a person whose money was stolen by his father. That person originally took the money to the hospital to pay for his daughter's surgery fee, but the money was stolen midway by Stone's father. Because he couldn't pay the medical expenses, his daughter eventually died from her illness. Witnessing all this, Stone vowed to become a police officer when he grew up and arrest all thieves.

At the age of seventeen, Stone saw his father steal money from a street vendor selling spicy hot pot. That money was prepared as tuition for his daughter who was admitted to a music college. Such an art institute has expensive tuition, and the vendor must have emptied all his savings. Stone managed to take the money from his father and return it to the vendor, but it was too late for the girl to register for school. She thus missed the chance to attend university. When returning the money, Stone was caught on the spot and sent to a juvenile detention center. During his education in the juvenile detention center, Stone shared his ideals with the instructor. Not only did the instructor not mock him, but he also provided many books for Stone to prepare for the police academy entrance exam. During his time in the juvenile detention center, Stone studied tirelessly, but his foundation was too weak, and he was far from reaching the level required for the police academy entrance exam.

After being released from the juvenile detention center, Stone didn't go home. He worked part-time in a restaurant while catching up on his studies. Thus, after three years, Stone finally got admitted to the police academy.

Stone grew up in a bad environment, surrounded by theft-related matters. Most people choose their careers based on their growing-up environment. In Stone's father's life, stealing was everything, a normal lifestyle. However, Stone didn't follow this path. Despite others' mockery, he persisted in his ideals and ultimately didn't replicate his father's shameful life of petty theft. He could walk proudly under the sun. Thoughts determine one's life, which doesn't need much explanation. If someone wants to do something, they must necessarily prepare for it and make efforts. Those who do nothing will have no thoughts or ideals. Such people will live their whole lives like machines, letting fate push them around.
