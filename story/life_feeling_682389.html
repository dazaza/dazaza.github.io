Because of love

My name is Dongdong. We fell in love at university. After graduation, we returned to this city together. Dongdong worked for the TCL Ace Company while I was a regional agent for Ace, responsible for opening up sales channels here. I ran around all day with a very flexible working hours. At noon, I would go to a restaurant near the company, order her favorite dishes, and then call her to have lunch together. At that time, her colleagues were very envious of her having such a loving boyfriend, and she also felt very happy. Less than a year after graduation, we got married with the prospect of happiness.

In the autumn of the second year, she became pregnant. The first feeling of pregnancy for me was not happiness, but panic, because I had never been a father before. Because of her pregnancy, she had to quit her job. I knew she was very lonely at home, so I often took time to call her. If she didn't call, I would call her. Sometimes, while chatting, I would say I had to work and would call her back later. She would wait by the phone, but it was common for there to be no reply. The more she waited, the angrier she became, and she would pick up the phone and call me. I would answer as if waking from a dream: "Look at me, I was so busy that I forgot you were waiting for my call." She felt wronged and argued with me on the phone until I had nothing left to say. She said, "How can work be more important than a pregnant wife?" As we argued more times, she got a little angry, so I told her: "Dongdong, I know you are very hard during pregnancy, but I am also very hard. Can you understand me?"

That day, she held the phone and didn't say anything for a long time, tears dripping down. Her silence scared me, so I had to leave my work and run home. I knew she would hurt herself when she was angry. When I got home, she was pulling her hair fiercely, wrapping strands around her fingers. I immediately hugged her and said, "Dongdong, I'm sorry, I'm sorry. Please don't treat yourself like this." It was then that I promised to go home every noon to have lunch with her.

When our daughter was one year old, her figure had basically returned to what it was before giving birth. She planned to go out to work and found a cosmetics sales company, which changed everything. I called her, and she said she couldn't come out because the company had social engagements. Many times, I was alone facing the well-prepared food, eating with tears streaming down. Later, I began to doubt her so-called business engagements. Watching the hot food turn cold made me increasingly angry, so in a fit of anger, I went to their company cafeteria.

Standing at the door of the restaurant, my eyes blurred with tears. She was eating her work meal, laughing happily. But I saw someone else beside her. I angrily stared at her, staring until she lowered her head, then I turned and left. Late at night, I came back, sitting heavily beside her with a strong smell of alcohol: "Dongdong, let's get a divorce. If you want the daughter, she's yours; if you don't, she follows me." She didn't say anything, just kept looking at me.

We didn't sleep all night, sitting and waiting for tomorrow. Because tomorrow, we would each go our separate ways. I kept thinking why it had come to this. I knew that boy was her colleague and wealthy. Perhaps following him would make her happy. For her happiness, my pain doesn't matter. As the sky slowly brightened, it also meant she was leaving me. I saw her busily packing clothes and then said, "The daughter is still young, let me take her." I didn't say anything, and thus they left.

On such a noon, after waking up from a nightmare, it was a bit cool, overcast. I stood on the balcony holding a teacup. I just stood there, inexplicably sad. Rarely did I have such a moment, the atmosphere so gloomy, the world so quiet, standing alone in sadness, trembling slightly in the cold air. I told myself, "Why not now? Let's indulge in sentimentality," and without hesitation or preparation, tears really came, and my heart started to ache sharply, as if all my feelings suddenly awakened.

I originally wanted a photo of her to look at when I missed her. However, after not seeing her for a long time, her chubby face became blurry in my mind. Unfortunately, this wish couldn't be fulfilled. Later, I asked once again, but it was still unfortunate... Maybe heaven thought that such possession was a luxury for me, a spell that bound me! In this life, we could only meet quietly, watch from afar, pass each other by. There should be no intersection...

Sometimes I think, rather than enduring such pain, it would be better as written: "Life has never met." If there was no meeting, there would be no past brilliance and beautiful memories. Perhaps the most beautiful and moving love is not forever, but the once possessed destiny without an edge.

Today, I suddenly thought of my daughter. So I called her, asking, "Is your daughter okay? Does she like the clothes and toys I bought for her?" "Yes, the daughter is fine, and she likes the things you bought." Ouyang, I'm getting married. I thought for a moment and said "Oh." Wishing you happiness, and I hung up the phone.

Every day, every day, wasting time, groaning in painless agony. Lazily doing nothing. I don't know what I will or can do in the days to come? Just going down this mediocre path. The world I once longed for has battered me to pieces, washing away all my former aspirations and happiness. The cruelty of reality attacks my still fragile body, heavy burdens press down until I can't breathe, years of growth, the burden behind growing too arduous, my weak shoulders can no longer bear it! All the carefree, happy, innocent times have melted into helplessness, sorrow, and sadness under the years of growth, never to find the self I once was. Perhaps the real me now is just naive and foolish.

Looking at the computer, I suddenly felt a wave of fear. I don't know when I started habitually opening my blog first, reading those texts that warm yet pain me. On NetEase, I met many children like me, burying all their unease and reluctance with their youth.

Each time, greeting and leaving messages one by one, my heart is filled with gratitude, and my face smiles. Here, I started to feel unable to write, because every child here is so excellent, talented, using loneliness to restore smiles. More often, I just quietly observe, sometimes wanting to write something, but ultimately it remains blank.

Facing the computer, facing those words, I suddenly feel lost, suddenly wanting to escape, my brain starts making buzzing sounds.

So, I turn off the computer, and my heart starts falling empty, waves of pain coming towards me. Walking out, my head is still confused. Those pains of the past once again surround me. I am forced to tear open all wounds.

I always thought I could give up anything, but now I realize I was wrong. Some people I want to give up, to forget, but I simply can't. Like wisps of smoke, they are everywhere, seeping through every pore, impossible to extricate myself. Thus, I can only bury her deep in my heart. Someone said, "When you cannot possess, the only thing you can do is not to forget." I don't know if these words are right or wrong.

Drinking with friends until drunk, my friend asked me what exactly was so good about her? Actually, this question has been asked in my heart many times, but I could never find an answer. Maybe loving someone doesn't need a reason, maybe love itself is the best reason. Because of love, love.

There have been too many pains, too many sleepless nights, too many drunken selves. Looking back, is it worth doing all this for a girl who has no possibility? Perhaps when the clouds clear, the wind calms, and time reaches its end, I will forget this pain.

Sometimes, people are really helpless, always struggling between wanting to forget and not being able to forget. So they choose to confuse themselves with cigarettes and numb themselves with alcohol, believing it can temporarily forget troubles. But the more cigarettes smoked, the stronger they become, the more wine consumed, the colder it gets, and the troubles we want to forget become even clearer.

If humans really have another life, I wish to be a free-spirited poet, living a life like leisurely clouds and wild cranes, being a passerby in the red dust, smiling at the affairs of the red dust.

We cannot endure too much pain. So let's try to forget. The world is still beautiful as ever. As long as we keep gratitude in our hearts, we can see the prosperity of the world.
