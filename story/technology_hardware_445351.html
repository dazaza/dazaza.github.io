On April 26th, when the baby gets the preventive injection

by was28119 on 2011-06-13 16:26:00

By the way, I had a physical examination.

9 months and 20 pounds, teacher's story, 73 centimeters.

Can crawl;

Can pull on the bed rail to stand up;

Can change from standing to sitting;

Can pass toys from one hand to the other;

Can poke small holes and buttons;

When called by name, will look up for the sound and make eye contact through the hole;

When held under the arms, can walk for a long time;

Can find hidden toys;

Can use the index finger to pinch and pick up button-like objects;

At the window, will pull the curtain to look outside;

Can take small things out of the cup;

Can understand some words, such as "turn on the light", "turn off the light";

Will respond to familiar music with sounds like "humming" or "singing";

Will laugh out loud;

Will make a series of sounds with tone changes;

When seeing peers, will reach out to touch;

Can read adult expressions; when strictly prohibited, he will follow;

Can only say "Dad", cannot say "Mom";

Cannot bang building blocks together;

Cannot use fingers to turn pages;

Cannot tiptoe to reach items;

Does not know how to wave goodbye, clap, or salute;

Cannot point to the corresponding picture of an object;

Does not know how to pretend to eat or drink with a spoon or bowl;

Does not know how to point at his nose or eyes in the mirror.

The diagnosis is: excellent motor skills but lacks language and social abilities.

The doctor said we must teach him actions like waving goodbye. This little guy, we teach him every day, but he won't learn! When we ask him to wave, his arm is like an iron rod, refusing to bend, instead reaching for the toy, not even looking at me. When teaching him to bang blocks together, he shows the same indifference!

However, for activities he likes, he does them tirelessly. Sitting in the stroller, he throws all the toys within reach to the ground. Once we pick them up, he immediately throws them again. We can't keep up with his speed! If we don't pick them up, he keeps looking up and tries to reach for them. My mom says if there was a toy-throwing competition, he would surely win first place. When placed in the crib and told to lie down while we prepare his milk, before we even get to the bottle, he flips over and crawls to the head of the bed, pulling on the railing to stand up, holding on with one hand while touching the pictures on the wall with the other. Even if we push him back down, he gets up again immediately! Unless he's really tired.

In the past week, the baby likes to sleep on his stomach occasionally. At night, I have to get up several times to check if he has kicked off his blanket and adjust his sleeping position to prevent his nose from being blocked.

When his hair was longer, I didn't notice much. After his haircut, I suddenly realized he had bald spots on the back of his head! Recently, due to taking medicine, having his stomach washed, and experiencing fever and cold, we haven't given him calcium supplements, fearing interactions between medications, so he might be lacking calcium. Also, we've been using calcium gluconate, and I'm not sure if constantly using one type affects absorption? We plan to switch types. Or maybe the dosage isn't enough? We need to follow the instructions strictly. His father wants to take him for testing, but I hesitate since we've been to the hospital many times already, and it breaks my heart to see him get blood drawn again. I bought test strips online to measure calcium and zinc levels in urine, which haven't arrived yet. Once they arrive, we'll test him.

Although he is mischievous and tiring to take care of, there are many troubles in raising him, but the little guy is indeed adorable. Sometimes he shows affection, putting his little mouth on someone's face and smearing saliva around, making people laugh uncontrollably.
