Furthermore, nutritionists have analyzed the nutritional content of various vegetables, closely linking the nutritional value of vegetables to their color. The deeper the color of the vegetables, the higher their nutritional value generally is; the lighter the color, the lower the nutritional value generally is. The nutritional value of vegetables from high to low can be divided into several categories: green, yellow, and colorless. Green vegetables, such as rape, celery, shepherd’s purse, spinach, leeks, have the highest nutritional value; those yellow vegetables, such as carrots, tomatoes, cabbage, lily, Chinese cabbage, green beans, and potatoes, have medium nutritional value; melon, bamboo shoots, wild rice, and colorless vegetables have the lowest nutritional value. Nutritionists point out that the shepherd’s purse has the highest nutritional value among vegetables. Although the shepherd’s purse is a native wild plant, its vitamin A content is higher than carrots, its vitamin B content is higher than alfalfa, its vitamin C is higher than oranges, its calcium is more than tofu, and its iron is similar to red amaranth. Moreover, it is rich in vitamin P, which has hemostatic, diuretic, and antipyretic medicinal functions. Nutritionists have also found that different colors of the same kind of vegetables have different nutritional values. For example, purple eggplant has a higher nutritional value than white eggplant. The reason is that purple eggplant is rich in Vitamin P, which enhances microvascular resistance and prevents blood vessel embrittlement.
