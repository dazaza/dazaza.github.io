[Updating] Yearly Dragon Tiger Fight: Kindle Fire VS. B&N Nook Tablet

by diglog on 2011-11-20 12:30:11

Our review is still ongoing, and this article will continue to be updated. We hope everyone stays tuned! Yesterday, we just published a comprehensive picture-and-text detailed review of the Amazon Kindle Fire tablet. Today, another highly anticipated 7-inch tablet, the Barnes & Noble Nook Tablet, has officially gone on sale nationwide. Although Barnes & Noble is the largest chain bookstore in the U.S., the waves of digitization, socialization, and cloud computing are forcing industry leaders to remain vigilant and keep up with the times. Remember when Barnes & Noble launched its first-generation e-ink reader, the Nook, it was already two years behind Amazon's Kindle. But now, at least in terms of pace, Barnes & Noble has caught up with Amazon. Both companies have released their heavyweight readers of the year one week before Thanksgiving, not only aiming to seize the golden opportunity of the shopping season but also engaging in a direct head-to-head competition. We believe that you, as a reader, are just as interested as we are in experiencing these two tablets that are different yet extremely similar. So, let's join together for a full comparison.

Packaging: B&N's Nook Tablet comes in an elongated box, with the tablet placed in the upper half of the packaging. The USB charger and USB cable are separately housed in two small compartments in the lower half. Additionally, like Amazon, there are no other accessories included.

Appearance Details: We already know that the new Nook Tablet completely inherits the design of its predecessor, the Nook Color. Therefore, it hasn't brought any surprises. However, compared to Amazon's purely monotonous rectangular design, Nook has established a unique design style.

Placing the two devices side by side, the Nook is slightly larger than the Kindle Fire, possibly because B&N still retains a physical Home button on the front of the device. Both devices place the USB port on the lower side without dust protection.

On the top edge of the Nook Tablet, there is a standard 3.5mm audio output jack and a small microphone. Amazon Kindle’s speaker is located on the top of the device, while B&N Nook’s is on the bottom back.

In the design of the back, both coincidentally etch their logos in the center of the back. The front, back, and side frames of the Nook use three different materials. In particular, the front frame and back cover have a stronger matte feel, giving a velvety sensation when touched.
