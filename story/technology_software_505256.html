Where is Haier's future after the Zhang Ruimin era? -_1941

by ctonyogc00 on 2011-10-11 11:01:03

Haier has become a banner of "Made in China", but the future of Haier is a mystery. This mystery consists of three suspense:

Does the core competitiveness support continuous growth? In the eyes of many industry assistants, Haier has many praiseworthy strengths: daily clearance and daily improvement in management, the "ball on an inclined plane" practice, Zhang Ruimin's own excellence, and high-quality services, etc. Unfortunately, my judgment is contrary to that of most people. In my view, Haier's core competitive ability lies in its experience of supporting its brand through service, which is a combination of technology and knowledge. The question now is whether this competitiveness can sustain Haier's continuous growth. The answer is, it depends on whether this core competitive ability aligns with Haier's growth strategy.

How to challenge the limits of strategy? If we carefully examine Haier's product structure, we will find that summarizing Haier's current strategy as internationalization alone is not comprehensive. Because Haier has clearly entered a diversified industrial operation pattern by investing in computers, mobile phones, biopharmaceuticals, integrated home kitchens, etc., seeking corporate growth through a diversification strategy.

This is a truly trap-filled growth path. Because the business model during diversification has only two prospects: either pick a point in the new business to become an industry leader; or like GE, become the leader in various fields through excellent strategic control. Before a clear choice appears, no matter which way Haier goes, its continuous growth depends on the coordination and balance of related businesses at three levels and then incorporating them into pipeline management: profit-generating core business (such as refrigerators, TVs, air conditioners, washing machines); emerging business full of opportunities (computers, mobile phones, integrated home kitchens); seed projects for creating the future (biopharmaceuticals, etc.).

If Haier's continuous growth depends on the reasonable coordination of these three levels in strategy, then Haier needs to establish corresponding mechanisms to achieve strategic transfer from the first level to the second level and pay attention to maintaining the cultivation of the third level from R&D.

Can Haier culture match internationalization? Haier's omnipresent service system also reflects the warmth of the "big family" in traditional Chinese values: the products may not be the best, but the service must be the best. This is a successful enterprise operation strategy with era significance, and also a successful enterprise growth strategy with environmental significance, but it is not a successful enterprise culture with continuous meaning. Elevating Zhang Ruimin's thoughts or Haier's competitive strategy to the realm of corporate culture and giving it the status of "core competitiveness" is the biggest misunderstanding in the process of professional environment creation in China. Imagine, in a competitive environment that cannot yet produce professional managers, the culture generated by capable operations will only be a further obstacle to progress.

Welch left an important lesson in corporate culture construction: he not only positioned corporate culture as the philosophy needed for the company's own growth and adaptation rather than personal thought, but also used culture to support strategy. More importantly, he could clearly separate the corporate culture adapted to market changes from the competitive strategy that maintains the current excellent operating status, otherwise, strategies or tactics with short-term success meanings would evolve into some kind of "invincible treasure."

Perhaps this is why Haier always dreams of being listed as a case by Harvard or Switzerland's IMD. In fact, the attention that Harvard or IMD pays to Haier is not much more special than Gong Li being invited several times by the international film circle to be a judge or guest in recent years. Gong Li is not an international-level movie star, neither is Haier, but while Gong Li is about to be eliminated, Haier may gain rebirth, provided that it understands that the true internationalization path must begin in the post-Zhang Ruimin era.
