What is an online store, the development of an online store, promotion, and the store opening process

by anonymous on 2011-03-25 13:29:28

Definition of an Online Store:

An online store, as the name suggests, is a shop opened on the internet. As a form of e-commerce, it is a website that allows people to browse and make actual purchases simultaneously, completing the entire transaction process through various payment methods. Currently, most online stores are opened using third-party platforms such as Taobao, eBay, Youa, Paipai, and Goupu Mall. Creating your own e-commerce site involves a large amount of technology and significant initial investment.

Development of Online Stores:

Online stores have evolved from initially being just for product display into websites that not only showcase products but also allow visitors to make actual purchases.

Characteristics of Online Stores:

1. Convenient and fast; no need to go through processes like decoration and procurement required by traditional shops. A few clicks of the mouse can open an online store.

2. Quick transactions; after both parties reach an agreement, payment and transactions can be completed immediately, with goods delivered to buyers via logistics or courier services.

3. No risk of bulk inventory pressure; you can operate without a physical store, merely running a web-based shop, which doesn't require stockpiling.

4. Easy management; no need to hire staff or travel far to restock shelves. Everything is done online, and re-stocking can be achieved with a simple click of the mouse when items are out of stock.

5. Diverse forms; regardless of what you sell, suitable formats can be found. With sufficient funds, you can choose general online store programs for building, or register with good online store service providers to conduct transactions.

6. Trust is paramount; online transactions cannot provide tangible personal experiences, leading buyers to prefer transacting with trusted merchants. Successful first-time transactions often lead to higher customer return rates.

Preparations for Starting an Online Store:

Starting an online store means owning a website with online shopping functionality. First, basic infrastructure for a website must be in place: web space (which can be a virtual host, independent host, or VPS host), domain name, database (for static websites, no database is necessary, but online stores require databases to store product, customer, and order information). An online store system is a set of software programs running on the website server. To manage the site, some software must be installed on your computer, with the most important being FTP software. FTP is a network tool that allows you to manage files on your web space. Common FTP software includes FlashFXP and CuteFTP. Most importantly, you need your source of goods.

Online Store Systems:

An online store system mainly refers to third-party independent online store systems that arose due to the inability of some platforms to fully meet the needs of online merchants. These systems are known for their ease of use, flexibility, and independence.

Development Languages for Online Store Systems:

.NET - Microsoft's main promotion platform, currently gaining popularity. It has a trend to replace JSP, with optimistic prospects.

JSP - Mainly used for large web sites, such as portal sites, enterprise government website management systems, etc., with mature frameworks and technologies. Currently, the mainstream for website construction.

PHP - Similar to ASP, but in better condition. Good security, occupying a certain share. For example, the domestic market share-leading online store system ShopEx is based on PHP technology.

Here, .NET systems are recommended, following Microsoft is definitely not wrong. Many well-performing domestic websites, such as JD.com, Newegg, Vancl, PPG, use .NET.

Management of Online Stores:

A good online store operator needs to pay attention to selecting the right service provider and service form: choosing between an online store service provider or setting up your own online store; selecting good sources of goods and handling product images and placement after stocking up; then decorating the online store; communicating with customers; ensuring safe transaction processes, etc.

Three Steps of Online Store Management:

1. Selecting the Business Direction

Running an online store is completely different from operating a physical store offline. Offline, as long as your store's location isn't too bad, small businesses can do quite well, even selling very common items can lead to substantial profits. However, doing business online requires a unique approach. Generally, it's best to sell items that are hard to find offline (such as special crafts, limited edition items, branded clothing, electronics, etc.). This way, dedicated enthusiasts will come to your store. If you collaborate well with them, your business will continue steadily. Choosing unique items that others find hard to obtain is a good start, ensuring quality at competitive prices will retain your customers.

Step 2: Stocking Products, Pricing

In online sales, there's no pressure from store rent, no worries about industrial and commercial taxes, and no harassment from organized crime. Therefore, as long as we have good sources of goods, every profit counts, making it quite easy. Thus, prices must be cheaper than offline, don't be greedy. Compare other sellers' prices, try to offer lower prices where possible. This will attract many cost-conscious customers who may become your long-term clients if served well. After selecting products, detailed product descriptions should be prepared. Some sellers provide very brief product descriptions, leaving potential buyers confused, which could reduce trust. A good description shows respect for buyers and the product itself. Not only does it attract knowledgeable buyers, but it also helps beginners understand the product, generating interest in your offerings and affection for your store. Detailed product descriptions also enhance the seller's credibility.

Step 3: Opening for Business, Integrity as the Foundation

Respond promptly and honestly to messages, removing buyers' doubts and increasing their trust. Regularly update your products, even if there’s no immediate business, keep adding new items and clearing out old stock. Only continuous capital flow brings profits. Some stores use "discounts," "gifts," "lotteries" to benefit customers. In online business, credit is crucial. Good reputation wins more stable customers. Some stores turn their shops into communities by introducing star-rated customers, sharing life experiences, publishing essays, turning customers into friends. Always cherish your credit; doing business is like living, only integrity ensures success.

Two, Goods Sources

How to find low-priced goods?

1. Be a Market Hunter

Closely monitor market changes, take full advantage of product discounts to find low-priced goods. For example, sellers often find high-quality brand-name clothes during seasonal changes or clearance sales, reselling them online to gain sufficient profits through regional or time-price differences.

2. Focus on Foreign Trade Products

If familiar with foreign trade manufacturers, directly source from the factory. Among leftover foreign trade orders, there are many good items. These products usually have only 1-3 pieces, often next year's or current trends, priced at 40-70% off mall prices, making them very marketable.

3. Buy Brand Surplus Inventory

Some brands have a lot of surplus inventory, which some merchants sell entirely to professional online sellers. If you have good bargaining skills and can buy their surplus inventory at low prices, substantial profits can be made.

4. Obtain Overseas Discounted Goods

World-class brands abroad are very cheap before seasonal changes or holidays. If sellers have relatives or friends abroad, they can help secure attractive discounts for online sales. Even if sold at 40-70% off traditional mall prices, there's still a 10-40% profit margin.

5. Wholesale Goods

Visit regional wholesale markets frequently, like Xizhimen, Xiushui Street, Hongqiao in Beijing, Xiangyang Road, Chenghuang Temple in Shanghai, to familiarize yourself with market conditions and get wholesale prices.

After finding a source, test with a small quantity. If sales are good, consider increasing the order volume. Some sellers maintain good relationships with suppliers, ordering only after selling, avoiding tying up capital and inventory buildup.

In summary, regardless of the channel, low price is the key factor. Finding high-quality, low-cost goods lays the foundation for your online store's success.

Three, Naming Goods and After-sales Service

1. Choose a Good Name

Product names should succinctly summarize the product's characteristics, aiming for standardization so that viewers can quickly grasp basic information and easily find it through search engines.

The general format is: Brand + Product Name + Specifications + Description

For example, the product name for "15ml Lancome oil-free Radiance Nutritive Eye Cream" should be: Lancome (Brand) + Radiance Nutritive Eye Cream (Product Name) + 15ml (Specifications) + (Oil-Free) Description, i.e., Lancome Radiance Nutritive Eye Cream 15ml (Oil-Free).

Avoid various so-called "personalized" symbols in product names, such as 【】●★▲■. First, it appears unprofessional, like a roadside stall, reducing trust. Second, some symbols may cause product names to display incorrectly.

2. Pictures Decide First Impressions

Product pictures are the first impression you give to customers. Blurry, messy pictures give a poor impression, like a dirty face, failing to capture attention. Pictures can be searched online; most manufacturers have their own websites where you can select pictures from product introductions. Alternatively, scan product manuals at appropriate resolutions for clear images. If neither works, seek someone skilled in photography and edit the photos with image processing software for good results. Learning Photoshop over a few weekends to beautify images appropriately is even better.

3. Welcome with Smiles, Act Quickly

Finally, someone likes your item and pays for it despite a difficult-to-open wallet. Without a doubt, handle orders efficiently and provide excellent customer service. Furthermore, this person might bring unexpected benefits: word-of-mouth advertising is powerful, requiring no persuasion, potentially yielding long-term returns from one thoughtful effort.

4. Build Relationships

For past customers, regularly follow up. Shortly after shipping, ask if they've received the item, inquire about satisfaction a month later, suggestions two months later, or if they need other items... Let customers feel valued and cultivate their consumption habits. Once accustomed to buying from you, you gain an unpaid promoter.
