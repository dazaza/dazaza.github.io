Dr. Yang Minghui Profile

Dr. Yang Minghui, male, Chief Physician, Professor, Department Head, Doctoral Supervisor, born in 1962, graduated with a master's degree in integrated traditional Chinese and Western medicine from Shaanxi College of Traditional Chinese Medicine. He concurrently serves as Vice President of the China Association of Chinese Medicine, Standing Director of the China Association of Integrated Traditional Chinese and Western Medicine, Vice President of the Beijing Association of Integrated Traditional Chinese and Western Medicine, Vice President of the PLA Association of Chinese Medicine, Editor of "People's Military Doctor" and "Chinese Journal of Integrated Traditional Chinese and Western Medicine Emergency", etc.

He has been engaged in TCM clinical practice, teaching, research, and health care work for a long time. Academically, he actively advocates the integration of traditional Chinese and Western medicine, promoting the use of modern technological means to study and develop TCM, emphasizing the holistic perspective. His main research focuses on geriatric diseases, with cardiovascular diseases, respiratory and digestive system diseases, tumors, diabetes, and Parkinson's disease as his primary research directions.

He uses the Qi-enriching and blood-activating method to treat coronary heart disease; adjusts liver, kidney, and spleen to treat hypertension; treats heart failure based on the theory of heart-kidney yang deficiency; stages viral myocarditis treatment; combines disease and symptoms to treat arrhythmia; clears liver heat and harmonizes heart-kidney to treat cardiac neurosis; differentiates between deficiency and excess to treat pulmonary heart disease, all of which have shown good clinical effects.

He uses the body-strengthening and cancer-fighting method to treat tumors, and his clinical research results won the third prize of military medical achievements in 2001; his kidney-nourishing and blood-activating treatment for Parkinson's disease has achieved remarkable clinical efficacy, receiving funding support from the National Natural Science Foundation and has completed the project; he proposed the unique understanding that the pathogenesis of diabetes is "spleen-kidney qi deficiency as the root, yin deficiency dryness heat and blood stasis as the marker", emphasizing the treatment principle of spleen-kidney tonification, nourishing yin and clearing heat, combined with blood-activating and stasis-resolving, gaining ongoing research topics from the National Administration of Traditional Chinese Medicine; In addition, he has conducted in-depth research on infertility in both men and women, and his research "Study on Infertility Models" won the third prize of military scientific and technological progress in 2001. He undertakes multiple research projects at national and military levels. He has authored more than 10 specialized books such as "Analysis of TCM Medical Records" and "TCM Clinical Handbook for Geriatric Diseases", and published more than 40 papers.
