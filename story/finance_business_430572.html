The Fatal Love of a Leftover Woman

That year, I was 28 years old. All my female classmates and girlfriends had gotten married, but I still hadn't found a partner, let alone experienced love. I was extremely worried, dreaming about getting married every night. Especially when my mother urged me, I almost wanted to grab a man from the street and get married immediately. To quote one of my classmates: "Eager to get married but unable to find a partner." I often cursed under my breath, cursing men for being superficial. In my youth, when I was as beautiful as a flower, I didn't want a boyfriend, yet they swarmed around me like flies. Now, even though I'm not yet a faded beauty, with just a few wrinkles, they avoid me like the plague. Even worse, during a class reunion, a male classmate asked if I was married. I said I was an old maid, and he actually said, "Old? Are you still a maiden? Back in the day, you were so proud, always followed by a long line of admirers." I was so angry I wanted to spit blood. At that moment, I thought: Why are women like vegetables? Once they age, they lose their value. I also hated myself for not realizing that I would end up this way during my youthful prime. When men seemed worthless back then, I easily dismissed them with a sneer. Now that I've aged, why do I see each one as a treasure?

To find a partner, I spent those two years trying every possible method, putting forth great sincerity, but fate wasn't on my side. I went on blind dates with dozens of men, but none of them caught my eye. Isn't that strange? Although I went on many blind dates, I didn't become discouraged—not because I wasn't disheartened, but because I couldn't afford to be discouraged since I hadn't found the right person yet. Until one day, I completely lost hope when someone introduced me to another older bachelor. The meeting signal was for me to hold a roasted chicken in my left hand and him to carry a bottle of beer in his right, meeting at the overpass of Zijin Mountain Department Store. Can you imagine how discriminating against older bachelors this is? From that point on, I refused to go on any more blind dates.

At that time, the internet was gaining popularity. Encouraged by newspapers, television, magazines, and other media, going online became synonymous with being trendy, avant-garde, and fashionable. Due to work requirements, I had no choice but to learn computers. Who would have thought that after learning, I would discover chat rooms, which became a place to kill my idle time. I enjoyed it immensely, immersing myself there. There, I could forget all the troubles of life, forget the arduous task of finding a partner, and breathe freely and comfortably. I could pretend to be a pure young girl, babble nonsense to make the gentlemen chase me frantically on their keyboards before disappearing; I could also pretend to be a scholar and engage in banter with fake ladies called Silk Stockings or Twisted Women. Occasionally, I'd encounter a sincere person with whom I could confide my worries without hesitation. Though I bought a computer specifically for going online, I only saw the internet as a tool for leisure and entertainment, never expecting to gain anything from it. However, shortly after starting, I met a partner online and began my first emotional journey at the age of 28 within this virtual world. Perhaps the heart of an old maid is easier to move here.
