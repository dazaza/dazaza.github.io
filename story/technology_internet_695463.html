Which types of postgraduate students can obtain dual certificates while working?

by qindingdangx on 2012-03-07 15:45:51

I. Classification of Part-time Postgraduate Studies

The methods for part-time employees to participate in postgraduate education are divided into two categories: academic education and non-academic education.

1. After completing academic education, you can obtain dual certificates (graduation certificate + degree certificate).

2. After completing non-academic education, you only get a single certificate; the final obtained is a completion certificate and a master's degree certificate.

II. Dual Certificate Programs for Part-time Postgraduate Studies

For part-time postgraduate studies, only the Master of Business Administration (MBA) and Juris Master programs offer dual certificates.

1. Master of Business Administration (MBA)

January MBA ([http://mba.eol.cn/](http://mba.eol.cn/)) has the same registration time, exam time, and subjects as the January national unified examination. After admission, you can choose to study part-time and obtain both a degree and academic qualification.

Eligibility Criteria:

1. Bachelor's degree graduates with three years or more of work experience (calculated from graduation to enrollment, such as those applying for the 2010 MBA program need to have obtained their graduation certificate before September 1, 2007).

2. Associate degree graduates with five years or more of work experience.

3. Graduates with a postgraduate degree or already holding a master's degree with two years or more of work experience.

Study Methods: The main study methods for MBA include full-time, non-full-time (weekends, evening classes, or concentrated teaching), with a general duration of 2-2.5 years.

2. Juris Master ([http://fashuo.eol.cn/](http://fashuo.eol.cn/))

Eligibility Criteria: In addition to meeting the conditions for the national unified examination, the following must also be met: Undergraduate major was not law (the following 13 majors cannot apply for the Juris Master professional degree: Law, Economic Law, International Law, International Economic Law, Labor Reform Law, Business Law, Notary, Legal Affairs, Administrative Law, Lawyer, Foreign Economy and Law, Intellectual Property Law, Criminal Law).

Study Methods: Full-time and non-full-time study methods are adopted. Full-time study lasts two to three years, while non-full-time study does not exceed four years. A credit system is used, including required courses and elective courses. Required courses account for 30 credits, and the total credits must not be less than 45.

III. Examination Time for Dual Certificate Part-time Postgraduate Studies

Online Registration Time: Mid-October

On-site Confirmation Time: November 10th to 14th

Examination Time: January 9th-10th (11th)

IV. Examination Subjects for Dual Certificate Part-time Postgraduate Studies

Preliminary Exam includes two subjects: English and Comprehensive, where the Comprehensive exam is divided into Mathematics, Logic, and Writing. The difficulty of the English exam is roughly equivalent to the CET-4 level, or slightly easier.

Re-examination generally covers Politics, English Listening and Speaking, Management Knowledge, and Management Case Analysis.

Generally speaking, taking the January postgraduate entrance exam leads to obtaining dual certificates, while taking the October exam leads to obtaining a single certificate.
