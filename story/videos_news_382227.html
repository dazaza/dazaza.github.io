The most beautiful female captain is only 29 years old and talented, flying the A321 model (picture)

by djs365 on 2011-02-25 08:36:12

She is under 29 years old this year and has been promoted to be a female captain of the Airbus A321 model in the ninth flight squadron of Air China.

In fact, Wang Zheng's initial university major was electronic information science and technology. In her sophomore year, her school, Civil Aviation University of China, began recruiting female pilots. She was very curious and secretly signed up without telling her parents, thinking she would just give it a try. Unexpectedly, she made it through all the rounds and was eventually admitted. It was only before signing the contract that she called her family to tell them: "My parents' first reaction was not very supportive. They thought girls should work in an office rather than as pilots, which is a high-risk occupation. They didn't want me to take on this career, but seeing my persistence, they ultimately chose to support me." In 2005, Wang Zheng joined Air China, started flying in October 2006, and was promoted to captain in July 2010. She is currently the only female captain at Air China. Wang Zheng said that during her first class at the American aviation school, a teacher told them that girls who want to achieve the status of captain in this industry must work three times harder than boys. However, Wang Zheng frankly admitted that from the aviation school onwards, from every theoretical course to flight practice, and through private licenses, commercial licenses, instrument exams, and more, she had always been ahead and solid in her progress. Actually, she didn't put in extra effort; perhaps this industry requires a bit of talent, and Wang Zheng just happened to possess this talent. Many people believe that flying is purely a male-dominated field, but Wang Zheng disagrees: "Since the country has begun recruiting female pilots, it shows that relevant departments have scientifically proven that women can also handle such jobs." Before her sophomore year, Wang Zheng was a quiet, delicate girl. Since switching to flying, she deliberately spent time playing ball and climbing mountains with male pilots to blend in with them. "Because pilots must have an outgoing personality and remain calm in emergencies. As a woman, life often makes us weak, but doing this job means you must become strong. Only then can you integrate into this male-dominated group and make them feel that this girl is competent enough for the job. I've made many efforts in this aspect and undergone some changes; my eyes are now more determined, and my tone of voice is more steady. When I do passenger announcements, passengers don't see the captain, but they feel reassured and secure through your voice." Wang Zheng said. Spending long periods among men, friends find her gradually becoming a bit dominant, but this is what the job demands, and it’s unavoidable. "Especially after becoming a captain, billions of yuan worth of national property and the lives of passengers on board are entrusted to you. The responsibility is immense. In special situations, everyone is waiting for you to make decisions. At such times, you must not panic first, then calmly analyze the situation, and finally communicate well with the crew, cabin crew, and even passengers, informing them of what has happened, what needs to be done, and how to cooperate. With such resource management, you will be able to handle special situations more easily and comprehensively."

Although female captains are rare, they still hold their own against the men. Wang Zheng believes that compared to men, female captains have incomparable advantages: "Female captains are meticulous. There are hundreds or even thousands of switches and buttons in the cockpit, allowing no room for error. Women are meticulous and less likely to make mistakes. Women are sensitive; I can feel subtle changes in the plane's flight state. Women have better endurance than men. Flying isn't as interesting or romantic as people imagine; it's actually quite monotonous and boring. Women can endure loneliness, sit patiently, and stay focused." Therefore, once female captains overcome gender-related limitations, they often perform exceptionally well. "Sometimes when the plane lands smoothly, passengers might sincerely compliment the chief stewardess saying the captain flew really well today. But they don't know that the person sitting upfront is a female captain. Every time this happens, I feel deeply gratified." Family members treat rest days as holidays.

During annual holidays, when people reunite with their families, pilots are all working on the front lines. Years of professional training have instilled a rigorous style in them. After turning off their mobile phones, they block out all joys and sorrows related to the ground and focus entirely on their high-altitude tasks. Once the plane lands, they often feel inexplicable guilt inside, "There was a period where I couldn't go home for three consecutive weeks. Coincidentally, my mother underwent surgery. Perhaps due to maternal intuition, I sensed something wasn't right when I called home, but they kept saying everything was fine. When I finished my flight and returned home, it turned out to be my mother's first day of chemotherapy. I felt extremely guilty because, as their only daughter, I couldn't be there for them when they needed me most." Although her parents hope she spends more time with them and celebrates festivals together, what they say more often is, "Go fly with peace of mind, we'll talk about anything when you come back." Thus, Wang Zheng said that their family holidays are entirely tied to her profession; whenever she returns home, that's when they celebrate. During her first year of flying, she spent the Spring Festival Eve in Chengdu, "After finishing the flight, the entire team gathered for a meal to celebrate. If the flight was late, everyone wouldn't have the energy or enthusiasm to celebrate further since we need to ensure good flying conditions the next day. We could only wash up and sleep. We often console ourselves that while others are resting, we're working, but we happen to avoid peak congestion. When we rest, we can enjoy the convenience of fewer people around." When she's not flying, Wang Zheng prefers spending time with her parents. She says her parents are getting older, so she likes talking to them and making them feel warm. In her spare time, besides accompanying her parents, she tries to give herself some space, gathering with friends, exercising, or traveling, relaxing and adjusting her tightly strung nerves to the maximum extent, then taking off again with a good state of mind.
