Daughter-in-law takes care of bedridden father-in-law for 12 years; after husband's death, she carries the heavy burden alone

by xue94fwsh on 2012-03-03 16:57:15

Every day, Zhu Xinhua feeds water, food and medicine to her father-in-law in this way. By/Photos: Reporters of the People's Daily Online Chen Wei In Taoyuan North Community of the provincial capital city, when people talk about Zhu Xinhua, the good daughter-in-law, they all give thumbs up. Since 1999, Zhu Xinhua has been taking care of her bedridden father-in-law for a full 12 years. After her husband passed away in 2009, she shouldered the burden of the family alone. Although life is hard, Zhu Xinhua never complains. Her greatest wish is "to make sure that there will be no regret in the rest years of my father-in-law". On the 22nd, the reporter came to Ms. Zhu's home. It was an ordinary peasant courtyard with various items piled under the gate. The yard was so small and the house was so dark that there was hardly any decent furniture inside, but it was very clean. In 1993, at the age of 22, Zhu Xinhua married into her husband's family from Ji Yang. At that time, both her and her husband worked at a lighting institute. Although their income was not high, they both had formal jobs after all. In 1996, her father-in-law suddenly got cerebral thrombosis, which disrupted the family life. In 1999, her father-in-law's condition suddenly deteriorated and he could only stay in bed and could not take care of himself. "At that time, the unit benefits were average, so I took leave from work and focused on taking care of my father-in-law." Zhu Xinhua said that at that time her father-in-law could not accept the fact that he was bedridden, and his temper became irritable. He would shout and cause a scene at the slightest dissatisfaction. Now, every morning after getting up, Zhu Xinhua turns her father-in-law over, massages him, cooks for him, feeds him and gives him medicine, etc. "In the past 12 years, he has never had bedsores." At night, it was the most tiring time for Zhu Xinhua. If her father-in-law needed anything, he would shout loudly. Drinking water in the middle of the night, going to the toilet, sometimes he would call out five or six times a night. Actually, taking care of the elderly did not make Zhu Xinhua feel too bitter. What made her the most heartbroken was the death of her husband in 2009. "He passed away on February 14, 2009. From being diagnosed with liver cancer to passing away, it was only a short month. At that time, in order to treat him, the family fell into a debt of 30,000 yuan." Zhu Xinhua said that the death of her husband made her feel much older overnight, and since then everything in the family needed her to shoulder alone. In the past two years, seeing how hard Zhu Xinhua's life was, her family members have repeatedly advised her to remarry, but she refused. "After my husband passed away, there were only my bedridden father-in-law, my 73-year-old mother-in-law, and my 15-year-old daughter left in the family. If I left, the family would fall apart." Zhu Xinhua said that she felt the most indebted to her daughter because of the limited economic conditions. After graduating from junior high school, her daughter attended a vocational secondary school instead of having the opportunity to go to university. Now, her daughter who will graduate soon is interning at a large supermarket with a monthly salary of 800 yuan, and Zhu Xinhua herself also found a job in the lighting market. The family's financial situation has improved somewhat. Looking back on the years she has gone through, Zhu Xinhua said that she does not regret at all, "Hard days will always pass. Since I have persisted for these 12 years, what can't I persist in the future?"
