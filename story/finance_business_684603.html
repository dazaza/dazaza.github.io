Two elderly people were beaten by many people in the petition office and the staff did not stop it

by wang858jy on 2012-03-01 09:10:24

On November 30, 67-year-old Mr. Wu and 66-year-old Mr. Huang, representing more than 50 villagers at the Wenzhou Petitions Bureau, were assaulted by a group of young people. They were diagnosed by doctors as having suffered from multiple soft tissue injuries.

The elders said that even when the police officers were taking statements for evidence collection, those who had assaulted them were sitting in nearby cars, watching from afar.

Seven elders felt something was amiss, so they lied and said they were not from Shangtoumen Village, and those people then left the room.

Yesterday, this reporter and a journalist from Wenzhou Network attempted to contact the staff members of the Petitions Bureau several times. The staff member on the line claimed that all the leaders were out of the office and he did not know the situation, then hung up the phone. Subsequently, the attempts to communicate with the staff members of the Petitions Bureau were unsuccessful.

Five or six minutes later, a leader of the Petitions Bureau arrived at the scene, seeing a large group of people making a commotion, and loudly said: "If you want to talk, go outside," and only then did those people leave.

Through online searches and inquiries via 12580, the telephone number of Wenzhou Guoda Real Estate Development Co., Ltd. was found. When the call was connected, the operator told the reporter that it was a wrong number and hung up the phone.

However, according to the descriptions of the elders present and the surveillance video, the group of assailants went in and out of the reception room several times without anyone stopping them.

Mr. Wu is a villager from Shangtoumen Village, Lucheng District, Wenzhou.

The Petitions Bureau has not responded to the assault incident.

Subsequently, someone reported the incident, and the police officers from Xiushan Police Station took the two assaulted elders to the police station for investigation and evidence collection.

In the surveillance video, at 11:35 AM, a young man in a floral coat pointed his finger at an elder and started cursing, followed by throwing punches at the elder.

Why could the assailants enter and exit the petition reception room at will? The reporter tried to learn about the situation from the security guards of the Wenzhou Petitions Bureau, but the guards all claimed they were unaware. At the registration desk of the Petitions Bureau, the staff members also refused to answer questions about the assault and declined the reporter's interview.

The elders also said that during the assault, the staff members of the Petitions Bureau were present at the scene, and the security guards did not come out to stop the violence.

Who were the people who broke into the petition reception room and assaulted the petitioners?

But a few minutes later, these people rushed in again, asking the elders if they were from Shangtoumen Village?

At 10 PM last night, the reporter called Mr. Wu. As soon as the topic of the day's incident came up, Mr. Wu still felt terrified.

Police officers from Xiushan Police Station, Lucheng District, Wenzhou, stated that the case was under investigation, and they declined to provide information or accept interviews from reporters.

Later, Mr. Wu told the reporter: "Afterwards, they took pictures of us with cameras, threatening us, saying 'If you are young, we'll beat you to death. Be careful when you go out. We'll stab you dead, and it won't matter if we have to compensate with a few hundred thousand yuan.' Later, a person claiming to be Mr. Zhang, the general manager of Guangda Real Estate Development Company, came in and ordered several young men to leave..."

In the surveillance footage, when the assailants stormed into the petition reception room and assaulted the elders, a young man resembling a staff member sat behind the table and kept making phone calls.

Who exactly were these assailants? Why did they assault this group of elders whose average age exceeded 60 years old? This newspaper will continue to follow up.

However, according to what the reporter learned, after learning about the incident, Secretary Chen Derong of the Wenzhou Municipal Committee immediately made a directive to severely investigate the matter.

That day, due to disputes over demolition issues with Guangda Real Estate Development Co., Ltd., more than 50 villagers went to the Wenzhou Petitions Bureau to report the problem. Seven people were allowed to represent the group and enter the Petitions Bureau, while the rest waited in the hall of the Petitions Bureau. Elder Wu Yidong and Elder Huang Zhaoping were among these seven representatives.

It was learned that after learning about the incident, Secretary Chen Derong of the Wenzhou Municipal Committee immediately made a directive to severely investigate the matter.

While petitioning normally at the Petitions Bureau, why were they innocently assaulted? Why didn't the security guards and duty personnel stop the violence in time?

Young people pointed at the elders and loudly insulted them.

Young people started hitting the elders, while the staff member shown at the bottom of the screen showed no reaction.

The reporter learned that according to the rules of the Wenzhou Petitions Bureau, before entering the reception room to file a complaint, the public must first register at the registration desk in the hall, then the staff will announce the numbers, and the petitioners can enter the reception room in order. Therefore, without registering or obtaining permission from the security guards present, ordinary people cannot casually enter the petition reception room.

According to the elder's recollection, at 11:30 AM, they entered the No. 1 petition reception room on the first floor, where a staff member came out to receive them. Soon after, six or seven people suddenly burst into the reception room and shouted at the elders, asking if they were from Shangtoumen Village?

The staff answered yes, and they proceeded to assault the elders.

"If these people are found, we won't be afraid anymore. The key is that these people dared to break into a government department and assault others, showing how audacious they are. Now the police say the assailants haven't turned themselves in, so we are naturally scared." Mr. Wu still doesn't dare to sleep at home, hoping the police can find the assailants as soon as possible.

According to the description of several elders present, a person claiming to be Mr. Zhang, the general manager of Guangda Real Estate, came to "communicate" with them, and the content of their visit was related to Guangda Real Estate.
