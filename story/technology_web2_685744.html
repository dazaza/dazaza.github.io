Man's request for overnight stay with ex-wife was rejected, leading to her murder; he turns himself in after fleeing to another place

by ysweh5kdf on 2012-03-01 16:39:00

On the morning of December 19, community police officers from Xiwu Road Police Station discovered an unusual situation in a unit on the third floor of the second unit of a residential building in a neighborhood on Xi Qi Road while making a visit. After reporting to the police station, the police called a locksmith who opened the door. Upon entering the room, the police found a woman's corpse lying sideways on the bed, with large amounts of blood on her head and the bed. There was a wound on the right side of the woman's neck, and next to her head lay a kitchen knife. After examining the scene, the police ruled out the possibility of self-inflicted harm and estimated the time of death to be around two days prior.

After committing the murder, Wei Mou fled overnight by long-distance bus to Shanghai. After wandering in Shanghai for two days, Wei Mou turned himself in to the Shanghai police. At the Xiwu Road Police Station, speaking about his ex-wife Gao Mou who he killed, Wei Mou expressed deep regret, stating that he still loved her very much. Currently, the case is still under further investigation.

Since this case occurred during the winter severe crackdown period, the Director of the Public Security Bureau of Xincheng District, Feng Changsheng, paid great attention and immediately instructed the Xiwu Road Police Station to fully investigate the case.

After investigation and interviews, the police learned that the landlord Wei Mou had been missing for the past two days. Combining other aspects of the investigation, the police determined that Wei Mou was a major suspect in the case, suspected of intentional homicide. Subsequently, the Xiwu Road Police Station arranged for police officers to pursue Wei Mou and issued an online warrant for his arrest.

At the age of 31, museum employee Wei Mou had gone through marriage and divorce complications over the past six months. Six days ago, he killed his ex-wife at home and fled to Shanghai. Two days later, he turned himself in to the Shanghai police. Yesterday afternoon, the suspect Wei Mou, accused of intentional homicide, was escorted back to Xi'an by police officers from the Xiwu Road Police Station of the Public Security Bureau of Xincheng District.

Just as the Xincheng police were pursuing and issuing an online warrant for Wei Mou, on the afternoon of December 19, the Xiwu Road Police Station suddenly received a call from the Shanghai Public Security Bureau, saying that a murder suspect had turned himself in. After verifying the situation, the Xiwu Road Police Station confirmed that the person who turned himself in to the Shanghai police was indeed Wei Mou.

A murder case occurred in the unit of a residential neighborhood.

According to the police, the suspect Wei Mou is 31 years old, a technician at a local museum, with a university education level. The victim Gao Mou is 32 years old, the cashier at a law firm in the southern suburb of our city.

Two years ago, Wei Mou and Gao Mou met through an introduction, and they obtained their marriage certificate in May this year. Due to various reasons, only six months later, in November, the two agreed to divorce. Although they divorced, Wei Mou and Gao Mou still had feelings for each other and often spent time together. On the afternoon of December 17, Wei Mou invited Gao Mou to have dinner at Wanda Plaza. After dinner, they shopped together and around 8 PM, they both went to Wei Mou's house. Wei Mou learned that after their divorce, Gao Mou had dated someone else, which made him extremely dissatisfied. Around 9 PM, Gao Mou prepared to go home. Wei Mou asked her to stay, but she refused. In anger, Wei Mou took out a kitchen knife and violently slashed her neck, causing Gao Mou's death.

The suspect said he still loved his ex-wife after committing the crime.

In order to quickly bring the criminal suspect to justice, Chief He Jianjun of the Xiwu Road Police Station led two police officers who drove overnight to Shanghai on the evening of December 19 to escort the suspect Wei Mou. With the strong cooperation of the Shanghai police, they successfully escorted the suspect Wei Mou back to Xi'an at 3:40 PM yesterday.
