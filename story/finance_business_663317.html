How Ardor Can Empower Your Blog

Every single one of the challenges mentioned above, and others, come up from forcing stuff. When you write about a thing you don’t care much about, or in a specific radius, you’re forcing it. Forcing things makes them of poorer quality. It'll surface in your writing. Readers aren’t interested in reading something you've pressured, and they'll go elsewhere. Ardor is the exact opposite: it will infuse your writing with joy, make it more interesting, compel people to read. They'll share your posts, and the fiery content will attract others. It's not the solution to all challenges. But focusing on ardor can actually change everything about your blog and you as a blogger. Here are a few ways:

1. Write what you're passionate about

What do you really care about right now? What have you been reading avidly from the internet, and what changes have you been making recently in your life? If you write about these things, you'll write well, and won't need enthusiasm to write.

2. Seek something to be passionate about

If you don't already have this passion, you'll need to find it. Don't live a passion-less life — not only is it dull, but bloggers who don't lead interesting lives are boring. You might not find this ardor on your first try, but by seeking, you'll start the journey and in time find it.

3. Write when you're excited, not on a schedule

You need to notice when you're getting excited about something, and take that opportunity to sit down and write, right then. Let the excitement of the moment pour out into the writing. Seize the moment, wherever you are, to write.

4. Tune in to the right music for you

The right music can get you excited, and help cause you to write with zeal.

5. Write passionately, become a better writer

When you write with passion, not only does it show in your writing and help inspire you, it actually makes you better. You will make mistakes at first, but the strength that this ardor gives you will strengthen your writing. The more you do a particular thing, with passion, the better you'll be at it.

6. Read others who are passionate

The best writers and bloggers have passion, and reading them will inspire you to do the same.
