Due to their nobility and texture, they have become the first choice for indoor stairs. The main materials of solid wood stairs available on the market are: rubber wood, oak, beech, ash, rosewood, and birch.

1. Rubber wood belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family. It is the raw material for making rubber. Its wood is relatively loose, not corrosion-resistant, and extremely prone to decay and deterioration under natural conditions after being felled. In the past, it was only used as firewood or pulpwood.

Rubber wood and oak are not in the same class of wood. In the introduction and advertisements of solid wood stairs, [rubber wood] should not be called [oak]. The market's abbreviation of [rubber wood] as [oak] is a misleading act towards consumers.

2. American oak is divided into white oak and red oak. Oak is heavy and hard with great strength.

3. Beech: Basically imported from Europe, beech is also a hardwood. It has delicate grain patterns, high stability, and exudes nobility. Therefore, in the past few years, beech became overly popular, leading to a monotonous style in solid wood stair decoration. The price of beech was very high, even resulting in almost every household being decorated with beech. As a result, in recent years, beech as the main material for decoration has been replaced by black walnut and American oak. However, due to its fine texture and stable noble characteristics, beech is still often used as secondary material for decoration, such as solid wood stairs. Currently, among the commonly used solid wood stair materials on the market, beech stairs are genuine and authentic. Since it is imported, no one would spend high prices importing garbage.

4. Ash: The situation is similar to that of birch. When made into engineered wood, it is basically inferior material.

5. Birch: Hard in texture, high specific gravity, great strength, and tightly structured. Resistant to moisture and wear, difficult to glue, good coloring performance, relatively rough grain pattern, relatively coarse vessels, prominent wood rays, and difficult to dry. Generally used for making wooden floors or furniture, solid wood stairs.

Currently, the high-quality large-diameter birch in the domestic market is mostly imported from Russia. Because the birch pattern resembles American oak, most of this raw material is exported to the United States as flooring material. Therefore, domestically produced birch, due to its small diameter, large color difference, and instability, is made into engineered wood. It can be said that the birch currently used for engineered wood is of the poorest quality. The birch stair treads commonly found on the market are basically made of engineered birch.

Birch has excellent properties, but because its material tends to turn black, it has never been recognized as high-end material. Therefore, some manufacturers will perform bleaching. Additionally, for the aforementioned reasons, birch has not been regarded as high-end wood domestically.

In summary, beech is the most suitable material for making solid wood stairs, and it is also the most expensive; ash and birch come next; rubber wood is the worst and cheapest.
