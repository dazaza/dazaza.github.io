Apple China launches iPhone battery replacement service

by ygzb321 on 2009-10-23 16:14:07

According to the content displayed on Apple's official website, Apple has recently launched a paid battery replacement service for iPhones out of warranty. The cost is 710 yuan RMB and it takes three working days.

Previously, on July 8th, Apple China posted Chinese information stating that "if an iPhone only needs repair due to reduced battery storage capacity, Apple can help resolve the issue but will charge a fee for repairing the iPhone."

Everything is still in the planning stages

This plan is exclusively for users who need to replace their phone batteries, and does not include users whose phones are damaged and require repairs. All iPhones damaged due to accidents or improper use cannot enjoy this service. In addition, the cost of replacing batteries for domestic iPhones is slightly higher than Apple's usual pricing due to tariffs.

A relevant PR person from Apple, interviewed by C114 in Shanghai, said that this service is still in the planning stage and currently cannot provide services to domestic iPhone users. Regarding whether gray market phones can also enjoy this service, which is a common concern among consumers, the person stated that it has not been finally determined and further confirmation is needed for detailed situations. According to relevant statistics, the capacity of the domestic gray market for iPhones has reached millions of units.

Providing paid services for products out of warranty is not Apple's original idea. Prior to this, mobile giant Nokia also launched a corresponding service, but Nokia clearly stated that this service would only be provided to legitimate domestic phones.

Battery issues have become a major problem for Apple's iPhones

The battery problem with iPhones is an old issue, mainly due to the special design of the iPhone battery where users cannot replace the battery themselves. If the battery is severely worn out, it needs to be sent to Apple for repair.

Previously, in the U.S., there were users who filed a lawsuit in the Federal District Court of Oakland, California, claiming that iPhone batteries would be scrapped after about 300 charge-discharge cycles, and the usage time of a battery was usually less than a year. The related support service fees for replacing an iPhone battery were as high as over $100, and Apple did not inform users of this situation beforehand.

Subsequently, Apple specifically opened a column on its website to tell consumers how to extend the life of an iPhone battery. "If used and maintained properly, an iPhone battery can still hold 80% of its charge after 400 complete charge-discharge cycles," Apple's website claimed. "When a fully charged battery no longer meets your needs, you can consider replacing the battery."

Apple China reminded that the repair process will erase all data on the iPhone. Please synchronize your iPhone with iTunes to back up contacts, photos, email account settings, text messages, and more, which is very important. If the repair process causes loss of iPhone information, Apple will not be responsible. Additionally, Apple does not provide any data transfer services.
