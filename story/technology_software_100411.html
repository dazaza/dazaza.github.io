First, let's get familiar with the software. Open an existing template project to see how static images can become dynamic ones! We chose a waterfall pool; we can see that the water areas in the image are painted over with different brushes. Click on automatic preview - wow, the water starts flowing~

How is this achieved? Take a look at the taskbar on the left. Let's first look at the water - there are four brushes to choose from, and for each one, we can specifically adjust the water effect. The left mouse button is for drawing, while the right button is for erasing. Go back to the image and make some minor adjustments, then click on preview again - can you see some changes?

Is just having flowing water not enough for a dynamic effect? Don't worry, you can also set the weather and sound! You can choose rain or snow, and even select the wind speed and the number of rain or snow particles. Whether you want to transform your photo into a heavy snowfall like goose feathers or a drizzle is entirely up to you!

Finally, you can save the image as a screensaver, exe file, avi video, or gif image~
