Chongqing's Political and Legal Affairs Secretary says that the anti-mafia campaign requires the full cooperation of the public security, procuratorial and judicial departments.

by king3h0a on 2012-03-07 15:10:02

Yesterday afternoon, National People's Congress representative and Secretary of Chongqing Political and Legal Affairs Commission, Mr. Liu Guanglei, indicated in an interview that the anti-mafia campaign is not something that can be operated by an individual or a single department; it requires the full cooperation of the political and legal system. Currently, the anti-mafia campaign is proceeding routinely, and there is confidence to continue maintaining the high level of security in Chongqing.

In addition, Standing Committee member of the 11th National People's Congress, Deputy Director of the Environment and Resources Protection Committee, and the first mayor after Chongqing became a municipality, Mr. Pu Haiqing, criticized bank monopolies during a group discussion, believing that this is not conducive to national competition and the development of the real economy.

□ Anti-Mafia

Not something one person can operate

Liu Guanglei indicated that the current anti-mafia campaign in Chongqing is at a routine stage. [As soon as there are signs, we act; as soon as we discover, we act.] Additionally, an effective prevention mechanism must be established, otherwise, criminal forces could grow. He emphasized that the anti-mafia campaign cannot be carried out by an individual or a single department but requires the full cooperation of the public security, procuratorial, and judicial systems, and also needs the support of the broad masses of the people.

Currently, the morale of the public security officers is very high, and all work is being carried out without slackening. Liu Guanglei has great confidence in the current status of Chongqing's political and legal teams. [They will strive to do what they should do.]

□ Security

Chongqing ranks first in two indicators

Regarding Chongqing's security issues, Liu Guanglei listed two indicators, proving the city's currently good security situation. The first is the public safety perception evaluation index done by a third-party institution, which increased by 1.9 points last year compared to the previous year, reaching a total score of 96.58, ranking first nationwide.

The second is the comprehensive social management ranking made by the Central Comprehensive Management Office in 2010. After evaluating more than 70 indicators, Chongqing ranked first nationwide. [This is the first time; in the past, places like Jiangsu, Zhejiang, Beijing, and Shanghai were often the centers.]

Regarding this year's security situation, Liu Guanglei expressed confidence in continuing to maintain it.

□ Household Registration Reform

Key is to ensure livelihood issues

As a pioneer city for household registration reform for migrant workers, in the past one and a half years, more than 3.2 million people in Chongqing have changed their household registration. Liu Guanglei stated that initially, there was concern that the influx of population into the city might bring instability to society, but after one and a half years, no significant problems have been found. He explained that this is because the livelihood issues of the population whose household registration has changed have been well resolved, not only by establishing numerous micro-enterprises to solve employment problems, but also by building public rental housing to address housing issues. In addition, the original residents and new populations have maintained harmonious relations. [This has all been incorporated into the overall arrangements for strengthening and innovating social management.]

□ Comprehensive Governance

Large number of personnel resolving conflicts at the grassroots level

Liu Guanglei revealed that Chongqing's multi-dimensional comprehensive governance office tasks cover all streets and towns, and a large number of personnel have been dispatched to the grassroots level to resolve conflicts for the people.

According to statistics from the beginning of this year, the number of petitions has decreased significantly compared to 2008. The number of abnormal visits to Beijing has been ranked in the top 20 nationwide for nearly 40 months, dropping to the top 30 since the second half of last year. Additionally, since April 2007, there has been no occurrence of a major traffic accident resulting in the death of more than 10 people at once.
