Working Principle and Maintenance Method of Flotation Machines

by hx515317 on 2012-02-28 13:34:26

Principle of operation of the flotation machine: After the pulp and reagents are thoroughly mixed, they are fed into the bottom of the tank in the first chamber of the flotation machine. When the impeller rotates, a negative pressure forms in the wheel cavity, causing the pulp from the bottom and inside of the tank to enter the mixing zone through the lower suction port and upper suction port of the impeller respectively. Air also enters the mixing zone along the air guide sleeve, where the pulp, air, and reagents mix. Under the action of the centrifugal force of the impeller, the mixed pulp enters the mineralization zone, where the air forms bubbles and is crushed, making full contact with coal particles and forming mineralized bubbles. Under the action of the stator and turbulence plates, these bubbles are evenly distributed across the cross-section of the tank and move upward into the separation zone, where they enrich to form a foam layer that is discharged by the scraper mechanism, forming refined coal foam. Coal particles at the bottom of the tank that have not been mineralized will be remixed, mineralized, and separated again through the circulation hole and upper suction port. Part of the pulp at the bottom of the tank that has not been sucked into the impeller enters the bottom of the second chamber through the middlings box submerged in the pulp, completing the entire process of the first chamber before entering the third chamber. The flotation machine repeats this process until the pulp passes through the last chamber and enters the tailings tank for final discharge.

Maintenance methods for the flotation machine:

1. The working capacity and lifespan of the flotation machine, like other machines, depend on maintenance and keeping all major components functioning normally. All working parts should be carefully inspected every 3-4 months, especially during the first few months of operation for new flotation machines when inspections should be more frequent.

2. Only skilled personnel should carry out inspections.

3. To promptly replace worn parts and reduce downtime, it is essential to keep a full set of spare parts in stock at the warehouse. Determine the usage period and reserve quantity of wear parts based on specific circumstances.

4. Ensure that the operators of the flotation machine receive adequate technical guidance or training so that they can become fully familiar with the machine.

5. To prevent dirt and foreign objects from mixing with the oil, use a filter when adding oil. The oil cap should remain sealed while the flotation machine is operating.

6. Before starting the flotation machine, conduct an inspection and coordinate with upstream and downstream processes before initiating the start-up.

7. The start-up sequence for the flotation machine is: switch the upper gate valve, then press the start button.

8. After starting the flotation machine, it is strictly forbidden to touch the rotating parts with your hands or other objects.
