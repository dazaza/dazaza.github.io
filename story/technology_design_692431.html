Futures - China's first futures code theft case has been solved

by yanlaban on 2012-03-06 09:38:17

CSRC Reminds Futures Investors to Enhance Security Awareness

Last Friday, the CSRC informed the media about China's first case of futures password theft. The Fang Chen group stole others' futures trading passwords and transferred the margin through matched trades. After investigation by the public security organs, four people have been criminally detained so far, and one person has been judicially sentenced.

Trojan software stealing numbers, illegal profits exceeding 30,000 yuan

Between November 2010 and January 2011, Fang Chen used trojan software such as "Great White Shark" and "Paul" to steal futures trading passwords from multiple investors' computers. In order to appropriate others' futures margins, Fang Chen colluded with Peng Yong and others to borrow Wang You-sheng's, Feng San-bao's, and Xie Feng's ID cards to open futures accounts at a futures company branch in Changsha. They then took advantage of the inactive nature of futures forward contracts and conducted multiple matched trades of forward contracts between controlled futures accounts and victim accounts, causing losses to the victims' accounts while making profits on the actual controlled accounts. After making profits, they quickly transferred funds and withdrew cash.

According to a relevant official from the CSRC department, on December 1, 2010, Fang Chen and others used Xie Feng's account to conduct matched trades with two victim accounts, including Mr. Tu, earning 7,502.4 yuan; on December 6, they used Feng San-bao's account to conduct matched trades with victim Pan Mou's account, earning 9,304 yuan; on January 13, 2011, they used Wang You-sheng's account to conduct matched trades with three victim accounts, including Mr. Wang, earning 19,060 yuan. "After completing the matched trades, Fang Chen and others could immediately transfer the margin to their bank accounts and quickly withdraw the money. On average, it only took them 30 minutes from the start of the matched trade to withdrawing the money and leaving."

After preliminary investigations, the CSRC initially locked onto several suspects, including Fang Chen, and immediately handed over the case to the public security organs. On February 23, 2011, Wang You-sheng was arrested by the public security organs, followed by the arrest of four other criminal suspects, including Fang Chen, Peng Yong, Xiong Chang, and Xie Feng. According to the Xiazhen District People's Court of Hangzhou, Wang You-sheng provided his futures account for Fang Chen to engage in criminal activities and received 1,000 yuan of stolen money, which was determined as theft. He was sentenced to one year in prison and fined 1,000 yuan. Other criminal suspects have also been prosecuted.

Reminding investors to carefully protect their passwords

The aforementioned official stated that recently, the CSRC has investigated several cases of futures password theft. In these cases, the criminal suspects often obtain the accounts and passwords of futures customers through various means, conducting matched trades of futures contracts in opposite directions but at the same price between their own controlled accounts and the victims' accounts without the victims' knowledge. By high selling and low buying, they transfer the funds within the victims' accounts and seek improper benefits.

Criminal suspects mainly use three ways to steal investors' account passwords: First is trojan password theft. The Fang Chen group used this method, exploiting website vulnerabilities to upload password-stealing trojans to websites to achieve the purpose of stealing others' accounts and passwords. Second is eavesdropping password theft, where criminal suspects listen in on customer conversations or peek at the accounts and passwords recorded by customers. Third is occupational password theft, where some investors entrust professionals to operate their futures accounts and do not change their passwords after the entrustment ends. These professionals then illegally trade the account passwords to others.

"The CSRC solemnly reminds futures investors to enhance their security awareness, carefully protect their trading passwords and modify them irregularly, be vigilant against network trojans and other hacker programs, do not disclose your password to others or let them peek, and pay attention to the risks when entrusting others to trade your account. If futures investors find that their accounts have been stolen, please promptly report to the CSRC or file a complaint with the public security organs." The official emphasized that the CSRC will further increase monitoring and punishment efforts and resolutely crack down on illegal acts such as futures password theft according to law.
