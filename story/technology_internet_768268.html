On the tablet, we need a WeChat reader.

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 19:51:35

WeChat has evolved from a simple communication tool into an integrated information platform that combines news and content consumption. With its massive user base, WeChat has endless possibilities. As micro-payments are integrated into WeChat, we will eventually transition from consuming content to purchasing goods.

Besides being a communication tool, WeChat is also an excellent reading platform.

Nowadays, when our parents' generation shares various motivational stories on their Moments and groups, as a group of people who are highly sensitive to information, we also yearn for the latest updates on WeChat public accounts and are willing to share them.

I have more than once hoped that WeChat could be adapted for use on an iPad. While simply stretching an iPhone app to fit a tablet's larger screen can勉强 meet the needs for reading, it is far from satisfactory for someone like me who has "experience obsession."

Why doesn't WeChat launch a tablet client? There is already a satisfactory answer on Zhihu. WeChat only supports one device login at a time, aiming to become a center for information sending and receiving, gathering personal information streams that must always be by the user's side, which only a mobile phone can achieve.

In an interview with Zhang Xiaolong, we see his team's dedication to WeChat:

"We indeed don't prioritize the iPad version because iPads are mostly used under WiFi conditions. If users register for WeChat using an iPad, they may not receive messages immediately, resulting in a poor experience and losing the characteristic of always being online. This does not mean we won't create an iPad version; if we do, it will be after we've thoroughly considered why we should, rather than just adding another platform terminal."

However, my desire for an iPad version of WeChat has not ceased. What I value on tablets is not necessarily WeChat's communication function...

As mentioned at the beginning of the article, WeChat is not merely a communication tool but also currently the hottest content reading platform. The countless self-media accounts, official accounts, and friend forwards on WeChat have led to an explosion of information, reinforcing WeChat's crucial role as a source of information.

Spending a lot of time reading on a small screen makes my eyes crave a larger one. Is there an iPad version of WeChat reader that aggregates articles from my Moments and official account feeds, allowing only simple operations such as reading, forwarding, and liking, thus creating a pure content reader?

It is important to note that while modern urbanites engage more in fragmented reading, a larger screen facilitates better content layout. Larger fonts, more comfortable paragraph spacing, and line spacing all enhance the reading experience.

Aside from simple content reading, leveraging WeChat's account system is this reader's advantage. Today's digital content reading methods emphasize interaction between people and content, as well as interactions between people based on content. Therefore, Medium changed the traditional commenting method, and Zaker introduced an account system. An iPad WeChat reader would naturally be a socialized reading platform.

Really, I hope someone can develop a WeChat reader specifically designed for tablets. If Zhang Xiaolong's team insists that mobile phones are temporarily the only platform for WeChat communication, designing a WeChat reader for tablets or providing a WeChat content interface would certainly be a good idea.

The current WeChat is truly more than just a communication tool.

On a side note, there are already some reading apps that provide WeChat public account interfaces, but the experience remains at the level of subscribing to RSS feeds, lacking significant social elements. These third-party readers do not adapt the information presentation according to the dynamics of the public accounts I actually follow, let alone the most important source of information in social networks: friends' forwards.

Another side note: With the launch of the WeChat PC client, many netizens express hope for WeChat's multi-platform deployment. However, similar to the previous web version of WeChat, the current large-screen WeChat still pairs your phone with a keyboard via QR code scanning, still far from the expected separate login.
