I. {Rain on a Leaky Roof}

The VIP ward at Benevolence Hospital.

By now, the first light of dawn had begun to appear. Yin Yutang lay in bed, stubble growing faintly on his lips, looking much more haggard than just the night before. I mustered my strength, handed him a glass of water, and consoled him: "The doctor said there's no serious damage to your hand; it didn't affect the tendons or bones. It'll be fine after a few months... Here, drink some water, your lips are all dry."

As I said this, I suddenly remembered that night when Du Chenzheng forcibly kissed me and commented on how dry my lips were… My heart sank with sorrow, and my hand loosened slightly, almost letting go of the cup.

But Yin Yutang quickly grabbed hold of the cup before it fell, looking apologetic as he said, "I'm sorry for making you worry."

His eyelashes were still long and distinct, his irises shimmering like they held water, even though he looked more worn out, his side profile was still breathtakingly beautiful. But since when had things changed between us? Through so many people and events, we became closer yet further away.

At this moment, the regret in his eyes made me feel even sadder. I placed the cup on the bedside table, staring straight at the rippling water inside, and said, "Actually, it's me who owes you an apology..." My voice dropped lower, "In fact, I'm just like Bai Xiaodie – I've fallen for Du Chenzheng..."

I felt remorseful and somewhat confused, speaking incoherently, "Later, I thought about it – maybe my insistence on being with you, thinking I liked you, was actually to escape my feelings for Du Chenzheng? I don't know why I've fallen for him, why I like him?" I felt frustrated, like a child who's done something wrong, but I knew these facts couldn't be changed by anyone.

Yin Yutang suddenly reached out and covered my lips, showing exhaustion on his face. He shook his head and said, "That's enough, Xinyong, stop talking."

The room fell silent, the morning light streaming through the window illuminating the entire space. Yin Yutang's face was pale, his side profile still handsome like jade, so beautiful it seemed like he would shatter at any moment. Tears streamed down my face uncontrollably. Why had my life turned into this? Why did I have the heart to hurt someone so beautiful and innocent as Yin Yutang?

Just then, Yin Yutang suddenly pulled me into his arms, his chin resting on my shoulder, giving off an unreal sensation. His voice carried a hint of frustration as he whispered in my ear, "Do you think I haven't considered letting you go? When you look at Du Chenzheng with that kind of gaze, do you think my heart doesn't ache? What can I do? Xinyong, I simply can't let you go..."

I closed my eyes, tears falling in succession like beads from a broken string, unable to stop.

Yin Yutang gently patted my back, as if consoling a lost kitten. He said, "Xinyong, no matter where you end up or whom you marry, I will always wait for you right here... Waiting until you turn around and tell me that among all the worldly distractions, you only wish to be a bystander – but with me."

II. {End of Summer}

Just then, there were hurried knocks on the door. Before either of us could respond, a middle-aged man dressed in a black suit had already pushed the door open. I recognized him as one of the servants who often attended to Grandpa Yu, having served my father for many years. Everyone called him Uncle Cheng.

Before I could ask, he stepped forward and whispered in my ear, "Miss, it's bad news! Grandpa Yu has fallen ill! It's a stroke, and he's been rushed to the hospital!"

"What?" I was stunned. Just a few days ago, he had been full of energy. How could he have suffered a stroke so suddenly? Instinctively, I sensed something was amiss. I hurriedly asked, "What happened to my father? Who was with him when he collapsed? Which hospital is he in now, and is there anyone guarding him?"

Uncle Cheng didn’t answer immediately. Instead, he stepped out of the room and glanced around the corridor, then returned and closed the door carefully. With a grave expression, he said, "Grandpa Yu is in this very hospital, but all the guards are from the Dragon Tiger Hall. Du Chenzheng won't let us see him! When he collapsed, he was with Chen Lisha, but she denies everything, and no one knows what really happened!" Uncle Cheng’s age was similar to Grandpa Yu’s, and his worried expression highlighted his old age. He patted my hand and regretfully said, "Miss, I disagreed with Grandpa Yu spending time with that woman. But he's the master and I'm the servant, so there wasn't much I could say. Now, for Grandpa Yu, only his own daughter can be trusted..."

I sighed, realizing how misfortune seems to pile up. Just after our fallout with Du Chenzheng, this happens. Actually, I had known all along that Du Chenzheng wouldn't stay subordinate forever. I just didn't expect this day to come so soon.

"Uncle Cheng, what should we do now?" Seeing his panic, I consoled him, "Don't worry too much. For the time being, they probably won't dare to harm my father."

Uncle Cheng glanced at Yin Yutang, hesitating to speak.

I understood and said, "Yin Yutang is one of us. You can speak freely without hesitation." Yin Yutang looked at me, warmth passing through his eyes.

Uncle Cheng then spoke, saying, "Grandpa Yu once mentioned foreseeing the future and leaving a contingency plan for you, Miss. There's a document he signed, kept in the bottom drawer of the study. It states that after his retirement, all of his assets including cash, real estate, and the Green Cloud Gang will be left to you."

Uncle Cheng's face showed deep concern, "Miss, remember, no matter what, you are the heir of the Green Cloud Gang! While Du Chenzheng isn't wary of you yet, hurry home and retrieve that document... You must keep hold of that paper tightly until Grandpa Yu wakes up! Otherwise, all the wealth Grandpa Yu worked for might fall into someone else's hands!"

On the way back to the Yu residence, the sky hung low, marking the waning summer. The occasional sound of vehicles passing by rang clear, making the city seem even quieter.

Pushing open the door to the study, perhaps due to my state of mind, it no longer felt grand and luxurious. Without turning on the lights, I relied on the dim light filtering through the golden gauze curtains to make my way to the desk near the large bookshelf. Following Uncle Cheng’s instructions, I searched the last drawer on the left, but found nothing.

Just then, footsteps sounded outside the door, one of them making the "taptap" sound of high heels. Instinctively, I hid behind the curtain, peeking toward the small living room in front of the writing desk.

A woman dressed in a tight gold-yellow cheongsam threw her handbag onto the sofa, sat down with her legs crossed, and scolded, "Big Brother Zheng, are you blaming me?"

The tall figure following closely behind quietly sat on the opposite large sofa. His face was hidden in shadow, his expression unclear. However, his eyes remained piercing, shining like cold stars in the blurred light. After a pause, he said, "Lisha, such impulsiveness isn't like you."

This woman was Chen Lisha. Hiding in the dark, I couldn’t help but take a closer look at her.

Chen Lisha stared at Du Chenzheng for a while, the atmosphere subtly changing, seemingly softer. She stood up and sat next to Du Chenzheng, softly saying, "Isn't it all for you, Big Brother Zheng? You know it." She wrapped her arms around his neck, her white hands gleaming like lotus roots in the darkness, as she looked at his face and said, "Back then, I helped you without hope of return, waiting foolishly for her. I schemed to marry Grandpa Yu, but not only were you ungrateful, you got angry with me... So I acted out and went on a honeymoon with Grandpa Yu abroad, but I missed you even more in my heart..." She leaned her head on his shoulder, saying, "Actually, I also wanted to be with you sooner... Once Grandpa Yu falls, Shanghai will be ours. We won't have to constantly guard against him finding out about us anymore. Besides, Miss Yu Xin has grown stronger, becoming smarter than before and gaining favor with Elder Duan from Nanjing... So, it's better to act sooner rather than later."

It was perhaps my imagination, but when Chen Lisha mentioned my name, Du Chenzheng's gaze flickered, as if touched by something. Hiding behind the gauze, I only felt bitter. It turns out their relationship was like this.

Du Chenzheng didn’t push her away, sighing lightly with a tone as indifferent as discussing the weather, "Since it's done, I won't blame you anymore. Grandpa Yu did have some grace towards you in the past, so let him live."

Chen Lisha seemed to be throwing a tantrum, saying, "No! I won't allow it!" She hugged Du Chenzheng tighter, cooing, "You spent so many years on him, so it's only fair he pays you back. Every time I think about those days I spent with him, I feel sick. He must die to satisfy my hatred."

Her words were casual, yet they concerned a life, especially that of her former husband. I was shocked, wondering how this woman could be so cruel? Suddenly, clumps of dirt fell from above. Looking up, I saw the horizontal bar supporting the curtain swaying precariously and with a "whoosh," it fell.

I had no choice but to step aside, moving to the empty spot beside the window, fully exposed to the two of them. Suppressing my panic, I maintained a blank expression as I looked at them, thinking, "Is today the day fate abandons me? To encounter the curtain falling entirely is quite unlucky."

Du Chenzheng stared at me for a long while, his lips curling slightly into a smile with unclear meaning. He said, "Miss, have you been here for long?"

I turned my head to look at the uncovered window, the shadows of trees swaying under the moonlight. There was no need to hide anymore, so I said, "Yes, I've been here for a while. I heard everything you said."

Chen Lisha walked over in high heels, "taptap," her chin raised as she looked down on me, her beautiful face filled with undisguised schadenfreude. She said, "Oh my, isn't this Miss Yu Xin? Sneaking around here like a ghost... truly hilarious! (Students playing on phones during class shouldn't read this.) Recommend... What are you looking for?"

Only then did I clearly see her appearance - almond eyes and red lips, even prettier in person than on posters.

I glanced at her and said, "This is my home. I can stay wherever I want. What sneaking around? In contrast, someone sits openly on the sofa, spewing secrets that shouldn't see the light of day!"

"You shut up!" Chen Lisha slapped me, the pain stinging my cheek. She grabbed my hair and said, "Yu Xin, I've never liked you! Do you think you're still the Miss of the Yu family?"

I endured the pain, coldly looking at her and taunting, "Look at yourself now, behaving like a fishwife. Aren't you afraid of scaring your lover?"

I glanced coldly at Du Chenzheng, then turned back to her, saying, "My father is at least your husband. You talk about wanting his death so casually, like a black widow spider. Who would dare to have you in the future?"

"Bitch! Can't get pearls from a pig's mouth!" Chen Lisha tightened her grip, pulling my hair harder, raising her hand again to slap me. I grabbed her wrist and twisted it, saying, "Addicted to hitting, huh? Or are you upset because I hit a sore spot?"

Chen Lisha's anger intensified, suddenly releasing my hair and drawing out a revolver from the drawer, pointing the cold gun at my head, saying, "Yu Xin, I will definitely kill you here today!"

I froze, terrified inside, but stubbornly said, "Then try! Even if I become a ghost, I won't let you off!"

Perhaps it was strange even to myself, why I was so angry, why I disliked her so much? Was it merely because she betrayed the Yu family, or because of her relationship with Du Chenzheng...

Chen Lisha "hmpfed," ready to pull the trigger. But just then, Du Chenzheng suddenly grabbed the gun from her hand, saying, "Lisha, that's enough."

Chen Lisha was startled, shaking off his hand, her eyebrows arching as she said, "Why are you pleading for her? Recently, I've heard from the servants that you've been close to Yu Xin, is it true?"

Du Chenzheng didn't answer, continuing to hold her hand and said, "Lock her up for a while. I'll deal with her later."

A flame of unwillingness and confusion flashed in Chen Lisha's eyes, but it eventually faded under Du Chenzheng's cold gaze. After a tense moment, she released her hand, glaring at me but sweetly saying, glancing at Du Chenzheng, "Big Brother Zheng, naturally I listen to you."

She harshly threw the gun on the table, looking at me and smirking, "Big Brother Zheng, you're far-sighted. Sending people to burn all the documents in the study beforehand certainly left Miss Yu Xin empty-handed."

My heart sank. Were all the documents burned?

It seemed the Yu family's fortune was truly beyond saving.

III. {White Moonlight}

I was placed under house arrest in my own room. It was a full moon tonight, and the room was brightly lit by the moonlight despite the absence of electricity. Exhausted, I sat alone by the window, wide awake despite my weariness. Too many unexpected events had occurred these past couple of days, leaving me no time to face them or even mourn.

Thinking about my mundane modern life, everything now felt like a dream. Watching the tree shadows sway outside, bathed in silver-white moonlight, I couldn't help but sing a song I used to like in modern times, "White moonlight, somewhere deep within, so bright, yet so cold. Everyone has a sadness, trying to hide, but it only becomes more obvious..."

Perhaps due to the overly sentimental melody, when I sang the last line, I couldn't help but recall the day Du Chenzheng held me in his car and told me about his childhood. Though those memories had healed into scars, I could still see the unknown sorrows of the past reflected in his eyes...

How much I wished he would tell me more about himself, how much I hoped to understand him better... I thought I could get closer, but instead, I moved further away, leading to the situation we are in now.

"This song is beautiful." At that moment, a familiar male voice came from the doorway, making me feel surreal.

I didn't speak, nor did I turn around, but I heard his slow steps approach. His hands rested on my shoulders from behind, saying, "Xinyong, why don't you continue singing for me?"

Startled, I felt a mix of bitterness and anger welling up, transforming into uncontrollable rage in the dark night. I grabbed a vase from the table and turned abruptly, smashing it down, yelling, "Go away! I don't want to see you!"

Maybe subconsciously I didn't want to hurt him, because the vase missed him and shattered on the floor.

There was no trace of panic or surprise in Du Chenzheng's eyes. Standing slightly to the side, his hair and face illuminated by frosty moonlight, he chuckled softly, "Are you sure you don't want to see me?"

My heart ached, and I grabbed another item from the table to throw at him. Du Chenzheng dodged each one until there was nothing left on the table... Still unsatisfied, I thought, since I've already been labeled a shrew in his eyes, I might as well play the part. I stood up and rushed to him, punching his chest...

But my punches were weak and ineffective. Du Chenzheng simply endured them, and after a while, when I grew tired, he caught my hands and gently pulled me into his embrace, soothing me like a child, "Alright, stop acting up."

Stunned, I struggled to break free, but he tightened his grip, saying, "If you keep this up, I'll really stop visiting you."

Frustrated, I looked up and shouted, "Who wants you to visit me? Du Chenzheng, I'd rather never see you for the rest of my life! I hate you, hate you, hate you!"

Out of breath, I paused to catch my breath and shouted again, "You plotted with Chen Lisha to seize my father's property, deceiving and manipulating me. Even a hundred 'hate's wouldn't be enough!"

He remained silent for a moment. The night was quiet, the moonlight like water, and the room was covered in white frost.

After a long while, he only held me, his chin resting on my head. There was a faint scent, and he gently played with my hair, suddenly whispering in my ear, "I'm sorry."

I was taken aback, my heart skipping a beat. A myriad of emotions flooded my mind, indescribable. Staring blankly at him, I found him looking back at me, his pitch-black eyes suddenly softening.

"Uh..." I wanted to say something, but before I could, Du Chenzheng had already leaned down and kissed me deeply. His tongue was warm and soft, both familiar and tender...

I wanted to resist, but I couldn't. After a while, I instinctively wrapped my arms around his neck and responded to his kiss... His large hand caressed my back, the heat of his palm seeping through the cheongsam into my skin... He began kissing my neck, his breathing gradually becoming heavier. He slowly undid the first two buttons of my blouse...

I was getting frantic, using the last bit of rationality to stop his hand, asking, "Last time you said it was an accident... Then what about now? Du Chenzheng, what exactly do you see me as?"

He looked at me intently, a trace of confusion in his eyes, and said, "I don't know." Then he lightly kissed my earlobe and said, "All I know is that at this moment, I really want to be with you."

I was stunned, just staring into his eyes, feeling myself gradually drowning in his deep, ink-like pupils...

Du Chenzheng picked me up and carried me to the gilded bed draped with English lace curtains. I circled my arms around his neck, deeply buried in his embrace, recalling how I had mistakenly entered his room and how I inadvertently let him into my heart...

Clumsily and actively, I kissed his lips. Du Chenzheng paused, gently laying me down on the bed, passionately and skillfully responding to my kiss... I closed my eyes, forcing myself to suppress the tangled heartache beneath the sweetness.

Truly, how foolish... I felt like a fool. But I couldn't help it.

If loving is destined to last only a moment, I volunteer for him to remember this moment of me.

Outside the window, the sky was beginning to lighten with the first hints of dawn.

He had already awakened. Clutching the edge of the quilt, I felt like a fool. Why did I fall into his trap of tenderness time and time again, unable to extricate myself, unwilling to awaken?

"Do... do you like me?" Although I knew it was foolish, I couldn't help but ask him.
