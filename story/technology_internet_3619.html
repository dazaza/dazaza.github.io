This article provides 14 methods that can significantly increase your blog’s page views. The size of your blog's page views is a critical factor for advertisers in deciding whether to place ads, so those who wish to achieve success through blogging might find this useful. Small Tip:

To avoid confusion, "Blog" below refers to the blog site or page, while "blogger" refers to the person who writes the blog.

People often discuss articles about "how to do a Blog," focusing on increasing the number of visitors to a blog. However, for some bloggers, another type of statistic is equally important — page views (in English, Page Views, abbreviated as PV). Most statistical packages include two aspects: "visitors" (or unique visitors), which count the number of visitors, and PV values, which count the number of times each blog post is read (also referred to as popularity). The number of pages visited by each visitor varies across different blogs and deserves consideration. The reasons why bloggers want to increase PV values generally fall into two categories:

- **Attraction**: Pages with high popularity attract more accidental visitors who are likely to return.

- **Revenue**: Pages with high popularity bring continuous advertisements (meaning more income).

Whether or not one wants to increase PV values depends on the blogger's own positioning, with each having their own priorities based on their goals for their blog. If you're looking to quickly increase your PV values, the following 14 tips will be very helpful.

1. **Interlink Articles**

The simplest way to increase PV values is to link related articles within your own Blog. Although some may dismiss this method, readers appreciate it. Adding links to other relevant themes within an article can be labor-intensive but enhances the visitor's reading experience, making it worthwhile. Links to similar content from others can deepen the depth of your written articles.

2. **Highlight Related Articles**

Adding article links doesn't have to mean simply creating a "related topics" list at the end of an article. As you browse this article (note: referring to the original online version), you'll notice many yellow highlighted texts throughout, which are examples of links to related topics. In WordPress, there's a plugin called "related posts" that automatically finds relevant topics based on your content. You can also manually run "related posts" to simply add related topic links at the end of your articles.

3. **Provide Newsletters**

Publishing update notifications via newsletters can quickly increase PV values. Subscribers to my ProBlogger newsletter know that each issue includes a "hot posts" section, collecting the top 5 most popular blog articles from the site that week. Many people use this method to stay updated on the latest blog posts without needing to log into the website. This increases both the site's traffic and PV values. Other automated email notification methods for updates are also effective in increasing PV values.

4. **Highlight Important Articles in Prominent Positions**

Recently, I've noticed that many friends visiting ProBlogger for the first time tend to browse the articles I've highlighted in the three menu bars at the top of the site. Here, I predefine the purpose of these menu bars and how they help visitors find key articles, making the Blog more attractive.

5. **"Best Of" Pages**

In the top menu bar of your site, it's best to have links to pages like "Best of Something" or "Editor's Picks." A good example is "Top 20 Posts at ProBlogger," which I understand many first-time visitors to ProBlogger start with.

6. **Write Series Articles**

Writing series articles is a highly effective way to increase PV values. First, writing a series keeps readers engaged on your Blog for longer periods because they want to know what your next topic will be. Second, even after the series ends, you can cleverly link them in other related articles, allowing those who enjoyed the series to still find it. My "Blogging for Beginners series" is a good example, which I highlight prominently on the site, knowing that many new readers will read it.

7. **Expand Your Front Page Headlines**

If you write a very long article, you can use the "read more" feature to direct people to a separate page to read it. While I don’t prefer doing this with all articles (it can be annoying), for very long articles, it helps manage your homepage better and increases PV values if used moderately. However, I believe some blogs may overuse this function.
