Please cherish your marriage.

by zhouzhou56 on 2011-12-22 16:21:54

As the saying goes, "a man should not enter a wrong industry and a woman should not marry a wrong husband." In fact, in reality, men and women are the same. Women fear marrying the wrong person, and men also fear marrying the wrong wife.

Adults often warn children to study hard because knowledge can change fate. We should also warn men and women who are about to enter the marriage hall that marriage can also change fate.

Whether it's men or women, choosing what kind of partner is equivalent to choosing what kind of life, because whether your life is happy or tragic mostly depends on the person you hold hands with.

In history, many poets and writers have written poems and articles filled with romanticism about love and marriage. However, these splendid writings are mostly about others, while they remain silent about their own marriages, because their marriages do not become extraordinary due to their romanticism. These romantic talents lavish ink on singing praises for others' marriages, merely to pour out their pursuit and yearning for a good marriage.

Marriage is like a cup of wine, or a cup of tea. Intensity is not as good as simplicity, and simplicity is not as good as longevity, because marriage will consume love.

Some people say, "The one who loves you may not be the one who marries you, and the one you love, you may not necessarily marry him." Yes, some marriages are always misaligned due to shallow destiny and left with regrets because of the confusion of the matchmaker. The person who shows deep affection towards you, despite promising solemnly: "to grow old with you and never leave or abandon you," may not really spend a lifetime with you. On the contrary, the person who is speechless, not good at talking, silently cares for you, holds your hand when you are about to leave this world, and quietly tells you "we will still be together in the next life" is your true partner.

It's really not easy for a person in this life. Life is not easy, and neither is marriage. Many people say they will hold hands, but end up not being able to stay together. Marriages miss each other in the confusion of love, while some people, who have no feelings for each other, end up holding hands, and life is wasted in an inevitable marriage.

Marriage, in a popular sense, is living together. As for whether it lasts, it completely depends on whether the two people can have compatible temperaments, adapt to each other, and tolerate each other. Some people say: "If you have a wonderful marriage, even if the sun and moon are cold, and the mountains and vegetation are all gone, the glow of marriage can still warmly burn in the beginning of memory, in any visible and invisible corners..."

A happy marriage is "sharing the same bed and the same dream," while a tragic marriage is "sharing the same bed but having different dreams." People who love each other may not necessarily be happy or last long when they come together, and people who don't love each other may not necessarily be unhappy or short-lived when they come together. Men usually marry the person they deeply love, while women often marry someone they don't love. In a marriage, as long as one person has love, it is enough, and that person must be the man. Only when the man gives his true love will the woman not leave or abandon him.

We should cherish marriage, because five hundred glances in the previous life bring a fleeting encounter in this life, which already brings great happiness. So, coming together? That must be a very deep bond!
