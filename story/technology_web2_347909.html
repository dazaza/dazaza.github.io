The radio frequency channel at home can actually receive pornographic content. Photo by Wang Daliang, a former reporter of the Southern Metropolis Daily. The incident occurred in the Xinhua Town area of Huadu District near South Road, possibly due to signal interference.

■ Written by: Former Southern Metropolis Daily reporters Zhao Haisheng and Ruan Jianhua, and intern reporter Yang Jun.

Why does a TV station at home broadcast only pornographic videos? This situation has been happening for several years among residents living near South Road in Xinhua Town of Huadu District. Many residents are concerned about the mental health of their children. After investigation, it was discovered that the pornographic content originated from a hotel falsely named "Zhongshou." Residents within a 50-60 meter radius were able to pick up the signal. Staff from the Huadu District Cable Television department said it might be due to signal interference.

Pornographic Content Broadcasted All Night

Due to work reasons, Mr. Ou rented a small apartment less than 10 square meters on South Road. A few weeks ago, while watching TV with his family, they accidentally came across some indecent images. "I found this when I was changing channels; all were pornographic videos. I quickly changed the channel because I didn't want my child to see it."

Mr. Ou explained that these pornographic videos only had visuals without sound, and once turned on, the videos played all night long. "Evenings are also affected; after we finish work and want to watch the news, if we switch to Channel 0, we can see these videos. They play dozens of such films every day." Mr. Ou learned from his neighbors that this situation has been going on for several years, and some households have clearer images.

Concerns About Children's Mental Health

Mr. Ou said, "When watching with my wife, we don’t pay much attention, but our child is only a few years old. If he sees these images, it will affect his mental health." To prevent their child from seeing these images, Mr. Ou and his wife hide the TV remote control after work and place the TV out of reach. Mr. Ou added that today’s children mature early and may easily come into contact with inappropriate material, which could lead to wrong thoughts.

During our interview, other residents also expressed concern. "In the evenings, we don't dare to watch TV, or we make sure the children aren't alone when watching." Neighbor Xiao Liu said, even though his child is already a teenager, for healthy growth, he doesn't allow him to be exposed to such content too early.

Hiding the Remote Control Isn't a Permanent Solution

Residents nearby hope the source can be identified and stopped.

Signal Originates from a Hotel

Mr. Ou said he couldn't figure out why this was happening. "We watch cable TV, so how can there be such content? Moreover, these pornographic videos are broadcast like regular programs, just received on Channel 0."

Since China strictly controls adult content, these signals cannot come from the cable network. Where do they originate? On South Road, there are many small hotels. Neighbors mentioned that after investigating multiple sources, they learned that households within 50-60 meters of one hotel could receive such signals.

This afternoon, when we tested a supermarket TV near Mr. Ou's house and switched to Channel 0, explicit sexual content appeared. Neighbors claimed the signal originated from the hotel. "Someone playing mahjong in the hotel saw the same content on the TV."

Investigation by the Reporter

The Hotel Indeed Broadcasts Pornographic Videos

After receiving complaints from Mr. Ou and other neighbors, last night the reporter conducted an undercover visit to the "Zhongshou" hotel. When asked about card games and accommodation, after paying a 200 yuan deposit, the staff opened a small room divided into three sections: a backroom with an automatic mahjong machine, two beds inside, and a bathroom in front.

Turning on the room TV, pornographic videos were being broadcast on Channels 1 and 7, with clear visuals and sound. "This is what the hotel uses DVDs to play. If you're not comfortable, you can change to another channel," the hotel staff said, explaining that only room occupants would get such "treatment," and the videos played all night long.

To confirm whether the hotel's broadcasts matched what residents received, the reporter split into two groups. Observations showed that the hotel's broadcast matched the signals received by residents, although the latter was slightly blurry. A female hotel staff member confirmed that previously, neighbors had complained about this issue, but she wasn't sure why the signal leaked. "It might be a wiring problem, and not many TVs can pick up the signal."

Legal Analysis

Severe Actions Could Lead to Criminal Charges

Attorney Zhu Yongping from Guangxi Xiaotong Law Firm stated that if it is confirmed that the hotel is responsible, then the hotel has committed the act of broadcasting and disseminating obscene videos, violating relevant public security regulations. The responsible individuals could face fines ranging from 10,000 to 20,000 yuan and administrative detention for 15 days.

Attorney Zhu further noted that since the obscene content has spread to surrounding residents, causing significant social impact, criminal charges could be pursued against the responsible parties based on the severity of the influence.

Cause Investigation

Likely Due to Signal Interference

Subsequently, the reporter consulted a staff member from the Huadu District Cable Television department. He explained that cable TV transmits signals through distribution amplifiers to various users. Each amplifier has two ports: an output port and an input port. The output port delivers normal TV signals to users, while the input port receives external signals. If other signals are mistakenly connected to the input port and fall within the amplifier's passband, they could interfere with other signals.

The staff member added that if the hotel connects its DVD player to the input port of the distribution amplifier, it could cause reverse signal transmission, leading to signal interference. Since the hotel's DVD player needs to provide signals to all rooms, the signal strength should be strong enough to transmit over short distances via the amplifier, allowing nearby users to receive the signal. "We've handled similar cases before, but now without reports, it's difficult to monitor." The staff member suggested either adjusting the signal or reporting the issue to the police.
