Detailed Analysis of the Causes of Female External Itching

by loves5h8 on 2012-02-23 16:15:34

Pruritus of the female genital area not only causes unbearable itching for patients, but can also lead to marital discord. The following are some common causes of pruritus in the external female genitalia:

1. Local skin uncleanliness: Some women use improper methods when using toilet paper, leading to chronic inflammation of the vulvar skin due to prolonged exposure to menstrual blood, vaginal secretions, or even urine, feces, and sweat, which in turn causes pruritus.

2. Vaginal trichomoniasis infection: When the acidity of the vaginal mucosa decreases in women, it reduces lactic acid production, making them more susceptible to trichomonas infections, characterized by foamy vaginal discharge and vulvar pruritus.

3. Fungal infection: Women with diabetes or those who have taken antibiotics for a long time causing normal bacterial flora imbalance are prone to Candida albicans infections, leading to fungal vulvovaginitis. This condition is often associated with cottage cheese-like vaginal discharge and intense pruritus.

4. Parasitic infection: Pinworms mainly affect young girls, but adult women can also be infected. At night, when the anal sphincter relaxes, pinworms crawl out from the rectum to mate and lay eggs around the vulva, stimulating the skin and mucous membranes and causing local pruritus. Scabies caused by mite infections leads to severe external dermatitis, thus causing significant pruritus. Pubic lice cause intense pruritus in areas with pubic hair; small gray-white lice or nits can be found at the root or on the shaft of the pubic hair.

5. Drug rash: Women with allergic constitutions may develop "fixed drug eruption" after taking sulfonamides or other medications, commonly affecting the junction of the skin and mucous membranes of the vulva. Besides local pruritus, erosion and exudation may occur. Using drugs for vaginal irrigation or intravaginal insertion may also cause contact dermatitis if an allergic reaction occurs, leading to vulvar pruritus.

6. Vulvar skin diseases: Tinea cruris (jock itch) can extend to the vulva, causing intense pruritus. Vulvar eczema and neurodermatitis cause even more severe pruritus; the former shows unclear borders with papules, vesicles, erosion, and exudation, while the latter presents with thickened skin due to scratching and lichenification. Vulvar leukoplakia causes pruritus along with vulvar nutritional deficiency and skin atrophy.

7. Viral infection: Genital warts mostly occur on the vaginal wall, cervical os, and vulva in women, accompanied by foul-smelling vaginal discharge. Contagious molluscum located on the vulva often appears as round papules with umbilicated centers, which may also cause vulvar pruritus.
