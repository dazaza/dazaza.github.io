40 Simple Ways to Boost Your Baby's Intelligence

by tuoqing1188 on 2011-08-03 12:42:37

The intellectual development of a baby is a matter that parents are always very concerned about, but it requires a gradual process. Here, we provide 40 methods to enhance a baby's intelligence, hoping to help improve your child's intellect.

One. Enhancing Baby’s Logical Thinking

1. Test Reasoning

Before you and your baby start eating breakfast, before uncovering the box, you can ask your child to guess: "Guess what we're having for breakfast?" "Do you prefer milk or soybean milk?" This method can enhance the child's mathematical logical intelligence.

2. Counting Stairs

Going up and down stairs may make children feel tired and uninterested, but if parents use some interesting methods, not only can the child's intelligence be improved, but also the situation where the child insists on being carried will be greatly reduced. Parents can lead the child to count the steps one by one, enhancing the child's mathematical logical intelligence in the process.

3. Recognizing Roads and Counting Doorplates

When going out with the child, use the time coming back home. On the way home, because there is plenty of time and the child is usually more relaxed, let the child distinguish and memorize different roads, using landmarks to recognize roads, improving the child's observation ability.

On the way home, guide the child to count doorplates, distinguishing which side is even numbers and which side is odd numbers, reinforcing the child's number concept, which is helpful for improving mathematical logical intelligence.

4. Finding Program Timetables

In the evening when watching TV, what time is the child's favorite program broadcast? You can find the time on the TV schedule and teach the child how to recognize time. This can cultivate the child's mathematical ability, and the child might unconsciously learn some Chinese characters as well.

5. Counting Toys Together

Before bedtime, have the child tidy up their toys. Although it's their own business, parents can assist from the side, helping the child acquire new skills. Initially, parents can count the toys loudly with the child, putting them away after counting each one. Through imitation, the child will know the order of counting, and later, they can count and put away the toys themselves. This can cultivate the child's mathematical intelligence.

6. Looking at Product Labels

When shopping, parents and children can look at product labels together, letting the child know that every product has its price tag. Then calculate with the child, such as buying two items or having 10 yuan, how many things can be bought... This enables the child to initially understand the concept of money and enhances their mathematical intelligence.

7. Learning Math through Clothing Adjustments

When it gets warmer, reduce clothing; in winter, add layers accordingly. During this process, count the pieces of clothing with the child, like wearing one shirt in summer, three layers (underwear + sweater + coat) in autumn, and four layers (underwear + sweater + coat + cold-weather gear) in winter. The child will connect weather conditions with clothing needs, enhancing their self-awareness and understanding of nature through specific life experiences.

While dressing or undressing, let the child button up the clothes themselves, either from top to bottom or vice versa, enhancing their mathematical logical intelligence.

Two. Enhancing Social Interaction Skills

8. Greeting People

Teach the child to greet acquaintances they meet and say hello. When greeting, teach the child to use appropriate polite titles, such as "uncle," "aunt," "grandma," etc., and also teach them polite phrases like "Hello" or "Are you going to work?" This can enhance the child's interpersonal intelligence.

9. Letting the Child Introduce Themselves

When visiting someone's house, let the child give a simple self-introduction. They don't need to say too much; if shy, just introduce their name and age. This can both cultivate the child's social interaction skills and enhance their introspective intelligence.

10. Visiting Houses with Children of Similar Ages

Parents sometimes should choose the right places to visit. Visiting houses with children can create opportunities for social interaction. Playing with other kids helps develop group awareness and cooperation.

11. Letting the Child Help Out

Parents can pretend to be in difficulty and ask the child for help, like wringing out a cloth. Through labor, the child can establish self-service and service-to-others awareness. Parents' requests for help give the child a sense of participation and train their initial cooperativeness, benefiting their interpersonal interactions.

12. Letting the Baby Ask for Directions

Moms can frequently take the child to the street and encourage them to ask for directions. This can enhance their interpersonal intelligence, as asking strangers for directions fully exercises the child's social interaction skills.

Three. Enhancing Spatial Intelligence

13. Recognizing Directions

On the road, teach the child to pay attention to recognizing roads and distinguishing directions, such as using buildings as identification marks. Especially at complex terrains like crossroads, let the child observe all directions—up, down, left, right, front, and back. This enhances the child's spatial intelligence and makes them familiar with roads.

14. Distinguishing East, West, South, North

Teach the child to use surrounding landscapes to recognize roads, such as balconies, tree leaf directions, road signs, etc., to help identify east, west, south, north. This training helps the child's sense of direction and enhances their spatial intelligence.

15. Giving Instructions to the Baby

Mom can let the baby be a little helper, giving appropriate instructions, such as "Help mom get the can of milk inside," or instructing them "Look behind you," "A bit to the left," etc. When speaking to the baby, use words like inside, above, below, in front, behind, topmost, lowest, etc. This enhances the baby's spatial intelligence.

16. Observing Changes in Leaves

Seasonal changes are most evident in plants. In daily life, observe the changes in leaves throughout the four seasons, letting the baby understand seasonal concepts while enhancing their visual-spatial intelligence.

In summer, leaves are emerald green; in autumn, they turn yellow; in winter, they fall off, leaving only lonely branches. But when spring comes, tender green buds sprout again. Parents can take the baby to observe the same tree over a year and explain the reasons to them.

The alternating changes in leaf colors, growth, and decay enhance the child's spatial and natural intelligence within their line of sight.

17. Using Maps to Find Directions

Before traveling, it's best to buy a map of the destination beforehand, or purchase one upon arrival. Don’t underestimate this map—it is an effective tool to enhance the baby's spatial intelligence.

Tell the baby your next goal, let them find your current location and the destination on the map, and determine the correct direction to proceed. Along the way, remind the baby to look at road signs, and after reaching the destination, confirm the target with the map again.

This process, though seemingly complex, is very effective in enhancing the baby's spatial intelligence, direction sense, spatial imagination, and execution abilities.

18. Learning Names and Recognizing Scenery Along the Way

Point to streets, stations, and buildings along the way, telling the child their respective names and construction years. This enhances the child's language intelligence and increases their knowledge.

Let the child observe the natural scenery along the way and introduce it to them, telling them the names, characteristics, functions, seasonal changes, etc., enhancing the child's observational abilities.

Four. Exercising Speaking Abilities

19. Letting the Child Describe Their Favorite Animation Character

Children have a deep impression of their favorite characters. Let the child describe to the mother what their favorite character looks like—is it a duckling or a clumsy bear, what color is its mouth, etc. This cultivates the child's observational and language expression abilities.

20. Singing Nursery Rhymes While Walking

When parents walk with the child, there are many opportunities to increase the child's knowledge. You can give riddles like "A hemp house, red curtains, a white fat man sleeping inside." The child may not guess correctly, but they will be interested in these vivid nursery rhymes, benefiting their language intelligence.

21. Describing Item Characteristics

Mom can hold an item that interests the baby and describe one or multiple features of the item, such as its color (grass-like green), shape and size (small round ball), quantity (two oranges), texture (soft or hard), or sound (loud and noisy TV). You can also let the baby try to describe the item's features.

Through this process, the baby learns to use and organize language, enhancing their language intelligence.

22. Deciding Together What to Buy

During shopping, focus the baby's attention on what you're doing, such as picking bread. Discuss your thoughts with the baby and let them express their opinions, like why to buy this bread—it might be because it looks nice or smells good, etc. Finally, make a decision that satisfies everyone. While expressing their opinions, the baby significantly enhances their logical and language intelligence.

Five. Cultivating Artistic Sensibilities

23. Singing Songs

Let the child sing nursery songs taught by their kindergarten teacher on the road, which can stimulate their emotions since not every child gets the chance to sing alone in kindergarten. If they can sing alone, they will surely become interested in music.

24. Laboring While Listening to Cheerful Music

Do you perform a weekly family-wide cleaning? Have the child join you, listening to cheerful music while cleaning. This raises everyone's labor enthusiasm, making the child feel this is a happy time, helping them develop a love for labor and enhancing their musical intelligence.

Six. Training Observation and Memory Skills

25. Recognizing Vegetables

Let the child recognize vegetables like spinach and bok choy, finding their common points and individual characteristics, enhancing their natural observation intelligence.

26. Observing Color Changes in Vegetables

When mom cooks, let the child practice judging when the vegetables are cooked by observing the color differences between raw and cooked vegetables, training their observational abilities.

27. Observing the Cooking Sequence

When mom cooks, let the baby observe the cooking procedures closely. Afterward, let them recount the steps of a dish, such as whether stir-fried celery with meat is first stir-fried with the meat or the celery, etc. This trains the child's memory and increases their life knowledge.

28. Introducing Nutritional Values

While accompanying the baby during meals, parents can first introduce the nutritional value of each dish and then see how much the child remembers, increasing knowledge and training memory.

29. Drawing Liked Characters

Let the child draw the appearance of their favorite character based on what they see on TV. Since TV images aren't fixed, this not only cultivates the child's observational ability but also trains their memory.

30. Saying Toy Colors

You can let the child first say the color of a certain toy, then say which toys have the same color, teaching the child to recognize colors and cultivating their visual discrimination and observational abilities.

31. Teaching the Child to Find Toys of the Same Material

Teach the child to group toys of different materials, such as plastic toys, plush toys, wooden toys, etc. This cultivates the child's tactile perception and visual discrimination, teaching them how to classify.

32. Recognizing Colors

Parents can seize opportunities to let the child learn to recognize various colors, such as leaf colors—green or yellow—and ask the baby, "What things in our home are black or red?" etc. There are also many colorful flowers outside that can be used to teach the baby to distinguish flower species and colors, such as the diverse varieties and colors of chrysanthemums, which serve as excellent natural teaching materials.

In the process of recognizing colors, the baby's visual intelligence is continuously stimulated, fully utilizing their visual abilities, cultivating their recognition and observational abilities. Try not to hint while the baby is identifying colors; let them observe things around them independently and recognize the sought-after visual objects.

33. Recognizing Sounds

When taking the baby to a natural environment, opportunities to hear natural sounds increase. Parents can tell the baby to listen carefully to certain sounds, such as animal calls, water sounds, wind sounds, thunder sounds, etc., which can enhance the baby's auditory abilities. In urban environments, there are also many sounds, such as motorcycles starting, car horns, fire engine sirens, construction noises, etc., which can stimulate the baby's hearing.

After the baby memorizes these sounds, let them identify the sounds immediately upon hearing them. Stimulating the baby's hearing makes them more perceptive and agile.

34. Guessing Objects by Touch

During outdoor activities, parents can use various materials, shapes, and sizes encountered outdoors to enhance the baby's tactile abilities.

First, let the baby place their hands behind their backs, palms facing up. Parents can place various small objects into the baby's palm, allowing them to describe the feeling of the object and then guess what it is, such as touching a smooth, cold, heavy pebble and saying, "I touched something hard, smooth, cold, and heavy—it must be a stone." Then let the baby check if their guess was correct.

For this type of intelligence-enhancing activity, besides using static objects, animals can also be used. See if the baby can guess what they're touching—a silkworm or an ant.

Seven. Enhancing Introspective Intelligence

35. Deliberately Saying Something Wrong

When talking with the child, deliberately say something wrong that everyone knows is incorrect, or tell a wrong story, such as saying today's sunny weather is bad. Observe the child's reaction, enhancing their introspective and logical intelligence.

37. Expressing Emotions

When the whole family chats, suddenly ask the child to judge whether others in the family are happy and the degree of happiness; or tell a story and let the child practice expressing emotions like joy, anger, sadness, and happiness, enhancing their introspective intelligence.

38. Letting the Baby Participate in Preparations Before Traveling

Before traveling, parents prepare items, but don't think this is solely the adults' responsibility and unrelated to the baby. Allowing the baby to participate in pre-travel preparations is an effective opportunity to enhance their logical and introspective intelligence.

Let the baby think about what items to bring for the trip, maybe something they particularly hope to carry that the parents forgot. During preparation, consider everything from living arrangements to food, encouraging the baby to fully contribute their efforts. This subtly enhances the baby's logical intelligence. Respect the baby's opinions, letting them feel their needs, which effectively cultivates their introspective intelligence.

Eight. Enhancing Physical Abilities

39. Letting the Child Tidy Up Toys

At the beginning, you can have the child help you tidy up, gradually teaching them to do it themselves. Encourage the child when they try, even if they don't do it perfectly. This trains the child's manual dexterity and lets them understand that children should learn to do what they can.

40. Long-Distance Communication

Parents can take the baby to a relatively open grassy area or square. The father, baby, and mother stand apart from each other. The father uses both words and gestures to clearly express a sentence, and the baby mimics the father's actions to pass the message to the mother on the other side.

This activity can exercise the baby's expressive abilities (both verbal and physical expression), enhancing their language intelligence and physical movement intelligence in an interesting game-like activity.
