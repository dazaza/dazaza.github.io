Cook's Top 10 Answers in the Conference Call: iOS Embedded in Cars Becomes a Key Focus of Business

by anonymous on 2013-07-29 18:23:37

On July 28, Beijing time, according to foreign media reports, during the teleconference after Apple's third-quarter earnings release, when asked why Apple’s sales performance in the Chinese market has slowed, why Apple has not yet launched a complete product lineup to respond to competitors' challenges, and why people should not believe the various rumors about Apple circulating in the market, Tim Cook, Apple's CEO, always responded with calmness and composure. However, he would occasionally suddenly criticize his competitors without naming them.

Apple's third-quarter earnings report released this week showed that driven by the continued strong demand for iPhones, the company's revenue and net profit both exceeded analysts' expectations. This was undoubtedly good news for Cook and Apple. Investors were also quite satisfied with Apple's financial report, prompting Apple's stock price to rise again this week. As of Friday's close, Apple's stock price was $440.99 per share, up 3.8% from last Friday's close.

Cook will celebrate his two-year anniversary as Apple's CEO next month. In the approximately one-hour teleconference, he discussed core issues ranging from the threat posed by competitors' tablet computers to the future of cars connecting to networks. Below is a summary of Cook's responses to ten key questions during the teleconference:

1. Competitors' Tablet Computers: Useless

Cook: "I don't know what other competitors are doing. What I can tell you is, based on the latest statistics I have received, iPad's share of mobile network traffic increased in the third quarter, currently reaching as high as 84%. So, if other tablet products are selling well, I don’t understand what they're being used for. Because network traffic is one of the most basic functions of a tablet computer. We are very satisfied with the iPad's market position."

2. Sources of Apple's Revenue Growth

Cook: "From the perspective of Apple's performance growth, our main sources have always been new products and new services; these are also the primary revenue sources in our current and future product strategies. In addition, we have many opportunities to increase revenue in many areas, such as partnerships with mobile carriers, expanding retail stores and online app stores, broadening indirect revenue channels, and market expansion opportunities. CFO Peter Oppenheimer just mentioned the enterprise market in his speech. The penetration rate of iPads and iPhones in the enterprise market exceeds 60%. I think we are in a leading position in the industry, so we have many growth opportunities."

3. New Product Philosophy and Whether This Philosophy Limits the Number of New Products

Cook: "I have a different view on this issue. I think we need to create great products, so we believe that if we focus on doing this well, then achieving excellent financial results is a natural outcome. Therefore, we do not see a conflict between the two. On the contrary, we believe there are many intersections between the two, and if you look back at Apple's execution over the years, you will find it is achievable. We firmly believe that the most convincing evidence is that consumers love these products and want to own them. If you don't start by trying to create products that consumers like, then it's easy to develop something that consumers don't actually want."

4. New Products for This Year and Next Year

Cook: "We are developing some products that we are proud of, and we will continue to pay attention to their specific application situations. When the time is right, we will release these products."

5. Smartphone Users Increasingly Favoring Products Priced Lower Than the iPhone

Cook: "I don't agree with the widespread belief in the market that the high-end smartphone market is already saturated. I don't believe in this talk, and time will prove everything."

6. Before Launching New iPhones and iPads This Autumn, Apple May Reduce Existing Product Production

Cook: "Considering the iPad itself or combining the iPad and iPhone together, we have been reducing inventory pressure, and the reduction has been quite significant. In the early part of the third quarter, iPad inventory decreased by more than 700,000 units, and iPhone inventory decreased by more than 600,000 units. We don't want to keep too much inventory. Therefore, we will use all possible opportunities to reduce inventory."

7. The Appeal of Low-Cost iPhone 4 and iPhone 4S Models with iOS 6 to Consumers in Emerging Markets

Cook: "This provides a new way for consumers to experience the iOS operating system and the iOS 6 rating system, and the platform attachment degree of iOS 6 is also very high. Therefore, this benefits consumers, and we are very willing to launch such products."

8. Unexpected Decline in Revenue in the Chinese Market

Cook: "Although revenue figures cannot reflect our entire performance in the Chinese market, the fact that demand was weak in the third quarter in China is undeniable. Actually, the revenue decline in the Hong Kong market was even greater, while the year-on-year revenue growth in mainland China still reached 5%. However, this is also one of the lower year-on-year growth rates we've experienced in the Chinese market. This can be attributed to many reasons, such as poor macroeconomic conditions. For tourists and some retailers, Hong Kong is undoubtedly an international shopping paradise. But our third-quarter revenue in the Hong Kong market dropped significantly. Although we are not yet sure exactly what changes occurred in the Hong Kong market, the 20% year-on-year revenue drop indeed had a serious impact on the company's overall revenue in Greater China."

9. Why Apple Has Not Launched an iPhone Trade-In Program So Far

Cook: "Trade-in programs are very popular among consumers, both in the U.S. and globally. Due to the high residual value of older iPhones, and the growing calls for Apple to launch an iPhone trade-in program, this will be an attractive win-win measure. However, we do not currently have any plans to launch a trade-in program. I am not opposed to such programs."

10. More Than a Dozen Car Manufacturers Will Integrate the iOS Platform and Siri Voice Navigation Services into Their Products Next Year

Cook: "I attach great importance to this. This is part of what the iOS operating system should do, just as iOS plays a role in the App Store, iTunes, all other content and services. Being able to collaborate with the automotive industry is very important for Apple, proving that it is something consumers actively desire. I believe that Apple can achieve all this in a unique way and overcome other competitors. Therefore, this will be one of the company's main focus areas in the future."
