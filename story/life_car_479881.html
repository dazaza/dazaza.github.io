Small Car Maintenance Tips for Summer Have Big Uses

The hot summer is not only a challenge for people, but also a tough season for vehicles. Car owners must pay extra attention to some car maintenance details while taking care of themselves to prevent their cars from "heatstroke."

1. Pay Attention to Cleanliness

In the summer when windows are often open, dust can easily blow into the car. After some time, the inside of the car may become sticky, dirty, and even develop unpleasant odors. The dashboard, door panels, and other plastic parts should be cleaned with a brush and cleaning agent. To prevent cracking and aging caused by UV rays, a layer of dashboard wax can be applied after cleaning.

2. Protect the Paintwork

Excessive sunlight can cause significant damage to the car's paintwork. For car owners who care about the appearance of their vehicle, it's important to wash the car frequently and apply wax regularly. If you want to provide more thorough protection, consider doing a paint sealant or coating to give the car paint an additional layer of "crystal protection."

3. Check the Brakes

During the rainy season, wet roads reduce the friction between the tires and the road surface, especially when water accumulates in the tire treads, further reducing the friction coefficient. A responsive braking system becomes crucial in such conditions, so it's important to regularly check the brake system to ensure preparedness.

4. Prevent Tire Blowouts

The high temperatures on the road during summer make the car tires operate in a more challenging environment. It's essential to pay attention to checking and maintaining the car tires, regularly inspecting the tire pressure to prevent blowouts.

5. Smart Use of Air Conditioning

Before entering the car, open the windows to ventilate and turn on the external circulation mode. Once the hot air inside the car has been expelled, turn on the air conditioning for faster and better cooling effects. When driving at speeds below 25 km/h, set the air conditioning to a lower gear to avoid insufficient power generation and cooling. On rainy days, the car windows can easily fog up, affecting visibility. In such cases, adjust the airflow direction toward the windshield, turn on the compressor for cold air, and the fog will clear within a few seconds.

6. Prevent Fires Before They Happen

Summer is the peak season for vehicle fires. Improper driver operations or hidden issues with the car are the main causes of car fires. Therefore, it's important to focus on checking the fuel and electrical systems. If any signs of aging wires are found, send the car for repairs promptly to prevent fires.

7. Pay Attention to "Antifreeze"

Some people think that antifreeze isn't necessary in summer and can be replaced with regular tap water, but this practice is inappropriate. Professionally formulated antifreeze not only resists low temperatures but also performs well in high-temperature conditions. High-quality antifreeze can withstand temperatures close to 200°C before boiling. Using antifreeze in summer helps car owners avoid the trouble of overheating. Additionally, antifreeze has rust prevention and scale removal functions. Therefore, it's recommended to continue using the original factory antifreeze in summer, which can help "cool down" the car.

8. Prevent Evaporation

With higher temperatures in summer, the evaporation of oil and water increases. Therefore, it's important to tightly close the fuel tank cap, prevent oil leaks in fuel pipes, and regularly check the water level in the radiator, the oil level in the crankcase, the brake fluid level in the master brake cylinder, and the electrolyte density and liquid level in the battery. If anything doesn't meet the specifications, timely adjustments and replenishments should be made.
