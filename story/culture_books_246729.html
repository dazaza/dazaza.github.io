"Grafted Marriage: The Complex Love of a Married Man"

by jqrxass on 2009-10-12 16:21:28

Anjie has been harboring secret feelings for her brother-in-law, Liang Chao. After studying abroad, Anjie met a teacher, Dr. Cang, who looked and acted strikingly similar to her brother-in-law, and she fell in love with him at first sight. Meanwhile, her classmate Wu Gang was also relentlessly pursuing her. After a car accident, Anjie unexpectedly moved into Dr. Cang's home. During their time together, Anjie learned that his divorce might have been due to infertility. Due to his inferiority complex and past experiences, Dr. Cang refused to accept Anjie. At this time, Anjie discovered another fact - her sister, whom she thought was always happy, was also infertile. She began to realize that love ultimately must yield to reality, and she decided to save these pairs of separated lovers caused by infertility. Anjie became a surrogate mother for her sister and gave birth to a son for her sister and brother-in-law. She then found Dr. Cang and hoped to be a surrogate mother for him and his ex-wife to save their marriage. In the end, Dr. Cang and Anjie became a couple and had a boy with the help of advanced IVF technology.

Three Walk Together Part One

No one understood why she made such a move, as everyone had heard that computer science was no longer popular in the U.S. in 2002, and Anjie had a good job domestically with a decent income. Moreover, unless one wanted to teach, there was no need to pursue a master's or doctorate degree in computer science. Studying endlessly would only increase one's age, give job opportunities to others, and turn new knowledge into old knowledge. Having a doctorate degree might even make it harder to find a job.

Three Walk Together Part Two

Later, she heard from her sister that it wasn't just about which finger the wedding ring was worn on, but also about the ring itself. Some Americans wear their graduation rings on their left ring fingers. Her sister once made a joke out of mistaking an American classmate's graduation ring for a wedding ring, mentioning "your husband" multiple times during a conversation. Later, the American classmate told her sister that she didn't have a husband yet, and what she wore was just a graduation ring.

Three Walk Together Part Three

She got so excited that she became a little confused, making her unable to understand what Dr. Cang was saying in class. She only hoped that he would walk to the door again to drink water, so she could confirm once more that he was drinking mineral water. When he really walked to the door to drink water, she stared at him without blinking, finding him very handsome as he drank not by putting the bottle directly to his mouth, but by pressing his thin upper lip against the bottle opening, and when swallowing, his Adam's apple rolled up and down, showing a lot of masculinity.

Three Walk Together Part Four

Anjie read Dr. Cang's email several times, and the more she read, the more she felt like Dr. Cang was angry. Since coming to the U.S., although she had been trying hard to "think in English," habits die hard, and she always translated the English in her heart to Chinese to understand. Therefore, she thought Dr. Cang's sentence basically equated to "Don't worry anymore, I know what to do." Reading it a few more times, she even felt like it was "Don't meddle anymore, I don't know what to do," with the underlying meaning being "Are you annoying me?"
