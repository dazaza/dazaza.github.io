"Event" Wudaokou Salon Issue 4 Preview: The Overseas Path of Chinese Social Games (Beijing, Feb 26)

by ybcz0519 on 2011-02-23 07:23:54

As Facebook's valuation surpasses $50 billion, the value of social networks is immeasurable. More importantly, numerous applications can be derived around these social networks. Among them, social games that effectively enhance user stickiness and have a simple yet effective business model undoubtedly become the fastest-growing link in the massive industrial chain. Starting from the first batch of ventures into Facebook and Japan's social platform Mixi, Chinese social game developers have been exploring overseas gold mining for three to four years. How do they face the challenges brought by foreign cultures and unfamiliar markets? What are their judgments on the future of overseas markets? When Facebook enforces the mandatory use of Facebook Credits by game developers on July 1st, narrowing down payment channels, how do domestic game developers perceive the unpredictable changes in overseas markets and the various challenges they may encounter? Is the overseas market full of gold or fraught with danger?

The fourth session of NetEase Technology's "Wudaokou Salon" will focus on the theme "The Overseas Path of Chinese Social Games," to be held at Rainbow Bar in Beijing on February 26th, 2011 (Saturday). We will invite entrepreneurs, developers, and investors in the field of domestic social games to exchange and discuss this topic, specifically including the current status of overseas operations of social games, how to succeed in overseas markets, as well as the challenges and opportunities in overseas markets.
