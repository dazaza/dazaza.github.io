The best treatment for anal fistulas in Taiyuan requires different measures based on the stage of development.

(a) During the acute infection phase, antibiotics, local physiotherapy, and hot water sitz baths should be applied. If an abscess forms, incision and drainage should be performed.

(b) Fistulotomy - applicable for low-positioned simple anal fistulas and external fistulas between internal and external sphincters. Incising the fistula damages only part of the internal sphincter, subcutaneous, and superficial parts of the external sphincter, preventing postoperative fecal incontinence. Under saddle anesthesia, a probe is inserted through the external opening, passing through the internal opening of the fistula, and then the fistula is incised along the probe's direction. Corrupted granulation tissue is removed, and to ensure the fistula grows outward from the bottom, a small amount of skin on both sides of the incision can be trimmed to create a [V]-shaped wound with a smaller base and larger opening. Attention must also be paid to any branch ducts, which should all be incised.

(c) Thread Therapy for Anal Fistulas - suitable for high-positioned simple anal fistulas where the internal opening is above the plane of the anorectal ring. Cutting this during surgery could cause fecal incontinence. Using thread therapy allows the sphincter muscle to first adhere to surrounding tissues. After about 10-14 days of ischemia and necrosis due to ligation, it breaks naturally without causing contraction or incontinence. The fistula opens up and gradually heals. Method: Insert a probe through the external opening via the fistula and out through the internal opening. Guide a sterile thick silk thread or rubber band through the probe from the internal opening to the external opening, then tighten the thread. When performing thread therapy, note: (1) Confirm the exact position of the internal opening to avoid creating false channels and prevent surgical failure; (2) Before tightening the silk thread or rubber band, cut open the skin and subcutaneous part of the sphincter to reduce postoperative pain and shorten the thread removal time; (3) Tighten appropriately, as too loose may fail to break the fistula. Post-surgery, take hot water sitz baths, and tighten again after 3-5 days. Generally, full rupture occurs within two weeks.

(d) Anal Fistula Excision - suitable for low-positioned simple anal fistulas. This differs from incision in that the fistula and surrounding tissues are separated and excised until healthy tissue is exposed, with the wound usually left open and not sutured. Post-surgery, sitz baths and medication changes continue until healing. For high-positioned or complex anal fistulas, care must be taken during surgery to protect the anorectal ring to avoid postoperative fecal incontinence.
