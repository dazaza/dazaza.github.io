Furniture fair praises the online platform, "Da Vinci" becomes the keyword

by jdhgsa078 on 2011-09-07 10:36:32

The Internet has long been regarded as a threat to traditional trade fairs. However, the 26th International Famous Furniture (Dongguan) Exhibition, which opened yesterday, held a press conference to announce the launch of Kaodianke, a professional furniture B2B website. At the press conference yesterday, reporters also noticed that Da Vinci became a buzzword, not only used by merchants as a publicity gimmick but also became a subject of debate and reflection for industry insiders.

Da Vinci Becomes a Promotion Gimmick

Quickly find out if I still have my business cards! Where is the card swiper?... Yesterday, the booth of the Da Fengdi brand furniture was bustling with activity. The Da Fengdi brand is one of the six brands of Hong Kong Haosen Industrial Co., Ltd., and it is not particularly outstanding. It made its debut to mainland consumers in the second half of last year.

However, because the brand name is similar to the recently popular Da Vinci, Haosen Company used this as a gimmick to set up advertising boards everywhere. This move attracted many dealers and professional audiences to visit the exhibition hall, thus creating the lively scene mentioned above.

The 1000 business cards I prepared today were all handed out just after lunchtime," said Zeng Huaqing, regional manager of Haosen Company, with a beaming smile. The Da Fengdi brand mainly features sofas, and its style is relatively close to Da Vinci. During the 20 minutes of on-site interviews by reporters, dealers from Inner Mongolia, Guangxi, Henan, and other places kept coming to inquire about cooperation matters.

At the "2011 China Furniture Industry (Dongguan) High-Level Forum" held yesterday, many people in the furniture industry proposed that the Da Vinci incident gives the industry the insight that the luxury furniture market is gradually expanding, and suitable Chinese furniture enterprises should promptly follow up to avoid the pie being monopolized by international furniture brands.

Yu Xuhui, Deputy Secretary-General of the Dongguan Famous Furniture Club, believed that the reflections triggered by the Da Vinci incident in the furniture industry undoubtedly benefit the further development of the domestic furniture industry.

Desire to Set Up an Unending Famous Furniture Exhibition

At the press conference, a responsible person from the Famous Furniture Organizing Committee said that the Kaodianke website, positioned as a professional furniture B2B platform, officially went online after several years of planning and preparation. Currently, more than 400 furniture brands have opened online stores on this platform. It is understood that Kaodianke is jointly created by the Famous Furniture Exhibition Organizing Committee and a local Internet company. It can not only provide services online but also allow enterprises, dealers, and malls to no longer be restricted by time and space.

Upon logging into the Kaodianke webpage, it was found that the platform gathers over 100,000 high-quality furniture dealers. Currently, many furniture companies have posted product information here, including well-known Dongguan brands such as Shengsen Furniture, Daguangguan Furniture, Huawei Furniture, Huahui Furniture, and Aimu Home.

Creating a never-ending exhibition at a relatively low cost on the Internet, global participants do not need to fly around all day, and they can complete viewing exhibitions and placing orders by simply clicking the mouse in the office. For this reason, the Internet has always been seen as a major rival to traditional exhibitions. However, why does the Famous Furniture Exhibition dare to go online at the risk of losing exhibitors and audiences?

Regarding this, Fang Hui, Secretary-General of the Famous Furniture Club, stated that the online platform will not have a negative impact on the Famous Furniture Exhibition. On the contrary, the constantly updated information and consultations on the website will attract more exhibitors to dig for gold at the Famous Furniture Exhibition more efficiently.

An industry insider analyzed that currently, many companies and portal sites are quite interested in building an online platform for the furniture industry. There are already multiple B2C websites targeting ordinary consumers launched domestically. If the Famous Furniture Club does not seize the opportunity to develop the first B2B platform in the domestic furniture industry, someone else will soon take its place. Regardless of how much impact the online platform has on physical exhibitions, it is better to try it actively than to be forced into it.
