Male employee steals company truck upon leaving cohabiting female boss

by zzf000zxyn7 on 2011-06-29 16:34:06

Male employee stole company's truck, Sohu Space, motive is laughable:

The crime was committed in order to leave the cohabiting female boss

At 9 am on November 20, 2010, Ms. Liu, the female boss of a moving company in Dalian, suddenly received a call from her worker Mr. Chen. Mr. Chen asked Ms. Liu: "Is there any work near Houyan? Why didn't you call me?" Ms. Liu replied: "No." "Then why is the company's truck parked here?" Mr. Chen asked. After hearing this, Ms. Liu felt uneasy and quickly asked Mr. Chen to check if the driver, Mr. Yu, was still on the truck. Mr. Chen confirmed that Mr. Yu was not there. "You keep an eye on the truck, I'll take a taxi and be right there," Ms. Liu said anxiously.

The car owner wasn't present but the car was sold

Why was Ms. Liu so anxious? It turned out that the previous evening, she had received a call from Mr. Yu. Mr. Yu told her that he wanted to commit a crime and took away the company's truck. Ms. Liu thought Mr. Yu was just scaring her and did not take it seriously, hanging up the phone immediately. Now, after Mr. Chen told her the truck was in Houyan, she realized something might be wrong with what Mr. Yu said last night.

At 11 am that day, Ms. Liu rushed to Houyan and saw that the truck was still there. She went forward to open the car door. A young man nearby stopped her and asked what she was doing. Ms. Liu said it was her car. The young man became agitated: "This car was just bought by our boss this morning, how can it be yours?" Ms. Liu also got upset: "Your boss bought it? I'm the car owner, was I present when he bought it?" After saying that, she tried to open the car door again. The young man stopped her while calling his boss, Mr. Cao. Ms. Liu thought arguing like this wouldn't lead anywhere, so she decided to report it to the police.

Money wasn't fully paid and the seller disappeared

At the police station, Mr. Cao explained that he had seen a man driving a truck in the used car market in Houyan that morning. He stopped the truck and asked if it was for sale. The truck driver said yes, asking for 15,000 yuan. After some bargaining, they agreed on 14,500 yuan. Then, Mr. Cao checked the documents of the truck and found that the owner was Ms. Liu. He asked what was going on, and the truck driver claimed Ms. Liu was his wife. Mr. Cao believed him but noticed that the motor vehicle registration certificate was missing.

"Alright, you give me 10,000 yuan as a deposit first, and we'll go to my place together to get the registration certificate. You can pay the rest then," the truck driver said. Mr. Cao agreed, and they wrote an agreement. Afterward, Mr. Cao gave the truck driver 10,000 yuan, and they drove to a certain neighborhood in Shahekou District together. The truck driver asked Mr. Cao to wait outside while he went inside to get the registration certificate. However, the driver never returned, and his phone could not be reached. Helpless, Mr. Cao drove the truck away.

Crime committed out of emotional distress for freedom

After identification by Mr. Cao, the truck driver was indeed Mr. Yu, who was subsequently listed as an online fugitive. In the following days, Ms. Liu called and texted Mr. Yu multiple times. On December 2, 2010, Mr. Yu turned himself in. "Ms. Liu said I didn't dare to surrender myself, so I came," Mr. Yu explained to the police the reason for his surrender. According to Mr. Yu's confession, he started working at Ms. Liu's moving company in August 2009. They began dating in early 2010 and started living together.

A month before the incident, they had a quarrel, and Mr. Yu proposed leaving the company to work elsewhere. Ms. Liu disagreed and tried every way possible to prevent Mr. Yu from leaving, even accusing him of having another woman outside. They argued frequently, and in a fit of impulsiveness, Mr. Yu came up with a ridiculous idea: I would rather commit a crime and go to prison to leave her! At 5 pm on November 19, 2010, Mr. Yu went to the moving company, handed over the truck keys to Ms. Liu, and said, "I quit," before turning and leaving.

At 8 pm that night, Mr. Yu returned to the company and found the spare key to the truck in the desk drawer. Since the spare key couldn't open the car door, Mr. Yu used a screwdriver to pry open the door and drove the truck away. Afterward, Mr. Yu called Ms. Liu, telling her that he intended to commit a crime and had taken the truck away. Ms. Liu, thinking the keys were still in her possession, naturally didn't believe him and hung up the phone... Currently, the case is still under further review.

By Wu Yan, reporter Li Yi
