Your embarrassing moments are countless, they make me laugh till I cry.

by wuyan188 on 2011-11-27 09:43:09

A girl in our class, when it was cold in winter, she put a bag of milk on the radiator. Before class, she shouted to a boy sitting next to the radiator: "Hey, help me turn the milk over." The whole class burst out laughing and there was no class in the morning. In college, one day at a school-wide student meeting, the head teacher wanted the physical education monitor to count if all the girls in the class were present. He said to the PE monitor (a lustful boy): go check if all the girls are here. The PE monitor was very honored and asked softly: kiss... which one first? The teacher thought for a while and said: of course according to the student ID!

Not long ago, a friend gave me a Pekingese puppy named Lele. This dog is pure white all over and very hygienic. It never defecates or urinates anywhere in the house. Every time it feels the urge, it will bark twice and then defecate or urinate in the tray I prepared for it. This way, it saves a lot of trouble. On Sunday morning, I took Lele to the bank. Just after finishing the transaction in the business hall, Lele suddenly barked at me. I knew it had to go again. Although this is not our home, we must still follow public morals! Thinking quickly, I took out the newspaper I just bought at the newsstand for Lele's convenience. Lele happily relieved itself. Afterward, I carefully wrapped this waste in the newspaper into a package, holding it in one hand, leading Lele with the other, heading outside to throw it into the trash can by the street. Just as I reached the roadside, I heard a "screech", a motorcycle braked sharply beside me. In my daze, the young man wearing sunglasses sitting on the backseat snatched the package from my hand and rode away with the loud roar of the engine. I stood by the roadside stunned for a long time. I faintly heard some passersby who had just witnessed the scene talking quietly: "This guy is really unlucky, he got robbed right after leaving the bank, probably lost tens of thousands?"

One day while wandering down the snack street, I found a store selling egg tarts. Each one looked incredibly delicious, so I wanted to try one. I asked the shopkeeper: "Is this sold individually?" The shopkeeper replied: "No, it's from Japan."

When I was in college, I once danced a very scary group dance, which required violent actions like falling quickly and lifting legs high. After practicing for only a few days, everyone couldn't handle it anymore. Our bodies were all bruised, and some even pulled their leg muscles. I was injured quite badly. In the afternoon, I went to class on the third floor. One of my legs couldn't lift up at all, so I just forced myself to climb the stairs, practically dragging that leg straight up. While I was walking, I heard a girl behind me say to her boyfriend: "The schools in big cities are more standardized. In our hometown, people with polio wouldn't be able to attend school."
