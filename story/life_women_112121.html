"The distant mountains have long eyebrows, and a smile is worth a thousand pieces of gold." Which beauty of the ages does not possess exquisite eyebrows and tender, lustrous lips? Permanent makeup is not merely a beauty technique, but also an art. It not only enhances the three-dimensional appearance of the face's brows, eyes, and lips, but should also achieve a result that appears natural. This requires beauticians to have a high level of aesthetic sensibility and proficient skills. Designing ideal eyebrow shapes must be tailored to each individual, based on the shape of the eyebrows and the face, without straying from fundamental brow shapes. For those with slightly asymmetrical faces or uneven brow bones, careful comparisons should be made repeatedly until satisfaction is achieved. We often see people whose overly dark and thick eyebrows appear rigid, giving an impression of a stiff facial expression. It's important to ensure that the skin tone and eyebrow color of the person receiving the permanent makeup are properly coordinated. Following the natural direction of the original eyebrows, the start of the brow should be lighter, gradually deepening towards the end, creating a sense of three-dimensionality.

Permanent eyeliner should enhance the charm of the eyes, yet some cases involve eyeliner that is too wide or injected too deeply, causing the ink to diffuse on the skin surface, forming "panda eyes" and leading to failed cosmetic results. When doing permanent eyeliner, it must be colored just outside the lash roots, achieving a finer line inside and a thicker one outside, combining naturalness with enhancement for an aesthetically pleasing effect. In terms of pigments, most Easterners can directly use black-brown, which closely resembles their hair color, resulting in naturally realistic effects. For customers with fair skin and yellowish hair, adding a small amount of coffee brown to natural gray is recommended for use, but pure coffee brown should not be used for eyebrows, as it would create an extremely uncoordinated look.

True natural permanent makeup should first be designed by a highly artistic permanent makeup artist according to the customer's personal temperament, attire, etc., crafting suitable brow, lip, or areola shapes. Then, using a machine or needle, the design is carefully followed, allowing the pigment to naturally seep into the dermis through the needlework, forming a permanently non-fading, naturally vivid, and lifelike permanent makeup style.

During the procedure, strict adherence to one needle per person is essential. Hands should be washed before the operation, and the area to be tattooed should be wiped with disinfectant cotton. Sterility must be maintained throughout the process to prevent cross-infection. The depth of the color in permanent makeup is determined by the number of passes. In permanent makeup, movements must be light, steady, and accurate, controlling the depth well. Otherwise, the tattooed eyebrows may appear stiff, and the eyeliner may easily cause color bleeding. It is crucial to prioritize shorter over longer and finer over thicker. Therefore, beauticians must hold valid certificates and possess skilled techniques.
