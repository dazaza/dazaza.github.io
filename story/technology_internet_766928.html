Google's 15th Anniversary

by anonymous on 2013-10-02 10:38:06

Today is the day Google celebrates its 15th birthday. Google has only been celebrating its birthday on September 27th in recent years; previously, it was celebrated on different dates each year. So, what’s the significance behind each celebration date? And which day is the correct one for its birthday? In truth, this question is quite difficult to answer.

January 1996:

This is when Larry Page and Sergey Brin began the BackRub project. The exact date is unreliable, and perhaps even these two have forgotten.

September 15, 1997:

This is the day the Google.com domain was registered.

September 7, 1998:

This is the official founding date of Google Inc. Google once replied in a support forum (now inaccessible) saying:

September 7, 1998, is Google's official birthday. If Google were a person, he would have started elementary school last summer (around August 19th), and today marks the completion of his first grade education. In other words, we are just getting started.

September 26, 1998:

In August 1998, Yahoo! issued a press release stating that their indexed web pages outnumbered Google's. On this day, Google announced that they were no longer interested in playing the "who indexes more web pages" game with Yahoo!, shifting their focus instead to search quality. Thus, they removed the number of indexed web pages from their homepage on this day.

September 27, 2005:

Google first published an official blog post announcing the celebration of its birthday on September 27th. They also announced that their indexed web pages were already more than three times those of other search engines. In a 2006 thread titled "When is Google's birthday?" (now inaccessible) on their official forum, Google responded by saying:

Google officially opened its doors in September 1998. The specific date of our birthday celebration has varied over the years depending on when people felt like eating cake.

However, there's no need to worry now as Google has fixed its birthday on September 27th. As for what significant event occurred on this day historically to make it the chosen birthday, or if it was simply picked at random, no one knows.

Google Inc. (NASDAQ: GOOG, FWB: GGQ1, officially translated into Chinese as "谷歌"), is an American multinational technology company specializing in internet search, cloud computing, and advertising technologies. It develops and provides a wide range of internet-based products and services, with its primary profit coming from advertising services such as AdWords. Google was co-founded by Larry Page and Sergey Brin while they were pursuing their doctorates in computer science at Stanford University, hence they are often referred to as the "Google Guys." Google was founded as a private company on September 4, 1998, designing and managing the internet search engine "Google Search." The Google website went live in the second half of 1999. On August 19, 2004, Google's stock was listed on NASDAQ. At the time, the company's two co-founders and CEO Eric Schmidt, known as the "three musketeers," promised to work together at Google for at least twenty years, until 2024. Initially, Google's official mission was "to integrate global information and make it universally accessible and useful." Its unofficial motto, "Don't be evil," was coined by engineer Amit Patel and supported by Paul Buchheit. Googleplex, the headquarters of Google, is located in Mountain View, Santa Clara County, California. In April 2011, Page replaced Schmidt as CEO.

It is estimated that Google operates over a million servers across data centers worldwide, processing billions of search requests daily and approximately 24 petabytes of user-generated data. Since its rapid growth at inception, Google has driven a series of product developments, acquisitions, and partnerships beyond its core online search business. Google offers a variety of online software services, such as Google Drive, Gmail, social networking services like Orkut, Google Buzz, and Google+. Google's products also enter users' desktops in the form of applications, including the Google Chrome web browser, Picasa photo management and editing software, and Google Talk instant messaging tool. Additionally, Google has developed Android, an operating system for mobile devices, and Google Chrome OS, an operating system for netbooks.

Data from Alexa shows that Google's main domain, google.com, is the most visited site globally. Furthermore, multiple sites under Google Search in other countries or regions (such as google.co.in, google.de, google.com.hk, etc.), along with subsidiaries YouTube, Blogger, and Orkut, all rank within the top 100 in terms of traffic.
