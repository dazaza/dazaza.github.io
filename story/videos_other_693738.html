Imagine How Huge International Air Freight Can Get From letters to air planes, it is now possible to ship just about anything through air freight services. All these things can be carried by commercial airplanes and various large flying machines. Although international air freight can be divided into simpler categories, while as we have said, everything can be shipped via air, there should always be some form of separation between items to avoid various risks. The three basic categories of air freight are:

1. Air freights that are carried through passenger airlines.

2. Air freights that are carried through special cargo planes.

3. Air freights that are huge enough to be carried by super cargo planes.

International airlines, along with passengers, can also carry goods destined for various air freight service locations. These goods will then be delivered by rail or road to their proper recipients. The United States Postal Service leases between 15,000 to 25,000 passenger planes daily to haul freight. This accounts for 5 to 10 percent of all air freight revenue. This type of international air freight is packed along with other shipments in special containers designed to fit the storage area beneath the passenger compartment. However, some air freight can be placed on pallets, while other relatively smaller items can be made to fit into remaining open spaces. The Boeing 747-400 is usually used as the freight carrier in the passenger airline category. However, when in Combi mode, it can hold up to 10,000 cubic feet of cargo. This is when some of the passenger compartments are partly converted into cargo areas. But when reconfigured, this plane can be transformed into a huge cargo plane capable of hauling as much as five semi-trucks.

Since Boeing 747-400s are not specifically made as air freighters, there isn't as much room as in a cargo plane to fit a forklift truck for loading pallets. Therefore, special electric rollers are equipped on the load floor. These rollers are used to move the cargoes into the inner chambers of cargo areas.
