Button: Document and share gaming life based on pictures, texts, and community format.

by daodao on 2010-12-19 13:51:13

"Button" is a recently launched in-game embedded tool that uses images and text as carriers to record, publish, and share gaming experiences. It also presents on the Web in a microblogging format. "Button" is a toolkit that appears in games in the form of a plug-in. It combines functions such as screenshot recording, viewing/editing, and microblogging publication, all of which encourage real-time interaction among gamers. It has three major features: single shot, burst shooting, and timer shooting. With less system resource usage, better screenshot quality, and more optimized dissemination methods, the Button tool allows users to take screenshots and add simple text for real-time publishing without affecting normal gameplay. The entire process can be completed directly within the game interface without switching windows. Users can directly use the Button tool to share every memorable moment of their gaming life on the Button website in the form of pictures and text, while also making more like-minded friends on this platform.

The distribution pattern of the online game market basically follows the unchanging rule of "one game determines everything." Shanda established its dominant position in China's online gaming industry based on the Legend series. Formerly, NcSoft also solely relied on World of Warcraft to earn substantial profits. For NcSoft, success came from Warcraft, but so did failure. How to maximize user retention outside of the game and enhance players' loyalty to the operator's brand has been a topic discussed most in recent years among online game operators. In terms of current trends, MMORPGs' user migration to Web SNS might be an idealized, low-cost, and easily operable form. The key is to accurately grasp the main needs of this special group of players in the virtual world outside of the game. What are the main needs? Personally, I believe that SNS ultimately cannot completely detach from the specific games that players are enthusiastically playing. Undoubtedly, Button.net has made many exploratory attempts in this aspect: they use plug-ins as intermediaries to instantly spread players' most enthusiastic "achievements" and "honors" in the form of images + text to the Web. Meanwhile, "sharing" and "interaction" help players better achieve viral marketing across the entire network. Personally, I feel that this demand point is very clear and worth continued attention.
