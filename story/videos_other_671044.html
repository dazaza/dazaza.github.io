Four Key Approaches to Enhancing Website Stability

by xwkj0983 on 2012-02-21 15:03:14

According to a joint report by the McDonald's online ordering website and coupons: gaining search engine trust through website optimization is of paramount importance. With Baidu's new algorithm increasing the recognition of original content, this task has become very tricky. For individual webmasters, updating one or two original articles daily is still manageable, but for comprehensive websites, it is not easy to achieve. Stability of the website can be improved from the following four aspects:

First, the website domain goes international.

This may sound a bit exaggerated, but it is a profound insight gained from years of experience in the industry. Don't laugh, because it was cheap, Hexin registered many CN domains crazily since 08. I believe many webmaster friends have had similar experiences. When you impulsively build a site with a CN domain, tragedy struck in 09. Fortunately, my COM domain is still alive, and one has never been interrupted even without filing! Therefore, website domains should go international, and it is best to register them abroad. The recent suspension of parsing of慧聪网(Hc360.com) by Wanwang is a lesson. For example, top registrars like GoDaddy.

Second, ensure that the site can withstand policy changes.

Since December 09, due to the network rectification by the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology, many webmasters were forced to shut down their sites because they did not file for recordation. Many sites' inclusion and rankings returned to prehistoric times. After much difficulty moving to overseas servers, the speed was not ideal. In this process, all the hard-earned weight we accumulated earlier turned into bubbles. Therefore, if we want to run a good site, we must first ensure that our operational websites do not skirt the edge and can withstand policy changes.

Third, ensure the stability of the website space.

If the software and hardware of the IDC are not up to par, our website will encounter bottlenecks no matter what kind of optimization we do. For example, the number of IIS connections. We are actually quite cautious when choosing hosting services, so we study the parameters carefully. However, there is one thing that cannot be studied, which is their stability. Hexin recently encountered an issue where the site opened extremely slowly. Contacting the hosting provider didn't yield any breakthroughs; they kept saying someone was attacking and the technical team was handling it. Another time, it might have been due to spider crawling, causing the snapshot to roll back. This made me very frustrated. Also, if you choose a virtual host, you may occasionally face the problem of other websites under the same IP being penalized. Because once this happens, the entire IP is affected, and although the website can open, the friendship links cannot be detected. Therefore, webmasters using virtual hosts should also check frequently.

Fourth, ensure the stability of the website framework structure.

This is something we can control, so it must be done well. For old sites and sites with weight, if the main program is frequently updated, even if we keep the absolute path unchanged, it is difficult to gain the trust of search engines. New sites need to pay even more attention to this issue. I won't elaborate further here.

The above four points are experiences in improving search engine trustworthiness from the perspective of stability.
