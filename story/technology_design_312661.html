The Arrival of Luneng's New Coach was Extremely Mysterious: Reporters Exclaimed He Played Hide and Seek Right After Coming

By Zhang Yuan, Jinan Reports - Before Ivankevic even arrived, news about him had already been spreading. This Croatian football coach, known for his tough style, may not necessarily be labeled as "Terrifying Ivan" in the future, but from the start of his arrival at Luneng, he has fully displayed his mysteriousness.

The replacement of a head coach, even in world-class famous clubs like Real Madrid and Barcelona, is an ordinary occurrence. However, at Luneng, it unfolded like a suspenseful detective novel. Despite countless speculations and guesses by fans and media, the club remained tight-lipped. Speculations arose one after another, with rumors flying everywhere. But the club still kept the identity of the new coach under wraps. Eventually, after a long list of coaches including Matthäus, Klinsmann, Fink, and Oguntalé were repeatedly speculated, Ivankevic gradually came to light and moved closer and closer to becoming the new head coach of Luneng.

On the 15th, there were reports that Luneng had signed a contract with Ivankevic, with an annual salary not exceeding 500,000 US dollars. Moreover, Luneng Club would hold a press conference on the 16th to officially announce the Croatian's appointment. Subsequently, insiders claimed that Ivan had already arrived in China, and club executives would all go to the airport to welcome him that evening. The only flight from Beijing to Jinan that evening was SC1158, departing from Beijing Capital International Airport at 7 PM and arriving at Yaoqiang Airport in Jinan at 7:50 PM.

That evening, the Guiyou Grand Hotel had already reserved rooms for Ivan's group and prepared welcome flowers. "The club booked six rooms in total. I heard that the new coach is coming," the front desk receptionist told the reporter. She also mentioned that Tujia used to stay on the 11th floor of Guiyou, but due to the recent room shortage, Ivan's group wasn't arranged on the same floor.

Flight SC1158 landed in Jinan on time, but Ivan's figure was nowhere to be found at the regular passenger exit. The reporter then rushed to the VIP area, where the receptionist pointed to a foreigner who had just walked out and asked, "Look, there was only one foreigner who just left. Is this him?" Judging from the slim build of that foreigner, it was clearly not Ivan. The reporter then searched every corner of the VIP lounge but found nothing.

To confirm whether Ivan had arrived in Jinan, the reporter called a friend at the airport and checked the passenger information of flight SC1158 through the internal system. Yao Jie's name was clearly listed, meaning that, barring any mishaps, both of them should be on this flight.

Since the name appeared on the passenger list, why couldn't he be seen? It was an airport staff member who reminded the reporter, "It's possible that the people from Luneng drove directly onto the tarmac to pick up their guest." Not long ago, during the National Games, the organizing committee also used this high-specification reception method to greet Liu Xiang at the airport. If Luneng really picked up Ivan from the tarmac, they might already be on their way back. The reporter immediately drove off in pursuit but never discovered the "greeting convoy." Upon arriving at the hotel, ten minutes before Ivan's scheduled arrival time, the doorman said, "Ivan hasn't come yet." Looking around, there were no welcoming banners, only a bunch of flowers quietly placed on a table near the entrance.

More and more reporters gathered at the hotel, enthusiastically discussing the scene of Ivan's appearance. No one doubted that they would soon meet the new coach of Luneng. However, as time passed, the reporters became disappointed. A manager-like person approached and told everyone, "Please go home. We've just received notice that Ivan won't be coming today." The long-waiting journalists could only look at each other helplessly: This Ivan, right after arriving in China, played hide and seek. He learned his first lesson fast enough!
