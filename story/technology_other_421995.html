Right is right and wrong is wrong.

by imadin on 2011-04-27 16:02:59

"The internet leaves no place for blemishes to hide. 'Small controversies' have repeatedly tripped up Western politicians." Under the relentless pursuit of the public, another batch of "star-level" Western politicians "painfully" chose to leave. This was an international incident triggered by "controversy," causing one to sigh with regret. Excerpted from the website rhinestone necklace sets.

"Controversy" has several meanings: 1) correctness and error; 2) praise or criticism; 3) disputes or verbal conflicts. For example, spreading rumors. (Cihai Dictionary) Generally speaking, what people refer to as "controversy" mainly pertains to disputes or verbal conflicts. Such phenomena are not only prevalent abroad but also manifest domestically in events like the "plagiarism scandal" or the "donation scandal," which continue to emerge endlessly. The harmful effects are immense, leading ordinary people to feel that everywhere there are "internet trolls," and society lacks any sense of integrity.

Despite media professionals writing articles in public opinion asking "Is truth more important, or is controversy more important?" attempting to encourage people not to easily believe in rumors or controversy, but rather respect the truth, ordinary people who experience situations akin to "the more famous you are, the more controversy surrounds you, such as Zhou Dongyu facing ten major controversies," are countless.

For instance, when some individuals encounter unfair treatment and seek to confide in relevant individuals, they are misjudged by some "controversial" people as being full of complaints. Instead of receiving enthusiastic help, they are criticized or even severely punished for allegedly misleading others. This leaves them speechless, resorting to extreme self-help measures, thereby triggering some social tragedies. Furthermore, in some workplaces, those who do not work often criticize diligent colleagues. When others take a break after working hard, these "controversial" people might accuse them, saying, "Look, they're suffering from intermittent fever again!" If someone dislikes eating or drinking together or forming cliques, these "controversial" people might say, "Look, this person is entirely isolated and different from society!" When someone discusses work experience with a few female colleagues, these "controversial" people immediately jump in, accusing them of potential extramarital affairs, etc. Such "controversial" people who "only see faults in others but never themselves" are very dangerous. They not only make it impossible for ordinary people to act ("stirring excrement in a cesspool—neither smelling nor moving"), preventing them from doing anything and affecting normal work operations, but also trap them in a vortex of controversy, seriously impacting their emotional lives.

Such examples are countless, and many netizens frequently vent their frustrations on body brush forums.

The root cause of "controversy talk" lies in the lack of moral cultivation. Those who often talk about controversial matters are themselves "controversial people." For "controversial people," the famous saying by Zhang Heng, "Do not worry about not having a high position, but worry about not having noble virtues; do not be ashamed of not having much wealth, but be ashamed of lacking broad knowledge," is indeed a wonderful remedy for this ailment. Once put into practice, I firmly believe it will lead to the state of "often reflecting on oneself while sitting quietly, and not discussing others during idle conversations." It may even help achieve the lofty goal of "abandoning selfishness, restraining selfishness, stepping on the selfish self, living for the happiness of others, for the interests of compatriots, the country, and humanity, loving truth and goodness without seeking rewards, but striving for truth and goodness."

Therefore, "controversy talk" should draw everyone's attention. "Do not impose on others what you yourself do not desire. Never neglect your own moral cultivation and become a 'controversial person,' leaving behind ridicule."
