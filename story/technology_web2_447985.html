I. Leadership Thinking:

With the theme of "comprehensive management, Inner Mongolia Xing'an Talent Network, ensuring safety", and taking the establishment of a "safe and harmonious campus" as the carrier, a series of insurance education activities will be developed to further enhance the safety prevention awareness of teachers and students, improve the self-protection and self-rescue capabilities of students, strengthen safety education and management, further promote the construction of school safety culture, and create an excellent atmosphere of "constantly talking about safety, catching safety everywhere, and everyone ensuring safety". This plan is specially formulated.

II. Activity Theme:

"Comprehensive Management, Ensuring Safety".

III. Activity Time:

March 31, 2008 - April 30, 2008.

IV. Activity Focus:

1. Increase the publicity of insurance education, strengthen the legal awareness of insurance work, and improve the safety awareness and self-protection awareness of teachers and students.

2. Carry out regular safety inspections, identify hidden dangers, eliminate hidden dangers, and prevent problems before they occur.

3. Strengthen student management, standardize student behavior, and prevent students from taking actions that may trigger safety accidents.

4. Seek the cooperation and support of public security, cultural, health and other functional departments, parents, and communities to form a safety and public security prevention network, create a safe, civilized, and harmonious campus environment and good school surrounding order.

V. Activity Measures: (Focus on implementing "five implementations")

1. Implementation of Responsibility: Implement leadership responsibility; implement faculty and staff responsibility; implement student and parent responsibility.

2. Implementation of System: Further improve relevant systems for school safety work; strictly enforce the system of reporting, inspecting, and registering school safety hazards and incidents, and the incident emergency response system, formulate and implement safety measures.

3. Implementation of Institutions: Further improve safety work institutions and clarify work requirements; establish and improve the joint defense system between schools, public security, parents, and communities.

4. Implementation of Publicity: Strengthen safety publicity efforts, carry out long-term safety education in various forms, through multiple channels, and comprehensively.

5. Implementation of Activity Forms, Content, and Results: In conjunction with the reality of the school, organize a variety of colorful and healthy cultural activities that are popular among teachers and students, focusing on practical results.

VI. Activity Arrangement:

School safety is the prerequisite and foundation for carrying out normal educational and teaching activities. A safe and stable campus environment is the fundamental guarantee for maintaining good educational and teaching order. All teachers and students must remain vigilant about "safety education" and adhere to it consistently without any侥幸psychology or numbness of thought. In view of our school's actual situation, apart from strengthening safety education, safety inspections, and hidden danger rectifications during normal times, closely focus on the theme of "comprehensive management, ensuring safety, establishing a safe and harmonious campus." Set April as the "Safety Month," and deeply and effectively carry out the "eight ones" activities:

1. Special publicity through a bulletin board, exhibition boards, and display cases.

2. Organize a launch event. Use the flag-raising ceremony speech to educate all teachers and students about safety, mobilize the development of multi-theme activities, and call on teachers and students to actively participate.

3. One week of radio safety publicity. During breaks and extracurricular activity times, use the school's "campus radio" program to educate all teachers and students about safety.

4. One comprehensive safety inspection. The school safety office, each department, grade group, and class will conduct a thorough safety inspection within their respective jurisdictions, promptly report any identified hazards, and handle them appropriately.

5. Distribute a safety education material to all teachers and students and organize learning sessions.

6. Organize a safety lecture.

7. Conduct fire safety knowledge training and drills.

8. Conduct a special summary of the activity. Acknowledge achievements, identify problems, and explore effective solutions.

VII. Activity Requirements:

In order to make this "School Safety Month" activity truly effective, the following specific requirements are proposed for all teachers and students:

1. Each class should organize students to learn and discuss through various theme activities to popularize safety knowledge, enhance safety awareness, and improve self-protection abilities. Conduct safety inspections, eliminate safety hazards, and promptly report any unresolved issues to the school. Improve self-education and self-management abilities, overcome unsafe behaviors, and actively combat other behaviors that endanger personal and property safety.

2. According to the actual situation, require each student to strictly adhere to the following aspects, and homeroom teachers should strengthen education and supervision diligently.

(1) Strictly prohibit climbing over railings on each floor, do not chase, run, or play around in the staircases or corridors of each floor, and do not read, rest, or play on the top floors of all buildings in the campus.

(2) Do not climb over walls or fences at any time or place.

(3) To prevent fires and accidents, the use of open flames in violation of regulations (such as candles) is strictly prohibited in all classrooms and dormitories, as well as privately connecting power sources or using home appliances (such as electric kettles, rapid heaters, battery charging, etc.) in any location.

(4) Strictly abide by traffic rules, pay attention to traffic safety, do not play or chase around on the road, do not engage in various activities, and students who ride motorcycles, electric bikes, or scooters to school are responsible for their own safety.

(5) Students should be civilized, polite, united, and cooperative, abide by the "Middle School Student Code of Conduct," and prohibit fighting.

(6) Safeguard personal belongings and cherish public property.

(7) Physical education, laboratory classes, and extracurricular activities must strictly follow teacher instructions, adhere to operational standards, prohibit unauthorized actions, and prevent the occurrence of various safety issues.
