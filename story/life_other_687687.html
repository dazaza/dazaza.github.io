The past is the past, what's gone is gone.

by gege on 2012-03-02 17:15:07

What has passed will never come back

Times Online Submission

The weather is really getting cold, I don't know why this autumn came so early. People wrap themselves up tightly, just like a meat dumpling. Yeah, it's like the saying goes, "Everyone knows whether the water they drink is hot or cold," it's pretty much this meaning.

At six o'clock in the morning, I lay in bed, extremely unwilling to leave the warmth of the quilt. It's cold outside. Look, I'm also a self-aware person. But I've always been unaware that my younger self was actually like this late autumn, with a bone-deep cold arrogance.

My mother once told me that when I was very small, I was an extremely quiet person. When I was born, I didn't cry or make a fuss; in my childhood, I didn't talk or speak; for strangers or strange things, I didn't show any surprise or joy; and I never showed any emotion towards the temptation of toys or food. Of course, there were people who tried to tempt me with money, but it ended up being fruitless. I just treated everything calmly and quietly, without asking any questions.

My mother said that I grew up being held by adults. I remember one time when my mother put me on the ground and stopped holding me, I just stood there silently. To scare me, my parents hid around a corner not far from me to watch, and I didn't panic or lose composure at all, standing there stubbornly motionless. In fact, I knew that if I cried, they would definitely appear no matter what, but I wouldn't do it. For me, stubbornness always outweighs timidity.

Another time, we had guests at home. From beginning to end, the elders encouraged and praised me, and the guests gave me snacks and toys to praise me, but I never opened my mouth to call them or say a word. This time, the elder really got angry and locked me in the narrow corner of the bathroom to reflect. In the end, either due to my arrogance, or because I never understood how to start loving this world, I disappointed the elders again.

So many years later, I am no longer that little girl, and over these years, I have gradually lost that original integrity. Perhaps the helplessness of life, the difficulties of life, can make every person unable to escape its ordinariness.

Therefore, whenever I feel fragile and at a loss, apart from running away far away, going to those unfamiliar places and seeing unfamiliar faces, I won't be so at a loss that I can't handle it. As for the rest, I can't do anything, helpless.

Just as it says in the book, what has passed will never come back...
