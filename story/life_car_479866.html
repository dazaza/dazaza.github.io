The main performance indicators of car beauty amplifiers include output power, frequency response, distortion, signal-to-noise ratio, output impedance, damping factor, etc.

Peak Power: It refers to the maximum music power that the amplifier can output when the volume is turned up to the maximum under non-distortion conditions.

Rated Output Power: The average output power when the harmonic distortion is 10%. Also known as the maximum useful power.

Output Power: The unit is W. Due to different measurement methods used by various manufacturers, there are different names. For example, rated output power, maximum output power, music output power, peak music output power.

Music Power: It refers to the instantaneous maximum output power of the amplifier for music signals under the condition that the output distortion does not exceed the specified value.

Generally speaking, peak power is greater than music power, music power is greater than rated power. Usually, the peak power is 5-8 times of the rated power.

Frequency Response: It indicates the frequency range of the amplifier and the unevenness within the frequency range. The flatness of the frequency response curve is generally expressed in decibels [db]. The frequency response of a home HI-FI amplifier is usually 20HZ--20KHZ ±1db. The wider this range, the better. The frequency response of some top-notch amplifiers has reached 0--100KHZ.

Distortion: An ideal amplifier should faithfully reproduce the amplified input signal without any change. However, due to various reasons, the amplified signal often undergoes varying degrees of distortion compared to the input signal. This distortion is expressed as a percentage, and the smaller the value, the better. The total distortion of a HI-FI amplifier is between 0.03% and 0.05%. Amplifier distortions include harmonic distortion, intermodulation distortion, crossover distortion, clipping distortion, transient distortion, and transient intermodulation distortion.

Signal-to-Noise Ratio: It is the ratio of the noise level to the signal level output by the amplifier, expressed in db. The larger this value, the better. Generally, the signal-to-noise ratio of a home HI-FI amplifier is above 60db.

Output Impedance: The equivalent internal resistance presented to the speaker is called output impedance.
