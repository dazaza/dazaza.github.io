Summer requires extra attention to car maintenance, with safety precautions being the key!

The peak of summer has arrived, and under the scorching weather, car maintenance is the most headache-inducing issue. China Auto Beauty Recruitment Network (www.qcmrrc.com) recommends focusing on three major potential hazards for cars in summer along with their protective strategies.

Three Major Hazards and Protective Strategies:

1. Spontaneous Car Combustion

The most common cause of spontaneous car combustion is a short circuit in the wiring, either due to aged wiring which leads to slower heat dissipation in high temperatures, or because some car owners add various additional electronic devices after purchasing the car, such as electronic alarms and high-end sound systems, which increase the load on the car’s electrical system. To prevent car combustion,

Solution:

First, the most basic method is to regularly maintain your car by checking the engine, electrical system, and fuel lines for signs of aging or oil leakage. Second, try to reduce unnecessary modifications or installations that could burden the electrical system. If the car has been modified, especially with respect to lighting or sound systems, a comprehensive inspection should be conducted. Additionally, there are some important daily car maintenance tips: always keep a fire extinguisher in the car just in case; during this season, do not leave lighters, perfumes, air fresheners, or other flammable items in the car under prolonged exposure to high temperatures to avoid expansion leading to physical explosions and subsequent spontaneous combustion; after long drives, let your car rest appropriately to avoid overloading.

2. Cars Are Prone to Tire Blowouts

Due to high temperatures, if the tire quality is substandard or the tire pressure is too high, the gas inside the tire expands, leading to blowouts. It's recommended that car owners reduce the tire pressure by 10%, adjusting it to 2.2 kPa. Additionally, car owners can inject nitrogen into the tires, as nitrogen is an inert gas that can reduce the likelihood of blowouts. Of course, car owners need to pay more attention to tire maintenance during this season, promptly repairing or replacing any damaged tires.

3. The Car’s Radiator Temperature Reaches Boiling Point, Causing “Boiling Over”

Once a car "boils over," the owner should immediately find a shaded area to park and cool down the car, allowing the engine to idle while opening the hood to accelerate cooling. After the temperature drops, add sufficient water to the radiator. On a regular basis, car owners should frequently check the cooling system and ensure that the radiator is not low on water. During long-distance or extended driving, always monitor the water temperature gauge readings.

Proper car maintenance requires car owners to be both diligent and detail-oriented:

First, Car Owners Must Be Diligent.

For example, many car owners suggest thoroughly cleaning the air conditioning system in summer, as the car's AC will be used extensively during this time. A thorough cleaning is essential. It's also highly recommended to purchase interior cleaning agents or visit a car beauty shop for a complete interior clean-up. Frequently check if the coolant is low and replenish it promptly. Do not find parking troublesome; try to park your car in a shaded area whenever possible.

Second, Car Owners Must Pay Attention to Details.

For instance, you must carefully check the tire pressure and maintain it at the correct level. After driving through water, inspect the tires for punctures caused by foreign objects. Avoid running the internal circulation for long periods to ensure proper airflow within the car, and never sleep inside the car with the internal circulation on. Under extreme heat conditions, try to avoid prolonged driving as much as possible.
