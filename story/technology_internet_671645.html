These may all be facts, but there is a deeper cause that cannot be overlooked. Sometimes, the irrational sacrifices women make for their families are actually a special manifestation of a lack of security. They depend heavily on the family, so they want to get more involved in the family, thereby establishing an inseparable relationship with the family members. When they feel needed and relied upon by their family, they experience a deep sense of security. That is to say, the family's dependence can provide them with a sense of security. Without the family's dependence and without the family's need for them, they would worry about being abandoned by the family at any time.

Women should adjust their mindset, make necessary and reasonable sacrifices for the family, and use the remaining time and space for necessary self-construction.

Traditional Chinese women are very willing to sacrifice for their families. On Mother's Day, many children post on Weibo: "My mother is a very traditional Chinese woman. Her whole life has been devoted selflessly to our family. She gave up and sacrificed a lot for my father and me."

However, sometimes family members also feel, or even complain, why women meddle in so many things! If her husband sleeps late, she will interfere; if her daughter buys a new piece of clothing, she will also interfere. It seems like there’s nothing she won't meddle in. At this point, someone might defend the woman by saying: "She cares about the family and it's for the good of the family members." Others might say: "She's been managing for a lifetime, it's become a habit. Even if the family doesn't need her to worry so much now, she can't stop. After all, she is the mother of the children and the wife of the man."

Perhaps, the family members' dependence on women comes from the details of daily life, while women's dependence on the family stems from the existence of the family itself and their importance within it. We may all have had such experiences: when we were young, as long as mom was at home, even if she didn’t play with us and was busy with her own affairs, we felt particularly safe and happy playing with toys nearby. But if mom wasn't around, even if we were playing with the same toys, we still wouldn't feel secure or interested. As we grow up, it's the same for women and men. Even if the man plays computer games and completely ignores the woman, the woman would still prefer it over being alone. But if the husband isn't at home, doing anything feels dull. This is actually dependency. Women's dependence on the family is similar. I know a lady whose husband worked abroad for years, rarely coming back. After several years, due to various reasons, they divorced. Friends consoled her: "Dear, don't be sad. Anyway, whether divorced or not, he wasn't around you, what difference does it make? It might be better to divorce and feel relieved." But this friend said: "That's completely different. Although he wasn't around, any difficulties I encountered in life couldn't be realistically solved by him, but at that time, I still felt like I had something to rely on. Now, it's gone, everything is gone." Women's dependence on men is often spiritual and existential.

People always take it for granted that women's sacrifices are because the family needs them to sacrifice. Men have to work outside, so women must take care of the family comprehensively. No matter how you look at it, someone has to manage the household, right? Thus, millions of women, although seemingly reluctantly, are forced by life to make these sacrifices.

People always think that the disproportionate sacrifices women make for their families are a kind of selfless and necessary sacrifice. They have no choice, it's the mission given to women, they have to do it. But have you ever seen such women? Even though the men manage themselves well, they still meddle unnecessarily; even though the men already have mature ideas and can handle current issues appropriately, they still offer unnecessary advice and worry about unworthy matters. Some women must constantly worry about men. Once the men have any issue, they immediately act as if ready to jump into action, busying themselves with various tasks. In fact, these tasks don't need their interference. In other words, without their help, perhaps things could be resolved faster and more smoothly. And when the men really have nothing left for them to manage or sacrifice for, they appear depressed and empty, finding some things to worry about regardless. While shouting about how tired and busy they are, they enjoy it. At such times, the essence of lacking security is revealed even more clearly. Although many times, women's sacrifices are necessary and practical, we cannot deny that sometimes some women's excessive sacrifices are completely unnecessary and self-inflicted, an unconscious expression of their lack of security.
