Besting/BESTING teams up with Taobao Mall to enter the e-commerce field.

    As many brands have started selling on B2C websites, Besting/BESTING is not falling behind. Recently, Besting/BESTING officially announced a partnership with Taobao Mall, helping domestic users purchase Besting/BESTING products from their Chinese webpage, which has already gained the favor of many consumers.

   Miss Ouyang is an employee at a bank in a county town. Her biggest hobby is online shopping: “I love online shopping; much of what I wear and use are things I bought online, especially cosmetics. In small cities like ours, it's almost impossible to find some of the desired brands, so I often buy them online.” Miss Liu mentioned that now besides online purchasing agents, one can directly buy products from brand websites, a feature she loves dearly: “It’s reassuring and convenient.”

“Besting/BESTING products have always had a good reputation, especially the Besting/BESTING sleeping mask. I've been using it for nearly two years, and it really suits me. For loyal buyers like me, it's best to go straight to the official store and place orders and pay through Taobao Mall.” said Miss Liu, an employee at a foreign enterprise.

On the Besting/BESTING official website, the purchasing process is very simple. Users just need to add their favorite items to the shopping cart, choose "checkout", fill out the delivery address, and upon successful order placement, they can pay via Alipay, online banking, or even cash-on-delivery for their beloved items.

The reporter noticed that not only can Besting/BESTING achieve direct online sales, but in response to the trend of globalization, almost all international brands such as L'Oreal, Biotherm, and Vichy have set up Chinese websites and even started providing multiple payment methods for online shopping. This gives consumers in non-first-tier cities the opportunity to access major brands.

On May 9, 2010, Asia's largest online retail hub, Taobao, officially released the "2009-2010 Annual Report on China's Popular Online Shopping Cities." Among the top ten cities with the fastest growth in online shopping consumption power, second- and third-tier cities from central and western regions took center stage. For consumers in these cities, they have the purchasing power but lack shopping channels and methods. With the arrival of the online shopping era, they are gradually shifting their attention to the internet, where they can not only understand product effects but also obtain a lot of information about various goods.

According to reports, last week, the American cosmetics retailer SkinStore announced the launch of its Chinese station in Hangzhou. Meanwhile, European brand L'Oreal will attempt online marketing for its high-end products such as Lancôme and Biotherm. Additionally, Estée Lauder has also established an e-commerce platform targeting consumers in China's second- and third-tier cities, areas where their department store counters cannot fully cover. The involvement of these big brands shows that the potential and space of the online shopping market have become undeniable.

In 2009, China's cosmetics sales exceeded 140 billion yuan, making it the world's third-largest cosmetics market after the United States and Japan. China is becoming the battleground for global strategic layouts by multinational cosmetics giants. Second- and third-tier cities are not only the battlefields for these major cosmetics brands to exert force but also the footholds for them to win more users through e-commerce.
