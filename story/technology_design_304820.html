Real Madrid Exposed: Eight Players on the Winter Transfer List - Some True, Some False; Only Two Determined to Leave

Sina Sports - Recently, a Catalan media outlet revealed that Real Madrid is preparing for a major overhaul during the winter break. Players who are not part of Manuel Pellegrini's plans have become surplus to requirements, simply collecting salaries without contributing on the field. As a businessman, Florentino Perez must consider costs and profits, hence these players will be cleared out.

The publication listed eight names: reserve goalkeeper Dudu, backup left-back De Laet, unused defensive midfielder Gago, frequently excluded striker Van Nistelrooy, reserve holding midfielder Mohamed Diarra, backup center-back Garay, Real Madrid vice-captain Guti, and substitute attacking midfielder Van der Vaart. The report also stated that Real Madrid hopes to raise 40 million euros from selling these eight players. However, judging by the prices listed in the article, this claim appears to be more of an opinion piece as some of the valuations do not align with market trends or player values. In fact, the pricing can be described as rather "amateurish."

For instance, Garay was valued at just 5 million euros—where in today's European transfer market could you find a potential center-back for such a price? Moreover, Van der Vaart was priced at merely 1.5 million euros. Such a fee would barely cover a loan deal. Any club aiming to sign Van der Vaart would surely discard such an offer unless the entire Real Madrid administration had lost their minds.

From the list, it’s clear that most of these players aren't getting much game time, and some are often excluded from the matchday squad. However, regardless of whether Florentino prioritizes commercial value, respecting basic team-building principles is crucial. A football team needs both starters and substitutes. If Real Madrid were to sell all eight bench players, how would the squad maintain balance? With the challenging fixtures in the second half of the season, how would Real ensure sufficient depth on the bench? For the sake of 40 million euros, Real Madrid wouldn’t turn itself into a team relying solely on one starting lineup. Just thinking about it logically, one wouldn't create such a headline about Real Madrid cleaning up eight players during the winter break. After all, this is the winter break, not the summer transfer window.

Real Madrid executives Baldano and Pardeza have repeatedly emphasized that the club's principle for this winter break is neither to sell nor buy players. Of course, if a player insists on leaving and the other club offers a reasonable bid, Real Madrid might consider it. However, Real Madrid won't approve all transfer requests, and so far, they haven't received any satisfactory offers.

Dudek knew from the day he joined Real Madrid that his role would only be as a backup. The Polish goalkeeper has never complained about his limited playing time. This summer, Real decided to keep Dudek, and he accepted the new contract. So why would he think of leaving during the winter break, and why would Real release a backup goalkeeper for free mid-season? Similarly, De Laet leaving is unlikely. He still gets some playing opportunities at Real Madrid, and the club's left flank requires a backup. There are currently no buyers offering a reasonable price for De Laet, and he himself has expressed his intention to succeed at Real Madrid.

Mohamed Diarra, after recovering from injury, has indeed become a substitute but shows no complaints. He now occasionally comes off the bench and believes that through his efforts, his playing time will increase. Recently, his agent stated, "Mohamed will participate in the Africa Cup of Nations in January. At least during the winter break, he won't consider a transfer."

As for Garay, he is clearly the least likely to leave Real Madrid. On one hand, Real views him as a future asset, and on the other, the defense needs to ensure backup strength. After all, Pepe and Albiol cannot play every single match. Real Madrid will absolutely not sell Garay for a mere 5 million euros. Metzelder has a slightly higher chance of leaving due to dissatisfaction over his lack of playing time. He is concerned that not playing regularly might affect his chances of participating in the German World Cup. It's reported that Zenit St. Petersburg wants to sign Metzelder, but Real Madrid approving the transfer is unlikely for the same reason mentioned above — they need two reliable backup center-backs in addition to the two starters. Unless Real finds another backup center-back to replace Metzelder during the winter break, they won't accept Zenit's offer.

Van der Vaart never thought about leaving during the toughest times, and now that his situation has improved, he certainly won't leave. Moreover, his wife Silvie wishes to remain in Madrid for her breast cancer treatment. After Kaka's injury, he became a key starter for the team. In a recent interview, Van der Vaart explicitly stated that he would not leave Real Madrid, saying, "At the start of the season, things were very difficult, but now I feel happy and satisfied. My playing time is increasing, and I hope to become an important member of the team. In the last few matches, I've played in a key position, and I'm gradually adapting to the rhythm. I hope to stay at Real Madrid next season and become an essential part of the team. Additionally, I hope the team wins both the La Liga and Champions League titles this season."

Guti also has no motivation to leave Madrid. We've already mentioned that he doesn't want to leave the Madrid environment, and with his career nearing its end, he doesn't wish to wander elsewhere. Currently, the ones most likely to leave Real Madrid are Gago and Van Nistelrooy. Both players aim to participate in the World Cup and are unhappy with their lack of playing time. Their agents are actively helping them find new clubs. However, suitable buyers have yet to emerge. No one is willing to pay the 15 million euros transfer fee for Gago, and Van Nistelrooy's request for a free transfer hasn't been approved by Real Madrid. They are still searching, and Real Madrid, seeing their eagerness to leave and sufficient depth in those positions, reluctantly agreed to let them go, provided the other club offers a reasonable bid.

Italian agent Giuliano Bronzetti stated that Van Nistelrooy joining an Italian team is unlikely. In fact, based on the information he gathered, Real Madrid does not intend to let any player leave during the winter break in principle. One of Real Madrid's top executives, Pardeza, also revealed that Real Madrid will take no action during the winter break, saying, "The winter transfer window opens for a short period and carries risks. We have no plans to buy or sell."
