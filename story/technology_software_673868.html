The Spring Festival holiday of the Year of Dragon had just passed, and the Chinese Football Association sent the draft schedule of the 2012 season of CSL to each club. Except for minor adjustments to the specific kickoff time, this schedule would become the final schedule of CSL this year. Among the traditional powerhouses that have the strength to compete for the championship, such as Guangzhou Evergrande, Beijing Guoan, Shandong Luneng, Shanghai Shenhua, etc., Shenhua's schedule was the most difficult. The team will face a tough devilish schedule in the first 8 games of the league, which is a severe test for the team that has just undergone a major blood change. In addition, with Nanchang Hengyuan Club moving its home ground back to Shanghai, the Shanghai Derby, which has not been seen for a long time, will return to the Shanghai滩in May and September this year.

In the first five opponents of Shenhua this year, there are two small derbies in the Yangtze River Delta, namely Suning and Greentown, and the biggest rival in the league, Beijing Guoan. In addition, the promoted Fuli, which has made a lot of new signings, must not be underestimated. Evergrande is the defending champion of the league. Evergrande, whose off-field limelight was stolen by Anelka, will certainly hope to defeat Shenhua on the field. In the next three games, Shenhua will also face three traditional powerhouses in the league, Tianjin Teda, Dalian Shide, and Shandong Luneng. Considering all factors, Shenhua's opening schedule of the league this year is absolutely devilish.

Compared with title contenders like Evergrande, Guoan, and Luneng, Shenhua has the greatest changes in the lineup this season. Not only did they hire a new head coach Paul Le Guen, but they also replaced all 5 foreign players who played for the team last season, and introduced two promising rookies Wang Fei and Zheng Kaimu in the domestic player market. Although the new foreign players Anelka, Joel, Moises, Posch, and Manset have proven their abilities in different leagues, and Anelka can even be called the most famous foreign player in the history of Chinese football, the coach's tactical intentions still need more time to be instilled, and the team urgently needs more time to blend. The devilish schedule is a severe test for the team, but from the opposite angle, once the devilish schedule is successfully overcome, the subsequent easier schedule will become relatively easy for the team with high morale.

In fact, Shenhua's opening schedule last season was not easy either. The dual competition of the league and AFC Champions League made the team only get 1 point in the first 2 rounds of the league. Fortunately, thanks to a wave of five consecutive victories at home, Shenhua once ranked second in the points table, only behind Evergrande. But then the team's chronic problem of being poor in summer campaigns reappeared, plus continuous one-week double matches, the team once got only 1 point in 9 rounds of matches. Quite fortunately, since the league does not rest during the Euro Cup and Olympic Games this year, there is no week where two matches are arranged, and the team will have sufficient time to prepare for every match.

Compared to the past, in 2012 season, with Nanchang Hengyuan Club moving its home ground from Jiangxi back to Shanghai, the CSL city derby will return to the city again, which will, to a certain extent, greatly stimulate the ball market. It should be noted that the last time Shanghai had a CSL city derby was in the 2006 season, when Shanghai had three top-tier league teams: Shenhua, United City, and Zhongbang. At that time, as the investor of United City, Zhu Jun used a series of means before the first derby with Shenhua to stir up the ball market to the point of tickets being hard to come by. According to the schedule, the two Shanghai derbies between Shenhua and Hengyuan Club will be held on May 11 and September 21 respectively. Times have changed, what tricks will Zhu Jun use for the first time as an investor of Shenhua facing the Shanghai Derby? This will be very much anticipated.
