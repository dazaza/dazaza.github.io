The incidence of hemorrhoids is increasing nowadays, seriously affecting people's lives and work. Many people are eager to know an effective way to prevent and treat hemorrhoids. For methods of prevention and treatment of hemorrhoids, see what the experts from the Nanjing Armed Police Hospital's Proctology Center [http://www.njgcyy.com] have to say.

Experts at the Nanjing Armed Police Hospital's Proctology Center point out that most patients with hemorrhoids are those who sit for long periods without much movement, such as office clerks, accountants, computer operators, editors, mahjong enthusiasts, taxi drivers, etc. This is because about 90% of hemorrhoid patients usually prefer sitting on soft cushions or sofas. Most of these individuals are white-collar workers.

Experts at the Nanjing Armed Police Hospital's Proctology Center explain that maintaining a prolonged sitting position leads to a slower blood flow in the abdomen, which hinders circulation. The venous blood from the lower limbs cannot return, causing obstruction in the blood circulation. The venous plexus of the rectum is prone to varicosities, leading to blood stagnation and the formation of a venous mass, which results in hemorrhoids. When people sit on soft sofas for extended periods, their bodies sink into the sofa, obstructing blood circulation and potentially worsening or triggering hemorrhoids. Similarly, overly soft chairs can have the same effect.

The Nanjing Armed Police Hospital's Proctology Center warns patients: Hemorrhoids need timely prevention and treatment.

To prevent hemorrhoids, one should sit more often on hard benches. Many people are likely to encounter hemorrhoids, and the best method to treat them is to prevent them beforehand. People who frequently lift heavy objects, pregnant women in late stages, and women post-delivery also belong to the high-risk group for developing hemorrhoids. Especially during pregnancy, as the fetus grows, it exerts pressure on the pelvic and abdominal cavities, increasing abdominal pressure and causing hemorrhoids. Therefore, extra care is needed. Hard chairs are more suitable for patients with hemorrhoids.

Nanjing Armed Police Hospital provides two tips for office workers to stay away from hemorrhoids:

1. Sit more on hard benches. When a person sits on a hard bench, the buttocks are supported by two ischial tuberosities, reducing the obstruction to blood circulation and decreasing the occurrence of hemorrhoids.

2. Maintain regular bowel movements. It's best to defecate immediately after waking up in the morning. Once you feel the urge to go, you should promptly use the restroom. Additionally, if your stool is dry, drinking a glass of lightly salted water every morning may help. If necessary, consider taking some laxatives, but only after consulting with a doctor at the hospital.

Experts from the Nanjing Armed Police Hospital's Proctology Center remind that the most important aspect of preventing and treating hemorrhoids is to start from our daily habits. Hemorrhoids stem from our lifestyle, so it’s essential to develop good living habits. Wishing all patients with hemorrhoids a speedy recovery.
