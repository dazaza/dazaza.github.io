Why Twitter Becomes the First Social Network Partner of NFL in the US

by anonymous on 2013-10-02 17:34:10

Last Thursday, the U.S. National Football League (NFL) officially announced a partnership with social networking site Twitter. In the future, the NFL will launch video programs on Twitter including game highlights, instant replays, and match analysis. This kind of cooperation is the first for the NFL, and Twitter has gained an enviable, excellent opportunity that even Facebook hasn't obtained.

The NFL has always been reluctant to cooperate with websites, only authorizing television networks to broadcast its best content. This is the first time in its history that the NFL cooperates with a social network and shares "some of the most valuable content in the entertainment industry" on the website.

According to the cooperation plan, the NFL will produce some video clips specifically and release them through its official Twitter account in the form of "promoted tweets". These video clips will include instant replays of Thursday night games, Sunday game highlights, match analysis, news, etc. These video programs will feature pre-roll ads lasting 5-8 seconds. Advertisers include Verizon and another company whose name has not yet been disclosed. Twitter and the NFL will share advertising revenue, but specific terms have not yet been announced.

For Twitter, this deal means a huge income outside of IPO. Perhaps equally important is that this deal gives Twitter a long-coveted opportunity that even its biggest rival Facebook couldn't obtain. Facebook has five times as many active users as Twitter and has been actively striving to enter the TV and live streaming fields.

So, why did the NFL choose Twitter?

"Because of the nature of the Twitter platform, its openness and real-time features... We do see a synergistic effect... We think it's an excellent, groundbreaking collaboration," said Hans Schroeder, Senior Vice President of Media Strategy and Development at the NFL.

Schroeder revealed that contacts between the NFL and Twitter had been ongoing for years, but serious discussions about advertising cooperation only began this summer. Verizon, as the operator of the NFL mobile application, also actively participated in this cooperation with Twitter.

"The content on Twitter has broader dissemination and participation, which is very important for us as content producers," Schroeder said, "What really attracted us about this cooperation is Twitter's dissemination ability. We believe these programs can attract far more than 5 million viewers on Twitter and let them know what news and discussions are going on about the NFL that day."

Schroeder said that the NFL has about 5 million followers on Twitter, but the NFL hopes to build a wider bridge between the 190 million NFL fans in the United States and more than 100 million native Twitter users in the U.S. The NFL indeed wants more attention and plans to attract more sports enthusiasts and super fans by producing programs specifically for Twitter.
