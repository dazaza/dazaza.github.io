Although the specific policy of "building materials to rural areas" has not yet been introduced, domestic wood flooring companies have already begun to prepare. In the first half of this year, Life Family, Jusheng, Fulin and other flooring companies either opened new factories to expand production capacity or launched new products to strengthen their brands, laying out their strategies for entering the rural market in advance.

Experts pointed out that although the relevant policies have not yet been introduced, as the most suitable ground material for large houses in towns, wood flooring companies have already sensed the signal of explosive demand in third- and fourth-tier markets. They are competing to layout in rural markets. The key for these companies to successfully occupy the rural market in the future is to make their products "suitable for local conditions", meaning they must meet the different levels of floor consumption needs of town residents.

The urban market is nearly saturated, targeting rural market demand

According to information from the China Building Materials Federation, the policy of building materials to rural areas will boost domestic consumption by 550 billion to 600 billion yuan, and wood flooring with its advantage of large-area installation will account for at least one-fifth of the share. Facing the big pie of the rural market, any flooring company would be excited.

It is reported that the newly opened Nantong factory of Life Family Flooring has invested two billion yuan, mainly engaged in the R&D and production of wood flooring, with an annual production capacity of 4 million square meters, aiming to lay the groundwork for its strategy to enter the rural market. Professor Gao Zhihua of the China Timber Industry Circulation Association believes that enterprises have already sensed the signal of explosive growth in third- and fourth-tier markets. With the timely introduction of national related policies, all these give the flooring market a strong shot of expanding production capacity and deepening channel development.

Compared with the gradually saturated first-tier city flooring market, the town market will become the treasure land for flooring companies to mine. Industry insiders pointed out that currently, real estate developers like Vanke have started to focus their industrial layouts on second- and third-tier cities. The expansion of Walmart, Tesco and other foreign retail giants towards third- and fourth-tier cities has also become an important trend, which undoubtedly is the inevitable direction for the flooring industry to open up the market.

It is reported that Nature Flooring has more than 2,500 brand specialty stores, Life Family Flooring has over 900, and Seyou Flooring has over 800. Currently, these brands are accelerating the layout of their store numbers in second- and third-tier cities.

Multiple flooring brand executives expressed that the goal of expanding the strategy to rural areas is to open flooring specialty stores in every prefecture-level city, every county-level city, and even in towns with market consumption capabilities, in order to seize channel discourse power.

Enriching product lines and increasing awareness are the keys

It is reported that due to the vast geographical distribution of rural areas in our country, customs and consumer concepts vary greatly, resulting in huge differences in the demand for flooring products among residents in different places. If the consumers in first-tier cities pursue product differentiation to meet individual emotional and psychological needs, then the product differences in second- and third-tier markets, especially rural markets, are the rigid materialized needs of "suitability for local conditions."

Chairman of Life Family Flooring, Liu Shuo Zhen, believed that suitability for local conditions poses a more severe challenge for manufacturers because such rigid materialized needs usually find it difficult to have alternative solutions. For example, in rural areas, people are more accustomed to wearing shoes indoors, so the floor not only needs to have long-lasting fresh color but also very strong wear resistance, without expecting villagers to change their lifestyle because of the installation of flooring. Therefore, flooring companies must take enriching product lines as the starting point for entering the rural market.

Industry experts pointed out that when entering rural markets, not only must the products be robust, but the communication and marketing aspects also need to be tailored. According to the consumption habits and psychology of rural consumers, marketing strategies different from urban dissemination must be carried out. Currently, the main medium through which rural consumers come into contact with the outside world is television. Therefore, if flooring companies want to gain more shares in the rural market, they should carefully select provincial satellite channels and terrestrial channels. For general small and medium-sized flooring companies, embedded advertising can be used to increase the awareness of flooring among villagers. Additionally, paying attention to local opinion leaders and seizing wall media are quite important for brand communication.
