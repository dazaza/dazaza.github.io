When a car accident occurs, the pregnant woman's chest cavity and pelvis will be compressed by the seat belt, but her abdomen can still move freely. Therefore, for safety reasons, pregnant women traveling by car should keep the following points in mind:

One, try not to drive yourself or sit in the front seat. If there is a strong impact, the airbag deploying may have some impact on the pregnant woman's stomach, injuring the baby in the belly.

Three, if there is a pregnant woman in the car, the driver must drive cautiously, remembering stability and slowness. Too much turbulence and excitement can have adverse effects on both the pregnant woman and the fetus. In case of an accident, the consequences would be unimaginable.
