Four Heavenly Kings, guarding and protecting.

Today, the "Four Heavenly Kings" make a dazzling appearance, belonging to different soldier systems, each with unique skills. Let us witness their brilliance:

Southern Heavenly King of Growth - Guan Yu, title: "King of Knives"

Commanding troop type: Knife Shield

Analysis: Famous general Guan Yu, one of the most respected figures in the Three Kingdoms, though not a king, has an aura of ruling over all under heaven. Not only is he brave and unmatched in battle, but he can also command troops independently. The combination of General Guan Yu and the skilled defensive knife shield soldiers is a perfect match, with a renowned general leading the charge and a large army defending, making them a crucial force for victory.

Northern Heavenly King of Much Hearing - Huang Zhong, title: "Bowman"

Commanding troop type: Archers

Analysis: In the Three Kingdoms, no one was more famous for archery than Huang Zhong. This man could draw a bow with the strength of two stones by himself, every arrow hitting its mark, and at the age of 70, he was ranked first among the "Five Tiger Generals." With Huang Zhong leading the long-range attack-type archers, he enhances the already powerful archer troops' killing power, truly like adding wings to a tiger, ensuring invincibility in righteous battles.

Western Heavenly King of Wide Eyes - Zhang Fei, title: "Spear Hero"

Commanding troop type: Spear Soldiers

Analysis: One of the three brothers of Peach Garden, Zhang Fei, with his "Lion Roar," wielding a long spear, taking enemy heads as easily as picking objects from a pocket. Zhang Fei unifies the short-range attack-type spear soldiers, which can effectively disrupt cavalry charges with their unique defensive and assault capabilities at close range, sweeping all before them, and conquering wherever they go.

Eastern Heavenly King of Protecting the Nation - Lü Bu, title: "Rider God"

Commanding troop type: Cavalry

Analysis: Known as the "Unparalleled in the Three Kingdoms," Lü Bu is hailed as the first mighty general of the Three Kingdoms, unmatched by ten thousand men, and difficult for a thousand to resist. Compiling the persistent attack-type cavalry under Lü Bu's command is a stroke of genius. Their strong attack power and swift attack speed will quickly break through the defenses of the knife shield soldiers, leaving no trace wherever they go.
