Beijing Love Story Chen Sicheng: Please call me "crazy" director now

by qiuman8013 on 2012-02-14 17:33:58

These days, I have been dealing with a man called "Crazy" in the TV series *Beijing Love Story* (short for *Bei Ai*). I despise his restlessness, hate his flaunting of wealth, and detest his lack of affection. At the same time, I admire his sense of righteousness, like his straightforwardness, and am moved by his deep affection. It wasn't until I had to face him over the phone that I realized this 33-year-old man named Chen Sicheng is far more interesting than "Crazy".

Before Chen Sicheng became "Crazy", people used to call him "Chengcai", who was the smart but overly clever fellow from *A Soldier's Glory*. This time it's different; Chen Sicheng's intelligence is fully displayed in *Bei Ai*, which is currently popular on Zhejiang TV. He not only plays "Crazy" but also writes and directs the character "Crazy". And when a director's first TV drama receives unprecedented attention, he clearly has a lot to say.

In his early thirties, he finally had his own "child."

Before officially entering the film and television industry, Chen Sicheng had several experiences that were very eye-catching: he started as the host of "Student Era" at Shenyang TV in high school, then entered Xie Jin Art School with the top score in his major in 1994, and again entered the Central Academy of Drama with the top score in his major in 1999. During his schooling, he won the title of one of the top ten university singers nationwide with his original songs. After graduation, he successfully signed with Warner Music.

Without a doubt, before officially entering the film and television industry, Chen Sicheng was already a "star." However, he obviously wasn't satisfied and quietly created another self behind the scenes. That’s why he remained calm when others questioned him being the director of *Bei Ai*. "I started writing martial arts novels in junior high school, and I wrote a graduation play for our class. I also wrote a movie script called *Miracle*, which had already been set up for filming. I am a person who really likes to write. I've also thought about how to make my first drama, and finally, I waited for the right opportunity."

Compared to his acting career, Chen Sicheng frankly said, "Directing involves touching on many complex things. Sometimes you have to become a social activist, communicating with all kinds of people, even networking and raising money. So, *Bei Ai* coming out makes me feel that now, at over thirty years old, I finally have a child of my own, and the joy is different. I hope everyone will stop calling me Chengcai and call me Crazy instead."

"The Soldiers" reunion was originally a "collective romance."

The biggest promotional highlight of *Bei Ai* is the "collective romance" of the "Soldiers' Strike" team. In the end, three members of the team—Chen Sicheng, Li Chen, and Zhang Yi—became the three leading male characters in the drama. Regarding this planned hype, Director Chen said, "This reunion was planned ahead of time. If there hadn't been some unexpected events, everyone would have seen a complete Soldiers' Strike again because Baoqiang, Guoqiang, and Lao Duan had already coordinated. But due to some external factors, it was impossible in the end."

The initial concept of *Bei Ai* originated from a novel written three years ago by Li Chen on his blog titled *Invincible*. "Actually, we briefly discussed it. His blog post was only two or three thousand words, so it couldn't be called an adaptation. The rest of the entire content was added later. I had previously written a movie script called *First Love*, which later became the conceptual prototype for *Bei Ai*." Regarding the ambition that people could feel after watching *Bei Ai*, he didn’t avoid the subject: "With regard to my inner ambitions, I want to create an epic work for contemporary youth."

For the collective subversion of the characters from the Soldiers group, Chen Sicheng had his considerations: "The structure of three men and four women was intentional. You will see that my character settings range from rich second-generation heirs to white-collar workers to phoenix men, covering three mainstream groups in society. Zhang Yi played Shi Jin too perfectly in the past, but today's Shi Xiaomeng is a real person. The four actresses Yang Mi, Zhang Xinyi, Mo Xiaoqi, and Tong Liya are all my good friends. Their roles represent spring, summer, autumn, and winter respectively. I'm very satisfied with these main actors."

Heaven rewards diligence; with sincerity, one can overcome everything.

With a good story, good friends, and a good team, the release of *Bei Ai* seemed to only need favorable conditions. However, all the trials for Chen Sicheng, who was directing for the first time, were just beginning.

Speaking of the shooting process behind the scenes, Chen Sicheng wasn’t as melancholy as imagined, "In fact, many things are predetermined by fate. Initially, *Bei Ai* was set as a summer story, including rain scenes and such, but due to everyone's schedules, we could only shoot in winter, so some adjustments were made accordingly. Now I think this story is more suitable for winter, and last year, Beijing's winter was unusually cooperative. There hasn't been such clear weather for decades, look at how hazy Beijing's sky is this year."

Two other events were narrated by Chen Sicheng in a mystical manner, "In the drama, I wrote about a heart-shaped building. When I thought we might have to use computer graphics if we couldn't find one, on the second day of shooting, we suddenly passed by a new housing development. To attract customers, they had actually made the windows into heart shapes. I was shocked, you know, it was such a coincidence."

"There's another scene where Xiaomeng defiles Lin Xia out of revenge after drinking alcohol. When they walked out of the shooting location, I thought it would be nice if there was snow, after all, a winter story without snow would be incomplete. Just then, last year's first snowfall in Beijing began, it was miraculous."

Regarding these coincidences, Chen Sicheng attributed them to the principle of Heaven rewarding diligence, "I firmly believe in the value of this drama. I believe it was sincerely crafted, and we all believe that sincerity can touch people, including the heavens."
