Do you know how to use liquefied gas safely? 18671079388

by hbjndfwang on 2012-03-03 09:03:25

One, use qualified gas cylinders with warning labels. Otherwise, they shall not be used.

Two, a safety check should be conducted before using the gas cylinder. Focus on checking the bottle valve, pressure reducing valve, rubber hose and stove turn core valve. If leakage is found, deal with it immediately. Replace the aged rubber hose immediately.

Three, the bottle supply system should have a separate storage room for bottles, which should be well-ventilated and meet fire protection requirements.

Four, physical gas cylinders shall not be placed in basements. It is strictly forbidden to place them near open flames, power sources and heat sources.

Five, the distance between the gas cylinder and the stove shall not be less than 0.5m, and the room should be ventilated.

Six, when igniting, you should [fire wait for gas], that is, ignite first and then open the gas; when extinguishing the fire, close the bottle valve first and then the stove valve.

Seven, when boiling water or cooking, there should be a dedicated person to watch, to prevent water overflow from extinguishing the fire and liquefied gas leakage.

Eight, if a fire occurs, stay calm, immediately close the bottle valve, cut off the gas source, and quickly move the accident cylinder to a safe place. If personnel cannot approach the steel cylinder, quickly cool down the bottle temperature with cold water, and immediately call [119].

Nine, liquefied gas residue shall not be dumped randomly, but shall be returned to the filling station for unified recycling and treatment to prevent fires.

Ten, gas cylinders should be inspected regularly. Except for YSP-50 type steel cylinders which are inspected every three years, the inspection cycle for other model cylinders is four years for the first three times. Any model of gas cylinder with a service life exceeding 15 years should be scrapped.
