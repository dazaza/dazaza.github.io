Oral: My wife helped me find a bed assistant

by tj970 on 2008-03-15 02:43:15

Remember the first sentence in the book "Anna Karenina": Happy families are all similar, and unhappy families each have their own misfortunes. But it's really hard to say whether the love story of the family below is a kind of happiness or a kind of misfortune.

Stealing someone else's girlfriend, she became my wife

My wife and I have been married for nineteen years. I actively pursued her myself, and she was taken from someone else's hands. I still remember when she came back from school and was assigned to the work team where I was interning. A girl undergoes great changes as she matures; the originally smart her became more feminine, and more importantly, her intelligence made her stand out with a unique temperament. I heard that she already had a boyfriend at school, and I would often see the letters her boyfriend wrote to her. In order to get closer to her, I would often help her deliver the letters, wanting to hear her say thank you.

Later, their letters became fewer and fewer. I thought they broke up, so I started pursuing her. Especially when she was working, I would enthusiastically help her load and unload work pieces, showing her my manly side. One day, she was working overtime, and I accompanied her. That day, she seemed a bit tired, lying on the tool cabinet sleeping, looking so tempting. I couldn't help but kiss her. However, at the time, I was really afraid she would slap me, and I was mentally prepared for it.

But the result was not what I expected. After I boldly kissed her, I saw her face slowly turn red. She didn't say anything, which made me feel awkward. I embarrassingly said, "It feels a bit weird." Later, we often laughed uncontrollably about this sentence. I told her, "Marry me." But she said, "I don't love either of you. I just like being with you. If I can break up with him for you today, one day if I meet the person I truly love, I will also break up with you for someone else." I thought she was saying it out of anger. Besides, after getting married, where could she run off to? So without thinking, I said, "No problem. In the future, if you really meet the person you love, I won't hold you back." Little did I know that these words would come true later.

When we got married, my wife was quite happy. After marriage, she was also good to me and gave birth to a son for me. My wife is very capable, managing both inside and outside the home well. The best thing about her is that she not only takes care of my son's eating and wearing but also because of her intelligence, she passed it on genetically to my son, and she started early education for my son early on. As a result, my son really showed signs of being very intelligent. People within dozens of miles knew that I had a very smart son. As my son grew up, his fame in our area surpassed ours because he was really intelligent. For this, I am really grateful to my wife.

But my wife has one flaw: she likes making friends, and most of them are men. There are always many male friends around her helping her. They are willing to help her, whether it's factory matters or everything at home. As long as she steps forward, things can be handled. She doesn't need me to pick her up or drop her off when she travels on business because there are always other men doing it for her. All kinds of favors big and small at home are handled by my wife, and she is very willing to do so. Therefore, there are often rumors saying that my wife is close with certain people at the factory, but my wife remains unperturbed. Although I feel uncomfortable in my heart, I cannot make baseless accusations. But when I see that my wife handles everything properly, I think maybe she just gets along well with others, and those men are willing to put in the effort for her. If I really demand that my wife be like other wives and not interact with other men, we might end up divorced because I know her temper. It cannot be changed. If I want to keep this family, then it can only be my tolerance. As long as she doesn't go too far, I'll just turn a blind eye.

Separated by distance, upon reuniting, my wife became frigid

In recent years, my wife was unwilling to stay in our small town anymore. She wanted to go out and explore. I thought it was fine. Changing the environment might be good for both of us.

My wife left, and my ears were finally quiet. But I missed her a lot. It seemed like I forgot all her past faults, and all I could think about were her good qualities. So a few years later, I quit my job and went to work in the city where she was.

But after reuniting, I found that my wife's heart was no longer on me, even though she was still as capable and family-oriented as ever, and she was still as devoted to our son. People often say that women who have strayed will seem distracted at home, but she showed no such signs. At home, she was either doing housework or cooking up new dishes for us. But I still had a feeling because she began not letting me kiss her lips anymore. We could still have sex, but she wouldn't kiss me. Once, I deliberately said, "Kissing is the touchstone. Not kissing means there's no love." She didn't react after hearing it. She just wouldn't let me kiss her. Later, having sex became difficult too. She would either say she wasn't ready or that her body wasn't feeling well, making me lose interest despite my initial excitement. So I stopped trying. Over time, I lost the desire altogether. Being constantly rejected isn't a pleasant experience, especially at my age. Constant rejection makes me feel embarrassed. Once, I couldn't help but get angry and said, "You're my wife, you need to fulfill your duties." She replied, "Alright, I'll fulfill my duties today." During the act, she didn't make a sound. I didn't know what was wrong with her until I turned on the light and saw her biting the quilt tightly, tears streaming down her face, enduring it with an expression of unbearable pain. I felt heartbroken and disheartened. What was this? It was worse than rape. Was I even human? From then on, unless she initiated it, I wouldn't force myself on her. And she, every ten days or half a month, would take the initiative once. But when I acted, she fell silent again. I wanted to turn on the light to look at her, but she wouldn't let me. So, every time, the image of her biting the quilt and crying flashed before my eyes, making me unable to muster any enthusiasm. Later, we actually spent two or three months sleeping in the same bed without touching each other. Sometimes I thought it was funny. A man and a woman sleeping in the same bed without any desires—wasn't that reaching some sort of realm? Engels once said a very classic sentence: "Marriage without love is immoral." I thought the morality foreigners talk about must not be the morality we understand—it should mean humanity. I often lay in bed thinking about this sentence. Truly, marriage without love is inhumane. It almost turns a good man into a eunuch.

Last year, I lost my mobile phone. Because I couldn't remember many numbers, my wife lent me hers while she borrowed someone else's. The next day, I received a text message that read: "My love for you will never change." When I saw it, I was extremely angry and confronted her, asking her to explain what it meant. My wife calmly said, "There's nothing else. It's just something people send around for fun." But I knew it couldn't possibly be just for fun. Later, I asked her how she planned to handle the matter, and unexpectedly, she said, "If you really don't believe me, let's get divorced. But I'll give you a month's time, so you don't regret it. People tend to make decisions they regret when they're impulsive." Her calmness was something I never expected. But if there was really nothing going on, as she said, why did she bring up divorce? I wanted her to explain more, or make a commitment or assurance of some kind. I thought I could forgive her. But she did nothing, only proposing divorce. The month gave me time to cool down. Even if I hadn't caught her in the act, if I saw them together, would I divorce my wife? Despite harboring doubts about her over the years, thinking about leaving her and making my son leave such a loving mother wasn't something I was prepared for. Reflecting on it, maybe I should be more tolerant. For the sake of the family, the son, her, and myself, it might be the best option. So after a month, I didn't bring up the matter again, and neither did she mention divorce. We acted as if nothing had happened.

But I know, she doesn't love me anymore. There's really someone else in her heart. Why hasn't she left me, left home and our son? Maybe it's because of her kindness.

My wife helps me find a "wife" under the guise of recruitment

Later, we had the opportunity to become our own bosses. I became the general manager, and my wife managed finance and personnel. One day, my wife said she wanted to hire an assistant, claiming she was too busy. Three months passed, and people kept coming for interviews, but no one was hired. I asked her, "What kind of person are you hiring? Are you selecting a partner? Why is it so hard?" She looked at me and said, "Yes, I'm choosing a wife." "Haha," I laughed, "Go ahead and choose. See if you can find someone as good as you." I joked with my wife.
