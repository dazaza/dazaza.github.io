June 20th - Diary

by anonymous on 2012-02-09 14:18:45

Another day has passed, and I feel that time seems to go by very quickly but also very slowly. I think the fast part is because every day there are so many things to do, and before you know it, a day has gone by. The slow part is because it feels like we've done so much - buying goods, opening Taobao stores, finding storefronts, opening shops - and it feels like we've done a lot of good things, almost as if we've become a different person. Now, all I think about is how to make my store better, how to make myself a useful person. It feels like it's been a very long time, but when I look back, from when I first started with Taobao to now having a physical store, it's been less than 20 days.

The reason why time goes by so quickly and in such a rush actually has something to do with my personality. I always work very urgently; when I encounter a problem, I will keep thinking about how to solve it and then want to fix it as soon as possible. From the moment I suddenly had the idea to start a business on Taobao, I was very eager to plan every step. I love to think and plan, but I don't like to investigate. I always act based on my feelings, and my feelings have always been overly idealized, which often leads to disappointment. This time, indeed, I became overly idealistic again, not conducting proper research or asking others for their opinions, completely acting based on my personal feelings. It's just like when I play StarCraft - I don't like to scout or learn from others; I completely rely on my own understanding and feelings to play. I am a stubborn person who can be very obstinate about my own understanding and ideas. I decided to complete my "StarCraft Legend" and make it into a CD to sell, and I would import some premium anime dolls to sell. I believed that since I sold them cheaply and had the StarCraft community as promotion, my products would sell well. But this time, I was overly idealistic again. I made too many CDs and bought too many dolls. I thought that even if I couldn't sell them all in a month, I could definitely sell them all within six months. But reality was harsh - the sales were far from what I had anticipated, and I ended up with unsold inventory, unresolved problems. So, I began to consider solutions. According to my original plan, I would buy a batch of goods to sell on Taobao first, and then when the situation improved, I would open a physical store, selling both online and offline. However, due to overbuying and weak sales on Taobao, I realized that relying solely on Taobao wouldn't work. So, I hurriedly opened a physical store. Finding a storefront wasn't easy, and eventually, I found one downstairs from my home. Although the monthly rent was low, and I could transfer the broadband without cost, the location inside the residential area didn't have enough foot traffic. As a result, before solving the first problem, a second issue arose, making the storefront a burden.

So, I began to think hard about new solutions. Currently, what I've come up with is to turn my store into a distinctive boutique shop, attracting young people to specifically come to my store to buy. Therefore, advertising is needed. I've already decided on my latest action plan. I plan to print a batch of business cards introducing my Taobao store and physical store. At the same time, I will go to bustling areas every evening to set up a stall, handing out cards while vending. This way, I will advertise for both my Taobao store and physical store and quickly sell off the dolls I have in stock.

I always work very urgently, without investigation, relying on my feelings and personal understanding to act. I hope this time I can resolve some issues. I believe that as long as I keep trying and thinking, there will be good results. The key is continuous learning and correcting my own flaws.
