ThinkForex: Euro holds gains in early trade, pound edges lower

by think5627 on 2011-09-16 09:36:52

Source: Asia Foreign Exchange Network

Author: ThinkForex

The latest news on the 16th showed that due to the coordinated actions of major central banks to increase liquidity to the European banking system, the euro against the dollar retained gains in the early Asian session on Friday. The current quote is 1.3849. However, the Greek debt crisis is still at a critical moment and it is unlikely that the rising trend of the euro against the dollar will continue.

The European Central Bank, the Federal Reserve, the Swiss National Bank, the Bank of Japan, and the Bank of England will coordinate actions to provide dollar liquidity to help the banking industry get through the end-of-year period of liquidity shortage. Eurostat data showed that the seasonally adjusted employment rate in the Eurozone increased by 0.3% in the second quarter, rising from 1.465 billion people in the first quarter to 1.47 billion people. This is the largest increase since the first quarter of 2008. Yves Mersch, a member of the Governing Council of the European Central Bank, said that Eurozone countries with AAA credit ratings could issue common bonds. However, if one country loses its AAA rating, it must repay the debt.

The Bank of England (BOE) announced the latest results of the BOE/GfK NOP Inflation Attitudes Survey which showed that in August this year, public expectations for UK inflation over the next year were 4.2%, compared to 3.9% in May, reaching the highest level since August 2008. Nearly one-third of people expected that the UK inflation rate would exceed 5%.

The market may now focus on the informal meeting of the Council of Economic and Financial Affairs of Europe, where US Treasury Secretary Geithner will attend. It is speculated that Geithner may propose ideas similar to the US Troubled Asset Relief Program/Term Asset-Backed Securities Loan Facility to EU finance ministers to help troubled European banks out of difficulties and release more funds. Earlier today, newly appointed IMF Chairman Lagarde just stated that European banks need to raise more funds.

Euro against the dollar: After the announcement of joint provision of dollar financing by the world's major central banks, the euro/dollar once touched a high of 1.3935, refreshing a one-week high. It is expected that risk sentiment will return to the market in the Asian session on Friday, and the euro/dollar is expected to continue exploring space above 1.39. From a technical point of view, the euro/dollar is near the 10-day moving average line, the MACD indicator green energy column contracts, and the KDJ indicator turns upward. If the exchange rate breaks through the 1.3980 level, then the rebound target will point to the 1.4100 level. If the exchange rate falls below the 1.3480 level, then the adjustment target will point to the 1.3310 level.

Pound against the dollar: The pound/dollar slightly declined, currently trading around the 1.5800 level. From a technical point of view, the pound/dollar is below the 5-day moving average line, the MACD indicator green energy column contracts, and the KDJ indicator is flat. If the exchange rate breaks through the 1.6110 level, then the rebound target will point to the 1.6230 level. If the exchange rate falls below the 1.5650 level, then the adjustment target will point to the 1.5600 level.

Australian dollar against the dollar: The Australian dollar/dollar maintained consolidation near 1.0330. Since the overnight New York session, the exchange rate has been consolidating at this level. Some analysts pointed out that it is expected that the Asia-Pacific market will continue the risk-positive sentiment since last night, and regional stock markets are expected to follow the trends of overnight US and European stock markets. Key resistance for the Australian dollar/dollar is in the 1.0360-80 area, and there is hope to initiate an attack within this time zone. Once it breaks through, the rebound target further points to 1.05.
