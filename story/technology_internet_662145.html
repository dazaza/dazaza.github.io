Oriental Zun noticed an unusual force infiltrating his body.

by bacc6n91 on 2012-02-09 19:18:50

The inn attendant didn't need to be called and already knew to serve the two men ten taels of beef and four taos of wine. On every table in the small shop, there was a bundle of chopsticks, but both Qie and Fengchen preferred to grab the beef with their hands. When they got excited during their scrambles, they would even resort to direct combat with fists and palms. Unfortunately, Fengchen's character in the game has always been somewhat frail, so he could never outcompete Qie, often resulting in a four-six split of the beef.

As soon as the two sat down, the inn attendant had already brought out the beef, just about to fetch the wine when he was pulled back by Qie.

"Attendant, don't bring that lousy wine, bring Dukang!" said Qie, then turning to look at Fengchen with disdain, "How can you drink this kind of murky wine all day? Don't even know where it’s dug up from some sewer."

The attendant seemed puzzled by the term "sewer," but said nothing and went inside to fetch two jugs of Dukang wine.

"If it's Dukang, you're paying." Fengchen said nonchalantly, taking advantage of Qie's momentary distraction to quickly grab two more pieces of beef.

"Tch, is that all the ambition you have?" Qie feigned indifference, but his hands were merciless in snatching the beef. "By the way, you're still paying for the beef."

"I say, the cost of buying beef compared to buying Dukang is just a fraction..." complained Fengchen.

"I don't have enough money." Qie mumbled unclearly while chewing on the beef.

"Damn, no money yet still drinking Dukang." The Dukang wine in the small shop might not be top-notch, but its taste was still quite authentic. This one meal would consume most of the money earned from fighting monsters all day. There were many discussions on the game forum arguing that the main city of Chang'an should be in Xi'an, but the official reason, based on the background being set in the Later Tang Dynasty of the Five Dynasties and Ten Kingdoms era, kept the Chang'an city located in the capital Luoyang.

"Haha, today is Qingming Festival, must drink Dukang..." Qie gulped down large mouthfuls of wine.

"So drunk already...? Isn't today April 23rd?"

"That's right, today is my fifteenth Qingming Festival..." Qie suddenly paused mid-grab for the beef, raising his head to look at Fengchen, seemingly tipsy, "You and her, you look really alike..."

On the desolate mountain road, inside an apparently dilapidated wooden house, five people were scattered across the four corners resting. There were no lights in the house, not even any windows through which moonlight could pass, only a small skylight on the roof casting a bit of light. Dongfang Zun carefully reconnected his dislocated left arm, suppressing the pain without making a sound. He used the faint moonlight to examine the other four people in the room. Each one was meticulously cleaning the bloodstains and wounds off their clothes and skin. Recalling the previous battle, Dongfang Zun couldn't help but feel a sense of relief, relaxing his body slightly.

Suddenly, Dongfang Zun sensed an unusual force infiltrating his body. He hurriedly checked his internal power (Tai Li), but found that his fire-attribute Tai Li, upon encountering this external force, was extinguished like a flame, emitting a slight sizzling sound, and was suppressed back. Then, like a flood breaking through a dam, the invading Tai Li suddenly dissolved into his bloodstream. Dongfang Zun felt a severe shock to his spirit, instantly losing control over his body, unable to move even a finger. The originally subtle sounds of his companions tending to their wounds abruptly stopped, quiet enough to hear a pin drop. Dongfang Zun's heart sank; he had already realized that what controlled him was the Blood Fixation Technique inspired by the Yi Jin Jing created by the Water family, and clearly, in terms of Tai Li level and spirit, he was no match for the person wielding this Blood Fixation Technique.

"Kill them all." A deep male voice came from outside the room. Dongfang Zun watched as the wooden door opposite him slowly opened, and several rapid dart sounds were suddenly cut off. His heart turned cold; the mechanism had failed, and the wooden door, which was actually made of fine steel and multiply locked, had provided no obstruction whatsoever.

Three masked black-clad figures walked in from the now wide-open doorway, carrying no weapons, yet swiftly pinched the throats of those sitting paralyzed in the corners. Dongfang Zun watched as the three deftly killed his four companions, countless strategies flashing through his mind, none of which were feasible.

The moonlight streaming in from the doorway was suddenly blocked by a figure, another voice coming from outside, a magnetic elderly male voice, "The third son of the Eastern lineage of the Fire family... cannot be killed. Just cripple his legs..."

Dongfang Zun had no time to ponder the meaning of these words before the figure in front of him flashed forward at incredible speed. He only felt a sharp pain below his knees before everything turned pitch black, losing consciousness.

Dongfang Zun abruptly bolted upright in bed, drenched in cold sweat. He wiped his body, changed clothes, using his arms to support himself onto his wheelchair, and slowly exited the room.

That night's great fire, the red flames blooming in the night sky like fireworks, and he was discarded not far from the wooden house, able only to gaze sideways at this feast of high temperatures. That instant of pain, after all these years, he still relived in his dreams repeatedly. Every time, he tried to resolve the situation in his dream, but each attempt ended in failure. The Blood Fixation Technique against an opponent of lesser skill was almost an absolute killing move, undefeated in ten matches.

He spread out his right palm; the calluses formed from practicing spear techniques had gradually smoothed out. Not far away, voices could be heard, but he knew without looking that they were irrelevant conversations to him.

"I'm leaving first; this trip will probably take five or six days to return. If the old man asks, just tell him I went to Syria to arrange an economic suppression plan with Huo Chuzai." This was the second brother's voice. Actually, he didn't need anyone to relay messages for him because his loud voice ensured almost everyone in the villa could hear what he was saying.

"Um, travel safe," This was the eldest brother's voice, an extremely pleasant baritone that contrasted sharply with the second brother's voice, sounding like a mosquito hum, "I'll go check on Zun, today..."

"Why bother checking on him? It's been ten years, still can't let go of things, what a wimp!" Dongfang Zun heard the loud slam of a door and sighed, turning his wheelchair in the opposite direction.

"Zun," a voice behind him called out, the footsteps of the eldest brother approaching, "You usually take an hour nap in the afternoon. Why are you up so early? If you're uncomfortable, I can push you around a bit."

"No need." Silence descended, Dongfang Zun instinctively held his breath, wanting to feel suffocated.

"Is avoidance useful?" The footsteps stopped, "Come, I'll show you someone."

Dongfang Zun didn't respond, allowing himself to be pushed to the west courtyard. Spring was deep, and the courtyard was full of vitality. In the central open space, there were several people practicing gun techniques or boxing. One person in the center, regardless of the weather not yet warming up, was bare-chested practicing spear techniques, every movement serious and precise. The only thing not quite harmonious was that this person's left arm was severed at the shoulder, leaving only his right hand to hold the spear.

"Huo Nanfei isn't much older than you, but he can be your master for a reason. The blow he once received may not have been as profound as yours, but as long as he could rise again, he's better than you. Understand?"

The footsteps gradually faded away. Dongfang Zun squinted, gazing at the scene before him.

Am I? Am I yearning for strength?

Tianzun was once again startled by the sight of the two people in front of him at the resurrection point. He awkwardly watched as Qie, supported by Fengchen, continuously vomited, unsure of what to do, silently marveling that even getting drunk in this game was possible.

When Qie slowly woke up from his drunken state, Xin You Ling Jie and Yu Nanfei had already logged online. Since Hei Yueya did not intend to join the guild they were forming, she left to find other teams to level up. After teasing Qie a bit, the group set off towards Baihua Tower to handle guild affairs. Tianzun applied to establish a guild, and the others responded first. Soon, the people Tianzun contacted also gathered, quickly surpassing the required thirty members. A new line appeared in their attribute panels: "Guild: Ronin's Home."

System Notice: Due to your guild "Ronin's Home" being the second guild in the main city of Chang'an, it automatically gains the qualification to purchase an outpost outside the city.

A batch of people joined the guild and began conversing with each other. For a time, not only the street side but also the guild channel was in chaos. Someone requested the guild leader to speak, so Tianzun gave an impromptu speech on the street, naturally extolling the benefits of not registering for a specialized profession. Because it was the second guild in the main city of Chang'an, the system broadcasted it on the public channel. Many passersby, seeing that the guild seemed to have decent popularity, also applied to join. Within an hour of the guild's establishment, the number of members exceeded one hundred, nearing the upper limit of a level one guild. Tianzun didn't expect to recruit a full roster immediately, so he promptly organized the members into groups to level up.

As groups left one by one, Tianzun did not lead any group to level up. Instead, he pulled the four people to the side of Baihua Tower, where someone was waiting for him. The woman had waist-length jet-black hair, a delicate face but a cold expression, wearing a blue gown that reached her ankles. Surprisingly, her bare jade-like feet were not on the ground but floated about five centimeters above it. She merely nodded at Tianzun, then her gaze drifted to Fengchen who was still supporting Qie behind Tianzun.

"You're awake." Her voice was like a gentle stream, steady yet revealing a hint of excitement.

"Are you...?" Fengchen looked at this emotionless ice beauty, feeling a wave of familiarity wash over him. That exquisitely carved face seemed to appear many times in his memories, but at the moment, he still couldn't recall anything.

The woman suddenly smiled faintly, and this smile was like a ray of sunlight piercing through the icy snow, giving Fengchen a sense of warmth. But in an instant, the woman returned to her cold demeanor, her tone indifferent, "It doesn't matter, you'll remember..."

Xin You Ling Jie timidly asked beside her, "Sister, are you an NPC...?"

Tianzun couldn't help but laugh, "Let me introduce you, this is the expert I mentioned, Wind Seven Seven. She's also a player, but having accepted a unique quest, she obtained a unique profession, which makes her different from ordinary players. It's due to some characteristics of her unique profession. Alright, let's go level up."

The group stopped asking questions and prepared to fight monsters. Who knew that Qie, still being supported by Fengchen, suddenly shouted loudly, "Beautiful lady, marry me!"

Fengchen pushed Qie, causing him to directly tilt and faint.

"Seems like drunkards all react slowly..." Fengchen commented.
