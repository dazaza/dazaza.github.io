Ancient Qing Dynasty Civilian Residences with Blue Bricks and Tiles Discovered in Shimen, Hunan: Extremely Rare to Find Them So Well-Preserved

Journalists learned from the cultural relics department of Shimen County that provincial and municipal cultural relics experts recently gathered in Shimen to further investigate two Qing Dynasty civilian residences discovered on October 23: Zheng Jincái's Beacon House and the "Scholar" Beacon House.

Zheng Jincái's Beacon House is located in Group 16, Shan Yangchong Village, Sansheng Township, Shimen. According to the *Zheng Family Genealogy*, this beacon house was built in the 35th year of Emperor Qianlong's reign during the Qing Dynasty (1770). It features a blue brick and tile construction made of bricks and wood, with a pier-and-gable style architecture. The open-air courtyard is surrounded by 24 sections. Both the main gate and side gates are in the form of memorial archways, with flying eaves at the top featuring brick-style dougong brackets and painted decorations. The windows and doors are adorned with patterns of Bagua trigrams, landscapes, and human figures, with vivid stone carvings and wood carvings. About 200 meters west of the beacon house lies an ancient tomb cluster of the Zheng family, which is also well-preserved.

The "Scholar" Beacon House is located in Group 1 of Yijia Bay Village, Taiping Town. This building was originally constructed during the Daoguang era of the Qing Dynasty and underwent major renovations in the fourteenth year of the Republic of China. Originally consisting of two sections, it suffered partial damage during the Cultural Revolution. In the 1970s, part of it was dismantled for the construction of Jiudu River Middle School. Now only the front five rooms remain, with the central hall flanked by two rooms on each side and an upper floor as a loft. The main structure is made of brick and wood, with the gate being made of bluestone.

Staff members stated that these two well-preserved civilian residences, both in the style of ancient Qing Dynasty blue brick and tile architecture, are extremely rare in the Changde area. They provide physical evidence for studying the evolution of ancient architectural art in the Li River Basin.
