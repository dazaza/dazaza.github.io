Apple under Cook's leadership

by anonymous on 2013-08-26 14:17:59

Two years ago today, Tim Cook officially became the CEO of Apple. Prior to this, Cook had been handling the company's daily operations and even served as interim CEO. However, on August 24, 2011, Steve Jobs resigned from his position as CEO, and Tim Cook’s role transitioned from Chief Operating Officer to Chief Executive Officer. So, what has Cook done in these two years?

**Foxconn Issues**

Less than six months after becoming Apple's CEO, the company faced a tricky public relations issue. The New York Times published an article criticizing Foxconn, Apple's largest contract manufacturing partner, highlighting poor working and living conditions for Foxconn workers. Although similar reports had surfaced before, the New York Times claimed that Apple was aware of these issues but had taken no action.

Cook quickly responded by sending emails to employees and the media, assuring them that Apple cared about every worker in its global supply chain. The company had done everything possible to improve working conditions, and subsequently, Apple partnered with the Fair Labor Association to review its supply chain. Recent surveys also mentioned improvements in Foxconn's working environment.

**iOS 6 Maps Issue**

By the end of 2012, Apple decided to remove Google Maps from iOS 6 and use its own mapping service. Subsequently, Apple Maps faced criticism due to crazy overlays and data errors. Consumers worldwide discovered mistakes in the map data, placing Tim Cook in a PR crisis. In response, Cook quickly issued a public apology letter. This time, Tim Cook apologized for Apple's failure to meet expectations, acknowledging that his team hadn't provided "the best experience" to consumers, and promised that the company would do everything possible to correct these data errors.

**Apple Stock Breaks $700**

Apart from the Maps scandal, sales of the iPhone 5 were very strong. Within 24 hours of pre-orders opening, over 2 million orders were placed, and within three days of release, sales reached 5 million units. This propelled Apple's stock past the $700 mark for the first time. However, Apple's stock is currently at $501, and AAPL has shown a downward trend since surpassing $700. At that time, just one year into his role as CEO, Cook had driven the stock to $700, indicating that Wall Street still had confidence in him.

**Executive Restructuring**

On October 29 last year, Apple issued a PR announcement stating that Scott Forstall, head of iOS and Senior Vice President, would be leaving the company. Since the launch of the iPhone, Scott had led iOS development and was rumored to be one of Jobs' favorite executives. Needless to say, this was one of the biggest decisions made by Cook since taking office. Recent Reuters reports suggest that Scott's departure was due to the Maps scandal.

After dismissing Forstall, Cook assigned responsibility for map service development to Eddy Cue, design to Jony Ive, and iOS development to Craig Federighi. While we don’t know if the new executive-led iOS development team will succeed, everything will become clear this fall.

**iPad mini Launch**

Apple launched the iPad in 2010, but it wasn't until last autumn that they introduced the 7.9-inch iPad mini. In November 2012, Apple released the iPad mini, which, despite lacking a Retina display, many analysts believed would outsell the regular iPad.

Although Tim Cook lacks the foresight of Steve Jobs, he still needs to release new products. The reviews of these products not only directly affect Apple's profits but also its brand image and investor relations. The iPad mini was the first entirely new product released under Cook’s leadership, fortunately, it was a success.

In the past two years, Cook has played a crucial role during times of problems, changes, and product launches at Apple. Under Cook's leadership, the giant ship that is Apple is steadily moving forward. Although AAPL is currently around $500, it's important to note that the stock has risen by $100 in the past two months. The next two years will truly test Cook's abilities, with multiple new products expected to be released this fall, and we may see entirely new categories of products like iTV and iWatch in the coming years. We await Apple's innovations together.
