The kindest person in the world

by bogcwkdj on 2012-03-09 10:36:56

A guest was about to arrive at a restaurant - Mr. Selle, a French journalist who was paralyzed in the lower limbs and sitting in a wheelchair. On this morning, after the hotel front desk received the notice, they immediately arranged for a dedicated person to go to the airport to meet him. After arriving at the hotel, the bellboy directly took him to the guest room that had been arranged long ago, and the reception at the front desk sent someone to the room to handle the check-in procedures...

After settling in, he received a series of special treatments in the hotel, which deeply moved him.

After entering the room, he slightly tidied up his luggage and then lay down on the bed to rest. Not long after, the doorbell rang, and it was Xiao Fu, the server responsible for his floor. After some greetings, Xiao Fu told Mr. Selle that although he had mobility issues, he didn't need to have any worries here. Every employee in the hotel would not only be ready to follow his commands at any time but also willing to fulfill all his special requests.

Mr. Selle candidly told Xiao Fu that he indeed had many concerns before coming here, but seeing the hotel staff giving him so much unexpected care and attention, he was no longer afraid. Then he mentioned his specific plan for this visit.

First, he would attend a large-scale trade seminar tomorrow. The afternoon after tomorrow, he would visit a certain light textile factory in the suburbs to learn about their support for the Hope Project. He would also visit several service stars. At this point, he paused slightly, as if there was more to say, but he seemed hesitant. Xiao Fu encouraged him to speak freely, assuring him that the hotel would do its best to ensure the successful completion of his trip to Shenyang. Encouraged by Xiao Fu, Mr. Selle finally revealed his thoughts: it turned out that he had heard about the Forbidden City and Beiling Park here when he came, both closely related to the history of the Qing Dynasty. He wanted to visit them, but he hesitated to ask because it would bring too much trouble to the hotel.

"Mr. Selle, thank you for your trust in us. Although our hotel does not offer the service of accompanying guests on tours, your situation is special. I will report to the leadership, and we will try our best to make you satisfied," Xiao Fu said sincerely.

Half an hour later, the manager of the housekeeping department arrived at Mr. Selle's room, telling him that all the work and activity plans would receive full support from the hotel. In addition, the housekeeping department assigned Xiao Fu and another server, Xiao Feng, to specifically take charge of all his activities here. After hearing this, Mr. Selle tightly held the manager’s hand, with tears welling up in his eyes.

Not long after the manager left, Xiao Fu entered Mr. Selle's room again, asking if he wanted to have dinner in the room. After dinner, Xiao Feng was on duty, helping Mr. Selle undress, bathe, and get dressed... Mr. Selle stayed in this city for 4 days. On the morning of the day he was leaving, he asked Xiao Fu to accompany him to the Catholic church, where he thanked God most devoutly for allowing him to encounter "the most kind-hearted people in the world" here. He prayed that God would bless all the kind people who treated him well here.

Analysis:

Mr. Selle's prayer was heartfelt, and it was also the fairest evaluation of the hotel's service quality.

This hotel truly implemented the service tenet of "making guests feel at home" during the entire process of receiving Mr. Selle.

First, Mr. Selle experienced convenience and warmth right after he arrived at the hotel. Sending someone to pick him up at the airport, directly taking him to the room, and handling the check-in procedures in the room were all things Mr. Selle had hoped for before he arrived. What even more reflected the hotel treating guests like "emperors" was that soon after Mr. Selle entered the room, the server Xiao Fu was ordered to promise the guest that they would do everything possible to help solve all his problems. For a guest who was paralyzed in the lower limbs and unfamiliar with the new environment, this was the warmest assurance, reflecting that the hotel indeed stood in Mr. Selle's shoes, thinking what he thought and acting urgently on what he needed. Due to Xiao Fu's promise, Mr. Selle completely trusted the hotel and was willing to reveal all his plans.

During the 4 days Mr. Selle spent here, he indeed received meticulous care from all aspects of the hotel, successfully completing his work plan and realizing his long-held wish to tour historical sites. It was natural for Mr. Selle to be moved to tears.

Receiving guests like Mr. Selle is undoubtedly labor-intensive and requires a higher cost, but it is a good opportunity for the hotel to showcase its level of service. All hotels should seize such "God-given" opportunities to fully display the skills they have honed in everyday practice.
