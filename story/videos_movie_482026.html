Louisiana in the 18th century. After losing his wife and daughter, Louis was heartbroken. He met a vampire named Lestat who cast a spell on him, turning him into a vampire. With a kind heart, Louis refused to kill for blood and survived by drinking animal blood. Lestat, however, was determined to teach Louis to become a true vampire.

In New Orleans, Louis met a young girl named Claudia who had lost her family to a plague. Unable to resist temptation, Louis eventually drank Claudia's blood. Despite Louis' objections, Lestat turned Claudia into a vampire as well. The three of them formed an unusual family.

Time flew by, and decades passed. Claudia began to resent Lestat for robbing her of the chance to grow up. To leave Lestat with Louis, she tricked Lestat into drinking the blood of a dead person and then killed him, throwing his body into a swamp. However, just as Louis and Claudia were preparing to go to Paris, Lestat, who had been grotesquely transformed, suddenly appeared. In the ensuing fight, Louis grabbed a lamp and threw it at Lestat, immediately engulfing him in flames. Amidst the roaring fire that consumed half the city, Louis and Claudia boarded a ship bound for Paris.

The two lived a life in Paris that resembled both father and daughter and lovers. They also encountered others of their kind - a group of vampires led by Armand who lived underground in a theater. Armand gave Louis much guidance. However, in the world of vampires, harming one’s own kind is the only crime. As a result, a rift appeared between Louis and Claudia. At Claudia's request, he turned her into a vampire as a substitute for leaving her. However, Santiago, a prominent vampire, suddenly appeared with a group, capturing them. Louis was sealed in a wall, while Claudia and her companion were executed by exposure to sunlight.

Louis was saved by Armand and vowed revenge. Soon, he set fire to their lair when the vampires were asleep, killing Santiago and many other vampires. He declined Armand's invitation to live together and left Paris alone. Without Claudia, Louis was deeply affected. He wandered aimlessly, never able to heal the wounds in his heart. In 1988, Louis returned to New Orleans and found Lestat, weak and living in solitude. Louis recounted his experiences to a young journalist named Malloy, hoping to warn future generations. Unexpectedly, after hearing the story, Malloy was deeply attracted and insisted that Louis turn him into a vampire. Disgusted, Louis left him. Disappointed, Malloy drove home alone. However, Lestat suddenly appeared in the back seat of the car. He drank Malloy's blood, healed his injuries, and drove off into the distance with Malloy. A new vampire story was about to begin...
