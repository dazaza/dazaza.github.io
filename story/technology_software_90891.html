Yonyou Software: No Revenue Growth Despite Incremental Increase; Software Industry Accelerates Internet Transition

by xingyuehebut on 2008-09-10 15:46:27

Yonyou Software (600588) has recently disclosed its semi-annual report. According to the financial report, Yonyou Software achieved a revenue of 685 million yuan and a net profit of 112 million yuan in the first half of this year, with earnings per share at 0.24 yuan. Compared to the same period last year, Yonyou Software's revenue increased by 30.19%, while its net profit decreased by 25.12%.

The increase in Yonyou Software's revenue but the decrease in net profit has caught the attention of industry insiders. They believe that this situation is caused by multiple factors. In the current severe economic situation, if Yonyou Software wants to reverse the decline in net profit and achieve better development in the future, it must accelerate its transition to the Internet.

Mixed Results in the Semi-Annual Report

According to Yonyou Software's semi-annual report, the company achieved a revenue of 685 million yuan in the first half of this year, an increase of 30.19% compared to the same period last year; net profit was 112 million yuan, a decrease of 25.12% year-on-year; return on net assets was 5.48%, a reduction of 6.09 percentage points.

In terms of specific software product sales, the company's core software sales business grew by 30.59%, with good profitability; technical services and training business grew by 27.70%, with a gross profit margin declining by 6.64 percentage points but still maintaining above 80%. Notably, Yonyou Software's non-operating income in the first half of the year reached 126 million yuan, mainly including government subsidies, VAT refunds received by the company, and the proceeds from the disposal of the Yonyou Building, among which the proceeds from the sale of the Yonyou Building amounted to 78.52 million yuan, and non-operating income accounted for approximately 94% of total profits.

Overall, Yonyou's performance in the first half of this year was mixed, with revenue increasing but net profit decreasing. Tong Sheng, an analyst at Orient Securities, believed that Yonyou Software's main business performed well, and the main reason for the significant drop in operating profit and net profit was serious investment losses. Currently, Yonyou Software holds about 25% of the domestic management software market share, ranking at the top for many consecutive years, and has strong R&D capabilities, consistently keeping up with market trend changes. With the high entry barriers in the management software industry, the company is expected to maintain certain advantages in the coming years. However, the decline in net profit needs to be taken seriously by the enterprise.

Two Factors Weighing Down Financial Data

In general, two main factors dragged down Yonyou Software's financial data for the first half of this year.

Firstly, although Yonyou Software's main business revenue steadily increased, securities investment weighed down the company's net profit. In the first half of the year, Yonyou Software achieved a revenue of 685 million yuan, an increase of 30.19% year-on-year, operating profit of 12 million yuan, a decrease of 89.84% year-on-year, and net profit of 114 million yuan, a decrease of 22.97% year-on-year. The main reason for this situation was the company's securities investment, as the securities investment gains last year were 71 million yuan, while this period incurred a loss of 96 million yuan.

Secondly, various cost expenditures significantly increased in the first half of this year, leading to a decline in Yonyou Software's gross profit margin. In the first half of this year, Yonyou Software's net cash flow from operating activities was 23 million yuan, a decrease of 36.19% year-on-year. The decline in net cash flow from operating activities was mainly due to a 31.82% increase in labor costs in the first half of 2008, reaching 383 million yuan, and a 55.36% increase in taxes paid, reaching 169 million yuan. The company's gross profit margin decreased by 1.58 percentage points year-on-year, mainly because the company encouraged project outsourcing, resulting in increased costs paid to outsourcing companies. Additionally, rising prices and employee wages led to higher personnel expenses, which also resulted in a significant increase in operating expense ratios compared to the first half of last year.

Although these two factors dragged down Yonyou Software's financial data, overall, the company's main business revenue continued to grow steadily, showing a good development momentum. In the future, Yonyou Software may likely expand the market through acquisitions, quickly occupying niche industry markets.

Accelerating Transition to Internet Services

Although Yonyou Software's overall performance in the first half of the year was good, in the context where the connection between the Internet and software products is becoming increasingly close, all software manufacturers will face the impact brought by the Internet tide, everything is moving towards the Internet. CCID Consulting research believes that the demand-driving effect of SaaS for small and medium-sized enterprises became evident in the second quarter. As the traditional management software market demand gradually stabilizes, SaaS will be the next new growth point for software manufacturers.

Currently, the software service sector has become the growth center of the software industry. In recent years, although the proportion of software products in total revenue remains above 50%, generally speaking, this proportion is gradually decreasing. On the other hand, software service revenue has grown significantly, becoming the fastest-growing area. Data since 2002 indicates that China's software growth focus has shifted from software products to the software service sector, and the connection with the Internet is getting tighter. At the same time, providing software services via the Internet can effectively reduce corporate operating costs and improve gross profit margins, which is very beneficial to software companies under the current economic conditions.

Major software manufacturers have already accelerated their transition to Internet services. Yonyou Software's Weiku.com, which previously provided software services to users in the ASP model, "restarted" in July, focusing on online bookkeeping, online ordering, and customer marketing projects. Kingdee held a press conference in Beijing on April 2nd to announce its transformation from a product-oriented company to a service-oriented company. Moreover, in Kingdee's "new three-pronged approach," YouShang.com has been elevated to the same level as management software and middleware, demonstrating Kingdee's emphasis on YouShang.com.

In the future, software manufacturers may open up new markets by strengthening cooperation with portal sites and e-commerce websites. Software companies can interact with portal sites and leverage the enormous influence and customer base of portal sites and e-commerce websites to promote products and services to customers who need them. This not only saves time for software companies in finding customers but also expands the market through the brand influence of portals and e-commerce websites.
