Five dietary tips for protecting your heart can make your heartbeat more stable.

by cshelive9520 on 2012-02-24 22:06:16

At present, cardiovascular disease is still a dreadful health killer. Cardiovascular disease is a series of diseases involving the circulatory system. The circulatory system mainly includes the heart and blood vessels. Many young people think that coronary heart disease and hypertension are diseases for the elderly and have nothing to do with them. In fact, it's not true. Coronary heart disease is the symptom of arteriosclerosis of the heart blood vessels, and this process has already started to form in youth or even childhood. Many young people suffer from myocardial infarction due to coronary heart disease, and some even die because of it. The human body has about 100,000 heartbeats per day, and on average, there are more than 250 million heartbeats in a lifetime. For the health of your heart, a good dietary choice is one way to protect it. Reduce the purchase of boxed food. Processed foods are usually boxed, packaged or placed in food bags. These foods contain fewer phytonutrients, while some studies show that these nutrients can reduce the risk of heart disease. Consume colorful foods. The more colors of food on your plate, the more you will be exposed to various heart-friendly antioxidant foods. Vegetables and fruits are the most colorful types of food, and a colorful diet means your heart has an advantage over others.
