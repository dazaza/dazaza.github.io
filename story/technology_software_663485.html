3-Year-Old Boy with Cancer Receives Donations, His Parents Grateful and Register to Donate Corneas

by nif8dfug on 2012-02-11 19:56:40

On August 12, Wang Xianbo signed the cornea donation registration application form with his wife. This photo was taken by our reporter, Zhang Weijian.

Our Binzhou Report on August 12 (Reported by Zhang Weijian and Intern Yu Ronghua) In the morning of August 12, Wang Xianbo and his wife Wang Sue'e, a peasant couple from Liangcai Office in Bincheng District, signed their names separately on the "Shandong Province Cornea Donation Registration Application Form". They became the second couple in Binzhou City to simultaneously donate corneas and also the first peasant couple in Binzhou City to donate corneas.

Their 3.5-year-old son developed malignant tumor

Wang Xianbo is 35 years old this year and is an ordinary farmer from Liangcai Office in Bincheng District. His wife Wang Sue'e is a teacher at a private kindergarten. The reason they did this was entirely because of their son, Wang Zi'shuo.

Wang Zi'shuo was born in 2007. In late August 2010, when he was only 3.5 years old, little Zi'shuo often used his hand to scratch his ear. At the beginning, Wang Xianbo and his wife didn't pay much attention. One day, while scratching his ear, little Zi'shuo took out a coffee-colored item the size of a mung bean. The doctor treated him for otitis media.

One day, when Wang Sue'e was dressing her son, she found that her son couldn't stand up and there was a lump growing in his ear. "When we saw this situation, we were scared. How could a healthy child suddenly not be able to stand? We had no idea what that abscess was." On September 20, 2010, Wang Xianbo and his wife took little Zi'shuo to Beijing 301 Hospital. After a pathological examination, the doctor confirmed that little Zi'shuo had a malignant tumor called rhabdomyosarcoma of the middle ear and mastoid.

Good Samaritans brought hope for life

After the diagnosis, the couple took their child back to Binzhou People's Hospital for treatment. In late April this year, the couple took their child to Beijing 301 Hospital for a follow-up checkup. "The doctor told us that if we continue the treatment for 13 more courses, there might be a possibility of recovery. Currently, we have completed 4 courses of treatment, and there are still 9 courses left." Wang Xianbo told reporters that each course lasts 21 days, and the cost of each course is at least over 10,000 yuan.

Wang Sue'e is a private teacher at a kindergarten in Liangcai Office, earning five or six hundred yuan per month; Wang Xianbo does construction odd jobs in the village. Since little Zi'shuo fell ill, Wang Xianbo and his wife have spent all their savings. "Currently, we have already spent over 200,000 yuan. 140,000 yuan was borrowed from neighbors and relatives, and we have almost borrowed from all acquaintances, but there's nothing we can do." Wang Xianbo told reporters that because they need to take care of little Zi'shuo, neither of them can continue working normally.

After learning about little Zi'shuo's condition, the Liangcai Street Office Education Committee organized a love donation event involving teachers and students from schools within the jurisdiction. "That time, teachers and students together donated over 20,000 yuan for us." Wang Sue'e told reporters that later on, kind-hearted people from the town continued to donate to them, which deeply moved the couple.

After receiving love donations from all sectors of society, Wang Xianbo and his wife started thinking about how to give back to society. "We are both farmers, we don't have any special skills, and we can't afford to give money. But after we pass away, we can donate our corneas to give back to society. This is also our way of showing gratitude. To those who need it, it is also a way to repay the good-hearted people who helped us."
