Safety awareness is required when using calcium hydroxide - toxicity protection.

by malayo on 2012-02-25 11:37:23

Calcium hydroxide is a fine white powder. It absorbs carbon dioxide in the air to form calcium carbonate. It dissolves in acids, ammonium salts, and glycerin, slightly dissolves in water, does not dissolve in alcohol, has strong alkalinity, and can corrode skin, fabrics, and utensils.

For the regular use of calcium hydroxide, the following safety issues should be noted:

1. When it gets into the eyes, flush with running water as soon as possible, then rinse with a 5% ammonium chloride solution or a 0.01% CaNa2-EDTA solution, and finally drop in a 0.5% dyclonine solution.

2. Its dust or suspension droplets irritate the mucous membranes, causing sneezing and coughing. Like alkalis, it can saponify fat, absorb moisture from the skin, dissolve proteins, and irritate and corrode tissues.

3. The maximum allowable concentration is 5 mg/m³.

4. Inhaling lime dust may cause pneumonia. When inhaling dust, steam, codeine, and yao ning can be inhaled, and mustard plaster can be applied on the chest wall.

5. During work, pay attention to protecting the respiratory organs. Wear work clothes, gloves, sealed dust-proof goggles made of dust-proof fibers, and apply ointments containing oils to prevent inhalation of dust.

Packaging and storage:

1. It should be stored in a dry warehouse, strictly preventing dampness, and avoiding co-storage and mixing with acidic substances.

2. In case of fire, water, sand, or general fire extinguishers can be used for firefighting.

3. It is packaged in plastic woven bags lined with polyethylene plastic film.

4. During transportation, rain protection is required.
