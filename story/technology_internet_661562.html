China's group-buying sites, represented by Tuangou.com and 55tuan, are almost all venture-capital funded.

by beetgk2958 on 2012-02-09 10:23:05

Elimination in the Speed-up Phase

Internet analyst Sun Jie believed that "Group-buying websites represented by Tuanbao.com and Wowotuan are almost all using venture capital funds to expand scale irrationally nationwide, crazily advertising, price wars and malicious competition has made the entire industry suffer from heavy losses. None of their dreams of going public have been realized until now, but they are trapped in a dilemma of broken capital chains, forced layoffs and even closing down."

On the first working day after the Spring Festival, nearly a hundred dismissed and unpaid employees of Tuanbao.com entered the Beijing headquarters to "demand salaries", negotiating face-to-face with the company's CEO Ren Chunlei. Ren admitted that "the company is out of money". He took out 200,000 yuan from his own pocket, giving each employee a 500-yuan compensation. On January 30th, Ren posted on Weibo that he was raising money in Shenzhen and said that "if I can't raise money within 15 days, the company may declare bankruptcy."

Analysts said that if 2011 was the reshuffling year for small and medium-sized group-buying websites, then 2012 would be the reshuffling year for large group-buying websites. "In 2011, a total of 1,968 group-buying websites went bankrupt and exited the market, most of which were local small and medium-sized group-buying websites. In 2012, the reshuffling of group-buying websites is happening and will continue. With continuous industry integration and elimination, market concentration will become higher and higher. In the next one and a half years, the competition between group-buying giants will be the main theme."

Just as the long holiday of the Dragon Year Spring Festival ended, two group-buying websites, Tuanbao.com and Wowotuan, encountered an unlucky start to the year. First, Tuanbao.com got involved in the "unpaid salary scandal" during the Spring Festival. Its CEO Ren Chunlei admitted that the company was facing serious financial problems and could not continue. Subsequently, Wowotuan's IPO plan failed again, rejected by its underwriters UBS and Merrill Lynch, among whom Merrill Lynch has withdrawn from the underwriter list. The crisis of these two formerly well-known websites at the beginning of 2012 heralded the entry of the group-buying industry into a larger reshuffling phase this year.

According to third-party data statistics, in 2011, a total of 1,968 group-buying websites went bankrupt and exited the market, most of which were local small and medium-sized group-buying websites. Wowotuan and Tuanbao.com, which were considered dark horses in 2011, started the year with various "misfortunes" and "disadvantages", seemingly announcing that the main theme of the group-buying industry in 2012 would be the acceleration of elimination competitions, making the competitive environment more severe. Past dark horses, due to their own operational issues, might fall into the eliminated camp at any time, just like those fallen meteors whose glow would only flash for a moment before disappearing forever.

The opening match of 2012 began with the failure of two group-buying websites, Tuanbao.com and Wowotuan, seemingly indicating that the reshuffling of the group-buying industry in 2012 would enter a new phase - the acceleration of elimination competitions among large group-buying websites, making the competition more intense and cruel. In fact, before the Spring Festival of the Dragon Year, 24 Coupon had already fallen into the predicament of a broken capital chain, company closure, arrears of merchant payments, large-scale layoffs, station closures, unpaid wages, salary cuts, etc., becoming the first group-buying giant to exit.

An Unlucky Start

The situation of another former group-buying giant, Wowotuan, was even less optimistic. The Spring Festival roadshow plan was rejected by the lead underwriter UBS, and the original underwriter Merrill Lynch chose to withdraw, adding frost to the snow of its IPO path. According to the roadshow PPT of Wowotuan circulating before the New Year, in the first three quarters of 2011, Wowotuan's net revenue was 3.6 million US dollars, and operating losses were 65 million US dollars. If there is no further IPO financing to replenish its blood, it will soon fall into the same outcome as Tuanbao.com due to a broken capital chain.

It seems like a domino effect; Tuanbao.com and Wowotuan quickly fell after 24 Coupon. According to the statistical report of the third-party navigation website Tuan800, both Tuanbao.com and Wowotuan were once among the top 12 group-buying websites nationwide. However, since November 2011, due to a sharp decline in performance, Tuanbao.com has been removed from the list of first-tier group-buying websites and is no longer included in the statistics. Although Wowotuan still ranks among the top 10 currently, its sales in August, September, and October 2011 have continuously plummeted, with particularly poor performance in the fourth quarter.

According to the latest report from Tuan800, the top three group-buying websites have occupied 49% of the market share, compared to 39% in September. The "Matthew Effect" in the group-buying industry is becoming increasingly evident. In the fierce competition among giants, 24 Coupon, Tuanbao.com, and Wowotuan have taken the lead in entering the "elimination list." E-commerce expert Lu Zhenwang revealed the latest news: "After the failure of Wowotuan's IPO, it is expected that major turmoil will arrive, including possible second-round layoffs, station closures, and salary reductions." He also disclosed that Wowotuan plans to reduce the salaries of middle and upper-level management by half, "most of whom were high-salary recruits during the period of crazy expansion."
