Previously, we used cookies to comfort him, but after hearing that feeding cookies at midnight isn't good, we switched to using hawthorn flakes to comfort him. He also drinks water frequently, repeating this five or six times every night, which prevents both me and my partner from getting a good rest. I would like to ask all experienced parents: Is this situation good for his health? If not, how can we change this situation?

Also: My son's physical condition is good. We are in Ruian, Zhejiang, where it gets dark at 6 PM and daylight begins at 6 AM. We have dinner at 4:30 PM when we get off work, and my son usually sleeps before 7:30 PM and wants to get up before dawn.

To supplement: My son has almost no appetite during each meal, and he will even try to put things that cannot be eaten into his mouth.

According to your situation, it might be anorexia, and it is recommended to seek traditional Chinese medicine treatment at the hospital.

My son is already 11 years old, and indeed such phenomena exist. Every first-time mother encounters similar issues. I would like to share my small experience for your reference: you shouldn't always feed him cookies because they won't provide sufficient nutrition. You should give your child more fresh fish and shrimp, as well as more buns and dumplings with a variety of vegetables inside. Don't let him eat the same food every day; children won't like it. Gradually adjust the mealtime according to the adults' schedule. When he doesn't want to eat, don't chase him around while trying to feed him. This will form bad habits. He will tell you when he's hungry, and at that time, make sure he eats enough. Also, don't give him too many snacks between meals because eating them will make him less hungry when it's time for meals. This is a common issue among children. Regarding sleep time, don't let him go to bed too early. After you finish dinner, you should spend more time playing games with him or read illustrated books to him to spark his interest. Gradually delay his bedtime. Rest assured, the child will grow up slowly, and everything will become as you imagine.
