How to manage an online store so that customers come in voluntarily?

Satisfying Answer 1:

Online shoppers are a combination of goal-oriented consumers and experience-seeking consumers. On one hand, they value fashion, convenience, and speed, and show little concern for bidding wars; on the other hand, when choosing a website or merchant, they exhibit significant randomness and impulsiveness in their decisions. Therefore, by closely aligning with these characteristics of online consumers, we can offer the following suggestions to businesses:

4.2.1. Meet personalized needs and cater to the tastes of young people and those with higher education

a. Most online consumers have fixed incomes, and their shopping time is mostly after work or during holidays. Companies can use this information to choose appropriate times to communicate online with consumers, promptly understanding their needs and better recommending products.

b. By studying the demand characteristics of online consumers, it's not difficult to find that individualized needs and proactive consumption are the main features of online consumers. This requires companies to value the opinions and needs of each consumer. This can be achieved by asking consumers to complete a survey before finishing their purchase, providing feedback email addresses, or requiring email addresses during registration for sending surveys and post-purchase evaluations to gain detailed insights into consumer needs.

c. Considering both the external and psychological traits of online consumers, we notice that this group is mainly composed of young people who seek novelty, have curiosity, and lack sufficient patience. Many consumers find the online shopping process cumbersome and difficult to understand, which hinders their online purchases. Moreover, if the network transmission speed is slow and opening web pages takes too long, they will quickly close the current page and look for other websites. Therefore, merchants can enhance communication, optimize processes, improve website access speeds, and continuously introduce new shopping methods or products to attract consumer attention.

d. Online shopping provides young people with DIY opportunities and offers a wide variety of products. If online shopping has uniqueness, it can meet the personalized requirements of this group. Companies can launch products that are both high-quality and affordable, with prices or styles that are very attractive to consumers, and these products are unavailable offline. Consumers can order them online if needed. For example, ViewSonic, known for its monitors, once launched multiple beautifully designed cartoon-shaped monitors available only online.

4.2.2. Strengthen promotion and encourage online purchases

a. Online shopping in our country is still in its infancy, and most internet users know little about it, relying mainly on brand effects for product selection. This requires companies to strengthen advertising campaigns, such as placing ads on bus stop signs, magazines, TV, major portal sites, QQ advertisements, etc. If a company's brand can take the lead in the early development of online shopping and win consumer recognition, the brand is more likely to have an advantage in future fierce competition.

b. Additional value can be provided. From previous research results, it’s clear that online consumers in our country have yet to form stable online shopping habits, and there is still a large degree of randomness and blindness in online shopping. To incentivize and solidify online shopping behavior, merchants can provide additional value. For example, implement reward policies: if consumers meet certain conditions, they can receive points, desktop wallpapers, small games, mobile ringtones, etc., or participate in lottery events. These added values can reinforce consumer shopping habits.

c. Regardless of how sophisticated marketing strategies are, price remains one of the factors that most influence consumer shopping decisions. For companies, consumers choosing online shopping saves a significant amount of management fees, logistics costs, and intermediary fees. At the same time, to stimulate more consumers to choose online shopping, companies should offer substantial price discounts to online shoppers, which benefits both the company and the consumer.

4.2.3. Optimize corporate websites, improve access speed, and enhance network security

a. Before shopping, consumers conduct extensive information searches and analyses. In the online market, search engines become the primary source of information. Therefore, companies should optimize search engines so that consumers see them first, increasing click-through rates.

b. Combine online and offline stores. Network consumers' concerns about network security are very prominent, which is also a bottleneck constraining online shopping development. Most network consumers are willing to accept cash-on-delivery or third-party payment platforms. Therefore, on one hand, merchants should strengthen technical and managerial improvements to enhance "actual" network security; on the other hand, merchants can open offline stores to increase consumers' "perceived" security. Offline stores can be opened in the form of brand stores or flagship stores where consumers can visit, consult, and shop in person. The important distinction from regular stores lies in their main purpose: to establish brands and enhance consumer trust through tangible displays and intangible services.

I suggest a few promotional methods that might be useful to you:

1. Exchange friendly links

To boost your online store's popularity and increase traffic, pay attention to websites related or similar to your store's content, especially those with high traffic and popularity. Once your store is ready, you can discuss linking with them. In the initial stages, others may ignore you due to your lack of traffic, so approach them humbly and sincerely. If you've already linked to their site within your store, they're likely to reciprocate soon, mutual promotion after all.

2. Alumni directory promotion

If you're not far removed from campus life, you probably have an alumni directory. Have you considered utilizing it? Step forward and tell your classmates and friends about your online store. Not only does it allow them to share in your joy of opening a store, but it also gathers popularity. You might even attract some potential customers with products they like.

3. Print business cards

You might think that since most promotions are online where no one meets face-to-face, making business cards would waste cost. Wrong! The store may be online, but you live in real life, and shipping goods happens in reality. Design a unique business card with your contact information and store address, and include a few in your shipments. Due to their appealing design, buyers are unlikely to discard them. If given the chance, they might display it to others, effectively promoting your store. Though I haven't printed business cards for my own store yet, you can create your own using PS software, a printer, and colored ink cartridges—economic and practical. Give it a try!

4. Use keyword ranking

This refers to SEO technology. Collaborate with companies specializing in online promotion optimization, asking them to devise a plan for your store to comprehensively improve your website's ranking. A higher rank increases the likelihood of being found by customers several times over, attracting many potential customers. As long as your store offers what they need, it won't be hard to turn them into loyal customers.

5. Refresh content regularly

The reasoning is self-evident; refreshing pages gives a fresh impression. Don’t keep a stagnant face that bores visitors. Even if your store lacks new projects or products recently, consider rearranging items or adding new text. Regular updates attract attention.

6. Participate in forums

Do you enjoy forums? Don't assume joining them is just for fun. With skillful participation, it can bring benefits. Each forum usually has a personal signature section where you can include your store's address and contact details, either in text or as a beautiful image. When posting or replying, it automatically displays below, subtly promoting without seeming like blatant advertising.

7. Share experiences to attract customers

From the idea of opening a store to building the site, sourcing supplies, and starting sales, if you sit down and talk about it, the ups and downs will surely keep you talking all night. Grab a pen and write them down in words, place them on your own store or forward them to relevant websites and forums with high traffic and popularity. Don't underestimate the power of the pen—it will help you meet more like-minded friends, and the growing traffic and orders will make you smile.

8. Purchase ad space

Lastly, this requires spending money. Buy positions on pages with high traffic, such as "popular product recommendations." Bold the names of products in the categorized lists, change colors, or add beautiful images to attract browsers, bringing a considerable amount of traffic.
