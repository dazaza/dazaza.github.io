Parents have never been forced to bring their children for enrollment on September 1st.

by lieencq09 on 2011-09-17 13:10:42

Yesterday, Principal Gong Min expressed to the reporter: The main students who didn't come are from the middle class, because several new teachers have joined the middle class, and there is a lack of communication between the new teachers and the parents. The school side fully understands the concerns of the parents, and at the same time, will inform the parents daily about the content of the courses that the students have not attended.

"Parents can request retesting if they are not reassured"

Yesterday, parents of the school, when interviewed by relevant media, also mentioned that the testing agency - Shanghai Tongbiao Quality Testing Technology Co., Ltd. - once appeared on the blacklist on the website of the Shanghai Quality Inspection Bureau. "If there is any contamination in the classroom, it may affect the growth and development of children," the parents were very worried about this.

Yesterday, the reporter saw at the campus that several air purifiers were operating in the entrance hall of the kindergarten. On the second and third floors of the classrooms, spider plants and activated carbon packs were placed, electric fans were turned on in each classroom, and all windows were wide open.

Oriental Net (Weibo) September 2 news: According to the report of "Oriental Morning Post" (Weibo), the new Huaihai Middle Road campus of Wunan Kindergarten has just completed renovation and started classes. Parents expressed deep concern over the indoor air quality of the kindergarten on Weibo. On the first day of school, the kindergarten welcomed a total of 130 old and new students, which was 73 fewer than the number of people who should have reported, with a reporting rate of 64%. One class had as few as four students.

On August 31, the school building was still being painted

On August 31, a student's parent recorded on Weibo: Wunan Kindergarten Huaihai branch in Shanghai underwent full renovation this year (as of August 31, painting was still ongoing). Without undergoing the prescribed "Indoor Air Quality Standard" test by the health department, Principal Gong Min rudely required classes to start on schedule on September 1st, citing the need to consult with higher-level leaders.

The new Huaihai Middle Road campus of Wunan Kindergarten is located at No. 1788 Huaihai Middle Road. Yesterday, the kindergarten was supposed to enroll 203 students, but 73 students failed to report on time, with the majority absentees concentrated in the middle class. Out of 108 expected, only 42 actually arrived, including two classes where only 4 and 7 students reported respectively. The small class students were mostly present, with 95 expected and 88 actually attending.

Regarding the concerns of some parents about the indoor air quality, Principal Gong Min told the reporter: The students who did not report on time basically all took leave beforehand, because some new teachers joined the middle class, and the parents were not very familiar with the new teachers, resulting in less communication between both sides. Gong Min said, "We have never forced parents to bring their children on September 1st. Transferring schools or taking leave can be understood. For delayed enrollment students, our teachers will also inform the parents one by one about the daily course content."

In response to the doubts and concerns of the parents, Deputy Director Li of the Infrastructure Station of Xuhui Education Bureau stated that the construction of the school buildings strictly followed the standards. Shanghai Tongbiao Quality Testing Technology Co., Ltd. is a standard enterprise with relevant testing qualifications. "If the parents really do not feel assured, relevant departments will also invite other qualified units for retesting, and the standards will still refer to national mandatory requirements. If air quality problems are found, classes will be immediately suspended."
