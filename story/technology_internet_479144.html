The character "person" consists of only two strokes, one slanting down and the other across, yet it is not easy to write. From the perspective of calligraphy, the fewer the strokes, the harder it is to write well; from the perspective of sociology, these two strokes of "person" carry rich connotations and profound philosophy, making it even harder to write well. If either stroke is not written well, it cannot be called a complete life in its true sense.

One stroke writes growth, the other old age. Life is a process of metabolism, including thought and physique. New ones constantly reproduce, old ones constantly fade away. Only by continuously accepting and absorbing new things, criticizing and abandoning corrupt garbage, can one renew oneself and grow healthily.
