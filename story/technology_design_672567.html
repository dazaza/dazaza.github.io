At the time, Mahjong tiles are pushed out. 18, Transporting tile bend: commonly known as the corner, referred to as small corner inside, large corner outside, which changes the straight motion of the sorted tiles into a turning movement. 19, Tile transport cover: underneath it has brush strips that gently clean the back of the tiles. 20, Tile transport chain: the transmission component for transporting tiles. 21, Panel support frame: the four corners with broken pillars serve to fix and support the panel, relevant to mahjong machine repair. 22, S5 detector: also known as angular positioning photocontrol, controlling the light control midway through the tile transport by the chain. 23, Chain rod: installed on the chain, its upper part pushes the tiles while the lower part has a white reflector for S4 and S5 photocontrols. Reflection signal transmission. 24, Support rod: when the tabletop is flipped up, it supports the tabletop for cleaning the mahjong table. 25, Tile receiving board: high-grade aluminum alloy board for placing sorted mahjong tiles. 26, Dice button: button for rolling dice. 27, Continuous庄indicator lights: there are five in total, one green and four red, showing the number of consecutive wins, program detection display, and fault display. 28, Lift button: button for controlling the central lift door's ascent and descent, pressing any diagonal one during normal tile sorting, pressing two simultaneously for emergency lifting. It serves as a start button during testing. 29, S4 detector: also known as zero position photocontrol, reset photocontrol, controlling the delivery of sorted tiles to the preparation position. 30, Position switch: also called DIP switch, game rule setting switch; setting it to 0 enters the test program. 31, Reverse chain switch: point switch for controlling the reverse rotation of the chain. Press to reverse, release to stop. 32, Motor 03: power motor for pushing the lift rack. 33, Limit switch 03: control switch for the tile receiving board reaching the working position, along with the stop switch for the lift rack. 34, Tile suction ring: also called magnetic ring. 35, Main control board: the switch control system for the fully automatic mahjong table (see attached diagram). 36, Cam 07: rotates according to the working state under the action of motor 07, outputting power. 37, Lifting lever: driven by the lifting cam, it raises or lowers the central lift door. 38, Hall switch: switch group composed of Hall elements, controlling the central lift door rising to the top or descending to the bottom. Fault phenomenon: The central lift door should stop at both the highest and lowest points. If it doesn't stop or fails to lift, it indicates a fault. Solution: (1) Is the Hall switch functioning properly? (2) Is the polarity of the magnet wrong? (3) Are the screws loose? (4) Has the lever shifted? (5) Are there any jammed dead points?

Fault phenomenon: The four tile-receiving boards continue to work and should stop at the working upper position. If they don't stop at the working position, it indicates a fault. Solution: (1) Check the corresponding limit switch 03. (2) Check the corresponding cam 03 limit switch. (3) Check the corresponding plug-in limit switch using a simulation disc. This simulation disc has an outer diameter 0.5 smaller than usual and a V-shaped groove 0.8 shallower. If this disc can operate normally, switch 03 won't have issues.

Fault phenomenon: The shuffling plate and magnetic ring should rotate. When starting but not rotating or rotating intermittently, it indicates a fault. Solution: (1) Is there an issue with the plug-in core? (2) Is the motor capacitor connected incorrectly or damaged? (3) Is the motor wiring connected incorrectly? (4) Does the shuffling plate touch the iron ring? (5) Is the shuffling plate obstructed by foreign objects? (6) Is the magnetic ring installed accurately? Note: The guide wheels on either side of the magnetic ring should not jam the magnetic ring, they should be slightly loose to ensure sufficient friction force from the drive wheel. (7) Is there a sticking phenomenon between the magnetic ring and the guide rail? (8) Is the tension wheel installed correctly? (9) Is the magnetic ring or turntable deformed? (10) Is the main control board malfunctioning?

Fault phenomenon: Test if the conveyor belt reversal of component 04 is normal. If it doesn't reverse, it indicates a fault. Solution: (1) Check if the plug-in core is qualified. (2) Is the motor capacitor connected incorrectly? (3) Is there any damage to the motor wiring? (4) Is there any jamming? (5) Is the tension shaft functioning normally?

Fault phenomenon: Component 04 conveyor belt runs forward, blocking the S6 photocontrol causing the conveyor belt to stop working. Solution: (1) Check if the S6 is qualified and reliable? (2) Same handling method as above.

Fault phenomenon: Testing component 01 pusher reset. Each start should stop the pusher head at the front end. If it repeatedly moves back and forth or does not move, it indicates a fault. Simultaneously, the four corner sitting-庄 lights flash as an alarm. Solution: (1) Check if the S23 plug-in is reliable. (2) Check if the position of the square shading plate of S23 is accurate. (3) (When rotating 01, the pusher head reaches the front end, there should be more than 1mm gap between the S3 photocontrol and the square shading plate.) (4) Check if the motor and capacitor are damaged.

Fault phenomenon: The chain reset position is accurate. After starting the chain motor, the corresponding chain rod should stop at the photocontrol position for resetting. If it doesn't stop at the S4 photocontrol position, it is considered a fault. Solution: (1) Check if the S4 photocontrol device is reliable? (2) Check if the reflective sleeve of the chain rod is effective? Note: (referring to the height and relative distance of the reflection).

Fault phenomenon: Check if the chain angle positioning is effective. After starting, the chain operates and the corresponding chain rod should stop at the S5 photocontrol position. If it doesn't stop, there is a fault. Solution: (1) Check if the S5 photocontrol device is reliable. (2) Check if the reflective sleeve of the corresponding chain rod is effective.

Fault phenomenon: Check if the S1 photocontrol and dual-photocontrol S2 on the stacking-push mechanism of component 01 are normal. After starting, block the photocontrol with a tile or white paper, the stacking-push mechanism should function normally once. If it doesn't work or continuously works, it is judged as faulty. Solution: (1) Check the S1 photocontrol element. (2) Check the S2 photocontrol element. (3) Check the photocontrol plug-in. (4) Check for any open circuit or disconnection in the circuit. (5) Check if there are any foreign objects or improper assembly in component 01 causing jamming.

Power supply indicator light turns on after powering on, but no power to the motherboard.

If the motherboard has no power even though the power supply indicator light turns on after powering on.

(1) Is the plug-in core loose? (Tighten or replace the plug-in core.)

(2) Is the wire broken? (Use a multimeter ohm range to find the broken wire and repair it.)

(3) Is the component bad? (Replace or change the motherboard, note that the positive and negative poles of 5 volts must not be inserted incorrectly.)

Note: Some customers experience momentary power interruptions when using the mahjong table, often leading to missing tiles. This happens due to a loose power plug being bumped by moving objects. Customers need to pay attention.

Fault phenomenon: Deformation of the 03 guide rail board.

Solution: (1) Modify the guide rail under the 03 tile-receiving board to make its angle 90 degrees so it doesn't affect the tiles entering the curve.

(2) Adjust the deviation between the pusher board and the curve to about 1mm higher.

2. Fault phenomenon: Curve not properly installed, deformation of the 03 track surface.

Solution: (1) Adjust the gap in the curve so three stacks pass smoothly.

(2) Appropriately pad specialized washers to restore the 03 guide rail groove to normal, avoiding affecting the chain movement and the lifting of the 03 tile-receiving board.

3. Fault phenomenon: Incorrect height of the curve.

Solution: (1) Old installation method where the curve plane entrance is lower than the 03 guide rail exit and higher than the 03 guide rail.

(2) Current installation method ensures smooth entry and exit for better turning ease.

4. Fault phenomenon: S4, S5 photocontrol loss of control.

Solution: (1) Check if the S4 photocontrol is effective.

(2) Check if the S5 photocontrol is effective.

(3) Check if the reflective flag has shifted.

5. Fault phenomenon: Motor 02 loss of control, stops unexpectedly.

Solution: (1) Poor contact of the plug-in core, wires not fixed properly.

(2) Chainwheel fixing screws need to be tightened with a hex key.

(3) Capacitor is damaged.

(4) Motor is damaged.

(5) Main control board malfunctions.

Fault phenomenon: Cards get stuck when the four sides raise the tiles.

Solution: (1) Adjust the gap on both sides of the 03 tile-blocking board to 0.8-1.5 mm, the height greater than half the length of the tile, the front gap approximately 1.5 mm.

(2) Push the tiles with the pusher board to within 1-1.5 mm of the edge of the tile-receiving board.

Fault phenomenon: Tiles scatter when pushed and encounter a curve.

Solution: (1) Reshape the pusher board.

(2) Maintain the hinge behind the bristle strip with a torsion spring, allowing the bristles to deform by 2 mm.

(3) Trim the unsuitable curve section by about 2 mm.

(4) Adjust the S4 photocontrol.

(5) Re-position the white reflector tube according to instructions.

(6) Check if the height of the 01 lift platform matches the machine (Type 38: 105-105.5 mm), the first inlet of 03 can be chamfered to 160 degrees.

(7) The bristle strip inlet should allow the tiles to glide over the bristles.

Fault phenomenon: Pusher board hits the chain rod.

Solution: (1) Loose chainwheel screws for 02, chain not reversing.

(2) Damaged motor 02.

(3) Damaged plug-in core for motor 02.

(4) Relay on the main control board.

Using fault display to determine and handle faults

Fault One: One continuous win light is on, the corresponding seat win light flashes.

Possible cause:

1) Fault in the 02 chainwheel component

(1) S4 photocontrol not adjusted well. Reset position is off. Maintenance record: adjust the S4 reset point.

(2) 02 chainwheel loose. Maintenance record: tighten the fixing screws.

(3) 02 motor capacitor damaged. Maintenance record: replace the capacitor.

(4) 02 motor damaged. Maintenance record: replace the motor.

2) Fault in the 03 component

(1) Tiles and foreign objects on the 03 tile-receiving board. Maintenance record: remove them, caused by human error.

(2) Tiles loose or overturned inside the 03 track. Maintenance record: adjust the track and curve, check the height of the 01 lift platform and the 01 tile-pushing mechanism if necessary.

(3) 03 bevel gear misaligned or damaged. Maintenance record: replace or adjust.

(4) Limit switch not adjusted well. Maintenance record: adjust the limit switch.

(5) Top tile board. Maintenance record: adjust the 03 tile-blocking board to maintain a 0.5 mm gap with the tabletop frame.

(6) 03 tile-pushing board not flexible, unable to return due to loose coupling. Maintenance record: recalibrate the 03 tile-pushing board.

(7) 03 motor damaged. Maintenance record: tighten the coupling, replace the motor.

3) Fault in 01

(1) S1 photocontrol too weak, tile-pushing head does not move. Maintenance record: adjust the photocontrol as required.

(2) S1 photocontrol. Maintenance record: clean the photocontrol.

(3) Height of the 01 lift platform incorrect, tiles fall or lie horizontally. Maintenance record: adjust the height.

(4) 01 stacking-pushing component. Maintenance record: replace or repair.

Fault Two: Two continuous win lights are on, the four corner seat win lights flash.

Possible cause:

1) Card jam at the 01 tile-pushing area

(1) S1 photocontrol not adjusted well, photocontrol too strong, tiles do not leave the 04 guide rail. 01 pushes causing a jam. Maintenance record: adjust the S1 photocontrol, refer to the corresponding content in thirty clauses.

(2) Gap between the lift platform and the tile-pushing head is inappropriate. Maintenance record: adjust the gap, increase the washer thickness, tighten the screws.

(3) Bearings of the 01 component fell off. Maintenance record: secure with a locknut.

(4) Fork-type lever not properly installed. Maintenance record: reshape the fork-type lever to meet requirements.

2) Misalignment of the 02 chain rod

(1) S4 photocontrol out of control. Maintenance record: replace or adjust to maintain a reflective distance of 4-6 mm.

(2) Poor contact of the 02 motor. Maintenance record: check the plug-in core.

(3) 02 motor capacitor damaged. Maintenance record: replace the capacitor.

(4) Relay and corresponding components on the main control board damaged. Maintenance record: replace the relay or corresponding components.

Fault Three: Three continuous win lights are on, the four corner seat win lights flash, mahjong tiles fall outside and do not enter the shuffling bucket. Press two lift buttons to open the lift door, pick up the mahjong tiles and place them inside. Then press one lift button twice to close the lift door.

Note: During operation, press one button to close. Pressing two buttons simultaneously may result in the shuffling not rotating due to program-related issues.

1) Weak magnetism of the mahjong tiles, unable to suck up the tiles. Maintenance record: replace the mahjong tiles.

2) Disturbing tile pull spring touches something horizontally. Maintenance record: adjust the length and hardness of the pull spring so it touches the horizontal tiles but cannot rise, placed in the 04 scissor slot and unable to come down.

3) Tiles jammed between the shuffling circle and the way tiles. Maintenance record: adjust the gap between the way tiles and the shuffling plate, import gap 2.5 mm, middle and export gaps slightly larger.

4) Tiles jammed in the scissor slot or 04 guide rail. Maintenance record: adjust the width of the scissor slot entrance to be less than the length of the mahjong tiles by 4 mm, adjust the height of the scissor slot entrance so the gap between it and the magnetic ring is 23-24.5 mm to prevent side tiles from entering the import guide rail.

5) Rubber strip breaks, unable to touch the tiles. Maintenance record: replace the rubber strip.

6) The pressure plate at the 04 guide rail outlet shifts, blocking the tiles. Maintenance record: adjust and secure the pressure plate so there is a 0.5-1 mm gap between it and the mahjong tiles.

Fault 1: No response upon startup, power indicator light does not turn on.

Solution: The machine uses a 3A fuse, check if the fuse is blown and the incoming power line.

Fault 2: Upon startup, the surrounding lift panels automatically drop and cause a shutdown.

Solution: Check if the distance of the Hall magnetic control for the push-up is standard (2-3 mm).

Fault 3: Upon startup, the push head cannot close.

Solution: Check if the position of the double-slot switches K5K6 is standard.

Fault 4: Stacking tiles sometimes become abnormal, stopping intermittently.

Solution: Check if the sensitivity of the K4 photocontrol is normal.

Fault 5: The dice button is effective, but the motor does not rotate.

Solution: Check if the microswitch of the dice tray motor has fallen off.

Fault 6: The chain does not stop upon startup.

Solution: Check if the chain photocontrol K14 and the white plastic sleeve are in place.

Fault 7: The lift panel does not descend fully, preventing the mahjong tiles from being pushed out.

Solution: Check if the hook is broken and if the lift hole lacks oil.

Fault 8: Tiles collide during lifting.

Solution: Adjust the parallelism between the baffle and the panel.

Fault 9: The dice operating panel in the center of the machine does not rise.

Solution: Open the electrical box and check if the flange screws on the lift motor have fallen off.

Fault 10: The back of the mahjong tiles may also be shuffled up.

Solution: Check if the magnets on the shuffling magnetic ring are attached incorrectly.

Fault 11: Sometimes the four lift panels are uneven with the tabletop.

Solution: Check if the plastic heads on both sides of the lift panel are broken and check if the rocker arm below the lift panel is vertical.

Fault 12: The chain motor can only rotate forward, not backward.

Solution: Check if the motor wiring on the mainboard is loose or poorly connected.

Fault 13: The push head keeps advancing upon startup.

Solution: Check if the double-slot switches K5K6 are oil-soaked or improperly positioned.

Fault 14: Cards frequently jam or stand upright at the stacking outlet.

Solution: Appropriately adjust the spacing between the belt outlet and the small supporting tile plate.

Fault 15: During operation, three sides normally lift tiles, occasionally one side has no response.

Solution: Check if the motor plug has poor contact, or replace the start capacitor and retest.

Fault 16: Mahjong tiles get stuck at the corner.

Solution: Adjust the distance between the small baffle on the lift panel and the corner.

Fault 17: The chain suddenly stops during the pulling process.

Solution: Check if there are any white foreign objects between the chain and the sprocket.

Fault 18: The central operating panel moves up and down automatically.

Solution: Open the electrical box and check if the wiring for the magnetic induction K1K2 is normal or replace K1K2 and retest.
