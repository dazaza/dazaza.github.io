Nutritional Value of Soft-Shelled Turtle

The meat of the soft-shelled turtle is neutral in nature and sweet in taste; it enters the liver meridian.

It has the effects of nourishing yin, cooling blood, tonifying and regulating the middle energizer, strengthening the kidneys and bones, dispersing nodules, and eliminating masses. It can be used to prevent and treat physical weakness, enlarged liver and spleen, pulmonary tuberculosis, etc. [Nature and Taste] Cold in nature, salty in taste.

[Nutritional Components] Protein, fat, iron, calcium, animal gelatin, keratin white, and various vitamins, etc.

[Effects] Nourishing yin and clearing heat, tonifying deficiency and nourishing the kidneys, replenishing blood and liver.

Suitable & Taboo: Suitable for those with physical weakness to consume. Those with liver disease should avoid eating it; those with gastrointestinal inflammation, gastric ulcers, cholecystitis, and other digestive system diseases should not eat it; those with insomnia, pregnant women, and postpartum diarrhea should not eat it. The soft-shelled turtle contains high protein and fat, especially its edge meat skirt part which also contains animal gelatin, making it difficult to digest and absorb. It is not advisable to eat too much at one time.

The belly plate of the soft-shelled turtle is called "Gui Ban," a valuable Chinese medicine, which has the effect of nourishing yin and reducing fire. It is used to treat dizziness, vertigo, false fever, night sweats, etc. It also has good therapeutic effects on persistent headaches left over from head injuries (such as neonatal cephalohematoma). Gui Ban collagen is a large molecular collagen protein, containing various amino acids needed by the skin, with the effects of beautifying the face, moisturizing the skin, and enhancing beauty and fitness.

The neck of the soft-shelled turtle can be used to treat rectal prolapse. Take the whole neck, dry it over a low fire, crush it, mix it evenly with hot vinegar until it is wet and seeps with vinegar, wrap it tightly with gauze, sit on it for fumigation, generally 3-5 times will cure it. Although the soft-shelled turtle has excellent therapeutic effects on postpartum weakness and fatigue reduction, pregnant women and those with postpartum constipation should not eat it or should eat it in moderation.

The entire body of the soft-shelled turtle is a treasure, with high edible and medicinal value. The meat of the soft-shelled turtle is sweet and neutral, also a good medicine, mainly treating dampness and arthralgia due to heat, able to tonify the center and benefit qi, tonify deficiency and nourish yin. The soft-shelled turtle is rich in animal gelatin, keratin, copper, vitamin D, and other nutrients, which can enhance the body's disease resistance and regulate the endocrine function of the human body. It is also an excellent tonic for improving the quality of breast milk, enhancing the immunity and intelligence of infants.

The shell, blood, head, fat, and eggs of the soft-shelled turtle can all be used as medicine. The soft-shelled turtle shell nourishes yin and replenishes blood, reduces fever, and clears stasis. It is very effective in treating carbuncles, hemorrhoids, and yin toxins, and can treat "difficult childbirth, postpartum collapse." It has therapeutic effects on liver cirrhosis and splenomegaly, also regulates immune function, increases the transformation rate of lymphocytes, and promotes bone marrow hematopoietic function. The blood of the soft-shelled turtle is salty and non-toxic, which can be used as a blood supplement. Mixing the blood of the soft-shelled turtle with honey and letting diabetic patients drink it can lower blood sugar levels. The eggs of the soft-shelled turtle can treat long-term malaria and chronic dysentery.
