A Brief Discussion on the Impact of Accounting Computerization on Modern Enterprise Management

by pascxvty on 2012-03-03 10:50:42

It has changed the way that accounting information system processes data. Although the application of computers has not changed the functions of accounting work, it has changed the way that manual accounting information system processes data. Under manual conditions, accounting work is completed by many people together. After using computers, inputting a single original voucher enables the computer to automatically post accounts, summarize, transfer, and compile tables according to the pre-set functions. Under manual conditions, some reports can only be generated at the end of the month or at the beginning of the next month, while after applying computers, reports can be outputted at any time.

It has changed the quality of accounting information. The quality characteristics of accounting information mainly include relevance, reliability, comparability, materiality, timeliness, etc. After using computers, the quality of accounting information in these aspects has been improved, and it will promote the development of accounting information towards multiple measurement units, which can be quickly retrieved when users need it.

It has changed the method of developing accounting information systems. The selection of bookkeeping methods, the establishment of accounting subjects, the design of voucher and ledger formats, and the cycle choice of financial statements should be comprehensively considered from both manual and computerized ways.

It has changed the form of storing accounting archives. After using computers, accounting archives will mainly be stored on optical discs with paper media as auxiliary storage. Both have different requirements for preservation technical conditions such as environment, temperature, humidity, and anti-electromagnetic interference, and there are also significant changes in the form of querying historical data.

It has changed the internal control methods and technologies of accounting. Under computerized conditions, appropriate control measures are needed for the use permissions of computers, the verification of inputted original data, the correctness of data processing, and the preservation of data. Moreover, it affects the control methods and technologies that do not involve computer operations.

It has changed the functions and division of labor among financial personnel. The application of computers not only leads to changes in the functions and division of labor among financial personnel but also causes changes in the organizational structure and management methods of the finance department. The change in functions is mainly reflected in the positions established for various accounting purposes, such as cost accountants, salary accountants, and material accountants, which may be replaced by computers, while new positions such as operators and maintainers will emerge; personnel in financial analysis and other positions will be further strengthened, and the finance department will change its original organizational structure and adopt new management systems and methods.

The role of computerized accounting in accounting data. In accounting work, various primary accounting materials related to business operations obtained from different channels are called accounting data. Accounting data is usually reflected in various internal financial statements and external accounting statements.

Accounting information refers to the information reflecting capital investment, asset form conversion, equity internal conversion, and capital withdrawal in accounting work. Generally speaking, accounting information can be regarded as the result of processing accounting data.

In economic society and economic activities, timely, accurate, and economical accounting information is indispensable. Accounting information has the following characteristics: large data volume, complex data structure, strict requirements for data processing methods, and high requirements for data authenticity and accuracy.

Classification of accounting information: According to the role of accounting information, it can be divided into financial information (which reflects everything that has happened in the past period of operation and management, like balance sheets, income statements, and ledgers), directional information (which refers to specific information required for management, such as analysis reports comparing actual operating conditions with budgets or comparing current operating conditions with historical records), and decision-making information (which is the information needed for future planning such as annual plans, individual planning, and interim decisions in accounting budgets).

The relationship between accounting information and accounting data. Accounting information and accounting data are two closely related yet fundamentally different concepts. Accounting information is produced by processing accounting data, and accounting data can only meet management needs after being processed according to certain requirements or needs. Therefore, there is no clear boundary between accounting data and accounting information.

The role of computerized accounting in accounting work: First, in providing precise, timely, and large amounts of information, computers have great advantages, including fast operating speed, high processing precision, large storage capacity, flexible processing methods, continuous operation capability, and the ability to achieve real-time processing through networking of multiple computers. Therefore, only under the condition of computerized accounting can the accuracy, data volume, and timeliness of accounting information provided by accounting work be fundamentally improved. Second, providing more detailed accounting information is unrealistic under manual conditions but becomes possible after computerized accounting. Finally, the realization of computerized accounting provides vast opportunities for accountants to participate in decision-making. Computerized accounting frees accountants from tedious manual operations and daily routine tasks. Accountants, utilizing their comprehensive and timely understanding of enterprises, actively participate in the cost, investment, sales, and other decision-making work of enterprises, serving as advisors to enterprise leaders; accountants utilize their ability to analyze the entire production and operation process...
