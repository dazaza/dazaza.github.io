Skill in Grasping Short-term Selling Points

by gpgp6486 on 2009-11-20 22:48:35

Skills for Grasping Short-term Selling Points:

I. Determine selling points based on the position of candlestick charts and moving average lines:

1. The moving average line shows a significant upward trend.

2. After a period of increase, the moving average line begins to level off.

3. When the stock price falls from above and breaks through the leveled-off moving average line, it is a selling signal.

4. To verify the reliability of the signal displayed by one moving average line, you can select multiple moving average lines for cross-referencing. Meanwhile, under normal circumstances, trading volume also decreases correspondingly, making the selling signal more explicit.

II. Determine selling points based on the position of candlestick charts and moving average lines:

This signal has the following characteristics:

1. First, confirm that the moving average line is in a downward process.

2. Then, confirm that the stock price breaks through the moving average line from below.

3. After the stock price breaks through the moving average line, it retreats immediately. When it falls back below the moving average line, it is a selling signal.

4. This selling signal should be examined during the rebound after the stock price falls, i.e., when the stock price rebounds to about 1/3 of the previous fall's amplitude and then declines again, breaking through the moving average line should prompt a sell.

5. If this selling signal appears multiple times during the market downturn, the earlier you sell, the better, or consider short-term hedging.

III. Determine selling points based on the position of candlestick charts and moving average lines:

This signal has the following characteristics:

1. The moving average line is gradually declining.

2. After fluctuating under the moving average line for a while, the stock price starts to rise relatively clearly towards the moving average line, indicating market conditions.

3. When approaching the moving average line, the stock price immediately retreats and slides down, signaling the appearance of a selling signal.

4. If this signal appears after the stock price has fallen significantly, it should be used as a reference and not as a reason to sell hastily. If this signal appears when the stock price hasn't fallen too much, it can be considered as an indication that the time to sell has arrived. This situation should be handled differently.

IV. Determine selling points based on the position of candlestick charts and moving average lines:

This signal has the following characteristics:

1. Confirm that the moving average line is in an upward process.

2. The stock price rises sharply above the moving average line, and the deviation from the moving average line increases increasingly.

3. Calculate the lower deviation rate. If the obtained lower deviation rate has reached 30%-50%, and at the same time, the stock price begins to reverse rapidly and fall with the distance from the moving line shrinking, this is a selling signal.
