Guangxi Youai Hospital: Acupuncture can remove "needle eyes"

by lfiml496096 on 2011-10-10 14:17:17

Exclusive Interview with Expert: Director of the Acupuncture Department at the First Affiliated Hospital of Guangzhou University of Traditional Chinese Medicine, Dr. Zhuang Lixing

Ms. Xu, who works in public relations, has a pair of mesmerizing eyes that others envy, but unfortunately, she frequently develops styes on her eyelids. The meibomian cysts on both of her eyelids appear alternately, causing not only itching and swelling pain but also affecting her appearance significantly. She bought herself some anti-inflammatory eye drops and tetracycline ointment, but after using them for a month or two, there was no improvement. Ms. Xu is quite troubled: "I usually pay close attention to my eye hygiene, so why do I keep getting these meibomian cysts?" However, she is also afraid of eye surgery and doesn't know what to do.

According to Dr. Zhuang Lixing, Director of the Acupuncture Department at the First Affiliated Hospital of Guangzhou University of Traditional Chinese Medicine, a stye generally refers to a small abscess on the eyelid that looks like a grain of wheat, easily leading to pus discharge. It is also known as a "needle eye" or "eye carbuncle."

Based on traditional Chinese medicine theory, diseases presented by the eyes can reflect the health status of internal organs. The spleen and stomach are responsible for the upper and lower eyelids, so people with heat accumulation in the spleen and stomach are more prone to developing meibomian cysts. Modern office workers often stay up late, attend social engagements, and experience high stress levels, while also enjoying spicy foods, which can easily cause "fire accumulation" in the spleen and stomach. Once the temperature changes and external wind-heat invades, the accumulated heat combines with the external wind, obstructing the flow of qi and blood, resulting in the formation of redness, swelling, and hard lumps on the eyelids. Initially, this causes pain and tenderness, followed by gradual enlargement of the swelling. A few days later, a yellowish-white pus point may appear on the top of the lump, and when it ruptures, the pus will flow out.

Local "fire needle" therapy can often cure it in one session.

"When a stye first appears, with red hard lumps but no pus yet, acupuncture treatment is most effective. At that time, selecting acupoints from the hands, feet, or ears for needling to release fire is effective. If the stye has already formed pus, then local 'fire needle' therapy is needed to release the fire," Dr. Zhuang Lixing explained. Using the "fire needle" therapy for treating styes shows significant results. During treatment, the doctor heats a small needle tip over an alcohol lamp until it turns red, then punctures directly into the center of the slightly elevated part of the stye or the center of the pus point, inserting and withdrawing quickly. Generally, after needling, the pus and blood will flow out, and the patient will immediately feel a reduction in pain. Then, gently squeezing the stye and wiping away the pus and blood with a dry sterile cotton ball, apply tetracycline eye ointment externally. Most people can be cured with just one session. Patients with early-stage ocular inflammation may require 3-5 sessions, with a cure rate of over 80%.

The reporter found that currently, some community health service centers and private medical and health institutions in Guangzhou offer acupuncture services for styes. Experts remind that although local fire needle treatment for styes is fast-acting, due to the rich network of blood vessels around the eyeball, accidentally piercing a vessel could cause bleeding around the eyes. Therefore, it is best to seek treatment at qualified large hospitals. (Editor-in-Charge: Yang Lil)
