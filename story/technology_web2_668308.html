Are there any regulations on the hygiene standards of restaurants?

by uefbp54187 on 2012-02-17 16:39:06

Are there regulations for the hygiene standards of restaurants? For the operation and management of a restaurant, talking about service or quality without considering other requirements is meaningless. Hygiene work is related to the reputation and operation of the enterprise, affects consumers' cognition and dependence on products, and more importantly, it concerns the physical health and even life safety of the general public. Developing good hygiene awareness and habits is not only an excellent performance of each service personnel's work, but also an expression of good cultivation and habits of a person. It is also the foundation for a single store to establish its brand image. Therefore, the management personnel of a single store must strictly control the quality and take the hygiene management as one of the core of the daily management of the single store.

Article 1: Personal Hygiene

The personal hygiene of employees is an important part of the restaurant's hygiene management. Employees face customers directly and contact food and tableware directly. If they have bad hygiene habits or operate improperly, it will affect the hygiene of food. Therefore, it is necessary to develop good hygiene habits and implement them in work:

(1) All staff members of the restaurant must hold a health certificate to work. If there is no health certificate, the work should be stopped.

(2) Have basic health and hygiene knowledge, keep healthy, be full of spirit, and get enough sleep.

(3) Pay attention to personal cleanliness and hygiene, and do "four frequently": frequently cut nails, frequently take a bath and get a haircut, frequently wash clothes and quilts, frequently change work clothes, eliminate body odor and keep clean and tidy.

(4) Do preparatory work before going on duty, change into clean and tidy work clothes, wear caps for bar and kitchen staff, and store personal items in designated places.

(5) Avoid bad habits at work, avoid touching hair or face with hands, do not cough or sneeze facing food or customers, and do not spit anywhere.

(6) Do not eat food with strong smell or drink alcoholic beverages before going to work, keep breath fresh.

(7) Hand cleaning is particularly important. After the following actions, you should wash your hands immediately: ① touch hair or skin; ② wipe nose or cover mouth when coughing; ③ use handkerchiefs or tissues; ④ pick up used tableware; ⑤ go to the toilet or come out of the restroom; ⑥ carry boxes or packaging bags and other dirty objects; ⑦ contact raw materials; ⑧ sweep the floor or mop the floor.

(8) Take sanitary methods to take tableware and food, do not touch the entrance position of tableware guests with hands, do not grab food with hands directly.

Article 2: Storefront Hygiene

(1) The storefront hygiene is the responsibility of security guards when they are on duty. If there is no security post, the store manager shall designate personnel to be responsible.

(2) The standard for sign hygiene is that the sign is complete, clean, and eye-catching. If the standard is not met, it should be reported to the store manager for handling until the standard is met.

(3) The standard for the hygiene of the restaurant door is no dust, no obvious stains, and no random postings.

(4) The standard for carpet hygiene is no paper scraps, toothpicks, leaves, mud, cigarette butts and other debris, no damage, and no discoloration. If there is damage and discoloration, replacement should be recommended.

(5) The standard for the hygiene of the steps at the restaurant entrance is clean, free of debris, mud, and dirt.

(6) The standard for the hygiene of the glass at the entrance is clean, bright, no marks, stains, and no random postings.

(7) The standard for the water glass slot at the entrance is clear water quality, no fallen leaves or debris. The standard for the flowing glass is no water rust, iron rust, clean and bright.

(8) The standard for the items placed at the restaurant entrance: placed items (including flower pots, advertising boards), advertising boards need to be wiped frequently, with the standard of being clean, bright, and dust-free. The standard for flowers and potted landscapes is no dust, no paper scraps, cigarette butts, toothpicks, and other debris.

(9) The standard for the lighting at the restaurant entrance is intact, eye-catching, no discoloration, no paint peeling, and no dust. Unlit bulbs should be replaced in time.

(10) The standard for the hygiene of the wall at the main entrance is clean, no scratches, no random postings, and no dust.

(11) The standard for the hygiene of the floor at the restaurant entrance is clean, no obvious paper scraps, leaves, cigarette butts, and other debris. Placed items must be neatly arranged.

Article 3: Table Hygiene

(1) The table hygiene is mainly the responsibility of the service personnel on duty in the area.

(2) The standard for table hygiene is clean, no paint peeling, no water marks, and no oil stains. When wiping the table, use a wet cloth first and then a dry cloth until it is clean.

(3) The standard for the table glass is no oil stains, marks, no damage, and cracks. When wiping the glass, use glass water first and then newspaper until it is clean.

(4) The standard for the tablecloth is clean, tidy, no discoloration, no damage, and no stains.

(5) The standard for the items placed on the table (including ashtrays, sugar racks, tissue holders, journals, table cards, toothpick jars) is complete replenishment, neat arrangement, no damage, clean, no dust, no oil stains, and no dirt.

(6) The legs of the table should be wiped frequently, with the standard of no footprints, no paint peeling, no cigarette ash, no toothpicks, no mud, and no dust.

(7) Special attention should be paid to the hygiene and cleaning of the four edges of the table, with the standard of no dirt, oil stains, etc.

Article 4: Sofa and Chair Hygiene

(1) The sofa and chair hygiene is the responsibility of the service personnel on duty.

(2) The standard for sofa and chairs is no black spots, marks, no damage, and no foreign objects.

(3) The bottom of the sofa cushion and the back of the support should be free of foreign objects, dust, stains, and mud. This should be checked before starting work every day.

(4) If there is any damage or dirt on the surface of the sofa, it should be proposed for cleaning or replacement in time.

(5) Special attention should be paid to the hygiene under the sofa, the legs of the sofa, and the side of the sofa.

(6) The legs of the chair, etc., should meet the hygiene standard of no paint peeling, no dust, and no dirt.

(7) The hygiene of the pillows with pillows is the same as that of the sofas.

Article 5: Floor Hygiene in the Business Hall

(1) The floor hygiene in the business hall is the responsibility of the service personnel on duty.

(2) All staff in the restaurant during business hours must develop the habit of cleaning up at hand. Items that can be picked up by hand should be put in the trash can in time, and items such as food residues that cannot be picked up should be swept or mopped until they are clean.

(3) The standard for floor hygiene is no foreign objects, no paper scraps, no mud, and no food residues.

(4) The standard between tiles and the interface between the floor and the wall is no foreign objects and no dirt.

(5) If there is a marble restaurant, it must be kept clean and waxed frequently.

(6) The tiles in the business hall should be maintained frequently and painted.

(7) The standard for the hygiene of the stairs is no foreign objects, no dust, no dirt, and no standing water.

(8) Note that the mop used for mopping the floor must be clean, free of oil stains, and free of strange smells to prevent slipping when mopping.

Article 6: Cash Register Counter Hygiene

(1) The cash register counter hygiene is the responsibility of the cashier.

(2) Keep the cash register supplies such as telephone, banknote detector, calculator, computer, cash register clip, etc., free of stains, oil stains, no damage, and can be used normally as the standard.

(3) The drawers of the cash register should be free of pests, neatly arranged, and free of strange smells as the standard.

(4) The background display items of the cash register must be free of dust, no damage, no stains, no oil stains, and reasonably arranged as the standard.

(5) Keep the entire surface of the cash register clean and free of dust, no damage, and no scratches as the standard.

(6) The amplifier, VCD player, speaker, disc, telephone of the cash register should be neatly arranged, can be used normally, and free of dust and grease as the standard.

Article 7: Work Cabinet and Bookshelf, Newspaper Clip Hygiene

(1) The hygiene of the work cabinet and bookshelf, newspaper clip is the responsibility of the service personnel on duty in the area.

(2) The surface of the work cabinet and bookshelf, newspaper clip should be free of scratches, oil stains, dust, and foreign objects as the hygiene standard.

(3) The bookshelf and newspaper clip should be arranged neatly, free of damage, clean and tidy, and not messy as the standard. The magazines in the bookshelf should be updated regularly.

(4) Special attention should be paid to the fact that the newspaper clips and newspapers must be clipped well to facilitate customers and avoid making people feel messy.

(5) The drawers of the work cabinet should be free of pests, kept clean, and the items inside should be neatly arranged as the standard.

(6) The backup items and tools (water cups, water jugs, tableware, etc.) placed on the work cabinet should be neatly arranged, free of water marks, and meet the hygiene standards of the tools.

Article 8: Bar Counter Hygiene

(1) The bar counter hygiene is the responsibility of the bar staff on duty.

(2) The front of the bar counter should be free of dust, lights undamaged, free of stains, oil stains, no postings, and kept clean as the standard.

(3) The chairs in front of the bar refer to the hygiene standard of the sofa and chairs.

(4) The two-hole two-stove group and exhaust group should be clean, free of strange smells, no residual matter, transparent and free of stains, no gas leakage, etc., and can be used normally as the standard.

(5) The tea maker, small cute, Italian pot should be free of dirt, water marks, rust, residual matter, and clean as the standard.

(6) The bar glasses should be clean and bright, free of tea scale, water marks, fingerprints, and damage as the standard.

(7) Bar equipment: ice machine, refrigerator, grinder, ice crusher, pancake machine, juicer, etc., should be able to be used normally, free of damage, surface clean, no other colors, neatly arranged and convenient to use as the standard.

(8) Bar tools: snow level pot, stirring stick, etc., should be clean, free of foreign objects, dirt, and grease as the standard.

(9) The back of the bar should be free of damage and dust as the standard.

(10) The items placed on the back of the bar (tea cans, alcohol, various glasses, certificates, decorations) should be free of dust, clean, neatly arranged, and convenient to use as the standard.

(11) The layered glass and mirrors on the back of the bar should be free of marks, dust, and clean and bright as the standard.

(12) The items placed on top of the bar should be neatly arranged, clean, free of foreign objects, water marks, and grease as the standard.

(13) The cabinets on the back of the bar should be free of pests, neatly arranged, classified storage, and clean as the standard.

(14) The hygiene and placement of bar tools should meet the standards of the local health department.

(15) All power wires of the bar should be free of stains, oil stains, water marks, and neatly arranged as the standard.

Article 9: Food Serving Area Hygiene Standard

(1) The hygiene of the food serving area is the responsibility of the food serving personnel.

(2) The things on the countertop of the food serving area should be neatly arranged, easy to use, free of water marks, oil stains, foreign objects, and food residues as the standard.

(3) The cabinets of the food serving area should be cleaned frequently, free of pests, neatly arranged, and free of strange smells as the standard.

(4) The bowls, chopsticks, knives, and forks of the food serving area should be free of water marks, dirt, food residues, foreign objects, and dirt as the hygiene standard.

(5) The trays, iron plate covers, food serving papers, napkins, etc., of the food serving area should be neatly arranged, free of strange smells, marks, oil stains, dirt, and water marks as the standard.

(6) The posted items and hanging items of the food serving area should be free of dust, oil stains, and clean and neat as the standard.

(7) The condiments placed in the food serving area should be neatly arranged, free of oil stains, dust, and not expired as the standard.

(8) The placement of utensils in the food serving area should meet the health bureau standards.

(9) The ground of the food serving area should be free of oil stains, water marks, paper scraps, etc., as the standard.

Article 10: Warehouse Hygiene

(1) The warehouse hygiene is the responsibility of the warehouse manager.

(2) The items in the warehouse should be neatly arranged, classified and labeled with item names for easy retrieval.

(3) The warehouse should be free of pests, spider webs, dust, clean, and ventilated as the standard.

(4) The warehouse floor should be free of dust, dry, free of paper scraps, dirt, and pest droppings as the standard.

(5) The items placed in the warehouse should be free of expiration, dust, and damage as the standard.

Article 11: Restroom Hygiene

(1) The restroom hygiene is the responsibility of the on-duty personnel.

(2) The restroom floor should be clean, refreshing, free of standing water, mud, cigarette butts, paper scraps, and dirt as the standard.
