Manchester United crushes Inter Milan and Juventus can't beat AC Milan, Sir Alex Ferguson aims to fulfill Busby's lifelong wish

According to a fan survey on Manchester United's official website regarding the Champions League draw, AC Milan was the top choice among Red Devils fans for a potential opponent. This is likely due to the fact that in previous Champions League knockout stages, Manchester United faced AC Milan four times and was eliminated every single time. After the draw results were announced, the Manchester United camp expressed satisfaction with their draw.

Let us first review Manchester United's four experiences of being eliminated by Milan in the Champions League. In the 1957-58 season, Sir Matt Busby and his young team made all of Europe tremble, but the Munich air disaster claimed the lives of eight Manchester United players, including Edwards, who was widely regarded as the most talented player within the squad. Under these circumstances, Manchester United faced AC Milan in the semifinals, with the schedule set as home first, then away. Despite the Red Devils defeating the opponent 2-1 at home, AC Milan crushed Manchester United 4-0 at San Siro in front of 80,000 cheering fans, eliminating "Busby's Babes," who had lost eight players, from reaching the final with an aggregate score of 5-2. Unfortunately, AC Milan also lost 2-3 to Real Madrid in extra time in the final.

After ten years of hard work since the 1967-68 season, the Red Devils finally won their first "Big Ears" trophy. In the semifinal, they faced Real Madrid, winning 1-0 at home in the first leg; in the second leg, Manchester United conceded three goals in the first 12 minutes at Bernabeu, but the resilient Busby's Babes equalized 3-3 just before the final whistle, eliminating the opponent with an aggregate score of 4-3. In the final, after 120 minutes of extra time, Manchester United defeated Benfica 4-1 thanks to two goals from Bobby Charlton, one from George Best, and another from Kidd, claiming their first ever Champions League title.

In the 1968-69 season, defending champions Manchester United and AC Milan met again in the Champions League semifinal. This time, Manchester United’s schedule was away first, then home, but they lost 0-2 at San Siro. Although they returned home and defeated their opponents 1-0, they were still eliminated with an aggregate score of 1-2. Busby and his young team fell once again under the guns of the Rossoneri. At the end of this season, Sir Matt Busby chose to retire, and AC Milan remained the unbreakable obstacle throughout his career.

In the 2004-05 season, during the Champions League Round of 16, Manchester United, led by Sir Alex Ferguson, played home first and then away, but AC Milan, with two goals from Crespo, defeated Manchester United 1-0 both home and away. However, AC Milan, who advanced all the way to the final, experienced a once-in-a-lifetime nightmare in Istanbul. They let Liverpool stage an incredible comeback despite leading 3-0, losing the almost secured championship.

In the 2006-07 season, during the Champions League semifinal, Manchester United, led by Sir Alex Ferguson, met AC Milan again. Despite Wayne Rooney scoring twice and Cristiano Ronaldo heading in a goal to help the Red Devils win the first leg 3-2, in the return leg at San Siro, AC Milan scored through Kaka, Seedorf, and Gilardino, advancing to the final with an aggregate score of 5-3. In the final, AC Milan defeated Liverpool 2-1 to claim the championship smoothly.

It is worth noting that in the second leg where Manchester United suffered a 0-3 defeat against AC Milan, the Red Devils' starting left-back Evra was suspended, key center-back Ferdinand and then-starting right-back Gary Neville were injured, leaving United to rely on Vidic, who had just recovered from injury, along with Heinze, Brown, and O'Shea, who were then substitutes. As a result, they were overwhelmed by the well-rested Rossoneri. However, in that season, AC Milan lagged 36 points behind the Serie A champions Inter Milan, finishing only in fourth place. That season, Manchester United was heavily constrained by domestic league pressure; whereas AC Milan, having abandoned the league, clearly had the advantage of rest in the Champions League.

From its establishment in 1878 until now, Manchester United has a history spanning 131 years, during which it has seen 19 head coaches. Sir Matt Busby, who coached for 24 years, and Sir Alex Ferguson, who also coached for 24 years, are recognized as the two legendary managers of the Red Devils. However, both have records of being eliminated twice by AC Milan in the Champions League. It can be said that never having triumphed over the Rossoneri in Champions League knockouts remains the greatest regret of these two legendary managers.

Nevertheless, against other Italian giants, Sir Alex Ferguson and his Manchester United have many impressive records. Against the current Serie A leader Inter Milan, the manager and his team have had very good results in their encounters over the past decade. In the 1998-99 season, Manchester United eliminated Inter Milan in the quarterfinals of the Champions League with a 2-0 home win and a 1-1 away draw, advancing with an aggregate score of 3-1; last season, Manchester United again eliminated Inter Milan in the Round of 16 of the Champions League with a 0-0 away draw and a 2-0 home win, advancing with an aggregate score of 2-0.

Against Juventus, Sir Alex also holds a significant advantage. In the 1998-99 season Champions League semifinal, Manchester United eliminated Juventus with an aggregate score of 4-3, playing home first and then away; in the 2002-03 season, Manchester United in the Champions League group stage defeated Juventus 2-1 at home and 3-0 away. As for another Serie A giant, Roma, Sir Alex and his Manchester United have a particularly good understanding. In the 2006-07 season, in the Champions League quarterfinals, Manchester United drew 1-2 away and crushed Roma 7-1 at home, advancing with an aggregate score of 8-3; in the 2007-08 season, Manchester United achieved 3 wins and 1 draw in 4 matches against Roma in the Champions League group stage and knockout rounds.

Sir Alex Ferguson leads Manchester United with great confidence against Inter Milan, Juventus, and Roma, but he and the legendary Matt Busby could never find a way to deal with AC Milan during their managerial careers, which is undoubtedly a regret. If Manchester United can eliminate AC Milan this time in the Champions League, Sir Alex Ferguson would be fulfilling a long-held wish before retiring.
