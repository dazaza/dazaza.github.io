Merchant - the hidden profession in WOW!

by lk0gamei on 2009-07-18 23:31:25

I am Liu Yunruo Huo from the Free Wind of Zone Three. In one of my previous articles about the battlegrounds for level 49 priests, I mentioned that I am a merchant. Someone asked me what a merchant is. In fact, this profession outside of the nine major ones is not mysterious. Perhaps out of consideration for trade secrets, or because there are few merchants, there aren't many articles written about merchants. I have decided to reveal the secrets of being a merchant not to encourage everyone to become one, but simply to let more people understand and comprehend merchants. Let me remind some people at the outset: "We have no right to discriminate against anyone, but we do have the right to discriminate against those who discriminate against others, because they are not really human."

…………………………………The Concept of a Merchant………………………………………

Playing any online game requires the support of virtual currency, and in World of Warcraft (WOW), merchants make money. Moreover, their operational model is very suitable for casual players who aren't often online. Simply put, merchants are resellers. Don't think all merchants are unscrupulous; truly unreasonable dishonesty belongs to scammers, not merchants. Merchants may be cunning, but there is a way to their cunning. Such cunning is predetermined by fate, and there's nothing we can do about it since we are merchants. Generally speaking, WOW merchants buy low and sell high, but low-priced goods aren't always available, so sometimes they need to go to the enemy faction to procure them. I will explain how to do that later. So, what impact do merchants have on WOW?

Positive: Merchants actually engage in reasonable labor. We spend game cards, time, and effort transporting goods that BL doesn't have from LM, and vice versa. It's more like being in the transportation business. Take raw materials, for example. LM has prison-originated raw materials while BL does not. Naturally, raw materials are scarce in BL, making prices higher. When we transport raw materials from LM to sell in BL, we not only meet BL's demand but also help sell LM's raw materials, thereby balancing prices. I dare say that at least one-third of the raw materials in our zone's BL are supplied by me. I believe you've already studied market economics in high school politics, so I won't elaborate on why prices balance. If there are buyers and sellers, why wouldn't we do it?

Negative: There shouldn't be too many merchants in one zone because merchants don't produce goods. They are destined not to become a common way for everyone to make money, and they require a certain amount of business acumen. If everyone in the zone becomes a merchant, where would the goods come from? However, merchants are still necessary. You should consider the number of merchants in your zone before deciding whether to become one. Additionally, merchants might hoard goods during a certain period, leading to shortages of certain items and skyrocketing prices. But this is limited to what large merchants can afford, and such situations rarely occur under normal circumstances.

………………………………………My Situation………………………………………………

Let the facts speak for themselves. I am a level 49 priest in BL, with a level 49 Druid and a level 49 Shaman. I also have a level 49 Hunter in LM and a level 20+ SS. All characters use 18-slot bags, with the SS using 28-slot glyph bags. The bank is fully expanded with 16 bags, and both the warehouse and my characters carry 16 bags each. My equipment is mid-to-high level, and all characters are prepared with complete sets of level 59 equipment, including Crusader and Life Tap gear. Four mining skills are maxed out through mining, four engineering skills are fully leveled, one tailor, one alchemist, and one enchanter are all maxed out as well, proficient in Crusader and Life Tap enchantments. First Aid is fully leveled, cooking is over 300, and fishing is over 225. Total funds exceed 5000G, with a large amount of unsold goods still listed on the auction house. Although I can't compare with experts who have tens of thousands of G, I am never short of money to spend. I have everything I should have, and even things I shouldn't have are prepared. Anyway, I don't know where to spend my low-level money, and I have never sold a single game card. I'm not saying these things to boast but rather to give everyone a clear picture of the financial base of this merchant, so you can see what being a merchant can achieve. All of this was earned from being a merchant.

………………………………………Specific Operations……………………………………

In fact, the specific operations are quite simple, and anyone can understand them at a glance.

Preparation: One BL character and one LM character. Create an Orc and any other character on the BL side, and create a Dwarf and any other character (preferably an Elf) on the LM side. In addition, you'll need 1000G in capital, though 500G can work too, albeit with more difficulty.
