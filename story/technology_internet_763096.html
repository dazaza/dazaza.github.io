Construction begins on the world's tallest building

by anonymous on 2013-07-29 14:57:59

Rendering of the "Sky City".

After arriving by plane, President Zhang Yue of Broad Group ran to the groundbreaking ceremony site.

At 2:10 PM on July 20th, in Huilong Village, Wangcheng District, Changsha, a US Bell Helicopter B-7748 had just landed in a field when its owner eagerly disembarked and jogged towards his dream of the "Sky City."

This man is Zhang Yue, the conceptualizer of the world's tallest building, "Sky City," which is 10 meters taller than Dubai Tower, and also the president of Broad Technology Group.

In the evening of July 18th, CSCEC Fifth Engineering Bureau signed the project construction general contracting contract for "Sky City" with Broad Group. On the afternoon of July 20th, the "Sky City Foundation Groundbreaking Ceremony" was held in Wangcheng. Broad Group believes that the significance of this building goes beyond being the world's tallest building; it will serve as an important exploration of the "energy-saving and land-conserving urban model" and "healthy living style."

President Zhang Yue of Broad Group ran from the helicopter.

The estimated $90 billion "world's tallest building" underwent signing and groundbreaking in a low-key and simple ceremony. Industry insiders stated that this has always been Broad Group's style.

If it weren't for the many vehicles, villagers, and staff gathered on a piece of land on the east side of Jinxing North Road in Wangcheng District, perhaps no one would have known that the foundation of the world's tallest building was laid here.

Reporters at the scene observed that a temporary road made of crushed stones was only opened on July 19th to reach the site. The music of Joe Hisaishi's "Castle in the Sky" floated in the air. On the opening ceremony stage, a rudimentary backdrop board displayed detailed planning diagrams of Sky City (see illustration).

The groundbreaking ceremony for "Sky City" was originally scheduled to start at 2 PM. When 2 PM arrived, there was still no sign of Zhang Yue. About ten minutes later, Zhang Yue appeared wearing sunglasses after descending from the helicopter and jogging about 200 meters to the site.

Planned to be built underground in six months and above ground in four months.

From the beginning, there were various rumors about whether "Sky City" would be completed in three months or seven months. On July 20th, Zhang Yue provided the answer: six months underground and four months above ground. The project plans to complete the foundation work by January next year and top out by April.

24 hours: 7 floors; 6 days: 12 floors; 15 days: 30 floors... This time, it's 4 months: 202 floors, 838 meters!

Due to the use of a "building blocks" style of rapid construction, the "Broad speed" has drawn global attention.

"Progress is our biggest challenge," Zhang Yue admitted. "Our foundation construction takes 6 months, and our above-ground construction takes 4 months. By April next year, the building will be topped out, and by mid-May to June, people will move in. By July, it will already be bustling with activity, bright with lights, filled with birdsong and flowers." He emphasized that although safety, quality, and environmental protection are all very important for Sky City, "regardless of quality, cost, environment, or overall urban planning, we must complete it on schedule."

"Sky City changes the mode of urban construction. In the past, urban construction was like 'spreading pancakes,' but now it becomes 'making steamed buns'—building upwards." As the general contractor for the project, CSCEC Fifth Engineering Bureau seems quite confident. CSCEC Fifth Engineering Bureau Director Lu Guiqing told reporters, "Although the project poses some difficulties, we can still strive to complete it well."

Skepticism One: Is the location too remote?

"Some say this place is too far? For us, it's a treasure." Zhang Yue responded to the skepticism about the location during the groundbreaking ceremony. "Although this place is about fifteen or sixteen kilometers away from downtown Changsha, it will become the most desirable place for humanity. Remember, this place will soon become the most desired place for humanity."

Skepticism Two: Can existing technology be applied to a height of 838 meters?

One of the most questioned issues regarding the Sky City project is how 100-meter technology can build a structure reaching 838 meters.

On October 21, 2012, the Hunan Provincial Housing and Urban-Rural Development Department organized an expert review meeting to evaluate the key issues of Broad Group's steel structure. The conclusions formed by the expert group showed that "under the premise of meeting national and industry standards, this structural system can be used for multi-story buildings under 100 meters."

A series of doubts were placed before Broad Group. In December 2012, a super-high-rise project coded J97 located in the "Broad City" headquarters quietly began construction. On the afternoon of July 20th, reporters drove to the site of the J97 project and found that the foundation work had been completed, the base was finished pouring, and the staff indicated that the current infrastructure project was in the final stages, with the main construction of the ground level starting this month. It is understood that the "Little Sky City" project will construct 97 floors above ground and 2 floors below ground, reaching a height of 349.2 meters, with a foundation excavation depth reaching 20 meters. Compared to other traditional reinforced concrete buildings, the main difference lies in the use of a strong concrete structure for the basement and full steel structure above ground. After completion, the project may be used by Broad Group employees.

When the reporter asked Zhang Yue if the "J97 project was a trial run for Sky City," Zhang Yue repeatedly shook his head and said, "No, no. We have many more projects to build!"

Skepticism Three: How are earthquake, wind resistance, and fire safety issues addressed?

Can Broad Group's construction model withstand earthquakes and winds above 100 meters? How are fire safety issues resolved?

In response to these questions, Zhu Linfang, spokesperson for Broad Group, stated that a press conference will be held soon to address all concerns.
