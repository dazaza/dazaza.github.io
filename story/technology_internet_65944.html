Night Fire & Trajectory's Blog - Summary of Blog's First Year Income

by xloong on 2008-04-28 23:19:45

Nightfire: Today is April 28, 2008. On this day last year, the blog that I share with Ji Gu was born. Today marks its first anniversary and I am preparing to write a brief summary of the blog's earnings.

Current Blog Status:

Total number of posts: 450

Total number of comments: 378

Total number of trackbacks: 2

Total pageviews: 238,165

Total messages: 80

Current theme: default3

Current style: default3

Average daily IPs: 400 (from 0 IPs per day at the start to 600~800 IPs per day now)

Actually, when I first set up this blog, it was to learn ASP, but then it just fizzled out. I'm the type who gets enthusiastic for only three minutes; there's nothing I can do about it. Later, after encountering the "Guess Earnings, Win Cash" event initiated by President's blog, I started exploring blog monetization. However, I didn't place any ads at the time because the blog had just opened, lacked content, and had no traffic.

Later, as the traffic increased slightly, I placed some ads like Ali Mama and Google AdSense. I won't go into too much detail, but here are the earnings reports:

Ali Mama: 187.36 RMB

A newly emerged online advertising alliance from last year where money is calculated based on the time an ad space is bought, weekly, and you can set your own price.

Feedsky: 63.29 RMB

RSS feed hosting, paid comments, and in-feed ad insertion. Of the earnings above, 60 RMB comes from paid comments and 3.29 RMB from feed ad displays.

Bolaa Frame Ads: 12 RMB

Bolaa provides paid comments and frame ads (video broadcasting). Due to too many requirements for their paid comments, I didn't participate.

Private Ads: 70 RMB

If you want to place ads on my blog, please leave us a message or contact QQ: 153886799 directly.

Google AdSense: $53.12

I don't need to say much about this one; it's Google's ad network.

Adtoll: $8.19

An overseas ad alliance similar to Ali Mama where you can set your own price. The placement of this ad wasn't good, always in the bottom right corner, so the income was pitiful.

WidgetBucks: $34.54

This alliance no longer supports Chinese websites for ad placements. Friends with English sites can try it out; it's easier than Google AdSense.

Ziddu: $5.763

A file-hosting service where you get 0.001 USD every time someone downloads a file you uploaded.

ShareaPic: $0.3450 USD

Image sharing, similar to the one mentioned above. For every thousand views of images you upload, you get paid 0.22 USD. I haven't done much with this because getting image links after uploading them is quite troublesome.

There were also some other alliances that I can't recall now, but I've stopped working with them.

Total: 332.65 RMB + 101.958 USD

I'm not sure what the current exchange rate is, but if we assume 1 USD = 7 RMB, the result would be 713.706 RMB. Searching "101.958 USD to RMB" on Google gives the result: 101.95800 USD = 713.911607 RMB

Final RMB earnings: 1046.56 RMB

It's quite pitiful... President earns at least 1000 RMB every month, and I only made a bit over 1000 RMB in a whole year. But our blog types are different; he specializes in studying how to make money through blogs, while I focus on internet technology, internet news, and internet resources, never deviating from the main topic. Otherwise, by riding on trends like the recent艳照门(photoscandal) or Zhang Xiaoyu, traffic could have skyrocketed.

Moreover, I don't expect to be like John Chow, making thousands of dollars a month from his professional blogging career. My expectations aren't high; as long as I can cover the normal operating costs of my blog, I'm fine. With 1000 RMB a year, after deducting domain and hosting fees, there will still be plenty left over. Hehe, the remaining amount will slowly be divided between me and Ji Gu.

And I almost forgot one important point: I only started placing ads in late August last year, so technically, in a full year, it would be more than 1000 RMB... HOHO~
