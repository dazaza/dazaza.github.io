Precautions for eye corner surgery:

1. Preoperative preparation:

This is very important as it relates to the effect and success rate of the surgery, as well as the likelihood of complications. Therefore, attention must be paid. This includes avoiding medications that reduce platelet coagulation function before the surgery; patients with hypertension and diabetes should fully inform the doctor of their condition during the initial consultation so that the attending doctor can confirm the surgical plan; ensure physical health before the surgery, free from infectious diseases or other bodily inflammations.

2. Postoperative care:

This is crucial for consolidating the surgical effect and avoiding complications. This includes trying to avoid water contact at the surgical site postoperatively; keeping the surgical area clean to prevent infection. After the surgery, local wounds can be pressure-dressed or cold-compressed with ice packs, but the pressure should not be too high to avoid eye damage. A quiet and comfortable environment should be provided for recovery after the surgery. In the short term after the surgery, reduce watching TV or reading newspapers. When resting in bed, it’s best to lie semi-recumbent (raising the pillow) to avoid excessive eye fatigue or a low head position which could exacerbate wound swelling; avoid eating stimulating foods like chili peppers; strictly follow the doctor's advice on medication and follow-up visits.
