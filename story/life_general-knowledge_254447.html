How to Choose Second-hand Furniture

by longersing on 2009-10-31 22:45:53

The second-hand home furnishings industry has been very popular in recent years, and how to choose second-hand furniture has also become a hot topic. From selecting goods, inspecting them, to finally picking them up, there is indeed a method to it all. Today, let us learn from others' experiences.

Selecting Goods: First pick the seller, then look at the photos; have a plan in mind.

There aren't many websites for buying and selling second-hand furniture. They usually won't help you monitor the seller's credit or the quality of the goods like Taobao does. Therefore, filtering out furniture merchants who are just advertising and picking out genuine sellers who are willing to transfer furniture at a low price due to personal reasons is the most important. Secondly, make sure to carefully check the real-life photos of the furniture uploaded by the seller. Only contact the seller to view the goods if the style, color, and design suit your preferences. As Mr. Zhang said, only by having a plan in mind can you hit the mark directly.

Tips: 1. Diverse sources, be cautious when choosing. Most of the low-priced second-hand furniture sold online is mainly made of wood panels, without brands, and the sources are relatively chaotic, so one must be careful when selecting. 2. Outdated styles, uneven quality, more suitable for rental properties. Most sellers originally bought these pieces for temporary accommodations, so outdated styles and uneven quality are big issues. Compared to owner-occupied housing, this type of second-hand furniture bought and sold online may be more suitable for rental properties.

Inspecting Goods: Buy what you like; remember to stay focused.

Directly arrange to see the goods at the seller's home. Picking out what you like is the most effective way to avoid mismatches and wrong purchases. The furniture that the seller usually displays online typically consists of complete sets that can furnish two or three bedrooms, mainly large items such as sofas, beds, and wardrobes. Some smaller pieces might take a back seat, but that doesn’t mean they lack purchasing value. Take Mr. Zhang as an example; he initially selected nine pieces of furniture, but after seeing the goods, he thought the shoe cabinet and the storage cabinet on the balcony were also good, so he spent an extra 400 yuan to buy them together.

Of course, it's not necessary to buy a whole set of furniture. You can select according to your needs. Ignore those you don’t like or that don’t fit properly. However, if you buy too few pieces, the price will naturally be higher.

Tips: 1. Environmental protection first, material second. Even though things are cheap, pollution cannot be ignored. Whether there is a strong, irritating smell or whether it makes your eyes water is the most direct way to confirm whether the furniture is environmentally friendly. Determining the material lays the foundation for subsequent bargaining. 2. Having receipts is safer. Due to the chaotic sources and varying usage periods, purchase invoices and warranty cards are the best proof of the quality of the goods. Therefore, try to ask the seller to show the receipts.
