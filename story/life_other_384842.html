What are the maintenance and precautions after uterine fibroid surgery?

by tjfk999 on 2011-03-14 09:32:24

What are the precautions and maintenance after uterine fibroid surgery? First of all, understanding the postoperative precautions for uterine fibroid surgery is extremely important for the patient's recovery. So, what should be noted after uterine fibroid surgery? Experts from gynecological hospitals answer questions for a wide range of uterine fibroid patients!

Firstly, attention should be paid to a balanced diet after uterine fibroid surgery. Reasonable eating can avoid the recurrence of fibroids caused by insufficient intake and utilization of trace elements. Avoid long-term and excessive stimulation of estrogen, use hormone drugs reasonably, and eat fewer nutritional supplements. Consume more foods rich in protein, vitamins, and iron, such as fish soup, grapes, cherries, vegetables, etc.

Secondly, pay attention to cleanliness and abnormal symptoms after uterine fibroid surgery.

1. Pay attention to cleanliness after uterine fibroid surgery

After discharge, the incision on the abdominal wall needs to be kept dry, and bathing (no tub bath) can be done one week later. This is because the stitches have just been removed at the time of discharge, and the wound has not fully healed yet. Moreover, the scab on the incision has not fallen off, so it is not suitable for bathing. Of course, the whole body skin still needs to be kept clean, and wiping the body is appropriate. Wash the vulva every night or after using the toilet.

2. Pay attention to possible abnormal symptoms after uterine fibroid surgery:

A. Vaginal bleeding: For those who underwent intramural fibroid or submucosal fibroid resection, there are wounds on the uterine wall, which can lead to a small amount of vaginal bleeding after the operation. Generally, it will not last more than 10 days, all of which are normal. If it lasts more than half a month, you should go to the hospital for a check-up;

B. There is generally no bleeding after subtotal hysterectomy, but if the cervical cut edge is high, there may be a little vaginal bleeding on the day of menstruation each month. The amount of bleeding depends on the height of the cervical cut edge. If the cut edge is high and there is more residual endometrium, then the vaginal bleeding will be relatively more, otherwise, the vaginal bleeding will be less;

C. After total hysterectomy, there may be a small amount of yellow secretions or bloody secretions 10-15 days later, which can be observed for a few days and naturally fade away. If purulent secretions appear, it may be an infection at the vaginal incision stump or vaginitis, and you should go to the hospital for diagnosis and treatment, find out the cause, and deal with it in time. If patients with subtotal or total hysterectomy experience heavy vaginal bleeding, it may be due to suture rupture, and they should immediately go to the emergency department of the hospital for examination.

Finally, pay attention to diet and recurrence after uterine fibroid surgery.

1. Dietary Precautions for Uterine Fibroids

After discharge, it is advisable to eat light, easily digestible foods high in protein, vitamins, and minerals. Eat more vegetables and fruits to keep bowel movements smooth, as constipation can make the vaginal stump sutures thin and prone to rupture and bleeding.
