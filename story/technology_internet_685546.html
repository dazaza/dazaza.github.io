On February 23rd, the result of the trademark infringement case filed by Shenzhen Proview Technology against Apple Trading (Shanghai) Co., Ltd. was announced. Pudong Company Registration [http://www.shanghaijingshi.com/]. The Pudong court rejected Shenzhen Proview's request for Apple to stop selling the [iPad] tablet computer.

The trial of this case opened two days ago, but no result was announced on that day.

During yesterday’s trial, the court believed that one of the prerequisites for Shenzhen Proview's application to be supported by the court is that the defendant is currently implementing or about to implement an act infringing upon the exclusive right to use a registered trademark.

In this case, the [iPad] tablet computers sold by the defendant Apple come from Apple Inc. in the United States. A dispute over the ownership of the involved trademark due to a transfer contract between Apple Inc. and the applicant is currently under second-instance review at the Guangdong Provincial Higher People's Court. Before the final judgment is made by that court, the ownership of the involved trademark remains uncertain.

Under these circumstances, it lacks basis to determine that the defendant's sale of [iPad] tablet computers in Shanghai constitutes infringement. According to Article 140, Paragraph 1, Item (11) of the Civil Procedure Law, and Articles 4, 10, and 16 of the Interpretation of the Supreme People's Court on Several Issues Concerning the Application of Law in Handling Cases of Pre-litigation Injunctions Against Trademark Infringement and Preservation of Evidence, the court dismissed the application.

Although Proview's pre-trial application for a sales ban against Apple (Shanghai) was rejected, since the ownership dispute over the iPad trademark is still under second-instance review at the Guangdong Provincial Higher People's Court with the hearing scheduled for the 29th of this month, whether the iPad can ultimately be sold in mainland China remains unknown.

If Apple still loses the case, then the iPad will be banned from being sold in mainland China, and the release of the iPad3 may be postponed.
