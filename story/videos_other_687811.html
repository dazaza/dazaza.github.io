15-Year-Old Sister Raises Younger Siblings Alone After Mother's Suicide and Father's Death

by xue94fwsh on 2012-03-02 18:42:10

In the home of 15-year-old Lu Shengzhen from Group 3, Gaojian Village, Longdong Township, Yunyang County, she sits together with her 12-year-old sister Lu Xiaobi and 10-year-old brother Lu Xiaoling. Photo by Li Bin, Chongqing Morning Post.

"I dream of us three living together every day. Being separated in two places, I haven't taken good care of my sister and brother, it's my fault, sorry to our deceased father and mother. If this continues, my sister and brother's future will be ruined by me." —— Elder Sister Lu Shengzhen

"Our parents have been gone for many years, elder sister is our parent. During these two months when the elder sister went to the city to study, we were so unaccustomed, no one hit us, no one scolded us, but the two of us often cried because we missed our elder sister so much. We are a family, we must not be separated." —— Second sister Lu Xiaobi and younger brother Lu Xiaoling

A

The Difficult Journey Home

40 kilometers by car takes two and a half hours, and costs 60 yuan by motorcycle.

Not long after staying in this rural stone house, Shengzhen's youngest grandfather and second grandfather both came. "These three children, their lives are truly hard. So young, and they've already lost their parents."

Shengzhen only has two impressions of her mother: one is that she loved her very much, and the other is that she was always taking medicine. She started school early, entering the first grade at the age of five. At that time, her mother would buy Wahaha and come to school to see her, then take her home and cook meat for the three children.

But her mother often went to her cousin's house. Her cousin's brother was a barefoot doctor who treated people. Her mother went there to get medicine. Later, her mother took her to several barefoot doctors to see them. How her mother passed away, she didn't know at that time.

Later, she heard from her second grandfather that when she was six years old, her mother couldn't bear it, thinking that she couldn't be saved, so she ended her life with a rope. When she grew up, she realized that her mother had contracted rheumatic heart disease at a young age, and it was very serious, almost sentencing her to death.

After her mother's death, Shengzhen found that her father's temper became irritable, and he began to beat the three of them. But her father was known for being diligent, working tirelessly on the farm, getting up at four in the morning and coming home at eight in the evening was common.

"This nephew also had it tough, his wife died, and everyone chipped in money to bury her. In the following years, he was both father and mother, besides taking care of the three children, he also had to take care of his blind elderly mother, and there was still so much work in the fields." Shengzhen's second grandfather said that Shengzhen's father had a hard time supporting the family. He would find time to go to the town to do temporary jobs, earning tuition for the children and covering some daily expenses. He lost his life while working hard to earn money.

Shengzhen remembered that back then she hadn't started school yet, shortly after the Spring Festival, one day her second grandfather called her, her second sister, and younger brother and said: "Are you still playing here, do you know, your father had an accident."

"What happened?"

"Dead!"

While wiping his tears, her second grandfather told the three children to wait at home. On the way to handle the funeral, Shengzhen, who was already mature enough, heard the whole story from relatives: her father set off explosives at the construction site, earning fifty to sixty yuan per day. He worked for half a month and then had an accident because one of the explosives failed to detonate. When her father went back into the cave, the bomb exploded.

C

Destiny Full of Hardships

Mother committed suicide, father died from an explosion, leaving the three as orphans.

On November 4, Friday, it rained heavily. At 2:30 PM, Lu Shengzhen boarded the interview vehicle of Chongqing Morning Post, heading for her home deep in the mountains. She wanted to return to see her second sister and younger brother. This was the second time she returned home since school resumed in September, but she couldn't find the way home.

She is already fifteen years old, but she only came to the county town once in March this year. Before that, the farthest place she had been to was about five kilometers away from her home at the mountain pass. Her home faces west and looks east. During clear weather in July and August, she could see the sun rise from the mountain pass every day. One day, she brought her second sister and younger brother to the mountain pass to look around. But outside the mountain pass, there were still mountains, nothing else.

With the map in hand, the interview vehicle departed from Yunyang County. After 40 minutes, it exited at the Hongshi exit of the expressway. Shengzhen said that previously, she spent 25 yuan to take the expressway bus here, then spent 60 yuan to hire a motorcycle to go home. "This is the cheapest option. The driver said that during festivals, it costs 80 yuan to ride a motorcycle from here to Gaodian Village, not a penny less."

After exiting the expressway, it was all downhill, passing by the backwater ditch formed by the 175-meter water storage of the Yangtze River. The scenery here was quite nice, with red leaves on the slope land. Driving along the river for 25 kilometers, they arrived at Longdong Township. They asked a pedestrian on the road where Gaodian Village was. He said it was still far away!

Following the directions given by the pedestrian, the interview vehicle entered a dirt road. The road was slippery after the rain, and multiple times they had to ask villagers to help push the car from behind. Later, they kept climbing uphill, not knowing how long they climbed, how many mountain peaks they crossed, and how many winding dirt roads they overcame. By 5:30 PM, the interview vehicle reached the flat road on the mountain, drove onto the tractor road, and Lu Shengzhen pointed to the mud house halfway up the opposite mountain and said, "That's my home."

Looking at the mileage meter of the car, it wasn't far from the expressway exit to here, less than 40 kilometers, but due to the poor road conditions, it took two and a half hours to travel. Shengzhen said that the road surface was bad, which might be the reason why there was no public bus service.

Lu Shengzhen picked up her sister Lu Xiaobi and her brother Lu Xiaoling to go home.

Shengzhen herself didn't know how she got through those years after her father's death. "From then on, there were only four people left in this family: my sister was seven years old, my brother was five years old, and my grandmother was completely blind."

Shengzhen was afraid to recall how she got through those five years: "I got up very early in the morning to cook, then took my second sister and younger brother to school. After returning from school, I continued doing housework. But there was never any peace at home, especially during the three years when my grandmother was still alive."

Shengzhen said that after her father's death, her grandmother's mood was also not good, causing trouble every day, and she also fell ill, losing control of her bladder and bowels. "I didn't know what to do, washing while crying. The thing I feared most every day was going home after school. At that moment, all the troubles came at once."

As for her second sister, she was more active, not serious about homework and studying, needing her supervision and reminders. Her younger brother's body was weak, "Every night I slept poorly, having to wake him up to go to the toilet, otherwise, he would wet the bed."

However, fortunately, several grandfathers and cousins lived near her home, sometimes helping out. During festivals, they never forgot them, inviting them to celebrate together.

"In those years, I had no ideals, felt inferior, and didn't like talking. The teacher even consoled me," said Shengzhen. Luckily, her cousin's sister, who was about the same age as her, often came to talk to her, reducing her inner distress.

Of course, she also had her own way to relieve stress. She would shout loudly at the big mountain or cover herself in the quilt and cry hard.

"My grandmother passed away two years ago, and then my second sister and younger brother moved to live in the boarding school," said Shengzhen.

D

Strong Eldest Sister

At the age of ten, she had to take care of her sister, brother, and blind grandmother.

On the mountain road, the sun that had just emerged from the clouds shone brightly, and the red leaves on the tops of the mountains looked beautiful under the sunlight. However, the sun soon set, and the sky gradually darkened. The reporter from Chongqing Morning Post and Shengzhen found her second sister Lu Xiaobi and younger brother Lu Xiaoling at Gaojian Village School by the roadside.

This was a rural boarding school. Children in the mountains had to walk for two hours to reach the school. Considering the distance, the school built dormitories so that children didn't have to walk tens of miles to school and home every day. They attended classes for ten days and then had four days off. Lu Xiaobi and Lu Xiaoling were now such students, attending a boarding school. Xiaobi was in fifth grade, and Xiaoling was in fourth grade.

The two children were naturally very happy to see their elder sister come back. Since their elder sister went to the city to attend vocational school, she hadn't returned. "During these two months when our elder sister went to the city to study, we weren't used to it. No one hit us, no one scolded us, but the two of us often cried because we missed our elder sister so much," Xiaoling told the reporter from Chongqing Morning Post.

Of course, they hadn't returned home for a while either. After picking up the two children, driving for a section, and walking for about one kilometer, they arrived at the children's home, where it was already dark. Lu Shengzhen skillfully took out the key and opened the door.

Inside the house was pitch black. Shengzhen said that there was no light in the hall. To save electricity, the house only had one light bulb in the bedroom.

What was their home like? With a flashlight, the reporter took a look. Such houses were very common in the countryside, built with stone walls and tiled roofs. The children's house had three rooms: a hall and two bedrooms. One of the bedrooms was empty, with only a wooden bed frame left.

The three children slept squeezed together. Shengzhen said that since their father's death, they had lived together. "At night, my sister and brother said they were scared. Sleeping together was also to take care of my brother, who had been sickly since childhood and still wet the bed."

There was almost no furniture in their home, and the room was empty. The only usable electrical appliance was a light bulb. "The rice cooker was broken by my brother, and it hasn't been repaired yet."

A House with Only One Light Bulb

Like the rural areas of the 1980s, she and her two siblings slept in one bed.

Yunyang Teacher Training School, Lu Shengzhen practicing the piano in the classroom.

"I never thought I could continue my studies." Last July, Shengzhen graduated from junior high school. She tried to learn farming like other rural children. "Cutting pig grass, digging soil, these things I hadn't done before, one by one I learned."

She thought that her younger brother and sister's academic performance was still okay, so she decided to raise them in the countryside, hoping they could attend a good school and work in the city.

Her junior high school graduation and subsequent farming activities were known by her aunt who worked in the city. Her aunt had almost the same experience as her. Both of her aunt's parents passed away early, and she started managing the household at the age of 18, raising four younger sisters.

Her aunt sympathized with her and borrowed money for her to attend the teacher training school to study preschool education. "At first, I hesitated because no one would take care of my younger siblings." Later, her aunt persuaded her, saying that after three years of study, she could start working. "Once I have a stable income, the tuition fees for my younger siblings will be worry-free."

She chose to continue her studies, and her teachers said she was very diligent. However, a problem arose: without her older sister around, her younger siblings didn't even know how to wear sweaters. Their academic performance plummeted, dropping from the top few in class to the bottom.

"I'm conflicted, I don't know what to do," Shengzhen said. It was unrealistic to go back every week because of the expensive transportation costs and her motion sickness. "Each trip home feels like a severe illness, and I can't recover for a whole week."

"Every year, there are city people wanting to adopt my younger sister, but for someone who has lost so many family members, none of us want to lose each other," Shengzhen said. Currently, her younger brother's heart condition isn't good; he cannot exert himself, run, or climb stairs.

Thinking about these issues, "My heart aches," Shengzhen said. Now, she dreams of the three of them living together every day, tutoring her younger sister with homework, and taking care of her younger brother's health.

Chongqing Morning Post Reporter Ren Mingyong

E

Can Barely Hold On Anymore

She dreams of the three of them living together every day, tutoring her younger sister with homework, and taking care of her younger brother's health.

Hotline for Chongqing Morning Post 966966

"Second sister, come quickly, it's our elder sister, she's back!" At 6 p.m. on the afternoon of November 4, at the primary school located deep in the mountains of Gaodian Village, Longdong Township, Yunyang County, a short boy wearing slippers pulled a similarly thin girl towards the entrance.

The second sister and younger brother call Lu Shengzhen their elder sister, although she is only 15 years old. However, she is indeed the head of this household: a family of three children, all orphans, with no father or mother. As the eldest sister, she manages everything related to their daily life, including studying, clothing, eating, and living.

But after graduating from junior high school, she could no longer manage everything. She came to the county teacher training school to study preschool education and lived separately from her second sister and younger brother. Just the round-trip transportation cost was nearly 200 yuan, which she couldn't afford. Moreover, she would vomit as soon as she got on the vehicle, vomiting ten times during each trip home until there was only acid water left in her stomach.

"Each trip home feels like a severe illness, and I am uncomfortable for the following week," Lu Shengzhen said. Since she started studying in the city in September, her younger brother's health has deteriorated, with no improvement in his condition. Furthermore, the academic performance of both children has plummeted, dropping from the top few in class to the bottom.

Since the age of 10, she has played the role of "parent" in this family, single-handedly supporting it for five years. Now, this elder sister is struggling to hold on.
