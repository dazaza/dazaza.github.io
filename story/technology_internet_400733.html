Following this way of online shopping, you won't be deceived ~~ Original history of blood and tears

by anonymous on 2011-04-01 13:33:20

The first and most important thing is that you must not be lazy when shopping online!!! You must check out multiple stores!!!

If you hastily buy something out of laziness, it's highly likely that you won't be satisfied with it!!!! Isn't that right? Especially for us students, these unscrupulous store owners are unbearable!!!

If you're doing a broad search, like for shirts or trench coats, it's best to scroll through at least fifteen pages (I usually look through seven or eight pages).

The biggest risk of online shopping is that you can't see the actual product photos. It's easy for sellers to steal pictures from other stores or use magazine images to pass off as real ones, which often leaves you disappointed when you receive your order. After scrolling through so many pages, you'll directly know if this store is using the same pictures as others. If all the pictures are the same, they are likely magazine images. Don't even bother looking at stores without actual product photos!!!

Secondly, do you know your body measurements?! You might only know that you wear size 160A or 170B. If you don't want to go through the hassle of returning items due to wrong sizes and pay expensive return shipping fees, the best way is to get a tape measure immediately!!!

Record your measurements in a notebook: shoulder width, bust, waist, thigh circumference, calf circumference, and foot length. You can also measure clothes that fit you well. This way, it's much easier to avoid buying the wrong size. Size is very important in online shopping. Many clothes come in one-size-fits-all, especially for smaller and thinner girls who may not be able to fill them out properly. So when shopping, everyone must pay very close attention to the size. It's best to ask the store owner if their sizing differs from the market standard.

Thirdly... Never buy shoes online!!! Especially certain foreign sports shoes - nine out of ten are fake. Even if they aren't fake, the quality may still be much worse than those sold in physical stores. A classmate of mine was scammed once. Can you prove it's a counterfeit?!! It doesn't have obvious quality issues, has a label, but it's still fake!!! If it's just for vanity... it's not worth it. You might end up with sweaty feet...

And for women, high heels easily peel or break. If you bought them over a month ago and there's an issue, you can't protect your rights or would have to pay return shipping fees. In physical stores, within a month, they are covered by warranty.

Fourthly, do you know how to read pictures?!

Many people fall for scams because the pictures look great and powerful, leading to naive trust. Do you know how store owners mock you foodies for falling for it? You deserve to be scammed!!!

We've already discussed magazine pictures. Pictures with white backgrounds or beautifully decorated house interiors are mostly magazine pictures. Of course, some large malls can achieve this too, but you must ensure that the model is exclusive to their mall. If you see the same model in another store, it's probably a magazine picture.

Don't those girls with big eyes, oval faces, and long calves look familiar?! Correct! They are Korean SZ magazine pictures, heavily edited to lure you into scams.

Many students say, "Then what pictures should we look at?" Let me tell you downstairs...

This experience should be my most unique one... forged through tears and blood...

Here's how to look at pictures so you won't be scammed:

First, understand the lighting and color differences: This is almost unavoidable in online shopping, but now the clothes I buy rarely have this problem.

Firstly, it's best to buy the color shown in the picture you clicked on. The reason the seller dares to put this picture up is that this color attracts more clicks and possibly fewer loose threads.

Secondly, avoid bright colors. Red is the color most prone to color differences. Purple and beige can easily appear dirty, and black needs caution as it tends to fade significantly. It's recommended to wash separately and add salt for soaking. Are there colors less prone to fading? Light lemon yellow, pure white, camel, light gray, and blue are less likely to have significant color differences.

Thirdly, this step is crucial. Models generally have good figures, and their specific poses (especially bending over, side views, pulling or looping their own clothes) may mask flaws in the clothing. If the seller provides natural light (not too strong sunlight) flat-laid product pictures, it's undoubtedly the best.

Second, carefully read the seller's description and classification. Many people overlook this, especially on Taobao, under the detailed information section.

Pay attention to the lining, fabric, thickness, and sleeve style. If buying wool, note the wool content.

Let's share a basic common sense: If you're buying a blazer, avoid pure cotton without interlining as it wrinkles easily and looks cheap. Some fabrics, especially long sweaters, tend to pill easily. Rabbit hair absolutely cannot be bought as it pills after a few wears. For wool, check if it's blended; blends tend to pill easily.

Third, carefully review the seller's ratings and basic information.

Ratings reveal a lot, such as size, color differences, and product quality. Everyone knows this.

How many positive reviews can you trust? Honestly, even 99%+ need scrutiny. There are many such reputable stores on Taobao.

Actually, the seller's location should also be considered. Hangzhou and Suzhou have relatively good quality, followed by Shanghai and Beijing, then Guangzhou. Other places may have fewer styles.

Other tips for buying cheaper items:

1. Group buying saves shipping fees? NO, Guangzhou VIP Information Technology Co., Ltd.! If you find a store with good quality and reputation, why partner with others to save a few shipping fees? Let me tell you, it's best not to. It can cause disputes with friends and make returns inconvenient. If you really want to, it's better to accumulate a few items and buy them together yourself. This way, you can get a VIP card. VIP cards offer many benefits, such as free shipping, discounts, and priority customer service.

2. Buy clothes out of season.

Many people know this, but it's hard to implement in reality. Online shopping makes this possible. Winter clothes bought in March or April are usually about 50% off. Summer clothes are randomly priced at 29 or 39 RMB, and you can get pretty good ones. Buying spring and autumn clothes in winter is better as summer clothes hit the market too quickly, while winter offers more choices.

3. Small shops are cheaper.

Many people think malls are trustworthy, but small shops often have decent items. Not only are they less likely to result in wardrobe clashes, but they are also cheaper. Especially if the owner personally takes real-life pictures, they are more reliable, and the clothes appear more realistic.

Supplement:

There are several types of sizes on online stores:

1. Strict SML: The S here might be slightly small, so be cautious. Suitable for those around 160 cm and under 90 lbs.

2. MLXL: The M size here generally fits well, allowing for a long-sleeve or thin sweater underneath, suitable for autumn.

3. One-size-fits-all: Be very cautious with this. TB has a lot of these. If buying one-size-fits-all, shoulder width shouldn't exceed 39 cm, otherwise, no matter how tall or big you are, you might not be able to fill it out. The garment length shouldn't be shorter than 60 cm unless you specifically want a short top above the waist. Bust size over 100 cm should generally avoid it unless for special cases. Girls around 160 cm and under 94 lbs generally fit well. Lengths between 77 to 79 cm suit long sweaters or general dress lengths, ending about five centimeters below the hips.
