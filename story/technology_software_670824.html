Soaking feet is not suitable for everyone, Medical Consultation Network

by lokes350 on 2012-02-21 11:47:36

As the saying goes: "The tree withers when its roots dry up first, and a person grows old when his legs weaken first." In Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM), it is believed that: "The nose is the root of the sprout opening, the ear is the root of divine mechanism, the nipple is the root of primary energy, and the foot is the root of essence energy." The nose, ear, and nipple are the fundamentals of three aspects of the human body, while the feet are the total source of the body's essence energy. Therefore, feet are especially important for the human body.

Dr. Qi Xiulan, a TCM expert and chief physician, introduced that soaking feet, especially with medicinal herbs, allows the drug components to enter the body through the skin, meridians, and vessels to take effect. For those who cannot take warming yang-supplementing medicine, foot bathing can help them warm their limbs and avoid the symptoms of excessive heat caused by eating medicinal food.

1. Soaking Feet Can Prevent Yang Deficiency and Regulate Meridians

Dr. Qi Xiulan pointed out that the feet are the farthest part of the human body from the heart. Due to the cold stimulation in winter and spring, the blood vessels in the feet contract, causing obstacles in blood circulation, which can easily induce various diseases. Hot water foot soaking can improve local blood circulation, remove coldness, promote metabolism, thereby preventing yang deficiency, cold intolerance, and other symptoms, achieving health preservation and fitness effects.

Moreover, the feet are one of the places where the body's meridians converge. Among the twelve main meridians of the human body, the three yang meridians of the foot terminate at the foot, and the three yin meridians of the foot start at the foot. There are more than 60 acupoints distributed on the feet. Through herbal soaking, stimulating these acupoints can regulate the meridians, smooth the flow of qi and blood, adjust the function of internal organs, thus playing a role in disease prevention and treatment.

2. Foot Soaking Is Not Suitable for Everyone

People often say: "Soaking feet in spring raises yang and consolidates the loss; soaking feet in summer removes dampness and heat; soaking feet in autumn moistens the lungs and lubricates the intestines; soaking feet in winter warms the dantian." It can be seen that soaking feet in winter makes people feel warmer and reduces fatigue after outdoor activities.

However, although foot soaking is good, it is not suitable for everyone and should be used according to individual conditions. Overall, the elderly, hypertensive patients, and those who are weak and sick should not soak their feet for too long in hot water because prolonged soaking can cause sweating and palpitations.

In addition, children should not soak their feet in hot water either. Because the arches of the feet begin to form during childhood, it is important to protect them from an early age. If children frequently soak their feet in hot water, the ligaments on the soles of their feet will become loose, which is not conducive to the formation and maintenance of the arches and can easily lead to flat feet. Children should not frequently soak their feet in excessively hot water, nor should they soak their feet in hot water for a long time.

For the rest of the adults, generally speaking, they can achieve health preservation through foot soaking.

3. Soaking Feet Before Sleep Yields the Best Results

Dr. Qi Xiulan pointed out that when soaking feet, attention should be paid to the selection of containers, timing, methods of soaking, and the choice of medicinal water. Specifically:

1. Selection of Containers: Currently, there are various types of massage foot-soaking devices available on the market. However, Dr. Qi Xiulan advocates buying a relatively deep wooden bucket, with a depth that reaches the calf muscles being ideal. Choosing a wooden basin for foot soaking has two advantages: one is that wooden basins do not dissipate heat as quickly as plastic or iron basins, making it easier to maintain warmth; the second is that wooden basins are closer to nature and are very beneficial for the human body.

2. Water Temperature Control: The best water temperature for foot soaking is "hot but not scalding," with temperatures preferably below 50 degrees Celsius. During soaking, if the water cools down, hot water should be added promptly to maintain a constant temperature.

3. Duration Control: Although foot soaking is beneficial, the duration should not be too long and should be controlled within 15-20 minutes. Slight sweating on the forehead indicates that the foot soaking has reached its optimal therapeutic state. Avoid soaking until you sweat profusely, as this can deplete yin. The specific duration should be adjusted gradually based on the age, gender, health condition of the person soaking, and their personal feelings after soaking.

4. When to Soak Feet: It is not advisable to soak feet when eating, feeling too full, or overly hungry, as foot soaking accelerates systemic blood circulation and can easily cause dizziness. Additionally, soaking within half an hour after a meal is not recommended as it may affect gastric blood supply. What is the best time to soak feet? As the saying goes, "Take three hundred steps after meals, and have a bowl of soup before sleep" and "Washing feet before sleeping is better than taking tonics." Therefore, the best time is before sleep, as it not only warms the body but also aids in sleep.

5. Massage After Soaking for Better Results: It is best to massage the soles of the feet promptly after soaking. There are more than 60 acupoints on the soles of the feet. For ordinary people, rubbing both soles of the feet with both hands can achieve excellent results. Dr. Qi Xiulan particularly emphasized that every toe gap must be massaged, as these areas are usually impossible to reach otherwise.

6. Choose Herbal Foot Soaking: When using herbal foot soaking, make sure to boil the herbs in a pot first, then cool them to below 50 degrees Celsius before pouring them into a wooden basin for use. Otherwise, the efficacy of the herbs cannot be fully utilized. To save resources, the boiled herbs can be reused for two days in winter. Furthermore, all oral medicines can be used externally unless they are particularly irritating to the skin, in which case caution should be exercised.
