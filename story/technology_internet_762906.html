Taobao draws a national "insomnia map": Shanghai is the city where people are most sleepless

by anonymous on 2013-07-24 17:58:16

"I go to bed around 11 o'clock every day, then I watch videos and browse online stores on my phone, and I don't feel sleepy until around 1 o'clock." Mr. Ding, a 30-year-old white-collar worker who works in a commercial building on Moganshan Road in Hangzhou, complained. In fact, more and more people are starting to have trouble sleeping. "People who sleep are all similar, but those who can't sleep each have their own reasons." "Dear, why can't you sleep?" The owners of Taobao shops take advantage of this trend and start selling 'sleep aid' products.

Yesterday, a set of data published by Taobao was also quite interesting. People who can't sleep have very distinct regional characteristics. In the last five months, among those who bought eye masks and earplugs as sleep aids, the top 5 cities with the highest per capita purchase volume all included Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, and Shenzhen, four first-tier cities. Hangzhou is the only non-first-tier city in the top five, ranking third.

The most active "insomniacs":

23 million people shop online at midnight

"Is there anyone still awake at this hour?" Nowadays, such late-night insomnia-related Weibo posts are becoming increasingly common. "Insomniacs" instantly transform into "night shoppers," browsing Taobao on their phones while lying in bed, with no reduction in shopping enthusiasm despite the deep night. On the contrary, late night becomes one of the times with high mobile shopping order rates.

"In such hot weather, I definitely wouldn't go out shopping during the day, so when I can't sleep at night, I use that time for shopping." Ms. Li from Hangzhou is a typical night owl. She says she often doesn't feel sleepy after 12 o'clock, so she opens her phone and browses Taobao. "Yesterday, I bought two pairs of sandals and one dress within an hour." Finally, with the satisfaction of shopping, she finally fell asleep. However, her money flows out daily.

There are not few such "night shoppers." According to data released by Taobao, in 2012, a total of 22.832 million people shopped on Taobao between 11 PM and 5 AM. Among them, men who stayed up late to shop on Taobao were also not few, accounting for 48% of the total, while women accounted for 52%.

The most insomniac province:

Jiangsu, Guangdong, Zhejiang

What should you do if you really can't sleep? More and more people are starting to rely on external tools like earplugs and eye masks to fall asleep. Clever online store owners have obviously already spotted the business opportunity. When I searched for earplugs on Taobao, I found over 200,000 items for sale, and nearly 90,000 eye masks.

The "sleep aid market" is actually quite promising. Just the product of eye masks alone comes in various types: 3D, cartoon patterns, summer-appropriate ice packs, silk eye masks, bamboo charcoal eye masks, etc. Since the start of summer, ice pack eye masks have become very popular. I saw on one Taobao store that the monthly transaction volume for a single item exceeded ten thousand pieces.

Quite a few sellers have made significant efforts with pillows, launching calming lavender pillows, buckwheat pillows, etc., and even foods like millet and jujubes have been labeled as promoting sleep.

The "insomnia map" shows that according to Taobao's transaction data over the past five months, from the proportion of people buying earplugs and eye masks to the total population of the city, the top five include all first-tier cities like Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, and Shenzhen. Hangzhou, which ranks third, is the only non-first-tier city among them.

Top 10 cities for purchasing earplugs: Shanghai, Beijing, Hangzhou, Guangzhou, Shenzhen, Nanjing, Suzhou, Wuhan, Chongqing, Ningbo.

Top 10 cities for purchasing eye masks: Shanghai, Beijing, Hangzhou, Shenzhen, Guangzhou, Chongqing, Nanjing, Suzhou, Wuhan, Chengdu.

Shanghai can be considered the most severely insomniac city. Jiangsu, Guangdong, and Zhejiang are the three provinces with the worst sleep.

"Can't sleep at night, can't wake up in the morning" forms a vicious cycle. The cities where people have more trouble sleeping at night are also the cities where people have the hardest time waking up in the morning. In the past five months, the top five cities with the largest population purchasing alarm clocks are the same: Shanghai, Beijing, Hangzhou, Shenzhen, Guangzhou.

"I need to wear earplugs every night to fall asleep, but then I can't hear my phone's alarm in the morning. After setting my phone alarm to vibrate, I'm worried it won't be strong enough to wake me up, so I bought another vibrating alarm clock." Xiao Mao's "sleep aid" and "wake-up" equipment has become essential for many insomniacs.

The most helpless reason for buying earplugs:

Husband snores

So, who are the insomniacs? Online store owners have the clearest understanding. "The number of people who need earplugs to sleep increases every year, and the number of consulting customers has grown, and the age range is getting wider. Basically, students and urban white-collar workers account for 90% of total sales," said the owner of an earplug-selling online store.

Indeed, the store's flagship product—a 19 yuan earplug—sold 19,000 units in just one month.

Why can't everyone sleep? There are actually many reasons. It's understood that students who buy eye masks and earplugs may live in school dormitories. Mobile devices are now very common among students, and after bedtime, some classmates may still be playing games. To avoid being disturbed, they might as well "seal themselves off."

White-collar workers, on the other hand, have relatively higher work stress during the day, leading to sleep problems at night. Some amusing buyers give the reason for purchasing earplugs as "My husband snores too loudly."

Taobao data shows that most buyers of earplugs and eye masks are "post-90s" and "post-80s." Taking the eye mask sales over the past five months as an example, "post-90s" are the largest demand group, with 36% of eye masks purchased by people aged 18-24. Additionally, people aged 25-29 account for more than 30%.

From the perspective of product purchase ratios, men are more likely to have trouble sleeping than women, with about 20% more male insomniacs than females.
