by fengmi7v on 2012-02-19 23:48:12

(September 7, 1993) Public Security Bureaus and Departments of all provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities:

Since last year, a few large and medium-sized cities have seen the emergence of some private detective-like institutions under names such as "Security Affairs Investigation Office," "Civil Affairs Investigation Office," and "Social Economic Affairs Detective Office." Due to media coverage, this matter has attracted attention from all sectors of society. Investigations reveal that most of these institutions are run by idle members of society and former employees of the legal and political system. Their business scope generally includes: handling civil and economic disputes among the people; debt collection; finding relatives; security prevention technology consultation; and investigations involving personal privacy.

Given that these civilian organizations lack legal basis for their operations, rights, and obligations, and that the various businesses they operate are already managed by divisions of the public security organs, procuratorates, courts, and judicial departments, some of the means used in their operations violate relevant provisions of the "Criminal Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China," exercising partial powers of national law enforcement agencies, which have already caused some problems. Therefore, it is now decided:

1. Strictly prohibit any unit or individual from establishing various forms of "Civil Affairs Investigation Offices" or "Security Affairs Investigation Offices" and other private detective-like civilian institutions.

2. Existing private detective-like civilian institutions should be carefully cleaned up and abolished together with the industrial and commercial administrative departments. Prohibit continuing similar businesses under the guise of changing names or methods.

3. Strengthen management and education of internal personnel within the public security system. Prohibit any unit (including schools, associations, and societies affiliated with public security and armed police forces) or individual (including retirees) of public security organs and armed police forces from organizing or participating in the work of private detective-like civilian institutions.

Please report the above to the local Party committee and government, and conscientiously implement according to actual conditions. Relevant implementation situations should be reported in a timely manner.

Ministry of Justice Approval Regarding Various Social Organizations Not Establishing Legal Consulting Service Institutions

(July 24, 1991)

Hainan Provincial Department of Justice,

Your July 6 request regarding issues on the establishment conditions of legal consulting service institutions has been received. The following approval is given:

1. Article 4 of the State Council's Regulations on the Registration and Management of Social Organizations stipulates: "Social organizations shall not engage in profit-oriented business activities." Based on this, social organizations shall not establish legal consulting service institutions to provide paid legal services to society and cannot serve as the sponsoring units of legal consulting service institutions.

2. Legal consulting service institutions must completely decouple from sponsoring units in terms of personnel, finances, and materials, implementing independent accounting and self-responsibility for profits and losses. Full-time staff of legal consulting service institutions must number no less than eight and may not hire part-time staff. Their charges and financial management should be subject to supervision by price and finance departments. Sponsoring units may not charge management fees to legal consulting service institutions.

The Ministry of Justice and the State Administration for Industry and Commerce Notice Regarding Further Work on the Reorganization and Re-examination of Legal Consulting Service Institutions

(May 9, 1990)

Judicial Halls (Bureaus) and Industrial and Commercial Administrative Bureaus of all provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities:

After the issuance of the Several Provisions by the Ministry of Justice and the State Administration for Industry and Commerce regarding the strengthening of management over legal consulting service institutions, the majority of regions have collaborated effectively between judicial administrative organs and industrial and commercial administrative organs, promptly conducting reorganization and re-examination of legal consulting service institutions. The chaotic state of legal consulting service institutions has initially been rectified. However, progress remains uneven. Some provinces and cities face numerous difficulties and resistance; some regions have insufficient emphasis on this work and inadequate measures. To further improve the re-examination of legal consulting service institutions, the following opinions are proposed:

1. Carefully approve and register newly applied units. All places should strictly follow the Several Provisions issued by the Ministry of Justice and the State Administration for Industry and Commerce, ensuring strict approval and registration without favoritism. Units not meeting the conditions must be firmly denied approval and registration. During the period of reorganization and re-examination, no new legal consulting service institutions will be approved. According to actual circumstances, the approval and registration work for re-applied units should be completed before the end of July 1990. Legal consulting service institutions that fail to apply for approval and registration by the deadline will all be abolished. Industrial and commercial administrative organs will cancel or change the registration based on actual situations for legal consulting service institutions that have not been approved upon re-application or do not meet registration conditions.

2. Legal consulting service institutions must thoroughly decouple from sponsoring units in terms of personnel, finances, and materials. In terms of personnel, it refers to full-time staff of legal consulting service institutions not holding concurrent positions in sponsoring units or other units, stopping enjoyment of all benefits in their original units. In terms of finances, it means that legal consulting service institutions should implement independent accounting and self-responsibility for profits and losses, bearing risks themselves, and sponsoring units (or competent authorities) may not gratuitously transfer or occupy the funds and assets of legal consulting service institutions.

3. Retired cadres who meet the conditions for legal consulting work, if not giving up their original retirement benefits, may not serve as full-time staff of legal consulting service institutions and may not enjoy full-time staff benefits, only serving as specially invited personnel. The number of specially invited personnel in legal consulting service institutions may not exceed the number of full-time staff.

4. The administrative division mentioned in the name of legal consulting service institutions refers to the administrative division where the institution is located.

5. A "National Legal Consulting Service Company" refers to legal consulting service companies directly under the ministries and commissions and directly affiliated institutions of the State Council approved by the State Council or authorized departments of the State Council. It does not include legal consulting service institutions established by subordinate units of the ministries and commissions and directly affiliated institutions. Non-national institutions will be reviewed and approved by the judicial departments of the provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities where they are located.

6. Full-time or specially invited personnel of legal consulting service institutions may be referred to as "legal workers," and are not allowed to be called "lawyers" or "lawyer workers." Personnel with lawyer qualifications cannot conduct cases under the name of lawyers.

7. Legal advisory offices (departments) established internally by enterprises, institutions, and social organizations may not engage in external paid legal consulting services.

8. Legal consulting service institutions named as companies, in addition to executing this notice, must also be approved according to the relevant provisions for company reorganization and consolidation.

Several Provisions by the Ministry of Justice and the State Administration for Industry and Commerce on Strengthening the Management of Legal Consulting Service Institutions

(July 15, 1989)

To strengthen the management of legal consulting service institutions, satisfy societal needs for legal services, and according to the relevant provisions of the Enterprise Legal Person Registration and Management Regulations of the People's Republic of China and the Notice of the General Office of the State Council forwarding the Request of the Ministry of Justice to Strengthen the Unified Management of Legal Service Institutions (State Council Office Document No. 82 [1985]), the following provisions are made regarding the establishment and management of legal consulting service institutions:

1. Name

The name of a legal consulting service institution is composed in the order of administrative region, trade name, nature of business, and organizational form. Without review and agreement by the Ministry of Justice and approval by the State Administration for Industry and Commerce, the words "China," "National," "Chinese," or "International" may not be used.

A legal consulting service institution meeting the conditions for establishing a company will be called "... Legal Consulting Service Company"; those not meeting the conditions for establishing a company will be called "... Legal Consulting Service Department (Society, Office)."

2. Nature

Legal consulting service institutions provide legal consulting services to society, implement independent accounting, bear profits and losses independently, and can independently assume civil liabilities as corporate legal persons. Their economic nature is determined according to the relevant provisions of registration management regulations. Legal consulting service institutions must completely decouple from sponsoring units in terms of personnel, finances, and materials.

3. Establishment Conditions

Establishing a legal consulting service institution must meet the following conditions:

1. More than eight full-time staff members, at least five of whom have obtained a diploma in legal studies at the college level or above; or have obtained lawyer qualifications; or have actually engaged in legal professional work (including work in public security, procuratorial, judicial, and administrative law enforcement agencies, as well as teaching and research in law) for more than five years. Departed or retired cadres meeting these conditions are implemented according to national regulations.

2. Fixed office locations and necessary office conditions.

3. Self-owned funds exceeding 30,000 yuan (for companies, more than 100,000 yuan).

4. Sound financial accounting systems.

4. Scope of Business

Legal consulting service institutions can provide the following legal services:

1. Answering legal inquiries;

2. Drafting, reviewing, and revising legal documents;

3. Serving as legal advisors;

4. Handling non-litigious legal affairs.

5. Approval, Registration, and Supervision

1. Approval and Registration

Applications to establish legal consulting service institutions must first be reviewed by the department responsible for setting up the institution, then submitted to the local judicial administrative organ. After being reviewed and agreed upon step-by-step by local judicial administrative organs, it is submitted for approval by the provincial, autonomous region, or municipal judicial department. The formal approval document should notify the application unit and be filed with the Ministry of Justice. Applications to establish nationwide legal consulting service companies must be approved by the Ministry of Justice.

Within thirty days of receiving approval, legal consulting service institutions must hold the judicial administrative organ’s approval document and apply for registration with the industrial and commercial administrative organ according to the relevant provisions of graded registration management, obtaining a Business License for Corporate Legal Persons. After registration, legal consulting service institutions should file their bank account details with the original registration authority.

When applying for establishment and registration of legal consulting service institutions, the following documents must be submitted: ① the approval document from the supervisory department; ② an application report; ③ articles of association; ④ identity proof of the main person in charge and educational background, title, and experience proof of the members; ⑤ use proof of the business activity location; ⑥ credit or capital verification proof; ⑦ other necessary documents.

If legal consulting service institutions wish to change registered items, they must submit applications to the original approving agency, and within thirty days of receiving approval, apply for changes in registration with the original registration authority.

For termination of legal consulting service institutions, approval must be sought from the original approving agency, and after clearing taxes, debts, and other relevant matters, deregistration must be processed with the original registration authority.

The establishment, name changes, or terminations of legal consulting service institutions must be announced in newspapers at the provincial level or above.

2. Management

Legal consulting service institutions must accept management from judicial administrative organs and industrial and commercial administrative organs, and pay management fees and registration fees as per regulations.

Members of legal consulting service institutions (including members with lawyer qualifications) conducting consulting activities under the institution's assignment must not use the name of lawyers and do not enjoy the various rights of lawyers prescribed by law.

Legal consulting service institutions must not employ part-time personnel.

By January 31 each year, legal consulting service institutions must report to the judicial administrative organ that approved their establishment about the business development of the previous year and their annual summary, and must accept supervision and inspection from industrial and commercial administrative, taxation, finance, and auditing departments. They must also undergo annual inspections by the original registration authority as per registration management regulations.

3. Penalties

For violations of the following provisions ①, ②, ③, and ④, judicial administrative organs will impose warnings or revoke the institution depending on the situation; for violations of ①, ②, ⑤, and ⑥, industrial and commercial administrative organs will impose warnings, fines, suspension of business for rectification, confiscation of illegal gains, withholding, or revocation of business licenses according to relevant registration management regulations:

① Operating without approval and registration;

② Exceeding the approved scope of business;

③ Conducting activities under the name of lawyers;

④ Causing significant losses to parties due to incompetence;

⑤ Failing to handle changes in registration, deregistration, and annual inspections as required;

⑥ Violating other registration management regulations;

6. Financial Management

Legal consulting service institutions providing legal services shall temporarily charge fees according to the Lawyer Fee Charging Methods and must not exceed the standard.

The operating income of legal consulting service institutions must legally pay taxes, and post-tax profits must set aside certain proportions for development funds, risk funds, social insurance funds, welfare funds, etc.

The wage standards for personnel of legal consulting service institutions are determined by the provincial, autonomous region, and municipal judicial departments in consultation with relevant departments. Personal income from retired personnel engaged in legal consulting services must comply with relevant State Council regulations.

7. Before issuing these provisions, existing legal consulting service institutions must complete supplementary approval and registration procedures with judicial administrative organs and industrial and commercial administrative organs within sixty days of the issuance of these provisions. During the supplementary process, judicial administrative organs and industrial and commercial administrative organs must strictly review them according to these provisions. Legal consulting service institutions that do not conform to these provisions must be rectified or revoked.

8. Matters not covered in these provisions should be handled according to relevant laws and regulations.

9. These provisions take effect from the date of issuance.

Notice from the Ministry of Justice on Issuing the Implementation Plan for the Detachment and Reform of Law Firms and Social Legal Consulting Service Institutions

(August 14, 2000, Judicial Communication [2000] No. 100)

Departments of Justice of all provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities, and the Department of Justice of the Xinjiang Production and Construction Corps:

We hereby issue the "Implementation Plan for the Detachment and Reform of Law Firms and Social Legal Consulting Service Institutions." Please conscientiously implement it. Please report progress and issues encountered during execution to our department promptly.

Implementation Plan for the Detachment and Reform of Law Firms and Social Legal Consulting Service Institutions

Based on the Notice of the General Office of the State Council on Clearing and Rectifying Economic Authentication Class Social Intermediary Institutions [State Council Office Document (1999) No. 92] and the Opinion of the Leading Group of the State Council for Clearing and Rectifying Economic Authentication Class Social Intermediary Institutions on Implementing Detachment and Reform Between Economic Authentication Class Social Intermediary Institutions and Government Departments, to ensure the successful completion of the detachment and reform of law firms and social legal consulting service institutions, this plan is formulated.

1. Goals and Principles of Detachment and Reform

The goal of this detachment and reform is to perfect the practice organization forms of lawyers and social legal consulting services, establish and improve self-regulatory operation mechanisms adaptable to market economy development needs, promote the independent, objective, and fair practice of law firms and social legal consulting service institutions, making them truly become practicing entities that manage themselves, bear risks themselves, constrain themselves, develop themselves, and compete equally.

The basic principles of this detachment and reform work are to correctly handle the relationship between reform, development, and stability, advancing reforms positively and steadily while ensuring the team does not disintegrate and business is uninterrupted; correctly handling the interests of the state, collectives, and individuals, preventing the loss of state-owned assets while protecting the enthusiasm of a wide range of lawyers; encouraging law firms and legal consulting service institutions to implement mergers during detachment and reform, taking the path of scale development.

2. Scope and Content of Detachment and Reform

The scope of this detachment and reform includes: (1) National Capital Law Firms that have achieved self-financing; (2) Law Firms attached to public institutions, enterprises, or social organizations; (3) Social Legal Consulting Service Institutions approved by judicial administrative organs, attached to government departments, public institutions, enterprises, or social organizations.

The content of detachment and reform involves four aspects of personnel, finance, business, and name, where detachment units must completely detach from the attached units (hereinafter referred to as attached units), specifically achieving:

(1) Detachment units no longer belong to administrative or public institutions, no longer possess administrative levels. Staff members included in the compilation exit the compilation, and their personnel files are transferred to talent flow service institutions designated by provincial-level judicial administrative organs;

(2) Attached units fully withdraw their investments in detachment units, or convert them into lease relationships. Attached units are no longer investors of detachment units and no longer enjoy investor rights, nor may they obtain improper economic benefits from detachment units in any form;

(3) Detachment units may not undertake any functions belonging to attached units and independently conduct business activities according to law. Attached units may not interfere with the normal business activities of detachment units;

(4) The names of law firms must comply with the provisions of the Measures for the Administration of Names of Law Firms. Names already verified by our department must be used after reform; names not verified must be submitted to our department for verification.

The names of social legal consulting service institutions may not carry the names or traces of attached units, nor may they arbitrarily use the words “China,” “National,” or “Chinese” without approval, nor may they merely use administrative regions or place names as their names.

3. Organizational Form After Reform

After detachment, law firms should be reformed into partnership law firms or cooperative law firms. Law firms may entirely transform into partnership or cooperative law firms, or several partnership or cooperative law firms may be formed based on voluntary combinations. Newly established law firms after reform must meet the provisions of the Lawyers Law and our department’s regulations on the establishment of law firms. Local areas may explore and improve the internal management mechanisms of cooperative law firms according to modern company internal management mechanisms based on local conditions.

After detachment, social legal consulting service institutions will all be reformed into “limited liability companies.” After detachment, they should be re-approved and registered according to the conditions and procedures specified in the Several Provisions by the Ministry of Justice and the State Administration for Industry and Commerce on Strengthening the Management of Legal Consulting Service Institutions. For provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities that have issued local regulations on the management of social legal consulting service institutions, they should be handled according to local regulations. After detachment, social legal consulting service institutions that do not meet the conditions for establishing companies will all be revoked; those meeting the conditions for establishing law firms, if practitioners voluntarily form partnership or cooperative law firms, judicial administrative organs should allow it.

4. Steps and Time Arrangements for Detachment and Reform

Local areas should implement according to the following requirements, ensuring that the detachment and reform work is completed before October 31, 2000. Detachment units that have not completed the detachment and reform work before the specified deadline will all cease practice or be deregistered.

(1) Mobilization Stage. By August 20, local areas must convey the spirit of detachment and reform to detachment units and their staff. Detachment units must carefully organize all staff to study relevant documents, unify thoughts, and enhance understanding.

(2) Asset Definition and Scheme Formulation Stage. This stage should end before September 20. This stage is organized and implemented by the attached unit, mainly including: (1) asset settlement of detachment units. Attached units may entrust social audit institutions to conduct asset checks, financial audits, clean up creditor-debtor relationships, and issue audit reports for detachment units; (2) negotiate and formulate personnel placement schemes; (3) define property rights of detachment units’ assets. Property rights definition is conducted by the state-owned asset management department of the attached unit, issuing relevant reports. When defining property rights, sufficient personnel placement costs must be reserved according to the personnel placement scheme; (4) negotiate and formulate asset disposal schemes; (5) formulate plans to establish new law firms and social legal consulting service companies.

Attached units must fully listen to the opinions of detachment units and their staff, reaching detachment agreements through negotiation. Detachment agreements must include the above five contents. Law firms should submit detachment agreements to provincial-level judicial administrative organs for review before September 20, and detachment agreements for social legal consulting service institutions need to be submitted to the original approval institutions for review. After approval of the detachment agreement, it enters the implementation stage.

(3) Implementation Stage. This stage should end before October 20, mainly including: (1) dispose of assets and place personnel. Attached units must strictly dispose of the assets of detachment units and place staff members according to the approved detachment agreement; (2) establish new law firms and social legal consulting service institutions. Staff members of detachment units should determine reform forms, generate initiators, partners, or cooperators, investors, formulate articles of association, partnership agreements, and prepare application materials needed to establish new firms or companies based on voluntary combinations
