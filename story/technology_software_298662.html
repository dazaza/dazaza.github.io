What is a Barcode (II)

by goodgirlj9 on 2009-12-08 09:01:14

Understanding Barcodes: A barcode is not merely a group of bar and space symbols arranged according to a certain encoding rule, but it also represents specific characters, numbers, and symbols. The barcode system is an automatic identification system composed of barcode symbol design, manufacturing, and scanning reading.

Barcode Encoding Methods (Code Types) Introduction: There are many types of barcodes, with about twenty common code types, including: Code39 (Standard 39), Codabar (also known as "Code 27"), Code25 (Standard 25), ITF25 (Interleaved 25), Matrix25, UPC-A, UPC-E, EAN-13 (EAN-13 International Product Barcode), EAN-8 (EAN-8 International Product Barcode), Foreign Postal Code (a variant of Matrix25), Code-B, MSI, Code11, Code93, ISBN, ISSN, Code128 (including EAN128), Code39EMS (EMS-specific 39 code), and two-dimensional barcodes like PDF417.

Currently, the most widely used barcodes in China include EAN, UPC (used for printing product codes globally to uniquely identify a type of product; this is the most common barcode seen in supermarkets), Code39 (can represent numbers and letters, and is widely used in management fields), ITF25 (commonly used in logistics equipment management), Codebar (often used in medical and library fields), Code93, and Code128. Among these, EAN is the most widely used product barcode worldwide and has become the basis for electronic data interchange (EDI); UPC is mainly used in the United States and Canada; in various barcode application systems, Code39 is widely used within industries due to its ability to use both numbers and letters; in blood banks, libraries, and photo studios, Codebar is also widely applied.

In addition to the one-dimensional barcode printers listed above, two-dimensional barcodes have also been rapidly developing and have found applications in many fields.
