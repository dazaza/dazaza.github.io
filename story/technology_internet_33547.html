TCL's new computer had pornographic films, so it was forced to refund money

by netbeta on 2007-11-07 14:06:03

According to a report on November 7, the newly purchased computer could not be used normally after multiple repairs, so the manufacturer decided to replace it with a new one for the consumer.

The new computer was actually an old hard drive

The new computer he bought had malfunctioned several times within a year. After the motherboard was replaced several times, it still could not be used normally. Surprisingly, there were pornographic movies stored in the hard drive of the new computer the manufacturer replaced.

Consumer Mr. Liang sighed: "I originally bought the computer to make work and life more convenient, but I didn't expect so many troubles."

Recently, Mr. Liang told the reporter about his experience of buying a computer. On June 18, 2006, he spent 4998 yuan to buy a TCL "Yue Xiang" desktop computer. Unexpectedly, the computer malfunctioned within a week. It often froze at first, then turned into a black screen, and later couldn't start up.

On June 25, 2006, at Mr. Liang's request, TCL Company replaced Mr. Liang with another model of desktop computer "Pirate 8855". Because the configuration was different from the previous "Yue Xiang" computer, Mr. Liang paid an additional difference of 1200 yuan. He thought that he could use the computer comfortably now. However, a week later, the "Pirate 8855" also had the same fault: freezing, black screen, unable to start. Thus, Mr. Liang's troubles followed one after another. From July 2006 to September 2007, his computer was repaired 6 times, almost breaking down every one or two months. During this period, the computer motherboard was replaced 4 times, the graphics card was replaced once, the memory bar was replaced once, and the independent graphics card was replaced once. Mr. Liang said, "This computer has almost become my heart disease."

Since it could not be used normally after multiple repairs, on September 24, 2007, at Mr. Liang's request, TCL replaced his computer again. "An engineer brought me a TCL 8855 desktop computer host and said it was a new machine. When I turned on the computer, I found a lot of messy programs inside, such as securities, games, etc." Mr. Liang said. To his surprise, when he opened the player, he found movies inside. After checking the hard drive carefully, he found that it occupied 16G of space.

Mr. Liang said: "It is clear that the manufacturer passed off an old computer as a new one to me."

Unable to bear such after-sales service, Mr. Liang planned to return the machine. He called TCL's after-sales service and made his request, hoping that TCL would fully refund his more than 6000 yuan purchase price. A staff member surnamed Liu told him that according to the regulations, only refunds could be made after deducting depreciation fees, and after deducting a certain percentage of depreciation fees per day, Mr. Liang could only get about 500 yuan in cash. "Only refunding so little, how can I agree." Mr. Liang immediately rejected this solution.

A few days later, Mr. Lü from the TCL Customer Service Headquarters called Mr. Liang, indicating that he was willing to replace the same model of computer for Mr. Liang again. But Mr. Liang refused again, "I know that the Pirate 8855 model has been discontinued. What should I do if it breaks in the future?"

To verify Mr. Liang's statement, on November 3, the reporter called the TCL Customer Service Headquarters. A Mr. Lü accepted the interview by the reporter. Mr. Lü confirmed that Mr. Liang's computer was indeed repaired, but only the motherboard was replaced twice and the graphics card was replaced once. Regarding the incident of replacing Mr. Liang's old computer with a new one, Mr. Lü said that it was caused by the repair personnel's work error, and the company had criticized the relevant personnel. Regarding Mr. Liang's hope for a full refund, Mr. Lü said that according to the relevant provisions of China's Consumer Rights Protection Law, the company only needed to replace the same model of computer for Mr. Liang in principle, or refund the money after deducting depreciation fees. But considering Mr. Liang's situation, the company had agreed to fully refund Mr. Liang.

Yesterday afternoon, Mr. Lü from the TCL Customer Service Headquarters promised to refund Mr. Liang's purchase money within this week.
