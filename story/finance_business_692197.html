[Tissot Watches] Website optimization is just a few tricks

by vzqdtr4825 on 2012-03-06 00:27:25

Many webmasters are troubled by one problem, which is that Baidu does not include enough pages, and the inclusion is not fast enough. If the snapshots are not updated in time or are of poor quality, it means the website's weight is not high. Some webmasters even use Baidu snapshot times and inclusion numbers to exchange links. If the snapshot updates quickly, the ranking will also rise quickly. Therefore, updating snapshots daily is very important.

Making Baidu update snapshots daily is actually quite simple. Let me take my Shenzhen SEO Research Platform as an example. A month ago, I changed the program and restarted seriously. In less than a month, I improved the inclusion and snapshot updates. The articles I published were generally included on the same day, and the snapshots were updated daily.

How did I achieve this? Here are a few simple points to help you realize daily snapshot updates from Baidu.

First: Website URL inclusion is crucial.

Some webmasters often overlook this issue. In search engines, the website URL is also very important. What kind of URL do spiders like? URLs like xxx.com/htm with three levels, or xx.com/aaa.asp?a=xx with batch pages, or even longer URLs, are all disliked by spiders. Normally, spiders only crawl up to the second-level directory unless your site has a high weight, making it difficult for pages to be included.

The correct URL should ideally generate static pages, which helps users open them and makes it easier for spiders to index them. It is recommended that webmasters create content pages in the format of XX.com.html, which is simple and reasonable, and very beneficial for Baidu inclusion.

Second: Website optimization keyword links.

We often find that many webmasters, when optimizing the homepage or content page, like to pile up keywords and make the keyword density very high. When we optimize keywords, we like to bold the keywords or add hyperlinks to them. Here’s a note: if a page has too many keyword links, there are two results: either it won't be included, or it will be directly penalized (the latter being more common). So, when optimizing your site, pay attention to the keyword links, generally not exceeding four. Try to optimize from the user experience perspective, which is friendly to search engines and improves inclusion.

Third: Website page description.

Whether we are optimizing the homepage or the content page, don’t neglect the page description (Description). Write the page description reasonably. For example, for the homepage, we need to let the search engine know what your site is about and what it can offer users so it can provide appropriate feedback to users. The same applies to content pages. The Description is not for piling up keywords but for enhancing search engine friendliness and user experience. Try to make each page's description different. For article content pages, it’s best to describe based on the explanatory fields and avoid keyword stuffing.

Fourth: Originality of articles.

The website needs original content, and you must stick to originality, a topic discussed daily. However, truly achieving this seems rare. Actually, when building a website, if we are not familiar with the website content, writing original articles is also difficult. The benefit of original articles is that they can be published on large websites, allowing users to help reprint your articles, thereby obtaining external link promotion, which greatly aids inclusion and ranking. When you cannot write original content, you can choose pseudo-original content by slightly modifying others' articles. Specific pseudo-original techniques can be searched on Baidu.

Fifth: External link factors of the website.

External links are now a hot topic, and selling links is also quite common. The inclusion and ranking of a website also depend on external links. After we finish building the website and updating the content, we need spiders to crawl our site content, and external links are your good helpers. A simple analogy is, for example, a site linked via a friendship link where the spider crawls to that site, and that site contains a link to our site. Thus, the spider will also crawl our site based on the friendly weight established on that site, thereby increasing the site's exposure to spiders. Here's a suggestion: when looking for links, don't look at PR, just see if the other site has snapshots and whether its keywords have ranking requirements for exchange. Another point is that black links are not recommended for excessive purchase. If you really want to buy, please buy quality ones. The standard for links is generally that the other site's links do not exceed 40~50, and you can link with them. Exchanging 5~7 links per day is sufficient.
