Handbook of Online China

3. This year, the number of Chinese Internet users became the world's largest, at 253 million, accounting for 17.3% of the total population, while the United States is at 223 million, accounting for 15% of the total population;

4. The ratio of mobile phone users to Internet users is 2.59:1 in China, and 1.18:1 in the United States;

5. Half of the top 20 websites in terms of traffic in the Asia-Pacific region come from China (Baidu, Tencent, Sina, NetEase, Sohu, Alibaba, Xunlei)

7. As of June 08, the Internet penetration rate in China was 19.1%, slightly lower than the global average of 21.1%;

8. Statistical analysis:

68.6% of Internet users are under 30 years old; 39% have a high school education; 30% are students; 30.5% earn less than 500 yuan per month; 46.4% are female;

18 years old and under account for 19.6%, 18 to 24 years old account for 30.3%, 25 to 30 years old account for 18.7%, 31 to 35 years old account for 11%, 36 to 40 years old account for 8.7%, 41 to 50 years old account for 7.8%, over 50 years old account for 3.9%;

9. Analysis of online behavior: (All people/People under 25 years old)

IM instant messaging 81.4%/ 91.3%

Online music 86.6%/ 91.1%

Online video 76.9%/ 82.9%

Search 72.4%/ 73.4%

Online games 59.3%/ 68.2%

News 63.4%/ 63.4%

Email 56.5%/ 58%

10. 85% of the public supports Internet censorship; 93% of Internet users care whether website content is suitable for children; the results of more than four years of tracking surveys show that user trust in online content has dropped from 52% in 03 to 26%;

12. Top ten of Chinese Internet: Tencent, Baidu, Sina, NetEase, Yahoo, Google, Sohu, Sou Sou, MSN, Taobao;

13. Traffic ranking: as above;

14. Search market share:

Second quarter of 2008

Yahoo 8%, Google 25%, Baidu 64.4%

17. Blog readership ranks second (Japan first, South Korea third), blog writing ranks third (South Korea first, France second)

18. Social networking site usage ranks second alongside the US (UK first, South Korea third), photo uploading ranks second (South Korea first)

19. Video uploading and video watching both rank first, followed by South Korea and Japan

20. Podcast downloads rank first, RSS subscriptions rank third, the other two are still Japan and South Korea

21. Average time spent on BBS daily:

60% of people visit at least three BBS sites more than three times a week; 98% post, reply, or participate in voting

36.3% spend 1 to 3 hours daily, 44.7% spend 3 to 8 hours daily, 15.1% spend more than 8 hours daily;

BBS has become a center for business and public opinion dissemination in China.

22. Video website rankings: Tudou, Youku, QQ, Baidu, 6.cn, Sina, 163, CCTV

By the end of 07, there were over 160 million online video users, 65.9% watched videos through video websites, 63.7% through friend recommendations, 56.3% through search engines, 42.5% through links from other websites; 86.3% like to watch movies and TV shows online; 94.1% plan to share videos with friends via QQ or MSN;

23. IM market share:

QQ ranks first with a peak of 40.3 million concurrent online users at 79.9%, MSN at 4.9%, Sina UC at 4.1%, Feixin at 3.7%, Wangwang at 3.1%, others at 6.2%; Instant messaging software is most popular among people aged 18 to 30.

27. Per capita advertising spending in 07 was $11, compared to $594 in the US, $382 in the UK, and $325 in Japan;

28. Online advertising expenditure accounts for 8.06% of the total, UK at 18.32%, South Korea at 12.77%, Japan at 12.39%, US at 8.75%;

29. Per Internet user online advertising expenditure is $8, UK at $116, Japan at $95, US at $83;

30. Portal revenue statistics:

In 04, mobile value-added service revenue was twice that of online games and three times that of online advertising, now gaming and advertising make up the absolute majority;

31. Forecast:

50% growth in the online advertising market comes from the 08 Beijing Olympics; brand advertising growth will reach 38%; PPC search market growth 82%, Baidu continues to dominate;

33. Mobile user behavior analysis:

83.7% use SMS, entertainment is led by photos, music, and games;

34. iPhone

68% have heard of this product; men buy mainly because of its features, women because of its fashion and design; 88% of iPod owners are interested in the iPhone.

Apple products appear in as many as 67% of Hollywood blockbusters.
