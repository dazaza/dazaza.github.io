Silent Love

The falling leaves swirl in the autumn wind, fiercely smashing against the glass. They spy on the students in uniforms inside the classroom. In an easily overlooked corner, there sits a silent girl, often forgotten by others. From the start of school until dismissal, she just sits there, not speaking to anyone and never leaving the classroom. Occasionally, she waves her hand, as a fly buzzes around.

Today is like any other day; the first thing the teacher does upon arriving is to take attendance. "Number six, number six... oh, not here today," the teacher loudly announces, even though she raises her hand high, the teacher's gaze never sweeps to that corner. "I... I am here..."

From somewhere unknown, courage surges within her, and she suddenly stands up to speak. When she sits back down, she notices all her classmates are busy with their homework, none paying attention to her. She feels dejected and picks up her book, only to find a pair of eyes from the front row looking at her continuously.

"That... isn't he the class heartthrob? Why..." she wonders, quickly flipping through her book, flustered in a way she has never been before. But curiosity draws her eyes forward, where she sees him smile briefly before turning his head back to the blackboard.

A strange sensation makes her heart beat wildly. She begins to understand what it means to like someone, what happiness is...

Several days pass, and she continues living the same life, easily forgotten by the teacher. "Number six, number six," she hears her name called, but it's definitely not the teacher.

"It's him..." she realizes as he slowly approaches, the "rabbits" in her heart starting to leap again. He sits at the front row, flashing the same smile from a few days ago. "Hi, I'm Zhengyang, nice to meet you. And you, what's your name?" His sudden words leave her more at a loss, and she replies in a very soft voice, "You... hi, I'm Chen... Lan."

"Chen Lan, that's a great name. So, I'll call you Little Lan, okay?" It's the first time she hears someone address her this way, and her heart almost jumps out. "Alright... alright." "Then see you tomorrow, Little Lan," he smiles as he stands up and waves at her.

"See you..." A certain feeling wells up in her heart, something she's never experienced before. She starts to fall in love...

After several weeks of interaction, she discovers that he is a very outgoing, passionate, and intelligent person, completely opposite from her. But as they spend more time together, she begins trying to talk to others and make efforts to get along with them. Finally, she starts being remembered by teachers and classmates.

All of this is because of his "training." Being with him requires her to say many words and learn how to interact with strangers. Soon, she truly becomes an outgoing person. But towards him, she remains silent. Her heart tells her: she likes him...

Winter arrives, and the fallen leaves put on their coats. She waits quietly for the bus to come, alone. "Little Lan..." she hears someone calling her. "I've been looking for you for a long time. Here you are," he says, slightly out of breath.

"Is there something wrong? Why are you so hurried?" She looks at him curiously. "Be my girlfriend!" She thinks she must have heard wrong. "Be my girlfriend, will you?" The second question makes him even more nervous. "Isn't this exactly what I hope for? With him, I can be extremely happy. I like him. But why do I feel hesitant..."

She lowers her head, her mind racing with thoughts. "Let me think about it, okay?" She slowly answers... Minutes pass, the bus has already gone past the stop.

"Alright, maybe this is too sudden for you. We can talk about it later..."

He finally speaks, and she nods silently, saying nothing more.

Snow begins to fall, and they sit on a park bench, neither speaking. At this moment, the air is filled with either love or silence, she doesn't know. All she knows is that there's now a scarf around her neck, and they remain silent...

But this time, she leans on his shoulder, quietly accepting...

This silent love...
