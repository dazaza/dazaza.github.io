&nbsp;&nbsp; The ingredients you need to prepare are 5 grams of ginger, 30 grams of brown sugar, and 50 grams of jujubes (if there are seedless and pre-washed golden thread jujubes, those would be the best choice. If not, regular red dates or Huai dates will also work fine.)

Wash the jujubes and remove their seeds, slice the ginger, and place them all into a clay pot. Add an appropriate amount of water (mineral water is preferred), cover the pot, and bring it to a boil. After boiling, continue cooking for another 20 minutes. Then, add the brown sugar, stir evenly, and drink it while it's hot. Prepare this soup once daily and use it as a tea substitute in a thermos cup, once a day.

It’s best to use both hands simultaneously: first press the Yangchi穴 on your left hand with your right middle finger, then switch and press the Yangchi穴 on your right hand with your left middle finger.

This posture is simple and easy to learn, and no assistance from others is needed. You can do this while waiting for the bus, watching TV, or resting. There’s no need to apply too much force; naturally transmit the force from your middle finger to the Yangchi穴 for the best effect.

Yangchi穴 is located on the back of the wrist, precisely at the area where the metacarpal bones converge. To locate Yangchi穴, lift your hand so that the back of it is facing upwards. Several wrinkles will appear on your wrist. On the side closer to the back of the hand, press along the folds and you'll find a tender point at the center, which is the Yangchi穴.

It acts like a small solar energy source, storing the body's heat. Stimulating it can transmit this thermal energy to the extremities where blood circulation may be less effective.

At the same time, it is also a key acupoint that regulates the entire body's blood circulation. Stimulating Yangchi穴 can quickly promote blood circulation, warm up the body, and thereby alleviate coldness. Yangchi穴 can not only treat cold aversion but also regulate the functions of internal organs. Therefore, it is beneficial for treating colds, asthma, gastrointestinal diseases, kidney dysfunction, and more, making it a veritable "universal acupoint."
