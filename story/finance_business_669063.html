Untitled Document Fans of the Georgia State University men’s basketball team have a tremendous amount to be happy about this season. At the halfway mark of the 2011-12 season, the Panthers have already accumulated significantly more wins than they did in all of last season. The team had a disappointing performance throughout the 2010-11 season with an overall record of 12-19. Georgia State is currently 13-4 this year and 5-1 in their conference. The Panthers play NCAA Division I basketball in the Colonial Athletic Association (CAA). Conference rivals in the CAA include Delaware, Drexel, George Mason, Hofstra, James Madison, UNC Wilmington, Northeastern University, Old Dominion, Towson, Virginia Commonwealth University (VCU), and William & Mary. New head basketball coach Ron Hunter appears to be the momentum behind positive changes in the GSU program. Hunter comes to GSU with a successful resume as a Division I head coach. He spent 17 years as head coach at Indiana University-Purdue University Indianapolis (IUPUI). Under Hunter's direction, the program transitioned from the NAIA to NCAA Division I. He amassed a record of 274-219 at IUPUI and led the team to an NCAA Tournament appearance in 2003. Hunter is one of 48 college basketball coaches participating in this year's Infiniti Coaches Charity Challenge. Fans can vote for the coaches, and the winning coach will receive a $100,000 donation for their designated charity. The GSU Panthers have several new games remaining this season. On January 23, the Panthers will play James Madison University, followed by Virginia Commonwealth on January 28. February 8 looks like it’s a new game against Northeastern University, followed by Delaware on February 11 and Old Dominion on February 22. All of these are conference games. On February 18, the Panthers will play in the 10th Annual Sears Bracketbusters. This three-day event pits potential NCAA Championship hopefuls against each other. This year's event features the largest pool in the history of the Bracketbusters, with effectively 142 teams from 16 conferences participating. GSU is designated as a new team, and the visiting team will be announced on January 30. The CAA Tournament will begin on March 2 in Richmond, Virginia. The Championship final will likely be played on March 5. The Panthers play their games in the Georgia State Sports Arena, located at 125 Decatur Street in downtown Atlanta. Single game tickets are $12. Parking is available in Parking Deck G, with entrance off Collins Street. Those taking public transportation can exit MARTA at the Georgia State Station on the East/West line.
