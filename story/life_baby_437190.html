by fengtie on 2011-05-26 07:28:21

The stick拼game cultivates children's divergent thinking ability, allowing them to use small sticks to form triangles, squares, trapezoids, and various patterns such as houses, tables, and chairs. It nurtures the child’s ability to think of multiple transformations of a single object. Cultivating a child's divergent thinking from an early age helps them better learn and apply knowledge when they grow up, enhancing their problem-solving abilities.

For toddlers aged two to three years old, parents can use small sticks to create things for their baby to see and then let the child play with the sticks themselves. It is important to note that since babies are too young to have self-safety awareness, parents need to supervise during the game.

After the age of three, parents can try teaching the child to assemble some simple items. Once the child becomes proficient, gradually increase the difficulty.

Divergent thinking, also known as diffusion thinking, radiative thinking, or creative thinking, refers to considering and exploring problems from multiple angles, seeking diverse solutions. The characteristics of divergent thinking are: fully utilizing one's imagination, breaking through the original circle of knowledge, thinking outwards in all directions from a single point, and finding new and more assumptions, answers, or methods by recombining knowledge and concepts.

Children under the age of three do not yet possess deep thinking abilities. At this stage, the main goal is to coordinate the child's movements, laying the foundation for future cognitive development. Parents can create a relaxed, interesting, and enjoyable gaming environment for their child, sparking their interest in thinking, and encouraging them to operate things by themselves as much as possible, keeping the child in a state of active engagement. This helps foster habits of divergent thinking in entertainment.

The stick拼game uses small sticks and encourages children to make various little things with their hands. As the child gains success, they will become more proactive in creating, thus cultivating their divergent thinking ability.
