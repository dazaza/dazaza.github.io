TV Series "Tangshan Earthquake" Starts Filming

Produced by Huayi Brothers and adapted from the novel "Aftershock," the 40-episode TV series "Tangshan Earthquake" recently began filming in Beijing. On September 20th, the production team held a grand press conference to mark the start of filming. Attendees included the show's producer, Huayi Brothers Chairman Wang Zhongjun; executive producer, Huayi Brothers President Wang Zhonglei; director Yao Xiaofeng; as well as main cast members Chen Xiaoyi, Zhang Guoli, Xu Yabin, Tong Liya, Yuan Wencang, Wu Yue, Wang Ruoxin, and Zhu Tie. At the press conference, all actors appeared in their character costumes, making their first public appearance as their on-screen personas. Director Yao Xiaofeng revealed that the TV series would incorporate unused computer special effects footage from the movie version, combining it with newly filmed material to recreate the earthquake scenes.

Huayi Brothers' "Dual-Sword Union" Creates the First Heartfelt Drama of 2011

Similar to the film adaptation directed by Feng Xiaogang, the TV series "Tangshan Earthquake" is also based on the novel "Aftershock." The drama tells the story of a mother in Tangshan during the 1976 earthquake who must choose between saving her son or daughter. She ultimately chooses to save her son, but her daughter miraculously survives and is adopted by PLA soldiers. Thirty-two years later, the family reunites unexpectedly, and the emotional scars left by the tragedy need healing. Executive Producer Wang Zhonglei stated that the TV series "Tangshan Earthquake" is Huayi Brothers' largest investment for 2011, with meticulous attention paid to every aspect of production. Scriptwriting and revisions alone took two years. "We hope the TV series can touch hearts like Feng Xiaogang's movie did, moving every viewer at home just as much as the movie moved its audience," he said. Due to the national-level brand influence of the movie "Tangshan Earthquake" and the uniqueness of its subject matter, Huayi Brothers carefully considered how to maintain the high standards of the brand while adapting it for television. They decided to appoint Yang Shanpu and Zhuang Liqi, two of their most experienced producers, to oversee the TV series' production, ensuring its artistic quality and technical excellence.

Chen Xiaoyi and Xu Yabin Entangled for Life, Passing Zhang Guoli By

"I am Fan Xu from the movie," "I am Chen Daoming from the movie"... At the beginning of the press conference, leading actors such as Chen Xiaoyi and Zhang Guoli introduced their characters using the names of their movie counterparts, helping everyone quickly match the faces with the roles. Zhang Guoli explained that his character, Wang Deqing, was entirely different from the role played by Chen Daoming in the movie. The TV series' character differs even from the original novel: "His own child also perished in the earthquake. He desperately wants another child but has conflicts with his wife. Ultimately, they adopt Xiao Deng. This father figure will be very profound and complex in the TV series."

Both Chen Xiaoyi and Wu Yue have previously acted alongside Zhang Guoli as his on-screen wives in other productions. Speaking about these two "virtuous wives," Zhang Guoli joked that in "Tangshan Earthquake," he only shares scenes with Wu Yue, missing the chance to reunite with Chen Xiaoyi on screen. Chen Xiaoyi, sitting nearby, expressed relief at this missed connection, teasing Zhang Guoli: "The production brought you in just to make the whole show feel older!"

If Wang Deqing and Li Yuanni only cross paths briefly in the story, the emotional entanglement between Li Yuanni and Xiao Lin spans their entire lives. Chen Xiaoyi revealed that her character, Li Yuanni, is a retired dancer injured in an accident, while Xiao Lin works at the Cultural Station. There's always been an ambiguous emotional connection between them: "Before the earthquake, there was already an unclear emotional bond between Li Yuanni and Xiao Lin, noticed by Xiao Deng. Her hatred towards her mother partly stems from this." Chen Xiaoyi believes that "Xiao Lin becomes the emotional pillar Li Yuanni rebuilds herself upon after the quake. He supports her to keep living."

Speaking about her second collaboration with Xu Yabin, Chen Xiaoyi finds working with good friends much more harmonious and effortless. With Xu Yabin around, laughter never ceases on set: "He talks non-stop and never lets any silence linger." Chen Xiaoyi couldn't resist teasing Zhang Guoli again, recounting a recent encounter between the two male leads: "This morning, they passed each other on set. Guoli didn't recognize Xu Yabin at first. After walking past, he suddenly turned back and shouted: 'Oh, it's you! How come you haven't aged a bit in over ten years?'"

Unused Earthquake Special Effects from the Movie Make a Comeback

The movie "Tangshan Earthquake" extensively used computer-generated imagery to vividly portray the devastating earthquake scenes, leveraging IMAX technology to immerse audiences in the terrifying power of natural disasters.

Discussing how the TV series would recreate the Tangshan earthquake scenes, Director Yao Xiaofeng explained that the post-earthquake ruins were being reconstructed on the site of a demolition project. For the earthquake sequences, they would use footage not included in the movie and splice it with newly shot material for the TV series: "Due to the movie's runtime constraints, many computer-generated special effects showing building collapses and weather changes were not fully utilized or were cut from the final edit. Producer Zhuang Liqi considers these special effects invaluable assets for the TV series, and given Huayi's unique advantage, we've decided to incorporate them into the TV adaptation."

Yao Xiaofeng further noted that the TV series would continue the core themes of the movie and original novel, focusing on the "reconstruction" of familial bonds and inner worlds following the disaster.
