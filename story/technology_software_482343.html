Experts' Review of the 2009 National College Entrance Examination Essay Topics in Various Provinces

1. National Paper I: Shaanxi, Hebei, Henan, Shanxi, Guangxi 2009 College Entrance Examination Essay Topic

The rabbit has been the sprint champion at every small animal sports meeting, but it can't swim. Once, the rabbit was chased by a wolf to the riverbank and almost caught. The Animal Management Bureau sent the little rabbit to a swimming training class for all-round development. In the same class were also the puppy, the turtle, and the squirrel. The puppy and the turtle learned how to swim, gained another skill, and felt very happy; the little rabbit and the squirrel spent a lot of time but still couldn't learn, so they were very troubled. The coach, the wild duck, said, "I can swim with two legs, why can't you with four? Ninety percent of success comes from sweat. Come on! Quack quack!" The critic frog remarked, "What rabbits are good at is running! Why only train their weaknesses and not develop their strengths?" The thinker crane said, "Survival requires more than one skill! If the rabbit can't swim, it can learn to dig holes; if the squirrel can't swim, it can learn to climb trees." Requirements: Choose the right angle, clarify the theme, select your own style, and come up with your own title; don't go beyond the material content and meaning when writing, no plagiarism.

Review: The essay topic of the national first paper is also an allegorical story. Allegorical stories reflect reality, which is how we should cultivate talents in real life, focusing on this issue. How to recognize people's strengths and leverage them while avoiding their weaknesses, as you just mentioned, one is to take advantage of others' strengths to make up for one's own weaknesses, and the other is to leverage one's own strengths. The words of the coach, the frog, and the crane are not exactly right on target, but each makes sense. So, you can choose any one of these angles to write about, but you cannot stand in the shoes of the swimming coach one moment and the frog the next. I think a good exam essay, around 800 words, should have a smaller angle, which I always emphasize. The angle should be smaller and the content more concentrated. You cannot talk about one angle for a few sentences and then another for a few sentences, or your article will become scattered and disorganized. An allegorical story radiates reality from a distance, immediately bringing to mind what corresponds to reality, and then writing about real life, making the content more focused. As I just said, digging deeper, the content will be fresher.

2. National Paper II: Heilongjiang, Jilin, Gansu, Guizhou, Xinjiang, Qinghai, Tibet, Inner Mongolia, Yunnan 2009 College Entrance Examination Essay Topic

Based on the reading material, create your own title! Reading Material: On Christmas Eve, Dalton bought his mother a pair of "brown-gray" socks as a Christmas gift. When his mother saw the socks, she thought the color was too bright, so she said to Dalton, "How can I wear these cherry-red socks?" Dalton found it very strange: the socks were clearly brown-gray, so why did his mother say they were cherry red? Confused, Dalton took the socks to ask his younger brother and the people around him. Except for his younger brother who agreed with him, everyone else said the socks were cherry red. Dalton didn't let go of this small matter easily. After careful analysis and comparison, he discovered that his and his younger brother's color perception was different from others. It turned out that both he and his younger brother were colorblind. Although Dalton was not a biologist or a medical doctor, he became the first person to discover color blindness and also the first diagnosed patient. For this, he wrote a paper called "On Color Blindness," becoming the first person in the world to raise the issue of color blindness. Later, people named color blindness after him, calling it Daltonism.

Review: The essay topic of the second national paper follows last year's model. The difference is that this year there are three different stories (color blindness, instant noodles, dry cleaning machine), whereas last year there was only one complete story. Therefore, the main idea contained in the material is much clearer than last year, making it less likely for students to stray off-topic, and the score won't be too low. The principle contained in the material is: discovery and resolution of contradictions / emergence and pursuit of problems. The insufficient supply of noodles and the inability to wash stains are all contradictions and problems. Contradiction is the basic category of dialectics, existing in all processes of thing development and running through the entire process. It is the fundamental reason for the development and change of all things. The invention of instant noodles and dry cleaning machines is the ultimate solution to contradictions and problems. Writing this essay gives liberal arts students an advantage. Exam essays need only be expressed plainly and smoothly, with accurate, clear, coherent, and appropriate language. If the language can be vivid and distinct, with penetrating power and感染力, it would be considered a good article.

3. Beijing Volume 2009 College Entrance Examination Essay Topic

A song sings: "I've always had invisible wings, taking me flying and giving me hope; I've always had invisible wings, taking me flying towards the distance." Please write an essay with the title "I Have a Pair of Invisible Wings", no less than 800 words, genre unrestricted.

Review: This year, Beijing's essay question adopted a relatively stable method, namely the form of a topic essay; although it is a topic essay, two lines of lyrics are provided, offering some direction and guidance. A topic essay reduces the difficulty of reviewing for candidates, allowing many possible angles to write about, making it easier to write argumentative essays and emotional prose. This type of topic essay mainly examines the depth of students' thinking. There are many restrictions in topic essays, but this year's Beijing essay question has rich connotations; because it is a metaphorical topic, wings help people fly, meaning helping people succeed and realize their ideals. Confidence, resilience, optimism, and other positive will qualities can all be used as writing objects, making the topic quite broad. Candidates can write excellent articles as long as they carefully review the questions.

4. Tianjin Volume 2009 College Entrance Examination Essay Topic

With the topic "I Talk About Post-90s", genre unrestricted (except poetry), around 800 words.

Review: This topic is rated as the easiest to write because most of this year's candidates belong to the post-90s generation, writing about their own group, which they understand best. The characteristics of personality and individuality divided by era appeared only after the post-80s generation. Whether there are indeed differences between the post-90s and the older post-80s siblings needs comparison. Rebellion, individuality, and self-expression can be summarized well worth discussing. Simply pointing out differences isn't enough; don't forget commonalities with the post-70s and post-80s generations. Which traits should be continued and learned, and which individualities can be promoted, can be fully discussed. In the writing process, one point can be elaborated, but remember not to drift aimlessly. Let the teachers acknowledge that their children indeed possess these characteristics.

5. Shanghai Volume 2009 College Entrance Examination Essay Topic

Material-based essay: Zheng Banqiao was a calligrapher and litterateur of the Qing Dynasty, one of the "Eight Eccentrics of Yangzhou." From a young age, he loved calligraphy and aspired to master the essence of ancient calligraphy masters. He studied diligently, but initially, he only repeatedly copied famous calligraphy scripts, making little progress, which deeply troubled him. Until later, he realized: calligraphy is valuable for its originality, forming its own style; merely copying others' inscriptions wouldn't work. From then on, he sought innovation, exploring ways to incorporate bamboo painting techniques into his calligraphy art, eventually forming his unique style—the Banqiao style. Based on this material, write an essay, genre unrestricted, no less than 800 words.

Review: The Shanghai essay question has no specific requirements, making it easy to write. The first angle is that Zheng Banqiao's calligraphy style was his own creation, representing innovation. Candidates can write from this perspective, relating it to reality or their own similar experiences. The second angle is to write an argumentative essay about calligraphy art or other disciplines, emphasizing the need for innovation based on inheritance and learning for society to progress. Simple imitation won't lead to success; one must form their own style to achieve success, otherwise, they will remain under the influence of others. It is estimated that most candidates will write from these two angles. Alternatively, one could argue that Zheng Banqiao's unique calligraphy style is irreplaceable, but does the evaluation standard deserve scrutiny? Or discuss the network era where fewer people practice calligraphy, and how to promote traditional Chinese art, etc.

6. Chongqing Volume 2009 College Entrance Examination Essay Topic

Requirement: Life has many stories; you may be a participant, listener, or commentator in these stories. The essay should be no less than 800 words, with free choice of theme, no plagiarism allowed.

Review: This topic is easy to write, involving either the candidate themselves or storytelling. First, extract a story, which can be something experienced personally, read in various story publications, or heard from others. However, the story should not be chosen casually but filtered. Choose those with positive significance, as college entrance examination essays primarily require a positive and progressive mindset; otherwise, high scores cannot be achieved. The essay should provide insights and move readers, offering enlightenment and inspiration.

7. Liaoning Volume 2009 College Entrance Examination Essay Topic

Celebrity endorsements. Discuss the phenomenon of celebrities endorsing false advertisements, presenting five viewpoints in a material-based essay. Person A: 47% of people choose celebrity-endorsed products when purchasing similar goods. Person B: Celebrities are innocent; they might not know the product is false. Person C: Slightly. Person D: Celebrities receive large endorsement fees. Person E: Government supervision is not strict, and public opinion and inspection departments also fail in oversight; Hollywood stars have been fined millions of dollars for false endorsements.

Write an essay of more than 800 words based on the above material, choosing your own angle, creating your own title, genre unrestricted (except poetry).

Review: Celebrity endorsements are a current hot topic. The viewpoints are listed, so it is straightforward to write an argumentative essay, with plenty of examples available. Candidates should focus on moral and legal aspects, social ethics, etc., closely tied to the theme, such as how effective regulation can achieve a win-win situation. Writing well on this viewpoint can easily earn high scores.

8. Shandong Volume 2009 College Entrance Examination Essay Topic

Testimony is an experience, also a condensation of personal and societal memory. Throughout life, we witness the joys and sorrows of human existence and the changes in society; throughout history, many people or events become historical witnesses. Please write an essay titled "Testimony," no less than 800 words. Requirements: 1. Self-selected angle. 2. Self-determined theme. 3. Genre unrestricted except poetry. 4. Distinct genre features.

Review: This year's Shandong essay combines material-based and topic-based essays, reflecting the intention not to overly challenge candidates in terms of interpretation and theme selection. The material serves as both a restriction and a hint for candidates' thoughts. It suggests we can approach the theme from perspectives like contemporary life and societal changes, or from the perspective of people or objects in history. That is, we can focus on reality or look back at history. But we must clearly state what testifies to what, starting with a narrow scope, the more specific, the better. Another aspect of this topic is guiding students to pay attention to social hotspots and national livelihoods. In summary, there should be much to write about from a social hotspot perspective, but it must be unified under one theme.

9. Jiangsu Volume 2009 College Entrance Examination Essay Topic

Fashion manifests as new and novel phenomena in language, clothing, literature, etc., imitated and spread within a certain period. Various fashions emerge endlessly. Among them, there are interwoven views of good and bad, elegant and vulgar, beautiful and ugly. Innovation and imitation interact ceaselessly. Some fade like fleeting clouds, while others become classics. "People chase fashion not because it suits their temperament, but because everyone else is doing it." Please write an essay titled "Tasting Fashion." Requirements: ①Self-select an angle, self-determine the theme. ②Genre unrestricted except poetry. ③No less than 800 words.

Review: This topic is not difficult to write, close to life, but achieving excellence is not easy. To write well, one needs divergent thinking, crucially understanding and appreciating fashion from different angles without being confined to narrow thoughts. Some candidates may find this topic easy to tackle, while others may struggle. The reason is that some people in daily life pay close attention to fashion and find it interesting, thus handling it effortlessly. Other candidates might not understand fashion, feeling it is far removed from themselves. Actually, fashion is often on the lips of some people and frequently appears in newspapers and media. Pursuing fashion seems to have become a prevailing trend. But what is fashion? Whether spiritual or material, upon careful appreciation, everyone has their own fashion. Fashion is an all-encompassing concept, its reach extending into every aspect of life. Different eras have different definitions, such as the 2006 World Cup or the 2008 Olympics. Different people also have different understandings, such as reading books or exercising. As long as one loves life, fashion is everywhere. To win high scores, candidates should delve deeply into the meaning of "fashion," paying attention to rational reflections related to social hotspots, humanistic care, or historical trends, avoiding trivialities like food, clothing, shelter, and mass culture, and certainly not promoting vulgar culture or negative phenomena.

10. Anhui Volume 2009 College Entrance Examination Essay Topic

Read the following text and write an essay of no less than 800 words according to the requirements. (60 points)

Overtaking on a curve is originally a term in racing, referring to overtaking others while navigating a curve. Curves are something every driver faces. Compared to straight roads, curves are more challenging. While navigating a curve, those who were originally behind may overtake, and those leading may be overtaken. Now, "overtaking on a curve" has taken on a new meaning, widely applied in fields like finance and politics. Curves have also gained special significance, referring to moments filled with difficulties yet containing opportunities and challenges. As long as one seizes this moment well, they can surpass opponents and oneself.

Requirements: Choose an angle to conceptualize the essay, independently determine the theme, genre, and title; do not deviate from the material's content and meaning when writing, avoid rote responses, and no plagiarism.

Review: It is evident that the purpose of this material is to guide candidates to reflect on those "moments filled with difficulties yet containing opportunities and challenges" in life and express their genuine feelings. Candidates must first understand the original meaning of "overtaking on a curve." Afterward, they should focus on the extended meaning of "curve," i.e., "moments filled with difficulties yet containing opportunities and challenges." Only after mastering these two steps can one truly comprehend the last sentence of the material—"Seizing this moment well enables surpassing opponents and oneself." What this "moment" refers to is key to writing.
