Rich and handsome men, come to Taobao to bid for planes. The highest starting bid price is 16.8 million yuan.

by anonymous on 2013-09-11 14:38:56

Since September 4, Taobao's official WeChat account has been releasing mysterious hints, saying that "an unprecedented new species is about to arrive." Subsequently, three clue images were released - an elephant weighing two tons, a giraffe standing at a height of 14.97 meters, and a fierce tiger consuming 348 liters in one meal. However, yesterday the mystery was finally revealed: the new species wasn't any animal at all, but rather airplanes. Starting at 10 AM tomorrow, five brand-new small aircraft will be auctioned on Taobao, along with one second-hand helicopter being sold at a fixed price starting from 1 yuan.

Six small aircraft will be auctioned starting from as low as 1 yuan, with the highest base price reaching 16.8 million yuan.

According to reports, the aircraft models participating in the Taobao auction include three European and American-made helicopters such as the "Hummingbird" and "Thunderbird," as well as two light sport aircraft certified by the Aviation Safety Bureau. Depending on their origin and function, the base prices for the aircraft auctions range from 1.05 million yuan to 16.8 million yuan, with the most expensive being a "Hummingbird" Eurocopter EC-120 helicopter. Additionally, there is a Jabiru J-160 light aircraft developed in Australia, which will be auctioned off starting at 1 yuan RMB, with the auction ending at 10 AM on September 16.

Why did they think of auctioning airplanes? "Big items, high-end! We want to make airplane transactions e-commerce accessible." A relevant person in charge of Taobao Auctions said to reporters, "In foreign countries, online airplane transactions have already become very common, while in our country, airplane transactions have only been accessible to niche consumers. Taobao hopes to conduct a trial run for the e-commerce and informatization of airplane transactions, while simultaneously popularizing knowledge and sales information about private airplanes among more ordinary people."

According to data provided by the company Feistone, from November 2009 to February 2011, China registered a total of 28 private aircraft, with a total selling price exceeding 1.1 billion US dollars. To date, including Hong Kong and Macau regions, the number of private aircraft owned in China has reached approximately 200, with Guangdong being the province with the most private aircraft.

If you successfully bid on an airplane, you need to go to Beijing to collect it, and to test fly it, you must possess a pilot's license.

Airplanes are quite expensive, so for most people, buying airplanes online is just something to watch idly. However, this time, the omnipotent Taobao set a precedent by introducing a new button in the auction - "pretend to buy an airplane." Currently, the formal airplane auction hasn't started yet, but the "pretend to buy an airplane" game has already begun.

Yesterday, when I logged into "The Omnipotent Taobao," the pages of six private aircraft immediately appeared, with their models and starting auction prices clearly visible. Next to the "about to start auction" button, there was a "pretend to buy an airplane" option. After clicking it, a Weibo post would automatically be sent out, indicating that I had "bought" the airplane, of course, it was just a joke.

It is understood that these six aircraft all come from Beijing Hangqu Trading Co., Ltd., which originally provided one-stop services for purchasing private aircraft. Relevant personnel from the company introduced that whether you buy them to fly or keep them as a display piece, you can participate in this aircraft auction. Once the auction is successful, the buyer needs to personally inspect the aircraft in Beijing, and if they wish to conduct a test flight, they must be someone who has already obtained a private pilot's license.
