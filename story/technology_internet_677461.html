When the object to be weighed or a loaded vehicle is placed on the platform of the load cell, under the action of gravity, the load cell transmits the weight to the weighing sensor, causing deformation of the elastic body of the sensor. The strain gauge bridge attached to the elastic body loses balance, outputting an electrical signal proportional to the weight value, which is amplified by a linear amplifier. Then, after being converted by an A/D converter into a digital signal, the microprocessor (CPU) of the instrument processes the weight signal and directly displays the weight data. If configured with a printer, it can print and record the weighing data. If configured with a computer, the weighing data can be input into a computer management system for comprehensive management. Whether it's a scale or a weighbridge, the basic principle is to use a sensor (force sensor) to convert the weight of the object into an electrical signal, which is sent to the conditioning circuit, A/D converter, and computational circuit (MCU), then converted back into a digital display.
