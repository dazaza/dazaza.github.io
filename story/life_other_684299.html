First Love

Many years ago, at the age when one's heart first opens to love. In the countryside, in summer, there was my brother, Shun'er, and us.

During winter and summer vacations, my brother would come back and we would be with Shun'er.

My brother lived in the city and only came back for a while during the holidays. Grandma loved him very much, and we also liked our brother. My brother was the son of my uncle, good at studying, and enjoyed playing with the village children, so he was very popular. He was obedient and well-liked by adults. My brother was also handsome, an excellent boy.

That summer, we graduated from elementary school, at the age of blooming youth, the age when one's heart first opens to love. At thirteen or fourteen, it was a beautiful, simple longing, a kind of infatuated and hazy intoxication with what is called "liking" someone. Liking was such a vague yet clear matter.

That summer, my brother came back wearing shorts, a white shirt, and white shoes, looking very clean. With a bright smile, though he had just graduated from junior high school, he still had a kind of innocent childlike charm.

Shun'er was our age but started school a year later than us. She was more mature than us, you could say she knew more and was bolder than us. She was from the opposite village, her grandmother lived in our village, so she often came over and we grew up as childhood friends.

Shun'er then had long hair, average looks. Her mother's reputation wasn't good; the adults said her mother was flirtatious. Maybe because of her mother, she also gave people a flirtatious impression. We looked at her that way back then. After all these years have passed, we've all crossed the threshold of twenty, and when we look back, we feel she wasn't really like that person.

On that day, Shun'er came to my house and saw my brother, such a clean-cut boy, then she left. Later, my brother went out, and she found me and said, "Your brother...hehe..."

At that time, I didn't notice anything, I was just proud of my brother, who was so likable, and I felt happy.

We and Shun'er lived our days back and forth, and I didn't know what was happening on the other side of Shun'er's heart, where feelings were stirring.

Shun'er always talked about my brother.

Afterward, Shun'er liked coming to our house.

"Your brother is very handsome."

When I heard Shun'er say this sentence, I suddenly became excited, but at the same time, there was a hint of displeasure because I didn't like her character. It was then that I realized the reason why Shun'er came to our house so frequently, but honestly, I wasn't quite sure. I was at that age, knowing that Shun'er liked my brother made me very excited, I don't know why, maybe it was a reaction typical of that age, that unique sensitivity and gossip.

"Your brother always likes wearing shorts."

"Does your brother go fishing with them?"

"When will your brother go back?"

"What is your brother's home phone number..."

I kept teasing her at that time, she wouldn't let me laugh, she hit me, lightly hitting me. Then she gave us those heart-shaped paper clips folded in seven colors, which we all treasured because we couldn't afford to buy them, and they were very popular at that time. Naturally, we were excited as if we had gotten a treasure. Because of these heart-shaped paper clips, we told Shun'er many things about my brother.

Shun'er listened to us talk, smiling sweetly. Using today's feelings to describe it, it was a sweet smile of being in love.

Shun'er gave my brother a love letter.

On a hot summer afternoon, my brother went fishing with the villagers.

The pond water was a dull green, a gentle breeze swaying the wild grass, then lazily disappearing.

Shang Chang walked up to my brother and handed over a letter. My brother looked up at Shang Chang. Shang Chang was also our age, and I believe my brother must have thought this letter was from Shang Chang.

The envelope was pink, a classic color for love letters, naturally belonging to the color of affection.

My brother knew it was a love letter.

After bringing it back, my brother didn't read it. He lay in bed, fanning himself.

My sister and I climbed onto the bed, picked up the love letter, and asked my brother why he didn't read it, laughing there. I was even more excited, tore open the letter, and read the love letter together with my sister... I don't remember the contents anymore.

The only thing I remember is that my brother remained silent, only giving us his back. Even now, many years later, I still don't know the meaning of that back or the reason for his silence.

The news of the love letter spread quickly, and the whole village knew. The adults laughed ambiguously, and we laughed openly. Shun'er started not daring to come to our village much.

My brother seemed to have an expression of avoidance.

Was this my brother's first love letter?

Yet we continued to discuss it enthusiastically. This summer had a subtle factor spreading within us. And what about Shun'er and my brother? Perhaps there was a difference, one from the perspective of an observer, and one from the perspective of a participant. As the saying goes, "Those involved are confused," perhaps it was like that.

My brother's reply

About a week later, I heard from my younger brother that my brother had replied to Shun'er.

In the empty space behind the old house, my younger brother gave Shun'er my brother's reply. I read the content of that letter. It basically said not to be self-absorbed, but the reply was very cold and decisive.

My brother was present at that time, holding Shun'er's love letter in his hand. Shun'er looked at him, and suddenly my brother tore up the letter, tearing it into pieces. Those pink paper pieces, along with the melancholy words written on them, silently fell into that little stream, drifting away with the water, soundlessly quiet.

Shun'er's expression blurred. A lazy wind blew, slightly disheveling Shun'er's hair.

We were stunned. We didn't expect my brother...

Suddenly, there was another sound of paper being torn.

Shun'er fiercely tore my brother's reply, her world blurring. Paper scraps like snowflakes fluttered around her, falling, floating in the air, silently soundless, such a sorrowful background...

We all stared blankly, unable to say a word...

The second "love letter"

Days passed slowly in the lazy summer breeze and the whirring of fans, carrying those ambiguous factors away.

My brother was going back to the city.

I told Shun'er this news.

On the rooftop of Aunt Er's house, Shun'er told me: "I just like your brother."

At that time, I looked down on her. I was also very confused, really, that day both of them had torn up the letters, and she still told me she liked my brother.

I thought she was beyond saving.

Shun'er gave me another paperclip, then shoved some things into my hands, telling me to put them in my brother's bag.

After I went home, I took them out, my goodness!

It turned out to be a pack of heart-shaped paperclips, and there were...

I secretly stuffed those things into my brother's bag, and after finishing, I sighed in relief, not knowing why.

The next day, my brother left.

The secret in the bag, only I knew.

Is that all?

A few months later, I went to my brother's house in the city and secretly asked him if he had found anything in the bag?

My brother casually said, "I saw a piece of rag."

My eyes lit up, "Where is it?"

"It was just a rag, I threw it away on the bus..."

What? Really? Didn't you see the words on the rag?

"No."

"Ah, never mind."

I expected such an ending. Actually, I did give my brother's phone number to Shun'er, but apparently, she didn't call my brother.

Actually, that piece of cloth embroidered nine words:

"I like you, little man."

Back then, seeing this sentence made me feel nauseous, and even now, I don't like this sentence.

Such words should not come from a young girl. I think so. That's why I don't like it.

That piece of cloth and those words are probably lost somewhere by now, and even this matter might have been forgotten by some people.

The end is like this. Afterwards, Shun'er never spoke to my brother again.
