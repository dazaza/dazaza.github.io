Three types of people are not suitable for liposuction

by 865512 on 2008-12-22 10:27:49

"Weight loss" is an eternal topic for women, and the methods of weight loss are endless. Liposuction for weight loss is a relatively advanced method. Relevant experts point out that three groups of people should not undergo liposuction for weight loss.

According to Professor Tan Xindong, an expert in plastic surgery at the Guangdong Armed Police Hospital, the three groups of people who should not undergo liposuction weight loss surgery are: 1) obese people suffering from heart disease, high blood pressure, diabetes, and blood function disorders;

2) Those with pathological obesity caused by excessive endocrine or certain diseases;

3) Children under the age of 18, whose physical development is not yet complete and whose number of fat cells has not stabilized. If liposuction is blindly performed, it will not only easily rebound but also disrupt the growth cycle of children, endangering their health.

It is understood that when traditional weight loss methods such as exercise therapy, drug therapy, and starvation therapy are not very effective, French cosmetic doctors proposed "liposuction for weight loss" in the early 1980s. Currently, this weight loss method is popular in European and American countries due to its special efficacy. In recent years, this technology has also been introduced into our country. Liposuction for weight loss, with its advantages of short operation time, painlessness, quick results, and low rebound rate, is gradually becoming a new favorite among the obese. However, many people, eager to reduce fat and knowing little about liposuction for weight loss, casually choose a beauty salon for liposuction surgery. As a result, they not only fail to achieve the expected results but also experience uneven surgical wounds, skin necrosis, and infection complications, which harm their health. Professor Tan said that the quality of liposuction weight loss surgery depends on the equipment, the technical skills of the operators, and the balance of the amount of fat sucked during the process. Experts suggest that consumers carefully consider the hardware technology and personnel quality of the institution before undergoing liposuction weight loss surgery; otherwise, blindly undergoing liposuction surgery is indeed dangerous.

Professor Tan particularly reminds consumers that human fat exists between the skin and muscles. Overemphasizing the slimming effect and excessively or blindly sucking out fat is detrimental to the body. Liposuction weight loss surgery is limited to the subcutaneous enlarged cell fat layer. In fact, reducing the fat to one-fifth of the original amount can achieve the slimming effect. Before liposuction, doctors usually perform an ultrasound scan on the person to clearly display the thickness of the fat layer on the screen, thereby determining whether liposuction is feasible. Generally speaking, if the fat layer is within 2 centimeters, there is no need and it cannot be subjected to liposuction. If it is 3.5-4.5 centimeters, then surgery is necessary.
