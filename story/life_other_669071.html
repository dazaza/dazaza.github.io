Untouchable Tearful Eyes

I cried because you said: it's over.

Our love has always been wrapped up under the computer blue screen, unfathomable.

We can't, like ordinary couples, hold hands and go for a walk; we can't, like ordinary couples, embrace each other deep into the night until dawn; we can't be the first to see each other's smile when we're happy; we can't be the first to wipe away each other's tears when we're sad; we can't eat together, kiss together, shop together, or watch the movies I like. We have so many things we can't do, but at first, we were still determined to be together, swearing to love each other forever.

I started getting used to not shutting down my computer at night.

I started getting used to hearing the special ringtone that belongs to you,

I started getting used to being invisible every day just to talk to you.

I started getting used to hearing you say "goodnight" every night.

We didn't talk about the future because when I said I wanted to travel with you, you were silent, so I became afraid. I didn't dare to think about what would happen later. During that time, I just cherished every day, every hour, every minute, every second, carefully yet happily. That feeling was dangerous, but it was like an addiction, impossible to quit. At first, I thought online dating was not much different from normal dating except for the fact that we couldn't meet and touch each other. Later, I realized that the problem wasn't that simple, as a relationship cannot be maintained permanently by just a computer. So when you told me: I have someone else now, I had a moment of blankness, then a moment of heartache, very bitter and helpless. I couldn't grab your clothes and curse you out, I couldn't slap you and say it doesn't matter, I couldn't use a pitiful look to make you change your mind, I couldn't beg you tearfully not to leave me. Later, I thought, maybe that was the predetermined ending from the start.

I started getting used to shutting down my computer at night.

I started getting used to not hearing the special ringtone that belongs to you.

I started getting used to going online every day but no longer talking to you.

I started getting used to not waiting for you to say "goodnight" to me every night.

You said I shouldn't chat with other strangers, and I agreed. You said I should think about you every day, and I did. You said I should only love you, and I only loved you. But later, you said, this is where we stop, I'm leaving, you be happy. That was the most heart-wrenching thing I've ever heard, okay, I'll let you go.

I cried, many times, incessantly, like drizzling rain that never ends. I know you won't care anymore, but I can't help it.

My eyes hurt so much, you can't see it, nor can you understand why I am so devoted.

Enough, I wish you happiness.
