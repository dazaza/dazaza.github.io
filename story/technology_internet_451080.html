Waiting for the start of the finals, at 7:30 pm on the 15th at Guangdong Hongyuan's home court. The tournament adopted an unreasonable 1-2-2-2 format for a best-of-seven series. For me as a super fan, CCTV5's live broadcast became the core work during that time period. From the start of the entire league's regular season, I had my eyes on the Xinjiang team. It was not because they spent heavily to bring in the best foreign player in the CBA so far (referring to a 10 million RMB salary much higher than other teams' foreign players), but rather the spirit of the Xinjiang team that has made it to the finals for two consecutive years, and their positive teamwork and progressive momentum! Plus, coach Jiang Xingquan's confidence, you can't help but believe, whether you admit it or not, just like in the Euroleague!

I also analyzed the situation of the Hongyuan team. Foreign player Park played for Guangdong team over the past two seasons and was an absolute key contributor to Guangdong's two consecutive championships. Over the past two seasons, he averaged 17 points, 5 assists, and 5 rebounds per game, becoming an indispensable player at the point guard position for Guangdong. Park's departure was a heavy blow to Guangdong's backcourt. This season's recruited foreign players Jones and Hadson were not as effective as Park. The interior was even more of a major shortcoming for Hongyuan this season. The foreign players were not ideal, relying on domestic players Su Wei and Wang Zheng, who I didn't rate highly. Su Wei has some strength, but his fundamentals are terrible; he seems unable to play basketball, only knowing how to fight hard, which is what national team coach Howard saw in him. Wang Zheng is still learning and improving, not yet ready for big responsibilities. Although young players Zhou Peng, Chen Jianghua, and Dong Hanlin have grown up, veterans Du Feng and Zhu Fangyu's skills have declined. Overall momentum appears slightly insufficient. But I never gave up on Hongyuan; their regular season results were not ideal, instead, Dongguan surpassed them in many ways. At the time, I predicted that the finals would still be Hongyuan's affair, supported by experience and confidence!

Given such obvious strengths, on-court performance and adaptability, Basketball Master Jiang Xingquan is superior to Hongyuan's head coach Li Chunjiang, making Xinjiang's chances of winning greater!

The first match was the most critical, and as expected, Xinjiang won the first victory away with a score of 100:92. Quincy Douby scored a personal 53 points, creating a historical record for single-game scoring in the CBA! A thrilling and exhilarating performance!

I wanted to write about this game in detail, but accidentally saw a post from my younger son Qian Yuhe, who is studying at Beijing Sport University's graduate school, and he had already written a piece expressing similar sentiments. His insights and viewpoints were even higher than mine, offering broader perspectives and ideas, so I spared myself the effort. I now transcribe it here for shared understanding.

An "unexpected" final (April 16, 2011, 00:10:21) The CBA Finals between Xinjiang and Guangdong commenced tonight as scheduled. Hoping that Xinjiang, led by figures like Batel, Douby, and Zhang Qingpeng, along with local Xinjiang warriors, could overthrow the Guangdong Dongguan Bank dynasty, reach the pinnacle of the CBA, and achieve the grand cause of a Xinjiang dynasty. As a supporter of the Xinjiang team, I am fortunate because my favorite team won the away game without much trouble; as a viewer of the first game, I am lucky because I witnessed the god-like performance of Quincy Douby. This game had only one theme: God-like Quincy Douby taking on the Guangdong team alone.

Looking at the whole game, whether it was the mutual probing in the first quarter or the fluctuations in the second quarter, it all seemed like a calm prelude before a great show. The real climax of the game began in the second half. After being restricted by Guangdong for two quarters, Quincy Douby finally erupted. His nearly unreasonable three-point shots from beyond the arc, like heavy bombs, shattered Guangdong's strict defense fortress and destroyed the confidence of Guangdong's players repeatedly regained. Guangdong's double foreign players, Hadson and Haislip, attempted to replicate (dare not use the word surpass) Quincy Douby's magic, but their three-point attempts failed every time, losing multiple opportunities to catch up. Ultimately, they had to admit that Quincy Douby was possessed tonight. In the entire game, Quincy Douby played for 48 minutes, shooting 6 out of 14 two-point attempts, 10 out of 17 three-point attempts, and making 11 out of 12 free throws, totaling 53 points. Not only did he refresh his own career-high record of 51 points, but he also broke the previous record of 49 points in a single finals game held by Liu Yudong of Bayi.

The first game of the finals ended, Xinjiang's players celebrated their victory and triumphantly returned to Xinjiang, waiting for the opponent to come back for revenge; Guangdong's players retreated with disappointment, determined to seek revenge for this loss. When both sides' excited emotions gradually calmed down and their nerves settled, I also calmed my initially blind admiration for Quincy Douby and rationally re-examined this extraordinary game.

In the first half of the game, the strength of both sides was almost evenly matched. The strategic battles between the two veteran coaches kept the half-time score difference within five points. Following this trend into the second half, the entire game could likely have been a tightly contested tug-of-war. Each team needed its five players on the field to focus and cooperate sincerely, strictly implementing their respective coaches' tactical instructions based on the lineup arrangements. However, Douby, as Xinjiang's absolute scoring mainstay, was akin to Kobe Bryant of the Lakers. Once the situation on the field couldn't be opened up, he relied on his personal ability to deliver the decisive strike in football terms. Douby was truly divine today, shooting three-pointers like crazy. But apart from proving Douby's extraordinary personal ability and that he fought alone like a possessed soul tonight, does it explain any other issues beneficial to the team? Did Coach Jiang's tactics and team strategy come through? Were the other players' experiences gained on the precious finals stage adequate?

Nowadays, in our CBA league, the level of foreign players often directly determines a team's overall level. Tonight, more than half of Xinjiang's total score of 100 points came from their foreign player Quincy Douby, leaving many domestic originally brilliant star players relegated to supporting roles in the game. We can't help but ask: How much room for improvement do our domestic players have? After being infinitely overshadowed by foreign players, regaining confidence will be the biggest challenge facing these national players. Imagine, if our CBA league had no foreign players, how awkward and bleak would its level be?

We can only say that this was an "unexpected" final. We must admit, Quincy Douby was truly divine tonight. But this game only explains that anything is possible in basketball games. Beyond that, the 100-92 score, with Xinjiang taking the lead, is merely a result and doesn't explain any problems. What Guangdong needs to do next: Mentally, don't lose heart or become discouraged, maintain high spirits, and aim to defend their title; Tactically, develop a comprehensive defensive strategy to curb Quincy Douby's outside shooting, as Sun Wei Ping suggested, assign someone to closely shadow Quincy Douby throughout the game, preventing him from receiving the ball, or if necessary, just hold onto him! And what Xinjiang needs to do: Mentally, avoid arrogance and complacency, realizing this was an "unexpected" game, winning this game is just the first step of a long journey, the revolution is not yet complete, comrades still need to strive, just like in the Euroleague; Tactically, develop N sets of regular strategies once Quincy Douby returns from divinity to humanity, showcasing the team's characteristics, true strength, and level.

Finally, Xinjiang must win!
