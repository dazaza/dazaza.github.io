Number (N)ine

Number (N)ine.jpg (6.18 KB)

There are very few Japanese streetwear brands that have successfully transitioned from street fashion to high fashion. One of the rare examples is Under Cover, which entered the European market with its "dissolving" clothing, followed by Number (N)ine, which stormed New York with its "decayed" style. Both have become must-haves for fashionistas. Recently, Number (N)ine officially landed in Hong Kong, showcasing its Military Style, and is expected to be the top target for fashion enthusiasts during the seasonal change.

Takahiro Miyashita, the designer of Number (N)ine, has always been extremely low-key but was already regarded by professionals in the Japanese fashion industry as a brand destined for greatness. Since its establishment in 1996, Number (N)ine has achieved remarkable success and opened its first overseas boutique in New York on August 9, 2003, becoming a legendary tale.

Abercrombie & Fitch

Abercrombie & Fitch, the number one American brand that sparked a global fashion craze, features jackets specially designed for women, made from cotton fabric that is breathable and comfortable. The perfect fit creates an alluring silhouette. The material feels comfortable and is processed with exclusive technology to prevent deformation, eliminating special washing concerns and saving time when organizing clothes, making it ideal for modern women with classic styles.

FRED PERRY

FRED PERRY.jpg (2.51 KB)

Recently gaining attention in Hong Kong, Taiwan, and Japan, FRED PERRY’s renowned polo shirts have also become a favorite of singer Jordan Chan. The classic laurel wreath logo is inspired by the Wimbledon Tennis Championships, as FRED PERRY was the first male seeded player to win the championship and repeatedly reclaimed the title. Thus, FRED PERRY became synonymous with professional tennis, especially favored by the British. In Japan, it is quite popular, often leading to sell-outs!

Y-3 and Yohji Yamamoto

Y-3 and Yohji Yamamoto.gif (3.13 KB)

Yohji Yamamoto, one of the most important and influential Japanese designers in today's fashion world, was born in 1943. His father passed away during World War II. Yohji was raised by his mother, who was a tailor. Influenced by his mother, Yohji developed a passion for fashion design and graduated from BUNKA FASHION COLLEGE (where he studied alongside Nigo, the creator of APE, and Takahiro Miyashita). He later established his own brand...

His designs and cuts are full of wisdom, creativity, and difficulty, transcending time and cultural boundaries, redefining clothing concepts with a neutral aesthetic and exaggerated proportions. His works are eternal and classic! The Y-3 series, his collaboration with ADIDAS, is one of the most anticipated CROSSOVER works in recent years!

Neighborhood

Neighborhood originated in 1992, originally named Neighborhood? NYC (NEW YORK CITY), initially sold at UNION in SOHO. Due to the designer being American, the design style back then leaned towards military patterns and simple graffiti. Although Neighborhood did not become a top-tier brand in the U.S., since its inception, it has infiltrated the entire Asian market, particularly Japan. According to UFO, the owner of the Hong Kong streetwear boutique UNISEX, the situation is somewhat similar to SOCIAL STUDIES from the U.S. In recent years, TRANSACTION handed over the copyright and design to the Japanese, so there is also a touch of Japanese design in the graphics, and even the simple font design reveals their dedication and characteristics.

loverhouse

Hectic

BEAMS BOY

Under BEAMS, which has over twenty years of history in Japan, BEAMS BOY is widely loved for its unique design, representing the fashion of Japanese teenage girls.

Founded in 1976, BEAMS has nearly thirty years of history, and its works have consistently led and influenced Japan's fashion trends. As for BEAMS BOY, the subsidiary brand focusing on cute teenage girl apparel, it opened its first independent boutique in Shibuya, Tokyo, in April 1998 and now has twenty-four sales points across Japan.

PAUL FRANK

PAUL FRANK.gif (7.18 KB)

Seven years ago, designer Paul Frank sat at the sewing machine using red sterile artificial leather to create a big-mouthed monkey named Julius. Since then, this seemingly expressionless silly monkey has appeared on pajamas and wallets. Initially on a small scale, now? It's truly an incredible "monkey business"! A trendy accessories company generating millions in sales. The image of Julius the big-mouthed monkey strongly appeals to the younger generation and is highly popular worldwide. Featured in movies like "Austin Powers: The Spy Who Shagged Me" (Parts 2-3), "American Pie" (Parts 1-2), "Daddy Day Care," "Miss Congeniality." On TV shows like MTV, E!, Dharma and Greg, Mad TV, Ally McBeal. Thematic fashion magazines like Seventeen, Vogue, InStyle, Glamour, W can also reveal its presence.

Paul Frank does these things exceptionally well! However, what makes them look so special is that they are made from a material rarely seen nowadays - Vinyl. Vinyl is as durable as leather and isn't derived from animals; moreover, it looks better than leather! This is a perfect gift for Paul's original design concept. Besides accessories, in 1999, the company expanded into sportswear, themed around youth, developing a series of athletic wear with prominent patterns. Recently, it added underwear, swimwear, sleepwear, furniture, and sunglasses.

UNDERCOVER

UNDERCOVER.jpg (3.57 KB)

UNDERCOVER designer Takahiro Miyashita was born in Japan in 1969 and developed a deep interest in fashion design while still in school. After studying fashion design at Bunka Fashion College in 1989, he worked at a fashion store in Shibuya until 1993 when he co-founded NOWHERE boutique with NIGO from A BATHING APE in Harajuku, immediately skyrocketing his fame!

VISVIM

VISVIM's rapid rise is directly related to Hiroshi Fujiwara, the "Godfather of Japanese Street Fashion," personally showcasing its products in various settings. Whatever Hiroshi Fujiwara wears becomes something coveted by trendsetters—a rule that has been set. Whether it's the FBT or the SKYNARD RING MOCCASIN released in collaboration with FRAGMENT, both have received support from this "Godfather"; hence, VISVIM smoothly rose to prominence and became a renowned streetwear brand.

In addition to various shoe models, VISVIM's current apparel series incorporates WORKER STYLE with OUTDOOR elements, including windbreakers made from GORE-TEX and washed denim jeans based on the 501 model, all of which are highly anticipated works.

STUSSY: Trendy and Classic

In 1980, 25 years ago, SHAWN STUSSY began selling surfwear and accessories on Laguna Beach in California. He probably wouldn't have foreseen that his name would be idolized by countless people and even worshipped in the future.

Today, STUSSY's style is more diverse, ranging from surfing to skateboarding, from fashion to streetwear, and even from REGGAE (reggae music) to HIP HOP. In any form of style, STUSSY stands out uniquely. Regardless of how the direction of trends changes, STUSSY's position remains unshakable.

25 years have passed, and STUSSY continues to stand at the forefront of trends. Trends are like rebels, rebelling against classics. But after a long period, trends transform into new classics. STUSSY has created such a classic within trends through each of its products, spending a quarter of a century doing so. As STUSSY celebrates its 25th anniversary with a series of commemorative events, let us pause, reflect, and pay our deepest respects.

Initially, Stussy's design elements revolved around surfing, later evolving towards X-Games and other diversified designs. Stussy's design elements mostly include skulls, crowns, Jamaica, black culture, Old Skool, and other collage elements.

Stussy is not very popular in the U.S., but due to the promotion by the Japanese, it dominates the Asian Street Wear market, and for this reason, its current design style resembles many Japanese brands!

supreme

supreme.gif (1.48 KB)

The success of SUPREME, or the success of American streetwear, cannot go without mentioning JAMES JEBBIA. When he was in his twenties, he came to New York to pursue his lifelong passion for streetwear. Initially, he assisted in the development of street brands like UNION and STUSSY, earning much acclaim.

In 1994, inspired by skateboarding and HIP HOP cultures, JAMES JEBBIA opened the first fashion store focused on skateboarding called SUPREME, actively developing functionality and diversity, transforming SUPREME from a single skateboarding brand into one of the top street fashion brands in New York and even the entire U.S.

In 1997, it was introduced to Japan through collaborations with Harajuku brands, quickly becoming a sensation in Japan. Since then, SUPREME has ascended to the ranks of world-leading streetwear brands.

evisu

evisu.gif (8.42 KB)

On September 23, 2007,

In 1991, HIDEHIKO YAMANE founded the vintage-style denim brand EVISU in Osaka. Coinciding with the peak of VINTAGE JEANS, EVISU became a representative of the "post-501 era," combining authentic vintage denim elements with fashionable street style, rapidly gaining fame and becoming one of Japan's top denim brands. Prominent figures like CRAIG DAVID, David Beckham, and ALL SAINTS have been frequently spotted wearing EVISU garments in media coverage. YAMANE's designs not only remain faithful to the original vintage styles but also brim with humor and subversive spirit. Regardless of how successful the sales may be, EVISU maintains a minimal production volume and emphasizes a significant amount of handcrafted details. Purchasing EVISU gradually took on a collecting significance among VINTAGE JEANS enthusiasts.

In late 2003, HIDEHIKO YAMANE gave an interview to "City Magazine," recalling the creation of vintage-style denim: "The denim pants we made back then were certainly not as perfect as those we can achieve now, but the entire process was filled with fun. Actually, many of the elements we see today stemmed from casual ideas back then—like the seagull-shaped logo, which I drew on the back pocket after completing the entire process, feeling it lacked something. It was almost like random doodling. Initially, every seagull mark on each pair of pants was drawn by me... I became obsessed with vintage denim pants and every detail about them, such as fabric texture, roll labels, back pocket shapes, rivets, stitching patterns... I was very interested in all the techniques related to making vintage denim pants. However, these techniques date back 40 years, and it seems no one before me cared about them, so it indeed took some time to recover them..."

EVISU pants are super expensive in Japan. In 1991, a Japanese man named HIDENHIKO YAMANE bought a Levi's denim loom, used it to weave some denim fabric, and handmade a pair of jeans. The entire design and production adopted antique denim-making techniques, with an M-shaped seagull emblem on the back pocket, filled with humor and street vibe. These jeans, named Evisu, soon became a hot pursuit among the youth. A street-style pair of jeans might seem unrelated to luxury items, but an Evisu NO.1 costs 3000 yuan, and limited editions go for over 5000 yuan. Listen to the luxurious declarations of those chasing it: "If you have a great figure, you'd want to walk around in a white T-shirt and Evisu jeans, paired with a Rolex watch. You might even get a nickname like 'Evisu Handsome,' how carefree and stylish!" This is the attitude of young people towards luxury, unrelated to prestigious status, aiming to subvert the solemn sense of luxury.

Currently, the hottest-selling item is the top Japanese denim brand Evisu (several thousand yuan per pair). This is a Japanese brand deeply adored by Japanese youth, even captivating David Beckham, who once purchased a pair of Evisu jeans adorned with 18-carat gold buttons and golden stitching for 25,000 pounds.
