WeChat's Future Game Growth Prospects Seen from Kakao's Estimated Game Revenues

by geekzhang on 2013-05-08 12:39:28

Let's take a look at KakaoTalk's latest data: as of now, the number of registered users has been refreshed to 86 million, with 70% of users being from South Korea. The number of daily active users has reached 29 million, and the daily message volume is 4.8 billion.

KakaoTalk was officially launched in March 2010. The table below lists KakaoTalk's user growth journey.

Before launching KakaoTalk, the company Kakao had already been established and operated for three years. In April this year, at the BDashCamp industry conference held in Japan, Sirgoo Lee, co-CEO of Kakao, disclosed some operational data of Kakao on-site. Through these revenue and profit data, it helps us understand Kakao's business logic. This publicized data may be the most complete disclosure of operational data so far. Therefore, this article also attempts to interpret these data to quantitatively describe Kakao's current and future operating strategies, and then make some conjectures about WeChat in China based on these data.

Rapid Growth, Achieving Profitability

First, let's take a look at the revenue and profit chart from 2009 to 2012:

From the chart, we can see that from 2009 to 2012, the total expenditure over four years reached approximately $543 million. If calculated based on 70 million users by December 2012, the average acquisition cost per user is $1.3. Moreover, from 2009 to 2011, due to the sharp increase in KakaoTalk users, the losses for the three years increased year by year. From 2009 to 2010, the loss doubled, and from 2010 to 2011, the loss quadrupled. In April 2012, with cash infusions from Tencent and the Korean online game company Webzen, they completed their continuous market expansion.

In September 2012, with the launch of the Kakao game platform, its revenue situation changed significantly within a short period. By the end of 2012, according to the latest disclosed data, the entire fiscal year turned from a loss to a profit, achieving a profit of $6.5 million. Looking at the revenue, it grew from $1.6 million in 2011 to $45 million for the whole year of 2012, completing nearly a 30-fold skyrocketing growth. And the main contributor behind this was the launch of the game platform.

Profit Structure Breakdown

To analyze the role of the game platform, let's first look at the profit structure table disclosed by Kakao (unit: million USD).

From the above, we can see that games account for more than half of the revenue structure, making up over 60% of total revenue. Considering that KakaoTalk's game platform was only launched in September 2012, and by the end of 2012, it took only four months to achieve such a growth rate and turn from a loss to a profit, once again verifying the powerful money-making ability of games.

Since the performance of games is so outstanding, let's take a closer look at the profit composition of the platform category to better understand the source of its growth profits. The platform's profits mainly come from two parts: one is the currency trading in games, and the other is the sale of emoticons and virtual items. Since the disclosed data does not directly provide the revenue ratio of these two parts, but through last year's performance and user scale, we can make some speculations to estimate the ratio of these two.

Because there was no game business in 2011, we can attribute the $1.33 million income to the sales of emoticons and virtual items. Combining the user numbers from 2011 and 2012, we can estimate the income of this item for 2012 proportionally. Therefore, in 2012's platform revenue, the two business incomes were: $25.2 million from games and $3.07 million from virtual items.

From the analysis of the revenue structure of the game platform, combining the chat app with such a business model shows extremely strong monetization capabilities. Based on a user base of 70 million, it took only three months to grow from zero to a revenue scale of $7 million. However, from the estimated data for November and December, it seems that the growth scale of game revenue has encountered certain bottlenecks. The revenue from October to December basically remained at around $7 million, and the growth trend hit a bottleneck.

Analyzing the reasons for the bottleneck, they are mainly related to the total number of users and the limited capacity of the platform for the number of games. The number of game users depends on the scale of the total number of users, and currently, Kakao's user base has grown to 80 million users. As the user base continues to expand, the growth speed will slow down.

The second factor is that the number of games that the KakaoTalk platform can carry simultaneously also has a limit. Since it cannot expand like a PC in terms of quantity, it needs to consider increasing the ARPU value in limited resource positions. It can be foreseen that the frequency of game changes on KakaoTalk will be greatly accelerated in the future. This business model may regularly contribute a considerable amount of cash flow each month, but its contribution to growth will soon reach its limit. Kakao is expected to explore more business models in the future.

Kakao's Future Development

Based on Kakao's profit model in 2011 and 2012, we attempt to predict Kakao's future operational data. From the current revenue sources, the largest proportions come from games and advertising revenue from public accounts. The growth rates of these two revenues will directly determine Kakao's success in commercialization.

Firstly, overall revenue fundamentally still depends on user growth. Currently, the domestic market in South Korea is saturated. In the process of expanding outward, each major battlefield faces intense competition from Line, WeChat, WhatsApp, and Facebook. Whether Kakao's user base can continue to grow rapidly remains to be proven over time.

Secondly, the game business relies on the user base effect. If the user base cannot develop on a large scale, then the growth potential of this business model will quickly reach its limit. According to our previous calculations, the lack of subsequent growth potential has already been revealed in the last two months of 2012. Besides focusing on quantity, improving ARPU values will also be an important consideration. Therefore, in the coming time, games need to capture market attention in a shorter period, leading to increasingly fierce competition, and the time to be replaced will be compressed even further.

Thirdly, regarding the advertising business, currently Kakao has developed 200 PlusFriend public accounts, generating an average monthly income of $1 million. The future growth of this part is relatively easy to estimate, mainly based on the future number of public accounts for speculation.

Tencent and WeChat

At the beginning of the article, it was mentioned that Tencent invested in Kakao in 2012, investing 403 million RMB to acquire 13.84% equity. In other words, Tencent valued Kakao at 2.9 billion RMB (403 million / 13.84%), which is approximately 500 million USD or 550 billion Korean won.

Is this deal worth it?

If we simply measure the current valuation, we can see that the profit in 2012 was $6.5 million. Calculating with a P/E ratio of 25 times, the valuation would be approximately $163 million. Looking at 2013, if we assume the game revenue to be $10 million/month (the revenue level at the end of 2012 had already reached $7 million/month, and here we assume a 40% growth), the annual revenue will reach $120 million. Calculating with a profit margin of 50%, reaching $60 million, and multiplying by a P/E ratio of 25 times, the valuation of just the game revenue reaches $1.5 billion, resulting in a threefold increase in Tencent's investment. This hasn't even taken into account the contributions of other parts of the revenue to the valuation.

Additionally, Tencent's WeChat is in competition with Kakao. According to recent news reports, WeChat's game platform is expected to go online within the year. If we simply use Kakao's profit model, with Kakao's 70 million users generating approximately $7 million/month in game revenue, to speculate on WeChat's game revenue, considering Tencent's scale of 300 million users, the game revenue after a period of development might stabilize at around $30 million/month. Over a one-year cycle, this translates to $360 million in revenue, or 2 billion RMB. This data can be referenced against the disclosed financial report of Tencent's full-year online game revenue of 6 billion RMB in 2012. The game revenue contributed by WeChat could potentially account for one-third of the current mature game business revenue.

Of course, all the above estimates are just simple model projections, intended solely for reference on possible future development paths and cannot be used as a true valuation basis. However, through these analyses, it can help us understand to some extent the directions various parties may think about in the future and the effects they want to achieve.
