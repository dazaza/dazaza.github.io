There are numerous popular methods for hosting a golf tournament. If you're struggling to decide which format to use for your golf tournament, there are a few considerations to keep in mind. Some formats are casual and fun, while others are more formal and serious for true competitors. The Scramble is the most common format when hosting a team-based golf tournament. This allows your tournament to feature 2, 3, or 4-person teams. In this type of play, team members can choose the best shot from each stroke. After selecting the best stroke, all players will then hit their balls from that position. There are various types of Scramble games, including Texas Scramble, Florida Scramble, and Ambrose.

If you opt for the Best Ball format in your golf tournament, all participants will play their own ball on every hole. This is also a team play format, but everyone hits their own ball. After completing each hole, the lowest score of the team serves as the team score. Best Ball is also known as Four Ball, with variations like 1-2-3 Best Ball. This format is favored because players enjoy hitting their own balls.

The Alternate Shot format is designed for two-person teams and is sometimes called a Foursome game. In this format, two players on a team alternate hitting shots from the tee. They use one ball between them, taking turns until the ball is in the hole. Alternating occurs between strokes and off the tees. Games like odds and evens and Scotch Foursomes also fall under the alternate shot category.

The Modified Stableford can be played by teams or individuals. In a Modified Stableford, the highest score wins. Each hole's score is worth a certain number of points based on par. The more points you accumulate, the higher your rank. A well-known example of a Modified Stableford format tournament is the PGA Tour.

Another format is the Chapman, also known as Pinehurst. This format involves team play with two people per team. Chapman combines several formats into one. Teammates switch balls after each of their tee shots, select the best ball after the second shot, and then alternate balls until they complete the hole.

A Bingo Bango Bongo is one of the most popular formats for a golf tournament, commonly used in golf association tournaments and league tournaments. This format rewards players for three specific achievements on each hole: the first player on the green, the first team to have all players on the green or closest to it simultaneously, and the first player to put the ball in the hole.

Flags is another format for playing in a golf tournament. In this format, all golfers start their round with a set number of strokes, determined by their handicaps. Players continue until their strokes run out, and the one who makes it the furthest wins.

The Lone Ranger format is another team play option, also known as Money Ball or Yellow Ball/Pink Ball. In this game, one player on the team owns the hole and must make it. Each team has four players, rotating roles at each hole as the Lone Ranger. The scores of each team member are added up, and the team with the lowest score wins the round.

There are many formats for golf tournaments. If you're hosting one at a specific course, consider asking around to find out what the participants prefer most. The Scramble is the most common game format for golf tournaments.
