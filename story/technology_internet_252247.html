Wang Yuyang: How to Build Effective Website Links?

by nbldnet123 on 2009-10-26 23:16:16

We all understand that the weight of links is different. That is to say, if A and B both link to your website, the effects they bring you are different, which depends on the quality of A and B themselves. In addition, the time we spend acquiring a link is also different. Some good links may not be obtained even after spending a whole day, while some links can get you many in just an hour.

Below, Wang Yuyang, the online marketing consultant at Lian Dong, lists different levels of links and estimates the time required to create these links.

1. Gold Links: Specific links organically combined within an article. The sources of these links can vary, and the linking methods are diverse. However, the most common ones are as follows. First, publishing an article on some industry platforms, if you can seamlessly direct the links in the article to your website, that's the best. Another method is to establish your own blog, write some original articles, and appropriately add some links in this original content, which will greatly benefit your website. These two types of links are relatively arduous, but spending two or three hours to obtain a gold link is worth it.

2. Silver Links: Acquiring reciprocal links from other websites. These connections are currently relatively easy to find. Through blog links, it is very easy to achieve. Now there are specialized forums and QQ groups for link trading. If you spend some time, it is very easy to find these gathering places for transactions. You can list your resource websites and provide them to the other party. If the other party is interested in your website, they will add your website to their reciprocal link library. But more often, the other party requests mutual linking, meaning both parties exchange links with each other’s websites. In such cases, I do not recommend doing transactions unless the other party's website indeed has significant relevance to your website and there are sufficient reasons for the exchange. This reason should be that there is indeed some complementarity and relevance between the two websites, rather than linking for the sake of linking. In this case, you can use the most relevant website in your hand to give the other party a link while letting the other party link to your website. It takes at least half an hour to create one of these links.

3. Bronze Links: Article links. You can publish some popular articles on some hot websites. If you properly place links in these articles, it will bring a large amount of targeted traffic to your website. Although these articles may not be original and may reappear on the network, as long as your website is intricately linked with these articles across the entire Internet, it will still bring you very good results in the end. With this method, you can create five or six outbound links within an hour.

4. Iron Links: Blog/forum comments, directory links, etc. Appropriately placing some links in comments on some popular blogs can still be very beneficial to your website. However, I oppose posting spam comments in blogs or forums. Such behavior is what blog/forum managers dislike the most. Therefore, when making comments, you should take it seriously. With this method, you can acquire around 10 links within an hour. As long as the Internet exists, target navigation will not disappear, so we can still make good use of them. Before submitting URLs to directories, it's best to check the status of the directory. If the directory is completely ignored by search engines or has been penalized, it's better to use it less. With this method of creating links, you can probably create 20 links in an hour.

The above is just a rough introduction to several methods of obtaining external links. There are still many specific methods, which are not listed here one by one. In short, "High-value links are better than low-value links, and having links is better than having none."
