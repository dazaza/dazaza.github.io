22 Photos to Show You the Charm of Facebook's New Headquarters

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 10:48:45

Just a few days ago, the media reported that social network Facebook had spent $120 million to build an employee dormitory complex with 394 units near its headquarters in Menlo Park, California. It is said that this complex includes a complete set of community facilities from a gym to a pet center, and employees can walk to work.

To be fair, it is no longer common for modern tech companies to provide employee dormitories, but after you briefly understand Facebook's new headquarters campus officially moved into in May 2012, you might have a completely different interpretation of the company's actions.

Below, let us take a simple look at this "dream estate" created by Facebook through slides:

When you see the highway sign standing by the roadside, you are actually already within the scope of Facebook's headquarters building complex in Menlo Park. Facebook's headquarters is located in the heart of Silicon Valley, but its surroundings are all open areas, giving one the feeling of being in a foreign land.

The building complex in the picture doesn't look like the location of an internet company at all, but more like an ordinary apartment.

This is the aerial view of Facebook's new headquarters. Did you notice? The ground of Facebook's new headquarters has four huge letters pre-laid out.

Facebook's giant campus includes a "town", main street, and many small shops around. In fact, this place was previously the headquarters of Sun Microsystems. During the design process, Facebook specially hired two Disney consultants to help design their headquarters campus.

On the day we entered the campus for interviews, Facebook was gathering under a small yellow crane to participate in the 24-hour "hackathon" competition. The "hackathon" event basically takes place every three months, has been held over 30 times, and has become one of the most important activities within the company. The basic rules are very simple: everyone can put aside their daily work during this time and develop entirely based on their own interests, and the company provides drinks and food for the employees. In fact, the new version of the personal page — Timeline — launched in 2011 was built by Sam Lessin during a hackathon.

Pedram Keyani, senior engineer at Facebook and head of the "hackathon" activity, and Facebook software engineer Bob Baldwin stated during the event: "Don't continue doing your regular work during this activity, don't be afraid to experiment, and don't be afraid to fail."

In fact, even this crane has its own story. It is reported that Facebook discovered this equipment in the basement of their rented Palo Alto office and then moved it to the new headquarters campus.

The two pedestrian bridges in the picture within the Facebook campus have colors similar to those of the Golden Gate Bridge in San Francisco. It is reported that in the old company in Palo Alto, employees had to frequently cross the road between two buildings, which was quite inconvenient. Therefore, in their new campus, the designers did not hesitate to build pedestrian bridges between the two buildings.

This is the main square of Facebook's new campus, where an LED screen scrolls motivational slogans (such as the current "Keep it simple"). However, what is puzzling is that there are four chairs placed below the LED screen.

The tiles on the ground of the new headquarters, when viewed from above, actually form individual letters. The picture shows part of the English word "hacker" (Hack). It is reported that Facebook did this mainly to make the new headquarters look as similar as possible to the old company in Palo Alto.

This office building even has a real garage; this is the garage door. It is reported that these garages will serve as temporary offices during "hackathon" events.

Facebook has a total of 9 dining rooms, providing free breakfast, lunch, and dinner from Monday to Friday (of course, restaurants serving sushi and Philz coffee are not free, but Facebook subsidizes employees).

Google usually marks almost everything in its headquarters with the Google logo, while Facebook labels everything with the "hacker" (Hack) tag. However, the cold drink cabinet in this restaurant escaped unscathed.

Facebook's new headquarters is extremely practical, with stores selling all kinds of items, health centers, electronic game rooms, even banks, hair salons, and music classrooms. Moreover, all these facilities are freely available for employees to use.

Since most engineers within Facebook like to "make things," naturally, this new campus also has a fully equipped carpentry room for employees to use.

The carpentry room is equipped with full-time professional carpenters who teach novices how to perform carpentry work. Moreover, the curiosity of these employees seems to be very strong because the shelves and garage spaces are already filled with various things they have built.

Perhaps, the most attractive feature of Facebook's new campus is its free laundry and dry cleaning services. Employees just need to drop off their clothes and pick them up on time.

It is reported that all the food in Facebook's campus is homemade. This store named "Facebook 15" makes its own ice cream, yogurt, candy, and pastries. New employees at Facebook may find this very unusual because, although the food is delicious, it can also quickly lead to weight gain.

In fact, you don't need to go to the dining room for every meal, as you can also fill your stomach using the various equipment and snacks in Facebook's kitchen.

In the office, Facebook employees usually work at long tables in open-plan office areas.

If you are overly fond of the food at Facebook 15, you can also reduce your guilt by working in this glass room with a treadmill after enjoying a feast.

In short, Facebook's new headquarters campus has been transformed into a complete little world where employees can fully enjoy their work and unleash their imagination.
