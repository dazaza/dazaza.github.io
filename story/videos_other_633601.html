Movie

Yesterday, I was once again struck by a sudden happiness when a colleague invited me to watch a movie. After much deliberation, we finally settled on "Huolan Biologic." It wasn't until the opening credits that I realized it was adapted from Geling Yan's novel. In my memory, there were always only twelve women, but why did it become thirteen? It turns out it was all because of a cat. Throughout the movie, there was a sense of shaky camera work, especially during scenes involving Guangzhou Lan Biological. Running seemed to be his most frequent action in this film. I'm not sure if it's just my imagination, but whenever there were war-related scenes, the entire picture was gray and somber. On the other hand, scenes involving Yumo and the others were so splendid and colorful, yet they lacked vitality. PS: The art director is a Japanese person who seems quite famous.

To be honest, I didn't particularly enjoy Ni Ni's performance. She tried hard to portray Yumo's arrogance, but her acting felt too forced and didn't make me feel that Yumo's pride came from within. In comparison, I liked the actress who played Shujuan more. Every time she looked outside through the stained glass, her eyes showed a mixture of longing and fear. Actually, Shujuan and Yumo share the same heart—equally proud and lonely. I really liked the male lead's line: "Looking at you is like seeing everything you've been through."

Another highlight is the background music, "Qinhuai Scenery." Take your time to savor it~

Winter

As winter approaches, both body and mind slow down. I've become increasingly reluctant to speak, especially around strangers. When friends chat, I just silently agree, lacking the chattiness of summer. I don't like going out, but I keep getting invitations to gatherings from friends, which I can't easily decline, so I have to attend. I prefer staying at home or going to Mushroom's house, daydreaming, reading, or watching movies. I bought a stack of books online, and I'm currently reading Han Han's "Youth," but I can't stop thinking about Guo Jingming's "Tiny Times 3.0." I've finally collected the complete set of "Li." The one closest to me now is "Li: Addiction." Addiction is something unpredictable and irresistible.

Recently, I've enjoyed movies with many voiceovers, books filled with illustrations, and music with melancholic lyrics. Each of these interests seems so off-topic. I remember someone telling me at a viewing event in September that a movie director should convey his thoughts through the lens, not through excessive narration. My love for illustrated books might stem from the influence of "Silence." Music should be appreciated for its melody, right? Sigh~ How far off am I?

Surprise

The biggest surprise came one evening when HK Yin informed me that he had finally bought an iPhone 4S. However, it coincided with a short-term financial crisis, and owing money feels terrible~ Of course, if I didn't have to pay, that would be another story!

Today brought another surprise—an international call from Australia. ErSong said she's coming back soon~ We talked about how she hugged her cat and told me the cake shop hadn't closed, recalling the tangerines outside the south gate of Sichuan University and the beverage shop where cream floated on tea. She talked about her current job and life with enoki mushrooms. Suddenly, it felt like the days waiting for "boyfriends" in the east area of Sichuan University were memories from long ago. Although I clearly remember how we planned to use bones to reserve seats in the teaching building, which ultimately failed due to ErSong being outdone by the girl in front of her.

We're waiting for postcards sent from Europe, choosing between two pictures, not knowing which one will end up in our hands~

I won't say more. Mushroom reminded me that literary youths have now transformed into 2B youths. Yes, if I continue being so pretentious, I might die early! Good night~ My future happiness!
