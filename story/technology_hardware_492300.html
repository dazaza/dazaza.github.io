Days without my husband

by wenazjlove on 2011-09-20 22:22:17

A few days ago, my younger brother came back from the factory. He brought two salary envelopes for himself and his wife, with a look of great joy on his face. However, I immediately felt a sourness in my heart and a prickling sensation in my eyes. I thought about how I used to be like this too, bringing money home every month with pride, sharing the economic burden of the family with my husband. Although the work was tiring, my heart was always sweet. With my optimistic nature, my face always flowed with endless happiness, and joyful songs floated throughout the house.

In the days without my husband, I always went to my mother's house for meals. Every day, I watched my over sixty-year-old mother hunching her back as she served me food and water, taking care of me like a child. It made me feel so painful and unbearable. In order to make me eat more, my mother had to rack her brains to prepare three meals a day. Thinking about it, at her age, she should be enjoying her late years with her grandchildren around, happily indulging in the joy of family life. But because of me, because of a virtually self-care unable person like me, my mother gave up the right to enjoy her twilight years, serving me daily without complaint.

Finally, my husband finally called to tell me that he would be coming back soon. Tears streamed down my face, but my heart was smiling because these were tears of joy. All the waiting and disappointment no longer mattered. The important thing is that I could hear my husband's familiar shout every day again: "Wife, the water is hot, you can wash your face." "Wife, it's time for dinner." Whenever I heard my husband's simple shouts, the pain in my body would temporarily disappear completely. But I still pretended not to hear, just to hear it one more time: "Old woman, come for dinner." Then, I secretly smiled and ran quickly to have dinner.

I love how my husband calls me this way. Though it sounds rustic and common, it calls out the sweetness in my heart and the happiness in my life...

But now, I am like a living ancient person on the verge of death, finding it very difficult to wash and dress every day. Every time, I quietly call out in my heart: "Husband, come back soon, it's hard for your wife to live alone!" Thus, I repeatedly called to urge my husband to come home, and my husband kept promising, but still couldn't return. I know my husband must want to come back as soon as possible, but it's close to the New Year, and it's hard to find someone to replace him. After all, the workplace is not our own home where we can come and go as we please. This way, I was repeatedly pushed to the peak of hope, only to be thrown into the valley of disappointment time and time again, almost breaking my heart. But I still stubbornly wait, and desperately yearn.
