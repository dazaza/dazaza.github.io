The Capital of Love

By Zhao Jicai

Dear Mr. Zhao Jicai,

Hello! I think I am like most people who accidentally came across your writings. I have appreciated your interpretations of marriage and the relationships between men and women in your articles. After reading quite a few, I suddenly felt an urge to share my own story.

I am 35 years old, unmarried, and without a boyfriend. According to common parlance, I've become one of those "leftover women." As my mom would say, if I don't get married this year, I'll truly be considered an old maid.

In reality, I do want to get married as soon as possible, but marriage isn't like buying cabbage at the market where you can just pick any head and take it home. I need to meet someone I actually love before I can marry them. Although it's said that there are plenty of men out there, even when buying cabbage, you still need to be selective, right? And choosing a partner is far more important. I don't want to end up like some of my friends who get married and then divorced. I don't want all that trouble. Despite the fragility of modern marriages, I still want to find a reliable person and live honestly for the rest of my life.

I have been on blind dates, quite a few actually, and pretty much rejected all of them. A few were somewhat acceptable, but they didn't choose me either, so nothing came of it after our first meeting. This has been going on since I was 28, and now I'm 35, still single. There were a few I dated for a while, but eventually, it just didn't feel right, so we broke it off.

Many relatives and friends have asked me what kind of man I am looking for before I will agree to marry. Thinking carefully, my only requirement is that he must be handsome and treat me well. I have tried dating a few men who treated me well but were average-looking, but I couldn't get past that mental barrier. Looking at them, I simply didn't feel the spark of love. No matter how hard I tried, I couldn't feel anything. I attempted to imagine them as handsome men, but once I opened my eyes, they left me feeling as calm as a winter lake. I know that at my age, finding such a man is not easy, but this is what I want. If I can't find him, I'd rather stay single.

Do you think my requirements are excessive? Everyone has the right to pursue love, and this is the kind of love I want. If I encounter it, I'll get married; if I don't, I'm fine staying this way forever. Isn't that okay?

Sincerely,

Pure Water Fish

Dear Pure Water Fish,

Finding a handsome man to marry isn't just your dream; it's also the aspiration of countless other women. Marrying a good-looking man supposedly comes with many benefits, but the most significant one is the potential for a longer life. Eating meals and sleeping beside such a face every day must be a beautiful experience. Good mood leads to fewer illnesses, which naturally prolongs life. However, there are downsides too. With good food and sleep, aren't you worried about gaining weight? And with a heavier body, aren't you concerned that he might look at other women? These are things you should consider.

Many people had numerous dreams when they were young, dreaming of becoming this or that when they grew up. Gradually, as we age and gain more experiences, we discover that some people achieve their dreams while others develop new ones. We constantly waver between dreams and reality. What is a dream? It's our longing for a better future, even though it may contain much fantasy, but it keeps us holding on. The existence of dreams at least proves one point: fictional realities are still part of our real lives. Pursuing a dream is like crossing a narrow bridge; those who fall aren't necessarily lacking in persistence or focus. More importantly, reality is brutal. Either there are too many people, and you get pushed off, or someone sabotages you, and you fall unintentionally. Despite all this, we still hold on firmly because dreams and reality are only a step apart. Whether you can cross that gap depends on your luck.

Finding a handsome man to marry isn't wrong, and your requirements aren't excessive. If I were you, I would continue pursuing this. In an era where handsome men are rarer than cabbages, you need to learn to take the initiative and cast a wide net to increase your chances of success. Additionally, you need to enhance your own appeal, particularly your beauty. Without it, your chances of success will decrease by half.

Handsome men, like beautiful women, may vary in individual judgment, but they generally share one similarity: those who rely on their looks often lack depth. If they don't possess intellectual depth, you need to. Even if you're not extensively learned, you should at least have some knowledge and be able to guide them while making them feel comfortable and respected. This is wisdom. You must learn to be a wise woman and use your wisdom effectively, ensuring that it enhances your attractiveness rather than repels men.

At your age, the only capital to attract handsome men is being both beautiful and intelligent while appearing modest. If you lack these qualities or cannot achieve them, then perhaps you shouldn't aim for handsome men.

In this era where everything revolves around capital, love is no exception. Men need it, women need it, and life needs it too. What do you think?

Love Values
