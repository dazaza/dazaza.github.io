Woman claims that the logistics company increased the price halfway when she sent parcels back home during the Spring Festival

by aiipii12987 on 2012-03-08 14:50:55

Original price was 1160 yuan, final cost was 2580 yuan ——Parcel delivery; logistics company raises price midway; ■Reported by Wu Shenlin and Feng Yandan of Express Journal, intern Chen Huimin; At the end of last year, Ms. Zhang who worked in Guangzhou entrusted two batches of parcels to Qida Logistics Company for delivery back to her hometown and paid a total of 1160 yuan on site for freight. However, while she had already returned to Sichuan ahead of time, she suddenly received a call from the logistics company requesting a price increase before they would deliver. After failed negotiations, she ultimately had no choice but to pay an additional 1420 yuan to retrieve her parcels.

After signing and paying, she was told about the "price increase"; On January 6th, Ms. Zhang who lived in Dongpu Town, Tianhe District, prepared to move her family back to her hometown in Sichuan Province before the Spring Festival. Before leaving, she packed her clothes and other luggage, preparing to entrust them in two batches to Guangzhou Qida Logistics Company for delivery. After paying 280 yuan and 880 yuan respectively for the two batches of freight, Ms. Zhang signed two cargo waybills with the logistics company's staff. After finishing the luggage consignment, Ms. Zhang boarded the train home that night. On January 9th, after returning home, Ms. Zhang suddenly received a call from Qida Logistics Company. The staff informed her that due to the high logistics costs around the Spring Festival, the two parcels could not be delivered at the original price. "They said either we pay more or wait until February or March after the Spring Festival logistics peak has passed for the delivery," Ms. Zhang felt very helpless because the items inside the parcels were daily necessities. If delayed for one or two months, sending them home would lose their meaning. Since the items were still in the hands of the logistics company, she had no choice but to negotiate with the other party, requiring them to bear half of the increased freight for the largest batch of parcels, which gained the agreement of the logistics company, allowing the two batches of parcels to be smoothly sent out.

Customer suspects parcels being "sold off"; Around January 13th, the two batches of parcels sent by Ms. Zhang from Guangzhou successively arrived in Sichuan. To her anger, when collecting the parcels, she was not treated as initially agreed with Guangzhou Qida Logistics Company, but instead was required by the local logistics company to pay another 280 yuan and 1140 yuan for freight. Since neither of the two batches of luggage was handled by Qida Logistics in Sichuan, Ms. Zhang had to accept this "silent loss" in order to get the parcels.

Ms. Zhang noticed that the waybill for the goods sent in Guangzhou by Guangzhou Qida Logistics Company was marked as "paid upon arrival", but the waybills required for signature by the two logistics companies in Sichuan were marked as "pay on collection". She suspected that the sudden rise in parcel freight was not simply due to increased freight, but rather her parcels were "sold off" midway by the logistics company, and she unknowingly bore the cost for this process.

Logistics company: Customer complaint is "unreasonable troublemaking"; In the morning of the previous day, reporters from Express Journal called Guangzhou Qida Logistics Company. A Mr. Zhang who claimed to be a department manager said that Ms. Zhang's complaint was "unreasonable troublemaking." Mr. Zhang said that although a goods consignment form had been signed with Ms. Zhang, it was only known when the goods were delivered to the cargo yard that they could not be shipped according to the agreed price, "We called her to ask for her opinion. She said these were all new clothes, needed for the New Year, so even if it was expensive, we had to ship them, there was an oral agreement."

Regarding the claim that "the largest parcel should bear half of the increased freight," Mr. Zhang responded that Ms. Zhang indeed proposed this at the time, but the logistics company did not agree, "We can't do business at a loss, right? The goods stayed in the cargo yard for three days without being picked up because the price couldn't be agreed upon." Mr. Zhang said that the staff explained that if the customer felt it was too expensive, they could have friends take the parcels to another company for shipping, but Ms. Zhang eventually chose to let them ship. Mr. Zhang believed that Ms. Zhang complained "because it was expensive, she was psychologically unbalanced afterwards."

Guangzhou Consumer Association: Midway price increase is a breach of contract; Regarding the incident of consumer logistics delivery "being priced up," Secretary General Li Yongqiang of the Guangzhou Consumer Committee stated in an interview with Express Journal that logistics companies have no reason to raise prices midway. "Both parties have already agreed. The logistics company raising the price for some reason is a breach of contract. If there is evidence, Guangzhou's logistics company can be sued."

Attorney Liu Kai from Guangdong Tianqi Law Firm believes that the logistics company's practice of raising prices is both unreasonable and illegal. He thinks that once the logistics company accepts an order, it forms a contractual relationship with the consumer. Raising prices is a violation of the contract and must assume responsibility according to the Contract Law. Regarding the logistics company's claim of "getting the consumer's consent," Liu Kai emphasized that under such circumstances, the consumer was forced, and cannot be considered legal. "For example, if a consumer is on a boat, and the ticket price increases temporarily, can they jump into the sea?" Liu Kai stated that the logistics company's behavior constitutes forced buying and selling. Legally speaking, the consumer's verbal consent under such circumstances is invalid because it violates the principle of fairness and voluntariness.

Industry chaos

Complaints go unresolved, claims difficult

1. Time delays. If the recipient is waiting for express delivery urgently, it can easily cause problems. Some deliveries are ordinary food, which can spoil due to delays.

2. Lost packages. Once this happens, even if the consumer finds the courier company for compensation, it is hard to make up for the loss.

3. Goods swapped. Express Journal has previously reported on incidents where valuable herbs like Cordyceps sinensis were reduced in quantity or even replaced with samples.

4. Damaged goods. During sorting at courier transfer stations, items are often not handled carefully, and rough handling such as throwing is common, easily causing damage.

5. Unreasonable terms like refusal to inspect goods before signing. Some courier companies refuse customers' requests to inspect goods before signing. However, if the customer signs first and then inspects the package, any damage or loss puts the customer at a disadvantage procedurally.

6. Misdelivery and refusal to help retrieve parcels. Some place names and addresses differ by just one character, but may be miles apart in reality. If the courier makes a wrong delivery and refuses to retrieve the parcel, it causes inconvenience.

7. Complaints unresolved, compensation difficult. When consumers encounter situations where their interests are harmed, complaints made directly to the courier company often go unanswered. Even if relevant departments such as industry and commerce get involved, obtaining reasonable compensation is extremely difficult.

Industry standards

Yuexiu Industry & Commerce takes the lead nationwide in promoting standard contracts for courier services

Franchisees are not allowed to arbitrarily change fee standards

Express Journal reports, correspondent Ye Huaixin, He Jingxian, Jiang Jingying - We learned from the Guangzhou Industry & Commerce Bureau yesterday that the Yuexiu Branch took the lead nationwide in promoting the "Standard Contract for Franchise Operations (Joining) in Courier Services," standardizing the operating order of the courier industry from the source.

This standard contract clearly specifies the registered capital, employee numbers, service charge standards, etc., that enterprises joining the courier industry should possess. Among these, courier companies developing franchises need to have at least two direct-operated stores engaged in courier services for over a year within China, and franchisees must be corporate entities with courier business licenses. Franchisees are not allowed to arbitrarily raise or lower fee standards; otherwise, they must compensate for losses incurred by the courier company. Additionally, the text includes clauses such as the franchisor providing unified training to the franchisee.
