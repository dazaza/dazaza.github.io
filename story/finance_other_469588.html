Guangxi Faces "Electricity Shortage" - Hardware and Electro-Mechanical Industries May Suffer from "Excessive Speculation"

by anonymous on 2011-08-02 17:17:03

[China Hardware Tools Network] Various places in Guangxi suffered from the worst electricity shortage in 20 years. On August 1st, some roads in Nanning city district of Guangxi were illuminated by only half of their street lamps.

Director of Emergency Disaster Mitigation and Public Service Department of China Meteorological Administration, Chen Zhenlin, introduced that since July, the national average precipitation was 105.8 mm, which was 8.7% less than the long-term average for the same period (115.9 mm), making it the lowest precipitation in nearly 11 years. Apart from northern Xinjiang, eastern Inner Mongolia, most parts of Tibet, part of eastern Northwest China, most parts of Huai-Jiang region, and Hainan where the precipitation was 30% to 100% more than the long-term average, with some areas even experiencing a 100% to 200% increase, the rest of the regions had precipitation close to or less than the long-term average. The precipitation in the middle and western parts of Northwest China, central and western Inner Mongolia, western Huang-Huai region, Jiang-Han region, most parts of Jiang-Nan region, western Guang-Xi region, eastern South-West China, and eastern North-East China was 30% to 80% less than the long-term average, with some areas having an 80% reduction. Among them, Guizhou and Hunan had the least precipitation since 1951.

Chen Zhenlin predicted that this year's 9th strong typhoon "Meihua" would affect parts of eastern coastal Huai-Jiang region, eastern Huang-Huai region, eastern North China, southern Northeast China, and the eastern seas from August 6th to 9th; there would be more rain in eastern Huang-Huai region, eastern North China, and southern Northeast China in the first ten days of August, with rainfall generally ranging from 80 to 120 mm, and some areas reaching 150 to 220 mm. The main rainfall periods would occur on August 5th to 6th and around August 9th; under the scorching sun, Jiang-Nan and northern Guang-Xi would still experience hot and humid weather with temperatures ranging from 34℃ to 37℃ from August 1st to 3rd, but after August 4th, the hot and humid weather would gradually ease.
