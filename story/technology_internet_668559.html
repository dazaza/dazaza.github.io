Product Introduction: Bottega Petrarca Love Moscato Sparkling Wine, originally imported from Italy in its original bottle. The Moscato sparkling wine is brewed with fresh and juicy grapes from the Euganean Hills of Padua Province. The hills are famous for roses, and during the blooming season, bees carry the fragrance of roses to the grapevines. This is how this rich and sweet wine is created. The rose aroma of the wine naturally reminds one of the petals on the bottle.

The process determines the taste: Champagne can only be produced using the bottle secondary fermentation method, also known as the "Champagne Method." Grapes used for champagne are first fermented into still white wine without bubbles, then sugar and yeast are added to the bottle, and a small-scale fermentation occurs inside the bottle, which is solely for producing bubbles.

Although the champagne-making process sounds simple, it is actually quite complex. When pressing the grapes, it must be done gently and slowly. Each time, 4 tons of grapes are pressed, divided into three pressings. The first pressing produces 2050 liters, called Cuvee, and all high-quality champagnes are made from this juice. The second pressing produces 616 liters, which is rougher but stronger in flavor, suitable only for making low-grade champagne.

Blending is an extremely important step in making champagne, considered the essence of champagne production technology. Champagne is France's northernmost grape-growing region, with a cold climate and harsh growing conditions. To maintain the quality and taste stability of champagne every year, most champagnes are blended from base wines of different years, varieties, and origins. Every year, major champagne manufacturers taste a large number of base wines and precisely blend them together; sometimes these base wines can reach up to 300 or even 400 kinds.

The secondary fermentation of wine is another critical step. The sugar added to the bottle produces alcohol and carbon dioxide under the action of yeast. As the bottle is sealed, the carbon dioxide gradually dissolves in the wine. At this point, the pressure in the bottle can reach about 5-6 atmospheres. A problem arises: the dead yeast accumulates on the walls of the bottle, making it difficult to remove them from the bottle. In 1818, the cellar master of Veuve Clicquot invented a method. During the aging process after the secondary fermentation, the bottles are inverted on a perforated "A"-shaped rack. Workers turn each bottle by 1/4 circle daily and change the inclination angle of the bottle. By the end, the bottle is upright with the mouth facing down, standing vertically in the holes of the "A"-shaped rack. Then, the bottle mouth is frozen, opened, and the pressure inside the bottle ejects the sediment that has frozen like a jelly plug. Of course, this process inevitably loses a little wine, so a part of sweet wine needs to be added back to the bottle. The sweetness of the sweet wine added back directly determines the sweetness of the champagne.

Product Features: Bottega Petrarca Love Moscato Sparkling Wine, the most well-known and beautiful sparkling wine in Italy. The bottle is meticulously designed, elegant, noble, and extraordinary. This wine inherits the essence of Italian sparkling wines, being sweet, delicate, fragrant, and refreshing. Made from aromatic Muscat grapes, it is brewed through a slow fermentation process. It features floral aromas combined with fruity notes, blending white flowers, roses, and peaches, making it a perfect dessert wine, suitable for pairing with cheese and desserts.

Wine Review: Pale gold, with fine and persistent bead, integrating the scents of Moscato, fruit, and flowers; fragrant, delicate, sweet, leaving a lingering aroma of rose petals after tasting.
