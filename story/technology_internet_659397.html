Country Garden's profit fell by 67% last year.

by fdkrkkgfem0 on 2012-02-06 10:25:40

Country Garden's Profit Fell 67% Last Year - Corporate Updates - Industry News - Building Materials Network [Business Report Exclusive] Reporter Kin Ki Lam reported: Country Garden (2007) experienced a profit decline last year. The full-year profit amounted to RMB 1.378 billion, falling approximately 66.7% year-on-year. If not including the fair value loss generated from share swaps, the adjusted profit was RMB 2.62 billion, decreasing by about 36.7%. Earnings per share were 8.45 fen, and the final dividend was 3 fen.

The company's Chief Financial Officer, Ngai Kan Woo, pointed out during yesterday’s earnings release that the fair value loss from share swaps last year was RMB 1.241 billion. The swap contracts are purely for accounting calculations and do not involve cash flow, so the company has no intention of redeeming them early. This swap contract will expire in 2013.

Last year, Country Garden's total revenue was approximately RMB 15.713 billion, down 11.4%; among which, the income from real estate development was approximately RMB 14.737 billion, down 11.6%. The total confirmed income area was approximately 2.1475 million square meters, with the average selling price of confirmed income rising from RMB 6,359 per square meter the previous year to RMB 6,861 per square meter last year.

President and Executive Director, Chui Kin Bo, mentioned on the same occasion that the expected contract sales for this year would be RMB 19 billion, up 10% year-on-year; the confirmed area would be approximately 3.3 million square meters, up 54% year-on-year. He said that as of the end of March, the pre-sale area was approximately 2.68 million square meters, already reaching 80% of the target.

Additionally, Chairman Yang Guoqiang stated that the government is currently evaluating the Holland Village project, believing that the project will definitely go to auction. The company was previously invited to participate in the bidding process, but whether or not they will bid depends on their own financial capabilities. He also mentioned that he holds a cautiously optimistic attitude towards the domestic real estate market because the measures introduced earlier in the mainland have helped stimulate the property market.
