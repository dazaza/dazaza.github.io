Shanxi Securities: Qingdao Beer, Rationalizing Brand Structure Aims at Strategic Markets - Stock Market Basics

by dx0957095 on 2009-12-03 18:35:28

Investment Highlights: The industry layout is still unstable and competition remains fierce. After 10 years of consolidation, the beer industry has formed the current oligopolistic competitive landscape, but the competitive situation is still unstable. The market share of the top four players - China Resources, Yanjing, Qingdao, and Interbrew can reach 60%, but each of their market shares is less than 20%. Industry competition will not weaken in the next five years, and each company will seize the market on a provincial basis. In areas where their market share is between 30-40%, competition will become increasingly fierce, and it will be an inevitable trend for these regions to eventually have a brand with a market share of over 50%. However, in the advantageous regions where each major player's market share exceeds 50%, the degree of competition will decrease somewhat. Currently, the main regions where the market share of each major player exceeds 50% include Liaoning, Anhui, and Sichuan for China Resources; Beijing, Guangxi, and Inner Mongolia for Yanjing; and Shandong and Shaanxi for Qingdao. Provinces in the fierce battle zones mainly include Guangdong, Jiangsu, Zhejiang, Hunan, Hubei, and Hebei. In the future competitive environment, the company will adhere to rational competition, concentrate its superior forces to break through one by one, and strictly control sales expenses to maintain sustainable and healthy development.

Actively develop quasi-strategic markets such as North China, East China, and Sichuan. The company's advantageous markets are mainly concentrated in Shandong and Shaanxi. The income from Qingdao, Shaanxi, and Guangdong can account for more than 70% of total revenue, and the profit ratio even exceeds 100%. Among them, the home base of Qingdao region has a market share of around 90%, which is the company's main source of revenue and profit. After merging Yantai Beer and Baotuquan Beer, the company's market share in other parts of Shandong has also significantly increased, reaching above 50% level. In the future, the company will regard North China (Hebei, Beijing), East China (Shanghai, Jiangsu), and Sichuan as quasi-strategic markets for development and actively cultivate them. Currently, the company's development momentum in Anhui, Jiangsu, and Sichuan is good. As an emerging market, Shijiazhuang has also experienced rapid growth in recent years, with sales expected to nearly reach 2 million tons this year. Due to being located at the headquarters of Yanjing Beer, the competitor has a solid advantage in the Beijing area, making the company's progress there consistently difficult. However, this year, the company is expected to turn losses into profits with the help of product structure improvements and significant cost reductions.

Benefiting from cost reductions, the company's performance grew rapidly in 2009, and this cost advantage will continue to play a role in the near future. We have raised the company's earnings forecast, expecting the EPS for 2009-2011 to be 0.98 yuan, 1.19 yuan, and 1.40 yuan respectively. The corresponding dynamic P/E ratios are 36.8, 30.4, and 25.7 times respectively. Given that the market has consistently assigned a high valuation to the company's stable growth, we upgrade the rating to "overweight."

Risk Warning: Strict penalties for drunk driving may affect beer sales; volatility in the A-share market.
