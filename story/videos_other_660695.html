Meanwhile, AC Milan's "Plan B" for introducing Ganso has begun to start. It is reported that yesterday, Ganso and his agent met with the president of Corinthians and Ronaldo, which confirmed the "transfer bridge plan" previously reported by the media (Ganso's contract stipulates that domestic transfers cost 25 million euros, while international transfers cost 50 million euros. AC Milan plans to introduce Ganso through Corinthians for 25 million euros and then buy him from Corinthians next January). Quan Market directly considered the two as "AC Milan's envoys" and even stated that AC Milan would not let Corinthians spend a single cent. Officially, there are two transfers, but in reality, AC Milan is paying through Corinthians and then loaning Ganso to Corinthians for six months.

The signings of Mexes and Taiwo have been settled, and now the name being hotly discussed is Ganso. Regarding Ganso, his recent injury and AC Milan's use of their non-EU quota to sign Taiwo have brought many uncertainties to what seemed like a certain transfer. Chief transfer expert Di Marzio of Sky Sports said: "AC Milan using this quota on Taiwo shows that their interest in Ganso has decreased. On one hand, Ganso's physical condition is starting to make them doubt, and on the other hand, Ganso's price (breach penalty of 50 million) is too high."

Galliani revealed: "Eto'o is an excellent player and a good person. After the match between Manchester United and Barcelona held in Rome in the summer of 2009, I found Eto'o's agent Mezzale. I hoped to sign Eto'o, but that summer was obviously not the right time to sign him." This can't help but remind people that in that summer, when Eto'o was destined to leave Barcelona, Mezzale indeed first negotiated with AC Milan before involving other teams including Inter Milan.

Once again, thanks to Eto'o's goal, Inter Milan ultimately eliminated Roma and advanced to the Italian Cup final. And the name Eto'o is also one that makes AC Milan Vice President Galliani feel regretful and regretful. Coincidentally, just two days ago, Galliani publicly talked about Eto'o.
