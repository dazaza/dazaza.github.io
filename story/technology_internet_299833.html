Criminal Defense: How to Authenticate Old Evidence in Zang Tianshuo's Appeal

by guanlu369 on 2009-12-09 10:42:49

Zang Tianshuo Sentenced to 6 Years for Gathering a Mob to Fight: Appeal Already Decided

[News Recap]

According to Sina News, on the 27th of this month, the "Zang Tianshuo organized crime case" was sentenced in the Beijing No. 2 Intermediate People's Court. Zang Tianshuo was found guilty of gathering a mob to fight and sentenced to 6 years in prison. Currently, Zang Tianshuo has already decided to appeal. To ensure the success of the appeal, Zang Tianshuo's family specifically hired Mr. Xu Lanting, one of the most famous criminal defense lawyers in Beijing, to take over the case.

Mr. Xu admitted that he had already taken over from the previous lawyer, Mr. Luo, and would file an appeal with the Beijing No. 2 Intermediate People's Court within 10 days at the fastest. If accepted by the No. 2 Intermediate Court, the case would then be transferred to the Beijing Higher People's Court for review.

The previous lawyer of Zang Tianshuo, Mr. Luo, told Sina Entertainment that if Zang Tianshuo continues to appeal, the possibility of being acquitted is almost non-existent, but there is still a relatively large possibility of a reduced sentence: "From the actual situation of the trial that day, some evidence favorable to Zang Tianshuo was not adopted by the court, and no sufficient reason was given as to why these pieces of evidence were not adopted. If the second-instance court can carefully analyze and investigate, there is still a possibility of reducing the sentence."

At the same time, Mr. Luo revealed a key point for the successful appeal of this case. Mr. Luo believed that before the incident occurred, Zang Tianshuo took measures to limit and prevent harmful results from happening, rather than deliberately allowing Qiu Changchun to act that way. In fact, regarding Zang Tianshuo himself, he indeed did not hope for such a result.

Previously, Qiu Changchun pointed out that Zang Tianshuo had said, "If we can't reach an agreement, then we will fight." Mr. Luo pointed out that this statement was actually taken out of context. "Zang Tianshuo's original words also included another sentence: 'We cannot let troublemakers destroy our disco.' When he learned that someone was going to cause trouble, his intention was to protect the disco, which is understandable, and any operator would do the same. Later, when both sides changed locations to fight, Zang Tianshuo was indeed unaware."

Mr. Luo believed that if appealing with the goal of reducing the punishment, it should focus as much as possible on this point. Lawyer Xu Lanting expressed that he would adopt this suggestion.

[Case Review]

In 2003, a mass brawl occurred at the Langfang train station square. The reason was that on December 31, 2002, Zang Tianshuo and Sun Baohé, a Northeasterner, jointly operated the "Friend" disco in Langfang, Hebei Province. Later, due to economic disputes, Sun Baohé opened the "Heatwave" disco in Langfang and started independently.

On the evening of June 20, 2003, around 9 pm, Sun Baohé demanded a 200,000 yuan share transfer fee from the "Friend" disco and got into a big argument over the phone with the general manager of the disco, Lü Changchun. Both parties agreed to meet at the Langfang train station square, each bringing their own people.

That night, under Zang Tianshuo's instruction, Lü Changchun gathered nearly a hundred people armed with machetes and iron pipes, and engaged in a melee with Sun Baohé's people. During the fight, Meng Long, the security manager of Sun Baohé's side, was killed, two security guards and several innocent passersby were injured.

[Legal Focus Points]

1. According to Zang Tianshuo's representative lawyer, some evidence favorable to Zang Tianshuo was not adopted by the court during the first trial. Will these pieces of evidence play a role in the second trial?

2. Can the reason of "if we can't reach an agreement, then we will fight" to protect the disco become a reason for mitigating the punishment?

[Lawyer Interpretation]

Keyword: Evidence Adoption

Evidence Not Adopted in First Trial Hardly Plays Role in Second Trial

Shangquan Law Firm is the only law firm in Beijing specializing in criminal defense. According to lawyer Chang Zheng of the firm, so-called criminal litigation evidence refers to all facts presented in legal form during the criminal litigation process to prove the true circumstances of the case.

During the second trial, presenting and cross-examining evidence mainly targets new evidence. For evidence already presented in the first trial, unless these pieces of evidence have new content and supporting reasons, and the new supporting reasons have a significant impact on the case facts.

Therefore, generally speaking, for evidence not adopted in the first trial, if there are no new circumstances, they usually will not be presented or cross-examined again during the second trial.

Keyword: Mitigation Reason

Fighting to Protect the Disco Is Not a Reason for Mitigating Punishment

Lawyer Chang Zheng said that in cases of gathering mobs to fight, those typically subject to criminal penalties are the ringleaders and active participants. According to the Criminal Law, those who commit the crime of gathering mobs to fight shall be sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment of less than three years, criminal detention, or public surveillance for the ringleaders and other active participants.

If the ringleaders and other active participants fall into any of the following four scenarios, they shall be sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment of three to ten years: frequent gatherings of mobs to fight; large-scale gatherings of mobs to fight with serious social impact; gatherings of mobs to fight in public places or traffic arteries causing severe social disorder; using weapons in gatherings of mobs to fight.

Zang Tianshuo was sentenced to six years in prison for the crime of gathering mobs to fight in the first trial, mainly because he met the above four scenarios.

The lawyer pointed out that Zang Tianshuo gathering mobs to fight to protect his own disco is not a legal reason for mitigating punishment. Zang Tianshuo should have protected his disco through legal means, rather than gathering mobs to fight.

Lawyer Chang Zheng said that although it is not a legal reason, if the defense lawyer defends on this basis and the reasons are indeed sufficient, the judge may consider whether to mitigate the punishment based on the specific circumstances at the time.

[Know More]

Why Was the First Trial of Zang Tianshuo's Case Conducted in the Intermediate Court?

Generally, ordinary criminal cases should be tried by the basic-level people's courts. Why was the first trial of Zang Tianshuo's organized crime case conducted in the Beijing No. 2 Intermediate People's Court?

Regarding this question, Lawyer Chang Zheng answered that according to the law, the first-instance cases under the jurisdiction of intermediate people's courts specifically include: counter-revolutionary cases, cases endangering national security; ordinary criminal cases that may be sentenced to life imprisonment or death; criminal cases involving foreigners.

In the case of Zang Tianshuo gathering mobs to fight, more than one defendant was involved. According to previous media reports, two people were killed and many others were injured in this melee event.

Because it involved human lives, the relevant defendants might be sentenced to life imprisonment or death. This is likely the reason why the first trial of the case was conducted in the Beijing No. 2 Intermediate People's Court.
