My 2011 Tianya blog, people of insight are all here in this Tianya community.

by cfgsdftre77 on 2012-02-11 23:09:47

2011 is equal for everyone, but not everyone's 2011 was fortunate. This year, for me, was a mixture of joy and sorrow, a blend of fulfillment and emptiness. Although I didn't catch the bullet train to paradise, didn't ride on an easily collapsible school bus, or even get chased by urban management officers, or suffer from violent forced demolitions, in my ordinary life, I couldn't escape the harm done to my charitable heart by the Red Cross, the blow to my spirit of kindness by passers-by who refused to help the dying, or even the contamination of my delicious meals by gutter oil, and the erosion of my strong physique by drinking milk. I am just a small person, living a small life filled with bitterness, spiciness, sourness, and sweetness in a small corner of society.

I am a small employee. Busy all year round, at the end of the year, there is no bonus equivalent to 27 months' salary like those in state-owned enterprises. Since the beginning of my employment history, I have never received such a maddening big red envelope. Since there are no economic benefits, I can only seek spiritual satisfaction. Although I am not the Ah Q in Lu Xun's pen, the mental victory method of Ah Q really makes one feel balanced in loneliness. At the age of thirty, my career has yet to take off. I can only sway at the end of youth in an ordinary position, watching the dream of a successful career gradually disappear in the gray sky. I am neither outstanding nor lazy. Apart from doing my best to do my own duties, I cannot find any reason that would make my superior look favorably upon me. If there is any gain in my work, it is the convenience of my position that has allowed me to receive a provincial honor, and in the system paper evaluation, my humble work received a second prize. Of course, this is not boasting. I clearly know that these bizarre things cannot be eaten as food, nor are they qualifications for promotion. I am just a small employee, satisfying the vain heart of living in the current secular society with these illusory things.

I have a hobby, which is writing blogs. As a poor second generation, I don't have money to support a life of debauchery, nor do I have power to protect a life of commanding the winds and rains. I can only find a hobby that doesn't cost money and is meaningful in a calm and helpless life, in order to pass the time and cultivate healthy interests. I am not an expert, but I shamelessly registered on China.com Expert Blogs. However, the editor did give me face, occasionally recommending a few of my articles to the homepage of China.com Blogs or even the homepage of China.com, making me quite proud. But then I thought, blogging is not like a debate on Mount Hua, there's no need to argue over superiority, being happy is enough. Later, before last New Year's Day, I also opened my second base on Tianya Blogs. At first, the editor rarely recommended my articles for a long time. I secretly thought that since I am not a celebrity, I cannot bring enough popularity to the website, or perhaps because I am not from a scholarly family, my writing skills are really not good enough to be showcased on a grand stage. There was even a period when I felt very disheartened. I didn't give up. The courage to persevere is needed in the positions chosen after multiple strategic transfers (which is said to be another way of saying failure). Later, the senior editor "Eshi Yi" of Tianya Blogs recommended my articles many times, making me quite happy. My only reason is that this sufficiently proves that my previous feeling of inadequacy was completely false, my articles aren't that bad. During this period, some of my articles were reprinted by some websites or print media. Although I don't expect my insignificant words to change society, as a person, I can at least voice my own opinion. For a small person, having a place to speak and someone to listen is already quite lucky, I think.

My family is an ordinary three-person household. My spouse is a doctor, but had to resign from her formal job to solve the problem of living apart and found a temporary job in the city where I live. Of course, this temporary job is definitely not the kind of temporary worker who takes the blame in critical moments. Unfortunately, before I could fully enjoy the happiness of our reunion, another event occurred that made me have to bear the burden alone. It was during National Day. My spouse was diagnosed with cancer. Hospitalization, surgery, biopsy, treatment, and seven days later she was discharged. Cancer completely shattered my spouse's courage to continue living, as if an uncertain future day might mark the end of life. While this mental pain tormented my spouse, I bore the other kind of pain - alleviating her mental suffering. Time passed slowly, and my spouse gradually accepted this harsh reality, taking medicine, undergoing examinations, and tests according to the doctor's instructions, and her mood gradually improved. Death has become the topic we discuss most often while lying in bed. I don't know how to drive away the black shadow hovering over our heads in the night.

Our child has become the spiritual pillar supporting the continuation of our entire life. We寄托all our hopes on her, hoping she grows up well. We chose a very good local school for her, despite the high price, we don't care; we enrolled her in essay classes and dance classes, although it's hard, she doesn't feel tired, very proactive, never needing us to motivate her, she remembers her class schedule clearly. Her growth is about cultivating interests, she likes it, we won't stop her, just like dancing, an eight-year-old child always hurts her waist, we tried to persuade her to stop, but she didn't want to. Yes, children dancing not only improves their figure but also allows them to appear on TV from time to time, their hearts may be blooming with joy.

Take a look at the time, in less than an hour, 2011 will be gone forever. I can't hold back the steps of time, nor can I stop the arrival of dawn. Ordinary life is like this, year after year, day after day, showing the true colors of life in the midst of plainness. Tomorrow I still have to go to work, so I can only write until here. After all, at the beginning of the new year, we can't greet the new year with a listless attitude.

Wish everyone a Happy New Year!
