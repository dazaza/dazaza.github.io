Practice has proven that during evacuation in thick smoke from a fire, covering your mouth and nose with a towel and quickly rushing out of the fire scene can lead to self-rescue. Experiments have shown that the more layers a towel is folded, the better its smoke-removal effect. A towel folded 8 layers has a smoke-removal rate of about 60%. The smoke-removal rate of towels with different humidity levels also varies; the higher the water content, the greater the smoke-removal rate. When the water content reaches 3.3 times the weight of the towel, the human tolerance time is around 1 minute and 20 seconds.
