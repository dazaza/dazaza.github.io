Why Unmarried Women Can Also Suffer from Ovarian Cysts

Ovarian cysts are a type of broad-spectrum ovarian tumor and are common tumors in the female reproductive system, with various natures and forms. Many women believe that ovarian cysts only affect married women and that unmarried women, especially teenagers, cannot get them. In response to this phenomenon, gynecological experts point out that ovarian cysts are not exclusive to married women. In fact, ovarian cysts can occur at any age, but they are most common in women aged 20-50. Moreover, clinical data shows that the proportion of unmarried women affected by ovarian cysts is increasing.

So, why do unmarried women also suffer from ovarian cysts?

Experts explain: The cause of ovarian cysts is currently unclear, but experts believe that hormonal imbalances in women, leading to premature puberty due to estrogen, may be a contributing factor for unmarried women developing ovarian cysts.

Clinical findings suggest that the causes of ovarian cysts in women are related to the following factors:

[1] Long-term poor dietary habits, unhealthy lifestyles, excessive psychological stress, causing excessive acidification of the body, leading to a decline in overall bodily functions. This results in kidney deficiency, as liver and kidney share the same origin; when the kidney is weak, so is the liver. This further slows down the metabolic cycle of the lower abdomen, leading to ovarian diseases and endocrine disorders, decreased immune function, and abnormal proliferation of ovarian tissue, eventually resulting in ovarian cysts.

[2] Body acidification due to other types of cancerous changes, combined with reduced bodily functions and acidic tissue fluids, leads to ovarian cysts. Acidic body fluids place cells in an acidic environment, reducing their oxygen-carrying capacity, decreasing cell activity, and slowing down metabolic cycles. When this drops to 65% of normal levels, normal cells cannot survive. However, some cells may undergo chromosomal mutations to adapt, changing their phenotype and expressing tumorous characteristics. These cells rapidly proliferate, forming true tumor masses.

Can ovarian cysts be prevented?

Experts explain: Ovarian cysts can be prevented. This requires women to undergo regular gynecological examinations for early detection, diagnosis, and treatment.

Generally, during menstruation and postpartum periods, women should pay special attention to rest, avoid sexual intercourse, maintain cleanliness of the external genitalia and vagina, keep a stable and cheerful mood, reduce various competitive pressures in life, avoid excessive worry or anger, learn self-regulation, stay warm, avoid cold exposure, refrain from activities like swimming in cold water, and balance work and rest. A nutritious diet rich in light and easily digestible foods is recommended, avoiding raw or overly stimulating foods. Maintaining a robust immune system, ensuring smooth blood circulation, and keeping both physical and mental health intact are crucial.
