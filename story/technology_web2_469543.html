Police raid fake goods den: a house of over 10 square meters has three secret doors

by zzf000zxye0 on 2011-08-02 16:34:08

Comic

Shao Bo

Walking into a small house of more than 10 square meters on Xinle Road, a police officer from the Hunan Police Station of Xuhui Public Security Bureau said it was like walking through a maze, with winding secret passages and one hidden door after another. "At its peak, a tiny room had three hidden doors. Now, when police officers enter a house, they first observe the structure of the house and measure the size of the room by their stride. If anything unusual is detected, there must be some 'trick'!" Officer Hu from the Hunan Police Station told reporters, "In addition to using new investigation methods to deal with counterfeit dens, the frequency of joint crackdowns by the police and the industry and commerce departments has also increased, greatly raising the cost of illegal activities for counterfeiters."

The "counter-investigation" techniques of counterfeit dens have become increasingly sophisticated.

How to completely eradicate the counterfeit dens around the former Xiangyang Road Clothing Market? This has become an issue that the public security and industry and commerce departments carefully study and resolve. The bosses and scalpers of the counterfeit dens have long mastered a set of "counter-investigation" skills. Once they detect law enforcement personnel or vehicles, they immediately send mass text messages to alert each other. "These dens all have 'escape windows'. Within a few minutes, they can abandon their goods and flee. Once we rushed into a den and found that the tea in the cup on the table was still hot!" Officer Hu said that the boss would rather abandon goods worth tens of thousands of yuan to protect themselves because this money could be earned back quickly. Nowadays, most of the bosses of these dens remain 'hidden', and the people manning the counters are hired workers.

The "equipment" inside the black dens has become "professionalized".

Officer Hu said that after the relocation of the Xiangyang Road Clothing Market in 2006, the police have never reduced their efforts to crack down on counterfeiting. However, as the rectification efforts intensified, the "equipment" inside the black dens became increasingly "precise". Yesterday, I discovered at a counterfeit warehouse on Shaanxi South Road that the door was electric, just like the anti-theft doors in banks. After the law enforcement officers pushed open a hidden door behind the shelves, they found another hidden door disguised as a fitting mirror. Entering the hidden room, the shelves were empty. Just as everyone was preparing to retreat, an officer suddenly noticed that the ceiling was unusually low, and upon opening it, they discovered a hidden space filled with a large number of counterfeit branded products.

"High-frequency" crackdowns make counterfeit dens unprofitable.

I learned that although scalpers could earn a forty percent commission for helping the boss secure a deal, the profit from selling counterfeit goods was still quite substantial for the boss. "Under the current circumstances, we can only adopt 'high-frequency' and 'high-density' crackdowns to ensure that counterfeiters cannot make money!" Officer Hu said that usually, once a counterfeit den is dismantled, the boss's business for the month is ruined. Now, the police and industry and commerce departments conduct joint law enforcement, implementing a long-term mechanism. They firmly crack down on any counterfeit dens they discover without hesitation. Officer Hu said: "Now, we have 'observation posts' outside every day." He pulled out a set of data showing that since August 5th, the Xuhui police force has organized multiple operations involving economic crime investigators, public security officers, and police station personnel, working together with the industry and commerce department to carry out rectification actions. They have shut down 16 counterfeit dens in the surrounding areas of the former Xiangyang Road market and confiscated 8,588 related items. On August 22nd alone, they shut down six dens and confiscated 3,260 counterfeit items, administratively detaining 14 people. These numbers demonstrate the determination of the law enforcement departments.
