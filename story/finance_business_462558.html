3. The Lost Angel

Luozi lit another cigarette, took a deep puff, and then exhaled slowly, watching the smoke collide with his computer screen before dispersing.

Luozi enjoyed this local Chengdu brand of cigarettes for its mild tobacco flavor and the thin mist it produced when smoked. He was quite satisfied with these cigarettes called Jiaozi, which were very popular in Chengdu, just as everyone in Chengdu loved the giant panda, people also favored this cigarette with a picture of a panda on its packaging.

What would a panda smoking look like? Luozi often found himself lost in random thoughts.

Luozi is 26 years old. Counting on his fingers, he realized that it had been four years since he graduated from university. He worked at a company with a nine-to-five job. Every day at 7:30 AM, he woke up, left home at 8:00, ate two liang of pork rib noodles at a small noodle shop outside, and then rode his creaky bicycle to work. At noon, he would have a meal at the company, continue working, and at 5 PM, he would ride his bike back home with a jingle.

Day after day, Luozi dressed simply yet appropriately, wore a plain tie, and always carried a faint smile on his face. In everyone's eyes, Luozi was always a happy young man with a baby face and a hint of scholarly air.

However, there was another side of Luozi that no one knew about, not even his parents.

This Luozi sometimes wore a steel helmet and charged through gunfire with a machine gun in hand; other times he donned armor, riding a steed across vast grasslands; still other times, he held a magic staff filled with holy power, weaving through dark dungeons with a faint scent of herbs clinging to him.

This Luozi existed within the network.

Luozi loved the internet and games, so naturally, he loved online games.

Luozi once became deeply obsessed with an online game that continues to captivate countless people to this day. The name of the game was simple - just two letters - UO. In UO, Luozi's character was named Jack, a PK player with a red name. He wore heavy armor, his helmet covering his face, leaving only a pair of bright eyes that vigilantly observed the surroundings.

In reality, Luozi was actually a kind-hearted person, but painful experiences turned him into a cold-blooded and lonely killer. Everyone in UO called him the Silent Killer Jack. Just as Luozi roamed like a ferocious lone wolf in the forest, a girl appeared.

She was a girl whose voice carried the color of the sky, soothing Luozi's heart that was full of wounds and deep hatred. Her name was Qing.

I keep thinking of Qing, even after leaving UO for so long, I still frequently recall her. Luozi bitterly smiled, shook his head, and stubbed out his cigarette. When he shifted his gaze back to the screen, he instinctively gripped the mouse tightly. There was someone on the screen, his name was Starry Sky.

Since Wang Kai had fired him and issued a bounty on his head, Luozi knew this moment would come.

Luozi laughed, "What needs to come, finally has."

"Why did you do it?" Starry Sky asked coldly.

"You mean the elf I killed? It was simple, he deserved to die."

"Reason?"

"I've already said it, he - deserved to die!"

"You should know the oath and rules of the Blood Alliance."

"I know."

"You should also know the consequences of breaking the rules."

"I know."

Starry Sky took a deep breath, "Sorry, Luozi."

Luozi chuckled, "Don't say sorry, Starry Sky, you're not wrong, neither am I."

Starry Sky stood quietly, his sword glowing with a brilliant light.

Luozi smiled faintly, slowly raising his right hand, pale blue lightning crackling at his fingertips.

Extreme Path Lightning!

Starry Sky moved,

Person moves,

Sword moves,

Luozi didn't move.

The sharp blade cut from Luozi's right shoulder all the way down to his chest, deeply embedding itself into Luozi's body.

"Why didn't you fight back? Weren't you preparing to cast Extreme Path Lightning?"

"Isn't this better? I've repaid everything I owed you, we're even." Luozi slowly fell, a faint smile lingering on his lips.

When Luozi resurrected again in town, he knew that Luozi was no longer the same Luozi from the Angel Tribe. A new life had begun.

He sent Starry Sky a final private message: "Starry Sky, thank you."

Luozi met Starry Sky several months ago, when Luozi had just started playing Lineage. As a mage, the early days were extremely painful, not just because of his own fragility, but mainly due to the cruelty of others. Killing frail mages seemed to be some people's form of entertainment, and Luozi died multiple times in malicious Player vs Player (PvP) battles. Every time he practiced skills in the wild, his index finger on his left hand was always on the shortcut key for the return scroll. But even the city wasn't a safe haven, as powerful monsters were often lured there by malicious players. Luozi suffered endlessly in a cycle of death, resurrection, more death, and more resurrection.

Next to his computer, Luozi kept a piece of paper and a pen. Every time he died, he would jot down the name of the person who killed him. Among the names, the word "Myth" appeared most frequently. Day by day, Luozi silently endured, and his hatred for Myth grew stronger.

Until he met Starry Sky.

That day, Luozi was practicing in Kent's wilderness and had just dropped a spellbook from a werewolf. Before he could pick it up, suddenly his vision went black—Blind Spell! Someone, a mage, had attacked him. With no return scrolls left, Luozi waited for death in the darkness.

But nothing happened. After the Blind Spell wore off, Luozi found a knight standing next to him, with the corpse of a Myth mage underfoot. The knight's name was Starry Sky, and his title was "Angel of Justice."

"This book belongs to you," Starry Sky picked up the spellbook and handed it to Luozi, his face beaming with a radiant smile as he bid farewell to Atlantis.

From the moment Luozi saw Starry Sky's smile, he decided that Starry Sky was his leader.

In the days that followed, with Starry Sky's help, Luozi quickly grew stronger, becoming a powerful mage, and successfully joined the Angel Tribe.

In the first war in Lineage, Luozi and Starry Sky fought side by side, making great contributions to the Angel Tribe in capturing Kent.

Then came the war against Myth. Together with Starry Sky, they battled across various continents and islands in Lineage, turning many Myth knights and mages into corpses. During that time, Luozi felt especially exhilarated, a joy that had been suppressed for too long suddenly bursting forth.

But ever since meeting Dongdong in the cave at Haiyin, Starry Sky changed—he stopped killing Myth members. Luozi had to act alone, but without Starry Sky's protection, even the strongest mage couldn't match up to a knight. The situation changed, and Luozi instead often became a victim under the swords of Myth knights. This further intensified Luozi's hatred towards Myth.

In the end, Luozi not only killed members of Myth, but also those associated with them. Those few members of the Money Gang were killed simply because they were with Myth members.

As for that elf from the Angel Tribe, Luozi originally didn't intend to kill him. By chance, Luozi saw him trading with a Myth member, haggling over a piece of armor. Luozi swiftly dealt with the Myth individual with a few spells and was about to leave when the elf from the Angel Tribe shot an arrow at Luozi. This arrow didn't harm Luozi, but instead cost the elf his life.

I won't hold grudges if you trade with Myth members, but how dare you shoot me? Luozi thought angrily afterward.

Finally, it came to the showdown with Starry Sky.

The newly started Luozi soon joined the Black and White Blood Alliance, a blood alliance formed by players who had migrated from UO, previously known as the Black and White Guild in UO, where all members were old friends from UO. What made Luozi feel regretful was that shortly after joining, the Black and White Blood Alliance withdrew all their knights from the frontlines at Haiyin to battle Myth, because the Black and White faced a larger crisis.

That was the war between the Black and White Blood Alliance and the Money Gang—a civil war among Chinese players. No one knew the cause of the war, initially starting as small-scale conflicts between individuals from the two blood alliances, then escalating into chaotic battles between subordinate factions, eventually evolving into a full-scale war.

At the start, both kings wanted to stop the conflict, neither wanting to be the first to spark a civil war. They held negotiations, but somehow, the talks turned into debates, then into insults and personal attacks, leading to mutual accusations involving family members. In anger, both kings declared war.

Because it was a civil war, compounded by the excessive exploitation by Qiyan and Fengmu, all other players watched the events unfold with indifference, even feeling a sense of schadenfreude.

To quickly defeat the opponent and end the war, both sides recalled their forces from the frontline at Haiyin. However, contrary to expectations, the war did not end quickly but rather evolved into a protracted one. The war caused immense damage to both blood alliances, nearly depleting all the money and equipment in their treasuries.

What worried Luozi most was that the biggest beneficiary of this war was the Myth Blood Alliance. It finally managed to catch its breath from being besieged by several major Chinese blood alliances and during the internal war between the Money Gang and the Black and White Blood Alliance, focused on saving money and stockpiling strategic supplies, forming more sub-factions, making the Myth Blood Alliance even stronger.

The worst-case scenario finally happened. On June 17 at 8:00 PM, the Myth Blood Alliance launched an attack on the Fengmu Castle.

Although the Black and White Blood Alliance prepared for the siege battle, prolonged internal warfare forced the Black and White knights to fight with inferior weapons and equipment against the well-armed Myth knights.

The battle was intense, even brutal.

The Black and White knights lined up in neat phalanxes, falling row by row under the barrage of powerful magic and arrows. After firing their last arrows, the Black and White elven archers drew their swords and bravely charged forward, only to fall one after another.

The Black and White warriors expressed their final loyalty to the Black and White Blood Alliance with their blood and lives.

At 9:10 PM, the Myth broke through the gates of Fengmu and stormed into the castle. Intense street fighting ensued, the dazzling magic lighting up the entire castle brilliantly. The Black and White mages desperately cast powerful offensive spells. Myth knights fell, but more surged forward. Without the protection of knights, mages were extremely vulnerable, yet not a single Black and White mage fled. Each mage continued chanting spells even as they fell.

In the end, only one mage remained, his name was Luozi.

He stood alone, his pristine mage robe stained red with blood, his disheveled hair fluttering in the wind, while his raised magic staff emitted a brilliant light. The sounds of war drums and cries of battle could not drown out his soft yet determined voice.

"Divine ruler of all things, your faithful servant pleads with you, transform your holy power into scorching flames, reducing our enemies to ashes!"

Fierce flames burned, turning the Myth knights into ashes. But more Myth knights poured in like an unending tide.

Luozi fell, and in his final moments, he saw the wide-open gates and the last remaining few Black and White knights fighting fearlessly yet hopelessly.

We failed!

Luozi closed his eyes.

I am an angel,

A lost angel,

I fly alone,

My wings behind me

Emitting a faint glow,

I will use my meager strength

To defend the honor of the angels,

Because

I have a proud heart.

-Luozi
