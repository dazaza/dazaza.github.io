For the comprehensive training in the first phase, I recommend that children and parents clearly understand their current English status and conduct targeted training accordingly. For children whose English scores are consistently above 115, based on fully understanding the first and second mock exam papers, they should appropriately do some college entrance exam cloze tests and reading comprehensions to play a role in improving towards a perfect score. For children currently scoring between 108-115, they must focus on thoroughly understanding every knowledge point in the first and second mock exams, reaching a level of familiarity with similar knowledge points. For children scoring between 100-108, we must ensure "shallow water areas" (including multiple-choice, listening, reading comprehension A/B sections, and the first four questions of task-based reading) achieve full marks, and try to get high scores in "deep water areas" (cloze tests, reading comprehension C/D sections, the fifth question of task-based reading, and essays). If the child's score is below 100, it is necessary to find past high school entrance exam questions and first mock exam questions and seriously repeat the training until ensuring no similar mistakes are made.
