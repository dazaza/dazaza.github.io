The Love View of the Poor Boy and the Rich Second Generation

by lili1 on 2011-12-22 15:55:18

Preface [Introduction]

Whether or not to be in a relationship is not important; what matters is whether you have the ability to pay for it! Don't do things that exceed your budget if you don't have money, because no one is willing to endure hardships with you from the very beginning. Even if there is genuine love, it may not necessarily fall upon you...

The poor young man is too naive, only knowing how to love sincerely. The result is that sincerity alone cannot change this harsh reality. Reality is that he cannot yet meet the material needs of others. Nowadays, there are very few people who would wait for a promise that seems so far away...

The rich second-generation is smart and knows how to use money to express love because no one will spend their entire life on a "sincere promise" that lasts only three minutes. It still comes down to whether there's cash or not! The mother-in-law understands life better, and relatives are more adept at persuasion: "Where else can you find someone as good as him? If you follow him, you won't have to... you won't have to... you can... you can..." A person without strong opinions can hardly withstand such repeated guidance and persuasion, thus becoming even less decisive. They firmly believe that family and loved ones would never harm them, and as for whether they like each other or not, that can be decided later! Of course, the outcome is predictable, somewhat dramatic. Don't assume that everything in TV dramas is fake; it's all art derived from life but elevated above it!

The poor young man wants to change his fate, despite destiny always working against him. Fortunately, his stubborn nature has taught him the importance of persistence, so he continues to pursue relentlessly.

The rich second-generation is content with the status quo, enjoying everything given by others, taking all this seemingly due entitlement. As for effort, his parents have already put in all the effort needed. Why waste energy when it's unnecessary, in his view?

Ending [A Shout for Love]

Poor Young Man: Those prettier than me, those uglier than me, have all fallen in love; those older than me, those younger than me, have all gotten married! Oh God, why have you left me behind? Have you forgotten about me!?

Rich Second-Generation: Women are like chewing gum; once they lose their flavor, spit them out! O(∩_∩)O Haha~...
