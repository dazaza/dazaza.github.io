Two people were sentenced for borrowing huge sums to register a guarantee company and then embezzling millions in capital contributions.

by xue94fwsh on 2012-03-03 15:36:34

Xinhua News Agency, Nanjing, January 31st (reported by Liu Weiwei) - What to do if you don't have the money to start a company? You can borrow it, but under no circumstances should you repeat the mistakes of these two defendants. The People's Court of Zhangjiagang City, Jiangsu Province, sentenced defendant Chen Mou to two years and ten months in prison, suspended for three years, and defendant Pang Mou to two years and six months in prison, also suspended for three years, along with a fine of 2 million yuan each.

In 2009, seeing the booming business of non-bank financial institutions, Chen Xiuzhen and Pang Laishu conceived the idea of starting a guarantee company, but due to insufficient funds, they borrowed 4 million yuan and applied to establish Suzhou Jinzhou Guarantee Company in April of the same year, with a registered capital of 20 million yuan. On April 14th, Jinzhou Guarantee Company was approved by the industrial and commercial department, and after obtaining the opening account license, the two immediately transferred the initial 4 million yuan to Pang's account for repayment. They then borrowed more than 15 million yuan to increase the registered capital, and after each verification of capital, they transferred the money from the company's account to repay the loans.

In February 2010, Chen and Pang decided to increase the registered capital of Jinzhou Guarantee Company to 50 million yuan, so they approached their friend Yao who helped them borrow 28 million yuan through bank transfers. After passing the capital verification smoothly, they again transferred the 28 million yuan from the company's account to repay Yao.

After investigation by the court, it was found that after the establishment of Jinzhou Guarantee Company, Chen and Pang successively withdrew more than 40 million yuan from the company's registered capital to repay personal loans.

The presiding judge stated that the defendants Chen and Pang violated the provisions of the Company Law by withdrawing capital after the company's establishment, and the amount involved was enormous. Their actions constituted the crime of withdrawing capital, making it a joint crime. Based on the criminal circumstances, remorseful expressions, and the harm caused by the two defendants, they met the conditions for the application of probation, hence the announcement of probation for both.

In recent years, with the prosperity and development of the market economy, companies such as shell companies or empty-box companies have emerged frequently. The phenomenon of shareholders borrowing funds for capital contributions and then transferring the funds out after verification for repayment has occurred from time to time. The judge reminded that enterprise registered capital should be used for the operation and development of the company, and during the company's existence, its actual assets should remain at a certain level. For this reason, China's Company Law clearly stipulates that shareholders shall not withdraw capital; otherwise, they will be penalized or held criminally liable.
