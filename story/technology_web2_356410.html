The magnificent transformation: from a dormant volcanic rock desert to a world-class resort.

About 10,000 years ago, the volcanic cluster in Qiongbei, Hainan erupted, with molten lava flowing unimpededly for miles. Over time, this area formed an expansive and mysterious black rock geological relic.

Yangshan, located just a short distance from the center of Haikou, the capital city of Hainan Province, is deep within a region of volcanic rock deserts that have existed for thousands of years. The land here consists largely of volcanic stone rubble, with an average topsoil layer of only 5 centimeters, making it a typical tropical rock desert unsuitable for traditional agriculture. The barren land and water scarcity have long constrained local economic development.

Mission Hills • Haikou (Mission Hills • Haikou) was constructed on such a rocky desert through land reclamation and ecological construction. Inheriting the essence of the world's largest golf brand, Mission Hills, the resort integrates sports, competitions, health care, wellness, culture, entertainment, cuisine, business, exhibitions, training, and residential living into a comprehensive high-end leisure industry hub, becoming a new landmark for tourism and leisure in Haikou.

Turning wilderness into magic: the birth of the volcanic rock golf course.

For 500 years, the allure of golf courses has lain in transforming unique terrains into wonders; the creation of classic golf courses started from early seaside marshes and has since appeared on hills, wild mountains, deserts, and sandy lands. However, for centuries, few dared to build large-scale golf courses on exposed black volcanic rocks.

Now, a legendary masterpiece has emerged. The world's largest Mission Hills golf club presents a distinctively characterized volcanic rock golf course, marking a new elevation in the fashion of world golf history.

Surrounded by rugged, raw black bare rocks, with boundless horizons, lush ancient lychee trees, low and simple stone walls, and underground springs bubbling beneath, the Mission Hills volcanic rock golf course exudes vastness, desolation, and natural textures. Set amidst jagged rocks and towering cliffs, it feels as if one is standing on the moon, inducing a "weightless" sense of relaxation.

The dream of golfers is to challenge courses of various styles around the world. After conquering seaside and mountain courses, it's now time to challenge the distinctly styled volcanic rock courses.

Between heaven and earth, hitting a small white ball several kilometers away, does this ultimate experience feel like what astronaut Alan Shepard felt when he swung a club on the moon?
