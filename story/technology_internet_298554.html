Track hotspots - study the flow of dominant market funds - gain insights into the dominant market operation direction - closely follow the dominant market trading steps.

by 51hicsb on 2009-12-08 01:06:53

From the perspective of sector capital flow, the top three sectors with net capital inflow today are: banking, securities, and insurance sectors, with their respective net inflows amounting to 29.25 billion yuan, 23.18 billion yuan, and 19.35 billion yuan. The leading stocks in these sectors include CITIC Bank, Haitong Securities, and China Pacific Insurance. The top three sectors with net capital outflow are: pharmaceuticals, electronic information, and brewing & food sectors, with their respective net outflows at 20.98 billion yuan, 15 billion yuan, and 11.41 billion yuan.

Investment strategy and advice: Judging from today's index performance, the market continues to rise. The Shanghai Composite Index has once again approached its previous high. It seems that the bullish force is dominant, and the market may continue to rise. However, considering the majority of stocks fell today, this situation is still concerning. Investors have reason to believe that the main force might adopt the following method: switching hotspots, allowing the previously lagging large-cap blue-chip stocks to continue rising and driving the index upward continuously. Meanwhile, stocks that have risen significantly in the previous period may enter a phase of adjustment. Based solely on one day of good performance by large-cap blue-chip stocks, it might be insufficient evidence to determine a shift in hotspots. From a cautious perspective, it would be better to observe for a few more days before making a judgment. Reviewing the market conditions in the first half of the year, after entering June, due to the phased adjustment of theme stocks, the weighted blue-chip sectors represented by financial real estate, coal, and non-ferrous metals once directed a phase of accelerated growth. The current market stage is similar to the situation around June, and it cannot be ruled out that the market may replicate the accelerated growth trend dominated by blue-chip sectors in June and July. Therefore, in terms of strategy, investors should closely monitor the hotspot trends and adapt to the transformation of market styles.

On December 3rd, at the Fifth China Futures Conference held in Shenzhen, former CSRC Chairman Zhou Daoyong called for accelerating the introduction of the Futures Law and stated that "stock index futures will soon be launched." Simultaneously, Song Liping, General Manager of the Shenzhen Stock Exchange, also revealed at the Eighth China Securities Investment Fund Forum the day before that stock index futures and margin trading were ready. This has heightened market expectations for the launch of stock index futures and margin trading. Could this bring a trading opportunity for the long-dormant weight benchmark stocks and related beneficiary sectors? According to relevant data, funds flowing into the market in the past two months mainly come from the relocation of small and medium-sized investors' savings. Public mutual funds may have limited capacity to increase positions due to redemption pressures from insurance companies and other institutions, while private funds or scaled-up speculative funds guide the exploration of market hotspots. This could be an important reason for the prolonged dormancy of benchmark stocks at this stage. Then, under the background that "stock index futures and margin trading are ready," will the market style and hotspot transformation tend towards large-cap benchmark stocks? This requires careful consideration. Given the current market pattern, the round-robin hype of theme stocks has reached its climax, with valuations severely inflated. Although some themes have become obviously "aged," as various speculative funds are deeply involved, it seems not yet time for them to "fade away." At this moment, if the rally of benchmark stocks suddenly starts, it could likely cause a change in short-term active fund flows, forming a "20-80 phenomenon," and the final result may not necessarily lead the market to a new high for the year. From the market performance, individual stocks like Haitong Securities, Daheng Technology, and Zhongda Shares have been relatively active recently. Despite the activity of these stocks possibly being supported by other themes, the expectation of the upcoming launch of stock index futures and margin trading can clearly serve as a "catalyst" for market speculation. Especially in the securities sector, according to statistics, as of the end of November, the total turnover of stocks in the Shanghai and Shenzhen markets reached 48.33 trillion yuan, surpassing the historical highest level of the entire year of 2007. This year, brokers' transaction commissions may break the 2007 level, reaching or approaching a historical record. Hence, the revenue growth of securities stocks or partially participating securities-related stocks in 2009 can be anticipated. At the turn of the year, future market hotspots are highly likely to transform towards directions of revenue growth and high dividend expectations. Medium-term investments in low-valued securities stocks could yield substantial returns. From the Soufu Capital Monitoring Platform analysis, Southwest Securities (600369), Nanjing Gaoko (600064), and Changcheng Information (000748) show relatively significant net capital inflows over 1 and 3 days, and they can be kept under attention when prices are low.
