Treating ovarian cysts under hysteroscopy:

If the ovarian cyst is located under the uterine mucosa, medically referred to as a submucosal ovarian cyst, this type of cyst is suitable for removal using hysteroscopic electrocautery. Hysteroscopic surgery involves inserting a long, thin medical endoscope through the natural channel (the cervical opening) into the uterine cavity to perform the surgery. This method avoids any damage to the abdominal wall and features minimal trauma, short operation time, less bleeding, and quick postoperative recovery.

Treating uterine fibroids under laparoscopy:

This procedure requires only a few 3mm incisions in the abdomen. It offers advantages such as minimal trauma, fast recovery, the ability to get out of bed the same day, full recovery within 3-5 days, and no impact on physiological functions. Laparoscopic technology not only provides precise diagnosis and treatment but also helps separate adhesions, aids in pregnancy, ensures surgical safety, and prevents recurrence. It is widely adopted in international gynecological diagnostic and surgical procedures.
