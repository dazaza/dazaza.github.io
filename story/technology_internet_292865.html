(10) Before you kiss me —— the rich boy falls in love with me, a young widow struggling at the bottom. Those who struggle at the bottom are often kind-hearted and dedicated people, while the cunning and smart ones are more likely to seize opportunities to rise rapidly. Because in this society, there are far more people who look at appearances than those who look at the inner qualities. This is true in romance, and also in the workplace.

I left home earlier, but there was still traffic on the road. I thought about it and remembered that if I crossed a train crossing, I could directly reach Yang Guofeng's investment company building. So I abandoned my car and walked.

Breathless all the way, when I reached the train crossing, there were always few pedestrians here, only two lonely railway tracks extending to the horizon. Below the slope of the railway, lush and dense weeds grew wildly. Under the sunlight, a girl in a yellow T-shirt and jeans suddenly appeared walking unsteadily on one of the tracks, her posture swaying as if swaying with her mood.

The girl stretched out her hands horizontally, indulging in her own world.

The reason I noticed her was that I saw her exquisite small bag thrown on the grass.

What kind of person would be so careless as to abandon their own handbag?

I became alert. From afar, I saw a train already roaring and rushing towards us, but the girl, with her back to the train, kept looking down, unmoved.

In an instant, I started trembling again. Whenever I encountered an emergency, I couldn't help but tremble, a problem I hadn't been able to get rid of for years. I shouted loudly: "The train is coming, get out of the way!"

The girl continued to walk leisurely on the track like a bird maintaining its balance...

The wind blew her long hair violently, and under the harsh sunlight, I shivered involuntarily. Without time to think, I leaped forward and grabbed the girl forcefully.

The girl struggled fiercely. I lost control and slapped her hard. Taking advantage of her momentary confusion after being struck, I finally pulled her away from the track. As soon as we were three meters away from the track, the train roared past.

My legs went weak, and I fell into the grass, unable to stand up.

That girl looked at the departing train, then turned to look at me, somewhat angrily saying: "Why did you hit me? My mom has never hit me!"
