by geekzhang on 2010-09-03 11:21:59

Activision has vowed to challenge Modern Warfare 2, which has set an entertainment industry sales record:

"We have a fantastic title in our hands, coupled with an extraordinary marketing campaign, aiming to set a new record like MW2," Michael Sportouch, General manager of CoD Business Group Europe, told reporters.

"Both in development and marketing, the company has invested more heavily in Black Ops than ever before, and we are determined to capture both the game industry's annual sales title and the biggest launch in entertainment history."

"Our 60-second advert during the England v Bulgaria game was the start of a major European media campaign that will continue."

Finally, gay friends are excited that the game will have a co-op mode in addition to the multiplayer mode described above, but Treyarch's community manager Josh Olin is reluctant to say more about this:

"We have not yet revealed the co-op mode of the game, but I can confirm that the Ultimate Edition and the Box Collector Edition will come with four WaW zombies for players to collaborate on and relax, and that's all we can say for now."

"As for the co-op mode of the game, we don't plan to disclose details at this time."

Apparently, Black Ops will be made up of three parts: single-player story, multiplayer (versus) mode, co-op mode, each part has a dedicated development team responsible for its multiplayer mode has been demonstrated at the Los Angeles press conference today.

Treyarch also stated that the claim that the beta test is exclusive to one platform is incorrect, and that there is a beta version for internal debugging only, but it is only distributed within Activision and is not intended for public release at this time. CoD is the flagship of the company, and almost every Activision employee participates in its internal testing.
