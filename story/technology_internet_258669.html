Embezzlement and Bribery of 5000 Yuan Not a Crime? Should the Threshold for Embezzlement and Bribery Crimes Be Adjusted?

By Xu Shuguang, Chairman of GreenTree Hotels and 519.com Wine Direct Sales Network

Zhang Jun, Vice President of the Supreme People's Court in charge of national criminal trials, recently suggested adjusting the threshold for embezzlement and bribery crimes and extending the sentences for serious economic crimes. "The concept of 5000 yuan in 1997 is not the same as 5000 yuan in 2009, but the legislation has not changed since then," Zhang pointed out that in current judicial practice, many cases involving amounts of tens of thousands of yuan are not transferred to the court; once they are transferred, the court must sentence them according to law, "which itself lacks social fairness."

(November 4, Chongqing Evening News)

Recently, Vice President Zhang Jun of the Supreme People's Court proposed adjusting the threshold for embezzlement and bribery crimes, causing a great stir. In recent years, although the country's efforts to combat embezzlement and bribery have increased, we have also seen a fact: corruption has not truly disappeared, instead becoming more rampant, with high incidence and frequency, which the public detests. Therefore, at this time, the suggestion from the Vice President of the Supreme People's Court to "increase the threshold for embezzlement and bribery crimes" touched upon the public's "sensitive" nerve, and opposition was understandable.

If one day the "threshold" is adjusted, embezzlement or bribery of 5000 yuan or even more will no longer be considered a crime. Personally, I believe that embezzlement and bribery should not rise with economic development or because the amount of embezzlement and bribery increases. In theory, embezzlement and bribery do not distinguish between large or small amounts; you took what you should not take, regardless of how much, even if it is only one cent, the nature is still embezzlement and bribery, which is an unchangeable fact. In most countries around the world, there is no specific amount specified for embezzlement and bribery crimes, and "zero tolerance" is a more common choice. However, we add another "threshold", making it seem somewhat superfluous.

From 1979 to 1997, the threshold for embezzlement and bribery crimes gradually increased, rising from an initial 1000 yuan to 5000 yuan, but in actual practice, this "threshold" has been artificially raised in some regions. Once punishment is reduced, the phenomenon of corruption becomes even more rampant, which is an undeniable fact. The legal net is not woven tighter, but rather torn wider, which cannot help but worry people. If once the "small fish" who slip through the net become rampant, how can the people bear it? If we doubt our "net" due to too many such small fish and simply increase the "threshold", "investigate the big ones and release the small ones", allowing "minor corruption" to go unchecked, they will indulge themselves recklessly with侥幸psychology. They will slide further down the path of corruption until they are caught, leading to more corruption over time, worsening the corrupt climate, and causing enormous losses to the state and people's property.

Vice President Zhang Jun of the Supreme People's Court also pointed out that in current judicial practice, many cases involving amounts of tens of thousands of yuan are not transferred to the court; but once they are transferred, the court must sentence them according to law, "which itself lacks social fairness." This unfairness is not directly related to the threshold but is caused by lax enforcement by relevant departments. It is unclear who has given them this right. If the law is merely written on paper, it becomes meaningless. Compromise and retreat of the law before evil will only embolden the audacity of corruption, creating big crooks, with unimaginable consequences. We should eliminate corruption at its inception, rather than blaming the "unfairness" on the "low threshold"! When the "threshold" is raised to tens of thousands or even hundreds of thousands, those below these amounts will not be transferred to the court. Many officials would rejoice, but for our country and the common people, is this fair?

Our GreenTree employees also have supporters and opponents of this proposal. Whether supporting or opposing is not important. What matters is that we must adhere to anti-corruption, nip problems in the bud, start from ourselves, start from every detail, and be a "clean" person.
