Dingzai Network Secures Tens of Millions in Investment to Build a Chinese Version of Instagram

by ybcz0519 on 2011-01-06 17:11:07

This article is simultaneously published on Mobinode. Business media please do not reprint! Previously, I recommended the DingZai photo-taking service through Star Observation. DingZai Photo is a new shooting tool that integrates Lomo, online photo storage, and cross-platform management. The services it provides are similar to Instagram, offering photo-taking, photo processing, and sharing functions.

The main features include:

1. Photos will be automatically saved to the DingZai server, making it more convenient for you to use and share these photos.

2. Free online storage space can store up to 25,600 photos for free.

3. Currently supports: ActionSampler four-grid, Pola, LC-A, Colorsplash, POP9, black and white film.

4. Supports online sharing to Sina Weibo and Renren Network.

5. Supports Simplified Chinese, Traditional Chinese, and English.

DingZai Photo is a product under Shenzhen DingZai Network Technology Co., Ltd. Shenzhen DingZai Network Technology Co., Ltd. is a technology company fully invested by the well-known venture capital company Junwei Group, established in 2008. It was previously focused on the e-commerce field but has now shifted from e-commerce to mobile internet direction. According to the statistics of CVSource, a data product under ChinaVenture Investment Group, in January 2008, JW. Junwei Group invested in Shenzhen Baizhu Network Technology Co., Ltd. (now renamed as Shenzhen DingZai Network Technology Co., Ltd.), with an undisclosed amount. On December 28, 2010, JW. Junwei Group added investment in DingZai Network Technology, with a cumulative investment reaching 10 million RMB, holding 50% of its shares. It is understood that this round of additional capital will mainly be used for the expansion of the R&D team and equipment investment next year, enhancing the intensity and efficiency of product R&D, and expanding international support for products in multiple countries and regions. (Via ChinaVenture)

Compared to PC terminals, mobile devices such as phones have additional functions beyond calls and text messages, one of which is taking pictures. Therefore, photo processing and sharing based on mobile phones is a highly demanded application point. Since its launch this year, Instagram has been widely praised. This is due to its more reasonable application logic design, providing the function of sharing to multiple mainstream social networks simultaneously, offering optional geolocation functions, and providing simple yet easy-to-operate photo editing functions, making photo sharing evolve into a channel for photo processing and distribution. In 2011, the domestic Internet should be an open year, and this openness gives developers of Internet and mobile Internet applications more opportunities. Hopefully, more excellent applications will emerge in the new year domestically.
