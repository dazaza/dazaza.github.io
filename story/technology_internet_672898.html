Manifestations of Depression:

1. Sleep Disorders: Most patients experience sleep disorders. Typically, they fall asleep without difficulty but wake up after a few hours and remain in a depressive mood. They wake up 2 to 3 hours earlier than usual and cannot fall back asleep, thus falling into a sorrowful atmosphere. This is known as morning insomnia, middle-of-the-night awakening, and terminal insomnia.

2. Constant Fatigue: Loss of energy, feeling tired and weak with no obvious reason for continuous fatigue (www.yyz120.net), detecting patients early is essential. Mild cases feel physically exhausted, lacking motivation and enthusiasm for life and work; severe cases may neglect eating, drinking, and personal hygiene altogether.

3. Persistent Despondency: This is the primary characteristic of depression patients, experiencing mild sadness or suicidal tendencies. Patients spend most of their day in despondency (www.syzhiliao.net), almost every day, which can be evidenced in two ways: subjective expression and observation by others.

4. Excessive Self-Deprecation: Viewing oneself critically, negatively, and pessimistically regarding the present, past, and future (www.syzhiliao.com). Intense feelings of guilt, worthlessness, helplessness, and severe cases may involve self-blame, hypochondriacal ideas, or even choosing suicide as a form of self-punishment.

5. Suicidal Thoughts and Actions: This is the most dangerous behavior associated with depression. Severely depressed patients often resort to suicide to escape their pain.
