Yesterday Once More

by adbbs on 2010-03-13 16:07:11

Those bright but slightly sad past memories are always flipped over by us and cherished repeatedly. Then we sigh that the old things are like smoke, fading away with the wind, but some things and some people can never be forgotten. Many years later, I stood on the site after the brilliant flowers had faded, unable to let go for a long time. Just like this, an old melancholy song came to my mind: "How many old stories seem like they were just yesterday, how many friends seem like they're still by your side." Suddenly, I felt that time also has things that cannot be erased. Maybe some things are like wine; the longer it is stored, the more flavor it develops!

Unconsciously, four years have passed. In these four years, countless stories of joy and sorrow have unfolded. The class monitor and that girl have already started their own families. Some of our former classmates have fallen into ruin while others have become wealthy. Everything seems to have changed as if it's a different world. I wonder if he has changed himself to adapt to this society and the people in it, or if he continues to live his life as others used to find amusingly inverted. Some classmates lost contact immediately after leaving school, and even when alone, they could no longer bring up any memories. Even if they met again, they would look at each other in confusion for a long time, then slap their thighs and exclaim, "Oh, it's you!" Yet, he was someone they could remember clearly.

After that, we never met again. Two years later, I unexpectedly met a classmate. That classmate went to the same school as him. He said that he was still the same, often doing things that were hard to understand, and still being laughed at by everyone. Later, I heard that he had graduated and entered a company with several classmates. At first, he was assigned to serve tea, water plants, mop the floor, and was busy all day like a spinning top. Many people couldn't bear it and left, but he was still working there wholeheartedly.

Sometimes, when I'm bored, I think he's a very sincere person. When people ask him for help, he always says, "Sure, sure." Once, several classmates said, "We're graduating, so you treat us to dinner." He happily agreed, only to realize later that he had been tricked. During the dinner, he ordered a few simple dishes. When it was time to settle the bill, he asked the waitress to pack the few peanuts left. The guests who were invited said, "He really embarrassed us completely, and we won't go if he invites us in the future." Actually, his family background was good, and many times in the restaurant, I saw him paying for meals for others. I once subtly reminded him not to always let others freeload off him, but he shook his head and said, "Their family conditions are not good." I smiled, knowing further words would be useless. His home wasn't far from the school, so he returned home every now and then. His hometown was famous for its large dates, which he often shared with everyone. After I graduated, he, due to his excellent grades, and another classmate were admitted to another good school. Before leaving, everyone received a copy of the yearbook, and we parted with mutual reluctance. However, he didn't have a yearbook nor did he leave messages for anyone.

It was also around that time that a handsome guy in our dormitory became infatuated with an ordinary-looking girl, repeatedly getting drunk at the school gate because he couldn't win her heart. We helped him back to the dormitory more than once. Love, what exactly is it that makes people obsessed and drunk with it?

It was also at that time that he told me he liked a girl. Normally, if anyone teased him about girls, he would get red in the face and argue heatedly. This time, I was shocked beyond measure. The girl he liked was also liked by our class monitor, who lived in our dormitory. And at that time, I too had begun to develop feelings for that girl. A close friend knew and said that our dormitory was playing out a Three Kingdoms drama, then sighed about the troubles caused by beautiful women. No one blessed this relationship; it was absolutely hopeless, including the girl herself, who looked down on him from the bottom of her heart. I advised him to let go if he should. But he persisted, becoming more and more entangled, tossing and turning in the dormitory at night unable to sleep, muttering in his sleep, "I miss her." Later, he showed me his diary, which was filled with fervent love for her. Compared to him, my feelings were merely a temporary impulse. Indeed, reality proved it to be so, and I quickly cooled down from this emotion, clearing all caches. At that time, the football field was the sacred place of love in the school. Boys and girls who didn't fully understand love yet marked their territories here for their relationships. She often came here, but he would suddenly appear from behind, throwing a bunch of snacks into her arms and then fleeing in panic. Later, he began writing love letters to her. She complained to a few guys in our dormitory, asking them to remind him to stop writing. One evening, the door of the dormitory slammed shut heavily. Several guys surrounded him. I heard the crisp sound of slaps and his crying voice saying, "I'm sorry." After that day, everything seemed as if nothing had happened, calm and peaceful. The sun rose as brightly as ever, but it was so dazzling and blinding!
