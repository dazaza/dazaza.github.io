If you're having a hard time deciding which format to use for your golf tournament, there are several factors to consider. Some formats are casual and fun, while others are formal and serious for true competitors. The Scramble is the most common format when organizing a golf tournament with teams. This allows your tournament to have 2, 3, or 4-person teams. In this type of play, team members choose the best shot from each stroke. After selecting the best stroke, all players will then hit their ball from that position. There are various types of Scramble games people enjoy, including Texas Scramble, Florida Scramble, and Ambrose.

If you choose the Best Ball format for your golf tournament, everyone plays their own ball at every hole. This is also a team game but each player hits their own ball. After completing each hole, the lowest score of the team serves as the team score. Best Ball is also known as Four Ball. Variations include 1-2-3 Best Ball. This format is favored because people like hitting their own balls.

Alternate shot is a format for a golf tournament allowing two-person teams. This is sometimes called a Foursome game. In an alternate shot, two players on a team alternate hitting shots from the tee. One ball is used between the two team members. One player hits the ball off the tee, and they take turns until the ball is in the hole. Alternating occurs between shots and off the tees. This may result in the same person having the final shot in the hole and the next shot off the tee if they don't tee off on that particular hole. Other games that involve alternate shots include odds and evens and Scotch Foursomes.

The Modified Stableford can be played by teams or individuals. In a Modified Stableford, the highest score wins. The score on each hole is worth a certain amount of points based on par. The more points you have, the higher your rank. A good example of a well-known golf tournament in the Modified Stableford format is the PGA Tour.

Another format for a golf tournament might be the Chapman, also known as the Pinehurst. This format involves team play with two people per team. Chapman actually combines several formats into one. In an event like this, teammates switch balls after each of their shots at the tee. They select the best ball after their second shot and then play the alternate ball until they make the hole.

Bingo Bango Bongo is one of the most popular formats for a golf tournament. This is the most common game for golf association tournaments and league tournaments. This format rewards players for three specific things on each hole: the first player on the green, the first team to have all players on the green or closest to the green all at once, and the first player to put the golf ball in the hole.

Flags is another format for playing in a golf tournament. This is a common way to play. In this format, all golfers start their round with a set number of strokes related to their handicaps. Each player plays until their strokes run out. The player who can make it the furthest with their amount of strokes wins the game.

The Lone Ranger format of a golf tournament is another team play. This game is also known as Money Ball, Yellow Ball, and Pink Ball. This game assigns ownership of a hole to one player on the team to make the hole. Each team has four players. Players rotate at each hole as the lone ranger. The scores of each player on the team are added up, and the team with the lowest score wins the round of golf.

There are many formats of golf. If you're holding a golf tournament at a specific golf course, you might consider asking around to find out what the people favor the most. Scramble is the most common game to play at a golf tournament.
