Foreign Media Review of Amazon's 2013 Kindle Paperwhite E-Book Reader

by anonymous on 2013-10-10 18:29:02

It has been a month since the official release of the 2013 version of the Kindle Paperwhite e-reader. Foreign media outlets AllThingsD and TheVerge have each published their review articles today. Coincidentally, both media outlets believe that this e-book device is the best choice in its category on the current market; however, one downside is that Amazon still does not include a charger in the original packaging box, and the device lacks a headphone jack, making it unable to play audiobooks.

Below are the key points summarized from the reviews by the two media outlets:

Positive evaluations:

The improvements over the 2012 version are not significant, so for users who are already satisfied with the old version, an upgrade is not necessary. However, overall, the 2013 version of the Kindle Paperwhite, starting at just $119, is absolutely the best e-book device on the market and the top choice among similar products. The screen contrast is sharper than the 2012 version, and the "shadow" issue present in the older version has been resolved in the 2013 model. The Vocabulary Builder — a new feature that creates word cards using a web-based dictionary — is very practical. The 2013 version of the Kindle Paperwhite e-reader features a 6.7" x 4.6" screen, 0.36 inches thick, and weighs 7.3 ounces. The screen size is the same as the 2012 version, but it is 1 ounce lighter. However, in actual comparison, the difference is not noticeable. The Kindle Paperwhite comes with 2GB of storage space, which can store approximately 1000 e-books. At the bottom of the device, there is a power switch, a status indicator light, and a micro-USB charging port, with no other components. The exterior coating feels smooth and has a rubbery texture, giving an overall durable impression. When turning on the Kindle Paperwhite, there will be a row of options in the upper right corner of the screen, including home page, back, light adjustment, settings, and purchase list, etc. During reading comparisons, there will be a more noticeable difference between the 2013 version and the 2012 version. The old version would clearly show "shadows" at the bottom of the screen, but this problem does not exist in the new version. Moreover, the brightness of the new version is sufficient, and the black and white contrast is also sharper. The processor of the 2013 version of the Kindle Paperwhite is faster and stronger, resulting in significantly faster response times when switching screens or searching content. Amazon has added many new features to the new version of the Kindle Paperwhite, with three being particularly surprising: Page Flip (flip pages freely without losing the current reading page), In-Line Footnotes (add footnote information), and Vocabulary Builder (create vocabulary cards). The battery life of the 2013 version of the Kindle Paperwhite is the same as the old version, with the official data stating — if Wi-Fi function is turned off, the device can operate continuously for about two months. In actual testing, after continuous use for five nights, one hour each night, with Wi-Fi turned on, the remaining battery of the Kindle Paperwhite is still over 70%.

Negative evaluations:

The lack of a USB charger in the packaging box is the biggest drawback of the product (an additional purchase costs $15). There is no headphone jack provided, and it does not support audiobooks, etc. Additionally, the so-called "trial version browser" still feels "experimental" in use. The new version of the Kindle Paperwhite still lacks support for a large number of electronic document formats, defaulting to only PDF and Word documents. Without EPUB support, the device basically cannot open any e-book files not purchased from Amazon.
