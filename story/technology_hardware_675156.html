For more than 10 years, the "Sky Bar Grab" project has been one of the key high-altitude projects in Beijing's outdoor training venues. Its unique participatory nature and the profound reflections it brings to participants are unmatched by other projects. When clients inspect the outdoor training base, they often feel an impulse to try it when they see the equipment for the Sky Bar Grab. Among the trainees, this project generates the most discussion, which is sufficient proof of its popularity in Beijing's outdoor training programs.

To cater to the preferences of the masses, outdoor training companies have thoroughly explored and modified the project. For instance, the currently emerging projects such as "Sky Ball Grab" and "Sky Wave" all evolved from this project, but the classic traditional operation method remains the most favored.

In practical operations, the Sky Bar Grab project is quite complex, yet very convenient for participants. The complexity mainly lies in the application of coaches, coaching guidance, and the suspension of high-altitude equipment. First, regarding the application of coaches, since the Sky Bar Grab is a relatively challenging high-altitude project, both before and after the activity, coaches need to have a deep understanding of the project and possess high standards of team culture. This is the basis for testing whether a coach can skillfully master this project. If the coach’s own team level is not outstanding, there will be deviations in understanding the project, leading to an inability to profoundly influence the participants. During the activity, the coach needs to be fully serious and focused, without any slackness, always managing the safety rope in their hands to prevent participants from being in danger. Even so, dangers may still exist, which requires the coach to carefully check if the safety harness is worn correctly before the participant starts the project. This step demands extreme attention from the coach.

Coaching guidance is also crucial in this project. Not every participant has experienced similar projects before, and usually, people haven't experienced the feeling of leaping from a great height. Therefore, when doing the project, some participants may feel nervous and at a loss. Some participants might even hesitate for a long time on the climbing pole and fail to complete the challenge. As time passes, they gradually consume their physical energy and are unable to overcome themselves. At this point, the coach needs to have a special observational ability, timely reminding the participant of the next action to take, while also encouraging them appropriately to help them complete the challenge and achieve the training effect. Some coaches, when conducting this project, ignore such behaviors of the participants. They neither help the participants overcome difficulties nor save time for the following training sessions. Such behavior from coaches is highly undesirable.
