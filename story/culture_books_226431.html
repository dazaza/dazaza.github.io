"Ten Years of Marriage After 'Birds of the Same Forest' - A Medical Record Book" Author: Xia Jing

by jqrxass on 2009-08-20 15:54:00

Zheng Peier and Chen Xuan were once lovers who met at university, and they also had a time of deep love and mutual trust. After entering married life, facing the complexity of reality, their attitudes towards life began to differ. As an ordinary "young salaried" couple, due to dissatisfaction with such monotony, conflicts arose between them: they were no longer in tune, unable to strive together, and their lives were no longer synchronized - "I am striving, but you are avoiding!?" From superficial vanity to profound pain, from estrangement to despair and then mutual re-recognition, both have changed...

The novel starts with a crisis of marriage faced by a young couple from the post-70s generation, telling the story of how their deep affection gradually faded over ten years of marriage, leading to marital conflicts, infidelity, love's rescue, spiritual growth, and the healing process of their marriage. From caring for each other to torturing and hurting each other, they finally went through the toughest adjustment period in their ten-year marriage. In betrayal and tenderness, they gradually measured the happiness they could bear. They represent the common and typical "marriage growth model" that most men and women of the post-70s generation have already experienced, are currently experiencing, or will experience in their 20s, 30s, and 40s.

Part One of "Birds of the Same Forest"

He disliked most when Zheng Peier spoke this way. Talk? Talk about what? Who is talking to whom? This word made people instantly repulsed upon hearing it: leaders talk to the masses, teachers talk to students, parents talk to children, the strong talk to the weak. On what grounds did she need to talk to him?

"Let's get a divorce. If we're going to do it, we should do it sooner." Zheng Peier had already started the conversation, "I've thought it through carefully. First, while I'm still young, there might be something I can still accomplish. Second, even if I can't accomplish much, I can still quickly find a rich and powerful man to marry before I grow old and lose my charm."

Oh? Chen Xuan had heard clearly this time, thinking that Zheng Peier was upset because she didn't like Korean dramas. He was just about to comfort her, but Zheng Peier's tone was cold as she stood up: "You consider it, and give me your answer as soon as possible."

Part Two of "Birds of the Same Forest"

Chen Xuan had no choice but to go into the kitchen, lifting the pot lid only to find that the dumplings were already finished. So she had only cooked one bowl for him? Looking into the electric kettle, there was an egg lying conspicuously there. Without hesitation, he reached out his hand to grab it, but Zheng Peier called out from outside: "That egg is mine."

Chen Xuan understood that in this invisible battle, maintaining grace and not getting angry was the most important tool for victory. Thus, he said, "What? I still have half of this month's food allowance left."

After pondering all night, Zheng Peier also realized the same truth: absolutely no anger. The cost of anger would not only ruin things but also let the other party mock you. Therefore, throughout the night, both of them became extraordinarily polite and dignified. Eating the dumplings, Zheng Peier responded calmly: "Half of the food allowance is yours, and there’s still half the dumplings left in the fridge. If you want to eat, you can make them yourself. For so many years of labor costs, I'll let it slide."

Part Three of "Birds of the Same Forest"

Zheng Peier knew that he was afraid to eat across from her. She thought for a moment, trying not to feel touched by anything, washing her hands in the bathroom and muttering to herself in front of the mirror: "So what? We've been married for so many years, and it's the first time he's cooked, right? Shouldn't he? Haven't I done enough these past years?"

Having convinced herself, she sat back down at the dining table with righteous indignation and ate hastily.

In the middle of eating, she maliciously asked Chen Xuan: "Did you put poison in my food?"
