A Short Record of Raising Turtles

by lk0zuowen on 2009-06-14 04:17:23

"Shh, shh, shh... " My few turtles woke up from their quiet hibernation and slowly wriggled on the sand. They, with their carapaces and moving four limbs, looked exactly like a few haughty generals. Look, their heads slowly stretched out, blinking eyes, stretching necks, looking east and west, then they took steps forward, shaking their heads and swaggering, how adorable!

Staring at these small lives, the interesting life moments of my childhood instantly stirred and surged in my chest...

I remember when I was six years old, I hadn't started school yet. My parents had to go to work and thought that I was too young and ignorant, so they bought me a little turtle. The little turtle was so interesting, it would shake its head and swagger around, sometimes spinning circles, sometimes rolling on the sandy ground, sometimes stretching its neck and turning its head, sometimes trying to stand upright, leaning against the wall of the bowl and pushing itself up. But just as it climbed one step, it fell onto the sand, which scared it into pulling its four legs, head, and tail inside. The second time, it bravely stood up again, but just as it took a step, "bang!" It fell backward, landing on its back with all four feet in the air. I laughed happily.

One day, its back was wet, and it stuck its head out to bask in the sun. Soon, clouds covered the sun, and the turtle's neck drooped down as it slowly crawled. "Splashing splashing splashing..." A heavy rainstorm followed. Thus, I discovered that whenever the turtle's back was wet, it meant it was going to rain. I had better take my rain gear for school. This little turtle has helped me a lot!
