Issues to be noted and some modifications in the ballast crushing production line

by hx515317 on 2012-02-13 13:47:49

The capacity expansion and transformation project of the Pijiahe Stone Material Supply Section (originally named Pijiahe Quarry) under Zhengzhou Railway Bureau and Xian Railway Sub-bureau started construction in 1995 and was put into operation in 1996. It has been exactly three years so far. After continuous adjustment and transformation over the past three years, it can now fully reach the designed production capacity. Below, I will introduce issues that should be noted in the process design of the annual 100,000 cubic meters ballast crushing production line, as well as the transformation process of the process part.

### General Overview

The original design aimed at expanding capacity to form an annual production capacity of 100,000 cubic meters. The design principles were: less investment, fast construction, advanced equipment, short construction period, economical, and practical. Based on the actual situation of this site, the crushing and screening adopted a two-stage closed-circuit process flow.

During the construction process, we strictly controlled the quality, ensuring precise geometric dimensions, high structural strength, and good stability.

With this goal and policy in mind, we completed the entire supporting construction of the 100,000 cubic meter production line within half a year and put it into use ahead of schedule.

### Analysis:

1. **Crushing and Screening Process Calculation:** The calculation method for the crushing and screening process is accurate, and it can fully reach 63 cubic meters per hour.

2. **Maximum Feed Size for the First Stage:** The maximum feed size for the first stage should be chosen not to exceed 550 mm.

3. **Conveyor Belt Design:** The width of the belt conveyor and the belt speed are set at 1.6 m/s. The design of the material bin and ore car unloading system meets production requirements.

4. **Layout Considerations:** The linear layout is reasonable and feasible. However, during the design, due to the presence of the feeder, although the crushers were arranged in a straight line, the height difference between the crushers forced the rough stone hopper of the PE600×900 crusher to move back by 2 meters. This affects the overall aesthetics of the layout but does not affect production, so we did not make any changes. Here's my personal suggestion: The PE600×900 crusher could be installed in a 'T' shape with the No. 1 belt conveyor, allowing正面下料 (direct feeding from the front), which would reduce the lateral impact on the belt surface when feeding materials and extend the service life of the belt.
