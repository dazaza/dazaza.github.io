Xu Shuguang, a Chinese-American: Taxing leftover men to subsidize leftover women, what do you think?

------ Deputies and members of the National People's Congress and the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference should propose assisting leftover women in getting married

By Xu Shuguang, Chairman of GreenTree Inn and 519.com Wine Direct Sales Network

Currently, the phenomenon of leftover women in China is the most concerning issue for women. According to statistics, there are already 800,000 leftover women in Beijing, the highest in the world. The emergence of this issue has been described by some as reasonable, while others say it is unreasonable. Some say it is because Chinese men are too selfish, and excellent leftover women would rather remain single. Others say that perhaps Chinese men have lost their conscience, not daring to pursue women who are better than them and likely to cheat on women who are inferior to them.

Regardless, this is a social phenomenon, and behind this phenomenon lie some issues that we need to find an effective solution for. We must consider how to correctly face and resolve these problems instead of avoiding reality. Avoiding or ignoring the issue will only make it more severe.

Firstly, measure one, we must respect the choices of leftover women ourselves. Respecting the choices of leftover women is the premise for solving the problem because I believe they choose what they consider to be the best option for themselves. Their chosen lifestyle must have its reasons. Therefore, we should wish them happiness and not criticize them, as criticism is not a way to solve the problem. Respecting the choices of leftover women and guiding their thoughts effectively resolves half of the problem.

Measure two, in this world, relying on others is less effective than relying on oneself. Thus, the successful completion of any matter cannot be separated from personal effort. If leftover women are willing, they can actively seek suitable partners. In the process of finding a partner, they will inevitably encounter some issues, such as emotional or financial problems. Our society can also provide various opportunities or subsidies.

Measure three, regarding the phenomenon of leftover women, I believe that society should legislate to help them. Formulating effective legal texts, granting leftover women relevant rights and obligations, may also be a way to solve the problem. I suggest that during the next sessions of the National People's Congress and the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference, deputies should propose motions to assist leftover women, especially those who wish to become single mothers. Below are five suggestions I believe society can adopt.

1) Pregnancy and childbirth are issues some leftover women must face. If they choose to become single mothers, they will encounter the burden of pregnancy and childbirth. Personally, I believe society has the ability and obligation to pay the salary and benefits of single mothers during pregnancy and childbirth. Currently, companies bear this responsibility, but many companies need to survive and make profits, so some cannot afford the salary and benefits of pregnant women. They may then choose to hire men, leading to gender discrimination. This is unfair to women because children are societal resources, and society should provide sufficient guarantees.

2) Society should bear 50% of the costs of raising a single mother's first child, ensuring that if a single mother chooses to have a child, she will have appropriate economic support (for example, $500 per month for 22 years).

3) If a single mother is a full-time worker, her first child has the right to enter a full-time daycare center, and 75% of the daycare fees should be borne by society.

These additional expenses can be balanced by the following extra income.

4) Single men must pay extra child-rearing fees, which should be the same as the second item's amount (for example, $500 per month for 22 years). (Or according to a certain tax rate), ensuring that single men bear the same responsibility to society as women, otherwise, it will cause unfair treatment of women in society.

5) Other additional expenses should be resolved by society through increasing tax rates to achieve a balance between income and expenditure, avoiding fiscal deficits.

Of course, these proposals are just ideas without limits and do not necessarily represent the best or most perfect solutions. I hope these ideas can spark further discussion and encourage everyone to contribute strategies to resolve this issue well. I believe that with the efforts of society, we can certainly find very good and fair methods to solve these problems. There are always more solutions than problems. I firmly believe that the world will become increasingly beautiful.

On a side note, some people criticize our fellow townsman Ni Ping from Shandong for not independently fulfilling the responsibilities of a representative. I think everyone has different views. Perhaps Ni Ping supports all other people's proposals, so I suggest assisting our fellow townsman in putting forward some suggestions.
