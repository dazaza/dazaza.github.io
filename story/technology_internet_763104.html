Facebook: What were we thinking when we designed Facebook Home

by anonymous on 2013-07-29 15:30:28

"Designing Facebook Home" Video Gives Rare Look At Prototypes And Iteration Process

Although pinned high hopes on by Facebook, ever since the official launch of Facebook Home, it has been in an awkward position. Without mentioning the unsatisfactory downloads, even the Facebook phone launched in collaboration with HTC and with much fanfare was slashed in price from $99 to $0.99 for a two-year contract by AT&T within less than a month of its release. Despite these setbacks, they haven't lost confidence in Facebook Home, which is slowly regaining momentum, and many people are gradually starting to accept this way of using Facebook.

Recently, Facebook released a video where several key personnel involved in the development of Facebook Home shared some behind-the-scenes stories in this 40-minute video. At the very beginning, Julie Zhuo, product design director of Facebook Home, said: "Not many people pay attention to what happens along the journey. We only see the final product, the final finished form, but we don't see the ups and downs experienced along the way, the failed attempts, the endless iterations and discussions." Through the video below, we may be able to gain a deeper understanding of Facebook Home.

Reflections of the Real World

Justin Stahl, a designer at Facebook, believes that Facebook should have many similarities with the real world. He also mentioned that the lock screen and home screen are places that smartphone users browse most frequently and are also relatively private places. Therefore, they took these two places as a bridge, a bridge connecting Facebook with the real world.

At the same time, he also combined many of his life experiences to explain more vividly: for example, the desktop feed (Cover Feed) should be a place to directly obtain information. Users don't have to move between different apps to get information. All information acquisition should be natural, without any abruptness. The design of Chat Heads is also the same, aiming to be as natural as real life: being able to chat with friends anytime, just like in the office, when colleagues come to your desk to say hello or chat casually, all of this won't have too much impact on you.

Design Details

Initially, Facebook considered simply placing the traditional news feed (News feed) at the bottom of the lock screen. Francis Luu of Facebook explained: "We designed it this way so that users can check by simply swiping up, without blocking the desktop image." But this would lose the essence of Facebook.

Considering this aspect, Luu added: "Sometimes poor user experience isn't because something is blocked, but because the News feed loses its timeliness. So we thought, 'What if we fill the entire screen with updates from your friends?'" In the video, he also mentioned many similar design detail issues.
