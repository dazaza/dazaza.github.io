Wing Chun fitness exercises are suitable for both adults and children.

by anonymous on 2012-03-07 12:25:30

Yesterday, at Xishan Primary School in Shunde, Master Luo Dezhi, an expert in Yongchun拳 (Wing Chun), personally taught elementary school students. / Photo by Foshan Daily intern Chen Shuo Zhou.

Master Luo Dezhi, a Wing Chun expert, freely teaches middle and primary school students with the wish to promote martial arts culture.

Guangfo Metropolis Network reports by Foshan Daily journalist Hu Wenchun: In order to promote Wing Chun and carry forward traditional culture, Luo Dezhi, the head of the Shunde Wing Chun Training Base, has carefully designed a unique set of Wing Chun fitness exercises suitable for people of all age groups.

At 9 a.m. yesterday morning, Luo Dezhi led his disciples to participate in the Xishan Primary School Wing Chun Youth Team demonstration event, which was warmly welcomed by teachers and students.

Amidst the powerful music of "Men Should Be Strong," the demonstration officially began. More than a hundred students around 10 years old practiced Wing Chun, which has gained immense popularity in recent years. Each move was imitated with great precision despite having trained for less than a year. Many students had already become quite proficient in Wing Chun. On stage, Luo Dezhi led his disciples, performing Wing Chun fluidly and impressively. Among them, world rock climbing champion and world fitness bodybuilding Miss Champion from Hong Kong, Zheng Lisha, stood out remarkably among Luo's disciples. Although she only started studying under Master Luo in Shunde last year, her Wing Chun performance now exudes strength and vitality.

Following this, Luo Dezhi personally taught the students Wing Chun. Despite the hot weather causing many students to sweat profusely after a short while, they continued enthusiastically. "As long as I have time, I will practice. Learning Wing Chun not only strengthens my body but also allows me to protect myself," said nine-year-old Jing.

According to Xiao Ronghua, the educational director of Xishan Primary School, since last year, the school has begun cooperating with the martial arts training center established by Luo Dezhi. Luo often leads his disciples to teach Wing Chun to teachers and students for free and has even provided some performance costumes. "Initially, we thought there wouldn't be many enrollees, but unexpectedly, the students showed great enthusiasm for Wing Chun. Now every afternoon during physical education activities, our third-grade and fifth-grade students practice Wing Chun for half an hour. Not only are the students happy, but the parents are also satisfied," said Xiao Ronghua. He added that the next step is to promote it throughout the entire school.

"Almost every Friday afternoon, I come to Xishan Primary School to teach Wing Chun to teachers and students," said Luo Dezhi with a smile after finishing the practice session. To facilitate teaching and promotion, he specifically designed a set of Wing Chun fitness exercises. "The entire routine takes about 2 minutes, though brief, it incorporates various classic movements of Wing Chun, suitable for both adults and children."

Luo Dezhi mentioned that Wing Chun itself has infinite variations, and practicing the fitness exercises allows one to experience its essence. "We are currently considering recording the Wing Chun fitness exercises into videos for global promotion, but this requires the approval of Chen Guoji, the Shunde Wing Chun master, and my teacher Tan Huanbiao." In Luo Dezhi's view, learning Wing Chun as early as possible is beneficial. He himself started martial arts at 9 years old and by 17 had integrated the strengths of Changquan (Long Fist) and Nanquan (Southern Fist) into Wing Chun.

Currently, Luo Dezhi's greatest hope is to collaborate with more primary and secondary schools to promote Wing Chun. "In the future, I will participate more in public welfare activities to promote martial arts culture."
