Life-saving Tips -- Small Tricks for Daily Economy

Soap Ends

When your soap is almost used up and only a small piece remains, it's a pity to throw it away. Also, if you have a pair of stockings with a hole in them that you can no longer wear, they just become a pile of garbage. Combine these two items: put the remaining soap pieces inside the stockings. You can use this to directly scrub porcelain basins or wash socks. When wet, it lathers easily and can be used with a brush to clean shoes or backpacks, saving soap and being very practical.

Single-Strand Stockings with Holes

Besides the aforementioned use, you can gather these stockings together, tie them up, and use them specifically for scrubbing basins. They are quite useful and can also be used to clean the outer rim of the toilet bowl. This is better than using a cloth because they don't fray and easily remove the layer of dust and moisture mixed residue on the surface.

Discarded Bras

These items have a high淘汰 rate, and for the sake of one's figure and health, they need to be replaced frequently. What do we do with the discarded ones? Nowadays, bras generally have metal underwires inside. First, remove them and keep them together to sell as scrap metal along with bottle caps and such. The cups are mostly made of sponge. Cut them out, connect them together (they don't have to be perfectly neat, just form a large block), and what can they be used for? Most of our bathrooms and kitchens are tiled, right? Use this sponge block to wipe them. Pair it with the soap end mentioned earlier, and you have another great combination. This saves money on buying cleaning cloths, enough perhaps to buy a big cabbage. Finally, there are the shoulder straps left. In most cases, the elasticity of the straps is still good, so we can use them as elastic bands. If your husband's underwear elastic band breaks or becomes ineffective, use these straps as replacements. For other uses, apply them wherever an elastic band is needed but appearance isn't a priority. Any leftover scraps that can't be used anymore? Throw them away. We've saved enough already; let's call it a day here.

Unwanted Jeans

Jeans may not tear easily, but there comes a time when they are no longer wanted. What to do? First, cut off the pant legs. Since the material is usually thick and has good cotton quality, it can be used to make cushion pads, which are sweat-absorbing and comfortable to sit on. What about the rest? There are pockets on the jeans. Add some extra fabric from the edges, fray the cut parts a bit, and after washing in the washing machine, they take shape. You can hang them somewhere as storage bags for miscellaneous items like utility bills or small coins for supermarket trips. This keeps things tidy instead of scattered around, making everything much more organized.

Tofu Boxes

Preserved egg tofu is delicious, but what about the box the tofu came in? Should we throw it away? I don't. What do I keep it for? When you buy shrimp from the market and peel the shrimp yourself, if you can't eat all of it at once, this box comes in handy. Put the shrimp in the box, freeze it in the fridge, and whenever you want to eat it, simply thaw it. It's both tasty and economical. Other things can also be stored in it, such as meat fillings. Be creative!
