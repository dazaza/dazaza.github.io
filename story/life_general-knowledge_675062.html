26 Small Tricks for Car Maintenance

Trick 1: How to eliminate the vibration and noise of the wiper blade: Use pliers to adjust the gap at each joint and where the rubber blade is clamped.

Trick 2: How to extend the life of the bulb: After buying a car or replacing a new bulb, wipe the bulb with alcohol to remove fingerprints and oil stains. I did this after buying my car, and none of the bulbs have broken so far.

Trick 3: How to reduce tire noise: Stick a layer of black felt or velvet on the inside wheel guard. (After it rains, you'll notice that your tire noise decreases, but after washing your car, it increases because the mud on the inner guard absorbs noise.)

Trick 4: How to eliminate the noise caused by uneven wear of individual tires: After four-wheel alignment and balancing, find a bike repairman, borrow or buy a wood file, level the uneven parts of the tire tread, and the noise will stop.

Trick 5: How to extend the life of the muffler: Drill a small hole in the lowest point of the muffler. The principle is simple: drain water to prevent corrosion.

Trick 6: How to accelerate quickly when starting: In situations where the vehicle is empty or lightly loaded, start directly in second gear (no need to worry about damaging the vehicle because the first gear gear and clutch are designed for heavy loads, and most cars have difficulty shifting from first to second gear), then push into third gear once the RPM exceeds 3000, continue accelerating until over 3000 RPM. At this point, most vehicles will lag behind you, allowing you to smoothly shift into fourth and fifth gears, or even directly into fifth gear (suitable for highway toll stations), without increasing fuel consumption. Give it a try.

Trick 7: Simple handling of morning engine shaking: Due to poor operation of an individual cylinder or improper valve closure, one solution is to run at high speed (which costs money), or you can run at high RPM for a while in third or fourth gear (saving money), with the same effect. If the car doesn't shake when warm but shifting isn't smooth (manual transmission), it may be due to uneven fuel injection from the injectors, which requires testing and cleaning.

Trick 8: How to prevent the antenna from being stolen: Unscrew the antenna, apply strong adhesive to the thread, and tighten it. It works very well.

Trick 9: How to easily pass the brake test during inspection: Unplug the control harness connector on the ABS pump, and plug it back in after the test, not affecting normal use. Find time to visit a repair station to clear the fault code.

Trick 10: How to store items that are rarely used but must be carried in the car: Open the trunk and check the spare tire area; it's the largest storage compartment.

Trick 11: How to improve air circulation inside the car and defrosting efficiency: Replace the dust filter core (inside the engine bay) with sponge of the same size, increasing airflow and allowing for easy cleaning and reuse. Additionally, it speeds up winter defrosting, eliminating the need to run the fan during long trips, saving fuel.

Trick 12: How to keep the clutch operating smoothly for a long time: During each brake fluid change, ask the maintenance personnel to release waste oil from the clutch slave cylinder. Since the clutch and brakes share the same oil reservoir, it might be challenging but necessary.

Trick 13: What to do if you feel the brakes aren't as effective as before: Press the brakes forcefully several times while waiting at a red light, and they will return to their original performance.

Trick 14: A simple way to prevent vehicle drifting:

1. Increase the air pressure of the front wheel on the drifting side or decrease the air pressure of the tire on the other side;

2. Adjust the toe within the specified range;

3. After four-wheel alignment, hold the merchant accountable. Note that most vehicles drift to the right due to frequent curb climbing on the right side.

Trick 15: How to make the water pump last longer: It is recommended not to exceed 140 km/h on highways and keep the engine RPM below 4000.

Trick 16: How to extend the life of the spark plugs: The spark plugs originally equipped on the car generally have a lifespan of more than 100,000 to 160,000 kilometers. If there is no significant change in ignition, power, or fuel consumption, replacement is unnecessary. Carbon buildup should be cleaned at 60,000 kilometers, and the gap between the three negative electrodes and the positive electrode should be adjusted.

Trick 17: Each time the fuel system is cleaned, ask the maintenance personnel to measure the output pressure of the gasoline regulator (before the injector) and clean the regulator's filter. This is because the pressure regulator has a significant impact on the lifespan of the gasoline pump.
