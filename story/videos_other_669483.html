Many new mothers say that their babies suddenly get diaper rash, and start to cry when they urinate. How should we take care of the baby? If we ignore the diaper rash, will it heal on its own? Will there be any serious consequences? Let me explain in detail below! Diaper rash mainly refers to a clearly defined bright red patch on the skin where it comes into contact with the diaper, distributed in patches. Given that newborns have delicate skin, they are very prone to diaper rash. The local skin may present with red papules, and in severe cases, skin erosion, ulceration, peeling, and oozing can occur. If bacteria infect, it can lead to pustules, and in more serious cases, it can spread to the perineum and inner and outer thighs. In fact, diaper rash is a common skin condition in newborns, also known as diaper dermatitis or diaper rash. Medically, it is called "red buttocks," a common skin disease in infants, mostly caused by improper care. The common cause is due to untimely changing of diapers after bowel movements or urination, diapers not being washed properly, allergy to disposable diapers, or prolonged use of plastic wraps leading to urine not evaporating, leaving the baby's buttocks in a warm and humid state. Urine urea nitrogen is decomposed by bacteria in feces to produce ammonia, which irritates the skin. An important way to prevent diaper rash is to frequently change diapers. Infants urinate and defecate many times, and only by timely changing diapers can the cleanliness and dryness of the buttock skin be maintained. After each bowel movement, the buttocks should be washed with warm water, and after washing, it must be dried with a towel; powder should not be applied while wet. Secondly, powder and ointments should be used correctly. Generally, they can be used before diaper rash occurs, serving to smooth and protect the skin. After diaper rash occurs, different methods must be adopted according to the different wounds, and random application of powder or ointments should be avoided. In the daily care of the baby, the following points should be noted: 1. When choosing diapers, attention should be paid to using soft, highly absorbent pure cotton cloth, preferably white or light-colored old sheets, cotton shirts, pants for making diapers. If it is found that the baby is allergic to disposable diapers, use should be stopped immediately. 2. Do not use dark-colored fabrics, especially new coarse black or blue cloth, which does not absorb water easily and can easily rub and break the baby’s skin. 3. Diapers should be changed frequently, and should be replaced immediately after each wetting. 4. After each bowel movement, wash the buttocks and external genitalia with warm water, gently dry them, apply some talcum powder and disinfected plant oil. Special attention should be given to babies with diarrhea. How to handle diaper rash? Once diaper rash has occurred, hot water and soap should not be used for washing. After changing the diaper, apply tannic acid ointment or disinfected plant oil on the affected area. If there is erosion, the baby can lie face down, and a regular 40-watt bulb can be used for irradiation at a distance of 30-50 cm, for 30 to 60 minutes each time, to dry the area. During irradiation, a dedicated person should monitor to prevent burns. As long as the skin of the buttocks is well cared for during treatment, the diaper rash will improve quickly. In fact, as long as parents take good care, diaper rash is completely preventable, so all parents must do a good job of prevention. Here are a few suggestions for choosing and using diaper cream: 1. Must use pH neutral baby-specific products, and adult products cannot be substituted. When selecting wet wipes, choose alcohol-free ones to avoid irritating the baby’s skin; select ointments that are purely oily, fragrance-free, steroid-free, and have anti-inflammatory effects on dermatitis and skin erosion. 2. Conduct an allergy test before using a new product. Since a baby's skin barrier function is not yet fully developed and is extremely prone to allergies, if the baby has not used a certain care product before, the mother should first try it on a small part of the baby's skin for a few days to observe if the baby is allergic. 3. Do not use several products at the same time. If several diaper creams are applied haphazardly on the baby's skin, it will not only increase the skin burden but also make it difficult to determine which care product caused the allergy symptoms if they appear. 4. Ensure the baby's skin breathes. Some parents think that applying more moisturizer gives extra protection to the baby's skin. In fact, excessive moisturizer can unknowingly harm the baby's skin. Therefore, even with the most effective products, they should be applied appropriately.
