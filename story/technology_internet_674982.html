Top ten causes of prostatitis: what men need to know.

by abckvuul on 2012-02-24 17:04:15

Experts from the People's Livelihood Prostate Disease Network [http://www.0351qlx.com/] indicate that the causes of prostatitis are complex and mainly include the following ten factors:

(1) Abnormal sexual activity, such as frequent sexual activity, masturbation, interrupted sexual intercourse, or excessive inhibition of sexual activity can lead to prostate congestion, which may induce prostatitis.

(2) Patients with sexually transmitted diseases, especially gonococcal urethritis, where gonococcus enters the prostate through the urethra and prostate duct causing infection.

(3) Prostate stones or prostate hyperplasia leading to prostate tissue congestion, resulting in non-specific infections.

(4) Improper catheterization or urethral dilation allowing pathogens on medical instruments to enter the urethra, causing disease.

(5) Excessive alcohol consumption, prolonged sitting, cycling, horseback riding, and other straddling activities can cause prostate congestion and are closely related to the onset of prostatitis.

(6) Cold exposure can increase the sympathetic nervous system activity of the prostate, increasing pressure in the urethra and hindering excretion. The contraction of the prostate duct also hinders excretion, causing stasis and congestion.

(7) Excessive frequency and force during prostate massage can cause prostate congestion and edema.

(8) Inflammatory lesions in nearby organs such as the rectum, colon, and lower urinary tract can spread to the prostate via lymphatic vessels, causing prostatitis.

(9) Infections from other parts of the body can reach the prostate through the bloodstream, such as from skin infections, tonsillitis, cavities, and respiratory infections.

(10) Non-bacterial infections, viruses, mycoplasma, chlamydia, ureaplasma, gardnerella, trichomonas, and other types of infections can all lead to prostatitis.
