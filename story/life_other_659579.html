Love Shouldn't Come Too Late

School started, and the teacher was assigning our seats in the classroom. I was placed in the first row, but behind me was a girl. She had a ponytail, a mouth full of braces, dark skin, and a short stature. Everyone who saw her would laugh. Why did fate place her behind me? Was this some kind of joke?

I never spoke to the "ugly duckling" behind me. However, one day, the teacher said we should form groups with the person in front or behind us to discuss questions. Reluctantly, I had my first conversation with her. During our discussion, I asked her many questions and even mocked her several times, but she always responded with a smile. I asked Xiao Yun why her skin was so dark, why she was so short, and why she had a mouth full of braces. She didn't get angry; instead, she smiled and said that when she was little, she used to play outside with other children, regardless of the weather. By the time she started school, her skin was already dark. As for her height, she didn't know why she couldn't grow taller. Her braces were because her teeth hadn't grown properly when she was young, so her family had them fixed. I chuckled and said, "You had quite a few problems when you were little." That's how class ended, and our discussion came to an end.

After class, a few classmates asked me why I seemed to be getting along so well with the "little duck." I didn't know what to say, so I replied, "The teacher said we should discuss things together. What could I do?" One of them teased, "Do you really want to talk to that little duck?"

At that moment, another classmate said, "Look at our class monitor; she's pretty, has good grades, and is very charming. Do you dare to bet that if you can win her over, I'll buy you a month's worth of cigarettes." I replied, "Are you underestimating me? There's nothing I can't do. Prepare a month's worth of cigarettes, and don't back out later."

So, I launched a fierce pursuit of the class monitor, but it wasn't going well as she kept avoiding me. Then I remembered that the "ugly duckling" had been getting close to her recently. I decided to ask her for help, offering her some benefits in return. I went to her and said, "Xiao Yun, please help me. Can you tell the class monitor that I want to invite her to watch a movie on Sunday? If you can make her agree, I'll grant you a favor." After talking for a long time, she finally agreed to help me talk to the class monitor.

That evening, after school, she told me that the class monitor had agreed and asked me to pick her up at her house on Sunday. I was very happy because my plan had succeeded. I told Xiao Yun that I would fulfill the promise I made to her. She said, "Not now, let's talk about it later." Because of this, the "ugly duckling" and I became friends. We often chatted about what the class monitor liked, what she enjoyed doing, and also about her life. From her, I learned that she was very insecure. She knew the boys were mocking her, calling her an "ugly duckling." There was nothing she could do about it; she just had to accept her fate since she looked the way she did.

With the help of others, I managed to win over the class monitor, and the classmate who made the bet admitted defeat. I got what I wanted, and everyone envied me, making me feel very proud.

Because the "duckling" helped me pursue the class monitor, our friendship grew stronger. For over a year, we were always together, laughing and playing around. On the way to and from school, we were always together. Sometimes, the class monitor would tell me, "You two are the perfect pair, and I'm just a mistake." Not only her, but many people thought the same, always saying we were a couple. At the time, I didn't get upset or think much about it.

One day, Xiao Yun said she wanted to treat me to dinner. I asked why, and she said, "Remember the favor you promised me when you were pursuing the class monitor? This is that favor." We went to a restaurant, ordered some dishes, and because we were happy, we ordered some alcohol too. As we drank and chatted, I found myself enjoying talking to her immensely. We shared everything with each other, telling each other our innermost thoughts. Maybe it was the alcohol affecting my brain, and I told Xiao Yun that the reason I pursued the class monitor was because I had made a bet with someone. She was surprised, but after a while, she acted as if nothing had happened. She told me, "Do you know, I've actually liked you for a long time, but I'm too ugly. How could I compare with the class monitor? So I didn't tell you." At that moment, I felt something stir inside me, a sweetness I couldn't explain. I thought, "She likes me? It can't be true." Why did I have such a reaction? Could it be that I also had feelings for her? At that moment, Xiao Yun stood up and said, "It's late, I need to go home. See you at school tomorrow." And with that, she ran off into the distance. I silently watched her, feeling conflicted inside.

The next day at school, Xiao Yun seemed embarrassed to see me and kept avoiding me deliberately. Thus, we didn't speak for a long time. One day, Xiao Yun didn't come to school. The teacher said she had transferred. Upon hearing this, I felt heavy-hearted, not knowing why I reacted this way. Although she was gone, I thought about her every day, remembering the times we used to share our deepest thoughts. Every time I turned around, hoping to see her, the person behind me had changed.

Time passed slowly, and my relationship with the class monitor ended badly. Thus, my high school days came to an end, and I didn't get into university, so I spent my days aimlessly wandering outside. One day, after wandering around, I was preparing to go home when I heard someone calling my name. I turned around and saw a ponytail, a set of neat teeth, dark skin, and a tall figure wearing casual clothes waving at me. When she approached me, I said, "Who are you? How do you know my name?" She smiled happily and sweetly, saying, "I'm Xiao Yun! How could you forget your old friend after two years?" Only then did I realize that she was the "duckling" from those days, now transformed into a "swan." Although her skin was still a bit dark, she looked exceptionally elegant.

We started chatting, asking each other where we had been these past few years and why we hadn't seen each other. She said her family worked elsewhere, so she had to leave with them. I said, "When you left, you didn't tell me. I felt heavy-hearted every day, always thinking about you, hoping you would come back one day. But I waited for two years, and you never returned." She replied, "That's all in the past. Now I have my own life, and I have a boyfriend who loves me." When I heard the word "boyfriend," I felt so disheartened. Back then, I rejected a girl because of her appearance, only to realize I liked her when it was already too late. By the time we met again, it was already over. All I could say was that regret was useless~!!
