IFA Show: Sony, Samsung Unveil Curved Screen Smart TVs

by anonymous on 2013-09-08 19:33:06

The hustle and bustle of September is far from over. In Berlin, Germany, where autumn is gradually setting in, the IFA Conference is in full swing. Yesterday morning, we reported on a series of new product launches by Sony, as well as Samsung's Galaxy Note 3 and Galaxy Gear. However, that’s not all—Sony also unveiled its first smart TV with a curved LED screen.

Sony's product appeared slightly earlier, model number S990A. It features a 65-inch curved LED screen, making it the first smart TV to use such technology. The cost will be significantly lower than OLED screens. However, the resolution of the Sony S990A is only 1080p, which lags behind in clarity.

This screen has a 16:9 aspect ratio and provides eight speakers distributed on both sides of the screen, offering an excellent sound field. Paired with the curved screen, it delivers a slight cinematic viewing experience. It also supports content casting from iOS and Android devices. Content can also be projected from Sony's VAIO Windows 8 laptops via an installed application, and the device supports NFC.

Since it uses an LED screen, it performs worse than OLED screens in terms of color contrast and expressiveness. However, the advantage is that the price is much lower, with a pre-determined price of $4000, almost one-fourth the price of LG and Samsung's curved OLED TVs. The product will be available on the market in October.

Now let's take a look at Samsung's curved screen TV. Two sizes were showcased on-site: 65 inches and 55 inches, with UHD resolution, making it the first LED curved screen to adopt this resolution. This product also has a unique design, with a large frame serving as the stand following the curve of the border, fixing the screen through the bending of the border.

Samsung does not currently plan to release this product to the market but will introduce appropriately sized models based on user feedback. Currently, among Samsung's UHD TV products, there are already various sizes available, including 110 inches, 98 inches, 85 inches, 65 inches, and 55 inches. Additionally, this will be Samsung's second curved screen TV.

Curved screen TVs have been gaining attention since LG showcased them at CES earlier this year, prompting Samsung, which holds the core technology, to join in. Now, manufacturers are using this feature as a selling point for their products. According to manufacturers' theories, curved screens align better with the curvature of the human retina, allowing images to be more intuitively received and processed by the brain, resulting in a more comfortable viewing experience. The curved screens in cinemas are based on this principle, providing an impressive sensory experience.

However, in today's era where smart TVs have yet to fully meet consumer needs, this selling point may end up like 3D display technology did years ago—raising prices without delivering substantial benefits to consumers.
