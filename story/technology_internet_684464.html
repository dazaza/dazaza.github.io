Recently, Yang Dan, an assistant in the market research department of Youyou Baby Kids' Wear under Youyou Network (www.youyou518.com), released a report indicating that spring lethargy is a physiological response caused by seasonal changes. During the cold winter, human bodies are affected by low temperatures, causing the capillaries in the skin to constrict and blood flow to relatively decrease. Sweat glands and pores also close, reducing heat dissipation to maintain normal body temperature.

As spring arrives and temperatures rise, the body's pores, sweat glands, and blood vessels start to expand, and skin circulation becomes more vigorous. This causes a relative reduction in blood supply to the brain. With warmer weather, metabolism gradually increases, oxygen consumption constantly grows, and the brain’s oxygen supply inevitably appears insufficient. Additionally, warm temperatures have a benign effect that can inhibit brain activity. As a result, people feel drowsy and sleepy, always feeling like they haven't slept enough. However, it's important to note that some pathological factors may be involved. Some cases of "spring lethargy" are symptoms of diseases. For example, depressive symptoms before the onset of mental illness; low-grade fever and sleepiness during the early stages of hepatitis; fatigue due to chronic diseases like diabetes and heart disease caused by physical weakness. Some even discovered that frequent yawning and excessive sleepiness in hypertensive patients during spring could be a precursor to stroke. Therefore, if “spring lethargy” is caused by a disease, one should promptly seek medical examination and confirmation.

How to Relieve Spring Lethargy

What are our "defense tricks" against "spring lethargy" in daily life?

1. Soothe Emotions

Spring is the season when liver qi flourishes. Since the liver prefers smoothness and dislikes depression, it is advisable to adapt naturally and keep emotions cheerful. Regular outdoor sports and entertainment activities can increase social interactions. Laughing with friends, sharing interesting news, and relaxing the body without anger or frustration can promote blood and qi circulation to alleviate spring lethargy.

2. Early to Bed, Early to Rise

In spring, it is preferable to go to bed early and wake up early rather than staying in bed too long, as prolonged sleep can make one more tired. Especially for the elderly, excessive spring sleep can accelerate the formation of cerebral thrombosis. Generally, it is ideal to sleep around 22:00 at night and wake up around 6:00 in the morning. In spring, it is also not advisable to stay up late, which might induce or worsen spring lethargy. Ideally, a 30-minute nap in the afternoon is recommended. If napping isn’t possible, closing your eyes for ten minutes to relax can effectively relieve "spring lethargy."

Simultaneously, attention should be paid to maintaining fresh airflow indoors. Stagnant air reduces oxygen content and increases carbon dioxide and other harmful gases, exacerbating "spring lethargy" and causing dizziness and fatigue. Regularly opening windows to maintain fresh indoor air not only helps prevent "spring illnesses" but also aids in overcoming "spring lethargy."

3. Dietary Considerations

To alleviate spring lethargy, it is important to increase protein intake, such as adding fish, eggs, milk, soy products, pig liver, chicken, peanuts, and other foods appropriately. Eating more red-yellow and dark green vegetables, such as carrots, pumpkins, tomatoes, bell peppers, and celery, can help restore energy and eliminate "spring lethargy." Fruits contain abundant potassium, which helps maintain cellular hydration. Avoid eating excessively cold, greasy, or sticky foods, and limit alcohol consumption. Increase vitamin intake, such as vitamins C and B.

Additionally, a lack of potassium can cause weakness and affect concentration. Foods rich in this mineral include raisins, oranges, bananas, and apples. People with noticeable signs of internal heat can consume cooling foods such as mung bean soup, honeysuckle tea, chrysanthemum tea, etc.

4. Regular Exercise

Physical exercise can significantly speed up the brain's information processing reaction time, effectively preventing spring lethargy. Choose gentle exercises such as brisk walking, jogging, aerobics, Tai Chi, etc. Additionally, you can go on spring outings to breathe fresh air, improving brain cortex function, uplifting mood, and invigorating the spirit. External visual and light stimulation can adjust the body's internal rhythm, quickly awakening the central nervous system and eliminating fatigue.

5. Appropriate Massage

When experiencing spring lethargy, simple massage techniques can help.

"Dry Face Washing" Method: Close both eyes, use the large fleshy part of your hands from the mouth corners and nostrils upwards, then from the forehead downwards to the chin, and back upwards again. Repeat 10-20 times.

"Comb Head" Method: Use your fingers spread out from the forehead to the back of the head as if combing hair, finally massaging the neck area. Alternatively, press the Hegu point between the thumb and index finger and the Laogong point in the palm center for 1-2 minutes until a tingling sensation occurs. This method can awaken the mind and clear blockages effectively.

Foot Bath and Massage: Prepare a basin of hot water with salt, soak your feet briefly, then proceed with self-massage. Curl your fingers and use the knuckles to massage the soles of your feet. Start massaging the toes from the back towards the front, including the tips. Grip your hand into a fist and use the knuckles to scrape and massage the entire sole from front to back. Focus on pressing points on the arch and heel areas.

6. Scientific Brain Usage

Scientific brain usage involves allowing the left hemisphere of the brain to rest appropriately. The brain has two different hemispheres with distinct functions. The left hemisphere mainly handles language, mathematics, and abstract thinking, while the right hemisphere is less used in intellectual work and thus relatively relaxed. Therefore, during daily work in spring, when your mind feels unclear or painful, put down your work, listen to music, admire flowers, or do some exercises. Let the right hemisphere get active and used, giving the left hemisphere a proper rest. More importantly, exercise ensures timely replenishment and supply of oxygen to the brain, the organ with the highest oxygen consumption in the body, thereby alleviating fatigue and relieving drowsiness.
