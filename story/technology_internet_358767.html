Google to roll out music download service globally within the year, sources say

by king5b2t on 2010-06-22 18:48:32

<!-- publish_helper name='Original Article' p_id='2' t_id='1' d_id='4336836' f_id='2' -->

<p> Sina Technology News, afternoon of June 22 Beijing Time, according to foreign media reports, insiders revealed that Google is preparing to launch a global music download service later this year, and then plans to roll out a cloud computing music service in 2011.</p>

<p> The insider stated that Google's plans are not yet clear, and it is uncertain whether Google has reached any agreements with the record companies. However, Google has indeed accelerated its negotiation pace, aiming to provide new music services for Android phones. The insider also mentioned that it will take several more months before Google's music download store is released.</p><a href='http://www.yt9998.com' target='_blank'>Telecom Customer Service</a>

<p> Previously, Google has been actively promoting its music business. As the first step in developing its music business, Google last year began offering links through its search engine pointing to iLike and Pandora, allowing users to listen to streaming music by simply clicking on the relevant link on the search page. Now, the company hopes to bundle its own services with the search engine.</p><a href='http://www.yt9998.com' target='_blank'>10000 Customer Service</a>

<p> In addition, Google is also vigorously developing entertainment content. The company has introduced a lot of professional content to YouTube, and plans to launch a digital bookstore this year.</p>

<p> The release of Google's music store will further strain its relationship with Apple. Apple's iTunes Music Store leads the U.S. digital music market, and Apple has recently started selling e-books. Since the Android operating system competes with Apple's iPhone, these two Silicon Valley giants have become rivals since the launch of Google's product. In retaliation, Apple recently launched an advertising platform for the iPhone and iPad tablet computer, but Google expressed that some terms of the platform raise concerns about limiting competition.</p>

<p> Both Google and Apple declined to comment on this news.</p>

<p> Due to Apple's increasing dominance in the U.S. music field making record companies increasingly uneasy, Google's entry into the music retail sector may be welcomed by record companies. According to data from U.S. market research company NPD, Apple's share of the national music retail market in the first quarter of this year reached 28%. Although record companies have always hoped for a company capable of competing with Apple to emerge, Amazon and Walmart's shares still significantly lag behind Apple, both being around 12%.</p><a href='http://www.yt9998.com' target='_blank'>Mobile Customer Service</a>

<p> Insiders said that Google is expected to first launch an online music store, providing users with music purchasing and downloading services. This store will be directly linked to the Google search engine, where users can obtain a link to the Google music store by simply searching for specific songs on Google.</p>

<p> The insider also stated that the music store is only a transitional plan, and Google also plans to introduce a subscription-based service based on cloud computing, which will be compatible with Android phones. With this service, users will be able to listen to internet streaming music directly via their phones without needing to store music on their devices.</p>

<p> A few months ago, Apple acquired the online music service Lala and shut down the service. It is widely speculated that Apple will soon launch an iTunes Music Store based on cloud computing. (Ding Hong)</p>
