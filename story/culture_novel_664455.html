There is a touching story about a female driver -

by anonymous on 2012-02-13 15:10:41

This story, which took place a few years ago, is one that many people are familiar with. Since it is a classic and touching tale, I will repost it for those who haven't seen it.

In a certain area of China, a female driver was driving a bus full of passengers along a winding mountain road. On the bus, three bandits had their eyes on the pretty female driver. They forced her to stop the vehicle so they could take her off the bus to "have some fun." In panic, the female driver called for help, but all the passengers on the bus were frozen in fear.

Only a middle-aged, frail man stood up to fight back, but he was beaten and lay injured on the ground. Enraged, the man shouted loudly, calling on all the passengers to stop the atrocity, but no one responded as the female driver was dragged into the bushes. Half an hour later, the three bandits returned with the disheveled female driver. As the bus was about to depart again, the female driver asked the injured, bleeding middle-aged man to get off the bus. The man refused, standing firm.

"Hey, you get off! My bus doesn't carry people like you!" The middle-aged man became angry and said, "How unreasonable you are! Wasn't I trying to save you?"

"Save me? What did you save me from?" The female driver flatly denied it, causing a few passengers to chuckle secretly. The middle-aged man was extremely upset, regretting his lack of strength like a great hero. He failed to rescue her, yet he shouldn't have been driven off the bus like this. Determined not to leave, he insisted, "Besides, I bought my ticket, I have the right to ride!"

The female driver raised her face coldly and said, "If you don't get off, I won't drive."

Unexpectedly, the same passengers who had turned a blind eye to the violence earlier now woke up as if from a dream and unanimously persuaded the man to get off the bus: "You'd better get off quickly! We have things to do and can't afford delays!" Some strong passengers even tried to drag the middle-aged man off the bus, reminiscent of the plot in Maupassant's "Boule de Suif."

The three bandits grinned and laughed smugly. One shameless black-skinned ruffian even boasted, "We had our way with her!" Another two bandits also spouted nonsense: "She's my girlfriend, what's it to you!" After a heated argument, the man's luggage was thrown out of the window, and he was pushed off the bus. The bus resumed its smooth journey along the mountain road. The female driver smoothed her hair, turned on the radio. The bus was approaching the top of the mountain, about to turn and head down. On the left side of the car was a road carved into the mountain, and on the right side was a hundred-foot cliff. The car began to quietly accelerate. The female driver's face remained calm, her hands firmly gripping the steering wheel, tears streaming down her eyes.

A bandit seemed to sense something and said, "Slow down! Slow down! What the hell are you doing?" The female driver didn't respond; instead, the speed increased. The bandit attempted to grab the steering wheel, but the car shot forward like an arrow towards the cliff...

The next day, the local newspaper reported: A tragedy occurred in Fuhu Mountain yesterday when a minibus plunged off a cliff. Both the driver and thirteen passengers on board perished without exception. The middle-aged man who had been kicked off the bus halfway saw the newspaper and cried. No one knew why he cried...
