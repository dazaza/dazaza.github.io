Some women experience menstrual delays due to a lack of knowledge about physiological matters and do not know the reasons behind it. For women of childbearing age who are sexually active, if there is a delay in menstruation, pregnancy should be considered first. If pregnancy is ruled out, timely gynecological examinations should be conducted to find the cause and cooperate with treatment. What are the influencing factors that lead to delayed menstruation in women?

The main factors leading to delayed menstruation in women include:

**Reason 1: Pregnancy**

For women of childbearing age who are sexually active, pregnancy should be considered first. If pregnant, symptoms such as amenorrhea, nausea, vomiting, drowsiness, frequent urination, and darkening of the areola may occur.

**Reason 2: Excessive Stress**

If a woman experiences excessive mental tension, grief, sorrow, anger, or heartbreak over a period of time, it may lead to delayed menstruation. In most cases, no treatment is needed; adjusting emotions with confidence can help.

**Reason 3: Use of Contraceptive Pills**

Some women who take contraceptive pills long-term and suddenly stop may temporarily experience menstrual irregularities, which can result in delayed menstruation.

**Reason 4: Surgery**

Procedures like contraception surgery or abortion surgery may cause cervical adhesions, leading to retained menstrual blood and delayed menstruation. Therefore, women should pay attention in daily life and preferably adopt effective contraception methods.

**Reason 5: Hormonal Imbalance**

Conditions like polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS) or premature ovarian failure can also cause delayed menstruation in women. Women are advised to undergo timely examinations, and if abnormalities are detected, prompt treatment should follow.

**Reason 6: Chronic Diseases**

Common chronic diseases such as hepatitis, tuberculosis, tumors, or hypothyroidism often lead to delayed menstruation due to nutritional deficiencies.

**Warm Reminder:** In general, menstrual periods that are advanced or delayed by up to 7 days (including 7 days) are considered normal. If menstrual delays exceed two weeks and pregnancy is confirmed not to be the cause, it should be considered menstrual irregularity. Early medical examination is recommended to identify the cause and then receive appropriate treatment.
