Precautions for installing electronic truck scales

by tueahew010 on 2012-03-08 09:52:19

Shanghai Qin Chou Industrial Company specializes in selling electronic scales, electronic floor scales, electronic balances, electronic desk scales, stainless steel electronic scales, hydraulic pallet truck scales, commercial goods parts cabinets, etc. Improper assembly of a floor scale can shorten its lifespan and cause sensor damage during operation. It may also result in floor scale displacement issues. Therefore, the assembly of floor scales must focus on the following points. The assembly of electronic floor scales should first have a relatively suitable and spacious area, ensuring that the foundation has a large load-bearing capacity.

Load-bearing capacity requirements for the foundation: construction must follow different regions and environments, and the load-bearing capacity of each support point must be greater than the maximum overload that may occur during the weighing process. Taking a 60-ton electronic floor scale as an example: its weighing platform weighs 10 tons, and the rear axle load of the loaded truck is 50 tons. Assuming an overload of 1.25 times, the impact load coefficient when mounting the weighing platform is 1.3. Thus, the maximum load at each support point is: W = 1/2 * 50t * 1.25 * 1.3 + 1/2 * 10 = 43.125t. During the construction of the foundation, it is best to integrate all support points into one overall structure. In individual cases, two support points can also be built on one concrete foundation. Under stress conditions, the bearing capacity of each foundation block must not allow independent sinking changes. For electronic bench scales, automatic control valves are used.

The resistance force of longitudinal and transverse anti-collision piers: the transverse collision force is smaller and can be manufactured according to the dimensions given in the foundation diagram, while the longitudinal collision force is larger. Taking a 60-ton electronic vehicle scale as an example: the maximum load of the car M1 = 60t * 1.25 = 75t = 75000kg, the weighing platform M2 = 10000kg, and the car speed is (maximum) v = 10km/h. To ensure minimal installation error and high accuracy of the scale, the following requirements apply to the support points: the levelness of a single weighing platform should be within 1/500, and the height difference between each support point should not exceed ±3mm.

If everything is done well during the installation process in your location, then over time, the lifespan of your floor scale will definitely be longer than before.
