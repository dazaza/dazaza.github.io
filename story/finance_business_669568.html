Response: Why is the Fourth Military Medical University recommended, but enrollment must be done at Shaanxi Yucai College? Wu Jiangtao was told that he needed to go to Yucai to exchange for an acceptance letter, and after that, he could report to the Fourth Military Medical University. With high hopes, Wu Jiangtao and his father boarded a train heading north, reaching Shaanxi Yucai College in Baoji. The students thought, "Recalling some events during our time at Hunan University, things seemed somewhat suspicious." Before publication, the reporter also learned a significant piece of information: Bai Hengli stated to the media that the Yucai School actually did not have the qualifications to offer a five-year bachelor's degree program. http://hnu.zikao.hn.cn/, foreign diploma processing. Like Chen Shanshan, many of her classmates found employers willing to hire them, but they couldn't sign contracts without their diplomas. Regarding Hunan University admission inquiries, on January 22nd in Xi'an, "At first, the intermediaries who introduced us to the Hunan Adult College all guaranteed that if there were any problems with schooling, they would always take care of it," said Wu Jiangtao helplessly. Now, when trying to contact those intermediaries, some have passed away, some have changed phone numbers, and others have simply gone into hiding. "When we arrived at Yucai, there were about 200 new students, some left unsatisfied after seeing the school." Ultimately, the 2009 graduating class of "undergraduates" totaled 86 people, including 70 clinical majors and 16 Hunan University majors. These students came from all over the country, introduced by intermediaries. To attend their dream military academy, they each paid between 30,000 to 130,000 yuan to intermediaries or middlemen. Claiming "honesty in education," after five years, they admitted not having the qualifications for undergraduate teaching. Facing student doubts, on January 20th, Dean Yan Dongyun of Shaanxi Yucai College told reporters, "The reason we issued diplomas from Hunan University of Chinese Medicine was because the Fourth Military Medical University's sponsored training certificates had not yet been processed. We issued these certificates first to facilitate student employment, considering the students' needs. The sponsored training certificates will still be processed and then distributed."

The students also harbored doubts about the school’s teaching quality. Upon entering, there were only two laboratories – one for specimens and another used as a classroom. “Clinical medicine students lacked hands-on practice.” When they arrived, many people welcoming the freshmen were dressed in military uniforms. At the orientation meeting, Wu Jiangtao and his fellow classmates were informed that they needed to sign an agreement with the sponsoring party – the personnel bureaus of Huanggang City and Xiaogan City in Hubei Province. They believed they were enrolled under the joint name of the Fourth Military Medical University, but even today, six months after graduation, they have yet to receive the graduation and degree certificates promised by the recruitment advertisements of Central South University's self-study examination.

On the "School Introduction" page of the Yucai College website, the reporter read: "Shaanxi Yucai College was founded in 1995 and is a vocational college specializing in medical and nursing programs. The school places 'honesty in education and quality in establishing the school' as its primary educational principle..." "During the first semester near winter break, students from other schools could buy discounted train tickets home with their student IDs, but we never received ours." After several days of negotiation with the school, the students received a dark red "student ID," marked as "Fourth Military Medical University Cadet Certificate," issued by the "Military Affairs Department of the Fourth Military Medical University." But not long after, the school took back these IDs, "saying they needed to register them, and they were never returned."

This person claimed to have admission quotas for the Fourth Military Medical University. He told Wu Jiangtao: You'll wear military uniforms at the military academy, though you won’t have military status, your treatment will be similar to regular military academy students. The diplomas and degrees you’ll receive will be the same, allowing you to pursue further studies or apply for physician qualification exams without issue. "Just pay 30,000 yuan, and you'll receive a pre-admission notice from the school, which can be exchanged for an official notice upon arrival." They didn’t know that even with a sponsored training certificate, they wouldn't be eligible for the national professional physician qualification exam since only students enrolled through the national unified system could participate, and sponsored students weren’t included.

In the "Sponsored Student Agreement" signed between the students and the Personnel Bureau of Qianjiang and Huanggang in Hubei Province, it was stipulated that graduates could participate in the practicing physician qualification exam. More bizarrely, after persistent urging, the school collected 3,800 yuan from each student and issued them a counterfeit junior college diploma. In 2004, at the age of 19, Wu Jiangtao (a pseudonym) failed the college entrance exam. Through a friend's introduction, he met someone claiming to guarantee his admission to a military academy. "This person works in education and has quite some influence locally," Wu Jiangtao said. This individual had successfully introduced many failed candidates to schools before. Hearing about the possibility of attending a military academy, Wu Jiangtao quickly gave up the opportunity to study in Nanchang. A few days later, he received a pre-admission notice from "Shaanxi Yucai College" – "You have been accepted as a five-year undergraduate clinical medicine major at the Military Medical University... Please hold this notice and proceed to Shaanxi Yucai College to exchange it for the formal acceptance notice and report."

At the time, everyone was puzzled. It was agreed that it was the Fourth Military Medical University, but the "Sponsored Student Agreement" showed that after graduation, the diploma would be from the Second Military Medical University's Nanjing Medical College. The school explained: "You need to stay in Baoji for a year first, then re-sign the agreement with the Fourth Military Medical University, and then return to the Fourth Military Medical University for study." In July 2009, as the only batch of "undergraduate" students at Shaanxi Yucai College, these 86 people graduated but failed to receive the graduation certificates and sponsored agreements promised in the original acceptance notices. After repeated requests from students and parents, in October 2009, some students, after paying 3,800 yuan to the school, received a junior college diploma, but the issuing institution turned out to be "Hunan University of Chinese Medicine," stamped with the school's official seal and the personal seal of the president "You Zhaoling." Guangdong student Chen Shanshan (pseudonym) finally found a job, "It’s already numbered on my Hunan University diploma, but when I presented it, they said it was fake." Chen Shanshan and her classmates hurriedly logged onto the "People's Republic of China Academic Qualification Certificate Inquiry Website" displayed on the diploma, only to find that their diplomas had no registration records.

To attend a "military academy," one must first pay the intermediary fee. Bai Hengli, the director of the school's admissions and placement office, stated that the reason for not issuing diplomas currently was due to the difficulty in managing this group of students. As of now, the materials required for issuing diplomas have not been submitted, causing delays in the issuance process. He simultaneously admitted to the media that Hunan University of Chinese Medicine and Shaanxi Yucai College had no cooperative educational relationship, and the diplomas were purchased by Yucai. Currently, these students are unable to find jobs or take relevant professional qualification exams. They remain stranded in Baoji, anxiously waiting in a hostel for the school's resolution. "In February, the National Practicing Physician Qualification Examination will begin registration. If we don't even have our diplomas, it will delay us for at least another year!" Originally believing they attended a prestigious military academy, after paying huge intermediary fees and enduring five years of study, the 86 graduates of Shaanxi Yucai College suddenly realized that their university "bachelor" experience may have been a scam, possibly a shattered dream.

Yan Dongyun stated that how this batch of undergraduates was recruited, the college had no idea, as "recruitment was handled by the three local personnel bureaus, completely unrelated to the school; the school only provided faculty and completed the training." As for the specifics of the cooperative education model, Yan Dongyun said only the school founder knew. She told reporters that the school had sent someone on a business trip to handle this matter, and they had not returned yet, so the situation was unknown. Five years of "bachelor's" study resulted in forged junior college diplomas. "Not only that, there were also issues with the diploma numbers," Wu Jiangtao said. According to relevant regulations, the first five digits of the "certificate number" on the diploma represent the school code, and the sixth digit represents the type of education code. After checking, the "Hunan University of Chinese Medicine" diploma numbers they received should belong to the Railway College, while the sixth digit code belonged to "online education." After five years of "bachelor's" study, they received a junior college diploma, which the students found hard to accept. However, subsequent events caused them even more despair. After multiple unsuccessful negotiations with the school, the students posted online about their experiences. Only after media involvement did the school issue a notice on January 13th: According to the teaching outline and university requirements, all students must have an internship report arranged by the college at a hospital, six typical medical cases, and one graduation thesis. Due to the lack of materials from the following students, your graduation process cannot proceed normally. Please send the above materials to the college by February 6th. Those who fail to comply within the deadline will bear the responsibility themselves… The attached list showed that out of the 86 students, only 29 had submitted all required materials. "This is obviously another deceptive tactic by the school." Students who have negotiated multiple times and seen no hope have lost trust in the school.
