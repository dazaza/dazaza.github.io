Tang, Xinjiang, transfer

Yesterday, news broke on the Internet site, stating that Tang's joining of Xinjiang is really a certainty, and the Jiangsu Provincial Sports Bureau has agreed in principle to release him. However, leaders from the Sports Bureau of Jiangsu Province and the Nanjing Steel club have denied this message for the first time, and Tang himself has also avoided commenting on it in the news.

The website reported the other day that a responsible individual within the CBA had disclosed that aspects of the principle of Jiangsu mean for him to go next season. The paper also analyzed that currently, the Dragons have played more than 10 warm-up matches, and Tang's figure hasn't been in the team, which indicates that Tang's departure from Jiangsu is in the countdown stage, and his transfer to Xinjiang is a certainty. In fact, due to injuries, Tang also arrived in Lianyungang on Thursday to fuel the race for the Dragons team. Yesterday afternoon, when the reporter called Tang regarding the transfer, Min Wang, the GM of the Nanjing Iron Club, replied: "As a club manager, I don’t know about the things which are being written online; they are just writings, but truly did not happen." Then the reporter called Director Zhang Shilin of the Jiangsu Provincial Sports Bureau CBA division, who was in a field meeting and said: "Let us call once I am back, the things I really do not know. Tang submits a transfer request every year, but I still have not heard of any confirmed switch, I am in the field, this isn’t clear." Whether at the club level or the CBA Sports Bureau, they have denied the claim that Tang has clinched a transfer to Xinjiang.
