Pharmacological properties of botulinum toxin:

Botulinum toxin is a high molecular protein exotoxin produced by Clostridium botulinum, divided into eight antigenic types: A, B, C1, C2, [url=http://www.manmeirong.com/zx/ztzflx/][color=black]cost of autologous fat breast augmentation[/color][/url], D, E, F, G. Types A, B, E, F, and G can act on the human nervous system, hindering the transmission of signals between nerve-muscle junctions; Type A is the most toxic, which is what we refer to as botulinum toxin injection.

There are three types of Type A botulinum toxin used clinically: Botox produced in the U.S., Dysport produced in the UK, and BTX-A produced in China. In terms of potency, 1U of BTX-A equals 1U of Botox, equivalent to 3-5U of Dysport. Clinically, there is only one type of Type B botulinum toxin used: Myobloc produced in the U.S.

The action of botulinum toxin on nerves can be divided into three steps:

1. Botulinum toxin binds to specific membrane receptors on pre-synaptic cholinergic neurons;

2. The toxin-receptor complex enters the nerve endings through cellular endocytosis;

3. The toxin cleaves certain proteins, preventing the release of acetylcholine from within the cell.

Through this process, it leads to muscle fibers no longer producing contraction. Botulinum toxin does not block the transmission of nerve excitation; neither nerves nor muscles suffer from excitability or conductivity damage. This effect is also called chemical denervation, or leg reduction methods.

Indications, contraindications, and safety of botulinum toxin injection for wrinkle removal:

Botulinum toxin is effective in removing dynamic wrinkles caused by muscle contraction in middle-aged and young people. Clinical observations have found that botulinum toxin injection is also effective in removing elderly people's wrinkles, but the difference is that elderly people's wrinkles are deeper and groove-like. After injecting botulinum toxin, the wrinkles become shallower rather than completely disappearing.

1) Although botulinum toxin has no teratogenicity, pregnant and breastfeeding women should preferably avoid it;

5) Infection at the injection site;

6) Patients allergic to albumin or botulinum toxin and those with allergic constitutions.

Effect of botulinum toxin injection for wrinkle removal:

Generally, muscles start to relax 1-3 days after botulinum toxin injection, with a "tightening" sensation locally; 7-14 days later, muscles fully relax and become paralyzed, resulting in smooth skin and disappearance or reduction of wrinkles; the efficacy lasts on average 3-6 months. This is because while botulinum toxin acts on the nerve-muscle junction, the myelin sheath of pre-synaptic neurons regenerates, forming new synapses near old ones, releasing acetylcholine again, restoring muscle function, and thus the wrinkle-removal effect disappears, a process that averages 3-6 months.
