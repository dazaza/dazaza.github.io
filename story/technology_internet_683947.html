Introduction to Hong Kong:

Hong Kong is located at 114°15′ East Longitude and 22°15′ North Latitude, along the southern coast of China. It consists of Hong Kong Island, Kowloon Peninsula, the inland areas of the New Territories, and 262 large and small islands (Outlying Islands).

Hong Kong is a free port. Except for tobacco, strong liquor, and fuel for power (such as gasoline and diesel), no tariffs are imposed on other imported goods. Hong Kong's economy is renowned for free trade, low taxation, and minimal government intervention. The financial industry, real estate, tourism, and manufacturing are the four pillar industries supporting Hong Kong's economic development. The top ten Hong Kong products include ready-made garments, electronic products, textiles, watches, printed materials, plastic products, metal products, food and beverages, industrial machinery, and jewelry. For many years, Hong Kong has been selected by international renowned rating agencies and organizations as the world's freest economic system. Hong Kong is an international financial center, an international shipping center, and a regional trade center in the Asia-Pacific region, possessing a superior geographical location that is irreplaceable by many neighboring countries and regions.

Advantages of Registering a Hong Kong Company:

1. There are no industry restrictions for Hong Kong companies. Any legal business can be operated without the need to apply for additional licenses;

2. Hong Kong is one of the freest trading ports globally with free logistics movement: no tariffs are levied on general cargo imports and exports; funds can move freely in Hong Kong, and various foreign currencies can be exchanged and transferred at any time;

3. Taxation Policy and Rates for Hong Kong Companies: Fewer tax types and lower rates in Hong Kong;

Hong Kong companies only have two taxes: one is a one-time stamp duty on registered capital, with a rate of 1/1000; the other is profits tax, with a rate of 16.5%.

Special consumer goods (such as tobacco and alcohol) are subject to import and export tariffs.

4. Utilize Hong Kong’s reputation to create a Hong Kong brand, enhancing the company's international image;

5. Allow shell companies to exist and permit offshore business operations;

Conditions for Registering a Hong Kong Company:

1. Company Name

The general provisions of Hong Kong's Companies Ordinance regarding company names are as follows:

(a) A company may use an English name, a Chinese name, or both English and Chinese names simultaneously for registration, but it cannot use a name combining English and Chinese.

(b) An English company name must end with [Limited], and a Chinese name must end with [有限公司].

If the company name includes the following words or terms, the Chief Executive's approval must be obtained before usage:

(01) Building society (02) Chamber of commerce (03) Cooperative (04) Kaifong (05) Mass transit (06) Underground railway (07) Municipal (08) Tourist association (09) Trustee (10) Savings (11) Municipal (12) Underground railway (13) Metro (14) Cooperation (15) Trustee (16) Housing cooperative (17) Trustee (18) Chamber of commerce (19) Trust (20) Tourism association (21) Neighborhood (22) General Chamber of Commerce (23) Savings

2. Company Shareholders (At least one)

3. Company Directors (At least one)

Shareholders and directors can be the same entity, which can be a natural person or a limited company.

Natural persons can be of any nationality, but they must be at least 18 years old (excluding undischarged bankrupts or those with mental health issues).

Limited companies can be from any place of registration.

4. Statutory Secretary (Must be a Hong Kong company or a Hong Kong resident)

5. Business Registration Address (Must be a Hong Kong address)

Used to receive government letters and notifications. The company does not need to rent or purchase specific property, but it cannot solely use a postal box as the business registration address.

6. Registered Capital (As stipulated by Hong Kong company law: Standard registered capital is HKD 10,000)

Information Required from Clients:

1. Company Name;

2. Registered Capital (As stipulated by Hong Kong company law: Standard registered capital is HKD 10,000);

3. Copies of identity proofs of shareholders and directors;

4. Shareholding ratios of each shareholder;

5. Permanent addresses and contact information of shareholders and directors;

Full Set of Statutory Documents for Hong Kong Companies:

According to Article 32 of the Hong Kong Companies Ordinance, the full set of statutory documents after the establishment of a company includes:

1. Certificate of Incorporation

2. Business Registration Certificate (Valid for one year from the date of incorporation, renewed annually)

3. Memorandum and Articles of Association (Generally 18 volumes)

4. Company Chop and Signature Stamp (One each, used for signing checks or other documents)

5. Company Steel Seal (One)

6. Stock Book

7. Company Statutory Registers (Statutory book), including:

(a) Minutes Book

(b) Register of Members

(c) Register of Transfers

(d) Register of Directors

(e) Register of Secretaries

(f) Register of Charges

8. All these documents come with a "precious hard box", commonly known as the "green box".
