Killing star refuses 25 million to join AC Milan, but the Rossoneri have already got a stronger one

by kersfe3f23 on 2009-12-09 09:28:23

The Killer Star Refuses 25 Million for AC Milan, But the Red and Black Army Already Has a Stronger One

According to Sina Sports, Manchester United and Arsenal have publicly announced their interest in Edin Dzeko, while Manchester City quietly offered Wolfsburg a staggering 25 million bid. However, this Bosnian striker, who has already scored six goals in the Bundesliga this season and three in the Champions League, rejected Manchester City's invitation. The UK's Daily Star revealed that Dzeko told the Wolfsburg club that his preferred transfer target is AC Milan, and he would only consider other teams if a move to AC Milan is not possible.

Italian media soon followed up with more news: within the next three weeks, possibly during Christmas, Galliani will meet again with Dzeko and his father to discuss future matters. They had a successful meeting in Sarajevo during the summer and even signed a contract then, but it was never officially announced because of opposition from Wolfsburg's coach Armin Veh. If AC Milan still wants Dzeko, the contract remains valid. Dzeko stated that money is not an issue; the annual salary in AC Milan's contract is about the same as what he currently earns at Wolfsburg. Dzeko has been a die-hard AC Milan fan since childhood and also likes AC Milan's playing style, which is why he made this choice.

Given Dzeko's enthusiasm, what stance will AC Milan take? On one hand, when Dzeko previously publicly expressed hope to join AC Milan next summer, and Italian media hyped it up, AC Milan remained silent. At least in the winter transfer window, AC Milan no longer has non-EU spots available, so things can calm down temporarily. The club definitely does not want transfer market issues to affect the mood of players like Borrello and Huntelaar. What about the future?

Firstly, despite receiving huge sponsorship from Emirates Airlines, AC Milan still needs to carefully consider every deal as they need funds in many areas. Within their limited budget, is the position of center-forward the most urgent one AC Milan needs to reinforce? The answer is no. If Dzeko comes, what about Borrello, who has performed so well? Or Huntelaar, who is starting to show improvement? And what about Paroschi, co-owned with Parma? Not to mention that Inzaghi may not leave AC Milan in the summer. In comparison, although Antonini and Abate have alleviated the problems at full-back positions, with Odo, Janco, and Zambrotta getting older, AC Milan needs at least one more full-back. They also need someone to replace Kaladze as the first-choice substitute at center-back. In midfield, Gattuso is more likely to leave than Inzaghi on the forward line. On the wings, AC Milan's squad depth is still insufficient, and Seedorf also needs a successor...

From a technical perspective, Dzeko scores better than Borrello, but Borrello moves laterally faster and holds the ball better. Dzeko excels at handling the ball in tight spaces within the penalty area, while Borrello can help push the team's attack into the opponent's penalty area and keep the ball there, creating opportunities for teammates. Both have their strengths, but Borrello has played in Serie A for many years, while Dzeko's adaptability is still unknown.

In such circumstances, if AC Milan makes a rational choice, Galliani will tell Dzeko during their Christmas meeting that with Borrello's recovery, AC Milan's recruitment needs have changed. Giving up on Dzeko is certainly regrettable, but using the money saved from not signing him to buy players for more urgently needed positions could be a wise decision from a global perspective.
