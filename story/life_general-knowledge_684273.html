Because it's you, because it's me, my life has also been brilliant.

In the late summer of the south, the sky slightly reveals a light blue, and I remember the moment I first met you. My heart feels peaceful, pure, full, and restrained. You looked at me from the end of the crowd, your eyes shimmering with a deep blue. It was like a layer of water, thin and transparent.

This is the south, where there is plenty of sunshine and the air is filled with the fragrance of narcissus. The coastline is calm in the misty flower fragrance, and the fine sands gently express their admiration for the seawater. Birds returning to their nests perch on the shallow water edge, humming to the sunset.

(1)

She came from the north, where it snows more than 300 days out of the year. The snowflakes flying all over the sky have turned this place into a snow city. People living here cannot see the sun; they only have winter, no summer.

She liked summer, liked the southern summer, and liked that golden orange sun, just as southerners like snow. Her childhood wish was to leave this place, go to the south, and see that golden orange sun, basking in it from sunrise to sunset.

She liked others to call her Xi, just like her name. Xi is a female protagonist in Gu Cheng's novel. She liked Gu Cheng's writings, becoming passionately obsessed with them. Oh, she also liked a painting called "Where Do We Come From? What Are We? Where Are We Going?" She liked this man who ran off to Tahiti because he abandoned the bustling Parisian life to run to the primitive society.

At the age of eighteen, in her eyes, she was independent. So, carrying her childhood dream and that innocence, she traveled alone by train to the south, chasing after that golden orange sun.

After several days of travel, she was already exhausted, but the scene before her eyes made her excited. A solitary journey can be lonely, but when she looked up, she saw the golden orange sun she had longed for so much. The summer sun shone brilliantly, and two streams of tears flowed down her eyelids. No one knew whether these were tears of pain or tears of emotion.

Suddenly, someone patted her on the shoulder. She snapped back to reality, seeing a blurry figure. Perhaps she wasn't yet accustomed to this golden orange sun. She closed her eyes for a moment and saw a tall, slim boy with glasses whose outline was particularly distinct under the sunlight. After a few seconds of hesitation, he said, "Hey, don't you think my arm is sore?" Only then did she notice the tissue he had been holding up for a long time. She smiled awkwardly and took the tissue, "I'm not 'hey,' but thank you for the tissue." "Then may I ask what your name is, miss?" She smiled and said, "You can call me Xi, how about you?" "Zuo," he coldly replied. Actually, he thought quite well of her. She was so fair-skinned, as if she came from a snowy domain. He liked her long hair reaching her shoulders and her dress with scattered floral patterns.

A solitary journey, a solitary loneliness, she seemed to have found someone to talk to, wanting to tell him all her dreams. "I came here to seek the golden orange sun of the southern summer. I like your sun here. I come from the snowy city in the north..."

There was a loud bang, and no one knew what happened. She just felt an excruciating pain, the kind that dislocates bones. Beside her was a flipped-over small car, and not far away was Xi. Blood was seeping out of her body, staining her floral dress red, which was especially glaring under the sunlight. Slowly, she closed her eyes, faintly hearing, "Help! Help!"

Fourteen days later, he learned that he and she had been in a car accident, but she no longer remembered it. The doctor said she had lost her memory, forever. I cried while laughing, thinking of this girl pursuing her dreams. She had just embarked on her journey of dreams, hadn't even properly admired the golden orange sun. Unfair, why does heaven treat her like this, just because she doesn't belong here?

Twenty-seven days later, she started to love painting the sun, perhaps a remnant of a dream in her memory.

She liked to bask in the sun, from sunrise to sunset.

Basking in the sun, whether sunny or cloudy, seeing her dazed expression caused him indescribable pain.

But there was one thing that could make her quietly stay indoors: painting the sun. As soon as someone mentioned painting the sun, she would become very excited, gripping her brush and meticulously painting suns of various sizes on paper. Once finished, she would ask if they looked good. Seeing his nod and hearing his compliment, she would smile happily. Then she would stick the painted suns on the wall, muttering as she stuck them, "Look at the sun, and you'll feel warm."

Sometimes, she would sit obediently in his arms, pointing at the suns plastered all over the room, happily talking to herself: "It's great, there are so many suns, you must not be cold anymore." In fact, it's you who isn't cold, he thought.

Yes, not cold anymore, he gently embraced her thin shoulders, cherishing her like a treasure.

Who would have thought that in this southern summer, so many things could happen due to a chance encounter.

But who can truly understand the cycle of fate, and who dares to love this grand change.

In the scorching summer, all memories are related to youth, unrelated to happiness.

(2)

"Humming to the beautiful sunset, infinite good though nearing dusk, people in the vast sea of humanity remain strangers. The mountains dotted with wildflowers embrace wisps of soft silk, gazing at the deep blue of the Sky Castle, listening to the gentle music of mountain streams, intoxicated by the clear wind, loving the end of summer in the south." I like calling you a talented woman, you describe our love this way, but I don't like: "Humming to the beautiful sunset, infinite good though nearing dusk."

You're named Ao. You always say you like a simple life, but you live rather complexly, just like me, I think. And I never claim to live simply.

You say I am melancholic, with a suspicion of pretending to be profound. I smiled, I laughed and said, actually, I don't even understand myself.

I love drinking milk tea, thinking it's my only hobby. I like sitting in the corner of a milk tea shop, listening to Jay Chou's songs playing inside. I like chewing pearls, and I like looking at you. I like dragging you along to drink milk tea with me, I like having you buy me milk tea, because I've started to like you.

The song playing is Jay Chou's "Simple Love," "The river breeze blows through the hair/ Holding your hand, a mysterious touch/ I want to take you back to my grandmother's house/ Together watching the sunset until we both fall asleep."

I like this song, simple and pure, just like you.

Fifty-four days later, the fourteenth day since I met Xi.

We began to grow distant, not contacting each other for a long time, only occasionally exchanging pleasantries.

"I have no mind, my thoughts withered yesterday. My heart died in the cradle of summer." This is the preface of a diary entry of yours. Yes, you have no mind because you are simple. Your heart withered yesterday because you don't have a yesterday, your yesterday you don't even understand yourself. If it's the cradle of summer, everything is so good, why does your heart die in the cradle?

You say we are merely passers-by in each other's lives. Love doesn't wait for anyone. You being able to appear in my life, I am moved. I say, you are an important passer-by in my life, and that's all.

In the flourishing years, meeting and knowing you was at the wrong time.

(3)

He is me, my name is Zuo.

Nothing special, except for being a bit melancholic.

I like the chapter of the Aegean Sea: "Wanting the dead branches to carve our full stop / Wanting the crescent moon to return again from round / Wanting answers to make who miss more / And you only favor whispers sweet / Listening petals fall how many times lingering / Listening birds sing whose poem / Listening heartbreak knock on whose past / And will you laugh ignoring yesterday."

Girl in the floral dress, stop chasing that golden orange sun. It will always leave you far behind. Live a simple life, and everything will be fine.

Because it's you, because it's me, my life has also been brilliant.

Because summer is summer, south is the south.
