Samsung's Promotion of Smartwatch Concept Benefits Rivals

Samsung is aggressively promoting its smartwatch, the Galaxy Gear. However, industry insiders believe that many of Samsung's competitors in this field, such as Sony and Apple, will benefit from this promotion. This may sound strange, but it is indeed the case.

Samsung aired its first TV advertisement for the Galaxy Gear on Sunday. Since smartwatches themselves are a relatively new product, Samsung focused its promotion on the concept behind the smartwatch rather than the product itself. This kind of preparatory promotion also benefits its competitors.

The two one-minute science programs and cartoon videos released by Samsung both feature smartwatches. The message these advertisements aim to convey is very clear: the future is here, all thanks to Samsung.

The advertisements created by Samsung for the Gear are very similar to the initial advertisements designed by Apple for the iPhone. In fact, they are both paving the way for the launch of new products.

Huge Marketing Budget

In the past, Samsung has shown its willingness to spend heavily on promoting its products in the U.S. market. It is said that last year its advertising expenditure in the U.S. exceeded that of Apple, and over the weekend it published a full 10-page spread advertisement in The New York Times. Judging from this, Samsung has set aside a large advertising budget to stimulate consumer interest in smartwatches, a new type of consumer technology.

Consumers who understand technology might appreciate Samsung's approach, but the real challenge facing Samsung is to get ordinary consumers who are not tech-savvy to accept this new technology. Therefore, it is likely to release more advertisements to actively promote this concept. If Apple or other smartwatch manufacturers launch more mature and complete smartwatch products at this time, the situation would be quite interesting.

Survival of the Fittest, Winner Takes All

Samsung's problem is not that it is the first to open up the smartwatch market. Just look at how high the brand recognition of the iPad is in the tablet market. Its problem lies in whether consumers will consider its Gear to be the best smartwatch product. It is too early to judge this point now, but this is definitely a weakness that its competitors can exploit.

The price of the Gear is $299, which, to be honest, is not cheap because smartwatches need to be connected to a smartphone to fully function.

Now, we just have to wait and see what Apple's upcoming smartwatch will be like, and whether other manufacturers can elevate the concept of smartwatches to a higher level.
