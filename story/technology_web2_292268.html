Key points for choosing a specialized school

by bridge2009 on 2009-12-04 14:17:45

The curriculum of specialized schools is formulated by each school according to its own teaching policy. Therefore, even if the schools are in the same field or even have the same major, the content of the courses may vary greatly. Therefore, choosing a good school is very important. One should have a clear understanding of three aspects: the target career, the condition of the school, and one's own qualifications.

1. Is it an officially recognized school? Every year, there are cases where students realize after enrollment that the so-called regular specialized school they entered is actually an unlicensed school without the approval of the governor of their prefecture. Graduates from such unlicensed schools do not receive diplomas recognized by the state, and their academic qualifications are not acknowledged. They also cannot obtain the titles of "Specialist" or "Advanced Specialist," apply for scholarships, or even purchase student monthly passes. Therefore, first, one should confirm with the local specialized school association whether the school is officially recognized.

2. Are the educational content and number of teachers sufficient? The course schedules at specialized schools are usually tight and the teaching density is high. Whether the curriculum is scientific and the quality of the teachers determine the strength of the school and its students. One can request materials from several schools of interest for comparison, such as which school’s curriculum content is more closely related to the career one wants to pursue, who the teachers are, and whether there are well-known figures in this field. If possible, it is best to find someone who has researched this specialty to help review the materials.

3. Are the facilities and equipment complete? If the curriculum and teachers are the software, then the facilities and equipment of the school are the hardware, which must not be overlooked. The applicability of the skills learned in actual work is highly related to the level of hardware. If the equipment is outdated and lacks cutting-edge new facilities, one will not be able to handle the actual work. However, it is difficult for laypeople to judge whether the equipment is complete. If possible, getting advice from people currently working in this field would be ideal.

4. Is the job placement guidance comprehensive, and how is the historical employment situation? In specialized schools, job placement guidance is usually jointly conducted by the homeroom teacher, department head, and the career counseling office. Most schools put a lot of effort into job placement guidance, but the guiding policies of different schools vary, as do the tendencies of the companies they place students in and the types of jobs. It is best to understand and compare the job placement policies, methods, and historical employment directions through the school's materials.

5. What is the total tuition fee? One needs to know beforehand how much the tuition fee is for the first year, how much it is for subsequent years, and when the payment time is. It is particularly important to check whether there are fees that are not clearly stated in the admissions guide but are actually mandatory. Additionally, consider textbook fees, qualification exam registration fees, and costs for participating in school-organized training activities. Do not simply focus on the amount of money; instead, see whether the amount matches the teaching content and whether paying a little more can truly result in learning more.

6. How is the reputation of the school? When choosing a school, it is advisable to listen to opinions from multiple sources. Do not make a decision hastily just because the admission guide describes the school in glowing terms. It is best to seek the opinions of teachers and seniors, understand everyone's evaluation of the school, and use it as a reference.

7. Actual investigation during school open days Many schools arrange school tours and one-day trial enrollments. You can take advantage of these opportunities to conduct an actual investigation. While the general content can be understood through the admission guide, the actual atmosphere of the school, its location, surrounding environment, and even the time required to commute can only be truly understood by seeing and walking around. Do not just skim over things. It is best to organize your questions or points you want to understand in detail beforehand, and seize the opportunity to ask school staff during the visit to learn more. Visit multiple schools and compare them. With careful consideration, you are sure to ultimately select the one you are most satisfied with.
