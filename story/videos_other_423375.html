Key Tip: Not long ago, Beijing significantly increased parking fees, which most people might have heard about, with the highest parking fee reaching 20 yuan per hour. Many cities in the country are also gradually increasing the charging standards for public parking spaces, hoping to curb the rapid increase in car ownership through this measure. However, the Zhenhai District of Ningbo recently went against this trend. Starting from April 8th, Zhenhai will cancel all parking fees for public parking spaces within the urban area that are state-owned, including on-street parking spaces. There will be nearly 2500 free parking spaces in the urban area of Zhenhai.

Analysis of Zhenhai's Cancellation of Public Parking Space Charges

This morning (April 8th), Ms. Wu, a resident of Zhenhai, drove to Gabai Supermarket on South Street to shop. After parking her car in a parking space, no one came to charge her, which made her feel somewhat surprised.

Ms. Wu, a resident of Zhenhai: Originally, or if you buy a bread or something, taking only a few minutes, having to pay a fee makes one feel like committing a traffic violation. Now we try to park in the designated parking spaces; surely no one wants to intentionally violate the rules.

Ms. Wu can park for free because today is the first day of free parking for all public parking spaces in the urban area of Zhenhai. The Zhenhai District Government had previously posted an announcement on its website regarding the rectification of illegal parking, one of the key contents being the cancellation of public parking space charges in the Zhaobao Mountain Street area starting from April 8th, 2010. It is understood that the annual average growth rate of small cars in Zhenhai District reached 34.6%, with approximately 12,000 private cars with less than 9 seats by the end of 2009, and about 5,000 small cars in the old city area, increasing at a rate of 150-300 vehicles per month. Compared to the rapidly growing number of vehicles, the number of parking spaces is much less.

Deputy Captain Wang Jifeng of the Zhenhai Joint Law Enforcement Team of Ningbo City: Zhenhai, being an old city, lacks complete urban infrastructure functions, thus causing more cars and fewer parking spaces.

In 2009, there were a total of 1,240 indoor parking spaces in the residential areas of the old town of Zhenhai. Around government offices and commercial buildings, there were a total of 2,842 ground and underground parking spaces, including 878 roadside parking spaces and 138 parking spaces in front of hotels and restaurants. To solve the problem of difficult parking, the Zhenhai government implemented the Travel Difficulty Improvement Project in 2009, constructing several state-owned public parking lots with over a hundred parking spaces each, and demarcating roads, squares, and sidewalks to increase parking spaces. These two measures added a total of 1,132 parking spaces, bringing the total number of parking spaces in Zhenhai to over 5,000.

With the parking lots built and the demarcated paid parking spaces established, according to the original plan, the problem of difficult parking in the old town of Zhenhai would be greatly alleviated, and parking fees would also bring certain economic benefits. However, half a year later, Zhenhai chose to cancel the charges for public parking spaces.

Deputy Captain Wang Jifeng of the Zhenhai Joint Law Enforcement Team of Ningbo City: One is our plan to cancel the charges for public parking spaces demarcated on urban roads, and another is to cancel the charges for several parking lots, including the former Zhonghui Textile Factory site and the Material Bureau site parking lot.

Currently, many cities across the country are gradually increasing the charging standards for public parking spaces, hoping to curb the rapid increase in car ownership through this measure. Taking Beijing as an example, Beijing's new parking fee policy was implemented on April 1st, with parking fees doubling in 13 congested areas. However, Ningbo's Zhenhai District chose the opposite path, canceling the charges for public parking spaces and switching these state-owned public parking spaces from a charged state to a free one.

Why Is Free Lunch Not Being Eaten?

On several main roads in Zhenhai, violations of parking rules are very severe, with some cars even parked directly on no-parking signs.

Law Enforcement Officer of the Zhenhai Joint Law Enforcement Team of Ningbo City: Parking here is not allowed, there are parking lots nearby.

Law Enforcement Officer of the Zhenhai Joint Law Enforcement Team of Ningbo City: If warnings don't work, we take photos according to regulations and impose penalties.

Law Enforcement Officer of the Zhenhai Joint Law Enforcement Team of Ningbo City: This road is originally very narrow, there are parking spaces nearby, and further ahead is a parking lot that can accommodate 160 cars. People just want convenience, unwilling to walk a few meters.

Parking Lot Manager of Zhenhai Station Road, Ningbo: Since September last year.

Parking Lot Manager of Zhenhai Station Road, Ningbo: There are over 100 parking spaces.

Parking Lot Manager of Zhenhai Station Road, Ningbo: Normally, when there are no cars, there are about thirty or forty at most.

Parking Lot Manager of Zhenhai Station Road, Ningbo: The charge is 5 yuan per night, free for one hour, and only 5 yuan for 24 hours, which is quite cheap. Monthly subscription is also only over a hundred yuan.

Parking Lot Manager of Zhenhai Station Road, Ningbo: I don't know.

Parking Lot Manager of Zhenhai Station Road, Ningbo: Yes.

Private Car Owner: There aren't many cars parked in the parking lot, mainly due to inconvenience. For instance, it's convenient to park in front of the bank.

Private Car Owner: Just park in front of the bank, but there are warning signs there, and fines are issued promptly. For us, it would be better if there were convenient spots near Ningbo Bank, instead of crossing the traffic light here.

Parking Lot Manager of Daximen Road, Zhenhai, Ningbo: They prefer convenience, even though parking is free, they still don't use it. Poor management by urban management and traffic police used to result in many cars parking in the parking lot, but now they all park on the road, making it hard for pedestrians to pass.

It is preliminarily estimated that during peak times, there is still a shortage of over 2000 parking spaces in Zhenhai. On one side, the parking lots are empty, while on the other side, vehicles are randomly parked on the roads. The cancellation of charges for public parking spaces was introduced under such conditions. So what will the cancellation of charges for public parking spaces bring to Zhenhai's transportation?

Authorities Say Free Parking Aims to Guide Citizens' Habits

Zhenhai car owners received a big gift from the government, with all state-owned property parking spaces in the urban area becoming free starting today. Can free state-owned property parking spaces be feasible? And what changes will it bring to Zhenhai's transportation?

Private Car Owner in Zhenhai, Ningbo: Now the Zhenhai government is canceling parking fees for parking lots, which is good because sometimes when we do things, it takes only a little time, yet we have to pay for parking. When we move to another place, we have to pay again.

Private Car Owner in Zhenhai, Ningbo: That's definitely good, free parking is certainly good.

Private Car Owner in Zhenhai, Ningbo: There are very few parking spaces.

Private Car Owner in Zhenhai, Ningbo: Definitely yes.

After the cancellation of charges for public parking spaces, Deputy Captain Wang Jifeng of the Zhenhai Joint Law Enforcement Team of Ningbo City said that the original street parking attendants would be transformed into road administrators, responsible for guiding traffic and parking services. Some people worry that the cancellation of public parking space charges could lead to more problems, such as whether the paid parking spaces within residential areas would become neglected, and whether the free parking spaces in front of some hotels and restaurants would harm the interests of merchants. Regarding these doubts, Captain Wang of the Zhenhai Joint Law Enforcement Team stated that relevant management methods still need to be explored and improved based on future actual situations.

Deputy Captain Wang Jifeng of the Zhenhai Joint Law Enforcement Team of Ningbo City: Increase parking spaces, tap potential, secondly cancel charges for public parking spaces, thirdly increase law enforcement intensity, fourthly educate and promote awareness among the general public to abide by traffic rules and voluntarily park their vehicles in designated spaces.

During interviews, regarding the question of whether the chronic problem of random parking in Zhenhai will be resolved after the implementation of the cancellation of public parking space charges, relevant unit leaders indicated that results would only be visible after implementing the policy for a period of time, as habits cannot be formed in just a day or two. I think, whether it's raising the charging standard or canceling parking fees, as long as parking becomes standardized, traffic flows smoothly, and travelers feel comfortable, the measure is successful.
