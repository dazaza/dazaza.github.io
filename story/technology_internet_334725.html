Why Can't "Leftover Men" and "Leftover Women" Get Married?

By Xu Shuguang, Chairman of GreenTree Inn Hotel and 519.com Wine Direct Sales Network

Illustration by Chunming

In my previous blog post, I proposed a suggestion: "Tax 'Leftover Men' to Subsidize 'Leftover Women'". From the voting results in that post, you can see that the majority of people agree with my viewpoint. In this blog post, I want to discuss why "leftover men" and "leftover women" can't seem to get married. First, how do we define the concept of "leftover men" and "leftover women"? Many people are not entirely clear about it. We have just become familiar with some new terms: "leftover warriors", "must-remain guests", "Monkey King victorious fighters", and "Great Remaining Heaven". It is understood that the starting age for "leftover warriors" is 24 years old, which is roughly around the time of undergraduate graduation. With such a definition, there will be an abundance of "leftover men" and "leftover women". It cannot be denied that many college students fall in love, but how many actually end up together? Therefore, considering the majority's situation, I define "leftover men" and "leftover women" as follows: those who are 27 years old without a spouse after obtaining a bachelor's or associate degree, or those who are 30 years old without a partner after obtaining a master's degree.

This relatively new term, which has only become popular in the past year or two, vividly describes a social phenomenon: a considerable number of older unmarried young men and women spend their days either laughing bitterly or feeling melancholic because they can't find a "suitable" other half. These "leftovers" often have "good conditions": cars, houses, high education levels, high personal qualities, and high incomes. Yet, what is perplexing is that these seemingly incompatible male and female "leftovers" never seem to consider why they don't have a partner and continue to proudly stand alone.

Some "leftovers" and "bystanders" have tried to analyze the causes behind this social issue. The more concentrated factors include delays in academics and careers, pursuing perfect marriages, and financial dissatisfaction. To be fair, whether it's the "parties involved" or the "outsiders," everyone makes valid points. After all, this is a topic where "wise men see wisdom and good men see goodness," and everyone has their own right to speak. My view is that the phenomenon of "leftover men" and "leftover women" is caused by three reasons.

First, we have made mistakes in our views on marriage, influenced both by family and, more importantly, by individuals. For example, some just wait idly for happiness to come, believing that doing well is less important than marrying well, being overly materialistic and greedy, or preferring leisure over hard work. Many people feel that when today's young men and women date or look for partners, they mainly consider two things: material conditions and emotional satisfaction. Material conditions mainly refer to visible wealth indicators like cars, houses, and money; emotional satisfaction refers to invisible emotional indicators like admiration, respect, or a sense of security between the two parties. Non-"leftovers" usually don't demand these things too strictly, requiring them not to be too high or having one of the two suffice. In contrast, most "leftover men" and "leftover women" either think the other party lacks "conditions" or claim they "don't have feelings," making them very "picky." In this negotiation towards a perfect marriage, their ages grow older, and their "remaining" status gradually escalates.

Second, on a deeper level, the influence of Western cultural infiltration plays a role. A glance at history textbooks reveals that whether it's the introduction of "Western learning to the East" or reform and opening-up, every introduction of foreign culture has brought about a "renewal of thought" in people's ideas. However, cultural infiltration is a double-edged sword. Western culture brings human rights and freedom but also takes away the traditional Confucian cultural values of knowing one's place. As a result, people increasingly indulge in material desires, are obsessed with material enjoyment, and restlessness becomes a widespread societal "ailment." This spirit of pursuit is especially evident in attitudes toward love and marriage. A typical example is, how many modern versions of the Liang Shanbo and Zhu Yingtai or Cui Yingying love stories exist? Thus, the profit-driven and greedy views of marriage in people's minds are shaped by mainstream social culture.

Third, the significantly improved social status and personal abilities of women. From the early advocacy of "gender equality" to the emergence of "career women" after reform and opening-up, women's competitiveness in society has grown stronger. The outdated model of depending on men for survival has changed greatly. Whether career women and leftover women can be equated is debatable. However, precisely because of these remaining "career women," their pride indirectly creates many "leftover men."

In summary, the formation of the "leftover men" and "leftover women" phenomenon is related to both subjective marital attitudes and societal trends, awareness, and culture. We have no reason to excessively criticize culture, nor should we blame the "leftovers." However, culture and people interact. People can choose their lifestyle based on their own requirements and don't necessarily have to conform to the overall societal cultural atmosphere, but they must never go against societal morality.

In "Tax 'Leftover Men' to Subsidize 'Leftover Women'," a netizen said, "Since you have World Expo housing, why not create a GreenTree Inn for leftover women? It would definitely be a hit." This seems somewhat interesting, but we still don't know how to design it. Should we give leftover women extra discounts? Or perhaps take another form? Another netizen pointed out that it might not be practical. Regardless, different people see things differently, and we welcome discussions and brainstorming. We will carefully listen to everyone's thoughts and try to find a feasible way. If others won't give us wings, we need to grow our own. "Leftover men" and "leftover women," if you're tired of being left over, please lower your standards a bit! Perhaps this is the moment to grow wings and fly into the matrimonial hall.
