Sina Technology News, May 19th afternoon news, Ucweb today announced the release of UC Browser 7.2 version supporting Flash 10 technology. In an exclusive interview with Sina Technology, CEO of Ucweb Yongfu Yu said that the choice of Flash technology should be left for users to determine by themselves, and the next step will be to increase support for HTML5 in UC Browser.

Video Interview with Yongfu Yu Media Source: Sina Technology

Ucweb announced the release of UC Browser 7.2 version today, which has functions such as Flash games, one-click microblogging, campus network acceleration, etc., providing comprehensive support for SNS social interaction Flash games and applications such as Happy Farm.

Yu Yongfu then revealed in a dialogue with Sina Technology that the reason why it took two years to develop Flash technology support is because the technical development difficulty far exceeded the previous expectations. The previously released UC Browser could play Flash videos, but actually supported the Flash 6 standard, while games generally use Flash 10 technology.

Recently, several companies including Apple, Google, and Adobe have engaged in fierce debates over issues such as the future and application of Flash, but obviously, Flash technology is widely used on the Internet and mobile Internet.

"Now we are not considering the issue of alignment," Yu Yongfu looked ahead to the future technological development trend and said that Ucweb's choice of technology mainly considers from the user's perspective. Yu Yongfu suggested that various companies and industry leaders should not try to dominate the direction of development, but let users make their own choices, as users are the force that determines the direction of the industry.

In fact, according to the cloud computing architecture of UC Browser, technically it is already possible to use Flash on the Apple platform, but due to policy restrictions, this technology cannot be launched yet. Yu Yongfu emphasized that once Apple lifts its restriction policy on Flash, "UC Browser can provide support the next day."

It is understood that UC Browser has started planning to support Flash and HTML5 and other standards in subsequent versions.

After completing "the largest single financing in China's mobile Internet field so far," Yu Yongfu frankly admitted that his pressure is increasing. However, Yu Yongfu said that this pressure does not come from investors and shareholders, but from the growing team. He said that only by leading Ucweb to grow bigger can this pressure be relieved.

According to data from Ucweb, the current download volume of UC Browser has exceeded 300 million, with more than 100 million global users, and more than 4 billion monthly uses. According to the current plan arrangement, the Ucweb team will exceed 800 people by the end of the year, with about 75% of employees engaged in R&D work. (Meng Hong)

Below is the text record of Sina Technology's exclusive interview with Yu Yongfu:

Sina Technology: Hello everyone, welcome to Sina Technology's Back-to-Back program. Today we are at Ucweb's company, and this person next to me is the CEO of Ucweb, Yu Yongfu. Hello, Mr. Yu.

Yu Yongfu: Hello everyone.

Sina Technology: Today we launched a new product, which took two years to technically support interactive technology in Flash games. But we know that the previous version actually supported browsing of Flash videos. Why did it take some time to launch support for Flash games? What is the difference here? Please introduce it to us.

Yu Yongfu: Today we officially launched the UC 7.2 version with support for Flash 10. From a technical innovation perspective, it is equivalent to the level of innovation when we started using the cloud computing basic architecture in 2004. We considered doing this kind of support in 2008, when we had some thoughts about the future of mobile internet. First, 3G was expected to start soon in 2008, and there were several issues to solve: terminals, networks, and fees.

After solving these three problems, what needs to be solved next? At that time, we thought about the changes from narrowband to broadband on PC internet, which happened between 2002 and 2004. This change led to increasingly rich broadband applications, requiring an important breakthrough in expressiveness. The emergence of Flash video and domestic video companies' breakthroughs in video were due to the differences in expressiveness brought by Flash technology. In 2008, we also saw the rapid development of Flash games, including American Facebook, Chinese Tencent, Xiaonei, and other companies in community-type games. We made a judgment that in the future, Flash video, SNS communities, and games would be important applications in mobile internet, especially in broadband mobile internet.

At this point, we need to consider that China is different from Japan in terms of phone expressiveness, where no breakthrough has been formed yet. Therefore, we decided to support Flash. During this process, the difficulty of Flash technology far exceeded our original expectations, resulting in a two-year development period. In this process, we previously announced support for Flash video. Now let's talk about the difference between Flash Lite, which supports Flash 6, and Flash 10. Most SNS games use Flash 10 applications. The previous version supported Flash 6 and Flash 7, and today's version supports Flash 10. That is the difference.

Sina Technology: This is a technical advancement. As ordinary users, we also want to know if using the new UC browser on a phone, for example playing games, will have any impact on data charges or the lifespan of the phone?

Yu Yongfu: This is a good question. If our phone performance is sufficient and the network bandwidth is fast enough, what else do we need to worry about? There are two issues: Are the data charges too expensive? Now 3G charges have decreased somewhat, but they are still charged based on data usage, which is still expensive for us. In our support for Flash 10, we still use the cloud computing architecture, converting large amounts of Flash 10 video content into low-bandwidth content. This way, we have considered the issue of data usage, and the technology solves it, reducing it as much as possible.

Secondly, does it consume a lot of power? After having these dazzling applications on the phone, will it run out of power in an hour? If it still has power after an hour, it's hard to call it a phone. During the gaming process, if you don't operate for a certain amount of time, we reduce power consumption by lowering data usage and display consumption. We have made many technical arrangements, ensuring optimal management of both data and power.

Sina Technology: So there's no need to worry too much. You mentioned earlier that the breakthrough is in Flash technology, and recently there has been a lot of controversy regarding Flash technology, including Apple's refusal to support Flash and their CEO's public letter criticizing it. As you see it, what is the future trend of Flash technology? Everyone is talking about more open platform technologies like HTML5. Can you share your predictions for future trends in technology since you made a prediction in 2008?

Yu Yongfu: Flash is a very important presentation technology, which is indisputable on PCs, but it has caused a lot of controversy in mobile internet, especially recently. In April, Apple's CEO issued a public letter stating that iPhones do not support Flash. In May, Adobe's founder said, "Apple, you're destroying the entire internet." Google also said in May, "I will support Flash in the next version, Android 2.2, but without specifying the exact version." The big players around the world are fiercely debating.

For us, we don't consider taking sides. We consider what users need on phones. Now users on phones clearly need SNS communities and these games. Currently, on PCs, Flash 10 is being used, so we continue with that. We don't care about taking sides; we just solve the problem first. Our next version will be compatible with HTML5, following how the entire industry develops. Another dimension we consider is that industrial development must take into account user opinions. It cannot be determined by the high positions of stakeholders. Users are already familiar with Flash and its rich applications, so we should let users make the choice. Whether users use or do not use Flash, the more users use it, the more the industry's development trend will move in that direction. We prioritize satisfying users first.

Sina Technology: Many friends are worried now that their Apple devices do not support Flash. Can we provide a software solution to meet this need?

Yu Yongfu: We are pleased that through the technical accumulation of UC in recent years, our global recognition has gradually increased. Actually, Apple is quite sensitive to browsers. We were the first browser app to be approved on the App Store. Moreover, just yesterday, we had an important upgrade for our iPhone version, which was also approved by Apple. This upgrade includes support for WAP, screen formats, and other needs that are important for Chinese users.

However, we have already solved Flash 10 on Apple's lower-performance hardware and 2G networks. It is definitely not a technical issue but rather a commercial one—whether it can be approved. We will continue to try, just as we did during the initial approval process for the Apple App Store, where I remember we were rejected at least three times, but we persisted. We will continue to strive for Apple's support for Flash. Once Apple opens up this policy, we believe that UC can support it the next day.

Sina Technology: We look forward to that day, but it's still up to Apple to decide. We know there was a small controversy before. Your new browser may have some similarities in certain applications, such as blocking. Sina knows that five companies signed an initiative advocating open competition in mobile internet. What role will this agreement play in promoting the entire industry? Additionally, what further cooperation will UC undertake with partners based on this agreement?

Yu Yongfu: Today, the leading companies in the entire mobile internet industry, including Sina, Qianxiang, Baidu, Kaixin, and ourselves, jointly signed an initiative. We advocate protecting user interests in mobile internet applications, avoiding coercing or punishing users. Secondly, we advocate creating an open environment for the entire internet application ecosystem. Personally, I interpret openness in a few ways. The first layer of meaning is to leave the choice to users. Here, we must respect user interests and cannot coerce or punish users, as we've seen over the past decade in the development of the entire internet—users are always the ultimate masters.

The second point about openness is that we encourage competition and promote healthy competition. Because if an industry lacks sufficient healthy competition, its efficiency will not be at its highest. Of course, we cannot allow an industry to have only one company, similar to many state-owned monopolistic industries in China today, where many problems arise. Can such monopolistic situations occur in the internet industry? They would certainly harm the industry. Thirdly, we need to consider how to expand the size of the industry. If an industry cannot scale up, even if it only reaches 50%, its significance is minimal. If we can collectively expand the industry through open cooperation, whether in China or globally, we all have such opportunities. By expanding it, I believe everyone will be a winner.

Sina Technology: Openness is also a major trend for the future. We personally care about UC's growth. Not long ago, UC received the largest single investment in mobile internet history. Have the new shareholders and investments brought more pressure to UC's path forward, such as demands for returns or listing? How do you consider profitability and listing?

Yu Yongfu: My pressure is increasing day by day, but this pressure does not come from investors and shareholders; it comes from leading hundreds of brothers. Because today, with more resources, our team is growing rapidly. Currently, the number of colleagues at UC has exceeded 600, and by the end of this year, we will exceed 800. Every time I face these 600 colleagues, I know I am facing 600 hopes. They stay at UC for better treatment in the industry, with the common goal of raising UC as a child. This represents the hope of 600 families. For me, this is a huge responsibility, and responsibility is pressure. Only when we raise UC as a child and each of my hundreds of colleagues achieves their personal aspirations will this pressure be relieved.

Sina Technology: You mentioned that the team is still expanding. Additionally, we noticed that despite the funding, your focus remains on talent care. Previously, you mentioned that for both the internet industry and mobile internet, only when user accumulation reaches a certain level can success be achieved. Since UC's browser currently has the largest number of users globally, can we shift the focus from user expansion to talent? Is our task still in the development phase, and should the focus remain on market expansion? How do you balance these two aspects?

Yu Yongfu: Actually, this is like the three sides of a ball, each with a keyword. One side is "user," another side is "product," and the third side is "talent." This forms a spherical entity. Any startup company goes through three stages, summarized as follows: the first stage is the "product stage," the second stage is the "market stage," and the third stage is the "management stage." To attract more users in the internet, the most critical thing isn't how much money you have but whether you can provide a good product. Today's internet is in the service sector of IT, not the manufacturing sector. You need to transform your service into the leading one in the industry. Who accomplishes this? Your colleagues, your talents! Only with an excellent talent pool can you create great products and attract the most users. These three elements form the spherical entity.

Sina Technology: We noticed that UC not only offers browsers but also many desktop products. What considerations do you have for your product layout in the future? Additionally, how significant are other products to you, and could you briefly introduce the situation?

Yu Yongfu: UC consistently emphasizes simplification as a strategic approach in its product core. In the process of simplification, we clarify one thing: what does UC do? UC provides tools and platform services to help users quickly access the internet. In the process of quick internet access, we currently offer three interconnected products. A browser is essential for users to access the internet—it serves as the entry point. What is UC's desktop? While users browse automatically, we push relevant content through research and judgment, presenting it on the desktop akin to an information express service. When viewing content, the browser is still utilized. The relationship is clear: UC Desktop serves as a conditional entry point. UC Player, within the current mobile operating systems, cannot effectively utilize the built-in streaming media player of the phone, so when using our entire browser to view more pages, videos, and other content services, we provide a video plugin, which is an add-on to the browser. These are the relationships among the three.

Sina Technology: Still centered around the browser?

Yu Yongfu: Yes, centered around the internet access service platform.

Sina Technology: You also mentioned earlier that there will be more surprises in the future. Could you give us a hint of what those surprises might be?

Yu Yongfu: In fact, today's mobile internet in China is in the stage of transitioning from narrowband to broadband, similar to the early days of PC internet. The UC Browser must consider satisfying user needs and experiences under a 2G-like narrowband internet scenario while also foreseeing that more users will enter the broadband mobile internet era. What changes will occur in the broadband mobile internet, or what support do browsers need to provide? We can think about it. In the broadband mobile internet, users will increasingly use streaming media services and videos, engage more with SNS communities, including SNS games, and use traditional games more often. Also, considering the unique features of mobile phones, such as cameras, microphones, GPS, etc., how will these characteristics influence mobile browsing? Following these lines of thought, you can envision the evolution of broadband browsers.

Sina Technology: Thank you for sharing so much with us. We also thank Yongfu for bringing us such a great product. Thank you, Yongfu, for accepting our interview from Sina. Thanks!
