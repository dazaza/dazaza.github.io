Best Answer:

The process of starting an online store begins not on the internet but in your mind. You need to decide what kind of store you want to open. In this aspect, opening an online store is no different from a traditional one. Finding a good market and ensuring that your products are competitive are the foundations of success.

Step Two: Choose a platform or website for your store. You need to select a site that provides a personal store platform and register as a user. This step is crucial. Most sites will require registration with your real name and valid ID documents. When choosing a site, factors such as popularity, whether it charges fees, and the nature of these fees are all important considerations. Many platforms now offer free store-opening services, which can save you a lot of money.

Step Three: Apply to the site to open your store. You need to carefully fill out the classification of the products your store will provide. For example, if you sell fashion watches, they should be categorized under "Jewelry, Watches, Glasses" and then further classified as "Watches," so that your target customers can easily find you. Then, you need to give your store a catchy name. The choice of name can influence which store users click on when browsing lists. Some online stores display personal information, which should be filled out truthfully to increase trust.

Step Four: Procurement. You can purchase goods from familiar channels or platforms. Controlling costs and buying at low prices are key.

Step Five: List Products. You need to fill out details about each product on the website, including its name, origin, location, nature, appearance, quantity, transaction method, and time limit. It's best to include pictures of the products. The product name should be as comprehensive as possible, highlighting its advantages, because when others search for similar products, only the name appears in the list. To increase appeal, the quality of the pictures should be as good as possible, and the descriptions should be detailed. If mailing is required, it’s best to specify who will cover the postage. An extremely important part of listing is setting the price. Typically, websites allow sellers to set starting prices, reserve prices, and fixed prices. For instance, if a seller wants to sell a piece of clothing purchased for $100 for $150, a traditional shopkeeper would simply label it at $150 and gradually lower the price if it doesn’t sell. However, online bidding is different. Sellers first set a starting price, and buyers bid upwards from there. A lower starting price can attract more interest from buyers. Some sellers start auctions at $1 to grab attention.

However, setting the starting price too low carries the risk of the final selling price being too low. Therefore, sellers should also set a reserve price, such as $105, to ensure the item isn't sold below cost. Another downside of a very low starting price is that it might suggest you're willing to sell at a very low price, causing bids to linger at low levels. If the seller feels waiting for the auction to end takes too long, they can set a fixed price. Once a buyer is willing to pay this price, the deal is immediately closed. The downside is that even if several buyers are interested, the price cannot be driven higher. Sellers should adjust these settings according to their specific situations.

Step Six: Marketing Promotion. To boost your store's popularity, especially in the early stages, appropriate marketing and promotion are necessary. However, relying solely on online methods is insufficient; multiple channels both online and offline should be used. For example, purchasing ad spaces on high-traffic pages like "Featured Product Recommendations," making product names bold in category lists, adding images to catch eyes, or using free advertising by exchanging links with other stores and websites.

Step Seven: Mid-sale Service. When customers are deciding whether to buy, they may need much more information than what you’ve provided. They will often ask questions online, and you should respond promptly and patiently. Note that many websites prohibit sharing any personal contact information (such as email addresses or phone numbers) between buyers and sellers to prevent private transactions that evade transaction fees, otherwise penalties may apply.

Step Eight: Transaction. After a successful deal, the website will notify both parties' contact information, and transactions can proceed through agreed-upon methods—either meeting in person, wire transfer, or mail delivery. Transactions should occur as soon as possible to avoid doubts about your credibility. Whether additional after-sales services are provided depends on prior agreements between both parties.

Step Nine: Evaluation or Complaint. Creditworthiness is a critical factor in online transactions. To jointly build a trustworthy environment, if the transaction is satisfactory, it’s best to give the other party positive feedback and earn their good reviews through excellent service. If the transaction fails, you should give negative feedback or file a complaint with the website to minimize losses and warn others. If you receive a complaint, handle it quickly to avoid tarnishing your credit.

Step Ten: After-Sales Service. There's no need to elaborate here. Providing perfect and thorough after-sales service is a very important factor in maintaining a business's longevity. Continuously engage with clients and manage customer relations well.

Currently, there are over a hundred large shopping websites in China offering online store services, but only a few have significant influence. Here are introductions to some major relevant websites:

eBay EachNet (www.eachnet.com): Founded in Shanghai on August 18, 1999, by Charles Zheng and Helen Tan, it is the largest Chinese-language online trading platform globally, providing C2C (individual-to-individual) and B2C (business-to-individual) network platforms and services. In March 2002, eBay invested $30 million in EachNet and formed a strategic partnership. In June 2003, eBay added another $150 million investment. To date, EachNet has attracted nearly $220 million in foreign investment, becoming the enterprise attracting the most foreign capital among all overseas student startups nationwide. EachNet (www.ebay.com.cn) was one of the earliest shopping websites to offer online store services. Registration for an online store comes with charges for bottom price setup fees, item listing fees, transaction service fees, and advertising value-added service fees.

Taobao (www.taobao.com): It is a leading domestic personal trading (C2C) online platform, founded on May 10, 2003, with an investment of $450 million from Alibaba, the world's top business-to-business (B2B) e-commerce company. It aims to become the world's largest personal trading website. Taobao currently offers free registration, free certification, and free store-opening services.

YiPai (www.1pai.com.cn): Established in early 2004 by a joint venture between Sina and Yahoo — Beijing Xinyao Online Information Technology Co., Ltd., YiPai is a newly established, fully functional high-quality online buying and selling community that provides smooth trading platforms and various trading models for Chinese small and medium-sized enterprises and individual users.

8848 Network (www.8848.com): Founded on May 18, 1999, 8848 was once the flagship of Chinese e-commerce enterprises. In September 2003, it began preparing a new business development plan, shifting its focus to establishing China's first professional shopping search engine — 8848 Shopping Engine, serving small and medium merchants through 8848 Online Stores, and providing e-commerce solutions to expand business for corporate users. 8848.com officially launched on January 1, 2004. Currently, 8848 offers free online store services.
