Step-by-step Guide to Synchronizing and Backing Up Your Phone Contacts

by xdash on 2010-12-31 11:12:08

(Author of this article: @Li Shuo, Original link: http://lishuo.me/archives/75)

As the year-end approaches, there have been far too many cases of people losing their phones recently. Losing a phone might not be a big deal, but losing your contact list could lead to potential and even immeasurable losses. To prevent such problems, it's worth spending a few minutes backing up your contact list while you still have your phone. This way, even if you lose your phone, you can recover your original contact information in just three seconds, minimizing any loss.

Below, I will guide you step-by-step through four methods to back up your mobile phone contacts:

### Method 1: Use QQ Sync Assistant for Backup

It seems like everyone has a QQ number, so the first recommended backup method is using Tencent's QQ Sync Assistant. The QQ Sync Assistant supports various types of phones, including iPhone, Android, Nokia, and more. You only need to download one software application to conveniently back up your data. When you switch to a new phone, simply install the QQ Sync Assistant on the new device to download the old phone’s contact list.

**QQ Sync Assistant Download Link**: http://mobile.qq.com/pim/

After downloading and installing the Sync Assistant based on your phone model, choose the backup option. You can back up either to the cloud or to a storage card. It is recommended to perform dual backups as a precaution.

### Method 2: Synchronize Your Phone Contact List with Google Contacts via Google Sync

Here are some benefits of using Google Sync to back up your contact list: no need to download software or use a data cable; Google currently is, and will continue to be, the most stable and secure data preservation service provider; Google Sync works across different phone models without being limited to a specific operating system or brand.

The following steps demonstrate how to synchronize your contact list using Google Sync with a Nokia phone as an example:

1. Open the phone's tools - settings - connection functions - data transfer - sync, options - new sync scenario mode.

2. Name the sync scenario mode as "Google sync."

3. Connection settings:

- Server version: 1.2

- Server identifier: Google (the letter G must be capitalized)

- Data carrier method: Internet

- Access point: Default connection

- Host address: https://m.google.com/syncml (Note the 's' after http)

- Port: 443

- Username: Enter your Gmail address xxx@gmail.com

- Password: Enter your Gmail password

- Sync type: Two-way

4. Contact list sync settings:

- Remote contact database: contacts (currently, Google only supports contact list synchronization).

5. After creating the new sync scenario mode, save it and start syncing directly. This way, your Google contact list will contain your phone’s contacts.

### Method 3: Use the Built-in Contact Synchronization Feature of Your Phone Brand

Nokia phones come with PC Suite, which allows you to connect your phone to a computer with PC Suite installed to export the contact list in vCard format and store it on the computer. This makes it easy to restore the contact list when switching to a new phone.

Android operating system phones come with built-in Google Contacts that automatically sync and update, making it very easy!

BlackBerry and Meizu also offer built-in contact synchronization features, which we won't elaborate on here.

### Method 4: Use Third-Party Software

Currently, some good third-party contact list software includes:

- Hozom Contacts: http://www.hozom.com

- Youlu Contacts: http://www.youlu.com/

(@XDash: Supplementary recommendations include Youbei, Quanniu, and Laxi.)

You can download and install these applications according to your phone model, all of which operate in a completely user-friendly manner.

This concludes the tutorial. It is hoped that everyone selects at least two methods to back up their contact lists, and avoid storing all your contacts on a single service provider!
