Learning the ladder of brand advancement for apparel enterprises

by shenli477t on 2012-02-11 12:40:20

Mr. Lu Xun once said: "If I can survive, I will certainly continue to learn." After more than 30 years of development, China's apparel industry is now at a critical stage for comprehensive transformation and upgrading. Looking back on the growth journey of some Chinese apparel enterprises, their impressive learning ability has been one of the main factors contributing to their rapid growth. When these companies' brands were developing rapidly, the entrepreneurial spirit and speed displayed by the entrepreneurs were equally astonishing. These corporate leaders have long realized that learning is the true propellant and source of power for the operation of apparel brands. The industry must adjust its structure despite severe conditions. In 2012, the textile and apparel industry faced a severe situation, but we should also be confident. As a competitive industry, the textile and apparel sector has weathered many storms every year, and the key lies in how to develop. Scientific development is the theme, and transforming the mode of economic growth is the main thread. Under this main thread, the future development of the industry should place structural adjustment in a very important position. Adjusting structure from five aspects At the first China Apparel Learning Forum, Sun Huaibin, spokesperson for the China National Textile and Apparel Council, stated that scientific development is the theme for the future development of the industry. Under the main thread of transforming the mode of economic growth, we need to accurately identify the direction of structural adjustment. Although structural adjustment is an old topic, it remains an important and timely issue. Regardless of how severe the situation faced by the industry may be, structural adjustment must continue as usual. So, how exactly should structural adjustment be carried out? Sun Huaibin indicated that the first step is to adjust the overall structure. The overall structure refers to the relationship between total supply and total demand. Last year, our fiber processing volume was 41.3 million tons, accounting for more than 50% of the world. The problem lies in the fact that facing such a large volume of clothing fiber processing, some companies are still expanding capacity, and these companies must exercise caution because under the condition of economic downturn, effective demand will present some issues. "Investment at this time must be cautious, as overcapacity already exists, and effective demand must be considered: whether mutual alignment and coordination can be achieved; capacity expansion must be controlled, especially when demand is insufficient and needs to be developed, the overall relationship must be coordinated well." The second step is industrial structure adjustment, matching and coordinating the upstream, midstream, and downstream of the industrial chain. Currently, some key industries must be highly focused on, such as industrial textiles, which have great potential for development in our country. The industry positions them as new growth points for the textile industry. Currently, industrial textiles account for only 20% of the total fiber processing volume, a proportion much lower than that of developed countries. This industry has supply and demand relationships with more than 20 departments related to the national economy, so with the development of these departments related to the national economy, there will be significant demand for industrial textiles. Additionally, printing and dyeing is an intermediate link in our entire textile industry and also an important source of added value for the industry. However, due to the close relationship between printing and dyeing and the environment, coastal areas have begun to restrict or even ban the development of this industry due to increasingly stringent environmental requirements from the state. "In fact, through technological upgrades, the environmental problems of garment printing and dyeing can be resolved. Printing and dyeing is an indispensable industry for the textile industry, and if it gradually shrinks, it will impact the connection of the entire industrial chain, as well as the enhancement of the industrial chain's added value and the industry's transformation and upgrading." Meanwhile, the internal structure of the chemical fiber industry needs adjustment, strengthening high-tech fibers, high-performance fibers, and high-performance materials representing the development of the chemical fiber industry; chemical fiber products need to enhance their ability to replace natural fibers while also strengthening natural chemical fibers. Thirdly, regional structures need to be reasonably adjusted, such as complementary regional industrial structures between the east and west. Based on current realities, some industries and enterprises are transferring to neighboring countries rather than to central and western regions, indicating that the issue of the central and western regions accepting industrial transfers from the eastern region has not been properly resolved. Therefore, in the macro perspective, China's textile industry regional layout should have a principal functional positioning. "The eastern region should focus on developing some high-end manufacturing and high-end sectors of the textile industry, such as R&D, design, marketing, branding, channels, logistics, etc.; the central region should combine local location advantages and resource advantages to mainly accept some processing and manufacturing links from the east, establishing and improving the textile industry manufacturing system; the western region should mainly develop relevant industries based on some resource advantages and characteristics of ethnic culture and clothing culture." Sun Huaibin mentioned. Sun Huaibin also believed that currently, local governments in central and western regions attach great importance to accepting industrial transfers, but in reality, industrial transfer is a market behavior and enterprise behavior, meaning how to use market mechanisms to allocate resources and adjust regional structures. That is, it is far from being resolved merely by the unilateral attention of local governments. Fourthly, adjustments need to be made to the organizational structure of enterprises. To build a strong textile and apparel nation, enterprises in the industry must have international configurations and establish influential textile and apparel multinational corporations with international influence, while simultaneously creating internationally recognized brands. "We need to use market-oriented mergers and acquisitions and capital operations to create multinational companies with international influence, making them influential, having a voice, and even control in the international market. Only then can they represent China's textile industry on the international stage." Small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) are the main body of the industry, and their development should be coordinated and complemented with large enterprises. Specifically, the overall quality of SMEs within some clusters should be improved, public services strengthened, allowing them to support the development of large enterprises and better address employment issues related to people's livelihoods. Finally, product structure adjustments need to be made, improving product quality and developing more products that meet domestic and international market demands—products with quality, taste, added value, and brand recognition. "Because textiles and apparel are part of the real economy, ultimately, they speak through their products." This article comes from Fashion Coordination Network (http://www.daban.org.cn).
