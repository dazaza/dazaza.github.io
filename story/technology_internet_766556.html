Jolla announced that the SailFish system will support Android hardware and applications.

by anonymous on 2013-09-18 21:57:08

At 9:16 PM Beijing time on September 16th, Finnish mobile startup Jolla announced that its mobile operating system, Sailfish, now supports the Android ecosystem, including application and hardware compatibility.

This means that the Sailfish operating system can now run on smartphones and tablets designed for Android. For manufacturers, using the Sailfish system is not limited to Jolla phones.

In addition, Jolla will cooperate with global app stores to ensure that Sailfish users can seamlessly download Android applications.

Industry insiders said that although Jolla stated it would continue to develop exclusive apps for the Sailfish system, due to its compatibility with Android, the appeal of developing exclusive Sailfish apps for developers might be limited.

The development of the Sailfish system aims to disrupt the current mobile operating system market landscape. However, Jolla believes that being compatible with Android is not a contradiction for the Sailfish system itself. On the contrary, this provides convenience for manufacturers to transition to the Sailfish platform.

Jolla was established in the second half of 2011 by five former Nokia employees, and the Sailfish OS originated from Nokia's short-lived MeeGo. In reality, the establishment of Jolla also received support from Nokia's Bridge project, which is a program that financially helps former employees start new businesses. (By Ming Li)
