While the then known group-buying sites had advertising placement plans worth over 1 billion yuan RMB.

by beetgk2958 on 2012-02-29 12:48:17

In fact, all the operators in all walks of life are actually in a race against their own capital consumption rate and sustainable income. It is very obvious that group-buying enterprises have lost in this race, so have TuanBao.com. Their capital consumption speed is much higher than their income. We say that a team with stronger execution ability can achieve better results with less money.

Now it makes no sense to comment on the disadvantages of the group-buying industry anymore because there were already wise men in China who predicted that China's "Battle of Thousands of Group-Buying Sites" would end up as "Death of Thousands of Group-Buying Sites". Li Kaifu from Innovation Works once bluntly said, "99% of group-buying websites will die out, only four or five can survive and dominate the market", and more insiders believe that the group-buying industry is about to enter an era where "the survivors reign".

I don't know whether Ren Chunlei and others now regret the huge advertising expenditure last year, but one thing is for sure: even if they still have such funds at hand now, the grandeur of last year won't reappear. Because currently, the growth of China's group-buying consumer market has stagnated and the difficulty of financing has further increased. "Burnout" cannot continue. For most first-line group-buying websites, what they face more is the extreme coldness of capital.

So, when Li Wei, the domestic business director of TuanBao.com consoled everyone by saying "TuanBao has found financing and got the life-saving money", the employees of TuanBao all expressed that they "dared not believe that there was such a foolish venture capitalist who dared to invest". People in the venture capital industry also expressed doubts "the investment may not necessarily exist". Investors value the operation team of an enterprise. Given the current situation of TuanBao.com where most employees have resigned, the possibility of continued capital follow-up is small. This scene is so similar to what happened in the video industry two years ago!

People with good memory should still retain the beautiful memories of the grandeur of group-buying at the beginning of last year. At that time, the number of China's group-buying websites exceeded 5000, becoming the largest group-buying army in the world. During the same period, group-buying websites surpassed video websites and became the richest Internet industry in China. All major group-buying websites spent big bucks to occupy outdoor billboards, bus bodies, elevators in high-grade office buildings in various medium-sized cities, mainstream websites, television media, etc. For a time, group-buying website advertisements could be seen everywhere. The known group-buying website advertising expenditure plan exceeded RMB 1 billion. Among them, Meituan.com stated that it had prepared RMB 130 million; Nuomi.com claimed that it would spend RMB 200 million on advertising on CCTV and local satellite TVs; TuanBao.com announced before investing RMB 550 million in advertising that it had splurged millions to sign celebrity endorsements; Groupon China, which had not officially opened yet at that time, also spent heavily to purchase prime positions on navigation websites.

The group-buying industry only cared about crazily throwing advertisements and burning money, forgetting this consequence: too many celebrity endorsements and bombarding advertisements actually diluted the brand effect of the advertisements to a certain extent.

Its rise was rapid, its decline was sudden. No one can escape the law of nature, and the group-buying industry is no exception.

In the spring of the Year of the Rabbit, in the extreme cold of the Year of the Dragon, in just one year, the group-buying industry went from being the hottest industry on the Internet to being an object of onlookers' sighs, creating the shortest-lived bubble in Internet history. In terms of the speed of bursting, it surpassed blogs.

It can be said that the bursting of the group-buying bubble was another lesson of a game and a dream.

The latest data released by shopping search engine YiTaobao shows that as of the end of November 2011, more than 35% of domestic group-buying websites did not update their products within 30 days. The number of group-buying websites that stopped updating, revamped their websites, or closed down approached 2000. YiTaobao predicts that in the three months before and after the Spring Festival, especially during the Spring Festival, the wave of group-buying website closures will further intensify, and at least 70% of group-buying websites will close down or be forced to transform.

And looking at the overall transaction volume, the group-buying market's transaction amount decreased by 11% month-on-month in October last year. The total transaction amount of physical goods group-buying and life service group-buying was only on par with June.

Thinking about the pitiful situation of TuanBao.com nowadays, where it owes wages to employees and payments to merchants, the act of its CEO Ren Chunlei claiming in 2011 that he would spend RMB 550 million on advertising promotion was extremely crazy. Now, the registered merchants he owes alone amount to nearly RMB 1 million.

"God wishes to destroy someone, must first make him crazy."

There is nothing wrong with the commercial idea of letting more consumers recognize the website through advertisements, bringing in the core sales revenue, and thereby opening up the market. But doing anything requires considering comprehensive factors.

On February 7th, Ren Chunlei, who promised to repay the money on Weibo, ultimately failed to appear in the company, leaving many employees and merchants who came to claim their overdue salaries and payments very disappointed. Of course, what disappoints people more is the investment institutions' dim view of the prospects of the group-buying industry.

Today's TuanBao.com is experiencing a vicious cycle: the company has no money, it owes money to employees and merchants, leading to employee resignations, branch closures, merchants losing trust in the group-buying platform and refusing to accept consumer spending, resulting in fewer consumers coming to TuanBao, thus making TuanBao have even less money, and so on in a repetitive cycle.

In the world of the Internet, there are many phenomena that "look good", but may not necessarily be so. Replacing real-life consumption behavior with online consumption and expecting new wealth returns is a beautiful wish of e-commerce businesses. However, due to the change in method, online operations and physical operations can never be completely identical.

Actually, there was nothing wrong with launching advertisements to the market at the beginning of the industry's prosperity. The mistake was that everyone ignored the cost-performance ratio.
