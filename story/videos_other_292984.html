For all wives who don't listen to their husbands. After reading it, I cried! Not because of being moved, but because of the heart...

by yang5230 on 2009-12-04 17:20:07

On a busy street, at an obscure bus stop, there was a boy and a girl. That's how they met... and fell in love. Time passed, and they were already in their twilight years together. The boy still loved the girl, and the girl loved the boy just as much. They planned to get married early after the girl finished elementary school. Thus, they both worked hard for their own goals.

One day, the boy said to the girl: "I don't mind you hanging out with your same-sex friends, but when you're with them, you're not allowed to drink alcohol—not even a drop."

The girl obediently nodded and softly replied, "Understood, my husband..."

With that, the boy didn't say anything more.

One afternoon at 3 PM, the girl called the boy and said, "Husband, I have no class tonight, so I'm going to sing with my classmates... Is that okay?"

The boy asked, "Who's going?"

The girl coaxed, "All my dorm mates, no outsiders. Alright, husband..?"

Reluctantly, the boy agreed, "Alright then, make sure to return to the dorm on time..."

The call ended!

By 6 PM, the girl hadn't called the boy once.

The boy started to worry a bit.

He called the girl, but after ringing for a long time, no one answered. The boy was both worried and angry...

A little while later, the boy received a text message from the girl. It read: "Dear husband, we've been singing all along. My phone was in my bag, so I didn't hear it. Don't be mad, husband. We'll be done soon. Hehe! Miss you~"

After reading the text, the boy felt somewhat reassured.

A little while later, the boy's phone rang. The girl said on the phone, "Husband ah, we've been singing ourselves hoarse. Now we're having dinner, and then we'll go back to the dorm. Don't worry, husband..."

The boy didn't want to restrict the girl too much, and since they were all girls, he let it go. After hanging up, he reminded the girl, "Don't drink too much, and call me when you're done."

The girl quickly replied, "I know, husband! Miss you~"

Doo... doo... doo... The call ended.

By 8 PM, the girl had still not called the boy once.

The boy became worried again.

So he called the girl. After ringing for a while, the girl finally answered. The boy asked, "Husband ah... Are you guys still eating?"

The girl replied, "Almost done, husband! We're drinking with friends, we'll be done soon."

"Friends?" The boy curiously asked, "Who are they?"

The girl calmly replied, "The ones from our dormitory, plus her boyfriend and his friend..."

The boy began to worry, fearing that the girl would drink too much and something might happen. He asked, "How many bottles have you had?"

The girl replied, "Just two!"

The boy sternly said, "That's enough, understand?"

The girl replied, "I know, husband. No worries, they're all classmates. Husband, I'll call you when I'm done..."

Doo... doo... doo... The call ended again.

The boy knew the girl would probably drink more, so he was very worried. He didn't go home, bought a pack of cigarettes, found a quiet place, and waited patiently for the girl's call...

An hour passed, it was already 9 PM.

The boy saw that the girl still hadn't called him, so he became very worried. He called her again, but this time it was the girl's friend who answered, saying, "She went to the bathroom, wait a moment, I'll ask her to call you..."

Before the boy could respond, the call ended again.

The boy anxiously waited...

Another half-hour passed.

Seeing that the girl still hadn't called, the boy called again. This time, the phone said, "The user you dialed is powered off. Please try again later." Doo... doo... doo...

Thus, the boy kept calling the girl, first time, second time... But the phone only repeated the same message: "The user you dialed..."

The boy was very anxious. Looking at the clock, it was already 11:30 PM. At this point, the boy had lost the mood for anything else.

The boy waited like this, cigarette after cigarette, making call after call...

The boy stood in this quiet place all night without sleeping, worrying... worrying...

Suddenly, a car's horn startled the boy.

Looking at the phone, it was already past midnight at 8 AM.

So the boy called the girl again. This time, the call connected. Just as the boy's spirits rose, a male voice came through: "Hello..."

At that moment, the boy's heart felt as if it were being cut by a knife because he had already roughly guessed what had happened during this entire night.

Without saying a word, the boy hung up the phone. His eyes began to blur... His thoughts began to wander uncontrollably.

The boy stood up, but his legs, which had cramped from standing all night, caused him to sit down again.

After a while, the boy slowly stood up, looked at the passersby on the street, turned around, and walked home.

Back home, the boy collapsed onto the bed face-down. At this moment, the boy's mind was so preoccupied that he forgot his fatigue. He thought deeply...

After a long time, someone sent some birds that could move and chirp to be placed in everyone's space to raise, they were very beautiful! The boy's phone rang, it was the girl calling. The boy looked at the clock, it was already 10:20 PM. He answered the call. Through the phone came the girl's sobbing voice saying, "Husband, I drank too much tonight... I'm sorry, at that time I didn't know what I was doing... Sorry, husband..."

At that moment, the boy was also crying, unable to speak. After a while, the boy hung up the phone and turned it off.

The boy got up and took out a bottle of sleeping pills from the bedside table. He swallowed all the pills in the bottle, then pulled the blanket aside and slept restlessly...

The boy thus slept and never woke up.

At the moment the boy hung up the phone, the girl was also calling the boy repeatedly, but after a long time, she still couldn't get through.

The girl hurried home and quickly turned on the computer, hoping to find the boy online.

When the girl opened the computer, she saw an email from the boy. She hastily opened it.

The email read:

"Husband, don't cry anymore. Seeing you cry breaks my heart. I don't blame you, I only blame myself for not taking good care of you... not watching over you properly! So I'm going to a faraway place to punish myself! Wife, take good care of yourself in the future, remember what we promised about getting married early. Make sure to listen to your mother-in-law! Be a good girl and listen to her. Your older brother will check on you to see if you've been good and listened. That's all. Take care, your older brother will still be waiting for you at the bus stop where we first met..."

Forever yours,

Your husband

Late XX year, X month, X day, 10:30 PM

The girl cried loudly, shouting, "Husband, in the next life, I will definitely listen to you..."

This story is dedicated to all the little husbands who don't listen to their wives, hoping you realize that besides your children, the person who cares about you the most in this world is your wife. Listen to your wife well.

And also, if you love ta (her), then for ta (her) sake, keep your phone on 24 hours a day, or if you need to turn it off due to certain tasks, send a text or make a call to inform ta (her). If the phone isn't reachable, leave ta (her) a way to contact you anytime. Because when you desperately need to reach your beloved one, and your calls aren't answered or your phone is off, that feeling... sigh, I don't even know how to describe it. Worry leads to wild guesses, and wild guesses lead to more worry!

Finally, cherish if you have money, fight if you are poor (and fight together)! Because you love him/her...

Lastly, may all the loving couples in the world remain together forever... (After reading this, be gentle and happy for a lifetime.)
