The Legal Evening News picture shows: the ward received the donation certificate. Chutian Metropolis Daily Beijing electric (reporter Chen Lingyan) Unacquainted, love follows. On the morning of August 8, Xiaogan University student Yang Liwei from Wuhan came to Beijing and donated hematopoietic stem cells (commonly known as donating bone marrow) at the People's Liberation Army Air Force General Hospital in Beijing (hereinafter referred to as Beijing Kongzong). On that day, these hematopoietic stem cells were urgently delivered to Shanghai and transplanted into Dongdong (a pseudonym), a 12-year-old boy with leukemia. It is understood that today Yang Liwei will also conduct supplementary collection of hematopoietic stem cells. In mid-June, Yang Liwei was successfully matched with Dongdong. Unfortunately, the donation time conflicted with the final exams, and he decisively stated that he was willing to abandon the exam to save lives. Fortunately, the school learned about this situation and specially adjusted the exam time for him (our newspaper had reported). His righteous actions moved many netizens and triggered widespread media attention, including CCTV. During yesterday's donation process, Yang Liwei appeared very relaxed. He has been hiding this matter from his parents. After the donation, Director Hong Junling of the China Marrow Donor Program personally presented Yang Liwei with a certificate. He highly praised Yang Liwei's righteous actions, "People's concept of donating bone marrow needs to be changed, and Yang Liwei is an excellent role model." Note: At Yang Liwei's request, all pictures in this section have been processed with mosaic treatment. A small body is full of great courage Chutian Metropolis Daily News Reporter Chen Lingyan Correspondent Zhang Sheng (from Beijing) Let love follow the "sui", sing with life. On October 18, 2009, when Yang Liwei, a college student at the New Technology College of Xiaogan University, registered to join the Hubei branch of the China Marrow Donor Program, he did not expect that just over a year later, he would be closely linked to the fate of a boy he had never met, becoming the benefactor of a family in Shanghai. He smiled brightly under the sun - this time the hematopoietic stem cells to be collected required his entire blood to circulate more than twice - Wang Hengxiang, director of the hematology department of Beijing Kongzong. July 8th is an ordinary day, the sun poured down, illuminating every corner of the capital city. Located in Haidian District, Beijing Kongzong, this day will definitely not be peaceful, video conference. At 7:30 am, outside the blood cell separator on the 12th floor of the hematology department, people gathered, and numerous media cameras were all aimed at ward number 1221. At 8:10 am, Yang Liwei arrived. When he saw a sea of cameras, he showed a shy smile on his face. "I'm not used to being surrounded by reporters," Yang Liwei said helplessly to our reporter on QQ a few days ago, "Actually, I just want to come quietly, video conferencing system, and go back quietly." At 8:30 am, Yang Liwei lay down on the bed, next to him was the blood cell separator. "Today's collection volume is about 55 milliliters," said Wang Hengxiang, director of the hematology department of Beijing Kongzong, "To collect this amount of hematopoietic stem cells, it requires 10,000 milliliters of blood circulation, equivalent to his entire blood circulating in and out more than twice." During the collection process, Yang Liwei occasionally showed a bright and relaxed smile, actively cooperating with questions from the media in various directions in the ward. "It's much simpler than I originally thought, just can't move, a bit boring," he suddenly became playful, shouting for his mobile phone, "Even if I could play games far away." Medical staff gave him a rubber balloon to hold in his right hand to assist blood flow. In the following time, this rubber balloon became his "only toy". At 11:40 am, the collection was completed. Medical staff placed the blood bag containing 60 milliliters of hematopoietic stem cell suspension on Yang Liwei's cheek, allowing him to say goodbye to a part of his body. At this moment, the blood bag still had the temperature of the body. After Yang Liwei affectionately touched the blood bag, he suddenly burst out with "it feels like giving birth," making everyone on the scene laugh. Finding you in the sea of people Such high compatibility probability is only one in ten thousand, Yang Liwei and Dongdong are really destined - Liu JinHong, director of the transplant department of Shanghai Daopei Hospital This is a donation across three places. Yang Liwei came from Hubei to Beijing, after donating, his hematopoietic stem cells were immediately transported to Shanghai Daopei Hospital and transplanted into Dongdong, who suffers from acute lymphoblastic leukemia. This process can be described briefly, but it is not easy to achieve. "Yang Liwei and Dongdong are really destined," Liu JinHong, director of Shanghai Daopei Hospital, told our reporter on the phone, his tone filled with astonishment, "Many patients spend more than a year looking for a match, but Dongdong found Yang Liwei in just a month; many volunteers have been in the bone marrow bank for eight or nine years without a donation opportunity, but Xiao Yang encountered someone waiting for his life-saving help in less than two years. You know, video conferencing software, their matching is ten points fully compatible, such a high compatibility probability is only one in ten thousand." Last night, our reporter called Liu JinHong. He said that at 8 o'clock in the evening, Yang Liwei's donated hematopoietic stem cells had already been transplanted into Dongdong, but due to transportation loss and other reasons, the first collection volume was slightly short of the final transplantation need. Yang Liwei will undergo another hematopoietic stem cell collection. Passionate love, heart to heart. To encourage the unseen Dongdong, Yang Liwei wrote him a letter, good meeting, the letter said: Since I knew there was a sick little brother like you elsewhere, my heart has been constantly tied to your condition! Your parents must have worried a lot for you and shed many tears! Therefore, I sincerely hope you recover soon, and let your parents' faces bloom with smiles! They are the ones who love you and care about you the most, video conferencing system! Youth is textured because of dedication Small bodies are filled with great courage - Yang Liwei Small bodies are filled with great courage, this is Yang Liwei's QQ signature. Doctors say that technological progress has made the process of donating bone marrow no longer painful, but it still requires some courage. And Yang Liwei's righteous action of abandoning academics to save lives is precisely the best embodiment of his courage. In fact, this time coming to Beijing to donate bone marrow, Yang Liwei has been hiding it from his parents at home, only his brother and uncle know. An hour into the collection, he suddenly said softly, "I miss my mom." Then he quietly added, "This is saving a life, my mom gave me life, so this is also her contribution to others." At this moment, his smiling eyes suddenly overflowed with tears. "I'll go home in a few days, I'll tell my mom this in person." Before leaving the blood cell separation room, Yang Liwei looked up at a large cluster of paper cranes hanging in the air. The nurse introduced that these paper cranes were sent by loving people in Beijing, they have been hanging here for several years, witnessing the loving donations of volunteer after volunteer. Yang Liwei is the 2263rd donation volunteer of the China Marrow Donor Program, and the 80th case in Hubei Province. Yang Liwei takes the last exam in Beijing today School faxes test papers, examination room set up in the hospital Chutian Metropolis Daily News Our Beijing Electric (reporter Chen Lingyan Correspondent Zhang Sheng) On September 9, Yang Liwei will complete the final exam of the last course in Beijing. It is understood that the subject of this special exam is electrical engineering technology, the examination room is set up in the cadre ward of Beijing Kongzong, this ward is also Yang Liwei's residence during his stay in Beijing. The test paper is faxed to the hospital confidentiality room by the New Technology College of Xiaogan University, then escorted from the hospital confidentiality room to the ward, and there will be soldiers standing guard outside the ward. After the exam, Yang Liwei will also connect with Dongdong in Shanghai, this will be his first direct contact with the recipient. Classmates postpone going home to cheer for Yang Liwei Chutian Metropolis Daily News (reporter Wang Jiliang Correspondent Lei Di) On August 8, it was the first day off for students at the New Technology College of Xiaogan University, and also the day that sophomore Yang Liwei donated bone marrow in Beijing. For this reason, Bai Xianglong, a classmate from Zhangjiakou, Hebei, deliberately postponed his return home by one day when buying train tickets, even though he had been away from his parents for more than five months and was eager to return. "He is the pride of our class and our school, although we cannot personally go to Beijing to accompany him, but waiting in front of the TV, blessing him, cheering him on, I believe he can feel it," Bai Xianglong said. Liu Dekai, who has already graduated and currently works at a company in Wuhan Optics Valley, also returned specially. He told reporters that when he saw the news in our newspaper that Yang Liwei was going to donate bone marrow to save lives, he asked his juniors about it, hoping to see him off before he left, but "he was very low-key and sneaked away." Later, when he learned that stem cells would be collected in Beijing on August 8, Liu Dekai quickly asked for leave from his company to return to his alma mater, "just wanted to give him some encouragement and support." Around noon, the good news was transmitted back by teacher Lu Jun accompanying in front: Yang Liwei has completed the donation of hematopoietic stem cells, the whole process went very smoothly.
