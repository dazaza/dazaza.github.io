New transfer policy is expected to be announced next week: two-phase registration to replace three restrictions on players

by qinglai888 on 2009-12-12 18:42:34

The new transfer policy is expected to be released next week, with two-phase registration replacing the three shackles on players. The terms "listed players," "draft system," and "30-month protection period," which are heavily associated with the planned economy of football, will be consigned to the dustbin of history. The latest transfer and registration system for China's professional league, expected to be officially launched next week, will take a significant step towards free transfers in line with international standards. 2010 will also mark the beginning of reform in player transfers in the Chinese Super League.

Following past conventions, the new annual transfer rules are usually released around December 1st, but this year is different. The revised version of the rules, which has been in preparation since the start of the year and undergone multiple drafts, is now basically finalized. From the beginning of this week, Yang Yimin, Deputy Director of the Football Management Center responsible for leagues, has been discussing with Zhu Heyuan, Director of the Youth Department responsible for transfers, and Ma Chengquan, responsible for league operations, to determine the final plan. Prior to this, Hang Quanming, the designated lawyer for the Football Association, and his team had meticulously scrutinized the transfer rules, almost word by word.

If all goes smoothly, this historically significant new version of the transfer rules will be officially implemented next week. After repeated consultations with local football associations and clubs, a balanced transfer rule that at least aligns with international trends is about to be implemented. From a macro perspective, the biggest difference between the new version of the transfer rules and previous ones lies in the abolition of outdated provisions such as "listed players," "draft system," and "30-month protection period."

Since the mid-period of the Jia-A League, the Football Association established a "reverse draft" system similar to the NBA draft, aimed at leveling the playing field and curbing improper transactions between players and clubs that disrupt the market. However, over time, it evolved into the current "draft system." A key aspect of this system is that if a player wants to transfer, they must first be listed on the so-called "transfer list" with the approval of their original club; without being listed, they lose their basic right to transfer. "Listed players" and the "draft system" have long been the first fetters preventing the free movement of Chinese players.

In the new version of the transfer rules, these two terms have disappeared, replaced by two registration periods. The first registration period is "from the end of last season's league until the period determined by the Chinese Football Association before the start of this season's league, with a span not exceeding 12 weeks;" the second registration period refers to "from the end of the first round of this season's league to the period determined by the Chinese Football Association before the start of the second round, with a span not exceeding 4 weeks." As long as the transfer and registration are completed within these two periods, they are considered valid.

In the future, domestic league player transfers will no longer require the cumbersome procedures of "application for listing, public display of the transfer list, determination of value," moving further towards the direction of a free market. Similarly, the highly restrictive "30-month protection period" has also been discarded. Although not entirely abolished, some restrictive measures are still in place, but it represents a substantive step towards international alignment.
