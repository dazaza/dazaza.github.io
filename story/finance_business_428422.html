CCMobi: A Review of the Top Ten New Phones This Week

by jokeonyou on 2011-05-10 15:22:18

Mobile World (www.ccmobi.com) Introduction:

After the May Day long weekend, the mobile phone market has once again seen a small wave of new device releases. In the past week alone, BlackBerry, Motorola, and Sony Ericsson have all launched their latest smartphones. These products are highly distinctive in terms of hardware configuration and operating systems. Notably, the military-grade rugged smartphone from Motorola, the Titanium, has caught the eye of many enthusiasts. Next, let us review the top ten new phones released or exposed in the past week!

New Device One: Motorola Titanium

The Motorola DEFY, which boasts waterproofing, dustproofing, and scratch resistance, is one of the hottest Android phones available today. However, the newly released Motorola Titanium by Motorola surpasses the DEFY in terms of ruggedness. This Motorola Titanium meets the military 810G standard for protection, allowing it to easily handle various extreme environments.

Motorola Titanium Phone

This Motorola Titanium is an Android smartphone jointly launched by Motorola and the carrier Sprint. It supports the iDEN network and is considered a continuation of the Motorola i1 released last year. As an iDEN phone, the Motorola Titanium features functions such as Push-to-Talk instant communication, in addition to having an 810G military-grade protective performance that effectively resists extreme conditions like dust, impact, vibration, low air pressure, solar radiation, high temperatures, and low temperatures (the official introduction does not mention whether it is waterproof).

Motorola Titanium Phone

Of course, the Motorola Titanium itself is also an excellent Android business phone. It uses a straight QWERTY design, similar to the previously released Droid Pro, making it convenient for users to send and receive emails and edit text. This phone is equipped with a 3.1-inch touchscreen, a built-in 5-megapixel camera, Wi-Fi, GPS functions, and a 1820mAh battery that ensures long-lasting endurance. However, this phone only runs on Android 2.1, which is somewhat outdated.

Motorola Titanium Phone

Motorola and Sprint will announce the release date and price of the Motorola Titanium at a later time.

New Device Two: Motorola XPRT

Released alongside the Motorola Titanium was the Motorola XPRT. Both phones use a straight full keyboard design, but compared to the professional-level Titanium, the Motorola XPRT is more suitable for mass users. This is a powerful Android business phone with impressive hardware configuration and global roaming support.

Motorola XPRT (left) and Motorola Titanium

The Motorola XPRT can be seen as a new variant of Verizon's Droid Pro, adopting a straight QWERTY keyboard design with a compact and stylish body. In terms of specifications, the Motorola XPRT features a 1GHz processor, a 3.1-inch HVGA resolution multi-touch capacitive screen, runs on Android 2.2, supports Adobe Flash 10, and comes with the Motorola MOTOBLUR interface.

Motorola XPRT uses a straight full keyboard design

In addition, the Motorola XPRT has a built-in 5-megapixel camera with dual LED flashlights. The phone supports microSD card memory expansion up to 32GB. Notably, the XPRT has a battery capacity of 1860mAh, providing excellent endurance.

The Motorola XPRT is a globally roaming phone that supports CDMA 2000 (EVDO Rev.A) networks as well as GSM/WCDMA networks, enabling seamless roaming in over 200 countries worldwide. This phone will officially launch on June 5th, with a two-year contract price of $129.99. Users can purchase this phone from Sprint's online store or retail stores.

New Device Three: BlackBerry Bold Touch

On the evening of May 2, 2011 Beijing Time, the annual BlackBerry World Conference officially opened in Orlando, USA. At this conference, RIM announced the new BlackBerry Bold 9900, BlackBerry Bold 9930, and BlackBerry OS 7 operating system.

BlackBerry Bold 9900 and BlackBerry Bold 9930 Officially Released

The BlackBerry Bold 9900 and BlackBerry Bold 9930 can be considered brother models, differing only in network standards. The BlackBerry Bold 9900 supports GSM/WCDMA, while the BlackBerry Bold 9930 supports both CDMA and GSM/WCDMA for global roaming. Both phones feature the traditional straight QWERTY full keyboard design of BlackBerry phones, but differ significantly from previous products as they both incorporate touchscreens.

The BlackBerry Bold 9900 and BlackBerry Bold 9930 come with powerful hardware configurations

Both the BlackBerry Bold 9900 and BlackBerry Bold 9930 run on the latest BlackBerry OS 7 system. In terms of hardware configuration, both phones feature a 2.8-inch VGA resolution screen, a 1.2GHz processor, and 768MB of RAM. Additionally, both the BlackBerry Bold 9900 and BlackBerry Bold 9930 are equipped with a 5-megapixel camera, supporting Wi-Fi and NFC functions.

New Device Four: Sony Ericsson Xperia mini

In 2010, Sony Ericsson launched two mini smartphones - the Xperia X10 mini and the X10 mini pro, achieving great success. In the wave of product line updates in 2011, Sony Ericsson introduced the latest two mini smartphones - the Sony Ericsson Xperia mini and the Sony Ericsson Xperia mini pro. Compared to the previous generation, these two new phones have seen significant improvements in design and configuration.

Sony Ericsson Xperia mini

Compared to the previous generation, the Xperia mini and Xperia mini pro have made significant upgrades in both software and hardware configurations. They now run on the latest Android 2.3 operating system, equipped with a 1GHz Qualcomm Snapdragon processor, upgraded to a 3-inch HVGA resolution screen supporting multi-touch, and feature Sony BRAVIA Engine-powered Reality displays for significantly improved visual effects.

Sony Ericsson Xperia mini

Furthermore, the Sony Ericsson Xperia mini and Xperia mini pro have enhanced multimedia capabilities, both featuring a built-in 5-megapixel autofocus camera with 720p HD video recording capability.

Sony Ericsson Xperia mini

Key Features of the Sony Ericsson Xperia mini:

・World’s smallest Android phone supporting HD video recording;

・Reality display powered by Sony Mobile BRAVIA Engine;

・New interface with space for 16 applications in each corner;

・Over 150,000 apps downloadable from the Android Market;

・Integrated SNS websites for easy friend communication;

New Device Five: Sony Ericsson Xperia mini pro

The Sony Ericsson Xperia mini pro has little difference in functionality compared to the Xperia mini. It shares the same design style and is equally compact, but while the Xperia mini is a full-touchscreen design, the Xperia mini pro features a side-sliding full keyboard, making it better suited for text input.

Sony Ericsson Xperia mini pro

Key Features of the Sony Ericsson Xperia mini pro:

・Ergonomically designed smart full keyboard for faster messaging;

・Reality display powered by Sony Mobile BRAVIA Engine;

・Convenient, secure, and fast email and Office document browsing function;

・New interface with space for 16 applications in each corner;

・Over 150,000 apps downloadable from the Android Market;

Sony Ericsson Xperia mini pro

The Sony Ericsson Xperia mini and Xperia mini pro will be globally available in the third quarter of this year, both offering multiple color options. However, Sony Ericsson has not yet announced the retail prices of these two phones.

New Device Ten: HTC Bliss

Smartphones are often seen as a man's domain, but in fact, many women are also potential smartphone users, especially those with fashionable designs that are quite popular among female users. HTC plans to launch an Android phone specifically targeted at female users. According to foreign media reports, this phone is called the HTC Bliss.

Concept image of the HTC Bliss female phone

According to reports, this HTC Bliss will still maintain a typical HTC-style design and may resemble the HTC Desire S. However, as a product specifically aimed at female users, the HTC Bliss will be unique in terms of color, material, and accessories. It will have a green appearance, a rubber back cover, and come with accessories like a charm with call-light effect. Moreover, HTC will provide the Bliss with trendy accessories such as a wireless charging dock, speaker dock, and fashionable Bluetooth headphones.

In terms of functionality, the HTC Bliss may not overly pursue top-tier hardware, but it will have mainstream configurations. Furthermore, HTC will pre-install some applications favored by women, such as calorie calculators and shopping comparison tools. Currently, there are no images of this phone available; the above is just a conceptual image provided by foreign websites. It is reported that HTC will launch this phone together with Verizon, and we will continue to follow its developments.
