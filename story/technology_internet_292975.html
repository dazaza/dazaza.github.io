by yang5230 on 2009-12-04 17:18:33

Two women, who gives her happiness?

A: She: "Little Mother, help me light the candle." He: "Rock-paper-scissors, whoever wins will go." She: "Never mind, I'll do it myself."

B: They stand together watching a Korean drama. She stops, he asks "Shall we do it?" She: "Let's light the candle." He: "You stay here and watch, I'll go get it for you."

A woman needs little to be happy; her only request of a man is to "cherish her." You can have nothing, as long as you cherish her, she will have enough courage to give you her entire life.

A: She calls him at night, "Darling, I'm going out to eat with friends tonight." She: "Didn't you promise to go shopping with me?" He: "Let's change the plan!" She silently sheds tears, why can't everyone keep their promises?

B: He calls her during work, "Dear, someone gave me an Olympic ticket, Barcelona! I'm going to watch the game now." She: "Oh, okay." He: "Why aren't you happy?" She: "You forgot, last week we agreed my friends and their girlfriends would treat us to dinner tomorrow." He: "Oops, sorry dear, I forgot. Let me give the ticket to someone else, I'll accompany you to dinner." She: "It's fine, we can change the plan or maybe next time. We'll wait for you." He: "That won't do, I must keep my promises to you. Besides, you wouldn't be comfortable being the third wheel with them." She: "No problem..." Before she finishes, he strongly tells her, "Alright, listen to me, get ready, I'll pick you up soon."

Women are not unreasonable, they just need a considerate man. In fact, couples can tease each other.

A: He: "I'm going out to eat tonight." She: "When will you come home?" He: "Definitely after nine." 9:30 PM, She: "Why haven't you come home yet?" He: "Ten o'clock, definitely coming home." 11 o'clock, 12 o'clock, one o'clock, two o'clock... At this point, she no longer calls to urge him because she knows that for those who don't keep promises, all "definites" are "not necessarily."

B: He: "I'm going out to eat tonight, definitely back by nine, then we'll go watch a movie." She: "Can you finish so quickly?" He: "Of course, I promised you, I definitely can!" When it reaches nine, he says, "Get ready, I'll be at your place soon." Trust is built piece by piece through small matters.

A: She feels unwell, struggles painfully to wash clothes and clean the house. He stands behind the computer playing online games. After finishing her chores, she lies in bed, sighing deeply. He glances at her: "Honey, you've worked hard!" Then continues playing his game.

B: She feels very uncomfortable, gets up to wash clothes. He pulls her: "Lie down on the bed, let me do it!" She: "Can you even do housework? Have you ever washed your own clothes?" He: "If I don't know how, I can learn. In the future, whenever you feel unwell, I will take care of everything!" Women need more than sweet talk; a few comforting words might earn her a smile, but genuine care will make her grateful for life and reward you with a warm home.

A: She puts a bag of hazelnuts in front of him, then goes to wash clothes. When she comes back, the hazelnuts have been mostly eaten by him.

B: She places a bag of hazelnuts in front of him, then starts tidying up the house. When she comes back, she sees a pile of shelled hazelnut kernels on the computer desk. A person is rational; she appreciates your thoughtfulness like showing off a diamond ring, it's so easy to buy, a little effort can bring extraordinary wealth.

A: He says, "You're the best." She replies, "What makes me good?" He: "High education, strong abilities, beautiful appearance, and you're so good to me." She cries.

B: He: "You're the best girl I've ever met." She: "What makes me good?" He: "You're kind to everyone around you, selfless, always grateful towards people and life. A person with a kind heart brings warmth to those around them. You're the most kind-hearted girl I've ever seen, anyone who hurts you should go to heaven!" She cries.

A person feels good because you are good to them. A person wants to be good to you because they understand your goodness. Happy lovers ultimately should be a pair of mutual admirers.

You say, "** You're too kind," but why do you want to hurt such a kind girl who loves you so much?
