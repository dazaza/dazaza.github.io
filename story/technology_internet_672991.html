Qile Mei -- Can collagen really reverse the aging process?

by hjs1233hy11 on 2012-02-23 12:35:32

The so-called "collagen" which claims to restore women's skin to baby-like smoothness and tenderness has recently been gaining popularity. Collagen creams, collagen oral liquids, collagen injections... All beauty products related to collagen, with hefty price tags, have become the new favorites of young women. Do collagen beauty products really have the miraculous effect of "recalling" youthful skin?

Investigation: Can collagen really remove wrinkles?

Experts introduce that collagen is distributed throughout all tissues and organs of the body, especially in the skin, where the weight of collagen accounts for 70%. Collagen is responsible for providing elasticity and firmness to the skin and plays the role of a "deep reservoir" by supplying moisture to the epidermis. It is the main "adhesive" supporting the structure of the dermis layer in the skin, working together with elastin fibers to form a network of support structures, like the steel frame supporting skin tissue. Therefore, sufficient collagen can indeed make skin cells plump, thus keeping the skin hydrated and presenting a tight elasticity and luster.

As age increases and with the damage from ultraviolet rays and free radicals, after the age of 25, women's ability to synthesize new collagen gradually decreases. The skin becomes loose, dehydrated, sensitive, dull, easily forming fine lines, spots, and enlarged pores due to the loss and degradation of collagen.

Survey: Three popular forms of collagen supplementation

In my investigation, I found that currently there are mainly three popular methods of supplementing collagen: external care (masks, cosmetic formulations), injection by specialized physicians, and oral health supplements (pure powder, oral liquid, capsules). The specific usage methods are as follows:

External use: Adding a certain amount of collagen or amino acids into cosmetics, according to beauticians, directly applying it on the skin has protective and anti-aging effects, can replenish highly penetrating and absorbable concentrated collagen, quickly replenishing the lost collagen in the skin and smoothing out wrinkles. However, its fatal flaw is the short duration of effectiveness, and even long-term use cannot convert it into one's own collagen.

Injection: Injection performed by specialized physicians can improve facial aging and wrinkle problems, mainly suitable for facial wrinkles, crow's feet, frown lines, nasolabial folds, acne scars, skin atrophy caused by diseases or injuries, etc. Its downside is that some people may be allergic to directly injected collagen, causing discomfort symptoms after injection, and its effects are temporary, only lasting from half a year to two years; the high cost of collagen injection is not affordable for most beauty-seeking women. Moreover, since relevant departments of the state have not yet approved this medical project, regular medical institutions have not yet carried out this project, while some beauty institutions are secretly doing it.

Oral intake: Oral collagen, when taken, will be hydrolyzed and absorbed in the gastrointestinal tract, serving as raw material for the human body to synthesize collagen. Of course, this is just what the advertisements claim, and users have mixed reviews about it.

Expert opinion: The effect of supplementing collagen is not significant

Director Wang Fei of Zhongda Hospital affiliated with Southeast University: Regarding the blind enthusiasm for "collagen" among many young women today, Director Wang stated that there is actually no need for it. Eating more foods rich in collagen such as pig trotters and fatty meat can also play a role in supplementation. Since collagen is high in fat, calories, and protein, moderate consumption to avoid obesity is also worth paying attention to.

Deputy Chief Physician Luo Wenfeng of the Dermatology Department at Nanjing Traditional Chinese Medicine Hospital: Collagen is the most abundant protein in animals and the human body, accounting for approximately 6% of the total body weight. They exist between cells, playing the role of connective tissue. Since collagen is a protein, its molecular structure is large, making it difficult to absorb when directly applied to the skin. The synthesis of collagen in the human body uses amino acids as raw materials, going through a series of combinations and arrangements.

Director Song Wei of the Dermatology Department at Gulou Hospital: These products now appearing on the market are called functional cosmetics. They lie between drugs and cosmetics. Since they have passed quality inspections by relevant national departments and are available for sale, they have gone through some clinical experimental research and obtained qualification certificates.

But do these products really achieve the effects advertised by merchants? Director Song said that two issues need to be considered: first, whether these functional cosmetics exaggerate their effects in their advertisements; second, whether every buyer's skin is suitable.

So, can oral pills supplement collagen? There are also many types of collagen supplements available on the market. Experts introduced that the medical community currently has no definitive conclusion, but many dermatologists have doubts about it. In fact, very few orally ingested nutrients can be absorbed and utilized by the skin. For example, another major new favorite in the beauty industry, L-vitamin C, only about 7% reaches the skin through food supplementation.
