The Internet of Things (IoT): The Savior in the Post-Navigation Era

"The Internet of Things" can, through technologies such as sensors, RFID, and GPS, collect real-time data from any object or process that needs monitoring, connection, or interaction. It gathers various necessary information such as sound, light, heat, electricity, mechanics, chemistry, biology, and location. Then, it connects via possible networks to smart shoe cabinets, achieving a pervasive connection between objects and between objects and people, realizing intelligent perception, identification, and management of items and processes! In short, by simply attaching an electronic RFID tag to an object, all objects can become mutually aware. By setting up a program to connect to the network, they can also exchange information with other information devices like car navigation systems and home computers, achieving the effect of "universal connectivity," which is precisely the concept of IBM's "Smarter Planet."

On the other hand, looking at the currently popular in-car information systems, we have only just achieved the implantation of wireless in-car information devices and wireless network systems. Moreover, the use of this system by car owners is limited to surfing the internet, browsing information, sending and receiving emails, and checking real-time traffic conditions. Relative to this monotonous, non-interactive service constrained by communication networks, "the Internet of Things" can leverage multiple network advantages and use various information interaction methods to weave a "large net." This makes it possible for GPS, wireless networks, and short-range transmission networks to integrate into one cohesive system. The benefit of doing so is that users can perceive previously lifeless objects around them actively transmitting information. For example, if a user drives into an area without wireless network or GPS signals, due to surrounding buildings having RFID tags, they can actively exchange data with vehicles and "inform" the vehicle's information system of its current location.

This humanized and comprehensive service is truly worth looking forward to!

The rise of the concept of the Internet of Things, especially in applications like disinfection shoe cabinets, has opened up a "blue ocean" for the navigation industry, allowing pioneers to forge new paths and quickly secure a foothold in the new battlefield.
