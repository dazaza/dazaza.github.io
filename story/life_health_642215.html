Eleven simple ways to prevent a cold

by zhouzhou56 on 2011-12-23 12:08:08

For a cold, although we can not control it, we still have ways to prevent and relieve the symptoms. When another cold season is coming, you may try some methods against cold.

1. Soak feet in hot water

Soak your feet for 15 minutes every night in very hot water (the temperature should be as high as you can bear). Note that the water level should be above the foot surface. After soaking, your feet should turn red, which can prevent a cold.

2. Eat raw green onions

When eating raw green onions, you can heat oil and pour it over finely chopped green onion shreds, then mix with other dishes and eat them cold. It is not only delicious but also can prevent a cold.

3. Gargle with salt water

Gargle with diluted salt water every morning and evening, after meals, to remove oral bacteria. During the flu epidemic, gargling with salt water should be paid more attention to. At this time, tilting your head back and gargling so that the salt water thoroughly rinses the throat will give better results.

4. Wash face with cold water

When washing your face every day, use cold water. Cup a handful of water to wash your nostrils, that is, inhale a little water through your nostrils (be careful not to inhale too deep to avoid choking) and then blow it out, repeat several times.

5. Massage nasal groove

Rub your hands together until they are warm, then massage the Yingxiang acupoint (located in the nasal groove, horizontally aligned with the midpoint of the outer edge of the nose) more than ten times, which can prevent a cold and alleviate nasal congestion after catching a cold.

6. Rub nose with green onions

What to do if your nose is blocked after catching a cold? You can insert a fresh green onion into each nostril when sleeping, take them out after 3 hours, and usually one time can cure it. If it does not work, you can insert them again the next day. It is worth reminding that: first, choose thicker green onions, because thinner ones have less medicinal power and are easier to be inhaled deeply into the nasal cavity, making them harder to remove; secondly, if the patient's nasal cavity is allergic to fresh green onions, you can wrap the green onions with a thin layer of cotton.

7. Rub body with white wine

Dip smooth hard objects such as copper coins or coins in white wine, and gently scrape the front and back chest, Quchi point, and the inner side of the lower limbs until the skin turns red and hot. Then drink a bowl of hot ginger sugar water. About 15 minutes later, you will sweat profusely. After sweating, you will feel relaxed and comfortable all over. At this time, be sure to avoid wind-cold, and the cold will recover quickly.

8. Inhale steam

At the beginning of a cold, pour boiling water into a cup, and do deep breathing towards the steam until the water in the cup cools down. Do this several times a day, which can alleviate nasal congestion.

9. Blow hot air on face

At the beginning of a cold, you can blow hot air from a hairdryer onto the temples for 3 to 5 minutes, several times a day, which can reduce symptoms and accelerate recovery.

10. Garlic honey

Mix equal parts of garlic paste and honey evenly, and take it with plain boiled water. Take one tablespoon each time, 4 to 6 times a day, which has excellent effects in treating influenza.

11. Sesame oil scrambled egg

Heat a liang (a traditional Chinese unit of weight) of sesame oil and beat in a fresh egg, then pour in boiling water and stir evenly, and drink it while it's hot. Take once in the morning and evening, and the cough after recovering from a cold can be cured in 2 to 3 days.
