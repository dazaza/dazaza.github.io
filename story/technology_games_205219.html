Behind the honor -- You. Supporting me!

by lk0gameh on 2009-07-18 21:25:47

My ID is in Zone 6, Area 9, Yi Yang, Level 180 Ranged Combat, over 1300 combat power. My wife's ID is Ambiguity, Level 172 Melee Combat, over 1100 combat power. Let's cut to the chase.

It has been a year since I first played Mobile Suit. At that time, it was the second online game I had ever played (the first one was Fantasy Westward Journey). Now, looking back, the reason why I have continued playing until now is not only because of its gorgeous graphics, realistic game effects, and exhilarating battle scenes, but also because of the countless friends who have brought me joy and my wife Ambiguity who has silently stayed by my side. We have spent countless days and nights together. Every corner of Mobile Suit bears our footprints.

At the beginning, we were playing in Zone 8. After much effort, I finally reached level 160, with decent equipment and floating devices. However, someone hacked my account and stole everything. I was utterly disheartened and sulking for quite some time. Later, under the encouragement of my friends, I started playing in the new Zone 6, where my love story with my wife Ambiguity began anew.

When the new zone opened, everyone started on the same footing, so the gap wasn't obvious at first. But after a few days, the gap widened. Since I had experience from before, it took me about a week to reach level 150. At that time, I was using Green A with XP to kill monsters when Ambiguity passed by. Due to a mode mistake, I accidentally killed her along with the monster. When I realized, I saw a soccer ball wandering around me. I quickly typed "Sorry, I didn't mean to kill you." She didn't reply, got up and attacked me without a word. When I couldn't hold out any longer, I reluctantly knocked her down again and said, "I already told you it was an accident!" She still didn't answer, got up and repeated her actions. Out of frustration, I quickly defeated her. Little did I know that as soon as she came back to life, she would run over and attack me again. This cycle of 'killing and being killed' lasted more than an hour. Finally, I couldn't keep going and said to her, "Boss, I admire you so much. I give up, I surrender, okay?" Then I stood still and let her kill me. After she killed me, she finally typed two words: "Haha." Lying on the ground, I felt helpless and asked her, "Are you crazy? Don't you care about losing so much experience?" She replied, "You don't understand. It's called rising from death. Aren't you the one who's so powerful? And yet, I've taken revenge." I couldn't help but laugh and cry at the same time. I thought to myself, anyone can be offended, but never offend a woman.

After that 'killing and being killed' incident, we became good friends. As time went by, we became inseparable 'good friends.' In later conversations, I learned that her name was Zhang Xi, she was 21 years old, one year older than me, and she was from Chongqing. She asked me what my name was. I told her that people generally call me Star, and she laughed.

"Xing Xing"? I knew she intentionally mistyped these two characters, but I didn't point it out. Instead, I said to her, "So you're calling your husband a gorilla, which means you're a female gorilla, right?" She immediately changed the subject.

"You... Is there only you in your family?"

"Are you checking my household registration?"

"So what if I am? Just confess."

"I'm not like you, a pampered only child. I have an older brother and a younger brother." (In fact, there were only me and my older brother in the family. I intentionally said I had a younger brother to scare her.)

"Ah!" She seemed a bit skeptical.

"Is he really your biological brother?"

"Of course."

"If you had a sister, wouldn't she be very popular?"

"Yes, my parents wanted a daughter, but they ended up with another son, which is why I have a younger brother."

"Haha."

"If you come to my house, wouldn't they feel like they have a daughter? They might even treat you better than their own daughter!" (I secretly calculated this in my mind.)

"Hmph..." The screen left a "hmpf" and a long string of periods.

The base prison was a place we often went to. We stood shoulder to shoulder there, sometimes chatting with friends, sometimes silent, often staying there for hours. It was our secret base, a place to share heartfelt words.

When we had nothing to do, we would go "shopping." (Our common language for the in-game market.) Sometimes we didn't see anything we liked or couldn't afford it! She would then immediately throw me a little money~ After buying something, I couldn't help but recall a classic line from comedian Wu Mengda: "Tofu-making wives work hard." She naively asked me, "Why make tofu?" I laughed and said, "To make money to support the family!" She had no response and sent me an angry face. Now that we both have money, we don't worry about not having enough money to buy things we like when we go shopping, but we rarely go shopping anymore. One reason is that once we have enough money and good equipment, I don't want people to think I'm a rich person who still scavenges for old goods and ruins my image. Another reason is that if our friends find out that we are going "shopping," they will follow us around and make noise, with a bright spotlight shining behind us, ruining the mood for shopping. (Speaking of spotlights, Cool Black should be the brightest one kilowatt.)
