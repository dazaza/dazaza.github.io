Our country should establish a military special zone just like it established economic special zones.

Author: Paradox

Submission Date: March 17, 2010, 11:26:07

In today's society, peace is the main theme, and developing the economy is the most important topic worldwide. Therefore, our government should cultivate different types of people in different regions based on the varying needs of the nation. In the "Mr. Lü’s Spring and Autumn Annals," it says: "People are not always brave, nor are they always timid. With spirit, they are solid, and with solidity comes bravery; without spirit, they are empty, and with emptiness comes timidity. The subtleties of courage, timidity, emptiness, and fullness must not be overlooked." It is quite contradictory for one person to both revere Confucianism and yet admire martial virtues, which could lead to mental illness or dual personalities. One of the key contents of Shang Yang's reforms was to make the people of Qin “courageous in public battles but timid in private fights.” He achieved this through heavy rewards for battle achievements and severe punishments for private brawls. However, during his time, the state of Qin was still small, and all citizens were soldiers. War was the main theme, making such measures feasible.

The book "Simafa" states: "In ancient times, the demeanor of a civilian did not enter the military, and the demeanor of the military did not enter civilian life. If the military demeanor enters civilian life, then civil virtue will be lost; if civilian demeanor enters the military, then civil virtue will weaken. Hence, within the state, speech should be refined and tone gentle; in court, respect and humility should be shown. One should improve oneself while waiting for others, not come when unsummoned, not speak when unasked, find it hard to advance and easy to retreat. Within the army, one should stand firm and act decisively; those armored do not bow, chariots do not salute, one does not rush on city walls, and dangerous matters are not discussed lightly. Thus, propriety and law are interdependent, as are civility and martial prowess."

Russia’s methods are worth learning from: The Cossacks have made great contributions to Russia's modern army. In 1993, Russian President Yeltsin ordered the re-establishment of Cossack units and organizations. They are known for their fierce fighting skills and numerous battle honors, having shed blood in Chechnya where Cossack special forces struck fear into the hearts of illegal Chechen armed groups.

China also needs its own Cossacks. Just as we build economic special zones, we should build military special zones to cultivate military talent for the nation and ensure that part of the Chinese nation always retains a particularly martial spirit. National talent cultivation should be layered, divided, and focused. Over a hundred years of humiliation has deeply taught the Chinese nation that military preparedness can never be relaxed, and soldiers must be fearless. Soldiers need an iron-blooded spirit, but not everyone needs such a spirit. Most people should become good citizens, practicing kindness, respect, frugality, and humility, respecting parents at home, raising children, following social order outside, and working hard.

We cannot make everyone Confucian scholars, nor can we make everyone soldiers. Society should be like an ant colony, with worker ants and soldier ants. Therefore, establishing military special zones is especially necessary. For example, Zhangjiakou, the northern gateway to Beijing, could be a pilot project. Zhangjiakou has historically been a place of strategic military importance, with a tough local populace. If the state implemented special military education here, cultivating military talent from a young age and making it a national priority for conscription, it would greatly benefit maintaining and consolidating the combat style of our army. In these military special zones, instead of promoting gentleness, respect, humility, frugality, and yielding, sacrifice, obedience, fighting, and honor—qualities that soldiers should possess—would be emphasized.

Throughout Chinese history, there have been many instances of national decline due to prolonged peace. The Anshi Rebellion and the turmoil at the end of the Qing Dynasty were partly caused by this. Nowadays, people's indulgence in pleasure is rising again. Male TV hosts speaking in effeminate voices and acting feminine reflect this trend and exacerbate it further. Therefore, our country needs a military special zone where people are fierce and warlike, valuing martial arts and righteousness.
