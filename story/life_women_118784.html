Shrinking the Masseter Muscle

Although "peach-shaped face" is no longer popular now, replaced by the preference for an oval face, it's undeniable that a small face always draws people's affection. Celebrities are all trying to make their faces smaller, and on TV, there are facial cleansers being sold that promise slimmer cheeks, as if being born with a square or large face was your "biggest fault."

What is the surgery for shrinking the masseter muscle?

Masseter hypertrophy is also called benign masseter hypertrophy. The human masticatory muscles include: masseter, temporalis, medial pterygoid, lateral pterygoid, etc. Therefore, the occurrence of masseter hypertrophy is generally thought to be related to chewing habits and dietary habits. For example, regularly eating hard foods or having the habit of snacking or chewing gum can contribute to this condition. Some also believe that masseter hypertrophy is related to genetic factors. In fact, from a clinical perspective, there indeed seems to be a familial tendency towards masseter hypertrophy. Masseter hypertrophy often accompanies conditions such as mandibular angle hypertrophy or mandibular angle eversion. Thus, in clinical settings, masseter hypertrophy is also referred to as mandibular angle hypertrophy or benign masseter hypertrophy. There are fewer cases where only the hypertrophic masseter is removed. If the masseter is indeed hypertrophied, surgery usually involves removing part of the masseter along with the mandibular angle.

Who needs the surgery to shrink the masseter muscle?

People with hypertrophic masseter muscles.

Methods of Shrinking the Masseter Muscle Surgery:

Method One: Surgical removal of part of the masseter muscle

This method easily leads to complications such as excessive bleeding and facial nerve injury (facial paralysis), so it is now rarely used.

Method Two: Injection of Type A botulinum toxin

This method can treat bilateral masseter hypertrophy. It works through the neurotrophic effect causing the masseter muscle to atrophy and shrink. Usually, 1-2 injection sites are chosen per side. Advantages: Within one month of injection into the masseter, one can observe the atrophy of the masseter and changes in the face shape. It's simple and easy to operate. Disadvantages: If the dosage and injection site are not well controlled, local adverse reactions may occur, mainly including excessive weakness of the masseter, stiff facial expressions caused by the diffusion of botulinum toxin to adjacent muscle tissues; some patients may experience fever, discomfort, and fatigue after injection; each injection's effects last for about half a year. Surgery time: about 10 minutes.

Characteristics after Shrinking the Masseter Muscle Surgery:

The masseter muscle appropriately shrinks and becomes smaller.

Contraindications for Shrinking the Masseter Muscle Surgery:

Pregnant women should not receive injections.
