Experts from Minsheng Gospel ENT Specialty Hospital are explaining the sinus CT situation for sinusitis patients. ●"What a close call, almost lost my life!" On January 26th this year, Ms. Zhou from Qingyang District fainted in the middle of the road. A car came rushing by, almost running her over. Who would have thought that the reason she nearly lost her life was due to a small case of sinusitis! Dizziness caused her to fall, nearly costing her life on the road.

The 25-year-old Ms. Zhou still feels terrified recalling the moment she fainted. Being young and agile, how could she have fallen? "I had severe dizziness, and before I knew it, I had passed out in the middle of the road," said Ms. Zhou. "Thankfully, the driver braked in time; otherwise, I wouldn't be sitting here today." Talking about her experience, Ms. Zhou showed signs of panic.

It was learned that after being taken to the hospital by kind-hearted people, the doctor found out that Ms. Zhou often suffered from headaches, nausea, and had a particularly poor memory. Her condition had worsened recently, leading to the situation today. It turned out that Ms. Zhou had suffered from sinusitis for many years, with constant nasal congestion, pus discharge, and coughing, which had already made her suffer greatly. But who would have known that the headaches and nausea causing inconvenience to her work had left her exhausted both physically and mentally.

According to Director Wu of the hospital, sinusitis easily triggers symptoms such as nasal congestion, sneezing, runny nose, and prolonged sinusitis can lead to dizziness, memory loss, headaches, and nausea, even causing high blood pressure or threatening brain health. "A very important reason why Ms. Zhou suddenly fainted in the middle of the road was due to sinusitis."

Unable to bear the pain, Ms. Zhou followed the doctor's advice and went to Minsheng Gospel ENT Specialty Hospital. After undergoing two examinations—nasal endoscopy and sinus CT—as well as other multiple checks, the results showed: bilateral middle and lower turbinate hypertrophy, sinus opening mucosal edema, pharyngitis, and accumulated secretions in the sinuses. She was diagnosed with chronic rhinitis and sinusitis.

Director Wu of the hospital explained that there is only a thin layer of bone plate separating the human sinus cavity from the brain. Inflammation in the sinus cavity or sinus opening can easily affect the brain, making people feel pressure in their heads, leading to dull or throbbing headaches, and also resulting in reduced or lost sense of smell. If sinusitis is not treated in time for a long period, it can cause complications such as pharyngitis, tonsillitis, otitis media, and tracheitis. Severe cases can even cause complications in the eyes and cranium, with common clinical manifestations including worsening headaches, nausea, vomiting, and slow pulse. After understanding the dangers of sinusitis, Ms. Zhou finally decided to undergo the 360° Navigation Functional Cleaning Procedure for sinusitis at the hospital.

Double confirmation: Sinus CT + Nasal Endoscopy Examination

Director Wu Fuxing of the hospital introduced that many people treat sinusitis as ordinary nasal diseases, which not only fails to solve the root problem but may also delay treatment. Director Wu, who performed the 360° Navigation Functional Cleaning Procedure for Ms. Zhou, also pointed out that whether one can undergo comprehensive nasal examination is the key to confirmation, and correct and timely treatment after confirmation is also crucial. Patients with suspected sinusitis, especially those with chronic sinusitis that cannot be cured, should go to a formal professional hospital for a comprehensive diagnosis as soon as possible, especially for the necessary nasal endoscopy and sinus CT examinations. Only by understanding the cause and current state of the disease can timely treatment be administered with targeted therapy. Do not give sinusitis a chance to spread due to delayed treatment.
