The Spring Festival has just passed, and Chinese merchants around the world are preparing to make a fortune in the new year. Unexpectedly at this time, Chinese merchants engaged in the construction industry in Paris, France, encountered an unexpected disaster. A fire turned their large-scale building materials exhibition hall, which they had painstakingly operated for many years, into ashes.

The fire at the Chinese merchant's warehouse was fierce, and the thick smoke could be seen from several kilometers away.

The more than 10,000 square meter Franco-Chinese Construction Industry Federation Exhibition Hall was once the pride of the Chinese merchants.

At around 4:00 PM local time on February 16, a large Chinese merchant warehouse in La Courneuve, northern suburbs of Paris, suddenly caught fire. The fire quickly spread, soon engulfing the entire warehouse area.

According to eyewitnesses, the fire started in a warehouse storing shoes. Since the surrounding warehouses stored flammable items such as clothes and building materials, the fire quickly spread. Later, even the neighboring Franco-Chinese Construction Industry Federation Clubhouse, its more than 10,000 square meter building products exhibition hall and market were also affected.

Local residents introduced that although the fire trucks arrived on the scene in the first instance, the fire was too big to control. When reporters from the French Chinese-language media "European Times" arrived at the scene at around 6:00 PM, the fire at the warehouse was still fierce. Reporters saw flames reaching the sky, accompanied by a pungent smell, and thick smoke rising dozens of meters high, visible from several kilometers away.

By 6:30 PM, the local fire department had dispatched 200 firefighters, used more than 20 fire trucks and several firefighting planes, and even the grass irrigation system in the football field next to the warehouse was used to spray water onto the fire scene, but with little effect. Finally, the firefighters could only try to prevent the fire from spreading to the surrounding houses to avoid greater losses. Local Chinese merchants could only helplessly watch their painstakingly operated building market turn into ashes.

According to French media reports, it was not until the afternoon of February 17 that the fire was completely extinguished, with a total burned area of 12,200 square meters. Currently, the French police have begun investigating the cause of the fire, and insurance companies have sent specialists to the scene to understand the situation and carry out damage assessment and claims work. However, local Chinese merchants generally lack strong insurance awareness, either not insuring their goods or insufficient insurance, so they have to bear many losses themselves. According to investigations, Chinese merchants stored goods in these warehouses, some with over a hundred containers, and others with more than a dozen containers, basically all spring and summer clothing that had not yet been sold. One Chinese boss who specializes in shirts recently had over a hundred containers of goods delivered, but they were all destroyed in the fire, suffering unimaginable losses. Reports say that preliminary estimates put the losses of Chinese merchants in this fire at over one billion euros. The only consolation is that this fire did not result in any casualties.

A heavy blow while preparing to fully enter the French real estate market

Among the losses caused by this fire, what pains Chinese merchants most is the burning down of the Franco-Chinese Construction Industry Federation Exhibition Hall and Market. The federation was established in December 2010, marking another industry association established by Chinese merchants in France after the clothing and footwear industries, signifying a new stage in the development of the local Chinese construction industry.

It is understood that as early as the early 1980s, Chinese people began engaging in the decoration industry in France. In the 1990s, the Chinese decoration industry welcomed its first major development. At that time, with the arrival of large numbers of Wenzhou people in France, the number of restaurants, department stores, clothing, jewelry, and leather goods wholesale shops operated by Chinese people surged, providing a huge space for the development of the Chinese decoration industry.

With the vigorous rise of the Chinese decoration industry, Chinese building materials companies also emerged successively, forming a preliminary scale of the Chinese construction industry. However, obstacles also followed. Due to France's numerous requirements for the construction industry and extremely complex laws and regulations, Chinese people found it difficult to participate in the design and construction of regular buildings through individual efforts, encountering development bottlenecks. The Franco-Chinese Construction Industry Federation was born out of necessity, aiming to integrate the resources of the Chinese construction industry and advance together into the French construction industry.

It is introduced that after more than a year of development, the Franco-Chinese Construction Industry Federation has grown from 40 member enterprises at its inception to more than 70 enterprises today. The association adopts a development model of "supporting the association with enterprises and promoting enterprise development with the association," establishing a complete set of architectural and decoration one-stop service systems.

Meanwhile, the association gradually integrates the clubhouse with company showrooms, decorating them on a considerable scale. Recently, many building materials enterprises have moved here, expanding the operating area to more than 10,000 square meters, forming a well-known Chinese building materials market in Paris and even throughout France. The association is full of confidence, hoping to rely on the strong resource advantages of the motherland, expand its business scale, and fully enter the French real estate market, but unexpectedly suffered this disaster at the beginning of the new year.
