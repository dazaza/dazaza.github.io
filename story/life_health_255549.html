Confronted with A/H1N1 flu, what should human beings reflect on?- Guancheng Traditional Chinese Medicine Hospital Gangrene of Peripheral Vessels

by maiguanyancom on 2009-11-03 17:25:27

According to the latest statistics from the health department, more than 40,000 people in China have been infected with H1N1 influenza A; according to authoritative predictions by the World Health Organization: 80% of the world's population will be infected with H1N1 influenza A. If there are no relevant prevention and control capabilities and measures, the consequences will be unimaginable. Recently, colleges, institutions and public places have constantly strengthened monitoring and prevention. Once someone has a fever or coughs, they are all regarded as dangerous individuals. When it comes to H1N1 influenza A, it seems like everyone is terrified. With seasonal influenza added on top of that, it makes people even more uneasy.

In response to the virus, some classes have been suspended and some schools have closed for holidays. Children who show symptoms of a cold are absolutely not allowed to attend school. Masks are selling well, and thermometers are in short supply. This is an expression of love for life, and it is always worth affirming. However, modern people, who are incredibly intelligent, have the divine power to reach the sky and the ability to dive into the sea for gold. Yet, when faced with sudden outbreaks of viruses, they often appear somewhat at a loss. Think about it, in recent years, starting from SARS, one moment it's foot-and-mouth disease, the next moment it's avian influenza, then it's swine flu------so many new viruses have appeared among the new generation of humans, and each time the root cause is attributed to lower animals, without considering the reasons related to higher animals themselves. Those animals that cannot write with words or talk eloquently are hunted down, feasted upon, and judged by humans, truly suffering great injustice. Under the same blue sky, on the same earth, as equally living beings, just because they cannot speak human language, they receive such treatment and bullying.
