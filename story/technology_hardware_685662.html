Woman embezzles tens of millions of yuan and gives it to her boyfriend to squander, lives on the run as a scavenger

by xue94fwsh on 2012-03-01 16:06:18

Cai Xiaohong and Zhang Hongguang during their trial. The "boyfriend" Zhang Hongguang requested a loan, and without hesitation, Cai Xiaohong, a financial staff member at the Wangfujing Foreign Language Bookstore, misappropriated public funds. Over 10 years, Cai Xiaohong willingly helped Zhang Hongguang embezzle nearly 10 million yuan in public funds, yet she did not take a single cent for herself. After the incident came to light, Cai Xiaohong and the man fled to Yunnan, where Cai Xiaohong lived by scavenging. Yesterday, the Beijing No. 2 Intermediate People's Court sentenced Cai Xiaohong to 18 years in prison for embezzlement and abuse of power, while Zhang Hongguang was sentenced to 16 years in prison.

Over the course of ten years, they embezzled nearly 10 million yuan. Cai Xiaohong, aged 57 this year, is from Beijing and has a college diploma. She started working at the Beijing Foreign Language Bookstore (later renamed Beijing Wangfujing Foreign Language Bookstore Co., Ltd.) in 1985. From June 1991 to June 1994, while serving as a statistician in the business department of the Beijing Foreign Language Bookstore, Cai Xiaohong colluded with Zhang Hongguang, an unemployed man from Beijing, using her responsibilities for handling payments between the bookstore and its branches such as Wangfujing Foreign Language Bookstore and Foreign Language Tools Bookstore, as well as preparing and keeping related accounting records, to embezzle over 1.9 million yuan through intercepting transfer checks.

Additionally, from June 1994 to May 2000, Cai Xiaohong again colluded with Zhang Hongguang, taking advantage of her position, to embezzle more than 6.5 million yuan through similar methods. All the money gained from Cai Xiaohong's embezzlement and abuse of power was spent by Zhang Hongguang.

During the trial, both Cai Xiaohong and Zhang Hongguang claimed they were only ordinary friends. Cai Xiaohong did not offer any defense for her crimes. According to her confession, she met Zhang Hongguang while riding horses at a horse farm in Haidian, where he introduced himself as a company boss. In 1991, Zhang Hongguang asked her for a loan due to a lack of business capital, though he didn't explicitly say to take it from her workplace, she knew she had no personal savings. They initially agreed that the money would be returned in a few days, but it kept dragging on.

Cai Xiaohong said Zhang Hongguang frequently asked her for loans, each time claiming there was a good deal and that all previous debts would be repaid once the deal was done. She was skeptical but realized if she refused, she wouldn’t recover the prior loans.

While fleeing, she survived by scavenging. Cai Xiaohong was unaware that Zhang Hongguang had squandered all the money and later became addicted to drugs, which created an insatiable financial void. On October 10, 2006, knowing her employer suspected issues with her accounts, the next morning, after discussing with Zhang Hongguang, they fled together, eventually reaching Jinghong City in Yunnan Province. Zhang Hongguang cohabited with a local woman and occasionally engaged in small businesses, while Cai Xiaohong lived by scavenging, eating together occasionally.

On March 17, 2010, Zhang Hongguang was arrested while watching a live football match broadcast in a local hotel room. The following day, Cai Xiaohong was also captured. It was understood that over the span of ten years, Cai Xiaohong continuously transferred nearly 10 million yuan in public funds to Zhang Hongguang, while maintaining a frugal lifestyle herself. Upon her arrest, police found she owned only one apartment, with no deposits or vehicles under her name. Eventually, the court froze this property, and proceeds from its sale will be used to compensate the victimized company.

By Yan Fei of the Morning Post
