Some parents rarely let their children go out, worrying about this and that. As a result, when children see strangers, they cry or hide, and when they grow up, they tend to be sensitive and withdrawn. Some young children are selfish and lack a spirit of unity, so they have few friends and when they grow up, due to strained interpersonal relationships, their talents may not be fully utilized. Therefore, when children start to understand things, they should be allowed to adapt to new environments. For timid children, encourage them to interact more with others or voluntarily stand up to answer questions raised by the teacher. This process is also called desensitization. If you don't give children opportunities, their adaptability will not naturally develop. From a young age, cultivate children's robust psychological qualities.

A child's growth isn't always smooth; there can be successes as well as failures, and sometimes unrealistic dreams. When encountering difficulties and setbacks, they need encouragement and support even more. Never pour cold water on them. Although their fantasies may seem strange to you, you should be pleased that they possess strong imagination, which is the mentor of creation.

Two, offer encouragement and support.

Three, interact more with society, experience storms and broaden horizons.

There was a story about a group of children who got lost during a mountain picnic. They spent a terrifying night in wetness and hunger, crying out helplessly: "People will never find us." One child sobbed in despair, "We'll die here." However, 11-year-old Eve stood up and said firmly, "I don't want to die!" She said, "My dad said if you follow the stream, it will lead us to a bigger river, and eventually we'll reach a small town. I plan to follow the stream, and you can come with me." In the end, under Eve's leadership, they successfully crossed the forest. People might think that girls like Eve are born with abilities, but talent isn't innate; it comes from her father's later education. Currently, Western countries, including Japan in East Asia, place great emphasis on survival education for children. From the time children understand things, they are taught how to survive and be independent, how to get up after falling, how to eat by themselves, how to tidy up their own belongings, and how to protect themselves in certain situations.

One, educate children to learn survival skills.

Children excel because educational methods are better.

There are no children who cannot be taught well, only bad teaching methods. Parents and teachers often become anxious due to slow progress in children, thereby rudely labeling them as "stupid" or "unchangeable personalities," or considering themselves unlearned and incapable of educating children. In fact, as long as the method is appropriate, any child can be excellent; as long as one makes an effort, suitable educational methods can always be found.

One, Weakness Strategy - Let the child play the role of a "little adult."

Parents often complain that today's children ignore their parents and lack a sense of responsibility. They don't realize that a child's sense of responsibility needs to be cultivated from a young age. If you always appear invincible in front of your child, they will think that parents don't need their care. Therefore, parents might occasionally pretend to be weak and seek help from their child. You will be surprised to find that the child becomes a considerate "little adult," and you can gain a lot from the child's help.

Two, Strengthening Strategy - Help the child transition from self-deprecation to confidence.

James Dobrin, a philosophy doctor from the University of California, proposed the "strengthening law." When a person's behavior receives satisfying results, this behavior tends to recur. Therefore, when a child is recognized, their self-confidence is triggered, leading them to repeatedly engage in satisfactory behaviors until they overcome self-deprecation and become confident, progressive good children.

Three, Induction Strategy - Help the child cross the swamp of academic aversion.

Almost every child has some degree of academic aversion, which can be understood but must not be left unchecked because this emotion can hinder a child's enthusiasm for learning and bring unforeseeable effects on their growth. However, forcing a child to study is not a good solution as it deepens their aversion. The smart way is to induce interest in learning, encouraging the child to take the initiative in studying.

Four, Indifference Strategy - Let the child have some self-control.

Today's children are mostly single children, receiving comprehensive care and attention from their families. However, such a life habituates them to being capricious, which poses significant hidden dangers for their future lives. Experts suggest using cold handling when a child is capricious. This makes the child control their nature due to lack of attention.

Five, Setback Strategy - Give the child an opportunity to experience failure.

American educator Carol Sandburg said, "Talented people can emerge in favorable conditions, but talented people can also emerge in adverse conditions. And children who grow up through setbacks and trials in adversity have stronger survival competitiveness." To enhance a child's resilience to setbacks, parents might intentionally create moderate setbacks, which greatly benefits enhancing a child's psychological endurance.

Six, Letting Go Strategy - Cultivate the ability for children to do their own things.

Don't do everything for your children. Parents should try letting go, allowing children to autonomously handle tasks. At first, they might not do well, but later they will improve. Parents shouldn't be overprotective; instead, they should create opportunities and platforms for children to act, fostering independence and reducing trouble for parents.

Seven, Permissiveness Strategy - Let the child develop potential while being "naughty."

Some parents complain that their children are "too naughty" or "destructive," but these children are often intelligent, with strong curiosity and determination. Therefore, smart parents should try "allowing" the child's naughtiness and "destructive" behavior and use it to uncover potential and foster interests. Your child might just be another "Edison."

Eight, Punishment Strategy - Let the child face their mistakes.

Educating children requires clear rewards and punishments. When a child does well, they should be rewarded, but when they make mistakes, they must not be excused, even for minor ones. Moderate punishment helps the child recognize and correct their mistakes, preventing deeper entanglement in error.

Nine, Cooling Strategy - Pour cold water on confident children.

Overconfidence refers to overly high self-evaluation, ignoring others. This psychology is extremely detrimental to a child's growth. Therefore, once signs of overconfidence are detected, parents should use "cooling" measures, pouring cold water on the child, helping them rationally evaluate themselves and correctly recognize themselves.

Ten, Tolerance Strategy - Let the child feel unconditional love from parents.

A child may not be very smart or may have many flaws, but as parents, you absolutely must not harbor prejudice, shame, or disgust towards your child. You must be tolerant and have faith, and you will receive the kind of child you deserve.

Eleven, Role Model Strategy - Parents should be the child's first good teacher.

Parents are role models for their children, and their words and actions influence the child. Therefore, parents must strictly discipline themselves, setting a good example for their children in all matters, guiding them to grow up healthily.

Twelve, Encouragement Strategy - Give children continuous motivation to advance.

Children easily lose confidence and abandon efforts due to difficulties. Therefore, parents must continuously encourage their children, giving them courage and unwavering belief, enabling them to correct mistakes and constantly improve with the expectations of their parents.

Thirteen, Listening Strategy - Seriously understand the child's feelings.

Each child has their own voice, and parents must patiently listen to truly understand the child's thoughts and feelings, making timely and detailed responses to psychological and physiological issues and changes. With this foundation, parents and children can communicate well and establish a good, harmonious parent-child relationship.

Fourteen, Reward Strategy - Let children improve in praise.

Educational experts believe that rewarding is a more effective way to educate children than punishing. Therefore, they suggest replacing punishment for errors with rewards for correctness and affirming strengths instead of denying weaknesses. This avoids harming the child while improving them further.

Fifteen, Hint Strategy - Cleverly guide the child to voluntarily move towards the right path.

Endless preaching and overbearing parents are disliked by children. Smart parents only provide subtle reminders when the child encounters difficulties or behaves incorrectly, helping them solve problems. This way, the child understands the principles, and the parents achieve their educational goals.

Sixteen, Fiction Strategy - Let the child develop a "I'm great" self-perception.

Japanese educator Suzuki Shinichi said, "With the feeling of genius, you will become a genius; with the feeling of a hero, you will become a hero. A child who finds the feeling of being a good child will become a good child." By creating a "I'm great" self-perception through fictional means, the child will gradually become better.

Seventeen, Tolerance Strategy - Let the child learn more from mistakes.

In life, children inevitably make mistakes, some unintentionally and some deliberately. If the nature of the mistake isn't serious, parents might forgive and empathize. This way, the child will seriously correct their mistakes in guilt and self-blame and develop a good tolerance mindset.

Eighteen, Admonition Strategy - Let the child recognize their mistakes and repent.

Children are bound to encounter various problems and mistakes. Some mistakes can be forgiven, but some cannot be indulged. Reasonable and effective criticism must ensure the child doesn't repeat similar mistakes. Even the most lenient parents cannot neglect this reasonable discipline.

Nineteen, Cultivation Strategy - Influence the child positively in daily life.

The family is the child's first classroom, and its influence on the child is profound. Therefore, if parents can influence the child through storytelling in family life, cultivating healthy interests and temperaments, it will help the child grow up healthier.

Twenty, Persuasion Strategy - Let the child change from resistance to cooperation.

The success of Yu the Great in flood control lies in "diverting" rather than "blocking." Parents can draw inspiration from this method. Too much authoritarianism in educating children causes rebellion, making them oppose you. But adopting rational, open guidance strategies makes children willing to cooperate pleasantly.

Twenty-one, Respect Strategy - Bring the child closer to the parents.

Conflicts between parents and children often arise because parents don't respect their children enough, making them fearful and dissatisfied, widening the gap. Children also need respect, and those who respect them will become the most beloved parents.

Twenty-two, Equality Strategy - Achieve good communication with the child.

The prerequisite for good communication with the child is treating them equally and being their good friend. This way, the child will willingly share their inner thoughts, allowing parents to promptly help them resolve various problems. If parents always act superior, dictating demands, the door to communication will gradually close.

Twenty-three, Defense Strategy - First give the child a "precautionary shot."

Military strategy says, "The best defense is offense." In educating children, it's better to give them a "precautionary shot" beforehand, enhancing their "immunity," helping them avoid detours and mistakes.

Twenty-four, Frugality Strategy - Help the child overcome extravagance.

Nowadays, children are "little emperors" and "little princesses," getting what they want. Some develop extravagant habits, spending lavishly and not cherishing items. This habit is highly detrimental to their healthy growth. Therefore, parents should properly educate their children about frugality, helping them form saving habits, which is true love for the child.

Twenty-five, Participation Strategy - Educate a good child through participation.

If parents participate in the child's life and spend more time accompanying them in activities, it not only promotes parent-child affection but also encourages the child. Moreover, parents can better understand the child, which is crucial for developing their potential and nurturing talent.

Twenty-six, Prevention Strategy - Don't let the child's small faults turn into big problems.

In life, children sometimes have small faults, such as being domineering, impolite, or lazy. At this point, parents should pay attention. Even though the problem is small, strict management is needed to prevent small defects from becoming big issues. Don't indulge in the child's small faults; strict management is also a sign of love.

Twenty-seven, Mediation Strategy - Let the child and teacher mutually tolerate each other.

Friction and misunderstandings can occur between teachers and students. At such times, parents should act as "mediators," helping both sides eliminate conflicts and misunderstandings, understanding and tolerating each other. Let the child continue respecting the teacher, and the teacher continue caring for the child.

Twenty-eight, Rule Strategy - Use reasonable rules to help the child learn self-control.

Children have poor self-control, and parents' repeated "don't do this" or "don't do that" can cause annoyance. Therefore, parents can establish an effective set of behavioral rules for the child, serving as a basis for determining their behavior and constraining their actions.

Twenty-nine, Stress Reduction Strategy - Don't put too much pressure on the child.

In life, more and more children suffer from psychological barriers, and child psychologists point out that excessive stress is a major cause of psychological problems. Reducing the child's burden has become urgent. To promote healthy growth, parents should not put too much pressure on their children.

Thirty, Motivation Strategy - Use stimulating methods to make the child love learning.

Educating children requires finding the right approach. When guidance and preaching fail, parents might try the stimulation strategy. Using the child's competitive spirit, rebelliousness, or even jealousy to intentionally stimulate the child, transforming their disinterest in learning into active learning.

Thirty-one, Acceptance Strategy - Let the child like and accept themselves.

If a child cannot comprehensively understand themselves or see their strengths, many problems can arise, such as arrogance or jealousy. Therefore, parents should let children see their advantages and like themselves, enabling them to confidently face life.

Thirty-two, Patience Strategy - Calmly correct the child's "extreme" behavior.

Children often exhibit "extreme" behavior that parents find intolerable. "Storm-like" education can push the child to extremes. Therefore, parents must "restrain their impatience," suppress anger, understand and tolerate the child more, guiding them based on this foundation to steer them onto the right path.

Thirty-three, Courage Strategy - Cultivate a brave exploratory personality in the child.

Often, those who dare to explore and are fearless achieve outstanding results and happiness. Therefore, for the child's future, parents should not excessively protect them or overly emphasize raising "obedient" children but instead give them more exercise opportunities, encouraging them to be bold.

Thirty-four, Emotional Strategy - Touch the child's heart with sincere emotions.

Educating children cannot solely rely on preaching, scolding, or counseling; sometimes emotional resonance is necessary to touch the child's heart and achieve the best educational outcome. To resonate with the child, sincerity is essential; otherwise, the effect will be significantly reduced.

Thirty-five, Equality Strategy - Cultivate a broad-minded child.

Most children now are single children, each a "little sun," accustomed to being self-centered. However, such a character is highly detrimental to interpersonal interactions. Therefore, in life, parents should not give the child special status, cultivating their sharing and cooperative spirit, gradually nurturing a broad-minded child.

Thirty-six, Hardship Strategy - Cultivate the child's willpower and perseverance.

Children now face a society full of competition, and many, due to overly comfortable lives, often lack the courage to overcome difficulties. Therefore, smart parents should let the child endure hardships and difficulties, cultivating tenacity and perseverance, enabling them to succeed in future competitions.

Five, Let the child think more.

A 12-year-old child with a promising future in gymnastics came to see the head coach. The coach did not immediately ask her to perform gymnastics but gave her four darts, asking her to throw them at the target opposite the office. The little girl fearfully said, "What if I miss?" The coach told her, "You should think about how to succeed, not fail." The little girl practiced repeatedly and finally succeeded. Therefore, in life, you should tell the child that when doing anything, they should first think about success, not failure, believing in oneself leads to success.

Six, Cultivate self-confidence and the ability to withstand setbacks.

The boy thought for a moment: "Drag my cart there and then stand on it." "Good, go ahead, child." The mother said. The boy did this, and everything became very easy.
