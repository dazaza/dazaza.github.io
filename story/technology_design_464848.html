The starting point for any research area is to propose the boundaries of the study and determine its objectives. The starting point of government accounting research is the purpose of government accounting. A clearly defined objective ensures that government accounting reform has the correct direction. Otherwise, the establishment of government accounting standards systems, government accounting institutional frameworks, and government-wide financial reporting systems may become "shoddy projects."

I. Two Major Schools of Thought on Accounting Objectives

Accounting objectives refer to the goals of providing financial information or preparing financial reports. In terms of accounting objectives, there are two major schools of thought in practice: the fiduciary responsibility school and the decision-usefulness school. The fiduciary responsibility school is based on the composition of the "principal-agent" relationship, believing that the purpose of accounting is to report on the entrusted management of resources to resource providers. On the other hand, the decision-usefulness school applies in various situations where the principal-agent relationship is unclear or indirect. Users make decisions by evaluating available options based on accounting information, with the belief that the purpose of accounting is to provide decision-useful information to users.

Since the division of labor between the government and the market has become increasingly clear in China's market economic system reform, the function of the government is to provide public goods and services, which requires the consumption of certain resources. The government represents national will in exercising the power to raise, use, and manage public financial resources. Therefore, it must be subject to the constraints of resource providers (the general public), national laws, contracts, agreements, and other stipulations. It must also be accountable for the effectiveness, efficiency, and economy of resource usage. A principal-agent relationship exists between the people and the government. Due to the clear and direct principal-agent relationship, defining the purpose of government accounting should adhere to the fiduciary responsibility perspective.

II. Issues with the Current Budget Accounting Objectives

(A) The definition of budget accounting information users is overly narrow.

Under the current budget accounting system, the users of budget accounting information are limited to higher-level financial departments and the current level of government (general budget accounting), or relevant parties and unit managers (administrative and institutional accounting). Other natural users of budget accounting information, such as representatives of the People's Congress, the general public, and others affected by government fiscal activities, are excluded. Finance involves the government using coercive power to raise, use, and allocate national wealth. This power is granted by the people, and the purpose of using and allocating national wealth is to safeguard and promote the interests of the people. Therefore, the most important users of budget accounting information reflecting national fiscal activities should be the people or their elected representatives. Excluding these natural users from the scope of budget accounting information users means that the design of the budget accounting system cannot consider their need for budget accounting information, effectively depriving them of their right to speak and know about national fiscal activities.

(B) The summarization of information needs for existing users is too abstract.

The defined scope of information users is very narrow, and the current budget accounting system does not clearly summarize the needs of these users with precise terminology. Instead, ambiguous terms like "financial management" (general budget accounting), "understanding the financial status and revenue/expenditure situation of institutions," and "strengthening internal operational management" (administrative and institutional accounting) are used to describe user needs. Such descriptions of accounting objectives cannot effectively guide the design of budget accounting systems.

III. Considerations for Improving Government Accounting Objectives

(A) At this stage, China's government accounting reform should emphasize the following types of information users and their information needs:

First, the People's Congress. All rights in China belong to the people, and the People's Congress authorizes the government and its units to manage public financial resources and public affairs. The government has an obligation to provide explanations regarding the management of public affairs and the use of public resources to the People's Congress, making the latter an important user of government financial information.

Second, internal government management personnel and higher-level governments. Internal managers, policymakers, and administrative personnel are also users of government financial information. Some of their needs can be met through general financial reports. However, managers also require additional information, such as cost information for specific activities and operations, to effectively fulfill their management responsibilities.

Third, international organizations and other governments. With the trend of economic globalization, government actions need further standardization, and the role of government in the market economy should better conform to international conventions. Government financial reports should meet these requirements. Additionally, international organizations and other governments may also be providers of government financial resources and are therefore concerned about the government's financial condition.

(B) To systematically construct a government accounting system, the objectives of government accounting in China can be divided into three levels: basic objectives, intermediate objectives, and ultimate objectives.

1. The basic objective of government accounting is to inspect, prevent fraud and corruption, protect the security of public financial funds, and ensure compliance with fiduciary responsibilities such as budget adherence. Focusing on processes and emphasizing internal fiduciary responsibilities, government accounting needs to strengthen budget control and provide compliance reports to achieve these objectives. Since 2000, China has successively implemented reforms in financial expenditure systems, including pre-emptive departmental budget preparation, mid-process government procurement, and centralized treasury payment systems, all aimed at strengthening control and management of government and unit budgets. The recent "audit storms" indicate that many issues still exist in the compliance of public sector budgets and financial management, with significant budget softening. The fundamental objective of government accounting can align with the requirements of financial expenditure system reforms, effectively supporting the three major reforms and strengthening government financial expenditure management.

2. The intermediate objective of government accounting is to promote sound fiscal management and performance management in the public sector, focusing on performance fiduciary responsibilities and project fiduciary responsibilities, while also emphasizing internal fiduciary responsibilities. Based on accrual-basis government accounting reforms, the scope of government accounting calculations should be expanded to enhance state asset management, prevent state asset loss, strengthen government debt management, prevent fiscal risks, establish government cost accounting systems, and even government management accounting systems to reinforce the fiduciary performance of the government. Under the influence of the international New Public Management movement, governments and their departments are required to assume more performance fiduciary responsibilities, meaning that the management and use of resources should be economical, efficient, and effective. The intermediate objective of government accounting reflects the demands of this era and effectively improves the management and use performance of entrusted resources by the government.

3. The ultimate objective of government accounting is to promote the fulfillment of comprehensive public fiduciary responsibilities by the government. This reflects the external reporting function of government accounting. To achieve this, an appropriate accounting foundation and comprehensive accounting object-based government financial reporting system should be established, providing information on the management and use of entrusted resources to fulfill the government's public fiduciary responsibilities to society.
