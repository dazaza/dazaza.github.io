Rising pork prices attract big players: Guangzhou real estate developers invest 120 million yuan in pig farming

by anonymous on 2011-08-01 10:27:37

The state's positive support for the pig farming industry has attracted the attention of many bigwigs. In the summer of 2008, rumors spread that Goldman Sachs and Deutsche Bank had respectively become involved in China's pig farming sector. Around the time of the latest rise in pork prices, Sichuan New Hope and Beijing Shunxin Agriculture Co., Ltd. successively became involved, announcing investments in pig farming. COFCO Group's investment of 15 billion yuan to establish three large-scale pig farming bases in Tianjin, Jiangsu, and Hubei with a capacity of one million heads each has also been largely completed recently. In Guangzhou, Tiansheng Weikang, backed by real estate company Meilin Industry, invested 120 million yuan to build a large pig farm with leading management standards nationwide. Even Guangzhou, which is not a major pig farming city, has seen the rise of a large pig farm known for "high-tech" pig farming. After lying low for three years, the pigs raised by Tiansheng Weikang, backed by Meilin Industry, have now entered the market.

Tiansheng Weikang Food Co., Ltd. was invested and constructed by Chairman Liu Yuande with an investment of 120 million yuan. It is claimed that its pigsty construction adopts the fully enclosed modern pig farming model from the United States, with all pigsty walls, roofs, and pig farming equipment imported from the US. The company’s management is entirely Americanized, and even every batch of new feed must be tasted by the senior technical director Lee Hartmann before being fed to the pigs.

Lee Hartmann has an annual salary of over a million yuan. In addition to him, Liu Yuande has also hired six American experts at high salaries to provide technical support. When reporters wanted to take a look inside Liu Yuande's "luxury pigsty," it proved difficult due to quarantine requirements, allowing access only through surveillance videos to observe the activities within.

When investing in the pig farm in 2008, pork prices had already started to decline. Liu Yuande, who had previously been involved in the slaughterhouse industry, keenly realized that this was a rare good opportunity to enter the pig farming business. Regarding why he transitioned from the real estate industry into pig farming, Liu Yuande was unwilling to elaborate much but stated that the current real estate industry is under policy regulation, whereas pig farming is an industry encouraged by the government. He expressed optimism about industries related to the daily meals of ordinary people.

Currently, Tiansheng Weikang's pig farming base in Huadu is designed to produce 100,000 heads annually. The company also plans to build four pig farms of the same scale, reaching a total annual output of 500,000 heads. If calculated based on the current profit of 200 yuan per head, the annual profit could reach 100 million yuan.
