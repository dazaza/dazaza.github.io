"Official Crash" A Major Corruption Case Triggered by a Car Accident

by jqrxass on 2009-09-30 15:20:25

The first two anti-corruption novels by writer Gao He, "Director of the Reception Office" and "Bureau Chief", were respectively set against the background of Jinzhou City and Yinzhou City. This novel, "Official Car Accident", is set in Tongzhou City and is also the final piece of Gao He's political anti-corruption novels.

Public vehicle corruption is deeply detested by the common people. Bestselling writer Gao He, who excels at writing about the political arena, has this time directed his pen to this open yet mysterious field, creating his third political anti-corruption novel. An accident leads to a major corruption case, causing a stir in the political circles. The work not only reveals the truth behind public vehicle corruption but vividly portrays the original ecology of the political arena, shattering those pale and weak slogans, lashing out at the souls of certain power holders...

Part One of "Official Car Accident"

Mao Mao Yu dared not speak up, but he was indignant in his heart. Vice Mayor Wang's threatening and contemptuous look made him even more angry: "Damn it, they should all die in a crash. Fewer common people to support. They act so superior, riding in good cars, eating seafood, drinking Maotai, singing songs, hugging girls, all for free. What kind of people are they? Why should they have these privileges? They should all die in crashes..." Mao Mao Yu cursed in his heart, ignoring one practical issue: China lacks energy resources but never lacks officials. When one dies, there will always be someone to replace them.

Part Two of "Official Car Accident"

Sima Da chatted with Xiao Yan as they walked on the street looking for food. The more he talked, the more he liked this little girl. Maybe it was true what Xiao Yan said herself - poor children grow up fast. This child spoke like an adult, her chatter sounding like a lively sparrow, clearly showing she was a very smart and quick-witted child. Most precious of all, she had an air of grace and composure about her, none of the unconscious inferiority or restraint often seen in children from poor families. Sima Da wondered why she was like this and asked her: "Xiao Yan, don't get mad at uncle for asking you something."

Part Three of "Official Car Accident"

The chairman of the People's Congress used to be the party secretary of the city. Becoming the chairman of the People's Congress was laying the groundwork for complete retirement. His words were like farting with sand, not only smelly but also hurtful. He had always been dissatisfied with changing Fanjia Beach to "Three Smooth Beach". It wasn't that he thought there was anything wrong with the name "Three Smooth Beach", but rather that Hong Zhonghua was too autocratic. Changing place names should follow a complete procedure, at least needing the approval of the Standing Committee of the People's Congress, then filing with relevant provincial departments. But Hong Zhonghua changed a place name used for hundreds of years with just one word. So when he said "anyway, it's just a matter of one word", he was mocking Hong Zhonghua.

Part Four of "Official Car Accident"

His opinion immediately faced opposition. Municipal leaders and bureau-level cadres all refuted Hua San Ba. Some said that such reform would be equivalent to cutting off the legs of government agencies, making it impossible to do work in the future... Others said that it was proper and deserved for leaders to ride in public vehicles, there was nothing wrong with it, it was done everywhere in the country. Why should Tongzhou City deprive its leaders of their right to use public vehicles...

Part Five of "Official Car Accident"

Chelun was greatly shocked, as if sitting on a scorpion under his buttocks. He suddenly jumped up from the seat, hitting his head hard on the car roof after jumping. He didn't care about soothing his aching head, urgently asking: "What? What did you say? Say it again." When Chelun said these words, it seemed as if someone had sprinkled sand into his throat, dry, coarse, and itchy, almost unable to make a sound.

Part Six of "Official Car Accident"

The meeting room was silent, embarrassingly so, making everyone feel uneasy. Hong Zhonghua couldn't bear it anymore, and had to tactfully express his own views: "I personally think that the reform of public vehicles is not merely an economic issue of curbing the abuse of public vehicles and reducing expenses, but also a political issue involving rectifying public vehicle corruption and eliminating cadre privileges. Therefore, regarding the issue of public vehicle reform, we must be resolute in our attitude while being cautious. We need to view this issue from a political high ground. If we cut down on public vehicles here, and give money to the cadres there, and give so much, imagine if we were the general public, how would we feel..."
