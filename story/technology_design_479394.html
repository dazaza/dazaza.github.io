When adding water, it should be slowly poured along the wall of the tank, so that the inner wall around the hatching nest is slightly moist. Do not sprinkle water directly into the nest, nor should the humidity inside the nest be too high. After hatching, although the young centipedes temporarily gather in clusters, they can also move and seek food independently. At this time, the female will gradually leave the hatching nest to move independently. Because centipedes have the phenomenon of fighting for food and large eating small, the female body should be moved out in time or the young ones should be separated for breeding.

Agricultural Science and Technology Humin centipede breeding technology is first-class, and the indoor hatching tanks should be properly arranged in advance. Once the eggs are laid, do not move them easily. The trial personnel should observe carefully and gently, do not move the covering glass (tile) at will, and do not shine with a flashlight. During the hatching period, no feeding is required, but since it is midsummer at this time, if the humidity in the hatching nest is too low, it will affect the normal development of the embryo and the growth pattern of the individual. Under artificial feeding conditions, the body length of centipedes can reach the medicinal small strip standard (8 cm) within two years, and after two years, it will approach the large strip standard. Therefore, it can be inferred that for artificially bred centipedes, from the egg-laying of captured adult centipedes, hatching until providing medicine, it takes 2-3 years.

Processing and medicinal use technology of centipedes: Medicinal centipedes are dried whole bodies. After catching, they are scalded to death with boiling water, then fine bamboo skewers with both ends sharpened are inserted into the head and tail parts. With the elasticity of the bamboo piece, it is stretched straight and dried in the sun. After processing and preparation, the centipedes are flat and long, about 14-16 cm in length, and about 0.6-1.0 cm in width. The whole body has 22 segments, the last segment is small and called the tail; the head is reddish-brown, with one pair of tactile organs and venom hooks each; the back is dark brown and glossy, with two raised ridges; the abdomen is yellowish-brown and shriveled; each segment has one pair of legs, yellowish-red in color, bending backward, the last segment like a thorn. It smells fishy and has a special pungent odor, with a taste that is pungent and slightly salty. There are two bee venom-like toxic components in the centipede's body, namely histamine-like substances and hemolytic proteins; In addition, it also contains tyrosine, leucine, formic acid, fatty oil, cholesterol, etc. Medicinal centipedes have the functions of calming wind and relieving convulsions, reducing swelling and detoxifying. They are mainly used to treat infantile convulsions, tetanus, seizures, facial paralysis, lymph node tuberculosis, abscesses and ulcers, etc. In recent years, it has been found that centipedes have anti-tumor effects.
