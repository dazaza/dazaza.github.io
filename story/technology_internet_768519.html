Twitter's share price rebounded by 3.0% on the third trading day after its IPO.

by anonymous on 2013-11-17 19:06:30

Twitter has only been listed for three trading days, making it very difficult to determine the fair trading price of this popular social network. On its first day of trading, it closed at $44.90, a significant 73% increase over its issue price of $26. Following a 7% drop on the second trading day, Twitter's stock rose by $1.25, or 3.00%, closing at $42.90 on Monday, its third trading day. The stock started low but climbed throughout the day, briefly dropping below $40 with a low point of $39.40.

On Monday, U.S. investment firm Sterne Agee released an investor report giving the company's stock a "neutral" rating. However, unlike past reports, Sterne Agee did not provide a specific target price but instead set a target price range of $13 to $48 for Twitter.

Sterne Agee pointed out in their investor report that Twitter is a company with a "mobile-first" strategy. Considering that only 25% of its revenue comes from markets outside the United States while 77% of its users are non-U.S. users, Twitter’s international business presents enormous growth opportunities. The main growth strategy and the current risk facing investors is figuring out how to generate revenue from these international users.

Sterne Agee’s target price range of $13 to $48 depends on how quickly Twitter's advertising revenue grows—whether it reaches 44% or 84% in 2014—and whether data licensing revenue increases by 20% or 30%.

Sterne Agee also noted that based on the price-to-sales ratio (the ratio of the ordinary shares' market price per share to sales per share), Twitter's current stock price is 125% higher than its high-growth peers, meaning there isn't much room for further price increases. Analysts from Sterne Agee also stated that Twitter's growth rate may far exceed that of other social media companies; stocks like Facebook declined to varying degrees on Monday.

Some market analysts said that investors need to give Twitter a week of trading time to observe what kind of trend the company's stock will follow. Scott Redler, Chief Strategist of T3 Trading Group, said that Twitter's initial public offering largely kept everyone at bay. He mentioned that after opening at $45.10 on the first day, reaching an intraday high of $50, and closing at $44.90, everyone is now waiting. In Redler's view, although Twitter's stock price dropped to $39.40 on Monday, the company's stock could bottom out between $33 and $35, where investors should consider buying.
