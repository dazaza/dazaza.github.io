The most exciting and stimulating FPS games of 2011 that everyone was waiting for

by xunzuosz6 on 2011-03-22 15:04:06

By mageCore

FPS games have always been a type of game that many players are crazy about. Wielding weapons and fighting against NPC's relentless firepower in a virtual space is also a very manly thing to do... What? You say there's no courage involved since it's just a game? Oh, then try getting beaten up by an abnormal AI until you're crawling on the ground looking for your teeth. Do you still have the courage to keep playing and get humiliated by the computer?

Getting back to the point, today we're going to talk about FPS games. Below, I will take everyone to look at some FPS games that are worth our attention and anticipation in the remaining half of 2011.

Crysis 2

Development company: Crytek Studios

Publishing company: EA

Release date: March 22, 2011

The reason why "Crysis 2" is placed at the front is because this game was officially released today. "Crysis 2" is the official sequel to "Crysis". The game scene has shifted from a secret jungle to a bustling city, and the story takes place three years after the first game. Aliens have ignited wars in large areas on Earth, and major cities are under attack. Players need to defend their homes. From the videos, the game still features exquisite and detailed graphics, and a variety of weapons will make your gaming experience even more enjoyable.

Battlefield 3

Development company: EA DICE

Publish company: EA

Release date: Autumn 2011

"Battlefield 3" is a game worth paying close attention to. Its predecessor "Battlefield 2" received good reviews from players and is one of the few successful games under EA's banner. The game development team also recorded real sounds of guns, tanks, helicopters, and other equipment for "Battlefield 3". It's worth noting that the PC version of the game will support up to 64 players battling simultaneously, which is truly exciting.

Ghost Recon: Future Soldier

Development company: Ubisoft Paris

Publishing company: Ubisoft

Release date: Unknown (rumored to be October 4, 2011)

Following "Ghost Recon: Advanced Warfighter 2", the new Ghost Recon game will meet players this year. "Ghost Recon: Future Soldier" is set in the near future where a rebellion occurs within Russia. The Ghost Team is tasked with combating the aforementioned new extreme nationalists. Since the story happens in the future, the game also includes some sci-fi elements such as optical camouflage, allowing you to "become invisible." In short, in the game, you can experience the feeling of being "armed to the teeth."

Duke Nukem Forever

Development company: Gearbox

Publishing company: 2K Games

Release date: May 3, 2011

This game, which has been delayed for 13 years, is finally going to be released. Just based on this fact alone, even if the game is terrible, everyone should play it to see what this king of delays is like. Judging from the videos already released, the battles remain bloody and crude, the monsters are still ugly and dangerous, and naturally, there are eye-catching sexy elements like strippers.

Rage

Development company: id Software

Publishing company: Bethesda

Release date: Autumn 2011

We currently know very little about this game, but there are rumors that its capacity will be extremely large. It's said that the developers plan to fill up a Blu-ray disc - you know, the standard capacity of a Blu-ray disc is 50G. However, there's no need to worry. id Software has been destined not to be an ordinary game company since the day it was founded. The game style still follows id's old path, where the player's mission is to kill all sorts of monsters.

New Medal of Honor Sequel

Development company: EA

Publishing company: EA

Release date: Unknown

EA's FPS shooting game last year, "Medal of Honor," sold 5 million copies, which, although far behind Activision's "Call of Duty: Black Ops" sales of 14 million, still qualifies as a bestseller. EA has already clearly stated that they are developing the next generation of the game. To be honest, the game plays by the book, but we still have reasons to look forward to this revitalized classic series. It doesn't matter if the game isn't perfect, but it must improve with each generation.

Call of Duty: Modern Warfare 3

Development company: Infinity Ward

Publishing company: Activision Blizzard

Release date: Unknown

Despite no news about "Call of Duty: Modern Warfare 3" being released so far, this does not affect the anticipation of tens of millions of players worldwide for the new installment of the Modern Warfare series. The first two works pushed the Call of Duty brand to its peak, and players are already eagerly waiting. Based on Activision's tradition of releasing one Call of Duty game per year in recent years, "Call of Duty: Modern Warfare 3" is expected to be released on a certain date in the second half of this year.
