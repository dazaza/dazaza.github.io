So many times

by a20110325 on 2011-04-01 11:12:44

I started to organize the photos.

I began sorting through current and old photos. They were simple snapshots of daily life, low-resolution selfies, silly little images, or just random silhouettes. Many of the newly taken photos needed to be printed for the elders, while many of the older ones were just for browsing, so familiar.

Suddenly, my face was wet with tears. "I want you to sit by my side again." There's only one photo of Auntie. In that picture, you sat next to me, my hand resting on your shoulder, and I had a big, foolish smile on my face. At first, your expression was also distorted by laughter, so later you tried to look more dignified. How great it was! You could laugh as exaggeratedly as you wanted, remain dignified when you didn't laugh, discuss little niece’s studies with me every time, cook delicious food every time I visited, yell at me for being lazy, tell me I've gained weight recently, ask if I need warm cotton shoes; complain about having more wrinkles on your face, ask me where to buy skincare products online, and even ask with frustration whether the red curly hair you just permed looks ugly... Actually, you used to be very beautiful, just a bit overweight, and your expressions were always too exaggerated. But back then, you were already beautiful enough. If only you could sit next to me and laugh like that again. I never thought I might not take any more beautiful pictures of you.

Every time I wanted to hug you, you hugged me first. This time, let me hug you first. I don’t remember from when, but every time I came home, you would come over to give me a hug, then kiss me and say, "Oh, I missed you so much!" Ha ha, this scene is quite funny —— the way a woman in her sixties expresses her longing and joy. Gradually, I got used to such enthusiastic hugs. You always remembered to call me on my birthday to wish me happiness, even though you're a woman in her sixties. I still remember how excited you were when you received the carnations I gave you; Mom said you kept them for a long time after taking them home. This time, I finally got the chance to hug you first, while you were paying attention to the camera. Ha ha, my expression was perfect, weren’t you a little surprised? That expression, ha ha.

I wish I could kiss you whenever you need me. I laughed uncontrollably when I kissed you, ha ha, but you were laughing happily. Every time you suggested we should kiss, I would disdainfully say, "Ew, your face is full of mites..." Then you would start scolding me, saying that raising you to this size was all for nothing since you started to despise me so quickly. And then, without thinking, you suddenly kissed me, alas, it was out of sheer helplessness... I know you only do this when you're happy, but when you're sad, I never remember doing it. I think I need more practice, otherwise, when you really need me next time, I'll laugh again, which wouldn't be good.

Yesterday, Zhong Hang came, and we skipped classes all afternoon, which passed quickly. It was still a mess of people and things, scattered here and there, and suddenly I missed them so much. Suddenly, I became very reluctant to part with a group of people. I wish I could see you every time I feel sad. When we parted ways, we wandered all the way to Yamao before he got on the bus. I can't even remember what topics I rambled about. Everyone was lost in their own confusion and busy with their own affairs. Then I walked back alone.

So many times I feel like being myself. To love who I love, care for who I care, and hug who I miss.

If only all of you could stay with me forever, how wonderful it would be.
