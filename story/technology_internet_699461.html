Villas can also be designed and decorated by oneself.

A villa renovation [http://www.winallchina.com] is a huge project, and the cost is naturally not small. For salaried workers, it represents a significant expense. Is there a way to save money while still being satisfied? Yes, and that is to design and decorate it yourself! Most people might not agree at first, but after hearing the following story, you might change your mind.

This is a case that the Soufun reporter interviewed in Hangzhou in 2006 [http://www.huashannaotan.com]. It was a villa that had not undergone any professional design, yet the moment you walked in, the feeling was simple: beautiful. The original area of this two-story villa was over 330 square meters, but if you include the later-added attic, the estimated total area is nearly 400 square meters. A renovation budget of 500,000 yuan included everything: central air conditioning system, boiler, stair renovation, large furniture, etc., making it very reasonable [http://www.163huishou.com].

The mistress of the house handled the renovation matters. When she first received the keys, like many others, she didn't know where to start. She tried contacting renovation companies, but their designs really shocked her. Their pursuit of highlights was not the plain style they wanted. In the end, she decided to do it herself. After working hard for half a year [http://www.yzteb.com], she finally achieved satisfactory results.

Even with a tight budget, treasures can still be found:

The tone of the living room upon entering is very simple, yet striking. Extremely light cream serves as the main wall color, so faint it's almost invisible, but it’s definitely present. The curtains are made of light beige and brown checkered fabric, which determines the style of the living room. After seeing it, one suddenly feels the comfort of tea and coffee together. The sofa also uses these two colors in checkered fabric, while the coffee table in the center is made of deep brown solid wood, square and modern in shape, yet paired with extremely classical details, such as the exquisite small rings on the drawers, reminiscent of a grandmother's dressing table from decades ago.

For this set of furniture, the mistress of the house was extremely proud. She discovered it by chance, just when someone was offering a discount. Hearing the heart-stirring price, she immediately brought them home without hesitation. In fact, this set of furniture is very consistent with the tone of the house, proving that good deals can indeed be found if you have good luck. Moving further inside, the dining room set is much more fashionable, but surprisingly, it is also a combination of deep brown and light yellow.

Simplified villa life:

Originally, the staircase leading from the first floor to the second floor [http://www.winallchina.com] was directly connected to the entrance of the living room on the first floor. However, to add a bit of winding charm, the owners cut off the staircase, adding a bend halfway through and erecting a wall. Thus, the living room was completely separated from the stairs and kitchen behind, forming two distinct spaces. Like all other details, the staircase naturally used brown flooring, with very simple handrails—no fancy ironwork, just straightforward balustrades. On the second floor, the study and bedroom walls changed color, becoming an extremely pale pink, naturally completing the visual transition and emphasizing different functions without being too abrupt. Although everything looked simple, for comfort, radiators were installed in the bathroom of the master bedroom, simple yet effective.

The walls of the study turned into a very pale pink, naturally completing the visual transition.

Renovation insights:

The mistress of the house is very capable, and being middle-aged, she considers issues thoroughly. With the longer renovation schedule, there weren’t too many regrets left. She particularly reminded those renovating villas to install heat insulation devices on the roof; otherwise, Hangzhou summers would be unbearable. Even with high-power air conditioning, it wouldn't achieve good results and would waste energy. Additionally, she advised those preparing to renovate: not everything that's popular outside suits your home, and villas don’t necessarily have to be renovated to look luxurious. Ultimately, everything at home should serve oneself, so one must consider their age, occupation, and interests. Otherwise, the home won't truly feel like one's own. (Compiled by search)
