If there were only China in the world, the present age would be the happiest era for Chinese people.

by hanhai1314 on 2007-05-08 14:27:53

From a historical comparative perspective, in today's China, although the happiness index of the people is falling straightly, if we measure with tangible contents, in the past 30 years, despite some very unfortunate major events that occurred during this period, it can still be said to be the greatest and happiest era in the history of China. Nowadays, China still retains many obvious traces of the old era, especially the political system of China is still a replica and "clone" of the political systems of the past two thousand years, and Chinese culture has not yet broken into a new realm. However, the Chinese people have enjoyed the most peaceful and tranquil period of time in the past 160 years.

In my view, the greatest paradox of China's social progress lies in the fact that compared with Western civilization, the progress of the country is a deformed one: the Chinese have sacrificed the cohesive faith of the nation, lost sublime and grand ideals, lost universally followed morals, and lost originally profound thoughts, in exchange for great progress in the material level and certain institutional levels of the country. What does this deformed social progress mean for the future of this great nation? Is it a disaster or a blessing? This question, I believe, is not only troubling me, a Chinese person with relatively low education and intelligence. Some high-quality, high-IQ Chinese will certainly think about this question repeatedly when lying on the mattress beds in five-star hotels in Beijing and Shanghai at night, and decide whether to stay or leave China in the future.

As long as we can look at problems realistically, almost all the signs of progress in China in the past 30 years have been cloned or directly copied from Western civilization. From the skyscrapers on both sides of the Huangpu River, to the CBD business activity center next to the Forbidden City, from the stock market in Shenzhen, to the aerospace center in Xichang, from CCTV's entertainment programs, to the routine press conferences of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, all these modern artifacts, systems, and human survival activities are without exception derived from the achievements of Western civilization being consciously or unconsciously copied and plagiarized by the Chinese. In its more than 56-year history since the founding of the People's Republic, China has never stopped shouting "resist complete westernization", "prevent peaceful evolution", "resist capitalist corruption", but precisely in such a "socialist" country, "westernization" and "evolution" have been so thorough: nowadays, I won't talk about what WTO is, because to explain it clearly, a monograph is needed. I will only say from the degree of acceptance of Western people and things by the public that I absolutely dare to conclude that among the vast majority of educated citizens, there has truly formed a social fashion of taking pride in using Western fashionable commodities, marrying Westerners, obtaining residency rights in Western countries, and speaking Western languages. The Chinese have never embraced Western civilization in such an all-encompassing way. For all of this, I am confident that no one can make an explanation that convinces everyone. I can only think that this is a人间tragedy-comedy arranged by the playful old man of history for all Chinese.

Nowadays, when someone in China attempts to revive Confucianism, restore Han clothing, and revitalize so-called Chinese civilization, all are mocked, ridiculed, and even "parodied" into a farce. The harsh reality is that so-called "Chinese civilization", except for the continued admiration of the long-standing common-sense life wisdom of the Chinese nation, has been completely suppressed by Western civilization and is barely breathing, only able to emit a few desperate moans.
