With the enhancement of people's economic ability, the speed of airplanes is unmatched by trains and cars. Although among various modes of transportation, aviation is the safest with a low probability of aircraft accidents, understanding some safety常识 about flying can also be considered a wise choice.

American aircraft safety experts have proposed: [Ten Flight Safety Rules]:

One, you should [choose direct flights]. Statistics indicate that most air disasters occur during takeoff, descent, climb or taxiing on the runway. Reducing layovers also reduces the chances of encountering flight accidents.

Two, you should choose planes with at least 30 seats. The larger the aircraft, the more international safety inspection standards it undergoes, and in the event of an air disaster, the survival rate of passengers on large aircraft is relatively higher than on smaller ones.

Three, remember the safety instructions before takeoff. The location of escape doors varies among different aircraft models. After boarding, passengers should spend a few minutes carefully listening to the safety instructions provided by the cabin crew so that they won't be at a loss in case of an emergency.

Four, recently many passengers like to bring large pieces of luggage onboard, which is not in line with flight safety practices. If the plane encounters turbulence or an emergency, the overhead compartments often cannot withstand heavy objects, and many passengers are injured or even killed by falling luggage.

Five, always keep your seatbelt fastened. In the event of the plane flipping over or encountering turbulence, keeping your seatbelt fastened provides an additional layer of protection for passengers, preventing them from colliding around the cabin.

Six, in the event of an accident, you must follow the instructions of the cabin crew. After all, the primary task of the cabin crew on board is to maintain safety.

Seven, do not carry hazardous materials onto the plane.

Eight, high-temperature beverages such as coffee and hot tea should be served to passengers by cabin crew who have received professional training. Passengers handling these high-temperature liquids themselves often lead to scalding accidents.

Nine, do not drink too much alcohol on the plane. Since the cabin pressure is different from that on the ground, excessive alcohol will slow down passengers' response capabilities in emergencies, losing valuable opportunities for escape.

Ten, always stay alert. Aviation safety experts point out that in the event of an accident, passengers on board should remain calm and quickly leave according to the instructions of the cabin crew.
