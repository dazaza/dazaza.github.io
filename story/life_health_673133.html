■ When you have acne, don't squeeze or pick at it with your hands or other tools. This will make the inflammation and pus of the skin tissue more serious and increase the chances of leaving scars.

What to do if you have acne? Anti-acne theory guides acne maintenance

Acne marks are the spots left after acne fades. Because of acne, the skin tissue becomes inflamed, which leads to capillary dilation. After the acne fades, the dilated capillaries do not immediately return to their original state, but rather have a certain degree of retention, so red spots appear. In addition, in environments where temperatures fluctuate between cold and hot, as well as under conditions of tension and excitement, the color will deepen, and the inflammation of the skin tissue will also cause pigmentation, resulting in dark brown spots.

Acne marks are temporary scars that will gradually fade and disappear. If used in conjunction with topical skincare products or ointments, it can help speed up the fading of acne marks. Generally, it takes several months for acne marks to disappear, and longer ones may take over half a year. If you're in a hurry, you can undergo medical treatments such as laser therapy, vitamin C infusion, and fruit acid peeling to constrict capillaries and break down pigmentation, helping you restore clear and radiant skin faster.

What to do if you have acne? Smoothing out acne marks and pits

For common acne marks, acne pits are even more annoying. They belong to true scars and have already involved the epidermis, dermis, and deep subcutaneous tissues. Regular skincare products are generally unable to solve them, and they won't fade on their own. Special medical methods are needed to eliminate them. After acne fades, hypertrophic scars may form, usually appearing as red protrusions. The main reason is the hyperactivity of fibroblasts in the dermis of the skin, undergoing inflammation and healing processes in the skin tissue, reacting strongly during these processes, and eventually forming thickened skin tissue growths. Another opposite situation is that skin inflammation and collagen tissue can cause the dermis to collapse, forming depressions on the skin's surface. This situation has a high incidence rate.
