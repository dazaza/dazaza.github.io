Wind power coating industry lacks standards and has difficulty in adapting to market development (1)

by djegskkgfeb6 on 2012-02-11 09:57:21

The absence of industry standards for wind power coatings makes it difficult to adapt to market development – Market Observation - Industry News - Building Materials Network. On our planet, wind energy resources are very abundant with enormous development potential. Globally, no fewer than 70 countries are utilizing wind energy, and wind power generation is the primary form of wind energy utilization. Under the backdrop of national policy support and energy supply tensions, China's wind power equipment manufacturing industry has rapidly risen, becoming the most active venue for global wind power investment. On November 9, a large wind turbine generator with a tower height of 87 meters and a rotor diameter reaching 85 meters was successfully installed in Longtou Port, Yangying Town, Lai'an County. This is Anhui Province's first wind power equipment. China's wind power industry has extended from regions such as Northwest and coastal areas to inland provinces and cities. In recent years, China's installed wind power capacity has continued to grow rapidly. The large-scale development of wind power has strongly driven the vigorous development of related equipment markets, also promoting the rapid development of wind power coatings.

As an emerging industry in China, the wind power equipment manufacturing industry initially relied mainly on the introduction and absorption of foreign technology, and the accompanying coating systems were once monopolized by imported products. However, with the explosive growth of the wind power industry in recent years, the localization of accompanying coatings has become an inevitable trend. Due to the vast geographical area where China’s wind power plants are located, the wind turbine system will encounter various harsh environmental erosions, such as sandstorms, ultraviolet radiation, temperature differences, rain and snow, icing, especially offshore wind power which is subject to long-term erosion from water vapor, salt fog, and sea waves' impact, causing corrosion problems. Therefore, to ensure the normal service life of 20 years for wind power equipment, relevant protective measures must be taken, and coating protection is an important part of this process. Wind power protective coatings mainly include tower anti-corrosion coatings and blade protective coatings, while tower bases, hubs, bearings, nacelle covers, fairings, and other electrical equipment also require paint protection.

By the end of 2009, the cumulative installed wind power capacity worldwide had exceeded 150 million kilowatts, and China's cumulative installed wind power capacity had broken through 25 million kilowatts. China's installed wind power capacity has grown rapidly, with annual new installed capacity growth rates exceeding 100% for six consecutive years, making it the country with the fastest-growing wind power industry. In the domestic wind power market, only the demand for tower and blade coatings is expected to reach approximately 7 billion yuan over the next 10 years, currently almost entirely monopolized by foreign brands. Taking Gansu as an example, according to the province's 20 million kilowatt wind power installation plan, the estimated demand for coatings is about 1 billion yuan, indicating the great potential of the wind power coating market. Accelerating the research and development and application of domestic wind power coatings not only relates to the steady development of the wind power industry but will undoubtedly lay the foundation for domestic-related enterprises to secure a place in this industry. Accompanying the development of China's wind power industry from nothing to something substantial, the scale of its matching wind power protective coating market has also formed significantly. Within the next 10 years, China's wind power protective coatings will enter a period of rapid development, with an average annual market demand reaching more than 10,000 tons. Relevant departments have already begun to formulate relevant national standards.

Especially with the vigorous development of China's wind power industry, the complexity of domestic wind farm environments makes the enhancement of wind power equipment corrosion resistance technology an urgent task. Fluorocarbon coatings have significant potential in future wind power markets. Introducing fluorocarbon coatings into the wind power corrosion protection market can greatly improve the weatherability and corrosion resistance performance of wind power equipment, extend the equipment protection period, reduce maintenance costs. Compared to the polyurethane system coatings currently used in wind power facilities, fluorocarbon system coatings will undoubtedly become upgraded products, playing a very positive role in promoting the updating and replacement of China's wind power coatings. Zhongyuan Guanxi Coatings was the first to launch a fluorocarbon corrosion protection solution specifically designed for wind turbine towers, also being the first fluorocarbon coating product specifically designed for wind power corrosion needs within the industry. Meanwhile, Zhongyuan Guanxi has reached a cooperation intention with Daikin Fluorocoatings, a giant in fluorocarbon resin materials, to jointly develop fluorocarbon coating products targeting wind power facilities. Additionally, large-scale wind farms are under construction in Northwest China, where strong winds and abnormal climate changes lead to the unsuitability of foreign wind power coatings. For this reason, since 2000, Northwest Yongxin has been conducting research and development work on wind power coatings suitable for China's climatic environment. A relatively complete product system has now been formed.

Currently, all of China's wind power equipment technology comes from foreign imports or international cooperation, with weak independent development capabilities, still having a considerable gap compared to foreign counterparts. However, wind power protective coatings are mainly produced domestically, including traditional foreign brands as well as products from many domestic emerging coating enterprises. But because the wind power equipment is still rapidly developing towards larger sizes, this poses new challenges to coating products. Currently, all coatings used in operating wind power facilities are either imported or foreign brands, all within their design periods, but their actual operational effects are not ideal, especially in the cold-dry-sandy areas of North China, Northeast China, and Northwest China. Due to different corrosion environments inside and outside the tower, there is insufficient performance of the outer wall coating and excessive performance of the inner wall coating, resulting in double waste; serious lag in research and development of coating protection, severe wind erosion damage to blades; high coating costs that do not match actual performance; and existing coatings are mostly solvent-based, causing serious pollution, which contradicts the environmental attributes of wind energy. As a result, the high cost of wind power manufacturing and maintenance seriously affects the development of the wind power industry and wind power equipment manufacturing industry. At the same time, China's wind power industry still has some "minor issues," such as weak independent innovation capabilities, excessive dependence on foreign technology; lack of a complete testing and certification system, etc., all of which restrict the development of domestic brand wind power coatings. The Chinese coatings industry urgently needs to establish corresponding industry standards or national standards. On one hand, this allows coating enterprises to have a clear technical reference when researching and developing related products; on the other hand, it helps regulate and guide the market to provide better and longer-term protection for wind power equipment. Breaking the monopoly of foreign products, promoting the localization process of wind power facility accompanying coatings in China, accelerating the development pace of China's wind power coatings industry, and promoting the development of China's national coatings industry has milestone significance.
