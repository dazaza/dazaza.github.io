Corporate Gift Culture

Corporate gifts are specially customized products with the corporate logo and a certain special meaning, used by enterprises and institutions in their business activities to enhance or expand their visibility, increase market share for their products, and achieve better sales performance and profits. These gifts have characteristics of novelty, uniqueness, craftsmanship, and practicality.

To discuss corporate gift culture, one must first understand the features of the products used as gifts and then conduct case studies based on the characteristics of the enterprise. Here, we mainly explore the general principles that companies should follow and the features that corporate gifts should possess.

When a company gives gifts, it should first clarify the purpose of giving. Friends give gifts to deepen friendship, husbands give gifts to wives to elevate love, and parents give gifts to children to enhance family ties. The purpose of corporate gifting is much clearer - expanding operations and increasing profits. Therefore, the purpose of gifting determines the level and timing of the gift.

Having a clear purpose naturally leads to identifying the recipient. Corporate gifting often targets an institution or group. However, as Beijing Gift Network kindly reminds, the recipients of gifts are living individuals with their own personalities, status, and tastes. Thus, the gifts given should also have different personalities.

The timing of gifting is very important. This timing not only includes the specific time of presenting the gift but also the regular intervals needed to maintain business relationships. Companies generally pay attention to holidays, anniversaries, events, and other appropriate times for giving commercial gifts, but they don't always focus on the intervals between gifting occasions. For key clients, simply giving gifts at the same reason and time each year may not be enough. Companies need to further analyze clients, gifts, and the environment to determine the right timing for gifting.

These three aspects are closely linked because corporate business gifting is a comprehensive activity. It needs to align with the overall business diplomacy of the company while meeting relevant requirements including customs, business etiquette, psychology, and sociology.

The corporate gifts from Beijing Gift Network differ from personal gifts; they must first be representatives of the corporate image. Companies in different industries have different corporate images. Some emphasize innovation, while others aim to demonstrate substantial strength. However, there is a common point: if the corporate gift is not intended for promoting products or services, it must reflect the cultural heritage of the company.

Secondly, corporate gifts often have the characteristic of being something that most people like but are reluctant to spend their own money on. If a company's gift can be easily purchased by the recipient, it would be difficult to show the company's unique concern for the recipient. This is especially important when dealing with key clients in public relations. In terms of gift selection, companies should not casually buy everyday gifts but should seek professional gift design companies to tailor-make gifts according to the characteristics of the company and its clients.

The biggest difference between corporate gifts and personal gifts lies in their consistency, wholeness, and planning. For important clients, relationship groups, and partners, the gifts given by the company should reflect both wholeness and targeted individuality. At this point, the concept of gifts goes beyond mere objects, requiring a deeper understanding of the company's own services and culture as well as a thorough understanding of the recipients. Thus, corporate gifts truly enter the realm of culture, reaching the highest level.

The key to operating corporate gifts lies in people, who have cultural affiliations and their own personalities. Only by elevating gift-giving activities to the level of culture and adopting commercial operational methods can a company gain an extra edge in China's business environment.
