[Symptom] The microwave does not heat, other components function normally. Beijing Samsung Microwave Oven After-sales Service Phone Number.

[Cause] High-voltage fuse has blown, high-voltage diode is leaking current.

[Analysis and Maintenance] The cause of the fault lies in the microwave system and power supply control circuit. Open the casing of the microwave oven, discharge the high-voltage capacitor, then set the multimeter to the R1kΩ range and inspect the various components of the microwave system that have a higher likelihood of failure, such as the high-voltage fuse, high-voltage transformer, high-voltage capacitor, high-voltage diode, and magnetron. It was found that the high-voltage fuse had blown. Based on experience, the blowing of the high-voltage fuse is generally caused by excessive current flow, so it is necessary to further investigate the reason for this issue in the unit. Set the multimeter to the R10kΩ range and retest the high-voltage capacitor and high-voltage diode. It was discovered that the high-voltage diode has resistance in both forward and reverse directions. Normally, the forward resistance should be around 150kΩ, while the reverse resistance should be infinite. Therefore, it can be concluded that the high-voltage diode is leaking current. Replacing the high-voltage diode and fuse resolves the fault.

[Warm Reminder] The high-voltage diode in the microwave oven, which is part of the high-voltage rectifier assembly (can be understood as several ordinary diodes connected in series), has a relatively large forward resistance and conduction voltage. The battery of the measuring instrument must provide at least 6V or more voltage. Thus, when testing the high-voltage diode, the multimeter must be set to the highest resistance range. For example, in a 500-type multimeter, the R10kΩ range is powered by a 9V battery, while other resistance ranges are powered by a 1.5V battery.
