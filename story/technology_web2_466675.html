Man drives under the influence and crashes into four cars, with alcohol test results reaching 460.

by zxyasdi20 on 2011-07-27 14:15:39

Beijing Morning Post (Chief Reporter Wang Bin) - A 21-year-old young man, upset and heavily intoxicated, drove a Jinbei van out of his home, causing an accident involving four vehicles. When the traffic police conducted an alcohol test on him, he set a record by blowing a blood alcohol content of 460 milligrams per 100 milliliters. The young man admitted that he couldn't remember how much alcohol he had consumed.

"Damn it!" On the evening of September 3rd, near the Qinghe Shida residential area, a female owner of a Bora car shouted angrily at a young man sitting inside a police car. Meanwhile, the traffic police were questioning other affected vehicle owners nearby.

According to the accounts from the vehicle owners, the young man was driving a Jinbei van when he scratched two cars parked in their designated spots within the residential area, one of which was the female owner's Bora car. However, instead of stopping, after leaving the residential area, the Jinbei van rear-ended a taxi waiting for a red light. The taxi driver noticed the young man was drunk, but the young man insisted on resolving the matter privately. While attempting to park his vehicle on the side of the road, he collided with another car behind him.

When the traffic police asked the young man for the vehicle registration, he claimed it was already in the hands of the police, but all the police had was his driver's license. In local slang, the young man had "blacked out" from drinking too much. When questioned by the police if he was drunk, the young man, swaying unsteadily with a smile on his face, denied it. However, the results from the breathalyzer shocked everyone present.

The young man took a deep breath and blew into the device. The reading started from zero and instantly surpassed the legal limit of 20 milligrams per 100 milliliters for driving under the influence, quickly rising to 100, indicating drunk driving. But it didn't stop there; amidst the astonished exclamations of the onlookers, the number continued climbing past 200, 300, and finally reached an astonishing 460. "Wow, his blood is practically alcohol!" The data showed that the young man's blood alcohol content was 460 milligrams per 100 milliliters, unheard of.

As the traffic police were handling the situation, the young man's mother arrived at the scene. She told the police she wasn't aware her son had been drinking, only knowing that he had been feeling down recently. However, the young man had lost his composure, first crying uncontrollably in the police car and shouting not to let his mother interfere in his affairs, saying, "I will definitely take care of you." Then he suddenly pushed his mother to the ground. Just then, his sister who had rushed to the scene upon seeing her brother showing no signs of remorse, slapped him three times, immediately subduing him.

"How much alcohol did he drink?" The onlookers were amazed by the slim and small statured young man's apparent incredible alcohol tolerance. The traffic police also questioned the young man about his alcohol consumption, but he couldn't recall. After dealing with the situation on-site, the police conducted a blood test on the young man, revealing his blood alcohol content had decreased to less than 300 milligrams per 100 milliliters. The traffic police stated that individual responses to alcohol vary, preliminarily estimating that the young man might have consumed over one liter of strong liquor.

The Traffic Brigade of Haidian District ultimately determined that the young man was not only guilty of drunk driving but also hit-and-run. He was sentenced to 20 days of detention, fined 3600 yuan, received 12 demerit points, and had his driver's license suspended for six months.
