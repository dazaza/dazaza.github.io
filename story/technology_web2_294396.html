Guoxin Securities: Yunnan Copper's short-term own-production copper output remains stable＿stock

by fj0953210 on 2009-12-05 21:51:06

Comment: Currently, there are no plans to expand existing mines. The copper metal production in 2009 is expected to be about 60,000 tons. The company currently owns four major mining enterprises: Yuxi Mining (100%), Chuxiong Mine Metallurgy (100%), Diqing Mining (75%), and Jinsha Company (51%). The production of the four mining companies is 29,000 tons, 13,000 tons, 7,000 tons, and 11,000 tons respectively. Among them, Dahongshan Mining, the company's largest mine at present, has a production of 15,000 tons.

It is estimated that the total mineral copper output of the company in 2009 will be 60,000 tons. Since the company currently has no mineral renovation and expansion projects, the production of the existing mines will remain unchanged in 2010 and 2011.

Resource Expansion - Proposed asset injection is expected to be completed in the first half of next year, with the prospect of injecting the Pulang Mine still promising. The current reserve of the company's mines is 1.5 million tons. The company is conducting in-depth exploration work on the existing mines to ensure that the annual consumption reserves and new reserves basically remain balanced. The Dahongshan Mine area added 370,000 tons of reserves this year and is currently in the process of obtaining mining rights.

Yunnan Province's mine succession resource capability is beginning to strengthen, initial results have been achieved in old district mineral exploration, realizing dynamic resource balance. From 2002 to 2005, the investment intensity of Yunnan Copper Industry (Group) Co., Ltd. has significantly increased, strengthening geological mineral exploration to extend the life of the mines. Six old mines of Yunnan Copper Industry (Group) Co., Ltd. invested in geological prospecting engineering: adit was 50,658 meters, drilling was 25,680 meters; geological prospecting funds amounted to 86.95 million yuan, equivalent to 124.2% of the total from 1996 to 2001, averaging nearly 22 million yuan per year, accounting for approximately 15% of Yunnan Province’s recent geological survey investment. Over four years, a total of 481.77 million tons of copper resources were discovered with a copper grade of 1.06%, equivalent to adding a large copper deposit. Among these, 311.66 million tons of near-term exploitable reserves were found. As a result, the service life of old district mines was extended by three years, achieving a basic balance between newly added reserves and current production consumption (current consumption was 34.16 million tons), reaching the goal of "low risk, high output" for mineral exploration in old districts, achieving phased results. In addition, with the support of local governments in Yunnan Province, a number of medium to large-scale copper-silver polymetallic deposits have been discovered and evaluated in the Southwest Three Rivers region, including Zhongdian Pulang, Deqin Yangla, Simao Dapingzhang, and Lanping Baiyangping, which are expected to become large non-ferrous metal resource bases. Among them, the Zhongdian Pulang copper mine has a projected reserve of 2 million tons under project control, with a forecasted total copper resource volume in the entire area reaching a super-large scale of over 5 million tons. The initial mining area has a copper reserve of 720,000 tons, and a project producing 50,000 tons of copper concentrate annually is nearing completion, eventually reaching an annual production scale of more than 150,000 tons of copper concentrate.

The main points of future resource growth for the company are: 1. The mining assets planned for injection in the 2009 additional issuance project: Chuxiong Siyuan (100%) (Chuxiong Siyuan completed a total of 3,648 tons of copper metal extraction in 2008, of which 3,350 tons were self-produced); Yunnan Daya (100%) (Yunnan Daya produced a total of 8,140 tons of copper content in copper concentrate in 2008. Among these: Shifengshan copper mine completed 4,000 tons, Lion Mountain copper mine completed 3,700 tons; Dali Hongzhizhu Company completed 740 tons.); Xingyan Company (100%) (Xingyan Company's main product (copper content in concentrate) production in 2008 was 2,284 tons); Jinggu Mining Metallurgy (74.78%) (production 800 tons). The total copper production from mines is 14,574 tons, with the injection expected to be completed in the first half of 2010; 2. Pulang Copper Mine (reserve 3 million tons) is currently undergoing environmental assessment, with the main difficulty being its location within a nature reserve, so the progress of the environmental assessment is slower, but the possibility of eventual injection into the listed company is very high. Additionally, another major copper mine of the group - Lala Copper Mine, it is understood that due to equity issues, the short-term injection expectation is relatively low.

External Purchasing Smelting - Basically Break-even, Smelting Capacity Remains Stable

One-third of the company's copper raw materials come from self-produced copper mines, one-third from domestic procurement, and one-third from foreign imports. The smelting cost of the company is basically around $50-60 per ton. Due to the rise in copper prices, the difference between the company's raw material procurement and actual sales offsets the current decline in processing fees, allowing the company to maintain break-even despite the drop in spot processing fees.

The company's significant advantage is advanced smelting technology and low smelting costs, having mastered the Isa furnace smelting process. Compared to flash furnaces used by Jiangxi Copper and Tongling, the Isa furnace has obvious advantages: simple structure and process, no need for deep drying of feedstock moisture, lower requirements for feed particle size, less heat loss, lower dust rate, less repeat processing required, higher efficiency, etc.; the main disadvantage is shorter refractory lining life. Due to the advantages of the Isa furnace process, the company's smelting costs have significantly decreased in recent years, with copper smelting costs around 2,000 yuan per ton, lower than those of Jiangxi Copper and Tongling Copper peers.

Currently, the company's smelting capacity: coarse copper is 200,000 tons (Yimen Copper Industry 50,000 tons; Chuxiong Dianzhong 100,000 tons (actual production 50,000 tons); Dongchuan 50,000 tons (actual production 30,000 tons)); electrolytic copper 650,000 tons (main plant 550,000 tons, Chifeng 100,000 tons). Chuxiong Dianzhong is currently undergoing a 100,000-ton/year coarse copper technical renovation project, changing from a 50,000-ton blast furnace technology to a 100,000-ton ISA furnace smelting. It is estimated that the total cathode copper production of the company in 2009 will be 300,000 tons, mainly due to excessive underproduction in the first half of the year. The possibility of expansion in smelting capacity in the future is small.

By-products - An Important Highlight of Company Profitability

The company's by-products include gold, silver, sulfuric acid, etc. It is estimated that this year's gold production will be 7 tons, silver 300 tons, and sulfuric acid 750,000 tons. The 2009 interim report shows that the gross margin of gold is about 19.49%, silver 10.55%, and sulfuric acid 31.83%, still one of the main sources of company profits.

Mining Costs - Higher Underground Mining Costs

Since all of the company's copper mines are currently underground operations, the cost of mined copper is relatively high. Among them, Yuxi Mining's mining cost is approximately 25,000-27,000 yuan/ton (including tax, including three fees); Diquing Mining has the highest cost due to smaller production scale, approximately 30,000 yuan/ton. We estimate that the company's average full cost of mined copper is about 27,000-28,000 yuan/ton.

Development Plan - Resources Are King

The company's future development focuses on resources as king. There is also the possibility that China Aluminum Corporation plans to integrate Yun Tong with other copper industry assets into a package and relist them.

Yunnan Province is rich in copper resources, but lacks large and super-large mines, and does not have open-pit mining conditions. Some mines are located in remote mountainous areas, making development difficult; some state-owned mines are facing the problem of copper resource depletion. A total of 217 copper deposits (points) have been explored, among which 156 deposits have proven reserves, distributed across 14 prefectures (cities) and 38 counties in the province, with proven reserves of 958.188 million tons, and retained reserves of 849.148 million tons as of 1994, accounting for 13.59% of the national copper retained reserves of 625.1188 million tons, ranking third in the country after Jiangxi and Tibet; large and super-large copper mines with reserves over 1 million tons include Tangdan in Dongchuan, Dahongshan in Xinping, Yangla in Deqin, and Minle in Jinggu, 24 medium-sized copper mines, and 127 small copper mines, retaining reserves accounting for 5.96%, 44.22%, and 19.82% of total retained reserves respectively. 77.3% of the reserves are concentrated in central Yunnan, southeastern Yunnan, and western Yunnan, accounting for 12.5% and 7.6% respectively, while others scattered elsewhere account for 2.6%. The retained reserves of copper mines currently in production and construction in Yunnan Province account for 57.9% of the province, those yet to be developed and utilized account for 36.1%, and those temporarily difficult to develop and utilize account for 6.1%.

Currently, the Yun Tong Group has reserves of about 5 million tons, except for the proven reserves of the Pulang copper mine of 3 million tons (not including prospective reserves), the company's other mine resources are relatively scattered, involving approximately 80 mining rights, making integration challenging. Even so, the growth rate of the company's copper reserves in the future is relatively optimistic.

In addition, after China Aluminum acquired Yun Tong, on one hand, it strengthens management of the company: the company's management team and management thinking are currently dominated by China Aluminum, following the China Aluminum model, which represents a significant improvement compared to past extensive management, helping further reduce the company's costs; on the other hand, China Aluminum attempts to use Yun Tong as a platform for developing its copper industry, building a complete copper industrial chain. Currently, China Aluminum has acquired several large domestic copper material processors and spent approximately $8.6 billion acquiring 91% of the shares of Canadian Peru Copper Corporation. Peru Copper Corporation is a Canadian company listed in Canada, the United States, and Peru. Through an option agreement signed with a Peruvian state-owned company, it holds the development option for the Toromocho copper mine in Peru. The copper mine has approximately 12 million tons of copper-equivalent metal resources and is one of the world's proposed super-large copper mines to be developed. In November 2008, China Aluminum spent approximately $2.15 billion on construction, expecting to complete it within 3-5 years with a planned production capacity of 200,000 tons.

The company's resource strategy focuses domestically, surpassing other companies in the entire copper industry.

We maintain our cautious recommendation investment rating for the company, suggesting continued attention to trading opportunities. We expect the company's EPS for 2009, 2010, and 2011 to be 0.29 yuan, 0.8 yuan, and 1.02 yuan respectively. Given our positive outlook for future copper prices and expectations for stronger integration of Yun Tong by China Aluminum in the future, we give the company a "cautious recommendation" investment rating.
