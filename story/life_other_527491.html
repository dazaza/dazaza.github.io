Ma Jiajue Strikes Again? A Third-Year Male Student "Goes Crazy" and Chops His Dormitory Mate to Death with a Cleaver

by mlwang on 2011-10-31 10:46:07

Do you still remember Ma Jiajue, who came from a poor family but had excellent grades? What "forced" him to take the extreme action? Around 11 o'clock in the morning on the 28th, a murder case occurred in the male dormitory of Guangdong Mechanical and Electrical Vocational and Technical College on Shatai Road in Baiyun District, Guangzhou. A third-year student held a kitchen knife and killed a roommate in the same dormitory, once again drawing great attention from society.

According to informed classmates, the deceased, Chen Mou, and the murderer, Lai Mou, were classmates and lived in the same dormitory. According to witnesses, at that time, Lai Mou was holding a kitchen knife and wildly chased after Chen Mou with ferocious chopping, like he was crazy, and nothing could stop him. Event Replay: "He was completely out of his mind at the time"

The "knife attack incident" happened in Building No. 7 of the college. For students living in Building No. 6, which is less than 10 meters away from Building No. 7, the half-hour knife attack incident felt like a "live broadcast."

Around 11 o'clock yesterday morning, Xiao Kong, who was playing computer games in the dormitory, suddenly heard a shout coming from Building No. 7. Then, "I saw someone running out of the dormitory on the 4th floor on the east side of Building No. 7. He was covering his head, first escaping towards the east, then immediately turning back and running towards the toilet," Xiao Kong recalled. The reporter observed that the public restroom was located at the far west end of the floor. Before the deceased, Chen Mou, ran into the restroom, he passed by the staircase located in the middle of the floor. As for why he didn't directly escape downstairs, the classmates who witnessed the entire event guessed that it might be because Chen Mou wasn't hit at the time and thought he would be safe if he hid in the restroom.

According to the classmates' recollections, after Chen Mou rushed into the restroom, Lai Mou followed with the kitchen knife, and cries of pain soon came from inside the restroom. "Not long after, the injured student stumbled out of the restroom, and it was clearly visible that blood was flowing from his head," Xiao Kong said. The victim ran about 10 meters before being caught up with. "The attacker swung the knife directly at his skull; he took a few steps before finally collapsing. It looked like the attacker was completely crazy."

According to the students, security guards armed with iron sticks arrived at this point. "However, the attacker walked down himself, still gripping the kitchen knife when he reached the first floor," one student said. Lai Mou's eyes were scattered, and he was silent and expressionless as he walked down. He collapsed in front of a hair salon on the east side. "Four security guards rushed forward, surrounded him, and carried him into a small black room on the first floor. After the police arrived, they took him away."

At around 11:30 AM, the 120 ambulance arrived at the scene. After medical personnel went upstairs to check, they declared the victim dead. Subsequently, the body was taken away by the funeral vehicle. Long-standing resentment due to different living habits

According to the hometown classmates of the victimized boy, the deceased, Chen Mou, and the perpetrator, Lai Mou, were classmates and roommates. "Chen Mou was very honest and also the captain of the school football team," classmate Xiao Chen said. Chen Mou didn't like staying in the dormitory much, while Lai Mou liked to play computer games loudly in the dormitory and often played dice games with loud noises in the dormitory.

Due to their differing living habits, the two had accumulated resentment over a long period. Before the incident, Chen Mou was interning at an automobile company and had to work until late every night, so he became even less able to tolerate Lai Mou's gaming sounds, leading to further tension between them.

Xiao Chen told Xin Kuai Daily that yesterday, Chen Mou was on holiday and resting in the dormitory. It was possible that during sleep, he couldn't bear the excessive volume of Lai Mou's gaming, which led to a fight and resulted in the unfortunate outcome.

Classmates' impressions:

Victim: Chen Mou, 23 years old, from a rural family in Leizhou, Zhanjiang, a third-year student majoring in automotive marketing at the Automotive Institute, captain of the school football team, liked sports, and didn't prefer staying in the dormitory.

Perpetrator: Lai Mou, from Yunfu, Guangdong, in the same class and dormitory as Chen Mou, liked to play computer games with high volume in the dormitory, looked very refined, preferred solitude, had an introverted personality, and didn't like making friends.

Lawyer's opinion: The school needs to bear partial civil responsibility

Regarding this matter, lawyer Wen Xiaobing from Guangdong Hebang Law Firm stated that since the murder occurred within the campus and during normal teaching and living hours, it indicates that the school has more or less issues of neglecting supervision. "For example, how did the kitchen knife get into the school? This is evidence of the school's negligence." Wen Xiaobing said that upon enrollment, the school has the responsibility to protect the personal safety of students. Although this case was caused by conflicts between two students, the involved school does not need to bear criminal responsibility but must assume partial civil compensation responsibility. The specific amount of compensation the school should bear can be resolved through litigation by the family of the deceased student.
