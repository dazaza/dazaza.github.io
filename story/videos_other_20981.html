by wlhch on 2007-05-31 18:52:35

The selection of the New 7 Wonders of the World, jointly inspired by UNESCO and the Swiss international cultural conservation organization - the New 7 Wonders of the World Foundation, was conducted worldwide, with a total of 77 candidates, and the final result will be announced on July 7, 2007 in Lisbon, Portugal. The Acropolis in Athens, Greece; the ruins of Chicheniza on Mexico's Yucatan Peninsula; the Colosseum in Rome; the Great Wall of China; the Eiffel Tower in Paris; the Inca forest city of Machu Picchu in Peru; Petra in Jordan; Stonehenge in England; Easter Island statues in Chile; and the Taj Mahal in Agra, India.

However, the Great Wall of China, represented by the Badaling Great Wall in Beijing, may once again miss the title of "New seven wonders" of the world. Yesterday, the Great Wall of China Society was forced to launch a canvassing campaign at Badaling Great Wall to encourage more citizens and tourists to vote before the voting deadline (0:00 on July 7, 2007). According to reports, the organizers said that the Great Wall is currently outside the top seven votes, but the specific number of votes will not be disclosed. (Source: The Great Wall may lose the vote for the new seven wonders is not publicly questioned)
