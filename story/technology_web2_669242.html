China to implement the strictest water resources management system

by yangliu435sh on 2012-02-19 11:31:33

The current situation of water resources in our country is very severe. Problems such as water resource shortages, serious water pollution, and deteriorating aquatic ecological environments are becoming increasingly prominent and have become the main bottleneck restricting the sustainable development of economy and society.

In response to this, our country will focus on the allocation, conservation, and protection of water resources, strengthen the management of water demand and usage processes, and through improving systems, implementing responsibilities, enhancing capabilities, and strengthening supervision, strictly control the total amount of water usage, comprehensively improve water efficiency, strictly control the total discharge of pollutants into rivers and lakes, accelerate the construction of a water-saving society, promote the sustainable utilization of water resources and the transformation of economic development models, and push for the coordinated development of economic and social progress with the carrying capacity of water resources and water environments.

The "Opinion" establishes the main objectives for water resource management: establishing a red line for water resource development and utilization control, with the national water usage controlled within 70 billion cubic meters by 2030; establishing a red line for water efficiency control, with water efficiency reaching or approaching world advanced levels by 2030, reducing water consumption per ten thousand yuan of industrial added value (calculated at constant prices from 2000) to below 40 cubic meters, and increasing the effective utilization coefficient of agricultural irrigation water to above 0.6; establishing a red line for restricted pollutant discharge in water function zones, controlling the total discharge of major pollutants into rivers and lakes within the pollution reception capacity range of water function zones by 2030, and increasing the water quality compliance rate of water function zones to over 95%.

To achieve these goals, by 2015, the national water usage will be controlled within 63.5 billion cubic meters; water consumption per ten thousand yuan of industrial added value will decrease by more than 30% compared to 2010, and the effective utilization coefficient of agricultural irrigation water will increase to above 0.53; the water quality compliance rate of important river and lake water function zones will increase to above 60%. By 2020, the national water usage will be controlled within 67 billion cubic meters; water consumption per ten thousand yuan of industrial added value will decrease to below 65 cubic meters, and the effective utilization coefficient of agricultural irrigation water will increase to above 0.55; the water quality compliance rate of important river and lake water function zones will increase to above 80%, and the water quality of urban drinking water sources will be fully compliant.

The "Opinion" points out that to strengthen the management of the red line for water resource development and utilization control, China will strictly implement total water usage control, strictly manage planning and water resource arguments, strictly control the total water usage in basins and regions, strictly implement water extraction permits, strictly enforce the paid use of water resources, strictly manage and protect groundwater, and reinforce the unified scheduling of water resources.

At the same time, China will comprehensively strengthen water conservation management, reinforce water quota management, and accelerate water-saving technology renovations.

Additionally, China will strengthen the management of the red line for restricted pollutant discharge in water function zones, strictly control the total discharge of pollutants into rivers and lakes, strengthen the protection of drinking water sources, strictly supervise water function zones, and advance the protection and restoration of aquatic ecosystems.

To ensure the smooth implementation of policies, China will establish a responsibility and assessment system for water resource management. At the same time, it will improve the water resource monitoring system, perfect the water resource management system, and establish a policy, law, and social supervision mechanism.

Establishing "three red lines" and implementing "four systems"

In January this year, the State Council issued the "Opinion on Implementing the Strictest Water Resource Management System," which is a comprehensive deployment and specific arrangement made by the State Council since the 2011 "Central Document No. 1" and the Central Water Conservancy Work Conference clearly required the implementation of the strictest water resource management system. This has profound significance and important influence for solving China's complex water resource and water environment issues and achieving sustainable economic and social development. On February 16, Hu Siyi, Vice Minister of Water Resources, interpreted this "Opinion" at a press conference held by the State Council Information Office and answered questions from reporters.

Establishing the control red line for development and utilization

Hu Siyi stated that having many people but few water resources, with uneven spatial and temporal distribution, is China's basic national condition and water situation. Currently, problems such as water resource shortages, serious water pollution, and deteriorating aquatic ecological environments in China are becoming increasingly prominent and have become the main bottleneck restricting sustainable economic and social development. For this reason, the State Council issued the "Opinion on Implementing the Strictest Water Resource Management System." Its main content can be summarized as establishing "three red lines" and implementing "four systems."

The "three red lines" are: the control red line for water resource development and utilization, with national water usage controlled within 70 billion cubic meters by 2030; the control red line for water efficiency, with water efficiency reaching or approaching world advanced levels by 2030, reducing water consumption per ten thousand yuan of industrial added value (calculated at constant prices from 2000) to below 40 cubic meters, and increasing the effective utilization coefficient of agricultural irrigation water to above 0.6; the control red line for restricted pollutant discharge in water function zones, controlling the total discharge of major pollutants into rivers and lakes within the pollution reception capacity range of water function zones by 2030, and increasing the water quality compliance rate of water function zones to over 95%.

The "four systems" include: first, the water usage total control system. Strengthening the management of the water resource development and utilization control red line, strictly implementing total water usage control, including strictly managing planning and water resource arguments, strictly controlling the total water usage in basins and regions, strictly implementing water extraction permits, strictly enforcing the paid use of water resources, strictly managing and protecting groundwater, and reinforcing the unified scheduling of water resources. Second, the water efficiency control system. Strengthening the management of the water efficiency control red line, comprehensively advancing the construction of a water-saving society, including comprehensively strengthening water conservation management, embedding water conservation throughout the process of economic and social development and public life and production, reinforcing water quota management, and accelerating water-saving technology renovations. Third, the restricted pollutant discharge system for water function zones. Strengthening the management of the restricted pollutant discharge red line for water function zones, strictly controlling the total discharge of pollutants into rivers and lakes, including strictly supervising water function zones, strengthening the protection of drinking water sources, and advancing the protection and restoration of aquatic ecosystems. Fourth, the responsibility and assessment system for water resource management. Incorporating the main indicators of water resource development, utilization, conservation, and protection into the comprehensive evaluation system for local economic and social development, with the main responsible persons of county-level and above governments being fully responsible for water resource management and protection in their administrative regions.

The strictest water resource control targets

So, where exactly does the "strictest water resource management system" manifest its strictness?

In response to this question raised by a reporter, Hu Siyi explained that the so-called strictest water resource management system is actually the strictest water resource control target, which is embodied in the "three red lines." The "three red lines" are actually the bottom line for water resource development and utilization. Once this bottom line is breached, economic and social development will suffer losses, and the ecological environment will be severely impacted. Specifically, its "strictness" mainly manifests in four aspects: clearer management goals, a more rigorous institutional system, stricter management measures, and more clearly defined responsible entities.
