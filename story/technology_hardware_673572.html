Website Optimization by Yi Xiang: The Choice of Keywords Determines the Success or Failure of a Website

by jackntav9612 on 2012-02-23 17:14:00

The choice of keywords determines the success or failure of a website. Whether a website correctly selects its keywords can decide its success or failure. If the wrong keywords are chosen, all the time and effort spent will be wasted without any results. Of course, some people might say that even if you make the wrong choice at first, it can still be corrected later. Personally, I think corrections can be made, but it wastes the previous time and effort. If the website has to reselect its keywords and start over, it would be a tragedy.

However, today is not mainly about introducing how to choose the right keywords for a website. It's closely related to choosing the right keywords for a website because after doing a joke website, I realized something: instead of choosing competitive popular keywords, it’s better to choose less popular keywords that can be ranked in a short time.

With more experience, I increasingly feel that keyword work should go from unpopular to popular. After getting some important precise long-tail keywords up, then slowly work on bringing up the core keywords. Because if the ranking of long-tail keywords is good and your user experience is well done, users will recognize it, bringing good page views (PV), making search engines consider your site as an excellent and trustworthy one. This can also boost the ranking of your core keywords. According to Baidu's click principle, only when your website enters the top 20 of Baidu can you enjoy Baidu's click principle; otherwise, it's just about competing with external links. Considering competitors are mostly old sites, it's really hard to outdo them in external links in a short time. Moreover, external link resources are becoming increasingly scarce. Therefore, it might be better to find some niche keywords and do them first. Let search engines feel that this is a good site, and at this point, I can focus on user experience rather than just competing with external links in the early stage.

But one thing needs to be emphasized: core keywords must not be abandoned and are even the most important. If you want to build a brand in your industry, you must spare no effort to bring the core keywords up. Because if core keywords can occupy the top three positions in search engines, a brand sometimes forms automatically. Let me give the most familiar example: our SEO industry. When we talk about SEO, everyone knows ZHANWEI SEO. Isn't it because when searching for "SEO," his site always ranks on the first page? Over time, it naturally became a brand in the hearts of SEOers. But the formation of a brand is not achieved overnight; it takes a long time. Rome wasn't built in a day either. However, if your business needs to sell products promptly and building a brand is secondary, you should follow my earlier suggestion: get the most effective keywords to the front page in the shortest time to receive orders and sell products. Sometimes, the boss's patience is limited.
