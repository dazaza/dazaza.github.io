Home blood glucose monitoring "smartly saves costs and pain"

by tfbgfnbv on 2009-11-25 09:09:05

In China, the mechanical model of gear pump; family blood glucose monitoring has not yet been popularized. Only diabetic patients with better economic conditions in big cities use blood glucose meters for monitoring at home. Most people are affected by economic factors and do not conduct blood glucose monitoring at home. The main reason is that the cost of blood glucose test strips is too high. During the usage process, improper use may lead to a large amount of waste of test strips, thereby increasing the cost. Therefore, choosing and using blood glucose meters and test strips wisely is the main solution to "save" expenses.

The waste of test strips generally occurs in two aspects. One is the waste during the preservation process, and the other is the waste during the usage process. Test strips are very sensitive to the humidity environment during preservation, and moisture can affect the measurement results. Experts suggest that single-strip packaged blood glucose test strips should be used as much as possible so that when one strip is used, the other strips are not affected. In addition, during the usage process, due to irregular blood sampling by patients, they may discover insufficient blood sampling only after the test strip and blood drop are separated. At this point, traditional test strips become useless. However, if test strips with secondary blood sampling function are used, patients can add blood within a certain time frame, continue the detection, and avoid wasting test strips. Currently, Omron's HEA-214 type blood glucose meter uses single-strip packaged test strips with a secondary blood sampling function.
