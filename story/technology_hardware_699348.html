Brief Introduction to Yuntai Mountain: Yuntai Mountain is a national scenic spot, one of China's first 5A-level tourist attractions, and part of the world's first batch of global geoparks. It is located in Xiuxian County, Jiaozuo City, Henan Province, at the southern foot of the Taihang Mountains, at the intersection of the boundaries between Henan and Shanxi Provinces, covering a total area of 190 square kilometers. Yuntai Mountain features eleven attractions including Hongshixia, Tanpuxia, Quanpuxia, Qinglongxia, Fenglinxia, Macaque Valley, Zhuyufeng, Diecaidong, Wanshan Temple, Zifang Lake, and Baijayan. This is a comprehensive scenic area mainly characterized by rift valley structures, hydrodynamic actions, and geological landform landscapes, supplemented by natural ecology and cultural landscapes, featuring alternating peaks and valleys, steep cliffs, cascading waterfalls, clear streams, and serene pools, combining aesthetic and scientific values. Yuntai Mountain is renowned for its unique mountains, stunning waters, majestic peaks, and secluded gorges, presenting different sceneries throughout the year. In spring, flowers bloom profusely; in summer, waterfalls cascade; in autumn, red leaves blaze like fire; in winter, everything is covered in silver snow, making it suitable for visits all year round. Here, you can experience both the grandeur of northern landscapes and the delicacy of southern water towns. Within the scenic area, there are world-unique strange gorges known as Red Stone Gorge; Asia's highest waterfall drop called Yuntai Heavenly Waterfall; Tanpu Gorge acclaimed as the most beautiful water scenery in Huaxia Central Plains; Zhuyufeng where Tang Dynasty poet Wang Wei wrote the famous poem "Alone in a Foreign Land I Am a Stranger, Missing My Family More During Festivals"; Fenglins Gorge praised as "A Lake of Water Between Heaven and Earth, Countless Spectacles Within"; Qinglong Gorge - an eco-tourism canyon with primitive simplicity and winding paths; and Baijayan, the historical residence of the "Seven Sages of the Bamboo Grove." The unique geological landforms of Yuntai Mountain have been named "Yuntai Landform" by geologists and hailed as "Canyon Encyclopedia" by tourism experts. It is akin to a "Ten Thousand Scroll History Book" in a geological museum and a "Hundred Li Painting Gallery" in terms of natural beauty. Visiting Yuntai Mountain not only allows you to appreciate the external beauty of nature but also understand its miraculous evolution, elevating a mere scenic tour into a geological science education journey. Transportation Guide to Yuntai Mountain: Address: Xiuxian County, Jiaozuo City, Henan Province. Self-driving Routes to Yuntai Mountain:

(1) Beijing to Yuntai Mountain: Jing-Shi (Beijing-Shijiazhuang) Expressway - Shi-An (Shijiazhuang-Anyang) Expressway - An-Xin (Anyang-Xinxiang) Expressway - Chang-Ji (Changyuan-Jiyuan) Expressway - Exit at Xiuxian South Station - Scenic Area.

(2) Xian to Yuntai Mountain: Depart from Xian through Chaoyang Gate northward, take West-Tong Expressway (Hu-Lian Expressway), enter from Baoqiao, exit at Tongguan. Exit at East Luoyang, get on Er-Guang Expressway (Luoyang-Jiyuan), turn to Jiao-Wen Expressway (Jiaozuo-Wenxian) at Jiyuan, reach South Jiaozuo, enter Jiaozuo city, follow road signs towards Yuntai Mountain. At the large roundabout crossroad 8 km away from the scenic area, there is a large gas station at the northeast corner, and the road sign in the middle of the roundabout indicates: 8 km eastward to Yuntai Mountain Scenic Area.

(3) Nanjing to Yuntai Mountain: Depart from Nanjing, cross Yangtze River Second Bridge onto Nan-Luo Expressway via Jiangsu and Anhui provinces to Henan Luohe, transfer to Jing-Hong Kong-Macao Expressway towards Zhengzhou, after crossing Zhengzhou Yellow River Bridge, transfer to Zheng-Jiao-Jin Expressway, exit at Xiaoxugang in Wuzhi, follow signs through Xiuxian to Yuntai Mountain. At the large roundabout crossroad 8 km away from the scenic area, there is a large gas station at the northeast corner, and the road sign in the middle of the roundabout indicates: 8 km eastward to Yuntai Mountain Scenic Area.

(4) Wuhan to Yuntai Mountain: Depart from Wuhan via Jingzhu Expressway passing through Xiaogan, Xinyang, and Zhumadian cities, transfer to Zheng-Jiao Expressway in Zhengzhou, exit at Xiaoxugang in Wuzhi, follow signs through Xiuxian to Yuntai Mountain. At the large roundabout crossroad 8 km away from the scenic area, there is a large gas station at the northeast corner, and the road sign in the middle of the roundabout indicates: 8 km eastward to Yuntai Mountain Scenic Area.

Bus Route to Yuntai Mountain: From Zhengzhou to Jiaozuo, there are two types of bus tickets. The first type costs 26 yuan, with older vehicles not taking the expressway, requiring three hours to arrive at Jiaozuo Long-distance Bus Station. The second type costs 35 yuan (starting at 7:00 AM and ending at 17:40 PM, with buses every forty minutes), traveling entirely on expressways without stops, requiring two hours to arrive at Jiaozuo Long-distance Bus Station. After arriving in Jiaozuo, take a taxi (approximately 45 yuan) directly to the Yuntai Mountain Scenic Area.
