by chengshangdao on 2007-05-31 13:42:58

With the approach of Children's Day on June 1, a wide variety of children's toys and children's products have been put on the shelves. The huge business opportunity has also tempted some unscrupulous merchants to entertain improper thoughts: according to an investigation report recently aired by CCTV’s "Weekly Quality Report", in some places of Hebei Province, a type of industrial waste known as carpet fluff from textile factories has become a hot commodity. Originally, they were directly used as filling materials for plush toys. Since the filling material is made up of some discarded materials, short-term contact may cause itching on children's bodies and even lead to some red spots, while long-term contact might breed bacteria harmful to children's health.

The product quality supervision special inspection results released by the General Administration of Quality Supervision, Inspection and Quarantine on the 27th showed that the average sampling pass rate of four types of products, namely jelly, infant formula milk powder, toys, and infant clothing, was 84.9%. This time, the General Administration of Quality Supervision, Inspection and Quarantine inspected 265 products produced by 240 enterprises (not involving exports). Among them, toys were inspected from 105 products produced by 104 enterprises in six provinces and cities including Fujian and Guangdong, with a product sampling pass rate of 77.1%.

Children's Day is coming soon, perhaps you are planning to buy a cute toy for your child. Experts suggest that parents should not be greedy for cheap prices when choosing toys for their children. Before purchasing, attention should be paid to whether the toy's marks are complete, and the appropriate toys should be chosen according to the age range indicated on the toys. If you choose a plush toy, it would be best to unzip the zipper and check the toy's filling material, only then can you possibly buy safe and qualified toys for your child.
