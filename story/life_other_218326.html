Military training for city managers: are ordinary people really that difficult to manage?

by dfdfffffffffffffffffffff007 on 2009-08-05 12:06:47

Recently, Kunming Integrated Law Enforcement Bureau in Yunnan Province held the first large-scale training for urban management law enforcement. This will become a key to ensuring the smooth implementation of future integrated urban management law enforcement. The training lasted for ten days and was carried out in two batches starting from August 2nd. It involved more than 1000 integrated administrative law enforcement officers from 14 counties (cities) districts in Kunming and three development (resort) zones. The first batch of trainees had already moved into the training base of the Military District Brigade of Yunnan Provincial Military Region on the 2nd, where they received full closed military management training.

"Pay attention to make sure that the other party's face has no blood, body has no injury, and surroundings have no people." - Several months ago, an Internet user exposed that such "teaching" was given to urban management team members in Beijing's law enforcement training manual against violent resistance. Netizens who replied to the post directly called it "the most immoral trick" and "a thug training manual". This book, named "Urban Management Law Enforcement Operation Practice", appeared online and immediately drew a lot of criticism. By 8:30 pm last night, within just nine hours, the post received over 24,000 clicks and nearly 500 replies.

On July 11th, the Huacao Town Urban Management of Minhang District in Shanghai was rectifying illegal vendors in the jurisdiction when they got into an argument with Peng Lin, an Anhui-native vendor, and beat him up, resulting in diffuse axonal injury and right hemiplegia for Peng Lin. Nearly a month has passed since the incident, but relevant departments have still not provided a reasonable explanation of the event process. Even the condition of the victim has become mysterious.

All signs indicate that the urban management, the protector of social security, has become unpopular. And the urban management team, under the banner of the state, is "fraudulently pretending" everywhere. Are ordinary people really that difficult to manage?
