"Future enterprises must get rid of price competition and shift to value competition; only then will you have the capital to survive" - Liu Chuanzhi. "Business is a game, the rules are always changing, those who don't adapt will be eliminated" - Li Ka-shing. "One of the biggest problems in business is: maintaining the previous successful model unchanged, even one year is too long" - Pratt, CEO of HP. "A dollar, through your company, becomes eleven cents after going around. Business model refers to where this ten-cent increment occurs" - Robertson Steven, the most famous risk investment advisor in Silicon Valley. The above four sentences vividly express the meaning of introducing a business model in enterprises, the need for innovation in business models, as well as the simplest and most direct way to test whether a business model is on the right track. Without any doubt, the purpose of introducing a business model in enterprises is to make a profit, therefore, any mode constructed or adjusted with this at its core is correct and effective. Underfloor heating companies need business models, requiring continuous exploration, adjustment, and innovation of business models to keep up with the market and the times. However, they should not tie themselves down to the tree of business models, after all, business models are not the entirety of commerce. Clear: Profitability is the ultimate meaning for underfloor heating companies to introduce business models. Business models can bring profits and help enterprises achieve sustainable profitability. Profitability is the first issue every company should consider. Every big brand today has achieved sustained profitability through business models, step by step from their initial status: Coca-Cola used to sell soda, Microsoft used to sell software, Walmart was originally a grocery store, and Hai Di Lao started as a small hot pot restaurant. However, a business model is not a profit model; the latter is the channel through which a company makes money, while the former is the value the company can provide to customers and the factors that enable sustainable profitability. Some people say that business models are things that only high-tech industries can 'play with', and since underfloor heating is a low-tech industry, most of which are small and medium-sized companies, they don't need business models. In fact, regardless of whether it's high-tech or low-tech, if you want to succeed, you need to find a successful business model and quickly maximize the profitability of the business model. At the same time, this also indicates: the most direct and fast method to test whether a business model is on the right track is to see if customers are willing to pay and if the company has achieved profit revenue. Consideration: How to control the degree of innovation in business models? Among all innovations, business model innovation belongs to the most fundamental innovation of an enterprise. Without a business model, other management innovations and technological innovations lose the possibility of sustainable development and the foundation of profitability. Mr. Yan Huiping, chairman of Shanghai Huiton Industrial & Trade Co., Ltd., stated: The topic of business model innovation is too broad, it relates to corporate culture, the concepts, thinking patterns, strategic vision of the company's boss, as well as the company's positioning, so it cannot be generalized. Mr. Wu Zhiyuan, general manager of Jiangsu Kening Comfort Home System Integration Co., Ltd., said: "Business model innovation cannot be copied, nor can all companies adopt the same model. The premise of business model innovation is to practice internal skills diligently, reducing total costs to the lowest. All adjustments, improvements, and executions of the company's operating methods and models should revolve around reducing total costs, achieving maximum profitability through the lowest total cost, thereby enhancing market competitiveness." Mr. Guo Chunyu, general manager of Chongqing Xinqinshidai Warmth Equipment Co., Ltd., believed: Business model innovation is a product of full market competition, but all innovations carry risks, and they cannot change endlessly, otherwise there will be no end, and may even 'change' away the originally good things. The company needs to preserve and inherit existing excellent models and maintain its own positioning. In summary, all changes and innovations must return to the fundamental principle of honest operation. Dr. Chen Mingzhe, founder of the Dynamic Competition Theory and world-renowned business strategy master, pointed out: "In fact, business models do not need to change for a long time, only continuous adjustments and modifications are needed, rather than fundamental changes." Exploration: How can underfloor heating companies sustain profitability through business models? This year's China Underfloor Heating Ten Thousand Mile Journey and National Tour Promotion Event for Comfortable Homes has made more underfloor heating companies start to deeply think about the concept of business models, and innovators of business models have emerged. For example, Sanmenxia GeRui Warmth Equipment Co., Ltd. launched an alternative 'work': the Comfort Home System Experience Mobile Vehicle, and Chairman Huang Guocang of Nanjing Tianma Far Red Underfloor Heating Technology Co., Ltd. and General Manager Ma Kaiyun of the American Quiet Water China Operation Center jointly opened the 'Nanjing Comfort Home Experience Store'. The particularity of the underfloor heating industry determines that it is difficult to become large and strong with a single underfloor heating system. Developing towards comfort home integration from a single underfloor heating system is the best prospect. The continuous popularity of the Comfort Home System Experience Mobile Vehicle and the Nanjing Quiet Home Experience Store sufficiently proves that this path is feasible, customers are buying into it, and it will succeed. Of course, there are many ways of business models, and all roads lead to Rome. As for how underfloor heating companies specifically obtain inspiration for business models and use business models to profit, based on the views of industry insiders, the author summarizes the following points: Exchange Mr. Wu Zhiyuan, general manager of Jiangsu Kening Comfort Home System Integration Co., Ltd., believed: The prospect of discussing innovative business models for underfloor heating companies lies in exchange, like the exchange meetings frequently held by China Underfloor Heating Ten Thousand Mile Journey and Warm Century Media. Exploring business models cannot be done in isolation; communication between peers can spark ideas, and good inspirations come from such exchanges. Creativity GeRui Warmth's Comfort Home System Experience Vehicle has shown what Mr. Lu Xun meant by "originally there was no road, but when more people walk on it, a road appears". GeRui pioneeringly integrated the underfloor heating system and core subsystems such as new air, central air conditioning, and clear water systems onto a large truck that can move anywhere, saving costs and promoting what underfloor heating and comfort home systems are while driving, naturally making passersby remember the "GeRui" brand. Combination of Physical and Virtual Networks By "physical network", we mean the company's marketing, service, and logistics networks, and by "virtual network", we mean the Internet, including the company website, electronic stores, industry websites, online malls, etc. The virtual network is used to retain and track customers, while the physical network is used to promptly understand customer needs. The physical network emphasizes efficiency, while the virtual network should cover as wide an area as possible across the country to maximize customer acquisition and establish contact with customers. The result of combining the two is to acquire and serve the most customers at the lowest cost. Inverted Pyramid Profitability For underfloor heating, wall-hung boilers, and other comfort home system manufacturers, inventory has always been a headache. Frontline salespeople, in order to meet performance targets, will try to lay down as much stock as possible, ultimately leading to increased inventory. Under the new business model, salespeople should first secure orders before pushing back to the production department for production, thereby adjusting the pace of the entire supply chain and related services. The best outcome of this approach is achieving zero inventory, indirectly enhancing overall market competitiveness. Service Must Be Done Even If It Costs Money Ms. Wang Fang, President of Liaoning Underfloor Heating Association and General Manager of Liaoning Huayuan Warmth Engineering Co., Ltd., highly approves of Haier's "service supremacy" model: "Even if it costs money, service must be done to the extreme." Ms. Wang expressed: Huayuan follows an extremely responsible service principle, regarding service as a promise that must be kept. For example, if it is agreed to arrive at the user's home within two hours, the worker will definitely not arrive at two hours and one minute. Sometimes, even if it costs money, it must still be done because service is a value-added business, doing a good job in service equals indirect profitability, and meticulous service will yield high returns. Transition from Workshop-style to Project Team System Small companies operating with the strategic vision of large companies may not necessarily grow larger and stronger, but thinking with the mindset of a small company will certainly prevent them from growing larger and stronger. "Currently, many underfloor heating companies have yet to escape the workshop-style, multi-role management system, making it difficult for them to adapt to intense competition, ultimately leading to their elimination," said Chairman Huang Guocang of Nanjing Tianma Far Red Underfloor Heating Technology Co., Ltd. He pointed out: "Currently, 10% of users in Nanjing are doing radiator renovations, and these users are potential customers for underfloor heating companies and comfort home system integrators. We can say that we have a very broad market prospect. For this reason, I am more in favor of underfloor heating companies developing towards project-based management. The comfort home system is a highly integrated system, not something that can be achieved simply by stacking various products or systems together. To realize such a 'large' system and maintain it for a long time, companies should have a complete corporate management system, including: clear job responsibilities and close cooperation, technical sharing teams, as well as clear organizational structures and production-sales-service processes, which is known as the team cooperation system." Editor's Note: Companies Should Not Over-rely on Business Models In today's commercial society, it is undeniable that competition is no longer between products but between business models. Companies need to explore business models that follow market economic trends and suit their own needs. However, without certain internal strength, including integrity, product quality, technical capability, service quality, and industry responsibility, all business models are just empty shells. This is akin to learning acrobatics; even with the best methods, without solid, hard practice and commitment, one will never truly learn, let alone reach a higher level of skill. Business models should not be the lifeblood of companies; companies should not overly indulge or rely on business models. Products or services should still be able to survive independently of business models, which represents a healthy development state. After all, business models are not the entirety of commerce.
