Xu Shuguang: Competing for Industry "Benchmark" with Informationization

by hostinns on 2010-04-19 12:13:23

Information Construction - Strengthening Cooperation for Mutual Benefit

From nothing to something, the informatization of GreenTree Inn can be considered quite successful. However, looking back at GreenTree Inn's history, its path to informatization was not smooth. Due to a portion of its subsidiary hotels being franchise-based, and the configuration and hardware environment of these franchise hotels, along with their overall operational environment, differing greatly from GreenTree Inn's system, this has caused many troubles for GreenTree Inn. At the same time, as the company developed, various types of data grew geometrically. In such circumstances, ensuring the safety, speed, and stability of the information system was quite difficult.

If these are problems that must be faced and solved in the development of an enterprise, then in Xu Zhiguang's view, there is another unnecessary trouble: external software issues. Coming from the United States, where the level of information technology is already quite mature, Xu Zhiguang, who is well-versed in IT, was very frustrated by the low efficiency of domestic software development and after-sales service. The result of cooperating with domestic software manufacturers was that all sorts of problems appeared during the initial implementation of the informatization project. After multiple communications with the manufacturer, the system underwent three updates before it met the company's initial requirements.

When asked about the reasons for this situation, Xu Zhiguang gave two answers. "Firstly, foreign software management systems are more mature and professional. The preliminary research work is more thorough, and the survival and development of software companies themselves are also more stable. Therefore, the standards and quality of the software they develop are very good. On the other hand, domestic software has many problems in terms of stability, secondary development, and source code. As a result, many domestic software manufacturers have a tendency to start strong but finish weak. Initially, their ideas are grand, but when the actual product comes out, it is very average. When it comes to maintenance and repair, it becomes even more difficult. Moreover, many software programs become obsolete after just two years of use, leading to a lot of repetition and waste."

However, at the same time, Xu Zhiguang did not completely blame the domestic manufacturers. In his view, whether it is for software manufacturers or enterprises as users, the efforts of both parties complement each other in improving the overall quality of domestic software and the level of after-sales service. Clearly, manufacturers develop according to the needs of enterprises. If enterprises seek cheap solutions, manufacturers will find it hard to ensure quality. However, Xu Zhiguang has always tended to choose software manufacturers based on development time. Because high-quality products always take a longer time to develop. For software companies, what they need to do is to put customer service first, rather than solely aiming for short-term profits. Only in this way can the quality of domestic software improve significantly.

Outlook on Informatization: A Step Higher

When talking about the future informatization construction of GreenTree Inn, Xu Zhiguang was full of confidence: "Further perfecting the construction and implementation of informatization projects, enhancing customer experience, accurately and quickly obtaining information, and minimizing human intervention to increase automation, handing over repetitive and complicated tasks to computers." This will undoubtedly allow GreenTree Inn's performance to "take a step higher" in the new year.

Of course, in order to achieve this goal, GreenTree Inn already has an excellent IT team. Under the leadership of veteran IT manager Wang Zhen, they not only deeply understand serving business processes conceptually, but also continuously maintain and repair the system during the deployment and implementation of software, ensuring 24-hour normal operation of the system.

The reason for having high hopes for informatization construction, Xu Zhiguang believes, is that "informatization is the embodiment of an enterprise's core competitiveness, especially for the hotel chain industry. Informatization is equivalent to a country's information highway. Information itself flows through informatization. Once the informatization system is well established, it can greatly improve the efficiency and competitiveness of the enterprise, laying a solid foundation for providing timely and effective services. Therefore, the informatization system is crucial for GreenTree Inn."

Informatization - The Soul of Hotel Chains

In Xu Zhiguang's eyes, hotel chains are a field where informatization is highly concentrated and widely applied. In this industry, almost all work is related to informatization. Recognizing the importance of informatization to the hotel chain industry, Xu Zhiguang promoted a relatively complete informatization project from the beginning. From information management to financial management, and then inventory management, without exception, IT construction plays a leading role. In addition, the connection between various departments of the headquarters and the human resource management established on this basis all depend on the information system.

To fully leverage the advantages of IT's efficiency and speed, GreenTree Inn has established central service systems and website service systems. The business process of central reservation is relatively complex, not only including customer service aspects but also involving information transmission and sharing among various stores. The involvement of informatization has greatly improved this situation. In terms of the website, the company has carried out large-scale construction and maintenance of data exchange and data sharing. Nowadays, GreenTree Inn's official website receives visits from millions of customers every day.

Xu Zhiguang also mentioned an impressive small event in the informatization construction, which was about popularizing corporate culture. Originally, there was an important sentence in GreenTree Inn's corporate culture: "Learn from others' mistakes." However, for hundreds of stores across China and overseas, and tens of thousands of employees, achieving quick and efficient popularization was not easy. The construction of informatization made such things very simple. Through the editing and publishing of information on the company's website, and organizing employees for daily training, this concept was effectively implemented and popularized.

A sound informatization construction naturally requires support from a high-quality IT elite team. Xu Zhiguang's requirements for IT personnel are also very high. He believes that "for CIOs leading the IT construction of enterprises, if they want informatization to keep up with the pace of enterprise development, CIOs must have strong foresight and resolve issues that can be handled by computers in advance.

That is, CIOs are not only technical supporters but also resource managers. In management, they can utilize the company's business and resources to assist the overall development of the company. At the same time, CIOs should have a deep understanding of the company's business and possess comprehensive coordination abilities. Through continuous communication and coordination, they can simplify processes and improve the proportion of automation."

Without a doubt, Xu Zhiguang, who studied abroad in the United States and graduated from the University of Southern California with a doctorate in operations management and financial analysis, embodies the spirit of entrepreneurship. Therefore, whether it is a world-class company like Broadway Holdings in the United States, the largest entertainment real estate investment fund company in the U.S., or the American Unity Investment Company, none of them were destined to be Xu Zhiguang's career destination.

Since 1997, Xu Zhiguang began his entrepreneurial journey. He co-invested in founding Pacific Home APH in the United States and served as its chairman. In 2004, seeing the bubble in the U.S. housing market and the huge commercial opportunities in China, Xu Zhiguang returned home resolutely, investing in and founding GreenTree Inn, a business chain hotel known for its high quality and cost-effectiveness, and serving as its president. The success of GreenTree Inn can be attributed less to Xu Zhiguang's accurate judgment of the economic environment and more to his investment philosophy.

In his career, Xu Zhiguang has always adhered to the principle of thinking ahead while staying safe. "No matter when, in any industry, or under any circumstances, while maintaining stable enterprise development, one must pay attention to changes around them because, in a fiercely competitive market environment, danger is everywhere." It was under this line of thought that at the end of 2007, when many domestic chain hotels were scrambling for properties regardless of quality, allowing substandard products and services to seize the market, Xu Zhiguang promptly adjusted the standards for investing in hotels, calmly weathering the subsequent financial crisis. GreenTree Inn emerged from the crisis still maintaining steady growth. Reviewing the measures taken by Xu Zhiguang, informatization played an indispensable role.

Despite Xu Zhiguang's emphasis on informatization construction, GreenTree Inn never had the position of CIO. This question was seen through by Xu Zhiguang before the interview. He told reporters that due to his emphasis on informatization, he personally took charge of this department from the beginning. However, he also indicated that in the future, he would focus on cultivating the company's informatization leader, helping him quickly become a qualified CIO.
