Chinese audiences are not unfamiliar with the story of Zorro. This legendary character comes from the 1919 serialized story "The Curse of Capistrano" by American writer Johnston McCulley. It describes the period when Spain ruled Mexico in the 18th and 19th centuries. At that time, California was still under Spanish colonial rule. The last governor, La Fleur, used high-pressure dictatorial rule against the local people, causing them great suffering. The noble Diego stood up as a masked knight to oppose tyranny, thus giving birth to "The Legend of Zorro." In McCulley's pen, Zorro (which means "fox" in Spanish) is based on the story of an English legendary figure combined with the deeds of three heroes during the Mexican Revolution.
