The open-style Google mobile operating system

by coiuay1276 on 2009-11-25 19:28:34

On October 20th this year, Dopod announced the launch of two Android-based smartphones in mainland China. This was considered by many to be the first mass production of Android phones in mainland China. However, in this race with branded manufacturers for Android dominance, Shenzhen's knockoff phone producers showed no inferiority, at least not in terms of speed.

A significant signal emerged at this year's Beijing International Communication Exhibition, where "the father of knockoff phones," MediaTek, officially launched a smartphone solution based on Windows Mobile system. "Smartphones will be an indispensable market for MediaTek next," a relevant person from MediaTek China told this reporter.

CEO of Chuangyang Communication, Jiao Xuening, informed us that their Android phone solution could bring the domestic market terminal selling price down to below 1500 yuan. Liu Wengquan mentioned that the expected retail price of Le Pai Android phones would be between 1500 and 2000 yuan.

Compared to iPhone (mobile internet) and Windows Mobile, Google's developed mobile operating system Android might become a breakthrough point for the proliferation of knockoff smartphones.

According to our investigation, even before the debut of Android phones, multiple Shenzhen manufacturers had attempted to introduce smartphones based on pirated Windows Mobile systems, priced around a thousand yuan, but with lukewarm market responses.

Is it a breakthrough for smartphones?

Liu Wengquan told us that judging from the current cooperation with dealers, channel support is not an issue; "Dealers are quite optimistic about its prospects." Several cell phone dealers admitted to us that despite the Android market still being in the cultivation phase, for traditional cell phone sales with increasingly declining profits, "this market needs new stimulation."

"The key lies in how much real demand there is for smartphones in the market; now the market lacks a star model that can drive growth." Wang Yang analyzed.

According to what we learned, among the two Android phones Dopod launched for the first time, one was priced at 4980 yuan, and the other at 3280 yuan. "With the large-scale mass production of Android phones, we expect the price to drop below a thousand yuan by the second half of next year," Liu Wengquan said.

"Unlike traditional cell phone sales, the concept of Android has already garnered considerable market appeal," Liu Wengquan informed us. From the grey market in Huaqiangbei, we learned that the price of grey-market Gphone (Google Phone) has dropped below 2000 yuan and continues to sell well.

"Chuangyang Communication's R&D team exceeds 50 people, jointly formed by several partners," CEO of Chuangyang Communication, Jiao Xuening, told us. Chuangyang Communication is responsible for the development of the phone hardware and some upper-layer applications, while Yicheng Communication undertakes the development of the Android operating system, drivers, system programs, part of the application software, and other software parts.

The sample machine of this Chuangyang model I once saw differs from general Android phones, particularly adding PushMail functions similar to BlackBerry phones.

As early as July this year, Shenzhen's mobile phone design company Sanji Google Communication announced that its first Android OS-based phone had been successfully developed. Our interviews revealed that unlike Android phones commonly using Qualcomm chips, Sanji Google's solution uses Samsung's mobile phone chips, which have faster processor speeds.

Targeting Android

Compared to other smartphone operating systems, Android is an open platform that can arbitrarily embed application software. iSuppli China Research Director Wang Yang believes that with the advent of the 3G era, smartphones will be "application kings," so Android's open mode undoubtedly lays the foundation for the popularization of smartphones.

Our interviews revealed that compared to the mere 10 yuan profit of traditional knockoff phones, Shenzhen's Android phone manufacturers can achieve a profit of around 200 yuan, which undeniably holds great appeal for channels.

Affordable prices are undoubtedly the powerful weapon for knockoff phones against branded manufacturers. Whether or not the price advantage can continue in the smartphone era remains crucial to their success or failure.

However, whether the price advantage can truly ignite the knockoff smartphone market remains uncertain. "Smartphones face a high-end market significantly different from the low-end market of traditional knockoff phones, and the pulling effect of price advantages isn't obvious," Mao Hongjiang, General Manager of Shenzhen Longtu Mobile Communication Co., Ltd., analyzed. The market prospects for knockoff smartphones still need observation.

Interestingly, almost simultaneously, another Shenzhen mobile phone design company, Chuangyang Communication, also completed the research and development of Android phones.

As an open Google mobile operating system, Android's knockoff trend has quietly taken shape in Shenzhen.

iSuppli China Research Director Wang Yang believes that currently, the smartphone market in mainland China is still in its infancy. "It will take another one and a half to two years for the smartphone market to mature, which includes not only the development of terminal products but also awaits the maturity of 3G networks and related applications," Wang Yang believed. In the long term, smartphones will replace existing functional phones, and under this industrial logic, Shenzhen's knockoff smartphone power cannot be ignored.

From various sources, we learned that currently in Shenzhen, at least five manufacturers with annual shipments of over a million units each have Android phone development plans, expected to hit the market in bulk soon.

Moreover, compared to the exorbitant licensing fees of the Windows Mobile system, adopting the free Android system reduces basic costs by approximately 20 to 30 US dollars. "For knockoff manufacturers whose profits are becoming increasingly thin, Android undoubtedly helps compress costs more effectively," Wang Yang analyzed.

Sanji Google General Manager Liao Jicheng informed us that currently, their Android phones are evenly distributed between domestic and overseas markets. Among their domestic market clients, Le Pai Communication is one of their partners. Le Pai General Manager Liu Wengquan stated: "After mass production, we plan to reach a monthly shipment volume of 20,000 units."

"Our Android phones initially mainly target overseas markets such as Southeast Asia," Jiao Xuening told this reporter. With the activation of the domestic Android market, they have already reached a cooperation agreement with a domestic integrator to enter the domestic market.

"We started planning Android phone R&D at the end of last year," Sanji Google General Manager Liao Jicheng revealed to the reporter. To develop Android phones, the company formed a nearly 30-person R&D team, "core R&D personnel have come from Samsung and Dexin." An unverified message suggests that some R&D personnel from branded manufacturers may have had prior experience developing Android phones.

An interesting phenomenon is that both Android phones Dopod launched for the first time in the mainland market were not operator-customized but sold through social channels. More than that, there are rumors that Google will also launch an unlocked version of the Gphone outside operator channels. "Branded manufacturers launching products through social channels help cultivate the Android phone market, which is good news for Shenzhen's Android manufacturers," Liu Wengquan indicated.

"Our Android phones have begun trial production, and we're preparing for bulk listing," a person from a Shenzhen knockoff manufacturer told us. In Shenzhen, China's largest "knockoff phone capital," a wave of industrial momentum surrounding smartphone knockoffs has been secretly brewing, and Android phones have the potential to become their breakthrough into this market.
