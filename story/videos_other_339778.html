Beach Basketball is an exciting, non-stop action game played on the beach, enjoyed by HOOPSTERS of all ages. The game was created over four decades ago on the physical education fields of Gulf Shores School. This modified version of basketball, invented by Philip Bryant, has grown from a highly effective skill improvement game to a very popular and widespread competitive sport. For the past 20 years, the World Beach Basketball Association has developed grassroots player interest, resulting in gameplay in 15 of the WBBA's 36 worldwide regions. The WBBA Upper Caribbean Region, with commissioner Mickey Muñoz, was selected as the "2009 WBBA Region of the Year" for their efforts in administering tournaments for Beach Basketball players. The 2010 Beach Basketball 17th Annual World Championships will be hosted by the WBBA in Gulf Shores, Alabama, the birthplace of Beach Basketball. Currently, seven WBBA World Regions are hosting upcoming tournaments. What is Beach Basketball? The world's fastest-growing beach sport, played on a circular court with no backboard on the goal. There’s no out-of-bounds, non-stop action, make-it-take-it scoring format, 3’s outside the circle, 2’s inside, ball movement via pass or 2 1/2 steps - no dribble allowed.
