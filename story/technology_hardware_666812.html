73-Year-Old Woman Brought Back to Life 35 Minutes After Sudden Death

by anonymous on 2012-02-16 10:31:01

CHANGSHA, December 12 (reporter Tang Jiangpeng, Liu Xiang) - At 73 years old, Wan (a pseudonym) was at the electrocardiogram (ECG) room of Xiangya Second Hospital in Central South University to get an ECG done when she suddenly collapsed and died unexpectedly. Upon receiving the distress call, doctors from the emergency department rushed to the scene and immediately performed cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR), including external chest compressions, artificial respiration, and defibrillation. After 35 minutes of life-saving efforts, Wan was successfully revived.

The elderly woman suddenly fainted and passed away

Wan is originally from Dao County in Yongzhou. She had been hospitalized locally a few weeks prior due to feelings of chest tightness and shortness of breath, where she was diagnosed with coronary heart disease and heart failure. Due to the poor treatment outcomes, she was transferred to Xiangya Second Hospital for further care.

At around 10 a.m. on the 9th, accompanied by her daughter, Wan visited the cardiovascular medicine outpatient clinic at Xiangya Second Hospital. The attending physician recommended that she undergo an ECG test. At 11:35 a.m., after completing the ECG, Wan suddenly collapsed, losing consciousness with labored breathing and turning blue all over. Those present quickly helped her onto a bed and called the emergency department for help.

Revived after 35 minutes

Upon receiving the call, Dr. Rongyao from the emergency department's residency program rushed to the ECG room. He found that Wan had suffered sudden cardiac arrest. As he dispersed the onlookers, he began immediate resuscitation measures. At this point, Wan's ECG showed a "straight line," indicating no heartbeat or breathing, dilated pupils, and cyanosis throughout her body. Dr. Rongyao immediately started external chest compressions. Subsequently, multiple doctors from the emergency department arrived at the scene with various resuscitation equipment. Under continuous heart massage and urgent rescue measures, including endotracheal intubation and manual ventilation assistance, at 12:10 p.m., Wan's heartbeat was restored.

Wan was immediately transferred to the emergency department on a stretcher for further treatment. Half an hour later, Wan gradually regained consciousness.

"My mother and I would have been separated forever if it wasn't for the timely rescue by the doctors," said Wan's daughter, still shaken recalling the incident.

Cardiac massage buys precious time for rescue

Deputy Director Chai Xiangping of the hospital's emergency department stated that acute myocardial infarction is the most common cause of sudden death in patients. After the heart stops beating, the effective rescue window is only 4-6 minutes. Beyond this timeframe, successful resuscitation is almost impossible. Wan's successful revival can be considered quite fortunate.

So, what should you do if you encounter a sudden death case at home? Chai Xiangping emphasized that one should immediately begin manual cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR) while asking someone nearby to call "120" for emergency services. Before professional rescuers arrive, never stop performing chest compressions, as this can secure valuable time for effective patient rescue. Winter is the peak season for myocardial infarction, especially among the elderly. Therefore, elderly individuals should promptly add clothing layers to prevent colds that could affect heart and lung functions; when going outside, it’s best to wear hats, gloves, and masks; appropriately control salt and fat intake, eat more fresh vegetables and fruits, and supplement with adequate vitamins.
