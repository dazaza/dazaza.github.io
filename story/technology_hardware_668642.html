My Own Movie February 16, 2012 -- Written for Y. I believe anything is possible "An endless void surged into my heart, just felt sitting still and holding back tears. Never knowing how to hide the truth in my heart, I am still pretending to be natural at this moment... Possessing surprises given by fate, not knowing also adds concerns. Saying goodbye with pain but feeling sorrow inside, only knowing that it was once possessed!" In the end, we will all be defeated by time. Fortunately, heaven has given us both a complete life to appreciate the surprises and sorrows in life. Many people and many things will eventually become a drifting boat in the river of time. What matters is that during the time when lives intersect, even if it's just looking up at the twinkling of a star, it should be treated as solemnly as attending a banquet. Even if the music ends and the guests leave, what regrets are there? The reason why memories are so beautiful may simply be because they can't be owned again. Looking back suddenly on past events is like watching a movie, you are the main character, along with her, him, it, them... Do you understand? This very second will eventually become a memory of the next second. If at this moment, we are both melancholically reminiscing about the past, then the only thing we can reminisce about in the next second is the sadness of this second. Have you ever thought about treating life as a movie, where you yourself are the director and screenwriter of this movie. Whether it's a comedy or a tragedy, a farce or a drama, it's all decided by oneself. Suddenly, I'm thinking that an ordinary life is, many years later, often sorrowfully reminiscing about the past due to boredom and loneliness; while a brilliant life is, when you are still searching for the scenery ahead, too many people are rushing to study your past which you haven't had time to look back on yet.
