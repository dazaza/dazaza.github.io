[Qingdao News] Ten-thousand-yuan civil service exam training class no different from ordinary class, high pass rate is exaggerated

by qssqwe215 on 2012-03-06 15:16:02

The written examination for the national civil service exam has just ended, and on March 24th, the Shandong Provincial Civil Servant Examination is about to begin. With the arrival of the civil servant examination season, all sorts of national exam training courses are vying with each other to showcase various gimmicks in order to attract candidates to enroll. Some boast about their "[no pass, full refund]" policy, while others claim that their lecturers are "[members of the question-setting group]." Moreover, prices range from a few hundred yuan to tens of thousands of yuan, showing a huge disparity. Recently, reporters have conducted in-depth investigations into the various irregularities surrounding these civil servant exam training courses.

An abundance of "[expert-level]" teaching staff

Non-stop consultation calls and a steady stream of students enrolling, two employees at Lianchuang Shihua started their busy day as soon as they arrived at work. This national civil service exam training institution located in the A-zone of Qingdao University Science Park has become the liveliest company in this office building. A candidate from out of town, having just gotten off the train, rushed over with her luggage to pay the fee. She enrolled in the special class for selected graduates, with three days of training costing 590 yuan. The staff informed her that there would be only one teacher per day, and the classes would be held at the Hanting Business Hotel. When the candidate asked if the teacher was part of the question-setting group, "[Our teachers have previously served as members of the question-setting group, but not this year.]"

On the website of Lianchuang Shihua, among the introductions of the teaching staff, apart from the first two who are clearly labeled as "[having participated in question setting and grading]," most of the so-called chief experts are noted as "[question-setting research experts]." An insider who once worked at a training institution told this reporter that the so-called experts mentioned by the training institutions are mostly university professors working part-time. According to the reporter's investigation, there are many reseller outlets around universities. From February 25th to March 21st, within just one month, seven courses were arranged, including intensive lecture classes, crash courses, and agreement training camps, with teaching times ranging from two to seven days. Prices range from four or five hundred yuan to ten or twenty thousand yuan. However, none of them specify the instructors or locations, "[the specific location and teachers are yet to be determined. We have four or five hundred teachers, traveling across the country, whoever is available will teach. As for the location, we will notify you the day before.]"

The "number game" between 10,000 and 4,000 yuan

Among the various types of training courses, the agreement training camp, also known as the closed class, is the most expensive. Taking a platinum full-process agreement class offered by a certain training institution as an example, before the class begins, the training institution signs a passing agreement with the student, priced at 15,990 yuan. If the student fails the written exam, 10,000 yuan will be refunded; if the interview is not passed, 8,000 yuan will be refunded. Due to the limited enrollment in such closed classes, they are highly sought after by students. "[If you register now, you can only get into the third session, and there are already more than a dozen people signed up.]"

Is the high tuition worth it? Industry insiders say that training classes costing tens of thousands of yuan do not fundamentally differ from other classes. The same teachers and materials are used, the only difference being the limitation on the number of students. The agreement is more like a gimmick. In fact, this is true. A teacher from a reseller outlet revealed to the reporter, "[students paying 10,000 yuan and those paying 4,000 yuan attend the same classes, taught by the same teacher.] In the detailed course fee table sent by Lianchuang Shihua to the students, the price of the training camp is divided into two types: one costs 3,980 yuan, and the other 9,980 yuan, signing a refund agreement, with no fees charged if the student does not pass. The reporter calculated that if the student does not pass, 7,500 yuan of the 9,980 yuan tuition will be refunded, equaling 2,480 yuan, which is 1,500 yuan less than the 3,980 yuan paid by other students. Wouldn't the training institution lose money? An industry insider exposed the truth to the public: some training classes promise to only charge auxiliary fees during the training period for students who fail, fully refunding large tuition fees, essentially making those who pass exams pay for those who fail, with the training institution always profiting.

The "watered-down" suspicion of high pass rates

According to the reporter's understanding, generally six months before the civil service exam, major training institutions start visiting various universities. They attract candidates through free trial classes, distributing free review materials at the end of the trial. Some even creatively hold lottery events where winners receive free courses. Such large-scale marketing campaigns focus on the subsequent advertising effect. A student registering for a crash course at Qingdao University said, "[my senior brother took this class when he took the exam, so I came here.]"

High pass rates are undoubtedly one of the most important self-promotional elements in free trial classes, extremely tempting to candidates. Staff at Lianchuang Shihua claim a pass rate of over 50%, while Huatu staff boast an interview pass rate of 70%.

Are these high pass rates really accurate? According to publicly available data, in 2011, 1.3 million people nationwide passed the registration review, competing for 18,000 positions in the "national exam," with a ratio of applicants to hires reaching 73:1, and a hiring rate of only 1.38%. The application-to-hire ratio for individual positions was as high as 4124:1. This is far from the 50% pass rate claimed by national exam training institutions.

"[Every day is mechanical training, the two sentences the teacher repeats the most are don't be nervous and don't use filler words.]" Mr. Fu, who is now a civil servant, does not look favorably upon such training institutions, "[the effect varies greatly depending on the individual, the key still lies with the person themselves.]"

Irregular "training" under the "regulatory vacuum"

"[Even though my preparation is okay, I still need to enroll in a class to feel secure. Whether it works or not is secondary, it's just for psychological comfort.]" A student registering for the training expressed the sentiments of some candidates.

The reporter learned from the Examination Center of the Qingdao Municipal Education Bureau that this department has never organized a civil servant training class, nor has it commissioned any organization to do so, and there are no designated textbooks. It is understood that authoritative materials for civil service exams only include the examination outline and past real questions. So-called "internal lecture notes" from various training institutions are merely simulation practice questions based on these materials.

Compared to the self-compiled textbooks for the written exam, the situation with interview training classes is even more concerning. Mr. Fu enrolled in a currently popular agreement closed class, "[the simulated interview was just interactive simulations between students, with only one teacher, who couldn't possibly handle everything, roughly checking in every two days.]" Mr. Fu recalled. Meanwhile, the training institutions organize a set of response frameworks for various interview questions. "[For example, if the examiner asks you to analyze the bucket principle, your answer must cover discussing the question itself, discussing the theory behind the question, and discussing its implications for yourself.]" Wang Shanshan expressed her concerns, "[after attending the training class, students can basically fluently recite several such answers, but this results in everyone giving identical responses.]"

The reporter successively learned from the city's commerce and pricing departments that there is no regulation or price control for such training institutions. A relevant official from the city's Department of Education told the reporter that training classes belong to private educational institutions, subject to local management. However, the headquarters of these training institutions are all located outside the region, leaving local supervision effectively blank.
