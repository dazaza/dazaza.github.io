Today, I will introduce a new ad service provider to you all, AdToll. There are currently few introductions to AdToll domestically, but everyone can easily understand AdToll's ad model: AdToll helps you make money by selling ad spaces. If you still don't quite understand, then think of it this way: AdToll is the international version of Ali Mama.

Making money by selling ad spaces through AdToll

Yes, AdToll is an ad service provider similar to Ali Mama. You can sell your ad spaces on AdToll, and if others find your ad space suitable, someone will come to buy it.

AdToll also sells ad spaces on a weekly basis. For example, the default price for each ad space per week is $0.5; if you think the price is too low, you can set your own price. However, one ad space on AdToll can host multiple ads, rotating them for display. If your ad space allows up to 10 ads to be displayed, then your weekly income from this ad space would be 10 * 0.5 = $5, which is not bad at all.

AdToll also allows you to set backup ads. That is, when no one buys your ad space, you can set your own ads to generate income. For example, you can use your AdSense ads as backup ads, ensuring a baseline income when no one buys your ad space.

In cases where there are no backup ads and the ad space is not fully sold, AdToll will display click-based ads. As long as netizens click on your ads, you can also earn ad revenue.

It can be said that AdToll basically has all the functions that Ali Mama (Alimama) possesses. Therefore, for most netizens who are familiar with Ali Mama, mastering AdToll is quite easy.

Requirements for websites using AdToll

AdToll requires publishers to use their own domain names. Websites using free domain names and shared domain names, including blogs provided by blog service providers with second-level domain names, will not be accepted by AdToll.

Also, websites participating or planning to participate in "paid reading", "paid clicking", pyramid schemes, and similar plans are not allowed to participate in the AdToll program.

AdToll's ad code is simple; as long as HTML code can run, you can place AdToll ads.
