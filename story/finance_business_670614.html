The mysterious and long-standing historical background of Anji White Tea is a highlight in its brand building.

Anji White Tea has actually been around for a long time as a variety of white tea, but it was lost at one point and rediscovered again in the 1980s. Once we understand its origins, white tea can be named correctly and appropriately, specifically Anji White Tea.

In Emperor Huizong of Song's "Da Guan Cha Lun," it says: "White tea is a kind on its own, different from regular tea. Its stems spread out, and its leaves are bright and thin. It occasionally grows between cliffs and forests, something that cannot be achieved by human effort. There are only four or five families who produce it, with no more than one or two plants each, making only two or three catties. ... Imperially bestowed white tea thus became the first renowned tea."

Although Emperor Huizong was not a good emperor, he had rare literary talent and artistic temperament. His specialized work "Da Guan Cha Lun" also holds a high historical status. So, is the white tea mentioned here really today's Anji White Tea? As one of the six major types of tea, white tea is produced in many places besides Anji, such as Fujian, Anhui, and Shengzhou.

In this regard, experts conducted serious and rigorous research and confirmed that Anji White Tea is a different concept from Bai Hao Yin Zhen and Bai Mu Dan in the "white tea category" of China's six major tea types. Bai Hao Yin Zhen and Bai Mu Dan refer to white teas made from green and hairy young leaves, where the whiteness comes from processing; whereas Anji White Tea is a famous green tea made from the white young leaves of a special white leaf tea variety using green tea production methods, where the jade-like color comes from the variety itself. Anji White Tea is both a rare variety of tea plant and a prestigious name among white teas.

Experts further concluded that Anji White Tea is indeed the white tea mentioned in Emperor Huizong's "Da Guan Cha Lun." The origin of Bai Hao Yin Zhen in Fuding, Fujian, dates back to 1796. Therefore, in terms of time, Anji White Tea predates the white tea of China's six major tea categories by nearly seven centuries.

With the opportunity provided by history and time, and recognized by experts and scholars in China's tea studies community, Anji White Tea officially established its brand and name in 2002. Since then, with its historical and cultural heritage, Anji White Tea has become much more confident and renowned than before.

The depth of brand culture is hard to come by, and the Anji government has seriously engaged in its construction and accumulation. In April 2001, the "2001 China - Anji White Tea Festival," hosted by the Chinese Anji White Tea Price Society, the Anji White Tea Price Research Institute of the Chinese Academy of Agricultural Sciences, and the Zhejiang Provincial Department of Agriculture, and organized by the Anji County People's Government, was grandly held in Anji. Anji County was named the "Hometown of Chinese White Tea" by the Organizing Committee of the Publicity Campaign for Famous and Special Hometowns of China. Currently, white tea has become one of the main contents of Anji's annual ecological cultural festival. In 2002, the first white tea cultivation and grafting took place in Xilong Township, the main production area of white tea, where the White Tea Fairy Site Park was built, and the White Tea Theme Park was constructed in Dipyu, the seat of Anji County. In 2003, the Anji White Tea King was awarded the title of "White Tea Ancestor," and the White Tea Valley was developed into an Anji tourist attraction.
