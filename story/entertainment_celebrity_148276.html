Name: Li Wenhua

Gender: Male

Occupation: Crosstalk actor

Born in Beijing in 1927.

Entered Xingping Machinery Factory as a spray painter in 1946.

Voted as a national model worker in 1956, and served as a representative of the third and fourth sessions of the Beijing Municipal People's Congress.

Transferred to the China Broadcasting Art Troupe Storytelling Group in 1962.

Diagnosed with laryngeal cancer in 1983.

Underwent a total laryngectomy in 1985, lost his ability to speak, and was forced to leave the stage he loved dearly.

Voted as one of the top ten comedians among national crosstalk actors in 1985.

Passed away on May 9, 2009.

A simple and unadorned name, yet one that countless people will remember. On the stages of the eighties, with him and Jiang Kun, there were many heartfelt laughs that lingered in the ears of people for a long time. The performances of the Jiang and Li duo were filled with the youthful passion of young people, while on Li Wenhua's wrinkled face was the innocent and carefree smile of a child. Each of the famous crosstalk comedians who specialized in supporting roles had distinct characteristics: Guo Qiru's kindness, Zhao Shizhong's steadiness, Zhao Yan's cheerfulness, Tang Jiezhong's honesty, and Li Wenhua, the people specially gave him the term "quiet joke," which was a great honor for him. This unremarkable-looking old man hid his wit within his quietness; his slow, listless speech and his ordinary-sounding voice would unexpectedly burst forth with sparks of humor. Li Wenhua was born for the art of crosstalk "quietly," and only within the extraordinary world of crosstalk could the quiet Li Wenhua shine brightly.

The renowned crosstalk comedian Li Wenhua passed away due to chronic cor pulmonale (pulmonary heart disease) at Beijing Chaoyang Hospital at the age of 82. Li Wenhua was diagnosed with throat cancer in 1983 and underwent a total laryngectomy in 1985, losing his ability to speak and being forced to leave the stage he loved. Starting tomorrow, there will be three days of mourning at Li Wenhua's home. Address: Building 3, Unit 5, Shiyun Haoting, east of Shuangjing Bridge.
