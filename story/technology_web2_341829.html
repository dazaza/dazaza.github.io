The humor of the hotel lady (don't laugh too hard)

by yanshan731 on 2010-04-07 21:42:14

Taking colleagues to a banquet outside the city is always good. The local colleagues were very hospitable, and that evening they hosted a dinner in a private room at a special hotel. More than ten people sat down and immediately began chatting non-stop, while only one person was ordering the dishes. After finishing the order, this person asked the waitress for confirmation: "The dishes are all set. Is there anything else you'd like to add?"

In Nanjing, we usually ask the waitress to repeat the dishes that have been ordered. So, one of the Nanjing guys said: "Miss, please repeat the dishes."

The waitress glanced at him but didn't respond.

"Miss, could you please repeat it!" The guy urged her again.

The waitress's face turned pale, but she still didn't move.

"What? Can't you just repeat it?" The guy got a little impatient.

A female colleague quickly intervened: "Miss, could you just quickly repeat them?"

The waitress hesitated and asked: "Is repeating allowed... or only reporting?"

"Poof!" A colleague beside her sprayed the tea he had just sipped all over the person behind him. Everyone burst into laughter, leaving the waitress even more flustered.

When the dishes started arriving, one of the first was a plate of cold noodles with various seasonings and sauces on the side. As the waitress wasn't careful, a drop of sauce fell on a guy's pants. This guy decided to play around and asked the waitress with a poker face: "What should we do now?"

The waitress calmly replied: "Anything works."

"So what do you suggest we do?"

"You decide what to do."

"How do you usually handle this kind of situation here?"

"Shall I help you out?"

"That would be great."

Then the waitress swiftly mixed all the seasonings and sauces directly onto the cold noodles, grabbed chopsticks with one hand and a spoon with the other, and stirred a few times. She then turned to the guy and said: "Sir, your noodles are ready, you can eat now."

The guy stared at the dish of noodles without saying a word, while another colleague helped defuse the tension by apologizing to the waitress.

Next came the roasted lamb leg, a large plate of meat bones with a small dish of salt and pepper. One of the Nanjing guys loved this dish and picked up a lamb leg without hesitation, taking a big bite and chewing loudly. The waitress saw this and said:

"Sir, this dish is supposed to be dipped before eating."

The guy looked at the waitress uncertainly, then glanced at his colleagues. A local colleague confirmed: "Dipping makes it taste better."

So the guy stood up holding the lamb leg, took another bite, and chewed loudly again. The waitress quickly approached and asked: "Sir, do you need anything?"

"Huh? No, I don't."

"Please stand up while eating this."

The guy mumbled as he stood up, looking at the waitress confusedly. He carefully brought the lamb leg close to his mouth and bit cautiously.

The waitress repeated: "Sir, this needs to be dipped before eating."

The guy suddenly stood up, waving the lamb leg around and shouting cheerfully: "You want me to sit and eat, then you want me to stand and eat. How exactly am I supposed to eat!?"

As the feast continued, the leader arrived late.

Everyone stood up to greet him warmly.

One of the waitresses serving the table seemed relaxed, perhaps due to her lack of experience.

Someone called out: "Miss, bring some tea!"

The waitress lazily pointed with her finger: "1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7 – seven people!"

Everyone chuckled. The leader added: "Real tea!"

The waitress counted again: "7, 6, 5, 4, 3, 2, 1 – still seven."

Someone asked: "What are you counting?"

The waitress hesitated for a moment and whispered: "People or dogs."

Everyone laughed heartily and shouted: "Call your manager over!", and the manager came running, bowing apologetically and asking: "What can I do for you, gentlemen?"

The leader said: "Don't bother answering. Go check the age and zodiac sign of this young lady."

The manager was puzzled but followed the order, returning shortly with: "18 years old, dog sign!"

The leader burst into laughter, and everyone followed suit. The leader, being generous, decided not to pursue the matter further, and others, also generous, let it slide.

Both the waitress and the manager were left utterly confused.

After several rounds of drinks, another dish arrived: "Clear-stewed turtle!"

Everyone was worried, yet forgot the rules. Someone used their chopsticks to poke the turtle's head and said: "Leader, please start, Leader, please start!"

The leader looked at the trembling turtle's head with displeasure, neither wanting to spoil the directness of his words nor wanting to ignore the goodwill of the group. Thus, he picked up a spoon, ladled some soup, and said: "Good, good! Please, help yourselves."

Another person remarked: "For turtles, it's best to just drink the soup!" which almost made the leader burst out laughing.

Not long after, as the soup was about to finish, something square surfaced. Someone asked: "Miss, what is this?"

The waitress casually replied: "Turtle egg."

Everyone cheered again: "Let the leader eat last, let the leader eat last!"

Hearing the phrase "unlucky", the leader was quite pleased and called the waitress: "Divide it among us!"

After a long pause, the waitress didn't move, prompting the leader to ask happily: "What's wrong? Can't you divide it properly?"

The waitress, embarrassed, said: "Seven people, six turtle eggs. How do you expect me to divide it?"

After hearing this, everyone bent their necks, widened their eyes, and ate heartily, finding it hard to suppress their laughter.

If you're laughing, share it with others so they can laugh too.
