There are seven tips for diabetic foot care.

Why does diabetic foot occur [http://www.tangzk.com/]?

Diabetic foot lesions are one of the chronic complications of diabetes. Why do diabetic patients develop foot lesions? The main reasons are threefold:

First, peripheral neuropathy caused by diabetes can make the feet lose sensation, becoming insensitive to hot, cold, or pain. For example, burns from hot water or injuries from foreign objects may go unnoticed, leading to easy ulceration and infection.

Secondly, vascular disease leads to local ischemia and poor circulation, especially in the toes. When the arteries supplying blood become blocked, it results in foot gangrene.

Thirdly, infections act as a trigger for diabetic foot lesions. For instance, minor trauma, scalds, bleeding from cutting nails too short, or improper treatment of athlete's foot can lead to infections. Once infected, the infection can rapidly spread, and in severe cases, it can spread to nearby bones, causing toe osteitis or osteomyelitis.

Why are the elderly more prone to this condition?

In younger patients, foot ulcers often occur on the soles, whereas in the elderly, due to dual vascular and neural lesions, ulcers are more common on the outer side and top of the foot. The progression of the lesion is faster. The most typical symptom of vascular disease is intermittent claudication, which means experiencing leg pain after walking a certain distance or climbing stairs, often forcing the person to stop and rest to relieve the pain. At this point, it indicates that the lower limb artery is at least 70% blocked, putting the patient at risk of foot gangrene. If pain persists even during rest, or worsens at night, it suggests that gangrene is imminent. If foot gangrene is not treated in time, it can progress to complete necrosis of the lower limb, necessitating amputation surgery.

How to prevent diabetic foot [http://www.tangzk.com/] lesions?

Firstly, diabetes should be actively controlled, with strict control of blood glucose levels, including a reasonable diet, use of hypoglycemic drugs. Most patients with diabetic foot [http://www.tangzk.com/] lesions will need insulin therapy, while also controlling high blood lipids and various factors that lead to early arteriosclerosis.

It is recommended to take action in seven areas: 1. Wash your feet daily with warm water to keep them clean and protect them from injury. 2. Regularly check if there are any cracks, blisters, redness, or pain on your feet. If any of these occur, seek medical attention promptly and do not handle it yourself. 3. Do not cut corns yourself or use chemical agents to corrode them; they should be handled by a doctor. 4. Trim your nails when they are soft after washing your feet, don't cut them too short or too close to the skin to avoid injury and subsequent skin infection. 5. Protect your feet well, wear suitable shoes and socks, change socks every day, choose socks with good water absorption and breathability, and check your shoes before wearing them to ensure there are no pebbles or other debris to prevent foot ulcers. 6. Avoid using electric blankets to prevent burns on the feet. 7. If the skin shows signs of flaking or dryness, moisturizing lotions can be used to protect the skin.
