Apple's new patent: automatically inform users of the way they should contact others

by geekzhang on 2013-05-03 19:02:30

According to AppleInsider, Apple has just obtained a patent for a technology that can automatically inform users of the best communication method to use when contacting others. For example, if you want to invite Xiao Ming to have dinner together in the evening, but he may be driving and unable to answer the phone, this new technology will remind you that it would be better to contact him via text message.

Of course, many other factors can affect this result: time, location, mobility, or other concurrent variables. Users can control the collection of these data themselves and allocate them in many different ways.

Firstly, through built-in GPS, cameras, microphones, and other monitors, the device can determine the user's location and environment. Based on these data, the device can tell those who want to contact the user which method is more reliable. For example, when you are in the office, your phone can automatically switch to work mode and tell others that it is best to contact you via email or text message. In addition to location, users can also choose their preferred method of contact with others. They can set this by synchronizing GPS and calendar data, or by categorizing contacts. For example, when you are having dinner with your family, you don't mind if your close friends call you at this time. However, if your colleagues try to call you, they will be advised to contact you via text message. Conversely, when you are having dinner with friends, the device will automatically adjust so that anyone can directly call you.

The core of this new technology lies in: this third-party system acts as a medium between the user and those trying to contact them. Your GPS, clock, and microphone will transmit data to the terminal. When people try to contact you, this system will automatically determine the optimal method of contact.
