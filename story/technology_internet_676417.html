[Working Principle]

Firstly, it is necessary to understand the working principle of the scale/car scale: The car scale/scale mainly consists of components such as load cells, weighing display instruments, weighing sensors (resistive strain type), connectors, limit devices, and junction boxes, as well as optional parts like printers, large screen displays, computers, and voltage stabilizers. The object to be weighed or the loaded vehicle is placed on the platform of the load cell. Under the force of gravity, the load cell transmits the force to the weighing sensor, causing the elastic body of the sensor to deform. The strain gauge bridge attached to the elastic body loses balance and outputs an electrical signal proportional to the weight value, which is amplified by a linear amplifier. Then, through A/D conversion, the signal becomes a digital signal, and the microprocessor (CPU) in the instrument processes the weight signal and directly displays the weight data. When configured with a printer, it can print records of the weighing data, and if configured with a computer, the measurement data can be entered into a computer management system for comprehensive management. Whether it's a scale or a weighing machine, the basic principle is to use a sensor (force sensor) to convert the weight of an object into an electrical signal, which is then sent to the conditioning circuit, A/D converter, and computing circuit (MCU), and finally converted into a digital display.
