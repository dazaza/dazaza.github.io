Method for calculating wallpaper usage (precise calculation)

There are various methods for calculating wallpaper usage. Below, using a specification of 70CMX10M, pattern distance of 53CM (parallel pattern), room perimeter of 11M, and room height of 2.6M as an example, we explain a commonly used calculation method.

(1) Actual length of each piece of wallpaper cut during construction = Room height / Pattern distance (take the larger whole number) × Pattern distance

That is: 2.6÷0.53=4.9 (taking the larger whole number from 4.9 gives 5)

So, the actual length of each piece of wallpaper cut during construction = 5×0.53=2.65m

(2) Number of pieces that can be obtained per roll = Roll length / Actual length of each piece of wallpaper (take the smaller whole number)

That is: 10÷2.65=3.77 (taking the smaller whole number from 3.77 gives 3)

(3) Total number of pieces required for the entire room = Room perimeter / Width of each piece (take the larger whole number)

That is: 11÷0.7=15.7 (taking the larger whole number from 15.7 gives 16)

(4) Total number of rolls required for the entire room = Total number of pieces required for the entire room / Number of pieces that can be obtained per roll (take the larger whole number)

That is: 16÷3=5.33 (the larger whole number should be 6 rolls)

The above calculation method simply explains the principle and method for calculating wallpaper usage. In actual construction, it is hoped that accurate calculations based on doors, windows, columns, etc., can be made to avoid adverse effects on construction.
