Lee's great dream and global strategic thinking

by seosh on 2008-03-29 18:59:25

Today, Shanghai SEO (SWJ) came across an article about an interview with the CEO of Baidu. I'd like to share it with everyone while listening to the beautiful voice of Mariah Carey. I feel there's something worth noting here. Let’s first take a look at the original interview:

Robin Li has a dream for a great nation.

This founder of Baidu who loves the documentary "The Rise of Great Nations" once compared Baidu to a rising great power, hoping that Baidu would become an international leader company. Under his leadership, Baidu, which achieved great success in the Chinese market through its localization strategy, is accelerating its steps to conquer overseas markets.

"Internationalization is an inevitable choice, and now is the right time." Robin Li said: "After 15 years, more than 50% of Baidu's revenue should come from outside China."

This is not the first time Robin Li has said this. Since 2002, Robin Li has proposed internationalization ideas at the company board meetings every year, but they were repeatedly rejected. Even by the end of 2006, when Baidu's success in China was hard to shake, doubts about its internationalization strategy still persisted.

Two years ago, Baidu began its steps towards internationalization. Last year, Baidu officially entered the Japanese market with a significant investment and continued to increase its input. Robin Li claimed that the European market will be Baidu's next target.

Most of the time, people see Robin Li as not a strong intervener. He appears introverted, calm, cautious, with meticulous thinking and good at listening to others' opinions. Usually, Baidu has nothing to do with adventure, but this does not prevent Baidu from becoming a flexible company. "Baidu started building its channel system in 2002, and at that time, it wasn't the strongest. It might have been difficult to say so even in 2004, but after 2005, no network product channel could rival Baidu." A Baidu employee said.

Robin Li wins with "popularization". Baidu's ace is to be good at launching popular products. Nowadays, among these new products, community features are becoming increasingly prominent, such as Baidu Zhidao, Baidu Baike, and Music Chief.

"The Internet changes rapidly, and users' habits and preferences change very quickly. Good products take time, and they need to be developed online. I will be the first experimenter of Baidu products," Robin Li said.

Economic Observer Report: What is the biggest difficulty Baidu encounters in the Japanese market?

Robin Li: When Baidu entered Japan, I once asked Mr. Nobuyuki Idei, the former president of Sony and currently an independent director of Baidu, for advice. He told me that the most important thing is to find the right people and the right team.

And Baidu's current biggest challenge is how to attract more excellent localized internet talents. This is not only a problem for Baidu Japan but also a difficulty faced by all search engine companies.

Since the end of 2006 when Baidu officially announced its entry into the Japanese market, we have always had great confidence in the Japanese market. In March 2007, Baidu started testing servers in Japan, conducting service tests for Japanese web pages, images, and video searches; on January 23, 2008, Baidu Japan officially began operations in Tokyo. We upgraded and revamped our existing web page, image, and video search services and launched the beta version of blog search. Currently, Baidu's operation in Japan has stabilized, and Baidu's investment in the Japanese market will continue to increase. In 2008, Baidu's investment in the Japanese market will reach $20-25 million.

Although Yahoo and Google currently occupy the majority of the Japanese search market share, they are not Japanese companies. Therefore, when Baidu competes with these companies in the Japanese market, it does not have any inherent weaknesses. (What is a localization strategy? It is to turn "local snakes into dragons", "turning passivity into initiative.")

Economic Observer Report: How to solve the talent issue? How to achieve localization?

Robin Li: In my view, internationalization is the process of achieving localization in the local market. Localization means deeply understanding a country's culture and habits and developing corresponding products and functions. Compared to American and European companies, Baidu's advantage in entering Japan lies in our greater respect for Japanese netizens' culture and habits.

On the other hand, for search engine companies, I think true localization refers more to product localization rather than technology localization. The R&D team may be in Japan, but the basic technology is still developed in Beijing. In Japan, what Baidu needs most right now is a product manager who decides how Baidu's products should be made and what they should look like. The localization of product talent is our path to achieving localization. They don’t need to know Chinese or English, just Japanese.

Economic Observer Report: Baidu has just launched instant messaging. What do you think it will bring to Baidu?

Robin Li: Every product and service that Baidu launches is based on user demand. The birth of instant messaging is also due to the one-on-one communication needs of Baidu's netizens.

Baidu has many products like Tieba and Zhidao. Netizens want to make friends in different spaces and communicate with them instantly. Baidu's instant messaging emerged under such circumstances.

In the eight-year development process of Baidu, we realized that solving the needs of hundreds of millions of netizens is not a simple problem. Therefore, our competitor is always ourselves. How to develop better products and services and comprehensively improve user experience is what Baidu needs to do.

Economic Observer Report: While expanding into international markets and entering new business areas, is Baidu facing a talent bottleneck?

Robin Li: What Baidu needs most is talent. Servers become obsolete after three years, and our office buildings are rented, none of these have real value. Talent is the real core competitiveness of internet companies.

Earlier, I said that I spend one-third of my time looking for excellent talent. Besides computer science and software engineering graduates, we also need a lot of talent in marketing, management, etc. And Baidu's talent acquisition goals are not limited to the Chinese market. Including commercial operations vice president Hao Yu Shen (former vice president of American Express), chief scientist William Zhang (former CTO of Infoseek), these are all senior talents that Baidu has recruited globally. I wonder if Baidu needs SEOers... ^_^

Economic Observer Report: What is Baidu's strategy in the mobile search field? What preparations has Baidu made for the 3G era?

Robin Li: Currently, the future of China's mobile search business is unclear. Although Baidu has launched WAP search and related businesses and established extensive cooperation with well-known international mobile terminal device manufacturers like Nokia, how to develop in the mobile search field in the future will depend on the development situation of the Chinese market and the needs of netizens.

Economic Observer Report: At the beginning of 2007, Baidu had 3000 employees, and by the end of the year, it had 6000 employees, with 300 having stock options. How to incentivize new employees?

Robin Li: Stock option income carries certain risks. Employees must share the risks with the development of the enterprise. During Baidu's entrepreneurial stage, giving employees certain stock options is a way of motivation. After eight years of development, Baidu has become a medium-sized company with 6000 employees and international influence, and it must adopt new ways to motivate employees to make greater contributions to the company.

On the one hand, Baidu's development prospects and corporate culture are still the main reasons why excellent talents join the company. Baidu has a famous "invincible red line". This steep upward red curve tracks the development curve of engineers who joined Baidu over the years, showing their rapid growth in ability, influence, and harvest far exceeding those of other famous multinational companies.

On the other hand, Baidu has also established a more internationalized human resources management system and training mechanism. For newly graduated college students, Baidu offers salaries up to 300,000 yuan. At Baidu, employees can freely debate technical issues with anyone, including myself, and immediately implement any new creative ideas.

Economic Observer Report: Outsiders often use the word "cautious" to describe you. Is that true?

Robin Li: Regarding the evaluations outsiders give me, I think it also relates to Baidu's character. Baidu's corporate culture is "simple and reliable", making fewer promises and fulfilling more. Being responsible for every word and action. Baidu's internet user coverage has reached 90%, and we still need to maintain caution, speak less, and do more. (Always keep a sense of crisis. Actually, everyone should be like this. Premier Wen Jiabao himself says he is someone with constant awareness of potential dangers. SWJ is no exception.)

Economic Observer Report: How do you view Baidu's performance in the U.S. stock market?

Robin Li: So far, I haven't reduced any of the original shares granted to me by Baidu.

Although the subprime mortgage crisis in the U.S. has greatly affected its stock market, the Chinese concept stocks on NASDAQ have also been affected. However, Baidu's internal operations have laid a solid foundation. Our revenue and performance are very stable, and we have continuously doubled our revenue for 12 quarters. In the future, we can also achieve rapid and steady development. When the stock market bubble disappears, Baidu will definitely be more anti-recessionary than other companies, and I am very confident about this.
