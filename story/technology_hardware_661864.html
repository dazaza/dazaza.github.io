How to Properly Use a Pipeline Pump

by chichuang55 on 2012-02-09 14:36:59

Precautions for using pipeline pumps: *During operation, the bearing temperature of the pipeline pump must not exceed the ambient temperature by 3℃. The maximum temperature must not exceed 80℃. *Add bearing lubricating oil to the bearing body of the pipeline pump. Observe that the oil level should be at the centerline of the oil gauge. Lubricating oil should be replaced or replenished in time. *Unscrew the priming plug of the pipeline pump body and fill with priming water (or slurry). *Close the gate valve and outlet pressure gauge on the discharge piping of the pipeline pump as well as the inlet vacuum gauge. *Test start the motor of the pipeline pump to check if the motor rotation direction is correct. *Start the motor of the pipeline pump. After the pipeline pump runs normally, open the outlet pressure gauge and inlet vacuum pump. After it shows appropriate pressure, gradually open the gate valve while checking the motor load situation. *Try to control the flow rate and head of the pipeline pump within the range indicated on the nameplate to ensure that the pipeline pump operates at the highest efficiency point, thereby achieving the best energy-saving effect. *If abnormal sounds are detected from the pipeline pump, stop immediately to check the cause. *When the pipeline pump needs to stop being used, first close the gate valve and pressure gauge, then stop the motor. *Check whether there is any looseness in the pipeline pump and piping connections. Rotate the pipeline pump manually to see if it runs smoothly. *Regularly check the wear condition of the pipeline pump shaft sleeve. If the wear is significant, replace it in time. *When using the pipeline pump in cold winter seasons, after stopping, open the lower discharge plug of the pump body to drain the medium completely to prevent freezing and cracking. *If the pipeline pump is unused for a long period, disassemble the entire pump, dry the moisture, apply grease to the rotating parts and joints, and store properly. *Within the first month of operation, change the lubricating oil after 100 hours. Thereafter, change the oil every 500 hours. *Frequently adjust the packing gland of the pipeline pump to ensure normal dripping in the packing chamber (preferably in drops).
