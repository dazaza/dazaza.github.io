DNA test identifies unknown Titanic baby boy

by vivianzhang101 on 2007-10-30 15:57:03

For nearly a century, the identity of the baby boy has been a mystery, the only child on the Titanic whose origins remained unknown.

According to an August 1 report by the UK's Daily Mail, the latest DNA test results from Canadian scientists have confirmed that the baby boy was named Sidney Leslie Goodwin. He came from Fulham, London, England and was only 19 months old at the time of his death.

Sidney was the youngest of eight in his family. They had all planned to travel aboard the Titanic to Niagara Falls, Canada, but tragically, none of them survived.

Sidney's father, Fred Goodwin, was 42 years old at the time. His mother, Augusta, was 43. The other five children were aged between nine and sixteen. After the maritime disaster, the bodies of Sidney’s family members, except for him, were never found.

Sidney was later buried in a cemetery in Halifax, Nova Scotia, Canada. For 95 years, his tombstone read "Unknown Child." Now, the baby boy finally has a name.

The Latest Conclusion

In fact, as early as 2001, scientists from Ontario, Canada, conducted DNA tests on the baby boy and announced in 2002 that they had solved the mystery of his identity.

They tested three teeth and a piece of bone from the baby boy. Then, by comparing the complete passenger list of the Titanic with verified information about victims, the scientists declared that the baby boy was Eino Panula, a 13-month-old Finnish child who had perished along with his parents and four brothers.
