Rapist Fled for 17 Years and Became a Billionaire

by ekjsje8js on 2011-11-26 10:05:39

LuoYang News (reporter Chen Jun, special reporter Chang Xiangyang, correspondent Yu Jie) - Recently, the Dongguan Police Station of the LuoYang Public Security Bureau arrested an escapee suspect, Zhang Mou, who had been on the run for nearly 17 years. At this point, despite being wanted, Zhang had become a tycoon in real estate and advertising with total assets amounting to several billion yuan.

After the "Clear Web Operation" intensified, the Dongguan Police Station of the LuoYang Public Security Bureau listed the fugitive suspect Zhang as one of the key targets for arrest and organized a special case team of police officers to carry out the operation.

Zhang is originally from XuChang City. Between 1986 and 1987, under the guise of participating in collective economic activities, he engaged in speculation and illegal trading with an illegal operating amount exceeding 120,000 yuan. Between 1985 and 1987, either by luring with money and material goods or promising job placements, he engaged in sexual relations with 14 underage girls, including 7 minors.

In 1990, Zhang was sentenced to death for three crimes, including rape, which was later commuted to suspended death sentence, and he was sent to serve his sentence at the Fourth Provincial Prison. In January 1995, during a medical parole, Zhang escaped.

After escaping, Zhang changed his name and over the following decade established companies in Zhengzhou, Beijing, Guangzhou, Hunan, and other places, engaging in real estate and advertising businesses. He now owns multiple fixed residences and personal assets worth several billion yuan.

During the "Clear Web Operation," the police officers from the Dongguan Police Station of the LuoYang Public Security Bureau conducted extensive and meticulous investigations into Zhang's activities, making multiple trips to PingDingShan, XuChang, NanYang, SanMenXia, and other cities for investigation purposes. On November 9th of this year, while conducting investigations in Guangzhou, the police finally managed to track Zhang’s work and living patterns, discovering that Zhang had assumed the alias Chen Mou and had built a successful identity as a businessman.

On November 14th, the arresting police officers secretly monitored multiple companies, residences, vehicles, family members, and movements related to Zhang. At approximately 17:50 that day, the police decisively arrested Zhang in a residential area in Zhengzhou. Justice may be slow, but it is inevitable. After nearly 17 years on the run, Zhang was finally caught.
