Polyacrylamide (abbreviated as PAM) is divided into anionic (HPAM), cationic (CPAM), and non-ionic (NPAM). It is a linear high molecular polymer and one of the most widely used varieties in water-soluble macromolecular compounds. Polyacrylamide and its derivatives can be used as effective flocculants, thickening agents, paper strength enhancers, and drag reducers for liquids, etc., and are widely applied in water treatment, papermaking, petroleum, coal, mineral processing, geology, light textile, construction and other industrial sectors.

1. Physical properties: Molecular formula (CH2CHCONH2)r. PAM is a linear high molecular polymer. It is easily soluble in water but almost insoluble in common organic solvents such as benzene, ethylbenzene, esters, acetone, etc. Its aqueous solution is nearly transparent viscous liquid. It belongs to non-hazardous products, non-toxic, and non-corrosive. Solid PAM has hygroscopicity, which increases with the increase of ionic degree. PAM has good thermal stability; it remains stable when heated to 100°C, but decomposes easily at above 150°C, producing nitrogen gas due to imidization between molecules and becoming insoluble in water. Density (g/ml) at 23°C is 1.302. Glass transition temperature is 153°C. PAM exhibits non-Newtonian fluidity under stress.

2. Usage characteristics:

- Flocculation: PAM can cause suspended substances to coagulate through electrostatic neutralization and bridging adsorption.

- Adhesion: It can act adhesively through mechanical, physical, and chemical actions.

- Drag reduction: PAM can effectively reduce the frictional resistance of fluids. Adding a trace amount of PAM to water can reduce resistance by 50-80%.

- Thickening: PAM has thickening effects under neutral and acidic conditions. When the pH value exceeds 10°C, PAM hydrolyzes easily, presenting a semi-network structure, thus enhancing thickening more significantly.

3. Brief introduction to the principle of PAM's action:

- Flocculation principle: The flocculation effect of PAM depends on the surface properties of the flocculated substances, especially zeta potential, viscosity, turbidity, and the pH value of the suspension. The zeta potential on the particle surface is the reason for particle aggregation resistance. Adding PAM with opposite surface charge can rapidly lower the zeta potential and cause coagulation.

- Adsorption bridging: PAM molecular chains are fixed on different particle surfaces, forming polymer bridges between particles, causing particles to aggregate and precipitate.

- Surface adsorption: Various adsorptions of polar groups on PAM molecules onto particles.

- Enhancement: PAM molecular chains connect the dispersed phase through various mechanical, physical, and chemical actions, forming a network, thereby enhancing the effect.

4. Main uses of polyacrylamide:

- Polyacrylamide (PAM) has a high molecular weight, good water solubility, adjustable molecular weight, and can introduce various ionic groups to obtain specific properties. Low molecular weight is an effective dispersant or stabilizer, while high molecular weight is an important flocculant.

- It can form hydrophilic but water-insoluble gels and has good adhesion to many solid surfaces and dissolved substances.

- Due to these properties, PAM is widely used in flocculation, thickening, drag reduction, gel formation, adhesion, scale inhibition, and other fields.

5. Technical indicators:

- Appearance: White granules

- Solid content: ≥88%

- Molecular weight: 6 million to 20 million

- Anionic: Ionic degree: 20%-30% (adjustable)

- Cationic: Ionic degree: 5-60%

- Non-ionic: Ionic degree: 1-4%

6. Method of use: PAM can be used alone for water treatment or in combination with PAC. However, stirring must be done separately. The amount of water added during dilution and the dosage should be determined according to each situation.
