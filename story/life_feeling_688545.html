Host Tian Yuan of "Day Day Up" was accused of betrayal

by chaojip57li on 2012-03-03 11:18:48

Recently, a netizen named "He Yangyang" posted on Weibo exposing that Tian Yuan, the host of Hunan TV's "Upward Everyday", had been cheating on his wife. Shortly after, the tipster also posted screenshots of ambiguous text messages suspected to be between Tian Yuan and herself. Although the tipster deleted the exposure post on the same night, Tian Yuan was still forced to make a "clarification" on Weibo and threatened to "resort to legal action and pursue it to the end." However, when the reporter found the new Weibo account of the tipster after she changed her identity, it was revealed that she deleted the Weibo post completely out of consideration for Tian Yuan's wife's feelings.

In the text message screenshots published by the netizen "He Yangyang", there exists an ambiguous relationship between her and a man suspected to be the host Tian Yuan. In the messages, "He Yangyang" questions the man: "Can you tell me what this means?" "What do you consider me as? Are you just playing around?" "So are we considered a one-night stand or is there any feeling involved?" "I just want to know if you're using me for a one-night stand." But the man keeps briefly dodging and replying with excuses like: "Let's talk later, okay?" "It's not like that." In another screenshot of the text messages, "He Yangyang" questions the man: "What does this mean? You even unfollowed me on Weibo." The man then replies: "I'm at home, it's inconvenient to talk. Let's talk over the phone tomorrow." She then resentfully replies: "Don't tell me you've hooked up with someone else already. This is hurtful."

Afterwards, the reporter called the phone number of Tian Yuan published in "He Yangyang"'s text messages, but the prompt tone indicated that the phone was turned off. The IP address showed that the phone belonged to Shijiazhuang, Hebei Province, which is exactly where Tian Yuan's hometown is located.

After the Weibo post went viral, many netizens expressed their surprise: "No way, Tian Yuan shouldn't be that kind of person, right?" Some netizens also questioned if it was a publicity stunt. On the same night, the netizen "He Yangyang" deleted the Weibo post and made significant changes to her Weibo profile, transforming into "HEYangYangGYS" and switching to a male profile picture. In the self-published photos, the reporter noticed that the girl has a petite and well-proportioned figure, and looks quite pretty. She appears much younger than Tian Yuan's wife, Zhao Nan. In a subsequent Weibo post, she subtly revealed her lingering feelings for Tian Yuan: "I once passed by your heart, not because I didn't want to stay, but because you wouldn't let me stay."

Just half an hour after this Weibo post, Tian Yuan issued a statement on his own Weibo: "Regarding the malicious release of photos and text messages on the Internet, I hereby angrily declare: the original owner of the mobile phone is my mother's fellow townsman. Recently, she contacted me through me to ask for tickets for the 'Upward Everyday' show. That day, due to lack of tickets, I took her inside the venue, but there was no further contact. I just learned that her phone was stolen, leading to today's selective quoting and fabrication of malicious information. Although I don't understand the intentions of the publisher, I hereby request them to immediately stop." He subsequently added: "Otherwise, I will resort to legal action and pursue it to the end."
