Hemophilia is a hereditary coagulation factor deficiency disease, due to the lack of coagulation factor VIII - commonly known as Factor Eight. Clinically, hemophilia is manifested as bleeding after joint and muscle trauma or spontaneous bleeding. If not treated in time, it can lead to extreme pain, joint damage, deformity, lifelong disability, and even intracranial and abdominal bleeding, seriously endangering life. This disease is carried for life but does not worsen; currently, there is no cure, and only replacement therapy can be adopted, i.e., injecting coagulation factors, to maintain a normal life.

Since 2007, Factor Eight has been in extremely short supply domestically. There are three manufacturers nationwide that can produce Factor Eight, but the supply of raw plasma has sharply decreased, and almost all blood products, including Factor Eight and albumin, face shortages.
