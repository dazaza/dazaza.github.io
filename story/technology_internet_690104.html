Badminton World Federation Super Series French Open - China wins two championships

by loved7w8 on 2012-03-04 23:23:16

Beijing, October 31 - The 2011 BWF Super Series French Open has come to an end, with the Chinese team winning two championships. Wang Xin and Wang Xiaoli/Yu Yang respectively won the women's singles and doubles titles, while Cai Yun/Fu Haifeng lost two consecutive sets after winning the first one, again falling to the Korean pair of Jung Jae Sung/Lee Yong Dae for second place; Xu Chen/ Ma Jin also fell in the mixed doubles final, missing out on the championship. Malaysian star Lee Chong Wei easily claimed the men's singles crown.

The head-to-head record between Cai Yun/Fu Haifeng and Jung Jae Sung/Lee Yong Dae in BWF competitions shows the Korean pair leading with 9 wins to 8 losses. In last month's China Masters final and last week's Denmark Open final, Jung Jae Sung/Lee Yong Dae both emerged victorious, marking the only two defeats for the Windstorm duo since the Singapore Open in June.

In the first set, the Windstorm duo took the lead early at 3-0, but Jung Jae Sung/Lee Yong Dae handled the serve and return well and focused on controlling the net, scoring five consecutive points after trailing 4-8 to take the lead. Cai Yun/Fu Haifeng quickly responded, taking two straight points to lead 11-10 at the mid-set break. After resuming play, the Cheng Long pair caught up with the score, but Cai Yun/Fu Haifeng successfully controlled the net, scoring six straight points to widen the gap. Thereafter, Jung Jae Sung/Lee Yong Dae were unable to mount a comeback, and the Windstorm duo took the first set 21-14.

The second set saw more intense competition between the pairs, with the lead changing hands multiple times, and Jung Jae Sung/Lee Yong Dae leading 11-10 at the mid-set break. After Cai Yun/Fu Haifeng leveled the score at 11-all, the Korean pair used continuous smashes to regain the lead. The Windstorm duo twice narrowed the gap to one point, but they were unable to equalize. After leading 18-15, Jung Jae Sung/Lee Yong Dae scored three straight points to win the set 21-15, leveling the match at one set all.

In the decisive third set, Jung Jae Sung/Lee Yong Dae capitalized on their momentum, quickly leading 8-3 and then switching courts leading 11-5. After the switch, Cai Yun/Fu Haifeng continued to play passively, while the Korean pair grew stronger with every fight, frequently scoring points with smashes and net play, leading by 16-6. The Windstorm duo recovered two points, but Jung Jae Sung/Lee Yong Dae promptly replied with three points to expand their advantage. At this point, Cai Yun/Fu Haifeng had no chance of recovery, losing the deciding set 11-21 and falling 1-2 overall, as Jung Jae Sung/Lee Yong Dae claimed their fourth Super Series title of the season, marking the third consecutive loss for the Windstorm duo against this Korean pair.

The women's singles final was contested between two Chinese players, with Wang Xin facing Li Xuerui. In their previous three encounters in BWF events, Wang Xin had always come out on top. From the start, Wang Xin quickly took the lead, and although Li Xuerui managed to tie the score at 8-all, 13-all, and 14-all, Wang Xin soon responded to re-establish her lead. After tying the score at 14-all, she scored four consecutive points to widen the gap. Li Xuerui recovered one point, but Wang Xin pressed on to win three straight points and took the first set 21-15.

In the second set, Wang Xin once again started with a lead, and Li Xuerui briefly led 11-10. However, after the mid-set break, Wang Xin returned to the court and scored six straight points to regain the lead. Thereafter, Li Xuerui struggled to catch up, narrowing the gap to just one point twice, but still unable to recover. With a final score of 21-19, Wang Xin won the second set 2-0 to claim the championship, marking her back-to-back Super Series victories over two weeks and her third Super Series title of the year.

The women's doubles final was another Chinese derby, with Wang Xiaoli/Yu Yang meeting Tian Qing/Zhao Yunlei a week after their last final encounter. The first set was fiercely contested, with frequent ties on the scoreboard. Wang Xiaoli/Yu Yang reached game point first at 20-18, but Tian Qing/Zhao Yunrei saved both game points. Thereafter, each side wasted two game points, tying the score at 24-all before Wang Xiaoli/Yu Yang seized the opportunity to win two straight points and took the first set 26-24.

In the second set, Tian Qing/Zhao Yunlei initially led 8-5, but Wang Xiaoli/Yu Yang quickly tied the score at 9-all. Both sides remained closely matched until 14-all, whereupon Wang Xiaoli/Yu Yang handled the serve and return better, scoring three consecutive points to take the lead. Tian Qing/Zhao Yunlei recovered one point, but Wang Xiaoli/Yu Yang scored four straight points to win the set 21-15, taking the match 2-0. This marked their fifth Super Series title of the year.

The mixed doubles final was a replay of the Denmark Open final from the previous week, with Xu Chen/Ma Jin having a head-to-head record of 2-1 against Nielsen/Petersen, though they had lost to them last week in Denmark. At the start of the final, both pairs exchanged leads several times, and after a 6-all tie, Nielsen/Petersen scored four straight points to take the lead. Xu Chen/Ma Jin narrowed the gap to one point, but Nielsen/Petersen quickly responded with four straight points to stabilize their position, maintaining their lead throughout to win the first set 21-17.

In the second set, Xu Chen/Ma Jin quickly established an early lead, but they were unable to widen the gap. After a 11-all tie, Nielsen/Petersen scored four straight points to establish their lead. Whether in smashes or net play, Xu Chen/Ma Jin performed worse than their opponents, allowing the Danish pair to maintain their advantage. After leading 17-14, they scored four straight points to win the set 21-14, taking the match 2-0 and claiming their second consecutive weekly title.

In the men's singles final, Lee Chong Wei increased his lead after a 12-all tie in the first set. After Tanaka Kenichi narrowed the gap to 16-17, Lee Chong Wei scored four straight points to win the set 21-16. In the second set, the quality of Tanaka Kenichi's defense declined, allowing Lee Chong Wei to score frequently with smashes. His control of the baseline was also clearly better than his opponent's, and after leading 8-6, the top seed scored nine straight points to secure victory, winning the set 21-11 and taking the match 2-0 to claim the championship. This marked Lee Chong Wei's fifth Super Series title of the year.
