Google Apps Cover 90% of US Smartphones, Apple Only Penetrates 44%

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 20:39:34

US tech market research company comScore released its Q3 report on the US smartphone user market on the 5th, showing that the US mobile phone market has become a "duopoly" - only Apple and Samsung are able to grow, while all others are declining. Additionally, comScore also published rankings for smartphone app coverage by brand and individual apps.

comScore's statistics measure the cumulative number of existing carrier mobile phone users (subscribers). The data shows that as of the end of September, Apple's iPhone accounted for 40.6% of US smartphone users, an increase of 0.7 percentage points compared to 39.9% at the end of Q2.

Apple's rival, Samsung, saw its user share grow from 23.7% at the end of Q2 to 24.9%.

Besides these two companies, all other top five manufacturers either declined or remained stagnant. HTC, ranked third, saw its user share drop by 1.4 percentage points, Motorola shrank by 0.4 percentage points, and LG, ranked fifth, remained unchanged.

In terms of mobile operating system shares, Android remains in first place in the US market with a share of 51.8%, declining quarter-over-quarter by 0.2 percentage points. Apple's iOS share increased by 0.7 percentage points, BlackBerry's share shrank by 0.6 percentage points, and Microsoft's WP grew slightly by 0.2 percentage points. However, the shares of BlackBerry, WP, and Symbian, ranked fifth, are not in the same league as Android and iOS.

comScore's statistics found that there are currently 149.7 million smartphone users in the US, with a market penetration rate of 62%, an increase of 4.5 percentage points compared to the end of Q2.

comScore also surveyed the 15 most popular mobile app brands among smartphone users, finding that Google apps can cover 90% of US smartphone users, Facebook ranks second with a coverage rate of 84%, Yahoo! apps have a coverage rate of 82.2%, and Amazon ranks fifth with 65.5%.

The most popular smartphone applications are Facebook, Google Play, the Google search client, and the YouTube client. Additionally, Pandora's music streaming service client ranks fifth with a penetration rate of 49.3%.

Apple's official apps rank seventh in the corporate list with a coverage rate of 44.2%. Furthermore, the smartphone coverage rate of Apple Maps is 25.6%, far lower than Google Maps' 43%.
