Sorry, I just want you to be happy.

by xiaoxiao67 on 2012-03-02 15:36:39

I'm sorry, I just want you to be happy.

Do you still remember? In elementary school, we often played the game of rock-paper-scissors together. I always habitually threw "rock", and you always won with "paper". Every time you won, the corner of your mouth would rise in a victorious curve as you laughed at my foolishness for not changing my gesture and always throwing "rock". After losing to you, I called you Big Brother Rock. Looking at your smug face, I said dumbly: As long as you're happy, I'm willing to be your rock.

Later, we went to middle school together and were classmates. Because I was tall and you were short, the teacher didn't arrange us to sit together. You sat with a boy about your height, while I sat with a girl slightly taller than you. Your desk mate was very mischievous and domineering, always bullying you by drawing a "38-line" on the desk and not letting you cross it even a little bit. Sometimes when you accidentally crossed over by half a centimeter, he would push you hard, making you, who was so small and thin, unable to withstand his forceful push, and you slid off the chair onto the ground, crying bitterly. Brother, how could Rock bear seeing you bullied by other boys? Forgetting the class was in session, I suddenly rushed out of my seat, pulled your desk mate out of his seat, and shook my "rock"-like fist in front of him fiercely: If you dare to bully her again, watch out for my rock (fist). This action violated the classroom rules and we were both called to stand in a corner of the classroom as punishment, and of course, the boy who bullied you was also not spared.

After class, on the playground, you found me with tears in your eyes and said: Big Brother Rock, it's all my fault, causing you to be punished by the teacher. I smiled dumbly at you again: Who asked me to be Big Brother Rock? A rock has to protect you.

At this moment, your desk mate brought a few troublemakers from the class and surrounded me. I pulled you behind me and used my stone-wall-like body to shield your small and weak frame.

Ha ha, with your condition, you actually want to protect her? Let's see if rock is harder or iron head is harder?

Your desk mate then swung his iron fist at me, followed by countless fists raining down on me like a storm. I heard you shouting anxiously nearby: Stop hitting! Stop hitting! Please stop hitting! It will cost lives! Stop hitting! Please stop hitting! Being outnumbered, I was finally knocked to the ground. Little brat, with your condition, you still want to be a hero saving beauties? A group of people surrounded me, laughing wildly, and then walked away triumphantly. There's nothing wrong with being a hero; what's scary is that the hero doesn't fight back. I grabbed a small stone from the ground and threw it forcefully at his retreating figure. Relying on their numerical advantage, they returned and gave me another round of punches and kicks. Finally, you cried and called the teacher, stopping this war. You helped me up, took out a handkerchief, and wiped the blood from the corner of my mouth while asking painfully if it hurt? It must have hurt a lot, right? I still smiled dumbly and said: It doesn't hurt, rocks don't feel pain.

Afterwards, I was summoned to the school guidance office along with him and received a reward.

Do you still remember? After graduating from middle school, we both entered the same high school. Later, because of your consistently good academic performance, you were admitted to a key university in a big city, while I failed to get into even an ordinary university in the provincial capital and stayed in the countryside. Thus, fate separated us.

While studying in the big city, you often worried about home, thinking about your aging parents, your young siblings, and me, your Big Brother Rock who had always protected you.

Although I couldn't be with you every day like before, we still kept in touch through frequent letters. You said you couldn't let go of your aging parents and young siblings. I said, don't worry, I'll often visit your house and take care of them. You said, how could I do that? I said, there's nothing to it. We've always been good neighbors, our homes are close, so just focus on your studies and after graduation, get a good job. Why am I called Stone? For you, I'm willing to be your stepping stone.

To allow you to finish university more peacefully and become someone extraordinary sooner, bringing glory to our small village, I took care of all the household chores. During harvest and planting seasons, your parents didn't have to work in the fields, and I took care of picking up and dropping off your younger siblings every day. Unlike before, we no longer exchanged letters frequently. Every day, I deeply buried my longing for you in the deepest part of my heart, just so it wouldn't affect your studies.

Time flew by, and finally, you graduated from university and got a great job. Now grown up, beautiful and poised, you've reached marriageable age. Your parents found you a well-matched rich man and wanted you to marry early, fulfilling their wishes and providing your family with support. But you were still concerned about me, believing that without my care for your family all these years, you wouldn't have achieved what you have today. So, you never agreed to this arranged marriage.

I know, I've liked you since we were kids, and I've loved you ever since I grew up. But I can't be too selfish. You're so excellent, so beautiful, while I'm just an unknown but pretty rock, incapable and unaccomplished, not worthy of you, unable to give you a comfortable and leisurely life. Therefore, I can't be too selfish. Since I can't give you a happy life, I shouldn't prevent you from enjoying the happiness you deserve. Because I'm your Big Brother Rock, because I told you when we were kids that as long as you're happy, as long as you're happy, I'm willing to be your silent rock.

After secretly wiping away my tears alone, I told you, just agree to the marriage your parents arranged for you. Don't worry about me. Your future husband is very excellent, I believe he can bring you happiness. Don't worry about my parents, they've already arranged a marriage for me. My future wife is very good to me and my parents. I'm sorry, I didn't tell you while I was still in university, fearing it would affect your studies.

There's a kind of love that is cruel. I don't know how you feel about hearing this. I don't know if you'll hate me. Regardless of anything, as long as you're happy, as long as you can live a happy life, that's all that matters. Regretfully, you finally agreed to the marriage your parents arranged for you.

Do you know? On the day of your wedding, I transformed into a quiet little stone by the door. When the joyous bridal procession came, when you blushed red, smiling happily as you walked towards your new husband, by the roadside, I bloomed the most beautiful flower in the world for you, wishing you eternal happiness! Wishing you eternal joy!
