58 square meters -- the cool cozy nest of the SOHU tribe

by shichuang on 2011-08-13 10:15:38

Archives of Elegant Rooms

Usable area: 53 square meters

Apartment features: one bedroom, one living room, one bathroom

Decoration style: simple

Resident population: young SOHO couple

Project cost: 30,000 RMB (excluding main materials)

Project duration: 2 months

Main decorative materials: Walls: real stone paint, wallpaper

Floors: metal glazed tiles, PVC stone floor, wood floor

Ceilings: waterproof gypsum board

Cool and cozy nest for the SOHO crowd

Consultation phone: 132 6411 2782

This unique case was designed for a young SOHO couple: the male owner is an expert in film and television production, enjoys collecting Transformers, limited edition Coca-Cola bottles, practical decoration, as well as various film and television posters, and is also an enthusiast and DJ of electronic music; the female owner works in animation design, and the dolls covering the walls are her favorite.

When considering the space for such owners, the designer faced a building from 1897, not much younger than the owners themselves. Therefore, the redivision of space was necessary:

Firstly, the designer focused on the renovation of leisure and work areas to meet the needs of both owners working at home. On this basis, the dining area can be weakened because the owners don't cook or eat at home often. Finally, the designer also helped expand the comfort and convenience of the bathroom.

In terms of interior decoration, the designer used more black and white as the overall tone of the residence: the black metal glazed tiles in the dining room, the black imitation steel plate PVC stone floor in the living room, and the white real stone paint used on all walls, which created rich visual changes in the originally strongly contrasting black and white colors.

Considering the practicality of this case, the designer used red as an accent color in the overall color coordination, bringing vitality to the cold space. The white furniture blends with the white walls, making the originally small space appear spacious due to its tidiness.

The choice of classical window pattern wallpaper in the bedroom softens the resting space while maintaining a unified overall style. The pivot door between the living room and the bedroom adds fun to the entire space.

In terms of furniture selection, the designer chose minimalist Nordic style designs, which better meet the owners' needs for fashion and modernity.
