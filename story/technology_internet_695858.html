Post-80s police officer's original song in memory of fallen comrades moves netizens

by yans281mxue on 2012-03-07 17:35:42

Peng Pei is an ordinary police officer in Lankao County and also a member of the post-80s generation. Last year, one of his comrades died in the line of duty, and at the farewell ceremony, the large number of local people who came spontaneously deeply touched him. As a result, he created the song "Because I Am a Policeman" and posted it on Weibo.

This song moved many netizens and also touched the hearts of policemen. An old traffic policeman in Zhengzhou exclaimed after listening to it: "Serving the people, being tired doesn't matter because we are policemen."

Reported by Henan Business Daily's Li Zheng

The song was well-received and moved many netizens

"To be a policeman, I want to protect my country; the reliance of the people makes me feel proud inside; to be a policeman, no matter the wind or rain, no matter how hard it is, I am not afraid because I am a policeman..."

Yesterday afternoon, the reporter found Peng Pei's Sina personal Weibo account. On the afternoon of April 1st, Peng Pei released the MV for "Because I Am a Policeman" here. Currently, his "fans" have exceeded twenty thousand.

This song was not only enthusiastically forwarded by many netizens but also included by several music websites. In the song's comments, most netizens said they were "deeply moved." Netizen "56" commented: "It sounds good and moving. Being a policeman is not easy. After hearing this song, I think the work of the police is very sacred and arduous. Wish good people peace all their lives! Listening to this makes me want to be a policeman!"

On the morning of April 3rd, Peng Pei's latest Weibo post read: "As Qingming Festival approaches, as a grassroots policeman, I pay my highest respects to all the heroes across China who have sacrificed their precious lives for the health and safety of the people! It is you who have exchanged your lives for the peace of thousands of homes, and you will forever live in the hearts of the people!"

Colleagues

"Serving the people, being tired doesn't matter"

Yesterday, an old traffic policeman in Zhengzhou who had worked for ten years particularly liked one sentence from the song "Because I Am a Policeman."

"Shuttling through streets and alleys, shining with brilliance, this is what I have chosen for the glory in my heart." The old traffic policeman said that he believed being a policeman was something to be proud of, "Serving the people, being tired doesn't matter because we are policemen."

"It's normal for shirts to be soaked, sometimes when clenching fists, gloves can squeeze out sweat," the old traffic policeman said. Although riding motorcycles under scorching sun or snowstorms, shuttling through streets and alleys, every time he sees smooth traffic and resolved accidents, he feels gratified.

A police officer from Miaoli Police Station also agreed: "This song is a true portrayal of us policemen. It's impressive that a post-80s policeman could write such an emotional song, and I am proud of him."

Dialogue

Creating this song,寄托 my grief

Regarding some questions netizens were concerned about, yesterday afternoon, Henan Business Daily reporters connected with Peng Pei.

Reporter: I heard you're a post-80s individual, introduce yourself first.

Peng Pei: Hello everyone, I am Peng Pei, from Kaifeng, Henan Province, 24 years old, an ordinary police officer in the Publicity Department of the Lankao County Public Security Bureau, and also a member of the post-80s generation.

Reporter: What made you think of creating this song initially?

Peng Pei: In March 2010, a deputy director of the Lankao County Public Security Bureau died on the way back to Lankao after finishing a case. At his farewell ceremony, many locals came voluntarily, and I saw many people crying, following his hearse all the way, which deeply touched my heart.

At that moment, I had an idea to write a song for those policemen who died in the line of duty, to express my grief. Over the next year, I used my spare time to create "Because I Am a Policeman."

Reporter: The singing, lyrics, and composition of the song were all done by you alone. Have you received any musical training before?

Peng Pei: I just like music, and I'm quite amateur. When I was in kindergarten, my family enrolled me in a guitar class, where I fell in love with music. Later, I also formed a band. Being a policeman is my job, but music is my hobby.

Reporter: Many netizens commented that this song is very touching, what do you think?

Peng Pei: Even now, when I listen to this song that I composed myself, I still feel deeply moved because it embodies my profound respect for the profession of a policeman.

My father is a policeman, and watching his busy figure since childhood inspired me to become a policeman. In late 2007, I became a People's Policeman. More than three years of police work has let me truly understand the burdens and responsibilities of frontline policemen, but just as I wrote in my song, "the reliance of the people makes me feel proud inside."
