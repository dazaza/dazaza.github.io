Qu Qiubai said of himself that he was "a person unsuited to being an official, who in a wrong period of history became an inevitably error-prone leader." His literary talent had impressed many people. Zheng Zhenge, who would later become a cultural history expert and Vice Minister of Culture of the new China, was preparing for his wedding at that time and wanted Qu Qiubai to engrave a pair of seals. Qu's fee was 50 yuan. Unable to afford it, Zheng turned to Mao Dun. On the day of the wedding, Qu Qiubai arrived with a handkerchief-wrapped small package, saying he had come to present 50 gold coins. Zheng was greatly alarmed, but upon opening the package, he found two stone seals instead.

Scholars of the Republic of China era in Beiping often developed a penchant for collecting old books, and this penchant was contagious. Once, Yuan Tongli, the director of the National Beiping Library, asked Jiang Tingfu to accompany him to a private collector’s place. They watched each other closely while looking for materials. Yuan asked Jiang which area he was particularly interested in. Jiang found two small booklets, one being "Wenxiang's Chronology," and the other about opium trading. Yuan seemed uninterested, and Jiang was privately delighted. After they parted ways, Yuan returned to the library, and Jiang went back to the club. Jiang then immediately returned to buy those two books. However, when he reached the seller's place half an hour later, the owner told him that Mr. Yuan had already beaten him to it and bought the books.
