Top 10 surprising transfers in football: Two Inter Milan stars leave

by seo1751 on 2012-03-08 11:38:19

Sina Sports News - The most captivating aspect of football for fans is undoubtedly the stunning goals, but off the pitch, transfer operations by major clubs often grab the attention of football enthusiasts. Below is a carefully curated list of the top ten surprising transfers in history, taking you back to those shocking moments that stunned the entire world.

10. Johnston (Celtic – Nantes – Rangers)

In 1989, Johnston moved from French side Nantes to Glasgow Rangers, but he had previously played for their fierce rivals, Celtic. Celtic fans despised his betrayal, and Rangers fans hated him for scoring many goals against them. This controversial transfer was not only about football but also involved religious factors. As a Roman Catholic, Johnston chose to join Rangers, who represented Protestants, breaking the clear religious divide between the two clubs at the time. He became the first Roman Catholic player to join Rangers. Some radical Rangers fans even refused to celebrate with him after he scored.

9. Cantona (Leeds United – Manchester United (Weibo))

In 1992, Manchester United signed Cantona from Leeds United for £1.2 million, shocking the football world. This could be considered one of the best deals in Ferguson's managerial career and one of the biggest mistakes made by then-Leeds United manager Howard Wilkinson. Later, Wilkinson recalled: "I called Ferguson just to inquire if Manchester United was interested in selling Dennis Irwin, but I don't know why, in the end, we ended up selling our most influential star, Cantona, to Manchester United." Leeds United has long since disappeared from the Premier League stage, while Cantona's outstanding performances and crucial goals helped Manchester United win the Premier League title four times in the next five seasons. Leeds United fans still can't understand why Cantona was sold to their arch-rivals.

8. Andy Cole (Newcastle – Manchester United)

Some transfer news seems fake, so people choose to ignore it. The rumor of Andy Cole moving to Manchester United fell into this category, but unlike other fake news, this one eventually came true. In January 1995, Andy Cole transferred from Newcastle to Manchester United, leaving everyone wondering why Newcastle sold their star striker to their main title rival. When Newcastle fans protested against the club’s decision, Kevin Keegan appeared on the steps of St James' Park to explain the rationale behind the transfer. “Look! We got Gillespie!” Okay, you got Kenny Gillespie. But may I ask, who is Gillespie?

7. Campbell (Tottenham Hotspur – Arsenal (Weibo))

Sol Campbell was a star nurtured by Tottenham Hotspur; he joined as an apprentice at 16 and moved to the first team at 18. After becoming famous, Spurs appointed him captain, and Spurs fans saw him as the hope for the club’s revival. In 2001, Campbell's contract was nearing expiration, and knowing they couldn’t keep their star forward, Spurs hoped Campbell would renew his contract first and claimed they wouldn’t interfere with his transfer. Spurs’ move was no doubt to get some transfer fee, but faced with waving checks, Campbell rejected Spurs and joined Arsenal, their North London derby rivals, as a free agent. Of course, during his four years at Arsenal, Campbell achieved great success, winning the Premier League twice and almost reaching the peak of Europe. However, transferring freely to a bitter rival during his prime, Campbell was destined to forever bear the name of traitor.

6. Ferdinand (Leeds United – Manchester United)

The former chairman of Leeds United, Peter Ridsdale, described Ferdinand most accurately as a "standard hypocrite." During the transfer scandal, Ferdinand cried in public, expressing his unwillingness to leave Leeds United, but secretly flirted with Manchester United, getting very close. Eventually, he forced the club to accept Manchester United's £30 million offer, and Ferdinand transferred to Manchester United for a record-breaking £31.1 million. Leeds United fans withdrew the award for the club’s season-best player to express their dissatisfaction with his defection. At the time, British media believed that Manchester United was crazy, calling it an "unbelievable" deal. However, Ferdinand's performance afterward was convincing; he formed the premier central defender duo in the Premier League with Vidic and later wore the England captain's armband. But when he looks back on his past, will he remember Leeds United, where he once cried and shouted that he didn’t want to leave?

5. Vieri (Inter Milan – AC Milan (Weibo))

It’s not easy to make Inter Milan fans fall in love with a player, but it’s easy to make them hate one—just have him transfer to AC Milan. Vieri was one of the best strikers in world football at the time. In the summer of 2005, Vieri parted ways with Inter Milan, and as Inter fans were lamenting his departure, no one expected that just a few days later, Vieri would wear the AC Milan jersey. Inter fans were utterly enraged, and extreme fans even smashed his car and restaurant. After transferring to Inter's arch-rival, Vieri said something that forever eliminated any chance of being forgiven by Inter fans. He said, "AC Milan is the best club I've ever played for in my career. And in the past, I mistakenly stayed with another team in Milan." After Vieri left, Inter Milan began to continuously win Serie A titles, while Vieri only won one Serie A title in his lifetime, at Juventus.

4. Beckham (Manchester United – Real Madrid (Weibo))

Beckham and Manchester United were once seen as a "perfect match," until February 13, 2003, when a flying shoe changed everything. After Manchester United lost 0-2 to Arsenal, in the dressing room, Ferguson kicked a shoe in the corner, which hit Beckham right in the face, causing blood to flow profusely from the studs. On June 17, 2003, Beckham transferred to Real Madrid for £25 million. It's hard to say whether Real Madrid brought in Beckham for the 90 minutes on the field, but after Beckham left, Manchester United's position as the "richest club in football" was taken over by Real Madrid. Meanwhile, Real Madrid officially ushered in the era of the "Galacticos."

3. Ronaldo (Inter Milan – Real Madrid)

After transferring from Barcelona to Inter Milan, Ronaldo's nearly perfect performances drove Milan into a frenzy, but he could never shake off the "demon" of injuries. During his time away from the field due to injury, Ronaldo received unprecedented care and fatherly encouragement from Moratti. Ronaldo gave Inter Milan his best, but he always waited for his first league championship. In the final round of the 2001-02 Serie A season, Inter Milan faced Lazio away. A draw would have secured their first league title in nearly ten years, but ultimately, Inter lost 2-4. The image of Ronaldo sitting on the bench covering his face in tears after being substituted became a lasting pain for Inter Milan fans. That was also his last appearance in blue and black. After shining brightly in the 2002 World Cup, Ronaldo transferred to Real Madrid for €45 million. The following year, Ronaldo won his first league championship trophy.

2. Roberto Baggio (Fiorentina – Juventus)

In the hearts of Italian fans, the pony-tailed Roberto Baggio has always been a symbol of purple. In the summer of 1989, out of "consideration for the team's economic interests," Fiorentina owner Count Pontello reached an agreement with Juventus owner Agnelli, and Baggio transferred to the Bianconeri for $12.8 million. When he learned of the news, Baggio, who deeply loved Fiorentina, tried to stay by approaching Pontello, even though joining Juventus would triple his salary at Fiorentina. Baggio said, "To stay in Fiorentina, I am willing to take a pay cut." But he couldn’t convince the money-hungry boss. Months later, Juventus challenged Fiorentina away. Representing Juventus, Baggio created a penalty but refused to take it. When he was substituted, all Fiorentina fans stood up to applaud him, and as he walked down, he picked up a Fiorentina scarf from the ground and kissed it. Later, Baggio said, "My heart will always be purple."

1. Luis Figo (Barcelona – Real Madrid)

In July 2000, Figo transferred from La Liga's Barcelona to their fierce rivals, Real Madrid. Figo was regarded by many as the best player in the world, and Real Madrid and Barcelona were considered the fiercest adversaries globally. Naturally, this transfer became the biggest news of that summer. Real Madrid president Perez's election largely depended on his verbal promise during the campaign to bring in Figo, who was still under contract with Barcelona. Ultimately, Figo transferred to Real Madrid for a record-breaking $56 million. In interviews, Figo stubbornly defended himself, saying, "Although Barcelona has always treated me well, I cannot neglect myself. I am very satisfied with this transfer and have no regrets." Naturally, Figo, who chose to oppose the world, "enjoyed" being pelted with lighters, water bottles, and the deafening cries of "Judas" from tens of thousands of Barcelona fans when he accompanied Real Madrid in an away game against Barcelona.
