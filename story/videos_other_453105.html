Following the continuous development of China's citizen economy, housing construction and abstracts are gradually moving towards the track of commodification. People's living environment will increasingly demand higher standards. We are requested to build a natural environment that integrates with the living environment on the spiritual and cultural level. Green building is based on the principle of harmony between construction and the environment as part of a benign cycle within the natural ecosystem, combining environmental factors, construction methods, appropriate technology, and design.

Keywords: Living Environment, Green, Design, Social and Economic Development

As people become more concerned about their living environments, residential areas have become a focus for our survival space and environmental design. Without a green environment, there would be no oxygen for human habitation. How to create a beautiful, peaceful, and comfortable living environment is a question every designer should seriously consider and plan. With the improvement of people’s lifestyles and living environments, more and more individuals are emphasizing the selection of houses in a new scope, stressing the landscape environment, the natural scenery, and the rich cultural landscapes within residential areas and their surroundings—whether it is green or dynamic.

This modern life injects new content into neighborhood environmental design and also imposes higher requirements on the design philosophy of these neighborhoods. Buildings without flowers can be accompanied by flowers during construction. Isn’t good foreign residential area greening an important component of urban green spaces, the most widespread, closest to the people, and most frequently used environmental space for residents? Green ecology plays an important role not only aesthetically but also in balancing the environment, making the air fresh, cool, and humid, preventing dust pollution, regulating light, temperature, humidity, and airflow, improving weather conditions, etc. Moreover, beautiful landscapes and environmental impacts bring joy while also eliminating urban noise, relieving physical and mental fatigue, and relaxing one's mood, thus creating a peaceful environment conducive to gardening activities, promoting residents' health, and playing a significant regulatory and exemplary role in improving urban ecological quality. Green housing can also be called sustainable ecological housing, meaning it does not destroy the fundamental conditions of the ecological environment; hence, it constructs a spatial environment that satisfies human health needs over a long period under the conditions of social and economic development.

Greening areas not only reduce energy consumption but also involve reducing water usage, minimizing material and resource utilization, decreasing waste, improving air quality, lighting, rainwater treatment and retention, replenishing groundwater, restoring the natural environment, and reducing dependence on vehicles, among other issues.

Green residential buildings promote economic development and social progress. The development of more green areas promotes the sustainable development of the economy and society. To create a good living environment that provides aesthetic enjoyment and cultivates moral character, providing society with a stable foundation, planners, designers, and architects must have a strong sense of social responsibility and duty. From an ecological perspective, green areas seek harmony and interactive development with the natural environment. Planners, designers, and architects must understand and respect the specific geographical environment, topography, climate, and unique characteristics of a particular humanistic environment to minimize existing ecological and cultural environmental damage.

Green residential design aims to minimize energy and other resource consumption, avoid generating waste and pollutants during construction processes, maximize recycling, and use reusable materials to reduce non-renewable energy consumption. It utilizes solar, wind, geothermal, and other green, clean energy sources. Optimizing outdoor space is a crucial factor in green residential design. Green residential areas consist of land covered with trees, flowers, and green plants. Green residential areas serve two functions: one is to establish outdoor living spaces that meet various leisure environment needs; the other is to create a natural environment using landscape elements to produce beautiful outdoor facilities. In addition to necessary embellishments in the residential area's outdoor environment, elements such as sculptures, water features, lights, tables, chairs, benches, ladders, railings, and flowers cannot be neglected. They not only bring convenience to people's lives but also enrich the outdoor space. Environmental design in green residential areas must pay special attention to ecological protection: avoiding the use of toxic and harmful substances, preserving natural resources, protecting the environment through design work, and beautifying the environment with green plants, which itself is a form of natural resource. On one hand, green residential areas expand the scope of natural resources by increasing planting areas. Residential area greening design starts from improving residents' safety, health, hygiene, and small climatic conditions in cities. Through high-quality green space environment construction, it creates a natural atmosphere for urban life that is peaceful, beautiful, hygienic, and environmentally friendly. When designing modern residential areas, attention should be paid to the following issues:单一草坪 (single lawn) and residential area greening plants. There is a tendency towards large-scale single lawns, which is unscientific under relative community conditions where lawns are strong in appreciation but weak in practicality. Establishing regional natural and geographical conditions, structurally configuring reasonably, having rich layers, harmonious relationships among species, scenic garden plants, making natural harmony, fully utilizing space, allowing light, moisture, nutrients, air, and other resources to be fully and reasonably utilized.
