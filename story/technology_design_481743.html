Seven Things to Note When Purchasing Agricultural Machinery

Buying a piece of agricultural machinery, especially large-scale equipment, often requires the savings of a family for several or even over ten years. In some cases, loans are also required. If the selection is inappropriate, it will not only affect agricultural production but also bring unnecessary troubles and economic losses, so careful consideration is necessary.

1. Pay attention to the regional adaptability of agricultural machinery products

Any type of agricultural machinery has its own application adaptability, mainly constrained by natural conditions such as soil conditions, plot conditions, power conditions, local farming habits, farmers' understanding of mechanized operations, etc. If the selection is inappropriate, the purchased agricultural machinery cannot function normally, leading to unnecessary losses. For example, although large agricultural machinery has high work efficiency, due to our city's mountainous terrain with small and scattered plots, poor rural roads, and field passage conditions, it is difficult to fully utilize their functions. For corn combine harvesters, it is important to note whether the cutting row spacing is consistent with the local planting form; otherwise, normal operation cannot be carried out.

2. Choose products from large enterprises or well-known brands

Before purchasing agricultural machinery, carefully consult surrounding farmers or experts from relevant departments about the quality status of the products sold on the market. Especially when purchasing expensive large-scale agricultural machinery, it is essential to thoroughly understand the product and the manufacturing enterprise. You can ask farmers who have already purchased machines or consult technical personnel from the local agricultural machinery department. It is recommended to purchase products from large enterprises, preferably well-known brands with high recognition.

3. Purchase from qualified and reputable agricultural machinery dealers

Do not be tempted by low prices or blindly believe in dealers' product promotions. Consider comprehensively the quality of the product, price, and three-warranty services. Compare multiple options. It is worth reminding the farmers that a small tractor may only be a few hundred yuan cheaper, and an accompanying machine tool might only be dozens of yuan cheaper, but once deceived, the loss could be significant.

4. Choose products with sufficient spare parts supply and good technical service

Before purchasing, make sure to inquire at the local agricultural machinery dealer and those with experience using such tools about the availability of spare parts and the manufacturer's after-sales service situation. Generally speaking, popular models used locally have more abundant spare parts supply and better manufacturer after-sales services. When purchasing, learn about the relevant matters concerning the use, maintenance, and upkeep of the product, clarify the three-warranty method and duration, and record the repairer's phone number and address.

5. Carefully inspect the goods with the seller upon purchase

1. Check if the product certificate, user manual, warranty card, and other accompanying documents are complete. Ensure that these documents include the address and telephone number of the manufacturing enterprise and after-sales service unit.

2. Check if the accompanying tools, accessories, spare parts match the packing list, and inspect the appearance quality of the product. Check if all components and parts of the purchased agricultural machinery are intact and undamaged.

3. Conduct a trial run for power machinery, promptly addressing or replacing any products found to have quality issues.

4. For tractors and combine harvesters, carefully verify whether the frame number, engine number, and production date match the content marked on the product and product certificate to avoid future complications when claiming compensation or applying for a license at the local agricultural machinery supervision department.

6. Obtain a purchase invoice

Make sure to obtain a formal invoice supervised by the tax department from the dealer. The invoice should have both the tax seal and financial seal, clearly stating the purchaser's name, product name, purchase time, quantity, specifications, model, and price. Do not forego the invoice because it does not need reimbursement. It serves as an important basis for resolving future quality disputes.

7. Special Reminder

Before putting tractors and combine harvesters into use, timely register for licenses, driver’s licenses, safety technology inspections, and related certificates and procedures with the local agricultural machinery authority. Otherwise, relevant departments will impose penalties according to national laws and regulations, causing unnecessary losses. (Feng Guomin, Longzhuang Town Agricultural Science Station, Daan City, Jilin Province)
