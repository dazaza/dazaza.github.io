Car Beauty Knowledge: Car Washing

Several Misconceptions about Car Washing

1. Using car wax colors randomly

Black cars have black-specific car wax, red cars have red-specific car wax, white cars have white-specific car wax, and they should not be used indiscriminately. The main difference between these waxes lies in their cleaning components, with some also containing brightening formulas. Especially for metallic paint, using the wrong car wax must be avoided.

2. Adding interior equipment arbitrarily

Private cars are like a mobile home for the owner, pursuing comfort and luxury is understandable, but some owners install various devices inside the car themselves. Such modifications, if not handled properly, can adversely affect the performance and reliability of the car, leaving potential problems or affecting driving safety.

3. Washing the car under intense sunlight dries it quickly

Many private car owners like to wash their cars under the scorching sun, thinking that this way the water on the body will dry quickly after washing. In fact, this is incorrect. Under intense sunlight, the convex lens effect formed by water droplets can cause localized high temperatures on the top layer of the car paint. Over time, the car paint will lose its luster. Moreover, applying wax at this time can easily lead to uneven car color. Therefore, washing and waxing the car should ideally be done in a shaded area; if this cannot be guaranteed, then it should be done on a cloudy day or in the early morning or evening of a sunny day.

4. Circular waxing is the most scientific method

Many people habitually wax their cars in circular motions, which is an incorrect method. The correct way to wax is in straight lines, alternating horizontally and vertically, and finally following the direction of water flow. This can reduce the appearance of concentric circular halos on the car paint surface.

5. Wrong order

Roof, front and rear cover plates, side body, lights, bumpers, skirts, wheels.

6. Washing the car with stagnant water

Always use running water; stagnant water is one of the main causes of car scratches. At least two buckets of water should be prepared because the lower third of the car body is always the dirtiest and should be wiped separately from the upper two-thirds.

7. Washing and waxing the car every day

Washing and waxing the car once a month is sufficient. Excessive washing and waxing can gradually diminish the car's brightness. If you must wash your car weekly, choose a neutral cleaning agent and car wax without abrasive components.

8. Excessively wiping the engine hood

The engine hood is the face of a car, and many owners pay special attention to this part. When washing and waxing the car, they put extra effort into this area and may even wipe it casually when idle or waiting. Additionally, the engine hood is lower than the roof and trunk lid, making it easy to apply full force. The result is that under the sunlight, numerous fine scratches appear, accompanied by a slight loss of luster.
