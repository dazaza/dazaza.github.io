International Online reported on November 24th that according to recent reports by American media, with global warming and accelerating ice cap melting, the abundant energy resources and convenient shipping routes of the Arctic are becoming increasingly "practically usable". Surrounding countries are formulating relevant strategies to gain the initiative in the competition for Arctic interests. The US Navy has also launched an "Arctic Roadmap", planning to deploy an "environmentally friendly" aircraft carrier battle group in the Arctic region to seize the opportunity.

Nuclear-powered aircraft carriers paired with "environmental fighter jets"

According to a report on the website of Wired magazine on November 18th, various countries' attention to the Arctic is increasing. Russia, Canada, Denmark, and even China plan to strengthen their military presence in the Arctic. Some countries have even planted flags, attempting to compete for the rich natural gas resources under the Arctic ice cap. In light of this, the United States has also introduced corresponding strategies to ensure its own strategic interests in the Arctic. Recently, the US Navy's Climate Change Response Special Forces published the "Navy Arctic Roadmap" document, pointing out that the temperature increase in the Arctic region is twice that of other regions, and the Arctic ice cap has become unstable. Scientists even believe that at some point in the 2030s, the Arctic will experience ice-free summers.

The US Navy is taking a series of actions to address the challenges brought about by climate change. Experts from the US "Military Website" revealed that in October, the US Navy announced that it would deploy an aircraft carrier strike group known as the "Great Green Fleet" (Great Green Fleet) in the Arctic. It is named "Great Green Fleet" not only because its aircraft carrier uses nuclear power and its carrier-based aircraft use green biofuels, but also corresponds to the "Great White Fleet" (Great White Fleet) in the history of the US Navy. From December 16, 1907, to February 22, 1909, under the guidance of Mahan's sea power theory, the US Navy dispatched a large fleet for a grandiose circumnavigation of the globe, which was named due to all ships being painted white.

The US Navy's Climate Change Response Special Forces hope to catch up with other countries' momentum in developing the Arctic, deploying more personnel in the Arctic, and beginning to evaluate various options for deploying fleets in the Arctic, studying everything from surface warfare to oil spill response. At that time, the US Navy will also expand training in the Arctic region and observe foreign actions, such as Canada's "Polar Bear" military exercises.

Dominating the Arctic with the "Green Fleet"

The "Navy Arctic Roadmap" document lists the United States' three main interests in the Arctic: energy reserves, shipping routes, and potential territorial disputes. The article in Wired magazine believes that the US Navy also needs to respond to the escalating military competition in the Arctic. The US Navy will assess the "risks and threats" posed by stakeholders in the Arctic and the motives of these countries, and propose "cooperation plans." Therefore, it can be seen that the US Navy is building the "Great Green Fleet" to seize the initiative in the competition for the Arctic, using aircraft carriers as "mobile maritime bases" to maintain a long-term military presence in the Arctic.

Currently, the US Navy possesses the world's largest nuclear-powered aircraft carrier battle group, and the development of biofuels for fighter jets has entered the practical stage. This means that the US Navy can play the "environmental protection card" in the Arctic, emphasizing the "green environmental protection" of its own aircraft carrier battle groups and fighter jets while requiring other countries' warships and fighter jets to also be "environmentally friendly." However, apart from the United States, no other country has the ability to build so-called "green fleets," which will completely become an excuse for the United States to accuse other countries of "polluting the Arctic" in the future and exclude other countries' involvement in the Arctic.

The US "Great Green Fleet" takes the lead

Some analysts pointed out that the United States is vigorously building the "Great Green Fleet" in order to maintain a strong military presence in the Arctic through aircraft carriers. Compared with other countries' icebreakers and warships, the US nuclear-powered aircraft carrier battle group has inherent advantages.

Firstly, nuclear-powered aircraft carriers do not need frequent refueling and can be stationed in the Arctic seas for a long time. Secondly, the carrier-based aircraft and early warning aircraft on the aircraft carriers can monitor large areas of the sea and airspace and can "closely monitor" the future-formed Arctic shipping routes.

Finally, the United States owns multiple nuclear-powered aircraft carrier battle groups, which can implement "rotational deployment," while countries like Russia and France, although they have aircraft carriers, have very few numbers and cannot be deployed in the Arctic for a long time.

Therefore, in the future competition for Arctic resources, the US "Great Green Fleet" will take full advantage. In this regard, the international community should strengthen cooperation in Arctic affairs, advocate peaceful development of the Arctic, and prevent any one country from monopolizing the Arctic through military superiority.
