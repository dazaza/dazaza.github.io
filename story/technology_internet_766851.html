Microsoft is at it again: Trade in your iPhone for WP cash equivalents

by anonymous on 2013-10-01 22:17:10

It can be seen from Microsoft's advertisements that, whether it is a tablet or a phone, they are doing their utmost to compete with Apple. In order to get Apple users to switch camps, Microsoft has recently revived an old trick - trade-ins. As part of the "device exchange" program, Microsoft will launch an iPhone trade-in campaign on Friday, where each iPhone 4S or iPhone 5 can be exchanged for a shopping voucher worth at least $200.

In the past few weeks, Microsoft has been implementing this trade-in activity, where consumers can exchange iPads for shopping vouchers, with the aim still being to encourage consumers to buy Microsoft Surface tablets. Microsoft has even launched an online trade-in campaign, where consumers can exchange any Apple, Android, or BlackBerry device for a Visa gift card.

Apple previously launched a new iPhone model and sold millions of units. Despite Microsoft's vigorous persuasion for users to switch to Windows Phones, it is evident that these millions of consumers clearly prefer the iOS platform over WP. It seems that Microsoft's trade-in plan has not been very successful.

However, for users who have not switched to the iPhone 5S or 5C due to price reasons but own older iPhones, Microsoft's iPhone trade-in campaign does have some appeal. The $200 shopping voucher provided by Microsoft can be used to purchase any product in Microsoft specialty stores, not just limited to Windows Phones.

Microsoft's iPhone trade-in campaign may offer some help in pushing Windows Phone into the mainstream market, but its impact won't be significant. If Microsoft wants to gain equal footing with Apple and Google in the fiercely competitive smartphone market, it still needs to continuously strengthen its own capabilities; as the saying goes, one must first be strong oneself if one wishes to succeed.
