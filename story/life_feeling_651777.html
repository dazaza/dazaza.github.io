Facing the sea, with spring flowers in bloom

I. Facing the sea

Remember, Bing Xin wrote an article describing the sea. She depicted it as so beautiful, so carefree, so graceful, and so unrestrained. She compared the sea to a goddess, a young girl, and a progressive youth. Her fresh, elegant, simple, and profound writing style intoxicates people with the various forms of the sea.

The first time I learned about the sea was on TV. The sea is mostly calm. It's like a quiet maiden, shimmering with silver light under the sun, that silver light swaying rhythmically with the waves, just like a maiden's pure eyes glistening with clear, noble, and flawless tears. But I think the sea is more like a mother who nurtures all life. Look, in the shallow sea, there are schools of small fish changing shapes as they chase after whales; sometimes like a galaxy, dazzling, sometimes like spirulina twisting their waists. Look, there's a blue whale spouting a high water column. Look at the colorful coral reefs underwater, jellyfish floating like lanterns, unknown fish with "sea line" wings, seahorses gracefully shaped like hooks, and dolphins full of empathy and intelligence...

All these wonderful creatures can exist vividly in this world, bringing people such a profound visual feast. Without the "mother's" milk, they would never survive. Mother, sea, sea, mother, you are so great, so selfless, so loving, so devoted, so clear and transparent. Your intentions, "children," understand, but "we" cannot repay. Without the ocean, there would be no life, without the ocean, there would be no us humans. Please protect this mother well, don't pour oil pollution into her milk anymore, don't capture and harm rare marine animals anymore.

II. Spring warmth and flowers in bloom

Stretching for dozens of kilometers along the coastline, driving along the edge of the sea, looking out at the endless sea where the sea meets the sky, all in a gray-blue hue, with mist shrouding the distant sea, creating a beautiful and comfortable blue melancholy. On the sea, separated by not too far distances, there are small islands dotted here and there. Green trees and pebbles on the islands complement each other beautifully. On the beach, further away is damp mud, with little fish, shrimp, and crabs, various adorable shells lying on it. Picking up one of the larger shells and putting it to your ear, you seem to hear the breathing sound of the sea. Nearby, there is a stretch of golden sand where men dressed in patterned shorts stroll, women in bikinis lie on the sand enjoying its warmth... The salty smell of the sea fills the air. This smell is like the fishy aroma of seafood cooked by your mother at home, a homely and familiar scent. The sea has the taste of spring, giving people a spring-like mood, always filled with vibrant vitality, offering hope, encouragement, and warmth. Looking at the sea, our hearts also blossom brilliantly.

Facing the sea, with spring flowers in bloom. Our moods are like spring warmth and flowers in bloom, and so is this world...
