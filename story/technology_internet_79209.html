**Report by Beijing Morning Post on July 21:** Known as the "First Beauty of the Internet," Liu Yuqi was sued by her signed company, Yingkai Culture and Media (Beijing) Co., Ltd., for secretly shooting advertisements without permission from the company. Recently, Daxing District Court made a first-instance ruling, ordering Liu Yuqi to compensate the company with 4 million yuan. This case is reportedly the highest compensation amount in artist breach-of-contract cases handled by local courts in the city.

According to the lawsuit filed by Yingkai Culture and Media (Beijing) Co., Ltd., on July 13, 2006, the company signed an *Exclusive Brokerage Contract* with Liu Yuqi, agreeing that she would appoint the company as her exclusive global broker. Liu Yuqi promised not to accept any form of performance independently or sign contracts of a similar nature with any third party. In the event of a breach of contract, Liu Yuqi would have to pay Yingkai Company 5 million yuan in compensation.

Yingkai Company stated that it had invested significant human resources, finances, and materials into promoting Liu Yuqi, including image promotion, song purchases, recording, production, release, and publicity. However, Liu Yuqi secretly worked as an exclusive model for a magazine and shot advertisements without informing the company, ignoring their warnings. As a result, the company filed a lawsuit against her, demanding 4 million yuan in compensation.

After the case was filed at the Daxing District Court, Liu Yuqi failed to appear in court without valid reasons, leading the court to consider her as having waived her right to defense and cross-examination. Ultimately, the Daxing District Court ruled that the contract provided by Yingkai Company was valid and binding on both parties. The court also determined that Liu Yuqi breached the contract and ordered her to pay Yingkai Company 4 million yuan within 10 days after the judgment took effect.

**Personal Information:**

Liu Yuqi: Winner of the "Best On-Screen Award" at the 2006 International Miss China Final and one of the top ten contestants in the 2006 Asia Sister Online Competition. She is referred to as the "First Beauty of the Internet."

(Source: Beijing Morning Post, Author: Jiang Jingjing)
