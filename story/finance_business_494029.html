The UK's Cadbury Schweppes is one of the largest confectionery companies in the world. Its beverages also have a significant influence in America and Australia. Cadbury’s history can be traced back over 200 years. Today, the Cadbury Schweppes brand can be found in almost every country around the globe. To date, the company employs more than 50,000 employees worldwide and has factories or offices in 35 countries. The global headquarters of Cadbury Schweppes is located in London, UK. In 2004, Cadbury was rated as one of the most popular companies in the UK.

Belgian Leonidas:

Classic romantic European chocolate:

Belgium is renowned for its long history of chocolate making. The term "Praline" represents high-end Belgian chocolates, and "Leonidas" is the leading brand of "Praline." Originating from 1913, Belgian Leonidas chocolates currently have over 2,000 specialty stores worldwide, spread across major cities such as Brussels, Paris, London, New York, Washington, Tokyo, Hong Kong, Singapore, Taipei, Shanghai, Beijing, Guangzhou, Wuhan, and more.

All Leonidas chocolates are made in Brussels, using only the finest quality ingredients: pure cocoa, fresh milk and cream, Turkish hazelnuts, Italian almonds, Maraschino cherries, offering up to 80 different flavors ranging from delicious chestnut chocolates to exquisite coffee, pistachio nuts to champagne wine, even low-sugar chocolates. Each type features a unique recipe, ensuring everyone will be captivated by its distinctive charm.

The most important feature of Leonidas is its freshness and lack of preservatives, being 100% handmade. This makes the shelf life of its products only 60 days. Therefore, all Leonidas distributors worldwide must air-freight fresh chocolates from Brussels every 14 days, paired with beautiful packaging, absolutely satisfying customers' high-quality pursuits.

Most female customers need not worry about weight gain with Leonidas chocolates. Purely handmade and using high-quality materials, Leonidas chocolates offer low-calorie options. There's also a recently launched sugar-free series that allows you to enjoy the delicious taste without worrying about weight gain, experiencing the true smooth texture.

Belgian Godiva:

Originating from Brussels, Belgium, Godiva was founded by the master chocolatier Joseph Draps, named after the legendary noblewoman Lady Godiva. It has over 175 years of history and is known worldwide for its elegant aroma and texture. "Godiva" uses top-grade imported ingredients and manufacturing processes from Belgium, resulting in rich and smooth chocolate flavors with an elegant, lingering aftertaste; vibrant colors with distinct layers.
