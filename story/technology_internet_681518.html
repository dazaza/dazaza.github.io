Introduction

When mobile phones integrate various functions such as radios, MP3 players, cameras, camcorders, TVs, PDAs, etc., they have long exceeded their initial simple positioning as communication tools and have transformed into an important part of people's daily lives. The rapid advancement of communication technology has further accelerated the arrival of the 3G era. Compared to the previous 2G, 3G brings the Internet to mobile phones, allowing users to handle images, music, browse web pages, hold teleconferences, and conduct e-commerce on their phones. On the other hand, mobile phone manufacturers seize the huge opportunities brought by 3G, continuously increasing research and development investment. Smartphones with large screens, full keyboards, supporting WIFI and WAPI continue to hit the market. Meanwhile, the emergence of knockoff phones has significantly reduced the average selling price of smartphones, greatly lowering the user threshold for high-performance phones, leading to a surge in the number of smartphones, providing a complete hardware foundation for users to use mobile Internet services.

According to data from the International Telecommunication Union: In 1980, the global number of minicomputers was around 10 million, in 2000, more than 200 million terminals were connected to the Internet; by the end of 2010, the number of global Internet users exceeded 2 billion, and the number of mobile phone registrations exceeded 5 billion; the rapid growth of users in the mobile Internet era is an inevitable trend. The rapid increase in the number of users means enormous opportunities and will create tremendous commercial value.

In the mobile Internet era, terminals become one of the key focuses of mobile Internet development. Around the needs of mobile Internet development, the development of terminals in the mobile Internet era presents a clear trend: closely revolving around user needs, providing users with comprehensive services and experiences, i.e., user experience is king. Today, as mobile Internet begins, user experience has gradually become the ultimate pursuit of terminal development. The iPhone launched by Apple has raised user experience to a new level, winning the favor of global mobile phone users. The application experience based on Android (Google mobile operating system) has also reached its best level.

Based on 3G networks and smartphone platforms, various needs of Internet users based on PCs are slowly shifting to mobile terminals. The trend of 3G includes the integration of desktop Internet and WAP content. The development of 3G in China is making mobile phones constantly surpass PCs. According to DCCI's "2010-2011 China Internet Market Data Report", the growth rate of mobile Internet in China is significantly faster than PC Internet. It is expected that by 2013, the scale of mobile Internet users in China will reach 721 million, exceeding the scale of PC Internet users of 716 million. China will truly enter the mobile Internet era. Faced with a promising market, mobile Internet quickly gathers a group of entrepreneurs to begin staking out territories.
