The breeding core of "Yi Yi Kou Tian" is less than a hundred meters from the chemical plant. Photo by Tan Chao, reporter of Yangcheng Evening News.

The promotional brochure of "Yi Yi Kou Tian" emphasizes its "organic identity" everywhere. Photo by Zhou Wei and Tan Chao, reporters of Yangcheng Evening News.

Due to the very strict requirements for agricultural planting and breeding environment and process, the price of "organic products" sold on the market is often four or five times higher than that of ordinary similar products. A restaurant staff member told the reporter that the customers of Tianke Restaurant are "mainly high-end customers." According to the introduction, Tianke Restaurant has many regular customers who "are used to eating organic products", and the restaurant is also mainly "targeting people who understand eating organic products and green products."

On August 27, a reporter from Yangcheng Evening News secretly visited the "Tianke Restaurant" under the jurisdiction of "Yi Yi Kou Tian" company. The reporter found that this "organic agriculture company" selling high-priced products under the banner of "organic" actually did not obtain any organic product certification issued by the state.

Is the breeding environment of "Yi Yi Kou Tian" as excellent as Ms. Ning said?

Reporter Tan Chao of Yangcheng Evening News

Can such a distance really meet the stringent "origin environmental requirements" for organic products?

"Our products are all organic, self-produced and self-sold, including vegetables, fish, chickens, etc., with no additives," the restaurant waitress said, "All organic certifications have been registered, and can be checked online." However, subsequently, the reporter did not find any evidence that "Yi Yi Kou Tian" had obtained organic certification on the "China Food and Agricultural Products Certification Information System."

Less than a hundred meters from the chemical plant

Ms. Ning insisted on the "organic" identity of the company's products: "The environment, soil, and water of our base have reached the standards for organic certification, and there are test reports. So our products are organic products, there is no problem with this."

During the investigation of the so-called "organic vegetables" of "Qingyi" (see the detailed report in the A4 edition of August 30th), the reporter of Yangcheng Evening News found that there are quite a few counterfeit "organic" products in Guangzhou's organic food market. In Zengcheng, there is an "organic agriculture company" called "Yi Yi Kou Tian" which claims to sell "organic agricultural products" without obtaining "organic product" certification or "organic conversion" certification. It has successfully entered the high-end consumption circle in Guangzhou, and its chickens sell for as high as 168 yuan each. More shockingly, this company, which claims to produce "organic products", has its free-range chicken breeding site right next to several chemical plants!

In the promotional brochure of "Yi Yi Kou Tian" provided by Tianke Restaurant, the reporter saw that it vividly displays its own "organic vegetables", "organic fresh fish", and "live chickens, ducks, and geese" and other agricultural products. A waitress at the restaurant said that the "organic silk rice" (10 pounds per pack) displayed on the sales rack is priced at 56 yuan, and the "organic vegetables" produced by "Yi Yi Kou Tian" are priced at 88 yuan per box (8 pounds per pack). On the first floor of the restaurant, the reporter saw several customers purchasing large quantities of "organic" products from "Yi Yi Kou Tian", including live chickens and eggs. It was learned that the price of castrated chickens after beautiful packaging is as high as 168 yuan each.

The reporter's investigation found that there were two chemical plants and two glass factories around this free-range chicken breeding center. Among them, the straight-line distance between the breeding base and Guangzhou Shun Chemical Co., Ltd., as measured by the reporter, is less than 100 meters. There is another chemical plant just dozens of meters in front of this chemical company.

According to the regulations, the "National Standard for Organic Products in China" stipulates the following regarding "origin environmental requirements": "Organic production must be carried out under suitable environmental conditions. Organic production bases should be far away from urban areas, industrial mines, main transportation lines, industrial pollution sources, garbage dumps, etc."

However, during the undercover visit to one of the free-range chicken breeding centers of "Yi Yi Kou Tian", Ms. Ning, the vice general manager of the company, admitted to the reporter: "'Yi Yi Kou Tian' products currently have not obtained any organic certification, and the earliest they will obtain 'green food' certification for chickens and eggs will be at the end of this year."

Why can't such a company that has successfully entered the high-end "organic" food market present "organic product certification"? In response, Manager Peng of the marketing department of "Yi Yi Kou Tian" stated that the vegetables of "Yi Yi Kou Tian" are "planted according to organic standards, but the organic certification has not yet come down", "Pigs, chickens, and rice will soon be able to obtain organic certification".

The distance between "Yi Yi Kou Tian" breeding farm and the chemical plant is so close, does it comply with the above standards? In response, Manager Peng said, "The chemical plant will move away in the future, and it won't have any impact."

Everywhere claiming "organic"

"Organic production base", "organic agriculture", "organic living circle", "organic vegetables", "organic fresh fish"... During the undercover visit to the "Yi Yi Kou Tian" organic agriculture company in Zhongxin Town, Zengcheng, the most common slogan seen by the reporter was the word "organic". However, when the reporter asked the staff to show the organic certification certificate, "Yi Yi Kou Tian" was unable to provide it.
