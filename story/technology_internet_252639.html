The Medical Basis of "Stick to What You Know" - Job Hopping Shortens Lifespan - Career Planning Reference

A BBC report states that the University of Maryland in the United States conducted a tracking survey from 1992 on 12,189 people aged 51-61 who were nearing retirement. It was found that after retirement, individuals who continued to work part-time in their original field showed better mental and physical development than those who pursued other fields. They also tended to be healthier.

Researchers discovered that the mental and physical health of fully retired individuals deteriorated relatively quickly. The study demonstrated that people still need to work, as it helps maintain good mental acumen, which in turn promotes good health. However, if a retiree chooses a career different from what they did before retirement, their physical and mental well-being is not as good as those who continue with their original work.

I find this research very useful as it medically proves that everyone needs a job they like and are good at. This requires us to actively learn and diligently practice our specialties in our daily work and life, improving our abilities to become experts in a specific field, valuable members of society, and individuals in demand. Only then can we easily continue part-time work in our areas of expertise after retirement, greatly benefiting our physical and mental health.

The principle of "stick to what you know" follows the same logic. For the sake of your health and longevity, I hope everyone can find joy and develop expertise in their work! This is also an important reason why GreenTree Inn frequently trains its employees.
