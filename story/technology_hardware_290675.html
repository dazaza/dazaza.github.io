Guoxin Securities: Yunnan Copper's short-term self-produced copper output remains stable＿Stock Market Basics

by dx0957095 on 2009-12-03 18:43:08

Comment: There are currently no plans to expand existing mines, and the copper metal output is expected to be around 60,000 tons in 2009. The company currently owns four main mining enterprises: Yuxi Mining (100%), Chuxiong Mine Metallurgy (100%), Diqing Mining (75%), and Jinsha Company (51%). The production of these four mining enterprises are 29,000 tons, 13,000 tons, 7,000 tons, and 11,000 tons respectively. Among them, the current largest mine Dahongshan Mining has a production of 15,000 tons.

It is estimated that the total mineral copper production for 2009 will be 60,000 tons. Due to the lack of current mine expansion or renovation projects, the production of existing mines in 2010 and 2011 will remain unchanged.

Resource Expansion - Proposed asset injection is expected to be completed by the first half of next year. The resource injection of Puliang Mine remains promising. Currently, the company's mine reserves are 1.5 million tons. The company is conducting deep exploration work on its existing mines to ensure that annual consumption of reserves and newly added reserves remain basically balanced. This year, the Dahongshan mining area has added 370,000 tons of reserves, which is currently in the process of obtaining mining rights.

The ability of Yunnan Province's mine succession resources is beginning to strengthen, with initial results achieved in old mining areas, achieving dynamic resource balance. From 2002 to 2005, the investment intensity of Yunnan Copper Industry (Group) Co., Ltd. has significantly increased, strengthening geological prospecting in mines to extend mine life. A total of 86.95 million yuan was invested in geological prospecting projects in six old mines belonging to Yunnan Copper Industry (Group) Co., Ltd., equivalent to 124.2% of the total investment from 1996 to 2001, averaging nearly 22 million yuan per year, accounting for about 15% of Yunnan Province's recent geological survey investment. Over four years, a total of 481.77 million tons of copper resources were discovered with a copper grade of 1.06%, equivalent to finding a large copper deposit. Of this, 311.66 million tons of exploitable reserves were identified. As a result, the service life of old mining areas was extended by three years, achieving basic balance between the newly added reserves and production consumption during the period (consumption during the period was 34.16 million tons), reaching the goal of "low risk, high output" in old mining areas. Initial success was achieved in old mining areas. Additionally, under the joint support of local governments in Yunnan Province, several medium to large-sized copper-silver polymetallic deposits have been discovered and evaluated in the southwestern Sanjiang region, including Zhongdian Pulang, Deqin Yangla, Simao Dapingzhang, and Lanping Baiyangping, with the potential to become a major non-ferrous metal resource base. Among them, the Zhongdian Pulang copper mine has a projected reserve of 2 million tons after engineering control, and the predicted copper resources in the entire area could reach over 5 million tons, making it a super-large copper mine. The first mining area has copper reserves of 720,000 tons, and the project to produce 50,000 tons of copper concentrate annually is about to be completed, eventually reaching an annual production scale of over 150,000 tons of copper concentrate.

The main points of future resource growth for the company are: 1. The proposed injection of mining assets in the 2009 additional issuance project includes Chuxiong Siyuan (100%) (Chuxiong Siyuan produced a total of 3,648 tons of copper metal in 2008, of which 3,350 tons were self-produced); Yunnan Daya (100%) (Yunnan Daya produced a total of 8,140 tons of copper in copper concentrate in 2008. Of this, 4,000 tons came from the Shifeng Mountain copper mine; 3,700 tons from the Lion Mountain copper mine; and 740 tons from Dali Hongzhizhu Company.); Xingyan Company (100%) (Xingyan Company's main product (copper content in concentrate) output in 2008 was 2,284 tons); Jinggu Mining & Metallurgy (74.78%) (output 800 tons). The total mineral copper output is 14,574 tons, and it is expected to be injected by the first half of 2010; 2. Pulang Copper Mine (reserves 3 million tons) is currently undergoing environmental assessment, mainly due to the location being in a nature reserve area, so the progress of the environmental assessment is slower, but the possibility of final injection into the listed company is very high. In addition, another major copper mine of the group, Lala Copper Mine, is understood to have a lower short-term injection expectation due to equity issues.

External Purchased Smelting - Basically Break-even, Smelting Capacity Remains Stable. One-third of the company's copper raw materials come from self-produced copper mines, one-third are purchased domestically, and one-third are imported internationally. The smelting cost of the company is approximately $50-60/ton. Due to the rise in copper prices, the difference between the company's raw material procurement and actual sales offsets the decline in current processing fees, allowing the company to maintain break-even even under conditions of declining spot processing fees.

The company's significant advantage lies in advanced smelting technology and low smelting costs, having mastered the Isa furnace smelting process. Compared to flash furnaces used by Jiangxi Copper and Tongling, the Isa furnace has obvious advantages: simple structure and process, no need for deep drying of feed water, lower requirements for material particle size, less heat loss, lower dust rate, fewer repeat processing needs, and higher efficiency; the main disadvantage is the shorter lining life. Due to the advantages of the Isa furnace process, the company's smelting costs have significantly decreased in recent years, with copper smelting costs at about 2,000 yuan/ton, lower than those of Jiangxi Copper and Tongling and other peers.

Currently, the company's smelting capacity: coarse copper is 200,000 tons (Yimen Copper Industry 50,000 tons; Chuxiong Dianzhong 100,000 tons (actual output 50,000 tons); Dongchuan 50,000 tons (actual output 30,000 tons)); electrolytic copper 650,000 tons (headquarters 550,000 tons, Chifeng 100,000 tons). Chuxiong Dianzhong is currently undergoing a 100,000 tons/year coarse copper technical renovation project, changing from a 50,000 ton blast furnace technology to a 100,000 ton ISA furnace smelting. It is estimated that the total cathode copper production for 2009 will be 300,000 tons, mainly due to excessive underproduction in the first half of the year. The possibility of expansion in smelting capacity in the future is not significant.

By-product - An Important Highlight of Company Profitability. The company's by-products include gold, silver, sulfuric acid, etc. It is estimated that the gold production this year will be 7 tons, silver 300 tons, and sulfuric acid 750,000 tons. The 2009 interim report shows that the gross profit margin of gold is approximately 19.49%, silver 10.55%, and sulfuric acid 31.83%, still one of the main sources of company profitability.

Mining Costs - Higher Underground Mining Costs. Since all of the company's copper mines are underground operations, the cost of mined copper is relatively high. Among them, the mining cost of Yuxi Mining is approximately 25,000-27,000 yuan/ton (including tax and three fees); Diqing Mining, due to smaller production scale, has the highest cost, approximately 30,000 yuan/ton. We estimate that the company's average complete cost of mined copper is about 27,000-28,000 yuan/ton.

Development Plan - Resources Reign Supreme. The company's future development focuses on resources as king, and does not exclude the possibility of China Aluminum Corporation planning to re-list after integrating Yun Tong with other copper assets.

Yunnan Province is rich in copper resources, but lacks large and super-large mines, without open-pit mining conditions. Some mines are located in remote mountainous areas, making development difficult; some state-owned mines are facing the problem of copper resource depletion. There are currently 217 known copper deposits (points), among which 156 have proven reserves, spread across 14 prefectures (cities) and 38 counties in the province, with proven reserves of 958.188 million tons. The retained reserves as of 1994 were 849.148 million tons, accounting for 13.59% of the national copper retained reserves of 625.1188 million tons at the same time, second only to Jiangxi and Tibet, ranking third nationwide; large and super-large copper mines with more than 1 million tons include Dongchuan Tangdan, Xinping Dahongshan, Deqin Yangla, and Jinggu Minle, 24 medium-sized copper mines, and 127 small ones, with retained reserves accounting for 5.96%, 44.22%, and 19.82% of total retained reserves respectively. 77.3% of the reserves are concentrated in central Yunnan, southeastern Yunnan, and western Yunnan, accounting for 12.5% and 7.6% respectively, while others scattered throughout account for 2.6%. The retained reserves of copper mines currently in production and construction in Yunnan account for 57.9% of the province, those yet to be developed account for 36.1%, and those temporarily difficult to develop account for 6.1%.

Currently, Yun Tong Group has reserves of about 5 million tons, excluding the proven reserves of the Pulang copper mine of 3 million tons (excluding future reserves), the company's remaining mine resources are relatively scattered, including about 80 mining rights, making integration challenging. Nevertheless, the growth rate of the company's copper reserves in the future is relatively optimistic.

In addition, after China Aluminum acquired Yun Tong, it strengthened management on one hand: the company's management team and management thinking are now mainly led by China Aluminum, inheriting the China Aluminum model, greatly improving compared to the previous extensive management style, which is conducive to further reducing the company's costs; on the other hand, China Aluminum aims to use Yun Tong as a platform for developing its copper industry, creating a complete copper industry chain. Currently, China Aluminum has acquired multiple large domestic copper material processing plants, and spent approximately $860 million to acquire 91% of the shares of Canadian Peru Copper Company. Peru Copper Company is a Canadian company listed in Canada, the United States, and Peru. The company holds the development option for the Toromocho copper mine in Peru through an option agreement signed with a Peruvian state-owned company. The copper mine has approximately 12 million tons of copper-equivalent metal resources, making it one of the world's largest planned copper mines. In November 2008, China Aluminum invested approximately $2.15 billion in construction, expecting to complete within 3-5 years, with a planned production capacity of 200,000 tons.

The company's resource strategy focused domestically is unparalleled by other companies in the entire copper industry.

We maintain our cautious recommendation investment rating and suggest continuing to pay attention to transactional opportunities. We expect the company's EPS for 2009, 2010, and 2011 to be 0.29 yuan, 0.8 yuan, and 1.02 yuan respectively. Given our positive outlook on future copper prices and expectations of increased integration efforts by China Aluminum in Yun Tong, we give the company a "cautious recommendation" investment rating.
