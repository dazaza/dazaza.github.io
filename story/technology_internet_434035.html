Catalogue of Furniture Spray Booth 123456 Catalogue 1234567 A furniture spray booth mainly refers to the place for surface paint spraying and drying. Depending on different objects, such as furniture, sheet metal, etc., color or glossy products are added to their surfaces.

I. Working Principle of Furniture Spray Booth

During spraying, external air passes through the primary filter at the inlet and is then sent into the paint room static pressure chamber by the supply fan. After passing through the top filter cotton for secondary filtration, it evenly flows into the spray booth working space, forming a wind curtain around the workpiece. At this point, the loaded airspeed inside the spray booth is 0.3 m/s, ensuring that the paint mist produced during spraying does not linger in the operator's breathing zone but quickly descends with the airflow. The airflow is then filtered through water at the bottom and discharged via the exhaust duct.

II. Working Principle of Furniture Spray Booth - Infrared Heating

Generally, organic coatings have strong absorption capabilities for infrared rays, especially far-infrared rays. Therefore, infrared rays have a very significant effect on drying coatings. They promote fast curing of the coating layer, good quality, and prevent defects such as pinholes and bubbling.

III. Air Purification System Filter System Device

The filter system uses two layers of filters. The coarse filter layer effectively captures dust particles larger than 10um, while the precision high-efficiency filter layer has multiple structures and can effectively capture dust particles larger than 4um. The entire filter system has a large dust capacity, low resistance, long service life, and a filtering efficiency of over 98%.

IV. Wind Circulation System

The wind circulation system consists of a complete set of equipment. The unit is equipped with a group of fans, with 2 3KW fans providing air, resulting in a total airflow of 24,000 square meters, making the indoor loaded airspeed 0.3 m/s, thus ensuring rapid indoor air circulation without residual paint mist, safeguarding the health of operators and the spray painting effect. The fans use Siemens technology-produced air conditioning style fans specifically designed for spray booths, featuring compact structure, low power consumption, and stable performance.

V. Paint Mist Treatment System

Paint mist treatment adopts water filtration, with low usage cost, ideal paint mist adsorption, and good effects. The indoor noise complies with GBJ87-85 "Industrial Enterprise Noise Control Design Specifications" and does not exceed 85 decibels. Principles and Structure of Spray Booths:

The entire spray booth features a modular structure, with the booth body using insulated spray-painted wall panels, providing good sealing and insulation performance. The aluminum alloy-framed doors have observation windows in the center for real-time monitoring of internal dynamics. The side of the booth body is equipped with a work door for easy entry and exit of staff. High-quality stainless steel heat exchangers ensure high heat exchange efficiency and long service life. Imported filter cotton and low-noise high-volume fans ensure perfect spray results.

A spray booth provides for the spraying of workpieces: (1) a clean working environment; (2) full collection of paint mist. The configuration of spray booths varies according to the size, shape, weight, and production guidelines of the workpieces, including open and closed types, continuous and intermittent types, rotating or fixed workpieces, single-side and double-side spraying. Recently, oil-curtain spray booths and downward spray booths have also been developed. Structurally, they should include an air supply filtration system, workpiece hanging (conveyance, rotation) device, water curtain system, water washing system, exhaust system, water tank, lighting device, and booth body.

Principle of Paint Mist Recovery:

Paint mist recovery can be divided into two parts: one is when paint mist collides and mixes with the water curtain during spraying of the workpiece, part of the paint mist dissolves into the water and falls into the water tank; the second is when paint mist that hasn't dissolved into the water curtain enters the water washing room through gaps at the bottom of the water curtain board, mixing fully with the water mist sprayed by fogging nozzles, separating gas and water before settling into the water tank. Paint mist coagulants are regularly added to the water tank to form paint sludge.

In the furniture manufacturing industry, after completing production, the final process is spray painting. A furniture spray booth is where spray painting occurs within a room. Traditional spray painting was done outdoors using a spray gun for tools like cars and furniture, but this method reduces the quality of spray painting for large tools. For example, during spray painting, airborne dust mixes with the paint, reducing quality; there is uncertain waste of paint, and the spray area becomes very dirty. For workers, it is both messy and harmful to inhale large amounts of paint mist. Later, people confined spray painting to a specific area, leading to the earliest spray booths—exhaust spray booths, which first appeared in the Chengdu region in the 1990s.

1. Cross-flow spray booth: Airflow moves from one end of the booth to another. This model is low-cost and easy to install. However, the paint mist moves along the side of the vehicle, and the mist cannot be removed from around the operator’s face, making the working environment less than ideal.

2. Side down-draft spray booth: Air enters the spray booth through a canopy filter and exits through the side walls near the ground. This is a mid-priced spray booth. Since the paint mist stays away from the object itself and can be removed from around the operator, the spray painting effect and working environment are more ideal.

3. Full down-draft spray booth: Represents the latest technology today. Air enters from the canopy and is discharged through trenches or basements outside. This exhaust method achieves a cleaner spray painting process and a safer working environment. However, it is costly and occupies a large space. It can be divided into dry spray booths and wet spray booths (water treatment).

Commonly used spray booths mostly adopt dry treatment, so the filter system (mainly referring to primary and secondary filter cotton) needs regular inspection and replacement; otherwise, it could either affect the spray painting quality or potentially cause a fire. With further scientific and technological advancements, methods for handling excess paint mist have become increasingly advanced. Nowadays, many high-end cars use water treatment spray booths, dissolving excess paint mist in water, achieving significant progress in handling paint mist.

Key points of exhaust spray booths:

1. Ventilation - Key operation of exhaust spray booths

2. Filtration - Removing small particles from the air

3. Exhaust - Discharging waste air and introducing fresh air

Principle: Air (outdoor air) - spray booth door filter cloth - spray table - using spray equipment for splashing spray - exhaust fan (removing generated paint mist)

Weaknesses still exist in this type of spray booth:

1. Filter cloth is easily damaged, allowing dust to enter

2. Easily allows unclean outdoor air to enter

3. Not environmentally friendly, wasting paint

4. Requires frequent cleaning, regularly removing dirt from the filter cloth

Currently, new spray booths — full-pressure spray booths — have emerged and are widely applied in China's furniture industry. The basic principle of full-pressure spray booths is similar to that of exhaust spray booths, but it utilizes directional pressure, applying vertical spray painting from top to bottom, making spray painting more precise. Additionally, hardware-wise, it includes a main room, replacing filter cloth with "multi-layer filter cotton" for higher air cleanliness, using infrared lamps to save drying time, and employing water curtains for a more environmentally friendly way to discharge excess paint mist.
