Graduates shoot micro-films to encourage students taking college entrance examination

by xue94fwsh on 2012-02-28 11:31:54

It is hoped that the movie will set an example for younger students preparing for the college entrance examination. The story is based on a true story, which can resonate with junior students. It is hoped that the movie will record real life. Predicament: no sponsorship received, so they have to pay out of their own pockets. The crew also put forward "micro needs" here. Today, they will shoot scenes at Dongguan No.1 Middle School and need 12 graduates from the class of 2011 to participate in the shooting; besides, they also need two actors to play the roles of dormitory manager and teacher. Yesterday, Zan Zilin mentioned to reporters that the crew urgently needed post-production equipment such as an Apple all-in-one computer, and hoped that netizens who had conditions would support them by contacting "@Dongguan 2012 College Entrance Examination Inspirational Micro-Movie Crew".

For the practice of students shooting inspirational micro-movies, Hu Bin, the teacher in charge of publicity work at Guangming Middle School, said that he had watched a similar student self-shot movie before, which was generally good but had some flaws in content, such as complaints and parody content. "Therefore, if the students want to screen this movie in the school, it still needs to be reviewed by some teachers in a small scale first. This is necessary. Since it spreads among the student group, the school and teachers must consider the influence of the movie on the majority of students."

Expectation: to premiere before the countdown of 100 days to the college entrance examination.

Netizen's opinion

According to Zan Zilin's introduction, this short film named "College Entrance Examination! What’s the fuss? Wet wet sui la! 50 college students give infinite strength to college entrance examinees" involved students graduated from Dongguan No.1 Middle School in 2010 who are now studying in Zhongshan University, Jinan University, South China Normal University and other provincial colleges and universities, and their messages were very touching. "I remember when this piece premiered at No.1 Middle School, some female students were moved to tears."

It is understood that soon this piece will enter post-production. Zan Zilin told reporters that like last year, they hope this micro-movie can premiere before the countdown of 100 days to the college entrance examination. He had already thought of the premiere location: "I hope it is at No.1 Middle School." The crew members took photos during their spare time. Still photo.

Do you still remember the hard-fought days of your senior three? I believe what you needed most at that time was inspiration and blessing.

Viewpoint

However, when expressing his expectations for this micro-movie, Lu Xuchang said that he hoped the micro-movie would first maintain the correct orientation, then relatively truthfully reflect the protagonist's struggle from average grades to getting into the ideal university, thereby setting an example for younger students preparing for the college entrance examination, achieving the effect of inspiration.

Zan Zilin, the initiator of the micro-movie, was a senior three student at Dongguan No.1 Middle School one year ago. Yesterday, he told reporters that when there were less than 100 days left until the college entrance examination, some graduates from the class of 2010 at the same school initiated the production of a video full of blessings and inspiration, which was played within the school.

Zan Zilin said that this was not a plot created according to the needs of dramatic conflict, but a true story. "It is based on one of my close friends, showcasing his joys and sorrows, happiness and anger throughout his senior three year. Realism makes it easier for junior students to resonate."

Origin

They have just entered the ivory tower. Looking back at life a year ago, they decided to give a different kind of strength to the warriors about to embark on their journey.

"Inspired by the inspiring short film created by my seniors,"

This is a micro-movie. A student who used to have average grades strives hard in the college entrance examination and gets into the ideal university, and it is completely true. Yesterday, the main creative personnel said that they hoped to move the audience with stories instead of words.

"After the premiere of this piece at No.1 Middle School, I thought, after I graduate, can I also make such a piece and give it to the next generation of juniors?"

Yesterday afternoon, the reporter interviewed Lu Xuchang, principal of Dongguan No.1 Middle School. He told reporters that although he had not yet seen this micro-movie and did not know how the students would shoot it, "but a few days ago, some senior three students applied to the school, saying that some alumni wanted to shoot some scenes at the school, and I agreed immediately."

According to the understanding, many scenes of this movie will be shot at Dongguan No.1 Middle School, and the crew has also received support from the school side.

As a past student, you would feel that a few warm words, a few spirited actions, and a few sincere smiles are the best encouragement for younger students, but in the view of "@Dongguan 2012 College Entrance Examination Inspirational Micro-Movie Crew", this is far from enough.

Besides, regarding the crew members' hope to use this micro-movie to promote the good image of Dongguan middle school and college students, Hu teacher believed that the students' strong sense of responsibility and mission was commendable and admirable.

Since going online at 8:38 pm on February 6th, the popularity of "@Dongguan 2012 College Entrance Examination Inspirational Micro-Movie Crew" began to increase. As the Weibo continuously released new content, the attention and support from netizens also continued to increase.

Lu Xuchang said that after graduation, the former classmates still have feelings for their alma mater, coming back to the school to encourage and bless their juniors, and this starting point is worth affirming.

Zan Zilin said that Zhang Yikang was the producer of the micro-movie, but until now, Zhang Yikang had not been able to secure any sponsorship for the crew. In more common terms, all members of the crew were working for "zero remuneration". He looked at Zhang Yikang teasingly and said, "There's nothing we can do but pay out of our own pockets."

Say it and do it. A few people, a few copies of the script, a single-lens reflex camera, from February 5th, they have been busy for 3 consecutive days for a movie of over ten minutes. According to the plan, today they will go to Dongguan No.1 Middle School to shoot scenes, and together with several 2011 graduates recruited from the official Weibo account, record a few words of blessing and encouragement. In addition, they will also go to Beijing, to the school where the lead actor is located, to shoot scenes.

The online popularity of the piece firmly established Zan Zilin's determination. After entering Zhongshan Institute of Electronic Science and Technology, Zan Zilin started to prepare for this matter. He brought in his fellow classmate Zhang Yikang graduated from Guangming Middle School, and then found Wang Jiaxin, a senior graduated from Dongguan Senior High School and others to create together.

In Zan Zilin's mouth, this yet-to-be-born micro-movie was different since its conception. At 8:38 pm on the evening of the day before yesterday, their official Weibo account "@Dongguan 2012 College Entrance Examination Inspirational Micro-Movie Crew" officially went online.
