"I need you to step aside and not block my sunshine." In terms of dignity, this article is reprinted from Zhang Tianwei's blog.

"All that we do is to make people live happier and with more dignity, and make the society more just and harmonious." Such an expression not only means that the concept of "dignity" has appeared in the government work report for the first time, but also means that maintaining citizens' dignity and ensuring people's survival have been listed as the government's natural responsibility and work goal.

Before the "two sessions", Premier Wen Jiabao had already warmed up on the topic of "making people live happier and with more dignity" on different occasions, and explained the meaning of dignity through exchanges with netizens. According to his explanation, "letting ordinary people live with more dignity" includes three aspects: First, every citizen enjoys the freedom and rights granted by the constitution and law within the scope prescribed by the constitution and law, and the state should protect everyone's freedom and human rights. Everyone is equal before the law; Second, the ultimate purpose of national development is to meet the growing material and cultural needs of the masses, and there is nothing else besides this; Third, the comprehensive development of the whole society must be premised on the development of each individual, therefore, we need to create favorable conditions for people's freedom and all-round development, so that their intelligence can burst forth.

Frankly speaking, such a thorough explanation of human dignity, even in the most idealized writers' pens, was once only found in the words of appeals and visions, but now it comes out of the mouth of the prime minister of a country and is written into the government work report, the changes and surprises are self-evident.

Of course, the word "dignity" is originally a Chinese vocabulary, but Premier Wen Jiabao's interpretation of dignity is a new interpretation of the old term, whether it is the relationship between rights and the constitution, or the relationship between individuals and the state, these are the semantics that the word "dignity" does not have under the original context. In traditional China covered by royal power and Confucianism, there was no "freedom and rights granted by the constitution and law", and thus the dignity defined based on these freedoms and rights did not exist either. In the context of that time, "dignity" was roughly similar to "decent", more of a social evaluation or psychological feeling generated in the ethical relationship between people. After filling one's stomach, one can, and only then, have the backbone not to bow for five measures of rice, and be able to maintain courtesy and decency in front of others. As the saying goes, "When granaries are full, people know propriety; when they have enough food and clothing, they know honor and shame." Survival rights based on basic physiological needs such as warmth and food always take precedence over other rights based on law such as free speech.

Of course, in the traditional Chinese view, there has been a eulogy for personal dignity that is not threatened by power and is not conditional on material conditions, namely "not being swayed by wealth and honor, not being moved by poverty and lowliness, and not being subdued by force". But this kind of dignity belongs only to those "great men" who rise above the common people, not only irrelevant to the common people, but neither derived from the granting of law nor protected by law.

The ancient Greek philosopher, however, told the world another story. It is said that Diogenes, the most famous Cynic in ancient Greece, lived in a barrel and made a living by begging and cursing. When Alexander the Great, who had conquered the world, humbly knelt beside the barrel and asked him what he needed with curiosity and concern, Diogenes' answer was: "I need you to step aside and not block my sunshine."

I suspect that this story was specially fabricated to explain what "rights" and "dignity" mean. According to the legend's description, Diogenes' appearance probably resembles the currently popular "Sharp Brother" quite a bit. But despite the same situation of being watched, Diogenes' performance was completely different from Sharp Brother's confusion. Even if the onlooker was as noble as Alexander, Diogenes still had the backbone to order him to move away, because he knew, and deliberately wanted to remind Alexander: although I am in the barrel and you are in the hall, under the sun (natural law), you and I are equal; as a ruler, not disturbing and invading my freedom is your natural and only responsibility. For this ragged self-exiled person, dignity depends solely on the freedom of choice and the right to be undisturbed, and has little causal relationship with Alexander's tolerance, or the material conditions of "granaries are full" and "enough food and clothing".

Simple comparisons between China and foreign countries always carry the danger of generalizing from partial data. Perhaps, regardless of China or foreign countries, the basic human nature is not fundamentally different, and Diogenes' "Cynical" way of life may be just another form of "great man". For the common people, the value sequence of prioritizing subsistence over freedom may be an inner helplessness that cannot be shaken off. The difference lies only in that the reason why a civilized society is civilized is that it first grants every citizen the right to choose freely in the name of the constitution and law, even if it cannot exclude some people choosing to give up dignity for survival. Furthermore, it requires society to provide conditions for every citizen who yearns for dignity to uphold their dignity, including basic material conditions.

A civilized, progressive, and developed society has no right to refuse, but also does not need too many unconventional figures like Diogenes, nor can it pin hopes on everyone becoming a spiritually self-sufficient "great man". The civilization and dignity of a society lie in allowing every ordinary person living in it to enjoy the rights granted by the constitution and law, and to gain prosperity and dignity by freely exercising these rights.

The word "dignity" has become a buzzword due to its appearance in the government work report, which proves the awakening of the Chinese people's awareness of dignity and their desire for a dignified life, but it also proves from another angle that how to make every citizen live happier and with more dignity is not only a long and arduous task, but also just beginning to be addressed.
