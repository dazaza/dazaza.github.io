Rebirth: A Teenager Applies to Donate His Body After the Relapse of Leukemia (Photo)

by sznstejx3q3 on 2012-03-03 10:10:31

In return to society, Yu Bowen wants to donate his body after death.

News Review

On November 9, 2009, in Victory Village Group 2, Fenggu Town, Mianyang City, Wen Zhenghai, the father of Wen Chunrong, was diagnosed with stomach cancer. Meanwhile, her son Yu Bowen was diagnosed with leukemia, which posed a difficult choice for Wen Chunrong: both her father and son were suffering from fatal diseases, and she had to choose between saving her father or her son. After Chengdu Business Daily reported on this story, people from all walks of life generously donated money to help them.

In April last year, Yu Bowen successfully underwent a bone marrow transplant, receiving hematopoietic stem cells from a volunteer in the China Marrow Donor Program.

In January this year, after Wen Zhenghai gave up treatment and passed away, he resolutely donated his corneas in return to society. Two patients in Shenzhen accepted the corneal transplants.

On the afternoon of the day before yesterday, in the Hematology Department of West China Maternal and Child Hospital of Sichuan University, 12-year-old Yu Bowen solemnly pressed his fingerprint on two application forms for voluntary donation of bodies and corneas. In 2009, Yu Bowen, who was once diagnosed with leukemia, luckily survived thanks to the help of many kind-hearted people. Two years later, his leukemia recurred. This time, he decided that if he really died, he would donate his body to repay society.

Deep in Debt as Son's Leukemia Recurs

In the Hematology Department of West China Maternal and Child Hospital, Bed 27, 12-year-old Yu Bowen sat on the hospital bed, pale-faced. Wen Chunrong told Chengdu Business Daily that after the transplant, Yu Bowen had recovered well for a period of time, which made the whole family very excited.

However, in June this year, an unfortunate blow came. On June 29, Wen Chunrong found that Yu Bowen was sweating excessively all over his body. After being sent to the Mianyang Central Hospital for examination, Wen Chunrong was informed that Yu Bowen had been infected by a virus, leading to a drop in platelet count. On July 4, Wen Chunrong went to West China Maternal and Child Hospital, where Yu Bowen was eventually diagnosed with a recurrence of leukemia.

Upon hearing the bad news, Wen Chunrong felt as if her legs had gone limp. The sky seemed about to collapse. Her husband worked odd jobs outside, earning only more than 2000 yuan per month. After Wen Zhenghai passed away, their mother over 60 years old worked in a restaurant to earn money and support her economically struggling daughter. "In order to treat our son, our family has spent nearly 700,000 yuan and borrowed 180,000 yuan," Wen Chunrong said. After Yu Bowen's leukemia recurred, each check-up and chemotherapy cost was a considerable expense for the Yu family. To save money, Wen Chunrong and her 60-year-old mother basically ate pickles with rice. At night, Wen Chunrong often cried silently behind her son, feeling helpless about the current situation.

In Return to Society, Applying to Donate Body After Death

For all these things, 12-year-old Yu Bowen made the worst plan: if there was no hope of rescue, he wanted to donate his body. December 24 is Yu Bowen's 13th birthday, and he hopes to make this important decision before then.

At around 3 PM yesterday afternoon, volunteers Xu Jie from the Sichuan Red Cross Eye Bank and Dr. Yang from Kangqiao Ophthalmology Hospital affiliated with Chengdu Medical College came to the ward. Yu Bowen signed his name on the "Application Form for Voluntary Unpaid Donation of Bodies in Chengdu" and the "Agreement for Voluntary Unpaid Donation of Corneas in Sichuan Province" and pressed his fingerprint heavily. Xu Jie stated that if Yu Bowen unfortunately passes away, Chengdu Medical College will be the receiving unit for Yu Bowen's body. Since Yu Bowen suffered from leukemia, his body can only be used for medical research and teaching.

Why did he make this decision? Yu Bowen said, "So many kind-hearted people donated money to treat me before. To repay these strangers, my grandfather donated his corneas, and I want to take him as an example."

On the evening of November 29, during Yu Bowen's chemotherapy, he suddenly experienced unstoppable nosebleeds, vomiting blood, and bloody stools. Multiple medical staff rushed to save Yu Bowen. Wen Chunrong said that Yu Bowen had never been so severely ill before, and at that moment, she thought her child might not survive. But the strong child finally defeated death and pulled through. Perhaps realizing the severity of his condition, Yu Bowen decided afterward: if he really dies, he would entrust his mother to donate his body, as a way to repay society!

Secondary Transplantation is His Only Hope

Regarding his son's decision, the parents were initially quite surprised. "When I first heard him say he wanted to donate his body, my heart ached painfully," Wen Chunrong said. Thinking that her child was only over 10 years old but already talking like this, her tears couldn't stop flowing. However, Yu Bowen consoled her beside her, saying that so many kind-hearted people had helped their family before, and it would be better to donate the body to repay society after death. After multiple persuasions from Yu Bowen, Wen Chunrong finally agreed to her child's decision.

Yesterday, Yu Bowen's attending physician, Zhu Yiping, told Chengdu Business Daily that after the recurrence of Yu Bowen's leukemia, the only hope was to undergo a secondary transplantation surgery by transplanting bone marrow from his parents. Chemotherapy could only temporarily extend his life. However, the cost of bone marrow transplantation surgery was as high as 200,000 to 300,000 yuan. For this reason, the hospital would make every effort to reduce Yu Bowen's economic burden.

Wen Chunrong said that in order to save the child, they could only sell their house in Victory Village, Fenggu Town, Mianyang City. But compared to the hundreds of thousands of yuan needed for bone marrow transplantation, selling the house was just a drop in the bucket.
