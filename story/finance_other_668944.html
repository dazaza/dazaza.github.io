The new national standard for rosewood furniture will be implemented on August 1st, but some insiders in the industry believe it will be difficult to enforce.

Recently, the "General Technical Conditions for Rosewood Furniture" National Standard (hereinafter referred to as the new standard) was approved and announced by the General Administration of Quality Supervision, Inspection and Quarantine and the Standardization Administration of China. It will be officially implemented starting from August 1st this year. It is understood that due to the previous rosewood furniture standards having been enforced for 11 years, many standards were unclear, leading to a chaotic rosewood furniture market, making it hard for consumers to identify genuine rosewood.

For the introduction of the new standard, insiders generally consider it a step forward, but they also believe that enforcing the new standard will be challenging and its effects in the market will be limited.

Current Situation:

Chaos in the Rosewood Furniture Market

Counterfeiting, substituting inferior products, and selling fake goods have always been recurring phenomena in the rosewood market. After visiting the Letong Furniture City, I found that there are not many rosewood furniture items in Foshan, but the prices are quite disordered. Due to the variety of rosewood furniture types, non-experts cannot tell what material is used just by looking at the appearance.

Mr. Li, who operates a rosewood furniture store in Letong, told reporters that the rosewood furniture field has always been complicated, and most ordinary consumers lack the expertise to distinguish between genuine and fake pieces. According to insiders, the current chaos in the rosewood furniture market mainly involves issues such as counterfeiting, excessive use of brand labels, and disorderly pricing.

Insiders revealed that under the previous national standard, it was allowed to use a small amount of white edges in rosewood furniture production without affecting the overall structure and aesthetics. However, some merchants do not remove these white edges and use them entirely in the production of rosewood furniture. Some even mix other cheap woods with rosewood, only treating the surface so that laypeople find it difficult to differentiate.

Moreover, most stores selling wooden furniture display signs claiming "100% solid wood," yet among these, many are actually veneered counterfeit "solid wood furniture." Ordinary consumers find it hard to discern the actual materials used with their naked eyes. Some so-called "pure solid wood furniture" uses plywood, particleboard, or even medium-density fiberboard in areas such as back panels and drawer boards, which are less noticeable to consumers. They sell "impure" furniture at "pure" prices, earning high profit margins.

New Standards:

Rosewood Furniture Comes with an "ID Card"

So, can these chaotic situations be resolved? Recently, the new standard was approved and announced by the General Administration of Quality Supervision, Inspection and Quarantine and the Standardization Administration of China and will be officially implemented starting from August 1st this year. It is understood that the newly compiled standard mainly includes ten aspects: scope, terminology and definitions, requirements, quality disclosure, testing methods, etc., for rosewood furniture. This standard clearly defines and regulates the definition of rosewood furniture for the first time and stipulates that the product should be named and labeled based on the main timber species used. Unlike the old standard's restrictions on tree species, the new standard encourages the development and use of new types of rosewood materials and requires testing of the wood before production.

In addition, the new standard explicitly states that each piece of rosewood furniture must come with an "ID card," namely the "Quality Disclosure Card for Rosewood Furniture Products." Currently, the rosewood furniture sold in the market only has simple annotations regarding dimensions and materials, without a unified and standardized labeling system. Therefore, many merchants exploit this "loophole," adulterating and selling counterfeit goods. The new standard mandates that rosewood furniture products must be accompanied by an "ID card," requiring clear and uniform labeling. If consumers find discrepancies between the product and its description, they can use the "ID card" as evidence for complaints and claims.

It is understood that the content disclosed on the "ID card" should include at least the product execution standard, product classification, product name, product model, specifications, production date, primary materials used in the product, product coating and decoration processes, product warranty, and information about the product delivery party (manufacturer or dealer).

Industry Voices:

Limited Effectiveness of the New Standard

Will the introduction of the new standard curb the chaos in the rosewood furniture market and thereby regulate the market?

Mr. Li stated that as a merchant, he believes the introduction of the new standard is a good thing. "It standardizes the relevant standards of the rosewood market, making rosewood furniture more transparent, and allows 'fake rosewood' to be regulated." He said that as long as there is no major conflict between the old and new standards, it would be beneficial for merchants.

Gao Hongbin, the Executive Deputy Secretary-General of the Foshan Furniture Industry Association, similarly believes that the introduction of the new standard for rosewood furniture will play a certain role in regulating the market. "However, enforcement in the market will be difficult because there are many fake rosewood furniture pieces in the Chinese market, and there are insufficient experts to conduct evaluations." Gao Hongbin believes that regulating the rosewood furniture market is not solely effective through the introduction of the new standard.

An unnamed insider in the industry stated that the new standard cannot prevent fakes. "The new standard expands the scope of rosewood, actually giving unscrupulous merchants more room to counterfeit." This person said that rosewood is not just one type of product, making it inherently difficult to define, and the effectiveness of issuing a national standard on the healthy development of the rosewood furniture market is limited.
