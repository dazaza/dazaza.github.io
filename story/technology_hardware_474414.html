Here is a travel guide compiled and shared by Hongyuan Holiday:

Perhaps due to being tired, we all slept particularly soundly that night in Weihai, waking up only when it was completely light outside. Pulling back the curtains, we found it was a rare sunny day. After having breakfast at Home Inn and storing our luggage, we set off lightly equipped for Liugong Island immediately.

The combined ticket price for Liugong Island is originally 138 yuan per person. While queuing, I noticed how clear the seawater was here, much better than in Qingdao. It was this crystal-clear water that carried us towards Liugong Island. During the journey, a documentary about the history of Liugong Island was played on the ship. A humiliating history of the Chinese nation's efforts to resist foreign aggression but ultimately failing unfolded before each passenger, as if reminding all tourists: Liugong Island is not just an island, Hongyuan Holiday, but a carrier of history. It is precisely this point that has been attracting me here like a magnet for many years. This has been my dream: one day, I could personally come to Liugong Island and touch that long-sealed history.

While lost in thought, the ship had already docked. At the call of my companions, I stepped onto Liugong Island with the crowd. Looking up, I saw a huge statue far away that suddenly caught my eye. It was an officer of the Beiyang Fleet, holding a long-barreled telescope and looking into the distance, Hongyuan Holiday, as if he was investigating the enemy situation. At this time, the tour guide told me that beyond the sculpture was the Sino-Japanese War Memorial Museum that we were about to visit.

This is a museum that comprehensively introduces the First Sino-Japanese War. From the historical background of the outbreak of the Sino-Japanese War to the entire naval battle process, Hongyuan Holiday, to the impact of this sea battle on later generations, it provides a detailed introduction. There are many photos and historical materials displayed inside, as well as various physical objects from the naval battles, all salvaged from the nearby sea. Among them are torpedoes with special lethal power. This is the first time I have seen a real torpedo. To be honest, it is much longer than I imagined, about three or four meters. Once powerful in the Sino-Japanese War, it now quietly lies in the museum, witnessing that history.

Next, we visited the Liugong Island Museum Park with the guide and watched excerpts from the movie "Sino-Japanese War." I felt there wasn't anything special in the museum park, just some man-made scenic spots. The only highlight was a sperm whale specimen inside. I had heard about the war between sperm whales and giant squids before, but I didn't expect their enmity to be so intense that they almost always fight to the death upon encountering each other; I used to think sperm whales were the nemesis of giant squids, but it turns out their win rate is only fifty-fifty; I knew sperm whales produce a mysterious fragrance - ambergris, but I've never seen it with my own eyes, let alone know how fragrant it really is. This time, I not only saw the legendary ambergris but also smelled its rich and lasting fragrance. I further learned that this is a special substance secreted by sperm whales to digest the parrot mouth of giant squids, entering the seawater through excrement. Moreover, the longer it stays immersed in seawater, the more concentrated and refined its fragrance becomes, making it higher quality. Here, I am like a child seeking knowledge, and my curiosity is greatly satisfied.

After leaving the museum park, we decided to take the cable car up the mountain to overlook the scenery of Liugong Island and visit the ancient artillery platform on top of the mountain. Sitting in the semi-open cable car, enjoying the refreshing sea breeze, we slowly ascended along the mountain terrain. Perhaps because it is rarely disturbed, the vegetation here grows lush and verdant. Looking around, a herd of sika deer grazing leisurely in the forest pays no attention to our unexpected arrival. Gradually, as the elevation rises, the entire Liugong Island comes into my view. It is indeed a beautiful island, a gem embedded on the coast of the Yellow Sea. At this moment, the heavy sense of history seems to dissipate, replaced by endless admiration for the beautiful landscapes of our motherland.

Soon, the cable car took us to the top of the mountain. Due to time constraints, we did not visit the Monument to the Loyal Souls standing on the mountain but only visited the ancient artillery platform located at the top. It consists of gun positions and barracks. Among them is the world-renowned Krupp cannon. Gently touching it, which once dominated but now is covered with rust, it feels like touching history again. Standing on the top of the mountain, overlooking the sea in front, watching the surging waves crashing against the coast and reefs, it makes one feel broad-minded. I can't help but exclaim: Liugong Island, truly a perfect combination of nature and history.

Gathering is always so brief, and it's time to say goodbye to Liugong Island. Our group took a photo in front of the huge rock inscribed with "Liugong Island" and then waved goodbye to this island I had longed for so much, Hongyuan Holiday.

Standing on the railing of the cruise ship, I saw the Monument to the Loyal Souls standing on the mountain, as if seeing us off. Farewell, Liugong Island.

After getting off the cruise ship, we immediately took a taxi back to the nearby Home Inn hotel to pick up our stored luggage and then headed to the Weihai long-distance bus station. In the KFC opposite the bus station, we solved our lunch problem, and at 3 PM, we boarded the Golden Dragon coach bound for Penglai (49 yuan per person). Thus, the short trip to Weihai officially ended. The city of Weihai left me with a good impression, and I believe I will return here if I get the chance in the future to explore the beautiful sights I missed this time.

Although we have left Weihai, our story has not yet ended, because today is destined to be an exciting day. The story continues after we arrived in Penglai.

Penglai is small, according to the car rental driver's statement, the entire urban area covers only about 20-30 square kilometers. We took a taxi to the Penglai Jin Gangwan Grand Hotel where we stayed. After putting down our luggage, we began arranging dinner for the day.

Being new to the place and unfamiliar with the surroundings, we went to the front desk for consultation. Unexpectedly, we met a benefactor of this trip. She looked like the manager of the lobby, always smiling and patiently guiding us. From her, we learned about Mazhi Kou, close to the fishing village, where you can eat authentic seafood and barbecue. Sure enough, there we found numerous seafood barbecue stalls, reasonably priced and high quality, truly a great feature of Shandong. Since it was mealtime, the popularity there could be described as booming. We picked a table and ordered a full table of seafood plus barbecue. I remember there were crabs, sea snails, oysters, clams, etc., all fresh; plus grilled squid, lamb skewers, chicken hearts, etc.; paired with chilled beer. That evening, everyone enjoyed themselves, eating large chunks of meat and drinking large gulps of alcohol, feeling as hearty as Shandong people. When we were happy, I suggested trying some baijiu, and everyone responded enthusiastically. Soon, someone bought a bottle of Penglai Pavilion from a nearby small supermarket. That evening, everyone enjoyed the happiest and most comfortable meal of this trip. When we settled the bill, the average consumption per person was only over 40 yuan, truly delicious and affordable. The day's trip concluded perfectly with frequent toasts and cheerful conversations.
