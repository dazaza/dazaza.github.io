Chapter 5, March 10th (1) Word Count: 1242

“Based on my observations, you've indeed been a bit off recently,” Xiao Bai said seriously to Old Zhu.

Now, it was about 4 o'clock in the afternoon. The scent of spring was drawing near, as if it were lingering nearby, preparing to suddenly jump out and surprise you. Unfortunately, there are too many sensitive people or things in this world, so this gentle breeze has already silently revealed everything.

Xiao Bai and Old Zhu were walking on land that wasn't quite barren. The soil beneath their feet was actually full of sprouts ready to break through the surface, but unfortunately, we all have blurry eyes and can't see what's coming. The second half of senior year is indeed leisurely: classes have ended, the graduation thesis is essentially just for show, people everywhere are exceptionally friendly due to impending farewells, and besides, spring and summer are both beautiful and vibrant seasons. So, this afternoon, Xiao Bai and Old Zhu surprisingly decided to go for a walk together.

Old Zhu thought for a moment, then shook his head uncertainly, saying, “Probably not... But, have you ever really wanted something, yet were also afraid that possessing it wouldn’t be as wonderful as you imagined? Or, let me put it this way: you're eagerly looking forward to meeting someone, but you're also worried that she might be completely different from what you imagine?”

Xiao Bai kept shaking his head in confusion.

Old Zhu sighed helplessly, wanting to explain further, but as soon as he raised his hands to gesture, he immediately felt it was futile, so he dropped his hands again. Yet, it seemed he still wasn't satisfied with leaving it unexplained, so he stood there sighing.

Xiao Bai looked at him and felt a bit apologetic, so he cautiously asked, “I really don't understand. Could you explain more clearly?”

Old Zhu glanced at him, smiled helplessly, and said, “I can't make it any clearer. It's just this feeling I've had lately, it's been strange. Maybe it's because I always feel like we're about to graduate, and I think my college years haven't been perfect enough, so I want to make up for some deficiencies.”

Xiao Bai finally understood and laughed, saying, “So, now I get it, right? Actually, I've been thinking the same thing lately: how quickly these college years have passed! Many things I wanted to do in high school and planned to do in college only come to mind now, and when I think about them, I feel frustrated! Looking back now, these four years of university – freshman year was already a quarter of college life, sophomore year marked two-thirds being over. Now, it's almost all finished. In this last half-year, there’s nothing left but frustration."

"Frustration?" Old Zhu laughed after hearing this and said, "That summary is really good!"

"By the way, Old Zhu, isn't Qin Hua and Old Tang getting along well? But why did I hear they've been having issues recently?" Xiao Bai suddenly asked.

Old Zhu stopped abruptly, looking rather startled, then smiled and said, "Old Tang? Master Tang? Oh, there's nothing between them, it's just that by graduation time, everyone starts digging up old hearsay stories. Look at them two, calling each other brothers, how close they are, how could there be any problems?"

Xiao Bai nodded and said, "If there's nothing wrong, that's good. I heard that Old Tang has connections everywhere; it doesn't hurt for Qin Hua to be cautious."

Old Zhu also slightly nodded and said, "That's true, but why are you telling me this?"

Xiao Bai smiled and said, "Big Brother, don't you know him well? How can I say anything bad about him? If you get a chance, please convey it for me."

Old Zhu "Hmm"-ed, then continued, "Alright. Look at us two, going out to relax our minds, but still ending up talking about these troublesome matters… Look, look, there's a beautiful girl over there, let's go check her out!"

Chapter 5, Mid-March (2) Word Count: 1579

In Room 405 of the Third Teaching Building, the Student Union was holding a meeting, but Qin Hua wasn't present. Ye Lan glanced at her watch, then looked around at those seated, smiled satisfactorily, and said to everyone, "It's time. Let's start the meeting now. First of all, thank you all for being punctuous even for the first regular meeting. Unlike me, your big sister, you all have many things to take care of. However, by the time you reach my position, you should better understand my feelings - to have a little more opportunity to do something for your classmates, for the entire college, is a good thing. Therefore, I sincerely hope this academic cultural festival will be successful and splendid!" Although her words were indeed kind, and she was very pretty, everyone listening felt somewhat uncomfortable.

She paused briefly, looked at everyone's expressions and eyes, as if pondering, but almost immediately continued, "After careful consideration, I believe that each department should collaborate to accomplish this task. Therefore, during the planning and execution process, there will be no division by departments. Instead, we will establish a unified Cultural Festival Organizing Committee. My temporary idea is as follows: if there are any inappropriate places, everyone should feel free to speak up, and we can discuss them. First, I will take overall responsibility and handle communication. Ouyang, you will lead the conference group, responsible for all venue arrangements, volunteer coordination, including the purchase of souvenirs and all conference affairs. These tasks are messy and hard work, and not particularly glamorous, but I hope you will do your best."

Ouyang was a rather refined young man but evidently experienced, who readily agreed without hesitation. Ye Lan smiled approvingly and continued, "Next, Li Qing. You are originally the publicity minister of the school, so continue doing your usual job, responsible for all publicity activities, and ensure thorough documentation throughout the event, taking lots of good photos. We can create a beautiful pictorial collection at the end."

Li Qing was initially somewhat apprehensive, but upon realizing the task was relatively simple, breathed a sigh of relief and nodded vigorously. Ye Lan looked at her adorable expression and couldn't help but almost burst out laughing, then continued assigning tasks: "Li Qianfan, you will be our chief manager responsible for financial matters. Of course, our basic funds are already secured, but Teacher Tang specially discussed with me through Qin Hua, insisting we must strive to secure more funding and support. Thus, although the pressure may not be immense, it's certainly not negligible. This activity has financial backing, but future ones may not always be as abundant, so raising some funds now will definitely benefit future events. That depends on you. As for Jiang Yu, your task will be more challenging. This cultural festival is not just about showcasing economics and management; more importantly, we aim to display the humanistic spirit embedded within, finding the lineage of cultural heritage. Only thus can this event become a true celebration. Therefore, selecting appropriate, profound, and widely acceptable topics becomes crucial. I know you have always loved reading, hailing from Jiangnan, and are confident as a talented individual—please understand, I mean no sarcasm, but I do expect you to complete this task well and showcase your own brilliance. By the way, remind you, firstly, you may not be entirely up to the task, and secondly, even if you are, you should seek collective wisdom. Discuss thoroughly with everyone. Especially, you have two exceptionally talented seniors in your year, who you can consult frequently. One is Xiao Bai, the only undergraduate selected among the 'Top Ten Academics' in the past thirty years, rarely encountering academic problems he can't solve; the other is Old Zhu, excelling in poetry, calligraphy, and painting, having served concurrently as president of the school's literary society and calligraphy-painting society. Matters of culture, you can certainly discuss with him."

She spoke with such grace and warmth that Jiang Yu was already unknowingly drenched in sweat. Ye Lan saw this and pretended not to notice. She knew since her third year that there was a capable younger brother called Jiang Yu who had published a poetry collection and a novel collection, but was overly arrogant and unpopular with others. Now, given the opportunity, Ye Lan naturally intended to properly educate him, ensuring he didn't turn into another wooden figure skilled only in poetry and prose.

However, with just a few words, everyone re-evaluated Ye Lan. Initially, everyone thought she was unfamiliar with most people and rumored to have an extremely decisive style, which made them all uneasy like Liu Qing. Now, though the image of a robust iron lady remained unshaken, her sharp side was enriched while the brutal aspect seemed lacking in real support. Moreover, her subtle and unintentional satire of the consistently arrogant Jiang Yu showcased her girlish nature and playfulness, making everyone feel a bit closer to her—after all, who didn't know that Ye Lan was the top female talent of the Business School? There was no need for Xiao Bai and Old Zhu to serve as contrasts.

Chapter 5, March 10th (3) Word Count: 1565

"You absolutely have a problem recently, I'm becoming increasingly certain!" Xiao Bai panted as he ran behind Old Zhu, still clinging to his initial judgment.

"Is that so? Hurry, hurry, what if that beauty changes place soon?" Old Zhu grinned mischievously.

"So far away, how do you know she's beautiful? Don't you remember the graceful silhouette from 'Tang Bohu Points to Qiuxiang'? Besides, even if she truly is beautiful, does she necessarily want to be seen by you? I really don't understand you, why are you so enthusiastic?" Xiao Bai complained unhappily.

Old Zhu continued undeterred, pulling Xiao Bai along as he walked and smiled, "I've long said you're book-smart but life-dumb! On such a fine day, with lush grass and blossoms falling, spring light rippling across waves, green mist covering the water, the scenery is so beautiful that it uplifts your mood. Why don't you know how to enjoy life? I'm not seeking beauties; it's all about the mood. I often drag you around looking for beauties, but have I ever asked for their phone numbers? So whether she's beautiful or not makes no difference to me. It's about seizing the moment and returning contentedly... Are you lacking knowledge?"

"What lack of knowledge? Just enjoy this season and come out for a walk. Why act like you're imitating Wei-Jin elegance? What's so great about it? Huan Ziyeh would merely exclaim at beautiful landscapes, not lose himself like you do every time you see a girl..."

Old Zhu chuckled and said, "Alright, alright, stop showing off your ancient texts. Furthermore, whenever we see a beauty, isn't it always you who loses yourself? Especially that girl in the library last time, she nearly drove your soul out of your body! Look at you, shouting her name at least a hundred times a day in the dormitory, claiming everything you do is for her, despite not even knowing her name. Thinking about it gives me headaches!"

Xiao Bai reflected but found no fault: "I just think she's amazing. I keep thinking about her, and I don't care about knowing her name. So what? Didn't you just talk about Wei-Jin elegance? I can't stand you, loudly discussing in front of so many people... Ah, my stamina really isn't what it used to be. After running for a bit, I'm already tired. Remember, in our first year, we'd wake up early to run, then play basketball for four hours, eat something random for lunch, and swim in the afternoon. Who knew fatigue back then? Sigh, slow down, slow..."

After a chaotic chase, strategic route determination, and infinite deliberation, Old Zhu and Xiao Bai finally managed to circle around in front of the beautiful silhouette, about 50 meters away. They sat down on a bench and began panting heavily. Old Zhu, gasping for breath, laughed and asked Xiao Bai, "How's that? Good, good, isn't it fun?"

Xiao Bai had nearly collapsed dozens of meters before, now struggling to catch his breath, barely glaring at Old Zhu and muttering, "Fun? Our aesthetics differ..."

Old Zhu immediately put on a serious face, pretending earnestness, "That's not it! People need the sensitivity to discover goals, the courage to pursue higher aspirations, and unwavering perseverance and confidence. Otherwise, how can one succeed? This time, though it seems I'm bringing you here to chase beauties, in reality, I'm teaching you an important lesson in life!"

Xiao Bai rested for a while, finally regaining some strength, and couldn't help but laugh, "Stop holding back, laugh if you want to. Why wear a grim face like a turtle shell? Hey, look, look, your target is heading down that road!"

Old Zhu couldn't hold back his laughter anymore, but upon hearing this, hurriedly turned his head and saw that the girl had turned onto the path leading to the library. Annoyed, he shouted, "Ah, how can she be going to the library? Skipping dinner?"

His voice wasn't too loud, and the girl wasn't too far away, so it seemed she heard. Perhaps glancing back once, unsure who was speaking to whom, she briefly tilted her head and continued walking forward.

Just that casual tilt of her face caused Xiao Bai to leap up like a spring, staring intently at the girl while speaking to Old Zhu, "It's her! It's her!"

Old Zhu, surprised by Xiao Bai's intense reaction, momentarily froze but quickly recovered, nodding vigorously to affirm Xiao Bai's keen observation, then immediately began shaking his head, saying, "If I were you, I'd declare my feelings right now!"

Xiao Bai's face twisted, seemingly torn between laughter and bewilderment, yet sensing something unusual. He looked at Old Zhu strangely and said, "You might as well ask me to jump into the Unnamed Lake!"

As Xiao Bai spoke, he stared dumbfounded at the girl's retreating figure.
