My Love, on the Day it Faded Away

My Love

Came with the swirling autumn leaves and brisk autumn winds,

Pure yet slightly bashful like the snowflakes that drift in winter,

Thrilled by the gentle spring rain and vibrant blossoms of all hues,

Yet slipped away amidst the scorching sun, chirping cicadas, and lush greenery during a rainy midsummer.

Perhaps love never truly came at all,

But it faded so completely.

The sentiments deep within my heart,

Thus, became indifferent.

The gloomy weather always accentuates sorrowful feelings, and today is no exception. Entering the exam room, the rain was falling; stepping out of the exam hall, the rain continued, though with a bit more ease, also accompanied by some loneliness. Lately, my mind has been preoccupied with worries, and only exams can bring me back to the path of learning. Sometimes melancholic, sometimes lonely, sometimes conflicted, sometimes reconciled, sometimes tranquil, but mostly tinged with a faint sadness and inner turmoil.

Loving someone isn't hard, but not loving someone is incredibly difficult. I've tried to immerse myself in the flowers and fresh greens of spring, their fragrance and vitality; I've tried to lose myself in the glow of moonlight, forgetting the passage of time and past sorrows; I've even tried to let those No. 5 Songti characters in books replace our memories, covering every trace of you within me. But later, I realized all I gained was endless yearning and complete insomnia. The more I try to forget, the more vividly I remember you. Every time I lie down or have a moment's leisure, your image fills the world around me. I've tried to make longing a habit. I fell in love easily, but reality forces me to let go, and thus I began the arduous journey of learning not to love.

On the road to chasing dreams, striving alone might feel lonely. Having someone watching over you could make it feel more fulfilling. I chose you as that "someone," but you firmly kept me out of your sight. So, I quietly worked hard, hoping one day I could enter your line of vision, into your heart. I silently watched you, hoping you would notice me in the instant you turned back. But time passed, and you drifted further and further away, blurring my view. You left me forgotten at the other end of the world. Perhaps you are destined to find your prince at the other end of the world, but I just want to tell you: I may not be your prince, but you have always been my princess. You can choose not to love someone, but you cannot stop someone from loving you. And I also learned that there is a kind of love called letting go, and a kind of love called fulfillment.

No one knows that the campanula once shattered its heart, no one knows that the daisy in the garden so adored the skylark, and no one knows that the stars have shed silent tears for the moon. Spring slipped away unnoticed, then early summer, midsummer, late summer, and summer itself quietly passed, without grandeur, without dramatic ups and downs, without shattering hearts. Only upon reflection did I realize that I had lost a love, a love that perhaps never truly came. Thus, I can only carry my scarred heart to encounter the falling leaves of autumn.

The distance between two cities is too far, far enough for us to forget each other's scent; the distance between two cities is also close, close enough for us to still miss every little detail about each other. So what about the distance between two hearts? Maybe it's getting farther, maybe it's drawing nearer.

In the end, I just want to say to you: I love you! Even if one day you find your prince, don't forget there was once a boy who deeply loved you. That boy wants you to know, he cannot give you the love you seek, but he hopes you find the happiness you desire.
