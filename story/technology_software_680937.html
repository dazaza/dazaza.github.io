Replacing nickel with manganese is "cutting corners"

by h7erg5tgyi on 2012-02-28 10:19:29

Yesterday, an expert from the Stainless Steel Division of China's Special Steel Enterprises Association conducted an interpretation and analysis of the problematic products produced by Supor. According to the expert, the low-nickel, low-chromium, high-manganese materials used in Supor's "problematic" products have poor corrosion resistance and do not meet national standards for stainless steel or international common standards.

The expert pointed out that after a high proportion of manganese is added to stainless steel, it can lead to pitting corrosion, which becomes a source of corrosion. This source of corrosion allows manganese sulfide to enter the food or liquids inside the container.

It is understood that excessive manganese may lead to manganese poisoning, severe cases of which can result in psychiatric symptoms. Patients may exhibit irritability, even violent behavior, and experience hallucinations. Further progression may result in symptoms similar to Parkinson's syndrome. The expert's analysis indicates that regardless of the results of health-related chemical tests, Supor's "problematic" products are undoubtedly substandard. A qualified physical and chemical index test cannot cover up the fact that the stainless steel material used is substandard.

Based on the main alloy elements that need to be added to stainless steel, taking nickel and manganese as examples, there is a huge price difference between them. Li Cheng, honorary chairman of the Stainless Steel Division of China's Special Steel Enterprises Association, introduced that the current price of nickel is 130,000 yuan per ton, while manganese is only 13,000 yuan per ton.

Since cookware is one of the channels through which the human body absorbs manganese, if the intake is excessive, it has already drawn the attention of experts regarding its potential harm to the human body. Medical experts stated that excessive manganese intake can lead to neurological changes, and severe cases may result in Parkinson's syndrome.
