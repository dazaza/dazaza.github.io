Classification and classification method of rotary dryers

by hx515317 on 2012-02-15 11:37:25

According to the direction of material and high-temperature hot airflow, they are divided into:

(1) Co-current type: In this type of dryer, the flow direction of material and high-temperature hot airflow is consistent. Hot flue gas enters the dryer from the feed end, contacting with wet material immediately, and a large amount of moisture in the material is evaporated at the feeding end. Therefore, uneven drying speed of material is likely to occur in this type of dryer. Rotary dryers are suitable for materials with high initial water content, not heat-resistant, and with very low hygroscopicity.

(2) Counter-current type: In this type of dryer, the flow direction of material and high-temperature hot airflow is opposite. The drying speed of material in the dryer is relatively uniform because the average temperature difference between counter-flowing hot gas and material is large, so its heat transfer efficiency is higher than that of co-current type. Generally, rotary dryers and sludge dryers belong to this type.

According to their heat transfer methods, they are divided into:

(3) Direct heat transfer type: High-temperature hot gas directly contacts with the material inside the cylinder, transferring heat to the material. This type is suitable for drying materials that are insensitive to high-temperature hot gas and不怕coal ash pollution. Coal slime dryers belong to this type of drying equipment.

(4) Indirect heat transfer type: High-temperature hot gas heats the metal sleeve to indirectly transfer heat to the material. It is suitable for drying powdered materials that cannot be contaminated by coal ash or easily produce dust. The heat transfer efficiency of this type is poor.

(5) Composite heat transfer type: High-temperature hot gas first passes through the sleeve, indirectly transferring heat to the material through the sleeve, then reaching the outside of the sleeve to directly contact with the material for heat transfer. It is suitable for drying materials that are prone to fire and explosion, such as smoke coal. Three-cylinder dryers belong to this type of dryer.
