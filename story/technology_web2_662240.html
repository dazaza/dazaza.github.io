Electronic teaching technology and educational hybridization

by phonics6168 on 2012-02-10 02:14:41

In today's fast-paced world, where time has become a critical factor, learning paradigms have undergone significant changes. People hope and need to learn according to their schedules, anytime, anywhere, so learning must be available around the clock, not just delivered during office hours, at home, or on vacation. In this regard, information technology has become a revolutionary vehicle, giving rise to creative learning modes such as e-learning. Any form of learning that utilizes networks for delivery, interaction, or convenience through computer-based instruction (LAN, WAN, or Internet) is referred to as e-learning or online learning. E-learning is an umbrella term that includes distributed learning, web-based education, and computer-based training (CBT) or web-based training (WBT) provided over the internet. As e-learning is self-paced and hands-on, it provides a very rich learning experience that can even surpass the educational quality one might encounter in a crowded classroom. It can be synchronous, asynchronous, instructor-led, or computer-based—or a combination of both.

Furthermore, with the power of communication technologies, today's impact of e-learning affects various aspects of education and training. Now, as a corporate training option, for disseminating information and other information management needs among employees, companies can save millions of dollars in educating and training their workforce through such teaching models.

Although used for various purposes, e-learning is one of the most promising innovations for enhancing classroom teaching and learning after class hours. E-learning allows teachers to develop research materials in more engaging ways by utilizing global networks and communication resources. Providing a wide range of texts, charts, images, videos, and sounds, including virtual reality technology, makes the teaching process interesting and effective, considering the benefits for students in the classroom.

Since learning is a continuous, cultural process, real-time access and learning opportunities should be provided to students after class hours. E-teaching promotes self-paced learning, allowing students to access study support around their speed and convenience. Real-time interaction with subject experts via virtual classrooms further supports student learning, addressing their doubts and making them more proficient in the subject. It is highly appreciated because it eliminates the compromise on quality and saves travel time and costs for both teachers and students, providing economic value.

Despite all these benefits, e-learning has a major drawback: the lack of face-to-face interaction between students and professors. However, quality education for learners' convenience and flexibility hides the economic cost embedded in e-teaching, making it the top choice for today’s knowledge-driven new generation.
