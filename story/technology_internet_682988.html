According to the National Bureau of Statistics data, in 2008, the most important indicator for measuring inflation, the Consumer Price Index (CPI), rose by 5.9% throughout the year. Generally speaking, when the CPI increase exceeds 5%, it is considered severe inflation.

Inflation is the archenemy of wealth, especially for those who have large bank deposits and bonds, as its erosive effect is more pronounced. Therefore, many people resort to investment as a way to counteract inflationary pressures. However, most people only focus on the stock market, often neglecting the importance of "diversified investment," which has caused many investors to suffer heavy losses during events like the 1997 Asian Financial Crisis, the 2000 Dot-com Bubble, and the recent financial tsunami. The key to "diversified investment" is allocating funds across various assets with low correlations in their returns. This way, risks can be reduced—essentially, not putting all your eggs in one basket. Currently, the main categories for "diversified investment" include stocks, bonds, and money market instruments.

The King of Currencies—Gold should be your best strategic investment choice.

Investment Value of Gold:

The investment value of gold is mainly reflected in two aspects: long-term preservation of value and asset diversification.

(1) Long-term Preservation of Value

There are only over 150,000 tons of gold reserves globally, and they are increasing at an annual rate of only 2%. However, the enthusiasm for investing in gold is rapidly rising; in just the second quarter of this year, demand surged by 51%. Rarity increases value, and the tight supply-demand relationship in the gold market determines its long-term value preservation function against inflation. Over the past four centuries, compared to the substantial devaluation of global currencies, the purchasing power of gold has continued to grow. The famous Western economist Keynes pointed out that gold, as the last line of defense and emergency reserve, has yet to be replaced by anything else. This highlights gold's ability to effectively resist changes in currency value and price hikes brought about by inflation.

(2) Asset Diversification

International gold prices are primarily determined by the US dollar, crude oil prices, and international geopolitical factors, with the influence of the dollar exceeding 50%, showing an inverse relationship with gold prices. Under the backdrop of the United States persisting with a quantitative easing monetary policy and allowing expectations of dollar depreciation, more professional investors are buying gold from the perspective of asset allocation to hedge against the risk of a falling dollar exchange rate. The great bull market for gold has arrived, and after breaking the historical high before the financial tsunami, international gold prices continue to rise strongly. This is precisely the optimal time for you to choose gold products as a stable investment to optimize your financial portfolio.
