Online shopping surge leads to logistics "nightmare" and frequent complaints

by anonymous on 2011-04-01 13:29:17

(ECOMmerce Research Center of China News) Recently, the Consumer Complaint Acceptance Center of the postal industry under the State Post Bureau released its latest data: from February 2 to 8, it received a total of 497 consumer complaints. Among these, 450 were related to express delivery services.

It is understood that although most private express companies started operations from February 9, express services still seem to be recovering slowly. "I ordered a few pieces of clothing on Taobao before the Spring Festival, and at that time the merchant said they would deliver on February 10, but the goods haven't arrived yet. When will they arrive?" On February 13, Miss Wang in Beijing still hadn't received her package, so she had no choice but to consult the website's customer service again, and was told to continue "waiting patiently."

Like Miss Wang, many online shoppers have had similar experiences recently. Behind this, group-buying merchants have not yet recovered from the logistics bottleneck encountered during the Spring Festival - express delivery personnel returning home for the New Year and logistics companies closing for holidays. The backlog of goods caused before the Spring Festival has become a big burden for online merchants after the festival.

It is well known that logistics has always been a weak link in the development of e-commerce. During the Spring Festival, online shopping reaches its peak for the whole year, and the logistics distribution problems in online shopping are fully exposed as a result.

The Logistics "Nightmare" Hits Online Shopping

A survey by the Beijing E-Commerce Association showed that the online shopping peak occurred two weeks before the Spring Festival, with sales during this period increasing by 20% to 50% compared to normal days. Dangdang.com introduced that the order volume one week before the Spring Festival and one week after was 20% to 130% higher than usual. However, in the face of huge consumption potential, the "Spring Festival migration" of logistics blocked the way for merchants to make money.

"During the Spring Festival, many logistics companies do not accept orders, or only accept but do not deliver," Wu Bo, CEO of Lashou.com, told reporters from China Business News. Group-buying websites are currently very popular online shopping merchants. Besides service products, there are also many group-buyings that require delivery. "This logistics situation makes online merchants very passive, directly affecting the interests of merchants."

Based on this, Wu Bo-founded Lashou.com terminated group-buying businesses requiring delivery during the Spring Festival, "Logistics companies began to raise prices, and the delivery speed was also very slow, making it impossible to continue normal cooperation."

Besides the newly emerging group-buying websites that are severely constrained by logistics, old online shopping enterprises like Taobao.com cannot escape the logistics "nightmare".

The owner of a children's clothing store on Taobao.com lamented to reporters that the period before the Spring Festival was the best season for clothing consumption all year round. The order volume in the week before the Spring Festival was equivalent to the order volume of a normal month, but because of the lack of logistics delivery services, they could only painfully refuse consumers.

It goes without saying that for merchants such as Lashou.com and Taobao.com vendors who completely rely on third-party logistics, due to insufficient logistics support during the Spring Festival, their business will be greatly affected.

Then, can online merchants with self-built logistics systems safely get through the logistics difficulties?

The answer seems less than optimistic.

"With the support of self-built warehousing and logistics, receiving and shipping orders is guaranteed, but with fewer courier personnel at the end of the year, the shipping speed has slowed down a lot compared to before. Moreover, self-built logistics are limited to a few large cities like Beijing, and many second and third-tier cities as well as remote areas still mainly depend on third-party logistics." An internal staff member of Vancl told reporters from China Business News.

This insider also revealed that although Vancl's logistics and distribution seemed normal during the Spring Festival, a large number of orders were actually backlogged, mostly from other places where some cooperating logistics companies did not accept orders at that time.

In the face of a booming market, various online merchants can only watch helplessly. Actually, this is not what merchants worry about the most. Guangzhou VIPshop Information Technology Co., Ltd. pointed out another concern of online merchants: "Temporary impact on business is secondary; the key issue is that logistics problems will affect the company's reputation." An online staff member of a branded store on Taobao.com pointed out another concern of online merchants.

Reporters learned from various shopping websites that during the Spring Festival, many shopping websites saw a doubling increase in logistics complaints. According to incomplete statistics by reporters, from January 22 to 25, over nearly three days, there were 170 complaints about logistics issues on the Vancl website, accounting for 95% of the total complaints during those three days.

Industry experts also pointed out that logistics is a key service provided by merchants to consumers. If logistics distribution services are not done well, it will be difficult to win the favor and trust of consumers, adding obstacles to establishing the corporate brand.

Online Shopping Logistics at a Crossroads

The series of delivery problems faced by online shopping enterprises have pushed online shopping logistics to the forefront.

Managers of various online merchants have obviously realized this point. "To improve the enthusiasm of couriers, the company has detailed plans to increase salaries and implement shift work during the Spring Festival." An internal staff member of Vancl told reporters that these measures worked well at the time.

Among those who acted quickly on incentives was Dangdang.com. Dangdang.com cooperates with more than a hundred delivery suppliers. To improve the quality of deliveries during holidays, Dangdang.com introduced new reward measures, including increasing single-ticket subsidies and conducting separate evaluations for deliveries within 21 days before and after the holiday, offering up to 100,000 yuan in rewards.

In addition to motivating delivery personnel, making information transparent and allowing consumers to understand possible delivery delays before placing orders has also become an emergency plan adopted by more and more e-commerce companies.

It is understood that recently, under the support of the Beijing Municipal Commerce Commission, the Beijing E-Commerce Association jointly launched transparent delivery services during the Spring Festival with nine online shopping enterprises, including JD.com, Dangdang.com, Joyo Amazon, Vancl, Redbaby Mall, Kuba Mall, Leyou Baby & Kids, Beijing EMS Company, and Zhaijisong.

Notices such as "Due to the Spring Festival holiday, there may be slight delays in product delivery. We apologize for any inconvenience." have already been prominently displayed on the main pages of various shopping websites.

It is worth noting that transparent delivery services are not just about issuing notices. Many online merchants have also increased investment in their consultation departments, providing timely delivery inquiries and urging services to consumers, allowing them to clearly know where their orders are and when they will arrive. When necessary, staff members will also urge couriers.

At the same time, some smart online merchants have begun to advocate consumer self-pickup of goods, which has also become a new measure for many e-commerce companies to relieve delivery pressure.

JD.com is undoubtedly a pioneer in this measure. It is understood that even before this logistics "nightmare," JD.com had already advocated consumer self-pickup of goods, including supporting self-pickup at many of its physical stores.

Clearly, this model now shows its advantages more fully - bypassing logistics, combining offline and online, not only reducing merchants' logistics costs, but also allowing consumers to pick up goods sooner.

Or perhaps inspired by JD.com and the increasingly severe criticism of logistics, some qualified online merchants have begun to layout consumer self-pickup models.

However, some experts pointed out that although the self-pickup measure is practical, not all online merchants have the capability to implement it. Only merchants with physical stores have the conditions to do so. "Salary incentives and transparent delivery have become the preferred choices for many merchants to deal with logistics delivery problems." Experts expressed this view accordingly.

It goes without saying that salary incentives and transparent delivery services require a large amount of manpower and resources from enterprises, which undoubtedly adds to the cost pressure on merchants. "Currently, the logistics costs of enterprises are indeed high, but these investments are essential." Wei Gang, Director of Market Operations at Aibangtuan, pointed out that in the face of the market and corporate credibility, even if merchants have to "foot the bill" themselves to promote logistics delivery, they will spare no effort. (China Business News / Jiang Yongxia)
