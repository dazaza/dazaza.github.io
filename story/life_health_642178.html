8 Types of Fruit Should Not Be Eaten on an Empty Stomach

by huiming on 2011-12-23 11:56:34

Everyone knows that eating fruit can help lose weight. But do you know that some fruits should not be eaten on an empty stomach during dieting because they can severely affect your health. Let's take a look at which fruits these are!

Tomatoes: They contain large amounts of pectin, persimmon phenol, and soluble astringents, which easily react with stomach acid, forming lumps that are difficult to dissolve. These hard masses can block the pylorus, the exit of the stomach, increasing pressure inside the stomach and causing acute gastric dilation, leading to feelings of bloating and pain.

Persimmons: When consumed on an empty stomach, the high amount of stomach acid can react with the persimmon tannins, gums, pectin, and soluble astringents, forming a condition known as "gastric persimmon stone," which can cause heartburn, nausea, vomiting, gastric dilation, ulcers, or even perforation and bleeding.

Bananas: They contain a large amount of magnesium. If eaten in large quantities on an empty stomach, it can cause a sudden rise in magnesium levels in the blood, disrupting the balance between magnesium and calcium, inhibiting cardiovascular function and affecting health.

Oranges: They have a high sugar content and organic acids. Eating them on an empty stomach can irritate the stomach lining, increasing stomach acid and causing discomfort such as bloating and acidity.

Black Dates: Contain large amounts of pectin and tannic acid, which can combine with stomach acid and form hard masses in the stomach. They should especially not be consumed excessively before bed, and people with chronic gastrointestinal diseases should avoid them.

Sugarcanes: They have a very high sugar content and are not suitable for consumption on an empty stomach, as they can irritate the stomach lining, causing stomach pain and fullness. Additionally, eating excessive amounts of sugarcane or fresh lychees on an empty stomach can lead to "hyperosmolar coma" due to a sudden influx of high sugar levels into the body.

Fresh Lychees: They also have a high sugar content and should not be consumed on an empty stomach, as they can irritate the stomach lining, causing stomach pain and fullness. As mentioned earlier, eating too many on an empty stomach can result in "hyperosmolar coma."

Hawthorns: They are sour and aid digestion by promoting qi movement, but if eaten on an empty stomach, they can deplete energy and increase hunger while worsening stomach problems.
