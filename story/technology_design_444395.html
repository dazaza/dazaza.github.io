The differences in various channels are often described in many practical books as "channel breadth (width)," showing that we tend to habitually place them on an equal footing: for a marketing objective, treating different channels equally. However, if viewed from a different angle, the situation becomes completely different.

Nowadays, the role of advertising in promoting brands and promoting products is gradually fading. On the contrary, doing a "down-to-earth" job in channels, terminals, and the product itself is being increasingly advocated by more and more enterprises. For a newly launched product (especially one from a relatively new company), traditional promotional methods often fail to reach the expected penetration goal due to consumer aesthetic fatigue. At this point, we might consider operating certain specific channels as a method for product promotion and brand promotion. Choose those channels that are relatively easy to control and can quickly and on a large scale reach the target market within a relatively short time. Concentrate resources, conduct detailed investigations into the target group, and formulate promotional methods that fit the company's capabilities and are highly attractive to the target group. From the brand’s essence to the core value of the product, to promotional materials, product packaging, price strategy, and communication management with the target group, everything should be comprehensively planned and implemented. The aim is to make the target group recognize the product and brand in as short a time as possible, thereby driving the sales of other (similar) products under the same brand within that group, while using word-of-mouth effects to expand the breadth and depth of brand communication in the target market. This means taking the consumer experience as the core carrier of brand communication, first categorizing the target market and channels, treating channel operation management and audience control as the core tools for brand promotion, reasonably structuring, seizing opportunities, and effectively implementing.

For example: using "group buying" as a channel method for brand promotion, targeting different products and thus resulting in different target market structures and priorities, thereby selecting different corporate (organizational) groups, choosing appropriate specific products (with prices that are widely accepted while also meeting specific needs with high quality satisfaction), accompanied by corresponding promotional materials, product packaging service methods, price and profit management strategies, conducting product promotion (sales). At the same time, ensure the construction management and terminal work of other non-promotional channels to timely meet the secondary purchases of the initial target market consumers.

Next comes the real-time detection and evaluation of the effectiveness of this brand communication. After achieving the objectives of this stage, adjust marketing goals and overall channel operation strategies in a timely manner, and when necessary, appropriately "abandon" or adjust the content of the promotion.

Of course, this does not mean that adopting such a promotional method requires abandoning traditional promotional methods. We can flexibly manage based on the company's own resource conditions and market environment, as long as there is clear prioritization and orderly management.

It is just like how we do not ignore the positive effects generated by the interaction between traditional media and new media when we use various new media to promote brands, enriching the nature of the media used to better serve the spread of the brand.
