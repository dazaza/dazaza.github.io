Bricks for reconstructed houses as soft as tofu when encountering water, newly built houses are removed immediately after completion

by mg716ojz54 on 2012-02-22 09:11:53

Villagers said that there was actually no cement in this pillar.

■ Reconstructed housing residents: We dare not live in such houses.

■ Construction party: The bricks were bought, we were deceived by the brick factory.

■ Brick factory: The supplier passed off inferior goods as good ones, we are the victims.

■ Supplier: Some words are hard to say, there was no passing off inferior goods as good ones.

■ Construction party: All three buildings will be demolished, and a report will be made to the police.

The Qijiang County government built more than ten reconstructed housing units for 500 relocated farmers. Unexpectedly, the new houses were just built and then torn down like old houses. On June 1st, workers from a demolition company in Chongqing swung their hammers, smashing the newly built houses...

On June 4th, I went to the construction site of the reconstructed housing in the Taohuashan Industrial Park in the Qiaohe area of Qijiang County, and witnessed this strange event.

The bricks were soft like tofu.

More than ten six-story residential buildings were being constructed simultaneously in the Taohua Valley, all of them brick-concrete structures. In front of a building that had already been completed, there was a large pile of red bricks.

Chen Shaofu, a member of the Fei'e community in Qijiang County, picked up a red brick at random and easily broke it, just like breaking rice candy.

When they saw me interviewing, the villagers gathered around. They picked up the red bricks on the ground and broke them with their hands, or even crushed them between both palms, turning the bricks into piles of debris in their hands. "Our reconstructed houses were built with these bricks, it's too scary, who would dare to live in them?" the residents of the reconstructed housing said.

"Some of the load-bearing structural columns in the houses have no cement, only a few hollow rebars, which cannot bear any weight." Chen Siduo, aged 64, took me to a hollow rebar structural column. Indeed, there were two structural columns about one meter above the ground that were completely hollow, with no cement inside. And on the top end of the structural column over one meter high, it was filled with cement.

"My goodness, these are load-bearing structural columns, how dare they deceive us like this!" said Chen Pengju, aged 74.

New houses were built and immediately demolished.

At the construction site, I observed that on one hand, new houses were continuously being built, with one after another rising from the ground. However, three newly completed houses were being smashed by workers swinging hammers.

Wei Huawei, deputy director of the office of the Qijiang County Industrial Park Management Committee, told reporters at the scene that the demolished new houses were all built using the substandard bricks that the reporter had just seen. "Fortunately, we discovered it in time, otherwise if people had moved in, it would definitely have been a 'collapsing building.'"

Wei Huawei said that the construction party used this type of substandard bricks to build three buildings, covering an area of more than 3,000 square meters, accommodating 28 households. Additionally, the tops of other several buildings also used a large number of defective bricks, which the government will also partially demolish.

Wei Huawei said that those structural columns without cement filling indeed posed safety hazards, and they suspected someone of sabotage. Currently, the construction party has reported the case to the police.

Residents discovered quality issues.

Chen Xiaojun, director of the office of the Qijiang County Industrial Park Management Committee, told reporters that on May 30th, the management committee received feedback from the residents of the reconstructed housing, saying that the construction party was using problematic bricks, and the buildings would surely collapse afterward. "We were all scared to death and immediately sent people to investigate on-site. Indeed, the bricks had serious quality problems, becoming crumbled in the hand when wet with water, let alone building houses."

It is understood that this batch of substandard bricks probably entered the site on May 7th and 8th. Why did it take so long to be discovered? What was the on-site construction supervisor doing?

Chen Xiaojun said that on May 8th, these bricks looked normal in terms of hardness. On May 20th, the rain soaked the bricks, and on May 21st, the supervisory party found that there were many white substances inside the bricks, and the hardness was insufficient. They had suggested to the construction party not to use these bricks, but the construction party continued to use these bricks to build houses. The supervisory party not only failed to stop it but also did not report it to the government.

It was not until May 30th that the substandard bricks were discovered by the residents of the reconstructed housing. The management committee immediately held an emergency meeting overnight, proposing a rectification plan, and decided to unconditionally demolish all the new houses built with the defective bricks. The construction party will bear all the costs of the demolition and reconstruction of the new houses, totaling more than 2 million yuan.

Chen Xiaojun told reporters that the management committee will not only hold the construction company accountable but also hold the supervisory party Yong'an Supervision Company responsible.

Where did the substandard bricks come from?

On the 5th, I tried to contact Liu Bin, the person in charge of the Shi Qi Construction Company four times, but failed. Wei Huawei, deputy director of the park office, told reporters that the person in charge of Shi Qi Construction Company told them that the company had been deceived by Yezhen Brick Factory.

Deputy Director Luo Wenliang of the Qijiang County Quality Supervision Bureau told reporters that after testing, this batch of problematic bricks numbered about 200,000 pieces, and they were all unqualified products. Currently, the Quality Supervision Bureau has sealed the Yezhen Brick Factory.

Luo Wenliang introduced that coal gangue should originally be added during brick burning. However, instead of coal gangue, limestone was added to this batch of problematic bricks. Lime becomes soft upon contact with water.

Why didn't the factory use coal gangue? Luo Wenliang said that coal gangue costs more than 100 yuan per ton, while limestone is almost free. To save costs, some people added limestone to the bricks.

Who is passing off inferior goods as good ones?

At Chongqing Yezhen Building Materials Co., Ltd., the large factory had ceased production. Li Hua, the person in charge of the company, told reporters that late at night on May 7th-8th, Wang Mou and Liu Mou delivered two truckloads of "coal gangue" to the company. Since it was late at night, the company's quality inspector did not check it and proceeded directly to production, delivering the bricks to the construction site of the reconstructed housing in the industrial park on the same day, resulting in an accident. Now the brick factory has been sealed, production has stopped, and the losses are enormous. "At that time, we didn't know that someone had added limestone to the coal gangue," said Li Hua.

I asked Li Hua to press the speakerphone button on his phone and call Liu Mou and Wang Mou, the suppliers of the coal gangue, to ask about the limestone issue.

Liu Mou said on the phone that he also heard that the brick factory had an accident, some things he couldn't say, but he definitely did not add limestone to the coal gangue. Wang Mou gave the same answer as Liu Mou and firmly denied adding limestone to the coal gangue.

On the afternoon of the 5th, Chen Xiaojun said that the management committee of the park would immediately report the case to the police and capture the counterfeiters through the police.
