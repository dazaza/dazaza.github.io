The Complete Handbook of Starbucks Pretentiousness

by 3985325xscod on 2011-03-30 14:41:20

STARBUCKS is known as one of the three holy places of the petite bourgeoisie in China (the other two are Häagen-Dazs and IKEA), everyone likes to go there to act snobbish. However, before acting snobbish, everyone must have a high awareness of the matter. I have now gained a new level of understanding about acting snobbish. Only I in the whole department had the privilege of studying "Marxist Philosophy" twice, it says: people's understanding of things always shows a spiral upward trend. Acting snobbish has become a kind of performance art for me, it is art! Do you understand? Hmph! (Friendly reminder: STARBUCKS in the United States is similar to our common tea restaurants or beverage shops. In China, if you want to act snobbish, don't expose your flaws.)

Performance art can't do without props, here is the list of props for you:

First, you must bring a magazine. What? "Reader"? Pfft, you're only fit to buy a cup of soybean milk at the train station and squat to drink it. All Chinese magazines are rejected! It must be an English original edition! What? "Reader’s digest"? Are you going to keep bothering me?! According to the Bible of the petite bourgeoisie - Paul Fussell's "Class", even magazines like "National Geographic" and "TIME" will only expose your vulgar petit-bourgeois taste. It's laughable that we always talk about someone being on the cover of "TIME". You should at least take "The Economist", or the Chinese versions of the latter two magazines can be applied for free gifts, I don't tell this to just anyone!

As for mobile phones, you must have at least an iPhone. No matter how bad it is, all versions must be cracked into Arabic or Hebrew, super face-saving. When the phone rings, say "Bonjour!" first, then "Guten Tag!" If you say "HELLO" you wouldn't even dare to greet others.

You also need to bring a notebook, LV branded. After answering the phone, take out your Montblanc pen and write on the notebook, frowning and looking extremely busy.

You must also bring a laptop computer. Women use MacBook, men all use MacBook Pro. Since there is wireless WIFI in STARBUCKS, turn on BT and eDonkey, occupy the network bandwidth completely, so only you can surf the internet, let others envy! Remember to install Jinshan Ciba on your computer before you go, otherwise, you won't be able to read the English magazine.

Never use the coffee cup from the store. Isn't it just a big porcelain cup? Use Hermes, 1000 yuan each. After paying at the checkout, hand over the cup politely and say: "Could you please put the coffee in this cup? I'm not used to other cups..."

You must drink freshly brewed coffee, preferably like eating hot pot, bubbling in front of you, that's called taste.

After sitting down, throw your car keys on the table first, either BENZ or BMW, at least AUDI. Throw them with force, startling everyone, then loudly mutter to yourself: "This German product is really good quality, it doesn't break even when thrown so hard." (Friendly reminder: if you can't afford a car, can't you afford a key? Go to ***)

Your attire must never be suits and ties, it's laughable. You want that leisurely air. First, wear a long-sleeved POLO shirt, then a long-sleeved corduroy shirt, and finally a Scottish plaid wool shirt. What is style? The more collars, the more style. Wear slippers on your feet, it looks like you're always in an air-conditioned room, a nobleman indeed.

Go to the bank and exchange dozens of one-dollar bills. When leaving, leave them on the table. As you walk through the hall, you'll hear the waiter shouting: "Sir, you forgot your money!" That's the result you want. Slowly turn around, smile back at her, and say: "Oh, I got used to tipping in America, you can keep it!"

Then you can walk out of STARBUCKS under the admiration of everyone, the sky is so blue, the air is so fresh, what a perfect snobbish action!
