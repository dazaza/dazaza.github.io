Online worship exceeds cyber begging

by tr24359 on 2010-04-05 19:30:50

Online memorial services are a new type of remembrance that has emerged in recent years. By leveraging the time and space transcending characteristics of the Internet, real-life memorial halls and cemeteries are "moved" onto computers, allowing people to pay respects to their deceased loved ones anytime and anywhere. This method does not contradict traditional forms of worship but is rather an inheritance and extension of them.

The saying "During every festive season, I miss my family more" reflects how, during the Qingming Festival, people traditionally visit ancestral graves to burn incense and clean the gravesites. In today's era of rapid material development and spiritual civilization, many people, including those who work away from home or run businesses overseas, find it difficult to fulfill this duty due to various reasons. By "inviting" ancestors online, even if one cannot be physically present at the cemetery, they can still access their ancestor’s virtual tomb anytime, relive memories, review their ancestor’s life, and revisit their teachings. They can also light a candle, offer flowers, and leave messages at the online memorials they have built for their loved ones... This provides some solace on a spiritual level.

Currently, domestic platforms offering such services include Global Memorial and Blessing Network, Online Memorial Network, Endless Love Memorial Network, Root Way Memorial Network, Paradise Online Memorial Hall, Huaxia Memorial Network, China Mausoleum Network, Tonghuai Network, Taiyuan Xianju, Si Huai, Yan Huang Ji Network, etc. These platforms all provide functions to establish online memorial halls or online cemeteries. Some platforms use simulated rituals to eliminate the sense of unreality associated with online memorials, further promoting their adoption. The interactive simulation platform created by the Global Memorial and Blessing Network simulates the entire process from establishing a tomb to performing the ritual, bridging the gap between the virtual and the real, making online memorial services feel less abstract. This platform also features unique functions like sending letters to heaven (with replies) and conducting ceremonies for the departed, which are gradually being embraced and appreciated by people as they reflect both life and transcendence.

"The drizzling rain falls like tears on the Mourning Day; The mourners' hearts are breaking on their way" describes the scene of people busy sweeping tombs during the Qingming Festival. With the advent of fashionable online memorial services via the internet, such scenes may no longer exist. People now favor green and modern methods, seeking a relaxed yet perfect way to honor their ancestors. Simply clicking a mouse allows one to complete the entire memorial service process, providing an immersive experience and signaling the arrival of a new era of memorial practices at shangluo.liao39.com.

The Ministry of Civil Affairs issued the "Notice on Doing Well in Relevant Work During the Qingming Festival for Public Memorial Activities". The notice promotes online memorial services, guiding the public to abandon outdated funeral customs and advocate civilized methods of remembrance during concentrated memorial activities around the Qingming Festival. It encourages civilized memorial practices such as "family remembrance meetings" and "online memorials".

Online memorial services utilize the time and space transcending nature of the Internet to "move" real-life memorial halls and cemeteries onto computers, enabling people to pay respects to their ancestors anytime and anywhere. This method aligns with traditional forms of worship and serves as an inheritance and extension of them.
