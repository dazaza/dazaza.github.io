Henan Zhengzhou Strong Brick Making Machine Turns Construction Waste into Treasure

In recent years, Henan Strong has found that the construction waste produced by urban new buildings, house renovations, expansions, reconstructions, and re-planning of old buildings for new constructions has reached 2.3 billion tons per year. If such a huge amount of construction waste is not properly processed, it will inevitably bring serious consequences to our entire society and future generations.

Experts have verified that the soil-free brick making machine, cement brick making machine, hollow brick making machine, block brick making machine, and spacer machine produced by Henan Strong Road & Bridge Engineering Co., Ltd. are most suitable for processing construction waste!

Currently, there are mainly two methods for handling construction waste in China. The first method is collection and landfilling. This method not only incurs substantial transportation costs but also occupies large tracts of land, polluting land resources. The second method is comprehensive recycling and utilization. This method generally uses construction waste mixed with various inorganic chemical materials, undergoing reactions at normal or high temperatures, solidifying, and pressing to form building wall materials. Although this is currently a better approach for recycling and utilizing waste, it still has shortcomings. The main issue lies in the relatively high cost of firing bricks, which also brings about secondary pollution problems.

To enhance the utilization rate of construction waste in China and to better recycle and utilize construction waste through the production of building wall materials on a large scale, it is imperative to research new technologies for producing building wall materials. Henan Strong Machinery Co., Ltd. has developed brick-making equipment specifically designed for processing construction waste in order to fully utilize this renewable resource of construction waste. Construction waste, using construction debris as raw material, is processed through crushing and screening to be used as aggregate. Cement, additives, and other admixtures are added and mixed with water to create multiple types of new building materials according to needs, such as concrete solid bricks, paving bricks, wall materials, and recycled pre-mixed concrete.

Construction waste is not "waste." After reasonable resource utilization, it can once again become a valuable resource for human use. Construction waste has been a persistent problem in urban construction, and its resource utilization treatment has drawn attention from all sectors of society. It is believed that the widespread application of construction waste processing equipment will bring new horizons to urban construction development.

There are no absolute wastes or useless things in nature. What may seem like waste or useless now could potentially become a treasure or useful item elsewhere or at another time. Clearly, the comprehensive utilization of construction waste is an effective way to conserve resources and protect the ecological environment.
