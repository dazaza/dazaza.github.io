The procedures for buying and selling stocks are as follows: You must specify whether you want to buy or sell, along with the name (or code) of the stock, quantity, and price. This order is only valid on the day it is placed. The content of the commission includes the abbreviation (code) of the stock you wish to buy or sell, the quantity, and the price. The abbreviation of the stock usually consists of three to four Chinese characters, and the code is six digits long. The code and abbreviation must match when placing an order. Additionally, there are certain regulations regarding the quantity of stocks being traded: the quantity for purchasing stocks must be a whole multiple of 100, but the quantity for selling stocks does not have to be a whole multiple of 100.

There are four methods of commission: counter submission, telephone automatic commission, computer automatic commission, and remote terminal commission.
