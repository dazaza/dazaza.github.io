Seize Structural Opportunities Amidst Plunge

by huaxun880 on 2010-04-19 14:58:33

Today, the two city stock markets continued the falling trend from the previous weekend, both opening significantly lower with large gaps. The Shanghai Composite Index opened at 3096.78 points, while the Shenzhen Component Index opened at 12248.02 points, down by 33.52 points and 168.84 points respectively. Subsequently, under the leadership of real estate stocks, bank stocks, and coal-oil resource-heavy stocks that were continuously hit by negative news, these indices saw a persistent trend of downward oscillation and bottom probing. Currently, heavily influenced by real estate stocks, the Shenzhen Component Index has fallen over 3%, while the Shanghai Composite Index has also dropped more than 2%, showing signs of short-term weakness.

From today's news perspective, although there are some slightly positive messages such as an insider revealing on the 18th that the list of second batch margin trading pilot brokers is expected to be announced early in May, and five brokers not included in the first batch might directly enter the second batch, there aren't any major positive news. Instead, there are more significant negative impacts, making it a clearly bearish situation. First, last weekend, the U.S. stock market and international commodity futures market fell sharply due to Goldman Sachs being charged by the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission for alleged securities fraud, which had a significant adverse effect on today’s domestic securities market operation, especially on heavyweight sectors like coal, oil, and non-ferrous metals. According to the trends of the three major U.S. indexes, although the U.S. stock market does not yet show clear signs of a medium-term peak, it is now likely entering a short-term peak region, which could form an important negative impact on the investment psychology of some investment institutions. Second, following the announcement on the 14th of increasing the down payment ratio and interest rate for second home loans by 1.1 times, the State Council issued a notice on the 17th requiring banks in areas with excessively high commercial housing prices, rapid price increases, and supply shortages to suspend issuing loans for purchasing three or more homes. A series of new measures to control housing prices have caused new developments to show signs of price reductions, accelerating the withdrawal of real estate investors. This news will continue to exert significant adjustment pressure on real estate stocks. Moreover, due to their linkages with heavyweight sectors like banks and steel, as well as their special weight and popularity in the market, they will also have a significant negative impact on the entire market.

Under the influence of major negative news, the market experienced a sharp decline today. From the 15-minute K-line chart of the market, although there isn't a clear sign of a bottom yet, there is a possibility of retesting the support near the 3000-point level close to the annual line. However, after continuous declines, the probability of sustained sharp falls at the current point is not very high. Therefore, aggressive investors with good technical analysis skills can consider moderate low-entry purchases and short-term operations of about 60% position on potential hot sector leaders such as pharmaceuticals and agriculture, and pay attention to possible mid-short term low-absorption opportunities in heavyweight sectors such as insurance stocks.
