LED promotes plant growth, leading to higher yields with lower energy consumption

"Red-blue LED combinations can provide an ideal spectrum for plant photosynthesis and are important artificial light sources for future agriculture and biological fields, especially wavelengths between 400-700 nanometers that are more beneficial for plant factory lighting," said Professor Furutani Toyoki from the Research Center for Environmental Health Urban Horticultural Science Education at Chiba University, renowned in the global greenhouse horticulture community.

At the same time, industry experts generally believe that applying LEDs in agricultural fields has many advantages. For instance, LEDs have a rich variety of wavelengths and offer significant room for adjustment. They can concentrate specific wavelength light uniformly onto plants, regulate crop flowering and fruiting, and control plant height and nutritional components. Additionally, LEDs belong to cold light sources with low thermal load, preventing scorching when placed close to plants, resulting in high light utilization efficiency and energy savings. Moreover, LEDs are small in size, enabling the creation of various shaped devices and lamps that occupy little space, allowing multi-layer cultivation and easy installation, making plant factories compact.

Currently, in greenhouse and shed planting, the light sources used to compensate for sunlight shortages are usually fluorescent lamps, high-pressure sodium lamps, and incandescent lamps. Since fluorescent lamps generate considerable heat, they cannot be placed too close to plants to avoid scorching. This not only increases electricity consumption but also adds to the indoor thermal load, prompting researchers to replace fluorescent lamps with LED cold light sources.

Especially in recent years, numerous research results have proven that LEDs can serve as artificial light sources for tissue culture, greenhouses, and closed plant factories. For example, comparative experiments on plant growth using LEDs, fluorescent lamps, and natural light conditions have shown that plant growth rates under LED light environments are higher than other treatments, evidenced by larger leaf areas, more leaves, faster leaf growth, deeper rooting, and uniform and superior growth.

Great potential for industrial development

Professor Xu Zhigang from the College of Agriculture at Nanjing Agricultural University stated, "Currently, the area of facility horticulture in our country has reached 337 hm2, with solar greenhouses accounting for about 25%, becoming the most profitable important planting industry. In the southeastern coastal regions, the trend of reduced solar radiation received at ground level is more severe compared to the overall national downward trend. Using LED supplementary lighting is an effective way to solve the weak light constraints in facility production."

Currently, the application and research of LED light sources in the field of plant cultivation are increasingly receiving widespread attention worldwide. In countries such as the United States, Japan, Germany, and Nordic Canada, various types of plant factories have been developed, opening up new paths for future agricultural production. Due to shorter daylight hours in Northern Europe, Central Europe, and Canada, LED lighting has tremendous market potential in these countries and regions. Indeed, during this forum, Mr. Udo Van SLOOTEN, Global Director of Horticultural Lighting at Philips Solid State Lighting Systems, clearly stated, "In the future era, urban plant factories will become a major trend, and LED lighting will play an important role in urban plant factories. We need to tell growers that applying and investing in LEDs will be very worthwhile."

Application in tomato growth

Especially in Japan, the application of LEDs in the agricultural field has already demonstrated vigorous vitality. Vegetable "plant factories" relying on artificial light have been established throughout Japan. In plant factories, multi-layer cultivation systems can be arranged, and plant growth is fast. For example, lettuce planted as seedlings can be harvested after two weeks. With just a few dozen square meters, it can produce products equivalent to those from 20 hectares of farmland, with benefits equal to 2000 times that of farmland, and it has the advantages of not using pesticides and being unaffected by climate. Especially under the current global energy shortage, the promotion and popularization of LEDs are receiving high attention from all over the world. It is expected that in the near future, artificial supplementary lighting technology centered on LEDs will inevitably play a role in controllable environments, underground garage lighting, and even bring unprecedented development to this industry.

Urgent new topics to explore

LEDs will become the most promising artificial light source in the future agricultural field and will have increasingly wide applications. At the same time, many domestic and international manufacturers are also entering the agricultural lighting sector. The Philips Research Institute has achieved quite significant results in applied research. Domestically speaking, the series of research achievements displayed by Nanjing Agricultural University, such as "LED Plant Tissue Culture Lamp", "LED Plant Lamp", "LED Intelligent Biological Incubator", and "LED Plant Cultivation Intelligent Light Control System", have already attracted widespread attention in the industry.

However, currently, plant factories still represent an industry more forward-looking than sunrise industries. On the afternoon of May 12, 2011, a special forum titled "The Combination of 'Light' and 'Energy' - Application of LED in Agriculture, Forestry, and Horticulture" was held by the National Semiconductor Lighting Engineering Research and Industry Alliance. Professionals from various fields also expressed their views on LED entering the plant lighting field, believing that there are still many topics to explore in this field currently. For example, growers generally lack a deep understanding of the benefits of LEDs, and LED manufacturers also fall short in shaping lamps suitable for agriculture. Additionally, the mechanisms of single-color LEDs on different plants, appropriate LED combination parameters and their specialized lamps, as well as how to further improve light efficiency and reduce costs, are all pressing issues that need to be addressed immediately.

Some professionals in the industry indicate that chips are key components of LED plant lighting light sources. LED plant lighting lamp manufacturers have high requirements for upstream suppliers' R&D standards, especially the production capacity of products matching specific wavelengths for plant growth, which presents a new challenge for LED chip suppliers.
