How to Use Red Wine for Beauty and Skin Care

Red Wine Bath for Beauty and Skin Care

Adjust the water temperature to 2-3 degrees Celsius higher than body temperature, fill the bathtub with two-thirds water, and pour about five glasses of red wine. Note that the water temperature should not be set too high, as the vitamins, fruit acids, and other nutrients in the wine can easily deteriorate and lose efficacy at high temperatures.

Immerse your entire body in the bathtub. After soaking for a while, gently massage your whole body with both hands until your body feels slightly warm, giving you a feeling of being "slightly tipsy." Under the influence of alcohol, the nutritional substances in the wine enter the skin.

After the bath, thoroughly clean your body with clear water. The alcohol remaining on the skin can take away a large amount of moisture, so it's necessary to apply moisturizing lotion and strengthen hydration care promptly.

TIPS:

◎ Allergy Test: Since wine contains alcohol, even after treatment where some alcohol evaporates, there are still some people with sensitive skin who may be allergic to it. Therefore, before using it for the first time, conduct an allergy test. Apply a small amount of red wine on your elbow or neck; if there are no redness, swelling, or itching symptoms after 24 hours, then proceed with the wine bath or mask.

◎ The key step in DIY red wine bath is selecting the wine. The wine doesn't necessarily have to be expensive, but it must be genuine red wine rather than cheap wine made by mixing grape juice with alcohol. Otherwise, it can easily damage the skin and may cause unnecessary allergic reactions.

◎ Before taking a bath, wash your face first to avoid dirt clogging pores.

Red Wine Mask for Beauty and Skin Care

While bathing, take advantage of the expanded pores all over your body and apply a homemade red wine mask.

◆ How to Make a Red Wine Mask for Hydration, Moisturization, and Brightening Skin Tone

Materials: 10 ml of red wine, two tablespoons of honey, a little pearl powder, 2-3 drops of lavender essential oil (choose essential oil based on skin condition), one sheet mask. 1. Pour the red wine into a sterilized glass cup and soak it in boiling water for about 20 minutes to evaporate some alcohol, avoiding excessive irritation that might trigger skin allergies. 2. After cooling, mix honey, pearl powder, and lavender essential oil into the red wine. 3. Add a few drops of lavender essential oil and place the sheet mask in the prepared mask mixture. 4. Apply the sheet mask on your face, leave it on for about 20 minutes, then remove the mask and wash your face with clear water.

◆ How to Make a Whitening and Spot-Reducing Red Wine Mask

Materials: 20 grams of rice powder, 10 ml of red wine, one egg white, one sheet mask. 1. Use rice powder as the base material, add red wine, and stir into a paste. 2. Open an egg and take out the egg white, beat the egg white until foamy, and mix it evenly into the red wine mask paste. 3. Apply the mask paste onto the sheet mask, smooth it out, and stick it on your face. Remove it after 20 minutes and wash your face with clear water.

◆ How to Make a Red Wine Mask to Prevent Pigmentation and Delay Wrinkles

Materials: 20 grams of rice powder, 10 ml of red wine, 10 ml of milk, one sheet mask. 1. Use rice powder as the base material. 2. Add a tablespoon of milk and a tablespoon of red wine, mix the ingredients together and stir evenly. 3. Place the sheet mask in the prepared mixture, let it absorb fully, then apply it on your face. Remove it after about 10 minutes and wash your face with clear water.

◆ How to Make a Gentle Exfoliating Red Wine Mask to Eliminate Acne

Materials: One apple, 10 ml of red wine. 1. Peel and core the apple, cut it into chunks, and mash it into a pulp. 2. Add an appropriate amount of red wine and mix evenly, then apply it uniformly on your face. 3. Massage for about 15 minutes, then wash off with clear water.
