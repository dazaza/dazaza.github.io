How should pregnant women protect themselves from the sun, and how should they choose sunscreen?

by xiejinhua3 on 2012-03-07 16:54:13

How should pregnant women protect themselves from the sun, and how should they choose sunscreen? During pregnancy, hormonal changes in expectant mothers can lead to pigmentation. Skin care experts at Qinrun indicate that during this time, the color of the areola, midline of the abdomen, moles, or freckles of pregnant women may darken. If they are frequently exposed to the sun, the darkening will become more severe.

Experts at Qinrun believe that some pregnant women may become more sensitive during their pregnancy. If their previous sunscreen is still suitable, they can continue using it. However, if their skin becomes sensitive, pure physical sunscreens are recommended.

In spring, if there is less rainfall and the air is relatively dry, the skin of pregnant women and new mothers tends to become dry and flaky. Extra care should be taken, especially for those with dry skin. According to measurements, the content of ultraviolet (UV) rays in sunlight is highest in spring and lowest in winter. Scientists have also found that people's sensitivity to UV rays is highest in spring and lowest in winter. Therefore, skin care experts at Qinrun remind sensitive pregnant women and new mothers to prevent facial dermatitis in spring. So, how should pregnant women protect themselves from the sun?

Tips for UV protection:

1. Pregnant women with springtime dermatitis can appropriately use external sunscreens such as titanium dioxide cream or soap-free cleansers.

2. Avoid one-time excessive exposure to strong sunlight. When going on a spring outing, wear a wide-brimmed hat or carry an umbrella.

3. Do not use excessively hot water to wash your face, avoid using alkaline soaps, and do not scrub your face with rough towels.

4. After washing your face every day, you can perform a 5-minute acupressure massage or steam your face to enhance blood circulation and improve the skin environment, which helps maintain skin temperature.

5. In daily diet, consume more foods rich in vitamin A, fresh vegetables, and fruits. Ensure adequate daily water intake.

How should pregnant women choose sunscreen? Another common label on sunscreens is the PA value, ranging from PA++ to PA++++++. Although more "+" signs indicate a higher sun protection factor, pregnant women do not necessarily need products with too many "+" signs. Since everyone's skin type differs, when the "+" count is higher, the texture tends to be oilier. Usually, after pregnancy, sebaceous gland secretion increases, making it difficult to accept overly oily sunscreens. Therefore, skin care experts at Qinrun recommend soy milk and rice series isolation creams for different types of pregnant women. Japanese scientists discovered during their research on ancient soy milk beauty secrets that soy milk, naturally fermented from soybeans, contains a crucial component called soy isoflavones, a gentle plant estrogen for pregnant women's skincare. Soy isoflavones can improve the damage caused by female endocrine to the skin, eliminate active oxygen and free radicals in the body, delay the aging of female cells, keep the skin elastic and delicate, and soothe the unease caused by physiological changes in women.
