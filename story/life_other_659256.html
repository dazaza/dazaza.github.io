The things you don't know...

My tears fall like a heavy rain, shattered all over the ground, clear in my heart. Every minute and second I've gone through is like a wooden boat sailing, not knowing where it will drift to or what it will meet.

I often see an isolated island in the distance, covered with grass and trees. When the wind rises, it forms a vast landscape. The scenery reminds me that I have gone through storms, walked through days and nights, and arrived at such a desolate and strange place. When I stepped ashore, looking into the distance, the clouds were swirling, and it was about to rain. The winter rain, cold and mixed with broken ice, melts quickly into water when it hits my hands and slides quietly along my palm lines. Thus, I can't tell whether it's rainwater or seawater, and the vision becomes gradually blurred.

The things you don't know are all in the past, lingering today. People persistently give their best just for a warm embrace, a pair of hands appearing in blurred vision. Hold them tightly, and this world will no longer be just a heavy rain. While walking, I forget the distance, whether it's getting closer or separating. I long so much for a small house to appear ahead, with smoke curling up from the roof. I know that perhaps I am close to my belonging space.

The things you know happened in the youthful years of the past. I once loved a cute and beautiful girl. Standing under the sunset, watching her walk away. It turns out that those times were only memories of mine, something no one knows.

Go and brew a cup of coffee, eating bread full of my mouth. It turns out that I am already hungry and thirsty. Those strangers I met on the journey, do they speculate that I still hold a glimmer of hope? Facing breathing, all I can do is persevere, and the things you don't know are beyond reach.

Many years later, I met a woman who loved me, but I left her so ruthlessly. It was just because I didn't have enough courage to stand by your side, giving you the safety and happiness you want.

You, so proud, seeing other women in my arms, I believe you will leave. After you go, I am lost alone, those years I loved so deeply, it turns out, how easy it was to lose everything. All these are the things you don't know.

How many times does a butterfly blink before it learns to fly? The night sky is filled with stars, but how many will fall to the ground? I fly, but when you fall, we are so close, and I can even hear your breath. We recall bit by bit those carefree times, silly like straw, swayed by the wind, tanned by the sun. These are exchanged by persistence, the things behind you don't know, the sweeter the love, the sweeter the bitterness.
