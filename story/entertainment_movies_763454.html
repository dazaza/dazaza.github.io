Synopsis:

Any success is hard-won, even for normal people. It's even more so for someone like Christy who has cerebral palsy. However, Christy was a man who dared to defy his fate and succeeded. Born on June 5, 1932, this new life did not bring joy to his family. Spasms caused by congenital cerebral palsy made it impossible for him to express his emotions through body language or speech. He could only rely on his one movable left foot to do everything he wanted. Though God did not give Christy a healthy body, he endowed him with intelligence and a skillful left foot. From a young age, Christy loved painting, and his works won awards repeatedly. His outstanding performances not only gained recognition from others but also changed his life. After years of relentless effort, Christy became a well-known painter. At the age of 19, his mother invited Dr. Eileen, a specialist in treating cerebral palsy, to help Christy correct his pronunciation and massage his brain. Under Eileen’s patient care, Christy's condition improved significantly. He could now communicate verbally with others. Meanwhile, Christy gradually fell in love with the kind-hearted Eileen. When he learned that Eileen was getting married, he was deeply hurt and lost interest in picking up his paintbrush for a long time. With the support and help of his family, he emerged from his depression again. While battling his illness, he became a world-renowned artist and writer. Ultimately, he not only achieved success in his career but also, like everyone else, entered into marriage with his beloved.

Behind-the-Scenes Production:

Daniel Day-Lewis' dedication to his role is shocking, earning him the Oscar for Best Actor. Also winning an Oscar was Brenda Fricker for her portrayal of the mother, who steadfastly believed in her son when everyone else thought he was a fool. The film avoids the typical sentimental scenes found in motivational films, offering nuanced and humorous character portrayals, such as the protagonist's attempts at seducing women, which are both amusing and deepen the character.

Trivia:

- Based on the real-life story of Christy Brown.

- In the "Making My Left Foot" segment of the DVD special edition, it is revealed that Daniel Day-Lewis broke two ribs while maneuvering over obstacles in his wheelchair during filming. He also refused to break character, requiring assistance to eat during meal scenes. At one point, his exasperated British agent visited him, but Lewis insisted he was Christy Brown, sending his agent away.
