Journalists were surrounded and beaten when they were investigating and interviewing in Nanxi River, and the assailants are still at large.

Sanyo TV repair - "I happened to see a sign on the tree behind these two women that read 'Bring your own swim圈, charge twenty yuan,' so I took a few steps away and took out my phone to secretly take pictures. The thinner woman ran towards me and asked me to delete the photos in my phone." Xiao Ying introduced that after being refused, the woman went back to where she was sitting, picked up a walkie-talkie and called for help. Soon, more than 10 men from the village gathered around Xiao Ying and demanded that he take out his phone and delete the photos inside. A reporter's arms were scratched and bleeding. There was a notice hanging in the scenic area that read "Bring your own swim圈, charge twenty yuan" (photographed by another journalist).

"At that time, seeing there were many of them, I said that my phone cannot be given to you casually, if you want to see it, call 110 and ask the police station to come over. When I was about to take out my press card to reveal my identity, someone in the crowd shouted 'It's all because of your random picture-taking, you will complain later, journalists are the same, they should be beaten too.' Seeing the situation wasn't good, I quietly waved my hand to hint to my colleague standing nearby to quickly go out and report the incident." According to Xiao Ying's introduction, at this time, more and more people gathered around. Several men rushed forward and twisted Xiao Ying's hands, one person grabbed his neck, and others hit him on the back. In a panic, Xiao Ying kept explaining to them not to hit anymore, and that things can be discussed properly. But one man said, "Don't say anything, if you keep talking, we'll beat you to death. Quickly delete the photos in your phone."

At around 2:40 PM, when they arrived at a place near Lion Rock where swimming circles and bamboo rafts were rented out, they heard a tourist arguing with two women. The tourist asked the two women, "Why do I still have to pay 20 yuan when I bring my own swimming circle to swim?"

[Exclusive Report by Wenzhou Network] On the afternoon of August 26, two reporters from Wenzhou Network were investigating and interviewing an issue near Lion Rock in Nanxi River, Yongjia, regarding the charging of 20 yuan even for bringing one's own swimming circle. They encountered a group beating by more than ten villagers who claimed to be managers, resulting in one reporter being injured. As of 10 PM last night, the attackers were still at large.

According to the injured reporter Xiao Ying, a few days ago, they received a tip-off from netizens that the sewage discharge problem along the banks of Nanxi River was very serious. He and his colleague Xiao Wan went to the Lion Rock scenic area of Nanxi River yesterday afternoon to understand and interview the situation.

As of 10 PM, when Xiao Ying and his team left Yantou Police Station, the police said they had not yet found those involved in assaulting the reporters. This website will continue to follow up on this matter. (Reported by Wenzhou Network)

At around 3:15 PM, the police arrived at the scene, and Xiao Ying pointed out the main assailants on the spot to the police. However, the officers only took the injured Xiao Ying to Yantou Hospital for some treatment, then brought Xiao Ying and Xiao Wan to Yantou Police Station to make statements, but did not bring the villagers Xiao Ying identified as the attackers to the police station for questioning and making statements. Later, due to the persistent pain in the back from the beating, under the guidance of other colleagues who subsequently arrived, Xiao Ying went to Yongjia County People's Hospital for further examination. The doctor preliminarily diagnosed it as "bruising of soft tissue in the waist and back."

After being surrounded and attacked by more than ten people for several minutes, for his own safety, Xiao Ying reluctantly followed their demands, took out his phone, and deleted all the relevant audio, video, and photos they requested. By this time, Xiao Ying had been beaten in multiple places, with multiple scratches and bleeding on his right forearm and multiple bruises.

Out of professional sensitivity, and recalling some posts seen online recently mentioning the arbitrary charging here, Xiao Ying went up to these two women and asked, "Why do you charge 20 yuan?" One of the thinner women said, "This river is contracted by our villagers, we have to charge, don't ask why."
