Two men blocked the ATM cash outlet at night, and the 400 phone number became their enemy

by cu22f009 on 2009-12-08 17:49:59

On May 21, an employee surnamed Peng from the business department of a bank's Sichuan branch reported to the Chunxi Road police station that the bank had set up an automatic alarm function for ATMs. At 12:05 AM on April 4, the control center received an alert from a self-service bank inside Jinjiang District’s Fanku Street. Upon reviewing the remote surveillance footage, the staff noticed a man attaching something to the cash dispensing outlet of an ATM. In a hurry, the staff addressed the man through a loudspeaker. Startled, the man fled in panic.

Subsequently, the staff arrived at the scene and found that both an automatic deposit machine and an automatic teller machine's cash dispensing outlets were glued shut with strong adhesive. Additionally, each machine had a 10 square centimeter sticker attached to its front. The stickers bore text instructing users to call a "400" telephone number if they found the machine malfunctioning to contact the "bank." It was later confirmed that the "400" phone number was used by scammers and had no relation to the bank. The scammers exploited the powerful service features of the "400" phone, prompting the "400" phone service provider to warn everyone about such fraudulent activities to protect the safety and credibility of the "400" phone market.

On the same day, the bank discovered identical issues on the ATMs located outside the Dongfeng Road North First Alley, Liberation North Road Section One, and Caoshi Sub-branch North Sub-office. All machines displayed the "400" phone number. The bank immediately cleaned the contaminated machines, but ultimately four machines were damaged beyond use, with each replacement costing 7,200 yuan, resulting in a total loss of 28,800 yuan for the bank. On May 7, the bank received a notice from the Chongzhou branch discovering that the man who tampered with the ATM in Chongzhou shared similar characteristics with the man captured on camera at the Jinjiang branch. On the 21st, after learning that the man and his accomplices had been apprehended by the Chongzhou police, the bank immediately sent Peng to report the case to the Chunxi Road police station. The next day, the Jinjiang police criminally detained the two suspects. The prosecution revealed that the two men were a 29-year-old Guangdong man nicknamed "Monkey King" (Liu Fumo) and a 40-plus-year-old Guangxi man surnamed Li. "Monkey King" confessed that one of them would sleep during the day while the other would scout out which ATMs could be tampered with, leaving behind a "400" phone number as a way to "contact" the bank. They would act every nightfall. Before coming to Chengdu to commit crimes, they specifically watched a DVD to learn how to operate.
