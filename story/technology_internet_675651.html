Concerns about the future development trend of the 5D film industry

by abcsftkb on 2012-02-25 11:19:42

The Leike research group [http://www.leike5d.com/] expressed that we are very confident about the development prospect of 5D movies [http://www.leike5d.com/] in China. After all, this emerging industry is driving a series of technological progress and enhancing the entirely new movie experience. However, as an industry leader, we have to admit that there are still shortcomings in this developmental process that constrain the development trend of the 5D movie [http://www.leike5d.com/] industry.

As the saying goes, "Worry before the world worries", confidence is one part when doing anything, but blind optimism can also lead to failure. Therefore, the people of Leike always temporarily stop to think during the process of moving forward: what problems still exist now, and how should they be improved?

The constraining factors for 5D movies [http://www.leike5d.com/] at this stage lie in the imperfection of technology, and there is still a long way to go. The reasons are as follows:

1. The key reason is that software support is temporarily insufficient, or in other words, the film sources are not rich enough. Because 5D film sources mainly rely on the conversion of 3D film sources, adding dynamic seats and special effect systems compatible with 5D makes the production difficulty quite high. Moreover, there are less than 200 excellent 3D film sources worldwide currently, and even fewer can be converted into 5D movies [http://www.leike5d.com/]. Compared to traditional films, the quantity is negligible. In terms of the current domestic 5D market, Leike can provide 60 film sources within a year, mainly including types such as literature and art, science popularization, education, adventure, time-space exploration, etc., which alleviates the audience's demand for more new films to a certain extent. But converting more and richer film sources into 5D remains an unchanging goal for Leike.

2. Limited audience awareness. Since 3D technology has only just matured, and due to the excessively high shooting costs, crude pseudo-3D has become rampant, greatly affecting the development of potential users. A considerable number of viewers have lost interest in 5D movies [http://www.leike5d.com/] due to the harm caused by pseudo-3D films, which is a great loss for the film industry. Moreover, 5D cinemas generally appear in tourist attractions, science and technology museums, animation cities, etc., and the number of audiences is still limited. This aspect needs further expansion.

3. High selling price of 5D cinema [http://www.leike5d.com/] equipment. Fewer cinemas, higher investment, and more difficult maintenance compared to traditional cinemas. Building a 5D cinema requires a cost investment of several million yuan, making many investors hesitant. Moreover, most domestic 5D movie equipment manufacturers can only provide one year of after-sales service, causing investors to be wary. In response to this, Leike was the first to propose a franchise model for 5D experience halls, reducing the scale of 5D theaters to small experience halls with six seats, significantly lowering the franchise threshold, thus triggering a domestic 5D movie franchise [http://www.leike5d.com/] craze. However, from a national perspective, the popularization rate is still not high, and the era of universal 5D is still a certain distance away.

In summary, regarding the currently popular 3D cinemas, transforming them into 5D is just a small move in terms of hardware; however, from a developmental perspective, in the current strong momentum of 3D, the rise of 5D cinemas [http://www.leike5d.com/] is not a simple matter. This road is arduous and long, standards need to be unified, and only through the concerted efforts of everyone in this industry can we realize the dream of universal popularization of 5D movies [http://www.leike5d.com/].
