The compulsory insurance inquiry system has been launched.

by xuntian8746 on 2012-02-13 16:45:25

Since 2009, the Beijing area has successfully built a mandatory insurance inquiry platform (compulsory traffic insurance joint information platform). Twenty-three property insurance companies have achieved network interconnection and can share approximately 40 million pieces of information. This information platform has nine functions, and insurance companies can use real-time uploaded vehicle information to block loopholes such as false claims and insurance fraud.

Insurance fraud has become a thing of the past. The compulsory insurance inquiry information platform was jointly constructed by the 23 property insurance companies operating compulsory traffic insurance in Beijing, all of which have achieved network interconnection. Real-time upload of auto insurance claims information enables industry-wide sharing. If the insured party has objections to the claim information, they can query the information platform at any time, which serves as a certain filter and deterrent against the creation of false claims by unscrupulous repair shops or internal company personnel. Beijing traffic management departments can also use the information platform to manage vehicle registration, annual inspections, vehicle checks, and road checks, eliminating the possibility of fake compulsory traffic insurance policies or offline issuance of policies. Additionally, the compulsory traffic insurance information platform has developed a unified management module for damage assessment personnel's professional information, which has been opened up for inquiries by all companies, standardizing the professional behavior of auto insurance damage assessors.

Insurance experts believe that the compulsory insurance inquiry platform is an information-sharing platform between insurance institutions and public security traffic management departments. It aims to improve the capital's traffic congestion and lays a good foundation for scientifically and reasonably determining the compulsory traffic insurance rate based on accumulated true and complete underwriting and claims data, adhering to the principle of non-profit operation, and effectively safeguarding the legitimate interests of the insured.

Rewarding good drivers and penalizing bad ones: Similar to Beijing, most regions across the country have established compulsory insurance inquiry systems and achieved information sharing on this basis, serving as a wake-up call for car owners who try to game the system.

For example, since August 1 last year, Xiamen launched a vehicle insurance information sharing platform including compulsory insurance inquiry, allowing all insurance companies to check the repair and claims status of any vehicle insured in Xiamen over the past three years through this platform. In other words, vehicles with frequent repair and claims incidents will find it almost impossible to obtain discounted insurance rates by switching insurance companies, thus realizing the fair principle of rewarding good drivers and penalizing bad ones.
