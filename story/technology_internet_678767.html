Skills and Precautions for Brewing Tieguanyin Tea

by meitaocha123 on 2012-02-27 16:30:37

& nbsp; Characteristics of Tieguanyin tea tasting [http://www.teaguest.com/article-348.html] focus on savoring the aroma rather than the appearance. First, one should smell its fragrance and then taste its flavor because the brewing method is highly emphasized. From the preservation of tea leaves, water temperature for brewing, to brewing time, there are certain requirements. So how do we brew Tieguanyin to bring out its proper flavor? Below, I have collected and organized nine points to share with you.

　　First, tea is most susceptible to contamination by other odors, so it must be isolated from other smells, especially alcohol;

　　Second, Tieguanyin tea is generally packaged in sachets. This type of tea packaging uses vacuum compression packaging, often accompanied by outer canister packaging. It usually only needs to be stored in a cool place, away from light. If you want to achieve the best results and longest storage time for preserving Tieguanyin tea, it is recommended to store it at -5 degrees Celsius in a freezer, which will yield the best results.

　　Third, the water used for tea is very important. Spring water is the best water for brewing Tieguanyin. In the absence of spring water, unpolluted river water, lake water, or well water becomes suitable water for brewing Tieguanyin. Tap water contains more chlorine, which can harm the tea's flavor. It is best to store it in a barrel for a day or two, allowing the chlorine to dissipate before using it to brew tea. Alternatively, you can extend the boiling time appropriately to drive off the chlorine before brewing tea. Nowadays, many teahouses use mineral water, purified water, or distilled water to brew Tieguanyin, and they can still produce good tea.

　　Fourth, tea tasting should involve some good tea friends. Drinking tea is also an exchange of emotions among friends, using tea as a medium;

　　Fifth, the normal color of tea is generally light yellow. Mid-range and above Tieguanyin mostly appears bluish-green or bluish-green with a golden hue;

　　Sixth, Tieguanyin tea produced in the origin area [http://www.teaguest.com/] has a lingering aftertaste known as "huigan," which can only be felt half a minute later when the tea's chemical components interact with the salivary glands;

　　Seventh, tea must be consumed immediately after brewing and should not be left too long in the pot;

　　Eighth, Tieguanyin tea must be brewed with freshly boiled water;

　　Ninth, it’s best not to taste other foods while drinking tea.

　　Precautions:

　　Do not drink Tieguanyin while taking medicine, as consuming the decoction of these medicines together with tea can cause precipitation and affect the efficacy of the medicine. At the same time, tannic acid in tea has astringent effects and can prevent the absorption of proteins and other nutrients by the human body. Therefore, when taking tonics such as dangshen, huangqi, and yam, drinking tea, especially strong tea, can reduce the efficacy of the medicine.
