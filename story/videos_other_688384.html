Who benefits from the madness of art auctions?

by xyxandy8 on 2012-03-03 10:11:54

Who Benefits from the Frenzy in Art Auctions?

This year, the art auction market in Fujian Province has been exceptionally active. According to rough estimates by industry insiders, there have been no fewer than 20 art auctions held or scheduled between November and December alone.

With so many art auctions taking place, how many of these items are truly worth collecting? And behind the rush of auction houses getting involved in art auctions, who is really benefiting?

Warm Reminder:

Industry experts point out that "those with eyes buy genuine pieces, those without buy fakes," which has always been a centuries-old rule in the art trade. Although in Fuzhou,寿山石 (Shoushan stone) auctions are generally authentic, newcomers to the field should still approach rationally and not blindly follow trends.

As investors, one must first adjust their mindset: learn professional knowledge and art appraisal skills; secondly, make friends with like-minded people; and lastly, focus on preservation and appreciation.

"During turbulent times, gold is sought after; during peaceful times, collections are valued. Nowadays, more and more people are blindly entering the market without understanding the situation, resulting in paying large amounts in tuition fees."

— Qi Zhongfu, President of the Fuzhou Collectors Association

Current Situation:

One or Two Auctions a Year → Three Auctions in a Day

"Recently, I've been receiving non-stop SMS messages about various art auctions, it's too dense, I can barely keep up." Ding Jiannan, Executive Vice President of the Fujian Collectors Association, frankly admitted that in the past, art auctions were held once or twice a year, then once per quarter, once per month, and now multiple times a week, which is abnormal. It is understood that on December 18th alone, Fuzhou had two simultaneous auctions: one was a joint effort between Fujian Lipu Auction Company and the Shoushan Stone Cultural Exchange Center titled "2011 Overseas Shoushan Stone Collector's Fine Art Auction"; the other was organized by Fujian Trade Trust Auction House called "2011 Autumn Art Auction." According to collector Zhou Ye, Fujian began to see some art auctions around 2005, but at the time, the success rate was very low, with only two or three auction houses conducting art auctions. In recent years, especially this year, there has been an explosive growth. "According to what I know, for example in Fuzhou, there are approximately 60 auction houses registered, among which over 20 are involved in art auctions." Some insiders lamented that although Fuzhou is the resource center for Shoushan stone, having two auctions on the same day, or even three consecutive auctions from morning till night, is overly concentrated and somewhat abnormal. Many items seen in one auction reappear in another auction held by a different company just two weeks later.

Behind the Scenes:

Blind Entry into the Market → Many People Get Stuck

"Especially since the beginning of this year, countless people have blindly entered the market without seeing through the layers of fog surrounding the auctions and have gotten stuck." Zhou Ye frankly admitted that although Fujian has not yet seen fraudulent cases like those in Beijing and Shanghai, such as the Xu Beihong seven million yuan fake painting incident, many people have still been trapped. "Originally, they thought they bought a piece worth over a million yuan from a famous artist, only to find out that the market price is only thirty plus ten thousand yuan, leaving them stuck with it." Citizen Mr. Li from Fuzhou is still regretful about a Shoushan stone carving he impulsively purchased earlier. Originally, Mr. Li decided to enter the art market this year to try his luck. He frequented various auctions in Fuzhou, learning about the hottest "famous" Shoushan stone carvings, preparing to act when the time was right. A few months ago, he noticed that the price of works by a certain Shoushan stone sculptor kept rising, reaching over a million yuan, making him feel the time was ripe. So, he started looking for this sculptor's works in the market. "Not long after, I learned that someone had the sculptor's work for sale at just forty to fifty thousand yuan, and I immediately jumped at the opportunity." Without hesitation, Mr. Li spent around fifty thousand yuan to purchase the sculptor's work. However, soon after, Mr. Li received a harsh blow: the market situation had changed, and the expert appraisal of the piece revealed its value to be only thirty plus ten thousand yuan. An insider pointed out that actually, Mr. Li fell victim to speculators. Zhou Ye told reporters that "currently, the attendees at auctions mainly consist of three types of people: collectors, who collect to sustain their collection, capable of long-term investment, accounting for about 10%; those with a casual attitude, mainly entrepreneurs and wealthy individuals, not concerned with cost, primarily for gifting purposes, accounting for about 40%; and more than half are people seeking preservation and appreciation, and these people are often easily exploited by speculators."

Inside Information:

1. How Much is Actually "Real" at Auctions?

Due to familiarity with responsible persons from several auction houses within the province, since the beginning of this year, Mr. Zhang, a Fuzhou-based appraiser expert, has frequently been invited to participate in art auction appraisals.

"Two months ago, I was invited by a well-known large auction house in the province to attend their autumn auction. After reviewing, I found that about 40% of the items were 'real.'" Mr. Zhang said that upon hearing this, the auction house's responsible person was delighted, indicating that this result was already satisfactory. The "real" here refers to items being worth their value, possessing collectible value.

Mr. Zhang candidly stated that with such frequent art auctions being held this year, it is impossible for everything to be a masterpiece. "However, this is normal. After all, auction companies are enterprises, and auctions themselves are commercial activities. Under tight schedules and heavy tasks, it is impossible for a manager or business manager with merely a few years of experience to accurately grasp all art pieces."

Industry insiders directly pointed out that taking calligraphy and paintings as an example, if every collected piece followed the opinions of museum-backed experts, buyers would be pleased, and authenticity would be maximally guaranteed, but then the auction house might only be able to hold an auction every two to three years, making it hard for the auction house to survive.

Zhou Ye further pointed out that "in fact, only about 10% of the items in an auction are true masterpieces, and many people confuse art with craft art."

Yao Chunmao, vice president of the Fujian Precious Stones and Jade Association, whose main business is operating jewelry stores, also serves as the deputy director of the Chinese Folk Arts Association's Shoushan Stone Special Committee and is also a major customer at the annual spring and autumn Shoushan stone auctions.

He said, "with so many art auctions, it's hard to ensure that none of them are engaging in 'underhanded operations,' or because of the limitations of the auction institution's own qualifications and network, the auction items may not be ideal. Therefore, I basically only pay attention to the auctions organized by auction institutions with better qualifications and good reputations built over many years, while ignoring some suddenly emerging art auctions. Sometimes, I just flip through the catalogues, see nothing good, and don't go."

2. Who Truly Benefits from Art Auctions?

With so many frequent art auctions, who exactly benefits?

"The speculators have definitely made money, and if the auction company operates properly, they can also make money. Also, early entrants into the market, for instance, those who bought cheap items seven or eight years ago, have seen appreciation." Looking at so many Shoushan stone auctions this year, Zhou Ye revealed a phenomenon currently existing in the market: the works of a few Shoushan stone sculptors are being repeatedly hyped, usually every two to three months focusing on one sculptor.

Regarding the phenomenon of many auction institutions that previously did not engage in art auctions now rushing into the field, Yao Chunmao finds it quite normal, "it indicates profit. Moreover, whether domestically or internationally, the auction industry itself is uneven. Some auction institutions may consider short-term gains, aiming for profit, making hype inevitable."

In recent years, the phenomenon of hiring "plants" at art auctions has become rampant, a fact known to everyone in the circle. Some auction companies collude with certain artists or collectors, luring innocent collectors to blindly follow, falling into traps, spending money and emotions, only to buy back beautiful lies.

An unnamed insider told reporters that there are now many cultural dissemination companies in Fuzhou, actually operating the炒作of some artworks, "a well-known cultural dissemination company from Guangzhou has been helping auction institutions hype across Beijing, Shanghai, and other places. Some of our provincial collection institutions have also cooperated with the company before, but due to inability to accept their excessively vicious hype tactics, cooperation was eventually abandoned."

Zhou Ye frankly admitted that many so-called "famous artists' works may not possess such high value, but after being auctioned, they gain a transaction record. Speculators then put the other works of this "famous artist" on the market, selling them at lower prices to lure "newcomers", then withdraw, moving on to hype other "famous artists". Within less than half a year, the price of the previously hyped "famous artist's works quickly drops by a third. In fact, "newcomers" have exhausted the value appreciation space of these works for many years in the future.

Countermeasures:

The Industry Urgently Needs Regulation

It is understood that currently, the entire art auction industry lacks relatively sound laws and regulations. Insiders revealed that many auction institutions do not even possess auction qualifications, and sometimes the art auctions themselves may not have been registered. According to Lin Wenjie, president of the Fujian Auction Industry Association, there are currently 167 auction institutions in our province, but only 12 actually have the qualifications to auction cultural relics and artworks. "Art auctions have indeed increased significantly this year, and personally, I feel that Shoushan stone auctions have become overheated. However, compared to Beijing and Shanghai, the current art auction market in Fujian remains relatively lagging, still in its initial stages." Lin Wenjie frankly admitted that the association has also negotiated with the provincial Culture Department and the provincial Cultural Relics Bureau, currently allowing the market to "bloom freely," letting these auction institutions undergo natural selection under the market economy.

Lin Wenjie said that it is undeniable that some auction item prices indeed contain speculative elements, and they are also negotiating with cultural and cultural relics departments on how to further regulate the auction industry, particularly art auctions in the future.
