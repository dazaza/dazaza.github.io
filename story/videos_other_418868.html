"I am worried that one day he will drink too much and die," Xiaolong said that he wanted to quit the "cough syrup addiction" through Laomeng's help.

"One day later, my brother-in-law regained his normal energy and demanded to go home," Xiaoyu said that after her brother-in-law returned home, he started drinking again.

Xiaoyu said that for Laomeng, drug addiction can be cured, and alcohol dependence should also be curable, "I hope he can help my brother-in-law out of this predicament."

□ "The Anti-drug Knight has come to Quanzhou! Urgently seeking drug users" follow-up

Xiaolong is 23 years old. Six years ago, when he was still in middle school, he started drinking "cough syrup." At that time, he heard others say that drinking that stuff was "very stimulating," so he and his classmates went to the pharmacy to buy it and drank it every few days. After graduating from high school, he found himself addicted to it and had to drink every day without interruption, "If I didn't drink, I would feel weak all over and couldn't work." Therefore, he often lost his job and borrowed money from relatives and friends when he ran out of money.

Reported by N People's Daily reporter Peng Jianwen and Yang Jiong

Laomeng, 48, from Inner Mongolia, once committed crimes due to drug addiction, leading to the breakup of his family; after being released from prison, he embarked on a path of anti-drug publicity, cycling through 700 counties and cities in three years, successfully helping more than 30 people quit drugs. These days, Laomeng has arrived in Quanzhou, looking for those who need help to quit drugs.

Among yesterday's seekers, two cases were relatively unique: seeker Xiaolong (a pseudonym) was addicted to long-term drinking of "cough syrup"; another seeker Aming (a pseudonym) was obsessed with alcohol, and was detected with "moderate alcohol dependence" in the hospital. Last night, Xiaolong had briefly described his situation to Laomeng via text message. Laomeng said that these two seekers were addicted to "alternative drugs": one was drug dependence, and the other was alcohol dependence. On the road to摆脱 dependence, he was willing to provide them with assistance. However, based on his experience, compared to real drug addicts, it wouldn't be easy for the above two seekers to quit. They need to be convinced psychologically first, forced to quit at home for a period of time, and then undergo relevant treatment, which would be more effective.

"I just got married, and all the money at home is earned by my wife, I want to quit," Xiaolong said that now he spends 100 yuan on "medicine water" every day, making his family feel a heavy burden. Because of this, he also wanted to quit the "medicine water addiction", but each time he could only last half a day before his hands would start shaking.

According to Xiaoyu, her brother-in-law's business had always been smooth, and there was no emotional trauma, so it was unclear why he became addicted to alcohol. At first, her brother-in-law only drank a little "small wine", but later he developed a habit of drinking. He would gulp down a small bottle of 38-degree alcohol in one go, drinking eight or nine bottles every day.

"I hope more people using traditional drugs will actively contact me," Laomeng said that some drug users lose confidence in themselves and are also worried about exposing their personal secrets, so they are not very willing to contact him. Next, he wants to participate in Quanzhou's community-based detoxification work, hoping that more people will truly return to society under his help.
