The iPod family moves forward.

by jagger on 2007-09-08 11:19:55

It has to be said, this might be the most infuriating product launch - The beat goes on, everything is just like a dream, the impossible has become reality.

Jobs is truly deserving of the title "magician," perfect!

First, let's talk about the most disappointing one (inevitably having some flaws) — the chubby Nano.

Unfortunately, the iPod Nano failed in its weight loss attempt, "Fat" became a reality — Nano is no longer slim and sexy, but instead has transformed into a square shape.

Although Nano remains compact as usual, surprisingly, the iPod Nano really got bigger.

It includes a 2-inch 320x240 LCD with 2" QVGA resolution, almost comparable to the screen performance of the 5th Generation iPod, and it's also Apple's PMP with the highest pixel count currently. The user interface predictably added CoverFlow, still using the button dial to spin around, while simultaneously having three built-in games, which can of course also be purchased from the iTunes Store!

In terms of battery life, it lasts for 24 hours of music, 5 hours of video, capacities of 4GB priced at $149, 8GB priced at $199, one could say there isn't much difference compared to the current nano.

Apple restructured its product line, all four new models of iPods made their appearance.

iPod Classic

As the name suggests, taking out the current iPod and continuing to sell it, calling it Classic is justified — it is indeed quite classic (no creativity within no creativity), but they did add a metal casing version, similar to Nano2, though it seems to have gotten even slimmer.

The interface is the same as the iPod Nano, both have CoverFlow, the rest should be the same as the original (5G), 2.5-inch screen, QVGA resolution. Battery life is 30/40 hours of music, 6/7 hours of video, depending on capacity, yes, there are 80GB and 160GB versions, priced at $249 and $349 respectively. Shipping time is 3-4 weeks.

Apple restructured its product line, all four new models of iPods made their appearance.

iPod Shuffle

Shuffle doesn't have a display, so wanting innovation but not achieving much, changing colors is still fine, the only update for Shuffle is: orange and some other color disappeared, replaced by more appealing light violet and grass green. Price remains unchanged, still $79. Shipping time is within one day!

iPod Touch —— this is the most satisfying one!

In a sense, it is actually an iPhone without phone function and camera. In terms of hardware, it's basically the same as the iPhone, screen size, resolution, etc. (it's rumored that even the binary code is the same!), capacities come in 8GB and 16GB, and it's even slimmer! Only 8mm thick; operation-wise, multi-touch is a must, other features such as quick photo browsing, touch slider unlock are also present, then there's WiFi functionality (802.11b/g Wi-Fi), although the antenna looks rather unsightly, at least you can use it to surf the web later, whether through Safari or watching free videos on the same YouTube channel as others, unfortunately there's no built-in Google Maps, but it should still be accessible via Safari?

Being able to directly purchase and download music via WiFi is a big step forward! In terms of battery life, 5 hours of video, 22 hours of music, which is somewhat disappointing, network browsing needs further testing. Finally, the price, $299 for 8GB, $399 for 16GB! It's the same as the 8GB version of the iPhone, "This is technology. If they bought it on Wednesday morning for $599, then they can go back and get it. But if it was a month ago, then they'll just have to accept it. This is the inevitable development of technology." Jobs said. Those who bought earlier can only cry.

We hope the battery for Touch doesn't end up like the iPhone's, just a hope.
