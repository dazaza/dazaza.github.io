China will encounter the largest rain and snow weather range after autumn, affecting 30 provinces and cities

by kevine45yd on 2009-11-17 11:25:12

&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp； Beijing, November 9th - After causing a sharp drop in temperature in Northeast China and other areas, the cold air continues to move southward. In the next three to four days, China will experience the largest-scale autumn temperature drop and rainy snow weather since the beginning of the season. The scope of the temperature drop and snowy weather will affect thirty provinces and municipalities, excluding Tibet and Yunnan.

This cold front began affecting China from Xinjiang, Inner Mongolia, and Northeast China on the 7th. On the 8th, it already brought intense cooling weather to the northeastern part of Inner Mongolia and Northeast China. Within one night, some areas in Inner Mongolia, Heilongjiang, and Jilin experienced a temperature drop exceeding 16 degrees Celsius.

According to forecasts by the National Meteorological Center, under the joint influence of cold and warm air masses, China will experience a large-scale rainy and snowy weather process over the next three days. Xinjiang, central and eastern parts of Northwest China, central and western Inner Mongolia, North China, and other regions will have light to moderate rain or snow, or freezing rain. Among these, some areas in Inner Mongolia, Shaanxi, Shanxi, and Hebei may see heavy snow or blizzards. The Huai River region, Jianghuai area, Jianghan area, South China, most of southern China, as well as eastern parts of Southwest China will experience light to moderate rain or showers. Some areas in Shaanxi, Henan, Hubei, Jiangxi, Anhui, Jiangsu, Shanghai, Zhejiang, and Fujian may see heavy rain.

Expert Xiaoquan Li from the China Meteorological Administration stated that from the 9th to the 12th, China will witness the broadest autumn precipitation this year. The range of the temperature drop and snowy weather will cover most parts of China, excluding Tibet and Yunnan.

Chief Forecaster Lifu He from the National Meteorological Center indicated that the cold air currently invading China is moving relatively slowly, leading to prolonged rainfall and a significant cumulative temperature drop with a wide impact range. Currently, the strong warm and humid airflow affecting central and eastern China is not only due to the cold air but also due to the interaction between the two, which mainly brings about rainy and snowy weather.
