What should I do if I'm too busy with development work and trivial matters to mentor an intern?

by softdong5j2 on 2011-03-30 14:01:44

Two weeks ago, I was sent by the company to Beijing on a business trip. While working with a client, I noticed an intern working on a small software project. After observing him for a while, I felt that although this young man usually worked hard, his work lacked professionalism. So, I recommended he use my general permission management system component to maintain the backend data of this project.

For example, using the general permission management system component can help manage the backend users, user permissions, roles, role permissions, module menu configurations, single sign-on control for users, organizational structure, basic data management of some option types, serial number generation functions, setting user passwords, and many other common tasks without having to write programs for them. If counted in detail, these features would require over 100 configuration management pages and thousands of functional points. It is not easy to carefully complete all these tasks and ensure they are robust. Even if completed, they may not be versatile enough or withstand the changes in requirements during daily application usage. Moreover, spending a lot of time managing these generic foundational information isn't always worthwhile.

Also, newly developed software projects often have changing menus, internal roles, permissions, and functional requirements. Programs written by inexperienced developers tend to be fragile and cannot withstand the client's constant changes. After being subjected to such changes multiple times, the entire system might crash or become incapable of handling the client’s repeated demands, leaving the developers exhausted.

Meanwhile, the end-users of software projects often want to freely perform some configuration management. Software projects developed by beginners often lack strong configuration management functions. For instance, clients might want to adjust the order of the main menu, rename the menu items, modify account permissions, etc., in C#. These needs are often impossible for clients to list clearly before developing the software project. We cannot complain about clients always changing their minds; these are merely the basic functions of management software. The reason why they seem difficult is due to insufficient professionalism and experience, especially for newcomers who need to pay more attention.

For example, if we buy a car without reverse gear or brakes, and the dealership tells us, "Sorry, when you bought the car, you didn’t specify these features. The car only has forward gears and no brakes." We would feel very dissatisfied.

Clients who customize software development can also require developers to use a general permission management system component to develop information management systems. Companies can train new employees using the general permission management system component as a standard training tutorial. IT-related university students can also learn the source code and functionality of the general permission management system component, which will greatly help with future employment and practical information system management.

The software development intern at the client's organization seriously studied the functionality of the general permission management system component for more than two days, improving their overall understanding of small software projects significantly. Usually, everyone is busy, and no one has time to teach them essential foundational knowledge every day. Letting them self-study for more than two days saves their supervisor from repeatedly explaining simple matters in the future.

When the intern has time and interest, they can study how the database is designed, how detailed functions are implemented, what features are provided, and what factors the designer considered, gradually improving their skills. When they don’t have time or interest, learning just how to control and manage it is sufficient to meet the flexible change requirements of actual work needs for the client. There’s no need to know how to do everything yourself; knowing how to apply things flexibly is also fine. Our goal is achieved.

Some interns have a keen interest in development and naturally look at the accompanying database design, study the source code, architecture, and watch the related videos carefully. All they need is a brief explanation of where to look and where things are located, saving time and effort.

My active efforts were well rewarded. This relatively diligent and ambitious intern helped me find 9 bugs. I updated these bugs over the weekend while resting at home in Hangzhou. Once paid customers need updates, I provide them with a unified program update. This labor result can now be shared by more than a hundred people, eliminating the need for each person to go through the hassle individually. This is creating wealth for society, serving the people, and providing repeatable, robust tools necessary for information system construction.

I've done my best to perfect permission management and workflow management. A person can only excel at a few things.
