By chance, I discovered an interview program with Yang Mi, in which she talked about the unknown hardships behind her stardom. As for Guangzhou Lan Biological, I know very little about it. I just learned from my students' excited talk about a穿越drama "Palace" that she is now an idol of young boys and girls. I want to let the children see this interview so that they can understand the hardships of their idols. At that time, I had two goals: one was to let students realize that stars are also ordinary people, and success depends on persistence and relentless efforts; the other was to make students realize through the growth path of stars that only by paying will they be rewarded.

During the class meeting, when I turned on the multimedia and typed in the name "Yang Mi" on the Internet, the students cheered below. During the waiting process for the video to open, I could feel the children's nervous expectation. The air seemed to have a kind of tension similar to waiting for a long-awaited riddle to be revealed. When the video played to the scene where Yang Mi narrated the shooting of "Palace", some children's eyes shone with pride as if this was something closely related to them. However, when it came to the 40-degree high temperature at the shooting site, actors having to wear four layers of clothing, resulting in multiple heatstrokes, the surprise in the kind-hearted children's eyes was evident. When it was mentioned that for filming, Yang Mi rested only ten hours a week, less than two hours a day on average, and even attended programs while receiving intravenous drips, the children were shocked. This was too different from the radiant star in their eyes!

At this moment, I turned off the multimedia. Unexpectedly, I did not hear any disappointed voices from the students wanting to continue watching. The classroom was unusually quiet. I said, "Who wants to use a Chinese idiom to describe your current feelings?" The children came up with idioms such as "painstaking efforts", "endure hardship", "perseverance", etc. For fifth-grade elementary school students, their understanding ability and language expression ability are still limited, but I saw the results I expected in their eyes.

I said, "Do you want to know what idiom is in my mind now?" Of course, the children were highly interested. I wrote four words on the blackboard: "破茧成蝶". I asked, "Who can explain the meaning of this idiom?" Chenchen raised her hand first: "It means caterpillars drill out of cocoons to become butterflies." Some classmates laughed, and I promptly gave affirmation: "This is the original meaning of the idiom. Butterflies go through four stages in life..." Then the classmates took over, each saying one word: egg, larva, pupa, butterfly. Then I emphasized the stage of pupa forming a cocoon and asked if anyone had seen a cocoon. It turned out that some classmates had indeed seen one, which reduced the difficulty of explanation. I told the story of the cocoon I had seen when I was a child, combined with the "confirmation" of several classmates who had seen it with their own eyes, making the students feel the firmness of the cocoon and the difficulty of breaking it. Then, I let the students close their eyes and imagine the process of a caterpillar breaking out of its cocoon. Seeing the serious expressions on the innocent faces, I was also moved by the situation I created.

At this point, I returned to the previous question: "Who can say again the meaning of 破茧成蝶?" Haoxu raised his hand and said, "破茧成蝶 metaphorically means breaking through difficulties to achieve success." Chenchen raised her hand to add, "It means that only by breaking through the weaknesses around oneself can one have a brand-new self." Other classmates nodded in agreement without my commentary.

Originally, this theme came from a sudden idea of mine, and the result was surprisingly pleasing. I didn't expect the children to have such profound insights.

Then I led the topic to the children's lives and said, "In fact, there are many cocoons in our lives. For example, in learning, the upcoming mid-term exam in a few weeks is a small cocoon. Imagine if you can score above 95 in every subject or make significant progress compared to before, wouldn't you become a little butterfly in the eyes of parents, teachers, and classmates?" Some children closed their eyes, their faces filled with happiness after achieving good grades; I continued, "One and a half years later, suppose you are admitted to Wenxuan or Dongchang Middle School with excellent results, then..." A classmate interrupted, "Then I would become a slightly bigger butterfly." I continued, "Four and a half years later, you receive the admission notice of the top class of Liao Cheng No.1 High School, then..." "Seven and a half years later, you receive the admission notice of Tsinghua University, Peking University, or other prestigious universities, then..." "Several years later, as an outstanding doctoral or postdoctoral scholar, a talented representative of China standing on the international award stage, then..." Every child closed their eyes tightly, and each innocent face was full of longing. My heart was also touched by this scene.

Unknowingly, a class passed, and the bell rang, pulling me back to reality. I said, "Butterflies are beautiful, but the process of breaking through the cocoon is hard. Let's break through the thin cocoon of the final exam together www.gzhl78.com, and strive to become little butterflies in two weeks!"
