Plot Introduction of "Silver Mansion Golden Powder":

In the first silver house of Foshan, Guangdong - "Shang Silver House", it faces a business crisis. Master Shang, Shang Keng (Qin沛), in order to obtain a loan from the Shen family, forces Second Master Shang Yun (Wu Wei Guo) to marry Miss Shen Yongtong (Yang Wanyi) through scheming with his wife Sai Xiaodie (Xue Jia Yan). This causes Yun’s beloved Cheng Xiuqing (Wu Yongwei) to angrily marry into the Shang family as the third concubine. From then on, Yun and Keng become sworn enemies, and Yun travels overseas, severing all ties with his brother.

It isn’t until the day of the Old Master’s birthday that Yun returns home, only for their youngest son to be kidnapped. Keng refuses to admit to falsely accusing a shop assistant at the Shang Silver House, which ultimately leads to his death, causing another conflict between the brothers. Yun decides to split the family.

On the other hand, since Yun returned, Xiuqing has been neglected by Keng, causing her great pain. She is also falsely accused of causing the miscarriage of the fourth concubine Feifei (Hu Dingxin). Determined to seek revenge on the Shang family, she uses the lingering feelings between her and Yun to manipulate the entire Shang household. She further exposes the truth about the maid Qiujun (Yang Siqi), who turns out to be the illegitimate child of Keng and a married woman. These series of events bring disgrace upon the once prestigious family, leading to its ultimate downfall…

Episode-by-Episode Summary 1-20 Grand Finale:

Episode 1-5: The story begins with the Shang family facing financial difficulties. In order to secure a loan, Shang Keng devises a plan to force his second son Yun to marry Shen Yongtong against his will. This decision angers Yun’s lover, Xiuqing, who retaliates by becoming the third concubine in the Shang family. Tensions rise within the family as personal ambitions clash with familial duty.

Episode 6-10: Yun, disillusioned by his father’s actions, leaves for overseas, breaking off all relations with his family. Meanwhile, back in the Shang mansion, conflicts arise among the wives due to jealousy and power struggles. The third concubine Xiuqing feels increasingly isolated and mistreated by the rest of the family.

Episode 11-15: Upon returning for his grandfather’s birthday, Yun discovers the kidnapping of his youngest son. Accusations fly, and secrets begin to unravel within the family. Keng’s refusal to take responsibility for his mistakes pushes Yun over the edge, leading him to decide on splitting the family estate.

Episode 16-20: Xiuqing takes matters into her own hands and seeks revenge against the Shang family. Using her wit and charm, she manipulates the family dynamics, exposing long-buried secrets and scandals. Her revelations about Qiujun’s true parentage shock the family and tarnish their reputation. As the truth comes to light, the once-mighty Shang family crumbles under the weight of its own sins, leaving a legacy of bitterness and regret.

Grand Finale: The final episodes see the culmination of years of resentment and betrayal. The Shang family, once a symbol of wealth and prestige, finds itself torn apart by internal strife and public scandal. Each character faces the consequences of their actions, leading to a bittersweet conclusion where justice is served, but at a heavy cost.
