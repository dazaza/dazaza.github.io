The Battle for Fabregas Begins: Real Madrid Prepares a 50 Million Euro Check

It is no secret that Real Madrid covets Arsenal's midfield star Cesc Fabregas. The question is, when will they formally offer the Gunners an acquisition price? The latest news from Marca reveals that Florentino Perez has already prepared a check worth 50 million euros, launching the battle for Fabregas six months ahead of Barcelona.

After being tested in both La Liga and the Champions League, it has become evident that apart from defensive vulnerabilities, Real Madrid also needs improvement in midfield. Specifically, the combination of Kaka and Xabi Alonso has not yielded the desired results greater than the sum of its parts. To address this issue, Real Madrid's sports department spent several months searching for new reinforcements in midfield.

Liverpool midfielder Steven Gerrard briefly entered Real Madrid's radar, but at 29 years old, he is considered too old compared to players like Kaka and Cristiano Ronaldo, and this season he has been plagued by injuries, thus being excluded from the selection list. French midfielder Yoann Gourcuff was another option for Real Madrid, but his inconsistent performances at both club and national team levels made him less appealing. Moreover, after evaluating prospects from Germany, Brazil, and Argentina, Real Madrid concluded that aside from Fabregas, it would be difficult to find a more mature and comprehensive midfielder.

Based on these considerations, Real Madrid has resolved to acquire Fabregas next summer. Fabregas has decided to leave Arsenal on June 30th next year, with Arsene Wenger's agreement. Additionally, Arsenal has set an initial asking price of no less than 50 million euros for the Spanish player.

Marca suggests that under the competition between Real Madrid and Barcelona, 50 million euros may only be the starting price for Fabregas's transfer. However, if Real Madrid can reach an agreement with the player first, this might also be his final transfer fee.

Barcelona is currently playing the emotional card, trying to lure Fabregas back to his hometown club. While Barcelona has advantages, it also has disadvantages. Barcelona already boasts a strong midfield lineup including Busquets, Xavi, Iniesta, and Keita, meaning there wouldn't be an immediate position available for Fabregas if he were to join. Furthermore, former Barcelona vice-president Josep Maria Bartomeu is using Fabregas as a bargaining chip for his potential presidential campaign next year. These are two critical factors Fabregas must consider.

On the other hand, joining Real Madrid would present a different scenario. Currently, the only holding midfielders in Real Madrid's squad are Xabi Alonso and Lassana Diarra. If Fabregas were to join, Real Madrid could almost guarantee him a stable starting position. Although there might be positional conflicts with Kaka, these issues can be resolved through tactical adjustments and role changes.

In summary, Real Madrid has firmly resolved to acquire Fabregas. Now it all depends on the player's choice. Marca indicates that all signs point to Real Madrid officially contacting Arsenal in January next year to launch a full-scale offensive to acquire Fabregas, making a decisive move against Barcelona in this high-stakes battle.
