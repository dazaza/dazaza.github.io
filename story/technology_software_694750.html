Banana Diet Gains Popularity Among Celebrities

The banana is a very common tropical fruit. Because it's so common, some people may doubt: Can bananas really help with weight loss? Is it a scam? But don't be skeptical—banana dieting has now become one of the popular weight loss methods among many celebrities.

For instance, Big S, a beauty who is almost neurotic about her appearance as revealed in her book "Beauty Queen," once survived on just one banana per day for three weeks and lost 3 kilograms. Additionally, Sumi Kyoko, an overweight woman who tried the banana diet, lost 7 kilograms in a month and a half, causing the banana diet to immediately gain attention. This also led to a shortage of bananas in Japan.

The Secret of Banana Weight Loss

The reason lies in the banana itself. It is highly effective for weight loss because it contains very low calories and is rich in dietary fiber. Many people believe that bananas are very sweet and high in calories, but this isn't true. A single banana (weighing around 100 grams) only contains 87 calories, which is much less than a typical meal of rice (150 grams containing 220 calories). In comparison, a banana has about half or even less of the caloric content.

Besides the low calorie count and rich fiber content mentioned earlier, bananas also contain large amounts of sugars and various nutrients needed by the body. When necessary, they can provide sustenance while replenishing nutrition and energy. Therefore, eating bananas during weight loss can effectively reduce fatty foods while still providing energy and nutrition.

Precautions for Banana Dieting

Since bananas are a cold and sweet food, their cooling properties can clear intestinal heat. However, for those with cold intestines or damp-heat constitutions, overconsumption should be avoided to prevent diarrhea. Furthermore, individuals with excessive stomach acid, stomach pain, poor digestion, or diarrhea should consume them sparingly.

How to Follow the Banana Diet

For breakfast, eat only bananas and chew them slowly. You can eat as many bananas as you want, but only bananas. Avoid frozen bananas, as they can overly chill the stomach and intestines, affecting gastrointestinal function.

When following the banana diet, it's important to drink plenty of water. When consuming bananas in the morning, drink room temperature water. Throughout the day, make sure to stay hydrated. There's no specific requirement for how much water to drink daily; just drink frequently when you're thirsty. However, avoid forcing yourself to drink water when you're not thirsty, as drinking too much water can lead to swelling and worsening health conditions.

Lunch and dinner can be eaten as usual without controlling lunch portions. You can increase the proportion of rice at lunch and reduce the proportion of vegetables.

For afternoon tea, you can have a small snack, but limit it to one type of food. It's best to choose seasonal fruits or dried fruits. Avoid meat and high-calorie foods.

Dinner should be eaten early. Most weight gain issues stem from improper dinner timing. If dinner is eaten late, your digestive system will still be active while you sleep, preventing good rest. Poor sleep leads to fatigue, which can worsen your health condition, resulting in swelling and an increased tendency to gain weight. It's recommended to eat dinner before 8 PM.

Although the banana diet focuses on eating bananas for breakfast, it's essential to adjust the amount based on your individual constitution to avoid counterproductive results. Maintaining a good sleep schedule is crucial to ensure your body enters a healthy metabolic cycle.
