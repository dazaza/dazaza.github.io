15-Year-Old Girl to Sail Around the World Alone, Will Do Homework on Boat

by yiboyou40 on 2011-07-14 16:49:20

A 15-year-old Australian teenage girl Jessica Watson has recently made a bold decision: in November this year, she will set off alone from Brisbane Port. She will drive a yacht and take sufficient food and navigation equipment, then continuously circumnavigate the earth without interruption. It is expected that the entire journey will last for seven months and cover a distance of 2200 nautical miles (about 4074 kilometers).

Jessica's round-the-world voyage is scheduled to begin on November 7th. She will sail past the southern end of New Zealand and Cape Horn at the southern tip of South America, along the equator of the Atlantic Ocean, then through the Cape of Good Hope in South Africa and the Indian Ocean, finally returning to Brisbane Port.

She will do her homework on the boat.

For her daughter's stubbornness, Julie Watson, Jessica's mother, feels "both nervous and confident." She said: "Many people have said to me, 'How funny, she is only 15 years old.' But what I want to say is, if they had a daughter like mine, they would definitely let her go. Because she always brings you confidence." Jessica's grandfather Gordon Kithsom was equally confident, saying: "Even if things don't start well, she has enough wisdom to get out of trouble. This is very important."

In preparation for this astonishing feat, Jessica made meticulous preparations. As part of her warm-up training, she recently departed from Bluff Port in New Zealand aboard the "Evohe" sailboat, heading to the Auckland Islands in Australia, personally experiencing the harsh weather of the Southern Ocean (also known as the Antarctic Ocean, the ocean surrounding Antarctica, also the southern parts of the Pacific, Atlantic, and Indian Oceans) with a group of sailors.

If everything goes smoothly, Jessica will break the Guinness World Record for "the youngest solo circumnavigator" by one year. The current world record holder is Australian David Dicks, who achieved it at the age of 18 in 1996.

Mother is "both nervous and confident"

Regarding Jessica's almost fanciful idea, her father was initially very concerned, while her mother worried about bad weather. However, what Jessica herself fears most is that the navigation equipment might malfunction halfway and she wouldn't know how to repair it. It is understood that during Jessica's solo circumnavigation of the earth, she will drive a motor yacht approximately 10 meters long. Most of her time will be spent maintaining equipment, doing homework, watching DVDs, and reading thrillers. Among the many supplies on the boat, there will naturally be her favorite chocolate.

At the age of 8, Jessica began practicing driving a small sampan, dreaming of one day circumnavigating the earth. While most girls focus on their appearance when they reach adolescence, Jessica is uniquely passionate about conquering the sea. Under the guidance of the famous local one-armed sailor Bruce Armstrong, young Jessica learned basic sailing skills such as climbing masts, steering, and hull maintenance.

According to reports, Jessica comes from Moroolaba, Queensland, Australia, growing up in a family with a free-spirited adventurous nature. From an early age, she heard stories about American Joshua Slocum completing the first solo circumnavigation of the world more than 100 years ago.
