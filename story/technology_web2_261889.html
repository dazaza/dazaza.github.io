So what exactly is acrylic?

by bjpmma3 on 2009-11-19 23:27:17

1. The term "acrylic" may sound unfamiliar because it is a relatively new word that has only appeared on the mainland in the past two years. It was not until 2002 that it began to be gradually understood by a few people in the advertising, furniture, and crafts industries. "Acrylic" is a transliteration of the English word ACRYLIC, which refers to a chemical material. Its chemical name is "PMMA," belonging to the propylene alcohol class, commonly known as "specially treated organic glass." In the application industry, its raw materials generally appear in the form of granules, sheets, or tubes.

2. Acrylic Sheets and Organic Glass: "Organic glass" originates from the English term Organic Glass. In recent years, in some areas, all transparent plastic-made sheets have been collectively referred to as organic glass, which is incorrect. Acrylic specifically refers to pure polymethyl methacrylate (PMMA) material, and PMMA sheets are called acrylic sheets.

3. Characteristics and Advantages of Acrylic Sheets: Acrylic has high transparency with a light transmittance of 92%, earning it the reputation of "plastic crystal." It also has excellent weather resistance, especially when used outdoors, surpassing other plastics. Additionally, it possesses good surface hardness and luster. Acrylic can be easily processed into various required shapes and products. Another feature is the wide variety of sheet types and rich colors (including semi-transparent color sheets). Moreover, even thick sheets can maintain high transparency.

4. History of Acrylic: Acrylic (ACRYLIC), commonly known as specially treated organic glass, has been researched for over a hundred years. In 1872, the polymerization of acrylic acid was discovered; in 1880, the polymerization of methyl methacrylate became known; in 1901, research on the synthesis method of acrylic polypropionic ester was completed; in 1927, attempts were made to industrialize using the aforementioned synthesis method; in 1937, the industrial production of methyl methacrylate esters succeeded, thereby entering large-scale manufacturing. During World War II, due to acrylic's excellent toughness and transparency, it was first applied to aircraft windshields and tank drivers' vision mirrors. In 1948, the world's first acrylic bathtub was born, marking a new milestone in acrylic applications.

5. Uses of Acrylic: PMMA has the advantages of being lightweight and inexpensive, making it easy to mold. Its molding methods include casting, injection molding, mechanical processing, and thermal forming. Especially injection molding allows for mass production, simple processes, and low costs. Therefore, its applications are increasingly widespread. Currently, it is widely used in instrument parts, automobile headlights, optical lenses, transparent pipes, etc.

Introduction to Acrylic: Acrylic comes from the English word Acrylics, a general term for acrylic and methacrylic chemicals. It includes monomers, sheets, pellets, resins, and composite materials. Acrylic sheets are made from the polymerization of methyl methacrylate monomer (MMA), namely polymethyl methacrylate (PMMA) sheets, also known as organic glass. "Organic glass" originates from the trade name "Oroglas" (a type of PMMA sheet), derived from "Organic Glass." However, in recent years, all transparent plastics like PS, PC, etc., have been collectively referred to as organic glass.

Acrylic Production Technology: Acrylic sheets can be divided into cast and extruded types according to their production processes. Cast sheets perform better than extruded ones but are more expensive. Cast sheets are mainly used for carving, decoration, and craft production, while extruded ones are typically used for making advertisements signs and light boxes.

Characteristics of Acrylic:

1. Excellent Transparency: Colorless transparent organic glass sheets with a light transmittance of over 92%.

2. Good Weather Resistance: Strong adaptability to natural environments, maintaining performance even after prolonged exposure to sunlight, wind, and rain, with good anti-aging properties, suitable for outdoor use.

3. Good Processability: Suitable for both mechanical and thermal forming. Acrylic sheets can be dyed, painted, silk-screened, or vacuum-coated.

4. Outstanding Comprehensive Properties: Available in many varieties and colors, offering designers diverse choices. Acrylic sheets can be dyed, painted, silk-screened, or vacuum-coated.

5. Non-toxic: Safe for long-term human contact, and does not produce toxic gases when burned.

Specifications and Thickness: Specifications: 1.22*2.44m, 1.22*1.83m, 1.25*2.5m, 2*3m, 2.05*3.05m. Thickness: 2mm-50mm.

Product Advantages:

1. Hardness: Hardness is one of the parameters that best reflects the production technology and quality of cast acrylic sheets, an important part of quality control. Hardness reflects the purity of MMA, the weather resistance of the sheets, and high-temperature performance. Hardness directly affects whether the sheets will shrink, bend, deform, or if the surface will crack during processing. Hardness is one of the key indicators for evaluating the quality of acrylic sheets. Our company's imported material cast acrylic sheets have the highest hardness index among domestic counterparts, averaging around Rockwell hardness value 89.

2. Thickness Tolerance: Control of sheet thickness tolerance is an important reflection of quality management and production technology. The thickness tolerance of our imported material cast acrylic sheets is controlled within +0.2mm.

3. Transparency/Whiteness: Strict raw material selection, advanced formula updates, and modern production techniques ensure excellent transparency and pure whiteness of the sheets. They become crystal clear after flame polishing.

Precautions:

1. Acrylic sheets should not be stored with other organic solvents or come into contact with organic solvents.

2. During transportation, the surface protective film or paper should not be scratched.

3. Should not be used in environments exceeding 85°C.

4. When cleaning acrylic sheets, only use 1% soapy water. Use a soft cotton cloth dipped in soapy water, avoid hard objects or dry wiping, otherwise, the surface can easily be scratched.

5. Acrylic sheets have a large thermal expansion coefficient, so temperature changes should consider leaving a stretch gap.
