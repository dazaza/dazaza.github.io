"Duoshan" is more of a satirical novel like "Fortress Besieged" than "Triple Door".

by jqrxass on 2009-09-06 12:39:37

In the late 1930s, as the Japanese army launched a full-scale invasion of China, the city of Beiping was in turmoil. Universities such as Jinghua University were preparing to relocate inland to continue their operations. The protagonist, Lin Han Yun, a female lecturer at Jing University, was known for her intelligence and extraordinary talent. At this critical time for the nation, Jia Hongyi, the vice president of Jing University who was obsessed with power, hoped to be promoted to president during the relocation. He made various efforts, learning that the nephew of the head of the Beiping Chamber of Commerce was actually the deputy minister of education in Nanjing. One of Jia's subordinates, Zeng Wuliao, tried hard to ingratiate himself with the chamber head. Coincidentally, the head's son Ma Jun had just returned from Stanford University and was looking for a girlfriend. Unknowingly, the beautiful lecturer Lin Han Yun was tricked by Zeng Wuliao into attending a banquet, unknowingly falling into a matrimonial trap. The charming Ma Jun fell in love with Lin at first sight, determined to marry her, and began an aggressive pursuit...

Shuangshan Related Part

When I met Song Haohao, he was still studying Chinese literature at Nanjing University, writing the precursor to this novel, "Ten Years." My impression was that he only had interest in reading and writing, not paying much attention to the various literary scene trends ignited by the "post-80s" generation; in his reading and writing, he showed more interest in classic writers and works of traditional literature, especially having great admiration for Qian Zhongshu, whose scholarship spanned both East and West. These interests and hobbies allowed him to focus on writing what he wanted without caring whether it would become popular or trendy.

Shuangshan Chapter One

Lin Han Yun was born on this island, seemingly imbued with the spiritual essence of the Jiangnan water towns. In this dull and monotonous school, she was like a lotus flower transplanted from the south, so graceful and intelligent that she aroused jealousy. Suzhou, with its outstanding people and geographical advantages, has historically produced many talented individuals. Talented men are often born from accomplished women, making it hard to imagine the mother of a talented man being uglier than Dongshi. Therefore, there are naturally no shortage of accomplished women in Suzhou. Lin Han Yun could not exactly be said to come from a prestigious family, but her family was considered scholarly in their hometown.

Shuangshan Chapter Two

The Chinese have a great fondness for circles. Therefore, ancient Chinese believed the sky was round and the earth square, the Taiji Bagua is also circular, and the philosophy of moderation encourages flexibility and compromise. Thus, meals are served at round tables, probably because a circle provides the largest area to place dishes, allowing for lavish feasting; simple cocktail parties are products of Western civilization. However, enjoyment seems to be a universal human trait, regardless of East or West. Even Plato listed four cardinal virtues in "The Republic," and later the Roman Catholic Church added three more, none of which included frugality. Therefore, the invention of cocktail parties can be considered an unexpected development.

Shuangshan Chapter Three

It is often said that if you want to be restless for a day, invite someone for dinner; if you want to be restless for a year, build a house; if you want to be restless for a lifetime, take a concubine. Zeng Wuliao, hiding his affair from his wife, seemed ready to engage in lifelong unrest. His lover was willing to become the concubine of the Zeng family, and Zeng Wuliao also thought it would be best to divorce his current wife. Despite knowing his position as the dean of the university, he valued appearances, though he was already tired of his wife, he lacked the courage to publicly divorce her for another woman, akin to a bracelet too small to easily remove once placed on the wrist.

Shuangshan Chapter Four

After sending Lin Han Yun home, Ma Jun thought of a plan on the way: tomorrow, he would visit his uncle Yang Shukai, the secretary-general of the Beiping Municipal Government. Ideally, he could arrange a job for Lin Han Yun in the Beiping Municipal Government, away from Jing University, preventing her from relocating south. Ma Jun knew that Lin Han Yun was an ambitious woman with pursuits, and if he kept her at home as a young lady enjoying life, she would never agree. After returning home, he discussed with his father, who said Uncle Yang would certainly help. The next day, Ma Jun was sent to speak first, and if difficulties arose, his father would step in. This was also Ma Dongxian testing his son’s ability to handle matters upon his return to China.
