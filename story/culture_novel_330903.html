How Villains are Made

by ccg811 on 2010-03-01 23:04:58

The first part of "How the Villain is Made" has come to an end, and it was reported that the second part would be recorded after October. In the 1990s, in the corridor on the first floor of the teaching building of the No. 2 Middle School in J City.

"Hey, kid, give me all your money!" Two teenagers with colorful dyed hair forced a thin student into the corner.

The student lowered his head and said in a low voice: "I don't have any money."

"Bang!" One of the taller teenagers slapped the student in the face. "Shit, stop wasting my time, hurry up!"

With his lips red from being hit, tears uncontrollably fell down the student's cheeks. At this moment, the short and fat teenager beside the tall one said: "Forget it, don't beat him too much. This guy is the top student in my class, hahaha!"

The tall one looked at the student: "Shit, look at you like a coward. What's the use of being smart?" He then turned to the fat one and said: "Old Fatty, check his pockets, how could I believe he doesn't have any money?"

The fat one grunted and came in front of the student saying: "Xie Wenhao, let go of your hands." It turns out that upon hearing the words of the tall teenager, the student tightly held onto his trouser pocket.

Seeing Xie Wenhao acting as if he didn't hear, the fat one kicked him in the abdomen. Xie Wenhao's body collided heavily with the wall. The fat one pulled his hand away and reached into his trouser pocket with another hand. He took out a crumpled five-yuan note.

The fat one handed the money to the tall teenager and spat on the ground: "Shit, you don't know how to respect others." After saying that, the fat one left happily with the tall teenager, leaving behind Xie Wenhao full of pain.

Xie Wenhao is a third-year student at No. 2 Middle School in J City. He studies hard, is intelligent, and his academic performance is excellent, ranking first in the entire school. However, he is somewhat introverted, has no friends, and due to his small stature, he is often bullied by others. No. 2 Middle School in J City is not a key middle school, and the school management is very lax. There are often young delinquents entering and leaving the campus. These people are all young, and for various reasons, they no longer attend school and instead loiter around in society. Upon seeing weak and easy-to-bully students, they either pick fights or demand money, perhaps this way they can feel a sense of accomplishment.
