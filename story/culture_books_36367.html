The murder took place at 9 o'clock in the morning.

According to those who were present, after Yuan Yiping was stabbed, he stood upright for a while as if waiting for something. At this time, the clock tower of the building suddenly struck the hour, and only then did he unwillingly fall to the ground.

The bell sounded deep and lingering, mournful yet profound like a funeral toll.

More than twenty years later, some people said that this chime marked the beginning of a new round of struggles over fate.

That day was December 28, 1968. After several heavy snowfalls in the Beijing area, the sky suddenly cleared up, leaving the air crisp and clear. The blinding sunlight slanted down from the open sky without any obstruction, reflecting off the white snow and splashing countless bright spots into the air, shimmering with golden brilliance and dazzling splendor.

Wang Xingmin said that everything about that day seemed too direct, too bright. All pretenses were stripped away, revealing an ominous clarity.

The location of the murder was the square in front of Beijing Railway Station, which was originally a bustling place with constant traffic and crowds. Recently, due to the daily departure of several groups of educated youth on special trains to rural areas of Shanxi Province for resettlement, the square had become an ocean of red flags and heads, filled with drum beats and clamorous voices, reaching a peak of noise.

However, at this bright and splendid moment, in this place crowded with dignitaries and spectators, under the broad daylight and in full view of everyone, one young man killed another with a real weapon—a triangular scraper knife.

Based on all indications, this was a sudden case of fatal assault.

Two groups of youths quarreled over a trivial matter, neither side yielding, leading to a fight, then knives were drawn, resulting in one person's death.

An analysis of the testimonies collected from the scene showed that the background of this case was simple, containing no political or class elements; the process was straightforward, almost devoid of any plot. From its sudden onset to its end, it lasted merely half a minute. As for the motive for murder, people did not bother to study it further because, like many other injury cases that occurred during that era, the motive for this case was both absurd and simple.
