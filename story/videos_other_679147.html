Obstacle Phenomenon:

When the clutch experiences poor disengagement, it causes difficulty in gear shifting; severely, it will only allow gear shifting when the engine is off.

Inspection Method:

When the clutch shows signs of poor disengagement, you can confirm its disengagement point and engagement point through the following methods:

1) Set the engine to idle (transmission in neutral).

2) For safety, pull up the handbrake and press the foot brake.

3) Without pressing the clutch pedal (clutch engaged state), slowly move the gearshift towards reverse until you hear the gear whining noise, then stop operating the gearshift while maintaining the gear whining sound.

4) While keeping the gear whining sound, gradually press down on the clutch pedal to find the position where the gear whining sound disappears. This position is called the "disengagement point."

5) Under this condition, fully depress the clutch pedal to completely disengage the clutch, keep the gearshift stationary, and slowly release the clutch pedal. You will hear the gear whining sound again, and this position is called the "engagement point." If the disengagement point and engagement point cannot be found even after eliminating abnormalities in the disengagement mechanism, it can be determined as poor clutch disengagement.
