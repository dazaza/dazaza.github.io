Gui Zhen Tang denies coercion, makes a statement to vie for public opinion

by king2z9o on 2012-02-24 11:40:26

On the 22nd, Gui Zhen Tang initially opened its bear farming base to outsiders. However, the two-hour viewing was not enough to completely dispel external doubts. The media, which came with questions, left with new doubts at the end of the visit. In response to these doubts and online criticism, Gui Zhen Tang replied in a letter sent yesterday.

1 Is the five-minute bile drainage process too short?

Criticism: The entire five-minute bile drainage process is too short, the lighting is dim, and there is not enough time for observation.

Response: The timing for bile drainage from the farmed bears is absolutely fixed, with a set time period. This is the first large-scale opening of a bear farm in 20 years. Due to the large number of participating media, adjustments were made to ensure that everyone could observe the bile extraction process up close without congestion.

2 Was the Asian Animal Fund's request to visit denied?

Criticism: The Director of External Affairs of the Asia Animal Fund (AAF), Zhang Xiaohai, founder Jill Robinson, veterinarians, pathologists, and others arrived in Hui'an, Fujian province, ahead of schedule but were told they could not enter the bear farm.

Response: Mr. Zhang Xiaohai of AAF visited suddenly with several foreigners and did not register according to the event rules. After urgent consultation and considering the special nature of AAF, it was decided with the approval of relevant departments to specially open the bear farm to AAF on the afternoon of the 22nd, and an invitation was extended via telephone. However, Mr. Zhang Xiaohai did not accept this proposal.

3 Were visitors forced to comment on "live bear bile extraction"?

Criticism: After the visit, Gui Zhen Tang requested visitors to fill out a questionnaire indicating their stance on "live bear bile extraction." Was this a public relations tactic?

Response: For Gui Zhen Tang, this was the first large-scale opening since its establishment. To better gather experience for future tasks, we designed this questionnaire with no intention of swaying public opinion.

4 Why were visits restricted to designated bear pens?

Criticism: Except for the designated bear pens, other areas were locked and not allowed for viewing. Is there something being hidden?

Response: To ensure quarantine requirements, the designated bear pens must undergo disinfection for more than three days. Initially, the event plan was for 100 people, but the conditions for registration were relaxed the day before the opening, resulting in over 200 participants on the 22nd. Due to time constraints, other bear pens could not meet the required quarantine standards for opening. Therefore, visitors were not allowed to enter freely.

5 Why was there no guide at the opening site?

Criticism: Since the visitors are not professionals, there should have been someone on-site to explain, but instead, visitors were left confused when encountering issues.

Response: Prior to the event, rumors such as "anesthetized bears," "model bears," and "model rooms" were spread. This opening aimed to allow everyone to freely tour and independently inspect the entire breeding base, demonstrating our determination not to deliberately arrange anything.

6 Why did some bears roar during the visit?

Criticism: During the visit, black bears indeed emitted roars, sounding as if they were in pain.

Response: Wild black bears have not deteriorated to the point where they cannot vocalize. Roaring is a form of communication and emotional release. Assuming that the roaring is due to pain during bile drainage shows a lack of understanding of animal habits.

7 Did all participants receive transportation fees?

Criticism: Inviting individual media members seemed like a publicity stunt, as they were all given transportation fees.

Response: All participating media voluntarily registered and covered their own travel expenses. We did not provide transportation fees to any media outlet.

8 Are bears injected with anesthesia before bile extraction?

Criticism: The bears seem calm during bile extraction, constantly looking up and eating, possibly because they were injected with anesthesia.

Response: This is a completely fabricated rumor.

9 Did the opening only showcase active bears?

Criticism: Did Gui Zhen Tang, as stated in the "Invitation Notice," only showcase active bears?

Response: Gui Zhen Tang has never internally communicated this so-called "Invitation Notice." This is a blatant fabrication by those with ulterior motives to defame the openness of the event. It aims to discredit the credibility of this large-scale opening.

Statistics:

Gui Zhen Tang starts bile extraction from bears aged three, twice daily, except during pregnancy.

Bile secretion per day is approximately 2000 milliliters, with 140 milliliters extracted.

Dialogue:

[Bear farms may open two to three times a week in the future]

Gui Zhen Tang states it may allow visitors to extract bile themselves; black bears begin bile extraction twice daily starting at age three.

[Dialogue Participants]

Cai Zitu: Vice Chairman of Gui Zhen Tang, entered the company in 2007, one of the main shareholders.

Chen Zhihong: General Manager of Gui Zhen Tang.

After the opening, negative news still predominates.

Q: Why did Gui Zhen Tang consider opening the bear farm? Did the opening day achieve the expected goal?

Gui Zhen Tang: Originally, national regulations did not permit openings, as bringing viruses or bacteria into farms in the animal husbandry industry could be catastrophic. However, due to public pressure, relevant departments considered allowing us to open.

By letting people see the truth on-site, some individuals can no longer speak untruthfully or slander us. The goal of letting the media see firsthand has been achieved. But currently, the majority of feedback is still negative. We have never asked the media to definitely say good things about us; impartiality would suffice.

Q: What is the next step after the opening?

Gui Zhen Tang: We want to continue opening specifically for organizations like AAF, not just for two days. In situations that do not affect normal production, we aim to open to the public. Although the quarantine pressure is heavier, shareholders are discussing the possibility of having two to three public openings per week in the future, and even allowing visitors to extract bile themselves.

Black bears have 140 milliliters of bile extracted daily.

Q: Where do Gui Zhen Tang's bile-extracting bears come from?

Gui Zhen Tang: The bears were originally introduced from Yunnan, coming from some animal shelters. These black bears were captured from the wild, where poachers killed mother bears and brought back the cubs.

Initially, we purchased over 100 bears, then bred over 200 more. To improve the population, we also introduced over 100 breeding bears from other bear farms. In total, self-bred bears now outnumber purchased ones.

Q: Does Gui Zhen Tang extract bile from every black bear daily?

Gui Zhen Tang: Bile extraction begins when the bears are three years old, twice daily, except during pregnancy. Bile is secreted by the liver, with approximately 2000 milliliters produced daily, of which we extract 140 milliliters. Initially, continuous 24-hour drainage was used, but that was from the 1980s and cannot be compared to 2012.

Gui Zhen Tang's annual turnover is between 100 million to 200 million yuan.

Q: How is the company's profitability? Is it easy to make money in this industry?

Gui Zhen Tang: Currently, we are in a quiet period, so profit details cannot be disclosed too much. We can only say that the turnover is between 100 million to 200 million yuan.

1 gram of bear bile powder requires 7-9 milliliters of bear bile, meaning 1000 milliliters of bile can produce about 125 grams of bear bile powder. Bear bile powder is divided into several grades, with the highest grade, golden bile, priced at 118 yuan per gram.

We spend at least fifty to sixty yuan daily on feeding each bear, excluding labor costs, venue costs, etc.

Q: How large is China's bear farming industry currently?

Gui Zhen Tang: There are over 20,000 farmed bears, with 40%-50% undergoing bile extraction. This depends on the physical condition of the bears.

Consider industry integration after going public.

Q: What is Gui Zhen Tang's ultimate goal?

Gui Zhen Tang: Our predecessor was Qianshan Special Animal Breeding Base, which originally bred bears. Later, following national requirements, it became a pharmaceutical factory capable of producing bear bile.

Q: How significant is the IPO step for Gui Zhen Tang?

Gui Zhen Tang: The purpose of going public is to raise funds. Modernizing medicine must be combined with the capital market. Gui Zhen Tang was originally a family business, but now it has institutional investors and natural persons, making it more standardized. Even if we don't go public in the future, we will continue to move forward.

Among traditional Chinese medicines, tiger bones, musk, bear bile, and rhinoceros horns are the four major animal-based medicines, with the bear bile industry being the most standardized. After going public, the bear farming industry will become more transparent under public scrutiny, accepting more public supervision, clarifying procedures, and representing social progress. Currently, bear farms are weak, small, scattered, and of poor quality, making the situation very difficult. After going public, we will also consider industry consolidation.

Q: If "live bear bile extraction" is abolished, what will Gui Zhen Tang do?

Gui Zhen Tang: Abolishing it would be relatively difficult. We can only increase animal welfare. Deoxycholic acid and artificial bear bile synthesis may occupy part of the market share, but we are also researching deep processing products. As long as the state does not order us to stop, we will continue operations.
