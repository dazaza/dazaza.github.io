Hey, did you buy counterfeit high-end brand alcohol?

by chjdcj on 2012-02-24 09:42:43

The year-end has just passed, and I imagine everyone had a great time drinking during the Spring Festival. What did you drink? Maotai? Wuliangye? Yanghe? Tianzhi Lan? Erguotou?

No matter what kind of alcohol it is, whether for personal consumption or gifting, the biggest fear is buying counterfeit liquor.

Why? Drinking it harms your health, and giving it as a gift embarrasses you. If this gift happens to be for your company leader or father-in-law, that would be quite awkward. For hotels and tobacco and alcohol merchants who buy in bulk, it's not only a significant loss in costs but also the risk of losing customers and being reported.

So, buying alcohol really is an art. With so many different sales locations, how do you identify the good ones?

Today, I'll give you all a tip.

What we call fake alcohol is essentially inferior alcohol poured into counterfeit high-end bottles. It truly harms both others and oneself.

Therefore, when choosing where to buy alcohol, one must be extra cautious. Whether you're buying as an individual or a company needing large quantities, this applies.

Firstly, for individuals, if you're just buying a few bottles for personal enjoyment or gifting, here are two tips:

1. Find a trustworthy direct distributor. Remember, a direct distributor. That means directly authorized by the distillery with no middlemen, avoiding situations where the alcohol source is unclear. As a customer, you can request to see their authorization letter.

2. Look at the service. Generally, regional alcohol management is strict, like Yanghe. They won't let direct distributors ship to other regions. Therefore, if the seller promises to ship from the local area to another region, there’s likely an issue. Deliveries within the city are generally possible, but shipping fees are calculated separately.

(Examples) Everything must be complete.

Next, for bulk purchases, besides the general guidelines mentioned above, one should pay more attention to the scale and credibility of the direct distributor. You can visit them directly to observe their scale. Additionally, when buying in cases, there are barcodes that can be checked to verify authenticity. Buying alcohol is like buying home appliances; you need to recognize the channels and brands. Why do people naturally go to Suning or Wuxing when buying electronics? It's about recognizing the brand. Good service always attracts customers. Some lesser-known brands might offer slightly cheaper prices, but quality assurance is difficult. So, you must choose those with verifiable evidence.

Additionally, some large direct distributors have a points system for bulk purchases, with rebates available. Why not take advantage of that?
