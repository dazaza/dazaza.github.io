Magnetoencephalography - the cutting-edge device for locating epileptic foci

by king0r8k on 2010-07-03 08:48:57

<div>The term &ldquo;Magnetoencephalography (MEG)&rdquo; is still very unfamiliar to the majority of domestic patients. It refers to a brand-new diagnostic device capable of precisely locating foci of refractory epilepsy, significantly increasing the cure rate of epilepsy. The Beijing Jingke Epilepsy Specialty Hospital has uniquely developed the &ldquo;Neurotransmitter Balance Seizure Therapy,&rdquo; which utilizes MEG. By using analytical software to integrate the data from MEG with cranial MRI, this method accurately identifies the epileptic lesion, providing precise guidance for subsequent treatment.</div>

<div></div>

<div>Regarding the question of &ldquo;What is the diagnostic value of MEG in epilepsy?&rdquo;, after making a telephone appointment at 010&mdash;59796169, I visited Professor Ding Ziwei at the Beijing Jingke Epilepsy Specialty Hospital <a href="http://www.dx110.com">www.dx110.com</a>. Professor Ding pointed out that epilepsy is a chronic brain disease caused by abnormal discharges of neurons in the brain, leading to transient dysfunction of the central nervous system. Brain activity is essentially electrical activity, and electrical activity generates magnetic fields. The magnetic field around the human brain is known as the brain magnetic field. However, this magnetic field is extremely weak and requires special equipment to measure and record it. A strictly shielded electromagnetic field room needs to be established. In this shielded room, through specialized instruments, the extremely weak brain magnetic waves can be measured, recorded, and presented graphically. This graphic is called a magnetoencephalogram. It reflects changes in the brain's magnetic field, differing from the electroencephalogram which reflects changes in the brain's electric field. Magnetoencephalography provides more accurate localization diagnosis of brain injuries compared to electroencephalography, with clear and easily distinguishable images. Additionally, MEG can fuse its positioning data with cranial MRI through analytical software. Therefore, undergoing an MEG examination essentially means also undergoing a cranial MRI examination. In fact, MEG is the perfect combination of human brain bio-magnetic fields and morphological examinations, thus having more accurate diagnostic value, especially for epilepsy.</div>

<div></div>

<div>When asked about the advantages of MEG in diagnosing epilepsy, Professor Ding emphasized that first, it can accurately locate the epileptogenic focus, and this ability surpasses any other examination such as electroencephalography, PET, SPECT, etc. Therefore, it is very helpful in diagnosing and differentiating the causes of epilepsy and determining the type of epilepsy. Secondly, MEG can very accurately locate the functional areas of the brain. One could say that the advent of MEG marks a new era in the study of epilepsy and other neurological diseases. MEG can be integrated with imaging examinations like CT and MRI, which provide anatomical imaging of the brain, opening up another high-tech area in the medical field known as magnetic source imaging. Magnetic source imaging not only shows the regions or locations of tissue damage but also provides the functional state of the affected tissues. MEG has significant value in studying and observing the plasticity and reconstruction of damaged nerve functions, predicting, tracking, and evaluating the therapeutic effects of nerve function recovery. Currently, MEG is widely used not only in epilepsy and similar neurological diseases but also extends to neuroscience, psychiatry, and psychobehavioral medicine.</div>

<div></div>

<div>At the end of the interview, Professor Ding took us on a tour of the treatment area of the Beijing Jingke Epilepsy Specialty Hospital and revealed that the hospital's uniquely developed &ldquo;Neurotransmitter Balance Seizure Therapy&rdquo; has successfully cured nearly a thousand epilepsy patients, currently being hailed by world-renowned medical research centers as the &ldquo;most effective epilepsy treatment technology.&rdquo;</div>
