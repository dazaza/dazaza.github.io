Chinese domain names cover the globe in progress, protecting the uniqueness of corporate brands.

by now on 2009-04-02 16:12:56

Since the comprehensive popularization of CN domain names, they have been highly favored by enterprises. The corporate image and brand effect represented by these domain names are also recognized in the market. Now, as another national top-level domain, the ".中国" (".China") domain is equally hot, and its global deployment initiative is currently underway. It has been reported that the policy content of the "Chinese Domain Global Upgrade Action" released by the China Internet Network Information Center (CNNIC) has been adjusted to: all registrants of "Chinese.CN" domain names will automatically obtain the corresponding "Chinese.中国" simplified and traditional character domains, and be upgraded synchronously to the global root domain name system registration database of "Chinese Domain Names", enabling global internet access and a "second upgrade" for Chinese network brands. This news undoubtedly opens a new chapter in the history of Chinese domain names, with their development set to sweep across the globe.

In addition to this, where else does the vast market potential of Chinese domain names manifest? According to relevant executives at Times Interconnect, in fact, the favorable development of Chinese domain names is inseparable from the impetus of the market economy. Fierce competition in the market requires enterprises to continuously enhance their visibility in various aspects, seizing broad markets before developing further. With Chinese enterprises' products beginning to enter the world stage and receiving widespread acclaim, "Made in China" has become a golden brand on the global market, and many companies have already gained fame through leveraging brand effects. Furthermore, the registration policies for ".中国" domains ensure that enterprises simultaneously obtain both simplified and traditional character domains, which is equivalent to proactively completing certain defensive brand protection measures for the enterprise. This not only fully covers the global Chinese-speaking population, including Hong Kong, Macao, Taiwan, and overseas Chinese netizens, but also well accommodates the language usage habits of different Chinese-speaking netizens. Therefore, more enterprises are beginning to value and take advantage of the favorable conditions under the current environment, shaping their own brands on the internet.

Moreover, according to the author's understanding, by the end of 2008, if categorized by the language used by internet users, out of the global total of 1.574 billion netizens, 321 million use Chinese. It is predicted that it won't be long before Chinese replaces English as the most widely used language for internet users worldwide. These services allow real-time registration and immediate activation; offer full domain management rights with the ability to modify domain contact information at any time; support VDNS synchronized pointing for simplified and traditional character domains; support unlimited wildcard domain pointing, as well as IDN (Internationalized Domain Name) standards and TTL (Time To Live) controllable technology. Users can obtain high-quality after-sales service while registering Chinese domains, helping enterprises maximize their brand image during the optimal period when Chinese domains have enormous development potential, seize unlimited markets, and further ensure the global uniqueness of internet enterprises' Chinese brands.
