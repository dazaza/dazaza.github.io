The iSight camera gives the iPhone 5s a big lead over the 5c.

by anonymous on 2013-09-18 21:42:34

iPhone 5s

Since Apple unveiled its new iPhone last week, the question everyone has been asking is: Should I buy the iPhone 5s or the iPhone 5c? Fellow tech enthusiasts, don't hesitate—go for the iPhone 5s.

According to Chris O’Brien, a contributor to the Los Angeles Times, the iPhone 5s boasts three main selling points: first, a 64-bit processor that makes the 5s twice as fast as the iPhone 5; second, a fingerprint recognition scanner that offers enhanced security. These two upgrades are impressive but not enough to ignite a buying frenzy. In his view, the most dazzling feature of the 5s is its newly upgraded iSight camera.

Like most smartphone users, phones have become personal cameras. However, anyone who has used an iPhone 5 or older models knows that no matter how advanced technology becomes, capturing moving objects or shooting in low-light conditions remains relatively limited. But the iPhone 5s introduces some new features that elevate the quality of photos taken with an iPhone significantly.

The author admits that while he isn’t a professional photographer and can’t explain the technical details, as a mainstream user, some of the new features on the iPhone 5s genuinely excite him.

Both the 5s and the 5c come with an 8-megapixel iSight camera. However, the 5s has larger individual pixels, meaning it performs better in low-light situations. Additionally, the flash on the 5s is a significant improvement. Apple calls it “True Tone” and equips it with two differently colored flashes that layer together.

The True Tone flash works in conjunction with smart software algorithms, combining white LED and yellow LED flashes from the phone to calculate the ambient color and use over a thousand unique combinations to adjust the intensity and color temperature of the flash, firing simultaneously at different strengths during shots.

Finally, the iPhone 5s introduces a new setting called "Burst Mode," which captures 10 photos per second. This means users can quickly take hundreds of photos. The iOS 7 operating system allows users to select the best photo and quickly delete the others. Additionally, the ability to capture high-quality images at high frame rates enables the iPhone 5s to shoot slow-motion videos.

Just days after Apple's event, luxury brand Burberry announced it would use the iPhone 5s as its official camera device for its runway show at London Fashion Week on Monday. Burberry stated that the new camera on the iPhone 5s was outstanding and allowed them to capture behind-the-scenes video and photos to share with fans.

Last year, other brands introduced many great features in their smartphones. However, Apple’s improvements to the iPhone 5s seem targeted towards fulfilling the greatest desire of mainstream users—to be able to take good pictures easily with more types of settings. Compared to the 5s, the iSight camera on the iPhone 5c is similar to the iPhone 5 and still very good.

When it comes to choosing between the 5c and the 5s, O’Brien compares them this way. The 5c is $100 cheaper than the 5s, so for those looking to save money, the choice is clear. A smartphone's lifespan can last at least three years, so when making your decision, remember that this phone will be with you for at least three years, during which time you may take thousands of photos. Spending an extra $100 today will get you a top-of-the-line camera that won’t feel outdated in three years.

In other words, if money isn’t an issue, consider the long-term benefits. Spending an extra $100 on the iPhone 5s will give you photos that justify the price difference.

The iPhone event will be held on September 10th.
