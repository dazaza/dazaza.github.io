"Illustrated Dictionary of Hacker Terminology"

In the everyday learning of many hacking enthusiasts, you may often encounter unfamiliar terms. These terms can cause a lot of inconvenience for those who love to learn and can hinder your progress. This dictionary can provide you with maximum help at such times. Also, through this book, you can broaden your horizons and gain a wider and deeper understanding of both hacking technology and overall computer technology.

This dictionary took two years to compile, staying hidden underground during its creation, and is an unprecedented work. It is the first domestic professional hacker terminology quick-reference toolbook, including the latest and most popular technical terms of the information age. Its content almost covers all fields of computer technology, network technology, and security technology, subdivided into 15 major categories as follows:

001 Basic Hacking Techniques

This category basically includes all aspects of computer and network technology. Therefore, the basic hacking techniques are the most extensive among all categories, including almost all common and popular terms, common protocols, ports, and common intrusion methods, such as "Google Hack", "ping", "XSS", "TCP", etc.

002 Attack Methods and Behaviors

There are numerous attack methods in hacking technology, and when different attack methods are combined in different ways, they become even more attack behaviors. This category mainly includes common methods and behaviors during intrusion or attacks, such as "CC attacks", "SQL injection", "DDoS attacks", etc.

003 Operating Systems and Related

The operating system is the foundation of all activities on a computer. Whether it's program development, intrusion, or privilege escalation, a detailed understanding of the operating system is required. This category mainly includes some mechanisms of the operating system, common files, commands, and other related terms, including the operating system itself, such as "X-Window", "Swap", "Shell", "PID", etc.

004 Scripts

Scripts are an important technology on the internet. Nowadays, various dynamic websites are built on the basis of scripts. If there are problems with the script programs, it could lead to the collapse of the entire website or even the entire server. Therefore, it has always been a hot topic in China's hacking community. This category includes technologies related to scripts and some famous script programs, such as "FSO", "PHPMyAdmin", "OBlog", etc.

005 Databases

Database technology, together with scripts, forms the ever-popular SQL injection technique. This category includes introductions to mainstream databases and the core parts of database technology, such as "SQL Server", "SQL statements", "Oracle", etc. Many entries have detailed introductions and specific applications in hacking technology.

006 Viruses and Malware

Viruses are probably the most watched security terms on the network. Meanwhile, the harmful effects caused by malware are no less than those of viruses, so these two are combined into one category. It includes entries like "CIH", "BOTNET", "Slammer worm king", etc.

007 Applications

When performing many hacking activities such as intrusion and penetration, excellent programs are needed. Here, many neutral programs are gathered, which can be used for defense and maintenance of computers or networks, or for attacks, such as "Achilles", "Sniffer Pro", "WSockExpert", etc.

008 Hacker Tools

Hacking technology cannot fully depend on tools but also cannot do without good tools. This category includes many famous and excellent hacker tools, including "ARPSniffer", "LCX.exe", "NBSI", etc., some of which include basic usage methods and principle analysis.

009 Security Technology

Where there is attack, there is defense. This category includes many relevant entries related to security defense, including common security vulnerabilities, security defense tools, and security defense mechanisms, such as "ACL", "Acunetix WVS", "SSH", etc.

010 Penetration Technology

Penetration technology, as the most fascinating technology in hacking, is full of flashes of wisdom. Penetration is not just simple intrusion; it should be the process of entering the internal network from the external network until obtaining control over each host within the internal network. It requires taking different countermeasures according to different situations, making it the best indicator of a hacker's technical level. This category includes some basic penetration methods, such as "IP spoofing", "DNS spoofing", "Man In The Midlle", etc.

011 Debugging Technology

Debugging technology is an important branch of hacking technology. Through it, you can understand the program execution flow, which is the basis for discovering program vulnerabilities. If you truly master debugging technology, perhaps you can reach the realm of "Give me a program, and I will give you the source code." This includes some simple terms related to debugging technology, such as "DEBUG", "EIP", "ESP rule", etc.

012 Specific Names

This category includes many organizations and conventionally accepted names related to hacking technology, such as "ANSI", "Black hat", "IBM", etc.

013 Programming Languages and Development

"A true hacker must first be a good programmer." From this statement, the importance of programming skills for hacking technology can be seen. This category includes common programming vocabularies in hacking technology, such as "C32ASM", "DLL thread insertion", "SOCKET", etc.

014 Encryption and Decryption

Encryption and decryption are eternal topics in hacking technology. This category is not only about cryptography encryption and decryption but more about what people commonly refer to as "cracking technology" (i.e., protection and cracking technology of programs) and some related vocabularies and commonly used tools, such as "IAT", "MIRACL Big Number Library", "SOFTICE", etc.

015 Hardware

Hardware is the foundation of computers and naturally also the foundation of hacking technology. This category includes hardware closely related to hacking technology, such as "RJ-45", "HUB", "ROM", etc.

From the above classification, it can be seen that this dictionary basically includes all aspects of computer and network technology, reflecting the breadth of hacking technology. This hacking technology dictionary can also be regarded as a comprehensive dictionary of computer technology, network technology, and security technology. Therefore, this book is suitable not only for friends who love hacking technology but also for all friends who love computer and network technology.

Specifically, this dictionary has the following features:

1. Dual catalog vocabulary index provides maximum convenience for lookup.

This book provides two catalogs, one sorted alphabetically by "A-Z" (Catalog A), and another sorted by classification (Catalog B). When you encounter unfamiliar words, you can look them up in Catalog A, and when you want to find related words, you can flip to Catalog B to find words in the same category.

2. Concise and powerful word explanations.

When explaining words, the aim is to let readers roughly understand the meaning and function of the vocabulary after reading the first sentence, and key and important sentences are specially marked so that readers can grasp the essence at a glance.

3. Appropriate divergence in word explanations while closely linked to hacking technology.

Each word's explanation tries to connect with other knowledge and words, allowing readers to more easily expand their horizons. At the same time, it links its role in hacking technology and elaborates on it.

4. Connections between words are marked to prevent misunderstandings.

In the explanations of many words, some other technical terms might be involved, which could confuse some readers when looking up a particular word. To avoid this problem, this dictionary marks all existing words in the book, indicating that the term is already included in the dictionary, allowing readers to check it in the catalog.
