The founder of Omron, Kazuo Ikeda, has always emphasized in his writings: "Let every employee think about their work like the president." This philosophy has accompanied Omron's growth for 70 years.

Omron Corporation was founded in 1933 and was Japan's first company specializing in the production of industrial automation products. After nearly 70 years of relentless effort, its branches are now spread all over the world. Omron's products include industrial automation equipment and systems, control equipment components, public automation equipment and systems, healthcare equipment, and office automation equipment, which have been widely applied in various aspects of social production and life.

The founder of Omron, Kazuo Ikeda, has always emphasized in his writings: "Let every employee think about their work like the president." This philosophy has accompanied Omron's growth for 70 years. Despite being a world-class large enterprise, Omron can still continuously produce automation products that adapt to the rapid progress of society because the vitality of the enterprise can stimulate the noble aspirations of each employee.

What is the essence of Omron's corporate philosophy?

Omron's motto is "Work enriches life, creating a beautiful world." Every morning, employees of Omron around the world first silently recite the motto in their hearts before starting their day's work. To achieve this motto, Omron has six fundamental management spirits: maximizing user satisfaction, continuously challenging the spirit, valuing gaining shareholders' trust, respecting individuals, striving to be an excellent corporate citizen, and developing activities with high corporate ethics.

What are the manifestations of Omron's employees "thinking like a general manager"?

The phrase left by the founder, Kazuo Ikeda, has always been the guiding principle for Omron's healthy corporate growth. Based on my experience, the main manifestations are as follows:

1. Employees work proactively: In Omron Japan, to cultivate a healthy value system among the management, department managers must take a three-month leave after serving the company for six years. During this time, they rethink their lives and work. I myself have had such a three-month thinking experience. Despite being very concerned about stepping away temporarily, when I returned to the company after my leave, I was deeply moved by the disorganized yet excellent performance of the work situation. Without direct leadership supervision, employees can still voluntarily contribute their efforts to the company. If they didn't think like general managers, it would be impossible for employees to have such initiative and responsibility.

2. Positive innovation: Omron is a company that dares to take risks. Its DNA is "creating social needs," which means timely grasping social changes and users' potential needs, continuously creating new values, and opening up new markets. The driving force behind "creating social needs" is the entrepreneurial spirit. Thirty years ago, Omron, as a small parts manufacturing company, undertook many projects that large companies dared not attempt, such as the "ticket vending and checking machine." Under conditions of insufficient prerequisites and no reference materials, 12 technical personnel started from scratch and successfully completed the project within 12 months.

3. Unity and cooperation: Although advocating that employees think like general managers, this does not mean that everyone can act independently. In Omron, all positions are set up to meet customer needs. Whether in product development, production, or sales, one cannot only consider their own环节. Employees must think like the company's general manager, considering the big picture, and allocate resources.

How can each employee be made to think like a general manager? What specific methods are there?

Firstly, full delegation of authority. Only when employees have certain functional powers can their innovative thinking be converted into actual productivity. Moreover, delegation allows upper-level managers to focus on higher-level, more profound strategic issues. Omron has grown to be a large enterprise today but advocates managing large enterprises like small ones, absolutely resisting bureaucracy, arrogance, and complacency. Omron implements a branch company system, establishing many branch companies, thereby giving more autonomy to various regions.

Secondly, encouraging employees to innovate. Omron not only provides continuous education in business philosophy but also has many institutional guarantees. One of the most distinctive actions in Omron factories is the "improvement team." Improvement teams consist of employees from different departments who regularly sit together and discuss areas for improvement based on information obtained from customers. At Omron headquarters, there are awards such as "Improvement Award" and "Innovation Award." These awards are not just for new products but also recognize various innovative actions, and any staff member from any department may receive an award for their outstanding creativity.

Additionally, Omron has abolished the seniority-based promotion system, encouraging internal entrepreneurship, and giving young people opportunities to display their talents. It has established many "micro-companies," encouraging potential employees to step out and become general managers.

What do you think is the most important for achieving success in your career?

To become a successful person, one cannot just work hard; one must have ideals! Omron China's ideal is to create a second Omron in China. Thirty years ago, Omron's sales volume was 25 billion yen, and today its total production and sales have reached 600 billion yen. And China's current sales are also exactly 25 billion yen, at the same level as Japan back then. Our goal is to double it within three years. We believe that working together with proactive innovating and cooperative Chinese employees, our ideal will certainly become a reality.
