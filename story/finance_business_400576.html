Online shopping "phishing" traps are one after another

by anonymous on 2011-04-01 13:08:14

From the early "lottery scam" and then "fake website scam" to the current Trojan phishing cases, the means of phishing are becoming increasingly diverse and the harm is getting bigger.

The Spring Festival is coming, and online shopping has ushered in a new wave. However, Guangzhou VIPshop Information Technology Co., Ltd. has always been a high incidence period for online shopping "phishing" events during holidays.

Case 1:

The seller actively sent the "clothes detail picture"

On the evening of December 19, 2010, Miss Wu saw a brand coat priced at 649 yuan in the mall, so she found this coat in a one-diamond credit shop on Taobao and prepared to buy it. Then, Miss Wu opened Taobao Wangwang and chatted with the seller.

Miss Wu said that the seller emphasized that he directly purchased from the specialty store, no bargaining allowed, if not satisfied, free replacement within 7 days, etc. Later, the seller also "very thoughtfully" sent a file to Miss Wu, saying it was the detail picture of the clothes, allowing Miss Wu to see the goods more clearly. "That seller was very enthusiastic and easy to talk to, so I didn't pay too much attention and received the file." However, when she opened the file, her computer immediately popped up a Trojan warning.

At this moment, the seller emphasized that it might be a computer problem, so the computer could not be opened.

The bank has deducted money but the "payment" was not successful

Then Miss Wu took a screenshot of the coat and paid with her credit card online. "As usual, I clicked on the payment page and paid directly." After the payment, her mobile phone received the deduction information sent by the bank.

However, at this time, the seller sent a message saying that he did not receive Miss Wu's payment and sent a screenshot of "System Upgrade, Payment Not Successful, Please Contact Buyer Again".

So Miss Wu paid again, and this time the payment was successful.

Then, she logged into the online banking page of her credit card and found that her account had been deducted twice for 240 yuan, once by "Alipay" and the other by "YeePay". She immediately contacted the "YeePay" customer service, who said they did not receive her payment, which meant she was deceived by scammers using third-party software to steal her payment.

Then, when she contacted the seller again, the seller had already gone offline.

Police: Amount too low to file a case

Subsequently, Miss Wu called Alipay, whose customer service personnel said that before purchasing, Miss Wu accepted the "picture" file sent by the owner, but these files were not pictures, but "phishing" software. After the software started, the payment did not go into Alipay, but was immediately transferred to another account. After such a transfer, there was no payment record on Taobao, and it was impossible to investigate the seller.

Customer service personnel reminded that you should not casually open web links or files sent by the other party, because they might be Trojan phishing files, and consumers' money could easily be transferred away.

Then, Miss Wu called the police, and the police said that since the amount involved was less than 2000 yuan, the police would not file a separate case, but if other victims were found, they could report together and let the police file a case.

Case 2:

On October 17, 2010, Jiang Rong saw a sweater in the mall, and the price tag of more than 800 yuan made her think of online proxy purchase. When she returned home, she searched on Taobao and found an online store named Yi Yang Bridal Gown, which could proxy purchase this style of sweater, and the price was only 415 yuan.

"The seller's credit is only two stars, and the level is very low." Although Jiang Rong was a little worried, the owner claimed that they specialized in proxy purchases, and all were genuine products. Thinking that she had already seen the physical item, online proxy purchase should not have much of a problem, so Jiang Rong decided to buy it.

At this moment, the owner sent a file, claiming it was the detail picture of the sweater, asking Jiang Rong to look carefully before purchasing.

After receiving the picture, Jiang Rong immediately opened it, but the computer prompted "file damaged." "Since I've already seen the physical item, not looking at the detail picture doesn't matter much." So Jiang Rong took a screenshot of the product and paid.

When she successfully paid through online banking, a prompt appeared on the screen saying "Successfully Purchased NetEase Products."

Payment transferred to another account

"I obviously bought it on Taobao, how did it become NetEase?" Jiang Rong immediately checked and found that there was only her purchase record on Taobao, but no payment record. In other words, the 415 yuan she just paid had been transferred to another account.

Jiang Rong contacted Taobao customer service. The other party said that Jiang Rong chose instant payment for the transfer, the money was not in Alipay, and it could not be recovered.

Jiang Rong tried to contact the seller again, but the other party added her to the blacklist and ignored her.

The picture turned out to be phishing software

Taobao customer service said that recently there were many such cases. These victims all accepted the "picture" files sent by the owners before purchasing, but these files were not pictures, but "phishing" software. After the software started, the relevant information was tampered with when Jiang Rong transferred money, and the payment did not go into Alipay but was immediately transferred to another account. After such a transfer, there was no payment record on Taobao, making it impossible to investigate the seller.

Regarding this situation, Taobao is trying to find solutions.

Staff reminded that when shopping, consumers should not casually accept files sent by sellers, and should observe the seller's credit and buyers' reviews of the products when purchasing.

If the amount exceeds 2000 yuan, you can report it

When reporters clicked on the "picture" received by Jiang Rong yesterday, the computer firewall showed that the program was damaged - meaning what Jiang Rong received was not a picture, but a program. Reporters tried to contact the seller, but the other party was never online. Internet user Jiang Rong was moved by the seller's meticulous service when shopping online. Little did she know that behind the thoughtful service was a scheme to defraud her payment.

Yang Zhi, a lawyer from Lianzong Law Firm, said that if the transfer amount exceeds 2000 yuan, the seller is suspected of fraud, and consumers can report to the police; if the amount is less than 2000 yuan, due to the small amount, the police will not file a separate case, but if multiple victims are found, they can report together and let the police file a case.

This is the most common and typical phishing process. Through the organization of phishing-related complaints on the 315 Consumer Electronics Complaint Network, complaints related to phishing can be summarized in the following aspects:

1. Payment phishing. Scammers send fake third-party links during payment, and after the user pays, the scammer quickly buys other items with the money.

2. Sending virus phishing. Send compressed packages containing viruses under the guise of product pictures, etc., users click and their computers get infected, taking the opportunity to steal users' bank account numbers and passwords, or automatically replacing payment links during the payment process to defraud remittances.

3. Counterfeit bank official website phishing. Use methods such as bank payment confirmation emails to let users log in to phishing websites for confirmation, and once users log in, they may fall victim.

4. Prize-winning website phishing. Another form of phishing scams is setting up prize-winning schemes to entice netizens to remit money. For example, the most common ones include counterfeiting CCTV's "Very 6+1 Prize" and "Tencent Prize," etc.

There are five ways to prevent phishing when shopping online

From the early "lottery scams" to the subsequent "fake website scams," until the current Trojan-style online phishing cases, the means of phishing are becoming increasingly diverse, and the harm is getting bigger. Cybersecurity experts recently said that safe online shopping should follow the following five key points:

1. Follow the rules and procedures of the relevant platform;

2. Be cautious when receiving links and files sent by the transaction party;

3. Enhance security awareness, install professional antivirus software, and maintain a safe internet environment;

4. It is recommended to use Alipay's security products, especially Alipay cartoon, digital certificates, payment shields, etc., or install some online security tools, regularly repair computer vulnerabilities, and scan for malware;

5. If you encounter phishing, report it to the public security organs in the first place, and Alipay will help users recover losses.

Don't assume that these things are far from you. It's better to know more methods and enhance your prevention awareness.
