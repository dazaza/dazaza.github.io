The story of my younger aunt and brother-in-law

by aoki2013 on 2011-09-13 09:57:02

The girl works as a wedding dress designer, but she has never thought about designing one for herself. Because the person she loves can basically not marry her. Yet she strives so hard, creating one beautiful wedding dress after another, just hoping that her own regret can be compensated by other happy women.

Many years ago, the girl's sister was involved in a serious traffic accident on a misty Christmas Eve. When she arrived at the hospital, she saw her sister's fiancé, who struggled to ask her: "Will your sister die?" The girl, with red eyes, said: "No, you love her so much, she won't die." Indeed, the man she loved most had already agreed to marry her sister, and even the style of the wedding dress had been chosen. How could she suddenly let go? In her final moments, she ultimately left no words, but gave the person who loved her a lasting pain. The girl knew he must be very sad. Because he spent days guarding her sister, but never shed a single tear. Truly heartbroken people don't cry.

During those days, they both had a hard time. Finally, the girl decided to cheer up. She couldn't bear to see him getting thinner and more emaciated day by day. So one evening, she went to the man's house, made him dinner, and kept him company for the night. She just wanted to give him some comfort, some warmth. Since then, whenever the girl had time, she would go to his house, cook for him, and tidy up for him. She had never been so good to a man. If he came home late, she worried. If he ate little, she thought her cooking didn't suit his taste. She told herself that she was just being kind to him in place of her sister.

One day, while drawing design sketches at home, the girl designed a strapless wedding dress with pearl embellishments on the front. He looked at it and said: "Pearls are actually the secretion produced by oysters due to long-term external pressure and pain." She laughed and said: "No bride ever thinks about this issue; they don't know that their happiness comes at the cost of someone else's pain." "I want to visit your sister," he suddenly turned and said. He didn't ask her to come along, and the girl felt a bit disappointed. After he left, she quietly tidied up the design drafts on the table and went home, unable to think of any excuse to stay. It seems that small happiness truly comes from great pain, and he still loved her sister. That summer, it rained day and night.

One day, the girl walked down the street carrying a sample wedding dress. Suddenly, it started pouring rain. She didn't have an umbrella and couldn't find a place to take shelter. Just as she hesitated, she spotted his car. She got in and asked him: "How did you know I didn't bring an umbrella?" "Because you left it at my place," he replied. She was deeply moved and couldn't say a word. That night, the girl stayed overnight at the man's house. He slept in the living room, and she in his bedroom. Lying on his bed, the girl tried to feel his remaining warmth and scent, and thus felt quite satisfied.

The next morning, after getting up, she found him in the living room. He was curled up on the sofa, sleeping soundly. The girl wanted to kiss him, but she didn't have the courage. She pressed her fingers to her lips and then gently kissed him with her fingers. He woke up and looked at her with a confused expression. Originally, the girl wanted to ask him: "Will you fall in love with me?" But in the end, she only said: "Good morning!" Because she couldn't afford the price of losing him forever. At that time, one of the girl's close friends was getting married, and she attended the wedding. The bride, wearing a wedding dress, was radiant. The girl went up to offer her blessings, and the bride said: "We once said we would get married together." The girl bitterly smiled. "Let me introduce you to some good men," the bride enthusiastically said. Later, the bride really introduced her to some men. "I don't want to," she said. "He is very nice," the bride expressed regret. "Tomorrow evening, I have a date," originally, the girl didn't have any dates, but because of this excuse, she called her sister's fiancé, "Do you have time tomorrow evening?" "What's up?"

"I just got paid, shall we eat Korean food?" "Tomorrow evening, I have to go home and have dinner with my family, let's do it another day."

When she hung up the phone, she suddenly realized that she was nothing special to him. Since her sister passed away, she hadn't seen his family for a long time, nor had she been to their house. Maybe all along, she overestimated her importance to him. Without her, his life could still be fine. Thus, she accepted the invitation from the bride and her friend.

The man's face was hard to look at, yet he was gentle to the girl. The bride went to the restroom and asked her: "What do you think of him?"

"Better than I imagined," the girl thought, "let's be together, with him, I should be happy." So the girl attempted to start dating him. "Didn't you say last time you wanted to eat Korean food? Are you free tonight?" One day, her sister's fiancé found her. "Sorry, it's too late," she told him, also telling herself. The girl and the new acquaintance watched movies, had meals, went shopping, and every time after a date, he would send her home.

"What's wrong?" After she got out of the car, his taxi didn't drive away. "Nothing, I just want to look at you a bit longer."

"Fool," she laughed and waved goodbye to him. Is this what love feels like? The girl had never dated anyone before, and she almost thought she was in love. However, on the night of her birthday, she happened to encounter the man coming out of a hair salon nearby her home, where the lights were dim. At that moment, he was putting money into his wallet. "You're shameless!" She hit him with her handbag. She suddenly felt these days were absurd. Was she in love with him, or did loneliness make her crave love? Perhaps she once had feelings for him, but that emotion could not withstand any test.

Years ago, the girl's birthdays were always celebrated with her sister and her fiancé. Later, after her sister passed away, only her sister's fiancé remained to celebrate with her. Why did she forget those days when they relied on each other? This year, on her birthday, she actually wanted to spend it with another man, but that man betrayed her. As she walked, she suddenly realized she had reached the building below her sister's fiancé's apartment. She called him. "Is that you?" It was his voice. "Happy Birthday!" Her eyes watered slightly. "Where are you?" "Below your apartment." "I'll come down now," he said and hung up. She had walked down this street many times. How could she not understand? She belonged here, her love was here, and she wouldn't leave anymore.

"This book talks about the trends in wedding dress designs for the next few years, I hope you like it." He appeared before her with a book.

"Thank you." The girl took her birthday gift. His smile today was particularly good.

"Are you busy lately?" He asked.

"Hmm, but not after this." She sweetly replied.

Suddenly, the old days seemed to return. The girl gathered her courage and asked him: "Shall we go have coffee?"

But unexpectedly, he shook his head: "Sorry, my girlfriend is still at my place."

The girl looked at him and suddenly felt he was distant. She forced a smile and asked: "How long have you two been together?"

"About two months," he looked deeply at her: "Do you think I'm terrible?"

"No, you don't need to be trapped in sadness for life."

As soon as he turned around, she squatted down and cried.

It turns out, during those absurd days, she had already lost him.

But what could she do? She had done so much for him,

and he had never shown such a smile as today. Was it because she was his fiancée's younger sister? Then she had misunderstood from the start. If not, it was because she lacked the ability to make him like her.

In the book he gave her, the model in the wedding dress smiled radiantly. She remembered him saying that pearls are originally the product of an oyster's pain. Back then, she laughed and said: "No bride ever thinks about this issue; they don't know their happiness comes at the cost of someone else's pain." But if she could wear the wedding dress today, would she hesitate? She wouldn't hesitate, but it was something that would never happen.

A very melancholic love story.
