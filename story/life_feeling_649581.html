Dear, I will be your wife again in the next life.

When they graduated from university, the girl said to the boy: "I'm going to Beijing. Zhongguancun in Beijing is known as China's Silicon Valley, there are more opportunities, and it will be easier to get rich in the future." The boy said: "Then I'll go back to my hometown in Sichuan, which is called the Land of Abundance, with a lot of beautiful women. If you become successful and don't want me anymore, it will be easy for me to find someone else." The girl lightly tapped her fists on the boy's broad chest and pouted, saying: "You just think about beautiful women. Hmph, even if I don't want you in the future, you can only love me and think of me. You're not allowed to find other girls." The boy held the girl's hand and kissed her forehead affectionately, saying: "Silly girl, our parents are in Sichuan. Since you're going to Beijing, I, your good son-in-law, should go back and take care of my father-in-law and mother-in-law." Tears welled up in the girl's eyes, she threw herself into the boy's arms, and didn't want to get up.

Although the two were separated by distance, their feelings did not diminish. Their deep longing could only be conveyed through radio waves - sending text messages and making phone calls, both of them contributing wholeheartedly to the revenue of the country's telecommunications industry. One day, the girl read a story online about a couple. Every time they made a phone call, the boy would wait for the girl to hang up first. After the girl had experienced the vicissitudes of life, she realized that the man who loved her most in this world was the boy who always waited for her to hang up first.

The girl remembered this touching story that made her sigh and shed tears. That night after the call, she said to the boy: "You hang up first." The boy was surprised and said: "Silly girl, does it matter who hangs up first when we make a call?" The girl acted coquettishly and said: "No, no, you have to hang up first. If you don't listen, I won't talk to you in the future, and you won't be able to find a wife." The boy paused for a few seconds, then chuckled softly and said: "Alright silly girl, so that I don't end up being a bachelor, I'll hang up first. You're just wasting phone fees." When the girl heard the busy tone after the boy hung up, she smiled happily and said to the boy in her heart: "Dear, I love you, more than you love me."

From then on, every time they talked on the phone, she would hold her phone and listen quietly until the boy hung up first. And the boy would always laugh and affectionately call her "silly girl" before hanging up. As time went on, the girl gradually felt a faint sense of委屈 (unfairness): "Do you know? Hmph, every time I wait for you to hang up first. I silently love you so much, but you don't even realize it." She wanted the boy to also read that article and understand how much she loved him, so that one day he would also wait for her to hang up first, allowing her to truly feel what it means to be loved. But the girl restrained herself. Her happiness was sweet yet tinged with bitterness. She thought: "To deeply love a man in such a unique way for a lifetime is also a kind of happiness."

Like all the 'Beijing drifters', the girl's life wasn't comfortable. However, living in a dormitory building was better than those who lived in basements. The initial ambitious goals had been worn down to just a small tail, but the strong-willed girl never complained to the boy. Instead, she became more accustomed to telling the boy "I love you." The community where the dormitory building was located had poor security, and there was even a pervert who stole women's underwear. Before, with a roommate accompanying her, the girl didn't feel anything unusual, but the roommate left due to family issues, leaving the girl alone in the two-bedroom, one-living room, one-kitchen, one-bathroom apartment. Naturally, she began to feel lonely and scared.

That night, the girl woke up from a sound sleep startled by some rustling sounds. Listening carefully, she realized it was coming from the door. She clutched the edge of the quilt tightly, shaking all over, not daring to breathe. Helpless tears streamed silently from her eyes. Suddenly, her hand touched the mobile phone under the pillow, like grabbing a lifeline, she immediately sent a text message to the boy: "Dear, I'm scared." In fact, the boyfriend was far away in Sichuan. Even if a message could do anything, it was already late at night, the boy might have turned off his phone and gone to sleep. The girl forgot to call the police first, and at this most fearful moment, she could only think of the boy.

To the girl's surprise, a few seconds after sending the message, the boy's phone call came. She quietly answered, hearing the boy's concerned voice inside: "Silly girl, are you missing me?" The girl tried to lower her voice as much as possible, telling the boy that she was alone in the house, there might be a thief outside the door, and she was very scared. The boy consoled the girl not to be afraid. He thought for a moment and said to the girl: "Turn on the external speaker of your mobile phone, turn up the volume to the maximum, and slowly go to the door. Don't be afraid, dear, trust me, don't be afraid." The girl was smart, and as soon as the boy spoke, she understood his intention: the boy shouted loudly, letting the person outside know that there was a man inside, anyone trying to steal or having other intentions would leave quickly if they knew better. The girl trembled as she reached the door, turned on the external speaker of the phone, turned up the volume to the maximum, and then gently said into the microphone: "Okay, I'm at the door, and the external speaker is also turned on." At this point, the girl confirmed that there was someone outside, and not just one, she could hear their slight conversation.

Just as the girl's body was trembling to the point of almost unable to stand, suddenly there was a loud shout from the phone: "Who the hell is messing with my door out there? All the brothers inside wake up, we have guests." The boy's voice was high-pitched and rough, startling the girl greatly in the quiet night. But the people outside were probably even more frightened, the girl only heard a series of "plop plop" footsteps getting closer and farther away, looking like they had been scared away. The girl breathed a sigh of relief, her legs went limp, and she collapsed on the ground. The boy waited for a while, then gently asked: "Are the people outside gone, baby?" The girl finally cried, speaking into the phone: "Dear, I miss you."

Still shaken, the boy continued to comfort the girl. That night, the two held the phone and talked until dawn. The girl said: "Let's hang up soon, we've been talking long-distance for so long, it must cost a lot of money." The boy laughed and said: "What a silly girl." The girl said: "I am silly, that's why I fell for you. Hang up, dear, be careful not to fall asleep at work today and get scolded by the boss." After hanging up the phone, the girl's heart was filled with sweetness. She enjoyed the sense of security the boy gave her, but there was one regret: the boy seemed to have formed the habit of hanging up first, which was no exception this time. The girl thought: "Even though he is good, he doesn't seem to love me as deeply as the boy in that story loves the girl. He has never let me hang up first."

As the weather started to get hot, many of the girl's single-layer clothes didn't have pockets, so she often forgot to bring her mobile phone. For example, she forgot it on her desk during lunch breaks or left it in the rental place when going out with her roommate. Each time she returned, she would find missed calls and messages from the boy. Only at these times did she feel a bit fair: "Hmph, you always hang up on me first. Not being able to answer your call in time is a small punishment. Don't feel wronged, stupid pig."

May 12th, an ordinary day that couldn't be more ordinary. The girl was diligently doing her job in that small company, tirelessly striving for her little dream. Just before work ended, a piece of news spread around the office: A massive earthquake occurred, with the epicenter in Wenchuan, Sichuan, reportedly similar in magnitude to the Tangshan earthquake. The girl was shocked and instinctively reached for her mobile phone in her pocket, only to remember she had forgotten it!

She immediately picked up the office landline to call the boy, but the signal broke as soon as she dialed. Then she tried the home landline number, still no connection. It appeared that the communication facilities in Sichuan had been completely destroyed by the earthquake. A foreboding sense of unease surged in her heart. Unable to wait until the end of work, the girl rushed down from the office building, hailed a taxi, and hurried towards the dormitory building she rented. Turning on her mobile phone, she found more than fifty missed calls, all from the boy. She checked and found an unread text message: "Baby, darling, silly girl, I call you with all the love I have in this life, I love you, more than you love me."
