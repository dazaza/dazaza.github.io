It seems that we can no longer stop the pace of group buying informatization.

by beetgk2958 on 2012-02-24 13:24:36

Zhang Shaofeng, COO of Percent Point, believes that group-buying informatization can achieve the purpose of increasing revenue and reducing expenditure. On one hand, it allows businesses to work faster, improve efficiency, and cut costs; on the other hand, it enables group-buying companies to perform better. In the face of massive data, informatization can be used to quickly process information. Zhang Shaofeng also indicated that informatization can further help improve the operational efficiency of group-buying businesses, assist in fine-grained operations, and ensure a harmonious ratio between input and output for group-buying companies. The informatization prospect of the group-buying industry seems bright. However, due to various constraints, informatization may only be feasible among first-tier group-buying companies. Wang Rulin expressed, "Many people have too narrow an understanding of informatization. Informatization is a comprehensive, networked, and multifunctional system." From Zhang Shaofeng's perspective, some group-buying companies need to first solve the issue of survival before considering whether to engage in the informatization process. The key term for the e-commerce and group-buying industry in 2012 will be meticulous calculation. "The higher the degree of informatization in business operations, the greater its significance for development," said Irene Wang, an analyst at iResearch. The significance of informatization for the group-buying industry is mainly reflected in two aspects: First, informatization refines operations, allowing for more efficient and cost-effective handling of various business activities; second, companies can manage different sales channels more easily, avoiding channel management chaos and high costs. Currently, ERP, CRM, and other management systems are already applied in the daily operations of international large corporations, yet the group-buying industry has not fully utilized information technology, making informatization particularly necessary. In Wang Rulin's view, the traditional human-sea tactics can no longer keep up with the rapid development of group-buying. Informatization can enhance the management efficiency of group-buying, thereby promoting the development of the mutual support system and optimizing the efficiency of group-buying operations, significantly improving the systematic management performance of the group-buying system. For group-buying companies, the operation of informatization systems not only positively impacts their own enterprise efficiency but also effectively integrates various links in the group-buying ecosystem. As a pioneer in group-buying informatization, Lashou.com carries more profound implications. According to statistics from Group800, Lashou.com led the 2011 group-buying market with annual sales of 1.63 billion yuan. Industry insiders speculate that this bold introduction of the ERP system might be a powerful measure for Lashou.com to consolidate its leading position and lead the trend of informatization. Analyst Chen Shousong of Analysys International believes that informatization in the group-buying industry can enhance merchants' operational capabilities, which in turn can increase consumer repeat purchases, improve shopping experiences, and influence customer connectivity, "We need such specialized services." "In 2012, the group-buying industry needs to focus more on refined operations," according to Professor Su Meng. In his eyes, corporate informatization is not limited to internal management but also includes source management, involving data mining to enter the behavioral level of individual consumers, thereby promoting traffic conversion, increasing per-customer transaction volume, enhancing user retention rates, while ensuring backend procurement and supply chain, guiding corporate operations. Due to the unique nature of the industry, group-buying has been under constant scrutiny since its inception, experiencing rapid growth. This newborn seems to have found its direction and positioning. As informatization gradually integrates into every aspect of the group-buying industry, we see not only the improvement and enhancement of the group-buying industry's appearance but also the expectations and hopes placed on group-buying by the entire e-commerce industry. On the path to informatization, the group-buying industry may usher in a new round of competition. The enhancement of efficiency and the prominence of systematic management highlight professionalism. Wu Bo also publicly expressed his determination for group-buying informatization: "Collaborating with world-class management experts like IBM will effectively enhance the maintenance services of Lashou.com's core business systems. Meanwhile, I hope Lashou.com can become a typical case for IBM in the group-buying field, providing beneficial exploration and attempts for the informatization management of group-buying enterprises." The Matthew effect in the group-buying market has gradually become apparent. With the increasing maturity of consumer stickiness, the allocation of market resources has become clearer. After the reshuffling, first-tier group-buying websites face more severe development challenges. It seems we cannot stop the pace of group-buying informatization.
