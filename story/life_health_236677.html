Prostatitis is complex with various causes and types; a uniform treatment method should be avoided.

Why is treating prostatitis so difficult? Experts from the No. 254 Hospital of Tianjin analyze that it mainly relates to the cause, type, and therapy. Prostatitis is caused by both bacterial and non-bacterial pathological factors and is generally divided into acute and chronic prostatitis. Clinically, it manifests as frequent urination, urgent need to urinate, painful urination, cloudy urine, bloody urine, blood in semen, reduced sexual function, and pain during intercourse. In clinical practice, prostatitis is further classified based on its causative pathology into acute and chronic bacterial prostatitis, acute and chronic non-bacterial prostatitis, and chronic refractory prostatitis. For different types of prostatitis, personalized treatment methods targeting specific causes and pathologies are required.

For bacterial prostatitis, the main focus is on anti-inflammatory antibiotics, while symptomatic treatment is provided based on individual situations. Non-bacterial prostatitis focuses on physical and rehabilitation therapies, complemented by appropriate therapies tailored to the patient's specific situation. Refractory prostatitis is a chronic prostatitis that is hard to cure and prone to recurrence, requiring special treatment to achieve results. However, in clinical treatment, the reasonable classification and standardization of prostatitis treatment are not followed. Both bacterial and non-bacterial prostatitis, as well as acute and chronic prostatitis, are treated with the same "one-size-fits-all" method, leading to no therapeutic effect. Moreover, the abuse of antibiotics and inappropriate treatment cause imbalances in the body’s bacterial flora, drug resistance, and complications such as prostate cancer, male infertility, urinary tract infections, and nephritis, posing significant risks to subsequent treatment and patient health. Therefore, scientifically reasonable classification-based treatment is crucial for the recovery from prostatitis.
