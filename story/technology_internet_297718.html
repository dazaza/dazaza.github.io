Jacky Cheung travels with his family instead of attending the Spring Festival Gala, and hopes that CCTV will inform him earlier next time

by 8clt601y1m on 2009-12-07 13:19:50

The title "God of Singers" for Hong Kong's Leslie Cheung is truly deserved. Despite the continuous emergence of new talents in the music industry, every album of Leslie Cheung still sells very well, and his tours remain extremely popular. In a recent interview with reporters, he stated that because he had already promised his family to go on a trip during the Spring Festival, he would not be able to attend the CCTV Spring Festival Gala.

Leslie Cheung accompanies his family on vacation instead of appearing on the Spring Festival Gala (file photo). On December 6, Huashang.com - Huashang Daily reported that the reputation of Hong Kong's "God of Singers" is well-deserved. Even as new talents continue to emerge in the music scene, every album released by Leslie Cheung still sells remarkably well, and his concert tours are consistently successful.

In a face-to-face interview in Hong Kong, this down-to-earth "God of Singers" revealed his deep attachment to his family due to his Cancer zodiac sign. For him, singing and acting are merely work; accompanying his wife through their children's growth is what brings him the most joy.

Regarding his new film role, he described it as "vulgar but cute." As the "God of Singers," Leslie Cheung has also achieved significant success in the film industry, with his comedic talent long recognized. This time, in the New Year film "72 Tenants," he plays an old and vulgar character, describing his vulgarity as being of the cute type.

Reporter: You have two films, "72 Tenants" and "Hot Summer Days," competing in this year's New Year season. Are you worried about the box office?

Leslie Cheung: Box office is ultimately not something we actors need to worry about. Initially, one film was scheduled for the New Year season, and the other for Valentine's Day, so I thought they were perfectly staggered. However, this year, the Spring Festival coincides with Valentine's Day, so even someone like me who rarely makes films ends up competing with myself.

Reporter: This time in "72 Tenants," you play an old and vulgar character, and it's an antagonist. Are you worried about affecting your image? The other three of the Four Heavenly Kings are still trying to appear youthful or maintain their handsome looks. Why are you willing to play an old man?

Leslie Cheung: Actually, at first, I wasn't playing this character. Zeng Zhiguo switched roles with me just before filming started. He wanted to look younger, so I played the older character to match him. Moreover, my personality is quite good, so even if I play a vulgar character, it's more of a cultured vulgarity, making it cute. I've even played monsters before, so there's no role I'm afraid to take on. Liu Xiaqing once said, "How can you only act as yourself? If you do, why even become an actor?" So playing an older character isn't an issue. In fact, the other three kings aren't really trying to appear youthful either; they're just playing different types of characters.

Work Focus: Family

Among the Four Heavenly Kings, Leslie Cheung was the first to get married and have a daughter. His life hasn't been weighed down by the title of "God of Singers." Talking about his daughters, Leslie Cheung's face lights up with happiness, just like any other father.

Reporter: I heard that your condition for accepting jobs now is not leaving Hong Kong?

Leslie Cheung: If everything is filmed in Hong Kong, there are no restrictions. But if it requires leaving Hong Kong, it usually takes two or three months, which I really can't manage. My daughter needs to go to school, and I can't leave her. Everyone has their own role in the family. My role at home is relatively important. If I leave for too long, it causes some chaos in the family.

Reporter: Is your family more important than your work now?

Leslie Cheung: My basic work pattern is family-oriented. I only go out to work after everything at home is arranged. Regarding the CCTV Spring Festival Gala, since I've already promised my family to go on a trip during the Spring Festival, I won't be able to attend the gala. I hope they inform me earlier next time so I can apply at home. Anyway, missing a child's growth is something you can't get back. I hope to be by their side as they grow up.

Reporter: What household chores do you handle at home?

Leslie Cheung: Picking them up from and dropping them off to school, helping with homework. My elder daughter is a bit older, so I talk to her about my views on things and try to have heart-to-heart conversations with her. Otherwise, they will gradually stop talking to you. My younger daughter is still fine, always sweet-talking me.

Reporter: You're known as a good husband and father. How do you achieve this in such a restless entertainment circle?

Leslie Cheung: (laughs) No, it's just that people outside see me and call me a good husband and father, but they don't know how I am at home. It only counts if my wife and daughters say I'm a good husband and father. I really can't claim that myself.

Reporter: Since you prioritize family in everything, does that mean you don't have much ambition?

Leslie Cheung: I don't have much ambition. But I am a rather persistent person, which might make others think I'm ambitious. For example, when I did musicals before, I was just very persistent in wanting to do it well. But eventually, outsiders might think it's ambition. Actually, I just have a strong sense of mission towards my work.

Reporter: The movie "Forty" you starred in expressed some middle-aged crises for men. Do you feel such a crisis yourself?

Leslie Cheung: I haven't felt that way. I'm already old, so if there's a crisis, it should be an old-age crisis, haha.

Next Year, Very Much Wants to Visit the Ancient City of Xi'an

This year, Leslie Cheung appeared more in movies, but he said singing is his career, and acting is his hobby. Although Leslie Cheung has been to Xi'an, he hasn't toured the city. He hopes to hold a concert in Xi'an next year and then take a good tour of the ancient city.

Reporter: Your work this year has mainly been in filming movies. Does this mean a shift in your work focus?

Leslie Cheung: Let me put it this way. My career is singing, and acting is just my hobby. Being an amateur filmmaker is great, but singing is the most important thing for me. It's just that work arrangements follow a process. For instance, there are more movies this year, so there's less record work. Next year, music will be the main focus, and I might not take on any films.

Reporter: With new talents emerging in the music industry, there was Jay Chou earlier, and recently Karry Wang. Does their emergence put pressure on you?

Leslie Cheung: I was once a newcomer, putting pressure on my seniors. Now that I'm a senior, feeling pressured is normal. You can't say it's okay to put pressure on others but feel unhappy when others put pressure on you. This is a normal process. However, music has its advantages; each singer has unique qualities, and it's impossible to say who replaces whom. There will always be people who like them.

Reporter: You've performed in Xi'an twice. What impression do you have of this city?

Leslie Cheung: Every time I go to Xi'an, it's very rushed. Before going, I actually planned to visit some historical sites, but once there, I didn't have the opportunity or time. So when you asked if I want to hold a concert in Xi'an next year, I really hope to go, so I can explore the city properly.
