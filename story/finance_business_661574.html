The latest national standard rules for billiards - let's study together~ Definition: Free Ball Right 1. When one party fouls, the other party can place the cue ball at any position on the table to start hitting. 2. A free ball behind the line means that when the opening party fouls, the party gaining the free ball can only place the cue ball anywhere behind the opening line and can only hit any target ball between the opening line and the top edge.

I. Competition Method

8-ball competition uses one cue ball (white) and 15 target balls numbered from 1 to 15. Balls numbered 1 to 7 are solid-colored balls, number 8 is a black ball, and numbers 9 to 15 are two-tone balls (also known as patterned balls). The two parties determine one type of ball (solid or patterned) as their legal target ball according to the rules. After all the target balls of one side are legally pocketed according to the rules, the side that pockets the 8-ball wins the game. If one party mistakenly pockets the 8-ball or knocks it off the table during the game, the opponent wins the game.

II. Equipment

1. Billiard Table: Inner dimensions 2540mm x 1270mm, height from ground to cushion top is 800mm-850mm.

2. Cue Stick: Must meet the standards recognized by the Chinese Billiard Association.

3. Bridge Stick: The tournament-provided equipment must be used in the match.

4. Ball Placement Point: The point located 635mm from the head cushion along the long centerline of the table.

5. Opening Line: A straight line parallel to the foot cushion, 450mm away from the bottom edge and intersecting with the left and right cushions.

6. Opening Zone: An effective opening zone drawn on the table by the event organizing committee (commonly seen in professional events).

III. Arranging the Balls

Before the opening break, the target balls are arranged in a triangular shape, with a total of 5 rows, each row containing 1 to 5 balls respectively. The first row's single ball is placed on the 'placement point', the 8-ball is positioned in the center of the third row, and the remaining target balls are interspersed with solid and patterned colors and placed randomly but must be tightly adjacent. Both sides of the competition have the right to check whether the arrangement of the balls complies with the rules and request modifications.

IV. Opening Break

1. Determination of the first break is decided by both parties simultaneously striking two identical cue balls toward the foot cushion from behind the opening line. After rebounding and coming to rest, the party whose ball is closer to the head cushion gains the opening break. If the struck ball does not touch the foot cushion or falls into the pocket, it is a foul, and the opponent gains the opening break; if the distance of both balls from the head cushion cannot be determined or both parties commit fouls, the process is repeated until one party gains the opening break.

2. The competition organizing committee may determine before the match that multiple games will alternate between the two parties breaking or the winning party breaks.

3. After the opening break, at least one target ball must fall into the pocket or at least four target balls must touch the table cushion. If after the opening break, the cue ball jumps off the table or falls into the pocket, or fewer than four target balls touch the cushion, it is a foul.

Penalty for violating this rule: The opponent may require repositioning of the balls and the breaker to re-break; or the opponent gains free ball rights after the line.

4. If the 8-ball directly falls into the pocket after the opening break, the breaker must re-break.

5. If no balls are pocketed and there is no foul after the opening break, the opponent continues to strike.

V. Determining Color

1. After a party legally pockets a ball in an open ball situation, the type of ball they pocket (solid or patterned) becomes their legal target ball for that game, and the other type of ball becomes the opponent's legal target ball for that game, closing the game.

2. If a ball is pocketed during the opening break, regardless of quantity, color, order, the opening party continues to strike and has the right to continue choosing types. Thereafter, if the selected type of ball is legally pocketed, the game closes; if the selected type of ball is not pocketed, the game remains open, and the opponent strikes and can continue to choose types; if the selected type of ball is pocketed but accompanied by the cue ball falling into the pocket or the cue ball or target ball leaving the table, the game remains open, and the opponent gains free ball rights.

3. In an open game, the striking party can use balls of one color to legally pocket balls of different colors or use the 8-ball to legally pocket other target balls without committing a foul, but this does not close the game, and the opponent strikes.

VI. Striking Rules

1. Players do not need to designate which ball or pocket (except when playing the 8-ball), if the entire striking process has no fouls, then all pocketed target balls of their own side are valid, and they gain the right to continue striking: if their own target balls are pocketed simultaneously with the opponent's target balls, the opponent's target balls are not retrieved; if only the opponent's target balls are pocketed, it is not a foul, and the opponent strikes.

2. After either party strikes, the first ball the cue ball contacts must be their own target ball (after all their own target balls have been pocketed, the 8-ball becomes their target ball).

Penalty for violating this rule: The opponent gains free ball rights.

3. After the cue ball strikes a target ball, if no target ball is pocketed, at least one ball must contact the table cushion (including the cue ball).

Penalty for violating this rule: The opponent gains free ball rights.

4. After striking, the unpocketed target balls and the cue ball must remain on the table.

Penalty for violating this rule: If the 8-ball stays off the table, the striker loses the game; if other balls stay off the table, the opponent gains free ball rights, and the jumped-off target balls are reasonably removed.

5. During the striking process (including before and after the stroke), except for the tip of the cue, no part of the striker's body (including clothing), equipment (including the cue shaft, bridge stick, chalk) should touch any ball on the table.

6. In one striking process, the tip of the cue cannot strike the cue ball more than twice (including twice).

Penalty for violating this rule: The opponent gains free ball rights.

VII. Clinging Balls

1. When the cue ball clings to one of the player's target balls on the table, after the player strikes the cue ball, the target ball can be moved, and there is no restriction on the angle of the shot, but the striking action must be obvious. If the player strikes backward, the target ball does not move, and it is not considered to have touched the target ball.

Penalty for violating this rule: The opponent gains free ball rights.

2. When the cue ball clings to a non-player's target ball on the table, after the player strikes the cue ball, the target ball cannot be directly moved by the strike.

3. When a target ball clings to the table edge, after the cue ball strikes the target ball, the target ball must leave the table edge and then touch it again or another ball touches the table edge or a target ball is pocketed.

Penalty for violating this rule: The opponent gains free ball rights.

VIII. Jump Shots

1. The player can jump the cue ball off the table to leap over other target balls and directly hit their own target ball according to technical needs, but Rule Six still applies.

2. During a jump shot, the player can only strike the upper half of the cue ball with the tip of the cue, and the cue used must be no shorter than 90 centimeters.

Penalty for violating this rule: The opponent gains free ball rights.

IX. Losing the Game

1. The opponent pockets the 8-ball into the designated pocket without committing a foul.

2. Pockets the 8-ball before all their own target balls are pocketed.

3. Knocks the 8-ball off the table (except during the opening break).

4. The cue ball and the 8-ball fall into the pocket simultaneously while striking the 8-ball.

5. The pocketed ball is not the designated pocket when striking the 8-ball.
