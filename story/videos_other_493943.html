This early winter

by vghfeifei on 2011-09-23 15:02:03

This early winter, the sunshine is brilliant, and basking in the sun every day should make one's mood as fierce as this sunshine. But for some unknown reason, I feel inexplicably low and sad! Maybe it's the pressure of work, the dullness of life, or perhaps the skyrocketing prices, or the risks of the stock market...

To possess sorrow, to cherish sorrow, on the journey of life, emotions will surely be rich and colorful, and life will certainly be delicate and beautiful! Truly, sometimes, sorrow is also a kind of beauty.

They all say that optimism is a kind of beauty. Thinking about it, sorrow is also a kind of beauty - a most genuine, purest beauty, a calm beauty after the storm, a tranquil beauty after experiences, an utmost passionate beauty, a mature beauty! Understanding sorrow, comprehending melancholy, only then can feelings be the richest, sentiments the warmest, and moods the kindest. Sorrow is the green leaves of life, often decorating the beauty of life unintentionally. In the midst of busyness, after sorrow, I still like to write some words, allowing myself to always have an intimate connection with my soul, making days beautiful due to spiritual richness, and thoughts full due to the flow of words.

Time really flows like water. Regardless of whether we want to grow up or not, it always rushes forward with a completely different appearance. Unknowingly, our innocent faces gained maturity, and unknowingly, the face of youth left many marks of time. The ruthlessness of years, the hardships of life, bit by bit change our appearances and our hearts. The once clear-spring-like eyes no longer see the world as a beautiful painting; innocence has been squeezed into the deepest part of the heart. Everyone's life is not perfect, so everyone's life will have more or less moments of sorrow.

At some point, I always hoped to be with happiness, to be with joy. But when sadness comes, I realize that happiness was originally a luxury for me, always unexpectedly accompanied by sadness. Happiness cannot be erased, and neither can melancholy be waved away.

Often, I enjoy listening repeatedly to sentimental music in quiet afternoons or peaceful nights, reading repeatedly pieces of sad texts. Quietly, I give my heart to this sorrow, getting intoxicated in sorrow, contemplating in sorrow, and experiencing another unique, real beauty in sorrow. Now, my weary heart bears many burdens of life, rushing ceaselessly in the river of years, striving towards the ideal goals I set for myself in the alternation of wind and rain, climbing continuously towards what is called the height of life.
