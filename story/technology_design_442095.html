2. Social Etiquette

Koreans are strong in character, diligent and brave, with a strong sense of national self-esteem, strong organizational discipline, unity, and strong group consciousness. In recent years, due to the rapid economic development and relatively affluent life, they have a strong sense of inferiority. Koreans are hospitable, good at singing and dancing, and women respect men very much.

Koreans generally use handshakes as a greeting ritual in more formal social occasions. When shaking hands, they usually use both hands or only the right hand. When juniors, subordinates shake hands with seniors and superiors, after the latter extends their hand, the former should first shake hands with the right hand, and then lightly place their left hand on the back of the other person's right hand. This practice by Koreans is to show their special respect for the other party.

In normal circumstances, Korean women do not shake hands with men but often bow or clap to express greetings. The meeting etiquette performed by Korean children towards adults is roughly the same.

In many situations, Koreans sometimes use the method of bowing first and then shaking hands as a way to greet others when meeting.

When Koreans meet or part ways with others, if the other party is a person of status or identity, they often bow multiple times. Bowing three to five times is not considered excessive. Some Koreans may even bow once for every sentence they speak during such occasions.

In certain situations, Koreans love to use honorifics and polite language when referring to others, but rarely call out the other person's name directly. If the person they are addressing has a title that reflects their social position, Koreans will certainly use it repeatedly when addressing them.

In social settings, especially among the younger generation of Koreans, most can speak English and view it as a sign of being well-educated and having received good education.

When Koreans deal with outsiders for the first time, they pay great attention to making prior arrangements, following the time schedule, and place significant importance on the use of business cards.
