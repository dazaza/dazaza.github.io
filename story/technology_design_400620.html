[One, Nine Common Sense Tips for Preventing Online Shopping Scams]

1. When shopping, buyers should not have the mentality of looking for small bargains. For example, if seller A is selling an item much cheaper than seller B, you need to ask yourself, can it really be this cheap? Or is it a counterfeit product? I once had such an experience. Because of the temptation of a small bargain, I bought a camera memory card that was 50 yuan cheaper than other sellers', but it broke after only a week of use. At that time, I immediately asked the seller for a replacement, but his phone was already disconnected and there was no way to contact him.

2. Issues with large brand manufacturers. On platforms like EachNet and Taobao, there are many big brands being sold. You just need to type in "Sony" and a bunch of products you might need will appear. However, at this point, you must not act impulsively (of course, because their prices will be much cheaper than those on the market). When you see a product from a certain seller, you must check their sales authorization certificate, because according to the rules of large manufacturers, they will definitely give sellers an authorization letter to sell branded items. However, based on my knowledge, the examination for online sales authorization by large manufacturers is very strict. Due to the chaotic online pricing, random authorizations could directly affect their brand's status.

3. If you cannot confirm whether the seller is authorized, please do not rush to make a purchase. First, check the seller's reviews. Do not just look at one or two reviews; you should look at at least a page worth of them. Look at other people's feedback after receiving their goods, preferably seeing their neutral and negative reviews. Those who have transacted with the seller will definitely leave comments. However, it is worth noting that if all the buyer's comments in the seller's reviews are either "good" or "very good", you need to be cautious. This is because he may be using constant exchange evaluations to gain high credibility, then profiting through fraudulent means. Here, 77dao suggests that if you are transacting with a seller whose credit rating is below 500, you should try to require the seller to use Anfutong for the transaction.

4. When buying goods, the buyer must carefully read the seller's product description. There was once a seller who marked the price of the product as 8 yuan, but in the product description, it said that the buyer needs to pay 8000 yuan, and if they don't buy after placing the bid, they will receive a bad review and warning. Don't think this is fake - it really happens! Reading the product description carefully can help you understand how much postage you need to pay when bidding on the product, whether there are any other additional fees, and how long it will take to receive the product after payment. All these are necessary!

5. Before purchasing, you must first inquire about the product's after-sales service from the seller, including the return policy if the product has not been unsealed or the tag has not been broken upon receipt. If it's an electrical appliance, you must understand the warranty period. Please note that return/exchange policies and warranties are different. Return/exchange means you can get a new one back, while a warranty involves repair if it breaks down. Who pays for the parts costs? These are important details!

6. When remitting money or returning goods to the seller, ensure that the seller's name is personally signed. Additionally, after remitting money or returning goods, be sure to keep all your transaction receipts to prevent the seller from not shipping the goods or not refunding the money.

7. If you believe you have been scammed, please do not panic. Immediately contact the seller through various methods. Then, immediately file your complaint to EachNet or Taobao, detailing the seller's username, the transaction ID of the item you purchased from him, your remittance slip or mail receipt during returns, the pictures of the items you need, and the time of the transaction. All these are crucial evidence. Through these methods, the seller can usually be traced. Why? Because Both EachNet and Taobao collaborate with police nationwide. If your evidence is sufficient, the police can take action. Your indulgence will make online scammers even more rampant!

8. Remember to avoid private transactions as much as possible.

9. Always use third-party guarantees, such as Alipay-type payment guarantees, to ensure safety. Avoid direct bank transfers as much as possible.

[Two, Technical Guide for Preventing Online Buying and Selling Scams]

Part One: Buyer Anti-Scam Techniques for Online Shopping

1. Substitution Trap (Username and Address Traps)

This situation involves someone using account name A to bid on your item and pay, while account name B contacts you. The two usernames are very similar, for example, Zizimian and Zizitu, tyreal0316 and tyrea10316. Alternatively, during the chat, they request you to send the item to an address different from the one provided in the Alipay record. At this point, you need to be careful. If you're not careful, you'll fall into the scammer's trap. At this point, the scammer might complain about you instead, saying you "received payment but didn't ship the item." Since you shipped it to an address different from the one left in the scammer's Alipay, you won't be able to provide sufficient evidence to Alipay that you actually shipped the item. In such cases, your chances of successfully defending against the complaint are slim.

Countermeasure: Strictly ship to the address of the paying buyer. If the buyer wants to change the delivery address, insist that they modify it in the Alipay transaction record. Otherwise, it's better not to proceed with the transaction and not give the scammer any chance. Pay attention to whether the user ID contacting you matches the ID that made the payment. If there are discrepancies, you need to be more vigilant.

2. Empty Pocket Scheme

The buyer claims they urgently need the item, but currently, their account doesn't have enough money. After you ship the item, they become unreachable. Or, the buyer places a bid on your item, tells you they've already paid, urging you to ship quickly. But when you check, the transaction information shows "waiting for buyer's payment"! This situation is common for sellers of prepaid cards... You need to be cautious.

Countermeasure: Don't release the eagle until you see the rabbit - only ship after the customer has paid.

3. Trojan Horse Scheme

The buyer sends you a picture or link, asking "Do you have this style?" When you open it, your computer gets infected with malware, resulting in stolen account passwords and significant losses.

Countermeasure: Digital certificates, antivirus software, and U-Shields are all essential...

Additionally, don't casually accept pictures sent by others, and always check if links are suspicious addresses.

4. Blossom on the Tree

The buyer says they have a large order and currently want to place a single order first, requesting a discounted price.

Countermeasure: Stick to retail prices; wholesale discounts can be discussed for larger orders later.

5. Transfer of Goods Scheme

The buyer says they received the item, but inside the package is a large brick. You clearly remember sending them digital products, and you certainly aren't in the business of selling construction materials. The user demands a refund and threatens to return a brick unless you agree, otherwise they'll leave a bad review and file a complaint.

Countermeasure: Preferably use express delivery and request the customer to open the package in front of the courier. If using regular mail, clearly write the shipping details in front of the postal staff when mailing.

6. Threatening with Bad Reviews (Demanding Compensation)

The buyer receives the item and says they're not entirely satisfied but don't want to return it. Instead, they demand compensation, which means partial refunds. Otherwise, they threaten to leave malicious reviews.

Countermeasure: Ultimately, credit is the lifeblood of sellers online, so it depends on whether the buyer's request is reasonable.

Suggestion: In such cases, it's better to persuade them to return the item, as even if you give them some compensation, you won't know how they'll evaluate you.

7. Deceptive Call Charges

The buyer bids on your item but doesn't pay. They don't respond to messages on Wangwang or QQ. When you call the number they left, you receive a huge phone bill at the end of the month.

Countermeasure: Don't force people who bid on items but never contacted you again... Even if you decide to call, check what numbers the left phone starts with...

8. Zombie Revival Scheme

The buyer receives the item and expresses dissatisfaction, demanding a refund or exchange. With a responsible attitude, you agree to their request. You refund the money or resend an item, but you never receive the item they were supposed to return.

Countermeasure: Strictly follow the process - only refund or re-ship after receiving the returned item...

Part Two: Seller Anti-Scam Category

We all know that when buyers choose bank transfers or remittances to purchase items, there is a certain level of risk involved. If the other party lacks integrity, they might likely engage in fraudulent behavior like collecting payments without shipping goods. But as a seller, are you completely safe and risk-free in such transactions?

Below, we present several examples of scams involving remittances and transfers to deceive sellers.

1. After obtaining your account, the other party quickly informs you that the transfer was successful and urges you to ship the item soon - when you log in to your online banking to check your account, the system prompts that the password input attempts exceeded the limit for the day and you cannot log in today!

Just as you're pondering this, the other party sends a screenshot showing a successful transfer, urging you again to ship the item as soon as possible. At this point, some sellers might believe it to be true and, unable to resist the other party's urging, ship the item. - Once you ship the item, you've fallen for the scam!

In fact, it's precisely because the scammer continuously logs in with incorrect passwords after obtaining your account, preventing you from checking your account. As for the screenshot of a successful transfer, it's naturally forged. Meanwhile, the scammer repeatedly insists they urgently need the item, asking you to ship it as soon as possible.

2. The other party leaves a message saying they want to buy a certain item and provides their email address, hoping you'll send your bank remittance account along with the item's link to their email.

One or two days later, you find an email in your inbox titled: "There's an inter-bank payment waiting for you to collect." The email contains a link claiming it's a website of a certain bank. If you click on the link, you might see a page almost identical to the standard version of CITIC Bank's online banking. Where's the trap? It's in the section where it asks you to activate the so-called UnionPay function, including selecting the card issuance location, entering the card number, password, username, and ID number! Good heavens~ Never fall for it! Once you enter your important personal information, all the RMB in your account will be taken away by the scammer!

Key points to identify the scam:

First: The email address sending this email is usually a regular registered email.

Second: Several secure connections on the linked website are invalid. Fake websites cannot use the bank's https.

Reminder: Never enter your bank account information on unfamiliar websites. Once stolen, you lose all the money in your bank account!

3. Add Zero Scam: The amount to be transferred should be 68 yuan, but the scammer buyer transfers only 0.68 yuan, adding an extra zero. When checking the online transaction query, if the seller isn't careful, they might truly mistake it for 68 yuan!

4. Partner Scam: This scam mainly targets sellers with high sales volume. Two different usernames simultaneously buy items of the same price and notify the seller of remittances at different times. In reality, only one remittance is made! Careless sellers see the money in the bank and ship the items.

5. Another type involves clearly not making a remittance but informing the seller that they have done so.

The scams mentioned above all exploit the seller's carelessness in an attempt to defraud both money and goods. Sellers simply need to be slightly more attentive when checking accounts to avoid falling victim.

[Three, Anti-Scam Tips for Online Appliance Purchases]

Buying appliances online is gradually becoming a new trend. According to the latest survey data from CNNIC, China now has 338 million internet users, over 55 million of whom are enthusiastic about online shopping. Among them, young people aged 25-35 have become the main force in online appliance purchases. While online appliance shopping is convenient and cost-effective, how can one avoid pitfalls related to authenticity and quality? To address this issue, journalists conducted in-depth interviews with some internet users and appliance industry professionals, summarizing the following experiences for consumers' reference.

First, choose reputable online malls. What constitutes a reputable online mall? Just like traditional malls, check if it has a business license. For online malls, primarily confirm if they have an ICP license, i.e., a Value-Added Telecommunications Business Operating License. Any legitimate online company will possess this document. Generally, reputable online companies display their ICP license number on the bottom of their homepage. To avoid counterfeit ICP licenses, internet users can verify them at www.miibeian.gov.cn.

Second, it's best to choose malls that offer direct delivery services from manufacturers. Appliances are not the same as clothes; generally, they have higher value. Therefore, when buying appliances online, it's best to choose third-party platform malls like Brand Home Network. Goods are delivered and collected by manufacturers or distributors, reducing the likelihood of counterfeit products. Moreover, if issues arise, both manufacturer and online mall after-sales services can be enjoyed, providing more security.

Third, remember to request official invoices. Generally, genuine or branded goods come with official invoices. If the online-purchased goods cannot provide users with official invoices, caution is advised, as resolving problems might be difficult.

Fourth, check if the after-sales service is comprehensive. For online appliance purchases, after-sales service includes aspects such as support for cash-on-delivery, responsibility for delivery and installation, provision of national joint warranty, and enjoyment of three-guarantee after-sales services. If all these after-sales services are available, procurement can be done with confidence.

Fifth, always inspect the goods upon delivery. This seemingly minor step should not be overlooked. During inspection, first check if the outer packaging is intact and undamaged. Additionally, each appliance packaging box has a unique barcode indicating the product's factory origin; confirm if this barcode exists. Next, check if the warranty card, usage instructions manual, and installation precautions manual within the packaging are complete. Generally, professional home appliance malls like Brand Home Network have customer service personnel who promptly track the delivery process, reminding and guiding customers to inspect the goods.

Sixth, assess if the customer service personnel are professional. This is a relatively detailed aspect. Generally, customer service personnel in more formal online malls are trained and deal with products daily, thus becoming very familiar with them. If they are unfamiliar with their own products, it indicates at least two things: first, this online mall is not professional, and second, this online mall sells few products. In such cases, beware of counterfeit risks.

Moreover, online reputation is also a significant factor. Generally, websites with good reputations have little chance of selling counterfeit goods. For instance, in the current home appliance industry, well-reputed specialized online malls for home appliances include Brand Home Network and others.

[Four, Anti-Scam Guide for Online Notebook Computer Purchases]

Nowadays, everyone likes to buy laptops online, but scammers are everywhere, making it hard to guard against! Looking at prices, there are all sorts of cheap computers available online.

I never trust those items that are thousands of yuan cheaper. Why?

The first principle of online computer and digital purchase secrets:

The safest option is the most cost-effective.

A penny saved is a penny earned – this truth applies everywhere. If you want to buy a laptop for 4000 yuan, and it's a legitimate channel product, don't expect to buy it for 3000 yuan. Genuine products go through strict channel price controls from the manufacturer to the primary distributor and then to regional distributors.

Online shopping truly saves money by finding merchants with the greatest price advantages through this relatively lower-cost channel. These primary distributors target business clients and rarely handle retail customers directly.

If you don't get good products, even the cheapest prices are not worth it.

After all, a computer costs thousands of yuan. Anyone buying it would think thrice, unless it's Li Ka-shing, but even he wouldn't buy a computer online to save a few hundred yuan. Generally, ordinary people like us buy computers online, and one computer often equals two months of our savings.

On the other hand, if risks cannot be effectively prevented, it's better to buy from a computer city.

The second principle of online computer and digital purchase secrets:

You must shop on large, reputable online shopping sites.

The threshold for online shopping is low. Anyone with basic computer skills can create a few pictures and start an online store. If you just look at prices, there are always cheaper items online. Some websites claim to sell Apple computers worth tens of thousands of yuan for just a few thousand. If they really had such capabilities, Apple would probably stop production altogether, shut down its factories, and buy computers from them instead. Clearly, such offers are all scams.

So, where can you buy laptops online at the lowest price and with the highest safety?

Products like computers generally operate under a regional general agency system. Manufacturers select one general agent for each region, and all the products produced by the manufacturer are purchased by these general agents. The general agents then sell the products to dealers in various cities and counties. These general agents are extremely powerful, requiring billions of funds to meet the manufacturers' purchasing tasks. Moreover, these general agents fully represent the regional market in negotiations with manufacturers and have the most say in pricing.

Goods we usually buy from specialty stores are sold by general agents to regional dealers, who then sell them to specialty stores. By the time they reach us, multiple layers of profit have been added. Then, specialty stores add their profits... making the final price quite expensive.

If you try to buy directly from the general agent, unless you're purchasing dozens of units, they won't even consider it. General agents typically only sell to merchants who sign contracts for continuous purchases and do not sell to retail customers.

However, things are different now. Online shopping for laptops and other products has become a major trend.

Therefore, many general agents have started to pay attention to online sales.

If you shop on an online site opened by a general agent, the quality will definitely be the most reliable and the cheapest, as these goods are directly shipped from the manufacturer.

Many young parents choose to buy infant and toddler products online. It's not only convenient and fast but also cheaper. However, while cheaper is attractive, it's important to be wary of online shopping traps. Below are tips for parents buying infant and toddler products online, and hopefully, they will also be helpful to all online shoppers.

Clarify Objectives

Before buying, you can discuss with other parents on parenting forums and set a clear goal in advance. Only after selecting the right goal can you compare quality and price. Online shopping relies heavily on credibility. Be sure to check the evaluations given by buyers to the store and see others' opinions about the products. Also, branded baby clothes and products are generally much cheaper when bought online compared to in stores, offering great value.

Sharp Eyes, Distinguishing Authenticity

Sometimes on shopping websites, there are instances of fake reviews. I believe this can be detected. Generally, if a store's seller credit lacks specific content and only selects positive reviews, there might be an issue. A serious online shopper will usually provide some evaluation of the product. Additionally, when unsure about product selection, you can search for popular items on platforms like Ju Baopeng, giving you a reference. Remember, it's just a reference, as some may cheat by having many collections but few actual sales and few positive reviews. Such options should be avoided; choose those with many sales, many collections, and relatively moderate prices.

No Fear in Asking, Ask for Peace of Mind

Baby products and clothes are of utmost importance to mothers. Whether it's the material, craftsmanship, brand, feel, you should inquire thoroughly. Although you can't touch the physical item online, besides relying on others' evaluations, you can ask directly. For example, ask about loose threads, whether it's 100% cotton, if there's any odor, whether the baby feels comfortable wearing it, and if the size is accurate... Don't hesitate to ask. Friendly and hospitable store owners enjoy introducing their good products to you.

When paying for goods, I generally choose Alipay on Taobao, which is safe, convenient, and quick. Payments are released only after receiving the goods, and you can evaluate the product afterward. Of course, some stores require immediate payment. Unless you know or fully trust the other party, you should never choose the immediate payment method, as it offers no protection.

Goods Arrival, Sign Receipt in Person

Each time you receive goods, you should see the items and sign for them in person. For special transportation issues, you can consult the courier company. If you don't accept the goods, they will handle the matter, and the seller will assist.
