10.162010-12-03 14:23:41 Reads 1 Comment 0 Font size: Large Medium Small Subscription The strongest cold air in a month launches an offensive; the Central Meteorological Observatory issues another cold wave warning [Font size: Large Medium Small] December 3, 2010, 07:03:03 Source: China Weather Network Expert on the line: Chief Forecaster of the Central Meteorological Observatory He Lifu (photographed by Guanyu) China Weather Network News Starting from today (December 3), a strong cold air current will affect most areas of our country from west to east. The temperature drop for the Yangtze River Delta and its northern regions is generally 6~12℃. So, how does this cold air current affect us? Does it meet the standard of a cold wave? What preventive measures do people need to take? Chief Forecaster He Lifu of the Central Meteorological Observatory pointed out that this cold air has characteristics such as wide-ranging effects, strong winds, severe temperature drops, and heavy snowfall in northern Xinjiang, which will have adverse impacts on agriculture, animal husbandry, transportation, and daily life in our country.

Most parts of the north experience sharp temperature drops accompanied by strong winds. The Central Meteorological Observatory predicts that from December 3 to 7, a strong cold air current from the Siberian region will affect most areas of our country from west to east, which is also the strongest cold air process since November. Due to this cold air current, the general temperature drop across the country is 6~12℃, with some parts of Xinjiang, most of North China, and southern parts of Northeast China experiencing a temperature drop of up to 14~16℃.

"This cold air has features such as wide-ranging effects, strong winds, and severe temperature drops. Most parts of the north may experience cold wave weather," He Lifu told reporters. This cold wave is divided into two stages, mainly affecting the northwest region from December 3-4, and impacting the central and eastern regions of our country from December 5-7. The Central Meteorological Observatory issued the second cold wave warning of the second half of the year.

National Temperature Drop Forecast Map (December 3~7, 2010) From December 3~4, the strong cold air will mainly affect most parts of Xinjiang, northern Qinghai, central and western Gansu, western and northern Inner Mongolia. These areas will experience winds of level 4~6, with mountain passes in northern Xinjiang reaching wind force 9~10. Temperatures will drop by 6~12℃, with some parts of northern Xinjiang and southwestern Xinjiang seeing temperature drops of 12~16℃. On the morning of December 4 and 5, the lowest temperatures in the Altay region will drop to -25~-35℃; along the Tianshan Mountains in northern Xinjiang, the lowest temperatures will drop to -15~-22℃; in the Ili River Valley and southern Hami, the lowest temperatures will drop to -10~-15℃. From December 5-6, the lowest temperatures in most parts of southern Xinjiang will drop to -8~-15℃.

From December 5~7, the strong cold air will successively affect eastern Northwest China, North China, Northeast China, Huaihai region, Jianghuai region, Jianghan region, South China, East China, Southwest China's eastern regions. The Yangtze River Delta and its northern regions will experience northerly winds of level 5~6, with temperature drops of 6~12℃. Some parts of North China and southern Northeast China may see temperature drops of up to 14~16℃. Southern South China, eastern parts of East China, and eastern parts of Southwest China may experience temperature drops of 6~10℃, with local areas exceeding 12℃. From the night of December 5 to December 7, there will be strong winds of level 7~9 in the eastern and southern seas of our country.

"Due to the very noticeable warming in most parts of our country earlier, it can be clearly seen from the national temperature anomaly chart over the past 10 days that the temperatures in most parts of our country are generally about 2℃ higher than normal. Therefore, this cold wave will cause a very severe temperature drop, and preparations should be made for keeping warm to prevent cold-related diseases like colds," He Lifu added.

In addition to strong winds and temperature drops, sandstorms will occur in some parts of the Tarim Basin and western Inner Mongolia from December 3~4. From December 4~5, five cities in Hexi Corridor and Baiyin City will experience northwesterly winds of level 5~6, with some places having sandstorms.
