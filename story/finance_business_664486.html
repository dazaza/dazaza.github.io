Rondo said he was the better man.

by dekokfo399 on 2012-02-13 15:37:38

"You can see it," Rivers said. "We're trying to run. That's how we need to play. If we don't turn the ball over" -- the Celtics had 19 turnovers -- "we'll have more points. I just like our pace, something we talked about a lot after the game in Toronto."

Rondo posted his 15th career triple-double (including playoffs) and second this season with a 32-point, 15-assist, 10-rebound performance.

"I just feel like he wants to win," Rivers said. "I think we play better when he plays fast."

Yeah, Toronto. Fine, a bad game can be a "teachable moment," they say. And Toronto was bad because of it.

Tom Thibodeau, coach of the Chicago Bulls: "Rondo's a great player, and even if you're guarding him, he still has the ability to make great plays."

This was Rondo's 15th career "triple-double," nine coming in the regular season. In overall impact, it may not rank with his 29-point, 18-rebound, 13-assist gem against the Cleveland Cavaliers in Game 4 of the 2010 Eastern Conference semifinals. That remains his personal gold standard.

Paul Pierce: "When he's confident, aggressive, playing the way he is, we are one tough team to stop when he's out running the break, pushing the ball, taking shots, rebounding, he's just all energy, he does it all, and he's fun to watch."

Rondo made sure everyone knew from the start that Sunday's game would be different. He did so the best way he knows how -- by running the show, feeding his teammates and getting everyone involved.

In that regard, this effort nearly mirrored, save for the close call at the end, the Celtics' trip to New York Knicks on Christmas Day where Rondo delivered (31 points, 13 assists, five steals). This time, against the No. 1 team in the Eastern Conference, the Celtics never trailed, building a 14-point lead midway through the fourth quarter before sweating out the rest of the way.

And let others speak for him.

If that's what it takes for Rondo to go, who knows? Rivers doesn't.

"I wish I could get inside someone else's head deep," Rivers said.

Rondo's ninth career regular-season triple-double led the Celtics to a 95-91 win over the Bulls. The Bulls fell to the Celtics without Rose.

CJ Watson missed two free throws with 1.9 seconds left in Sunday's game between the Boston Celtics and Chicago Bulls but the outcome was already decided. The Celtics had sealed the deal, ending a two-game losing streak and arguably their game of the season.

When things go wrong offensively -- the Celtics scored 74 points against a bad Toronto team -- point guards often hear it from the coach. He really would, too, since his coach also happens to be a point guard.

Rondo played 40 minutes, and was at his most efficient on the floor with Derrick Rose, who was hobbled, helping out. Rondo finished with a season-high 32 points and 15 assists along with 10 rebounds in the Celtics' 95-91 victory.

You might say Rondo was due. You might even say his coach told him he was due. Rondo didn't play Friday night in Toronto (he had plenty of company in that regard), having played only the night before against the Los Angeles Lakers, which coincided with news that he wouldn't be an All-Star for the first time in three seasons.

On the next possession for Chicago, Johnson picked off a Luol Deng pass, and Rondo secured the ball. The capper for Rondo was another alley-oop dunk to Chris Wilcox, pushing the lead back above 14 points and prompting a Thibodeau timeout. Yes, you see, correct; the Celtics dunked again. We know Rondo responded. Maybe it was his response game, a big "you know what," to all those Eastern Conference selections, one person from the New Jersey Nets -- the Nets' coach!! -- instead of him. We'll have to wait to learn Rondo's take on events since he chose not to speak postgame.

But the missed free throws meant everything as Rondo dove and secured the rebound, picking it up off the TD Garden floor before anyone else could get to it. There it is: Rondo's second triple-double of the season for the Celtics' point guard capped off a monstrous afternoon.

There were big times, but none stood out more than in the fourth quarter. The Celtics led 82-72 with 5:33 remaining when Rondo picked up a pass from Boozer and completed a perfect alley-oop feed to rookie JaJuan Johnson for the slam. Johnson stepped up in the absence of Brandon Bass and Jermaine O'Neal, finishing with 12 points in 33 minutes.

That's what Rivers means when he talks about the pace of the game. When the Celtics can get out in transition like that, Rondo is a certified threat. He wasn't just slicing, dicing and dishing. He was constantly breaking to the basket. He took 22 shots, making 11, and went 13-for-13 from the line. Only one Celtics player has reached double figures in makes in a game this season: Rondo. He did it on Christmas Day. He didn't Sunday.

But for a team that had lost two in a row and needed a statement win, this is exactly what the doctor ordered. And against a top-tier opponent, in front of a national TV audience, Rondo delivered.

Nowhere was Rondo's presence more evident than in the Celtics' running game. Boston scored 95 points against a team that was allowing 87 per game and had given up 67 and 64 in its previous two contests (albeit against Charlotte and New Orleans, but both on the road). The Celtics shot over 50 percent for much of the game, finishing at 49.3.

But here are the stats that tell the story: The Celtics outscored Chicago 33-7 in fast-break points. They turned Chicago's 13 turnovers into 18 points. That's where Rondo can run wild, and that's exactly what he did against the Bulls.
