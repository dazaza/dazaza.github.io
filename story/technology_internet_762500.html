Zhengzhou workers outside the production line

by anonymous on 2013-07-24 15:47:43

Labor costs are a major part of a company's expenses. To cut labor costs, profit-driven enterprises either lay off employees or relocate to places with lower salary levels. This is exactly what Foxconn has done - moving from Shenzhen to Zhengzhou, where Zhengzhou has gradually become a new manufacturing hub. Around the clock, the factory produces international goods like the iPhone. Here, young Chinese workers refuse to become mere "worker ants."

Near the factory, there is a bar where people of all kinds come in the evening to listen to explosive disco music and dance their bodies away. Lian Yulong (phonetic), a 19-year-old inspector who works on the iPhone motherboard at Foxconn, is a regular visitor to the bar. "Dancing helps me vent my anger and stress. Whenever I'm here, I forget everything else." He uses dancing to escape the monotonous and dull reality of his day.

An increasing number of leisure spaces attract workers back to the assembly lines. These workers manufacture products that others desire, yet they endure hardships themselves. Because opportunities are rare, their rest time is precious, and now they endure the present for a better future. Each worker looks forward to the weekend with excitement, even though some have simple plans. For instance, Bai Si Hai (phonetic), a 24-year-old worker, plans to indulge in gaming online and then call his girlfriend far away.

Now, the supervisors in the factory are beginning to see the benefits of vacations. In recent years, "worker suicides" have been an ominous cloud over companies like Foxconn and Samsung factories. After several reports of "sweatshops," under external pressure, Foxconn improved workers' benefits and promised not to make them work overtime. However, after another suicide incident at the Zhengzhou factory, Foxconn initiated a "silent mode," prohibiting workers from discussing non-work-related topics during working hours. Despite claims from Foxconn that this rule was canceled amid external doubts, workers say everything remains the same as before.

The domestic factory environment cannot compare with foreign ones. Overseas workers enjoy high allowances and various benefits and entertainments such as haircuts, climbing walls, table football, etc., and their offices are luxurious. Domestically, workers wear uniform uniforms and listen to routine scolding and yelling from their supervisors. As for dormitories, a room houses eight hard metal bed frames, along with a combined bathroom and toilet, with nothing more.

Perhaps because of this, there is a large market in front of the factory gate. As the sun sets, Mandarin pop music drifts out from the hair salon, DVDs are sold by street vendors, slices of watermelon and roses are displayed under dazzling silver light. A flatbed truck loaded with different colored teddy bears draws the attention of young girls. Some boyfriends, at the command of their girlfriends, immediately take out 10 RMB to buy one. At this market, there are vans with artistic tattoos and large arcade games. Young workers drink light beer while continuously smoking.

Occasionally, elaborately dressed singers will perform on nearby stages. As for the youth, they find ample entertainment in strong liquor. Luo Haojie (phonetic) left the factory in May, quitting his job making iPhone 5 components. He said, "Our factory supervisor is vicious." Speaking to a circle of drinking friends, he complained about the unbearable food in the factory cafeteria.

Ultimately, he will seek another job, but for now, he enjoys his youth, drinking with friends and dating girls. Luo says, "I stayed because of my buddies. Without them, I'd be miserable."

Summer is the low season for Chinese factories, allowing many workers to enjoy weekends off, sometimes even double days. The diverse entertainment options near the factory satisfy their recreational needs. In the evening, mobile food stalls are set up, named to resemble KFC. Acrobats perform, and someone blows fireballs. Workers say it would be better if you came a few months earlier when the acrobatics troupe had real lions and tigers.

On Saturday, another iPhone 5 motherboard tester, Zhou Peng Zheng (phonetic), wearing roller skates worth thousands of RMB, carefully avoided plants and started spinning. These skates amount to a third of his monthly salary. He said, "It feels like I'm flying." Before the crowd gathered, he quickly skated another lap.

Quad skates and roller skates are gradually forming a cultural atmosphere among those striving at Foxconn. Several teams have been formed, and on weekends, they glide through the city in their skates. Fang Ti Ma (phonetic), 17 years old, joined a hundred-person skating team named "Shadows" soon after starting her job at Foxconn. However, due to age restrictions, she could not work overtime and left the factory. She said, "I used to come to the skating rink twice a week, now I come every night."

At 11 p.m., the performers have gone, and clock hotels begin to get busy. "We work every day, why not go on dates?"
