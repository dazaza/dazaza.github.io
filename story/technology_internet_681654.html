Odd news: Beef is more expensive than pork! Butchers complain that it's too expensive and no one is buying meat.

by uefbp54187 on 2012-02-28 14:44:19

Due to insufficient supply of live pigs and rising feed costs, pork prices which started to rebound from the bottom since the second half of last year have maintained an upward trend in the past year. This has not only reduced the amount of meat on some citizens' dining tables, but also made the pork "small vendors" in the market feel a bit strained. "Too expensive, can't sell." Experts predict that as we enter the off-season for pork consumption in July and August, prices will temporarily fall.

Stall owner: No business all afternoon

"I'm about to close up, and I haven't done any business all afternoon." At around 3 PM yesterday, in the pork trading hall at Baliqiao Market, a female stall owner was complaining to an old acquaintance. Mr. Zhang, the stall owner, told reporters that the current pork price is more expensive than during the Spring Festival, "Back then, the back thigh meat was only 9.5 yuan per catty, now it's 13.5 yuan." "It's too expensive, no one wants to buy. Only the morning is okay, there's almost no one in the afternoon."

Another female stall owner at another counter complained to the reporter that now she works all day but hardly makes enough to cover her stall fee. "Nowadays, I sell at most two or three sides of pork, two or three hundred catties, much less than before. The stall fee is over 4000 yuan a month, about 130 yuan a day. After deducting the stall fee and other expenses, I make only ten or eight yuan net profit per pig sold. When business is bad, sometimes I can't even earn the stall fee in a day."

Price: Hasn't fallen since the first lunar month

"A pound of ribs is 15 yuan, a pound of back thigh meat is 13.5 yuan, it's almost as expensive as beef!" Yesterday afternoon, in the pork trading hall at Baliqiao Market, Aunt Wu was looking at the fresh meat on the counter, picking it up while expressing surprise.

According to Mr. Cui, the boss of a meat stall, since the second half of last year, the price of live pigs and chilled fresh meat has been continuously rising. "Since the first lunar month, the price hasn't dropped," he said, now he has to go earlier to get supplies, "almost everything has to be snatched, otherwise you might not be able to get any supplies."

Reporters learned yesterday that the retail price of beef is still at 15 yuan per catty. The retail price of pork back thigh is 13.5 yuan per catty, front thigh is 12.5 yuan per catty, and loin is even selling for 15 or 15.5 yuan per catty.

Bun Shop: Meat buns make half the profit

Mr. Tong, who runs a small steamed bun shop, was pulling a cart full of vegetables and meat out of the pork trading hall. He revealed to the reporter that the rise in pork prices has had quite an impact on him, reducing his profit from selling buns by almost half. "For example, originally selling a basket of 10 meat buns could earn me 1 yuan, now I can only earn 50 fen."

He pointed to a bag of meat on the cart and said that previously he would need to buy at least 50 catties of fresh meat daily to chop into filling, now he only buys 20 catties at a time.

"With less meat coming in, if there isn't enough meat filling, will each bun contain less?" In response to the reporter's question, Mr. Tong smiled without answering and rode away on his tricycle.

Subsequently, reporters investigated some restaurants near Shuangqiao in Chaoyang District and Beiyuan in Tongzhou District, and found that there hasn't been any noticeable increase in the price of meat dishes. On the menu of a dumpling restaurant, reporters saw that pork scallion, pork cabbage, and pork sauerkraut filled dumplings were still priced at 5 yuan per liang. Some dumpling restaurants indicated that if pork prices continue to rise, they may consider adjusting the price of dumplings appropriately.
