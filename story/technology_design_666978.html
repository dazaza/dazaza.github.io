Analysis of the Characteristics of Internet Advertising

by hebei56cn02c on 2012-02-16 12:17:22

The Extensiveness of Online Advertising Dissemination

Online advertising can spread advertising information around the clock, 24 hours a day, through the international Internet to all corners of the world. This could be described as an uninterrupted form of communication regardless of weather or obstacles. Internet users can browse advertising information anytime and anywhere on the Internet, which is an effect that traditional media cannot achieve. In fact, this is one of the key advantages of online media over traditional media, reflected in online advertising as the ability to broadcast globally. However, for many clients, this advantage does not seem to have particularly strong appeal.

As we all know, companies hope their advertisements target specific regional audiences rather than everyone, otherwise it may lead to reduced attraction and effectiveness of the advertisement. For example, manufacturers of health supplements would prefer their ads to reach people who need such products. Such requirements also cause some traditional industries to misunderstand online advertising, such as real estate, because real estate developers' clientele has a strong regional nature, mainly targeting residents and businesses within a city. Therefore, placing advertisements in local newspapers, TV stations, or roadside billboards will undoubtedly be more effective than websites that "target the whole world." It can be said that no matter what kind of enterprise, its marketing strategy ultimately needs to focus on a certain area or group of people. Although CCTV's advertising revenue is substantial, compared to numerous local media outlets across the country, it still represents only a small portion. From this, we can see that the extensive characteristic of online advertising dissemination has yet to fully translate into an advantage for online advertising.

The Statistic Nature of Ad Audience Numbers

A mainstream viewpoint often encountered is: "With traditional media advertising, it is difficult to accurately know how many people receive the ad information. On the Internet, however, authoritative and impartial visitor traffic statistics systems can precisely count how many users have viewed each client’s advertisement, as well as the time distribution and geographic distribution of these users, thus helping advertisers correctly assess ad effectiveness and review advertising strategies."

However, in reality, the number of ad audiences cannot be statistically counted in absolute terms. Using click-through rates to measure the effectiveness of banner ads is fundamentally flawed; the role of online advertising is not solely determined by whether you click on it. Therefore, evaluating ad effectiveness based on so-called statistical numbers leads to absurd conclusions.

The Sensory Nature of Online Information Dissemination

Online advertising allows consumers to personally "experience" products, services, and brands comprehensively. Transactions, including reservations and settlements, can also be conducted online, which is something traditional media cannot achieve. By comparison, whether it's TV shopping via television or mail-order sales through newspapers and magazines, none of them can compete with the Internet in this regard. From this perspective, compared to traditional media, online advertising indeed demonstrates unique advantages.

The Interactivity of Online Information Dissemination

For online advertising, if the audience is interested in the ad, they simply need to click the mouse to learn more detailed and vivid information. The interactivity of online information dissemination is most evident in e-commerce websites, where product categories are detailed and layers are clear, allowing direct transactions online.

However, current network bandwidth imposes various restrictions on the interactivity of online advertising. Consumers cannot truly "fully experience" products. While online advertising claims to be multimedia with images, text, video, and sound, in practice, it falls short of achieving this. Although the content of online advertising is richer than newspapers and television, in terms of communication effects, it lacks the impact of traditional media advertisements like those on TV. Clearly, due to limitations in network conditions, much of the interactive advantage of online advertising has been offset. To achieve greater development, online advertising must see breakthroughs in network technology.
