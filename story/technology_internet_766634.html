Six Questions About Apple iPhone 5c: Where are the Changes? Should I Buy It?

by anonymous on 2013-09-24 21:02:08

Apple introduced two iPhone models during a single launch event, which is a first since the release of the original iPhone in 2007. Prior to the announcement, rumors had been circulating that Apple would unveil a low-cost version of the iPhone, leading many budget-conscious consumers to anticipate the possibility of acquiring a new iPhone at an affordable price. However, reality proved harsh as the high price tag of 4,488 yuan for the iPhone 5c dampened the enthusiasm of many potential buyers. Tencent Digital obtained the retail version of the iPhone 5c just before its official release. Let us conduct a comprehensive analysis before you decide whether or not to purchase the 5c.

**Q: Is the positioning of the iPhone 5c awkward?**

Although initial "misdirection" led many to believe that the iPhone 5c was essentially a cheaper version of the iPhone 5 (i.e., iPhone 5 cheap), it became evident after the launch that "c" stood more accurately for "colour." The hardware configuration of the 5c mirrors that of the 5 entirely. Considering the current lineup where Apple China still sells the iPhone 4 and 4S, the 5c's pricing would naturally be higher than these two models. Therefore, the price of 4,488 yuan seems reasonable.

Many have questioned why the release of the 5c meant the discontinuation of the 5. It’s standard practice for older models to be phased out when new ones are launched. However, it’s unusual for Apple to continue selling products from two years ago while discontinuing last year's model. From this move, it's clear that the 5c has strategically replaced the 5 as the mid-to-high-end representative in the iPhone product line. Currently, the price difference between the iPhone 4, 4S, 5c, and 5s is approximately 1,000 yuan each. In terms of market positioning, the introduction of the 5c aligns with consumer demand.

**Q: What changes does the iPhone 5c bring compared to the iPhone 5?**

The most significant change lies in the design of the casing. The 5c's shell switches from the anodized aluminum used in the 5 to plastic, and adopts a vibrant color palette instead of darker hues like black. Bright colors such as pink, white, yellow, green, and blue make the 5c visually appear more budget-friendly but also more appealing to younger users.

The 5c features a unibody design for the back and sides, unlike the iPhone 5, which separates the front and back panels from the frame. In terms of dimensions, the 5c measures 124.4×59.2×8.97 mm, slightly larger in all directions compared to the 5. Additionally, the 5c weighs 132 grams, making it 20 grams heavier than the 5 despite using plastic instead of metal. This might disappoint some users.

The primary differences in detail are on the side. Firstly, the bottom design retains a single speaker, but the microphone has changed from a honeycomb pattern on the 5 to a dot pattern on the 5c. Similarly, the speaker holes on the right side of the 5c differ from those on the 5. Without a metal frame, the 5c doesn't need to worry about signal interference, so the ceramic antenna on the side of the 5 has been removed. Lastly, the volume buttons on the 5c resemble those on the fifth-generation iPod Touch—long strips instead of dots.

**Q: Are there any changes to the camera on the iPhone 5c?**

In terms of hardware, the camera on the iPhone 5c is identical to that of the iPhone 5—an 8-megapixel iSight camera. The 5c simply continues to use the same camera as the 5. Although both feature an 8-megapixel iSight camera, the one on the 5s is more advanced, with larger pixel sizes and an f/2.2 aperture, giving it an edge. Simply put, the 8-megapixel lens on the 5c can now be considered a "lower-end iSight."

However, in practical imaging tests, the iPhone 5 clearly outperforms the 5c at the same level. Whether in color reproduction, white balance, or temperature control, the iPhone 5 has a noticeable advantage. Despite identical hardware, the main reason for this disparity lies in software. The latest 6.14 firmware on the iPhone 5 performs better in camera calibration than iOS 7. It seems improvements will depend on Apple enhancing the camera's software.

**Q: Has the battery life improved?**

Battery performance on the iPhone 5c remains unchanged from the iPhone 5, still lacking significant improvement. I conducted a five-hour battery stress test comparison between my current 6.14 version iPhone 5 and the iOS 7 version iPhone 5c I received. Details are shown below:

Both phones were tested simultaneously under the same conditions. However, it should be noted that the iPhone 5 I tested had been in use for half a year, with some battery wear, whereas the 5c was brand new. From this perspective, the battery life of the iPhone 5c shows no significant difference compared to the iPhone 5.

**Q: Is the official iPhone 5c case worth buying?**

With the launch of the new iPhone, another notable change involves official accessories, specifically the phone cases. Previously, during the iPhone 5 and iPhone 4S eras, the official cases were border-encircling rings. Now, both the 5s and 5c come with true back covers.

The iPhone 5c case is made of silicone, offering a pleasant feel. If I had to choose between the 5c's plastic shell and the official silicone cover, the latter would win hands down. However, if you ask me whether you should buy this cover, I would advise opting for third-party options for three reasons:

1. **Subjective Factor**: Aesthetically, the design of the official case leaves much to be desired. The dense holes on the back serve no apparent purpose. Perhaps without these holes, the back cover wouldn't look too different from the current 5c rear panel. The designers may have added them to differentiate, but this simple solution appears flawed to me.

2. **Objective Factor**: After having the iPhone 5c for less than a week, I encountered two issues with the case. First, it deformed at the most vulnerable point below the Home button due to its thin design. If you absentmindedly touch this area frequently, your case may develop similar problems. Second, the matte silicone surface stains easily from items like pens, colored paper, or even new jeans. My case has two noticeable red marks from a pen accidentally falling on it.

3. **Price Factor**: The official silicone case costs a hefty 228 yuan. Anyone who has purchased iPhone cases knows that for 228 yuan, you could buy 4-5 cases that offer comparable quality and various designs. Why pay extra?

**Q: Is the iPhone 5c worth purchasing?**

Finally, regarding purchase intent, I personally don’t think the iPhone 5c is terrible. Given the limited innovation between the iPhone 5 and the 4S, many users continued using their 4S for two years. These individuals form the main group upgrading to the 5c. Compared to the 5s, the 5c offers a nearly 1,000-yuan price difference, and choosing overseas channels to buy Hong Kong or U.S. versions further reduces costs. At around 4,000 yuan, Apple and the iPhone still hold considerable appeal.

The 5c's biggest advantage lies in its colorful design. As the iPhone user base becomes increasingly younger, the vibrant 5c attracts a large group of potential users. Additionally, for users with limited budgets, the 5c is a good option. Based on current pricing trends, the 5c has the potential to enter the 3,000-yuan range. If it reaches this price point, domestic smartphones priced around 2,500 yuan will face significant competition.

I believe the 5c is suitable for those who appreciate youthful products and have a favorable impression of Apple, as well as those who wish to own a new iPhone despite financial constraints.
