Seoul Semiconductor releases Acrich2 linear module

by ledlamp8 on 2012-03-03 23:44:15

February 13, 2012, Seoul, South Korea - Seoul Semiconductor, the world-famous LED professional enterprise (legal representative: Lee Jung-hoon, energy saving lamp, www.acriche.cn) today announced the launch of the exchange LED fluorescent Acrich2 linear module. The life of the module is twice that of the DC LED products and reduces actual power consumption by half. Compared with the existing DC linear modules, the main difference of the Acrich2 linear module is eliminating the need for AC/DC conversion and associated design optimization. Therefore, the converter of the product no longer contains the electrolytic capacitor that shortens the time of LED lighting, thus extending the life of the product to twice that of the DC linear module. The life of the DC linear module is usually around 10,000 to 20,000 hours. Lighting companies favor replacing ballasts in fluorescent lights with LED-compatible systems. This kind of compatible system eliminates the need for necessary fluorescent ballasts and introduces a separate LED converter to complete the replacement of LED lights. However, this approach poses fire hazards and will also reduce energy efficiency. Clearly, the life of the converter cannot be compared with that of the LED light, and the system's entire life does not exceed 10,000 to 20,000 hours. The Acrich2 linear module uses state-of-the-art technology, while eliminating the need for ballasts and converters, ensuring the security of the LED light source. The power factor increases to 97% and achieves up to 90% power efficiency. In addition, the Acrich2 linear module helps users avoid problems arising during the replacement of fluorescent lamps, using the module’s LED lights which can realize exactly twice the service life equivalent to the existing modules. Let us work together to plant two trees! The module can bring an effect similar to planting two trees. Mr. Li Jianming said: "It can effectively reduce carbon dioxide emissions, allowing customers to use more environmentally friendly lighting systems while reducing electricity costs." Seoul Semiconductor will provide samples to customers later this month.

**Glossary**

- **Acrich2**: Second-generation solution to replace semiconductor light sources; it can be directly used with home and office AC power, successfully developed by Seoul Semiconductor as the first worldwide.

- **Circuit Power Factor**: When the device is working, the ratio of real power to apparent power.

- **Electrolytic Capacitors**: A capacitor with electrodes known for ionic conductivity in liquid electrolytes, achieving greater capacitance per unit volume than other types of capacitors.
