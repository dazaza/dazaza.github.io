Some doctors have no moral bottom line

NPC deputy Zhong Nanshan criticizes the chaos in the medical community

Some doctors have no moral bottom line

Shenzhen Evening News reported that since the SARS in 2003, people have remembered the name of Zhong Nanshan. From being a member of the committee for three consecutive terms to the 11th NPC deputy, his style of being straightforward and "high-profile political questioning" has won him the titles of "cannon king" and "conscience of the medical community", and has made him a focus of media attention at the two sessions for many years.

Zhong Nanshan said that promoting China's medical reform and eliminating the chaos existing in the current medical community requires the active participation of medical elites represented by Grade A tertiary hospitals. He also believed that medical humanities should be included in the medical reform.

Throwing live babies as dead babies, giving people abortion drugs instead of pregnancy preservation drugs... Zhong Nanshan narrated the various chaos he encountered in his work, "This shows that some medical personnel have lost their basic medical moral bottom line." Zhong Nanshan said that if medical personnel can practice medicine with conscience, many doctor-patient conflicts can be resolved.

When talking about the monitoring of PM2.5, Zhong Nanshan said that comprehensive monitoring across the country should begin now. Just monitoring is not enough, specific measures must be taken to solve the problem of air pollution. "If the environment is not good, even if you have a good lifestyle, it is useless, those diseases will still occur."

For many years, Zhong Nanshan has been known for telling the truth, and is called the "artillery hand" of the two sessions. When asked "Why dare to tell the truth and why different from other deputies and members", Zhong Nanshan smiled and countered: "What does it mean to tell the truth? Is telling lies advocated? We should tell the truth."

Spring Festival holiday

Should be extended to the Lantern Festival

CPPCC member Gu Yeli

Spring Festival holiday

Should be extended to the Lantern Festival

Shenzhen Evening News reported that "Our citizens have too few holidays." Gu Yeli, a national CPPCC member and vice president of Guangdong University of Foreign Studies, told the New Express in an interview that he planned to submit a proposal suggesting restoring the May Day long holiday and extending the Spring Festival long holiday to the Lantern Festival, "Holidays can make ordinary people feel real happiness. Isn't it happy to sleep until you naturally wake up?"

Gu Yeli said that when the May Day long holiday was canceled, it was considered that it caused huge pressure on transportation and tourist attractions at that time, but now citizens have become more rational about holidays, unlike before when everyone rushed out, and they can arrange their holiday travel plans well. And now the management of tourist attractions is more mature, and there is no problem of overcrowded attractions.

Gu Yeli finally pointed out that increasing holidays can also improve work efficiency. Gu Yeli believed that being immersed in work every day does not necessarily lead to high efficiency, "To be honest, a week's work can actually be done in three days."

The crime of illegal absorption of public deposits should be abolished

NPC deputy Guo Guangchang suggested

The crime of illegal absorption of public deposits should be abolished

Shenzhen Evening News reported that Guo Guangchang, a national NPC deputy and chairman of Fosun Group, said that private enterprises played an important role in increasing the income level of middle- and low-income groups, especially in expanding the middle-income group. Guo Guangchang said that in order to promote the development of private economy, it is suggested to relax the field of民间capital entering into finance and abolish the crime of illegal absorption of public deposits; cultivate more private banks like Minsheng Bank to reduce the financing burden of small and medium-sized enterprises; expand the loan scale of small loan companies and support the trustworthy small loan companies to take the road of bankization; liberalize direct financing and actively encourage the development of equity investment enterprises.

Guo Guangchang also suggested reducing enterprise taxes and fees, such as abolishing the cultural undertakings construction fee; reducing personal tax burdens, studying individual income tax collection methods based on families, stimulating consumption, and making economic development more vibrant.

Legislation needed for online shopping

It is understood that so far, more than 30 countries and regions in the world have formulated comprehensive e-commerce laws. Our country should accelerate the legislative progress of online shopping, make online shopping enter the legal track, make the supervision of online shopping and consumer rights protection have basis, and fundamentally ensure the long-term development of the online shopping industry.

An adaptive regulatory method to the characteristics of Internet operation should be established. Promote the establishment of a credit mechanism through legal means, linking the credibility of enterprises with the qualification certificates and policy preferences of enterprises, focusing on supervising and checking behaviors such as false promotions on group-buying websites, intellectual property protection, sale of banned goods, product quality, price fraud, etc.

National People's Congress representative, General Manager of China Telecom Hunan Company Liao Renbin

Tax increase faster than Liu Xiang

Tax cut slower than snail

When we increase taxes, the speed is like Liu Xiang; when we cut taxes, the speed is like a snail. For many years, China's tax revenue has grown at a speed much higher than GDP growth. Last year, the tax revenue increased by 24.8% compared to the previous year, while the GDP growth was only around 9.2%. This has far exceeded the normal range.

So far, local governments or departments that publish details of government budgets are still very few. Ordinary taxpayers have no way of knowing how tax money is allocated and used, and tax supervision is even more impossible to talk about. At the same time, what people often see is that the annual average expenditure on official vehicles nationwide is hundreds of billions of yuan, official feasting, going abroad, training, private villas for officials, violation construction of halls, face-saving projects, etc., which are repeatedly prohibited. This greatly damages people's understanding and support for public budgets and tax systems.

Zhang Guojun, a national CPPCC member and audit partner of PricewaterhouseCoopers Zhongtian Certified Public Accountants Limited Liability Company
