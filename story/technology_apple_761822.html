Sharp Reduces Dependence on Apple by Increasing Samsung Panel Supply

by geekzhang on 2013-05-11 14:42:08

Tokyo, May 11th (Reuters) - According to informed sources, Japanese liquid crystal panel manufacturer Sharp plans to increase its supply of small-sized panels to Samsung Electronics while continuing to provide panels to Apple Inc., a competitor of Samsung, in order to boost factory shipments and maintain the company's vitality.Sources said that when Sharp announces its latest earnings next week, it will also announce new performance targets: raising its operating profit forecast for the fiscal year ending March 2016 to $1.5 billion, with expected revenue at $30 billion.This pioneer in Japan's television industry avoided bankruptcy last year with a $4 billion bailout from Mizuho Financial Group and Mitsubishi UFJ Financial Group. Sources added that Sharp will borrow another 150 billion yen ($1.47 billion) to help repay convertible bonds worth 200 billion yen due in September.These banks will send special envoys to serve as senior managers at Sharp, including a financial executive, and will reduce the number of Sharp’s board directors by 12.In October last year, Sharp mortgaged its office buildings and factories in Japan, including a plant that exclusively supplies screens for Apple's iPads and iPhones. The company also agreed to cut 10,000 jobs and find buyers for overseas assets such as TV assembly plants in China, Malaysia, and Mexico.Sharp is increasing cooperation with Samsung Electronics at a time when Apple's growth has slowed and screen orders have decreased. Analysts expect Apple's average profit growth over the next decade to be less than 5%, far below the average of 60% over the past five years.Sharp was forced to cut production of its 9.7-inch iPad display panels earlier this year. Informed sources said that Sharp has also begun limiting the supply of panels for Apple's next-generation iPhone, which are set to begin mass production in June.In March, Samsung announced it would invest $103 million in Sharp to acquire a 3% stake in the company, ensuring an adequate supply of small-sized LCD panels. However, Sharp rejected Samsung's offer to acquire its printer and copier business.In December last year, Sharp raised $120 million by selling equity to U.S. mobile chipmaker Qualcomm. These two companies agreed to collaborate on developing new displays using Sharp's IGZO panel technology.Informed sources said that Sharp is also trying to expand home appliance sales in Southeast Asia to boost profits over the next three years.Prior reports revealed that Sharp may post a net loss of 500 billion yen for the fiscal year ending March 31, higher than the previously estimated 450 billion yen in November last year. Sources added that the company's operating profit in the second half of the fiscal year was 20 billion yen, better than its projected 13.8 billion yen.Since the start of the year, Sharp's stock price has risen by 49%, compared to a 41% rise in the Nikkei 225 Index during the same period. The stock closed at 450 yen on Friday, up 6.4% from the previous trading day. (Ding Hong)
