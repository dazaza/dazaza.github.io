**How Wedding Gown Preservation Works**

It’s no mystery that brides put a lot of effort and spend countless hours preparing for their special day. With all the focus on perfecting every little detail for the big day, most brides forget to consider wedding gown preservation. Once you take your wedding dress off, you need to think about where to store it. Wedding gown preservation ensures that your gown is free from stains, dirt, and other particles that could discolor or damage your gown over time. Proper wedding gown preservation also ensures that the gown is stored in an environment free from temperature changes, insects, mildew, and mold. Here are some common methods of wedding gown preservation:

1. **Acid-Free Box**

Using an acid-free box is a very popular method of gown preservation. To do this yourself, you will need to purchase an acid-free box, along with acid-free tissue paper. Some boxes have a small window where you can see the gown. Try purchasing these supplies from a dry cleaner. The first thing you need to do is remove all the fabric-covered buttons and pins found on your gown, then store these in a separate container. Later, properly fold your wedding dress and place sheets of acid-free tissue paper between the folds. Place your gown in the box, and it’s ready for storage. Since the box is not sealed shut, the fabric remains able to breathe. To prevent your wedding gown from getting permanent creases, make sure to refold it every few years.

2. **Cedar Wood Chest**

Many brides also choose to store their wedding dress in a cedar wood chest or closet. A chest or closet is a great place to keep your gown away from light while allowing the garment to breathe. Cedar wood is known to repel insects, which is another reason it is a favorite method of wedding gown preservation. Keep in mind, however, that your gown will likely smell like cedar wood after being stored for a long period of time.

3. **Garment Bag**

Another method of wedding gown preservation is the use of a garment bag. While your wedding gown may have already come in a garment bag, this is probably not the kind of bag you should be using. You will need an acid-free and breathable garment bag, which is best for long-term storage. Using a garment bag for your wedding gown is an excellent way to protect it from permanent creasing and mildew development. It also keeps your wedding gown free from dust and light.

These are just a few of the wedding gown preservation methods that brides use. No matter which method you end up choosing, make sure it is stored in a climate-controlled environment. Also, make sure to check your wedding gown every few years to ensure it is still in good condition. Oxidation spots often occur during the first year of storage. The sooner you spot these stains, the sooner you can have them treated.
