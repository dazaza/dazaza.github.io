An 80s-born Hunanese created an impromptu body painting by the riverside, and was questioned by the onlookers as "hype"

by mlwang on 2011-09-28 13:19:16

"The blue background represents the ocean, the white skin represents the purity of the mother of Xiang River, the black hair symbolizes Chinese people and Hunan culture, and the black waves represent the polluted river water." At the Zhuzhou welcome ceremony of "Protecting Mother River, Miao Sheng Xiangjiang", Tang Xiangfei, a post-80s avant-garde artist in Hunan, created an impromptu body painting work "The Crying Mother River - Xiang River" on the spot, which attracted many people to stop and reflect.

Tang Xiangfei painted carefully stroke by stroke. First, a layer of blue background color was laid, then a black hair, a fair face, black tears, and the turbid black river water rolling away, all finished in one go. Tang Xiangfei told reporters that the public welfare environmental protection activity of Xiangjiang drifting touched many environmental nerves of people in Hunan and inspired his creative inspiration. He hoped that this work could arouse more people to take action to protect the mother river of Xiangjiang.

"My heart was touched, everyone should have the responsibility to protect Xiang River," Ms. Li, a citizen of Zhuzhou, watched the work with her child, "I will teach my child not to litter, not to waste water, to love the environment, and to love our mother river."

"Do you want to attract attention with this?" In response to the questioning voice of "hype", Tang Xiangfei said, "This is an expression of love. I hope everyone will not focus on the art works and behavior itself, but pay more attention to the protection of Xiang River and the mother river."
