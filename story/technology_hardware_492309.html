What is happiness?

by ydjfeifeib on 2011-09-20 22:32:19

In spring, we pulled a bunch of shepherds purse from the wild field. I rolled out the dough and he wrapped the fillings. He always laughed at my ugly dough, but he could make them change into small dumplings with grace and life. Eating these hand made dumplings, a warm sense of family quietly flowed through the years.

What is happiness? Money is essential, but happiness does not correlate with money.

Five-year itch, seven-year pain, maybe there are also experiences that have already drifted away in the bends of life. Now when I look back, it's like ripples on the water. Personality needs to be adjusted, and love isn't always fresh. In order to save money, we also lived frugally; because of his laziness, I also complained... Because of some trivial matters, I once packed up the burden and decided to leave. When I picked up the burden, he just held my hand, neither too loose nor too tight, and a warmth flowed in my heart. In fact, I didn't really want to leave.

Before marriage, we had no house, wandering through the streets and alleys. The lights floating out of ten thousand windows made me inexplicably excited. We were both salaried workers. Occasionally, we ate in a restaurant, two dishes and a soup bowl. Our soup was warm and our vegetables were moist, and we thanked for the gifts of life. A taciturn look, a cup of nameless strong wine, and we were enlightened and drunk in the most beautiful scenery. When we got married, we bought a second-hand house of less than 50 square meters, located on the top floor. We cried with joy. Luxury is not necessarily a scene, and simplicity can also be filled with warmth.

In summer, the small room was airtight and scorching like a steamer. No matter how late he came back from social gatherings, he would not forget to bring back a cold noodle. If he wasn't at home, I wouldn't turn on the air conditioner. When he came back, I always cooled down the room first. When my parents came, he held the spoon and chopping board. He always laughed at my bad cooking. Drinking white wine with my father-in-law, my mother and I occasionally couldn't resist the temptation of the wine aroma. My father-in-law was still alive at that time. The old man couldn't drink alcohol. Watching everyone get drunk and talk happily, his face was always full of smiles.

Now, we have a spacious new house, but we have passed through the youthful years with ups and downs, and there are no longer big waves in our hearts. Looking back, that small house has scattered the most warmth and love. The days of living in a small space have become the most warm years in our hearts...
