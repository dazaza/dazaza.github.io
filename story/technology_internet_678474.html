Sometimes when you see things with indifference, your heart won't be so tired.

by loveq7s0 on 2012-02-27 14:42:14

1. At that time, I was very tired. I was used to pretending to be strong and facing everything alone. I didn't know what I really wanted. Sometimes, I could happily talk to everyone, even being reckless, but no one knew that it was just an act, a deliberate disguise. I could make myself very happy, but I couldn't find the source of happiness, just laughing foolishly.

2. Sometimes, for no reason, I feel down, don't want to talk to anyone, just want to be alone and daydream. Sometimes, in the quiet night, I suddenly realize that it's not insomnia, but stubbornness that keeps me awake. Sometimes, hearing a song, I would suddenly think of someone. Sometimes, others suddenly tell me, "I think you've changed," then I start feeling all sorts of emotions—my lost self can only be slowly picked up again.

3. No one has the patience to listen to your whole story because everyone has their own things to say; no one likes to hear you complain about life because everyone has their own pain; most people are lonely, few in this world are willing to listen or accustomed to silence. —I will never talk about my past with others anymore, those lonely struggles in nightmares, those desolations, are better left to time to slowly fade away.

4. If love could be explained, vows could be revised. If our meeting could be rearranged, life would be easier. If one day, I finally forget you. However, this is not a casual tale, nor a drama to be played tomorrow. I cannot find the original manuscript, and thus cannot erase you completely.

5. Someone told me that fish have only 7 seconds of memory. After 7 seconds, they forget the past, everything becomes new. So, the fish in the small tank will never get bored. I wish I were a fish, forgetting everything after 7 seconds, the people I've met, the things I've done, all could vanish like smoke. But I am not a fish, I cannot forget the person I love, I cannot forget the bitterness of longing, I cannot forget the pain of yearning.

6. A person always has to walk unfamiliar roads, see unfamiliar scenery, listen to unfamiliar songs. In the end, you'll discover that the things you tried so hard to forget are truly forgotten.

7. What we call practicing smiling is not mechanically moving your facial expressions, but striving to change your mindset, adjusting your mood. Learn to calmly accept reality, learn to tell yourself to go with the flow, learn to face misfortune with equanimity, learn to look at life positively, and learn to think positively about everything. This way, sunlight will shine into your heart, driving away fear, darkness, and all.

8. Psychologists recommend things that can make you happy: take a few photos every day; watch happy movies; daydream on weekend mornings; send cards to friends; walk by the water; occasionally indulge in a big meal; exercise once a week; sing loudly while driving; read novels while drinking coffee; doodle while talking on the phone; sing in the shower.

9. Whether you see me or not, I am there, neither sad nor happy; whether you miss me or not, the feelings are there, not coming or going; whether you love me or not, love is there, not increasing or decreasing; whether you follow me or not, my hand is in yours, unwilling to let go. —Come to my arms, or let me live in your heart, silently loving each other, quietly rejoicing.

10. Once, in a moment, we thought we had grown up. One day, we finally realized that growing up means not only desires but also courage and strength, as well as certain sacrifices. —In front of life, we are still children, we have never grown up, we do not yet understand love and being loved.

11. There should be at least one time in life when, for someone, you forget yourself, not seeking results, not seeking companionship, not seeking possession, not even seeking your love, just hoping to meet you in my best years.

12. Some wounds, over time, will slowly heal; some grievances, endured and understood, will eventually dissipate; some pains, endured long enough, become habits… However, in many lonely moments, they resurface. —Actually, some words hidden deep in the heart are not deliberately concealed, it's just that not all pain can be shouted out.

13. The best revenge isn't hatred, but indifference from the bottom of your heart. Why waste energy hating someone who doesn't matter?

14. When encountering something—if you like it, enjoy it; if you don't, avoid it; if you can't avoid it, change it; if you can't change it, accept it; if you can't accept it, deal with it; if it's hard to handle, let it go. —Actually, the hardest thing for people is [letting go]. Let go, and you'll feel relieved.

15. You can sing alone, drink coffee alone, doodle alone, travel alone, wander alone on the street, stroll in the rain alone, listen to music alone, talk to yourself alone, daydream alone, dance alone, watch TV alone, flip through books alone... Only love is something you can't do alone.

16. Simplicity brings happiness, sophistication makes one old.

17. No matter how deep the feelings, how strong the friendship, distance can make it gradually distant—not because hearts have changed, nor because they no longer consider each other friends, but because far apart, joys and sorrows cannot be shared. —Originally, we are already too far to do more than greet each other, but at least we haven't forgotten each other.

18. Life is full of fleeting moments, like farewells at the station. Just now we embraced, and now we're at opposite ends of the world. Often, you don't understand, I don't understand, and thus, speaking turns into change, listening turns into weariness, watching turns into boredom, following turns into slowness, walking turns into separation, loving turns into indifference, thinking turns into giving up.

19. If we were all children, we could stay in the same place in time, sitting together listening to stories that never grow old while we slowly age.

20. The space formed by singing preserves youth against the passage of time, protecting appearances unchanged and guarding a boundless, unending hate.

21. Some people will remain etched in memory, even if their voices, smiles, faces are forgotten, whenever they come to mind, the feeling is forever unchanged.

22. I don’t ponder whether I can succeed; since I’ve chosen the distance, I’ll press on despite wind and rain. I don’t contemplate whether cold winds will strike from behind; since the horizon is my goal, all I leave the world is my back.

23. Many people say loving until you stop is deeper than love, but I don't agree. Can real love be so easily abandoned? Is this just an excuse for oneself?

24. People we often want to forget are usually unforgettable; those we want to remember are often forgotten; those we want to resist are often accepted; those we want to get used to are often painful. Life is so unpredictable, when you think you'll never forget and need help from Mencius' soup to forget, actually, you've started forgetting, and soon you won't remember the memories you didn't want to forget... When you want to remember happy memories, they often slip away unnoticed in the mundane life.

25. People who once loved you must have had a karmic connection with you in the past life, don't abuse them verbally. If he betrays you in this life, it’s because you betrayed him in the past life. Karma cycles endlessly, no need to calculate.

26. Tears exist to prove sadness isn't an illusion. Ultimately, one must personally get hurt to learn wisdom and joy, merely finding excuses to cry while smiling; happiness is just looking for reasons to keep joy alive.

27. Some people will remain etched in memory, even if their voices, smiles, faces are forgotten, whenever they come to mind, the feeling is forever unchanged.

28. Life is like a high-speed train, first love is like the beautiful scenery along the road. We can sit on the train and quietly appreciate it, but we cannot get off and linger. After all, the final destination is our ultimate goal.
