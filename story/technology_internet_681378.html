The Rights and Obligations of a Third Party as a Guarantor

A third party acts as a guarantor by using their own assets and name to guarantee for another. Whether they are willing to provide this guarantee is fundamental to the establishment of the guarantee in property mortgage loans [http://www.zhetong.cc/]. It is important to note that providing such a guarantee is a voluntary act on the part of the third party, not a legal obligation. Therefore, it is not permissible to force a third party to act as a guarantor; otherwise, the guarantee will be invalid.

If a third party has voluntarily agreed to act as a guarantor, and if the borrower fails to repay the loan upon maturity, the guarantor will bear the responsibility of repaying the loan within the scope of the guarantee. Repayment on behalf of the borrower is the main obligation of the guarantor. Additionally, properly preserving the mortgaged property and maintaining its value are also obligations of the guarantor (mortgagor).

Although the guarantor primarily assumes obligations, they do have certain rights as well. For instance, under certain conditions, they legally possess the right to defense. They also have the right to demand the return of pledged items after the guaranteed party fulfills their obligations. Moreover, after fulfilling the obligation of repaying the loan on behalf of the borrower, the guarantor has the right to seek compensation from the guaranteed party.

According to the general principle of rights and obligations, these are equal and consistent. Whoever bears the obligation gains the corresponding rights. However, in loan guarantees, a major right obtained by the third party acting as a guarantor is enjoyed by the principal debtor. That is, the loan obtained from the lender is possessed, used, and disposed of by the guaranteed party.
