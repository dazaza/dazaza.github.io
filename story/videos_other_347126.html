Football is the abbreviation for football sports or football matches. Of course, it also refers to the ball used in football matches. Here it represents the former. It is a ball game played by two teams of 11 players each, competing against each other on a rectangular grass field. Each team has a maximum of 10 players and 1 goalkeeper participating in the match. The aim of the game is to score as many goals as possible by shooting the football into the opponent's goal. Each goal scored earns one point, and at the end of the match, the team with the most points wins. If the scores are tied within regulation time, the result will be determined according to the match rules, which can include drawing lots, extra time, or penalty shootouts. In football matches, only the goalkeeper can use their hands to touch the football inside their own penalty area. Every player on the field can only control the football using parts of their body other than their hands (except for throw-ins).

Modern football originated in England and is considered one of the most widely practiced and influential sports in the world today, often referred to as the "World's No. 1 Sport." In peacetime, it is also called "War without Smoke Between Nations," and due to its rich connotations and appeal, it is even regarded as an art form [1].
