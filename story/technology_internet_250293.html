Server Upgrade Shows Advantages, Sparking a Corporate Buying Frenzy

by now on 2009-10-21 11:30:01

A few years ago, servers were considered high-end products for many enterprises due to the lack of understanding about them. Back then, many companies preferred to choose high-end PCs instead of servers, thinking that it was cheaper and more effective. However, after a few years of development, servers have been widely applied in the informatization development of enterprises. They have become an essential infrastructure for enterprise development, so the upgrade of server performance and product updates are highly concerned by people.

As a high-end product, providers need to invest heavily in operating costs, and enterprises also spend a lot when renting or hosting servers, which is a significant burden for small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs). Nowadays, the server market is also filled with mixed quality products, so many enterprises are very cautious when purchasing and hope to get their money's worth. In this situation, the more types of servers available, the higher the performance requirements enterprises have for servers, especially in CPU and memory configuration, which are particularly emphasized.

Recently, in response to this requirement from enterprises, Times Interconnect (www.todayidc.com) has made a comprehensive upgrade to the CPU of an economical business-type I server, upgrading it from the original Intel E2200 to the Intel E5200 for free, and the E5200 can be further upgraded to Dual-Core E7400. The performance of the server is continuously optimized on the original basis, but the premise is not to increase the price of the server, which remains at 6888 yuan/year. In addition to the CPU upgrade, this server from Times Interconnect also launched a "Add 1 yuan to get 1G memory" campaign, achieving higher expandable memory for the server, which is the most considered issue for SMEs when purchasing.

It is understood that due to its high cost-performance advantage, and also because buying for one year comes with ownership as a gift, this server has been the first choice for enterprise website construction in the past six months. Now, with another upgrade to the CPU, it has attracted more attention from enterprises. Especially after the economic recovery, the demand for enterprises to add server equipment is also greater, and economical business-type servers are their top priority.

When enterprises start setting up servers, the functions of the server do not lie in quantity but in practicality. If too much emphasis is placed on multifunctional servers, the cost invested will be more, and for many enterprises, some additional functions may not be used temporarily. It seems that the saying "Only buy what's right, not what's expensive" also applies to the purchase of servers. With processor technology and virtual technology becoming increasingly mature today, only through upgrades based on the original foundation can servers build more solid infrastructure for enterprises.
