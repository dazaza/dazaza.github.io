How to Build a Product Enterprise Website and Make Money

I have been working on product-based enterprise websites, and recently a good friend from the forum mentioned wanting to create such a site. So, I will share some different ideas here, especially for those friends transitioning into building enterprise sites. Some concepts must change; otherwise, it will be difficult to successfully build an enterprise website.

What is our purpose in selling products? The goal is to make money by promoting our products. First, you need to have this mindset: we aim for rankings, but rankings are not the most important thing. The main goal is still to bring profit. I will use this as the starting point to provide a thought process on how to build a good product-based enterprise website.

Site Internal Strategy - "Storefront Renovation"

We start with the website itself. For a product enterprise site, the website primarily serves as a "storefront." Many people can quickly understand that customers judge whether your company is legitimate or large-scale based on their first impression of the website. Although this is not a scientific way to differentiate, it does affect customer conversion rates. Therefore, you must choose an easy-to-remember domain name and a pleasant-sounding website name. After handling these "storefront" issues, the next step is to deal with the website text. When selecting keywords, remember to consider them based on the industry or product you're working on. What kind of words have a high conversion rate? What kind of searches lead to higher closing rates? This depends on your own industry, as each industry and product varies. For example, searching for "XX price," "XX agency," etc., leads to higher closing volumes, while searches like "how is XX" or "what is XX used for" lead to more inquiries. Not all inquiries lead to sales, but the former generally results in fewer conversions. Therefore, the primary focus of our website should be on the former type of words, while the latter type of words will be addressed in the next section on external strategies.

For website descriptions, including the homepage text, my personal opinion is to think about what customers need, what makes them comfortable, and what keeps them on the site. Don't worry too much about keyword structures or word frequency density; write from the user's perspective. I believe this produces the most natural keyword structure. If you try it, the results will be excellent.

External Strategy - "No Stone Left Unturned"

I do not advocate focusing solely on rankings when building product-based enterprise websites. If you're only focused on rankings, then perhaps pursuing a traffic-driven site would be more suitable. An enterprise site should learn the strategy of "surrounding the city from the countryside." Why? Because there are many tools outside the site that can be utilized: B2B commerce sites, free information posting platforms, blogs, forums, microblogs, etc. We should fully utilize these tools to achieve a comprehensive presence, naturally leading to no shortage of clients. Many people might feel they are already doing all these things. Yes, you may be doing them, but what are you using them for? You are using them mainly for backlinks, focusing solely on building backlinks for your main site without considering user experience. By doing so, you're only utilizing 30% of their potential. If you improve the user experience on these platforms, they can directly bring you clients, and the low-conversion long-tail keywords mentioned in the internal strategy can also be handled through these platforms. Let me explain specifically how to handle these:

B2B Commerce Sites and Information Posting Platforms: Many of these sites have shops, which you should take full advantage of. Once you open a shop, whether it's free or paid, take the time to decorate it and make it look professional. Finally, leave a link to your main site. Many people checking your posted information may enter these shops. If the shop looks professional, clients will be willing to further explore and eventually visit your main site. This not only brings relevant backlinks to your main site but also brings clients.

Blogs: Blogs may not be as effective for backlinks as before, but there are plenty of reasons for us to use them for enterprise sites. Blog marketing is something many friends have heard of—it involves promoting through blogs. We should also utilize this aspect for product-based enterprise sites. Create a blog on high-authority platforms, preferably related ones, and post authoritative articles daily about our products and strong industry-related content. This will attract many customers who want to learn more about this area, and then you can find ways to attract them to your main site. One method is through images—you can create a promotional image, leaving your phone number and website at the end of each article. Since blogs don't allow anchor texts, you can certainly post images.

Forums: We can post soft articles on relevant forums connected to our industry. This not only builds backlinks but can also directly bring client traffic. However, remember that quality over quantity is key. If there are few relevant forums, don't waste time posting on unrelated ones, as it is inefficient.

Microblogs: This is currently the most popular social platform, and microblog marketing is no longer a foreign concept. It is similar to blog marketing, so I won't elaborate much here.

Building a product-based enterprise site requires a different mindset—a marketing mindset. Our goal is to generate profits and more profits. Therefore, your thinking must be broad and not limited to just the main site's ranking. Otherwise, if one day your site gets manually interfered with by Baidu (just a hypothetical scenario), you'll realize you have nothing left.
