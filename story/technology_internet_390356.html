Weibo marketing is gaining popularity, and the Internet is once again swept by a "rush registration wind."

by edivista on 2011-03-25 15:43:45

Compared with trademark squatting, Weibo squatting has a lower cost and is easier to operate. Brands like Starbucks, Yùquán, Língyǐn, and Exploring Plum Blossoms at Língfēng Peak have all been squatted. Once squatted, it's very difficult to reclaim them unless you can persuade the squatter to return them.

I am a reporter covering the garden and cultural line. Recently, the QQ group of the internal system of the Garden and Cultural Bureau has been buzzing with discussions on how to register on Weibo. Meng Fei, the correspondent for the Botanical Garden, told me that yesterday when I dropped by his office, he smiled and said: I've already registered the username Hangzhou Botanical Garden on Sina Weibo.

While talking, Meng Fei seemed to think of something: I wonder if 'Exploring Plum Blossoms at Lingfeng' has been registered by someone? Then, Meng Fei typed in 'Exploring Plum Blossoms at Lingfeng', and the screen showed such a prompt: The nickname you registered is too popular. This is a tactful way of indicating that this username has already been registered.

Meng Fei then tried typing in 'Yùquán' and 'Língfēng'. These are the two most representative names of the botanical garden. Unfortunately, both usernames have been registered.

Wang Shan, the head of the office at the Língyǐn Management Office, also encountered a similar awkward situation. Keywords like 'Língyǐn', 'Língyǐn Zen Sect', and 'Flying Peak' have long been registered by others. Helplessly, Wang Shan had to add 2011 after 'Língyǐn Zen Sect' as the official Weibo account of the Língyǐn Management Office. Doesn't it look like a knockoff? Wang Shan bitterly laughed.

Someone even reminded everyone on QQ whether they should register all the keywords related to the thirty scenic spots of West Lake on Weibo. Meng Fei told me. Not only on Sina, but also on Sohu Weibo, words like 'Lingfeng' and 'Yuquan' have been registered by others.

Websites begin to protect Weibo keywords

The online registration for Weibo is free, and it is relatively easy to activate an account, just like setting up an email, much simpler than registering a trademark.

In addition to these well-known scenic spots in Hangzhou, well-known merchants are also prone to being squatted on Weibo. A journalist found two Weibo accounts about Starbucks on Sina Weibo. One belongs to a media person from Beijing, who registered it in May 2009 according to the first tweet. Another one called Starbucks China is the official Weibo of Starbucks Coffee, which was registered in May 2010 based on the time of the tweets. Clearly, this Beijinger opened a Weibo account a whole year earlier than Starbucks Corporation.

Legal vacuum exists for squatting Weibo usernames

However, squatting Weibo usernames is different from squatting trademarks, there are no relevant legal norms. If someone else really registers it, Sina, the manager, can only guide users to communicate and coordinate with the registered party. Mao Taotao said. In this completely free open community, the Weibo management will not take mandatory measures. If the coordination fails, the later registrant can only distinguish themselves from others by adding some symbols or additional words.

Mao Taotao mentioned the Starbucks case: When Starbucks Corporation applied for certification, it found that 'Starbucks' had already been registered. After reporting this situation to us, we guided them to communicate with the registered user. The final result was that the communication failed, but Starbucks Corporation found a solution by adding 'China' to differentiate.

Businesses are enthusiastic about Weibo marketing

By February, the number of registered users on Sina Weibo has exceeded 100 million, while Tencent Weibo, although started later, utilized the advantage of QQ to reach 130 million registered users, with an expected total of 300 million by the end of the year. Sohu Weibo also has a large number of registered users.

Recently, an online survey initiated by Wanrui Data in collaboration with NetEase, Sina, Sohu, and more than ten other media outlets showed that young users use Weibo at a high proportion, users with a bachelor's degree or above prefer it more, and Weibo users are often seasoned netizens who have a high acceptance of online marketing. These young, highly educated seasoned netizens are undoubtedly the target customer groups of many manufacturers. That is to say, using Weibo for marketing has infinite potential, and doing business through Weibo is very promising.

Some well-known companies have already gained astonishing numbers of followers shortly after opening their Weibo accounts (detailed data see the figure below left).

Xinmin Ji is a teahouse in Hangzhou, with branches in Wanhxiang City and Huanggushan Road. The boss, Mr. Ma, told reporters that they opened a Weibo account on Sina last September, and now they already have more than 1500 fans. Except for sleeping time, I am always on Weibo. Mr. Ma said that Weibo is a very useful promotional tool. On the day Zhang Xueyou held a concert, it rained heavily, so we posted a tweet saying, 'Heavy rain, come to Xinmin Ji for a warm cup of milk tea.' The response was overwhelming. That night, our late-night snack business boomed. Usually, we close at midnight, but that night we didn't close until 1 a.m. the next morning. Mr. Ma said that Weibo deserves much credit. Now, whenever the store launches a new product, they announce it on Weibo to see what the netizens think. Whenever a celebrity visits the store, they post real pictures on Weibo. Currently, the sales performance of the teahouse in Wanhxiang City indirectly verifies the effectiveness of such a Weibo marketing method - Xinmin Ji is the restaurant in Wanhxiang City that generates the highest profit per unit area.
