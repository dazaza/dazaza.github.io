Today, the new women's national football team officially made its debut with Mexico women's football team as its opponent. Theoretically speaking, the three opponents of the Chinese team in this invitation tournament are ranked from weak to strong. Head coach Hao Wei expressed that the main purpose is to understand the recent situations of the Korean and North Korean teams. It is worth noting that the press conference of the Chinese team only lasted for four minutes, which could be called the shortest one in history. Wei Di arrived here last night, which was his first appearance in a national-level match in more than half a year.

Almost 40 journalists applied to interview in Yongchuan, but by yesterday, only seven had signed in, including a local journalist from Chongqing. The coldness at the press conference site made people sigh over the current situation of the Chinese women's football team. Hao Wei seemed to have gotten used to this "small atmosphere" and went straight to the point: "Our overall goal is the Olympic preliminaries in 2015 and 2016, and the World Cup preliminaries. The average age of the team is now 22.84 years old, relatively young. We don't know much about the Mexican team. North Korea and South Korea each have their own team-building goals, different from last year. There are new personnel and new combinations. We want to use this game to understand them."

Subsequently, during the field adaptation training, the head coach of the North Korean team watched for a long time. After knowing this, Hao Wei smiled and said: "This doesn't matter, I also watch their training." Some people worried that the tactics of the Chinese women's football team would be exposed in advance. Hao Wei thought it was no big deal: "There is no confidentiality on tactics, maybe just the set-piece kicks won't be shown."

Hao Wei is a coach who pays attention to details. He did not bring Xu Yuan, the oldest player in the team, to the press conference, but chose Han Peng, a newcomer to the national team from Tianjin Women's Football Team. This sent a clear message to the outside world: the future national team will be thoroughly rejuvenated. Han Peng stated: "There are many young players in this national team. The new women's football team needs to showcase the spirit of the new women's football team, bringing different games to the audience. I hope more audiences can support women's football." Slightly regrettable is that Han Peng got injured in the previous stage of training and will not participate in today's China-Mexico match.

A formal press conference lasting 3 minutes and 41 seconds; a supplementary interview after training lasting 1 minute and 3 seconds, adding up to less than 5 minutes. Tickets for the invitation tournament cost 20 yuan/30 yuan/40 yuan, allowing you to watch two consecutive matches. A ticketing point in the downtown area did not see a single ticket buyer in two hours yesterday morning. This is the real "popularity" of the Chinese women's football team at present.

After the training ended, the three goalkeepers played penalty shootouts under the guidance of coach Li Wei. The three "beautiful girl goalkeepers" showed quite remarkable footwork skills, especially Zhang Yue, who scored all three penalties, leaving Li Wei without any temper. Not only did it make people applaud, but it also raised a question: Should Zhang Yue be arranged to take penalty kicks in official matches? Hao Wei clarified: "No, we have already arranged the players who will take penalty kicks in official matches." The reason for such thoughts stems from the fact that the personal technical skills of the women's football team members are not optimistic.

Yesterday's training was one of the more relaxed sessions in recent times. It only included basic offensive patterns such as midfield passing, crossing from the wings, and surrounding shots, and was conducted without any opposition. The central midfielder passed to the wings at a distance of about 20 meters, and generally, the wing players had too large ball control, necessitating a second adjustment. In formal matches, teams like North Korea and South Korea, which emphasize pressing, will not give their opponents time for calm adjustments. The Chinese women's football team has long faced a fatal problem of not scoring goals, seemingly due to lack of attacking power, but in reality, the first link of the offense runs smoothly, self-sabotaging its own attack.

The wing attackers return passes to the central midfielder, who then sends through balls to the returning wing players. This behind-the-back tactic requires appropriate weight in the pass; if it's too heavy, it's equivalent to giving it to the opposing goalkeeper, and if it's too light, it's for the defenders. According to incomplete statistics, the success rate of this offensive link is only around 50%. Considering that this is training and conducted without any opposition, it is still unable to achieve greater precision. In formal matches, it may still be the old problem of the women's football team - thinking well but not being able to execute.

At yesterday's pre-match press conference, Mexican women's football team head coach Leonardo expressed: "I am very clear that the Chinese team needs the victory in this match. I also know that the Chinese women's football team is undergoing changes, and I hope to see them in the game. I can see that the Chinese women's football team is striving to return to the ranks of the world's top teams, and I hope the Chinese women's football team achieves success." A considerable part of this women's national football team comes from the Youth National Team coached by General Coach Yin Tie Sheng. At this age, technical actions have basically been fixed, and there is not much room for improvement in basic skills. The roughness of footwork skills might be the biggest stumbling block preventing this team from advancing to higher goals.
