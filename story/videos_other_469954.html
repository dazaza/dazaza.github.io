Strive to create a warm and harmonious family.

by tuoqing1188 on 2011-08-03 12:52:23

Are we a puppet?

Have we been puppets for a long time?

Do we truly know ourselves?

Do we know the basis of our frequent empty talk?

Do we know our own ideological pillars?

If we have deeply pondered over the above questions and found answers, then we would surely possess warm and harmonious families, living happily ever after. But we don't know, so keep reading, perhaps the current us can gain something. In our lifetime, we will create many thoughts and absorb a lot of inherited ideas. However, many times we are completely dominated by thoughts, being entirely ideologized without knowing how to analyze them. Our topic is about warm and harmonious families, living happily ever after; everyone hopes for this. Hope, after all, is just hope, it doesn't equal reality.

Chapter One: Children

Let's first talk about "spare the rod and spoil the child." This idea is recognized by many people, and some have deeply analyzed it and changed their lives accordingly. Its origin dates back to its era, as early as in the slave society when people's understanding was insufficient, believing in the slave system, oppression, and exploitation. Under such conditions, this concept was born. Its essence is obvious: oppression and exploitation only produce fear and hatred. It makes third parties submit before cruel facts, making them obey or act according to its will. Such obedience is not lasting. Even a dog would remember these things and, when the opportunity comes, the dog's awakening would change the original submission. In human society, we might think that children are our most precious assets, we might hope that children become outstanding, we might hope that children are healthy and happy. But hope, after all, is just hope; it doesn't equal reality. In fact, children often become appendages, commodities, burdens. These factors are all caused by our own thoughts, and this is also the positioning that most thoughts inherit for children. Once a commodity fails to show residual value, what guides our behavior must be the inheritance of thought, and "spare the rod and spoil the child" falls into this category. Logically, using it is reasonable, and commodities will also show residual value. However, children are not commodities or dogs; they have bodies and souls like ours. They exist to continue our great footsteps. Seeing this, our actions originally only address the symptoms but not the root cause, and the residual value of commodities will still gradually fade.

Now, we should come to understand the essence of humans. In our understanding, humans are miracles. Traditional ideas hold that humans are mostly the products of a creator. In today's scientific context, these traditional concepts may seem vague. Precisely because of this, we remain confused. In fact, we aim too high without considering fundamentals. The pursuit of life creates thoughts, and under the influence of thoughts, there arises further life pursuits. As life's desires change, thoughts expand accordingly. This cycle continues, and humanity progresses. We recognize the body as the fundamental element; its vision, hearing, smell, taste, touch, and various sensations make us move. Life repels pain and seeks happiness. Under such conditions, we will move due to the pain and happiness triggered by events. A child is also a human, seeking happiness and rejecting pain. "The path to learning is through diligence, and the sea of knowledge has no end but hard work as the boat," written by someone in modern times, "Without enduring hardship, one cannot become an extraordinary person," an ancient couplet. We often use examples like these to encourage children to strive forward. However, here lies the contradiction: the body repels pain, yet we ask children to accept pain. Even for us now, if we were asked to study quantum theory, partial differential equations, and their applications, we would probably feel tired just looking at them, let alone studying them, because we don't know why we need to learn them, especially for children. In the choice between happiness and pain, the body will inevitably choose happiness, and so do children.

Grasping the characteristics of things, hope emerges, turning hope into reality, and no longer remaining confused. So, let's make learning enjoyable. The monotony and boredom of studying are well-known. This is like cooking; if you don't know the feeling of eating the dish, then cooking will definitely be monotonous and boring. What we should do is add seasoning to the monotony and boredom of pain, such as giving appropriate rewards or setting appropriate goals, transforming the dull pain into a wonderful sense of happiness. Children will pursue it rather than reject it. There's no need to force them under our "spare the rod and spoil the child" or "the path to learning is through diligence, and the sea of knowledge has no end but hard work as the boat." There's no need to encourage them with incomprehensible phrases like "people aim high, water flows low." Guiding children from the aspect of happiness during their growth process will become our true hope.
