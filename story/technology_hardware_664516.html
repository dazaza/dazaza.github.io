Life, in an ordinary world

by mobuyybu on 2012-02-13 15:50:21

Life, in an ordinary world Amazed by the author's skill, he really uses a one-million-word book to place an ordinary world right in front of the readers. It's so real; the thoughts and actions of the characters in the book are vividly portrayed.

It's hard to describe what kind of world this is, whether it's ordinary or great. However, this book indeed gives people the motivation to strive upward. From material scarcity and spiritual poverty, to material abundance and spiritual civilization. The poorest family in a poor mountain village gives us a good explanation.

The inspirations it gives me:

Attitude towards life, strive upward. Generation after generation of poverty, for the sake of younger siblings' education, one must give up their own schooling. At a young age, they have to support the family. All these things are placed on the shoulders of teenagers. In this ordinary world, there aren't so many fairy tales, just many ordinary people doing ordinary yet great things.

Love, cannot be forced. Love, sincere dedication does not necessarily bring returns, mutual affection does not necessarily lead to being together. However, some of the relationships in the book are truly enviable. Pure and clean, thousands of miles apart, yet sharing common life interests, communicating through letters, expressing love through correspondence. Even so, it may not end perfectly because this is an ordinary world.

Work, love what you do. Work, make the greatest contribution in your position, show personal value. Clean and decent jobs naturally attract people, but dirty and tiring jobs also need to be done. Regardless of the position, it's about the attitude towards work.

Learning, tirelessly. Only through tireless learning can one continuously improve and progress, enrich oneself, and reach new heights.

Dealing with people and matters, be upright and virtuous. The essence of survival lies in finding greatness within the ordinary.
