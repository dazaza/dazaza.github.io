I am strong in my vulnerability.

by lili1 on 2012-02-19 22:49:05

I am strong in my vulnerability.

Before work ends, Xiaobai secretly told me that she had changed her personal signature. When I sat back at my desk and saw these words right before my eyes, tears started welling up, but they didn't fall. The liquid in my eye sockets contained all of my emotions: hardship, moved, grievance, yearning... Maybe it's because I've been too tired recently, not having truly rested since after National Day; maybe it's because I'm feeling unwell, and the busyness has caused my immunity to continuously decline, finally unable to withstand a cold; or maybe it's because the people around me have disappointed me, making me feel unsupported time and time again... My heart feels as if it's blocked with many lumps of coal, not knowing how to vent, not knowing who to talk to, not knowing how to express myself?

On the way home, the wind was blowing coolly. The road was so quiet, without any people. I listened to the sound of my shoes hitting the walls beside the roadside, then echoing. I wanted to squat by the side of the road and cry, but I just thought about it, and did not do it, nor would I do it. I even felt like I was alone, I couldn't find any support, no place to rest when I'm tired, just drifting. How can I vent the grievances in my heart? I could only say: every time I think about relying on myself, I feel grounded. I feel like I'm on the verge of collapse, not just because of work.

Finally, I couldn't help but take out my phone and sent a text message to Ultraman: You only know how to play everyday, don't you see that I'm so tired I'm about to collapse? Are you going to let me live like this forever? I knew I was venting, I knew I couldn't hold it in anymore, I knew I didn't want to compromise anymore, I knew I once again thought about leaving. Yes, I'm tired, I don't want to force myself to say I can still persist, I can't persist anymore. I don't need a puppet, I already can't feel any warmth, I know that I'm not strong enough, brave enough right now, but I don't want to be strong or brave anymore. I admit my own vulnerability, my own helplessness... releasing is not a sin, right?

In the room alone, I kept shedding tears in front of the computer screen, my shoulders felt heavy, my fingers ached, my heart felt empty. It was already ten-thirty, after venting, I still had to continue working overtime in front of the computer, becoming a word maniac. This is responsibility, thinking about what D said to me: I feel reassured giving this book to you. I know she's also worked hard, I can't let her down, I hope I can help her share some burdens, consider the company. And thus, I learned to take responsibility.

I still wanted to leave him a message, tell him my feelings, but I restrained myself, not wanting to do it. I was afraid I would cause trouble for others, and felt it wasn't necessary. So, just like this! When I'm tired, I'll cry a little by myself, wipe away the tears, and carry on as usual, everything will pass, right? In the past, every single time, it was like this, and it eventually passed, right? Persist, persist, endure, endure, grit your teeth, and tomorrow will be a sunny day, as long as tonight passes.

After tonight, I hope everything becomes clear. Maybe, it's time to make a decision... not wanting to, sorry for oneself.
