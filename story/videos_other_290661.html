Galaxy Securities: Weichai Power's main business is at the peak of prosperity, and the future lies in product line extension＿Stock Theory

by dx0957095 on 2009-12-03 18:36:03

The sales structure of engines shows a positive trend. In the third quarter, the company's engine sales volume increased slightly by 2.4% compared to the second quarter, but the revenue from sales increased by 11%, reflecting an improvement in the sales structure of engine products. The proportion of heavy truck engines and high-pressure common rail engines in heavy truck engines has significantly increased, and these products have higher unit prices and gross profit margins than other engine products.

Production and sales have reached historical peaks of prosperity while the prices of major raw materials remain low, leading to the company's gross margin levels climbing quarter by quarter.

We predict that in 2010, there is not much probability for semi-trailer tractors sales to experience significant growth or decline. First, domestic logistics and transportation demand will not see a sharp drop; secondly, from the perspective of update demand, with the current stock, calculated on an average update cycle of 5 years, the annual update volume of heavy trucks is approximately 500,000 units; thirdly, the sales volume of semi-trailer tractors in the first quarter of 2009 was at an absolute low point in recent years, so there will still be significant year-on-year growth next year in the first quarter and even the first half of the year.

Potential competitors are unlikely to pose heavy pressure on Weichai in the short term. The production and matching technology content of heavy truck engines is high, and due to scale and brand advantages, Weichai's industry status is difficult to shake. Moreover, Weichai will also establish its advantageous position in the general powertrain field by expanding to smaller displacement engines in the future.

We maintain our forecast for the company's earnings per share (EPS) for 2009-2011 at 4, 4.76, and 5.33 yuan respectively. The company's current valuation level is still relatively low, and we maintain our "cautious recommendation" investment rating for the company.
