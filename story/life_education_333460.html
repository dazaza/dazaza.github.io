Every day is "3.15", and every day consumers should dare to protect their own rights.

by alexsgxu on 2010-03-16 16:32:55

By Xu Shuguang, Chairman of Green Tree Inn and 519.com Wine Direct Sales Network

Once again, it's "March 15th." On this annual "Consumer Rights Protection Day," many media outlets and consumers will focus on cases where consumer rights have been violated. After this day passes, perhaps everyone will return to their daily lives, and the issues that harm the rights of ordinary consumers may still exist and continue to happen.

I believe that protecting consumer rights is one of the most important supervisory mechanisms for promoting the development of China's market economy. As ordinary consumers, each of us should continuously enhance our awareness of protecting our own rights. If we encounter situations in life where our rights are violated, we should dare to stand up and actively seek redress through legal, regulatory, and other formal channels. We shouldn't give up protecting our rights just because the matter is too small or the complaint process is too troublesome. Especially when it comes to food hygiene safety, environmental pollution, and other issues, even the smallest matters are big deals because they relate to the health of millions of other consumers and their safety. The incidence rates of stomach cancer and colon cancer in China are the highest in the world, and the incidence rate of lung cancer in China is also very high. This is mainly because we do not clearly recognize and protect our own rights. For example, when someone around us smokes, few people stand up to point it out and ask them not to smoke. At such moments, as ordinary consumers, being able to bravely stand up and say "no" to things that endanger everyone's health and to fraud is very important.

From the perspective of our Green Tree Inn, in order to better protect consumer rights, we have always insisted that guests staying at Green Tree Inn hotels review the hotel on our official website. The content of these reviews is all publicly available on the Green Forum. Guests' evaluations of the service, whether good or bad, and the hotel manager's response can be seen by everyone. We use this method to place the hotel's service under public scrutiny and accept everyone's supervision.

To look at it from another angle, the hotel industry does not always do well in this regard. Some intermediary websites, when recommending hotels to guests or providing so-called hotel rankings, are not open and transparent but depend on how much money the hotel pays them. This is equivalent to "buying information," using information asymmetry to mislead consumers, which greatly reduces the credibility of the internet in the hearts of consumers. Therefore, it is not only detrimental to protecting consumer rights but also hinders the development of the hotel industry, intermediary industry, and internet industry.

On this special day today, I hope that every one of us always cares about our rights as consumers and reminds ourselves to be braver and willing to stand up to protect our own rights and jointly protect everyone's rights.
