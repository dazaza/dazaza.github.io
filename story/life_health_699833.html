Qing Hou Discusses "Food Safety" with Netizens

Zhejiang Online, March 8th (Special Correspondents Tong Jun, Ye Hai, Wu Xian) – Over the past year, despite relevant departments imposing heavy penalties for food safety issues, events such as the "lean meat powder," "colored steamed buns," and "gutter oil" have continued to challenge psychological endurance. In various "surveys," food safety remains a prominent issue.

Regarding food safety problems, on the afternoon of March 8th, national representative, chairman, and general manager of Hangzhou Wahaha Group Co., Ltd., Qing Hou, visited Zhejiang Online's news center to interact and exchange views with netizens.

Heavy on Approval, Light on Management in the Food Industry

This year, Qing Hou has discussed food safety issues on multiple occasions.

He believes that food safety has already drawn significant attention from the state, and there should be considerable progress. However, in reality, the problem has not been thoroughly resolved.

Why are the "institutional barriers" for food safety frequently breached? He stated that it mainly stems from the source. Because industrial development has brought about much pollution—air is polluted, water is polluted, and land is also polluted. The heavy metal content in the soil has also increased significantly.

The country lacks standards and early warning mechanisms. In food production, many issues are either undetected or lack standards, leading them to be overlooked.

At the same time, the food industry is managed by multiple departments, but these departments often fail to take responsibility, focusing heavily on approval processes while neglecting management. Currently, segmented management is practiced; for instance, the Ministry of Agriculture oversees agricultural products, the State Food and Drug Administration manages enterprises, and the Administration for Market Regulation supervises commercial distribution channels. However, crucially, standards and testing should originate from one department; otherwise, conflicting standards will lead to inconsistent test data.

Domestic vs International Food Safety Standards

Relevant data shows that over the years, China’s food export qualification rate has consistently remained above 99.8%, whereas domestically marketed food, despite years of rectification, only reaches around a 90% qualification rate, presenting an embarrassing situation where domestic and international standards differ.

In response to this, Qing Hou said that exported food undergoes customs inspection first, followed by checks by relevant foreign departments, and then further inspections upon entering the market. If requirements are not met, entry is denied. Due to strict supervision of export goods, the qualification rate tends to be higher.

Conversely, domestically, there exist numerous small enterprises and even some small workshops focused solely on profit, resulting in regulatory issues. Although the situation has improved somewhat with the issuance of production licenses in recent years, neither enterprises nor relevant departments have fully addressed the management gaps.

Product Quality as the Lifeblood of Enterprises

A netizen asked how Wahaha, as a leader in the food industry, strictly controls food safety from its source to production and sales?

Qing Hou replied that Wahaha regards product quality as the lifeblood of the enterprise. Operating in the food industry carries great risks and requires caution, as failure can mean the end. Everyone works diligently under close scrutiny.

Over the years, Wahaha has recruited many talents and acquired advanced instruments and equipment to enhance food quality.

Even though Wahaha's material suppliers are large enterprises, they still undergo certification. After bidding, on-site inspections are conducted, and materials must pass inspections before being stored.

For example, when producing fruit-flavored beverages that require powdered milk, specialists are sent to the powdered milk supplier's factory to supervise every step from milk collection to milk output. Once materials enter the factory, they undergo further testing before reaching the workshop. Each link ensures strict quality control.

Due to Wahaha's relatively secure quality, Qing Hou humorously mentioned that he drinks their products regularly.

Qing Hou Responds to the "Latex Gate": All Nutritional Substances

Recently, a netizen conducted an experiment where they poured Wahaha's Nutrition Express into a porcelain plate. Overnight, the beverage turned into a white gel-like substance, quickly drawing attention and raising doubts among netizens.

During the event, a netizen asked Qing Hou about his thoughts on the "latex gate" involving Nutrition Express.

In response, Qing Hou said this was due to some netizens lacking knowledge.

Actually, besides solid substances, beverages primarily consist of water. Even if milk were evaporated, a similar phenomenon would occur.

Later, Wahaha submitted Nutrition Express to a national-level testing institution for examination, discovering all components to be nutritional substances, confirming no issues.

Solving Food Safety Issues Requires Nationwide Efforts

So, how can we solve or prevent similar food safety issues from recurring?

Qing Hou emphasized that companies need to exercise self-discipline, take social responsibilities seriously, and rigorously manage quality control. Quality is the lifeblood of an enterprise; encountering food safety issues will undoubtedly lead to business failure.

Simultaneously, regulation must be stringent. The state should establish specialized institutions to frequently conduct tests and analyses, providing an early warning mechanism for the food sector since unexpected new problems may arise.

At this session, Qing Hou also submitted "Several Suggestions for Improving Food Safety Supervision and Establishing Long-Term Guarantee Mechanisms." It mentions that solving food safety issues requires strengthening the construction of food source control capabilities, aligning with international standards, enhancing the formulation and revision of national food safety standards and regulations, and increasing the role of risk communication in food safety management.

He stated that from the perspective of strengthening food safety, it is necessary to fully mobilize the public in waging a nationwide battle against food safety issues.
