Let me first explain what marble renovation is. Simply put, it's about renewing the crystalline surface of marble by grinding away a damaged layer of the marble surface and then applying a crystalline liquid to form a new crystalline surface, restoring it to the shine expected of new stone flooring.

What types of stones need renovation and maintenance?

1. In indoor decoration projects, there are often many trades working together. The movement of large numbers of construction workers or decorative materials can wear down or corrode the smooth surface of the stone, causing serious damage that prevents the project from meeting delivery standards as per the contract. Sometimes, due to the lack of skill of the laying personnel, unevenness or scissors difference occurs after laying, requiring stone renovation.

2. After long-term use, even with proper maintenance, stones will lose some of their luster due to reasonable wear. If undergoing secondary comprehensive renovation, stone floors generally don’t need replacement; they only require relatively simple renovation and polishing to achieve a brand-new decorative effect.

3. Joint filling treatment: After the stone is laid, a similar-colored cloud stone adhesive is used to fill the gaps between adjacent laid stones. Then, a grinder is used for grinding and polishing. This method enhances the overall aesthetic appeal of the stone, making it appear seamless, while also reducing the chances of water stains. Many stone problems start from these adjacent gaps, but seamless treatment can reduce various issues with the stones.
