Nokia agents clear out inventory and switch to Android smartphones

by jdhgsa078 on 2011-08-16 15:44:38

On July 14, Tianyin Holding announced that due to the adjustment of inventory product structure and the tightening of national macro credit and other factors, the company was expected to have a loss of 56 million yuan to 60 million yuan in the first half of this year. Nokia's sales accounted for nearly 50% of the company's total mobile phone sales, and the sales amount accounted for more than one third of the company's total income. The main reason for the company's loss was clearing Nokia inventory, said an insider.

Loss-making agents

The former darling has now become a lump of soil, as if it happened overnight.

Tianyin Holding's 2010 annual report showed that in 2010, Tianyin Holding had an income of 20.877 billion yuan, an increase of 19.84% over the previous year, and realized a net profit of 311 million yuan, an increase of 9.62% over the previous year. Among them, Tianyin Communication, which is 70% owned by Tianyin Holding, sold 34.67 million mobile phones in 2010, with sales revenue reaching 23.079 billion yuan.

In late March last year, Tianyin Communication signed a $1 billion procurement contract with Nokia. In fact, Tianyin Communication completed 118.18% of the contract amount in 2010, reaching 7.8 billion yuan, accounting for 33.79% of the total mobile phone sales revenue of Tianyin Communication.

A well-informed person said that under the large agency buyout model, it was easy to be pressured by Nokia for stockpiling goods. Last year, Tianyin Communication and Nokia's large procurement order only realized the procurement, meaning that the mobile phones were transferred from Nokia's factory to the warehouses of agents and distributors, but were not completely sold out by retail terminals, forming a huge inventory, which also led to Tianyin Holding's rapid decline in performance in 2011.

In the first quarter of this year, Tianyin Holding had an income of 4.528 billion yuan, a decrease of 18.11% compared to the same period last year, and a loss of 51.5 million yuan, while the inventory amount reached as high as 3.372 billion yuan, an increase of 33% compared to the end of 2011. An informed person said that the change in the above figures exactly shows that Nokia China pressured agents to stockpile goods in order to maintain its performance.

But Tianyin Communication is not the only one being pressured to stockpile goods. The situation of several other large agents is almost identical to that of Tianyin Communication. A relevant person in charge of a large mobile phone chain store in South China revealed to reporters that most Nokia China agents did not make money in the first half of this year because the market quickly shifted towards the smartphone market, causing everyone to busy themselves clearing Nokia mobile phone inventory.
