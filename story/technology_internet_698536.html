Crime Scene

Rednet, Jiangyong, March 7th (Communicator Huang Hai) - On March 7th, the He Guoliang couple from Guanjia Village in Yunshan Town, Jiangyong County, Hunan Province, returned home in deep sorrow from Guangdong. Their 80-year-old father and 12-year-old son were both found dead. The He Guoliang couple and their eldest son have been working outside for years, and no one knows their exact workplace or contact information. It took four days of work by local government departments and police to get in touch with them.

The Tragedy: Two Bodies Found in a Farmer's Home

At 8:00 AM on March 3rd, He Hui, a nine-year-old left-behind child from Guanjia Village in Yunshan Town, Jiangyong County, went to his cousin He Jun’s house. The door was tightly closed. He called out but received no response. Climbing up to look through the window, he saw He Jun lying motionless on the ground wearing water rubber shoes. Not realizing the impending disaster, He Hui met his classmates Jiang Wei and He Bin on the way back home, and they all went to He Jun’s house again. Jiang Wei, slightly older, climbed up the window and was so scared that he ran quickly to tell the adults in the village...

At 9:30 AM, the 110 command center of the Jiangyong County Public Security Bureau received an alarm: two bodies were found in a farmer's home in Guanjia Village, Yunshan Town. Police officers rushed to the scene immediately. The crime scene was located at No. 107, Group Seven, Guanjia Village, Yunshan Town, in the home of He Guoliang. After examining the scene, one body was identified as He Jun, He Guoliang’s 12-year-old second son. His head had been slashed seven times, his right wrist severed, and there were two bloody knives and a pig-slaughtering knife pressed against him. The other body was identified as He Huangu, He Guoliang’s 81-year-old father. He had 800 yuan in cash in his pocket, a deep fruit knife wound on his throat, and there was a bloody fruit knife beside him. All wounds were inflicted by a left-handed person, and the time of death was estimated to be between 7:30 PM and 10:00 PM on March 2nd. The front door was bolted from the inside, the back door slightly ajar, and no bloodstains or traces of outsiders were found at the entrances and exits of the house.

That day at 1:20 PM, Deputy Brigade Commander Chen Yunsheng and Chief Forensic Pathologist Zuo Humin of the Criminal Investigation Brigade of Yongzhou City Bureau, along with Instructor Jiang Zheng of the Major Case Team of the Criminal Investigation Brigade and Trace Technician Nie Weilin of the Technical Brigade, rushed to the scene to guide the investigation.

Investigation: Grandfather Demanded Dinner from Grandson; Refusal Led to Tragedy

According to He Xiaonü, He Huangu’s 47-year-old daughter, they are a family of three siblings: her brother He Guoliang and sister He Lijuan. Her father was lazy and had a very bad temper. After they got married, they rarely came home, and their father hadn’t visited them for over 20 years. Her brother and sister-in-law worked outside, leaving their young son He Jun to live with him. Every day, it was He Jun who would cook breakfast, wash dishes, and do laundry. Meanwhile, He Huangu played cards outside. If He Jun did not comply or made mistakes, he would be beaten and scolded by his grandfather.

Nephew He Zhixiong mentioned that his uncle He Huangu often boasted about having three knives, saying that if anyone dared to make him unhappy, he would kill them and then commit suicide. Once, his daughter-in-law was late in cooking dinner, and He Huangu cursed her heavily. When she retorted, he chased her with a knife. Fortunately, she ran fast and hid in a neighbor’s house, escaping a tragedy. At the end of last year, when He Jun's older brother He Haiwen came home for the New Year, He Huangu asked him for money to play cards. When He Haiwen refused, He Huangu pulled out a knife and chased after his grandson. However, the grandson managed to snatch the knife away.

On the evening of the incident at around 5 o'clock, a neighbor saw He Jun crying while carrying a plastic bucket to fetch water by the well. From time to time, shouts from He Huangu could be heard coming from the house.

During their investigation in the village, many villagers said that He Huangu had a habit of petty theft and few people in the village interacted with him. He ate, worked, and played cards with his left hand and frequently abused his grandson He Jun. Through the identification by the villagers, the knives left at the scene were confirmed to belong to He Huangu.

After several days of investigation and on-site examination, the investigating police officers preliminarily determined: Before the incident, the victim He Jun wanted to watch TV, but He Huangu demanded that he cook dinner instead. He Jun refused, leading to an argument. During the argument, He Jun grabbed a knife in self-defense. In retaliation, He Huangu picked up a knife and slashed He Jun multiple times, killing him. Then, he used a fruit knife to stab himself in the throat and lungs, committing suicide.

Reflection: Lack of Care for Left-Behind Elders Leads to Human Tragedy

This case is tragic and heart-wrenching, and any description would not be exaggerated. On March 7th, I revisited the site of the incident and still felt the heavy atmosphere of sorrow enveloping the area, with the gloom yet to dissipate...

According to the police, villagers recounted to the investigating officers that He Huangu was unpopular in the village due to his petty theft habits. Over time, without receiving care from his family, unable to confide his confusion and grievances to his loved ones, he gradually developed narrow-minded, resentful, and selfish psychological reactions. Especially after failing to get money from his grandson, his resentment and hatred escalated, surpassing the psychological barrier... Ultimately, this led to the later tragedy, which was actually entirely preventable.

The crime scene was not isolated; there were houses nearby, and people often passed by the doorstep.

This means that if neighbors had paid attention, it wouldn't have been difficult to spot the signs of the series of unfortunate events. Despite being so close, it was as if they were miles apart. The estrangement and indifference among neighbors were the true killers, the most shocking aspect of this story.

How can we care for left-behind elders? How can we ensure children grow up healthily in a warm society? In today's frequent occurrences of problems involving left-behind elders and children, what should society do? Hopefully, this case will serve as a warning.

(Note: The names of the left-behind children in this article are pseudonyms.)
