Japanese horror director Iokoro, who has previously directed horror films such as "Fujou Unlimited" and "Spooky Encounters," has recently released a new horror film titled "Zombie Butts."

Just from the title, it's clear that this is another parody horror film by Iokoro. He seems to be endlessly enthusiastic about this type of movie! His previous works, "Machine Gun Girl" and "Robot Geisha," are of similar styles. Iokoro appears to be immersed in his own cinematic world, treating these movies as comedies. The frequent appearances of blood and entrails flying everywhere, the lack of meaningful plot, and countless cheesy scenes have led audiences to give his films consistently poor reviews.

Iokoro seems not to care much about his reputation, and now his new horror film "Zombie Butts" is ready to meet with the audience. Judging from the posters and stills revealed so far, the character designs and poster styles are almost identical to those in "Machine Gun Girl." The female lead continues to tease in her uniform, showing off her undergarments with exaggerated leg splits. As an editor, I can't say I'm particularly impressed with this kind of film, but fans of pure parody and cult-style movies might still look forward to it.

The release date for the film has yet to be determined. Here's a synopsis of "Zombie Butts":

Eri, a young and beautiful high school girl skilled in karate, goes on a trip to the woods with four old friends. While fishing, they discover a strange parasite on the fish. Then, while using the restroom, they are attacked by zombies. Terrified, the group finds the home of Dr. Tanaka and his daughter and rushes inside to take refuge. However, they don't know that the terrifying parasites and zombies are the results of Dr. Tanaka's experiments. What is the connection between the parasites and the zombies? Can Eri's karate skills help her friends escape?
