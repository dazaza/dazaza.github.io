Common Sense: 7 Wrong Beliefs About Putting Babies to Sleep!

by childrenstory126 on 2012-02-27 17:47:40

As parents, you may be troubled by various sleep problems of your little baby, and the various claims about baby sleep also make you more anxious. Below are some misconceptions about putting babies to sleep that will help parents analyze.

1. [No matter what time the baby goes to sleep, as long as the total amount of sleep is enough, it's fine.]

Adequate sleep is very crucial for a baby's growth and development because the endocrine system releases three times more growth hormone during sleep than usual. However, the quality of a baby's sleep is also important. Different bedtime can change the proportion of deep and light sleep. The later the baby falls asleep, the more shallow sleep and less deep sleep he gets. Deep sleep is directly related to the baby's growth and development because growth hormone is mainly secreted during deep sleep. Shallow sleep is more closely related to the development of the baby's memory. Therefore, try to let your "night owl" go to bed early.

2. [If the baby's sleep time does not reach the standard, it will definitely affect his growth and development.]

In fact, although each age group of babies has its specific sleep demand, there are individual differences between babies. For example, a baby's temperament type may determine that he sleeps less.

In principle, as long as the baby's mental state is good, appetite is normal, there are no digestive problems, and weight gain is good, it's fine. However, if it deviates too much, for example, newborns need to sleep 16-18 hours a day, but your baby only sleeps 12 hours, then you might need to consult a doctor and monitor the baby's growth and development.

3. [You must get the baby to form a good habit of taking naps.]

For children over 3 years old, they have a lot of energy during the day, and playing and continuous activities can consume their energy, ensuring they sleep well at night. As long as the baby has sufficient nighttime sleep and the quality is good, it can meet the needs of his growth and development. Therefore, if the baby does not need a nap, parents do not need to force him.

4. [Holding the baby while sleeping gives him a sense of security and makes him fall asleep faster.]

Holding the baby while sleeping indeed gives him a sense of security, but it can also easily make the baby dependent. When the baby grows older, we may find it difficult to change this habit. This dependency can prolong the baby's falling-asleep time, making it harder for him to fall asleep. Moreover, when the baby wakes up at night, if the parents cannot give him timely comfort, he finds it hard to fall back asleep on his own. This adversely affects cultivating the baby's independent sleeping habits and forming the natural conversion between deep and light sleep at night. It is suggested that you start now to gradually let the baby sleep in his crib and cultivate the ability to fall asleep independently.

5. [When the baby makes a sound, gently patting him can help him continue to sleep.]

The baby's sleep is divided into two states: deep sleep and light sleep. For small infants, especially newborns, deep sleep and light sleep basically account for 50% each, and they alternate continuously. During deep sleep, the baby is completely resting, except for occasional startle reflexes and extremely slight mouth movements, there are no other activities; during light sleep, the baby's arms, legs, and entire body often move, and the face may show strange expressions, frowning, smiling, etc., all of which are normal manifestations during the light sleep period.

Therefore, if the baby makes a slight sobbing or movement, don't rush to pat him, hold him, or feed him milk. Observe first to see if the baby can continue to sleep. Otherwise, excessive intervention will artificially interrupt the natural alternation between the baby's deep and light sleep, disrupting the baby's sleep pattern. If the baby shows more crying or other behaviors, we can intervene then.

6. [Snoring means the baby is sleeping soundly.]

Occasional snoring in babies may be caused by a cold, and after the cold is cured, the snoring symptoms will disappear. However, if the baby snores for a long time, it may be due to enlarged adenoids, enlarged tonsils, or other reasons affecting the breathing through the nose and throat. In such cases, some babies may also experience nasal blockage and open-mouth breathing even when awake. Over time, this can harm the baby's brain development. If your baby indeed has sleep snoring and open-mouth breathing, it's best to take him to the ENT department of the hospital for examination.

7. [When the child is young, there is no need to emphasize his sleep habits, just adjust them two or three months before entering kindergarten.]

In fact, from 0 to 1 year old is the critical period for the formation of the baby's sleep behavior, and the 24-hour circadian rhythm is usually established within one year. However, many parents only think about adjusting the child's sleep habits when they themselves have to go to work or the baby has to enter kindergarten. Once the baby's sleep habits are formed, it becomes difficult to correct them. Therefore, it's best to consciously cultivate good sleep habits in the baby around four or five months of age.
