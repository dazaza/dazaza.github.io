Harbin Hyperthyroidism Hospital answers whether hypothyroidism is hereditary. Hypothyroidism, or thyroid function reduction, occurs due to a lack of thyroid hormones, which leads to a decrease in metabolic activity in the body, causing clinical symptoms. When it occurs in adults, it is called "adult hypothyroidism." Severe cases are characterized by myxedema, hence the term "myxedema," while those who fall into a coma are referred to as "myxedema coma." If it occurs during the embryonic stage or infancy, it can severely affect brain and physical growth and development, leading to cretinism or dwarfism, also known as "cretin disease."

Is hypothyroidism hereditary? Experts from Heilongjiang Hyperthyroidism Hospital say that hyperthyroidism or Hashimoto's disease and other autoimmune diseases may be hereditary, but most people will still have healthy children. Nowadays, there are screening tests for thyroid diseases after childbirth. Even if the child has the condition, as long as it is detected in time and treated promptly, the child can recover. This information is provided by a specialized thyroid hospital in Harbin.

Is hypothyroidism hereditary? Hashimoto's hypothyroidism can be hereditary. However, if hypothyroidism is caused by an obstacle in the synthesis of thyroid hormones, it could also be hereditary. Generally, TSH levels in newborns peak physiologically between 20-60 minutes after birth and gradually decrease over the next 1-4 days. Therefore, it is best to conduct tests within 30 minutes of birth or between 4-7 days after birth. The earlier a child with hypothyroidism receives treatment, the better.
