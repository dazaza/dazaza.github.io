Why do companies need to build websites and what are their functions?

1. What is the use of a company building a website? In today's rapidly growing collection of technologies, websites have become as essential as mobile phones, fax machines, business cards, printed materials, TV and newspaper advertisements. Regardless of the size of the company, from large enterprises to small farmers' households or salespersons dealing with agricultural by-products, all require websites.

1. Saving money for the enterprise: Websites operate 24 hours a day, 365 days a year, equivalent to hiring hundreds of product promoters who work tirelessly without pay, gratitude, or fatigue. It acts as a never-closing storefront, exhibition center, trade fair, free product consultation center, news release center, tens of thousands of distributed product manuals and promotional prints, a brand nameplate without time and space limits, and newspaper and TV ads. But the operational cost is much lower! Significant market promotion costs can be saved immediately! (Savings include part of the sales staff salaries, travel expenses, venue fees, participation fees, mobile phone fees, fax fees, long-distance call fees, printing fees, advertising fees, etc.).

2. Making money for the enterprise: Breaking regional limitations, obtaining greater survival space. Improving transaction methods, increasing efficiency. Opening up new markets, finding new customers, acquiring new orders. An unmanned website is the most effective treasure.

3. Competing with peers: Building a website greatly enhances the competitiveness of the enterprise, leveraging existing industry advantages and seizing industry high ground. As competitors rush to build websites, the market is like a battlefield; if you don't build a website, you risk elimination.

4. For business convenience: Having a website means having an online home or factory, giving the company a fixed trademark code, making it convenient for others to visit, store customer information, orders, and other data. Online consultations and corresponding after-sales services are guaranteed.

5. To meet integration needs: E-commerce, online customs declaration and taxation initiation, etc., force companies to accept networks.

6. For image needs: Printing the company’s brand or website URL and email on promotional materials naturally becomes a symbol of corporate identity, greatly enhancing the company's level. Increased fame and brand awareness significantly increase intangible assets.

2. My company's products are in short supply, there's no worry about doing business, why should we build a website?

Generally speaking, things seem good, but in reality, there are many problems!

Firstly: Precisely because your products are in high demand, one reason is due to too small scale, so production scale should be expanded to reduce operating costs through volume. This requires building a website to post information for bidding recruitment, project cooperation, introducing equipment, expanding factories, letting others know your strength; another reason is that raw materials cannot keep up, leading to the inability of the enterprise to produce at full capacity. This requires building a website to post information, understand more sources of raw materials, raw material prices, and achieve greater development.

Furthermore: After-sales service is an important link. The interactivity and ease of the Internet make it easy to maintain customer loyalty over the long term. For continuous development, the company must remain vigilant and continuously promote itself, allowing more customers to understand the company, becoming potential customers, gaining more orders, and gradually expanding production scale to remain invincible in market competition.

3. My company is small in scale and poor in benefits, lacks money for network investment, why should we build a website?

Precisely because the company is small and inefficient, it is most necessary to use the low-cost Internet to save personnel and office expenses while seeking raw material information and broader markets, achieving low-cost expansion. Letting the whole country or even the world know the company, establishing the company's image online, promoting its products, and establishing contact with more customers will bring unimaginable benefits.

4. My company belongs to the type of processing incoming materials, merchants require confidentiality, disallowing publicity, why should we build a website?

There is a saying that you shouldn't hang yourself on one tree. That is to say, you shouldn't always be led by the nose. A company's production should not depend on one merchant alone. Instead, it should let global commercial companies and more merchants know about the company's production capabilities through the Internet, establish contact with more customers, and achieve greater development, consolidating the company's market competitiveness. Confidentiality that needs to be maintained still remains confidential.

5. My company's product profit margin is low, long-distance transportation/cross-provincial operations are not profitable, why should we build a website?

Low product profits indicate that the company should drastically reduce costs. Using the Internet, a low-investment, low-cost medium, allows real-time access to more customer information and raw material information, achieving a larger sales scale with lower marketing costs. Large-scale production naturally reduces costs.
