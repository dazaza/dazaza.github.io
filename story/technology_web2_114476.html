Maybe you've had this experience: You share a link with someone, often via IM or email to your friend. However, when your friends see a link in an IM message, they often do not click on it, and even suspect that the linked page contains a virus or Trojan horse. Not to mention the many links you send to your friends. That would make him more suspicious. All right. Then use E-mail. But now that many emails contain links, the spam weight will become very high, and often the mail service provider will directly dispose of your mail as junk and put it into the recipient's junk mail box, or even refuse to notify the recipient.

image

ShareTabs is a very simple link sharing tool. You can submit a single link or a large list of links, and get a new link that contains all the urls you want to share with your friends, and automatically generates a snapshot of those urls for your friends to preview, and then decide whether to click on those links. And in the newly generated URL page, the links you share are generated a label, just like IE7 supports multi-page browsing browsers, click on the label to access the URL you share. Then you can share it with your friends via email, instant messaging, open source, or SMS.
