The overall foreign capital control in DNF's small high-tech industries has reached a level of nearly 70% in recent years.

by dnfmeimei on 2009-12-01 00:52:43

The "2009 China Foreign Capital Control Report" recently released by Beijing Jiaotong University's Industrial Security Research Center states that over the past 10 years, foreign capital’s market control of China’s secondary industry (i.e., manufacturing) has steadily increased. The average control rate has approached one-third, surpassing the general industry market control warning line.

Currently, there are more than 600,000 foreign-funded enterprises in China, and almost all of the world's top 500 companies have entered the Chinese market. Foreign capital mainly influences the host country's industrial security through market control, equity control, brand control, and technological control. In the industrial sector, foreign capital has relatively low market control over China's mining, electric power, gas, water production, and supply industries, while the highest degree of control is in the manufacturing sector. Over the past decade, foreign capital's control of the manufacturing market has generally remained above 30%, reaching over 35% between 2005 and 2007. Although this figure declined somewhat in 2008, it still remains above 30%.

Taking the automobile industry as an example, since 1998, the market control rate of foreign-funded enterprises has stabilized at around 30%, with the equity control rate rising to 43.62% in 2006. Whether in the whole vehicle or parts sectors, the equity control of foreign enterprises continues to increase, especially in the engine field where foreign equity control reached as high as 78.26% in 2006. This poses a certain threat to the safety of China's automotive industry.

In the steel industry, the foreign capital market control degree was 12.9% in 2008; in the petrochemical industry, it was 18.8%. In the highly competitive textile industry, the foreign capital market control rate exceeds 28%, with the control degree in the clothing and hat manufacturing sector ranging from 45% to 50%. In the light industry, the foreign capital control rate over the past decade has exceeded 37%.

Particularly alarming is the electronics information industry, where the market control degree of foreign enterprises in China has been over 80% since 2004, with foreign capital controlling an average of 36% of patents in this field. Additionally, as a new decisive factor for economic development, the overall foreign capital control degree in high-tech industries has reached nearly 70% in recent years.

The report suggests that China must establish a national industrial security management and early warning mechanism for introducing foreign capital. While maintaining reasonable growth in attracting foreign investment, efforts should focus on improving the quality and level of utilizing foreign capital and increasing policy support to encourage substantive technology transfer from foreign capital.

Ji Xiaonan, chairman of the board of supervisors for key large-scale enterprises under the State-owned Assets Supervision and Administration Commission of the State Council, said in an interview that the biggest danger facing current industrial security is the lack of product competitiveness. The model of China's exports relying on simple processing and low-end products to earn meager profits is unsustainable. It is necessary to strengthen innovation capabilities in technology and management. (Reported by Tu Lufang) (Source: Beijing Daily)
