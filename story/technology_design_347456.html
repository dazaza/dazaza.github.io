"Limited edition" Nokia mobile phone is 800 yuan more expensive than the market price, which is not honest at all.

by shoujitpd on 2010-04-18 01:30:52

Our newspaper's hotline 4249400 received a report: "They said it was a limited edition Nokia phone, and that the phone’s software system had been upgraded. It was priced 800 yuan more expensive than other sellers in the market, but in fact, it was merely a different color." Yesterday, talking about his experience of buying a phone, a second-year student surnamed Xiang from a university in Hefei expressed his indignation.

On April 4, Xiang picked out a Nokia E52 phone worth 2030 yuan at a mobile phone store near the City Government Square. After paying, the salesperson suddenly persuaded Xiang: "You'd better buy the 5233 instead of this E52." The salesperson told Xiang that their store's black 5233 phone was a limited edition. Different from the white regular version priced at 1480 yuan, this phone had an upgraded software system, and its camera pixel was 3.2 million. Under the persuasion of the salesperson, Xiang chose the "worth 2150 yuan" limited edition 5233 phone.

After returning to school, Xiang found that a classmate's phone was exactly the same as his, but only cost over 1300 yuan, and the actual pixel of the phone camera was only 2 million. On April 7, Xiang went to the store for an explanation, but the store refused to refund the price difference. Helplessly, Xiang dialed the mayor's hotline 12345, which transferred the call to the industrial and commercial department. With the intervention of the industrial and commercial department, the store made Xiang fill out a price difference form on the same day, promising to give a reply on the 8th.

Yesterday, our reporter called Nokia's after-sales service hotline 4008800123. The operator informed us that there was no such thing as a "limited edition" or "regular edition" for the Nokia 5233; the only difference was in the packaging. Perhaps the so-called "limited edition" came with additional headphones, batteries, etc. The reporter also inquired several stores selling Nokia phones, finding that the price difference between the black and white versions was only around 100 yuan.

Subsequently, the reporter contacted the mobile phone store. A staff member surnamed Tao told us that the store had already negotiated with Xiang, both parties agreeing to refund the price difference according to the market price, and they had agreed to refund on Saturday afternoon this week. Xiang told the reporter that although it was agreed to refund, the store claimed that the purchase price of that phone was 1680 yuan, so he was still unsure about the specific standard for refunding the price difference. (Reported by Qiu Qingqing and Xing Linlin)
