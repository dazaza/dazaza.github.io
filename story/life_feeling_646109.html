How much love is worth waiting for a lifetime?

by lili1 on 2011-12-27 22:09:29

When the man took the woman home as his wife, she was already mad, and mad to the point of being unconscious.

In the dead of night, the relatives and friends who came to the wedding banquet had gradually dispersed. He slowly walked towards her sitting in the lamplight. In the midst of all the festive red, the woman in the bright red bridal gown suddenly giggled: "Big Brother, everyone has gone home to sleep, why haven't you left yet?" Looking at the woman's face filled with the innocence and confusion of a baby, a faint sadness lightly enveloped the man's face, but soon his smile returned: "Come on, let Big Brother wash your face and feet, you should rest early, alright?" The woman was quite obedient, sitting quietly on the edge of the bed, putting her feet in the basin of hot water he had brought over. He gently massaged her feet while she kept asking him questions, which were scattered and illogical. Two warm tears unknowingly fell into the basin in front of the woman. They were from the man. He still couldn't understand how such a wise and kind woman could have ended up like this.

Yes, once, she was smarter, kinder, and more understanding of his thoughts than all the girls in the village. Back then, they were from the same village, the same class, the same school, and later secretly became lovers. Decades ago, in the countryside, even if their youthful love was passionate, it could only be carried out secretly. At that time, in the village, his family was the poorest, and both his parents had passed away early. He was an orphan raised by the kindness of many families. Her family was the richest, and she was the only pampered daughter. A poor man and a rich woman, their love was destined to be separated by the secular river of heaven. When their relationship was exposed, it also marked the end of their love. Her parents strongly opposed this marriage. No matter how much she resisted with threats of suicide, in the end, she was forcibly stuffed into the sedan chair that had come to take her away.

She got married, and he left in despair. He went far away to Beidahuang (a region in Northeast China), hoping that the black soil there could heal the wound in his heart. Since then, they were apart for many years.

When he returned to his hometown again, he was already a university professor returning in glory. The shiny black soil of Beidahuang could not cover his brilliance; he participated in the college entrance examination and luckily entered university. Afterwards, his career path could be said to have been smooth sailing, from lecturer to professor, the road that others had to strive for most of their lives, he traversed in just a few short years. However, his emotions did not go as smoothly as his career. Past middle age, although he was surrounded by beautiful women, no matter how many ships had sailed, he could never find the small boat he had originally set sail with.

It is often said that wanderers feel timid when nearing their homeland, and such timid feelings were more pronounced in him than in others. He originally thought that she would already be living a life of prosperity with many children, and that they would have a warm and touching reunion. But when he faced the woman before him, dressed in shabby clothes and only able to laugh foolishly at him, he was stunned. It turned out that in the years he had been away, too many terrible things had happened, too much heaviness and sorrow. That year, she was forcibly taken to her husband's house, where she refused to eat, drink, or sleep for several days, only muttering one name over and over—his name. A month later, her in-laws discovered she was insane and unceremoniously sent her back to her natal home. From then on, the village gained a crazy woman who wandered around calling out, "Ah Jun Ge, Ah Jun Ge..."

Listening to the neighbors recounting this sad story, and looking at the woman's emaciated, frail body, his eyes moistened: "These past years, you've really suffered a lot..."

He decided to marry her and take her to the city where he lived. A prestigious university professor wanting to marry a crazy woman and bring her into the city, almost everyone thought he had gone mad. Regardless of others' opinions, he brought her into his long-empty house, starting their marriage life that had been delayed for more than ten years.

After their marriage, under his meticulous care, the woman's physical and mental health improved greatly, but her condition remained unstable, sometimes good, sometimes bad. When she was well, she would sit quietly and chat with him; when she was ill, she would smash and throw things. His face often bore inexplicable scratch marks. None of these mattered to him; he said that the pain in his skin was nothing compared to the pain she had endured from losing him. There was one thing, however, that troubled him greatly—she never recognized him, always calling him "kind Big Brother." For more than twenty years living together, she called him that. She called him "kind Big Brother" because for more than twenty years, he had wiped her face and washed her feet daily, and for more than twenty years, he had held her hand and strolled through the beautiful campus. Over those twenty years, he tolerated her unpredictability. Whenever she regained some clarity, she would say that without this kind Big Brother, she would have died long ago. Towards him, she had respect, but no love.

The woman passed away in the twenty-fifth year of their marriage, suffering from late-stage breast cancer. Despite his best efforts to treat her, he was unable to save her. On her deathbed, she lapsed into a coma several times and woke up several times. When she woke up, she seemed especially clear-headed. She moved her lips, signaling him to lean down: "Kind Big Brother, I'm leaving, you can rest now. These many years, you've suffered enough. I... can finally go find my Ah Jun Ge..." These were the last words of the woman. Her life came to an abrupt end in a peaceful and serene atmosphere.

He devoted his entire life to her, and she loved him foolishly for her entire life. Their deep and foolish love ultimately failed to meet in this world. Leaning on her gradually cooling body, his tears silently fell.
