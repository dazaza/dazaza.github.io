I will remember you.

by kuailaira2 on 2012-02-14 16:16:17

I will remember you. Time is a wonderful thing; it makes us remember some things while unknowingly forget others. For instance, after you grow up, your parents still often recall the funny things from your childhood. You open your mouth in amazement and say: "I did such things before? I wouldn't have remembered if you hadn't said so." Another example is the person you were infatuated with when you were young. As time flies by, there's only a blurry shadow left. If it weren't for looking at the diary from back then, you would almost have forgotten all those silly little things that you did, which now seem purely foolish. Unfortunately, the human brain is ultimately not a mobile hard drive with infinite storage space. You can't recover lost data, nor can you delete those unforgettable memories that are deeply etched in your heart. Often times, the loneliness we fear actually shares a common essence - the fear of being forgotten by others. It's like when you deliberately disappear for a day, but don't receive any text messages, comments, or phone calls. Children who are abandoned by their birth parents are probably the loneliest, because they feel forgotten from the moment they were born. In families with many children, the one who isn't favored definitely feels more lonely. Even playboys who change girlfriends as frequently as they change clothes keep saying that they are lonely. There are also faded stars, former lovers who became strangers after breaking up, and so on. We wander between what we cannot obtain and what we have lost, lamenting our loneliness, yet failing to see the warmth around us. If we just hide in a cold corner waiting for warmth, even if sunshine is given to us, we won't be able to escape the situation of loneliness because even the sun will eventually burn out one day. Please cherish those who willingly reach out to give you warmth. If you can't melt a block of ice, at least give a friendly response to the sunlight that gives selflessly. Although I'm not a memory expert, and I may even be a bit forgetful, I will strive to remember every precious detail. Even if I really lose my memory, there will always be a moment when I remember you, recalling everything from a tiny detail. Maybe I'll smile, maybe I'll cry and make a fuss, regardless of how it turns out, I will remember you.
