There are seven types of e-commerce models, etc.: B2B, B2C, C2C, B2M, M2C, B2A (i.e., B2G), and C2A (i.e., C2G).

B2B = Business to Business.

Business-to-business e-commerce refers to the exchange of products, services, and information between businesses over the internet. In layman's terms, both parties involved in the e-commerce transaction are merchants (or enterprises, companies). They use Internet technology or various business network platforms to complete the business transaction process. These processes include: publishing supply and demand information, ordering and confirming orders, payment processes, and the issuance, transmission, and reception of invoices, determining distribution plans and monitoring the distribution process, etc. Sometimes written as B to B, but for simplicity, its homophone B2B (2 is "two") is used. Typical examples of B2B include China Suppliers, Alibaba, Made-in-China.com, DHgate, Hc360.com, Yingshang.com, Needsee, E-commerce Study Bar, etc. B2B can be divided into foreign trade B2B and domestic trade B2B based on service targets, and into comprehensive B2B and vertical B2B based on industry characteristics. Vertical B2B includes: Chemnet.com.cn, Luwen Construction Service Network.

B2C = Business to Customer

The B2C model is the earliest form of e-commerce model in our country, marked by the official operation of 8848 Online Mall.

First type:

Comprehensive mall: Taobao, Tianhecheng, Zhengjia Plaza online.

A mall called a city naturally has many stores. Yes, a comprehensive mall is like entering large malls such as Tianhecheng, Zhengjia Plaza, Xin Daxin, etc., in real life. The first floor of the mall may be top brands, then the second floor is women's clothing, the third floor is men's clothing, the fourth floor is sports/decoration, the fifth floor is mobile phones/digital products, and the sixth floor is clearance sales... Numerous brand specialty stores are assembled here, this is a mall. And later Taobao Mall is naturally this form, no different from traditional ones. It has a large shopping group, a stable website platform, a complete payment system, an integrity safety system (despite still having many shortcomings at present), promoting sellers to sell goods and buyers to buy goods. Like traditional malls, Red Baby Website, Taobao itself does not sell things, it provides complete sales support.

Online malls, with enough popularity, rich products, and convenient logistics, show cost advantages, being a 24-hour non-stop city without regional restrictions, and more abundant products, etc., demonstrating the role that online comprehensive malls will play in the trading market.

These malls offline are divided by region, each big city usually has three or five large malls. And in this field of the Internet, it is also destined that three or five comprehensive malls dominate. Currently, Taobao is in the awkward position of being the only dominant player. Actually, similar ones include Paipai/Ebay/Youa, but they are far behind, so much so that people don't even want to mention them.

How many of these online malls exist? Because each has differences in inventory and delivery environments, each platform is given its own unique elements, meaning there is a brand effect. With a brand effect, actually this mode of online shops can have multiple players, not just three or five, but can be multiplied. Consumers may choose to try other options due to a certain experience, a small price difference, or a bad shopping experience.

Among them, dangdang.com and Joyo tried the store-in-store model, but it was relatively unsuccessful, showing signs of wanting to transform into a mall.

Fourth type:

Vertical store: Maikailin, Red Baby, JD.com, Youge.com, Qianshediandian online, Gome, 360 Mall, Xiaogouwang

Vertical stores serve specific groups of people or meet specific needs, providing comprehensive products related to this field or need and offering more professional services.

For example, Maikailin is positioned towards young women aged 18-25 as a sales carrier without physical stores, although it started with DMs, it gradually shifted towards online stores.

Red Baby started by targeting the maternal and infant market.

JD.com, doing online Gome, specializes in selling electrical appliances/3C products.

The products of these malls have more similarities, either meeting the needs of a certain group of people, or satisfying a certain need, or a certain platform (such as electrical appliances).

How many vertical stores exist on the Internet? This depends on market segmentation. If the number of segmented categories is set as X, then it is three to five times X, because in every field, there are always three to five competitors, even though everyone says there is no second place on the Internet, which is not entirely true. Although the second place lives toughly, they endure and persist. And precisely because of good competition, services are improved.

Here we find a phenomenon, for example, JD.com and Red Baby, judging from their recent actions, both have started expanding their product lines. For instance, Red Baby expanded from the original maternal and infant market to a family-based shopping platform; JD.com attracted a large number of buyers through low-priced electrical appliances/3C products, then enriched its product line, adding/recommending products with higher profit margins to seek profits. Clearly, they are all transforming into comprehensive stores. Another phenomenon is that stores like Maikailin and Youge.com, targeting segmented populations, tend to form their own brands.

Fifth type:

Compound brand store: Giordano, Belle

Giordano is a traditional clothing brand with N direct and franchise stores. When Zhengjia Plaza opened, Giordano moved in, and when Taobao Mall opened online, Giordano also entered. Even if all the malls collapsed, Giordano would still have its independent image store, which is a traditional brand. When Giordano found that online consumers were different from offline consumers, they boldly operated with different prices, and their perfect warehouse allocation management better controlled cash flow and goods circulation through online sales.

Like Belle, which has nearly 8,000 stores offline, announced a market of 16 billion yuan in 2008. Now moving online, they are boldly experimenting. Online styles or brands have certain distinctions and produce in large quantities because, if they cannot sell out online, those 8,000 stores are excellent outlets for clearing inventory. Selling 200,000 pairs online, each store selling more than twenty pairs solves the problem.

Similar stores, as e-commerce matures, will see more traditional brands joining the e-commerce battlefield to capture new markets, expand new channels, and optimize product and channel resources. A wave of aggressive entry is imminent.

How many of these online stores are there? How many there are offline, there will be that many online. Including Gome, they all belong to compound-type online stores, but the integration is not yet strong enough, and the model is not mature, so it is temporarily not mentioned.

Sixth type:

Lightweight brand store: PPG, VANCL, Mengbasha

The cases of PPG and VANCL have spread throughout the streets and alleys. Despite many controversies, new things are always born in controversy. Here, Mengbasha is included for two reasons. It is also a representative worth paying attention to.

China is already a production powerhouse with numerous contract factories and mature industry chains sufficient to meet production needs. Chinese people make money through product profit premiums rather than brand profit premiums (for example, Chinese MP3s make ten yuan in profit, while American APPLE makes more than ten US dollars in profit). Under the influence of the environment, creating a brand is not necessarily required to have one's own factory. On the contrary, China has unparalleled advantages. Branders can focus more on providing personalized products that meet the needs of their audience groups. Based on brand positioning, strengthen product design, through the application of informatization, cooperate with increasingly mature internet sales platforms, and continuously improve logistics distribution and various services. The entire chain is becoming increasingly detailed and perfected, allowing branders to focus on what they are good at, outsourcing everything else, using the best raw material providers, finding the best manufacturers, seeking high-efficiency effective promotion channels, combining strengths, and your advantages will be fully displayed. Looking back, aren't world-class branders doing the same?

Lightweight brand stores are feasible, the key is not to lose oneself, find one's core competitiveness, and let stronger people take care of the rest. And now, bosses who think they can do everything themselves need to rethink, don't trip yourself up.

Previously, Mengbasha opened several physical stores, even expanding from selling underwear to covering all aspects of female consumption. This shows that lightweight online stores are also transforming into compound types. At the same time, in the future, lightweight brand stores will challenge traditional brand stores, anyone might win, and the losers are always companies unwilling to accept others' strengths.

C2C = Consumer to Consumer

C2C, like B2B and B2C, is one of the several models of e-commerce. Different from C2C being a user-to-user model, C2C business platforms provide an online trading platform for both buyers and sellers, where sellers can actively list products for online auctioning, and buyers can choose products for bidding. Typical examples of C2C include Baidu C2C, Taobao, and Paipai.

B2M = Business to Manager

B2M is a completely new e-commerce model relative to B2B, B2C, and C2C. The fundamental difference between this type of e-commerce and the previous three lies in the nature of the target customer base. The target customer base of the first three appears as consumers, whereas B2M targets the sellers or workers of the enterprise or product, not the end consumers.

Enterprises release their products or services via an online platform, and professional managers obtain the enterprise’s product or service information through the network, providing product sales or enterprise services for the enterprise. Enterprises achieve the purpose of selling products or obtaining services through the services provided by the managers. Professional managers obtain commissions by providing services to the enterprise.

Compared with traditional e-commerce, B2M has made tremendous improvements. Besides the essential differences in the user groups targeted, B2M has a greater characteristic advantage: the offline development of e-commerce! The characteristic of the above three traditional e-commerce modes is that both buyers and sellers of goods or services can only be netizens. However, the B2M model can bring the goods and services information online to the offline realm entirely. Enterprises release information, managers obtain commercial information, and provide goods or services to all citizens, whether online or offline. In essence, B2M is a kind of agency model.

Business to Marketing

An e-commerce enterprise (e-commerce company or a company where e-commerce is an important marketing channel) oriented towards marketing. B2M e-commerce companies establish marketing sites centered around customer needs and widely promote and standardize site guidance management through various online and offline channels, making the site an important marketing channel for the enterprise.

M2C = Manager to Consumer

M2C is an extended concept derived from the B2M e-commerce model. In the B2M phase, enterprises release their products or services via an online platform, professional managers obtain the enterprise’s product or service information through the network, providing product sales or enterprise services for the enterprise. Enterprises achieve the purpose of selling products or obtaining services through the services provided by the managers. In the M2C phase, managers will face Consumers, i.e., the ultimate consumers.

M2C is an extension of B2M and an indispensable subsequent development phase in the new B2M e-commerce model. Managers ultimately need to sell products to the ultimate consumers, and a large part of this involves e-commerce, similar to C2C but not entirely the same. C2C is a traditional profit model, earning basically the price difference of goods. The profit model of M2C is richer and more flexible, it can be the price difference or commission. Moreover, the logistics management model of M2C can be more diverse than C2C, such as zero inventory; in terms of cash flow, it also has advantages over traditional C2C.

Internet-based e-commerce can be divided into three aspects: information service, transaction, and payment. The main contents include: electronic commerce advertising; electronic selection and transaction, exchange of electronic transaction vouchers; electronic payment and settlement, as well as post-sale online services, etc. The main transaction types are business-to-individual transactions (B to C method) and business-to-business transactions (B to B method). The entities participating in e-commerce are four types: customers (individual consumers or corporate groups), merchants (including retailers, manufacturers, storage and transportation merchants), banks (including issuing banks, acquiring banks), and certification centers.

From the perspective of trade activities, e-commerce can be realized in multiple links, thereby dividing e-commerce into two levels. Lower-level e-commerce includes electronic commerce, electronic trade, electronic contracts, etc.; the most complete and highest-level e-commerce should be able to conduct all trade activities via the INTERNET network, that is, fully realizing the information flow, commerce flow, capital flow, and part of the logistics online, meaning that you can start from finding customers, all the way through negotiation, ordering, online payment (receipt), issuing electronic invoices to electronic customs declaration, electronic tax payment, etc., completing everything seamlessly via the INTERNET.

To realize complete e-commerce involves many aspects, besides buyers and sellers, it also requires the participation of banks or financial institutions, government agencies, certification agencies, distribution centers, etc. Since the parties involved in e-commerce do not physically meet, the entire e-commerce process is not a replica of physical world business activities. Online banking, online electronic payments, data encryption, electronic signatures, and other technologies play important and indispensable roles in e-commerce.
