Analysis of the Development Trend of China's Ceramic Industry in 2011

by sffgskkgfel9 on 2012-02-11 10:28:38

Analysis of the Development Trend of China's Ceramic Industry in 2011 – Domestic News - Industry News - Building Materials Network

China’s ceramic industry has now become the world's largest ceramics producer without a doubt, with a wall and floor tile production reaching 7 billion square meters in 2010, and sanitary ceramics exceeding 140 million pieces. However, we still cannot claim to be a ceramics powerhouse; whether it is in terms of production technology or equipment, or even brand value, we are far behind developed countries such as Italy, Spain, the United States, and Japan. At this critical moment of national transformation, industry professionals are speculating about the future direction and trends of the industry. In response to this, I would like to present my views on the development trends of ceramics in 2011.

1. With the increasing popularity of online shopping, some forward-thinking enterprises will experiment with e-commerce, promoting online service channels. According to relevant data, the global online trade volume reached $20 trillion in 2010, accounting for approximately 42% of the total global trade volume. Eighty percent of the Fortune Global 500 companies have already engaged in marketing activities online according to statistics from the American magazine "Fortune".

2. China's rural market will exhibit an unprecedented sales boom. A number of brand enterprises will re-design their product lines to create products more suitable for farmers' consumption in order to tap into the rural market. According to surveys, current tile consumption is concentrated among 20% of the urban population, while nearly 80% of the rural population does not use tiles. According to the National Bureau of Statistics, there are currently about 720 million rural residents and approximately 220 million households in China. As the living standards of rural residents improve gradually, building new houses and renovating old ones have become one of the major highlights of rural consumption. It is understood that the lifespan of rural houses is generally short, with an average rebuild cycle of every ten years. Therefore, companies will aim at the rural market, seizing the high ground of the future market. This will lead to further improvements in logistics channels, quality supervision, and after-sales services.

3. The Chinese ceramics industry will see a shift from industry and regional brands to mass-market brands, and 2011 will be a turning point in brand construction, which is also a precursor to the arrival of the oligopoly era in the industry. In terms of products, in the building ceramics industry, full-glazed products will no longer just be trendy products that attract attention and eyeballs. With the participation of various enterprises, plus the rise and maturity of inkjet printing technology, full-glazed products will truly enter the market and become pioneers in the field of floor tiles. Currently, almost all antique tile companies in Foshan have launched full-glazed products. Inevitably, fierce competition in full-glazed products will occur in 2011; in the bathroom industry, intelligent bathrooms and integrated bathrooms will slowly replace traditional products and become the first choice for mainstream consumers.

4. With the acceleration of the country's legalisation and democratisation process, the cost of illegal acts such as tax evasion, illegal employment, patent infringement, environmental pollution, commercial bribery, illegal land occupation, and manufacturing of substandard products will increase. Some companies will have to pay a heavy price for these actions.

5. With the continuous rapid growth of the urban population and the worsening of urban traffic conditions, coupled with restrictions on urban land use, a wave of old-to-new reconstruction fever will inevitably sweep through major and medium-sized cities across the country this year. Old professional markets that affect the urban landscape and cause traffic congestion will become targets for demolition. This year, provinces and cities across the country will successively witness large-scale market relocations.

6. Influenced by national macro-control, China's real estate market will gradually achieve rational return. The housing prices in major and medium-sized cities in the country will decline due to tightened monetary policy and the introduction of property taxes, with expected price reductions ranging from 15% to 35%. In general, the greater the past price bubble, the higher the decrease in housing prices. Thus, the pace of new building construction will slow down this year, affecting the domestic sales of ceramic and sanitary ware products, especially in the first half of the year. On the export side, affected by foreign anti-dumping measures and changes in the RMB exchange rate, the situation will become increasingly severe.

7. Regarding ceramic production bases, the pattern of Foshan being the dominant player, with other production areas competing fiercely, will remain. According to statistics from the Guangdong Building Materials Industry Association, from January to October 2010, Guangdong Province produced 1.698 billion square meters of ceramic tiles, up 23.8% year-on-year, accounting for 25.7% of the national share, ranking first nationwide. Sanitary ceramic production was 31.01 million pieces, up 11% year-on-year, accounting for 25.7% of the national share, ranking second nationwide. Export of building ceramics increased by 41.3%, accounting for 71.3% of the national share. However, each production area will grow stronger due to resource advantages, regional advantages, and policy advantages, gradually eroding the market shares around their respective regions, especially the terminal markets in underdeveloped areas. To seize the market, competition between production areas will become more intense. As Gao'an, Faku, Jiajiang, Yichang, Tangshan, and other production areas gradually develop and mature, along with the formation of local sales platforms and exhibitions, they will form a surrounding posture towards Foshan.

8. Under the strong promotion of energy conservation and emission reduction by the state and increasingly stringent measures, the environmental protection and energy-saving awareness of ceramic enterprises has started to strengthen voluntarily. Under these conditions, the costs of ceramic enterprises will increase to a certain extent, leading to a group of non-brand enterprises facing either transformation or closure. According to statistical data, only in Foshan, more than 20 ceramic enterprises went bankrupt in 2010, including “Shengtaofang” which closed its doors at the end of November. Enterprises that persevere will gradually embark on the path of brand development. The entire ceramic production area will place greater emphasis on environmental protection construction, and environmental protection and energy saving will be the primary criteria for any enterprise wishing to establish itself.

9. With the official release of the "Chinese Retailer After-Sales Service Standard," the threshold for engineering bidding across the country will continue to rise, and manufacturers' after-sales service work will become more standardized and orderly. Non-standard manufacturers will face market penalties.

10. Upstream production enterprises will transition from independent operations into an era of complementary strengths, differentiation, and capital integration. Cross-border cooperation and corporate mergers and acquisitions will continuously emerge. The downstream market will see consolidation through alliances, forming an era of capitalization, chain operation, branding, informatization, and standardization of enterprise management. With the rise of large dealers, some provincial capitals will see merchants using capital to implement a retail chain model at the provincial level. Outstanding retailers in various cities, counties, and districts will become their acquisition targets. Consolidators will gain the favor of a large number of high-quality brands upstream due to their industry influence and strong market distribution capabilities. The promotion of various unified behaviors by consolidators will further refine the division of labor between production, supply, and sales.
