Video Made Before Jiangsu Father and Son Set Themselves on Fire; Town Government Admits No Demolition Permit

by pah201seg on 2010-04-16 11:27:06

■&；nbsp；"Jiangsu Donghai Demolition Self-Immolation Incident" Follow-up 　　Xinhua News Agency report &；nbsp；(reporter Cui Muyang) Yesterday afternoon, the news spokesperson of Donghai County Government issued a statement on the latest situation of the "villager casualty accident in Huangchuan Town", stating that the accident investigation and accountability would be conducted by a working group led by the County Supervision Bureau. Due to the improper handling of the sudden accident by the Huangchuan Town Government, the county party committee decided to suspend the position of Zhou Wenjun as town mayor.

The statement said that the burned elderly man, Tao Xingyao, received good treatment. The hospital invited burn experts from Beijing Jishuitan Hospital for consultation and formulated a scientific treatment plan. Currently, the patient's vital signs are stable, and the injury has been further controlled. According to the relatives' request, the Huangchuan Town Government specially rented two minibuses to take them to the county funeral home to view the bodies.

The statement also mentioned that in order to make the accident investigation and handling open and transparent, Donghai County specifically announced the name and contact information of the government news spokesperson.

■&；nbsp；Investigation

At around 7:00 am on March 27th, in Huangchuan Town, Donghai County, Lianyungang City, Jiangsu Province, when Hu Zhenxia stepped out of her house, inside the house, on the kang bed, her 92-year-old father-in-law Tao Xingyao and her 68-year-old husband Tao Huixi were chatting casually.

She went behind the house to feed the pigs. The pig farm currently had 80 pigs. This was the main source of income for the family.

More than half an hour later, all this was shattered.

At around 8:00, Tao Xingyao and his son resisted the demolition by the town government by setting themselves on fire at home.

After igniting the gasoline, the two elders resulted in one death and one injury, but this did not change the fate of the pig farm being demolished by the bulldozer.

The fruitless demolition event with "life-or-death struggle"

Before their self-immolation, Tao's father and son recorded a video as a memento. Eyewitnesses claimed that the people involved in the forced demolition also participated in the rescue.

Excavators and bulldozers surrounded the house

Li Sheng (a pseudonym), whose home is less than 20 meters away from the Tao family, witnessed everything before the Taos' self-immolation twice.

Li Sheng first went outside at 7:30, where he saw Tao's courtyard completely surrounded by people and vehicles.

Li Sheng remembered that at the south side of Tao's house, there were many town officials standing, while bulldozers, excavators, and sedans were parked on the north side. On both ends, security personnel in uniform were driving away the curious villagers.

"I knew that the town officials came for demolition, so I went to see what was happening, but I was shouted back by a police officer," Li Sheng said. Before going back into the house, he saw a group of people forcing a woman into a minivan.

The woman forced into the minivan was Hu Zhenxia, wife of Tao Huixi. "My mother said she was preparing to feed the pigs outside the house at that time," said Tao Qiuyu, Tao Huixi's son. The town officials took his mother into the car and drove her all the way to the neighboring county.

When Hu Zhenxia was taken away, Mu Ping (a pseudonym), who happened to come to Tao's pig farm to collect pig manure, arrived just in time. He saw that after Hu was taken away, Tao Huixi immediately closed the door. Then the town officials shouted at the door, but there was no response from inside.

At this moment, Li Sheng went out of his house for the second time, and he saw several town officials knocking loudly on Tao's front door.

Li Sheng said that the officials knocked on the door for two or three minutes without any response. Then a group of people went around to the pigpen behind the house and started catching pigs. At almost the same time, the bulldozer and excavator engines roared to life.

Li Sheng recalled Tao Huixi once saying: "They don't have a demolition permit; we will die rather than move."

Yesterday, the Huangchuan Town Government admitted they indeed did not have a demolition permit, and the law does not specify how to handle forced demolitions on such collective land.

It is understood that last year, the under-construction National Highway 310 in Donghai County was planned to pass through the Taoli Industrial Park, requiring the demolition of Tao Huixi's house and five other households nearby.

Huangchuan Town officials informed the owners that their buildings were illegal structures and ordered their removal. The owners appealed to Donghai County, where it was determined that the buildings were not illegal.

Multiple villagers confirmed that Tao Huixi repeatedly told the town officials, "If you bring the forced demolition procedures and court judgment, we will move immediately." However, "until Tao Huixi died, the town never brought the procedures."

"Men must protect their homes"

Looking back, gasoline was left in the house beforehand. "The gasoline was brought by my father. He heard that as long as gasoline was splashed around the house, the demolition people wouldn't dare to come," said Tao Qiuyu. His father and himself had said many times that men must protect their homes, and the 92-year-old grandfather also said the same.

Around 8:00, Tao Huixi set himself on fire, as well as his 92-year-old father who was guarding the house with him.

Yesterday, Wang Xianguang, the planner of Huangchuan Town, said that Tao Xingyao and his son committed self-immolation in a tightly closed room.

Wang Xianguang said he was about 5 meters away from the house at the time. He stated that Tao's father and son neither argued nor made a fuss; only the door opened slightly. Wang claimed that before Tao's father and son self-immolated, he approached the door "to try to persuade them."

He claimed that before he reached the door, the house caught fire. Immediately afterward, everyone scrambled to pry open the door and rescue the two elderly men who had self-immolated.

Wang Xianguang said that at that moment, 68-year-old Tao Huixi was already dead, and 92-year-old Tao Xingyao was on fire, his skin splitting apart.

While Wang Xianguang and the town officials were prying the door open, Mu Ping ran to the back of Tao's house. "The bulldozer, the excavator, and the people catching pigs stopped only when they saw the house was on fire," Mu Ping said. He heard someone inside shouting for help.

Mu Ping claimed that after Tao Huixi and Tao Xingyao self-immolated, the town officials carried out rescues. First, Tao Xingyao was rescued, followed by Tao Huixi, who was already charred black. Someone looked and said, "This person is beyond saving." Later, an ambulance first took Tao Xingyao away and then Tao Huixi.

What impressed Mu Ping deeply was that as soon as the people were taken away, the bulldozer immediately leveled Tao's house completely.

"Seizing Pigs Came Before Self-Immolation"

After Tao's father and son self-immolated, local official and public opinions drew two different conclusions regarding the cause of the victims' self-immolation.

The official stance believed that Tao's father and son self-immolated entirely due to their own one-sided thoughts.

"When asked why they self-immolated? I don’t know. I guess it might be because they saw me through the door crack," said Wang Xianguang, the planner of Huangchuan Town.

Wang Xianguang emphasized that Tao's father and son self-immolated very suddenly, without any prior warning, causing the town officials to be momentarily at a loss.

However, the Huangchuan Town Government stated yesterday that they were not conducting a forced demolition at the time but merely doing ideological work; the bulldozers and excavators on-site were from nearby construction sites.

Witnesses like Mu Ping and Li Sheng said that after the self-immolation occurred, the town officials used fire extinguishers to save people while simultaneously using bulldozers to demolish the house. More than 80 pigs were orderly transported away. Police officers maintained order at the scene.

The town government responded yesterday that they were protecting Tao's pigs at the time, fearing that if they were not managed properly, they could easily be lost.

"Tao Huixi owed a lot of money for raising pigs, and the compensation for the house was insufficient, leading to his inability to understand. It’s strange that he didn’t self-immolate when the pigs were seized," said an informed villager.

Self-immolation was preceded by videotaping

"In the matter of demolition, the government made the greatest effort to mediate, but the other side insisted on unreasonable demands," said an official from Donghai County on March 28. If Tao's father and son could "handle things according to the law," the tragedy could have been avoided.

But Tao Qiuyu claimed that the town government had not actively coordinated beforehand. Half a year ago, when notified about the demolition, there was even no mention of compensation. At that time, his father strongly resisted, "with a determination to die," which prevented the demolition.

He introduced that later, Huangchuan Town issued an evaluation report, stating a compensation of 75,000 yuan. His father Tao Huixi was very dissatisfied with this compensation, believing that the ground structures alone were worth more than 150,000 yuan. But the town officials said that the pig farm was not within the legal protection scope, "the government compensates whatever amount it decides."

Tao Qiuyu said that under the circumstances of unsuccessful negotiations, his father heard that the town government would come for forced demolition, so he prepared gasoline and, together with his 92-year-old grandfather Tao Xingyao, holding a liquefied gas cylinder, videotaped as a keepsake, saying he would "resist to the death."
