Juventus fans threw shoes at Balotelli, Inter Milan fans' fireworks injured Juventus fans

by kersfe3f23 on 2009-12-07 17:00:58

Juventus Fans Threw Shoes at Balotelli, Inter Milan Fans Hurting Juventus Fans with Firecrackers - By Zhang Kai

On the same night, two completely different matches unfolded. Milan played smoothly and naturally, showcasing the beauty of football, whereas the clash between Inter Milan and Juventus was more like a war drama, where football was merely a secondary seasoning. Amidst the flurry of red and yellow cards, fouls, elbow strikes, and verbal abuse, fans and players alike engaged in gang fights. Was this really the Derby d'Italia? When will Italy's top-tier matches deliver performances akin to Liverpool vs Chelsea's 4-4 or last week's pure and enjoyable Real Madrid vs Barcelona match?

No one is asking for representatives of defensive football to produce dazzling displays, but it's unacceptable to suppress the true face of football with muscles, fouls, and uncivilized warfare tactics, leaving only a faint trace of the game. The Milan Sports Daily questioned: "Is this our football?" Sacchi criticized: "Our stadiums are filled with violence; we take pride in destroying our opponents, reducing matches to just a few fragments amidst prolonged periods of deadlock and destruction, yet some people cheer for this. Is this how we convey the values of football?" While Catalan fans applaud intricate passing plays, Turin fans applaud precise positioning by defenders like Caceres and even dismissed Morini.

When Juventus returned to Serie A two seasons ago, both teams staged an attacking battle in the cup, with a 10-man Inter relying on Balotelli's brace to eliminate Juventus 3-2. Last season, Mourinho brought flair and excitement. Yet, the new edition of the Italian Derby seemed possessed by demons, transforming into a class struggle. Before the match, Mourinho said, "We need to pour water on the fire, not oil. So I instructed my players to remain silent in front of the media." Ferrara praised Inter, saying, "Their 8-point lead isn't accidental; they've made far fewer mistakes than us." Even before the match, Mourinho embraced Ferrara in front of Juventus' bench, breaking from the adversarial stance during Ranieri's tenure, exuding peaceful and friendly derby vibes that couldn't alter the situation.

Balotelli as the Powder Keg

The fierce battle stemmed from several instigators: Balotelli, Turin fans, referee Sacchi, Melo, and Motta, with all confrontations unfolding along these main lines.

Balotelli has become Juventus' nemesis, scoring 3 goals in his last 2 matches against them, making him a recurring nightmare. He is one of the few current Inter players to have scored at Juventus' home ground, apart from Eto'o. Last season, Juventus fans were banned from their home stadium due to racially charged chants directed at Balotelli, leaving a bitter legacy. Prior to this derby, Juventus fans chanted "Balotelli is dead" without provocation during away matches against Udinese and Bordeaux, resulting in a 20,000 euro fine and igniting a nationwide discussion on racism in Italian football. Inter even raised the possibility of boycotting the match.

Last Thursday, a scientific high school in Arezzo renamed itself "Balotelli-Cissokho High School" to promote anti-racism, with Balotelli's childhood idol and former Juventus player Gaetano Scirea's widow writing to the Turin Post to encourage fans to oppose racism and support Balotelli. Juventus president Blanc also distributed an initiative letter calling for "the civility of the Old Lady" among fans.

Preparation was one thing, reality another. True to form, Turin fans pulled out all the stops. Two hours before the Inter bus arrived at the Olympic Stadium, fans welcomed them with eggs and insults, escorted by police onto the field. At 7:30 PM and 8:10 PM, during pre-match preparations, the stadium announcer twice attempted to drown out the "buu" chants and "Balotelli is dead" cries with fair play slogans, to no avail. During the match, the announcements ceased altogether.

At halftime, while Balotelli warmed up, numerous shoes were thrown at him accompanied by boos. Black-and-white portraits of Balotelli appeared in the stands, inscribed with "Your black surpasses our black and white!" No direct racial slurs were used, so Inter never followed through on their week-long strike threat.

Juventus fans primarily vented their hatred for Inter through Balotelli, inevitably leading to fan disturbances. Both sides hurled objects at each other, resulting in 13 people being sued by the police post-match. One Juventus fan was injured by a firecracker thrown by an Inter supporter, while another was arrested for attempting violence.

Even Buffon Joined the Fray

Balotelli refused to stay quiet, confronting Juventus fans directly. His first touch after entering the field involved a physical altercation with Caceres along the sideline, causing Caceres to fall. Balotelli turned around and accused Caceres of diving, telling him to stop pretending. Sissoko intervened, offering solidarity to Balotelli.

In the final five minutes, Balotelli committed a foul while chasing Melo, who then angrily elbowed Balotelli in the chest from behind, prompting a confrontation between players from both sides. Subsequently, Chivu headbutted Sissoko in retaliation for Balotelli.

While Turin fans' deliberate attacks on Balotelli were inappropriate, Balotelli failed to demonstrate the moral integrity expected of professional athletes, exaggerating his actions to an unbearable degree. Goal-scorer Marchisio remarked: "I'm friends with Mario, and I wish him well. Everyone makes mistakes, and Mario made one today by falling to the ground after just a couple of collisions. It was foolish."

Cannavaro had earlier stated that some fan chants were expressions of frustration rather than racism, needing to be addressed differently, hinting at disdain for Balotelli. During the incident involving Melo's elbow strike on Inter's black player, Cannavaro did not step in to calm Melo, tacitly approving the action. Buffon displayed leadership by stepping in to mediate but was provoked by Motta's excessive words, leading to a shoving match. Buffon, like Del Piero, is typically a model of sportsmanship and a peacemaker, but his involvement as captain highlights Juventus' underlying emotions towards Inter and the match's loss of control.

Melo received his first red card of the season, having accumulated 12 yellows and 3 reds last season at Fiorentina, known for his fiery temperament. This time, incited by the fans, he made a grave mistake. Both Melo and Chivu, who headbutted someone, could face additional suspensions for their aggressive behavior.

After the match, Mourinho stayed in the locker room until 11:05 PM, ignoring reporters at the press conference, and later, accompanied by marketing advisor Oriali, bypassed the press conference to head straight to the bus. The Milan Sports Daily described him as "grim-faced, angry about the red card and also about the penalty Inter deserved but didn't get."
