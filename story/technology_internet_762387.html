Who says we're dull and lack sex appeal? Seven reasons to choose big companies

by anonymous on 2013-07-24 15:15:12

　　English Original: 7 Reasons Why You Need To Work For A Big Company

　　This article was written by Mark Stephen, the founder and CEO of IDR Solutions.

　　Large companies are often seen as slow and lacking in excitement, while startups are perceived as agile, efficient, and full of fun. I am currently running a startup, but to be honest, I am grateful for the memorable time I spent working at a large company. Below are the reasons why I find working for a big company advantageous:

　　1. Witnessing Excellence and Absurdity

　　In large companies, you will encounter both brilliance and nonsense under one roof. You will witness groundbreaking ideas about source code control and also experience long-winded meetings that demotivate employees. While big companies may not be as dynamic, they have their own reasons for growing and thriving. Even in the most ordinary organizations, there are bound to be talented individuals. Understanding the logic behind how large companies operate and identifying employees who excel in both technical expertise and political acumen can be a significant asset to you.

　　2. Working Alongside Talented Individuals

　　In certain departments of large companies, you will find clusters of intelligent people. In my opinion, collaborating with such talented individuals is incredibly rewarding, as it feels like the air is filled with wisdom. These smart people often choose large companies due to family and responsibility considerations, avoiding the risks associated with startups. Experienced core employees can teach you many things, whereas in startups, your colleagues are usually around your age and less experienced.

　　3. Leveraging Strong Networks

　　Twelve years ago, I worked for a large company. To this day, I still maintain contact with my former colleagues and frequently run into them in various places. This connection is very valuable. In today's era of social media, it remains crucial for people to know who you are and what you can do. Your employment history at a large company is documented, and when you need to conduct business with other large companies, this experience can provide you with a green channel.

　　4. Enjoying Excellent Benefits

　　I miss the canteen and gym at large companies, as well as that summer in Amsterdam... Company-funded Oracle training courses, fully reimbursed MBA programs, these perks were truly enjoyable! In contrast, startups might only reimburse you for the cost of books. Would you rather spend someone else's money to gain knowledge at a large company or work 100 hours a week at a startup chasing a distant dream?

　　5. Mastering Political Skills

　　At large companies, you learn not only technical skills but also "diplomatic" and "political" skills. When you transition from being an employee to a partner of a large company, these skills will come in handy. Large companies tend to buy products and services generously, and understanding their operational rules can help you conduct business more effectively and uncover more potential clients through them.

　　6. Embracing a Slower Pace

　　Startups have a fast-paced, meteor-like rhythm, whereas large companies often move at a slower pace. Although it may lack some of the excitement, it provides more leisure time for learning, recharging, and self-reflection. Sometimes, this "slower pace" can have unexpected benefits. For example, a major media company once avoided spending millions on a ridiculous internet project simply because its decision-making process was too slow, allowing the dot-com bubble to burst first...

　　7. Staying True to Your Principles

　　When you first enter the workforce, you are full of ambition and fearlessness. After experiencing the ups and downs of working at a large company, you will understand what is worth doing and what is not, realizing that some things are crucial while others are trivial. You will also discover what your lifelong dreams are and what your unbreakable principles are. Clarifying your professional creed can help you perform better.

　　These are the reasons why I enjoy working for large companies. What are yours?
