by q78220q81 on 2011-11-01 11:39:13

New Express - By reporter Qiuling Li and intern Lingli Huang: In pursuit of convenience, a woman forged the seal of a relevant unit and stamped it on her "Residency Application Form" when applying to transfer her blue-print hukou (household registration) to a permanent Guangzhou residency. She was caught red-handed at Suizhi Yue Hot Spring. Recently, the Tianhe District Court made its first-instance verdict on this case.

After investigation, the court found that on August 4, 2004, the defendant Wu registered as a blue-print hukou holder in Tianhe District, Guangzhou, and on October 14, 2009, she obtained an unmarried and childless certificate for floating population at her place of household registration. On July 20, 2010, in order to process the transfer from blue-print hukou to permanent residency, Wu paid RMB 100 to have a state organ seal of the Population and Family Planning Office of a street office in Tianhe District forged. On July 28 of the same year, Wu was discovered by police officers and arrested while carrying the forged-seal "Residency Application Form" and the counterfeit seal to the Tianhe District Public Security Bureau to process her residency application.

The court ruled that Wu's actions constituted the crime of forging state organ seals and deserved punishment. Consequently, Wu was sentenced to four months of detention, with a one-year reprieve.
