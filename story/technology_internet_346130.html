Starting control of three-phase asynchronous motor

by sycaibo on 2010-04-15 16:06:19

10KW and below capacity three-phase asynchronous motors usually adopt full-voltage starting, which means that when starting, the stator winding of the motor is directly connected to an AC power source at rated voltage. When the motor capacity exceeds 10KW, due to the large starting current, reduced-voltage starting is generally used. Reduced-voltage starting refers to lowering the voltage applied to the stator winding during startup. After the motor starts, the voltage is restored to its rated value, allowing it to operate under rated voltage. Reduced-voltage starting can reduce the starting current, decrease line voltage drop, and thereby minimize the impact on the line during startup. However, the electromagnetic torque of the motor is proportional to the square of the stator terminal voltage, so the starting torque of the motor correspondingly decreases. Therefore, reduced-voltage starting is suitable for no-load or light-load startup. Methods of reduced-voltage starting include star-delta reduced-voltage starting, autotransformer reduced-voltage starting, soft starting (solid-state reduced-voltage starter), extended delta reduced-voltage starting, and stator series resistance reduced-voltage starting. The most commonly used are star-delta reduced-voltage starting and autotransformer reduced-voltage starting. Soft starting is a modern motor control technology that is being promoted in some situations, while the other two starting methods are rarely used.

### One. Star-Delta Reduced-Voltage Starting Control

For normal operation, the stator winding is first connected in a star configuration. When the motor speed approaches the rated speed, the stator winding is switched to a delta configuration, and the motor enters normal operation under full voltage.

Circuit working principle: Close the power switch Q, press the start button SB2, KM2, rotary valve, KT, and KM3 coils are simultaneously powered and self-locked. The three-phase stator windings of the motor are connected in a star configuration and connected to the three-phase AC power source for reduced-voltage starting. When the motor speed approaches the rated speed, the time-delay type time relay acts, the normally closed contact of KT opens, and the KM3 coil is de-energized and released; at the same time, the normally open contact of KT closes, and the KM2 coil is energized and self-locked, connecting the winding in a delta configuration for full-voltage operation. After KM2 is energized and closed, it prevents the time relay from operating continuously. The normally closed contacts of KM2 and KM3 are interlocking contacts to prevent simultaneous connection in star and delta configurations, which could cause a short circuit in the power supply.

### Two. Autotransformer Reduced-Voltage Starting Control

Motor autotransformer reduced-voltage starting involves connecting the primary side of the autotransformer to the grid, and during startup, connecting the stator winding to the secondary side of the autotransformer. This way, the motor obtains voltage from the secondary side of the autotransformer during startup. When the motor speed approaches the rated speed, the stator winding is then connected to the grid, i.e., the rated voltage of the motor, entering normal operation. This reduced-voltage starting method is suitable for no-load or light-load starting of larger capacity motors, and the starting torque can be obtained by changing different shaft heads.

Circuit working principle: Close the main circuit and control circuit power switch, HL1 lamp lights up, indicating normal power supply voltage. Press the start button SB2, KM1 and KT coils are simultaneously powered and self-locked, connecting the autotransformer, and the motor is supplied with reduced voltage from the secondary voltage of the autotransformer. At the same time, the indicator light HL1 goes out, and HL2 lights up, showing that the motor is undergoing reduced-voltage starting. When the motor speed approaches the rated speed, the time-delay closing contact of the time relay KT closes, causing the KA coil to be powered and self-locked. Its normally closed contact opens the KM1 coil circuit, and the KM1 coil is de-energized and released, removing the autotransformer from the circuit; another pair of normally closed contacts of KA opens, and the HL2 indicator light goes out; the normally open contact of KA closes, causing the KM2 coil to be powered and closed, applying the full power supply voltage to the motor stator, and the motor enters normal operation under rated voltage. At the same time, the HL3 indicator light lights up, indicating the end of reduced-voltage starting. Since the current in the star-connected part of the autotransformer is the difference between the primary and secondary currents of the autotransformer, the auxiliary contact of KM2 is used for connection.

### Three. Start Control of Three-Phase Wound Rotor Asynchronous Motors

The rotor winding of a three-phase wound rotor asynchronous motor can be connected through a copper ring via brushes to external circuit resistances, reducing the starting current, improving the power factor and starting torque of the rotor circuit, making it suitable for heavy-load starting.

According to the different types of devices connected in series during the starting process of the wound rotor, there are resistor-starting and frequency-sensitive variable resistor-starting circuits.
