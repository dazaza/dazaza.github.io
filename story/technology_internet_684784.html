Weibo becomes a new weapon for recruitment in the workplace

by qingian007 on 2012-03-01 10:32:13

In January this year, Long finally left us and ushered in a vibrant March. Every year at this time is the busiest season for job seekers and employers. From the perspective of job seekers, after the Spring Festival, there are opportunities just around the corner: you can leave your original company [without complaints or regrets] after receiving a substantial year-end bonus.

From the perspective of recruiters, after the peak of business sales and the rest of the Spring Festival, they will undoubtedly have a clearer understanding of their human resource needs.

In March this year, the recruitment festival has arrived. As a general rule, most job seekers and employers expect to exchange jobs through professional recruitment websites such as Boleduo. These vertical recruitment websites provide users with very professional recruitment class information. For job seekers, talent recruitment websites offer a series of complete services from personal career planning to resumes; for recruiters, these websites help them easily segment industries and accurately obtain information about talents.

It can be said that traditional recruitment websites have obvious advantages. They continuously improve services and support in all aspects. In a society where only the fittest survive, how can one gain more resources and advantages than others or other competitors? In today's rapidly developing era of social networking and internet models, finding the most effective channel among the latest waves of job fairs can yield unexpected results!

The internet is no longer limited to recruitment websites. With increasingly rich internet applications, a new communication platform has been established, thus completing a new hunting and recruitment process.

Liu, the CEO of a website, usually has the habit of playing Weibo and can be considered a celebrity on Weibo. Regarding his company's construction, Liu has some ideas of his own. He hopes to strengthen the construction of the website content and find a "News Director" for the website, which has become an inevitable choice. However, selecting candidates or hiring through headhunters is a lengthy process and also costs a small amount of money. Liu feels that there are many users on Weibo with unique viewpoints, so the concept of hiring a News Director was born.

Liu posted the recruitment information on Weibo, sharing his thoughts under his real name while clarifying the requirements. Soon, due to Liu's authority in mobile phone real-name system and his influence and status, the recruitment for Liu's "News Director" was widely spread, and the number of people interested grew rapidly. Liu received a large amount of employment information daily on Weibo. Not only did he acquire so many job seekers for free, but he could also communicate and understand these job seekers in real-time via Weibo. More importantly, Weibo records allowed Liu to clearly know all past comments and industry views of the job seekers, thus enabling him to evaluate them more accurately and objectively.

Through Weibo, besides unexpectedly recruiting talents, the recruitment information increased the fame and recognition of Liu's website further. The idea of adding a News Director position to the website received much praise in the industry, and Liu's demeanor was also applauded. This Weibo recruitment was a "bountiful harvest" for Liu.

Advantages: Weibo recruitment has advantages such as high-speed transmission and precise targeting. It also allows real-time communication and understanding of candidates, eliminating geographical and time factors. Communication on Weibo is easier and more direct.

Disadvantages: The uncontrollable nature of Weibo transmission, difficulty in grasping direction and development trends, and the authenticity not being accepted by most users.

Fair search and filtering on online recruitment platforms, suppliers post [warehouse manager] job demands on the platform. The monthly salary treatment for the warehouse manager is usually 3000 - 5000, plus five insurance fees, scholarships, accommodation, and other welfare benefits should be complete. Faced with such favorable conditions, it's inevitable that people will question the authenticity of the recruitment information. Issues regarding information security on classified recruitment websites receive slight attention, but through in-depth understanding and observation, market-business license verification, detailed false information reporting buttons, and other safety measures are available so that job seekers can feel more assured.

Finally, the network found a warehouse manager who was quite satisfied with the income, and students also found part-time jobs on professional recruitment websites during the same period.

Advantages: Classified websites already have clear traffic resources, attracting different users to browse. Recruitment on classified sites is completely free and simple and convenient to operate. Searchers do not need to pay any fees for candidates and can even search by company scale, job type, remuneration, or workplace to find accurate and efficient matches before the first interview, effectively improving recruitment efficiency.

Disadvantages: A large amount of information without strict review on some classified websites may lead to issues with the security of recruitment information.

[Hunting] and [Hunting] Insights from More Social Networks

Tao is a technical staff member of a company, often engaging in technical discussions within social networks, making him quite famous in digital technology circles. On Pre-Owned Tank 681006, Tao frequently helps some "newbies" solve technical problems and participates in discussions and research on technical issues. A headhunter from a company "roams" quietly through the technical circle on social networks, posing as a "newbie," engaging in "chatter." The "newbie" often asks Tao technical questions and bothers him with "curious" questions. Tao does not refuse the "newbie's" requests and provides detailed assistance in solving their problems.

After discussing with the "newbie" and eventually gaining their "support," Tao discovers that the penname "newbie" was actually a headhunter tasked with finding a technical director for a multinational company. Understanding the skills and professional ethics required for recruitment, the headhunter chose to cut into the social network, deeply investigate target maps, and then understand the intentions. Career planning advice came to Tao, and the headhunter successfully convinced Tao to accept the position with generous treatment in the multinational company, allowing him to "retire" successfully.

Advantages: Social networks in circles usually gather a similar number of hobbies or technologies, making communication and exchange more convenient and direct. Exchanging in circles better reflects the professional skill level and emotional intelligence of a series of important positions, making it more suitable for large enterprises.

Disadvantages: It is difficult to timely communicate and understand objects through social networks and improve efficiency.
