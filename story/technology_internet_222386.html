Which range do the three concepts of population density, unit cost, and average score belong to: relative indicators or average indicators?

by lk0cswk on 2009-08-13 16:39:53

In the first statistics class, the teacher raised the above question. The whole class discussed it for an entire period but couldn't find an answer. Which ones belong to relative indicators and which ones belong to average indicators? What is the difference between the two? I hope that knowledgeable individuals can provide more guidance!

First, let's understand the related concepts:

1. Concept and form of expression of relative indicators: Relative indicators are also known as statistical relative numbers. They are the ratios of two interrelated phenomenon values, used to reflect the development level, structure, intensity, prevalence, or proportional relationship of phenomena. In statistical analysis, the use of relative indicators allows us to better understand the relationships between phenomena, making it possible to compare phenomena that cannot be directly compared. A relative indicator is a comprehensive indicator that uses comparative methods to reflect the degree of numerical connection between certain related things in social and economic phenomena, expressed in the form of relative numbers. Relative indicators can reflect the mutual relationships between phenomena, illustrate the quality of total phenomena, economic benefits, and economic strength, and by using relative indicators, originally incomparable quantitative relationships can become comparable, facilitating comparative analysis of the subject under study.

Since relative indicators use comparative methods to reveal the degree of connection between phenomena and reflect the degree of difference between phenomena, whether the numerator and denominator indicators have comparability when calculating relative indicators is an important condition for whether the calculation results can correctly reflect the quantitative relationships between phenomena.

The comparability of numerator and denominator indicators mainly includes: whether the indicator content is appropriate; whether the overall scope is consistent; whether the calculation method is the same; and whether the measurement units are unified.

2. Types and calculations of relative indicators: The characteristics and calculation methods of various relative indicators are as follows:

(1) Structural relative indicators: Structural relative indicators are based on grouping the overall population, using the overall total as a comparison standard to calculate the proportion of each group's total to the overall total, reflecting the internal composition of the overall situation comprehensively.

Structural relative indicators = Total of each group (or part)/Overall total

Calculating structural relative indicators can reflect the internal structure of the overall situation and the type characteristics of phenomena.

(2) Proportional relative indicators: Proportional relative indicators are relative indicators comparing different parts within the overall population, used to analyze the proportional relationships and coordination balance status between different localities and groups within the overall range.

Proportional relative indicators = Value of one part of the overall population/Value of another part of the overall population

(3) Comparative relative indicators: Comparative relative indicators are relative indicators determined by comparing similar phenomenon quantities across different units, used to explain the imbalance degree of similar phenomena development across units within the same time frame, indicating the quantitative comparison relationship of similar objects under different conditions.

Comparative relative indicators = Indicator value of unit A/Indicator value of similar unit B

(4) Intensity relative indicators: Intensity relative indicators are contrasts between two total indicators of different natures but with some connections, used to indicate the intensity, density, and prevalence of one phenomenon developing within another. Its fundamental difference from other relative indicators lies in its not being a comparison of similar phenomenon indicators. Intensity relative indicators are expressed in dual measurement units, forming a compound number.

Intensity relative indicators = Total indicator of a certain phenomenon/Total indicator of another connected but different-natured phenomenon

The positions of the numerator and denominator in intensity relative indicators can be interchangeable, thus having positive and inverse indicators. When applying them practically, distinctions should be made from average indicators.

After mastering the concepts, functions, and calculations of several commonly used relative indicators, it is necessary to distinguish between different relative indicators.

Structural relative indicators use the overall total as a comparison standard, calculating the proportion of each group's total to the overall total, reflecting the internal composition of the overall situation comprehensively. For example: the proportion of workers of each trade among all workers. Proportional relative indicators are relative numbers comparing different parts within the overall population, used to analyze the proportional relationships and coordination balance status between different localities within the overall range. For example: the proportion of light to heavy industry. The distinction between proportional relative indicators and comparative relative indicators is: (1) Different contents of sub-items and denominators, proportional relative indicators are comparisons of indicator values of different components within the same overall population; comparative relative indicators are spatial comparisons of similar indicators at the same time. (2) Different explanations, proportional relative indicators illustrate the proportional relationships within the overall population; comparative relative indicators illustrate the imbalance degree of phenomenon development. Comparative relative indicators are relative numbers determined by comparing similar indicators across different units, used to illustrate the imbalance degree of similar phenomena development across units within the same time frame. For example: the average income of employees in area A is 1.3 times that of employees in area B. The main distinctions are: (1) Other various relative indicators belong to quantity comparisons within the same overall population, while intensity relative indicators, in addition to this, can also be contrasts between total indicators of two different but related natures belonging to different overall populations. (2) Differences in the form of calculation results. Other relative indicators are expressed as unnamed numbers, while intensity relative indicators are mainly expressed as named numbers. (3) When the positions of the numerator and denominator in calculating intensity relative indicators are swapped, positive and inverse indicators are generated, whereas other relative indicators do not have such distinctions.

(5) Degree of plan completion relative indicators: Degree of plan completion relative indicators are relative indicators used to check and supervise the execution of plans. They compare the actual completion numbers with the planned numbers within a certain time frame to observe the degree of plan completion.

Degree of plan completion relative indicators = Actual completion number/Planned number

This indicator has multiple calculation methods depending on the length of the planning period and the form of the planned task values, and distinctions need to be made during practical applications. The difference between the numerator and denominator in the formula represents the absolute effect of plan execution.
