The Crosstalk Chronicles tells the story of crosstalk artists living around Tianqiao in Beijing during the 1930s and 40s. Dou Tianbao, struggling to make ends meet in Beijing's Tianqiao area due to his family’s declining fortune, finds it hard to survive without true talent and connections amidst the turbulent times. With his charitable nature and sense of justice, Dou wins the affection of the artist Shi'er Hong, securing his lifelong love. After entering the entertainment world, Dou ventures into various art forms like Pingju opera, Peking Opera, storytelling, and eventually crosstalk. By chance, he switches to crosstalk, learns from veteran artists, diligently practices his skills, and ultimately becomes renowned in Beijing as "Little Wanrenmi."

In the drama, Zhang Nara and Li Yanbing play a pair of sisters who rely on each other until they meet Dou Tianbao, which drastically changes their lives. Guo Degang plays Dou Tianbao, the son of a major warlord fallen on hard times, facing numerous difficulties and setbacks in performing crosstalk. He not only suffers bullying from the tyrant Liang Dayuan portrayed by Yu Qian but also faces challenges from within the crosstalk industry.

Li Yanbing plays a girl singing Xihedagu in a theater troupe with many scenes opposite Guo Degang's Dou Tianbao. The Crosstalk Chronicles comprehensively displays the hardships, joys, and sorrows of street performers in the old society, revealing behind-the-scenes details of life on and off stage. It educates viewers about little-known aspects of the entertainment industry such as its rules and traditions, as well as the arduous journey of artists learning their craft. The show explains the necessity of oral teaching methods for preserving the traditional art of crosstalk.

The drama involves various art forms including crosstalk, drum music, operas, storytelling, magic, acrobatics, and wrestling, proving that crosstalk is a comprehensive art form. It portrays the subtle relationships between artists—admiration, rivalry, and love—and exposes the essential weaknesses of human nature. The dialogue is light-hearted and humorous, showcasing wisdom while the story structure is intricate, accurately depicting the quality of crosstalk performances and the skill levels of crosstalk artists. The overall style is lively, healthy, and intriguing, satisfying the audience's curiosity.

Dou Tianbao (played by Guo Degang) is an accomplished crosstalk artist known as "Little Wanrenmi." Having studied Pingju opera, Hebei Bangzi, Peking Opera, drum music, and finally crosstalk, Dou becomes the main performer in a small crosstalk troupe, gaining fame in Beijing. Despite having a flawed character shaped by his era, industry, and family background, Dou remains likable due to his charm and frequent entanglements in scandals. His kindness, intelligence, generosity, and outgoing personality help him maintain numerous friends in the entertainment world, allowing him to overcome adversities.

Liang Dayuan (played by Yu Qian) is a tyrant in Beijing, unpredictable in temperament, self-righteous, and somewhat narrow-minded. Once childhood companions with Dou Tianbao, their relationship turns competitive due to changes in their families. Despite appreciating Dou's artistry, Liang struggles to control Dou, often failing due to various reasons, leading to mixed feelings towards him. Liang's interest in entertainment culture leads him to mingle with many artists, eventually becoming Dou's biggest rival.

From childhood, Dou Tianbao and Liang Dayuan lived together; their fathers were powerful warlords. After Dou's father fell into misfortune and Liang's father retained influence, Dou became disheartened while Liang became a tyrant in Beijing. Dou enters the entertainment world, facing obstacles from the president of the Pingju Opera Guild. Though unsuccessful in pursuing Pingju opera, he meets his love, Shi'er Hong. Later switching to Hebei Bangzi, Dou never forgets his passion for Pingju opera. By chance, he learns storytelling from a veteran artist before being misled into crosstalk. Establishing his own school, Dou teaches apprentices and gains fame.

After successfully debuting in Tianjin with his apprentice Xia Chahu, Dou becomes widely recognized under the name "Little Wanrenmi," attracting large audiences. Reuniting with his fiancée in Tianjin, they confess their feelings. With her support, Dou's career flourishes. Returning to Beijing, Dou suggests renaming the original theater to Dexinyuan, arranging appropriate jobs for old friends, deeply touching former peers who then become his disciples. Performing the "Four Simulations" with unprecedented success, Dou's original partner returns. Persuaded by Little Wanrenmi to learn more, they begin rehearsing traditional programs like "Yellow Crane Tower." After reopening Dexinyuan, famous figures attend their first performance back in Beijing, achieving great success. Following the performance, Little Wanrenmi invites colleagues to confirm the status of their ancestor Dongfang Shuo, hosting a memorial ceremony. Dou Tianbao has made indelible contributions to the development of crosstalk, leaving a significant mark in its history.
