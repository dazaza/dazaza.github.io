Treatment for "Old Rot Legs," Beauty Without Compromise

"Old Rot Legs," also known as "Calf Ulcer Leg," is often referred to as "Pant Cuff Ulcer" or "Skirt Edge Ulcer." It is both a complication of varicose veins and superficial phlebitis, as well as a syndrome occurring in deep vein thrombosis.

"Old Rot Legs" commonly affects people who stand for long periods, walk with heavy loads, and have varicose veins in the lower limbs. The affected area is mostly characterized by chronic ulcers located on either side of the lower one-third of the tibia in the calf.

In the early stages, the initial symptoms of "Old Rot Legs" may only be inflammatory exudation or nodules that cause changes in appearance without any special discomfort. However, its most prominent feature is one word: rot. This kind of rot doesn't heal quickly; instead, it worsens over time and with the progression of the disease, repeatedly ulcerating and becoming deeper until amputation is necessary to save lives. Therefore, this condition should not be ignored or abandoned!

Patients suffering from "Old Rot Legs" endure much hardship because it is a chronic disease. Symptoms can progress from intermittent claudication to an inability to walk, causing unbearable pain when walking and even during rest. Severe cases can lead to sleepless nights due to intense pain. If the condition further develops, gangrene can occur in the lower limbs, particularly the feet, with wounds that refuse to heal. In severe cases, amputation may be required, leading to disability. In some cases, amputation surgery might be necessary to save the patient's life.
