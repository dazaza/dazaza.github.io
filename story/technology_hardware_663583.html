A real estate company in Yinchuan plans to use a fresh air system to create a new selling point for its property projects and is actively searching locally for companies capable of handling fresh air system engineering construction. However, there aren’t many such companies in Yinchuan, leaving the company’s management quite helpless. Meanwhile, Mr. Huang Hailong, General Manager of Yinchuan Qiling Floor Heating Engineering Co., Ltd., has recently been feeling a bit troubled: he sees the enormous business opportunity hidden in the fresh air system installation industry but isn’t clear how to seize it.

So, how should underfloor heating construction companies approach entering the fresh air system installation industry?

Experts suggest the following:

Choose the right fresh air brand.

Selecting the right fresh air brand is crucial for companies wanting to enter the fresh air system operation and installation business. This is because high-quality fresh air systems not only guarantee the quality of the project but also win user trust, thereby securing more projects for the company. Currently, there are over 300 brands of fresh air systems in China, including dozens of foreign brands such as Panasonic, Honeywell, Brown, Reebro-Bruyer, Hitachi, and Sharp. There are also many domestic brands like Mann瑞德 (Mannred), Midea, Gree, Haier, Aodo’ao, Yadu, Philips, and Wande'er. Faced with so many options, how should underfloor heating construction companies choose?

Experts point out that Chinese consumers tend to trust well-known brands like Panasonic, Aodo’ao, and Honeywell, which currently dominate half of the fresh air system market in China. However, since these brands have different characteristics and target different consumer groups, underfloor heating construction companies should choose based on their own advantages. For instance, high-end brands like Panasonic and Honeywell mainly target large public buildings, while brands like Aodo’ao focus on home renovation projects. Therefore, companies with extensive public building resources should partner with Panasonic or Honeywell, while those aiming for the home renovation market should choose brands like Aodo’ao.

Apply for appropriate construction qualifications.

It is common knowledge that the qualifications required for underfloor heating system construction differ from those needed for fresh air system construction. Thus, underfloor heating construction companies must first obtain the necessary qualifications to enter the fresh air system construction field. However, our survey found that most underfloor heating construction companies have little understanding of the qualifications required to enter the fresh air system engineering field.

In fact, the national "Construction Enterprise Qualification Grade Standard" (hereinafter referred to as the "Qualification Grade Standard") has already specified the qualifications required for enterprises engaged in fresh air system installation. According to the "Qualification Grade Standard," enterprises undertaking boiler, ventilation air conditioning, refrigeration, electrical, instrumentation, motor, compressor set, and broadcasting movie and television control equipment construction must possess the professional contracting enterprise qualification for mechanical and electrical equipment installation, and fresh air systems fall under the ventilation air conditioning category specified by the "Qualification Grade Standard." Therefore, if underfloor heating construction companies want to engage in fresh air system construction, they must first possess the professional contracting enterprise qualification for mechanical and electrical equipment installation. In reality, most underfloor heating construction companies do not yet have this qualification. Thus, obtaining the professional contracting enterprise qualification for mechanical and electrical equipment installation will be a significant hurdle for many underfloor heating construction companies seeking to enter the fresh air system engineering field.

Master specialized construction techniques.

Underfloor heating systems are installed under the floor, while fresh air systems are usually installed at the top of the room via suspended mounting. These two systems have significant differences in construction methods. Therefore, whether underfloor heating construction companies can smoothly enter this industry depends greatly on their ability to master the relevant technical skills for fresh air system installation.

So, what should be considered when installing fresh air systems? Cai Yijin, Sales General Manager of Shanghai Brown Environmental Technology Co., Ltd., suggests that when designing a fresh air system, first, the principle of introducing fresh air from cleaner areas and expelling it from dirtier areas should be maintained. Generally, dirty air should be expelled from bathrooms, toilets, and kitchens, while fresh air should be introduced into living rooms and bedrooms. If conditions allow, try to follow the principle of air entering low and exiting high (i.e., fresh air enters the room from a lower position, and indoor exhaust exits from a higher position). The intake and exhaust vents of the fresh air system should ideally not be on the same plane; maintaining an opposing relationship is better. Second, the minimum exhaust volume inside the house should meet the daily life, work, and rest requirements of people for fresh air. According to national ventilation standards, each person must ensure access to 30 cubic meters of fresh air per hour. At the same time, when installing a fresh air system, the layout of the pipes should minimize the loss of airflow. Additionally, the ceiling height requirements of the owner must be met. If wall penetration drilling is required, the structural integrity of the walls should be checked to ensure that the overall structure of the room is not compromised. It is understood that there are many other considerations during the installation of fresh air systems. Any oversight in any one of these considerations could affect the overall performance of the fresh air system. Qin Xiaoming, Vice President of Beijing Yateweida Cold and Warmth Technology Engineering Co., Ltd., revealed that although the construction of fresh air systems is not difficult, the challenge lies in integrating the fresh air system effectively with other systems in the homeowner's house, such as the air conditioning system, dust removal system, water purification system, etc., without damaging the interior decoration style. Although his company has been involved in fresh air system engineering construction for more than five years, they still do not dare to take on too many fresh air system projects at once. Therefore, mastering all the related technical skills for fresh air system installation is another significant hurdle that underfloor heating construction companies aiming to enter the fresh air system engineering field must overcome.
