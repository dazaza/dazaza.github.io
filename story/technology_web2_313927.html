Milan Giant Recalls Moving Scenes from the Past Decade, Reveals He Didn't Dare Watch Shevchenko's Penalty in Champions League Final

Sina Sports News - At 1 PM on December 16, 2009, it was the tenth anniversary of the launch of AC Milan's official website channel. It was a decade filled with passion. Invited by Mauro Soma, the head of the Milan Channel, AC Milan vice president Adriano Galliani warmly recalled the birth story of the Milan Channel and the complementary experiences between AC Milan Club and the Milan Channel. Establishing a channel entirely dedicated to their own team, allowing AC Milan to enter into thousands of households among fans, was a long-thought-out conception by Berlusconi and Galliani.

With the advent of digital satellite broadcasting technology in 1999, this conception was finally realized. On December 16, 1999, the Milan Channel was officially born. Looking back, Galliani sincerely thanked all those who helped and realized this special television service that was first pioneered in Italy and opened up an entirely new field. As Mauro Suri played pictures, video clips, music, and recordings, Galliani affectionately recalled the various moments of joy, sorrow, happiness, and pain he had experienced during his time at AC Milan. Galliani stated that the power of images plays an indispensable role in documenting and showcasing AC Milan's classic charm. He said: "AC Milan is currently acquiring all audio-visual files related to its history. This is a long-term project, and we hope to eventually complete it one day." A series of important historical moments of AC Milan moved Galliani's emotions, such as AC Milan's second-round match against the Slovaks in the UEFA Champions League qualifiers during the 2002/03 season. Galliani said: "That was a special moment; I remember it clearly. The referee said there would be three minutes of stoppage time, but everyone thought it was four minutes based on his gesture. In the penultimate minute, the Slovak team launched a quick counterattack with a 2 vs 1 situation, and the referee blew the whistle because three minutes had already passed. There was a bit of controversy, but the referee still ended the game on time. If they had continued attacking, we might not have made it to Manchester."

Speaking of Manchester, the Milan Channel recorded the first UEFA Champions League final, playing a series of iconic and moving scenes from that night, including Shevchenko's decisive penalty goal. Galliani recalled with a smile: "I wasn't watching Sheva at that time; I was looking at our fan stands. I only knew Sheva had scored when I saw them celebrating. I watched the real goal replay on TV later. When I rewatched the footage of Sheva taking the penalty, for several months, I was still worried that he might miss it."

Looking back over the past decade, the Milan Channel has broadcast countless moments experienced by the team, featuring key figures from different eras, naturally not missing Kaka, who shared some of the most beautiful moments with Galliani. Recalling Kaka, Galliani said: "I really like Ricci. Let's let him continue his new life at Real Madrid, but he spent six extraordinary years with us. I want to thank Braida and Leonardo again; it was they who urged AC Milan to sign Kaka, even though we already had two attacking midfielders at the time. Despite this, we realized we had to sign Kaka, and indeed, we signed an excellent player. Ancelotti didn't know him before, but I remember after Kaka's first training session, Ancelotti called me and asked where we found him, saying he was truly an exceptional player."

Since November 2001, AC Milan, under the leadership of Ancelotti, began another unique and extraordinary period in the club's history. Recalling that period, Galliani said: "Incredibly, I thought he had already signed with Parma. After deciding to change coaches on Sunday evening, I called Carlo early the next morning, waking him up. He was about to go to Parma to sign the contract. I told him to stay home. He didn't know that I had already gotten into my car and was driving from Milan to his house to find him. I rushed to his house and told him to ignore the calls from Parma and sign with us."

As one precious video, image, and audio clip after another was played, Galliani emotionally spoke about Pato, Nesta, Dida, and Yokohama, recalling the moment in 2007 when AC Milan successfully avenged its loss and tactically defeated Boca Juniors to reclaim the top of the world. Without time constraints, Galliani could have continued to recall each moment of the past decade since the establishment of the Milan Channel for hours. From his words, people could feel his deep love for AC Milan. Galliani concluded emotionally: "Everything I've done, I've done out of endless love for AC Milan. Happy birthday to the Milan Channel, and happy birthday to our dear old friend Milan."
