Inky Jiangxi Wuyuan, a colorful spring guide to see the rape flowers in Wuyuan

by anonymous on 2012-03-02 10:23:40

The blossoms spread across the mountains and fields in full bloom, brimming with vitality and creating an overwhelming visual effect that brings joy and excitement.

These flowers, blooming passionately in the fields, have a short flowering period. However, their blooming process is unrestrained and exuberant, filled with a wild beauty. They are the grand chorus of life, the collective dance of life, and the long scroll painting of life. People standing before them are infected by their passion, and the hidden wildness in their hearts will also be awakened. This, perhaps, is the fundamental reason why people go out in groups to see the rape flowers in early spring.

Jiangxi Wuyuan is a world of ink and wash colors. It's the season of March when rape flowers are in full bloom. In Wuyuan, there are several best places to see rape flowers: one is Xiaqi, and the other is Jiangling. This time, Jiangling was not on our itinerary, so we specially visited Wuyuan's Jiangwan on the way to Xiaqi.

Wuyuan Jiangwan is located in a river valley surrounded by three mountains. To the east is Ling Mountain, to the south is You Mountain, and to the north is Houlong Mountain. A pear garden river flows from east to west, forming an S-shape as it passes through the three mountain valleys, blending water and mountains together, adding much charm to Wuyuan's Jiangwan.

It is said that the Jiang surname in Wuyuan Jiangwan are descendants of Xiao He, the founding prime minister of the Western Han Dynasty. The architectural layout of Wuyuan Jiangwan is very distinctive, with one street and six alleys crisscrossing each other, orderly new and old, extending for a thousand years without disorder. Each alley has its own personality, no duplication, ancient and elegant, forming its own scene. Seen from Houlong Mountain, the central alley forms a huge "An" character.

The old street of Wuyuan Jiangwan still retains the style of the Ming and Qing Dynasties. The street is still narrow, with a few old shops like Peixin Hall, Yin Su Hall, and Risheng Hall. The shop doors are still made up of wooden planks, the shop interiors still have old counters and shelves, and the outer walls are still the ancient fireproof walls. Walking among them, we seem to be traveling through a time tunnel, still able to feel some of the commercial atmosphere of Wuyuan's ancient Jiangwan during the Ming and Qing Dynasties.

We can only admire the newly bloomed rape flowers in Wuyuan from across the river, golden patch after patch, extending in terraces. The flowers are closely clustered together, lifted by countless green branches, dancing drunkenly in the spring breeze. Around them, there are red peach blossoms, white pear blossoms, green willow buds, white walls of Huizhou-style residences, and black roofs. Such rustic scenery in Wuyuan would probably enchant any visitor.

The next day, we actually saw large areas of rape flowers again at Moon Bay in Wuyuan. But because we arrived early, the morning mist had not yet dissipated, and we could only view the rape flowers from afar across the river. The rape flowers seemed to be bowed down by the morning dew. Later, when we arrived at Likeng, we unexpectedly found a large area of rape flowers waiting for us. The plan was to see the Ming and Qing architecture, but when the group entered the village entrance, we were stunned and cheered after a few seconds.

In Wuyuan, we finally saw such dazzling gold under the strong sunlight, bursting forth with the most brilliant moment of life. That pure golden yellow is incredibly bright, even somewhat blinding. If you look closely at a single rape flower, it may not have a majestic or charming posture, nor the noble air of peonies, but the vast carpet woven from countless petals trembles excitedly before the tourists, and you cannot help but be enchanted by it.

I know that these rape flowers in Wuyuan are not planted by villagers for oil extraction, but rather designed as earth art to welcome and amaze tourists, the most natural and painstakingly crafted scenic spot. Therefore, they are arranged in terraces, and the stems are slightly taller than usual. Tourists can walk into the rape fields, let clusters of yellow flowers sway excitedly close to their chests, and then press the shutter button enthusiastically.

When the wind blows, the rape flowers happily tremble. An array of not too intense fresh fragrance tightly wraps around the tourists. It is a wild fragrance, an exposed and unashamed fragrance, a candid acceptance of sunlight, and an uninhibited emotional expression.
