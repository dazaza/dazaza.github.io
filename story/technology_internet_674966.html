### Five DIY Curtain Design Tips

#### Author: Eilun Dynasty [http://www.eilun.com/index.htm] &；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp； Website: www.eilun.com [http://www.eilun.com/index.htm]

#### Article Summary: Who says curtains [http://www.eilun.com/wenzhang/10002.htm] are only for windows? Curtains can also be used to decorate doors. Transparent glass doors can make a room bright, but they may compromise privacy. In such cases, simple sheer fabric becomes a stylish solution. It enhances the beauty of the space without obstructing light and protects privacy. Below, we introduce five DIY curtain design tips.

1. **Separately Controlled Arch Window Roller Shades** [http://www.eilun.com/index.htm]

Curtains for arch-shaped windows are typically divided into two parts—upper and lower—and roller shades are the best option. The upper roller shade is designed in an arc shape to match the arch and can be controlled independently. The lower part is divided into left and right sections, each of which can also be controlled separately.

2. **Customizable Corner Window Arc Curtains**

Some windows are not on a straight line or form a right-angled corner, or they might be curved bay windows. In these cases, even if the curtains are divided into several sections, the curtain rods must have angles to match the window's direction. The key for corner windows is the curtain rod. Since the windows have angles, avoid directly fixing the curtain rod to the wall as it may lack stability. Instead, fix brackets to the ceiling first, then attach the corner curtain rod to the brackets.

3. **Sloped Roof Window Draw Curtains (Top and Bottom Fixed)**

In the top level of duplex houses, sloped roof windows are common. Making curtains for such windows is both fresh and interesting. These windows are best suited for draw curtains that are fixed at both the top and bottom. First, thread curtain rings onto the curtain rod, then fix the rod to the wall using brackets. Finally, hang the curtains with hooks onto the curtain rings, completing the installation.

4. **White Floor-to-Ceiling Curtains**

Full-length floor-to-ceiling windows allow the maximum amount of sunlight into the room. However, due to seasonal changes or personal scheduling needs, you might want to adjust the brightness of the indoor light manually. In this case, designing double-layered curtains is a good solution.

5. **Zippered Curtains for Adjustable Light Control**

Full-length floor-to-ceiling windows allow the maximum amount of sunlight into the room. However, depending on the season or personal schedule, you might need to control the intensity of the light entering the room. Zippered curtains are ideal for this purpose. Simply connect the curtains with zippers at three-quarters of their length, then hang them on a dual-track curtain rod. This creates a double-layered curtain effect.
