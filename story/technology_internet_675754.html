What to Do If Your Phone Gets Wet - How to Handle a Water-Damaged Phone

If your phone is not a ruggedized model (anti-drop, waterproof, dustproof), then in daily use, it's hard to avoid situations such as getting wet in the rain, splashed with water, submerged in a sink, or even dropped in a toilet. So, what should you do if your phone gets wet? Let's take a look at how to handle a water-damaged phone.

First, don't panic when your phone gets wet. We can divide the situation into three categories:

1. Dropped into clean water while powered on.

2. Dropped into non-corrosive liquid while powered off.

3. Dropped into non-clean water or corrosive/sticky liquids, whether powered on or off.

The first two scenarios have a good chance of being salvaged with little to no after-effects, while the third scenario depends more on luck.

First and foremost, do not attempt to disassemble the phone recklessly just because it has gotten wet.

1. **Dropped into clean water while powered on**: This situation generally does not require disassembling the phone.

1. Turn off the phone as soon as possible.

2. Shake the phone vigorously to remove as much water as possible. Be careful not to drop the phone during this process. Pay attention to removing water from the screen; if water droplets remain on the screen, they may leave marks once dried.

3. Place the phone near a mild heat source like a desk lamp or spotlight to allow the moisture to evaporate slowly. This process will take over six hours. Do not use strong heat sources like a hairdryer.

After these three steps, the phone should be able to power on and function normally.

2. **Dropped into non-corrosive liquid while powered off**: In this case, the device is likely undamaged, but the LCD screen might look bad.

1. Shake the phone vigorously to remove as much liquid as possible, but be careful not to drop it.

2. If there are no visible marks on the LCD screen, follow step 3 from the first scenario.

3. If there are significant residues on the screen, do not follow step 3 from the first scenario, as this could result in functional electronics but an unsightly screen.

You can either send the phone to a nearby repair shop or disassemble it yourself to clean it.

3. **Dropped into non-clean water, corrosive liquid, or sticky liquid, whether powered on or off**: This is the most complicated scenario, but with proper handling, there is still a good chance of restoring the phone.

1. Turn off the phone as soon as possible.

2. If the phone was dropped into a corrosive liquid (such as seawater), immediately rinse the phone with clean water. Alternatively, submerge the phone in clean water to dilute the corrosive liquid inside.

3. Disassemble the phone as soon as possible. Take your time during this process.

4. Clean all components thoroughly with alcohol or professional cleaning solution.

5. Reassemble the phone. Alternatively, take all the disassembled parts to a repair shop for them to clean and reassemble the device.
