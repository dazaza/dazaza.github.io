One-Tail Shukaku

Mythology: Born in the Nara Desert, influenced by the magic of the same realm, buried and piled up by sandstorms, a raccoon monster that was born from the spirits of those who died in the desert trapped by wind and sand. Its true form is a raccoon. It has a playful nature and enjoys burying everything in front of it with majestic sand. Its white body is violet, symbolizing the pattern of the wind god.

Two-Tails Cat Yōkai

Residing in the Death Forest at the southernmost part of the Night Plains in the South Sea, a black cat demon with enormous raven wings. Originally an artifact of the God of Death, it later escaped through the three realms' gate to the human world, feeding on the souls of the dead. When there aren't enough souls, it kills people first to obtain the spirits of the dead. It can freely summon the spirits of the dead for battle and call forth demonic creatures from the underworld. The third eye in its forehead opens to devour the souls of the dead when activated.

Ancient 9 Divine Beasts Battle: Overconfident, believing no one could match it, allied with Peng Hou to defend against the Nine-Tailed Fox but suffered a crushing defeat. In the last moment, it was saved by the God of Death.

Three-Tails Ishi Wabi

A shark monster with a crescent-shaped horn on its forehead, living in the North-West sea area near the Shikoku Island in Western Japan, residing at the top of the Sugaha Trench in the Japanese Sea. Every few months, it surfaces to breathe. When Ishi Wabi surfaces, thunder and lightning gather, creating violent storms and huge waves, sinking all ships in the Japanese Sea as food. One of Ishi Wabi's underlings, a shark-like fish, actually a Shark Mechanism, sticks to Ishi Wabi's belly, responsible for cleaning leftovers and extracting food from Ishi Wabi's stomach, producing large amounts of chakra, which is then transferred into Ishi Wabi's body. The Shark Mechanism can extract five times more chakra than normal from food, and during Ishi Wabi's battles, this stored chakra is continuously transferred into Ishi Wabi's body.

Ancient Divine Beast War: Participated in 4 battles, 2 wins, 1 loss, 1 escape

When Ishi Wabi couldn't produce large amounts of chakra due to exhaustion, it was also sealed in the Shark Pot, which was then sealed on the Fire Altar.

Four-Tails Mouse Shark

Long-term resident near Mount Fuji, influenced by volcanic ash and volcanic chemical reaction toxic gases, formed by the fusion of Chicken Demon Spirit Ghost and Snake Demon Ma Gui. Within a hundred miles around where Mouse Shark resides, the atmosphere is completely devoid of grass and trees due to the spread of toxic gas, making it a highly toxic substance. Every few years, during Mouse Shark's hibernation on Mount Fuji, the toxic gases caused by its snoring lead to volcanic eruptions on Mount Fuji.

Five-Tails Peng Hou

A canine monster born from receiving partial powers of the Wind, Thunder, Water, Fire, and Earth altars. Its five tails can release five types of elemental strong attacks. It delights in destroying nature to seize the essence of natural element forces. After defeating Mouse Shark at the base of Mount Fuji, it became arrogant. After defeating a strong enemy Cat Yōkai in the ancient battlefield, it joined forces with Cat Yōkai to defend against the Nine-Tails, but the Nine-Tailed Fox's chakra quantity was infinite, and Cat Yōkai and Peng Hou couldn't keep up. Peng Hou managed to escape with wind but was heavily injured.

Six-Tails Thunder Beast

The Thunder God worshipped at the Thunder Altar since ancient times, turned into a demon under the influence of the evil power of the Eight Entertainment Great Serpent. Bloodthirsty, its six lightning-shaped tails can emit infinite voltage. It likes to release powerful lightning and rain during the day, destroying everything around it.

Ancient Divine Beast War: 5 battles, 2 wins, 3 losses

As a result, its power flowed back, and the Thunder Beast was injured by its own attack. It was then sealed in the Thunder Altar by Lord Sarutobi using the "Divine Instrument Present Lightning Circle."

Seven-Tails Dog-Like Beast

The most cunning Tailed Beast, not very large in size (note: the footnote says it's 17 meters tall), skilled in disguise, rarely appears on the ground, usually digs tunnels underground. It waits until prey is directly above it, then destroys the soil beneath the prey, causing it to fall directly into its mouth. It likes to attack merchants, transforming into various shapes each time it comes to the surface, making it hard to distinguish. It cannot transform in places without clay, and once in danger, it changes color and escapes.

Ancient Divine Beast War: 4 battles, 1 win, 3 escapes

Eight-Tails Eight Entertainment Great Serpent

Japan's strongest S-class monster since ancient times, an eight-headed and eight-tailed massive serpent possessing the power of the netherworld, the source of dark and light powers, representing good and evil. The eight heads represent "Soul, Ghost, Good, Demon, Evil, Slaughter, Spirit, Death." Although its inherent dark power was originally inferior to the elemental force of Five-Tails Peng Hou and the death force of Two-Tails Cat Yōkai, the reckless ancestors of the Soshigeya clan misused their clan's divine artifact, the Soshigeya Sword, to forcibly kill the Great Serpent. As a result, the Great Serpent concentrated all its dark and light powers in its neck, preventing the sword from fully severing it. The astonishing regenerative ability of the Eight-Tailed Beast absorbed the Soshigeya Sword into its body, turning it into a superpower within itself, allowing it to surpass even the powerful foes like Cat Yōkai and Peng Hou. Arrogantly, the Great Serpent believed the Nine-Tails was no match for it, thus gaining control over the forces of good and evil across the three realms. To unify the three realms, it emitted powerful evil forces, breaking the seals of the severe Thunder, Lightning, Fire, Earth, and Wind altars, releasing the Sand Shukaku, Thunder Beast, Fire Nine-Tailed Fox, Water Ishi Wabi, and Earth Dog-Like Beast to harm the human realm. The evil aura even startled the three monsters, Mouse Shark, Cat Yōkai, and Peng Hou, residing far in the west. However, the eight monsters refused to obey the Eight Entertainment Great Serpent, leading to the prolonged Ancient War of the Nine Great Divine Beasts lasting 522 years. Despite its overwhelming magical power defeating many beasts, it underestimated the strength of the Nine-Tails among the nine great divine beasts.

Nine-Tails Nine-Tailed Fox

The Nine-Tailed Fox is the strongest of the nine divine beasts. Its strength mainly lies in its chakra production, differing from other Tailed Beasts (One-Tail, Seven-Tail, Four-Tail, Six-Tail consume humans to create chakra; Two-Tail, Eight-Tail use inter-realm forces; Three-Tail uses the helper Ban Fish Shark Mechanism; Five-Tail absorbs the essence of nature). The Nine-Tails does not need external assistance to produce chakra. The fire god seal on the Nine-Tails' body automatically creates chakra, making its chakra infinite and giving it excellent endurance in prolonged battles. During the battle with the Eight-Tails, although their strengths were similar, after a hundred years of continuous fighting, the Eight-Tails' chakra ran out first and was defeated by the Nine-Tails. The Nine-Tails' nine tails possess immense energy, and their simultaneous movement can cause hurricanes. Combined with its fur, it can shoot chakra like meteors, raining destruction. In the Ancient War of the Nine Great Divine Beasts, it repeatedly defeated strong opponents. Stronger Tailed Beasts like Mouse Shark and Shukaku dared not approach the ancient battlefield. The Fire Altar is the head of the five great altars. Besides the water seal's chakra generator, it is said to have two unique special skills. Affected by the dark wave of the Eight Entertainment Great Serpent, the Nine-Tails was awakened from the Fire Altar. The Nine-Tails is the most intelligent divine beast with human wisdom, unlike others such as Shukaku and Peng Hou whose sole purpose is killing. The Nine-Tails aims to find the creature that awakened it and kill the ninjas who originally sealed it. However, the Eight Entertainment Great Serpent never revealed that it was the one who awakened the Nine-Tails until its defeat.
