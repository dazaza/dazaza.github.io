A document jointly issued by six departments

On April 22, the Publicity Department of the Luohe Municipal Committee, the Civilization Office, the Municipal Directly-Administered Work Committee, the Federation of Trade Unions, the Youth League Committee of the city, and the Women's Federation, among other six departments, issued a document titled "Notice on Deeply Carrying Out Activities to Read 'Shuowen', Recognize Traditional Characters, and Interpret Chinese Characters" throughout the city (hereinafter referred to as the "Notice"). After being widely communicated and forwarded, this Notice caused much discussion in various circles in Luohe.

"A few days ago, I had a terrible headache. After my child came home at night, he was by my side the whole time, serving tea and medicine. He told me that 'when parents are sick, children should taste the medicine first, attend day and night without leaving the bedside,' as stated in 'Disciple Rules'. This is what he should do, and these words moved me to tears!" A parent of a student from Luohe No. 2 Middle School told reporters, "Since the classic recitation activities began, the child has become more sensible and understands what love and humility mean."

One stone stirs up thousands of waves. Subsequently, the successful practice of classic education like "reading millennium beautiful articles and becoming young gentlemen" quickly entered families, government agencies, communities, enterprises, and all sectors of society, and was praised as the "small hand pulling big hand, children leading parents" experience. Many kindergartens have carried out "one-to-ten" recitation projects: letting one child lead ten family members, including parents and grandparents, to recite together; many activities even included scenes of kneeling before parents or mothers-in-law...

However, some people hold opposing views. "My child just started junior high school. Letting him read 'Shuowen', can he understand it? The country does not require mandatory learning of traditional characters," said Mr. Zhang, a citizen of Luohe. "We shouldn't forcibly instill 'zhī hū zhě yě' into children!" Ms. Liu, another citizen, said, "Nowadays, children already have heavy academic burdens. Making them learn those dry and difficult classical Chinese texts, I don't approve." Another parent expressed concern that some classic ancient texts contain negative ideas, which could be detrimental to children with immature minds.

"Don't understand 'Shuowen' and traditional characters means not loving Luohe?" Some employees believe that they can "advocate and call" for learning, but cannot require "recognizing at least 500 traditional characters and knowing their shapes, sounds, and meanings."

The second focus of heated discussion is: Should this activity be linked to primary and secondary school students? The Notice requires: Education administrative authorities should promote 'Shuowen' into campuses, textbooks, and classrooms; organize schools to use media such as broadcasts and bulletin boards to publicize 'Shuowen' and Xǔ Shèn culture; utilize themed class meetings and Chinese classes to organize all students to recognize traditional characters, guide junior and senior high school students to read 'Shuowen' and interpret Chinese characters; build Xǔ Shèn Primary School into a base for inheriting Xǔ Shèn culture.

Statements

"Classic culture entering campuses does not follow a boring mode, nor will it increase students' academic burden. In previous promotion activities, there were never any stipulations about how many classes must be taught or how many meetings one must attend, and it would never be tied to the performance of teachers and students or the evaluation of employees!" Xie Anshun said that classic training can be effective and welcomed if fragmented time is cleverly utilized well and interesting forms are organized. "For example, everyone spends 10 minutes each morning reciting a 100-character text segment, persisting for three to five years, one could memorize over 100,000 characters of classics. What role does this play in cultivating moral character and refining sentiments?" Xie Anshun said that these classics are carefully selected, and many schools have adopted lively and interesting educational methods, making learning and reading engaging.

Regarding the controversy caused by this matter in society, Xie Anshun, member of the Standing Committee of the Luohe Municipal Committee and Minister of Propaganda, expressed "it was expected, journalists may proceed with the report," and explained some of the "operational techniques" of the activity.

Explanation

The first focal point of heated discussion is: Should we go to great lengths to require everyone to recognize traditional characters? The Notice requires in the "Activity Goals": each person should read 'Shuowen Jiezi' once; each person should recognize at least 500 traditional characters; each person should know the shapes, sounds, and meanings of at least 500 commonly used characters. In the "Methods and Measures": Party and government agencies at all levels should treat this activity as an important part of building a learning-oriented party organization; use central group study, full staff meetings, etc., to organize cadres and masses to read 'Shuowen'; through visiting the Xu Shen Memorial Hall, organizing "Read 'Shuowen', Love Hometown" speech competitions, etc., to improve the participation of cadres and masses.

Supporters and opponents both exist

Currently, more than 140 primary and secondary schools in Luohe City have carried out Chinese cultural classic recitation activities, educating over 400,000 students, making daily recitation of classics a prevailing trend. "The enormous educational function of Chinese classics lies in improving the cultural quality of the entire nation," Xie Anshun said. Conducting classic recitation activities in Luohe City not only enhances the ideological and moral qualities of minors but also improves the ideological and moral standards of all citizens in the city. Research shows that reciting classics not only enhances cultural cultivation but also fosters filial piety, love, sincerity, and good behavioral habits.

Tracing the origin

It is understood that the "classic education" activities organized by Luohe's official bodies have been ongoing for 6 years since 2004. One of the larger-scale events was the classic recitation conference held in front of the People's Auditorium of Luohe on May 31, 2008, themed "Celebrating Dragon Boat Festival, Reading 'Li Sao', Thinking of Qu Yuan". At the time, 16,000 students from municipal direct-administrated schools participated in the recitation, and each county and district also simultaneously held recitation activities, with a total of 130,000 students participating across the city.

"Each person should read 'Shuowen Jiezi' once, recognize at least 500 traditional characters, and know the shapes, sounds, and meanings of at least 500 commonly used characters."
