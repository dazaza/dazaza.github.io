Galaxy Securities: Qingdao Haier's washing machine assets injection into the listed company unfolds as scheduled＿Stock Market Basics

by dx0957095 on 2009-12-03 18:40:29

1. Event: On November 30, 2009, Qingdao Haier (600690.SH) announced that the company was in negotiations with its actual controller, Haier Group, regarding the transfer of a 31.93% stake in Haier Electronics Group Co., Ltd. (a listed company on the Hong Kong Stock Exchange, stock code "1169", hereinafter referred to as "Haier Electronics") held by Haier Group, and had preliminarily reached a consensus, but no letter of intent or agreement had been signed yet.

2. Our Analysis and Judgment: Qingdao Haier (600690.SH) is a white goods leading enterprise we have consistently favored. Especially after the company launched policies such as equity incentives in May this year and started asset consolidation, it became one of our main investment products that we strongly recommended to investors. Currently, Qingdao Haier (600690.SH) already owns refrigerator and air conditioning assets. With the control of Haier Electronics, the company will own washing machine and water heater assets, thereby possessing all white goods assets under the Haier brand, which are also the most profitable industrial assets under the Haier Group.

The announcement did not specify the method for the future acquisition of the 31.93% stake in Haier Electronics. We believe there could be two possible methods:

(1) Cash Acquisition or Asset Swap: As of November 30, Haier Electronics closed at HKD 4.31 per share, with a market capitalization of HKD 8.735 billion. If acquiring a 31.93% stake, it would require HKD 2.789 billion. From a financial perspective, Qingdao Haier (600690.SH) is not short of funds, as the company's cash balance reached RMB 6.514 billion at the end of the third quarter. However, from the perspective of control rights, a cash acquisition of Haier Group's shares in Haier Electronics may not be the optimal choice. There is more uncertainty regarding whether an asset swap is feasible, and we will continue to monitor the situation.

(2)定向增发收购资产（Directed Placement for Asset Acquisition）: If this option is chosen, Qingdao Haier (600690.SH) will issue stocks to Haier Group specifically for the purchase of the 31.93% stake in Haier Electronics held by Haier Group. Through this method, Haier Group can still gain part of the rights in Haier Electronics through holding Qingdao Haier (600690.SH), while Qingdao Haier (600690.SH) does not need to provide additional funds. Therefore, we believe the company is likely to adopt this solution.

Haier Electronics reported revenue of HKD 8.549 billion, HKD 11.409 billion, and HKD 5.309 billion in 2007, 2008, and the first half of 2009 respectively, with post-tax net profits of HKD 226 million, HKD 175 million, and HKD 171 million respectively. The net profit margin has been maintained between 1.53% and 3.22%. If we do not consider the impact of share issuance increasing the capital base, Haier Electronics is expected to bring a profit of RMB 0.13 per share in 2010.

Currently, Qingdao Haier (600690.SH) holds a 19.38% stake in Haier Electronics. Upon completion of this transaction, the company will hold a 51.31% stake in Haier Electronics. Both the company and Haier Group have sought opinions from the Hong Kong Securities and Futures Commission on whether a full offer acquisition should be made to Haier Electronics shareholders, and have received a preliminary response from the Hong Kong SFC agreeing to exempt the company from making a full offer acquisition to Haier Electronics shareholders. This matter still requires approval from the board of directors, shareholders' meeting, relevant government departments, and Haier Group of Qingdao Haier (600690.SH).

3. Investment Recommendation: We believe Qingdao Haier (600690.SH) is entering a period of rapid profit growth. As white goods assets are gradually injected into the listed company, the company will quickly improve operational efficiency and profitability through a strong supply chain management system and a just-in-time production model under zero inventory conditions, providing continuous returns to investors. We expect the integration effect of Haier Electronics to become apparent in 2010. We adjust the earnings per share estimates for 2009, 2010, and 2011 to RMB 0.87, RMB 1.12, and RMB 1.27 respectively, corresponding to PE valuations of 27X, 20.97X, and 18.50X. We continue to maintain a "recommend" rating for the company.

For buying and selling stocks, the process involves specifying buy/sell orders, stock names (or codes), quantities, and prices. These orders are valid only on the day they are placed. The content of the order includes the abbreviation (code) of the stock you wish to trade, the quantity, and the price for buying or selling. The abbreviation usually consists of three to four Chinese characters, and the code is six digits. When placing an order, the code and abbreviation must match. Additionally, there are certain rules for trading quantities: the number of shares to buy must be a multiple of 100, but the number of shares to sell does not need to be.

There are four types of orders: counter submission, telephone automatic, computer automatic, and remote terminal orders.
