"Seven Weapons" for Buying Tickets Online

by hipposhen on 2010-04-06 21:53:38

Not long ago, the hot screening of "Avatar" really made everyone experience the difficulty of getting a ticket. Even if you buy a ticket that is not in high demand, running to the ticket office from afar and being in such a traffic-congested city is no less arduous than obtaining scriptures from the west. "Phone booking" sounds useful, but it's just good to hear when you use it. Those customer service ladies are like professional escorts, talking endlessly without any concern for phone charges. Originally, you had a clear goal before making the call, but after hearing the introduction from the lady, you become confused instead. If you were unclear about your goal before making the call and only wanted to consult, after listening to the lady's introduction, you would become more determined: still better to buy tickets online!

Indeed, every ticket carries not only the price but also your expectations for the performance. Before buying a ticket, you want to know not only how much money you will spend, but also the characteristics of the performance venue, the highlights of this performance, the background of the performing troupe, which position offers the best sensory satisfaction, whether parking is available, how to change subway lines, whether there will be any signing events on the night, etc. If all these questions were asked in one phone call, "escorts" would turn into "cursing".

As a "professional ticket friend", watching performances is my greatest hobby in life. In my wife's words, that is, "not only accompanying me to watch performances, but also accompanying others to watch performances." So-called "pigs run" and eat pork also have experience - bought a lot. Here I will share with everyone my experience in online ticket purchasing, introducing a few ticket websites that friends usually use and feel most useful. Borrowing Gu Long's words, that is - seven kinds of weapons!

First place on the weapon list: China Ticket Online

Website: www.piao.com.cn

Reason for selection: Old qualification, big reputation

Prestige value: ★★★★★

Experience value: ★★★★★

Integrity value: ★★★★

Practical value: ★★★★

China Ticket Online is the Shaolin Temple of ticket websites. Although it may not necessarily be the orthodox martial arts, it is definitely a famous school. This ticket website has been around since 1999 and was once rated as one of the "Top Ten Shopping Websites Recommended by Netizens". It has developed for twenty years now, and its technology and service are basically mature. In the entertainment, sports, and movie industries, and among customers, its reputation is quite good. Electronic tickets, online seat selection, mobile seat selection, printing at home, bank networking zero delivery, RFID, and other new and old ticketing technologies are all available. If you don't have special requirements for online ticket purchases and aren't buying "rare goods," then this website will suffice. Uncle San recently bought tickets for the play "Falling in Love with Peach Blossom Spring" on this website, with payment upon delivery, and both the speed and service were stable.

Second place on the weapon list: China Ticket Pass

Website: www.piaowutong.com

Reason for selection: Full range of tickets, high prestige

Prestige value: ★★★★★

Experience value: ★★★★

Integrity value: ★★★★

Practical value: ★★★★

If China Ticket Online is the Shaolin Temple of ticket websites, then Wudang must belong to China Ticket Pass. "Wudang" started selling tickets in 2003. This ticket-selling website operated by Beijing Zhongyan Oriental has already established a "one-stop" online ticket purchase system. Let me show off my knowledge here. "One-stop" means browsing online performance news, real-time ticket inquiries, online ticket bookings and payments, offline ticket distribution and delivery are all handled by them. I heard they are also working on electronic air tickets, showing their comprehensive development and having the potential to rival Shaolin Temple. We look forward to seeing this.

Third place on the weapon list: Yongle Ticketing Network

Website: www.228.com.cn

Reason for selection: Full range of tickets, high prestige

Prestige value: ★★★★

Experience value: ★★★★

Integrity value: ★★★★

Practical value: ★★★

Yongle Ticketing Network is also one of the earliest batch of ticket websites in China, founded in 2003. However, its business scope is relatively narrow, mainly focusing on commercial performances and sports competitions. As the saying goes, "specialization leads to excellence," and Yongle Ticketing performs quite well in the field of Chinese LIVE entertainment ticketing. If you want to see some special commercial performances and can't find them elsewhere, perhaps Yongle is the only option.

Fourth place on the weapon list: 311 Ticketing Network

Website: www.311piao.com

Reason for selection: Good platform, wide channels

Prestige value: ★★★

Experience value: ★★★

Integrity value: ★★★★★

Practical value: ★★★★★

Speaking of the 311 Ticketing Network, Uncle San must tell a story. A few days ago, neighbor Uncle Liu wanted to buy tickets for the play "If Life Were Only As Beautiful As Our First Meeting" and entrusted Uncle San to handle it. Uncle San said, "Uncle Liu, go to OK convenience store, it's very convenient." Uncle Liu was confused, but due to Uncle San's professional image in this area, he followed the advice and indeed bought the tickets.

It is said that each generation has its talents, and we cannot help but admire the younger generation pushing the older one. For the sake of this first place in the martial arts world, various sects spare no effort. Take this 311 Ticketing Network, for example. Although it has just established its own domain, its first move was extraordinary. The website launched using the ticketing system of the Asian Games and even signed almost all chain convenience stores, laundries, and subway bookstores in Guangzhou City. Moreover, in subway stations, scenic spots, including bank branches, terminals of YibaoLian's machines, such as Ibon, are installed. With the help of store clerks or self-service online, you can buy e-tickets, buy and go anytime, which is very practical for those who are not accustomed to buying tickets online or cannot wait for delivery. Therefore, there are phenomena like Uncle Liu who never uses the internet but can still buy tickets online.

We cannot help but admire its "rich second-generation" background. This subsidiary of the well-known Lixin Group in Guangdong, YiBaoLian and Urban Online ticketing platform, has a father company, Guangzhou YiBaoLian E-commerce Co., Ltd., which is the largest ticketing company in the country, and a mother company, Guangdong Province Urban Online E-commerce Co., Ltd., which is the exclusive ticketing operator for the Guangzhou 2010 Asian Games. Its grandfather, the Lixin Group, is the largest electronic payment enterprise in the country, so it is "not short of money" and does not lack channels. However, although the company's website currently sells tickets nationwide, its offline e-ticket machines and physical franchise stores are still limited to Guangzhou City and its surrounding cities. After all, setting up machines is an expensive affair. If you are in large cities in the Guangdong region such as Guangzhou, Shenzhen, and Zhongshan, choosing tickets from this company is still more convenient because chain stores like 7-11, OK, Circle K, Daily Laundry, and Bookstore are everywhere, and in the subway, you see them everywhere you look.
