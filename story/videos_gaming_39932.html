Reintroducing: Super Bomberman 2

by samara on 2007-12-20 15:15:45

This is a foreign Flash game, the second installment of Super Bomberman. Compared to the first one, it has an additional item that allows you to grab bombs. After playing it thousands of times myself, I've discovered many secrets.

For player controls: Player 1 uses the "up, down, left, right arrow keys" for movement and the " : ; " keys to place bombs. Player 2 uses "W, A, S, D" for movement and the "\" key to place bombs. There are also three game modes for players to choose from.

Eating the bomb-grabbing item in the game allows you to steal enemy bombs. The specific method is: First, you must eat two things - the bomb-grabbing item (which lets you throw bombs) and the shoes combined with bombs item (remember these two together, as they let you kick enemy bombs). Only after eating both can you steal bombs. Then, by pressing the " : ; " key between the enemy's bombs, you can steal them. To avoid being the "barefoot immortal" (moving slowly), eat two pairs of "shoes" to move faster. Eating the shoes and bombs together item (remember they're together) allows you to kick bombs. The more lightning you collect, the longer and more powerful the bomb flames become.

Playing a lot will occasionally create a wormhole. If you enter it and don't come back, you'll lose yourself. The wormholes in the game are like real-life wormholes - they occur rarely, but they definitely exist. 39 seconds after the game ends, the borders will automatically lock until the game is over. If you don't return from the wormhole during this time, you won't be able to get back into the game (placing bombs at the wormhole exit when the border locks will allow you to return, or you can place many bombs when the border locks, creating a hidden space). If you can't return from the wormhole, it won't affect your ability to play the next level (each game mode has three levels). This is the wormhole phenomenon in the game. Sometimes, holding the " : ; " key one second before the game starts will make the bombs appear in the top-left or bottom-right corner of the screen, or sometimes the bombs will fly away outside the bomb frame.

Different arrangements of bombs create different explosion shapes. For example, arranging bombs in a straight line is called "one-character explosion", which is very large; arranging them in a well shape is called "well-character explosion", which is more powerful and destructive; arranging them in a "C" shape is called "retreat strategy"; arranging bombs from the top-left to the bottom-right in a zigzag pattern is called "zigzag explosion" or "flying dragon tail"; arranging bombs from the bottom-right to the top-left is called "flying dragon in the sky". More explosive techniques can be created and named by you.
