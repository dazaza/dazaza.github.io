In line with this trend, SBS will launch a TV drama in October titled "It's Okay, It's a Man," which focuses on the development journey of teenage idols. As the follow-up to "Swallowing the Sun," "It's Okay, It's a Man" is set against the backdrop of the growth process of idol group members, depicting their experiences of love and challenges on the road to fame. It showcases the various encounters of these dazzling stage performers when they're off-stage. Currently, the production team is in the midst of casting, selecting actors around 20 years old, as well as actual idol group members to star in the series. The Hong sisters, who gained immense popularity through "Fantasy Couple," will once again collaborate to write the script for this drama.

Recently, South Korea's television industry has frequently turned its lens toward the entertainment world. Following the great success of SBS's "On Air" last year, several hit dramas such as "Lover of a Star" and "That Fool" have emerged. This time, SBS has focused on the most attention-grabbing teenage idols, once again drawing widespread public interest. Whether "It's Okay, It's a Man" can surpass previous works remains to be seen.

According to Korean media reports, actor Jang Geun-seok was recently selected as the male lead for SBS's new drama "It's Okay, It's a Man," which will air in October, marking his return to the small screen after more than a year away. On the afternoon of July 27, director Hong Sung-chang announced that after much consideration, Jang Geun-seok was chosen as the male lead, and an oral agreement had been reached with his agency regarding his participation. "It's Okay, It's a Man" tells the story of a pure-hearted boy from the countryside who accidentally becomes a member of an idol group and undergoes various experiences. Through the growth journey of the male protagonist played by Jang Geun-seok, it reveals the ordinary side of teenage idols. The drama is written by the Hong sisters, who also created "Fantasy Couple," with Park Shin-hye playing the female lead. The drama will premiere in October as the follow-up to "Swallowing the Sun."

Currently, in the era where groups like TVXQ, BIGBANG, 2PM, Girls' Generation, and 2NE1 dominate half of the music scene, a drama featuring them is about to be launched.

The new SBS drama "It's Okay, It's a Man" already shines brightly with its cast alone. The drama narrates the growth stories of young men and women in a band, exploring friendship, love, and conflicts among the members. Due to an accident involving one of the highest-popularity group A.N.JELL's member "Go Mi-Nam (Park Shin-hye)," the band faces a crisis. His twin sister "Go Mi-Nyeo (Park Shin-hye)" steps in to replace him, leading to a series of events.

On the 24th, at the press conference for "It's Okay, It's a Man" held at SBS Mudeung in Seoul, producers Cho Nam-guk and Hong Sung-chang PD, along with main actors Jang Geun-seok (22), Park Shin-hye (19), FTisland's Lee Hong-gi (19), After School's UEE (21), Jung Yoo-na (20), Kim In-kwon (31), and others were present.

In the drama, Jang Geun-seok plays the role of the talented composer and piano prodigy, captain of A.N.JELL, "Hwang Tae-kyung." Despite being a stubborn and self-centered character, he is inwardly more vulnerable than anyone else. Jang Geun-seok said, "I'm glad to finally play a more natural and relatable role." "Most of the other actors are younger than me, so instead of being taken care of, it's my turn to take care of them."

He also mentioned that he currently has no plans to become a singer, stating that singing in the drama is purely part of the acting. He intends to stay true to his role as an actor. On the 10th, Jang Geun-seok released a single titled "Just Drag."

Park Shin-hye plays the twins "Go Mi-Nam" and "Go Mi-Nyeo" in the drama. Her character is very rigid yet obedient but also playful. She mentioned, "There are many scenes where I jump around and even run alongside tractors. Through this drama, you'll see many different sides of me." She also noted that her character has quiet moments too, but overall, this movie has made her smile more, perhaps making her a bit more lively. Additionally, she expressed her dream of becoming a singer, finding joy in the numerous singing scenes in the drama.

The real-life idols Lee Hong-gi and UEE essentially play themselves in the drama. Lee Hong-gi portrays A.N.JELL's drummer, a cheerful and outgoing second-generation overseas Chinese. UEE plays a character who has been a star since childhood, someone who captivates everyone at first glance.

Lee Hong-gi said, "I'm putting in effort so that everyone can recognize me as an actor named Lee Hong-gi, not just as FTisland's Lee Hong-gi." UEE stated, "As it's my first time acting and playing the role of a villain, I was initially unsure how to approach it and felt nervous. But I will do my best; it's my biggest effort recently."

Jung Yoo-na, who has real-life band experience, plays A.N.JELL's guitarist "Kang Shin-woo" in the drama. Despite appearing cold on the outside, he exudes charm. He also expressed that he would approach the filming of the drama with a sense of responsibility and mission.

"It's Okay, It's a Man" will officially premiere on October 7 at 9:55 PM (Korean time) following "Swallowing the Sun."
