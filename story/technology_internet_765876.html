Mars One Project with 100,000 applicants: Departure in 2022

by anonymous on 2013-08-26 14:37:18

Mars One, a Dutch company, announced that more than 100,000 people have signed up for their one-way trip to Mars, including about 30,000 Americans. They will settle on the red planet and spend the rest of their lives there. Mars One plans to launch the spacecraft in 2022. Before the launch, they must solve the funding issue and also prove that humans can survive on Mars.

A One-Way Journey

Bas Lansdorp, CEO of Mars One, said: "You can see some candidates' names on the website of this program. The candidates listed on the website are only part of all applicants. Many people are still working on their resumes, some have not decided whether to pay the registration fee or are shooting videos, or filling out questionnaires. The people listed on the website are those who have completed these matters and made their resumes public." During the interview, Lansdorp did not disclose how many people had paid the registration fee, completed their resumes, or requested the company to keep their information confidential.

Any adult over the age of 18 can apply for the Mars One program, and the specific registration fee depends on the applicant's nationality - American applicants need to pay $38, while Mexican applicants only need to pay $15. Mars One said that the registration fee depends on the per capita GDP of the applicant's country. Lansdorp said: "We hope to make the registration fee high enough for some applicants so that they must seriously consider it, but at the same time, we hope the registration fee is affordable for anyone."

Lansdorp pointed out that the first mission of the Mars One program will cost $6 billion. Mars One will raise funds through sponsorships and media power. They will film the entire process from astronauts training on Earth to heading to Mars and establishing colonies, and sell the broadcasting rights of this reality show and related documentaries to raise funds.

In 2013, Mars One will select 40 people from the applicants, coming from different continents. Among these 40 people, four Mars colonists will be finally selected - two men and two women. They will launch into space in September 2022 and land on Mars in April 2023. Two years later, another four Mars colonists will also launch to Mars. Since it is a one-way journey, they will never be able to return to Earth.

Eight-Year Training

The selected applicants will undergo eight years of training at a secret location. The Mars One website states that they will learn how to repair the equipment of the Mars habitat, how to grow vegetables in a closed space, as well as how to deal with dental diseases, muscle tears, fractures, and other medical issues during the cultivation process. Lansdorp said: "We want to tell this story to the world. Humans will eventually land on Mars, settle down, and build a new Earth. This is one of the most exciting things ever, and we hope to share it with the world."

Each lander of the Mars One program will carry about 5510 pounds (approximately 2500 kilograms) of supplies. After completing eight transport missions, more than 44,000 pounds (approximately 20,000 kilograms) of supplies will be transported to Mars to meet the needs of the explorers. The landers themselves are not included in this number, as they will become part of the Mars habitat. The supplies transported by the landers include food and solar panels. Since it is impossible to transport large amounts of water and oxygen, the explorers will obtain these supplies on Mars. They will filter water from Martian soil. Lansdorp said: "They will let the water evaporate and then condense it into liquid. Subsequently, they can extract hydrogen and oxygen from the water. Oxygen will meet their breathing needs in the habitat. Before humans arrive, robots will complete a lot of preliminary preparations."

The entire process sounds like terraforming an alien environment, but Lansdorp said it is not so. He pointed out: "We will create an atmosphere similar to Earth on Mars. Therefore, you can understand it as terraforming an alien environment. However, terraforming targets the entire planet and requires tens of thousands of years."

A Dangerous Mission

Despite the numerous risks of space travel, Lansdorp said he believes the Mars One program can succeed. However, some spaceflight experts pointed out that manned Mars trips are extremely risky and difficult to become a reality. Mars is too far from Earth, and humans have never completed such a long journey.

One of the major risks of the Mars journey is radiation. NASA does not allow their astronauts to be exposed to radiation levels that would increase their cancer risk by 3%. Eddie Semones, the official responsible for spaceflight radiation at NASA, pointed out that according to NASA's radiation exposure standard, the longest space stay time for astronauts during the solar minimum period is approximately between 300 to 360 days. During the solar maximum period, the longest stay time is approximately between 275 to 500 days.

According to recent research conducted by NASA scientists, a round-trip to Mars would expose astronauts to the maximum radiation level allowed under NASA's current standards. Although the Mars One program is a one-way trip, the issue of radiation cannot be ignored. On Mars, astronauts will be exposed to even more radiation, depending on their stay duration and protective facilities. Radiation can damage cell DNA, causing cell death or permanent changes that could lead to cancer. Semones pointed out: "There is currently no convincing human evidence that adults exposed to radiation will have severely deformed offspring."

Epidemiologist Robert Reynolds from the University of Texas Health Science Center stated that when orbiting around Earth, astronauts are exposed to higher cosmic background radiation intensity than on the ground. In addition, they are also exposed to charged particles from the upper atmosphere and the sun. When traveling into deep space in a spacecraft, the people onboard will be exposed to more cosmic radiation and solar particles, which is very dangerous. However, astronauts have a lower risk of dying from cancer than the general public because they exercise regularly, pay attention to their diet, do not smoke, and are closely monitored by doctors. However, no astronaut has been to Mars yet, so it is unclear what challenges a Mars trip will bring. Semones pointed out that NASA has not yet studied the health impacts of Mars colonization but focuses more on shorter Mars exploration missions. He said: "We do not intend to colonize Mars yet, so we do not emphasize related research."

Is It Feasible?

Mars One is not the only team hoping to send humans to Mars; the Inspiration Mars Foundation also aims to make history. This foundation plans a Mars-Earth round trip, scheduled for 2018. At that time, they will send a married couple into Mars orbit. The couple will spend 501 days in the confined space of the spacecraft.

501 days means that no existing technology can protect astronauts from excessive space radiation. Semones pointed out: "According to our standards, the longest space stay time is 500 days. Therefore, any mission exceeding 500 days is unfeasible. We completely cannot execute such a manned Mars mission unless we can shorten the mission time or develop better protective devices to protect the spacecraft."

Chris Moore, director of NASA's Advanced Exploration Systems, stated in an interview with CNN in 2013 that NASA is developing engines for Mars missions in the 2030s to shorten flight times. However, such engines will take many years to become a reality. Additionally, engineers will design spaceships that provide more protection to reduce the radiation exposure of astronauts.

Mars One founder Lansdorp insisted that his team can make humans land on Mars in 2023. He said: "The risk of space travel itself is already high, so radiation is not our biggest concern." If you think going to Mars is an amazing adventure, sign up for Mars One's plan. But you must remember that once you start this journey, you will never have the chance to return home.
