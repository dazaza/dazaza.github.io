What is the meaning behind the significant reduction in volume by Chen Xiaohui?

by huaxun924 on 2010-04-16 15:29:59

The market presented a wide-range oscillation and缩volume falling situation today. On the first trading day that the stock index futures officially landed in China's capital market, the stock index welcomed with a green disk, which was more or less unexpected by the market. After the two cities opened low in an inertial manner, the stock market quickly fell, breaking through the support positions of the 5-day and 10-day lines. Although the bulls resisted somewhat, the stock index still rebounded gradually after breaking through the 120-day line. The small and medium-sized subject matter sectors such as Growth Enterprise Market, environmental protection, Tibet, papermaking, and electronics became market hotspots again after slight adjustments. However, the key stocks heavily weighted by funds such as finance, coal, real estate, non-ferrous metals, power, and steel all fell. After 1:30 pm, stimulated by the strengthening of the stock index futures market during the trading day, the market had a quick rally. But due to the lack of strong performance from blue-chip stocks, the participation enthusiasm gradually waned. At the end of the session, the market fell again and broke through the half-year line. By the close, the number of rising stocks was slightly less than the number of falling stocks. There were 4 limit-up stocks, and the trading volume was significantly lower than the previous trading day.

If we attribute the reason for the significant shrinkage of today's trading volume to the diversion of funds to the stock index futures market, it seems a bit far-fetched. The current market is filled with too much观望 sentiment, and the weakness of institutions has amplified this emotion. The repeated speculation of themes that have not yet adjusted adequately by speculative funds can no longer attract many followers. This somewhat rational market is waiting for the directional choice of the market. The collective avoidance of institutional investors at least reveals two concerns about the future: first, how far will the real estate control policies go? The strictest mortgage policies are just the beginning, and measures in taxation and land management to combat speculation will further be introduced in the future. This will affect not only the real estate and banking industries but also continue to squeeze other cyclical sectors. Second, there is still concern about future inflation. The National Development and Reform Commission just indicated that there is room for adjustment in electricity prices, coal prices, natural gas prices, and oil prices, which will put greater pressure on prices in the second quarter.

Looking ahead, the 3180-3200 range is a strong resistance area for the market, while the 3070-3100 range provides strong support. The stock index continues to consolidate within this small box, although the medium-term upward trend has not been damaged, the short-term direction remains unclear. Although the performances of several major weighted stocks in the CSI 300 Index are weak, they have at least avoided a crazy opening for the stock index futures, which is what the regulators want to see most. So, have these individual stocks been consistently reduced in position by funds? The answer is definitely no. The banking sector is waiting for further clarification of real estate control policies and the determination of future monetary policies, while considering insurance stocks as the wind vane of the index is not excessive. In terms of operations, investors should continue to accumulate high-quality large-cap blue-chip stocks such as financials, while temporarily avoiding the previously frenzied theme stocks. Additionally, opportunities in central enterprise restructuring concepts and Q1 pre-increase (high dividend) stocks can still be monitored.
