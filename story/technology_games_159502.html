Devil May Cry 4 - A Must-See Guide for New Players

by lk0hngame on 2009-06-12 07:54:14

The Devil May Cry series has always had some traditions, let's introduce them one by one.

On-the-spot small jump:

This can be said to be the most important action in DMC. Even when challenging the DMD difficulty, not mastering this skill can make it very tough.

Don't write it off as just a small jump; it is more useful than rolling. In the DMC series, the on-the-spot small jump not only happens quickly with little stiffness and no displacement, but also allows for direct counterattacks. Most importantly: invincibility time. The on-the-spot small jump has invincibility time, and it's not short at all. This is why in some videos, you see Uncle Dante's RG (Royal Guard) style often jumping to BLOCK, which has a higher success rate than staying on the ground, precisely because of this invincibility time.

Of course, not all moves can be dodged with a small jump (most can), and sometimes you still need to roll.

Damage Bonus:

The damage bonus in the DMC series is extremely powerful. For example, if move A deals 5 damage and move B deals 6 damage, if the player is hit by both moves separately, the damage will be 11. However, if the player is hit by move A and then immediately by move B, the damage won't be 11, it might be 15 instead. The more combos the player gets hit by, the higher the damage bonus becomes.

In such situations, if you can't avoid it, then you can only transform into Devil Trigger mode, which not only enhances defense, but also repels enemies at the moment of transformation.

Devil Trigger:

The status of Devil Trigger in this game has been unprecedentedly enhanced.

The Devil Trigger in DMC4 is much stronger than in previous installments (though the strongest is still the Super Devil Trigger from the second game, please WS). It's not just about increased speed and defense anymore.
