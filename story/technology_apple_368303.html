Windows Phone 7 vs. Apple iPhone 4: Who Will Dominate?

At the press conference, Microsoft CEO Steve Ballmer officially announced the Windows Phone 7 mobile operating platform, as well as several phones from different manufacturers that feature this latest system. Smartphones running Windows Phone 7 will join Android phones in challenging the iOS platform’s iPhone 4. As such, phone enthusiasts can't help but make a detailed comparison between the newly launched Windows Phone 7 phones and the iPhone. Between Apple's iPhone 4 and Windows Phone 7, who will dominate?

iPhone 4 vs. Win7 Smartphone Specifications Comparison:

The above is a comparison of the hardware specifications of the two phone platforms. Of course, most people would also like to compare the iOS and Windows Phone 7 mobile platforms.

Multitasking: Windows Phone 7 does not support multitasking, at least not yet. Multitasking was something iOS users had long awaited, so it is reasonable to believe that future updates of Windows Phone 7 will also support multitasking.

Multimedia:

Apple iPhone 4 users can use Microsoft's Zune media player to play music and movies, and synchronize multimedia to the phone via the PC version of the Zune software. iOS devices synchronize content to the iPhone through iTunes.

Internet Email: We are already very familiar with the Safari and Mail programs built into iOS, both of which have made us appreciate the convenience brought by mobile devices. Especially in iOS 4.1, Mail has been unified, which is convenient for users with multiple email accounts. The Safari home page can display bookmarks we have saved. Windows Phone 7 naturally comes with the IE Mobile browser, allowing tabbed browsing and touch control just like Safari. Its built-in email program is Outlook Mobile, featuring simplified account login, supporting Windows Live, Google, and Yahoo mailboxes, as well as multiple Exchange accounts.

Network Sharing (Tethering): Unlike the iPhone, current Windows Phone 7 devices do not support internet sharing. However, it is believed that future updates of Windows Phone 7 will definitely support sharing functions.

Flash: In this aspect, Windows Phone 7 seems to be in agreement with the iPhone, as neither supports Adobe's Flash.

Camera Program: The Camera program on the iOS platform is very suitable for capturing moving objects, and synchronization with computers is very convenient. You can also send photos to friends via Email or MMS, or upload them to MobileMe albums. Windows Phone 7 has a built-in "Pictures Hub," which stores all pictures on the phone. Users can synchronize photos to the PC, send photos, and even directly upload photos to Facebook or Windows Live.

Software Games: Of course, Microsoft has its own place to sell applications, called "Marketplace," which is integrated with Windows Phone 7. However, whether Marketplace can match the excellence of App Store still needs some time to observe. Major game developer EA has agreed to develop games for Win7 phones. Together with Xbox Live online gaming functionality and Microsoft's own Office program, Win7 seems to have no shortage compared to iOS. Apple currently does not have its own office software built into the iOS platform.

Carriers: At launch, Microsoft stated that Windows Phone 7 would be sold in 30 countries by 60 carriers. In the United States, Win7 is sold by three carriers including ATT and T-Mobile, and in Europe by Orange, Vodafone, and O2.

Summary: As a fan of the iPhone and iOS, I really don't want to admit that Win7 indeed looks good. Compared to previous versions of Windows Mobile, it has improved significantly. But it cannot yet be said whether it can rival iOS, despite Windows Phone 7 adding a few features that the iPhone lacks, such as a physical keyboard. These functions or features seem not to add much value to Win7; compared to the iPhone 4, it seems to fall short somewhat.
