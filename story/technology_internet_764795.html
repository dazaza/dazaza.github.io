Taobao Answers for the First Time: Why Are External Image Links Banned?

by anonymous on 2013-08-06 18:47:00

Taobao's decision to close external image albums and ban sellers from using externally linked images has been met with opposition and questions from Taobao sellers. The Taobao official team has provided a detailed explanation regarding this matter.

E Bang Dynamics: Last year, Taobao issued a statement clearly indicating that it would not interrupt the cooperation between sellers and third-party image companies. However, changes have now occurred. What is the real reason behind this?

Taobao: This time, what caught Taobao's attention is that, with the arrival of the data era, the information data of sellers and consumers is being spied on by illegal elements. In recent years, there have been multiple incidents where codes were embedded in external linked images provided by third-party service providers, resulting in the theft of user information. This poses a significant risk to the safe operation of sellers and seriously threatens the information security of the platform.

On the other hand, Taobao often receives user feedback that different users in different regions see inconsistent images for the same product. For example, normal product images are displayed in Hangzhou, while in Shanghai or Hunan, they are shown as yellow images or banned items designed to attract users. The reason is that the images are stored on external servers, which are not open to some city IPs.

E Bang Dynamics: Taobao has always maintained an open attitude towards sellers and the third-party service market. After this adjustment, Taobao will exclude third-party image companies from its ecosystem. Does this contradict the advocated "open" spirit? Or will Taobao collaborate with third-party companies in other forms in the future?

Taobao: "Openness" and closing external linked images on Taobao are unrelated. Image storage services are a basic e-commerce service with relatively low capital and technical thresholds, and there is little room for extension and expansion. Services lack differentiation and mainly compete on price. Therefore, we have never included this in the platform's service market for openness.

Moving all external domain images back to Taobao's image space is done for two reasons: first, to ensure the data security of sellers and the platform, as we have found risks of third-party companies embedding codes in external domain images to obtain store data; second, to enhance the user experience of buyers by increasing loading speed and eliminating broken images or expired images. Regarding image storage services, we have no plans to collaborate with other third-party companies.

E Bang Dynamics: It is reported that many image companies are communicating with Taobao under judicial intervention. How is the current progress? What will be the final negotiation result?

Taobao: We have not yet received any news from the court, so the claim of judicial intervention seems somewhat exaggerated.

We are currently holding individual talks with service providers and coordinating these service providers to ensure the normal display of store images. Meanwhile, we have decided to delay the implementation of the external link image ban policy. Over the next six months, Taobao will help these service providers transform and upgrade, together exploring new e-commerce service opportunities. During the migration period, if we discover that external linked images jeopardize user data and platform security, Taobao will take immediate and serious action without leniency.

E Bang Dynamics: In this adjustment, sellers generally react with feelings of being "forced", "completely unprepared", and "labor-intensive and costly". Did Taobao consider the various inconveniences this might bring to sellers beforehand? How is Taobao handling the situation after stopping sellers from using external images for some time?

Taobao: Four years ago, we began encouraging sellers to use image space services and provided various preferential policies. Additionally, we have extended the relocation deadline by six months, so there is no basis for claims of "being completely unprepared" or "forced".

Regarding the issue of "labor-intensive", we will improve and optimize the one-click relocation function, extend the seller's relocation time, and ensure the smooth execution of the relocation plan.

As for "costly", sellers can cancel their subscriptions to image service providers. Other issues raised by sellers will also be collected by designated personnel for centralized handling.

E Bang Dynamics: Currently, the functionality of Taobao's official album seems unable to meet the diverse needs of sellers. How do you plan to improve it?

Taobao: We will collect more user feedback to upgrade our product services and meet user needs. Currently, 90% of users are using Taobao's official album. From my understanding, their feedback is better than what you mentioned.

E Bang Dynamics: Many angry sellers have formed an "anti-monopoly alliance." They are discussing how to "defend their rights," how to seek explanations from Taobao, and request compensation. What is Taobao's attitude towards this? Do you believe this behavior carries monopolistic characteristics? What does Taobao want to say to these sellers?

Taobao: We are communicating individually with these service providers. Over the extended six-month period, Taobao will help these service providers transform and upgrade, together exploring new e-commerce service opportunities.
