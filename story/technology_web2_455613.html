The commodity room's trading volume has been declining consecutively.

by czzz2008 on 2011-07-02 10:02:49

The "929" second round of new real estate market regulation policies has been in effect for a full month. From the vague but unproven "cancellation" and "return of housing" trends in various places, we can see not only the power of the "loan restriction" and "purchase restriction" policies, but also the emerging expectations of buyers in the market for a drop in housing prices. However, after reviewing Beijing, Shanghai, and Guangzhou three months after the implementation of the new policy, our reporter found that although measures to regulate the real estate market have gradually intensified and have already been "very effective", the decrease in housing prices during this adjustment period remains quite limited. Nevertheless, many industry experts expect that with the shrinkage in transaction volumes, as the market's wait-and-see sentiment becomes increasingly strong, developers will have no choice but to trade price for volume under financial pressure. A "real fall" in housing prices is still foreseeable in the future.

Around National Day, with the introduction of purchase restrictions in Beijing, Shanghai, Shenzhen, Nanjing, and other places, within less than a month, 14 cities had implemented purchase restrictions. Judging from the effects after the introduction of these restrictions, combined with strict limitations on third-home loans and interest rate hikes, the atmosphere of waiting-and-see sentiment in major cities has become very strong. The popular sales trend of September has disappeared, with quite a number of investment-oriented buyers temporarily holding off due to purchase restrictions and loan factors. However, despite the fact that most cities did not specify the duration of the restrictions, many people are still worried that once the purchase restrictions are lifted, the previously suppressed strong demand will flood back. Therefore, how to ensure that the real estate market remains healthy and stable after the lifting of purchase restrictions may ultimately necessitate the introduction of property taxes.

### First-Tier City Scan

#### Guangzhou: New Buildings Open Quietly, Prices High

In October, influenced by rumors of an impending purchase restriction order, Guangzhou's real estate market experienced a process of first rising then falling, marked by the issuance of the restriction order on October 15. Online signing volumes remained astonishingly high at over a thousand units per day before the 15th, but rapidly dropped to triple-digit levels afterward. However, the total monthly transaction volume still set a new record. According to monitoring by some institutions, as of now, the transaction volume in Guangzhou's ten districts in October has exceeded 15,000 units, increasing more than double compared to September. The average transaction price for the ten districts was approximately 13,277 yuan per square meter, a slight decrease of 0.15% compared to the previous month. Currently, there has been no substantial drop in Guangzhou's housing prices.

Many industry insiders believe that the surprisingly high transaction volume under the high-pressure policies is caused by multiple factors. Firstly, many new projects launched at the end of September had ideal sales, but due to the lag in online signing, their transaction situations were mostly reflected in October’s data. Some projects even sold nearly a thousand units on the opening day. Secondly, during the National Day holiday, despite policy pressures, properties priced below 1.2 million yuan targeting rigid demand were still highly sought after by the market. Projects such as Times Nanwan, Zhizhao Jinshan Valley, Yipin Hushan, and Yabaonewtown enjoyed significant sales. As a government project, the Asian Games City also saw red-hot sales due to its special nature and good investment prospects. Thirdly, after the "929" new policy, several cities introduced purchase restrictions, and Guangzhou also hinted at it. This led to a pre-restriction "buying spree," where homebuyers rushed to make purchases, and developers accelerated signing processes, causing a surge in online signing volumes in the few days before the 15th.

Our reporters found that under the influence of the new regulations, some projects that opened against the trend became more low-key. With fewer promotional activities, they adopted a marketing strategy where the announced price was significantly higher than the actual opening price to attract buyers into the market. One project located in the Nanzhou area opened quietly post-regulation. Apart from offering a 5% discount for a deposit of 50,000 yuan after opening sample flats, there were no special marketing tactics. In terms of pricing, sales staff consistently quoted high prices, creating the illusion of a large price increase. However, on the opening day, the price was slightly lower than the previously quoted average of 20,000 yuan per square meter. In reality, only a small portion of high-rise units exceeded 20,000 yuan per square meter, while other floor units averaged around 18,000 yuan per square meter, with some units priced at 17,600 yuan per square meter. Ultimately, about half or over a hundred units were sold on the opening day.

Xu Wanting, director of the research center at Jingwei Real Estate, believes that following the secondary regulation policies at the end of September and local purchase restrictions, the People's Bank of China announced a 0.25 percentage point interest rate hike on October 19, and the Ministry of Housing and Urban-Rural Development subsequently raised the deposit-loan interest rates for provident funds, further strengthening the intensity of real estate market regulation and expressing the government's determination to control housing prices. The first interest rate hike in three years signifies an adjustment in credit policies. Moderately loose monetary policies are beginning to tighten, and asset price bubbles caused by previous loose monetary policies are expected to be controlled, alleviating inflationary pressures. Some homebuyers seeking asset preservation due to inflation expectations may return to a wait-and-see stance. Additionally, interest rate hikes mean increased costs of home purchasing and greater motivation for developers to lower prices. An increase in bank loan interest rates means higher existing loan costs for developers. Although this currently does not pose too much of a shock to developers, it doesn't mean they can withstand continuous interest rate hikes. Financial institutions have tightened new development loans, narrowing developers' financing channels, so many choose private capital and trust platforms as financing channels, increasing corresponding costs. If the cost of existing bank loans rises further due to continuous interest rate hikes, developers' financial operations will become more difficult, and they will need to accelerate sales to recover funds. Under this expectation, it cannot be ruled out that some developers facing significant financial pressure or adopting rapid-sales strategies might lead the way in lowering prices in this round of interest rate hikes to quickly recover funds. Changes in housing price expectations mean more homebuyers will enter a wait-and-see state. When the market enters a prolonged wait-and-see situation, developer price cuts are not far away.

#### Shanghai: Luxury Home Sales Dramatically Increase Average Prices

A month after the "929" new policy, Shanghai's real estate market has also encountered a cold front like the city's weather, with residential property transaction volumes continuously declining. However, housing prices, which ordinary people care about the most, have "performed poorly," with only a few peripheral projects making slight adjustments, far from reaching the level of a "drop." Industry insiders pointed out that one of the main reasons why Shanghai's average housing price remains above 22,000 yuan per square meter is the concentrated transactions of luxury homes like Shanghai Xinghe Bay this month. According to primary housing data released by Han Yu Real Estate Market Research Department, from October 18 to 24, the average transaction price of apartments across Shanghai rose to 22,406 yuan per square meter, a 13.4% increase from the previous week, setting a new high for weekly transaction averages in the past two months. However, after excluding the price impacts of Shanghai Xinghe Bay and Renhe Yiting, the average price of apartments across Shanghai was only 17,958 yuan per square meter. But our reporters found that even excluding expensive luxury homes, there was no noticeable price drop in Shanghai's real estate market. In fact, the prices of general commercial residential properties in Shanghai remained stable, and those that previously shouted "price reduction" slogans were mainly some once-popular suburban peripheral projects, especially those outside the outer ring road. Data from Han Yu Real Estate also showed that even for these remote sector projects, price drops were all within 5%. Industry insiders told reporters that compared to downtown projects, these projects themselves are not scarce and thus find it difficult to "retain value" when market conditions are poor. Moreover, before the introduction of regulatory measures, the prices of these sector projects had all seen significant increases.

According to survey data from 100 stores under Han Yu Real Estate, since October, the number of customers who voluntarily withdrew their purchase intentions accounted for about 30% of total transactions, with first-time homebuyers and improvement-type purchasers each accounting for half. But this does not mean that homebuyers have completely abandoned their purchase intentions. Apart from those who indeed cannot afford down payments and interest rates and are forced to "return houses" and "wait-and-see," many people are still "waiting for opportunities." Aunt Fu from Shanghai represents the mindset of many homebuyers. She told reporters that due to the impact of the "purchase restriction order," her family's originally gradual home-buying plan has been completely disrupted. Now, the plan is to achieve everything in one step.

Currently, Shanghai's real estate market widely promotes "discounts." According to statistics from SouFun Data Monitoring Center, as of October 19, an estimated 148 discounted projects are planned for November, an increase of 4% over the previous period. However, more than half of the discounted projects offer total discounts of 90% or more, with total discounts within 100,000 yuan, far from meeting buyers' psychological expectations.

Affected by the sporadic appearance of "returning houses" in October, many projects chose to delay their openings, especially more than a dozen high-end projects within Shanghai's inner ring.

Meanwhile, on October 28, the last residential land plot offered in Shanghai in October was acquired by CIFI Group for 4.56 billion yuan, with a premium of 193%, resulting in a floor price of 8,160 yuan per square meter. CIFI expressed that it was "worth the price." Industry insiders said that various signs indicate that although the impact of regulation is gradually becoming apparent, relatively scarce high-quality residential plots still attract developer attention, and developers remain "not short of money."

#### Beijing: Housing Price Fluctuates, Adjustments Minimal

Yuan Xiuming, senior analyst at China Index Academy, said in an interview with our newspaper yesterday that the new policy has a significant impact on Beijing's real estate market. After the introduction of regulatory policies in April this year, Beijing's residential sales volume clearly declined, and by August and September, there was a significant upward trend. In late September, the country introduced a series of regulatory policies, and Beijing also introduced measures such as pre-sale fund management, leading to a clear downward trend in transaction volumes after a series of peaks in October. "Overall, the policy impact is significant. From a price perspective, after the introduction of the regulatory policies in April, Beijing's housing prices have not risen significantly, showing an adjustment trend, with individual projects rising, but more projects showing a downward trend in prices, though the decline is minimal," she said.

We learned that regarding housing prices and transaction volumes, from September 20 to 26, the weekly sales area of commercial residential properties in Beijing was approximately 209,400 square meters. The following week, it surged to 337,000 square meters, but after the National Day holiday, it dropped to 179,300 square meters, and then rose again to 210,000 square meters. By the week of October 18 to 24, the sales volume was only 150,200 square meters. Regarding sales prices, after October, the sales price of commercial residential properties in Beijing hovered between 18,000 and 20,000 yuan per square meter.

"We believe that within a fairly long period, Beijing's housing prices are unlikely to rise rapidly again," Yuan Xiuming said. This regulation is relatively thorough, but not entirely so. Mechanisms driving housing price increases still exist, so the possibility of a significant drop in housing prices is also minimal. Previous regulatory policies were primarily administrative measures, which, although not fully thorough, will definitely play a role and continue for a longer period. "Overall, the country's determination to regulate stems from the overall economic growth status. Currently, China's economy is in a new phase of recovery with a good economic situation. In such circumstances, it is easy for housing prices to rise significantly, and it is necessary to curb such increases. In the long term, if there are signs of rapid housing price increases, such regulatory measures may follow closely behind, and new regulatory policies may be introduced or existing ones may become stricter," she said. If the government does not have concerns about a slowdown in economic growth, the first industry it would regulate would be the real estate industry because it involves both the financial and real estate sectors, posing financial risks and the risk of economic imbalance due to excessive industrial growth. Therefore, whether from the perspective of financial security or economic structural adjustment, the government must curb excessive housing price increases and prevent overheating in the real estate market. Thus, the possibility of a sharp rise in Beijing's housing prices has become very small.

Regarding Beijing's second-hand housing market, a professional research institution in Beijing analyzed the impact of the new policy and pointed out that it indeed affects Beijing's second-hand housing market. According to relevant statistical data, since October, up to October 27, the number of online signed transactions for second-hand residential properties in Beijing decreased by more than 10% compared to September, and the number of listed properties decreased by 8.44% compared to September, while the number of potential buyers decreased by 1.11%. It is almost certain that the transaction volume of Beijing's second-hand housing market in October will decline.

#### Tianjin: Will the "Purchase Restriction Order" Become a "House Grabbing Order"?

On October 14, the Tianjin municipal government officially issued a notice titled "Temporary Implementation of Restrictions on the Number of Houses Purchased by Resident Families," with the start date of the restriction being October 13, but without a clear end date. It is understood that Tianjin's "purchase restriction order" does not count the number of houses previously purchased by individuals, and the scope of enforcement includes only six urban districts, excluding the Binhai New Area and the four suburban districts.

Compared to the "strict school" cities like Shenzhen and Nanjing that directly impose "purchase restrictions" on third homes, Tianjin and other cities adopt a more relaxed policy that does not count the number of houses previously owned by individuals, allowing families to purchase one more new home. Indeed, this appears much gentler.

From inquiries at multiple real estate companies and projects in Tianjin, we learned that after the implementation of the "purchase restriction," the overall housing prices and the prices in the six urban districts of Tianjin both showed a trend of increasing volume and decreasing prices, while the new home and second-hand housing markets in the four suburban districts and the Binhai New Area exhibited uneven growth trends. In this regard, industry insiders believe that restricting the purchase of new developments within the six urban districts could have a positive effect on curbing speculation in urban real estate, a "leader" in Tianjin's real estate market. Whether limiting the purchase of urban new developments would bring a "spring" to the housing markets in the four suburban districts and the Binhai New Area remains to be seen over time.

"Can 'scarce resources' hold up?"

Due to the relatively small area and scarcity of housing sources in the six urban districts of Tianjin, for many families, this policy aimed at restricting the number of houses each family can purchase could also lead to a situation where "each family would seize this unique opportunity to buy a house in the urban area, causing locals or local investors to rush to purchase properties in the six urban districts in a short period." Thus, Tianjin's "purchase restriction order" in the six urban districts could potentially evolve into a "house grabbing order."

In response, relevant persons in charge of Lianjia Real Estate's market department stated that currently, Tianjin's real estate market mainly relies on rigid demand for support. The introduction of the purchase restriction order, while severely impacting speculative actions, undoubtedly has a certain impact on the rigid demand group of partial improvement-type housing buyers, possibly even causing irrational "madness" in the market. Yesterday, reporters visited multiple sales offices in the city center, including Laochengxiang and Nanjing Road areas, and almost unanimously received the same answer: "Our houses are located in the city center, belonging to scarce resources, and regardless of how regulation occurs, prices will not drop."

At the opening of Phase I of Tianjin Poly Metropolis on October 17, more than 300 units were sold in the morning of the opening day. According to Ms. Liu, a real estate consultant at the sales office, the average selling price of the development was 20,000 yuan per square meter. "Since the location of the development is in the central Nan Shi area of Tianjin's Peace District, and the price is slightly lower than that of nearby developments, the sales were exceptionally strong after the opening."

Subsequently, reporters visited Wanda Plaza on Jibin Avenue in Hedong District. "All our commodities have been sold out," Sun Ying, the sales manager of the project, told reporters. The average price of the development was only 8,000 to 9,000 yuan per square meter when it first opened after the Spring Festival, but by the end, the average price had risen to 15,000 yuan per square meter.

"I feel that the purchase restriction order has little impact on me personally, especially since I don't need a loan. There are already very few new developments in the six urban districts, and I estimate that in a few years, I will only be able to buy second-hand houses," Mr. Cai, selecting a house at Huajinmen Lake in Meijiang, Tianjin, believed that implementing the "purchase restriction order" fundamentally cannot stop people who truly want to buy houses. Even if they use up their quota for purchasing a new house, they can find relatives' or friends' "spare quotas."

At several sales offices near Hebei District and Hongqiao District, staff informed them that there were no special discounts. Even if individual projects launched a few special-priced units, the discount range was very small.
