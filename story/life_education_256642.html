10 Essential Study Abroad Tips for Studying in the U.S.

by keinol on 2009-11-06 09:31:45

1. You should learn English well before going abroad. It is generally assumed that Chinese students studying in the U.S. will have a good command of English. If you arrive in the U.S. with poor English skills, you'll find it difficult to adapt, hard to participate in classes, and challenging to communicate with teachers and classmates. Your daily life will also be severely affected. Don't expect to improve your English after arriving in the U.S. In reality, if your English is too weak for even basic communication, American classmates may become impatient when interacting with you. This is different from China, where people usually think foreigners don't speak much Chinese, so when you occasionally say a sentence in Chinese, they might consider your Chinese very good. I remember when my younger brother came to China, I introduced him to my Chinese friends who said "Hello!" My brother replied with "Hello!" and the friend commented, "Your brother's Chinese is so good!" But at that time, my brother only knew how to say "Hello." In China, knowing terms like mantou (steamed bun), baozi (dumpling), and jiaozi (dumpling) might make someone think you're a "China expert," but in the U.S., knowing how to say "French fries" in English won't earn you the title of an "America expert." They consider this knowledge fundamental and necessary, as once in the U.S., your English should already be decent.

2. Clarify the reasons for studying abroad. Many people spend too little time on this aspect; some blindly follow trends without ever carefully considering why they truly want to go abroad. While there may not be completely right or wrong reasons for this question, at least every one of us should know why we want to study abroad. Some wish to understand more cultures and meet more people, others aim to gain more knowledge and receive better education, and some even hope to make their parents proud. Regardless of the reason, no matter how simple or insignificant, as long as it comes from within, it can inspire our potential because it will run through the entire preparation process for studying abroad, influence your studies, and even impact your future university life abroad. I recommend spending at least half an hour to an hour in a quiet place to think about why you want to study abroad. Finding the motivation is crucial for the entire study abroad plan.
