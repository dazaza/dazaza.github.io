Experts from the Nanjing Armed Police Hospital's Anorectal Center point out that anal fissures can be classified into different types, each requiring a distinct treatment method. Early-stage fissures are generally treated conservatively with food and medication, while chronic fissures may require surgical intervention, significantly impacting daily life.

### I. Conservative Treatment:

1. Internal medications, external applications, and fumigation therapies in conservative treatments are effective for fresh anal fissures. It is best to use these treatments after professional examination at a specialized anorectal hospital to ensure proper medication. Self-administering conservative therapy might lead to complications of various anorectal diseases, complicating treatment.

2. Additionally, the conservative treatment method of anal dilation, though quick-acting and simple to care for, could result in complications such as hematomas, perianal abscesses, prolapsed hemorrhoids, and temporary fecal incontinence if improperly used.

### II. Painless Minimally Invasive Surgery for Anal Fissure Removal:

Currently, the Nanjing Armed Police Hospital suggests that the first step in treating anal fissures should involve a clear diagnosis. Treatment should also be staged; early-stage fissures can be managed with medication, whereas second and third stage fissures require painless minimally invasive surgery.

1. **Mild Anal Fissures**: The unique HCPT painless minimally invasive technique relieves sphincter spasm and provides pain and bleeding control. Combined with electronic anoscopy for internal and external regulation, it effectively treats anal fissures.

2. **Chronic Anal Fissures**: PPH painless minimally invasive surgery is used to repair the fissure and reduce recontamination. This is followed by electronic anoscopy. To treat the fissure well, it is best to simultaneously address constipation issues to prevent its recurrence. For this, the Nanjing Armed Police Hospital employs American colon hydrotherapy, adhering to the principle of "cleaning first, regulating afterward, fast treatment for chronic diseases," which shows significant efficacy in treating constipation and has good clinical results for conditions like colitis.

Experts from the Nanjing Armed Police Hospital's Anorectal Center suggest that if the fissure is caused by other anorectal diseases, then both the fissure and the underlying condition should be treated simultaneously. For the treatment of anorectal diseases, the current painless minimally invasive therapy is characterized by being "small, quick, and pain-free." Mild cases can be treated and leave immediately, ensuring thorough treatment without recurrence.
