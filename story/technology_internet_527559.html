The Experience of Taking Wedding Photos: How to Make a Choice When Facing Dissatisfaction

by aoki2013 on 2011-10-31 11:05:57

Every new couple hopes to present their perfect image during the wedding photo shoot. Regardless of whether you are a perfectionist or not, I believe that for your most important day, you will "spare no expense" in searching for various tips on taking wedding photos. For this reason, the editor has provided some small suggestions for the soon-to-be couples, hoping that it will be helpful for the couples!

Bridal photo-taking experience: How to make decisions when facing dissatisfaction.

Clothing coordination

Netizen Dingding: My package is 4+2 (the studio provides 2 wedding dresses + 1 formal dress + 1 cheongsam, and I bring 2 casual outfits myself). The indoor wedding dress and formal dress that I previously selected turned out to be the best, especially the formal dress photos; my colleagues all said they look like sample photos. However, the hastily chosen seaside short wedding dress and cheongsam were just average. Actually, I wanted to choose a trailing wedding dress, but because we were rushing to go outside for the shoot, the costume designer randomly picked one and said let's go with a short one. There was no time to think, I put it on and immediately got into the car to go to the beach.

Casual outfit selection

Netizen Dingding: I originally thought my two dresses were pretty good, one for students and one for the workplace, used to shoot 80s personalized wedding photography in a plot style. I even brought two pairs of shoes to match them. But before he left, he was in such a rush that he forgot to bring the white T-shirt to pair with the jeans and refused to wear black trousers with a shirt. In the end, it was a workplace shirt plus jeans combination. When the photos came out, I just wanted to crop them, keeping only the upper body.

Men should preferably wear their own black leather shoes as the studio ones may not fit well. It’s also advisable to have both black and white socks ready. On the day of the photo shoot, he lost his black socks, which was frustrating. The stores downstairs weren’t open in the morning, so there was nowhere to buy socks. If going to the beach, both of you should bring an extra pair of slippers. After playing in the water barefoot at the beach, our feet were dirty and couldn't be wiped clean with tissues. I didn’t want to get my high heels dirty, so I ended up getting into and out of the car barefoot, only washing and putting on my shoes back at the studio.

Wedding dress and styling

Netizen Keane: Before taking pictures, brides shouldn't just focus on picking out their own clothing. If possible, both partners should try on their outfits together to see if they harmonize and if they achieve the desired 80s personalized wedding photography style. Slim figures suit strapless dresses, while fuller figures suit halter neck styles. Hairdressers style hair based on the clothing, so the first step is selecting the clothing, followed by choosing hairstyles. I suggest ladies grow their hair longer, as it makes styling easier. Since my hair is shoulder-length, we added some fake curls at the back. Except for the hairstyle for the first set of formal wear, the others were just average.

Communication with the photographer

Netizen Keane: In May, I communicated some ideas with him. On the 23rd, we spent the whole morning shooting the formal wear + white dress, which were the two sets we were more satisfied with. Knowing that my boyfriend and I don’t usually see each other often, he specially took many group shots for us. In the afternoon, although I wasn’t entirely satisfied with my boyfriend’s outfit for the street scenes, the photographer still managed to capture the 80s personalized wedding photography plot effect and caught some touching details. Ladies, after placing your order, if the studio assigns your photographer, you can find time to communicate with him beforehand. This way, you'll feel more familiar and comfortable, which helps with on-site performance.
