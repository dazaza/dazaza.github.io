Entertainment Background

Cheng Kui'an (February 1, 1955 - ), nicknamed "Big Dumb," was an original resident of Nam Wai in Sai Kung, Hong Kong. He was a film and television actor, tall in stature, skilled in playing villains such as gang leaders. Cheng dropped out of school at the age of 13 and had worked as a thug. In 1978, he was discovered by Stephen Chow and began acting in films. Cheng once ran for election in the Hong Kong District Council, becoming one of the candidates in Sai Kung District, but ultimately lost. In recent years, he was diagnosed with nasopharyngeal cancer and underwent chemotherapy and radiotherapy.

Since being discovered by Stephen Chow in 1978, during his 23-year career in the arts, Cheng has acted in over 350 films and more than 600 episodes of TV series. In these productions, the majority of his roles were antagonists. Even when he tried playing positive characters like a police officer or father, he couldn't shake off his image as a "villain" in people's minds. Due to the nature of his roles, the nickname "Big Dumb" became widely recognized despite his efforts.

More than ten years ago, watching John Woo's classic film "The Killer," the character Cheng played as a triad boss could make you hate him with passion. At the end of the movie, he deliberately shoots out the eyes of the assassin Xiao Zhuang (played by Chow Yun-fat), destroying Xiao Zhuang's last wish before death (to donate his cornea to the singer Jenny whom he accidentally injured in his past). By this point, all viewers would want to tear him apart. Therefore, when Chief Inspector Li Ying (Stephen Chow) decides to shoot the notorious "Big Dumb" rather than face charges in front of the police, many audiences applauded instinctively. Cheng's portrayal of the villain was so fierce and vivid that the audience's applause was the best praise for his masterful acting skills in negative roles.

In 1983, Cheng Kui'an first portrayed the role of "Big Dumb" in the movie "Auspicious Man," leaving a deep impression on the audience. Since then, "Big Dumb" has become his synonym. Regarding his well-established image as a triad boss, Cheng once clarified publicly: "You can't judge a person by their appearance. I have been re-elected as the village chief of our village, which proves that I am a good person! Moreover, when I'm not filming, I stay at home, making me a 100% 'good man.'"

In movies, there are always a group of lackeys surrounding the triad boss, making them appear very powerful. As a "gangster figure," Cheng Kui'an was no exception. However, the so-called friendships were built on money. Cheng enjoyed eating and drinking with a large group of friends, generously paying for gatherings, spending his money in this way. To emphasize the grandeur of a "gang leader," Cheng Kui'an indulged in alcohol and smoked incessantly, offering cigarettes to his friends as well, consuming almost two packs a day. Back then, Cheng Kui'an lived a life of revelry and indulgence.

In 1996, Cheng Kui'an started earning money in mainland China. The peak of his career was in 2003 and 2004, where he could earn over a million yuan a year. However, a serious illness depleted his savings accumulated over the years. Despite this, Cheng remained optimistic, grateful to have survived. Thinking that he is still employed in his forties, he feels content. Cheng has a good mindset and is open-minded about everything. Even though his income is less now than it used to be, he faces it calmly, believing that as long as one has a clear conscience, that's all that matters. Cheng's father left behind a dilapidated house, so he demolished it and rebuilt it into a three-story building, living happily with his family.
