The Most Controversial Family Ethical Novel: Double-Sided Tape

by jqrxass on 2009-09-07 15:35:43

All women who have been daughters-in-law or mothers-in-law, and all men caught in the middle, look this way! Six's most controversial novel on family ethics will certainly leave a deep impression on you! The novel tells of a family tragedy between a Northeastern mother-in-law and a Shanghai daughter-in-law. Mother-in-law and daughter-in-law relationships have always been difficult to describe, and the man caught in between is like double-sided tape. What should he do?

In Yaping's eyes, his wife is one with him, within the range he can manage, someone he can negotiate and unite with; but for his mother, you can only bow your head and lower your eyebrows. Some things, he clearly knows that the old lady will definitely say something that doesn't suit his wife's heart, but he can't say to his mother: "If you keep talking nonsense, I'll show you!" This kind of anger, these words with implicit threats, can only be said to the wife who shares his bed and perhaps will accompany him for life. There is an ancient teaching here: respect for rulers and fathers. To the wife, the two are equal, but to the mother, you can only be humble and grateful. With your mother, you have no reason.

Part One of "Double-Sided Tape"

The fabric sofa had towels spread from the backrest to the seat. Because one towel wasn't enough, two different patterns were squeezed into the corners, looking very uncoordinated. Originally, the colorful sofa matched the decoration, but now it looks strange, like a patched coat full of holes. "That sofa left out in the air won't stay bright for long. You need to know how to maintain it. When there's no one around, cover it with a cloth. What's the harm? Remove it when people come over. It's easier to wash this way." Lijuan wanted to say: It matters. It affects my quality of life and the mood at home. I could roll with Yaping on the sofa and make love, but now I've lost interest. She endured it, didn't say anything, and didn't agree with her mother-in-law. Her mother-in-law felt a little awkward.

Part Two of "Double-Sided Tape"

"Is that called eating? I cut a bowl of braised pork into 28 pieces, you eat 8, your dad eats 7, she eats 13! If this dish was at home, adding some radish and potatoes, your dad and I could eat it for a week! Think about it, how much food waste is there in a month? Look at all those clothes in your cabinet! How many bodies does a person have? You can change outfits every day for a whole month without repeating. Gas, electricity, phone, your mobile phones, transportation costs, which one doesn't cost money?... Mom has been through it all. Things you haven't experienced, mom will tell you beforehand. I can't just watch as you walk by the river without helping. Your lives are too precarious!" Yaping nodded in agreement.

Part Three of "Double-Sided Tape"

Here, Lijuan wants to settle things down, but Yaping's mother isn't willing to let go. Yaping's mother sees Lijuan's recent coldness as the result of making her wash dishes once. At first, Yaping's mother could pretend not to see Lijuan's expressionless face, which actually dripped with resentment, and still greeted her warmly, trying to win her over with the grace and dignity of an elder. Unfortunately, Lijuan had already firmly taken the position that well water and river water don't mix, unwilling to have any connection. The kindness shown by her mother-in-law was coldly analyzed by her as a policy of appeasement, a sign of submission in front of her son. Once she softened and responded, she would immediately return to the previous state of being arranged, controlled, and ordered around. She had only agreed not to have a direct conflict with Yaping's mother, but she didn't agree to sacrifice her own wishes to please her mother-in-law. In Lijuan's view, what she was doing now was already the greatest contribution she could make to the family.

Part Four of "Double-Sided Tape"

Yaping's mother left, and Yaping's life returned to its original track. He slept until the last minute before being late, only to find no neatly prepared work clothes on the bedside table, but a deeply sleeping wife. He hastily grabbed a wrinkled shirt from the hanger and put it on. Looking in the mirror, the vigor of a few days ago had turned into disheveled attire. Entering the kitchen, the stove was cold, and opening the refrigerator revealed nothing but milk and bread. Previously, he thought eating dry rice with tofu brine or pickled cabbage for breakfast was uncomfortable, but after only two days, he missed his mother's cooking. By the shoe rack, his leather shoes were covered with fine dust. He searched for a cloth to wipe them but couldn't find one.

Part Five of "Double-Sided Tape"

"Hu Lijuan! I'll kill you!!!" Yaping leaped up, grabbing Lijuan's neck with both hands and pressing down with all his might. Lijuan's back of the head slammed hard against the wall. Lijuan didn't resist, didn't kick or struggle, her face quickly turning purple as she looked at Yaping with despairing and reproachful eyes, the whites of her eyes growing larger and larger. Suddenly, Yaping came to his senses and loosened his fingers. Lijuan collapsed on the ground, and for a long time, no sound of breathing could be heard. Yaping panicked, pinching Lijuan's nose with one hand and pressing her philtrum with the other. On the other side, Yaping's mother lay motionless.

Part Six of "Double-Sided Tape"

Baby showed no gratitude and retaliated sharply: "Mommy bad!" This was the baby's first three-syllable word, a complete sentence with a subject and predicate, personal pronouns and adjectives, even carrying class emotions in tone. Lijuan stared straight at Yaping and said: "Li Yaping! In this house, I think there can only be one mistress. That's me!" Lijuan gritted her teeth, her eyes glowing green like a trapped beast. If eyes could eat, Lijuan would have already swallowed the panicked Yaping whole. "What I mean is, you have no choice. I stay, and this old woman leaves! Or I stay, and the two of you leave! My words are very clear. Tell me your decision tomorrow morning. Exactly how many people will leave this house."
