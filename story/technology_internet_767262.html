Farewell Cookie: Microsoft Develops Its Own User Tracking Technology

by anonymous on 2013-10-11 19:11:05

According to a report on Ad Age, Microsoft is currently developing its own user tracking technology to replace the widely used "cookie".

The report said that Microsoft is developing new user information collection technology for computers, tablets, smartphones running Windows system and its Xbox gaming consoles. This technology will also be applied to Internet services such as IE browser and Bing search engine.

As a result, Google, Apple, and Microsoft, three industry giants, have all joined the ranks of abandoning cookie tracking.

For third-party cookies, this is another big blow. This technology has long been used to track users' online browsing history to push precise advertisements, but it is now gradually falling behind the times.

Cookies cannot record mobile user behavior that accounts for 20% of global Internet traffic, nor are they suitable for TV and online video services. Advertisers have been working hard to encourage tech companies to abandon cookies in order to break down the barriers between desktops and mobile devices.

Microsoft's move indicates that third-party cookies will give way to information collection systems designed by Internet giants (Microsoft, Google, Facebook, and Apple). Microsoft, Google, and Facebook will track users' information when they log into social networks, while Apple tracks iPhone and iPad users through a system called IDFA.

Analysts say that because Microsoft's user tracking technology only applies to its own products, advertisers may not be so willing to accept it, but a cookie substitute applicable to Xbox will undoubtedly be welcomed, and it is also an excellent opportunity to connect traditional TV channels with digital channels.
