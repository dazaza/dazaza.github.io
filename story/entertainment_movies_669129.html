This film is adapted from the best-selling novel of the same name by Harry Black and Tony Titeltz. The story revolves around the three siblings of the Gris family - 9-year-old twin brothers Jerry and Simon (played by Freddie Highmore), who settle down with their mother (Mary-Louise Parker) and 13-year-old sister Malory (Sarah Bolger) in a mysterious large house in the countryside of Montreal. The shadow of their parents' recent divorce makes the three children feel bored in the serene rural life, but as strange events occur one after another, they realize that their lives are not as simple as they imagined. The brothers can hear strange noises coming from the walls when they strain their ears; Malory's hair gets tied to the bedhead by an invisible hand while she sleeps; and some mysterious force wreaks havoc in the mother's kitchen. When the adults completely refuse to believe that the abnormal situations at home are due to this mysterious house, Jerry, driven by curiosity, begins an investigation into the incidents - starting from the abnormal situation in the elevator, he traces it back to finding a secret room, and in the dirty and messy attic, he finds a strange book recording legends of goblins and fairies. Undoubtedly, everything changes when Jerry bravely opens this book. Originally, this ancient house belongs to the territory governed by "Spiderwick" (Trap Fantasy Chronicles). After the strange book is opened, it immediately awakens various small goblins and ugly little devils from the Spiderwick Chronicles. Most critically, they also awaken King Arthur (David Strathairn) of the Spiderwick Chronicles, making an already bizarre situation even more uncontrollable. Is this hideous king a benevolent being or a tyrant? The three children face a great challenge...
