The Birth of the Black Knight

McTi Siong has always been a business-minded individual since he was young and deeply understands that doing business is the way to success. Therefore, he worked hard on his studies and successfully completed his MBA at Stanford in the US, while also seeking opportunities there.

After graduation, Siong saw potential in Ho Man Tin's company and took the initiative to recommend himself to Man Tin. He plainly stated that he didn't want to be an employee but rather a partner, willing even if it was just for 0.01% of the shares. Seeing that Siong was different from others, Man Tin allowed Siong to join as a partner. Their relationship became both teacher and friend, cooperating seamlessly in specializing in acquiring companies to sell off, becoming well-known snipers in the American business world.

Later, Siong became the notorious Black Knight in the business world, known for being ruthless and merciless. Gradually gaining power, the differences in opinions between him and Man Tin also grew larger. For the sake of the company's interests and development, Siong decided to fully acquire Man Tin's company. Unable to bear the blow, Man Tin drove drunk. Siong learned that Man Tin's car lost control and fell into the sea. After the incident, Man Tin's body could not be found, but Siong believed that Man Tin was dead, feeling extremely guilty. Is the value of a deal really greater than friendship, life? Siong reflected on everything in the past and decided to no longer be the Black Knight, retiring from the business world. From the newspaper, Siong learned that Man Tin's wife and child were living in hardship in Hong Kong. Siong believed the responsibility was on him and decided to return to Hong Kong to take care of Man Tin's family.
