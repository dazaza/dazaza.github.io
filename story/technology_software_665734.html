Love is just three words.

by yn6bh5s6tf on 2012-02-15 09:33:00

Jennifer and Steve's wedding was scheduled to take place next spring when the flowers bloom, because Jennifer hoped that her wedding banquet would be filled with spring blossoms. As the wedding day approached, she was filled with sweet anticipation, the feelings of a soon-to-be bride were indescribably wonderful.

That day, Jennifer had an appointment with Mrs. Russo in the evening at her tailor shop to pick up her custom-made wedding dress. The weather wasn't great; it was foggy in the morning and didn’t clear until noon. Then light rain started, lasting all the way into the evening. In Mrs. Russo’s little shop, as the sound of the drizzle hitting the window glass could be heard, Jennifer suddenly felt uneasy. That day, Steve was supposed to go to the city to purchase some wedding supplies. But with this weather, and his old car which had been used for many years... "I hope nothing happens to him," Jennifer worriedly said.

Mrs. Russo, who had just taken the wedding dress from the hanger, smiled and comforted Jennifer: "No, my dear, cheer up. You two love each other so much, you will surely grow old together." Jennifer took the wedding dress from Mrs. Russo's hands. With its exquisite tailoring and beautiful lace edges, she seemed to see herself walking down the aisle towards the altar of marriage. Maybe she was worrying too much, Jennifer shook her head and dismissed those unnecessary thoughts.

Just then, the telephone in the tailor shop rang sharply, startling both her and Mrs. Russo. Mrs. Russo turned around to answer it. Her expression instantly became very serious. Seeing Mrs. Russo's face, Jennifer's heart leaped into her throat. Mrs. Russo told her an unfortunate piece of news: Steve had been in a car accident on his way back to town and was now in the hospital.

When Jennifer rushed to the hospital, the doctor could only regretfully inform her that Steve's life was saved, but he would spend the rest of his life in a wheelchair. No words could describe Jennifer's feelings at that moment; a dream of spring was shattered on this winter night. Her tears flowed down her red cheeks, frozen by the cold wind, and fell to the ground with a distinct sound.

In the hospital bed, Jennifer saw Steve, who had survived the accident. He looked so tired and disheartened. Under the white blanket lay his lower body, which had undergone surgery and was now empty. Jennifer went forward to comfort him, but she was already sobbing uncontrollably.

The hospital installed prosthetic limbs for Steve, but since Steve had spinal cord damage, these prosthetics could only serve as decoration. When Jennifer wheeled Steve out of the hospital in his wheelchair, Steve made a surprising decision: he wanted to break their engagement. Everyone knew that Steve had decided this out of fear of dragging Jennifer down, and Jennifer naturally understood this as well. However, no matter how Jennifer expressed her love for him, Steve remained unmoved. He even refused to see Jennifer again.

Seeing her lost-and-then-found lover, only to lose him again, Jennifer was overwhelmed with pain. Although Steve lived in the same small town as her, he felt as distant as if they were thousands of miles apart. Spring was approaching step by step, and the wildflowers bloomed splendidly in the suburbs, but Jennifer's heart still lived in winter.

One day, while spinning his wheelchair to the town hospital for a follow-up visit, Steve saw Jennifer at the hospital entrance after a long absence. She was crying alone by the lake in the hospital, holding a diagnosis report in her hand. Steve was somewhat worried, after all, he still deeply loved this kind-hearted girl. He wheeled himself closer and called out Jennifer's name. As soon as Jennifer saw him, she immediately threw herself into his arms and cried her heart out. It turned out that Jennifer had been diagnosed with a tumor in her throat. Although it was benign, it needed to be surgically removed, and the operation would damage her vocal cords. In other words, after the surgery, Jennifer would never be able to speak again.

A gentle spring breeze blew across the lake and lightly brushed against Steve's face, yet he felt a bone-chilling cold. It was Jennifer's tears drying on his face one by one. At that moment, when Jennifer's frail body trembled gently in his embrace, he realized how deeply he loved this girl. Jennifer had endured too much hardship, and his heart ached repeatedly. So he softly said, "Don't be sad, Jennifer. By the time your surgery is over, the spring flowers will have bloomed. Shall we get married then?"

Jennifer's surgery was scheduled to take place in two weeks. To ensure post-surgical safety, she had to undergo the procedure at a major hospital in New York City. Due to Steve's mobility issues and the long distance, Jennifer did not want him to come along and instead went to New York accompanied by a doctor from the local hospital. Steve promised Jennifer that he would prepare for their wedding at their future home, complete with curtains like rosy clouds, tablecloths dotted with tiny flowers, and rooms full of flowers.

Before leaving, Jennifer told Steve three final words before she lost her voice: "I do!" These were the three words Jennifer was supposed to say to the priest during the wedding ceremony, because by that day she might no longer be able to speak. She wanted to solemnly tell her beloved these three words in advance.

Jennifer's surgery went smoothly. Even though on the day of the wedding, she could no longer verbally promise every commitment of love to the priest, there was no denying that everyone heard her promise of love through her tears.

After their marriage, Steve and Jennifer opened a small cake shop. Jennifer made delicious pastries, and Steve stayed in the shop to sell them one by one. Every evening, they would close the cake shop and go for a walk by the beautiful lake. Jennifer pushed Steve, and they communicated with pens, gestures, glances, and hearts. Anyone could see their happiness.

Such days continued for more than forty years. Their silhouettes walking in the twilight had become the most touching scenery in the town. Until one day, while searching for an old friend's address at home, Steve couldn't find it for a long time. Just as he was about to give up, he saw a yellowed piece of paper - Jennifer's diagnosis report from years ago. Steve accidentally flipped open the diagnosis report and found a line that shocked him: Hospital Misdiagnosis Record!

Steve feared that he might have hallucinated due to his age and occasional vision errors, so he put on his glasses. But there was no mistake; the diagnosis report indeed bore the hospital's misdiagnosis stamp.

That evening, they didn't go for a walk by the lake. Steve handed the diagnosis report to Jennifer. Jennifer didn't deny it. She used gestures to tell him the truth, something she had grown accustomed to doing over the years to communicate with her husband. That day, she had indeed received the diagnosis report and thought she would lose her beautiful voice, so she cried bitterly by the small lake. But at that moment, she met Steve and heard his proposal. She was so happy at that moment; she even thought God wanted her to exchange her voice for lifelong happiness. With Steve, she felt that even losing her voice wasn't such a terrible thing. Soon after, the doctor told her that it was a misdiagnosis; the so-called tumor was just a problem with the equipment.

Jennifer hesitated. She was afraid that this correction would make her happiness fly away, because she knew Steve too well - he wouldn't want a perfect woman to stay by his side and take care of him. She really wanted to enter the marriage hall with her beloved in the springtime, surrounded by flowers, and wear that beautiful wedding dress. She didn't want it to gather dust anymore. After much deliberation, Jennifer chose to deceive, asking the doctor to help her keep everything secret because she was so eager to obtain this hard-won happiness.

For over 40 years of silence, Jennifer had already lost her ability to speak, and the only words she could utter were three: "I do!"

To not forget these three words, she often repeated them silently when she was alone, because behind these three words were countless unspoken promises.

When Jennifer once again stammered out "I do," Steve was already in tears.

Love, in fact, is a very simple matter. Sometimes, it's just three words: "I do!"

(Translated from Reader's Digest)
