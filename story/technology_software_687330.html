Wu Dong Qian Kun: Youth Suffers from Rare Disease with Persistent High Fever; Parents Incur Debts to Fund His Treatment

by sznstejx3q3 on 2012-03-02 15:10:57

The mother fed Yafei water and, bringing up this hard-to-cure disease, couldn't help but cry. The photo was taken by Wang Di, a reporter from Henan Business Daily. By Sun Yirui, Henan Business Daily.

Yafei, a 21-year-old young man from the countryside, has been suffering from persistent high fever, chest tightness, and coughing since December last year. He visited four hospitals one after another, but his condition did not improve.

Yesterday, the cause of the illness was finally identified: he had a very rare disease called Langerhans cell histiocytosis. According to reports, the incidence rate of this disease is only one in two million.

After three months of persistent high fever, the doctors said that Yafei had a rare disease.

At 11 o'clock yesterday morning, I came to the Third Respiratory Department of Henan Chest Hospital and saw Yafei lying on the hospital bed. His eyes were slightly closed, his face was sallow, and he looked very weak.

"Every day, he just drinks some congee. He doesn't have the strength to talk," said his mother, Wang Furen. They are from Dawan Zhang Village, Longquan Township, Ye County, Pingdingshan City, Henan Province. There is only one son in their family, Yafei. Since December last year, Yafei started having repeated high fevers. They went to three hospitals, and after being hospitalized for many days, his condition didn't improve. Two days ago, the Henan Chest Hospital performed an open-chest biopsy on Yafei and found that Yafei had a very rare disease - Langerhans cell histiocytosis.

"This disease is not a tumor, but its malignancy is no less than a tumor," said Dr. Zhang Manlin, Yafei's attending physician. Langerhans cell histiocytosis is an immune system disease with an incidence rate of about one in two million.

To treat his son’s illness, the father sold the dog they had raised for many years.

Although the cause of the illness was identified, the money needed for treatment became a difficult problem for the whole family.

"The hospital made an exception and performed the biopsy surgery even though we hadn’t paid," said Wang Furen. Now, there is no more money at home. The hospital said that if the fees were not renewed, the treatment would have to be interrupted.

In April last year, Yafei's father suffered from hemiplegia and spent 70,000 yuan on hospitalization. Although his life was saved, he became disabled, unable to normally stretch his hands, and walking with difficulty in his left leg. He even needs others to feed him when eating.

"I can't work anymore, and Yafei was the mainstay of our family. I never expected him to get such a disease," said the father. Treating Yafei's disease has already cost 70,000 to 80,000 yuan, not including the more than 9,000 yuan owed to the hospital for the surgery fee.

The father said that all the family's savings have been spent, and they borrowed several ten thousand yuan from relatives and friends. To raise medical expenses for his son, he sold everything sellable at home, including a dog they had raised for many years, which he sold to a dog trader for 70 yuan.

Yafei's sister is only seven years old. After her brother was hospitalized, her parents took care of him around the clock, leaving no one to take care of her schooling. She could only ask for leave from school and accompany her parents in the hospital to take care of her brother.

Multiple organs invaded, he urgently needs chemotherapy.

"The patient's current condition is relatively serious. The disease has already invaded multiple organs including his lungs, liver, spleen, and bone marrow. He urgently needs chemotherapy," said Zhang Manlin.

Subsequently, she showed the reporters a recent chest X-ray of Yafei. Compared to a healthy lung, Yafei's right lung has additional honeycomb-like abnormalities, which is a sign of the disease invading the lungs.

Zhang Manlin said that if not treated promptly, other organs in the body will continue to be invaded, posing a life-threatening risk.

However, she also indicated that if Yafei's condition stabilizes, it will not significantly impact his future life. Regarding Yafei's next treatment, Zhang Manlin said, "Immune supplements and anti-tumor drugs will be required."
