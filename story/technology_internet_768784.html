Alibaba teams up with Zop to launch the Zop Little Black 2 smartphone, equipped with the octa-core chip MT6592.

by anonymous on 2013-11-22 11:51:50

Zhuopu Xiahei 2

November 21 news: Yesterday MediaTek released the MT6592 octa-core processor, and previously, domestic smartphone manufacturers such as Xiaomi, Huawei, and Zhuopu had all announced plans to launch octa-core phones equipped with MediaTek's MT6592. Today, the Zhuopu Xiahei 2 phone was unveiled at a launch event in Shenzhen, marking the debut of the first Alibaba-Zhuopu collaboration featuring an MediaTek octa-core phone.

According to reports, Alibaba has joined forces with Zhuopu to integrate its smart operating system, Yun OS, into the newly launched Xiahei 2 phone. Both parties have reached a strategic cooperation agreement, and Zhuopu’s entire domestic product line will fully support the Yun OS operating system going forward. This marks another strategically significant collaboration between Alibaba and Zhuopu since their initial partnership in April when they launched the first flagship device, the Zhuopu Xiahei C2.

According to data provided by Zhuopu, the Xiahei 2 is equipped with MediaTek's MT6592 octa-core 1.7GHz processor, utilizing ARM Mali-450 MP graphics processing unit, along with 2GB+32GB memory. Display-wise, the Xiahei 2 features a 5.5-inch FHD full HD screen with a resolution of 1920×1080 that supports glove operation and wet hand usage. For the camera, the Xiahei 2 boasts a 14-megapixel back-illuminated CMOS sensor on the rear and a 5-megapixel front-facing camera with an 80° wide angle. It also includes an external quick-capture button that allows users to take photos with just one press, enabling one-touch fast shooting.

In addition, the Zhuopu Xiahei 2 offers some distinctive operational features: under the black screen state, users can easily access desired interfaces by drawing different gestures on the screen. For instance, drawing an "e" brings up the browser page, while drawing a "c" opens the dialing interface.

At the launch event, Zhang Chunhui, General Manager of the Yun OS Mobile Division, introduced the future release plan for Yun OS and officially launched version 2.3 of Alibaba's Yun OS. Yun OS 2.3 can intelligently identify payment scenarios, supporting context-based payments, quick payments, and secure payments. It establishes security mechanisms from the bottom layer, protecting passwords and accounts in real-time through secure controls to prevent theft, ensuring user safety. The cloud sync feature offers backup storage functionality, including a new system configuration backup function.
