If you're planning to buy solid wood stairs, understanding how to purchase, select, and maintain them becomes crucial. Below, we'll learn how to choose and maintain solid wood stairs.

**Purchasing and Selecting Solid Wood Stairs**

1. First, roughly determine the type of wood you need. This is generally based on the decoration standard of your home. You can refer to the materials used in your furniture, doors, or flooring to choose wood of the same or higher grade. If you’re unfamiliar with wood types, don't worry—Muyuan Shangpin will provide detailed references.

2. Choosing the material is relatively simple. Next, you’ll need to select the style of the stairs or doors. The style of the stairs is usually determined by the design of the stair posts and the overall color scheme of the staircase. Therefore, you should choose a stair post that suits your home and a color that complements your main interior palette. If the color isn’t available in the swatch, you can provide your own sample, and Muyuan Shangpin will match it to create a satisfying solid wood staircase for you.

3. There are numerous styles of stairs, so picking the one that suits you best is key! The next and most important step is to accurately measure and reasonably design the stairs for your home. Before customizing the stairs for our customers, Muyuan Shangpin will conduct a detailed preliminary measurement at your home. Based on this, our designer will create a plan. Once confirmed by the customer, we’ll revisit with the design plans for another precise measurement to make any necessary adjustments. After these steps, production can begin.

**Maintaining Solid Wood Stairs**

1. Proper use and maintenance of the stairs are essential to ensuring their durability and lasting appeal.

2. For daily cleaning, simply use a vacuum cleaner or soft broom. If there’s dirt, you can clean it with a slightly damp cloth.

3. Stubborn stains can be removed with alcohol or cleaning agents, but avoid using strong corrosive solvents like turpentine or paint thinner.

4. It's recommended to wear soft-soled shoes when stepping on the stairs to reduce wear and tear.

5. Handrails are designed to bear dynamic force; slight vibrations are normal due to elastic support.
