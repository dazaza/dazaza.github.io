Six Mistakes to Avoid When Decorating Small Apartments

by longersing on 2009-10-28 14:46:53

Avoiding Six Common Mistakes in Small Apartment Renovation: 1. Dull wall colors, 2. Complicated ceiling designs, 3. Zoned floor decorations, 4. Hard dividers, 5. Monotonous lighting, 6. Oversized furniture. These details might make your home decoration appear disorganized.

1. Dull Wall Colors

Choosing the main color for a small space is challenging. Typically, you should choose a color system with low brightness and purity, commonly known as the gray system. The more intense the color's purity, the more it catches the eye first, which can give a feeling of compression and make the space feel smaller psychologically. Brighter colors should be chosen to create an expansive sensory experience, making the space feel "spacious and bright."

2. Complicated Ceiling Designs

Most small apartments have lower ceilings, so the ceiling design should be minimalistic. Thin, simple-shaped ceiling decorations should be preferred, or no ceiling at all. If the ceiling shape is too regular, it will emphasize the spatial boundaries of the ceiling. Consider irregularly shaped ceilings or wooden or aluminum grid ceilings. You can also experiment with new materials that are unconventional, adding creativity without making the space feel cramped.

3. Zoned Floor Decorations

Small apartments often have narrow and winding spaces. Many people decorate different areas with different materials and heights to highlight regional differences, often echoed by the ceiling. This creates even more complex spatial structures and many "corridors," leading to visual obstructions and wasted space. The floor color should be unified into a brighter but less pure color system. The floor color should be slightly darker than the furniture to avoid a top-heavy feeling. You can also use mosaics, pebbles, etc., to create some directional decorative lines, breaking the monotony.

4. Hard Dividers

Hard dividers should be used cautiously in small apartment renovations. If not necessary, minimize hard dividers. If needed, consider glass dividers. Transparent glass, frosted glass, and carved glass do not obstruct light or vision and can enhance the sense of spatial integrity, thus becoming increasingly popular among young people in modern decoration. Glass dividers are commonly used between bathrooms and kitchens. This method makes the space feel less confined, removes strict room boundaries, and is simple, practical, and cost-effective.

5. Monotonous Lighting

Because of the simple ceiling design and weak regional boundaries, choosing and using appropriate lighting becomes more difficult. People often only place one or a few main lights, making the space seem overly monotonous. Lighting in small spaces should have primary and secondary elements. Main lights should be large and bright, typically simple flush-mount ceiling lights, supplemented by table lamps, wall lamps, and spotlights. Properly matched lighting and light usage can improve the spatial atmosphere. Emphasize the functionality and layering of the lighting, cross-using different light sources to highlight the main features and clarify functions.

6. Oversized Furniture

Furniture selection for small apartments should prioritize practicality and compactness. Avoid particularly large furniture and decorations, as they may cause inconvenience in use. Carefully compare options before purchasing and always follow the principle of "better small than large." Also, consider storage functionality. Beds should be surrounded by drawers; wardrobes should be narrower but multi-layered, such as tie compartments, belt compartments, shirt compartments, coat compartments, suit compartments, etc., allowing the owner to categorize different items. It’s best to plan the furniture dimensions on a blueprint before selecting and purchasing.
