After losing 0-1 to Novara at home last weekend, the protests faced by Moratti and his Inter Milan exceeded imagination. After the game at Meazza Stadium, when Moratti was about to leave, he encountered hardcore protests: "We thank you for your contributions, but now please let go of our beloved Inter Milan!" In the following two days, fans kept protesting outside Moratti's Saras oil company headquarters. Even some elderly female fans became angry, directly posting slogans like "Shame! Inter Milan is finished!" to protest against Moratti and Inter Milan's inaction or even reckless actions in the transfer market. In an interview with Corriere della Sera, Moratti said: "I am still thinking about the present. In the current situation, we still have goals. We have no reason to give up on our objectives in Serie A and the Champions League. The season is still long, and now is the time for us to give it everything."

Regarding many criticisms of Inter Milan in the transfer market, Moratti also gave his own explanation: "We must improve some structural issues in our management. Everyone must realize that UEFA's Financial Fair Play policy is not a joke, so we need to integrate our resources. You need to know that no one wants to get back on the winning track more than me, and we will work hard to accelerate this process. We are still as ambitious as before, and we will build a new future based on Inter Milan's past glories." When talking about the widespread protests over the sale of Motta and Eto'o, Moratti said: "You need to know that we had no choice but to sell them; we simply didn't have the conditions to keep them. Maybe we made mistakes in some things, but I don't think we made many mistakes or did anything more serious. Our problem might be that we adapted too well to UEFA's policies, and now what we need is time. Everyone needs to be patient with us, and then we should evaluate some appropriate solutions for the future." Moratti also emphasized that his passion for Inter Milan has not disappeared: "From June 2005 to May 2011, we were constantly winning championships, almost winning everything, but I didn't stop. I still want to start another winning cycle. The current situation is indeed dangerous because the media and fans seem very tense now, and they seem to want to tear everything down and start again."

When leaving the office of Saras Petroleum Company, Moratti made further comments to the media regarding fan protests: "Fan protests? I wasn't there after the game where they protested, and I wasn't in Milan yesterday, but I read the newspapers. Fans have the right to freely express their thoughts. Some people will continue to wait, while others will complain outright, which is all normal. Our fans have always been in this state, showing the close connection between them and our club. This group of fans easily gets excited and hopes for Inter Milan to improve and remain unique forever. Sometimes we must endure difficult situations, but sometimes we need to send out the right messages. Fans now seem to exaggerate by implying that the team's condition is worse than they know, because I understand the real situation of the club. I think they are afraid we will sell Sneijder, just as an example, or similar situations. At such times, you need to tell everyone the truth. The current reactions from fans can be understood, but honestly, I'm not happy. Do protests give us more motivation? No, true motivation never comes from protests; true motivation only comes from the desire to do better. Of course, protests are also a special form of encouragement."

On the previous Friday, Gasperini criticized Inter Milan in an interview with Gazzetta dello Sport, accusing the club of deceiving him on transfer issues and shifting the blame for poor results onto the club. Regarding this, Moratti said: "To be honest, I didn't read his complaints. Are his complaints true? At the right time, we (management) will do very well."

In the evening, Moratti met with members of the local Inter Milan fan club in Somma Lombardo, where his father was born. He openly discussed the current state of Inter Milan with these fans: "Let's talk about serious topics, such as our championship team. Obviously, I cannot be happy with the current state of the team, but you need to know that this is a team that has won many championships. If this team has won a lot, it means these players fit well together as a team, and each player is individually very good. In this process, we have become accustomed to these players, and we are also accustomed to how each of them plays, so it's not easy to replace them because it means you must find a player of the same level, or hope that the player can rejuvenate. It's hard to replace players one by one according to a template, especially those who are already good players. What you can do, and what you must do, is to slowly and calmly complete the transformation in a gradual process, and this process requires time. This process will not always be smooth, which is what is currently happening, and it's also what happened at the start of this season."

"I need to explain the current situation. At the beginning of this year, we didn't perform well. Before the start of the year, we were all full of hope and confidence, believing that a new formation would create some sparks, but it didn't happen. The effect was completely opposite to what we expected, so we decided to make changes during the season, which was changing the coach, and this change was positive. However, it's not easy for someone who temporarily takes over the captain's duties to steer the ship because he takes over in the middle of the season. I've talked to Ranieri, and I think he's great. He had some trouble at the beginning, but he successfully transplanted his tactical ideas into the players' minds and motivated them to achieve their goals. But then we encountered some unlucky games and events, except for the game against Roma because it had nothing to do with luck; it was just a poorly played game. We were very unlucky against Novara and Palermo. Luck was not on Ranieri's side, nor on the club's side, but this did not change our overall plan."

Moratti also talked about future plans: "Let's continue discussing our plans, and this makes me think that perhaps someone will talk to you about what might happen in the future, but who really knows what will happen then? As for the plan, we deal with football every week, including passion, fear, pain, and joy, so talking about a plan may not be as attractive as talking about football. But I believe that by the end of the season, all of us here who are desperately seeking Inter Milan's way forward will be more satisfied than now. And we are currently working hard to understand how to build a team that can restore our confidence, and this is the team we wanted at the start of this season. I initially thought this team was at least decent, but the results have been unsatisfactory, but I am confident that in the coming years, we will introduce young elements."

Finally, Moratti summarized: "Inter Milan's history tells us what we used to be, what we are now, and what we will be in the future, and we don't want to return to our past standards. However, you fans still maintain your essence, so I won't promise you a peaceful transition, but after this chaos, we will surely get back to work, without a doubt."
