Water is Crucial for Babies' Growth and Development

Water is an essential component of human tissue. The water in the body, collectively referred to as body fluid, is concentrated within cells, between tissues, and inside various ducts. It is a key substance in the composition of cells, tissue fluid, and plasma.

Due to water's high solubility and fluidity, many substances dissolve in it. Water participates in the metabolic processes of all organs in the body and plays a "critical" role in the absorption, digestion, transportation, and excretion of nutrients.

The heat generated during large-scale metabolic processes in the body is absorbed by water, which dissipates the heat through evaporation, maintaining normal body temperature.

The glands in the body are all liquid-based. If there is a lack of water, glandular secretions decrease, especially those from endocrine glands, which can affect the growth and development of babies.

Water makes up 80% of blood, serving as the "main force" in maintaining blood volume. If there is substantial water loss, blood volume will inevitably decrease, leading to low blood pressure and affecting the function of organs.

Drinking Water Scientifically

Q: What kind of water is good to drink?

A: Purified water removes impurities, bacteria, and viruses from tap water using activated carbon or purification membranes, but it also removes trace elements and minerals. Moreover, industrial materials used in the process can have adverse effects on infants' liver function:

Mineral water comes from deep underground groundwater and contains trace elements needed by the body. However, excessive metallic elements can burden a baby's kidneys;

Distilled water is formed by vaporizing water using distillation equipment and then condensing it. Since trace elements are removed, long-term consumption is not beneficial for babies;

Packaged water is reused repeatedly, and after being opened for two days, bacteria can easily grow, causing gastrointestinal diseases in babies;

Boiled water is drinking water made by boiling tap water. It has a higher concentration of aluminum ions, and excessive intake can affect bone and nerve development.

Therefore, none of these types of water are suitable for babies to drink long-term. However, you can alternate between different kinds of water to reduce any negative side effects on your baby's health.

Q: How much water should I give my baby each day?

A: For full-term newborns, within the first 24 hours of birth, they need about 20 milliliters of water per kilogram of body weight daily. If there is sufficient breast milk, generally no additional water is needed;

For infants aged 1-3 months, water should be given 3-4 times a day, with each serving being 30-50 milliliters;

For infants over 4 months old, water should be given 3-4 times a day, with each serving being 50-100 milliliters.

The amount of water a baby needs depends on their diet and the weather temperature. If their main food is milk and porridge, less water is required; when the weather is cold and activity levels are low, water intake decreases; during hot weather or when the baby has a fever and sweats more, water intake should increase.

Warm Reminder

If a baby becomes dehydrated, the situation can be more severe than in adults. Therefore, when a baby has a high fever, vomits, or has diarrhea, mothers should ensure that the baby gets enough fluids promptly.

There are rules regarding when to drink water. Do not give water within one hour before meals, during meals, or before sleeping, as this can dilute gastric juices and affect digestion and sleep.

Do not replace drinking water with cold drinks or juice, and do not let babies drink sugary water, as excessive carbohydrates in the body can lead to obesity.

Observe the baby's urine output. If the baby urinates very little in half a day and the urine color is dark, then the baby needs water.

Never give babies raw water, water re-boiled, or water boiled for a long time, as this can disrupt their gastrointestinal system and cause bloating and diarrhea.
