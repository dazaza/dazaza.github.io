Wang Xiaoliang Catches Frogs

Column: Fairy Tales Added Time: January 13, 2011, 1:50:53 PM Source: Admin Clicks: 40

The troublemaker Wang Xiaoliang went to his grandma's house and saw many frogs in the rice fields beside her home, so he started catching them. Someone noticed and said that frogs are friends of humans, advising him not to catch them, but he wouldn't listen. In the end, he caught them until it was dark, filling a large bucket with frogs, capturing all the frogs in the field. That night, when he returned home, he didn’t have a moment’s peace.

First, that big bucket of frogs croaked loudly, giving him a headache. His uncle scolded him and ordered him to release the frogs back into the field the next morning. Then, just as he fell asleep, he heard a knock on the door. When he opened it, he saw a group of grasshoppers, leeches, and rice bugs banging drums, playing gongs, and setting off firecrackers to thank him. They were holding several large banners that read: "Thank you, great hero Wang Xiaoliang, for removing our natural enemies and exterminating harmful insects. The insect population of Xishuigou rice fields will be forever grateful to you. We hope you don’t spare those bad frogs." Afraid that his uncle would hear the noise and come out to yell at him again, Wang Xiaoliang quickly told them to stop the drumming, fireworks, and to take down the banners and return to the rice fields.

Just after the locusts and rice bugs left, there was another commotion outside, followed by more knocking on the door. Wang Xiaoliang thought it was the locusts returning, but when he opened the door, he saw a large group of frogs, toads, and even some toadstools. They too were beating drums, playing gongs, and holding banners, though they weren’t setting off firecrackers. The banners read: "Return our flesh and blood compatriots, or we will repay blood debts with blood. Illegal detention is a crime." As Wang Xiaoliang stood at the door wondering what “repaying blood debts with blood” meant, the frogs inside heard their kindred coming to rescue them and began croaking as well. Outside, hearing the cries from inside, they became even more urgent. A toad jumped onto Wang Xiaoliang's foot and looked up at him threateningly: "Wang Xiaoliang, will you release my frog brothers? If you don't, I'll bite you to death!" Before Wang Xiaoliang could react, a toad leapt onto his shoulder, its tongue flicking as it spoke: "I am the cousin of the frogs. If you don't release my younger cousins, I'll spray venom into your mouth. Will you release them or not?" Scared, Wang Xiaoliang's legs trembled, and he hurriedly said: "Yes, yes, I'll release them right away. Please get down."

He released all the frogs, who then happily beat their drums and gongs as they left. Frightened, Wang Xiaoliang thought to himself: "From now on, I must never catch beneficial insects again."
