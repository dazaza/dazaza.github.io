Experts from the Laser Cosmetology Center of Huaxia Hospital in Jinan introduced that there are several laser methods for treating port-wine stains: traditional carbon dioxide lasers, single-wavelength pulsed dye lasers, photodynamic therapy, dual-wavelength pulsed dye lasers, also known as (Cynergy) vascular lesion workstations.

Port-wine stain treatment method one: Traditional carbon dioxide laser. The principle of the traditional carbon dioxide laser is to use the photo-thermal reaction generated after the tissue absorbs high-energy laser, causing the local temperature to rise sharply to hundreds of degrees or even higher within seconds. This leads to coagulative necrosis of the tissue, or even carbonization or vaporization. At the same time, due to the sudden intense heating, the water content in the tissue suddenly decreases significantly. After focusing, it can be used to cut or burn diseased tissues, so the skin may be damaged, and infection is easily caused. If it is on important areas such as the head and face, scars may be left behind, leading to adhesion and other consequences.

Port-wine stain treatment method two: Single-wavelength pulsed dye laser. The single-wavelength pulsed dye laser has been updated compared to the traditional carbon dioxide laser, but due to the limitation of wavelength, the depth and range of treatment are limited.

Port-wine stain treatment method three: Photodynamic action. Photodynamic therapy is also effective for port-wine stains. Photodynamic action refers to the process where, under the participation of a photosensitizer, organic bodies, cells, or biomolecules undergo functional and morphological changes after being excited by light. Severe cases can lead to injury or necrosis. This process requires oxygen participation, hence it is also called photosensitized oxidation. In chemistry, it is referred to as photosensitization. In biology and medicine, it is called photodynamic action. A disadvantage of photodynamic therapy is that after treatment with first-generation photosensitizers, one needs to avoid light for a month. Second-generation photosensitizers only require a month of light avoidance but are still in the experimental stage. Additionally, for children, general anesthesia is required because they may move around unknowingly and cannot cooperate with the treatment. Common knowledge about congenital pigmentation marks.

Port-wine stain treatment method four: Dual-wavelength pulsed dye laser. The working principle of the dual-wavelength pulsed dye laser for treating port-wine stains is to use laser physical engineering technology to integrate 585nm pulsed dye laser and 1064nm Nd:YAG neodymium-doped yttrium aluminum garnet laser together.

The dual-wavelength pulsed dye laser uses a specific program emission mode, first emitting 585nm dye laser, then emitting 1064nm laser. The main idea is to use the former line to irradiate vascular lesions, converting oxyhemoglobin into methemoglobin, while methemoglobin absorbs 1064nm wavelength stronger than oxyhemoglobin. Using these two wavelengths to treat vascular lesions can achieve the purpose of destroying both superficial and deep blood vessels. Due to the increased absorption of 1064nm, lower laser energy can be used to treat hemangiomas.
