Exclusive interview with the WeChat technical team: They have changed the meaning of WeChat

by anonymous on 2013-11-28 11:09:32

We often hear about Zhang Xiaolong's research on WeChat design, but rarely pay attention to the technical team behind WeChat. In its early versions, due to its focus on information communication functions, there were no standout technologies in WeChat until after version 4.3 when voice recognition and scanning features were added. The combination of new technology with sensors is redefining WeChat.

At yesterday's "WeChat·Public" Partner Conference, WeChat released a voice open platform, officially stepping out of the WeChat realm to compete with voice companies like iFlytek and Unisound. Meanwhile, at this conference, WeChat demonstrated its potential to play a pivotal role in the Internet of Things by showcasing its ability to control devices such as refrigerators, print beautiful pictures, and televisions.

All these technical teams are grouped under the Pattern Recognition Center team. The Pattern Recognition Center has two main areas in the application layer: voice and scanning. In the eyes of the WeChat team, they are extensions of the human body with high strategic significance, so it is essential to have core proprietary technologies.

Voice started from a higher starting point

WeChat's voice recognition may seem to have suddenly emerged. Its leader is Lu Li, who graduated with a Ph.D. from the University of Science and Technology of China, specializing in voice recognition research. After Apple launched Siri in 2011, voice recognition suddenly gained everyone's attention, including Tencent. At the end of 2011, Tencent initiated voice recognition as a scientific research project at the Beijing Research Institute, where Lu Li entered Tencent to lead a team of three people for intensive research.

Technically speaking, although the old-established iFlytek had accumulated ten years of experience, due to significant advancements in related industry papers, the knowledge base was much more complete, so Lu Li could be said to have started from a higher point and achieved faster progress than iFlytek.

Furthermore, according to Liang Jia'en, founder of Unisound, voice recognition is "not difficult once you know how." It's much easier for those who understand it. Lu Li and Liang Jia'en know each other, both being graduates of the University of Science and Technology of China. The voice leaders of Baidu and Sogou also come from the same school.

Of course, the hardships involved in technological breakthroughs are not well known. The process of voice recognition technology evolving from simple to usable and then to user-friendly is a long one. Lu Li considers every six months as a milestone, from the start of development to the release of WeChat 5.0, a total of three milestones, with each milestone bringing a 40% improvement in performance. However, it wasn't until the last six months that the voice recognition underwent a true qualitative change.

"It's like someone eating nine pancakes without feeling full, but after the tenth one, they're satisfied," Lu Li said.

During this period, they experienced two periods of closed development and received guidance from Zhang Xiaolong. After the product went live, the user growth curve was healthy, with high usage rates.

In the voice-to-text function of WeChat 5.0, many people found its performance comparable to iFlytek. Although Tencent started late (less than two years ago), it has already taken the lead in practical applications.

Integrating naturally into products

Although Siri was successfully launched and ignited the market, it eventually became an object of ridicule, considered a half-finished product. How should voice be presented in WeChat to avoid being overly ambitious? This not only tests the technology but also the product capabilities.

Lu Li believes that whether voice is an entry point or not is unimportant. What matters is what voice recognition can achieve. It's akin to traveling from one place to another; the most important thing is reaching the destination, not what happens along the way.

The forms of voice presentation in WeChat include: voice search of contact lists, voice alarms, and voice-to-text conversion. From WeChat 4.3 to WeChat 5.0, the integration of voice gradually deepened, but it was also strictly limited to specific functions. They believed that once the scope of use was expanded, it would easily become superficial.

Siri includes voice recognition, semantic recognition, and search, forming a closed loop. Most voice apps are similar. However, the problem is that products like Siri suggest to users that they can do anything, which, when the technology isn't ready, can easily make the product redundant. WeChat's approach is the opposite: making users aware of what it can do, avoiding creating extra expectations. Moreover, the combination of voice with communication tools naturally works better. Lu Li feels:

"Voice search in the contact list is so natural within WeChat."

With the convening of the WeChat·Public Partner Conference, WeChat's voice has finally become platform-based, enhancing the experience in vertical fields, creating opportunities for WeChat's commercialization. Their next R&D goal remains improving accuracy, perfecting technical details, and competing with the human brain.

There are many new technologies in Scan

According to Liu Hailong, the person in charge of WeChat's scan feature, the scan function can recognize images, text, faces, object retrieval, and even augmented reality. Currently, WeChat only offers text and image recognition, which is the result of various factors. Notably, WeChat's scan function uses video mode, with data being transmitted in real-time without relying on local resources. Although the scan function originated from the previously launched app "Soso Wisdom Eye," the latter still requires taking a picture before uploading for recognition.

Real-time automatic detection reduces one step of user operation, similar to shaking your phone to identify a song, where the results will display the song's playback position in real-time. Transitioning from post-upload recognition to real-time recognition isn't just a simple technology shift; it challenges the requirements for traffic, processing speed, and cloud database construction. The benefits of doing so enhance user experience, lighten the front-end load, and assign heavy tasks to the back-end, keeping WeChat lightweight as much as possible.

In terms of the scan feature, WeChat has reserved a batch of cutting-edge technologies, including business card recognition, face recognition, real-world recognition, object retrieval, and augmented reality. In the current street view scanning, WeChat uses geographical location rather than image matching. However, Liu Hailong mentioned that real-world scanning is feasible, but the issue lies in the requirement of large amounts of traffic and high-speed transmission networks, making it unsuitable for launch currently. The advent of 4G will create opportunities for many new applications, and much work is still focused on optimizing traffic usage.

Why does the scan feature have five functions? Why aren't QR codes and barcodes grouped together? This reflects WeChat's thinking on product design. Liu Hailong believes that the five buttons clearly tell users what they can do, while also reminding them of the limitations. The ideal state of the scan feature is that it appears when needed and disappears when not.

In the future, if the traffic issue is resolved, scanning a movie poster won't just direct users to a review site but bring characters out of the screen to automatically play trailers. Additionally, item retrieval and product scanning—things that PCs couldn't handle—are now possible.

The future of scanning is to make the camera an extension of human vision, connecting the real and virtual worlds. Due to its critical importance, WeChat's scan feature will soon become even more powerful.

It's certain that many functions are already being tested internally, and whether they go live depends on their maturity and user demand. It’s said that before each version update, WeChat creates dozens of versions, selecting the best one to go live. Even more interestingly, they might send a version with new features to one million test users to study the usage rate. The voice-to-text feature was tested and approved in this manner.

Despite the fast-paced version iterations, their work status hasn't been overly strenuous. Doing cutting-edge research involves a lot of algorithm work; brute force doesn’t yield results. Instead, insights often come during moments of leisure. Once understood, implementation becomes swift. However, because of WeChat, they are almost always online, communicating about work at any time.

During interviews, they repeatedly mentioned that thanks to the WeChat platform, their technologies have such vast application spaces. WeChat closely aligns with users, exploring applications through cameras, microphones, and other sensors, greatly changing WeChat's essence. WeChat is no longer just a communication tool but a personal and enterprise service assistant.
