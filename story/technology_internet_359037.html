 【Daily Focus: Who Benefits from the Convergence of Three Networks】

「Report Says Pilot Plan for Convergence of Three Networks Has Passed and Will Begin Implementation on the 25th」

According to reports by the Shanghai Securities News, yesterday Vice Premier Zhang Dejiang presided over a meeting of the National Coordination Group for the Convergence of Three Networks. The specific pilot plan will be published on the government website in the coming days. The broadcasting and television industry will still be responsible for the construction and management of the IPTV integrated broadcast control platform. Applications for pilot projects must be submitted independently by provinces and cities before the 18th, and implementation will begin on the 25th after approval.

「Pilot Cities Not Yet Determined」

Although the pilot plan for the convergence of three networks has been approved, the corresponding list of pilot cities has not yet been finalized. "Previously reported pilot cities were all inaccurate," said Bao Ran, editor-in-chief of China Digital Television. Qualified cities need to submit their pilot applications before the 18th, awaiting approval from relevant departments. It was understood that the State Council has put forward several requirements for pilot regions, including that the region's cable network has completed two-way transformation, the existing cable network users have a certain scale, and the pilot region has stronger consumption capabilities. There are also analysis reports pointing out that Beijing and Changsha are expected to become pilot cities for the convergence of three networks.

「Local Forces and Private Capital May Ultimately Truly Benefit」

CEO of Streaming Media Network Zhang Yanxiang pointed out that traditional broadcasting and television business is difficult to fully open. After the convergence of three networks, the broadcasting and television industry may take its newly obtained telecommunications operating qualifications to carry out external cooperation. However, the broadcasting and television industry itself lacks market operating capabilities, so the ultimate beneficiaries may be local forces and private capital entering the telecommunications market. "Local forces and private capital indeed see huge business opportunities from the convergence of three networks, which will be the real active participants in the future convergence of three networks," said Zhang Yanxiang.

【Market Trends】

「Shenzhen Index Recovers Ten-Thousand Point Major Threshold」

The two markets opened slightly lower and quickly turned red. The Shenzhen Component Index recovered the ten-thousand point major threshold. Both market indices fluctuated slightly around the integer thresholds. As of the morning, the SSE Composite Index was reported at 2520.95 points, and the SZSE Composite Index was reported at 10060.24 points.

「Technical Analysis」

The continuous collapse of the surrounding markets made the Shanghai Composite Index approach the previous low point again during trading hours yesterday. The tightening of domestic real estate regulation and the escalation of the European debt crisis undoubtedly brought enormous pressure to A-shares. From a technical perspective, both markets are expected to show a pattern of opening lower and then rising higher on Tuesday. In the future, even if the index breaks down and falls again, from a valuation perspective, the market has little room to fall here. Therefore, investors should not easily cut positions here. In terms of market hotspots, attention should be paid to the media sector benefiting from policy support.

【Hotspots】

「Ministry of Finance Official: Export Rebate Rate for "Two Highs and One Resource" Products to Be Reduced」

The state is about to reduce the export rebate rate for "two highs and one resource" products to limit their exports. Yesterday, a responsible person from the relevant department of the Ministry of Finance confirmed the possibility of this measure to the media. It is understood that this reduction in export rebate rates does not mean a tightening of China's foreign trade policies, but rather aims to cooperate with national policies to suppress excess capacity. Therefore, industries such as textiles and clothing are not included in the adjustment.

「Funds Withdraw from Large-Cap Stocks, Shenzhen Market Trading Volume Surpasses Shanghai for the First Time in Ten Years」

After multiple trading days of testing last week, the Shenzhen market's trading volume of 7.273 billion yuan historically surpassed the Shanghai market's trading volume of 7.138 billion yuan yesterday. According to statistics from Wind, this is the first time since November 8, 2000, that the Shenzhen market's trading volume has surpassed the Shanghai market's. Some private fund managers believe that the symbolic significance of the Shenzhen market's trading volume surpassing the Shanghai market on Monday is significant, at least reflecting the current attitude of the market—whether funds prefer emerging industries or traditional blue-chip stocks.

【Industry】

「Real Estate Market Volumes in Beijing, Shanghai, and Shenzhen Dropped Significantly in May, Negotiation Space Increased」

According to the latest monitoring data obtained by Century 21 Real Estate, real estate market volumes in Beijing, Shanghai, and Shenzhen collectively dropped significantly in May. Among them, Beijing's second-hand housing transaction volume decreased by 58.8% month-on-month, Shanghai decreased by 69.0%, and Shenzhen decreased by 24.1%. Statistics from the China Index Academy also showed that first-tier city real estate market volumes halved in May, with almost all areas monitored experiencing a drop in transaction area, with a total decrease of approximately 44%. Meanwhile, the negotiation space for housing prices increased, and transaction prices generally declined.

「New Energy Vehicle Support Policies Coming, Electric Vehicles as Main Direction」

It was reported that the development plan for energy-saving and new energy vehicles and a package of support policies would be submitted to the State Council for deliberation in July. If approved, it could be implemented within the year. Implementing a package of support policies for an industry is unprecedented in China, indicating the level of importance placed on the new energy vehicle industry. An authoritative insider revealed that after long-term discussions, the route has now been clarified. The main content is to take pure electric vehicles as the main strategic direction for China's auto industry transformation, focusing on breakthroughs in battery, motor, and electric technology, promoting the industrialization of pure electric vehicles and plug-in hybrid vehicles, achieving a leapfrog development of China's auto industry.
