Taobao draws a national "insomnia map": Shanghai is the city where people are most likely to be sleepless.

by anonymous on 2013-07-24 17:28:43

"Every day, I go to bed around 11 o'clock, then watch videos and browse online shops on my mobile phone. I don't feel sleepy until around 1 o'clock." Mr. Ding, a 30-year-old white-collar worker in a business building on Moganshan Road in Hangzhou, complained. In fact, more and more people are beginning to have trouble sleeping. "People who sleep well are all similar, but those who can't sleep each have their own reasons." The owners of Taobao shops take advantage of this trend and start selling "sleep aid" products.

Yesterday, a set of data published by Taobao was also quite interesting. People who can't sleep have very obvious regional characteristics. In the top 5 cities with the highest per capita purchase volume of sleep aids such as eye masks and earplugs in the past 5 months, the four first-tier cities of Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, and Shenzhen all made the list collectively. Hangzhou is the only non-first-tier city among the top five, ranking third.

The most active "insomniacs":

23 million people shop online at midnight

"At this hour, is anyone still awake?" Nowadays, such late-night insomnia tweets are becoming increasingly common. "Insomnia sufferers" instantly transform into "night shoppers," browsing on mobile Taobao while lying in bed. Their shopping enthusiasm does not diminish due to the quiet of the night; instead, late at night becomes one of the times with a high rate of mobile online shopping orders.

"In such hot weather, there's no way I'd go shopping during the day. When I can't sleep at night, I use that time to shop." Miss Li from Hangzhou is a typical night owl. She says she often doesn't feel sleepy after 12 o'clock, so she opens her mobile phone and browses Taobao. "Yesterday, in just one hour, I bought two pairs of sandals and one dress." Finally, with the satisfaction of shopping, she finally fell asleep. However, her money flows out every day.

There are quite a few such "night shoppers." According to the data released by Taobao, in 2012, 22.832 million people browsed Taobao between 11 PM and 5 AM. Among them, many men stayed up late browsing Taobao, accounting for 48% of the total, while women accounted for 52%.

The most insomniac provinces:

Jiangsu, Guangdong, Zhejiang

What to do if you really can't sleep? More and more people are starting to use external tools like earplugs and eye masks to help them fall asleep. Clever online shop owners have obviously already spotted the business opportunity. A search on Taobao revealed over 200,000 items for sale related to earplugs and nearly 90,000 items for eye masks.

The "sleep aid market" is quite promising. Just the product of eye masks alone comes in various types such as 3D, cartoon patterns, summer-appropriate ice packs, silk eye masks, bamboo charcoal eye masks, etc. Since the start of summer, ice pack eye masks have become highly popular. On one Taobao store, the monthly sales volume of a single item exceeded ten thousand pieces.

Many sellers also make significant efforts with pillows, launching lavender pillows, buckwheat pillows, etc., which promote relaxation. Even foods like millet and jujubes are labeled as promoting sleep.

The "insomnia map" shows that according to Taobao's transaction data over the past 5 months, from the proportion of people buying earplugs and eye masks in relation to the total population of the city, all the first-tier cities of Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, and Shenzhen made the top five list. Hangzhou, ranked third, is the only non-first-tier city among them.

Top 10 cities for purchasing earplugs: Shanghai, Beijing, Hangzhou, Guangzhou, Shenzhen, Nanjing, Suzhou, Wuhan, Chongqing, Ningbo.

Top 10 cities for purchasing eye masks: Shanghai, Beijing, Hangzhou, Shenzhen, Guangzhou, Chongqing, Nanjing, Suzhou, Wuhan, Chengdu.

Shanghai can be considered the city with the most severe insomnia. Jiangsu, Guangdong, and Zhejiang are the three provinces with the worst sleep quality.

"The cycle of 'can't sleep at night, can't wake up in the morning' has become a vicious circle. The cities where people have the hardest time sleeping at night are also the ones where people find it hardest to wake up in the morning. In the past 5 months, the top five cities for the total population purchasing alarm clocks were also Shanghai, Beijing, Hangzhou, Shenzhen, and Guangzhou."

"I have to wear earplugs every night to fall asleep, but then I can't hear the alarm on my phone in the morning. After setting my phone alarm to vibrate, I'm afraid it won't be powerful enough to wake me up, so I bought a vibrating alarm clock." Xiao Mao's "sleep-inducing" and "wake-up" equipment has become essential for many insomniacs.

The most helpless reason for buying earplugs:

Husband snores

So, who are the people suffering from insomnia? Online shop owners have the most insight. "The number of people needing earplugs to sleep increases every year, and there are more customers consulting. The age range is also getting wider. Basically, students and urban white-collar workers account for 90% of total sales," said an owner of an online shop selling earplugs.

Indeed, the shop's best-selling product — earplugs priced at 19 yuan each — sold 19,000 units in just one month.

Why can't everyone sleep? There are actually many reasons. It is understood that students buying eye masks and earplugs might live in school dormitories. Mobile devices are now widely used among students. After bedtime, some classmates may still be playing games, so to avoid being disturbed, they simply "shut themselves off."

White-collar workers, on the other hand, have relatively high work stress during the day, leading to sleep problems at night. There are even amusing buyers whose reason for purchasing earplugs is "my husband snores too loudly."

Taobao data shows that the majority of buyers for earplugs and eye masks are from the "post-90s" and "post-80s" generations. Taking the eye mask sales over the past 5 months as an example, the "post-90s" group is the largest demand demographic, with 36% of eye masks purchased by people aged 18-24. Additionally, people aged 25-29 account for more than 30% of the purchases.

From the purchasing ratio of various products, men are more likely to suffer from insomnia than women, with about 20% more male insomniacs than female.
