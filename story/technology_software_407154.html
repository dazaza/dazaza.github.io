Five Precautions for Diabetes in Spring

by lkts1ji2kod on 2011-04-07 11:29:39

"Breaking Spring: All grasses return to spring, and all diseases occur." Spring is the season when diseases are prone to happen. For diabetic patients, spring is a dangerous "checkpoint". If not properly adjusted, it can easily lead to elevated blood sugar levels and worsening of the condition; conversely, if properly cared for with great attention to various aspects of health care, such as Yihong capsules, the condition can stabilize, allowing one to safely get through the "troublesome spring."

The unpredictable weather of early spring is a test for diabetic patients. Affected by the fluctuating temperatures of early spring, diabetic patients are easily susceptible to respiratory diseases and emotional fluctuations, which in turn cause blood sugar levels to rise and fall unpredictably, worsening the condition and even easily inducing various diabetic complications. Therefore, diabetic patients should note the following points when "embracing" spring:

Prevent Colds

Diabetic patients have this experience: after catching a cold, blood sugar changes significantly. Blood sugar levels that were previously well-controlled suddenly rise, followed by uncomfortable symptoms. This is because the body's disease resistance in diabetic patients is very poor. After catching a cold, the body cannot effectively drive out pathogens, making it harder to cure than in normal people, with the course of illness extending to several weeks, or even one to two months. Once blood sugar rises due to a cold, it is difficult to return to normal, leading to various complications such as neuropathy and retinopathy, resulting in irreversible outcomes. Therefore, diabetic patients must not take colds lightly and must strictly prevent them. It is important to keep the living space ventilated, avoid crowded public places during cold outbreaks, wear masks when going out, and adjust clothing according to temperature changes.

Stay Warm

There is a saying: "Spring covering, autumn freezing," which is especially important for diabetic patients. Since the cold weather has not truly passed yet, diabetic patients, due to peripheral nerve damage, sometimes cannot accurately sense temperature changes. Therefore, do not rush to take off warm clothes, paying special attention to keeping the limbs warm. Dietary Adjustment

In traditional Chinese medicine, it is believed that in spring, the diet should "reduce sourness and increase sweetness to nourish the nature." In other words, eating more acidic foods can make liver fire excessive, harming the spleen and stomach. Therefore, it is advisable to consume more foods rich in high-quality proteins, vitamins, and trace elements, such as lean meat, poultry, eggs, fresh vegetables, and fruits, to nourish yang energy, collect yin, protect the liver, and maintain the spleen, preventing various vitamin deficiencies. Thus, diabetic patients should appropriately increase the intake of foods like seaweed, bitter melon, yam, carrots, onions, garlic, and black fungus in the spring.

Regulate Emotions

In traditional Chinese medicine, it is believed that if emotions are too intense or suppressed, it can manifest as irritability and anger upon slight stimulation. Anger harms the liver, disrupting its function, leading to stagnation transforming into heat, and excessive heat burning yin fluids, causing blood sugar to rise. Modern medicine also proves that when emotions fluctuate, the adrenal glands secrete large amounts of adrenaline, stimulating the release of liver glycogen while inhibiting insulin secretion, thus raising blood sugar levels. Therefore, patients themselves need to regulate their emotions. Family members should give patients more care and cherish in daily life, allowing them to fully enjoy a beautiful and warm family life. However, it is important to pay attention to the way of care, avoiding making patients feel inadequate and increasing mental pressure, with the goal of stabilizing the condition.

Spring Activities

In the sunny, pleasant, and melodious spring, one can go for walks in the countryside, enjoy greenery, play by the stream, or climb mountains for a distant view to cultivate one's moral character, aligning one's spirit with the nature of spring to vent liver qi. Practical evidence shows that hiking or short trips not only improve mood but also lower blood sugar levels, improving the condition.

Attention to Daily Routine

In early spring, winter's chill has not yet dissipated. Going to bed earlier at night and getting up slightly later in the morning, waiting for sunrise before going out for activities, avoids the morning's cold stimulation. At the same time, appropriate physical exercise should be increased to promote blood circulation, making qi and blood flow smoothly, achieving the goals of enhancing immunity, reducing diabetic complications, and stably controlling blood sugar levels.
