The stay or leave of midfield pivot Pirlo is the most concerned

by fevqqkvv on 2012-03-08 09:09:42

After winning the championship as expected, Milan has completely shifted its strategic focus to contract renewals and recruitment. In an interview with Italian Rai TV, head coach Allegri commented on the team's renewal work and the possibility of signing Santos genius Ganso. At the same time, Allegri also talked about the possibility of Ganso joining, giving the Brazilian international a high evaluation but stating that he is no longer the top target for Milan's summer recruitment. "Ganso? Without a doubt, he has quite excellent ball skills, and his performance in Brazil's domestic league and the Copa Libertadores this season has been very good as well. He will have a bright future. However, for him to truly stand on his own feet in Serie A, he still needs to go through the experience of European football, and he has just suffered an injury, so Ganso is currently just one of the members on our list of candidates." Among the many veterans waiting for contract renewals, the midfield core Pirlo's departure or stay is the most watched. Regarding this, Allegri said: "Andrea (Pirlo's name) had less than ideal luck this season due to being troubled by injuries, and he was away from the field for a long time. Nevertheless, he is still an extraordinary player, which I don't need to elaborate on because he has already proven his strength on the field. However, it is difficult for me to predict whether he can complete the renewal at this moment, because the renewal negotiations between the club and the player have not officially started yet. Whether the renewal can be smoothly completed depends on the willingness and needs of both the team and the player, and I cannot give a definite answer." According to reports from the mouthpiece media 'Milan News Network', Milan's renewal negotiations will begin tomorrow local time in Italy. Among the 9 players whose contracts are about to expire, captain Ambrosini, starting center-back Nesta, and veteran forward Inzaghi are considered to have a higher probability of staying, while Pirlo is still listed as having more difficult renewal prospects. With Ganso suffering a thigh muscle strain in the Copa Libertadores, the Santos genius has gradually faded from the vision of the Rossoneri, while the emergence of other targets such as Lamela and Pastore clearly shows signs of the Rossoneri shifting their strategic focus, and Allegri's statement further confirms this point.
