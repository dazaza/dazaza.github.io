How to take care of a decent pair of MBT sandals?

by manpaoxie on 2010-06-01 14:02:25

How to take care of a better pair of MBT sandals? First, you should remove the stains on the surface of the MBT sandals. Stains on leather uppers cannot be wiped with a wet cloth, and they must not be soaked in water for washing, otherwise it will destroy the color light paste on the shoe surface, and also make the leather shoes harden and deform. For light-colored leather MBT sandals, you can use a soft cloth dipped in the same color shoe cream to gently wipe. The shoe cream should not be applied too thickly, because it has a certain volatility. If applied too much, it will cause the shoe surface to dry and crack after a long time. In addition, it is best to apply a little lard before storage, which can keep the leather soft and glossy, and not easy to deform. For smooth leather MBT sandals, you can use a dry cloth to wipe off the stains and then apply some shoe cream.

For suede MBT sandals, you can use fine sandpaper to gently rub the stained area to keep the velvet surface standing. When dirty, just wipe with a damp cloth. Note that you should use a dry cloth to wipe off the moisture, then dry the moisture in the shade, but do not expose to direct sunlight. Do not soak them in water for washing, which is bad for the leather. The soles can be brushed with a toothbrush dipped in water. If you like to apply shoe cream, you can polish the shoes with a cloth after the shoes are dry each time after using the shoe cream. This way, the shoes will be very shiny and won't dirty the socks.

Before storage, MBT sandals must be naturally air-dried. If there are already mold spots, use a soft cloth or soft brush to remove the mold spots and promptly replenish the shoe cream. Otherwise, after the shoes are stored, the mold phenomenon will become more serious. Mold spores diffusing into the air will pollute the indoor environment and endanger human health. To prevent deformation of MBT sandals, it is best to place shoe trees inside and store them in shoe boxes, which can prevent the shoes from deforming.

If the MBT sandals have natural leather straps, they cannot be washed with water or dried by fire. Of course, you should also be careful to avoid rainwater. If they get wet, they may come apart at the seams or deform. They should not be in prolonged contact with chemicals or grease. Since MBT sandals generally have a loop structure, they are not suitable for wearing under heavy load conditions, as the shoelaces may break. When not in use, clean them and support them with shoe trees, put them into shoe boxes to avoid deformation.

Secondly, do a good job of anti-mold and anti-moisture treatment inside the shoes. Before storage, the black women's MBT sandals must be placed in a cool and ventilated place and naturally air-dried for several days.
