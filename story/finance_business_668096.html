II. True or False

1. The individual water scale survey is applicable for bulk goods with low value or difficult to weigh. YES

2. Among the ship registration standards, maximum dimensions, and hypothetical dimensions, the standard most closely related to the water scale survey work is the hypothetical standard. YES

3. The calculated length refers to the length of the summer full-load waterline. NO

4. The fore perpendicular of a ship refers to the intersection point of the forward edge of the bow post and the summer full-load waterline. NO

5. The baseline of a ship refers to the straight line parallel to the imaginary waterline along the upper edge of the keel, also known as the keel line. NO

6. The molded draft refers to the vertical distance from the base line to the summer full-load waterline at the midship section; the molded draft is also called the design draft. YES

7. Both the total length and maximum length of a ship belong to the maximum dimensions of the ship. YES

8. When the water scale uses the metric system, Arabic numerals are used for marking, each numeral is 10 cm high, the width of the font lines is 2 cm, and the spacing between two digits is 10 cm, measured from the bottom edge of the digit. YES

9. The load line mark includes the deck line, load line circle, and various load lines. YES

10. The minimum freeboard of a ship refers to the freeboard corresponding to the molded draft, also called the safety freeboard or summer freeboard. YES

11. The lower edge of the horizontal line in the middle of the load line circle passes through the center of the circle, and this horizontal line's lower edge is the maximum allowable loading water scale limit line for summer. NO

12. The load line refers to the maximum draft line when the ship is fully loaded. The width of the load line is 2 centimeters. NO

13. The gross tonnage and net tonnage values in the ship's tonnage certificate should be integers, disregarding decimal points below, and only numbers are filled without units. YES

14. Volume tonnage is also called measurement ton, which uses volume as the basis for calculating freight charges, with one volume ton being 2.83 cubic meters or 100 cubic feet. NO

15. The full load displacement refers to the maximum weight the ship can carry according to the load line mark regulations. NO

16. Net deadweight refers to the maximum weight of cargo that a ship can carry. YES

17. The longitudinal moment is the moment required to produce a 1 cm trim in the ship. NO

18. As the draft increases, the center of flotation moves toward the stern of the ship. YES

19. China's "Procedure for Weight Appraisal of Import and Export Commodities by Water Scale Measurement" defines the trim as the difference between the stern draft and the bow draft. YES

20. When measuring ballast water together with the ship side, if the ship side claims that a water tank is empty, it can be recorded directly as zero depth without measurement. NO

21. When checking the tank capacity table to calculate the ballast water capacity, corrections may not be necessary for very small total capacity tanks such as bow peak tanks and stern peak tanks. YES

22. The ship's constant changes only after repair. As long as the ship is not repaired, the ship's constant remains an unchanging number. NO

23. A long ton is the same as an imperial ton, and a short ton is the same as a metric ton. NO

24. When reading the water scale, if one side of the ship's midsection cannot be seen from the deck, the water scale number estimated based on the heel degree shown on the inclinometer and the midship width should be taken as the standard. NO

25. When the water scale mark adopts the imperial system, each digit is 6 inches high, and the spacing between two digits is also 6 inches, measured from the bottom edge of the digit. Each digit's line width is 2 cm.
