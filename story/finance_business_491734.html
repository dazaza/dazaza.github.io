Gloomy sky

by tdvhaihaii on 2011-09-19 20:16:42

In the deep autumn night, yearning gradually opened its eyes. Is the tranquility behind the sunlight the beautiful scenery of the sun setting in the west? Old memories swirl like clouds and smoke in my heart. Drinking a cup of rich and fragrant wine, reading about the ten-thousand-li Great Wall's mighty wind, watching the sky's clouds roll and unfurl, feeling carefree and refined, thoughts entwined.

The morning lotus faces the afterglow, gracefully extending its tender hand, caressing the shadow of the wind, as if taking a sip of fruit wine, intoxicated deeply. The morning mist scatters in clarity, reflecting the beauty of the sky, like a clear spring gushing forth, trickling continuously without tiring.

Yearning is like a spring, slowly flowing into the brows, slightly frowning, lightly flicking, in just an instant, it can mark the regret of autumn, with waves of once-upon-a-time dreams flooding through thoughts. Who once made lasting promises to whom? Accompanying each other under the moonlight, holding hands through fields of mulberry trees? That faint whisper, is it still by the ear or far away at the edge of the horizon?

Who hums soul-stirring songs in the curtain of night? Lamenting that since ancient times beauties often part ways. Narrating a light visit of longing, pulling a branch tip's freshness, who resides alone in dreams?

Poets pick up their pens to write melancholy, using words and poetry to express heartfelt emotions. How many sweet and bitter experiences are there in life, how many places are dreamt of as distant lands? Perhaps, yearning isn't constant thinking, but merely having had an ideal in one's heart. The boat of yearning runs aground in everyone's heart, leaving a faint glow. Whose heart is filled with lush tulips?

Quietly gazing, on that day, those trees, those falling leaves, originally, I sat alone in the solitude of an entire night.

An oppressive sky, regarding New Year's couplets, erodes my solitary appearance. Sweat gathers into heat on my back, dissipating the burns within me. Fingers lightly touch the quality of the air, waving, slapping, yet unable to make any sound.

The spreading darkness quickly wraps thoughts into the heart, and the tranquil night arrives.

This time, it is most suitable for one person, a cup of tea, a favorite piece of light music, a segment of unforgettable thoughts flowing long and far in the heart. Opening the window, reclining on the windowsill, smelling the faint fragrance of flowers outside the window, not knowing what kind of flower emits such a bewitching aroma, is it wisteria? Or palm tree? Not sure, really very fragrant. This scent permeates the heart, swirling around every cell, refreshing the spirit, calming the mind, possibly allowing one to slowly release a smile.

Slowly rising, stepping out of this place that belongs solely to oneself, a cup of tea, a chair, a bed, quietly living in a lonely space, like a thousand-year-old glacier, never considering melting and merging into rivers, tightly closing the door, leaning against the door's indulgence, the familiar before one's eyes no longer worth asking about. Given a second, one can learn to shake off youth, when blending into the crowd, one must exude vitality.

Elderly people exercising in the morning wear brightly colored clothes, happiness filling their small groups. The bustling city, at this moment, seems not yet wanting to wake up. A streak of bright sunlight has already slid into the eye chamber. Opening the window, feeling the fragrance of fresh grass, like a girl whose face has been washed, emitting a faint daughterly fragrance, so enchanting, so fragrant.

Like the brilliance of summer, sunlight outside the house constantly weaves colorful patterns. Leaves rustle in the wind, somewhat lazy, somewhat reckless. This autumn scenery is at its peak, sultriness has completely dissipated, only leaving behind a hazy confusion, which is both a look back at summer and a vision of winter.

Yearning rises slowly in the heart like cooking smoke, the most genuine sentiment in the world of bridges and streams. As the sun sets, the halo overflowing in the afterglow moves back and forth with the flow of air, one circle, two circles, like blooming calla lilies, radiating beautiful halos.

Underfoot, a hint of green ripples in the heart like ripples. It is the body of the grass, rubbing against my cool heels, a bit warm, a bit smooth. In this late autumn evening, the evening breeze gently brushes against clear clothing, highlighting a white back. The skin filled with sweat beads slowly unfolds in this faint fragrance, chasing after coolness, merging into a transparent world.

You suddenly puff out your chest, that glimmer of brightness in the darkness, shining with the sharpness of a glance back. Is it because you've already grown tired, so you have no lingering attachment? You walk out of my world, like the deeper sorrow of silence, burning the long yearning in my cells. Don't awaken my resentful silence anymore. Under the sunset, the disappointment of swallows flying down has already secretly turned into sorrow.
