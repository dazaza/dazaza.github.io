The B2B market size will reach 9.3 billion yuan this year, and cross-border trade is becoming a new trend.

by officefurniture on 2007-07-11 15:51:50

The "White Paper on China's B2B Market" recently released by Analysys International, a new economic consulting agency, shows that in 2006, the revenue from B2B market websites in China only accounted for 25%, while the rest was taken up by offline businesses such as exhibitions and industry information publications; the share of foreign trade business in the market scale reached 70%, significantly higher than domestic trade. Analysts believe that based on these two characteristics, foreign trade business integrated online and offline will become the mainstream of e-commerce development in the future.

B2B is the most important type of e-commerce, which refers to procurement transactions from business to business, distinct from transactions from business to consumers (B2C). Data shows that in 2006, the scale of China's B2B market reached 7.3 billion yuan, and it is expected that China's B2B market scale will reach 9.3 billion yuan in 2007.

In the B2B industrial chain, there exists a third party between buyers and sellers that provides information and builds a trading platform, which is the B2B service provider. A certain proportion of remuneration must be paid to the intermediary for each transaction. Therefore, the market scale of B2B service providers is also quite considerable. Data shows that more than half of the shares are monopolized by four industry giants. Among them, the Canton Fair occupies the largest market transaction share, reaching 18%; while Alibaba, China's largest B2B website, has a share of 9%, Global Sources, a multinational e-commerce giant, has a share of 9%, and慧聪网, another industry vertical e-commerce website, has a share of 3%.

Taking Global Sources as an example, two-thirds of the company's business is offline services such as exhibitions and industry publications. By the first quarter, there were 600,000 active buyers globally. Chief Operating Officer Pei Kewei believes that in terms of creating value for customers, various platforms such as B2B websites, exhibitions, and industry information publications have their own advantages and limitations. Integrating multi-platform services online and offline can not only meet the differentiated needs of different customers for B2B services but also help achieve a balance in the breadth of customer service and the depth of information communication.

The white paper analysis points out that due to the improvement of China's economic development and openness bringing about a good macro environment, in the future, B2B service providers will be able to create more value for customers in foreign trade.

Pei Kewei stated that China has now become the world's manufacturing center, and more and more Chinese enterprises are starting to enter the international market. However, the low prices they previously relied on are now being challenged by factors such as environmental protection, safety, labor costs, and RMB appreciation. When all these factors are considered in product prices, Chinese enterprises lose their export advantage. Therefore, for Chinese enterprises to sell their products to the international market, they must understand international buyers and need to obtain more professional information. This is also the opportunity and focus for the future development of B2B service providers.
