The 7 Best Types of Food to Support Pregnant Moms

by ciaifuke on 2012-02-27 17:11:53

1. Best Anti-Nausea Foods

Morning sickness is one of the most uncomfortable and common reactions experienced by pregnant women, causing them significant distress. Choosing foods that suit a pregnant woman's taste can have excellent anti-nausea effects. Nutritionists believe that lemons and potatoes contain various vitamins that are particularly suitable for pregnant women.

2. Best Vegetable for Fetal Protection

Spinach is rich in folic acid, with 350 micrograms of folic acid per 100 grams, ranking first among vegetables. The primary function of folic acid is to protect the fetus from neural tube defects such as spina bifida, hydrocephalus, and anencephaly. Therefore, experts recommend eating more spinach or taking folic acid supplements during the first two months of pregnancy. Additionally, the abundant B vitamins in spinach can prevent common pregnancy complications such as pelvic infections, depression, and insomnia in pregnant women.

3. Best Beverage

Green tea is a "rich mine" of trace elements, with zinc being one of the key elements that plays a prominent role in fetal development. According to measurements, pregnant women who regularly drink green tea obtain 14 milligrams more zinc daily compared to those who do not. Moreover, green tea is also rich in iron, making it effective in preventing anemia.

4. Best Food to Prevent Premature Birth

Studies by Danish experts show that regularly eating fish has a preventive effect against premature birth. Pregnant women in the Faroe Islands of Denmark have an average pregnancy duration that is over five days longer than in other regions, due to the higher proportion of fish in their diet. Thus, experts speculate that a specific fatty acid in fish meat plays a positive role. With the extension of pregnancy, the average birth weight of babies is also 107 grams higher than in other areas, laying a good foundation for future development. Therefore, pregnant women should eat more fish during pregnancy.

5. Best Snacks

Eating some snacks outside of main meals can broaden the supply channels of nutrients for pregnant women. Experts suggest eating seeds like sunflower seeds, watermelon seeds, and pumpkin seeds. Sunflower seeds are rich in vitamin E, watermelon seeds contain more linoleic acid, which promotes fetal brain development. Pumpkin seeds stand out because they are nutritionally comprehensive, containing protein, fat, carbohydrates, calcium, iron, phosphorus, carotene, vitamin B1, B2, niacin, etc., and the nutrient ratio is balanced, which facilitates human absorption and utilization.

6. Best Sour Foods

Pregnant women often have a preference for sour foods, and eating sour foods indeed has benefits. After becoming pregnant, the placenta secretes a hormone called chorionic gonadotropin, which inhibits gastric acid secretion, leading to reduced digestive enzyme activity, weakened appetite, and decreased digestive function in pregnant women. Therefore, eating sour foods undoubtedly compensates for this reaction.

At the same time, fetal development, especially bone development, requires a large amount of calcium, but the deposition of calcium salts into bones cannot be achieved without the assistance of sour foods. In addition, sour foods promote the absorption of iron in the intestines, benefiting both the mother and the fetus.

However, pregnant women need to be careful when choosing sour foods. Hawthorn is relatively nutritious, but it can accelerate uterine contractions, posing a risk of miscarriage, so pregnant women are advised to avoid it. On the other hand, tomatoes, waxberries, cherries, grapes, oranges, and apples are excellent choices for supplementing sourness, and pregnant women should consume them.

7. Best Delivery Food

During delivery, mothers need sufficient energy, which comes from food. Among all foods, chocolate is considered the best, referred to as the "best delivery food" by American obstetricians. Chocolate is rich in nutrients and calories, such as 100 grams of chocolate containing 50 grams of sugar, which can be quickly absorbed by the body and rapidly converted into energy. The digestion and absorption rate of chocolate is five times faster than that of eggs, making it a "timely relief" for mothers in urgent need of energy. Therefore, eating a few pieces of chocolate before delivery can help shorten labor and facilitate a smooth delivery.
