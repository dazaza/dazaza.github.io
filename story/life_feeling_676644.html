At seventeen, it's a rainy youth.

by zhouzhou56 on 2012-02-26 12:14:34

At the age of seventeen, it was a rainy season of youth.

Encounter, in the Rainy Season

They say that if you have destiny, people will come from afar to meet; if not, they'll pass by without knowing each other. An encounter in the sea of people might be the opening act of a drama - either a comedy or a tragedy.

I woke up from a dream, and the spacious classroom was already empty. The scorching afternoon sun pierced through the blue window paper, and the floating dust particles in the air were clearly visible. Rubbing my sleepy eyes, I heard the door creak open, and a streak of sunlight came in. In the glaring light, she suddenly entered my line of sight.

I shook my sluggish brain and picked up the white paper in my hand, chewing on the pen in frustration. I couldn't help but grumble about the teacher - we were all new students, yet he specifically asked me to be the class leader and set the class rules. Thinking of this, I sighed deeply. "What are you doing?" I looked up and met a pair of bright eyes filled with sincerity. "Oh, I'm setting the class rules." I handed over the draft casually.

I don't know what it means to feel like old friends at first sight, but in that sunny afternoon, in that lazy classroom, we, strangers, faced each other and chatted about our loneliness at our desks. It was like two lonely children relying on each other, or like lonely travelers meeting a companion on the same journey.

Seemingly intentional, seemingly unintentional, she sat in front of me. Every time I looked up, I could see her focused back. Perhaps feeling my gaze, she turned around and gave me a radiant smile. Her bright big eyes were pure, as if a fairy tale world could be seen inside them. Her slightly upturned corners of her mouth carried a hint of mischief and stubbornness...

The cloudy sky gradually started raining, and the September weather had a slight chill. Tightening my coat, I retracted my wandering thoughts. Suddenly, I saw her in the front row wearing only a thin shirt, shivering from the cold but still stubbornly holding herself upright. Shaking my head, I took off my coat and handed it to her. "Thank you," she said. I shrugged indifferently, but out of the corner of my eye, I noticed a strange gleam flash across her beautiful eyes.

It seemed like self-talk, or an outburst of sentimentality. Gazing at the misty mountains in the rain, I sighed softly, "The rainy season has arrived."

Remember, my name is Yi.

I don't believe in angels in this world, but I do believe there are girls who carry wings like angels.

Sniffing the faint fragrance at the tip of my nose, my mind wandered for a moment. Then, there was a click, and the scene froze. In the photo, her face was red, and she was smiling beautifully. I had my eyes half-closed, my nose close to the tip of her hair. Since it was a large sticker photo, only the part above the chest was visible, creating the illusion that I was hugging her from behind.

When we came out of the photo studio, her cheeks were still flushed pink against her fair skin, making her look extraordinarily charming, almost breathtakingly beautiful. I quickly averted my eyes, afraid that if I continued looking, I would be entranced. As we walked, she suddenly turned around and looked at me. Caught off guard, I nearly bumped into her. "Do you know?" She looked at me seriously, and I smiled faintly to show I was listening. "Today is the happiest day of these years, thank you." I smiled again, "I am also very happy with you, so let's be happy every day from now on." She glanced at me and said softly, "Let's always be this happy and be happy forever." As for why she used "we" instead of "me", I didn't bother to think too much about it.

Wigs can always be exposed by the wind, just like how someone without real talent gets revealed. Losing the identity of being a class committee member, Yi and I lost another reason to stick together. Gradually, I felt her treatment towards me wasn't ordinary friendship. Regarding her "concern", I could only pretend to be dumb and ignore the whispers around me calling me dull and rigid. I remained indifferent, pretending not to hear. It's not that I didn't understand, but I chose not to.

On a moonlit night after lights-out, at the entrance of the building, a girl hesitantly yet resolutely handed a boy a letter. The light was dim, making it hard to see their expressions.

"Remember, my name is Yi. This is my pen name. Remember, a girl named Yi is formally declaring war on you. Get ready..."

Even if I cannot touch the sky, I won't let go of my hands.

Some words are better left unsaid, because once spoken, it means losing.

Without turning back, I knew there was a pair of eyes that used to be bright but are now dim, watching me. Time passes, and we are all growing up. The pain of growing up is clearly etched into those eyes. That pure her, that beautiful her due to her cuteness, was probably taken away by the flow of time.

With a detached smile, I passed by her naturally and gracefully, ignoring her sad gaze and her dreamlike sighs. Maybe they're right; I am a cold-hearted person who keeps others at bay with a passionate smile.

The fallen flowers covered the paths of the campus, and the autumn wind lingered among the swirling dead leaves, marking another season of falling. I always believed that time takes away everything it can, changes everything it cannot take. But there are always some people who are so stubborn, so determined, unwilling to let certain things slip through their fingers with the passage of time. Clearly, classmate Yi falls into this category.

Pulling out a perfectly folded piece of paper from my pocket:

"I know, it's deliberate, deliberately cold, trying to make me give up. But don't forget, I'm that stubborn Yi, the one who won't let go even if I can't touch the sky..."

Stars and Meteors

She left, as sudden as migratory geese returning south. She left me a pile of unwritten letters and a bottle of lucky stars.

She stood in front of me for the last time, saying she wanted to be my sister, a sister spoiled by me. I remained silent. Actually, she was giving us an excuse, an excuse for her to stay by my side. She simply thought I was cold because I wanted to refuse her, but in fact, I felt guilty and didn't dare to face her, nor that past.

There's no denying that being with her was pleasant. She was a very pure girl, someone whose inner thoughts could be easily read. She was strong, stubborn, yet delicate like any other girl. Many times, I felt sorry for her, letting such a strong and beautiful girl be disheartened and tearful. Everything started with my ambiguity.

She's gone, and it's probably for the best. Going to a strange environment, meeting new people, new things, starting anew. Perhaps, this is good for both of us.

I heard there would be a meteor shower of Leo, so I skipped evening study and went to the playground. There were already many people waiting, and the dark playground was dotted with the soft glow of phone screens.

I lay down in a corner full of weeds, resting my arms under my head and staring blankly at the sky, which seemed pretty clear.

In the hazy night, music floated around my ears. The melody was intermittent, sometimes fading, making me drowsy. Occasionally, a breeze passed, carrying a bit of coolness from early autumn, refreshing my mind. Listening carefully again, it was Zhang Jie's voice.

...

You are a meteor,

I am a star,

You are destined to leave,

I am destined to stay,

...

The first time I heard this song was when she recommended it, saying it was our story.

Yes, I am a meteor. I didn't wait for the Leo meteor shower. Friends said it did appear, and he waited.

That year, we were seventeen.

"On my seventeenth birthday, I met you, and I firmly believed it was a predestined fate."

While tidying up miscellaneous items, I found a green-covered diary from a pile of old textbooks. After brushing off the dust, two envelopes fell out, signed by her. Inside were small pieces of paper, all evidences of our mischievous actions during class. Opening the diary, from beginning to end, it was filled with longing for a girl, but it wasn't Yi, it was Xue.

I laughed bitterly, finding it ironic that I used a diary given by a girl who secretly admired me to write about my admiration and longing for another girl. Unexpectedly, I saw a sentence in the blank pages of the diary - "On my seventeenth birthday, I met you, and since then, I foolishly believed it was a predestined fate, a fated connection, destined but unattainable."

Classmates said she came back six months ago, but that day I asked for leave to go home. She sat at my seat all morning and flipped through my diary...

That year, you were seventeen, still a child. Coming from a family with few members, living under someone else's roof, lacking warmth and care. On my lonely journey, I came, coincidentally. Everything was like the breaking of a fairy tale - knowing there wouldn't be a result, yet refusing to forget.

That year, I was seventeen, during a season of frequent rain. Innocent youth, budding spring heart, overflowing emotions. Starting to understand, starting to worry, starting to become composed. I left your world. Everything was like a popular saying - if you don't love, then stop the ambiguity.

Using fragmented words to write about the scattered past, using poorly crafted sentences to record the unpredictable rainy season, using clumsy phrases to commemorate the fiery youth.
