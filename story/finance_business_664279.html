Introduction to Android Electronic Market (Android Market)

by dream2012 on 2012-02-13 12:48:46

Android Market is Google's online application store developed for Android devices. The Android Market is pre-installed on most Android phones, and Android users can use it to browse and download Android applications released by third-party developers. Currently, the Android Market has over 100,000 applications, making it the second-largest mobile application online store in the world after Apple's App Store.

**History**

The Android Market was announced by Google on August 28, 2008, and officially opened to Android users on October 22, 2008. Paid app purchases on the Android Market were first introduced to U.S. users in mid-February 2009, with UK users being able to purchase paid apps through the Market starting March 13, 2009.

On March 17, 2009, according to T-Mobile CTO Cole Brodman, there were only 2,300 apps available for users to download and use on the Android Market. By December 2009, the number of apps available on the Market had exceeded 20,000.

By August 2009, the total number of apps on the Android Market surpassed 100,000, with cumulative downloads exceeding one billion. Starting from May 2020, the Android Market saw more than 10,000 new apps added each week.

According to a Distimo report in July 2010, the proportion of free apps on the Android Market was the highest among all global mobile app stores, with 57% of the apps on the Market being freely downloadable. In contrast, only 28% of the apps on Apple's App Store are free, which is less than half of the Android Market's proportion. Other major mobile app stores, including Nokia's Ovistore, BlackBerry's App World, and Microsoft's Windows Marketplace, also lag behind the Android Market in terms of the proportion of free apps.

**Paid Applications**

Not all countries and regions support the purchase of paid apps on the Android Market. After Google launched its paid app purchasing service for U.S. and UK users at the beginning of 2008, it wasn't until April 2009 that support for Austria, France, Germany, the Netherlands, and Spain was added. Currently, the Android Market supports users from 14 countries to purchase paid apps, with South Korean users gaining support for paid apps in July this year. The list of countries currently supporting paid apps on the Android Market includes:

- Australia

- Austria

- Canada

- France

- Germany

- Italy

- Japan

- Netherlands

- New Zealand

- Spain

- Switzerland

- United Kingdom

- United States

- South Korea

For developers of paid Android apps, they receive 70% of the app's listed price as revenue for each sale, while the remaining 30% covers operational costs associated with mobile carriers and payment processing. Currently, both the purchase of paid apps from the Android Market and the collection of sales proceeds by developers must be completed through Google Checkout.

However, recent reports indicate that Google has partnered with the renowned payment company PayPal to integrate PayPal as an alternative payment method within the Market. Additionally, U.S. telecom operator T-Mobile has started experimenting with allowing users to settle app purchases from the Market through their phone bills.
