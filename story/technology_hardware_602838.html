Weight loss, smoking, drinking... Modern young women have developed various "individualistic" living habits in order to keep up with fashion trends. However, these habits not only bring individualistic charm and personality but may also bring a certain amount of trouble.

"Diseases 'reduced' out"

Weight loss has been a hot topic in recent years. However, many people experience discomfort and health issues due to weight loss. According to relevant surveys, improper weight loss can lead to amenorrhea, induce gallstones, damage brain cells, osteoporosis, hair loss, neurotic anorexia, depression, nephroptosis, and affect libido.

"Diseases 'covered' out"

Wearing bras at appropriate times indeed benefits breast health and physical beauty. However, bras should not be worn for too long, especially during sleep when they must be removed. Otherwise, lymphatic circulation and blood circulation in the breasts will be obstructed, increasing the risk of breast diseases or even breast cancer.

"Diseases 'cleaned' out"

Healthy women have a sufficient amount of beneficial bacteria in their vaginas, which maintain a certain level of acidity and inhibit the growth of other bacteria. If vaginal washes are frequently used, it not only irritates the external genitalia and vaginal mucosa, absorbing moisture and causing dryness, heat, and itching in the area, but also makes it difficult for beneficial bacteria to survive. This often leads to the vagina losing its acidic environment, severely weakening its self-cleaning function, thus resulting in conditions such as vaginitis, cervical inflammation, or even pelvic inflammatory disease.

"Diseases 'wiped' out"

Many women use regular toilet paper to wipe tableware, fruits, or faces, which is unhygienic. Toilet paper is only meant for bathroom use, and many contain large amounts of bacteria due to incomplete disinfection. They should never be used as disinfectant wipes. The habit of using toilet paper to clean your face or tableware might cause acne on the face and cannot guarantee the cleanliness of the tableware.

"Diseases 'inhaled' out"

Some women smoke to keep up with fashion, while others believe in rumors that smoking helps with weight loss. This not only causes diseases but also increases the chances of developing lung cancer.

"Diseases 'drunk' out"

Since alcohol has the effect of burning body fat, some overweight women attempt to lose weight and reduce fat through drinking. However, drinking usually involves a lot of delicious food, especially meat, leading to more fat intake. Additionally, drinking promotes the secretion of large amounts of gastric juice, stimulating appetite, and ultimately overeating high-sugar and high-fat foods, making obese individuals even fatter.
