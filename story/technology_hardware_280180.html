Tonight, I suddenly wanted to learn how to write songs because I've been listening to a lot of good songs and then thought it would be great if I could write them too. Haha, I'm someone with a heavy sense of curiosity, always wanting to do everything. Later, I found this blog post, "How Common People Write Songs" by Zhong Zhigang, which is very practical:

I haven't done deep research on composing, but I have been bravely trying to write songs. So let's try to briefly discuss "how common people write songs."

"Composing," for academics and professionals, is very profound. For example, Beethoven had to consider counterpoint, harmony, orchestration, etc.

But "writing songs" isn't that profound. In fact, almost everyone can do it. Many catchy and widely popular songs were created by ordinary people who didn't have much professional training. Listen to folk classics; most of them were created and sung by hardworking and wise laborers who gradually improved them without much technical knowledge.

Let me share a few experiences, some practical tips. This is an outline, and I'll go into more detail later with examples.

01. When you see lyrics, first study the lyrics, read them, recite them, and find the rhythm in them.

02. After finding the rhythm, try humming and mainly determine the mood of the main melody. When a good melody appears, grab it.

03. The melody you catch might be short. Affirm it, hum repeatedly, remember it, and then try to develop it.

04. Or, in the absence of lyrics, first imagine the rhythm or a fragment of the melody in your mind, grab it, and record it with any writing tool at hand.

05. About recording, you can use your phone, computer, or scraps of paper. Record it and organize it later.

06. About organizing, I recommend using an MP3 editor, such as various audio editing software. Continuously record smooth melodies, then refer to a metronome to connect all musical phrases and melodies.

Once the melody and structure (such as A section, B section, C section) are basically determined, you can transcribe based on the recording.

07. About transcription, we don't need to care about complicated notations when starting out; just use simplified notation. It's very useful. Find some songbooks and start with familiar songs to practice reading scores. Practice by omitting the lyrics of the familiar song and singing "do re mi." Over time, you will be able to record the melodies you hum casually.

08. About writing music first and then adding lyrics (filling in lyrics), this requires comprehensive skills. You need to read more, study literature and things beyond music, such as observing various people and things, and listen to more classical, local, and classic music.

09. Accumulate fragments, i.e., inspirations.

10. Everyone has their own way of recording inspiration. "Develop good habits!" and stick to them.

11. Once you accumulate enough, you can easily come up with songs.

12. Regularly train your ability to improvise (lyrics and music). Ideally, do it every day, especially in crowded places, where you see something and sing about it.

13. Learn or become familiar with (at least have a better understanding) one or two instruments. It's incredibly beneficial for melody writing.

14. Trust your first impressions (melody, rhythm, lyrics) more often. Sing it out loud, quickly affirm it, and immediately record it (it's easy to forget).

15. Experience more: human "natural attributes." Gain rich natural rhythms and tones from various movements, physical labor, even walking under different conditions or doing housework.

16. Talk about mindset. Don't think you're superior because you can write songs. If someone admires you, don't blindly elevate yourself. Always learn to "empathize" (whether with people, events, or objects) - "If I were him (her, it)."

17. Talk about mindset. Songwriters need a relaxed state of mind, learn to take deep breaths. I am both an ordinary person and an artist, which is not strange. We just have different roles, but ultimately, I am a normal person with flesh and blood like everyone else. Be strict with oneself, stay humble; be tolerant towards others, maintain harmony. Classics are not accidental.

18. Maintain curiosity, impulsiveness, participation, and a full-hearted love for life because joys, sorrows, anger, and happiness are all musical materials, and music comes from life.

19. Finally, talk about responsibility. Maybe you are a defender of your lifestyle, maybe you are dissatisfied with society, maybe you are experiencing love, maybe you feel sympathy for many things, or maybe you just want to express your feelings at the moment, or perhaps you hope for a little entertainment for yourself and others... But if you hope to cater to certain people or markets with low tastes, hoping to become famous or be a troublemaker in this entertainment era, you are wrong.

Everyone knows that people need health, and there are many reasons for illness. Everyone needs a healthy society, and there are many ways to achieve health. Songwriters without a sense of social responsibility may be toxins, tumors, or maggots hindering social progress and health, and this has nothing to do with rulers' propaganda. The righteous path of humanity is rugged. Songwriters should stand tall and firm. Although we are just common people, society is made up of common people, and we ultimately drive the development of history. As time goes by, only the good songs and good music that remain are truly good. You will have no regrets even in death.

20. Songwriters need to cultivate their spirit, improve themselves, and learn to love.

Please note the author: Zhong Zhigang
