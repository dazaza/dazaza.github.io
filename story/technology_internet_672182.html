The national policy vigorously promotes the service trade industry to become the current economic development in our country.

by jintaimqr on 2012-02-22 16:09:47

With the advancement of economic globalization, the service trade industry is becoming increasingly important in the economic and social development of various countries. As a crucial component of the service trade industry, the logistics sector [http://www.jt077.cn/index.php?m=wlps] will fully benefit from industry development in 2012, with its business expected to flourish comprehensively.

Since 2011, under the circumstances of high CPI and continuous public attention on distribution costs, vigorously promoting the service trade industry has become an urgent need for China's current economic development. On November 28th, the formal release of the "Service Trade Development [12th Five-Year] Plan Outline" which was formulated over two years by 34 departments, marks that the government's planning and support for the service trade industry has reached a new level. The plan clearly proposes to focus on supporting 30 key areas, among which the logistics sector covers 7 major areas, basically encompassing the entire industry, demonstrating the determination and confidence of the country to fully tap into the development prospects of the logistics sector in 2012.

According to the plan, China strives to achieve an average annual growth of around 15% in the total retail sales of consumer goods, reaching 32 trillion yuan by 2015; while also significantly increasing service exports, expanding the opening-up of the service sector, and enhancing the proportion of service trade in foreign trade. The dual engines of [domestic demand] and [exports] working together will rapidly increase the rigid demand for the logistics industry, providing ample impetus for its development.

Currently, China's logistics industry still consists mostly of small companies with low market concentration. Haitong Securities points out that in the logistics industry chain, the lower the level, the more tangible assets are required; the higher the service level, the more intangible assets are needed. Generally speaking, companies with higher service levels have faster asset turnover, higher return on assets, and better growth potential.

By comparing with international experiences, advantageous enterprises will have the opportunity to gain a higher market share and return. In the late 20th century, the top 11 logistics companies in the U.S. collectively earned 66% of the entire industry’s profits. Historical experience shows that asset-light logistics companies enjoy higher asset premiums and relatively more prominent growth potential. Combined with the current era's opportunities of strong policy support for the development of logistics, large asset-light leading enterprises will have a first-mover advantage and broader development prospects during the industry consolidation process.

Recently, the China Securities Regulatory Commission (CSRC) Review Committee approved the initial public offering (IPO) application of a large logistics enterprise, China Travel Service Huamao International Logistics Co., Ltd. This company plans to issue 100 million shares, with a total post-issue capitalization of 400 million shares. Its main business includes providing cross-border one-stop integrated logistics services centered on international freight forwarding and supply chain trade services based on an integrated logistics service platform [http://www.jt077.cn/index.php?m=wlps], ranking among the top in the industry.

From the prospectus, it can be seen that cross-border logistics services centered on international freight forwarding are the core strengths of Huamao International. The company is one of the earliest first-class international freight forwarding enterprises in China to obtain national operating permits, and also one of the few large-scale Chinese local companies that simultaneously possess rich maritime and air resources. It provides comprehensive services throughout the international transportation process, owns a full-coverage information system, has established the most complete online operation platform domestically, and offers warehousing services such as pickup, import and export goods regulatory storage, general goods warehousing, and delivery services.

Through its strong integration capabilities and a globally extensive service network, supported by experienced professional teams and advanced comprehensive management information systems, Huamao International has become one of the largest cross-border one-stop integrated logistics providers in China currently. In the periods of January-June 2011, 2010, and 2009, the company's cross-border integrated logistics services amounted to 19.899957 billion yuan, 44.179167 billion yuan, and 26.976789 billion yuan respectively, accounting for 58.10%, 63.09%, and 60.45% of the main business revenue for each period.

In the future, with the rapid growth of China's economy, the cost advantage of the vast domestic market will make China gradually develop into a global logistics hub. The cross-border integrated logistics industry centered on international freight forwarding will face broader market space, and large cross-border integrated logistics enterprises will gain larger survival and development space.

Notably, Huamao International's market positioning and investment direction mainly focus on asset-light models, effectively combining heavy and light assets. By integrating industrial chain resources, it provides customers with complete, low-cost, efficient, and high-quality cross-border integrated logistics services, showcasing obvious competitive advantages in the industry.

As Huamao International's IPO application coincides with the period of strong policy support for the logistics industry, industry insiders are optimistic about its development prospects. After successful listing, the A-share logistics sector may welcome another excellent main-board listed company, adding brilliance to the industry's vigorous development in 2012.
