People who are often melancholy

Sometimes, I feel inexplicably sad.

I sit there blankly, head down, thinking about things without any clear direction. I don't want to talk, just be alone quietly. It's as if everything has nothing to do with me, and I want to be far away from the noise and secular world.

My heart drifts far away, but I am still sitting in the bustling city, surrounded by people wearing masks, shackled by their own fetters, going through life like walking corpses. I don't know where their souls are; perhaps even God doesn't know that they have already lost their souls. If I were God, I would certainly stay away from them, find a pure land, redeem my own soul, and then come back to see the regret in their eyes when they die.

But for them, that is an incredibly luxurious kind of release. They cannot let go, they cannot abandon their wives and children, nor can they abandon their aging parents. Therefore, they have no choice but to put on their masks and fetters again and set off blindly. Even occasional stops for rest have been skipped.

No one wants to be born this way, but life is unpredictable, how could it allow them to control it? They are all forced into it, forced! Some are forced to become slaves, some become landlords, some become angels, and some even become God - what remarkable achievements! But the disparities are so starkly placed before you, you must face them regardless, there is no time for contemplation.

Actually, not only do slaves have hardships, angels cannot be free from worries either. Slaves worry about food, drink, and freedom, while angels tread carefully according to God's will. Even God is busy rescuing humanity from suffering, though, there are also some extreme individuals who need purification.

If you need help, maybe someone among them will lend you a hand. When you thank them tearfully, you might realize that you once did them a favor. Thus, the principle of cause and effect holds true. Everyone has obstacles to overcome, and with mutual support, we can leap over them more easily.

We all know that God does not exist, yet many believers still make the sign of the cross. Each person's ideals remain hidden in their hearts, speaking them out loud won't make others believe, but who can stop them from being realized?

Melancholy seems to always accompany us, but it is not entirely useless. At least it allows us to reflect deeply on why we feel this way. Once we understand, we can let go, open our minds, and continue to strive forward.
