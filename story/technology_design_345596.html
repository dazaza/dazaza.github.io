We analyze problems differently depending on the object. For cyclical industries, there are generally three types: one is price sensitivity, mainly focusing on product prices, such as resource and agricultural industries; the second is related to economic cycles and fixed asset investments, such as cement and machinery equipment; the third is consumer goods industries, which are related to policies, consumption habits, population structure, and other long-term trends, such as automobile and clothing industries.

Colored metals fall into the first category, so we will break through this way. Most commodities have their own production and demand cycles, despite violent fluctuations in commodity prices, but supply and demand relationships are always the main thread connecting them. Under the influence of supply and demand relationships, many commodities' prices show seasonal characteristics, with off-seasons and peak seasons. For example, during harvest season, product prices often fall; while during lean periods, supply decreases and prices rise, which is the impact of seasonal factors on prices. Through time series analysis, we can identify the seasonal characteristics of commodity prices.

Based on the multiplicative model of the Census X12 method, the time series can be expressed in the form Y = TC (trend and cycle factors) × S (seasonal factors) × I (irregular factors). The chart below shows the performance of each factor after the decomposition of copper futures prices.

From the trend and cycle items in Figure 1, it can be seen that from mid-2005 to the end of 2008, copper prices experienced a complete cycle, and since 2009, they have entered a new cycle. From the seasonal factors of copper prices, there is a seasonal upward trend from January to April and from May to July.

From the above, we understand: "Since commodity prices have seasonal fluctuations!" Will this price fluctuation characteristic be transmitted to the stock market, causing the share prices of companies selling these commodities to also exhibit this seasonal fluctuation? For this purpose, we selected copper, aluminum, and gold as research commodities and chose Jiangxi Copper, Nanshan Aluminum, and Shandong Gold as corresponding stocks to analyze whether the seasonal characteristics of commodity prices can be reflected in the corresponding stocks. The chart below shows the seasonal characteristics of copper futures prices, showing that copper has a seasonal upward trend from January to April and from May to July. We found that this seasonal characteristic basically reflects the actual market conditions of copper in real life.

Seasonal characteristics of copper futures prices

Data source: China Nonferrous Metal Research Institute

Copper prices tend to rise seasonally mainly due to copper enterprises preparing ahead for increased downstream demand, getting ready for the arrival of copper consumption peaks in April and September; from September to December, entering winter, consumption shrinks, and enterprises mainly clear inventory, leading to a higher probability of falling copper demand. In previous years, after the Spring Festival, Shanghai copper usually sees a wave of rising prices. Since the Spring Festival is the longest legal holiday in China, customs clearance cannot be conducted for a relatively long period, and spot imports cannot undergo normal procedures, so spot traders usually choose to prepare goods in advance. Moreover, the beginning of the year generally has lower prices compared to the whole year, and post-festival prices usually rise, making it highly attractive to spot traders. The sharp increase in pre-festival inventory naturally causes copper prices to rise. Another festival factor is weather and cultural factors. The Spring Festival usually occurs in January or February, often accompanied by heavy snow weather, plus busy transportation during the Spring Festival travel rush, which is extremely unfavorable for the transportation of spot goods, leading to smooth spot supply causing copper prices to rise. From the data since 2005 (Table 1), copper prices have a higher probability of rising in February, April, and July. Related stock Jiangxi Copper, its price fully rises in February and April, and also has a higher probability of rising in July and November, at 80%.

For gold, through the data, we can see that gold has a higher probability of rising from August to September and from October to February next year. Since October is the Indian Diwali Festival, which is the local peak season for gold consumption, gold prices in August to September are prone to rise due to consumption expectations, and the end of the year and the beginning of the next year are also the peak seasons for gold jewelry consumption, usually leading to rising gold prices.

From the data since 2005, gold prices have a higher probability of rising in January to February, September, and November. Related stock Shandong Gold also has a higher probability of rising in February, September, and November.

If you can grasp their seasonal characteristics, you can better control the investment rhythm. The seasonal characteristics of metal prices revealed in the previous section, as well as the interconnectivity of related stocks, indicate that grasping this rhythm can bring stage-based market opportunities to investors. The seasonal features extracted through statistical analysis are merely general rules, while specific commodity price fluctuations are influenced by various factors such as production, inventory, weather, transportation, and consumer demand at the time. However, analyzing the seasonal characteristics of commodity prices can help investors pay attention to relevant influencing factors in advance and seize investment opportunities.
