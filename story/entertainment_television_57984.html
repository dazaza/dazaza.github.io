Introduction:

The male protagonist Ji Cunxi (played by Jimmy Lin) has the power of life and death over the development of the island. However, the island owner only wants to preserve this pristine land and does not want it to be used as a money-making tool that would destroy the environment. The eccentric island owner plans to "entrap Cunxi" to stop the development of the island... A rather plain office lady, Chen Xinyi (played by Ruby Lin), yearns to tie down her handsome and upright boyfriend. By chance, her boyfriend invites her aboard the Love Boat. Secretly rejoicing, Xinyi starts calculating how she can lose her virginity to bind him forever. But things don't go according to plan; suffering from seasickness and excessive drinking, she ends up in the wrong room... On the same day, Cunxi is planning to propose to his girlfriend. He sets up a proposal banquet on the Love Boat but unexpectedly fails. His girlfriend doesn't even board the ship. The island owner drugs Cunxi's drink. With his mind clouded, Cunxi returns to his room while Xinyi, disoriented due to seasickness, sees her boyfriend enter a room and decides to seize the moment... But again, things don't go as planned. Not only does Xinyi enter the wrong room, but she also ends up with the wrong person. Worse still, she... mistakenly wins a prize - she is pregnant...

The story begins with a type of girl found all over Asia: the "sticky note girl." She is simple, unassuming, content with little, never being anyone special, nor expecting to become one. Her function is small but indispensable, like a sticky note that can be torn off at will - inconspicuous and ordinary, but she is definitely around you! A 'luxury cruise' trip allows such an ordinary girl to encounter the most extraordinary situation. From a young age, Xinyi was always afraid of others not liking her, so she worked hard to be good to others. If someone gave her a little response, she easily liked them back. Everyone calls her a sticky note girl because when someone needs her, they can tear her off and use her, and when they don't need her, they throw her away without a second thought. Yet, she is a sentimental fool who finds it hard to let go due to her weak character, making her indecisive at crucial moments. One day, in order to win back her womanizing boyfriend Gucci, she spends a large sum of travel fees for a Love Boat tour. Unexpectedly, she walks into the wrong room, wakes up to find herself having had a one-night stand with a stranger, and discovers that her boyfriend Gucci is cheating on her with a wealthy widow, leaving her devastated.

A month later, when Xinyi sees the result of the pregnancy test, she regrets not jumping off the boat. At 25 years old, working as a law firm clerk, she is now in debt of 200,000 due to her lousy boyfriend and carrying the child of an unknown man, which is undoubtedly the most ironic joke in the world. She takes a week's leave from work, preparing to return to her hometown, "Ginger Island," to figure out what to do.

That night’s stranger is named Ji Cunxi, the ideal prince charming in every girl's dream – wealthy, handsome, and accomplished. When he meets the ordinary office girl Chen Xinyi, it marks either the start of trouble or the beginning of happiness.

Due to a hidden genetic condition in the Ji family, each generation has seen a decline in male descendants, continuing through nine generations as single heirs. This makes the elder grandmother Pearl sleep uneasily every night, fearing that this tiny spark might accidentally extinguish. The grandmother hopes that her grandson Ji Cunxi will be like other young entrepreneurs, spreading his affections everywhere. But this grandson remains pure and chaste. One day, the grandmother accidentally discovers that Xinyi is carrying Cunxi's child. Perhaps blessed by the ancestors of the Ji family, heaven has truly bestowed upon her a "little golden grandson," leading the grandmother to insist that Cunxi marry Xinyi. Thus, before her passing, she can face the ancestors of the Ji family with dignity.

Therefore, the grandmother threatens Cunxi with exposing his three-year relationship with his girlfriend, forcing Cunxi to marry Xinyi to fulfill her last wish. But Cunxi finds himself unable to face the reality of marriage and cannot decide whether to run away or have the courage to take responsibility. At this time, they suddenly discover that under the grandmother's request, they must start dating.

In fact, Cunxi has been dating a ballerina named ANNA for three years. Despite numerous romantic proposals, ANNA has repeatedly turned Cunxi down. Still, Cunxi silently waits for the day when ANNA will finally agree to his proposal and put everything aside. ANNA chooses not to marry for her career, while Cunxi unintentionally gets Xinyi pregnant due to an accident. Fortunately, ANNA is on an international tour, allowing Cunxi to hide this blunder. However, Cunxi secretly signs a prenuptial agreement with Xinyi, stating that after the child is born, they will divorce. Cunxi promises to give Xinyi a sum of money, then they will part ways with no further obligations. After careful consideration, Xinyi agrees for the sake of her child's future. Cunxi's plan is that after Xinyi gives birth, when ANNA finishes her tour and returns home, their contractual relationship will have expired, allowing him to reunite with ANNA.

Unaware of the situation, Xinyi moves into Cunxi's house and begins learning to coexist with him. The evolution from discomfort to habit between two strangers is very subtle. Initially believing they were just living under the same roof without any connection, their lives gradually intertwine due to many daily trivial matters. Under the grandmother's requirement, Xinyi moves into Cunxi's bedroom, prompting Cunxi to move to the guest room. However, he increasingly dislikes sleeping alone and often returns to his original room late at night. Over time, having someone beside him while sleeping becomes a habit. Cunxi, who disliked eating at home, begins returning home for meals. Swearing never to help with anything, Cunxi starts buying wallpapers for the baby's room and toys for the child. Just as they think this is happiness, the long-ignored bombshell ANNA appears. Cunxi never imagined that his unclear relationship with ANNA would hurt Xinyi. Leaving behind only a divorce agreement, he disappears. Thinking this ending is the best conclusion, Xinyi unknowingly occupies his heart. He vows that if he ever finds Xinyi again, he will pursue her once more, no longer as just the father of the child to the mother, but as a man pursuing a woman...
