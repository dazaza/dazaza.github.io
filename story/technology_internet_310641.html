Barcelona's Alleged "Champion Escort" Ignites Controversy: Rivals Protest - "We'll Give You 70 Points, We'll Compete for Second Place"

by qinglai888 on 2009-12-16 12:31:12

Barcelona's alleged championship handout ignites controversy: Rivals say, "We'll fight for second place even if you're given 70 points."

Sina Sports - Last weekend’s Barcelona derby left Espanyol feeling aggrieved over their loss, and Barcelona feeling their win lacked dignity. All thanks to referee Iturralde. He "mysteriously" awarded Barcelona a penalty, helping them secure a 1-0 victory over their local rivals Espanyol. Subsequently, he red-carded Spanish player Baena in the players' tunnel after Baena continuously questioned his penalty decision.

To be fair, this penalty seemed to deliberately disadvantage Espanyol. Interestingly, from this one penalty call, two entirely different versions of the truth emerged from the Barcelona and Espanyol camps.

If Buell is considered a "Real Madrid affiliated" referee, then Iturralde, one of Spain's most famous referees, is undoubtedly a "Barcelona affiliate." In previous matches refereed by Iturralde at Camp Nou, Barcelona has never lost. This season, he officiated only one match involving Barcelona, where they triumphed 2-0 away against Getafe. Another telling statistic is that in Iturralde’s last six matches involving Barcelona, five penalties were awarded—all benefiting Barcelona. According to *Marca*, four of these penalties were questionable, suggesting there was a possibility of "gifting" penalties to Barcelona.

The contentious moment occurred in the 39th minute of the match against Espanyol when Ibrahimovic crossed from the left edge of the penalty area, and as Xavi challenged for the ball, he suddenly fell. Iturralde awarded a penalty, citing Baena's foul by pulling Xavi back with his hand. This decision sparked strong dissatisfaction from the Espanyol side. Coach Mauricio Pochettino cryptically remarked post-match: “I didn’t see clearly what happened in the box, but maybe the referee did. Even after watching the replay, I can't confirm it was a penalty. My players told me at halftime that nothing happened, so in my view, this penalty does not exist.”

Espanyol player Luis Garcia expressed frustration over Iturralde’s unfairness towards Espanyol: “If we are judged by this standard, we could also get penalties in Barcelona’s box. Sure, Barcelona is very strong, but they shouldn't score points through such penalties.”

In contrast, the reaction within the Barcelona camp was completely different. Xavi, the player involved, stated: “It was an obvious penalty, didn’t you see? I was grabbed by the jersey inside the box. Baena pulled my shirt, preventing me from jumping. There’s no doubt; it was a penalty. He held me, and since I couldn’t use my legs, I had to fall.” After Xavi fell, Guardiola innocently spread his hands on the sidelines, signaling support for his player's demand for a penalty. However, in discussing this call post-game, Guardiola admitted that he actually wasn’t sure if it was a penalty, merely stating, “Xavi told me Baena pulled him, and I trust Xavi.”

Clearly, Espanyol manager Planes didn’t believe Xavi: “How can Xavi say that? By Iturralde’s standards, this Barcelona derby could have resulted in 12 penalties! I insist there was no physical contact between Baena and Xavi, and even if there was, it wouldn’t justify his fall.” CEO Collet added, “Why do we always get hurt in derby matches? Last time, it was a penalty in the 93rd minute, and Messi's handball. This time again... If the opponent were Real Madrid, someone would have surely shouted 'Down with Franco.' The federation should consider this issue—if it continues like this, why not just give Barcelona 70 points and let the rest of us teams fight for second place?”

All other arguments were futile. Regarding the penalty controversy, Baena, the party involved, remained tight-lipped, refusing to comment until two days later when he appeared on Catalonia Radio. “Yes, I pulled Xavi,” confirming the existence of the penalty. But either Xavi’s fall was overly exaggerated, or Iturralde made an incorrect call, so Baena continued, “But it definitely wasn’t a penalty. My pull on Xavi wasn’t strong enough to make him lose balance and fall. Xavi was positioned very far forward, and I reached back to feel for his position. That’s all. I slightly obstructed Xavi, but the force wasn’t enough to make him fall. You can check the TV replay. Honestly, I found it hard to believe it would be called a penalty because I was thinking before turning around that Xavi might shoot.”

Reports also mentioned that Baena confronted Iturralde in the players’ tunnel after the game, resulting in a red card. What did Baena say to anger Iturralde? “I simply told him, ‘Go watch the replay!’ But I certainly didn’t use any aggressive tone, that’s all.”

What further upset Baena was Guardiola's post-match press conference statement: “Xavi told me that Espanyol players admitted it was a penalty.” “I really don’t understand how they can say that—I never told Xavi it was a penalty. If we ever had any conversation, I would have definitely told him it wasn’t a penalty.”

This controversy gave Madrid media plenty to sensationalize. Media opinions were highly critical of this controversial call, with both *Marca* and *As* targeting Iturralde, believing it was his protection that helped Barcelona secure three points. Otherwise, Real Madrid, having played fewer games, would only trail Barcelona by two points. Conversely, Catalan media, despite Espanyol being a Barcelona team too, wouldn’t support this club bearing the Spanish royal crest. To counter the capital city’s media, *Sport* specifically analyzed the match footage and included screenshots showing Baena indeed pulling Xavi. Deputy editor Carrazo said, “Clearly, it was a penalty.” More interestingly, the paper also presented a screenshot from Real Madrid's match against Valencia, showing Benzema possibly offside when receiving the ball for their first goal against Valencia. The headline provocatively read, “Real Madrid’s first goal was offside—so what’s there to argue about?”

Fortunately, the Barcelona, Xavi, and Guardiola entangled in the penalty controversy have now left the eye of the storm in Spain, starting their FIFA Club World Cup journey in Abu Dhabi. In their eyes, securing this championship is much more meaningful than debating whether that was a penalty. (Liu Hui)
