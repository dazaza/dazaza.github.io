Enjoy the leisure of the fields

by langrentoh1 on 2012-02-20 11:09:56

Enjoying the leisure of the fields, lying outdoors reading a book, and looking at the distant beautiful scenery when tired. The next morning, we got up early to watch the sunrise. The green bean fields and yellow rape flowers emitted a gentle fragrance, and we enjoyed the energy brought by the first ray of sunshine in the morning while cycling on the country roads. On the way, we heard from the local villagers that there was a very interesting cormorant show nearby, so our group rushed there to see the charm of the legendary cormorant. Several small boats were parked by the Erhai Lake, and the cormorants stood lazily on the boats waiting for their owner's instructions, whispering secretly like a couple teasing each other. Luo Haiqiong's first reaction upon seeing the cormorants was the fear that their fish-eating mouths would peck her. While she was unaware, a villager quietly placed one on her straw hat. After the initial panic, Haiqiong calmed down when she found the cormorant obediently staying on the hat, then touched its tail and poked its wings mischievously. "Originally, they are also very gentle animals!" In the end, she even dared to let two cormorants stand on her oar simultaneously, teasing them as if doing weightlifting, scaring the cormorants flapping their wings constantly.

Beauty is everywhere. After the cormorant performance ended, we began to wander around the town. The streets of Xizhou Town were narrow and deep. Most of the people weaving through them were local farmers. They wore traditional Bai ethnic costumes or khaki blue Zhongshan suits, with reserved and silent expressions. Yunnan's sky and light often made me feel strange and amazed, and when such light shone on the old streets of Xizhou and the faces of passersby, the feeling of being separated from the world was extraordinarily strong.

Perhaps because of the connection with Yunnan, Luo Haiqiong, who played a Yunnan girl in "If You Are the One" and has filmed multiple times in Yunnan, particularly loves this place where it feels like spring all year round. Beyond the bustling city life, giving herself a long weekend break to let nature soothe her weary body and mind, what could be more wonderful than that?
