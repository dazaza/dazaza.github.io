Cai Shusheng, an official with the Ministry of Finance, said that China's proportion of indirect taxation on enterprises is too high.

by abcfofas on 2012-02-27 15:29:33

In the future, more than 70% of China's tax expenditure will be indirect taxes. Is this proportion too high? Will it increase the burden on residents? Is there a need for reform?

[Last year, the state adjusted personal income tax, and indeed the tax burden on the ordinary working class increased significantly. If the tax contained in ordinary goods could be reduced further, then our citizens would feel more confident about consumption.] A parent shopping for schoolbags for their child at a mall in Tongzhou District, Guangzhou said.

Recently, discussions about the high level of taxes contained in Chinese goods have become a hot social topic. How much tax is contained in Chinese goods? Are the taxes in Chinese goods higher than in developed countries? How should China's tax structure be adjusted in the future?

The proportion of indirectly collected taxes from enterprises is too high, increasing the burden on businesses and ordinary consumers.

Data shows that in 2011, more than 70% of China’s total tax revenue came from turnover taxes, while less than 30% came from income taxes and other types of taxes. The proportion of tax revenue paid by various enterprises was as high as 92.06%, whereas the proportion of tax revenue paid by residents was only 7.94%.

"These figures tell us that currently over 70% of China's tax revenue comes from indirect taxes," said Gao Peiyong, director of the Institute of Finance and Economics at the Chinese Academy of Social Sciences. "Indirect taxes, as one of the components of price, are often directly embedded in the selling price of goods and can be shifted through price channels."

Gao Peiyong believes that taxation levied on enterprises via goods prices is a typical feature of China's actual tax operation format. It also reflects the imbalance in China's current tax structure. The drawbacks of this unbalanced tax structure and tax revenue structure continue to be exposed.

For example, a high proportion and large scale of indirect tax revenue concentrated in the goods price channel being passed on to the whole society not only makes taxation highly correlated with inflation but also tends to push up prices in real life. Especially in the case of necessities, where the tax content is too high, it essentially becomes a mass tax borne by a wide range of consumers.

At the same time, such a high proportion and large scale of tax revenue concentrated on various enterprises generally increases the tax burden on all types of enterprises. Given the differences in competitive ability between enterprises of different sizes, small and micro-enterprises bear heavier actual tax burdens. Moreover, the proportion of direct taxes such as income tax and property tax being too small does not help in regulating income distribution and narrowing income gaps, which are important functions of taxation.

The high tax content in Chinese goods mainly stems from the fact that China's tax system differs greatly from that of developed countries.

"In the United States, over 40% of federal government revenue comes from personal income tax. If payroll taxes are included, the proportion reaches around 80%, while in China, personal income tax only accounts for 6.7% of total tax revenue. Property taxes have just started pilot programs, and there are almost none otherwise," said Jia Kang, director of the Institute of Fiscal Sciences under the Ministry of Finance.

Jia Kang analyzed that currently, China's tax structure differs greatly from that of developed countries. China mainly levies turnover taxes, which belong to indirect taxes and can be directly included in the selling price of goods as part of the cost. In contrast, some developed countries mainly levy direct taxes on residents, with very little taxation during the circulation phase, so the tax content in goods is low.

Why is the proportion of indirect taxes levied on enterprises in China high, while the proportion of direct taxes levied on residents is low? Jia Kang said this is determined by the stage of economic and social development.

In 1994, when China implemented tax reforms, the vast majority of people did not own property like houses, and their income mainly came from wages. Due to low wage levels and small income disparities, many residents' incomes did not reach the threshold for paying personal income tax. Let alone collecting direct taxes like property taxes or social security taxes from residents as done in developed countries. The feasible method was to collect taxes from enterprises during consumption and circulation phases, allowing enterprises to incorporate the taxes into consumption or operational costs, and eventually pass them on to consumers through price channels.

"Objectively speaking, such a tax structure was suitable for the socio-economic development at the time. But now, with the accumulation of individual wealth and the continuous increase in the rich and asset-owning classes, the original tax structure has become less appropriate and should undergo corresponding reforms and adjustments."

Jia Kang emphasized that the high tax content in Chinese goods does not mean that China's overall tax burden is heavier than in developed countries. To measure a country's macro tax burden level, the commonly used indicator is the proportion of fiscal revenue to GDP. In 2011, China's macro tax burden level was around 30%, while the average in developed countries was above 40%. China's overall tax burden level remains within a reasonable range.

Increasing direct taxes primarily based on income tax and property tax must also consider tax administration capabilities and people's psychological tolerance.

Gao Peiyong believed that the direction of China's future tax reform and adjustment should be, under the premise of appropriately reducing the macro tax burden, increasing direct taxes while decreasing indirect taxes; increasing taxes paid by enterprises while decreasing taxes paid by residents.

Reducing the tax burden on enterprises can achieve the goal of reducing the tax content in goods. In this regard, policies such as raising the VAT and business tax thresholds for micro and small enterprises, piloting the transformation of business tax to VAT, eliminating double taxation, and a series of structural tax reduction policies have already been implemented or are ongoing.

The pilot program of transforming business tax to VAT differs from previous tax reform actions. As China's largest tax type, changes in VAT affect the entire tax system, providing an important opportunity to optimize the tax structure and tax revenue structure. With the gradual promotion of the transformation of business tax to VAT nationwide, not only VAT but also other turnover taxes will see an important reform aimed at reducing the tax burden and further perfecting the turnover tax system. The space thus freed up will pave the way for increasing direct taxes and advancing overall tax reforms aimed at optimizing the tax structure.

Jia Kang believed that relatively speaking, reducing indirect taxes primarily based on turnover taxes is relatively mature and easier to implement, but increasing the proportion of direct taxes such as personal income tax and property tax requires substantial foundational work.

For instance, in terms of personal income tax, recent adjustments to individual income tax policies have mainly focused on tax cuts. After the personal income tax reform in 2011, only about 7% of salaried workers were covered. If taxes on the poor are to be increased, it is necessary to determine how many people fall into the lower-income bracket and effectively supervise and accurately calculate their incomes.

In terms of property tax, China currently only pilots property tax collection in Guangzhou and Chongqing. Despite the pilot period being only over a year, numerous problems have arisen. One of the most prominent issues is the excessively high tax administration cost. For example, some properties within the scope of taxation are unoccupied, and some property owners are not local. Tax officials can only repeatedly call or even visit in person to collect taxes, leading to low efficiency.

Jia Kang indicated that tax structure reform and adjustment cannot be achieved overnight. [Currently, tax authorities have no experience in collecting direct taxes, and personal income tax is mostly withheld and remitted by employers. Increasing direct taxes means that tax authorities will directly tax residents, requiring innovation in tax administration methods and improvement in tax administration efficiency. What we need to do now is clarify the reform direction, explore the establishment of a framework, and move forward step by step.]
