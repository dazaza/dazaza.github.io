Agent reveals: how does 360 compete with Baidu?

by anonymous on 2013-07-24 14:27:44

After causing a stir online, 360 Search ultimately still needs to focus on monetization. Thus, establishing a search marketing system has become one of the main tasks for 360 this year. Under the immense pressure from Baidu's vast nationwide channels, how is 360, as a latecomer, able to enter the market?

The reporter interviewed some local agents of 360 Search to uncover the expansion status of 360’s search marketing system. We learned that 360’s rapidly growing market share allowed the agents to smell an opportunity to make money. Additionally, Baidu’s tightening of agency rights in recent years indirectly contributed to the agents switching sides. The number of 360’s agents and orders have both significantly increased compared to the beginning of the year.

Specific details are as follows:

1. In fact, the agency rights for 360 Search are highly sought after by the agents. So far, 360 Search agents cover 16 provinces and municipalities, totaling 27 agencies, whereas four months ago, this number was only 13. Moreover, many local agents have started developing lower-tier agents. The agency rights for 360 Search are quite popular locally, “just like during the early stages of Baidu’s development when many agents earned substantial benefits. Therefore, with 360 entering the search market, they all eagerly want to secure this agency as soon as possible.” An agent from Henan told Sina Tech that they obtained the agency rights last month.

2. The development of 360’s agents also benefits from the void created by Baidu reclaiming its agency rights. The aforementioned Henan agent stated, “After so many years of Baidu’s development, many places have had their agency rights reclaimed, and branch offices or provincial general agencies have been established.” 360 does not have branch offices in various locations yet and mainly relies on agents. Among these agents, some have previously represented Baidu ads, some have represented Sogou or SOSO ads, and even some have represented 3721 ads. However, currently, they have shifted their focus to 360, and most agents have established independent companies specifically for representing 360 ads.

3. Low price is an important advantage of 360 Search ads in local markets. A Wuhan-based agent believes that the current advantages of 360 Search ads are: less competition, low prices, and high conversion rates. "The prices of some 360 Search keyword ads are less than one-fifth of Baidu’s." He said. With the reputation as the "second largest search engine" and the prior education provided by Baidu in the market, this agent claims promotion isn't too difficult. Nevertheless, many clients remain观望 (on the fence), because 360 just entered the market, and they "also need to see if 360 is truly willing to develop the search engine properly."

4. In the past six months, the order volume of 360 Search ads has grown rapidly. In March, we reported that 360 Search completed approximately 1,000 orders in Beijing over three months. A salesperson from Tian Di Online, one of 360’s Beijing region agents, told us that now they can exceed 600 orders per month alone. There are two agents for 360 Search in Beijing: Tian Di Online and Ju Tao Culture, the latter having entered later and being smaller in scale. Henan San Bai Liu, which just secured the agency rights last month, has already completed more than 1,000 orders.

5. The average order value of completed transactions is around ten thousand yuan, which is not low. The minimum opening fee for 360 Search ads is 4,600 yuan, including a 600 yuan service fee and a 4,000 yuan prepayment for ad placements. A Wuhan agent revealed that the current average order value is about ten thousand yuan. For comparison, Baidu's customer average revenue last quarter was 14,500 yuan.

6. 360 Search ads have covered all industries. Currently, 360 Search ads cover all industries except healthcare. Recently, they have also opened up previously restricted health supplement and adult product industries. Notably, 360 only accepts corporate customers and does not accept individual customers (Baidu can accept individual customers). Furthermore, one company can only place ads for one website (Baidu allows multiple websites per company). Many agents indicated they are developing offline customers such as educational and consulting firms.

But can 360 threaten Baidu? No.

Although 360 Search's agency system has initially taken shape, its competition with Baidu is still at a very basic stage. One Baidu agent frankly stated: "Currently, 360 does not pose a threat to Baidu."

Based on feedback from various 360 agents, the current competition is not yet intense. "Right now, 360 Search primarily offers clients a new market and a new choice," one agent said, noting that many clients advertise on both platforms.

However, this is undoubtedly a temporary situation. 360 Search's scale is still small, requiring more subsequent investment. Nonetheless, monetizing through search ads will certainly be its next most imaginative income growth point. When 360's client base truly grows and prices rise, there may inevitably be another direct confrontation between 360 and Baidu.
