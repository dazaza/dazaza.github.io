As the year-end is approaching again, after calculating Liu Peng realized that he still lacked 100,000 yuan to achieve his sales target. With only a few days left in the year, this was the fourth year in a row that he had failed to meet his sales recovery target. Liu Peng felt extremely disappointed and almost lost confidence in himself. After ten years in sales, he even began to fundamentally change his perception and initial intentions about the marketing profession. Faced with the reality of failing to meet the sales recovery target for four consecutive years at the same company, Liu Peng wanted to use all his strength to prove his ability and strength once again this year. However, it seems that he has failed again! His hope of achieving his target was once again cut down by "marketing - this white horse"! It's really regrettable! Moreover, as a regional sales manager whose performance wasn't even among the worst and who had previously achieved remarkable results, Liu Peng is now reflecting on the situation while the company also needs to reflect.

The enormous pressure of the company's retention tasks has fallen on the shoulders of the sales department and personnel. Either they are treated like "masters" or they are not valued at all. If marketing personnel fail to make sales or recover payments, they will face disdain and be let go. Sales teams are constantly changing, and sales personnel are the most unstable group within companies. Extensive, one-size-fits-all, and simplistic approaches are the biggest problems currently prevalent in corporate marketing. This leads to increasingly poor marketing performance and operational environments for companies, which in turn affects many marketers like Liu Peng, causing them to frequently fail to meet their annual sales recovery targets. Consequently, marketing becomes meaningless to these marketing personnel, they lose touch with their true self-worth, and the company's objectives become unattainable. In which direction should the sword of marketing be wielded? How can hope become reality? These are questions considered by many marketing personnel who often fail to meet their sales recovery targets. There's a saying: "Success must have a method, failure must have a reason." Therefore, there must be hidden reasons behind the inability of marketing personnel to consistently meet their targets. Summarized, I believe the main issues are:

Due to intense competition and the company's eagerness to succeed, unrealistic targets and tasks are set for the marketing team and individuals. Excessive expansion of targets, especially when given very short timeframes, leads to the entire marketing team's behavior becoming distorted when they cannot complete their tasks. This includes disregarding standards, misusing resources, harming channel interests, etc. Unreasonable targets inevitably affect the team atmosphere, leading to problems in marketing, management, and culture. Both companies and individual marketing personnel pursue short-term actions. Short-term goal management by companies leads to short-term thinking and behavior among sales personnel, focusing solely on short-term goals. At the end of the month, how many products can be sold and how much money can be recovered? It can be said that long-term goals set by companies are deceptive, fooling the market and themselves, resulting in no marketer caring whether customers are satisfied with them. Without retention, even if customers are satisfied, it's useless; no one cares about potential projects for next year since they can't get through this year. As long as this machine doesn't have any problems this year, I'll sell it to the customer. When problems arise, I will already be gone anyway. Companies overly focus on performance orientation, neglecting the development of marketing personnel capabilities. Overemphasis on performance easily damages internal rules within the team, promoting individual heroism rather than focusing on overall system orientation and the overall capability development of the team. Capability development requires a period of cultivation, including personal skills training, following various regulations, standardizing management templates, technical support, etc. Lack of nurturing in marketing teams leads to slow overall marketing capability development in companies, with systemic deficiencies present in both small and medium-sized enterprises as well as large companies.

Excessive emphasis on emotion and variability, while a large number of our marketing theories and courses aim to teach sales personnel various sales techniques, warning them to maintain certain sales mindsets, such as confidence, overconfidence, and more confidence! Marketing and handling affairs do not emphasize institutional construction, detailed market analysis, or the improvement of intrinsic qualities. Instead, it relies on sheer determination: I must win! A preference for "storm-like movements" brings about speculative tendencies, disregard for game rules, and corruption issues, among others.
