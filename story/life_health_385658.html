6 Essential Anti-Radiation Foods for Every Home

Anti-Radiation Recommendation 1: Lycopene

Reason:

Representative: Tomatoes, watermelons, and other red fruits. Lycopene is found in many red fruits, with the highest content being in tomatoes. Lycopene is by far the strongest antioxidant among carotenoids, with an antioxidant capacity 100 times that of vitamin E. It has an extremely strong ability to clear free radicals, offering benefits such as anti-radiation, prevention of cardiovascular diseases, improved immunity, and anti-aging, earning it the nickname "Plant Gold." Recommended dish: Tomato scrambled eggs. Since lycopene is a fat-soluble vitamin, cooking it with oil improves absorption, and heating promotes the release of lycopene.

Anti-Radiation Recommendation 2: Vitamin E, Vitamin C

Reason:

Representative: Various beans, olive oil, sunflower seed oil; mustard greens, cabbage, radishes, and other cruciferous vegetables; fresh jujubes, oranges, kiwis, and other fresh fruits. Beans, olive oil, sunflower seed oil, and cruciferous vegetables are rich in vitamin E, while fresh jujubes, oranges, and kiwis are rich in vitamin C. Both vitamin E and vitamin C are antioxidants that can reduce oxidative reactions caused by computer radiation, akin to putting on a layer of "anti-radiation clothing" for our skin, thereby reducing skin damage. Additionally, fresh vegetables and fruits have an anti-radiation effect because they can make the blood alkaline, dissolving toxins deposited in cells and excreting them through urine. Recommended dish: Olive oil mixed salad. Both vitamins E and C are not heat-resistant, so eating them raw maximizes their effectiveness. Moreover, salads allow you to consume multiple vegetables and fruits at once, paired with olive oil for better results.

Anti-Radiation Recommendation 3: Vitamin A, Beta-Carotene

Reason:

Representative: Cod liver oil, animal livers; chicken, egg yolks; broccoli, carrots, spinach, etc. These foods are rich in vitamin A and beta-carotene, which can effectively protect the eyes. Natural beta-carotene is a powerful antioxidant that can effectively protect human cells from damage, thus avoiding cellular cancerous changes. Long-term consumption of carrots can reduce the harm caused by radiation and excessive ultraviolet exposure. Currently, natural beta-carotene is also used in cosmetics abroad for its anti-radiation, protective, moisturizing, and anti-aging effects on the skin. Recommended dish: Stir-fried broccoli with carrots. Beta-carotene is a fat-soluble vitamin, so stir-frying it with oil aids absorption.

Anti-Radiation Recommendation 4: Selenium

Reason:

Representative: Sesame seeds, malt, astragalus; yeast, eggs, beer; lobsters, tuna, and other seafood; garlic, mushrooms, etc. The trace element selenium has an antioxidant effect, protecting against radiation and aging by blocking body peroxidation reactions. Foods rich in selenium include sesame seeds, malt, and astragalus. Recommended dish: Black sesame paste. Sesame seeds are not only rich in selenium but also in vitamin E, which has antioxidant properties, making the effect even stronger.

Anti-Radiation Recommendation 5: Lipopolysaccharides, Vitamin A Precursors

Reason:

Representative: Green tea, mung beans, etc. If you're not accustomed to drinking green tea, chrysanthemum tea works just as well. Modern medical research confirms that mung beans/green tea contain lipopolysaccharides and vitamin A precursors, which help detoxify the body, accelerate metabolism, and effectively resist various forms of pollution. Recommended dish: Mung bean soup, green tea. There's a saying in folk wisdom that "mung bean soup cures all poisons." Drinking some green tea and mung bean soup daily not only provides effective components against radiation but also hydrates and enhances metabolism.
