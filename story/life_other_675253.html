Love Should Be Expressed Aloud

At one o'clock in the morning, the moonlight was bright.

I wheeled myself to the balcony and gazed at the bright moon, my heart heavy with sorrow. It's been a year, I wonder if Dayu has been doing well this past year? Has his stomach ailment healed? Does he hate me or does he miss me? I lit a cigarette, having forgotten when I started smoking. People who are empty inside say that it's not the smoke they're after but loneliness. But for me, it's more than just loneliness.

"Ziruo, I will definitely prove to your father that choosing me was right!"

"Don't worry, even if you can never stand up again, I will still love you as much as before!"

"Ziruo, why don't you answer the phone?"

"Ziruo, where have you gone?"

"Ziruo, Dayu is looking for you everywhere, where have you gone?"

"Ziruo, Dayu is falling apart..."

"Ziruo..."

I couldn't continue recalling, I threw away the cigarette and started drinking. Alcohol could temporarily numb the nerves, causing a short-term memory loss. Without friends or family, I was drunk every day, sinking into darkness. I threw the wine glass, hearing a "bang" from downstairs. The glass broke, but what broke wasn't just the glass, wasn't it my heart? Looking back at the empty house, such a large four-bedroom, two-living room place, yet there was only me. What's wrong with me? What do I need?

Ten years ago, after graduating from university, Dayu and I returned to our hometown of Hunan. Dayu brought up marriage to my father, but what we thought would be a smooth romance was met with fierce opposition from my father. My father had no valid reason, only full of沧桑(cang1sang1 - vicissitudes) and the smell of cigarettes asking me, "What about Xiaofeng?" I never expected that a small joke from childhood would become a stumbling block for my marriage. "That was just a joke from childhood..."

"Nonsense!" My usually kind father harshly interrupted me before I finished my first sentence. "That wasn't a joke, that was a promise! That's a guarantee for your happiness!"

I was frightened out of my wits. Dayu was also so shocked that he didn't dare to make a sound.

Xiaofeng was an arranged marriage set up by my father and Xiaofeng's mother when we were two years old. We read in the same class in junior high school, then went our separate ways for schooling, without contact. Occasionally, I heard my father say that Xiaofeng joined the army. He also said that Xiaofeng, despite being young and spirited, could have advanced in the military if not for his habit of fighting with others. After five years, he eventually left. For such a person, I indeed had no feelings or evaluations.

To give Xiaofeng a fair chance, my father called him over and in front of the three of us said, "Alright, give you five years. Let Dayu and Xiaofeng compete. Whoever surpasses the other, I'll marry Ziruo off to them." My father's tone left no room for negotiation. A perfectly good relationship was suddenly shrouded in pressure. Both Dayu and I were extremely unhappy. To give my father some closure and to provide me with a satisfactory answer, the day after my father announced the competition, Dayu said he wanted to try his luck in Shenzhen. I, wanting to achieve my own dreams while also staying close to Dayu, convinced my father with countless reasons.

Dayu and I boarded the train bound for Shenzhen. Just as the train started moving, Xiaofeng rushed up and shouted loudly towards the slowly departing train: "We aren't an arranged marriage, it isn't our parents' decree!" I didn't know the true meaning behind Xiaofeng's words, but a sour taste welled up in my tear ducts, and I burst into tears in Dayu's arms...

Five years passed in the blink of an eye. Through his efforts, Dayu established his own company in Shenzhen and bought a house; meanwhile, my father proudly announced Xiaofeng's good news over the phone, saying that Xiaofeng had graduated from military school and become an officer in the army. I thought my father would continue to strongly interfere with my marriage, but unexpectedly, after five years, my father let me choose for myself. In fact, my father knew my final choice, but there was an extra sigh in his long lamentation, adding, "Ah Ziruo, your father cannot save you."

Half a month before our wedding preparations, I was unexpectedly involved in a car accident, severely injuring my spine and becoming paralyzed from the waist down, relying on a wheelchair to get around. Dayu worked tirelessly to send me abroad for treatment, earning money night and day. The excessive strain caused him chronic gastritis and weakened immunity. I entrusted classmates to buy a lot of spirulina and kudzu extract, hoping to protect his health. Since I was already like this, he couldn't afford any mistakes. But the more I cared for him, the harder he pushed himself. The immense work pressure and life stress made Dayu sleepless every night. The lavender essential oil placed by his bedside would run out in less than ten days. I couldn't imagine how many hours Dayu could sleep in a month without the help of lavender oil. I knew Dayu loved me and felt responsible for me, along with a sense of vindication against my father's disapproval. Therefore, concealing the truth was the best way to protect our loved ones.

Until one late night, Dayu came home drunk and collapsed onto my lap, crying loudly: "Mom asked why we haven't had a baby yet? Ruohua, I really, truly love you..." I used all my strength to embrace this increasingly gaunt man, the man I loved with my life but who was draining his energy for me. Tears streamed down my face. It was time for me to leave. I couldn't let this half-disabled body drag him down for the rest of his life. Loving him meant hoping he lived well, happily. And I, I could no longer give him happiness. Suddenly, I remembered my father's warning, a chill running down my spine. Was it destiny or prophecy?

I used the savings I earned from working part-time to buy a house, taking all my clothes and personal items from Dayu's place. Leaving was to give Dayu a fresh start. I asked friends to buy a bunch of preventive health products from a wellness center, not wanting Dayu to fall ill, really not wanting him to! With handwriting ruined by too much computer use, I carefully wrote:

"...

Narong Xiong Feng capsules - strengthen the body, improve kidney function, the kidneys are the foundation of life!

Kudzu Extract - detoxifies alcohol, protects the stomach and liver;

Deep-sea fish oil capsules - lower cholesterol, prevent high blood pressure, high cholesterol;

Spirulina tablets - repair enteritis, relieve work tension, maintain energy;

Lavender essential oil - improve insomnia, promote sleep;

Relieve headache essential oil - treats various headaches, no side effects;

Tongjing Huoluo essential oil - treats shoulder periarthritis, neck soreness;

I - your Ziruo - will always love you!"

I turned off my phone. The phone had lost its meaning to me. Moving into my own apartment, I was alone, licking my wounds, silently blessing everyone.

"Knock knock" I woke up in the morning to urgent knocking on the door. "Ziruo, open the door," my father's voice came from outside, so distant yet near, so unfamiliar yet familiar. How could it be? Who knew I was here? I hurriedly dressed and walked out with crutches to open the door.

The people outside the door took my breath away. Father, Xiaofeng, Xiaofeng behind Dayu, Dayu's mother, a whole group of people came...

In an instant, tears flowed uncontrollably.

Father held out a small bottle to me: Knowing you like essential oils, this is sandalwood, to bless your health.

Xiaofeng also held out a small bottle: This is lavender, it's the love I've waited for you for thousands of years. If not in this life, I'll wait for you in the next.

Dayu held a small bottle with both hands: This is rose, each drop of rose is pressed from 8000 roses, there are 200 drops here, gathered from 1600000 roses, gathering infinite love. Don't leave me, don't leave everyone.

Dayu's mother held up a small bottle: This is geranium, it rekindles hope in your life. Illness is not scary, what's scary is your avoidance. Let's face it together.

Tears flowed endlessly like threads.
