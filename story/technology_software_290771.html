Seven remedies for a healthy life

by edifier183 on 2009-12-03 20:59:15

Life in this world is both short and bumpy, both an honor and helpless, both rich and poor, both glorious and fallen, both strong and fragile, both happy and sad, both joyful and lost, both bright and dim, both kind and ruthless, both热情and cold, both magical and ordinary, both beautiful and bitter, both loving and jealous, both upright and stingy, both gain and loss, etc., always complementary entanglements of our life circles. However, we must understand the seven heart medicines of life: "a good heart, willing to do good; a broad mind, magnanimous; an upright heart, open and aboveboard; a calm heart, tranquil as water; a pleasant heart, contented; a peaceful heart, adaptable; a sincere heart, wholeheartedly." Only then can we treat our own "hundred ailments" and thus be full of energy and radiant! A good heart, willing to do good A good heart is everyone's innate nature, no need for artificiality or pretense. Having a good heart means having one's principles for being a person and dealing with matters, not always sitting in a well looking at the sky with an attitude of "none of my business, high hanging." Instead, one should take voluntary actions, which is the "daily good deed" of merit "abundant fruits." Doing good willingly is a conscious genuine contribution, a natural true expression, a sincere charity. Being friendly with people is very approachable reciprocity, doing good is the spread of great love without borders, accumulating virtue is making love fill the world beautifully and attractively. Broad-mindedness, magnanimous As the saying goes: "broad-mindedness leads to a healthy body." A person who is often broad-minded will surely be someone who is always cheerful and smiling. Seedlings are not as big as trees, trees are not as big as mountains, mountains are not as big as seas, but seas are not as big as hearts! One should remember to be magnanimous often, not always haggling over trifles, not quarreling over small profits, and not fighting for temporary promotions and wealth! If there is mutual betrayal, in the end, everyone will be hurt, giving others the chance to "fisherman's advantage"! Therefore, no matter when or where, one must be magnanimous. Sometimes it is also acceptable to "retreat bravely, keep a distance, and preserve oneself wisely." This is a strategy of advancing by retreating, a calmness of wisdom, a profundity of cutting off the fuel at the bottom of the pot. Upright heart, open and aboveboard An upright heart has no private desires, rather straightforwardly prominent. An upright heart is not taking risks opportunistically, but sincerely humble; an upright heart is not cunning coercion, but open helping others; an upright heart is not treacherous collusion, but transparent openness; an upright heart is not intentional falsification, but intentional correction of omissions; an upright heart is not impulsive substitution, but firm prudence. Calm heart, tranquil as water A calm heart is the manifestation of peace in life, the ease of smiling at风云, the transcendence of renewal. Calm like water does not mean riding the wind and breaking the waves courageously forward like a vast ocean, but rather the tranquility and serenity of still pond water slowly rippling. Thus, those with calm hearts often deeply flow, showing a profound highest realm! Pleasant heart, contented A pleasant heart always holds an optimistic upward unique sentiment, nothing mixed up. Remember this sentence: "As long as one's heart is clear, there will be no storms!" Yes, a pleasant heart will sincerely cherish, will appreciate leisurely. Contentment is the eager hope during favorable times, the fervent expectation during abundant harvests, the passionate hope during successful returns. Contentment is the optimism and letting go of persistence, the detachment and wisdom of unwavering loyalty. Peaceful heart, adaptable A peaceful heart is the stability and comfort after achieving ideal results, while remaining calm is a solid elegance. Of course, adapting to circumstances is the greatest essence of contentment, as long as one can face all changes in life habitually, one will gain credibility and reliability, respect and elegance. Sincere heart, wholeheartedly A sincere heart is a mirror reflecting one's culture, knowledge, taste, personality, character, temperament, upbringing, and cultivation. Without sincerity, it is impossible to have a place in the space of life. Clearly, sincerity is the most direct way of expressing interpersonal relationships, sincerity is a firm trust that everyone looks forward to, sincerity is the high moral integrity that people must pursue in the end. With the "seven heart medicines of life" always carried around, one will also possess miraculous medicine, and can always perform miraculous cures. Even if one's life is more "nothing is difficult in the world for one who sets his mind to it" leading to smooth sailing...
