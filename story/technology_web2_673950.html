Jiangsu College Entrance Examination 2012: If there were no college entrance examination, would you be able to compete with the rich second generation? http://bbs.gaokaocn.com/index.asp?/6519-1-0-1-1.html The reason why "If there were no college entrance examination, would you be able to compete with the rich second generation?" was "decisively" included in the "college entrance examination motivational slogans" and caused heated discussions is probably mainly due to its strong motivational effect from both positive and negative aspects. On one hand, it positively emphasizes the extreme importance of the "college entrance examination" channel for social competition and "making a name for oneself." In other words, the college entrance examination is still a relatively fair and justifiable "upward mobility" channel that students should take seriously. On the other hand, the negative and somewhat tragic motivational effect is that it actually highlights such a narrow reality: currently, the channels that can help children from ordinary families fairly and justly achieve "upward mobility" are already very few, and the college entrance examination is almost the "only" one. In other words, other competitive channels outside the college entrance examination are not something that ordinary family children who are not part of the rich second generation can hope or rely on. Therefore, you can only earnestly cherish this rare "only" channel of the "college entrance examination."

However, following this negative motivational effect, if we further examine the social reality, we will find that, to some extent, the reverse question is also completely valid: "If there is a college entrance examination, can you definitely compete with the rich second generation?"

In the context of continuously intensifying "social class solidification" and increasingly evident "intergenerational inheritance" trends, the fairness and justice value and role of the current college entrance examination system are constantly being eroded. In this channel of the college entrance examination, the rich second generation and other privileged children have much smoother paths than ordinary impoverished children, with more opportunities. The trend becomes more obvious in prestigious key universities. Research shows that the proportion of rural students in China's key universities has been continuously declining since the 1990s. The proportion of rural students at Peking University dropped from thirty percent to ten percent, and the rural student source for Tsinghua University's Class of 2010 accounted for only seventeen percent. Quality educational resources are increasingly concentrated in urban and wealthy regions.

Moreover, even if impoverished children manage to keep up through the college entrance examination and "compete with the rich second generation," it does not mean that they can continue to fairly compete with the "rich second generation" outside the college entrance examination. For example, in employment channels within government agencies and public institutions, impoverished children without "family background" or "dad-comparison" advantages have fewer chances compared to "rich second generation" and "official second generation."

"If there were no college entrance examination, you would definitely not be able to compete with the rich second generation," but even "with the college entrance examination," can you compete with the rich second generation? Such a tragic and helpless situation not only constitutes a motivation and inspiration but also a kind of frustration and sorrow.
