Man lives under bridge for 4 months: I don't want to be a burden to my family

by xue94fwsh on 2012-02-28 11:51:34

A man lived in a cave. The cave entrance was too small, and one could only go inside by bending over. It was relatively dark inside. With the light of a flashlight, a reporter from the Shangbao newspaper saw a space about six to seven square meters large. On the ground were several beddings neatly spread out. One side of the bedding was close to the slope's yellow soil, while the other side touched the protruding bridge structure. In the gap between the bridge and the bottom of the cave, there was a water cup and a few white medicine bottles. Aside from these, the only remaining items in the cave were some collected scraps. A team of 17 people had been volunteering as guides for three years without a break. Above the cave was the bridge structure. Every time a vehicle passed, a slight vibration could be felt inside the cave.

"Those who used to live here have moved away, so I came," he said. Three years ago, due to hypertension, he suffered a stroke which paralyzed half of his body, leaving him unable to work. To not burden his family, last year around August or September, he left home alone and wandered by collecting scraps. "I left so as not to burden my children," Lian Deyou said. His two sons were very filial and had spent a lot of money on his medical treatment. To avoid his family, after wandering, he did not dare to call home.

The "house" was very small and low, so Lian Deyou could only sit bent over. Lian Deyou dragged his semi-disabled body in and out of his "home" every day.

If you know of any similar job opportunities nearby, please call the Shangbao hotline at 0371-86088666 to help him so that he no longer has to live in fear inside the cave.

He does not want to go home but would like to find a job.

At 1 p.m. yesterday, under the guidance of patrol officers, reporters arrived at Jinshui Overpass. On the slope, a cave about 70 centimeters high and half a meter wide came into view. The exposed yellow soil on both sides of the cave entrance had long been dried by the wind.

He said that during the day, he collects scraps now, eats steamed buns bought casually by the roadside when hungry, and drinks cool water from the pipe outside the cave when thirsty. He earns about 3 yuan a day, and if more, then around 5 yuan.

The man said his name is Lian Deyou, 50 years old, a farmer from Lianzhuang Village, Doumen Township, Huaiyang City, Zhoukou. He has been wandering in Zhengzhou for more than half a year and moved to this cave four months ago.

Patrol officers mentioned they could send him back to Huaiyang, but Lian Deyou refused. He said that returning home would mean he couldn't do anything, "watching my children suffer for me makes me feel uneasy; it's better to wander outside." When asked whether living in the cave worried him about safety issues, Lian Deyou lowered his head, paused for a moment, and said, "At first, I couldn't sleep with the noise of passing vehicles, worrying the bridge might collapse. But slowly, I got used to it."

Wang Yajun, a patrol officer, said that he had previously heard about a cave being dug here, but didn't expect someone to live inside. Based on the color of the yellow soil inside the cave, he judged that the cave had been dug open for more than half a year.

Lian Deyou said that actually, he didn't want to live like this, but couldn't find a more suitable place. "I want to find a job, such as guarding a construction site," Lian Deyou said. Due to physical limitations, he can't do heavy work, but jobs like gatekeeping are still manageable.

Subsequently, the Shangbao reporter met the man living inside the cave on the slope outside. He was not tall, slim, with a dark complexion. His gray hair was visible under his hat, making him look older. When the reporter approached, he was tidying up a bundle of broken wires. Because his right hand was disabled, he stepped on one end of the wire with his left foot and carefully peeled off the plastic bags wrapped around the wire with his left hand. "The copper wire inside the wire can be sold."

Yesterday at noon, patrol officers from the Weilai Road Office of Zhengzhou discovered a cave dug into the slope under the elevated bridge south of the intersection of Jinsui Road and Zhongzhou Avenue in Zhengzhou, where someone was living.

Due to illness leading to hemiplegia, in order not to burden his family, the 50-year-old man from Zhoukou, Lian Deyou, has been living in a cave under the south side of the Jinshui Elevated Bridge in Zhengzhou for the past four months. Whenever vehicles pass over the bridge, he worries it might collapse.
