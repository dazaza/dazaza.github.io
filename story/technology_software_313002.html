Sichuan-Chongqing match clouded by goalkeeper Lan Yi's avoidance; teammates support: Would he be that stupid?

by yinding999 on 2009-12-17 21:25:21

The Sichuan-Chongqing match was shrouded in doubt, with the goalkeeper Lan Yi being questioned. His teammates stood by him, saying "Would he be that stupid?"

On October 5th this year, when Chengdu Sheffied United lost to the Chongqing team at home, there were many objections from outside sources. However, within the Sheffied United team, there wasn't such a deep taboo about that match.

"It's not like that. If that game was a fixed match, it would have been too bizarre," one player told the reporter. A few days ago, CCTV broadcast some speculations about that match, which clearly put enormous pressure on Lan Yi, the Chengdu Sheffied United goalkeeper who made low-level mistakes during that game.

In the morning of the 15th, while other team members were testing their YOYO skills on the field, the goalkeepers, under the guidance of their coach, were training on another field. Lan Yi participated in the training normally. However, when the reporter came to take photos, Lan Yi intentionally or unintentionally avoided the camera. At this time, he didn't want to be overly exposed.

According to the reporter's understanding, Chengdu team placed great importance on the home match against the Chongqing team. The Sichuan-Chongqing derby has always been intense. It is said that Xu Hongtao had reminded each player before the match, saying that they were shouldering the responsibility of the entire city and must work hard. If they played poorly, everyone in the club would bear the responsibility. However, they still lost the match.

After the match, all eyes were on Lan Yi. Several leaders of the club had serious talks with Lan Yi, asking him to explain the situation. However, his teammates didn't think there was any moral issue with Lan Yi: "Lan Yi might have been too nervous that day, making him lose his composure. You think, in such an occasion, with the bosses reminding them before the match, would he be that stupid? All those who have been goalkeepers know that if he wanted to let water in, there wouldn't be a need for such obvious actions. Just slowing down the action by zero-point-something seconds would let the ball in. Would he need to make such exaggerated movements? We feel that there might really be no problem with that match. Can't the Chongqing team win against us once? This is football."

Ma Mingyu expressed that Chengdu Sheffied United would maintain a positive and open attitude towards everything, "We will actively cooperate with this anti-fraud and anti-corruption campaign. However, regarding the match against the Chongqing team, I still hope that evidence can be provided."

On December 13th, CCTV News Channel aired a news segment titled "The 'Sichuan-Chongqing Match' Questioned by Fans as Being Fixed." In this news report, several Chengdu fans claimed that the match where Chengdu Sheffied United lost 2-3 to Chongqing Lifan on October 5th was a fixed match because of the three low-level mistakes made by the Sheffied United goalkeeper Lan Yi, which seemed too amateurish. One female fan said, "At the time, many fans took out hundred-yuan notes and shouted 'fixed match'. Some even said, 'If you want money, we can give it to you, but playing like this is an insult to the fans!'" Currently, there is no news indicating that relevant departments have started an investigation into this match.

This year's Chinese Super League has seen many such confusing matches.

On September 12th, 2009, Shandong Luneng defeated Tianjin 4-0 at home. There were also issues with this match. Recently, China News Weekly disclosed that a bookmaker had known beforehand that Tianjin would lose by four goals.

On September 27th, 2009, Chongqing Lifan defeated Jiangsu Sainty 2-0 at home. In the second half, the visiting team suddenly substituted their main forward Tan Si and others. The host team scored two goals in the last moments, with the first goal being a penalty caused by an unnecessary foul inside the penalty area by the Jiangsu player who replaced Tan Si.

On October 24th, 2009, Chengdu Sheffied United lost 0-3 to Shenzhen at home. After the match, Sheffied United players threw footballs to thank the fans, but the fans shouted "fixed match" and threw the balls back.
