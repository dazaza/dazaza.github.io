Web2.0Share Weekly: Qianfang Payment, Time Network, Ye Ai, Shadow Cities, etc.

by ybcz0519 on 2011-08-12 21:42:45

Web2.0Share Weekly is a featured column from Tianya Haige. Each week, it selects the latest excellent startups domestically and shares them with everyone. Startups can submit via Weibo: @web20share or email: watsonxu{at}web20share.com.

Qianfang Payment: China's Square?: Qianfang is an innovative mobile payment companion that allows anyone to collect debit card and credit card payments anytime, anywhere. The team consists of senior product and technical personnel from previous companies such as MasterCard, IBM, ZTE, Motorola, Baidu, Tencent, Yicha, etc. Additionally, there is a similar company in Shenzhen called iboxpay (http://iboxpay.com/).

Brief Review: Given the growth trend of smartphones, the prospects look good; however, for innovation in the financial sector, resources and background are more important than the product itself!

Link: http://qfpay.net/

Ctrip's Lvping iPhone App officially lands on App Store. This application aims to help users better share and discover highly-rated hotels, attractions, and destinations. Key features include: finding hotels and attractions based on review rankings; GPS search for nearby hotels and attractions; providing hotel details and traveler reviews for booking; publishing reviews anytime, anywhere, etc.

Link: http://itunes.apple.com/cn/app/id453438200?mt=8

Vasee Event Network: A website offering event organization solutions, it is a professional platform for event publishing, participation, and sharing. Positioned as a software platform specifically for organizing, registering, and convening events, it provides services for various activities such as meetings, training sessions, outdoor travel, gatherings, sports events, performances, including publishing, convening, registration, charging, etc.

Link: http://www.vasee.com/

Baijia: Self-publishing Platform - Create your own iPad magazine.

Link: http://mag.mycolorway.com/

Time Network is a time service network platform based on micro-information, also a social networking platform. With the website as the base point and mobile internet as the development direction, it closely follows the "time" concept, emphasizing online and offline interactions. The core functions of the site are: Time Blog, Selling Time, Buying Time, Information Interaction, Mobile Applications, among five blocks.

Link: http://www.ayotime.com/

YeaI - The first independent anonymous secret admirer notification push service: This seems to have a similar service on Facebook before, but now it's finally available domestically.

Link: http://www.yeai.com/

Adobe releases free HTML development tool: Adobe released the first preview version of Adobe Edge on Monday. Adobe Edge is a brand new HTML5 WEB dynamic interaction design and development tool that can provide effects similar to Flash, but its construction standard is similar to HTML—JavaScript and CSS.

Link: http://labs.adobe.com/technologies/edge/

Shadow Cities, a Finnish iPhone game, takes the top spot in Finland's App Store, surpassing Angry Birds. It's a Location-Based Game (LBG) that turns the streets around players' lives into game stages, allowing adventures in the neighborhoods around their homes. Check out the promotional video for this game.

Link: http://www.shadowcities.com/

Editions by AOL: AOL launches a news information reading iPad app similar to Flipboard, named Editions. Those interested can download and try it out.

Link: http://editions.com/

Baidu releases Baidu Maps iPhone client. Key functions include: 1. Search: places, buses, subways; 2. Routes: public transport, driving, walking; 3. Nearby: convenient and quick search for surrounding areas; 4. Offline maps; 5. Traffic conditions: real-time traffic updates and predictions, etc.

Link: http://itunes.apple.com/us/app/id452186370?mt=8
