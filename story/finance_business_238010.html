Jack Ma blasted the banking industry for being prejudiced against the poor and favoring the rich: banks can be ignored by small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs).

by sky110 on 2009-09-15 01:58:25

Alibaba Chairman Jack Ma Criticizes Banking Industry

Last Saturday, at the closing ceremony of the APEC SME Summit, there was a heated discussion on the difficulties faced by small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) in obtaining financing. Why, after so many years of talking about the difficulty of financing for SMEs, has the situation not improved? Jack Ma, chairman of the Alibaba board, took the lead in criticizing the banking industry for favoring the wealthy over the poor, stating that this is the root cause of the financial pain for SMEs. He suggested that SME entrepreneurs can completely ignore banks just like Alibaba does.

Why Banks Ignore SMEs

"Why do state-owned enterprises and real estate companies receive loans while SMEs do not? I've been hearing the same question for six years now. How many more years do I have to listen?"... During the summit, when discussing the long-standing issue of financing difficulties for SMEs, Jack Ma became quite agitated.

In his subsequent speech, Jack Ma's words remained strong. He said that the reason he no longer needs to actively seek out banks but instead banks come knocking on Alibaba's door is simply because he now has money. A bank general manager once told him that banks are most afraid of dealing with situations of information asymmetry. However, Jack Ma bluntly stated that the problem between banks and SMEs is not one of information asymmetry, but rather of interest asymmetry, trust asymmetry, and responsibility asymmetry. If banks truly intend to provide financing for SMEs, it is not a difficult task.
