Q Enjoyment: O2O Based on Interactive Screens for Targeted Audiences

by ybcz0519 on 2011-11-20 19:10:27

Q Xiangle is a coupon and discount service based on Focus Media's interactive screens. The services it provides are similar to Weiluo Cheng, except that the感应machine has been transformed into Focus Media’s interactive screen. Users can obtain discount information via SMS using the Q Card provided by Q Xiangle when they see some advertisements and coupons on the screen. Currently, the Q Xiangle website has not officially launched yet; it only offers Q Card applications and feature introductions. However, in offline areas, all the old interactive TVs have been replaced with new versions of Focus Media's interactive TVs. Compared to the original interactive TVs, these new ones have an additional interactive advertising zone at the bottom, where users can directly obtain discounts or coupons related to the advertisements using their Q Card. They can also get a Q Card directly from the interactive TV.

To experience Q Xiangle's service, I specially applied for a Q Card for testing. The application process required filling out my name, address, and mobile phone number (which needed SMS activation). Soon after, I received the Q Cards, which were delivered quickly—the next day I got three Q Cards (I think this is a pretty good marketing method). Naturally, I gave the other two cards to my colleagues and introduced them to what it was about, successfully helping them become two additional users. The Q Card needs to be activated via SMS.

At the building where my company is located, I conducted a test by selecting an advertisement (a trial experience pack). After swiping the Q Card, I received an SMS asking me to reply with my mailing address, after which the experience pack would be confirmed and sent to me.

Watsonxu says: Previously, I've discussed many O2O models here with everyone. Now, there are many services doing O2O, but in my personal view, not many people truly understand the essence of O2O. From Focus Media's perspective, they have many interactive TVs. In the past, their value was merely to deliver advertisements to office building users, but how effective those advertisements were is hard to quantify, and it was difficult for users to interact directly with the ads. With Focus Media's interactive TVs now offering the Q Xiangle service, I believe it holds significant value for Focus Media: First, the conversion rate of advertisements can now be quantified; Second, more real user information can be collected through this method, whether it's for future mobile push advertisements or precise marketing via other means, it's very valuable; Third, Focus Media's advertisements, through interactive ads, form a complete loop between advertisements, interaction, and user behavior, making Focus Media's advertisements more attractive to local merchants. Therefore, in my opinion, Focus Media's Q Xiangle will be a successful O2O model.

From another angle, many offline services can reach users but lack interaction with them. So, whether it's about making offline services networked (like Opentable) or finding suitable ways to create interaction between these offline services and users—whether through Q Xiangle's method or QR codes—there are great opportunities. O2O needs to form a closed-loop circuit for services.
