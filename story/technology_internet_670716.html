[China Car Network] After the latest road test spy photos were exposed, BMW has finally officially released the official images of the 2012 M6 Coupe and Convertible. Both new models feature BMW's new family-style front face design, with multiple details in appearance and interior redesigned. They are equipped with a 4.4-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine. It is expected that the new car will be officially launched and sold in June this year.

The 2012 BMW M6 Coupe model features a new aluminum alloy wheel, and both the 2012 BMW M6 Coupe and convertible have the same distinctive appearance. The new styling also makes these two models appear more fashionable and sporty. This includes a new front bumper, wheel arch expansion, front ventilated fender, LED headlight group, redesigned rear spoiler and aerodynamic side skirts, as well as four exhaust pipes at the bottom of the rear and 19-inch aluminum alloy wheels. Additionally, the BMW M6 will offer optional 20-inch wheels. Chief designer Adrian van Hooydonk told the media that the badge on the grille of the M6 will become a cornerstone of M. If you like subtlety, it seems to increase the factor of showing off.

Meanwhile, the interior of the new car has been upgraded to appear more luxurious and majestic. Features include leather sports seats, new dashboard, carbon fiber trim strips, sill plates/interior environmental lighting, custom gear knob, multi-functional sports three-spoke steering wheel, and a unique gearshift lever. The steering wheel also has an electric heating function, and inside it features GPS navigation, Twitter infotainment system, Bang & Olufsen sound system. In addition, it offers optional tech configurations such as head-up display, high beam assistance, BMW night vision system with pedestrian recognition, lane change/deviation warning system, and rearview camera.

In terms of power, both new models are equipped with a 4.4-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine, delivering a maximum power output of 412 kW (552 hp) and a maximum torque of 680 Nm. They are also equipped with a 7-speed dual-clutch transmission. This allows the M6 Coupe to accelerate from 0 to 100 km/h in 4.2 seconds with fuel consumption of 9.9 liters per 100 km, while the convertible takes 4.3 seconds and consumes 10.3 liters per 100 km.

Under electronic speed limitation, the highest driving speed of these two new cars can reach 250 km/h, and under M high-performance tuning, the highest driving speed can be increased to 305 km/h. Additionally, both cars use a sport-tuned suspension system and offer optional high-performance brakes (carbon ceramic brake discs). How fast is the new BMW M6? It only takes 4.2 seconds to reach 62 mph and 12.6 seconds to reach 124 mph (200 kph). The top speed should be around 155 mph or 189 mph.
