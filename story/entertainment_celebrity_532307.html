Eason Joseph slams TVB on Weibo for forcing his girlfriend to share a room with 8 others; Wong Hee reveals his own miserable past experiences to substantiate the claim.

by mlwang on 2011-11-03 17:22:45

In a Weibo post, Joe Junior spoke out against the unfair treatment of his girlfriend Priscilla Chan and actress Ruby Zhu by TVB. During the filming of a program in Japan, not only were they required to share a room with male staff members, but they were also forced to drive, which resulted in a harrowing car accident. After the report was published, it caused quite a stir. Yesterday, the two female artists returned to Hong Kong for medical treatment, and TVB's senior executives, Louise Leung and Tsang Wing Ming, specially went to the airport to greet them and offer condolences.

However, TVB's high-profile gesture did not win Joe Junior's forgiveness. At 17:50 yesterday, he posted on Weibo a picture of Priscilla Chan and her group's "cramped" living quarters in an 8-person room in Yokohama, Japan, criticizing TVB for practicing formalism and calling for self-reflection.

"Crammed living" is not just a commoner's privilege; even stars live in 8-person rooms. It’s no wonder Joe Junior was so angry that his own girlfriend was forced to cohabit with male staff members. Joey Wong further fueled the fire. After this bombardment incident, TVB, known for producing stars, TV series, and the "TVB style," was pushed to the forefront of public attention, prompting timely public relations actions. What the situation will evolve into remains to be seen. Please stay tuned and patiently observe as events unfold.
