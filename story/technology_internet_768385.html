Lenovo's net profit in the second quarter was 2.2 billion US dollars, increasing by 36% compared to the last year

by anonymous on 2013-11-17 17:46:41

According to a report on November 7, Lenovo Group released its financial report for the second quarter of the 2013-2014 fiscal year today. By September 30, Lenovo's turnover in the second quarter reached $9.8 billion, an increase of 13% year-on-year. The net profit was $220 million, increasing by 36% year-on-year.

The financial report showed that Lenovo sold 29 million devices globally in the second quarter. For two consecutive quarters, Lenovo remained the world's largest personal computer supplier with a global market share of 17.7%, up 2% year-on-year, setting a new historical record.

In terms of personal computer business, the sales volume reached 14.1 million units, again becoming the fastest-growing company among the top five manufacturers, outperforming the overall industry growth for 18 consecutive quarters.

Lenovo's Mobile Internet and Digital Home (MIDH) products, including smartphones, smart TVs, and most of the group's tablet business, increased from 8% of total turnover last year to 15% this quarter, while it was only 4% two years ago, with steadily improving profitability.

Lenovo ranked second globally in the combined sales of personal computers and tablets, and is also the third largest intelligent terminal manufacturer globally (including smartphones, tablets, and personal computers), with a year-on-year increase of 36%. Meanwhile, Lenovo continued to be the fourth largest smartphone supplier globally, with a significant year-on-year growth of 78%.

Yang Yuanqing, Chairman and CEO of Lenovo Group, said that Lenovo has an optimistic attitude towards future market development. Driven by the corporate PC replacement wave and the warming of the Chinese market, the personal computer market will gradually recover. The shift of tablets to mainstream entry-level markets and emerging markets will also continue. These are all areas where Lenovo excels.
