Sina Weibo deals with 100,000 Weibo accounts that release illegal and unlawful information

by anonymous on 2013-11-17 19:26:11

Reporters learned from the Beijing-based United Rumor-refuting Platform today that recently, Sina Weibo has intensified its management and dealt with 103,673 accounts that posted illegal or rule-violating information. Among them, 1,030 accounts were for posting inaccurate information, 75,267 accounts were for personal attacks, 14,357 accounts were for harassment, 9,246 accounts were for impersonation, plagiarism, privacy disclosure, and other violations, and 3,773 accounts were closed for posting vulgar, obscene, and pornographic information.

According to the introduction, for disaster-related reports involving social public order information, Sina Weibo has adopted strict control measures to prevent false information from disrupting public order. During this year's floods in Fushun, Liaoning, and Shantou, Guangdong, after receiving reports from netizens, the Sina Weibo community quickly verified and promptly handled a batch of false information such as using news pictures from the 2008 Wenchuan earthquake and typhoons in the Philippines to impersonate disaster scenes and falsely reporting death tolls, and imposed penalties such as banning comments on users who posted the false information, maintaining rescue order.

According to the "Community Convention", infringement behaviors between users can be reported online. Currently, among personal user complaints received by Sina Weibo, the most common types are network personal attacks, privacy disclosures, and other infringing behaviors. In early November this year, Sina customer service received a complaint call from a user, claiming that due to offline emotional disputes, multiple users maliciously attacked her and her family on the Sina Weibo platform. After the Sina customer service reflected the user's needs to the Sina Weibo Community Convention Group, the staff of the convention group consoled the user and suggested that the user directly report the alleged personal attack violation content online. After the user reported the alleged personal attack violation content online, Sina Weibo quickly deleted the violation content, protecting the user's reputation rights.

In complaints from institutional users, the largest number is for impersonating institutional names. This year, relevant channels of CCTV reflected to Sina that some users impersonated their names and logos, causing confusion among ordinary netizens. After receiving the complaint, Sina Weibo quickly verified the matter and handled it in the first time, canceling the account of the reported user.

It is understood that the Sina Weibo "Community Convention" currently details the handling methods for various violations. For accounts posting inaccurate information, in addition to labeling related Weibos, different degrees of punishment will be given based on the number of forwards of the Weibo. If the Weibo is forwarded more than 100 times, the account will be banned from commenting for 7 days, banned from being followed for 7 days, and 5 credit points will be deducted; if the Weibo is forwarded more than 1000 times, the account will be banned from commenting for 15 days, banned from being followed for 15 days, and 10 credit points will be deducted; for serious cases or severe consequences, the heaviest penalty will be freezing the account. For reports on user disputes such as personal attacks, in addition to deleting infringing violation content and deducting credit points, serious cases will also impose penalties such as banning comments, banning being followed, and even freezing accounts on related violating accounts.
