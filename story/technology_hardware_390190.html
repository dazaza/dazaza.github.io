The pile of express slips shows that the online shop boss has a good business.

by anonymous on 2011-03-25 14:24:20

After returning home from working in the city at the beginning of this year, Shi Jiliang, who is over 30 years old, never left the city again. He and his wife opened two online stores at home. In just half a year, they easily earned 50,000 yuan.

"Before, as a worker, I could only save 20,000 yuan after a year of hard work, which can't be compared with now," Shi Jiliang scratched his head and smiled humbly.

In the village where he lived, originally two or three out of ten young people worked outside, but now almost no one goes out. "Everyone stays at home to open online stores and make money," said Wu Guoren, the Party Secretary of Yanji Town. In just one town, there are 1,500 households like Shi Jiliang's.

This is not an isolated phenomenon. Over the weekend, the reporter visited Yanji Town in Shuyang County, Jiangsu Province and the Shaji Town in Cuining County, Xuzhou City. The scenes witnessed were shocking: farmers holding their children skillfully typing on computers, express delivery personnel shuttling back and forth on the village roads... In just these two towns, more than 3,000 farming households are "panning for gold" on the Internet.

A statistical data can better illustrate the point: 90% of furniture sellers on Taobao.com come from Shaji, and 80% of flower sellers come from Yanji.

Three young men changed a town

Xia Kai did not expect that an accidental business trip to Nanjing would change the entire industrial structure of Shaji, and even bring the whole village and town onto the Internet.

In 2005, middle school art teacher Xia Kai was sitting on a bus in Nanjing when he suddenly noticed Taobao advertisements everywhere on the street.

"After I returned home and searched online, I found out that it was a website where you could open a store and sell things," Xia Kai told reporters. With a try-it-out attitude, he put some small items online for sale.

Business was not good, but this quickly made him decide: use his own advantages to do something special.

What to do? The art teacher thought of simple furniture, after all, he had some design skills. Once decided, he and his two friends Sun Han and Chen Lei immediately embarked on this completely new field. At that time, Sun Han had just quit his customer manager position at the local mobile company, which was a high-paying job with a monthly salary of 3,000 yuan in the town!

The direction was set, but it wasn't easy to implement.

No processing plant. "In 2006, we wanted to process wooden strips, but no one was willing to do it," recalled Xia Kai. They looked for furniture factories in the village and the town, even went to factories that produced urns, but still, none were willing to produce the extremely simple wooden strips: cutting wood into specified sizes, then chiseling a few indentations.

When they were disheartened, a relative of Chen Lei finally agreed to help them try making a batch. "We immediately ordered several kilometers of wooden strips to make simple furniture," said Xia Kai.

Express delivery became a problem. Three years ago, there was only one express delivery company in Shaji Town, and the price was quite high: 15 yuan for anything under 10 kilograms. "Shipping was painful." Xia Kai started in such an environment.

At first, they could only earn a few thousand yuan a month, but later Xia Kai's business got better and better: 10,000 yuan, 20,000 yuan, 30,000 yuan, 100,000 yuan, 200,000 yuan... Now, Xia Kai, who is still a middle school art teacher earning a monthly salary of 1,000 yuan, makes an annual profit of 200,000 yuan from his online store; while Sun Han, who has fully committed himself to the website, reportedly earns more than 2 million yuan annually, with a growing business, he has hired three customer service representatives to take care of customers, and opened two wood processing plants.

3,000 farmers flocking online to "pan for gold"

Xia Kai's wealth effect made Dongfeng Village in Shaji Town, originally known for collecting scrap, see a new way to make money.

It is common to see such scenes in the village: in small houses, a bed and a computer are placed. The owner sits on the bed, using hands that have just finished farm work to type on the keyboard, responding to questions from distant buyers.

"I brought four or five relatives along, and they each brought another four or five people," described Xia Kai about this chain reaction.

At first, some older villagers thought this was neglecting their main business, "Can you really make money just by surfing the web?" But soon they were convinced by the facts: their sons followed Chen Lei and others to open online stores, and within two or three months, they brought back more than 10,000 yuan, and the amount kept increasing.

The number of online stores in Shaji increased geometrically: there were only more than 10 in 2007, reaching 100 by the beginning of last year, and already 1,200 by the start of this year.

Walking around Shaji Town randomly, no matter if the front stores sold stationery, fruit, or did wedding photography, there were always one or two computers placed in the back rooms, and the owners were selling simple furniture online.

"They only need to take care of the online business, and once they get an order, they go pick up goods from nearby furniture factories," said Sun Han. Even so, one can earn around 3,000 yuan a month.

There are also many such stories in Yanji Town. In Yaxia Village beside the town, Zhou Yun, over 30 years old, is a doctor in the morning, but becomes an online store owner in the afternoon. On his desk, there are three computers. "My wife and I handle everything together," said Zhou Yun, who has been a user of Alibaba since 2004, which was absolutely novel for the local villagers who mainly relied on flowers and trees for living at that time.

Zhou Yun's online store ran very smoothly, with an annual net income of 200,000 yuan before. Even now with more competition, he can still earn 150,000 yuan a year.

This result is not the highest. The reporter met a villager named Li Min, who revealed that this year the estimated net profit online could reach 500,000 yuan, "Next year, I aim for 1.5 million yuan." In the village, most people can use Taobao, Alibaba, Baidu, Tencent Paipai proficiently.

Opening online stores has also significantly improved the living standards of these villagers compared to other villages. "Just count the number of cars in the village, and you'll know the difference," said a villager. It is said that in just Yaxia Village, there are more than 100 cars worth over 200,000 yuan, and the most expensive one is a BMW 750 worth 1.5 million yuan.

More and more online stores drive the industrial chain

The villagers who took the lead in going online seem to never refuse the inquiries of those around them.

"As long as they are willing to learn, we will teach them," Xia Kai and others told reporters. In their minds, only when everyone around does it can an industry truly grow, all raw material costs become lower, and purchasing goods become more convenient.

His foresight has become reality. A complete industrial chain is forming in Shaji.

"Before, in Shaji, no one wanted wood," said Xia Kai. In just a few years, local wood supplies are far from enough, and almost every week, several large truckloads of wood materials are transported from elsewhere.

Furniture factories have increased. When Xia Kai and others started, it was difficult to find a furniture factory, but now, it is the furniture factories that are seeking them. Xia Kai casually counted, and there are dozens of furniture factories in the town, and it is rumored that even the former coal briquette factory has switched to making furniture.

Woodworkers have returned. Local woodworkers used to have to travel far away to find small jobs, but now, even 80 yuan per day is considered too low.

The express delivery services have also become more evident. The days of having only one express delivery service three years ago are gone forever. The small Shaji Town now has more than ten express delivery services. EMS moved in at the end of 2006, changing its high-price face in other places, setting the price for anything under 15 kilograms at 10 yuan to compete with other express delivery companies. Xia Kai, who also runs an express delivery company, revealed that EMS currently generates between 150,000 to 300,000 yuan per month, and others generate around 30,000 yuan, "Shaji pays express delivery companies nearly 6 million yuan a year."

The fierce competition among express delivery services has also continuously reduced logistics costs. It is understood that this year, the starting price of almost all express deliveries has dropped to 5 yuan, and EMS is only 6 yuan, which is unimaginable in the Pearl River Delta region.
