Tutorial for Flashing CyanogenMod6 ROM on Android System Phones (Part 1)

by dream2012 on 2012-02-16 10:41:23

We will use the HTC Desire smartphone as an example to introduce a tutorial on flashing the CyanogenMod6 ROM for Android phones. The process for other smartphones supported by CyanogenMod is similar, and you can refer to this method:

### Beginner's Guide to Flashing CyanogenMod6 ROM

1. **Obtain Root Access**: First, you need to root your phone. There are plenty of methods available online to root your device. For Chinese users, the phone they buy is often already rooted by the seller and has a third-party ROM installed, so they can skip directly to step 2. If your phone is not yet rooted, it is recommended to use the Unrevoked one-click root tool.

2. **Install Clockwork Recovery**: If you used the Unrevoked one-click root tool to obtain root access, you already have the Clockwork Recovery program installed, so you can ignore this step. Otherwise, you need to search for and install the ROM Manager app from the Android Market. Through the ROM Manager app, you can install Clockwork Recovery (when you click "Install ROM from SD Card" in the ROM Manager app, it will automatically prompt you to download and install Clockwork Recovery).

3. **Backup System Settings and Apps**: Enter recovery mode via ROM Manager, then choose Nandroid backup to back up your system settings and apps (this step is optional; if there’s nothing important on your phone, you can skip it. Skipping this won't affect the data on your SD card). To perform a Nandroid backup, turn off your phone, press the volume down button and power button simultaneously to enter the ClockworkMod Recovery program, then select Nandroid Backup.

4. **Download the New Radio Program for Android 2.2**: Download the latest radio file and place it on your phone's SD card. Use the ROM Manager app to select "Reboot into Recovery" to enter the ClockworkMod Recovery mode, then choose "install zip from sdcard". Select the radio-5.09.05.30_2.zip file on your SD card, confirm with the power button, and after completion, you will have successfully flashed the latest radio file.

5. **Perform a WIPE Operation**: Stay in Recovery mode and execute a WIPE operation once.

6. **Download and Flash CyanogenMod6 ROM**: Download the CyanogenMod6 ROM file and place it on your SD card. Again, use the ROM Manager app to select "Reboot into Recovery" to enter the ClockworkMod Recovery mode, then choose "install zip from sdcard". Select the update-cm-6.0.1-Desire-signed.zip ROM file on your SD card, confirm with the power button, and wait a few minutes. After the process is complete, your phone will automatically reboot into the system, meaning that your phone is now running CyanogenMod6 ROM.

7. **Install Google Applications**: After entering the system, you'll notice that CyanogenMod6 ROM does not include Google applications such as gtalk or gmail. This is due to legal disputes between CyanogenMod and Google. You need to install the Google application package separately. Download the HDPI-20100917 addon file, place it on your phone's SD card, then follow the previous steps to enter recovery mode. Select "install zip from sdcard", choose the gapps-hdpi-20100917-signed.zip file, confirm with the power button to start flashing the Google application package. After completing this process, reboot your phone and you'll find all the Google programs included in the original Android system have returned.
