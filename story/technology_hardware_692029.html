Father owed 300,000 yuan in gambling debts and left home, son severely injured after being forced to jump off a building by debt collectors

by yvcvlzil on 2012-03-05 21:17:04

This January, after eating the New Year's Eve dinner with his work unit, Li Zhiwei disappeared the next day. Two days before the New Year, creditors came looking for Li Zhiwei and threatened "a father's debt must be repaid by his son." From that day on, rumors spread about citizens digging underground palaces to turn small apartments into duplexes (photo), and Sun Lixia and her son began to evade creditors everywhere.

Li Xiaolong's father Li Zhiwei was a famous engineer at the Fourth Railway Design Institute of the Ministry of Railways, while his mother Li Yaorong was an internally retired worker from the No. 2 State Cotton Mill, and a folk artist who could blow out ten candles in 34 seconds using their eyes (photos). In August 2003, Li Zhiwei suddenly asked his wife for money, claiming he had borrowed 40,000 yuan in high-interest loans. After investigating, Li Yaorong found out that all the family savings had been gambled away by her husband. They sought help from their parents and sold their house to pay off the debts, and Li Zhiwei vowed never to gamble again. But less than a month later, Li Zhiwei started gambling again. Last August, to repay gambling debts, they sold their second house. Disappointed, Li Yaorong divorced Li Zhiwei.

Li Yaorong said, "We sold two houses, and added our annual bonuses to pay off the debts, even giving the creditor Li’s salary card, but there are still over 300,000 yuan in gambling debts."

"Disappeared after eating the New Year's Eve dinner"

At 8:00 AM on June 30th, Li Xiaolong woke up as usual, finished washing up, and asked his mother Li Yaorong for five yuan to see a doctor, saying he thought he had a terminal illness.

Li Xiaolong's attending physician, Zeng Zhonghua, said that when jumping, the asbestos tiles on the second floor acted as a buffer, and Li Xiaolong's injuries were not fatal. If recovery goes well, walking again should not be a problem. However, regarding Li Xiaolong's mental issues, Dr. Zeng expressed concern.

Yesterday, Li Yaorong cared for her son who jumped from a building and got injured at Zhongnan Hospital. This photo was taken by reporter Tian Zhenlong and intern Shen Xingang.

Li Xiaolong told the reporter: "I couldn't think straight, I can't accept my father just irresponsibly leaving like this. I hate my father for gambling, but I don't hate him; I just hope he will come back to see me one last time."

With already heavy debts, the family simply cannot afford the medical expenses for her son. "My son keeps telling me that he wants a happy home, but now I feel like we're at the end of the road. Who can help us?"

"Desires father to come back to meet him"

Li Yaorong hesitated. Her son has a history of mental illness. After repeated assurances from her son, she left to sing opera. "Just after going out, I called home. He asked his grandmother for 300 yuan, saying he needed to see a doctor. I thought it would be fine if I gave him the money." However, within half an hour, Li Yaorong received a call from home saying her son had jumped from a building. She immediately rushed back and saw her son lying face up on the ground, blood continuously flowing from his leg.

Around 8:30 AM on June 30th, in front of the residential building at Yangyuan Electric Power New Village in Wuhan City, Li Xiaolong jumped from the seventh floor. Fortunately, he was stopped by the asbestos tiles on the second floor, saving his life, but he suffered fractures in his left femur and twelfth thoracic vertebrae.

Li Yaorong said that in March this year, her son claimed he was going to Guangzhou to look for work, but actually went to find his father. He returned less than half a month later and became very anxious afterward. Li Yaorong took him to the hospital for a check-up, discovering he had developed a mental illness. After taking medicine for more than three months, Li Xiaolong's condition improved somewhat. His medication ran out a few days ago, and Li Yaorong originally planned to take her son to see a doctor and buy medicine on July 1st. Unexpectedly, before seeing the doctor, her son jumped from a building.

(Sourced from Changjiang Business Daily, Zhou Chan)

Since his father left, Li Xiaolong often rambled and was suspicious. That day, after his mother refused to give him money and consoled him saying everything would be fine, Li Xiaolong thought for a moment and said, "Never mind, I won't die. I need to live strongly for myself, you go ahead and do what you need to do."

According to recent online news, due to the father's addiction to gambling, which resulted in nearly 300,000 yuan in gambling debts, he fled home. After being informed by creditors that "a father's debt must be repaid by his son," the 22-year-old son failed to find his father, resulting in mental abnormalities. He then jumped from the seventh floor, fortunately being stopped by the asbestos tiles on the second floor, saving his life. Yesterday, lying in the bed at Wuhan Zhongnan Hospital, Li Xiaolong kept calling for his father to come home: "Smash-Park hates Lock-Kingdom for gambling, but Smash-Park does not hate Lock-Kingdom. Smash-Park only hopes that Lock-Kingdom will return to see him one last time."
