In 2011, new achievements of universities "enclosed electric vehicles" provincial leaders reminded to dare to use the concept of "borrowing". The opening of the enclosed electric vehicle minibus technology exhibition was a wonderful highlight. Do you still remember the stunning appearance of the large-faced Hunan-produced enclosed electric vehicle at last year's science and technology exhibition? This year, its improved version has arrived - a 20-seat enclosed electric vehicle that is already ready for industrial production. Six of these vehicles are currently in use at the foot of Mount Yuelu.

Lightweight! -- The enclosed electric vehicle minibus weighing only 1.8 tons uses aerospace aluminum materials for its body. Normally, ordinary minibuses weigh about five tons. However, this enclosed electric vehicle minibus uses aerospace aluminum materials for its body, and the aluminum itself has a special shape that can interlock with each other, plus welded reinforcement, so its weight is only 1.8 tons, one-third of an ordinary minibus.

"So now it can only be applied regionally, as an electric sightseeing vehicle," said the director of the Research Institute of Changsha University in Hunan Province, introducing that the electric patrol vehicles and electric sightseeing vehicles currently developed can only be driven within certain areas.

At the science and technology exhibition opened later, Chenggang, accompanied by a group of leaders including Yuanxian, first visited the venue. As an automotive engineer by training, Chenggang, upon seeing several enclosed electric vehicle minibuses exhibited by the Research Institute of Changsha University in Hunan Province, kindly inquired about the current speed and other related information of the car. Upon learning that the vehicle had undergone lightweight treatment using aviation aluminum alloy, he repeatedly reminded the developers of new energy vehicles, particularly "enclosed electric vehicles," to pay attention to safety performance.
