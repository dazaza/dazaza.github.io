Ultraviolet rays are the culprits of aging, and post-sun exposure skin changes occur in four stages.

Stage 1: Immediately after sun exposure - Redness, heat, and stinging

If your cheeks turn red from the sun, the main reason is that UVB causes the capillaries to dilate and triggers inflammation. If it hurts slightly when touched, it indicates that the skin's surface has slight burns!

Once you feel redness and heat, use this immediately to calm it down! High temperatures will activate melanin pigments, making you darker faster! So, once the skin feels hot, quickly retreat to a cool indoor area and immediately lower the temperature with cold water or a hydrating spray! Minerals like silicates have soothing effects, so spray it as soon as you feel heat, not only suppressing inflammation but also preventing melanin from forming!

Stage 2: The day after sun exposure - Dull complexion

If the inflammation isn't suppressed in time, the epidermis will start to harden and thicken, causing the skin to lose its transparency! Plus, UVA increases melanin pigment significantly, resulting in uneven skin tone across the face and forming sunspots quietly.

Key within 6 hours - Post-sun repair cream

Apply a thick layer of repair cream to calm down the deep melanin surge! Ultraviolet radiation has an alarming accumulation effect; even though you're indoors, the ultraviolet rays continue to cause inflammation! Regardless of whether the surface redness and heat have lessened, apply aloe vera gel or a post-sun repair cream containing licorice extract, oats, etc., within 6 hours to soothe deep inflammatory tissues.

Stage 3: 2-3 days after sun exposure - Dryness and flaking

The thickened epidermal cells die due to inflammation, and around the 2nd to 3rd day, new keratinocytes begin to grow. At this point, the whole face starts peeling, and the skin becomes less receptive to makeup.

Key within 24 hours - Hydrating mask

Gel-type freeze masks offer excellent breathability and heat dissipation. After sun exposure, the skin loses water, and using a mask is a quick way to hydrate. However, sheet masks can easily suffocate the skin, causing contact dermatitis! It's recommended to choose gel-type freeze masks without oils, or use a hydrating gel cream as a night mask, which offers better breathability and heat dissipation than regular moisturizing lotions or creams.

Stage 4: One month after sun exposure - Fine lines appear

When the skin is in a very fragile state of peeling, moisture evaporates more easily. If you don't strengthen hydration and lock in moisture, fine lines will gradually appear within a month, making you look much older!

Key within 3 Days - Elasticity essence
