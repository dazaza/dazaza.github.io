How to Play Warrior PK Well

by lk0gamei on 2009-07-18 23:34:20

There are advantages between professions, but skills can counteract each other. There is no strongest profession, and there is no strongest skill. What exists is only the gradual understanding and reasonable use of them, bringing out the strengths of one's own profession, suppressing the opponent's strength, and trying to compare our strengths with the opponent's weaknesses.

Many people say that warriors are weak, and this is largely because warriors require extremely high operational skills. Everyone knows that warriors can only bring out their maximum power when they get close to the enemy. High-level players play by countering each move, ultimately bringing out their strengths. The warrior's advantage is close combat, and only by bringing the battle into close combat can the warrior bring out its advantages. I believe no profession would dare to let the warrior fight at close range for output, because everyone knows that the warrior's burst power at close range is very strong. Close-range combat determines that moves must be fast and reactions must be quick. Therefore, I said that warriors require extremely high operational skills.

Hunters have Dispel + Feign Death + Trap, a universal skill that breaks all moves and creates distance, allowing them to play their specialty of ranged attacks.

Mages have Frost Nova, Blink, and Polymorph, which create distance and prevent warriors from utilizing their specialties. Frostbolt and Fireball are the main killing tricks of mages.

Rogues have various stunning skills that prevent warriors from bringing out their attacks even when they get close. Backstab is the main means of dagger rogues.

Warlocks have Fear and Death Coil that prevent warriors from entering close combat. Then they use various chanted skills to attack, of course, there are also instant Pain spells and various big skills relying on shards.

Priests also rely on fear to prevent warriors from getting close. Appropriately timing shields and healing is the priest's expertise.

Druids, Shamans, and Paladins are jack-of-all-trades professions, so I won't talk about them here. It depends on luck when fighting.

Bringing out the advantages of your own profession and inhibiting the advantages of other professions is the key to victory.

VS Hunter: What do we warriors fear? Dispel + Feign Death + Trap, this is a universal skill, almost unsolvable except for using Cold Reflection and Mocking Banner to lure the hunter's pet. Hunters have strong DPS at a distance, so holding a shield is a good choice when we can't get close, it can reduce unnecessary damage to the greatest extent. When you can't catch up, use grenades, Gnome nets, and Intimidating Shout. With certain skills and opportunities, you can use them successfully. Although hunters have a 41-yard range, what if you don't chase him but retreat instead? He will chase you, once he chases you, you turn around and kill back. Due to delay and reaction reasons, he will enter the range of your grenades, Gnome nets, and Intimidating Shout. Coercion? You are immune after activating Berserker Rage. Intercept appropriately, try to output as much as possible after getting close, this is your hope to beat the hunter. The battlefield is changeable, each person's habits and playing styles are different, but the skills are the same. Learning to counter moves gives you hope for victory. If you only know how to intercept, Die or Bloodlust, what else could happen besides death?

VS Mage: You must stick to the mage and bring out your burst power. The three moves that mages use to maintain distance are Ice, Blink, and Sheep. Ice can be countered with Cold Reflection and badges. Cold Reflection requires good awareness to predict the mage's skills. Immediately intercept after Blink. Sheep? Shield Bash, Jab, Intercept, and Grenade can all interrupt casting. Keep a slowing effect on the mage, Deafening and Broken Shin must be present. A well-operated mage can indeed make an average warrior unable to touch the body, but a well-operated warrior can also stick to an average mage. Dual wielding is recommended, whether weapon warrior or berserker, the higher the attack frequency, the higher the chance of interruption. It helps interrupt spells and reduce the damage received.

VS Rogue: Berserker Rage immunity to rogue stuns, I won't talk about that, everyone knows. How much suppression damage there is, how high the chance of dodge appears when fighting a rogue, I won't talk about that either, everyone in the world knows.

VS Warlock: Warlocks are now called the BUG class by everyone, why? Because warlocks have almost no weaknesses, high damage, high health. How do we defeat him? Key point 1. Don't be feared, once feared, not only can you not hit the warlock, but you will continue to take damage. Of course, the damage from current warlocks is terrifying, even without fear, there's a good chance we'll die. But as long as he cannot fear and open on us warriors, we have a chance to defeat him. Interrupt skills (Jab, Shield Bash, Grenade, Intimidating) watch for the warlock to release fear and immediately use Berserker Rage to remove the fear effect once feared. Just like fighting a mage, sticking to the warlock gives you hope for victory. Dual wielding is also recommended, whether weapon warrior or berserker, the higher the attack frequency, the higher the chance of interruption. It helps interrupt spells and reduce the damage received.
