There are many traditional festivals in China, such as the Spring Festival, Lantern Festival, Mid-Autumn Festival, Dragon Boat Festival, Chongyang Festival, etc. Among them, my favorite should be the Spring Festival. The Spring Festival is the most bustling festival in China. On this day, people decorate with lanterns and ribbons, wishing each other good luck for the coming year. I am a girl who loves beauty, so on this day, I can wear beautiful clothes that I have been looking forward to for a long time, then visit relatives and friends with my family. Putting up couplets is something every household does, and pasting the big "Fu" character upside down symbolizes that blessings have arrived. In the evening, there are fireworks and colorful lanterns everywhere, making my heart feel bright and cheerful. There is also the large CCTV Spring Festival Gala, where the whole family gathers around the TV, eating sunflower seeds, and enjoying wonderful performances together in harmony.

The Spring Festival is also called celebrating the New Year. After the New Year, one grows a year older. With a new year comes new气象, everyone looks forward to better omens in the coming year. Also, I like the Mid-Autumn Festival. I am a gluttonous girl, and all kinds of mooncakes always make me drool. In the evening, a big, round moon hangs in the sky, and sitting under it with my family, tasting mooncakes, and talking about the never-tiring story of Chang'e flying to the moon is truly a blissful thing!

My relationship with sports: Everyone excels in various sports, and I am no exception. Throwing shot put and skipping rope are my strengths, while running and long jump are my weak points. I can play various ball games such as basketball, volleyball, and badminton.

After entering university, sports are no longer just simple exercises. I have learned skillful projects like Tai Chi and Tai Chi sword. The most rewarding has to be yoga. Initially, I learned about yoga from TV, and later my good friend learned yoga at a fitness club during the summer vacation, sparking my strong interest in yoga. In my sophomore year's physical education class, I signed up for a yoga class and started practicing yoga with Teacher Xu. At first, I was not very adaptable because stretching muscles and bones caused muscle soreness after each class. However, gradually, I became accustomed to it and could follow Teacher Xu to complete every set of movements. By the end of the semester, my yoga skills had significantly improved. In the second semester, I continued learning unfinished actions from the teacher and began studying yoga theory knowledge. I realized that yoga originated from ancient India, and it is a mind-body cultivating exercise. Not only does it strengthen the body, aid in weight loss, but it also purifies the mind and relieves heavy mental stress. Additionally, practicing yoga massages internal organs, regulates endocrine, and to some extent, can treat certain diseases such as constipation, indigestion, sciatica, etc. Anyone can practice yoga, except some patients may not be suitable for certain moves. Before practicing yoga, prepare a yoga mat and wear loose clothing. Some yoga poses do not need to reach the same standard as the instructor; as long as you reach your maximum ability, it is fine. When practicing yoga, follow the clear and gentle yoga music, perform each movement slowly, hold the position for a certain period after completing it. A single workout session generally lasts about an hour. Yoga is a tranquil sport, so maintain a peaceful mindset while practicing. After a year of diligent practice, the teacher said I was the best in the class because I practiced quietly, fully immersing my mind and body, possessing both strength and flexibility. Encouraged by the teacher's affirmation, I decided to continue practicing in the future. I love yoga because it is indeed a great sport.

25. Talk about personal cultivation

Humility is a virtue, a precious quality that is hard to come by.

Since ancient times, our people have possessed the virtue of humility, with many proverbs inspiring future generations. For example, "Modesty brings benefits, arrogance invites damage," "Humility makes one progress, pride makes one fall behind," "A modest bamboo bows its head, a proud plum has no upward-facing flowers," "Even at the top of a hundred-foot pole, one must strive further!" Indeed, this is true. No one has the capital to be arrogant because even if someone has profound expertise in a particular field, they cannot claim to have thoroughly mastered or completely studied everything. "Life is limited, knowledge is infinite." Any discipline is an endless ocean, an unbounded sky... So, no one can think they have reached the highest level and stop advancing, becoming conceited. If one does so, they will soon be caught up by peers and surpassed by successors.

Albert Einstein was one of the greatest scientists of the 20th century. His theory of relativity and his other research achievements in physics left us with an inexhaustible wealth. Yet, even someone like him continued learning and researching throughout his life, living and learning until old age.

Someone asked Einstein, "You sir are unprecedented in the field of physics, why bother to learn tirelessly? Why not rest comfortably?" Einstein did not answer immediately but instead took out a pen and paper, drawing a large circle and a small circle, saying to the young man, "In the current situation, within the field of physics, I might know a little more than you. As you see is this small circle, what I know is this large circle, yet the entire body of physics knowledge is boundless. For the small circle, its circumference is small, meaning it has less contact with the unknown field, thus feeling less unknown; whereas the larger circle has a greater circumference, hence feeling more unknown and striving harder to explore. Ah! What a wonderful metaphor, how profound an explanation!"

Each of us should cultivate a humble heart, possess a spirit of "modesty and prudence, guarding against arrogance and impatience." Let's use our finite lifetimes to seek more knowledge!

The place I yearn for

I stopped lurking on the Xinjiang forum for a few days and read everyone's writings about Tianshan Mountain, which made me feel both strange and familiar. Many people here are from Xinjiang, where Tianshan is your hometown. As a person from Hunan, perhaps I don't have the familiarity with Tianshan that you all do, but I can understand the deep love and longing you all have for your hometown...

Tianshan has always been a beautiful and mysterious place in my heart. When I was a child, watching martial arts dramas, I often heard the term "Tianshan Snow Lotus," explained as an antidote, and many martial arts experts would fight over it. At that time, I thought this flower was very mysterious. As I grew older, I often saw pictures of Tianshan in books, finding it truly beautiful, breathtakingly so. Tianshan's glaciers are famous; Qing dynasty scholar Xu Song vividly described the Muzart Glacier in his book "Xiyu Waterways," saying, "This ridge is a hundred miles long, over a hundred zhang tall, solid ice formed into layer upon layer of peaks, shining brightly. The ice has three colors: one is light green, one white like crystal, another white like chank shell." The description of Tianshan's ice having three colors, though unseen with my own eyes, already filled me with great longing...

This year, the TV series adapted from Liang Yusheng's novel "Seven Swords Descending from Tianshan" aired, filmed on location at the foot of Tianshan. Although I didn't see the dreamlike Tianchi (Heavenly Lake), watching the entire series gave me a sense of another kind of charm, making me feel the allure of Tianshan even more.

Perhaps because Tianshan is far away, I have never personally experienced its charm, and what I wrote is not as profound as yours. But as an outsider, Tianshan is truly a place I have always dreamed of going to... In the future, when I have time, I will definitely go to this beautiful, mysterious, and longed-for place to experience its unique scenery!!

Mandarin - My Favorite Literary Art Form

My favorite literary art form is novels. Novels are rich in content, with vivid characters and gripping stories. To say that I like novels, it can start from when I was a child. Back then, although I hadn't seriously read any novels, I already knew some names and began developing a great interest in them. By middle school, I slowly started reading some novels, from "Journey to the West" to "Romance of the Three Kingdoms," from "Hundred-Grass Garden" to "Three-Taste Study House." Although I didn't read much, I already felt that it would become a part of my academic life. It was only due to the pressure of entrance exams that I restrained myself.

In university, thanks to the extensive collection in the school library, I began avidly reading novels, whether Chinese classics or foreign masterpieces. As long as I found them interesting, I would borrow and read them. Among these were Qian Zhongshu's "Fortress Besieged," Wang Shifu's "Romance of the Western Chamber," Lao She's "Camel Xiangzi," Alexandre Dumas fils' "La Dame aux Camélias," Stendhal's "The Red and the Black," Tolstoy's "Resurrection," and so on. Besides these, if I had to choose my favorites, they would be Ba Jin's "Trilogy of Turbulent Streams": "Family," "Spring," "Autumn." It depicts the painful lives of youth in a feudal family constrained by feudal rites, reflecting their spirit of rebellion. They broke free from the shackles of old rites to pursue a free ideal life and free marriage. Among foreign classics, my favorite is Colombian writer Gabriel García Márquez's "One Hundred Years of Solitude." It is said the author spent eighteen years preparing this novel. Those who have read it know this novel can be compared to China's "Dream of the Red Chamber." Its writing style is unconventional, as it writes about events spanning over a hundred years simultaneously, almost condensing time and space together, leaving a lasting impression. I hope everyone finds time to read it.

30. Experience of Consumption

I have always believed that the quality of a product determines its value. Good products let us consume with confidence. A product of good quality makes us feel satisfied when we buy it and secure when we use it, thereby realizing its value. Today, while people pursue quality of life, they also pay attention to the quality and utility of products, as commonly said: "Good things, everyone wants!"

But as the demand for products in the market increases, some merchants, full of business strategies, have "cut corners" during production, leading to products on the market that consumers cannot distinguish as genuine or fake. The quality of a product can affect the normal order of consumers' lives and their physical and mental health. Therefore, a nationwide "anti-counterfeiting" campaign has emerged, giving merchants a chilling breeze. Speaking of this, I can't help but recall an incident.

Last winter, my mother and I bought a water heater at the market. We chose a "Red Cherry" brand. The seller enthusiastically introduced its manufacturer and partner companies, convincing us. He said he sold three or four units that day, guaranteeing the quality of the water heater and offering a one-year warranty. We trusted the seller and purchased it for 563 yuan. In the first few days, using it went smoothly, but on the eighth day, it wouldn't ignite. Our whole family fiddled with it for half a day but couldn't find the problem. We disassembled it and took it back to the seller for warranty service. Though it was repaired, once we brought it home, it kept malfunctioning intermittently. Our whole family doubted the quality of the water heater, guessing it would turn into junk before a year passed.

I believe, in many households, similar situations must exist. How can we buy quality products? We need discerning eyes. But most importantly, we hope merchants produce quality goods, letting us consume with satisfaction and confidence!

The collective I belong to

The class collective I belong to is a vibrant, united, helpful, warm, and happy large family. Most of our classmates come from rural areas, with the same attire, the same simplicity, and the same countryside customs, allowing us to live, study, and get along harmoniously without distinctions of high or low status, only equality, mutual assistance, and friendship.

Our class collective is united. Every semester, the school conducts sports competitions divided by grade level, including basketball, volleyball, football, badminton, etc. Regardless of which competition, as long as our class participates, you'll see male and female classmates cheering on the sidelines as cheerleaders. Whenever players come out to rest, classmates immediately hand them a bottle of mineral water and a towel to wipe sweat. Because of the unity of off-field classmates, the players on the field are encouraged, and each time, our class's male and female teams win awards. The male team has won the basketball championship multiple times. Of course, winning depends largely on the players' skills, but if they weren't united, if there were conflicts among the players and they didn't cooperate with each other, could they achieve victory? Thus, the united power of the class is immense, and the unity and friendship of our class is a guarantee for each victory. The united, harmonious, and loving class atmosphere makes every student feel secure and warm. If a student encounters a problem they can't solve themselves, their first thought is to turn to the class collective, seeking help from classmates to solve it together. If a student faces difficulties, the first ones to extend helping hands are our own classmates. If a student's grades fall behind, classmates organize efforts to help them catch up. In short, our class is a vibrant, united, mutually loving, mutually assisting, warm, and happy large family. I love our big collective.

Discussing Food

As the saying goes: food is the paramount necessity of the people. Regarding cuisine, I haven't done special research, so I can only talk loosely. Now, I estimate I probably can't become a chef, but I have some confidence in being a gourmet. Normally, even though I eat just a few dishes, I've still tasted their flavors and pleasures.

Chinese snacks are abundant in every alley. Just knowing a few, I feel they're no worse than McDonald's. Perhaps it's because we consider them mere food rather than culture to promote, so their prices are much cheaper. For instance, Chinese people like drinking tofu pudding. Tofu pudding has simple ingredients, is crispy and fragrant, leaves a lingering taste, and is extremely reasonably priced. I remember when I was a child, I would go to school half an hour early most of the time just to enjoy a bowl of tofu pudding leisurely at the foot of the city wall. At the time, it felt like drinking Pearl Emerald Jade Soup, thinking it was one of the rare delicacies in the world. Even now, during lunchtime, I sometimes think about eating tofu pudding. Talking about Chinese cuisine, I must mention Chinese dishes. My favorite dish is scallion-mixed tofu. You might laugh, but such a side dish is worth mentioning. I believe the simpler the dish, the more flavorful and enjoyable it is. This is like how people should be: the clearer and whiter, the more upright and dignified. I don't appreciate today's so-called master chefs who use extremely expensive main ingredients to create rare delicacies. I believe discovering extraordinary qualities in the ordinary shows true difficulty and skill. Having said that, I actually know nothing about culinary arts. As for cooking methods, don't ask me. In summary, I think Chinese cuisine is profound and vast. If you want to truly experience it, besides being a gourmet, you should also be a chef. If you feel you have the time, you can fully engage in promoting Chinese cuisine, allowing it to flourish worldwide like McDonald's.

My Growth Path

My growth path has been smooth but not without obstacles, calm but with waves, filled with joy and sorrow. Along the way, it has all become sweet memories.

When I was a child, I used to cry a lot, and my mom had to carry me wherever she went, otherwise, I would burst into tears. Of course, these are things my mom told me later; I don't remember them. Growing up a bit, I entered kindergarten, began crying less, and maintained good academic performance all the way through junior high. In the class, I was a notable class officer, and at home, I was a source of pride for my parents. However, I always made mistakes during major exams. My essays were usually good, and the teacher often read them as examples in class. Moreover, I frequently published articles in newspapers. But during the exam transitioning from elementary to junior high, I went off-topic in my essay simply because I misread the question. Fortunately, I performed exceptionally well in the other subjects, so I managed to pass with a scare.

On my growth path, many funny incidents, big and small, have occurred. When I was young, I had short hair because my mom thought girls shouldn't be too vain and should focus on studying. Also, since I didn't know how to braid my own hair and my mom was too lazy to help me, my hair was always as short as boys'. I tried resisting, but ultimately failed. However, girls are naturally fond of beauty. When I was little, I often wore scarves on my head and draped bed sheets around my body, pretending to be a fairy. One time, my dad happened to be holding a camera and captured this moment, so there's always a silly picture of a descending fairy in our photo album.

Additionally, I fell frequently when I was young. Each time I came home after playing outside, I looked like I had just fought a guerrilla war on the plains, covered in bruises. When I was three, I even fell from the third-floor balcony. My mom told me I landed in the gap between six pots of cacti in the courtyard below. Thanks to divine protection, I wasn't turned into a porcupine. Then, I was hospitalized. Afterwards, I often thought: if I fell from the third floor at three years old, I should fall from the fourth floor at four years old, and so on, until when I'm eighty, I should climb to the top of the World Trade Center and jump off.

The road of growth is filled with laughter and bitterness. My mother's criticism, exam failures, and quarrels with friends seemed serious to me at the time, but now I believe in one saying: when God closes a door, He opens a window.

The Place I Know Well

The place I know well is my alma mater. Everyone has their own alma mater, and everyone retains a bit of memory for their alma mater. Remember that day when my mother told me, "Tomorrow is your first day of school." With excitement and mystery, I walked into my alma mater "*** Primary School," beginning to swim in the sea of knowledge. During these five years, I transformed from an ignorant child to a primary school graduate who can grasp a lot of culture. How much effort did my teachers put in for this! Everyone knows that primary school lays the foundation. No matter what career one pursues in the future, all foundations are laid in primary school. As I gradually step into society, I realize how much my alma mater has given me, making it unforgettable and useful for life! I can't forget the playground where classmates gathered, holding hands and shouting together "Unity is Strength!" I can't forget the classroom with those diligent and wise teachers; I can't forget classmates expressing themselves freely or debating and exchanging ideas, producing sparks of thought in seemingly endless chatter and fostering brotherly and sisterly affection!

Although so many years have passed, the image of my alma mater remains fresh in my memory. I can still clearly remember the names of the teachers who taught me in primary school. These teachers devoted all their energy to us—children who were cute yet mischievous, smart yet studious, obedient yet trouble-making, annoying yet lovable. Today, my achievements in my job are inseparable from their hard work back then. I sincerely want to say: thank you, my teachers! Thank you, my alma mater! *** Primary School is a place I know well, and a place I will never forget.
