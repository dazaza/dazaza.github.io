The specific composition ratio of social security is as follows:

- Old-age insurance: The company pays 21% of your monthly salary, and you pay 8%;

- Medical insurance: The company pays 9% of your monthly salary, and you pay 2% plus a 10-yuan fee for major illness pooling (mainly covering hospitalization);

- Unemployment insurance: The company pays 2% of your monthly salary, and you pay 1%;

- Work injury insurance: The company pays 0.5% of your monthly salary, and you don't have to pay anything;

- Maternity insurance: The company pays 0.8% of your monthly salary, and you don't have to pay anything;

- Housing provident fund: The company pays 8% of your monthly salary, and you also pay 8%.

Therefore, the total percentage of social security paid by the company every month should be 21% + 9% + 2% + 0.5% + 0.8% + 8% = 41.3%.

The percentage of social security you personally pay every month should be 8% + 2% + 10 yuan + 1% + 8% = 19% + 10 yuan.

If we temporarily ignore the 10 yuan you pay, the ratio of what the company pays to what you pay should be 413:190. This means if you pay 190 yuan for your social security every month, the company will deposit 413 yuan into your social security account, making a total increase of 603 yuan each month.

In fact, the amount the company pays on your behalf is more than twice what you pay, so don't feel sorry when paying your social security fees every month. The company actually pays much more than you do.

Now let's talk about the three insurances and one fund versus the five insurances and one fund. You should already see that what people commonly refer to as the three insurances and one fund is actually the same as the five insurances and one fund. The former refers to the insurances you personally contribute to (old-age, medical, unemployment, and housing provident fund), while the latter refers to those the company contributes to (old-age, medical, unemployment, work injury, maternity, and housing provident fund). Calling it five insurances and one fund just sounds better.

Apart from companies contributing to social security for their employees, individuals without jobs but with income or those with jobs but whose companies do not contribute to social security can also contribute themselves. However, individuals can only contribute to old-age and medical insurances; they cannot contribute to work injury, maternity, unemployment, or housing provident funds.

Next, I will introduce what the social insurance contribution base is.

We already know that the ratio of the company’s contribution to yours is roughly 413:190. Now, there is another question: What is the relationship between the social security fees you pay each month and this ratio? How are these fees determined?

You may not have noticed, but each province and city announces the "minimum social insurance contribution base" around early July every year. This base is determined through statistics and calculations based on the average salary, benefits, various subsidies, and other miscellaneous expenses of workers from the previous year. In Nanjing, the minimum base announced in July this year was 1189 yuan. This means that for any regular company paying social security for its employees in Nanjing, the minimum amount contributed per employee each month is 1189 × 41.3% = 491 yuan, while employees must contribute at least 1189 × 19% + 10 = 236 yuan per month. Thus, the social security fees you and your company should pay each month become clear.

However, a new problem arises: There is a significant wage disparity among Nanjing employees. Some earn 1000 yuan per month, while others earn 10000 yuan. Should those earning 1000 and those earning 10000 pay the same social security fees?

Please note that 1189 is the minimum base, and above it, there is also a maximum base. This year's maximum base in Nanjing seems to be over 8000 yuan, though I forgot the exact number. If your monthly salary is below 1189 yuan, the company must still contribute according to the minimum base, i.e., 1189 × 41.3% = 491 yuan, and you must contribute at least 1189 × 19% + 10 = 236 yuan per month. This is the significance of using 1189 as the minimum contribution base: Even if you earn 1000 or 800 yuan per month, you must still contribute according to the 1189 base.

However, if your salary exceeds 1189 yuan, say you earn 3000 yuan per month, then when the company goes to the labor bureau to pay your insurance (both the company and your contributions are deducted by the labor bureau), they should inform the labor bureau: "One of our employees earns 3000 yuan per month. Please deduct the social security fees based on 3000 yuan." Then the labor bureau will use 3000 yuan as the contribution base to deduct both the company's and your money and deposit it into your social security account. At this point, the company should contribute 3000 × 41.3% = 1239 yuan per month, and you should contribute 3000 × 19% + 10 = 580 yuan per month.

This means that if your salary is below 1189 yuan, you contribute according to 1189; if your salary is above 1189 yuan, you contribute according to your actual salary, unless your salary exceeds the maximum base. For example, if you earn 10000 yuan per month and the maximum base is 8000, then both you and the company contribute according to 8000.

Here, I want to expose some despicable practices by certain companies. These companies might give you several thousand yuan per month, but when they report your contribution base to the labor bureau, they won’t report it according to your actual salary. For instance, if you earn 3000 yuan per month, they might tell the labor bureau that you only earn 1189 yuan. As a result, the labor bureau deducts only 1189 × 19% + 10 = 236 yuan per month from you. You might think this is great because less is deducted from you, meaning you take home more money. Wrong! If this happens, your company is being extremely despicable! Since the company contributes more than double what you contribute, the less you contribute, the less they contribute too. If they calculate based on your actual salary of 3000, they should contribute 3000 × 41.3% = 1239 yuan per month for your social security fees, but if they calculate based on 1189, they only contribute 1189 × 41.3% = 491 yuan per month! In reality, you are at a great disadvantage! A legitimate company will only report your actual salary to the labor bureau and deduct accordingly! Only those unethical companies will always contribute according to the minimum standard regardless of how much you earn!! Don’t assume that the less your social security fees are deducted each month, the better! You might be getting taken advantage of by your company without even realizing it!!!

It's very simple to determine how much your company is actually contributing for you: If 300 yuan is deducted from your salary for social security each month, your base is approximately 300 ÷ 19% ≈ 1578 yuan. Check if your salary matches this figure. If your salary is clearly higher than 1578, such as 3000 or 4000 yuan per month, then you are definitely being taken advantage of by your company. After being taken advantage of, you can report your company to the local labor inspection team. Generally, once reported, the issue is resolved. However, it's best to gather evidence (such as payslips) before reporting, otherwise, you might not be able to continue working there after reporting. To reiterate, the social security composition ratios differ in different places. If you're not in Nanjing, your contribution ratio might not be 19%, but it shouldn't differ significantly. For instance, your base might be 20% or 22%, but it absolutely wouldn't be 30% or 40%! Although 19% is the ratio in Nanjing, people elsewhere can still estimate their base using this figure.

Next, I will explain the specifics of these social security fees and how to use them.

As previously mentioned, Social Insurance = Old-age Insurance + Medical Insurance + Work Injury Insurance + Maternity Insurance + Unemployment Insurance + Housing Provident Fund

Let's first talk about old-age insurance.

Generally, you need to contribute for at least 15 years to enjoy a pension for life after retirement. Therefore, if you want to receive a pension, you must start contributing at least 15 years before retirement. This rule applies almost universally outside of Nanjing as well. If you reach retirement age but have contributed less than 15 years to old-age insurance, the state will refund all 8% of the pension saved in your personal account upon retirement.

You might ask, where does the 21% contributed by the company go?

Don't expect the state to return that to you. After you withdraw your personal contributions upon retirement, the state transfers the 21% contributed by the company entirely to the national pension统筹fund, and it no longer has any relation to you. You might say, "Hey! That's my money! Why don't I get it?"

Because that's how the state has set the rules.

When refunds are given, only the personal contributions deducted from you are refunded. All contributions made by the company on your behalf are considered contributions to the nation - -

Next, let's see how your pension is calculated upon retirement.

The calculation of pensions is quite complex because the contribution base changes annually. Let me give an example: If you're 30 years old now and your current contribution base is 3000 yuan, and the retirement age is 55, then you must start contributing to old-age insurance before you're 40. If you start contributing from 30 and continue until 55, that's 25 years, so first, you'll definitely be entitled to a pension. Second, if after 25 years, your contribution base of 3000 yuan has become 6000 yuan (hypothetically speaking), then at the age of 55, you'll first receive 6000 × 20% = 1200 yuan as basic pension monthly, which is provided by the state. Additionally, your personal account accumulates quite a bit over 25 years. Let's average the contribution base: (3000 + 6000) ÷ 2 = 4500. Over these 25 years, your personal account should have 4500 × 8% (your personal contribution ratio for old-age insurance) × 25 years × 12 months = 108000 yuan. So, apart from the initial 1200 yuan, you'll also receive 108000 ÷ 120 = 900 yuan monthly. Therefore, starting at the age of 55, you'll receive at least 1200 + 900 = 2100 yuan monthly as pension. Of course, the state's base continues to rise annually, so besides your own 900 yuan, you'll receive more than 1200 yuan monthly after retirement, making your pension increase progressively.

Therefore, the more you contribute to old-age insurance, the better. The more you contribute, the more you'll enjoy after retirement. Moreover, every year after the state adjusts the base, the amount you receive will increase. It's possible to contribute 1000 now and receive 1500 ten years later.

There's a bizarre policy here: Regardless of where you pay social security fees, you can only enjoy the retirement benefits of your registered residence when you retire. From this perspective, those who pay social security fees in areas with high bases but return to areas with low bases to enjoy pensions lose out significantly. Why is that? Let me give an example: If you worked in Nanjing when you were young and paid social security for 20 years before retiring, but your registered residence is in Heilongjiang, then you must return to Heilongjiang to enjoy your pension. If your average contribution base in Nanjing for 20 years was 3000 yuan (hypothetically speaking), and when you retire, the contribution base in Heilongjiang is only 1000 yuan (hypothetically speaking), then upon retirement, you can only enjoy benefits equivalent to 1000 yuan! That's a big loss! In short, if you pay social security in a wealthy area but return to a poorer area to enjoy social security upon retirement, you end up paying a lot but enjoying very little! Paying 3000 yuan might only yield 1000 yuan! That's a terrifying situation, but unfortunately, that's the policy. Therefore, please note: If your registered residence is in a western region or other areas with low bases but you work and pay social security in Beijing or Shanghai with high bases, either pay social security in the west or transfer your registered residence to Beijing or Shanghai before retirement; otherwise, you're making a huge losing deal in life.

Next, let's talk about medical insurance.

The state's policies on this are relatively good, especially regarding hospital reimbursement.

Previously, it was mentioned that the company contributes 9% of your monthly salary to medical insurance, and you personally contribute about 2% plus a 10-yuan major illness pooling fee. This pooling only covers your hospitalization. Out of the 11%, the state deposits 2% monthly into your medical insurance account. If you pay social security at the minimum base of 1189 yuan monthly, then 1189 × 2% = 23.78 yuan is the amount the state deposits into your personal account monthly. This money can accumulate and be used directly to buy medicine or visit clinics. The remaining 9% is included in the medical coordination fund.

According to Nanjing regulations, if you start paying medical insurance from January 2007, you can use your card to buy medicine or visit clinics from February 2007, and your hospitalization costs can be reimbursed from July 2007. The reimbursement process is rather complex, so let me give an example:

If you were hospitalized on August 1, 2007, in the best tertiary hospital in Nanjing, and all the medicines used during your stay were within the medical insurance coverage, with surgery and hospitalization costing a total of 5000 yuan, then when reimbursing, the medical insurance center would first deduct 1000 yuan, which is the starting price. The remaining 4000 yuan can be reimbursed at 4000 × 86% = 3440 yuan, and you only need to pay 4000 × 14% = 560 yuan. Adding the initial 1000 yuan starting price, you spent 5000 yuan but only had to pay 1560 yuan yourself. Moreover, this 1560 yuan can be deducted from your medical insurance card (if you rarely use it), so you actually spend very little on hospitalization.

Medical insurance is more important for us young people than old-age insurance, considering how expensive medical treatment and hospitalization can be. This is one of the most significant reasons to participate in social insurance.

However, Nanjing regulations stipulate that you must contribute to medical insurance for 25 years to enjoy it for life after retirement. Therefore, if you retire at 55, you must start paying medical insurance no later than 30.

Next, let's discuss work injury insurance.

This type of insurance is generally less used in practice, and I haven't encountered many cases. It's important to note that if something happens to you at work or on your way to/from work, this insurance comes into play.

In practice, many people fail to preserve evidence when something happens, preventing them from enjoying work injury insurance, which is regrettable. If you're hit by a car on your way home, you should immediately call the police to investigate, record, and collect evidence through photos. After the police handle the situation, they'll provide you with an accident appraisal certificate, which you can use to request work injury compensation from your company. If you let the person responsible escape without finding witnesses or calling the police, it's unlikely that your case will be accepted as a work injury without evidence.

Work injury insurance also has a time limit. If you suffered a work injury on July 1, 2007, you must immediately report it to your company and submit the evidence and accident appraisal certificate issued by the police, along with your medical records after the injury, to your company for work injury appraisal. Your company must submit your valid materials to the work injury appraisal center by August 1, 2007. If more than a month has passed since the injury, the work injury might not be appraised. If your company doesn't conduct the appraisal for you, you can take the materials to the appraisal center yourself, preferably within a month, otherwise, it could get very complicated.

Next, let's talk about maternity insurance.

Let me illustrate with an example. If you're a female earning 1000 yuan monthly, and you start paying maternity insurance on January 1, 2007, with a contribution base of 1189 yuan, and you get pregnant in March 2007 and give birth at the end of December 2007, discharging from the hospital in January 2008, then after discharge, you should quickly submit all the required documents to your company: marriage certificate (unmarried childbirth cannot be reimbursed), single-child certificate (generally, having two children cannot be reimbursed), medical records, invoices for all prenatal checkups and hospitalization/surgery costs, hospitalization list, and discharge summary. If you spent 500 yuan on prenatal checkups and 2000 yuan on hospitalization and surgery during childbirth, generally, the company can reimburse you almost all the costs within the medical insurance regulations. The reimbursed amount includes: 500 yuan for checkup fees + 2000 yuan for hospitalization and surgery + 1189 yuan/month × 4 months = 7256 yuan. The 1189 yuan/month × 4 months is a special subsidy from the medical insurance center for women claiming reimbursement, which only women can receive.

National regulations stipulate that women must be reimbursed four months' average salary when claiming maternity insurance, so if you give birth, not only do you not spend money, but you also earn four months' salary! If your contribution base is higher than your salary, for example, earning 1000 yuan but contributing 1189, you actually make a profit.

Maternity insurance must be paid for at least one year to enjoy the benefits, remember that.

Additionally, men also pay maternity insurance. Can men enjoy maternity insurance? If you're male, your wife has no job or her company hasn't paid insurance, and she gives birth while you've been paying maternity insurance for more than a year, then you can also claim maternity insurance. Using the previous example, you can claim half of the 500 yuan checkup fee + 2000 yuan hospitalization and surgery fee = 1250 yuan. The 1189 yuan/month × 4 months salary subsidy is something only women can enjoy when claiming.

For the same expenses, women can claim 7256 yuan, while men can only claim 1250 yuan. This is probably one of the few "gender-discriminatory" policies in China. Therefore, women planning to have children should plan ahead and start paying maternity insurance a year in advance to make a profit! And if you're a man planning to marry, it's best to marry a woman who has already paid maternity insurance for a year and whose contribution base is higher than her actual salary when giving birth! However, if she really has no insurance, you can still claim maternity insurance for her, though you won't get the four-month salary subsidy, and you'll only get back half of the reimbursable costs.

Unemployment Insurance

Unemployment insurance also requires you to pay for at least one year to enjoy the benefits. Generally, paying for one year gets you two months of benefits, paying for two years gets you four months, but a lifetime maximum of 24 months. Let me illustrate with an example:

If you start paying insurance on January 1, 2007, and you're laid off by the company in January 2008, you can ask the company to claim two months of unemployment insurance for you.
