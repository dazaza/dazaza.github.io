England Striker Derby: Owen Left with Regrets, Aging Golden Boy May Not Impress Coach Capello

Reported by Ke Han - The match between Manchester United and Aston Villa was also a striker derby for the England national team: United had Wayne Rooney and Michael Owen, while the visiting team had Emile Heskey and Gabriel Agbonlahor. In terms of performance, the combination of Rooney and Heskey as the main strikers for the Three Lions remains unshakable. However, Owen, who just scored a hat-trick in the Champions League midweek, might once again fall into the depths of disappointment: Agbonlahor is younger, better at scoring, and more energetic... If you were Capello, who would you choose?

The only goal of this match came from Agbonlahor's header. Gabrielle Logan, who temporarily replaced Gary Lineker to host BBC, joked about her shared name with Agbonlahor: "For Villa fans, the most important thing is that Gabrielle made history by scoring the winning goal at Old Trafford." Although their names are slightly different in spelling, they are both nicknames derived from "Gabriel." And Gabriel, well-known in the Bible, is the name of the archangel, familiar to everyone in the West.

This goal first demonstrated Agbonlahor's ability to control the ball in the front field and wait for reinforcements when playing alone. In Villa's recent 3 matches, Heskey has started every game. But Heskey’s primary focus is actually on the England national team, with Villa being his secondary job. Why does Capello insist on using a striker who doesn't get much playtime in his club? Simply because of Heskey's ability to control the ball in the front field and create space for teammates. This goal by Agbonlahor was received from Warnock's pass in the front field, where he used his body to block Wes Brown's attempt to disrupt, then forcefully controlled the ball and waited for Ashley Young's support from the left wing before passing it out wide. Standing at only 1.80 meters, Agbonlahor can also become a bridgehead like Heskey.

Next, Agbonlahor showcased his off-the-ball movement skills to find space. After Ashley Young went down the flank, Agbonlahor beautifully ran past Brown and then inserted himself into the gap between Brown and Vidic towards the small area... By the time the cross arrived, he was already unmarked, 1-0! For a player from a near-premier team, what could be better than scoring against one of the top four teams to grab Capello's attention? This was his ninth goal (eighth in the league) of the season, and his fourth consecutive season scoring against United.

At the 30th minute, Agbonlahor seized Fletcher's mistake in receiving the goalkeeper's pass, making a late run to intercept the ball and forming a breakthrough. Facing the advancing Kuszczak, Agbonlahor didn’t take the glory but instead passed it to Ashley Young in the center, fortunately saved by the Polish goalkeeper. In the final moments, Villa began to defend desperately, and Agbonlahor, standing alone upfront, was their only outlet.

In contrast, after coming on as a substitute for Giggs at the 45th minute, Owen had few standout moments. Even after scoring a hat-trick in the midweek Champions League, he still hasn’t earned a Premier League start (this season Owen has only started three league games, while Agbonlahor has played all Villa games), which itself speaks volumes. Moreover, with Bent raising his goal tally to 10 in this round, Owen has no reason to be optimistic.

Owen is already 30 years old, an age that raises concerns unless your name is Peter Pan Giggs. For such an aging player eager to participate in the World Cup, sitting on the bench watching young, ambitious opponents is not pleasant. This wasn't United's night, nor was it Owen's night. On the other hand, the young Gabriel left with a radiant smile on his angelic face.
