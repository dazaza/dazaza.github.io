From the market perspective, the overall trend of index stocks was weak. However, driven by the General Manager of Shenzhen Stock Exchange's statement that margin trading was ready, broker stocks performed well in the early session, with Haitong Securities leading the gains, up 3.11%. Small-cap thematic stocks remained active. Agricultural stocks were stimulated by reports that as the world's largest rice producer and consumer, the Chinese government has approved the first genetically modified rice variety for commercial planting, leading to a comprehensive strengthening. The leading stock, Denghai Seed Industry, was locked at the upper limit.

In terms of news, funds are busy with year-end research and portfolio adjustment. The annual ranking battle of funds at the end of the year has already begun. Yesterday, Bu Yongxiang, head of the Financial Markets Department of the Research Bureau of the People's Bank of China, said that it is expected that domestic loans will increase by 5-7 trillion yuan next year, and the growth rate of broad money supply (M2) will remain between 16%-17%, and interest rate policies may adjust next year. Previously, the market generally expected new loans next year to be at least 7 trillion yuan.

Hengji Information believes that after the continuous rise of the market in the first half of this week, today's adjustment was within our expectations. From a technical analysis, the mid-rail of the large institutional yin-yang heaven-and-earth belt and the golden life line converge around the 3215 point mark, which will form effective support for the short-term market. Investors do not need to worry about the market index, but should take advantage of the market volatility to actively adjust their portfolios and buy high-quality stocks at low prices. Reviewing the market trends since the beginning of the year, as long as the market runs above the golden life line of large institutions and the golden life line is red, every time the market retraces to this line, it is an opportunity. This proves a saying: history often repeats itself, and opportunities always reappear cyclically.

Looking ahead, the agricultural stocks in the large consumption sector that we have repeatedly mentioned recently performed strongly today. Although the overall increase was only about 2%, it was enough to attract attention in the current environment. Therefore, we must reiterate our unchanged view: with the year-end market already underway, considering the long term, we should focus on the consumption sector that benefits significantly from economic development, and focus on capturing small-cap high-quality stocks with high distribution topics whose annual reports are expected to exceed expectations.

The process of buying and selling stocks is as follows:

When buying or selling stocks, you must specify whether you want to buy or sell, the name (or code) of the stock, quantity, and price. Furthermore, this order is only valid on the day it is placed. The content of the order includes the abbreviation (code) of the stock you want to buy or sell, the quantity, and the price. The abbreviation of a stock usually consists of three to four Chinese characters, and the stock code is six digits. When placing an order, the code and abbreviation of the stock must match. At the same time, there are certain regulations regarding the quantity of stocks being traded: the quantity of stocks ordered for purchase must be a multiple of 100, but the quantity of stocks sold does not have to be a multiple of 100.

There are four ways to place an order: counter submission, telephone automatic order, computer automatic order, and remote terminal order.
