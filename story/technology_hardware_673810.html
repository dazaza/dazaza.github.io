Pornography and corruption have become a "popular disease" among greedy officials, and experts point out that punishment is not enough.

by dahslkh6 on 2012-02-23 22:26:05

Core Tip: "Sexual corruption" has become one of the "epidemics" among corrupt officials today. The saying goes, "flags are flying outside, but the red flag at home remains standing," and many corrupt officials have even taken pride in this. However, in recent years, incidents where mistresses turn against their patrons or wives expose their husbands' dark deeds have been on the rise. These events undoubtedly throw corrupt officials into disarray and a difficult situation, sounding an alarm for all officials that playing with fire will surely lead to self-destruction.

When endless love turns into boundless hatred, the consequences are devastating. For those corrupt officials who thought they were doing well in their romantic affairs, they could never have imagined that one day, it would be their own wife or so-called "red-faced confidante" who would bring them down.

A wife exposing her husband's corruption: Deputy Director loses his position

The deeper the love, the sharper the hate.

For Zhu Xiaohong, the former deputy director of the Land and Resources Bureau of Kaihua County, Quzhou City, he never expected that it would be his wife, with whom he had shared a bed for nearly 20 years, who would ruin his reputation.

In July last year, a netizen named "Autumn Leaves Under the Setting Sun" posted multiple "exposure" posts on Weibo, accusing her husband Zhu Xiaohong of keeping a mistress.

On July 14th, she posted her first Weibo message: "In some time, I will expose how land officials buy cars and houses for their mistresses and sign purchase agreements with third parties. It's infuriating!"

Five days passed without any attention on Weibo.

On July 20th, "Autumn Leaves Under the Setting Sun" posted again: "My husband is committing adultery with a married woman. They've quietly rented a place outside and bought a house in the city under a third party's name using illicit funds. Do you want to know who the adulterers are?"

Still, no one forwarded or commented.

On August 1st, a Weibo post finally caught public attention: "Will the next murderer be me? My husband, Zhu Xiaohong, the deputy director of the Land and Resources Bureau of Kaihua County, Zhejiang Province, frequently engages in prostitution and keeps a long-term affair with a married woman named Jiang, buying her a car and a house... I live in agony every day. I often wonder, will the next murderer be me?"

It was later confirmed that the Weibo user was indeed Zhu Xiaohong's wife.

Soon, this Weibo post was widely shared and commented on by netizens. Some expressed deep sympathy, while others hoped relevant authorities would investigate thoroughly and give society an explanation.

On August 15th, the Quzhou Municipal Commission for Discipline Inspection announced that after preliminary investigations, Zhu Xiaohong's behavior was suspected of violating discipline. The Party Committee of the Quzhou Municipal Land and Resources Bureau decided to dismiss Zhu Xiaohong from his positions as a member of the Party Committee and deputy director of the Kaihua County Land and Resources Bureau, and initiated an investigation into him.

Currently, the case has not yet been fully resolved, but for Zhu Xiaohong's wife, reporting her husband was out of sheer desperation. Originally, the couple was very affectionate and had an 18-year-old son. However, since 2008, she noticed her husband often staying out all night and having an affair with Jiang. Eventually, it led to their separation, and their son was taken away by her husband.

Thus, she reluctantly abandoned their marital bond and made the difficult decision to report her husband.

A final blow: Husband sentenced to prison

There is a popular American TV series called "Desperate Housewives." The lives of the female protagonists seem perfect on the surface, but they are all masking their already chaotic lives with "happiness."

In Shaoxing, a real-life version of "Desperate Housewives" unfolded. With her husband's infidelity, her marriage life came to an end. Ultimately, the wife exposed her husband and his mistress, sending them both to prison just before her death.

The main character in this story is Zhou Guoqiang, the former director of the Real Estate Management Office in Shaoxing.

Let's go back to March 14, 2000, known as White Valentine's Day, which would become an unforgettable day for Zhou Guoqiang.

At a hotel in Shaoxing, while Zhou Guoqiang was enjoying drinks with friends, Jin Jianhong, dressed in bright colors, approached him with a glass of wine.

"Director Zhou, you manage all the houses in Shaoxing. When will you take care of us too?" Jin Jianhong's flirtatious words and affectionate gaze instantly captivated Zhou Guoqiang. They conversed happily at the table and exchanged contact information.

Afterward, Zhou Guoqiang and Jin Jianhong maintained a lover relationship for 10 years.

As the saying goes, there's no such thing as a free lunch. After indulging in Jin Jianhong's beauty, Zhou Guoqiang naturally had to pay the price.

Soon, Jin Jianhong, who ran a small business in Kechiao Textile City in Shaoxing County, became the chairman of a textile company thanks to Zhou Guoqiang's support. The company smoothly obtained the right to engage in foreign trade and export operations, selling products to over 20 countries and making substantial profits.

Jin Jianhong was also no ordinary woman; she divorced her husband and clung tightly to Zhou Guoqiang.

To please his lover, Zhou Guoqiang used various means to abuse his power and amass wealth.

Once, Jin Jianhong proposed buying a better car. Zhou Guoqiang immediately agreed. When he informed Xiao Zhuang, who worked in real estate intermediation, about his lover's wish, Xiao Zhuang quickly understood the implication. He knew his investment would certainly yield double returns.

So, Xiao Zhuang accompanied Jin Jianhong to a Mercedes-Benz dealership in Hangzhou and paid 330,000 yuan for the car.

Later, as Xiao Zhuang anticipated, Zhou Guoqiang utilized his position to provide internal information about property acquisitions and transactions, allowing Xiao Zhuang to profit immensely. Moreover, Zhou Guoqiang extended his black hand into other areas to gain benefits and please his lover.

On July 14th last year, Zhou Guoqiang's critically ill wife, under the pseudonym "Desperate Housewife," exposed her husband's illegal activities on a local forum in Shaoxing and reported him to the local disciplinary committee.

In the end, Zhou Guoqiang was sentenced to 18 years and six months in prison and fined 150,000 yuan of personal property. Jin Jianhong also faced an eight-year jail term.

Moral bottom line collapse: Playing with fire will inevitably lead to self-destruction

"Currently, moral bottom lines continue to retreat, and traditional family values and views on marriage and love are facing unprecedented challenges," commented netizen "Ling Han."

"Ling Han" believes that now, some officials regard keeping mistresses as an indispensable identity embellishment, seemingly taking pride in being surrounded by beautiful women; while some women seek power or profit, colluding effortlessly, each getting what they want. In their eyes, propriety, integrity, and ethical consensus cease to exist.

Unbeknownst to them, "the word 'sex' carries a dangerous edge." When individual interests cannot be resolved, yesterday's passionate lover may quickly transform into a cold-blooded "killer." A fleeting encounter might very well be a fatal dagger leading one to the abyss of crime.

According to statistics compiled by Professor Wu Changzhen, one of the principal drafters of the current Marriage Law, 95% of corrupt officials investigated had mistresses, and more than 60% of corrupt leaders were involved in keeping mistresses.

Professor Wu Changzhen discovered during research that the phenomenon of keeping mistresses was increasing, becoming more open, and expanding its scope to include officials, causing severe consequences, particularly leading to corruption and serious criminal cases. Some cases of official-business collusion and murder to cover up crimes were caused by keeping mistresses.

Due to the escalating trend of keeping mistresses, some wives had no choice but to report their husbands, disregarding "a hundred days of grace for a day of marriage."

Chen Xingliang, deputy director of Zhejiang Guangzhengda Law Firm, believes that wives reporting their husbands further warns corrupt officials about keeping mistresses, reminding them that they shouldn't think "you know, she knows, but no one else does." It also reflects the need for relevant authorities to pay attention to the conduct issues of some corrupt officials.

Chen Xingliang said, "One who lacks virtue cannot govern the people." Today, this can be interpreted as "one who lacks virtue cannot be an official." Conduct is the external manifestation of a leader's party spirit, political quality, and moral integrity.

Recently, various levels of the party have reiterated the principle of appointing people based on both virtue and talent, prioritizing virtue. "This is not only the party's standard and guidance for selecting personnel but also imposes strict requirements on officials' conduct." Some experts believe that relevant departments should not only focus on officials' conduct during appointments but also strengthen supervision on a daily basis.

Experts propose that the harm of sexual corruption cannot be underestimated. If the relationship between corrupt officials and their mistresses is seen as a transaction of power for sex, then to eradicate this畸形transaction market and reduce the number of officials falling to "skirts," punishing the "buyers" (corrupt officials) alone is insufficient. Clear warnings must also be issued to the "sellers" (the supply chain of "skirts").

Expert Opinion:

Mao Zhaohui, Director of the Anti-Corruption and Clean Government Policy Research Center at Renmin University of China: The loss of public ethics among some leaders reflects an official culture where "keeping mistresses is not shameful but rather honorable." This concept, if left unchecked, can have a pernicious influence on officials. Even more alarming is when officials, despite their affairs being "common knowledge," still experience simultaneous corruption and promotion. This indicates that incorporating "private life" into the anti-corruption perspective currently lacks effective operational methods. Improvements should be made in the standards for selecting officials, including constraints under criminal law.
