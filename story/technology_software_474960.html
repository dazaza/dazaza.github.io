Series Three of Agricultural Supermarket Connection:

Meeting Standards Can't Replace Marketing

Lao Xiangpeng, Chairman of FULAI Brand Marketing Advisory Institution

Everyone says that supermarkets have many and strict requirements for agricultural products. Are there any specific demands and standards? Yes, they are pollution-free agricultural products, green food, organic food, and geographical indication agricultural products, collectively referred to as "three products and one mark."

If agricultural products meet one or all of the "three products and one mark" standards, can they definitely sell well? Not necessarily. Meeting the standards is just a basic market entry condition, like a pass to enter the competition, but the ultimate success or failure in sales depends on whether agricultural product companies know how to market.

Meeting the standards is just the qualification round

Pollution-free agricultural products, green food, organic food, and geographical indication agricultural products are four officially recognized standards promoted by the state in the agricultural sector. In the agricultural-supermarket connection, these are important criteria for supermarkets to evaluate agricultural enterprises and the quality safety of agricultural products.

Although not all agricultural products entering supermarkets currently have "three products and one mark" certification, it will almost be impossible for agricultural products to enter supermarkets without meeting these standards in the future. As the agricultural-supermarket connection becomes more in-depth and widespread, "three products and one mark" will inevitably become the basic conditions for market entry. That is, meeting the standards is not omnipotent, but not meeting them is absolutely unacceptable.

Currently, the total number of certified "three products and one mark" products has reached more than 70,000. Measured by output or commodity volume, the certified origin accounts for about 30% of the edible agricultural product production area, and the certified "three products and one mark" products account for about 30% of the edible agricultural product commodities.

Introduction to "Three Products and One Mark"

Standards and Certification for Pollution-Free Agricultural Products

Pollution-free agricultural products refer to unprocessed or minimally processed edible agricultural products whose origin environment, production process, and product quality comply with national standards and regulations. They are certified and qualified, obtaining certification certificates and permission to use the pollution-free agricultural product mark. Among "three products and one mark," this is the lowest level.

On April 29, 2002, the Ministry of Agriculture and the General Administration of Quality Supervision jointly issued the "Management Measures for Pollution-Free Agricultural Products."

The certification for pollution-free agricultural products adopts a combination of origin identification and product certification. Only products that pass the origin identification can apply for product certification. The certification process for pollution-free agricultural products includes six fundamental steps: origin identification, preliminary review of product certification, product testing, technical review, expert evaluation, and certificate issuance announcement.

The Ministry of Agriculture established a specialized institution for pollution-free agricultural product certification, namely the Agricultural Product Quality Safety Center (hereinafter referred to as the "Center"), which manages the unified certification and identification marks for pollution-free agricultural products nationwide. Currently, many provincial and municipal agricultural management authorities have conducted pollution-free food certification work, but only under the formal registration of trademarks with the State Administration for Industry and Commerce or the promulgation of provincial regulations does their certification have legal effect.

Standards and Certification for Green Food

Internationally, common certifications include pollution-free food certification and organic food certification. Green food certification is unique to China.

In May 1990, the Ministry of Agriculture of China officially defined the name, standard, and mark for green food. Green food is divided into two types: Grade A and Grade AA. Grade A green food allows limited use of chemically synthesized production materials during production, while Grade AA green food strictly requires no use of chemically synthesized fertilizers, pesticides, veterinary drugs, feed additives, food additives, and other substances harmful to the environment and health during production. Essentially, green food is a transitional product moving from conventional food towards organic food.

The green food standard is an agricultural industry recommended standard (NY/T) announced by the Ministry of Agriculture, and the certifying body for green food is the China Green Food Development Center.

Standards and Certification for Organic Food

Organic food refers to agricultural by-products and their processed products derived from organic agriculture systems, produced according to international organic agriculture requirements and corresponding standards, and certified by specialized organic food certification agencies. These include grains, vegetables, fruits, dairy products, poultry and livestock products, honey, aquatic products, seasonings, etc.

Organic food is truly a natural, nutritious, high-quality environmental protection safe food, representing the highest international standard.
