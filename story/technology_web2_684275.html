Blood Donor Rejected for Free Blood Use Due to Hukou Restrictions

A blood donor was denied free blood use due to hukou (household registration) restrictions. The Yangcheng Evening News reported that Mr. Wu, originally from Zhanjiang but currently working in Kaiping, expressed his dissatisfaction with the "Provisional Regulations on the Reimbursement of Blood Usage Mutual Fund" in Kaiping.

According to regulations, blood usage should be reimbursed, but it was rejected. Mr. Wu explained that he is from Lianjiang, Zhanjiang, and since he was in good health and aware of the societal need for blood, he began donating blood at local blood stations starting in 2002. In July 2002, he donated 200 milliliters of blood at Zhanjiang Central Hospital, and after moving to Jiangmen for work in 2004, he donated another 400 milliliters in two separate instances at the Jiangmen Central Blood Station. In total, he had donated 600 milliliters of blood.

In November 2011, his father was diagnosed with acute monocytic leukemia and was admitted to Kaiping Central Hospital for clinical treatment, requiring a large amount of blood. A total of seven units of blood were used, costing 440 yuan per unit. Recalling that the staff had informed him that his immediate family members could use blood for free if needed in the future, Mr. Wu attempted to claim reimbursement for the over 3000 yuan spent on the seven units of blood after his father was discharged in February 2012. However, the hospital refused his claim, citing that neither he nor his father were registered as residents of the Wuyi region of Jiangmen.

Frustrated, Mr. Wu then tried to have the expenses reimbursed in Zhanjiang, but the Zhanjiang medical insurance bureau also denied his request because his father did not receive the blood transfusion in Zhanjiang. With the healthcare system now connected throughout Guangdong province, Mr. Wu was confused whether it was truly impossible to get reimbursed or if the two regions were passing the buck.

Mr. Wu showed the reporter proof of his blood donations in both Zhanjiang and Kaiping, along with his father's hospitalization and blood usage fee receipts, and a promotional booklet jointly issued by the Kaiping Red Cross Society and the Kaiping Voluntary Blood Donation Office titled "Provisional Regulations on the Reimbursement of Blood Usage Mutual Fund," which clearly stated that reimbursement conditions apply only to those with household registration in the Wuyi region of Jiangmen.

The leader of the blood station responded with uncertainty. Subsequently, the reporter interviewed the Jiangmen Central Blood Station regarding Mr. Wu's situation. A deputy director surnamed Li told the reporter that in 2009, the station had issued the "Provisional Management Measures for Voluntary Blood Donation in Jiangmen" and distributed it to all jurisdictional blood donation offices. It explicitly stipulated that any citizen who voluntarily donates blood within this jurisdiction (Jiangmen), their spouse and direct relatives can claim mutual funds according to relevant regulations when clinically using blood.

Li believed that the reason why Mr. Wu encountered difficulties in reimbursement was due to the hospital's insufficient understanding or misinterpretation of the blood station's policies. He explained that previously, blood donation mutual funds were processed centrally at various blood donation offices, but later, some places directly handled it at hospitals providing clinical blood usage. Some hospitals might not have organized proper training for relevant staff on these policies, leading to many being unaware and thus assuming that reimbursement was not possible.

As for whether Mr. Wu could reimburse the mutual fund in Zhanjiang, Deputy Director Li said it was uncertain, as each city-level region in Guangdong Province formulates its own management methods for voluntary blood donation based on its actual situation.
