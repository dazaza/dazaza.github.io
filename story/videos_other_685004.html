The Beijing Football Association is looking forward to the match being held on the eve of the London Olympics.

by wsdqwd2dc on 2012-03-01 12:09:49

Arsenal and Manchester City have successively announced on their official websites that they will play a "China Cup" match at the Bird's Nest Stadium in Beijing on July 27th this year. That day also marks the opening of the London Olympics. The China Cup is scheduled to kick off at 8 PM Beijing time on July 27, with the London Olympics beginning 7 hours and 12 minutes later (at 20:12 on July 27 in London). Arsenal's official website...

Manchester City CEO Malcolm Boswell expressed his praise for the China Cup event: "This game is highly anticipated for Manchester City. The Bird's Nest is one of the iconic sports buildings in the world, and it is most appropriate for two of the best teams from England to compete here."

The Manchester City official website also quoted Chen Wei, Deputy Secretary-General of the Beijing Football Association: "The Beijing Football Association looks forward to this match being held just before the London Olympics. We are committed to the development of football in Beijing and are very pleased to see two top clubs playing at the Bird's Nest. We will work together to achieve our goals."

On the morning of February 29th local time, Arsenal announced on its official website that the club is delighted to confirm that the first team will travel to Beijing, China in July for a match at the National Stadium against Manchester City. Arsenal and Manchester City will participate in the first-ever "China Cup", which will be decided in a single match, taking place at 8 PM local time on July 27 at the Bird's Nest.

Arsenal CEO Ivan Gazidis said: "This will be an extension of last year's trip to Hangzhou. Everyone at Arsenal was deeply impressed by last year's tour of China. We enjoy the tremendous support we receive in Asia, and in Beijing, we will once again connect closely with our loyal Chinese fans. We can't wait to return to this great country."
