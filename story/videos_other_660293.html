Wuxi Taike Electronic Technology Co., Ltd. Preface: According to the explanation of the lyricist, Mr. Shi Rencheng

by anonymous on 2012-02-07 15:40:16

Introduction: According to the explanation of lyricist Mr. Shi Rencheng, the story is about a couple who have been broken up for a long time. One day, the girl suddenly invites the boy out for dinner. In fact, her purpose is to send him a wedding invitation, telling him, "I'm getting married!" They meet at their old dating spot - a restaurant. As soon as they sit down and start chatting, the boy begins his battle to defend his self-respect. Although he feels a mix of emotions, including heartache and helplessness, he can't show it. After all, a man also has his pride!

Mr. Shi Rencheng also mentioned that although the male protagonist in the lyrics keeps saying he's not lying, he starts lying from the very first sentence. This is the structure that Mr. Shi Rencheng considers himself to have used quite cleverly.

He said that You Jia's difficulty in singing lies in how to use his voice to perform the "stubborn denial" state of the male protagonist in the lyrics. Even though he's lying, and gradually being exposed by the other party, he still insists, "I'm not lying," until finally breaking down in the later chorus.

Based on the clues provided by Mr. Shi Rencheng, I deduced and expanded the story, adding annotations. I found that among the 42 lines of lyrics, only four sentences can be considered truthful. I believe after reading this, everyone will have a deeper understanding of this song, "Lying".

1. There have been some good partners (Actually, I haven't dated anyone since our breakup because there's only you in my heart).

I don't feel lonely or alone (In fact, I've been living alone and feeling very lonely).

Maybe I'm just too carefree (Actually, I'm not at all promiscuous).

Making people uneasy, so it ends up failing (In fact, I haven't started anything with anyone, so how could it end?).

Paragraph Analysis: The first part, starting the conversation at the table, mainly involves concealing one's loneliness after the breakup to the ex-girlfriend and fabricating facts, intending to make the ex-girlfriend believe that one is leading a dissolute and happy life. But ultimately, there's nothing of the sort; there's only ever been the former partner in one's heart. The ex-girlfriend questions why the male protagonist appears to be so promiscuous but is still single, believing he is lying. The male protagonist can only explain his current single status as due to his own excessive promiscuity making others feel unsafe, thus leaving.

2. I have no psychological scars (Actually, our breakup had a great impact on me).

Don't worry about it (Live happily with your current partner; I'll bear the pain myself).

I'm not fragile (Actually, I'm human too and extremely fragile).

Moreover, that's not even a wound (That's not just a wound; it's an injury I'll never recover from).

Anyway, isn't love always like this (Actually, I can't let go at all)?

Paragraph Analysis: The second part, the male protagonist wants to make the ex-girlfriend believe that his psychological resilience is strong, and the breakup wasn't worth causing any attachment. He pretends to have floated through the world of love for many years and has long seen through it all. In reality, the male protagonist can't let go, can't figure it out, and has suffered severe emotional wounds.

3. I'm not lying (Everything I just said was false).

Why would I lie (To protect my self-respect, I must lie).

You know me (You used to understand me well).

I've never pretended with you (I didn't before, but now I have to).

I'm not lying (I really am lying).

Don't think you're unforgettable (After such a long break-up, I still can't forget you).

My laughter is genuine, not forced (I'm actually forcing a smile).

Paragraph Analysis: The third part, based on her past understanding of him, the male protagonist's ex-girlfriend suspects he's lying. However, the male protagonist tries hard to cover it up and uses the example of "never lying to my ex" to disguise himself, wanting his ex to believe he's easily forgotten her and that his current laughter comes from within. But since he's lying, the feelings in his heart cannot be concealed and gradually begin to surface.

4. I haven't been to this restaurant for a long time (Actually, I often come here to find our memories from back then).

Never expected the decoration has changed (If I came regularly, wouldn't I know?).

That corner window (Our fixed position when we dated).

Smells of roses (Roses were witnesses to our love back then).

Now that you mention it, I do remember (Actually, without your reminder, I would always clearly remember).

Paragraph Analysis: In the fourth part, the male and female protagonists talk about the restaurant where they used to date frequently. To conceal himself, the male protagonist deliberately lies to his ex, saying he rarely came here after the breakup, implying that everything has changed, including his relationship with his ex. The female protagonist mentions the strong rose fragrance at the place where they often sat during their dates. The male protagonist seizes the topic and says he barely remembers, now that it's been mentioned, he has some impression, intending to imply that his feelings for the restaurant are like his feelings for his ex, both have faded.

5. I'm not lying (Everything I just said was false).

Why would I lie (To protect my self-respect, I must lie).

You know me (Back then, you indeed understood me).

One of my flaws is that I'm forgetful (I am forgetful, but how could I forget everything about us?).

I'm not lying (I really am lying).

Thank you for keeping me company tonight (Thank you for pitying me, this lonely person).

But I somehow feel a bit uncomfortable (We're not the same as before; now it hurts so much I feel like I can't breathe).

Paragraph Analysis: In the fifth part, the female protagonist starts exposing all the male protagonist's lies. The male protagonist uses "my ex knew I was forgetful" to dodge the issue, but anyone knows, no matter how forgetful, how could one forget such profound memories of two people? The male protagonist is deceiving himself. Thinking about his current loneliness while his ex is about to enter the marriage hall, she actually came to deliver a wedding invitation tonight, but he described it as coming to have dinner with him due to his self-respect. His inner emotions can no longer be suppressed; he even seems a little out of control, so he adds a line, "Someone like me, who sees through love and plays with romance, even finds it strange under such an atmosphere tonight, which is why I seem a little sad." Fortunately, the male protagonist didn't say he looked uncomfortable because he suddenly fell ill...

6. I'm not lying (Everything I just said was false).

Why would I lie (To protect my self-respect, I must lie).

If loving someone doesn't lead to anything, does it matter? (Of course it matters; it makes me depressed, melancholic, and deeply sorrowful).

Don't say I'm lying (Give me a little dignity; let me pretend to be happy once in front of your happiness).

Life is already so difficult (The pain of losing you, the pressure of life and career makes me unable to breathe).

Some things shouldn't be exposed (I just can't forget you; I lied earlier).

Paragraph Analysis: In the sixth part, the female protagonist sees the male protagonist's distress and asks if he was deeply affected by not being able to be with her, leading to his current suffering. The male protagonist stubbornly counters by asking how much damage the breakup caused him, insisting it didn't affect him much. At this point, the male protagonist's feelings are fully revealed, and both parties understand each other. As his mask is gradually peeled off, the male protagonist almost pleads with the female protagonist not to continue, admitting that the breakup has already severely impacted him. His decadence in life, coupled with repeated failures in entrepreneurship with friends, has made him lose his pursuit of life. He believes that the female protagonist's current situation is far above his, so she, as a happy person, should not pierce through his last bit of dignity as a failure.

7. I'm not lying (Everything I just said was false).

It's love that's lying (It's not love; it's me who's lying).

It brought you to deceive me into thinking there's hope for what I yearn for (I originally thought you invited me out tonight for a chance to reconcile, but unexpectedly, you came to deliver a wedding invitation, inviting me to your wedding).

Paragraph Analysis: However, the male protagonist still refuses to admit he's lying voluntarily, trying to attribute it to "being forced to lie because of love," and says that he thought the female protagonist came tonight to give him another chance to reconcile, but completely unexpected, not only is it not that way, but his ex-girlfriend is delivering a wedding invitation, meaning she's going to be someone else's bride! How could he forget those joys they shared together back then? Now, he has to watch her become someone else's wife, and he might even mistakenly think she came tonight to flaunt!

8. I'm not lying (Everything I just said was false).

Wishing you become a happy bride (How could I be happy when you're someone else's bride? I wish you were my bride!).

Please forget all my secrets (Actually, I still hope you remember me; after all, love is selfish, even though you're about to be someone else's bride).

Paragraph Analysis: As the drama concludes, the male protagonist has completely collapsed. All the waiting for his ex after the breakup, all the loneliness and solitude he endured, have come to naught. Now, it's his ex who's getting married. Besides giving blessings, what else can he offer? He can only let the female protagonist forget about him, this joke, and as for his future, who knows?
