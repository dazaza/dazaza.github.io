What causes prostatitis? The etiology of prostatitis is complex, and many male friends often do not understand the causes of prostatitis after they have it. Abnormal sexual life, such as frequent sexual activity, masturbation, interrupted sexual activity, excessive inhibition of sexual activity, etc., can cause prostate congestion and induce prostatitis. Patients with sexually transmitted diseases, such as gonococcal urethritis, gonococci can enter the prostate through the urethra and prostate ducts, causing the disease. For how prostatitis is caused, prostate stones or prostate hyperplasia can cause prostate tissue congestion and non-specific infection. How is prostatitis caused? It is often due to the introduction of pathogenic bacteria by urinary instruments during catheterization or urethral dilation due to improper operation. Excessive drinking, prolonged sitting, cycling, horseback riding, and other straddling movements can also cause prostate congestion and are closely related to the onset of the disease. Cold exposure can stimulate the sympathetic nerve activity of the prostate, leading to increased pressure in the urethra, obstructing excretion, and causing congestion and stasis in the prostate due to contraction of the prostate ducts. Excessive frequency and force during prostate massage can also cause prostate congestion and edema. Inflammatory lesions in nearby organs such as the rectum, colon, and lower urinary tract can also cause prostatitis through lymphatic vessels.
