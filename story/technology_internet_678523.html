Don't let curtains, fabrics, towels, and bedding become sources of infection.

by bery201209 on 2012-02-27 15:07:22

When we use towels everyday, do you know that there are also many rules regarding the hygiene of towel usage? Experts from Bery Home [http://www.beryhome.com/] suggest that personal-use towels should be replaced after about 30 days of use, and dry towels should be used to wipe your face and hands after washing them. Therefore, everyone should have multiple towels on hand.

Many people may feel that when a towel is used for too long, it becomes hard and uncomfortable. Therefore, we need to frequently steam or boil our towels at high temperatures to disinfect and soften them.

Most towels are made from pure cotton yarn. Cotton fibers have a tubular structure that can store water; their main chemical component, cellulose molecules, contain a large number of hydrophilic groups that can absorb moisture. The hygroscopic and water-storing properties of cotton yarn make the texture of towels comfortable to touch, with strong cleaning power, making them suitable for facial cleansing. However, it is precisely because of these characteristics that they provide convenient conditions for bacterial growth. Bacteria thrive in warm and humid environments.

Therefore, if a towel remains in a warm and humid state for a long time, after a certain period, it will become a breeding ground for bacteria. In addition, oils, dust, and impurities from human skin, along with bacteria from the air and water, accumulate on the towel. Using such a towel to clean your skin not only fails to clean it, but can also contaminate the skin and clog pores. Women who like makeup and children with fast metabolism have more oil on their skin, making it easier for bacteria to grow on towels, posing a greater health risk.

When washing your face or taking a shower, it's best to first rinse thoroughly with shower gel and clean water, then dry with a dry towel. This reduces the attachment of dirt to the towel. After using the towel, it should be cleaned promptly. Every week, it should be boiled in hot water for 10 minutes to disinfect it. It should be hung in a ventilated area, and ideally dried or sun-dried as soon as possible.

Qualified towel products should have standardized labels, indicating the manufacturer, origin, telephone number, trademark, execution standard, washing method, etc.

In terms of texture, one should choose towels that are soft and fluffy. Such towels feel elastic in the hand and give a soft, face-hugging sensation when pressed against the face. Towels should avoid being dry and hard, as this can harm the skin. Generally speaking, quality towels have a softer texture and are fluffy and elastic. Additionally, families can perform some quality checks when using towels, such as whether a drop of water placed on a new towel is quickly absorbed, which indicates good water absorption. Quality towels have elasticity and friction during use, and won't fade in water. Inferior towels have poor water absorption, loosen easily, lack elasticity, feel slippery, and fade significantly when washed, causing greater irritation and damage to the skin and eyes.
