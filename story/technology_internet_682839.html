The Wuxia Complex of 5D Movies from the Perspective of "Flying Swords of Dragon Gate"

by abcsftkb on 2012-02-29 10:00:37

Recently released "Flying Swords of Dragon Gate" is known as the first 3D blockbuster in China, which made countless martial arts fans sigh. Because before this, there had been movies hyped as the first domestic 3D blockbusters, but they turned out to be very disappointing. However, this time, "Flying Swords of Dragon Gate" can indeed be called a true 3D movie, which also gives some comfort to the Chinese. Just at this time, Leike's first domestic martial arts 5D movie, "The Divine Bird of Jiamen", gave another comfort to martial arts fans on another timeline.

"The Divine Bird of Jiamen" tells a story of a war on the Shu Road of Jiange in the Three Kingdoms period. The Shu army led by Wu Hou fought hard with the enemy, and under the witness of a magical divine bird, let the audience personally experience the brutality and grandeur of the ancient battlefield. Although it is a war theme, in terms of selection of materials, it belongs to the first 5D movie in China based on the historical background of ancient China, which is also a great innovation in this industry. The fighting scenes in the film are more like a 5D version display of a martial arts movie. Compared with "Flying Swords of Dragon Gate", although it lacks the suspense of a dramatic plot, it surpasses "Flying Swords of Dragon Gate" in scene rendering and realism.

5D movies are also similar to traditional films and TV shows, having a strong martial arts sentiment, which is also the dream of every Chinese person.

Martial arts may be an adult fairy tale, but the pursuit of justice and the enthusiasm for chivalry in martial arts are the embodiment of the traditional cultural spirit of the Chinese people. Martial arts are dreams and寄托s for ideals. Once, Deng Xiaoping was a loyal reader of Jin Yong's novels, which shows its influence significance in Chinese culture.

"The Divine Bird of Jiamen" is Leike's first attempt to present martial arts through 5D movies, turning the sword fights and roof-top chases that could only be seen in previous martial arts epics into real sensory enjoyment. Through the simulation of feelings such as the shaking of 5D movie seats, lightning, snowflakes, and wind, martial arts is no longer just a picture on the screen, but a tangible feeling beyond the shadowy images. It is believed that it will be very popular among martial arts fans, and this popularity will also promote the further development of the 5D movie industry, giving birth to more excellent martial arts versions of 5D movies.

Leike's development team said that the application of martial arts sentiment in 5D movies is just the beginning. In the future, we will launch martial arts versions of 5D movies that can rival 3D blockbusters. And it won't be just about describing kung fu itself; we will add the concept of chivalrous tenderness to 5D movies, letting 5D movies have their own soul and not be a thin movie anymore. Only in this way can our 5D movie business develop faster, and Leike can go further.
