Enrollment 2011 Shandong College Entrance Examination: Nearly 570,000 candidates can go to college

by anonymous on 2011-05-10 15:11:23

In 2011, nearly 570,000 candidates in Shandong's college entrance examination could enter university.

Shandong College Entrance Examination Enrollment Network reported that in recent years, our province's college entrance examination enrollment has carried out many reforms and achieved good results. The next step will be to accelerate the reform of the college entrance examination system. Our province's 2011-2015 general higher education enrollment system reform implementation plan has already been announced. The plan focuses on strengthening the physical fitness and physical quality assessment of candidates, launching comprehensive exam reforms, conducting general undergraduate and higher vocational classification exams pilot programs, actively promoting provincial universities' independent recruitment pilot programs, and steadily implementing the registration admission methods for higher vocational colleges, proposing specific measures for the next five years. To refine the college entrance examination reform plan, the Provincial Department of Education recently established two leading groups and corresponding working teams. One is responsible for incorporating students' physical fitness into the scope of the college entrance examination assessment; the other is responsible for studying and issuing the specific plan for the comprehensive reform of the 2014 college entrance examination.

Regarding our province's ongoing acceleration of the college entrance examination enrollment system reform, Huang Sheng emphasized that the reform is necessary, but we must fight a prepared battle! This year marks the beginning of the 12th Five-Year Plan, as well as the first year of our province's implementation of the medium- and long-term education reform and development plan outline. Everything is difficult at the start, but we must ensure initial success.

According to relevant officials from the Shandong Provincial Department of Education, this year there are only 570,000 participants in our province's college entrance examination, with an estimated admission rate exceeding 85%. This means that our province's education has entered a new stage, meaning that expanding educational opportunities is no longer the main contradiction in educational reform and development. The main contradiction has shifted to improving quality, which is the connotative development level. It is reported that in 2008, the number of people registered for our province's college entrance examination reached its peak, with over 780,000 people, after which it began to decrease annually.

In the past five years, 1.09 million people in our province have been admitted to undergraduate programs

During the "Eleventh Five-Year Plan" period, the number of people who participated in the cultural examination of the general college entrance examination was 3.685 million, with 2.606 million new students being admitted. More than 1.09 million were admitted to undergraduate programs. The undergraduate admission rate increased from 25.94% in 2006 to 37.23% in 2010, rising by nearly 12 percentage points. The overall admission rate for both undergraduate and junior college increased from 58.13% in 2006 to 79.72% in 2010, increasing by 21.59 percentage points.

The number of candidates in the 2011 college entrance examination decreased by 50,000 across the province

Source: Qingdao Morning Post

Yesterday, I learned from the Provincial Department of Education that the number of people registered for this year's college entrance examination in our province is 587,000, a decrease of 50,000 compared to last year, again setting a new low for the number of people registered for our province's college entrance examination. This is also the third consecutive year since 2008 that the number of people registered for the college entrance examination in our province has decreased.

Recent college entrance examination situations in Shandong Province

2010: 660,000 people, 79.72% admission rate, 39,300 admissions

2009: 700,000 people, 72.27% admission rate, 47,500 admissions

2008: 780,000 people, 65% admission rate, 53,100 admissions

2007: 778,000 people, 63.4% admission rate, 54,500 admissions, 76.77%

2006: 770,000 people, 64.4% admission rate, 56,900 admissions, 69.53%

2005: 731,000 people, 72.1% admission rate, 51,700 admissions

Note: After 2008, our city no longer publishes the college entrance examination admission rate (Li Shuai's table)

Two reasons for the reduction in numbers

It is introduced that the main reason for the continuous reduction in the number of college entrance examination candidates is the decreasing total number of eligible population in our province. This can be seen from the current number of students in high school. In 2010, the total number of senior three graduates in our province was only 550,000. The Provincial Department of Education's estimate of the number of college entrance examination participants in the coming years is also based on the number of high school students.
