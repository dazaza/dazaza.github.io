Feelings and Impressions at the Class Reunion

There are countless things in life that go against our wishes, and today such a thing happened to me. Yesterday, I had already prepared to attend a banquet at a classmate's house with my husband, but alas, the heavens did not favor us as the rain poured down incessantly, and heavily at that. The mood I had prepared yesterday was completely washed away by this heavy rain. I got up at five to wait for the rain to stop so we could set off. However, the rain kept falling without pause. This reminded me of Zhang Yu's song: "The rain keeps falling, and the atmosphere isn't harmonious." So, I went online to check if any classmates were around, since it takes two hours by car to reach their place, making the trip somewhat inconvenient.

Some classmates haven't seen each other for over twenty years, and I truly miss them! Meeting them is no easy feat because after graduation, we all went our separate ways, striving for the same goal - earning money to support our families, leaving us no choice but to be busy with our livelihoods. Fortunately, through the annual opportunity of children's promotions, combined with ample vacation time, we can finally have the chance to meet. But today, it rained heavily, and I lost heart, deciding not to go. Ha ha, I decided to give this opportunity to my husband, letting him go alone to meet up with the classmates. Watching my husband go out into the rain with an umbrella, I felt a weight lifted off my chest. At least I've given an explanation to the classmates there. By the time I'm writing this diary entry, the banquet might have already started and entered its main theme!

In my mind, I can picture the long-lost figures of my classmates, raising their glasses gracefully and laughing heartily. Although I cannot reunite with my classmates again, my heart of longing has already flown there. I only wish my classmates enjoy their reunion happily, and don't forget that I am here, deeply blessing you all! I also hope my husband can convey my sincere greetings on my behalf!

As we grow older, I wonder if you share the same feeling - I am becoming more emotional. A story or a song can make me shed tears. The feeling of seeing objects and thinking of people, or being moved by the scene, becomes increasingly intense. I am placing family ties and friendships at the forefront of my thoughts more and more. Some say I am a person full of emotions, and those who know me well say I am a person of sentiment. I do not deny this point. If people valued feelings and righteousness more, the world would be more harmonious, wouldn't it? What's wrong with that? Perhaps this kind of personality means you lack sharp edges and some individuality. But I would rather choose this way of being emotionally rich. Give up the cold smirk, and welcome every day of life.
