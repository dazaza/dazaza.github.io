Sanhao Follows Her Mother, Enters the Palace as a Slave

In the late Tang Dynasty, after Emperor Xianzong's death, Empress Guo (played by Xie Xuexin) with the help of the powerful eunuch Ma Yuanzhi, installed her son Li You as the new emperor, known as Emperor Muzong. However, Sanhao's (played by Charmaine Sheh) father supported Xianzong’s second son, Li Yun, to be the emperor and was later punished by Emperor Muzong, being exiled to the frontier. His wife, Jiang Caiqiong (played by Tabitha Ti), along with her seven-year-old daughter Sanhao and their maid Yao Jinling (played by Yang Yi), were enslaved in the palace. Renowned for generations for making exquisite jewelry, Caiqiong, having been trained since childhood, possessed skillful hands. She was assigned to the Si Zhen (Jewelry) department, responsible for the final process of Empress Dowager Guo's Phoenix Pearl Hairpin, using the luminous pearls as the phoenix eyes. One early spring day while enjoying flowers, Empress Dowager Guo used a brazier to keep warm. Suddenly, a nearby palace maid saw red wax dripping from the phoenix eyes and cried out that the phoenix was shedding blood. As a result, Caiqiong was beaten with sticks, and the head of the Si Zhen department was dismissed. Seriously injured and having caught a cold, Caiqiong fell gravely ill. On her deathbed, she longed to smell the fragrance of her hometown's flowers once more. To fulfill her wish, Sanhao went to the garden under the Qionghua tree. Just then, a Qionghua flower fell, which Sanhao picked up. Aunt Xu passed by and, without asking details, accused Sanhao of picking palace flowers and slapped her. Prince Li Yi (played by Chris Tang), the thirteenth younger brother of Emperor Muzong, saw this and, understanding Sanhao's filial heart, took the initiative to pick a flower for her. When Sanhao returned and offered the flower to Caiqiong, she was moved. In her dying moments, she reminded Sanhao to adhere to the principles of life - "Do good deeds, speak well, and have good intentions."
