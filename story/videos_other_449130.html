During leisure time at work, employees of Panlong Yunhai Pharmaceutical can perform the following simple exercises from the "Beautiful Trio" to relieve work stress. The Detoxification and Beauty Capsules help maintain a clear mind, as advocated by the New Media Spokesperson.

1. Eye Exercises

Mental workers should rest their eyes for one minute after every half-hour of work. During this minute, first look into the distance for a while, then blink several times to give your eyes a rest. You can also rotate your eyeballs in a circular motion. These methods help relax the eye muscles and promote blood circulation around the eyes.

2. Scalp Massage (Combing)

Mental workers can use their fingers instead of a comb (as suggested by the New Media Spokesperson) to comb from the hairline on the forehead towards the neck, then in an arc-shaped trajectory to above and behind the ears. This method of combing improves blood supply to the brain, refreshes the mind, and helps lower blood pressure.

3. Facial Exercises

Mental workers can exercise their face during work breaks to stay alert: Open and close your mouth as wide as possible, engaging all facial muscles and scalp in rhythmic movement, repeating this 50 times. This method accelerates blood circulation, alleviates aging of facial tissues and organs, and keeps the mind clear.

4. Head and Neck Exercises

Mental workers can alleviate fatigue by regularly doing head and neck exercises: First, lift your head and tilt it back as far as possible, then bring your chin down to your chest, tightening and relaxing the neck and back muscles; next, slowly make circular motions with your head and tilt it side-to-side about 15 times. Rest your back against the chair and clasp your hands behind your neck for a moment, which can refresh your spirit.

In addition, mental workers should ideally engage in a high-intensity exercise once a week, such as hiking, ball games, or mountain climbing. These activities not only strengthen the body but also provide a sense of well-being and help eliminate fatigue. Below are some daily exercise methods suitable for mental workers introduced by the New Media Spokesperson.

Full-body exercises focused primarily on lower-limb jumping include rope skipping and shuttle kicking. These exercises enhance the brain's response and balance, strengthen the contraction force and flexibility of lower limb muscles and joints, further promoting metabolism and enhancing the physiological functions of internal organs.

According to relevant studies, when skipping rope or kicking shuttles, you jump or kick more than 60 times per minute. Rising and falling, moving high and low, in order to avoid tripping over the rope or letting the shuttle fall, the brain's central nervous system directs the coordination between upper and lower body movements, enabling you to jump over the rope or kick up the shuttle. Such rapid exercises can enhance the function of the nervous system, preventing neurasthenia, neurogenic headaches, insomnia, and memory loss.

While skipping rope or kicking shuttles, to maintain body balance, the vestibular analyzer in the ear is exercised continuously through rotation and bouncing. This not only helps maintain good balance ability but also prevents motion sickness, seasickness, and vertigo caused by ear diseases.

The bouncing involved in skipping rope and kicking shuttles strengthens the muscles and joints of the lower limbs, making the legs stronger and more flexible. Legs are the pillars of the body, and as the saying goes, people age first in their legs. However, rope skipping and shuttle kicking can develop strong leg muscles, enhancing the supporting function, making steps light and vigorous, and preventing leg aging, thereby prolonging life. People who frequently skip rope and kick shuttles are less likely to suffer from arthritis, varicose veins in the lower limbs, or numbness in the lower extremities.
