PayPal - the most popular online payment tool

by qiuyitian on 2009-09-29 12:55:04

PayPal is an online payment tool under eBay, similar to Alipay on Taobao. In terms of global usage, there's no doubt that PayPal has the most users among online payment tools. Note: Although PayPal and Baofoo are from the same company, PayPal mainly serves a global audience, while Baofoo targets mainland China. Within mainland China, Alipay is still more practical as Baofoo can hardly match it and has little practical value. I'm introducing PayPal because its adoption rate is extremely high abroad, especially for accessing many free resources (especially free online earning opportunities) where PayPal is essential; you simply can't do without it!

If you don't plan to use PayPal to accept credit card payments, then applying for a personal account will suffice. Advanced accounts and business accounts, which can accept credit card payments, incur handling fees when receiving money. It's best to fill in your name using pinyin, which will be more convenient for future use. After completing registration, your account will be in an unverified state. To verify your account, you'll need a bank account with Industrial and Commercial Bank of China, Construction Bank, or招商Bank, or an international credit card (with Visa or MasterCard logos). Linking a domestic bank account allows you to verify PayPal and remove limits on payments and withdrawals. Linking an international credit card not only verifies PayPal and removes limits but also lets you pay using the funds in your credit card. If your PayPal account remains unverified, it's still fine for basic use, though there will be restrictions on payment and withdrawal amounts, and some merchants may not accept payments from unverified PayPal accounts.

Chinese users have several methods to withdraw funds from PayPal: bank wire transfer (fast but expensive), check (slow but low-cost), and finding someone to exchange (fastest, but involves exchange losses and safety risks). For those dealing with large amounts of money, it's recommended to apply for a dual-location One-Card-Through issued by the Hong Kong branch of China Merchants Bank for withdrawals. It’s not fast but has very low charges—3.5 HKD for amounts below 1000 HKD, and free for amounts over 1000 HKD. Additionally, the dual-location One-Card-Through from China Merchants Bank waives handling fees for transfers whether in HKD, USD, or CNY to personal or corporate accounts in Hong Kong or mainland China banks. To apply for the dual-location One-Card-Through, you need a permit for traveling to Hong Kong and Macau, and not all branches of China Merchants Bank offer this service. You can call China Merchants Bank's customer service hotline at 95555 and transfer to the dual-location One-Card-Through customer service for inquiries. Lastly, if you have any questions about PayPal, you can find answers on the eBay Foreign Trade Community.
