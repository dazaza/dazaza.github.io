7-Year-Old Girl Lives with Her Grandpa in a Public Toilet and Takes the Bus to School Alone Every Day

by xiaoyou029k on 2011-06-10 10:56:47

The little girl holding an iron spoon ready to scoop porridge and eat beamed at the camera. Netizens provided photos.

Yesterday, on Sina Weibo, this photo went viral: a little girl holding an iron spoon ready to scoop porridge and eat beamed at the camera.

The story of this child also spread wildly: she is seven years old and lives with her grandfather in a public restroom on Wenshu Road in XiaSha. Every day, she takes a one-hour bus ride to attend a migrant workers' children school. The smile that netizens called "touching the soul" originated from the fact that this day, she and her grandfather had porridge for dinner, "so we didn't have to eat instant noodles."

[Story]

Living in a public restroom with her grandfather

This photo was personally taken by Weibo user @ahLeileileilei. Under her guidance, at 2 PM yesterday, the reporter found the public restroom.

Directly opposite the restroom is the student cafeteria of China Jiliang University. At the entrance of the restroom, an uncle in a blue worker's uniform was kneeling down, using a wrench to fix a bicycle tire, his hands covered in grease.

The uncle's name is Tan Rongyun, from Xuzhou, Jiangsu Province, 58 years old, and he is the little girl's grandfather. "She will be back a bit later," the uncle said, warmly inviting the reporter inside to wait.

The place the uncle referred to as "inside" is actually the small room between the men's and women's restrooms. The room is only three square meters, and a bunk bed occupies most of the space, costing 30 yuan. Clothes and a small cabinet are piled on the top bunk, while blankets are on the bottom bunk. An old student desk stands nearby, with a 14-inch second-hand TV on it, and the chair doubles as a dining table.

With just the reporter and the uncle in the small room, it was already very crowded, unable to accommodate a third person. "I live here with my granddaughter, and usually, she is alone inside. If she sees me tired from work, she will go out to play, letting me lie down for a while," the uncle said.

The little girl has good grades

At around four o'clock, it was drizzling outside. A B4 bus arrived, and the uncle quickly ran to the platform with an umbrella. A little girl wearing a red coat and carrying a backpack with cartoon patterns hopped off the bus. This was the uncle's granddaughter. Hand in hand, the grandfather and granddaughter entered the house.

The little girl was not tall, with bright eyes and short hair. Her red coat had decorative bows, but two buttons were missing, and the pocket button was about to fall off. She giggled and told her grandfather, "Grandpa, we had an exam today. I did well in Chinese, but not so well in math, probably couldn't get over 90 points." Seeing someone else in the house, the little girl put down her bag and said respectfully, "Grandpa, I won't disturb you. I'll be outside; call me if you need anything."

The uncle smiled, his wrinkles filled with affection, saying, "Go ahead, don't go too far."

From XiaSha to Jiubao, going to school alone

The uncle told the reporter that his granddaughter's name is Xiao Wen, seven years old, and she just started second grade. "She is very sensible! Since she was six, she has been taking the bus to school alone, never making me worry."

In fact, when Xiao Wen first took the bus to school, the uncle was worried and secretly followed her on an electric scooter. "She studies in a migrant workers' elementary school in Jiubao. It takes ten stops on the bus, about an hour each way," the uncle said. After getting off the bus, she still has to walk for ten minutes and cross the street. "The first time she went to school, she fell asleep on the bus and missed her stop at DeSheng Road and YueYa Intersection. I was really worried!" The uncle said thankfully that she got off at the next station.

How could you let a five-year-old child take the bus to school alone?

The uncle said, mainly because the company rules state that he cannot leave his post for 24 hours. "There's really no other way," the uncle said apologetically.

The grandfather and granddaughter are both content

The uncle earns 1500 yuan per month, and Xiao Wen's tuition for a semester is 1560 yuan, plus 100 yuan for food expenses each month, which they can manage to scrape by. "Every day, we eat steamed buns, pancakes, shredded carrots, and rice, so our expenses aren't large. Therefore, my salary is enough to support her. However, she is too young, and her parents are working in Suzhou and rarely visit her. She feels lonely sometimes."

Living in a restroom saves on rent, and the uncle is content, because it would be a big expense for others. Moreover, the company never delays payment.

At this moment, an electric scooter stopped beside the public restroom, and the uncle grabbed the wrench from the head of the bed and ran out. "Sometimes, I secretly help people fix their tires to supplement household expenses," the uncle smiled and told the reporter. Actually, this is against company regulations, but now, he needs to earn more money because the child will attend university in the future.

[Interaction]

The radiant smile goes viral online

At around 6 PM, a college student came to the public restroom looking for the uncle. It turned out she was the Weibo user @ahLeileileilei. She introduced herself as Yao Yunjie, a senior student at Zhejiang Sci-Tech University. Last year, she noticed this optimistic and adorable little girl.

"Every afternoon at 4:30, Wenwen opens her pink MeiYangYang backpack and sits on the sidewalk in front of the restroom to do her homework. Sometimes, she reads the questions aloud clearly, and other times, she hums cheerful songs. Her desk is an old round-backed chair, with a broken corner fixed with a wooden stick. Thin sponge is exposed through the gaps in the chair's backrest and seat cushion."

This family, which urban children might see as struggling in poverty, and this little girl who always beams like a flower regardless of the circumstances, deeply moved Yao Yunjie.

Yao Yunjie uploaded the casually taken photo of the girl to the 19th Floor forum and then to Sina Weibo. Immediately, the smiling face went viral online. By 11 PM last night, within six hours, the post on Sina Weibo was forwarded over 800 times, with nearly 200 comments.

College students want to help Xiao Wen

Shi Tingzhi from Zhejiang Sci-Tech University's radio station and Yang Keji from the Student Union of China Jiliang University were also paying attention to Xiao Wen.

"I immediately contacted the uncle after seeing the post online," Yang Keji said. Everyone felt deeply moved by Xiao Wen's optimistic smile. The Student Union plans to hold a charity gala next week to raise donations for the little girl. In addition, the college students plan to contact the XiaSha Experimental Foreign Language Elementary School to arrange for teachers and students there to tutor Xiao Wen.

Seeing the child eating instant noodles every day, the college students feel heartbroken and plan to contact the canteen across the street to provide a hot dinner for the grandfather and granddaughter.

Recently, our newspaper published a report very similar to this incident.

On one side of the Yandao Avenue along the Qiantang River in XiaSha, there is a public restroom where 57-year-old Uncle Deng lives alone, responsible for cleaning duties. Uncle Deng is from Anqing, Anhui Province.

In 2009, Uncle Deng's only son lost his right hand in an accident, losing his ability to work, and his daughter-in-law left home as a result. Having worked in agriculture all his life, he took on the responsibility of raising his two grandsons. Both he and his wife work in Hangzhou. His wife is responsible for another restroom, 12 kilometers away. Due to the company rule of not being allowed to leave their posts for 24 hours, the couple hardly sees each other throughout the year. Phone calls became their only means of communication. To save money, they agreed that each phone call should not exceed three minutes.

Eventually, the student reporters from the Times University News decided to help Uncle Deng watch over the restroom, allowing him to reluctantly agree to take a trip to see his wife. This was their first meeting since coming to Hangzhou.
