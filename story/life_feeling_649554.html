For the last look at my old house

by lili1 on 2011-12-31 00:44:39

Finally, I took a last look at my old home.

In response to the development of the situation, the college has decided to relocate entirely to the city after more than 40 years of operation in the county town.

My family had a hard time moving because there were too many things, especially books. It took a whole week just to tie up and pack the books! But I couldn't bear to throw away even one of them, so I moved them all.

The new house in the city covers over 100 square meters with a reasonable layout, spacious and bright. After everything was settled, I wanted to go back to the county town for a last look at my old home.

I got the keys from the caretaker at the old home, unlocked the door, and found the four rooms empty and desolate!

I first walked into the bedroom. My youngest daughter was born here. At that time, the midday sun shone through the window, bright and warm, and the scene of bathing the baby came back to me. She splashed water in the basin, wetting a large area of the carpet, but she was so happy! Later, she grew up gradually, and her figure darting around each room seems like it was just yesterday.

I saw that windowsill! It was right there on the windowsill that I slapped my youngest daughter's bottom, turning it red! She was about four years old then, chubby and cute. In the summer heat, the windowsill was made of terrazzo and was cool to the touch. Unable to endure the dry heat of summer, she lay on the cool windowsill looking out. The window was still open at that time! If she fell from the fifth floor, what would have happened? I picked up my daughter and said: "You must not play up there, if you fall, you will never see dad and mom again!" Although she was young, she was always obedient and understanding. She cried and promised never to climb onto the windowsill again. Later, it was still hot, and she forgot my words completely and lay on the windowsill again. This time I scolded her again, and she sincerely promised to remember. However, two days later, she climbed onto the windowsill again. I felt the seriousness of the problem and decided that she must remember. So I slapped her little bottom hard! She must have been hurt, as she burst into tears! My wife blamed me for hitting her too hard. I said, "Is it better to hit her until she is red or better to let her fall from the fifth floor?" Actually, how could I not feel heartbroken for this obedient and precious daughter! Child, please don't blame Dad. It's because Dad can't bear to let you leave this home! Fortunately, after that, my youngest daughter never climbed onto the windowsill again! This was the only time I hit her in more than ten years. And it was right on that windowsill!

I pushed the door and stood on the south balcony for a while. Looking across, all the buildings were already empty. Only the trees by the roadside remained lush and green, their leaves swaying gently in the breeze under the sunshine. There were no pedestrians on the campus paths, except for a few sparrows chirping, and no other sounds could be heard, so cold and lonely! This reminded me of the lively scenes during past Spring Festivals, especially on New Year's Eve. Outside, the lights were dazzling, colorful lights lit up every window, and firecrackers popped continuously. People were laughing and celebrating happily. Through this window on the balcony, my daughter saw fireworks blooming outside, colorful and beautiful, and called excitedly: "Dad, look quickly, how beautiful!" The clear voice rang in my ears again! But now, it was so quiet before my eyes!

This room was where my father lived. His life was full of hardships and setbacks. But for the sake of our family, he was self-reliant and constantly fought against fate, raising us four siblings. In this room, my father and I often talked until late at night. He looked at the moon outside the window and kindly told me: "No matter how difficult the situation is, it will eventually pass." These words inspired me to overcome one difficulty after another, and I will never forget them throughout my life! Later, my father suffered from cerebral hemorrhage and became paralyzed. Also in this room, my father sat by the window eating meals. This was also where I changed his medicine and gave him massages. And the ground where I supported my father walking... Now, my father has left me forever! No matter how much I long for those moments of serving him by his side, they will never return! Recalling my father's indomitable spirit, his gentlemanly heart, and deep paternal love, tears quietly streamed down my cheeks.

After leaving this room, I walked around the other rooms. Every door handle, every lighting switch, every faucet, and even the places on the wall where the white lime peeled off were so familiar to me!

I went back into the study and saw the tube light in the study. It buzzed when turned on, and I've always wanted to fix it but never had the time. Now it doesn't need fixing anymore! In this very room, I wrote five technical books while working at my desk, which were officially published, and I published more than twenty papers. One patent was approved. Here, I completed all the work I should do in my life. I remember saying in a class of 150 university students in June: "This pulse digital circuit class is the last class of my life. After class, I will retire!" Initially, the students were surprised for a moment, then applause gradually rose, echoing throughout the classroom. At that time, I had countless feelings. Was it to retain me or to bid farewell?

My son took the entrance exam for Harbin Institute of Technology here. After graduation, he got married and spent his honeymoon with his wife here. Soon they both stepped out of the door together and went to the other side of the earth.

In this study, I often talked with my son until midnight. He said: "Dad, do you know? When I was a child, I had a lot of complaints about you!"

"Really? Why?" I asked in surprise and confusion.

"Whenever I saw other children playing outside, I had to memorize words and do math problems! I thought I didn't have a childhood, just because I had such a father!" But he continued: "Now I see that Dad was right. Otherwise, how could I achieve today's success!"

I was overwhelmed with emotion! In fact, I knew the child lacked the joy of childhood, but China's education system relies solely on that test paper, thousands crossing a narrow bridge! For the sake of the child getting into college and having a future, it was an unavoidable action! Hearing these words, I felt that my son had become sensible and grown up! Now he can stand on his own abroad, it's the result of learning to strive hard in adversity.

After passing multiple exams, my son obtained approval for his visa to study abroad. I still clearly remember the scene when the whole family sent him off.

At the green channel entrance of Beijing Airport, he finally held my hand for a long time, firmly and deeply said: "Dad, take care!" Watching his tall and broad back, I felt melancholy. This mountain that I could rely on has gone to the other side of the ocean! I turned around and saw my daughter squatting beside, covering her face and crying: "Dad, I don't want my brother to go! I miss my brother!"

That scene, I will never forget!

It was also in this room that I kept receiving emails from my son across the ocean. He said: "In the future, I will take care of my younger sister's schooling, I will definitely bring her overseas to finish university and make her better than me in the future!" I know, the sibling bond is deep, but Dad forcibly separated them!

Fighting alone in a foreign land, I told him: "Remember, no matter how difficult the situation is, it will eventually pass."

His longing for me increased day by day. In his reply email, he said: "I feel that we are not only father and son, but also close friends and master-apprentice, etc. It's like two parts of the same life body, so closely intertwined that it's impossible to distinguish which part belongs to whom." I love him, not only because he is my son, but also because he is my friend!

In this old house, my wife was persecuted and laid off for two years, often sitting on the living room sofa shedding silent tears, muttering to herself, nearly breaking down mentally! I told my wife: "Don't worry, as long as I am still alive, we won't starve! No matter how difficult the situation is, it will eventually pass." Sure enough, a righteous director of the institute restored her job, allowing our family to live a normal life again. We tried to forget the unpleasant past and brought laughter back to our home. On the stage of power and politics, once the small-minded people who lust for fame and wealth gain power, those who refuse to humble themselves will inevitably be harmed!
