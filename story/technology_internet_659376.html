Construction Machinery Companies Battle in Jiangsu, the Cluster Effect is Endlessly Attractive (1)

by lljrkkgfew2 on 2012-02-06 10:08:01

Construction machinery companies converge on Jiangsu: the charm of cluster effect - Market Observation - Industry News - Building Materials Network. With Liugong's high-profile entry into Changzhou, people's attention has been drawn to this fertile ground for construction machinery in Changzhou. However, Caterpillar's subsequent plan to expand its excavator plant has even more "shocked" the entire construction machinery industry. Observers may notice that Jiangsu Province, located on the eastern coast, is increasingly favored by construction machinery companies. Is it a coincidence, or does it really harbor unlimited business opportunities?

On June 21, 2010, Guangxi Liugong held a groundbreaking ceremony for its eastern R&D and manufacturing base in Changzhou, Jiangsu. It was reported that Liugong established a wholly-owned subsidiary, Changzhou Liugong, with an investment of 280 million yuan, to construct its eastern R&D and manufacturing base in Changzhou, Jiangsu. The base mainly focuses on the production and research of construction machinery and related components, targeting the East China region and overseas markets. The establishment of Changzhou Liugong Excavator Co., Ltd., and the groundbreaking ceremony for Liugong's eastern R&D and manufacturing base marked the completion of Liugong's strategic layout in the east. Its product range expanded to include the research and manufacture of excavators, which will have incalculable strategic impacts on Liugong's future development.

On June 29, 2010, Caterpillar announced its plan to expand the excavator factory located in Xuzhou, China, supporting the company's long-term global strategy and meeting customer demand for excavators. As part of its overall excavator strategy, Caterpillar also reached an agreement with XCMG Group to purchase 15.87% equity in Caterpillar (Xuzhou) Co., Ltd. By the time the expansion plan is completed in 2014, Caterpillar's excavator production capacity in China will be quadrupled.

Liugong's eastward expansion and Caterpillar's expansion highlight the significance of Jiangsu's construction machinery market. In recent years, far more than just Liugong and Caterpillar have chosen to invest and set up factories in Jiangsu. Investing in Jiangsu seems to have become a popular trend in the industry. What exactly makes Jiangsu so attractive that it draws so many companies? Let me analyze it as follows:

Geographical Advantage

From a geographical perspective, Jiangsu has unparalleled advantages. It borders Shanghai, China's largest economic center, to the south, connects with the Bohai Rim region to the north, faces Northeast Asia across the sea to the east, and links to the New Eurasia Land Bridge and the Yangtze River Golden Waterway to the west. It is a strategic chokepoint for the coastal access of the Longhai-Lanxin corridor and is also part of the Yangtze River Delta region. The well-connected transportation network significantly reduces logistics costs for construction machinery enterprises and provides a good platform for their overseas strategic layout through convenient maritime transport.

Thanks to such excellent geographical location, China's largest construction machinery brand enterprise, XCMG Group, is based in Xuzhou, Jiangsu. Additionally, several other domestic and foreign joint venture construction machinery manufacturers, such as Caterpillar Xuzhou Co., Ltd., XCMG Liebherr Co., Ltd., XCMG Rotec Bearings Co., Ltd., and XCMG Meritor Axle Co., Ltd., are also located here. With a large number of complete machine enterprises, corresponding component enterprises naturally emerge. It is reported that apart from XCMG Group, there are nearly 400 large-scale construction machinery enterprises in the city, employing over 100,000 people.

Changzhou, another advantageous geographical location, is the second-largest construction machinery production base after Xuzhou and is home to China's second-largest construction machinery enterprise group, Changlin Group. Located at the northern end of China's most developed "Suxi-Chang" industrial belt, Changzhou municipal government considers construction machinery as one of its pillar industries and gives it key support. Therefore, Changzhou has a very good investment environment for construction machinery. Renowned international construction machinery companies have successively invested and set up factories in Changzhou, making Changzhou one of China's most promising construction machinery manufacturing bases. Here, a large number of construction machinery enterprises such as Hyundai Jiangsu Construction Machinery Co., Ltd. and Komatsu (Changzhou) Construction Machinery Co., Ltd. have gathered, making Changzhou's status as a construction machinery base increasingly prominent.

It can be seen that in Xuzhou and Changzhou, Jiangsu, there is a very good foundation for the development of construction machinery, with a complete industrial chain and numerous whole machine and parts manufacturers. With its well-connected transportation and coastal advantages, Jiangsu has become a true treasure land for the development of construction machinery.

Local Government Support

The natural geographical advantage and convenient transportation hub form the basic conditions for Jiangsu as a major province of China's construction machinery production. On this basis, the local government's support for the construction machinery industry is indispensable. Taking Xuzhou as an example, after the Spring Festival in 2010, the Huaihai Construction Machinery Equipment Market, a key planned project in the Xuzhou Economic Development Zone, was fully launched. This market mainly includes sales of whole machines, parts, as well as leasing, logistics, and related businesses. Once completed, it will become the most influential professional construction machinery market in the Huaihai Economic Zone. Imagine, the establishment of such a uniquely scaled professional construction machinery market will bring tremendous appeal to the attraction of construction machinery investment. Perhaps in the near future, a construction machinery industry belt radiating from Xuzhou in all directions will be formed, all of which cannot be separated from the strong support of the local government for the construction machinery industry.

In Jiangsu Province's "Eleventh Five-Year Plan," it was clearly stipulated that equipment manufacturing should be prioritized for development, listing construction machinery as one of the key targets for development. At the same time, it supports Xuzhou in developing heavy trucks and supports the construction machinery bases in Xuzhou, Changzhou, and Zhenjiang. Moreover, the Eleventh Five-Year Plan stipulates that railways should be vigorously constructed, with approximately 1,000 kilometers of new railway lines. A total of 1,200 kilometers of expressways will be built, 3,000 kilometers of trunk roads will be newly built or expanded, and 40,000 kilometers of rural roads will be newly built or renovated. This will initially form a modern expressway network, a smooth trunk road network, and a well-connected rural road network. The new railways and highways not only increase Jiangsu Province's demand for construction machinery products but also provide faster and more convenient transportation guarantees for the development of the construction machinery industry.

Industry Agglomeration Effect

The initial sprouting of a region's industrial development requires government support. However, as the entire market continues to grow and develop, matching industries will form a clustering effect, with significant aggregation and radiation effects. After XCMG Group settled in Xuzhou and then established its subsidiaries and joint ventures, the increasing number of whole machine enterprises inevitably led to a greater demand for components. Some component enterprises saw the business opportunity and also settled in Xuzhou, providing locally available component products for the whole machine enterprises. Then, when the component enterprises reach a certain scale, they attract many whole machine enterprises to invest here, reducing production costs. Like a snowball rolling down a hill, the industrial radiation effect becomes increasingly powerful. When it is no longer limited to one place, surrounding cities respond to this demand and begin developing and expanding the construction machinery market. Just like the success of Xuzhou's construction machinery market, surrounding areas such as Changzhou, Zhenjiang, and Kunshan have also become fertile grounds for the investment and development of construction machinery enterprises. Such corporate and industrial agglomeration effects and regional radiation actions drive the development of the entire region. It is precisely because of this step-by-step virtuous development that Jiangsu's construction machinery industry has grown stronger. I believe that the formation of industrial clusters occurs mainly in the following three ways:

1. Establishing Joint Ventures

Taking Changzhou as an example, the first company to settle in Changzhou was China's second-largest construction machinery enterprise group - Changlin Group. Its predecessor was the original Forestry Department Changzhou Forestry Machinery Factory, founded in 1961. Starting from 1994, Changlin successively established joint ventures with Hyundai Heavy Industries Co., Ltd. of South Korea, forming "Changzhou Hyundai Construction Machinery Co., Ltd." and "Hyundai (Jiangsu) Construction Machinery Co., Ltd.", and with Kobelco Construction Machinery Co., Ltd. of Japan, establishing "Komatsu (Changzhou) Construction Machinery Co., Ltd.", producing construction machinery products such as excavators, large-tonnage loaders, and dump trucks with international advanced levels. In 2004, Changlin also established a joint venture in Malaysia, forming "Changlin (Malaysia) Construction Machinery Co., Ltd.", extending the production and sales of Changlin's construction machinery products further into Southeast Asia. In 2002, Changlin formed Changlin Construction Machinery Group, with Changlin Company at its core and Changzhou Hyundai Construction Machinery Co., Ltd., Hyundai (Jiangsu) Construction Machinery Co., Ltd., and Komatsu (Changzhou) Construction Machinery Co., Ltd. as its backbone domestic well-known construction machinery host enterprises. Currently, it includes nearly 47 construction machinery whole machine and component manufacturers, with employees reaching 11,000, total assets amounting to 7.96 billion yuan, and construction machinery industry centered around Changlin becoming one of Changzhou City's pillar industries. Changzhou area has thus become one of the six major domestic construction machinery R&D and production manufacturing bases. From the development history of Changlin Company, we can see that after a company grows larger and stronger, it will continuously expand its industrial chain, and cooperation with other companies is inevitable. In Changzhou, more and more construction machinery joint ventures have emerged, creating a clustering effect where large companies can lead and attract investments from other companies.

2. Whole Machine Drives Components

Komatsu (Changzhou) Construction Machinery Co., Ltd. is a Sino-foreign joint venture enterprise invested and established in Changzhou in 1995 by Kobelco Construction Machinery Co., Ltd., the world's second-largest supplier manufacturer in the construction machinery industry. On June 10, 2009, the new factory of Komatsu (Changzhou) Construction Machinery Co., Ltd., covering an area of 300,000 square meters, officially broke ground in the Changzhou Electromechanical Industrial Park of the High-Tech Zone, forming an annual production scale of 7,000 units of construction machinery. Following closely, on June 28, 2010, Changzhou Shengdai Machinery Co., Ltd., which provides supporting services for Komatsu Construction Machinery (Changzhou) Co., Ltd., laid its foundation in the Electromechanical Industrial Park of the High-Tech Zone. Changzhou Shengdai Machinery Co., Ltd. is a wholly foreign-owned enterprise invested and established by Japan's Shengdai Fuses Co., Ltd. in the High-Tech Zone, with a total investment of 195 million US dollars and registered capital of 7.8 million US dollars, mainly engaged in the manufacture and sale of construction machinery, industrial machinery, and its parts, expected to start production in 2011. Currently, the Komatsu Industrial Park is under intensive construction, expected to be completed and open by the end of the year, and at that time, Shengdai Machinery will provide relevant component supporting services for Komatsu. It can be seen from this that the gathering of whole machine enterprises will attract more component enterprises to develop, forming an industrial cluster effect.

3. City Driving City

As shown in the figure above, with Changzhou as the center, it drives the development of surrounding cities such as Zhenjiang, Wuxi, and Kunshan into construction machinery enterprise bases. In 2000, Guangxi Liugong's roller division was built in Jiangyin City. In 2003, a concrete machinery production base was built in Yangzhou, Jiangsu, and in the same year, Guangxi Liugong Group placed its small machinery production base in Zhenjiang City. In 2008, Perkins, a world-renowned engine producer, opened its new factory in Wuxi, announcing the official establishment of its production base in China. In June 2010, Perkins' second production line in China went into operation, making Wuxi the only factory producing Perkins' Series 1100 6-cylinder engines. In Kunshan City, in 2002, Sany Group's Sany Heavy Industry Co., Ltd. was established here. Sany Heavy Industry is a core subsidiary integrating construction machinery production, sales, and R&D, producing and selling construction machinery products such as excavators.

It is not difficult to see that the industrial cluster effect of the construction machinery industry in Jiangsu Province is played out to the fullest extent, and it is developing towards an even larger regional market.

Battle for Jiangsu

In a place where similar products gather, each whole machine enterprise and even each component enterprise will face fierce and severe competition. How to maintain its share and reputation in the intense market is the first consideration for every enterprise. Especially since the beginning of 2010, the booming construction machinery market has further intensified this competitive awareness. Every company wants to get a piece of the market, and everyone hopes to get the biggest "cake". Taking excavators as an example, at the end of 2008, China proposed a 4 trillion yuan investment to deal with the financial crisis, and in 2010, it entered the critical implementation year. Judging from the new projects started in the first three months of 2010, both the number of projects and the project investment amounts increased significantly compared to the same period last year. These engineering projects have gradually entered the construction phase, leading to a new peak in demand for construction machinery products such as excavators.

Secretary-General Li Hongbao of the Excavator Branch of the China Construction Machinery Industry Association also expressed optimism about the excavator market in 2010 in media interviews. He stated that the growth rate of excavators this year would be much higher than in 2009, and market opportunities would outweigh challenges. Opportunities come from the continued release of 588.5 billion yuan of new funds in 2010 from the national 4 trillion yuan investment, exceeding the 487.5 billion yuan of new central funds in 2009; from regional economic revitalization plans and urbanization processes; and from the global economic recovery in 2010, with the export market expected to show restorative growth, and excavator exports still having significant growth opportunities. The market sentiment is generally optimistic.

Not only is the demand for excavators increasing, but there is also a large market prospect for construction machinery products such as loaders, forklifts, and bulldozers. In the face of such a rich "big cake", how could construction machinery companies not be tempted? Especially for companies producing similar products, the competition will be even more intense. It is understood that in Changzhou alone, companies producing medium and small tonnage excavators include Liugong, Hyundai, Komatsu, and Yuchai. In Xuzhou, there is XCMG Group and Caterpillar planning to expand its excavator production capacity. Surrounding Kunshan has gathered Sany Heavy Industry... So many large and powerful construction machinery companies have gathered in Jiangsu to research, develop, produce, and sell excavators. The author can't help but ask, how to become the "darling of the market"? We may not know the answer to this question temporarily, but what is worth looking forward to is that in Jiangsu, this major province of construction machinery production, an intense construction machinery "battle" is bound to take place.
