China Mobile teams up with Japanese and Korean companies to promote TD-SCDMA technology overseas

by b48560406 on 2009-11-22 22:24:16

Sina Technology News, evening of November 20 Beijing time - According to foreign media reports today, China Mobile is currently testing the TD-SCDMA network with its Japanese and Korean partners. This is one of the measures to promote China's self-developed 3G technology overseas.

Currently, China Mobile is the only mobile operator in the world using TD-SCDMA technology, but the company hopes more mobile operators will experiment with this 3G technology. China Mobile is the world's largest mobile operator by number of users.

Wang Jianzhou, chairman of China Mobile, said at a press conference in Shenzhen on Thursday: "We are working very hard to encourage these tests." China Mobile has taken steps to prompt network equipment and mobile phone manufacturers to support this technology, even though these manufacturers already widely support other 3G technologies.

In order to reduce dependence on foreign technology, the Chinese government is encouraging the development of self-developed 3G technologies such as TD-SCDMA and helping TD-SCDMA overcome some obstacles in its development through R&D subsidies and industry development funds. For example, Gao Nianshu, general manager of China Mobile Data Department, said that a special incentive fund aimed at promoting the development of TD-SCDMA mobile phones has prompted various mobile phone manufacturers to bring out 11 new smartphones. These new smartphones include Motorola Snapper, Samsung GT-i8180C, LG GW880, and Dopod 8388. These phones are currently being tested and are expected to be available by the end of the year. These new phones also include five low-cost TD-SCDMA phones.

Wang Jianzhou said that the lack of affordable TD-SCDMA smartphones is one of the reasons for the slow growth in 3G users. He hopes to see smartphones priced around RMB 1,000 (approximately $147) in the Chinese market as soon as possible. Wang Jianzhou refused to disclose the names of Japan and South Korea partners currently collaborating with China Mobile. In July, China Potevio, a Chinese network equipment manufacturer, signed an agreement to operate a trial TD-SCDMA network in Italy. (Yifei)
