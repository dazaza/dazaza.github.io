by yhsh3768 on 2012-03-06 00:33:31

AC Milan crushed Palermo 4-0 away, with Ibrahimovic completing a hat-trick in the first half, showing great form. In the second half, Thiago Silva's header sealed the score at 4-0. The offensive end was sharp, and the defensive end kept Palermo off the scoreboard. However, does Milan's clean sheet in this match really mean that their defense is impregnable?

Five players took turns on the backline for Milan in this match. Abate played steadily, Antonini and Mesbah performed satisfactorily, and Silva shone brightly. Besides scoring a goal, the Brazilian center-back also had a direct free-kick in the second half that nearly broke through Palermo's goal. Compared to them, Bonera, who played as the right center-back, could be considered a relatively weak link. Several of Palermo's threatening attacks all broke through from Bonera's side.

At the 11-minute mark, Palermo's No.5 Barreto dribbled past Bonera with a clever move outside the penalty area and shot, which was collected by Abbiati, as shown in Figures 1 and 4.

From Figures 1 to 4, we can see that Barreto cleverly broke free from the tight spot between Bonera and Ambrosini before shooting. Especially in Figure 2, Ambrosini was in a side-on defensive position while Bonera was in a face-on defensive stance. However, Bonera easily stretched his foot out and was bypassed, even giving up the most critical central area. If Bonera had chosen to defend inside and let go of the outside, forcing Barreto to the outer line, it would have at least deviated his ball-carrying route from the goal, reducing the shooting angle.

At the 24 minute 30 second mark, Palermo gained a free-kick on the right side of the field. Miccoli delivered the ball and No.19 Budan headed towards the far post just slightly missing the post, as shown in Figures 5 and 6.

Bonera's body posture at that time was completely facing the goal, making it impossible to see the situation behind him, and it would be difficult to jump high while retreating.

In the 60th minute, Palermo executed a smooth and brilliant play, but Barreto's left-footed direct shot was denied by the post. Aguiregaray first bypassed Ambrosini, who seemed to be standing still in defense, then passed the ball straight to Miccoli, which slid past Bonera's feet... At that moment, Montari was already blocking Aguiregaray's passing route. All Bonera needed to do was choose a slightly offset position from Montari, thereby restricting Aguiregaray's passing routes from both sides. But Bonera's positioning and choice were not ideal, as shown in Figures 7 to 10.

At the 65th minute, Palermo coordinated on the left wing. Aguiregaray smoothly bypassed Bonera after receiving and sending a through pass, but it was slightly too powerful and was collected by Abbiati, as shown in Figures 11 to 13.

As seen in Figure 12, Bonera once again failed to defend inside and let go of the outside, allowing the opponent to easily break through from the inside line.

Although he is not yet steady or bold enough, Bonera has improved significantly compared to himself. However, if it weren't for the poor shooting skills and bad luck of Palermo's players, Milan's goal would have been breached.
