"Without him, my world has collapsed."

In the mind of Wang Ying, a 39-year-old woman from Sichuan, her husband Li Bing was her entire world.

He helped her earn money to fund her younger brother's education; he borrowed money to treat her illness; eventually, he brought her from the mountains to Beijing. Wang Ying never imagined that one day this reliance would suddenly disappear.

On the morning of August 3, 2007, a sudden misfortune struck, and Li Bing was stabbed during a robbery and died on the spot.

To continue her husband's mission of supporting their two children’s education and taking care of her in-laws who were already in their seventies, after handling the funeral arrangements, Wang Ying stayed in Beijing alone, working tirelessly day and night. However, misfortunes never come singly. Just a month before the verdict, an earthquake struck her hometown in Sichuan, collapsing three rooms of their old house. Her in-laws had no choice but to live in a makeshift tent.

On June 16, 2008, the two murderers were sentenced in the First Intermediate People's Court, ordered to jointly compensate Li Bing's family with a total of 310,000 yuan in economic losses. However, due to the defendants' impoverished families, not a single penny has been paid so far. Without any income, the children risked dropping out of school. Wang Ying refused to give up. This strong woman supported her family, which included elderly parents and young children, all by herself through foot massages. For a full 3 years and 7 months, she never took a holiday or returned home for a family reunion. Sitting on the edge of the bed, Wang Ying wiped away her tears and quickly pulled her hand back. Those hands, severely deformed from long-term labor, were filled with calluses.

**Hardship - Dropping out of school after her father's death**

In a damp basement of a residential complex in Haidian District, the sound of the public washing machine rhythmically echoed, and wastewater seeping from the toilet had accumulated in large patches in the hallway. Wang Ying's employee dormitory was located at the very end of the passage.

Each dormitory had four bunk beds, divided into two sides tightly against the walls, leaving only enough space for one person to pass. Under dim lighting, Wang Ying sat on the edge of the bed, recounting past events with a painful expression on her face as she took a deep breath. Bazhong City is located in the northwest corner of Sichuan Province, in the mountainous area. The village where Wang Ying lived, Taibai Village in Yuxi Town, Baizhou County, was even more remote. Every year, when returning home, she would take the train southward from Beijing. After a 26-hour train ride to Guangyuan, followed by a 5-hour bus ride winding into the mountains, and finally a 5-hour motorcycle ride, the village was nestled halfway up the mountain, at an average altitude of 2000 meters, which was Wang Ying and Li Bing's home.

Twelve years ago, Wang Ying followed this path to Beijing with her husband; for eight years, the couple traveled along the same road, bringing their hard-earned savings back to their hometown, handing them over to their parents and children. However, since Li Bing "left," Wang Ying hasn't returned to her hometown, making it a total of 3 years and 7 months now. "Twenty years ago, the entire village had a population of nearly 200 people." Wang Ying recalled that there were four people in her family: her parents, herself, and her younger brother.

When Wang Ying was still young, her father suffered from hypertension and colon cancer, unable to do heavy work; her younger brother was also young, with no able-bodied labor force, and the family's life was always very difficult, often unable to pay tuition fees. Until 1990, when Wang Ying's father was again diagnosed with kidney failure and underwent several major surgeries, he passed away in March of the same year. That year, Wang Ying was 18 and had just entered high school. With the loss of the mainstay of the family and her younger brother still in elementary school, Wang Ying made the tough decision to stop attending school and return home to help her mother with household chores. Seeing the plight of the children, relatives helped arrange a marriage for Wang Ying, and the other party was Li Bing.

**Love - Husband borrows money to treat his wife**

Wang Ying knew from a young age that just over 100 meters away, across a small hill, lived a family surnamed Li. The "head of the household" was the village team leader, and the youngest child was a boy named Li Bing, who was a few years older than her.

Every winter and spring, when the water level in the mountains decreased, Li Bing's father would carry buckets and go to the stone well in front of Wang Ying's house to fetch spring water, sometimes accompanied by Li Bing, but they never spoke. "Back then, the village was very conservative, and parental discipline was strict, so it was impossible to even glance at him more than once. I only remember he was skinny, about 160 cm tall." Talking about her husband, the already 40-year-old Wang Ying counted on her fingers, looking up and smiling shyly.

Li Bing was very amiable. Although young, anyone in the village with disputes would find him, and he would resolve them. He was also very enthusiastic. In the spring, when it was difficult to get water for farming in the mountains, he led a group of young people to dig a pond (water reservoir), solving the irrigation problem for half the village. However, for some reason, every time he saw Wang Ying, Li Bing became speechless.

During the day, Li Bing worked in the fields and on the mountains helping his father with household chores. In the afternoon, he would carry farming tools and run to Wang Ying's house to continue working, chopping firewood, plowing fields, digging sweet potatoes, finishing his tasks, wiping the sweat, and leaving without saying much. Hearing that Wang Ying's younger brother didn’t have tuition fees, Li Bing tied a linen bag around his waist and went alone into the mountains to pick pine nuts. After being busy for several days, he sold them for 20 yuan in town and handed the money to Wang Ying.

In 1993, Wang Ying officially married Li Bing and gave birth to their first daughter, Li Qing, by the end of the year. Just after welcoming their first daughter, before she could enjoy the joy of becoming a mother, Wang Ying was diagnosed with peritoneal tuberculosis and intestinal adhesion by the hospital. She lay in bed in pain every day, unable to sit up, weighing only 60 pounds. To reduce his wife's suffering, Li Bing spent all the family's savings and borrowed money everywhere to treat Wang Ying. In 1995, when the average monthly income of local villagers was less than 100 yuan, Li Bing had already incurred debts of five to six thousand yuan. To repay the debt, Li Bing brought Wang Ying to Beijing. "He came out to work to repay the debt for me." Turning around, covering her face, Wang Ying burst into tears.

**Reliance - "Marrying him, I felt very happy"**

A sky-blue down jacket, the corners washed bright, Wang Ying gently smoothed it down along the zipper. She couldn't remember how many winters this coat had seen, but she couldn't bring herself to replace it because it was picked out for her by her husband.

At the end of last month, Wang Ying spent dozens of yuan to buy a pair of white sneakers at the market. It was the first pair of shoes she bought for herself in ten years in Beijing. Previously, these things were always bought for her by Li Bing.

"He knew my clothing and shoe sizes." Wang Ying remembered that the things her husband bought for her were always perfectly fitting.

In 1999, when their son Li Jun was just three and a half years old, Wang Ying left the children with her in-laws and moved to Beijing with her husband to make a living.

After leaving the train station, her husband walked ahead, and Wang Ying carried a small bag following closely behind. At every traffic light, Li Bing would stop and hold her hand, instructing her gently, "Beijing has a lot of cars, it's different from home. Be careful when crossing the street."

Passing by a pastry stall, Wang Ying slowed down and looked twice. Li Bing stopped and bought a little bit of everything, putting it in a cloth bag, then continued walking, holding Wang Ying's hand.

"With him, I never had to say what I wanted; he just knew." Red-eyed, Wang Ying quietly reminisced.

"Marrying him, I felt very happy." After a brief silence, a rare smile appeared on Wang Ying's face.

**Tragedy**

**Husband killed by club security guards**

"I'll go to my sister's place early tomorrow morning to deliver the train tickets. On my way back, I'll buy you some fatty pork. Bitter melon stir-fried with pork slices. Sleep well." Li Bing folded his clothes while softly speaking to his wife. Wang Ying responded and fell asleep.

"I didn't know that was the last thing he said to me." Wang Ying covered her mouth, paused silently for a moment, and then burst into uncontrollable sobbing.

On the morning of August 3, 2007, Wang Ying was still sound asleep in bed when Li Bing locked the door and left.

It was still dark, on Yongfeng Middle Road in Haidian District, two figures wandered aimlessly. Just as they turned out of the alley, Li Bing lowered his head and quietly walked forward.

"Hey, buddy, stop, I need to ask you something." Suddenly, a voice from behind startled Li Bing, who didn't turn around but quickened his pace instead. The two men caught up, blocking him, with knives tucked in their waists.

After exchanging a few words, the three started wrestling. The two strangers dragged Li Bing a few meters away into a small grove of trees nearby and stabbed him in the left chest. Li Bing's belongings were robbed.

Perhaps around 10 o'clock in the morning, Wang Ying had just woken up when several policemen knocked on her door with a photo in hand. She only glanced down briefly and immediately fell to her knees, "This is my husband!"

The investigation proceeded exceptionally smoothly, and the next morning, the police arrested the two suspects at a club in Haidian District. Both were 19 and 16 years old respectively, both security guards at the club.

On June 16, 2008, the case was heard in the First Intermediate People's Court. The court sentenced the two defendants to 15 and 14 years in prison respectively for intentional injury and ordered them to jointly compensate a total of 310,000 yuan in economic losses. After the verdict, the parties did not appeal.

However, due to the extreme poverty of the defendants' families, not a single cent of the 310,000 yuan was compensated.

"Without him, my world has collapsed." Wang Ying's pain was evident on her face.

**Farewell**

**Saying goodbye to her family and staying in Beijing to work**

Upon hearing the news of her son's death, Li Bing's father in their distant hometown in Bazhong immediately stopped his farm work and rushed to the bus station with his granddaughter. Due to the urgency of departure, they didn't have time to prepare anything, only carrying a few crumpled hundred-yuan bills given by neighbors.

By the time they reached Beijing West Station two days later, Wang Ying met the elder at the station. Their eyes met, and they cried uncontrollably. The old man, with white hair and dirty clothes, stared at her blankly.

"Dad!" Wang Ying called out twice, pulling him, but the old man seemed completely deaf, tightly closing his lips and stiffening his face.

Not until entering the morgue, where the staff opened the plastic bag and revealed his son lying inside, did the old man speak, "Son, Dad has come to see you off," and then collapsed, crying uncontrollably.

Leaning against the white wall, seeing her aged father-in-law and her 15-year-old daughter standing before her, Wang Ying barely managed to stand, vowing silently within herself, "I must stay in Beijing to earn money. I must support this family."

After borrowing money to handle her husband's funeral, Wang Ying sent her father-in-law and daughter onto the train bound for Guangyuan. Suppressing her tears, Wang Ying placed the urn in her father-in-law's hands, "Dad, please send him off one more time for me."

After losing her husband, her hometown was hit by an earthquake. To support the elderly and her children, Wang Ying earned money through foot massage, working day and night until her fingers became deformed. She said,

"These hands have supported our entire family."

Wang Ying stayed in Beijing. Less than a month after her husband's murder, she returned to work. To support her elderly in-laws and save enough money for her children's tuition, she worked tirelessly giving foot massages.

Since her husband's death, due to overwork and lack of sleep, she frequently fainted and was rescued several times on the street by passersby.

After learning about Wang Ying's situation, Judge Lai Qi from the First Intermediate People's Court immediately requested assistance from the High Court Criminal Division One, applying for funds from the Minors Litigation Assistance Fund. The materials were submitted to the judges of the High Court Criminal Division One, and after approval, the foundation donated 20,000 yuan to Wang Ying's two children, specifically for their study and living expenses.

"With this money, the children's tuition for this year is finally secured!" Receiving the 20,000 yuan in cash, Wang Ying was moved to tears, sending all the money, along with thousands of yuan she had saved, back to her hometown.

However, disasters kept coming. A few days ago, Wang Ying was diagnosed with bilateral uterine fibroids and needed immediate surgery.

Sticking the diagnosis certificate into her backpack, Wang Ying dared not undergo the surgery, "The money should be saved for the elders' medical treatment and the children's tuition."

**Remembrance - "No one can compare to him in my heart"**

Li Bing was gone, and for Wang Ying, who regarded her husband as her entire world, everything changed. Opening her palm-sized notebook, the transportation routes to the court, train station, supermarket, etc., were densely written. Since her husband left, Wang Ying always carried this notebook with her, recording any important matters on it. In this way, she learned to survive in this city and tried to adapt to life without Li Bing.

"At first, after leaving the bath center, I didn't know anything else." Wang Ying recalled that in the winter of 2007, right after her husband's death, she often got on the wrong bus, "Previously, whenever we went out, he would lead me. Now that he's gone, no one cares about me anymore."

Besides taking buses, Wang Ying began learning to shop at the supermarket with her sisters, asking about the cheapest vegetable prices; going to the bank alone to deposit money; buying phone cards and recharging them to call home.

Every time she learned something new or was instructed to do something, Wang Ying recorded it in her little notebook, worried that if she forgot, she wouldn't have the courage to ask others again.

Gradually, Wang Ying learned to live independently. Changing her workplace, moving far away from everyone who knew her story, she never mentioned the past. Every day, Wang Ying worked hard, trying to keep herself busy until she was exhausted and fell asleep. But her husband's shadow always lingered, and whenever she had a moment of leisure, memories of the past dozen years with him flooded her mind.

Family and friends around her tried to introduce her to someone else, but Wang Ying always refused. "In my heart, no one can compare to him." Wang Ying said quietly.

**Exhaustion - Working 365 days without a single day off**

Everyone who has worked with Wang Ying knows, "She works like there's no tomorrow." To earn more money, just after the Spring Festival in 2010, Wang Ying quit her job and, through a fellow townsman, entered a bathhouse in Haidian District.

At the bathhouse, the working hours for foot massage therapists are from 2 PM in the afternoon to 7 AM the next morning, paid according to the type of work and the number of customers. Earning 8 yuan for each pedicure and 15 yuan for each foot massage, each customer takes approximately 50 minutes to an hour. When a customer arrives, the service staff calls the dormitory to see who is willing to take the job. By late night, the technicians are too tired to want to come out. "Only Wang Ying is always ready to come at a moment's notice." Accountant Li Hong said, "For 365 days in a year, she has never taken a single day off, earning over 1000 yuan more than her colleagues each month." Colleagues noticed her pale complexion and advised her to take care of her health. Wang Ying thanked them but continued working. "I need to earn money. Besides sending money home, I don't have any other plans." Wang Ying said.

Since her husband's death, Wang Ying's mother-in-law has been bedridden and needs medication year-round; her father-in-law, due to excessive shock, has become significantly weaker. Both are over seventy years old and cannot do any work. The family's original six acres of land have been given to neighbors to cultivate, leaving them without any income.

In 2008, the Sichuan earthquake, and in 2010, the southern floods, natural disasters struck one after another. Out of the family's eight mud houses, only two remain habitable. After a light rain, tiles fall from the roof, flooding the entire house. Two years ago, the town government sent people to inspect the village, identifying Wang Ying's house as the only one needing reconstruction. "Where will we get the money?" Looking at the already dilapidated old house, the two elders showed helplessness on their faces. Villagers pitied them, helping to build a tent in front of the Li family's old house. The elderly couple sent the children to boarding school and lived in the tent themselves. During winter, it was even colder inside the house than outside. But they knew that their daughter-in-law was struggling alone in Beijing and didn't want to add more pressure to her.

After knowing this, Wang Ying worked even harder, "In my dreams, I see the house collapse. Waking up, I sit on the bed stunned for a while. They can't have any more accidents; otherwise, this family will really be finished." Wang Ying said.

**Persistence - Not returning home for four years to earn money**

Wang Ying hasn't returned home for several Spring Festivals, and this year's Spring Festival was no exception.

Early on New Year's Day, fireworks exploded loudly outside the bathhouse. After working three all-night shifts, Wang Ying caught her breath and lay on the bed, dialing home. On the other end of the phone, her mother-in-law couldn't resist persuading her, "Come home, it's almost four years since we've seen you. We miss you." Holding the phone, Wang Ying burst into tears. After Li Bing's passing, Spring Festival became a kind of torture for Wang Ying.

Technicians at the store took holidays and returned home, but the customers increased. She worked the jobs of three or four people, working 24-hour shifts without rest. She didn't want to stop, "Throughout the year, this is the time when I can earn the most money." Too busy to wash her face, with no time to sleep, every time she just laid down for less than 10 minutes, a service staff member would come to call her. Several girls in their early twenties were too tired to stand after a few days, but Wang Ying continued working tirelessly
