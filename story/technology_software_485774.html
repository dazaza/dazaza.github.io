Online buying and selling of mooncake coupons leads to excessively high prices of mooncakes

by jdhgsa078 on 2011-09-06 09:48:10

As the approaching of the Mid-Autumn Festival, scalpers are once again ushering in a season of annual harvest. Due to the decrease in offline profits, this year's scalpers have also developed new ways of doing business: selling moon cake coupons online, and great business opportunities are brewing within these small moon cake coupons.

Decrease in Offline Profits

With notices pasted on windows that read "recycling moon cake tickets" and signs placed at the entrance that say "recycling moon cake tickets", I saw a grocery store specializing in moon cake tickets on Rushan Road on August 31st.

"How much do you sell your Häagen-Dazs moon cake tickets for?" I asked as I approached. "This is popular this year. I'll give you a maximum of 70% off, and my price will definitely be higher than other scalpers." The shopkeeper auntie told me.

At this moment, a fashionably dressed white-collar worker came to the front of the store and sold a moon cake ticket worth 228 yuan from Krispy Kreme to the shopkeeper for 120 yuan.

Afterwards, a man with a shoulder bag (later according to my understanding, this man was also a scalper) took out several moon cake coupons to sell. The shopkeeper skimmed through a stack of moon cake coupons and then said to him: "Most of what you brought are no-name brand coupons, no one wants them." In the end, the shopkeeper only accepted a few of them. I found out that for a moon cake coupon worth 168 yuan, the shopkeeper's purchase price was 90 yuan, but according to the seller’s introduction, the initial purchase price of this coupon was 75 yuan.

From their conversation, I learned that due to reasons such as the tax on moon cakes and fewer issued coupons this year, the profit from reselling moon cake coupons has decreased compared to previous years, but it is still possible to earn several thousand yuan.

Shifting to Online Sales

"Massive demand for moon cake coupons from Xin Hua Lou, Krispy Kreme, Häagen-Dazs, Yuan Zu, etc. If you have a large quantity, we can come to your place to purchase. For smaller quantities, we can transact near convenient subway stations. Specific prices can be discussed via e-commerce platforms." I saw such a post on Baixing.com. Subsequently, I called the poster and asked: "Do you buy Häagen-Dazs moon cake coupons?" "Yes, and you should sell quickly, otherwise it will be too late to sell." Mr. Ge was very eager to persuade me.

In addition to posting ads, online stores are another quick way to sell moon cake coupons. "Our coupons come from companies and hotel employees, some are specifically collected, and they have all been verified. We offer a tenfold compensation if any are fake." A Taobao store owner told me. I also noticed that the online prices of moon cake coupons are mostly between 70% to 90% off. For example, a nationwide usable Häagen-Dazs ice cream moon cake coupon originally priced at 228 yuan is now priced at 182.6 yuan online. I also learned that the scalper price for this coupon is around 140 yuan, yielding a profit of up to 40 yuan.

Moreover, according to my understanding, some scalpers have also targeted group buying by registering many fake accounts to participate in groups improperly, thereby preempting limited cheap coupons. However, major group-buying websites have already noticed this phenomenon and are taking action to combat online scalpers.

Giving Birth to Moon Cake Bubbles

Through investigation, I discovered that there are two types of processes for the circulation of moon cake ticket consumption. The first type is: manufacturers print moon cake tickets → hotels or merchants stamp them and sell them to corporate clients → corporate clients distribute them to individual customers → individual customers redeem the products. Another process is more complicated: manufacturers print moon cake tickets → hotels or merchants stamp them and sell them to corporate clients → corporate clients distribute them to individual customers → individual customers then resell them to scalpers at 20% to 50% of the original price → scalpers resell them on the market at 50% to 60% of the original price, or resell them to professional moon cake ticket recycling intermediary companies at 40% to 60% of the original price → intermediary companies resell them.

If moon cake tickets follow the first normal process, scalper gangs would not emerge. However, due to various existing reasons, the market currently follows the second process more often. As a result, in multiple low-buy high-sell transactions, an ordinary moon cake coupon actually acquires the function of a financial product.

During the circulation process, a single moon cake coupon enriches a large group of people including moon cake companies, scalpers, and intermediaries. However, upon careful examination, the main reason lies in the excessively high pricing of moon cakes on the current market, which far exceeds the cost and generates enormous profit margins, making each link profitable. An insider who has operated a moon cake store for many years sighed to me, "Like the real estate market, the current Mid-Autumn Festival market has also given rise to moon cake bubbles."
