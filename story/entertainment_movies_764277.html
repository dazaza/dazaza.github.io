Behind-the-Scenes Production

Romantic love and horror themes are no longer rare in campus films. What captivates youth audiences more powerfully is a suspenseful mystery drama filled with reasoning. In "Reasoning Realm of the Fourth Period," the witnesses, suspects, and victims are all ordinary high school students. Such character settings tightly grip the hearts of young viewers who are both fearful yet curious. Supporting characters in reasoning films often play critical roles. A sudden murder occurs in the everyday space of a school, with only "40 minutes" left before the bell of the fourth period rings. High school students use their sharp minds to piece together fragmented clues, adding interest to the movie. This film boasts the tagline of "High School Version of The Chaser," aiming to create a tense sense of speed. The cast includes Yoo Seung-ho, known as the "National Younger Brother," and newcomer Kang Sora, portraying the subtle romance between boys and girls during adolescence with a touch of wildness.

Yoo Seung-ho: The National Younger Brother in the "Transformation Period"

In the flower boy-filled Korean entertainment industry, Yoo Seung-ho stands out as an anomaly. His single-lidded eyes make him a standard face of the Republic of Korea's male population. Back in 2002, the film "Love, Lies" established his status as the national younger brother. Following performances in popular dramas like "The King and I," he gradually grew into one of the most beloved male actors among fan sisters.

In this film, he plays the role of Jung-hoon, a model high school student who finds himself inexplicably entangled in a murder case and must uncover the real culprit to clear his name. Yoo Seung-ho, a high school student in reality, expressed excitement about playing a role perfectly suited to his age. Currently in the "transformation period" towards becoming a charming man, he has been praised by Korean media as "one-of-a-kind Yoo Seung-ho." As a top student nationwide, with an excellent appearance and behavior shaped by strict parents, Jung-hoon has many female fans. Due to the sudden murder, he closely collaborates with justice-driven classmate Da-jung, revealing wisdom and protection for Da-jung in moments of tension. Yoo Seung-ho admits that this character is "perfectly unbelievable," fulfilling the "all vanity" fantasies of boys at this age.

The 40-minute reasoning battle requires not only basic reasoning knowledge but also complex emotional handling, which poses a significant challenge. Before filming began, Yoo Seung-ho practiced acting repeatedly with the script. Despite the physically demanding running scenes, the crew saw his face always wearing a "seemingly never-fading" smile. In the Korean film industry, there's a saying that "actors who debut as child stars have short careers," but Yoo Seung-ho seems to be proving otherwise through steady growth in various TV series, movies, and even music videos. The boy affectionately called "IT BOY" by sister fans has a promising star path ahead.

Controversy 1: Rapid Cast Change

The originally appointed director, Shen Dongye, gained fame nationwide in 2000 with his screenplay "Infatuated with Walkie-Talkie." This was his second feature-length film after "Slave Lover." However, the production company announced that both he and the leading actress, Kim So-eun, had withdrawn from the project, replaced by commercial director Lee Sang-yong and newcomer Kang Sora. Filming for "Reasoning Realm of the Fourth Period" began in April 2009. Regarding the reason for changing directors, the production company did not provide a clear explanation, but Korean media speculated that the conflict between Shen Dongye and the investors triggered this incident. At the time, Kim So-eun had already completed 60% to 70% of her scenes, but due to the change in direction, almost all shots needed to be redone. Her ongoing new drama "Can't Marry a Man" made it impossible to coordinate schedules, so she exited the project. Additionally, to ensure the summer release plan wasn't affected, shooting after the director change took only two to three weeks, leading some media to question whether the quality would suffer significantly.

Controversy 2: National Younger Brother Gets "Forced Kissed"

If statistics were to be gathered, probably most kissing scenes in movie history are initiated by the male lead. Recently, with the rise of feminism, instances of females taking the initiative to kiss on the big screen have increased, adding a playful yet romantic touch. In "Fourth Period," a scene where Kang Sora forcefully embraces and kisses Yoo Seung-ho was revealed, drawing enthusiastic reactions from the online community of Yoo Seung-ho's sister fans. Kang Sora herself expressed concern over potential backlash from fans. She admitted to feeling nervous during this slightly shy yet pure forced-kiss scene, which required several NGs before completion. As for how the chemistry between the leads sparked, the production team kept it under wraps, refusing to reveal any details beforehand.
