Plot Summary

The Birth of the Black Knight

Since childhood, Mai Ti Shuang has been full of business acumen, deeply understanding the principle that only through business can one become prosperous. Thus, he studies hard, completing his MBA at Stanford University in the U.S. through sheer effort, and seeks opportunities there.

After graduation, Shuang sees potential in He Wen Tian's company and approaches Wen Tian voluntarily, straightforwardly stating that he doesn't want to be an employee but rather a partner, willing even for just 0.01% of shares. Impressed by Shuang's uniqueness, Wen Tian lets Shuang join as a partner. Their relationship is both teacher and friend, cooperating seamlessly, specializing in acquiring companies and splitting them for sale, becoming renowned snipers in the American business world.

Later, Shuang becomes a well-known black knight in the business world, ruthless and merciless, gradually gaining prominence. Similarly, the differences of opinion between Shuang and Wen Tian also increase. For the company's interest and development, Shuang decides to fully acquire Wen Tian's company. Unable to bear the blow, Wen Tian drives drunk, and Shuang learns that Wen Tian's car lost control and fell into the sea. After the incident, Wen Tian's body was not found, but Shuang firmly believes Wen Tian is dead, feeling extremely guilty. Is the value of a deal really greater than friendship, life?

Shuang reflects on everything in the past, deciding not to be a black knight anymore, and exits the business world. From the newspaper, Shuang learns that Wen Tian's wife and child are struggling financially in Hong Kong. Shuang feels responsible and decides to return to Hong Kong to take care of Wen Tian's family.

Miaomiao's Debt Collection Pursuit

Shuang returns to Hong Kong and finds Wen Tian's wife, Sha Mu Lian, coincidentally staying at a hotel run by Tang Ji. He meets Tang Ji, who is troubled by the hotel's operation issues. Tang Ji is a middle-aged skirt-chasing heir, born into a commercial family, notorious outside, but filial to his father, surrounded by beauties.

Coincidentally, Shuang meets Lin Miaomiao in a luxury store, thus creating animosity between the two. Miaomiao is a crazy shopping addict, owing a large credit card debt, forced to sell herself as a debt collector. With a spirit of no success, no fee, Miaomiao becomes the ace of the debt collection company.

Mu Lian owes a large debt to Miaomiao's financial company due to her grandmother Zhou Xiaoqiao being scammed. Miaomiao is responsible for collecting the debt, thus encountering Shuang again. Shuang takes on Mu Lian's debt, making Miaomiao mistakenly believe Shuang is Mu Lian's ex-husband, abandoning his wife and children, giving Shuang a bad impression.

Mu Lian is unaware that her husband's death was indirectly caused by Shuang, and she still believes her husband Wen Tian is alive. Mu Lian's grandmother often accuses Shuang of being the murderer of her son Wen Tian. Initially shocked by being discovered by Mu Lian, later it is confirmed that the grandmother suffers from early-stage senile dementia, and everyone thinks her words are unreliable.

Shuang Turns into a White Knight

Shuang is a regular customer at Qi's Noodle Shop. Seeing Mu Lian's living difficulties, he introduces Mu Lian to work at Qi's Noodle Shop. Mu Lian grew up in the U.S., straightforward in nature, not knowing how to navigate social complexities, but works hard enthusiastically at the noodle shop, injecting new vitality, earning praise from Zhong Qi.

Qi's Noodle Shop is threatened by a newly opened cart noodle shop nearby. Shuang offers the term CEO, and Miaomiao, to save the noodle shop, entrusts her brother Lin Musen as the noodle shop's CEO. Musen takes over the noodle shop as its CEO but performs poorly, causing chaos. Shuang advises Zhong Qi not to hand over the noodle shop to Musen, emphasizing nepotism should be avoided. Seeing Mu Lian's hardworking and diligent attitude, Shuang recommends Mu Lian for an important position to Qi and Miaomiao, but Miaomiao dismisses it, thinking Shuang is just talking nonsense.

Until Shuang helps the Bo De Group acquire the old building where the noodle shop is located to convert it into a hotel and convinces Sen and Qiao to sell the shop. Not wanting her father's efforts to go to waste, Miaomiao fights to protect the noodle shop, replacing Musen and appointing Mu Lian as the CEO. As the noodle shop's CEO, Mu Lian teams up with Miaomiao to negotiate with the Bo De Group. With Shuang's covert assistance, Tang Ji regains autonomy for the first time, and Qi's Noodle Shop successfully overcomes the crisis, achieving a win-win situation for both parties. Since then, Miaomiao firmly believes Shuang is a business genius and decides to learn business from Shuang to escape poverty. Miaomiao constantly pesters Shuang, who although hasn't verbally agreed, secretly teaches Miaomiao business strategies. Unexpectedly, on Miaomiao's journey in business, she re-encounters her ex-boyfriend Tong Yiben.

Miaomiao Gradually Develops Feelings for Shuang

For work, Mu Lian reluctantly sends Xiaoqiao to a nursing home. To comfort Xiaoqiao, Shuang requests to move in himself, only to find out that the nursing home is funded by Tang Ji for his girlfriend, facing operational difficulties. While Tang Ji is caught cheating at the nursing home, he meets Shuang and Miaomiao. Knowing Shuang's capabilities, Tang Ji hopes Shuang will help solve the nursing home's problems. Miaomiao relentlessly pursues learning business skills from Shuang, forcing Shuang to act reluctantly.

After agreeing, Shuang appears indifferent, and Miaomiao sees that Shuang is leisurely about reforming the nursing home, not taking it seriously. She decides to test him and proposes the "Plant Filial Piety, Gain Bonus" plan, which creates chaos. Ultimately, Shuang steps in to resolve the disturbance at the nursing home and surprisingly merges it with the neighboring kindergarten, forming a new generation of elder-child center, solving all problems. After this battle, the master-disciple relationship between Shuang and Miaomiao deepens.

Miaomiao discovers that she is gradually developing feelings for Shuang, but Shuang pays no attention to her. Miaomiao assumes Shuang is a man deeply in love, having a woman he can't let go of behind his back. Later, she finds out this woman is Shuang's mother Qingqing. Shuang transfers his maternal affection to Miaomiao, and Miaomiao tries her best to dissolve it. Seeing that Shuang still refuses to accept her, she believes Shuang looks down on her, deciding to strengthen herself so that Shuang will look at her differently and thereby gain the man she loves.
