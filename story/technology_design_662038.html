The latest chapter of "Immortal Reverse": Family members compete to donate eyes to restore a man's sight, sister donated bone marrow for him 4 months ago.

by lulultt913 on 2012-02-09 16:42:49

The father said: the family pooled 400,000 yuan for Zheng Changwei's bone marrow transplant. All photos were taken by reporter Chen Liang. The names of those who donated money for Zheng Changwei's bone marrow transplant surgery are all recorded by the father.

After donating bone marrow to his younger brother Zheng Changwei, the second sister appeared a bit fatter after recuperation. This photo shows the second sister with her husband.

By reporter Yin Qinbing

On February 17, the Lantern Festival of the Year of the Rabbit, an ordinary farmer's home in Xiong Jiayuanwan Village, Wulijie Town, Jiangxia District held a special family meeting. At the meeting, the 61-year-old father Zheng Zeliu made a decision: as long as it can restore the sight of his 26-year-old son Zheng Changwei, he is willing to donate one of his eyes. Both of Zheng Changwei's sisters also expressed their willingness to donate one of their eyes.

They do not know if this will really help Zheng Changwei. Their idea is very simple: since the bone marrow transplant four months ago saved the seriously ill Zheng Changwei, what does it matter to give up one eyeball?

》》》Illness

A bolt from the blue

Zheng Zeliu and his wife Xiong Ying have two daughters and one son. The eldest daughter Zheng Jinrong is 33 years old, the second daughter Zheng Xiaorong is 32 years old, both are married. The 26-year-old son Zheng Changwei was entrusted with great expectations. The two sisters loved this little brother especially.

In 2002, at the age of 18, Zheng Changwei graduated from the mechanical and electrical engineering department of Jiangxia Technical School. He went far away to work for Midea Group in Shunde, Guangdong, engaged in air conditioner quality inspection work. In November 2004, he was dispatched back to Wuhan. The young man is 1.7 meters tall, loves playing basketball, lives in the factory dormitory, and is very popular. He would return home every few days to see his parents.

Last August, Xiao Zheng began to feel a sharp decline in his health. "Often feeling very tired, walking a little or climbing stairs makes me feel powerless." Pale complexion, unexplained bruising on the body, eyelid bleeding, gums often spontaneously oozing blood, and waking up at night with a mouth full of blood.

After examination at the Jiangxia District People's Hospital, Xiao Zheng vaguely sensed something wrong: seeing the hematology department, the doctor did not admit him and suggested immediate transfer to another hospital. After examination at Union Hospital, he was diagnosed with "severe aplastic anemia."

"Hurry up and treat it, otherwise there may be a danger to life," the doctor's words made Zheng Zeliu feel as if the sky had collapsed.

The doctor gave three options: one, take medicine to control the condition; two, undergo chemotherapy and radiotherapy; three, perform a bone marrow transplant. After a few days of conservative treatment, the son's condition gradually worsened.

》》》Contribution

Father and daughters vie to donate bone marrow

On August 31, Zheng Changwei was admitted to the hematology ward of Union Hospital. Until his discharge on November 20, these three months became an unforgettable difficult time for the entire family.

Bone marrow transplantation, using donated bone marrow from the China Marrow Donor Program, not only requires additional costs but also has greater rejection reactions compared to bone marrow provided by relatives, and the success rate is also lower.

After listening to the doctor's introduction, Zheng Zeliu rolled up his sleeve first, "Draw mine!" He said that the two daughters are still young, and unless absolutely necessary, he doesn't want them to take this risk.

Medical regulations stipulate that for successful bone marrow transplantation, at least six matching points are required. Unfortunately, the results of Old Zheng's matching test showed only four points. When the news came out, the eldest daughter Zheng Jinrong and the youngest daughter Zheng Xiaorong found the doctor and vied to donate their own. After several rounds of arguments, the older sister convinced the younger sister.

It seemed like fate was testing them. A few days later, the results of the older sister's matching test came out, "Five points, one point short of the required six."

It was now the turn of the younger daughter Zheng Xiaorong —— the day she received the successful matching result, Zheng Xiaorong was very excited, "My younger brother is finally saved!"

"People live in the world to be filial to their parents, siblings should unite and help each other..." During the interview, whether it was the elder sister or the younger sister, their language was simple: "Being family in this life is a blessing from the previous life."

》》》Borrowing

"Relatives are afraid to see me"

To perform a bone marrow transplant for his son, money was the first "stumbling block."

"That period, all I could think about was how to find the money," Father Zheng Zeliu said, the family only cultivated three acres of poor land, and he worked odd jobs outside, with no savings.

"During that time, relatives were afraid to see me," Old Zheng said he understood. After all, leukemia is a dangerous disease, and people lending money must consider his repayment ability.

To treat her younger brother, the eldest daughter Zheng Jinrong took out more than 70,000 yuan of hard-earned money she had saved over many years working outside. Mother Xiong Ying said, this money was originally given to her daughter as dowry. In addition, the daughter borrowed 50,000 yuan in her own name from close friends. Zheng Jinrong said that last year, her in-laws and father-in-law passed away one after another, and her child was just over one year old. However, on the day her younger brother was diagnosed, her husband did not hesitate to let her send 10,000 yuan in cash for emergency use. Her husband worked on a construction site pulling concrete, earning about 20 yuan per truckload. To raise money, he often worked beyond his capacity.

One morning, Mother Xiong Ying was hit by a car while borrowing money on the road, unconscious for half an hour before being discovered by neighbors. Fortunately, she had no serious injuries and spent more than 1,000 yuan on medical expenses. After a brief observation in the hospital, Xiong Ying didn't dare to stay longer and returned home, "There's no time to argue, my son is waiting for me to save his life."

The total cost of Zheng Changwei's bone marrow transplant was more than 430,000 yuan, of which nearly 100,000 yuan was donated by the factory and coworkers, and the rest was mostly borrowed from relatives.

》》》Saving Money

Father hasn't eaten meat for half a year

Watching his son suffer on the sickbed, all the father could do was grit his teeth and persevere.

"To tell the truth, I know the worst outcome, but he's still young! We are almost in the grave, he is the rising sun..." Zheng Zeliu said, although he also suffers from shoulder arthritis and osteophytosis, he still has to bear this family with his not-so-strong shoulders.

"For several months while my son was sick, I haven't eaten meat..." To save on medical expenses for his son, he set his food budget not to exceed 8 yuan. In the morning, he would spend 2 yuan to eat two steamed buns and a bowl of porridge at "Little Sichuan" across from Union Hospital; at noon, he would either make do with a 2-yuan vegetable noodle or eat the leftover buns from the morning; for dinner, he would buy a 2-yuan eight-treasure porridge from a roadside stall or eat two buns with plain water. In the evening, he would rent a chair for 5 yuan and lie down in the hallway to spend the night.

When he was really hungry, he even went to nearby small restaurants to pick up leftovers from others.

》》》Gratitude

"Without my family, I wouldn't be here"

"Without my family, I might not be here today. The blood flowing in my veins comes from my sister, and in the future, I will treat them better."

October 5th and 6th are dates that Brother Zheng Changwei will never forget. Lying in the sterile cabin, he had three catheters inserted into his shoulder. Watching the two bags of bright red "hematopoietic stem cell" plasma being infused into his body. Not far away, in the blood collection room, his second sister Zheng Xiaorong lay quietly, with one catheter drawing blood from her left hand, passing through a "hematopoietic stem cell extraction machine", and then being reinfused into her right vein.

At that time, after two weeks, the brother underwent chemotherapy in the sterile cabin, reducing his immune system to zero; the sister received injections for four days to promote the proliferation of hematopoietic stem cells in her body.

A week later, the brother began to show reactions, catching a cold and having a fever, indicating that the hematopoietic stem cells from his sister had begun to proliferate in his body, forming its own immune response. From measuring white blood cells at 13 per cubic millimeter one week later, to 100, 200, 1000. By October 31st, Zheng Changwei's white blood cells began to gradually rise to 8000~9000. Slowly, Zheng Changwei started eating a little, and his complexion gradually regained some color. The doctor said, fortunately, the sister's bone marrow matched the brother's bone marrow extraordinarily well.

On November 20th, the brother was officially discharged, announcing the success of the transplant. Currently, the brother still needs to take five kinds of medication daily to gradually recover.

》》》Relay

Willing to donate eyeballs to save the younger brother

Misfortunes never come singly. One week after Zheng Changwei's bone marrow transplant surgery, he suddenly felt a darkening in front of his eyes. Now, his right eye can no longer see clearly, and his left eye has severely blurred vision, unable to recognize even the topmost letter on the vision chart.

After examination, Zheng Changwei was diagnosed with "retinal detachment of the right eye, vitreous opacity of the right eye; obvious blood vessels in the left eye; suspected tumor in the bulging wall below the right eye."

The doctor said this might be related to the side effects of the bone marrow transplant treatment. And any surgery would have to wait until after Zheng Changwei's recovery period before it could be performed.

"The son's life has been saved, we cannot let him go blind!" During the interview, Father Zheng Zeliu firmly said that during the New Year, he and his wife, along with their two daughters, discussed multiple times that no matter what, they could not let their son become blind. If it really couldn't be helped, they were all willing to donate one of their eyes to regain their son's vision.

》》》Doctor:

Donating eyeballs is unethical

Professor Zhang Mingchang, head of the Ophthalmology Department and doctoral supervisor at Union Hospital, stated that retinal detachment is a type of fundus lesion. This disease is divided into two types: one is the rupture with holes, and the other is the detachment caused by inflammation. The rejection reaction in transplantation surgery often leads to "dry eye syndrome," which increases the chance of disease compared to ordinary people. Specifically regarding treatment, it usually only requires surgical repositioning, and there is no need to replace the cornea.

Professor Zhang said that in routine surgeries, living corneal donations are limited to partial replacement, and the donor's vision will definitely be affected. If there is severe retinal necrosis and a retinal replacement surgery is needed, there are developments abroad, but it has not been done domestically.

Professor Zhang believed that even if surgery is possible, making one person see again while making another blind, despite voluntary consent between family members, is still unacceptable. "This is ethically indefensible."

》》》Lawyer:

Legally feasible

Chairman Liu Yuanbo of Hubei Penglai Law Firm said that Article 18 of the "Regulations on Human Organ Transplantation" stipulates that after receiving an application for review of human organ removal, the Human Organ Transplantation Technology Clinical Application and Ethics Committee shall review the following matters and issue written opinions of approval or disapproval: whether the donation intention of the human organ donor is genuine; whether there is any buying or disguised trading of human organs; whether the matching of human organs and the adaptability of the recipient comply with ethical principles and human organ transplantation technology management standards. With the agreement of more than 2/3 of the committee members, the committee may issue a written opinion approving the removal of human organs. Therefore, although the father donating one eye becomes blind in one eye, it illuminates the son's world, demonstrating great paternal love. Specific situations must be approved by the Human Organ Transplantation Technology Clinical Application and Ethics Committee.

》》》Small Information

Aplastic Anemia: Anemia caused by the significant reduction of bone marrow hematopoietic tissue leading to the failure of hematopoietic function.

Donating Bone Marrow: Originally, it involved drawing bone marrow blood, which is rich in hematopoietic stem cells. Nowadays, hematopoietic stem cells are directly collected from peripheral blood, similar to the process of donating component blood, but initially requiring mobilizing a large number of hematopoietic stem cells from the bone marrow to the peripheral blood.
