Many people choose to renovate their new homes in the summer, but due to weather conditions, there are many quality and safety hazards in summer renovations. Today we look at the summer renovation tips summarized by experts, which will surely be very helpful to you.

Precaution One:

Summer Renovation Safety First

The summer temperature is relatively high, and the climate is dry. There are many materials piled up at the construction site. From a safety perspective, the materials should be placed in a cool place as much as possible to avoid combustion caused by poor air circulation and high temperatures, leading to fires. In summer, materials should also be reduced on-site to reduce potential accident hazards. Additionally, electrical appliances and tools used during work should be kept at a distance from the materials. When working with electricity, it's best to stay far away from the materials and make a reasonable division between the work area and the material area.

Precaution Two:

Frequent Ventilation to Prevent Pollution Caused by Renovation

To avoid being harmed during the "high incidence period" of summer renovation pollution, apart from trying to choose non-toxic or less toxic decoration materials and hiring reputable home renovation companies for construction, it's also important to ensure proper ventilation and air purification in the renovated rooms. If conditions allow, try to ventilate the room as much as possible; if not, indoor ventilation equipment or air purifiers that can reduce indoor harmful gases can be selected.

Precaution Three:

Moisture Prevention and Sun Protection

When purchasing wood products in the summer, including major construction materials like plywood and auxiliary materials such as white wooden strips, corner lines, door edges, window frames, and baseboards along the wall, it’s necessary to test the moisture content of the wood products. At building material markets, most wood product specialty stores should have wood moisture testers. According to national regulations, the moisture content of standard boards should not exceed 12%. Experts suggest that during the rainy season, when buying materials, you can choose slightly drier ones. Once the materials arrive at the site, let them sit for a day or two to acclimate to the surrounding humidity, making them more suitable for use.

Additionally, boards should be protected from intense sunlight. If it rains and the humidity is high, measures should be taken to prevent dampness and deformation. Do not leave boards or wood exposed to direct sunlight, as this can cause the paint and glue on wood products to age faster. Also, prevent rainwater from causing wood products to swell, deform, or even mold. Therefore, special attention should be paid to these issues in daily life.

Precaution Four:

Wallpaper and Paint Should Dry Naturally in Shade

Paint and wallpaper are commonly used wall decoration materials. In spring, due to the humid air, windows and doors are often opened wide to quickly dry the walls. However, summer is different because it's dry. Before laying wallpaper, it is generally soaked in water first, then brushed with glue and laid down. Opening the windows and doors at this time would expose freshly laid wallpaper to "cross-drafts," causing it to lose water too quickly and resulting in shrinkage and deformation.

Precaution Five:

Eliminating Mold Odor

In home renovations, latex paint often has problems such as drying slowly and becoming moldy and smelly in hot and humid weather. The common solution used by renovation companies is to use air conditioning to dehumidify and remove moisture from the air. Furniture cabinet doors should remain open to maintain ventilation. After renovation, various odors may linger, affecting people's health. Renovation companies suggest placing more green plants after the renovation or putting two or three lemons, oranges, or bananas in the room to achieve rapid odor removal effects.

Precaution Six:

No Painting on Rainy Days

Experts recommend that for wood products, whether brushing clear varnish or applying mixed oil, remember not to do it on rainy days. On rainy days, ask the construction team to do other tasks first, or even temporarily stop work for two days, rather than doing painting jobs on rainy days.

Precaution Seven:

Seasonal Issues Should Not Be Patched Up Haste

In spring, the air humidity is high, and the water content in walls, floors, and wooden furniture is relatively high. As summer approaches, the air gradually becomes drier, and some problems may arise due to seasonal changes, such as floorboard contraction, increased gaps between boards; different shrinkage rates between walls and door frames causing gaps; and wall cracking... These are all normal phenomena during seasonal transitions. Repairing at this time, as moisture continues to evaporate, walls may still crack. It's better to wait until the wall moisture matches the external climate before making another repair, achieving better results.

Precaution Eight:

Consider Radiators in Your Plan

Although you won't need radiators in the sweltering summer, you'll soon realize their importance in future living. Moreover, home renovation projects have "irreversibility." Therefore, no matter when you renovate, always consider replacing your radiators. In current home renovations, replacing traditional cast-iron radiators with new steel pipe radiators that provide good heating and attractive designs has become a trend. However, relevant departments should be notified.
