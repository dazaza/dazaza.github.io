The housing price is not within the control of common people, but the renovation cost is in our own hands. How can we save money and renovate our home properly?

For the same house, using similar materials, why does someone else spend less on renovation than me? Let me tell you, one of the biggest factors is waste. In September this year, relevant persons from the China Interior Decoration Association revealed a number that might come as a shock to many people: China wastes over 30 billion yuan each year due to waste in decoration and renovation projects!

Several responsible persons from decoration companies told me that some landlords are just like what was said in "The Tycoon": they don't buy the right ones, only the expensive ones. When selecting tiles, these landlords tend to choose products with famous brands, high-frequency advertisements, and so-called latest technology, without considering whether their own design style suits these materials.

"A 120 square meter house can differ by 3000~3600 yuan in wall and floor tiles," a designer said. Buying what kind of product is the right of each homeowner, but it's too foolish to deliberately go against your budget. In fact, high-end products from big brands also have good cost-performance. New products definitely do not have as high cost-performance as classic products, and their performance is also not as stable as classic products.

As a senior designer put it, purchasing behavior should be slower than fashion trends. His reasoning is that newly launched products, whether bathroom fixtures, tiles, or furniture, will definitely have immature aspects in terms of performance and color. Since choosing luxury renovation means considering the product's lifespan, it's wise to purchase after these new products have been tested in practice. Moreover, even if the most high-end product breaks after a short period of use, even with lifetime warranty, it still feels uncomfortable.

During interviews, I also heard an old story in the decoration industry: some companies calculate labor costs based on the quantity of materials used. One company, in order to earn more labor costs, stuffed the owner's self-purchased wooden boards into the ceiling. Several years later, when the owner opened the ceiling, they found that it was full of wooden boards. From this, it can be seen that the analysis of these forty percent of netizens is quite reasonable.

An independent designer said that if the on-site management method is appropriate, 3000 yuan can be saved from 50,000 yuan. This depends on whether the homeowner can closely monitor the construction party to carefully calculate the materials. Calculating each type of building material before cutting or using it can save a lot of materials.

How to save materials during construction first depends on the skills of the construction party. For the same area of the ground, calculating the rationality of tile laying may save a lot of tile costs.

If you think calculating piece by piece is too troublesome, a fairer way for the decorating family is to agree with the decoration company beforehand to calculate fees by area. For example, the actual amount of latex paint used is calculated by the square meter, rather than by the amount of materials used. This calculation method can force the decoration company to save materials.

So, which things from the previous renovation can be reused without affecting use? Do all wires need to be replaced? Is it really necessary to redo the water pipes? These questions are probably what many homeowners, especially second-hand home owners, want to know.

Money-saving tips:

What basic renovations can be reused? Many decoration companies keep this information secret because the saved workload could become their profit.

They don't say, but we ordinary people have a lot of ideas. Netizen Huakai Qianmo 05 suggests reusing the doors and windows of the house because the original doors and windows are already well-made door frames and window sills. The materials may be better than newly bought ones, just outdated in style. In fact, after repainting, these doors and windows are still usable, thus saving demolition costs. Some netizens also suggest using the wall tiles cut by masons, sticking them behind cabinets and other corners, which neither affects aesthetics nor saves part of the tile cost.

To reduce waste, my friend Ms. Zhu only bought 1 square meter more than the actual area when selecting the floor. As a result, she had to make several trips to the flooring market and ended up spending more money due to the cancellation of the promotion price on her selected floor.

How can we minimize the waste of excess materials as much as possible? First, naturally, we must accurately calculate the required amount of building materials before purchasing. However, it's impossible to be precise enough to have no waste at all. Even many professional decoration companies' budgeters have to write "calculated according to actual usage" on the budget list, let alone ordinary foremen or homeowners themselves.
