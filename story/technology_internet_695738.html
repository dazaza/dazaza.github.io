Cheongsam show has been suspended, local market rescue measures frequently halted, making it hard to reverse the downward trend of housing prices.

by abcj5hehe on 2012-03-07 17:04:01

The "cheongsam show" of female deputies and committee members from the Zhejiang delegation has been called off

Reporters learned yesterday that the "cheongsam show" of female deputies and committee members from the Zhejiang delegation was canceled by relevant departments due to controversy on the Internet.

Cao Mianying, a people's representative of the Zhejiang delegation, said she had not yet received her custom-made cheongsam. People's representative Cui Wei confirmed to reporters that the originally scheduled "elegant attire" cheongsam show for today had been canceled. Cui Wei said that the staff of the Zhejiang delegation informed her that due to the controversy caused by the non-officially organized cheongsam show activities online, after comprehensive consideration, relevant parties decided to halt this activity. Whether it be the "[century cheongsam exhibition]" or Women's Day, the female deputies and committee members have already determined that they will not appear in cheongsams.

Previously, the media reported that 30 female deputies and committee members from the Zhejiang delegation would collectively wear custom-made cheongsams and make an appearance at the Great Hall of the People.

Frequent local policies to rescue the market are being halted

On February 10, the Anhui Province Wuhu City government announced the "Notice of Several Opinions Issued by the Wuhu Municipal Government on Further Strengthening Housing Security and Improving Residents' Housing Conditions." The subsidies under the new policy were unprecedented, involving a 100% subsidy for deed tax, subsidies for properties under 90 square meters, and those with vocational high school diplomas or junior technician qualifications or above receiving subsidies. However, on the evening of the 12th, the Wuhu municipal government suddenly announced that the previously issued "Notice" would be suspended. What was hailed as the "first shot in rescuing the property market in the Year of the Dragon" quietly ended just three days after its introduction amidst widespread controversy.

Since last October, when Foshan first relaxed purchase restrictions only to see them reversed within 12 hours, to last November when Chengdu relaxed buyer qualification reviews which were then halted after a week, and again to Wuhu's "first shot in rescuing the property market in the Year of the Dragon," all these have quickly fizzled out. The central government's stance remains unchanged; there is no empty talk about not relaxing real estate control policies. At the same time as Wuhu's new policy was halted, Premier Wen Jiabao once again emphasized "not wavering in promoting reasonable housing price returns" and pointed the policy focus towards affordable housing rather than commercial housing by stating "not everyone needs to own their own home, but everyone should have a place to live." From this, we can see that real estate control policies will not be relaxed, and the "fake fall" of the real estate market in 2008 is unlikely to reoccur.

Recently, the focus of the real estate market has been on Shanghai's "new interpretation of old policies" regarding home purchases by non-locals being halted after being implemented for just one week. Previously, responsible persons from relevant Shanghai departments had indicated that non-local residents holding Shanghai long-term residence permits for three years could enjoy the same home purchasing qualifications as Shanghai locals, meaning such non-local resident families could buy a second home. Once this measure was introduced, it was widely interpreted by the outside world as Shanghai's "loosening" of purchase restrictions. After enduring immense pressure for a week, on the evening of February 28, the Shanghai Housing Security and Housing Administration Bureau's official website issued a notice stating that the strict housing purchase restriction policy would be strictly enforced, explicitly defining "Shanghai household registration resident families" as resident families with Shanghai permanent residency or unit collective residency. This means non-locals will completely lose the possibility of buying a second home in Shanghai.

Shanghai is not the first city where local policies have been halted. Previously, Wuhu's local policy exempting both new and existing homes from deed tax and providing specific subsidies for properties under 90 square meters was halted after being implemented for only four days. Foshan's policy relaxing the individual income tax proof requirements for non-local buyers and allowing local residents to purchase an additional home priced below 7500 yuan per square meter was halted on the day it was introduced.

"From the previous 'one-day tour' and 'four-day tour' of minor adjustments to the property policies in Foshan and Wuhu, we can see that the central government has a bottom line for the local governments' attempts to adjust property policies slightly. This bottom line is that new policies can only offer policy benefits to first-time homebuyers and cannot extend to other investment or speculative home-buying demands," Han Peng, technical director of Shiyibang Real Estate Investment Advisory Co., Ltd., told New Finance Journal. Once local government policy adjustments touch upon the "red lines" of relaxing purchase restrictions and supporting home purchases by non-locals, they will definitely be halted.
