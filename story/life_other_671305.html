Late autumn, reminiscing about sorrow

by huiming on 2012-02-21 18:30:36

At the end of autumn, memories of sorrow

That year, I accidentally read this poem in a book and was instantly captivated. Before that, I wasn't unfamiliar with poems about friendship, but none compared to this one - sincere, open, yet subtly tinged with sadness, giving off an air of fate. Looking back at Gu Liangfen's two "Golden Threads Odes" and understanding the background of this poem's creation, I became deeply infatuated with the poet Nalan Rongruo.

I've always been a sensitive and conflicted person. Sometimes, even I find it hard to understand myself. My personality seems like a mixture, and my perception of myself is sometimes chaotic and sometimes clear. During my second and third years of high school, I continued to study methodically along the predetermined path. However, outside of studying, there was one more thing: using the limited resources around me to explore every bit of information about Nalan - his poems, his person, his stories. And for Nalan Rongruo, what I have always loved most are his poems about friendship. I can't quite explain it; since elementary school, I haven't enjoyed reading fairy tales. Thus, I don't dream of being a prince or princess, nor do I watch pure romance TV shows. Similarly, I don't believe in the existence of love in this world. I stubbornly believe that in my life, apart from family affection, the most important thing should be friendship.

I consider myself lucky; in these twenty years of life, I have met countless friends. When we meet, a faint smile serves as a greeting, occasional small talk, occasional gatherings, all bring happiness. Yet, deep inside, there's always a tinge of loneliness. What I truly long for, beyond ordinary friends, are kindred spirits who share similar interests, who can laugh and scold together and enjoy life fully. I always excessively pursue that kind of intellectual and spiritual exchange.

Perhaps, if a person is never satisfied, even heaven can't bear to see it. After the college entrance exam, an accidental meeting and conversation on QQ with a high school classmate made me think that fate finally pitied me. After searching for so long, it finally granted me a confidant, allowing me to say those words - "Promise heavily, you must remember." Two years, not too long, not too short, during which we truly laughed and scolded each other, occasionally having small arguments. When feeling down or disappointed, we listened to each other's complaints and offered appropriate comfort; when happy and content, we could share our joy together. Sometimes, we gave small evaluations or jokes about each other's writings. In such exchanges, there was genuine happiness. So much so that after the sudden rupture of our friendship, looking back at the chat records of these two years, I could almost see my own state at that time - relaxed, happy, rarely does anyone give me such feelings. Most of the time, I feel like I'm just dealing with some people's chats. But reality cruelly told me that the beautiful friendship I once had has faded away like the cooling weather this autumn, eventually ending with an inexplicable severing of all contact. Without any reason, without any explanation, thus shattering all the happiness. Consequently, I was plagued by confusion and sorrow, exiling and sinking into self-pity. Along with it, the little trust I had in this world gradually disappeared. I know I am selfish; I have many caring friends around me who offer constant comfort, yet I remain stubbornly unable to let go, instead causing them trouble and distress. I apologize and can only say: "Sorry, thank you..."

Perhaps, this year's autumn and winter are destined to be the coldest times of my life, with coldness seeping from within. It's no longer merely due to physical reasons but also includes the chill in my heart. Nevertheless, no matter what, I will strive to keep myself warm...
