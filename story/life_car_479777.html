Over-maintenance of cars can be counterproductive and is not recommended!

Car owners should know that a car needs "one-third repair and two-thirds maintenance." Although maintenance cannot keep a car perpetually young, some car owners have inadvertently damaged their beloved vehicles due to excessive care. This usually happens when the owner has only partial knowledge of maintenance practices. Car maintenance experts believe that over-maintaining a car is not only wasteful but can also cause unexpected harm to the vehicle.

1. Excessive Maintenance Frequency

Mr. Li from Huangyan is not very knowledgeable about cars, so he frequently follows up on maintenance messages sent by dealers. His new car had already undergone three oil changes and three filter replacements after driving only 8,000 kilometers, with intervals of less than 3,000 kilometers between each maintenance session.

The maintenance cycle refers to the interval mileage or time for car maintenance. Mr. Chen, the technical manager of Fengnan Car Repair Co., Ltd. in Taizhou, told the author: "High maintenance frequency isn't necessarily bad as it can more effectively ensure the car's performance, but overly frequent maintenance is unnecessary. The effective mileage for car maintenance is generally between 5,000 to 10,000 kilometers or around six months." Scientifically determining the intervals for various maintenance tasks can keep the car in good technical condition while saving on maintenance and repair costs.

2. Pursuit of High-Octane Gasoline

Mr. Zhang, who believed that higher-octane gasoline was always better, has been using 97-octane gasoline for his new car for the past two years out of care.

Most car owners know that if high-end cars use low-octane gasoline for a long time, problems such as engine knocking, reduced power, increased fuel consumption, and internal engine part damage may occur, significantly shortening the life of the engine. Lower octane gasoline generally contains more sulfur, which can damage the car's exhaust purification system. Therefore, many car owners prefer high-grade gasoline. In reality, higher octane levels are not always better, even for luxury cars.

Manager Chen believes that gasoline with an octane rating lower than what is specified for the car should not be used, but excessively pursuing high-octane gasoline is also not advisable. "Although higher octane ratings provide better anti-knock properties, if you use 97-octane gasoline in a car designed for 93-octane, it can actually harm the car. Higher octane means higher combustion temperatures, requiring longer ignition times, which wastes resources and is environmentally unfriendly."

3. Preference for Imported Tires

Some people emphasize "imported" when buying tires. Mr. Wang, a car enthusiast, installed imported tires on all three of his family cars, including his wife's small commuter car, claiming "imported tires are worth the cost."

However, after several experiences, Mr. Chen no longer holds this view. Once, a new set of imported tires developed bulges after just one mountain road trip. It turns out that some foreign tires may not suit domestic users and not all imported tires are of high quality.

Manager Chen of Fengnan Car Repair Co., Ltd. in Taizhou reminds car enthusiasts: "When selecting car tires, consider the brand's professionalism, the manufacturer's regularity, and the tire's wear resistance. Not all imported tires are suitable for domestic cars." There is a significant difference between the smooth roads abroad and domestic road conditions. Tires suited for foreign roads might not perform well domestically, especially regarding sidewall impact resistance.

After some foreign brands started joint ventures in China, they added an extra layer of fabric to the tire sidewalls to adapt to domestic road conditions, greatly enhancing sidewall impact resistance. Jointly produced tires perform just as well as imports at a more affordable price. Some domestic brands like Triangle tires also receive positive feedback from car enthusiasts.

4. Too Much Engine Oil

After the first 5,000-kilometer maintenance, Mr. Xiao Chen, a car owner, learned how to check the oil dipstick. During a 2,000-kilometer highway trip during the New Year holiday, fearing insufficient oil, he added a bottle of oil given by a friend. Soon after, his new car began burning oil.

Experts explained that this happened because there was too much oil in Mr. Xiao Chen's new car. When the engine operates, the crankshaft and connecting rod generate violent stirring, increasing internal power loss and causing more oil to splash onto the cylinder walls, leading to oil-burning issues. Manager Chen reminded car owners that more oil is not necessarily better; the oil level in the engine sump should be kept between the upper and lower marks on the dipstick.

5. More Expensive Car Films Are Better

Recently, at the large 2011 New Year car exhibition in Taizhou, I saw many exhibitors offering various "film gifts," such as discounts on car purchases along with a 3,000-yuan film package or free full-car films, attracting many citizens' attention. However, I learned that not all cars need films, and having films doesn't necessarily benefit the car or the human body. A repair worker from Fengnan Car Repair Co., Ltd. in Taizhou told me: "Many car films are dyed, and dyeing agents usually contain benzene and other chemicals that can harm the human body, so not all films are beneficial."

The car film industry currently suffers from excessive profits. It is understood that front windshield films can cost up to three or four thousand yuan, marketed as "gold front windshields," while ordinary films charge around two or three thousand yuan. In fact, as long as the quality is guaranteed, car films are not necessarily better when more expensive.
