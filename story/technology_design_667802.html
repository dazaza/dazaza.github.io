Rebate websites use a cash-back-on-shopping model to gather a large number of online shopping members. Members shop from various online malls through the rebate site. After completing an order, the rebate site, as a partner of the mall, receives a certain percentage of sales commission from the mall. The rebate site then returns the vast majority of this commission to its members, which is the source of the cash rebate. This does not affect any discounts or benefits the member would have otherwise received. So, how should one operate this?

Taking Aizhe.com as an example: 1) Register and log in to the rebate website → find Aizhe.com's introduction page, click to enter Aizhe.com → select and place an order for the product; 2) Under normal circumstances, within 1-25 minutes after placing an order on the mall, you can check the detailed information of the order at the "Rebate Website - Order Inquiry" section. Customer service is available at the 360 Help Center; 3) Since Aizhe.com needs to ensure that the order has not been returned before paying the commission to the rebate site, this cycle usually takes 1-2 months. At that time, the rebate site will transfer the corresponding cash rebate for the order into the member's virtual account on the rebate site. You can apply to exchange the rebate for cash by going to "My Return → My Rebate" on the rebate site; 4) After receiving the application, the rebate site generally transfers the funds to your specified bank account or Alipay within 1-4 working days.

Overall, rebate websites are safe. During the entire online shopping process, the rebate site merely plays the role of a money-saving platform.
