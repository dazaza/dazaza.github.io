Two villagers died in the collapse of the foundation pit of a water diversion project, with no security facilities on site

by zxyasdw90 on 2011-07-25 15:02:46

The sister of the deceased hugged her younger brother's feet, hoping to shake him awake. Photo by reporter Xia Derui.

Dabanchiao Town, Shangduilong Village 14:10:00

"If someone had been there giving instructions and shouted a warning, they might have had time to run into the pipe and hide to escape the disaster. Now my cousin is gone, and the family's mainstay has collapsed," said Sun Jinqi with furrowed brows.

At 14:10 yesterday, Sun Jinshou and Yang Juxian were clearing soil at the bottom of the pipeline foundation pit when the soil on one side of the pit suddenly collapsed. They didn't have time to dodge and were buried under the soil. When the workers dug them out with shovels and their bare hands, they had already stopped breathing.

Incident

Foundation Pit Collapse Buried Two Villagers

Sadness hung over a pipeline construction site beside the Shangduilong Residents Group in Dabanqiao Town, Guandu District. When Sun Jinshou and Yang Juxian were carried out from the foundation pit, they had already stopped breathing. Relatives who heard the news opened umbrellas and sat around them, their cries of grief echoing throughout the construction site.

"Brother, get up, quickly get up and go dig!" Sun Jinshou's sister shook his feet hard. She couldn't believe that her younger brother was gone. Perhaps she still harbored a last glimmer of hope, wishing that this could awaken her "sleeping" brother.

This was a construction site where pipeline laying operations were underway. Beside this foundation pit, which was about 4 meters deep and wide, a crane was parked. Not far away, an excavator that had stopped working was also present. The sides of the foundation pit were piled high with freshly dug soil, and at the bottom of the pit, two safety helmets lay quietly amidst the collapsed soil. As soon as the workers heard that reporters had come to interview, fearing to offend their boss, they all fell silent and were unwilling to recount the scene of the incident.

Mr. Sun, the husband of Yang Juxian, sat trembling uncontrollably beside his wife, unable to speak clearly. After choking back sobs several times, he gradually regained some composure. Mr. Sun said that both he and his wife were from the Xishui Nian Village of the Shangduilong Residents Group. His wife was 46 years old. More than a month ago, when the project started, he and his wife found work clearing soil from the foundation pits in the second section of the construction site, earning between fifty and sixty yuan per day. At the time of the incident, it was her first day working in the first section.

Around 2 PM, while working in the second section, Mr. Sun's mobile phone rang. The company told him, "Someone has been trapped here (in the first section foundation pit), hurry over!" Mr. Sun had a premonition that something was wrong, and the company's vehicle immediately sent him to the construction site of the first section.

At that point, one male worker had already been lifted out of the foundation pit, but his wife was still nowhere to be seen. Mr. Sun and the other workers continued digging with shovels and using their hands to clear the soil. After more than twenty minutes, he finally saw his wife under the soil. Unfortunately, she had already stopped breathing.

When asked about his two sons, Mr. Sun became even more convulsively upset. He said that his elder son had just gotten married and worked with his daughter-in-law in the Yanglin Development Zone, while his younger son was still in junior high school. He came to work on the construction site to earn money for his children's tuition fees. Last month, his wife had just received over 1000 yuan in wages to pay for her younger son's tuition. He never imagined that now the family would be separated forever between life and death.

Narration

With Someone Directing, They Could Have Escaped

Sun Jinqi covered his cousin Sun Jinshou with a plastic sheet and held an umbrella to shield him from the sun. Blood kept dripping from Sun Jinshou's head, wetting the blanket beside him and pooling into a small puddle on the plastic sheet. A shoe was placed beside his muddy feet. Sun Jinshou's sister, daughter, and granddaughter sat around him, their cries of grief echoing across the construction site.

Sun Jinqi told the reporter that the incident happened at around 14:10. The workers had just started work, and the excavator hadn't been turned on yet. The two people went down to the bottom of the foundation pit to clear the soil first, and the soil on one side of the pit suddenly collapsed. They didn't have time to dodge and were buried under the soil. "My wife was nearby at the time. If she had gone down too, she wouldn't have survived either," Sun Jinqi said, feeling somewhat fearful.

Sun Jinqi introduced that his cousin was 50 years old and also from Xishui Nian Village. His eldest son worked outside, his youngest son was in the second year of junior high school, and he also had a daughter suffering from congenital polio. The family relied on working and national subsidies to get by, "living very tough lives."

Sun Jinqi believed that there were no safety protection facilities at the scene. At the time of the incident, the two people were only focused on clearing the soil. The soil dug out from the foundation pit was piled too much and too heavy on both sides, causing the collapse. "If someone had been directing them at the time and shouted a warning, they could have run into the pipe and hidden to escape the disaster."

Guan, a worker building temporary housing, told the reporter that he worked in the second section of the construction site, earning 60 yuan per day, with accommodation provided but no meals included. There was no labor contract signed with the boss, let alone insurance. Guan mentioned that neither the first nor the second section had any safety facilities. Normally, after digging the foundation pit, the pipeline was buried directly without any additional measures. Thinking about the incident, Guan often felt lingering fear.

At 18:00, the workers set up a cordon beside the incident foundation pit, and the Guandu District Safety Supervision Bureau immediately issued a notice ordering rectification to the person in charge of the construction site.

Response

Rain Loosened Soil, Impossible to Predict

Mr. Li, the technical supervisor of the incident construction site, stated that this was one section of a water diversion project in Kunming. The project was contracted to a qualified labor company, which then hired these two villagers to work. They did not know the two deceased workers.

Ms. Zhao, the safety supervisor of the construction party, also emphasized that the contracting party was a qualified company. At the time, large machinery was not in operation, and the foundation pit had already been cleared to the bottom. The two workers went down to the foundation pit bottom to level the soil. After the incident, the workers responsible for management immediately began clearing the soil to rescue the victims, but unfortunately, it was too late to save them.

Ms. Zhao analyzed that it had rained a few days earlier, making the soil at the construction site loose. The collapse occurred where a crane had been parked a few days prior. It was likely the result of these two factors combined. "The sudden change in soil conditions is unpredictable for anyone."

Regarding why there were no safety precautions on-site, Ms. Zhao explained that their construction involved acquiring a piece of land, laying a section of pipeline, filling in a section of soil, and then restoring it for the villagers to grow crops. The work proceeded in segments with breaks in between, rather than large-scale excavation. Therefore, no safety measures were implemented. If normal construction methods were followed with large-scale excavation, safety measures would definitely have been required.

Ms. Zhao stated that next, they would cooperate actively with the safety supervision and public security departments to handle the matter and negotiate with the families regarding the aftermath arrangements for the deceased.

Reporter Wu Kun, Intern Yang Wen (Yunnan Information Newspaper)
