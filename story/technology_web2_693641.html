[Repost] In interpersonal relationships, one should restrain one's emotions.

by koucai4472 on 2012-03-06 17:15:13

People who are extremely irascible generally don't have good interpersonal relationships. Usually, getting angry shows a certain disrespect for others and is also a reflection of one's own quality. You wouldn't want others to think of you as a hedgehog, would you? Although having a fiery temper has some minor benefits, in most cases, it's not recommended, especially when interacting with others. Doing so will only damage your reputation. In fact, you won't gain anything from it. It could be said that it's "all harm and no benefit." Therefore, during social interactions, you must learn to control your fiery temper. At the very least, you should restrain yourself from being too impulsive. In social life, people adapt to their environment and seek its approval and acceptance, which is also an instinctive behavior. This mainly manifests in social interactions as getting along well with friends and colleagues, not getting angry or losing one's temper, and restraining oneself from various aspects. Next, the Oral Communication Training Network will discuss how to control one's temper.

1. Stay calm and think carefully when encountering issues. The brain is meant for thinking, and the reason why humans can be considered the wisest among all creatures is largely because we have thoughts and the ability to think through things. Therefore, during social interactions, we must learn to stay calm and avoid reacting impulsively, which is bad for both you and others. Lacking basic manners and etiquette is something that must be avoided.

2. Think more for the other party and consider problems from their perspective. Simply put, you need to learn to see things from another's point of view. Everyone's thoughts are different, and you shouldn't judge others solely based on your own ideas. By doing this, you can identify your own shortcomings to better adjust your opinions. Moreover, treat others with kindness and politeness. Each person has their own independent personality and unique character, their own living habits and interests, and their own private life that should not be interfered with by others. Respecting others, identifying your flaws, and treating them kindly and politely can demonstrate your cultivation, style, and demeanor. It can help you establish a good reputation and win the trust and respect of more friends.
