Fairchild Smart LED Lamp Driver IC

by ledlamp8 on 2012-02-28 22:35:36

A new device can solve small space, luminous body life, and energy problems. With LED lighting applications, especially the growing popularity of residential lighting applications, designers are looking for solutions that are more efficient than TRIAC and suitable for dimming with existing outlets. Additionally, these solutions must be reliable, working with high efficiency in a small space. To help designers address this challenge, Fairchild Semiconductor, a world-leading supplier of high-performance power and portable products, has developed the FL7701 Intelligent Non-Isolated Buck LED Driver with Power Factor Correction (PFC). The device uses digital technology to automatically detect AC input voltage conditions to generate an internal reference signal, achieving high PF correction. The FL7701 can also work with DC input voltage and automatically detects whether it is in AC or DC input mode. For MR lighting applications, traditional transformers cause additional wear and tear due to conversion efficiency. Now, energy-saving lamps using offline DC or AC input directly connected to the FL7701 can replace traditional low-efficiency methods.

The FL7701 LED driver combines a small form factor with high functional density, allowing designers to save up to 20% on overall bill of materials (BOM) cost and up to 60% on circuit board space. These combined features provide a solution for the limited PCB space in LED lighting applications and support analog dimming. The highly integrated design of the FL7701 provides the required high reliability and extends the service life to more than five years compared to programs lasting up to three years. The FL7701 does not require electrolytic capacitors for its input, output, or Vdd supply, further improving reliability. It achieves digital power factor (PF) (> 0.9) and low total harmonic distortion (THD), with electrical efficiency greater than 90%, providing more energy savings for end-systems. Together, these features allow the FL7701 to save energy and meet requirements such as Energy Star (Energy Star ®), the world’s most stringent regulations. Fairchild’s IC design techniques improve power efficiency (> 90%) to provide the necessary high-quality lighting for residential applications.

The FL7701 uses SOP-8 packages, making it especially suitable for interior lights; under spotlights, LED bulbs, or PAR lamps, reflector lamps, MR lamps, or mainstream E26/E27 or two-pin lampholders (Bi-Pin), and LED lights for low-power lighting equipment. Fairchild offers a complete solution series for large (> 50W), medium (20W-50W), and small power range (<20W) LED driver applications. Our highly integrated PWM and PFC controller solutions reduce the number of components in the overall bill of materials (BOM) to achieve smaller lamp sizes, simplify product design, and improve the overall efficiency of lighting. Fairchild’s product lines range from discrete components to integrated solutions containing PFC controllers, high-voltage gate drivers, and MOSFETs. Fairchild is committed to promoting energy conservation and meeting the most stringent energy efficiency regulations, developing a series of innovative products that maximize performance while reducing board space requirements, design complexity, and system cost. Moreover, Fairchild, composed of online support tools, field application engineers, and regional centers for power engineers, has established a global power resource center (Global Power ResourceSM Center) to provide customers with product design and technical support industry models.
