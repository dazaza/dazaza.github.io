Geometric Love in That Year

Accidentally flipping through the diary of that year, I remembered several popular love poems at that time called "Geometric Love". I don't know how much of that love remains today, and how much is still remembered by people. After searching on Baidu, I couldn't find those poems that left countless sorrows in our youth. Nor could I count how many still remember the ten-mile pavilion of that year.

In that year, I stood at the origin point. In the blink of an eye, it has been many years since I left the classroom, and my grass-like youth was not as wild as the grass. Instead, my belly grew more and more like a bar of soap. Life is like a ray, moving forward without looking back, leaving behind sighs and laughter. In the blink of an eye, it passed without stopping for me to express my longing. And you went along another direction, with your back to me, gradually moving further and further away until the water and sky meet. You were so far away that I could no longer see anything, yet I foolishly looked back, thinking that those laws were just words and would not cruelly separate us - never to meet again in this life.

Perhaps it's better if we are not at the same origin point. Even if we are two parallel lines, there is no longing, no absence, no reunion, and no gradual distance. Although we will never have an intersection point, you will always be on the left and I on the right bank, your left hand next to my right hand, but unfortunately, we will never have the chance to hold hands. At least when I think of you, I can see your side face, unchanged for many years. Maybe one day you will casually ask me, "Hey, you're here too," while you will never know that someone unrelated to you will wait for you by the window.

Looking back, we should be considered as two intersecting circles, not only having two intersection points, but also having more or less intersections. Even if they are small and pitiful, there are still young figures in my sight, melodious voices echoing in my ears for years, and boys with long hair in my heart. After all the hustle and bustle, we each returned to our own half circle, walking past the two intersection points without looking back, taking nothing and leaving no farewell words. From then on, the remaining two half circles, one forever in the night and the other forever in the day.

Suddenly, I am very afraid that you and I will be like hyperbolas, having missed the nearest station, unable to take a look at you at the nearest point and tell you my reluctance to part, and even unable to find the departure station, aimlessly wandering in our own latitudes. Seeing others' love bloom like summer flowers, the moment the fireworks fall, they quietly illuminate my loneliness. Perhaps, you are not in the opposite interval, perhaps you already knew my affection in some BC era, perhaps I can see your side face once again, perhaps in front of some display windows, you will smile and say to me, "Long time no see."

I wish and hope that we are like two intersecting lines, not yet reaching the unique intersection point. Perhaps, after enduring the wind for five hundred years, your appearance will pass over the bridge. Perhaps there is still an intersection point, then let's endure the rain for five hundred years, the sun exposure, and the rain for five hundred years...
