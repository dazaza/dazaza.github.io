What are the symptoms of phlebitis? Phlebitis is a peripheral vascular disease frequently encountered in clinical settings. It is an acute non-purulent inflammation occurring within the venous vessel cavity, accompanied by thrombus formation. It is a common vascular thrombotic disease and poses significant harm to human health. However, many people fail to notice its symptoms, which often delays treatment for phlebitis. What are the symptoms of phlebitis?

1. Symptoms of superficial phlebitis include sudden appearance of cord-like or columnar swelling in the superficial veins of the affected limb, with mesh-like swelling. The affected vein tissues become red and swollen or edematous, with increased local skin temperature, palpable heat, obvious tenderness and pressure pain, restricted limb movement, and generally mild systemic reactions such as slight fever and discomfort. After rest or treatment, redness, heat, and swelling gradually subside, leaving behind dark brown or dark red pigmentation.

2. Symptoms of deep vein phlebitis commonly occur in the calves, thoracic veins, and femoral iliac veins of the lower limbs. The former causes calf swelling, while the latter mainly causes thigh swelling. The affected limb swells in a cylindrical shape, accompanied by pain that worsens with walking. There are pressure marks at the distal end, with light gray-purple skin and significantly dilated superficial veins. In some cases, it can turn into chronic venous return obstruction, leading to varicose veins in the superficial veins of the affected limb, thrombophlebitis, and even congestive leg ulcers with infection.
