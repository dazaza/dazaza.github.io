Qingfeng City, a place that makes martial artists pale at the mention of it. No one knows where this city is located, but those who leave it are not only breathtakingly beautiful but also highly skilled in music, chess, calligraphy, painting, divination, astrology, and more. More chillingly, their martial arts skills are unmatched. Someone once witnessed the abilities of Mudan, the head of the Mudan Hall, but they were never able to speak again. When asked by others in the martial world, all that could be seen on the face of those in the know was terror and standing hair. There are also those who have seen the eerie lightness of Yueshuang, the head of Feihua Hall, disappearing in an instant with just the scent of flowers lingering in the air.

If these were merely rumors, then the thousand-faced disguise technique of Yuxin, the head of Meixie Hall, would be enough to make everyone tremble with fear. No one knows if a close friend or lover might suddenly stab them and laughingly reveal themselves as a demon, a demon in the rain.

Thus, such an organization in the martial world commands respect from all, though this respect is laced with inner fear.

(1)

On the tenth day of the tenth lunar month, late autumn brought a cool breeze. Maple leaves, red like blood, twirled and fell onto the roof of an inn.

Ziye sat in a street-facing position inside the inn, lazily leaning against the wall, one leg resting on a stool, carelessly watching the bustling crowd outside. He always had that careless demeanor.

This city appeared calm, no different from any other. Yet, the world often works this way: the quieter the water, the more turbulent the hidden currents beneath.

Ziye knew that here was the main base of Qingfeng Hall.

On the table were already two empty jugs of huadiao wine, but the person he awaited had yet to come. Ziye grew restless, shifting his back off the wall, straightening his waist, moving his numb leg off the stool, and changing his sitting posture. Now he could see whoever entered or exited the inn.

He knew the person he waited for would never break their promise. Despite years of acquaintance, he and she had developed a deep understanding. Though her coldness deterred others, it was not directed at him.

As the third jug of huadiao wine neared its end, Ziye smiled gently. His smile was because of a woman who appeared at the inn's entrance, adorned with red hairpins and silver jewelry, her snow-white robes trailing on the ground. Her skin, like congealed fat, was as white as snow. Although it was still deep autumn, the desolate coldness emanating from her made all the patrons in the inn shiver involuntarily. Indeed, her name suited her well—Xueluo, the one he awaited.

No one saw how she arrived; she simply appeared silently like a snowflake, lightly landing beside Ziye's table.

"You've come." Ziye's gentle smile remained unchanged after many years, peaceful like the weather in March.

Seeing Ziye's familiar smile again, Xueluo's heart ached momentarily as memories of tender times resurfaced. She clearly remembered the day he left, when snowflakes fell continuously from the sky. From that day onward, she changed her name to Xueluo.

Yes, once named Shuluo, she and Ziye had deeply loved each other, but due to societal norms and family expectations, they were forced to part ways. Now, Ziye was a father, while she remained alone, aging gracefully.

"You've lost weight again." Xueluo softly spoke, her voice melodious like a bird's song.

"Is that so? I don't feel it." Ziye's smile faded, his face now filled with frost. Indeed, if it weren't for his master, the former leader of Qingfeng Hall and Xueluo's father who opposed their union, they should have become a celestial couple.

"Speak, why did you urgently summon me?" Ziye's tone remained rigid.

Tears welled up in Xueluo's eyes but she held them back. She knew Ziye hadn't been happy in these years, even somewhat dispirited. But what could she do? Her father had told her that her destiny carried a floral calamity, and if she married, her husband would meet an untimely death according to fate. However, could she tell Ziye this? No, she couldn't. She could only keep this secret to herself.

"The demon's disguise manual has been stolen." Xueluo slowly took her seat.

"It's none of my concern." Ziye's expression remained emotionless.

"Of course, it concerns you." A shadow appeared on the windowsill, laughing cheerfully at Ziye and Xueluo. The red attire fluttered gently in the autumn wind, and the green embroidered shoes swung freely in the air.

"Duanhua, can't you come down and talk?" A hint of a smile appeared on Xueluo's lips.

Everyone in the martial world knew that within Qingfeng Hall, there were two most dazzling flowers—one as cold as ice, the other as fiery as fire. These were Mudan and Xueluo.

So, Ziye also knew.

Yet, Ziye often wondered how such contrasting personalities could get along so harmoniously.

"First, the stolen disguise manuals are the fruits of your master's labor. Second, this book records some secrets of Lu'er (Xueluo). What exactly, I will tell you later when you find those manuals." After finishing, Mudan playfully winked at Xueluo.

Xueluo lowered her long lashes, appearing composed.

Ziye looked at Mudan, then turned to look at Xueluo. With a heavy sigh, it seemed this matter was unavoidable.

2

On the sixteenth day of the tenth lunar month, the setting sun cast its glow as yellow leaves quivered in the wind. Autumn was nearing its end.

Inside the main headquarters, Ximen Chuixue, the unseen leader, paced back and forth anxiously. Zhulin Qingke, the deputy leader, sat solemnly in a corner. They knew that if the secrets of Qingfeng Hall were revealed to the martial world, the hall would face a catastrophic disaster.

All members of Qingfeng Hall knew that the so-called disguise manuals were far from being mere appearances. They contained all the martial arts secrets and confidential documents of Qingfeng Hall.

The stolen manuals not only recorded the secrets of the holy maiden Xueluo but also the sword techniques of the Ximen family. In the past, the old ancestor Chuixue wielded a sword that dominated the martial world for decades without encountering a worthy opponent. Alas! Thinking of this, Ximen Chuixue sighed deeply.

"Has the head of Dianhua Hall, Anzhilan Ting, arrived?" Zhulin Qingke's raspy voice broke the tense silence.

"Long ago." As soon as the sound ended, a figure appeared.

Anzhilan Ting's Dianhua Hall was responsible for routine inspections of all branches of Qingfeng Hall. Upon receiving urgent reports and recall orders, she hurried back without rest several days ago.

"How is Ziye's investigation going?" Zhulin Qingke asked in the same raspy voice, like bamboo peeling.

"There hasn't been much change. He just wanders around every day, eating and sleeping."

"Are Mudan and Xueluo always by his side?"

"Yes, they haven't left his side for a moment." Anzhilan Ting's response was both lively and sharp.

"Hmm, understood. You may rest early." A fragrant breeze passed, and Anzhilan Ting's figure disappeared instantly.

"Chuixue, can we really trust Ziye?" Zhulin Qingke turned back.

"Probably. He was the former leader's prized disciple and left Qingfeng Hall angrily because of his relationship with Xueluo. Now, since this matter involves Xueluo, wouldn't he give it his all? Besides, Ziye's tracking skills are unparalleled under heaven. To my knowledge, no one has ever escaped him." Ximen Chuixue gazed at the increasingly dense twilight, filled with myriad thoughts.

"Hmm." In the darkness, Zhulin Qingke's eyebrows twitched slightly.

Night finally enveloped Qingfeng Hall in darkness.

In the southeast corner of the wing room, a shadow flashed out, leaping several times before vanishing. If the enchanting lightness of Yueshuang, the head of Feihua Hall, was considered a top-level expert, then this person's lightness could be ranked as a top-tier expert.

Indeed, this shadow was Ziye. After several days of covert investigation, he discovered many suspicious points. First, Meixie Hall was heavily guarded; even a fly would find it difficult to pass through its defenses. Second, the thief specifically targeted certain manuals concerning Qingfeng Hall's secrets, which would require insider knowledge to execute cleanly. Thus, he could only investigate at night to avoid alarming anyone prematurely.

3

On the twentieth day of the tenth lunar month, morning light pierced through the autumn mist. Chrysanthemums bloomed gorgeously.

"Truly beautiful!" Ziye marveled at Qingfeng Hall amidst the mist. How long had it been since he last returned? Five years? Ten? He didn't dare to calculate.

"Hehe! Then don't go back to your Peach Blossom City. Stay and accompany Lu'er." On the pavilion eaves, Mudan's green embroidered shoes swayed. She always appeared unexpectedly.

"Mudan, what are you saying about me?" Xueluo glanced sideways at Ziye, blushing and turning away.

Ziye suddenly felt a pang in his heart, a slight ache in his nose. It wasn't that he didn't want to, but some things were beyond his control.

"Can you perform Peach Blossom Branding?" Mudan persisted relentlessly.

"Hmm."

"Wonderful! Hehe! Lu'er has hope!"

"How so?" Ziye was confused.

"Ah! Fate!" Mudan sighed wistfully and continued: "You must have heard the legend of the Holy Maiden of Qingfeng Hall."

"No."

Deep in Mudan's eyes, there was a misty haze: "It's said that every fifty years, a Holy Maiden is born in Qingfeng Hall. Their destinies conflict with the floral calamity, and if they marry, they will surely harm their husbands. Each Holy Maiden has a plum blossom mark on their right shoulder. Among the four Holy Maidens Qingfeng Hall has produced, two who disregarded this and married died within three days. The next two spent their lives alone. However, it's recorded that only the Heart Sutra of Peach Blossom Branding can remove this calamity. Unfortunately, the art of Peach Blossom Branding has been lost, despite numerous searches. Therefore, the former leader forbade Lu'er from marrying you, truly out of necessity."

"Who would have thought that after you left, you learned Peach Blossom Branding. Isn't that fate?" Mudan's tone shifted, her smile blossoming, captivatingly beautiful.

Ziye was stunned. He never imagined that everything back then was done for his own good. After leaving in anger, he destroyed his Qingfeng Hall skills, wandering into a valley and accidentally learning an art that was actually the lost Peach Blossom Branding.

In an instant, all resentment, all grievances, all doubts dissipated like the mist before him, gradually fading away. Some things aren't as simple as they seem. Ziye's eyes became moist.

"Lu'er, you've suffered injustice." Seeing Xueluo's tears, Ziye was filled with remorse. He recalled his harsh attitude towards her over the years.

"We know you'll eventually discover that we stole the disguise manuals, but we only took the Holy Maiden Scripture. Why were Chuixue's sword manuals also lost?" Xueluo wiped her tears, stepping forward to change the subject.

"That's right! There's a traitor within Qingfeng Hall, clearly framing us. In haste, Xueluo thought of finding you to investigate." Mudan spoke quickly.

Looking at the increasingly dim courtyard of Qingfeng Hall, the pale yellow chrysanthemums swayed gently in the morning breeze. Ziye's lips curved slightly upward, his brows furrowed as a figure came to mind.

4

On the twenty-fifth day of the tenth lunar month, drizzling autumn rain soaked the chrysanthemums in the courtyard, tearing them apart.

Qingfeng Hall was filled with various division leaders, whispering among themselves. Only the two leaders maintained serious expressions, silent.

Amongst the falling petals, Ziye and Xueluo walked slowly.

"Ziye, I hear from Mudan that you've uncovered the truth?" Chief Leader Ximen Chuixue's tone lacked the usual heaviness.

The hall fell silent, except for Deputy Leader Zhulin Qingke, whose expression turned frantic.

"That's correct, the truth is clear." Ziye retrieved the Holy Maiden Scripture from his bosom.

"How is there only one volume?" The charming division leaders were momentarily stunned, then erupted in chatter.

"Exactly, this Holy Maiden Scripture triggered the simultaneous theft of the other volumes." Ziye's gaze swept across the room, focusing on Zhulin Qingke who feigned composure.

"Regarding the legend of the Holy Maiden of Qingfeng Hall, I'm sure the various division leaders and leaders understand better than I do!" Ziye continued calmly: "This scripture holds no value to others but is a treasure for the Holy Maiden of Qingfeng Hall. Thus, Xueluo stole it to free herself from her destined entanglement."

"Hmm... That's understandable." Ximen Chuixue nodded slightly.

"What about the other volumes?" The previously silent Yuxin Demoness finally spoke.

"Well asked. This matter requires an explanation from your Deputy Leader Zhulin Qingke." Ziye smiled faintly, his gaze sharp like an arrow, directly targeting Deputy Leader Zhulin Qingke.

Under everyone's gaze, Zhulin Qingke's complexion turned from greenish to ashen white. He realized that a fleeting greed had plunged him into eternal damnation.

"Let me explain." Two pairs of feet—one in light green and one in pink embroidery—appeared before the assembly. They were Mudan and Anzhilan Ting. In Mudan's hands were the stolen volumes, matching perfectly with the Holy Maiden Scripture Ziye held, accounting for the exact number of stolen books: "That night, Xueluo and I quietly infiltrated the secret chamber of Meixie Hall and stole the Holy Maiden Scripture. However, we were spotted by Zhulin Qingke on patrol. Thus, he went further, stealing the sword techniques of Chuixue he had long coveted, including some secret disguise manuals of Qingfeng Hall."

"Later, I informed Ziye that only three people could enter this hall—the main and deputy leaders, and the head of Yuxin Demoness. That night, coincidentally, it was Deputy Leader Zhulin Qingke on patrol. So Ziye deduced that it must be the deputy leader who committed the theft." Mudan paused briefly: "With the permission of the Chief Leader, Anzhilan Ting and I searched the deputy leader's bedroom and found these disguise manuals."

As soon as Mudan finished speaking, Zhulin Qingke sprang from his seat, like a wisp of smoke heading toward the door.

However, faster figures blocked his path—Ziye, Xueluo, and Anzhilan Ting from Dianhua Hall.

"Let him go!" Ximen Chuixue's melancholic voice was filled with helplessness. After years of acquaintance, how could he bring himself to enforce the hall's rules?

5

In the eleventh lunar month, winter arrived. The first snowflakes fell gently. Plum blossoms exuded fragrance, swaying elegantly outside the study window.

The plum blossom on Xueluo's right shoulder was gradually fading.

"Lu'er, congratulations, you're finally free from the constraints of fate." Mudan remained as boisterous as ever.

Xueluo's gaze was bashful, her cheeks flushed pink: "Where is Ziye?"

"Oh! Haven't seen him for several days. Could he have left?"

Tears streamed down Xueluo's eyes. He had indeed left. Even if she was no longer the Holy Maiden, what did it matter if her beloved wasn't by her side? All worldly affairs passed by like a tragic yet beautiful dream.

Outside Qingfeng City, on the snowy path leading to Peach Blossom City, a solitary trail of footprints extended endlessly to the horizon…
