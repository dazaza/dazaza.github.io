I. Ground Source Heat Pump Cooling Principle

In cooling mode, the compressor inside the ground source heat pump unit works on the refrigerant, causing it to undergo a gas-liquid conversion cycle. Through the evaporation of the refrigerant in the refrigerant/air heat exchanger, the heat carried by the indoor air circulation is absorbed into the refrigerant. As the refrigerant circulates, the condensation of the refrigerant in the refrigerant/water heat exchanger causes the heat carried by the refrigerant to be absorbed through the water loop circulation, eventually transferring the heat to the groundwater or soil. During the continuous transfer of indoor heat to the ground, cold air at 13°C or lower is supplied to the room via the refrigerant-air heat exchanger.

II. Ground Source Heat Pump Heating Principle

In heating mode, the compressor inside the ground source heat pump unit works on the refrigerant and changes its flow direction through a four-way valve. The water loop circulation absorbs the heat from the groundwater or soil, and through the evaporation of the refrigerant in the refrigerant/water heat exchanger, the heat in the water loop is absorbed into the refrigerant. As the refrigerant circulates, the condensation of the refrigerant in the refrigerant/air heat exchanger causes the heat carried by the refrigerant to be absorbed through the air circulation. During the continuous transfer of heat from underground to indoors, heating is provided to the room in the form of forced convection, natural convection, or radiation.

The Development Trend of Ground Source Heat Pumps

With the development of the economy and the improvement of people's living standards, heating and air conditioning in public buildings and residences have become universal requirements. Traditional coal-fired boilers used for heating in China not only have low energy efficiency but also cause serious atmospheric pollution, so they are being gradually phased out in some cities, while oil and gas boilers have high operating costs. The ground source heat pump is an alternative way to solve heating and air conditioning that has significant advantages in both technology and economics. In the United States, ground source heat pump air conditioning systems account for 40% of all air conditioning systems and are vigorously promoted by the US government as an energy-saving and environmentally friendly technology. In 1998, the US Department of Energy issued regulations requiring the promotion of geothermal heat pump air conditioning systems with buried pipe soil heat exchangers in federal government buildings nationwide. To show support for this technology, President Bush installed such a ground source heat pump air conditioning system in his villa in Texas. Currently, in Central and Northern European countries such as Switzerland, Sweden, Austria, and Denmark, the use of ground source heat pump (soil heat exchanger) technology is leading, and buried pipe soil heat exchanger heat pumps are widely applied.

The Origin of Ground Source Heat Pumps

The concept of "ground source heat pump" was first proposed in 1912 by Swiss experts, and the technology originated in the UK and the USA.

In 1946, the first ground source heat pump system was built in the central district of Portland, Oregon, USA. However, this method of energy utilization did not attract widespread attention from various sectors of society at the time, and there was little technical or theoretical development.

In the 1950s, Europe began the first wave of research on ground source heat pumps. However, due to low energy prices at the time, this system was not economical, so it was not promoted. It wasn't until the first global energy crisis in the early 1970s that it began to receive attention, and many companies started researching, producing, and installing ground source heat pumps. During this period, many horizontal buried pipe soil source ground source heat pumps were established in Europe, mainly for winter heating. Although Europe is the most mature region in the world for the development of ground source heat pumps, it also took a detour because of factors such as the lack of installation skills among ground source heat pump experts and the lack of knowledge about ground source heat pump principles among installation workers.

With the advancement of science and technology, laws regarding energy consumption and environmental pollution have become increasingly strict, and the development of ground source heat pumps has reached another peak. Countries like Switzerland, Sweden, and Austria in Europe have vigorously promoted ground source heat pump heating and cooling technologies. Governments have implemented corresponding subsidy policies and protective measures, rapidly expanding the production and usage scope of ground source heat pumps. By the late 1980s, ground source heat pump technology had matured, and more scientists focused on studying underground systems, striving to improve heat absorption and heat transfer efficiency while paying increasing attention to environmental impacts. The production of ground source heat pumps showed an annual upward trend, with growth rates exceeding 10% annually in Switzerland and Sweden. The production and promotion of ground source heat pumps in the United States progressed rapidly, with technology advancing swiftly, making the U.S. the world's largest producer and user of ground source heat pumps.

From the application perspective of ground source heat pumps, Northern European countries mainly focus on winter heating, whereas the U.S. emphasizes year-round supply. Since the climate conditions in the U.S. are very similar to those in China, studying the application of ground source heat pumps in the U.S. can provide valuable reference for the development of ground source heat pumps in China.

According to statistics from 1999, the proportion of ground source heat pumps in family-used heating equipment: Switzerland 96%, Austria 38%, Denmark 27%. Due to the special pricing of energy in our country, along with people's understanding of energy conservation and environmental protection, as well as other factors, the application and development of ground source heat pump air conditioning technology in our country has been relatively slow, and people are not yet fully familiar with it, making promotion difficult. However, with the improvement of people's living standards, the increase in per capita energy consumption, the gradual depletion of one-time mineral energy sources, and the worsening environment, ground source heat pump technology is attracting more attention. Under the current trend of energy conservation and environmental protection, this technology has caught the industry's attention for its unique energy-saving and stability characteristics. Many domestic universities and research institutes have conducted extensive application studies.

The Ministry of Construction specifically recommended ground source heat pump systems in the "Design Standard for Energy Conservation of Residential Buildings in Summer Hot and Winter Cold Regions." To successfully host the Olympic Games, the relevant departments in Beijing and scientific research institutions collaborated closely, conducting advanced technology researches successively. Domestic and international experts proposed that clean energy represented by ground source heat pumps aligns with the "Green Olympics, Technology Olympics" philosophy and should be promoted and used in Olympic construction projects. After detailed study and evaluation, the ground source heat pump central air conditioning was designated as the selected type of central air conditioning for the 2008 Beijing Olympics. Many years ago, ground source heat pumps were already in use in national government buildings, with the most representative example being Chairman Mao's Memorial Hall. On July 1, 2007, the Beijing Development and Reform Commission and eight other ministries jointly released the "Notice on Issuing Guidelines for Developing Heat Pump Systems" (Beijing Development and Reform [2006] No. 839). The "Guidelines" clearly stipulated that a subsidy of 50 yuan per square meter would be provided for using ground source heat pump air conditioning systems in buildings, with a subsidy of 35 yuan for water source heat pumps.

Since last year, the state has selected three cities as pilot cities for ground source heat pumps: Beijing, Tianjin, and Shenyang, vigorously developing ground source heat pumps. Now, in these three cities and even nationwide, the development of ground source heat pumps is flourishing. The state is actively guiding the development of ground source heat pumps. Some government department buildings, schools, hospitals, etc., have undergone ground source heat pump renovations. With the Olympics approaching, many projects are also using ground source heat pumps, such as some Olympic venues and the Olympic Forest Park. Not just national projects, many private enterprise office locations and villa areas are also looking for manufacturers and dealers of ground source heat pumps. While we are seeking projects, they are also seeking us.

The ground source heat pump is a new energy utilization technology that uses shallow geothermal energy for heating and cooling. It is a type of ground source heat pump that transfers cold and heat using the Carnot and reverse Carnot cycle principles. Typically, a ground source heat pump refers to a device that transfers heat or cold from underground soil to where it is needed. Usually, ground source heat pumps are used for air conditioning cooling or heating. Additionally, ground source heat pumps utilize the enormous thermal storage capacity of underground soil, transferring heat from underground soil to buildings in winter and cold to buildings in summer, forming an annual cold and heat cycle.
