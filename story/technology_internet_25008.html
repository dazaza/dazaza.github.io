Ridiculous网通: "Automatic disconnection upon connection" suspended in less than half a year

by 127702 on 2007-07-17 17:55:30

Beijing Netcom announced on its broadband portal website www.bbn.com.cn yesterday that it would suspend, starting from July 15, the automatic disconnection measure for broadband users who had been online continuously for 48 hours, a measure which was introduced the previous week.

Due to external doubts and criticism, Beijing Netcom announced the postponement of the execution of the "disconnection after continuous 48-hour internet use" measure. The continued implementation of this policy will depend on the opinions and suggestions from Netcom users and all sectors of society.

Journalists confirmed yesterday from Beijing Netcom that, starting from July 15, the company stopped this measure. Beijing Netcom's 10086 customer service staff told reporters that in recent times, many customers and media outlets have raised objections to this "internet disconnection regulation," causing inconvenience to daily network usage, leading the company to decide to temporarily halt the new regulation. In the future, the company will further listen to the opinions and suggestions of a wide range of users and all sectors of society, thoroughly argue the case before introducing relevant services.

A week ago, Beijing Netcom launched the "disconnection after continuous 48-hour internet use" policy for all its broadband users, including ADSL monthly subscription users, hourly package users, and community broadband users. Beijing Netcom stated that this move aims to protect the interests of most users, avoiding high network usage fees due to misoperation or the installation of routers with automatic dialing functions.

The introduction of this policy has elicited different reactions from users. For hourly package users, if they forget to disconnect during internet use, their accumulated online time is likely to exceed the time included in their monthly package. According to the company’s current charging standards, users will have to pay a high fee of 3 yuan per hour for the excess part. In interviews with journalists, internal personnel from Beijing Netcom once indicated that the launch of "disconnection after continuous 48-hour internet use" also helps prevent ADSL account theft. It was also revealed that after automatic disconnection, users can reconnect to the broadband again. Some ADSL users with automatic dialing functions can also reconnect by setting up automatic dialing functions.

However, for monthly subscription users, since they pay a fixed monthly internet fee without additional charges for overtime, some users feel that the new policy affects their normal internet life, which is also the biggest controversy from the outside. Some users even pointed out that when signing up for the monthly subscription service, Beijing Netcom did not specifically mention or propose this clause, so it does not have the right to unilaterally change the content of the service terms.

Although the "disconnection measure" has been postponed, Beijing Netcom still emphasized that when using ADSL products for internet access, users should ensure that their internet devices comply with national network access permits and relevant regulations, and pay attention to their own online duration and limits.

Netcom stated that the purpose of the disconnection measure is to avoid non-monthly subscription users incurring high network usage fees due to misoperations or the installation of routers with automatic dialing functions. Netcom expressed that after further listening to the opinions and suggestions of users and related parties, they will carefully consider and introduce new user protection measures.

Some users believe that the disconnection measure is unfair to those who have already paid for monthly or annual internet subscriptions; if monthly subscription users are conducting online banking transactions or stock trading, it could lead to unnecessary losses. Furthermore, Beijing Netcom's action is essentially limiting the download behavior of some BT (a type of network download technology where users leave their computers on 24/7 to download, potentially occupying a large amount of other users' bandwidth).

Beijing Netcom reminded users that when using ADSL for internet access, they should use network access devices that meet national network access permits, and be mindful of whether their online duration exceeds the monthly subscription limit to protect their rights. Users should also take care to keep and regularly update their broadband access account (ADSL\community broadband) passwords to prevent unauthorized use by others.

Yesterday, Beijing Netcom also disclosed a potential fraud risk during broadband internet use: users should be cautious if they receive pop-up windows similar to password verification prompts while online. Prompts such as “System detected your ADSL account being simultaneously logged in on another machine” or “System detected another computer using this account for internet access, please log in again” asking for personal information like username, phone number, and password, or requesting changes to personal passwords, are illegal actions mimicking Netcom's name, logo, and business webpage interface. These actions may lead to the leakage of user account information and subsequent unauthorized consumption. If users have accessed similar interfaces, they should promptly modify their account passwords to avoid losses.
