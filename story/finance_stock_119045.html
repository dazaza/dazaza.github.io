Vanke's internal judgment: housing prices will hit bottom in 2012

by weiwei0518 on 2009-01-13 21:42:25

On January 5, Vanke announced its results for 2008. Although it fell short of the adjusted plan, it was better than market expectations. Despite firmly holding the top position, Vanke still felt significant pressure.

The market has proven that Vanke's predictions about the real estate market have always been accurate. Regarding when the bottom will be reached, opinions vary, and Vanke has extended its original prediction of a bottom in 2009 to 2012.

Senior management bonuses are not secure

On the evening of January 5, Vanke announced its sales performance for the last month of 2008. In December, Vanke achieved a sales area of 6.61 million square meters and sales revenue of 5.34 billion yuan. Affected by the year-end home-buying peak and macroeconomic policies, there was a noticeable increase in December, with month-on-month increases of 57.0% and 49.6%, respectively.

Thus, Vanke cumulatively achieved a sales area of 55.7 million square meters and sales revenue of 47.87 billion yuan in 2008, representing decreases of 9.2% and 8.6% compared to 2007, respectively.

Despite the reduction in performance, Vanke remains the industry leader, widening the gap with its competitors. Currently, Vanke's market share has increased from 2% at the beginning of 2008 to around 2.6%. Even so, Vanke still feels significant pressure.

Ping An Securities real estate analyst Liu Xihui told reporters that this result is basically in line with Vanke's full-year plan. The sales situation in December was even better than expected, and after recovering some funds, Vanke's cash flow has become more stable than before. Financially, Vanke continues to cut expenses and minimize costs as much as possible.

According to information obtained by the reporter, the 2008 year-end bonus, which constitutes a large portion of employees' salaries, will also be reduced from top to bottom: no bonuses for senior management, half for middle management, and full issuance for lower-level employees. The Vanke management team voluntarily reducing their salaries has won them public support.

In the past, Vanke would always make plans for the next year's sales targets at the end of the year, but times have changed, and now Vanke appears very conservative.

A senior executive inside Vanke told reporters that this economic crisis has expanded from local to global and from finance to the macro level, commonly referred to as a once-in-a-century event. It is already difficult to accurately predict the market, so we must prepare for the worst-case scenario. "Vanke has not made a sales target forecast for 2009; we can only follow the market and will not set limits for ourselves. There is no need or significance."

A marketing manager from Vanke Dongguan told reporters that they are under a lot of pressure. The market in 2009 will be very challenging. Vanke Dongguan's new projects for 2009 have not been reduced and remain the same as in 2008, with five projects. "After all, the market capacity is limited, and many new projects will open in 2009, leading to fierce competition."

Price cuts in exchange for cash recovery in 2009

From an internal Vanke employee, I learned that Vanke originally predicted the bottom of housing prices would occur at the end of 2009, but it has now been adjusted to 2012. Clearly, Vanke has made the worst-case prediction and preparation regarding the market.

"From a general economic cycle perspective, it takes five years to emerge from a low point, but the real estate bubble experiences in Japan and other countries cannot be used as references because urbanization processes and economic levels differ," said real estate expert Wu Qilun.

Liu Xihui believes that current housing prices are still hovering at high levels. In the context of an economic crisis, income relative to housing prices is not low. For instance, in Shenzhen, housing prices within the city generally exceed ten thousand yuan, leaving significant downward space in the property market.

The market trend in 2009 is hard to stabilize, and housing price trends mainly depend on income. Policies can bring short-term warming to the market, and the real estate market may behave similarly to the stock market, rebounding slightly before continuing to decline. "But a straight decline is difficult; mostly, it will slowly decrease," Liu Xihui indicated.

For 2009, Vanke believes that playing three strategic moves should be enough to cope with the worst-case market conditions. First, ensuring company safety from a financial perspective; second, seizing opportunities to expand market share during the adjustment cycle; third, enhancing professional capabilities as much as possible.

To ensure financial security, apart from cutting daily expenses and minimizing land acquisitions that tie up funds, the most important thing is to recover funds through price reductions to drive sales. "The specific discount range will be determined based on the market," said a sales manager from Vanke Dongguan.

Market evidence shows that Wang Shi's turning point theory proposed at the end of 2007 was correct. Leading the way with price reductions allowed Vanke to continue firmly holding the top position in the industry. It cannot be denied that Vanke has a good grasp of the market, so every word and action by Vanke draws particular attention from outsiders.

"It's better to be overly pessimistic than blindly optimistic," said a senior Vanke executive to reporters.

One year after proposing the turning point theory, Wang Shi again expressed the view that "there's no need to wait two or three years to buy a house." He explained that the turning point refers to people who cannot afford homes, while those with the ability to buy houses can enter the market now. Industry insiders analyzed that, besides needing to make the most accurate judgment for enterprise management itself, Wang Shi is also politically astute. To expand domestic demand, the country hopes that policies and developers' price reductions will jointly lead to a recovery in the real estate market. Of course, Wang Shi will seize the opportunity accordingly.
