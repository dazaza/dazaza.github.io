The Origin of Campus Street Dance

by longersing on 2009-05-23 14:49:03

Campus street dance, also known as HIP-HOP, originates from black street dance. Therefore, another name for it is STREET FUNK. Campus street dance has naturally gained the favor of young people due to its relaxed, casual, free personality and spirit of rebellion. After filtering out the original street dance's roughness and exaggeration, campus street dance rightfully ascended to the grand stage —— the gym.

HIP-HOP is the general term for various types of campus street dance, including mechanical dance and breakdance. It originated from the impromptu dance moves of American street dancers. These street dancers were mainly black or Mexican, and most of these popular campus street dances originated in Brooklyn, New York. Some black or Mexican children spent their days dancing on the streets, forming various factions, and naturally developed different styles in the dances they performed.

In the early 1990s, a type of "stationary HIP-HOP" emerged. It lacked large movements and footwork transitions, and even more so, it did not include gymnastic-like moves on the ground found in breakdancing. Its unique style lies in emphasizing body coordination, focusing on the rhythm of the upper body, and adding many head and hand movements. This new style of dance can be seen in the MTVs of Michael Jackson, Mariah Carey, and Backstreet Boys.

In the daily training of professional aerobics athletes, this HIP-HOP is often used to train the athletes' coordination, expressiveness, and other comprehensive qualities, and sometimes it is also used for performances. In terms of external expression, campus street dance has moderate exercise intensity, making it possible to enter the gym. However, the selection of actions in the gym version of campus street dance places greater emphasis on safety, fitness value, health promotion, and individual expression. Therefore, practitioners burn fat while also relieving mental stress. Personally, I believe that the HIP-HOP in the gym plays a more prominent role in regulating the practitioner's psychology. Therefore, some people call it "the only exercise that makes people smile while training."

The classifications of campus street dance are:

Hip hop is the most commonly encountered type of dance by people. It has large and simple steps that can express complex dance feelings. Because it is easy to learn and looks good when danced, it is very popular with the public.

Mechanical dance uses the muscles and joints of various parts of the body, following the beat of the music, and adds rich imagination to create surprising dance steps. It belongs to a higher-difficulty type of campus street dance.

Breakdance is also a high-difficulty type of dance step, which can generally be divided into two types: spinning on hands, heads, or bodies on the ground, called 'floor work'; using limbs to perform complex changing footwork on the ground, plus tricky handstands, called 'footwork'. Of course, while dancing, you can freely combine the actions you want to express.

Free style is a dance step that blends various types of dances together, expressing itself freely without any limits on dance style, breaking away from the norms of regular dances. It can be considered a personalized type of campus street dance.

House is a type of dance step performed with complex and magical footwork following House music. It can incorporate the waist-twisting of Latin dance, the aerial flips of martial arts, the basic steps of tap dance, and the pirouettes of ballet. When danced, it can be both very elegant and extremely wild.

HIP-HOP entry-level

You can choose some HIP-HOP music to get familiar with its rhythm characteristics and move along with the HIP-HOP rhythm casually. When learning with a coach, follow the coach's breakdown of actions, such as first learning the lower body movements, then gradually adding the upper body and head movements, starting slowly and speeding up later without rushing for quick results.

Fitness value of HIP-HOP

The explosive power of the actions, mostly involving circular motions and small joint movements, can improve the practitioner's coordination ability quite well, and fully exercise the small joints and small muscle groups that are not usually moved. Combined with moderate exercise intensity, it achieves the effect of aerobic exercise, allowing the practitioner to improve cardio-pulmonary function while achieving weight loss goals. Practitioners can also choose music of different rhythms according to their own physical condition and exercise foundation to adjust the intensity of the exercise.
