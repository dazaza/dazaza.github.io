Why Apple Won't Compete With Facebook For Waze

by geekzhang on 2013-05-11 11:42:08

May 11, Beijing Time - According to foreign media reports, rumors have been circulating since the beginning of this year that Apple is also considering bidding for the social navigation app company Waze. However, at that time, Apple quickly clarified that it had no intention of acquiring Waze. But in recent days, a flood of messages from informed sources has emerged.Currently, Facebook seems to be leading the charge, initiating acquisition negotiations with Waze first. It's reported that Facebook’s offer is quite substantial. Amidst these developments, rumors about Apple's renewed interest in Waze have also surfaced, claiming that Apple is one of the main participants in the bidding negotiations. Back in January, some reports indicated that Apple and Waze had already engaged in some negotiations prior to that.However, according to some insiders, there are significantly different attitudes and views between Apple and Waze regarding the tone and content of those negotiations. The extent of the differences remains unknown as of now.At present, Facebook appears to be intensifying its pursuit of Waze, raising the acquisition bid to over a billion dollars. In light of this, people can't help but wonder: will Apple intervene? The answer is highly likely. After all, last year's disastrous outcome with Apple's iOS Maps provides a good reason for the company to seek external alternative map data resources. The question remains, if Apple really wanted to acquire Waze, why didn't it complete the deal then? Especially when Apple was in a crisis situation, why didn’t it acquire Waze earlier to improve the map user experience?Here are four possible reasons why Apple may not want to acquire Waze:First, and possibly the most significant reason, is that a large amount of Waze's data is collected through its Google Android clients. If Apple were to acquire Waze, it would need to provide a perfect answer to the Android issue. So far, Apple does not seem to have any corresponding answers. If it abandons Android users, Apple risks losing a large amount of data and user credibility. Supporting an application from its main competitor in the mobile field would also pose a dilemma for Apple.Second, even if Apple fully acquires Waze, the integration could raise serious concerns. Apple has already absorbed a large amount of map data into its own mapping terminal. When iOS Maps launched, it used data from more than 20 sources. Adding another channel like Waze would require employees to invest more engineering efforts and perhaps expend significant energy to check Waze's data to ensure its accuracy and completeness.Third, acquiring Waze would require Apple to rethink, or possibly even restructure, its current map strategy and potentially the patent deals on which Apple currently relies.Fourth, and lastly, Waze's asking price is very high. According to information from informed sources, Waze CEO Noam Bardin has no problem inflating low-ball offers, but now, with Facebook showing willingness to acquire Waze for $1 billion, this sum is already considerable for Bardin. Apple has never historically paid a high price for any acquisition, but Facebook, driven by ambitions in mobility and localization, might be willing to spend more to acquire Waze.Of course, to put it another way, anything is possible, but under these circumstances, it would be completely illogical for Apple to acquire Waze.Currently, Apple continues to refuse to comment on its relationship with Waze and declines to comment on their past negotiations. Meanwhile, Waze also refuses to make any comments.
