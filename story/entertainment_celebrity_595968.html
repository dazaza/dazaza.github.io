In recent days, the plight of Wu Xiaofeng, a graduate of the Foreign Languages Department at Huaqiao University, has drawn attention from many netizens. Her classmate "Happy Little Second Sister" issued a Weibo plea, hoping that society would lend a helping hand to this unfortunate girl. Three years ago, she was abandoned by her biological parents on a truck and was adopted by a kind-hearted couple; 23 years later, after graduating from university and finding a good job just as she was about to repay her adoptive parents, she suddenly fell seriously ill.

Weibo Plea: Unfortunate Girl Suffers from Serious Illness

A few days ago, a Weibo post spread quickly online. Netizen "Happy Little Second Sister" issued a plea for help, stating that her classmate Wu Xiaofeng, a student in the Class D English major of the Foreign Languages College at Huaqiao University and originally from Nanping, was actually an abandoned infant who had been adopted. Unexpectedly, after her adoptive parents struggled to raise her through university, she unfortunately contracted systemic lupus erythematosus and lupus nephritis, facing potential organ damage. Neither of her adoptive parents have jobs, and she is currently being treated in Bed No. 2 of the Nephrology Department at the 174 Hospital in Xiamen. It is hoped that people with kindness can help her.

Tough Background: Abandoned 23 Years Ago

Xiaofeng is from Yuankeng Town, Shunchang County, Nanping. 23 years ago, shortly after her birth, she was abandoned by her biological parents on a local truck. Growing up, she learned from her neighbors that her biological parents were actually from Wuyishan. Because her mother had a younger brother in Shunchang, they abandoned her here.

Speaking about being abandoned, Xiaofeng is already quite calm. She said that her adoptive father was once an employee of a local cooperative store, and her adoptive mother had no job. The adoptive parents also had two biological sons, making five people in total relying on the father's meager salary. Despite difficulties, the adoptive parents managed to raise Xiaofeng until she went to university, where she worked hard, entering Shunchang No.1 High School during middle school and subsequently enrolling in the Foreign Languages Department at Huaqiao University. Unforeseen Calamity: Falling Ill Before Graduation

Xiaofeng's university classmate, Li Zhaoye, who posted the Weibo plea, said that Xiaofeng studied very diligently during her university years, receiving a 5,000 yuan scholarship every year to pay her tuition fees so as not to burden her family. During her university years, due to her impoverished background, Xiaofeng worked part-time during her free time to support herself through school.

Unexpectedly, before graduating in her senior year, Xiaofeng suddenly contracted a serious illness—systemic lupus erythematosus. Her first episode was relatively mild, and her condition was controlled after some treatment.

In July this year, upon graduation, Xiaofeng originally planned to work in Xiamen to earn more money to repay her adoptive parents. However, she unexpectedly received a call informing her that her adoptive mother had hyperthyroidism, along with diabetes and hypertension. To care for her elderly adoptive mother, she gave up the opportunity to work in Xiamen, returned to her hometown of Shunchang, and chose to teach English to high school sophomores at a local private high school.

Unfortunately, just one month into teaching, Xiaofeng fell ill again. This time, the illness came on fiercely, forcing her to resign and seek treatment in Fuzhou, eventually transferring to Xiamen for treatment in October.

Difficult Situation: High Medical Costs Cause Worry

Currently, Xiaofeng’s household relies solely on her two brothers' monthly salaries of over 2,000 yuan each, while neither of her adoptive parents have jobs. The pressure on her two brothers is immense as they need to take care of their parents, maintain the household, and also treat Xiaofeng.

It is reported that students from the Foreign Languages Department at Huaqiao University have spontaneously initiated activities to care for and assist Xiaofeng, with some alumni already visiting her at the 174 Hospital.

Xiaofeng's doctor mentioned that systemic lupus erythematosus and its induced lupus nephritis are autoimmune diseases that cannot be cured for life, only controlled by medication. If well-controlled, the patient can work without problems; if poorly controlled, the patient faces the threat of death, and the cost of treatment is extremely high.
