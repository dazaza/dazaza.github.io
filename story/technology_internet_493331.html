The birthday has arrived.

by tpvtaoirp on 2011-09-22 15:44:28

After many birthdays have passed, looking back, life is truly as fleeting as a dream, just like the song says.

Life can leave some space for hope in the future, but don't live in the yearning for those hopes. For me, for example, don't always mutter in your heart: my son will be extraordinary in the future, stocks will rise like a rainbow, my unit will look at me differently, my cross-decision will be written into textbooks, etc...

After getting married, my wife took over my mother's role and she takes good care of me. At least she never forgets each of my birthdays. For instance, last year on my birthday, she bought me a pretty good razor, since then, I haven't hurt my face while shaving anymore. Another example is that a few years ago, she bought me a hair clipper, ever since then, I no longer go to the barber shop to get my hair cut.

That's all I hope for.

On my birthday, I remembered an old lyric: life is as fleeting as a dream. The song goes like this: busy footsteps blindly hustle, for whom do we pay the price, for whom are we lonely. To whom should we confide our inner pain, life is as fleeting as a dream. Even the most beautiful dreams will wake up, after tonight comes dawn.

I've never liked reading so-called best-sellers, considering them as trivial. Recently, my wife bought a book called "A Calm Life Is Not Lonely," I was forced to flip through it, and found some contents quite interesting and flavorful. I thought: for years I've been anxious, perhaps it's because I'm not calm enough.

When I was in college, I didn't have any grand ambitions. Now past my youth, I appear even more aged. A few years ago, my wife started calling me "old man," when I first heard this title, I had a peculiar feeling that was hard to explain. After watching the TV series "Painted Skin" recently, I realized that this situation is just like Xia Bing calling her grandfather "old man," truly time flies and makes people age.

This happened twenty years ago.

Even though my current life is going well, and I should be very busy without being sentimental, I still feel that life is fleeting. It's unimaginable that everything has turned out to be this way, the person I used to be has disappeared completely. Even if I could go back to the past, I might not even recognize myself.

The old song I listened to, titled "After Tonight Comes Dawn," was sung by Singaporean singer Zhang Yun'er. Strangely, it seems she only sang during the time I was in college, not much before or after that. People who are more than 2 years older or younger than me aren't very familiar with her or her songs. Therefore, I can understand that it was as if she sang specifically for me.

If we take today seriously, with a broad mind to tolerate and accept all the unpleasant things in the future, then we won't feel sad or self-righteous. Regardless of how time rushes by and life changes, if our hearts are broad enough and our love for life vast enough, even if today is the last day of our lives, we can still smile at it, and our hearts will be full and content.

After so many birthdays, I feel: the most important part of life is today, now, the things at hand, and what's right in front of us.

In the past, I always lived in waiting, waiting for good luck to come, waiting for certain results, waiting for a turnaround, feeling extremely anxious inside, forgetting about today's life, and missing many wonderful moments that shouldn't have been missed.

My university classmates almost never go online via QQ. I, however, due to a significant change in personality over the past two years, enjoy observing and lurking online, occasionally writing something small to pass the time, so this space hasn't been closed.

When I was a child, on my birthday, my mother would always make me eat a few eggs, either in soup or boiled in plain water. In short, my mother used eggs to distinguish this day from other days.
