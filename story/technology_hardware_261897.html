Huang Chengqing: The Internet has no threshold in the process of starting a business.

by siyleo6g on 2009-11-19 23:48:44

Sina.com: The Internet Society is a third-party organization. Is the help from department leaders to the real economy declining, or is there an accelerating trend towards more practical implementation?

Why do we have internet revolutionary martyrs? Perhaps during their entrepreneurship, people did not understand them, and policies and environments were not mature enough, so they became martyrs. However, the blood of these martyrs will not be in vain, paving the way for us, the later entrepreneurs. This is what I want to say. In the future, I think the prospects will be bright. From the perspective of the country, the government, and the understanding of the Internet, it will be increasingly valued. With everyone's attention, I believe this industry will develop easily. In the future, it will be a very good opportunity for both entrepreneurs and industry development. Under such circumstances, everyone should seize this opportunity. I also hope that more young people in this industry take the time to start businesses and contribute to economic growth.

In terms of regulation, I think we need to gradually recognize issues in the process of development. Without everyone's hard work and entrepreneurship—whether successful or failed, whether experiences or lessons—we might not realize some problems, and the introduction of certain policies might also be overlooked, lacking such understanding. Through these experiences and lessons, successes and failures, our understanding deepens. Everyone's impression on how to improve and introduce policies deepens, and I believe this will be helpful for the future.

Huang Qinghua: I think what you said is very correct. In fact, the Internet provides a great platform for entrepreneurship. The biggest feature of the Internet is that during the entrepreneurial process, there are no barriers, and the threshold is very low. You can create a website with little money. Whatever ideas you have, they are easy to implement. For example, we have many websites that have been successful entrepreneurial cases in the past, such as 265 and hao123. Actually, those websites are very simple, just navigation sites, but now they have become very successful, later sold at high prices, and some have even gone public. Many of our successful Internet entrepreneurs have risen this way. Nowadays, people increasingly recognize that, whether in terms of institutional construction or capital support, it should greatly assist our Internet entrepreneurship. For instance, the recent opening of the Growth Enterprise Market (GEM) is actually significant support for emerging industries. Like Baidu and Google, many have reached their current status due to NASDAQ's support, which is crucial institutional construction.

Sina.com: In the past year, many issues have continuously emerged. Secretary Huang, what are your views and opinions on these issues, including copyright?

Huang Qinghua: This Internet Conference was mainly held under the context of the financial crisis and economic crisis. First, the theme is "Crisis, Turning Point, Opportunity". Under such economic or financial crises, what problems does this Internet Conference aim to solve? First, I think the Internet should become an amplifier and multiplier for economic growth, enabling the economy to transition out of the doldrums. Discussions revolve around this topic. Therefore, our subtitle is "The Power and Confidence of the Internet." This topic encourages deeper thinking about the role the Internet plays in the development of our national economy, regardless of whether it is in a low point or developing rapidly. What role should the Internet play, and how should it function? This triggers more thought. I think the most important aspect is that in our past development phase, we focused more on leisure and entertainment. In the future, we hope that through the financial crisis, people can realize that the Internet is not merely a platform for leisure and entertainment but should become a platform for e-commerce, an amplifier, multiplier, and converter for economic development. Therefore, discussions focus on these aspects.

On another front, this conference designs new business developments within the Internet context, including network copyright protection, forums on network integrity, and risk investment into the Internet, all related to the economy. Of course, the conference also covers various situations regarding the development of the entire Internet industry, such as industry contribution awards and Internet public welfare enterprise awards, which are developed and announced annually. I believe this serves as a guide for industry development. Other aspects are not much different from previous years.

Sina.com: The Internet has always been one of the hottest fields for entrepreneurship. Compared to other fields, entrepreneurs may differ in their ability to bear or understand funding and policies. Do the association or Secretary Huang have any views on promoting more policies to help entrepreneurs better start their businesses?

Huang Qinghua: I believe that copyright issues are inevitable in development. After several years of development, everyone's understanding of copyright becomes clearer. The awareness of copyright protection within the industry is getting stronger because everyone knows that if copyright cannot be effectively protected, the healthy development of the Internet itself will be impossible. During the process of copyright protection, it is important to note that there needs to be a balance between rights holders, disseminators, and users. To protect copyright well, the main criterion is whether original works are flourishing. If original works are thriving, then the protection is successful. If the protection only emphasizes the interests of one party, like only emphasizing the rights holder's interest without considering the disseminator, the work might not reach its audience and thus be useless. If the disseminator does not protect the rights holder's interest, the rights holder's labor goes unprotected, making them lose motivation, possibly leading to a lack of original works and content. Therefore, I think in terms of network copyright protection, everyone increasingly recognizes that it is about balance, establishing a win-win relationship. Everyone should protect copyright, and the key is how to protect it, deepening recognition and understanding in this process. Thus, the Internet Association has established a copyright mediation center, primarily mediating conflicts to deepen mutual understanding. I believe that in the development process, from a developmental trend perspective, copyright protection should become increasingly standardized.

Huang Qinghua: Judging from various reflections, according to the situation we have gathered from the Internet Association, everyone feels that the self-development of the entire Internet industry is still quite good, not significantly affected by the financial crisis. Can it help our real enterprises in any way? I think it indeed is a good opportunity for traditional industries to accelerate the "two modernizations" - industrialization and informatization integration. If we promote technological transformation, what should be transformed? Focus on informatizing the traditional, increasing automation levels, and enhancing labor productivity. Therefore, I believe it can play a significant role in this area.
