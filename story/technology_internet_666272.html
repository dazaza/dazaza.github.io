I want to learn animation, but I don't have an art background. What should I do??

by njsilucg879 on 2012-02-15 16:01:41

I want to learn animation, but I have no art foundation. What should I do?? Today is the opening day of the 35th architectural representation class at the Nanjing Center of Silk Road Education. A group of students from all over the country gathered here for the same ideal. Their faces are full of thirst for knowledge and infinite anticipation for the future. At the opening ceremony of the new class, a student asked such a question: "I have no art foundation. Can I learn well? I'm afraid that the pictures I make after learning will be very ugly." Perhaps many students have the same doubt, or perhaps more students are hesitant to step into the architectural animation industry because of this issue. So, is it necessary to have a good art foundation to learn architectural animation well?

Firstly, whether you have an art foundation or not is not particularly important. What matters is whether you have a persistent heart. Of course, having an art foundation is good, but if you have none, it doesn't matter. Although the architectural animation industry has the word "animation," it does not require you to be a painter. In fact, there are few people who truly understand art. Even though many people have studied art, they only have a simple understanding of it. As long as we practice more, it is not difficult to learn architectural animation well. The training targets of the Nanjing Center of Silk Road Education are mainly for those with no foundation, so you are all on the same starting line. There is nothing to worry about. It mainly depends on whether you work hard.

Secondly, what we call aesthetic sense, simply put, is whether the renderings you create look good. This is actually even less challenging. As long as you are familiar with the main characteristics of various architectures and master which images are popular, with practice comes proficiency. The more you see, the better your aesthetic sense becomes naturally. It's also about practice. Spend more time on your works. Find the pictures you think are beautiful to copy. Repeat this process, and over time, with practice comes proficiency, and your architectural animation skills will greatly improve!

Finally, we must have confidence in ourselves. Many people say they are not good at art before learning architectural animation, and this and that don't work well, which is actually a sign of lack of self-confidence! It's normal to lack confidence when first entering a new field, but confidence always comes from one's own efforts. When facing something entirely new, because you haven't made any effort or even tried it, you naturally won't have corresponding confidence. If you don't make an effort, you can't produce good renderings, and without good results, where does the confidence come from? Therefore, when first encountering architectural animation technology, you should encourage yourself to try it out. With some practice over time, you will definitely improve. This is the source of your confidence.

All courses at the Nanjing Center of Silk Road Education are designed for beginners with zero foundation, starting from scratch. Our teachers will effectively guide the students' learning status during the teaching process. Architectural animation is not as difficult as we imagine. As long as you are willing to work hard, you will surely find your own life value in this emerging field.
