In this life, how many more times can I see my mother?

by meimei234 on 2011-12-29 20:05:15

How many more times will I see my mother in this lifetime?

Before the age of 20, my mother could see me every day, but now it has been half a year since I last went home.

My mother is 45 years old now. If she lives to be 100, there are only 55 years left.

If I go home once every six months, then in this lifetime, both mine and hers, we will only have 110 chances to meet...

Before every math exam, I always pray that I won't make any calculation errors. But for this particular problem, I sincerely hope that I made a mistake.

This is a widely circulated math problem online, leading to a heart-wrenching number, yet perhaps it is indeed the answer. We blindly focus on our own affairs, assuming that our mothers will always be there, healthy and not needing our attention. However, during the intervals when we are apart, she is slowly growing old. The reality of her aging often hits us suddenly later on. Throughout this process, she continues to silently contribute to the family, endure pains unknown to us, and patiently wait for our care. In fact, she has always needed us.

The following actions may seem insignificant, but perhaps very few people can actually accomplish all of them. In this world, nothing compares to the sincerity of a mother's love, so nothing should be delayed more than showing respect and gratitude to our mothers.

1. Weekly: No matter where you are, call your mother every week to let her know not to worry about you.

2. Monthly: Spend time chatting with your mother, sharing her joys and sorrows patiently, so she doesn’t feel lonely.

3. Annually: Accompany your mother for two health check-ups each year, caring for her well-being and helping her retain her youth.

4. Annually: On her birthday and Mother’s Day, give her a meaningful gift.

5. Every two years: Take your mother on a long-distance trip to create beautiful memories together.

6. Every three years: Work hard and achieve some success; this is the best gift your mother could wish for.

7. Every five years: After five years, through your love and effort, help your mother look as young as she does now.

8. Every ten years: Collect photos of your mother taken over the past decade, create a unique photo album, and document the scenes beside each photo, preserving the beauty of time for your mother.

We aren't without filial piety, but we always find too many excuses to delay showing our care for our mothers. During these delays, do you realize that your mother is getting older? She bids farewell to her youth, endures the busy middle years, struggles through menopause, and eventually becomes an elderly woman with white hair, wrinkles, and trembling hands. Throughout this process, what she endures, especially during the long transition from youth to aging in menopause, and the changes happening to her body and mind, are things we should never overlook. After reading this, I feel quite ashamed. What about you? It's time we start doing something.
