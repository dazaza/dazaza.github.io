18 farmers were sentenced for hunting and selling national rare wild animals

by zzf0zxyh00 on 2011-06-27 16:11:21

Urumqi Online News (Reporter Ma Ting, Correspondent Han Baoku) - Recently, 18 farmers from LunTai County were sentenced to prison terms ranging from two to thirteen years for hunting and selling rare and endangered wildlife species. The local forestry protection department will receive a compensation of 625,000 yuan, which will ultimately be turned over to the national treasury.

This high-profile case involving an illegal hunting gang targeting protected animals was publicly tried in the Changji City People's Court. After the first-instance verdict, none of the 18 defendants appealed. In this case, the Forestry Bureau of Bayingolin Mongol Autonomous Prefecture acted as the plaintiff in the criminal civil suit and will receive a compensation of 625,000 yuan paid by the eight main defendants.

All 18 defendants are farmers from LunTai County. Since the spring of 2008, they have been involved in the illegal hunting, acquisition, transportation, and sale of precious and endangered wild animals such as snow leopards (a first-class nationally protected animal), argali sheep (a first-class nationally protected animal), brown bears (a second-class nationally protected animal), and their products. This has caused severe damage to the country's endangered wildlife population and had a negative social impact.

According to the prosecution's indictment: defendant Xiao acquired and sold four snow leopard carcasses, two snow leopard skeletons, and one bear gallbladder. While acquiring these himself, he also instructed others to acquire, transport, and sell them; Mu hunted one snow leopard and one brown bear; Wu hunted two snow leopards; Yu and Ke together hunted one snow leopard and three argali sheep; Aba and others hunted one snow leopard and two argali sheep; Ai captured one snow leopard and acquired and sold two snow leopard carcasses; Mai acquired and sold four snow leopard carcasses and one bear gallbladder; Ma acquired and sold two snow leopard carcasses.

On January 8, 2010, Wu, Aba, and Mu were arrested by the police during a transaction at the scene.

The prosecution argued that among the 18 defendants, 11 had particularly serious offenses and were charged with illegally acquiring, transporting, and selling precious and endangered wildlife and their products, as well as illegally hunting and killing precious and endangered wildlife. The remaining seven defendants' actions constituted illegal acquisition, transportation, and sale of precious and endangered wildlife, making them accomplices.

In October 2010, after the Changji City People's Court accepted the case and held a public trial, it issued a first-instance verdict: considering the relatively good attitude of the 18 defendants towards confession, this was taken into account during sentencing. Among them, 11 defendants with particularly serious offenses were sentenced to prison terms ranging from ten to thirteen years; among the other seven accomplices, one was exempted from criminal responsibility, while the remaining six were sentenced to prison terms ranging from two to eight years.

In the handling of this case, the 18 criminal defendants received criminal penalties along with fines ranging from five thousand to eighty thousand yuan. Additionally, nine defendants must pay compensation for the civil part of the criminal case, amounting to 625,000 yuan. These compensations will be handed over to the Forestry Bureau of the Xinjiang Bayingolin Mongol Autonomous Prefecture and ultimately turned over to the national treasury.
