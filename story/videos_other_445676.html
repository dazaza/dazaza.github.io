Tomb of Gods and Demons Part 2 Chapter 10: The Cause of Yu Xin's Death

This was an exceptionally vast space, the glow of the surrounding barrier reflecting off this area making it appear somewhat crystalline. However, for some unknown reason, despite the bright white light, it gave off a chilling sensation.

Row after row of bookshelves were neatly arranged with ancient books. Chen Nan was quite excited; he summoned Leon to help him search for useful books. Any books of extraordinary age were collected together by him, almost forming a small mountain, causing Leon to silently gawk in disbelief, not knowing what he was up to.

The formation of the common script used on the continent roughly occurred a thousand years ago. Before that, the East and West had their own distinct scripts. To Chen Nan, the writing in these books appeared like ghostly scribbles, with no word recognizable.

However, he had been prepared. The spatial ring Kelly gave him wasn't just for storing food; its primary purpose was for today's endeavor. He intended to store all the books he found valuable inside, intending to find someone later to slowly translate them.

Chen Nan quickly flipped through the books in the first layer of hell, tossing one after another into the spatial ring. Leon, standing nearby, watched in astonishment—this was outright plunder!

Stealing a few or even dozens of books would be acceptable, but if Chen Nan continued at this rate, this entire library would be emptied, surely causing the Holy Church to go berserk. The people in the cathedral would likely lose their minds, imagining the chaos that would ensue.

"Brother Chen, what type of books are you looking for? Let me help you. If you raid the Holy Church's main base like this, the people in the cathedral will go insane. They'll dig us out from under the ground if they have to."

"I want all the books related to ancient gods and demons."

"In that case, take out the books from your ring. Most of these are religious scriptures. I've studied Western ancient characters a bit, so I can help you filter them."

Leon softly chanted a spell. The pile of books in front of Chen Nan, resembling a small mountain, floated up, all beginning to flip pages automatically with a continuous "rustling" sound.

After a thorough selection, only ten books remained from the original thousand. Then Leon walked towards a distant bookshelf, much to Chen Nan's surprise. Following Leon closely, the two began a frenzied sweep of the library.

Not long after, the little dragon suddenly tugged at Chen Nan's collar, extending a golden little claw, pointing to the central row of bookshelves and saying, "Look, those books are glowing!"

A golden bookshelf emitted waves of treasure-like light, the books enveloped in a misty aura, radiating a sacred brilliance. Chen Nan and Leon rushed over, realizing that these books were clearly unusual.

Leon said, "This is because these books have additional seals on them. It seems they are the most important books of the Holy Church."

Chen Nan told the little dragon, "Dragon Baby, fetch all the books inside for me."

The little dragon wobbled as it flew onto the bookshelf. This time, breaking the seal proved difficult, managing to retrieve only about a dozen books after great effort. It complained, "It's like struggling in the mud of a swamp, too exhausting. I'll need to soak in a wine vat tonight to relieve the fatigue."

The strength of the seal here was undoubtedly greater than the power outside the barrier. Chen Nan smiled and said, "Alright, let you soak in the wine vat tonight. Now, quickly get all the books out."

The little dragon happily blinked its big eyes and began working diligently.

Under Leon's magic, the ancient books floated up one by one. The rustling of turning pages never ceased, when suddenly a sheet of paper fell out of a book.

Chen Nan caught it in his hand and carefully examined it. Suddenly, his eyes widened in shock as he stared at the single page in his hand. He almost couldn't believe his eyes. He saw a font he once knew very well ——

Beautiful Eastern ancient small characters filled the entire sheet of paper, with many words written intermittently. However, they seemed disorganized, unable to form coherent sentences, as if randomly jotted down on rough paper.

"Gratitude, Du family, traitor, forced marriage, angry killing of the War God, severe injury, seeking survival or reincarnation, West, searching for the Goddess of Life, traveling, Great Dragon Sword, shocking secret..."

Chen Nan nearly screamed in shock! This was Yu Xin's handwriting, this was a note left by Yu Xin! She had been here before!

Although the words on the paper were elegant, they were arranged haphazardly, even overlapping within each other, making it incomprehensible to outsiders. However, Chen Nan, who understood Yu Xin deeply, gradually comprehended the gist.

From the chaotic words on the paper, it was clear that Yu Xin was extremely angry at the time. After arranging the words, the general meaning was: The Du family showed ingratitude and betrayed the Chen family. They actually wanted to force Yu Xin into marriage. After a life-and-death battle, Yu Xin slaughtered the War God of the Du family, but she herself suffered severe injuries, hesitating whether to reincarnate. Later, she decided to travel far to the West, hoping to seek the Goddess of Life to prolong her life.

As for the mentions of "traveling", "Great Dragon Sword", and "shocking secret", Chen Nan didn't fully understand. Perhaps Yu Xin discovered clues about the Great Dragon Sword and uncovered certain shocking secrets.

Clearly, this piece of paper was a record unintentionally left behind by Yu Xin, documenting her sorrow and anger.

Chen Nan was overwhelmed with rage, almost going mad. He really wanted to travel back five thousand years and kick the so-called War God of the Du family to death!

Five thousand years ago, Yu Xin, a lone woman, fought against those powerful enemies, who turned out to be the despicable Du family, the wicked slaves who had stolen the Chen family's benefits. He now understood why Yu Xin mysteriously disappeared in the West and later became the ferocious Spirit Corpse King.

Her body was severely damaged, and she must not have found a way to continue her life, eventually perishing. Her death was caused by the Du family, all orchestrated by the Du family!

Chen Nan shouted wildly in his heart, "I must become stronger, the strongest! I will massacre across thousands of miles and annihilate everyone in the Du family!"

Chen Nan's anger was so intense it could burn through the nine layers of heaven. The mystical powers within him surged wildly along with his emotions. A shadow appeared behind him, its eyes seemingly about to open. The Death Magic Knife separated from the shadow's right hand, rotating continuously around Chen Nan. Additionally, several more shadows emerged, appearing to be weapons, floating up and down around Chen Nan, though they were too faint to see clearly.

All the bookshelves in the first layer of hell floated up. Leon watched in astonishment, shocked and said, "Brother Chen, what's wrong with you? You need to control yourself. Don't let it out, or all the ancient books here will be shattered by your outwardly released energy."

Chen Nan's eyes tightly closed, his hands clenched into fists, veins bulging, his appearance somewhat fierce and terrifying. After a long while, he slowly unclenched his fists, all the bookshelves and ancient books slowly descended to the ground. The shadow and the weapons behind him also gradually disappeared.

Leon, seeing him finally recover, cautiously asked, "Brother Chen, what happened?"

"Nothing." Chen Nan carefully tucked the piece of paper into his bosom.

"Oh oh oh... ah, tired, tired, tired..." The little dragon, immersed in the sealing power of the bookshelf, unaware of the situation outside, twisted its chubby dragon body, laboriously pushing the bookshelf outward.

Chen Nan opened his spatial ring, no longer using Kelly's help to select, emptying all the books on the ground. After all, these books had all been touched by Yu Xin, and he ended up stuffing the entire shelf into the ring.

"Finished." The little dragon wobbled as it flew out, landing on his shoulder.

"This... Brother Chen, aren't you being a bit too ruthless? Shouldn't you leave at least half of them?"

"Let's go, to save your necromancer's wise man." Chen Nan had already adjusted his emotions and headed towards the entrance leading to the second layer of hell, with Leon following behind.

This entrance spiraled downward, similarly blocked by a barrier, emitting a sacred glow, yet subtly exuding an evil aura.

Arriving here, the two finally understood why this library, despite its bright whiteness, still felt somewhat gloomy. It was because the evil aura emanated from the lower layers of hell, which the holy barrier's power struggled to suppress. One could imagine how terrifying the evil beings sealed in the dozens of layers of hell below were.

"Ah, such thick seals, such powerful forces. Are you planning to continue?" The baby dragon wore a dissatisfied expression but still entered first, finding it effortless and disappearing completely after a while.

When the little dragon returned, it was surprisingly fast, "whoosh" darting out from the barrier.

"Goodness gracious, it's too scary inside. What kind of demon monsters are there? It's almost like the dark underworld."

The little thing used its golden little claws to touch its chest, appearing startled.

When the little dragon brought Chen Nan and Leon into the second layer of hell, the two indeed felt the scenario the little dragon described. Here, the light was dim, sinister and terrifying, with ghostly cries and beast roars filling the air, evil qi soaring skyward.

The second layer of hell was relatively small, a long corridor lined with dozens of deep, bottomless stone caves on both sides, each cave mouth sealed by holy attributes.

The people, beasts, and monsters trapped in the ancient caves sensed the arrival of outsiders and all crazily shouted:

"Release me!"

"I want to see the sun again!"

"I've been locked up for over a hundred years!"

"I've repented!"

The cries and roars intertwined, making one's scalp tingle, truly indistinguishable from the underworld.
