Tencent product manager responds to the issue of all lit-up mobile QQ avatars: the revised version will differentiate statuses.

by geekzhang on 2013-05-16 17:16:58

May 15th news: The recent release of the new mobile version of QQ has sparked numerous complaints from users, and industry insiders as well as media have also paid close attention. The Tencent QQ Mobile Version team has already announced on their official microblog that a revised version will be released within two weeks. Despite insider information suggesting that the revision will differentiate between online and offline statuses, there are still many voices in the industry regarding the design where all friends' avatars in the contact list are lit up. On May 15th, a product manager from Tencent QQ responded on Zhihu as follows:Firstly, it needs to be explained that in the 2013 version of QQ, the avatar does not represent the online status; this major change has yet to be fully understood by everyone.The content expressed by the avatar has always represented a friend, and whether it is lit up or not has additionally indicated the online/offline status. This habit has continued for over a decade and is one of the most important recognitions in QQ. The direct impact of this recognition is that being online means available for communication, while being offline means even if a message is sent, it cannot be replied instantly—a typical feature of the PC era due to computers not being portable and unable to connect to the internet at all times.In recent years, significant changes have occurred. Every day, 65% of QQ messages are sent via mobile phones. Most of the time, mobile phones are carried around, along with push notifications, making messages instantaneously reachable. QQ users are also very active; although they are not online 24 hours a day, they use it frequently. With the push notifications of mobile QQ, even if the other party is not in front of the PC, messages are generally seen quickly. For the message sender, the inner expectation is to convey the information as soon as possible and receive feedback promptly. However, due to the cognition that an unlit avatar indicates an offline status, it hinders the motivation to leave a message. Therefore, the original intention of the entire adjustment is to make QQ possess the recognition of being constantly available for communication, similar to SMS, which has a very high reliability and doesn't consider whether the other party's phone is turned on before sending a message.In QQ, there are quite a number of interactions that go beyond just chatting, including space dynamics. What the avatar expresses should be a more vivid contact, with rich data and dynamics behind it, not just a chat status. Changes in the contact view have been prepared for a long time, analyzing user behavior data, including the number of communication objects, message volume, usage time, etc., and conducting many interviews. Good friends with whom daily communication frequency is high are very concentrated, and when communicating, they do not care whether the other party is online, leaving messages without hesitation. For those with less frequent communication, even if the other party is online, the typical opening line is: "Hi, are you there?"There are quite a number of gray avatars on QQ. Although invisibility is a reasonable demand, this information to some extent hinders communication. Words originally intended to be said are swallowed back because of the gray avatar, or other tools are used instead. Our ideal is to provide a relaxed communication service without much thought, ensuring reliable and deliverable messages. Thus, we have made many preparations for offline messages and cross-terminal message roaming, but the motivation to send messages has not been thoroughly resolved.Therefore, we have made a very bold attempt, redefining the information expressed by the avatar, no longer representing whether someone is online, but merely expressing personal image, because friends are all lively. In a lot of behavioral observations and interviews, communication with close friends is very direct through messages or comments, while for idle scenarios or infrequent communication with friends, communication partners are selected from online friends. Hence, we also provide a new feature for online friends, directly displaying the full online status, addressing this communication scenario.Due to the large number of users providing feedback, we have started researching optimization plans for the online display status, trying to smoothly transition user habits. Mobile and multi-terminal are the firm strategic directions for QQ in the future. We will work together with users to complete QQ's self-renewal in the mobile internet era.The above response reveals the original intentions behind Tencent's decision to adopt the design where all avatars are lit up. However, users' habits of over a decade make the transformation of the QQ mobile version even more difficult. A one-sentence comment: The direction is good, but it must be done step by step.
