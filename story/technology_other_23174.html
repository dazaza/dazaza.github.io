The yellow mealworm was originally a kind of warehouse pest. It is commonly known as breadworm and its nutritional value ranks first among various kinds of living animal protein. Hence it is called "a treasure trove of protein feed", and is an edible insect with high protein, calcium and nutrition. Zoos at home and abroad use it as meat feed to breed rare birds and fish. In recent years, it has gradually been developed into pet feed and applied in high-grade nutritious food for human beings.

Yellow mealworm breeding is called the third largest insect industry after "silkworm" and "honeybee" by entomologists because of its characteristics such as small investment, strong adaptability, high economic benefits, wide range of feed sources, simple breeding facilities, easy-to-learn technology, and suitability for both factory and home breeding. According to research by relevant experts, insect active protein not only contains a large amount of chitin, antimicrobial peptides, defensins, and exogenous lectins that have special effects on the human body, but also is rich in nine essential amino acids for the human body, as well as proteins, free amino acids, vitamins, mineral elements, unsaturated fatty acids and other nutrients, and its proportion is consistent with the normal proportion of the human body, making it easy to absorb and utilize. The large-scale production and industrialization of yellow mealworms are key sponsored projects of the national agricultural and fishery harvest plan; they have been listed as key development projects of the national 4050 project and the national star fire plan project. Breeding yellow mealworms has a promising market prospect and is a good way to get rich in both urban and rural areas. The yellow mealworm currently supplied by our group is a new variety of yellow mealworm cultivated by experts from the Senior Expert Center of the Chinese Institute of Agriculture Research and the Chinese Society of Animal Husbandry, Fisheries, and Economy after many years of effort. This variety has characteristics such as high reproduction rate, short production period, and large individuals.

So how to ensure high and stable production of yellow mealworms?? The specific methods are as follows:

1. Temperature and humidity are conditions for high and stable production

The basic condition for breeding yellow mealworms is temperature and humidity. They grow normally when the temperature is between 10-38℃ and the humidity is between 60-70%.

In winter, breeders can generally raise the temperature to above 20℃ during the day, but it usually drops by 4-5℃ at night. Yellow mealworms grow slowly below 10℃. If the temperature cannot be kept above 15℃ at night, no matter how much feed they eat during the day, they will not gain weight, and no matter how much coal is burned, it is wasted. The ratio of invested feed and coal to output is zero. Therefore, keeping the indoor temperature above 15℃ both during the day and at night in winter is the most critical.

Inappropriate humidity can easily cause the death of yellow mealworms, especially during the pupation stage. Excessive humidity can cause the pupae to rot, while too little humidity can prevent them from hatching successfully. Methods to solve low humidity: (1) Sprinkle water in the breeding room; (2) Adjust the frequency of water supplementation; (3) Increase the water content of the main feed. Methods to solve high humidity: (1) Open the windows for ventilation when the temperature is high; (2) Install exhaust fans when the temperature is low; (3) Place desiccants like quicklime and charcoal in the breeding room, or use dehumidifiers if conditions permit.

2. Feed is a prerequisite for high and stable production

Poor feed not only affects the growth of yellow mealworms but can also cause large-scale deaths. Leaves sprayed with pesticides should not be fed, nor should wheat bran mixed with talcum powder. Main feed and supplementary feed should be reasonably combined. When feeding wheat bran, green feed should also be provided, then based on the humidity in the breeding room, additional green feed should be given. When the humidity is too high, green feed should be provided every 4-5 days; when the humidity is too low, it should be given every other day. When feeding green feed, it should be evenly distributed so all the worms can eat it. If there is no main feed left in the feed tray and only worm droppings remain, do not feed green feed.

3. The quantity and nutrition of adult insects are the keys to high and stable production

Adult insects need to supplement their nutrition during egg-laying. Insufficient nutrition will shorten their lifespan and reduce production. Adult insects need sufficient feed during egg-laying. Green feed should be updated daily, and complex vitamins should be fed once a week. This not only increases the hatching rate but also produces fat and strong worms. The lifespan of adult insects is 60-90 days. The number of adult insects should not decrease and should be replenished in time, i.e., select active and large individuals from the commercial worms to supplement the adults. Generally, at least 15-20 kilograms of adult insects should be maintained for every 50 kilograms of seed purchased, which will stabilize production.

4. Hatching rate is the decisive factor for high and stable production

Methods to increase the hatching rate: (1) Set up a dedicated incubation room where the temperature can be controlled and maintained at 25℃, which can achieve a hatching rate of over 90%; (2) Maintain the required humidity for incubation; (3) Timely remove adult insects from the egg box to prevent them from eating the eggs.
