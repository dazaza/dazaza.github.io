Earth Tragedy Again - Blu-ray Review of "2012" Mainland Edition

by diglog on 2010-10-06 12:35:09

This is yet another disaster movie, once again reminding us that our lives could turn tragic at any moment. However, it seems that disaster movies are always popular, or at least in mainland China, they always achieve box office success, not much worse than the catastrophic events depicted in the films themselves. Perhaps life is too boring, and we urgently need some kind of stimulation to wake up our brains, or deep down inside, we enjoy watching everything be destroyed, once again. "2012" doesn't have a lot of new ideas, but it satisfies my most wicked psychological desire: the earth-shattering disasters and the collapse of the world. This is something that almost no other disaster movie has been able to satisfy me with. Add to that the film's high-quality special effects and bone-rattling sound mix, even though I suffered from headaches after watching it, I didn't hesitate to watch it three times in the cinema. Some people mentioned themes like family and home in their reviews, which might give this movie a hint of serious meaning, but I prefer not to search for such meanings and simply enjoy the audio-visual impact. After all, when it comes to family, many films can surpass this one. "The Day After Tomorrow" has a more realistic and profound environmental theme, which this movie doesn't match. In short, this is a disaster blockbuster worth watching at least once, and preferably with good audio-visual effects, otherwise, you might as well watch some boring TV dramas. As for the movie itself, I don't want to say much, I just know that when I walked out of the theater, I felt "it's great to be alive." Mainland China released this movie synchronously with the rest of the world, and quickly issued Blu-ray standard edition, Blu-ray iron box edition, and several DVD versions, another market-oriented release strategy to meet the needs of different consumers. At a price of 140 yuan for the standard Blu-ray disc, this work is still worth buying. Of course, this film was released by Sony in the mainland China version, reducing some content that might make viewers uncomfortable, and changing some subtitle annotations. For this, I am not opposed, as long as it's not the result of reckless editing. Many scenes in this film were shot using digital HD cameras, so there was no problem with the Blu-ray disc's video transfer. Overall, the picture quality is smooth, without any flaws of film stock, and the graininess is kept to a minimum, only appearing in some darker scenes. There is no excessive artificial processing, the details of the image are acceptable, the colors are rich but not overdone, and the skin tones of the characters are natural. The overall picture of the movie is slightly dark, and some low-light scenes seem a bit strained, but the grayscale performance is basically up to standard, with no indistinguishable images. However, personally, I think that as a new movie, the picture quality could have been better. Sound effects are undoubtedly an important part of this film. The two DTS-HD MA soundtracks perform almost identically, with no significant difference upon switching between them. The cinema dubbing is convenient for family viewing - the images use international version material, and the disc does not have separate annotation subtitles, meaning some dialogues are incomprehensible, like chanting scriptures. These dialogues are directly dubbed into Chinese, which may not be as convenient for audiences listening in English. During the big disaster scenes, the feeling is like your underwear might get blown off, bringing the cinema's stunning experience back into your own room. The bass is exceptionally active when the ground rises, and the surround sound never stops, with alarm sounds, shattering glass, screams, and explosions heard non-stop, making you feel like you're in a fleeing car while watching. In static scenes, such as the dialogue between the president and the doctor in the church, the sound is steady and clear, and in action scenes, the dialogue is also clearly audible without any ambiguity. The bonus features on the disc are mainly divided into two parts: behind-the-scenes clips and deleted scenes. The former includes: The Mystery of the Mayan Calendar, Designing the End of the World, Roland Emmerich: Modern 'Disaster Master', Actors' Perspectives on 'End of the World', Science Behind the Destruction. The latter includes: Alternate Ending, Twin Brothers in the Luxury Car Ride, Conversation Between President Wilson and Sally, Mistakes Occur, Jackson Underwater, Government Parliament Apology. All are recorded in 1080i HD with Chinese subtitles. They are very helpful for understanding the behind-the-scenes of this disaster epic. This Blu-ray disc issued in mainland China should be separately produced in Hong Kong. Other regional versions will likely include more trailers, multiple subtitles, and soundtracks, and perhaps a longer version. Therefore, whether to buy this one is a personal choice. For me, this disc is sufficient for now, unless there is a qualitative change.
