Analysts: Firefox OS prospects are not optimistic

by anonymous on 2013-08-20 14:47:05

According to a report by the American technology news website CNET, browser developer Mozilla has launched the Firefox phone in the US and UK markets. Despite the company's claim that this phone has attracted much consumer interest, industry analysts hold differing opinions and are not optimistic about its prospects. Below is the main content of the article:

Can a browser also become a mobile operating system? The developer of the Firefox browser, Mozilla, is striving for this. The company claims that its compact, plastic-bodied Firefox phone has drawn considerable consumer interest. However, analysts have differing views and are not optimistic about the phone's future.

Firefox Phone Launch

Although the Firefox phone was just released, it has already entered the sales channels of several countries. Initially, the Firefox phone was only available in the Spanish market, then expanded to Venezuela and Colombia, and now it will be listed on eBay in the US and UK.

Gartner analyst Carolina Milanesi pointed out that Mozilla's launch of the Firefox phone could serve two purposes: "First, it allows Mozilla to successfully enter the mobile field; secondly, it helps mobile operators reduce their reliance on low-cost, highly homogeneous Android phones."

The Open phone powered by the Firefox OS is about to land in the US and UK markets. This phone is jointly developed by Mozilla and ZTE and is priced at $79.99 in the US and £59.99 in the UK. Currently, Mozilla has reached sales agreements with mobile operators in Spain, Venezuela, and Colombia, but so far only Spain's Telefonica has begun formal sales.

Mozilla's chief developer, Christian Heilmann, revealed that the Firefox phone has been well-received by consumers. He said, "User feedback has been very positive, with most people indicating they like using the Firefox phone. This phone is either their first mobile device or an upgrade from their feature phone."

Mozilla Senior Vice President of Mobile Devices and President of Asia, Li Gong, said, "In Spain, a young man from China even hoped to buy 100 phones to bring back to Hong Kong."

Advantages of the Firefox Phone

According to Mozilla, besides being cost-effective, the Firefox OS used in the Firefox phone is developed based on HTML5 and CSS3 technologies, making app development as simple as developing websites.

Heilmann said, "Writing Firefox applications will never be a waste of time. You can easily port HTML5 applications to the iOS platform, but doing it the other way around is impossible."

In addition, the highlights of this phone include direct payment through wireless carriers without needing credit cards, as well as retaining features popular in emerging markets, such as built-in FM radio.

However, these advantages alone may not be enough for the Firefox phone to succeed.

Analysts' Views

Ovum's Chief Telecommunications Analyst Jan Dawson pointed out that Mozilla's inability to persuade US or UK mobile operators to sell this phone reveals the inherent limitations of the company's strategy. He said, "I don't see anything particularly eye-catching in the Firefox system compared to other mobile operating systems."

Milanesi noted that over-reliance on mobile operators might not be beneficial for Mozilla. She said, "Many mobile brands rely on mobile operators for brand building and marketing, but end up having to follow the operators' demands. I hope Mozilla can create demand through high user loyalty."

Mozilla plans to announce its specific mobile strategy by the end of the year and aims to launch the Firefox phone in Brazil's market in the fourth quarter, which will be sold through Telefonica Brasil, the Spanish telecom company's subsidiary in Brazil. Milanesi pointed out that import tariffs in Brazil are very high, significantly increasing the entry barrier for this market. She said, "Alcatel is also in the Brazilian market, which could be an advantage for the Firefox phone." Alcatel currently produces the One Touch phone running the Firefox system.

"I believe we can definitely make breakthroughs in the Brazilian market. We've conducted multiple surveys targeting local users to uncover their needs, such as including built-in FM radio. The Brazilian market is very exciting," she added.

Hunting Fox Traps

Will the Firefox phone become a trap for hunting foxes?

This is hard to predict. Dawson said, "It's still difficult to predict how unsaturated smartphone markets will respond to the release of low-cost smartphones. But judging from past situations, this possibility is quite high."

He further pointed out that Mozilla's biggest challenge is how to monetize applications on the Firefox operating system. Dawson stated, "Even though Android dominates the smartphone market, people are still willing to spend more money on iOS apps. The key issue now is how Mozilla convinces developers to develop apps for the Firefox operating system and persuades users to purchase them."

Milanesi believes, "Mozilla's idea of launching the Firefox operating system is correct. If the system had been introduced when Android held 40% of the smartphone market share, its chances of success would have been significant. Now, however, things are more complicated because Chinese smartphone manufacturers have driven down the lowest price of Android phones to just $80-90, diminishing Firefox's price advantage."

According to Mozilla, starting from 2014, the company will collaborate with Sony to develop high-end phones in Japan while promoting the Firefox phone in emerging markets such as China, India, and Brazil.

However, even in emerging markets, the Firefox phone faces many challenges. Li Gong said, "We position it as a low-end phone, but in some markets, its price isn't cheap. For example, in the Chinese market, if we want to succeed there, we must implement different strategies and offer products with different hardware specifications."

For Mozilla, the current priority is addressing the lack of awareness of the Firefox operating system. Unlike Windows Phone, the Firefox OS does not receive sufficient financial support. Additionally, it is unclear whether web developers will voluntarily develop HTML5 applications as Mozilla claims.

In the past, Mozilla reaped substantial rewards with new desktop and Android versions of the Firefox browser, but now the Firefox phone might very well become a trap for Mozilla's hunt for market share.
