Introduction to the Digital Control Technology of Ultrasonic Flow Meter

The application of digital control technology in ultrasonic flow meter ultrasonic generator usually has three forms:

1. Ultrasonic flow meter using high-end AVR microcontroller control

A microcontroller is a microcontrol chip that integrates CPU, RAM/ROM, timer/counters, and I/O interfaces on a single chip. It has the advantages of fast speed, strong function, high efficiency, small size, reliable performance, and strong anti-interference ability, and is widely used in various control systems. The CPU of the microcontroller has gone through the development process from 4, 8, 16, 32 to even 64 bits. In the ultrasonic generator, the microcontroller is mainly used for data acquisition and operation processing, voltage and current regulation, PWM signal generation, system status monitoring, and fault self-diagnosis, generally running as the main control chip of the entire circuit to complete multiple comprehensive functions. Cooperating with D/A converter and MOSFET power module to realize pulse width modulation. In addition, the microcontroller also has interruption protection and monitoring functions for overcurrent, overheating, undervoltage, etc.

Microcontroller control overcomes the inherent defects of analog circuits. Through digital control methods, it achieves high-precision and high-stability control characteristics and can realize flexible and diverse control functions.

2. Ultrasonic flow meter using DSP control

Digital Signal Processor (DSP) is a new generation of programmable processors that have rapidly emerged in recent years. Its internal integrates baud rate ultrasonic generator and FiFO buffer, provides high-speed synchronous serial port and standard asynchronous serial port, some chips also integrate sample/hold and A/D conversion circuit inside, and provide PWM signal output. Compared with microcontrollers, DSPs have faster CPUs, higher integration, and larger storage capacity.

DSP belongs to Reduced Instruction Set Computer (RISC), most instructions can be completed within one cycle and can finish multiple instructions within one instruction cycle through parallel processing technology. Meanwhile, DSP adopts improved Harvard architecture, which has independent program and data spaces, allowing simultaneous storage of programs and data. Built-in high-speed hardware multiplier increases multi-level pipeline, making it have high-speed data computing ability. On the other hand, microcontrollers belong to Complex Instruction Set Computer (CISC), most instructions require 2-3 instruction cycles to complete. Microcontrollers adopt von Neumann structure, where programs and data are stored in the same space, and can only access instructions or data separately at the same time. The ALU of the microcontroller can only do addition, and multiplication needs to be realized by software, thus requiring more instruction cycles and being relatively slower. Compared with 16-bit microcontrollers, DSP executes single instructions 8-10 times faster, and one multiplication operation is 16-30 times faster. In ultrasonic generators, DSP can complete all functions except power transformation, such as main circuit control, system real-time monitoring, and protection. Although DSP has many advantages, it also has some limitations, such as the selection of sampling frequency, PWM signal frequency and its accuracy, sampling delay, calculation time and accuracy, etc. These factors will more or less affect the control performance of the ultrasonic flow meter circuit.

3. Ultrasonic flow meter using FPGA control

Field Programmable Gate Array (FPGA) belongs to reconfigurable devices, whose internal logic functions can be arbitrarily set according to needs, with the advantages of high integration, fast processing speed, and high efficiency. Its structure is mainly divided into three parts: programmable logic blocks, programmable I/O modules, programmable internal connections. Due to the very large integration degree of FPGAs, one FPGA contains thousands of equivalent gates at least and millions or even tens of millions of equivalent gates at most. Therefore, one FPGA can realize very complex logic, replacing circuits composed of multiple integrated circuits and discrete elements. It designs the system with the help of hardware description language (VHDL), adopts three-layer (behavioral description, RTL description, gate-level description) hardware description and top-down design style (starting from system function description), can perform mixed simulation of three-layer descriptions, thereby facilitating digital circuit design, and has considerable advantages in reliability, size, and cost. Comparatively speaking, DSP is suitable for use in occasions with low sampling rate and high software complexity; while when the system sampling rate is high (MHz level), the data rate is high (above 20MB/s), conditional operations are few, and tasks are relatively fixed, FPGA has more advantages.
