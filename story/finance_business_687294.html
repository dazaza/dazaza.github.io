Ten grape varieties worth trying in your lifetime

Red grape varieties:

Agiorgitiko (Greek: Αγιοργιτικό) is one of the most noble and ancient Greek red grape varieties. It is widely planted in the Nemea appellation, located in the central part of the Peloponnese peninsula. Wines made from this variety are fresh and aromatic, ready to drink, but also have great aging potential.

Mavrodafni (Greek: Μαυροδάφνη) is widely grown in the Aegean region and can be blended with other indigenous Greek varieties to produce a range of different flavors.

Syrah is originally from France and prefers a mild climate. It performs exceptionally well on volcanic slopes. The wine has a deep near-black color, with intense and complex aromas. When young, it exhibits blackberry and violet scents; with age, it develops notes of pepper, tar, and leather. The mouthfeel is dense and rich, with surprising tannin levels that make it highly resistant to oxidation, making it ideal for long-term oak barrel aging.

Merlot is an early-ripening variety, tender and productive. It has low tannins and abundant fruit flavors, used to produce large quantities of delicious and smooth wines.

Pinot grapes originate from Burgundy, France. Pinot varieties include Pinot Noir, Pinot Gris, and Pinot Blanc. They have low tannins and a light body, but due to the difficulty of cultivation, their yield is low.

Cabernet Sauvignon originates from Bordeaux, France. This variety is adaptable and produces high-quality wine, being one of the world's most popular winemaking grapes.

Malbec is originally from France and is one of the six authorized red grape varieties in the Bordeaux region, used to add color and structure to the wine.

Nebbiolo derives its name from "nebbia," meaning "fog" in Italian. It is one of the world's most unique and distinctive grape varieties, being the most important wine grape in Italy's foothill regions.

Tempranillo typically exhibits aromas of berries, plums, tobacco, leather, and vanilla. When young—referred to as "jovenes"—it offers vibrant fruitiness. After several years of oak barrel aging, it transforms into a full-bodied red wine.

Xinomavro is a native red grape from the Macedonia region of northern Greece, known for its exceptional aging potential and robust tannins. Its layered and structurally complex aroma includes red currant fruit notes, along with unique olive, spice, and dried tomato flavors.

White grape varieties:

Assyrtiko originates from the famous island of Santorini and is cultivated in the Mediterranean region. This grape is planted in volcanic soil, producing wines with viscosity, high acidity, and mineral-rich flavors. In recent years, Greek producers have enhanced its fresh, soft taste and enriched its aroma.

Malagouzia is a white Greek grape variety originating from the northern edge of the Corinthian Gulf near Nafpaktos. It has been described as the "forgotten queen of Greek winemaking grapes." Indeed, Malagouzia was almost extinct until Domaine Carras revived and researched this ancient variety in the late 20th century. This grape produces soft, fragrant, and elegant wines.

Malvasia is originally from Greece. It has a wonderful aroma of honey and pears, with high sugar content and low tannins, often used as a base for sweet white wines. Dried Malvasia grapes are fermented and aged in oak barrels to produce sweet white wines that are rich and fragrant, with high alcohol content, residual sugar, and a unique flavor.

Muscat is a general term for white grape varieties with a musky aroma, such as Muscat d'Alexandrie and Muscat à Petits Grains. These varieties share certain common characteristics. Muscat grapes often grow in hot climates and are suitable for producing aromatic natural sweet wines. Muscat à Petits Grains is one of the oldest Muscat varieties, producing wines with citrus or spice scents.

Moschofilero is a slightly pink variety found in the Peloponnese peninsula of Greece. The wine produced from it is fresh, with a strong and refreshing rose-like aroma. Its best feature is its elegant fragrance, which uplifts the spirit and carries a subtle floral scent (rose petals).

Chardonnay, also known as Chardonnay or Chardonnay, produces wines that usually have aromas of pineapple, green apple, or pear, with a round and neutral mouthfeel. Except in special cases, they are not very acidic and have a slight sweetness.

Sémillon has low acidity, making it very suitable for sweet wines. The wine produced from it is thick and rich, with a deep golden color, showing obvious citrus flavors, sometimes accompanied by a hint of honey, figs, and cigars. Due to its thin skin, it easily develops botrytis cinerea, which can be used to produce very sweet botrytized wines.

Riesling is a famous German white grape variety. Because of its cold resistance, it is the main winemaking variety in Germany's cold regions and Alsace, France, and is also common in other wine-producing areas around the world.

Gewürztraminer, also known as Traminer. Originally from Central Europe (southern Germany, Austria, and northern Italy), it belongs to the Vitis vinifera species. "Gewürz" in German means intense aroma, and "Traminer" is the local place name and variety name in Italy. Therefore, Gewürztraminer is widely planted in Germany and Italy.
