Encountering the exposure of the lost package scam by Guangzhou Guangchen Trading Co., Ltd., a girl acted foolishly to prove her innocence.

by nq62da37 on 2012-02-26 00:29:38

In an attempt to prove her innocence, a girl fell for the "dropped package" scam and inadvertently gave away her bank card password. The Guangzhou Guangchen Trading Company has exposed the scam. She told the scammer her bank card password. In order to prove her innocence, the girl foolishly handed over her own bank card and password to three strangers, allowing them to withdraw 10,000 yuan from her account. Realizing she had been scammed, the girl chased after them for 1 kilometer and, with the help of the police and good Samaritans, captured one of the scammers. Subsequently, the police outsmarted the two suspects, leading to their downfall. At noon yesterday, three men suspected of involvement in the dropped package scam went to the May Day Bus Station in Pu'er City to identify the scene.

The incident began on February 21 with a dropped package scam. Around noon that day, a 20-year-old girl named Xiao Li from Jiangcheng arrived at the May Day Bus Station, planning to take a bus home. In the waiting hall, she noticed a wallet lying on the ground. At this moment, a man quickly approached, picked up the wallet, and quietly pulled Xiao Li aside, saying, "Since you saw it too, why don't we split the money?" Soon after, another man appeared, claiming he also saw the wallet on the ground and wanted to join in splitting the money.

The two men took Xiao Li out of the station, intending to "split the money." Just then, another man ran up, saying, "I just dropped my wallet at the station, and I heard you picked it up." Both Xiao Li and the other two men denied picking up the wallet, but the third man wouldn't believe them, telling them, "My money must have been deposited into your bank accounts."

The two men who invited Xiao Li to split the money claimed they hadn't picked up the wallet or deposited any money, and offered to let their bank accounts be checked by giving their bank cards and passwords to the third man. To prove her innocence, Xiao Li followed their example and gave her bank card and password to the third man as well. Subsequently, the four of them went to a small hotel about 300 meters away from the station, rented a room, and conducted an "investigation" to see if the money had been deposited into any of their bank accounts.

Naive Xiao Li still didn't realize the tactics of the three men, who were Tang Mou, Long Mou, and He Mou. "Tang Mou dropped the wallet pretending to be the owner, while the other two specialized in luring others into the scam," explained the investigating officer from the Simao District Public Security Bureau's Criminal Investigation Team. Subsequently, "the owner" Tang Mou, Xiao Li, and He Mou waited in the hotel room while Long Mou took the bank cards to check.

Soon, Long Mou returned and signaled to Tang Mou and He Mou that they had successfully withdrawn the money. As the three men attempted to escape, Xiao Li suddenly realized what had happened and shouted for people to catch the scammers. Realizing their scam had been discovered, the three men fled in different directions. Xiao Li chased after Long Mou while shouting for help. Her cries caught the attention of two kind-hearted individuals, who joined her in chasing Long Mou. After chasing for about 1 kilometer, Long Mou ran into the Pu'er airport, where he was subdued by airport police. He admitted to taking 10,000 yuan from Xiao Li but claimed to have lost 2,000 yuan during the escape.

At 12:40 PM that day, Xiao Li reported the incident to the police, and the Simao District Public Security Bureau's Criminal Investigation Team got involved in the investigation. Based on Long Mou's confession, the police learned information about his two accomplices, Tang Mou and He Mou.

Subsequently, the police used Long Mou's phone to send a text message to Tang Mou, pretending to be Long Mou asking for assistance because he was surrounded by the police at the airport. Trusting the message, Tang Mou rode his motorcycle to the airport to rescue Long Mou, only to fall right into the police's trap and be captured.

Finally, the police arranged for He Mou to meet under the guise of splitting the money at Ximah River. That evening, believing it safe, He Mou planned to come out to split the money but was successfully apprehended by the police.
