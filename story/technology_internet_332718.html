The ticketing website with the most purchase options

by epaylinks on 2010-03-12 14:35:21

Speaking of 311 Ticketing Network, A San has a story to tell. The other day, Uncle Liu from the neighborhood wanted to buy tickets for the play "If Only Life Were Like the First Meeting," and he asked A San to handle it on his behalf. A San told Uncle Liu to go to the OK convenience store as it was very convenient. Uncle Liu was completely confused, but given A San's professional image in this field, he still followed the advice and ended up successfully purchasing the tickets.

It must be said that talents emerge in every generation, and we can't help but admire how the younger generation pushes the older one aside. For the sake of the top position in this industry, all factions have been using every possible means. Take the 311 Ticketing Network for example; although it is a newly established platform, its first move was extraordinary. It launched with the ticketing system website used during the Asian Games and also signed agreements with almost all the chain convenience stores, laundries, and subway bookstores in Guangzhou. Moreover, it even included metro stations, tourist attractions, and bank branches. This means that wherever there are terminals installed by Yibiao Lian, such as Ibon, you can purchase electronic tickets either with the help of the store staff or independently online. You can buy and go immediately, which is highly practical for those who are not accustomed to buying tickets online or cannot wait for delivery. Therefore, there are phenomena like Uncle Liu, who never goes online, being able to buy tickets online.

We can't help but admire the "rich second generation" background of this company. As a subsidiary of the well-known Lixin Group in Guangdong, Yibiao Lian and Urban Online Ticketing Platform have a father company, Guangzhou Yibiao Lian Electronic Commerce Co., Ltd., which is the largest ticketing company in the country. Its mother company, Guangdong Urban Online Electronic Commerce Co., Ltd., is the exclusive ticketing operator for the Guangzhou 2010 Asian Games. Its grandfather, Lixin Group, is the largest electronic payment enterprise in the country. So, it is neither short of money nor channels. However, currently, although the company's website sells tickets nationwide, its offline electronic ticketing machines and physical franchise stores are still limited to Guangzhou and its surrounding cities. After all, setting up machines is an "expensive" matter. If you are in large cities in the Guangdong region like Guangzhou, Shenzhen, and Zhongshan, choosing tickets from this company is more convenient because chain stores like 7-11, OK, Circle K, Everyday Laundry, and Ruyuetang are everywhere on the streets, and in subways, they are almost omnipresent.
