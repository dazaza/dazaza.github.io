Six top candidates to replace Microsoft CEO Ballmer: Nokia's Elop included

by anonymous on 2013-08-26 14:06:58

Microsoft has started searching for a successor to Steve Ballmer. Below are the possible candidates who might appear on the list.

Microsoft announced that Steve Ballmer will retire within 12 months, and the company has officially begun its search for a replacement.

Who will appear on the candidate list? Both internal and external candidates are possible.

Kevin Turner, Microsoft's Chief Operating Officer: Turner was once seen as the surefire successor to Ballmer. He isn't very popular with employees, but people who are good at calculating usually aren't. In Microsoft's recent reorganization, Turner lost his power, so it is unlikely that he will become Microsoft's new CEO.

Tami Reller, Executive Vice President of Marketing: Reller joined Microsoft when the company acquired Great Plains Software in 2001. Since 1984, she has been working at this company. In 2007, Reller joined the Windows department, responsible for the business and marketing strategies of Windows and Windows devices.

Tony Bates, Executive Vice President: Bates joined Microsoft during the acquisition of Skype and served as President of the Skype division. In the recent reorganization, Bates' power increased, making him responsible for corporate development and evangelism. Before joining Skype, Bates was the General Manager of the Enterprise, Business, and Small Business Group at Cisco. The current Microsoft CEO needs expertise in both enterprise and consumer markets. If the next Microsoft CEO is a woman, Bates would be a very strong contender.

Satya Nadella, Executive Vice President: Nadella has worked in multiple business divisions at Microsoft. Currently, he is the President of the Server and Tools Division. Previously, he was the Vice President of the Online Services Development Division (Bing, MSN, and advertising). He also served as the head of Microsoft's Business Solutions Division (Dynamics ERP and Dynamics CRM). He undoubtedly has leadership experience in multiple departments.

Other possible candidates include:

Stephen Elop, CEO of Nokia: When Elop left his position as President of Microsoft's Business Division in 2010 to become CEO of Nokia, some joked that he might become a Trojan horse. There were rumors that Elop went to Nokia under the authorization of Ballmer and Microsoft's board of directors, with the aim of turning Nokia into Microsoft's new Windows Phone headquarters.

Steven Sinofsky, former Windows chief: Sinofsky suddenly left in 2012, and many believed he was forced out, though his own statement denied this. Sinofsky recently announced that he has become a board partner at Andreesen Horowitz. Although he still has fans within Microsoft, the possibility that he will succeed Ballmer is small.

Kevin Johnson, former CEO of Juniper Networks: Johnson recently retired from Juniper Networks. Before Juniper, Johnson was the head of Microsoft's Platforms and Services Division. He retired from that position in 2008.
