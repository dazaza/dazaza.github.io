Comment: Sister Lotus angrily enters the mobile phone circle

by anonymous on 2013-09-04 14:09:38

Just as before a movie is released, the stars go around to boost popularity, the mobile phone industry is becoming increasingly entertaining with endless topics. These days, it's not about avoiding trouble, but about being forgotten when there's no news.

In recent days, there have been quite a few events in the IT circle. There are some serious big events, such as Balmer stepping down after ten years at Microsoft (the stock price rose immediately upon the announcement, poor Balmer) and Baidu acquiring Nuomi, entering the era of group-buying sites competing for resources. There have also been many sensational events recently, one involving a famous venture capitalist which I won't mention. This time, I want to talk about how Sister Furong is entering the mobile phone market and launching a goddess phone. As the saying goes, "Wu Sangui was enraged for his beauty, Sister Furong is determined to create a miraculous machine." But is Sister Furong really angry or just pretending?

The incident started with Lei Jun's Redmi phone. After a long buildup, a month ago Xiaomi launched the Redmi phone targeting the low-end market, which greatly upset Meizu CEO Huang Zhang (English name J.Wong), who has always disliked Xiaomi and Lei Jun. Seeing that many people were discussing the Redmi phone on his Meizu forum, J.Wong exploded and posted an open letter titled "To the students who like to discuss Fengjie, Sister Furong, Redmi, and Xiaomi on the forum". The post wasn't long, and its content was as follows: "Perhaps setting lower limits will attract more attention, perhaps having no bottom line will make success more possible, success often requires not choosing means. But this is not my pursuit, if you like heavy tastes, please look clearly that this is the Meizu forum, please don't buzz around here like flies, I want to kill you."

J.Wong compared Xiaomi and Redmi to Fengjie and Sister Furong, mocking them for being "immoral and without limits". J.Wong's actions shocked many, Xiaomi Vice President Li Wanqiang retaliated, calling the "no bottom line" Redmi phone making Huang Zhang "without pants". However, this post didn't anger Lei Jun, but rather Sister Furong.

It's not surprising that Sister Furong was angry, she was truly caught in the crossfire. To be honest, J.Wong was too harsh. Thus, Sister Furong retaliated fiercely: "I never knew about Meizu, nor did I know about this dog called J.Wong!" and demanded an apology from Meizu.

After Sister Furong retaliated, everyone thought there would be a showdown, but the event didn't develop as expected. It ended abruptly at its climax. After posting that Weibo in early August, Sister Furong didn't comment further on the matter on her Weibo, and whether Meizu apologized or not remains unknown.

And so, nearly a month passed in peace. Just as people were about to forget this farce, the ever-surprising Sister Furong made waves again, announcing on Weibo that she was entering the mobile phone market, "Making phones is like being a person, it needs conscience and integrity. Some people become godfathers by making phones, some turn them into futures, others into big mouths! Sister will tell you what is the conscience of the industry, wait until I customize a goddess phone, quad-core top configuration costs only 1949!" Sister Furong alluded to a group of people, naturally including J.Wong.

Is this the ruthless move Sister Furong had been holding back for a month? According to my understanding, the actual situation is more complicated than outsiders imagine, because a third party joined in, making the event more dramatic. The joiner was an internet mobile phone company, they approached Sister Furong, hoping to collaborate and launch a customized phone for her. The company's intention is easy to understand, conduct event marketing, while Sister Furong can gain benefits, possibly endorsement fees, or possibly a share of phone sales. I also learned beforehand about the specifications of this phone, the hardware configuration is indeed high: 5.0-inch FHD screen, 8 million + 13 million dual cameras, 6.98mm ultra-thin body, 1.5GHz quad-core CPU.

Initially, it was a conflict, then evolved into a commercial interest collaboration, this is the truth behind "Sister Furong's fierce determination to create a miraculous machine".

I won't comment on this matter itself, neither denigrating nor praising. I just want to say that the mobile phone industry is becoming increasingly entertainment-oriented. Just like before a movie is released, stars go around to boost popularity, and even create emotional entanglements unrelated to the film for fans to gossip about, to increase attention to the film. These are common tactics. In this day and age, it's not about avoiding trouble, but about being forgotten when there's no news.

For mobile phone manufacturers, this approach is also acceptable, as long as it doesn't cross the line, everyone can understand and accept it. Who says mobile phones cannot engage in cross-border entertainment marketing? However, the old saying still holds true, one must practice internal strength. Making phones isn't a one-time deal, nor is it a movie, it's not enough to just lure the audience into the cinema. Even if you catch the high-attention marketing point and get users to notice you, if your product quality and experience aren't solid, in the end, you'll ruin your own brand and harm yourself.

The competition in the mobile phone industry is becoming increasingly intense, traditional methods may not be effective, so I don't oppose the entertainment marketing of mobile phones, but I do oppose excessive hype without limits and detached from reality.
