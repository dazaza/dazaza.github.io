Pacific island country discovers suspected pterodactyl strange bird, wingspan exceeds 3 meters (picture)

A "Pterosaur-like" Strange Bird Lives in Papua New Guinea Rainforest

According to Russia's Truth newspaper on August 13th, villagers in remote areas of the Pacific island country of Papua New Guinea often claim they have seen a terrifying strange bird that the locals call the "flying devil" (phonetically translated as "Ropen"). According to local villagers' descriptions, the "flying devil" has a wingspan of 3 to 4.5 meters, and it has a long beak filled with dagger-like sharp teeth. In addition, it has sharp claws and a whip-like tail, and its appearance looks like the pterosaur, which was believed to have gone extinct 65 million years ago!

Photographs of the "Flying Devil"

After World War II, rumors spread that a pterosaur-like strange bird called the "flying devil" lived in the primeval tropical jungle of Papua New Guinea. Since then, many explorers have gone to the primeval forest of Papua New Guinea to search for traces of this mysterious strange bird. American explorer David Wotherspoon and missionary Jim Blume actually managed to take some photographs of the "flying devil"! Investigators believe that this strange bird is obviously the aerial master, the pterosaur, which was thought to have gone extinct 65 million years ago.

According to local villagers, the "flying devil" usually rests during the day in dense primeval jungles and cliff caves. They come out at night to hunt. This strange bird mainly preys on fish in seawater but sometimes also attacks humans! In the mid-1980s, a local man was unfortunately killed after being attacked by a "flying devil" in his garden.

Testimony from 70 Local Natives

Blume recently told reporters that he had interviewed about 70 local indigenous islanders who claimed they had personally witnessed the "flying devil." Blume and Wotherspoon also found evidence of this strange bird's nests in some mountainous cliff areas. The two not only saw this "pterodactyl-like" bird soaring in the sky with their own eyes, but Wotherspoon also captured video evidence of it flying in the sky!

Descendants of Pterosaurs?

Blume and Wotherspoon believe there is sufficient evidence showing that two types of "living pterosaurs" - the "flying devil" and "Dua" - still live in the primeval forests of Papua New Guinea. These two "living pterosaurs" are similar in appearance, but differ in size. Investigators suspect that the larger-winged "flying devil" may be a surviving and reproducing "rhamphorhynchoid pterosaur," while the "Dua," which has a wingspan of about 1.2 meters and likes to build nests in cliff caves, could be another type of "pterosaur with a beak" that was also thought to have been extinct. However, many traditional zoologists still do not believe that living pterosaurs exist today on Earth. They think that the mysterious strange birds appearing in Papua New Guinea might just be a new species of animals that have not been discovered before.

World War II U.S. Soldier Claims Sightings

Duane Hodgkinson, a U.S. soldier stationed in Papua New Guinea during World War II, also claimed to have seen the flying "flying devil" with his own eyes. One day in 1944, while he was walking with his comrades on the island, he was shocked to see a huge strange bird flying in the sky. It had a "tail at least 3 to 4.5 meters long," and there was also a prominent large crest on its head. Hodgkinson believed he had seen a "living pterosaur." In 2004, American TV producer Jonathan Whitcomb interviewed Hodgkinson and believed his testimony to be entirely credible.
