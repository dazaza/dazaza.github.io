The only way is to write down the foolish words and complaints with pen and ink.

by ruydyir02 on 2012-02-09 10:31:41

Without the knowledge of man or ghost, the footsteps of time have secretly walked to the end of the year. Looking back on the past year, since we stepped onto the wheel of time until now, shedding the dust of bygone days, how many colorful moments, how much floral redness, have adorned our path so beautifully.

The years flow on the left bank, reflecting the fragrance of time; the loneliness on the right bank overflows with the scent of sorrow. I stand at the endpoint of this season's end, gazing afar at the direction leading to the past. In a daze, it feels as if something inside me has shattered, entangled in the tenderness of love, lost in the desolate world.

I have always enjoyed indulging in my own stories, hiding behind words in the dead of night, reminiscing about the fleeting past like the wind, laughing and sighing at the graceful dance of youth, gathering countless thoughts into one being. After the clamor subsides, I invite a gentle breeze to intoxicate my pen tip, writing out experiences as dreamlike as illusions, pausing memories at a still moment, guarding this affectionate tenderness alone.

I know that there will always be some unshakable knots in life, accompanying me across the distance between dreams and reality, to listen to the sounds of this world, to touch the holiest softness deep within.

Although many memories of the past have become blurred, even forgotten, the steps of nostalgia have never ceased. Those bittersweet experiences of the past seem like wisps of smoke drifting through the years, permeating the entire face of time, eventually transforming into ancient legends.

Perhaps, it is precisely because these encounters were too beautiful, that the faded memories are so dreamlike, just like the brilliance of blooming flowers, turning and twisting, in the wind, in dreams, transforming into fallen red petals and dust, leaving only their lingering fragrance.

As the old year draws near, the plum blossoms have withered. The passing years are so tranquil, as if apart from the bits and pieces in my memories, there is nothing else.

I often ask myself, is it true that only memories can stir the gaze of the heartstrings? If so, why am I so afraid of the melody of those heartstrings being plucked? If not, why does everything but memories return to the most primitive black and white? Is it because the events in memory are too heavy, or are the traces left behind too vivid?

Unknowingly, nightfall has arrived, and the brilliance of yesterday has long since quietly turned into the dust under my pen. Those past colors once again return to my heart, transforming into a dried flower bud, tucked between the pages of a book to sleep alongside the years.

Life is unpredictable, so why burden oneself with sorrow and hesitation? The only solace is to write down foolish complaints with ink, continuing day after day the joys and sorrows of parting and reunion, faintly rippling in the deepest part of the heart, painting ink into beauty, sketching tree after tree of blooming flowers, singing in the wind, stirring the heartstrings.

The youthful knocking of time has long since melted away without a trace, the slight intoxication in my heart has nowhere to escape. Imagining my own springtime bloom, how can I, in the icy winter, meet again with my past?

The wind blows silently, rising into the clouds, swirling up countless plum blossoms, each petal transforming into spring soil, forming a lush mound, yet still able to hope for the bright scenery of the next season. But we cannot look back, for a person's life is like the autumn of grass and trees, never to return.

The passing years mean eternal separation, leaving only shattered fragments in the mind. Those times of flowing water and falling flowers always pause in the fluttering butterfly dance, always unexpectedly sweeping over the heart.

The clouds of yesteryear linger in the depths of my heart, every light dance of the wind becoming a perfect poem, every cessation of the wind and settling of dust becoming a touching and entangled dream. From this shore's biting cold wind, they look back at me, smiling for me.
