【Initial Symptoms of Scabies】

Experts from the dermatology department of Shanxi Huimin Hospital introduced that after the incubation period of scabies, the initial rash often appears on moist and soft skin, such as between the fingers and wrist areas where the skin is easily parasitized, then spreading to other parts of the body, such as elbows, waist, armpits, abdomen, and genitals. The initial symptoms mainly include pink, blister-like small bumps appearing in thin-skinned areas, with transparent liquid oozing out when squeezed. If there are other patients around or if you have traveled recently and experience these symptoms, you should be highly vigilant.

【Precautions】

Given the long incubation period of scabies, patients need to pay special attention and ideally discover it early for prompt treatment. In addition, scabies needs to be differentiated from the following diseases:

1. Papular urticaria: This condition is more common in children, with no specific predilection sites. The rash presents as spindle-shaped wheal papules or wheal vesicles.

2. Pruritus: The lesions in this disease have no primary rash and occur unpredictably. They are rarely seen between the fingers. The onset is often related to emotions, seasons, climate changes, visceral diseases, and menopausal disorders.

3. Pediculosis: This disease is caused by lice, with secondary lesions being common. It tends to occur on the sides, waistband area, genitals, or skin areas in contact with clothing seams.
