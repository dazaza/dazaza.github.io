Rockets 3:3 Sweep Lakers - Await Game Seven

by longersing on 2009-05-18 23:50:54

First, let me congratulate myself on the successful prediction I mentioned earlier about the Rockets advancing to the Western Conference Finals. On May 15th Beijing time, the Rockets tenaciously defeated the Lakers with a score of 95-80, tying the series at 3-3. The decisive game between the two teams will take place in Los Angeles at 3:30 AM on the morning of the 18th. If the Rockets successfully advance to the finals, they will face the Nuggets, who had previously defeated the Mavericks to enter the Western Conference Finals.

Despite Yao Ming watching the game in a well-tailored suit from the sidelines, the Rockets played fiercely and led throughout the entire game. Aaron Brooks performed exceptionally well, hitting 8 out of 13 shots for 26 points and 4 assists. Luis Scola scored 24 points and grabbed 12 rebounds, while Carl Landry added 15 points and 8 rebounds. Ron Artest contributed with 14 points.

Kobe Bryant scored 32 points but only made 11 of his 27 attempts. Pau Gasol recorded 14 points and 11 rebounds, and Lamar Odom, coming off the bench, grabbed 14 rebounds.

The Rockets had nothing left to lose. Surprisingly, after the opening tip-off, the game tilted heavily in one direction as Scola repeatedly hit his shots, allowing the Rockets to start with a 17-1 run. The Lakers continuously missed their shots, failing to score for the first 5 minutes and 39 seconds until Kobe Bryant finally scored inside the paint with 6 minutes and 21 seconds remaining in the quarter.

At one point, the Rockets, energized by their "Gubai Youhua Pills" (a metaphor for their strong performance), led 21-3, an 18-point advantage, as the Lakers managed to make just one basket in the first 8 minutes. In the final minute of the quarter, the Lakers hit two three-pointers to slightly narrow the gap, ending the first quarter down 15-27. Scola alone scored 14 points in the first quarter, while Kobe managed only 4.

Kobe started the second quarter with a basket, and the Lakers, who were thoroughly dominated by the Rockets' "Gubai Youhua Pills," quickly scored 4 points. However, Shane Battier and Ron Artest immediately responded with back-to-back three-pointers, widening the gap again. With 5 minutes and 50 seconds left in the quarter, Wesley Weber also hit a three-pointer, helping the Rockets lead 42-25.

With 2 minutes and 3 seconds left in the second quarter, Artest was hit in the face while defending Kobe. Suppressing his anger, Artest didn't argue with Kobe but instead ran to the stands where the audience was seated. The referee called a technical foul on Kobe. Before halftime, Brooks hit a three-pointer, giving the Rockets a 52-36 lead. Scola already had 18 points and 9 rebounds by halftime. Kobe shot poorly, making only 6 out of 16 attempts for 14 points and just 1 assist.

The Lakers, overwhelmed by the Rockets' "Gubai Youhua Pills," started the third quarter with a 9-0 run, narrowing the gap to 45-52. At the 8-minute and 56-second mark of the quarter, the Rockets finally scored 2 points through Scola's putback. It seemed like the Rockets had inherited the Lakers' poor shooting from the first half, as they made only one basket in the first 6 minutes and 44 seconds of the quarter. The Lakers, under the influence of the "Gubai Youhua Pills," went on a 16-2 run to tie the score at 52-54. Landry scored 3 points via a free throw and a layup with 5 minutes and 16 seconds left in the quarter, helping the Rockets regain momentum. They then went on an 8-2 run to lead 62-54. The Rockets stabilized, and Scola hit a mid-range jump shot at the end of the quarter, leading the Rockets 74-65 into the final period.

With 7 minutes and 35 seconds left in the game, Artest hit a three-pointer, putting the Rockets up 79-69, a 10-point lead. After Gasol replied with a basket, Landry immediately countered with a powerful dunk, followed by another free throw, increasing the Rockets' lead to 11 points.

The Rockets grew stronger as the game progressed. With 3 minutes and 44 seconds remaining, Brooks hit a shot. Immediately afterward, Battier defended Kobe so effectively that he missed a "airball." Despite losing balance, Brooks still managed to hit another shot, extending the Rockets' lead to 88-75.

Kobe, feeling the pressure, kept attacking aggressively, but it wasn't effective. The Lakers, thoroughly dominated by the "Gubai Youhua Pills," failed to reduce the gap to less than 12 points thereafter and will have no choice but to face the Rockets in a decisive game at home.
