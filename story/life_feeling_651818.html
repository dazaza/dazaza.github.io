Faded Flowers, Years of Loneliness, and Solitude as Fragrant

Because I clearly understand that the rain of fireworks will eventually fade from my life, I have been searching for an unknown ending.

The broken tears cannot string together the lingering fragrance of bygone years. When all the splendor fades away, who still lingers in the passage of time, softly chanting the fossilized vows of that season?

— Preface

[Dancing Drunkenly Amidst the Mortal World, Lights Fading Slowly]

Petals flutter gracefully on the other shore, clusters of them singing with sorrow at midnight. They collect the lingering attachment of a shallow summer, while white feathers dance silently under the moonlight.

Indifferent to the full moon blooming from fireworks, who accompanies you in your infatuation by the lakeside of the mortal world?

Graceful fingers flit about, unable to play the pretense of beauty. Wandering through memories amidst the fading lights of the years, I have forgotten how far forever is.

Tears slide over the eyelids, hastily embracing prophecy.

Who knows, for whom does one stitch of embroidery cause pain? Waiting for a display of fireworks until the winds and moons decayed into splendor, yet never encountering anything beautiful.

Who knows, for whom does a flower bloom in intoxication? This cycle of life follows you, tossing and turning amidst the mortal world. Quietly, I dance drunkenly across the horizon.

Who knows, for whom does a plain brush sketch throughout a lifetime? The times passed through could not make clear which face, and upon turning around, tears reach the point of overwhelming the city.

At the end of the day, what has devoured youth? Unable to touch the former brightness, only memories remain scattered across the pages.

[Memories Like Smoke, Flowers Blooming Rampantly]

Time rushes by silently, following you through solitude, darkening a grand encounter.

My heart aches faintly; the faded vows crinkle memory.

Unable to grasp this fleeting life, it is because you forgot to wait for me. The fragrance swirls through the years, broken threads of rain sing songs of loneliness.

Unable to hold the brilliant colors of youth, I look back to see your faint smile.

Those pains flowing through the years are remembered. When time reveals the ending of fairy tales, I can only strive to discern your gradually retreating figure.

Those dreams sinking into midnight are shattered. The actor entering the painting was destined for a life of sorrow, and I am ultimately powerless to pick up the scattered fragrance amidst the floating dust.

Those facades frozen in the years are entangled. Residual flowers dance indifferently to a long-lasting eternity, stopping at the hurt between eyebrows, scattering imaginations of that season.

Warm-colored dreams shatter everywhere, and I crouch down to pick them up piece by piece...

[Residual Flowers Through the Years, Loneliness as Fragrant]

The hidden fragrance falling through the years quietly leaps onto the brows. Thoughts escape their bounds, accompanying me in burying the end of heaven and earth.

The colors blooming from fireworks pale my lifelong wait. I walk quietly until I can no longer find the way back to memory.

Picking up a page of floral rafts, I engrave the tender emotions of days past. In the vast sea of people, I sink low into the dust.

Drunk on a sheet of poetic lines, I reminisce about the rampant flower events of bygone years. Beautiful beyond measure, I recall your smiling glance.

Dancing within a dream, I grow indifferent to the vows made yesterday. As night falls and the moon is hurt, time swiftly departs.

Immersed in the years with you, through the rolling red dust, I solely cherish the joy you granted me for a season.

Residual flowers through the years, petals filled with sorrow, tingeing the loneliness like fragrance. Splendor fades completely, who will build a haven of lasting eternity for me?
