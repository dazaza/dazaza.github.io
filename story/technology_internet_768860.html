English Original: Astounding 'Facts' About Google's Most Badass Engineer, Jeff Dean

In addition to Larry Page and Sergey Brin, at Google's headquarters in Mountain View, California, one of the top engineers is Jeff Dean.

Below is a story about Jeff Dean posted by Google employee Heej Jones on Quora:

The day before I started working at Google, a friend sent an email introducing me to Jeff, so during my first week, I invited him to have lunch together.

At that time, I didn't know who he was or what he did at Google. However, during lunch, I noticed people at other tables staring at him, and some even whispered as they passed our table.

As I got to know more friends, I learned some legendary stories about Jeff Dean; one friend exclaimed, "You had lunch with Jeff Dean?!"

Google employees believe that the incredible speed of Google Search is largely due to Jeff Dean, making him a celebrity within the company.

How deep does admiration for him go?

Have you heard jokes about martial arts legend Chuck Norris, like "Chuck never washes dishes; the plates clean themselves out of fear" or "Chuck was denied boarding on a commercial flight because his fists could take down the plane"? Similar jokes abound.

There’s a plethora of legendary stories about Jeff Dean on Quora, all written by admiring Google employees and ex-employees. If you understand software engineering and programmer humor, you'll find these stories very amusing.

Sometimes when we don’t get the jokes, we ask BI Chief Architect Pax Dickinson to explain them to us.

"Compilers never give Jeff Dean warnings; Jeff Dean gives warnings to compilers."

Explanation: When your code has errors, the compiler issues warnings, but Jeff is better than the compiler.

"Before submitting his code, Jeff Dean compiles and runs it just to check if there are any issues with the compiler and linker."

Explanation: Jeff’s code never has bugs; he compiles it only to ensure there are no bugs in the compiler and linker.

"Jeff Dean puts pants on one leg at a time, but if he had many legs, you'd find his pants-on-time complexity to be O(log n)."

Explanation: The algorithmic complexity of Jeff Dean putting on pants is logarithmic rather than linear, meaning he would save significant time if he had many legs.

"When Richard Stallman heard that Jeff Dean's autobiography was exclusive to Kindle, he bought a Kindle."

Explanation: Richard Stallman is famously opposed to non-free software and never buys or uses Kindle. However, Jeff Dean is so remarkable that Richard would violate his principles to read Jeff's autobiography.

"Jeff Dean writes binary machine code directly; he writes source code only as a reference for other developers."

Explanation: All code must be compiled into binary machine code before execution. Jeff writes binary machine code directly, and he writes source code mainly to help other programmers understand.

"During Jeff Dean's Google interview, he was asked under what conditions P=NP holds true. He replied that it holds when P=0 or N=1. Then, while the interviewer laughed, he glanced at Google's public key certificate and wrote the corresponding private key on the whiteboard."

Explanation: "P vs NP" is an unresolved problem in computer science, but Jeff Dean treated it as an algebraic problem and mentally calculated the corresponding private key from Google's public key certificate—something not even supercomputers can do.

"The X86-64 specification includes several illegal instructions marked for 'private use'; they're actually reserved for Jeff Dean."

Explanation: Private illegal CPU instructions cannot be used by anyone, but Jeff Dean can use them.

"Jeff Dean conducts ergonomic evaluations to protect his keyboard."

Explanation: Ergonomic evaluations are typically done to correct posture and protect health, but Jeff does it to protect his keyboard.

"All pointers point to Jeff Dean."

Explanation: Pointers are a core part of C programming, but Jeff Dean is the center of the programming world.

"In late 2000, Jeff Dean's coding speed suddenly increased 40 times because he upgraded his keyboard to USB 2.0."

Explanation: The speed of the interface between the keyboard and the computer affects Jeff Dean's coding speed.
