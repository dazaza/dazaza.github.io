Seven out of ten hypertensive patients have abnormal blood pressure --提示篇

by 60888888 on 2010-02-02 11:23:08

According to the results of a recent nationwide survey on blood pressure control among 5,186 hypertensive patients in 100 top-tier hospitals, despite over half of these hypertensive patients taking two or more antihypertensive drugs at the time of the survey, only 31.1% of them achieved their blood pressure targets. Experts pointed out that for hypertensive patients with comorbidities such as coronary heart disease and diabetes, the blood pressure control target should be stricter than for the general population. Patients with high cardiovascular risk factors should ideally start combination therapy from the beginning of treatment. Currently, there are two forms of combination therapy: prescription-based combination therapy and single-pill combination formulations. If patients need to take other medications for chronic diseases, using single-pill combination formulations can reduce the likelihood of missing doses.

For hypertensive patients with combined coronary heart disease and kidney disease, the blood pressure control target is stricter. It is understood that currently, the main global hypertension guidelines set 140/90 mmHg as the target for achieving blood pressure control for ordinary hypertensive patients. However, for high-risk patients with comorbidities such as coronary heart disease, diabetes, or kidney disease, the blood pressure target should be below 130/80 mmHg.

Although for the general population, a blood pressure above 140/90 mmHg is considered hypertension, within the range of 115/75 mmHg to 140/90 mmHg, it actually falls under "high-normal blood pressure." International hypertension research shows that when blood pressure exceeds 115/75 mmHg, the incidence rate and mortality risk of cardiovascular events increase with rising blood pressure. Conversely, for hypertensive patients, each decrease of 10/5 mmHg in systolic/diastolic blood pressure reduces the risk of myocardial infarction by about 20%, the risk of stroke by nearly 40%, and the risk of heart failure by half.

More than 40% of hypertensive patients miss doses

The Chinese Medical Doctor Association Cardiovascular Internal Medicine Physician Branch and the Chinese Hypertension League initiated and hosted a survey on the current status of hypertension control in China last year. The project included 5,186 hypertensive patients from cardiology, nephrology, and endocrinology departments in 100 top-tier hospitals across the country. The results showed that the proportion of moderate and severe hypertensive patients among the respondents exceeded 70%. Compared to 2006, the blood pressure control rate for Chinese hypertensive patients is even more concerning: "Only 31.1% of the surveyed patients had overall blood pressure control." Chen Luyuan introduced that the survey also showed that more than half of hypertensive patients took two or more antihypertensive drugs to control their blood pressure, but more than 40% of the patients missed doses, which was one of the reasons affecting the blood pressure achievement rate. In addition, poor efficacy of antihypertensive drugs and side effects might also be influencing factors.

Cardiovascular high-risk individuals should start combination therapy initially

Currently, some hypertensive patients begin combination therapy only when the effect of a single drug is not good. However, a foreign study showed that starting combination antihypertensive therapy from the beginning of treatment can bring clinical benefits, especially for patients with cardiovascular disease high-risk factors who need early blood pressure control. He explained that combination therapy actually includes two methods: prescription combination and single-pill combination formulations. The former involves doctors prescribing two or more drugs simultaneously for patients to take, but this requires patients to take more than two antihypertensive pills each time, and many elderly patients also need to take other medications, making it inconvenient and leading to missed doses.

Single-pill combination formulations refer to combining two effective components into one pill, making it easier to take. Chen Luyuan stated that prescription combinations may not achieve the optimal ratio, while single-pill combination formulations can optimize the dosage ratio of the two effective components during research and development, thus achieving better blood pressure control results. He indicated that hypertensive patients with blood pressure higher than 160/100 mmHg, those with combined diabetes, metabolic syndrome, or confirmed cardiovascular or kidney diseases, are suitable for obtaining stronger antihypertensive effects through single-pill combination formulations.

Special Reminder

Eating too much salt weakens the effectiveness

In Northeast China, each person consumes an average of 16 grams of salt per day; in Yunnan, Guizhou, and Sichuan provinces, around 12 grams; and in the Pearl River Delta region, where the diet is lighter, approximately 9 grams per person per day. However, this still exceeds the World Health Organization's recommendation that each adult should consume no more than 6 grams of salt per day. Hypertensive patients need to pay attention to their lifestyle during medication treatment (please refer to hypertension dietary therapy), because consuming too much salt not only increases the risk of hypertension but also affects the effectiveness of the medication.
