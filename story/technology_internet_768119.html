Facebook Q3 2013 Results: Revenue at $2.02 Billion, Net Profit at $425 Million

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 18:19:14

Facebook has just released its Q3 2013 financial report, which shows that last quarter Facebook generated a total revenue of $2.02 billion, representing a year-on-year increase of 60%; net profit was $425 million, compared to a loss of $59 million during the same period last year.

The GAAP diluted operating profit was $736 million, up 95% year-on-year. The EBITDA operating profit was $987 million, increasing by 88% year-on-year.

In all business segments, advertising revenue reached $1.8 billion, accounting for 66% of total revenue, with mobile advertising revenue making up about 49% of advertising revenue. Revenue from payments and other services was $218 million, growing by 24% year-on-year.

User activity also saw growth. In Q3, Facebook's monthly active users (MAU) reached 1.19 billion, growing by 18% year-on-year, with an average of 874 million mobile monthly active users; daily active users reached 728 million, increasing by 25% year-on-year, with an average of 507 million mobile daily active users.

The quarterly revenue of $2.02 billion exceeded market expectations, as analysts had generally predicted that Facebook’s Q3 earnings per share would be $0.19, with total revenue at $1.9 billion. In the report, Facebook founder and CEO Mark Zuckerberg praised the company's excellent performance while reiterating that "connecting the world" remains Facebook's ultimate goal:

"Our outstanding results in the last quarter indicate that we are ready for the next phase of the company. We will strive to keep the future 5 billion users online and connected to the knowledge economy."
