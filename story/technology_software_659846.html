Tang Chuncheng / Drawing On February 1, more than four hundred third-year junior high school students from the West Campus of Shenyang Yutian Experimental Middle School, along with staff members carrying bedding and luggage, boarded ten buses to travel 100 kilometers away to Tanggangzi Town in Anshan City for extra classes. After this incident was exposed, the Shenyang Education Bureau asked the school to immediately end the supplementary classes and severely deal with the school and related responsible parties. It is reported that the involved school has a "precedent" (as per Jinghua Times on February 5).

The exposure of the school's violation of supplementary class regulations by the media owes its thanks to the parents' reports. However, what makes people feel quite awkward is that the reason for the parents' report is not the "violation of supplementary class rules," but rather the safety hazards associated with "cross-city supplementary classes." For instance, whether the accommodation and study environment at the supplementary class location 100 kilometers away can ensure students focus on their studies and whether there are any safety hazards on the road back and forth, etc. This means that, in a sense, the parents may not oppose the act of violating supplementary class rules itself, but rather whether this method poses a threat to the personal safety of the students.

It should be said that this is not a phenomenon unique to a certain school or region at a particular time, but rather a widespread issue. Logically speaking, following-the-herd violations of supplementary class rules are not beneficial to the physical and mental health of students and run counter to the concept of reducing the burden on children that various sectors of society and educational administrative departments have been calling for and advocating. However, despite the continuous calls from all sectors of society to reduce the burden on children, frankly speaking, the efforts of the education administrative departments have not made much substantial progress, and have even resulted in the embarrassing situation of "the more the burden is reduced, the heavier it becomes."

One very important reason for such an embarrassing situation is that many measures to reduce the burden only address the symptoms but not the root cause, failing to touch on the fundamental issue of why students have such heavy academic burdens. The root cause of the heavy academic burden on primary and secondary school students today is the long-standing exam-oriented education system. Under the context of exam-oriented education, perhaps the key factor for success in competition is the students' ability to memorize knowledge or recite information. The result of this "cramming-style" competition is that whoever crams the most "ducks" into the system has a higher chance of succeeding. This also leads to the phenomenon where many parents, who should be the best supervisors against illegal supplementary classes, sometimes even collude with or indulge those who violate the rules.

Therefore, to change the current widespread phenomenon of illegal supplementary classes, fundamentally speaking, we still need to quickly transform exam-oriented education into quality-oriented education, making illegal supplementary classes that compete for time and energy useless. Of course, one point that needs clarification is that the curbing of illegal supplementary class phenomena does not necessarily mean a reduction in students' "burden," because as long as there is increasingly fierce social competition, there will never be so-called "easy learning." (Beijing, Zhiling)
