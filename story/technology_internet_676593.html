Ergonomic furniture is gaining increasing attention in our modern life. By studying excellent cases from the West, we can conclude that a good ergonomic chair is not just about data values, but also other factors.

1. The principle of chair design is analyzed from the perspective of health usage. Based on human physiological conditions and fatigue measurement, the external curve design of the chair is defined.

The main consideration for the chair's curve is whether the cushion and backrest curves match the physiological curves of the human body. There are mainly two views regarding the backrest: one suggests no backrest, which would naturally keep the person's back straight to reduce back pressure. However, this view is not very popular because it is impossible for people to maintain a straight posture for a long time. The second view, which is more widely accepted today, is the need for a backrest to lean on. Nowadays, people no longer sit still on chairs; they present various postures. The backrest gives their lumbar muscles a chance to rest and relax. However, true relaxation depends on whether the backrest curve design of the chair is reasonable.

2. Specific dimensions of chair design vary according to their different functions. Based on anthropometric data and national standards, continuous testing and adjustments are made to select appropriate numerical values to meet scientific design requirements.

For most designers and manufacturers, determining the proper scale of furniture is the first step in making ergonomically designed furniture, and it is an extremely important one. This is because the determination of the length, width, height of furniture, as well as the spatial relationships formed by their placement, all depend on human measurements and data. However, most companies cannot measure large amounts of data when designers are designing furniture. Without professional equipment and dedicated mapping personnel, the data is unscientific and unreliable. At the same time, manufacturers do not have a lot of time and energy to spend on data measurement. Therefore, designers must be adept at utilizing existing authoritative data and flexibly apply and appropriately modify and adjust it based on the acquired data to truly design ergonomic furniture. To grasp the principles of human scale design, one should focus on the following aspects:

1) Understand national standard data

2) Be skilled at using anthropometric data in design

3) Learn to adjust and update dimensional data

In addition to being able to flexibly use the measured dimensions, we must also consider its timeliness. Everything in the world is not static; it evolves and changes. Therefore, these measurement data will change with the passage of time and societal cultural changes. Hence, if one does not understand how to reasonably use the data and comprehend the essence of ergonomics data utilization, and merely mechanically applies it, it could lead to counterproductive results.

3. Truly ergonomic furniture must pay attention to the detailed design of each part of the chair.

Renowned foreign design masters place great importance on the details of the chair, which is what they refer to as the "millimeter effect." In their view, often a difference of a few millimeters can bring vastly different levels of comfort. We can see that the selection of data values often falls within a range. If randomness occurs during value selection along with the existence of errors, the final dimensions of the designed chair may be unexpected. This is why masters are so meticulous in the selection and testing of data.

Moreover, regarding the use of connecting components for the chair, from the form of connection to the choice of connection position, even down to the selection of screws, they are meticulous. The chair is a whole entity, and the connecting parts are indispensable and important components. They are also crucial elements in the structural form and load-bearing construction of the chair. Good components can give the chair a unique feel. Therefore, the pursuit of detail is also key to the success or failure of ergonomic furniture.

4. Emphasize the combination of functional beauty and formal beauty in the chair, paying attention to the different psychological emotions brought about by different environments, thereby achieving a sense of satisfaction in ergonomic psychology.

Ergonomic products are not the unattractive yet useful products traditionally imagined by people. With the advancement of science and technology and the significant development of industrial design, ergonomic products have become practical artworks that possess both practical and artistic value. Practical art, as a part of aesthetics, is closely related to ergonomics because practical art is most closely related to people's daily lives such as clothing, food, and use, directly serving human needs. Similarly, ergonomics is also a discipline centered around "people," starting research from the physiological and psychological aspects of humans, exploring human tools and environments, and ultimately aiming to serve humanity — the research results will be used to improve human production efficiency and quality of life. Furthermore, there exists a mutually dependent relationship between the two. On one hand, practical art is the object of ergonomic research, and simultaneously serves as the medium for converting research value. For ergonomics, without practical art, research results are merely theoretical discussions. For practical art, without ergonomic research, the practical and aesthetic values of artworks would significantly diminish. Only by fully combining the two can they complement each other.

Finnish furniture design master Kukkapuro believed that human curves are the most beautiful, and that chair forms designed according to human curves are also natural products of beauty. This concept runs through his own works consistently.

Renowned Chinese furniture designer Zhu Xiaojie believes that the aesthetic effect of materials is the foundation of formal aesthetics. The "Aobu" black gold wood furniture series integrates the texture of the material itself into the shape design of the chair, forming a natural decorative beauty.

In summary, besides appropriate dimensions, a good ergonomic chair design should also pay attention to adapting the form design of the chair to the properties of objects: adapting to the characteristics of scale; adapting to the characteristics of function; adapting to the characteristics of change; adapting to the characteristics of material; focusing on the combination of form and color; having a sense of the times; meeting the requirements of the working environment; considering the national conditions and preferences of different countries; emphasizing technical craftsmanship and economic efficiency.
