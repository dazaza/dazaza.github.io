Yesmywine Reveals: Lie 1 - Cured Meats and Sausages as Accompaniments

During feasts, it's absolutely crucial not to drink on an empty stomach. If you cannot eat something beforehand to line your stomach, it’s best to eat while drinking. Additionally, avoid using salted fish, sausages, or cured meats as accompaniments for alcohol. These smoked and cured foods contain large amounts of dyes and nitrosamines, which react with alcohol, not only damaging the liver but also harming the oral cavity and esophagus lining, potentially even leading to cancer.

To minimize the harm alcohol causes to the stomach and liver and reduce the occurrence of fatty liver disease, it's best to eat something before drinking, such as having a glass of milk or eating some eggs and meat. Yesmywine suggests that these high-protein foods can combine with alcohol in the stomach, reducing alcohol absorption.

Yesmywine Reveals: Lie 2 - Inducing Vomiting by Scratching the Throat When Drunk

In general social engagements, many people use the trick of inducing vomiting by scratching their throat after drinking too much, feeling better afterward and even continuing to drink. However, experts point out that this is a dangerous action.

Inducing vomiting by scratching the throat should only be done when fully conscious or under medical supervision, as intoxicated individuals may easily inhale vomit, causing suffocation and potentially endangering life. Furthermore, violent vomiting increases abdominal pressure, easily leading to gastric bleeding and causing the contents of the duodenum to flow backward, triggering acute pancreatitis and other emergencies.

Yesmywine Reveals: Lie 3 - White Spirits Harm, Red Wine Nourishes

Many believe that drinking white spirits harms the body, while drinking red wine benefits health, and drinking more doesn't matter. In fact, whether it's red wine or white spirits, the key lies in controlling the amount consumed.

Experts indicate that weekly alcohol intake should be less than 140 grams for men and 70 grams for women; exceeding these limits increases the risk of alcoholic liver disease. 140 grams of alcohol is equivalent to 3-4 taels (Chinese unit) of 50-degree white spirit, meaning adult males should not consume more than 3-4 taels of 50-degree white spirit per week, and red wine should be limited to one or two taels daily.

Yesmywine Reveals: Lie 4 - Suddenly Quitting Alcohol Harms the Body

Many are advised by doctors to quit drinking due to health issues, but many fail to successfully quit, even using the excuse that suddenly quitting alcohol harms the body to continue their lavish drinking lifestyle.

Experts point out that the belief that suddenly quitting alcohol harms the body actually refers to withdrawal symptoms. For those who have developed alcohol dependence, suddenly quitting may cause symptoms like hand tremors, palpitations, seizures, and vomiting. However, at this point, it is more important to quit drinking rather than thinking that a small amount of alcohol will alleviate symptoms. Clinically, there are appropriate medications that can effectively control withdrawal symptoms.

Yesmywine Reveals: Lie 5 - Smoking and Drinking Go Hand-in-Hand

Some believe that smoking a cigarette and drinking a glass of wine brings immense pleasure. Especially during lively drinking sessions, even those who don’t usually smoke might accept a cigarette offered to them, saying "rarely do we have such joy today."

However, smoking while drinking harms both the liver and lungs. Nicotine from cigarettes weakens the effects of alcohol on the body, making one feel as if they're being anesthetized. Unconsciously, the amount of alcohol consumed greatly increases.

Yesmywine Reveals: Lie 6 - Strong Tea or Coffee Sober You Up

Some think that strong tea or coffee helps sober up after drinking, but this is actually a misconception.

Drinking strong tea after alcohol consumption causes caffeine and other components to quickly exert a diuretic effect, promoting the rapid entry of acetaldehyde (a substance highly stimulating to the kidneys) into the kidneys, damaging them. Meanwhile, caffeine, the main component of coffee, stimulates the central nervous system and muscles. Drinking coffee after alcohol makes the brain transition from extreme inhibition to extreme excitement, stimulating blood vessel dilation and accelerating circulation, greatly increasing cardiovascular burden. The harm to the human body exceeds that of simple alcohol consumption many times over, potentially even inducing hypertension.

Yesmywine Reveals: Lie 7 - Mixing Drinks with Beverages is Fashionable

Nowadays, mixing drinks has become a trendy way of drinking. Red wine with Sprite, whisky with iced black tea, beer with cola... various combinations abound. Since mixed drinks have lower alcohol concentration, they feel like drinking beverages, so many people prefer them.

However, experts warn that carbonated beverages used for mixing release carbon dioxide in the stomach, forcing alcohol to enter the small intestine quickly. The small intestine absorbs alcohol faster than the stomach, thus increasing harm.

Moreover, mixing drinks appears to dilute alcohol, but it often leads to drinking more. Initially, drinkers feel like they’re consuming a beverage and drink heavily. By the time they realize the effects of alcohol, they've already had too much.

Yesmywine Reveals: Lie 8 - Red Face Means Less Drunkenness

The statement that people who turn red when drinking are less likely to get drunk is often used as a reason to persuade others to drink at banquets. But in reality, intoxication has little to do with facial color.

Some think turning red-faced like Guan Yu after drinking is good, believing it indicates good blood circulation and the ability to quickly break down alcohol, making one less prone to drunkenness. However, experts point out that tolerance and facial expression have little relation, varying from person to person. In fact, the reason many believe red-faced drinkers are less likely to get drunk is because they are generally persuaded less, drink less, and fall asleep after drinking, regaining vitality after 15-30 minutes. Conversely, those with pale faces often don't know their limits and drink excessively in high spirits.

Yesmywine Reveals: Lie 9 - Deep Feelings Mean Drinking in One Gulp

Some enjoy drinking fast and frequently urge others to "drink in one gulp if you have deep feelings, lick if you have shallow feelings, let's go."

In reality, the speed of drinking should be slow rather than fast. Drinking quickly causes blood ethanol concentration to rise rapidly, leading to quick intoxication. Slow drinking allows the body sufficient time to metabolize ethanol, producing less ethanol and making one less prone to drunkenness.

Yesmywine Reveals: Lie 10 - High-Alcohol Content Makes It More Potent

In daily life, some always think low-alcohol content drinks are made by mixing alcohol with pure water, making them less potent, while high-alcohol content drinks are mostly grain-based, causing less headache after intoxication and feeling more potent.

Yesmywine reveals: In fact, higher alcohol content means more alcohol entering the body. Since over 90% of alcohol is metabolized by the liver, excessive alcohol increases the liver's detoxification burden. The higher the alcohol content, the greater the intake, and the more severe the damage to the liver. Moreover, enzymes and vitamins are required for liver metabolism, and the higher the alcohol content, the more enzymes and vitamins the body consumes.
