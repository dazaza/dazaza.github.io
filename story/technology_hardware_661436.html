Most people clean their range hoods by disassembling and washing them, which not only wastes time but also easily damages the parts. Here we introduce a method that does not require disassembly and is both time-saving and labor-saving.

Microwave Oven

1. Unplug the power cord. Wipe the exterior with a damp cloth or sponge.

2. When cleaning the interior, place a cup of water inside and steam it for three minutes; the water vapor helps remove stains.

3. The turntable and support can be wiped with a cloth dipped in detergent. After wiping, make sure to dry thoroughly. Keep it dry.

Tip: Do not use hard steel wool to clean the inside of the microwave.

Pots

1. For non-stick pans, pour in some dishwashing liquid first and scrub with a scrubbing pad.

2. To remove adhered substances from stainless steel pot bottoms, pour dishwashing liquid and water into the pot, boil for 10 minutes, then clean.

3. After scrubbing iron pots, coat them with a layer of vegetable oil to prevent rust.

Tip: For dirt on the outside of the pot, soak the pot in hot soapy water for 20 minutes, then scrub with a hard scrubbing pad.

Cooktops

1. Spray a mild cleaner first, then wipe with a damp cloth.

2. Use fine wire to brush off carbonized materials from the burner ports, and use fine wire to unblock each ignition hole one by one, then use a brush to remove dirt.

3. Remove control knobs and wash separately.

Tip: For stubborn grease on the cooktop, mix hot water with detergent, apply more cleaner on the greasy area with a cleaning cloth, wait a few minutes, then clean, www.lyak.net, and it will be easy to handle!

Sink

1. Apply kitchen cleaner on a wet cloth to clean the sink.

2. If necessary, pour coffee grounds into the sink and rinse with water to remove odors and grease from the drain pipe.

Tip: Avoid using hard stain-removing steel wool to clean the sink to prevent damage to the surface and leave scratches.

Refrigerator

1. Unplug the power cord and take out all items inside the refrigerator.

2. Remove detachable accessories for washing.

3. Wipe the inside with a damp cloth.

4. Wipe the outside with a soft dry cloth.

5. Use a vacuum cleaner to remove dust from the back.

Tip: If the inside is too dirty, you can wipe with a cloth dipped in dishwashing liquid, then clean again with a clean cloth dipped in clear water.

Cabinets

1. Wipe the exterior with a soft damp cloth, if necessary dip a small amount of cleaner and gently wipe. Be careful not to scratch the surface.

2. It's best to use an antibacterial detergent to clean the inside. Make sure not to wet the joints.

Tip: After cleaning and wiping, make sure the cabinets are completely dry before putting items back in to prevent bacteria growth and mold.

Walls

1. Spray some all-purpose kitchen cleaner on the walls, then stick kitchen paper towels.

2. After about 15 minutes, peel off the paper towel and proceed with the wiping work.

3. For harder-to-clean places like grout between tiles, use an old toothbrush to scrub.

Tip: Stick aluminum foil specifically designed for kitchens near the cooktop to prevent grease from staining wall tiles. Simply replace the foil when it gets dirty, convenient and practical.

Kitchen Glass

1. First, remove the dust inside and outside the glass with a feather duster, then spray a large X-shape on the entire glass with glass cleaner.

2. Fold the wrung-out cloth and wipe around the window edges.

3. Turn the cloth over and slowly wipe inward in the same manner.

4. After the whole glass is cleaned, let it air dry until it's seven-tenths dry, then wipe once more with a dry cloth.

Tip: For patterned frosted glass, you must first scrub with an old toothbrush, then rinse with clean water, and finally wipe dry with a dry cloth.
