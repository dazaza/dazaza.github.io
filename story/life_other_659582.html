Being young and ignorant of love at that time

by zhouzhou56 on 2012-02-06 14:10:51

Back then, we were young and didn't understand love.

If you compare him from five years ago to now, the difference could be described as night and day. No one would dare to believe that the boy who is now so silent, calm, and full of depth was once so unrestrained and reckless.

For example, back then, he would grow his hair long and dye it grape red just to keep up with the trends. During the New Year's Eve party, he would suddenly jump onto a table, grab a broom as a guitar, and start playing while singing Tang Lei's "Lilac Flower." He would join the group of bullies to show off in front of younger students.

However, despite being like that, someone actually liked him. Perhaps it was because of his good grades; although he was very playful and wild, he took studying seriously. Back then, he always ranked among the top few in exams.

The youth of thirteen or fourteen years old is like a lotus about to bloom, ready to release its fragrance. At this time, love quietly sprouted in the heart of a young girl.

Su Yan would often turn her gaze to the seat by the right window where a boy with an innocent face sat. The boy seemed to have noticed faintly and turned around. Su Yan quickly pretended to be listening to the teacher, but her shy expression and red ears couldn't hide her feelings. However, she managed to deceive him, maybe because Su Yan and she were friends.

In the twilight after the rain, the initial glow of the sky illuminated a few playing boys. A girl stood silently on a distant pavilion, watching him quietly. His carefree laughter, his skillful basketball dribbling, his loud and clear shouts, all lingered deeply in the girl's mind. Even with her eyes closed, they wouldn't become blurry. She really wanted to tell him that she liked him. On her pink notebook, she had written and rewritten love letters many times. She imagined that on a certain evening, on a certain small road after school, she would catch up to him and give it to him, then run away. Or when he went out to play, she would secretly put it in his schoolbag. But the stubbornness of a young girl's dignity was too strong, and Su Yan ultimately failed to gather the courage to hand over the paper filled with her thoughts. She knew that as long as she saw him every day, she would be satisfied.

The girl became a landscape on the pavilion, standing in the same place every day, not knowing whether she was looking at the scenery from the building or secretly watching the people far away.

The campus, permeated with a faint floral fragrance, was filled with the laughter of a group of children, brimming with the youth of countless children.

In October, the sky remained the same, but the falling leaves in the evening carried a hint of melancholy. The quiet sky echoed with the aimless cries of geese. Beside the maple forest, the thin figure was stretched long by the setting sun. Su Yan cried silently, her tears dropping onto the maple leaves on the ground, fading away soundlessly. She knew he had transferred schools, quite suddenly. It wasn't until a few days after he stopped coming to class that she found out. Su Yan tightly held the paper full of affection and hopelessly tore it into pieces that fell with the wind-blown leaves.

Su Yan still liked standing on the pavilion, but her gaze no longer fell on the backs of playing figures. She merely gazed quietly at the distant sky.

The boy, due to his parents wanting him to study at a better school, was forced into a strange environment. There, he gradually got to know many people. Three years of time forged him into someone who understood the preciousness of friendship. He was no longer naive, no longer mischievous, no longer reckless. Puberty made him yearn for love.

One day, upon accidentally discovering an old class photo, he suddenly saw that familiar face. Beneath the neatly combed black bangs was a pure face - Su Yan.

Three years of time weren't enough to fade his memories of the past, nor would he forget that there was once a girl silently accompanying him. He remembered that there was once a girl who liked to look at him during class, shyly turning her head. She would use asking for homework help as an excuse to chat with him endlessly while still maintaining her ladylike dignity. He suddenly realized that after he left, he did care about her.

He asked his friends about her whereabouts and learned about her current school and her fondness for Annie Baby. He walked through streets and alleys until he finally bought a collection of Annie Baby's works. He specially attached what he considered his best-looking photo tightly on the blank space behind the last page cover. He sent it by mail, and to his relief, he received a reply from Su Yan. However, when he saw the few words of thanks and some irrelevant topics in the letter, a sense of suppression and sadness arose. Perhaps she blamed him for having been out of touch for three years after he left.

In the high and dim sky of October, by the desolate roadside, he looked up at the sky while his player played Xiao Wu's "Back Then We Were Young" - "If my memories of youth are a notebook, how should I write you so that I will never forget? The traces of the brush of those years still remain, can our past be erased?" The past will always become the past. He thought that perhaps the pursuits and emotions of their youth had already disappeared in the fleeting moments of time. He thought that perhaps he shouldn't disturb her. In fact, back then, when he was young, he didn't understand love. By the time he realized, fatefully, he had missed her. Strangely, he felt no sorrow over the loss.

Because he knew that even such a rebellious version of himself had once quietly resided in a young girl's heart.

He thought, let the past become the past. He knew that Su Yan would never discover the area behind the last page cover of Annie Baby's book where he had written, "I like you."

He decided to forever seal the echoes of his youthful years.
