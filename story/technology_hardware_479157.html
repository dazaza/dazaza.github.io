I. What is a slimming face surgery?

So-called slimming face surgery refers to reducing the height or thickness of certain areas on the face through surgery or medication, making the facial contour more distinct and enhancing its three-dimensional appearance. From a medical perspective, the main factors affecting appearance and facial contours are hard tissues and soft tissues. Hard tissues refer to bones, among which the jawbone, cheekbone, and frontal bone have the most noticeable impact on facial contours. Soft tissues include skin, muscles, subcutaneous fat, and various fat pads. Slimming face surgery actually adjusts the height and thickness of these two types of tissues to achieve a slimming effect. Due to individual differences, the reasons for facial thickness and width vary; some are mainly due to hard tissues, while others are mainly due to soft tissues, and some people have both. Therefore, before undergoing slimming face surgery, it is necessary to consult an experienced doctor with good aesthetic judgment for a comprehensive assessment to choose the right focus to complete the slimming face surgery, achieving satisfactory cosmetic results for both the doctor and the patient. The surgery should aim at overall beauty, choosing appropriate methods through harmonious combinations of each component to obtain a well-coordinated facial contour.

II. Different symptoms require different surgical methods!

1. Bilateral facial swelling caused by enlarged masseter muscles and mandibular angles, presenting a triangular face shape, often gives a rough impression. Mandibular angle osteotomy and partial masseter muscle resection can be used to achieve a softer and more delicate face shape.

2. Excessively high and prominent bilateral zygomatic bones (more common in Easterners) and temporal concavities leading to a diamond-shaped face, making the cheek area appear protruding and the midface overly wide, often giving an impression of ferocity or exhaustion. It is advisable to use bilateral temporal implantation of biomaterials and bilateral zygomatic reduction surgery to make the temporal area fuller and the cheek area smoother.

3. Short or recessed chin causing disproportion with the upper and midface, making the lower face feel wider. This can be treated through chin osteotomy, bone grafting, or chin augmentation using biocompatible materials to balance the proportions of the chin and face, creating an oval-shaped beautiful face.

4. For those with swollen cheeks and submental fat accumulation, liposuction is recommended to remove excess fat from the cheeks and submental area, restoring a clear contour and a three-dimensional, attractive face shape.
