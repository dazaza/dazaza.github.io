Rain Water and Health Preservation

Rain Water is the second of the 24 solar terms. The original meaning of Rain Water refers to the complete passing of winter, the arrival of spring, the warming weather, and the melting of ice and snow as all things begin to revive. At this time, there will be frequent spring rains. After the arrival of the Rain Water solar term, it does not necessarily mean that it must rain on this day, but rather indicates the end of the phenomenon of less rain in winter. From now on, precipitation will gradually increase. In the Yellow River Basin, the origin place of the 24 solar terms, the weather was cold before Rain Water, but only snowflakes were seen flying, and no sound of drizzle could be heard. In recent years, due to the warm winter effect in the north, it rarely rains at this time, while in the south, both rainy days and rainfall have significantly increased. It is truly the season of Rain Water, but cold air is also active frequently during this solar term. As the saying goes, "the biting spring chill" and "late spring cold" are most common at this time. Rain Water means that geese begin to return from the south, farmers are busy fertilizing the land, northern wheat and southern rapeseed all need the nourishment of rainwater. As the farming proverb says: "The weather gradually warms after Start of Spring, and Rain Water brings fertilizer busyness."

Diseases Associated with Rain Water

—Prone to Colds

Firstly, in early spring, the weather is alternately warm and cold, with still low temperatures and large daily temperature differences, making people with weak constitutions prone to catching a cold. Secondly, do not rush to take off winter clothes; it is important to keep warm and prevent the cold. Thirdly, exercise more, go outdoors for deep breathing, get more sunlight, and those who are prone to colds can eat Yufeng Powder (composed of Fangfeng, Huangqi, and Baizhu).

—Joint Pain

During the Rain Water solar term, initial rains may bring joint pain, especially shoulder joint and waist joint pain. Pay attention to keeping warm.

—Fatigue and Drowsiness

In spring, with rising temperatures and looser skin pores, brain blood flow relatively decreases, often causing fatigue and drowsiness, just as the poet said: "Spring sleep knows no dawn." At this time, one should go outdoors more to breathe fresh air.

Since cold air activities are frequent during this solar term, it is necessary to mention the commonly said "spring covering." So-called "spring covering" means not to hastily take off thick clothes when spring arrives. This is a health preservation principle regarding clothing proposed by ancient people based on the characteristics of climate changes in spring. In early spring, Yang energy gradually arises, and the climate becomes increasingly warm, so people gradually change from wearing cotton clothes to single-layer clothes. However, at this time, the Yin energy in the north has not completely dissipated, and cold air occasionally moves southward, resulting in large temperature fluctuations. Although the weather during the Rain Water solar term is not as cold as in mid-winter, since the human skin pores have become relatively loose, resistance to wind-cold evil will weaken, making it easy to catch wind evil and get sick. Therefore, "spring covering" makes sense, especially as the south often experiences sudden warmth followed by cold at this time.

The air during the Rain Water solar term is humid and not dry, making it a good opportunity for health preservation. Of course, the primary focus should be on regulating the spleen and stomach. Traditional Chinese medicine believes that the spleen and stomach are the "basis of life after birth" and the "source of blood and qi production," and the strength or weakness of the spleen and stomach determines an important factor in a person's longevity. Ming dynasty medical expert Zhang Jingyue proposed: "Earth qi is the source of all things, and stomach qi is the mainstay of health preservation. A strong stomach leads to strength, a weak stomach leads to weakness, having a stomach leads to life, lacking a stomach leads to death. Therefore, health preservation practitioners must prioritize the spleen and stomach." (In the relationship between the five elements and the five organs, earth corresponds to the spleen in the five elements). The Book of Illustrations on Zang-Qi Virtues points out: "Nourishing the spleen means nurturing qi, and nurturing qi is the key to health preservation." It can be seen that a healthy spleen and stomach function ensures that the human body fully utilizes nutrients and maintains health, whereas lack of nutrition leads to a decline in physical fitness.

Why does the liver wood of spring relate to spleen earth? In the application of the five-element theory in traditional Chinese medicine, the characteristics of the five elements explain the physiological functions of the five organs. The liver belongs to wood, which is flexible and straight, smooth and thriving, with the characteristic of growth. Therefore, the liver likes smoothness and dislikes depression, with the function of promoting the free flow of qi. The spleen (stomach) belongs to earth, which is thick and has the characteristic of transforming all things. The spleen also has the function of digesting food, transporting essence, and nourishing the five zang-organs, six fu-organs, limbs, and hundred bones, serving as the source of blood and qi production. The five zang-organs are physiologically interconnected and pathologically influence each other. In the transmission of the five-element relationships of mutual generation and mutual restriction, excessive wood overcomes earth, meaning that the overly vigorous liver wood suppresses the spleen earth, i.e., due to excessive promotion of the liver wood, the spleen and stomach become qi-deficient. If the liver qi is excessively depressed, then the spleen and stomach become qi-stagnant. Both are cases where liver wood overcomes spleen earth. The Nanjing refers to this as "reverse transmission," i.e., liver disease transmitting to the spleen. Therefore, in spring health preservation, it is necessary to pay attention to the characteristics of the rising yang energy in spring, assist yang energy, and avoid harming the spleen and stomach.

Traditional Chinese medicine refers to the spleen and stomach as the "sea of water and grains," with the function of generating vital energy and blood. The material basis of human bodily functions, such as nutritive and defensive qi, blood, body fluids, and essence, are all generated by the spleen and stomach. A strong and vigorous spleen and stomach ensure sufficient sources of transformation, leading to robust organ functions. The spleen and stomach are also the hub of the ascending and descending movement of qi. When the spleen and stomach coordinate, they can promote and regulate the body's metabolism, ensuring the balance and coordination of life activities. Human primordial qi is the foundation of health, and the spleen and stomach are the root of primordial qi. Famous Yuan dynasty medical expert Li Dongyuan proposed: "When the spleen and stomach are injured, primordial qi declines, and when primordial qi declines, lifespan shortens." In his Treatise on the Spleen and Stomach, he states: "True qi is also called primordial qi, which is the essence of life before birth, and without stomach qi, it cannot be nourished." He also pointed out: "Internal injuries to the spleen and stomach lead to the emergence of a hundred diseases." This shows that spleen and stomach weakness is the main cause of many diseases.

The Bencao Yanyi Zonglun says: "Those who are good at health preservation nourish their inner being, while those who are not good at health preservation nourish their outer appearance. Nourishing the outer appearance strengthens the exterior, focusing on satisfaction, pleasure, greed, and indulgence, without realizing that the exterior is full while the interior is empty. Those who are good at nourishing internally make the internal organs peaceful and harmonious, the triple burner remains in its proper position, and diet is always appropriate." Thus, it can be known that the spleen and stomach are the foundation of life and health, and medical practitioners and health preservation experts throughout history have attached great importance to the care of the spleen and stomach. Modern medical experiments have proven that regulating the spleen and stomach can effectively enhance the body's immune function and prevent aging.

Specific methods for regulating the spleen and stomach can be chosen according to individual conditions, including dietary adjustment, medicinal regulation, and regulation of rest and labor.

1. Dietary Adjustment: In spring, the climate becomes warmer, yet the winds are frequent and the environment is dry, with colder mornings and evenings. Wind evils gradually increase, often causing symptoms such as dry skin, tongue, and lips. Therefore, it is advisable to consume more fresh vegetables and juicy fruits to replenish body fluids. Since spring is the beginning of all things' growth and the season of yang energy's release, it is best to eat less greasy foods and more red dates, Huai yam, lotus seeds, leeks, spinach, citrus, honey, sugarcane, etc., to avoid assisting yang energy's external leakage. Otherwise, if the liver wood grows excessively, it will harm the spleen earth. During this solar term, porridge is suitable for northern dietary therapy, such as lotus seed porridge, Huai yam porridge, and red date porridge. In the south, especially in the Pearl River Delta region, soups are preferred for dietary therapy, such as monkey head mushroom stewed with fresh chicken soup, Yunling Huai yam stewed with lean pork soup, spinach boiled with beef slices soup, Huai yam and North Qi stewed with pig pancreas soup, etc. It is also suitable to stew soups to nourish the spleen and stomach during cold and damp weather, such as Cordyceps sinensis stewed with duck, broad beans and peanuts stewed with chicken feet, wolfberry and Huai yam stewed with pig trotter meat, etc. When using Chinese herbal medicines for regulation, consider the characteristics of the spleen and stomach functions. For example, use the method of promoting yang energy to tonify the spleen and stomach, selecting herbs like American ginseng, radix codonopsis, Cassia seeds, white chrysanthemum flowers, etc. During the Rain Water solar term, the liver yang, liver fire, and liver wind in the human body rise with the spring yang energy. Therefore, special attention should be paid to the smooth flow and release of liver qi. Nature is full of vitality, especially in the south where all things flourish. Health preservation enthusiasts should also invigorate their spirits, exude vigor, harbor aspirations in their hearts, and engage in meaningful activities.

Tang dynasty health preservation expert Sun Simiao stated in the Qianjin Fang: "During the seventy-two days of spring, reduce sour flavors and increase sweet flavors to nourish the spleen qi." In the five-element theory, the liver belongs to wood, with a sour taste, while the spleen belongs to earth, with a sweet taste. Wood overcomes earth. Therefore, in spring, one should eat less sour-tasting foods and more sweet-tasting foods to nourish the spleen qi. Foods such as leeks, Chinese toon, lily bulbs, pea shoots, coriander, shepherd's purse, bamboo shoots, yams, lotus roots, taro, radishes, water chestnuts, sugarcanes, etc., can be selected. The Qianjin Monthly Orders state: "In the first month, it is suitable to eat porridge... One kind is rehmannia porridge, which supplements the kidneys..." (fresh rehmannia 150 grams, mashed and juice reserved, washed glutinous rice 50 grams, appropriate amount of rock sugar, cooked together in a pot with an appropriate amount of water until it becomes porridge, then pour in the fresh rehmannia juice and simmer for 20 minutes until ready.) Two kinds are fangfeng porridge, used to dispel wind from the four limbs. Take one portion of fangfeng, decoct and remove the juice to cook porridge. Three kinds are perilla porridge, taking one portion of perilla, stir-fried until slightly yellow and aromatic, decoct the juice to cook porridge." Avoid eating raw cold sticky mixed foods to prevent injury to the spleen and stomach.

2. Herbal Regulation: Consider the ascending and descending transformative functions of the spleen and stomach, using the method of uplifting yang energy to tonify the spleen and stomach. Herbs such as radix codonopsis, American ginseng, cassia seeds, white chrysanthemum flowers, polygonum powder, and Bu Zhong Yi Qi Tang can be selected.

3. Mental Regulation: "All anger, sorrow, and fear injure primordial qi." Therefore, in mental regulation, one should maintain tranquility and few desires, not overwork unnecessarily, to preserve primordial qi.

4. Regulation of Rest and Labor: Maintain regular routines, balancing work and rest. That is, follow nature's rhythms, protect life's vitality, and adhere to natural changes' laws. Adjust life activity rhythms according to time, space, and seasonal climate changes to achieve the purpose of strengthening the spleen and stomach, regulating the acquired constitution, and extending life.

5. Foods to Avoid: In the first month, one should eat fewer warming foods like dog meat, mutton, sparrow meat; avoid spicy and hot foods like Sichuan pepper and fennel; refrain from eating raw onions and garlic, and it is better to boil peanuts rather than fry them.

Living, Exercise, and Dietary Practices During the Rain Water Solar Term

1. Living Habits

The weather before Rain Water is relatively cold. After Rain Water, we can clearly feel the approach of spring, with fragrant flowers and refreshing scents invigorating mind and body. During the Rain Water season, the weather changes unpredictably, being one of the times with the most cold surges throughout the year. Such unpredictable weather easily causes emotional fluctuations, even affecting mental peace, impacting physical and mental health, which is particularly detrimental to patients with hypertension, heart disease, and asthma. To eliminate these adverse factors, besides continuing to wear warm clothing, one should adopt positive mental health practices and exercises. Maintaining emotional stability plays an important role in physical and mental health. The Shao You Jing states: "Less thinking, less longing, less desire, less work, less talking, less laughing, less worrying, less joy, less happiness, less anger, less fondness, less hatred, practicing these twelve 'less,' is the key to health preservation."

In spring, the liver is vigorous while the spleen is weak, and the weakness of the spleen affects the transportation and digestion functions of the spleen and stomach. Symptoms such as depression, bloating, and abdominal pain may occur. Therefore, it is important to nourish the spleen and strengthen the stomach in spring. Nourishing the spleen also requires maintaining tranquility, focusing on mental regulation. First, stay calm and composed to prevent the liver qi from becoming excessive, keeping the spleen and stomach at peace and ensuring normal digestive functions to achieve the goal of strengthening the spleen. Second, maintain tranquility and nourish the qi, avoiding disturbance to the heart blood and depletion of heart qi, allowing the heart qi to be abundant and thereby nourishing the spleen, achieving the goal of strengthening the stomach through nourishing the spleen. In response to the changing weather in spring, it is essential to maintain a peaceful mind. Only with a balanced mood, combined with dietary regulation, can the effects of strengthening the spleen be significant.

2. Exercise - Sitting Exercises in the Middle of the Month

The Zunsheng Bajian states: "The governing element is Jueyin's primary energy, corresponding to the Sanjiao hand Shaoyang phase fire. Sitting exercises: every Zi and Chou hour, press thighs with crossed hands, twist the body to the right side, repeat three to five times, tap teeth, practice breath control and swallowing saliva. Treatment: stagnation of evil toxins in the Sanjiao meridians, throat dryness and swelling, hiccups, sore throat, deafness, sweating, sharp eye pain, cheek pain, all these symptoms can be treated."

The specific method is: perform the exercise from 11 PM to 3 AM every night. Sit naturally cross-legged, relax all muscles, clench fists with bent elbows raised level with the chest. Regulate breathing and use intention to guide qi along the hand Shaoyang Sanjiao meridian. During practice, pull the elbows back, tuck the neck and shoulders inward, slowly turn the head to the left while inhaling; after returning to center, extend the hands forward, tuck the chin, lift the ear roots, and slowly turn the head to the right while exhaling. Repeat 15 times on each side. After finishing, rinse the mouth, swallow saliva, stretch legs and kick feet, then stand up and walk naturally. These exercises have corresponding disease prevention and health benefits.

Method: Decoct prepared Polygonum multiflorum to obtain a concentrated liquid, discard the residue, and cook porridge with glutinous rice and red dates. Three nine-day periods of supplementation in winter, and no pain next year.

Author: Health Advisor

A common saying goes "winter training during the three nine-day periods," indicating it is a great time to exercise and strengthen the body. However, it is crucial to pay attention to the methods. Outdoor temperatures are too low, causing surface blood vessels to constrict, reducing blood flow speed, increasing muscle viscosity, lowering tendon elasticity, and decreasing joint flexibility, making it very easy to suffer sports injuries if engaging in intense physical activity immediately. Therefore, some preparatory activities should be done before exercising, such as jogging slowly, rubbing the face, and patting the entire body's muscles. If there is time, one can hold fists with tiger mouths connected, rotating back and forth. This increases finger flexibility, prevents frostbite, and has the added benefit of preventing colds.

During the Minor Cold period, although it is cold, yang energy is slowly accumulating and rising. Dietarily, it is a great time for nourishment. Regarding nourishment, there has been a saying since ancient times: "Supplementing during the three nine-day periods of winter will result in no pain next year." After the consumption of spring, summer, and autumn, the yin and yang qi and blood of the internal organs may become imbalanced. Reasonable nourishment can promptly replenish qi and blood fluids, resist the invasion of severe cold, and reduce the likelihood of illness the following year, achieving twice the effect with half the effort in health preservation. During winter nourishment, food and herbal supplementation should be combined, with a focus on warming nourishment. It should follow the principle of "tailoring diets to individuals," choosing nourishment based on personal conditions without blindly supplementing.

Commonly used tonics include ginseng, astragalus, donkey-hide gelatin, Cordyceps sinensis, Polygonum multiflorum, wolfberry, angelica, etc.; dietary supplements should be selected based on the balance or imbalance of yin, yang, qi, and blood, combined with the nature of foods, such as mutton, dog meat, pork, chicken, duck meat, eels, soft-shelled turtles, bream, and shrimp.
