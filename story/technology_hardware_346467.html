8 Lies a Mother Told in Her Life (Highly Recommended with Tears)! ◆ Premium ◆

by zhiyun0972 on 2010-04-16 09:48:52

1. When the little boy was young, his family was very poor. During meals, there often wasn't enough rice to go around, so the mother would give her share of the food to her children. She said, "Children, eat quickly, I'm not hungry!" — The first lie told by the mother.

2. As the boy grew taller, the hardworking mother used her Sundays off to catch fish from the river in the county outskirts to provide calcium for her children. The fish were delicious and the soup was fresh. While the children ate the fish, the mother would sit nearby gnawing on fish bones, licking the meat scraps off with her tongue. Feeling sorry for her, the boy placed some fish from his bowl into hers, asking her to eat it. But the mother refused, instead using her chopsticks to put the fish back into the boy's bowl. She said, "Child, eat quickly, I don't like fish!" — The second lie told by the mother.

3. In junior high school, in order to pay for the tuition fees of the boy and his siblings, the mother, who worked as a seamstress, would take home matchboxes from the neighborhood committee to glue together in the evenings, earning a few cents to help make ends meet. One winter night, the boy woke up in the middle of the night and saw his mother still hunched over gluing matchboxes under the oil lamp. He said, "Mother, go to sleep, you have to work tomorrow morning." Smiling, the mother replied, "Child, sleep quickly, I'm not tired!" — The third lie told by the mother.

4. During the college entrance exam year, the mother took leave and stood outside the test site every day to cheer on her son taking the exam. It was the height of summer, with the blazing sun overhead. Stubbornly, the mother stood there for hours under the scorching sun. When the exam ended and the bell rang, she greeted her son with a jar of strong tea she had prepared for him, urging him to drink it. The tea was strong, but the sentiment was even stronger. Seeing her chapped lips and sweat-drenched face, the boy handed her the jar asking her to drink. She said, "Child, drink quickly, I'm not thirsty!" — The fourth lie told by the mother.

5. After the father passed away, the mother acted as both father and mother, relying on her meager income from the sewing cooperative to raise her children, sending them to school while enduring immense hardships. Mr. Li, who repaired watches under the power pole at the alley entrance, knowing this, would come over whenever he could to lend a hand, carrying coal, fetching water, and giving money and grain to help out the boy's family. People are not plants; who can be without feelings? Neighbors noticed this and advised the mother to remarry, why should she suffer alone? But for many years, the mother remained chaste, never remarrying. No matter how much others persuaded her, she wouldn't listen. She said, "I do not love!" — The fifth lie told by the mother.

6. After the boy and his siblings graduated from university and started working, their mother, who had been laid off, set up a small stall near the farmers' market to make a living. Knowing this, the children working far away frequently sent money home to support her, but the mother firmly refused and returned the money. She said, "I have money!" — The sixth lie told by the mother.

7. After teaching at the university for two years, the boy was accepted into a prestigious American university's doctoral program, later working at an American research institution with a generous salary. With better conditions, the boy living abroad wanted to bring his mother over to enjoy a comfortable life, but she refused. She said, "I am not used to it!" — The seventh lie told by the mother.

8. In her old age, the mother fell seriously ill and was hospitalized. When the boy flew back from across the Atlantic after her surgery, his mother was already on her deathbed. Looking at his mother being tormented by illness, the boy was overwhelmed with grief, tears streaming down his face. But the mother said, "Child, don't cry, I don't feel pain." — The last lie told by the mother.
