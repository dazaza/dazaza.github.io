Ten Guesses on the Real-name System of Weibo

by edivista on 2011-12-21 11:15:50

Click to enter Sina Weibo to participate in the real-name system discussion.

Beijing has officially issued regulations stipulating that starting from December 16, 2011, Weibo users must register with their real identity information in order to use the commenting function. That is, real-name registration at the back-end and voluntary disclosure at the front-end. Users are not allowed to create fake Weibo accounts; see "Several Provisions on the Development and Management of Weibo in Beijing." This indicates that the implementation of the Weibo real-name system is underway, bringing another major change to the Weibo community, which also gives us ten conjectures!

Firstly, the Weibo real-name system will have a significant impact on Sina Weibo, the leader in the Weibo space, and it could be a fatal blow to early Weibo marketing agencies operating on Sina Weibo. The real-name system will primarily affect those plagued by zombie followers, returning Weibo to a fair platform and creating a harmonious community environment. Secondly, the Weibo real-name system sends a signal indicating the government's emphasis on online culture and Weibo usage. Implementing this measure will be very challenging, and we hope all operators take it seriously. Although the initial changes may significantly impact various interests, from a long-term perspective, the Weibo real-name system will undoubtedly promote the healthy development of Weibo culture.

Thirdly, the Weibo real-name system accelerates the pace of internet real-name systems. Initially implemented in 2003, the internet real-name system has faced numerous obstacles over the years, and as of now, no genuine real-name system has been established. The Weibo real-name system presents an excellent opportunity to promote the implementation of internet real-name systems. We hope both Weibo operators and users take it seriously, and that governments at all levels fulfill their supervisory functions to perfect this system as soon as possible.

Fourthly, the Weibo real-name system will foster the emergence of true grassroots Weibo users. Weibo is increasingly favored by internet users, especially SEO professionals. In its early stages, Weibo had many loopholes, with countless accounts requiring no real-name registration. Previously, the phenomenon of a few accounts monopolizing quality content was common, but this is likely to become a thing of the past with the implementation of the Weibo real-name system. Only when the real-name system is genuinely enforced will the Weibo community form a true network cultural membership, which is eagerly awaited.

Fifthly, the Weibo real-name system will significantly enhance the value of Weibo. Domestic Weibo platforms have developed rapidly over the past few years, accumulating tens of millions of large accounts before everyone fully understood the situation, such as Sina Weibo and Tencent Weibo. Whether celebrities or government agencies, they should support the implementation of the Weibo real-name system. Only then can Weibo, originally an unremarkable tool, be effectively utilized, achieving authority. Just as CCTV is the authoritative media on television, we hope that one day Weibo can become the most influential online media.

Sixthly, the Weibo real-name system increases the technical requirements for Weibo operation platforms. To implement the real-name system, the first step is to update and supplement the information of new and old Weibo users. This requires high standards for data protection and storage. Therefore, this technical feature must ensure complete confidentiality; otherwise, it would cast a shadow over Weibo users, inevitably leading to user traffic loss.

Seventhly, the Weibo real-name system may make it possible for Weibo to move towards a paid model. As mentioned earlier, the Weibo real-name system will restructure Weibo marketing, inevitably impacting existing Weibo users. However, this will not hinder the development direction of Weibo operators. They must first improve and optimize their technical platforms to continue attracting new and old users. This undoubtedly increases operational costs. Should these costs be passed on to users or corporate Weibo clients? In my opinion, the likelihood of Weibo charging users in the future is low, but not impossible. Initially, the most probable scenario is increasing the operational costs of corporate Weibo activities.

Eighthly, the Weibo real-name system will play a greater role in promoting the development of social public welfare. What does social public welfare currently need most and lack most? From recent scandals involving the Red Cross, it is evident that the public is disheartened by the lack of transparency in public welfare and is wavering in their support for love aid and other public welfare activities. If the Weibo real-name system can truly achieve real names, it will allow Weibo to genuinely play a supervisory role in future public welfare activities.

Ninthly, the Weibo real-name system will completely eliminate illegal and non-real profit-making online activities. Of course, this premise is that Weibo operators fully implement policies, and Weibo users voluntarily comply with relevant laws and regulations. Yes, it sounds easy but is extremely difficult to achieve, with a long road ahead.

Tenthly, what will be the next real-name system after the Weibo real-name system? This question awaits thorough research by internet friends. In previous years, real-name systems for online games and blogs were successively implemented. Today, the Weibo real-name system has just begun, and we do not know what other real-name systems might emerge in the near future. After the Weibo real-name system is truly implemented, it is likely that new online developments will arise. Because our current society is a networked society, and it will definitely be in the future!
