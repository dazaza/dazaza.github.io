I. Car Navigation System

A car navigation system refers to a vehicle road traffic information communication system, which includes a microcomputer and display screen, a synchronous satellite positioning system, radar, etc. A large amount of road-related system data is stored in the microcomputer. Radar installed on the road determines vehicle spacing and traffic volume, transmitting this information to a computer control center and then up to a communication satellite. The satellite sends the traffic signal back to the car receiver, allowing the driver to grasp traffic information from the in-car display screen. When the driver inputs the starting point, route, and destination into the computer, the computer can plan the optimal route and provide detailed information, enabling the navigation function, and even achieving automatic navigation functions (automatic acceleration or deceleration, automatic lane changes or overtaking, and automatic braking for collision prevention in emergencies). This communication system can also serve as a maintenance service system and safety warning system when a car malfunctions, as all these functions can be realized through communication satellites and ground service centers. Electronic navigation devices began being used in cars in the 1990s. Their main purpose is to make it convenient for users to reach their destinations easily and quickly.
