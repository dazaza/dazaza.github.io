**Process of Registering a Hong Kong Company**

**Conditions for Registering a Hong Kong Company**

1. Provide a new company name → Name check → Results available in 15 minutes, informing whether the name can be registered in Hong Kong.

2. Provide a copy of the shareholder's ID card or passport (at least one shareholder aged 18 or above, any nationality or corporate relationship is acceptable).

3. Registered capital (no capital verification required; if the registered capital exceeds HKD 10,000, a 0.1% stamp duty will be charged by the government, similar to mainland China's stamp duty).

- Example: For HKD 100,000, pay HKD 100 in stamp duty; for HKD 1 million, pay HKD 1,000 in stamp duty (calculated accordingly).

4. Confirm the share distribution ratio of the company's directors and chairman.

5. Fill in the usual contact address, phone number, mobile number, and email of the shareholders and directors.

- Note: If there are more than two shareholders, an additional document processing fee of HKD 200 will be charged per shareholder starting from the third shareholder.

**Selection of Hong Kong Company Name**

In Hong Kong, regardless of the size of the registered capital, according to Hong Kong's company law, companies registered in Hong Kong are allowed to include names such as Federation, Promotion Association, Foundation, Association, Society, Chamber of Commerce, Research Institute, Center, Travel Agency, Publishing House, Business Organization, Group, Enterprise, Holding, Investment, Stock, Trade, Import & Export, Culture, Film & Television, Design Institute, College, University, etc. The scope of business operations is unrestricted (except for industries controlled by the Hong Kong government).

Note: Registration of chambers of commerce, associations, foundations, or other social organizations comes in two forms: private and partnership. The former requires only one person, while the latter requires at least two people. Social organizations are non-profit institutions but must bear unlimited liability. Only residents of Hong Kong or Hong Kong Limited Companies can establish chambers of commerce, associations, and foundations. The registration process for these organizations follows Chapter 310 of the Hong Kong Government's Societies Ordinance. **Hong Kong Company Registration Process**

1. Sign the entrustment letter and agreement for registering a Hong Kong company.

2. Prepay 50% of the total fees for registering a Hong Kong company.

3. Obtain statutory forms and data from the Companies Registry and Inland Revenue Department through Midtown Business Accountants, draft the articles of association.

4. Shareholders personally sign the statutory documents and related materials for company registration. Return the statutory documents to Midtown Business Institution.

5. Midtown Business submits the statutory documents to the Hong Kong Government's Companies Registry and the Inland Revenue Department for registration.

6. Provide the above information, and we will complete the new company within 10 working days, providing the following documents:

- Company Registration Certificate: Issued by the Hong Kong Companies Registry.

- Business Registration Certificate: Issued by the Hong Kong Inland Revenue Department.

- Articles of Association: Includes the Memorandum and Articles of Association.

- Company Seals: Steel seal, atomic round seal, director signature seal (for bank check endorsement).

- Company Shares: An internal stock issuance book.

- Major Records: A major record book recording the company's shareholders, directors, and related information.

- Green Hard Box: For storing company documents.

7. Collect the full set of registration documents for the Hong Kong company and pay the remaining 50% balance.
