Homepage > Entertainment > Entertainment News: Bruce Wen, the Stunt Double of Chow Yun-fat, Visits "Man's World" to Share His Bittersweet Acting Journey

China.com BinHai New Area: www.022china.com Time: 2011-06-23 15:19:53

Tianjin TV's "Man's World" episode guest introduction: He became an overnight sensation for being the stunt double of Chow Yun-fat in the movie "Let The Bullets Fly." His imitation of Brother Fat's movements is so convincing that it could fool anyone. However, his ten-year career as an impersonator led to the breakdown of his marriage. Super imitation star of Chow Yun-fat, Bruce Wen, visits "Man's World" to recount his tough and challenging journey as an impersonator.

He slapped Chow Yun-fat twenty times - In 2009, director Jiang Wen found Bruce Wen and invited him to act in the movie "Let The Bullets Fly," serving as Chow Yun-fat's stunt double. After ten years of imitating his idol, Bruce Wen's greatest wish was to meet him. Director Jiang's invitation not only allowed Wen to meet Brother Fat but also perform alongside him, which thrilled Wen immensely. However, there was one difficult task; a scene required Wen to slap Brother Fat. Facing his idol, Wen struggled to bring himself to do it, but due to the needs of the plot, he reluctantly slapped Chow Yun-fat twenty times.

Resembling Brother Fat leads to a raise and promotion - In 1996, at just 17 years old, Wen dropped out of junior high school and moved to the city to work and pay off his father's debts. He started as a waiter in a big restaurant. Not long after he arrived, both colleagues and customers said he looked like Chow Yun-fat. Soon, the city's party secretary came to dine at the restaurant and specifically requested to meet Wen, who resembled Chow Yun-fat. From then on, Wen gained fame because of his celebrity-like face, received a hundred yuan salary increase from the boss, and was promoted to a receptionist, no longer having to wash dishes. In 2000, Wen went south to Guangzhou, eventually finding a job as a supermarket security guard. This security guard who bore a striking resemblance to Chow Yun-fat demonstrated the wit and bravery of "Little Ma Ge" inside the supermarket, becoming an expert thief-catcher! Due to the presence of this fake Brother Fat, theft cases in the supermarket quickly decreased. Consequently, Wen was highly regarded by the supermarket leadership and was promoted to the position of security supervisor.

Devoting to performing arts led to financial hardship and even suicidal thoughts - After creating a stir with his first imitation of Chow Yun-fat at the supermarket’s evening gala, Wen pursued his dream of becoming a star. He quit his job and fully committed himself to a performance career. However, breaking into the entertainment industry wasn't easy. For half a year, no one paid attention to him. During his most difficult days, Wen managed to survive on just ten yuan for a whole week, eating only one-yuan instant noodles daily. When he ran out of money, he sold household items like fans and rice cookers, and even picked up bottles on the roadside to exchange for a few bucks to eat. He lived in a two or three square meter attic, barely enough space for a bed, where his 1.8-meter tall frame had to curl up to lie down. Despite years of hard work, he didn’t achieve any success and instead faced utter failure. Deeply entrenched in despair, Wen considered giving up and even entertained thoughts of suicide.

Ten years of impersonation led to marital breakdown - After devoting himself to the performing arts, Wen often had to perform outside his hometown. Once, after driving from Urumqi to his destination, he got into a car accident. After receiving a shock, Wen called his wife. To Wen's regret, not only did his wife show indifference towards his safety, but she also scolded him for choosing such an unstable and unreliable line of work. From then on, their relationship grew increasingly strained. In 2008, Wen divorced his first wife. In May of this year, Wen married a young woman from Guangdong. His current wife strongly supports his acting career, bringing Wen immense comfort. In the program, Wen, along with Tianjin young man Zhang Liangsheng and female host Qi Miao, reenacted classic scenes from the TV series "The Bund," causing all the audience to burst into laughter. Additionally, Wen's impersonation of the gambling king performing the dice trick "One Pillar Supporting the Sky" left everyone in awe. For more exciting content, please stay tuned for this episode of "Man's World."
