Shenzhen Felix, I despise you, do you deserve my 960 yuan?

by hli313juko on 2010-04-07 23:31:13

Fei Xiang, I despise you, can you even justify the 960 yuan that I paid for? On April 19, 2009 in Shenzhen, the weather was sultry and sticky. "The Clouds of Hometown," Fei Xiang's Golden Melodies concert in Shenzhen 2009, this was an absolutely terrible concert, the worst one I've ever seen, even worse than a patchwork gig in Henan.

From the first time I heard that Fei Xiang would be holding a concert in Shenzhen, I decided to go see it. One reason was to support the songs that drifted through my youthful years; buying a ticket was the most practical way of showing support. Two, I hoped the live performance effect of the纵贯line could continue. Three, the sound effects at the Shenzhen Concert Hall are excellent, close to home, just a five-minute walk away - it would be like quietly listening to a duet between the audience and Fei Xiang.

So, without hesitation, I spent 960 yuan on two tickets at 480 yuan each. Fortunately, I went with the mindset of just listening to the music, so I didn't buy the most expensive ones at 880 yuan, otherwise, I would have been completely sickened.

This is the bouquet prepared by the audience next to me to be sent on stage for Fei Xiang. For those who aren't very familiar with the name Fei Xiang, they wouldn't know who Shanghai is. Shanghai remains in our memories as emotional songs, dynamic dance moves, and those hybrid blue eyes.

I believe that those who bought tickets tonight came here for Fei Xiang, for the fire that blazed more than twenty years ago, for the memories of youth. With Fei Xiang's fame and talent, his current fan base isn't large, but even in Jiangsu, no matter how high the ticket prices were, there was still a certain market. In Shenzhen, a place of high consumption, most of Fei Xiang's fans are people with a certain economic capability who can afford the tickets. The ticket sales were definitely good because additional seats had already been added at the scene. In the Shenzhen Concert Hall with its 1600 seats and the best audio equipment for concerts in Shenzhen, it was actually quite suitable for Fei Xiang's voice. Quietly listening to your once idol sing quietly would be so beautiful.

But, Fei Xiang presented a concert worse than those held in nightclubs. There was no quietness, no anticipation, no music, and certainly no memories... Many in the audience were elderly people, especially when I saw pairs of middle-aged couples supporting pairs of elderly people slowly walking out of the concert hall, it was truly speechless.

The entire concert was nothing more than a patchwork gig, unbearable were also the scantily dressed backup dancers and their seductive poses, the promotional flyer read: "Backup Dancers: Shanghai Ballet Troupe," OH! MY GOD!

From start to finish, the only songs Fei Xiang completed were "Reading You," "A Fire in the Winter," "Clouds of Hometown," and "Annoying Autumn Wind." There were a few medleys with beginnings but no ends, plus he sang two songs with Zhu Hua, one of which was "Phantom of the Opera" which was quite good, especially Zhu Hua's singing.

Four guest performers each sang three songs, the purple-clad host rambled on a bit, and half the time was gone. The names of the singers weren't familiar, nor were the songs they sang. No wonder some of the older, cultured audience couldn't help throwing glow sticks and mineral water bottles onto the stage, shouting loudly at their idol Fei Xiang: "Apologize, apologize, apologize... Fraud!!"

After finishing, as we walked out of the concert hall, we still saw the young man who had been singing on the road and in the rain, in the cold wind when we arrived. He persisted for two hours, giving him the 960 yuan would make me feel much better.

Regardless of whether it was the title of the concert, the advertisement, or the printed program, the audience would feel that this was Fei Xiang's personal concert, with two invited guests, one being Zhu Hua and the other Zhang Zhenyu (I mistakenly thought it was Zhang Zhenyue before, how could a famous rock star invite someone unknown as a guest?).

Walking among the audience, Fei Xiang started off with "Reading You," singing while walking among the audience, shaking hands, immediately igniting the atmosphere. Then came "Olive Tree," followed by an unknown song.

Then came a girl in a black miniskirt and garish pink stockings, singing three incomprehensible songs; then Zhu Hua, with her signature style of "The Moon Represents My Heart," a bit strange, she sang another song and then sang one with Fei Xiang; then came Zhang Zhenyu, whom I had always mistaken for Zhang Zhenyue on the promotional flyer, wondering how Shanghai, busy with the纵贯line tour, had time to guest perform. Thankfully, Shanghai sang three somewhat familiar old songs, and the audience began to sing along and clap rhythmically. Everyone probably thought this was the last guest, hoping Shanghai would finish quickly so the main character Fei Xiang could come out sooner.

At this point, the audience was still patient and polite, giving polite applause to these mixed-up guests. By now, the concert was more than halfway over, yet Fei Xiang's "A Fire in the Winter" hadn't even started.

Then, the verbose purple-clad host came out again to introduce another female singer. The audience finally couldn't hold back, protests from the 880 yuan section broke out first. The host hadn't even finished speaking when the audience kept protesting, boos kept rising, glow sticks and mineral water bottles started flying towards the stage. The shouts of "Fei Xiang, come out" echoed repeatedly. To make the somewhat mature audience in the Shenzhen Concert Hall unable to bear it, Fei Xiang was indeed quite capable. The female singer persisted through the boos and shouts of "get down" to sing two songs before hastily leaving. This unlucky singer could only blame herself for performing at such an unfortunate time and place.

Then, Fei Xiang immediately came out, accompanied by four female backup dancers in fiery red miniskirts, bare backs, and exposed stomachs. The angry audience did not calm down, continuously shouting "apologize, apologize..." especially the audience in the 880 yuan section whose protests were the loudest. No wonder, they spent the most money, mobilizing the whole family of four, costing them 3520 yuan?

A loud voice clearly shouted at Fei Xiang on stage: "Apologize, fraud!" Look at the costumes of the backup dancers, and the glow sticks left on the ground.

After all, he is an idol, Fei Xiang didn't apologize, just explained a bit, and the audience stopped making noise. "A Fire in the Winter" started playing, then a medley, lukewarm, Fei Xiang and the audience weren't really into it. During this time, Fei Xiang showed no professional ethics, talking nonsense that wasn't funny or engaging, dragging out time. It was estimated that he had negotiated with the organizer to sing a certain number of songs for a certain amount of money, if the organizer didn't pay, he would fill the time with boring idle chatter. Even under such protest from the audience, even if the organizer used Fei Xiang's name to sell tickets for publicity, Fei Xiang himself must have known about it. If he could tolerate this, he should take responsibility. In such a situation, he should at least sing a few more songs sincerely to save the situation, right? To show respect to the audience who spent so much money on tickets?

But, Fei Xiang didn't.

Then, the melody of "Clouds of Hometown" started, and the chorus began throughout the venue. Just as everyone started to get excited, he sang "Annoying Autumn Wind," finished, and the lights came on, and Fei Xiang went backstage.

The Shenzhen Concert Hall should reflect on itself, hosting such a garbage concert in Shenzhen's highest level musical temple can only mean it has fallen into the trap of greed for money. After all, this is built by the municipal government with taxpayers' money, not a commercial nightclub, karaoke bar, or bar. Shouldn't there be a reflection on the quality control of performances?
