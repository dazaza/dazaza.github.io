Sina joins hands with NBA as strategic partners to launch 24-hour Sina Sports Channel

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 10:51:17

On October 17, 2013 in Shanghai, China – Sina Corporation (NASDAQ GS: SINA), a leading online media and mobile value-added services provider, together with the National Basketball Association (NBA) of the United States, held a joint press conference to announce their strategic partnership. Sina officially became the official internet partner of NBA China and the official online community partner of NBA China. Sina becomes the first sports media platform to provide NBA viewing experiences across PC, mobile internet, and social networking platforms. At the press conference, both parties unveiled the official launch of the NBA China Official Community (nba.weibo.com) and Sina Sports Channel.

"Our strategic cooperation with Sina will give fans more direct ways to follow NBA games. They can interact with millions of fans anytime, anywhere via computers, mobile devices, and social media," said NBA Commissioner David Stern. "China has some of the most passionate fans in the world. We will continue to work with our excellent partner Sina to provide them with the most authentic NBA experience."

"As one of the most influential sports media platforms in China, we are very pleased to establish a strategic partnership once again with the globally successful professional sports league and further expand our advantageous position in the Chinese market through this cooperation," said Charles Chao, Chairman and CEO of Sina Corporation. "Sina will integrate products such as Sina.com, Sina Weibo, video, and mobile clients to jointly create a new NBA viewing experience platform and fan interaction platform that spans multiple platforms and terminals with the NBA."

Building an All-round Official NBA Community in China

As the official online community partner of NBA China, Sina will comprehensively integrate its social product resources to bring NBA fans a one-stop service including personalized information, fan interaction, video live streaming, officially authorized merchandise, online and offline activities, online games, points-based guessing games, and seamless connectivity with Sina Weibo.

According to Lu Yang, Vice Editor-in-Chief of Sina.com, the NBA China Official Community will deliver richer personalized content to users. All the content presented to users will be the core content they focus on, completely changing the traditional model of serving NBA information websites through massive amounts of content. Meanwhile, Sina will integrate its social product resources for innovative content reporting and fan interaction with the NBA's official accounts, including exclusive operation of the NBA's official Weibo account and all NBA team Weibo accounts, exclusive release of NBA team locker room videos, and exclusive development of an officially authorized NBA game, among other official rights. Through participation in unique content and services of the official NBA online community, fans have the opportunity to get closer to their favorite teams and stars.

All the information and user comments on the NBA China Official Community will be seamlessly integrated with Sina Weibo. Users just need to follow their favorite NBA team's Weibo account to obtain information and participate in discussions and interactions on Weibo. It is also understood that the mobile version of the NBA China Official Community will go online by the end of the year, and the Sina Sports app launched at the end of April will also be seamlessly integrated with the NBA China Official Community. Mobile users will be able to experience all the functions of the NBA China Official Community. In addition, the official community will provide more rich and diversified NBA content through user-generated content (UGC) and PGC (expert-generated content).

Moreover, the NBA China Official Community provides users with a large number of genuine, low-cost officially authorized goods. For example, limited edition Jordan dolls, Kobe-signed game floors, star-signed jerseys, and other important peripheral goods for hardcore NBA fans can all be found in the team mall of the NBA China Official Community. Fans can also earn community points by participating in game result guesses, real-time score guesses, and Weibo interactions, and use these points to exchange for team mall vouchers and officially authorized equipment at lower prices.

Notably, during each NBA season's off-season, the NBA China Official Community will select community fans to participate in the NBA star's China tour activities. The lucky fans selected will have the opportunity to engage in face-to-face exchanges with their favorite stars or even watch a pure NBA game on-site in the U.S.

Launching the 24-Hour Sina Sports Channel

With the start of the new NBA season, a 24-hour Sina Sports Channel integrating live broadcasts, on-demand viewing, original programs, viewing interactions, guessing games, and lottery purchases is about to meet netizens. According to reports, there are obvious differences between the Sports Channel and traditional TV stations in program settings. Traditional TV stations arrange programming schedules based on different time slots for various events, such as broadcasting NBA at 9 am and Chinese Super League at 10 am; whereas the Sports Channel fully leverages the advantages of the Internet to continuously broadcast and stream various sports events around the clock, and allows switching between different matches simultaneously, such as switching to another match during halftime of an NBA game.

In addition, in terms of program interactivity, the Sports Channel is fundamentally different from traditional TV stations. Netizens can interact in real-time with players, famous commentators, and other netizens while watching matches on Sina Sports Channel, make comments on the game, participate in guessing games to earn community points, and engage in various interactive forms such as buying lottery tickets.

Through this cooperation, the Sina Sports Channel will have over 400 live video streams of NBA games for two consecutive seasons, including exclusive video broadcasts of one selected regular-season game daily from Monday to Sunday, exclusive video broadcasts of the 2014 New Orleans All-Star Weekend, exclusive video broadcasts of all games in the Eastern and Western Conference Finals and the Finals, as well as video broadcasts of the summer league, preseason games, seven weekly first and second round playoff games.

Besides the NBA, Sina also holds over 90% of the live streaming and on-demand rights for events such as the UEFA Champions League, English Premier League, Italian Serie A, Spanish La Liga, Chinese Super League, AFC Champions League, World Cup qualifiers, German Bundesliga, French Ligue 1, and more. These events will be gradually available on the Sina Sports Channel.

Lu Yang believes that after experiencing the impact of massive information on the Internet, users' demands for the accuracy and personalization of information are getting higher and higher. Based on Sina's long-term exploration of user behavior habits and data accumulation and analysis over the past decade, the launch of the Sina Sports Channel will further cater to users' visiting habits. According to users' interest preferences, it will provide users with personalized menus to meet their content needs. Especially with a 24-hour non-stop broadcasting method, it can better explore user preferences and further satisfy users' viewing needs for different events at different times.

By integrating various sports video resources, the Sina Sports Channel will focus on several aspects such as network integration, popular commentary, brand reviews, and multi-terminal presentation to comprehensively connect video, mobile, communities, and Weibo, establishing a three-dimensional sports event aggregation platform.

About Sina

Sina is the most influential and credible Chinese-language portal site and social networking platform globally, being the most admired internet brand among Chinese mainlanders and overseas Chinese communities. With global coverage spanning North America, Hong Kong, and Taipei, Sina leads the development of China's internet and deeply influences the mainstream process of China.

Sina's current core businesses are divided into two sectors: Sina.com providing online news and content services, and Sina Weibo offering social services. In 2013, the company’s strategic focus will be "mobile-first," where both sectors will include mobile and PC-end businesses. Sina is committed to integrating network resources to provide exciting online experiences for global netizens.

About the NBA

The NBA is an international sports and media group consisting of three professional sports leagues: the National Basketball Association (NBA), Women's National Basketball Association (WNBA), and NBA Development League (NBDL). The league currently covers 215 countries and regions in 47 languages. NBA merchandise is sold in over 125,000 retail outlets across 100 countries on six continents. There are currently 85 international players from 36 countries in the NBA. The NBA's digital business includes the NBA TV channel covering over 61 million American households and the NBA official website (NBA.com) with an average of 42 million daily visits, with more than half coming from outside North America. The NBA is the most influential professional sports league on social media, with combined official accounts of the league, teams, and players having over 445 million followers across various social platforms. Through the NBA Cares initiative, the league and all teams and players have collectively donated over $225 million to charitable organizations, personally participated in over 2.6 million hours of community volunteer service, and built more than 830 living, learning, and recreational places for children and families in need.
