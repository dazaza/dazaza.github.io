Ota's nevus is a benign pigmented dermatosis that affects the sclera and the skin in the distribution area of the trigeminal nerve. It is characterized by periorbital bluish-brown macules and is essentially a condition of increased dermal melanin, often consistent with the distribution of peripheral branches of the trigeminal nerve, commonly known as a blue birthmark. Ota's nevus has no genetic predisposition and no clear relationship with malignant lesions.

Experts from the Laser Cosmetology Department of Huaxia Hospital in Jinan stated that if Ota's nevus appears on the face, it can severely affect one's appearance, sometimes covering half of the face and appearing brownish-blue, affecting both men and women at a relatively high rate. Statistics show that the vast majority of patients with Ota's nevus tend to be introverted or have low self-esteem. Laser treatment has brought good news to patients with Ota's nevus, showing significant improvement after one to three treatments, and leaving almost no marks after five treatments.

Experts from the Laser Cosmetology Department of Huaxia Hospital in Jinan explained that Ota's nevus presents as blue, brown, black, or mixed patches or spots distributed around the eye socket. Severe cases can manifest as brown, bluish-brown, or bluish-black pigmented spots on one side or both sides of the face. Ota's nevus may have a family history, with most cases presenting at birth or shortly after. Some are late-onset, developing during puberty, postpartum, or after trauma. Regardless of when it occurs, Ota's nevus significantly impacts personal appearance, prompting countless patients to seek effective treatments.

The advent of high-tech ultra-pulsed lasers in the 1990s revolutionized the treatment of Ota's nevus. This method does not damage all epidermal cells but selectively acts on melanocytes, leading to the removal of melanosomes by phagocytes. The treatment is highly selective, capable of eliminating Ota's nevus without leaving scars or causing depigmentation. The number of laser treatments for Ota's nevus varies depending on individual conditions, usually requiring only a few sessions, with an extremely high success rate, making it undoubtedly the ideal treatment method for Ota's nevus.
