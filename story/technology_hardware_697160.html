An article dated February 27, 2012, at 09:34:08, discusses an investigation by Huang Xiaohui into a 72.7 billion yuan underground money operation in Hainan Province involving capital from 29 provinces. This massive underground case was cracked in Hainan, where funds from across the country amounting to 72.7 billion yuan were transferred over two years through registered shell companies and online bank transfers. This sum involved money laundering and illegal profits of about 32 million yuan.

Hainan police recently uncovered this large-scale underground operation. The article explains how the case was detected and what loopholes it exposed. Investigators interviewed for this report from Hainan's "1·29" underground investigation team and experts on economic crime investigation detailed that the underground banks received funds from 2,337 upstream companies spread across the nation’s 29 provinces, totaling more than 72.7 billion yuan, which were then distributed to 4,473 company accounts, generating illegal profits exceeding 32 million dollars.

Liu Yue, a 42-year-old from Guangdong Puning with primary school literacy, had been engaged in illicit money transactions as a money changer in Shenzhen. In August 2008, Liu Yue extended his "business" to Hainan, registering a dummy investment company in Shenzhen, purchasing 20 IDs to register 20 shell companies in Sanya, Hainan, opening commercial bank accounts, and activating online banking services. These companies had no staff, office space, or actual business operations, serving merely as tools for Liu Yue to extort money.

The process involved receiving public funds transferred from clients into Hainan accounts, subsequently transferring these funds via online banking to specified accounts, charging a fee between 0.4% - 3.5% of the transaction turnover. This facilitated the avoidance of bank customer supervision and tax authority regulation while concealing the flow of funds for illicit purposes such as money laundering.

After screening nearly 100,000 data points and identifying over 100 suspicious accounts, investigators eventually locked onto 20 company accounts. Further investigation revealed that one of the 20 shell companies’ bank account belonged to Cao B from Hunan province, whose ID photo did not match his appearance due to identity theft after losing his ID card in Shenzhen.

Through further investigation, a person referred to as the "General" came under police scrutiny. It was found that the main perpetrator was Liu Yue. Investigators traced him and two other suspects, identifying their true identities. A suspect Xie Dongwei used fake ID photos resembling Cao B to forge new identity cards, enabling the opening of online banking accounts.

This case highlights the complexity, concealment, and difficulty in gathering evidence regarding underground financial operations, severely disrupting financial order and endangering national financial security. Experts recommend stricter penalties and improved laws to combat such crimes. Bank supervision inadequacies are also identified as key reasons for the perpetuation of these illegal activities.
