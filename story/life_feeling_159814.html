If you love too deeply, you tend to see the scars.

by mingzhuang2009 on 2009-06-12 17:08:45

Sometimes, absurd thoughts will rise from the depths of one's heart. Perhaps it is because of a literary work that was read or perhaps due to an emotional story heard from someone else. At times, one might foolishly think: everything has a limit, and going too far often leads to the opposite result. Is there also a limit to love? There is a saying, "The deeper the love, the more intense the pain." It seems that love and pain are directly proportional. Originally, love should make people feel happy and joyful, so why is it associated with pain instead? It may be because deep emotions are more likely to cause harm! Observing the infatuated men and resentful women in the world who cannot live lightly in this mundane life, it might be because they are burdened and hurt by love! Too much love makes one easily see the scars, and many love stories seem to confirm this sentence.

When reading Jin Yong's novel "The Heaven Sword and Dragon Saber," I am often moved by the love between Yin Susu and Zhang Cui Shan. One comes from a prestigious orthodox school, while the other is from the heretical Ming Sect. Not only do their origins differ, but their personalities also vary greatly—one is upright and kind-hearted, while the other is ruthless. In the martial arts world, these two individuals were originally like fire and water, incompatible. However, due to some coincidence, they had the opportunity to interact and were together captured by Xie Xun, the Golden-Haired Lion King. Initially, both harbored feelings for each other, and as they spent more time together, their affection deepened rapidly. Love soon blossomed in their hearts, becoming increasingly difficult to suppress. Xie Xun took them only to prevent them from revealing the secret of the Dragon Slaying Saber, but unexpectedly, he helped create a marital bond. However, Zhang Cui Shan did not want to be held hostage by Xie Xun and constantly sought ways to escape. Due to his limited abilities, he had to seek Yin Susu's help. On the ship, when Zhang Cui Shan engaged in an internal force battle with Xie Xun, he had previously arranged with Yin Susu to use silver needles to assist him. But at the critical moment, Yin Susu refused to act. Fortunately, Zhang Cui Shan, being a loyal and honest gentleman, thought that Yin Susu was afraid of hurting him and thus did not suspect her. Later, after their hearts were in sync, Yin Susu revealed to Zhang Cui Shan that she refrained from helping because she wanted to stay with him forever. If they escaped from Xie Xun, Zhang Cui Shan might leave her. Therefore, she could not help him; she wanted to stay with him forever, no matter where they went, as long as they didn't separate. It appears that Yin Susu's feelings for Zhang Cui Shan were too deep, and she was willing to be controlled just to stay with her beloved. She did not realize at the time that if she refused to help and something happened to Zhang Cui Shan, she might regret it endlessly. But when deeply in love, people often lose rationality. Deep emotions are more likely to leave scars, thinking only about how to fully possess the other person without considering various realities. Often, after being hurt, one realizes they were wrong, but by then, the damage is often irreparable, and the scars will remain, causing pain in the heart, precisely because the love was too deep!

The world's affairs are strange—two people who clearly love each other deeply will always end up hurting each other, making love full of scars. Perhaps because of deep love, one demands more from the other, making it easier to cause harm. In reality, even a naive child will only act cute in front of parents who deeply love them because the child knows the parents will never harm them. Two deeply loving individuals, the more they understand each other's hearts, the more they criticize their partners, and the more likely they are to cause harm.

Because of deep love, one becomes overly sensitive. Perhaps a single action or glance feels wrong, and it stirs up waves in the heart, making one feel the other does not care enough, leading to uncontrollable anger. The other party, still unclear about what happened, gets scolded without reason, naturally feeling unhappy. They think they haven't done anything wrong, yet the partner claims to love them while inexplicably lashing out, showing no tenderness. Where did the love go? Because of deep love, one wants to be spoiled by the other; because of wanting to be spoiled, one becomes demanding; because of demands, one hurts the other; because of hurting the other, one ultimately harms oneself. Deep love makes one easily see the scars, and this pain is felt in both hearts because of their deep love. Love brings joy, but it also brings pain. How can love not be intertwined with pain?

Deeply loving someone is not easy because it requires both parties to offer genuine sincerity and endure the pain of love while confronting its scars!

Every lover wants to give the deepest and truest love to their partner, assuming that love will allow them to live a heavenly life. However, few consider that love can also bring harm, pain, and scars. Love gives people more opportunities to hurt each other because their hearts are open to each other, without any guard, exposing the most vulnerable parts to each other, making mutual harm more likely.

Lovers all think that they can deeply love someone without ever harming them, but almost no one can achieve this. Love and harm are twin brothers, as love causes harm and being harmed by love. However, we need not worry that this wound will become fatal as long as true love remains. This wound will only deepen the memory of past feelings, leaving a poignant taste. If there is no real emotion involved, the wound will bleed endlessly and never heal. Love can cause harm, but love can also heal itself, and there is no need to worry. This love, marked by scars, becomes stronger after enduring trials. Just like the love between Yin Susu and Zhang Cui Shan, when Zhang Cui Shan heard Yin Susu's reasons for not helping, he was deeply moved by her profound love for him, deepening their relationship further.

Deep love makes one easily see the scars, but as long as true love remains, those scars become mementos of love. When looking back on one's emotional journey, those scars of love will shine brightly in the depths of memory, becoming precious pearls in the heart!
