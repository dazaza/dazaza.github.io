Adopt nitrogen for pressure testing and leak detection 7Q.

by jzr14gw67 on 2011-08-24 14:50:01

Fault phenomenon: The refrigerator can be started but does not cool when reused after being stored for a year.

Haier refrigerator, starts normally but does not cool.

Edited and organized by the Beijing Haier Refrigerator Repair Center.

Analysis and inspection: This fault is generally caused by a leak in the refrigeration system. Conduct separate pressure tests on the high and low-pressure sections to detect leaks. Weld a section of capillary tube at the filter outlet and seal the end with welding. Connect a three-way valve pressure gauge at the pipe opening connected to the compressor's high-pressure tube. Seal the connection between the capillary tube and the filter with gas welding, and install another three-way valve pressure gauge at the pipe opening where the capillary tube connects to the compressor's low-pressure tube. After installation, introduce 1.2Mpa ammonia gas into the high-pressure end and 0.8Mpa nitrogen gas into the low-pressure end for pressure testing - maintain pressure for 12 hours. After observation, the high-pressure pressure remains unchanged, while the low-pressure pressure drops to 0.3Mpa. Based on this, it is determined that there is a significant leakage fault in the low-pressure part of the refrigeration system. This refrigerator is a double-door, medium-temperature direct-cooling type with an internal condenser. To find and repair the leak, it is necessary to damage the cabinet and remove the insulation layer. Therefore, first remove the top panel and the upper and lower doors, invert the box body forward, back up, pry open the spot welds, and pull down the back steel plate by more than half from top to bottom. Dig out the foamed layer and discover many corrosion spots on the freezer evaporator. Use nitrogen gas for pressure testing and use clay to enclose suspected points into concave shapes. Charge nitrogen gas through the low-pressure three-way valve until the pressure reaches 0.8Mpa, then inject soapy water into the clay concave area. It is found that bubbles overflow from three places on the back of the freezer evaporator, indicating that these three places are leaking. Wash and dry the areas around the leaks, remove the oxide layer with sandpaper, clean with acetone, and repair each leak point with JC-311 dual-tube glue. Then fill with 0.7Mpa nitrogen gas for 24 hours of pressure testing, ensuring stable pressure, which indicates that the leak points have been repaired.

The method for filling the foaming agent is: Before filling, clean the surface of the refrigeration system pipes with ethanol. Mix two liquids A and B in a container for foaming, heat them to 40℃, and quickly pour them into the area requiring foaming to refill the excavated part. After reshaping the back panel of the box, apply adhesive to the new insulation layer. Then flush the entire refrigeration system with nitrogen gas, perform evacuation treatment, charge the specified amount of refrigerant according to regulations, test the machine, and ensure normal cooling operation of the refrigerator, thereby resolving the fault.
