An anal fistula is an embarrassing condition for many people, bringing not only physical pain but also inconvenience in daily life. So, what should be done if you have an anal fistula? Experts from the Yangzhou Armed Police Hospital's Colorectal Center point out that self-care for patients with anal fistulas is particularly important. It can not only reduce local adverse environmental stimuli but also provide a good foundation for treatment and recovery.

1. Establish normal dietary habits because the occurrence of anal fistulas is related to damp heat. For greasy diets, internal damp heat can be generated, so such foods should not be consumed excessively. One should eat more light foods rich in vitamins, such as mung beans, radishes, winter melons, and other fresh vegetables and fruits. For long-term unhealed anal fistulas which are often caused by weak constitutions, one should consume more protein-rich foods like lean meat, beef, and mushrooms.

2. Timely treat anal sinusitis and anal papillitis to prevent perirectal abscesses and anal fistulas.

3. If there is a burning sensation or heaviness in the anus, it is important to promptly determine the cause and receive timely treatment.

4. Preventing and treating constipation and diarrhea is significant in preventing perirectal abscesses, as hard stools can easily injure the anal sinuses and lead to infection when bacteria invade. Diarrhea often involves rectitis and anal sinusitis, which can further develop the inflammation.

5. Actively treat systemic diseases that may cause perirectal abscesses, such as ulcerative colitis and Crohn's disease.

6. Develop good bowel habits. After daily bowel movements, sitz baths can help maintain anal hygiene and play a positive role in preventing infections.
