Wang Chuan of Xiaomi Box responds to the accusation from Tongzhou Electronics: Chairman Yuan Ming is being sentimental.

by anonymous on 2013-10-22 19:22:40

October 17 - In response to the accusation by Tongzhou Electronics that Xiaomi Box was suspected of copying the control method of set-top boxes, Wang Chuan, CEO of Xiaomi Duokan and the overall supervisor of Xiaomi Box, made a rebuttal on Weibo, stating that if Xiaomi products were to reference Tongzhou's design, it would be starting from a low point, and the other party was "self-deluded."

Yesterday evening, Tongzhou Electronics posted on Weibo claiming that Xiaomi Box was accused of copying Tongzhou Electronics' related set-top box control methods. Tongzhou Electronics believed that when operating Xiaomi Box with a remote control (or phone), the method of using the remote control menu button to call up more user-related items was suspected of infringing on the patent application in 2007 for "Set-top Box and Set-top Box Remote Controller Operating System and Method" (Patent No. ZL 200710073318.2).

Tongzhou Electronics announced that whether this alleged copying constituted patent infringement was under legal evaluation, and if confirmed, formal litigation would be initiated.

This morning, Wang Chuan responded to the matter on Weibo with a touch of sarcasm: "This time, I was really shocked. Chairman Yuan Ming is too self-deluded! Does Xiaomi have to copy our Tongzhou design? Isn't that a bit low-level? When I first created Xiaomi Box, I just wanted to make an easy-to-use and practical little product for users. I didn't expect it to become everyone's target, where everyone wants a piece of the pie."

Xiaomi Box has recently been embroiled in disputes. Prior to this, Youku Tudou claimed that Xiaomi had broadcasted TV series such as "Beijing Youth," "Love Apartment 3," and "Chu Han Legend" on its Xiaomi Box without authorization, infringing on the content rights purchased by Youku Tudou. They filed a lawsuit with the court, demanding that Xiaomi immediately cease the infringing behavior, issue a public apology, and compensate RMB 5.1 million. Xiaomi's official response stated that the video content was managed by CNTV, and thus Xiaomi did not engage in any infringing behavior.
