iPhone 4 Is Apple's Secret Weapon in Emerging Market Push

by anonymous on 2013-07-29 17:33:47

July 25, Beijing Time - According to foreign media reports, Apple has long been renowned in the tech industry for its innovation, consistently attracting large numbers of users with its latest and most powerful technologies. However, for certain markets, Apple's most potent secret weapon was actually unveiled three years ago.

This secret weapon is the iPhone 4, released in 2010, which will assist Apple in boosting its sales in price-sensitive markets like India, countering the fierce competition posed by a flood of Android smartphones from competitors.

The Charm of Older iPhones

Apple on Tuesday reported its latest quarterly earnings, surprising investors: iPhone shipments in the third quarter reached 31.2 million units, an increase of 20% year-on-year, exceeding market analysts' projected sales target of 26.5 million units by 18%. A significant factor behind this remarkable performance can be attributed to the iPhone 4, which has been available for three years.

Although Apple claims that the iPhone 5 remains its best-selling product, it also added that demand for the iPhone 4 was one of the key reasons driving the growth in smartphone sales.

Selling older versions of the iPhone to emerging markets isn't a new strategy for Apple. Doing so might also bring about some side effects, as cheaper iPhones could lower Apple's overall gross margin.

Despite positioning itself as a high-end smartphone manufacturer, Apple plans to adopt more proactive tactics to sell older versions of the iPhone. There's rapidly growing demand for these older models in emerging markets, where many consumers have never purchased a smartphone before.

Apple CEO Tim Cook stated that the iPhone 4 attracts a large number of new smartphone users. During the earnings call after the report release, he said, "We want to attract as many new smartphone users as possible."

The role of the iPhone 4 in increasing revenue is crucial for Apple since it currently lacks new products to attract new smartphone users.

Cheaper Version of the iPhone

According to informed sources, Apple has been developing a cheaper version of the iPhone, which is likely to be released later this year. Unlike the iPhone 4 and 4S, the screen size of the cheaper iPhone will match that of the iPhone 5, but it won't feature a metal casing.

Prior to Apple's statement, its spokesperson declined to comment on the matter.

Currently, the iPhone 4 is Apple's cheapest iPhone product; U.S. consumers can even get it for free if they purchase a two-year service contract. In comparison, the starting price for the iPhone 5 is $199. Without carrier subsidies, the iPhone 4 costs $450, the starting price for the iPhone 4S is $549, and the starting price for the iPhone 5 is $649.

Due to the shift in product sales towards cheaper models, the average selling price of Apple's iPhone in the third quarter further dropped to $581, reducing by $32 compared to the second quarter.

However, Apple doesn't seem worried about this issue. In India, Apple adopted a very aggressive pricing and marketing strategy to promote the iPhone 4. According to market research firm Cybermedia Research, Apple sold 205,000 iPhones in India during the third quarter. This sales figure is approximately triple what it was a year ago when only 72,000 iPhones were sold in India; furthermore, iPhone sales in India increased by 41% compared to the previous quarter.

Cook told investors, "India's growth rate exceeded 400%." However, he did not disclose specific financial data.

In contrast, iPhone sales in the U.S. and Japan grew by 51% and 66%, respectively.

In April, Apple launched a "trade-in" promotional event in India. Consumers purchasing a standard iPhone 4 could receive a discount regardless of whether they traded in an iPhone, Android smartphone, or BlackBerry device. The minimum trade-in value was 7,000 rupees (approximately $117).

Apple recently expanded this promotional offer, allowing consumers to use their old smartphones for discounts when upgrading the storage capacity of the iPhone 4.

Unlimited Business Opportunities in China

Apple also expressed its intention to enhance the appeal of the iPhone 4 in the Chinese market, where the sales pace of iPhones has recently slowed down. Cook said, "I still believe that today, China represents tremendous business opportunities for Apple." He added that he wasn't disappointed with the performance of the Chinese market in the third quarter.

However, IDC analyst TZ Wong indicated that Apple would leverage its sales partners to promote older versions of the iPhone. A merchant at a computer city in Beijing's commercial district mentioned that 30% of his smartphone sales still come from the iPhone 4 and 4S, while 50% are from the iPhone 5, and the rest are contributed by Samsung Electronics products.

The merchant stated, "Older versions of the iPhone still sell very well because their current prices are only half of what they used to be. Many customers buy the iPhone 5 as gifts for others, but for personal use, they opt for the iPhone 4 or 4S."
