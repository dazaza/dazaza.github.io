Synopsis:

James Barrie (played by Johnny Depp) is a writer living in London whose repetitive daily life leaves him lacking inspiration. Every day he passes Kensington Gardens twice and each time he sees a group of children tirelessly playing on the grass. One day, a scene in the park catches his attention, making him pause to observe what unfolds.

The children have transformed Kensington Gardens into their pirate game base camp. They build a small house with branches and mold dirt into pastries as offerings to the pirates. The boys tie wide red cloth around their waists, insert their proud swords, don pirate headscarves, and elect a captain for their pirate ship. Barrie watches as the children play earnestly and upon noticing him, they warmly invite him to join their pirate crew. Thus, the Kensington Gardens Pirate Squad gains another "one-eyed sea pirate." Barrie plays games, pranks, and pretends alongside the children, entering worlds of castles, kings, cowboys and Indians, pirates, and adventures. It's Barrie who turns a small hill into a sailboat, whittles sticks into swords, paints kites into fairies, and dresses up four little boys as the "Lost Boys of Fantasy Island."

Through his interactions with these innocent children, their rich imaginations inspire Barrie, opening the door to his own creativity. Not only does he recruit the children's mother (played by Kate Winslet) to join the pirate squad, but he also draws inspiration from them to create the now world-famous character—Peter Pan. Initially, Barrie’s theater troupe doubts the appeal of this fairy tale, even his long-time supporter Charles (Dustin Hoffman) expresses concerns. However, through Barrie's relentless efforts, "Peter Pan" ultimately captivates countless children and adults alike with its childlike innocence and imagination.

Nevertheless, just as James Barrie is ready to introduce "Peter Pan" to the world, an unexpected blow makes everyone acutely aware of the harshness of reality...
