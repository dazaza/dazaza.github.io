Google plans to attract Android developers by holding seminars.

by tuoqing1188 on 2011-08-04 09:58:36

Sina Technology News, early morning of August 4 Beijing Time - Google has started to roll out a series of workshops since Tuesday, hoping to attract software developers who can design high-quality Android applications.

According to an Android developer blog, the workshop series will be organized by Google's Android Developer Relations team, and the Android Developer Labs (ADLs) will guide developers on how to load applications onto Android tablets during these workshops.

The main topic of the workshop is "how to launch applications on tablets running Google's Honeycomb system." ADLs will introduce to developers how to develop applications in full-screen mode, the user interface of Android tablets, and how to integrate applications from mobile phones onto Android tablets.

However, these workshops are only aimed at experienced developers, especially those who have already published applications on Google's Android Market.

This blog post points out: "The ultimate goal of this series of activities is for you to be able to hand over your application to the ADLs, allowing your product to bring joy to Android tablet users."

The first round of workshops was held this Tuesday in Bangalore, India. The next two workshops will be held on August 15th and 16th in Seattle. Following that, two more workshops will take place on August 23rd and 24th in New York. The final round of workshops will be held on September 2nd in Los Angeles.

According to Google's introduction, any developer with relevant background can register. However, only a small portion of registrants will be selected to participate in the workshop, based on the quality of the Android applications they have developed.

Without doubt, one of Google's many objectives with this move is to spark interest among developers for Android, thereby gradually driving market demand for Android tablets. Nevertheless, attracting more users remains challenging. According to research findings from mobile device research firm Flurry, mobile application developers are increasingly aligning themselves with Apple, with the appeal of iPad and iOS far surpassing Android.

Despite a flood of various brands of Android tablets entering the market this year, Apple's iPad continues to firmly control the majority share of the market. However, CEOs of several companies including Dell and Nvidia have stated that they believe the sales performance of Android tablets will eventually surpass the iPad. Reports have already surfaced indicating that as different brands of Android tablets push collectively in the market, iPad users have begun to decline somewhat.

Yet most analysts still believe that Google and its tablet partners face significant challenges in fundamentally shaking up Apple's sales performance. Analyst Wayne Lam from market research company HIS iSuppli said on Monday that Google has little chance in the short term to surpass the iPad and iOS. Lam believes the key reason why Android manufacturers currently lack the capability to design products like the iPad is that Apple simultaneously controls the production of both the operating system and hardware devices.
