According to Information Times (reported by Yan Xiaoguang), Mai Qunying and Chen Weiwen, individual shareholders of Zhongshan Jinye Trading Co., Ltd., filed a lawsuit against the Zhongshan municipal government because they were dissatisfied with their shareholder identities being forcibly revoked by a government order. The Guangdong Higher People's Court held the second trial yesterday.

Mai Qunying and Chen Weiwen used to be employees of Zhongshan Jinye Trading Co., Ltd. and also individual shareholders. They obtained shares in Jinye Trading due to corporate restructuring several years ago.

Mai Qunying and Chen Weiwen used to be employees of Zhongshan Jinye Trading Co., Ltd. and also individual shareholders, holding 0.89% of the shares each. Mai and Chen believed that the actions of the Zhongshan municipal government seriously infringed upon their legitimate rights and interests, demanding that the court revoke Document No. 50 issued by the Zhongshan municipal government, restore their individual shareholder status, and change the industrial and commercial registration.

According to the complaint: On February 26, 2008, Jinye Company notified Mai Qunying and Chen Weiwen that the Zhongshan Municipal Administration for Industry and Commerce had canceled their personal shareholder status based on Document No. 50 [2008] issued by the Zhongshan municipal government office. After applying for information disclosure, Chen and Mai viewed this document at the Office Secretary Department of the Zhongshan municipal government. The document stated that "Zhongshan Jinye Trading Co., Ltd., because it holds equity in Guangdong Tobacco Zhongshan Co., Ltd., is an affiliate enterprise of the tobacco industry. According to relevant regulations, its assets must be 100% state-owned... agree to administratively clear the equity held by 45 individual shareholders in Zhongshan Jinye Trading Co., Ltd."

According to Mai Qunying and Chen Weiwen's lawsuit, in 2001, after approval by the Zhongshan Public Asset Management Committee Office, Jinye Company underwent corporate restructuring, with city-level shares accounting for 65%, and internal employees holding 35% of the shares, receiving dividends.

Mai Qunying and Chen Weiwen appealed, stating, "The government initially forced us to buy the shares, and now they take them away without compensation, how can this be reasonable?" The agent of the Zhongshan municipal government expressed agreement with the first-instance judgment. Under the court's mediation, all parties involved in the litigation expressed willingness to mediate.

The first trial at Zhongshan Intermediate People's Court believed that regarding the issue of staff and workers participating in shareholding in the tobacco industry, both the State Tobacco Monopoly Administration and the Guangdong Provincial Tobacco Monopoly Administration issued regulatory documents successively, requiring the cleanup of individuals' participation in shareholding in enterprises associated with the tobacco industry, and the refund of personal equity. As an administrative agency responsible for leading the tobacco monopoly work in Zhongshan, the Zhongshan municipal government issuing "Document No. 50" to administratively clear the equity held by 45 individual shareholders including the plaintiffs in Jinye Company was an act of exercising authority according to law. Based on this, the court rejected the plaintiff's litigation request.
