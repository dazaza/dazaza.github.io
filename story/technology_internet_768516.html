Taobao Villages Go Wild on Singles' Day: Selling as Much in Half a Day as They Normally Do in Three or Four

by anonymous on 2013-11-17 19:06:00

According to reports from Xinhua News Agency, receiving orders, packing, sticking labels, and sending express deliveries... Since the early hours of November 11, villagers in Wantou Village, Boxing County, Shandong Province, have been busy without rest. For this specialized "Taobao Village," the "Double Eleven" is like a collective carnival festival.

On November 11, reporters saw in Wantou Village that on a street less than 100 meters long, both sides were filled with straw willow woven products ready to be loaded onto trucks and shipped across the country. Villagers operating straw willow weaving businesses along the street frequently took stock out of their warehouses and handed them over to villagers responsible for shipping the packages. The staff of courier companies almost ran back and forth to collect and load parcels.

Wantou Village is called a "Taobao Village" because it has more than 500 professional Taobao stores. The craft of straw willow weaving has been passed down through generations here for over 600 years. In Wantou Village and its surrounding areas, more than 1700 farming households specialize in producing and selling straw willow woven goods. The number of people employed in the straw willow weaving industry exceeds 10,000. There are more than 20 express delivery companies that have long-term operations in the village.

From midnight to 4 PM on that day, An Baokang, a villager from Wantou who operates a straw willow woven product store on Taobao, hardly slept. Looking exhausted, he watched the order notifications flashing on his computer screen while filling out customer addresses, then packed the items together with his father to hand over to the courier company.

"Today was really tiring, but it was worth it. I started preparing inventory 10 days in advance. My online store has over 100 varieties of straw willow woven products, and I prepared 20 pieces of each type. In just half a day, I sold as much as I usually do in three or four days, and my sales increased two or three times compared to normal days," said An Baokang.

Jia Peixiao, one of the larger-scale online store owners in Wantou Village who operates shops on Tmall Mall and Taobao, had his front yard and backyard filled with various straw willow woven products. Some places were so crowded that you had to tiptoe to walk past. On one wall in the living room, there was a line of words written: "Dear, let's go all out in 2013! Let's aim for 10 million yuan!"

Jia Peixiao said: "Normally, our daily sales are less than 30,000 yuan, but by noon today, they had already reached nearly 70,000 yuan. The promotional effect of 'Double Eleven' is very obvious. It is expected that achieving an annual sales volume of 8 million yuan should be no problem."

The reporter noticed that in Wantou Village, an operational model of "online store + family workshop" has been formed. Besides engaging in the processing and sales of straw willow woven products themselves, online store owners also involve neighboring farmers in the production process. An Baokang said: "Based on the latest trends online, we place orders with other families, then purchase the finished products back for online sales. Different families have clear divisions of labor, and the rhythm is also very fast."

"The 'Double Eleven' not only brings joy to online shoppers across the country but also is a festival worth celebrating for our village," An Baokang said.
