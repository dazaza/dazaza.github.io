What causes hypochondriasis? Hypochondriasis patients often exhibit personality traits such as over-concern for their own health, perfectionism, or being stubborn, stingy, cautious, etc., before the onset of the illness. Causes of hypochondriasis:

1. Psychosocial factors.

Such as changes in marriage, departure of children, reduced social interactions, loneliness, disruption of life stability, lack of security, can all become triggers for the illness. Some patients are iatrogenic, caused by inappropriate words, attitudes, or behaviors from doctors leading to excessive suspicion in patients, or uncertain diagnoses made by doctors repeatedly asking patients to undergo various tests, which leads patients to believe they have a certain disease. Some patients develop hypochondriasis after physical illnesses through self-suggestion or association.

2. Constitutional factors.
