Grassroots Entrepreneurship: Three Years of Hard Work, Fragrance from the Cold

Another deep winter night. The north wind howls outside the window.

As I sit under dim light, writing these words to reminisce about the past, I can't help but feel a myriad of emotions. Three years ago, on a similar night, in Zhangjiang, lying on a makeshift bed, I came up with the idea for an online ledger. Three years later, Caakee Online has grown into a robust and thriving tree!

### Chapter One: Winter - Recalling the Beginning

To be honest, when I first started Caakee Online, I didn’t imagine it would become a lifelong career. Although in 2004, Google introduced the concept of online applications, it wasn't until 2006 that this concept truly gained attention in China.

The decision to start Caakee Online was based on two simple reasons:

1. I firmly believed that online applications were the future trend of software development. I believed that one day, most software would be able to run on the internet.

2. With the great enrichment of material life, people's financial awareness would grow stronger. Financial management mainly involves two aspects: increasing income and reducing expenses. To manage finances, one must first record them.

However, when I proposed this idea to those around me, everyone opposed it.

"Putting accounts online? That’s a joke!" This was the reaction of most people.

Nevertheless, this reaction was within my expectations. Because the birth of any new thing is initially not understood by the majority. Just as before, people didn’t believe in online shopping, but now they do. Before, they didn’t trust online banking, but now they do.

What about online bookkeeping now?

### Chapter Two: Autumn - Encountering Difficulties

Caakee Online went online at the beginning of 2006, but by the end of 2006, there were still fewer than a hundred users. On one hand, promotion efforts were insufficient. More importantly, people did not yet accept online bookkeeping as a viable concept. By 2007, the situation slowly improved. With the continuous efforts of big companies like Google, people began to gradually accept the new mode of using software online. Also, by mid-2007, several domestic websites of the same type had appeared. It was precisely the emergence of these competitors that slowly heated up the market.

However, it seems that the growth of any new thing always comes with twists and thorns.

Just as people began to accept online bookkeeping, Ynnny.com, a well-developed domestic bookkeeping website, closed down without any notice or explanation.

Ynnny's closure poured cold water on the trust in online bookkeeping that had just been established. Many media outlets reported on this incident and raised significant doubts about the security of online bookkeeping.

In early 2008, another well-known bookkeeping website, Jizhangla.com, suddenly announced its closure due to server crashes and difficulties sustaining personal salaries. Subsequently, rumors spread that Caakee Online had attacked their servers.

Unfortunately, the setbacks continued.

In September 2008, another relatively large-scale bookkeeping website, JuBao.com, announced its closure due to the withdrawal of investment.

On January 11, 2009, Jizhangla.com, which had resumed service after receiving funding, once again announced its closure. This time, it was even worse because of disputes between investors and developers, leading to the physical removal of the server and loss of user data. As a result, many uninformed members mistakenly accused Caakee Online, assuming it had also closed down.

Unfortunate incidents also occurred at Caakee Online.

In early 2008, Caakee Online suffered an attack, damaging two weeks of member diaries. The technical team worked all night and restored all damaged data by 10 o'clock the next morning.

In September 2008, Caakee Online was hit by a malicious attack of unknown origin. While actively gathering evidence and pinpointing the perpetrator to the Qingpu area of Shanghai, relevant evidence was handed over to the police.

In November 2008, a very well-known financial software provider (very well-known, one of the top companies in the financial software industry, name withheld here) inexplicably attacked Caakee Online, causing nearly half a day of inaccessible service. Fortunately, Caakee Online handled the situation promptly.

...

Winter was drawing to a close, but the true spring seemed far away.

### Chapter Three: Spring - Breaking Through Difficulties

With such a challenging environment, Caakee Online was struggling step by step. The successive closures of competitors gave us a huge shock!

How to establish user trust in online bookkeeping and how to create a stable and professional brand image became urgent issues. Pondering... deeply pondering...

To eliminate user concerns about security, there are two key points:

1. Solving the issue of team stability.

2. Finding a sustainable development path, in other words, solving the profitability problem.

Currently, most existing online bookkeeping sites are run part-time by individuals, lacking both time and financial guarantees. When work is not busy, they can keep up, but when work gets hectic, especially if there are major changes in life, it inevitably affects the site. Inevitably, the site will have to close down.

Only through corporatization and formal operation can we remove user suspicions. Realizing this, coincidentally, Alibaba approached Caakee Online in early 2008 with a cooperation proposal. We took the opportunity to register a company, form a team, and embarked on the path of standardized operations.

Solving the team problem alone cannot completely solve the issue. For online ledgers to break out of difficulties, fundamentally, they need to address the profitability problem.

However, in the context of Web 2.0 websites generally burning money, how could Caakee Online make a profit? It was incredibly difficult. Fortunately, through the joint efforts of Caakee Online team members and our wide range of users, Caakee Online successfully resolved this issue through revenue sharing with advertisers. From then on, Caakee Online moved forward lightly, embarking on a fast track of development. Thousands of daily registrations, Alexa ranking soaring to over 40,000 positions. Thanks to Caakee Online's good corporate image, dozens of media outlets widely covered it. This was the best recognition of Caakee Online.

### Chapter Four: Summer - Devotion

During the summer, sipping tea.

I said to Xiaotian: "Devote yourself, users can feel it."

Caakee Online started almost from scratch, lacking everything. So what does Caakee Online's development rely on? Timing and location are hard to come by. Only 'people harmony' can be relied upon.

Devote yourself to listening to customers' voices.

We opened every possible way to communicate with customers: phone, MSN, QQ, email, even mobile phones...

We provide almost 24-hour service. At 2 a.m., customers still call asking about bookkeeping issues.

Forum questions are basically answered on the same day, at most by the next day.

The first function of Caakee Online was called: Export Data, followed by unique features like sub-user functionality and multi-ledger functionality on the market, and powerful online bookkeeping capabilities, truly recording 99% of accounts, achieving statistical analysis of the three major financial statements, etc.

I smile here, but I think you can feel it.

"Vast rivers embrace all waters, tolerance makes greatness."

Competitors' members come to us and say we're not easy to use. We patiently guide them, even recommending other websites to them.

Because we know our positioning.

"Caakee Online is really hard to use," we admit.

"But Caakee Online is really useful."

We have this confidence. In Caakee Online, you can record almost all accounts.

### Chapter Five: Conclusion

One Chinese person is a dragon, two Chinese people are worms. The lack of cooperation among Chinese people is evident here.

Several bookkeeping websites have failed due to team splits, affecting the development of the websites. For bookkeeping websites to develop, having a united and stable team is crucial.

Team stability cannot exist without effective communication. Therefore, from the beginning, I have paid great attention to emphasizing communication issues.

Rather than arguing, it's better not to harbor ill intentions.

Ill intentions give birth to monsters.

Fortunately, Caakee Online's team is united, positive, upward-looking, and pragmatic.

We are a group of young people, full of passion and determination to achieve something, coming together.

We will strive, for everyone, and for ourselves.
