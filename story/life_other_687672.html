Letting Go of Sadness

Online Submission

September 25th is a day I should remember because on this day, I got divorced.

When we left the office, we both acted so nonchalantly, neither of us looking back. From this moment on, you and I have become strangers. I let out a sigh of relief, finally putting an end to this so-called bond. Though it wasn't a perfect ending, I had said that I wouldn't cry. But in the last moment, tears still fell from my eyes. It wasn't for you, nor was it for the marriage; it was for myself. Perhaps it could be considered burying a part of myself—burying the past and starting anew.

More than ten years of emotion ended in just over ten minutes. The marriage certificate was exchanged for a divorce certificate. After more than a decade, I can finally let out a sigh of relief, albeit with some sadness.

When I saw our child again, I secretly shed tears, but I quickly wiped them away. What's the big deal? Haven't I always been strong? Life goes on as usual. I will continue to raise my son happily as always. Leaving seemed to have little effect on me.

Perhaps the child sensed something. In the morning, he hugged me tightly, kissing my forehead with his little mouth saying, "Mommy, I will always be with you." He knew mommy wouldn't abandon him.

The child smiled at me brightly and said, "Mommy, I will be very well-behaved."

I replied with a radiant smile, "Of course, Mommy's treasure is the best."

Afterwards, I was very brave. That afternoon, I went to work as if nothing happened. All the way until after work, I didn't cry. When it got dark, sitting in the car, looking out the pitch-black window, I cried again. I believe that even though night falls, it will always brighten again. After returning home, for the first time, I aimlessly wandered on the road for a long time, feeling much more comfortable inside.

Today, the friend who should have consoled me didn't come, but a stranger listened to my story completely.

Autumn, a season of many events. We met in autumn, began in autumn, welcomed the arrival of our child in autumn, and ended in autumn. Alas! The autumn of life! Autumn is a season full of incidents. Is this considered a harvest or merely life experiences? Is it misfortune in life or a wealth of one's own? Does going through all this make oneself stronger continuously...
