Romance is a rather lonely affair.

by haiktaodah on 2011-09-19 20:08:20

If we set aside the appearances and only talk about the essence, love and cigarettes are fundamentally the same in many ways. For instance, both require a spark to ignite. The spark of love occurs between attracted opposite sexes; scientifically speaking, it's the electromagnetic reaction produced by two opposing yet interactive magnetic fields at a certain moment, which stimulates hormone secretion and ultimately leads to love between or within individuals. On the other hand, the spark for a cigarette is created by a match or a lighter, belonging to the realm of science as well, but in the form of a chemical reaction.

The arrival of love, like buying cigarettes, is unpredictable. When you buy a pack of cigarettes from a street vendor or a glittering mall, you don't know which one will be your love, or even if there will be any love at all. Similarly, when you walk on the street or daydream during a warm afternoon, you also don't know whether you'll light up a cigarette. The arrival of love has no warning signs. Without any premeditation, you encounter love, or you light up a cigarette.

Similarly, both love and cigarettes have a burning process, the duration of which depends on an individual’s fortune and mindset. The best way to smoke is to inhale and exhale slowly, savoring it carefully. The best approach to love is to let it flow naturally, without forcing it. We all understand that everything with a beginning must have an end. Cigarettes are like this, and so is love. The demise of love is like ashes, eventually falling into dust, leaving no trace. By then, what binds two people together may just be habit and emotion, or even duty and responsibility, but it will have nothing to do with love anymore.

Therefore, Bai Suzhen, the white snake of West Lake, transformed herself after a thousand years of cultivation into a woman named Bai Suzhen to achieve her only love in endless time. This loyalty is admirable, yet pitiful. Because Xuan is merely an ordinary person who will grow old eventually. Bai Suzhen exchanged a thousand years of practice for just a brief burn, and since then, the quiet lake bottom, green water and clear sky, misses every night. Love does not last forever. Hence, the legend of the White Snake stops at the fall of the Leifeng Pagoda, which is people's remembrance and pity for the White Snake, letting her stay forever in the joy of having both love and affection.

So, let love remain lonely, quietly waiting for a spark to strike in a secluded corner. In the moment of burning, it is like the cigarette between your fingers.

Love and cigarettes are equally short-lived. Their only difference lies in the fact that you can smoke one cigarette after another, but you cannot experience love repeatedly. Even if you have attained immortality and can live for thousands of years, the true love that truly belongs to you will only happen once, the only one time you burn and then perish. Of course, love will not leave the world, but it will leave you and return to its eternal loneliness, waiting for someone else's next combustion.

In fact, loving someone is really a very lonely matter. No one likes endless loneliness, so deep down, we still prefer such an ending. Although fleeting love always brings boundless longing, this brevity is more in line with human nature and needs. We cannot imagine a cigarette that never ends, just as we cannot achieve a love that remains passionate forever. Not only is it unimaginable and against common sense, but it would also be unbearable.

Love is a rather lonely thing, like the cigarette between your fingers, except for the brief moment of burning. Its origin is an obscure corner. Waiting for a finger to pick it up, and its destination is the vast emptiness of turning to ashes, like the disappearance of life. At the moment of passing away, it remains lonely.

The Buddha smiles, not because there is no pain; the Buddha is benevolent, not because he can forget suffering. The Buddha has past causes, so there is love in his heart; the Buddha has no future lives, so his love is profound. Therefore, he cannot forget, therefore he suffers, therefore the Buddha smiles.
