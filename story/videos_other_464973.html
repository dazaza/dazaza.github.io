Value Scale Drives Changhong's Profound Transformation 76

by ufg81qt25 on 2011-07-23 11:29:41

Changhong Group has recently announced that it will focus on intelligent transformation and rely on three major transformation strategies: towards high-end manufacturing, services, and internationalization. It aims to achieve sales revenue exceeding 100 billion yuan and double its net assets to 40 billion yuan by 2013. By 2015, the group plans for its overall revenue to exceed 150 billion yuan. The redefinition of Changhong's core value concept represents a direction for the development of China's home appliance industry. Starting from this issue, our newspaper will interpret the new Changhong with redefined values in aspects such as business transformation, high-end manufacturing, business model, internationalization, and plasma strategy.

Changhong Group ranks seventh among the top 100 enterprises in China's electronics information industry in 2011 with a revenue of 61.5 billion yuan. Recently, Changhong Group also announced its goal of achieving 100 billion yuan in revenue by 2013. Calculating at an annual growth rate of over 30% since 2004, achieving the billion-yuan target in two years is not difficult. In fact, in the strategic plan recently released by Changhong Group, apart from achieving the billion-yuan target, this enterprise with over 50 years of history will also attempt to create a new ecological chain in the home appliance industry by integrating the upstream and downstream industrial chains and focusing on intelligent transformation.

Scale: Crossing the billion-yuan mark is a threshold

Among the top 100 enterprises in China's electronics information industry in 2011, Haier Group, Hisense Group, Changhong Group, and TCL Group ranked in the top 10. Only Haier Group had an annual revenue exceeding 100 billion yuan. In the home appliance industry, another company surpassing 10 billion yuan is Midea Group, but none of the home appliance companies starting with color TV business have yet surpassed 10 billion yuan.

China's home appliance product shipments rank among the global leaders. Taking the color TV industry as an example, the shipment volume reached as high as 118 million units in 2010, accounting for 49% of the global shipment volume. Samsung, Sony, and LG shipped approximately 85 million LCD TVs in 2010, almost equal to the total shipment volume of China's color TVs. Last year, the shipment volume of LCD TVs for individual Chinese brands generally did not exceed 8 million units. Six major TV manufacturers (Hisense, Changhong, Skyworth, TCL, Konka, Haier) accounted for 18% of the global LCD TV shipment volume, while Samsung occupied 22% of the global share with more than 35 million units shipped.

Lin Yuanfang, the executive vice president of the China Electronic Visual Information Industry Association, told reporters from the "China Electronics News" that cultivating 3 to 5 enterprise groups with annual revenues exceeding 10 billion yuan will be a key focus for the development of China's home appliance industry. The size of the whole machine manufacturer directly determines the negotiating power with upstream and downstream supporting component manufacturers. Home appliance enterprises that cross the 10-billion-yuan revenue scale threshold will face a new competitive landscape, such as the industry influence brought by Foxconn's massive production scale.

Among the enterprises proposing to achieve revenues exceeding 10 billion yuan in the next 3 to 5 years, the industry generally looks favorably upon Changhong Group, Hisense Group, and TCL Group. Experts believe that these three enterprises have the following common characteristics: they have achieved diversified business development in consumer electronics, household appliances, IT communication; they have the production capacity of upstream key components such as LCD modules, LCD panels, plasma panels, compressors; they own multiple listed companies and have strong external expansion capabilities based on capital mergers and acquisitions.

Changhong Group's proposal of the billion-yuan target naturally stems from its intrinsic confidence. Liu Tibin, the deputy chairman of Changhong Group, told reporters from the "China Electronics News" that Changhong Group already possesses a relatively complete industrial structure and a complete industrial chain. Through various means including internal and external expansion, it has accumulated substantial funds, brands, and technical resources. These all lay a solid foundation for the realization of the billion-yuan Changhong target.

Focus: Full industrial chain integration

In the case of significant changes in the global electronics industry environment, China's home appliance industry must establish a shock-resistant industrial structure that can generate synergies to form the amplification effect of economies of scale and the synergy effect of scope economies, quickly enhancing its inherent comprehensive competitiveness.

In November 2010, Changhong Group initiated organizational reforms and constructed an industrial system architecture mainly consisting of seven industrial groups: multimedia, home appliances, overseas, military, components, emerging industries, services, and three direct subsidiaries: Changhong Jiahua, Guohong Communications, and Changhong Real Estate. However, Changhong Group did not allow each industry to develop independently, but instead worked hard on the coordination between the industries.

Objectively speaking, although Changhong Group started with the color TV business, due to fierce competition in the domestic and international color TV markets, the share of color TV business in the future billion-yuan Changhong value composition will not exceed 30%, or even lower.

However, compared with other color TV manufacturers, Changhong's advantages in multimedia business are very obvious. When LCD TVs dominate the market, Changhong can maneuver between LCD TVs and plasma TVs thanks to its advantages in plasma TVs and their upstream panels. In fact, this year's first half saw a decline in LCD TV growth, while plasma TVs rebounded significantly, which is a great benefit to Changhong.

In 2010, the global shipment volume of plasma TVs set a historical record of 19 million units, reflecting good market prospects and potential, especially with the explosion of the 3D TV market, where PDP's unique superiority in 3D display will enable it to gain more market share.

Market analysts pointed out that from the current industrial prospects, the global plasma TV market is warming up. As one of the important pivots, plasma TVs have brightened the optimistic expectations of Changhong's multimedia business. According to the statistics of Aowei, since November 2010, Changhong's domestic market share of plasma TVs has steadily remained above 50%.

Among the three listed companies under Changhong Group, two belong to the white goods industry. Meiling Appliances and Huayi Compressor are outstanding players in the domestic refrigerator and refrigerator compressor industries. In the next three years, the white goods business will achieve a net profit growth of 20% to 30%, with refrigerator compressor sales exceeding 30 million units, becoming the global leader.

Emerging businesses represented by PDP, OLED, and IC design relate to Changhong Group's future and even determine its rise and fall. While Samsung Electronics, LG, Panasonic, and other Japanese and Korean companies start constructing high-generation OLED panel production lines, Changhong takes the lead in laying out OLED early, showing its foresight and accuracy in grasping industrial development trends.

The integrated effects of Changhong Group's core businesses across the entire industrial chain have initially emerged. The profit source of plasma TV sets benefits from the integration with plasma panels. Hefei Meiling and Huayi Compressor achieve win-win through the cooperative model of refrigerators + compressors. The integrated research and development of Changzheng Optoelectronics and the multimedia industry ensures the cost leadership of LCD TV sets. Overall, Changhong has benefited from the integration of the entire industrial chain, according to Liu Tibin who told reporters from the "China Electronics News."

Thickness: Intelligent Transformation

The lack of core technology and related patent technology is a bottleneck hindering the development of China's home appliance industry. Recently, Apple and Microsoft have begun charging patent fees, citing that HTC and Samsung's Android system phones infringe on their patent technology. Patent disputes are escalating. This also serves as a warning bell for China's home appliance manufacturers.

To support the billion-yuan scale of the industry, Changhong Group does not dare to slack off in its investment in patent technology. We study core technologies, not simply copying them, but mastering the true underlying technology. These are our real core competencies that others cannot imitate. With our own core technology, we also have the capital to compete with global consumer electronics giants, Liu Tibin told reporters from the "China Electronics News."

He believes that future competition in the home appliance industry will no longer be about isolated products or isolated technologies. Taking the TV industry as an example, the focus of competition is no longer just the TV itself, but rather an ecosystem surrounding the TV. In this ecosystem, applications are the soil, sunlight, and nutrients, which include content and services; embedded software and SOC are the trunks; and various forms of terminal products are the fruits on the tree. By integrating innovations upstream and downstream, a complete industrial ecosystem is formed.

In terms of future R&D priorities, Changhong Group will focus on intelligent technology, using self-developed smart Xuan Yuan technology software platform, smart TV drive chips, and SOC smart chips as the basis, striving to build three systems: smart platforms, smart applications, and smart controls. At the same time, establishing a unified diversified open operating system to realize human-computer interaction, through air mice, motion sensing applications, multi-screen interactive applications, deepening smart interactions, and realizing smart control. On this basis, Changhong vigorously promotes the research and development of smart homes centered around smart TVs and mobile smart terminals mainly consisting of smartphones, thereby meeting consumers' needs for human-machine dialogue, automatic control, and other application requirements, said Wu Yingjian, Vice General Manager of Sichuan Changhong, to reporters from the "China Electronics News."

Liu Tibin revealed that besides intelligent technology, in the next 3 to 5 years, Changhong Group will further increase independent innovation efforts based on six core capabilities: industrial design, embedded software, IC design, engineering technology, reliability technology, and frequency conversion technology, further playing the role of intellectual property rights platform in areas such as technical capability cultivation, differentiated product development, and innovation system construction.

According to reports, Changhong's industrial design technology has expanded from initial pure appearance design to user research, user interface, and other professional directions, providing design services for the seven major industrial groups under its umbrella, supporting over 20 billion yuan in sales revenue. Currently, Changhong has established leading user experience laboratories, color material laboratories domestically, and jointly established interaction design laboratories with Hong Kong Polytechnic University, providing a support platform for domestic innovative designs.

Standing on the frequency conversion technology of white goods business is a highlight in Changhong Group's system. In 2010, Changhong established the Frequency Conversion Control Technology Research Institute, set up a doctoral research team, cooperated with Tsinghua University for joint research, achieving the optimal combination of industry, academia, and research, mastering the core frequency conversion technology. The Frequency Conversion Control Technology Research Institute, together with Changhong Dongyuan, Huayi, Gasis Beller Compression Manufacturing Plant, and Changhong Air Conditioning, Meiling Refrigerator Whole Machine Manufacturing Plant, collectively formed a complete industrial chain from R&D to main component production and then to whole machine production.

As of the end of 2010, Changhong Group's cumulative number of patent applications was 2,515, of which 673 were inventions; the number of patents authorized was 1,596, of which 118 were inventions. The proportion of patent technology in new technology R&D exceeded 85%, and the implementation rate of patent technology reached more than 98%. In the next 3 to 5 years, Changhong will intensify technological development in important fields such as new flat-panel display devices, digital TVs, white goods, integrated circuits, basic software, information technology, forming a comparative competitive advantage, Wu Yingjian stated.
