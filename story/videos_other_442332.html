Fuzhou Google optimization, woman was robbed and beaten, disabled villager died after being stabbed 7 times while chasing the thug

by a147896456 on 2011-06-07 01:51:26

The modified bulldozer driven by the deceased during his struggle with the thug was covered in blood. All photos were taken by Wang Yongbo, a reporter for this newspaper.

At the scene of the incident, bloodstains on the ground were clearly visible. The rescued girl expressed deep remorse when talking about her benefactor. The deceased's disability certificate was also shown.

At around 15:15 on May 1st, near the Yangqu highway, about a thousand meters away from Nanan Town in Gaoshi City, a woman was robbed and assaulted by a man on a motorcycle. When none of the four passing cars offered help, Wu Wendeh, a villager from Nanan Town, arrived at the scene driving a "small bulldozer" (a tractor modified into a bulldozer) to stop the fleeing robber. As a result, Wu Wendeh was attacked by the man and stabbed seven times.

At 16:30, Wu Wendeh, aged 48, was pronounced dead. Guo Min (a pseudonym), the victimized woman, learned that her savior was actually a disabled man with limited mobility. On the afternoon of the following day, Guo Min and her family knelt before Wu Wendeh's coffin for a long time, unwilling to get up.

Currently, the second team of detectives from Gaoshi City has intervened in the case and is making every effort to capture the suspect.

**Incident**

A woman was robbed in broad daylight, but none of the four passing vehicles stopped to help.

In the afternoon, Guo Min, the victim, came to Wu Wendeh's home accompanied by her aunt. Upon entering Wu's house and seeing his coffin, both women immediately knelt down and cried uncontrollably, refusing to get up even after being consoled by others.

Guo Min's face was bruised, especially her left cheek which was swollen high. She told reporters that these injuries were inflicted by the thug on May 1st. Guo Min, 20 years old, from Zhangjiazhuang Town, was riding an electric bike to visit her aunt in Nanan Village. At around 15:00, she was approached by a man on a motorcycle wearing sunglasses who asked her where she was going. Sensing something wrong, Guo Min replied curtly, “What’s it to you?”

The man parked his motorcycle in front of her and walked towards her saying, "You're quite tough!" as he began grabbing her bag from the basket of her electric bike. Guo Min quickly dropped her bike and clung tightly to her bag. "We pulled back and forth several times, scattering the contents of my bag all over the ground," said Guo Min. Enraged, the man grabbed her neck and punched her face. Instantly, Guo Min felt warm liquid flowing from her nose. Still gripping her neck, the man dragged her towards the nearby wheat field. After a few steps, Guo Min slipped and fell, and the man began punching her relentlessly.

"I started crying and shouting," said Guo Min. While the man was beating her, four vehicles passed by without offering any help. "The first one was a white Buick car, which stopped ahead of us, and someone in the passenger seat rolled down the window, took a look, and then drove away. Two large trucks followed, slowed down briefly, and then sped off." What upset and saddened Guo Min most was that the driver of the third car even smiled at them.

**Using the "small bulldozer" to block the thug, Wu Wendeh was stabbed.**

After hitting her a few more times, the man ran towards his motorcycle and attempted to flee. In a state of shock, Guo Min stood up to gather her scattered belongings when she suddenly noticed a man driving a "small bulldozer" coming from the front, trying twice to block the fleeing thug and knocking him down.

"After he fell, he took out a newspaper-wrapped item from his motorcycle, drew something, and swung it at the man sitting on the bulldozer," Guo Min said. Since the bulldozer was facing away from her, she only saw the two men struggling. Soon, the thug mounted his motorcycle again and fled.

"When I ran to the bulldozer, I saw Brother Wu Wendeh, who had helped me, covered in blood, already slumped motionless over the steering wheel," said Guo Min.

**Recollection**

The benefactor was a disabled person, and there was blood covering more than three meters.

Guo Min said that after the incident, she learned that Wu Wendeh, who had helped her, was a disabled person with limited mobility, using a single crutch. "I feel extremely guilty inside," said Guo Min. Her only hope is to catch the murderer as soon as possible, punish him severely, and thus console her benefactor.

Wu Wendeh's youngest daughter, Wu Shihong, recalled that at around 15:00 on May 1st, her father Wu Wendeh was driving the "small bulldozer" with her to a garbage dump located beside the Yangqu highway west of the village. That day, her father was transporting garbage for an iron factory here. Before heading to the iron factory, her father intended to pile up the garbage at the dump site. "I had just parked my bicycle when I heard someone shouting for help," said Wu Shihong. She and her father looked in the direction of the sound and saw a man and a woman struggling about hundreds of meters away. "The man kept hitting the girl and dragging her by the neck into the wheat field."

While Wu Shihong was wondering what was happening, she suddenly noticed her father had already driven the small bulldozer towards the location of the struggle. Wu Shihong said that when she saw her father tangled up with someone, she immediately called a relative and ran towards her father.

When Wu Shihong reached her father's side, she was completely stunned. "(Father) was covered in blood, and so was the bulldozer." Wu Shihong tried hard to hold back tears, but they still streamed down her face, dropping onto the ground. She said that at the time, her father's blood was dripping onto the bulldozer and continuously falling through the gaps onto the ground.

Subsequently, Wu Wendeh was rushed to the Nanmeng Town Health Center by relatives and then transferred to the Gaoshi City Traditional Chinese Medicine Hospital. Ultimately, Wu Wendeh died despite medical efforts to save him.

On the afternoon of the same day, at the scene of the incident, dark red blood was clearly visible. From a pool of blood, several "blood rivers" spread out, the longest stretching over three meters. The small bulldozer parked at the Nanmeng Police Station had forever lost its owner, but Wu Wendeh's bloodstains could still be seen all over the bulldozer.

**Progress**

Local police are working hard to capture the thug.

On the same day, Wu Wendeh's body underwent a forensic autopsy. Wu Wendeh's cousin participated in the autopsy and said that he counted seven wounds on Wu Wendeh's body. According to Guo Min, after the incident, she called her aunt and described the thug's appearance, asking her to intercept him. However, at the entrance of Nanan Village, the thug injured a relative of her aunt's hands before fleeing. Currently, the injured person is receiving treatment in the hospital.

Journalists learned that the suspect is about 30 years old, approximately 1.7 meters tall, with darker skin, and speaks with a local accent.

Currently, the second team of detectives from Gaoshi City has intervened in the case. Journalists learned from this team that the incident occurred at around 15:15, and the case was registered as a robbery. The police stated that they are actively investigating and doing their utmost to capture the suspect.

**Personality Sketch**

He was regarded as a good person in people's eyes.

Nanmeng Village seemed different yesterday compared to usual. The streets were quiet, while at Wu Wendeh's home in the western part of the village, villagers kept coming to comfort the bereaved family.

Wife Wang Xiangtai: Despite poverty, he never gave up.

According to Wu Wendeh's wife, Wang Xiangtai, Wu Wendeh suffered from polio since childhood and was classified as having a second-level disability. However, Wu Wendeh never abandoned himself or waited for others' help because of his disability. Wang Xiangtai wept, saying that Wu Wendeh's death made her feel like the pillar of the family had collapsed. He had elderly parents nearly eighty years old and three daughters, two of whom were unmarried.

Wu Wendeh's relatives mentioned that due to family reasons, Wu Wendeh did not finish elementary school. To reduce the burden on his family, he began learning repair skills at around ten years old. "Handy, he would study and figure things out." Wu Wendeh's younger brother, Wu Wenhui, said that because repairing items such as radios did not yield much profit, Wu Wendeh started raising pigs. Not earning much money, Wu Wendeh then opened a repair shop to fix agricultural machinery and other equipment for the villagers. Due to his physical limitations, Wu Wendeh's business was not thriving. A few years ago, he eventually found a job transporting garbage. To save money, Wu Wendeh transformed an old tractor used for over ten years into a "small bulldozer" for easier garbage transportation.

Wang Xiangtai said that although the family was still poor, Wu Wendeh never gave up and worked hard to support the family. With the help of relatives a few years ago, they even renovated their house.

Disabled Person Wang Jingu: He voluntarily gave me the business he had secured.

Wang Jingu, a villager one year older than Wu Wendeh, was also disabled. Talking about his younger brother, he was full of gratitude. Due to his disability, Wang Jingu's family was very impoverished. A few years ago, Wu Wendeh secured a vegetable delivery business but voluntarily handed it over to Wang Jingu. Without funds, Wu Wendeh took out money from his meager savings. "He has given me thousands of yuan over the years," Wang Jingu said. During festivals, Wu Wendeh would often visit him and give him some festival money.

Villager Wang Junhua mentioned Wu Wendeh repeatedly using the words "good person." She said that sometimes when she was busy working, she would ask Wu Wendeh to watch her children, and he would never refuse.

Deputy Director of the Village Committee, Wu Pingyi: He voluntarily gave up his low-income household status.

Due to poverty, Wu Wendeh began receiving low-income assistance in 2003. The hundreds of yuan provided monthly by the government became an important source of income for the family. In mid-April this year, the new round of low-income household selection began, and Wu Wendeh, as a previous low-income household, once again received the registration form for low-income households. A few days later, when everyone else had submitted their registration forms, Wu Wendeh did not submit his.

Deputy Director of the Nanan Village Committee, Wu Pingyi, said that when the village committee questioned Wu Wendeh about the reason, he told them that his living conditions had improved somewhat in recent years, and he wanted to give up his spot to families in greater need.

Wu Pingyi said that according to Wu Wendeh's family situation, he had the opportunity to continue receiving low-income assistance. "As a disabled person, voluntarily giving up his low-income status made many young people admire him," Wu Pingyi said. Wu Wendeh's bravery in fighting against the thug was the pride of the Nanan villagers.

**Written by reporter Ren Li of this newspaper.**
