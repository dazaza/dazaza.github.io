Qingdao Housekeeping, some small common knowledge before moving in after the new house is decorated.

by lxbj2009 on 2009-11-29 09:13:10

Here are some precautions for the newly renovated house before moving in:

1. When using water pipes for the first time, colored water may come out. After a period of time, the water will gradually become clear.

2. When cleaning the faucet, do not use acidic or alkaline liquids, nor rough cloths or steel wool balls, otherwise the surface of the faucet will be scratched.

3. If you don't plan to move in immediately after renovation, or if you need to be away for a long time due to business trips after moving in, or if there is public water pipe maintenance, it's best to close the main water valve.

4. Before using electrical appliances for the first time, it's best to check if they are damp, and whether the plugs and wires are intact and safe. Especially for electric kettles, electric water heaters, rice cookers, and disinfection cabinets. For new appliances, read the instructions before using them.

5. When using the gas stove, pay attention to the direction of the switch, and turn on/off the gas according to the directional sign. It's best to close the main gas valve every night before going to sleep.

6. Tiles need regular cleaning to keep the patterns clear, but when cleaning, use soft cloth only, do not use acid-alkali liquid and hard brush, otherwise the surface of the tiles will be damaged.

7. Marble is a natural mineral, relatively delicate and prone to fading. Be careful when using it. Do not let colored liquids stay on the surface for a long time, and do not place rust-prone metal items on it for a long time. When cleaning stains, do not use acid-alkali liquid. Also, be careful not to let heavy objects impact its surface, or scratch the surface with sharp objects.

8. When walking on solid wood floors, avoid dragging your shoes on the surface, as this can cause the paint layer to thin and reduce the service life. Shoes with iron nails should not walk on it. Do not hit the surface with heavy objects. Avoid direct sunlight on the floor. When cleaning the floor, pay attention to the dryness of the mop, and never mop when the mop is fully saturated with water.

9. Wood products include cabinets, bookshelves, doors and windows, etc. When opening and closing doors and windows, pay attention to moderate force, and pay attention to the order of opening and closing the left and right sides of the doors and windows.

10. For places that are frequently touched, pay attention to protecting the paint surface.

11. Keep the connectors and slides lubricated and clean. Pay attention to adjusting and protecting the spacing of spring hinges to prevent them from slipping out. For doors and windows that have not been opened for a long time, pay attention to preventing rust on the connectors.

12. When moving furniture, lift it up instead of dragging it on the ground.

13. When cleaning, pay attention to the dryness of the cloth, especially when cleaning the side edges, prevent moisture from seeping in to avoid delamination of the edge strips.

Secondly, cleaning and maintenance of carpets:

Carpets are high-end decorative materials laid on the ground, currently widely used in commercial, office, exhibition, and residential buildings. According to statistics, in modern hotels and office buildings, carpet laying accounts for more than 60% of the total area, showing how widely carpets are used.

1. Create a solemn atmosphere indoors

2. Aesthetic and diverse

3. Helps absorb sound and reduce noise

4. Provides a comfortable stepping ground for people

5. Provides clean and safe working and living places

Types and characteristics of carpets:

1. Wool carpets: made of natural protein fibers, with a soft luster and elasticity.

2. Acrylic carpets (polyacrylonitrile): synthetic fibers with good gloss.

3. Nylon carpets (nylon 66): synthetic fibers with poor texture.

Thirdly, structure of carpets:

The first layer, the surface layer: usually woven from natural fibers and artificial fibers, loose, soft, and strong fiber feeling.

The second layer, the supporting layer: usually woven from fibers, plays a supporting role, improves the stability and durability of the carpet, and the surface yarns and this layer material are intertwined.

The third layer, the auxiliary support layer: generally woven from hemp or chemical fibers, attached to the support layer with adhesive, firmly combining the entire carpet structure.

Padding layer: generally a plastic porous structure, which isolates the carpet from the ground, increasing breathability and elasticity.

Fourthly, stains on carpets:

Most carpet stains belong to water-based and dry substances, including dust, sand particles, starch, soft drinks, tea juice, fruit juice, accounting for about 80%-85% of all stains. Oil-based stains include animal and plant oils, cosmetics, shoe polish, ballpoint pen oil, etc. Protein-based stains include blood, vomit, urine, food, etc., accounting for about 15-20%.

Fifthly, cleaning agents and their principles:

Cleaning agents contain surfactants that penetrate insoluble dirt and emulsify it off the fiber surface. The detergent molecules form space obstacles and electrostatic repulsion forces, making it difficult for oil clusters to re-aggregate and precipitate on the fiber surface.

Sixthly, carpet care methods:

1. Vacuuming: brushes and loosens carpet fibers, removing more than 80% of dry dirt.

2. Stain treatment: removes stains promptly to prevent fibers from becoming brittle, adhering, or breaking due to stain penetration.

3. Dry cleaning with foam: cleans the carpet surface through the action of foam.

4. Rotational shampoo dry cleaning: cleans the carpet surface and support layer through the action of foam.

5. Extraction carpet cleaning: thoroughly sucks out deep dirt in the carpet, sprays water, rolls the brush, and sucks water all at once.

Seventhly, daily care tips for fabric sofas:

1. Do not place near fire sources, high temperatures, or direct sunlight.

2. Do not get stained with colored water solutions or acid-base solutions.

3. Removable fabric covers can be dry-cleaned only.

4. Regularly use a household vacuum cleaner to remove dust from the sofa surface.

Eighthly, simple method for cleaning range hoods:

Boil water in a pressure cooker and open the exhaust fan. Direct the steam nozzle towards the rotating blades. As the hot steam continuously hits the blades and other parts, the oily wastewater flows into the waste oil cup. Once the cup is empty, the range hood is clean.

Ninthly, removing soup stains from carpets with salt:

In households with children, soup often spills onto the carpet. Never use a wet cloth to wipe it. First, use a clean dry cloth or towel to absorb the moisture, then sprinkle some salt on the stain. Once the salt absorbs the moisture, use a vacuum cleaner to suck it up, then brush the carpet flat.

Tenthly, maintenance knowledge for wooden floors:

After installing wooden floors indoors, they indeed bring a luxurious decorative effect. However, to keep the wooden floor smooth, clean, bright, and comfortable, while nourishing the wood and effectively preventing insect damage, attention should be paid to maintenance. Generally speaking, the floor should be kept dry and clean, avoiding contact with large amounts of water, and must not be wiped with alkaline water or soapy water. If there are stains, just use steel wool to polish them off. Wax the floor regularly. You can also apply varnish for maintenance, brushing a layer of polyester clear varnish (UV varnish) or polyurethane varnish once a year or so. If there is a radiator leak, it must be wiped clean immediately. Do not expose the floor directly to sunlight or electric heaters, as excessive drying can cause cracks in the floor. After installation, painted wooden floors should minimize direct sunlight exposure to prevent premature cracking and aging of the paint. Do not throw lit cigarettes or matches onto the clean wooden floor to avoid scorching the surface. At the same time, avoid direct contact between hot basins, hot rice pots, and other high-temperature objects and the floor.
