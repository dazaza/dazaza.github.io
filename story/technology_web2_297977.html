"Autumn freezing" health preservation: appropriate to the time, varying from person to person

by bbeij62s on 2009-12-07 16:07:13

In autumn, people should not wear too many or too thick clothes. Gradually adapting to the climate changes in autumn and enhancing the body's cold resistance can be beneficial. "Autumn chilling" is a health preservation method that has been passed down by the folk for a long time. However, it is necessary to pay attention to timing and individual differences.

In early autumn, there is still the "remaining heat" of summer, with the heat not yet dissipated and the temperature still high. Sometimes the "autumn tiger" even wreaks havoc. Clearly, it does no harm to be "chilled", and there is no need to rush to add more clothes. At this time, it is not only not about "autumn chilling", but also unnecessary to get "cold". If we say "cold", it can only mean paying attention to the drop in temperature, eliminating the residual heat of summer, and not letting the body get too hot.

In mid-autumn, the temperature begins to gradually decrease. Although there is some coolness, it is not very cold. The weather is clear and pleasant, making it an excellent time for "autumn chilling". Especially for young and middle-aged people, clothing should be somewhat controlled. If they are wrapped too warmly, it can lead to sweating and physical discomfort, which goes against the health preservation principles of autumn - preserving yin essence internally and restraining yang energy internally. There should be a conscious effort to let the body "get chilled" to adapt to the climatic changes from summer heat to cold, while engaging in various sports exercises to lay a foundation for winter.

By late autumn, the climate changes significantly, and the temperature difference between morning and evening increases. As the saying goes, "one autumn rain makes it colder." Especially during the transition from autumn to winter, strong cold air often invades, causing a sudden drop in temperature. Children are still in the stage of growth and development, with an immature central nervous system function and poor body temperature regulation ability. When encountering cold stimulation, if not properly protected, the body will quickly react negatively. Elderly people, due to their weak constitution, declining yin and yang, and reduced overall resistance, have weakened cold resistance and cannot effectively protect themselves like young and middle-aged people when exposed to cold. Therefore, if children and the elderly continue to force "autumn chilling" at this time, it may backfire and cause health damage.

Moreover, in late autumn, after being exposed to cold stimulation, chronic bronchitis, bronchial asthma, gastritis, gastric and duodenal ulcers may easily recur. Coronary heart disease patients may experience angina pectoris or myocardial infarction, and hypertensive patients may suffer from strokes. Therefore, people suffering from these diseases should not only avoid getting "cold" but should also add clothes in time, keep warm, and especially strengthen the protection against cold in areas such as the abdomen, back, and both feet.
