The plastic expansion sleeve anchor rod, also known as a recoverable anchor rod, comes in detailed models such as size 32 and size 42. The plastic expansion sleeve anchor rods produced by our company are composed of a plastic cone, a plastic expansion sleeve, a metal rod body, and accessories. This type of anchor rod has advantages such as being lightweight, compact structure, easy and quick installation, long service life, high anchoring force, and reusable metal rod when recovery is needed. Its working principle involves pulling the plastic cone with the metal rod so that the plastic expansion sleeve tightly adheres to the rock hole wall, achieving anchorage without using traditional resin bonding or anchoring agents. It saves costs, reduces workers' labor intensity, transportation volume, energy consumption, and support costs, and can be widely used in safety support fields such as coal mine roadways, culverts, and tunnels.

### Two, Model Explanation of Plastic Expansion Sleeve Anchor Rods

For example: MTS42/16*1600: M stands for anchor rod, TS: plastic expansion sleeve, 42 refers to the applicable drill hole diameter, 16 refers to the metal rod, with a length of 1600mm.

### Three, Working Principle of Plastic Expansion Sleeve Anchor Rods

The anchoring principle of the plastic expansion sleeve anchor rod is to generate radial force on the hole wall through the initial anchorage of the plastic expansion sleeve and the inclined surface of the cone, achieving friction anchorage. Then, by pulling the plastic cone axially during final anchorage, the interaction between the plastic expansion sleeve and the rock hole wall increases, reaching the required anchoring force.

### Four, Installation Method of Plastic Expansion Sleeve Anchor Rods

(1) Screw the threaded head of the metal rod into the plastic cone nut until it cannot rotate further, then put on the expansion sleeve and use a specially designed anchor tool of equal length to steadily push the plastic cone together with the plastic expansion sleeve into the bottom of the drilled hole, ensuring the fit between the plastic expansion sleeve and the plastic cone.

(2) Use a hammer weighing no less than 1.5 kg to hit the end of the specially designed anchor tool, causing the plastic expansion sleeve to move axially to the marked line range (marks should be made on the specially designed anchor tool to determine the axial movement distance), making the expansion sleeve adhere tightly to the hole wall, generating self-locking to complete the initial anchorage.

(3) Install the bearing plate, then use a torque wrench to tighten the locking nut. The tightening torques are respectively: 70N・m for an M14 metal rod, 110N・m for an M16 metal rod, and 140N・m for an M18 metal rod, thereby completing the final anchorage, and the installation is finished.

### Five, Recovery of Metal Rods

Use a wrench to engage the tail of the rod body, and forcefully rotate it counterclockwise to detach the rod from (completely screw out) the cone. Remove the bearing plate, and recover both the rod and the bearing plate.

### Six, Precautions

(1) Drill holes with a 42mm (or 32mm) drill bit, controlling the rock hole diameter to 42+2.0mm (or 32+2.0mm). The hole depth should be about 40mm-50mm shorter than the total length of the plastic expansion sleeve anchor rod. The rock hole must be cleaned of dust and sand particles (water cleaning or air washing can be used), and the rock hole must be straight.

(2) This product is mainly used for side wall anchorage. For extremely loose rock formations (compressive strength <14.75MPa, f<1), environments with temperatures below 0℃ or above 40℃, enhanced testing is required before cautious application.

(3) Avoid performing final anchorage without initial anchorage. Tensile tests need to be conducted after the final anchorage reaches the required torque.
