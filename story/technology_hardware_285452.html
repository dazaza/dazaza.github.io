Rogue: Van Persie, please don't walk around with a placenta

November 29, 2009 00:21:19 Source: NetEase Sports Follow-up 0 posts Mobile event viewing Let Van Persies stay away from the field, not because of others, but due to their own rash actions...

NetEase Sports November 29 report:

Arsenal's Van Persie will undergo surgery next week and may be out for the entire season. This news is truly shocking. Just days ago, after receiving so-called "placenta therapy," Van Persie was rumored to be making a comeback. In just a few days, such bad news has come, which is really baffling. If Arsenal club regrets it now, the main party, Van Persie, probably sighs even more. This way, it's not only delaying the Premier League, but the South Africa World Cup is imminent, and Van Persie's status as a Dutch national team player will also be shaken.

Athletes and injuries are closely related to personal physical fitness. Even strong players like Drogba and Essien get injured inevitably. Not to mention technique-focused Van Persie. Listening to some so-called "quack doctors" and risking their athletic careers is countless, but on the field, the intelligence of these stars clearly doesn't match their IQ. Taking a long view, wasn't Van Persie's predecessor Marco van Basten ruined by quacks? Under repeated chest-pounding guarantees of cure, Van Basten's career ended prematurely. Closer to home, when Du Wei, the captain of Shen Xiangfu's Olympic team, fractured his bone, he brought in a sage hidden in the city, and not only didn't heal the injury, but almost sent Du Wei directly to the Disabled Persons' Federation. Blindly trusting some folk remedies and believing they can miraculously cure diseases and instantly heal. Besides adding absurd laughter, what remains is self-deception.

The final results of Van Persie's examination revealed three torn ligaments. Such serious injuries were thought to rely on Serbian massage to revive like dry wood and fully recover. It's truly laughable. Football skills need to be practiced hard on the training ground and gradually improved. However, regarding medicine, this newer and stricter discipline than football, football stars naively believe in hearsay, joking with their international bodies, making it necessary to reiterate the importance of professional athletes improving their own cultivation. Giants on the field, dwarfs in life. What do quacks repeatedly prove on the green pitch? It's not that their deception is sophisticated, not that their schemes are clever, but that these so-called globally watched stars all think while holding placentas.

The weekly salary keeps rising, the value keeps rising, but what comes with it are countless stupid moves off the field. Describing them as very silly and cute is an insult to these words. Comparing them to legends, the ultimate reality is either imprisonment or lying in bed moaning after being treated by so-called "miracle doctors." Pitiful and laughable to the extreme, yet it makes one realize a fact. With full wallets, intelligence is lost, and apart from money, what else is left for footballers? On the other hand, clubs that turn a blind eye to players' actions, claiming to be professional and elite, have team doctors who not only fail to stop such reckless behavior but often play the role of "matchmakers." One feels that even buying livestock wouldn't lead to such waste.

Removing football stars walking around with placentas on their heads, life only happens once, and football life is the same. Without thinking, blindly listening and believing ultimately harms oneself. Those extravagant club owners, stop boasting about your advanced systems. Please first treat players as people, not livestock. Count how many stars have fallen under quacks, and you'll suddenly realize that those who keep them off the field aren't others, but their own rash actions.
