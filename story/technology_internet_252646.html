Should fat people be discriminated against?

The United States is the country with the most obese people in the world. It is understood that nearly 60% of American adults are overweight or suffer from obesity. This means there are as many as 130 million obese people in the U.S., and according to recent surveys, this number continues to grow. This large group of people is suffering discrimination from all aspects of society, becoming a serious social problem in America.

Recently, I read a report stating that one foreign state had passed a law prohibiting doctors from questioning patients about their obesity issues if the patient does not wish to be questioned. Regarding whether excessively obese individuals should be looked down upon by society, there are two viewpoints for your reference:

First: If excessive obesity is not a disease but rather a result of bad habits, such as lack of self-control leading to binge eating, which causes severe obesity, then protecting and indulging these bad habits through the law would be irresponsible towards obese patients.

For those who are excessively obese, doctors should provide professional medical advice, and society should also offer proper guidance to the obese. On the surface, although obese people may not harm others like smokers do, extreme obesity can lead to health problems, increasing the likelihood of losing work capacity and eventually becoming a societal burden. Other healthy individuals will have to pay extra, either through higher taxes or other means, to support these individuals, thereby increasing the overall societal burden. This actually severely affects the normal rights of others and has an impact on society. Therefore, society has a responsibility to regulate these undesirable behaviors. Why shouldn't doctors be allowed to point out the patient's problems? If society continues to develop in this direction, won't more problems arise?

In the case of smoking, some developed countries have already started regulating such behaviors and impose high taxes to compensate for the additional burdens their health issues place on society. I think this is very reasonable. However, society currently cannot bear the cost of compensating for the health problems caused by obesity through high taxation due to the inability of some individuals to control their diet.

Second: Obesity, like having moles on the face or hair loss, falls under personal bodily rights. I have the right to choose to be fat or thin. I can be as fat as I want, even if it has already caused me health problems. It’s like having a disease; I have the right to choose not to seek treatment or go to the hospital. Doctors can't force me to be healthy!

Some obese patients are born that way. For these individuals, we should care for and protect them instead of discriminating against them. This societal burden is normal and should be borne.

Which viewpoint do you agree with more? I hope you can tell me.
