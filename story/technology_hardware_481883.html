Driving in summer, how to use the air conditioner more fuel-efficiently

by koti47375 on 2011-08-26 13:24:19

1. The most suitable temperature for air conditioning is 25 degrees Celsius.

One of the important reasons for the persistently high fuel consumption of cars in summer is the excessive use of car air conditioning. Therefore, reducing the fuel consumption brought by car air conditioning will reduce the actual fuel consumption of the whole vehicle. Some car owners set the temperature of the car air conditioner to the lowest temperature value in order to make the car cooler. In this way, the air conditioning pump will keep running, and the blower will also be in a high gear position all the time, which will increase fuel consumption instead. Moreover, the large temperature difference between inside and outside the car can easily cause colds. Therefore, when setting the temperature of the car air conditioner, it is generally sufficient to set it at 25 degrees Celsius.

2. Do not adjust the air conditioner to the highest gear, and the wind direction is best blowing upwards.

When a car is exposed to the sun for a while, the temperature inside the car may reach sixty or seventy degrees Celsius. It would be terrible to drive under such circumstances. You would have to rush into the car with the speed of a hundred-meter dash, start the engine, turn on the air conditioner to the maximum gear, then wait outside the car until the temperature inside drops after a few minutes before getting back in. Have you ever had such an experience?

In fact, this is not the only effective method of cooling. Manager Chen told us that if the temperature of the air conditioner is set to the lowest value, the air conditioning pump will keep running, and the blower will be in a high gear position, which naturally increases fuel consumption.

The correct method is to immediately lower the car window after entering a car that has been exposed to the sun. At this point, do not turn on the air conditioner yet. After one or two minutes, when the temperature inside and outside the car is balanced, close the windows, turn on the air conditioner, and set the air conditioning circulation to the indoor circulation mode.

In addition, the wind direction of the air conditioner should preferably blow upward during cooling because cold air sinks. When choosing the wind direction, it is best to choose blowing towards the front of the body rather than the front windshield, as this achieves the best effect. If you choose to blow towards the front windshield, it would offset a large part of the cooling effect due to the high temperature of the windshield.

Some people might think that a larger airflow results in better cooling effects. However, when selecting the wind speed gear, do not always use the highest gear. If there are only front-seat passengers, using gears 2 or 3 for wind speed is sufficient.

3. Open the car window first before turning on the air conditioner.

Experts say that long-term use of air conditioning increases the load on the engine and also increases fuel consumption. After getting in the car, the driver can first open the window to let out the hot air inside the car, then turn on the air conditioner, and change the air conditioning gear every so often to avoid letting the air conditioner work at high gear all the time.

Turn off the engine when parking for a long time.

"When others leave their engines running while waiting in the car restaurant lane, I go into the restaurant to buy coffee with the engine turned off. Generally speaking, idling the engine for 15 minutes consumes nearly one liter of gasoline, which means your breakfast coffee costs one dollar more."

Accelerate gently and brake less.

"In urban roads, I choose to accelerate smoothly, while many drivers commit a common mistake we often see on the road: stepping hard on the gas pedal at each traffic light and braking suddenly just before colliding with the car in front. Driving this way doesn't get you home faster, but smooth driving can improve fuel efficiency by about 20%."

Maintain the optimal speed.

"On highways, some drivers travel at a speed of 120 kilometers per hour, while I maintain a speed of 110 kilometers per hour. In daily commutes, a difference of dozens of kilometers per hour does not reduce travel time, but it can improve fuel efficiency on highways."

Use cruise control wisely.

"I try to maintain a constant speed while driving, whereas some drivers' speeds fluctuate greatly. Try to use cruise control as much as possible and maintain a constant speed, which is much more economical than continuously accelerating and decelerating."

Roll up the windows.

"Whether to use air conditioning while driving is one of the most common questions we encounter. Closing the air conditioning at low speeds indeed saves fuel, but on highways, I always keep the windows closed and the air conditioning on. Some drivers don't use air conditioning but keep the windows open, increasing air resistance like a parachute, which consumes more fuel than using air conditioning."

Tire pressure must not be overlooked.

"The tire pressure of my four tires is five pounds lower than the recommended value. This is not enough to change the performance of the Cruze, but it makes it harder for the engine to drive the wheels. Check the tires at least once a month, and if the tire pressure is ten pounds lower, fuel efficiency will decrease by more than three percent."

Try to avoid using external decorations.

"To show my support for my favorite team, I insert the team flag on my Cruze's window for every home game. I only take the flag down when they are away. For highway driving, up to one-third of the fuel is used to overcome wind resistance. So even slight changes in the car's aerodynamic performance can significantly affect fuel efficiency."

Lighten the trunk load.

"I always carry six large bags of softener bought during promotions in my trunk. According to the Environmental Protection Agency's estimate, every 100 pounds (about 45 kilograms) of weight reduces fuel efficiency by two percent. My 240-pound (about 109 kilograms) softener purchased to save money increased fuel costs by almost five percent."

"Concentrated" use of vehicles is more efficient.

"The continuous operation of the engine is fifty percent more efficient than restarting after cooling. Therefore, if possible, handling five errands in one afternoon is more fuel-efficient than making one trip each day for five days."

Six "Don'ts" for saving fuel while driving in summer:

Turning on the air conditioner early and turning it off late

In summer, many car owners turn on the air conditioner as soon as they start the engine. Here, I would like to remind everyone that doing so not only provides poor cooling effects but also increases the initial pressure on the engine. After getting in the car, be sure to open all the windows first, and then turn on the air conditioner when the temperature inside the car has noticeably dropped. For coolness, most people turn off the air conditioner just before turning off the engine, which is unscientific. It is suggested to turn off the A/C switch 1.5 kilometers before reaching the destination and select outdoor circulation to prevent the air conditioner from developing unpleasant odors.

Setting the air conditioner temperature as low as possible

In the scorching summer, fuel consumption rises significantly mainly due to the high usage rate of car air conditioners. Therefore, many students reduce the usage rate of air conditioners and improve their efficiency by setting the air conditioner temperature very low. Remember that when setting the temperature for automatic or semi-automatic air conditioners, try to avoid setting it to the lowest temperature. If set to the lowest temperature, the air conditioning pump will keep running, and the blower will be in a high gear position, increasing the car's load and failing to achieve the purpose of saving fuel. Generally, the temperature inside the car should be no more than 5°C lower than outside to ensure comfort upon exiting the car.

Turning off the stereo saves electricity and fuel

The use of electrical appliances in the car indeed consumes electricity, and the power consumption of devices like DVD audio-visual systems is quite significant. However, the power consumption of commonly used car stereos can be negligible, and it is unnecessary to believe that using the stereo consumes electricity and fuel. At the same time, it is necessary to warn those who have modified their cars that vehicles equipped with high-power electrical devices such as sound systems consume significantly more electricity than ordinary vehicles, and their electricity consumption ultimately translates into engine fuel consumption. Therefore, attention should be paid to the use of electrical appliances.

Opening the windows instead of using air conditioning

Despite the summer heat, many car enthusiasts still prefer not to use air conditioning if possible, allowing them to breathe fresh air and save fuel. However, they seem to overlook the impact of wind resistance on the vehicle. In fact, opening the windows undoubtedly increases fuel consumption to some extent, especially when the speed exceeds 80 km/h, where the extra fuel consumed by opening the windows is basically the same as using air conditioning. Additionally, the impact of the sunroof on vehicle fuel consumption is relatively small, but it should be avoided at high speeds.

Filling up the tank too little and too frequently

To save fuel, people have thought of all sorts of ways, including the idea of "eating less but more often." Some believe that reducing the car's weight can reduce fuel consumption, and the best way is to refuel less and more frequently. Actually, appropriately reducing the amount of fuel to lighten the car's weight is fine, but this "appropriate" amount is relative to the general concept of filling up the tank. It is absolutely not about filling the vehicle with only one-third or less fuel. Frequent refueling is not only unnecessary but also leads to incomplete combustion due to inconsistent fuel quality.

Believing higher air conditioning fan speed equals better cooling

The role of air conditioning in summer is crucial. To stay cool, many people set the air conditioning fan speed to the highest gear without realizing that a higher fan speed often fails to reach the displayed temperature. Here, it is suggested that after starting the car, wait for the temperature inside to drop before turning on the air conditioner and setting it to the highest gear. This not only quickly expels the remaining warm air but also cools down immediately.
