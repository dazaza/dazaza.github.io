Regardless of the former invincibility or today's plight, the "national derby" between Dalian and Shandong has always been fiery and genuine. Though they share the same roots, their rivalry is intense. The game will happen as expected without any need for mobilization. Will Dalian, riding on the spring breeze of their last-minute victory over Henan away from home, deliver another heavy blow to Luneng? Or will Luneng rise after a fall and sound the counterattack horn in the CSL? It's worth waiting to see.

In terms of strength, there is a world of difference between Dalian and Shandong. In terms of staff and equipment, there is no comparison. If both sides were cars, then Dalian would be a Chery QQ, while Shandong would at least be a Beetle. But every time these two meet, from the situation on the ground, the gap between the two teams is not obvious. Watching these young players from Dalian, it's as if they've all been injected with adrenaline, each one excited, full of youthful vigor and slowly radiating killing intent. It's unclear whether Rongcheng has psychological barriers due to being at a disadvantage in the history of confrontations between the two sides. (In the history of professional league encounters, the two teams have faced off 34 times, with Rongcheng winning 6, drawing 10, and losing 18.)

Looking just at the past two seasons, in 2009, Rongcheng, who had just experienced an AFC Champions League disaster, hosted Dalian. In a match where fans played the main role, the two teams drew in silence. In fact, judging solely by that game, the fans of Shandong indeed showed rare blood and spirit, but why doesn't Rongcheng fight back? This seems to have become a global problem. In recent years, Rongcheng's humiliating performances in the AFC Champions League have been too womanly. We hope that Rongcheng can perform well in domestic competitions.
