Primary school teacher who has been away from his post for more than 10 years to start business still receives salary

by makemsuper on 2011-11-24 09:22:20

Business Journal Graphics Xu Qiaowei On August 10, the "Interactive Report" section of our newspaper reported on Zhang Bin, a teacher at Tietai Primary School in Gelan Town, Changshou District. Despite being away from his post for over a decade to pursue business ventures, he was still drawing a "phantom salary" from the school. The day after the report was published, it was learned that Zhang Bin had sent a resignation letter to the school. Meanwhile, the Changshou District Education Commission also launched an investigation into the matter and will hold accountable those involved.

Principal: Admitted management oversight

"He said that once he feels better, he will return to complete the resignation procedures," Zhou Jinxian, principal of Tietai Primary School, told reporters yesterday. Currently, Zhang Bin has already sent a resignation application to the school and informed the principal of his intention to resign over the phone. When asked about the reason for Zhang's resignation, Principal Zhou said that Zhang Bin did not disclose any specific reasons. "I guess it might be because Zhang Bin's wife has a substantial income, and working here is relatively far," regarding the issue of phantom salaries, Principal Zhou admitted that there were indeed lapses in school management.

District Education Commission: Will hold those involved accountable

Changsha Jiang Chaopei, Director of the Personnel Department of the Changshou District Education Commission, told reporters yesterday that the Education Commission took this matter very seriously and had organized staff to investigate the incident. As of August 10, preliminary investigation results showed that the education management center in Gelan Town did not strictly manage Zhang Bin's sick leave. According to the provisions of the Changshou District Education Commission's document No. 238 [2010], after two months of sick leave, unless under special circumstances requiring further leave, long-term continuous leave is not allowed.

Currently, the Education Commission has decided to hold accountable those involved in this matter. In addition, the Education Commission will continue to investigate this matter and will handle it seriously based on the investigation results and relevant procedures.

Dialogue with the protagonist

Living expenses received for over a decade

On August 10, reporters finally managed to reach Zhang Bin by phone.

Reporter: You have been on sick leave for two years now, how is your condition?

Zhang Bin: In early 2009, I was diagnosed with liver cysts, and I am currently receiving treatment at Huaxi Hospital in Chengdu. By October this year, I will need another surgery.

Reporter: What do you think about the accusations online regarding your phantom salary?

Zhang Bin: In 2009, when the school called me back to resume work, I had gone through the reinstatement procedures, but I was hospitalized in Chengdu due to illness before returning to the classroom. Therefore, I have been continuously applying for leave, so every month I receive my sick pay.

Reporter: How much sick pay do you receive each month? Is it the same amount as what you received before 2009?

Zhang Bin: Now I receive 70% of my original salary. Before 2009, I had been away from my post for over a decade. Initially, I received a basic living allowance every month, later only during winter and summer vacations. However, I cannot remember the exact amounts anymore.

Reporter: How is your law firm in Dujiangyan doing? Who manages it during your illness?

Zhang Bin: Business is okay. Managing? I am undergoing intermittent treatment...

Reporter: I heard you are planning to resign, why?

Zhang Bin: I have already submitted my application and will go to process the paperwork within this month. (Suddenly becomes angry) After resignation, whether I go into business or beg for food, it is my own business!

In-depth analysis

Netizens point out that the "phantom salary phenomenon" only exposes the tip of the iceberg

After the exposure of the "phantom salary" report, it was reprinted by dozens of websites nationwide. Netizens believe that Zhang Bin's case, in the education sector, is just the tip of the iceberg of the "phantom salary phenomenon." Many netizens expressed that they personally know of similar cases where people dare not speak out against such practices. In fact, just this year alone, in February, media revealed that over a hundred teachers in Yongzhou, Hunan were receiving phantom salaries, causing a huge stir. But a month later, the "Sanya Teacher Phantom Salary Scandal" occurred again. Regarding the causes of the "phantom salary phenomenon," netizens also offered their own analyses.

Schools falsify payroll lists to save costs

Netizen "Tian Yan" said, the fundamental reason lies in schools having something to gain. Schools help conceal information about teachers because by falsifying payroll lists, they can still apply for funds and possibly even siphon off part of the profits.

Netizen "Lafier" believes that management should be assigned clear responsibilities. If something happens, there would be no room for mutual buck-passing, making it less likely for individual leaders to falsify rosters to defraud government fiscal funds.

Parties want to retain public positions

Netizen "Fengyun" said, actually many parties enjoy "retained position but suspended salary," they just hope to keep their public positions so they can return to their original units upon retirement.

Netizen "Datao" suggests issuing an "ID card" to all personnel on the payroll, detailing and registering basic information and category of employment, linking personnel to their positions.

Netizen "Jingzhang" said, schools bear undeniable responsibility. How could a teacher take sick leave for several years? Teachers who fall ill are supposed to be treated locally according to regulations and have medical insurance. It doesn't make sense for someone to seek treatment outside of their place of work in Chengdu instead of in Chongqing or Changshou. If this is acceptable, wouldn't every employee be able to avoid coming to work indefinitely with just a sick leave note?

Netizen "Jin Chan Chan" said, schools should fundamentally abolish "suspended salary but retained position," and conduct on-site investigations into teachers' conditions to prevent some teachers from disliking low wages, starting businesses elsewhere, and exploiting loopholes to draw phantom salaries.

School Criticizes Sick Leave Loopholes

If deliberately done, it involves suspected embezzlement of state property

How does the law define the "phantom salary phenomenon"? Professor Liu Xin of China University of Political Science and Law said that there is no specific penalty clause for this issue in the "Civil Servant Law." However, if this situation occurs, it is definitely due to negligence in school management. If responsible individuals engaged in such behavior to accept bribes (small amounts) or benefit their units, they can be held accountable for disciplinary actions; if the bribe amount reaches a criminal level, they will be held criminally liable.

Lawyer He Zhiqiang from Chongqing Yubo Legal Call Center said that first, it needs to be determined whether this was a mistake made by relevant school personnel or if it was intentional. If it was the former, then Zhang Bin would only be considered unjustly enriched, and returning the money would suffice; if relevant personnel knowingly continued to pay salaries to those who had left their posts, since Tietai Primary School in Changshou District is a public school funded by the state, both the relevant school personnel and Zhang Bin would be suspected of embezzling state property and should face corresponding penalties.
