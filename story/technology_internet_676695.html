Starting from 1957, all Zippo lighters have markings representing the manufacturing year engraved on the base, making it easier for collectors to identify them. Products before this period can only be identified by S serial numbers or design types. There are four types of marks representing the manufacturing year: (•) (used since 1957), () (used from 1966-1973), (/) (used from 1974-1981), and (\) (used from 1982-1986). Each mark was used for 8 years. These marks can appear up to 8 times, engraved on either side of the Zippo trademark (up to 4 on each side). They decrease gradually, with a minimum of 1 remaining.

3. Starting from 1986, in addition to the year, month markers were also added. We can also compare through actual use of genuine and counterfeit Zippo lighters.

1. Weight. Genuine Zippo lighters are generally lighter than imitations because counterfeit Zippo lighters are made of steel or iron materials, not Zippo-specific materials; some unscrupulous manufacturers increase the thickness to make the shell harder.

2. Check the flame and heat resistance. When counterfeit Zippo lighters burn, they emit blue smoke, the flame flickers, and after less than 4 minutes, the entire body catches fire. However, a genuine Zippo lighter continues burning until the fuel is exhausted without igniting the body. This is because the steel casing of counterfeit Zippo lighters is too thick, leading to poor heat dissipation; the oil-absorbing cotton core inside is of poor quality and prone to leaking oil.

3. Windproof capability. Place genuine and counterfeit Zippo lighters 40 cm away from an electric fan and turn it to maximum speed; the flame of the genuine Zippo lighter will not be extinguished. However, when the electric fan is set to its lowest speed, the counterfeit Zippo lighter is already extinguished. This is because the genuine Zippo lighter has a higher windproof wall; the air vent design is scientifically reasonable; and the dedicated wick absorbs oil well.
