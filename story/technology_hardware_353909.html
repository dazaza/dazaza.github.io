Free Dragon's Dogma Korean Server comprehensive information and tips!

by sdhyxcu5h on 2010-05-01 22:30:15

The Korean version of Dragon's Dogma can be simply understood as a 3D version of DNF (Dungeon and Fighter). Both Dungeon and Fighter (called DF in Korea) and Dragon's Dogma (called DN in Korea) are games developed by the South Korean company NEONX. These two games have many similarities in terms of currency collection. In terms of operability, DN is much higher than DF, requiring a high level of operational skills. The game is also divided according to fatigue levels and dungeons. If someone says this game imitates DNF, then they are greatly mistaken; this game and DNF are developed by the same company with almost identical names, DN and DF. Therefore, this game can be considered an evolved version of DNF. It is believed that after the Chinese server opens, it will attract a large number of DNF players. The difficulty of the game's dungeons is divided into many types, from the simplest Easy (simple difficulty) to the hardest Abyss (abyss difficulty). Among these, Easys can be cleared solo by anyone, even fools can do it. It's very simple but won't drop any equipment; even dropping a white piece of gear is difficult. Usually, only some junk stones are dropped. Generally, individuals clear Normal (medium difficulty), which usually drops white gear and occasionally green gear. For Hard (hard difficulty), it is recommended for two people; if your operation is extremely proficient, you can clear it solo. Early maps can be cleared solo, but not later ones. More green gear is dropped, and there's an extremely low chance of opening a box to get blue gear. For 2-4 people, it's recommended to clear Master (master difficulty), which has a higher difficulty with more monsters and thicker blood. A lot of green gear is dropped, and there's a medium chance of opening a golden treasure chest to get blue gear. For a four-person team, it's recommended to clear Abyss (abyss difficulty), where there are many strong monsters with thick blood. There are many purple monsters (drop blue gear) and bosses with many health bars and small bosses. In some maps, the final boss at abyss difficulty will have two instead of one, making it very challenging. On average, our team spends 2 revival coins per person to clear abyss difficulty once. Generally speaking, clearing abyss difficulty once will drop about one piece of blue equipment from the monsters (Note: Dropping blue gear or better requires rolling points. You must not roll randomly if it's not for your own class because the equipment cannot be traded after acquisition). With good luck, two or more pieces of blue gear may drop. If you encounter a big treasure chest, it can drop 2-5 pieces of blue gear. I've opened up to three pieces in my highest attempt. Over four days of playing, I've seen the big treasure chest three times. The final evaluation score for clearing abyss difficulty is generally A, S, SS, SSS, and usually opens golden and silver treasure chests. The golden treasure chest definitely contains one blue item, though whether it suits your class or is just blue material depends on luck. With good luck, you might open purple gear or blue gear... Some people in our group claim to have opened yellow gear!!!! Silver treasure chests usually contain green items, copper treasure chests contain white gear. With good luck, you might open a green item, but with bad luck, you'll get junk materials or stones. Iron treasure chests generally contain junk stones. Even with good luck, you still get stones, and with bad luck, still stones.

Regarding Dragon's Dogma tasks: They aren't too numerous. Usually, after accepting a task, you just complete it to level up, and the tasks clear very quickly. At each dungeon entrance, there's a signboard where you can typically accept six tasks. The first task usually involves clearing the dungeon once. The second to fifth tasks, since I don't fully understand Korean, involve collecting items inside the dungeon, gathering resources, or clearing the dungeon. The sixth task is generally clearing an abyss difficulty dungeon once. Each task gives abundant experience, so completing all tasks is definitely beneficial!

In Dragon's Dogma, SP (Skill Points) for learning JN (Job Skills) is quite tight. You gain 20 SP per level-up, and SP-related tasks are few. From level 1 to 16, tasks have only given me two bottles of 5SP potion. In the early stages (level 1-15), learning a JN requires 10-15 SP, and upgrading a JN requires 3-6 SP. During this period, you can add points freely as points will reset automatically after the job change at level 15. By level 18, you'll notice the shortage of SP. In the middle stage (level 15-20), learning a JN usually requires 20 SP, and leveling it up requires 6-8 SP. In the mid-late stage (level 20-35), learning a JN usually requires around 48 SP (and probably more than 6 SP per level). In the late stage (level 30-45), learning a JN requires 50-100 SP (the last JN at level 45 requires 100 SP... Each level only gives 20 SP... and it's uncertain how many points are needed now, but definitely more than 6 SP!). In Dragon's Dogma, there are many second-job JNs, about 20 in total. First-job JNs are roughly 10. Many first-job skills are basic and need to be leveled up slightly higher. My second-job JNs are divided into four major categories, and it's impossible to master all four due to insufficient SP. Considering each level only gives 20 SP, learning a second-job JN after level 20 requires over 48 SP, and leveling it up consumes a lot of SP. Given the vast number of JNs in Dragon's Dogma, point distribution should be carefully considered!

Dragon's Dogma has a mail and trading system. Trading occurs through an exchange, but for unknown reasons, it seems you can only sell six items per week at the exchange. Buying is unrestricted. Due to this limitation, I created a secondary account to send items via mail. Another frustrating issue occurred: mails are limited to five sends, either daily or weekly (this applies to adult accounts, not children's accounts).

Dragon's Dogma has a fatigue system. Every day, you receive 600 points of PT (Player Time), also known as PL. Players familiar with DNF might think 600 points would last forever, but they're wrong. PL in Dragon's Dogma depletes quickly; clearing a map generally costs 95 PL, meaning 600 PL allows for only seven clears. Each clear takes about ten minutes. On Saturdays, an additional 1200 weekly PT is granted, totaling 1800 PL. This extra 1200 PL can be used throughout the week if not finished on Saturday. Korean internet cafes grant an additional 300 PL daily (all Korean discounts apply in internet cafes, unlike certain brain-damaged systems like "Black Diamond"). Korean DNF internet cafes offer over 220 PL. Since the Chinese version is operated by Shanda, it will likely introduce VIP systems.

In the PVP system of Dragon's Dogma, I find there is significant imbalance between classes, especially with the Swordsman. Every time I fight him, I get chained to the ground repeatedly. His knockback seems high. Moreover, before level 10, the Swordsman is somewhat sluggish, but after level 10, he becomes incredibly fast, running swiftly and catching enemies rapidly - like a magpie! The lowest PVP mode is 1v1, and the highest is 8v8. 8v8 might cause slight lag on small maps. In 1v1, I feel the Swordsman is the strongest, followed by the Priest. The Priest's cross can electrify enemies, causing knockback and chain effects, though its range is somewhat limited. In single PVP, I feel the Mage is the weakest. In group PVP, the key is relying on rear firepower while front-line Warriors and Priests harass the enemy. Rear Mages and Archers operate to win, and it's important to protect the safety of rear Archers and Mages.
