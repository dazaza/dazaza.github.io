Qing'er, would you love me?

One

The Great Song Dynasty, I am a female thief who can climb walls and walk on roofs.

I am thin and live in a small house made of straw and green bamboo in the bamboo forest outside the city. Eighteen years ago, my master found me in a pile of grass under a willow tree by the river and raised me to adulthood, naming me Liu Qing'er. Now, I am alone, with no attachments, and only my sharp sword keeps me company.

I am a thief, but I never steal from ordinary people.

I sit on the second floor of a teahouse, drinking tea and enjoying the view, watching a white-robed scholar selling calligraphy and paintings on the street opposite. No one stops at his stall all day. He is neither anxious nor restless, his gentle nature like the spring breeze. Finally, at dusk, he paints a fan for someone, and I am the owner of that jade bone fan. He hands me the painted fan with both hands and says thank you, sir, then gives me a pale smile. Yes, I appear as a man, which makes it more convenient for me to move around. After he packs up, I follow him and see him exchange the silver coins I gave him for half a bag of rice, then return to a dilapidated house where he cooks a large pot of congee to feed the old and young in the yard.

Although the house is dilapidated, it has a beautiful and elegant name, Luo Ya Zhai.

This is a kind poor scholar. I laugh inside.

That night, I wore a tight-fitting black outfit, climbed over the wall into a wealthy family's home, filled a whole bag with silver, then climbed out again unnoticed, without anyone knowing. When I left, I left a willow branch as usual, inserting it into a blue-and-white porcelain vase on the table. I have visited many corrupt officials and rich merchants' mansions. They are frightened by my thefts and spend a lot of money hiring many skilled warriors to guard their homes. I am as light as a swallow, still coming and going freely. They cannot see my shadow, they do not even know whether I am male or female, they only know I have been there after seeing the willow branch.

Even Zhao Huian, the famous detective known as the "God Detective" in the police station, is helpless against me. Once, I left a letter in his room saying that I would steal the pearl Emperor had given to Mr. Song at the third watch of the night. I hid on the roof beam, wanting to see what expression was on his face after I left. At first, he stared at my willow branch and began scratching his head, then became very angry. He said, Mr. Liu, wait for me, I will definitely catch you. There was a "Liu" carved on the willow branch.

I covered my mouth and started to giggle quietly; his appearance was quite adorable. Suddenly, he looked up towards the roof beam, his eyebrows like swords and eyes like stars, his nose straight and lips thin, not the appearance of a rough warrior. In that instant, I saw the fierce light in his eyes and my heart trembled, skipping several beats.

I give the gold and silver I have stolen to those poor and suffering commoners, throwing the money into their yards from outside the wall. The next morning, they will find it and kneel down, thanking heaven for granting them wealth. Later, some people began to spread the rumor that there is a mysterious person robbing the rich to help the poor, secretly helping the common people, and each time leaving a willow branch. They all call me Mr. Liu. I laugh foolishly, Mr. Liu, sounds pretty good.

Two

I changed into a dark green women's outfit, dressed as a well-bred young lady, and even specially applied rouge. The person in the mirror smiled charmingly, her willow-like eyebrows delicate, her teeth white and her lips red, the previous plain-looking man disappearing completely. I carried a basket of pastries and went to Luo Ya Zhai. Perhaps because of my sudden appearance, they were initially very afraid of me. I shared the food with them and also gave them some silver. They were so hungry that they grabbed the steamed buns and ate them greedily. They called me the Green-clad Fairy and bowed to me.

I left before the scholar returned, not wanting him to see me. Then I started wandering around the streets. Suddenly, a dirty child collided with me when I wasn't paying attention, and someone nearby helped me steady myself. It was Zhao Huian. My face immediately turned serious, and I gripped the dagger hidden in my sleeve tightly.

Young lady, are you alright? He looked at me, his face actually turning red. I looked down at my green embroidered shoes and couldn't help but laugh silently.

I touched my waist and realized my money pouch was gone. I looked up at him and shrugged helplessly.

Let me retrieve it for you, please wait here.

No, it's not necessary... Before I could finish speaking, he had already run off. I stood there dumbfounded, remembering his dazed expression when he looked at me, unable to suppress a chuckle.

A stick of incense's worth of time passed, and I was getting impatient. I thought, if he can't even catch a child, no wonder he can't catch me. Just as I was about to leave, I saw him running towards me, panting and waving his hand.

Miss Liu, is this yours?

My smile immediately froze. How do you know my surname is Liu?

He chuckled and scratched his hair, saying, Miss, there's no need to be nervous, because of this. I took the money pouch and secretly sighed in relief. Originally, there was an elegant 'Liu' embroidered on the pouch. My master said that women should be skillful and clever, teaching me not only to have the chivalrous demeanor of a woman traveling through the world, but also the inner beauty of a woman secluded in a chamber. Therefore, I not only inherited my master's swordsmanship but also learned embroidery.

Thank you so much, Inspector Zhao. I gave him a radiant smile and turned to leave.

Miss! He called out from behind.

I turned around, Is there anything else?

He stepped forward, his eyes suddenly becoming cunning. May I ask, how did you know my surname is Zhao?

I covered my mouth and giggled secretly, my money pouch has a 'Liu', and your face has 'Zhao Huian' carved on it.

This...

I walked far away, looking back to see him still standing there, puzzled. Who doesn't know the famous Inspector Zhao? Goodbye, Inspector Zhao!

Seeing his handsome yet cute appearance, my heart suddenly started pounding. Especially those bright eyes that made me, usually calm, suddenly lose my composure.

Passing by the place where the white-robed scholar used to set up his stall, today there was a coarse-clothed man selling kites instead. I glanced hastily and didn't stop. Because of the earlier tension, I didn't notice the sharp, eagle-like eyes behind me. I thought I was no longer suitable to be a thief. My master said, if you are distracted, then you can no longer steal, otherwise your life will be in danger.

Three

Half a month later, at dusk, I was practicing swordsmanship in the bamboo forest. My Qing Feng Sword is incredibly sharp, cutting hair like mud, but I have never taken it out. A sword is a deadly weapon, an instrument of death, and my livelihood is not killing.

My master said, Qing'er, you are ultimately a kind-hearted woman.

Isn't being kind good?

My master smiled but remained silent, holding my hand. My master was not old, still having a bewitching charm when she glanced back, just with two extra brilliant flowers at the corners of her eyes. Though her hands were full of calluses and no longer as delicate as spring orchids, they were still strong. She said these were formed from daily practice of swordsmanship in her youth. My master also said that everyone is destined to sacrifice everything for another person. Who sacrifices for whom depends on who falls in love first. When she said this, the corners of her eyes and eyebrows seemed filled with love, and also hate.

I understood what my master meant. My master was the one who fell in love first.

My master left a year ago, saying she had important matters to attend to and asked me to wait here for someone. Before leaving, she gave me her Qing Feng Sword, saying I would need it someday. I said, Master, I don't kill people.

She still smiled, didn't say anything else, and then disappeared into the endless night. There was no star or moon that night, and my surroundings instantly turned pitch black. I was a bit scared, trembling slightly, but I held back my tears. From then on, I was alone, starting to live independently and engaging in the business of robbing the rich to help the poor.

When the moon came out, I returned to my little bamboo house. A person was standing with their back to me at the entrance. It had been days since I last saw him, but he was still in his white robes, refined and courteous.

It was the scholar selling paintings. Oh no, it should be Su Yanan, the master of Bai Yan Tang.

So, he was the person my master wanted me to wait for. And my master, was actually the previous master of Bai Yan Tang. I knew my master was not an ordinary person, but I never expected her to be the true mastermind behind Bai Yan Tang, the formidable assassination organization in the martial arts world despite its elegant name.

I know my life will undergo a complete change. Perhaps, my life has never been under my own control. Or perhaps, I am a pawn, predetermined from the start.

In the moonless night, scaling walls and hanging from beams. I became a thief again because Su Yanan said, Qing'er, your task is to steal the imperial gift, the blue horse, from Mr. Wang's study. I said I don't steal from loyal officials and honest citizens. But he said it was Master's wish. Before leaving, he specifically reminded me, Master wants you to complete the mission. I know, he is reminding me that Master saved my life, and I must not disobey.

I know Mr. Wang is a loyal official, not a villain. He cares for the people and dares to speak out frankly. Whether in the court or among the common people, he is highly respected. So I hung on the beam in his study for a long time without making a move. Until I remembered Su Yanan's words and realized the great kindness Master had shown me in raising me, so I descended from the beam. But I never imagined, it was all a trap.

There were two pieces of paper on the desk. One read: You can't escape. The other said: Tonight in Mr. Wang's study, the imperial gift, the jade horse. Signed with the single character Liu.

I was greatly shocked, realizing I had fallen into a trap. No wonder there were only a few guards when I sneaked into the prince's residence; they had known I would come.

Just as I was about to leave, I saw bright lights outside and the sound of drawn swords. Zhao Huian's voice came, Listen up, you've been surrounded, you can't escape! I regretted my carelessness deeply but was unwilling to be caught like a fish in a barrel. I leaped out, determined to fight to the death.

Ms. Liu, today you are truly doomed, surrender now!

If you want to catch me, you'll have to show your skills.

Indeed, Zhao Huian's martial arts were extraordinary. After a few moves, I was no match for him, especially since he had a weapon while I only had a jade bone fan to defend myself. Looking at his serious expression, I suddenly felt that dying at his hands would be acceptable. A stabbing pain in my chest - it was his sword. I let out a muffled groan as warm red liquid flowed out, but I bit my lip and stayed silent.

I knew I was doomed, so I closed my eyes, tears streaming down my cheeks.

Have we met before? Your eyes seem familiar to me.

I suddenly became happy, he actually remembered me. The world quieted down, no wind moved the grass, no swords clashed, only the round bright moon, me, and him.

I didn't know how to answer, but heard the guards shouting from the neighboring courtyard, "Catch the assassin!" Everyone was stunned for a moment, then chaos ensued. I took the opportunity to leap onto the roof and managed to escape. As I looked back one last time, he was looking at me too, his eyes filled with confusion, but I felt content. My life or death is in heaven's hands, I don't know if I will ever see him again. Clutching my chest, I didn't run far, but was severely injured by Zhao Huian's sword, losing too much blood, and fainted.

Four

When I woke up, I saw Su Yanan's haggard face and sunken eye sockets. I tried to get up but was weak and powerless, my chest still hurting. He said, Miss, you've finally woken up.

We faced each other coldly. At this moment, I urgently needed an explanation.

Sorry, Qing'er. I deceived you. My mother passed away half a month ago due to a severe illness.

Your mother?

Yes, your master is also my mother. She said before she passed that there would be a woman who would replace her and help me, asking me to find you. Qing'er, this was Mother's plan. She said, you wouldn't become a killer as she imagined, because you are inherently kind. But, you can help me divert people's attention...

So, you sent the message to Mr. Wang's residence in my name, right?

Yes.

So your assassination attempt would be easier, right?

Qing'er...

So, I was just a pawn. Do you know, Mr. Wang was an innocent and loyal official. For profit, how could you recklessly kill innocents!

I had no choice either. Moreover, Mr. Wang was the first person I killed. Because he intended to accuse my father in the emperor's court, placing my father in mortal peril.

Su Chengqin was the one my master loved. Over the years, his career has been smooth sailing, all thanks to Bai Yan Tang and my master who risked her life for him. And my master, was known as Blood Swallow in the martial arts world, responsible for countless deaths of loyal officials and civilians. Due to setbacks in his career, Su Chengqin refused to marry my master, thus establishing Bai Yan Tang. Eventually, my master married Su Chengqin as she wished. But from then on, she no longer smiled, continuous killings had already shattered her heart, so she chose to escape, rescuing me as an infant. However, Master, Mr. Wang's death is related to me, how can I face the common people, and how can I bear the torment within my heart.

Moreover, if I had known that Zhao Huian would injure you so badly, how could I have asked you to take such a risk...

I turned away, tears streaming down my face. Zhao Huian, if your sword had pierced deeper, I wouldn't have to endure all this pain now. Su Yanan, how can I trust your words anymore. And you, how long do you intend to deceive me.

Qing'er, there's something I must tell you, and it's sincere.

Please, marry me.

Five

The sunset was like blood.

I slowly removed my veil and turned around.

Zhao Huian wasn't too surprised. He said, Miss, I guessed it was you.

I smiled faintly. Zhao Huian was imprisoned for failing to protect Mr. Wang and further inflamed by another inspector who had always been overshadowed by him. He was sentenced to exile. Knowing he was innocent, and because of that sword strike and those words, I risked saving him.

Thank you, Miss Liu, for saving my life. I, Zhao Huian, will never forget this debt.

No need for thanks, consider it my way of atoning for the sins against the common people.

Miss, the common people will never forget your great kindness.

You, do you know...

Yes, the 'Liu' on the money pouch and the handwriting on the note are exactly the same, but at that time I wasn't sure. Miss, do you know, after meeting you on the street that day, I couldn't stop thinking about you, especially your eyes.

So, that was why he questioned me that night.

Miss, that strike, it was my fault, Huian felt as if he struck himself.

No, that strike, Qing'er will never forget it...

I decided to leave, to depart from this place full of deceit and falsehood. In the drizzling rain, I couldn't see anything behind me. I strained my eyes but it was all in vain. Well, well, all the scenery here has already been etched in my mind, and I will remember the people here for the rest of my life.

Perhaps my life has been full of mistakes. My master shouldn't have rescued and raised me, shouldn't have bestowed upon me the grace of rebirth. And I shouldn't have harbored resentment towards that white-robed man with a smile as gentle as jade who promised to marry me. He was also helpless, for us, he could only do so much. I understand his inherent kindness, proven by the Luoya House.

That day when he promised to marry me, I said, can I avenge Mr. Wang? His smile spread wide, like sunlight streaming through the window lattice, blinding me.

He said, yes, as long as it makes you happy.

Thus, I used all my strength to stab him, using the Qing Feng Sword.

At Master's grave, I buried the Qing Feng Sword. I bowed repeatedly, the grace of upbringing that Qing'er received from Master can only be repaid in the next life. Qing Feng Sword, Qing'er returns it. The debt owed by Master, Qing'er shall repay.

Postscript

Here, there are bright springs and rain falling like crowns, but without her, everything is lifeless. The willows beyond the fence sway gracefully, just like her waist.

That day, in the misty rain, I watched her figure, blurring my vision.

That stubborn and kind woman, chose to leave.

Qing'er, do you know, I met you before selling paintings beneath the teahouse. Do you know, the green figure became the most beautiful scenery of my life. My life may be full of mistakes, my mistake lies in loving you. However, fate plays tricks, if it weren't for our predetermined roles, Qing'er, would you love me?

This life is hard to settle, hard to predict!

We can't bear too much pain. So let's try to forget. The world is still as beautiful as ever. As long as we keep gratitude in our hearts. Look at the world's prosperity. Paintings. Flowers.
