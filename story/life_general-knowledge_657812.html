Gushing Spring

I think perhaps I could also use such a sentence in the first line of my story: A few years before this great drought arrived, he would not have thought that when he realized her existence, she had already quietly melted into his life. Her traces were everywhere, yet her tracks were unattainable.

Yes, this is the story of Chi and Quan.

"Before the great drought came," Chi looked at the white clouds floating across the sky and said leisurely, "I never knew what sorrow was." This was the first sentence of a story he repeated every year after the great drought. The fish in the pool had gone through generation after generation, and now the grandchildren's little fish gathered around the water plants by the edge of the pool to listen to this story. The various species of fish from the older generations listened to this story with fresh interest until they became bored, and later even felt heartache. And now the grandchildren's fish still filled with fresh curiosity just like their ancestors did back then. Would they also go through the same process as their ancestors? The old fish blew bubbles and swam away with sighs - so many years have passed, but he still couldn't forget, couldn't let go, and couldn't stop telling this one story.

Back then, he could only be described as "ignorant youth". He was a small pond near a village on the North China Plain. Beside him was a group of locust trees, and beyond that was knee-high wild grass, rarely visited by people.

He thought he was the king of this land, enjoying boundless happiness of spring flowers and autumn moons. Especially in summer, when the rain was abundant, his reach would extend through the small ditches to the small hill on the other side of the village, listening to the frogs come back and say that there was already a lush scene there. He was wildly happy. Why not? Around here, he was the biggest pool. Locust flowers sprinkled fragrance onto his water surface - he had always been so full and strong. Even the fish returning home said that his water plants were the most lush. Even in some summers, swans would rest here, how could he not be proud? There was never anything that made him feel pain, even in the cold winter, when his water was dormant, he still had the comfort of snow. His water was always full, which was the greatest proof of his abundance and strength. He was arrogant, he was happy, he saw nothing else.

So, before the great drought came, he would never know about her hiding in the corner.

She was a small spring nestled in one corner of him. All year round, she gently poured out a thin stream to him. So light, so light, that even when he tried hard to recall, he still could not remember her traces at the time. She knew everything about him. His drowsy eyes in spring, his arrogance in summer, his calmness and slight sadness in autumn, and in winter, his inability to withstand loneliness and his endless questioning of the fish hiding at the bottom of the water about when the spring breeze would return. She smiled faintly, watching and listening - that was enough - still gently pouring out the stream, every drop was her confession to him. She didn't know how much energy she had stored up, she was just quietly speaking, watching his happiness, and she was happy too. Maybe one day, he would discover it? But she didn't dare to hope that his gaze would turn towards the clouds in the sky, or towards the birds or weeds by the pool, but never towards her secluded corner. She hid behind the water plants, only the red little carp knew her heart. The little carp said, why don't you tell him? You deeply love him but hide from him? "Shh...", she whispered softly, "I love him, so I gave my life to him. But only when he truly understands me and gives me his attention will I tell him. If I don't have the strength to wait for that day, I'll have to borrow your tears to tell him. But who knows? That day shouldn't be far away." When she said this, anticipation and joy flowed in Quan's eyes.

"Who would have known that her words really came true?" The little carp that survived the great drought had become an old carp, whenever Chi told the story of Quan, warm tears would flow from the old carp's eyes. "Only I know your sorrow." Chi would say to the old carp at this time. "No, it's her sorrow." The old carp's warm tears were like her former confessions, gushing drop by drop, scalding his heart.

In the year when Quan was eagerly awaiting his gaze, the terrible great drought began. The heavy rains of summer and the drizzles of autumn were no more, replaced by days on end of sunny skies. At first, he was carefree, sunny skies meant that many friends from afar would come here, and he liked the hustle and bustle. More and more animals came to drink water. However, when the news brought by visitors from afar was not joy but the restless news of the drought, he became genuinely worried. Listening to the mountain chickens from the small hill say that the mountains had turned yellow, the leaves were almost at the point of catching fire. That night, Chi saw the red glow from the direction of the village, the mountain was really on fire. That night, Chi heard the painful cries of animals coming from the mountain. Black ashes floated with the wind, falling onto the water surface of the pool, making Chi, who used to be clean, look like a stagnant pool. Restlessness and unease filled Chi's world. Chi remembered the words of the mountain chicken during the day: "Still your power is great, all the nearby pools have dried up, but your water can still last for a long time." His uneasy mood immediately cleared up, "Don't worry, I will protect you all well." He said to the water plants and fishes. His arrogance did not allow him to say discouraging words.

"How foolish I was back then, how could I not think of her? Don't I know how much energy I actually have? It was just that the stubbornness and competitiveness overcame doubts and reason, and I still didn't find her presence." Every time he spoke to this point, Chi's heart couldn't stay calm, ripples would spread on the water surface. Chi said that he wanted to send his love and thoughts to her, layer by layer, circle by circle. The old carp sighed beside him.

At that time, she was as stubborn, always refusing to let the little carp tell Chi the truth. During the days of the great drought, Quan was using her life to preserve Chi. But the number of animals coming to drink water every day was just too much, and the duration of the drought was too long, even if Quan exhausted all her life force, she could not continue.

The painful moment was finally going to arrive. Chi's water level dropped to the position where Quan was, slightly lower than the small spring eye. Chi always remembered that it was a deep night in early autumn, all the animals looking for water had dispersed, the dusty moments had already stagnated. In that quiet moment, Chi heard Quan's weak breathing. "Who are you?" Chi's tired voice seemed to give the thread of weak breathing some power. Before Quan finished spouting her last stream of water, she said to Chi:

Chi, I am a small spring hidden in the corner of your life... Now ask nothing, say nothing, just listen to me... I thought I would wait for you to discover me, staring at me... saying... I love you... But now you must forgive me, I cannot wait for that moment... I can no longer use my life to love and protect you... Even if it's just quietly watching you... If there was no you in my past life, how lonely my four seasons would be... But, Chi, I love you... Even if now I can only use my last strength to look at you... Nothing has ever stopped me from loving you with my whole life... Dear Chi, even if I disappear, my love will turn into the tears of the fish flowing into your heart... Turning into timely rain after a long drought to approach you, remember... I love you...

The moment Quan's breath disappeared, it started pouring rain.

Chi lived on. Quan disappeared.

"How could this happen?" "What happened later?" "Yeah yeah, what happened later?" The little fish gathered together and chattered noisily. Chi started to spread his worries again, ripples layer by layer, circle by circle. "Later?" he mused.

Later, Chi's heart was wet. The little carp that survived in the last bit of dirty water shed tears - that was the first time Chi's heart was burned. Chi's ripples only spread to one place - the direction of the former small spring.

Every year thereafter, ripples spread, burns.
