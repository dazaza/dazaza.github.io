"Remy Martin" was founded in 1724 by its visionary founder, Remy Martin, who had the belief of providing the best cognac in the world and finally realized his ultimate dream. The noble Centaur constellation logo symbolizes the company's commitment to the highest quality, the finest soil, traditional and reliable methods, and exquisite distillation techniques to meet the prestigious demands of brandy connoisseurs around the world. To this day, the Remy Martin family is dedicated to preserving its tradition of producing Extraordinary Champagne Cognac, ensuring that Remy Martin cognac continues to enjoy widespread renown and holds an important position in the international premium cognac market.

It is well known that Remy Martin only uses grapes from two of the finest grape-growing regions in the heart of Cognac - Grande Champagne and Petite Champagne - to produce cognac. And they have long signed agreements with grape growers in these two regions to ensure a steady supply of high-quality grapes. Although Remy Martin's distillation methods can be imitated, the "eaux-de-vie" (the clear grape juice obtained after double distillation) created by the superior soil and geographical characteristics of these regions cannot be replicated or exported.

By the end of the 19th century, Andre Renaud, a lawyer born into a wealthy grape-growing family and partner of the Remy Martin family, realized that to ensure the quality of cognac, national legislation must be enacted to stipulate that only cognac produced in designated areas is legitimate. Ten years later (in 1909), the French government issued the "Appellation d'Origine" decree. This decree centered on the town of Cognac, located 100 kilometers north of Bordeaux, dividing the Cognac region into six main production areas. After evaluation by wine experts, it was determined that cognac produced in the Grande and Petite Champagne zones, which make up 9% of the entire Cognac region, is of the highest quality. Therefore, according to the law, only cognac made by blending spirits from the Grande and Petite Champagne zones can be called Extraordinary Champagne Cognac, with at least 50% of the spirit coming from the Grande Champagne zone. This title is legally defined, and no wine merchant can adopt it arbitrarily. So far, all of Remy Martin's products carry this title. This honor belongs exclusively to brandy or more precisely to "Remy Martin."
