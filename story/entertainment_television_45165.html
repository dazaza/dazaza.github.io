Introduction to the Episodes of The Northeast Passage

Episode 50

Chuanjie wrote a complaint against Morita Industries. Zhukai Shan asked Chuanwen again, but he still refused to admit it, causing Zhukai Shan to faint. Xiu'er and Ichiro arrived, seeing Zhukai Shan bedridden, Xiu'er turned her back on Ichiro completely.

Director Yao found out the truth about Ichiro's business through connections. Zhukai Shan called Chuanwen, finally forcing him to tell the truth, which made Zhukai Shan so angry that he fainted.

Ichiro came to Zhukai Shan's bedside, and on his deathbed, Zhukai Shan still acknowledged Ichiro as his fourth son. Overcome with guilt, Ichiro returned to the company to retrieve evidence of his accepting funds from Morita Industries.

Episode 51

Chuanwu rushed home; Zhukai Shan, in his final moments, did not forget to urge Chuanwu to avenge the family. Enraged, Chuanwu beat Chuanwen and drove Ichiro away when he came to deliver evidence.

Chuanjie discovered that what Ichiro had delivered was crucial evidence to win the case. When Xiu'er, Chuanwu, and Chuanjie arrived at Ichiro's residence, they found that Ichiro had already committed suicide by gunshot, filled with regret in his will.

On September 18, 1931, the Japanese Kwantung Army launched an attack on the Northeast Army, and upon hearing the news, Morita was overjoyed. Zhukai Shan woke up at dawn, trying to strangle Chuanwen who was resting at his bedside, forcing Chuanwen to leave home and seek refuge with Morita.

Chuanwu commanded the defense of Shuangcheng. Xian'er suddenly arrived with the brothers of Er Long Mountain, supporting Chuanwu, resulting in a great victory for the Northeast Army. However, the counterattacking Kwantung Army forced the Northeast Army to retreat to Harbin.

Zhukai Shan took his grandson Shengzi to participate in the trial at the East Province court. Although Morita lost the lawsuit, he was dissatisfied with the verdict and caused a disturbance in the courtroom.

Episode 52

Chuanwen secretly called Na Wen from Morita's place, intending to bring Na Wen and his son Shengzi over. Unexpectedly, upon meeting, Na Wen tried to stab Chuanwen, but Chuanwen luckily dodged the attack.

The Zhu family brought food to the front lines to console the Northeast Army, even Yushu, who was pregnant, came along. Wen's mother met Xian'er, who was exhausted. Chuanwu proposed blowing up the Shanhe Mine, saying it should never fall into enemy hands. A war correspondent took a family portrait of the Zhu family, with only Chuanwen missing.

The sound of gunfire grew closer, and Yushu went into labor, with all the women in the house busy helping. The battle for the defense of Harbin began. Leading the Northeast Army, Chuanwu fought desperately, and Laosi from Er Long Mountain heroically sacrificed himself.

To stop the Kwantung Army's armored vehicles, Chuanwu personally took a bomb pack onto the battlefield. With a loud explosion, the armored vehicle was destroyed, and Chuanwu fell in a pool of blood.

Yushu's child was born, but Xian'er carried Chuanwu's body back home, plunging the Zhu family, who had just felt joy, into deep sorrow.

Chuanwen brought Morita and Japanese soldiers to the Zhu family. Morita forced Zhukai Shan to surrender to the Japanese. At a critical moment, Xian'er killed the Japanese soldiers with dual pistols.

Upon hearing the news of Chuanwu's death, Chuanwen shot and injured Morita with Chuanwu's pistol before his death. In the final moments, Zhukai Shan ended Morita's life with a dart.
