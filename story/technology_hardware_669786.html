The Education Bureau (www.jdmy88888.com) stated that the cheats in education reform require parents' support.

by vhfi7555 on 2012-02-20 11:07:25

Education Bureau: Whether the educational reform is implemented needs the support of students and parents. "Schools have not experimented in one or a few classes; if the results of the experiment show that the experimental class's performance does not decline compared to normal classes, but instead improves, I believe the parents will approve." A relevant official from the Basic Education Department expressed that it is also difficult to find classes for experiments; if a class is selected and the parents of that class do not agree, there is nothing that can be done.

Regarding some parents who say that since 2009, when You Jianzhong began small-scale educational reform experiments at Changshu Foreign Language School (Chang Wai), the school’s teaching quality has declined, widening the gap with Jiangsu Changzhou Middle School (Jiang Zhong) and Changzhou Middle School (Shi Zhong). A relevant official from the Basic Education Department of Changzhou Municipal Education Bureau stated that the reason for the fluctuation in results is due to objective factors causing a certain degree of diversion of excellent students from Chang Wai. "Before Chang Wai was converted into a public school, it was a six-year integrated public-private junior and senior high school where junior high students could directly advance to senior high, allowing early admissions for junior high. After becoming a public school, junior and senior high were separated, and this policy no longer exists." He said that in recent years, Kun Cheng Middle School, a newly established private junior high school in Changzhou, has diverted part of Chang Wai's quality student base. Affected by the student source, the academic performance of some students advancing directly from junior to senior high is not ideal.

You Jianzhong

Not Feeling Discouraged

Currently, Chanshu City has a total of seven high schools (two of which are comprehensive high schools) and 34 junior high schools.

Like Jiangsu Changzhou Middle School and Changzhou Middle School, Chang Wai is one of the three four-star general high schools in Chanshu; it is also a national foreign language experimental school and a vice chairman unit of the National Foreign Language Characteristic Education Research Association. In Jiangsu, or even nationwide, schools that generally carry out large-scale educational reforms often have relatively weak educational foundations, so-called "change out of necessity." It is hard to imagine someone implementing educational reform in a prestigious school.

Because once it fails, the pressure and criticism received are unimaginable. However, for You Jianzhong, these pressures are not an issue; his personality is such that once he sets his mind on something, he goes ahead and does it.

Unlike what might be imagined, although the educational reform has been halted and met with initial failure after only a day, You Jianzhong does not seem particularly discouraged. This internet user's nemesis and offline parent's adversary does not seem to care about his current situation, "If it stops, then it stops; I can continue my research. Theoretically, there is always freedom without resistance." In his view, including his own educational reform, education reform is a great cause. He believes that in the broader sense, within the next five to ten years, Chinese education will change.

Speaking of educational reform, You Jianzhong said he has been conducting classroom research for more than ten years. Over a decade ago, while serving as principal at Mocheng Middle School in Changshu, he began exploring classroom teaching reform. At the time, he noticed that classrooms were always dominated by teachers lecturing endlessly while students passively listened. He felt this was not a good phenomenon; in his view, students are the main subjects of the classroom, and their initiative must be stimulated. From 1998 to 2000, You Jianzhong studied under Zhang Xiongfei, a professor at Shaanxi Normal University who specialized in induced thinking exploration experiments, and Mocheng Middle School became one of Zhang Xiongfei's experimental bases. Afterwards, he worked in the teaching research office of the Changshu Education Bureau for six years, during which time he attended many classes and studied numerous reports. He found that many teachers merely treated students as containers, filling them with knowledge without considering them as living beings, respecting their individuality and personality.

This failed or prematurely terminated educational reform was called the "Life Classroom," and You Jianzhong said he started having this idea in 2004, deeply inspired by Professor Ye Lan of East China Normal University's concept of "making the classroom full of life vitality."

In 2006, he came to Chang Wai as vice principal, and two years later, he was promoted to principal. Thus, his originally theoretical "Life Classroom" had a practical opportunity. "In 2009, we began implementation, mainly focusing on changing the teachers' perspectives; in 2010, we proposed three main lines: passion interaction, induced thinking exploration, and successful experience." You Jianzhong introduced that in 2011, he began establishing the model for the "Life Classroom." To broaden horizons, he took teachers to visit successful educational reform schools like Dulongkou Middle School in Shandong, Canglang No. 2 Middle School, Xinjiang Middle School in Shanxi, and Tanghu Middle School in Sichuan. Subsequently, they summarized five key steps in teaching: 1) self-study and exploration; 2) collaborative learning; 3) questioning and guidance; 4) expansion practice; 5) summarization and enhancement. After the model was established, it began to be practiced among some teachers.

"The results were very good," You Jianzhong said. One feature of the "Life Classroom" is "surrounding tables," breaking the traditional passive arrangement where students face the teacher and blackboard straight-on. Students in a class are divided into eight groups, with six people per group, sitting together around tables. "How can this be considered chatting? This group learning method aims to enhance students' learning initiative and cooperation skills, improving learning efficiency." Regarding the claim in online posts that teachers lecture for only five minutes per class, You Jianzhong denied it, saying, "How can it be five minutes? In this learning method, the teacher is present throughout all five steps, providing clarification and guidance based on actual situations."

You Jianzhong said that parents always pit educational reform against good grades and college entrance exams, but actually, one of the most important goals of the "Life Classroom" is to improve grades. "How can we abandon grades? In our view, the college entrance exam is still the most important, but it is not the only thing; we look further ahead. We hope that today's learning will continue to promote students' success in university and their future careers." In his view, parents are too pragmatic and cannot afford to lose. "But in reality, we have not lost."

You Jianzhong admitted that communication between himself, the school, and parents was indeed insufficient. "If possible in the future, I would invite them to come, and I would patiently explain things to them."

As for the future of the "Life Classroom," You Jianzhong firmly believed it was a good thing and would not be abandoned. "If a class's students and parents do not oppose, I will apply to the Education Bureau to establish an elite class, continuing to implement educational reform."
