Traditional Chinese Medicine association responds to质疑 of "live bear bile extraction": the bears look very comfortable

by vorx2473 on 2012-02-20 08:41:06

Today, the Chinese Traditional Medicine Association held a press conference to respond to the recently controversial topic of "extracting bile from living bears". "Some organizations repeatedly use outdated technologies that have long been canceled and old photographs from before, as well as some backward cases, to criticize. They deny reality with history and replace individual cases with generalities, which is a distortion and defamation of China's actual black bear protection and utilization industry, misleading some uninformed members of the public," said Fang Shuting, president of the Chinese Traditional Medicine Association.

Fang Shuting introduced that for a long time, from the Tang Dynasty when bear bile was first used in medicine until the 1980s when the technique of extracting bile from living bears was introduced from North Korea, people obtained bear bile through the method of "hunting bears for their bile." Nowadays, the method of "hunting bears for bile" has been eliminated. In the late 1980s and early 1990s, Chinese scientists developed a non-tubular, painless drainage technology. This non-tubular drainage technology uses surgical procedures to create a bile funnel between the gallbladder and abdomen using the bear’s own soft tissue. During bile collection, a sterilized drainage tube is inserted to let the bear's bile flow out. The entire process takes only about 10 seconds.

"The black bear can normally and quietly eat during the bile extraction process. After the extraction, the tube is removed, the duct is disinfected, and the bear can go play after eating. The bear shows no abnormalities throughout the process and even appears quite comfortable," Fang Shuting said. He added that these were conclusions drawn from his on-site investigations. "Studies by relevant experts show that there is no significant difference in physiological indicators and health conditions between引流bears and non-引流bears. The bodies of引流bears are very healthy, and the quality of the bile is reliable."

Fang Shuting also mentioned that the country strictly prohibits extracting bile from hunted wild black bears or from wild black bears directly. The population of wild black bears has been steadily increasing. "The bear farming industry effectively prevents killing behavior, promoting the increase of wild black bears and protecting the development of the wild population."

According to the introduction, there are currently 68 government-approved bear farms in China. According to national laws, these bear farms are all under closed management. "However, if media reporters are interested, the Chinese Herbal Medicine Association can organize everyone to visit one of them collectively," Fang Shuting said.

Regarding the claim that "bear bile can be replaced," Fang Shuting stated that artificial bear bile research has achieved preliminary results. Clinically, it has certain similarities in function to natural bear bile, but there are still differences, and further comprehensive research by experts is needed to determine whether it can become a substitute like artificial musk or artificial bezoar.

A reporter asked if the pressure from the bear farming industry caused the delay in the launch of artificial bear bile. Fang Shuting firmly responded: "The state will definitely not refuse to approve a drug because of an industry. I have never seen this approval!"

Regarding the recent media hotspot of the "communication letter" and "response letter" between the Chinese Herbal Medicine Association and the Animals Asia Foundation, Fang Shuting responded that the reason was "last year we organized journalists to investigate a bear farm, and a journalist's report was strongly attacked by some organizations and individuals. In such a situation, we issued a communication letter to the media, hoping they would rationally and objectively view this issue."

In the media communication letter, the herbal medicine association wrote: "After they successfully eliminate bear bile, their next target will be musk." Fang Shuting said, "We foresee this."

In the communication letter sent to the Animals Asia Foundation, the foundation was accused of being sponsored by Western interest groups and opposing China's black bear farming and precious traditional Chinese medicine enterprises. The Animals Asia Foundation recently responded, stating that these claims were untrue and demanded an apology from the Chinese Herbal Medicine Association. Fang Shuting indicated that the herbal medicine association would provide a response after further study and consultation with lawyers.
