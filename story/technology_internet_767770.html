Free + Price Cut: Apple's Self-redemption and Invasion of Microsoft

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 13:29:23

Rather than saying today is Apple's new product launch conference, it might as well be a discount promotion conference. The topics of "free" and "discounts" have become the main thread throughout the entire conference.

Apple has started to appear in front of users with a new attitude while launching new products, and "free" has become the best footnote. Some media friends said that Apple also learned this from the Chinese market. Just across the street from the Moscone Conference Center where the Apple event took place, a group of Chinese mobile internet practitioners were gathering there.

Let's take a look at what's free:

1. The major version upgrade of OS X, which originally cost $99 each time, is now free for Mavericks, and can be downloaded today.

2. The entire iLife series is now free. Previously, iMovie and iPhoto were paid software. First, the iOS version of iPhoto was free, and now all iLife series software on iOS and OS X are free.

3. The office software suite iWork for desktop and iOS is now fully free. Previously, each iOS app like Keynote, Numbers, and Pages cost 68 RMB (about $9.9).

Apple did not mention how many copies of these software are sold annually or how much revenue they generate, but it must be a significant number.

Now, most of the software services provided by Apple are free, except for a few extremely professional fields such as FinalCut Pro for video editing and Apture for professional image processing.

(MacBook Air and Pro, the main personal computer products, have been discounted)

Now let's see what has been discounted:

1. The 13-inch MacBook Air has been reduced by $200, from $1499 to $1299.

2. The 15-inch MacBook Pro has been reduced by $200, from $2199 to $1999.

3. After the introduction of the iPad mini Retina, the original iPad mini has effectively been reduced to $299.

Except for the iPhone, both of Apple's main product lines, the iPad and Mac computers, have been discounted.

(In China, consumers prefer free services)

Why go free?

The only reason for Apple to lower prices and go free is to promote sales. While the mobile terminal market for iPhones is growing rapidly, in fact, the global sales of personal computers are facing a growth bottleneck. Therefore, Apple has employed these three strategies to address the slowdown in personal computer growth:

1. Hardware price reduction.

2. Software made free, including all office and lifestyle application software.

3. Entering professional fields, the Mac Pro priced at $2999 (about 20,000 RMB) is not intended for individual consumers but has a broad market in professional areas such as film, video, images, and printing.

(What does this look like in Cook's eyes?)

Who is most hurt by Apple waving the free flag?

By doing so, Apple has successfully taken up the banner of being free. Such a move is unprecedented in the industry, and who is affected the most? Microsoft and manufacturers in the Microsoft camp.

Apple subtly poked fun at Microsoft twice during this conference. Once was when Cook mentioned on stage that some companies' products take detours to achieve their goals, while Apple helps users reach their goals directly.

The second instance was when Eudy Cue, Apple's software director, mocked Microsoft's Office 365 for still costing $99 while announcing that iWork is now free.

Would Jobs have done it this way?

Apple has never offered its products for free on such a large scale. If Jobs were here, would Apple do this? As Jobs once told Cook, "Never think about what Jobs would do, do what you believe is right." Attracting more users through free offerings is something Apple under Cook's leadership believes is correct.

For opinions on this conference, our U.S.-based reporter Lisa interviewed several overseas media outlets. Most of them thought Apple's products were good. It was particularly gratifying that the free applications and Apple's refocus on true professionals were appreciated. There was also regret, as the rumored Touch ID did not appear on the new iPad.

From the voices of domestic media and users on WeChat and Weibo, the Retina-screen iPad mini became the most watched product. It will be equally popular when it launches domestically, but this time around, there is one less reason for hype - no gold edition.
