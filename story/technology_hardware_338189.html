Related thematic articles: 7 COLORS 9 COLORS. Nowadays, as soon as you casually walk into a paint market in Beijing, someone will recommend "liquid wallpaper" to you. Even if you don't buy it, COLORS alone can make the "renovation crowd" feel refreshed with its concept of "neither wallpaper nor ordinary wall paint". Song Jinqiang, president of the China Indoor Decoration Association, introduced that "liquid wallpaper", also known as wallpaper paint, is an environmentally friendly water-based coating that combines the advantages of wallpaper and latex paint. It is made from acrylic emulsion, titanium dioxide, pigments and other additives, and can create different visual effects according to the wishes of renovators. It overcomes the shortcomings of single color and lack of hierarchy sense of latex paint, and avoids the disadvantages of wallpaper such as easy discoloration, curling edges and seams.

In the interview, the person in charge told the reporter that applying "liquid wallpaper" is a technical job that ordinary people find difficult to complete. Therefore, "liquid wallpaper" is generally sold together with wall decoration. When brushing, a layer of putty should be evenly applied on the wall first, then dried for 24 hours, and the rough parts are sanded off with sandpaper. Then, a thin layer of "liquid wallpaper" paint is applied on the surface, followed by repeated alternating scraping. Using "liquid wallpaper", different brushing strengths, different brushing directions, or different color combinations will present different visual effects.

According to the reporter's understanding, there are currently about 10 major categories of "liquid wallpaper", which can transform into more than 300 patterns and have more than 90 color effects. For example, "night light wallpaper paint" (absorbing visible light for 10-30 minutes and then continuously emitting light for more than 12 hours in the dark), "chameleon wallpaper paint" (one color under incandescent light and another color under sunlight), and "relief wallpaper paint" (after special process, the wall can present a relief effect).

The reason why "liquid wallpaper" is called an environmentally friendly green coating is because no 107 glue or polyvinyl alcohol is needed during construction, so it does not contain heavy metals such as lead and mercury or aldehyde substances, thus achieving non-toxicity and pollution-free. Since it is a water-based coating, the anti-pollution performance of "liquid wallpaper" is very strong, while having good moisture-proof and antibacterial properties, making it less prone to insect infestation and aging.

Step One Gongfa: Get up at dawn every day, stroll lightly to the outdoors, and let both feet touch solid ground. Stand facing south with feet spread about four fists apart. Close your eyes and stand quietly for a moment, then slowly raise both arms to the side until palms face each other while breathing uniformly and slowly. When the arms rise level with the ears, lift the heels and let the toes touch the ground. Continue raising the arms and cross them above the head while starting to lower the head and bend the waist. Slowly lower the hands in front of the body while exhaling uniformly and slowly, then let the heels touch the ground. Forcefully press down on the ground when the hands reach the knees (touching the ground is also acceptable). At this time, imagine that true qi is drawn from the earth, gathered into a beam, and slowly injected into the qi sea (about three inches below the navel) through the laogong point. The flow path of true qi is: true qi enters through the laogong point, travels along the inner sides of the arms to the huagai point, goes down to the shanzhong point (between the two breasts), and finally returns to the qi hai (i.e., dan tian). Practice this skill once a day for thirty minutes. Regular practice can invigorate energy and prolong life. Rub the palms together several times to finish the session. This skill can be practiced for about ninety days before moving on to the second step.

Step Two Gongfa: Get up at dawn, stroll lightly to the outdoors, and let both feet touch solid ground. Stand facing south with feet spread about four fists apart. Close your eyes and stand still, breathe naturally, and focus on the dantian for three to five minutes. Imagine that the true qi of heaven gathers into a beam entering the body through the baihui point, and the true qi of earth enters the body through the yongquan point, both slowly gathering in the dantian. Then, slowly lift both palms from under the abdomen with palms facing up while focusing on the dantian. Then the true qi rises with the lifting of the palms to the middle dantian (shanzhong point), then divides into two streams running through the arms and gathers in the laogong points of both hands. To finish, imagine the true qi in the dantian overflowing after being gathered, then turn the palms outward and bring them down to the sides slowly while rubbing the dantian lightly for a short while to finish (when rubbing, men place the left hand inside and the right hand outside; women do the opposite). This skill can be practiced daily for thirty minutes and for about ninety days before moving on to the third step.
