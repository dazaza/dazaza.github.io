(I) The Combined Force Model of Stock Price Dynamics

Why can world-class stock investors, such as Warren Buffett and other great investors, consistently make money in the stock market and achieve returns above the market average, whether the market is declining or rising? To explain this phenomenon, ordinary investors might either read articles about these top investors and mimic their strategies, or simply attend stock investment training courses or read books on stock investment strategies. However, the results still leave them disappointed and even more confused. This is because there are many so-called winning strategies for stock investment available in the market, and sometimes they contradict each other, leaving ordinary investors unsure which to choose.

For example, some look for value stocks, while others seek growth stocks; some are obsessed with finding undervalued stocks by the market; some focus on fundamental indicators (company financial conditions), while others emphasize technical indicators (historical price patterns, trading volume, and time patterns); some try to find the best timing for entering and exiting the market, but often find that the actual stock price movements do not align with their predictions. For instance, if stock prices are driven by company profits, why do some stocks' prices fall instead of rise when companies report profits higher than the same period last year, the previous quarter, or analysts' estimates? In other cases, some stocks seem extremely overvalued, with P/E (price-to-earnings) ratios nearing 100, like Baidu (Bidu), far exceeding the average North American P/E ratio of 17, yet their prices continue to rise.

To address the confusion of ordinary investors and uncover the true driving forces behind stock prices, as well as decode why top global stock investors always remain unbeaten, my team (www.4858.cc) and I analyzed over 100 years of historical data from the North American stock market and studied the investment portfolios and strategies of the top 10 contemporary global investors.

We discovered that the driving force behind stock price movements is not merely fundamental financials, technical price history, market psychology, or one or a few factors, but rather a combined force made up of 28 factors. These 28 factors cover fundamentals, technical analysis, industries, products, operations, research and development, market psychology, institutional investor trends, market maker behavior, and more. Based on this, we developed a unique model for selecting quality stocks. Testing over several years has shown that our stock selection model has a high level of accuracy, and the returns of investment portfolios built based on our model significantly outperform the market average. Our stock selection model is simple and easy to operate. Once mastered, anyone can use it to build their own stable investment portfolio without paying expensive investment advisory fees.
