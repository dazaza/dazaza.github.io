These so-called "mobile phone number-changing" software are essentially typical IP software. When someone's SIM card has activated the number-changing software service, both calls and text messages not only go through the mobile operator network of the SIM card but also through the IP cracking call network of the software. That is, when calling the other party, the call will definitely enter a server, and through the illegal gateway set by the "number-changing" software, you can make calls with this "phone number."

Through some channels, they purchased the communication list of Zhanjiang leaders at or above the department level, then used the "arbitrary number display" software to impersonate leaders for fraud. Yesterday, I learned from relevant departments in Zhanjiang that two criminals used number-changing software to impersonate Zhanjiang "city leaders" and called county-level cadres to "borrow money." The court recently sentenced defendants Xie Zhi and Xie Hu to 14 months and 10 months in prison respectively for fraud.

An insider in the telecommunications industry told me that "number-changing software" comes in computer and mobile versions. The computer version involves installing number-changing software on a computer, then using earphones and microphones to conduct network calls with the other party's mobile phone, landline, or PHS. After the software modification, any number can be displayed on the other party's phone; the mobile version involves binding related mobile phone numbers on the computer backend and setting an arbitrary number to be displayed to the other party, which can modify the incoming call display through the bound mobile phone.

Subsequently, Zhanjiang police discovered that the suspects were committing crimes in Loudi City, thousands of miles away. Five days after their first crime, the police arrested suspects Xie Zhi and Xie Hu in a neighborhood in Loudi City, Hunan Province. The police also found number-changing software and crime phones at their residence.

It was learned that suspects Xie Zhi and Xie Hu obtained a number-changing software, then Xie Hu bought specialized mobile phone numbers for fraud. They modified their own mobile phone numbers into those of a hall-level city leader using the number-changing software and began committing crimes.

At the end of last year, a so-called Zhanjiang "city leader" suddenly borrowed money from more than a dozen county-level cadres via text message, causing a stir in the local officialdom. However, the criminal investigation department later confirmed that this was just a "trap" set by criminals using number-changing software for county-level leaders.

This case went through the investigation, filing, and trial stages by the public security authorities and was announced recently. The court believed that suspects Xie Zhi and Xie Hu worked together to impersonate state organ staff multiple times, committing telephone fraud crimes to seek huge profits. Their actions violated Article 266 of the Criminal Law of the People's Republic of China, constituting fraud. Considering that the crimes committed by the two defendants did not cause serious harm or influence to the victims, it was considered an attempted crime. Moreover, defendant Xie Hu was under 16 years old when committing the crime, so they were sentenced to 14 months and 10 months in prison for fraud respectively.
