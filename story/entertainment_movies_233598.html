Introduction to the Film G.I. Joe: Rise of Cobra

The film is adapted from Larry Hama's popular American comic book series of the same name and is another big-budget movie produced by Paramount Pictures after Transformers, based on Hasbro toys. It was directed by Stephen Sommers, who also directed the first two Mummy movies. The story revolves around the battle between the "G.I. Joe" special forces, headquartered in Brussels, using various advanced weapons, against an evil organization called "Cobra" established by a Scottish arms dealer.

Character Introductions:

"General Hawk" (Dennis Quaid) - The supreme commander of the G.I. Joe Team. Born into a wealthy family in Denver, Colorado, he entered West Point Military Academy under good influence and graduated with top honors. He has been tested on multiple battlefields in turbulent regions. He is described as having "keen intelligence and understanding, demonstrating completely selfless combat abilities when working with teammates. An outstanding general!"

"Duke" (Channing Tatum) - Field Commander and Deputy Commander of the G.I. Joe Team, second only to General Hawk. He is one of the key characters in the G.I. Joe series and is much loved by fans. He achieved top rankings at Fort Benning, underwent rigorous training in the special forces, and participated in battles with the South Vietnamese army.

"Ripcord" (Marlon Wayans) - Leader of the anti-reconnaissance high-altitude parachuting unit. Confident in his skills and experience, he remains calm and composed under pressure.

"Heavy Duty" (Adewale Akinnuoye-Agbaje) - As the heavy weapons expert of the G.I. Joe Team, he loves classical guitar and the composer Bach. It is said that no matter how fierce the enemy's artillery fire may be, he shows no fear.

"Breaker" (Said Taghmaoui) - Early communicator for the G.I. Joe Team, fluent in seven languages, often chewing gum. Familiar with all NATO and Warsaw Pact communication devices, as well as most world export equipment. Praised as "efficient, confident, possessing unique abilities to turn adverse situations into desired ones."

"Destro" (Christopher Eccleston) - The mastermind behind the "Military Armaments Research System" (MARS). For him, war is humanity's most natural state: survival of the fittest, where the greatest technological advances are created. He travels the world maintaining a luxurious lifestyle, providing high-end weapons to any military force that satisfies his wealth, and inciting wars in peaceful places. Wearing a silver combat mask, he personally participates in battles, either collaborating with Cobra Command and providing them with primary weapons or opposing them for his own commercial interests.

"The Baroness" (Sienna Miller) - Intelligence officer of the Cobra organization, serving as the deputy to the Cobra Commander. With long black hair, wearing black-rimmed glasses, and dressed in a black leather jacket, she is referred to as a dark, cold, and sexy enchantress. In the comics and animated series, she has a romantic relationship with Destro.

"Storm Shadow" (Byung-hun Lee) - Commonly known as the ninja bodyguard and assassin of the Cobra Commander. He is the only member to change sides, weighing loyalty between the G.I. Joe Team and the Cobra Organization several times, having long-term connections with his sword brother Snake Eyes.

"Scarlett" Character Introduction: Scarlett is the intelligence officer of the G.I. Joe Team. She is undeniably a genius, graduating from university at the age of 12. Using a bow as her weapon, she can launch various types of projectiles to achieve different striking effects.

Scarlett is also a stunning beauty, portrayed by the beautiful Rachel Nichols. Director Stephen Sommers said, "This character is not only incredibly smart but also a great beauty. Rachel does an excellent job capturing both traits, making the character very realistic."

"Snake Eyes" Character Introduction: The silent ninja warrior of the G.I. Joe Team. Silent as stone, cool-headed, decisive in action.

Ray Park previously played another iconic role in cinematic history, Darth Maul in Star Wars: Episode I – The Phantom Menace. Producer Barry Darsay said, "We chose Ray Park for Snake Eyes because he played Darth Maul and also because he is a martial arts expert." Snake Eyes vowed never to speak, Ray Park said, "As an actor, this is a very appealing role. I must use parts of my body I wouldn't normally use to communicate with others, even moving a finger can convey his meaning."
