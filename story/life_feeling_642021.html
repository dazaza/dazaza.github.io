Emotions should not be recklessly indulged.

by gege on 2011-12-23 10:52:11

Last weekend, she went on an outing with her husband. Unexpectedly, they quarreled over a trivial matter and returned home in low spirits. Back at home, she complained to her husband again. After a while, she locked herself in the bedroom for nearly 10 hours, neither eating nor drinking.

Every time it was like this: whenever she had a disagreement with her husband, she would punish him by resorting to "fasting" as a form of self-harm. The longest record was 23 hours. Seeing her husband's anxious and repentant look made her feel gratified deep down.

This time was no exception. After a few hours, her husband lost his patience first. He paced back and forth in front of the door, then gently knocked, and finally banged heavily.

She could no longer bear it and opened the door. Her husband walked into the room without saying a word, full of helplessness on his face. He patted her head and pulled her to have dinner.

After a long while, her husband remained silent. What was going on this time? According to the usual routine, her husband should be sitting beside her telling jokes to make her happy!

"What are you thinking?" Finally, she stopped using her chopsticks and broke the heavy silence first. "Suppose," her husband stared at her, "after several years, if there is only you left in this house, and you still fast, who will come to persuade you to eat?" "Myself! And you?" She blurted out without hesitation. "I'll leave earlier or abandon you." Her husband looked calm, but his calm tone revealed a little sadness, "Everything may happen!"

How could that be possible?! She had never imagined losing her husband. Usually, she always acted wilfully and even somewhat dominantly, venting all her troubles and dissatisfaction. It was clear that she regarded her husband as a family member who could be easily attacked or an emotional reservoir where she could vent freely.

However, wasn't her husband just a "big boy" who was less than three years older than her? She once saw his real demeanor in front of his parents: lazy and spoiled. Perhaps this was how her husband re-experienced the care and affection from his parents during the rare times he visited them? But rarely did she see his laziness and spoiling; instead, he always showed her sincere love and care.

At this moment, looking at her husband's slightly tired eyes, she couldn't help feeling guilty and blaming herself: why did I repeatedly hurt my husband's deepest love? Why do I care so little about my husband's inner feelings? All because I believed that only my husband could accept and tolerate all my mistakes, forgive all my shortcomings and caprices without any complaints.

If we don't cherish anything properly but keep taking and hurting, how can we guarantee it won't decline? Even the most profound emotions between the dearest people can be affected.

It was this weekend when she fully realized that there was something in life that cannot be recklessly squandered - and that is emotion! It was also this weekend when she deeply understood that she must treat her lover kindly.
