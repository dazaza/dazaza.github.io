Little Yi Yi's sensation in the soles of her feet gradually returns as she learns to walk; Weibo netizens send blessings

by edivista on 2011-10-20 09:23:12

Xiao Yi Yi, the miracle girl from the Wenzhou 723 high-speed train derailment accident, can finally walk on her own! This is the news that reporters received yesterday from Shanghai Xinhua Hospital. However, the attending doctor also expressed that although she can stand, due to severe injuries when she was rescued, Xiao Yi Yi's left lower limb weight-bearing walking function has still not completely recovered. (Click on the picture to enter Xiao Yi Yi's Weibo)

Bending of the left lower limb remains difficult

Yesterday morning in the treatment room at Xinhua Hospital, the doctors carefully removed the surgical sutures from Xiao Yi Yi's left lower limb. After more than 50 days of treatment and a total of 9 debridements, the initial 20-centimeter-long wound has now fully healed. The calf, which was mostly necrotic due to crush injuries, now appears smooth and clean. The only regret is that when Xiao Yi Yi stands up, it is clearly noticeable that her left leg is much thinner than her right leg.

Under the support of nurses and her grandmother, Xiao Yi Yi also attempted to walk on flat ground. Reporters observed that Xiao Yi Yi's feet in sports shoes can basically step flat on the ground, but because a brace is still attached, bending of the left lower limb remains difficult. Her family revealed that the sensation in Xiao Yi Yi's soles is gradually recovering.

Next steps will focus on improving muscle strength and sensory function

Attending doctor and head of pediatric orthopedics at the hospital, Zhao Li, introduced that since Xiao Yi Yi was transferred to Xinhua Hospital in late August, the hospital has corrected Xiao Yi Yi's foot plantar flexion deformity without increasing the scope of surgery. Necrotic muscles have been cleared, but serious muscle and nerve damage has led to atrophy of her left lower limb muscles, loss of skin sensation, and dysfunction of the left ankle joint, which will seriously affect her left foot's weight-bearing walking function. Du Qing, the responsible doctor who will soon implement a rehabilitation course for Xiao Yi Yi, stated that there are still no signs of recovery in the motor conduction of Xiao Yi Yi's left lower limb fibular nerve, so the prospect of Xiao Yi Yi returning to normal gait is not optimistic.

The hospital expressed that they will next promote nerve repair through neurotrophic therapy, improve muscle strength and sensory function, maximize nerve function recovery, and make every effort to restore Xiao Yi Yi's affected limb function.
