It commonly occurs in postmenopausal women and elderly men (due to hormonal changes or insufficient calcium and vitamin D). The incidence of osteoporosis in middle-aged and elderly women is much higher than in men, mainly because the decline in ovarian function leads to a decrease in estrogen levels, resulting in a negative balance in bone metabolism and a reduction in bone mass.
