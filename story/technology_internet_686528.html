Analysis of Double-sided Non-adhesive Label Printing Method

Nowadays, the application of double-sided printing labels is becoming increasingly widespread, especially in cosmetic and health supplement labels. The label is attached to the back of a transparent bottle, and through the transparent liquid, the first layer of patterns can be seen, greatly enhancing the visual impact (such as Herbal Essences' petal labels). The second layer of patterns on the back can be directly seen, mainly identifying the simple introduction of the product and the company's contact methods. The traditional technology for producing such labels is very complex and costly. The process involves first printing the first layer of patterns on the surface material of transparent non-adhesive materials, then laminating it with non-transparent white material (usually PP), and finally printing the second layer of patterns on the white material to achieve the effect of patterns on both sides.

The Birth of a Label — Print First, Then Laminate

Let's see how Labeline provides solutions for non-adhesive label production. A 10-color printing coating laminator will demonstrate how to print double-sided labels: BOPP surface material and PET base material are placed on two unwinding stations respectively. The BOPP material is first dusted off, then both materials undergo corona treatment before entering the feeding mechanism. After 4-color printing on the front side of BOPP, it is flipped via a flipper frame for reverse printing. The specially designed flipper frame by ETI has air holes in each part, keeping the film suspended during the paper transport process to avoid scratching the film, especially suitable for printing film materials. The base material is first coated with silicone and glue, then laminated with the printed surface material, and finally die-cut and rewound. There is also a slitting device at the end of the equipment for finished product slitting.

In addition to standard UV printing units, printers can also choose to add cold foil or hot foil devices, screen printing units, gravure color groups, or even offset color groups. All units can be interchanged drawer-style with flexographic units. Six units of this demonstration equipment use UV (ultraviolet) for drying, while the rest of the water-based inks use IR (infrared) for drying. Since the material printed on Labeline is a substrate-free film before being laminated into non-adhesive, tension control is very important. Its tension control range can be applied to films as thin as 12 microns to 300-gram cardstock. The equipment uses BST Promark front-back register system, maintaining precise registration regardless of how the equipment speed changes, saving material and preparation time.

ETI equipment can easily achieve inside printing of non-adhesive labels because ETI's process is to print first and then make it into non-adhesive. Compared to the traditional peel-off film process, not only can full-color printing be achieved (the traditional peel-off film process prints on the adhesive surface, where the printed graphic area cannot exceed 30%, otherwise the adhesion of the adhesive surface will be damaged. Moreover, the print quality on the adhesive surface cannot be very fine), but the process is greatly simplified. ETI's specific process flow is to print inside on a substrate-free film, then coat with silicone and glue to make it into a non-adhesive label. In this way, the printed pattern is between the glue and the surface material, effectively protecting the print without requiring varnishing or laminating.
