The Other Shore Is Not You

Others' Narration:

It was still an ordinary day, with him carrying his bag to his piano teacher's house for a tutoring session. The sky had been drizzling all day, and he liked this feeling—it gave people a sense of calmness. On the day he was born, the sky was also drizzling, so his mother named him "Rain." He looked up at the sky, letting the raindrops splash freely on his face.

Just then, a very pretty girl stood in front of him, carrying a black guitar. Her long hair was blown by the wind, and her expression was so innocent. He froze there, but he quickly realized that he had been tricked—tricked hard by fate.

"Hello!" She greeted him naturally, and he awkwardly replied, "Hello!"

She's back, returning after leaving him behind for a year!

She asked him, "Can we start over?"

He bitterly smiled and shook his head.

She said that she had always carried that black guitar because it was the first gift he ever gave her. She said she would wait for him, waiting for the day when he would accept her again. He told her, "Don't wait anymore. It won't happen. You've changed, I've changed, and those days from before can never come back."

His Narration:

"The Kiss The Rain," it's still this piece, still this familiar melody, but the people have changed while the things remain the same. I still wear the cross earrings you loved most, but my beloved cross necklace now rests on another girl's neck. Like you, she also likes "Kiss The Rain," but unlike you, she doesn't dare to listen to it at night; she fears losing control and crying. She prefers to feel the heartache under the blue sky, basking in the sunlight. You always listened to it at night because beside you, there was me, a shoulder you could lean on.

It's still the same rainy day, the same place, but the feelings are no longer the same. I can't help but play this sorrowful tune again, and tears stream down once more. She once told me not to cry, maybe my true happiness is waiting for me just a little further away. She said that one day in the future, she must hear me play this tune because she wants to know how you felt when you heard me play it. She said it definitely isn't a feeling of heartbreak, but rather a feeling of happiness.

I asked her, "Can I like you?" She said no. She said that if one day, the cross earring on my ear comes off, she will lean on my shoulder and listen to me play this tune. Now, I realize that she is the happiness waiting for me not far away. Today, I play this tune once again, saying goodbye to you with this piece. From now on, this song belongs only to her.
