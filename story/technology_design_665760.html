Earthquake hero pig 'Strong' spoiled his stomach with extra feed before the festival (picture)

by anonymous on 2012-02-15 10:03:22

The pig named "Strong" fell ill, and Grandpa Wang took good care of it.

Earthquake "celebrity" pig Strong - The "miracle pig" that survived for 36 days during the "5・12" Wenchuan earthquake has fallen ill with diarrhea and is unable to "work". It is currently undergoing treatment.

Yesterday, reporters learned that the two-and-a-half-year-old pig "Strong" had fallen ill with diarrhea and could not work. Thus, we visited the Jianchuan Museum where we unexpectedly found a diary titled "The Happy Life of Pig Strong." Flipping to the latest page, we read:

The New Year is approaching, and I believe many of my kind will not escape the disaster of being cooked. However, these past few days, due to the extra meals for the Spring Festival, I have upset my stomach. But still, I consider myself to be living the happiest life in the world:

My job

9-to-5 model show

I am happy because I found a job at the Jianchuan Museum 42 days after the 2008 earthquake, as a model: using my body language to show people how to live a strong life after an earthquake. To be honest, this job now is quite challenging. Every morning at 8:30, I have to get up, quickly eat breakfast, and then rush to the outdoor venue of the "United We Stand" Wenchuan Earthquake Memorial Museum. The distance from home to the workplace is about two and a half miles, and since I don't have a car, I can only walk. Looking at my big belly and thinking "exercise can help me lose weight," I console myself when I feel tired.

By the time I arrive at work at 9 o'clock, colleagues like the Audi car damaged in the earthquake and the first inflatable boat to reach Yingxiu have already arrived. People come to visit one after another. When they compliment me on my happiness, I am willing to do a little show. When they laugh at my obesity, I simply ignore them. I often hear humans say that jobs are hard to find nowadays. At that moment, I use my loud voice to display my confidence, telling them that as long as you are strong, there are no difficulties. However, sometimes I also slack off at work; when there are fewer visitors, I don't want to "perform," but when there are more people, I become more spirited. Last time, a TV reporter interviewed me, and I licked the camera lens, which made him praise me for being a good actor. To be honest, my boss says I am a "people pleaser."

I continue working until 5:30 PM. Unfortunately, there is no restroom in the office, and I, being such a clean pig, cannot dirty the office. After countless trials, I have mastered the art of holding it in and only go to the grass to solve my problems after work.

My weight loss plan

Private doctor's "special weight loss method"

As the saying goes, those who survive great disasters are bound to have good fortune afterward, and I am a perfect example. Thanks to everyone's careful care, I have lost the "good figure" I had during the earthquake, though that was a joke. In one and a half years, I have grown from over 100 pounds to over 410 pounds now. My boss is worried about my obesity, probably fearing that I might develop "three highs." Therefore, he specifically hired a professional veterinarian to take care of me. When tourists outside ask, they are told I weigh only over 380 pounds, possibly to encourage me to lose weight.

In the past, I ate three meals a day, and my weight skyrocketed. I had to ask a private doctor to create a weight loss plan for me: First, reduce three meals to two; Second, ask sponsors to stop providing fattening feed and instead adjust to nutritional slimming products; Third, use the walking therapy. Human weight loss is always a long-term battle, filled with pain; whereas my plan can be described as leisurely. After simple daily visits, the doctor arranges for me to walk for weight loss. Think about it, how can my little pig hooves bear a weight of around 400 pounds? Plus, my hooves suffered cracks due to malnutrition after the earthquake, leaving behind some sequelae. Therefore, sometimes walking causes unbearable pain. However, I can freely stroll along the forest paths, even running wild occasionally. What used to be a luxury has become an ordinary matter, so I don't feel any pain at all, instead finding joy in it.

The private doctor comes to see me every week, and someone massages me for fitness. They first make me relax my whole body, stretch out my limbs, and then start massaging my hooves. I enjoy it thoroughly, "Even the most fashionable weight loss methods today are exclusively enjoyed by pigs like me, truly overflowing with happiness!"

My best friend

Grandpa Wang is my "intergenerational friend"

Nowadays, I have food, housing, work, and health care doctors. Humans all say my conditions are too good. But what makes me particularly happy is my neighbor Grandpa Wang Fucheng, who loves me the most. He is already 59 years old and cooks for me, takes me to the park every day, and works tirelessly. He doesn't have a spouse, so I am very obedient, doing whatever he tells me, rarely being naughty.

On our way to and from work every day, we go for a walk in the Jianchuan park. We often walk side by side. On the road, we always hear Grandpa Wang's loud voice: "Qiangqiang, Strong, go straight, faster." Whenever this happens, I become especially obedient and walk in a straight line. When we need to turn left, he touches my right ear, and when we need to turn right, he touches my left ear. However, the fragrant soil is irresistible to me, and only then do I linger slightly.

After losing weight, my physical condition became unstable. Once, lime water was sprinkled to disinfect my "villa," causing me to bleed from the nose. Just like these days when I have diarrhea, Grandpa Wang was very anxious. Without a mobile phone, he ran to the office to borrow a phone to contact my private doctor. After diagnosis, he was instructed to eat more sweet potato vine porridge, and he worked tirelessly cooking and simmering for me.

Thus, I can only coquettishly call his name softly to cheer him up. He advises me that as long as I can successfully lose weight, living for several more decades should be no problem. Scientists say that pigs generally live between 10 to 20 years. I think my new year's wish may be the same as many human women: to lose weight healthily.
