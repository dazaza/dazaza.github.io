What pathological factors cause habitual abortion? Habitual abortion is a very painful experience for couples, especially for women who are deeply affected. After finally getting pregnant, they suffer from repeated miscarriages. What exactly causes habitual abortion? What pathological factors lead to habitual abortion? What can be done in the case of habitual abortion?

Habitual abortion is a type of natural abortion that refers to a pregnant woman experiencing the cessation of fetal development and miscarriage at the same stage of pregnancy three or more consecutive times. What pathological factors cause habitual abortion? What leads to such a severe condition?

Causes of habitual abortion:

Maternal diseases: Endocrine disorders, abnormal uterine development, cervical problems, cervical insufficiency, uterine fibroids, and other gynecological conditions can all lead to habitual abortion. Additionally, serious internal medical conditions such as kidney disease, heart disease, and hypothyroidism can also result in miscarriage.

External factors: Excessive alcohol consumption, smoking (including passive smoking), exposure to chemical toxins, severe noise and vibrations, high-temperature environments, etc., can all damage the placenta and fetus, leading to miscarriage.

Genetic factors: If there are abnormalities in the number or structure of the embryo's chromosomes, it can result in poor fetal development. This is the most common cause of miscarriage, accounting for 60%-70% of natural miscarriages. Approximately 2%-4% of habitual abortions are related to genetic factors.

Now that we know what pathological factors cause habitual abortion, what should be done when it occurs?

Firstly, women who experience habitual abortion must promptly seek examination at a reputable hospital to check for any uterine or other reproductive organ diseases or serious internal medical conditions. It is recommended that the husband also undergoes testing to determine if either partner has chromosomal abnormalities.

Secondly, in fact, most miscarriages are not related to the mother or father but are due to chromosomal abnormalities that occur during embryo formation. This situation is often accidental and may happen consecutively three times to the same woman. Most couples who are healthy and have no chromosomal abnormalities can still conceive healthy babies after experiencing habitual abortion.

Thirdly, women with conditions such as abnormal uterine development, cervical issues, uterine fibroids, kidney disease, or heart disease should first actively treat these conditions before considering pregnancy.
