Top executives of Beijing Guoan FC expressed their dissatisfaction with the arrangements for the two-front match: it is completely an internal consumption and the Football Association fails to function well.

by ztt0288868 on 2010-04-19 18:25:16

In the early morning of today, at the registration office of Hong Kong companies, after 19 hours of arduous journey, all officers and men of Guoan were exhausted. At this moment, the players saw that the senior management of Guoan Club was waiting collectively in the airport terminal building. The comfort from the senior management was the best confirmation for their long expedition. However, ten great kills: Kobe will always be a mystery, the miracle of 0.01 seconds and the referee as the main character. In five days, Guoan flew nearly 20,000 kilometers. The exhausting odyssey was due to the dual battle lines of CSL and AFC Champions League. If the Chinese Football Association could adjust the CSL schedule, perhaps Guoan and Yatai would achieve better results in the AFC Champions League.

Take Guoan and Yatai as examples. The two teams played in CSL last Saturday and then faced their respective AFC Champions League opponents on Wednesday this week. The interval between the CSL and AFC Champions League schedules is so short. Can the CSL match time be adjusted to give the two teams enough preparation time? After all, domestic training is for united external confrontation and bringing glory to the country. Guoan Club proposed long ago to move the CSL match last Saturday one day earlier to last Friday for a better performance in the AFC Champions League, giving the team one more day of rest. This reasonable request was ignored by the Chinese Football Association. A senior official of Guoan angrily criticized this as another case of non-action by the Chinese Football Association. The original intention of the league was domestic training and united external confrontation, but now it has become internal conflict and external loss. It is really unknown which brilliant person of the FA made such a clever arrangement of the schedule.

On February 23, before the away challenge against Guoan, Melbourne Victory requested the Australian FA to move the domestic league match ahead by one day, and the Australian FA immediately adjusted the schedule. The competent authorities of the Korean and Japanese leagues are no exception, only the Chinese FA turns a blind eye. Such management level and working style are why Chinese football lags behind South Korea and Japan! J080
