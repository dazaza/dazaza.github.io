Inter Milan's Champions League defeat would cost 20 million Euros, legendary figure exposes the contract: Mourinho will be fired if he doesn't advance from the group stage.

by kersfe3f23 on 2009-12-09 08:20:50

Inter Milan to lose 20 million Euros if they lose in the Champions League: Legend reveals clause in Mourinho's contract that he will be fired if they don't qualify

This coming Wednesday, Inter Milan has more than just an important game awaiting them; it is also a match that will determine the club's cash earnings. If Inter Milan loses to Rubin Kazan or draws by two or more goals (2-2, 3-3, or 4-4), then not only will they say goodbye to the Round of 16 in the UEFA Champions League, but also around 20 million Euros in match bonuses from this stage until the final. Furthermore, if Dynamo Kyiv defeats Barcelona under these circumstances, then Inter Milan will also lose their qualification for the Europa League. Should this unfortunate situation come true, it would be a significant blow to the team built by Mourinho, and thus, there is a tense atmosphere surrounding Inter Milan as they prepare for their crucial match against Rubin Kazan.

In Saturday's match against Juventus, Inter Milan's head coach Jose Mourinho received his fourth red card in Serie A. Although he was never one to shy away from controversy during his time in Portugal's Primeira Liga and England's Premier League, Mourinho managed to surpass his predecessor Roberto Mancini's record within just 15 months of being in Serie A. Over four years at Inter Milan, Mancini accumulated only three red cards, whereas Mourinho has already surpassed him with four red cards in just 15 months. Remarkably, all four of Mourinho's red cards were received within this year alone (on January 25 against Sampdoria, March 15 against Fiorentina, September 20 against Cagliari, and last Saturday against Juventus). Interestingly, Sacchi, who sent off Mourinho last Saturday, had also previously sent off Mancini during a Coppa Italia match between Inter Milan and Lazio on May 7, 2008.

The Turin-based sports newspaper "Tuttosport" reported that Mourinho did not attend the post-match press conference after the Juventus game due to "physical condition issues," though this does not mean he left Turin in a rage. Certain incidents witnessed at the Stadio Olimpico di Torino left Mourinho extremely dissatisfied, mainly regarding some refereeing decisions made by Sacchi, but also certain behaviors exhibited by his own players. Similar to what happened in Barcelona, Mourinho felt let down by his players in Turin. Among those players, Mario Balotelli stood out negatively as he not only got involved in the scuffle following Marchisio's goal celebration but also failed to make any significant impact on the game.

Upon returning to Milan on Sunday, Mourinho addressed the team, avoiding overly harsh criticism when reviewing the loss at the Stadio Olimpico di Torino. Mourinho clearly did not want to further exacerbate the tension within the team, which had already been heightened during the Juventus match, especially with the upcoming decisive Champions League game. However, this match is crucial for him. Yesterday, Inter Milan legend Giuseppe Meazza revealed in an interview: "I believe if Inter Milan doesn't qualify from the Champions League group stage, Mourinho will be fired. As far as I know, there should be such a clause in his contract. After all, being eliminated by Rubin Kazan in the group stage is different from losing to Manchester United or Barcelona in the final."

Now, Inter Milan must consider how to win this vital match for the team. For a team that has only won one of their last ten Champions League matches (away against Dynamo Kyiv on November 4), it seems almost like an impossible task. To boost team morale, Inter Milan plans to organize fans at San Siro to cheer on the team, believing that "this way, the cheers won't be chaotic and dispersed, and the atmosphere will be very lively." However, from another perspective, this plan may also increase the pressure on the visiting Rubin Kazan team and the referee appointed by UEFA.

"Tuttosport" also disclosed that over the weekend, while enjoying a short holiday in the mountains with his family, Massimo Moratti watched the match against Juventus on TV. The second-half goal by Marchisio weighed heavily on everyone's mind at Inter Milan. As the crucial match against Rubin Kazan approaches, the heavy defeat in Turin has added more pressure to Mourinho's squad. Before the home game against Rubin Kazan, Moratti, who is currently on vacation, will return to the club to support and encourage the team. Disappointed by the outcome in Turin, which didn't meet his expectations, Moratti was even more displeased by the fact that Inter Milan almost always loses away against strong teams (previously in Manchester, Barcelona, and now in Turin) and tends to wake up only after receiving a harsh blow (such as in Kazan and Kyiv). Moratti is disappointed by the lack of identity in his team, and more so because the defeat in Turin means that Inter Milan has suffered losses in almost all major competitions this season except for the Milan Derby. From the Italian Super Cup held in Beijing during the summer to the match against Barcelona at Camp Nou, where Moratti attended his first away game this season accompanied by his good friend Joan Laporta, he witnessed his team getting thrashed. Now, facing a critical moment in European competition, Moratti knows that this is not the time for blame; rather, a crucial victory — such as against Rubin Kazan — could help restore the team's composure. The previous match against Fiorentina has already proven that Inter Milan knows how to survive adversity, and thus, the most important thing now is to relieve the team's pressure. However, on Wednesday, the ball beneath the feet of Inter Milan's players might feel as heavy as lead...
