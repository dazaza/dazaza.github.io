The same kind of tea drink actually has different colors.

by zuipin1 on 2012-02-18 09:11:22

Buying one or two bottles of the same tea beverage in the same store, the color difference is quite significant. Such incidents have puzzled many citizens of Xi'an: is there a problem with one of the bottles? Is this beverage still safe to drink?

The first time encountering such a situation was a netizen who published a comparative picture online. In the picture, the color of two bottles of "oolong tea" with identical packaging was clearly different. The netizen who bought these two bottles chose not to drink them and instead asked: could this phenomenon be due to excessive food coloring? Is it still safe to continue drinking? Early in the morning, reporters began an investigation on the streets of Xi'an. At the first two large supermarkets found in the suburbs, although there were tea beverages on the shelves, no obvious color differences were discovered. However, during the continued investigation at noon, in a convenience store's refrigerator on Dongmutou Street in Xi'an, reporters found a bottle of oolong tea with a significantly different color.

Xi'an citizen: Oh, this is oolong tea, why doesn't it look right? Clearly, the two are different, but the writing is the same.

Xi'an citizen: They're different, vastly so.

Xi'an citizen: One must be fake, or counterfeit.

Xi'an citizen: It can't be drunk because it's food.

Xi'an citizen: I estimate it's oxidation, like when you cut an apple, it rusts.

Reporters also purchased a bottle of oolong tea from the store, and the other bottle had a different color.

Upon close inspection, the darkest colored bottle was produced in July 2011, while the lightest one was produced in December 2011. The longer the storage time, the deeper the color. Is this what the lady said about oxidation? According to the contact number on the packaging, we reached out to the brand's customer service personnel to hear the manufacturer's explanation regarding this phenomenon.

Customer service staff of Nongfu Spring: This beverage is made of tea components, without adding anything else. When left for a long period, it will be affected by external sunlight exposure. Therefore, its color changes. This is a normal phenomenon.

This statement from the manufacturer was endorsed by Professor Tian Chengrui, Director of the Food Engineering Department at Shaanxi Normal University. Professor Tian stated that under such circumstances, consumers mainly need to check if the beverage is still within the warranty period. As long as it hasn't expired, there shouldn't be any issue with drinking safety.

Vice President of the Shaanxi Food Safety Association: Because of its (tea) internal components, it easily changes color and oxidizes, as tea polyphenols are prone to oxidation and color change. This is normal and shouldn't pose a problem.
