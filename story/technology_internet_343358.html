[Forward] Former President of China Hewlett-Packard, Sun Zhenyao, Talks about Work and Life (Part Three)

by syer5314k on 2010-04-11 10:39:23

Sun Zhenyao writes about his retirement and discusses life

Although it has only been fifteen days since I left HP, it feels like HP is already far away from me. My mind is more focused on planning the second phase of my life, which does not mean that I have no attachment to HP. The main reason is that I want to use this to drive myself forward.

The experience of Wang Shi, chairman of Vanke, climbing Mount Everest gave me a lot of inspiration. Although he carried a large amount of supplies when he set off, during the process of reaching the summit, he had to continuously reduce his load. In the end, he only climbed to the top with one oxygen cylinder. Climbing mountains is like this, and isn't a long life also the same?

After I announced my retirement, I received congratulations from colleagues, friends, and classmates. Most people think that I can choose to retire at such a position and age, which is a kind of courage and also a blessing.

There are some people who suspect that I am just using this opportunity to change jobs, and there are also some people who say that I couldn't continue working at HP and took this chance to leave.

Over the years, I have become accustomed to others talking about me, but for good friends, I do care whether everyone truly understands my thoughts, and this is the purpose of writing this article.

Due to the influence of my father's early death, I made up my mind very early to realize my wishes in my lifetime. I don't want to be like my father, busy with family life all his life, regretting and lamenting having many unfulfilled ideals before he passed away.

An article in a magazine mentioned that we should consider our epitaphs before we die because it represents our own definition of a perfect life. We should try our best to achieve it within our lifetime.

I hope my epitaph will also read, apart from content related to family and good friends:

1. This person once served a global IT giant (HP) for 25 years, experienced several major transformations with her, and watched her transform from primarily an electronics instrument business into the world's largest IT company.

2. This person worked in the fastest developing country globally (China) for 16 years and served as the president of HP China for seven years, witnessing and experiencing the critical latest breakthrough stage of China's reform and opening-up, growing together with China.

3. This person loved flying and was once a licensed pilot, accumulating over X hours of flight time, taking off and landing at X airports.

4. This person obtained a master's degree in management, paying special attention to the organizational behavior and performance of Chinese enterprises in leadership management, and gaining recognition in this field from Chinese enterprises.

It took me 25 years to summarize the first two achievements, and I don't know how much longer it will take to achieve the third and fourth goals, especially the fourth goal, which requires academic training to summarize my experience into knowledge.

Otherwise, my experience will not effectively influence or teach others. Therefore, re-entering school to obtain a management degree is necessary, let alone that this is a very important wish of mine.

On the other hand, I spent 25 years in the operations field, diligently doing the work of a professional. It was a good job, especially at HP, and this job also helped me establish a financial foundation to support my family's development.

But I don't want to spend my entire life stuck in the operational field. I imagine being like an entrepreneur, having the opportunity to make money from some ideas (ideas), even though the risk is high, it's worth a try. Even if I fail, it won't be in vain, and this is part of the fourth wish.

Carly Fiorina once told me, "There are many people in this world with good ideas, but few have the ability to realize them." On May 21, 2007, during a speech at Peking University, someone asked which books influenced me the most. One of the books that influenced my worldview is called "Trigger Point." Its main point is: what life needs most is not planning, but grasping opportunities at the right time and taking action.

These wishes have been brewing in my heart for a long time. At first, I might think about them once or twice a year, then forget them after a while. But gradually, the voice in my heart grew louder and appeared more frequently. When it almost came to talk to me every week, I knew the timing was ripe.

Like anyone else, giving up what you currently have, the familiar environment, and stable income, and turning to a path you've never experienced with unknown risks, requires immense courage, family support, and encouragement from good friends. There must be sacrifice to gain, which is easier said than done. I'm glad I finally took the first step.

I want to thank HP's EER early retirement preferential policy; it is one of the key trigger points. Another key factor happened last May or June.

At that time, my eldest son graduated from university, and my younger son graduated from high school. Before they continued with their work and studies, it was a golden period for our whole family to spend a longer time together. I planned a long vacation early on, taking them to travel around various places.

But this plan had to be canceled due to an important work matter (Mark Hurd's visit to China). This incident prompted me to seriously consider the voice in my heart. Would I continue to miss many key opportunities?

I am over 50 years old. Would I follow the same path as my father? The head of HR, Charles, told me that many people miss opportunities even when all the stars align in their favor.

I know the reason - because giving up and changing is so difficult for people. I believe that most people have their own ideals in life, but I also believe that many people ultimately just treat these ideals as fantasies and constantly find excuses for why they cannot be realized. Former South African President Mandela once said, "Changing oneself is more difficult than changing the world," which is indeed insightful.

What is a happy and meaningful life? I believe everyone's definition is different. For me, achieving the content on my epitaph is my definition.

Being in the position of President of HP China certainly attracts a lot of attention, but my wife and closer friends know that it is not what I pursue. That is just where I need to excel to play this role well.

Being a person without a business card, although it has only been more than ten days, I have already found that my brain has freed up a lot of space and energy, allowing me to carefully research and plan my new life in Chapter II.

I plan to spend two years completing the preparation for this transition and spend more time with my family. During these two years, I hope to obtain a pilot's license, earn a master's degree related to management, improve my English proficiency, build new networks, meet people from different industries, maintain ties with mainland China, and hope that after two years, I can smoothly return to the mainland to realize my fourth wish.

As expected, I discovered many places in life that need adjustment.

For more than twenty years, the pace and rhythm of my life have been almost entirely controlled by the company and work, with constantly emerging deadlines and tasks driving my daily schedule. Once leaving such an environment, the first thing that needs adjustment is relying on one's self-discipline and willpower to drive daily activities. The attitude of sleeping until natural awakening is absolutely incorrect. Relaxing oneself, not setting goals and timetables for things, or being indifferent to missing time targets, is also incorrect. Not having annual, quarterly, monthly, and weekly plans is also incorrect.

Having served as a senior manager for many years, I have developed the habit of delegating tasks. My time is mainly spent on thinking, decision-making, and tracking the progress of projects, relying more on a large team to handle specific matters and secretaries to deal with many coordination and tedious tasks.

After arriving in the United States, many things require calling an 800 number to contact, but these calls are often busy, making you wait for a long time on the waiting line. While waiting, I can understand the hard work of my previous secretary, but it also reminds me to change my attitude in this stage, cultivating greater patience and the ability to do things myself.

The content of life also needs to be greatly adjusted, making more time for physical exercise, paying more attention to family and friends, and spending more time experiencing different leisure activities and flying. Step by step, I hope life gradually adjusts to the track I expect, looking forward to these two years being both fulfilling and full of fun and meaning.

The first happy experience was preparing for and attending my eldest son's engagement ceremony. The feeling of being fully engaged without worrying about work figures was wonderful. At the same time, I also rented an apartment, bought furniture and a car, and accompanied my family to Reno and Lake Tahoe on weekends. I have been to Lake Tahoe many times, but this time the experience was different. I sincerely appreciated its beauty from the heart.

But at the same time, I am also accelerating my research work, preparing for applications to universities and flight schools, and meeting friends in Silicon Valley and some venture capital companies during this period to understand different industries.

My philosophy of life is "A perfect performance comes from adequate preparation", "Be brave enough to change yourself, adapt to the ever-changing environment, and opportunities will keep coming", "Happiness and a meaningful life come from realizing the desires in your heart, not from external applause".

When I left, two good friends gave me two different blessings. Baron's was "Years of success and failure turned to dust", and Yang Hua's was "In late spring, people go to the altar of the wind, sing, and return". They respectively represent my feelings of leaving HP and moving towards the future.

I summarize life into three stages: one stage is finding a job for reality, another stage is choosing a job you are willing to invest in for reality, and the final stage is doing things for your ideals.

I cherish my good fortune and am grateful for the support, encouragement, and assistance given to me by HP and my colleagues and good friends. I share this heartfelt article with my good friends.
