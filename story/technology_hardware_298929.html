Q&A on Establishing a Hong Kong Company and Related Matters

The following information is compiled based on customer inquiries, it may lack systematicness but helps us gain a deeper understanding of the establishment, management, and related matters of Hong Kong companies:

1. Question: When does the Companies Registry have records of the registration documents for newly established companies?

Answer: After the Companies Registry signs for receipt of the documents, the records or films will be available for public inspection on the 16th working day.

2. Question: After establishing a publishing house, how can one legally publish books?

Answer: Once the publishing house is established, you are qualified to handle book publishing matters. However, there are two tasks you need to complete: one is to apply for an International Standard Book Number (ISBN) or International Standard Serial Number (ISSN). The second is to apply for a distribution number before publishing. Once these two tasks are completed, you can legally publish books.

3. Question: What do ISBN and ISSN mean? What is the difference between ISBN and ISSN?

Answer: ISBN stands for International Standard Book Number. ISSN stands for International Standard Serial Number. Having these two numbers indicates that our publications have reached international standards and have been registered with the international ISBN and ISSN institutions. The fundamental difference between ISBN and ISSN lies in their objects; the former is issued to book publishers, while the latter is issued to journal publishers.

4. Question: How are taxes levied on Hong Kong enterprises?

Answer: Hong Kong is a free port with few tax items and low tax rates. The only tax an enterprise needs to pay annually is income tax, which is calculated based on the company's net profit. The tax rate is 17.5%. For example, if a company has an annual turnover of HKD 10 million, gross profit of HKD 2 million, and net profit of HKD 1 million, the company should pay HKD 175,000 in income tax to the government (1,000,000 x 17.5% = 175,000). If the company does not make a profit, it does not need to pay taxes.

5. Question: Are taxes levied on products entering and leaving Hong Kong?

Answer: Except for tobacco and alcohol, goods generally entering and leaving Hong Kong are not taxed. However, customs clearance is required, and the cost is approximately one-thousandth of the value.

6. Question: Are there any taxes to be paid when establishing a company?

Answer: Yes. When establishing a Hong Kong company, you must pay the stamp duty on registered capital. The stamp duty is charged at a rate of 1/1000 of the registered capital. For example, if the registered capital is HKD 1 million, the stamp duty payable would be HKD 1,000.

7. Question: Who is responsible for accounting and auditing after establishing a Hong Kong company?

Answer: Accounting can be handled by the company itself or entrusted to a Hong Kong accounting firm. However, auditing must be entrusted to an accountant.

8. Question: If business is bad and the company is suspended, how much must be paid annually?

Answer: A Hong Kong company can apply for temporary suspension at any time. Apart from paying the annual report fee to the Companies Registry and the annual review fee to the Inland Revenue Department, no other fees are required. If the market improves, the company can resume operations anytime, saving many costs.

9. Question: How should I open a bank account after establishing a Hong Kong limited company?

Answer: The signatories for opening an account must have a passport, and all shareholders must personally sign the documents at a Hong Kong bank. If unable to come to Hong Kong, the signature must be witnessed at a relevant mainland bank, then sent via express courier to the Hong Kong head office for processing. If you only have a mainland ID card, you can open an account at a Hong Kong bank branch in the mainland. For more details, please refer to "How to Open a Hong Kong Bank Account" on our website.

10. Question: Can all shareholders of my Hong Kong company be mainland residents if the purpose is to establish a foreign-invested joint venture or wholly-owned enterprise in the mainland?

Answer: Generally speaking, a Hong Kong company can be entirely composed of mainland shareholders. However, if you want to set up a foreign-invested joint venture or wholly-owned enterprise, the Ministry of Foreign Trade and Economic Cooperation usually invites at least one overseas shareholder to sign the documents. Therefore, arranging an overseas shareholder in the company is necessary.

11. Question: I only have some miscellaneous businesses through Hong Kong and don't want to establish a company. Can Ruifeng Accounting Firm provide this kind of service?

Answer: Due to the increasing frequency of business interactions between Mainland China and Hong Kong, many mainland businessmen need to handle some business through Hong Kong without having their own company. They often need to rely on certain Hong Kong companies to handle their business. To meet this demand, our parent company, Ruifeng International Group Limited, has launched a business package service plan. Any person who needs it can have access to Hong Kong company resources. For more details, please see the Business Package Service.

12. Question: Can our newly established Hong Kong company directly set up a foreign-invested joint venture or wholly-owned enterprise in mainland China?

Answer: After the company is established, it can directly invest domestically and set up joint ventures or wholly-owned enterprises.

13. Question: If we register a company in Hong Kong, can we set up the company account in a foreign bank in mainland China? For instance, a Hong Kong bank's branch in Shenzhen?

Answer: Yes. After the company is established, the directors can open an offshore account at a foreign bank in mainland China using their mainland ID cards. However, the directors must personally go to the bank to open the account.

14. Question: How long does it take to process a new company? How long does it take to buy a ready-made shell company? Can we purchase one first and then change its name? How long does it take to change the name?

Answer: It takes ten working days to process a new Hong Kong company after all legal documents are signed. Purchasing a ready-made company can be completed in two working days, and urgent cases can be completed on the same day. After purchasing, the company name and shareholders can be changed, and changing the name takes two days.

15. Question: How are various daily tasks such as tax reporting for Hong Kong companies resolved? Can we entrust you to handle them? What are the annual fees?

Answer: In Hong Kong's taxation system, taxes are reported once a year. Regarding the charging issue, it depends on your company's actual situation. If you choose our Business Secretary Service, tax reporting is free of charge. However, accounting and auditing must be paid for, and the fees depend on the turnover and the size of the invoices. If the company has no business, there will be no such fees.

16. Question: How is the tax rate for Hong Kong companies calculated? How is the specific tax reporting time defined?

Answer: Hong Kong's tax rate is calculated based on 16% of actual profits. Hong Kong companies typically use March 31st each year as the fiscal year-end date, and you can defer tax reporting for one year.

17. Question: If our Hong Kong company reaches tens of millions of US dollars in annual exports, what support does the Hong Kong government offer? How can we obtain relevant documents for free entry and exit to Hong Kong?

Answer: The Hong Kong government and banks have some plans to support small and medium-sized enterprises. If we meet the conditions, we can receive their assistance. Hong Kong is currently learning from the United States, and anyone with wealth or knowledge who can contribute to Hong Kong's economy will be considered for inclusion. Regarding obtaining documents for entry and exit between Hong Kong and Macau, it is recommended that you apply to the local public security department where your household registration is located.
