Online store strategies

by anonymous on 2011-03-25 14:47:27

1. Brainstorming.

The first step in opening a store is not online, but in your brain. You need to figure out what kind of store you want to open. In this aspect, opening an online store is no different from a traditional one. Finding a good market and having competitive products are the foundation for success.

2. Register on a website as a user.

You need to choose a website that provides a personal store platform and register as a user.

Some websites require registration with real names and valid identification documents such as ID cards. When choosing a website, high traffic and relatively low fees are important factors.

3. Apply to open a store on the website.

You need to fill in the categories of products provided by your store in detail. For example, if you sell fashion watches, they should be classified under "Jewelry, Watches, Glasses" in the "Watches" category so that your target customers can find you accurately. Then, you need to come up with a catchy name for your store. Which store netizens click on in the list largely depends on whether the name is attractive. Some online stores display personal information, which should be filled out truthfully to increase trust.

4. Stocking up.

5. Listing.

You need to fill in the name, origin, nature, appearance, quantity, transaction method, transaction deadline, etc., of each item on the website, preferably accompanied by pictures of the items. The name should be as comprehensive as possible, highlighting its advantages, because when others search for such items, only the name will appear on the list. To increase appeal, the quality of the pictures should be as good as possible, and the descriptions should be as detailed as possible. If mailing is required, it's best to specify who is responsible for the shipping costs.

When listing, there is another very important thing: setting the price. Websites usually provide options such as starting price, reserve price, and fixed price for sellers to set. Online bidding differs from traditional bidding. Sellers first set a starting price, and buyers bid upwards from there.

A lower starting price is more likely to attract buyers' interest. Some sellers set a starting bid of 1 yuan as a way to grab attention. However, too low a starting price carries the risk of too low a final selling price, so sellers should set a reserve price at the same time. Another downside of a very low starting price is that it may imply that you're willing to sell the item at a very low price, causing the auction to linger at a low price range. If the seller feels that waiting for the auction to end takes too long, they can set a fixed price. Once a buyer is willing to pay this price, the item is sold immediately. The downside is that if several buyers are interested, it's impossible to drive up the price. Sellers should use these settings based on their own specific circumstances.

6. Advertising.

To boost the popularity of your store, you should invest appropriately in advertising during the initial stages of opening a store, but only online. For instance, you can buy ad space on pages with high traffic on the website, make the product names bold in the product category lists, or add pictures to attract attention. You can also use free advertising methods, such as exchanging links with other stores and websites.

7. Mid-sale service.

When customers decide whether to purchase, they may need a lot of information that you haven't provided. They will ask questions online at any time, and you should respond promptly and patiently. But note that many websites prevent private transactions to avoid evasion of transaction fees, prohibiting both parties from providing any personal contact information such as email addresses or phone numbers, otherwise penalties will be imposed.

8. Transactions.

After a successful deal, the website will notify both parties of their contact information, and the transaction can proceed according to the agreed method. You can choose to meet in person, or transact through remittance or mail, but it should be done as soon as possible to avoid doubts about your creditworthiness. Whether to provide additional after-sales services depends on the prior agreement between both parties.

9. Evaluation or Complaints.

Credit is an important factor in online transactions. To jointly build a credit environment, if the transaction is satisfactory, it's best to give the other party a positive review and obtain a positive review through good service. If the transaction fails, a negative review should be given or a complaint made to the website to minimize losses and warn others. If the other party complains, it should be handled promptly to avoid leaving a stain on your credit.

10. Fees.

Websites usually charge some fees, including fees for purchasing "webpage recommendation positions," listing fees, transaction fees, store fees, etc. These should be paid promptly, but can be reasonably avoided. For example, guiding repeat customers to offline transactions or home transactions instead of online bidding. When calculating costs, these fees should be considered along with shipping fees, transportation costs for face-to-face transactions, phone bills, and internet fees.

In fact, opening an online store is not as complicated as imagined. Many netizens list a bunch of steps for opening an online store, such as sourcing goods, finding suppliers, making pictures, writing introductions, etc., which looks like a long checklist, but actually, these are not the main issues. Or rather, opening a physical store would probably require you to consider more things like business registration, taxes, utilities, etc. So, don't complain about things that can already be simplified.

Sourcing advice: Don't overthink it; choose something familiar and interesting to you.

Based on my years of studying online stores, preparing for a week is enough to start one. Choose goods you like and are interested in. I often see netizens seeking help on forums like Alibaba. While asking for help is important, I think even on a large platform like Alibaba, you should be able to find quite a bit yourself. Smart store owners aren't just looking for goods, but selecting them. Opportunities are discovered by people, and so are sources. If a store owner can't even secure a source, they probably lack potential. Also, don't over-research; if you like it, go for it. Otherwise, you might get overwhelmed and end up not finding anything. Alternatively, you could help customers save money on Taobao through cashback shopping (Taokè model).

Focus on promotion services: Spend less time researching appearances and more time learning promotions!

No matter how much graphic design or store templates you use, it doesn't matter much. Customers care about the content, not the style or aesthetics of your store. That's just your domain. As long as the store looks decent, don't waste time on it. Promoting an online store is a tough process. Of course, there's no easy struggle in this world. The reason for not making money is simple: where there's hatred, there's suffering. The reason for no income from the online store is one word: lazy. Spend less time studying the store and more time learning promotion!

Build good credibility with after-sales service, sacrifice small profits to retain customers:

Once the business is up and running and orders are coming in, you might think everything is fine, but that attitude is wrong. Otherwise, all your business will be one-time deals, and you won't have any repeat customers. I had such an experience: I wanted to buy a programming book, and only one store on Taobao had it. After much negotiation, the store owner refused to lower the price significantly. Eventually, I found that most customers who bought the book left comments saying: stingy store owner, profit-driven store owner, if it wasn't for this book, only fools would buy from you! Reading such reviews, you can imagine how poor the store owner's management skills are. So, don't underestimate small favors; even a keychain might help you retain a long-term customer.

First: Get an online bank card from the bank, second: register an account on Taobao and Alipay, third: verify your Alipay account. Specific steps are as follows: Before opening a store, choose a website that provides a personal store platform and register as a user. To ensure transaction security, identity verification and payment method certification are required. The reason for choosing to open a store on Taobao is that it's completely free. Three conditions must be met: one, register as a member and pass the certification; two, post more than 10 items (including 10); three, activate online banking for safe transactions.

1. User Registration: Log in to Taobao and click the "Free Registration" button at the top of the page. In the opened page, enter membership name, password, email, etc. (Email can be obtained for free from Yahoo, Sina, Sohu, NetEase, etc.) Click the "Agree to the following service terms, submit registration information" button. Then, the registered email will receive a confirmation email. Open the link inside and confirm to complete the user registration. Security tip: To ensure transaction security, the password should not be overly simple. It's recommended to use a combination of "English letters + numbers + symbols."

2. Identity Verification: Taobao stipulates that only after passing the real-name verification can you sell items and open a store. Therefore, after registering as a user, corresponding verifications (including personal real-name verification and Alipay verification) must be completed. Specific operational steps are as follows:

Step 1: Log in to Taobao, click "My Taobao" at the top of the page. In the opened page, click "Please click here" next to the text "Want to sell items? First, perform Alipay verification."

Step 2: On the opened page, it will prompt that the Alipay account has not been activated. Click "Click here to activate the Alipay account." In the pop-up page, enter your real name, type of ID, number, Alipay password, etc., then click the "Save and immediately activate the Alipay account" button.

Step 3: After successfully activating the Alipay account, return to the original page and press the F5 key to refresh the page. Click the "Apply for Alipay Personal Real-Name Verification" button, read the Alipay verification service terms, then click the "I have read" button to continue.

Step 4: First, fill in personal information according to the prompts, then click "Next Step"; Next, select ID verification. You can choose "Online Upload" or "Mail" copies of your ID, then click "Next Step"; Then, input bank card information, including bank branch, bank card number, province, city, etc., and wait for Alipay to transfer funds within one day.

Friendly reminder: If uploading ID copies online, control the image file size to within 200KB; If it's an IC ID, you also need to provide a backside picture.

Step 5: One day later, re-open "My Taobao," click the corresponding link in the certification area to open the "Alipay Verification" page. In the "Bank Account Verification" area, click "Confirm Transfer Amount," then input the amount of funds injected by Alipay into your bank account, and click the "Confirm" button.

A key factor in successfully opening an online store lies in the sourcing channel. The same product can have different prices depending on the sourcing channel.

After passing the identity verification, you'll be busy organizing the items you already have. I'm in wholesale, previously specializing in single-item leftovers and inventory, going to factories to collect newly discontinued leftovers or appealing inventory, then utilizing online and offline channels to earn the difference. Basically, I relied on single-item sales. Now, with fewer people taking large quantities, I started a wholesale mall to do small-scale wholesaling. The financial crisis led to massive layoffs and salary cuts, prompting many people to choose low-cost online entrepreneurship. Therefore, I believe that small-scale wholesaling will be easier this year compared to previous years. Tail-end and inventory have been around for many years, becoming synonymous with low prices and high quality. Thus, many aspiring entrepreneurs continuously search for related keywords online hoping to find manufacturers for such goods. When I was doing single-item sales, customers often asked me if I was a manufacturer. I usually countered by asking what they did, and 80% of the responses were from new online store owners, which was quite amusing.

They asked the most common questions:

1. Are you a manufacturer? (Would manufacturers sell goods to small sellers? A few hundred pieces per style is already minimal, can you handle it? Even if they sell you small batches, the price will definitely be higher than the wholesale market!)

2. Is it original order? (This can be understood, everyone wants original order goods, but the following questions clearly show features inconsistent with original order goods.)

3. Are the sizes complete? (Original order goods can have complete sizes, customs confiscated or entire orders destroyed, but such goods are rare and generally not available in small batches.)

4. Are all tags included? (For brand orders, auxiliary materials are strictly controlled. Generally, 90% of tail-end goods have complete tags if considered good.)

5. Are there any defects? (Foreign trade original orders have a less flattering name called second-grade products. The reason we can get low prices is that we take all the good and defective products. Otherwise, just taking the good ones would cost more than the factory price. A genuine foreign trade factory sells a child's Lycra base layer with a 2 square centimeter rubber printing pattern for $3.5 per piece, and a New Balance 8020 color-woven collar T-shirt for nearly $5. However, I sell wholesale base layers for 6-8 RMB each and still make a profit. If I say there are no defective products, how could I operate? Generally, I guarantee a defect rate of 5% or less, and refunds are available for rates above 5%. If you want 100% genuine products, don't contact me; look for someone more expensive.)

Saying all this is to tell new sellers that the first step in opening a store is positioning. After determining your position, find suitable sources for your needs. It's unrealistic for retailers to seek wholesale sources. No matter how cheap, don't take too much because you're new to the industry and haven't yet developed the ability to grasp fashion trends. Initially, operate with fewer purchases and more visits. Find the nearest location to stock up, find wholesalers who allow the smallest orders, and restock after selling. Don't be afraid of hard work; one bad purchase could lock up half a month's profit! In the clothing business, having goods isn't profitable unless you see money in hand rather than goods in storage. Over time, as you become familiar with the seasons and styles for purchasing, increase your order quantities to reduce costs. With volume, you'll have bargaining power, but it's still not appropriate to source directly from factories. Don't accidentally turn all your profits into goods. Ten out of ten Taobao sellers claim to have direct factory sources, excluding a few big sellers who support multiple factories, but they are the minority. The rest, who can afford factory quantities? Just by looking at their selling prices, you can tell if they're direct sources.

What if you're not in a big city and there are no wholesale markets nearby?

Some people choose to travel long distances to other cities to source goods. At this point, cost awareness must dominate. How much goods do you plan to bring back? How much will the travel expenses be? Suppose you bring back 100 pieces of clothing at a cost of 1000 RMB in travel expenses; that's really not worth it, adding 10 RMB to the cost of each piece of clothing! Perhaps the clothing isn't even worth 10 RMB! We retail at least double the profit margin, so adding 10 RMB to the cost means the retail price will be 20 RMB higher, which is too obvious. People who shop around can easily tell that you're overpriced.

Online wholesale (net-batch), solves this problem for some people. Although current online wholesale has many shortcomings and payment methods now favor buyers, it solves the issue of insufficient trust between buyers and sellers. The biggest problem is quality. The pictures look good, but the actual product may not be. Sellers exaggerate, and the product received is completely different. These factors greatly constrain the development of online wholesale. Many of us doing online wholesale have started realizing this and are gradually establishing mechanisms of integrity.

Although I personally dislike Alipay (it deducts fees and delays time, and sometimes buyers wait until the transaction automatically expires, once holding nearly 100,000 RMB), standing from the buyer's perspective, for the first transaction, it's essential to use Alipay's guaranteed transaction. Do not accept clothes that do not support Alipay! Just because we don't cheat doesn't mean others won't. Search online, check the store name on Baidu, see if there are any negative reports. Even without any, stay vigilant, take a little sample at a higher price, if it works, proceed with an Alipay transaction. After building trust, transactions can be relaxed. Although online wholesale currently has many shortcomings, Mr. Ma Yun said, "If you don't do e-commerce now, you will definitely regret it in five years." Inspired by his admiration, I started, and it feels great. After all, I'm not facing customers from one region but nationwide. With proper promotion, my products can be sold worldwide. I've cooperated with foreign trade companies on several large orders!

Frequently, buyers propose paying more for less goods, but wanting only one size of clothing. According to their own words: "If it doesn't sell, I can still wear it!" Sounds reasonable, but in reality, they're already entering a misconception right from the start, already doubting themselves. Whatever you do, you need a detailed plan, preferably written down. What are my strengths? What are my weaknesses? How do I plan to proceed? What processes will I go through? What effects will I produce? Even detailed to when I will achieve certain goals. After writing all these, you'll truly discover what you have and what you lack. Fantasizing about the results before achieving them without considering the difficulties ahead, then I suggest you don't start a business; working is better suited for you. But after making a plan, move quickly to implement it. There's always a reason behind hard work. What you can think of, others can too, but what you achieve, others may not necessarily accomplish!

When online store sellers have gained a certain level of traffic and repeat customers, entering a virtuous cycle, sourcing goods should differ from before. Some online store sellers like mixed-style clothes, thinking it's the least money for the most goods, but online stores are not suitable for mixed goods. Selling one or two pieces of a particular style is the hardest for online stores. Even if purchased cheaply, unsold goods are useless. Mixed styles are most suitable for physical stores. A bit more refined mixed styles can sell for over a hundred yuan easily, while poorer ones can be placed outside the store as special offers. Edited by Wangdianle (www.wangdian6.com).

A few months ago, I noticed many laid-off workers starting businesses, street vendors, and small shop owners looking for sources. I gathered some whole-order goods, calculated the average price, added a little profit, and treated them as mixed batches, with surprisingly good results, truly in high demand. But this is not suitable for online stores. After a certain foundation is established, online stores should not pursue variety but focus on fewer styles with lower prices. At this point, rely on the trained eye to take more of a particular style. Taobao has a ranking system called popularity. The longer you sell the same item and the more quantities, the higher your rank naturally becomes. The benefits don't need much explanation. The more you buy and sell quickly, the lower your purchase price. Those interested in studying this can frequently visit 【Yishen】's store on Taobao. Although they also have suspected manipulation reported, watching daily reveals that their store has very few styles, but each style moves incredibly fast, even faster than our wholesale operations. By reaching this level, you can start eating leftovers from the factory or even place orders at large factories.

Finally, let's talk about getting leftovers from the factory.

I often mockingly say that our line of work is similar to begging, not exaggerated. Despite being the ones spending money, we humbly beg. Good leftovers are hard to get, and I have a friend who's a factory manager. Every time I go down to collect goods, I invite the managers of the factories I regularly visit for dinner, ensuring that the goods I get are cheap and of good quality. Sometimes, strong relationships even help by removing some minor defective products without charging you. Don't assume that taking goods directly from the factory is cheaper than from me. Usually, factory leftovers are handled by trusted personnel close to the boss. Without knowing these people, you can only get the market price, and those with poor bargaining skills might end up paying more than what I offer after reselling.
