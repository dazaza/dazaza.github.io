Police officer who was confined for alleged soliciting prostitution quit and sued the police team, but lost in two trials

by nif2dfoe on 2012-02-08 11:47:02

■ Character's Words: If the court reviews the prostitution case itself, based on today's evidence, I am sure that I will win the lawsuit and wear the police uniform again.

★ Character Review

Lü Huanbin, 42 years old, was once a civilian police officer at the Meilin Police Station. In 2005, he was placed under house arrest by the branch for suspected solicitation of prostitution and then resigned and left the police force. Lü Huanbin has never stopped trying to return to the police force. He believed that after the prostitution case occurred, the police did not initiate procedures to investigate the matter, which made him suffer injustice and humiliation.

★ Reason for Recommendation

For the public, the significance of this case does not lie in whether Lü Huanbin solicited prostitution, but why the police did not start the procedure when dealing with his alleged prostitution case?

Despite various setbacks, for six years, Lü Huanbin continued to ask these questions and consistently tried to bring the case back to the courtroom. Regardless of whether his efforts would change his own fate, at least it could urge the government to administer according to law.

It has been six years since he took off his police uniform, but Lü Huanbin is still trying to put it back on, believing that such a day will eventually come. On December 16th, when Nandu reporters met Lü Huanbin, although he had lost the second trial in his lawsuit against the Futian Public Security Bureau, he still talked about the case in an orderly manner, with bright eyes and a loud voice. He showed great confidence in subsequent litigation.

Being accused of soliciting prostitution and resigning voluntarily

On February 27, 2005, Lü Huanbin officially left the police force. At that time, he had been a police officer for 10 years, working in the security team of the Meilin Police Station as a security officer. His regular work included security patrols and handling various security cases, one of which was dealing with prostitution cases. He might not have thought that one day he would lose his job due to "soliciting prostitution."

A prostitute reported to the police station that on the evening of January 30, 2005, she was raped by multiple people, and before being raped, a police officer from the Meilin Police Station had engaged in sexual relations with her. Lü Huanbin was identified by the woman as the police officer who solicited prostitution, was then placed under house arrest, and eventually left the police force.

From an administrative procedure standpoint, Lü Huanbin left the police force voluntarily. In Lü Huanbin's view, making him resign was not out of consideration for his dignity, but rather for the face of the public security bureau. "They just wanted to resolve my issue quickly and didn't want the bureau to lose face."

Lü Huanbin said that this method of handling brought long-term consequences, leading to his continuous appeals over the years without any resolution. "If they had disciplined me for soliciting prostitution, I could have applied for reconsideration or sued the court," Lü Huanbin said. The current problem is that the public security organ did not make such an administrative disciplinary decision, leaving him without recourse for appeal.

Lost both trials

Since leaving the police force in 2005, Lü Huanbin has always reflected issues through the petition channel. However, the feedback he received often was, "We don't say you solicited prostitution; you resigned voluntarily, so why are you coming back now?"

Lü Huanbin said that although he wrote the resignation letter himself, it was out of necessity during the period of house arrest. If he hadn't resigned voluntarily, he might have faced criminal punishment for group debauchery. Lü Huanbin said that he compromised and wrote the resignation letter, leaving the police force.

Until April 7, 2011, in a reply opinion book regarding the handling of a petition matter issued by the Futian Public Security Branch, it mentioned, "After investigation, at around 3 a.m. on January 30, 2005, while on duty, you violated regulations and left your post without permission... Under Wang's arrangement, you engaged in sexual acts with prostitute Zheng in room 311."

Lü Huanbin said that this was the first time in five years after leaving the police force that he was confirmed to have solicited prostitution. From this, he began legal proceedings, suing the Public Security Branch, arguing that the branch made mistakes in handling the case.

Before the litigation began, he appeared very confident but also worried. He lost the first trial, where the court ruled that the reply from the branch to the petition was not a specific administrative act, dismissing his lawsuit without reviewing the core issue of whether he had solicited prostitution. Recently, he lost the second trial as well, with the Shenzhen Intermediate People's Court rejecting his appeal for the same reason.

Awaiting review of the prostitution case itself

Despite losing both trials, on December 16, 2011, when Nandu reporters met him, he still showed great confidence. "I will wear the police uniform again; such a day will surely come," he said. He and his lawyer studied the case and planned to appeal.

Lü Huanbin said that according to the Supreme People's Court's "Provisions on Several Issues Concerning the Trial of Administrative Cases Related to Government Information Disclosure," if other specific administrative actions by administrative agencies in the work of government information disclosure infringe upon their legitimate rights, citizens can legally file administrative lawsuits, and courts should accept them.

"The Public Security Branch has already released information through the media, saying that I had sexual relations with prostitutes. This is also a form of government information disclosure, and according to the Supreme Court's regulations, the court should accept my administrative lawsuit," Lü Huanbin said.

The biggest problem he encountered was that the court has never reviewed the prostitution case itself. "When the branch handled my case, it did not initiate the procedure. If I had solicited prostitution, it should have collected various pieces of evidence according to reason and then imposed administrative punishment, detaining me," Lü Huanbin said. However, the branch did not initiate the procedure, and he was not detained. The final result was just resignation and leaving the police force. "If the court reviews the prostitution case itself, based on the current evidence, I will definitely win the lawsuit and wear the police uniform again," he said.

Lü Huanbin's days as a police officer are far behind him. On the afternoon of December 16, Lü Huanbin rode his three-wheeled motorcycle urgently back to his shop. The rear compartment of the motorcycle was filled with beer and various drinks, ready to be delivered to various small stalls. The former policeman is now the owner of a small wholesale store in Qing Shui He. His shop is small and cluttered with goods. On either side of the shop are car repair shops and hardware stores, making the area full of oil stains and emitting an unpleasant smell. The toughest times are already in the past; there was a time when he fell victim to business fraud and had to borrow money to live. But a better life has yet to come.
