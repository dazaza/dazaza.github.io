Taobao draws a national "insomnia map": Shanghai has the most people who can't fall asleep easily

by anonymous on 2013-07-24 17:09:04

"Every day, I go to bed around 11 o'clock, then I watch videos and browse online stores on my phone. I don't feel sleepy until around 1 o'clock." Mr. Ding, a 30-year-old white-collar worker who works in a business building on Hangzhou Moganshan Road, complained. In fact, more and more people are starting to have trouble sleeping. "People who sleep are all similar, but those who can't sleep have their own reasons," the Taobao shopkeepers ask humorously, "Dear, why can't you sleep?" They take advantage of this trend to sell "sleep aid" products.

Yesterday, a set of data published by Taobao was also quite interesting. The group of people who can't sleep has very distinct regional characteristics. In the top 5 cities with the highest per capita purchase volume of eye masks and earplugs in the last 5 months, all four first-tier cities - Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, and Shenzhen - made the list collectively. Hangzhou is the only non-first-tier city in the top five, ranking third.

The most active "insomniacs":

23 million people shop online at midnight

"At this hour, is anyone still awake?" Nowadays, such midnight insomnia-related Weibo posts have become commonplace. "Insomniacs" instantly transform into "night shoppers," using their mobile phones to shop on Taobao while lying in bed. Their enthusiasm for shopping does not wane as the night grows deeper; instead, late-night becomes one of the high-order times for mobile online purchases.

"In such hot weather, I definitely wouldn't go out shopping during the day. When I can't sleep at night, I use that time to shop." Ms. Li from Hangzhou is a typical night owl. She says she often doesn't feel sleepy after 12 o'clock, so she opens her phone and browses Taobao. "Yesterday, in just an hour, I bought two pairs of sandals and one dress." Finally, with the satisfaction of shopping, she finally fell asleep. However, her money was flowing out every day.

There are no shortage of such "night shoppers." Taobao's data shows that in 2012, a total of 22.832 million people shopped on Taobao between 11 PM and 5 AM. Among them, a significant number of men also stayed up late browsing Taobao, accounting for 48% of the total, while women accounted for 52%.

The most insomniac provinces:

Jiangsu, Guangdong, Zhejiang

What to do when you really can't sleep? An increasing number of people start using external aids like earplugs and eye masks to fall asleep. Smart online store owners have obviously spotted the business opportunity. A search for earplugs on Taobao revealed more than 200,000 items for sale, and nearly 90,000 eye masks.

The "sleep aid market" is quite promising. Just the product of eye masks alone comes in various types such as 3D, cartoon patterns, summer-appropriate ice packs, silk eye masks, bamboo charcoal eye masks, etc. Since the start of summer, ice pack eye masks have been very popular. On one Taobao store, the monthly transaction volume of a single item exceeded ten thousand pieces.

Many sellers also focus on pillows, offering lavender pillows, buckwheat pillows, and even foods like millet and jujubes labeled as promoting sleep.

The "insomnia map" shows that according to Taobao's transaction data over the past 5 months, looking at the proportion of people buying earplugs and eye masks relative to the total population of each city, all four first-tier cities - Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, and Shenzhen - made the list. Hangzhou, which ranked third, is the only non-first-tier city among them.

Top 10 cities purchasing earplugs: Shanghai, Beijing, Hangzhou, Guangzhou, Shenzhen, Nanjing, Suzhou, Wuhan, Chongqing, Ningbo.

Top 10 cities purchasing eye masks: Shanghai, Beijing, Hangzhou, Shenzhen, Guangzhou, Chongqing, Nanjing, Suzhou, Wuhan, Chengdu.

Shanghai can be called the city with the most severe insomnia. Jiangsu, Guangdong, and Zhejiang are the three provinces with the worst sleep quality.

"The inability to fall asleep at night and the difficulty waking up in the morning" forms a vicious cycle. The cities where people have the hardest time falling asleep at night are also the cities where people find it hardest to wake up in the morning. In the past 5 months, the top five cities with the largest population purchasing alarm clocks are also Shanghai, Beijing, Hangzhou, Shenzhen, and Guangzhou.

"I need to wear earplugs every night to fall asleep, but then I can't hear my phone's alarm in the morning. After setting my phone's alarm to vibrate, I'm afraid it won't be powerful enough to wake me up, so I bought a vibrating alarm clock." Xiao Mao's "sleeping" and "waking up" equipment has become essential for many insomniacs.

The most helpless reason for buying earplugs:

Husband snores

So who are these insomniacs? Online store owners know best. "The number of people who need earplugs to sleep increases every year, there are more customers asking about them, and the age range is getting wider. Basically, students and urban white-collar workers account for 90% of total sales," said the owner of a store selling earplugs.

Indeed, the store's flagship product - a 19 yuan earplug - sold 19,000 pieces in just one month.

Why can't everyone sleep? There are actually many reasons. It's understood that students who buy eye masks and earplugs may live in dormitories. Mobile devices are very common among students, and after bedtime, some classmates might still be playing games. To avoid being disturbed, they simply "isolate" themselves.

White-collar workers, on the other hand, experience greater work pressure during the day, leading to sleep problems at night. Some buyers humorously state that they bought earplugs because "my husband snores too loudly."

Taobao's data shows that the majority of people buying earplugs and eye masks are from the post-90s and post-80s generations. Taking the eye mask sales over the past 5 months as an example, the post-90s generation is the largest demand group, with 36% of the eye masks purchased by people aged 18-24. Additionally, people aged 25-29 account for more than 30% of the total.

From the purchasing ratio of various products, men are more likely to have trouble sleeping than women, with about 20% more male insomniacs than female.
