CNTV Sues Guangdong and Jiangsu Telecommunications: Contradictions Between Radio and Television and Telecommunications Come to the Forefront

by tuoqing1188 on 2011-08-04 09:30:46

The much-touted convergence of the three networks has yet to show any sign of "integration", while conflicts between radio and television broadcasting and telecom sectors have been brought to the fore. Recently, a lawsuit was filed by the radio and television sector against the telecom sector.

Liu Lu, a relevant person in charge of the Copyright Department of China Network Television (CNTV), yesterday confirmed to our newspaper that CNTV had, in late July, initiated legal action with local courts over Jiangsu Telecom and Guangdong Telecom's infringement of copyright for broadcasting video content from CCTV-related TV programs on their IPTV platforms without authorization. The court hearing date is still pending notification from the court.

Guangdong Telecom responded, stating that the company has cooperated for many years with Shanghai Media Group, which holds an IPTV license, and there absolutely is no issue of infringement. As for whether they will go to court or seek reconciliation in the future, this remains unclear.

From the perspective of the telecom sector, CNTV's move is aimed at forcing telecom operators to abandon Shanghai Media Group and directly cooperate with CNTV.

"Radio and television pointing the finger at telecom operators still revolves around the old problem of access to the IPTV broadcast control platform." Wu Chunyong, editor-in-chief of Converged Network, believes that this round of conflict has also involved Shanghai Media Group, revealing initial power struggles within the radio and television system, potentially damaging relations between both sides.

Legal actions have already been filed at the Intermediate People's Court of Guangzhou and Nanjing.

Liu Lu, a relevant person in charge of the Copyright Department of CNTV, yesterday informed Nanfang Daily that CNTV started systematic investigation and evidence collection regarding the IPTV infringement by Guangdong Telecom and Jiangsu Telecom in April this year, and officially filed lawsuits in the Intermediate People's Courts of Guangzhou and Nanjing in late July. Regarding the court hearing dates, they are still awaiting notifications from the courts.

It is learned that Guangdong Telecom has collaborated with Shanghai Media Group on ITV (Broadband Internet Audiovisual) since 2006, currently offering eight service categories including live broadcasts, replays, on-demand viewing, information, convenience services, play buttons, bookmarks, and search functions.

In Guangdong, users can install ITV by paying 380 yuan per year, with access to over 60 channels, including the ability to watch CCTV programs either live or on-demand. Guangdong Telecom customer service explained that live broadcasts of CCTV channels 3, 5, 6, and 8 are not yet available, but these can be viewed on-demand.

"We have been cooperating for many years with Shanghai Media Group, which holds an IPTV license, and there absolutely is no issue of infringement," Guangdong Telecom emphasized.

However, from CNTV's perspective, all CCTV channels and their associated TV programs must be uniformly placed on CNTV's broadcast control platform. If other platforms use them without authorization, CNTV has the right to pursue legal responsibility.

"According to the authorization from CCTV, CNTV exclusively enjoys the rights to disseminate, broadcast (including real-time and delayed broadcasting) CCTV channels and their associated TV programs to the public through new media platforms such as the internet, mobile phones, and IPTV," meaning that any new media wishing to broadcast CCTV programs must do so through CNTV.

Why file a lawsuit now for a business that has existed since 2006? Liu Lu responded, saying they indeed knew about the infringement by telecoms long ago, but reasons for choosing this timing are inconvenient to disclose.

Promoting telecom integration into its broadcast control platform

Legal protection efforts in Guangdong and Jiangsu provinces are just the beginning.

Liu Lu emphasized that investigations have uncovered unauthorized use of large amounts of CCTV program content by IPTV platforms of several local telecom operators. Current findings involve Hangzhou, Wuhan, Chongqing, and other regions. CNTV will gradually launch large-scale legal protection actions. "This lawsuit against Jiangsu Telecom and Guangdong Telecom is only the first step in this legal protection campaign."

Liu Lu also revealed that "currently, telecom has not sent anyone to negotiate related matters with the radio and television sector."

It is understood that the Copyright Department of CNTV is a newly established department specifically targeting new media copyrights. In June this year, CNTV launched large-scale new media copyright protection lawsuits against PPTV (Shanghai Polymedia Technology Co., Ltd.).

Wu Chunyong, an expert in radio and television, believes that this copyright lawsuit by CNTV is a public declaration to place all CCTV channels and their associated TV programs uniformly on CNTV's broadcast control platform, with the core aim of warning telecom operators that they must integrate into CNTV's IPTV broadcast control platform.

At the beginning of this year, CNTV completed the construction of its central-level IPTV broadcast control platform. There are unconfirmed reports that CNTV plans to add 5 million IPTV users this year.

"The business model for IPTV cooperation with telecom is simple; CNTV's broadcast control platform only needs to provide content, while transmission, operation, maintenance, users, and markets are all handled by telecom operators." Unverified sources claim that for every new IPTV user developed, the broadcast control platform can earn 15 yuan per household per month, resulting in nearly 200 million yuan annual net income in a city with a population scale of one million.

Currently, the number of domestic IPTV users in China has exceeded 11 million households, with users in Jiangsu, Shanghai, and Guangdong ranking among the top three. For telecom companies, once connected to the radio and television broadcast control platform, telecom operators will completely become "pipelines," hence the various delays.

To promote telecom integration into the IPTV broadcast control platform, radio and television authorities have taken aggressive measures: issuing a notice in July to halt the provision of audiovisual program services to TV terminals via internet set-top boxes (DVD players, etc.), followed closely by CNTV's copyright lawsuit.

"For acts of infringement, we will initiate lawsuits, not solely targeting telecom operators. However, if telecom operators can connect their IPTV business with the local CNTV sub-platform in their respective areas, cooperation would be the best outcome," Liu Lu stated simultaneously indicating that CNTV has successful cooperation cases with local telecoms. Yunnan TV and CNTV jointly established Yunnan Love Network IPTV Company and fully cooperated with Yunnan Telecom to conduct IPTV business in Yunnan.

Internal competition begins within the radio and television system

The lawsuit between CNTV and telecom has also extended to Shanghai Media Group and Bestv, its subsidiary IPTV service provider.

Since 2005, Shanghai Media Group has collaborated with China Telecom and China Netcom, piloting and commercially launching IPTV services in Harbin, Shanghai, and other places. Guangdong Telecom has also maintained a long-term partnership with Shanghai Media Group.

According to the pilot policies of the triple network convergence and the regulations of the State Administration of Radio, Film, and Television regarding the IPTV broadcast control platform, CNTV's IPTV broadcast control platform is the sole central-level control platform, while Bestv continues to operate its IPTV control platform only in the four designated pilot cities. Broadcast control platforms in other regions must be transferred to CNTV through internal coordination.

Telecom insiders also indicated that CNTV's lawsuit against telecom is precisely aimed at forcing telecom operators to abandon Shanghai Media Group and directly cooperate with CNTV.

"CNTV, Bestv, Wasu, and others all belong to the radio and television system. In the future, other radio and television institutions will also face issues concerning the construction of content broadcast control platforms, making internal competition and power struggles within the system inevitable," said Wu Chunyong.

Nanfang Daily reporter Wang Xiaoxing, intern Xie Ting
