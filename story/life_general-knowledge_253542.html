You can claim compensation from the decoration company if the air quality is unqualified.

by longersing on 2009-10-29 16:52:18

If the air quality is unqualified, you can claim compensation from the decoration company. The house owners who have their houses decorated always complain: why does my house smell so bad after decoration? When they try to make a claim to the decoration company, they are rejected. In the future, there will be no need to worry about this. China has already implemented the "Management Measures for Residential Interior Decoration and Renovation". We can make claims through legal procedures if we are not satisfied.

If the air quality of the house is not up to standard after decoration, you can claim compensation from the decoration company. Yesterday, the Indoor Environment Monitoring Center of the Provincial Environmental Monitoring Center reminded consumers that if they entrust the decoration company to decorate their house, when the project is completed and accepted, in addition to checking the "visible details", they should also check the "invisible details" - air quality. If you entrust a decoration company to decorate your house, the indoor air quality must also meet the standards. For a long time, most consumers have ignored or do not know that they have such rights.

Ms. Zhu reported that she hired a decoration company to decorate her new house this year. When she accepted the work, although she was relatively satisfied with the overall effect, she found that the smell in the room was very unpleasant, so she suspected that the air quality was severely polluted. The decoration company told her that all new houses were like this, and it would be fine after one or two months. After ventilating the new house for more than a month, Ms. Zhu found that the smell in the room was still strong. She wanted to know whether this situation was a quality problem? Can she argue with the decoration company?

China has already implemented the "Management Measures for Residential Interior Decoration and Renovation", which clearly stipulates the requirements for "indoor environmental quality": "When the decoration person entrusts an enterprise to decorate the interior of the residence, after the completion of the decoration project, the air quality shall meet the relevant national standards. The decorator can entrust a qualified detection unit to test the air quality. If the test is unqualified, the decoration enterprise shall redo the work, and the responsible party shall bear the corresponding loss."

At present, there are two situations for consumers who entrust decoration companies to decorate their houses: one is full entrustment, and the other is that the decoration company provides design, auxiliary materials, and construction, while the consumer provides main materials. In the first case, consumers can completely ask the testing unit to test the air quality during acceptance, and require the decoration company to redo the work if it is unqualified; in the latter case, the decoration company may easily wrangle with the consumer because both parties may deny that the pollution is caused by the materials they provided.

How can consumers use national standards to protect their legitimate rights and interests? Mr. Wan introduced three necessary measures: First, add a sentence when signing the contract, that is, when consumers sign the decoration contract, if there is no indoor environment clause in the original contract, they must add a sentence: "Must comply with the national indoor environmental air quality standard." Second, keep a sample after purchasing the material, that is, when purchasing indoor decoration materials or asking the decoration company to purchase materials, consumers had better keep a sample. Once indoor environmental pollution problems are found after the project is completed, this can be used as evidence to determine responsibility. Third, have an indoor environmental testing report when accepting the project, because according to the national relevant requirements, newly built, expanded, and renovated civil architectural projects must undergo indoor environmental pollution testing, otherwise, the project cannot be handed over and accepted.
