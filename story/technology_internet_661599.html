Fire protection rules for steel structure engineering and selection of fireproof coatings

by lsjgkkgfef4 on 2012-02-09 10:42:23

Fire Protection Regulations for Steel Structure Engineering and Selection of Fireproof Coatings - Paints and Coatings - Industry News - Building Materials Network

It is reported that a fire broke out in a steel structure warehouse in a certain place. After the firefighters fought for several hours, two large steel beams lost their strength and fell from a high altitude. Such cases have triggered people's attention to fire protection in steel structure buildings.

Since the reform and opening up of our country, with the significant increase in steel production, steel structure architectural engineering has developed rapidly. There are two misunderstandings about the fire protection of steel structures and fireproof coatings: one is that steel structures are strong and not afraid of fire, so prevention is neglected; the other is that once steel structure projects are coated with fireproof coatings, they will be completely safe and cannot catch fire. In fact, fireproof coatings can only delay the time, allowing for personnel evacuation, firefighting rescue, and preventing collapse. It does not mean that it will not burn or cannot be damaged by fire.

Steel is a high-temperature sensitive material, its strength and deformation will undergo drastic changes as the temperature rises. For ordinary construction steel, at around 500°C, its strength decreases to 40%~50%; at 600°C, its load-bearing capacity is almost entirely lost. The fire resistance limit of generally uncoated (exposed) steel structures is only 10~20 minutes. Once a fire occurs, the building will quickly collapse, causing catastrophic accidents and severely damaging lives and property. Therefore, it is necessary to take fire protection measures for steel structures, enabling timely evacuation of personnel, reducing damage to steel structures during fires, and making every effort to extinguish the fire within the fire resistance limit (1~2 hours), avoiding local or overall collapse of steel structures which could cause casualties and property loss.

Fire protection for steel structure projects is the top priority, and design and production must comply with relevant standards such as GB14907-2000 "Fireproof Coating for Steel Structures", GB50205-2001 "Code for Acceptance of Construction Quality of Steel Structure Engineering", GB50016-2005 "Building Design Fire Protection Code", GB50045-95 "Fire Protection Code for High-Rise Civil Building Design", CECS24-90 "Technical Specification for Application of Fireproof Coating for Steel Structures", and DG/TJ08-008-2000 "Technical Code for Fire Protection of Building Steel Structures".

The selection of suppliers for steel structure fireproof coatings must include the following essential documents: valid inspection reports issued by the National Fireproof Building Materials Quality Supervision and Inspection Center; valid product type approval certificates issued by the Fire Product Qualification Evaluation Center of the Ministry of Public Security; professional contracting qualification certificates for fire protection facilities issued by the certification authority of the Ministry of Construction of the People's Republic of China; and safety production licenses issued by local construction administrative departments.

Rules for selecting steel structure fireproof coatings: For high-rise and multi-story building steel structures requiring permanent fire protection, when their specified fire resistance limit exceeds 1.5 hours, non-expanding fireproof coatings for steel structures can be selected; for indoor exposed steel structures, lightweight roof steel structures, and steel structures with decorative requirements, when their specified fire resistance limit is less than 1.5 hours, ultra-thin or thin-coat type fireproof coatings for steel structures can be selected; for outdoor steel structures, outdoor-appropriate fireproof coatings for steel structures should be chosen. The selected variety of fireproof coatings should have been tested for at least one year on outdoor pilot projects without significant changes in coating performance; for steel structure projects requiring a fire resistance limit exceeding 1.5 hours and for outdoor use, thin-coat type fireproof coatings are not recommended.

For ultra-thin and thin-coat type fireproof coatings after construction, the foaming condition of the coating under fire can be inspected on-site. The flame of the blowtorch should just touch the surface of the coating, and the flame should burn vertically against the surface of the coating for 15 minutes. Observe the foaming state of the coating. The foaming thickness of ultra-thin coatings should be more than ten times the thickness of the coating, while the foaming thickness of thin coatings should be five times the thickness of the coating.

Steel structure fireproof coatings are classified into three categories according to their thickness: ultra-thin type fireproof coatings for steel structures, with a coating thickness less than or equal to 3 millimeters; thin-coat type fireproof coatings for steel structures, with a coating thickness greater than 3 millimeters and less than or equal to 7 millimeters; thick-coat type fireproof coatings for steel structures, with a coating thickness greater than 7 millimeters and less than or equal to approximately 45 millimeters.
