JIAYUAN.COM's Fourth Quarter Revenue Reached 14.1 Million US Dollars, Increased by 54% Year-over-Year

by bingo123 on 2012-03-08 17:23:50

Sina Tech News - March 5, 5:30 AM EST (March 6, 6:30 AM CST) - Jiayuan.com (Nasdaq: DATE), a leading online dating platform in China, has released its unaudited financial results for the fourth quarter and full year ended December 31, 2011. The report indicates that Jiayuan's net revenue reached RMB 88.6 million (USD 14.1 million) in Q4, representing a 53.6% increase year-over-year. However, the company reported a net loss of RMB 1.2 million (USD 0.19 million), compared to a net profit of RMB 10 million in the same period last year.

**Fourth Quarter 2011 Highlights**

- Net revenues reached RMB 88.6 million (USD 14.1 million), consistent with the company’s previous guidance, marking a year-over-year increase of 53.6%.

- Operating loss was RMB 4.3 million (USD 0.69 million) compared to an operating profit of RMB 13.2 million in Q4 2010. Excluding the impact of share-based compensation expenses, non-GAAP operating loss was RMB 0.322 million (USD 0.051 million) compared to a non-GAAP operating profit of RMB 15.3 million in Q4 2010. This loss was primarily due to a special marketing campaign costing approximately RMB 10 million, which increased sales and marketing expenses. Additionally, year-end bonuses led to higher labor costs this quarter.

- Net loss attributable to Jiayuan was RMB 1.2 million (USD 0.19 million), compared to a net profit of RMB 10 million in Q4 2010. Excluding the impact of share-based compensation expenses, non-GAAP net profit attributable to Jiayuan was RMB 2.8 million (USD 0.449 million), compared to RMB 12.1 million in Q4 2010.

**Full Year 2011 Highlights**

- Net revenues reached RMB 330 million (USD 52.6 million), up 97.7% year-over-year.

- Operating profit was RMB 39.8 million (USD 6.3 million), up 66.2% year-over-year. Excluding the impact of share-based compensation expenses, non-GAAP operating profit was RMB 70.3 million (USD 11.2 million), up 127.5% year-over-year.

- Net profit attributable to Jiayuan was RMB 37.2 million (USD 5.9 million), up 122.8% year-over-year. Excluding the impact of share-based compensation expenses, non-GAAP net profit attributable to Jiayuan was RMB 67.8 million (USD 10.8 million), up 186.3% year-over-year.

CEO Haiyan Gong stated, “In the fourth quarter of 2011, Jiayuan saw record growth in new registered users and a commendable 53.6% year-over-year increase in net revenues.”

Gong added, “As the largest serious dating website in China, we continue to refine our model by leveraging Jiayuan's extensive national network to provide exceptional services on the largest online dating platform in the industry. Our strategic focus on wireless platforms is rapidly developing, with location-based mobile services complementing our online map search functionality to enhance the effectiveness of our matchmaking services. By the end of Q4 2011, over 2 million smart device activations had accessed Jiayuan through mobile clients.”

CFO Shangxiu Gu remarked, “Despite being the off-season for user activity, we achieved robust net revenue growth while increasing our marketing investment and focusing on user-centric product development. We launched a significant brand promotion campaign in Q4 to further strengthen Jiayuan's brand awareness and consolidate our market position. This campaign will benefit our marketing efforts in 2012. We remain committed to developing wireless products as a key driver of future growth.”

**Operational Highlights for Q4 2011**

- Registered users totaled 55,928,106 as of December 31, 2011, including approximately 5.4 million new users added in Q4.

- Average monthly active users were 5,179,713, compared to 4,403,262 in Q4 2010 and 5,560,387 in Q3 2011.

- Average monthly paying users were 1,172,159, compared to 761,271 in Q4 2010 and 1,250,439 in Q3 2011.

- ARPU (average revenue per paying user per month) for online services was RMB 21.5, compared to RMB 20.3 in Q4 2010 and RMB 20.2 in Q3 2011.

**Financial Highlights for Q4 2011**

- Net revenues were RMB 88.6 million (USD 14.1 million), up 53.6% year-over-year. Revenue growth was driven by strong performance in Jiayuan's online services. Sequentially, net revenues decreased by 2.5% from RMB 90.9 million in Q3 2011 due to seasonal declines in user activity.

- Online service net revenues were RMB 75.7 million (USD 12.0 million), accounting for 85.4% of total net revenues, up 63.4% year-over-year and slightly down sequentially from RMB 76.0 million in Q3 2011, mainly due to lower user activity during the off-season.

- Offline activities and premium matchmaking service net revenues were RMB 12.7 million (USD 2.0 million), accounting for 14.3% of total net revenues, up 42.7% year-over-year and down 7.2% sequentially from RMB 13.7 million in Q3 2011.

- Cost of revenues was RMB 29.6 million (USD 4.7 million), up 54.8% year-over-year and 5.9% sequentially from RMB 27.9 million in Q3 2011, primarily due to business expansion.

- Gross profit was RMB 59.0 million (USD 9.4 million), up 53.0% year-over-year and down 6.3% sequentially from RMB 63.0 million in Q3 2011.

- Selling and marketing expenses were RMB 43.0 million (USD 6.8 million), up 162.4% year-over-year and 66.5% sequentially from RMB 25.8 million in Q3 2011, mainly due to increased spending on promoting Jiayuan's wireless client and a large-scale brand promotion campaign in Q4.

- General and administrative expenses were RMB 16.0 million (USD 2.5 million), up 79.9% year-over-year and 27.6% sequentially from RMB 12.6 million in Q3 2011, mainly due to expanded employee headcount, increased year-end bonuses, and professional service fees required to meet compliance standards as a listed company.

- Research and development expenses were RMB 4.3 million (USD 0.686 million), compared to RMB 0.105 million in Q4 2010 and RMB 3.1 million in Q3 2011, mainly due to the expansion of the R&D team to enhance capabilities.

- Operating loss was RMB 4.3 million (USD 0.69 million), compared to an operating profit of RMB 13.2 million in Q4 2010 and RMB 21.5 million in Q3 2011. Excluding the impact of share-based compensation expenses, non-GAAP operating loss was RMB 0.322 million (USD 0.051 million), compared to a non-GAAP operating profit of RMB 15.3 million in Q4 2010 and RMB 25.2 million in Q3 2011.

- Foreign exchange gains were RMB 2.8 million (USD 0.439 million), compared to RMB 6.5 million in Q3 2011 and no foreign exchange gains or losses in Q4 2010. These gains were mainly due to the appreciation of the RMB against the USD during the period when IPO proceeds were converted into RMB and recorded on the books of the offshore company, whose functional currency is USD.

- Net loss attributable to Jiayuan was RMB 1.2 million (USD 0.19 million), compared to a net profit of RMB 10 million in Q4 2010 and RMB 24.9 million in Q3 2011. Excluding the impact of share-based compensation expenses, non-GAAP net profit attributable to Jiayuan was RMB 2.8 million (USD 0.449 million), compared to RMB 12.1 million in Q4 2010 and RMB 28.6 million in Q3 2011.

**Full Year Financial Results 2011**

- Net revenues were RMB 330 million (USD 52.6 million), up 97.7% year-over-year, mainly due to strong growth in Jiayuan's online services.

- Online service net revenues were RMB 280 million (USD 44.0 million), accounting for 83.7% of total net revenues, up 106.8% year-over-year.

- Offline activities and premium matchmaking service net revenues were RMB 51.2 million (USD 8.1 million), accounting for 15.5% of total net revenues, up 95.1% year-over-year.

- Cost of revenues was RMB 100 million (USD 16.7 million), up 71.7% year-over-year, primarily due to business expansion, increased employee headcount, bandwidth costs, and commissions paid to third-party suppliers.

- Gross profit was RMB 230 million (USD 36.0 million), up 112.5% year-over-year.

- Selling and marketing expenses were RMB 110 million (USD 17.6 million), up 91.0% year-over-year, mainly due to business expansion.

- General and administrative expenses were RMB 64.3 million (USD 10.2 million), up 164.2% year-over-year, mainly due to business expansion and professional service fees required to meet compliance standards as a listed company. G&A expenses included post-IPO share-based compensation expenses of RMB 17.5 million related to equity incentives granted to consultants, former and current employees.

- Research and development expenses were RMB 11.8 million (USD 1.9 million), compared to RMB 0.381 million in 2010, mainly due to the expansion of the R&D team to enhance capabilities.

- Operating profit was RMB 39.8 million (USD 6.3 million), up 66.2% year-over-year. Excluding the impact of share-based compensation expenses, non-GAAP operating profit was RMB 70.3 million (USD 11.2 million), up 127.5% year-over-year.

- Foreign exchange gains were RMB 8.9 million (USD 1.4 million), compared to no foreign exchange gains or losses in 2010.

- Net profit attributable to Jiayuan was RMB 37.2 million (USD 5.9 million), up 122.8% year-over-year. Excluding the impact of share-based compensation expenses, non-GAAP net profit attributable to Jiayuan was RMB 67.8 million (USD 10.8 million), up 186.3% year-over-year.

**First Quarter 2012 Outlook**

Jiayuan currently expects net revenues for the first quarter of 2012 to be between RMB 90 million and RMB 92 million. This guidance is based on the company's current and preliminary expectations and is subject to change.
