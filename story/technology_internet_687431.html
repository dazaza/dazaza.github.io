BMW parked at the entrance of CCTV for a year, was towed away by the traffic police after being reported

by yy2afg7tia on 2012-03-02 15:49:04

A BMW parked in front of CCTV for a year was towed away by the traffic police after being reported [http://www.hanyue-sh.com]

[What the hell, a horse [http://www.hanyue-sh.com]] is finally [led] away today.

A nearly 500,000 yuan new BMW X1 has been parked on the roadside outside the west gate of CCTV for almost a year. This morning, this [what the heck horse] finally moved, and the traffic police towed it to the law enforcement station.

This dust-covered BMW X1 is somewhat famous online. After its pictures were recently posted online, some [informed] netizens joked that the car was given by a big spender to a host at CCTV, but the host didn't accept it, so the big spender just abandoned the car on the side of the road.

Last night, reporters found this BMW on the bicycle lane of Liulinguan Road outside the west gate of CCTV. The body of the car was covered with about 1 centimeter thick dust, the gaps in the windshield wipers were filled with dry branches, and there were spots of bird droppings on the hood.

The car has completely lost its original color. It's a BMW X1 [http://www.hanyue-sh.com], model xDrive 25i, with an official guide price of 470,000 yuan.

Reporters noticed that the car had no license plates front or back, and the [fuel consumption label] on the windshield hadn't been removed. Wiping off the dirt on the glass to look inside, there were no personal items visible in the car. The entire car looked brand new and hadn't been registered yet.

"This car has been here for almost a year, I've never seen anyone come to drive it." Three parking fee collectors by the roadside said that although there are parking spaces on both sides of Liulinguan Road, this BMW was parked on the bicycle lane.

Security guards from a research institute near CCTV recalled that this car came around May last year.

Mr. Lin, a nearby resident who takes a walk every day to this place, could always see this BMW that went from shiny to "dirty-faced", "I feel heartbroken looking at it, such a good car, how can the owner be so heartless?"

Regarding the "legend" of the big spender giving the car as a gift, reporters did not get confirmation from relevant units.

●Latest Progress

After the reporter's complaint, the traffic police towed the car away.

After receiving the reporter's report, the traffic police of Princess Tomb Traffic Team went to check.

At 9:00 am, the traffic police arrived at the scene, wiped off the dust on the front windshield, and recorded the vehicle identification number (VIN) on the lower left corner of the windshield.

Subsequently, the traffic police called a tow truck to take the BMW away, and wrote on the ground with chalk: [Please go to the Princess Tomb Traffic Team Enforcement Station for handling].

The traffic police said that they had previously received reports from citizens, knowing that the car had been "abandoned" for more than half a year. But because the car did not have a license plate, the traffic police were unable to contact the owner.

It is understood that the law enforcement department will contact the owner based on the VIN. Since the car was illegally parked and did not have a license plate, the owner will be dealt with according to the law when the time comes.
