[Eat and Travel] Step into a millennium-old ancient city, feel the vicissitudes of life and modernity [Qiaozhi Forum]

by cozhi on 2008-05-29 12:20:04

The mighty 800-li Yimeng... The seventy-two Gu formations are its most beautiful scenery...

Jiwang Gu is the foremost of the Gu formations. Its top is broad, stretching several kilometers north to south, and at its widest point east to west, it's also two kilometers wide, displaying a regal grandeur. It's also the only Gu with human habitation, possessing a rich cultural heritage of an ancient walled city site and the stunning natural beauty of the Yimeng region, attracting more and more people to learn about Yimeng and the culture of Jiwang City.

In 256 BC, King Nan of Zhou, who had reigned for 59 years, was captured alive by the Qin State, thus bringing to an end the 879-year-old Zhou Kingdom. Amidst the fall of his nation and the loss of his family, Ji Zhao, then 42 years old, led the remaining troops to what was then called Xidagu or Jiwang Gu. He intended to use the perilous terrain and dense forests of Jiwang Gu to defend against enemies. He also planned to utilize the vastness of the Gu's top, which had arable land and drinkable water, to train and rest his soldiers in preparation for reclaiming his kingdom. Thus, he built houses on the top of the Gu that were later referred to as the palace and the Golden Hall, preparing to lay low and regroup here.

Today, there remain many scenic spots on the top of the Gu, such as the tomb of King Ji, the tomb of the queen, the drumming platform, the Ji Wang Hall, the royal palace, the military training ground, the Eight Treasures琉璃Well, Dita Spring, and Yin Fen Spring.

Standing atop the Gu, one can enjoy the magnificent view of a cluster of over twenty Gu formations. At the base of the Gu lie scattered villages, terraced fields, and curling wisps of cooking smoke. One can hear the distant crowing of roosters, barking of dogs, and calls of livestock like cattle and sheep from the villages at any time, vividly embodying Tao Yuanming's poem: "Warm and distant human villages, gently rising cooking smoke from the ruins."

With such perilous terrain and such picturesque scenery, no wonder King Ji chose this place as his stronghold for recuperation back then. Now Jiwang Gu has been developed into "Heavenly Royal City," aiming not only to restore "Ji Wang City" and recreate the scene of the decline and retreat of the Zhou State when King Ji trained his soldiers hoping to reclaim his kingdom, but also to fully develop the beautiful landscapes and simple folk customs of Greater Yimeng, allowing everyone to comprehensively understand Mount Yimeng and the Yimeng people.

"The seventy-two Gu are lovable, the seventy-two Gu are lovable, but the most lovable of all is none other than Jiwang Gu..." Marshal Chen Yi once exclaimed so while commanding battles in the Yimeng Mountains. "Sages often live in solitude, while tigers and wolves seldom live in groups." For a long time, Jiwang Gu remained silent. It is hoped that after development, "Heavenly Royal City" will fully display her profound cultural heritage and the beautiful scenery of Yimeng Mountain, offering everyone a brand-new experience.
