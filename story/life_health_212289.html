Six Weight Loss Tips for Fashionable Women

by lk0lifeb on 2009-07-22 01:14:30

1. Reduce Caloric Intake

Reducing caloric intake is the most practical way to promote body fat reduction. When fewer calories are consumed, the body will regularly use its fat reserves as energy. However, a sudden and drastic reduction in calorie intake is not advisable, as it can lead to muscle fatigue, atrophy, and slow down the metabolism.

If you want to reduce only body fat, the decrease in caloric intake should be controlled within 10-15%. For example, if a bodybuilder consumes 4000 calories per day, then it should be reduced to 3600 calories (10%) or 3400 calories (15%). The extent of the reduction depends on how much body fat you wish to lose.

2. Increase Caloric Expenditure

The easiest way to promote caloric expenditure is through aerobic training. High-intensity aerobic exercise can make you burn 10 calories per minute, which means burning 300 calories in just 30 minutes. Start your fat-reduction plan with aerobic training three times a week, each session lasting 30 minutes.

3. Extend Aerobic Training Time

Aerobic training burns a mixture of carbohydrates and fats; the former comes from glucose in the blood and stored glucose in the body, while the latter is your body fat. Studies show that after 20 minutes of aerobic exercise, the body begins to burn more fat for energy. Therefore, it is more beneficial to extend the duration of aerobic exercise rather than increasing the number of weekly sessions.

If you have already adopted an aerobic training plan of three times a week, each session lasting 30 minutes, then as endurance increases, adjustments can be made: first session 45 minutes, second session 30 minutes, third session 30 minutes. After some time, this can be adjusted to: first session 45 minutes, second session 45 minutes, third session 30 minutes. The final step is to reach 45 minutes for all three sessions until you achieve a satisfactory body shape.
