An elderly man in Zhuzhou claims to be the grandson of Qiu Jin, willing to undergo DNA testing (Picture included)

by relianba2 on 2012-02-21 23:37:28

Why Was Wang Jinxing Not Included in the Wang Family Genealogy? The recent dispute among Qiu Jin's descendants is closely related to a man named Liu Guang. Liu Guang, from Xinti Village, Meilin Bridge Town, Xiangtan County, said that in 2007, his uncle on his deathbed instructed him to do one thing: "He said that our grandmother had adopted a grandson, and we must find this person no matter what."

Wang Tieshan said that the preserved photos of Qiu Jin were left by his sister, Wang Qiuyao. He is already an elderly man of 70 years old and has no need to fabricate stories; he just wants to trace his ancestry. His children also expressed that their family situation is quite good, and they would not seek private gains for the recognition as descendants of a martyr.

Liu Guang introduced that after extensive searches, he not only found Wang Qiuyao in Xiangtan but also Wang Tieshan in Zhuzhou. After careful verification, Wang Tieshan is suspected to be a descendant of Qiu Jin, which may rewrite historical records. For this reason, he established a civilian organization called the "Xiangtan Qiu Jin Research Institute" to continue investigations.

Some professionals suggest that the best way to determine if Wang Tieshan is Qiu Jin's descendant is through DNA testing. Wang Tieshan said he is willing to undergo DNA testing, and his children are also in agreement. Liu Guang expressed that he will push for the progress of this matter.

Multiple elders have testified that Wang Tieshan is a descendant of Qiu Jin. DNA testing is the best method to clarify the truth.

However, during further investigation, Liu Guang encountered doubts. Although many locals claimed that Wang Jinxing was Qiu Jin's descendant, the Wang family genealogy did not include Wang Jinxing. "The local explanation is that he died dishonorably, so he was not included in the genealogy," Liu Guang said.

Historical records indicate that Qiu Jin married Wang Tingjun, a wealthy merchant's son from Heyetang Shenchong (now part of Shuangfeng), and they only had a son, Wang Yuande, and a daughter, Wang Canzhi. Recently, an elderly man from Zhuzhou named Wang Tieshan claimed to be Qiu Jin's grandson, with his father Wang Jinxing being Qiu Jin's second son forgotten by history. In response, Qiu Jin's adopted grandson, Wang Xiaomin, strongly denied this claim (see Huarong Online - Sanxiang Metropolis Daily, September 25, 2023, page 05). After the report was published, Wang Tieshan insisted he was Qiu Jin's descendant and expressed willingness to undergo DNA testing. The dispute over Qiu Jin's descendants has once again raised questions.

Wang Tieshan stated that he is willing to undergo handwriting and DNA tests to clarify the facts. Multiple elders locally have confirmed that Wang Tieshan is a descendant of Qiu Jin. DNA testing is the best way to clear up the truth.

In Zhang Zijin's account, "Yuan De Lao Ye" refers to Wang Yuande, and "Jin Meng Zi" refers to Wang Jinxing. He said that elders in the past mentioned that Wang Jinxing died dishonorably (his wife, Ms. Weng, committed adultery, and Wang Jinxing was killed while trying to catch her in the act).
