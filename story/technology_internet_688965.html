Memory given away with online purchases of computers on JD Mall suspected of being knock-offs

by tz654d88t9o on 2012-03-03 16:42:00

The memory given away when purchasing a computer online from JD Mall is suspected to be a counterfeit product.

Source: Webmaster News 365   2012-03-03

A lawyer stated that if the gift is fake, the merchant must bear the responsibility.

On February 21 this year, Mr. Ren bought a notebook computer on JD Mall's website, and the merchant randomly attached a memory bar as a gift. However, during use, Mr. Ren found that the memory bar was unusable. After inquiry, he learned that it might be a counterfeit product, so he contacted JD Mall's after-sales service for warranty or return, but no result has been reached so far. A lawyer believes that merchants generally do not take responsibility for the high or low quality of gifts, but if the gift is counterfeit or has quality issues that endanger personal safety and health, the merchant still needs to bear the responsibility.

Buying a computer with a memory bar as a gift

On the morning of February 21 this year, Mr. Ren from Guangzhou placed an order on JD Mall to buy a Fujitsu laptop priced at more than three thousand yuan. Three days later, the goods arrived, and the merchant also included a memory bar with the computer. After unpacking and using it, the laptop worked normally. However, when Mr. Ren installed the gifted memory in the laptop, the computer began to freeze every few hours, sometimes even showing a blue screen. At first, Mr. Ren thought that the computer software was infected with a virus, but after a full antivirus scan, the above situation still existed. When Mr. Ren removed the gifted memory bar, the laptop returned to normal. [He then realized that it was a memory issue.] Upon checking the packaging box of the memory bar, he found that there was no manufacturer indication on the package. After searching online according to the brand of the memory, it showed as a Fujitsu product. Calling Fujitsu company, they informed him that this memory was not their product.

Is the gift not guaranteed to be genuine?

After realizing that the memory bar might be fake, Mr. Ren said he reported the situation to JD Mall's customer service and questioned the website's claim of "[products are genuine goods]." The response from JD Mall's customer service personnel was that "[the gift is just a gift, not a product]," Mr. Ren said, implying that "they admitted giving away counterfeit products."

Therefore, Mr. Ren requested to return or exchange the memory bar, and JD Mall's customer service personnel said that the entire laptop needed to be sent back for inspection, and after confirming no issues, they would refund 80% of the cost to Mr. Ren.

"I just want to return or exchange the memory bar, but they require me to send the whole machine back for inspection. My personal information is stored in the notebook, which poses a privacy risk. Also, it was clearly stated at purchase that a branded memory bar would be given, but now it turned out to be a counterfeit." Mr. Ren could not accept this.

This reporter contacted Ms. Chen, the public relations staff of JD Mall yesterday, but had not received a response from her by the time of publication.

Legal opinion: Responsibility for harm caused by quality issues

According to Mr. Ren's statement, when communicating with JD Mall's customer service personnel, they responded that gifts are not included in the after-sales "[three guarantees]" scope, so they could not help him with returns or exchanges. Attorney Liu Kai Zheng from Guangdong Tianqi Law Firm believed that merchants generally do not take responsibility for the high or low quality of gifts because it is a unilateral gifting act. However, if the gift is counterfeit or has quality issues that may endanger the consumer's personal safety and health, the merchant still needs to bear the responsibility.
