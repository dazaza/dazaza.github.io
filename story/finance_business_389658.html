[1] Don't wait for me to tell you before you act

After Premier Wen's statement, "It's the people who are supporting you, so you'd better figure it out yourself!", another comment at a government work report consultation meeting, "Don't wait for me to tell you before you act," has once again gained popularity online.

Because the urban registered unemployment rate reported in the government work report was 4.6%, while the Chinese Academy of Social Sciences' Institute of Sociology published a social blue book that announced an urban surveyed unemployment rate of 9.4%. Why is there a 5.2% difference between the statistics from the Ministry of Human Resources and Social Security and the Chinese Academy of Social Sciences? The public has many questions about this. In response to this kind of "quarrel," Premier Wen stated:

"You should quickly go online, or quickly hold a press conference, and explain the difference between the 4.6% and the 9.4% from the Chinese Academy of Social Sciences. Don't wait for me to tell you before you act."

Why are these two numbers different? Li Peilin, director of the Institute of Sociology at the Chinese Academy of Social Sciences, explained it this way:

"For the past few decades, there has been a difference between the urban registered unemployment rate and the urban surveyed unemployment rate, usually the latter being higher than the former. When the job market is relatively stable, these two numbers are close, but when the employment situation is bad, there will be a large gap between the two. Because our unemployment insurance does not cover all urban workers, such as migrant workers who have not participated in unemployment insurance, they are difficult to include in the statistics after becoming unemployed."

However, the Ministry of Human Resources and Social Security had another explanation:

"The 9.4% surveyed unemployment rate is the result of a sample survey of seven thousand people, among whom 2288 are economically active urban residents, and among them, there are more than 200 unemployed people, which is the number derived from comparing these two figures."

In fact, we common folks have our own views on these two sets of data:

"Like me, graduates who have only worked in the city for a few years, our hukou (household registration) is still left at the school. Where can we register as unemployed? After becoming unemployed, we can only quickly find another job ourselves, how dare we tarnish the country's unemployment rate?"

For this method of calculation that does not include people who neither work nor register, both domestic and foreign scholars have long questioned it. Premier Wen should make them explain not only the severely underestimated urban registered unemployment rate but also give us a number that can withstand scrutiny.

[3] Everyone can save money if they try

The case of a Yunnan police officer shooting and killing someone has recently caused controversy due to a signed agreement. An agreement was reached between the Mengzi County Public Security Bureau and the deceased's family, with the bureau paying a huge compensation of 550,000 yuan to the victim's family.

Regarding the case of police officer Ji Zhongchun shooting and killing someone, the Mengzi County Public Security Bureau originally defined it as a personal act, unrelated to his identity and position as a police officer. If it was a personal act, why would the Public Security Bureau pay compensation? Deputy Director Zhao Xiangrong of the Mengzi County Public Security Bureau explained it this way:

"The reason the County Public Security Bureau paid this amount is considering Ji Zhongchun's specific circumstances. Ji Zhongchun is an old police officer who has worked at the County Public Security Bureau for over 20 years, with poor economic conditions at home. Out of humanitarianism, the County Public Security Bureau first gave a humanitarian compensation to the deceased's family. And this 550,000 yuan comes from the office expenses saved by the County Public Security Bureau."

It is well known that the incident occurred because Ji Zhongchun got into a dispute while reversing his car. How can a police officer who owns a private car be classified as having poor economic conditions? This cannot help but raise the question: what exactly constitutes good economic conditions?

According to Article 14 of the Regulations on the Use of Police Equipment and Weapons by the People's Police, if a police officer illegally uses weapons, causing unnecessary casualties and property damage, the police officer's affiliated agency shall provide compensation according to the relevant provisions of the State Compensation Law. Why does the Mengzi Public Security Bureau refuse to admit that this compensation is state compensation? If it is indeed not, why does the Public Security Bureau use taxpayers' money to "help individuals"?

[6] Overhyped Internet entrepreneurship

Suddenly, like a spring breeze overnight, Guangzhou VIPshop Information Technology Co., Ltd. saw millions of online stores sprout up everywhere. Amidst the current economic crisis, the internet seems to have become an important way to solve employment problems. Not only do media outlets repeatedly promote online employment as a great path for students, but even during the Two Sessions, there were representatives suggesting that the state should subsidize college students' Taobao entrepreneurship.

Low cost, low investment, low risk, internet entrepreneurship became an instant darling, and many domestic universities have opened online store training courses to assist student entrepreneurship. Yes, if everyone follows the trend and opens online stores now, the employment problem can be immediately solved, but what about one year later, ten years later? How many people can truly succeed? Let alone issues like medical insurance and social security. Network security and legal standardization have always been the most neglected difficulties in the information economy tide. Are our experts and professors considering this point while enthusiastically promoting internet entrepreneurship for college students?
