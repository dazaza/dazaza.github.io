How to deal with great workplace pressure? Write blogs to relieve it.

by qingian007 on 2012-02-29 10:29:34

When you feel troubled during the work process, don't wallow in self-pity or keep it bottled up inside. Instead, try talking to your colleagues and brainstorm solutions together. Many companies have professional counselors on staff who specialize in addressing employees' psychological issues. If you feel awkward sharing your stress with those around you, these professional counselors can be excellent confidants.

Our company's workplace atmosphere is surprisingly good, and communication between people is smooth. This isn't just boasting. However, even so, there are still some individuals in our company who occasionally experience overwhelming work pressure that they cannot relieve. As a boss, I find this very troubling.

In speculative businesses, young employees often bear significant responsibilities, and their tasks can be quite challenging. The expectations for their results are also strict. For those who can turn pressure into motivation, all of this may still be tolerable. Moreover, when solving problems, they frequently face various forms of evaluation. Even talented young people are strongly urged to develop independence. Naturally, this kind of environment can easily lead to immense pressure among employees. Employees who internalize this pressure without finding an outlet are prone to mental health issues.

Nowadays, more and more people suffer from various occupational illnesses due to excessive workplace stress. Compared to traditional seniority-based companies, working in a speculative enterprise like ours makes it easier for employees to develop mental health problems due to high job stress.

People who are prone to mental health issues are usually very serious about their work, extremely hardworking, and perfectionists. However, they have relatively little experience dealing with setbacks. Therefore, when faced with heavy work pressure or mistakes, they easily fall into self-doubt – “I’m not as capable as I thought.” Even if they complete their work without any errors, they still feel pressured – “This is all I can do; I won’t receive positive feedback.”

I often use blogging as a way to relieve work-related stress. As a boss, I am often praised by others, so I hope people can get to know the real, objective me through my blog. Some bosses hire their company’s PR team or secretaries to write blogs on their behalf, but I take no stance on this. I just believe that expressing the real self through blogging is beneficial.

Many artists also use blogging to alleviate the stress they encounter at work. Blogging allows them to express their true selves in their own words. Because of certain roles or positions, they must perform in ways that don't always reflect their authentic selves. The longer the gap between their roles and their true selves, the greater the pressure they feel.

When encountering stress at work, maintaining a normal mindset is most important. Often, when you objectively and calmly analyze a problem, you'll realize it's not as serious as it seems. Even if you make irreparable major mistakes, there's no need to panic excessively because, sometimes, such errors are within the expectations of business operators and superiors.

If you constantly focus on talented colleagues and set becoming outstanding as your competitive goal, you risk pushing yourself to the brink of collapse. Having a competitive mindset that motivates self-improvement is the most reasonable and important approach. Mental breakdowns will leave you with nothing. Therefore, I encourage everyone to reassess themselves with absolute and objective evaluations.

Personally, I recommend an effective method for relieving work pressure: writing a blog. By doing so, work-related stress can be easily released. You can write out your thoughts and feelings in your blog, helping you reconnect with your true self.

In daily work, many people also need to "perform." Writing down your genuine thoughts and feelings can help you reconfirm yourself objectively. Therefore, I suggest everyone write more blogs. Listing your efforts at work in your blog can boost your confidence. Comments and feedback from others can become motivating factors.
