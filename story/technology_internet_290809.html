Focus on the tone of next year's economic policy _ Stock Basics

by dx0966632 on 2009-12-03 22:33:19

I. Policy Tone or More Elasticity

The current situation is that the domestic economy stabilizes through investment-driven growth, but the endogenous momentum and structural problems of the economy are still far from optimistic. From this perspective, the overall tone of economic policy must be adjusted accordingly to adapt to time and place. However, in view of the management's steady style, in order to ensure a smooth transition from economic recovery to structural transformation, the adjustment of policies is likely to be gradual and with room for maneuver.

On the other hand, due to the fact that the current macroeconomic fundamentals are still facing a lot of uncertainties, it is difficult to adjust policies. Flexible statements will help reserve more space for next year’s governance. Therefore, we still hold the view that some stricter wording such as preventing asset price bubbles may not appear at this meeting. The general tone of the meeting should be mild and elastic.

II. Shift of Work Focus

Even so, among the several main goals of the policy, the position and importance of maintaining growth, adjusting structure, and preventing inflation may change. Adjusting the structure may be elevated to the top priority, reserving policy space for preventing inflation, maintaining reasonable growth in investment (but not rapid growth), and temporarily no longer overly focusing on maintaining growth, thereby gradually diluting the goal of maintaining growth, which should be a relatively reasonable and rational choice for the management. At the same time, this meeting may pay more attention to how to stimulate consumption, but there may be no specific measures. This is mainly because the nature of the Central Economic Work Conference has always been mainly to set the tone, and it is unlikely to involve too substantive measures. Moreover, proposing specific plans at present would be extremely difficult. However, Premier Wen Jiabao's recent remarks during his inspection in Shanghai, "We need to curb speculative home purchases and promote the healthy development of the real estate industry," clearly differ from the previous attitude that prioritized maintaining growth above all else, and also implicitly point out the possible direction of future policies. Although the shift in the focus of policy work is gradual, we should still pay attention to the early manifestation of expectations.

III. Mid-term Market More Likely to Maintain Volatility

Since this Central Economic Work Conference may not give a relatively clear directional tone, this also determines that the market expectations influenced by it will be vague. On one hand, the expectation of economic recovery has been basically overestimated, and the prospect of medium and long-term economic growth is actually unclear; on the other hand, after the Dubai incident, the management began to consider whether an early exit would lead to a second recession in the market, thus significantly reducing the willingness to suppress asset prices and tighten credit, and the market liquidity remains loose.

In this situation, we emphasize that it is difficult for a one-sided market to appear, and the market is more likely to wait for the clarification of expectations in the form of volatility. In this process, mainstream institutions in the market will continuously explore relevant structural theme sectors for speculation, which deserves our attention.

Therefore, from a strategic perspective, we still emphasize less index and more individual stocks, and deeply analyze the future pattern based on which to explore corresponding themes.

The procedure for buying and selling stocks is as follows: When buying and selling stocks, you must specify whether you are buying or selling, the name (or code) of the stock, quantity, and price. And this commission is only valid on the day it was placed. The content of the commission includes the abbreviation (code) of the stock you want to buy or sell, the quantity, and the price of the stock. The abbreviation of a stock usually consists of three to four Chinese characters, and the code of the stock is six digits. When placing a buy/sell order, the code and abbreviation of the stock must be consistent. Also, there are certain regulations regarding the quantity of stocks bought and sold: The quantity of stocks to be purchased must be an integer multiple of 100, but the quantity of stocks to be sold does not have to be an integer multiple of 100.

There are four ways to place a commission: counter submission, telephone automatic commission, computer automatic commission, and remote terminal commission.
