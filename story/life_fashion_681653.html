Three tricks for arranging classical lacelike partition furniture to brighten modern new life

Have you had such an experience? You saw a red-lacquered floral inlaid large wardrobe at a flea market, bought it after a hard bargain, but then found that the lacelike partition inside was not practical, with no place to hang clothes; the beautiful southern official hat chair made your feet not touching the ground when sitting; the side table bought back home seemed too long... Wanting to buy furniture full of Chinese unique charm, but it seems not very useful. So, how can Chinese furniture be used by modern people while retaining the traditional shape and style in design, maintaining the essence of Ming and Qing lacelike partition furniture in production, and making it more useful and comfortable?

The essence of combining ancient furniture with new life

Classical lacelike carved furniture Brightening modern new life

To move Chinese ancient furniture into modern families and make it perfectly blend with modern life, three essentials need to be mastered.

1. Proper proportion: The shape and color of Chinese ancient furniture are very eye-catching, so for families who do not want to change the original modern atmosphere, western furniture and Chinese furniture can be matched in a 7:3 ratio.

2. New use of antiques: Large cabinets used as money cabinets and medicine cabinets in the past can be used as bookshelves, CD racks, and TV cabinets, combining classical furniture with modern appliances to show alternative beauty.

3. Environmental enhancement: If you want to show the unique charm of Chinese ancient furniture in lacelike wooden grids, lacelike carvings, and colors, then you must pay attention to the enhancing role of lighting, flooring, and walls on ancient furniture. Soft lighting can highlight the natural texture of wood, light-colored walls can enhance the elegant taste of ancient furniture. For example, the enhancing role of wooden grid flower windows on ancient furniture.

Chair: Need to lower height without footrest

Chairs in traditional furniture are necessities of people's lives. Due to the thoughts of the Ming and Qing dynasties still being bound by etiquette, religion, and other concepts, feudal thoughts emphasizing distinctions between inside and outside, and seniority order also reflected in furniture design, those shapes considered not upright enough like triangles and trapezoids were completely negated. Unlike the myriad designs in the West, Chinese chairs have few variations in shape.

In the past, when sitting on Chinese chairs, feet were not directly on the ground but on a footrest about 10 centimeters high, with the general chair height around 54 centimeters. Since ancient households mostly had brick floors, to prevent feeling cold due to contact with the ground during the northern winter and the southern plum rain season, ancients used footrests to keep their feet off the ground. Nowadays, people live on cement floors or carpets, no longer needing footrests to isolate dampness and cold. Without the footrest, Chinese chairs appear too high, so new Chinese furniture should lower the chair height to around 44 centimeters.

Bookshelf: Dimensions need improvement

As for the bookshelves used by the ancients, there is no need to mention their shapes, but their functions are hard to meet the requirements of modern people. Ancient books were mostly made of bamboo paper bound with thread, usually stacked one by one. Therefore, the compartments were larger, and the thickness of the shelves was relatively thin. Today's books are mostly made of wood pulp paper, especially copperplate paper, which makes them heavy, and people are accustomed to placing books vertically. Traditional bookshelf dimensions are no longer suitable, whether it be the spacing between shelves or the load-bearing capacity of the shelves, all needing recalculation to meet modern people's book storage needs.

Canopy bed: Need to leave space for bedside tables

Ancient canopy beds were generally enclosed, with only one side accessible for getting on the bed, leaving no space for bedside tables, which obviously does not suit modern needs. In the new Chinese lattice panel furniture, there are beds that fully meet the needs of modern people. The bed frame has been changed to a form where both sides can be accessed, the bed size made into today's standard size, making it convenient to buy spring mattresses, and bedside tables can be embedded and placed on the inside of the canopy bed. Of course, the lacelike carvings and overall shape on the bed frame still retain the charm of classical furniture. (Editor Yaya)
