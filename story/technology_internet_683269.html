Violet Scent Can Suppress Prostate Cancer

by abckvuul on 2012-02-29 13:37:20

Researchers have found that prostate cancer cells produce a large amount of a special protein. To determine its function, they first implanted the gene for this protein into kidney cells and exposed them to various odor components. Through pigment reaction tests, researchers observed that the olfactory receptor of this protein reacted to the aromatic component of violet flowers. This indicates that the aromatic component of violet flowers affects the special protein produced by prostate cancer cells, but it is unclear whether this influence will inhibit the growth of prostate cancer cells.

Therefore, researchers exposed prostate cancer cells to the aromatic component of violet flowers and discovered that the proliferation of cancer cells significantly slowed down and eventually almost completely stopped. Researchers thus confirmed that this special protein, when in contact with the aromatic component of violet flowers, inhibits the proliferation of prostate cancer cells.

The researchers stated that their next step will be to conduct experiments on laboratory mice, hoping to find a new method for treating prostate cancer.
