Introduction:

Princess Jinlun (played by Lin Qingxia) seeks revenge and aims to obtain the true scripture to practice divine kung fu; the amorous Dongxie (Leslie Cheung) chases after a beauty with an initial evil heart; Tuoshi Luohan (Tony Leung) pursues immortality, cutting off emotional ties; Ou Yang Feng dreams of becoming a king, Hong Qi dreams of marrying his cousin, the old child who likes men (Carina Lau), Hong Qi (Jacky Cheung) who became the leader by dice game, Huang Yaoshi (Leslie Cheung) whose ball skills are as good as Pele, Ou Yang Feng (Tony Leung) who imagines himself as a duck, and the Nine Yin Manual carved on a toilet seat...

Adapted from Jin Yong's novel "The Legend of the Condor Heroes". The treacherous concubine of Jinlun Country has an affair with Xi Du, thus first killing the king and taking the jade seal to dominate the country. The three princesses guarding the jade seal escape from Xi Du's attack. Xi Du chases after them with rocket boots but loses one boot, accidentally killing Wang Zhongyang. Just then, the three princess passes by, and Wang requests her before dying to find her junior apprentice Zhou Botong to report the vengeance. The three princess receives guidance from the nine palace real person, going together with Huang Yaoshi to obtain the "Nine Yin Manual" to restore the country and take revenge; however, this provokes jealousy from the little apprentice sister. She fails to obtain the manual and can only go to the Da Dong Inn to meet Nan Di. Originally, Nan Di is a reincarnation of Luohan, who must find a sincere person to become immortal. Zhou Botong arrives mistakenly believing that the three princess killed his senior apprentice, but fortunately gets captured by Dongxie and Nan Di, being demoted to a servant. Nan Di discovers that Dongxie is the long-sought sincere person, thus disguising as the three princess to make Dongxie say "I love you", allowing him to ascend to immortality.

Production Background:

This large-scale production film gathers many big names from Hong Kong cinema. The star lineup is breathtaking, almost collecting most of the top stars of Hong Kong at the time. However, apart from some character names remaining unchanged from Jin Yong's original work "The Legend of the Condor Heroes", the storyline has been significantly altered and focuses on humor. In fact, the birth of "Dong Cheng Xi Jiu" was entirely accidental. Wong Kar-wai, while preparing to shoot "Dong Xia Xi Du" in 1993, was pressured by investors who feared not recovering costs like with "As Tears Go By". They asked Wong Kar-wai to use the same cast to produce a box-office hit comedy version. Thus, "Dong Cheng Xi Jiu" came into existence. However, in "Dong Cheng Xi Jiu", Wong Kar-wai was not the director but the producer, with the directing role taken up by Liu Zhenwei. To categorize "Dong Cheng Xi Jiu" under new wuxia films, even as an alternative, might be a bit forced or self-serving. The openly flashy martial arts and nearly self-revealing special effects usage in "Dong Cheng Xi Jiu" amounts to a defection from the entire new wuxia film genre. In the movie, Lin Qingxia's "Wuxiang Divine Skill" truly lacks form, the finger-snapping battle between Zhang Guorong and Liang Jiahui's One Yang Finger clearly resembles a gunfight, clearly explaining how new wuxia films are made. As for jokes like "Affectionate Knife", "Flirty Sword", "Dry Fire Palm", they not only mock the entire new wuxia film genre but also directly target its creator, Mr. Jin. The Old Child seems to have practiced leather shoe swordsmanship and has inappropriate relations with his master, the effeminate Nan Di eventually ascends to heaven as Ji Gong, and the Nine Yin Manual is originally carved on a toilet bowl. Such abundant satirical content makes it hard to enjoy despite causing uncontrollable laughter.
