2009.01.29 (Career Assessment)

by bjseeprof on 2009-12-07 11:29:41

I took a simple career assessment, and it seems quite accurate as I read through it. It points out some flaws, but that's okay. As the saying goes, "It's easier to change the kingdom than one's nature." Instead of trying to fix every shortcoming, it might be better to highlight our strengths even more prominently. Your test result type is: ISTJ.

Type Description:

ISTJ - The Most Responsible

Their Best Performance:

People with ISTJ preferences have a strong sense of responsibility and unwavering loyalty towards organizations, families, and various aspects of their lives. They work tirelessly and complete assigned tasks on time and according to requirements. They almost always complete tasks they deem necessary without hesitation but refuse to continue doing things they don't understand. People with ISTJ preferences generally prefer working alone and taking responsibility for the results of their work; however, when necessary for completing tasks effectively, they also enjoy working in teams where roles are clearly defined and everyone can accomplish their assigned tasks.

Personality Traits of ISTJ Types:

ISTJ types highly respect facts. They primarily use internalized feelings to store information and rely on this information to understand current issues. Therefore, they tend to be pragmatic, sensitive, and fact-based individuals who are organized.

ISTJ types make decisions using logic, taking objective, logical, and resilient steps. They view tasks or systems as a whole rather than separately. ISTJ types tend to be logical and analytical, independent yet empathetic. They express clear and firm views because these views are formed by applying logical criteria based on their experiences and knowledge. They support changes only if factual evidence indicates better outcomes.

How Others Perceive Them:

When satisfied with their role, ISTJ types are sociable. However, except with close friends, they generally do not reveal their valuable observations and memories to outsiders. Others may understand their standards and perspectives, their expectations regarding structure and arrangements, but may not fully comprehend their unique, sometimes humorous, personal reactions. People with ISTJ preferences may struggle to understand feelings that differ significantly from their own, but once they realize something is crucial to those they care about, they generously meet such needs even if they still find them incomprehensible.

General Perception of ISTJ Types:

Calm, cautious, serious, consistent, orderly, valuing tradition.

Potential Development Opportunities:

Sometimes, living environments may not favor ISTJ types developing and expressing their thinking and feeling preferences. If they haven't developed their thinking, they might lack reliable methods to deal with the world and instead focus on their internal memories. If they haven't developed their sensing abilities, they might hastily judge and act without considering new information. If ISTJ types cannot find opportunities to utilize their talents and receive recognition for their contributions, they often feel frustrated and may become overly rigid about time, schedules, and procedures, critical of others, and find it difficult to trust others to do good work.

For people with ISTJ preferences, they naturally pay less attention to perceptions and emotions they do not favor. If they excessively neglect these, they might:

- Overlook the broader impact of current temporary decisions.

- Focus too much on logic without considering its impact on people.

- Fail to appropriately respond to others' demands for connection and friendliness.

Under significant stress, ISTJ types might be unable to use their usual calm and rational judgment and fall into a "doomsday" scenario—imagining many negative possibilities for themselves and others.

Career Type:

ISTJ: Introverted, Sensing, Thinking, Judging Type

Personality Description: Meticulous, responsible, wise, and broad-minded, they are steadfast defenders of society. Practical and very pragmatic, they always strive for precision and order with great focus. No matter what they do, they can systematically and steadily complete tasks.

Career Orientation:

Technical work that produces tangible products or provides organized comprehensive services. Requires an independent working environment with ample time for independent work, allowing the utilization of exceptional focus to complete tasks.

Suitable Careers:

Auditor, logistics manager, information director, budget analyst, engineer, technical writer, computer programmer, securities broker, geologist, medical researcher, accountant, word processing professional, appraiser, inspector, judge, surveyor, mapper, evaluator.
