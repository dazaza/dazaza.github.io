Amidst the uproar of the "Pique Gate" incident, Barcelona (Weibo) welcomed their second leg of the UEFA Champions League Round of 16 match against Bayer Leverkusen. After a few days in an unfamiliar atmosphere, Barcelona finally returned to their familiar territory - the pitch. For Barcelona, football is obviously their best spokesperson. The Blaugrana must break away from the so-called referee controversy and speak through football once again. For Barcelona, advancing to the quarterfinals of the Champions League is not a difficult challenge, as they had already secured a 3-1 victory in Germany, making it highly unlikely for Leverkusen to achieve a comeback at Camp Nou.

Both Spanish and German media unanimously agree that Barcelona's 3-1 away win in the first leg has left no doubt about their progression to the Champions League quarterfinals. Messi was suspended and did not play in the league match last weekend, meaning this UEFA Champions League game would see him return with full motivation and energy. Barcelona remains undefeated in all home games this season across all competitions, only drawing three times against Real Madrid (Weibo), AC Milan (Weibo), and Sevilla. Leverkusen defeated Bayern Munich 2-0 in their league match last weekend, but playing away at Camp Nou is notoriously challenging for any team. Moreover, the intense battle against Bayern would have drained much of their energy.

Leverkusen themselves are quite humble regarding this match. Their players believe that the possibility of Leverkusen overturning Barcelona to progress is extremely low. Therefore, they openly stated that their aim for this match is simply to enjoy it to the fullest extent possible. Striker Klaas-Jan Huntelaar expressed: "I really hope to participate in this game, but we are realists; Leverkusen doesn't stand much of a chance in this match." Deliyork also said: "We just want to prove to Barcelona what Leverkusen can do and why we reached the knockout stage of the Champions League. We hope to enjoy the game, perform well, and that will be enough."

Leverkusen made it to one UEFA Champions League final back in 2002, losing to Zinedine Zidane's Real Madrid. Following that, key players like Michael Ballack and Ze Roberto left the team, and Leverkusen hasn't advanced past the quarterfinals ever since. This season, coach Robin Dutt hoped to lead his team to new heights, but unfortunately, they were drawn against Barcelona, the top favorites to win the title in the round of 16. Although their poor draw luck is partly due to failing to beat Genk in the final group match, allowing Chelsea to secure the top spot, Leverkusen's pessimistic attitude instead prompted caution within Barcelona's ranks, who referred to them as "wolves in sheep's clothing." Guardiola himself remarked after beating Sporting Gijon: "This victory has boosted our morale ahead of the Champions League match. Our opponent defeated Bayern Munich 2-0, and I'm glad we get to enjoy the knockout stages, but you cannot underestimate Leverkusen."

For Barcelona, Sanchez, Thiago, Abidal, and Puyol are out due to various injuries, while Xavi's participation remains uncertain due to physical discomfort, prompting Guardiola to potentially bench him. On the Leverkusen side, Dutt has already indicated he'll field the strongest possible starting eleven, though veteran Ballack remains injured and unlikely to feature, as does Sam. However, Deliyork, who missed the first leg, will return to the starting lineup.

Nevertheless, regardless of how modest Barcelona's players may be, an undeniable fact remains: Barcelona is truly a Bundesliga nemesis. They have remained unbeaten against Bundesliga teams for a continuous decade. In recent years, they've eliminated or defeated strong teams such as Werder Bremen, Stuttgart, Schalke 04, and Bayern Munich. Notably, Spain's technical superiority allowed them to dominate Germany in the 2008 European Cup final. Barcelona's quick and skillful style proves highly effective against Bundesliga teams, showcasing a significant technical advantage among their players, thus enabling them to consistently control the game. Lionel Messi's speed and technique are especially daunting for Bundesliga defenders. Having scored seven goals in eight matches against German sides, almost all top Bundesliga teams have become stepping stones for Messi's journey to championships.

Another player to watch is Andres Iniesta. Despite injuries preventing this season from being considered his best, Iniesta still showed up when needed most. In the previous La Liga match against Sporting Gijon, he not only scored a goal but also contributed significantly to the team's second-half comeback. Just a few days ago, he scored in Spain's match against Venezuela. "Iniesta scoring = Barcelona winning" has practically become a formula. This means Leverkusen must prevent Iniesta from scoring at all costs; otherwise, a defeat awaits the German side. Additionally, in every match Iniesta has played for Barcelona this season, including 16 league games, 1 European Super Cup, 3 Champions League matches, 5 Copa del Rey matches, 2 FIFA Club World Cup matches, and 2 Spanish Super Cup matches, Barcelona has remained undefeated. His presence guarantees Barcelona's invincibility, and as long as Barcelona doesn't lose, progressing becomes a reality.
