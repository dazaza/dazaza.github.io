Cosmetic Beauty

1. Corrective Makeup —— This is a makeup technique. In terms of operation, it mainly creates visual illusions by utilizing the color degree and the form and position of facial lines, thereby achieving corrective purposes. Corrective makeup is a relative technical means, and its key lies in seeking visual balance and discovering unique beauty. One must not isolate any single part to address and solve, to avoid over-correction that appears rigid and abrupt, losing the natural vitality and individual beauty of the face.

Basic corrective skills for face shapes with disproportionate vertical and horizontal ratios, eyebrows, eyes, and lips that do not conform to aesthetic standards can help improve the makeup skills of beauticians, providing better service for customers with different makeup needs.

Large Face Shape —— An overly large face gives an impression of lacking beauty and vitality, and may also make a person look fat. Referring to the definition of color depth in color science, choose a darker base color to create a tightening and shrinking effect on the face. If it belongs to a large and flat face shape, different color degrees can be boldly used to highlight areas that need to stand out and contract areas that need to recede. When modifying the eyes, eyebrows, and lips, they should be exaggerated as much as possible to harmoniously match the relatively larger face and features.

Small Face Shape —— Although small faces are trendy among beauties, if the face shape is not proportionate to the body, it also needs correction through makeup. When correcting, the best base color should be slightly lighter than the skin tone, such as ivory or light beige, giving the impression of an expanded facial area. When modifying the eyes, eyebrows, nose, and lips, try to present a simple, elegant, and gentle style. Note that small face shapes are unsuitable for overly decorated makeup styles, avoiding making the originally too small face look overly complex.
