The flowers have withered, walking on the country path.

by wangquan1986 on 2012-02-27 15:37:58

The flowers have already withered, and I am walking on a country path, always wanting to go to the mountains, to smell the taste of green grass, to listen to the ethereal bird songs, and to watch the trickling stream. This is enough to forget the烦恼of the mundane world and release the fatigue of the body. In the depths of winter, the flowers have already faded, and I am walking on a mountain path.

The mountain paths are winding and lead to secluded places; they twist and turn endlessly.

By the side of the road leading to the mountains, there is a spring with sweet and pure water. This spring has also attracted many people from the city, who fill plastic buckets with the spring water and carry them away in large and small barrels. Carrying the spring water at such an early hour is considered a form of exercise. There was once a relative of my family who did this, but he passed away shortly after turning seventy. People said he overdid it. It seems that exercising still needs to be done in moderation.

Climbing the steps feels like ascending into the clouds. Looking back at the cliff ravine, there are a few unripe fruits on the bitter almond trees; under the tree, a few big white geese are squawking, their red marks on their foreheads clearly visible. The sour alfalfa lies silently on the ground, its lush greenery unforgettable. I remember as a child, when playing in the fields, I would always pick a few leaves of sour alfalfa and chew them, filling my mouth with a sour taste. Now no one picks and eats these herbs, but the joys of childhood remain unforgettable.

"Two trees" are engraved on a large stone, perhaps as a creative idea. Here, there are no villages ahead and no shops behind. Looking around, there are only a few brick-tiled houses down the mountain. The farm we are heading to serves fragrant tofu pudding and steaming hot chicken with taro at noon. Sidedishes with peppers give a rustic flavor. Such a simple joy can relax our nerves for the entire day.

As we walk, a refreshing fragrance wafts by, and looking towards the source, plum blossoms are blooming on branches higher than a person's head, coldly yet beautifully. Although some flowers have withered, the fragrance cannot be confined by the wilderness. Smelling the fragrance at that moment, I suddenly recall Wang Anshi's poem: "From afar, I know it is not snow, because there is a faint fragrance coming." The plum blossoms attract Mr. Zhang, leaving behind several lively photographs. The flowers and people both move, releasing emotions in motion. Smelling the flower's fragrance, you cannot hear the sound of the flowers blooming. At this magical moment, it feels like seeing a beautiful woman silently without words, making one's heart instantly delicate and clear.

Pomegranates are the lovers of happiness. A few times before, passing by a farmhouse, bright red pomegranates hung on the trees, tempting passersby. After the winter solstice, the pomegranates still hang on the trees, dark brown and wilted, showing that the farmers are reluctant to pick and eat them, using them instead as ornamental fruits. Renowned essayist Feng Zikai often wrote rural essays which sometimes feel especially ingenious. Bean pods on tree tips may seem ordinary, but returning to the essence of real life naturally brings insights. Empress Wu Zetian was always seen as a bad person in my history books until I read her poem "Ruyi Niang," which made me sigh. The poem reads: "Seeing red turns to green, thoughts are disordered, emaciated and broken due to missing you. If you do not believe, recently I have been crying constantly, open the box and check my pomegranate skirt." A deep affection leaps off the page!

Apart from plum blossoms, it is really hard to find flowers in the mountains. That rustling sound gently stirs the eardrum and then escapes, lightly brushing against the wilds. Bamboo remains evergreen, its leaves like winter flutes, playing distant flute sounds; the vast sea of barbed grass sways its purple-white tassels in the wind. At this moment, the mood is like a babbling brook, naturally flowing. Even when Lingzi says her fur coat is a unique piece, we echo: "unique piece, fake piece." Loving mountains means recognizing how small humans are compared to nature, so what do humans desire? More than just a fur coat.

Leisure is a way of life, and we are not here just to admire flowers. We simply want to color our moods, whether green, blue, or purple. Our footprints will be left on a thin calendar. Worker Zhong is like a backpacker, carrying a travel bag, his face flushed, sweat appearing on his forehead; Fangfang lags behind, her dream peach blossom sweater swaying among the barbed grass; Cousin sings loudly all the way, his singing dispelling the chill on the grass tips. The scattered group resembles a collection of defeated scenes.

In the countryside, there are also karaoke machines, occasionally hearing such a song boosts one's spirit: "Walking on a country path, the old ox returning home is my companion, the blue sky adorned with the sunset on the chest, colorful clouds are the evening glow's attire." Life cannot be guarded everywhere, and when loneliness is cast aside, a pleasant emotion quietly grows in the heart.

Do not think about past lives or this life, let the footsteps trample the grass stems; do not think about the warmth and coldness of society, let all the gentleness gather in the chest. Even if revisiting the past, I do not feel desolate, the wonderful moment I have locked does not need others to understand.

A writer often thinks about trends, charm, sound, and rhythm. In the mountains, stretching out delicate fingers to the gurgling stream plucks melodious notes; although the mountain is not steep, it is sufficient to hold fluttering black hair. What else is there? These mountains and waters, in winter, appear even more profound, storing the scent of spring, hidden yet revealed, like a devoted young woman.

On the green grass slope, half a mu of lotus pond shines brightly, calm without ripples. The surrounding mountain scenery is like an ink painting. Remember every March, the hillsides here are covered with golden rape flowers; the rape flowers frame white plum blossoms, walking through them, near or far, steps break light clouds. Looking down at the farmhouse yard, fiery mountain tea flowers peek out of the walls, drunk red intoxicating those enjoying spring outings. At this moment, hopes can only be寄托on the peak in the mountains. Passing through a grove of camphor trees, the tassels of barbed grass greet us shoulder-high. These tassels are often collected, bundled together, and placed in vases as decorations. The peak is not high, abruptly standing on the plain, like a cleaved knife, exposing yellow-brown rocks, astonishingly without any moss.

Finally, I see colorful animals, it is a dry shrub, growing alone in the center of the dam, its leaves pale red. We circle around this withered shrub, taking photos or playing train games, pouring uncontrollable laughter onto the round leaves.

The flowers have already withered, and I am walking on a mountain path. Do not think, who will I return with.
