"The Dancing Elephant" intensifies market volatility - Stock Forum

by xl0958870 on 2009-12-05 02:33:44

After last week's sharp decline, the stock index experienced a strong rebound this week. Not only did it recover most of last week's losses, but it also successfully stood above the 120-week moving average, further enhancing market strength. In the short-term market, as we mentioned earlier, due to the inconsistency in the offensive rhythm between the Shanghai and Shenzhen markets, under the continuous strengthening of the Shenzhen market, the SSE Composite Index also began a powerful catch-up. Moreover, after a long period of consolidation in the mid-term support area for financial stocks such as banks and real estate, they not only provided strong support for the overall market but have also become potential upward driving forces for the stock index.

Notably, today's "dancing elephants" among the heavyweight stocks were not the previous pulse-like catch-up but formed a collective active upward trend. Accompanied by a significant increase in trading volume, it shows that market funds clearly recognize the start of their catch-up trend. This is an important reason why the stock index still performed well despite the weakness of most non-heavyweight stocks today. On the other hand, although non-heavyweight stocks have completed some exchanges at high positions after today's increased volume fluctuations, the divergence in technical indicators has yet to be resolved. In the short term, non-heavyweight stocks still require further fluctuations. Therefore, the requirements for selecting individual stocks will be even more stringent in subsequent operations.

Meanwhile, since heavyweight stocks like banks, real estate, and oil & petrochemicals are all starting from mid-term support levels, there is still space for oscillatory upward movement in the future. Regarding this, our New Flagship Center believes: Through today's wide fluctuations, the issue of the market overly relying on small and medium-sized stocks for attacks has improved somewhat. However, since non-heavyweight stocks still need further adjustments in the short term, coupled with the fact that the stock index has not effectively escaped the constraints of the previous resistance zone, once the heavyweight stocks experience a short-term rise followed by fluctuations, it cannot be ruled out that the stock index may have a retracement to test support. Therefore, it is still essential to focus on grasping the offensive rhythm of the stock index in the short term.

The procedures for buying and selling stocks are as follows: When buying or selling stocks, you must specify whether you are buying or selling, along with the name (or code), quantity, and price of the stock. Furthermore, this order is only valid for the day it is placed. The content of the commission includes the abbreviation (code) of the stock you intend to buy or sell, the quantity, and the price. Stock abbreviations usually consist of three to four Chinese characters, and the code consists of six digits. The codes and abbreviations must match when placing orders. Additionally, there are certain regulations regarding the quantity of stocks being traded: the quantity for purchasing stocks must be a whole multiple of 100, while the quantity for selling does not need to be.

There are four ways to place orders: counter submission, telephone automatic order, computer automatic order, and remote terminal order.
