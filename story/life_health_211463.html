Why is the root of the disease in the liver?

by mengmengyu on 2009-07-20 15:00:19

We have been saying that blood is not the fundamental cause of psoriasis. You may wonder: what evidence is there to prove that the liver is the deeper cause behind the blood? Perhaps the following experiences will resonate with you:

1. Colds and infections can trigger psoriasis

Many psoriasis patients have experienced psoriasis triggered by a cold or infection. This is because the lungs govern the body's respiratory system and manage all the energy in the body. Once a person catches a cold, coughs, or has tonsillitis, it directly affects the function of the lungs. The lungs belong to metal, and the liver belongs to wood. Metal overcomes wood, so the lungs have a restraining ability on the liver. When lung qi becomes weak, its restraining ability decreases, further aggravating the abnormality of the liver.

In addition, the lungs control the defensive qi. When lung qi is weak, the defensive qi's function decreases and cannot resist wind evil invasion. Since the liver belongs to wood, which is active and associated with wind, wind evil invasion will directly attack the liver, strengthening liver yang and causing further imbalance between yin and yang in the liver, worsening the symptoms of liver yin deficiency. This proves that the onset of psoriasis has a certain connection with the liver.

2. The liver has a direct impact on mental factors and emotions

During the treatment of psoriasis, does the doctor often remind you to stay cheerful and emotionally stable, saying it helps with treatment? Indeed, this is true. Mental factors have a significant impact on the onset and aggravation of psoriasis. Thirty to fifty percent of patients develop the condition after experiencing major life events, and seventy to eighty percent of patients experience recurrence or worsening due to mental stress. Compared to other skin diseases, stress causes the onset and aggravation rates of psoriasis at 70.2% and 65.7%, respectively, while for measles, acne, and alopecia areata, these rates are only 16.4% and 35.8%. It is evident that mental factors significantly affect psoriasis more than other skin diseases related to the spirit.

What does this have to do with the liver? According to traditional Chinese medicine theory, the liver governs emotional states. A person's emotions are directly influenced and constrained by the liver's regulating function. When the liver's regulating function is normal, the flow of qi is smooth, maintaining an optimistic spirit, a pleasant mood, peaceful blood and qi, and harmony among the five organs. Conversely, if the liver's regulating function is impaired, the flow of qi becomes disordered, leading to abnormal emotional activities.

Moreover, the liver's regulation of emotions is closely related to the liver storing blood. If liver blood is deficient, it loses its regulatory power, resulting in various emotional abnormalities such as shock, frequent dreams, and restlessness. Liver dysfunction in regulation can lead to emotional abnormalities. Conversely, external emotional stimuli, especially anger, or prolonged repeated adverse stimuli, can also cause dysregulation of the liver's regulatory function. Therefore, to ensure a good mood and mental state, the primary requirement is for the liver's regulatory function to be normal.

3. Nail damage occurring in the later stages of psoriasis is closely related to the liver

Nails are appendages of the skin, so skin diseases can affect the nails. At least 30% of psoriasis patients experience nail damage, with some cases reaching over 50%. However, this is more common in non-ordinary types of psoriasis, especially pustular psoriasis, where almost all patients have nail damage. Joint-type or long-term red-skin type psoriasis also has a higher incidence of nail damage, which is inseparable from the functions of the liver.

The liver governs tendons, and its manifestation is in the nails, including fingernails and toenails. In traditional Chinese medicine, nails are considered the extension of tendons outside the body, hence they are also called "the surplus of tendons." The prosperity or decline of liver blood is often reflected in the nails. When the liver's yin blood is abundant, the tendons are nourished, and the nails become strong, glossy, and red-lustrous. If liver blood is insufficient, the nails lose their nourishment, becoming pale, soft and thin, or dry and brittle, easily deformed and cracked. Hence, "The Suwen" says: "The liver combines with tendons, and its manifestation is in the nails." Clinically, changes in nail color can be used to infer the prosperity or decline of liver blood and qi. Nail diseases are also often treated through liver differentiation and treatment.

4. The relationship between diet, digestive function, and the liver

Everyone knows that psoriasis patients need to avoid certain foods, pay attention to their diet, and take care of their spleen and stomach health in daily life, which is precisely because digestive function is also promoted and coordinated by the liver.

The human digestive function includes the intake and digestion of food, the transport and absorption of nutrients, etc. Although these physiological activities are mainly governed by the spleen and stomach, they also require the promoting effect of the liver's regulating function to maintain the smooth operation of the digestive process. Summarizing the liver's role in aiding digestion, it mainly manifests in two aspects: one is that the liver promotes the production and secretion of bile; the second is maintaining the normal ascending and descending movements of the spleen and stomach qi.

The normal functioning of the liver's regulating function is an important condition for the normal operation of the spleen and stomach and the maintenance of a vigorous digestive function. If the liver's regulating function is abnormal, it will lead to dysfunction of the spleen and stomach, and the dysfunction of the spleen and stomach will prevent nutrients from reaching the skin surface, thereby exacerbating the symptoms of psoriasis.

5. The relationship between the cycle of psoriasis and the liver

The initial onset, recurrence, and severity of psoriasis lesions in patients are related to the seasons, mostly occurring in spring and autumn, worsening in winter, and naturally improving or even self-healing in summer. And what "Suwen · Zangqi Fashilun" mentions: "Disease in the liver improves in summer, worsens in autumn if not healed in summer, doesn't die in autumn but persists in winter," this exactly matches the onset and development process of psoriasis, which is no coincidence.

"Suwen · Zangqi Fashilun" also discusses: "When evil qi invades the body, it follows the principle of overcoming each other, getting better when it reaches its generation, worse when it reaches its non-generation, holding steady when it reaches its generation, and arising when it finds its own position." That is, the transmission process of disease has the characteristic of "overcoming each other," roughly following the rule of "getting better when it reaches its generation." The disease reaches the time it generates according to the Five Elements theory, meaning that the disease should be cured at that time. For example, if the disease occurs in the liver, then the liver disease should improve in summer since wood generates fire. "Getting worse when it reaches its non-generation" refers to the time when the diseased organ cannot overcome, i.e., the time it is controlled. That is, liver disease should worsen in autumn because autumn belongs to metal, and metal controls wood. "Holding steady when it reaches its generation" means the time that generates itself, the season that generates itself, and the disease holds steady. That is, liver disease enters a stalemate stage in winter. "Arising when it finds its own position" refers to liver disease belonging to wood, and spring is its position, so the liver should show improvement in spring. This again proves that the root cause of psoriasis lies in the liver.
