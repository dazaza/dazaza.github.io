Furniture Dimensions and Health Standards

by longersing on 2009-10-28 18:31:54

The furniture size and health standards are very important. When you choose furniture, do your requirements for material, color, and appearance exceed the importance of size? Inappropriate furniture size can affect people's health. The same principle applies when we say that the height of a bed determines health.

If the furniture size differs from the standard by a few centimeters, although there may be no significant difference in appearance, people may experience problems such as spinal deformities, lower back muscle strain, or declining vision after prolonged use. We have listed several common furniture standard sizes for your reference:

1. Sofa: The width of the sofa seat should not be less than 480mm; the seat depth should be between 480mm and 600mm; the seat height should be within the range of 360mm to 420mm. Nowadays, some simple and stylish low sofas are favored by young people. However, the height and width of the sofa are closely related to health.

If the front width of the sofa is less than the standard, the space will become narrow; if the seat depth is too deep, the calves cannot naturally hang down, and the calf muscles may be compressed; if it is too shallow, one may feel unstable while sitting; if the seat is too high, it feels like sitting on a tall chair, which is uncomfortable. If it is too low, it will be more strenuous to stand up.

2. Bed: 440mm is the healthy height (calculated based on the distance from the bedding surface to the ground). Many people prefer low beds like tatami, finding them simple and convenient. Some also use the space under the bed as a storage area, elevating the mattress with small cabinets. In fact, if the bed edge is too high or too low from the ground, the legs cannot land properly, and over time, the leg nerves may be compressed.

3. Pillow: The ideal pillow height for adults is 150mm, slightly lower for the elderly and children. This is because pillow height directly affects sleep. A pillow that is too high prevents natural relaxation, affecting sleep quality and hindering blood circulation, especially impacting the growth and development of children and the health of the elderly.

4. The height difference between a table and chair should be between 280mm and 300mm; the height under the desk should not be less than 580mm, and the width should not be less than 520mm. Tables that are too short or chairs that are too high can affect the spine and lumbar vertebrae, especially harming children's vision and growth. If the space under the desk does not meet the standard, the legs cannot move freely.

Additionally, since each family has televisions of different sizes, when choosing a TV cabinet, it should be calculated based on the height of the television and the sofa. If the viewer’s line of sight lands exactly on the center of the TV screen, it indicates that the height of the TV cabinet is appropriate.

The best way to judge whether the furniture size is suitable is to try it personally. If you feel back pain after working at a computer desk for a while, or if you feel tense despite having adequate sleep, it is likely that the dimensions of your furniture are problematic. Therefore, when selecting furniture, make sure to try out various options, ask questions, and you can also customize according to personal needs.
