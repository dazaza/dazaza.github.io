1. What is the function of a car air conditioning refrigeration compressor?

- Suction Function: When the compressor works, it sucks low-temperature and low-pressure gaseous refrigerant from the evaporator into the compressor cylinder. Due to the suction action of the compressor, the pressure inside the evaporator decreases further, making the refrigerant easier to boil, thus lowering the temperature of the evaporator.

- Compression Function: The compressor sucks in low-temperature and low-pressure refrigerant vapor, compressing it to transform it into high-temperature and high-pressure refrigerant vapor.

- Circulation Function: The compressor expels the compressed high-temperature and high-pressure gaseous refrigerant, sending it to the condenser to release heat outward. In this way, the refrigerant continuously circulates within the refrigeration system, with the compressor acting as the power source for the refrigerant circulation.

2. Write out the four working processes of the refrigeration cycle in the refrigeration system and what the state (physical state, pressure, temperature) of the refrigerant is in each process?

- Compression Process: The compressor compresses the low-temperature gaseous refrigerant from the low-pressure side of the evaporator (temperature approximately 0°C, pressure about 0.15MPa) into high-temperature and high-pressure (temperature 70°C~80°C, pressure about 1.5MPa) gaseous refrigerant, which is then sent to the condenser for cooling and temperature reduction.

- Condensation Process: The overheated gaseous refrigerant sent to the condenser releases heat when its temperature is much higher than the external temperature, exchanging heat and being condensed into medium-temperature liquid refrigerant with a pressure of 1.0MPa~1.2MPa.

- Expansion Process: After condensation, the liquid refrigerant passes through the expansion valve, causing its space volume to increase, resulting in a sharp drop in both pressure and temperature, transforming it into low-temperature and low-pressure wet steam (temperature about -5°C, pressure about 0.15MPa), allowing it to enter the evaporator and quickly absorb heat for vaporization. Simultaneously, flow control occurs to supply the required refrigerant to the evaporator, thereby achieving temperature control.

- Evaporation Process: The liquid refrigerant passing through the expansion valve turns into low-temperature and low-pressure wet steam, flowing through the evaporator and continuously absorbing heat to vaporize, turning into low-temperature and low-pressure gaseous refrigerant (temperature approximately 0°C, pressure about 0.15MPa), absorbing heat inside the vehicle. The low-temperature gaseous refrigerant is then sucked back into the compressor, pressurized, and pumped to the condenser for condensation, completing the cold cycle.

5. What are the functions of lubricating oil? How should lubricating oil be used correctly?

- Lubrication Function: Lubricates the friction surfaces of the compressor, reducing friction and preventing wear.

- Cooling Function: Carries away the friction heat from the friction surfaces, helping dissipate heat.

- Sealing Function: Acts as an oil seal between the compressor cylinder wall and the piston (or rotor), as well as at the shaft seal, reducing refrigerant leakage.

- Reduces Compressor Noise

- Usage of Lubricating Oil: (1) Add or replace lubricating oil according to the specifications provided by the original vehicle's air conditioning manual (2) Do not use deteriorated lubricating oil, nor mix different grades of lubricating oil (3) Lubricating oil has a strong hygroscopic capability; store it sealed (4) Add or replace lubricating oil according to the specified amount provided by the original vehicle. Excessive use will reduce the cooling efficiency of the car air conditioning; insufficient use will shorten the life of the compressor (5) When releasing refrigerant, do so slowly to avoid the simultaneous spraying of lubricating oil and refrigerant.

7. Briefly describe the working principle of the car air conditioning system.

- Working Principle: The compressor absorbs gaseous refrigerant from the evaporator, heats and pressurizes it, then sends it to the condenser for temperature reduction while maintaining high pressure. It then reaches the accumulator dryer for drying treatment before reaching the expansion valve where it undergoes temperature and pressure reduction. This point is the boundary line between high and low pressure in the system. Finally, it is sent to the evaporator to absorb heat.

- Minimal adverse effects on the car, requiring smooth compressor operation, low noise, minimal vibration, and little impact on engine speed when starting or stopping the compressor, with a small start-up torque

10. Analyze the working principle of the swash plate compressor.

- Working Principle: The swash plate compressor has a reciprocating single-direction piston structure. Its working principle is to convert the rotational motion of the pulley through the main shaft and swash plate into the reciprocating motion of the piston. The planetary disc connected to the piston, under the effect of a pair of anti-rotation gears, can only swing under the push of the swash plate and cannot rotate together with the swash plate, causing the piston to perform reciprocating motion axially within the cylinder hole.

12. What is the working principle of the expansion valve?

- Working Principle: Through the temperature sensing package sensing the changes in superheat at the outlet end of the evaporator, it causes the charged substance within the pressure-sensitive system to produce pressure changes, acting on the diaphragm, prompting the diaphragm to form up and down displacements, transmitting this force through the diaphragm to the push rod, thereby moving the valve needle up and down, closing or opening the valve, serving to reduce pressure and retain fluid. At the same time, it automatically adjusts the supply of refrigerant to the evaporator and maintains a certain degree of superheat at the outlet end of the evaporator, ensuring the full utilization of the evaporator's heat transfer area and reducing the occurrence of liquid strike phenomena.

13. What are the requirements for installing the expansion valve?

- Requirements: (1) The expansion valve should generally be installed upright, not inverted (2) The temperature sensing package is usually installed on the upper surface of the horizontal outlet pipe of the evaporator, securely wrapped to ensure good contact between the temperature sensing package and the pipe, with a clean and tightly adhered contact surface, and wrapped with thermal insulation and moisture-proof material. If necessary, the expansion valve body is also wrapped with thermal insulation material. (3) The external balance tube should be installed on the upper surface of the pipe segment behind the temperature sensing package (4) For externally adjustable expansion valves, adjustments must be made when the engine is running normally and should be operated by skilled air conditioning technicians.

14. Briefly describe the characteristics of the H-shaped expansion valve.

- Characteristics: (1) The system return gas directly passes through the valve body cavity as a sensing signal, therefore, it can not only eliminate the need for external balance tubes, capillary tubes, and temperature sensing packages but also improve the sensitivity of the system (2) Without temperature sensing packages, capillary tubes, and external balance tubes, it avoids issues such as fractures and exposure of the charging system due to vehicle bumps and vibrations, as well as loose wrapping of the temperature sensing package affecting the normal operation of the expansion valve, enhancing the shock resistance of the expansion valve. (3) A detachable power head is welded from stainless steel diaphragms and stainless steel shells, not only having high strength and long life but also having a significantly smaller charging volume compared to systems with capillary tubes and temperature sensing packages, thus having a very small thermal inertia in the charging system (4) The static superheat adjustment is external, facilitating system matching and debugging.

15. What is the function of the accumulator dryer?

- Function: Temporarily stores the refrigerant liquefied in the condenser in the refrigeration system, supplying the evaporator as needed based on the refrigerant load requirements, and supplements the minor leaks in the system.

16. What consequences would the presence of moisture in a 134a system cause?

- Consequences: Acid corrosion of metals, producing "copper plating" phenomenon

17. What are the requirements for installing a desiccant filter?

- Installation: (1) Installed in a well-ventilated and cooled place, away from heat sources, usually between the car hood and radiator (2) Must be installed upright, with a tilt greater than 15°. If the slope is too large, liquid and gaseous refrigerants will not be completely separated. (3) During the installation and maintenance of the air conditioning system, the desiccant must be the last component connected to the system to prevent air from entering the desiccant. Because the moisture and other non-condensable impurities in the air can corrode metals, causing small metal particles to peel off and block the system.

18. What is the function of the sight glass? Where is it generally installed?

- Sight Glass: Also known as an observation window, its function is to observe whether the refrigerant charge is sufficient during refrigerant addition and to determine if the air conditioning system has insufficient refrigerant due to leaks. Installation: Some are installed on the accumulator dryer, others on the high-pressure piping system.

19. What is the difference between a suction accumulator and a regular accumulator?

- Difference: (1) The suction accumulator is located in the low-pressure suction area of the refrigeration system (between the evaporator and compressor), whereas the regular accumulator is located in the high-pressure area (between the condenser and expansion valve). (2) The former retains liquid refrigerant, which slowly evaporates in the low-pressure area, leaving only gaseous refrigerant exiting the suction accumulator, thus serving as a gas-liquid separator; the latter retains excess refrigerant to adjust operational needs.

20. What are the advantages of a suction accumulator?

- Advantages: (1) Ensures that the compressor only sucks in gaseous refrigerant, not liquid refrigerant, preventing liquid strike in the compressor (2) Can reduce the compressor discharge pulse, balancing the system (3) In cases of insufficient refrigerant, can maintain a certain amount of lubricating oil returning, increasing the system's tolerance to refrigerant leaks.

21. Briefly describe the working principle of the suction accumulator.

- Working Principle: The refrigerant enters the container from the top, where the liquid refrigerant sinks to the bottom of the container, and the gaseous refrigerant at the top is drawn out by the conduit toward the compressor. On the draw-out conduit at the bottom of the container, there is a small hole that allows a small amount of lubricating oil to flow back to the compressor to maintain lubrication needs during compressor operation. This small hole also allows a small amount of liquid refrigerant to return to the compressor. Since the liquid refrigerant will continue to vaporize in the pipe before reaching the compressor, it will not cause "liquid strike," and also replenishes the flow requirement.

22. What are the main reasons for refrigerant leakage from the hose in car air conditioning?

- Reasons: (1) Improper selection of hose material (2) Poor ozone resistance of the outer layer material of the hose, quickly accelerating leakage at the hose joint and inner rubber layer (3) Poor cold resistance of the hose material, high brittleness temperature, and excessive permanent deformation of the rubber under compression (4) Improper crimping pressure at the joint and hose connection (5) Improper dimensions of the inner pipe at the joint, causing too loose or too tight connections.

23. Please describe the characteristics of natural ventilation.

- Natural Ventilation: Also called dynamic pressure ventilation. Utilizing the air pressure generated by vehicle movement, devices that allow external air to enter the interior are called natural ventilation devices. When the vehicle moves, different pressures are produced depending on the contact position of the airflow with the vehicle body. Installing the intake port in a position generating positive pressure and the exhaust port in a position generating negative pressure forms air flow driven by dynamic pressure.

24. Please describe the characteristics of forced ventilation.

- Characteristics: In forced ventilation devices, electric fans or similar devices force air to flow through the interior of the vehicle. The installation positions of the intake and exhaust ports are the same as those in natural ventilation devices. Forced ventilation devices are generally used together with heating or cooling devices. Forced ventilation uses blowers to forcibly introduce external air into the vehicle for ventilation and air exchange. This method requires energy and ventilation equipment, and in integrated climate control automotive air conditioners, most adopt combined ventilation, heating, and cooling systems that mix outside air with conditioned air before sending it into the vehicle. Such ventilation devices are commonly found in high-end sedans and luxury vehicles.

25. Please describe the characteristics of comprehensive ventilation.

- Characteristics: Comprehensive ventilation refers to the simultaneous use of natural and forced ventilation in a vehicle. Although the comprehensive ventilation system is structurally complex, it is energy-efficient, economical, and has low operating costs. Especially in spring and autumn, using natural ventilation to introduce cool outside air can replace the operation of the cooling system while still ensuring comfort.

26. What is the main function of the car air conditioning heating system?

- Function: The main role of the car air conditioning heating system is to adjust the air to a comfortable temperature for passengers along with the evaporator; provide pure heat to the vehicle in winter to raise the internal temperature; and deliver hot air to defrost and dehumidify when frost and fog form on the windshield.

27. What is the principle of the water-heating type heater device?

- Principle: The working principle of the water-heating type heater device is to divert coolant from the engine through a coolant control valve into the heater core of the heater blower, where the coolant releases heat and returns to the engine via the heater outlet. Cold air is forced by the heater blower through the heater core, heated, and blown into the vehicle through different outlets for defrosting and heating. Another route of coolant flows through the radiator, cools down, and returns to the engine via the radiator outlet pipe. By controlling the opening and flow rate of the coolant control valve, the heat output of the heater can be adjusted.

28. What is the basic structure of the car air conditioning air distribution system?

- Structure: It usually consists of three parts: the first part is the air inlet section, mainly composed of flaps and servos controlling fresh air and recirculated air inside the vehicle; the second part is the air mixing section, mainly consisting of heaters and evaporators to provide air at the desired temperature; the third part is the air distribution section, directing air towards the face, feet, and windshield. These parts are connected to the instrument panel control keys through manual control cables (manual air conditioning), pneumatic vacuum devices (semi-automatic air conditioning), or electronic pneumatic controls (fully automatic air conditioning) to execute air distribution.

29. What functional keys are there on the manual operation panel?

- Functional Keys: (1) Function Selection Key (2) Temperature Key (3) Fan Speed Adjustment Key (4) Defrost Key (5) Economy Operation Key

30. Briefly describe the working principle of the vacuum tank.

- Principle: When the engine vacuum is greater than that in the vacuum tank, the hollow diaphragm valve expands to the right, connecting the vacuum chamber and increasing its vacuum level. Simultaneously, the diaphragm overcomes the spring force to move left, opening the vacuum chamber and the vacuum outlet of the vacuum actuation system, forming a passage. When the engine vacuum is less than that in the vacuum tank, the external pressure flattens the hollow diaphragm valve, closing the path to the vacuum chamber, while the diaphragm moves right, closing the vacuum outlet to maintain the vacuum level in the tank.

31. What are the three types of operating systems for the car air conditioning air distribution system, and what are their characteristics?

- Operating Systems: Manual, semi-automatic vacuum operating system, fully automatic electronic-controlled vacuum operating system.

- Characteristics: Although the air distribution systems of manual and semi-automatic vacuum operating systems differ significantly in terms of overall structure and control circuits of the car air conditioning, there is no strict distinction in their working principles and control processes. The difference lies in the control of flaps and valves; the manual operating system partially uses steel cable linkage structures, while the semi-automatic vacuum operating system entirely adopts vacuum control structures. Their common feature is that system operations rely on manual conversion of the air conditioning panel control switches, and the air distribution work is completed through actuators.

32. What are the commonly used protection and control devices in the car air conditioning system?

- Common Protection Devices: Electromagnetic clutch, thermostat, idle control device, air conditioning amplifier, pressure switch, overheat and overpressure protection device, engine power protection device.

33. What are the components of the car air conditioning circuit control?

- Composition: Evaporator fan control, condenser fan control, temperature control (compressor control), ventilation system control, protective circuits, etc.

34. Briefly describe the working principle of the bimetallic strip thermostat.

- Working Principle: The bimetallic strip thermostat lacks a capillary tube and works directly through the air passing over its surface. Two metal strips with different expansion and contraction rates due to temperature changes extend flat, closing the contacts, and connecting the current, engaging the electromagnetic clutch of the compressor. Due to temperature changes, these two metal strips deform differently and bend, separating the contacts, interrupting the current to the electromagnetic clutch, and stopping the compressor's operation. When cold air passes over the thermostat, it causes one of the bimetallic strips in the thermostat to contract into a bow shape
