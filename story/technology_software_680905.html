Deep Maternal Love

Maternal love is the greatest and most selfless love in the world. She gave us life, she gave us love. This kind of love is something no one else can give, nor replace. Maternal love can inspire our motivation to move forward, and sometimes it can even bring us miraculous and great strength.

That day, after my electronic organ class, my phone rang. It was Dad calling, saying he wouldn't be home and couldn't open the door for me, so I had to do it myself. I casually agreed and quickly hung up the phone. I happily walked home, opened my bag, and prepared to take out the key, but no matter how I searched, I couldn't find it. I panicked, could it be that I forgot to bring it? I couldn't think of anything else, so I emptied everything from my bag onto the ground and carefully searched through it, but still found nothing. I thought to myself, "Oh no, what am I going to do?" While I was racking my brain for solutions, a person came to mind—Mom! Mom could come and open the door for me! With this thought, I didn't hesitate to call Mom. After knowing the situation, Mom immediately agreed to my request and said she would return as soon as possible. My heart calmed down a bit.

I waited for a while, then saw a familiar figure in the distance. I thought it must be Mom. I saw Mom riding her bicycle quickly, sweat dripping down drop by drop. When I saw this, I felt an indescribable taste in my heart. Mom stopped the bike in front of me, hurriedly took out the key, opened the door for me, and said, "Go home quickly, Mom will go back to the office and buy you some good food after work." As Mom was speaking, she pushed her bicycle and rode towards the office. Watching Mom's retreating figure, my heart became increasingly restless. I recalled how I had treated Mom before, and a sense of guilt welled up in my heart.

For us, they devoted all their energy to us, allowing us to grow up happily under their care and protection; for us, they worked hard every day, getting up early and coming home late, earning blood-sweat money to let us study; for us, they economized on clothing and food every day, not wasting a single cent; for us, they aged from middle age to old age, black hair turning white, wrinkles on their foreheads increasing significantly; for us, they persisted in working despite illness without caring about their own health... They have given too much, but we don't know how to appreciate or cherish their hard work, thinking it's only natural, it's what they should do.

Our parents have done so much for us, yet we never know gratitude, never know how to be filial to them. We can't even wash our own clothes cleanly or cook rice well, relying on our parents for everything. Today's incident gave me an opportunity to understand family affection, making me realize the depth of maternal love.

Responsibility

When I was young, the word I heard the most was 'responsibility'. At that time, I didn't think much of it, but after an event, I understood the meaning of responsibility.

One weekend, after returning from the youth palace, I imagined being able to eat ice cream and watch TV at home on such a hot summer day. I looked forward to it, quickening my steps, with only one goal in mind: home.

As soon as I entered the house, a cool breeze greeted me. To enjoy my favorite TV program as soon as possible, I decided to practice today's piece first. I opened my bag, reaching into a familiar place, but where were my scores? I couldn't play without them! I flipped left and right, finally dumping everything out of my bag, but still couldn't find them. I sat on the ground like a deflated balloon. Calming down, I remembered that I had forgotten to put the book in my bag because I was so eager to get home. Thinking of this, my heart turned cold.

Holding out a little hope, I approached Dad and tentatively asked, "Dad, I left my score at the youth palace. Can you take me to get it?" Dad shook his head. Considering the weather outside, I continued to beg, "Please take me!" Dad frowned and said a sentence: "Your own responsibility is your own to bear!" Saying this, he went inside. I watched his retreating figure and muttered to myself: "Responsibility again, responsibility!" Saying this, I stepped out of the house.

As soon as I exited the building, a wave of heat hit me. Alas, who made me forgetful? This is the punishment. I quickened my pace towards the youth palace, looking at the scorching weather, I couldn't help but hesitate, thinking: "Let's go back, explain to the teacher next time." But this thought was immediately rejected: "No, I can't let Dad laugh at me." My steps quickened, sweat dripped down my face drop by drop. I started hoping that Dad would come pick me up, glancing back as I walked: "Could he really be so heartless, not loving me anymore?" Thus, I walked all the way, hoping all the way, unaware that I had already reached the youth palace. After taking the book, realizing there was no hope of seeing Dad, I had no choice but to walk home drenched in sweat.

As soon as I entered the neighborhood, I saw a shadow downstairs far away. I took a few quick steps and realized it was Dad. Just as I was about to run over, Dad went upstairs. I finally got to the doorstep, and upon seeing Mom who had just returned from work, the委屈suddenly rose to my lips. Mom seemed to see through my thoughts. She took a towel and wiped the sweat off my face while letting me sit down, earnestly telling me: "Actually, Dad was very worried about you. After you left, he has been waiting downstairs. He did this to make you understand that a person must have a sense of responsibility. Only by being responsible for oneself can one be responsible for others. He doesn't love you any less!"

I suddenly understood. That's right, responsibility is something we need to have as people, we need to be responsible for ourselves and others. Such a small matter made me understand the meaning of responsibility, making me value it more in the future.

Reply: The Warmth of Love

Maternal love is the greatest love, though it is very ordinary and common, it brings warmth to my soul.

I remember once, on a rainy day, I forgot to bring an umbrella, got caught in the rain, and when I got home, I felt very cold, dizzy, and sleepy. Seeing my suffering, Mom gently kissed my forehead with her lips: "Ah, so hot, are you running a fever?" Then, Mom quickly helped me lie down on the bed, took out a thermometer, shook it twice, and placed it gently under my armpit, then covered me with a blanket. Five minutes later, Mom took out the thermometer, checked it against the light, 37.5 degrees Celsius. Though it was a low-grade fever, Mom was still very anxious.

Mom hurriedly soaked a towel in water, froze it in the fridge, and then placed it on my head. Mom kept changing the towel, trying to lower my temperature quickly. I was very tired at that moment, and in my daze, I saw the sweat on Mom's face. At that moment, I felt that Mom's love was like a cup of hot tea, warming my soul. After Mom brought me medicine and water, she fed it to me pill by pill, and I fell asleep in a daze.

I don't know how long it was, but when I woke up, I saw Mom with sweat all over her head, and I was moved to tears. Seeing that I had opened my eyes, Mom gently asked, "Are you feeling better now?" As she spoke, she lightly kissed my forehead with her lips: "You're doing much better." Then, she walked into the kitchen and brought out a tray with a bowl of steaming rice porridge and a dish of cold mixed green radish cubes, which were my favorite dishes. Mom put the green radish cubes into the rice porridge, stirred slowly, blew away the steam, and fed me bite by bite. As I ate, I wanted to thank Mom, but I didn't say anything. I didn't want to break the tranquility of the moment. I thought, if time could stop, please let it stay at this beautiful moment. I felt I was the happiest person in the world, and maternal love in my heart was like a sweet candy, giving me a sweet taste.

After dinner, Mom let me lie down and sleep a little longer. I closed my eyes lightly and unknowingly fell asleep again. When I woke up, I saw Mom tightly holding my hand, watching me. Suddenly, I felt that Mom was like a guardian angel protecting me. I gazed out the window, the stars opened their eyes and watched here, and the moon sprinkled its brightest moonlight into this warm little house. They were very quiet, seemingly unwilling to disturb this silence.

Before sleeping, Mom used warm water to clean my body. The soft and warm towel slid over every part of my body, and I felt a warm current flow into my heart, warming my soul. My body felt light, like a fairy freely flying in the blue sky, surrounded by seven colors of light, feeling extremely good.

In the morning, I woke up and surprisingly found Mom sleeping beside my bed. Her kind face was filled with a smile. To ensure I slept well, Mom stayed by my bedside all night and didn't return to her room to sleep. I gently supported Mom, lifted her onto the bed, and let her rest well. She was so tired.

Mom's love for me is like the stars in the sky, countless, always accompanying me as I grow. Whether in learning or life, Mom is always with me, caring for me, encouraging me, and helping me... It is because of Mom's love that I can overcome many difficulties and reach the shore of victory.

The greatness of maternal love, the preciousness of family ties, is not grandiose, but rather simple and ordinary. Family ties are the deepest imprint in human hearts, the purest, most sincere, and most passionate feelings.
