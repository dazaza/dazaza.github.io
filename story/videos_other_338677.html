〔One-piece dress〕 refers to a one-piece garment where the top and skirt are combined into a single piece. The one-piece dress is known as the "Queen of Styles" among various styles and designs, being the most unpredictable, versatile, and favored style by women. Depending on the wearer, there can be children's one-piece dresses and those for young women. Almost all factors that can vary in women's tops and skirts can be combined to form different styles of one-piece dresses. One-piece dresses can also take on various contours and waistline positions based on design requirements.

〔Qipao, Chinese dress〕 Also called a Chinese-style Qipao. Its main structural features include a standing collar, right large lapel, tight waist, and side slits on the hem. The Qipao is a traditional robe for Chinese women, originating from the clothing of the Manchu people during the Qing Dynasty. After the Xinhai Revolution and modern improvements, the traditional craftsmanship of the Qipao was retained, making it more suitable for representing the human body. This proportion is very suitable for the Eastern body type characterized by a flatter chest, lower hips, and longer upper body. The Qipao conveys modern humanistic ideas and the unique rhythm beauty of the East.

〔Bridal gown, bridal veil〕 Refers to the attire worn by women during their wedding. Traditional Chinese bridal gowns are typically bright red Chinese-style suits. In modern urban settings, brides often wear Western-style wedding gowns that originated from Europe and America. Western bridal gowns are predominantly white, symbolizing purity and sanctity.

〔Swallow-tailed coat, swallowtail〕 A formal suit worn by men in specific official occasions. Its basic structure consists of a short front with a western-style collar, a long back, and a split tail resembling a swallow’s tail, which originates from European carriage drivers' attire. The color is mostly black, indicating seriousness, dedication, and sanctity.

〔Evening dress, evening suit〕 Refers to the elegant gown worn by women at night social events. Evening dresses are made from high-quality materials with strong light-reflective colors, showcasing a powerful expression under stage lighting. Western evening dresses, set against the backdrop of the Western upper-class society’s nightlife, often feature low-cut backs and long dresses.

〔Overcoat〕 A long outerwear designed to protect against wind and cold, worn over regular clothing. It can be categorized by length into short, medium-length, and long overcoats. Based on fabric types, the main varieties include woolen, cotton, down, fur, leather, and faux fur overcoats.

〔Weather-all coat & cloak〕 An outerwear used for both wind and rain protection, available in single-layer, lined, long, or short versions. The style varies with fashion trends.

〔Dress smock, manteau, mantle〕 A sleeveless, neck-tied windproof outerwear worn over the shoulders. If it has a hood, it is generally called a cape. The mantle is usually a one-piece structure, commonly worn by northerners and children in winter.

〔Robe〕 A loose and long robe worn in the bedroom. Fabrics often come in soft, elegant colors and delicate floral patterns, with common materials including pure cotton or high-grade silk.

〔Sleepwear〕 Refers to two-piece sleepwear consisting of a top and pants worn together.

〔Suit〕 Refers to coordinated outfits worn as a set, usually made from the same material and color, creating a consistent style. These are commonly chosen in professional settings.
