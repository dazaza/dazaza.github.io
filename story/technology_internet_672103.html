Currently, the most popular skincare product is none other than collagen. As a beauty-conscious woman who loves collagen, the more you understand about collagen, the closer you are to health and beauty. Below, the editor will take you to understand relevant knowledge about collagen:

1. Collagen and skin: Collagen is the main component of skin collagen, accounting for 70-80% of the total protein in the skin. The collagen molecule has a triple helix structure that other proteins do not possess, and it is connected with a large number of anionic side chains. The triple helix structure and anionic side chains can firmly lock water molecules, thereby giving the skin high elasticity and tension. Not only can it stretch outward, but it can also quickly retract. A decrease in the amount and quality of collagen leads to a drop in elasticity. The loose skin on an elderly person's neck cannot quickly retract after being stretched.

2. Functions of collagen:

1) Locking water molecules to maintain skin moisture and elasticity, preventing the formation of brown pigmentation, and maintaining healthy skin (preventing spots, peeling, red blood vessels, etc.)

2) Nourishing hair to make it black and shiny

3) Nourishing capillary walls: Capillary walls are also composed of collagen. A decrease in collagen content in the capillary walls makes the vessels thinner and more transparent, leading to visible red blood vessels on the face. Supplementing sufficient collagen can prevent the formation of red blood vessels.

4) Cell proliferation, growth, and metabolism: Collagen serves as the nutrition and environment for cell growth, promoting cell proliferation, growth, and metabolism, which enhances human immunity and energy.

5) Promoting bone formation and preventing osteoporosis.

3. Collagen is the young molecule that keeps the skin beautiful:

As age increases, the content and quality of collagen in the skin gradually decrease. The collagen fibers become dry, coarse, and stiff, resulting in the loss of water in the skin. The face then shows signs of aging. The process of decreasing collagen content and aging collagen fibers begins at the age of 20.

4. Taking collagen has significant tightening effects on the skin, making loose skin tight and strong:

This tightening effect includes correcting sagging facial skin, loose leg skin, and drooping abdominal bulges.

5. Oral collagen can prevent and treat UV radiation damage:

UV radiation severely damages collagen fibers, causing them to age and reduce in quantity. Only by orally taking collagen and promoting the synthesis of new collagen within the body can one prevent UV-induced skin damage.

6. Collagen fibers are functional molecules that keep the breasts firm and beautiful:

1) Collagen has a unique triple helix structure, forming the suspensory ligament made of collagen fibers, acting like "steel cables" to firmly fix the breasts on the skin and body wall.

2) Collagen fibers are widely distributed in the breasts. Young collagen fibers have a dense structure, pulling and supporting breast tissue like an elastic net.

3) The collagen fibers in the surface skin of the breasts must be youthful and powerful to tighten the skin of the breasts, keeping them upright and reviving youthful charm.

7. Weight loss and collagen:

Weight loss involves expelling or sucking out excess fat accumulated under the skin through different methods. The relationship between fat and skin collagen is similar to that of "grain" and "bags." Pouring out part of the "grain" makes the "bag" shrink. After weight loss, wrinkles appear on the face and the abdominal skin sags due to the removal of fat. Oral collagen can promptly deliver highly elastic triple-helix collagen fibers to the skin, tightening the skin, increasing its tension, and preventing the skin from sagging or developing wrinkles after weight loss.

8. Taking collagen has a regulatory effect on many gynecological and obstetric diseases:

Since the endometrium is composed of collagen fibers, during a normal menstrual cycle, the endometrium matures (rich in collagen fibers) and sheds (loss of collagen fibers) according to hormonal changes. After shedding, the uterus needs to quickly synthesize new collagen to repair the endometrium. However, due to the lack of hydroxyproline, a raw material for synthesizing collagen in food, or excessive stress from study or work, the endometrium cannot reach sufficient thickness, leading to irregular bleeding and menstrual disorders. Many girls and women with menstrual disorders see their menstruation become regular after taking collagen for 1-2 months. Similarly, collagen plays a nurturing role in postpartum recovery and after induced abortion. Taking collagen can rapidly repair the damaged endometrium caused by childbirth or induced abortion, allowing women to receive timely care.
