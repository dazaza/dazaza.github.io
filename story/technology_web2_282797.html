Professional logistics express: Guangdong Logistics

by myztky32 on 2009-11-28 22:02:43

+ SINOTRANS South China Shanghai CR Express Center Expands Significantly: According to SINOTRANS South China Shanghai CR Express Co., Ltd., the storage area of the South China Shanghai CR Express Center will soon expand significantly. The main structures of Warehouse No. 7 and No. 9, with a total construction area of 75,000 square meters, were capped on November 18th, and the warehouses are expected to be operational around the Spring Festival next year.

+ Jiangmen Aims to Become the Western Pearl River Delta Regional CR Express Center: In ten years, Jiangmen is set to become the "Western Pearl River Delta regional CR Express Center serving the Pearl River Delta, radiating to western Guangdong, and facing Greater Southwest China." Jiangmen should fully seize the historical opportunities of infrastructure construction such as the Hong Kong-Zhuhai-Macao Bridge and the Guangzhou-Zhuhai Railway to vigorously promote the development of modern express logistics.

+ Chaozhou Establishes the Second Largest Station of the Xiamen-Shenzhen High-Speed Railway in Guangdong: The Xiamen-Shenzhen high-speed railway has chosen Chaozhou for its train station, which will be the second largest station in Guangdong after Shenzhen. After the line opens, it will only take two hours to travel from Chaozhou to Shenzhen, making Chaozhou a railway hub in the eastern Guangdong region, and forming a "two-hour economic circle" along the southeastern coast.

+ Construction Begins on the Eastern Guangdong Aquatic Products Logistics Express Center: The Eastern Guangdong Aquatic Products Logistics Express Center is being invested in and constructed by Rao Ping Hongzhou Fishing Port Construction Development Co., Ltd. The project covers a total area of 2,000 mu (about 133 hectares), with a planned total investment of 3.3 billion yuan, developed in three phases. The reclamation of 200 mu has been largely completed, and it is expected to be ready by the end of 2010.

+ Humen Port Bonded Logistics Center Passes Preliminary Acceptance: On November 10th, the bonded logistics center in Dongguan, one of the first seventeen approved nationwide, passed the preliminary acceptance inspection conducted by a joint pre-acceptance working group consisting of Huizhou Customs, Guangdong Provincial Department of Finance, Guangdong Provincial State Taxation Bureau, and the Guangdong Branch of the State Administration of Foreign Exchange.

+ Zhongshan Bonded Logistics Center Passes Preliminary Acceptance Yesterday: The Zhongshan Bonded Logistics Center (Type B) passed the preliminary acceptance yesterday. Of the nine planned bonded warehouses (approximately 159,200 square meters), the first two batches, Warehouses No. 3 to No. 8, totaling 108,200 square meters, have been fully completed and delivered for use.

+ Over 100 Shenzhen Logistics Companies Encounter Organized Robberies on the Guangdong-Jiangxi Expressway: In recent months, over a hundred logistics companies in Shenzhen have encountered organized robberies on the Guangdong-Jiangxi Expressway sections near Huizhou and Heyuan, with each robbery amounting to between 600,000 yuan to 3 million yuan. Frequent thefts have caused significant financial losses, leaving multiple freight companies struggling to continue operations.

+ Zhongshan Bonded Logistics Center Scheduled for Mid-November Preliminary Acceptance: The Zhongshan Bonded Logistics Center is a key project during the "Eleventh Five-Year Plan" period for both Guangdong Province and Zhongshan City, with an initial land use of 801 mu. This year, plans include constructing nine warehouses and comprehensive office buildings.

+ "Rail-Port Intermodal Transport" Helps Shaoguan Enterprises Reduce Logistics Costs: "Rail-port intermodal transport" refers to a joint transportation method where export transshipment goods can be transported via rail from the place of departure to the port of exit or from the port of entry to the destination via rail, reducing enterprise transportation costs through the low-cost, quality service provided by rail transport.

+ Suggestions for the Informationization Transformation of Modern Logistics Industry in Zhongshan: Zhongshan should seize the policy opportunity of "pioneering and piloting" granted by the "Development and Reform Outline of the Pearl River Delta", actively coordinate and cooperate with the national logistics technology standards committee and the national logistics information standards committee, and take the lead in promoting standardization work in specialized facilities, industry technology, personnel management, and product coding in Zhongshan's logistics sector.

+ Unlicensed Freight Yards on Sha Tail Road to Be Cleared Out by the End of the Month: The relocation of freight yards on Sha Tail Road has been ongoing for two years. To make room for the整治of the left tributary of the Shahe Stream, freight yards along the tributary and their tenants must relocate. According to the Guangzhou Transportation Commission's plan, all 22 freight yards along Sha Tail Road are scheduled to be relocated by June 2008; however, some freight yards remain as "nail households."

+ Zhanjiang Port Develops Additional Import and Export Freight Channels: The completion and commissioning of the North Gate checkpoint at Zhanjiang Customs ended the history of only handling customs business at the South Gate checkpoint in the Tiaoshun Port Area. This move will play an important role in further standardizing the management of customs supervision sites and promoting port economic development.

+ Zhanjiang Plans to Build a Western Guangdong Regional Trade and Logistics Center: The Zhanjiang Municipal Party Committee and Government emphasized the need to accelerate the development of modern services, innovate service formats, strengthen port-related logistics and other service industries with comparative advantages, continuously expand scale and improve service levels to build a regional trade and logistics center.

+ Domestic Demand Supports Gradual Recovery of Guangzhou Logistics Park Demand: Although global economic weakness has led to a decline in Guangzhou's industrial exports, domestic demand has cushioned the impact of weak exports. According to statistics from JLL, all positive impacts on the logistics market this year come from domestic trade enterprises. Local enterprises' leasing demand for logistics parks has improved, alleviating the decline in rents.

+ Nanwan Talent Market Hosts Hongwan Commercial Logistics Center Special Recruitment Fair: The Hongwan Commercial Logistics Center and Xiangzhou District Human Resources Center co-hosted a special recruitment fair at the Nanwan Talent Market, aiming to help warming-up enterprises find suitable employees. This was also the first special recruitment fair for the Hongwan Commercial Logistics Center.

+ Expansion of HACTL Inland Container Storage in Shenzhen to Become the Largest Logistics Storage in Southern China: HACTL Inland Container Storage in Shenzhen under the H Hutchison Ports is about to start expansion works. HACTL Inland Storage is among the first enterprises to implement immediate tax rebates upon warehousing and is one of the first 24/7 operation export supervised warehouses in the Day鹏Customs Zone and the only inland port gate. It has customs and CIQ offices on-site and offers import services.

+ Zhongshan Plans to Construct 5 Comprehensive Logistics Bases and 13 Logistics Centers: Zhongshan plans to establish five comprehensive logistics bases and thirteen logistics centers by 2020. According to the plan, Zhongshan will complete the restructuring of existing logistics resources in three phases, focusing on enhancing the competitiveness of backbone logistics bases like the Torch Zone Commercial Transit Logistics Base and logistics enterprises.

+ Shenzhen Increases Logistics Industry Support Funds to 3 Billion Yuan: Shenzhen's total logistics industry support funds this year will increase from a budgeted 2 billion yuan to 3 billion yuan, which is 1.5 times the total of the past four years. The special fund is formulated based on the principle of "appropriately increasing projects and moderately increasing support," mainly providing rewards, interest subsidies, and subsidies related to logistics.
