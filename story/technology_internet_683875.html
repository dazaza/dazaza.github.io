[Qingdao News] From next month, the minimum wage standard will be raised again, with the maximum reaching 1240 yuan

by qssqwe215 on 2012-02-29 16:24:50

The standing committee of the Shandong provincial government recently decided that, starting from March 2012, the minimum wage standard will be raised again. The monthly minimum wage standards are divided into three levels according to the economic development level of different areas: 1240 yuan, 1100 yuan, and 950 yuan.

The Shandong provincial government stated that the increase in the minimum wage standard is based on relevant national and Shandong provincial regulations, combined with the actual socio-economic development situation in Shandong. The hourly minimum wage standard was also adjusted at the same time, with the new three-tier levels being 13 yuan, 11 yuan, and 10 yuan respectively.

It is understood that last year's three categories of monthly minimum wage standards in Shandong were 1100 yuan, 950 yuan, and 800 yuan respectively, while the hourly minimum wage standards were 11.5 yuan, 9.8 yuan, and 8.7 yuan respectively.

The "Twelfth Five-Year Plan" for the Development of Human Resources and Social Security Undertakings in Shandong Province has proposed to accelerate the formation of a reasonable and orderly wage income distribution pattern, curb and gradually narrow unreasonable wage gaps, and ensure that the minimum wage standard increases by more than 13% annually during the "Twelfth Five-Year Plan" period.

In our city, the seven districts' minimum wage standard is expected to rise to 1240 yuan per month, and the five cities' standard to 1100 yuan per month.

News Focus

The minimum wage standards for the seven districts and five cities in our city are expected to rise to 1240 yuan and 1100 yuan respectively. Reporters learned yesterday that the standing committee of the provincial government recently decided that starting from March this year, the minimum wage standard will be raised again. From the Municipal Bureau of Human Resources and Social Security, reporters learned that this will be the 11th adjustment since 1995, but Qingdao's specific adjustment policy has not yet been released.

Reporters learned yesterday from the Municipal Bureau of Human Resources and Social Security that on March 1 last year, our province issued a notice adjusting the provincial minimum wage standards into three tiers: 1100 yuan, 950 yuan, and 800 yuan, with an average increase of 26%. According to requirements, our city adjusted the minimum wage standard for the seven districts from 920 yuan per month to 1100 yuan per month, and the monthly minimum wage standard for employees in surrounding five cities from 620 yuan to 950 yuan.

From this year's standards, it is expected that our city will continue to adjust the minimum wage standards at the higher two tiers, raising the seven districts' minimum wage standard to 1240 yuan per month, and the five cities' standard to 1100 yuan per month. The official adjustment document has not yet been issued.

Minimum Wage Increased Sixfold Over 16 Years

Relevant persons in charge of the Municipal Bureau of Human Resources and Social Security introduced that the first announcement of the minimum wage standard in the city was in 1995, and the standard was adjusted again after about one year of execution. "At that time, there was one standard for the four urban districts and Huangdao-Laoshan, and another for the five cities and Chengyang District, which were 180 yuan and 150 yuan per month respectively. In the following year, they were adjusted to 220 yuan and 180 yuan respectively." The person in charge introduced that looking at the adjustments over the past 16 years, the city's minimum wage standard has continuously increased, having been adjusted ten times up until last year, rising from the initial 180 yuan and 150 yuan to 1100 yuan and 950 yuan per month last year, increasing sixfold or more.

Hourly Minimum Wage Standards Also Set to Be Announced

It is known that last March's adjustment also announced the hourly minimum wage standards applicable to part-time employment, which were set at 11.5 yuan, 9.8 yuan per hour. For Qingdao, the hourly minimum wage standard for part-time employment in the seven urban districts was adjusted from 9.6 yuan to 11.5 yuan; for the five cities, it was adjusted from 7.8 yuan to 9.8 yuan. Compared to the minimum monthly wage standard, the increase in the hourly wage standard was even higher.

In this year's adjustment plan, the hourly minimum wage standards in our province, after adjustment, are set at three levels: 13 yuan, 11 yuan, and 10 yuan respectively. Relevant persons in charge of the Municipal Bureau of Human Resources and Social Security stated that the city is about to issue corresponding implementation policies, and at that time, the hourly minimum wage standard will also be announced.

Difficult Enterprises Use Minimum Standards

According to regulations, the minimum wage standard applies to a small number of enterprises facing operational difficulties, declining economic benefits, and indeed lacking normal wage-paying capabilities (those unable to normally distribute wages for more than three consecutive months), as the lowest labor remuneration paid to workers. Enterprises with paying capabilities must not use the minimum wage standard as the normal wage payment standard. Employers who need to pay wages at the minimum wage standard should follow necessary democratic procedures and report in advance to the same-level human resources and social security administrative department for filing. Employers should restore or raise the wage standards in a timely manner when their production and operation returns to normal. In addition, employers must strictly enforce the minimum wage regulations. Disputes between workers and employers regarding the enforcement of minimum wage regulations shall be handled according to the relevant provisions for labor disputes. For violations of the minimum wage regulations by employers, workers may file complaints with the labor security inspection agency.
