Are the speakers of car audio systems exactly the same as those of home appliances? No, this view is incorrect. In terms of frequency response, power, impedance, and sensitivity, they are basically the same, but in aspects such as manufacturing process, materials used, and structure, there are specially designed features. By understanding the performance, structure, and characteristics of car audio speakers, one can know the differences between them.

The environments of use are different:

a Dustproof: Home appliance speakers are used indoors with less dust, while cars have more dust inside, so a dust cover is needed to prevent noise caused by friction of voice coils.

b Moisture-proof: Cars are exposed to moisture when it rains, driving through water, or washing the car, so car speakers need to be moisture-proof. Nowadays, chemical synthetic material cones are mostly used.

c Vibration: Car speakers are constantly subjected to vibration shocks from the movement of the car, so the design must be sturdy and not loose.

d Limited installation positions for car speakers: To achieve sound field positioning, the angle of tweeters is designed differently.

e Noise: Considering various noises and reflections inside the car, the materials used are different, adopting various design methods to cancel out different types of noise.

f Impedance: Due to the low voltage supply in cars, to obtain high power, the impedance of the speakers is generally lower, often using 4Ω, 3.2Ω, 2Ω, or even 1Ω. Dual voice coils and parallel connection methods can reach 2Ω or even lower, which is rarely used in home audio systems.

g Temperature: Home appliance speakers are used indoors with small temperature differences, whereas cars work outdoors where temperature differences vary greatly due to weather changes. Therefore, they need to be resistant to temperature aging and structural deformation.

If you want to enjoy clear, natural music with wide frequency range from your CD player, you cannot do without high-quality speakers. The frequency response of the speaker is the range of sounds that can accurately reproduce from low to high frequencies. High-quality speakers can handle specific power and levels. Besides continuous power handling capability, they can also regenerate transient music peaks without distortion, which is especially important for digital sound sources like CDs. An important point in the manufacture of car speakers is testing their quality under various environmental and road conditions, designing the best speaker system to confirm the specific performance and sound distribution of the speakers under various installation conditions. Below is an introduction to the structure and characteristics of car speakers.

Most original car tape players use paper cone speakers or dual paper cone speakers (adding a small paper cone in the middle of a single cone). These speakers have narrow frequency responses, and the paper cones can easily become brittle over time, so they are not commonly used now. Why can't the original car speakers be used after replacing with a CD player? Because the original car speakers have low power and high sensitivity, the output power of the replaced CD player is high, making it easy to produce distortion. The high-frequency performance is poor, as the original car audio system is a tape player, and its paired speakers cannot reach very high frequencies; if the high frequencies were good, the background noise of the tape would be revealed. After replacing with a CD source, it is necessary to replace the original car speakers with ones that have strong high-frequency performance to reflect the characteristics of CDs.

Due to installation issues of car speakers, it's difficult to install high, mid, and low-frequency speakers on the same plane as home audio systems. Usually, a hybrid method is used, fixing the high, mid, and low-frequency speakers on a single cone frame, mostly adopting coaxial designs, some also use bridge structures. According to the internal structure of the car, better sound field positioning is achieved by separating the tweeter and mid-bass and connecting them with a passive crossover. To obtain high power and good dynamic characteristics of full-range sound, it's not necessarily required to use large-sized speakers. Box-type speakers can be used, such as semi-box four-way and passive radiation four-way. Due to the small area inside the car, yet needing a wide frequency range, the speakers are designed in elliptical shapes, such as 6x9 inches, 5x7 inches, 4x6 inches, etc.

Mid-bass speakers: The cone material is mostly polypropylene, which is a high-strength thermoplastic resin made from propylene polymerization. Polypropylene has excellent moisture resistance, oil resistance, solvent resistance, and heat resistance. It is very strong, able to withstand power bursts and large volumes. At the same time, it has high sensitivity and appropriate damping effects, showcasing clear and detailed musical nuances of human voices. Carbon-iron blended polypropylene cone membranes are often used. Voice coils use heat-resistant polyimide, which is an ideal material for the voice coil tube of high-power speakers. Natural rubber edge cones can prevent deformation and fatigue. Commonly used sizes for midrange speakers are 4 inches, 5-inch, 6 inches, and 6.5 inches, available in both coaxial and separate designs.

Tweeters: Mostly use neodymium iron boron half-dome titanium tweeters or neodymium iron boron half-dome silk tweeters. Neodymium iron boron magnets are small in volume but powerful in magnetism, with sound membranes using pure silk soft diaphragms. This type of tweeter produces natural and full-bodied sounds with clear high frequencies. There are also pure carbon dome tweeters, polyester dome tweeters coated with styrene rubber, etc. Tweeters are often installed on both sides of the car dashboard and above the side doors, with specially rotatable brackets allowing flexible selection of reflection directions. Commonly used sizes for tweeters are 1 inch and 2 inches. Installation forms include surface-mounted and embedded.
