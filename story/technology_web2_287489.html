Geely's bottom line for purchasing Volvo is 100% equity, two funds join the bidding war

by 0brq212j1x on 2009-12-01 13:06:19

Geely's bid is for 100% equity of Volvo Car Corporation, which is the bottom line of Li Shufu's negotiation with Volvo. More crucially, Geely will acquire ownership of key technologies and intellectual property rights through Volvo. On November 30, 21st Century Business Herald reported that the acquisition of Volvo has reached a critical stage, with Geely just one step away from crossing the finish line.

Geely’s executives have become increasingly cautious in their statements. On November 27, Yuan Xiaolin, Geely’s spokesperson, responded to media inquiries but only briefly touched on many issues.

However, there was one point he made very clear: Geely's target is 100% equity of Volvo Car Corporation. According to information obtained from relevant channels, this is the bottom line of Li Shufu's negotiations with Volvo. More importantly, Geely will gain ownership of key technologies and intellectual property rights through Volvo. The difference between holding 100% equity and acquiring core technical resources distinguishes Geely's acquisition of Volvo from Tengzhong's acquisition of Hummer.

Clearly, this negotiation equation has only one solution, but there are many unknowns. A detail that reflects Li Shufu's negotiations with various stakeholders is that on November 16, Li Shufu traveled from Beijing to London, then to Belgium and Sweden, where he met with deputy prime ministers responsible for industry and energy in both countries, as well as union leaders and Volvo management.

There are still potential competitors in the final stretch. This reporter recently learned that two funds have joined the battle to acquire Volvo. One comes from Sweden and is composed of Volvo executives and Swedish automobile union leaders, among others. The other is the CROWN fund, formed by former Ford directors.

Is this a deliberate move by the negotiating counterpart to create confusion or does it indicate something more complex? Clearly, focusing solely on the rearview mirror won't provide the answers.

The only certainty is that the Chinese market, with its golden opportunities, is Geely's biggest trump card, sufficient to place Li Shufu in the driver's seat of the acquisition campaign.

Bottom Line and Trump Card

On November 27, Yuan Xiaolin told reporters that both parties were refining and deeply discussing related contracts and terms, and the negotiations were proceeding smoothly.

Li Shufu's trip to Europe this time was to inform the relevant parties of the situation while further understanding the thoughts of government and union representatives regarding some aspects of the merger and acquisition case, such as how employees would be resettled and how pensions would be handled. Belgium and Sweden are the locations of Volvo's two European factories.

In the negotiation process between Geely and Ford, intellectual property issues have always been the focus of external attention.

Yuan Xiaolin clearly stated that first, Geely is Ford's preferred bidder, and the acquisition target is 100% equity of Volvo Car Corporation; second, Volvo will retain ownership of its key technologies and intellectual property rights, as well as all the Ford intellectual property rights necessary for the implementation of its established business plan; furthermore, Geely will acquire ownership of Volvo's key technologies and intellectual property rights through Volvo and will have the right to use a large amount of intellectual property, including Volvo's safety and environmental protection-related intellectual property.

According to information obtained from relevant channels, "acquiring 100% equity of Volvo Car Corporation" is Li Shufu's bottom line in negotiations. This reminder came last year when SAIC exited Korea's Ssangyong—without participation or control, a complete acquisition must be conducted.

Why does Li Shufu have such confidence?

Industry insiders said that compared to foreign bidders, the Chinese market is Geely's biggest trump card.

"For Volvo, Geely's greatest advantage lies in the opportunities in China. In fact, setting up factories and achieving a production capacity of one million units is still just a slogan and not the most practical advantage. Moreover, the funds Geely brings to Volvo are not the most substantial. What Volvo and Ford value is the development opportunity of the entire Chinese market," an informed source said.

He believed that Ford isn't simply looking to offload Volvo but has deeper considerations regarding the "Chinese market." "Currently, it's unclear whether Ford intends to completely sell Volvo or retain part of the equity to form a tripartite alliance body of Ford-Geely-Volvo."

"If it's the latter type of cooperation, actually, Ford has a greater chance," he commented.

But apparently, this mode of cooperation has been ruled out by Geely.

"Now, the general doubt in Detroit is whether Geely has the corresponding talent to digest Volvo. Being able to buy it is one thing, but managing it well is the biggest challenge," Tan Ming'an, president of the Detroit Chamber of Commerce, said in an interview with this reporter.

Additionally, can Geely, which has rashly entered Europe, find a truly effective way to solve deep-rooted problems that even Ford couldn't resolve within the shortest possible time? That's a huge challenge.

Two Funds Joining the Competition

It seems somewhat premature to consider the issue of 'a snake swallowing an elephant.'

There are two funds that don't want Geely to laugh all the way to the bank.

According to informed sources, one acquisition fund comes from Sweden domestically. "Its main personnel consist of Volvo executives, Swedish automobile union leaders, and financial institutions that usually have significant business relations with Volvo. This consortium has a strong Swedish local background."

The source pointed out that one can see that because the union personnel are not from Volvo factories but from the high-level ranks of Sweden's national automobile union, the starting point of this fund is not merely a management buyout but rather stems from the perspective of local national industries and Sweden's automobile industry, carrying a strong tint of 'nationalism.' This fund's advantage lies in its extremely strong lobbying and political power within Sweden.

According to the relevant data I found, this fund is very likely a joint organization of the Volvo Engineers Union and other Swedish national unions. The Volvo Engineers Union consists of 1,800 engineers. However, I was unable to find the detailed name of this fund.

Union members constitute the majority of the working population in Sweden. The largest and strongest unions have millions of members. As a powerful voting bloc, the current Swedish government itself gained power with the support of unions. Moreover, fundamentally, Sweden is a socialist welfare state - the corporate and personal income tax rates across the country exceed 40%, and the government uses these tax revenues to provide citizens with a very robust social safety net.

Under the strong government and powerful unions, the negotiation position of enterprises with these two entities differs greatly from the automotive industry in the United States.

"The other fund is led by a former global director of Ford Motor Company. He is very familiar with Volvo. Therefore, he organized an acquisition fund. This fund has American backing and deep connections within the automotive industry," the source indicated.

Upon investigation, this company is named CROWN, established by former Ford director Michael Dingman. CROWN will formally submit its bid next Monday, rumored to be between $2 billion to $2.5 billion, slightly higher than Geely's current offer.

The transatlantic CROWN Fund may be motivated by industrial considerations, valuing Volvo's brand and product strength. Since the leading figure is a former Ford executive who understands Volvo thoroughly, knowing its internal value well, they decided to make an acquisition attempt.

Regarding the news of these two funds participating in the acquisition, when contacted by the reporter, Li Shufu was straightforward: "We participate in fair competition."

(Source: 21st Century Business Herald, Author: Wang Kang, Xu Feng)
