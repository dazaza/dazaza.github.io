**What is SPF?**

SPF stands for **Sender Policy Framework**. It is a solution designed to prevent others from forging your identity when sending emails. By defining an SPF record for your domain name, the receiving email server will check the SPF record to determine whether the connecting IP address is included in the SPF record. If it is included, the email is considered legitimate; otherwise, it is deemed forged. For more detailed information, please refer to RFC4408 (http://www.ietf.org/rfc/rfc4408.txt).

---

### **How to Add an SPF Record**

It's quite simple—just add a TXT record in your DNS. Follow these steps:

1. Go to http://www.openspf.org/wizard.html and enter your domain name.

2. Click "Begin," and it will automatically generate some relevant information for your domain.

Here’s how you configure the options:

- **a**: Choose "yes" if your domain's A record could send emails (this is usually the case).

- **mx**: Choose "yes" as well because the MX server might handle bounced emails or other related tasks.

- **ptr**: Choose "no," as this is the official recommendation.

- **a**: Do you have any subdomains that send emails? For example, if `bbs.extmail.org` and `www.extmail.org` are on different servers, include `bbs.extmail.org`. Otherwise, leave it blank.

- **mx**: Typically, there won't be additional MX records unless specified.

- **ip4**: Do you have other IPs sending emails? If your SMTP server is separate, include its IP address or subnet here.

- **include**: If you might send emails through an ISP with its own SPF record, include the ISP's domain (e.g., sina.com).

- **~all**: This means that anything not listed above is not authorized. Set this to "yes."

After filling out the form, click "Continue." The system will automatically generate an SPF record for you.

For example, the SPF record for `extmail.org` might look like this:

```

v=spf1 a mx ~all

```

The wizard will also provide instructions on how to add this record to your BIND configuration:

```

extmail.org. IN TXT "v=spf1 a mx ~all"

```

Add this TXT record to your BIND configuration, then reload the DNS using `ndc reload`.

To verify the record, use the following command:

```

dig -t txt extmail.org

```
