The Ghost Awaiting Happiness Without Tears

Blue and I seemed destined to break up, as if arranged by heaven. Blue was actually a very good girl, both in appearance and character, leaving no room for criticism. I know many people laugh at how foolish my actions were. Yet, despite my deep reluctance, I initiated the breakup.

On the first day after our breakup, Blue woke up unusually late. The dormitory aunt entered three times and each time saw her lying still under the covers, curled up on her bed. The aunt said nothing, just sighed and gently closed the door. When Blue finally got up, it was already 11 o'clock, and she missed her morning classes. In the bathroom mirror, a face appeared—tired, with tear stains at the corners of her eyes.

With nothing to do and being alone in the dormitory, Blue went downstairs to the cafeteria to buy lunch for a few sisters. She ordered Tofu with Three Fresh Ingredients, Sweet and Sour Ribs, Tomato Scrambled Eggs, and Celery Fish Fillet—these were all dishes that I usually ate sparingly. Since their class hadn't ended yet, Blue sat there blankly, staring at the dishes without expression. Even when I quietly sat at the table next to her, she didn't notice.

As the cafeteria gradually filled with people, Xiao Mu and others came laughing to Blue's side but stopped when they saw her condition. Xiao Mu immediately said that today's dishes smelled delicious, but Blue remained silent. Everyone exchanged glances and started eating. Occasionally, some trivial conversation was inserted. Across from them, a couple was loudly flirting as if no one else was around, but Blue remained indifferent, chewing on a single grain of rice throughout, never picking up any dish with her chopsticks.

On the second day after our breakup, Blue showed up at a bar in the west district with heavy makeup and seductive clothing.

Sitting at the bar counter, Blue drank one glass after another of an unknown strong liquor. On the bar counter, more than ten empty bottles lined up in a row. Blue wouldn’t let the owner take them away. The dance floor was filled with unrestrained screams. Blue swayed rhythmically to the music. Beside her, a man approached her disrespectfully, saying, "Miss, you're so beautiful." Blue asked, "Am I?" Her eyes gazed ambiguously at the man who could have been her father. Then the man said, "Miss, you're drunk. Would you like me to drive you home?" Blue said, "Sure," and coldly laughed as she grabbed the wine glass and poured it onto the man's face. Extremely angry, the man raised his hand to slap Blue, but was caught by several strong hands almost knocking him down. It turned out Xiao Mu had brought a few boys from the class to find her here.

Xiao Mu said, "Blue, don't be like this. We will all feel heartbroken if you continue like this." Blue replied, "Don't interfere with me. Let me die drunk." No matter what, Blue refused to leave, so the boys dragged her out of the bar and stuffed her into a taxi.

I knew everything that happened in the bar because I was sitting in a dim corner watching Blue the entire time.

When Blue was dragged back to the dormitory, it was exactly one minute before the dormitory doors closed. Thrown onto the bed, she kept shouting for alcohol until she passed out.

By the fourth day after our breakup, Blue had already been lying unconscious in the hospital bed for two days. Originally, after getting drunk that night, she fell into a coma, continuously calling my name in her sleep. During this time, Xiao Mu and others took turns looking after her in the hospital.

On the sixth day after our breakup, I realized I should return to her side. Standing by her bedside, the white bedsheet highlighted her pale face. I silently accompanied her through the whole night, only quietly leaving as she was about to wake up.

On the sixth day after our breakup, Blue was discharged from the hospital.

The first thing Blue did after being discharged was lock herself in the bathroom for hours. Afterward, her roommates went in and exclaimed how clean it was!

The once cheerful Blue became quiet and talkative, always giving off a cold and stern expression—or perhaps cold beauty. Her close friends knew in their hearts that Blue had never forgotten me and continued to miss our past relationship.

Blue began studying hard, every day studying in the library until closing time. Her grades improved rapidly, and the various tasks she handled in the student union were also performed excellently, earning praise from teachers and classmates alike.

In the second year after breaking up with Blue, she was elected as the new president of the department’s student union. I knew how difficult this was, as she was a girl. Knowing the weight of her responsibilities, Blue studied even harder. At the beginning of her third year, she started preparing for postgraduate entrance exams. Naturally, Blue became a well-known figure in the department. The head teacher used Blue and a few other students as examples when educating the freshmen. Before graduation, several renowned companies wanted to sign contracts with Blue.

In the third year after breaking up with Blue, she successfully enrolled in a prestigious university to pursue a master's degree.

There were always many excellent men pursuing Blue, but she paid them no attention. Except for a boy named Ling. Blue treated him like an older brother, never engaging in anything beyond that. To others, their relationship seemed somewhat ambiguous.

In the ninth year after breaking up with Blue, I received news of her marriage. The groom was Ling.

Half a month before the wedding, Blue sat on the newly purchased sofa in the bridal suite, writing invitation cards for family and friends. A happy smile hung on Blue's face, making her look even more charming. I knew many people would receive Blue's wedding invitations, except for me.

Blue opened one golden double-joy red invitation card after another, carefully writing the names of the bride and groom.

When Blue wrote the twentieth invitation, I froze. Under the groom section, my name was clearly written! And it seemed she had no intention of stopping.

Finally, tears streamed down my face. It was the ninth year after our breakup, and it was the first time I cried. But Blue would never see them, nor would anyone else. Because ghosts don't cry.

If time could flow backward, I would never have rushed across the street, resulting in my soul leaving my body. That day happened to be Blue's birthday, and I was holding a cake from Christian Dior.
