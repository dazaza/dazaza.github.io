And by appointment

by ho8ngvuz3ng on 2011-09-24 19:50:43

So far, not only has Wotuan exposed the layoff controversy, Meituan and Kaixin001 have also laid off employees to contract their group-buying businesses. Groupon China has even laid off hundreds of people (for more information, please refer to B Edition). Other group-buying websites have also started to adjust themselves one after another.

Undifferentiated competition and low service quality are the biggest bottlenecks restricting the development of the group-buying industry, according to the tracking statistics from Group Complaint Supervision Zone.

Apparently, group-buying websites are money-burning machines. According to Marbridge Consulting's data, Groupon China spends over 10,000 yuan on advertisements on Google and Baidu every month, which is equivalent to half of its monthly sales alone. This does not include other costs such as rent, labor, and taxes for each site. Since its establishment, Groupon China has been burning through tens of millions of yuan every month.

Limited income and unlimited investment have left various group-buying websites struggling to survive. In the end, perhaps only the survivors will be the kings.

The rise was rapid, but the fall was sudden. The undifferentiated competition among thousands of group-buying websites, lack of attention to market cultivation, generally poor revenue, enthusiasm for expansion, reliance on IPO financing, and cooling down in the capital market may all lead to the rapid shrinkage of group-buying websites after their crazy growth.

■ Capital Theory

From public reports and self-promotion by various websites, most of the expenses of group-buying websites are spent on advertising and marketing, with very few websites investing heavily in improving their own service quality. As a result, they gain recognition but lack good reputation, and companies generally lack momentum for development.

The bubble stirred up by the e-commerce whirlwind comes quickly, but it bursts just as fast. A few months ago, fiber optic access prices were high, and group-buying websites were poaching talent from each other due to rapid development. Now, the fierce battle for talent has turned into internal layoffs.

According to data from the China E-Commerce Research Center, in recent years, group-buying websites have developed rapidly, growing from a few initial ones like Meituan and Manzuo to over five thousand today. The unknowns and huge potential opportunities of this emerging industry have driven all players to invest heavily in market promotion. Last year, Dianping invested 100 million yuan, while Nuomi invested hundreds of millions of yuan.

Perhaps in the later stages of reshuffling, the remaining large group-buying websites will still face a decisive battle. Where there is smoke, there is war; where there is no smoke, there is politics and commerce. The final outcome of the group-buying industry may be that one general succeeds at the cost of countless others' lives. Starting yesterday, the tenth Internet Conference opened in Beijing (for more information, please refer to B Edition). Industry leaders should carefully consider how to move group-buying from the virtual world into reality, transitioning from a bubble to sustainable industrial development.

Groupon China launched its website in January this year, as a joint venture between Groupon, the pioneer and largest group-buying website in the United States, and Tencent. Despite being the Chinese version of Groupon, its performance has been so poor, indicating the worrying state of the industry.

Even Groupon China, which claimed it would become the leader in China's group-buying industry, has struggled since its launch amidst doubts. Just from some recent media reports, it can be seen that after its launch, its first product order was sued for breach of contract and ordered to pay compensation of ten thousand yuan; an organized lottery event was found to have cheated, with the prize iPhone given to internal employees, resulting in a responsible person being fired; and its after-sales service has been frequently complained about.

The true essence of e-commerce is still in service, and the true essence of competition among group-buying websites lies in internal strength. Without honing one's own service quality and exploring and grasping the rules of the industry, even with massive funding, it cannot withstand the current level of money burning. Ultimately, the investment community's enthusiasm for group-buying enterprises will gradually cool down.

Poor revenue and inability to break even are clearly the primary reasons for layoffs in the group-buying industry.

As for profits, although group-buying websites often claim to be the best in the industry, similar to the approach of iPhone GS, none have yet made a profit and rely solely on venture capital. Even according to the data disclosed by Meituan itself, Meituan achieved the highest gross margin in the entire industry of less than 5% (the gross margin of group-buying websites refers to the share of income obtained by the website from a transaction without counting management and marketing costs), calculated based on its annual sales target of 1 billion yuan, its gross profit is only over 100 million yuan.

Taking Groupon China as an example, in terms of investment, Groupon's prospectus shows that by July 1 of this year, its investment in Groupon China was only 10 million US dollars. This is much lower than the previously committed total investment of 200 million US dollars by Groupon for Groupon China. And according to the agreement, the total investment by Tencent and Yunfeng Fund and other Chinese investors should also be 200 million US dollars.

Author: Chu Yu, financial commentator

The reshuffle in the group-buying industry has begun, and the result may be that only the survivors will be the kings.

Clearly, layoffs are no longer isolated cases within the industry, but are becoming common phenomena, with industry problems beginning to erupt collectively.

Li Kaifu, Chairman and CEO of Innovation Works, previously stated that scale operation might be the real way out for domestic group-buying websites. More than 90% of domestic group-buying websites will fail, and only four or five websites that have reached operating scale will eventually dominate the market.
