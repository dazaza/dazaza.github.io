Need for Speed 11 Trial Experience - RV Rally?

by netbeta on 2007-11-04 13:08:31

With great anticipation, I downloaded the demo of NFS11. However, after trying it out, I was somewhat disappointed.

If we talk about the game's effects alone, they are indeed quite good. But without the support of dynamic blur, it seems a bit monotonous. In the full version of the game, there will still be a career mode, but I'm not quite sure what kind of career it will be. Is it just one rally race after another? That seems a bit boring. I hope there won't be too many races as strict as the first track. It would be fine if they were more like the mountain road races in NFS10. There are still more than ten days before the official release. I hope NFS11 doesn't let us down and that the NFS brand doesn't fall. We are still looking forward to it.

I only knew about NFS from the 6th edition, so I can't be considered an old player. But since the 7th edition, I have really liked this game, and it has become one of the more important events every year. Until NFS10, when support for the antique GF4 finally ended, it made me gradually move away from NFS, but I still kept an eye on it. During the holiday break in early November, I finally got a new computer, and the first thing I downloaded was NFS10. Everyone says NFS10 isn't as fun as NFS9, and I indeed think so too. NFS9 is truly classic, I remember it even won some award for best annual visual effects game or something, not just the visuals, but all aspects were good. NFS10 has some connection with NFS9, such as the story framework and related plots in the career mode. NFS10 also added some new elements, like the mountain road races, which were probably somewhat influenced by Initial D. Actually, the idea of mountain road races is not bad. In terms of game design philosophy, it could be said to belong to the same category as the previous drag racing. After playing too much drag racing, you naturally want to try something different.

Now let's talk about NFS11, developed by Black Box studio, which is said to be the earliest development team for the NFS series. They should understand the meaning contained within NFS games better. However, NFS11 is too different from its predecessor, making me almost unable to believe it's a sequel to the same game. From the UI aspect of NFS11, it's filled with street elements, but the content of the game is relatively formal rally races. I think the scenes from previous works where illegal street racing was marked with glow sticks in city corners fit the street style better and conform more to the image of NFS in the hearts of fans.

The demo includes two tracks. The first one almost made me feel like it was Colin McRae Rally, except for the audience by the roadside, there was really no difference. The feeling of running is also strange, with many adjustments made to the control, requiring some time to adapt. More frustratingly, there is no dynamic blur effect when driving fast, and if you hit a utility pole, your car will be destroyed or flipped into a dirt ditch by the roadside. Before realizing what happened, checking the rear view, your car has already been smashed to pieces. I really doubt, is this still "Need for Speed"? Is it for practicing our driving skills, highlighting the strictness of real competitions, or do you want to experience the thrill of crashes? Anyway, even if you want some thrilling crashes, it won't be thrilling because you'll see a close-up of your car being scrapped and then be asked if you want to restart or quit the race.

As for the other track, at least it's not in the middle of nowhere, but in the city, yet it still feels unfamiliar. Cars won't be destroyed by hitting utility poles here, finally regaining a little sense of madness.

If we talk about the game's effects alone, they are indeed quite good. But without the support of dynamic blur, it seems a bit monotonous. In the full version of the game, there will still be a career mode, but I'm not quite sure what kind of career it will be. Is it just participating in these rally races one after another? That seems a bit boring. I hope there won't be too many races as strict as the first track. It would be fine if they were more like the mountain road races in NFS10. There are still more than ten days before the official release. I hope NFS11 doesn't let us down and that the NFS brand doesn't fall. We are still looking forward to it.
