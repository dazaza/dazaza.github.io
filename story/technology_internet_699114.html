Maintenance and Care of Industrial Washing Machinery

With the development of domestic washing equipment [http://www.sh-lijing.com.cn/], the consumer market's demand for washing machinery is increasing. Therefore, daily maintenance of washing equipment has become increasingly important. If operators take good care of the daily maintenance of washing machinery, it can not only extend the life of the washing equipment but also make the operation simpler and more flexible. The content of equipment maintenance usually includes daily maintenance, regular maintenance, periodic inspections, and precision checks. Maintenance of the equipment's lubrication and cooling systems is also an important part of equipment maintenance.

Daily maintenance of washing machinery is the foundation of equipment maintenance and must be institutionalized and standardized. Quotas for work and material consumption should be set for regular equipment maintenance. So how do we carry out the daily maintenance of washing equipment? Below, we introduce common daily maintenance characteristics of different types of washing machinery.

A: Four Aspects of Washing Equipment Maintenance

First, Cleanliness: Clean both inside and outside of the washing equipment, including sliding surfaces, screws, racks, gearboxes, oil holes, etc., ensuring no oil stains on industrial washing equipment, no oil or air leaks, and timely cleaning of chips, debris, and dirt around the machinery.

Second, Lubrication: Ensure proper lubrication, add or change oil on time, avoid dry friction, maintain normal oil pressure, keep oil markers clear, ensure smooth oil paths, use oil that meets machinery requirements, and keep oil guns, cups, and felts clean.

Third, Orderliness: Keep tools, accessories, workpieces (products) organized, pipelines, and lines orderly.

Fourth, Safety: Follow safety operation procedures, do not overload the equipment, and promptly eliminate unsafe factors.

B: Daily Operational Maintenance of Industrial Washing Machinery

First: Before each startup, open all main valves in the pipeline system, including water, steam, and air valves, check whether water pressure, steam, and air pressure are normal, and ensure there is oil in the three-in-one unit's oil cup to maintain gas flow.

Second: Install filters before all steam and water inlet ends, and install a drain valve at the steam outlet end. Regularly clean the air intake of the dryer heater, and clean the air intake before each drying cycle.

Third: Every six months, open the air ducts and thoroughly clean fibers attached to cold and hot coils, fan blades, and airflow channels to improve drying efficiency and reduce contamination-related machine corrosion.

C: Regional Maintenance of Industrial Washing Equipment [http://www.sh-lijing.com.cn/gsjj.html]

Regional maintenance comprehensively handles the maintenance and emergency repair work of production area equipment. Its tasks include:

a) Responsible for the maintenance and repair of equipment within this region, ensuring completion of indicators such as equipment integrity rate and fault downtime rate;

b) Strictly implement regular point inspections and regional patrol inspections, guiding and supervising operators in daily maintenance and scheduled maintenance;

c) Participate in equipment condition censuses, precision checks, adjustments, leak prevention, fault analysis, and status monitoring under the guidance of workshop mechanical personnel.

The advantage of regional equipment maintenance organization is that it minimizes equipment repair downtime. The regional maintenance team compiles plans for regular inspections and precision checks, specifying routine inspection times for each shift. To avoid affecting production, planned equipment inspections are scheduled during non-working days of the factory, and routine inspections during each shift are conducted during workers' lunch breaks.

D: Measures to Improve Equipment Maintenance Levels

To enhance equipment maintenance levels, maintenance work should basically achieve three standards: standardization, proceduralization, and institutionalization. Equipment maintenance work should be combined with enterprise production economic contracting responsibility systems for evaluation. Enterprises should also mobilize the masses to conduct specialized equipment maintenance work, including self-inspection and mutual inspection, and large-scale equipment inspections.

Standardization: Unify maintenance content, specify which parts need cleaning, which mechanical components need adjustment, and which devices need inspection based on the specific conditions of each enterprise according to objective laws.

Proceduralization: Develop various maintenance process regulations based on different washing machinery and follow these regulations for maintenance.

Institutionalization: Specify maintenance cycles and times based on different equipment and working conditions, and strictly enforce them.

For regular maintenance work, establish labor hour quotas and material consumption quotas and evaluate performance based on these quotas. Equipment maintenance regulations outline the requirements and specifications for daily equipment maintenance. Adhering to these regulations can extend equipment lifespan and ensure a safe, comfortable working environment.
