In the misty rain, that wisp of longing

These days, the rain and fog make everything hazy, and the temperature has dropped suddenly. It's a sign of the early spring when it's still cold after a brief warmth. Outside our dormitory building at school, there are many trees and two rows of water fir on the shaded path that I often mention. So with the rain and fog swirling around, it reminded me of the four words "smoke shrouds the cold water", a very beautiful imagery. I quite like this rare natural landscape.

Occasionally, I go through my old high school photos online, and it brings about many feelings. I finally have a strong sense of self-reproach. I remember the words my dad said to me during one of my rare visits home: "Why are you so unambitious?"

My dad has never scolded me, especially regarding my studies. My failure in the high school entrance exam and college entrance exam has caused him some hidden pain. And this belated reproach has made me wait for nearly half a year. In fact, I've long hoped that Dad would reprimand me like this. Dad doesn't speak as fast as I do; he is steady, even when he criticizes me, he doesn't raise his voice.

There is a shadow of disappointment in his eyes, always like that. It seems as if the circumstances of his youth resurface again on a dimly lit night when I return home. He often mentions to me the situations of his youth, always with the same tone, the same look, the same disappointment... It keeps repeating itself tirelessly, yet always filled with helplessness. That night, I didn't get annoyed by talking with him. I felt his helplessness clearly. Through the dark green window glass, it was pitch black outside, with an intense desolation and solemnity.

On rainy days, habitual thoughts always drift towards the same melancholic direction, just like when a pebble plops into the middle of a small pond, the ripples slowly spread out, becoming wider and deeper. I recall the winter of 2004 just after a snowfall. When Dad set off alone on a distant journey, that heavy bag was always a burden on his shoulders. Mom didn't see him off; I took him to the Jiangdong bus station. Dad turned around, still with that look, that tone, that disappointment. He told me to go back, that he could walk himself, then repeatedly instructed me to listen to Mom and study hard. He didn't say much else and then turned away and left. The winter drizzled with light rain, watching him gradually disappear into the sea of people and clouds, I really didn't want to let him go, and he must have felt the same way. Thoughts bring me back to the deep autumn of 2008, when Dad sent me to university. Coincidentally, that day also had light rain. To catch the evening train to Yiwu, he quickly arranged my luggage and had to leave in a hurry. Dad didn't say much, telling me to take good care of myself at school, and that both Mom and Dad will miss me. The school bus was about to leave, and in the driver's urging voice, he hurriedly got on the bus. Still that look, and that helplessness mixed with earnest expectations.

It's very cold, and my hands are icy while typing. Spring is the season when all things revive and regenerate, a healthy season. I hope Dad and Mom stay healthy at home. At this moment, I can vaguely recall Dad's retreating figure. Suddenly, I feel that the people I regret most in this world are my parents. It can be said that in the year away from home, I didn't gain much academically, but I deeply realized the unshakable position of my parents in my heart.

In the misty rain, I carry this wisp of longing. I cannot forget my father's silhouette, his tone, his eyes, his helplessness...
