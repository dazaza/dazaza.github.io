How far is 3G from us?

by rodari on 2008-11-28 18:11:59

The management made an explicit statement.

"So far, the progress of the restructuring has been very smooth. The most difficult part of the restructuring and merger between Unicom and Netcom is basically ready," said Guohua Xi.

In its previous announcement, MIIT clearly stated that the restructuring would be combined with the issuance of 3G licenses, and the 3G licenses would be issued after the completion of the restructuring.

Regarding TD-SCDMA, Guohua Xi believed it was a symbolic achievement that needed to be strongly promoted. Efforts should be made in the trial commercial use of TD, and the collaboration and coordination within the industrial chain should be strengthened. This corresponds with the ten development-promoting measures issued by MIIT last week. MIIT has clearly proposed to accelerate the R&D and industrialization of enhanced technologies for TD-SCDMA, as well as the standardization and research work for the subsequent evolution of TD-SCDMA technology.

Boosting economic growth?

For the increasingly heated discussion on the upcoming issuance of 3G licenses, a professor from Beijing University of Posts and Telecommunications believed that this was mainly stimulated by the country's measures to expand domestic demand. Lijiang Xiang, a senior insider in the communications industry, also believed that developing 3G was one of the important means to expand domestic demand. He indicated that after the issuance of 3G licenses, the three operators - China Mobile, China Telecom, and China Unicom - would start the network construction for 3G. The nationwide completion of 3G network construction would not cost less than 800 billion yuan, with an initial investment of nearly 200 billion yuan. This would have a significant role in boosting overall economic growth.

An analyst from Sandian Consulting believed that China's development of 3G would prompt users to replace their mobile phones, thereby increasing the annual output value by at least 500 billion yuan. An expert from the Telecommunication Research Institute of MIIT introduced that apart from the initial hard investment of 200 billion yuan for network and equipment updates and expansions, there would be development opportunities for enterprises such as transmission equipment, power supply, production cases, and power components. The issuance of 3G licenses could at least drive trillions of yuan worth of investments in the telecommunications industry.
