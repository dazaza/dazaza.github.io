Li Yuanwei, who retired from the Basketball Association, once publicly stated that China currently does not have a professional league. This is a candid statement. Indeed, China currently does not have a truly professional league. The CSL is not one, nor is the CBA. If we must define these leagues, then they can be called "sports leagues with professional characteristics under the national system."

The lack of professionalism and outbound tourism are the main causes of many problems in the league.

In terms of league nature, the CBA league and the CSL league, as well as other sports leagues in China, have never gone far on the path of professionalization. The national system of the CBA league was not brought by Xin Lan Cheng. The CBA league has always been a "hybrid," running under the dual tracks of the national system and marketization. The difference is merely the specific policy orientation, whether it is bold or conservative. The controversy caused by the "Xin Lan Cheng New Policy" has, to some extent, magnified this difference. In fact, whether emphasizing the national system or professionalization, the current CBA will not undergo substantial changes and will still be a "sports league with professional characteristics under the national system."

Under the large framework of the national system, the Basketball Association requires the CBA league to improve the level of Chinese basketball and provide as much talent as possible for the national team to ensure its achievements in international competitions.

Different nature, objectives, and national conditions determine the different development paths of Chinese basketball. The reserve talents of American basketball can be continuously produced through school sports, while the cultivation of reserve talents in Chinese basketball is in a dilemma. On one side, the development of school sports is imperfect, and on the other side, the professional training system is not thriving. Handing over the entire talent cultivation process to the clubs is clearly unreasonable, which has already put a lot of pressure on investors. Therefore, superficially, the CBA league has a weak foundation, but in reality, this is a problem caused by the new system being insufficiently reasonable and complete during the development of Chinese basketball from the professional system to the current stage.

In other words, Chinese basketball has reached a predicament today. The advancement of league professionalization lacks overall environmental support and lacks a solid foundation, leading to subsequent weakness. Therefore, regarding basketball, promoting professionalization cannot avoid the larger environment. The fundamental solution is to eliminate the difficulties of development obstacles by adjusting the system of Chinese basketball. A compromise model is to combine the organizational, resource, and training advantages of the national system with school sports, enabling high-level basketball reserve talents to emerge from schools, and transforming the CBA league into a purely market-oriented league without having to bear the responsibility of youth training. Talents needed can be selected from schools according to corresponding rules, and national team talents can be screened from clubs and schools. Of course, local protectionism obstacles need to be eliminated in this process.

Mapping out a scientific and reasonable Chinese basketball system is a task that must be accomplished sooner or later. In such a system, the needs and interests of fans, clubs, and the country can be jointly guaranteed. If the overall structure is unreasonable, neither professionalization nor the national system can smoothly achieve long-term development.

This kind of system integration and change naturally faces many difficulties, but perhaps this is the direction of the entire social development trend. Otherwise, we will only see more and more young talents ultimately choosing education over basketball or flocking to American high schools and college leagues, while our own lack of professional leagues will inevitably develop slowly or even stagnate.

Since the Basketball Association believes that the league needs to make changes, why not take on a more challenging mission?
