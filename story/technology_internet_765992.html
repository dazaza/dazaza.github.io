User's voice: iPhone 5S wants a bigger screen and longer battery life

by anonymous on 2013-09-04 13:54:15

As the release date for Apple's next generation iPhone approaches, CNET recently launched a poll asking netizens what their ideal iPhone should look like.

CNET surveyed 10,000 netizens on various topics including preferred body materials, size, battery life, iOS 7 features, the addition of a 128GB version, and functionalities that will never appear... Perhaps this is what your dream iPhone 5S looks like.

Bigger or Stay the Same?

The first survey item was about screen size to see if people wanted the iPhone to get bigger. The top choice was a 4.7-inch screen, slightly larger than the current 4-inch iPhone 5, similar to the Moto X, Lumia 925, and HTC One.

According to the overall voting results, 79% of voters hope that the iPhone 5S will have a larger display, suggesting that many find the existing 4-inch iPhone 5 too small. However, 21% of users still want the iPhone 5S to retain the current 4-inch screen. Based on leaked information so far, neither the iPhone 5S nor the iPhone 5C seems likely to change in display size. Shawn Lee, head of NPD Display research lab, said he expects Apple to adopt almost identical choices for the next generation iPhone as the iPhone 5, including size, resolution, and in-cell touch technology. Shawn Lee also believes that Apple might even use the same LED backlighting technology as the iPhone 5 due to power endurance requirements.

Favorite Body Material

Among all 10,000 votes, metal material received the most support with 39%, followed by glass at 36%. As for plastic casing, it only garnered favor from 6% of users.

Previously, plastic material was used in the iPhone 3G and 3GS. The reason plastic is again being applied to the cheaper version of the iPhone 5C includes mass production, mold design, contour, and ease of manufacturing in multiple color versions. Taking the iPhone 5 as an example, since wireless signals need to pass through the metal casing, a special area must be created. However, the plastic-cased iPhone 5C does not have this problem.

According to Morgan Stanley estimates, adopting a plastic casing for the iPhone 5C reduces the cost of the body material by half, dropping from $33 for metal to $16 for plastic. Therefore, as the cheaper version of the iPhone 5C, a plastic casing is the best choice.

What Features Do Users Want Most in the iPhone 5S?

For users, the biggest feature they hope for in the iPhone 5S is better battery life, which may come as a surprise. However, upon reflection, longer standby time, though not a new feature, is absolutely crucial for users. Although smartphone battery life has started to improve gradually, it still fails to meet the needs of the majority of users who require longer battery life. Larger screens and higher configurations are undoubtedly the biggest factors consuming power compared to traditional functional phones.

The previous iPhone 5 had a battery capacity of only 1440mAh, resulting in 8 hours of 3G call time, 8 hours of 3G/LTE connectivity, 10 hours of WiFi connection, 40 hours of audio playback, 10 hours of video playback, and 225 hours of standby time.

Remember when the iPhone 4S had a video playback time of 9 hours and call time reaching 7.37 to 8.48 hours? So, the biggest question now is how Apple can extend the battery life of the iPhone 5S. The most direct method would be to equip it with a larger capacity battery, similar to the improvement made in the iPhone 5. Remember when the iPhone 4 had a battery capacity of 1420mAh, and the later iPhone 4S increased slightly to 1432mAh? If there isn't much change in the body size of the iPhone 5S, based on experience, the increase in battery capacity won't be significantly improved either.

In the Android camp, Motorola’s approach to improving battery life is worth learning from. First, Motorola directly increases battery capacity to enhance battery life, such as the latest Droid Maxx, which comes with a non-removable battery with a capacity of 3500mAh, promising 48 hours of usage, albeit with some compromise in body size; while another Motorola product, the Moto X, uses the X8 mobile processing system, focusing more on hardware design, assigning different tasks like daily applications, voice search, motion gestures to different chips to handle, thereby achieving the goal of improving battery life. This point is something many users hope the iPhone 5S could learn from.

Best iOS 7 Feature

In the vote regarding the iOS 7 system, the results were somewhat unexpected. Most users found the addition of the control center in iOS 7 far more appealing than the completely flat interface design style.

In iOS 7, users can activate the control center by swiping up from the bottom of the screen. The functions include screen brightness, volume, Wi-Fi switch, and shortcuts to some basic applications. Although Google's Android system introduced similar functionality in Android 4.2 last November, and Android systems have supported quick switches for Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, screen brightness, and GPS since 2010, Apple’s decision to learn from its competitors is better late than never.

Additionally, in the survey, users seem uninterested in iOS 7's dynamic wallpapers. They believe that Apple hasn't fully demonstrated the charm of multi-layered dynamic wallpapers in iOS 7 yet. And remember, Android systems have had dynamic wallpaper functionality since 2010.

Most Desired Functionality That Won’t Appear on the iPhone 5S

Clearly, the most popular feature among users is NFC. There were rumors that the iPhone 5S would support NFC functionality, and during this period, Apple continuously submitted patents related to NFC. Moreover, many Android devices already support NFC near-field transmission, which can be used for secure wireless data transfer and payment functions. However, at least currently, NFC will definitely not appear on the iPhone 5S.

Apple has always seemed unenthusiastic about NFC functionality. Last year, Phil Schiller, Apple's senior vice president, told AllThingsD that Apple's Passbook application already covers most areas where NFC is used, and through iOS's AirDrop function, NFC modules can be entirely replaced. AirDrop allows iOS users to seamlessly transfer files via Wi-Fi, even between iOS and Mac devices.

An analyst from Gartner expressed in June that the application level of NFC was disappointing last year, but it will improve by 2016, with 6% of payments expected to be conducted via NFC technology. Gartner also predicts that future NFC payments will account for 40% of electronic payments, which is quite optimistic. Services like Google Wallet and Isis will undoubtedly play important roles in the future. It seems that Apple currently believes that NFC technology is not mature enough to be included in the iPhone 5S.

Besides NFC, Dolby sound effects, removable batteries, third-party input methods, SD card expansion, waterproof features, and wireless charging are all options that users highly anticipate but will definitely not appear on the iPhone 5S.

Storage Capacity

How much extra money does Apple charge for doubling the storage space of an iPhone? In reality, it's much more than the actual hardware cost. Previously, increasing iPhone storage from 16GB to 32GB, and then from 32GB to 64GB, each step required an additional payment of $100 (approximately RMB 600). However, according to research by HIS iSuppli, from a hardware cost perspective, increasing iPhone storage only raises costs by $10 to $20 (approximately RMB 60 to RMB 120). This means that increasing from 16GB to 64GB requires consumers to pay an additional $200 (approximately RMB 1200), while Apple only increases costs by $30 (approximately RMB 180).

NAND flash technology is rapidly developing, and the new 3D NAND vertical flash technology will offer better performance, more stable reliability, longer lifespan, and lower costs. More importantly for smartphones, it occupies less space under the same conditions, allowing for easier expansion of internal storage space and reduced component space occupation.

This day doesn't seem too far away. Samsung, Apple's biggest competitor and also its main component supplier, has announced the development of 3D vertical flash technology, capable of integrating 128GB of flash memory into a single chip. Another memory manufacturer, Micron, also stated that it will launch memory products using 3D technology in the first quarter of 2014.

However, Hans Mosesmann, General Manager of Raymond James, said that although everything looks beautiful right now, the price of flash products using 3D technology won't be cheap in the short term. The biggest issue with 3D NAND flash is production capacity and price. Although Samsung has started mass-producing 3D flash products, full-scale popularization will still take a few years.

Summary

No matter when, the iPhone is always one of the most anticipated phones. The rumored real iPhone 5S will debut alongside the iPhone 5C on September 10. Whether Apple's latest iPhone 5S will satisfy everyone will only be revealed after its official release. What do you think the iPhone 5S should look like?

The iPhone event will be held on September 10th.
