Everyone wants to stay young forever, but no medicine can achieve this. However, there are foods that make you healthier the more you eat them. Recently, nutritionists have recommended the following 7 foods that can make us younger as we eat them.

1. Dairy products

Low-fat yogurt is the best choice. It is rich in calcium, various vitamins, protein and potassium.

In addition, the probiotics in yogurt help maintain a balanced bacterial flora in the body. If you don't like yogurt, skimmed milk and cheese are also good choices. Dairy products contain almost all the nutrients the human body needs, with a balanced proportion of these nutrients.

2. Eggs

Studies show that eating an egg every morning does not increase cholesterol levels but instead makes people consume fewer calories throughout the day, helping to lose weight without realizing it. Eggs provide high-quality protein and also contain 12 types of vitamins and minerals, among which B vitamins help improve memory.

3. Nuts

High in fiber, protein, heart-healthy and anti-aging, these are the advantages of nuts. However, due to their high fat content, moderation is key. Experts believe that whether it's almonds, peanuts, walnuts or hazelnuts, consuming no more than half an ounce per day is optimal.

4. Kiwi fruit

Known as the "king of fruits," kiwi fruit contains a dense concentration of nutrients. One kiwi fruit can meet your daily vitamin C requirement, and it also provides abundant vitamin A, dietary fiber, and potassium. Kiwi fruit is also convenient to eat; simply cut it in half with a knife and scoop it out with a spoon like ice cream for full enjoyment.

5. Legumes

Eating more legumes is beneficial for the heart. The insoluble fiber they contain can effectively lower cholesterol, while other soluble fibers help remove waste from the body. Additionally, legumes contain protein, carbohydrates, magnesium, and potassium. Experts suggest consuming legume-based foods at least three times a week.

6. Broccoli

This vegetable is both delicious and common. It contains vitamins A, C, and K, which is beneficial for bone growth. Even beta-carotene, usually found only in yellow plants like carrots and oranges, is present in large quantities in broccoli.

7. Dried fruits

Dried fruits such as pineapple chips and dried apricots retain most of the vitamins and beneficial elements found in fresh fruits during the dehydration process. In addition to abundant vitamins, dried fruits also contain large amounts of iron and potassium. Moreover, the sugar content in dried fruits is lower than that in regular fruits, making them less likely to cause weight gain and serving as a very good snack.
