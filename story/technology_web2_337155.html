The car owner blocked the entrance of the community with his Porsche to protest against the difficulty of parking.

Nevertheless, the security guard expressed understanding: "There are more than 700 vehicles in the community, but only about 500 parking spaces. The parking space tension has been going on for a long time. Owners have no place to park, indeed feel upset, we all understand, but given the current situation of the community, we are really helpless."

When he drove around to the north gate, he had to ask the security guard where there were still parking spaces. The security guard told him that the parking spaces were already full. He could only drive around again to see if any vehicle would happen to leave. But the man still found no parking space.

Parking difficulties in high-end communities

No place to park? I'll park at the entrance.

Because of the limited space at the south gate, the Porsche parked here blocked most of the exit. Although there are not many vehicles entering and leaving at night, it still hinders traffic. There was nothing the security guard could do but call the car owner and hope that he would come to drive away his car.

"He didn't argue with us, Evisu Jeans, but used this special way of placing cards to vent his emotions, while hoping to reflect the difficulty of parking to higher authorities, drawing the attention of relevant departments, and alleviating the problem of parking difficulties in the community," said the security guard.

Just want to reflect some issues

This time, although he came out, he didn't plan to drive away. He took a card (with the three words "parking difficulty" written on it) and placed it on the Porsche.

The car owner also didn't argue with the security guard

"The car owner is pretty tough, but we can understand his feelings," said a resident of the community. Ziguigarden is a high-end community, some families have two or three cars, the problem of parking difficulties in the community is very prominent. He encountered it before, it's really frustrating when there's nowhere to park.

"The car owner didn't buy a parking space, but paid for road-side parking, about one hundred yuan per month," said the security guard. Finally, because the man refused to drive away for a long time, the community security had to report to the police that evening, but the man still refused to move the car until 8:00 AM yesterday morning when he finally drove away.

At around 10:30, the man drove the Porsche to the south gate of the community, didn't say much, stopped the car, locked the door, and walked away.

This incident happened yesterday at the entrance of Ziguigarden Community on Wenerxi Road. The car owner said, Diesel Jeans, that it was really because he had nowhere to park, so he used this method to vent his emotions, and also hoped to draw the attention of relevant departments, hoping to solve the dilemma of parking difficulties in the community.

After the first call, the man didn't come out, until the second contact, the man came out of the house and returned to the south gate of the community.

"Parking is not allowed here." The security guard at the south gate saw the situation was wrong and quickly shouted. No matter how the security guard tried to dissuade him, the man just wouldn't listen and turned around and went straight home.

We learned that other residents in the community often encounter such situations with the Porsche owner. They generally park their cars on the surrounding roads or nearby communities, but parking directly at the entrance of the community is the first time the residents have seen it.

The problem hasn't been going on for just a day or two

Parking the Porsche at the entrance of the community for more than 9 hours, isn't that enough dominance? But actually, the car owner claimed to be quite aggrieved, and it was out of necessity. He even wrote the three words "parking difficulty" on a card and placed it on the car.

The black Porsche owner is a man in his thirties who lives in Building 5 of Ziguigarden. Around 10 PM the previous evening, after driving around the community in his Porsche, he discovered that the community parking spaces were already fully occupied again.

"I paid money, why is there nowhere to park?" Yesterday, when the reporter came downstairs to the Porsche owner's home, the owner still refused to open the door for an interview, but through the intercom of the doorbell, it was still evident that the owner's tone still carried unrelenting resentment.
