The winter and spring months offer some of the best fishing for Striped Marlin, Mahimahi (Dolphin fish or Dorado), and Ono (Wahoo). While these species can be caught year-round, striped marlin show up in good numbers during the fall and winter months, and the full moon in May is known as the peak time for Ono fishing in Hawaii. Mahimahi and Ono are delicious tasting fish and are served on most regional restaurant menus in Hawaii.

Deep sea fishing on the Big Island of Hawaii in Kona is enhanced by a network of buoys, also known as Fish Aggregation Devices or FADs, that concentrate schools of fish around these buoys. The network of fish buoys just off the island of the Big Island of Hawaii near Kona are within easy reach of the Kona charter boat fleet and provide good fishing for blue marlin and other pelagic fish that pass through the area to feed on the bait fish that congregate near these buoys. Slow trolling a live skipjack (Aku) tuna or trolling lures in the areas around these fish aggregating devices can be very fruitful for anglers targeting Blue Marlin, Mahimahi, and Yellowfin Tuna on Hawaii fishing tours.

Each year, large schools of Yellowfin Tuna (Ahi) migrate just off the shores of Kona, Hawaii, making Kona one of the top destinations for anglers coming to the Big Island of Hawaii to target this lively fighting fish. Pound for pound, Yellowfin Tuna put up the best fight of almost any fish in the sea. These brutes are known to make explosive strikes on lures and mix up the fight with a series of blazing runs and deep dives. These hard-fighting fish are one of the biggest prizes for anglers who come to Hawaii each year to fish. Tuna fishing in Kona, Hawaii is good year-round but peaks during the summer months.

Marlin Fishing in Hawaii

Hawaii is one of the premier destinations in the world for anglers seeking to catch mammoth blue marlin. The town of Kona on the Big Island of Hawaii is ground zero for big game anglers from around the world who come to Hawaii to work fishing each year in search of the elusive “Grander” (marlin over 1,000 pounds). The fact is that the waters just off the Kona coast provide the opportunity to catch a blue marlin any day of the year. Kona, Hawaii is also a preferred fishing destination for novice anglers, and the fleet of Kona Charter Boats is well-equipped, with the fishing being very close to shore in the calm, protected waters just off the Big Island of Hawaii.

Hawaii Fishing Charters / Kona Charters

The deep blue waters off the Kona coast on the Big Island of Hawaii make this location a singular and ideal destination to catch Billfish species like Blue Marlin (Au), Striped Marlin, and the occasional Pacific Sailfish and Black Marlin. The calm, deep, blue waters off of Kona, Hawaii offer great fishing for other pelagic species such as Wahoo (ono), Yellowfin Tuna (Ahi), Dolphin Fish (Mahi-Mahi), and Skipjack Tuna (Aku). The waters just off the coast of Kona, Hawaii quickly drop off to over 1,000 fathoms within a few miles of the beach. The deep underwater currents push up against the shoreline shelf and concentrate massive schools of baitfish within easy reach of the Kona Charter Boat fleet. This means the trip is close to the coast so anglers on Hawaii fishing charters spend more time fishing and less time traveling out and back to the fishing grounds. The common methods of fishing in Hawaii are slow trolling live skipjack tuna or trolling large synthetic lures, such as the “Straight Runner” charted by the legendary Henry Chee. These Hawaiian Style fishing lures are designed to replicate small tunas or baitfish.
