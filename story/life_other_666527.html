Fine Snow Reflects the Sorrow of Parting

Silently walking, no longer trying to organize those memories, even if they mess up my mind, I don't care. The soft laughter spreads across the city, and the crying sounds at the street corner cannot be heard. I am reluctant to record the decaying scenery here. Holding your hand, I lower my head and count the footprints we've walked. In this bustling city, no one knows that we are the protagonists about to part ways. Some of them rush by, while others stroll leisurely. Laughter and whispers wrap the cold winter night in a warm atmosphere. But the person beside me keeps her head down, wiping away tears with both hands, and when I'm not paying attention, she looks up at the beautiful night sky.

My worries add another layer, for whom am I becoming so haggard? I see her off, not knowing where I should let go of this hand. She says she will come back, but we both know clearly in our hearts. There's no need for too much explanation, I can't find any reason to stay.

We walk silently by the bridge, very clear in our hearts that this is the most beautiful memory. I remember writing "Plain Girl" for her, and this is where we first met. But now, none of us know the fate's sign. We just don't know which way to go, like a lost child who goes wherever there is a path. My heart is full of melancholy, but the warmth in my palm remains. My body trembles slightly because I suddenly remember how to walk back alone. The traces in the snow, two rows of footprints...

As the moment of separation approaches, I still manage to hold back my tears. I want to bid you farewell with a smile, letting you remember my best...my most beautiful self. And you don't need to cry. It's really not pretty, let me remember your most genuine smile. When you let go of my hand, turn around, tears fall rapidly. I tell myself it doesn't matter, but I'm pretending so obviously. A chill runs through my heart. I don't want to leave here. The last train, you at the window, your face pale. I wave goodbye to you. At this moment, the slowly falling snow reflects countless pictures. As you gradually disappear on the horizon, I wave goodbye to you again.

Recalling the journey we've walked together, commemorating the years we've spent together, I have some emotions that need no control. Repressed for too long, I just want to talk to myself now, even if it's incoherent, I'll say it to myself.

At this moment, expressionless, I look up and see the fine snow slowly drift towards my eyes.
