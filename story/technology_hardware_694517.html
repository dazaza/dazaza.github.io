□Guo Hua Since August 2011, the comparative price ratio of Dalian plastic (L) to New York crude oil has been continuously declining. We believe that in 2012, this price ratio will be somewhat restored, and the undervalued state of plastic will improve. Therefore, a mid-term arbitrage operation of buying plastics and selling crude oil can be carried out.

The Price Ratio is at a Low Level

According to our tracking and analysis, currently, the price ratio of L1205 to NYMEX crude oil May contract is at a low range of 95-100, and the probability of future upward restoration of the price ratio is relatively high. Moreover, recently, due to the positive news during the two sessions period, there are signs of warming up in the domestic futures market; while crude oil, after the previous round of significant rise, is temporarily in a high-level fluctuation pattern, thus bringing about the space for cross-commodity arbitrage operations.

Given that the standard unit of NYMEX crude oil contracts is too large (minimum trading unit is 1000 barrels), the MINI contract (minimum trading unit is 500 barrels) can be used for operation. According to the principle of equal amount, the operation ratio of Dalian plastic to US crude oil is 25:1. The stop-loss point can be set at a price ratio of 15 (according to the price ratio excluding exchange rate impact), and the take-profit point can be set at a price ratio of 18-20. Currently, the price ratio of the two is about 16. If the price ratio returns to 18, the yield can reach 50.9%.

Plastic is Expected to Rise, Crude Oil Fluctuates within an Interval

From a cost perspective, whether it's LLDPE or upstream ethylene, production profits are in a declining trend. Calculated based on the spot price of naphtha at $895/ton (CFR Japan) and $882/ton (FOB Singapore), the cash cost of ethylene from cracking units: Northeast Asia $1203/ton, Southeast Asia $1203/ton; the cash gross profit is -$103/ton and -$123/ton respectively. And the linear situation is also on the edge of loss. Overall, linear production has almost no profit, which to some extent limits the downward space; if this loss situation persists, the possibility of petrochemical plants reducing production in the near future will increase, then the supply of LLDPE may become stage-wise tight, which is helpful for the price increase.

In terms of crude oil, benefiting from the improvement of U.S. economic data and the instability of the Middle East situation, the center of gravity of oil prices has shifted upward recently. However, it should be noted that Middle East issues affect BRENT crude oil more, and have limited impact on WTI; additionally, the improvement in U.S. economic data may be more due to seasonal factors, so whether its impact on oil prices can continue remains to be observed. Therefore, we believe that crude oil is more likely to maintain fluctuations around $100 in the medium term.

Whether from the historical statistical data of the price ratio or the fundamental perspective, it shows that the possibility of the upward restoration of the price ratio between the two in the medium term is relatively high, and the feasibility of the operation of buying Dalian plastic and selling US crude oil is strong. But at the same time, we noticed that since 2011, the correlation between Dalian plastic and US crude oil has declined, therefore, there are certain risks in the arbitrage operation of buying plastic and selling US crude oil. It is suggested that investors should not over-position and set a good stop-loss.
