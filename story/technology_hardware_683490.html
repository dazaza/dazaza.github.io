Beijing mission's building of restaurant by tearing down walls is accused of disturbing the people, while the staff denies it (Picture)

by aiipii12987 on 2012-02-29 14:40:57

The Haixi Prefecture Liaison Office in Beijing, located among a row of street-front shops, is not very noticeable. Photo by reporter Zhang Tailing.

Report (by reporter Zhang Tailing): "Twelve two-bedroom apartments have been entirely bought and connected together, with stairs leading directly to the bottom-level commercial space. The kitchen has been equipped with professional restaurant cooking appliances and axial flow fans. Frequently, the lights are shining brightly, cups are clinking, songs are ringing out, the aroma of dishes fills the air, and the fans roar..." A post criticizing the Haixi Prefecture Liaison Office in Beijing for disturbing the peace has drawn attention on the owner's forum of Xicheng District's Zhongxin Jinyuan (Zhongxin City) in recent days. Yesterday afternoon, the staff of the liaison office denied disturbing the residents and refused the reporter's request to enter and learn more about the situation.

Residents claim they were asked to move or swap houses

Yesterday afternoon, at the Zhongxin Jinyuan Building 1, the Haixi Prefecture Liaison Office in Beijing, which only has two small open doors, was inconspicuous among a row of street-front shops. It was reported that all the windows of the second floor, which they purchased, had uniformly installed white curtains, neat but not ostentatious.

"Often we can hear the roar of the exhaust fan, and we can smell whatever they're cooking," said Ms. Lü, an owner of Unit 3, Building 1. Later, someone from the liaison office downstairs apologized for the noise caused by the exhaust fan and gave her some dried yak meat. She felt it was inappropriate to accept gifts from neighbors and prepared some gifts in return. "When I knocked on the fire door downstairs, I found that all the second-floor residences had been connected and designed into a dining room format with both open seating and private rooms."

Ms. Lü claimed that she immediately pointed out that building a restaurant inside a residential building was unsuitable and demanded their withdrawal. However, the other side refused, citing that the liaison office restaurant does not operate externally. Afterwards, they instead requested her to move or swap houses, offering to help find another place, and providing compensation through funding for decoration and furniture purchase.

Unwilling to move, Ms. Lü reported this matter to the property management and distributed flyers to neighbors calling for joint rights protection. An owner from Unit 2 stated that after receiving Ms. Lü's flyer, she learned that the liaison office was downstairs. This owner also believed this situation was inappropriate and planned to report it to relevant departments.

Front desk claims no entry for "outsiders"

Subsequently, reporters attempted to enter the liaison office from the second floor via the stairs of Unit 3 but could not open the corridor's fire door. After several unsuccessful attempts, the personnel inside the "liaison office" indicated that the "magnetic door" in the corridor had been locked and could only be entered from the street-front main entrance. Similarly, on the second floor of Unit 2, the handle of the corridor fire door had been removed, and the door could not be opened from the outside.

After circling around to the main entrance from outside the community and entering the liaison office, the front desk staff claimed that the leaders were not present, and contact information was unknown. Regarding the reporter's request to take a look upstairs, the staff firmly refused, stating that the liaison office does not interact externally, nor allow "outsiders" to enter. However, she admitted that the entire second floor of Building 1 belongs to the liaison office.

"There is just a small dining room upstairs where only the staff eat." The staff denied any disturbance claims.

Yesterday, the front desk staff of the Jin Yuan Property Service Center of Zhongxin Property Co., Ltd. stated that they were still unclear about the disturbance issue involving the liaison office. Ms. Lü introduced that the person in charge of the property management had already expressed that they would contact relevant departments to coordinate and resolve this matter.
