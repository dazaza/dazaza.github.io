Nanjing's real estate market sees a price drop trend, with the largest decrease approaching 13% (Part One)

by oulalala94 on 2010-05-12 16:42:17

Nanjing's Jiangbei real estate market suddenly hit by price cuts yesterday

With super-large scale developments standing tall and consistently leading in Nanjing’s development and sales volume, the Jiangbei real estate market suddenly made waves yesterday! Following the Xuri Shangcheng development offering a fixed price of 4500 yuan/sq m for over 300 units, Suning Properties launched a similar fixed-price strategy for its three Qiaobei developments yesterday: The Tianruncheng development set a fixed price of 4088 yuan/sq m for 350 units, which is around 600 yuan/sq m cheaper than before.

The relentless discounting and promotions by Qiaobei developments - does their collective price drop indicate that Jiangbei housing prices can no longer hold up? Is this a precursor to an overall decline in Nanjing housing prices?

[Price Plunge] Three more developments offer fixed-price units

The super-large Tianruncheng development, which shockingly offered a fixed price of 4088 yuan/sq m, has 350 special-priced units distributed across different sections. These 550 special-priced units account for 16% of the total available units for sale.

Interestingly, two other developments in the same Qiaobei area also implemented fixed prices: Venetian Water City priced 150 units at 4480 yuan/sq m, while Tianhua Lugu priced 50 units at 4188 yuan/sq m. However, it was learned that these developments have strict restrictions on the floors of the units, such as ground floor and top floor units for one development, and floors 1-6 of high-rise buildings and ground and 6th floors of multi-story buildings for another.

In total, the 550 special-priced units represent a significant proportion of the available units for sale. According to Nanjing Online Real Estate data, these three developments currently have a total of 3360 available units, meaning the 550 special-priced units account for over 16%.

An industry insider stated that for a major player like Suning in Qiaobei, 550 units spread across three developments might seem insignificant, but considering the current total available units, 16% is still a considerable proportion.

Sold to specific groups? Sales office says anyone can buy

Interestingly, Suning Properties’ official stance yesterday was that the 550 units across the three developments were specially set aside to回馈 society and show social humanitarian care, targeting provincial and municipal model workers, advanced workers, military personnel, and others who have contributed to society.

However, when reporters inquired at these three developments yesterday, sales staff indicated there were no such restrictions, and buyers could purchase normally.

Why would the developer be so secretive? An industry insider speculated that the developer may be concerned that previous buyers might feel they bought at a loss and demand refunds or returns.

[Public Reaction] Reasons for both those wanting to buy and those not

Mr. Shi remarked that if the fixed-price discounts were only for ground and top floor units, it wouldn't mean much. But he noticed that some fixed prices weren't exclusively for ground and top floors; for example, Development C offered fixed prices for floors 1-6 of high-rise buildings, with units on the 4th and 5th floors priced at 4480 yuan/sq m, which he considered worth considering.

Nevertheless, Mr. Shi also believed that overall, these fixed-price developments were hundreds of yuan per sq m cheaper than what he had been tracking, with several hundred units available, indicating a broad scope of price reductions. He decided to visit the developments over the weekend.

However, some citizens were indifferent to the price drops in Jiangbei developments. Ms. Lu said that during her recent bus trip to view properties in Jiangbei, she was stuck in traffic for over 2 hours on the bridge. By the time she arrived at the development, she had lost all motivation to look further. With such a transportation environment, living there in the future would be too troublesome. Even though prices have dropped significantly, she remains cautious and won't consider buying in Jiangbei for now.

[Industry Analysis] Qiaobei housing prices' goal of reaching 5000 yuan shattered

Last year, Jiangbei developments aimed to break the 5000 yuan/sq m mark, but now it seems they've been brought back down to reality. Vice President Shi Dong of Nanjing Wangshang Real Estate Research Institution analyzed that from a February last year average transaction price of less than 3500 yuan/sq m to struggling to maintain at 4500 yuan/sq m now, breaking through 5000 yuan/sq m has evidently failed.

This can also be seen from the year-on-year comparison of sales volumes: In February this year, the sales volume of Pukou developments dropped by 74% month-on-month, putting pressure on developers.

According to forecasts by Wangshang Real Estate Research Institution, this year, Jiangbei will see the launch of 3.313 million square meters of new residential properties (slightly higher than 3.21 million square meters in 2007), with new approvals totaling 2.865 million square meters, ranking first among all Nanjing sectors. This year, the 45 developments currently on sale in the Jiangbei sector will add 2.8 million square meters of new residential supply, and the concentrated, large-scale supply of residential properties will inevitably intensify competition in the Qiaobei real estate market.
