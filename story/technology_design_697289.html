An octogenarian guards the forest alone for 33 years, and has walked a distance longer than the Equator during his mountain patrol.

by xue78awsh on 2012-03-08 14:38:14

Grandpa Sun Zhiqiang patrols the mountain. The elder has set up a simple bed in front of the stone house. Fetching water. Hubei Chutian Metropolis Daily - by reporter Xiao Min, Niu Tai, intern Yao Dandan; photography by reporter Lu Yanming, correspondent Li Xiaojun. The honeysuckle flowers have bloomed and withered again and again, 33 cold and hot years have passed irretrievably. His 33 years of persistence has turned him from a middle-aged man into an octogenarian. During these 33 years, the mountain roads he walked while patrolling and guarding the forest are longer than the equator.

The old man's name is Sun Zhiqiang, from Sizhuang Village, Taiping Town, Zaoyang City, and he is currently 81 years old. For 33 years, the old man has been guarding the forest alone. He, who knows some martial arts, has repelled tree thieves many times, firmly rejected tree vendors buying trees and his children wanting trees; he gave up family happiness, lived alone in the stone house on the mountain for 33 years, and the once barren Ziyu Mountain has now become lush.

The length of the equator is not as long as the old man's patrol road on the mountain. Recently, a post titled "Occupying the Mountain as 'King' to Obligatory Guard the Forest" has sparked heated discussions online. The post says that on Ziyu Mountain in Sizhuang Village, Taiping Town, Zaoyang City, an octogenarian lives with pine trees and has been guarding the forest alone for decades. The poster's name is Tong Changyu, an official from the Local Tax Bureau of Zaoyang City. In March this year, Tong Changyu participated in the "Three Ten Thousand" campaign and accidentally discovered Old Man Sun Zhiqiang's story of guarding the forest during a visit.

On the morning of the 7th, the reporter came to Ziyu Mountain to visit Grandpa Sun Zhiqiang. Ziyu Mountain is more than 300 meters above sea level, not very high, but the mountain road is very steep. Starting from the foot of the mountain, it took more than half an hour to walk to the depths of the forest.

There is an old stone house in the forest, built on the slope. The village cadres said that this was Grandpa Sun Zhiqiang's "home." Not far from the stone house, there was an elderly man about 1.6 meters tall, thin and frail, trimming branches; he was Sun Zhiqiang. Walking into the stone house, it was pitch black because there was no electricity here. There was not a single decent piece of furniture inside. It was in such a hard environment that the old man had persisted for 33 years.

At around 10:00 am, Sun Zhiqiang tucked a sickle into his waistband, picked up a槐tree stick weighing about 5 kilograms, and started patrolling the mountain.

He started from the southern end of the mountain, walking fast like flying, reaching the top in no time, leaving the reporter far behind.

Standing on the top of the mountain, he covered his eyes from the sunlight and looked around. After staying for more than ten minutes, the old man went around to the north side of the mountain.

Beside a grassland, he poked the grass skin with the槐tree stick and said, "The weather is dry, the grass is prone to fire, so we need to check carefully."

When descending from the north side of the mountain, he stopped under a pine tree again. He patted the trunk, broke off withered branches, muttering, "Good, the disease is almost gone."

The patrol route is nearly 3 kilometers one way, at least four times a day, covering at least 10.4 kilometers. The village party secretary Qu Zhenghua said that calculated this way, over the past 33 years, the old man's journey is at least equivalent to circling the equator once.

Bravely assuming responsibility, becoming the guardian of the mountain forest. Standing on Ziyu Mountain, one foot can step into Tanghe, Henan Province, and the other foot can step into Zaoyang, Hubei Province.

Forty-six years ago, Ziyu Mountain was still a barren hill: the bald ridge was full of ravines, with no vegetation growing. "Not even a piece of firewood can be found," the villagers of Sizhuang Village lamented when they looked at the mountain.

In the spring of 1965, a tree-planting and afforestation activity changed the appearance of the mountain. At that time, men and women of all ages in the whole village came to the mountain and planted seedlings of pine trees, and Sun Zhiqiang was also one of them. After careful care for several years, the seedlings gradually grew up, and Ziyu Mountain was no longer a barren mountain. In the lush green sea, birds chirped, the wind slapped the pines, rustling.

To protect this hard-won forest, the villagers built a stone house in the mountain and took turns guarding it. The forest guard was paid a monthly subsidy of 100 yuan by the village. However, due to the lack of material resources, harsh conditions, and unbearable loneliness in the forest, the forest guards all retreated and returned to the foot of the mountain. Without supervision, the trees were stolen and forest fires occurred frequently. The villagers saw this in their eyes and were worried in their hearts.

In the summer of 1978, the village held a meeting to discuss this matter. During the meeting, the villagers discussed. "Too bad, too bad! If this continues, Ziyu Mountain will turn back into a barren mountain. But it's too hard to stay on the mountain, that place is not fit for human habitation." "Yes, yes. To fetch water, you have to climb a mountain road of more than 1 kilometer."

At this moment, Sun Zhiqiang said, "I'll go! Planting trees is leaving wealth for future generations! I will use the rest of my life to guard Ziyu Mountain." "Your wife just passed away not long ago, what about your four children?" A villager asked him concernedly. "Let my younger brother take care of them, I can't worry about so much. If I don't take care of it, such a good forest will definitely be destroyed!" Sun Zhiqiang said.

Thus, Sun Zhiqiang embarked on his forest-guarding path, and he was 48 years old that year.

In 1985, due to various reasons, the village no longer paid the monthly subsidy of 100 yuan, but Sun Zhiqiang did not give up because of this, and guarded for another 25 years.

Preventing theft of trees, didn't attend the weddings of four children

A promise worth a thousand pieces of gold. The 33 years of persistence were all for that lush green forest.

During the days of guarding the forest, Sun Zhiqiang encountered multiple dangerous situations.

One day in 1998, the weeds caught fire, and Sun Zhiqiang fought the fire for a whole day and night before it was extinguished. After stopping, blisters appeared on his hands.

In July 2000, Sun Zhiqiang discovered three men stealing trees. He shouted loudly, "What are you doing? Stop!" Then, he, who knew a bit of martial arts, held a wooden stick and rushed up the mountain. Stones rained down like hailstones, Sun Zhiqiang dodged left and right, but he did not retreat. He rushed forward, swinging the wooden stick, and the three men ran away upon seeing this.

The next morning, five uninvited guests came to Sun Zhiqiang's home, one of whom was exactly the man who stole the tree. "Old man, you know some kung fu, but can you beat five people?" The five men rushed forward, and they fought together. After more than ten minutes, the five men left reluctantly.

In order to guard the forest, Sun Zhiqiang did not attend the weddings of his four children. Sun Qiaoyun, his eldest daughter, remembered that she and her fiancé went up the mountain to invite her father, and Sun Zhiqiang said, "Can't make it, recently there are too many tree thieves, and there are also more forest fires. If anything goes wrong, all these years of effort will be wasted."

Sun Qiaoyun told the reporter that for a long time, she did not understand her father. She asked, aren't the children more important to him than a few trees?

Now, she understands her father. The 33 years of persistence, those 300-plus acres of forest are her father's life.

Guarding the forest is protecting the villages below the mountain

In 2008, a man from Nanyang, Henan came to Ziyu Mountain to look for medicine and was attracted by the two honeysuckle trees in front of Sun Zhiqiang's residence. "How about selling me the trees? I'll give you 2000 yuan."

Sun Zhiqiang shook his head quickly. "Is it too little? How about 4100?"

Sun Zhiqiang still shook his head.

He said, "Although the trees were planted by me, the mountain belongs to the state. Without the mountain, there would be no trees. I'm just a tree watcher, how can I sell the trees?"

Despite repeated persuasion, Sun Zhiqiang refused to agree.

In 2009, Sun Zhiqiang's third son was building a new house and asked Sun Zhiqiang for three trees but was refused. The third son angrily asked, "So stingy, am I still your son?" Sun Zhiqiang said, "I'm a forest protector. If I agreed to let my son cut the trees, the villagers would curse me and destroy my lifetime reputation."

In others' eyes, Sun Zhiqiang is not a stingy person. Chang Yunqiang, a villager from Xingzhuang Village, Hubiyang Town, Tanghe County, Henan Province, often crosses Ziyu Mountain between Henan and Hubei, and Sun Zhiqiang's residence is his stopover. Chang Yunqiang said, "Although Sun Zhiqiang is poor, he is very generous. Every time I pass by, he takes out the best things to entertain me."

For 33 years, Sun Zhiqiang hasn't left Ziyu Mountain because he always worries about that patch of forest. He believes that guarding the forest is a way to repay kindness.

He told the reporter, "Sizhuang Village is at the foot of the mountain. In the past, there was not a blade of grass on the mountain. Every time it rained heavily, rocks rolled down. The village is right below the mountain, which is very dangerous. After the trees formed a forest, such incidents never happened again. We must repay the kindness!"

Luo Ping, a researcher from the Forestry Bureau of Xiangyang City, who came to visit after hearing the news, said, "Where there are trees, there is no soil erosion. Ziyu Mountain is a rocky mountain, and geological disasters such as mudslides are extremely likely to occur. If it weren't for these trees, Ziyu Mountain would have disappeared long ago. Old Sun's contribution is immeasurable."

Dialogue

"Dreaming of my wife scolding me"

Reporter: What was your life like before you went up the mountain?

Sun Zhiqiang: Those days were quite nice. During the busy farming season, we worked in the fields; during the leisure time, we played lions.

Reporter: Why did you choose to go up the mountain and guard the forest instead of enjoying such a comfortable life?

Sun Zhiqiang: You don't know! At that time, there was not a blade of grass on Ziyu Mountain, not even a piece of firewood could be found. Worse still, every time it rained, the water flowing down the mountain was all yellow. Stones often rolled down, and the village was right below the mountain, which was very dangerous. After planting trees, such incidents never happened again.

Reporter: Protecting the forest is everyone's responsibility, why do you have to bear it alone?

Sun Zhiqiang: Among neighbors, there's no need to calculate so much.

Reporter: Have you ever regretted?

Sun Zhiqiang: Once when I went down the mountain, I found that the village had changed greatly, the roads were wider, and the buildings were taller. Many of my peers were enjoying their retirement, and I envied them. But when I returned to the mountain and saw the pine trees one by one, my heart balanced again.

Reporter: I heard that your wife passed away early.

Sun Zhiqiang: She was a pitiful woman, she died at the age of thirty-something. I can't forget the look in her eyes when she passed away, thinking about it makes me heartbroken. I owe her all my life. When she died, she asked me to raise our four children. But I went up the mountain to protect the forest, and at that time, my eldest daughter was only thirteen years old. I dream of her scolding me.

Reporter: What are your current wishes?

Sun Zhiqiang: I now have two wishes. The first one is that after I die, bury me on the mountain. The second one is that I am old, and I can't guard the forest for many more years. I hope that someone will take over after I die.

Reporter: With such harsh conditions here, will anyone come?

Sun Zhiqiang: Yes, yes, there is no electricity in the stone house. No one willing to come is my biggest headache now, and when I think about this, I can't eat or sleep.
