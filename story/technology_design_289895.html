With 200,000, should I become a mortgage slave or a entrepreneurial wolf?

by liumeilian on 2009-12-03 10:19:29

With 200,000 yuan in hand, should one choose to become a "mortgage slave" or a "startup wolf"?!

As the saying goes: "No matter how luxurious a golden nest or silver nest is, none can compare with one’s own humble doghouse!" I believe most people would like to have a home of their own. The house doesn't need to be too large, as long as it's sufficient for living; the decoration doesn't have to be extravagant, middle-range is fine! Especially for our post-80s friends, they are even more pressured by potential mother-in-laws saying, "No house, and you can’t marry my precious daughter!" Therefore, many friends end up becoming "mortgage slaves." Meanwhile, there is also a popular saying in society: "Rather be a startup wolf than a salaried dog." Of course, this statement is rather radical, and I personally don't entirely agree with it because working for others is also a form of entrepreneurship. Who dares say that our "CEO Emperor" Tang Jun isn't fighting for his own career?!

200,000 yuan is not a small or large sum; many friends, through a few years of effort plus some family support, can gather this amount. Faced with the pressure of rising housing prices, especially the potential risk of "inflation," along with family urging, "Get married, have grandchildren!" I imagine many friends can't help but act immediately, thus dumping all 200,000 into a house, then working hard every month to pay off the mortgage, saving on everything to maintain the household.

In fact, as far as I know, many friends have entrepreneurial aspirations. For instance, researching in technical positions to become a technical backbone of their company, or striving in the market to become the head of the marketing department, or even going solo to start their own company and brand, and so on. But may I ask: when a person is crushed under the heavy burden of life, how much energy and time do they still have left to fight for these goals?

On the contrary, if we don't buy a house with the 200,000 yuan but use it as startup capital instead, for those who don't want to go solo, they could open a store with the 200,000, hire someone to manage it, and I think the annual income should be higher than the income brought by the appreciation of the house. For those who wish to go solo, the 200,000 at least can serve as startup capital to start a small business!

A house shouldn't become an obstacle to starting our careers! Those who have studied economics know that the faster the capital turns over, the better. However, houses belong to fixed assets!

Everyone is welcome to join the discussion...
