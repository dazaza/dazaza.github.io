Prepared to withdraw in 7 months, Mingcheng Property has not collected public electricity charges, leaving the community with a 400,000 yuan electricity bill.

April 2011

By reporter Xiao Lei

After the Spring Festival, Director Hong began deploying employees to collect public electricity fees from residents for the second half of last year. "Depending on the floor, each household should pay between 150 to 200 yuan. However, up to now, out of over 2700 households and tens of thousands of residents, only about 20% have paid. The money collected is not enough to cover the costs, so we can only pay as much as we collect. Currently, among the hundreds of elevators in the community, except for a few units where the public electricity fees have been mostly paid and are still operational, most others have stopped working. As for this year's property management fee, we've only collected around 10%. Now even the company's operation is very difficult, let alone maintaining surveillance equipment and security doors," said Director Hong.

The neighborhood elevators stopped operating due to power outage.

After receiving the tip-off, at around 2 p.m. on February 17th, I arrived at this residential complex located on Zhenxing Road in Pukou District. This is a commercial housing complex that was gradually delivered in four phases by the end of 2005, and the overall condition of the complex is still relatively new. However, upon entering the complex, I immediately saw a notice at the entrance gate stating: "As of February 8, 2012, your community still owes 239,702.5 yuan (excluding late payment fees) in public electricity charges. Given your long-term arrears in public electricity fees, which amount to a significant sum, our company will, in accordance with the provisions of the 'Electricity Law' and the 'Regulations on Electricity Supply and Use', cut off power supply to your community’s delinquent meters starting February 16, 2012. At that time, the public electricity section of the community will be powered down." Signed by Nanjing Power Supply Company Pukou Business Department, dated February 9, 2012.

The temporary working group of the owners' committee signed a contract with the new property management company, Wanyuan Property, but the contract is not standardized.

Recently, Mr. Xing from Nanjing contacted our news hotline at 025-96096 to report: "I am a resident of Jiang'an Shuicheng Residential Complex in Pukou District. Due to property management issues, the public electricity fees in our community have not been fully collected, resulting in all the elevators in the entire community being shut down."

Upon entering the residential building, I found that all the elevators were out of power, and some elevator doors were even open during the power outage. Ms. Wang, a resident living on the third floor of Building No. 41, told reporters that the elevators were shut down on the afternoon of February 16th. "After the Spring Festival, the property management company collected 200 yuan for public electricity fees, which were for the second half of last year. I heard that many people didn't pay. But I did pay, and now the elevators cannot be used, so wasn't my payment wasted? Luckily, I live on the third floor, so climbing stairs isn't too bad. It just feels like I paid the electricity fee for nothing."

In the second half of 2011

November 2011

Regarding this power outage in the community, Director Chen stated that only 300,000 yuan out of the 400,000 yuan owed from last year had been paid, and with additional public electricity usage since then, it quickly accumulated to nearly 240,000 yuan again. "When the power company informed us they would cut off the power, we stepped in to negotiate with them, hoping they could delay the action. In the end, out of humanitarian considerations, the power company only cut off the electricity for the elevators, while the public lighting and water pressure pumps, which directly affect residents' daily lives, were not turned off."

Conflict-ridden within the community, how to solve the problem? Director Chen expressed that public electricity is used by the residents of the community, and ultimately, it is the residents who must bear the cost. "There are more than 2700 households and tens of thousands of residents in the community. To elect a new owners' committee, more than half of the owners must participate. A meeting involving thousands of people is obviously impractical and cannot address the urgent needs of the community at present. We have already begun collaborating with the community where the complex is located, as well as the police station, along with the community owners, forming a management committee to serve the function of an owners' committee. Among the community owners, the main members are the temporary working group members and the building leaders; other owners who wish to participate may also register. If there are many such owners, we will conduct elections within this group. As for those owners who do not wish to register, naturally, they show no concern for their own community affairs. After the establishment of the management committee, it can sign a revised property management contract with Wanyuan Property, thus resolving Wanyuan Property's concerns. At the same time, the management committee can promote within the residents, reminding them to pay various reasonable fees. Regarding the funds taken away by Mingcheng Property when they withdrew from the community, as well as the non-return of the seal by the original chairman of the owners' committee, the management committee will negotiate with them. Since the management committee includes government department members and no property company members, I believe the credibility will be much better."

Ms. Wang's situation is still manageable, but Mr. Zhu, who lives on the 11th floor, is having trouble. "Since the elevator stopped, I haven't dared to go out. Climbing down 11 floors, my old bones would fall apart. Right now, after just one day, things at home are still usable. But if it continues for a few more days and the rice, oil, and vegetables at home run out, I'll probably have to ask my son, who works in the city center, to come back."

Why are the owners unwilling to pay the public electricity fees and property management fees? According to Director Chen from the Property Management Office of the Yanjiang Subdistrict in Pukou District, the previous property management company for the Jiang'an Shuicheng Residential Complex was Mingcheng Property Management Co., Ltd. Starting from April last year, due to conflicts within the owners' committee, the contract between the complex and the property management company expired and was not renewed. From that point on, Mingcheng Property had always been preparing to withdraw from the complex and stopped collecting any public expenses. By November, when Mingcheng Property left Jiang'an Shuicheng, the entire complex had already accumulated a 400,000 yuan debt in public electricity fees. At that time, the power company was about to cut off the public electricity, but after our mediation, Mingcheng Property contributed 50,000 yuan, Wanyuan Property contributed 100,000 yuan, and the subdistrict office prepaid 100,000 yuan for the complex, avoiding the power outage. Later, Wanyuan Property contributed another 50,000 yuan." Director Chen also described that since April last year, after the contract expiration of Mingcheng Property, disputes within the owners' committee regarding which property management company to choose increased, eventually leading to a falling out. "The former chairman of the owners' committee took away the seal of the owners' committee and hasn't returned it yet, so the subdistrict office had to step in, gather several former members of the owners' committee, and form a temporary working group. This group signed a property management contract with the current Wanyuan Property, with our Property Management Office serving as the witness. However, this contract is not a standardized property management contract. Wanyuan Property is worried that if a new owners' committee is formed, they will be forced out, so they dare not invest in areas like surveillance and security door repairs. As for the owners of the complex, they are also concerned that if a new owners' committee is established and fires Wanyuan Property, wouldn't the money they paid be wasted? Moreover, the residents don't see Wanyuan Property making investments within the complex, which further deepens their worries, so naturally, Wanyuan Property cannot collect the public electricity fees or property management fees," Director Chen stated.

Reasons for the power outage: Old property management company didn't collect public electricity fees, and the new property management company only received 20%.

From the property management company of the complex, Jiangsu Wanyuan Property Management Co., Ltd., we learned that for the public electricity fees of the second half of last year, the company only received 20%, and indeed does not have the ability to pay the public electricity fees that should be borne by the residents. The Yanjiang Subdistrict Property Management Office of Nanjing's Pukou District (Weibo) stated that they have started collaborating with the community and the police station where the complex is located, preparing to establish a management committee within the complex to resolve the current troubles faced by the complex.

Behind-the-scenes issue: Mutual distrust between property management and owners, inability to collect fees

Wanyuan Property only managed to collect 20% of the public electricity fees for the second half of 2011

So why did the property management company fail to pay the public electricity fees? I approached Mr. Hong, the director of the Jiang'an Shuicheng Property Management Office of Jiangsu Wanyuan Property Management Co., Ltd. Director Hong stated that the reason for the failure to pay the public electricity fees was simply the inability to collect them. "The complex started delivery in 2005, and the previous property management company was Nanjing Mingcheng Property Management Co., Ltd. Our company only took over this complex in December last year. At that time, the previous property management company had already accumulated a debt of 400,000 yuan in public electricity fees to the power company. Additionally, they took away all the parking fees and renovation service fees collected earlier, leaving us with a mess. Besides the financial loss, all the surveillance systems in the complex are broken, and half of the security doors are also malfunctioning. We have already advanced 150,000 yuan in two installments for the electricity fees, but we truly lack the financial capacity to continue advancing payments."
