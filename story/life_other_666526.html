You have gone far, and I am still here.

The残year has come to an end.

Lying in bed, listening to the ticking of my watch, my thoughts are disordered, and I no longer feel the slightest bit sleepy. Only the cold keyboard keeps me company through the rest of the night.

Back home, I always thought that the weather in my hometown would be very cold. Indeed, today I have experienced it, a feeling different from just being cold—it penetrates to the bone, makes my heart tremble. This is my most naked feeling about Hangzhou's weather.

I've always believed that three layers of clothing were enough for me. In fact, they were, truly enough.

On December 31st, the last day of 2010, I felt anxious. Unknowingly, half a year had passed again. Looking at everyone on QQ making signatures about looking forward to vacation, as if letting work dance wildly behind them, I felt overwhelmed. Perhaps, this is how we, as members of society, should behave—only through work can we prove our excellence and not betray the deep expectations placed upon us.

At three o'clock in the morning, hearing the ticking of the clock, with complicated thoughts, I suddenly realized how distant that seemingly reachable sleep feels. Faced with the inner turmoil, I have to admit that those familiar faces that appear in my dreams, those scenes I least want to see, those words that seem so matter-of-fact—I detest them. So, I chose to stay with my computer, which has accompanied me for many years, and a brother closer than any blood sibling.

Lighting a cigarette, doing it over and over again, a movement so familiar it couldn't be more so, yet still filled with joy and contentment, without even a hint of annoyance. Watching my gradually yellowing nails, the smoke swirling around me, white or yellowish, rising, spreading, diluting, sometimes dense, sometimes sparse, stinging my eyes, tears can't help but hover, blurring the already chaotic gaze.

Loneliness collides within our inexperienced hearts, aimlessly, self-righteously, heading southward, tearing open wounds that have long since scabbed over again and again. Then, with a grin that feels like it could tear apart, we hide the loneliness we haven't fully understood.

Afterwards, I often hear in my ears the solemn vows I once made, extravagant promises that didn’t require flowery language or sweet whispers. Just word by word, phrase by phrase, expressing my determination. But when all the memories are pieced together only to be torn apart into fragmented images, I realize how stupid I was. From the beginning to the end, it turns out that I relied on one lie after another to myself to get this far.

Tears, a term both familiar and strange. To others, perhaps for men, tears represent weakness, submission. However, everyone knows deep down that tears also signify indulgence, release. After innocence comes a body devoid of sensation. From ignorance to now, countless tears have been cheaply shed by me. After being hurt time and time again, I cry as expected, and I realize that these murky tears feel less and less real.

Anxiety, I tell myself repeatedly to stay calm. Reality is cruel and illogical, and I, still wet behind the ears, am so small, so powerless in its face. Yet, my heart still holds onto those small hopes, waiting for them to be shattered again, and in that shattering, seeking that sense of normalcy.

At the end of the year, in ten days, I must return home, find that still warm nest, soothing the ripples of excitement that stir within me, as if losing control and breaking free. However, inside, I resist, evade this inevitable outcome. Counting the abundant fruits I've harvested this semester leaves me utterly ashamed. Looking forward, seeing those warm smiles, hearing those warm greetings, and answering those unavoidable questions, I doubt I will have anything to say.

If near yet distant, facing the messy drafts composed of my own words, looking back, I find them so strange yet true.

In the end, there remains a cup of long-cold coffee and a floor covered with cigarette butts that naturally extinguished themselves.
