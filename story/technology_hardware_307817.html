Maradona: If he was white, he would be like Messi. Del Piero and Lippi strangle the miracle of Inter Milan together

by qinglai888 on 2009-12-14 22:58:57

Maradona, Juve Fans Kill Inter Milan's Miracle: If He Were White, He'd Be Like Messi

Compared to the game at Meazza 3 days ago, Balotelli was relatively calm in Bergamo. But even so, Balotelli's status in Mourinho's mind has become very obvious after starting consecutively twice within 3 days for Inter Milan.

This is not the first time Balotelli started consecutively this season; he also started consecutively in the 8th and 9th rounds of the league and performed well. However, these two different consecutive starts naturally have different values. In the midweek 2-0 win against Kazan Rubin, Balotelli scored one goal and assisted another, single-handedly sending Inter Milan into the Round of 16. For Inter Milan, this 19-year-old Italian youth is no longer just a "beneficial addition"; he is the type of player who can make a difference.

The blue stadium of Atalanta is undoubtedly a place worth remembering for Balotelli. In April 2008, he completed his first goal in Serie A here and assisted Vieira in scoring another goal. This time returning here, Mourinho placed him and Eto'o on both wings, somewhat limiting Balotelli's power. However, the brilliance of a genius can be covered, but it cannot be extinguished. Just at the start of the match, Balotelli caused a foul by Golic with his individual breakthrough. Subsequently, in the 3rd minute, his cross after moving wide helped Maicon complete a shot. The most spectacular performance came in the 30th minute when Sneijder made a direct pass, Balotelli received the ball and with a smooth turn and right-footed fake, almost from the same spot, he left Padoin behind him.

Sneijder was shown a red card in the 64th minute, and to maintain tactical balance, Mourinho immediately substituted Balotelli with Stankovic to ensure balance in the midfield and backline. Balotelli clearly did not expect this substitution and reluctantly left the field under the persuasion of Materazzi and the urging of referee Rizzoli.

Balotelli's so-called "character issues" reappeared, but for a 19-year-old child, showing such emotions is understandable. However, in Maradona's view, this is why Balotelli cannot enter his Italian national team: "I need to keep the atmosphere in the Italian team calm, please respect my choice." In Maradona's selection, players like Pepe and Quagliarella are more qualified than Balotelli because they can "keep the atmosphere in the Italian team calm." Such an attitude is indeed a strange phenomenon in the Italian national team, where the criterion for promoting new players is not ability but character. Isn't one of Maradona's jobs to integrate players with personalities into the team? Yet he avoids such issues. Of course, Maradona led the Italian team to win the 2006 World Cup championship, making him an authority that no one can refute. He is the Pope of Italian football; you can pray to him, but he will give you no return.

Juve legend Altafini said that if Maradona does not summon Balotelli, he might commit a great mistake. Altafini's reason is: "Balotelli possesses all the conditions to join the national team: he has technique, talent, and outstanding personal characteristics."

However, Altafini overlooked one issue: Balotelli is black. Extreme Juventus fans say, "Italians are not black." They spread such rhetoric even when facing teams like Bordeaux or Bayern Munich. To compare Balotelli's difficulties with a statement, Moggi said, "If Melo were Italian, then he would only be worth 6 million euros." If Balotelli were white, would Maradona still resolutely reject him? Marchisio's goal in the Italian Derby was called "having Messi's skills" by Turin Sports Daily. Fortunately, Juventus didn't pass Bayern Munich, otherwise, Marchisio with one assist might have been praised to no end. What Marchisio achieved, Balotelli had done earlier. In the 2008-09 Champions League round of 16 away game against Manchester United, Balotelli challenged O'Shea six times in the first half, causing the Irishman to foul five times. If he were white, would Maradona still ignore him?

Since Weah in 1995, the Ballon d'Or has never been awarded to a black player again, despite Ronaldinho never taking a stance on his skin color and Ronaldo saying in 2009 that he is black. However, even if Balotelli possesses individual talents comparable to Messi, he still struggles to shake off the prejudice brought by his skin color in Italy. Only a very few people outside the mainstream center dare to criticize such prejudices. Former Perugia owner Cechi-Gori said, "Maradona is an arrogant lucky guy, he insists on using Liedertale but never gives Materazzi a chance again." Balotelli and Materazzi have similar fates, but Materazzi is still luckier than Balotelli.
