Because of the need to pay high fees to Dingbang Company

by tangtwuwu8 on 2011-05-20 09:16:08

Among the items seized were a large number of stolen goods.

The police did not bring people in for questioning; it was a compulsory measure.

What was even more surprising is that the items they claimed to have picked up, after being investigated by the police, turned out to be mostly stolen or robbed items, some of which were even within the scope of cases the police were investigating.

Currently, suspects Chen and Zhao are under criminal compulsory measures by the Gulou police on suspicion of concealing, covering up, and profiting from criminal proceeds.

The Gulou police also stated that they will pay more practical attention to cases involving people's livelihoods. At the same time, they hope that citizens who pick up lost items should quickly hand them over to the police, and the police will provide swift, thoughtful, safe, and free return services to the owners.

According to information from the police, since January this year, the Zhongyangmen Police Station in the jurisdiction has received multiple reports from citizens: citizens had to pay high fees to Dingbang Company to retrieve their stolen ID cards, bank cards, and other items, forcing them to seek police assistance.

Yesterday afternoon, the reporter repeatedly called Zhao, the person in charge of Nanjing Dingbang Company. The phone was either unanswered or hung up. Around 8 PM, the reporter finally received a call back from Zhao, but after saying just a few words, he hung up.

"If the owner refuses to pay the redemption fee, Dingbang Company will illegally seize the items for various reasons, and may even destroy them," it was reported that the company's actions caused dissatisfaction among the owners, and the conflicts escalated continuously. When the reporter revisited the office where Nanjing Dingbang Company was located, a nearby neighbor revealed that the trigger for the police action might have been a lost item found near Changjiang Road recently involving an international student, which was reported again due to charging fees.

Just as our reporter interviewed on March 8th, around 11 AM that day, after receiving another report about Nanjing Dingbang Company, the police officers of Zhongyangmen Police Station took decisive action, swiftly arresting the legal representative of the company. At the same time, the investigating officers discovered over a hundred items suspected of being stolen or robbed, including wallets, ID cards, and bank cards, within the company. The information publicly disclosed by the Nanjing Gulou police to the reporters was: "The police quickly cracked down on a criminal gang suspected of receiving, selling, and harboring stolen goods under the guise of 'paid lost-and-found', seizing over a hundred items suspected of being stolen, including ID cards, bank cards, and wallets. The suspects involved in the case have currently been placed under criminal compulsory measures by the police."

●Police Appeal

Citizens who have paid to reclaim lost items should come forward to report the incidents promptly.

It was introduced that Zhao, the person in charge of the company, had been released on bail, meaning that after being subjected to criminal compulsory measures by the police, he was allowed to be released on bail. "Because of the low social hazard, he was allowed to be released on bail," said an informed person. After leaving the police station, Zhao told the reporter that he didn't want to say much. However, his company was still under investigation and would definitely not continue operating.

After listing Dingbang as an investigative target, the police officers of Zhongyangmen Police Station conducted nearly two months of investigations and visits, discovering that the paid lost-and-found business operated by Nanjing Dingbang Company was not within the scope permitted by its business license, making it suspected of overstepping its authorized operations.

"It's this company, sometimes with four or five reports in a single day, and sometimes even right after the police leave, another report comes in," an informed person told the reporter. It was precisely this continuous and repeated reporting between Nanjing Dingbang and its clients that eventually drew the high attention of the Gulou police, leading to its inclusion as an investigative target.

The police confirmed that dozens of documents retrieved involved theft and robbery cases.

On March 9th, our newspaper disclosed the news of Nanjing's first lost-and-found company being sealed by the police. Two days later, we learned from the Nanjing Gulou District Public Security Bureau responsible for investigating the case that the police took criminal compulsory measures against the suspects involved in Nanjing Dingbang Company, accusing them of receiving, selling, and harboring stolen goods under the guise of "paid lost-and-found." Currently, the police have already seized over a hundred items suspected of being stolen, including ID cards, bank cards, and wallets from the company.

However, based on clues provided by citizens, after thorough tracking and investigation by the police, it was discovered that many of these wallets, ID cards, and bank cards labeled as lost items by Dingbang Company were actually stolen or robbed items.

When the police compared the information on the Nanjing police platform, they found that dozens of ID cards and bank cards were indeed stolen or robbed items. These items were related to dozens of criminal and public security cases that the police had filed for investigation. The remaining items are also being further compared and investigated by the police to trace their origins.

At the same time, the Gulou police appealed for citizens who had previously paid to retrieve stolen or robbed lost items from this company to register and file complaints at the Zhongyangmen Police Station as soon as possible. (Address: No. 32 Heilongjiang Road, Gulou District, Contact Persons: Officer Ma, Officer Wu, Phone: 18913860223, 18913860296.)

There were as many as four or five alarms in one day.

Reported by Jianming Mei

The Gulou police told reporters that after preliminary investigations, the current basic situation is clear: most of the items for which Dingbang Company charged fees were ID cards, student IDs, driver's licenses, bank cards, passports, degree certificates, leather bags, car keys, and other documents and items. In a short period, Nanjing Dingbang Company accumulated fees from dozens of owners who paid to reclaim their lost items, amounting to tens of thousands of yuan.

It was introduced that since its establishment, Dingbang Company has been committed to expanding its business channels, i.e., actively collecting lost items. The more lost items collected, the higher the income. However, from the interview process, the company’s head, citing commercial secrets, was unwilling to disclose the specific sources of these lost items, only claiming that they were all picked up by cleaning workers and handed over to the company. In response, several cleaning workers in Xinjiekou told the reporter that in a whole year, they rarely pick up one or two lost wallets, and according to the company’s instructions, they must hand them over to the company or report them to the local police station.
