The space within a region reflects the natural and economic characteristics corresponding to the nation’s terminology. A regional theater art institute is a system’s abbreviation. Regionally speaking, in a narrow sense, it refers to the overall intensity of theatrical activities (institutions, staff, facilities, etc.) within a general region; drama refers to an open system of artistic and cultural performances that reflect the economic and social development characteristics of a smaller region. Regional theater art aims to establish a regional organization for broader spatial exchanges in theatrical art, achieving integration and optimal allocation of theatrical resources, constructing an open developmental framework for regional development, and promoting overall linkage in theater modes which favor unique applications of advantageous resources, thus advancing the development of theatrical art and industry.

Secondly, integrating regional resources and establishing a regional market space for the development of theater drama necessitates expanding the original theater space and strategically breaking through the constraints of theater space, administrative boundaries, and also the artistic and cultural space of theater. With this vast cultural space and background, theater art can enhance its competitiveness, expand the theatrical market, and steadily grow. First, from the audience's perspective: the fast-paced modern life and the legalities of theater production, from conception, scriptwriting, rehearsal to performance, make audiences in a particular region or city tired of seeing the same old plays repeatedly. Some cities have very limited markets; for example, Xining has only a few million people, so the number of theatergoers is limited. Additionally, there is only one theater in Xining. The small audience combined with the limited number of theaters means that they cannot perform many shows in a year, making it impossible to satisfy the audience's aesthetic trends and innovative ideas. A play performed repeatedly may cause aesthetic fatigue among some viewers, leading to their inability to survive in the market.

Secondly, from the market perspective of performances: if there is a larger cultural space and a bigger performance market, the development of high-quality theatrical performances will be faster. Therefore, to develop regional theater culture and create a strategic market framework, it must aim at the international market and not be limited to administrative jurisdiction on a regional basis. The market response leverages basic repertoires and fully mobilizes the entire artistic market, especially the mass entertainment market, to meet high market demands and promote the formation of community-based theatrical art. Let all sectors of society focus on the development of theatrical art and pay attention to the inheritance and development of traditional culture. In this way, all markets involved in market-oriented operation mechanisms, including supply and demand aspects as well as intermediaries, become market entities. Integrating these into a unified market-oriented operation mechanism expands our market space and achieves our strategic goals.

Thirdly, integrating resources for regional theater development requires the establishment of a chain of theater art institutes in the region, building a fusion culture that includes resources such as human resources, market resources, management resources, and regional theater art resources, extending from conceptualization to scriptwriting, scheduling, marketing, and theater line performances. Many theater groups lack relative integration in the production chain, specifically in marketing and creative rehearsals. Even if there are good ideas recognized by the market, it is difficult to achieve significant box office success. To achieve qualitative leaps, we need to build an industrial chain where a unit does not just form a theater but should represent an industry, a region, or even cross-industry, cross-regional collaboration incorporating multiple elements. Establishing a dramatic chain involves not only aligning thoughts, production, and marketing concepts within the same genre but also engaging in various operations. Due to today's Chinese society cultivating urban brands and implementing strategies like festivals, some cities have become national focal points for sustainable development. To establish their own economic development brands, they have launched events such as the Qinghai Lake Cycling Festival, East-West Trade Fair, Tulip Festival, series of festivals, Cultural Tourism Festivals, etc., providing a good platform for large-scale celebratory performances. Theater people want to utilize this platform, leveraging the advantages of creative packaging in theaters, developing theatrical activities, expanding the theatrical market, and promoting the development of the theatrical industry with successful government and hosting institutions.
