How to Write Effective Foreign Trade Development Letters

by lileiph43 on 2010-05-22 08:57:17

Abstract: Foreign trade development letters are very important for expanding foreign trade business and developing new customers. They extend an olive branch for establishing a friendly cooperative relationship, which may bring in endless streams of money. The reply rate of the development letter is the first factor determining its success. This article attempts to explore the factors affecting the reply rate of foreign trade development letters from multiple angles and levels. It inevitably covers only a fraction of the whole picture, merely serving as a starting point to attract attention and induce thought. If it can benefit the foreign trade industry, I would be greatly consoled.

1. What is a foreign trade development letter?

Simply put, a development letter is your first email or letter to a potential customer. A foreign trade development letter is your first letter to your potential customer, aiming to develop this potential customer, establish business cooperation, gain orders, and expand business. It extends an olive branch for establishing a friendly cooperative relationship, bringing possible endless streams of money. It sends hope and awaits a response. The most worthy of study is the development letter delivered by email, which is a fast, cost-effective, comprehensive, and less likely to cause misunderstanding effective communication method.

Before analyzing the factors affecting the reply rate of foreign trade development letters, let's talk about "love letters" that you are familiar with, because there are many similarities between foreign trade development letters and the first love letter.

As a foreign trade person, even if you have just graduated, you may already be familiar with love letters, and may have even written and used them successfully, ultimately winning the heart of your beloved or finding a wealthy partner. 3. Love Letters and Foreign Trade Development Letters

Alright, now that we have a basic understanding of love letters, let's begin our thinking process based on the familiar topic of writing love letters.

3.1 Who should the foreign trade development letter be written to?

Let's start with love letters again, who would you write a love letter to?

Passerby A: I would write to my ideal he/she, AIHONG, I miss you! Passerby B: I am a follower of Mozi, advocating "universal love", as long as it's the opposite sex, from sixteen years old to eighty-two, I will write to them all [shaking head and swaying], this is called "casting a wide net, catching big fish".

Judge: Passerby A, that's a correct answer, +10 points. Passerby B, do you realize how really "silly" that sounds... [I was going to say "stupid", but let's still care for the young]. Now talking about foreign trade development letters, if the recipient can be judged as your potential customer, then you must have gained some understanding through certain channels. If there is no understanding at all, then you're like Passerby B.

Of course, gaining this understanding comes at a cost, mainly in terms of time and energy, and channel investment costs. To obtain benefits based on the cost of investment, you need to strive to increase the reply rate of development letters. Since there are costs involved, they must be controlled to pursue maximum profit, so it is necessary to mention the channels.

The main channels we use to understand customers include: trade shows, paid B2B websites, free B2B websites, "posting product information, letting customers find you", search engines, government agencies, yellow pages, foreign-related BBS or communities, the target company's website...

In a buyer's market, there must be demand for purchase intention, market research, products suitable for the market, quality, price, and service suitability for easier transactions. Evaluating the quality of potential customers should involve analysis based on whether the product suits their needs, purchase intention, quality, price, service combined with possible profits generated by possible transaction volumes.

Generally speaking, potential customers obtained from trade shows have the highest quality. They already have some understanding of your products, and their channel investment is also significant, indicating strong purchase intent and larger quantities, making them key targets for development.

Paid B2B websites, free B2B websites, usually provide the latest purchasing information, but each case must be analyzed specifically and comprehensively evaluated. Based on the experience of the author and many foreign trade netizens and business friends using these websites, purchasing information on B2B websites should be treated cautiously, as there are scammers, market researchers, advertisers, and those genuinely interested in doing business. Therefore, combining your own experience, product, geographical coverage of the B2B website, country/region of the purchaser, and the detailedness of the information provided is crucial. Of course, the first thing you should concern yourself with is whether the product fits the need, and then examine carefully.

"Posting product information, letting customers find you", this is actually quite hard to explain. Where have you posted your information? How does your information introduce your company or product? When the other party contacts you, you may know nothing about them. Therefore, I support proactive action, knowing oneself and the enemy ensures victory in every battle.

Government agencies, company directories, the information collected here may not be detailed enough, possibly having three "no's": no contact name, no email, no product specifics, so it’s best to combine other network tools for a deeper understanding.

Search engines, this is my favorite, and I hope it becomes the favorite of all foreign trade colleagues. 3.2 How to deliver foreign trade development letters?

When we write love letters, after painstakingly writing them, we naturally hope that "the ideal he/she" opens and reads them carefully. Some tricks I used when I was younger include: 1. Getting the friend of the person you admire to pass it along, how to bribe them, well, I have my secrets, Buddha says: subtle, indescribable; Laozi says: the way can be described, but it is not the constant way. 2. Closely observing the habits of the person you admire, placing it inside items they often use rather than outside, avoiding others opening it, otherwise it could be embarrassing for both parties. 3. Making the medium beautiful, such as using colorful scented stationery...

For foreign trade development letters, the purpose of writing is naturally to hope that potential customers receive and open them. This requires you to be familiar with and use the delivery methods effectively. 1. Having old friends or clients recommend, note, don't cause "jealousy", once the vinegar jar is overturned, you'll have big trouble, possibly losing old friends or clients. So, it's essential to ensure that establishing a new friendly cooperative relationship with potential customers does not harmfully impact existing old friends or clients and can bring "benefits". What "benefits"? Hehe, think more, and "bribe" well. 2. Observing and understanding potential customers, sending to places they frequently check. 3. Creating a beautiful medium, for the most commonly used method of delivering foreign trade development letters, which is email, a good sending email address and a carefully considered email title are crucial for potential customers to open your email. Other aspects are to be supplemented, hehe, originally intended as a starting point.

3.3 How to write foreign trade development letters?

By now, if your love letter has been opened, congratulations, you've taken the first step in a long journey! However, if your letter is full of typos, coarse language, and empty words, oh dear! There's no hope. Remember the saying "words without taste, face to detest", be sure not to let "your ideal he/she" categorize your love letter under such evaluations.

Regarding foreign trade development letters, it's the same. Once your letter is opened, your intentions can be understood: Address, everyone likes hearing their name the most, so it's best not to use "Dear Sir/Madam" if possible, try to use the recipient's name; avoid letting the customer see at a glance that you are mass-mailing, putting their email among a bunch of recipient addresses.

Content, it should be concise yet substantial; attention should be paid to format and appropriate language; since the content of the development letter varies according to the understanding, analysis, and evaluation of potential customers, and there are numerous articles summarizing it, this article will not elaborate further.
