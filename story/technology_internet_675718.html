Wujiazhuang Village in Pangu Township, Qing County, has a history of more than 1000 years, with more than 900 residents. Due to various historical and geographical reasons, there has always been a lack of a flat bridge on the way out of the village. Villagers have to detour through the neighboring Zhouguantun Village and then onto National Highway 104, adding an extra 10 kilometers, which is not only inconvenient but also unsafe. "This cannot go on forever," said the elderly villager Wu Zhongjun, who was deeply concerned about the inconvenience of villagers' travel. "To get rich, build roads first - this is an old saying. I thought that while I am still able to walk, I would apply for the construction of this bridge and see it built," said Wu Zhongjun firmly.

In February 2009, Wu Zhongjun learned about the country's policies to help farmers build bridges, so he embarked on the journey to apply for the bridge construction. The approval process for the bridge project was very strict, requiring responsible individuals to handle everything from design drawings to providing materials, from topography surveys to collecting public opinions. Wu Zhongjun paid close attention to every detail and personally handled all matters. The villagers said that Wu Zhongjun's efforts were both touching and heart-wrenching.

Wu Zhongjun self-funded the hiring of staff from the Cangzhou Design Institute to draw up the plans, which facilitated the application to government departments. The staff of the Design Institute were deeply moved by the elder's persistence and enthusiasm, not only accelerating the work progress but also appropriately reducing the design fees. "At that time, my father often went to Qing County and Cangzhou, and even to Shijiazhuang. For long distances, he took taxis from the village, and for shorter ones, he rode his bicycle alone, almost obsessed," said Wu Zhongjun's daughter-in-law.

In June 2010, the bridge construction project for Wujiazhuang Village was successfully approved. To quickly secure the funds and facilitate public travel, Wu Zhongjun led the donation of 200,000 yuan, and the villagers enthusiastically responded, donating another 200,000 yuan. With the government's allocation, the construction funds for the bridge were quickly raised. Wu Zhongjun's family told reporters that although the elder ran a technology company, business was not going well at that time, and much of the 200,000 yuan donated was borrowed.
