Emotional reshuffling, sincere exit?

Be careful about the context. The term "reshuffle" on this card game frequently appears in this bizarre commercial era. Entrepreneurship reshuffle, product reshuffle, project reshuffle, institutional reshuffle, etc., which is a tangible expression at present. But the other kind of reshuffle in the intangible world is baffling: it's the increasingly thin emotional world. This emotion connects men and women, affects marriage and love; maintains brotherhood and sisterhood, relates to family affection; radiates social interactions, influences interpersonal friendship. Perhaps what makes you feel particularly helpless and confused is that when the material desire's huge waves erode the dam of emotion, drop by drop, (recommended excellent blog post: Chongchong Blog 2009 Emotional Foundation), over time, love depreciates, kinship begins to distance, and friendship becomes indifferent. The past vows of eternal love, the past blood thicker than water, the past brothers-in-arms, all three major components of the emotional world undergo a great reversal and tilt, presenting a reshuffled situation without your noticing. Under the circumstances of helplessness, you have no choice but to assess the situation, rethink and deal with this harsh reality of emotional reshuffle. Things are no longer tolerable for you to sigh secretly or feel sad alone, let alone be lucky and do nothing. In the face of the generalization and dilution of love leading to estrangement between couples, in the face of the indifference and numbness of kinship causing brothers to fall out due to disputes, in the face of the estrangement and vigilance of friendship making friends like strangers, you may start with surprise, then lamentation, and eventually have to choose to adapt to this change. And this adaptation, whether active or passive, whether taking the initiative or being forced, will make you feel deep inside: the emotional relationship now really needs to be reshuffled! This process is nothing more than adjusting the mindset and weighing the pros and cons. In a society where everything is leveraged by profit, the value orientation of emotions will undoubtedly change accordingly. That "sending a feather from afar, light gift but heavy righteousness" has become a distant legend; that "saving one from difficulty is better than building a seven-story pagoda" has become a vanished beautiful talk; that "being as close as siblings, intimate without gaps" has almost become a luxury; even that "unchanging heart even if the sea dries up and the rocks rot" kind of love has long been like a dream. Careful observation reveals that in the emotional world, the invisible wand of interest wildly swings, disrupting the existing social structure, ruining the healthy interpersonal ecology, and muddying the folk customs of our self-proclaimed land of propriety. It stirs things up so much that you can't help but follow its command to reshuffle your own emotional cards. Former sworn enemies can become close friends because of certain interests, which fits the saying "there are no permanent enemies, only permanent interests"; former close friends can snatch your love for some desires, fitting the idiom "cuckoo occupying the magpie's nest"; former difficult brothers, once successful, forget the promise of "if rich and noble, not forgetting each other", (recommended excellent blog post: Chongchong Blog 2009 If There Is Such an Emotion), echoing Mr. Lu Xun's earlier famous saying "a changed face means wealth"... As for those despicable acts like locals harming locals, friends harming friends, and brothers harming brothers, they are quite common. Without interest, wanting to turn swords into plowshares is undoubtedly a luxury; lacking interest, wanting to urgently help others is simply a fantasy. The world's wind thus declines, people's hearts thus degenerate. How many love flowers wither because of this, how many family green trees dry up because of this; how many friendship bridges collapse because of this. Then there is only reshuffling, and the result is that sincerity is expelled! Because on the emotional balance, weights of utilitarianism, snobbishness, and hypocrisy have already been placed. The heavier the weight, the more tilted the emotional balance, (recommended excellent blog post: Chongchong Blog 2009 Purity of Emotions), towards which side? All pointed by interest! Obviously, once emotions are tainted with the rust spots of interest and the copper smell of money, where is sincerity to be found?! Without sincerity, there is no true feeling, and without true feeling, how can the sky be clear and bright?! Think about our past teachings, often saying that capitalist countries lack love and family affection. However, after we stopped talking about "capitalist or socialist", with the opening of national doors, we realized that so-called capitalist countries, apart from having new-age lifestyles due to cultural differences, their love is richer than ours, their family affection is stronger than ours, and their friendship is more sincere than ours. Such unexpected comparisons are truly surprising. Here I want to say, their world is very colorful, but our world is very helpless. Such helplessness should be pondered upon! Amidst the re-shuffled emotional sky, many things I've heard, seen, and personally experienced make me calmer: learn to endure. In the past, there was no fame to compete for, today there is no profit to chase, and in the future, I won't look at everything through the lens of money. Do my best to exchange for a bowl of porridge and a meal; adhere to sincerity in dealing with all things. Regardless of honor or disgrace, only seek to create; regardless of truth or falsehood, only seek to be without regret. Continue to sincerely embrace my love, family affection, and friendship. Speaking of reshuffling, I can only remember the good of others for life, and forever engrave the difficulties and humiliations I've endured! While never forgetting the good friends, beneficial friends, and close friends who support me, I will forever not associate with villains, evil people, and small people! Because I always remember a proverb: you walk your sunny road, I'll walk my narrow bridge...
