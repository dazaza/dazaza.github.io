"We have already asked the Public Security Bureau, as the main responsible unit, to provide a specific detailed plan," said a person in charge of the Municipal Political and Legal Affairs Commission. He frankly admitted that "the transformation from temporary residence to permanent residence" is a complex system project that needs to be advanced steadily.

Xiamen's "Temporary to Permanent Residence"

7 years ago, there was already a conception

The latest data shows that the floating population in Xiamen has reached more than 2.2 million. The call for "temporary to permanent residence" has become increasingly high.

In fact, for Xiamen, "temporary to permanent residence" is not a new topic. Seven years ago, relevant persons in charge of the Public Security Bureau indicated to the Central Comprehensive Management Committee's Mobile Population Public Security Management Leading Group during their visit to Xiamen for research that in the future, Xiamen's residence permit will replace the temporary residence permit, "and the certificate holders will enjoy certain citizen benefits in aspects such as labor employment, work injury insurance, and medical insurance." Since then, efforts regarding "temporary to permanent residence" have never stopped.

Despite this, the "residence permit" remains just a conception to date. "This is not difficult to understand," said a seasoned media person familiar with the household registration system to our newspaper. "Let me give you an example that may not be entirely appropriate: a large number of mainland mothers rushing into Hong Kong would inevitably occupy local hospitals' public resources, naturally causing dissatisfaction among Hong Kong residents. Similarly, Xiamen, with its superior medical, educational, and residential resources, has a 'low-lying effect' on outsiders. Once the residence permit is fully implemented, mobile personnel will undoubtedly flood in; how to ensure that urban resources are not excessively overdrawn will be a great test of the decision-makers' governing wisdom."

On the other hand, Xiamen has legislative power. With the promotion of the residence permit, the previous "Provisions for the Registration and Management of Temporary Residents in Xiamen" will necessarily be abolished, and any amendment or even re-legislation must go through strict procedures. This also makes Xiamen's "temporary to permanent residence" more challenging than in other cities.

However, "the transformation from temporary to permanent residence is an inevitable direction, it's just a matter of time," revealed a person in charge of the Household Registration Department. Currently, the provincial Public Security Department and relevant departments are actively studying and promoting the residence permit.

"The relevant departments of the Provincial Department will determine the overall framework, and we will formulate details suitable for Xiamen based on its specific situation," said this informed person.

Achieving the Residence Permit System

Reflecting the Government's Service Function

From "temporary residence permit" to "residence permit", one character difference, spanning seven years. What charm does this small residence permit hold? The Notice perhaps can reveal some clues:

- In the future, policies and measures related to employment, compulsory education, skills training, etc., should not be linked to household registration status.

- Policies and measures that cause inconvenience for temporary residents in learning, working, and living should be thoroughly reviewed... those that need to be abolished should be firmly abolished.

- For rural populations who have settled in towns, they should be guaranteed the same rights as local urban residents.

"All of these ultimately boil down to 'gradually implementing the residence permit system for temporary residents,'" pointed out analysts. These regulations can, in a sense, be understood as "new policies during the transition period"; and the ultimate realization of the residence permit will also possess two major functions of social services and social security, thereby allowing mobile populations to enjoy necessary citizen-like treatments in aspects such as labor employment, medical insurance, children's education, house rental, car purchase, and home purchase, "which reflects the transformation of the government's role from focusing on management to focusing on service."

Citizen Treatment for Migrant Population

Xiamen Leads the Nation

Public reports show that as early as four years ago, Xiamen's police had envisioned that the future "residence permit" would be endowed with multiple functions - apart from serving as an identity recognition document for mobile personnel in Xiamen, it would also be a "family planning certificate," which could serve as proof for family planning registration and enjoyment of related discounts for mobile personnel; it would be a "work certificate," becoming a "pass" for mobile personnel seeking jobs in Xiamen; it would be a child enrollment voucher, allowing eligible mobile personnel to enjoy preferential treatment of free compulsory education for their children without tuition fees and miscellaneous fees; additionally, it would provide great convenience for mobile personnel in handling medical insurance, social security, identity verification in civil disputes, driver's license, and passport applications.

Some of these conceptions have been basically realized even before the issuance of the residence permit system - as a well-known "warm city" nationwide, Xiamen has always been at the forefront in granting citizen-like treatments to migrant populations.

In the compulsory education stage, currently, the ratio of migrant workers' children entering Xiamen's public schools is 70%, which will increase to 80% this year, and is expected to reach 90% by 2015; entering a public school means enjoying Xiamen's free compulsory education - no tuition fees, textbook fees, or stationery fees; even if entering a private school, meeting relevant conditions allows them to enjoy the above-free treatments.

In terms of labor employment, Xiamen has exempted migrant employees from charges when participating in job fairs and requires enterprises to pay "five-insurance-in-one" for migrant employees. Starting from January 1 this year, the medical insurance relationship of migrant employees coming to work in Xiamen can also be transferred and continued with employment.

"On this basis, what equalized treatments we can provide for migrants still require deeper research," said analysts.

Observation

The road to "leveling the gap" might still be long

Observers noticed that in recent years, the term "residence permit" has frequently appeared in central documents and meetings; many provinces and cities across the country have also gradually started practicing the residence permit system.

Shanghai was the first to try, launching the residence permit system in 2002; thereafter, Shenzhen, Chengdu, Kunming, Shenyang, and other places also began attempts at the "residence permit system"; starting March 1 this year, Hebei will promote the residence permit throughout the province, and by the first half of this year, Anhui will comprehensively implement the residence permit system...

In Fujian's process of transforming from temporary to permanent residence, Jinjiang took the lead - on July 1, 2011, Jinjiang officially implemented the "one-card pass" system for mobile population service management centered on the residence permit system.

"The differences between temporary residence permits, residence permits, and even household registrations, come down to resource allocation issues - everyone hopes to enjoy citizen-like treatments in areas with quality resources, but quality resources are limited. Ultimately, household registration has also become a 'resource,' and the competition for this 'strategic' resource sometimes makes people feel heartbroken."
