Tip: Are you suitable for a trailing wedding dress? (with pictures)_ Shenyang Wedding Photography Studio

by fj0926446 on 2010-04-01 18:00:46

Many trailing wedding dresses look very delicate on models, but when worn by brides, they appear cumbersome. This might be because the style of the dress does not match the bride's body shape and temperament. According to photographers from wedding studios, the train of a wedding dress can generally be divided into small trains (about 60-80 cm), medium trains (about 120-150 cm), and long trains (about 200-250 cm). Of course, there are even longer ones, which usually need to be custom-made. Wedding dresses with different lengths of trains are suitable for different wedding occasions, but more importantly, they should match brides of different figures.

Wedding dresses with small trains are comfortable to wear and easy to move in, making them suitable for brides of various figures. Medium trains are the best for walking down the aisle as they do not require flower girls to carry them and there is less risk of being stepped on, though they are not suitable for brides who are too petite. Long trains are every woman's princess dream since adolescence, and they are more suitable for tall brides. Shorter brides may feel overwhelmed by the length of the dress.

Key Point 1: Choose a spacious venue

Wearing a trailing wedding dress is very picky about the venue. If the venue is too narrow or the ground is uneven, or if there are dry branches and grass sticking together, it will definitely ruin the mood and cause regret. Therefore, the venue for the wedding is crucial; romantic beaches and sacred churches are top choices.

The wedding car should also be considered. According to designers, trailing wedding dresses are generally large, and regular sedans cannot fit them. Even if they are squeezed in, the long train will wrinkle after getting out of the car, significantly affecting the bride's image.

Key Point 2: Match appropriate accessories

The bride's accessories are the essence of a perfect wedding. Only jewelry that matches the wedding dress can make the bride shine brilliantly. According to wedding image designers, long trains are best paired with bold and striking accessories, and the bride's makeup can be exaggerated but not complicated.

Key Point 3: Rely on your partner

Does your other half know how hard you work to make the wedding more magnificent by wearing an extremely inconvenient large trailing wedding dress? Therefore, the groom should understand that he must become a diligent gentleman on the wedding day, happily "at the bride's disposal."

The bride also needs to learn some manners and postures because she will be moving around during the wedding. With a "tail" behind her, if she walks too fast or turns too sharply, she might accidentally trip over the train on her heel or other obstacles, which would be ungraceful. If choosing flower girls, try to use older ones who have enough strength.
