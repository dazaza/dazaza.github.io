The liver is the largest solid organ in the body. Traditional Chinese medicine metaphorically describes it as the general of the army, emphasizing the importance of the liver in traditional Chinese medicine. Relevant experts told reporters that when protecting the liver, using some methods from traditional Chinese medicine can be more effective.

Typically, there are five methods of liver protection in traditional Chinese medicine:

Firstly, TCM emphasizes emotional health. The five organs correspond to five emotions: anger, joy, thought, sorrow, and fear. Among them, the liver corresponds to anger, hence the saying "anger harms the liver." Therefore, attention should be paid to controlling one's emotions. Long-term depression or sudden bursts of anger can lead to imbalances in the liver’s qi and blood, affecting its function of free flow. When emotions are not smooth, one should find a way to vent negative emotions, such as confiding in a close friend about one's troubles, rather than suppressing them and harming the liver.

Secondly, TCM particularly emphasizes regular rest and routine living. In the theory of Zi Wu Liu Zhu (circadian rhythm), it is stated that the liver and gallbladder are most active from 11 PM to 3 AM, during which all the organs' blood passes through the liver, and its detoxifying function reaches its peak. Therefore, people should follow nature's course at this time and ensure adequate rest. Additionally, TCM believes that lying down returns blood to the liver. Thus, the best way to nourish the liver is to rest well. Avoid overworking, take timely breaks to replenish energy, allowing the liver to regulate qi and blood and eliminate fatigue.

Thirdly, TCM suggests dietary supplements to protect the liver. China's traditional medicine advocates the concept of five-colored diets, and the liver is associated with green color entering the liver meridian. Therefore, eating more green foods like spinach, kale, bok choy, cucumber, winter melon, and mung beans has effects of moistening dryness, soothing the liver, and nourishing blood. Moreover, the liver prefers sour tastes based on the principle of sour flavors benefiting the liver, so consuming vinegar regularly can benefit the liver and prevent colds. Some sour-tasting foods like hawthorn, cornus officinalis, and wolfberry have liver-protective effects. Those protecting the liver should also avoid spicy and greasy foods and limit alcohol consumption.
