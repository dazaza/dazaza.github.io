I want to briefly explain its origin. Initially, Zhougong interpreted dreams correctly by identifying a keyword from the dream arbitrarily and then using the economic or physical condition during the dream as the interpretation of the keyword. For example, dreaming of moving house. Here are two mistakes: first, failing to determine or analyze which event in life the dream refers to. Second, there are many keywords [referring to events or objects] in a dream—usually ten or twenty, at least seven or eight. For instance, dreaming of oneself fighting with a knife on the street corner against an enemy, there are oneself, the enemy, the knife, the street, and neighbors watching... In such a complex dream, there could be five or more events or objects forming keywords. If someone focuses on the neighbors interpreting this dream, why not focus on the enemy or the knife or the street instead? And how to determine which is or isn't the keyword? All based on personal feelings, without any basis... Why should the keyword represent the meaning of the dream...?

The specific origin of Zhougong Dream Interpretation is like this: If Zhang San dreams of a large snake and he has been very lucky recently, while Wang Wu dreams of a venomous snake and he has been unlucky lately. He collects another dream from Li Si, who also dreamed of a large snake and has been very lucky recently. At this point, the meaning of dreaming of a snake is defined as: dreaming of a snake brings good luck. If Li Si ends up being unlucky, the meaning changes to: dreaming of a snake is bad, contradicting the previous conclusion [or maybe related to marriage]. If he uses the majority rule, collecting 50 dreams involving snakes, where 8 turned out well and 6 turned out badly, the meaning becomes: dreaming of a snake turns out well. If the dream involves pigs or something else not a snake, then it could be good or bad depending on the collection situation. For example, among those 50 people, if 6 found money, 5 lost agricultural supplies, 4 bought old clothes, 3 made profits in business, 2 got rich... the meaning becomes: finding money. Suppose among those six people, three got rich and three made profits in business, and among these six people, some met friends or resolved early marriages or... anything could happen. If the good and bad collections are equal, it becomes a difficult problem. We don't know how to define whether it's good or bad or under what circumstances...
