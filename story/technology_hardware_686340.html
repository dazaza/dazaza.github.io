The most popular product, Amazon Kindle, may soon offer you a very different reading experience as it will carry advertisements for the first time. This step has alarmed literary traditionalists. Internet retailer Amazon is set to launch a new budget-priced Kindle e-reader next month that will display ads for products including beauty creams, vehicles, and credit cards as readers browse their favorite novels, reports The Scotsman.

The Kindle with special offers, priced $25 (15 pounds) less than the standard model as an incentive for buyers, will initially be available only in the USA. However, technology observers predict its arrival in the UK next year. Advertisers believe that Kindle could soon develop the concept of "profiling" readers and offering tailored ads for individual users based on their book choices.

However, the new device has come under fire from literary experts who believe that readers should not be targeted by companies trying to sell them products. Vanessa Robertson, owner of The Edinburgh Bookshop in Bruntsfield, said she was incensed against advertising on the Kindle. "I just don't think it's a good fit. You want to read a book and find yourself surrounded by blinking ads at the same time. I think it would be cheaper if people accept advertising to make it happen," she said.

Marion Sinclair, Chief Executive of the Scottish Publishing Forum added: "Keenest bibliophiles do not like seeing ads on their Kindle. People want to immerse themselves in their book and have an absolutely captivating reading experience - not constantly being reminded that they can save a few pence on their next latte."

The advertisements will replace the current screensaver images which consist of historical portraits of authors like Jane Austen. The device will also display ads and offers at the bottom of the home screen while readers rate their favorites, choosing between, for example, an image of a woman's face advertising Olay beauty cream and a photo of the product in a pot.

Companies advertising their products on Kindle include General Motors' Buick brand, Procter & Gamble's Olay cosmetic brand, and payment processor JPMorgan Chase promoting the Reward Visa Card.

Jay Marine, director of Amazon.com Kindle, insists that the ad platform is designed so that ads do not interfere with the reading experience. (ANI)
