Love, That Beautiful and Melancholic Sentiment

I laugh at you for wearing plastic shoes.

In the first year of junior high, you came to school wearing plastic shoes. Back then, you didn't care about such appearances.

It was when we went up the stairs together to a meeting, you were in front of me, and I saw your plastic shoes. They were made of plastic, resembling leather shoes, coffee-colored, and already quite old. At that time, I had a sense of looking down on you; I thought you were outdated. Ironically, I was just as unsophisticated back then, but I hadn't noticed it myself.

Later, I told my desk mate about this, and the two of us laughed without restraint. Back then, I was the best student in the class, proud and conceited, not putting anyone in my mind. But who would have thought later that I would actually like this boy who wore plastic shoes. And even more tragically, perhaps he never knew of my liking. It was always me walking this glamorous yet melancholic adventure alone, without the real you by my side...

You took off your plastic shoes and became the top student in the class

Finally one day, you found out about my mockery of you. You said nothing, did not take off your shoes because of that, and continued to wear them for some time. Later, I saw you come to school wearing white cloth shoes, so I asked you why you weren't wearing those shoes anymore? You casually replied, "They're broken."

From then on, I never laughed at you again.

However, in the spring of that year, I lost to you, completely lost! My failure was utterly abysmal. Indeed, your math score was several dozen points higher than mine, and I was left far behind. Everyone saw your success and my failure! Thus, my status plummeted, while you transformed into the top student in the class.

Since then, I've been unable to recover, but I fell for you

That one failure dealt a severe blow to my confidence. From then on, I couldn't recover, but you kept advancing, entering the top ten in the grade.

Yet, I didn't feel a single bit of jealousy towards you. Instead, I thought you were very capable.

As I experienced these ordinary stories, I still didn't realize that I was just an extremely ordinary girl. I viewed myself with the arrogance created by my former glory, thinking there was nothing wrong with me. Years later, when I finally realized my ordinariness, I understood why you didn't like me. These understandings, however, were so powerless and pale!

Your excellence made everyone look at you differently, including me. Gradually, I felt you were excellent, handsome, and good. Love, should probably begin slowly from these feelings.

Thus, I thought I liked you.

In fact, everything was quite laughable. My feelings were my own, not yours. Even more tragically, I thought you liked me too. My illusion led me further into this glamorous yet melancholic adventure...

You wrote your first love letter, but the recipient wasn't me

In the summer of the second year of junior high, I learned of my unrequited love. Before that, I always looked at you with shining eyes, copied your homework, because you sat behind me, because you were the top student in the class, all of which seemed so natural.

But I was immersed in my own fantasy world, ignoring the fact that your attention wasn't solely on me. At that time, I didn't know, nor could I have imagined, that you actually liked her.

My arrogance, my illusions, your indifference, and your lack of understanding - they weren't opposing directions.

If we were rays, I extended towards you, but you extended towards another direction. No matter how I tried to reach you, your direction, your gaze, never turned back to notice my existence...

Why do you have to like her...my best friend...I don't even have the slightest intention to compete with her...

Disappointment, confusion, heartache, self-mockery.

Afterward, I no longer smiled at you or borrowed your homework, but I acted like I didn't care in front of her. My coldness was no match for your affection for her. You could skip naps to tutor her in physics, and you could give her things without caring what others might say. No one understood my coldness, and it meant nothing to you. Just like how my affection meant nothing to you, because you didn't understand.

You wrote your first love letter in life, gave it to the first girl you liked, but the recipient wasn't me...

You gave her flowers with the same name as you, and I quietly left as you took pictures

When I went to your house, I asked if there were any flowers with the same name as you. I thought your name sounded nice, and I really liked it. You said yes, and I was overjoyed, asking for one. She also wanted one, and you immediately brightened up and agreed happily.

But seeing your change, I made an excuse saying I didn't want one.

How could I bear planting flowers with the same name as you after knowing? How could I plant different flowers given by you to the person you liked at the same time?

Years later, seeing those flowers brought forth vivid red sadness.

Later, when we talked about taking pictures, seeing both of you, I had no mood at all and quietly left.

Later, when I saw your picture together, my heart didn't know what to feel, pain? Suddenly, I remembered that before this, there was a group photo of me, you, and her in the literary club, but I had never seen that photo... The only photo taken with you was our junior high graduation photo.

Thinking about it, this kind of unrequited love should end, right?

I thought I would never see you again, but fate arranged it this way

After you became the top student in the class and my complete failure, I lost all confidence to rise again. In the third year of junior high, I played around a lot and didn't focus much on studying.

I knew everything about you and her. She told me she wouldn't be with you.

One afternoon, in the dormitory, she asked me if I liked you. It must have been a question she had thought about for a long time. I nodded, looking at the bamboo-covered mountain outside the door, its shells shining brightly under the blue sky, and said, "It's all in the past, it's all old news."

For the high school entrance exam, I never thought I could get into the Number One Middle School.

Indeed, I couldn't, but you did. However, the teacher approached me, saying I was only a few points short, and paying some money would allow me to enter. My mother loved me and decided to let me go.

In the campus of Number One Middle School, I saw you.

And you actually greeted me!

Who would have known! You never used to greet me!

I thought we'd be in different schools, I thought I'd never see you again, but fate arranged it this way.

You couldn't be together, but I ended up going home with you

She told me, during the National Day holiday, you held her hand.

At that moment, her tone was somewhat hesitant, fearing to hurt me, I knew. I just asked, "Then what?" She said there was nothing afterward...

At that time, I believed what she said, but now I don't believe it. Something must have happened, she was just afraid to hurt me.

But my heart was still sad.

Ultimately, you two couldn't be together, I don't know why... But somehow, something feels missing... What is it, I don't know...

After the college entrance exam, I walked away alone, a box of books, heavy. Moving it alone, with a lot of luggage, I felt helpless and wanted to cry.

In high school, I didn't forget you, but it was a kind of giving up that couldn't be forgotten.

While waiting for the bus outside, I actually saw you!

You dragged your luggage toward me, greeted me, waited for the bus, and silence ensued.

But my heart was still excited.

On the bus, you helped watch my luggage, and I still thought you were good.

When you arrived, got off the bus, and waved goodbye to me, I watched your retreating back with reluctance, letting myself come to terms with it.

Late Courage and Confession

Because of you, I started writing so-called novels. Those fabricated stories clearly bore traces of you, your story, my story, your story with her, our story... Now I realize how much you touched me! You left such a deep mark on my youthful years, hard to erase. But it wasn't you, it was my liking, abandonment, and inability to forget someone in such beautiful times, along with all the joys and sorrows.

Shouldn't such a story be over by now?

But that time when I went home, I saw her. She gave me your number, and it seemed she was hinting at something.

When I was wounded all over, when I was trying hard to forget something, I impulsively told you about my feelings for you.

I didn't expect anything, yes, I've always been without hope.

My phone received no messages, one day, two days, three days...

The result I knew, exactly as expected.

I finally realized that I had no intersection in your memories, I might not even be an ordinary character in your story. From beginning to end, it was always me, foolishly walking on that road alone, relying on imagination and sorrow to complete my journey. Tragically, you were always absent, and I was always absent from your voyage, absent from your sight...

Deleting your number, truly letting go.

But I can't forget, in those beautiful times, that beautiful and melancholic sentiment.
