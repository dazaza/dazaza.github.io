Microsoft to Acquire Stake in Facebook, Sources Say

by huahuaniu on 2007-09-25 10:51:47

Sina Technology News, September 25th, Beijing Time - According to foreign media reports, insiders have recently revealed that Microsoft is in negotiations with social networking site Facebook to purchase a portion of its shares. Based on Microsoft's offer, Facebook's total value could reach $10 billion or more.

Insiders disclosed that Microsoft is in preliminary talks with Facebook to acquire 5% of the company's shares at a price ranging from $300 million to $500 million. Facebook, which was founded three and a half years ago, has already received nearly $41 million in investments from venture capital firms and other private investors. Facebook declined to comment on the insider's statement, calling it merely "rumors and speculation."

Currently, it seems unlikely that Facebook will be completely sold. Mark Zuckerberg, the 23-year-old founder of Facebook, has repeatedly emphasized that he hopes for Facebook to remain independent. In 2006, he rejected Yahoo's acquisition offer of $1 billion. Meanwhile, Facebook has been seeking more funding from venture capital firms to expand its workforce and support other expansion plans.

Facebook's current annual revenue has exceeded $100 million, and analysts predict that the company will conduct its initial public offering in 2008 or 2009. If the negotiations between Microsoft and Facebook are true, Google might also join the bidding competition. Two years ago, Microsoft and Google fiercely competed for the acquisition of AOL shares, with Google emerging victorious. Google acquired 5% of AOL's shares for $1 billion and became its exclusive advertising service provider.

According to previous agreements, Microsoft has already started providing online advertising services for Facebook in the United States, with this agreement set to expire in 2011. Insiders revealed that Microsoft and Facebook are considering expanding their existing cooperation agreement to cover other regions around the world. Facebook is the second largest social networking site in the United States, only behind MySpace under News Corporation. By allowing third parties to develop Facebook applications, the website has further enhanced its appeal, with its user base now exceeding 30 million people.

After being rejected in last year's attempt to acquire Facebook, Yahoo recently launched a new social service called "Mash" and signed an advertising cooperation agreement with British social networking site Bebo. Meanwhile, there are rumors that Google will also launch a new social network product by the end of this year. Google issued a statement on Monday saying: "We are always looking for new ways to help users connect with others, share information, and express themselves. However, so far, we do not have any relevant information to announce." (Martin)
