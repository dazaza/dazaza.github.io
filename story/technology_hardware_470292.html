Urgent!!! My 3-month-old baby rolled over and fell off the bed.

What should I do??? My baby is only three months old and he/she rolled over and fell off the bed. Our bed is quite high. What should I do?? I'm so worried! Please teach me what to do! How can I check if my baby is okay?

Step One: Check for any ruptured or bleeding wounds

At this moment, Mom must remain calm, comfort the child first, then check if there are any ruptures or bleeding on the part of the body that was hit, or if there are any other abrasions or cuts. If the child has a minor skin wound, stop the bleeding with a sterilized gauze, then clean the wound with physiological saline water, and apply external ointment. If the wound is deep and the bleeding does not stop, you should seek medical attention immediately and inform the doctor that the injury was caused by a fall.

Step Two: Look for signs of fractures

If a fracture occurs, there will be local swelling. You need to stabilize the fracture with a wooden board or thick cardboard before rushing to the hospital for treatment. Since patients with fractures fear random movements, which could cause more serious bone displacement, you must carefully examine the situation before picking up the child.

Step Three: Observe for symptoms of brain injury

After the child falls, if any of the following symptoms appear, it may indicate signs of brain injury, and further medical examination is necessary.

1. Pale complexion.

2. Vomiting.

3. Seizures.

4. Unconsciousness.

5. Unable to eat.

6. Sleeps soundly but cannot be woken up, or when woken up, appears drowsy.

7. Lack of energy.

It's important to note that even if these symptoms don't appear on the day of the fall, parents still need to observe the child for three days to ensure there's no brain injury. During these three days, try to let the child rest in bed as much as possible, and after the child falls asleep at night, try waking them up 1-2 times to make sure they're just sleeping soundly rather than being unconscious due to a brain injury.

Step Four: Care for bruises

Typically, falling from a height easily causes bruises, and sometimes even a large bump on the head! After confirming there's no brain injury, fractures, or bleeding emergencies, bruises or swollen bumps can be taken care of at home.

First, apply an ice towel to the affected area for 30 minutes to reduce swelling and pain, and slow down subcutaneous bleeding. After 24 hours, switch to warm compresses to speed up the body's absorption of bruised blood. Warm compress three times a day, each time for 15 minutes, then rub the injured area with bruise-removal ointment to reduce the formation of hard lumps. Usually, the swelling will go down in about a week.
