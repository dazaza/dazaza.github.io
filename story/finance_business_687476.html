I am not only fond of Chanel handbags but also interested in Coco Chanel herself and like to seek out the things she left behind. A few days ago, I went to Paris and visited Coco's apartment. The small apartment was where she rested and received guests, while she slept at the Ritz Hotel across from her apartment. In her apartment, I could see many of her sources of inspiration. For example, the Chanel 2.55 was inspired by the cushions on her sofa. She rarely kept old artifacts; she was a lady who pursued the future. In her view, the past is the past, and we need not mention it anymore. After her death, the fabric she used to make clothes, manuscripts, and clothing were not preserved. Many of her belongings ended up in the hands of collectors, and the House of Chanel had to buy them back from auctions. They even exchanged Karl Lagerfeld's designs for Coco's. She never married in her lifetime and liked to collect things in sets. The small apartment is the lifeline of the House of Chanel. If you want to know more about the inspiration behind Chanel bags, you can visit there.
