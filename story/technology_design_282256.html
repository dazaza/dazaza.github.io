http://shop.shichenghy.com What is known as a Blackberry, is a wireless handheld email solution terminal device introduced by Canadian company Research In Motion (RIM).

Because it uses a standard QWERTY English keyboard, which looks like the seeds on the surface of a strawberry, it is named "Blackberry". Additionally, in later products, this device also incorporated phone functions, and thus domestically it is generally referred to as a "Blackberry Phone".

However, what is commonly known as a Blackberry device does not merely refer to a phone. It refers to the Push Mail real-time email service introduced by the Canadian RIM (Research in Motion) company, which includes server (mail settings), software (operating interface), and terminals (phones).

The "Blackberry" (Blackberry) mobile mail device is based on two-way paging technology. This device, combined with RIM's servers, relies on specific server software and terminals, compatible with existing wireless data links, achieving the dream of sending and receiving emails anytime and anywhere across North America. This device does not attract attention with amazing pictures and color screens, or even lack a speaker. After the "9/11" incident, because Blackberry timely delivered disaster scene information, it sparked a craze in the U.S. for owning a Blackberry terminal.

The most important principle behind the success of Blackberry - targeting high-level white-collar workers and business people, providing an integrated solution for enterprise mobile office. Enterprises have a large amount of information that needs to be processed instantly, and when traveling, they also need a wireless mobile office device. An enterprise only needs to install a mobile gateway, a software system, using the mobile platform to achieve seamless connection, allowing employees to use their phones for office work anytime, anywhere. Its greatest convenience is providing the email PUSH function.

Since its establishment in 1984, early products launched by RIM were RIM 850/950 and RIM 857/957. Their appearance was very similar to familiar pagers, just larger in size, with more keys, and had the function of sending and receiving emails.

Subsequent models such as the 5790/5810 and 6210/6280 series already looked no different from current Blackberry products in terms of appearance. They only had monochrome screens at the time, 16M memory, and lacked call functions.

The 6230 should be considered a groundbreaking product in the Blackberry lineup. Starting with the 6230, Blackberry officially supported call functions, becoming a true "phone" in the real sense. The contemporaries of the 6230 also included models like the 6510/6750/6710.

Afterward, there were the currently popular models in China, such as the 7230/7290, and the 7100/7130/8700 series. These generations of products generally came equipped with color screens, and the 7130 and 8700 series also provided support for polyphonic ringtones and MP3 ringtones. This marked RIM's determination to develop the Blackberry series into phones with more entertainment functions.

Thus, in the latest Blackberry models 8100/8800/8830 and the newest 8300, we see a series of fancy features that previous Blackberry phones did not have: storage card support, 2 million pixel camera, flash, MP3, video playback, and other multimedia functions that attract users.

Three stages of Blackberry phone development

From the appearance, the "Blackberry" product is more like a communicator or PDA with call functions rather than a regular phone. Using Push Mail technology, users can send and receive emails via this device at any time.

Actually, associating cell phones with email was not RIM's original idea. The earliest connection between cell phones and the Internet was around the year 2000, when ICQ still supported sending free text messages to cell phones through ICQ. Then, applying for a free email account, they supported notifying users' cell phones after receiving new emails. This stage is what the industry calls the second phase.

First stage: Users log into the internet to browse, check, and send/receive emails.

Second stage: Users can receive "internet email arrival notifications" via SMS.

Third stage: Users directly receive, read, and process emails from their internet mailbox via their mobile email box.

[Edit Section] [Entry into China]

Blackberry had never been officially released in China until early 2005, when some domestic cell phone enthusiasts started boldly "tasting" the "flavor" of Blackberry.

Soon, through word-of-mouth, Blackberry phones quickly attracted a large number of fashion-conscious cell phone enthusiasts due to their unique design and affordable prices.

At the same time, several well-known domestic digital forums, HIPDA and HIPALM, also opened Blackberry phone sections, making Blackberry phones popular nationwide in less than a year. As a result, domestic merchants began to illegally import Blackberry phones from overseas markets to the domestic market — what we call "gray market goods."

By the end of 2006, Blackberry services had entered China, and RIM had reached a cooperation agreement with China Mobile. Before long, we will be able to buy legitimate Blackberry phones from RIM in the domestic market.

Meanwhile, China Mobile has also launched Blackberry email services, but currently, these services are only available to corporate clients, offering the following two packages:

Package One: Monthly service fee of 398 yuan, with 50MB of GPRS data included per user each month; additional communication fees are charged at 0.01 yuan per KB beyond the included data.

Package Two: Monthly service fee of 598 yuan, with 400MB of GPRS data included per user each month (including 2MB of international GPRS roaming); additional communication fees are charged at 0.01 yuan per KB beyond the included data.

It is clear from the pricing that the current Blackberry email service is still out of reach for individual users.

[Edit Section] [Purchase Precautions]

After Blackberry became extremely popular in 2006, many merchants saw the potential of this market. However, supply could not keep up with the demand from Blackberry enthusiasts, leading to a large number of refurbished Blackberries appearing on the market. Gray market phones themselves are not scary, but refurbished ones are. The emergence of these machines essentially marked the complete unavailability of new units for models like the 7230/7290/7100T, which were widely popular among domestic players.

At the same time, a large number of uninformed, non-cell phone enthusiast consumers blindly followed trends, purchasing Blackberries without fully understanding them. Due to poor manual dexterity and inability to adapt to Blackberry's unique operating system, they began reselling previously purchased Blackberry phones, once again sparking a buying frenzy for second-hand Blackberry phones in the market.

As a result, the market became flooded with various sources of Blackberry phones, including original gray market units, used units, and refurbished units, leaving ordinary players unable to distinguish between them and hesitant to make purchases.

On platforms like Taobao, some unscrupulous merchants even sold poorly refurbished Blackberry units, claiming them to be 14-day-old units, with prices dropping as low as 200-500 yuan.

In summary, now is not a good time to purchase a Blackberry.

If you are confident in your technical skills and consider yourself a seasoned cell phone player who just wants to try out Blackberry for fun, you may opt for a cheaper refurbished unit to familiarize yourself with the system. You can then reconsider purchasing a legitimate unit once RIM officially launches its products in the domestic market.

If you are just a regular user who has never used other smartphone systems and frequently makes calls or sends texts in daily life, you should carefully consider before attempting to use a Blackberry phone.

BlackBerry Condition Identification

1. Shell. New machines have tightly joined shells, smooth seams without burrs. Refurbished machines usually use imitation original shells, with uneven seams, burrs, and loose closures with significant gaps between the upper and lower parts of the shell. During identification, you can try inserting your little finger's nail into the seam between the upper and lower covers of the phone. If it can fit inside, it is a refurbished machine. For phones whose shells look like new with a matte finish and small particles on the surface, avoid purchasing them at all costs. The paint on these shells is sprayed by JS, and over time, the paint will peel off (the dust particles on the shell are caused by spraying without a vacuum environment);

2. New machines emit a faint sandalwood fragrance when opened. If it's a refurbished machine, JS typically beautifies the body, making the exterior smooth but insufficiently dry. Opening the cover or removing the battery reveals a rubber smell, plastic smell, or chemical agent odor;

3. Keyboard and data line interface. Due to long-term plugging and unplugging of chargers and data lines, refurbished machines often show hard-to-remove black scratches on the charging interface. The keyboards of refurbished machines are mostly from old machines or imitations. These keyboards are softer and lack the resilience of new machine keyboards;

4. Charging test. The charging time should ideally be longer, preferably over 10 minutes. Many refurbished machines will cut off power if charged for too long, which is a very effective way to determine if it's refurbished;

5. SIM card chip contact points and battery contact points. These contact points are generally irreplaceable by JS. If it's a refurbished machine, the SIM card chip contact points will definitely have clear friction marks, looking shiny due to excessive wear. If it's a new unused chip, the contact point surface should be matte;

6. Fixed screws. Mainly check if there are obvious scratches on the screw and knife slot interfaces. Look for signs of tampering under the two rubber pads below the inner screen.

7. Call Quality. Test the machine with your own SIM card, call your friends, preferably a landline, and ask how the call quality is, whether the voice is clear, if there are any noises or current sounds, and if the volume suddenly drops;

8. Price. Since the components of refurbished machines are mostly older products with inferior quality, they are also priced lower. If you encounter a price that is absurdly low and the seller claims it's a gray market product, it is likely a refurbished machine.
