Looking ahead - Barca vows to avoid falling into the same trap as 3 years ago, Messi rests and misses the semi-final.

by qinglai888 on 2009-12-17 22:49:00

Prospect - Barcelona's reform vows not to fall into the same trap as 3 years ago; Messi rests and will not participate in the semi-final.

Barcelona will begin their Club World Cup journey today, with the first match against Atlante generally considered an easy game. As Guardiola said: "In which competition is Barcelona not the favorite?" Besides having similar kits (both red and blue), the two teams also share similar football philosophies. Atlante's coach Jose Cruz is a loyal follower of former Mexican national team coach Lavolpe, who is also a coach that Guardiola greatly admires.

In predictions, Barcelona is so favored that the Club World Cup organizers have decided to let Barcelona wear their traditional red and blue kit, while Atlante has to wear indigo blue. Mexican media commented that this color is also nice, reminding people of the Caribbean Sea. In fact, the two teams originally had an agreement to decide on the kit color by drawing lots.

Atlante is not very concerned about this because they only brought three fans and one journalist. Atlante was founded in 1916 and for a long time was known for representing the lower class. However, since entering the 1980s, the club gradually fell into the hands of big capitalists, moving further away from ordinary people. Currently, the club is still in difficulty; the team doesn't even have a locker room, and they can only train on a golf course.

However, Atlante's players are relatively young and play good football. If Barcelona underestimates them, it will make a big mistake. Barcelona's Mexican center-back Marcos said: "Atlante's lineup is quite good, especially the team's spine is very perfect." He is very familiar with Atlante's goalkeeper and forward. Interestingly, Atlante's forward is also named Rafael Marcos, sharing the same name. "The game will definitely not be easy," Marcos reminded his teammates.

The best compliment for Atlante comes from their opponent's mouth. Guardiola never takes any opponent lightly or looks down on any match. The Barcelona coach stated: "If we can't get past the semi-final, then there won't be a final either. You have to earn the opportunity to participate in the final on the pitch; everything else is empty."

Barcelona's trophy cabinet is filled with various trophies, but it lacks a 'Club World Cup' trophy. Historically, Barcelona has participated in the Club World Cup twice (including its predecessor, the Toyota Cup). In 1993, they lost 1-2 to Brazil's Sao Paulo, and in 2007, Barcelona lost 0-1 to Brazil's Internacional. If Barcelona can win the cup this time, then a major blank in the club's history will be filled. Moreover, Barcelona could achieve the miraculous feat of winning six titles. In 2009, Barcelona already won five major trophies: La Liga, Copa del Rey, UEFA Champions League, Spanish Super Cup, and UEFA Super Cup. If they can also win the Club World Cup, this team will create a miracle unprecedented in history.

However, Guardiola does not want to talk about the possibility of achieving six titles right now. "I am more interested in studying how to defeat Atlante in football terms. If we don't focus our attention on football itself, we will make a mistake. If we cannot prepare well for the semi-final match, then it is entirely possible that we won't reach the final."

Marcos also said that Barcelona must not repeat the mistake of arrogance. In 2006, Barcelona defeated Mexico's America 4-0 in the semi-final, making the whole team overly confident. As a result, they were defeated by Brazil's Internacional in the final. Guardiola also used the 1992 loss to Brazil's Sao Paulo as a reference: "At that time, the entire team, including the head coach, did not know how to win this trophy." Psychological counseling began on Tuesday, and Guardiola looked serious. Barcelona's players also didn't think there was anything wrong with seriously preparing for the match against a weaker team.

Finally, it is worth noting that Messi's absence will open the door to history for Pedro. Although the Argentine attended the team's normal training yesterday, the possibility of Guardiola arranging him to start in the semi-final is still low. Pedro said: "I hope to participate in the important matches of the Club World Cup. If I can score and be written into history, it would be perfect. I am looking forward to scoring a goal, but the most important thing is for the team to win." At present, Pedro has scored goals in five competitions: La Liga, Copa del Rey, Spanish Super Cup, UEFA Super Cup, and the Champions League. If he scores in the Club World Cup, he will create a legend of scoring in all six competitions the team participates in during a season.
