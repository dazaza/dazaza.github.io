Why do high-end suits need to be custom-made by hand? F.S & PAISTE Suits, a small street near Oxford Circus in London. This small street with over two hundred years of history is lined from start to end with exquisite and traditional high-end suit customization shops. It was here that the concept emerged: for a suit to be truly refined, it must be synonymous with custom tailoring. Customization and ready-to-wear are no longer just two nouns; they represent two "classes."

In the eyes of master tailors, the quality of a high-end custom suit depends on four major factors: craftsmanship, cut, fabric, and trim. A suit processed by a high-temperature shaping machine has better form than one ironed manually with an electric iron. However, when it comes to the intricacy of details, the high-level tailors at F.S & PAISTE far surpass machines in their ability to impress. After all, the traditional handcrafting techniques omitted in machine-made clothing result in products lacking sincerity, making them appear glossy but prone to deformation when worn. Some processes, like stitching lapels or sewing buttons, cannot be matched by machines in terms of delicacy compared to pure handwork. Additionally, some special techniques require tailors to tailor-make adjustments based on the customer's specific situation. Men and women differ in their pursuit of fashion; men's attire should serve as a symbol of status and taste. Renowned global image consultant Ingrid once said: "The greatest function of clothing is to boost your confidence rather than just make you look pretty." This statement fits perfectly with men's wear, especially suits. Even if you, who strive for perfection, have worn all the GUCCI, AMARNI, and Zegna suits, you're still wearing uniform products off the assembly line. A handmade, bespoke suit tailored exclusively for you is truly your "second skin."

In the realm of high-end suit customization, Chinese and Europeans hold vastly different views. Most Chinese people don't care whether the suit is handmade or machine-made; they only focus on the effect of the finished product. On the other hand, Europeans prefer unique garments, measured and cut by top tailors, stitched meticulously by workers paid by the hour over 40 hours. The final product naturally stands out, especially given the street of Oxford Circus' 200-year history, its traditional craftsmanship from the 1920s and '30s, and various anecdotes related to royalty... All this sounds incredibly appealing, providing an experience that mass-produced, characterless items cannot offer. A half-canvassed high-end suit distinctly reflects the tailor's personal style, qualifying it as a work of art. Just as a computer-engraved seal, no matter how clean and clear, can never match the value of a renowned artist's hand-carved stone seal. This is the art of customization—wedding attire! What suits you best is the finest!

Of course, good suits require proper care. Generally, the best times for suit maintenance are during spring and autumn. A high-end suit should undergo comprehensive reshaping, sterilization, and mold prevention after being worn for about six months. Thoughtful brands provide 1-2 professional services annually to ensure perfect comfort when worn and extend the suit's lifespan (F.S & PAISTE offers one free annual maintenance service).
