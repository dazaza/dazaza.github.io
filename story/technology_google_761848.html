Exciting and Diverse: A Preview of Google I/O 2013

by geekzhang on 2013-05-13 11:07:53

As Google's annual developer conference, the 5th edition of Google I/O is undoubtedly a feast for developers and tech enthusiasts. Last year at the Google I/O conference, Android 4.1, Nexus 7, and Nexus Q were released, while the high-altitude arrival of Google Glass caused quite a stir.

From May 15th to 17th, Google I/O will be held at the Moscone Center in San Francisco, where Google will deliver a keynote speech lasting three hours. Apparently, all of Google's new products and services for the next year will be included.

No Key Lime Pie, continuing with Jelly Bean

Although Android 4.0 has been around for a year and it's time for a major update, there are still no signs indicating that Android 5.0 (Key Lime Pie) will make an appearance at this year's I/O conference. Instead, there are many indications that Android 4.3, still under the Jelly Bean codename, will be released.

The confirmed information so far is that Android 4.3 will include Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) functionality. By opening up APIs, developers can create many derivative applications on this feature, such as health-related apps like heart rate monitors.

According to information from AndroidPolice, the latest Google Play Games service package file shows that Google plans to launch a gaming center for the Android platform. From the leaked files, Google Play Games will include features such as multiplayer, leaderboards, achievements, and cloud game saving. Clearly, Google has realized that social gaming has become an indispensable part of mobile platforms.

More details about Google Glass

Last year at the I/O conference, Google Glass stole the show, and this year's event is expected to reveal more details about Google Glass. In the past few weeks, developers have already started creating applications for Google Glass, and we might see some demonstrations by developers at this conference.

Nexus 7 upgrade, less likely for Nexus phones

The launch of the Nexus 7 last year ignited the small tablet market and achieved success. Google is very likely to announce an upgraded version of the Nexus 7 tablet at this year's conference, ahead of the next generation of the iPad mini to capture the market.

Speculations suggest that the new Nexus 7 will be equipped with Qualcomm's APQ8064 processor, a 1080p display, a 5-megapixel camera, and support for wireless charging. In terms of design, it may adopt a narrow bezel similar to the iPad mini, making the device lighter and thinner. As for price, it will likely remain at $199.

In addition, there are rumors that the 11-inch tablet Nexus 11, manufactured by Samsung, might appear at the I/O conference.

Regarding the fifth-generation Nexus phone, given that the Nexus 4 was released in late October last year, we believe the likelihood of a Nexus 5 is slim.

Motorola's X Phone?

Since Google acquired Motorola Mobility, rumors about Google entering the smartphone manufacturing business have persisted. Since last year when the phone known as the X Phone surfaced, various rumors have continued. It was previously reported that this phone, manufactured by Motorola, would offer customization services, including waterproofing, dustproofing, and long standby times.

Babel communication software

Leaked information disclosed that Google will launch a cross-platform communication app called Google Babel. This app aims to end the fragmentation of Google's existing communication apps, including GTalk, Google Voice, and Google+ Hangouts. At this I/O conference, we might see these services integrated together.

Android@Home may make a comeback

At the 2011 Google I/O conference, the Android@Home project excited the public. Its goal was to build a home automation network system controlled by Android devices. However, disappointingly, we still haven't seen any meaningful progress thus far.

In their I/O predictions, AndroidPolice believes that Android@Home will make a comeback and lists two pieces of evidence: in the Google search app, there is a file named "at_home_card.xml" whose function is to control light switches. Additionally, in the Android 4.2.2 update list, mention was made of Android@Home mesh network devices. Moreover, online information also indicates that Android@Home is still under development.

It is speculated that Android@Home might integrate Google Now and Knowledge Graph, which aligns with Google's vision of building the next generation of search engines to provide people with real-time and accurate information.

Apart from the above speculations, applications and services like Google Maps and Chrome are very likely to receive updates.
