College students run online stores with annual Taobao agency sales reaching more than 1.7 million yuan

by anonymous on 2011-03-28 09:50:13

Liu Xingqi was originally a migrant worker in Xuzhou doing decoration work. When he saw people in his village selling assembled furniture online, he came back and bought a computer, installed an internet line, and opened this "Tu Laomao Home" online store. He found some Korean pictures to upload online and made furniture according to those pictures. Due to the low price, plus rising living costs, more people were shopping online, and Old Liu never expected how good the business would be, making over ten thousand yuan a month.

Liu Xingqi: I feel that the business is much better. When prices go up, all the prices in the city rise too. Once they rise, people go on Taobao to search and buy because it's cheaper here.

Now, Liu's son doesn't work in Xuzhou anymore. He follows his father and has opened another online store as a Taobao agent. His wife takes care of cooking and shipping orders. People even worry about talking to him, fearing it might delay his business. Because the business is so good, they have expanded their furniture production in the yard. Now, in this small village in Northern Jiangsu with over a thousand households, more than 400 households have opened online stores. The business is getting better and better, and young people who used to work outside have all returned home to open online stores. After graduating from Nanjing Forestry University, Sun Po worked as a draftsman for a company in Xiamen. This year, in the first half, he also returned home and opened an online store, logging onto the online store supply guide.

Liu’s Son: The income there was okay, but the cost of living was high. At that time, I could only earn 3,000 yuan, and by the end of the month, it was basically gone.

Reporter: So what about this time when you came back?

Liu’s Son: The expenses at home are not high, and I can basically earn and spend accordingly.

Reporter: How much do you earn per month?

Liu’s Son: Around three or four thousand yuan.

However, this village had no foundation in furniture making, and all these changes happened within just two or three years. The one who brought about this change is this 28-year-old young man, Sun Han. In 2006, Sun Han quit his job at the local mobile company and returned to his rural home. Like many ordinary people in the city, he opened an online store selling accessories as a Taobao agent, but the business wasn't good. In 2007, Sun Han visited IKEA in Shanghai, and everything changed.

Sun Han, resident of Dongfeng Village, Shajitown, Suining County, Jiangsu Province:

Sun Han: Previously, I visited IKEA in Shanghai just for fun. I noticed its solid wood furniture and panel furniture; their craftsmanship was simple, yet they sold them at a high price, and they were quite popular. Then I bought a few pieces, replicated the styles, asked carpenters to process them, uploaded the pictures, and started selling online.

At that time, Sun Han was the only one on the entire network selling simple assembled furniture, and the products were highly favored online. Sun Han's annual earnings as a Taobao agent reached over 1.7 million yuan. Nearby villagers were moved. Some came to ask about the secrets of opening online stores, and Sun Han taught them. Since the village had a foundation in the recycled plastic business, Sun Han's model was quickly replicated. Within a short period, 10, 100, and then thousands of online stores sprang up vigorously. Sun Han's success was replicated over 1200 times — over 1200 online stores were opened by the town's 60,000 residents. Now Sun Han himself has opened a processing plant, and the goods sold by surrounding villagers all come from his place. Now Dongfeng Village has formed a strict social division of labor.

Wang Pu: Our village's online merchants sell these products on Taobao as agents. They give us the pictures, and we help them process them. They provide the pictures, and we design and make them.

Every evening at 5 o'clock, the express packages sent from each household converge at the courier service shop at the village entrance, shipped nationwide by seven container trucks. Initially, there were only one or two courier companies in the town, but now fourteen courier companies compete fiercely.

Reporter: Boss, how much is the daily shipment volume?

Wang Weiyin, boss of YTO Express as a Taobao agent:

Wang Weiyin: We ship over 1700 items every day.

Now, all services supporting online stores are fully available. While everyone was opening online stores, Sha Junchao took a different path and opened a hardware store, specifically providing metal accessories for online stores. He earns two or three hundred thousand yuan a month without any problem.

Sha Junchao: My daily turnover is 15,000 yuan, supplying goods to 80 online stores. The daily purchase volume is almost 15,000 to 10,000 yuan.

With the rapid rise of e-commerce in rural areas of Northern Jiangsu, Huang Hao, the Party Secretary of Shajitown, Suining County, Xuzhou City, has made a grand plan for the future of this small town.

Huang Hao: In the past two months, our daily online sales have exceeded two million yuan. Currently, our panel furniture accounts for 80% of online sales. Our goal is to create a concentrated center for rural commerce with national influence.

We just saw how, with the fast train of the Internet, Dongfeng Village now has a new name, Taobao Village. When urban white-collar workers browse online selecting exquisite furniture, they may never imagine that the other side of the network connects to a remote rural village in Northern Jiangsu. Every economic environmental change creates new business opportunities.

In many people's imaginations, inflation as a Taobao agent is like a flood or a ferocious beast, but this year's commercial retail industry has gained rapid development under the backdrop of inflation expectations. From January to October, the total retail sales of consumer goods increased by 18.3% year-on-year. The stock prices of commercial and department store companies such as Suning Appliances, Wangfujing, and Xidan Department Store have risen by at least 30% since the end of May, and some have more than doubled.

Currently, a new retail format has appeared on the market. Ten-yuan stores, five-yuan stores, and even three-yuan stores. Under this round of inflation expectations, they not only remain unaffected but are also occupying the market at a speed exceeding the average growth rate of the retail industry. Yang Gezi, 23 years old this year, graduated from school two years ago. With money borrowed from her parents, she invested in opening a jewelry store, a relatively standard "ten-yuan store," where most of the small jewelry items are priced around ten yuan. Due to her fashion sense and proper location selection, she currently owns 12 jewelry shops in Shenzhen. To expand her brand, Yang Gezi opened a franchise store on Dongmen Pedestrian Street, the most prime area in Shenzhen.

Eya Jewelry Chain Co., Ltd., Shenzhen Regional General Agent, Yang Gezi

Yang: No problem. Our highest monthly turnover was 520,000 yuan, averaging between 450,000 to 480,000 yuan. How much is it now? Shop Assistant: 13,000 yuan. (Is 13,000 yuan more or less?)

Yang Gezi has been in the jewelry business for only one year and three months, and currently, the monthly average turnover of her 12 stores in Shenzhen, after deducting rent, labor, and other costs, allows Yang Gezi to earn 1.2 million yuan in net profit, reaching as high as 14.4 million yuan per year. Yang Gezi has become a millionaire without a doubt.

Yang Gezi: Our cheapest eyebrow pencil is 80 cents, and the hair ties are also 80 cents.

Reporter: How much profit do you make on an 80-cent item?

Yang: We make 30 cents.

Reporter: What is the profit margin of your products approximately?

Yang: Without discounts, it's about 50%.

This round of living necessities price increases has made consumers feel that everything is getting more expensive, and they are tightening their wallets. Why do three or four hundred customers still visit Yang Gezi's jewelry store every day?

Citizen 1: Because she updates things every once in a while.

Citizen 2: Very practical, it feels very affordable.

Citizen 3: You can buy a lot with a hundred yuan. In a mall, a hundred yuan might only buy one or two items.

Yang Gezi told reporters that except for the cotton material used in toys, which caused the headquarters to raise the prices of some toys, the rest of the three thousand kinds of jewelry displayed in her Dongmen franchise store have maintained a unified national price and have not changed in price for years.

Yang Gezi: This eyeshadow style came out in 2005, and it was sold for 20.5 yuan then. It's still the same price now, so our prices have not gone up.

Currently, during the inflation period, the rise in raw materials and agricultural product prices has led many industries into a 'micro-profit era,' but Ye Guofu told us that his jewelry franchise stores are not significantly affected.

Chairman of Eya Jewelry Chain Co., Ltd., Ye Guofu

Ye: There are many products, including over five thousand styles. The proportion of products that have increased in price is relatively small. For example, the rise in cotton prices caused our toy and cotton swab prices to increase, but we haven't adjusted the prices of other products on the online store agent forum http://www.ecsou.cn.

The proportion of products that have increased in price only accounts for 10% of the over five thousand types of jewelry, and the increase is less than 10%. Taking a toy costing one or two hundred yuan as an example, the price increase is only one or twenty yuan. Guangzhou VIP Information Technology Co., Ltd. Under the current environment where raw material prices are rising but industrial finished product prices have not significantly increased, Ye Guofu's jewelry franchise stores are expanding at a rate of two or three stores per day.

Previously, we saw that whether it's group-buying websites in e-commerce, Taobao stores, or physical ten-yuan stores, their secret to success is the same: cheapness. This perfectly meets consumers' desire to save money amidst rising prices. Of course, in the face of inflationary pressure, how ordinary people can increase their income and keep up with CPI is also something many are concerned about. Let's take a look at a financial planning company.

On November 13, 2010, Saturday morning, the Zhanheng Financial Planning Center located in Beijing's Oriental Plaza office building was unusually bustling. In the meeting room, financial planner Wang Lin was giving a small lecture titled 'How to achieve certain returns in uncertain market conditions?' The reporter noticed that even the corridors were filled with people coming to listen.

Wang Lin, Financial Planner at Beijing Zhanheng Financial Advisory Co., Ltd.

Wang Lin: I didn't expect so many investors to come at once. (Our) space is relatively small, it's basically a small salon, generally controlling the number to within 10 people, but today unexpectedly nearly 20 people came.

What is the reason that makes investors flock here? The reporter noticed that during the exchange, everyone mentioned the rise in prices.

Female 1: It won't be lower than this level again. Only higher.

Wang Lin: Do you think prices will continue to rise? Do you think prices have risen by 50%?

Wang Lin: I think the reason so many investors have come today, how to put it? It's probably because they've realized the necessity of financial planning.

Since the beginning of the year, the Consumer Price Index (CPI) has continuously risen under people's watchful eyes. In September, the CPI rose by 3.6% year-on-year, and in October, it rose by 4.4%, setting a new high in 24 months. In comparison, the interest rate for a one-year fixed deposit after interest rate hikes is 2.5%. Clearly, if relying solely on fixed deposits, it's impossible to outpace the CPI. Therefore, ordinary investors hope to find worthwhile investment channels from financial institutions.

Wang Lin: (Previously) we would get 2 to 3 new clients per month, which was quite challenging for each financial advisor, but last week, in just one week, there were three new clients, and colleagues said they were signing contracts until their hands got tired.

Wang Lin works for Zhanheng Financial Planning, an independent third-party financial advisory service separate from banks, insurance, securities, and funds, established in 2004, adopting a membership-based service form. Since entering October, the number of new members has suddenly surged significantly, increasing by 300% compared to September. The amount of financial products purchased by clients increased by 100% compared to September, and in the first half of November, the number of new members and the subscription amount have already reached the level of the entire month of October.

Among these new clients, Wang Lin and her colleagues feel the biggest change is the increasing number of elderly people. One elderly person's investment rationale left a deep impression on Wang Lin.

Wang Lin: He told me, young lady, I'm coming to you because I want to pay for a single room in a nursing home. But with his current savings of over 200,000 yuan, with such high future inflation rates, he might not be able to afford the single room fees in ten years. Actually, his financial goal isn't particularly high; he just wants to maintain a stable 10% annual growth after accounting for inflation, ensuring the growth of his assets.

Half-hour Observation

Two years ago, during the international financial crisis, we were most worried about the economy entering a recession, and now we face the pressure of rising prices. The economy is constantly changing and shifting hotspots in such ups and downs. Every ordinary person, whether as a consumer or investor, obviously, they are rational economic actors. Their choices at every moment align with the basic state of the current economic operation. In the eyes of sensitive enterprises, capturing these details of consumption and investment mentality changes and creating suitable business models can find opportunities in market transformations. Successful or failed commercial cases prove that the conversion of economic cycles is not scary, the key lies in whether you can observe and experience them carefully.
