Peng Bo: The Chinese Internet is expected to be beautiful and green, great and healthy.

by cacard on 2008-02-24 14:38:12

ECNS, February 22 -- Today, eight central online media outlets including CCTV International, People's Daily Online, Xinhua News Agency, China.org.cn, CRI Online, CYOL.com, CE.cn and CNR.cn held a signing ceremony for the "Self-discipline Convention on Internet Audio-visual Program Services" in Beijing. Peng Bo, deputy director of the Internet Bureau under the State Council Information Office, said at the signing ceremony that from today's signing ceremony, we will have more expectations for China's Internet audio-visual program services - tomorrow, China's Internet will be beautiful and green, excellent and healthy, good and strong.

The following is the speech by Peng Bo:

Dear leaders, dear friends,

Today, we gather here to participate in and witness another significant event in the history of China's Internet development. The signing of the "Self-discipline Convention on Internet Audio-visual Program Services" marks the beginning of maturity for Internet audio-visual program services in China. On behalf of the State Council Information Office, I would like to express my congratulations.

Internet technology is developing rapidly, and various network applications are emerging one after another. Among many network applications, audio-visual program services have grown the fastest. About 80% of the 210 million netizens in China are users of online audio-visual programs. Internet audio-visual program services have become one of the mainstream application services on the Internet, comparable to email, search, instant messaging, etc. The joint efforts of all of you here have created another miracle for China's Internet.

Growth does not necessarily mean maturity. Like other new things, China's Internet audio-visual program services also experience naivety, impatience, impulsiveness and confusion during their rapid growth. Under the influence of various factors, some websites focus too much on short-term brilliance while ignoring their heavy responsibilities. "Young" should be synonymous with sunshine, upwardness and vitality, rather than an excuse for excessive entertainment, vulgarity or even pornography. Websites providing Internet audio-visual program services are not just technical platforms or information platforms, but also public tools with dissemination functions. Undertaking social responsibilities should be the starting point and end point of self-discipline. Understanding and learning self-discipline marks the path to maturity. From today's signing ceremony, we will have more expectations for China's Internet audio-visual program services - tomorrow, China's Internet will surely be beautiful and green, excellent and healthy, good and strong!

Maturity does not necessarily mean success. Hemingway once said, "We know where the future lies, but we don't know how to live until the future." The biggest problem currently facing China's Internet audio-visual program services is still survival and development. While everyone is looking for the way to the future, I suggest that you look beyond the "palm mountain" of entertainment and seek more business opportunities in areas such as education, healthcare, commerce and public services. A country with a high-speed developing economy and society, a market with the most netizens in the world, and a group of passionate and wise entrepreneurs - there is no reason why our Internet audio-visual program services should not succeed. In the garden of great development and prosperity of socialist culture, there must be this unique flower of Internet audio-visual program services. We are willing to share the joy of your successful careers and also willing to escort you to success!

Thank you all!
