Korean superstar Choi Ji-yoo sexily appears in an advertisement for KANS Hansoo.

by hanshu2009 on 2009-12-02 21:12:42

Korean film and television star Choi Ji-woo has officially signed with the Hansoo brand, becoming their spokesperson. The Hansoo Super Series advertisement featuring Choi Ji-woo, once aired, garnered continuous social reactions. Her elegant performance in a red evening gown left audiences awestruck. In the advertisement, Choi Ji-woo's fair and fine skin made many female viewers envious. The advertising slogan "Whiter than you, smoother than you" has now become an internet buzz phrase, indicating that Choi Ji-woo’s image as a flawless whitening icon is deeply ingrained in consumers' minds.

Choi Ji-woo reveals her beauty secrets and tips:

In "Winter Sonata," Choi Ji-woo's delicate but resilient, calm yet deeply emotional acting style left a deep impression on the audience. Following this, "Heaven's Stairs" portrayed profound kindness and tenderness with heart-wrenching intensity, elevating Choi Ji-woo's popularity through her exquisite acting skills. She has since become one of the top actresses in Korean dramas. Choi Ji-woo's skin is exceptionally fair and fine, representing the epitome of Asian female artists. She has her own methods for skincare and maintenance. During her student days, she often made her own fruit and vegetable masks, such as cucumber patch masks to hydrate her skin and improve its dullness. After starting her career, she frequently used homemade pearl powder masks to whiten her skin while ensuring it remained smooth and flawless. When asked about her beauty secrets, Choi Ji-woo stated: Skincare is a lifelong lesson for women, because the nutritional needs of the skin vary greatly at different ages. The most important thing is to choose what suits you best and opt for purely natural products.

Choi Ji-woo worked tirelessly during the advertisement shoot:

During the shooting process, Choi Ji-woo's gentle and elegant demeanor, along with her friendly and approachable attitude, earned her praise from the staff as an angel. However, once work began, she transformed into a "devil," being extremely meticulous about her performance, leaving everyone present speechless. Prior to shooting, she actively participated in discussions about the shooting script, and many creative ideas for the shots were influenced by Choi Ji-woo. Throughout the shooting process, she continued to provide numerous suggestions and insisted on asking the director to conduct shooting comparisons. After every shot, Choi Ji-woo would rush to the lens to review it with the director, not tolerating any flaws in her performance or visual angles. A simple shot was repeated many times.

During breaks, Choi Ji-woo declined the meals arranged by the staff, insisting on eating only after all the shots were completed. She expressed that she had already been a "fan" of Hansoo in Korea, and now having the opportunity to become its spokesperson made her very happy. She wanted to ensure the Super Series advertisement was flawlessly perfect.

At the end of the day's shooting, Choi Ji-woo bid farewell to each member of the staff individually. Her warm attitude and dedication deeply moved everyone on the scene. Even the director marveled that Choi Ji-woo was the most dedicated and professional actor he had encountered in his years of shooting advertisements.
