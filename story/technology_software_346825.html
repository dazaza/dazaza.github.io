Plan One: Planning insurance purchases early

For Ms. Huang, who has just started working, at the beginning stage of life planning, protection should be "targeted". From being a happy single person to later establishing a family, young singles experience the greatest changes in living environment and lifestyle. This is an important stage where insurance can be used for precautionary measures, and early arrangements should be made.

1. Clarify the focus of protection. Most life insurance products are long-term, so before purchasing, a long-term financial plan must be made. Taking protection insurance as an example, protection insurance mainly reflects responsibility to the family. The concept of family is different before and after marriage, and thus the responsibility to the family is also different, and the required amount will differ greatly. Since Ms. Huang is currently single and will face major life events such as marriage, buying a house, and having children in the next few years, pension insurance is not the most urgent need at this stage.

As a newcomer to society with not very high income and little family burden, what should be emphasized first is personal accident and accidental medical protection; secondly, a certain amount of life insurance can be considered to fully prepare for repaying the kindness of parents; at the same time, take advantage of being young now, and prepare critical illness insurance as early as possible.

2. Place the demand for accident insurance first. According to statistical data, young people are the group with the highest rate of accidents. One characteristic of accident insurance is low premiums, so this type of insurance is the most economical and practical for young people who have just started working. By spending about 200 yuan per year, you can get a general accident insurance with approximately 100,000 yuan coverage. As for the choice of accident insurance amount, it is generally advisable to choose 5-10 times your annual income.

3. Match well accidental medical insurance and hospital allowance insurance. Although Ms. Huang has participated in medical insurance, due to her short working period, her personal medical account reserve fund is not much, and the proportion of outpatient self-payment is relatively high. Therefore, it is best to obtain accidental outpatient emergency medical expense insurance. In addition, some hospital allowance insurance can be added to make up for the shortcomings of medical insurance and provide compensation for lost workdays.

4. Life insurance is a "conscience policy" for parents. Although young people mostly die from accidents, visa insurance, there is still concern that if death occurs due to illness, if only accident insurance is purchased, parents will not receive compensation, right? Therefore, to prevent any possibility, a certain amount of life insurance can be purchased. At this stage, making such an insurance plan is equivalent to preparing a "conscience policy" for parents.

In health insurance, it is especially important to purchase a critical illness insurance as early as possible. This is one of the indispensable insurances in life, and the earlier you insure, the more cost-effective it is.

From the perspective of quota arrangement, the author gives the following suggestions to Ms. Huang: 150,000 yuan for accidental insurance, 5,000 yuan for accidental medical insurance, 100,000 yuan for life insurance, 100,000 yuan for critical illness insurance, and additional hospital allowance insurance. It should be reminded to Ms. Huang that insurance should not be purchased too much, because as a young person, on the one hand, economic payment ability is limited, and on the other hand, there are always other expenses. At this point, the purpose of purchasing insurance is to form a basic living security. After the economic conditions and other situations change, the insurance plan can still be adjusted and modified.

Plan Two: Choose annual premium payment method

A popular saying nowadays is "If you don't manage your finances, your finances won't manage you." Regardless of current income levels, strong financial management desires combined with reasonable financial management measures will certainly help young people realize their wealth dreams.

Insurance, as an indispensable stabilizer of modern life, can make our lives more solid and comfortable. A person's life will go through many risk tests, such as unexpected diseases, unexpected accidents, unforeseen property disasters, and liability risks caused by negligence, etc. Therefore, the author suggests that young friends who have just stepped into society and started independent lives start from the most basic protection, gradually increase protection items and amounts as income increases. Moreover, since the monthly premium rate for insurance is slightly higher, it is recommended to adopt an annual premium payment method.

Plan Analysis:

1. Since Ms. Huang mentioned that her physical condition is not good, the author suggests choosing critical illness insurance. This type of insurance allows Ms. Huang to be insured until the age of 65 (after the age of 55, a person's physical functions gradually decline, and the risk of critical illness increases), which can maximize protection. Moreover, according to this clause, she can receive a 2,000 yuan health check subsidy every year when she turns 60-69, and if no critical illness occurs at the age of 70, she will receive a 100,000 yuan birthday gift.

2. Considering that Ms. Huang may have a higher chance of hospitalization than the average person, a special IPA plan from AIA Life was chosen for her. The reimbursement ratio of this plan can reach 100%, with no deductible, unlimited number of times per year, and includes protections such as accidents, illnesses, hospital subsidies, and surgical fees.

Insurance Subscription Tips:

Ms. Huang mentioned that her physical condition is not good, but it is unclear whether it is due to weak physical foundation, a history of serious illness, or genetic factors. If the latter two reasons cause her physical condition to be not good, each insurance company may propose physical examination requirements or even reject coverage when determining whether to underwrite, and prior consultation is necessary.

Plan Three: Best to buy universal insurance

For people in their twenties who have just graduated and started working, they are exactly at the starting phase of their careers. People at this stage often exhibit the following characteristics: 1. Due to lack of work experience after graduation, income is less and unstable. 2. After receiving salary, more or less, some money is given to parents for household expenses. 3. Monthly expenses are large, and many people unfortunately become "moonlighters" by the end of the month. 4. Must save for marriage and buying a house. 5. Due to fierce job competition, they are often physically and mentally exhausted. Developing a good financial management habit and customizing a reasonable and appropriate protection plan is particularly important.

Ms. Huang is a very typical example. Her monthly income is only 2,000 yuan, not only does she need to support her family every month, but she also needs to invest in funds besides consumption expenditure. Therefore, the remaining money available for her own disposal is not much, so we must make big achievements with small money, dividend insurance. The author believes that according to Ms. Huang's current financial situation, choosing to subscribe to a universal insurance is the most suitable. First, the characteristics of universal insurance lie in its lower periodic premium and very flexible payment period, and the coverage can also be appropriately adjusted according to different stages of life. Secondly, the personal account return of universal insurance is relatively stable, which can reduce the investment risk brought by Ms. Huang's regular fund investment, and the personal account value can also be partially withdrawn at any time, so as to prepare for emergencies. This way, Ms. Huang can have a high level of protection while having a stable investment return.
