Henan Mangshan Cemetery Price Hiked Higher than Housing Price, Tomb Trading Compared to Futures Trading

by bodamn2 on 2009-07-23 09:25:00

Mr. Zhu Tiejun, Director of the Department of Social Affairs of the Henan Provincial Civil Affairs Department, told China Economic Weekly that currently, the main laws and regulations for the management of cemeteries in Henan Province are based on the Funeral Management Regulations issued by the State Council, the Provisional Measures for the Administration of Cemeteries issued by the Ministry of Civil Affairs, and the Henan Province Funeral Management Measures issued by the Henan Provincial Civil Affairs Department. According to the provisions of the Henan Province Funeral Management Measures: It is forbidden for any unit or individual to transfer or illegally buy and sell cemetery plots or gravesites, and it also clearly stipulates that the area occupied by a single or double burial grave for ashes shall not exceed 1 square meter. The Henan Provincial Civil Affairs Department conducts inspections of the operation of cemeteries throughout the province every year, and overall, the operation of cemeteries is well regulated. Mr. Zhu Tiejun stated that the Ministry of Civil Affairs had twice issued documents requiring further strengthening of cemetery management and resolute investigation and prohibition of illegal sales of cemetery gravesites and columbarium niches. It is prohibited to speculate on the buying and selling of cemetery plots through pyramid schemes or other forms. For this reason, the provincial civil affairs department stipulates that when purchasing a grave, the purchaser must present a cremation certificate to be able to complete the purchase and use procedures, and cannot purchase graves or niches for living persons. The provincial civil affairs department conducts an inspection of cemeteries in various places every two years.

Mr. Zhu Tiejun also frankly admitted that in actual operations, some special circumstances and practical problems cannot be ruled out, such as elderly people at home who can pre-purchase graves or niches, but there are strict restrictions on the quantity, with each person only allowed to buy one plot.

According to Mr. Zhu Tiejun's introduction, in order to prevent speculation in the buying and selling of graves or niches, personal transfer and speculation of graves or niches are strictly prohibited, so listing transactions are not allowed. If individuals encounter special situations such as relocation or immigration after purchasing, they can only return to the original purchasing unit and cannot resell. Regarding the price of cemeteries, considering the significant differences in the location, size, material, and ancillary facilities of graves or niches, the current civil affairs department and price department have not yet determined the price or set a maximum price limit. In addition, regarding the usage period of cemeteries, the national regulation is a 20-year usage cycle. When mourners purchase graves, they should pay the basic rental fee, construction cost, interment fee, and tomb maintenance management fee according to the regulations. After 20 years, if continued use is desired, a certain amount of maintenance fee must still be paid, but the amount will be much lower compared to the initial fees. Currently, there are no so-called perpetual cemeteries domestically.

Since there are already clear regulations, why are there still so many under-the-table operations and private transactions in the cemetery industry? Authoritative figures believe that it is precisely due to obvious loopholes in the law that developers take advantage of public psychology and exploit these opportunities.

President Fan Ying of the Funeral Reform Professional Committee of the Guangdong Provincial Sociological Society and Professor He Bing of the China University of Political Science and Law both expressed in media interviews that there are currently many loopholes in our country's funeral system that need improvement. For more than half a century, our country has not enacted a specialized funeral law, but only managed through relevant regulations, and the situations in different places are not entirely the same, making management even more difficult.

Experts pointed out that the most obvious problem currently is that the managers of cemeteries and memorial parks are also operators, and operators also have management rights, resulting in confusion of roles and unclear division of responsibilities, inevitably leading to the phenomenon of rising cemetery prices. In addition, the funeral industry is a commercial activity, but it is currently monopolized by local funeral homes and crematoriums. When the industrial and commercial bureau investigates funeral homes for profiteering, the funeral homes can protest, saying that the industrial and commercial bureau has no authority to investigate because funeral homes are now registered as social welfare units. Moreover, the laws and regulations in different places are not uniform, increasing the actual difficulty of management.
