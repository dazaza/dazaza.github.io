Essential Android knowledge for beginners to get started easily

by dream2012 on 2012-02-22 14:28:25

What knowledge about Android does a novice need to understand in order to avoid being seen as someone out of touch with the times?

1. What is Android? Like the iOS system on iPhone and the BlackBerry system on Blackberry phones, Android is also an operating system developed for mobile devices. The Android system can be used on mobile devices such as phones, tablets, and laptops. It was developed by Google based on the Linux kernel and GNU software. On November 5, 2007, Google officially announced the establishment of the Open Handset Alliance and subsequently released the new Android operating system under this alliance. The world's first electronic device using the Android system was the G1 phone released by American carrier T-Mobile in October 2008, produced by HTC.

2. What makes Android special? Unlike the iOS operating system used by iPhones, which is completely controlled by Apple, Android is an open-source operating system. Google has made all the source code of the Android system available under the Apache license, ensuring the openness of the system while encouraging commercial use of open-source software. This means that both major manufacturers like Motorola and Samsung, as well as smaller companies, can adopt the Android system. Another unique feature of the Android platform is its powerful functionality. Although it is not as mature as the iOS system, the functionality of the Android system is no less than other mobile operating systems. In addition, the Android system seamlessly integrates Google services such as Gmail, Gtalk, and Calendar, making it very friendly for Google users.

3. Are all Android phones called G1, G2, G3...? This is a big misunderstanding. Android phones are produced by multiple manufacturers and released under different carriers in various countries, with different names. For example, Motorola's Droid series includes phones like Droid, Droid Eris, Droid 2, etc., and Samsung has the Galaxy S series of Android phones. The so-called G1, G2, G3... are all referring to HTC-made phones. These nicknames were created by domestic gray market merchants considering local users' unfamiliarity with the English names of phones. For example, after G1, HTC Magic was called G2, HTC Hero was called G3, HTC Tattoo was called G4, HTC Legend released this year was called G6, HTC Desire was called G7. Essentially, these naming conventions are misuses of phone names and are not recommended.

4. Why should I choose an Android phone over an iPhone? This question is hard to answer because both are excellent mobile operating systems. Choosing Android depends on your requirements for a phone. If you are a loyal user of Google services, the Android system integrates almost all mainstream Google services such as Gmail, Google Calendar, Google Contacts, and Google Voice. The synchronization function provided by the Android system allows you to easily send and receive Gmail emails, manage your calendar and contacts on your phone, offering a great experience of Google services on your phone.

Android provides an electronic market (Android Market) similar to the iPhone App Store, where users can download free mobile applications or purchase paid applications. However, iPhone users can only install applications from the App Store. If they want to install applications from other sources, they must perform a jailbreak operation. Android phones just need to check the option to allow installation of third-party source applications in the system settings to install them.

Unlike iPhones, which are all produced by Apple, almost all mainstream mobile manufacturers except Nokia have launched Android phones. You don't have to wait a year for Apple to release a new iPhone model. Every year, there are dozens of Android phones with different hardware configurations, positioning, and brands available for you to choose from. Whether you prefer full keyboards or large-screen phones, you can find an Android phone terminal that meets your needs.

5. What are the disadvantages of the Android system? For a mobile operating system that has been around for less than three years, Android is already very good. Android does not provide an easy-to-use iTunes program like the iPhone system to manage phone mail accounts, contacts, applications, and pictures. It may be difficult for new smartphone users, and it is not as easy to use as an iPhone.

Another problem often criticized about the Android system is the fragmentation of system versions. You will find that Android phones from different manufacturers, or even different models from the same manufacturer, may use different system versions. New features and system performance optimizations provided by the latest version of the Android system cannot be used on lower-version Android system phones, which many users find hard to accept.

6. What do Donut, Cupcake, Froyo represent, and what are their differences? System upgrades are a major feature of Android. Since Android's release, five main versions have been issued, experiencing four major system upgrades. Each new system release significantly improves Android's functionality and user experience. Moreover, starting from Android 1.5 version, each system version has a code name based on traditional American food, such as 1.5 (Cupcake), 1.6 (Donut), 2.1 (Eclair), 2.2 (Froyo).

7. Since the latest Android system version is 2.2, why do many users complain that their phones still run Android 2.1 or even Android 1.6 systems? This issue is mainly caused by two reasons: one is that Google has successively released five main system versions, the latest being Android 2.2 (code name Froyo), and Google also plans to release Android 3.0 by the end of this year and Android 3.5 next year. The other reason is that there are simply too many Android phone terminals. Apple has only released four iPhone models, so it is easier for Apple to upgrade the new system for iPhone users. However, the difficulty for Android phones in this aspect is much greater. Nevertheless, the openness of the Android system provides another solution for users, allowing rooted Android phones to choose third-party ROM installations. For example, the first-generation Android phone, T-Mobile G1, can now install and run the latest Android 2.2 system.

8. How many applications are currently available for the Android system? In July 2010, Google announced that the total number of applications in the Android electronic market exceeded 70,000. And since May 2010, the total number of new applications added to the electronic market every week has exceeded 10,000. Including a large number of third-party applications not provided for download in the electronic market, the total number of Android applications far exceeds 100,000. Although the total number lags behind the 200,000 applications in the App Store, the speed of adding new applications is much higher than on the iPhone platform.

In addition, according to a Distimo report in July 2010, the proportion of free applications in the Android electronic market is the highest among all online mobile application stores globally. 57% of the applications in the electronic market can be downloaded and used for free. This proportion for Apple's App Store is less than 28%, less than half of the Android electronic market.

9. How should I choose an Android phone? According to the current global market share of Android phones, the top three Android manufacturers are HTC, Motorola, and Samsung. Basically, you need to first figure out your requirements for a phone, such as whether you want a QWERTY physical full keyboard Android phone, a large-screen phone, or a compact model that is easy to carry. You also need to consider your budget range, the network of the telecommunications operator you use, your preferred brand and appearance. Additionally, consider the system version of the phone. When the phone configurations are similar, you can prioritize choosing a phone running the latest system version.

10. Which Android phones are currently the hottest? HTC Evo 4G was the flagship Android phone released in the summer, being the world's first phone supporting WiMax 4G networks. It is equipped with a 4.3-inch super-large screen, a 1GHz Qualcomm processor, an 8-megapixel camera, supports 720P HD video recording, and has HDMI output to play on TVs. Then Motorola consecutively released the Droid 2 and Droid X, both high-end models, with the Droid 2 having a side-slide full keyboard.

Additionally, Samsung released the Galaxy S series phones under multiple carriers, featuring a top-notch 4-inch Super AMOLED screen with excellent 3D gaming performance. Recently, the T-Mobile G2 phone has caught attention, adopting the new Qualcomm MSM7X30 chipset, set to be HTC's new flagship in the future.
