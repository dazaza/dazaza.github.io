Queue marketing is one of Uniqlo's great tools. By shifting the tedious and boring activity of queuing from real life to the internet under the name "Luck Line," a stark contrast is immediately formed. During the queue activity, netizens can choose their favorite animals and scenes, which are highly attractive to young people; while having fun, motivational factors are added, forming the self-motivated participation of netizens. Numerous generous prizes make netizens eager to try. Meanwhile, the concept of queuing is cleverly utilized, spreading the idea that it is appealing because many people are queuing, thus creating a queuing effect like ripples spreading outward when a stone is thrown into the center of a lake, attracting more and more potential consumers who come by reputation.

Moreover, Renren Network, as a real-name social platform, automatically refreshes users' personal homepages with new updates after participating in activities, thereby generating a联动effect within real social circles, achieving an effect where one call brings a hundred responses, spreading rapidly.

In fact, this is not the first time Uniqlo has used queue marketing. As early as May 2010 during the Shanghai World Expo, Uniqlo's global flagship store located on Nanjing West Road in Shanghai opened lively. In the days following its opening, the entrance and exit under the largest logo were set as the entrance, with the remaining entrances and exits only serving as exits. At the entrance, two barriers guided people to queue up, with the queue extending to the side of the store. From afar, the queue appeared as a precise right angle, very eye-catching.

Offline queue marketing not only effectively controls the number of customers entering the store to shop, creating a comfortable and safe shopping environment for customers inside the store, but also skillfully captures the psychology of Chinese nationals, producing a deep impression that the Uniqlo brand is extremely popular and causes long queues, expanding its influence.

At the start of the New Year, Uniqlo grandly launched the first wave of "Try-On" on Renren Network for 2011. Fans of Uniqlo could apply to become Uniqlo try-on participants by leaving messages on Uniqlo's public homepage. After receiving free gifts, they would post try-on diaries and photos on their personal Renren Network pages, mobilizing netizens to vote on the diaries. Those with high votes would receive Uniqlo coupons. Undoubtedly, the first wave of Uniqlo's New Year try-ons will once again trigger an online craze, using the power of netizens to actively promote the Uniqlo brand and continue the marketing effect after the queue fever.

Whether it is virtual online queueing, physical store queueing offline, or the New Year's new clothing try-on, Uniqlo firmly grasps the psychology of consumers. Due to more incentives, spread elements, and fresh creativity, online virtual queueing and new clothing try-on activities bring greater online sensation and social effects, making it a successful innovative marketing campaign combined with network media.

Uniqlo's Revelation:

Looking back at most Chinese apparel enterprises, they are still trapped in traditional marketing promotion ideas, lacking fresh marketing elements: such as solely relying on traditional advertising promotion, falling into the trap of using celebrity endorsements, seemingly needing to hire famous endorsers to build a brand. However, in the marketing 3.0 era led by creativity, traditional marketing promotion methods lacking interactive elements can only bring more and more visual fatigue and promotional fatigue to consumers.

As for Uniqlo, a Japanese casual wear brand, it has broken free from the constraints of traditional marketing thinking. Under the perspective of the new media environment, it fully utilizes the innovative thinking brought by the marketing 3.0 era to successfully carry out online marketing promotions.
