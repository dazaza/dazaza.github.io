In this series, there exists an unnatural food called "Devil Fruits." Of course, since they are called Devil Fruits, naturally, there is also a cursed aspect: anyone who eats the fruit will be abandoned by the sea! Oda Eiichiro grew up immersed in manga. When he was in middle school, Oda Eiichiro began creating his own comics. His first comic work was drawn on paper with a pencil, telling a story about pirates. Oda Eiichiro found that there was so much he could explore in the "pirate" theme, so he secretly resolved to serialize his pirate comic on Japan's top manga magazine, "Weekly Shonen Jump." To hone his drawing skills, Oda Eiichiro worked as an assistant to three manga artists: Shin Kibayashi ("Keiji Story"), Masaya Tokuhara ("Unwen Tarzan"), and Nobuhiro Watsuki ("Rurouni Kenshin"). During his time as an assistant to Watsuki, Oda Eiichiro used his spare time to create the pirate-themed "Romance Dawn." Although this comic was not published, it became the prototype for "One Piece." Now, the sales of "One Piece" tankobon have approached 1 billion copies, and Oda Eiichiro has risen from being an assistant to Watsuki to the peak of the Japanese manga world. His "One Piece" has been hailed as "the hope of the Japanese manga world that has the potential to catch up to 'Dragon Ball.'"
