Leonardo, Milan, Manchester United, all born for the Champions League: My formation existed 110 years ago.

by qinglai888 on 2009-12-20 10:30:40

Leonardo, Milan, and United were all born for the Champions League; my formation was designed 110 years ago.

Sina Sports - This Saturday AC Milan will play an away game against Fiorentina. At the press conference on Friday afternoon, team coach Leonardo emphasized the importance of this last game before the Christmas break. He also introduced the status of some players in the team and made special comments on the draw results for the knockout stage of the UEFA Champions League.

Speaking about the draw results for the round of 16 matches in the UEFA Champions League that were just announced, Leonardo said: "Our opponent in the round of 16 is Manchester United. Like Milan, they are also a traditional powerhouse in European competitions. Manchester United and Milan have the same goals, and I think we are evenly matched. It's too early to calculate the situation of the match now because the round of 16 doesn't start until February next year, and there is still a long time to go. Moreover, the interval between the home and away legs in the knockout stage of the UEFA Champions League this season is quite long, so there are many things that need to be considered at that time. Anyway, both Milan and Manchester United are natural competitors in the European arena. Looking back, every previous game has been thrilling."

As for the game against Fiorentina on Saturday, Leonardo said: "The last time AC Milan played in Florence was in the last game of last season, which was a very special moment. For me, it was the day my coaching career began, and I never imagined what would happen after that. I remember clearly that the day of the game was filled with our emotions: Ancelotti's farewell to everyone in the locker room, his words, and the lump in our throats that arose naturally. AC Milan secured qualification for next season's UEFA Champions League... That was also Paul Maldini's and Kaka's last game, although the EU didn't know Ricci was leaving yet. That was also the moment Gattuso and Nesta returned to us. Tomorrow we will return to the Artemio Franchi Stadium in Florence, and we will face a team with distinct personalities. Their style is clear, their players are in good form, and they have sufficient depth to adopt any possible tactics. For AC Milan, tomorrow's game will be an important test, although I don't know yet what lineup I will use because there are still many situations to consider. Ronaldinho has some physical issues, so I will make the decision at the last moment."

Leonardo said: "The loss to Palermo will not affect the positive momentum the team is currently in. I don't think the result of an individual game can change the team's mental state. Although AC Milan lost the game, we didn't play badly. We created plenty of opportunities."

Regarding the attack on President Berlusconi this week, Leonardo said: "What happened to President Berlusconi was unpleasant. We're not just talking about the chairman of AC Milan, but also the Prime Minister of Italy. The whole team sent him greetings, and we stand by him at this moment."

Talking about the unique formation he is currently using, Leonardo said: "The formation I am using now was designed 110 years ago. Almost all teams play like this now, even Palermo arranges many people to push forward during the game, just like Cagliari, Inter Milan, and Fiorentina. In this case, you can't put all the responsibility of being the team's lungs on the shoulders of two midfielders (Ambrosini and Pirlo). They can't be the only ones running in the game. Everyone needs to take their own responsibilities and complete their tasks, and if each player can do this, then this formation can withstand scrutiny."

Subsequently, Leonardo introduced the situations of certain players in the team, saying: "Huntelaar has less space compared to other players, and he paid a price for joining at a delicate moment. This season, he hasn't had many chances to play, but in Catania, he scored two goals, which made him feel better. There are players like Borrello and Inzaghi ahead of him who are in better form than Huntelaar, but despite these factors, I think he can become an important member of our team, and he won't leave. If we don't count the two months he was forced to miss due to injury, Storari spent six important months at Milan. Marco successfully integrated himself into the team. Anyway, the ranking among goalkeepers is judged based on the current status of all four, and it will absolutely not remain unchanged. As for Gattuso, I don't think I exerted any influence on his decision to renew his contract with Milan. I'm not surprised by his choice because I've been communicating with him, and I already knew everything. Anyway, it's normal for players to occasionally have some thoughts and emotions. We're talking about a player who has been at Milan for 11 years. When I was a player, I got injured too, and in that situation, a player starts to have time to think and ponder many things in their heart. I believe he went through this moment and made the right decision. He can play in the game against Fiorentina; he has recovered, although it has been a long time since he last played."

Regarding the January transfer market, Leonardo said: "We know we won't enter the transfer market, so I will strive to achieve excellent results with the existing roster in a very optimistic mood."

Regarding the possibility of Flamini playing as a right-back, Leonardo said: "I won't arrange for Flamini to play as a right-back because he doesn't feel comfortable doing so. Of course, if there is a need, he will play, but he prefers another position. Therefore, we found other options for this position."

As for his future, Leonardo said: "When I end my coaching career, it will be the time I end my journey with Milan. After I stop being a head coach, I won't go back to club management either. My office at the AC Milan headquarters is still there, but it's empty. I've made my choice, and when I no longer serve as a coach, it will be the time I end my cooperation with Milan."
