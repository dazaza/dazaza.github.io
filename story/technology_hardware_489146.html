The early hours of August 31, 1999

by dpwtaoiux on 2011-09-14 14:28:46

(Old Feelings)

On the early morning of August 31, 1999, I arrived at a brand-new classroom. Of course, it was mainly to get into a key high school and fulfill my long-cherished wish as well as my parents' expectations. As I felt unfamiliar with this new environment, a pair of eyes suddenly looked at me from seemingly out of nowhere. At that time, I didn't have the so-called sentiments of adults; I just felt my heart beating uncontrollably. Suddenly, I felt incredibly relaxed. Now, when I look back, it was so warm and meaningful. She was a girl who gave off a refreshing vibe and stood out in a crowd. I had never been so infatuated with any girl. Back then, I was like under a spell, wanting to see her every day, her smile, and her eyes. For her, I deliberately claimed that I was nearsighted so that I could sit in front of her. This way, I could see her every day.

Later, she noticed me. Whenever I looked at her, she would smile faintly and turn back to continue reading. For her, I became like some mischievous male classmates, pulling pranks every day, making myself seem like a clown, all to attract her attention. But I was wrong. She loved tranquility. Whenever she saw our antics, she would frown slightly, looking quite helpless. On her birthday, I secretly slipped her a love letter. Not knowing if she accepted it or not, I could only watch from afar. Seeing her gently toss the letter into the wind made me feel incredibly desolate. From that day on, she never looked at me again.

Life rushes by in decades; time is cruel to each of us. The day of separation came quickly. To have a keepsake with her, I specially prepared a yearbook for signatures.

I hoped to get messages from every classmate, but what I truly cared about was her message. Later, she wrote, "Wish you take care," just five simple words that I deeply engraved in my heart. Even now, sometimes thinking about it still causes pain. My life has experienced a few romances. Among these romantic passersby, I place her first in my heart. Although the current post-80s generation deviates from many things such as faith, emotion, dreams, including friends and family around us, there is one thing I can be sure we cannot betray: our childhood. Back then, children inherently had a sense of romance and innocence. What we liked was pure, without any taint. Ten years later, writing about childhood seems somewhat ironic. However, whenever someone asks me what the greatest love in this world is, I will tell them it's first love. What's the most painful thing in this world?

It's longing. What's the most unforgettable love? It's unrequited love. What's the most worth commemorating? It's memories. Personally, it's the memories with her. If I had the chance, I really want to tell her, "Long time no see, I've always deeply loved you." It's almost August 31st again, just like that day ten years ago. I will forever remember that day. Perhaps she has already forgotten this silly boy, but I will always remember those eyes that could melt my soul and that smile that haunts me for life. Her name is Yang Na, and I still love her.
