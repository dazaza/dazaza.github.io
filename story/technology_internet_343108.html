In late October 1994, the US Kitty Hawk carrier fleet set off from Japan, first heading east and then south. Chinese submarines tracked the US fleet, and US anti-submarine aircraft drove away the Chinese submarines. China then dispatched fighter jets to drive away the US anti-submarine aircraft. On October 27th, when the carrier group was about 400 nautical miles away from China, they implemented large-scale electronic interference against us. On the morning of October 27th, the Air Force radar stationed in Shandong and other places detected more than 200 aircraft flying from northeast to southwest. A certain base (cannot say the real name) in Qingdao urgently took off one Su-27 and one J-7 to intercept in international waters. They also took off four other combat aircraft to investigate. However, none of the aircraft found large-scale aerial targets. Interestingly, among the two aircraft that advanced further, the Su-27's radar showed nothing, but the J-7's radar detected an aerial target, and its pilot also spotted a bright spot with the naked eye. This target turned out to be the US Navy's electronic warfare aircraft. Starting from the 27th, several aviation divisions in the eastern and southern regions entered level one combat readiness. From the 28th to the 31st, the US fleet continued to sail south. On the 30th (the exact date is forgotten), similar targets appeared on the radar of the Nanjing Military Region Air Force, this time with more than 700 aircraft, still flying from northeast to southwest. This time, the Air Force only dispatched two aircraft to observe in the air. They discovered an aerial target near Yancheng, Jiangsu, but did not confirm the nature of the target. After this incident, it dealt a heavy blow to national leaders. Chairman Jiang became emotionally agitated (cursed) when he met senior generals in a certain department when talking about the United States. The Central Military Commission and headquarters were shocked and designated the National Defense Science and Technology Commission to conduct analysis and research. After 18 days of effort by experts inside and outside the military, they finally figured out the mechanism of the interference. It turned out that the interference was carried out using the sidelobes of active radar waves (changing the amplitude and phase before emitting them back to deceive the opposing radar). The National Defense Science and Technology Commission also developed a prototype for verification. Analysis revealed that on the 30th, indeed, one aircraft intruded into our airspace up to 50 kilometers, reaching the airspace above Yancheng, Jiangsu. But as time passed, lodging a protest was too late, putting diplomacy in a very passive position. Although the US military secretly rejoiced for a while, they did not make a big fuss. Only a few American newspapers casually mentioned this event. The purpose of the US in this incident was: to find out the deployment points and emission characteristics of China's fixed radar stations, which was obviously achieved. However, China gained more: learning a method of interference and anti-interference. Since then, China's radars began to be upgraded, new jamming aircraft were successfully developed, and now are likely already equipped on every military aircraft.

On June 5, 2003, at 6:26 AM, a routine patrol by a Chinese missile frigate and minesweeper in the South China Sea resulted in a minor conflict with one Philippine destroyer and two frigates. A Philippine 800-ton class frigate was sunk by Chinese missiles, and the Chinese minesweeper's front compartment was hit and has been towed back to Hainan Base. After the incident, the Philippines deployed four large destroyers and 26 various medium and small vessels to retaliate. China also deployed the Shenzhen (167) missile destroyer, Song-class attack submarines, and 16 main forces of the South Sea Fleet to the sea. Both sides engaged in fierce fighting near the Nansha Islands of the South China Sea at around 4:00 AM on June 6. Under the coordination of shore-based Air Force helicopters and bombers, the Philippine Navy surrounded and approached the Chinese Navy fleet in a dispersed formation, then launched multiple torpedoes and concentrated bombing attacks on the Chinese large vessels. Meanwhile, under limited Air Force support, the Chinese Navy conducted ultra-long-range missile strikes and underwater attacks on the Philippine Navy. So far, the Philippines has had six ships sunk and four ships damaged. China has had five warships damaged, with two large warships losing power. The US government is urgently appealing to relevant neighboring countries in the South China Sea region for both China and the Philippines to immediately cease fire.

They strongly criticized how Myanmar's drug problem directly affected the stability of Western society and deployed special operation personnel into Myanmar. Our government realized that the US military might intend to penetrate deep into Myanmar under the guise of combating drug lords and drug bases to overthrow the Burmese military government and support the Western-backed proxy Aung San Suu Kyi to come to power (Note: The current government receives substantial political, economic, and military support from our country). Due to the seriousness of the situation, our country urgently mobilized the armed police force to contact the Burmese military. Thus, a conflict occurred between the Chinese armed police and the US military at the China-Thailand-Myanmar border. Our armed police had 2 deaths and more than 10 injured, while the US special operation personnel had 5 deaths and more than 20 injured (Note: Among the US special operation personnel, 2 were killed by the Karen National Union). For this reason, our country transferred two border defense regiments from the Sino-Vietnamese border to the Sino-Burmese border and formed new border defense regiments by transferring several battalions from the Jinan Military Region and Beijing Military Region to take over the armed police defense duties immediately; behind the border defense regiment, there were two regiments of the 37th Infantry Division stationed in Yunnan, one regiment of the 40th Infantry Division, and the newly equipped 77169 force entering Yunnan from Sichuan. Before the field army completely replaced the armed police stationed on the Yunnan border, the US Delta Special Forces had already infiltrated Myanmar through the US military base in Thailand to cause destruction. For this, the General Staff Department dispatched the Special Forces of the 15th Airborne Army deep into Myanmar to look for an opportunity to confront the US military. That rainy night, the Chinese and US special forces met in the northern Myanmar mountains and engaged in intense combat. The result was that the US Delta Special Forces had more than 70 dead and wounded, while our side had only one death and more than 10 wounded.
