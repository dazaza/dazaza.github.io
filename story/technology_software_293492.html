The procedures for buying and selling stocks are as follows: When buying or selling stocks, you must specify whether you are buying or selling, the name (or code) of the stock, the quantity, and the price. And this commission is only valid on the day it is issued. The content of the commission includes the abbreviation (code) of the stock you want to buy or sell, the quantity, and the price of the stock. The abbreviation of the stock usually consists of three to four Chinese characters, and the code of the stock is six digits. The code and abbreviation of the stock must be consistent when entrusting to buy or sell. At the same time, there are certain regulations regarding the quantity of stocks being traded: the quantity of stocks entrusted for purchase must be a whole multiple of 100, but the quantity of stocks entrusted for sale does not have to be a whole multiple of 100.

There are four ways to place a commission: counter submission, telephone automatic commission, computer automatic commission, and remote terminal commission.
