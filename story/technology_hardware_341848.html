"Low carbon" is also a driving force for economic development.

by balloons on 2010-04-07 22:30:05

When it comes to environmental protection and low carbon, people often think of the side of reducing greenhouse gas emissions and so on. In fact, it also has an "increase" side — new markets, new economic activities, and new jobs. The UK put forward a macro concept in its programmatic document "The UK Low Carbon Transition Plan" last year, planning to make the low-carbon economy bring more than 1.2 million green jobs to the UK by 2020. (International Finance News, April 6) Such new thinking is refreshing. In contrast, there are still quite a few officials at home who think that "economy" and "low carbon" are a pair of contradictions. For example, a province once conducted a questionnaire survey, and as high as 91.95% of the mayors and department directors who participated in the survey believed that strengthening environmental protection (low carbon) would affect economic development. Thinking determines action. The wrong environmental awareness in the minds of local officials determines their basic attitude towards high-energy-consuming and polluting enterprises — as long as they can bring rich tax revenues to the locality, the energy consumption and pollution of enterprises can be ignored, or at most "pollute first and then control later"! The green building industry in the UK can create 65,000 jobs, offshore wind energy can bring about 70,000 jobs, and the development of marine energy such as wave energy and tidal energy can create 16,000 jobs... The new jobs created by the low-carbon economy have become a powerful tool for government projects to seek public support. The British government is about to develop natural gas in a certain town, and the relevant announcement focuses on introducing that the project will provide 500 low-carbon jobs for local residents. Developing a low-carbon economy can not only add new jobs but also harbor new growth points. Take our domestic situation as an example. On the one hand, resources are becoming increasingly scarce, while on the other hand, the effective utilization rate of resources is relatively low, and waste is difficult to curb. These two forces have exacerbated the dilemma of the unsustainability of China's economic development. Conversely, if we can seize the opportunity of the emerging low-carbon economy, vigorously develop green technology and clean development technology, we can not only greatly reduce energy consumption pressure but also occupy a strategic high ground in the political and economic game among major countries in the new century, cultivating a large number of high-tech multinational companies with international competitiveness, bringing our economy lasting development momentum. In the face of the reality that environmental protection has become the engine of some countries' economies, our local governments should abandon the wrong idea that "environmental protection affects the economy", actively guide enterprises to control pollution and improve production processes, and develop more low-pollution, high-efficiency projects. Environmental engineer Geng Haijun from Hubei Province provided this information. Shandong Lawyers Network provides http://sdlvshi.org/.
