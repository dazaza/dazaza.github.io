2014 Nanjing Youth Olympic Games Weibo responds to a 1-day difference shown on two Youth Olympic countdown boards

by edivista on 2011-12-02 16:02:21

Click to enter the official microblog of the 2014 Nanjing Youth Olympic Games

Yesterday, a citizen passing by Gulou Square discovered two electronic countdown boards for the 2014 Youth Olympics. One showed 989 days and the other showed 990 days, differing by one day. These two boards were only separated by a road, and citizens jokingly said, "Could it be that they are time-traveling?" The Youth Olympic Committee quickly responded on their official microblog: "Maintenance personnel mistakenly adjusted the system's 24-hour clock to a 12-hour clock during debugging, and will restore it as soon as possible."

In the morning yesterday, the reporter came to Gulou Square. Next to the stone carving in the Green Square, there was a countdown board shaped like a sundial. Its unique design attracted many passers-by to take out their phones to take pictures. The sundial face was engraved with the emblems of various sports events of the Youth Olympics. As the sun shifted, the shadow pointed to different sports projects. Above the electronic display screen, it read "Countdown to the opening on August 16, 2014," and below it, the display screen showed 989 days. (Click on the picture to enter the official microblog of the 2014 Nanjing Youth Olympic Games)

Dozens of meters away on the opposite side of the road, another countdown board stood at the edge of the flower bed in the center of the large roundabout at Gulou Square. With a white background and green display screen, it was quite eye-catching. The huge numbers on the display screen could be seen clearly from afar, showing 990 days, and below it rolled the promotional slogans of the Youth Olympic Committee, as netizens said, the times on the two countdown boards indeed differed by one day.

At 10:07 yesterday, netizen Shi Zaitetehao sent a microblog mentioning @2014 Nanjing Youth Olympic Games, pointing out the problem of the two timers having a time difference, which quickly drew attention. Within an hour, the 2014 Nanjing Youth Olympic Games made a response on Weibo, indicating that staff were already investigating and would resolve the fault as soon as possible.

At 14:23 yesterday, the 2014 Nanjing Youth Olympics issued an official statement regarding the countdown board malfunction: The countdown board at Gulou Square began normal operation on November 20th. On November 30th, due to rainy weather, the wires of the countdown board got waterlogged, causing a display screen malfunction. During the debugging of the countdown screen content, maintenance personnel mistakenly adjusted the system's 24-hour clock to a 12-hour clock, resulting in the displayed number of days differing from the actual number of days by one day. They would resolve this fault as soon as possible.

Some netizens felt that such countdown boards should have considered waterproofing issues during the initial design phase to prevent similar malfunctions from occurring again.

So, which countdown board is correct? The reporter raised this question on Weibo. The 2014 Nanjing Youth Olympics replied that yesterday was 989 days until the Youth Olympics countdown, so the correct one should be the sundial-shaped countdown board in front of Zifeng Tower.
