I: Transportation to Mount Huang

The Mount Huang we usually refer to is the Mount Huang Scenic Area. If you take a train or bus, you can only reach the urban area of Huangshan City (commonly known as "Tunxi"), then we need to transfer here to Tangkou Town at the foot of the scenic area, and take the scenic area shuttle bus (Xinguo Line) to the entrance of Mount Huang Scenic Area. Below, we will explain the three ways of transportation to Mount Huang Scenic Area:

1: Train

There are more than 30 trains passing through Mount Huang, with the farthest destinations being Beijing, Xiamen, Fuzhou, Kunming, and Guangzhou. There are fewer trains departing from Mount Huang Station, only to Shanghai K8420 and Huabei K8410. If you arrive at Mount Huang Station by train, you can take a minibus from the parking lot outside the exit to Tunxi to the Mount Huang Scenic Area (Tangkou), and then transfer to the scenic area shuttle bus up the mountain. The cost is about 15 yuan per person.

2: Airplane

Huangshan Airport is also in the city, but it's in the suburbs, about 5 kilometers away from the city center. You can take a taxi from the city center to the Huangshan Passenger Transport Center, then take a bus from Tunxi to Taiping and get off at Tangkou (15 yuan per person), and transfer to the scenic area shuttle bus up the mountain. Alternatively, you can directly hire a taxi from the airport to Tangkou (150 yuan per car).

3: Bus

If you come from Wuhan, Hefei, Tongling, Anqing, Nanjing, Wuhu, you can ask if the bus stops at Tangkou. If it does, you can directly get off at Tangkou to save time. For other places like Shanghai and Hangzhou, some vehicles stop in the urban area of Tunxi, while others go directly to the Mount Huang Scenic Area (Tangkou). If the vehicle stops in the urban area, you need to transfer to a bus from Tunxi to Taiping and get off at Tangkou (15-16 yuan per person), then transfer to the scenic area shuttle bus up the mountain.

III: Must-see scenery when visiting Mount Huang

1. Unique Pines

The first of the Four Wonders of Mount Huang, the Huangshan Pine is an independent species of pine tree, most of which grow on cliffs. The most famous one is the Welcoming Guest Pine located at the Yu Ping Tower on the front mountain. Additionally, on the back mountain at the Start to Believe Peak, you can see many unique Huangshan pines. It is said: "You haven't seen Huangshan pines until you've been to Start to Believe Peak."

2. Strange Rocks

The formation of Huangshan's strange rocks is due to the granite rock bodies experiencing long ages, exposed to sun and rain, weathered and eroded, washed by streams, etc., making those stone rough drafts undergo countless fine carvings and engravings. Representative examples include the Flying Stone, Dream Pen Producing Flowers, Monkey Watching the Sea, Squirrel Jumping to Tian Du, Fish Carrying a Golden Turtle, etc.

3. Sea of Clouds

The sea of clouds is one of the important landscapes of mountainous areas. Under certain weather conditions, the cloud layer forms, with the cloud top height lower than the mountain top height. When people look down from the mountain peak, they see endless clouds, like standing at the edge of the sea, with waves rising and falling, splashing water, and crashing against the shore. Hence, this phenomenon is called "sea of clouds." The sunrise and sunset formed by the sea of clouds create colorful scenes, known as "colorful sea of clouds," which is the most spectacular. The sea of clouds in Huangshan is particularly extraordinary. The peaks of Huangshan are stacked, with steep cliffs and deep ravines. Air currents flow through the mountains, rising and falling, creating active circulation. Endless clouds and layered clouds drift with the wind, sometimes rising, sometimes descending, sometimes spinning, sometimes stretching, forming a grand and ever-changing view of the sea of clouds.

Huangshan's scenic area has towering peaks and deep valleys. Whenever the sea of clouds surges, the entire Huangshan scenic area becomes divided into numerous seas of clouds. Therefore, according to different directions, the seas of clouds in Huangshan can be divided into: East Sea, South Sea, West Sea, North Sea, and Sky Sea.

4. Hot Springs

The hot spring scenic area is located halfway up the mountain. Whether you're going to Yun Gu Temple or Ci Guang Pavilion from the south gate, you must pass through the hot spring scenic area. The water quality mainly contains heavy bicarbonate, is exceptionally clear, colorless, odorless, and tastes sweet. The water temperature remains at 42°C year-round, suitable for drinking and bathing. The flow rate is stable, remaining undiminished even during prolonged droughts, with an hourly output of 48 tons. It has certain medical value and good therapeutic effects on various diseases. According to legend, Emperor Xuanyuan bathed here, turning his white hair black and rejuvenating, hence it is praised as a spiritual spring. An ancient poem says: "If the Five Great Mountains were compared with Mount Huang, they would still lack the magical spring." Currently, the full price for Huangshan hot springs is 198 yuan per person, and Youdofu Inn in Tangkou can help book discounted hot spring tickets, reducing the price to 148 yuan per person for groups.

5. Unique Peaks

Mount Huang has 77 peaks over 1,000 meters high. The entire Mount Huang is a granite peak forest, historically having 36 major peaks and 36 minor peaks. The highest peak is Lotus Peak, the most dangerous is Bright Summit Peak, and the best place to watch the sunrise is the Bright Top Peak, forming the three main peaks of Mount Huang. Other major peaks include Start to Believe Peak, Lion Peak, Vermilion Sandstone Peak, Lotus Bud Peak, Turtle Peak, Nine Dragon Peaks, etc.

6. West Sea Grand Canyon

Near the west gate of the Mount Huang scenic area, it is a successful recent development of Mount Huang. Those who have walked through it are reluctant to leave, and those who haven't are eager to visit.

The West Sea Grand Canyon starts from the Paiyun Pavilion entrance and ends at the Bushen Bridge exit in the Baiyun scenic area, with a total length of about 3,600 meters, covering an area of 25 square kilometers. It is U-shaped, with a maximum drop of 400 meters. Standing at the canyon entrance, looking around, the undulating mountains form a deep canyon belt, with steep canyon walls. Here, you can see towering peaks, strange rocks, marvelously crafted by nature; rare birds and animals roam leisurely, with rich plant diversity making the canyon lush and verdant. The canyon integrates the peculiarities, dangers, ruggedness, and seclusion of Mount Huang, presenting a grand and spectacular view.

7. Sunrise

Mount Huang's towering terrain, surrounded by thousands of peaks and covered by mist, presents a magnificent and grand sunrise scene. Before dawn, the horizon gradually brightens, and a golden border appears on the rolling sea of clouds. At this moment, mist and clouds swirl, with mountain shapes and tree shadows appearing and disappearing, ethereal and mysterious. Soon, the first light of dawn emerges, reflecting red sand hues, and suddenly a red dot jumps out of the sea. As it rises, it forms a semi-circle; in an instant, a round red sun bursts out of the waves, leaping upward and soaring higher. At this point, the peaks and clever stones, shrouded in a light veil, gradually come into view, and the entire mountain range is immersed in brilliant colors. In the sky, there are countless rays of light, like a giant kaleidoscope, dazzling and breathtakingly beautiful.

Sunrise viewing spots: Bright Top Peak, Dawn Pavilion, Cool Terrace, Lion Peak, White Goose Ridge, Turtle Peak, Vermilion Sandstone Peak, Welcoming Guest Pavilion, etc.

Sunrise times: Spring: 5:30-6:00; Summer: 4:40-5:20; Autumn: 4:50-5:20; Winter: 5:30-7:00

8. Sunset and Evening Glow

At dusk on Mount Huang, the sun sets low on the western mountain, with golden hues covering myriad peaks, creating a mystical and enchanting scene. Majestic and majestic peaks shine brilliantly, evoking countless emotions tied to this mountain.

Sunset viewing spots: Bright Top Peak, Paiyun Pavilion, Vermilion Sandstone Peak, Flying Stone, Turtle Peak, West Sea Fairy Terrace.

9. Buddha's Light

According to meteorological statistics, the average monthly occurrence of Buddha's Light on Mount Huang is 2-5 times, indicating that the frequency is relatively high. The best season for Buddha's Light on Mount Huang is generally before 9:00 AM after rainy days and after 5:00 PM after the rain clears up.

The best locations for viewing Buddha's Light on Mount Huang are: Tian Du Peak, Lotus Peak, Bright Top Peak, Start to Believe Peak, Vermilion Sandstone Peak, Chess Pavilion Peak, White Russian Peak, Welcoming Guest Pavilion, Cool Terrace, Tian Hai Phoenix Pine, Furong Peak, Cui Wei Peak, etc. Generally, these are all above 1,600 meters in altitude.

If you choose the best moment and occupy an ideal location according to the above introduction, your shadow will be reflected in the colorful halo of the Buddha's Light, leaving you with an unforgettable memory for life.

10. Rime Ice and Glaze Ice

In cold winters, temperatures are often below freezing. If foggy or drizzly weather occurs, the fog or raindrops freeze into tiny crystalline structures upon contact with cold surfaces such as trees and rocks. This natural phenomenon is known as "rime ice" in meteorology; when supercooled raindrops fall onto the ground or near-ground objects and form transparent and hard frozen objects, it is called "glaze ice." Most of the main scenic areas of Mount Huang are above 1,500 meters in altitude, with winter temperatures being relatively low. Additionally, Mount Huang experiences over 200 cloudy and foggy days annually, providing excellent necessary conditions for the formation of rime and glaze ice. Therefore, visitors to Mount Huang during the winter season can easily witness this seasonal beauty.

Mount Huang averages 62 days of rime ice and 35.9 days of glaze ice annually. The longest duration of continuous rime ice can reach 24 days, with a maximum diameter of 23.2 centimeters. The longest duration of continuous glaze ice can nearly reach 25 days, with a maximum diameter of 41.3 centimeters. Most of Mount Huang's rime ice is granular, forming easily when temperatures are between -2°C and -7°C. When the fog droplets expand to drizzle, glaze ice forms. Due to Mount Huang's complex terrain and significant microclimate differences, some areas have more glaze ice, while others have more rime ice, and sometimes both can appear simultaneously.

11. Flora and Fauna

Mount Huang is also known as a gene bank of plants and a paradise for animals. If you're lucky, you might spot short-tailed monkeys, squirrels, orioles, eyebrow birds, red-beaked lovebirds, and white partridges. Common plants include Huangshan azaleas, Huangshan magnolias, celestial maid flowers, four-sided flowers, and tulip trees.

IV: Precautions for Climbing and Sightseeing on Mount Huang

Essential items: disposable raincoat (windy so umbrellas aren't advisable), gloves, flashlight, trekking pole (recommended for those with poor stamina), 2 bottles of drinking water, energy foods like beef jerky and chocolate.

1. Wear hiking shoes, cloth shoes, or sneakers when climbing; leather shoes and plastic-soled shoes are slippery. For safety, buy a bamboo stick or trekking pole when climbing.

2. On steep mountains, climb slowly. For safety, always follow the rule: "walk without looking at the scenery, look at the scenery without walking." Walking and looking simultaneously is dangerous.

3. Lean slightly forward when climbing, and walk in a "Z" pattern. This is both easier and lighter.

4. Bring enough boiled water, beverages, and necessary medications when climbing.

5. During heavy rainstorms, floods can rise quickly and powerfully. Avoid washing clothes or playing in rivers before the rain to prevent accidents.

6. From April to October, on sunny days, the ultraviolet rays in Mount Huang are very strong. Avoid wearing short sleeves, bring sunscreen, and pay attention to sun protection.

7. When taking photos on dangerous and high peaks, choose your angle carefully and do not move, especially avoid stepping backward to prevent accidents.

8. Do not deface buildings, relics, rocks, or bamboo and wood with inscriptions.

9. To prevent fires, smoking is only allowed at designated points along the way. Smoking is permitted after reaching the liberated area, but cigarette butts should be disposed of in trash bins and not littered randomly.

10. To maintain cleanliness in the Mount Huang scenic area, do not litter. Trash bins shaped like trees are placed every few meters along the trail.

V: Mount Huang Cableways

Mount Huang Scenic Area has a total of three cableways: the front mountain's Jade Screen Cableway (Ci Guang Pavilion → Jade Screen Pavilion), the new Cloud Valley Cableway (Cloud Valley Temple → Little Fantasy), and the Peaceful Gate's Taiping Cableway (Songguan Temple → Vermilion Sandstone Peak). The Cloud Valley Cableway and Jade Screen Cableway enter from the south gate at Tangkou Town of the Mount Huang Scenic Area. The Taiping Cableway enters from the north gate of Huangshan District (originally Taiping County). Generally, only a few groups from Hefei, Tongling, and Anqing directions take the cableway up the mountain from the north gate, while most independent travelers start from the south gate at Tangkou.

Start to Believe Peak (viewing Huangshan unique pines and Monkey Watching the Sea), Dawn Pavilion (Immortal Backpack, Chancellor Watching Chess, Immortal Playing Chess), Scatter Flower Hollow (Dream Pen Producing Flowers, Brush Stand Peak), Hundred Step Cloud Ladder, Flying Stone, Paiyun Pavilion (Immortal Drying Boots, Fairy Playing Piano, etc.), West Sea Grand Canyon, Bright Top Peak (Fish Carrying a Golden Turtle), Turtle Peak, Lotus Peak, Heaven Capital Peak, Welcoming Guest Pavilion (Squirrel Jumping to Heaven Capital, Peacock Playing with Lotus), etc.
