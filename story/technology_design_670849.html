Monk Shang stole 1.3 million yuan from the temple to secularize after meeting a female netizen

by anonymous on 2012-02-21 11:56:35

The stolen property. Taking refuge in the Buddhist door was originally for seeking inner peace, reciting scriptures and cultivating oneself, which emphasizes having no desires or demands. However, Miao, who became a monk at the age of 14, first violated the Buddhist precepts due to his own wishes, then regretted it, losing his inner calmness. When Miao reached the age of budding emotions, he had a girlfriend whom he loved dearly and wanted to spend the rest of his life with her. For a monk, this was considered a violation of one's beliefs. Everyone has emotions and desires, so we can't simply judge whether Miao's worldly heart moved is right or wrong. If the dust root is not clean, returning to secular life is acceptable, but stealing property is unacceptable. This violates not only the golden rules of the temple but also the general laws and regulations of society. In the ten evils and five rebellions of Buddhism, "stealing" ranks second, "lust" ranks third, just after "killing". The attendant of the abbot of an ancient temple in Kunming not only had a girlfriend but also dared to steal all the temple's wealth, planning to take his girlfriend back to his hometown to live a happy secular life... Shockingly, the theft caused panic among the monks of the ancient temple. To ensure safety during the "two festivals, two sessions", the Guandu police were fully committed to implementing five special rectification works on social security, with the most important being to prevent "robbery and theft." While the rectification work was underway, on the 13th, there was suddenly a report of theft at an ancient temple. The stolen temple reported that the stolen items included more than 100,000 yuan in cash, gold bars, Apple iPads, etc. How dare someone violate the sacred ground of Buddhism? The Guandu Criminal Investigation Team immediately formed a special task force to conduct investigations. The stolen items were locked in a safe in the abbot's quarters, but there were no signs of forced entry on the safe. The video surveillance host in the room was also stolen. All signs indicated that this was not the work of an outside thief, but who among the Buddhist disciples would dare to violate the precepts of emptiness? After surveillance by the police, at noon on December 22nd, the criminal suspect Miao was arrested in a hotel near the train station. On the spot, from the bag of his girlfriend, they found three blocks of 1000 grams of gold bricks, 110,000 yuan in cash, an Apple iPad, some gold jewelry, and ivory prayer beads, with an estimated case value of around 1.3 million yuan. The suspect Miao was precisely the personal attendant of the abbot of the temple. Theft: Online acquaintance with girlfriend greatly moves worldly heart Miao, born in Wenzhou, Zhejiang in 1989, secretly left home at the age of 14 and became a monk in a temple in Zhejiang, taking refuge in Buddhism. Miao's insight quickly developed in the study of Buddhism, earning him much favor from his master. Three years ago, the abbot of an ancient temple in Kunming traveled to lecture at the temple where Miao took refuge. Due to his fondness for this intelligent disciple, he brought Miao back to Kunming. After arriving in Kunming, Miao always served as the abbot's personal attendant, responsible for the abbot's daily life. The abbot never avoided him and placed donations received from worshippers directly into the safe in the living quarters in front of Miao. As the abbot's personal attendant, Miao not only earned a high monthly salary but also had his own bedroom and could access the internet to understand the outside world. In September of this year, Miao met a girl from Chongqing selling clothes in Yuxi through QQ chat. The two young people, both at the age of budding emotions, gradually progressed from virtual conversations to real-life meetings. This had already violated Buddhist precepts, and Miao, seemingly unable to control himself, requested to leave the monastic life. Given Miao's high insight, the abbot naturally tried to persuade him to stay, but Miao's determination was firm; he couldn't let go of his romantic feelings. Money is an external object, and even the abbot himself did not know exactly how much was in the safe. But thinking about needing money after leaving the monastic life, Miao hatched a plan to steal from the safe. On the afternoon of December 12th, when the abbot was out, the key to the safe was still in the room, and Miao already knew the password by heart. He emptied the contents of the safe. At this point, he realized there was a camera installed in the living quarters. Determined to cover his tracks, Miao also stole the surveillance host, destroyed the evidence, and hid the stolen goods within the temple. Although the temple was in turmoil due to the theft, Miao pretended to remain calm until December 19th, when he thought the coast was clear and left the temple with the stolen goods. On December 20th, he went to Yuxi to meet his girlfriend. His girlfriend noticed the heavy package and upon inquiry, discovered it was all cash. Concerned about carrying large amounts of cash, they deposited part of it in the bank. Miao and his girlfriend planned to fly from Kunming to his girlfriend's hometown in Chongqing, then open a farm there and live happily ever after. Regarding the property, Miao told his girlfriend that it was his accumulated salary over the years in the temple. Regret: Kneeling before the abbot in confession After being captured, Miao reflected on the severity of his sins. Facing the police officer, he didn't want to say much but requested to see the abbot. Under police escort, Miao arrived at the ancient temple yesterday afternoon. As it was a sacred place of Buddhism, journalists were not allowed inside. In the police video footage, after identifying the scene, Miao saw the abbot calmly sitting aside and immediately knelt down. The abbot then began speaking. Although the content was unclear, it must have deeply impacted Miao, causing him to break down in tears and collapse into the abbot's arms. The abbot remained seated without touching this disciple who had broken the major precepts. Subsequently, Miao was taken to the scripture hall, where the temple held a meeting with all the monks, using Miao as a negative example, which made him extremely disheartened. Sitting in the police car, he repeatedly asked, "Why haven't we left yet?" Outside the car window, a classmate who had practiced alongside Miao for five years stood quietly. This fellow disciple of the same age as Miao had been constantly circling the temple since Miao entered. With tears in his eyes, he said, "I've been together with him for five years. He's not a bad person, just a momentary lapse in judgment." He knew Miao used the internet, but he didn't know Miao secretly had an online girlfriend. Unable to speak to Miao, he silently stood there, watching Miao leave. (Liu Xia, Intern Zhang Ke)
