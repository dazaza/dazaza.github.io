Who rejected whom?

All along, I thought he would always follow me, whenever I needed him. He said he would wait for me all his life. But he broke his promise.

My classmate and I in university. I entered the university at 16 years old, two years younger than him. In school, I was always a little sister, getting along well with many boys. Of course, we were especially close.

I really didn't think about romantic feelings, maybe because I was too young. Until that day, he suddenly blushed and handed me a movie ticket, stammering to say it was a love story. How old-fashioned, but cute. Still, I directly refused him. I told him he was not my type, and I wouldn't choose him, but he didn't give up.

In the following time, he was always around me, caring for me, protecting me. Anything I wanted to do, he had already done it for me. But I told him he wasn't my type, and he should stop. He said he would keep waiting until the day I agreed.

After graduating from university, both of us were recommended to be postgraduate students in our school, but not in the same major. All the experimental problems during the postgraduate period were basically handled by him. My tutor even joked with his tutor: Where is my son-in-law? Why haven't I seen him recently?

Five years of graduate school passed quickly, and I was 25 years old. I did indeed consider choosing him. But I never opened my mouth. Why... Maybe I still felt something was imperfect. He always looked sloppy, with messy hair, no attention to appearance. Moreover, he was too thin, not tall among southern people, weighing less than 100 pounds. I preferred athletic boys. I straightforwardly told him the reasons why I didn't choose him, and the usually talkative he remained silent for a long time. At that moment, I actually hoped he could leave after hearing these words, but he continued: "I am the best for you, forever."

After obtaining his Ph.D., I stayed as a teacher, and he chose to go abroad. When classmates saw him off, I didn't feel sad, instead, I had a sense of relief. The person who had been around me for seven years finally left, and I no longer had to repeat endlessly, "I don't like your type, give up" such words.

Soon I started dating a boyfriend, six years older than me, with a good family background, working in his father's company as a financial manager, earning over ten thousand yuan a month, with a house and car.

But as our relationship deepened, I found the gap. Whatever I said, he always mumbled "uh-huh", half understanding, half not. He couldn't understand my intentions. This relationship lasted intermittently for one and a half years, and finally ended in a breakup. It was then that I remembered the person who always stood behind me smiling, ready to do anything for me.

Since I was little, I have always been an excellent student, attending one of the best universities. During my undergraduate studies, I was directly recommended to pursue a Ph.D., and graduated as an outstanding Ph.D. student. So I thought the people around me should be like this, without needing me to express myself carefully. But I was wrong.

In my frustration, I welcomed my 28th birthday. A 28-year-old woman seems to give a different impression. Although looking in the mirror, I didn't feel inferior or old, my parents and friends began to worry about me. Thus, I started a series of blind dates.

Those blind date candidates were either rich but vulgar, or dull and slow, or timid and inarticulate. One by one, they only made me think more of him far away. That wise, lazy, caring, humorous, and eloquent him.

At my associate professor evaluation meeting, I suddenly spotted him. Sitting in the audience, his eyes were still so bright, and he was still so thin. His hair looked dirty, holding a pen, sitting in the front row and smiling at me. The only change seemed to be that he looked a bit older, no longer like the young man who used to debate with others nonstop.

It turned out that he had been introduced back to the school as a talent, not in our department, but in the same college. While I was applying for associate professor, the school had already promised him the position of full professor. Therefore, he was entitled to a modest welfare apartment in this land-scarce city, which was quite decent.

When he was renovating his house, I often went to his home to give advice, seemingly returning to the days four years ago. He served me water, peeled fruits for me, and answered various questions about work.

One day, when his house renovation was completed, he invited me to have dinner. Only the two of us, the evening lights were beautiful. I knew what he was going to say, a bit expectant, yet a bit nervous. Sure enough, he said, "I have a house, but I lack a bride."

I pretended not to understand, but in fact, I hoped he would be as direct as four years ago, clearly saying: "This lifetime, I will let you know I am the best for you." But he didn't. He just sighed, saying nothing.

When he sent me downstairs, he actually held my hand tightly, so tight that it hurt a bit. Fortunately, he soon let go. Then he walked back without even saying goodbye.

The days afterward became awkward suddenly. When he met me, he was indifferent, just nodding and greeting. He didn't sit with me on the school bus. He didn't talk to me while waiting for the bus. When I asked him to be a committee member for my master's thesis defense, he declined.

Finally, I saw him with another girl, chatting and laughing. I felt sour inside, and inexplicably angry. My best friend persuaded me, saying that since he started pursuing me in the third year of university, what was the harm in me chasing him once? I was almost thirty years old.

But when I gathered all my courage to tell him, he calmly said, "I already have a girlfriend!"

I also heard that he was dating someone, but how could it be so fast? From when I was 19 to now, so many years of feelings, he had waited for so long, could he forget everything in an instant? Didn't he say he would wait for me all his life?

I cried all night last night. For so many years, I've never cried like that. Are men really so easy to transfer their feelings? Really?
