Apple Plans First Tokyo Retail Store in Eight Years: Report

by anonymous on 2013-08-29 16:40:43

According to a well-informed source, riding on the "tailwind" of Japan's recovering economy, Apple plans to open a retail store in Tokyo's upscale Omotesando shopping district as early as March next year. This will be the company’s first retail store in Tokyo in many years.

Bloomberg reported that this unnamed source expects construction of the store to be completed by February next year. According to Apple's website, this marks the company's first return to Tokyo since August 2005. Currently, Apple is posting job advertisements related to this new retail store on its website. Takashi Takebayashi, Apple's spokesperson in Tokyo, has yet to comment on this news.

Sources revealed that the retail store currently under construction represents Apple's nearly decade-long preparation to re-enter Tokyo. Recently, Japanese Prime Minister Shinzo Abe announced the third part of his growth plan, which previously included fiscal stimulus and monetary policy.

"For Apple, the Japanese market is attractive both in terms of volume and price," said SMBC Nikko Securities analyst Satoru Kikuchi. "If Apple increases its mobile carrier partnerships, there is still room for expansion in tablet sales, and it is also likely to have opportunities for growth overall in the Japanese market."

On August 8, Kazuto Tsubouchi, the CFO of NTT DoCoMo, Japan's largest wireless carrier, stated that if the proportion of iPhone sales could be limited to less than 30% of total sales, the company would consider selling iPhones. Currently, DoCoMo's online store dmarket offers products such as music, videos, and games, competing with Apple's iTunes Store.

Real Estate

As part of Abe's "third arrow" plan, he has pledged to ease commercial regulations and implement more government support measures to assist various industries in Japan. Macroeconomic data released earlier showed an increase in Japan's consumer price index in June, and the annualized GDP growth rate for the quarter ending June 30 reached 2.6%. Currently, Japan is the world's third-largest economy after the United States and China.

According to Seth Sulkin, representative director of Tokyo-based real estate and asset management company Pacifica Capital KK, the land cost for Apple's upcoming retail store is around 16 billion yen (approximately $164 million). After completion, Apple will occupy the space as a tenant, at which point the property's value is expected to reach about 25 billion yen (approximately $256 million). Previously, Apple had opened seven retail stores in Japan, and Sulkin served as Apple's real estate advisor for all these stores.

"Apple wants the best possible real estate," said Sulkin. "They are particularly focused on factors such as size and shape. If they need to wait some time to secure the real estate they want, they will wait."

Earlier this year, Apple moved its Tokyo headquarters from Shinjuku to Roppongi Hills due to lower rent.

Expensive Rent

The construction sign at the site indicates that the architectural firm responsible for building the new Apple retail store is Jun Mitsui & Associate based in Tokyo. According to the company's website, projects it has worked on include the DE BEERS Ginza Building and the New Haneda Airport International Terminal.

A knowledgeable source previously disclosed that Apple plans to hold a launch event on September 10 to announce a new iPhone smartphone, and Apple has committed to releasing "several other game-changing products" as it seeks to counter competition from Samsung Electronics and Chinese low-cost smartphone manufacturers.

On the same day, Apple's stock price rose by $2.31 during regular trading on Nasdaq, closing at $490.90, up 0.47%. As of Tuesday's close this year, Apple's stock price has fallen by 8.2%.

According to the construction sign, the building accommodating Apple's new Tokyo retail store will have three floors, including two basement levels, with a total floor area of 20,109 square feet (approximately 1,868 square meters), though the sign does not specify how much space Apple will occupy. The construction site is located near other luxury brand stores such as Gucci and Coach under Kering.

Data from real estate services company CBRE Group shows that the rent for the best retail spaces in Tokyo is $864 per square foot annually (approximately $9,300 per square meter annually), ranking third in Asia. Hong Kong, which has three Apple retail stores, has the highest rents at $4,328 per square foot annually (approximately $46,600 per square meter annually).
