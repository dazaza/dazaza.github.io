Sony executive in Greater China: the first to launch a 5-mode 11-frequency TD-LTE smartphone

by anonymous on 2013-10-02 10:36:27

Sony Mobile Greater China Vice President and Marketing Department Director Shuren Zheng (center), Sony Mobile Greater China Chief Technology Officer and Vice President Chuan Liu (right), host Sina News Chief Reporter Zhao Kang (left).

The 2013 Telecommunications Exhibition entered its third day today. During a visit to the Sina "President Online" interview, Sony Mobile Greater China Vice President and Marketing Department Director Shuren Zheng and Sony Mobile Greater China Chief Technology Officer and Vice President Chuan Liu stated that Sony started early in the 4G era and is the only manufacturer to achieve a 5-mode 11-band TD-LTE smartphone.

Below is the full transcript of the interview:

Sina Technology: Hello netizens! The 2013 China International Telecommunications Exhibition has entered its third day. We at Sina Technology have invited important guests to our "President Online Interview Program." We are fortunate to have invited Mr. Shuren Zheng, Vice President and Marketing Department Director of Sony Mobile Greater China, and Mr. Chuan Liu, Chief Technology Officer and Vice President of Sony Mobile Greater China. Let's first ask Mr. Zheng and Mr. Liu to greet our netizens.

Shuren Zheng: Hello everyone, I am Shuren Zheng.

Chuan Liu: Hello everyone, my name is Chuan Liu, and I am responsible for technology at Sony Mobile.

Sina Technology: Sony Mobile is a very familiar mobile phone brand to everyone and holds an important position in the global mobile industry. We are delighted to have both of you visiting our interview program today. First, we would like to ask about the preparations Sony Mobile has made for China's 4G smartphone market during the pre-4G license issuance phase. We see that Sony Mobile should already be prepared with 4G smartphones. What preparations has Sony Mobile made for the Chinese 4G smartphone market?

Shuren Zheng: Sony Mobile was one of the earliest companies to participate in 4G, especially TD-LTE product development. If we talk about when we began our participation, it dates back to before 2011, approximately three years ago. At that time, we collaborated with China Mobile, Ericsson, and what was then Sony Ericsson to demonstrate a TD-LTE trial in Barcelona with China Mobile. We created a prototype model from that point onward, and we have closely cooperated with China Mobile to develop TD-LTE products. In the recently concluded MAE Asia Mobile Communications Expo in Shanghai in June, we also launched our first TD-LTE product, the Xperia SP M35t. Recently, this product has also been one of the first four products domestically to receive the LTE (TD-LTE) terminal network access license. We have great expectations and confidence in the development of 4G in China. We must utilize the opportunity presented by 4G to develop our market share and business well in the Chinese market.

Chuan Liu: As Mr. Zheng mentioned, there is one point I want to emphasize from a technical perspective. There are several "bests" among the currently certified four phones, including one or two models from Apple, and we are the only ones to achieve a 5-mode 11-band product. Currently, China Mobile requires only 5-mode 10-band. Why can only we achieve this? Because neither the chip nor the platform currently possesses such capability. We have invested a lot of manpower and resources to create it on the existing platform, and China Mobile is very satisfied with our efforts.

Sina Technology: Previously, Sony Mobile mainly focused on WCDMA phones, but had fewer TD phones. In the 4G era, why are you leading the way in developing and promoting TD-LTE phones?

Shuren Zheng: From Sony Mobile itself, we are implementing the "One Sony" strategy. A crucial aspect of the "One Sony" strategy is integrating the best technologies used in other Sony products into our smartphone products. When we integrate these technologies into our products, we can also achieve strong entertainment multimedia functions. Therefore, when we look at 4G, consumers will enjoy more and more active entertainment content through their phones, whether it’s music, video, or social activities. By offering the best experience through Sony phones, these are all opportunities for us. Through the 4G network, the download of such content differs from 2G and 3G. In 2G and 3G, watching videos would lag, and listening to music might not work. Now, with 4G, it is a great opportunity for consumers to fully experience the entertainment and multimedia experience we offer through the 4G network.

We have identified this opportunity and hope to differentiate our products through it, so consumers will know.

Chuan Liu: To supplement from the technical side, I want to emphasize that Sony Mobile has strong global and Chinese technical accumulations. Therefore, in the 4G era, we have strong technical accumulation. With close cooperation with operators, for example, from the voice solution perspective, operators were initially uncertain about which voice solution to adopt. We promptly provided various necessary voice solutions to meet their choices. Due to our accumulation and investment, we lead in 4G technology from the start.

Sina Technology: I have a very specific question. You have done relatively less in TD-SCDMA. Since 5-mode 10-band requires extensive use of TD-SCDMA due to compatibility, are there any issues?

Chuan Liu: Of course, there are no problems. Our investment and market strategy, as Mr. Zheng mentioned, still involves a commercial investment and return issue. During the explosive growth of 4G, our accumulation has always been here. So, in 4G, we see this business opportunity, see our leadership and close cooperation with operators, and the opportunities they bring to us and the entire industry chain. Thus, our investment and efforts become stronger.

Sina Technology: Where is your R&D team for 4G phones located, in China or abroad?

Chuan Liu: The TD-LTE R&D team is entirely in China, and the leading release is in China. Regarding 4G, there are FDD and TDD technologies. FDD technology is used in different parts of the world. TDD technology is entirely in China, and we also utilize other resources.

Sina Technology: A company under Japan's SoftBank Group is building a TD-LTE network. Have you participated?

Chuan Liu: SoftBank does have a network, and currently, we do not provide them with products because of the special demands of the Japanese market. You know that Sony Mobile provides DOCOMO with comprehensive products.

Sina Technology: We've been talking about TD-LTE phones. Globally, FDD-LTE phones are more commonly used, although China may not issue this license this year. How is your technology and global usage for FDD-LTE phones?

Chuan Liu: We are very leading globally, whether in Hong Kong or some European countries, our market share has reached the top three or even higher. Our FDD accumulation has always been leading, and our 5-mode 11-band includes both FDD and TDD. Globally, we are very leading, and our market share is also ahead. In China, we hope that through the investment of engineering and technical personnel and through our deep cooperation with the market and operators, we can also reach a leading position.

Shuren Zheng: For FDD, let's take Hong Kong as an example. Hong Kong was one of the earliest markets to launch FDD, and our market share ranks second or third there.

Sina Technology: What technical difficulties exist?

Chuan Liu: Domestic manufacturers are also working on FDD-LTE, and the industrial chain is relatively mature. From platforms to operators, the threshold is relatively low. TDD platform vendors have not yet been able to provide corresponding services, such as 11 bands and different voice solutions. We lead because of our technical accumulation and certain technical thresholds. I believe that in TDD, the threshold will decrease quickly due to the enhancement of platform capabilities and the participation of more platform vendors. Since we have already taken the lead, we will use this opportunity to expand our advantages.

Sina Technology: Cooperation with operators is a very important channel for mobile phone manufacturers. How does Sony Mobile view its cooperation with operators? What is the future development strategy?

Shuren Zheng: This will definitely be a main channel, the operator channel, including their own channels and open channels. Their customized products account for about half. Looking forward, we see this proportion increasing. In the 4G era, the three operators have subsidies, making this a necessary channel. We also recognize the 4G opportunity and place great importance on the Xperia SP M35t product. In June, we jointly released the first TD-LTE product, the Xperia SP M35t, with China Mobile. In the future, we will cooperate with China Mobile on more products.

We will also cooperate with China Unicom and China Telecom in other areas. China Mobile is relatively earlier, so we have products ready for the market first.

Chuan Liu: Technically, our long-term cooperation with operators in the backend has been ongoing. Looking ahead, a year ago, we planned this product. In leading provinces like Guangdong and Zhejiang, we invested a lot of resources. We prepared the 11-band technology early on. During the early stages of China Mobile's entire TLE network construction, we were already involved in debugging and optimization. On the technical level, our cooperation with China Mobile and operators is very tight until the release of this product today.

In fact, our cooperation with China Mobile has gone far beyond what you can see today. For example, the next step in voice technology, including multi-band aggregation, many technologies are being developed with China Mobile. We are also doing a lot with other operators, including many technologies from China Unicom. This is our global strength in FDD. Now, the FDD technology globally is closely cooperating with China Unicom, and the same applies to China Telecom. All three operators are involved, and currently, China Mobile is leading in the 4G race, so we focus more on this.

Sina Technology: My last question is about the Xperia SP M35t, which is the first shot fired by China Mobile in the 4G smartphone market. What are Sony Mobile's plans for 4G smartphones in China in the future?

Shuren Zheng: Sony Mobile focuses on developing mid-to-high-end products. Our first step in 4G products is also starting from mid-to-high-end products. The released Xperia SP M35t belongs to the mid-to-high-end category. By the end of this year and early next year, we will also launch some high-end products. We believe that in the initial phase, China Mobile will also aim to attract some high-end users. By the middle to late next year, some mid-to-low-end products will be launched.

Sina Technology: Due to time constraints, our interview is coming to an end. Please say a few words to our netizens.

Chuan Liu: In the 4G era, Sony Mobile is currently leading in technology. We hope to continue consolidating and strengthening our leadership in technology and bring more surprises to netizens and users.

Shuren Zheng: As we just mentioned, Sony Mobile has a strong multimedia entertainment advantage worldwide. We also hope to bring better entertainment experiences to consumers through the faster 4G network.

Sina Technology: Thank you to both guests for visiting our program, and also thank all the netizens who actively participated. This concludes our interview. Thank you and goodbye!
