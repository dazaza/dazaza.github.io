Volume One: "Time-Honored Chinese Figure Painting"

Figure painting is one of the three main categories of traditional Chinese painting and is the earliest category to emerge. It is a symbol of the independence and maturity of Chinese painting art. Respected as the "Sage of Painting," Wu Daozi was a master of figure painting. The content of figure paintings is diverse, with themes ranging from historical paintings, genre paintings, portraits, to images of court ladies. Techniques include meticulous brushwork with color, white-line drawing, simplified brushwork, splashed ink, and meticulous brushwork. Masters such as Gu Kaizhi, Zhang Sengyou, Yan Liben, Wu Daozi, Li Gonglin, Tang Yin, Qiu Ying, and Chen Hongshou have left behind masterpieces that excel in various ways and stand unparalleled through the ages. These works reflect the social appearances and customs of different periods beyond their unique artistic value, offering unparalleled historical and sociological document value compared to other painting genres.

Volume Three: "Time-Honored Chinese Flower-and-Bird Painting"

Flower-and-bird painting is one of the three main categories of ancient Chinese painting most adept at "expressing aspirations through objects" and conveying emotions through borrowed objects. Its content includes not only flowers, birds, but also livestock, fish, algae, trees, bamboo rocks, and even fruits and vegetables. Styles range from meticulously detailed gongbi flower-and-bird paintings to lively and free xieyi flower-and-bird paintings. Themes like plum blossoms, orchids, bamboo, and chrysanthemums, known as the Four Gentlemen, are widely loved by scholars and common people alike. From Emperor Huizong Zhao Ji to the "Eight Eccentrics of Yangzhou," Chinese flower-and-bird painting boasts the richest variety of painters, schools, and styles, as well as the most complex and varied techniques, earning the widest international acclaim for traditional Chinese painting and being highly favored by domestic and international art galleries, museums, various collecting institutions, and private collectors.

Volume Four: "Time-Honored Chinese Landscape Painting"

Landscape painting is the category of ancient Chinese painting that best represents Chinese philosophical thought and Chinese painting aesthetics. In the more than two thousand years of development from the Wei-Jin-Nanbei dynasties to the Yuan, Ming, and Qing dynasties, there emerged various painting methods such as blue-green landscapes, ink-wash landscapes, and scholar-small-scene paintings. Renowned masters appeared generation after generation, including Zhan Ziqian from the Sui dynasty, Li Sixun from the Tang dynasty, Jing Hao, Guan Tong, Dong Yuan, Ju Ran from the Five Dynasties, Li Cheng, Fan Kuan from the Northern Song dynasty, Li Tang, Liu Songnian, Ma Yuan, Xia Gui from the Southern Song dynasty, Huang Gongwang, Wu Zhen, Ni Zan, Wang Meng from the Yuan dynasty, Shen Zhou, Wen Zhengming, Tang Yin, Qiu Ying from the Ming dynasty, and Dong Qichang from the Qing dynasty. They created countless masterpieces that had an extremely profound impact on the painting world of later generations.
