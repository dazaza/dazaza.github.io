Beijing Time, the current Beijing Time, Beijing Standard Time

What is Beijing Time based on?

China adopts the standard time of the Eastern Eighth Time Zone, where Beijing is located, as the standard time, known as Beijing Time. Beijing Time is the mean solar time of the 120th meridian east, not the local mean solar time of Beijing. Beijing's geographical location is at 116 degrees 21' east longitude, so its local mean solar time is about 14 and a half minutes later than Beijing Time. Beijing Time is 8 hours ahead of Coordinated Universal Time (UTC).

The Earth is divided into 24 equal parts, each part being one time zone. Thus, one time zone spans 15 degrees of longitude. A day has 24 hours, so there is a one-hour difference for each time zone. The time at zero degrees longitude, or the Prime Meridian, is the world standard time. Since the Prime Meridian passes through Greenwich near London, it is also called Greenwich Mean Time (GMT), which is also the standard time in the UK. Beijing's longitude is 116 degrees 21', so it falls within the eighth time zone to the east of the Prime Meridian, also known as the Eastern Eighth Time Zone. 8×15=120, so the standard time of the Eastern Eighth Time Zone is the time at 120 degrees east longitude, which is Beijing Time.

So, where is Beijing Time calculated and released? It comes from the National Time Service Center located in Pucheng County, Shaanxi Province (detailed address: No. 3 Shuyuan East Road, Lintong District, Xi'an City, Shaanxi Province). This location was chosen because: Central Shaanxi is in the heartland of the continent, only 100 kilometers away from the geodetic origin of China, making it convenient to cover the whole country with the transmitted time signals; the local geological structure is stable, minimizing the risk of the timing center being destroyed by disasters such as earthquakes; due to its importance, it is safer to build it inland.
