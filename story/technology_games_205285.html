Is it happiness or pain?

by lk0gamei on 2009-07-18 23:32:30

The greatest fear in life is not death, but the inseparable loneliness. When you are in a sea of people and still feel that chilling sensation in your heart, have you ever felt a nameless fear rising in your heart?

In March 2004, at the age of 17, I went to Guangdong alone and spent two years working there. I didn't learn much, only how to be strong and live with loneliness. Finally, I returned home where my family was, and I wouldn't be so lonely. Under the influence of my brother, I started playing WOW. I chose a small LR because I thought LRs were lonely. But later, I found out that shadow Mages are even lonelier than LRs. LRs at least have pets for company, and when they're bored, they can go down to FB or make some money. However, no matter how lonely the shadow Mage is, I like this profession. It might be a reflection of my two-year experience in Guangdong.

I met Mei by chance during a dungeon run with friends, and we added each other on QQ. I was level 54, and she was level 23.

Later, I helped her with many quests, and we talked a lot. I learned some things about her from our conversations.

Every evening at 9 PM, it was work time. As soon as I logged onto QQ, she would request a video call. I admit, she wasn't very beautiful, but she had a certain charm that made her seem cute. She used voice chat while I typed. After chatting for a while, she said she wanted to play WOW. I stopped my work and helped her with her quests. She was always clumsy. I had already told her that she didn't need to heal me when I fought monsters, but whenever I pulled a mob, she would hover around me. Her level was low, so she often attracted extra mobs. I would help her fight them off. When I saw my health was down to half, she would heal me, causing all the mobs she had attracted to attack her instead. The result was predictable. Watching her fumble around, I didn't know whether to laugh or cry. That night, she stayed up late, probably until 4 AM. When she left, she put a big mooncake on my head. In the game, I gave her a flying kiss and asked if she had seen what I had given her. Her response was, "Yes, I saw it, but I'm too shy to say it..." Watching her slowly disappear at the edge of the Twilight Forest, that familiar chilling feeling rose in my heart again.

The next day, it was the same routine: going to work and logging onto QQ. She asked me to call her sister because she was four months older than me. But I really didn't want to call her sister. Eventually, she insisted I just call her "Mei." During that time, I really liked listening to the song called "Silk Road."
