Transformers 3

by hhhiii83805 on 2011-08-26 13:23:39

Don't tell me that Optimus Prime and Bumblebee are not real. That would be like telling Western kids "Santa Claus doesn't exist," which is heart-breaking. This kind of debate isn't about facts; I believe they are out there somewhere, maybe even in the car speeding by me, quietly protecting me, protecting you, protecting our planet.

Yes, I've always believed this, ever since I was very young.

One of my clearest childhood memories is sitting on a small stool every evening in front of a black-and-white screen no bigger than an A4 sheet, watching how Optimus Prime led the Autobots to dazzlingly defeat the Decepticons' plots. At such a young age, I didn't fully understand the plot, but I just needed to confirm each time: Oh yeah, the Autobots won again!

I remember the little boy next door had two Transformers toys that could fold into cars. Back then, I didn't envy the dolls the girls around me held, but I envied his Transformers. However, he only showed them to me and never let me play with them. I didn't dare ask my mom to buy one for me, nor did I dare take other people's toys. So throughout my childhood, the Autobot heroes were unattainable for me. I could only console myself: Even if I can't see where the Autobots are, they must be around me, somewhere in the world. No matter how far away, no matter how the Decepticons eye the Earth greedily, the Autobots will fly as fast as light to save the people in danger.

To this day, I can't remember the details of the cartoon's plot, but I can still hum the opening music from the Transformers cartoon.

Along with Doraemon who could conjure all sorts of magical gadgets;

The Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles who ate pizza and fought in the sewers;

A group of little tough guys in bronze armor who couldn't be killed no matter what;

Wataro driving the Shenlong through layers of mountain ranges;

He-Man holding a sword while wearing only underwear;

The human cannon that shot people out of a cannon;

...

A group of heroes who grew up with me.

Can you say they never existed?

No, they must have existed.

They saved me from the fears of my childhood,

They were my motivation to imitate,

Letting me believe even when bullied: Bad people will be punished.

Such a silly childhood was filled with images of various heroes. It's less about memory and more about being integrated into my entire life. Even though I'm no longer a naive little kid, I still stubbornly say:

There are heroes.

They may not be human, yet they are more than human.

The movie version of "Transformers" undoubtedly let me relive my childhood, reminding me:

The hero is still there.

After three movies, many Autobots have died (Ironhide fell in this episode),

There has been betrayal, failure, and unease.

However, Optimus Prime still stands on the cliff overlooking the sunset and vast land, shouting deeply to the universe:

We will defend the Earth, our friends, and my home.

That's why I gave "Transformers 3" five stars.

Because it made my childhood memories so vivid.

For two and a half hours, even though the air conditioning was fully on inside the room, I was sweating from my chest and palms due to the emotional pull of the plot.

Even though I know the director's rule that "heroes never die,"

When I saw Bumblebee's head being pointed at by Megatron's gun, I was still extremely tense;

When the Autobots were driven off the Earth and were about to leave on a spaceship, I wanted to jump out and bite the ignition switch;

And when the missile hit the spaceship, I felt like I had lost a group of friends I'd known for many, many years, unable to stop tears streaming down my face.

The movie ended, and reality returned.

I know the Autobots will never appear.

No, they will appear.

Just like Optimus said to Sam:

"Even if you don't trust us, trust yourself."

The greatest salvation is saving the heart, saving the soul.

These are my heroes.

When I walked out of the cinema and passed by a beautiful Chevrolet in the parking lot, I asked:

Hey, are you Bumblebee? Linkin Park's soundtrack for this movie was as excellent as always, highly recommended.
