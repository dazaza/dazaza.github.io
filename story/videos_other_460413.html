The fluctuations in the global economy have presented unprecedented challenges to private enterprises, especially Chinese apparel companies. However, within this changing landscape lie both crises and opportunities. For apparel export companies, it is time to stop following the OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturer) processing path and instead adjust their growth strategies to follow a brand-oriented route. The "return" of apparel brands also represents an exploration of survival paths for private enterprises in difficult situations.

In the face of export difficulties caused by the global financial crisis, many Chinese textile and apparel companies that are primarily export-oriented have started "returning" to the domestic market in search of new prospects. The strategic thinking behind the accelerated transformation and upgrading of China's textile and apparel industry is becoming increasingly clear.

Statistics show that in the first ten months of 2008, the total export value of textiles and clothing increased by only about 5.9% year-on-year, a drop of 13.84% compared to the same period in 2007, marking the lowest level in nearly six years.

Facing these difficulties, BASICEDITIONS, one of China's first high-end apparel brands to enter the international market, has recently begun to realign its growth strategy direction, returning to develop the domestic market. It has established its first brand showcase center in Beijing at Yabaicheng.

Mr. Li Kaiguo, President of BASICEDITIONS, stated in an interview that in response to the global financial crisis, the company brings with it years of experience in dealing with the ever-changing international markets and brand management concepts, returning to explore the domestic market. On one hand, this is because the government is actively expanding domestic demand and promoting consumption, which presents an opportunity for the enterprise; on the other hand, it is also to explore a survival and growth path for Chinese enterprises under the context of the global financial crisis.

According to Mr. Li Kaiguo, as early as ten years ago, BASICEDITIONS experienced the Asian financial crisis. "At that time, the impact on us was really severe. However, we also realized during that crisis the importance of building a brand. Therefore, in today's crisis, we are no longer as lost as we were back then because we now have our own brand and resilience."

Mr. Li Kaiguo added that although BASIC has been growing overseas over the years, it has always closely followed the domestic apparel industry situation, looking for opportunities to enter the Chinese market. In his view, with the development of these years, China has become a very large market. While many high-end foreign apparel brands have entered China, BASIC has been exploring in European fashion capitals and overlooked the Chinese market.

"In recent years, the domestic apparel industry has undergone many twists and turns, especially affected by the economic crisis in Europe and America. The apparel industry faces greater difficulties, and companies without their own brands are hit even harder. To address this, the government is vigorously supporting brand construction and encouraging leading enterprises to accelerate their growth. As a national apparel brand, BASIC has the responsibility and ability to return home and introduce its experience in responding to the ever-changing international market and brand management concepts to domestic peers. Together, we can explore ways to deal with the crisis. On the other hand, with the deepening of our country's economic capabilities and the formation of domestic high-end consumer groups, the fashion industry continues to grow. Chinese consumers' demands for clothing are increasing, providing us with an opportunity," Mr. Li Kaiguo said.

In Mr. Li Kaiguo's view, whether from external or internal factors, it is time to vigorously develop the domestic market. He introduced that the company's target consumer group is the domestic high-end consumer class. Therefore, the company will operate the domestic market according to the practices of high-end apparel brands. First, it will establish a brand showcase center, and next, it will set up flagship stores in high-end consumption venues such as Wangfujing and Shin Kong Place domestically. "Although domestic consumers may not yet be familiar with us compared to many other brands, we believe that BASIC already has enough strength to compete with other international brands domestically."

It is understood that BASIC currently holds a place in more than twenty countries, including France, Italy, Germany, and Russia, and has registered trademarks in 77 countries, including the UK.

As a self-owned high-end apparel brand that has undergone the baptism of the European market, Mr. Li Kaiguo believes that whether Chinese apparel enterprises are striving abroad or returning home to grow, they should pay attention to several points. On the one hand, they need to timely register trademarks to protect their own brands. On the other hand, they need to cultivate independent designer teams and form their own brand styles. Additionally, they should participate more in top-tier fashion exhibitions, integrate with international mainstream trends, let everyone know them, and continuously improve themselves through these exhibitions.

"The fluctuations in the global economy present unprecedented challenges to private enterprises, especially Chinese apparel companies. However, within this changing landscape lie both crises and opportunities. The key lies in recognizing the situation, seizing the opportunity, and adjusting growth strategies in a timely manner." Sun Xiaohua, Vice Chairman of the All-China Federation of Industry and Commerce, strongly supports BASIC's return to the domestic market. He stated that for apparel export companies, it is time to stop following the OEM processing path and instead adjust their growth strategies towards a brand-oriented route. The "return" of BASICEDITIONS is not only an action to align with the government’s 'expanding domestic demand' initiative but also an exploration of survival paths for private enterprises in difficult situations.

Zhang Yan Kai, Vice President of the China Textile Association, also pointed out that the return of more enterprises like BASIC to the Chinese market will bring confidence to the entire industry and provide technological and brand momentum for the sustainable development of China's textile and apparel industry.
