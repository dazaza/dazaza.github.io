Basic elements of search engine optimization_1513

by zcsmnif5 on 2012-03-05 16:24:21

My aunt and uncle in Cologne called me and asked how to make their new site successful on Google, so I told them some of my secrets - if you already know about SEO (Search Engine Optimization), you can skip this; if not, there are three steps that will help you rank well in search engines. First, create good content. Second, make the content easy to read. Third, share your content with others.

1. Create Good Content

You want a good Google ranking, but you should first ask yourself: why should your site rank higher? In the face of fierce online competition for website optimization services, which one is the main reason you want to rank higher? You need more visitors, you want to spread something you personally think is very important, you want to change the world, or you just want to sell something. Why should your site rank higher? In the eyes of Google and other search engines, you're just another webmaster - and search engines provide the important parts to searchers. So it's best to firmly believe that you should rank higher on Google, and that you're providing something to searchers. Once you've done this, you can move on to the second and third steps. So how do you do it? Search engines primarily aim to understand text. So you should strive to create interesting, deep, text-based content. Using Flash animations, videos, or some images won't have a negative effect, but they also won't greatly help search engines. The best way to test whether search engines can understand a webpage is to look at the source code (right-click and select "View Source", other browsers may call it something else) and see what text is displayed.

Afterwards, there might be a large number of search queries linking to your site. At this point, it's crucial to decisively abandon optimizing every query series. The most realistic strategy is to give your site more relevant content. If your site is related to dog supplies, then write about dogs, dog food, dog clothing styles, insights into making dog shoes; you can also include one or two articles on Renaissance-era dog-keeping tips (try translating them into different languages).

Let's assume you really sell dog supplies, and your competitor is the same, with a lot of relevant content on that site. What you need is only one thing: not just creating relevant content, but also striving to create original content; create games related to dog names; make incremental charts about dogs; provide distinctive wallpapers for dog lovers to download. You should understand - do something others haven't thought of. Being distinctive means giving internet users a reason to come to your site rather than your competitor's.

Creating a large amount of original content is relatively easy work. So what's difficult? That's establishing authority in the field, and there's no shortcut for that. Of course, it's likely that you're already an expert, which is great. In that case, I suggest you share your expert knowledge. People will love your site because they can gain some knowledge, and obviously, this will change their lives, even if the change is small (it could be that you bring them effective ways to do things, or you solve one of their problems). Conversely, if you're not an expert in the given field, I suggest you create a blog, spend an hour or more each day researching related knowledge and news, and write as you learn. After about half a year, you should become an expert in the blog topic.

2. Make Your Content Easier to Read

Now we shift away from your area of concern - dog supplies, or whatever else you're doing - to techniques that make your content easier to read. Unlike human visitors who hope to see pictures and text content, search bots see raw materials, such as HTML tags, webpage titles, links, bold text tags, etc. HTML changes aren't fast, but you might still do something wrong. So don't try to follow someone else's ideas blindly; understanding both sides of a matter is necessary: Each page should ideally focus on one thing. (This should be doable.) If you have a page selling dog clothes, then that page should have: a bold section containing the three words “dog clothes”; a picture of dog clothes; an article about selling dog clothes; a description of the garment's properties. Additionally, there should be some guiding links to related site content, such as links to all dog supplies; a link describing your company (which should at least include the company address, your email, and other contact information); a link returning to the homepage; etc.

Each page's title should be unique and ensure it accurately and convincingly describes the content of the page. If your page is about blue dog clothing, then the page's title should include “blue dog clothing,” for example, “Buy a blue outfit for your dog at [website name],” instead of just “[Website Name]” or “[Website Name] sells trendy inner and outerwear for cats and dogs.”

Use markup carefully with tags like h1 and h2 for bolding, p for paragraphs, and a for links, ensuring the site conforms to official HTML4 or XHTML1 standards. There are many ways to display navigation bars on websites, such as using dynamic Flash, multi-layers, dropdowns, or other JS scripts. These can lead to mistakes, so keep the HTML simple and use JS scripts only when necessary. Use CSS for page layout, which is the best option.

Ensure each page loads simply, stably, and quickly. For example, don’t use different URLs, don’t create redirects, and avoid overly long and complex URLs. Simple and stable webpages are convenient for people to bookmark or reference and will earn trust evaluations from search engines like Google. If your webpage is about red dog clothing, its URL should be “website name/clothes/red” or “website name/product/red dog clothing.html”.

Doing these key points well isn't easy, and sometimes mistakes can happen, but you don't need to perfect everything at once. Ideally, use a CMS system that you or your web developer can fully control and create a template, which is a good method. However, before enabling it, it's best to conduct some tests, such as adding new products or articles, adjusting the size of all bold text, or replacing the footer on all pages. If this causes you headaches, then it proves the system was chosen incorrectly.
