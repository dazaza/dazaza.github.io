Windows Phone gains record market share in Europe

by anonymous on 2013-09-04 14:39:36

According to TNW, the latest report released by market research group Kantar shows that from April to July this year, Windows Phone achieved an 8.2% share of smartphone sales in the five major European markets including the UK, Germany, France, Italy and Spain, setting a new historical record.

In addition to the above-mentioned five European countries, Windows Phone also achieved historic success in Mexico across the ocean, with a sales share as high as 11.6%. Dominic Sunnebo, Director of Strategic Research at Kantar, pointed out that the biggest success of Windows Phone lies in attracting a large number of former feature phone users who are trying smartphones for the first time. This is thanks to the precise positioning of mid-to-low-end models represented by the Lumia 520. Data shows that 42% of new Windows Phone users come from feature phones, a proportion much higher than that of iOS and Android.

Of course, the high proportion of former feature phone users among new Windows Phone users is related to its relatively low base. Moreover, Windows Phone has limited appeal to iOS and Android users, with only 27% of its new users coming from the iOS and Android camps. Kantar believes that as long as Windows Phone can capture the interest of feature phone users, it will be able to enjoy huge growth potential - taking the UK as an example, as of July this year, 34% of telecom users nationwide are still using feature phones.

Contrary to the growth of Windows Phone, during the same period, BlackBerry's sales share in the aforementioned five European countries was only 2.4%, leaving it on the brink of survival.
