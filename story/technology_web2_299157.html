Giants praise the new No. 10 of AC Milan as comparable to Boban, revealing that Kaka also felt regret for leaving.

by kersfe3f23 on 2009-12-08 16:26:18

Giants praise AC Milan's new No. 10 as being like Boban, revealing that Kaka also regrets leaving.

On Monday morning, before heading to Zurich with the team, AC Milan Vice President Galliani was interviewed by reporters at Malpensa Airport, briefly addressing some of the recent hot topics surrounding the team.

Galliani said: "Now we're not thinking about Serie A. The competition in these two leagues is different. We are facing both the UEFA Champions League and Serie A. Now all we can think about is the competition; it's the only thing on our minds. Serie A is important, and we will do our best, but the UEFA Champions League belongs to us. We'll consider Serie A when we return from Zurich. Right now, all we're thinking about is how to win in Zurich. We can't just sit nervously waiting to see if Marseille beats Real Madrid. Obviously, if Marseille doesn't win, then regardless of the result of our match, there won't be any issue with advancing, but we must strive for victory. After earning 4 points from Real Madrid and another 4 from Marseille, it's strange that we still don't have a secure spot in the knockout stage. It's regrettable that the only 3 points Zurich got were from us at our home stadium, and now we aim to take those 3 points back from their home ground. I don't make predictions; no one knows before the game whether we'll win or not. So far, we've earned 4 points from Real Madrid, 4 from Marseille, and none from Zurich."

Galliani continued: "AC Milan is playing very well right now. We've won a series of crucial matches, performing exceptionally well. As an appreciator, I watched almost all the games yesterday (last Sunday), and I believe our performance was extraordinary, almost like a different sport. I watched the game through Milan Channel, and AC Milan was incredibly brilliant in the first 30 minutes. Abate and Antonini? We've always had players like them. Clearly, after introducing world-class players in every position, some players will be loaned out to other Serie A teams to gain experience. And our youth team has consistently achieved great results. Now we've slightly changed our policy; we let these young players become starters, and they are doing very well. I remember Borrello came to AC Milan as a young player, he went through many teams, but he indeed came from our youth system. We also have Di Gennaro and other excellent young talents, but seeing Abate and Antonini start tomorrow night will be a great joy."

When discussing key players, especially Gattuso, Galliani said: "My relationship with Gattuso is unshakable, and we hope his relationship with Milan continues to be unshakable. Seedorf is an excellent player. In 1995, at just 19 years old, he represented Ajax against us in the European Cup final in Vienna, where his Ajax team eventually won. When Rui Costa gradually left AC Milan, Seedorf replaced him as the new attacking midfielder and number 10 for AC Milan. Seedorf is somewhat like Boban; if he were to play as a winger, he would undoubtedly perform excellently too. He has a glorious record, and Seedorf’s experiences are extraordinary. As an attacking midfielder, he is truly a superstar. After listening to Leonardo speak to him and watching Seedorf's performance on the field, I believe that whether we have possession or the opponent does, Seedorf has effectively fulfilled what Leonardo asked of him. Borrello is currently performing well, but Inzaghi is eternal. Kaka? I regret that he left AC Milan, and we both feel regretful about it."
