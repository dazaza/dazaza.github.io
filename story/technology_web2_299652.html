Jiangsu Mobile takes the lead in trying out new initiatives, bringing about a nationwide联动effect [organized 12-8]

by zasewat520 on 2009-12-09 01:05:39

The "QQ Hua Dong" game area was grandly opened, marking a new collaboration between China Mobile Jiangsu and Shanda. In addition to extending an olive branch to telecom operators, web browsing enterprises are actively exploring new cooperation models to gain more autonomy. Shanda acquired 51% of Huayou Century's shares for $46.2 million USD, gaining access to tens of millions of paying mobile users and establishing good relations with the operator. With the acquisition of Ku6, the old company's business has expanded further on the original Huayou Century platform, encompassing three major business systems: video (Ku6.com), music (Shanda Music), and cable (Huayou Cable).

China Unicom launched a mobile online gaming platform while Jiangsu Mobile initiated the "QQ Hua Dong" mobile community server. In 2009, telecom operators spared no effort in operating under the model of "content application + access + service." Jiangsu Mobile expanded its reorganized mobile broadband user base, adding "QQ Hua Dong" after collaborating with "Chuan Qi." With over ten million accumulated broadband users added in the second half of 2009 and nearly one hundred million total users, although China leads globally in broadband user numbers, domestic competition is becoming increasingly fierce.

Changsha Broadband introduced "Eternal World."

Broadband operation goes through three stages: providing access, offering value-added services, and ubiquitous broadband. The first stage focuses on technology requirements, the second stage emphasizes integration—wireless and wired, telecom, high-tech, and media—and this industry fusion manifests as integration among operators, network service providers, and media content providers.

China Unicom is accelerating its nationwide 3G business layout. Among the iPhone 3G phones sold in November, they bundled "Unicom Push Hand Online Gaming Platform," launching the "Development Team Entrepreneurship Plan." Game developers who collaborate can gain access to a unified player base, payment interface, SDK, servers, and bandwidth resources. Simultaneously, this year's nationwide upgrade from 1M to 2M bandwidth aims to capture family users by integrating gaming entertainment and lifestyle applications (such as network DVDs, FEMTO, and network printing).

Jiangsu and Guangxi are both regions with concentrated online gaming users. When Jiangsu Mobile initiated the creation of a mobile zone, Guangxi Telecom followed suit. In Guangdong, with its 8 million broadband users accounting for nearly one-quarter of the national total, Guangxi Telecom launched a "Self-Sailing Online Gaming Industry Support Plan," including channel-sharing promotion plans, flexible service cooperation plans, and themed online gaming exhibitions and tournaments inviting developers.

With the participation of mobile carriers, the dual dominance of telecom and网通in the online gaming user market could be overturned, while Changsha's strategic decisions represent a quietly emerging latent layout. As early as 2003, online gaming was identified as a core value-added service but had not been elevated to a strategic decision-making level by Changsha Group. In November 2009, Changsha Broadband launched the beta test of "Eternal World," boosting their operational confidence with continuously full servers at launch. They plan to release 2-3 more browser-based games this year, aiming ultimately to build a gaming platform.

In December, when Jiangsu Mobile opened the "QQ Xia Yu" mobile mega-zone, Nanguan International simultaneously released its top ten predictions for new media in 2010. The first prediction—"Telecom operators and terminal manufacturers will follow internet giants in acquiring and merging within the mobile internet application domain, rapidly enhancing their competitiveness"—mirrors the actual development of the industry. The explosion of "mobile and mutual" activities is set to become a leading indicator in the evolution of the internet.
