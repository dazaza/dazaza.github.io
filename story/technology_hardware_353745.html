Suddenly, someone who looked exactly like me rushed towards me. Before I could react, her sharp sword had already swung towards me, making me tremble with fear and run away. Several more "me's" appeared around me, relentlessly chasing and trying to kill me. I had already been slashed multiple times, and my strength was slowly fading. I thought this was the end for me... At that moment, a powerful knight appeared before my eyes. I shouted to him, "Save... me!" However, he was lost in thought. My strength gave out, and everything went dark as I lost consciousness... When I woke up, I was already in the village of Haiyin, with that knight standing by my side. "Was it you who saved me? Thank you!" "No need to thank me!" His expression was stern, without a trace of a smile. There was a cold evil and wildness in his eyes, making me shiver involuntarily. He turned and walked away, and I hurriedly followed. "Wait for me!" "What do you want?" He stopped but did not turn around. "What were all those 'me's' earlier? Why were they all trying to kill me?" I asked curiously. "Those weren't you; they were creatures that can transform themselves into your likeness," he said coldly. "You entered their territory, and they feared you would harm them. Humans always hunt their kind." I noticed there was a deep resentment in his gaze. "Oh... That's how it is," I understood. "Then how can we tell if they are human or monsters?" He stared at me with cold eyes and then spoke in a low voice: "With your current abilities, you cannot distinguish them unless you can defeat them. After they die, they will turn into 'slimes.'" I had seen "slimes" before; they are animals that resemble lotus leaves. "Will you take me hunting? I'm scared alone!" I softly said behind him. "No!" He firmly refused and continued walking. "If you don't take me, I'll follow you!" I ran up and blocked his path. "... " He opened his mouth to speak but remained silent. "Please, finish being good to the end!" I stared into his eyes. A hint of gentleness appeared in his eyes, though it disappeared instantly. Without saying a word, he headed towards the outskirts of the village, and I quickly followed. Outside the village, a snake woman slithered towards us, and I turned and ran in fright. "Why are you running? Fight!" He ordered deeply. I gritted my teeth, drew my bow, and shot an arrow at the snake woman. He watched coldly from the side, and when the snake woman approached me, he wielded his large sword and killed her in a few strokes. I looked at him with admiration, but he turned away indifferently. "This cold-blooded animal!" I cursed in my heart. Thus, he was forced to become my bodyguard. Gradually, under my persistent pestering, he started talking more, his gaze became peaceful, and occasionally, when I teased him, he would even reveal a faint smile. He told me his name was Huan. Under his protection and guidance, my skills gradually improved. I was now able to handle the monsters in Haiyin. One day, I remembered wanting to fight the shape-shifting monsters, the ones that had transformed into my appearance and tried to kill me last time. I learned that place was called Mirror Forest. "You're not allowed to go there!" His face immediately darkened. "Why?" I questioned. "Don't ask why. You're not allowed to go, period! If you insist on going, don't follow me anymore!" He said sternly. I didn't dare to ask more, a bit afraid of him. But I was puzzled and frustrated inside. "The monsters there are very powerful, especially their leader. Dragon's Valley? Forgotten Island? Tower of Pride?" He looked at me, seeking my opinion. I smiled and said, "Anywhere you go, I'll follow." "Aren't you afraid of those ferocious monsters?" he asked. I replied, "You're here, so what do I have to fear?" He chuckled, and his smile was captivating. I gazed at his face in a daze. "What are you looking at?" he asked. Realizing my awkwardness, my face flushed hot, and I quickly averted my gaze to hide my embarrassment. He affectionately cupped my face, staring into my eyes. I met his gaze, and I saw the flames of love burning in his eyes. He shifted his focus to my lips, leaning closer. I closed my eyes... I felt his breath, and at the moment his lips lightly touched mine, I sensed a violent tremor through his body as he forcefully pushed me away... Shocked, I looked at him, his expression disheartened. I couldn't understand his thoughts, and tears welled up in my eyes. I shouted at him, "Is it because I'm not good enough? You don't like me! Right?" Then, crying, I turned and ran away. He caught up, grabbing my hand urgently and said, "It's not that you're not good enough! You're a lovely elf. It's just that I shouldn't have fallen in love with you!" I tried to pull my hand away, but he held on tightly. Crying and struggling, he forcefully pulled me into his arms, silencing my cries with a kiss... From then on, the notes of our love resounded throughout heaven.
