Wired Reviews the Top Ten Startups to Watch in 2008

by cacard on 2007-12-26 10:24:24

Introduction: Wired magazine recently released its list of the top ten startups to watch in 2008, including BitTorrent, LinkedIn, and Slide, among others. These companies may go public through an Initial Public Offering (IPO) or be acquired in 2008.

Top Ten Startups to Watch in 2008:

1. 23andMe

Company Overview: 23andMe aims to take the genetic revolution to a whole new level by offering a secure, web-based service that allows users to explore, share, and better understand information about their genes.

Founders: Linda Avey and Anne Wojcicki

Funding: $12 million from Genentech, Google, and New Enterprise Associates

Number of Employees: 30

2. 37Signals

Company Overview: 37signals is a small team creating simple, focused software products that help users collaborate and organize teams. To date, over 35,000 individuals and businesses have used 37Signals' web applications for their business needs.

Founders: Jason Fried, Ernest Kim, Carlos Segura

Funding: undisclosed amount from Bezos Expeditions

Number of Employees: 8

3. AdMob

Company Overview: Founded in 2005, AdMob provides mobile advertising services. AdMob created a mobile ad exchange platform for clients, with an ad model similar to Google's, allowing advertisers to easily sell mobile ads to target customers.

Founder: Omar Hamoui

Funding: undisclosed amount in Series A from Sequoia Capital; $15 million in Series B from Accel Partners and Sequoia Capital

Number of Employees: 65

4. BitTorrent

Company Overview: BitTorrent is a P2P technology provider. Initially, its technology was mainly used for illegal file sharing and transmission. At the end of 2007, BitTorrent announced it would collaborate with software providers and media companies to use P2P technology as a platform, helping these companies' customers download large files more quickly. This news indicates that P2P technology will be legitimized and could have far-reaching impacts on the industry in the future.

Founders: Bram Cohen and Ashwin Navin

Funding: $28.75 million from Accel Partners and DCM

Number of Employees: 60

5. Dash

Company Overview: So far, GPS has been a one-way street, with satellites sending instructions to user devices. Dash will change this dynamic, as its product Dash Express supports the ability to retrieve RSS feeds. With Dash Express, users can not only navigate driving routes but also obtain other information or third-party service updates via special RSS feeds, such as Zillow information shown in the image.

Founders: Brian Smartt, Mike Tzamaloukas, Steve Wollenberg

Funding: $45 million from Kleiner Perkins Caufield & Byers, Sequoia Capital, Skymoon Ventures, Crescendo Ventures, ZenShin Capital Partners, Artis Capital, Gold Hill Capital, and several private investors

Number of Employees: 85

6. Fon

Company Overview: FON is a Spanish company dedicated to promoting community residents accessing the internet via shared Wi-Fi networks. Users who agree to share a small portion of their home broadband connection can join a large global Wi-Fi community, gaining free access to other users' FON hotspots. To date, FON has partnered with several heavyweight companies, including Time Warner Cable, British Telecom, and Neuf.

Founder: Martin Varsavsky

Funding: $35 million from Skype, Google, Index Ventures, Sequoia Capital, Excite, Digital Garage, and British Telecom

Number of Employees: approximately 90

7. LinkedIn

Company Overview: LinkedIn is the sixth-largest social networking site in the U.S., differing from MySpace and Facebook in that most of its users are professionals. According to the latest data from Nielsen Internet Research, LinkedIn's growth rate has surpassed its main competitors. For example, LinkedIn attracted about 5 million U.S. visitors in October this year, compared to just 1.7 million during the same period last year. LinkedIn projects revenues of $100 million in 2008, which is not far from Facebook's projected revenue of $150 million in 2007.

Founders: Reid Hoffman, Allen Blue, Konstantin Guericke, Eric Ly, Jean-Luc Valliant

Funding: $27.5 million from Sequoia Capital, Greylock, European Founders Fund, Bessemer Venture Partners, and several angel investors

Number of Employees: 200

8. Powerset

Company Overview: PowerSet, headquartered in San Francisco, is dedicated to developing "natural language" search engines, hoping to break Google's monopoly. "Natural language" searching means users can input more direct questions, unlike the "keywords" used by Google, Yahoo, Microsoft, and other search engines. Although PowerSet has yet to officially launch its own search engine, it has already become one of the hottest startups. PowerSet has secured ample funding, recruited several key engineers from Google, and obtained advanced language technology licensing from Xerox. However, making computers understand natural language and provide the most accurate search results based on it is no easy task.

Founders: Steve Newcomb, Barney Pell, Lorenzo Thione

Funding: $12.5 million from Foundation Capital, The Founder’s Fund, and several angel investors

Number of Employees: 60

9. Slide

Company Overview: Slide is the world's largest widget developer, with its widgets being very popular on several top social networking and blogging platforms, including MySpace, Facebook, Friendster, and Blogger. In the fierce competition between Facebook and OpenSocial, regardless of who wins, Slide stands to benefit.

Founder: Max Levchin

Funding: seed money from Max Levchin; rumored $20 million from BlueRun Ventures, The Founder’s Fund, Khosla Ventures, and Mayfield Fund

Number of Employees: 60

10. Spock

Company Overview: Spock is a website that allows users to search for people-related information. On this site, users can find someone's profession, address, hobbies, and more by simply entering a person's name. Alternatively, users can find people matching certain criteria by entering a profession or location.

Founders: Jaideep Singh, Jay Bhatti

Funding: $8 million from Clearstone Venture Partners and Opus Capital

Number of Employees: 25 (Moore)
