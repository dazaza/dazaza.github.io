(I) The Combined Force Model for Driving Stock Prices   Baidu Stocks        Why can the world's top stock investors, such as Buffett and other great investors, consistently make money in the stock market and achieve profits above the market average, regardless of whether the market is declining or rising? To explain this myth, ordinary investors may either read articles about those top investors, imitate their investment strategies, or simply attend some stock investment training courses or read books on stock investment strategies. However, the results still disappoint them and even leave them more confused. Because there are many so-called winning strategies for stock investment available on the market, sometimes contradicting each other, leaving ordinary investors unsure of which one to choose. For example, some look for value stocks, others look for growth stocks; some are obsessed with finding undervalued stocks by the market; some emphasize fundamental indicators (company financial status), while others focus on technical indicators (past prices, trading volume, and time patterns); some try to find the best timing for entering and exiting the market, but find it doesn't match the actual stock price trends. For instance, if stock prices are driven by company profits, then why do some stocks' prices fall instead of rising when companies report profits higher than the same period last year, the previous quarter, and analysts' estimates? In other cases, sometimes, some stocks seem extremely overvalued, with their P/E (price-to-earnings ratio) nearing 100, like Baidu (Bidu), far exceeding the average P/E of 17 in North America, yet their prices continue to rise.        To address the confusion of ordinary investors and find the true driving force behind stock prices, uncovering the reason why the world's top stock investors always remain invincible, our team (www.4858.cc) analyzed the history of the North American stock market over the past 100 years and studied the investment portfolios and strategies of the top 10 contemporary global investors. We found that the driving force behind stock price movements is not merely basic financials, technical price history, or market psychology, or any one or a few factors, but rather a combined force made up of 28 factors. These 28 factors cover fundamentals, technical aspects, industries, products, operations, R&D, market psychology, institutional investor trends, market maker behaviors, etc. Based on this, we developed a unique model for selecting quality stocks. Testing over several years has shown that our stock selection model has high accuracy, and the returns of the investment portfolios built based on our stock selection model far exceed the market average. Our stock selection model is simple and easy to operate; once mastered, anyone can use it to build their own stable investment portfolio without paying expensive investment advisory fees. Starting from the next issue, this newspaper will introduce in installments the unique stock selection model composed of these 28 factors.
