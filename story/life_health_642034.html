Three types of food that should not be eaten in the morning

by xiaoxiao67 on 2011-12-23 10:58:41

I. Avoid drinking large amounts of icy cold beverages: A large temperature difference can strongly stimulate the gastrointestinal tract, leading to sudden contraction.

II. Avoid eating bananas on an empty stomach: Bananas contain potassium that aids sleep, as well as a large amount of magnesium. If eaten on an empty stomach, it can cause a sudden increase in the magnesium content in the blood, and magnesium is one of the sensitive elements that affect heart function.

III. Avoid eating pineapples on an empty stomach: Pineapples contain strong enzymes. Eating them on an empty stomach can hurt the stomach, and their nutritional components are better absorbed after meals.

Small tip: Do not drink vinegar or eat garlic on an empty stomach in the morning. Drinking vinegar on an empty stomach can lead to excessive stomach acid and harm the stomach; allicin can irritate the gastric mucosa and intestinal wall, causing stomach cramps. Knowing the taboos of morning diet, it's time to take action and find a suitable stomach-nourishing plan for yourself. Here are a few recommendations:

Morning Stomach-Nourishing Soup

Ingredients: 50 grams of red dates, 20 silkworm cocoons with pupae, and an appropriate amount of sugar.

Directions:

1. Put the washed red dates and silkworm cocoons into a pot, add 800 grams of water.

2. Boil and then simmer for 15 minutes.

3. Strain the juice into a large bowl, add sugar to taste.

Effect: Nourishes the stomach and spleen, sweet and delicious, moistens the lungs and generates body fluid.

Eating Method One: "Nostalgic Breakfast"

Breakfast Content: Leftover food, or fried rice made from leftovers, noodles cooked with leftover dishes, etc.

Reasons for Popularity: Many families' "moms" will make more food during dinner so that they can make fried rice for children and family members in the morning, or heat up the leftover dishes. This kind of breakfast is convenient to prepare, rich in content, basically similar to main meals, and usually considered to be nutritionally comprehensive.

Expert Evaluation: After being left overnight, vegetables in leftover dishes may produce nitrite (a carcinogen), which can harm human health when consumed.

Suggestion: Try not to eat leftover vegetables; if using other leftover food as breakfast, store them properly to avoid spoilage; food taken out from the refrigerator should be heated thoroughly.

Eating Method Two: Fast Breakfast

Breakfast Content: Various Western fast foods.

Reasons for Popularity: Western fast foods like hamburgers and fried chicken wings have always been the dietary preference of fashionable people. Moreover, many fast-food restaurants now provide breakfast options such as hamburgers with coffee or milk, black tea, which are convenient, quick, and tasty.

Expert Evaluation: This high-calorie breakfast can easily lead to obesity, and long-term consumption of fried foods can also be harmful to health. If you choose Western fast food as breakfast, lunch and dinner must consist of low-calorie foods. Additionally, this Western breakfast has an unbalanced nutritional profile, high in calories but often lacking in vitamins, minerals, and fiber.

Suggestion: If choosing Western fast food as breakfast, add fruits or vegetable soup to maintain nutritional balance and ensure adequate intake of various nutrients. It is also best not to consume it long-term.

Eating Method Three: Traditional Flavor Breakfast

Breakfast Content: Fried dough sticks, soy milk.

Reasons for Popularity: Many people have grown up under the guidance of their grandparents, accustomed to eating fried dough sticks with soy milk in the morning. They are habituated to this eating method both in terms of taste and sentiment.

Expert Evaluation: Fried dough sticks are deep-fried at high temperatures, like sesame seed cakes, egg pancakes, and pan-fried dumplings, all having the problem of high oil content. After high-temperature frying, nutrients are destroyed, and carcinogens may be produced. Moreover, fried dough sticks have relatively high calories and are hard to digest. Soy milk also belongs to medium-fat food. The fat content of this breakfast combination is clearly excessive, and it is not recommended for long-term use.

Suggestion: Breakfast must include vegetables or fruits. It is best to eat fried dough sticks with soy milk less frequently, not more than once a week. On the day of consumption, lunches and dinners must be as light as possible, avoiding fried, grilled, or stir-fried foods, and more vegetables should be supplemented.

Eating Method Four: Snack Breakfast

Breakfast Content: Various snacks, such as snowflake crackers, cookies, chocolate, etc.

Reasons for Popularity: Many people keep some snacks at home for emergencies. In the morning, due to limited time, they often grab snacks for breakfast, which is convenient and quick.

Expert Evaluation: Eating biscuits, chocolates, and other snacks when hungry is acceptable occasionally, but using snacks as the most important meal of the day is very unscientific. Most snacks belong to dry food, which is not conducive to digestion and absorption when the body is semi-dehydrated in the morning. Moreover, snacks like biscuits mainly consist of grains, providing energy for a short period, but soon making the body feel hungry again. Blood sugar levels may significantly drop before noon. Eating snacks for breakfast can easily lead to insufficient nutrition, weakening physical fitness, and increasing susceptibility to various diseases.

Suggestion: It is not advisable to replace breakfast with snacks, especially too much dry food. Breakfast should contain sufficient water. If the breakfast is too dry, you can add a cucumber.

Eating Method Five: Exercise Breakfast

Breakfast Content: Breakfast bought on the street, eaten while walking, hands moving, feet moving, mouth moving, full-body exercise...

Reasons for Popularity: The mornings of working professionals are spent in haste, especially those living far from work who often solve breakfast on the way. Buns, tea eggs, meat sandwiches, pancakes, etc., sold near residential areas or bus stations, are their first choice. They buy a portion and eat it while walking...

Expert Evaluation: Eating while walking is detrimental to gastrointestinal health and affects digestion and absorption. Furthermore, street food often poses hygiene risks, potentially leading to illness.

Suggestion: If choosing street stall food for breakfast, pay attention to hygiene and preferably eat it at home or at the workplace. Try not to eat breakfast on the way to work to avoid harming your health.

Eating Method Six: "Nutritious" Breakfast

Breakfast Content: Nutritious foods like fruits, vegetables, eggs, milk, etc., but lacking "low-nutritional-value" staple foods.

Reasons for Popularity: This type of breakfast is generally popular among women because staple foods are the primary source of calories, and calories are the nemesis of slim women and dieters... Therefore, all kinds of high-nutrition foods are consumed, while calories are reduced.

Expert Evaluation: Many people mistakenly believe that staple foods only provide calories and are unrelated to nutrition. However, carbohydrates also fall within the scope of nutrition and are extremely important to the human body. Without sufficient caloric supply, the body automatically breaks down substances to release energy. Long-term avoidance of staple foods can lead to malnutrition and weaken various bodily functions. Additionally, yogurt, tomatoes, bananas, pears, plums, apricots, and other acidic-tasting fruits and coarse fiber fruits should not be consumed on an empty stomach.

Suggestion: Increase staple foods such as bread and steamed buns. These grain-based foods should be included.
