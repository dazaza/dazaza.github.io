Professor Xu Yan Cheng from the hospital's endocrinology department said that actually autumn is quite suitable for weight loss. People's body weight fluctuates over the course of a year; high summer temperatures affect appetite, and women tend to wear less clothing in the summer, which also leads them to consciously eat less to highlight their figures. Therefore, transitioning from summer to autumn generally makes people relatively thinner, providing a good foundation for easier weight loss in the autumn. However, people are also prone to gaining weight in autumn. After the start of autumn, the weather gradually becomes cooler, increasing comfort levels. Compared to summer, sweating decreases, water and salt metabolism in the body returns to balance, digestive functions return to normal, and energy metabolism becomes more stable. At this time, fat begins to accumulate gradually, leading to weight gain. Increased appetite and more sleep are noticeable signs.
