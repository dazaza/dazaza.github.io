Inter Milan's Scary Star-Making Efficiency Faces First Obstacle in 3 Years; Mourinho's Obsession with One Aspect Could Strangle How Many Talents

by yinding999 on 2009-12-18 13:21:48

Inter Milan's star-making rate, which has been terrifying, encounters resistance for the first time in 3 years; Mourinho's obsessive preference for one type of player may stifle many talents.

Sina Sports -- "He is not a phenomenon, not the next Pele or the next Maradona chosen by God, but God clearly said that this child is a footballer."

Donati, the "child" mentioned in Mourinho's above statement. In today's early hours' Italian Cup Round of 16 match where Inter Milan defeated Livorno 1-0, Donati made his debut with the first team. Mourinho said: "I prefer Donati. I've talked to him because he is full of passion for football, and it's not just verbal passion, as anyone can say they love football, but passion is reflected through training and how much football occupies in life. He enjoys training with the first team, even if it's just for a minute. With such passion, he can absorb any knowledge he gains."

Regardless of how things are in England, at least since coming to Italy, Mourinho's standard for judging young players has always been whether they are low-key and pragmatic, which is why he strictly manages Balotelli and Santon, and also why he favors Klisn and Donati. Therefore, it was no surprise to see a diligent and hardworking Donati who tried his best not to make mistakes at tonight's Meazza.

It wasn't until the 29th minute when Donati took the ball down the flank and sent a cross to Suarez, who missed with a scissor kick, that this was his first real assist of the game. For most of the opening period, Donati rarely crossed midfield, and Sneijder taking possession in front of the penalty area usually chose to cooperate with Balotelli or pass to Montari on the left. It was quite rare for Inter Milan's right flank to frequently appear vacant. Of course, Donati doesn't need to be anyone's clone or imitate Maicon. But during this season's league match when Inter Milan won 2-0 away against Livorno, Mourinho also let his favorite "diligent" Klisn start, and the similarly non-assisting only-defensive Klisn was substituted at halftime. Fortunately, today Livorno did not put too much pressure on Inter Milan's defense, otherwise, Donati might not have been able to play until the 82nd minute.

After winning four consecutive league titles, the Italian Cup is no longer as important to Inter Milan as it was in the 2004-05 season. However, interestingly, in the past two seasons of the Italian Cup, the two young players launched by Inter Milan have received great attention. In the 2007-08 season, Balotelli made his first start for Inter Milan in an Italian Cup match where they defeated Reggina 4-1, scoring twice; in the 2008-09 season, Santon also made his first start in the Italian Cup match between Inter Milan and Roma, thus beginning to show his potential. Now, Donati becomes the third person in Inter Milan's three-year Italian Cup star-making process.

"This is a wonderful Christmas gift for him and all the young players," Mourinho does not admit that Donati's performance was mediocre, "This is a message to all the children. If they are honest, professional, and give their utmost effort, then they can participate in the highest level of competition."

Unlike Mancini, Mourinho, when nurturing young players, does not send out a completely substitute lineup whenever there is an Italian Cup match. He prefers to make opportunities precious to motivate the young players. "Honesty, professionalism, and giving maximum effort," this is the standard he set for the youth team players of Inter Milan, excluding "talent" alone, while talent happens to be the place where Balotelli and Santon were recognized. Mourinho's decision to promote young players in this way may bury many individualistic yet talented geniuses in the future. Playing honestly and defensively in the backfield is definitely easier than rushing around recklessly in the front field. A good kid without the desire to perform or lacking enough confidence, his future should be closer to a craftsman than to a master. There's no need to give more examples, there are two right beside Donati, Materazzi and Lucio.

In the 82nd minute, Mourinho replaced Donati with Maicon, and this 19-year-old boy had a smile on his face, with sporadic applause in the stadium. He should feel satisfied, having joined Inter Milan from Lucchese in the 2007-08 season, he got the opportunity to play for the first team within 18 months, "Excited? Yes, but I started being excited when I knew I was included in the list. When I stepped into Meazza, I found it was a completely different feeling, which made me extremely excited." (Giotto)
