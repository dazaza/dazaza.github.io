IBM's Q3 Financial Report: Net Profit of $4.041 Billion, Up 5.7% Year-over-Year

by anonymous on 2013-10-20 18:34:11

IBM today released its financial results for the third quarter of the 2013 fiscal year. The report showed that IBM's net profit for the third quarter was $4.041 billion, an increase of 5.7% compared to $3.824 billion in the same period last year; revenue was $23.72 billion, a decrease of 4.1% compared to $24.747 billion in the same period last year. IBM's adjusted earnings per share for the third quarter exceeded Wall Street analysts' expectations, but its revenue fell short of expectations, driving its after-hours stock price down by more than 6%.

In the fiscal quarter ended September 30, 2013, IBM's net profit was $4.041 billion, an increase of 5.7% compared to $3.824 billion in the same period last year; diluted earnings per share were $3.68, an increase of 10.5% compared to $3.33 in the same period last year. IBM's revenue for the third quarter was $23.72 billion, a decrease of 4.1% compared to $24.747 billion in the same period last year, and a decrease of 2% after adjusting for currency fluctuations. IBM's gross margin for the third quarter was 48.0%, higher than 47.4% in the same period last year; non-GAAP operating profit margin was 49.1%, higher than 48.1% in the same period last year.

Excluding certain one-time items (non-GAAP), IBM's adjusted earnings per share for the third quarter were $3.99, exceeding analysts' previous expectations, but its revenue fell short of expectations. According to FactSet, analysts expected IBM's earnings per share for the third quarter to be $3.96, with revenue at $24.79 billion.

By region, IBM's revenue from the Americas for the third quarter was $10.3 billion, down 1% year-on-year (adjusted for currency fluctuations, it remained flat); revenue from Europe, the Middle East, and Africa was $7.3 billion, up 1% year-on-year (adjusted for currency fluctuations, it decreased by 2%); revenue from the Asia-Pacific region was $5.5 billion, down 15% year-on-year (adjusted for currency fluctuations, it decreased by 4%). IBM's OEM revenue for the third quarter was $534 million, down 1% year-on-year (adjusted for currency fluctuations, it remained flat). Revenue from IBM's growth markets organization decreased by 9% year-on-year (adjusted for currency fluctuations, it decreased by 5%). Revenue from the BRIC countries (Brazil, Russia, India, and China) decreased by 15% year-on-year (adjusted for currency fluctuations, it decreased by 12%).

In the third quarter of the 2013 fiscal year, IBM's Global Technology Services department revenue was $9.5 billion, down 4% year-on-year (adjusted for currency fluctuations, it decreased by 1%). IBM's Global Business Services department revenue for the third quarter was $4.6 billion, flat year-on-year (adjusted for currency fluctuations, it increased by 5%). Revenue from IBM's Systems and Technology Group department was $3.2 billion, down 17% year-on-year (adjusted for currency fluctuations, it decreased by 16%). Revenue from IBM's Software department was $5.8 billion, up 1% year-on-year (adjusted for currency fluctuations, it increased by 2%). Revenue from IBM's Global Financing department was $502 million, up 6% year-on-year (adjusted for currency fluctuations, it increased by 9%).

IBM's tax rate for the third quarter was 16.0%, a decrease of 8.6 percentage points compared to the same period last year; non-GAAP operating tax rate was 17.0%, a decrease of 7.7 percentage points compared to the same period last year. As of the end of the third quarter, IBM held a total cash balance of $10.2 billion, with free cash flow of $2.2 billion. IBM returned $1.9 billion to investors through stock repurchases and $1 billion through dividends in the third quarter, totaling approximately $3 billion.

IBM expects earnings per share for the 2013 fiscal year to be at least $15.01; non-GAAP, IBM expects operating earnings per share for the 2013 fiscal year to be at least $16.25, excluding amortization expenses of acquired intangible assets, other acquisition-related expenses, and retirement-related expenses of $1.24 per share; non-GAAP (excluding $1 billion in expenses from the second-quarter workforce rebalancing measures), IBM expects operating earnings per share for the 2013 fiscal year to be at least $16.90, in line with the company's previous expectations and exceeding analysts' expectations. According to FactSet, analysts expect IBM's earnings per share for the 2013 fiscal year to be $16.87 on average.

On the day, IBM's stock price rose by $2.07 in regular trading on the New York Stock Exchange, closing at $186.73, up 1.12%. In subsequent after-hours trading until 4:32 PM Eastern Time (4:32 AM Beijing Time on October 17), IBM's stock price fell by $11.26 to $175.47, down 6.03%. Over the past 52 weeks, IBM's highest price was $215.90, and its lowest price was $178.71.
