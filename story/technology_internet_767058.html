Google and Microsoft invest in cancer gene testing company Foundation

by anonymous on 2013-10-02 17:26:12

According to the US "Business Insider" website, last week more than a dozen companies conducted their Initial Public Offering (IPO) in the United States, including a biotech company named Foundation Medicine.

Foundation provides cancer genetic testing services to the public. Steve Jobs was the first well-known person to undergo this test, which also gained fame because of him.

For just $6,000, this test helps clients screen for all gene mutations that cause tumors. It promotes the arrival of the era of "personalized medicine." At that time, doctors will choose cancer treatments based on genetic knowledge.

According to Walter Isaacson's authorized biography of Jobs, Jobs spent $100,000 on testing, but ultimately could not escape his fate. However, Jobs firmly believed that such testing was very valuable. He said in an interview with "MIT Technology Review": "I am either one of the first people to defeat cancer in this way, or one of the last people to die from cancer."

After Jobs' death, several doctors who had worked on this testing project at Harvard University and MIT resigned and founded Foundation. Bill Gates and Larry Page visited Jobs shortly before his death, but they were unable to help. All they could do was fund Foundation.

According to documents from the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC), Gates participated in Foundation's initial round of financing worth $13.5 million and still holds 4% equity; Google's venture capital department Google Ventures also invested in Foundation and currently holds 9% equity in the company. In the past two years, Foundation has raised a total of $251 million.

In last week's IPO, Foundation raised $106 million, with good prospects. Foundation's opening price was $18, higher than the IPO price range of $14 to $16 per share. Currently, Foundation's stock price has exceeded $35.
