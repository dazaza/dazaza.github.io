Background Link

In the complaint, stockholder Mr. Wang stated that Beijing Shoufang Investment Advisory Co., Ltd. (Shoufang Company) is a qualified securities investment consulting institution, and Mr. Wang Jianzhong is the controlling shareholder, executive director, and manager of the company. Between 2007 and May 29, 2008, Shoufang Company released the "Gold Mining Report" through its company website as well as Eastmoney.com, Sina.com, and the Securities Times.

Among the securities manipulated by Wang Jianzhong and penalized by the CSRC, those involving CITIC Bank, Sinopec, and Vanke A shares led Mr. Wang to demand compensation for his losses exceeding 100,000 yuan.

Yesterday, lawyer Zhang Yuanchong, representing Mr. Wang, stated that he had already received the opposing party's defense opinion, "It might have been written by Wang Jianzhong himself in detention and then submitted via his lawyer."

Currently, the case is still under trial.

Lawyer Zhang Yuanchong mentioned that in the defense opinion, Wang also argued that the plaintiff's presented evidence could not prove a direct causal relationship between the trading of these three stocks and the gold mining reports issued by Shoufang Company.

In May 2008, the CSRC fined Wang Jianzhong the largest penalty for suspected market manipulation by confiscating illegal gains amounting to over 125 million yuan and imposing an additional fine of 125 million yuan.

Before each report release, Wang Jianzhong would purchase the recommended securities and sell them afterward to obtain illegal profits for himself.

Wang Jianzhong became the first person in China to be sued for civil compensation due to market manipulation, and this case is also the first instance of a civil claim for damages in a domestic securities market manipulation case.

Born in 1968 with a university education, Wang Jianzhong worked at a branch of the Industrial and Commercial Bank of China, Air China, and Beijing Zhongtouce Investment Advisory Co., Ltd. from 1989 to 2001. In August 2001, Wang Jianzhong registered and founded Shoufang Company, serving as its chairman.

On October 28, 2010, Wang Jianzhong was tried at the Second Intermediate People's Court on charges of manipulating the securities market.

The prosecution accused Wang Jianzhong of using multiple securities accounts under his control to buy related securities beforehand, then using the company's name to promote these securities through media outlets. After the information was made public, he immediately sold the related securities. Through this method, Wang Jianzhong manipulated the market 55 times across 38 different stocks, illegally profiting over 125 million yuan.

The verdict for Wang Jianzhong's case has yet to be announced.
