Motorola classic phone, early high-end 3G dual-camera entertainment audio-visual phone, supporting MP3, video playback, supporting China Unicom 186 number segment 3G. Although the hardware of the machine is relatively old-fashioned, it's still very practical for daily use, with a solid feel when held, large size, very suitable as a gift for elders.

Upon first holding the A835, primarily silver and black, it gives off a mature, stable, and very resolute impression. The matte-finished surface and smooth body design give it a royal sense of nobility.

With dimensions 137 x 55.6 x 26.5mm and weighing 160 grams, without any attachment point for phone accessories, it can be clearly stated that this phone is designed for men. Despite its larger size and weight, it doesn't feel cumbersome in hand, rather it provides a great feel and avoids a petty appearance.

SIM card placement is unique. Open the back cover, then lift the side protection cover, and the SIM card slot will be visible. No need to remove the battery, but this design makes it relatively difficult to take out the card.

In keyboard design, the top three function keys are slightly more protruding than others. In the center, a commonly used five-directional joystick is adopted, though I find the joystick too small, leading to errors during operation (possibly due to my large hands). Send and hang-up keys are located on either side of the rocker. There are also two dedicated 3G service keys for quick access to 3G internet and video calls. The number keys are silver and arc-shaped, unevenly distributed in size, but due to their larger area, there's generally no mistaken pressing. On the left side of the phone are two volume adjustment keys, while on the right is a slim photography shortcut key, small in area and not entirely harmonious with the overall design, but slightly protruding, providing acceptable tactile feedback.

The left function key is a shortcut menu key, allowing users to customize various shortcuts. The up, down, left, right directions of the rocker, along with the right function key, can all be self-defined for quick access to related functions. The main menu order can also be changed, supporting quick dialing and single-key dialing. All this is done to allow users to deeply customize their phones for simpler operations.

The phone comes with multiple wallpapers, various themes, and menu styles that can be set as a 9-grid or list format. Contrast and brightness can be individually adjusted, with levels ranging from 0 to 6. Moreover, there are six colors available for menu selection, allowing users to set screens according to their preferences. The A835 supports custom slideshows by selecting preferred images, editing them, setting delay times, and generating a slideshow automatically. This slideshow can play independently or serve as a screensaver, making it fun and personalizable. With thoughtful human-centered design, Motorola has put considerable effort into enhancing user experience.

For many mobile users, 3G technology is no longer unfamiliar, having been popularized abroad in countries like Japan and South Korea. However, in mainland China, this communication technology hasn't been implemented or widely adopted yet. In Hong Kong, however, 3G mobile communication technology has taken off rapidly, creating much excitement. To catch this wave, numerous phone brands have launched new 3G models hoping to capture a share of this burgeoning market. These 3G phones, leveraging high-speed wireless transmission technology, offer comprehensive features that could even be described as "powerful."

In sales, it's not just about the robust functionality of the device; effective marketing is equally important. In Hong Kong, phone manufacturers have employed various innovative strategies. NEC once attracted customers by offering its C313 model at a contract price of HK$998, causing shockwaves in the market. Motorola, as a renowned brand in the mobile phone industry, certainly wouldn't stand idly by. Collaborating with "3 Hong Kong," the first company in Hong Kong to operate a 3G network, they launched a stunning market promotion plan where a Motorola A835 3G phone, featuring a 65K color display, dual lenses, 64MB memory, Bluetooth, AGPS, and other powerful features, could be acquired for just HK$498. This showcased the full capabilities of this all-in-one 3G phone. Today, I will evaluate the Motorola A835 for everyone.

Just upon receiving this silver and black A835, it immediately conveys a sense of maturity, stability, and resilience. Its matte surface finish and sleek design exudes an aura of regality.

Measuring 137 x 55.6 x 26.5mm and weighing 160g, with no designated spot for phone accessories, it is evident that this phone is designed for men. Despite its larger size and weight, it does not feel cumbersome in hand but instead offers excellent grip and a refined presence.

The SIM card placement is unique; open the back cover, then lift the side protection cover to reveal the SIM card slot. No need to remove the battery, though this design makes it somewhat challenging to remove the card.

In terms of keyboard design, the top three function keys are slightly more prominent compared to others. In the center, a common five-way joystick is utilized, though I find it too small, leading to operational errors (possibly due to my large hands). The send and hang-up buttons are positioned on either side of the joystick. Additionally, there are two dedicated keys for 3G internet and video call shortcuts. The number keys are silver with an arc design, unevenly sized, but due to their larger areas, there is minimal chance of accidental presses. On the left side of the phone, there are two volume adjustment keys, while on the right, there is a slim photography shortcut key, smaller in area and less harmonious with the overall design, but slightly more prominent, providing acceptable tactile feedback.

The left function key serves as a shortcut menu key, enabling users to customize various shortcut menus. The up, down, left, right directions of the rocker, along with the right function key, can all be self-defined for quick access to related functions. The main menu order can also be customized, supporting quick dialing and single-key dialing. All this aims to enable users to delve deeper into customizing their phones for more convenient operations.

The phone includes multiple wallpapers with various themes. Menu styles can be set as a 9-grid or list format. Contrast and brightness can be individually adjusted across levels 0 to 6. Furthermore, the menu offers six color options for users to choose from, ensuring the screen can be set according to personal preferences. The A835 supports custom slideshows by selecting preferred images, editing them, setting delay times, and automatically generating a slideshow. This slideshow can play independently or serve as a screensaver, making it engaging and personalized. Clearly, Motorola has invested significant effort into thoughtful human-centered design.

The bright silver oversized speaker on the back of the phone is highly eye-catching. According to the manufacturer's specifications, the A835 has a 16-note ringtone, but in actual listening tests, it sounds indistinguishable from a 24-note. I believe the excellent sound quality owes much to the oversized speaker on the back, although the phone already comes preloaded with several ringtones, which sound quite ordinary and repetitive, leading to boredom. Therefore, I suggest downloading or obtaining other ringtones via other means for a better experience.

Function Section

In terms of ringtone settings, users can assign different ringtones to various events, categorized in great detail. When using headphones, the ringing range can also be set to either the speaker, headphones, or both simultaneously, making it very comprehensive. The only downside is that the ringtone and vibration cannot be set to occur simultaneously; only sequential activation is possible.

This standout feature of the A835 is the dual cameras and the bright silver oversized speaker on the back. Both cameras are located in the same position, one front-facing and the other rear-facing. The rear camera is mainly used for capturing scenes, while the front camera facilitates selfies and video calls, demonstrating a uniquely ingenious design that meets users' multifaceted requirements.

The front camera of the A835 is used for landscape photography, while the rear camera is used for selfies and video calls. Both have VGA resolution with 300,000 pixels. Due to the front-and-back design, taking photos or selfies becomes very convenient. You can switch between the front and rear cameras using the left and right keys, while the up and down keys control zooming, reaching up to 4x digital zoom. For a high-end 3G phone like the A835, its image quality is only considered average, with overly vibrant colors but lacking clarity. It can be said that the 300,000-pixel camera configuration is a drawback of this phone.

Although the A835 being evaluated is a Hong Kong version with traditional Chinese characters, it also supports simplified Chinese display. The input method continues to use Motorola's iTap, which operates simply and conveniently due to the five-way joystick. However, for unknown reasons, there is a pause during word spelling, with a slower response. The message categorization is extremely detailed, with clear entries and the option to choose whether to enable message reports when sending.

For users who are lazy or dislike sending messages, the A835 provides some commonly used preset messages, albeit in English, but they can be rewritten. Besides regular text messages, the A835 also supports up to 12-second video messages, MMS multimedia messages, and email sending and receiving. From this, we can see that the A835's technology in multimedia message sending and receiving is quite powerful.

In terms of the address book, the A835 performs excellently, especially regarding security and convenience. Each contact can store up to 6 phone numbers, with 4 preset groups. Besides general features like adding or deleting groups, voice dialing, custom ringtones, and caller ID pictures, the A835 adds a confidentiality function to protect private phone numbers from easy theft. Moreover, messages can be directly edited and sent within the address book, making it very convenient.

The A835 offers multiple address book sorting methods, performing well in terms of convenience. Users can search phone numbers using six methods: English, pinyin, stroke count, location, voice control dialing, and email. This eliminates the need to spend half a day searching for a phone number, saving a lot of time.

In terms of expandability, as a 3G phone, the A835 excels, featuring complete MMS, infrared, Bluetooth, and data cable synchronization. The Bluetooth performance is outstanding with good compatibility, and there were no disconnection issues during testing. Combined with data cable usage, the A835 achieves stable data transmission.

Next to the speaker are two ports used for connecting the phone to a car dock, enabling safe hands-free communication while driving through Bluetooth earbuds or headphones.

Other Features

The A835 does not come with built-in games, but users must download JAVA games via the network. This allows users to choose their games, unlike some phones with fixed built-in games that become boring after prolonged use. With strong hardware support from 3G and 64MB memory, gaming is absolutely suitable for many "greedy" users.

As a 3G phone, the A835 stands out in its business functions, arguably its main selling point. The AGPS logo on the back indicates that the A835 supports AGPS positioning services. With AGPS, information such as your location, nearby buildings, and your favorite stores can be clearly displayed, accurately guiding users who travel frequently.

Since it is a 3G phone, the A835 also supports video calling services, with a shortcut key on the keyboard. The entire video call can be initiated with one button press. This is particularly helpful for users who frequently communicate with people in other regions, as face-to-face conversations enhance mutual understanding. Unfortunately, 3G network services have not yet been implemented in mainland China, making this feature merely nominal.

Due to its inability to utilize local 3G networks, it's difficult to determine precise standby and talk times. If used as a regular phone, continuous use for three days should suffice.

The Motorola A835, in terms of styling and functionality, is clearly aimed at successful business men. Despite minor flaws in the camera, button design, and messaging, it remains an excellent phone. Even though China's current network isn't 3G, and some features cannot be used, as a regular GPRS phone, its functions and overall performance remain impressive. I believe that even without 3G implementation, it will attract many buyers and become a hot commodity.
