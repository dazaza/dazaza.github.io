Subsequently, the reporter counted together with Zhou Qiaoxi - 108 in total! Zhou Qiaoxi advanced 260 yuan of her pocket money from her father and bought all the remaining small brooms. With a "Goodbye grandma," Mr. Zhou and little Zhou Qiaoxi disappeared into the crowd. "Today is the earliest day I've finished work, seeing how clever the child is, my heart feels really warm." The old grandma said goodbye to Mr. Zhou and his son with her trembling hands.

The grandma said that her name was Zhang Guizhen (phonetic), she was 83 years old, and she lived alone in Leshan Town, Chaoyang District, Changchun City. Her husband had passed away more than 10 years ago. She came to Changchun five years ago to help a family take care of their child. Now, five years have passed, and that child missed her so much that she specially came from Leshan. Because it was the Dragon Boat Festival and her financial situation wasn't good, the child's parents helped her purchase these small brooms. "My children also don't have good living conditions, so I can't rely on anyone," the grandma said sorrowfully when asked about her children, and she didn't want to talk about it.

"At 5 PM, I found a highly forwarded post on Sina Weibo: 'On the opposite side of Hengkelong Road at Gui Lin Road, an 83-year-old grandma is selling symbolic 'prosperous people, prosperous wealth' small brooms. If you happen to pass by and meet this grandma, please buy two brooms. Even if you really don't need them, there's one thing for sure - we all hope the grandma goes home early!' At around 5 PM, I discovered that this Weibo post was sent by a Changchun citizen surnamed Zhou using his mobile phone.

Most netizens expressed that they would definitely buy one if they saw such a situation. One netizen said: "Love has nothing to do with relationship. But it has a close connection with your heart. If you have love in your heart, you will help others. If you receive love, you will give back love."

The grandma usually spends about 12 hours a day at her stall.

At around 6 PM, the number of people buying small brooms suddenly increased, and the grandma couldn't handle it. The reporter immediately started helping the grandma count and collect money. After receiving the money, the grandma repeatedly thanked each customer. When asked if she had eaten, the grandma smiled and said: "I almost forgot, I haven't eaten yet." Then, she took out two steamed buns from the plastic bag beside her, eating while continuing to sell her small brooms.

A Weibo post about an 83-year-old grandma selling small brooms on the roadside received enthusiastic replies from netizens.

A kind-hearted little boy prepaid his pocket money and bought all the small brooms.

At 6:30 PM, after finishing his English class, Zhou Qiaoxi, Mr. Zhou's son, saw the white-haired grandma selling small brooms and felt very sorry for her. Shaking his father's hand, he said: "Dad, dad, let's buy all the grandma's small brooms. I can advance my pocket money." Curious, Mr. Zhou asked: "Why do you want to buy all the small brooms?" Looking into his father's eyes, Zhou Qiaoxi said, "Because I want the grandma to go home early!" Hearing his son's words, Mr. Zhou immediately agreed with his idea.

Changchun is the most humane city, where an 83-year-old grandma was deeply moved by the actions of a 6-year-old boy. Around 5 PM on June 3rd, a post appeared on Sina Weibo about an 83-year-old grandma selling symbolic 'prosperous people, prosperous wealth' small brooms at the intersection of Tongzhi Street and Xikang Road. After the post was made, it received a wide response from netizens who left comments and even some citizens rushed to the scene to buy. At around 6:30 PM, a 6-year-old boy named Zhou Qiaoxi prepaid his pocket money and used 260 yuan to buy all the grandma's small brooms. His goal was simple - to let the grandma go home early.

In about half an hour, around 20 were sold, and enthusiastic citizens purchased without haggling.

The grandma said that she had been selling these small brooms for 3 or 4 days. She came at 9 AM and went home at around 9 PM. Normally, she could make around 80 yuan a day, and on good days, she could make around 100 yuan. These small brooms were purchased by a friend in Changchun, and she didn't know how much they cost. Her friend just let her sell them first.

Afterward, the reporter bought a small broom. The grandma said that this small broom was a lucky charm, especially suitable for the Dragon Boat Festival, bringing prosperity and fortune. From 5:30 PM to 6 PM, the reporter saw about 10 citizens buy around 20 small brooms. Almost all of them did not haggle. A couple came over to buy a small broom, and the boyfriend was about to ask for a discount but was immediately stopped by his girlfriend. "Seeing the grandma working so hard, we really feel bad," a citizen sighed after buying 2 small brooms.

At the scene, the reporter finally found Mr. Zhou, the Changchun citizen who posted on Weibo. "We must support the grandma. Changchun is the most humane city, and we will certainly buy from such an elderly person." Mr. Zhou said that at around 2 PM, he came to the intersection of Tongzhi Street and Xikang Road to pick up his 6-year-old son, Zhou Qiaoxi, from his English class. While waiting for his son, he saw Grandma Zhang selling small brooms and immediately posted the event on Weibo using his mobile phone.

At around 5:30 PM, the reporter arrived at the intersection of Tongzhi Street and Xikang Road and saw the grandma. She was sitting cross-legged on the sidewalk, with a plastic bag on each side. In front of her, a woven bag less than 1 meter long was laid on the ground, holding about 30 small brooms. The reporter saw that the grandma had gray hair, wearing a coffee-colored coat and black pants, tidying up the small brooms in front of her. There were two sizes of small brooms - large ones for 3 yuan and small ones for 2 yuan. The material of the brooms resembled the grass used in straw mats, about 5 centimeters long, tied with wire, and a red string attached to the wire.
