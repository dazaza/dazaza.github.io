In Long Zhanghuan's family, 8 children live with their grandparents. Wang Chunfen said that it is common in Fenghuang for rural children to live with their grandparents, but 8 children is indeed "too many". Such a living arrangement is not beneficial for the children. She said that she will contact the Pinyou Village Committee to investigate the family situation and contact the parents to communicate more with the children and give them more family warmth. If the family situation is found to be truly difficult and meets the national poverty policy standard, assistance will be provided according to national policies.

Before seven o'clock yesterday morning, Long Zhanghuan woke up. She sleeps in the dormitory of Shanjiang Primary School at night. Three sisters squeeze on the top bunk bed. She said there are no other beds in the school, "but it's quite fun." Her grandmother also hopes that the sisters can sleep together so they can take care of each other.

According to the survey data conducted last month by the Fenghuang County Education Bureau: There are a total of 46,207 primary and secondary school students in the county, among which 24,225 are left-behind children, accounting for more than half. Yesterday, Wang Chunfen, the government education supervisor of Fenghuang County, said that Fenghuang County is a poor county. The phenomenon of left-behind children is extremely widespread and has become a social issue. The county government and the Education Bureau have always paid attention to the situation of left-behind children.

Yesterday was Monday. In Pinyou Village, Shanjiang Town, Fenghuang County, 7 out of 8 children went to school. It happened to be the market day every five days, so the grandmother went to the town for shopping, leaving only 2-year-old Zhang Haojie and his grandfather at home. The mist-covered Pinyou Primary School consists of one-story houses, two classrooms, and 33 students, echoing with clear reading sounds.

"We need to care about the inner world of left-behind children," Wang Chunfen said, allowing teachers to provide "one-on-one" services to left-behind children. In recent years, Fenghuang County has carried out teaching services called "Care for Left-behind Children, Strive to Become Acting Parents One-on-One," enabling teachers to truly care about left-behind children. Each teacher must connect with one left-behind child, deeply caring about their studies and lives, communicating more often, "so that they don't feel lonely."
