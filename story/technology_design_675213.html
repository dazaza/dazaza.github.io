09 Notes from Hubei Part Four

by xue94fwsh on 2012-02-24 21:15:23

Yishu Ge has already gone home, washed up, and gone to sleep, so the only option is to take a taxi to Hankou. The taxi driver, upon hearing that you are a passenger crossing the river, becomes very enthusiastic due to the long distance. Crossing the Yangtze River Second Bridge from Wuchang leads you to the Hankou Riverside Boulevard. Although "Wuhan welcomes you" is a reiteration of the Olympic slogan, it still gives a stranger a sense of warmth. The signature soup dumplings and beef noodles are must-orders. The Mole Kart entering the top ten of the iPad list was tried and found to have a good taste, but there was no surprise. The side effects of breast enlargement cream were questioned, but the products like Aishangting were mentioned. The time-honored brand "Sijimei Soup Dumplings," recommended by Chen Nian Puer, which started in 1922, is located at the intersection of Jianghan Road and Zhongshan Avenue. He said that mentioning Sijimei brings back memories of eating soup dumplings as a child... The heavy history of Hankou can appear before your eyes at any moment, each inconspicuous old building may have a legendary story. Despite being architecture from the early 20th century, their classic Western architectural grandeur compares favorably with the nearby skyscrapers. In the evening, after leaving Jinjiang Inn opposite the Hubei Provincial Party Committee, I took a walk around the Hankou riverside area. The riverside avenue looks similar to Shanghai's Bund, with Western buildings from the early 20th century standing closely together... Getting off near the Jianghan Customs House built by the British in 1924, not far from here is the eastern starting point of the Jianghan Road pedestrian street. Just then, after waiting for half a day, a plate of soup dumplings was just placed out and was immediately taken away by a pair of Korean-loving students who had just entered! Along Victory Road to the north, this towering building with eight stone columns at its entrance is particularly eye-catching! It says Jincheng Bank on the outer wall of the top floor. Never heard of it! Checking on my phone, it turns out that this Hankou Jincheng Bank Building was built in 1930 and was one of the famous "Northern Four Banks." Passing through the old foreign bank buildings on the east section of the pedestrian street, after the Poyang Road intersection, the number of streets and alleys increased, and various restaurants and snack shops were scattered everywhere. How will this area be planned? Once super high-rise buildings are constructed, the entire style of the old Hankou building area will be destroyed... In 1938, after the Japanese army captured Wuhan, this place became the Japanese army headquarters... After liberation, it was converted into the Wuhan Children's Library, and now it is the Wuhan Art Museum. To be honest, I have always believed that the treaty ports and concessions forcibly opened in the past greatly promoted the economic, cultural, and commercial development of our country! This statement, I hope, won't get me attacked by patriotic youths... Modern people's taste buds have been completely ruined... Eating fragrant and spicy food all day, what used to be eaten during the New Year is now consumed daily. To make someone feel the kind of overwhelming joy towards food is basically impossible! Then, planning to return to the riverside avenue via Qingdao Road, all along the way are rows of old houses awaiting demolition. The residents have long since moved out, dark streetlights illuminate empty corridors, and the large character "chai" (demolish) is seen everywhere. To be continued, more pictures are stored in the space album... Returning to the riverside avenue, beside it is the Hankou HSBC Building, extremely similar to the one on Shanghai's Bund. Across the road is the Hankou riverside park and Wuhan Passenger Port. The size of Wuhan's urban area ranks among the top in the world. Wuchang, Hankou, and Hanyang are tightly connected by numerous bridges and tunnels. The Yangtze River is no longer an insurmountable natural barrier, and the jubilant scenes of the opening of the Wuhan Yangtze River Bridge cannot be reproduced today... This is the transformation and leap of the times! Sijimei soup dumplings typically exhibit the bureaucratic style of state-owned units: no numbering or calling system, if you order something, keep an eye out yourself, grab it quickly when it's ready; greasy tables, only half the lights are on; a few waitresses sit chatting idly, doing nothing, looking like they're about to close at any moment. Alas, no wonder many people say this shop has declined! In today's world, such a lazy service attitude simply won't do... Besides duck necks, the famous Wuhan snack is also crayfish. There was once a debate between myself and Chen Nian Puer about where the best crayfish could be found - I said Hunan, he said Hubei. Not sure who's right, anyone care to help clear this up? The Hankou riverside park doesn't have dazzling night views, the river surface is pitch black with very few visible boats. On the contrary, looking out from the exit, the HSBC Building and the Citibank Building to its left are steady and majestic. The Hankou concession area, once divided by the British, Russian, German, and Japanese powers, is dotted with countless Western-style villas, iron evidence of imperialism's invasion of Chinese territory!
