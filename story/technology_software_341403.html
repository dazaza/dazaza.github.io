Methods to Improve Logic Scores in the 2010 MBA Entrance Exam

by bjhzbeika on 2010-04-07 16:07:01

Logic is not difficult if you have the right approach, though it may not be as simple as it seems. If you adopt effective learning methods, it can actually be quite manageable. Here's a brief analysis of how to study logic.

### Logic:

Logic is a discipline of thought that relates to one’s thinking patterns accumulated over years of learning, working, and living. However, as the MBA joint examination increasingly aligns with the GMAT, the logical part of the MBA exam has become more standardized. The questions can generally be categorized into seven major types, making them highly solvable with strong problem-solving techniques. It's important to note:

1. **Most logic problems do not directly require knowledge of logic theory** (though subconsciously, these theories are still applied). Therefore, solving more problems is essential to train your logical problem-solving skills. You should know that MBA logic questions are extracted from the GMAT logic question bank with slight modifications. Thus, some students who practice many problems often find themselves encountering original questions, which is entirely possible. More importantly—doing more problems can help develop your latent logical thinking ability, allowing you to fully utilize your inherent logical capacity. Additionally—while having some necessary logic knowledge can assist in solving problems smoothly, when doing problems, unless absolutely necessary, don't get overly entangled with logic theory or terms like "antecedent," "consequent," S, P, etc., because if you cannot apply them proficiently, they might slow down your problem-solving speed.

2. **The most crucial aspect of logic problems is being able to simplify and extract the main idea from lengthy and convoluted question stems.**

3. **An important technique for solving logic problems is to "address the problem at hand"**, meaning you must focus on the question stem and avoid wandering off-topic or introducing emotional bias.

### Writing:

The writing section's argumentative effectiveness analysis essentially tests logic. You just need to identify logical flaws in the question stem and refute them. These types of essay questions primarily focus on refuting or supplementing the arguments presented in the question stem. Avoid accepting the arguments completely and ensure that your discussion revolves around the question stem, similar to finding the option that most weakens the argument in a logic test. Focus on identifying errors in reasoning rather than proposing new viewpoints.

For argumentative essays, everyone has experience writing such pieces since childhood, so there's no need for further elaboration here. Case analysis involves significant techniques, and Zhao Xinquan from Huazhang is a leading figure in this field domestically. Through his short-term training, most people can achieve a second-class level score. Additionally, regular writing practice is necessary. You can schedule practice once a week to cultivate quick and clear topic analysis and rapid composition abilities. In the MBA joint exam, the requirement for your viewpoints in the essay isn't very high; the key is to stay on topic and complete the task quickly. Writing speed is also important because you need to finish a 1200-word essay, 25 math problems, and 30 logic questions within 180 minutes. If you can save time on the essay, you can allocate more time to subjects that may require more careful attention, such as checking math problems, which would be greatly beneficial.

### Management:

Management is mainly tested in the writing section's "case analysis." Since only basic knowledge is required, excellent writing instructors will mention some commonly tested management knowledge points during their lessons. You just need to grasp them slightly. Accumulating management common sense is also useful for the later interview stage.

### Conclusion:

In summary, I hope all students can appropriately arrange their time based on their foundation. For those attending tutoring classes, before August, as long as you listen carefully in class and diligently complete the homework assigned by the teacher, it should be sufficient. However, more effort should be placed on English, where vocabulary is the most critical. For math, after mastering the basic concepts, you can strengthen your practice during the reinforcement phase. Everything will become much simpler afterward!

A recommended plan is: four months to build a foundation, two months for reinforcement, one month for a sprint, and one month to address any deficiencies, ultimately achieving high scores.
