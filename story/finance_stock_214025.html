Stock Knowledge - Niugu Web General Market Analysis:

Today, as expected, the stock index closed red on Friday and reached a new high for this round of market rally. This week, the stock index once again gained significantly with a weekly increase of 5.3%, marking the sixth consecutive week of gains in the weekly trend. Trading volume continued to expand, maintaining a strong upward momentum characterized by rising prices and increasing volume, with total trading volume at historically high levels. As anticipated by the author last Friday, this week saw consecutive breakthroughs above the 3200 and 3300 point thresholds. Resource stocks, emphasized by the author last Friday, became the leading force driving the market. In particular, when the market experienced significant volatility today, resource stocks once again played a crucial role in stabilizing the situation. The activity of resource stocks is closely related to the renewed strength of the international commodities market.

In terms of today's overall market conditions, the focus of hot sectors has shifted back to resource stocks. The continuous influx of new funds has made non-ferrous metals, coal, oil, and other sectors the short-term stars of the market. Despite significant fluctuations in the stock index, individual stock activity remained high, with multiple stocks closing at their daily limits. Moreover, the sustainability of stock increases is continuously strengthening. In terms of sector performance, the top sectors in terms of capital inflow were: foreign trade, coal and oil, engineering construction, insurance, etc., while the sectors with the most capital outflow were: new materials, agriculture, machinery, etc. From the flow of funds, it can be seen that mainstream hot sector funds remain relatively active, and accurately grasping the rhythm of the market is extremely critical!

Looking back on the first half of the year, the A-share market has been the best-performing stock market globally, with a cumulative increase of 62% in the first half, and a June gain of 12.4%. Due to ample market liquidity, stock market fund inflows in June exceeded the peak of the previous bull market, with continuous entry of new funds. Regarding the market outlook for July, the author's view is that under inflation expectations, the stock index will continue to climb with fluctuations, and new funds will continue to enter the stock market. The State Council announced the "transfer of state-owned shares to the social security fund and extending the lock-up period by three years," which, to some extent, mitigates the upcoming flood of small and large non-tradable share expirations. It also reflects the regulators' care for the stock market, creating a relatively stable stock market environment for the continued approval of IPOs. The author firmly believes that July will still be red! The stock index in July will truly enter the 'three era'!

This week, the cumulative net inflow of funds into both markets was approximately 30.98 billion yuan, marking six consecutive weeks of net inflows. This week, the sectors with the highest net inflows were: coal and oil, non-ferrous metals, real estate, chemical fibers, and brokers.

Under the broad context of economic recovery, the medium-to-long-term upward trend of the stock index has been established. The author's long-term bullish stance remains unchanged! The significant mid-week breakout above the 2800-point level signifies the opening of a new upward space. The author consistently believes that the medium-term target for the rise should be above the 4000-point mark!

In the short term, the market continues to exhibit extremely strong characteristics, with trading volumes continuing to expand. Adjustments are completed quickly and sharply. There is no such thing as a stock market that only rises and never falls; intraday volatility is necessary. Technically, the market needs to adjust to relieve overbought conditions and clean up floating chips. Only when there is still hesitation and divergence in the market can the trend last longer! Against the backdrop of China's economy recovering first, the A-share market will still be the brightest-performing stock market globally. The loose monetary policy will continue, laying the foundation for the continued bull run in the stock market. The author believes that maintaining a bull market mindset is key to success, as evidenced by the ability to remain calm and composed during sharp intra-day declines. As long as the trend does not change and mainstream funds do not withdraw, every adjustment is an excellent opportunity to buy into hot spots at lower prices. Today’s significant fluctuation was merely a beautiful gear shift for the main hotspots. The author anticipates that with global stock markets beginning to break upwards again and the commodity market regaining strength, and without any changes in policy or funding surfaces, the A-share market will continue its strong upward attack next week, making seven consecutive weeks of gains inevitable!!
