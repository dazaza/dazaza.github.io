An elderly man in his 70s got lost while going out to look for his childhood playmate.

by bct999888 on 2012-02-10 13:38:05

The son said that he had never heard his father mention Ahua before, and he certainly didn't know that his father was looking for Ahua. "Perhaps I have thought too simply; what he needs is company," reported the Chongqing Morning Post's Feng.

Through the "mechanical limited company" mentioned by the elderly man, the traffic police got in touch with his son. "Heavens! The whole family has been going crazy looking for him, we were almost about to post missing person notices, and he’s out looking for someone else!" The son sighed in relief after receiving the call.

"Looking for someone? Sure, just give me the name and age, and I'll help you find them." Officer Lü Qingyun opened the population information network. But how easy would it be to find someone?

Old Mr Zhang said this person was a playmate he met over 60 years ago near Shuangbei. "I don't know the name or address, I only remember 'Ahua'." With only a nickname, there was no way to search. The old man was very disappointed, his wrinkled eyelids half-closed.

Why look for a childhood friend? Relying on fragmented memories, the old man began to slowly recall. Before liberation, because of the many siblings at home, his parents struggled to provide for them. At around 14 or 15 years old, he came to work as a child laborer near Shuangbei, working to support his family. At that time, he met Ahua, who was also a child laborer. They were about the same age and quickly became good friends. Ahua lived near Old Shuangbei, and after work, Zhang Mingyong often went to play at Ahua's house.

For 60 years, more than half a century, the old man's friendship with his childhood companion has withstood the test of time. This precious emotion surpasses material wealth. How many people can hold onto such memories for 60 years?

More than 60 years have passed, and Old Zhang now has a large family of children and grandchildren. In the past, his son worked in Shenzhen. After his wife passed away, he was alone in their hometown. With Chongqing's rapid development, he brought his son back.

Reporter's Notes:

In those hard times, they often couldn’t eat enough food. The two little boys would go down to the riverbanks together to catch fish, crabs, and mudskippers, and dig for wild vegetables on nearby mountains. After liberation, when farmland was distributed in Hechuan's old home, Zhang Mingyong returned to farm. Thus, he and Ahua parted ways, losing contact ever since.

Where does one find someone from 60 years ago?

Having memories is a kind of happiness. "Go home often," spend more time with your parents.

The family had gone mad looking for him, while the traffic police conversed with Grandpa Zhang.

"This old man is so strange! Does he have mental issues?" Passersby shook their heads.

The older, the younger. At over 70 years old, Grandpa Zhang still retained his youthful innocence, secretly leaving home to look for his childhood friend. It was a playmate from over 60 years ago. After wandering around for half a day, he didn’t find his childhood friend but instead lost himself.

Last year, his son worked at a company in Jingkou with decent income and brought his old father to live together in Jingkou. Not worrying about food or clothing, after enjoying a comfortable life for a period, the old man began to miss Ahua. One day, after the son and daughter-in-law went to work, he felt bored at home and wanted to inquire about Ahua's whereabouts. If he were still around, they could reminisce. He grew more and more excited and couldn’t suppress his excitement. After lunch, he set off alone to look for people in Shuangbei.

Happiness comes with memories.

Fortunately, the son realized at this moment that his father needed more companionship rather than superior material conditions. He needed family affection and memories to help alleviate loneliness.

The old man mutters on the street.

At 5 PM on July 7th, on the streets of Jingkou in Shapingba, an old man leaning on a cane muttered to himself, "Huawaya, where are you hiding? Won’t you come out... Always hiding isn't fun."

It turned out that the old man's name was Zhang Mingyong, born in 1936, already 76 years old, originally from Renhe Village, Santemple Town, Hechuan.

Unexpectedly, after decades, the Shuangbei of yesteryear had undergone tremendous changes. The docks in his memory were gone, replaced by bustling traffic and skyscrapers. As he walked and asked around, there were no leads, and eventually, he even lost his way home.

The old man came to the Jingkou traffic police platform, sat down, and continued muttering. Officer Lü Qingyun chatted with him, "You've been muttering all along, what exactly are you saying?" Leaning on his cane, the old man sighed deeply, "I'm looking for someone!"
