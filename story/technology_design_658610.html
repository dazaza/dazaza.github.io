Common Misconceptions about Using Car Floor Mats

by chituma222 on 2012-02-04 10:51:37

1. One-sided emphasis on easy cleaning

Analysis: The purpose of using car floor mats is to absorb water, dust, reduce noise and protect the main carpet. Focusing only on easy cleaning and avoiding rain and snow pollution may lead to counterproductive results and losing sight of the bigger picture.

2. Repeated placement of floor mats

Analysis: Placing PVC, 3D, rubber or other floor mats on the original velvet floor mat provided by the car manufacturer to avoid damage and pollution to the velvet mat is actually increasing safety hazards that affect braking and acceleration.

3. Failure to remove the protective film of the main carpet

Analysis: The PE protective film of the main carpet is used to protect the main carpet from damage and pollution when there is no other protection. Before installing the car floor mat, this PE film must be removed to ensure that the TPR nails at the bottom of the floor mat can contact and rub against the main carpet, achieving the anti-slip function.

4. Not cleaning car floor mats or having them cleaned at car wash shops

Analysis: While the car floor mat performs its functions of "absorbing water and dust", it also gets polluted itself, which should be cleaned as soon as possible. Otherwise, it will cause bacterial reproduction, affecting air quality and the health of the car owner. Especially for looped fiber mats, the dirt and dust inside will accumulate more and more, making it difficult to clean.

Simple water rinsing by car wash shops cannot completely remove the hidden dirt in the car floor mats. The cleaning of car floor mats should be handled by dry cleaning shops or manual washing.

5. Using one set of floor mats for many years

Analysis: After continuous use, any item may lose its designed functionality even if it still looks intact. Car owners with conditions can purchase two sets of velvet car floor mats for alternate use, which helps maintain the car, extend the product's service life, and save economic expenditure.

6. Natural, odorless equals environmental friendly

Analysis: Almost all modern products have undergone synthetic processing. Whether a product is environmentally friendly should be determined by inspection certificates issued by professional institutions.
