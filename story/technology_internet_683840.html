South Korea raises the issue of North Korean defectors at the UN Human Rights Council

by loved7k9 on 2012-02-29 16:13:43

People's Network (Weibo) Brussels, February 28th - The 19th session of the United Nations (Weibo) Human Rights Council was held in Geneva from February 27 to March 23. This session will review the Secretary-General's report, the UN High Commissioner for Human Rights Office, and the annual report of the High Commissioner for Human Rights. It will discuss civil rights, political rights, economic, social and cultural rights, and development rights. In addition, it will focus on the human rights situation in Syria, Yemen, Afghanistan, and Palestine.

Video: The Ministry of the Interior claims that South Korean media hype about defectors does not conform to the actual situation.

Source: Western Television's "Western Noon News"

On the opening day of the high-level special meeting, South Korea raised the issue of North Korean [defectors]. When mentioning the problem of forced repatriation of [defectors], South Korea used the term [all countries directly concerned]. Clearly, the directly relevant countries refer to China. This is the first time that the South Korean government has mentioned the issue of China forcibly repatriating North Korean [defectors] at the UN Human Rights Council meeting.

[South Korea sincerely urges all directly concerned countries to adhere to the principle of non-refoulement, thus avoiding placing these (‘defectors’) in a desperate situation, which could have serious consequences.] South Korea's Deputy Minister of the Interior, Kim Bong-hyun (Kim Bong-hyun phonetic translation), said at this meeting: [The refugee and ‘defector’ issue in North Korea is not a political issue, but a humanitarian and human rights issue.]

After South Korea spoke at the UN Human Rights Council, South Korean media immediately hyped up this issue. Regarding this matter, Chinese Foreign Ministry (Weibo) spokesperson Hong Lei stated during the regular press conference on the 28th that some South Korean media's hype over the so-called [defector] issue has an emotional and politicized tendency, which does not conform to the facts and is not conducive to resolving the issue. He hopes that the South Korean side will approach it calmly.

Hong Lei said that according to information obtained by China, some illegal North Korean immigrants have been repeatedly deported multiple times, some as many as ten or more times. Their illegal entry is mainly for economic purposes and there is insufficient evidence to classify them as [refugees].

Hong Lei reiterated that China's legal dignity should be respected and protected. Illegal immigration and organizing smuggling are criminal acts that will not be tolerated in any country.

Hong Lei pointed out that the hype by some South Korean media over the so-called [defector] issue has an emotional and politicized tendency, which neither conforms to the actual situation nor is conducive to resolving the issue. China will continue to appropriately handle relevant issues based on domestic law, international law, and humanitarian principles.

[China and South Korea are important cooperative partners in many fields. Under the new circumstances, both sides face many challenges and problems that need to be addressed together. Regarding this current issue, we hope that the South Korean side will approach it calmly, resolve differences through effective communication and cooperation, and safeguard the overall situation of Sino-Korean relations with practical actions.] Hong Lei said. ★
