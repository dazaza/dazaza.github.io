National Energy Network is accused of making money in the name of NDRC, while the website claims it's legal

by nif2dfoe on 2012-02-08 11:48:07

Screenshot of the NDRC statement. Recently, many netizens have posted online to report that "the training provided by the National Energy Network is all about scamming money, and the certificates issued are not recognized in society." Upon investigation, it was found that the National Energy Network (www.chinaecce.com) is actually a corporate website. Recently, the Department of Resource Conservation and Environmental Protection of the National Development and Reform Commission (hereinafter referred to as the "Environmental Resources Department") has issued a statement on its official website stating that it has no connection with the training organized by this website.

The NDRC stated that it has no relationship with the "National Energy Network." According to complaints from netizens, the "National Energy Network" misled people into thinking that it is the official website of the "National Energy Administration," and that its provided training and services are authoritative. Investigations revealed that the National Energy Network is actually an enterprise website hosted by Beijing Guofa Huanjing Technology Development Co., Ltd. The registered name of the website is "Energy Network," not "National Energy Network," and the person in charge of the website is "Yu Hao." The staff of the website also confirmed the registration information of the website to the reporter and repeatedly emphasized that the "National Energy Network" is a legitimate website with business operating permits and is registered with relevant institutions.

On the 26th of this month, the Environmental Resources Department of the National Development and Reform Commission issued a statement (as shown in the figure above) stating that "the National Energy Network is a privately owned enterprise website, and the training it organizes has no relation to the Environmental Resources Department of the National Development and Reform Commission. Whether or not one participates in the training has no bearing on obtaining the qualification for energy-saving service company filing."

Defamation by competitors?

In response to the statement from the National Development and Reform Commission, on the 28th of this month, the National Energy Network issued a "solemn statement" saying: "There are unknown organizations and individuals impersonating our company personnel to carry out illegal business activities."

In the afternoon of the previous day, the aforementioned staff member of the National Energy Network told the reporter that the company takes the statement from the Environmental Resources Department of the National Development and Reform Commission very seriously. After investigations from top to bottom, they discovered that "it was malicious defamation by competitors."

The reporter found that there is another enterprise website called the "National Energy Network" (www.ocpe.com.cn) hosted by the Beijing Nengde International Cultural Communication Center, but its registered name is "Beijing Nengde International Cultural Communication Center," and the person in charge of the website is "Guo Neng." However, the aforementioned staff member denied that these two identically named "National Energy Networks" are competitors.

The website offers numerous training programs

The reporter found multiple training documents on the National Energy Network, including various qualification trainings, business trainings, and internal corporate trainings. Among them, a document titled "Energy Network [2011] No. 27" states that the National Energy Network will host several "Project Application Boot Camps (Planning Classes)," and the registration fee for the "two days and one night" training is as high as 19,800 yuan.

The aforementioned staff member told the reporter: "All the trainings provided by the company are legal and authorized by the government. The certificates obtained are issued by legitimate institutions, and many trainings are jointly organized with departments such as the NDRC, MIIT, and MHRSS."

Southern Daily reporter Ding Jianting
