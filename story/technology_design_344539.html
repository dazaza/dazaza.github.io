(Original) You come, I'll pick you up.

by wswzjsgs on 2010-04-13 11:03:02

I really like a saying of Mr. Liang Shiqiu. His simple words are especially touching and keep the anticipatory heart calm for a long time after reading.

"You go, I won't see you off; When you come, no matter how big the wind or rain is, I will meet you."

In these shallow 23 words, it reflects the grandeur of love. And this grandeur does not have any boastful words, nor any trace of pleading and attachment.

It is not difficult to see from these words that Mr. Liang's persistence and dignity in love. "You go, I won't see you off." Yes, many people have encountered love that has left. The union and separation of love depend on feelings, fate, and the efforts of both parties. Even if breaking up is also a beautiful end of fate.

Often, people today do not believe that there are two endings to love: separation and reunion.

People who often separate like to use all sorts of strategies to save a love that has already fallen into crisis. They cannot see that if they could truly be together, why would they really leave?

Actually, Mr. Liang's seemingly heartless words are not due to his lack of emotion, but rather his understanding of a truth: what belongs to you will always be yours, and what doesn't belong to you won't be yours even if you argue until you're blue in the face. It's just like the song says, "There is a kind of love called letting go." If you let go and give up, that shows your understanding. If you feel pain, helplessness, and return to your original self, you must return to the original starting point, make adjustments, and restart yourself. This is the inherent ending of love. Too many people who don't understand this truth have staged too many tragedies about human love. In fact, these tragedies could have been avoided.

Thus, Mr. Liang's next excellent line came: "When you come, no matter how big the wind or rain is, I will go to meet you."

Calmness often brews an unrest as tumultuous as stormy waves behind it. No one likes to be abandoned or accept failed love. Only when the person you love thinks again, makes another choice, and selects you, then walks back and gazes at you with their eyes, does that love truly find its foothold.

At this moment, you need to put down all your dignity, all your hypocrisy and arrogance, honestly pass through the wind and rain, wait at the intersection where she has been, holding an umbrella, waiting for the arrival of your love. The arrival of this rare love not only lets you gain true companionship, but also earns the other party's true respect.

Mr. Liang's words made me realize that everyone should learn to appropriately uphold their own dignity. Only in this way can you allow the other party to gain true dignity, and let each other get the true love and affection that only belongs to each other.

Compared with this, how we perform is something only we ourselves know. But I believe that every person who waits for love will remember Mr. Liang's heartfelt persistence in love. We will surely draw the essence of being a person and the promise of love from it...
