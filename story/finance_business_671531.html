Lin Shuhao's surprise streak was finally stopped, as the Nets' point guard Deron Williams shone today (March 21). The two faced each other for the second time this season in a fierce battle. Williams hit 8 three-pointers, scoring a season-high 38 points along with 4 rebounds and 6 assists. This performance overshadowed Lin Shuhao's all-around effort of 21 points, 7 rebounds, and 4 assists, leading the Nets to pressure Nick Young in the second half and eventually secure a win with a score of 100-92.

As the first victim of the "Lin-sanity" phenomenon, one of the NBA’s top point guards, Deron Williams, wasn’t about to let an opponent dominate him twice consecutively. “The last time we played against them, I had never heard of Lin Shuhao," said Williams before the game. "Now after eight games, we don't want to let him have his way..."

Williams didn’t disappoint the fans at the start of the match, scoring 36 points before sitting out, leaving Nick Young dazed alongside Lin Shuhao who managed only 11 points. Williams helped the Nets lead by 18 points going into the fourth quarter. Although Lin Shuhao still put up a commendable performance with 21 points and bright assists, he shot only 7 of 18, showing a significant gap compared to when he made over 5 shots. After three quarters under strict defense against Williams, Lin could only score 11 points. In the third quarter, Williams lit up with 18 points while Lin and Nick struggled to explode offensively.

"I think today we did not show enough momentum and efforts on the defensive end, especially myself," Lin Shuhao said after the game. "I took it for granted, and letting Williams score 30 is absolutely no way to make the team win. So I must be harder on the court, our team must be harder. I need to get to the bottom of why we lost today."

D-Will called Lin Shuhao's rise an impressive shock education. Those years we remember our 116-point performances, but how did the Nets shockingly achieve a 97-85 victory over the Bulls, ending their two-game winning streak?
