US Secret Service questions married couple who ate free lunch

by 3ryo950p3u on 2009-12-01 15:25:10

Core tip: An official of the US Secret Service involved in the investigation revealed on the 29th that the Secret Service had conducted a two-day inquiry and interview with Tareq Salahi and his wife Michaele, a couple from Virginia, USA, who crashed the White House state dinner. Moreover, two US senators requested on the same day that the federal judiciary file criminal charges against this "crash couple". Yangtze Evening News reported on December 1st that according to American media reports on November 30th, an official of the US Secret Service involved in the investigation revealed on the 29th that the Secret Service had already conducted a two-day inquiry and interview with Tareq Salahi and his wife Michaele, a couple from Virginia, USA, who crashed the White House state dinner. Additionally, two US senators demanded on the same day that the federal judicial department initiate criminal proceedings against the "crash couple". This unnamed Secret Service official said that the inquiry and interview with the Salahis took place on the 27th and 28th, not at their home or in the Secret Service's office building but at a neutral third location. However, the official did not disclose the specifics of the inquiry. Meanwhile, the US Secret Service is also conducting an internal investigation to determine what went wrong on the night of the state dinner on November 24th that allowed the couple to slip through. The public has been very curious about how the Salahis managed to infiltrate the White House state dinner. However, there is currently no information available regarding this aspect. "Further questioning and investigation are still needed, we will not make any public comments on this incident this week... Perhaps by December 2nd, we will submit a written report, but the content will not be made public," said Ed Donovan, spokesperson for the US Secret Service, in an interview on the 29th. Another Secret Service spokesperson, McKin, stated on November 26th that the investigation would develop into a criminal case, meaning that the Salahi couple could potentially end up in court. In a Fox News program on the 29th, Indiana Democratic Senator Evan Bayh and Arizona Republican Senator Jon Kyl also urged the federal law enforcement authorities to immediately file criminal charges against the Salahi couple. However, it was introduced that the Secret Service has yet to decide whether to file criminal charges against the two. "You must give them a severe lesson," said Indiana Democratic Senator Evan Bayh. Arizona Republican Senator Jon Kyl agreed with Evan Bayh's view, "They lied to people and do not intend to tell the truth, they must be severely punished so as to warn others." China Daily (Source of this article: Xinhua News Agency - Yangtze Evening News)
