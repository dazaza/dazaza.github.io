In the third year of Emperor Xianfeng's reign, the Taiping Heavenly Kingdom established its capital in Nanjing, causing shock and panic throughout the country. The Qing court dispatched all its forces to suppress the Taiping army with great effort. The Huai River area became the main battlefield between the Qing army and the Taiping army.

The constant warfare in the Huai River area led to the neglect of the largest salt-producing region under the Qing court - the Two Huai Salt Fields. The salt routes were blocked, and the important tax revenue source for the court - the salt tax - disappeared entirely. The already strained Qing court finances became even more dire. Compounding this, the lack of Huai salt affected the Yangtze River mid-lower reaches provinces and 250 prefectures and counties that traditionally relied on Huai salt. This put additional strain on the Qing dynasty's rule. To alleviate the suffering of hundreds of millions of people along the Chu coast who were eating bland food and to increase the court's tax revenue for military pay, Emperor Xianfeng was forced to issue an order: Sichuan salt to aid Chu. As a result, merchants from southern Sichuan gathered, vying to make fortunes.

In Fuding County of southern Sichuan, unlike the war-torn Hubei and Hunan provinces, there was a sense of leisure and tranquility. Local large salt merchants took advantage of their geographical location and quickly expanded their businesses.

Li You tang, the largest landowner in Fuding County, had vast tracts of land and several salt wells on his own land. However, following family traditions, he never sold any of his land or salt wells but instead rented them out to collect rent. His only son, Li Chonglin, was about eighteen or nineteen years old and studied with the Li family tutor, Mr. Su, and his daughter, Su Liuqing. Since Li You tang was illiterate, he placed all his hopes on Chonglin, allowing him to learn business from the manager Dong. After Mr. Su passed away, Liuqing was left destitute and taken in by the Li family to help manage contracts and documents. Later, she was taken as a concubine by Master Li. Disappointed by Liuqing, Chonglin left home.

Wang Zhengyun, a salt smuggler, was bold and resourceful. He bribed salt officials at all levels and secretly transported contraband salt between Hubei-Hunan and Sichuan, making a fortune. His steward Pan Xinshan and strategist Mou were loyal assistants. Not content to merely buy salt from others, Wang Zhengyun also expedited the drilling of salt wells, renting docks, and building salt factories.

Mei Zhenqing, a doctor for generations, incurred the wrath of Lord Lang, the salt commissioner of Sichuan, due to the illness of his concubine. Following his father's teachings, Mei abandoned medicine and focused solely on the salt industry. His daughter, Mei Jing, was naturally beautiful. Qin Rilang, a major salt merchant and owner of the Western Qin Cargo Store, believed in being reserved. Seeing the opportunity, he also extensively purchased land and drilled salt wells. His son, Qin Yulin, secretly admired Mei Jing.

The big salt merchant from Yangzhou, Master Zhao, fled to Sichuan to avoid danger.

To obtain the rich salt deposits of Shanzi Dam, Wang Zhengyun and Qin Rilang secretly competed, both determined to acquire it. Wang Zhengyun tried to recruit Master Zhao as a partner but was politely declined by the shrewd Master Zhao, leaving Wang Zhengyun disappointed. Just when Wang Zhengyun was at a loss for money, Pan Xinshan came up with an idea: Wang's workers and salt laborers could invest collectively to raise the funds. As a result, Wang successfully bought Shanzi Dam.

Wang Zhengyun had two wives; his second concubine, Xueqin, had been married for ten years without bearing children. After her parents passed away, her sister Xueyan also came to live with Wang Zhengyun, who took a liking to her.

After the Sichuan salt aided Chu, the enormous salt tax supported half of the Qing court's revenue. The emperor sent capable minister Luo Bingzhang as a special envoy to Sichuan and also sent the disgraced Prince Hui to Sichuan to "supervise salt affairs."

Master Zhao did not want to miss the opportunity to make money and decided to partner with Master Li.

To reduce oppression by the authorities and resolve disputes among salt merchants, Master Zhao proposed the establishment of a salt merchants' association, which was welcomed by the salt merchants. Both Qin Rilang and Wang Zhengyun were elected as "heads" of the association. When they visited the new county magistrate Lu Ji, they were refused entry.

Chonglin attempted suicide in Rongjing County but was saved. To make a living, he wandered around selling cloth. Due to selling "shrinking cloth," he won the favor of the boss's daughter and became a son-in-law.

Doctor Mei's salt well was located on the border between Sichuan and Yunnan. Shi Dakai, active in Yunnan, prepared to invade Sichuan. Doctor Mei agreed to plead with a leader of the rebel army to protect his salt well. Unfortunately, after drinking, the strategist revealed this information. Magistrate Lu Ji used this as an excuse to sentence the entire Mei family to death. Doctor Mei sought refuge for Mei Jing with Prince Hui while treating his wife. The prince agreed.

To alleviate the Qing court's empty treasury, Minister Luo established a water tax bureau, which was opposed by the salt merchants. On the surface, families Qin and Wang showed no resistance, but their servants caused trouble for the tax collectors. The tax collectors, in turn, extorted money from the salt merchants under various pretexts, escalating tensions between officials and merchants. Many salt workers were laid off and lived in poverty, causing public resentment. After meticulous planning, the water tax bureau was destroyed.

Li family's salt well workers pressured the Li family to reduce rent. Master Zhao voluntarily helped the Li family out of trouble to win Su Liuqing's favor. Liuqing managed the Li family's business and dared not offend Master Zhao, who then became increasingly demanding.

Magistrate Lu Ji vowed to capture the mastermind behind the water tax case and closely monitored Wang Zhengyun. Not long after, he found some clues. Wang Zhengyun was imprisoned but remained silent, even mocking Magistrate Lu Ji. Through careful arrangements, Qin Rilang and the Wang family framed the imperial envoy, reversing the water tax case and achieving a great victory for the Sichuan salt merchants.

The Wang family's business flourished, and Qin Rilang resigned from his position as head of the salt merchants' association, seeing through the world's illusions.

The Sichuan salt aiding Chu largely ended, greatly affecting the salt merchants. The Wang family colluded with the government to monopolize the docks, charging silver to passing salt ships and splitting the profits with the government. Sichuan Governor Ding Baozhen reported Wang Zhengyun's various illegal activities to the court. Wang Zhengyun fled overnight.

Master Zhao had been manipulating business deals, gradually leading to the decline of the Li family. Su Liuqing fell into Master Zhao's trap and was expelled from the Li family by Master Li. Master Zhao sued the Li family in court to seize their assets. Liuqing pleaded with the magistrate on behalf of the Li family, winning the case, but she swallowed gold and committed suicide afterward.

Chonglin returned to the Li family and took on the responsibility for the family.

During the Qingming Festival, the descendants of the four major salt merchants unexpectedly met in the suburbs and together paid respects to their ancestors.
