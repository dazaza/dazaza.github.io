A Few Hopes for the Official Version of Zhengtu

by lk0gameg on 2009-07-18 14:31:34

I haven't been playing Journey to a Long March for long, probably less than two months. I have a few suggestions about the game:

1. Basic quests are too time-consuming... This is my biggest feeling. Escorting convoys, family convoy, your own convoy, and trade missions basically take 5-6 hours a day. Moreover, the cost of teleportation by coachman is too high because you need to go to Qingyuan Village to get family experience. If you want to save time, you have to waste money. You spend no less than 10L on the coachman every day. Therefore, I suggest reducing the number of trade missions and convoy escort missions while increasing the experience and money gained each time.

2. Mining Area... For most players, being killed N times in the mining area is something they may be used to. As a result, it's very difficult for players to complete mining tasks, especially for players from low-level or weak countries. Therefore, I suggest setting up a neutral mining period without killing, which I think is very necessary. For example, after the Royal City Capture War, the country that gains control of the Royal City has about 3 hours of exclusive mining rights. Additionally, set up a 2-hour absolute safety period for players from all countries to mine safely.

3. Regarding betrayal... I once encountered players from other countries betraying to our country and then colluding with people from their original country to rob wildly in our country. These players greatly disrupt the game order. Their purpose is only to sell their account for a higher RMB price. This practice is absolutely detrimental to the long-term development of the game. I suggest deciding to expel certain players' nationality through a nationwide vote. Once more than 60% of the players in the country agree to expel a player's nationality, the system automatically sends him back to his original country.

4. Regarding malicious killing... If domestic players often maliciously kill domestic players, I suggest that if a player kills more than 40 domestic players, all attribute points are reduced by 4 points, and the highest skill point is reduced by 1 point. This can effectively control some players from malicious killing. Additionally, if a player kills another domestic player who is more than 10 levels lower, the killer must compensate for the corresponding medical expenses. If there is a 20-level difference, compensation is 20L; if there is a 30-level difference, compensation is 30L, and so on... This can effectively prevent incidents of fratricidal killings over BOSS disputes.

5. Regarding robbery... Players above level 130 robbing level 50 convoys and camels... This is undoubtedly very unfair. Some players log out when they go to another country, then log back in to rob, and log out again once they've robbed. This behavior can be described as shameless. I suggest that when a player goes to a foreign country and logs out and then logs back in, they can only return to their country's border guard. Robbing camels and convoys with a 20-40 level difference yields 40%, and robbing camels and convoys with a 40+ level difference yields no money. Additionally, at the border guard, an NPC can be set up where players state their purpose before going abroad and pay a certain amount of deposit. If they achieve their goal and return home, they can get their deposit back and receive a certain amount of money reward from this NPC.
