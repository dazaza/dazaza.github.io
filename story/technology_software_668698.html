Queen Bee User Manual

Queen Bee (QUEEN BEE)

1. Introduction

This machine is a standard 97 game with two built-in graphic modes: one traditional and one novel insect-themed. The mode can be selected in the settings. Universal Jack (JOKER): When the central wheel on the main screen shows the Queen Bee, it represents the Joker, which can substitute for any item, such as watermelon or bell, thus increasing your chances of winning big prizes.

Lucky Reel (LUCKY REEL): When four corner wheels among the nine wheels show the same item, the middle wheel will spin again to increase the chance of double-line wins.

Diamond Fever (DIAMOND FEVER): When diamonds of the same color connect (or orchids in the insect pattern mode), you enter the Diamond Fever bonus. In this special bonus, there are four spinning wheels in the center of the screen with running lights moving around the edges. Press the start button to stop the wheels and lights. The score depends on where they stop. After scoring, the wheels and lights continue until the light stops at EXIT (exit) in the corners, concluding the special game. Blue gem connections allow two plays, red gem connections allow three.

2. Mystery Box Bonus (BOX FEVER)

When three reels show connected treasure chests (trees in the insect pattern mode), you enter this bonus round. There are nine small squares hiding various mystery prizes (scores). A hand-shaped arrow moves sequentially; press the start button to select a square and determine the score. If you pass a level, you can select again until you pick a bee.

Double-Up (VOTE) Game

After winning in the main game, you can choose to collect the score or play a Double-Up game via voting. In the Double-Up screen, guess if the number is greater or less than 7 (7 guarantees passage). Correct guesses double the score, with occasional triple, quadruple, or quintuple rewards. The top left corner displays the last five voting records for reference.

Double Voting (Double Double-Up): Based on twice the score from the main game, with any shortfall deducted from CREDIT.

Half Voting (Half Double-Up): Based on half the score from the main game, with the remaining half directly added to CREDIT.

Operation and Adjustment Methods

Wiring Selection (CONTROL PANEL TYPE)

This game has two wiring modes available. Decide which to use and adjust the PC board's SWITCH accordingly.

Accounting (BOOK)

The accounting screen has five pages accessible by pressing the (accounting key). Pressing (start key) exits the accounting screen.

Reset (RESET)

Full Reset: After powering on, press the (reset key) to clear all data, including win rates. This clears all accounting data except for permanent counters. Avoid full resets to maintain accurate win rate and business results.

Clear Current Report Records: Used during account settlement. Enter the first accounting page with the (accounting key). The displayed data reflects operations since the last reset. Press the (reset key) to clear coin insertion, payout, opening scores, washing scores, total games played, total scores, and total winnings.

Settings (SETEP)

To make operation easier, this machine allows direct adjustment of all system settings on-screen, abandoning traditional DIP SWITCH methods. Two ways to access the settings screen:

1. After powering on, press the (test key) once.

2. Power off, hold the (accounting key) while powering back on.

In the settings screen, use the (double-up key) to navigate pages, the (big key) to select items, and the (small key) to adjust values. Changes take effect immediately without needing to power off.

Win Rate (WIN RATE)

The win rate in this game is calculated as follows: Win Rate = (Payout + Wash Score) / (Coin Insertion + Open Score).

Special Note: To increase the main screen win rate for business needs, first adjust the win rate to the desired value and then press the reset key on the motherboard, which may cause over-payout and result in business losses. To decrease the win rate, no reset is necessary as the motherboard adjusts automatically. However, avoid drastically lowering the win rate to prevent poor payout situations affecting business. It is recommended not to lower the win rate by more than 2%.

Payout Mode:

This game offers two payout modes adjustable based on market player acceptance and preference. Adjust the PC board's SWITCH accordingly. (This payout mode adjustment does not affect the main win rate setting.)
