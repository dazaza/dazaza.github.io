Fresh products continue to increase, and intelligent anti-theft follows closely behind.

by gdshstyle on 2012-03-02 16:46:49

Analysis of the smart home market in January: After the long holiday, the smart home market was relatively dull. However, it still maintained steady growth in various cities. Guangdong remained strong, Beijing and Shanghai showed stable growth, while second-tier cities were relatively quiet. Let's take a look at the specific situations in these cities.

Infrared cameras as fresh products continue to grow, followed by smart anti-theft systems. Entering February, infrared cameras became increasingly popular, reaching a search volume of 23,820, which surprised me. Smart anti-theft and infrared dome cameras also showed steady growth. Emerging industries are on the rise.

The New Year and Spring Festival holidays in January were close together, making the entire smart home industry relatively low-key. However, infrared cameras took advantage of this slow period in the market, surpassing previous popular smart home products to reach the top spot, an unexpected development. But fundamentally speaking, during the year-end period when thefts were rampant, infrared cameras indeed provided homeowners with more peace of mind.

Looking at electronic shoe cabinets and smart anti-theft systems, there was a purchase demand rate of nearly 7,600, further proving that security products were quite popular at year-end. Housing developments increased their anti-theft facilities to enhance homeowners' sense of security. As the saying goes, where there is demand, there is production, which is aptly reflected here!

South China and East China continued to be stable, while Central China and North China need more effort. South China and East China regions increased their investments, with Guangzhou being the most prominent, having an overwhelming 73% ratio far exceeding other cities. Together with East China, these two major regions completely accounted for more than half of the product lines, fully deserving the title of leading cities of the year.

Central China and North China could be described as steadily "retreating." Beijing and Shanghai were clearly in hibernation mode, and how they return to the growth ranks remains to be seen.

City rankings are not surprising, small cities rotate positions. There's no need to elaborate on Guangdong's growth momentum. These past few months, Shanghai and Beijing have been like this. However, second-tier cities alternately make the list. What are the reasons behind this?

Regarding this issue, the editor is not very clear but will continue to monitor the situation. If forced to speculate, it might be due to the unstable population in smaller cities, so their purchasing enthusiasm isn't that strong.

To sum up, after the long holidays, all regions maintained a similar level. However, there are no signs of recovery in Shanghai and Beijing yet. Therefore, the editor can only say that we need to observe for some more time. Spring is coming soon, let's see how the smart home market will recover!
