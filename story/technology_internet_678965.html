Answers to Parents' Questions: My Baby Has Diverse Interests, How Can I Make Him Focus on One?

by lmxbbwudi23 on 2012-02-27 17:37:09

Baby is interested in many classes, but we can't enroll in all of them

Mom of Duo asks Qianbao New Education: My child is 3 years old this year and is interested in many classes. But it's impossible for him to attend all of them. So how do we guide the baby to focus his interest on a certain point?

Qianbao New Education answers Mom of Duo: Being curious, asking questions, and having strong imitation abilities are all characteristics of children's personalities. Parents need to protect this precious spirit of exploration well. Ms. Zhang Jiajing, a teacher from Hangzhou Lan Garden Kindergarten, said that unlike middle-class children who will tell their parents what they like to do, Duo is now 3 years old and cannot express his preferences yet. This requires parental guidance, more observation of the child's personality traits, and cultivation of interests according to their true wishes.

So, what methods can be used to observe the child's interest points? Ms. Zhang's answer is: through games. For example, when a child is building blocks, whether he shows great concentration. Generally, focused children are mostly patient, so you can choose some interests that require patience for cultivation, such as music, painting, etc.

Will become a primary school student in the second half of the year. Should I attend preschool now?

Mom of Xiaodou asks Qianbao New Education: My child will start primary school in the second half of the year. Before that, he attended English, Taekwondo and other interest classes. However, I am now quite worried that he may not adapt to primary school. Is it necessary to attend preschool for training?

Qianbao New Education answers Mom of Xiaodou: The transformation from a kindergarten child to a first-grade primary school student is indeed very important. I estimate that many parents of senior kindergarten children are going through the same worries as Mom of Xiaodou. Ms. Yu Ping, a teacher from Hangzhou Chaohui Xincun No.5 Kindergarten, said that one of the main reasons why children don't adapt to primary school life is insufficient self-care ability, lack of self-learning management ability, and restraint ability.

Now, parents can let children recognize some words and listen to phonetic reading discs when they have time, because the most tiring part of first-grade primary school Chinese language learning is character recognition and phonetics. Also, organizing one's own tools can be cultivated from now on. As for whether or not to attend training classes, it depends on individual needs.

Ms. Yu's kindergarten recently held a lecture on the transition from kindergarten to primary school, with very practical content. Here are a few points for parents to refer to and compare:

Preparation for Chinese: Let children learn characters appropriately. Listen to more phonetic sounds and get familiar with some basic pronunciations.

Preparation for Mathematics: Focus on cultivating observation, comparison, classification, and logical thinking skills.

Training Attention: Play cards. Disrupt the order, cultivate observational skills and memory, gradually train, and increase the amount step by step without too much pressure.

2-year-old attends English class. Is it too early and will it scare her?

Mom of Benben asks Qianbao New Education: When Benben was 2 years and 10 months old, I took her to try out an American-style English class. Although Benben had never been to kindergarten before, she could sit still quite well.

The child now has a good reaction to the English interest class, twice a week. However, an English-teaching friend heard about this and reacted: [What are you doing? Attending English class at such an early age, isn't it too young? Don't scare her!] I'm not sure if my approach is correct.

Qianbao New Education answers Mom of Benben: English classes, art classes, and music classes are currently the top choices for parents enrolling in interest classes, but there are still techniques for selection. Mr. Ding Bo, with 25 years of early childhood education experience from Hangzhou Municipal Government Kindergarten, gives parents some advice based on his own experiences.

Mr. Ding's daughter also attended interest classes when she was in the middle class of kindergarten. At that time, she enrolled in Zhejiang University's "Cambridge English." Because the teacher taught very vividly, his daughter was very willing to attend the English interest class and even sang the songs she learned actively for Mr. Ding at home, although sometimes the pitch wasn't quite right. Mr. Ding believes that children should learn things they are interested in, as this yields particularly good results.

Mr. Ding suggests that before deciding to enroll, parents should preferably listen to two classes with the child first to get a feel. If the teacher teaches vividly and the teaching aids are fully prepared, allowing the child to learn through games, then participation can be considered.

Mr. Ding thinks that it's better to study English interest classes during the kindergarten stage, but it's not suitable for children in the first and second grades of primary school, as it may confuse with phonetics.
