51job.com Hosts the 2011 HR Night Award Ceremony

by juyaqufa003 on 2011-12-19 09:09:07

China's largest human resources service provider, 51job (NASDAQ: JOBS), recently held a grand event in Shanghai titled "2011 HR Night - Award Ceremony for the Most Talented Human Resources Companies in China." More than 300 HR executives attended the ceremony. The awards not only recognized 100 exemplary enterprises in Chinese human resource management but also presented individual awards for strategy, training, recruitment, and campus recruitment, as well as an annual outstanding contribution award.

Industry professionals discussed three main topics under the theme of "Loving Your Career, Enjoying Your Work," including the professional attitudes and preferences of the post-80s and post-90s generations, the current state of employee leadership development, and talent management strategies amidst rising labor costs.

51job CEO Richard Yan expressed that in 2011, most Chinese companies faced talent shortages and salary increases exceeding GDP growth. In 2012, companies will face three major challenges: continuously rising labor costs, further market consolidation, and shifts in young people's employment perspectives. Yan predicted that due to an uncertain global economic outlook, the labor shortage in the Pearl River Delta and Yangtze River Delta might ease due to reduced orders, but labor costs would continue to rise. With further market integration, strong players would become stronger, while irrational competitors risked facing a "cut-off" of resources. The unique employment views of the younger generation, as seen in phenomena like "naked resignation" and "career treasure hunters," deserve HR attention.

Hay (Hewitt) Group Vice President for Greater China, Star Liang, proposed five major challenges for Chinese human capital management: the speed of talent growth cannot keep up with salary growth; employees are "physically present but mentally absent"; the speed of talent transformation cannot meet the needs of strategic/organizational transformation; lack of leadership development; and functional culture hinders innovation and speed improvement. Liang hopes that companies will adopt multiple approaches, combining "soft and hard measures" to improve employee engagement and effectiveness, and use more innovative methods to quickly cultivate leaders and team leadership, making change management routine.

Chief Consultant of Huizhi Consulting, Jianhua Gao, delivered a keynote report stating that the post-80s generation is the driving force behind the evolution of the Chinese market. He noted that the post-80s generation, with characteristics such as "equality consciousness," "desire for fame," "demand for a voice," "pursuit of quality," "attachment to the internet," and "assertion of individuality," presents challenges to HR. He suggested treating post-80s employees as consumers and providing them with opportunities to work, earn money, receive training, and advance.

Deloitte Management Consulting Leadership Business Partner, Tuoxuan Wang, stated that the characteristics of Chinese management talent have changed, with the post-80s and post-90s generations desiring to become managers faster. As this generation moves into working and managerial positions, their unique traits and views on work render traditional talent retention methods ineffective. Companies should comprehensively review existing operations and promptly adjust policies suitable for this group.

HR executives from GE, IBM, Tencent, APP (China), Tenneco, Inspur, Whirlpool, Sandvik, and Bossmann Appliances actively participated in discussions and expressed that truly socially responsible companies do not just engage in charity but also carefully manage employees as they would customers. "Cultivating employees and nurturing talent" is also part of a company's social responsibility.

About 51job

"51job" (Nasdaq: JOBS) is China's first professional human resources service provider to integrate multiple media resources. Combining traditional media, online media, and advanced information technology with a team of experienced professional consultants, it offers comprehensive professional human resources services, including recruitment, headhunting, training evaluation, and personnel outsourcing. It now has service institutions in 25 cities nationwide.

In September 2004, 51job became the first and currently the only Chinese human resources service company listed on the NASDAQ in the United States, making it one of the most influential human resources service providers in China.
