Can pregnant women use cosmetics?

By Pregnant Women and Cosmetics at 2012-3-8 in Pregnancy Skin Care 365 | 0 comments Pregnant women can use skin care products, right? This is the question that the vast majority of already pregnant or soon-to-be-pregnant women ask the most. Today, makeup experts for pregnant women will answer these questions.

Of course, pregnant women can use skin care products, but they need to be carefully selected.

Cosmetics are definitely something that pregnant women should not touch. Cosmetics generally contain preservatives, aromatic compounds, and pigments, which are substances that should absolutely be avoided during pregnancy. Moreover, they are the three main causes of skin allergies. The more complex the fragrance composition and the larger the amount used, the stronger the stimulation, and the easier it is to cause skin allergies and phototoxic reactions. In addition, some cosmetics contain heavy metals, which may endanger the safety of the fetus.

Many expectant mothers know that cosmetics should not be used. For the safety of their babies, they assume that skin care products also cannot be used. In fact, cosmetics and skin care products are two completely different concepts!

The damage that pregnancy and childbirth bring to a woman's skin is equivalent to 10 to 13 years of natural aging. The natural aging speed of the skin after childbirth is 33%-48% faster than before pregnancy! Therefore, if effective skin care is not done during pregnancy and childbirth, one might truly become a "yellow-faced woman" after childbirth.

In fact, as long as the chosen products are made from pure natural plant ingredients, additive-free, and gentle, there is no need to worry about safety issues.
