Often, the customer's needs are gradually revealed, and the true practical demands often aren't what was initially stated.

by softdong5j2 on 2011-04-12 11:01:48

Often, the true and practical needs of customers are gradually uncovered, and they tend to be more complex than initially described.

Below, I will share a real example of how customer needs evolve step by step, using the .NET framework. Specifically, we will look at ASP.NET permission management source code to illustrate how customer requirements deepen over time. What may seem like a simple requirement at first can lead to many additional functional demands, proving to be far from simple or easy.

---

**Step 1: A Simple Internal Instant Messaging Tool**

Recently, a customer approached me asking for a basic internal instant messaging tool for their enterprise. I recommended a $500 internal instant messaging tool that could meet the simplest requirements. It included full source code and some simple development interfaces that could be set up quickly.

---

**Step 2: Functional Expansion – File Sending, Video, Offline Files, Group Chats**

Once the initial requirements were met, additional requests started pouring in. The customer now wanted features such as file transfer, video chat, offline file sending, and group conversations. At this point, it was clear that they needed a professional and mature instant messaging solution, not just a simple reminder system. Even though these features could be provided, the customer's demands kept growing.

---

**Step 3: Comprehensive Documentation**

Having just the software wasn’t enough. The customer also requested various documents, including installation manuals and user guides, before they could officially use the system. Otherwise, they wouldn't know how to operate it. This made sense to some extent.

---

**Step 4: Demo System**

With all the documentation provided, the customer then requested a demo system. As expected, one demand led to another.

---

**Step 5: Support for Organizational Structures with Unlimited Sub-Departments**

After meeting the above requirements, the customer further requested support for organizational structures, including unlimited sub-departments and branches. Without this feature, the system would be unusable. We had no choice but to continue fulfilling their needs.

---

**Step 6: No Time Limitations on the Software**

Even after addressing all the preliminary preparations, the customer insisted that the software should not have any time limitations.

---

**Step 7: Removal of Non-Core Features**

After reviewing the user manual, the customer decided that certain features were unnecessary and should be removed.

---

**Step 8: Performance Testing Report**

The customer also wanted to know about the performance of the instant messaging software under different hardware environments. They asked how many concurrent users the system could support. This was certainly not an easy request.

---

**Step 9: Formal Contracts**

After resolving all the previous issues, the customer now required a formal commercial contract, along with detailed payment terms. These conditions were quite stringent, with clearly defined responsibilities and obligations.

---

**Step 10: Workflow Integration**

The customer mentioned that this project was bundled with workflow integration. Having just the instant messaging product wasn’t sufficient; they also needed a workflow component that could be configured via a B/S interface. Without this, the collaboration couldn’t proceed. Once again, the customer’s demands deepened step by step, which was quite exhausting.

---

**Step 11: Advanced Workflow Requirements**

Once the workflow component was introduced, the customer raised even more specific requirements, including dozens of detailed workflow features and some advanced functionalities. This was truly challenging.

---

**Conclusion:**

In my experience, achieving excellence in permission management and workflow management is already a significant challenge. An individual can only handle a limited number of tasks effectively.
