The Most Promising Speculation on the Next Generation of Internet Tycoons

by geekzhang on 2013-05-03 19:07:49

Shi Yuzhu and Jack Ma have stepped down from their positions as CEO; two of Tencent's founding five tigers, Pony Ma and Charles Chen, have also retired. The old guard is aging, but the new generation has yet to catch up, creating a sense of a leadership gap.The Shanghai Business Daily, in collaboration with numerous digital media outlets and industry insiders, initiated a vote to identify the next generation of internet leaders with the most potential.Lei Jun, CEO of Xiaomi TechnologyLei Jun is considered one of the veteran figures in the internet world. However, he entered the industry through software development and is now building his mobile internet empire via Xiaomi, encompassing systems, software, hardware, and content.In addition to occupying people's mobile and living room time through Xiaomi, Lei Jun has penetrated various core segments of the mobile internet through investments, including payments, browsers, games, online education, digital content, and online healthcare.Wang Xiaochuan, CEO of SogouThe intelligence of Sogou Input Method largely depends on crowdsourced collaborative computing, and Sogou Number Pass is also an application of collaborative computing. Other key products of Sogou, such as Sogou Voice Assistant and Sogou Search, have the potential to utilize collaborative computing for intelligence. Wang Xiaochuan, leading Sogou with its input method, browser, search engine, and mobile app cluster, has already secured a ticket to the mobile internet era.Notably, Wang Xiaochuan is only 35 years old. Time is money, and Wang Xiaochuan has immense potential to become an internet mogul.Liu Qiangdong, founder of JD.comE-commerce has moved beyond the stage where price was king. Whoever can bring added value to users, such as reliable quality, smooth experience, fast delivery, and worry-free after-sales service, will have the opportunity to dominate the e-commerce market. Currently, JD.com has significant opportunities.At the age of 39, Liu Qiangdong already possesses absolute capability. Regardless of JD.com's future outcome, he is now undoubtedly a heavyweight in the e-commerce world.Yu Yongfu, CEO of UCWebUC Browser has amassed 400 million users globally, which offers immense imaginative space if placed in the traditional internet context. Although the role of mobile browsers as entry points is limited, UC Browser and 9You still generate hundreds of millions in revenue annually within the mobile internet space.If UC bets on WEBAPP, HTML5, and browsers and succeeds, Yu Yongfu could secure his fourth chair and naturally become one of the next-generation internet moguls.Zhou Hongyi, Chairman of Qihoo 360Zhou Hongyi is considered an internet elder. He worked at Yahoo! China early in his career, developed YiSou, then founded Qihoo, and later became successful with 360 through security. Zhou is known for his restless style. Now, he is aggressively attacking in areas such as security, search, browsers, and the APP market, fully unleashing the power of 360 security.Wang Xing, founder of MeituanThe appearance of Groupon in China sparked a thousand-deals war, among which one was called Meituan. After the dust settled from the group-buying frenzy, Meituan survived.Wang Xing is an entrepreneurial player, from Xiaonei to Fanfou. Although it seemed there were many opportunities to either disrupt giants or become one himself, each venture ended rather dismally.Zhang Xiaolong, Vice President of TencentZhang Xiaolong, Vice President of Tencent Corporation, developed the domestic email client Foxmail and later WeChat after joining Tencent, earning him the title "Father of WeChat" and recognition by the Wall Street Journal as one of China's most innovative figures in 2012.Zhang Tao, CEO of DianpingSocial + Local + Mobile (SoLoMo) has been a hotly discussed concept since 2011. This is not a futuristic concept, as Dianping has already proven it. Founder Zhang Tao, a graduate of a prestigious university, returned to China in 2003 to start his business. Riding the O2O wave, he is set to become one of the next-generation leaders.Jury MembersShi An: Founder of Gutan TechnologyChen Zhong: Founder of Bianews, Marketing Director of JZ Crystal Hotel GroupJury OpinionsMost Promising Next-Generation LeaderShi An: Lei Jun, CEO of Xiaomi TechnologyActually, Liu Qiangdong and Hongyi are also very likely candidates for the next generation of leaders. Like Lei Jun, they have followed the trends, riding the wave of the internet changing people's lives, representing a new force. Because they are closer to the mobile internet, I cast my vote for Lei Jun.Chen Zhong: Liu Qiangdong, founder of JD.comThe rags-to-riches story of the Zhongguancun street vendor, although still in fierce competition, being able to carve out a B2C path under the watchful eyes of the tightly-knit Alibaba Group's e-commerce empire, proves that there are no unbeatable opponents. With the massive scale of e-commerce and Liu Qiangdong's distinct personal charm, becoming the next leader is just around the corner.
