What should pregnant women do to treat psoriasis?

by shenqiang0 on 2012-03-04 10:35:43

& nbsp; What should pregnant women do for the treatment of psoriasis? Being pregnant is supposed to be a joyful experience, but pregnant women suffering from psoriasis are often distressed. As a special group of psoriasis sufferers, what should pregnant women do for the treatment of psoriasis? Experts from Shanxi Huimin Hospital introduce related knowledge below.

1. Pay attention to adjusting your mindset

It is well known that depression can be an important factor in triggering and worsening psoriasis. Many pregnant women with psoriasis worry about whether their condition will be passed on to their child. Here we must clearly inform everyone that clinical data shows that although the hereditary chance of psoriasis is around 30%, most of these cases are only latent inheritance. As long as various triggering factors of psoriasis are avoided after the baby's birth, it is basically unlikely that they will develop psoriasis. Therefore, pregnant women with psoriasis should let go of their concerns, maintain an optimistic attitude, which is beneficial not only for treating psoriasis but also for the fetus.

2. Adhere to treatment principles

For pregnant women treating psoriasis, normally during pregnancy, no medication should be used. Especially hormones and antibiotics, which are very harmful to both the mother and fetus, leading to fetal malformations, intellectual disabilities, etc. Therefore, during pregnancy, one should avoid abusing medication. In life-threatening situations where medication is necessary, the priority should be saving the mother's life, and once the emergency has passed, the dosage should be quickly reduced or discontinued. If pregnant women with psoriasis feel particularly itchy, some Chinese herbal baths with fewer side effects can be used to relieve itching, controlling the condition of psoriasis first, then proceeding with comprehensive treatment after the baby's birth.

3. Pay attention to dietary care

Shanxi Huimin Hospital suggests that pregnant women, as a special group, need comprehensive nutritional supplementation, but as psoriasis patients, there are many dietary restrictions. This dual identity often leaves many pregnant women with psoriasis unsure of what to do. At this time, mainly spicy and fishy foods should be avoided, while other foods can be consumed in moderation to ensure balanced nutrition. During pregnancy, supplements are usually bought to nourish the body, but it's important to avoid hot-natured tonics to prevent worsening the condition, which is very beneficial for treating psoriasis.
