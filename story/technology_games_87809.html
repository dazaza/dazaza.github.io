Giant's net profit in the second quarter was 51.1 million US dollars - Age1983

by hexiaoyao on 2008-08-28 15:29:16

Shi Yuzhu can take a sigh of relief again... Gaint Interactive Entertainment is really too profitable...

At 4:30 p.m. on August 27 in the U.S. Eastern Time (or at 4:30 a.m. on August 28, Beijing Time), Giant Interactive Group Ltd. (NYSE:GA) announced its unaudited financial report for the second quarter ended June 30, 2008. The details are as follows:

Key Financial Highlights for the Second Quarter of 2008:

-- Net revenues reached RMB504.8 million (approximately USD73.6 million), representing an increase of 36.3% year-over-year and 7.0% quarter-over-quarter.

-- Gross profit was RMB447.0 million (approximately USD65.2 million), representing an increase of 34.8% year-over-year and 6.2% quarter-over-quarter; gross margin was 88.6% for the second quarter of 2008.

-- Net income was RMB350.6 million (approximately USD51.1 million), representing an increase of 32.7% year-over-year and 3.5% quarter-over-quarter; net margin was 69.5% for the second quarter of 2008.

-- Basic and diluted earnings per ADS were RMB1.45 (approximately USD0.21) and RMB1.40 (approximately USD0.20), respectively, compared to basic and diluted earnings per ADS of RMB1.32 and RMB1.32, respectively, for the same period last year, and basic and diluted earnings per ADS of RMB1.38 and RMB1.33, respectively, for the first quarter of 2008. Each ADS represents one Class A ordinary share of the Company.

-- Non-GAAP net income that excludes share-based compensation expenses was RMB362.5 million (approximately USD52.8 million), representing an increase of 34.9% year-over-year and 3.3% quarter-over-quarter; non-GAAP net margin was 71.8% for the second quarter of 2008.

-- Non-GAAP basic and diluted earnings per ADS that exclude share-based compensation expenses were RMB1.50 (approximately USD0.22) and RMB1.45 (approximately USD0.21), respectively, compared to non-GAAP basic and diluted earnings per ADS of RMB1.44 and RMB1.44, respectively, for the same period last year, and non-GAAP basic and diluted earnings per ADS of RMB1.43 and RMB1.38, respectively, for the first quarter of 2008.

-- Average Active Paying Accounts ("APA") increased by 41% year-over-year and 21.7% quarter-over-quarter to approximately 1.76 million.

-- Average Revenue Per User ("ARPU") was RMB285.9, representing a decrease of 3.2% year-over-year and 12.1% quarter-over-quarter.

-- Average Concurrent Users ("ACU") was approximately 620,000, representing an increase of 20.3% year-over-year and 13.4% quarter-over-quarter.

-- Peak Concurrent Users ("PCU") was approximately 2.34 million, representing an increase of 118.1% year-over-year and 42.1% quarter-over-quarter.

The online games mainly include "Zhengtu", "Zhengtu Retro Edition" and "Giant".

In this respect, Shi Yuzhu, Chairman and CEO of Giant Interactive Entertainment, stated that: "During the second quarter of this year, our company made remarkable progress in the second quarter. The year-on-year growth rates of net revenue, gross profit and net income all exceeded 30%. We continued to expand our business while still emphasizing operational efficiency and maintaining the growth rate of interest. In terms of operations, our flagship game "Zhengtu" maintained a good growth trend, and the highest number of players simultaneously online reached 2.1 million on April 26, 2008, creating the highest record in the industry at that time. During the second quarter of this year, the year-on-year growth rate of activated paying accounts was 41%, and the average number of players simultaneously online also increased by 20.3% year-on-year. These data once again prove that our strategy of focusing on the development and marketing of high-quality games has been very successful. Looking ahead, we believe that this strategy will continue to drive our business development."

Shi Yuzhu further stated that: "Recently, we invested USD51 million in 51.COM, the largest independent social networking service provider in China, acquiring 25% of its shares. We believe that its huge user resources and rapidly developing social networking business can well promote our games and help attract more player groups and enhance players' attention to the games. In addition, we recently announced a stock repurchase plan with a total value of USD150 million. This move not only reiterates our confidence and optimism about the future potential of Giant Interactive Entertainment, but also reflects our consistent commitment to enhancing shareholder value."

Details of the Financial Report:

For the second quarter of 2008, the net revenue of Giant Interactive Entertainment was RMB504.8 million (approximately USD73.6 million), increasing by 7.0% compared to RMB471.6 million in the previous quarter, and increasing by 36.3% compared to RMB370.2 million in the same period last year.

For the second quarter of 2008, the net revenue from online games of Giant Interactive Entertainment was RMB503.3 million (approximately USD73.4 million), increasing by 7.0% compared to RMB470.2 million in the previous quarter, and increasing by 36.6% compared to RMB368.4 million in the same period last year. The quarter-over-quarter growth in net revenue from online games was mainly due to the increase in the number of users in the player group of "Zhengtu" and the good development of "Giant".

For the second quarter of 2008, the average number of concurrent users reached 620,000, increasing by 13.4% quarter-over-quarter and 20.3% year-over-year; the peak number of concurrent users in online games during the second quarter of 2008 was 2.34 million, increasing by 42.1% quarter-over-quarter and 118.1% year-over-year. The year-over-year growth was mainly due to the success of attracting new players through in-game marketing activities in "Zhengtu" and "Giant", enhancing player interaction, and improving the cohesion of the game community. The number of activated paying accounts in online games during the second quarter of 2008 was 1.76 million, increasing by 21.7% quarter-over-quarter and 41.0% year-over-year. The significant year-over-year growth in the number of activated paying accounts was mainly due to the overall scale expansion of the game itself and the player group; the quarter-over-quarter growth in the number of activated paying accounts was mainly due to the rapid growth of the average number of concurrent users and the peak number of concurrent users since the first quarter of 2008, which brought more paying accounts to the game.

For the second quarter of 2008, the average revenue per user in online games of Giant Interactive Entertainment was RMB285.9, decreasing by 12.1% quarter-over-quarter and 3.2% year-over-year. The year-over-year and quarter-over-quarter decreases were mainly due to the faster growth rate of the number of activated paying accounts compared to the growth rate of revenue. Giant believed that the strong growth in the number of activated paying accounts was an important factor for its long-term stable development.

For the second quarter of 2008, the service cost of Giant Interactive Entertainment was RMB57.8 million (approximately USD8.4 million), increasing by 49.6% year-over-year and 13.7% quarter-over-quarter. The quarter-over-quarter increase was mainly due to the increase of RMB2 million in server hosting fees and RMB0.8 million in depreciation and amortization expenses; at the same time, in order to provide better services to players and offer a high-quality gaming experience, we continuously strengthened our hardware facilities and their carrying capacity to maintain the stable growth of the average number of concurrent users and the peak number of concurrent users. In addition, we further consolidated and expanded our game operation team, resulting in an increase of RMB2.9 million in employee compensation expenses, which were the main reasons for the quarter-over-quarter increase. The year-over-year increase in service costs was mainly due to the support for rapid business expansion (including purchasing more servers and hiring game operation personnel).

For the second quarter of 2008, the gross profit of Giant Interactive Entertainment was RMB447.0 million (approximately USD65.2 million), increasing by RMB26.2 million quarter-over-quarter and RMB115.4 million year-over-year. The gross margin for the second quarter of 2008 was 88.6%, slightly decreasing from 89.6% in the same period last year and 0.6% from the previous quarter. The slight quarter-over-quarter decrease in gross margin was mainly due to the increase in service costs.

For the second quarter of 2008, the total operating expenses of Giant Interactive Entertainment were RMB118.8 million (approximately USD17.3 million), increasing by 19.0% compared to RMB99.8 million in the previous quarter, and increasing by 75.0% compared to RMB67.9 million in the same period last year. The quarter-over-quarter increase in operating expenses was mainly due to the increase in sales and marketing expenses and research and development expenses; the year-over-year increase was mainly due to the company's continuous investment in research and development, sales and marketing infrastructure to promote business development.

For the second quarter of 2008, the product research and development expenses of Giant Interactive Entertainment were RMB19.9 million (approximately USD2.9 million), increasing by 68.1% compared to RMB11.8 million in the previous quarter, and increasing by 502.8% compared to RMB3.3 million in the same period last year. The significant quarter-over-quarter increase was mainly due to the increase in employee compensation expenses to retain current talents in the company and hire more game designers with great potential in the game industry. In addition, the company rented a new building in the Shanghai headquarters for the research and development department, leading to an increase in rental fees, which was also one of the reasons. The year-over-year increase in product research and development expenses was due to the hiring of more senior game designers and engineers to further strengthen research and development capabilities, enrich game design, and generate innovative game concepts.

For the second quarter of 2008, the sales and marketing expenses of Giant Interactive Entertainment were RMB83.6 million (approximately USD12.2 million), increasing by 28.9% compared to RMB64.9 million in the previous quarter, and increasing by 88.3% compared to RMB44.4 million in the same period last year. The significant increase in these expenses was mainly due to the nationwide launch of the public beta version of the new game "Giant" and the promotion of the penetration of "Zhengtu" (including advertising, poster production, free gifts, and recruitment of temporary marketing personnel).

For the second quarter of 2008, the general and administrative expenses of Giant Interactive Entertainment were RMB30.3 million (approximately USD4.4 million), increasing by 7.9% compared to RMB28.1 million in the previous quarter, and increasing by 50.1% compared to RMB20.2 million in the same period last year. The quarter-over-quarter increase in these expenses was mainly due to the increase in professional consulting fees to further improve management efficiency; the year-over-year increase was mainly due to the expansion and improvement of functions in the management department.

For the second quarter of 2008, Giant Interactive Entertainment received government fiscal subsidy income of RMB15 million, which represented a refund of business tax by the municipal government.

For the second quarter of 2008, the interest income of Giant Interactive Entertainment was RMB43.4 million (approximately USD6.3 million), slightly decreasing compared to RMB53.8 million in the previous quarter. This decrease was mainly due to the decline in interest rates and the reduction in cash and cash equivalents caused by the payment of previously announced dividends totaling USD81.4 million.

For the second quarter of 2008, the income tax expense of Giant Interactive Entertainment was RMB17.5 million (approximately USD2.5 million), significantly decreasing compared to the previous quarter, mainly due to the increase in deferred tax assets.

For the second quarter of 2008, the net income of Giant Interactive Entertainment was RMB350.6 million (approximately USD51.1 million), increasing by 32.7% compared to RMB264.2 million in the same period last year, and increasing by 3.5% compared to RMB338.7 million in the previous quarter. The net margin for the second quarter of 2008 was 69.5%, decreasing compared to 71.4% in the same period last year and 71.8% in the previous quarter, mainly due to the increase in operating expenses.

As of June 30, 2008, the total value of cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments held by Giant Interactive Entertainment was RMB5.7296 billion, compared to RMB6.0455 billion as of March 31, 2008. The quarter-over-quarter decrease was mainly due to the payment of previously announced dividends totaling USD81.4 million in the second quarter of 2008. These dividends were disclosed in our prospectus for the initial public offering and in the annual report for 2007.

Outlook for the Third Quarter of 2008:

In July this year, we adjusted the revenue model of "Zhengtu" by reducing the number of chargeable items. Although this adjustment had a short-term impact on revenue, we believe it will play an important role in promoting the continuous growth of the number of players and activated paying users in "Zhengtu" in the future. So far, the players inside "Zhengtu" generally hold a positive attitude towards this adjustment in the revenue model. To implement our market segmentation strategy in conjunction with this adjustment in the revenue model, we launched the internal test of the retro edition of "Zhengtu" at the end of July. The market response to the retro edition of "Zhengtu" has exceeded our expectations, and we expect to conduct the public test of this game in late September. Given that the third quarter is in the transition period of adjustment, we currently still find it difficult to make precise expectations regarding the financial impact, therefore, we will not provide the revenue expectation target for the third quarter of 2008.
