Uniqlo China Official Website

by wgyuvs8a on 2012-03-07 19:50:55

For the big Taobao strategy, Taobao's favorite case study is none other than Uniqlo. This marks the beginning of Taobao's promotion of cloud computing and its transformation into an e-commerce infrastructure service provider. As an international affordable fashion leisure brand, Japan's Fast Retailing Co., Ltd.'s Uniqlo ventured into e-commerce in the Chinese market in April this year, simultaneously launching its official shopping website in China and its Taobao flagship store. Currently, Uniqlo's daily online transaction volume is approximately 300,000 yuan, equivalent to the single-day sales of its best-performing physical store offline.

Although the product displays and design styles of Uniqlo's official Chinese website and Taobao flagship store are different, their backend data, search, transactions, payment functions, etc., are all unified and interconnected. They both adopt the underlying architecture and technical support provided by Taobao. Uniqlo, on the other hand, focuses on brand promotion and product control, including product pricing, design, annual goods planning, promotions, and other businesses.

It seems that the cooperation between Taobao and Uniqlo has solved the IT shortcomings of traditional consumer goods enterprises when conducting e-commerce while also bringing a new profit growth point to Taobao. However, is the "two faces, one backend" e-commerce model being practiced by Uniqlo applicable to most traditional consumer goods enterprises in China?

This requires an investigation into why Uniqlo implements e-commerce and why it adopts Taobao's infrastructure services. "Uniqlo implements e-commerce in China because there are currently fewer offline physical stores, making it difficult to cover a large number of secondary cities and remote areas in China. We hope to use the Internet to radiate Uniqlo's products and brands nationwide," introduced an Uniqlo e-commerce staff member. Currently, Uniqlo has opened 35 stores in 10 cities across the country. Relying solely on the expansion of physical channels makes it difficult to spread brand influence and products nationwide, and the Internet is undoubtedly an ideal platform for crossing geographical boundaries.

In addition, Uniqlo's e-commerce platform in Japan has been very successful, providing a wealth of experience and support for its trial of online sales in China. That is to say, compared to many traditional enterprises in China that have just started online sales, Uniqlo's e-commerce experience did not start from scratch. Facing China's vast online shopping market, Uniqlo aims to understand the online shopping habits and behavior patterns of Chinese consumers through e-commerce.

"Using the power of third-party Internet trading platforms to open stores is because we are relatively cautious about initial investments and hope that each step will have predictable profits," said the staff member. For any overseas market's Internet sector, Uniqlo is very careful. In mid-September, Uniqlo logged onto Rakuten, a large third-party shopping site in South Korea, also based on considerations of initial costs and risks.

Even so, Uniqlo's initial investment in trying out e-commerce in China was close to 10 million yuan. Outsourcing all outsourcing functions and mastering the core capabilities they excel at is Uniqlo's e-commerce strategy. Even though daily online transactions can exceed 5,000 orders, Uniqlo's e-commerce team in China consists of only four people, including a Japanese project manager and three Chinese staff members. The latter is responsible for maintaining and updating the shopping website, interacting with outsourcing companies, controlling products, promoting brands, and handling promotions, among other tasks.

In addition to outsourcing the underlying technical architecture of Internet transactions to Taobao, Uniqlo outsources customer service in the e-commerce operation level to a Chinese company. They undertake the functions of Taobao Wangwang, call center, Email, customer relationship management, etc., with a team of more than 30 people serving Uniqlo; warehousing logistics are outsourced to a company that provides dedicated warehousing and logistics support for Uniqlo's e-commerce project; express delivery is outsourced to EMS and ZJS (Zhaijisong).

After outsourcing the technical architecture and backend supply chain operations of e-commerce, what are the core functions that Uniqlo controls? "Control over products is Uniqlo's strength." This is the key point repeatedly emphasized by Uniqlo's staff. Uniqlo's product control includes multiple factors such as brand and market promotion, promotions, annual product plans, quality, etc. Whether online or offline, Uniqlo's marketing strategies are almost consistent.

"Uniqlo does not have out-of-season products." Uniqlo's staff stated. Uniqlo's philosophy is to introduce products ahead of the season and also discount them early, rather than wait until the seasonal discounts to conduct large-scale clearance sales. "Even if it's a 10% discount, the products must be sold out."

However, Uniqlo's discounting is carried out in a covert manner, perhaps concentrating certain products in a particular store for promotion at the Uniqlo flagship store, or gradually reducing prices for promotions. Offline, Uniqlo conducts two product promotions each month, with two types of discount methods: the promotion of main products and the early clearance of inventory.

For main products, Uniqlo has a famous saying: "If a popular product sells out in a day, it is a failure." This is because popular products always attract foot traffic and drive the sale of other products. Therefore, Uniqlo always controls the rhythm of introducing main products through analysis of the market and consumers.

The concept of product control is equally evident online. In Uniqlo's online store, promotions take place every Friday, and new products are launched half a month earlier than in physical channels. Consumer behavior data from the Internet helps Uniqlo decide how new products perform better in physical channels.

Unlike some traditional companies that use Taobao as a channel for clearing inventory and discounted products, Uniqlo uses Taobao and its official website as channels for launching new products and normal sales. Since Taobao's current system has not fully supported clothing categories and colors, Uniqlo's online store displays more than 700 varieties of products, which is fewer categories compared to offline stores.

In fact, Uniqlo's rapid development in online sales in the Chinese market owes more to its offline efforts, thanks to the company's business strategy and operational philosophy. The rapid growth of e-commerce benefits from its superior product control capabilities, which are currently lacking in many Chinese consumer goods enterprises. This is one of the reasons why Uniqlo's e-commerce model is difficult to simply replicate. In an environment of abundant technical supply and warehousing logistics supply, finding modular outsourcing providers for e-commerce is not difficult, but the real understanding of commerce and backend operational capabilities are what Taobao currently cannot provide to traditional enterprises.

Taobao has brought Uniqlo's online sales a thriving popularity, and its complete transaction tools and transaction processes in e-commerce basic services have saved Uniqlo a great deal of time and cost in building its own website. However, Uniqlo also has some concerns regarding its cooperation with Taobao. From the launch in April until July, Uniqlo's e-commerce team tried all the promotional methods Taobao could offer, and by the end of July, it began to transition.

Uniqlo's e-commerce team believes that Taobao consumers are very sensitive to price and prefer cheaper products. Uniqlo's global product strategy is affordable fashion, with limited profit margins, making it difficult to sell products at lower prices online. Except for some discounted products, more than 90% of Uniqlo's online store products are priced the same as offline, with similar average order values around 200 to 250 yuan. In China, some brands' new products can typically be sold online at 30% to 40% off, but Uniqlo finds it hard to do so.

To solve such dilemmas, Chinese branded enterprises usually adopt the solution of placing different products on their official websites and Taobao flagship stores, positioning the two websites differently. For instance, Aimer Underwear presents new products and higher-priced items on its official website, while its Taobao store sells discounted products. However, Uniqlo's official website and Taobao store have the same products. Although the two online stores have different displays, all products are automatically generated by the same database, and their warehousing logistics, supply chains, product control systems, and inventory management systems all adopt the same business system.
