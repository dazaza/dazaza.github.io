The procedures for buying and selling stocks are as follows: when buying or selling stocks, you must specify whether to buy or sell, the name (or code) of the stock, quantity, and price. Moreover, this order is only valid on the day the order is placed. The content of the commission includes the abbreviation (code) of the stock you want to buy or sell, the quantity, and the price of buying or selling the stock. The abbreviation of the stock is usually three to four Chinese characters, and the stock code is six digits. When entrusting to buy or sell, the stock code and abbreviation must be consistent. At the same time, there are certain regulations regarding the quantity of stocks bought or sold: the quantity of stocks commissioned for purchase must be an integer multiple of 100, but the quantity of stocks commissioned for sale does not have to be an integer multiple of 100.

There are four ways to entrust: counter submission entrustment, telephone automatic entrustment, computer automatic entrustment, and remote terminal entrustment.
