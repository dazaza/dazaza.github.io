Zero-score essay from the 2008 college entrance exam

by yang5230 on 2009-12-04 17:16:36

Nanjing 2008 College Entrance Exam Essay Question: Write an essay based on the given material. Students choose their own angle and title to write an article no longer than 800 words, excluding poetry. In class, a young man placed a glass cup, inside which was a large stone almost as big as the cup itself. The teacher asked him, "Is the cup full?" A student answered, "Not yet, we can still add sand." After the student finished adding the sand, the teacher asked again, "Is it full now?" All the students in the class replied that it was full, except for one boy who said it wasn't, as water could still be added. The young man cried, poured out the sand and stones, leaving the cup empty. This time, the teacher filled the cup with sand and water first, then asked, "Is the cup full?" If one wanted to add stones, how would they do it? The boy poured out the sand and water, then placed the stones inside. A certain student's response to the essay question was that the stones must go in first to hold the sand and water; if water were placed first, the cup would fill up, and the stones wouldn't fit anymore. This is akin to saying that if I don't pass the university entrance exam, I'd have to bribe the right people before university admissions are finalized in order to gain admission. If admissions are already full, even the best bribes won't work.

Beijing College Entrance Exam Zero Score Essay

Prompt: "Fine rain moistens clothes unseen, idle flowers fall silently," is a line by Tang Dynasty poet Liu Zhaqing from his poem "Farewell to Yuan Guangshi." Some interpret this line as follows:

1. It celebrates the beautiful autumn scenery.

2. It conveys a hidden loneliness through the imagery of idle flowers and fine rain.

3. Unseen and unheard does not mean inaction but rather a calm way of life.

4. Such an atmosphere is no longer suitable for today's world.

Write an essay based on your interpretation of the poem. Title and genre are self-determined. Word count should exceed 800 words.

Severe winter, pale sun.

At the peak of Jingshan Mountain.

Below, two figures—a woman and a man—can be seen.

These two are the most feared assassins of the era, known as "Fine Rain Idle Flowers"—the woman named Fine Rain, the man Idle Flowers. Poet Liu Zhaqing once described them with the line "Fine rain moistens clothes unseen, idle flowers fall silently." For these assassins, "fine rain" refers to blood-soaked garments, and "idle flowers" refer to severed heads. They kill without a trace, and if they target you, you will be dead before seeing or hearing them.

The story involves a challenge between these two assassins, filled with dramatic twists involving poisons, seduction, and ultimate betrayal, all culminating in a climactic battle where the seemingly weaker opponent prevails using clever tactics.

Hunan Province 2008 College Entrance Exam Zero Score Essay

Prompt: Learning mother tongue, using mother tongue. One can say that every day we feel, learn, and use our mother tongue. Write an essay based on the material provided.

The mother tongue is rich and profound, so we must study and apply it well. Through my research, I discovered that to master the language, one must carefully distinguish unclean expressions. For example: The sun calls the grass. Sun: Hey, Grass, it's me, Night. Grass: What? Who's this? Sun: It's Day! Grass: Huh? Who are you? Sun: It's Day! Just grass! Grass: Who the hell are you? Sun: It's Day! Night! Grass: Grass! At this point, the sun’s mother picks up the phone: Night’s mom here, are you Grass? Grass your mom too!

I detest dirty language immensely, and anyone who swears will be despised by me! Therefore, while studying our mother tongue, we must critically examine such vulgarities or words that might cause misunderstandings!
