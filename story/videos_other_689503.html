Manuscripts become popular as artworks, and the value of literature collection is great.

by zhoukun103 on 2012-03-04 10:15:10

Editor's note: Manuscripts that integrate documentary value, collection value, and artistic value are priced low with few fakes, becoming a new favorite in contemporary art collections. The highest price for a manuscript can reach 700,000 to 800,000 yuan, which is quite expensive.

Recently, an exhibition named "Bie Zhi" (Notes) was unveiled at the Langqiao Space in Chengdu. The exhibition showcased nearly a hundred manuscript works by 16 artists. These "random doodles" and "casual creations" were fixed on furniture such as drawers, cabinets, and mirrors.

Are drafts also considered art? Yes, compared to finished artworks, manuscripts are the gathering of inspiration during the creation process of artists, and they have now become a new favorite in contemporary art collections.

Diverse forms like fragments in dreams

This is a pure manuscript exhibition - showcasing nearly a hundred manuscript works by 16 artists including Chen Liangjie, Chen Nong, Chen Xianhui, Ji Lei, Qin Qing, Tang Ke, Tu Hongtao, and Yin Xiangyang. Some are pen sketches, some are colored pencil quick sketches, and others are small watercolor paintings, all diverse and unrestrained.

More interestingly, the artists fixed these manuscripts, life studies, rough drafts, sketches, random doodles, etc., on common furniture like drawers, cabinets, and mirrors in daily life. Artist Qin Qing has two drafts painted on old cardboard, resembling fragments picked up casually from a dream. Curator Xu Sheng told Tianfu Morning Post reporter: "These fragments are always pinned on his studio workbench, waiting to be combined together, just like what we see in the exhibition hall." Many artists also meticulously recorded their ideas about the works and art itself next to their manuscripts.

Manuscripts are the scenery along the way to the peak

Since its opening, the exhibition has attracted many people to visit. Xu Sheng revealed: "For the artists, the exhibition awakens their creative desires and inspirations, and for ordinary audiences, they see another side of the artists."

Exhibition director Zhao Huan said that although the form of art manuscript exhibitions is not commonly seen domestically, abroad there are already small museums themed around manuscripts. "This exhibition displays the drafts created by artists for their own works. Regardless of the completion or perfection level of these drafts, they themselves were not created as finished works but rather as slices of the artist's preparation process." In her view, although these slices may not be complete, they can vividly show the efforts of different types and creation methods of artists in conceiving, preparing materials, or seeking inspiration. "The artists we invited all left clues worth viewing in their creation process. Their creation methods vary, and so do the forms of their drafts. If oil paintings are the peaks created by artists, then these drafts are the scenery along the way to the peak. Enjoying this scenery along the way sometimes is more interesting than reaching the peak."

Work Interpretation

It can be a small installation

Author: Liu Wei

It consists of four rectangular blocks of different colors, very much like an exercise in color and form. Each rectangular block opens outward in different ways, this simple treatment gives the basic rectangular form ample imaginative space.

It can be stored in the thought drawer

Author: Ji Lei

Ji Lei's works are placed inside drawers, looking very "nostalgic." Xu Sheng introduced that Ji Lei's manuscripts are small, somewhat like portable small cards, more like a self-record of the creative experience. They do not seek emotional expansion, nor deliberately pursue the connection with large-scale works, but are rather introverted - as if always stored in the thought drawer.

It can resemble plant sampling

Author: Tu Hongtao

Xu Sheng introduced, "In Tu Hongtao's recent solo exhibition, those uniquely shaped trees were all drawn based on real-life scenes. When we set up the exhibition, we put his manuscripts into plastic bags, just like plant sampling."

Value Discussion

Manuscripts have become a new favorite in contemporary art collections

Some insiders predict that compared to oils that can cost tens of thousands of yuan, manuscripts that integrate documentary value, collection value, and artistic value are priced lower with fewer counterfeits, making them a new favorite in contemporary art collections.

Single manuscript pieces exhibited are about 2000 yuan each

Currently, art manuscript collections have not yet formed a trend domestically, and manuscripts are mostly mutual gifts among artists. Although the works displayed in this exhibition are not for sale, Zhao Huan estimates that the market price for each piece of this batch of manuscripts is about 2000 yuan.

Artist Tu Hongtao discusses the inspiration stored in manuscripts, saying, "The value of manuscripts has not been fully discovered yet, and the inspiration and fun brought by manuscripts are also significant." But how much market value manuscripts actually possess, Tu Hongtao expressed that he hasn't carefully thought about it, "Manuscripts might give everyone a sense of authenticity, but on the other hand, perhaps some people think it's not a very serious matter and consider the technical aspect not particularly strong."

The highest price for manuscripts reaches 700,000 to 800,000 yuan

Collector Yu Sen revealed that in the past two years' collection market and auction market, there are more deceased artists' manuscripts, which are also highly valued, but the current market for contemporary artists' manuscripts is still relatively scarce. Yu Sen said that he himself and his friends around him also collect some manuscripts. Although they are manuscripts, their values are not insignificant, "As far as I know, the highest value of collected manuscripts is 700,000 to 800,000 yuan."

For Yu Sen, manuscripts are works, and their research value and collection value even surpass finished products. "Many artists create many small works before creating masterpieces, and these small works are manuscripts. They are the artists' true feelings about the philosophical and social levels and what they want to express. Therefore, in a certain sense, these manuscripts have a lot of research value."
