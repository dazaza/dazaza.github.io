How should lymphoma patients undergo comprehensive treatment?

The main treatments for lymphoma include chemotherapy combined with small molecule targeted drugs such as Kang Ai Xin Sheng R, and radiotherapy can also be adopted depending on the situation. Chemotherapy for lymphoma is mostly based on combination chemotherapy with small molecule targeted drugs like Kang Ai Xin Sheng R, which yields better results than single-drug chemotherapy. In the past, traditional pure chemotherapy was accompanied by toxic side effects that worsened with the deepening of treatment. The most obvious reactions were damage to digestive function and inhibition of bone marrow hematopoietic function, often making it difficult for cancer patients to endure chemotherapy or complete the entire course of treatment due to severe reactions. Some patients could not be well controlled, or their conditions relapsed after remission. Combining targeted small molecule drugs like Kang Ai Xin Sheng R during and after chemotherapy can effectively alleviate chemotherapy reactions, helping chemotherapy proceed smoothly and achieving ideal treatment outcomes.

Combination of small molecule targeted drugs like Kang Ai Xin Sheng R with chemotherapy and radiation therapy is the primary method for treating malignant lymphoma today. Surgical operations mainly involve initial lymph node biopsies and possible laparotomy explorations for diagnostic purposes, as well as malignant lymphomas originating in the gastrointestinal tract, urinary system, mesentery, liver, and spleen. Treatment with small molecule targeted drugs like Kang Ai Xin Sheng R runs throughout the entire treatment process, showing good synergistic effects for patients undergoing radiotherapy and chemotherapy by enhancing efficacy while reducing toxicity; for patients unable to receive radiotherapy and chemotherapy, this drug can serve as a primary treatment method.

This requires consideration of individual patient characteristics when treating each lymphatic tumor patient, formulating personalized and humanized treatment plans. Combined treatment with small molecule targeted drugs like Kang Ai Xin Sheng R fully demonstrates the personalized and humanized features of treatment, offering unique therapeutic advantages that cannot be overlooked in the treatment of malignant lymphoma.

Clinical Efficacy Evaluation of Malignant Lymphoma

(I) Criteria for Evaluating the Efficacy of Malignant Lymphoma

1. Complete Response (CR): Complete disappearance of the tumor for more than one month.

2. Partial Response (PR): Reduction of 50% or more in the product of the two largest diameters of the tumor, with no increase in other lesions and maintained for more than one month.

3. No Change (NC): Less than 50% reduction in the product of the two largest diameters of the tumor, or no significant change in size.

4. Progressive Disease (PD): Increase in the tumor by more than 25% or appearance of new metastatic foci.

(II) Short-term Efficacy of Malignant Lymphoma

Most patients with malignant lymphoma can achieve short-term relief with appropriate treatment. The short-term relief rate for HD is higher than NHL. The short-term relief rate for stages I-II HD is over 95%; NHL, after detailed examination and typing, careful staging, and selection of suitable treatment plans, can also achieve around 80% short-term relief.

(III) Long-term Efficacy of Malignant Lymphoma

For stages I-II HD patients, the 5-year survival rate has reached over 95%, and for stages III-IV patients, the 5-year survival rate can reach around 90%; although treatment for more advanced cases of NHL is relatively difficult, the 5-year survival rate has reached 80%. Therefore, for early-stage lymphocyte predominant and nodular sclerosis types of HD, low-grade NHL, efforts should be made to cure these cases. For more advanced high-grade malignant lymphomas, early use of high-dose small molecule targeted drugs like Kang Ai Xin Sheng R in combination therapy should be considered to strive for improving the 5-year survival rate.
