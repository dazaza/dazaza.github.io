Slam Dunk! Heat Captain Stuns the Crowd, LeBron Can't Hold Back and Starts Dancing

February 22, 2012 - The Miami Heat (Weibo) faced a weaker opponent, the Sacramento Kings, at home. Ultimately, the team won the game with ease, scoring 120-108, securing their seventh consecutive victory. The Heat's substitute forward, Udonis Haslem, played for 25 minutes, making 4 out of 6 shots, scoring 10 points and grabbing 7 rebounds, 4 of which were offensive rebounds. In this game, Haslem surpassed Ronny Seckle to become the second all-time leading rebounder in Heat history.

With four minutes left in the first quarter, Haslem entered the game but struggled to adjust his state promptly, committing one turnover. The Heat ended the first quarter trailing by five points. In the second quarter, Haslem remained on the court. Three minutes into the quarter, he successfully made a layup and then hit two free throws, helping the Heat take the lead.

In the second half, after switching sides, the Kings narrowed the score to 85-87 through inside attacks and fast-break layups. It was Haslem who stepped up again, hitting two mid-range jump shots for 4 consecutive points. He also grabbed an offensive rebound, creating a second-chance opportunity for the Heat. After Norris Cole (Weibo) hit a jump shot, the Heat's lead expanded back to eight points.

At the start of the final quarter, LeBron James and Dwyane Wade (Weibo) rested on the bench while Chris Bosh led a group of substitutes on the court. At the 6:33 mark of the fourth quarter, Mike Miller missed a three-point attempt. Haslem darted from the baseline and slammed the ball home with a single hand. It had been a long time since Haslem executed such a powerful dunk. Since recovering from injury last year, Haslem has mostly focused on perimeter shooting. Although his rebounding is still aggressive, he rarely jumps as high as before. This particular dunk, although not comparable in height to the young talents in the Kings' frontcourt, exuded a commanding presence reminiscent of the 2006 era. Watching this play, James couldn’t contain himself on the bench, disregarding past criticisms about dancing courtside during Cavaliers games. LeBron mimicked a traveling call gesture and started wiggling his hips in celebration.

During this period, the Heat quickly widened their lead to 11 points, thanks to the selfless teamwork, active hustle, and impenetrable defense displayed by Haslem and the other substitutes. Such performances allowed both James and Wade to rest comfortably for nearly half a quarter.

Notably, after grabbing seven rebounds in this game, Haslem’s total rebound count rose to second place in Heat franchise history, only behind the legendary Alonzo Mourning. However, like Jeremy Lin (Weibo), Haslem was undrafted in the NBA draft. Today, he has become the team captain, holding absolute authority in the locker room and serving as the team's spiritual leader on the court. Moreover, he has etched his name into the team's history. Haslem is the Heat's own underdog legend.
