Synopsis:

In an ordinary lane in Shanghai, there lived an ordinary little girl named Wang Qiya (played by Sammi Cheng). She had a life as happy as anyone else's until she met photographer Mr. Cheng (played by Tony Leung Ka-fai), after which everything changed. From being a naive student, she gradually transformed into a graceful and elegant beauty, effortlessly winning the third place in the "Miss Shanghai" beauty pageant just by posing for a photo. On the surface, Mr. Cheng was always helping Wang Qiya, but in reality, he secretly admired her. However, Mr. Cheng also knew clearly that this young lady was about to spread her wings and soar high.

In 1948, Director Li (played by Hu Jun), who held great military and political power, fell deeply in love with Wang Qiya without hesitation, taking her to the Bailam Dance Hall filled with soft music, accompanying Director Li through gunfire and bullets. In those days, women were willing to believe that their first man would be their last, and Wang Qiya was no exception. She daydreamed about spending her entire life with Director Li.

However, history never ceases to evolve. It seems that parting is destined to occur at the sweetest moments of love, especially in an era when the sky was about to change. Director Li never reappeared, only entrusting Mr. Cheng to deliver a message to Wang Qiya. Then, the fate of the new era arrived, giving every Chinese person a new life.

Looking back now, from 1949 to 1956 might have been the most romantic years of New China. The common people wholeheartedly devoted themselves to national construction, responding to calls for thrift and building a harmonious society. After handing over her house, Wang Qiya thought she would spend the rest of her life peacefully until the appearance of rich heir Kang Mingsun (played by Aaron Kwok).

Of course, historical turning points are not divided by normal years, and greater changes came later. Starting from 1958, real changes began to spread, making it impossible for Shanghai to stay the same. Good classmate Jiang Lili (played by Su Yan) had already left, and now it was Kang Mingsun's turn. They all went to a small island in the south. It was said to be a backward and chaotic small city called Hong Kong.

Kang Mingsun chose to leave, while Wang Qiya chose to stay behind and was pregnant with his child. She used her savings to exchange for a legitimate marriage, marrying a terminally ill person, with only the camera capturing that helpless gaze.

In 1968, Wang Qiya had the opportunity to take a step further with the divorced Mr. Cheng, but during the Cultural Revolution, personal aspirations couldn't make much of a difference. Mr. Cheng was going to a small town in Yunnan to support the frontier. The two had unknowingly spent a long time together, and this temporary farewell should soon turn into another quick reunion.

This farewell, however, lasted eight years. Wang Qiya's daughter (played by Michelle Ye) had grown up and made the choice that Wang Qiya didn't make before: to fly away. Perhaps it was destined for them to meet again, and Mr. Cheng introduced a boy named Laokela (played by Huang Jue), who was around the same age as Wang Qiya's daughter, to her. But what Mr. Cheng never expected was that Wang Qiya would fall into a shocking intergenerational romance due to Laokela's impulsive affection.

But all the people she loved ended up making the same choice: to leave. This young man of the 1980s told Wang Qiya that he was going abroad, and no one would remain loyal to their city like she did. She experienced prosperity and loneliness in Shanghai, eventually fading into ashes, while today, Shanghai seems to be regaining its former charm.
