The Inspirational Fable "The Frog Who Sought a Great Pond"

by diglog on 2007-05-15 18:08:48

Recently, the simplified Chinese version of "Ping, a Frog in Search of a New Pond" made its first appearance on the market and has been widely concerned by readers. This is a simple but profound workplace fable about how a dissatisfied frog named Ping overcomes obstacles to find a larger pond.

The frog is a metaphor. In fact, we can all be considered as frogs like Ping, and the "big pond" represents our dream wealth, career, health, academics, or spiritual home. We each have a big pond in our hearts. The same goes for a company. We all need to keep striving, surpass ourselves, and seek out the ideal big pond.

Just like the protagonist frog Ping in "A Frog in Search of a Big Pond", whose English name "Ping" means "seek" or "find" in computer language, each of us is looking for our own dreams. When your dream grows bigger, so does your ideal pond. "A Frog in Search of a Big Pond" precisely tells you how to find the big pond in your heart!

This book has continuously been listed on the New York Times bestseller list, translated into more than 20 languages, ranked among the top 10 bestsellers in South Korea and Japan in 2006, and sold more than 100,000 copies within two months of publication in South Korea. It was also recommended by the American Small Business Association as a must-read for corporate employees. Job hunting, employment, job hopping, and development are hot topics of concern for young people and society in recent years, and "A Frog in Search of a Big Pond" serves as a mental guide for young people's career development. The simplified Chinese version has been published by Wanjun Publishing Company and distributed nationwide by Liaoning Bertelsmann Bookstore Co., Ltd. Google Global Vice President Kai-Fu Lee, Zhaopin CEO Hao Liu, and many other career experts highly recommend it.
