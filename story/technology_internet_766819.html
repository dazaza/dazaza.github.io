The Unbreakable Grey Interest Chain Behind iPhone's "China Premiere"

by anonymous on 2013-10-01 21:55:05

At 0:00 a.m. on September 11 Beijing time, when Apple announced the launch list of the new iPhone 5s/5c, everyone was excited to see mainland China on the list for the first time. The most beautiful imagination at that time was that we would no longer be exploited by scalpers and price hikes, and could enjoy the iPhone "feast" in the first time. But when it was sold a week later, most people were immediately stunned - unable to order on the official website, scalpers everywhere, less stock for operators, and "Tuhao Gold" being hyped up to over ten thousand yuan. This situation seemed no different from before. So, is the gray interest chain still there behind this global synchronized launch?

Behind-the-scenes big boss, control of iPhone stock as the first trick

Xiao Wu never thought his job was very important. Sitting in the bustling Huaqiangbei area in Shenzhen, he earns even less than a porter. The most boring thing is to update the quotations of hundreds of mobile phones every day without holidays, over and over again. He didn't know what this quotation table was for. The manager just threw the copied prices to him, and he updated them according to the prices on the note.

However, rare five-digit numbers appeared on the note these days. "That was the second day after the release of the iPhone 5s. The price I updated reached 11,000 yuan, and even once exceeded 12,000 yuan. Although it was very expensive, this situation appears every year," said Xiao Wu.

In fact, the tedious work complained by Xiao Wu is precisely the most important link in China's mobile phone black market - daily updates twice of guidance prices. Water cargo/black market dealers across the country have to act according to this benchmark price, which is the so-called "shipping price" in the industry.

On the second day after the release of the gold version of the iPhone 5s, the "shipping price" in Huaqiangbei, Shenzhen, had already reached an astonishing 11,000 yuan. Incredible as it may sound, such a commodity almost reaching the price of luxury goods was still unavailable even if you had cash.

For the big bosses who own the top of the interest chain in Huaqiangbei, controlling the stock is the first priority. In the three days before the launch, they only obtained a few thousand units of the iPhone 5s through their private channels.

And then came "holding back" the stock, especially the "Tuhao Gold" golden version of the iPhone 5s that was going to be hyped up. Only a few relatives or close business partners were given a small amount of stock, and must sell a certain proportion of black and silver versions of the iPhone 5s. The purpose of doing this is, on one hand, to slowly digest the already scarce stock, and on the other hand, to create a tight supply situation and hype up the price.

[In a sense, these behind-the-scenes big bosses possess the ability to call the shots like Wall Street financial tycoons. They decide the stock and price of China's mobile phone underground market, rewrite the rules of the game, and become the real power brokers with the authority to supply the iPhone 5s outside the official channel.]

Upstream distributors, betting on "Tuhao Gold" means owning everything

Before the release of the iPhone 5s, Brother Dong was bullish on "Tuhao Gold". Therefore, when the price dropped to 9,100 yuan on the third day of the sale, he decisively "bought" 50 units, of course, under the rule that he must simultaneously take in dozens of black and silver versions of the iPhone 5s that he did not value highly. "Actually, I expected 80 Tuhao Gold units, but the big boss upstairs wouldn't give me the stock, fearing others might disagree. These 50 units were taken care of due to family relations," Brother Dong said with a lucky smile on his face.

According to the price disclosed by Brother Dong, his "purchase price" was only 8,000 yuan, a price that most people dream of but cannot possibly get. As for the 9,100 yuan price set by the big boss, it was the price he gave to the bottom shops, and even this price required personal connections to "ship the goods."

How about the shipping situation of these 50 "Tuhao Gold" units? When Brother Dong was drinking Kung Fu tea with us, the last 3 units had just been taken away, almost swept away instantly. Thus, in just three days, Brother Dong made a net profit of 55,000 yuan solely from the "distribution" of one type of mobile phone, not counting the sales of those dozens of black and silver versions of the iPhone 5s.

But the danger was that on the morning of the second day after the 50 "Tuhao Gold" units were sold out, Xiao Wu followed instructions to drop the "shipping price" of the big boss to 7,600 yuan. That is to say, if Brother Dong had acted slower, he would have lost at least 20,000 yuan. No wonder he had a lucky smile on his face.

[For upstream distributors like Brother Dong in Zhongguancun, the most important thing is interpersonal relationships and the ability to predict the market. Although their influence on the market and prices is extremely limited, most of them have mastered the channel resources of second- and third-tier cities around Beijing. Therefore, from a certain point of view, it is they who distribute the iPhone 5s to finer-level cities, and it is also they who become the hardest "exploiters" in this gray interest chain.]

Bottom stall owners, earning from the iPhone 5s is less than from counterfeit phones

The most important thing for Yang recently has been to refresh his computer screen to check prices at any time. This is almost what every merchant in Zhongguancun is doing. Taking the gold version of the iPhone 5s as an example, the price changes almost every day. On the second day of the launch, it surged to 13,000 yuan, then fell to 9,500 yuan, and on the third day, it continued to fall below 8,000 yuan. If he hadn't been selling mobile phones in Zhongguancun for many years, he wouldn't have had such strong psychological endurance.

"So we tell some old customers to buy it a few days later, but even so, there are still people asking for stock every day." Yang said while looking at the computer without turning his head, "With such an expensive mobile phone, the price keeps changing every day. Our store dares not stockpile goods, and only go to get goods when someone buys them." The goods Yang referred to are purchased from upstream distributors like Brother Dong.

"In fact, we only make a hundred or two hundred yuan per unit, and have to deduct the cost of film and case, but if you don't sell it, others will. So, make a little money where possible. It's better to sell counterfeit phones anyway."

For small stores like Yang's in Zhongguancun, it can be quiet during regular working hours. Sitting for an afternoon, you may not see anyone come to buy the iPhone 5s, but he has shipped quite a bit these days because the real buyers are mostly regular customers who often buy them as gifts and don't care about the price. And these people usually entrust the shop assistants to send express deliveries, so the busiest time is generally early in the morning when the shop opens.

However, Yang also revealed that for small stall owners like them, the most profitable time is usually before the Spring Festival, as it is the peak season for giving gifts and upgrading phones. "The 'Tuhao Gold' will inevitably fall into a shortage whirlpool again at that time, and the price will not fall but rise instead. Selling for seven or eight thousand yuan again is also possible, just like the iPhone 4s did during the holiday season," Yang analyzed with his own logic.

[For bottom-level stall owners like Yang who lack resources, selling the iPhone 5s every day can only earn them a thousand or two yuan as hard-earned money. They wait for the money earned from "volume" after the price stabilizes. However, Yang also hopes that the tension in the regular channel supply will last longer so that their business will be better.]

Epilogue

An ordinary gold version of the iPhone 5s, through the operation of a gray interest chain, was hyped up to 227% of its original price at its highest point, even causing a situation of high demand but low supply. The Chinese premiere of the iPhone 5s did not cut off this gray interest chain seeking overnight wealth, but instead added weight to their speculation, gambling, and deliberate scheming. When consumers find it difficult to get stock from official channels, it is also the moment when every link in the interest chain makes a fortune.

However, Apple got one thing right: the gold version really made everyone in China eye it greedily.
