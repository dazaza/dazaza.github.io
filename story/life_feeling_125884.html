In general, the situation of Chinese people's love tended to be "calm" last year.

Seven out of ten Chinese believe in love, but most are not satisfied with their emotional life.

In this year, those who were "fairly satisfied" with their sex lives slightly outnumbered those who were "not very satisfied."

Most believe that there can be love outside of marriage, but only three out of ten approve of extramarital affairs.

This was a web survey conducted by New Century Weekly with a certain website. The intention was to conduct a general examination of the state of Chinese people's love in 2008 through the survey. The questionnaire included 46 questions covering three major parts: love, sex, and marriage, basically covering all important aspects of Chinese people's romantic lives. From December of last year to January of this year, over 30,000 people participated in the survey. Due to different suitable respondent groups for each part or even individual questions, within 10 days, we received votes ranging from as few as 6,675 to as many as 33,172 for each question. In general, the situation of Chinese people's love in 2008 tended to be "calm."
