Dongguan plans to build the best tea center in China

by ipqess on 2012-03-07 22:31:25

Tea has a history of thousands of years and has always been highly sought after by people from ancient times to the present day. Recently, Cai Jinhua, the President of Dongguan Tea Culture Promotion Association and Chairman of Cheung Kong Holdings, told reporters that the group is currently in the process of establishing the Tiande Tea Trading Center in Dongguan, with plans to create the largest tea trading platform in the country.

Creating Dongguan's "Tian Exchange" for Tea

Tea is an indispensable part of Chinese life, and Dongguan is also one of the important tea collection markets in Guangdong. Over the past few years, there have been trends of speculating on Pu'er and dark tea, with some tea prices skyrocketing overnight by several multiples.

In Cai Jinhua’s view, who was re-elected as the President of the Dongguan Tea Culture Promotion Association for a second term, speculative tea trading isn't the true essence of tea culture. The planned trading center will combine tea investment with culture, serving as a trading platform for tea enthusiasts and collectors.

It is well known that the tea market in Dongguan occupies almost half of Guangdong's share, while Guangdong itself is one of the most important tea consumption markets nationwide. Cai Jinhua informed reporters that previously, almost everyone in Dongguan was involved in stock trading, but now many families have shifted their focus to collecting tea. He believes that although Dongguan's tea market is vast, it still requires more promotion of tea culture. As a lover of tea culture and the President of the Dongguan Tea Culture Promotion Association, he wishes to contribute his efforts.

"The trading center is currently going through relevant procedures, and it is being built on the foundation of Tiande Teahouse," Cai Jinhua said excitedly when discussing the upcoming trading center. According to Cai Jinhua's plan, the trading center will be developed into China's premier tea cultural base, creating the best venue for tea tasting, discussions, and commerce. Currently, the Tiande Teahouse under Cheung Kong Holdings has already established a tea culture museum, laying the foundation for the cultural aspect of the trading center.

The Tiande Teahouse under Cheung Kong Holdings spans nearly 4000 square meters, selling various branded teas, including aged teas such as Pu'er tea from one or two decades ago, Tieguanyin, aged Yuanbao tea, unknown-year tea paste, tribute rice brick tea, Yi Wu Hong Qing Hao green tea, Tea Horse Road tea, and 63-year-old longevity rice brick tea. Additionally, there are direct sales of over 30 leading national tea brands at factory prices.

The Trading Center Plans to Launch the "Angel Plan"

What deserves more attention is that Cai Jinhua's trading center also plans to launch the "Angel Plan."

Cai Jinhua stated that the target audience for this Angel Plan includes individuals who want to start a business but lack funds, especially recent university graduates. "Joining the Tiande Trading Center only requires tens of thousands of yuan, with relatively low rent. If one day the tea cannot be sold, or if you find another good business opportunity and no longer wish to continue, you can return the unsold goods. In the end, the greatest loss for investors will only be reflected in the rent."

For the franchise stores launched by the Tiande Trading Center, Cai Jinhua highly approves of the "7-11" convenience store model. "Investors only need to take charge of operations, and the trading center will have a professional logistics center for distribution. The tea sold in the franchise stores is divided into high, medium, and low grades, with a wide variety of options, not limited to just one brand of tea like other tea shops."

Unlike other tea trading platforms, Cai Jinhua mentioned that the trading center will break conventions. Similar to major securities company branches and futures company branches, it plans to install a large electronic quotation screen where people can see the latest quotations for black tea, red tea, green tea, oolong tea, etc., understanding market trends from the prices, and then conducting transactions based on the latest prices. "This is akin to Dongguan's 'Tian Exchange,' where prices will fluctuate according to market conditions, making it convenient for tea lovers to buy, sell, and collect."

After the establishment of the tea trading center, it is believed that it will have a significant impact on Dongguan's tea market. On one hand, it can meet the returns expected by investors, and on the other hand, it can provide more cultural enrichment for enthusiasts. In another sense, it is believed that it will play an active role in promoting the development of tea products in Dongguan.
