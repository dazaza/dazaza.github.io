Symptoms of Acute Sinusitis

Acute sinusitis is an acute inflammation of the nasal sinus mucosa, mostly occurring after a cold. The symptoms of acute sinusitis are related to which type of nasal sinus is inflamed. Below are different manifestations when each group of nasal sinuses is inflamed:

Frontal pain, lighter in the morning and heavier in the afternoon. There may also be facial swelling pain or upper molar pain, which is mostly due to maxillary sinusitis.

Morning forehead pain, gradually worsening, lighter in the afternoon, and completely disappearing in the evening, which may be frontal sinusitis. However, most people's symptoms may involve many places, so sometimes it is impossible to determine which nasal sinus is inflamed based on headaches alone.

In addition to headaches, the manifestations of acute sinusitis also include: nasal congestion, purulent nasal discharge, temporary olfactory dysfunction, chills, fever, poor appetite, constipation, general discomfort, etc. Young children may have symptoms such as vomiting, diarrhea, and cough. Purulent nasal discharge can irritate the throat and cause throat discomfort and pharyngitis. Severe acute sinusitis can cause ocular infections, but in recent years, due to the widespread use of antibiotics, complications of acute sinusitis such as orbital infections have become less common.

Chronic Sinusitis Symptoms

Abundant pus: nasal discharge is mostly purulent or mucopurulent, yellow or yellow-green, varying amounts, mostly flowing to the throat, unilateral foul smell is mostly seen in odontogenic maxillary sinusitis.

Nasal congestion: varying degrees of severity, mostly due to nasal mucosal congestion and swelling and increased secretions, nasal congestion often leads to temporary olfactory dysfunction. Nasal polyps can completely block the nasal cavity.

Headache: Chronic suppurative sinusitis generally does not have obvious local pain or headache. If there is a headache, it is usually dull pain or a feeling of heaviness in the head, worse during the day and lighter at night.
