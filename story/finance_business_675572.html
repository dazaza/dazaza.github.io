Mentally ill man sets fire to his home, affecting neighbors - started 5 fires in a year

by mtonyhlg02 on 2012-02-25 10:36:25

Residents of the unit expressed that they had repeatedly approached the police station and community workers to reflect the situation, hoping for a proper arrangement for this patient, but the issue has not been resolved. They hope that this big fire will draw attention from relevant departments so that all residents in the building can live peacefully as soon as possible.

According to Harbin News Network (reporter Zhang Daxing), at around 10:40 yesterday morning, a large fire broke out in room 801, unit 8, courtyard 75 on Shangjiashu Street in Nangang District. The Nangang Fire Station arrived and extinguished the fire. Although the fire was put out and no casualties were caused, the residents upstairs and downstairs have suffered. Residents of the unit reported that this is already the fifth fire in this resident's home this year because it houses a "violent madman."

Harbin News Network reporter Li Pengju took the photo.

"Continuing like this, something serious is bound to happen!" Ms. Liu, a resident of the unit, angrily said that all the residents of the unit are trembling with fear because of living next to such a "violent madman." Not only does he start fires, but he also frequently throws things downstairs, ranging from tables, window frames, and glass to bricks and teacups. Grandpa Li from the middle unit almost got hit once. All the windows in Qu's house have been destroyed by him, leaving only black holes, covered with plastic sheets to block the wind.

Mr. Niu said that a "violent madman" named Qu Zhen Gang (phonetic) lives in this house. He has set his home on fire several times this year, each time bigger than the last. Before the incident, he was warming up his car in the yard when he saw Qu Zhen Gang constantly changing positions downstairs, staring blankly at the windows of his own home. He instinctively felt that Qu was about to cause trouble, so he waited in the yard. Sure enough, after a while, black smoke started coming out of Qu's house, and he quickly called the police. At this point, Qu ran out from the hallway and disappeared in a flash.

In the crowd, the reporter found Qu's mother, Wu Shulan (phonetic). She said her son is 34 years old and developed mental illness before graduating from high school. He was sent to a psychiatric hospital for treatment at that time, but later, due to financial difficulties, they could not afford the cost of hospitalization and had to bring him back home. Later, whenever her son had an episode, not only did he beat her, but he also smashed things and threw them out of the window. This year, he started burning things, and she had no choice but to move out, returning occasionally to check on him.

At around 11:20, on the way to the scene, the reporter saw from Anfa Bridge that the two windows facing Jiaohua Street of the 8th floor residential unit on fire had been burned into black holes, and thick black smoke had melted and scorched the windows of the upper-floor residents. Traffic on Anfa Bridge was difficult due to the fire trucks extinguishing the fire. When the reporter arrived at the scene, it was found that the fire prevention passage of the community was blocked by a shed, preventing the fire truck from entering. Soldiers had to carry water hoses upstairs to extinguish the fire. A thick layer of ice formed on the windows and walls of the residents downstairs due to the firefighting water. At around 11:40, the fire was extinguished.

Subsequently, the reporter learned from the Nangang Fire Station that this is already the fifth time this year that the station came here to extinguish the fire, each time caused by Qu Zhen Gang starting a fire at home.

At the scene, the police officers from Quxian Street Police Station did not accept interviews from reporters and did not indicate how they would handle Qu Zhen Gang. The iron gate of the Chunming Community Office of Quxian Subdistrict Office, where the courtyard is located, was tightly locked, and no staff members could be found on site.
