Never let cigarettes be the trigger of your vitiligo.

by king0r8k on 2010-07-02 10:41:49

We are all familiar with the saying that smoking is harmful to health, but why do people turn a blind eye to such warnings and still indulge in the enjoyment of smoking. While people enjoy the feeling that cigarettes bring, their health also gradually deteriorates with each cigarette. Especially, the World Health Organization (WHO) has recently announced the top ten health killers for humans, among which smoking causes 2.9 million deaths every year, and drinking leads to 1.8 million deaths, and these numbers continue to increase rapidly each year.

And for friends with vitiligo who are enthusiastic about smoking, does smoking have an impact on the white spots on the body? Can patients with vitiligo smoke? For this question, our reporter made a telephone appointment at 010-63620988 with the experts from Beijing Vitiligo Specialty Hospital.

Experts point out that tobacco contains many harmful chemical substances, mainly nicotine, tar, multiple polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons, free radicals, radioactive elements, phenolic substances, carbon monoxide, and various heavy metals (cadmium, nickel, lead, arsenic, etc.). These substances cause great damage to the human body. Nicotine can cause peripheral small artery spasm, leading to blood flow obstruction, inducing microcirculation dysfunction, making cells lack blood and oxygen, thereby aggravating melanocyte damage and triggering vitiligo.

The blood and white spots of vitiligo patients, due to the lack of certain trace metal elements, reduce the activity of tyrosinase in the body, affecting the synthesis metabolism of melanin, thus causing the disease. Therefore, patients should eat more foods rich in copper, zinc, iron, and other metal elements, enhancing the activity of tyrosinase, accelerating the synthesis of melanin, and should quit smoking and limit alcohol consumption, maintaining good dietary habits.

(1) Eat more foods containing tyrosine and minerals, such as beef, rabbit meat, lean pork, animal livers, eggs, milk, radishes, eggplants, bamboo shoots, mushrooms, seaweed, and soy products, peanuts, black sesame seeds, raisins, snails, clams, and other shellfish.

(2) Grind 500 grams of walnut kernels and 300 grams of black sesame seeds into a paste, mix evenly, and store for future use. Take 50 grams each time, add it to 500 milliliters of soy milk, boil, add an appropriate amount of sugar, and consume, once in the morning and once in the evening.

(3) Use 15 grams of peanut kernels, 1.5 grams of safflower, 15 grams of Ligustrum lucidum, and 30 grams of rock sugar. Crush the Ligustrum lucidum, add peanut kernels, safflower, rock sugar, and water, decoct as tea, drink one dose daily, and eat raw peanut kernels.

(4) Avoid eating onions, garlic, fish, shrimp, mutton, bamboo shoots, pickled vegetables, peppers, alcoholic beverages. Do not eat spinach because spinach contains a large amount of oxalic acid, which easily makes the affected area itch.

(5) Eat less food rich in vitamin C. Vitamin C can lower serum copper and serum copper oxidase levels, affect the activity of tyrosinase, and interfere with the synthesis of skin melanin.

Finally, experts remind that vitiligo patients must not use hair dyes or come into contact with other chemicals containing phenol. This is mainly because the phenol inside can destroy and inhibit the formation of melanin, thereby affecting the treatment and recovery of vitiligo. Meanwhile, other components have strong toxicity to multiple systems and organs of the human body, reducing blood oxygen content, lowering immunity, and causing cancer mutations.

From this, we can see that whether or not they have vitiligo, people should try to reduce their intake of cigarettes. And for vitiligo patients, quitting smoking is even more necessary.
