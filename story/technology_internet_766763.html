Are you looking forward to the new Surface?

by anonymous on 2013-10-01 20:59:41

The launch of Surface reflects Microsoft's determination to transform towards a combination of software and hardware. Under the impetus of the tide of the times, the company is willing to let its hardware partners become "disheartened".

However, market reactions later proved that Surface still has much room for improvement. The application shortcomings of Surface RT and the tragic battery life of Surface Pro are all too obvious flaws.

If the first generation of Surface products can be considered as Microsoft's trial, then the second generation product directly relates to the company's prospects on the hardware path.

At 10:30 PM Beijing Time on September 23, Microsoft will unveil the second generation of Surface in New York. Before the event, let's take a look at what kind of product Microsoft might showcase this time.

According to previous leaked information (unconfirmed by the official), the successor to Surface RT is named Surface 2, which runs Windows RT 8.1 with the following configuration: Tegra 4 processor 10.6-inch 1080p screen 4G RAM micro-SD storage expansion 8-hour battery life 680g front and rear cameras Bluetooth 4.0 USB 3.0 Built-in Office Home & Student Edition 2013 RT No support for LTE

Another new product, Surface Pro 2, has the following configuration: Haswell Core i5 processor 10.6-inch 1080p screen Optional 4GB or 8GB RAM micro-SD storage expansion Battery life increased by 2 hours compared to the old model's 4 to 5 hours, i.e., 6 to 7 hours 907g front and rear cameras Bluetooth 4.0 USB 3.0 No support for LTE

Both Surface 2 and Surface Pro 2 will have adjustable two-stage stands and VaporMg shells, with no significant design changes from the previous generation, though there are rumors of a possible white version.

In addition, the appearance of two new devices will also feature Power Cover keyboards and a Surface dock. Among them, Power Cover has a built-in power supply that can power the new Surface. The Surface dock only supports Surface Pro and Surface Pro 2, featuring one USB 3.0 and three USB 2.0 ports.

ZDNet learned from insiders that the release dates for Surface 2 and Surface Pro 2 may be around October 22. Notably, Windows 8.1 will officially go on sale on October 18.

The aforementioned insider revealed the following prices for Surface: First-generation Surface RT will continue to be sold, with the 32GB version priced at $349 Surface 2: 32GB version $499, 64GB version $599 First-generation Surface Pro continues to be sold, starting at $799 Surface Pro 2: Starting prices for 64GB, 128GB, 256GB, and 512GB versions are sequentially $899, $999, $1299, and $1699 External keyboards still need to be purchased separately

If the above configurations and prices ultimately align with Microsoft's official announcement, then Surface 2 lacks a price advantage when competing with products like iPad (the 16GB version of iPad costs $499, different from the storage space of the low-end Surface 2). Similarly, the battery life of Surface Pro 2 does not compare favorably with other Haswell devices boasting up to 10 hours of battery life. Of course, if users are willing to pay extra for the Power Cover keyboard with a built-in power supply, it could be a different story.

Microsoft needs to present more convincing selling points during the event, such as improvements in the application ecosystem. After tasting the "bitterness" of Window RT and Window 8, the improved system should deliver satisfactory performance.
