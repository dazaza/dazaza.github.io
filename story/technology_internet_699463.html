Stairs play a crucial role in the design of villas (duplex houses). After all, they connect the previous and subsequent parts (or perhaps it should be called linking the upper and lower levels).

There are mainly several types of stairs [http://www.winallchina.com] used in homes:

1 Straight Stairs: These are now rarely seen because they occupy too much space. This design is only suitable for large duplex houses. Such stairs should be straight and rotate smoothly.

2 Curved Stairs: Some duplex houses still use this steel-wood stair design [http://www.winallchina.com]. They should not be crooked or square, as this is a major taboo in Feng Shui.

3 Folded Stairs: Most duplex house designs use this type. They should not be bent.

4 Spiral Stairs: A variation of folded stairs. As mentioned above.

Of course, there are many forms, but in terms of materials, they are relatively easy to count.

For example, solid wood, stone slabs, tiles, steel plates, carpets, etc. Stairs can be decorated with any material used in home decoration.

The key is the design of the handrail. The quality of the handrail design is crucial in evaluating the overall design of the staircase.

I recommend using matte stainless steel. Based on my experience, indoor handrail designs should avoid mirror-finished stainless steel or other shiny metal surfaces. If you indeed want to use stainless steel, the ideal material for making stair handrails is forged steel.

The second best option is cast iron, followed by wood, then porcelain.

And finally, stone. The most ideal handrail material is wood.

Although many homeowners choose to dismantle and reinstall stair railings, regardless of the shape, if you want to redesign them, the most ideal material is cement concrete, followed by steel structures,

and then wooden ones. Considering this aspect, it is essential that the stairs produce as little noise as possible when pedestrians walk on them. Another issue worth noting is that I have seen many temporarily modified stairs where people have to tread carefully, which takes away some elegance from duplex houses.

Other issues to consider in staircase decoration [http://www.winallchina.com]:

1 The slope of the staircase should not be too steep. This is not only a consideration for the elderly and children but also for ordinary adults.

2 Since the staircase is a high-wear area, sturdy materials should be used.

3 The width of the staircase railing should either be large enough so that a child's head cannot fit through or small enough to allow free passage.

4 For stair treads with gaps, attention should be paid to how ladies wearing short skirts appear, preventing embarrassing situations.

5 The edges of the stair treads should be rounded to prevent foot injuries.

6 The number of steps in the staircase should consider both the floor height and the individual step height. The number of steps should also be considered auspicious or inauspicious, generally following the four-word mantra [birth prosperity death extinction]. The last step at the landing platform should end with [birth] or [prosperity], avoiding [death] and [extinction]. Based on years of Feng Shui design practice, the accuracy rate is around 80%.

7 The entrance to the staircase should not directly face the main door. Since the staircase itself consists of sections, if the "Qi" enters and first encounters the staircase, it will encounter horizontal cutting lines that either completely cut off the "Qi" or disrupt its flow, disturbing the energy field. Once the energy field is disrupted, the overall environment of the home naturally suffers. Therefore, seeing a staircase immediately upon entering may not be convenient, and the homeowner's health could unknowingly be negatively affected. There are mainly three methods to address this: one is to turn the staircase in a direction opposite to the main door, such as designing it into an arc shape, reversing the stair opening direction, and facing away from the main door; two is to hide the staircase, ideally behind walls, enclosed between two walls, eliminating concerns about disrupting the energy field while enhancing safety during ascents and descents. The space under the staircase can be designed into a storage room or toilet; three is to place a screen between the main door and the staircase to guide the "Qi" smoothly into the home.

8 The staircase should not be placed in the center of the residence. Because the center of the house is referred to as the "Xueyan," the focal point where "Qi" gathers. Generally believed, over time, this has evolved into Chinese aesthetic habits such as centrality, balance, and impartiality. If the staircase is placed in the center of the house, it creates a sense of "guest overshadowing the host." People moving up and down create noise, wasting the precious "Xueyan" area and implying disrespect, which naturally won't bring good fortune to the homeowner.

Stairs are a highlight in villa (duplex) design, connecting the spatial energy hubs of the home. When designing, functionality, aesthetics, and auspiciousness must be considered! Stairs serve as a channel connecting all parts into an integral whole, focusing on achieving optimal ventilation and smooth airflow.

When decorating, the following basic principles should be followed:

1 Material of the staircase: Use wooden materials. This helps slow down the airflow, whereas stone or metal materials make it difficult to achieve this effect. If wooden and marble materials are combined, it is best to add two round columns with crystal balls at the staircase entrance (if space allows), enhancing the Feng Shui effect of retaining wind and gathering Qi for wealth accumulation.

2 Shape of the staircase: As a rapid air exchange and delivery channel, the staircase should enable airflow to quickly move from one level to another, promoting the gathering of vital energy. Therefore, the steeper the slope, the harder it is to achieve smooth airflow, and the smaller the slope, the better the effect! To prevent the Qi from upstairs rushing down directly, it is generally advisable to design a buffer staircase, avoiding straight stairs and opting for zigzagged staircases (usually referring to staircases with rest platforms) or spiral/curved staircases being the best choice!

3 Position of the staircase [http://www.winallchina.com]: The best position should be against the wall. Avoid placing it in the center of the residence, as this would divide the entire residence into two, causing disputes within the family, even affecting marital harmony and household harmony.

Do not let the airflow rush directly from the staircase to the door, as this causes financial leakage, which is a major taboo.
