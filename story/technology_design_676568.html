Introduction

When mobile phones integrate the functions of radios, MP3 players, cameras, video cameras, televisions, PDAs, etc., they have long surpassed the initial simple positioning as communication tools, transforming into an important part of people's daily lives. The rapid advancement of communication technology has further accelerated the arrival of the 3G era. Compared to the previous 2G, 3G moves the Internet onto mobile phones, allowing users to handle images, music, browse web pages, attend telephone conferences, and conduct e-commerce on their phones. On the other hand, mobile phone manufacturers seize the huge business opportunities brought by 3G, continuously increasing R&D investment, resulting in the continuous release of large-screen, full-keyboard smartphones that support WIFI and WAPI. Meanwhile, the emergence of counterfeit phones has significantly reduced the average selling price of smartphones, greatly lowering the user threshold for high-performance phones, leading to a surge in the number of smartphones, providing a complete hardware foundation for users to access mobile Internet services.

According to data from the International Telecommunication Union, there were about 10 million minicomputers globally in 1980, over 200 million terminals connected to the Internet in 2000; by the end of 2010, the global Internet user base exceeded 2 billion, with over 5 billion mobile phone registrations. The rapid growth in the number of users in the mobile Internet era is an inevitable trend. The rapid increase in the number of users implies enormous opportunities and will create tremendous commercial value.

In the mobile Internet era, terminals become one of the key focuses of mobile Internet development. Around the needs of mobile Internet development, the development of terminals in the mobile Internet era presents a clear trend: closely focusing on user needs, providing users with comprehensive services and experiences, i.e., user experience is king. At the beginning of today's mobile Internet era, user experience has gradually become the ultimate pursuit of terminal development. The iPhone introduced by Apple has elevated user experience to a new level, winning the favor of mobile phone users worldwide. The application experience based on Android (Google's mobile operating system) has also reached the best level.

Based on 3G networks and smartphone platforms, various needs of Internet users based on PCs are slowly shifting to mobile terminals. The trend of 3G encompasses the integration of desktop Internet and WAP content. The development of 3G in China is making mobile phones constantly surpass PCs. According to the DCCI's "2010-2011 China Internet Market Data Report", the growth rate of mobile Internet in China is significantly faster than PC Internet. It is expected that by 2013, the number of mobile Internet users in China will reach 721 million, exceeding the 716 million PC Internet users, marking China's true entry into the mobile Internet era. Faced with a market with promising prospects, mobile Internet quickly gathers a batch of entrepreneurs to begin expanding their territory.
