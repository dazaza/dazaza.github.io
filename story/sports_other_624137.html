Yu Yang (Weibo) and Wang Xiaoli (Weibo) won the award for Best Female Athlete of the Year, showcasing their trophies on Weibo.

In the shortlist for the Badminton World Federation (BWF) Best Player of 2011, there were a total of 12 men nominated, with Lin Dan leading five Chinese players competing for the best honor. In the women's category, eight players were nominated in total, among which three absolute mainstays from China’s female singles team—Wang Yihan, Wang Xin, and Wang Shixian—as well as the pairs Yu Yang/Wang Xiaoli and Zhao Yunlei all made it to the nomination list. On the evening of December 12th Beijing time, senior player Yu Yang of the Chinese Badminton Team posted on Weibo a picture of the BWF Annual Best Female Player trophy, sharing her joy with her long-time partner Wang Xiaoli who also shared this honor. (Click the image to enter Yu Yang's Weibo.)

The BWF Player of the Year Award was officially established in 2008, with Lin Dan and Zhou Mi winning that year. In 2009, Lee Chong Wei and Wang Yihan won the male and female awards respectively, while in 2010, the winners were Lee Chong Wei and Wang Xin. For the best female player award, there were a total of 8 candidates, with 6 being from China: Wang Yihan, Wang Xin, Wang Shixian, Yu Yang/Wang Xiaoli, and Zhao Yunlei who competed in mixed doubles. The two foreign players were Denmark's famous doubles player Pedersen and Indonesia's renowned player Nazar.

At the BWF Super Series China Open held in November this year, Yu Yang/Wang Xiaoli defeated their teammates Tang Jinhua/Xia Huan 2-0 to win the women's doubles title at the China Open Badminton Championships. This was their first victory at the China Open, achieving a clean sweep of the titles from the five Premier Super Series tournaments this year. Adding the World Championship title, they could be considered the only pair in international badminton capable of achieving the Grand Slam for the entire year.

After receiving the annual best female player honor tonight, Yu Yang couldn't hide her excitement as she showcased her trophy on Weibo, saying, "I am very happy to have received the 2011 BWF Best Female Player Award. We will continue to work hard. Thank you to the BWF and everyone who supports us." As the vice head coach of the national badminton team, Tian Bingyi also shared this honor via his mobile phone on-site, congratulating Yu Yang/Wang Xiaoli for winning the Best Female Player Award! Keep going! Wang Xiaoli promptly replied, "Thank you Coach Tian! Go for it in the finals!"

Yu Yang/Wang Xiaoli currently rank stably at number one in the world, effectively securing their participation in the London Olympics. Their current task is to maintain good form and avoid injury. In the final of the Chinese Masters, Wang Xiaoli had to withdraw due to a foot injury and missed the subsequent Japan Super Series, which sounded a warning bell. Yu Yang's previous partner Du Jing had to retire due to injuries, so Yu Yang and Wang Xiaoli must learn from this experience, avoiding injuries and striving to maintain good form for next year's Olympics. At that time, they have a good chance to achieve a Golden Grand Slam.

According to the BWF's official website on December 4th, in the voting for the annual best male athlete, Lee Chong Wei led with 34% support, while Lin Dan, the men's singles champion at this year's World Championships, garnered 22%. Lee Chong Wei will retain the highest honor after winning last year's male award. In addition to considering the players' performances on the court in 2011, their popularity and whether they serve as role models for fans are also important considerations. The BWF will comprehensively evaluate and select the best male and female athletes of 2011.
