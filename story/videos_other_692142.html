The heroic act moved many villagers to tears as they came to visit her. Yesterday, I learned from the hospital that Ke Meihua had temporarily escaped life-threatening danger. Currently, the Yangxin Fenglin traffic police team is investigating the specific cause of the "December 9" traffic accident. (Reported by Weihua) At around 11 a.m. on December 9, Ke Meihua was helping take care of her neighbor Ke Shuibing's 11-month-old baby girl, basking in the sun outside her door. In front of her house, the Jiefu Provincial Road is a must-pass road connecting Yangxin and Jiangxi Province. There were several children playing by the roadside. Suddenly, Ke Meihua saw a truck coming from the direction of Yangxin. Her neighbor Hu Lihua’s two-year-old son He Maomao was playing in the middle of the road without realizing the approaching vehicle. Without much thought, Ke Meihua rushed toward the middle of the road with the baby in her arms, pulling Maomao back.

According to Chu Tian Jinbao, at 9 a.m. on December 9, on the Jiefu Provincial Road in Fenglin Town, Yangxin County, 56-year-old farmer Ke Meihua from Shuiyuan Village saved her neighbor's two-year-old boy who was in the middle of the road but unfortunately got hit by a truck loaded with stones. Both of her legs were crushed and she underwent high amputation. The 11-month-old baby girl she held and the neighboring boy were both unharmed.

The driver quickly called for help, and nearly 40 minutes later, Ke Meihua was rescued and sent to the emergency room. Due to excessive blood loss, Ke Meihua's heart stopped beating momentarily inside the ambulance, but thanks to the rescue personnel performing artificial respiration and cardiac defibrillation, her heartbeat was restored. That afternoon, Ke Meihua's severely injured legs were amputated, with parts about 40 cm below her thigh being removed.

In the critical moment, she embraced the baby girl and saved the toddler.

On the morning of December 10, in the ward of Yangxin People's Hospital, Ke Meihua's bed was surrounded by villagers from Shuiyuan and neighboring villages who had come to visit her. "If it weren't for her, my child would have been gone long ago!" Neighbor Hu Lihua, wiping her tearful eyes, told reporters that she was washing clothes by the well at home and didn't notice her son Maomao running out to play. "If I had let go at that time, my daughter who is less than a year old might have fallen and gotten hurt!" Ke Shuibing said emotionally.

It is understood that Ke Meihua, 56 years old this year, has four daughters and one son. All her daughters are married, and her fifth-ranked son Hu Qing Shun is also married. Her 60-year-old husband, Ke Shanqi, collects scrap at home. The couple has always been kind to their neighbors in Shuiyuan Village and is deeply respected by everyone.
