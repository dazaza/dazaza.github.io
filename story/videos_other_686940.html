When buying furniture at the mall, there are many things we need to pay attention to, such as counterfeit brands, pungent smells from so-called "environmentally-friendly" furniture, etc. A slight lack of attention can cause trouble down the road. So, what exactly should we do to avoid these troubles? Let's take a look at the following few tips.

Non-environmental "eco-friendly" furniture: Sometimes, consumers spend a lot of money on furniture that may not be worth it at all. Strict quality control is lacking in the production process, resulting in products that don't meet national or industry standards when they leave the factory. There are also false advertisements in the sales process, where domestic products are passed off as imported ones, ordinary items as classics, plywood as solid wood, and imitation leather as genuine leather, etc.

Suggestion: Consumers should first carefully understand the credibility of furniture manufacturers, the name of the furniture, material, specifications, and model. Secondly, one should also understand the relevant three guarantees (repair, replacement, refund) regulations and industry standards for acceptable non-quality issues. Finally, if the purchased furniture has quality problems, consumers can rely on their prior knowledge of the brand to address the issue.

Brand Counterfeiting: Due to the uneven quality of some furniture products, some consumers are tempted by lower prices, while merchants seek to ensure certain profits by purchasing high-similarity products from small workshops and selling them to consumers.

Suggestion: Before purchase, request the merchant to show the quality inspection report of the furniture and pay attention to its validity period (usually one year). When making the purchase, clearly state the product’s brand, material, size, color, and after-sales service duration in the contract or order to reasonably protect your rights with the dealer.
