Let's be this way!

by sqpp7675y on 2010-04-17 21:41:06

Love is when, upon waking in the morning, you give her a gentle kiss;

Love is when she wants to stay in bed, and asks you to pull her up, only for you to unexpectedly draw her into your arms;

Love is when you savor the food she cooks, even if it’s not palatable, praising it as better than anything outside, then secretly eating something else;

Love is when she helps you copy something, and midway through, you suddenly say "I love you," and she writes it down without hesitation;

Love is when on Valentine's Day, she asks for flowers, and you seriously reply that they're too expensive due to merchants' price hikes. But when she comes home disheartened, she finds a large bunch of red roses smiling at her;

Love is when she's upset, and you circle around her anxiously: "Don't be mad anymore, look, I'm already worried sick!";

Love is when she has no appetite and doesn’t want to eat, yet you scold her loudly while placing her favorite dishes onto her plate;

Love is when, on a snowy night as she returns home late, you wait for her outside her workplace until you’re freezing, forgetting that a car could have directly taken her home;

Love is when she insists on taking medicine despite poor health but carelessly sets it aside, and you sternly tell her: "If you're in pain later, don't call me; I won’t care." Yet when she’s in pain, you feel it more acutely than she does;

Love is when, while eating, you absentmindedly offer her a bite, and she eats it without hesitation;

Love is when every piece of clothing you buy for her fits her perfectly, allowing her to proudly say: "This was bought for me by my boyfriend";

Love is when you happily accept items she buys that don’t suit your taste, keeping them aside to occasionally use once or twice;

Love is when she knits a sweater for you that turns out too small, and you refuse to let her give it away, saying: "I can wear it when I lose weight";

Love is when, during your happiness, you spin her around a few times, and in your sadness, she stays by your side, believing her delicate shoulders can bear your heavy heart;

Love is when you cannot take out frustrations from outside matters on her;

Love is when traveling away, you still make daily ordinary phone calls to her;

Love is trusting and respecting all of her;

Love is when, encountering something fun, beautiful, or delicious, you always think: "How nice it would be if she were here";

Love is introducing her with pride wherever you go: "This is my girlfriend";

Love is treating her with constant affection, not deliberately doing many things for her, but ensuring each act brims with deep love;

Love is mutual dependence and habituation, becoming accustomed to having each other in life, accustomed to each other's strengths and weaknesses, accustomed to his presence in the air;

In essence, loving someone is about ordinariness; true love lies in this simplicity.
