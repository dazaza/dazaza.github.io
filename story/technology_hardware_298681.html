Middle school student attacked by hornets on the way to school crawls one kilometer for help

by ccwm9472 on 2009-12-08 09:51:53

On his way to school, Li Fenglin, a second-year junior high school student from Heixi Middle School in Qianjiang District, was suddenly attacked by hornets. Despite the pain, he crawled for one kilometer to seek help from villagers. His first words were: "There are two more classmates below, please rescue them first." Yesterday, at the Children's Hospital of the city, after being rescued for more than ten days, Li Fenglin still hasn't escaped from danger due to lack of money for further treatment. However, he still asked his parents about the condition of the two classmates.

On November 8th, Li Fenglin, 14 years old from the second group of Jiulong Village in Baishi Township of the district, made an appointment with his fellow villagers Li Xianfeng and Wang Yunqiong to go to school together. When they arrived at the Chaofangling pass in the first group of Jiulong Village, a swarm of hornets attacked them. The three were immediately stung and fell to the ground in pain, rolling around. As there were no households nearby, the older Li Fenglin told the two companions not to move. He endured the pain, covered his eyes, and crawled on the mountain road for more than 20 minutes until he reached the pass and called for help from passersby. Upon hearing this, villager Gong Zhengxiang rushed over. Li Fenglin said to her: "There are two more classmates below, please save them first."

Gong Zhengxiang immediately called her family and the couple Wang Kaicheng. After several hours of effort, the three were sent to the central hospital of the district, which was 70 kilometers away. After emergency treatment, Wang Yunqiong, who had been hospitalized for five days, returned to school. Li Xianfeng was still under observation and treatment at the hospital. Li Fenglin, who was stung more than 30 times on the head, was transferred to the intensive care unit of the City Children's Hospital and has yet to escape life-threatening danger as of yesterday.

Yesterday, when Li Fenglin regained some consciousness, his first question was: "Where are Xianfeng and Yunqiong? How serious are their injuries?" Father Li Fanghong told him that Yunqiong had been discharged and returned to school, while Xianfeng's condition was stable and she had been transferred to the township health center for observation and treatment. Li Fenglin sighed with relief: "If these two are fine, I am relieved."

"Even in such a situation, she is still thinking about her two classmates," said Li Fanghong. Both he and his wife are farmers, and his wife is a chronic patient. During the farming off-season, he worked odd jobs in the city, but it was still not enough to cover his daughter's living expenses and his wife's medical fees. To save his daughter, he has already spent more than 70,000 yuan. Apart from the 10,000 yuan advanced by the local government of the township and the 5,000 yuan donated by the district sports bureau, the paired poverty alleviation unit of the village, and the 2,000 yuan donated by teachers and students of his daughter's school, the rest was borrowed from others. "If we don't pay soon, the medication will be stopped."

According to the hospital, after being stung by the hornets, Li Fenglin's crawling and calling for help accelerated blood circulation, allowing the hornet venom to invade her heart, liver, kidneys, etc., increasing the difficulty of treatment. Currently, Li Fenglin has not completely escaped life-threatening danger. After learning about Li Fenglin's selfless act, the patients in the same ward showed great respect for her.

Wang Yunqiong's mother Zhang Xiang said that Li Fenglin was the most severely injured. She endured the pain and crawled for more than a thousand meters to call for help, asking others to rescue Wang Yunqiong and Li Xianfeng first. "She is so sensible, and now she is still in danger, how can people not feel heartbroken?"

(Reported by journalist Xiang Jun)
