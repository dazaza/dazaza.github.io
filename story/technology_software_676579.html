"Rebirth of a Thief Who Ruled the World" is a web novel written by The Angry Snail. Lonely travelers, chasing the shadows, this is the ode to thieves. Carrying the memories of a level 180 master thief, Nie Yan returns ten years into the past, where fate plays a joke on him. What was once missed and taken away must now be reclaimed. Then, with a set of legendary gear, he will crush everything in his path, slay gods if need be, unstoppable. Lost in life, everyone has a god that guides them. This work commemorates the never-fading WOW and DND in our hearts - The Angry Snail.

"Rebirth of a Thief Who Ruled the World: The History of Faith." The history of the Atlantis continent can be traced back tens of thousands of years. Various creatures lived scattered across this vast land in tribal forms. There were many ancient races here: giants, dwarves, elves, goblins, orcs, humans, half-orcs, half-elves, and dragonkind. In the year -2678, the mighty Dragon King Zeyndenar began his conquest of the other races. This war lasted for two thousand six hundred seventy-eight years. The dragonkind conquered all other races and established the powerful Hibris Empire, setting its calendar to the first year of the Era.

The rule of the dragonkind was a catastrophic disaster for the other races. The brutal dragons slaughtered indiscriminately, which sparked resistance from all races. However, under the oppressive rule of Dragon King Zeynnard, the Hibris Empire remained unshakable. The reign of the Hibris Empire lasted for a millennium; it was a cruel and bloody period, often referred to as the "Age of Darkness."

"The Age of Chaos (230~873)" Zeynnard lived for three thousand six hundred twenty years, but finally succumbed to the passage of time. In his old age, Zeynnard divided the Hibris Empire into three parts and handed them over to his three sons Boyd, Valeria, and Theodore. Boyd controlled the northern swamps and jungles, Valeria controlled the western deserts and plateaus, and Theodore controlled the southern grasslands and hills. After Zeynnard's death, Valeria, who held the most power, was greatly dissatisfied with her father's distribution. Resentment grew between the three brothers, and thus, a war began. The war lasted for six hundred years, during which both sides used the powerful magic of the dragonkind, turning mountains into plains and hills into swamps.

The dragonkind suffered heavy losses in this war. The oppressed race of giants began their rebellion against the dragonkind, gaining the support of various other races. The uprising erupted, and in this grand war, the three great empires of the dragonkind collapsed. To prevent the resurgence of the dragonkind, the various races began a frenzied slaughter of the dragons. The dragonkind were nearly wiped out, losing their former glory, with only some members scattered in the wilderness. However, the retaliation against the dragonkind was endless, and tales of dragon-slaying heroes spread across the continent.

"The Age of Joint Rule (873-1235)" Under the leadership of the giant race, giants, elves, orcs, dwarves, and humans began to jointly rule the Atlantis continent and formed the highest ruling body, the Elder Council. The giant race gained the most seats, with six representatives. The elves had three representatives, while the orcs, dwarves, and humans each had two representatives. Giants possessed formidable strength but loved peace. Under their protection, the world returned to normal. Adult giants stood approximately 10 feet (about 3 meters) tall and weighed over 800 pounds (384 kilograms). They had robust physiques and were natural warriors. Occasionally, there would appear one or two giant shamans, but they were few in number. They inherited complex and sophisticated combat tactics and possessed the innate battle skill War Stomp. Their talents were second only to those of the powerful dragonkind.

During the war against the dragonkind, many evil creatures infiltrated the army, including wicked necromancers, dark elves, half-orcs, goblins, and a small number of malevolent humans. They manipulated the army and instigated a rebellion, attempting to seize control of the continent. To maintain their rule, the various races, under the leadership of the giants, waged a fierce war.

"Rebirth of a Thief Who Ruled the World: The Age of Turmoil (1235-1735)" The war lasted for five hundred years. The dark elves, half-orcs, goblins, and fallen humans were driven into the dark underground, while the necromancers were exiled to the barren lands of the northern part of Atlantis. The giant race suffered heavy casualties, leaving only about eight-tenths of their original population. The white elves also lost a significant portion of their numbers. Due to their strong reproductive abilities, the human population not only did not decrease but slightly increased, leading to an increase in their power. They were no longer satisfied with just having two seats in the council.

After the turmoil ended, the glory of the gods spread, and temples rose in cities across the continent.
