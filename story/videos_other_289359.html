Heartwatering recommendation Manchester United's first goal

by hartyqekb on 2009-12-02 17:28:45

After keeping Tottenham Hotspur at bay with a clean sheet, Ferguson specially praised two young players after the game. One was undoubtedly Gibson, who scored twice, and the other was Anderson, who is actually quite a bit younger than Gibson but has already made 88 appearances. Just like the match against Tottenham in September when he scored his first goal for Manchester United, Anderson once again played as if guided by divine intervention. Although he didn't score again, from the applause he received when he was substituted, you could tell that the Old Trafford fans were completely awed by Anderson's almost manic performance.

Here, "manic" does not mean that Anderson plays recklessly like some midfielders who only enjoy tackling. Anderson became famous in Manchester United for his tenacity and aggression in midfield. As Ferguson said after the game: "Once Anderson can make an impact in the game, he can attack throughout the midfield area, making our victory come very comfortably." In the 81 minutes Anderson was on the field, he not only formed a distinctive system of attacks from his more proficient left flank, but also continuously exerted his influence in the center and right flanks. This powerful mid-fielding style of Anderson had already disrupted Tottenham's well-planned tactics.

Anderson's attacking route relies on his strong physique and astonishing acceleration ability. The most typical example was probably the fast break at the 70th minute. Although the ball was eventually taken by Gomes, Anderson, under the easiest-to-be-blocked condition of carrying the ball straight through the center, surprisingly chose a risky path, rushing in with the ball past Dawson like lightning. Although this attempt did not result in a goal, it perfectly demonstrated the "terrifying" power of Anderson when he bursts out. Imagine, who would not be afraid of such a player who can still accelerate after playing for more than half of the game?

In fact, Anderson did not deliberately play as a lone hero. He had many comrades-in-arms responding to him on both sides, mainly Deleaithe on the left side, allowing the Belgian, who is about the same age but far less experienced, to play confidently. Of course, he also paired back and forth with Park Ji-sung, although the South Korean star is not currently performing well near the goal. Another success on the left was forcing Lennon to change his route. Due to Anderson's effective rapid defense and interception of Lennon's several attempts to pass this way, the England international had to switch to raiding with Neville to find opportunities. On the right side, Anderson sought more dual-speed mobile attacks with Obertan. With such responses from the Brazilian, despite being young, these kids in their twenties could still make the seasoned veterans of Tottenham look disoriented!

United's first goal came from Anderson's sudden cut-in from the left wing. Despite being tackled into the air himself, he had already safely and quickly passed the ball to Gibson's feet, allowing him to aim and score into the corner. When Anderson was active in midfield, he often followed this principle: intercept the ball with a tackle, then immediately turn around and carry the ball into the attack, observing the appropriate breakthrough direction and passing route while maintaining a high running speed with his feet, rather than simply looking for teammates upon receiving the ball like ordinary midfielders. This somewhat individualistic way of handling the ball, when executed by Anderson in form, often created thrilling moments on the field. Modern football emphasizes adrenaline-pumping excitement, and with Anderson on the field, the fans' adrenaline levels only go up!

Wearing green shoes, Anderson thus became the most unforgettable focal point for the fans, despite not being a shooting type of player. The first shot of the game was initiated by him, but despite playing the entire game, he was unable to score again. In his 88 games with United, he scored one goal against Tottenham in September, plus a penalty in the 2008 Champions League final which doesn't count as a personal goal. But this issue is acceptable to everyone. Since his debut in the 2005 World Youth Championship, where he won the Golden Ball instead of the Golden Boot, he established his controversial style trend in midfield. In today's Manchester United, which promotes offensive tactics, Anderson's dual-handling role in midfield, which is hard on both ends, naturally receives greater welcome. Who wouldn't want to see a midfielder who performs as if he never needs to take a breath?

Last year, Anderson won the European Golden Boy Award. Among the five previous winners, Anderson is definitely the least likely to score goals, but his anti-traditional influence on midfield is unique. With his clever guidance, even if Manchester United's overall pace quickens further, it seems there is always an axis flexibly controlling the situation, leaving opponents completely confused by such seemingly absurd speed. Of course, Anderson has yet to reach the level of playing at today's high standard every game; otherwise, he would have long become an absolute starter for Manchester United. But even judged by current standards, what the 20-year-old youngster has achieved already benefits Manchester United immensely.

In fact, Ferguson is completely satisfied with the current midfield configuration. The mainstays Carrick and Fletcher play steadily, Anderson and Gibson are growing rapidly, Giggs and Scholes are fully capable of playing, and let's not forget O'Shea, the quality-assured "all-purpose adhesive." A live broadcast captured a scene during today's game: Hargreaves watching the event on the field. Last week, he had already resumed training, and all signs indicate that this 28-year-old player is getting closer to his official return. Former player Ince believes: "Every team needs a midfielder like Owen (of course referring to Hargreaves) with comprehensive abilities." With such a lineup of central midfielders over the years, not only has no one been lost, but it has become even stronger with the addition of Gibson.

Given this, the recent rumors from Spanish media about Manchester City duo eyeing Guti seem quite strange. While Manchester City might have reasons for looking at Guti, it's hard to imagine what purpose a 33-year-old Real Madrid substitute would serve at Manchester United. Comparing current match capabilities is already meaningless. If we talk about tournament credentials and experience, Giggs and Scholes can easily overshadow Guti. Now Manchester United is probably worried about having too few matches, making it difficult for so many excellent midfielders to fully display their talents, let alone bringing in an older player to create "congestion"!
