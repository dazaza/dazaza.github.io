The participants of the real-stock trading competition benefited from the "no shortage of money trend", and the highest income doubled.

by xxywing on 2009-02-16 09:18:21

By the close of trading on the afternoon of February 13, the return on investment (ROI) of Dun Gen, a contestant in the real stock trading competition, had already reached 95.83%, while the ROI of the second-placed contestant, Old Man, was 80.99%, and that of the third-placed contestant, Mountain Wolf, was 79.63%. The fourth-placed contestant, Good Mood, had an ROI of only 59.35%, which was 20 percentage points lower than the top three contestants. The top three contestants formed a tripod-like structure, with one outstanding performer and two strong ones taking the top three positions. This week, the contestants' ROIs have made rapid progress, with fierce competition in the rankings. It is difficult to secure the top trading position without ultra-high returns. Below, we will analyze how the current leading contestant, Dun Gen, gradually moved to the forefront.

Currently, Dun Gen holds only one stock, Foton Motor. The cost price is 4.94, and the current closing price is 9.63, which is almost double the original investment. By checking the historical transaction records of this contestant, during the early stages of the stock trading competition when the stock market conditions were unfavorable, Dun Gen continuously performed T+0 operations on warrants, which steadily increased his ROI and allowed him to gain prominence. Subsequently, he entered Jinzhou Port and Xinlong Holding but sold them the next day, resulting in basically no profit or loss. The significant increase in ROI occurred after the post-holiday market rally. In fact, it was one stock, Foton Motor, that propelled Dun Gen to the top. Purchased on December 30, Foton Motor alone has yielded a return of 93.959% by today.

This week's event started on the Lantern Festival of the lunar calendar. After five days of trading, the difference in ROIs among contestants was enormous. Dun Gen achieved a 30.81% ROI to win the weekly championship. The runner-up, Old Man, also achieved a 29.01% ROI this week. Among the participating contestants, the one who suffered the largest loss this week was Tongtong, with an ROI of -13.29%. The best performer today was Mo Mo's Love, with a daily ROI of 10.35%. The stocks Century Star Source and Xianyang Bias held by this contestant have already generated 20% returns. Today, both stocks closed at their upper limit with increased trading volume, bringing significant returns to the contestant and significantly improving his ranking. As of Friday's close, the average holding rate of contestants was 75.18%. There were 47 transactions today, seven fewer than yesterday. Most contestants are enjoying the benefits of their earlier positions and are not chasing high prices to build new positions. The average ROI of contestants is 20.07%, breaking through the 20% mark, and steadily increasing. As of Friday's close, the top ten contestants are: Dun Gen, Old Man, Mountain Wolf, Good Mood, Seven Color Rainbow, Growth Power, Kung Fu Panda, Value Selection, Shanghai Lei Lei, and Jumping Dragon.

In the medium term, there are still positive reasons and space for the new bull market trend; in the short term, the market is expected to face a new round of volatility after a surge next week. The first wave of the bull market may end during this fluctuation. Even if it does not end, this fluctuation will be more intense than the previous two, last longer, and adjust more thoroughly. Only then will the market have the ability to truly challenge the 2500-point level and above. Prudent investors can find an appropriate position to sell high and avoid this risk at that time, but they should still hold the basic mid-term position. Operationally, it is suggested for contestants: 1. Mid-term investors can continue to hold up to 60% of their mid-term positions stably, while using no more than 20% of their positions to follow market hotspots in the short term; 2. Short-term traders can actively operate individual stocks, buying low and selling high, and enjoy the process.
