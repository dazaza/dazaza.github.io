The health of infants and young children is closely related to the cleanliness of the home environment, and parents all understand this principle. But how should toys that children often come into contact with, sometimes even put in their mouths, be cleaned and disinfected? Some mothers worry that cleaning will affect the function of the toy, such as some electric toys; other mothers worry that disinfectant residues on the toys will affect the health of the child. So what is the correct method?

Firstly, parents need to understand that our goal in cleaning and disinfecting toys is not to create a completely sterile environment for the child, but rather to regularly and promptly reduce the number and concentration of bacteria and viruses per unit area or volume through washing and disinfection. This is what we usually refer to as "clean" and "hygiene." Dr. Cui Yutao from Beijing Heqi Family Hospital says that children living in a completely sterile environment are more likely to get sick because their immune system does not get exercised and developed, turning them into susceptible populations who will get sick once they come into contact with a small amount of bacteria and viruses; if they can often come into contact with a small amount of bacteria and viruses, it will instead keep the human immune system in an active state, which is beneficial for the development of the infant's immune system and maintaining the health of the baby.

Outdoor toys. Such toys can be cleaned with a clean cloth or towel, soap, and water. If you want to thoroughly disinfect outdoor toys, you can use a clean cloth or towel dipped in 75% medical alcohol to wipe them.

Secondly, the frequency of toy cleaning and disinfection is usually once a week, and parents can flexibly control according to the usage frequency and material of the toy. On weekends, you can tell the child that the toys also need to take a bath, then do it together with the child, cultivating the child's hygiene habits and labor skills while cleaning the toys. In some families where nannies help clean the home environment, parents should instruct the nanny not to use the same cloth everywhere, especially not to use the cloth for cleaning the home to wipe the child's toys, as the cloth carries a large amount of bacteria and viruses. Using the cloth for cleaning the home to wipe the toys will only make them dirtier.

The fluffy, soft-to-touch, and cute plush toys are the favorite of children. Plush toys are very similar in material to the clothes we wear, so we can wash them with some baby-specific laundry detergent, and those with antibacterial and anti-mite functions are better. Since bacteria and viruses can also accumulate in the washing machine, it is best to hand-wash plush toys, rinse thoroughly, and hang them to dry in a sunny and ventilated place, where the ultraviolet rays in the sunlight can also serve a sterilizing and disinfecting function.

Plastic toys are the most common toys, and every household has many. Plastic toys are similar in material to baby bottles, so we can use baby-specific bottle cleaning liquid to clean them. Fill a clean baby bathtub or family bathtub with clean water, put the plastic toys inside, use a clean brush dipped in baby-specific bottle cleaning liquid to scrub the plastic toys, then rinse thoroughly with a large amount of clean water, hang them to dry in a net bag, or let them naturally air-dry in a clean and breathable plastic basket, and ensure they are exposed to sunlight and ventilation.

Firstly, toys should be cleaned before being given to the child to play with. Even though infant clothing, toys, and supplies bought from stores are new, they should still be cleaned before giving them to the child for use. If they are gifts from others, it is even more important to do so. Toys inevitably get infected with some invisible bacteria and viruses during production, packaging, transportation, and selling, so cleaning them before letting the child play with them is safer.

Electric toy cleaning is something parents find relatively difficult, worrying that accidental water ingress will damage the internal electronic components, causing the toy to lose its function. Before cleaning electric toys, remove the batteries first, then wipe with a clean damp cloth. To thoroughly disinfect electric toys, you can purchase sterile gauze from a pharmacy, dip it in 75% medical alcohol to wipe the surface, and let the alcohol evaporate completely before giving it to the child to play with.

Depending on the material and category of the toy, the cleaning and disinfection methods vary:

Some busy parents may not have too much time and energy to frequently and meticulously clean and disinfect toys at home, but even just using a clean damp cloth to frequently wipe the surface of the toys will have a certain effect. Parents should also remind the child not to touch the toys at home immediately after coming home from outside, but to wash their hands first before playing, so as not to bring bacteria and viruses from outside back home. Toys that have been played with outside should be washed with soap and water upon returning home before giving them to the child to play with again. For children who have already developed good hygiene habits, parents can relax a bit, and regular cleaning of toys with soap and water is sufficient.

Cleaning of electric toys

Children always bite on some teething rings when their teeth are growing, so how should these toys that directly contact the child's mouth be disinfected? The cleaning and disinfection of teething toys should be stricter. Some teething toys can be disinfected in the same way as milk bottles: when washing the milk bottle, you can simultaneously use the milk bottle brush and cleaning liquid to scrub the teething toy clean, then place it in the milk bottle sterilizer with the milk bottle for high-temperature steam sterilization. Teething toys that cannot withstand high temperatures can be placed in the household tableware sterilizer for ultraviolet and ozone sterilization. To be cautious, when purchasing such toys, parents should carefully read the instructions or ask the salesperson to clarify what methods can be used to disinfect the teething toy and what methods cannot, to avoid damaging the toy or degrading the material.

Wooden toys are increasingly favored by parents and children for their excellent feel and educational functions. However, wooden toys easily turn black and mold in humid environments. When cleaning wooden toys, first use a piece of clean gauze or handkerchief dipped in milk bottle cleaning liquid to wipe the surface of the wooden toy, then rinse with plenty of clean water, finally use a clean gauze or handkerchief to wipe off the water droplets on the surface of the wooden toy, lay each one separately on a clean flat surface to dry, and flip them over during drying to ensure that all sides are sufficiently dried.

Cleaning of plastic toys

Fourthly, after washing the toys, they should be rinsed thoroughly with plenty of clean water (except for electric toys). Rinsing with running clean water is very important, as it can minimize the residue of detergents as much as possible. After washing the toys, they should be dried in a ventilated and sunlit place until completely dry. The concept of "clean" includes two aspects: "dry" and "clean". "Dry" is very important, as bacteria and viruses multiply much slower in a dry environment than in a humid one.

Thirdly, appropriate cleaning and disinfection products should be selected. Dr. Cui Yutao reminds parents to choose baby-specific cleaning agents and disinfectants, and not to use ordinary disinfectants to disinfect the child's toys, as these disinfectants may irritate the child's respiratory tract.
