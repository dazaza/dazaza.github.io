I. How to understand the sudden strengthening of the financial sector

Recently, the financial sector represented by securities firms has shown initial signs of strength. We have the following understandings in several aspects. First, the need for market hotspot rotation. From the market performance in recent months, small and medium-sized cap stocks (especially new shares) have been quite active, while large-cap stocks have relatively flat performance; the phenomenon of polarization is particularly severe, and there is objectively a requirement for style conversion. Second, there are some catalysts in the overall financial sector. For example, margin trading and short selling, stock index futures and other financial derivatives may be launched soon, which will be a considerable boon to securities firms. In addition, there are new developments in mixed business operations in the banking sector. The China Banking Regulatory Commission recently issued the "Pilot Management Measures for Commercial Banks' Investment in Insurance Company Equity". Many industry insiders believe that as the first regulatory legal document for banks' investment in insurance equity, this is an initial exploration of mixed business operations. Third, the Central Economic Work Conference is about to be held, during which the economic policies for the coming year, including credit policies, will be set. Some experts believe that next year's credit will still maintain a considerable scale. If this forecast proves true, considering the possibility of an increase in the bank interest rate spread next year, it can be expected that the bank's profit will maintain a certain level of growth.

In summary, the financial sector's recent performance has significantly lagged behind other sectors, giving it a certain relative valuation advantage; under the circumstances where the market has a requirement for style conversion, potential favorable factors such as margin trading, stock index futures, and mixed business operations in banks, etc., may stimulate the financial sector to move into an upward trend.

II. How to seize the investment opportunities in the financial sector

In the financial sector, we believe that securities firms and banks are worth particular attention. Regarding securities firms, from the statistical data, the recovery situation of the securities industry is obviously better than expected, and the total turnover of A and B shares in the first 11 months has already exceeded the historical highest level in 2007 by about 5%. However, from the market performance, the trend of the securities sector apparently has not fully reflected this situation.

In fact, from the perspective of investment logic, the securities sector does not lack investment highlights. As a typical cyclical industry, securities firms should benefit considerably during the economic recovery process. Firstly, the stock market is a leading indicator of the economy, so under the background of economic recovery, the stock market's performance will be better. Secondly, the capacity of the stock market is rapidly expanding, with the proportion of tradable shares sharply rising as restricted shares are gradually released, thus significantly increasing the trading volume of the two markets. This will greatly increase the brokerage income of securities firms. It is worth noting that Chinese investment banks have entered the top five global IPO underwriters for the first time. This means that with the development of China's economy, the securities industry is emerging with a new, stronger, and larger posture.

For the opportunities in the securities sector, investors can grasp them from angles such as direct investment business and margin trading. Regarding direct investment business, since the pilot program for direct investment business started in 2007, more than ten securities firms have obtained pilot qualifications. After the smooth launch of the Growth Enterprise Market (GEM), securities firms with a larger proportion of direct investment business are worth paying attention to. Regarding margin trading, securities firms that obtain pilot programs often require higher net profits, relatively larger scales (such as network points), and good asset management history. That is, the possibility of large first-tier securities firms within the industry becoming pilots is greater, and investors can focus on the leading listed securities firms.

Regarding the bank sector, investors can grasp from several angles:

First, choose banks with the ability or qualification to develop mixed business operations. Whether it is the universal banking model represented by Germany or the model transitioning from separation to integration represented by the United States and Japan, all indicate that the international financial industry has comprehensively entered the era of mixed business operations. Therefore, banks with the ability or qualification to develop mixed business operations will take the initiative in future development.

Second, choose banks with strong expansion capabilities. Whether it is the deposit-loan business of banks or non-interest income business, they rely heavily on business outlets. Therefore, small and medium-sized banks with cross-regional expansion capabilities deserve particular attention.

Third, choose banks with high asset operation efficiency. Currently, interest rate spread income remains the main source of bank profits, and banks with high asset operation efficiency often can achieve relatively high interest rate spreads and interest rate spread income. Therefore, this type of bank deserves particular attention.
