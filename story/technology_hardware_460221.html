Company fined 1.5 million yuan for high-interest relending to others after taking bank loan

by zzfdhdb on 2011-07-11 12:57:32

□ Reporter Lu Hui, Correspondent Chen Xiahe

Evening Post News: Recently, Zhabei Court made the first-instance judgment on the city's first case of high-interest relending criminal case. The defendant company, Shanghai Dufeng Enterprise Development Co., Ltd. (hereinafter referred to as Dufeng Company), was fined 1.5 million yuan for the crime of high-interest relending; the defendant Mr. Zhou was sentenced to one year in prison, suspended for two years; the defendant Mr. Cao was sentenced to one year in prison, suspended for one year; and they were ordered to make restitution of illegal gains.

The prosecution accused that in April 2008, Mr. Cao, a temporary employee of the defendant company Dufeng Company, upon learning that the legal representative of Shanghai Longtan Property Management Co., Ltd. (hereinafter referred to as Longtan Company), Mr. Chen (handled separately), urgently needed funds to repay debts, in order to illegally seek profits, colluded with the legal representative of Dufeng Company, Mr. Zhou, claiming that Dufeng Company urgently needed working capital, applied for a loan of 10 million yuan from the bank, and then lent it at high interest to Longtan Company. Afterwards, the two companies signed a "Loan Agreement", stipulating that after Dufeng Company obtained the loan, it would deduct 970,000 yuan in advance as the due interest payable to the bank, 900,000 yuan as the "service fee" paid by Longtan Company to Dufeng Company, and 300,000 yuan as a guarantee deposit temporarily withheld in Dufeng Company's account. The remaining 7.83 million yuan would be used by Dufeng Company to repay creditors on behalf of Longtan Company, namely Huayu Pawnshop.

On May 27, 2008, Dufeng Company received a bank loan of 10 million yuan. Of this amount, apart from repaying 7.83 million yuan, 668,000 yuan was distributed as "kickbacks" to Mr. Cao and other individuals, and the remaining 1.502 million yuan left in Dufeng Company's account included 300,000 yuan as loan guarantee deposit, 137,000 yuan for Dufeng Company to pay loan advisory fees, appraisal fees, etc., 450,000 yuan for repaying loan interest, and the rest over 600,000 yuan was used for Dufeng Company's daily operations.

On February 1 and February 2, 2010, upon receiving telephone notifications from the public security organs, Mr. Zhou and Mr. Cao successively surrendered themselves to the public security organs and truthfully confessed the facts of the crime. After the incident, the defendant Mr. Cao and the defendant company Dufeng Company each returned illegal gains of 300,000 yuan.

During the trial, the defense attorney for Mr. Cao argued that the interest rate agreed upon in the "Loan Agreement" between Dufeng Company and Longtan Company was based on the actual bank interest rate, and Dufeng Company did not lend at a higher interest rate than the bank. Whether the service fee charged by Dufeng Company should be considered as interest is debatable. Even if it is considered as interest, it did not exceed four times the bank loan interest rate, so it should not be categorized as high interest. Moreover, since Mr. Cao had turned himself in voluntarily and actively returned the illicit gains, the defense requested leniency and application of probation for Mr. Cao.

After reviewing the case, the court partially adopted the defense's opinion and made the aforementioned judgment.
