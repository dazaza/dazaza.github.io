Essential Components of Virtual Education: S-e-Learning

by gulum2652 on 2009-11-24 16:36:07

The most attractive aspect of e-learning is that students can freely choose learning content according to their needs. More and more people find in their lives and work that they need more training and knowledge, but not everyone can leave their job for several days at a time to sit quietly in a classroom. To meet the needs of this group of students, it can only be done through synchronous, or live e-learning.

Currently, many companies have launched e-learning content, but much of it has not fully utilized the advantages of this method. Teachers do not use multimedia teaching aids to teach according to the dynamic needs of students; they simply place fixed slides online. Students have almost no opportunity to communicate with teachers or classmates, and the learning content is not tailored to individuals. The effectiveness of this approach is predictable. Companies that have wasted money on investments in this area will wonder: Why hasn't our implementation of e-learning worked? Truly effective e-learning is not a simple matter. Each classroom should be equipped with at least two qualified teachers, and under necessary conditions, there should also be an experienced instructor and technician online. They should promptly respond to student questions and guide discussions. For better visual and auditory effects, some high-quality video and audio tools are also essential. Who wouldn't get bored with repetitive, monotonous images?

Compared to the traditional way of sitting in a classroom, does online e-learning have the same effect? If operated correctly, the results of e-learning can surpass traditional methods. E-learning changes people's learning methods by using vivid multimedia tools to help explain complex concepts. Students can focus more easily and absorb knowledge more readily.

From experience, we know that the most effective learning process takes place in a simulated real-world work environment. Timely interaction between teachers and students, high-quality graphic demonstrations, a free and lively atmosphere, and hands-on practice procedures are all important features of e-learning. These conditions allow students to encounter potential problems and solutions they may face in actual work during their studies.

Although students are all studying in a virtual classroom and seem distant from each other, they don’t feel forgotten. The teaching team will always respond to student behavior and strive to make the teaching methods more comprehensive and interesting. When students ask questions, experienced mentors will provide one-on-one answers on the spot. E-learning starts in a dynamic virtual educational environment, but the role of this virtual environment should not be limited to just a "launch pad." While providing access to online courses, it should also make students feel like they're in a humanized learning environment. Students can freely enter chat rooms to discuss course content, ask teachers questions, or post their own opinions on forums.

More importantly, the information conveyed by teachers transcends the boundaries of sound and text. Simulated work tasks, laboratories, or other real-life scenarios designed according to different content allow students to acquire knowledge through their own hands-on processes. Another advantage of such simulation operations is that students can self-test in a relatively safe setting before participating in actual work, clearly recognizing their own abilities.

After a certain stage of classes is over, the communication between students and teachers does not stop there. Students can email their teachers at any time. Mentors must not only solve problems as soon as possible but also take the initiative to contact students and send test materials and reading materials in a timely manner. Some non-full-time students may fall behind due to busy work schedules, so class records are quite critical. Replays allow students to review missed or content that needs reinforcement.

These characteristics of e-learning make this web-based virtual education comparable to classroom learning methods, and traditional education may therefore introduce new standards.
