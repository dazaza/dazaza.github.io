A Guide to Observing the Total Solar Eclipse in Shanghai

by kaxiya on 2009-07-13 16:49:15

On the morning of July 22, 2009, a rare total solar eclipse that occurs once every three hundred years will present a spectacular show in the skies above Shanghai. This solar eclipse will last for a long time and be observable by many people, making it the most magnificent celestial phenomenon visible to Chinese people in the 21st century. The last total solar eclipse that occurred over Shanghai was in 1575, and after this year, the next total solar eclipse visible in Shanghai will not occur until 300 years later in 2309.

Since the timing of the solar eclipse coincides with the morning rush hour, even if one cannot go to an observatory to watch the total solar eclipse, the tall buildings, parks, green spaces, and other open areas in Shanghai are also excellent places to observe the solar eclipse.

One: Suitable for astronomy enthusiasts

1) Sheshan Astronomical Observatory

Admission: Tickets purchased at the foot of the mountain, priced at 30 yuan per person

The Sheshan Astronomical Observatory is also known as the Sheshan Station of the Shanghai Astronomical Observatory of the Chinese Academy of Sciences, located at the top of the West Sheshan National Forest Park. It is the only observatory in the path of totality for this total solar eclipse. There is a constellation square on the rooftop platform, with fences engraved with 44 constellation patterns, which not only adds to its interest but also increases the knowledge of visitors. Friends who love astronomy can visit there and will surely benefit greatly.

2) Yuehu Sculpture Park

Event package ticket: 150 yuan per set (includes admission)

Yuehu is adjacent to the Shanghai Sheshan Astronomical Observatory, and the Yuehu Sculpture Park is one of the recommended observation sites for this total solar eclipse. The Sheshan Astronomical Observatory has established a large observation base in the Shanghai Yuehu Sculpture Park, and experts from the observatory will personally provide professional explanations and guidance to tourists on site. In addition, the Shanghai Yuehu Sculpture Park has collaborated with the Shanghai Astronomical Observatory to plan a series of total solar eclipse observation activities, with the event period running from July 16th to 26th. On the day of July 22nd, within the park, there will be expert-guided observation exchanges on the "Water Stage" and nationwide online astronomical live broadcasts. Besides the on-site observation on July 22nd, during the activity week, the Yuehu Sculpture Park will also host a large-scale science exhibition titled "Total Solar Eclipse and the Beauty of the Universe" and a series of astronomical science lectures at the Yuehu Art Museum. After the "Activity Week," the Yuehu Sculpture Park will continue to hold a total solar eclipse-themed astronomical photography competition. Visitors with the event package tickets will have two opportunities to enter the park during the event period, one being on the observation day of July 22nd, and the other can be chosen at any time during the event period. Additionally, they can visit the astronomical science exhibition at the Yuehu Art Museum for free and receive the observation tools used on the observation day as a free gift.

3) Oriental Pearl Tower

Admission: 100 yuan per person (263-meter observation deck, exhibition hall, 259-meter circular glass corridor); 150 yuan per person (three spheres)

Most astronomy enthusiasts choose open areas in the suburbs or advantageous positions on tall buildings to watch the entire process of the total solar eclipse. Therefore, the Oriental Pearl Tower is an excellent observation point for the total solar eclipse. The Oriental Pearl Radio and Television Tower, ranked first in Asia and third in the world, stands at 468 meters high. Its dazzling spherical observation deck has a diameter of 45 meters and is 263 meters high, offering the best panoramic view of Shanghai. On clear days, you can see the outlines of Sheshan and Chongming Island in the distance, making the heart feel refreshed. However, tickets may be hard to come by at that time.

Two: Suitable for ordinary office workers

1) Jing'an Park

Located between Nanjing Road and Yan'an Road, just a stone's throw away from Jing'an Temple Station on Metro Line 2. It has been preserved in the land-scarce Jing'an Temple area, which is quite remarkable. Although surrounded by skyscrapers on all sides, the park has mountains and water, creating a world apart from the outside.

2) People's Square Leisure Green Space

The open public leisure green space integrated with the transfer hall roof of the People's Square metro station and the People's Park allows visitors to ascend the stone steps, enjoying the leisurely charm of the urban "hillside" while witnessing the grand spectacle of the total solar eclipse. Since there are open staircases and passages directly leading to the green space on Jiujiang Road and Zhongshan Middle Road, choosing to watch the total solar eclipse there offers very convenient transportation.
