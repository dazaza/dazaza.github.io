The clothes I gave you that year

by mhoeqtwrer on 2012-03-07 10:29:34

For all these years, I've heard from mother that you've kept the clothes I gave you back then. You would only wear it when you go out. You would proudly tell others that this is a gift from your daughter. I can't find appropriate words to describe your sense of satisfaction and accomplishment. I can't help but say: Grandpa, men said they would go out after the Spring Festival. You have three meals a day, and take medicine for every meal. Moreover, patients with high blood pressure should not smoke at all. She is kind, strong, and full of wisdom, she is the person who has influenced me for my whole life."

"At that time, adults were so busy that their feet never touched the ground. His Chinese was very good. As long as you don't freeze or starve, it's fine. It's nothing like now when one child lives such a leisurely life. But every time I found grandpa going out, I would cling to him and ask him to take me along. When I hear this noise, I feel unbearable sorrow in my heart. Father worked hard for us all his life. Now everything is fine, it's time for him to enjoy his old age, but he was diagnosed with malignant chordoma. Death - is because I cannot truly live in this world. Because I lost the whole world."

"Of course, don't always expect your child to suddenly become a good child. My younger brother had a hard fate! He didn't have the chance to enjoy family happiness!"

"My younger brother was smart, good at speaking, deeply understood local customs. Neighbors often asked him to handle wedding and funeral affairs. You didn't drive to school, in order to give me more living expenses; you didn't say anything, only told me to eat well and take care of my health; you didn't do anything, just quietly looked at me. I love you, my dad, you gave me a lot of things that can't be learned in school."

"After starting work, you also put in a lot of effort. From afar, father has obviously aged, walking with a limp. In the south, there is rain, lingering rain, delicate rain."

"The two were university classmates. He fell in love with her at first sight, but didn't have the courage to confess. The daughter leaned on her mother all the way, occasionally whispering in her ear. The daughter said it was okay, the teacher didn't criticize. For more than ten years, I have been the best teacher and friend in my son's heart. No matter what happens in daily life, he communicates with me. Seeing him so understanding, the sadness at this moment is swallowed by the love my son gives me. But your life is more important than mine. After I'm gone, just forget about me. Find a boy who loves you and cherishes you, and live a happy life. Wish you happiness, BYE-BYE, my love, I LOVE YOU."

"But in order to take care of the elderly grandmother, Aunt had to return to her hometown with her son."
