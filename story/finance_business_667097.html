Samsung announces Galaxy Tab 2 adoption of Android 4.0 Integrated Circuit

by ddenglk231 on 2012-02-16 14:28:38

Color us confused. Unless we're missing something, Samsung has essentially ported the full, slow version of Android 8.0 Ice Cream Sandwich onto the Galaxy Tab7.0+ and rechristened it the Galaxy Tab2. I hate to open with this accusatory tone, but after reading (and re-reading) today's press release, www.zhong-jian.com, this is exactly what I see as a spec-for-spec comparison between the "two generations." The originally designed Galaxy Tab2 shaves off some bulk from 2010's model, measuring thick 10.5mm and weighing 344g (down from 11.98mm and 380g), but it's still thicker than September's Galaxy Tab7.0 Plus (9.96mm). Assuming it uses the same chip architecture, www.wji.cc, the Galaxy Tab2 downgrades the Tab7.0 Plus' 1.2GHz processor to 1.0GHz (which is the same as the original Galaxy Tab7.0). It's almost identical to the Galaxy Tab7.0 Plus in all other innards. This includes a 7-inch WSVGA (1024x600 resolution) TFT touchscreen at par for the course, a 3-megapixel rear camera and VGA front-facing camera (downgraded from the Galaxy Tab7.0 Plus' 2-megapixel front-facing shooter), 1GB RAM, 32GB storage and 8GB (another via microSD32GB). Model options come together with Wi-Fi and Bluetooth 3.0 support for 3G. With Google's latest dessert-themed operating system, the Galaxy Tab2 will feature Samsung's newly launched AllShare Play service, which lets you stream multimedia content between compatible devices.
