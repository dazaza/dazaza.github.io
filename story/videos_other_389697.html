[1] Stop waiting for me to give orders before you act

Following his previous statement "It's the people who support you, so you should figure it out yourselves!", Premier Wen Jiabao once again gained popularity online during a seminar on the government work report when he said: "Stop waiting for me to give orders before you act."

Because the urban registered unemployment rate reported in the Government Work Report was 4.6%, while the Blue Book of Social Sciences published by the Institute of Sociology of the Chinese Academy of Social Sciences showed an urban surveyed unemployment rate of 9.4%. Why is there a 5.2% difference between the statistics from the Ministry of Human Resources and Social Security and the Chinese Academy of Social Sciences? The public has many questions about this. In response to this "quarrel", Premier Wen made the following statement:

"You, the Ministry of Social Labor Security, should hurry up and go online, or quickly hold a press conference, and explain the differences between the 4.6% and the 9.4% from the Chinese Academy of Social Sciences. Don't wait for me to tell you to act."

Why are these two data points different? Li Peilin, director of the Institute of Sociology of the Chinese Academy of Social Sciences, explained it like this:

"For nearly two decades, there has been a discrepancy between the urban registered unemployment rate and the urban surveyed unemployment rate, usually with the latter being higher than the former. When the job market is relatively stable, the two figures are close; when the employment situation is bad, there will be a large gap between the two numbers. Because our unemployment insurance does not cover all urban workers, such as migrant workers who have not participated in unemployment insurance, they are difficult to be counted after becoming unemployed."

However, the Ministry of Human Resources and Social Security had another explanation:

"The 9.4% surveyed unemployment rate is the result of a sample survey of seven thousand people. Among these seven thousand people, there were 2288 urban economically active individuals, of which 200 were unemployed, and this number was obtained by comparing these two figures."

In fact, we ordinary people have our own views on these two sets of data:

"Like me, graduates who have worked in the city for only a few years, our hukou (household registration) is still at school. Where do we go to register as unemployed? After becoming unemployed, we can only quickly look for a new job ourselves and dare not tarnish the national unemployment rate."

For this calculation method that does not include those who neither work nor register, it has long been questioned by domestic and foreign scholars. Premier Wen should make them explain not only the severely underestimated urban registered unemployment rate but also provide us with a number that can withstand scrutiny.

Some departments indeed act without waiting for the premier's directive. After the Guangdong provincial government officially announced the "May Day" holiday plan, Hangzhou, Suzhou, and Nanjing proposed to jointly restore the May Day Golden Week, making the issue of whether to restore the May Day Golden Week a focus of discussion.

In response to the current heated discussions, the General Office of the State Council officially responded: "Do not arbitrarily restore the May Day Golden Week."

Should the total number of working days in a year be unified for the whole country? Although some comments refer to Germany's central government only stipulating some unified statutory holidays and allowing other holidays to be determined by each state, proposing that "May Day" holidays could be decided by local governments, have they considered whether it suits China's national conditions under different political systems?

In fact, I just want to ask, why did various places choose to call out like this without reporting to the General Office for approval?

[3] Savings that every province could achieve

The case of a Yunnan police officer shooting and killing someone has recently attracted public attention due to an agreement. The Mengzi County Public Security Bureau reached a reconciliation agreement with the victim's family, paying a substantial compensation of 550,000 yuan to the victim's family.

Regarding the case of police officer Ji Zhongchun shooting and killing someone, the Mengzi County Public Security Bureau originally classified it as a personal act, unrelated to his identity or position as a police officer. Since it was a personal act, why did the Public Security Bureau pay the compensation? Deputy Director Zhao Xiangrong of the Mengzi County Public Security Bureau explained it like this:

"The reason the County Public Security Bureau paid this amount is considering Ji Zhongchun's specific circumstances. Ji Zhongchun is an old police officer who has worked in the County Public Security Bureau for more than 20 years, with poor economic conditions at home. Out of humanitarianism, the County Public Security Bureau gave a prior humanitarian compensation to the deceased's family. And this 550,000 yuan came from the saved office expenses of the County Public Security Bureau."

It is well known that this incident occurred because Ji Zhongchun had a dispute with someone while reversing his car. How can a police officer who owns a private car be defined as having poor economic conditions? This makes one weakly ask: what exactly constitutes good economic conditions?

According to Article 14 of the Regulations on the Use of Police Equipment and Weapons by the People's Police, if a police officer illegally uses weapons, causing unnecessary casualties or property damage, the relevant agency to which the police officer belongs shall compensate according to the relevant provisions of the National Compensation Law. Why doesn't the Mengzi Public Security Bureau admit that this compensation is national compensation? If it indeed isn't, why does the Public Security Bureau use taxpayers' money for "private gain"?

[6] Overhyped internet entrepreneurship

Suddenly, as if overnight spring breeze blows, millions of online stores sprout everywhere. In the current economic crisis, the internet seems to become an important way to solve employment problems. Not only do media outlets repeatedly promote online employment for students as a great path, but even during the Two Sessions, representatives suggested that the state should subsidize college students starting businesses on Taobao.

Low cost, low investment, low risk, internet entrepreneurship suddenly became a darling, with many domestic colleges opening online store training classes to help students start businesses. Yes, if everyone now follows the trend and opens online stores, the employment problem will be immediately solved, but what about a year later, ten years later? How many people can truly succeed? Let alone issues like medical insurance and social security. Network security and legal standardization have always been the most neglected difficulties in the tide of information economy. While our experts and professors advocate encouraging college students to choose internet entrepreneurship, have they considered this point?
