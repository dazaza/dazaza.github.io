Have you seen the pink limited edition iPhone 5S? It's going to sell for 18,000 yuan.

by anonymous on 2013-11-17 20:27:41

Are you sitting in front of your computer worrying about what to get your girlfriend for her birthday? Perhaps the product featured in today's pictorial review can help you solve this rather tricky problem. It's the iPhone 5s. From the article's title and the first picture, you can tell that this isn't an ordinary iPhone 5s; it's a specially customized product. Its color isn't Northeast Silver, nor is it High-end Black or even Gold for the Rich—it's Romantic Pink.

Apple hasn't officially released a fourth color for the iPhone 5s, so what's the deal with this phone?

This is a product newly crafted by ZG Company (full name: ZG HIGH TICKET ITEM CO.). PChome has already published multiple articles about the company's products. This Romantic Pink iPhone 5s is a new version recently launched by the company, following the Gold Edition and Rose Gold Edition, offering consumers who seek uniqueness another brand-new choice.

When I first got the box, no one could guess that it was actually a phone packaging. The sense of nobility is evident in every detail. This box weighs over 5 jin (approximately 2.5 kg).

On the corner of the packaging box, there's the full name of ZG Company, ZG HIGH TICKET ITEM CO., where the craftsmanship begins to show—extremely exquisite.

After opening the packaging box, the usual feeling of simplicity and grandeur remains. Besides the phone, there's also a metal plaque unique to each phone, engraved with relevant information about this phone, including its serial number and code, etc.

Turning it on and activating it, I won't go into much detail about this process. iOS7 operating system—by now, the debate over flat design has basically subsided. Perhaps everyone has argued enough, gotten tired, and started to accept or must accept whether it looks good or not.

As for the front camera, there’s nothing special to say. The Romantic Pink iPhone 5s is custom-made from a white Hong Kong edition iPhone 5s. If you don’t look closely, it’s almost identical to the Northeast Silver and Gold for the Rich versions of the iPhone 5s, except for the color difference at the edges, which we’ll talk about later.

If there’s anything different on the front other than the frame, it’s this specially customized metal ring. Functionally, it still serves as fingerprint unlocking, with sensitivity completely consistent with the official Apple version. What’s different is something on it—what exactly?

At first, we thought the craftsmanship of this metal ring was pretty rough. It felt uneven to the touch, like a product from a small workshop. But upon closer inspection, almost everyone was shocked. It turns out that on the fingerprint unlock ring of the customized Romantic Pink iPhone 5s, there’s a circle of extremely fine English letters, still bearing the full name of ZG Company: ZG HIGH TICKET ITEM CO. These words after stamping are very, very small, such that many people can barely see them with the naked eye, requiring macro lenses or magnifying glasses to photograph.

After finishing talking about the refined front, next comes its personalized back, which is its biggest selling point: Romantic Pink.

The bottom part of the back of the phone contains relevant information.

The power button and lock button at the top are made of 24K rose gold, including the side buttons which are also 24K rose gold, playing a great decorative role overall.

Compared to ordinary iPhones, the Apple logo on the back of the ZG customized iPhone 5s also uses stamping technology, making the logo and related information physically pressed onto the phone, greatly enhancing the phone's texture.

Besides the back cover, the sides of the phone are also a light pink.

The Nano SIM card slot is also made of 24K rose gold material.

There are no signs of use on the bottom microphone and data cable port.

The physical buttons on the side, though customized, still feel the same as the official version, very crisp.

The metal plate lists some information about this Romantic Pink iPhone 5s, marked with "Hong Kong." The total quantity is a limited edition of 200 units, and I'm fortunate to have secured the first unit. The IMEI number on the metal plate matches perfectly with the internal one of the phone.

Opening the first layer of the partition reveals the accessories for the Romantic Pink iPhone 5s. Everything the official version has, this does too, and some things it doesn't have, this does.

ZG Company provides exclusive leather protective cases for the recently launched customized iPhone 5s. The feel is excellent. The color matches the body color. This one is Romantic Pink, so the case is also pink. Because the metallic body tends to reflect light during shooting, the pink sometimes shows color variation, but overall, it's a light pink, basically the same as the protective case's color, which can be used as a reference.

Here's a family portrait of the accessories and phone. The power plug for the Hong Kong edition, headphones, and data cables have no issues.

Just looking at the pink version might leave some wanting more, so here are some comparison photos of the Romantic Pink, Gold for the Rich, and Northeast Silver versions of the iPhone 5s.

The front of these three versions is all white, so apart from the fingerprint unlock ring on the Home button, the other parts are basically indistinguishable. Observant users may have noticed that one iPhone 5s carrier reads China Mobile 3G. There are many online tutorials for unlocking, so I'll cut to the chase—this phone was unlocked by our editor. Netizens interested in the internet speed experience can follow PChome's articles.

In contrast, the customized metal ring with English engravings clearly looks much cooler.

Comparison of the back colors of the three versions. Compared to the previously introduced Gold and Rose Gold versions of the iPhone 5s, this Romantic Pink version obviously exudes more vitality. Although it doesn't give off the impression of being rich, it's filled with more energy.

Design of the speaker at the bottom. Here's a quick reminder for netizens: the speakers on Apple iPhones only have one speaker producing sound, while the other is a microphone. Some users were unclear about this issue, so I'll briefly mention it—it's not broken.

While taking pictures, a colleague asked me if the pink on this iPhone 5s was a film or if it was plated after purchase? Actually, neither. As far as I know, these customized iPhone 5s units are reprocessed from official iPhone 5s models, with the back covers entirely replaced through several processes such as replacement and stamping. Just from the metal-stamped logo and text on the back cover, you can tell that this isn't the original back cover that was simply painted or stickered.

Let's get back to the main subject—the Romantic Pink iPhone 5s. How much does this phone cost? The answer is: 2999 USD. Converted directly into RMB, it's around 18,000 RMB. To be honest, for ordinary people, this price is quite steep. However, compared to the previous Rose Gold and Gold editions of the iPhone 5s priced at 35,000 RMB, it's much cheaper.

Definitely, someone will ask who would buy such an expensive phone? Indeed, most people wouldn't buy it, but there are exceptions. There are three categories: one is for gifting, pursuing its price rather than its entire value; the second is for girlfriends, as mentioned in the article's title. For those with ample funds, if they can give their girlfriends a unique phone that they carry around, they'll surely be thrilled; the third category consists of true "tycoons." For these people, when money is merely a number, buying anything might just be for fun.

It is understood that this customized version of the iPhone 5s sells well in places like Hong Kong and Macau, generally in a state of high demand. This is due to tight supply and strong demand. But relative to mainlanders, most iPhone users mainly focus on domestic or grey market versions. As for these customized versions, enjoying the viewing pleasure is enough, so I won't include any purchasing links.
