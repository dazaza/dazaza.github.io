How to Select an Electronic Watch?

There are two types of electronic watches: quartz electronic watches and fully electronic quartz watches. The former has the appearance of traditional mechanical watches, such as Tissot electronic watches, Longines electronic watches, Casio electronic watches, etc. These contain about 50% mechanical components, use gear transmission, and display time with hands, so they are also called pointer-type electronic watches. The latter has a novel design, is entirely made of electronic components, and displays time digitally, thus called digital electronic watches. The surface of an electronic watch is flat, smooth, and highly transparent. The coating on the watchcase is bright without defects or peeling. For pointer-type quartz electronic watches, pull out the crown, and the second hand should immediately stop moving with its tip precisely aligned with the scale; when the hands are moved forward or backward, the second hand should remain stationary; after pushing the crown back to reset (for 3 seconds), the second hand should resume ticking; it moves one notch every 3 seconds, indicating that the watch is functioning correctly. If it moves two notches each time or there's excessive shaking during movement, these are abnormal phenomena. When the battery is installed and the watch is working, place it near your ear and carefully listen to the sound it emits, which should be even and crisp. If the sound is irregular in speed or intensity, it indicates poor quality. For selecting digital quartz electronic watches:

The display panel should not have obvious scratches, and the edges should not have large blackened areas; the displayed numbers should be clear, with greater contrast being better, and the numbers should not flicker noticeably; all buttons should operate smoothly and flexibly, neither too loose nor too tight. Place the LCD panel under direct sunlight or within 5 cm of a vertical strong light from a bulb over 60W, and observe its display. If the display disappears after exposure to light, the liquid crystal quality is substandard. Check each digit for seconds, minutes, hours, days, and months slowly to ensure their strokes are complete and there’s no missing or wrong number phenomenon. Press the illumination button to connect the power source of the miniature bulb. If the bulb shines brightly white, it indicates that the bulb is good and the battery voltage is sufficient. If the bulb appears dim red with yellow, it suggests insufficient battery power or issues with the bulb. Generally, most electronic watches have functions to display month, day, hour, minute, and second, and some also include features like displaying the day of the week, stopwatch counting, alarm timing, and solar power supply. Usually, having five functions—month, day, hour, minute, and second—is enough to meet needs. Researchers, sportspeople, and medical workers can choose ones with stopwatch counting functionality, while those who travel frequently or need timely reminders can opt for models with alarm timing functions. When purchasing an electronic watch, first inspect its appearance carefully to check for any collision damage, scratch marks, spots, electroplating peeling, or misalignment issues with the crown, buttons, dial, and back cover. For pointer-type electronic watches, pulling out the crown should immediately stop the second hand, with its tip precisely aligned with the scale. When moving the hands forward or backward, the second hand should remain stationary; after pushing the crown back for one second, the second hand resumes ticking. An accurate watch will have the second hand move one notch at a time. If it moves two notches each time or shakes excessively during movement, it indicates a quality issue. For digital electronic watches, the display panel should not have obvious stains or large blackened areas at the edges. The displayed numbers should be clear, with greater contrast being better, and there shouldn’t be severe flickering. Additionally, check each digit for seconds, minutes, hours, days, and months slowly to ensure their strokes are complete and there’s no missing or wrong number phenomenon. Furthermore, the buttons should operate smoothly and flexibly, neither too loose nor too tight.
