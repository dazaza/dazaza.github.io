How to Use Google to Find Customers for Foreign Trade - 16 Methods

Using search engines to find customers is a skill that foreign trade personnel must master. Many newcomers in foreign trade hope others will tell them how to use Google for searches. I believe that while learning techniques from others is certainly necessary, if one solely relies on methods summarized by others, it would be difficult to find effective customers. Even if some are found, they would face significant competition because everyone uses these methods to look for customers. Therefore, I think learning to use search engines to find customers should start from two aspects: one is learning the help instructions of the search engine itself and keeping an eye on new technology applications; the other is analyzing customer search habits. The former is about learning search engine usage techniques, while the latter is about practical application techniques. The former can be improved through systematic study, while the latter requires understanding common methods first, then practicing personally, especially learning combined search methods. Regardless of whether it's Google, Baidu, Bing, Yahoo, or any other search engine.

The following are common methods summarized by predecessors for using Google to search for customers.

How to Use Google to Find Customers for Foreign Trade - Method Two: Google National Website Searches

Method: Google.de search product translation word + email

If you have identified your product's end market, for example, your product performs well in Germany, then select Germany as your focus for development. The first method mentioned earlier can be used as a broad net cast, and now this method should be used for fine-tuning.

First, confirm the German Google, www.google.de, choose Google.de Use the following language: Deutsch

Then use Google Translate to translate your product and purchasing terms. For instance, bearing in German is Lager

You can try searching lager email first, then gradually add purchasing terms. If the websites found are in German and you don't understand, use the Google Translate tool to translate the webpage. As long as you find the correct email address, that's all you need.

How to Use Google to Find Customers for Foreign Trade - Method Three: Google Search for B2B Inquiry Emails

Method: Input the name, phone number, etc., of the procurement party into Google

In B2B, usually, you can't see the email of the procurement party; only basic contact information such as company name, phone, address, fax, contact person, and procurement products are visible. You can input these single or combined pieces of information into Google for a search, remember to add "email" to the search element. If you find the company's website, you can search "@abc.com" or add the contact person's name in Google to see which one is most suitable.

Specific methods I've written before, see: How to Develop Customers Using Expired Inquiries

How to Use Google to Find Customers for Foreign Trade - Method Four: Google Search Competitors (Peers)

Method: Search competitors' company names or phone numbers

Search the company names of domestic competitors or foreign peers on Google to see where your peers appear. You'll find peers of your peers or their customers in these websites, perhaps they are your potential customers (search both the full company name and the abbreviation).

Additionally, search the phone, fax, email, mobile phone, even the boss's name and mobile phone number of your peers. You'll discover significant business opportunities (when searching for the boss's or purchaser's name or mobile phone number, you can add "PDF"). Just try it, and you'll know the effect.

How to Use Google to Find Customers for Foreign Trade - Method Five: Google Search Famous Buyers in the Industry

Method: Same as above, if you know certain famous buyers in the industry, search their company names and emails thoroughly to see if there are any business opportunities.

How to Use Google to Find Customers for Foreign Trade - Method Six: Google Search New and Old Customer Emails

Search the emails of foreign customers (including old customer emails and new customer emails) on Google. You receive new inquiries every day; try searching these customers' emails directly on Google. (You can also search the customer's mobile phone number or full name + company abbreviation together.)

How to Use Google to Find Customers for Foreign Trade - Method Seven: Google Search Giant Industry Distributors

Google finds giants in the industry not to do business with them, but to find their distributors, who helps them distribute products. Can these distributors be developed? Can they act as agents for our products?

Method: Search the brand abbreviations of the industry giants, or add distributor, wholesale, or reseller afterward.

How to Use Google to Find Customers for Foreign Trade - Method Eight: Google Search Regional Chamber of Commerce and Industry Association Members

Industry associations: Association, Alliances, Bureau, Council, Institute, Society, Guild

You can input your industry or product words + the above chamber association names in Google to find members' emails. If there are no emails, search the company name (full name or abbreviation) in Google again to find the email.

How to Use Google to Find Customers for Foreign Trade - Method Nine: Google Search Industry Exhibition Members

Same method as above, enter known exhibition websites to find participating companies, search their names in Google to find emails.

If you want to know more exhibitions, you can check on exhibition websites or Google search exhibition websites. Product words or industry words + exhibition, trade show, fair can all be combined. www.eventseye.com is directly searched by inputting Trade Shows directory.

How to Use Google to Find Customers for Foreign Trade - Method Ten: Google Search eBay Sellers

Sellers on eBay are your potential customers. You can wholesale products to them and let them sell for you.

Try Google searching inurl:ebay bearing email or inurl:ebay bearing, which can search out links to different national versions of eBay websites. Of course, you can also open different national eBay websites one by one to search for your products and contact those sellers. Links to various national eBay websites can be found at the bottom of www.ebay.com homepage.

How to Use Google to Find Customers for Foreign Trade - Method Twelve: Google Search B2C/C2C Sellers shopping or shop

You can combine the above methods with Google web search, and separately search products or combined searches in Google Image, Google Video, Google Shopping.

How to Use Google to Find Customers for Foreign Trade - Method Fourteen: Google Find B2B Opportunities

To find B2B opportunities, you can input B2B-related words such as Manufacturers, exporters, suppliers, buyers, sellers, buy, marketplace, B2B, trade, products, portal, etc., in Google to search and find a series of B2B websites to see if there are any business opportunities.

If you want to find B2B websites in a particular country or continent, you can add the country prefix or continent name to the search combination, remembering to use three different ways to search, such as Canada, Canadian, CA, China, Chineses.
