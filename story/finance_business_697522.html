[Repost] A Brief Discussion on the Issues in Modern Parent-Child Education

by koucai4472 on 2012-03-08 16:04:12

China has long had a tradition: raising sons to support parents in old age. Especially the older generation, most of them probably have this kind of thought. Nowadays, many parents still think this way. Having children does not necessarily mean giving up many things. Wise parents indeed and should expect some repayment from their children, which is natural. It's not about making them overly express gratitude for their birth and upbringing, but rather to willingly consider accepting the standards and ideals of their parents with deep affection. Parents want their children to possess these qualities not only selfishly for themselves, but also because they want their children to be able to cooperate with others and live happily when they grow up.

Many parents are like this; what they want is for their children to repay them for raising them. To put it bluntly, you're just treating your children as an item or accessory. Many parents have this mistaken understanding, which is why there are so many issues with parent-child relationships. Because their communication capital comes from the grace of raising their children, from this little "thing" in parent-child education. In fact, this matter can be seen as small or big. Because raising children is your responsibility. What kind of person the child grows into largely depends on the parents' responsibilities. If parents understand educational methods, then naturally they can raise an excellent child, and vice versa.

Therefore, parents must pay attention to their educational methods during the process of educating their children. They cannot rush it. A child's growth cannot be accomplished overnight. On the path of a child's growth, parents should be like a lighthouse guiding the child in the right direction. This involves the issue of educational methods. Many parents use corporal punishment to educate and guide their children. I must admit that corporal punishment has its place in our society, but it is no longer the mainstream method of education. Therefore, parents must be aware of this point during the educational process.

When parents are too hesitant to demand reasonable behavior from their children because they misunderstand the theory of self-expression, or because they have been practicing self-sacrifice all along, or because they fear their children will dislike them, resentment towards bad behavior inevitably builds up. They increasingly feel angry inside and don't know what to do. Children also become troubled by this. It makes them feel guilty and panicked, and it even makes them more selfish and demanding. For example, if babies like staying up late and the parents are afraid to refuse their wish, the baby may turn into an unpleasant tyrant within months, wanting parents to walk around with them for hours. If parents were encouraged to strictly require good behavior from their children, both the children and parents would become gentle and amiable, and this transformation could happen surprisingly fast.

In other words, if parents cannot make their children's behavior reasonable, they will eventually fail to develop proper feelings for their children, and children are only happy when their behavior is reasonable.
