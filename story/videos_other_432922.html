TV series "Crazy Racing" released in mainland, audience reaction "worth watching" - Chinese films

by letaoehv on 2011-05-18 16:19:58

Because the previous 50-minute version of "Crazy Racing" was shown, some media worried that there were too many clues and the plot was too complicated, which might make it hard for the audience to understand. And at the premiere held in Shanghai Huan Yi Cinema the other night, some viewers who had watched "Crazy Stone" five times also proposed that they felt "Racing" was easier to understand than "Stone". Regarding this, Ning Hao said: I feel that the audience is growing up, and I am growing up with them. He explained that the audience who watched "Stone" has grown up now, while "Racing" will attract a new group of young audiences who are curious about such films, thus broadening the age range of his audience.

In "Crazy Racing", Huang Bo was promoted by Ning Hao to be the leading actor. And in the upcoming "No Man's Land" to be filmed in March, Ning Hao also chose Huang Bo as the first leading actor. Finding a good cooperative actor is not easy. We need our own works, our own stars. And local stars still need our own films to promote them. Why did Ning Hao choose Huang Bo? Ning Hao used a series of adjectives: Because he is as vulgar as me, as unreliable as me, not elite, not cultured, all common people, with the demeanor of ordinary people. Is it intended to form the best partnership like Feng Xiaogang and Ge You with Huang Bo? Ning Hao said that when he and Huang Bo have dinner, they often talk about this topic. The conclusion is that there is still a big gap between them and those two.

Ning Hao's new work, "Crazy Racing", was previewed for 50 minutes in various places across the country. Although the story was not finished, the more compact and complex plot setting compared to "Crazy Stone" has already made the audience who got a sneak peek call it worth watching. Yesterday (January 20), "Crazy Racing" was officially released nationwide on the peak day of half-price screenings on Tuesday. The box office situation on the first day still has a distance from the earlier released "Red Cliff (Part II)" and "Pleasant Goat and Big Big Wolf: Year of the Ox", but there are already quite a few old audiences who have watched "Stone" specifically going to the cinema to watch "Racing".
