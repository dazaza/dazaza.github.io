Yujingyong, who made his name in Beijing after starting out in Mianyang, Sichuan, announced yesterday via Weibo that he had given employees their year-end bonuses early. The girl who received a six-figure bonus was one of the lowest-paid employees receiving a year-end bonus. This Weibo post drew countless envious comments from netizens.

Is this news true? Who exactly is Yujingyong? In fact, the chairman of the board of directors of China Generations Investment Group first gained notoriety due to the Tang Jun academic scandal, when he supported Tang Jun as an alumnus of the American University of the Pacific. Subsequently, various media exposed him as a capital game master, skilled at empty-handed schemes.

A minimum six-figure year-end bonus?

As the New Year approaches, year-end bonuses are undoubtedly the topic everyone is most concerned about. At 1:38 PM yesterday afternoon, Yujingyong proudly displayed his company's year-end bonus: Today, I gave the employees their year-end bonuses early. I told them to fully share the company's profits. After the New Year holiday, we will take our annual leave, just like previous years, resuming work on the sixteenth day of the first lunar month. Everyone cheered and spread the word; a young girl cried, so I asked her if she thought the year-end bonus was too little? She said she had already received a six-figure amount, which was more than she expected, so she was moved to tears. The HR director quietly told me she was the one who received the least.

Yesterday, this Weibo post about the year-end bonus immediately attracted a lot of attention from netizens. Many netizens commented: The amount of the year-end bonus even exceeds my annual salary, making me cry. More people asked: Is your company still hiring? For a time, China Generations Investment Group seemed highly sought after by many white-collar workers because of its enviable benefits and vacation time.

However, many netizens also questioned whether Yujingyong was self-promoting. Netizen Wang Suixin_11 believed that Yujingyong was very good at marketing, and the speed and breadth of Weibo's dissemination could make more people interested in learning about this company. By 6 PM yesterday, the Weibo post had been forwarded over 27,000 times and received over 7,000 comments.

Once became famous through Tang Jun

The reporter from Huaxi Metropolis Daily privately messaged Yujingyong to verify the authenticity of this matter. However, by the deadline last night, no response was received from him. There is also no way to know how many people work at China Generations Investment Group or how much profit it makes.

Baidu Baike describes Yujingyong as follows: Male, Chairman of the Board of Directors of China Generations Investment Group. Doctorate in Business Administration from the American University of the Pacific, and an alumnus of Tang Jun. However, as netizens put it, Yujingyong is someone who rose to prominence by associating with Tang Jun.

On July 1st, Fang Zhouzi revealed the academic scandal on Weibo, causing public outrage. Subsequently, an unexpected alumnus of the American University of the Pacific, Yujingyong, suddenly appeared. Riding on Tang Jun's wave, Yujingyong managed to make himself well-known within half a month.

In July 2006, Yujingyong established China Generations Investment Holdings Group Co., Ltd. in Hong Kong, claiming a registered capital of 1 billion yuan and support from 13 overseas funds. In 2008, Yujingyong boldly announced in Chongqing an investment of 44 billion yuan for the overall development of the Jiasheng Town in Dadukou District. However, the final results showed that he was actually a capital game master, skilled at empty-handed schemes. In July 2010, official sources pointed out that Tang Jun's alumnus Yujingyong had forged land certificates.

September - 5 million yuan worth of prizes for fans

Hosted a Weibo fan meeting and rewarded fans with properties in Beijing, Mercedes-Benz cars, luxury watches, etc. Netizens estimated that the total value of these prizes provided by Yujingyong was approximately 5 million yuan.

June - Boasting with executives of state-owned enterprises

This afternoon, playing golf with shareholders and executives of state-owned enterprises at Feitebao Golf Course, drinking Maotai wine and eating exotic meats in the evening, enjoying a leisurely life.

May - Mocking poor people participating in politics

The cost of a meal for oneself is income they might earn in a year or even many years. Imagine, can someone who can't even afford a meal give advice to the country? Isn't this harmful to the country and the people?
