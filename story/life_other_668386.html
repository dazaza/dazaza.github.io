What Cannot Be Owned in Life

Submitting online

Love songs always like to use the loss of him to indicate a breakup. I've written them, it's a sin.

How can one person think that by love or marriage they have the ability to own another person?

Loving someone, you can own many gifts that are hard to part with, because they are things that anyone, including yourself, can buy in the market, and because of love, they are elevated into works of art, even to the point of buying another Louis Vuitton suitcase to cherish them.

Loving someone, you can jointly own a house, a bed, even a Schnauzer, and eventually a child. Oh, sorry, a dog, a child, you pay the price and put in the effort, but they have their own lives. The greatest achievement is just to derive happiness from them. Anything with life, no matter how you try to control or gaze at them with a thousand hands and eyes, they all have the right to grow and change. Your brain may follow your instructions and let your hands embrace, but it cannot command them to do certain actions for you. They are not stocks; the highest dividend is merely sharing a beautiful era with them, whether long or short, and there is no ability to hold onto them.

Loving someone, even the appearance of a certain character from their name in a book or newspaper stirs the soul, and some might even go so far as to write that name on a sweaty window with their finger. But his name comes from Cangjie, composed into the false names of billions of people, not even he himself has exclusive rights to it, so what do you really own? Loving his eyes, ears, mouth, nose, loving his voice and smell, loving to the extreme, yet you cannot own his childhood truancy and mahjong-playing past, the caressing after being intimate with a former lover, or the scars left from playing football with good friends. You cannot own his ID card, his profession, or the continuous changes in his growth. Each individual's journey can only be exchanged through love, shared memories turned into recollections, and these recollections are so abstract that nothing abstract can be placed in a safe.

Loving someone, don't easily claim ownership. After sweet words and honeyed phrases, you can own a bowl of old fire soup clumsily cooked by his hands, which tastes bad but warm, and can be swallowed. I want to swallow you, it sounds romantic on the pillow, but when landing back down to earth, who would willingly be swallowed by whom?

Love is never bigger than the sky, no one belongs to anyone else. Even if you lustfully fall in love with a statue-like beauty who can walk and talk, you can touch, hug, and walk alongside them, their molecular composition is still a coincidence with all beings, having happened to relate to you.

It's hard to love, but easy to scare away a heart. If you try to possess everything about him, he will naturally suddenly fear losing everything, yearning for his own independent world, because he is an independent being. Wanting too much love and losing it is an ironic phenomenon of love, yet it happens every day, becoming unreported real-life stories.

In the end, what is "having," and what is "not having"? The University of Hong Kong has a building named Ren Bai Building, but the students attending classes there do not belong to Ren Bai. We donate our love, we should expect no return, any expectation creates a burden, how dare we then speak of possession? The best possible outcome is simply gaining happiness. But happiness is not a physical thing; even the tightest embrace is still intangible. What is most tangible is perhaps our more illusory hearts. Only the mind can collect memories, enjoy happiness. All phenomena arise from the mind.

If love is difficult to hold in your hand, why not place it in your heart?

Postscript:

After reading this, I originally wanted to write something, but every time I put pen to paper, I find the original text expresses it so precisely, everything I want to say seems to already be there. But similarly, each love is different, where are the universal truths that apply everywhere? And even if there are, the protagonists differ from the observers.

It's hard to love, it's hard to hate. Knowing it cannot be attained, how many can gracefully miss it? Love is like a swamp, once you're stuck, it's hard to crawl out, often the more you struggle, the muddier you become.

Indeed, "We donate our love, expecting no return." Gaining and not gaining should be equally persistent, loving for love's sake. Lin Xi said: If love is difficult to hold in your hand, why not place it in your heart? In fact, if love truly resides in the heart, must it necessarily also be in the hand...

Therefore, waiting love, the beauty of a solo act exists in novels, but rarely in reality.

Love remains an unread philosophy until today.
