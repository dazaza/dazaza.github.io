The world's most famous website for selling diplomas

by fearless on 2009-03-16 04:36:49

Who would be so "stupid" to register such a long domain name? http://www.themostfamouswebsiteonearth.com. In fact, this domain name is quite impressive and very cool.

The Most Famous Website On Earth (the world's most famous website) - some friends might say this is too arrogant, purely a joke, and they may just laugh it off. This can't help but remind me of how some acquaintances have evaluated and advised me to tone down a bit and not be too arrogant. But how should one put it well? Arrogance is just the surface appearance. If someone judges a person or thing based on superficial aspects, I think it’s better not to deal with such a person because he is truly dumb enough. I once said that thinking is like a mirror; most expressions are judgments of others, but in reality, they are self-reflections! Of course, my friends advise me for my own good. They know me pretty well and are worried that I might get hurt.

The world's most famous website – naturally fools wouldn’t delve deeply into it, and I am even more foolish than them as I actually spent time pondering over this little site. We all know that the most famous website isn't this one; this title is undoubtedly self-proclaimed by them. However, what's quite funny is that users can register themselves and bestow themselves with the title of "the world's greatest," and they will generate an online certificate and PDF file for you, such as the handsomest, strongest, and if you write "the world's most despicable," no one opposes it. Let your imagination run wild!

Some friends might say, what's strange about this? Certificate generation websites are much stronger than this "world's worst." Most people who are "well-versed" on the internet would think this way, but such thoughts are naive and silly. "The world's most famous website" is much more advanced and noble than those fun certificate generation sites.

On "The world's most famous website," you need to spend 1 dollar to get a certificate, an additional 1 dollar to change the font on the certificate, 3 dollars to add your photo, and the application deadline is December 23rd of this year. Huh, they charge money? And restrict my time? Why should I generate it here? I definitely won't use this website. But if the fees you pay are used for charity, would you consider it necessary to spend money to buy the title of "the world's greatest"? You guessed it right. "The world's most famous website" is a charity site. 80% of the revenue from selling "honors" will be donated to a charity organization on December 23, 2009, and this charity organization must be "the world's most famous charity organization." Of course, at this point, being the most famous is real. When you purchase a certificate, there will be a voting option, with 14 charity organizations listed, and you choose the one you think is the most famous.

In addition to customizing titles, the site will also award you the title of "as one of the most famous people on earth in the year 2009". Why would you be one of the most famous people in the world in 2009? Because you are passionate about charitable causes.

Accumulating virtue and educating through entertainment, what a brilliant idea! The site administrators are organizing a marketing campaign called Kids Be Famous, targeting primary and secondary schools across the United States. Of course, it’s not about making kids pay, but rather the schools paying. Why should schools necessarily give the money spent on certificates to ordinary merchants? Isn't it better to give it to companies engaged in charitable causes? I think no one should refuse such a good thing for any reason.

To tell you the truth, "the world's most famous website" is trash and doesn't even deserve to be called creative. It's just that they thought of it and dared to do it, working hard to pragmatically realize an ultra-"terrible" idea that doesn't even qualify as an idea. Who could not be impressed?
