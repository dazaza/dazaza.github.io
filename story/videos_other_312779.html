30th Birthday + 300 Premier League Appearances = A Forlorn Owen; Capello Can No Longer Tolerate Sir Alex

Sina Sports - The day before yesterday, Owen celebrated his 30th birthday, and the former Golden Boy stepped into his thirties this week. Today, Owen also marked his 300th career appearance in the Premier League, although Sir Alex only gave him 15 minutes of playtime.

In the 71st minute, Obertan was substituted off for Welbeck, the player who provided Owen with the most assists thus resting off the field. Welbeck, who came on, preferred to work alone. In the 76th minute, Owen finally came on, replacing Rooney, who had scored the first goal for Manchester United in this match. Rooney was unhappy as he walked off the pitch, without any interaction with Owen. However, Owen still proactively went up to congratulate Rooney with a high-five, though Rooney never even glanced at his former national team golden partner.

In the 82nd minute, Evra crossed from the left flank, and Owen attempted a header ahead of Hannemann, the goalkeeper of Wolves, but it sailed over the bar. This was Owen's only shot of the game. Nevertheless, in that instant, we could still feel Owen's sharp sense around the goal. In the 85th minute, Valencia sent a cross from the right flank, and Owen received the ball at the edge of the penalty area, quickly passing it to Welbeck on the left flank before running into the box to follow up. However, Welbeck decided to shoot directly, and his weak attempt was easily saved by Hanneemann. Berbatov waved in dissatisfaction with Welbeck's decision, while Owen silently walked away with his head down. In the 87th minute, Valencia curled a pass with his right foot, and Owen controlled it with his chest inside the box but couldn't turn due to the close marking of a defender, eventually being forced out of the box by the center-back... Throughout his 14 minutes on the field, Owen’s statistics were very meager: seven passes all accurate, five precise forward passes, one shot which missed the target.

Without Obertan's assistance, without starting, without scoring, and without hearing the Happy Birthday song sung by the crowd of 73,709, Owen thus concluded his 300th Premier League appearance. It seems like his chances of getting on the plane to South Africa are growing slimmer. However, it appears that what is truly preventing Owen from flying to South Africa is Sir Alex Ferguson, not Fabio Capello. Capello subtly expressed his discontent with Ferguson's arrangements, especially since Owen has only started three times in the first 17 league games this season.

Capello hasn't forgotten about Owen; instead, the Italian has been quietly observing him. This can be easily inferred from Capello's pre-match comments: "Owen is one of the players I am watching. The last time I watched him play was on TV during the Champions League where he scored three goals, but in the next game, he didn't start. For this reason, I often ask myself why? Why does Rooney always start while Owen doesn’t? He is one of England's most important players."

England legend Gary Lineker also strongly recommended that Capello take Owen to South Africa: "In my view, the logic is simple: if Owen can play, he will play for Manchester United. If he can play for Manchester United, he can score. Owen's movement and ability to find space near the penalty spot are better than any of his competitors, despite Defoe's significant improvement in that area this season. When English players see Owen in the dressing room — the winner of the European Footballer of the Year award, a star on the World Cup stage, Germany's nightmare — every single one of them will become fearless."

However, within Manchester United, Owen is not the only one worrying about his dream of participating in the World Cup. Similar to Owen's situation is Hargreaves. The Manchester United midfielder has been sidelined for 14 months due to a knee injury. During this match, Hargreaves sat on the bench with O'Shea, and the camera caught several shots of his expressionless face. The Daily Mail analyzed that Hargreaves' delay in returning is entirely due to psychological reasons. Nevertheless, when Capello mentioned his situation, he offered him considerable hope.

"Two months ago, I spoke with him. I met him at the Manchester United club, where he was just about to start training, but he still can't play now. Why not (consider bringing him)? He has enough time to recover. Hargreaves needs to get back on the pitch. Players who don't play aren't in my consideration. He still has six months to regain his best form, but he needs to play. I will select players who are fit to play in May next year, those who can participate in all matches."
