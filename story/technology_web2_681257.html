Battle Magic God: Two men forged a certificate of the Culture Bureau to dismantle and steal a hundred-year-old stone bridge

by sznstejx3q3 on 2012-02-28 12:11:28

Early Morning Post reporter Chen Fang - A Qing Dynasty stone bridge, which is categorized as immovable cultural relics, was disassembled by criminals and transported to another place. In the Jinhai Community of Fengxian District, a well-preserved Qing Dynasty stone bridge caught the eye of a businessman dealing in stones. Despite the law, he spent 30,000 yuan to have the bridge transported to Suzhou for sale. Currently, the People's Procuratorate of Fengxian District is reviewing this case. It is estimated that it will cost nearly 500,000 yuan to restore the bridge.

The cultural relic bridge suddenly disappeared

On December 8, 2011, the Jinhai police station of Fengxian District received a report: a cultural relic bridge in Jinhai Community had gone missing. The person reporting the incident was a dedicated administrator of ground cultural relics from the Nangqiao Town Social Affairs Service Center of Fengxian.

After review by the People's Procuratorate of Fengxian District, the missing cultural relic bridge was located in Group 5 of Chenyi Village, Jinhai Community, Fengxian District. The central part of the bridge was inscribed with the words "Feng Le Bridge," spanning the Zhuli Port from east to west. The bridge was 17.5 meters long, 1.2 meters wide, and made of granite.

Yesterday, the Early Morning Post reporter observed at the scene that only two piers of the Feng Le Bridge remained, with signs of damage visible on the pier areas. Nearby were vegetable fields belonging to local villagers, and the area was rarely traversed by people.

According to Mr. Zhou, an elderly villager from Team Seven of the Chen family in Fengxian District, when he was born 60 years ago, the Feng Le Bridge already existed. "We all called it the Pawn Bridge because there used to be a pawnshop at the other end of the bridge." Mr. Zhou said that the Feng Le Bridge was once the necessary route for nearby villagers to cross the river. However, after a road was constructed nearby, no one used the bridge anymore.

Everyone now takes the main road because the bridge is somewhat narrow.

Upon verification by the Fengxian District Museum, the Feng Le Bridge was originally built in 1907. Before the theft, it was well-preserved. According to the provisions of the "Cultural Relics Protection Law" and the third national cultural relics census, this bridge was listed as immovable cultural relics and registered with the National Cultural Heritage Administration.

After receiving the report, the Fengxian police immediately launched an investigation. It was discovered that the cultural relic bridge had been targeted by Hong, a businessman dealing in stone materials. While passing through Fengxian, Hong noticed that this bridge "had some history" and decided to transport it away.

Forging a certificate from the Culture Bureau

In September 2011, Hong contacted Wang, who was engaged in landscaping business in Fengxian District, to discuss the "bridge transportation" matter. Hong promised Wang that if the "matter was settled," he would pay him 30,000 yuan. Hong said, "I saw that the stones of the bridge had patterns and were made of whole granite slabs. Selling them would definitely yield more than 30,000 yuan."

According to Hong's confession, to prevent inquiries during the "bridge transportation," they forged a certificate from the Culture Bureau of Fengxian District beforehand. Subsequently, Hong and Wang brought workers to the Feng Le Bridge. They first secured both ends of the bridge with steel cables and then used a crane to lift the body of the bridge. In total, they lifted 16 stone slabs, filling two trucks.

A few days later, Hong arranged for the bridge stones to be transported to Suzhou for sale. Before the sale could take place, Hong was apprehended by the police.

Currently, the Feng Le Bridge has yet to undergo restoration. However, according to estimates from garden architecture enterprises, the restoration cost of the Feng Le Bridge is close to 500,000 yuan.
