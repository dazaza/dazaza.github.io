Why do I repeat the introduction of prostatitis here? This is because spring is the high incidence period for male diseases, especially prostate diseases. How to avoid being troubled by diseases is the purpose of my introduction of health knowledge. I hope that male friends can patiently read my articles.

If you suffer from prostatitis, you must avoid smoking and drinking, and stay away from spicy food. Because repeated long-term congestion of the prostate is one of the causes of prostatitis. At the same time, whether it is bacterial prostatitis or non-bacterial prostatitis, the inflamed prostate tissue will be widely congested. Therefore, when preventing and treating prostatitis, it is very important to fully pay attention to prevent factors that may cause or increase prostate congestion. The following are examples:

1. Alcohol

Alcohol is a drink with vasodilatory effects. People often see the phenomenon that some people's faces turn red once they drink alcohol, which is the result of alcohol expanding facial blood vessels. Alcohol can also cause visceral organ congestion, and the prostate is no exception. Due to some young and middle-aged people having the habit of long-term drinking or even binge drinking, it is not easy to cure prostatitis, and even if it is cured, it is very easy to relapse.

2. Cigarettes

Smoking is harmful to physical health. Although people have some understanding of many hazards of smoking, they know little about how smoking can affect the prostate. In fact, nicotine, tar, nitrosamine, carbon monoxide and other toxic substances in cigarettes can not only directly poison prostate tissue, but also interfere with the nerve function that controls blood vessels, affecting the blood circulation of the prostate, and aggravating the congestion of the prostate.

3. Spicy Foods

Spicy foods such as green onions, raw garlic, peppers, and pepper can cause vasodilation and organ congestion. Some patients with prostatitis have the habit of eating spicy food. They can restrain themselves when the symptoms are severe, but when the symptoms ease, they return to their old ways. This is also an important reason why prostatitis lingers and is difficult to cure.
