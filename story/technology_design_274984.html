If you have acne that is easy to see, it must be treated scientifically. Under no circumstances should it be improperly handled.

1. Do not wash your face too often.

It is advisable for acne patients to wash their faces 2-3 times a day. Washing too frequently can stimulate the sebaceous glands to overproduce oil as the natural protective layer of the skin gets washed away. This will only lead to more acne.

2. Do not use scrub or astringent on acne.

Scrubs and astringents excessively irritate the epidermis and worsen inflamed skin conditions. They also stimulate the sebaceous glands to produce more oil, making the situation worse. Astringents may enlarge pores, which could exacerbate acne conditions.

3. Never pick, squeeze, or prick acne.

Each pimple has a natural life cycle of about 3-4 days. It will disappear naturally without intervention. Squeezing with hands or tools not only risks secondary infection from bacteria on your hands but can also cause bruising or permanent scars due to excessive pressure. Additionally, repeated stimulation caused by picking at pimples can result in raised scars.

4. Do not smoke.

Nicotine in cigarettes constricts capillary walls, causing toxins to accumulate in blood and lymph. This lowers the skin cells' oxygenation rate, weakening the skin's healing ability and making it harder to prevent cross-infections of acne wounds.

5. Avoid eating spicy, fried, and high-fat foods.

While these foods do not directly cause acne, they can worsen its condition. They increase the liver’s burden and lower the quality of vitamin K in the blood, indirectly triggering acne formation.

Acne is a chronic skin disease that occurs in hair follicles and sebaceous glands. Many patients stop treatment after some time because they don't see expected results and hope it heals itself. But can acne heal on its own?

Improper Handling of Acne Can Leave Scars

Early detection and treatment of acne and blackheads are crucial. Timely standard treatment can avoid or reduce damage to the skin. Some people think acne will naturally go away after adolescence and doesn’t need special treatment. This is a very wrong concept. According to authoritative reports, only about 12% of acne patients can recover on their own. In fact, improper treatment or handling of acne can worsen the condition, making future treatment difficult. It can even leave unsightly scars and pigmentation, bringing significant distress to young adults who are in their prime beauty years.

Washing Your Face Too Often Is Harmful to Treating Acne

Everyone hopes to have smooth and tender skin, and many are troubled by acne. In fact, finding the root cause, treating symptoms appropriately, combining internal and external treatments, and addressing both symptoms and causes make treating acne not so difficult. To treat acne, first change bad dietary habits; do not squeeze pimples with your hands as it easily triggers inflammation, worsens the condition, and leaves scars; pay attention to personal hygiene, but do not wash your face too often. Washing too often makes the skin oilier and enlarges pores, which is not beneficial for treating acne. The correct method is to wash your face twice daily with warm water to effectively clean the face and help sebum excretion.
