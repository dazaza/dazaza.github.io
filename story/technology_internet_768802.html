Baidu's Big Pivot: Internally Brewing a Comprehensive Platformization and Interface Orientation

by anonymous on 2013-11-22 12:02:21

This may be Baidu's biggest transformation in recent years.

In the past few days, "platformization and interface-ization" has been a hot topic among Baidu’s mid-to-upper level managers. This goal stems from Robin Li's internal strategic meeting instructions.

An insider from Baidu disclosed that currently, Baidu’s middle and upper management are conveying the spirit of "platformization and interface-ization" to their subordinates layer by layer. Each department is also undergoing learning sessions and beginning to propose corresponding thoughts and implementable KPIs (Key Performance Indicators). On Baidu's internal network, discussions on this topic have been quite intense.

Although this transformation might appear somewhat lofty and abstract, it indeed signals Baidu, as a traditional PC internet giant, releasing a comprehensive "open" signal internally.

I was fortunate enough to obtain the full text of Robin Li's speech at an internal executive meeting. The key points summarized from Li's speech are as follows:

1. Li hopes that within one to one and a half years, the entire company can transform into one with a platformized and interface-ized mindset.

2. In the PC era, the search ecosystem was gifted. Baidu was very lucky not needing to build its own ecosystem but could focus solely on improving technology.

3. With the advent of mobile internet, the closed mindset of relying solely on one's own technology to develop products has become outdated. Only by making Baidu's technology and services platformized and interface-ized, allowing anyone to connect equally and quickly, can it seize the innovation advantage in future competition.

4. A platform is accessible, equal and barrier-free for everyone, shared in benefits. Players on the platform should care more about whether relevant parts succeed than the platform itself.

5. Platforms can be both internal and external, but emphasize internal use more.

6. To build a good ecosystem, you need to operate with a service-oriented mindset and win-win ecological thinking, innovating operationally.

7. Platformization and interface-ization do not mean doing everything unlimitedly and freely for others without benefiting oneself. Baidu does not aim to be open just for openness' sake; it aims to create platforms and interfaces.

8. The platform mindset means that if I (Baidu) need something, I will give you (the developer) the requirements and ask you to do it. If I hope you do it faster, I will put pressure on you or let higher-level decision-makers in the company put pressure on you. It is not about you doing your own set of things or taking something and modifying it yourself.

After consulting insiders from Baidu and various seasoned experts, my analysis is as follows:

1. This is a choice that had to be made in the mobile internet era. Despite Baidu having various layouts like mobile search, maps, 91, etc., it is still difficult to replicate its absolute upstream monopoly position on PCs. Therefore, it needs to build its own ecosystem, attract more people to collaborate, deepen engagement, and truly become an irreplaceable giant in the mobile era, enabling scalable monetization.

2. This marks a shift in thinking for large Chinese internet companies. Veteran internet analyst Hongbo noted that Western platform thinking has a long history, hence companies like Facebook and Amazon. However, Chinese internet giants traditionally had a small-farm mindset, seeing a good product and making it themselves. But with time, their thinking has evolved. For instance, Baidu realized that after reaching a certain scale, only by relying on an ecosystem can it achieve long-term development.

3. Is this learning from Tencent? After clashing with 360 three years ago, Tencent started partially opening up and achieved good results. An insider from Baidu mentioned that Baidu's senior management is highly interested in Tencent's experience and has organized learning sessions to study Tencent's recent series of actions.

4. Internal interface-ization is also aimed at bridging communication gaps and breaking down silos within a large company. As a super-large company with over 20,000 employees, numerous business lines, departments, and "veterans," Baidu must use technical means to connect and collaborate internally.

5. Baidu is lowering its stance and starting to engage in service-oriented work such as laundry, cooking, and shoe shining. Li emphasized that when technology is not yet mature and standardized, operational effort is required. This indirectly says that in our less dominant phase, only by serving developers well can we build an ecosystem. It is rumored that the LBS division has already embraced this mindset. A developer mentioned choosing Baidu Maps over other map providers because of better service from Baidu's map department.

6. This is also for monetization. Li also said that platformization and interface-ization do not mean providing unlimited and free services to others. Although in the early stages of land grabbing, Baidu won't focus on revenue, once a real ecosystem is built, there will be possibilities for scalable monetization.

The following is the full text of Li Yanhong's internal speech (organized by relevant personnel at Baidu):

"During the last one or two Hackathon Black Horse Award lunches (Note: Baidu's internal programming marathon event where winners dine with Li), some colleagues proposed to me that they had some great ideas but lacked the necessary technology to implement them. I told them that we possess the technology they need, and it is globally leading, but he told me that even if we had it, how would he get access? People wouldn’t give it to him. This made me realize that while Baidu does have many technologies, there is no good mechanism within the company to ensure these technologies are fully utilized, severely affecting the speed and efficiency of company innovation. The solution to this problem is to make our technical platforms more platformized and interface-ized."

At the Q3 strategic communication meeting held on October 30th, Robin shared his thoughts on innovation over the past three months and proposed using platformization and interface-ization thinking to enhance innovation efficiency and establish an ecosystem. In the first ten years of Baidu's development, all technologies and products were in service of the big search engine because at that time, Baidu had a very clear goal — to create the best Chinese search engine. However, with the changing times, especially the advent of the mobile internet era, the closed mindset of relying solely on one's own technology to develop products has become outdated. Robin sees that only by making our technologies and services platformized and interface-ized, allowing anyone to connect equally and quickly, can we seize the innovation advantage in future competition.

Robin clearly defined a platform: a platform is accessible, equal and barrier-free for everyone, shared in benefits. Players on the platform should care more about whether relevant parts succeed than the platform itself. A platform can be both internal and external, but emphasizes internal use more. Robin pointed out that within the company, all technical departments are ITEs (Internal Technology Enablers), i.e., they provide technical services to other departments and product lines in a platform mode. Non-technical departments should similarly adopt a platform and interface mindset to improve and offer their services to other departments within the company.

Robin used Amazon as an example to illustrate this point. An article circulating online a couple of years ago was written by a Google engineer. He worked at Amazon for six years and then at Google for several years, so he understood the differences between Google's and Amazon's technologies. Even though Google is technically superior in almost every field compared to Amazon, there is one area where Amazon surpasses Google significantly — platformization and interface-ization, and not just slightly but overwhelmingly.

This is because Bezos strongly emphasized that all service interfaces must be designed from the ground up to be open. By adopting what seems to be an extreme full-open platform strategy, Amazon's technology utilization rate is very high and easily usable by others. AWS eventually became very successful. This is a successful example of technology platformization and interface-ization, and it is precisely because of this that Amazon has achieved today's success.

What are the criteria for the success of platformized operations? Robin pointed out that the most important factor is the ecosystem. In the PC era, the search ecosystem was naturally given. Baidu was very fortunate not needing to build its own ecosystem but could focus solely on improving technology. "However, even so, after more than a decade of development, we discovered some overlooked aspects, such as fake websites. In the past, we believed that a search engine only needed to bring users to related websites, and the quality of those websites was not the responsibility of the search engine. But from an ecological perspective, they are part of your upstream and downstream, and you must care about their quality. If you don't care, problems will arise in the ecosystem. Even if you are at the center of the ecosystem, you will inevitably be negatively affected. Therefore, we need to strengthen our ecological awareness. Many of the practices of the Big Search team over the past year have actually been aimed at finding better ways and the shortest path to meet user needs."

In the species-diverse mobile era, we need to build our own ecosystem. How to build it? The keyword is win-win. You need to make the players on the platform profitable and allow them to flourish, so they will care more about the success of the platform. If you don't build the platform with an ecological mindset, even if our own products reach the top industry traffic, if content providers cannot survive, they will naturally be unwilling to jointly build the platform, and Baidu will be unsustainable. Robin further informed everyone that to build a good ecosystem, we need to adopt a service-oriented and win-win ecological mindset and innovate operationally.

"Our video team has done a great job, with very rapid growth in daily active users. But there is one issue: after capturing videos from other sites, they removed the ads. I told them this is destructive to the ecosystem. They might feel satisfied, but other video websites do not benefit, and this is not sustainable in the long term. Later, they changed their approach. When we take videos, if there are ads, we keep the ads, and even if there are no ads, we can add ads and share the revenue with the video websites. This represents a change in mindset — what you do must not only benefit yourself but also benefit your upstream and downstream partners."

"In operations as well, for example, light applications (light apps). Before we introduced light apps, no application supported our system, so we had to build it ourselves and convince developers, websites, and native apps (local native apps) that this is a better way to reach users. When doing these things, you need to think first about why it is good for them and how to bring them benefits. Only by bringing them benefits can you succeed. For instance, if you tell a news website to create a light app, we support you, but after two months, you find the light app is not updated. Our team explained that maintaining Native Apps, website content, and light apps simultaneously is too troublesome, so they gradually stopped updating the light app. Why? Because the traffic we brought them was not enough. If you only bring them 10% of the traffic, they might stop updating after two months, but if you bring them 90% of the traffic, see if they update or not — they will definitely abandon the PC website, Mobile site, and Native App and focus solely on this thing. Why? Because you bring them more benefits."

To build an ecosystem, you must think about giving enough benefits to your upstream and downstream partners, making it more beneficial than other ecosystems, so they will follow you. How to bring benefits? Robin emphasized that when technology is not yet mature and has not become a standard, operational efforts are required.

In fact, many of Baidu's products have made significant progress in platformization and interface-ization, such as the Baidu Translation API (Application Programming Interface), which is not only used by multiple internal products but was also opened to the public as early as May 2012, responding to tens of millions of translation requests from outside every day. LBS (Location-Based Services) responds to 3 billion positioning requests from the outside every day, and a positive ecosystem is gradually being established. Products are easy to copy, but ecosystems cannot be imitated, which will form the most important competitiveness. This time, the voice open platform has taken a big step forward, bringing blessings to industry developers.

An ecosystem is mutual — beneficial to you and me, beneficial to everyone, so it can function properly. It is not about doing things infinitely and freely for others without benefiting oneself. We are not aiming to be open just for the sake of openness; we aim to create platforms and interfaces.

Regarding the subsequent implementation of platformization and interface-ization at the company level, Robin repeatedly emphasized the need for a change in mindset. "The platform mindset means that if I (Baidu) need something, I will give you (the developer) the requirements and ask you to do it. If I hope you do it faster, I will put pressure on you or let higher-level decision-makers in the company put pressure on you. It is not about you doing your own set of things or taking something and modifying it yourself. These two approaches are not platformized approaches. This may be uncomfortable in the short term, but in the long run, it will prove to be very good. Let professionals do professional work — if you are doing voice recognition, let him do it to the best extent possible, let him meet your needs, rather than doing it yourself."

For achieving platformization and interface-ization, Robin also set a goal for the entire company, hoping that within one to one and a half years, the company can transform into one with a platformized and interface-ized mindset. "This will certainly not be easy, but if we succeed in this transformation, Baidu's innovations will emerge endlessly."
