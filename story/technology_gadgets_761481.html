162 Yuan Magic Device: Complete Review of Nokia 1050

by geekzhang on 2013-05-03 14:22:16

Dear friends who are using smartphones, have you been already anesthetized by symptoms such as straight-board design, large screen, charging once a day? When your phone restarts unexpectedly or the battery runs out while you are having fun, have you ever thought of returning to simplicity? Unfortunately, the classic Nokia 1110 and 3310 have long gone, and even if you have money, it's hard to buy them now. On the other hand, times are changing quickly: Nokia is no longer dominant, Sony Ericsson has split up, Motorola married into a rich family but its situation is getting worse every day. Just like the lost time, we can't go back to the past. Although the trend of popularizing smartphones is irreversible, the market for basic feature phones that meet basic communication needs is still very large. Take Nokia as an example, in the first quarter of this year, Nokia sold as many as 55.8 million feature phones, while the shipment volume of smartphones was only 5.6 million. The ratio of shipments between feature phones and smartphones is close to 10:1! Therefore, even though Nokia is vigorously promoting Lumia smartphones, it still relies heavily on feature phones to maintain its market share. The new model 105 (the domestic version model number is 1050) was thus born.Nokia 1050The 1050, as Nokia's currently lowest-priced model, can be owned for only 162 yuan. By eating out less with friends, or buying fewer phone cases or accessories, you can easily save enough money to buy such a stable, practical, ultra-long standby, and rugged phone. Such distinctive selling points make it quite unconventional in today's mobile phone market. Plus, Nokia has accumulated a great reputation for feature phones over the years. The 105/1050 has received a lot of attention since its launch at MWC2013, and many netizens have been tracking its release and related information.Although I've tested countless phones over the years, the tester still vaguely remembers some feelings when testing Nokia's classic entry-level models like the 1110 in 2005 and the 1116 in 2007. When I got the retail version of the Nokia 1050, I found that it still adheres to Nokia's traditional concept of light and eco-friendly packaging and simple accessories for entry-level phones. The packaging can be 100% recycled, and the standard configuration includes the phone body, an 800mAh BL-5CB battery, a charger connector (CA-187C), and a charging plug (AC-8C). Of course, this is also due to cost control reasons.The first feeling of holding the real Nokia 1050 in my hand is how light and small it is, especially its weight of only 70 grams, which is much lighter than any smartphone on the market. Even holding two 1050s in one hand is not heavier than holding one Nokia 920 or Samsung Note2. Such convenience makes it easier to carry, and one of the main uses of the 1050 is as a backup phone. The lightweight characteristic fits its positioning. As for the two color options, black and blue, which one do you prefer?Retail Standard ConfigurationAlthough the price of the 1050 is quite low, the material of the shell is not careless. The finely sandblasted plastic frame and back cover feel delicate, and more importantly, this material has excellent anti-fingerprint ability and abrasion resistance. Everyday bumps and accidental drops generally won't cause obvious damage to the shell of the 1050. However, the Nokia 1050 itself does not promote durability as a selling point, so users are not recommended to torture the 1050 specifically (unless bought specifically for venting anger or breaking to scare off wolves).In line with the simple style of the whole machine, the back of the Nokia 1050 is designed simply without a camera. The NOKIA logo plate also serves as the speaker of the phone. There isn't much variety in the ringtones; loud sound, good penetration, and strong vibration are the key features. The way to remove the back cover of the 1050 is similar to models like the Samsung Galaxy S3/Note2. In terms of the robustness of the plastic clips, it performs even better, but unfortunately, there are some shortcomings in the details of gap control, and the fit of the back cover is not very tight.Back DesignAs a phone with physical buttons, the Nokia 1050 does not have any buttons on either side of the body. The main interface is located on the top of the body. Apart from the 3.5mm headphone jack and the round head charging port, there is also an LED flashlight. However, there are at least two things I want to criticize about the top design of the 1050. First, it still retains the old-fashioned 2mm round head charging port, which is not compatible with the mainstream microUSB interface. Second, the flashlight, although practical, surprisingly lacks the one-touch quick activation feature that used to be available, which is confusing.Top DesignThe 1050 is equipped with a 1.4-inch square screen with 65536 colors and 128*128 pixels. Many years ago, Nokia started using screens with similar configurations. There's no need to expect too much from its display effect. The fineness cannot compare with the mainstream 720p, 1080p screens, and the color representation is not strong. Outdoor use will also be affected by strong reflection affecting readability. You get what you pay for, there's nothing to complain about.The entry-level Nokia 1050 naturally does not choose a touchscreen. The human-computer interaction method is the traditional physical numeric keypad, equipped with a four-way navigation key, and there are dedicated keys for dialing and hanging up, making the operation straightforward and clear. To enhance the durability of the keyboard, the 1050 uses an integrated dustproof keyboard. After practical testing, the feel is excellent. On the other hand, returning from the rigid touch screen to the once-popular numeric keypad operation brings a sense of nostalgia when using the T9 input method that could be easily blind-typed in the past, accompanied by the sound feedback of each key press, giving the tester a sense of time travel.Besides phone calls and text messages, many friends often use their phones for internet browsing (including web pages, Weibo, WeChat, etc.). Unfortunately, the Nokia 1050 does not provide WAP functionality, so when a friend asked me to show off a Weibo post during the evaluation, I was immediately embarrassed. The reason for this anti-human design without internet functionality is actually quite simple. Firstly, it is related to the precise positioning of the 1050; even if it had this function, users would not want to bother with a 1.4-inch screen. Secondly, more directly speaking, do you really expect full service for a phone priced at 162 yuan? Don't be ridiculous...After removing the WAP function, the Nokia 1050 uses the S30 platform, and the overall experience is basically similar to the old version of S40. The classic nine-grid menu (which can also be displayed as a list) returns, just without font adjustment and game download services. However, the 1050 still comes pre-installed with two games: RapidRoll (Color Ball Slide) and Forbidden Treasures (Treasure Hunt). Both are simple-to-play and fairly engaging games, suitable for pure entertainment.Due to the lack of internet functionality, the main functions of the Nokia 1050 are limited: alarm clock (five groups), calculator, unit converter, calendar, lunar calendar, stopwatch, etc., which are all very practical and don't need much elaboration. Moreover, the power-on alarm clock feature is something that many smartphones don't even have (don't you feel superior?). As for the built-in radio function, although it is good, tests have shown that it must be connected through a 3.5mm headphone cable to work properly. Actually, Nokia had models like the 5030 a few years ago that could use the radio function without headphones. But don't complain. Compared to Nokia's entry-level models a few years ago, the 1050 has upgrades: it now uses a 3.5mm headphone specification (although headphones are not included in the standard configuration). Additionally, our tests showed that not all 3.5mm interface headphones can be used as radio antennas on the 1050, for example, inserting Meizu's EP-40 headphones into the 1050 shows that the accessory is unsupported.Let's take a look at the Nokia Life Tools. This tool has been around for several years (previously called OVI Life Tools). Users can choose to receive health, education, agriculture hot news, weather, and other information via SMS push notifications. Users of the 1050 can enjoy free services for two weeks after activation. There are also many similar SP services on the market, but the advantage of Nokia Life Tools lies in presenting relatively high-quality content in a better integrated way for users, making subscription and use more convenient.Nokia Life ToolsSince the Nokia 1050 does not support WAP, it naturally does not support MMS or WeChat either. It can only send regular SMS and enhanced SMS (EMS). Given that it only uses the basic S30 platform, the demand for memory and storage space is extremely low. Thus, it only equips with 384KB RAM and 8MB storage space. Considering that its contact book (business card-style management) has a specific limit of 500 entries, the 8MB internal storage is sufficient to handle the saving of ordinary SMS, memos, and other limited information.Technology is people-oriented, and Nokia clearly understands the communication needs of entry-level users, so it has added practical communication features such as message counters and Do Not Disturb mode for incoming calls.Small screen, no complicated power-consuming 3G (supports Mobile and Unicom 2G networks), no internet or multimedia functions, all these factors allow the Nokia 1050 to have an energy consumption performance that easily outperforms various popular smartphones. Although its battery capacity is only 800mAh, according to official data, its standby theoretical value is as high as 842 hours (35 days), and continuous talk time can reach 12.5 hours. Under normal usage frequency, it is impossible to achieve a month without charging, but maintaining a 5-day battery life with not too many calls is not difficult. Hehe, let all the flagship phones tremble!Equipped with an 800mAh BL-5CB batteryRecently, it was reported that in Shenzhen Huaqiangbei, a counterfeit phone can be bought for 75 yuan, leaving foreigners in awe. In the eyes of the reviewer, this definitely will not be the cheapest phone. The bottom line for counterfeit phones is lower than ever before. Indeed, there are many phones cheaper than the Nokia 1050 if you only look at the price, and there are also many phones with more functions at the same price. However, if we consider daily use, the 1050 comprehensively performs well in aspects such as brand reputation, maturity and stability, durability, and others, making it the trustworthy first choice without doubt. For only 162 yuan, you can own a device that can be used as an elderly phone, a backup phone, or even a stress-relief tool or a wolf deterrent, which is quite economical and practical. Is there any need to be picky? How can it not be popular?
