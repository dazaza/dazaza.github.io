Sun Wukong's Letter to White Bone Spirit VS White Bone Spirit's Reply to Sun Wukong

by aqzyanshengi on 2011-08-27 15:50:41

Ms. Bai, hello!

First of all, I sincerely apologize to you and say sorry. Yesterday, Master wrote me a letter, which I read over and over again. It truly hit my pain points, as if someone had dug into my heart, making me suddenly realize the truth. I spent the whole night lying down reflecting, and now everything is clear to me. After living for several hundred years, I've been fooling around without achieving anything, it's like I haven't lived at all. Thinking about it, I feel deeply saddened. Although Master may be somewhat hypocritical, he still has sincere feelings for me and said some heartfelt words, for which I am truly grateful from the bottom of my heart.

The journey to the West for scriptures was actually a trap set by the Jade Emperor and Bodhisattvas. The journey wasn't as difficult as we imagined; they could have sent a more skilled deity who could have retrieved the scriptures in no time. However, Master was an object for promotion but lacked grassroots work experience, so he was sent down for training. But they couldn't let him suffer alone, so they arranged for me, along with Bajie, Sha Weng, and Bai Long Ma, who had made mistakes, to accompany and serve him.

The eighty-one calamities on the road were all calculated by the Bodhisattva. Tang Monk was never in real danger. Wherever we went, Guanyin Bodhisattva knew exactly where we were. At critical moments, whether I sought her out or not, she would personally come or send her disciples. With the lotus in place, all evil disappeared.

And those so-called demons, except for ones like you who have no backing and fend for yourself, are either celestial beings' mounts or fish raised by Bodhisattvas. They all have exclusive divine weapons, how could I possibly defeat them? After exerting all my strength, when the fight reached its climax, their masters would arrive, saying, "Wukong, let me take them away." What could I say? The only ones who suffered were those like you, who had no connections, eventually being beaten back to their original forms.

I always thought that with my loyalty and demon-slaying efforts, I would receive recognition from my superiors and comrades. Who knew that after returning and attending the summary meeting, all honors went to the master, and we only received golden bodies. Fortunately, the master removed the tight-fitting hoop from my head, allowing me to breathe a sigh of relief.

Later, they were all appointed official positions, while no one cared about me. Helpless, I returned to my hometown of Huaguo Mountain to eat wild fruits and drink spring water. There were no benefits, but whenever there was something, such as studying documents, someone would come to check my learning situation, asking me to write self-examination materials. They said that the mistake I made five hundred years ago was still serious, requiring deep reflection from the depths of my thoughts. It feels like the tight-fitting hoop has been removed, but there's still an invisible one remaining.

If only I hadn't jumped into Laozi's furnace back then, causing this pair of broken fiery eyes that can see through others' tricks and ugliness that others cannot see. Seeing these things, I can't help but speak out, offending people when I do. Plus, during the scripture-fetching journey, I fought many subordinates or relatives of leaders, and everyone looks down on me, saying how capable I once was but now I'm worse off than before.

I naively believed that only Bajie was lustful, and the master was a righteous gentleman. But after reading his letter, I finally understood. On the journey to fetch the scriptures, he was just pretending; while we were worried to death, he was secretly romancing the Queen of the Daughter Country and the Spider Demon behind our backs. Seriously violating discipline but no one questioned him. In America, this would require resignation due to fault. Yet they are adept at maneuvering, obtaining fake diplomas, receiving commendations, and reaching high-ranking positions. Today, I still have nothing. Housing is too expensive to buy, so I can only continue sleeping in the Water Curtain Cave. Now, the age for promotion has passed, and I don't think about it much anymore. I just want to find someone to settle down and live a good life.

We became acquainted through conflict. Among the women I know, you are the one I admire the most. Although you are beautiful and capable, you don't rely on officials or rich men, but instead create your own world through your own abilities. Moreover, you are diligent, brave, kind, and virtuous.

Now looking back, I still really like you. Both of us are emotional people, romantic, upright, and hate evil. You're also getting older, soon becoming a leftover woman. Why don't we live together? Either going to your White Bone Cave or coming to my Huaguo Mountain would be fine. We can develop tourism based on Huaguo Mountain, and I can start a martial arts school. Life will surely be manageable. Whatever hobbies you have, I support. Let's properly raise our children, letting them grow up to attend Tsinghua University or Peking University, become officials, and avoid repeating our old paths.

I look forward to your reply... Wishing you happiness.

Wukong

July 16, 2011, Huaguo Mountain

White Bone Spirit's Reply to Sun Wukong

Mr. Sun:

Your letter has been received.

Thank you for remembering me, but I cannot marry you. I apologize.

I am not someone who holds grudges. In fact, I used to have a hero complex. When you turned me back to my original form, I thought, after you returned from the West fetching scriptures, I would find you, marry you, bear your children, wash clothes for you in the Water Curtain Cave, and till the soil for you on Huaguo Mountain. Back then, your master, along with Bajie and Sha Weng, already knew I was a demoness. Your master pretended not to understand, looking the other way, Bajie had ulterior motives due to my beauty, and Sha Weng, being timid, preferred fewer troubles. Only you still had some spirit and sense of justice, pursuing and striking me under everyone's watchful eyes. At that time, I indeed deserved punishment. Being young and inexperienced, I was daydreaming and hurting others. It was you who woke me up and gave me a lesson. I thank you for that.

However, things changed later, and my thoughts also shifted. After you fetched the scriptures, your master was praised for his leadership skills and decisive decisions, promoted to a higher-level position. Bajie, despite having a penchant for flirtation, was assigned to oversee women's affairs because such behavior isn't considered problematic these days. Sha Weng, although lacking achievements, was appreciated for his hard work in logistics and promoted to the position of transportation manager. Only you were labeled as impetuous, acting without thinking, lacking organizational discipline, and arrogant, thus left to fend for yourself. Since then, I realized that admiring heroes is useless, and one must be more pragmatic. Life is full of turning points, and one should not go astray.

I am doing well now. I have my own villa, car, and child.

You might not know, but when you returned to Huaguo Mountain to fend for yourself, I worked at Tian Shang Ren Jian (a brothel). Later, I met your master. Your master is no longer the bookworm who constantly called out for his disciples to save him. Due to his chanting background, he sings exceptionally well and often comes to our establishment, spending generously. And now, he travels with a large entourage, wielding considerable power. Your master still has a sense of humor; he says I have fair skin and a graceful figure, hence calling me White Bone Spirit, making me feel flattered.

Because we had met a few times, every time your master came, he specifically requested me to accompany him. He told me that after returning, he married the princess of the Daughter Country. He also mentioned that they had a strained relationship, as that woman had turned into a tiger, constantly nagging and checking phone calls and QQ chat records. More importantly, after giving birth, she gained a lot of weight, becoming quite unappealing. Your master also said that he had his eye on me back then, but since the organization was observing him, he didn't dare to act rashly. After returning, he originally intended to find me, but the princess's father was a close friend of his superior, so he couldn't offend him. In short, your master wanted me to be with him, offering everything except for formal status. Later, I moved into the villa he provided for me. He also replaced the white horse with a BMW, which he gifted to me. Although I know your master has other women, including the Spider Demon, whose files he altered and reassigned to another unit for reception duties, leaving traces everywhere, I have no choice. Last year, I bore him a son, unwilling to take the surname Tang, so I took my own, naming him Bai Tang.

Mr. Sun, please don't be angry or despise me. Being with you, I might have status, but no money. You might not know, but I am terrified of poverty. Nowadays, we can't afford houses, we can't live in caves forever. Peaches don't sell for much money; one season's earnings aren't even as much as what I make in one night at Tian Shang Ren Jian. How can we live, raise children, and provide for their education? It's too difficult. These days, as long as you have money, people will treat you differently, regardless of its source or cleanliness. You mentioned starting a martial arts school, but I think you'd better not. You only know some staff techniques, but you can't match Jay Chou's nunchucks, which are far more profitable. All I can say is, take care of yourself. Eat peaches carefully, peel them first; modern peaches are covered in pesticides that can't be washed off completely.

By the way, I heard that Niuhwang has passed away. Go visit Princess Iron Fan, who is now widowed. There might still be hope.

Farewell, Mr. Sun!

YOURS,

White Bone Spirit
