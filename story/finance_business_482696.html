What are the surgical treatments for cervical erosion, and how can cervicitis be treated to prevent recurrence? Cervicitis is a common disease among women of childbearing age, and it has both acute and chronic forms. When women suffer from cervicitis, various uncomfortable symptoms appear in the body, seriously affecting family harmony and threatening the physical health of patients. In addition to these troubles, the recurrent episodes of cervicitis have made patients lose confidence in being cured. So, how exactly should cervicitis be treated to prevent recurrence?

To prevent the recurrence of cervicitis, expert Zhang Ailian from Xi'an Ci'ai Gynecology Hospital pointed out the following points:

1. Before treatment, routine examinations such as vaginal discharge test, cervical smear cytology examination or colposcopy should all be negative;

2. During the wound healing period (4-8 weeks), avoid sitting baths and vaginal irrigation;

3. After physical therapy, there will be an increase in vaginal secretions, and even large amounts of watery discharge. There may be slight bleeding when the scab falls off;

4. The treatment time should be chosen within 3-7 days after menstruation ends;

5. Regular follow-up checks on the healing situation until recovery. Surgical treatment is suitable for cases where the above methods are ineffective or when there is cervical hypertrophy, deep and extensive erosion, and involvement of the cervical canal.
