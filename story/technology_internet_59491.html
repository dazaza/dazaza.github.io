Asia Domain Auction Postponed, Open Registration Proceeds as Scheduled

by now on 2008-04-02 16:58:04

According to DotAsia registry, the auction of Asia domain names during the land rush period will be scheduled in mid-April. For Asia domain names submitted during the trademark registration period, if there are two or more qualified applications for the same domain name, an auction will be held. The registry has already processed 99.5% of this type of domain name review and auction, with some still ongoing. The registry has postponed the auction date for Asia domain names during the land rush period to mid-April.

During the sunrise period and the land rush period, ".Asia" domains received over 500,000 applications. Among them, 45,000 domains received more than one application, so the owner will be decided by auction. This auction activity has successfully avoided the chaotic situation that occurred when new domains were launched in the past, where registrants competed for high-value domains. The Asia domain name auction is conducted by pool.com from Canada. Ten days before the auction, the company will send an email invitation to applicants to participate in the auction. The email will contain detailed information about the domain auction: an account activation link, username, and password. Once the applicant activates the account, they can view the auction information. This notification is sent by noreply@dotasia.pool.com, and the registry reminds relevant individuals not to filter out such emails.

At the same time, .Asia domains opened for registration at 20:00 on March 26th. Now.cn (www.now.cn) and the Asia registry simultaneously opened registration for Asia domains, becoming the first domestic registrar to do so, offering a low price of 159 yuan per year to capture the market. Global users can register their .Asia domains on a first-come, first-served basis in real-time. Due to registry reservations, place names and some other domains can only be registered by related governments or organizations. DotAsia CEO Hong-An Zhong said: "There are 10 million domain registrations in China, and global domain registrations exceed 150 million. People are beginning to realize the potential of the '.Asia' domain. Currently, there are still millions of high-value '.Asia' domains available for registration on a 'first-come, first-served' basis."
