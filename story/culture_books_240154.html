Yan Geling's latest masterpiece in 2009, the latest long novel work after "Little Aunt Duohuo".

A Shanghai, China version of "Schindler", and later a world-renowned press tycoon

A simple, melancholy Jewish refugee with artistic temperament, who later stepped on America with a false identity

A female pianist born in the United States and raised in Shanghai, struggling between love and betrayal, conscience and ideals

The story takes place in Shanghai during the Anti-Japanese War, narrated by the female protagonist "me" to a biographer in her old age.

In 1939, "I" fell in love with a Jewish man who had just escaped from the concentration camp in Shanghai. At that time, Joseph Meisinger's infamous "final solution" was about to be implemented. To send my lover to America, "I" hastily grabbed a savior - another American youth - to be my husband in Shanghai.

In the chaotic times, ordinary people began a series of interlocking fates for rescue. Finally, "I" redeemed "my husband" with "my lover's" money, used "my husband's" passport to save "my lover," and at the same time, destroyed the original understanding and belief of love to achieve love...

The ending of the story is unexpected and touching in terms of love.

Introduction to "The Boarders"

The book features Asians, Europeans, Americans, Shanghainese, Northern Jiangsu people, and Hakka people. In the bloody war years, no one could escape the fate of being a "boarder." Big Shanghai, small world - love and faith, loyalty and betrayal, self-seeking and self-loss, are all included. "Shanghai in the 1920s and 1930s was the least rigid place in the world. People from all over the world came to Shanghai to take a moral holiday."
