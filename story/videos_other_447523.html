The Industry and Commerce Bureau director whose family sold defective Zongzi made a scene at the newspaper office after being exposed

by xiaoyou234l on 2011-06-17 11:43:59

Recently, a post titled "Anger! Shenyang Shenhe District Industry and Commerce Bureau Director Yang Xiaosong came to the newspaper office drunk and claimed he wanted to challenge a reporter. The reason was that his wife and son's BreadTalk store had food problems which were exposed." went viral on the Internet. The post revealed that Shenhe District Industry and Commerce Bureau Director Yang Xiaosong caused a commotion at a relevant newspaper office due to his wife being exposed for selling moldy rice dumplings. Some netizens even nicknamed Zhang Xiaosong as the "Most Powerful Industry and Commerce Bureau Director in China."

After investigation and verification by the reporter, it was confirmed that Director Yang Xiaosong indeed caused a disturbance at the newspaper office and his wife led people to assault reporters. His son's "BreadTalk" high-end bakery required an initial investment of more than 5 million yuan, and the source of the large investment for the Yang family to open the store was indeed unclear. According to Xinhua News Agency,

The problematic rice dumplings were exposed, and the industry and commerce bureau director wanted to "duel" with the reporter.

Regarding the content disclosed in the online post, the reporter conducted verifications. Multiple staff members from a certain evening paper headquartered in Shenyang's Shenhe District confirmed to the reporter that the incident occurred at this newspaper office. On June 8, this evening paper published a news article titled "Group-buying rice dumplings as gifts, opened to find white mold growing." It reported that Shenyang resident An Sheng purchased 20 boxes of rice dumplings from BreadTalk's Shenyang Taiyuan Street branch before the Dragon Boat Festival. After giving these rice dumplings to clients, two relatively close clients called to say that the rice dumplings were moldy and smelt bad. An Sheng recovered these rice dumplings and went to the bread shop to negotiate. Among them, 8 rice dumplings showed varying degrees of mold growth. However, the staff inside the shop stated, "The moldy rice dumplings are not ours." After the publication of this news, it was reprinted by multiple websites.

Multiple employees from this evening paper confirmed to the reporter that on June 8, the day the news was published, Liu Mou (female), the person in charge of the BreadTalk store, went to the Liaoning-Shenyang Evening Paper office cursing loudly. The next day, Liu brought people again to cause trouble at the newspaper office. Since the two reporters who wrote this news were out interviewing, Liu and her group got into a verbal altercation with other newspaper employees, and physical pushing and fighting ensued. At the scene, Ye Feng, the director of the newspaper's multimedia digital center, and deputy director Yu Xin were assaulted.

After this incident occurred, Liu used the excuse of being bullied at the newspaper office and called her husband Yang Xiaosong, who then led two minivans full of people to rush to the newspaper office, causing a commotion in the office and demanding that the newspaper find the two reporters who wrote this news, wanting to "duel" with them. Reporters present were all very angry, taking photos and recording videos, with some even "live broadcasting" the event on Weibo.

Regarding this plot, Ye Feng confirmed over the phone, saying that at the time, his nose was broken by one of Liu's people with a punch, causing him to bleed profusely; his colleague Yu Xin was also struck on the face with an elbow joint.

Investment exceeding 5 million yuan once, netizens urge to investigate the source

Posting netizens claimed: "At the Shenyang Municipal Administration for Industry and Commerce archives, the Shenyang branch of 'BreadTalk' was registered in 2008, with the legal representative's name being Yang Jwei, born in 1984. Calculating by the timeline, when this store was registered, Yang Jwei should have just graduated from university. There are insiders who claim that Yang Jwei is the son of Director Yang."

Through multiple channels, the reporter learned that the legal representative of BreadTalk's Shenyang Taiyuan Street branch is indeed Yang Xiaosong's son, Yang Jwei. This franchise chain store, considered high-end in Shenyang, indeed requires an initial investment of more than 5 million yuan. As a civil servant, with an annual salary around 100,000 yuan, Yang Xiaosong made a substantial investment for his son's business shortly after his son graduated from university, and the source of the funds is indeed unclear.

On the afternoon of June 16, the reporter called the Shenhe District Industry and Commerce Bureau of Shenyang to verify the situation. A female staff member from the bureau's office answered the phone, claiming she was "unaware of the bureau director causing a disturbance at the newspaper office," and refused to disclose Director Yang's office phone number. Up until the time of submission, the reporter was unable to speak with Yang Xiaosong.

Several newspaper employees privately revealed to the reporter that after the incident, Yang Xiaosong used various means to block information, asking the newspaper not to publish articles about the matter and not to accept interviews from other media.

As of the time of the reporter's submission, the attention of netizens online regarding the "most powerful industry and commerce bureau director" remained high. Many netizens urged relevant parties to actively investigate Yang Xiaosong's despicable behavior because, at the very least, Yang Xiaosong's actions do not meet the basic requirements for civil servants set by the state. If investigated clearly, he should be handled according to regulations, and the disciplinary inspection department must ascertain the source of the huge investment he made for his son.
