Email: tdien@lhai.com Hong Kong, March 30, 2010 / PR Newswire Asia / -- Chinese lighting suppliers are committed to developing green energy-saving products to secure more orders. They anticipate robust growth in the export market over the next 12 months. The data comes from Global Sources (Nasdaq: GSOL) "China Sourcing Report – Lighting & Electrical" (http://www.chinasourcingreports.com/lightingelectricalsreport ). The report covers a range of products including bulbs, indoor and outdoor lighting, lighting equipment and accessories. It also points out that suppliers are increasing production of energy-saving lamps and LED lighting to maintain competitiveness as exports recover.

Su Huiwen, publisher of the "China Sourcing Report - Lighting & Electrical", Ms. Ye Qing, stated: "Due to many countries starting to implement plans to ban incandescent lamps, demand for energy-saving lamps has rebounded from the low point during the economic crisis; at the same time, LED has once again become a hotspot, with Ketdi Optoelectronics leading the trend. Exports of LED lighting products continue to grow, and domestic production of energy-saving lamps and LED lighting products is beginning to rise. We expect demand to continue growing as technological advancements lower production costs and product prices."
