13 Classic Hollywood Heist Movies

by hjdsk81902 on 2011-05-27 02:34:34

The most hated jobs in the world must be swindling, but sometimes there are also lovable thieves and scammers. A lot of times, the thieves on the big screen often have their own special charm. The 2008 new film "The Bank Job" was a great proof when it was released with great fanfare. Today, let's take a look at these lovable thieves and scammers compiled by Entertainment Weekly. These bad guys, whether elegant or tough, always exude an irresistible charm. When they steal their prey, they seem to unintentionally steal your heart too!

Ocean’s Eleven

Thief Team: Danny Ocean (played by George Clooney), the master thief, can't stay still for a moment. He starts a new life of theft as soon as he is out of prison. However, he is not alone. Danny Ocean has gathered a very powerful team of thieves, each of whom is an elite thief. This includes his former partner, an extremely skilled poker player (Brad Pitt), a quick-moving yet childish super pickpocket (Matt Damon), a blasting genius (Don Cheadle), and a Chinese acrobat (Shao Bo Qin).

Target: The target of this group of thievery elites is three casinos in Las Vegas. They plan to take $150 million from the underground vaults of these three casinos. The owner of these casinos is the cunning Terry Benedict (played by Andy Garcia). In fact, Danny Ocean not only plans to take $150 million from Terry Benedict, but he also wants to win back the heart of Tess (Julia Roberts), who was taken away by Terry Benedict.

Obstacles: Stealing $150 million from the underground vaults is no easy task! The vaults are the most heavily guarded places in Las Vegas, with impenetrable defenses that can withstand bullet attacks. Moreover, the cunning Terry Benedict is vigilant against Danny Ocean to protect his property and lovely woman. With so many obstacles, even the genius thief Danny Ocean faces challenges!

Who Wins: Usually, the main characters in movies become the ultimate winners, but this time, the biggest winner is not on the screen, but the audience in front of the big screen. This movie, starring many Hollywood top stars, and co-starring two of Hollywood's sexiest male stars, "Ocean’s Eleven" itself is a piece of art. Plus, the unexpected plot twists make "Ocean’s Eleven" a rare visual and mental feast for movie fans.

Authoritative Rating: A

How to Steal a Million

Thief Team: The charming thief duo consisting of Audrey Hepburn playing Nicole, the daughter of an art forgery maker, and Peter O’Toole playing a gentlemanly thief makes for a delightful sight. This elegant pair falls in love during a sophisticated theft, making for an interesting story.

Target: The target this time is a statue exhibit in the museum. This statue is actually a fake made by Nicole's grandfather. Nicole's father promised to let the museum exhibit it and buy a $1 million insurance policy before the insurance company discovers it's a forgery.

Obstacle: The sparks of love between this thievery duo distract them, preventing them from focusing on stealing.

Who Wins: The biggest winner of "How to Steal a Million" is undoubtedly Audrey Hepburn, who plays Nicole. On the big screen, Nicole wins love; off-screen, Audrey Hepburn once again captivates the audience with her outstanding acting and personal charm.

Authoritative Rating: B

Inside Job

Thief Team: Dalton (played by Clive Owen), a genius thief, gathers his subordinates. They dress up as painters, hijack a bank on Wall Street, and hold 50 hostages, including bank employees. Dalton has his own set of strategies. He makes all the hostages wear the same clothes and masks as himself to confuse police snipers and special forces, leaving them helpless. But is Dalton really just robbing the bank for money?

Target: On the surface, the target of the robbery is the bank, but is that really the case?

Obstacle: The head of the thief gang, Dalton, is very capable, but his opponent is no ordinary person either. Detective Fraser (played by Denzel Washington) is psychologically astute and engages in a battle of wits with Dalton. Arthur Case (Christopher Plummer), the founder of the bank, clearly has some secrets hidden in the bank that he doesn't want others to know. He hires Madeleine (Jodie Foster), who is both a lawyer and a political broker, to avoid Dalton or Fraser discovering his secrets.

Who Wins: "Inside Job" earned acclaim for its screenwriters, but the biggest winner is director Spike Lee. Critics widely consider "Inside Job" one of Spike Lee's best films. Of course, "Inside Job" brings unparalleled visual enjoyment to the audience, making the audience one of the winners too!

Authoritative Rating: B+

The Italian Job (1968 version)

Thief Team: Charlie (played by Michael Caine), who was just released from prison, didn't waste a moment. His first action after regaining freedom was to gather a group of partners to form a powerful theft syndicate, which even includes a computer genius (Benny Hill). Meanwhile, he receives financial support from the leader of an underground dark organization (Noel Coward). With favorable timing, location, and people, a good show is about to begin!

Target: The eyes of the theft syndicate are all on the four million pounds of gold being transported from Italy to China, drooling over it!

Obstacle: Charlie has meticulously planned the gold heist, except for one headache-inducing issue – to implement this plan, Charlie must disrupt the traffic in Turin, which has a dense population. This is no simple matter!

Who Wins: The 1968 version of "The Italian Job" had a profound impact, and the 2003 remake "The Italian Job" followed the same plot. However, if we talk about the biggest winner, it would be BMW, the manufacturer of Mini-Coopers, because the mini cars play a significant role in the movie!

Authoritative Rating: B+

The Thomas Crown Affair

Thief Team: The 1999 version of "The Thomas Crown Affair" is a remake based on the 1968 Steve McQueen-directed "The Thomas Crown Affair." In the movie, Pierce Brosnan plays a self-made billionaire, a playboy who loves adventure. Despite his immense wealth, he finds joy in stealing expensive artworks, a rather peculiar hobby.

Target: This time, the playboy sets his sights on a painting worth $100 million in the museum. Enthusiastic about challenging the limits of excitement, he plans to openly steal the painting from the heavily guarded museum in broad daylight.

Obstacle: Although he is no longer playing James Bond, Pierce Brosnan's character can't resist the allure of beautiful women. Everything was going smoothly until he meets the attractive and intelligent painting security officer (Rene Russo), causing him to lose control. The game begins to get dangerous.

Who Wins: The cat-and-mouse game between the detective and the thief is a popular movie theme, especially when combined with the classic element of love, turning "The Thomas Crown Affair" into a universally appealing dish. However, whether the protagonist achieves their goal is something the audience must discover themselves.

Authoritative Rating: B+

A Fish Called Wanda

Thief Team: American female con artist Wanda (Jamie Lee Curtis) teams up with Otto (Kevin Kline) to form a theft gang, which also includes Wanda's boyfriend George (Tom Georgeson) and a stuttering Kane (Michael Palin).

Target: This group of thieves plans to rob a jewelry store, targeting priceless diamonds. While stealing the diamonds proves relatively easy, they end up causing more trouble.

Obstacle: Wanda and Otto betray George, leading to his arrest. Before being captured, George hides the diamonds. Wanda seduces George's lawyer Ritchie (John Cleese) to find the diamonds, but her misfortunes don't lead her to the diamonds and instead cause plenty of laughs.

Who Wins: This film is hailed as the "culmination of modern black comedy films," with standout performances from the actors. Kevin Kline, who played Otto, won the Oscar for Best Supporting Actor for his excellent performance in "A Fish Called Wanda," laughing all the way to the end.

Authoritative Rating: C

The Lavender Hill Mob

Thief Team: Henry (played by Alec Guinness) originally worked as a diligent gold bar transporter for the bank, serving for 20 years without ever making a mistake. However, one day, he decides to rob the gold. He conspires with his old friend, Stanley Holloway, a souvenir manufacturer, to devise a stunning gold-stealing plan.

Target: This duo plans to melt the stolen gold worth one million into miniature Eiffel Tower sculptures and transport them from Britain to France.

Obstacle: Ironically, while transporting the small sculptures, they accidentally sell a few of them cheaply to British female students.

Must-See Reason: Although this is an old film, it's worth watching just to see Alec Guinness play a comedic role again, as he is usually remembered as Obi-Wan Kenobi from "Star Wars." Additionally, fans of Audrey Hepburn should check out this film because it records her big screen debut!

The Usual Suspects

Thief Team: The story revolves around a criminal gang composed of five members. Among them, the gang leader Keaton (Gabriel Byrne) and Verbal (Kevin Spacey) are captured after committing a crime, but even in jail, these restless individuals don’t stop plotting new thefts.

Target: This criminal gang plans to steal drugs worth ninety-one million belonging to the mysterious henchman Keyser Soze.

Mystery: During detention, Verbal narrates his story to the police, answering one question: Who is Keyser Soze?

Who Wins: Whether the criminals in the film achieve success isn't important, but Kevin Spacey won the Oscar for Best Supporting Actor for his brilliant performance in "The Usual Suspects"!

Authoritative Rating: B

The Asphalt Jungle

Thief Team: For a theft plan, the leader Alvin gathers a capable theft team, including a marksman, a safe expert, a racing driver, and a lawyer.

Target: This time, the target is jewelry worth one million dollars, with the lawyer keeping half of the cash.

Obstacle: The lawyer plans to use his special relationship with his mistress (Marilyn Monroe) to launder the one million, but will things go smoothly?

Who Wins: Directed by John Huston, "The Asphalt Jungle" is considered the pioneer of heist films. Therefore, if we talk about the winner, it must be all heist films! It is precisely this "The Asphalt Jungle" that inspired their creation, including every film mentioned in this topic.

Nine Queens

Cheating Team: Good-natured Juan and rational Marcos are a pair of thieving partners who live by scamming.

Cheating Target: This pair's job is to sell a set of forged stamps called "Nine Queens" at their original price.

Cheating Obstacle: Juan falls in love with Marcos' sister. Will love make someone give up scamming?

Who Wins: The final winner is not the love in the film, but the scriptwriter outside the film. This Argentine film received widespread acclaim, and the screenplay won multiple awards!

Heat

Thief Team: After a failed tank robbery, Neil McCauley (Robert De Niro), who knows the ins and outs of thievery, decides to make a last-ditch effort with his subordinates. Neil McCauley is a ruthless professional thief who works cleanly and efficiently, showing no mercy and leaving no loose ends. His principle is "not to possess anything you're unwilling to give up."

Target: Neil McCauley plans to rob a bank in downtown Los Angeles, but the bank is surrounded by heavy police presence, making a shootout inevitable.

Obstacle: Vincent Hanna (Al Pacino), a detective with the Los Angeles Police Department, is experienced, resolute, and a dedicated detective, posing a significant threat to Neil McCauley.

Who Wins: One side ultimately prevails in the battle between good and evil, but after watching the movie, you'll find it's not easy to determine who wins or loses.

Authoritative Rating: B-

The Killing

Thief Team: Johnny Clay, a seasoned con artist, forms his own con team. There are no big names in this team, and the members are nothing extraordinary, including a henpecked husband named George.

Target: Johnny Clay plans to shoot racehorses at the racetrack during a race to create chaos and steal two million dollars. He believes his plan is flawless and boasts about it.

Obstacle: However, unseen danger is approaching. George reveals Johnny Clay's plan to his shrewish wife, who then collaborates with her violent lover to push the situation towards an unpredictable direction.

Who Wins: The winner is undoubtedly the director of the film, Stanley Kubrick. "The Killing" is his groundbreaking work.

Authoritative Rating: A

Reservoir Dogs

Thief Team: This theft gang consists of eight people. Besides the mob boss and his son, there are six professional thieves hired by them. To maintain confidentiality, the mob boss gives these six people six color-related names: Mr. White (Harvey Keitel), Mr. Orange (Tim Roth), Mr. Blonde (Michael Madsen), Mr. Pink (Steve Buscemi), Mr. Brown (Quentin Tarantino), and Mr. Blue.

Target: The target is the diamonds from a jewelry store in Los Angeles.

Obstacle: Suddenly appearing police make the six colors start suspecting if they are caught in a premeditated scam.

Who Wins: "Reservoir Dogs" is Quentin Tarantino's debut work. The tense and exciting plot, witty and interesting dialogues, clever narrative structure, and unexpected ending make the audience fall in love with this style of movies.

Authoritative Rating: A
