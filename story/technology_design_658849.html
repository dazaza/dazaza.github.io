What should you do if your wife doesn't get pregnant? Experts from the Taiyuan Zhongshan Reproductive Medicine Hospital point out: Infertility refers to the inability of the female to conceive, which is caused by obstacles in the fertilization process or the early embryo failing to implant and being lost; while sterility refers to the female being able to become pregnant, but due to various reasons, miscarriage, premature birth, or stillbirth occurs, preventing pregnancy from reaching live birth. There are many factors and diseases that affect female conception, which can be roughly divided into ten aspects according to their location and cause.

--------------------------------Causes of infertility in wives--------------------------------

Infertility is a complex physiological process that must meet the following conditions: 1. The ovary releases normal eggs 2. Semen is normal and contains normal sperm 3. Eggs and sperm can meet and combine in the fallopian tube to form a fertilized egg 4. The fertilized egg is successfully transported into the uterine cavity 5. The endometrium is fully prepared for the implantation of the fertilized egg. Any abnormality in these steps can hinder conception. The cause of hindrance may lie with the female, the male, or both. It is recommended to go to a specialized hospital for detailed examination, treatment for azoospermia, clear diagnosis, targeted treatment, and not miss the best time for treatment. What should you do if your wife has been married for several months and hasn't gotten pregnant?

1. Blood tests may be involved in the examination, so breakfast should be avoided. Men and women should avoid sexual activity for 7-10 days before the examination.

2. Women should go to the hospital 2-3 days after the menstrual period ends for a systematic pelvic and reproductive tract patency examination and other tests. Men must abstain from sex for 3-5 days to check the quality of semen. Do not have sex before the examination, and both parties should be examined simultaneously. After the initial examination, the doctor will arrange the next step of examination or treatment based on the results.

3. In the process of finding the cause, both spouses need to undergo comprehensive and systematic examinations, and the first visit is particularly important. Because the cause of infertility may be the male, the female, or related to both, it is best for both spouses to go together during the first visit so that the doctor can understand the various situations of both parties, including past examination results and treatment processes. If clues are found, further examination priorities can be placed on either the husband or wife based on specific circumstances.

4. Bring all relevant clinical data before going, such as various lab reports, test results, and X-ray photos. Bringing previous examination results is just for reference, allowing experts to understand what tests the patient has undergone and their outcomes.
