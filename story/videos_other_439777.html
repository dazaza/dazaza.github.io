How to select tiles

by 888maggic on 2011-05-31 10:51:08

When it comes to home renovation, everyone needs to purchase tiles. There is a certain skill involved in buying value-for-money and satisfactory tiles. In general, the principle for selecting tiles is: 1) Look, 2) Listen, 3) Drop water, and 4) Measure with a ruler.

1. Look at the appearance. The color of the tiles should be even, with good surface gloss and flatness, regular edges, and complete patterns. Take out four or five pieces from a box and check for any color differences, deformations, or missing edges or corners.

2. Listen to the sound. Gently tap the tile with a hard object; the clearer and crisper the sound, the higher the degree of porcelain and the better the quality. You can also hold the tile by its corner with your left thumb, index finger, and middle finger, letting it hang loosely, and gently tap the middle-lower part of the tile with your right index finger. If the sound is clear and pleasant, it's a high-quality product; if the sound is dull and muffled, it's a lower-quality product.

3. Water drop test. You can drip water on the back of the tile and observe how quickly the water spreads and absorbs. Generally speaking, the slower the absorption, the higher the density of the tile; conversely, the faster the absorption, the looser the density, and the former indicates a better internal quality.

4. Measuring with a ruler. The higher the accuracy of the tile's edge length, the better the effect after laying. Buying high-quality tiles not only makes construction easier but also saves time and auxiliary materials. Use a tape measure to check the size of each tile and see if there are any differences in the surrounding dimensions; those with high precision are superior products.

Additionally, observe the hardness. Tiles with good hardness, strong toughness, and resistance to breakage are considered high-quality. Scratch each other's broken edges with tile fragments and examine whether the broken pieces are fine or loose, hard and brittle or softer, whether they leave scratches or scattered powder. For wall construction, if it belongs to the former, it is a high-quality product, and the latter indicates poor quality.

Whether the dimensions are standard is the key to judging the quality of the tiles. Use a tape measure or caliper to measure the diagonals, four sides, and thickness of the tiles to see if they are uniform. Good tiles produce crisp sounds, indicating high porcelain content. If the sound is "tatta" with a broken edge sound, it indicates hidden cracks inside the tile.

Good tiles make it convenient for professionals to install, resulting in standardized installation effects.
