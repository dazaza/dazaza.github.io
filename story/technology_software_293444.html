Tianxiang Investment: Star马Automobile Acquires Huali Automobile to Enhance Profitability＿Stock Basics

by xl0958870 on 2009-12-05 02:17:54

1. After the non-public offering, the company's main business will increase the production and sales of heavy trucks and chassis. Before the non-public offering, Xingma Automobile's main business was bulk cement trucks, concrete mixer transport trucks, and concrete pump trucks, making it the largest producer of tank-type special-purpose vehicles in China. In the first half of 2009, the company's revenue structure showed that the income from concrete mixer transport trucks accounted for 63% of total revenue, bulk cement truck income accounted for 29%, while concrete pump trucks and dump trucks each accounted for 4%. The purchased asset, Hualing Automobile, mainly focuses on the production and sales of heavy trucks, automobile chassis, and automotive parts. From the revenue data of the first three quarters of 2009, Hualing Automobile generated 2.5 billion yuan in revenue, which is 1.3 times that of the listed company Xingma Automobile's 1.9 billion yuan. It also realized a net profit of 200 million yuan, which is 3.9 times the 53.06 million yuan of the listed company Xingma Automobile.

2. Acquiring assets can improve the company's gross margin. Prior to the non-public offering, Xingma Automobile's primary raw material, automobile chassis, was mainly procured from Hualing Automobile, forming related party transactions. After the completion of the non-public offering, it is expected that the gross margin of existing products can be improved. Additionally, after the decline in raw material prices, the gross margin of Hualing Automobile's products has improved somewhat. In the first three quarters of 2009, the gross margin was 13.36%, higher than the 11.16% of the original specialized vehicle products. Therefore, it is anticipated that after the asset integration is completed, the overall gross margin of the company will be enhanced.

3. Positive outlook on the heavy truck industry where the acquired asset company is located. Currently, Xingma Automobile ranks first domestically in the specialized vehicle manufacturing industry with a market share of approximately 30%. Hualing Automobile ranks eighth domestically in the heavy truck industry with a market share of about 3%. With the impetus of the 4 trillion yuan investment plan, increased infrastructure construction, and the warming up of the logistics industry, since 2009, the sales of heavy trucks have recovered from their trough. The industry growth rate has shifted from a 60% decline in January to a 71% increase. It is predicted that the heavy truck industry will gradually become more prosperous as the economy warms, benefiting the company’s profitability.

4. The price of purchasing assets is relatively cheap, which can enhance the overall profitability of the company. Based on the disclosed net assets of the target assets for the first three quarters of 2009, the PB ratio for acquiring assets is 2 times and the PE ratio is 8 times. For the listed company, the PB ratio for the first three quarters is 3 times and the PE ratio is 32 times. The acquisition price is relatively low, which is beneficial for optimizing the assets of the listed company.

Profit forecast assumptions: 1) Completion of the non-public offering in 2009; 2) Expected improvement in the prosperity of the specialized vehicle and heavy truck industries due to economic recovery; 3) Improvement in gross margin with company integration; 4) Deduction of associated transaction income based on the 2008 associated transaction ratio. Assuming that the non-public offering to purchase assets will be completed in 2009, it is estimated to increase the 2009 EPS by 0.2 yuan and the 2010 EPS by 0.23 yuan. Thus, the projected EPS for 2009-2011 is 0.61 yuan, 0.71 yuan, and 0.86 yuan respectively. Calculating based on the closing price of 9.72 yuan on November 30th, the corresponding dynamic P/E ratios are 14 times, 12 times, and 10 times. After the transaction, the company's profitability will strengthen, and the valuation will have relative advantages. Based on a reasonable P/E ratio of 20 times for 2010, the corresponding price would be 14.3 yuan. Due to the current low valuation of the company and the positive outlook on the heavy truck industry, the company rating is upgraded to 'buy'.

Risk warning: 1) Approval risk for this transaction; 2) Risks associated with the progress and price changes of the restructuring; 3) Risk of tax back payments affecting current earnings; 4) Raw material price fluctuation risk.
