What to do if you get lymphedema

by sxykmgy on 2012-02-22 16:24:00

What to do if you have lymphedema? Lymphedema is a relatively common disease caused by an imbalance in the lymphatic system. This imbalance leads to the accumulation of lymph fluid in subcutaneous tissues. Over time, this can cause fibrous connective tissue proliferation in soft tissues, leading to tissue thickening and limb enlargement. In later stages, the skin becomes thicker, coarser, and as hard as elephant skin. So, what should be done if you have lymphedema? Let's invite experts from Shanxi Yikang Peripheral Vascular Hospital to introduce it below.

(1) Baking and Binding Therapy: This is a traditional treatment method in Chinese folk medicine. Zhang Dixin from Shanghai Second Medical University applied principles of traditional Chinese medicine combined with modern science and technology to systematize and standardize this therapy. It has good therapeutic effects on chronic limb lymphedema. The treatment principle is: through repeated heat stimulation, the temperature of the tissue increases, metabolism activities strengthen, promoting the regeneration of lymphatic vessels and the recovery of lymph fluid return. The application method is: place the affected limb into a radiative heat baking machine, with the temperature controlled around 90°C, once daily, each session lasting one hour, for a course of 20 days, using elastic bandages during the rest period.

(2) Microwave Baking Therapy: This is a new development of baking and binding therapy. Given that baking and binding therapy achieves over 90% effective treatment rate but has long courses and causes patients to sweat heavily and consume much physical energy, Zhang Dixin collaborated with Fudan University to develop a microwave baking machine. Its therapeutic mechanism is: microwaves are high-frequency radiation waves with strong penetration power. After tissue absorption, they quickly produce a thermal effect raising the temperature, promoting lymph fluid return and lymphatic vessel regeneration. The application method is: treatment once daily, each session lasting 45 minutes, for a course of 20 days. Rest for a month before starting the second course of treatment, using elastic bandages during the rest period, which can be loosened at night. Clinical use shows high efficacy, fast recovery, and none of the drawbacks of baking and binding therapy.

(3) Surgical Treatment: Most cases of lymphedema do not require surgical intervention. About 15% of primary lymphedema eventually need lower extremity plastic surgery. Current surgical methods, except for amputation, cannot cure lymphedema but can significantly improve symptoms.
