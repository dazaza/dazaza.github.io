Two Children Switched at a Wenzhou Hospital, Discovered Five Years Later

Same birth month and year, same gender, same hospital... With so many similarities, compounded by one oversight, the fates of two boys were rewritten.

Nearly five years have passed since Xiao Lu and Xiao Rui suddenly learned that the loving parents who doted on them every day were not their biological parents. The parents also discovered that the child they had cherished for five years was not theirs; their biological son affectionately called someone else "mom" and "dad," while being completely unfamiliar with them. This mistaken switch of children has unfortunately played out in two families — one in the urban area of Wenzhou and the other in Ruian.

Who Switched the Babies?

Let's go back to March 2005 at Wenzhou Youhao Hospital.

On March 19, Ms. Fu from Wenzhou gave birth to a son via cesarean section. The baby’s father, Mr. Bai, took a quick glance at his newborn son before leaving for work elsewhere, leaving only a nanny to accompany the mother and child in the hospital.

On March 22, Ms. Chen from Ruian also gave birth to a son via cesarean section.

On that day, the nurse routinely brought the newborns to be bathed. When the baby was returned, Ms. Fu noticed that the "305" tag hanging on her child's hand was gone.

Similarly, Ms. Chen's family noticed that after the bath, the baby's nose bridge had become higher.

Both families raised questions and approached the medical staff. Ms. Fu was told: "The tag fell off during the bath." Ms. Chen was told: "After the bath, the baby became more handsome— isn't that better?"

It was time for discharge, and both families happily took their babies home.

But was it really as the medical staff had said?

Ms. An reached out to Ms. Chen and Xiao Rui, inviting them to the hospital for a physical examination.

Among the many children entering the hospital lobby, Mr. Bai and Ms. Fu immediately recognized him: "This is our child!" They pointed to Xiao Rui.

The Truth No One Wanted to Know

"Dad, dad, catch my ball!" Xiao Lu (a pseudonym) came home from kindergarten and was playing joyfully with "Dad" Mr. Bai. Yesterday, when I knocked on the door of an ordinary household in Puxieshi, I didn't sense the enormous change this family was facing.

In Xiao Lu's room, there was a children's bed, a children's desk, and more toys. Xiao Lu wasn't shy at all; when the journalist arrived, he stuck close to Mr. Bai while we talked.

It was hard for others to find any resemblance between this "father and son." Xiao Lu's "mother," Ms. Fu, didn't look like him either. Outsiders would find it difficult to associate Xiao Lu with this couple. In reality, Xiao Lu had been living under the name of their son for nearly five years.

Mr. Bai said that after the birth of their son, relatives and friends kept asking if the child was really theirs, making them very uncomfortable. In July 2009, they conducted a paternity test, and the result confirmed what they feared most: Xiao Lu was not their biological son. When they learned that their biological son, Xiao Rui (a pseudonym), was growing up healthy and happy in Ruian, Mr. Bai and his wife were filled with mixed emotions of sorrow and joy.

The Bai couple hoped to reclaim their son as soon as possible. However, the family involved in the mix-up was struggling to accept the reality and remained hesitant.

Referring to this, Mr. Bai became agitated: "The earlier we exchange the children, the less harm it will cause to them. Isn't that true?"

"This Child Isn't Yours, Is He?"

Mr. Bai and his wife, Ms. Fu, ran a factory in Pingyang but lived in the urban area of Wenzhou. As Xiao Lu grew older, his distinct features became more apparent—his skin, eyes, and nose bridge did not resemble Mr. Bai or his wife. Some couldn't help but ask Mr. Bai, "Is this child really yours?" That was still a polite way of saying it. Others bluntly asked, "Is this child born to someone else?"

In contrast, Ms. Chen and her husband in Ruian were constantly praised by relatives for their child inheriting the best traits from both parents. Grandparents and in-laws were overjoyed.

When Xiao Lu was six months old, Mr. Bai, tired of the constant questioning, rushed to the hospital with the baby to match footprints left behind shortly after birth.

Unfortunately, because the baby's feet hadn’t been cleaned thoroughly at birth, the footprints were too blurry to match. Mr. Bai returned home with lingering doubts.

These doubts did nothing to diminish Mr. Bai and his wife's love for the child. It wasn’t until July 2009 that a kind-hearted person informed Mr. Bai, "Your Xiao Lu looks a lot like a tricycle rider." This made Mr. Bai even more uncertain: "Could my child have been switched with another baby whose parent rode a tricycle?"

Mr. Bai promptly took his child for a paternity test.

The Same Attitude From Both Children

After the hospital informed them of the possibility of a mix-up, about a month later, Ms. Chen also underwent a paternity test. The result, as expected, showed that the child was not biologically hers.

Both children had overheard snippets of adult conversations and seemed to sense something was amiss, though their young minds may not fully grasp the implications.

"Xiao Lu, who treats you the best?" "My mom and dad treat me the best." Here, "mom" and "dad" refer to Mr. Bai and Ms. Fu from the urban area of Wenzhou.

Xiao Lu had his own children's bedroom, desk, and bed. He boasted of having nine toy cars, four Transformers, and many other toys—even Dad's phone was his plaything.

Mr. Bai tested him: "What if I send you to a kindergarten in Ruian?" Xiao Lu immediately jumped onto him, raising his little fists: "I'll hit you! I'm not going!" Their deep bond was evident.

In Ruian, most people in the town had heard about the incident. Neighbors teased Xiao Rui: "We'll send you to Wenzhou." Normally friendly with neighbors, Xiao Rui would immediately get upset, turn around, and walk away. Now, Xiao Rui sometimes wakes up in the middle of the night looking for "mom." If "mom" is nearby, he calms down, but if she isn’t, he sits up crying loudly, calling for "mom." Ms. Chen noted that this behavior was new, likely due to his fear of being separated.

A Thunderclap Breaking the Peaceful Life

"What's the point of living anymore..." This was the sigh of the "grandfather" in Xiao Rui's home in Ruian upon learning that the child was not biologically theirs. Their lives were thrown into chaos.

The day before yesterday, I visited the Huang family in Ruian, where the child was mistakenly swapped. The family was engaged in business, and the father, Mr. Huang, avoided the journalist, refusing interviews and comments, saying only, "We can't accept it. We don't want the child to go to Wenzhou. We’ll think about it later." Then he hung up the phone.

Most people in the town knew about the mix-up involving the Huang family's child. A relative who partnered in business with Xiao Rui's "mom," Ms. Chen, said that the Huang family was still struggling to come to terms with the reality. They were unwilling to part with the child they had raised and found it hard to accept a stranger as their biological son. She added that Ms. Chen hadn't slept well in recent months, losing several kilograms and appearing much older.

Ms. Chen was traveling on business and instructed the journalist not to visit the grandparents, fearing they might be further distressed. Over the phone, she said that the entire family treated Xiao Rui like a precious gem. The elders emotionally could not handle the separation. The child's grandfather would lose his temper whenever they discussed the matter. Ms. Chen herself couldn't imagine what the future held. Xiao Rui was very attached to her, reluctant to leave her side even for a moment, and insisted on sleeping in the same bed. To separate them...

Speaking of Xiao Lu living in the urban area of Wenzhou, Ms. Chen said it was a completely unfamiliar child.

A week later, the results of the paternity test revealed no blood relation between Xiao Lu and his "mom," Ms. Fu. In other words, Xiao Lu was not Mr. Bai and Ms. Fu's biological child.

How could this be? Where was their biological son?

Mr. Bai turned to the Wenzhou Youhao Hospital where the child was born. Nearly five years had passed, and the head nurse and nurses from that time had already left the hospital. Deputy Director Ma Xiaobai convened relevant department personnel overnight to investigate.

Around March 19, 2005, twelve infants were born in the hospital. After excluding five female infants, seven male infants remained as potential candidates for the mix-up. From the records, some mothers were from Guizhou and Anhui provinces. Mr. Bai broke into a cold sweat at the thought of his child possibly being taken to Guizhou or Anhui. What would they do then?

The hospital was also concerned: Where was the other child?

With the support of relevant departments, the hospital eventually determined that the likelihood of Xiao Rui from Ruian and Ms. Fu's child being switched was the highest.

At the time, Xiao Rui was attending kindergarten in Ruian, growing up peacefully and happily under the care of his "grandparents" and "great-grandparents."

The Hospital Agrees to Compensation

The Bai family in the urban area of Wenzhou wanted to retrieve their child as soon as possible. On the other hand, the Chen family in Ruian, overwhelmed by the sudden news, could not accept the fact. They worried about how they would interact with the new child and whether the child they had raised for years would adapt to a different family. Too many uncertainties left Ms. Chen and her family hesitating.

During the interview process, almost everyone believed that retrieving the child as early as possible would be beneficial to his mental and physical well-being. Citizen Mr. Lu said, "Since the incident has happened, we should look at it positively. Exchanging the child means gaining a godson, having two sons—what's not good about that!"

Ultimately, emotions are not something that can be easily explained in a few words. After the exchange, both families would face issues regarding the child's education and psychological acceptance. Dr. Zhang Ying from the Children's Health Department of Wenzhou Medical College Affiliated Yuying Children's Hospital believed that exchanging or not exchanging would impact both children. The nurturing relationship is no less significant than blood ties. If both families communicate well and become friends, continuing to visit each other, it would be best for the children. Additionally, some suggested that children might compare their parents, potentially leading to thoughts of favoritism, which both families need to consider.

In between all this, there was also the issue of compensation from the hospital.

Wenzhou Youhao Hospital admitted the mistake from years ago, apologized for the harm caused to both families, and agreed to offer a substantial amount of money as compensation.

As for the Chen family in Ruian, they have yet to make a clear decision on whether to exchange the children. Unable to wait, Mr. Bai consulted a lawyer, considering legal action to retrieve his child sooner. Lawyer Liu Jun from Zhejiang Zhongjian Law Firm believed that parents have the right to raise their biological children, and the child should return to their biological parents. Lawyer Zhou Guang from Zhejiang Guangzhengda Law Firm stated that this involves social ethics; humans are not objects that can simply be exchanged, as emotional factors are involved. At five years old, the child is still naive, and the best solution would be for both sets of parents to negotiate and retrieve the child as soon as possible. The hospital failed in its duty during the care process and must take responsibility, and both families have the right to demand material and spiritual compensation from the hospital.
