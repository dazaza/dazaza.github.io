The person in the video was clearly a friend, but after transferring the money, it became apparent that it was a scammer.

by kaxiya on 2009-07-07 11:44:18

Mr. Qian was chatting on QQ at home when he suddenly noticed his brother-in-law's QQ avatar light up. Then, his brother-in-law opened a video chat window while sending the following text: "A friend of mine in Shanghai had a car accident and urgently needs money. Please transfer 10,000 yuan immediately to help. Account number: XXXXXXX." Mr. Qian immediately went to the bank and transferred 10,000 yuan to that account. Afterwards, his brother-in-law asked him to transfer another 20,000 yuan, which he did without hesitation. The next day, when Mr. Qian met his brother-in-law and mentioned this matter, he realized he had been deceived.

This is a real incident that happened in Xihu District a few days ago. Yesterday, the command center of the Hangzhou Public Security Bureau issued a warning that since April this year, online fraud cases involving impersonation of acquaintances on QQ have occurred frequently. Recently, scammers have even used high-tech methods such as playing video clips. In the past three months, there have been 80 such cases in Hangzhou, resulting in economic losses of nearly 340,000 yuan.

New tactics in QQ scams use high-tech "three-step" schemes to deceive people

Why did Mr. Qian see his brother-in-law in the video while chatting with the scammer? How did the scammer manage to do this?

The police explained that scammers first use hacker software to steal others' QQ accounts, then select targets from QQ friends, impersonate the QQ owner to chat with the target, and lure the victim into transferring money to a specified account or making online purchases for various reasons. Most of these victims are people who enjoy online chatting or online shopping. During the scamming process, in order to gain the victim's trust, some scammers constantly update their tactics and even use high-tech means.

The scam mainly involves three steps:

Step one: Scammers first use "virtual video" software to have video chats with unspecified QQ users and capture the other party's video during the chat.

Step two: After stealing the other party's QQ account using hacker software, the scammer impersonates the QQ owner and chats with QQ friends.

Step three: The scammer plays the previously captured video repeatedly in the video chat window, making the victim believe that the scammer is the QQ owner; after gaining the victim's trust, they find various excuses to defraud money.

Mr. Tan from Xiaoshan District also fell victim to this scam. Since the person on the other side was a very close buddy and they had a video chat, Mr. Tan didn't notice anything unusual and transferred 13,000 yuan to the scammer that day.

Police reminder: In case of similar situations, it’s best to confirm by phone

Although the tactic of showing a video is highly persuasive, some careful people may still have doubts. To prevent the other party from requesting voice chat, cunning scammers often avoid voice chat by claiming that the microphone is damaged, so as not to reveal the scam.

Since scammers send fraudulent messages to some friends on the stolen QQ contact list or directly send malware after stealing the account, this causes a chain reaction where one QQ account being stolen can lead to multiple QQ accounts being stolen, greatly increasing the occurrence rate of such fraud cases.

Such fraud cases involve a wide geographical area, strong concealment, and great deception. Besides Hangzhou, in recent times, similar cases have also occurred in Wenzhou, Ningbo, Jinhua, Shaoxing, Tongxiang, Lishui, and other places in our province.

The police remind netizens: 1. Do not casually accept files sent by others to prevent QQ numbers from being stolen due to Trojan virus infections. 2. When chatting, do not casually disclose personal information to strangers. 3. If an online friend makes financial requests, be cautious and verify through a phone call. 4. When QQ users discover that their QQ has been stolen, they should try to retrieve the password as soon as possible; otherwise, inform QQ friends of the theft promptly to prevent being impersonated.
