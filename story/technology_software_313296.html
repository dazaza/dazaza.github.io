Beckham: It's Not Impossible for Milan to Overtake Inter, Willing to Accept Any Position in the New Formation

by yinding999 on 2009-12-18 05:40:44

David Beckham: It's not impossible for Milan to surpass Inter Milan, willing to accept any position in the new formation

At seven in the evening, the bustling streets of Beverly Hills have become deserted. After sunset, neither the star-chasing tourists nor the enthusiastic vendors selling star maps can be seen anymore. On these intelligent maps indicating the residences of movie, TV stars, and sports celebrities, David Beckham's residence on San Isidro Avenue is one of the most clicked addresses. The taxi driven by the special correspondent Olivero of La Gazzetta dello Sport was immediately questioned by the security system as soon as it approached the intercom at the entrance to the villa. Initially worried about being denied entry without an appointment, but after confirming the identity of the visitor, the gates of the Beckham family unexpectedly opened slowly. Near the entrance gate is a garage where a motorcycle, two Lexus cars are parked, and there is also a basketball hoop hanging on the wall of the garage. In addition, the luxury vehicles parked on both sides of the road with low shrubs constantly attract people's attention: two black and white Porsche cars, a Cadillac, a Jeep, and a Land Rover. Walking along the road for about 150 meters, Beckham's house finally came into sight. Beckham and his two eldest sons, Brooklyn and Romeo, were waiting in the hall. Through the glass window of the entrance door of the residence, their figures were still clearly visible.

Entering the hall, Beckham was smiling in a white t-shirt, black casual pants, and red non-slip socks. The entrance hall of the villa was paved with inlaid marble floors, and a large wooden long table was full of various decorations, candlesticks, and a double-eared large vase. The door on the left side of the hall leads to Beckham's huge kitchen, where three servants were busy preparing dinner under the interested gaze of Beckham's eldest son Brooklyn and third son Cruz, while Victoria was nowhere to be seen. Romeo went to the living room alone to watch DVDs; a large LCD TV hung on the wall was playing an old Tom Hanks movie.

Due to the sudden visit, the special correspondent of La Gazzetta dello Sport obviously could not stay too long, especially since it was almost dinner time. However, looking at Beckham's smile, the reporter still asked if he could talk to him about AC Milan. "Of course," Beckham replied while hugging Brooklyn, who was dressed for summer and wanted to run out. Although Los Angeles' winter is not as cold as Milan's, the temperature that once reached 17 degrees during the day quickly drops after sunset. As soon as AC Milan was mentioned, Beckham felt a warm feeling in his heart. Returning to Italy seemed to encourage him, and his words proved it.

-David, how are you?

-I feel much better now. My ankle has healed, and I have no physical problems.

-Do you want to restart your journey with AC Milan?

-I am very happy to return to Italy: I can't wait to return to Milan, and I am counting down the days. There are only two weeks left now.

-Have you talked to Leonardo yet?

-We often send text messages. I think he congratulated me; he is doing a great job now, although the team lost their last game against Palermo. We will be able to chat face-to-face soon.

-Have you followed AC Milan's matches this season?

-Of course, I watched all of AC Milan's games, and I am very clear about what happened in Italy.

-So you know that AC Milan is ranked second in Serie A, five points behind Inter Milan at the top. Do you think AC Milan can win the Serie A championship?

-There is still a long way to go to win the Serie A championship, and the team needs to catch up and surpass Inter Milan. But anything is possible. Of course, it won't be easy, but all AC Milan players are very strong, and they are performing exceptionally well now, which makes me believe that AC Milan can win the Serie A championship.

-But you have always wanted the Champions League trophy more.

-That's normal. The teams competing in the Champions League are the biggest and strongest in Europe, such as Real Madrid, Barcelona, Manchester United, and Chelsea. I hope to win the UEFA Champions League trophy because it is a special performance. But traveling through Italian cities to play in the Serie A league is also a beautiful thing: I enjoy the Serie A league.

-AC Milan might face Manchester United in the round of 16. Would you feel happy or sad if that happens?

-I would be very happy if we had the chance to play against my former team. Since I left Manchester United in 2003, I haven't played at Old Trafford again: it's been seven years now, and I really want to step onto Old Trafford again. If I can play against Manchester United, it would be a wonderful thing, wouldn't it?

-Wonderful but challenging.

-It will definitely be very challenging, but also very exciting. I like big challenges, and I like playing such games.

-Since you are following the Serie A league and AC Milan, then you must know that Leonardo has changed the formation. Can Beckham find a position in the current 4-2-1-3 formation?

-Who knows... I can only say one thing: it doesn't matter whether I understand which position I will play or what Leonardo thinks of me now. I want to be part of AC Milan, and I will accept any position Leonardo assigns me to. Whether it's midfield, attacking midfielder, right wing, or even the bench, it doesn't matter. Which position I play is not important, what matters is how I perform in that position. And I want to reiterate: what I want most is to be part of this magical team.

-In June next year when the season ends, the South Africa World Cup will begin. Do you think England is one of the teams with a real chance to win the championship?

-Of course, there is no doubt about that. England is ambitious, but it is not the only one; many national teams are just as competitive as us. It will be a spectacular World Cup.

-Which team would you like to meet in the final?

-The Italian team, of course.

Well, David, see you in Milan, or even in Johannesburg.

(Garibaldi)
