Gold rush! Golden iPhone swept up globally

by anonymous on 2013-10-01 20:26:29

If you tried to buy the newly released iPhone 5S yesterday, you probably couldn't get the most dazzling one: the gold version. The golden 5S has completely sold out.

This situation is not surprising. The pre-sale situation a few days ago was already like this.

In China, consumers make reservations via their mobile phone numbers and then pick up at retail stores. Buyers need to send a text message to obtain a reservation code before entering the iPhone reservation interface. This effectively prevented scalpers from flooding in. According to Tencent Technology, starting at 6 am on September 17th, Apple launched online reservations for the iPhone 5s/5c. Successful reservers can pick up their phones from Apple's offline retail stores after September 20th. Within two hours of opening reservations that day, all versions and colors of the iPhone 5s had been reserved at three Apple retail stores in Beijing.

Yesterday (September 20th) early morning, at the Apple retail store in Wangfujing, Beijing, Sina Mobile asked the cashier about today's iPhone sales situation. As expected, almost everyone was buying the iPhone 5s, so much so that "I only processed two 5c phones in the morning," said the cashier. Among those purchasing the iPhone 5s, more than half chose the golden version. Many people believe that the golden 5s has the biggest difference compared to the previous generation product, the iPhone 5, which is the main reason they chose this color. The lack of buyers for the iPhone 5c also aligns with Sina Mobile's previous survey. The same configuration as the iPhone 5 and an overly high price made the 5c receive a lukewarm response.

A similar situation is also playing out in the Hong Kong market.

Do Asians prefer the gold color more? Apple may have underestimated Asians' admiration for gold, but it's not entirely true. In San Francisco's flagship store, due to stock shortages, the store could only receive 20 golden 5S units early on the 20th; at Manhattan's Fifth Avenue store, the golden 5S sold out within 20 minutes (of course, there were many Chinese faces in the long queue); someone on Twitter said that the entire state of Oklahoma had fewer than five golden 5S units available for sale.

Now, you can mail-order the iPhone 5s and iPhone 5c from Apple's online store. Currently, all colors of the iPhone 5s are available for order on Apple's official website, but the shipping date shows "October."

According to the Wall Street Journal, after seeing very strong market demand a few days ago, Apple has requested suppliers to increase production of the golden iPhone 5S by one-third. It is currently unclear whether component supply will affect the manufacturing of the iPhone 5s. Some media outlets suspect how experienced smartphone maker Apple could have such a big discrepancy between its sales forecast and actual conditions, suspecting it might be one of its marketing tactics. But there are also rumors that challenges related to the new fingerprint scanning function have limited the production of the iPhone 5s, and the situation of insufficient supply is not what Apple wants to see, which may affect the sales volume during the first weekend of its launch.

Compared to the strong demand for the golden 5S, the white and black 5S are slightly less favored, especially the black one. And the least popular is the 5c. In Zhongguancun, Beijing, you can get the 5c at a price 300 yuan lower than the official retail price.

Now, the 16G golden iPhone is selling for $1800 on eBay. On Taobao, Zhongguancun, and other places, golden iPhones are basically nowhere to be found, only reservation prices are available. The price of the golden iPhone in Zhongguancun has reached 9800 yuan, and there is no stock. In Chaoyang and Wangjing areas, it is said that the golden version of the iPhone 5s has been hyped up to 18,000 yuan (64GB).

The actual sales performance of the new iPhone will confirm whether Apple's smartphone strategy is correct. Strong demand may help alleviate concerns about Apple's position in the smartphone market. Currently, Apple phones are facing challenges from other manufacturers at both the high-end and low-end markets. CEO Cook recently told Bloomberg Businessweek that he only cares whether Apple is doing the right thing and providing high-quality products to specific users, and the low-cost business of other manufacturers is something Apple has no intention of getting involved in.

ISI Group analyst Brian Marshall pointed out that the "unprecedented queue" at New York's Fifth Avenue Apple flagship store is a very good sign, indicating that the demand for the new iPhone is strong. For Apple and its competitiveness, the iPhone 5c and iPhone 5s are very critical. He also pointed out: "Although some of the people waiting in line may likely be those who did not reserve the iPhone 5s, we still believe this indicates that iPhone users remain highly loyal."

Analysts expect Apple to achieve sales results this release weekend that are no worse than last year's iPhone 5 release weekend, especially since this year Apple included mainland China as part of the first round of releases. Analyst Carl Howe of Yankee Group believes that "the sales performance of the iPhone 5s/5c during the first weekend will depend on the market performance of the iPhone 5s." This analyst believes that as long as the supply is sufficient, the sales of the iPhone 5s/5c during the first weekend will exceed 7 million units. However, if the supply of the iPhone 5s is insufficient, the overall sales of the iPhone 5s/5c may not reach the 5 million unit sales of the iPhone 5 last year.

Apple is expected to announce sales data for the weekend next Monday.

The iPhone event will be held on September 10th.
