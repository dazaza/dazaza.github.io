My little darling is a "Madame Bovary"

by c0pmav on 2012-02-09 21:22:40

The money for buying train tickets to go home during the Spring Festival was still sent by me. She once expressed that she knows his financial capability, but she has fallen in love with him and it's hard to step back. Meanwhile, Chen Nan started to become lazier and lazier. So, I opposed her associating with that person, but she just wouldn't listen.

Her enthusiasm for brands, living paycheck to paycheck, and indulging in entertainment formed what I saw as three major faults of my beautiful wife. We quickly fell in love and stayed together for three years. I began to realize that Chen Nan didn't know how to live frugally. It wasn't that clothes couldn't be bought or that brand-name items were a must, but since we had a child, I felt that I could no longer be someone who lives paycheck to paycheck; I needed to save some money for the child's future education. However, Chen Nan completely disregarded our financial situation, visiting large stores every few days. Even a pair of summer shorts would cost hundreds of yuan, sometimes she would even go to big cities like Shanghai to shop, where a single pair of boots would equal my entire month's salary.

In reality, Chen Nan had already told me that he is from Jiangsu Province, a vice-general manager of a hotel, and when he came to Jinan to find Chen Nan, they had already spent three days together at a hotel. Therefore, I thought about delaying her for a few more years, waiting until the child grows up a bit before facing divorce.

The cost of going home for the Spring Festival still had to be sent by me. After working away from home for a year, there wasn't a single trace of money.

I thought, perhaps because Chen Nan stays at home all day, she might meet some unsuitable friends outside. Women like Chen Nan, who are passionate about brands and enjoy entertainment, may have been married for many years, and their children might already be old enough to buy soy sauce, but she is still young, only 28 years old, and very beautiful, with a figure that hasn't changed much after childbirth.

Last month, Chen Nan shouted again about wanting to work as the office director at a real estate company. I tried calling her several times, but whenever she rudely asked why I was calling, I didn't know how to respond.

This current vice-general manager of a certain hotel turned out to be a scammer, but it became clear that Chen Nan's calculations were wrong. Upon deeper understanding, this man doesn't even own a house. Later, her parents probably had no choice but finally agreed to our marriage. Friends around knew that I spoiled Chen Nan a lot. Although life back then wasn't affluent, I always accompanied her to big stores to buy brand-name clothes. They were expensive, but since she liked them, I was happy.

During the year she worked away from home, we rarely communicated by phone. Even when we did, there was nothing to talk about, and the calls ended coldly.

After working away for a year, I didn't even see a shadow of the money Chen Nan earned.

I also wondered why Chen Nan would look at me. Maybe it was fate.

Over the past year, she has constantly talked about divorce. Calming down, I realized it's not entirely Chen Nan's fault. Despite her extravagant spending and laziness, we've argued countless times, and our relationship has gradually grown distant.

Looking ahead, I don't think it's entirely her fault either. Given her endless spending and laziness, we've argued countless times, and our bond has loosened. When night falls at one or two o'clock and her phone vibrates incessantly, when she chats on QQ, and when she spends nights away from home, I realized there's someone else in her heart.

Last January, Chen Nan insisted on going to Jiangsu to work despite my opposition. During this time, her parents used numerous methods to try and reconcile us, but until the end, she never gave in to me. However, whenever I found her a job, she either complained about the low pay or the lack of ease, always finding excuses not to work. Instead, she went out with friends to bars and other places, even staying out all night.

At home, she should have had ample time to cook as a full-time housewife, yet I often had to cook dinner after returning from work.

Our marriage life was sweet and blissful, and even my mother-in-law had nothing to criticize. But ten years passed quickly, and now we're talking about divorce.

In early spring, Chen Nan returned for the Spring Festival, but she wasn't alone—she brought another man with her. Around Chen Nan, rumors swirled about this woman. The neighbors loved to gossip about the new men she had met.

One evening last year, my family and I were eating dinner together when a series of rapid knocks on the door broke the tranquility. Opening the door, there stood Chen Nan, drenched in the rain, her red dress clinging tightly to her well-proportioned body. With tears streaming down her face, she ran into my arms: "My parents kicked me out, I want to be with you."

Coincidentally meeting you with a respiratory infection and coughing, I happened to have cold medicine in my pocket. "Nannan caught a cold, fortunately, I have some cold medicine here."

And so, our romance began with an accidental greeting. Back then, she was working as a waitress in our single apartment. Her beauty naturally drew the attention of men, so I knew her name, but our interaction was limited to exchanging greetings when we met.

A year later, we had a child, and Chen Nan never went out to work, placing all the economic pressure on me.

When I noticed the frequent text messages, I jotted down the number and called it, berating the person on the other end. That man lacked the courage to admit anything and instead tried to cover up by saying, "We're just ordinary friends."

I thought, if she's determined, and the child will be taken care of by grandparents, then let her go.

I imagined that she must have had her own plans, trying to find a wealthy man in her prime to elevate herself into the upper class, much like the Cinderella who wore glass slippers to escape poverty. Chen Nan also longs for her glass slipper.

How could someone like Chen Nan, with almost no work experience, little understanding of the real estate industry, and no academic qualifications, easily become an office director? Of course, I knew this was a scam, likely involving under-the-table deals, but she insisted on going, claiming she was very close friends with the boss of the real estate company.

As the Spring Festival approached, Chen Nan returned for the holiday, but she wasn't alone; she carried thoughts of another man in her heart.
