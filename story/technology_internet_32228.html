Application acceleration of Chinese domain names puts native language Internet surfing in the fast lane

by now on 2007-10-25 14:42:38

Since the launch of Chinese-language domain names, they have been drawing attention from the government and enterprises. Their scope of use has gradually expanded and now their popularization is further accelerating. With the Olympics capturing global attention, to facilitate domestic residents buying tickets and participating, the National Olympic Organizing Committee activated the Chinese-language domain name "Olympic Tickets.中国" for online ticketing. This further promoted people's awareness of Chinese-language domain names, bringing them into a stage of rapid popularization and application, allowing native language internet access to enter the fast lane.

Chinese-language domain names are based on the Chinese language and leverage the advantage of the mother tongue. Not long after their introduction, over 5,000 national governmental institutions, including the National Development and Reform Commission, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, the State Administration for Industry and Commerce, as well as local governments at all levels, took the lead in activating Chinese-language domain names. Subsequently, enterprises such as China Telecom, Industrial and Commercial Bank of China, Haier, Lenovo, and Alibaba also almost universally adopted them. These enterprises have reaped the benefits brought by Chinese-language domain names, achieving twice the results with half the effort in publicity efforts and steadily increasing customers. This has further motivated enterprises to actively adopt Chinese-language domain names.

At the same time, the maturity of technology has made the application of Chinese-language domain names more widespread. Mainstream browsers worldwide, represented by IE7.0, Firefox2.0, and Netscape8.0, have all fully supported Chinese-language domain names. According to the latest statistics, more than 70% of mainstream computer models in the Chinese market have pre-installed the Vista operating system. It is well known that this system fully supports Chinese-language domain names, and based on this, including Internet Explorer, Outlook, and Foxmail email systems also support Chinese-language domain names. The technical support fully demonstrates the major trend of accessing the internet in one's native language and effectively promotes the popularization and application of Chinese-language domain names.

Industry insiders believe that the widespread use of Chinese-language domain names is inseparable from their unique characteristics. Compared to English domain names, over 90% of the public believes that Chinese-language domain names are easier to remember and use; more than 70% of internet users indicate that the native language Chinese better carries the brands of Chinese enterprises. For example, compared to www.tickets.beijing2008.cn, people find it much easier to remember the website address composed of Chinese characters like "Olympic Tickets.中国". To promote Chinese-language domain names and popularize native language internet access, Chinese internet service companies represented by Now.CN (www.now.cn) have been making relentless efforts in recent years. It is the domestic service provider with the largest number of Chinese-language domain names. Recently, Now.CN launched a bundled service offering both Chinese and English domain names: Chinese.CN + English.CN for 281 yuan, and Chinese.COM + English.COM for 288 yuan, effectively promoting people's awareness and usage of Chinese-language domain names.

Experts predict that with the joint promotion of the government, media, and societal forces, the use of Chinese-language domain names by the general public will inevitably become an important choice, and barrier-free native language internet access will truly be quickly realized.
