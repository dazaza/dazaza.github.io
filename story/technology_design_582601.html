IV. FAQs about Collagen Tripeptides:

1. How to distinguish between first-generation, second-generation, and third-generation collagen products?

Answer: First, explain what third-generation collagen is: Third-generation collagen is commonly known as collagen tripeptide or tripeptide collagen. Typically, a collagen molecule is composed of 3000 amino acids (3000 molecular weight), making it difficult for the body to absorb and utilize, and impossible for the skin to absorb directly.

Tripeptide collagen breaks down 3000 amino acids into the smallest collagen molecule structure, i.e., a combination of three amino acids.

Tripeptide collagen has better absorption properties than ordinary collagen and can directly penetrate into the dermis layer, bringing better moisturizing effects.

1) The molecular weight of large molecular collagen is around 300,000 daltons, that of collagen polypeptides is 1000-3000 daltons and above, while that of collagen tripeptides is 280 daltons.

2) In 100ml of cold water at 4°C, dissolve 50g of sample collagen: After 30 minutes, it does not completely dissolve, and the solution becomes turbid; CTP collagen tripeptides: After 9 minutes, it completely dissolves, and the solution remains clear. Collagen tripeptides have smaller molecular weights, better solubility, and stronger stability.

3) The absorption pore size in human skin and the small intestine is below 1000 daltons. Large molecular collagen cannot be absorbed, some collagen polypeptides can be absorbed, but collagen tripeptides can be 100% absorbed. So, their main difference lies in the size of the molecular weight. Collagen tripeptides are the basic structure of human skin, consisting of three amino acids combined. The size of the molecular weight determines the product's absorption properties and effectiveness in many aspects. Collagen tripeptides with a molecular weight of 280 daltons represent the most perfect collagen structure.

2. What is collagen tripeptide? What is CTP collagen tripeptide? What's the difference?

Answer: Collagen tripeptide (collagen tripeptide, abbreviated as CTP) is a sequence composed of three amino acids arranged as "glycine (G)-proline (P)-X (other amino acids)". It is the smallest unit that gives collagen biological activity. Its structure can be simply represented as GLY-X-Y, with a molecular weight of 280D. Collagen tripeptide and CTP collagen peptide or CTP collagen tripeptide are the same product, just expressed differently.

3. Compared to the first two generations of products, what are the advantages of collagen tripeptides?

Answer: Similar to question 1, mainly smaller molecular weight, better absorption, and higher utilization rate.

4. What is the technical advantage of collagen tripeptides?

Answer: Collagen tripeptides are obtained through globally patented composite enzyme gradient enzymatic hydrolysis technology and peptide separation technology, resulting in lower molecular weight after purification, with higher absorption and utilization rates.

5. Relationship between collagen, collagen polypeptides, collagen oligopeptides, and collagen tripeptides?

Answer: Collagen-collagen polypeptides-collagen oligopeptides-collagen tripeptides are arranged in order of decreasing molecular weight.
