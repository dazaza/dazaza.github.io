In 2008, how can Chinese Baijiu better and faster go global?

In the mid-20th century to the present, with the advent of economic globalization, multinational corporations have controlled more and more enterprises through forms such as "acquisition, holding, merger, brand output".

Multinational corporations control 70% of overseas direct investment and 50% of commodity exports globally. They master industry standards, core technologies, and business models, rather than adapting costumes for makeup, and largely determine the rules of competition and development trends.

Facing the approachable posture of multinational corporations and the grand internationalization wave, our country's alcohol enterprises actively implement internationalization strategies, improve their level of internationalized operations, cooperate or compete with "foreign capital giants", achieve the "going out" strategy, and accelerate the internationalization process of our country's alcohol industry.

Currently, the international development trend of our country's alcohol industry is good. The international operation level and management capabilities of alcohol enterprises have significantly improved, and the "going out" development strategy has yielded significant results.

In 2005, China's Baijiu export earnings were only $160 million. As a major Baijiu exporting province, Sichuan Province's Baijiu exports from January to September 2006 accounted for half of the national Baijiu export volume.

According to customs statistics, from January to September 2006, the total export value of alcoholic beverages in Sichuan Province was $42.48 million, an increase of 23.67% year-on-year; the total export volume was 2017 kiloliters, an increase of 24.81% year-on-year. Among them, Wuliangye Group's alcohol export earnings were $38.07 million, Jian Nan Chun Group's alcohol export earnings were $2.22 million, and Luzhou Laojiao Group's alcohol export earnings were $1.41 million.

In the first three quarters of 2007, China's Baijiu exports were approximately 30.5 thousand hectoliters, beer approximately 132.3 thousand hectoliters, and wine approximately 4.6 thousand hectoliters.

Wuliangye, Maotai, and others proposed Baijiu internationalization years ago and invested substantial human and material resources. Jian Nan Chun also spent a large amount of money to hire former US President Clinton to promote its internationalization. However, overall, domestic enterprises' overseas expansion of Baijiu is still far from sufficient, with little effect.

According to statistics from the commerce department, Wuliangye's export volume last year was only $100 million, Jian Nan Chun was $2.2 million, and the export data of other famous liquor enterprises was negligible. The international journey of Chinese Baijiu is long, and there is still a long way to go in the future.

Where does Baijiu face difficulties in going global?

Some people say that the difficulty for Chinese Baijiu to go global lies in the standards, and the first thing to solve is to brew according to international standards. Some people say that the first thing for Chinese Baijiu to solve is the excessive alcohol content problem. The mainstream alcoholic beverages in the world, such as whisky and French brandy, are all low in alcohol content; others say that Chinese Baijiu is a regional product and can only be accepted in areas with concentrated Chinese populations such as China; still others say that the difficulty for Chinese Baijiu to go global lies in culture and talent, etc. In fact, the main reasons why the internationalization of Chinese Baijiu is extremely difficult can be attributed to internal and external factors.

From the perspective of external causes, i.e., the external difficulties of Baijiu internationalization, they mainly manifest in the following aspects:

First, whether Baijiu standards meet international quality standards determines whether it can go global. Whether it can enter the international market, standards are an important factor. Internationally, food safety control standards are very high, and for alcoholic beverages, they are even stricter. Some products may be quite famous in China, but once tested against international standards, they would encounter significant crises. This greatly restricts the export of Baijiu products.

Second, whether the taste and flavor can suit foreign consumers. Different countries have different ways of consuming alcoholic beverages, and almost every country has its own unique ethnic alcoholic beverage. The internationalization of Baijiu means making consumers from other countries accept the consumption method of Chinese Baijiu, which is not easy to achieve.

Third, the concept of Baijiu internationalization itself is a big issue. Even if we consider Baijiu going global as Baijiu internationalization, it still means we need to figure out which country or region we will export Baijiu to. In international trade, each country has certain regulations for its imported goods, and completely free and barrier-free international trade does not exist.

From the perspective of internal causes, i.e., problems inherent to Chinese Baijiu, they mainly manifest in the following aspects:

First, the domestic Baijiu market is chaotic, with low market concentration and intense market competition.

A large number of marketing resources of Baijiu enterprises are concentrated in the domestic market. Although some Baijiu giants have made efforts for Baijiu internationalization, the results are not obvious. Many famous liquor enterprises hold the idea of "pacifying the inside before repelling the outside," believing that to enter the international market, one must first establish a foothold in the domestic market.

In recent years, with the warming up of the domestic high-end liquor market and the adjustment of national liquor taxation, although domestic market competition is intense, the profits of the domestic liquor market are still very objective, with Wuliangye's gross profit margin reaching over 70%. Therefore, Baijiu enterprises prefer to invest marketing resources in the domestic market, especially in the development of mid-to-high-end and high-end liquors. Developing the international Baijiu market involves great risks, and there are no truly successful cases yet. Therefore, although Baijiu enterprises raised the slogan of Baijiu internationalization early on, the actual implementation is "all talk and no action," and even used it as a means to operate the domestic market in reverse.

Second, cultural barriers and weak branding hinder Baijiu entering the international market.

Culture is the eternal soil of a brand, without culture, the brand loses its luster. Nowadays, China's attraction to other countries in the world is continuously increasing, so local Baijiu enterprises should actively dig and package the national wine culture and promote it to the world.

Wuliangye Group shouted out the slogan "China's Wuliangye, the world's Wuliangye," showing that the Baijiu industry has already developed a sense of national responsibility. However, the numerous brands under Wuliangye, such as Huobajiu, Laogongfang, Guoyuchun, Songfuye, Liuyanghe, etc., have issues like overlapping positioning, scattered selling points, and inconsistent images, affecting the overall brand image of Wuliangye. To create a world-renowned brand, Baijiu brand development must continue the traditions of national culture, using China's profound and inclusive historical culture to forge this national brand, showcasing the thoughts and spirit of Chinese wine culture, spreading the essence of Chinese national culture, and highlighting the spirit of the Chinese nation. In this regard, Chinese Baijiu enterprises clearly have much room for improvement.

Third, lack of talent. Chen Yu, General Manager of Guangdong Zhuge Brewing Company, believes that talent is a key factor constraining Baijiu exports. Currently, there are no dedicated talents to manage this market. Nowadays, many companies are targeting overseas markets, and Zhuge Brewing is preparing for exports, mainly facing Southeast Asia, Japan, South Korea, etc., with the Korean market currently being operational. Therefore, Baijiu internationalization differs from domestic market development, requiring knowledge of Baijiu, Baijiu marketing, and professional international trade knowledge. However, Chinese Baijiu enterprises lack these specialized talents. Baijiu internationalization requires not just enthusiasm and funds, but also talent.

Of course, the difficulties in Baijiu internationalization extend far beyond the above points. Some have pointed out that basic research on Chinese Baijiu is insufficient, the labeling of Baijiu components does not meet international requirements, the promotion of Chinese Baijiu internationally is lacking, market development work lags behind, and there is a lack of capital operations, among other issues.

What is the prospect of Baijiu going global?

The road to Baijiu internationalization is fraught with difficulties, but with the globalization of the world economy, the impact of foreign spirits on the domestic liquor market, and increasingly fierce competition in the traditional Baijiu market, domestically growing Baijiu enterprises are becoming more eager to enter the international market. It is a trend that China's Baijiu will go global.

From the current trend of internationalization in China's liquor industry, the pace of promoting internationalization has significantly accelerated. China's liquor enterprises will develop towards scaling up, creating brands, building networks, ensuring high quality, achieving high added value, and "going out." The prospects for the internationalization of the liquor industry are very promising. Accelerating the pace of exploration will undoubtedly speed up the process of internationalizing China's liquor industry. Now, many large liquor enterprises in China view going international, establishing international companies, and participating in international competition as strategic goals. They have gradually realized that internationalization is a comprehensive concept involving all aspects such as business management, core competitiveness, human resources, financial management, etc., and it is the fundamental guarantee for the growth and strength of enterprises.

China's liquor industry has begun to implement strategic cross-border mergers, acquisitions, and investments, which will make China's liquor enterprises stronger through foreign investments. In the coming period, cross-border mergers and acquisitions in China's liquor industry will rapidly develop. Some predict that in the next five years, China's liquor industry's actions to merge and acquire foreign production bases, brands, and channel assets will grow rapidly.

This year's liquor market seems to have entered the Warring States era, with acquisition wars breaking out everywhere. The world's largest distilled spirits group, Diageo, acquired 16.64% of the shares of Swellfun and became its second-largest shareholder. France's Hennessy acquired 55% of the shares of Sichuan Wenjun Liquor Factory Co., Ltd. under Jian Nan Chun (Group). These well-known domestic high-quality strong spirits enterprises are striving to open up international markets and enter the global sales network, such as the global procurement lists of international retail giants like Metro, Walmart, Carrefour, Tesco, etc.

From the current export situation of China's liquor products, there is enormous development space for the internationalization of China's liquor industry. The annual export volume of liquor nationwide is more than 30,000 kiloliters, accounting for only 0.04% of the total national liquor volume. Only eight enterprises have an export volume of more than 100 kiloliters, and the export volume of advantage brand liquor enterprises is also negligible. Such export volumes leave a large market margin for future exports.

All of these provide rich imaginative development space for the internationalization of China's liquor industry.
