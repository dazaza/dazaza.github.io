Symptom One: Dehydration and Dryness

UV rays can quickly dehydrate epidermal cells, leading to dryness, redness, flaking, loss of luster, tightness, and even flakiness, making the skin feel rough. Makeup may appear cakey, and skincare products may cause stinging and discomfort.

Beauty School Countermeasure: Hydration and Moisturization

Like us under the sun, our skin feels thirsty and needs refreshing drinks. Applying a post-sun repair mask can soothe swelling and pain while rapidly replenishing lost moisture. Alternatively, soak cotton pads in soothing toner and apply them to uncomfortable areas to relieve skin stress. After bathing, when pores are fully open, use repair creams containing aloe gel and seaweed essence to maximize skin comfort. To reduce skin burden, choose oil-free products. Use a hydrating essence before applying makeup in the morning, which will make foundation and sunscreen easier to spread, achieving dual benefits.

Symptom Two: Redness and Allergy

Sun exposure allergies may have two causes: one is UV allergy, where those sensitive to UV rays must be extra cautious during summer; the other is acute allergy caused by reduced skin resistance due to prolonged sun exposure.

Beauty School Countermeasure: Calming Relief

When facing sun-sensitive issues, first calm the skin and lower its surface temperature for physical relief of redness and heat. Be sure not to wash your face with hot or warm water. Many post-sun repair products contain cooling calming ingredients, bringing a refreshing sensation to the skin. If you don't have specific post-sun repair products, refrigerate daily skincare items for a similar effect. At this time, preventing inflammation and further stimulation-induced sensitivity is crucial. Avoid using rough items to wipe your face, minimize unnecessary hand contact, and temporarily halt exfoliation until the skin fully recovers.

Symptom Three: Aging and Slackening

The greatest harm of UV rays lies not only in tanning but also in accelerating aging, which is hard to prevent. Post-sun facial skin becomes slackened and thin, with enlarged pores. Fragile areas like the corners of the eyes and mouth easily develop fine lines with expression changes. Increased cellular aging and free radicals lead to skin laxity and loss of youth. Without remedial measures, irreversible wrinkles may develop.

Beauty School Countermeasure: Anti-aging Repair

Generally, post-sun fine lines can be partially relieved through hydration, but this often addresses surface phenomena. For fine lines that penetrate deeper than the stratum corneum, relieving and preventing more severe conditions requires addressing the skin's antioxidant issues first. UV exposure generates large amounts of free radicals, accelerating cell oxidation. Using products with antioxidants such as vitamin C and derivatives can effectively inhibit free radical production and also whiten the skin. Repairing sun-damaged and free radical-affected skin cells, supplementing collagen and peptides through beauty products to restore skin firmness and elasticity, is the second step in handling post-sun fine lines, restoring vitality and renewing the skin.
