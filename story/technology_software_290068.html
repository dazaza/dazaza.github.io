The so-called stain removal ability refers to the ability of detergents to remove visible stains on clothes, such as oil stains, bloodstains, sweat stains, food stains, and stains on collars and cuffs. On the other hand, maintaining whiteness means keeping the washed clothes bright and fresh without becoming gray or dull. A good detergent powder should perform well in both aspects, and the performance of the detergent powder is determined by its components.

Selection

Choose brands that are produced by regular manufacturers and sold through normal distribution channels to avoid counterfeit or inferior products.

Look at the appearance. The packaging should be intact, sealed tightly without leakage, and the powder should be white (if it is colored powder, it should be uniform, but it is recommended to buy white powder). When shaking the detergent powder bag, you should feel the product flowing freely without clumping.

Check the instructions. Do not easily believe incomprehensible content, such as magical particles, stain removers, etc., which are vague and beautiful names. Pay attention to whether there are specific components, especially the descriptions of enhancers such as biological enzyme preparations and bleaching agents. The use of these enhancers often determines the difference in quality between detergents.
