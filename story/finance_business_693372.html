Hemorrhoids are a disease growing on the anus, the human excretory outlet. Its growth and development have much to do with people's living habits, working and studying environment, walking and fatigue, diet and sleep.

The dentate line is located at the junction of the anal canal skin and rectal mucosa, which is the basis for classification. There are many ways to classify, type and stage hemorrhoids. Clinically, they are generally divided into three types according to the location: those occurring above the dentate line are called internal hemorrhoids; those occurring below the dentate line are called external hemorrhoids; those occurring both above and below the dentate line and connected together are called mixed hemorrhoids.

Hemorrhoidal nucleus located inside the anal door mucosa is called internal hemorrhoids, near the inside of the anal door is called external hemorrhoids, and having both is called mixed hemorrhoids. The symptoms of hemorrhoids include pain in the affected area, bloody stool, and in severe cases, hemorrhoid mass will protrude outside the anus (prolapse), shrinking back after defecation.

According to different symptoms of hemorrhoids and the degree of harm to human health, internal hemorrhoids can be divided into four stages:

Stage I: no pain, mainly characterized by bloody stool, excessive secretions, and itching;

Stage II: bloody stool, internal hemorrhoids prolapse during defecation but can spontaneously reduce;

Stage III (also known as late stage): internal hemorrhoids prolapse outside the anal door, or each time prolapse outside the anal door during defecation, cannot spontaneously reduce, must be manually pushed back;

Stage IV: internal hemorrhoids prolapse outside the anus and cannot be reduced back inside the anus. This is the most serious condition of internal hemorrhoids.

External hemorrhoids are mainly characterized by swelling around the anus, which can be divided into the following types:

(1) Thrombosed external hemorrhoids: the main characteristics are sudden onset, severe pain, obvious feeling of heaviness, occasionally systemic symptoms. Local examination shows a raised lump beside the anus, a hard and smooth lump can be palpated under the skin, with obvious tenderness.

(2) Inflammatory external hemorrhoids: often caused by damage and infection of the perianal skin, mostly accompanied by anal pain, which worsens during defecation, bloody stool, and a small amount of secretion in the anal area. Local examination shows a raised lump beside the anus, redness, obvious congestion, tenderness, sometimes accompanied by general discomfort and fever.

(3) Varicose vein type external hemorrhoids: the anus margin is raised into an elliptical shape, soft to touch, not painful, dark purple swelling can be seen when straining during defecation, the size can shrink after defecation or rest, caused by subcutaneous varicose veins, also the result of late-stage internal hemorrhoids. Generally, only feel swelling discomfort, worsen during defecation, and have pain symptoms when inflamed.

(4) Connective tissue type external hemorrhoids or skin tag external hemorrhoids: the perianal skin wrinkles become larger, connective tissue proliferates, forming many skin tags of different sizes and shapes.
