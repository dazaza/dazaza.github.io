Ivan: Players lose concentration, substitutions not giving up the game

by ztt0288868 on 2010-04-19 18:24:44

Our agency reports that Shandong Luneng lost 2-3 to Sanfrecce Hiroshima. The coach of Luneng, Ivan, said after the game: "The results of the AFC Champions League are not ideal. We need to find the reasons, and the players must stay highly focused during the 90-minute match."

At the post-match press conference, Ivan said: "Firstly, I congratulate the opponent on their success. Just as I had foreseen before the match, this was a fully open attack vs attack game. Both sides wanted to win, and the more reasonable result should have been a draw. We made mistakes at critical moments, which led to our ultimate defeat in the match."

In the first three rounds of this year's Chinese Super League, Shandong Luneng performed well, but their results in the AFC Champions League were not good. Ivan expressed regret about this, saying: "The results of the AFC Champions League matches are not ideal, but our performance on the field is still quite good, especially in the away game against Pohang Steelers when we got a penalty at the 85th minute but failed to score. In the game against Hiroshima, we once controlled the field situation, but were still scored against by the opponent in stoppage time. The group in the AFC Champions League is very strong, we lack experience, and there are some technical aspects we didn't handle well."

In the other group H match of the AFC Champions League that has already ended early, South Korea's Pohang Steelers drew with Adelaide United at home, and both teams advanced together. During halftime, Ivan already knew the result, but he frankly admitted that the team did not give up. "The substitutions in the second half were not for giving up the game, but due to player injuries. Han Peng pulled a muscle, Cui Peng had a cold. I originally planned for them to only play half a game. From these few AFC Champions League games, if we want to go further, we must maintain 100% concentration."

The coach of Sanfrecce Hiroshima, Petkovic, believed that effectively controlling the game and being good at seizing opportunities were the "treasures" that led to the team's victory. "Football matches are like this; if you don't seize the opportunities at the start, you will be punished. Our team didn't seize the opportunities in the first half, so we conceded a goal in stoppage time," he said.

Petkovic stated that conceding a goal in stoppage time in the first half put the team in a passive position, so they strengthened their offense in the second half, played well together, and took advantage of Shandong Luneng's mistakes to ultimately turn defeat into victory. (Xiaocheng)
