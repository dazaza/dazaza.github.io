Arsene Wenger's Outburst Fuels Arsenal's Comeback: "Now We Don't Lack Forwards"

By reporter Chuan Liu, London — After securing their first victory at Anfield in six years, Arsenal captain Cesc Fabregas revealed the key to the team’s second-half turnaround against Liverpool, which saw them score two goals to overturn a deficit. It was all thanks to an outburst by manager Arsene Wenger:

"So how did we manage the comeback? Honestly, it was because the boss woke us up during halftime in the dressing room. He was like a hairdryer; we've never seen him so angry. He was extremely disappointed with our first-half performance and said we didn’t deserve to wear Arsenal jerseys. And he was right; our first half was a mess. But fortunately, we quickly got back on track in the second half."

When asked about the outburst, Wenger commented: "Yes, I was very dissatisfied with the first half. In the first 45 minutes, Liverpool completely controlled the game, and we should have been grateful for only trailing by one goal, as they could easily have been leading by two goals at halftime, which would have ended the match prematurely. So, we needed to play at our normal level in the second half. As a result, the first half belonged to Liverpool, and the second half was ours. Luckily, we scored one more goal than them. Want to know exactly how furious I was in the dressing room at halftime? I don’t want to talk about that now, as it's an internal secret of our dressing room. The reason for our poor first-half performance wasn’t due to a lack of desire to win among the players but rather the young players being burdened by the common fear of failure in big matches. Fortunately, we managed to overcome it in the second half."

In response to media inquiries about signing a striker in the winter transfer window, the professor said, "You can look at our total number of goals this season (40 goals in 15 Premier League games, 17 goals in 8 Champions League games, 4 goals in 3 League Cup games, 61 goals in 26 games across all competitions). Andrei Arshavin could have scored two or three goals today, so I think we don’t lack forwards."

After securing their first victory in the top-four mini-league this season against Liverpool, Arsenal has reduced the gap with league leaders Chelsea to just six points. If they win the postponed second-round home game against Bolton, they will only trail Chelsea by three points and be level with Manchester United, which would greatly benefit their chances of reversing the standings to claim the title. However, regarding the renewed hope, Fabregas remains calm: "It's still too early to talk about the league championship. You know, the race for the league title has never been as open as it is this season. You can beat any team, but you can also lose to any team at any time, and I actually like this feeling." Talking about Arshavin's goal, Fabregas expressed, "Arshavin's goal was unbelievable, but none of us were particularly surprised because he does this kind of thing every day in training."
