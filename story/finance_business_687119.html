Insurance industry explores mandatory exit mechanism

by aochun8383 on 2012-03-02 13:47:13

Recently, Dalian Insurance Regulatory Bureau jointly issued the "12th Five-Year Plan for the Development of Dalian Insurance Industry" (hereinafter referred to as the "Plan") with the Financial Development Bureau of Dalian. The "Plan" clarifies the development goals, key tasks and countermeasures during the "12th Five-Year Plan" period for the insurance industry in Dalian. The "Plan" emphasizes that during the "12th Five-Year Plan" period, the Dalian insurance industry should take scientific development as its theme, accelerate the transformation of the development model as its main line, and use the prevention and resolution of risks as a guarantee. It should closely revolve around the two national strategies of revitalizing the old industrial bases in Northeast China and developing and opening up the coastal economic belt of Liaoning Province, achieving the goals of significantly enhanced industry strength, a more sound market system, effective function and role play, significant transformation of the development model, outstanding innovation and development, comprehensive strengthening of insurance regulation, and obvious improvement of social image. By 2015, premium income is expected to reach 30 billion yuan, total assets of the insurance industry exceed 60 billion yuan, insurance depth reaches 3%, insurance density reaches 5000 yuan per person, and the status of the insurance industry in the construction of Dalian's regional financial center will become more prominent.

The "Plan" proposes that during the "12th Five-Year Plan" period, the Dalian insurance industry will focus on completing three main tasks to promote the scientific development of the industry: First, accelerate market system construction, actively develop insurance headquarters, support the development of institutions in Lian, assist the development of intermediary agencies, and actively promote the construction of a regional financial center. Second, fully leverage the functions of insurance, vigorously serve the construction of Northeast Asia Shipping Center, participate in the construction of a multi-level social security system, serve the construction of a new socialist countryside, participate in public service and social management innovation, and comprehensively serve the overall situation of Dalian's economic and social development. Third, accelerate the promotion of transformation and upgrading, achieve "three transformations" in development concepts, ideas, and directions, optimize the "four structures" of market entities, businesses, channels, and regions, enhance the "five capabilities" of risk management, risk sharing, serving the economy and society, sustainable development, and profitability, and promote "six innovations" in products, channels, services, management, systems, mechanisms, and regulation to comprehensively improve the overall competitiveness of the industry.

To ensure the successful completion of key tasks, the "Plan" proposes the following countermeasures: First, adhere to integrity in business, shape an industry value system centered on integrity, and enhance the industry's social credibility; second, strengthen insurance infrastructure, consolidate the information technology foundation, cultural foundation, educational foundation, and organizational foundation for industry development, and continuously enhance the overall strength of the industry; third, strengthen and improve insurance regulation, maximize the protection of consumers' rights, and effectively enhance risk prevention capabilities; fourth, implement a talent-driven strategy, build high-quality management, technical, and marketing talent teams; fifth, optimize the environment, incorporate the development of the insurance industry into the overall pattern of economic and social development, and create a good atmosphere for scientific development.

Unlike the new leaders of the CBRC and CSRC, Xiang Junbo, the third chairman of the CIRC, has rarely appeared in the media since taking office. Compared to leading the stock reform and listing of Agricultural Bank, taking charge of the CIRC seems even more challenging. Xiang Junbo conducted extensive research after taking office on October 29 last year, stating that his "first truly comprehensive systematic speech in the industry will be after 60 days."

More than two months later, at the National Insurance Regulatory Work Conference held on January 7, Xiang Junbo made a detailed report on four major problems in the insurance market and various flaws in supervision. The 34-page report covered the supervision of insurance companies, market transformation, consumer rights protection, and support for future policy-based insurance markets.

Unlike previous conferences, this one was merely the "Insurance Regulatory Work Conference," not the "National Insurance Work Conference." Insurance company seats were also absent. Various signals indicate that insurance regulation in the Xiang Junbo era will undergo a major transformation.

Currently, the insurance industry is at a critical juncture of transformation. Relying solely on the industry's own forces for change will be slow and gradual. Policy guidance and moderate leadership are particularly important.

From administration to regulation

Under the pressure of multiple unfavorable policies such as macroeconomic environment and industry regulation, the insurance industry experienced a decline in premium growth rate, a decrease in investment return rate, and insufficient solvency in 2011. In 2011, the country achieved a premium income of 1.43 trillion yuan, an increase of 10.4% year-on-year. Among them, property insurance premium income was 461.79 billion yuan, an increase of 18.5% year-on-year; life insurance premium income was 969.98 billion yuan, an increase of 6.8% year-on-year. The annualized investment return rate was 3.6%, a reduction of one-fourth compared to the 4.84% investment return rate in 2010, and nearly half less than the average annualized return rate exceeding 6% over the past five years disclosed by the CIRC in July 2011. The solvency adequacy ratio of some life insurance companies decreased by 60 percentage points compared to the beginning of 2011, and 81 insurance companies increased capital and issued bonds worth 150 billion yuan last year to improve their solvency. The industry's solvency will be even more dire this year.

Long-standing ailments accumulated in the insurance industry have become increasingly evident: urgent need for improvement in social image, urgent need for transformation in industry development model, low quality of insurance talent teams, and the need for further improvement in the external environment for the development of the insurance industry.

Gao Li, analyst at Huachuang Securities, believes that currently, the insurance industry is at a critical juncture of transformation. Relying solely on the industry's own forces for change will be slow and gradual. Policy guidance and moderate leadership are particularly important.

Xiang Junbo attempts to cure the long-standing ailments troubling the insurance industry through a regulatory revolution, abandoning the model of both regulating and promoting development, expecting to promote development through regulation. Professor Tuo Guozhu from the Department of Insurance at Capital University of Economics and Business commented: "There is a world of difference between the two. The former indicates that the CIRC is both a regulator and a supervisor; the latter indicates that the CIRC is only a regulator, not a supervisor. Clear positioning ensures no role conflict."

In fact, compared to other industry changes that need to be promoted, the transformation of supervisory concepts, the construction of supervisory teams and systems are considered the most executable. Dean Hao Yansu of the School of Insurance at Central University of Finance and Economics, when interviewed by New Finance Journal, stated: "Reforms like deferred tax-type old-age insurance that require comprehensive coordination and promotion by multiple departments often face the greatest resistance, while reforms within the regulatory system itself are relatively easier. Xiang Junbo pointed out at the regulatory conference that most cadres have not undergone systematic training, and quite a part do not have professional backgrounds. Such remarks also indicate the strength and determination of regulatory reform."
