Be Cautious When Choosing Collagen Supplements! Be Sure to Watch Out for Side Effects...

Collagen is great for skin, bones, and even anti-aging benefits — I won't go into too much detail as you might all be more aware of this than me. However, I do think that when it comes to choosing a brand, it's better to be cautious. If there are side effects, it could get quite troublesome. Although my experience isn’t overly dramatic, I’ll still share it briefly.

I started taking collagen supplements when I was on an overseas assignment in the U.S., and it happened because a good colleague at the time recommended it to me... As women, we tend to pay attention to things like anti-aging as we age, so after trying it for a while, I did feel it was effective (at least my wrinkles didn't increase:P, and my figure was well-maintained…). So, I kept taking it for over two years.

After returning to China last year, I found that collagen supplements were also gaining popularity domestically, with many well-known brands available. I was quite happy about this. The collagen supplement I took in the U.S. was from an American brand called Nature’s Sunshine, which should translate to "Natural Sunshine." You may not know it, but it's basically unavailable in China. I'm the type who doesn't like changing brands once I've gotten used to one, but the supply I brought back only lasted two months. It wasn't practical to bring more, and asking my colleagues repeatedly would have been inconvenient. Since there were many good brands available now, I thought it would be convenient to try switching to another brand. After hearing some recommendations from friends and doing some research... I carefully considered and eventually chose FANCL's collagen drink.

However, after consuming it for three months, I gained weight and started breaking out with acne!! ORZ!

If I had indulged in excessive eating and drinking after returning, I would have accepted it, but I was eating and sleeping normally. Moreover, the collagen I had been taking earlier was dual-purpose: it not only beautified but also had fat-burning effects. Eating the daily dose in one go at night helped burn fat, keeping my weight stable... I never expected that after coming back, this would happen... So, I wondered if it was related to the collagen. After searching online, I found numerous reports about the side effects of FANCL's collagen: allergies, acne, hormone content, and even negative protein results during health check-ups... It was truly terrifying!!! I was so scared, but luckily, my reactions weren't too severe.

Of course, it could have been caused by other reasons, but regardless, I no longer dared to continue taking FANCL's collagen. I got rid of the remaining month's supply... After stopping for more than half a month, my acne improved, but the weight I gained didn't come off... At that moment, I really missed the weight-loss effect of NS collagen, so I shamelessly asked a colleague going on a business trip to the U.S. to bring me a few bottles... After persisting with the weight-loss regimen for nearly two months and exercising occasionally, I finally managed to lose the weight again... What a hassle!

In short, I will never randomly switch brands again. If you're already accustomed to a certain product, it's better to stick with it; otherwise, it can be quite bothersome. Luckily, NS products are now available in China under the Chinese name 'Natural Sunshine'... Perhaps it's because they just entered the market, so I haven't seen them widely available yet. I do know they have always been sold in Hong Kong... If any of you sisters are just starting to take collagen and are interested, you can try my brand. After all, it's an old American herbal dietary supplement brand, and its formulations might be more natural than regular health supplements? Though, I'm not entirely sure, shrug...

If you already have a brand you're accustomed to and it works well for you, then don't switch...

To be honest, aside from the possibility of some having side effects, collagen itself is still pretty good... Um, at least my skin elasticity is still maintained well... Don't ask me how old I am &%#@%￥
