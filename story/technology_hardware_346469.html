My favorite eight Chinese rock singers (Part I)

by zhiyun0972 on 2010-04-16 09:49:38

1. Having Nothing In 1986, at the "International Year of Peace - Concert of 100 Singers", Cui Jian stepped onto the stage wearing a long coat with his trouser legs rolled up unevenly. He played his worn-out guitar with such brilliance that it left me deeply moved. Everything happened so suddenly - what had occurred? I needed to pause, rub my bloodshot eyes, and then accept Cui Jian again. Had I become accustomed to passively accepting everything? Yes, for a long time, I had been very passive in this way or that. Please don't blame me, it was history that caused it. But indeed, I need to rest, look back at my past, and reach out to touch the scars of history. Rock appeared on such a night with the emergence of Cui Jian, as if a major event had occurred. Can it be called an event? I think it can. In China, rock went from nothing to something. That "something" - on the stage that night - was dazzling and unsettling, yet unbelievable. Below the stage, there was silence, and finally, everything ended with tears and cheers. "Rock saved my life!" is a famous quote by German director Wim Wenders. For me, that night was equivalent to "saving" something within me. Many brain cells filled with old patterns, or deadened, or pompously grandiose, began to die off and regenerate - I must love rock, I told myself secretly. And so, I fell in love with it. I didn't want to love it, but I did. Because, from that night on, I realized that there was such a kind of singing in the world, and you would immediately feel that it was the kind of singing you most needed. The emergence of Cui Jian was more important than his existence; he was the beginning of Chinese rock. He sang about many things that people in that era had suppressed for a long time, but hadn't figured out yet, or even if they had, they still dared not speak plainly about them. That was the reality before and after the eighties. Our lives, ideals, love, confusion, truth, falsehood... all of it - turned out to be having nothing! "I once asked incessantly / When will you walk with me / But you always laughed at me for having nothing... At this moment, your hands are trembling, at this moment, your tears are flowing / Are you trying to tell me that you love me, having nothing." Cui Jian sang "Having Nothing" with such pain that it resonated across the land. What kind of having nothing? A lack of humanity makes one feel "having nothing" even in any situation of "having". Having is also not having, not having is even more not having. Having nothing becomes an unresolvable barrier between the present situation and expectations. That year, I think Cui Jian wasn't consciously making his songs have a universal philosophical quality; he was singing a kind of intuition - vacuous yet accurate; passionate yet calm; helpless yet sincere... Starting from himself, there was a girl in front of him. A girl at a certain age symbolizes the entire world. Cui Jian told her, "This is who I am, it's up to you." He didn't force himself, didn't lie, and certainly wouldn't be despicable. However, Cui Jian knew that this girl would be moved by him because he was clean, honest, and loved her deeply. With pure love, Cui Jian embarked on a new Long March journey of rock, naming that album "Rock on the New Long March Road," signifying the beginning and the hardships to come for Chinese rock. From original lyrics to music, and then to stage performances, Cui Jian left an unforgettable impression. Even today, Cui Jian remains unsurpassed. It's imaginable how the explorations and relentless pursuits of Cui Jian are worth documenting. "My life has suddenly encountered a new problem / which is that I want to keep distance from everyone / I don't want to see friends, I don't want to say empty words anymore / I don't want people to know I have such a bad temper / I maintained silence all night without doing anything / and discovered that I quite like this kind of characterful sadness / because I can still see my attitude towards life / and still feel that my soul seems alive / everywhere around, the sounds transmitted are truly annoying..." In "Buffering," problems arose, the transformation of the times, bringing an irrationality that aimed to eliminate everything from the past. What to do? This is the tension and panic that every person of our generation has experienced. The meaninglessness of existence outweighed meaningfulness; enjoying today collapsed idealism. You suddenly became incredibly lonely, as if abandoned by your lover. What's wrong with today? What's wrong with me? What's wrong with you? What's wrong with love? Terrifyingly, "but who knows, the madness disappears and fear appears when waking up the next morning." Yet youth persists, just like poets persist, while readers of poetry disappear. Sadness shattered awakening all at once. Ten Haizi! Are there only ten Haizi left?" "Hey, I'm back, hey, I'm back / I greet all my acquaintances with the same call..." Haizi committed suicide by lying down on train tracks, Cui Jian continued to wander with his guitar on his back. I really like "Egg Under the Red Flag," it seems no matter how I try to express my feelings with other words, none can surpass that lyric, "stones may be hard, but eggs are life," another philosophy. Its background remains unchanged; we grew up under the red flag, seemingly related to our parents, yet not entirely. The red flag flies everywhere, without a fixed direction, without other colors, swallowing too much, and when suddenly, "money floats in the air and we have no dreams / although the air is fresh, we cannot see farther places..." Besides loss, there is still loss! Cui Jian seemed to be placed on an invisible, massive billboard, representing us - that collective memory which can never be humiliated, obstructing us from storing new files. Time passed by a lot, whether with ideals or without, both seem to have been ignored. Then, what remains? Why must something remain? Only when there is not having nothing, does the possibility of remaining exist, including spirit and material. In reality, the spirit is not only lacking, but also brutally destroyed, or starting all over again. Time proved that the greatness of this "egg" is not so easily self-generated or self-destroyed. Cui Jian saw many things, not all were tragic, "sudden blossoming is actually not sudden / now the opportunity has arrived, perhaps knowing what to do..." Cui Jian is sensitive and wise, possessing conflicting aspects in his heart, two, three, four... They resist, dissolve, and rebirth together, which might be exactly the broader artistic qualities of Cui Jian. In a bar, I saw a girl singing Cui Jian's "Flower House Girl." The girl held a guitar and sang in a way that made me feel an indescribable excitement. Time flows, today's girls reinterpret rock, "I walked silently past you / Without words to tell you, / I dare not look up at your face, oh... " The girl closed her eyes - Cui Jian simply used a red cloth to cover his eyes - the girl's false eyelashes trembled in the light - Cui Jian's red passion extended boundlessly. Although that red cloth was in "Resolve." Perhaps its location doesn't matter. But its unity with Cui Jian, in my abstract mind, floated vividly red. On one side is today, on the other side is yesterday, I can't see what time is, I only see a rock song, still so tangible. "You want me to stay here / You want me to be like them / I look at you silently and say, oh... no, / I want to go back to the old place / I want to walk on the old road / I know I can't leave you! Oh... girl / I'm going back to the old place / I'm walking on the old road / I know I can't leave you! Oh... girl" Time receded between them, a song echoed as if in the same place. That wasn't the stage, it was youth. I suddenly felt that current pop music has very pale lyrics. I want to say, are poet-like genius singers of the twenty-first century yet to be born? In 1998, Cui Jian released his sixth album "The Power of Impotence" "I'm still powerless now / You still need to wait / Do you still want me / If I fail..." This album, in musical style, embodies the persistence of Cui Jian's conscience and the inheritance of rock'n'roll's essence. The times that couldn't change were what, even though you could rely on them, they remained in life and in practice. Cui Jian. Will he still give us answers? We can hope. I looked at a photo of Cui Jian taken by a friend of mine. Cui Jian wore a large floral short-sleeve shirt, his hair was slightly disheveled, and wrinkles appeared at the corners of his mouth. It was a non-public photo, and Cui Jian appeared very reserved. I suddenly felt as if I had walked into a reason - why Cui Jian is Cui Jian! Because he possesses the flatness and steadiness of the earth, even if a certain spring is somewhat cruel, when it's time for leaves to grow, they will inevitably take over everything. Cui Jian continues to sprout avant-garde versions of himself on his own ground. Over the years - winds have gone, mists have gone, flowers have gone, people have gone, but Cui Jian remains.

2. Hold Me Tight

What rock encompasses is like the name of the band "Overload," creating a sense of weight for both the singer and the listener. Therefore, rock cannot be soothing or soft. I wonder, what is the cargo that rock overloads? Can it be considered this: everything in life, at a certain turning point in your growth, poses a threat to you? Thus, you suddenly feel a chasm between life and your situation, a chasm so deep you can't see the bottom. In fact, you haven't moved from your spot; it's just that your grown height invades the sky above your head and everything around you. How could a long-standing "society" easily accept an unexpected you? Conflicts first arise internally; why can't you see the brightness and simplicity of yesterday? Can you maintain it? Sweat drips down your forehead in large beads, along with labored breathing and heartache. Your hair stops being cut short, your eyes shut like doors, and you strum your guitar and the endless nights with youthful fingers until they bleed, scab over, and disturb the neighbors. That direct, reckless, self-mutilating youth and rock intertwine from that point onward.

In 1991, the Overload band was formed, with Gao Qi as the key figure. Overload was China's first heavy metal style band. Their wildly distorted vocals and rapid, intense rhythms captivated my younger self, "Hold me tight / Tell me what you're still hoping for / The night still makes me feel cold / Your warmth takes me into a dream / The arrival of dawn / The allure of the distance / I must set out..."

Aside from melodic rhythm elements, the emotions expressed in the lyrics are vast and weighty. I reminisce! I reminisce about such an era - hold me tight! Who hasn't done this gesture? However, the implication of holding tightly isn't merely romantic as it is today. In that era, holding tightly led to scrutiny, doubt, resolve, and incorruptibility. "Corruption" in the eighties and nineties was still a distant term, even death was preferable. I recall K from Kafka's novel "The Trial." As K is about to be executed, he sees a water jar full of water beside a young girl in the distance. Kafka magnifies this instant, capturing the poignant beauty of life frozen at the moment of its end. And Gao Qi and his band sing their aspirations and helplessness in a "Kafkaesque" instant, "Feel the world, feel longing / Feel the wind, feel uniqueness / Feel the moon half-red / Is your beauty still as it was / Struggling in the breeze."

Their faces bear contempt and defiance. They still view cleanliness as purity, disgrace as shame. They haven't fully emerged from the tragic memories of the Chinese nation. Yet, dreams have already set sail - an unintentional intersection of such an age and such a period. At the center of X, the form and content of the brilliant "Overload" were born.

The coolest lineup of "Overload" consisted of Gao Qi (lead vocalist), Li Yanliang (lead guitarist), Ouyang (bass guitarist), and Wang Lan (drummer). In 1993, the Overload band contributed the single "Ancestors' Shadows" to the compilation album "Beijing Rock 1." Subsequently, they embarked on a magnificent national tour. I, as part of the rock music fan base, remember what most fascinated me - the integrity, dignity, and humanity conveyed through rock - a high standard indeed. And this "high," I realize today, is something I've only recently discovered. I suddenly want to ask myself: What exactly is mourning?

3. I Look at You, and Believe

Zhang Chu - whenever I think of this name, I don't want to write about him in the third person; rather, I want to invite him out, sit in the teahouse near my house, and talk through the night. It's a dim room, with a basket of artificial freesias on an old wooden table. That evening, we sat facing each other, each ordering a glass of lemon water. Zhang Chu said he couldn't sleep, and I replied that I hadn't slept well for a long time either. I stared at the few slices of lemon floating in the glass, seeing the intricate threads inside them, perhaps even sensing a faintly bitter aroma escaping. The lighting seemed to lose its radiance, arranged only where it should shine. The unlit areas were devoid of substance. In the entire room, only the sparse words exchanged between Zhang Chu and me drifted above the wooden table - "How have you been lately?" "Not great." "If it's not great, so what?" "If it's not great, what can you do?" ... Later, Zhang Chu lowered his head and stopped speaking. Suddenly, I felt that Zhang Chu had been like this for many years, bowing his head deeply. "I like your 'Sister.'" I added. "But I think 'Sister' isn't quite rock, at least the title doesn't sound like it..." I kept talking, and the night passed. Actually, this is just my imagination. Today, as I tidy up my imaginings, I almost believe it really happened. Just now, I can no longer smell the lemon, I can't smell anything. I'm extremely grateful for this imagining because it allowed me to directly tell Zhang Chu what I most wanted to say - Rock should be angry, shouting, criticizing. But looking at 'Sister,' it's not entirely true. 'Sister' conflates sentimentality with a refusal to be sentimental, solidifying hours later in mid-air like a fortress. The key lies in: how to grasp the balance between expressing sentiment and refraining from sentimentality? "Oh sister / I want to go home / Hold my hand / I'm getting sleepy oh sister / Take me home / Hold my hand / Don't be afraid..." The lyrics are sentimental, but rock isn't solely defined by its lyrics. I don't particularly like sentimentality, or overly sentimental expressions. Unavoidable sentimentality often leads to self-aggrandizement or blind faith in authoritarianism. In the literary circles of the 1990s, Wang Shuo pioneered a bizarre journey of his novels against nobility and sentimentality with his disdain, arrogance, digressions, mockery, and sharpness. Wang Shuo opened a path for writing novels differently, while 'Sister's emotional language was still delivered by Zhang Chu. By the second verse, sentimentality departs from itself and reaches its opposite, "My dad always drinks, he's a jerk / Before he dies, he won't be sad anymore, he won't raise his fists, he sits on the stairs, already old / No longer an opponent..." Here, I see something else. A pure sentiment excluded by brutal living conditions suffers embarrassment. This narrative isn't avoidance but resistance. A private family story, difficult to articulate, but Zhang Chu tells the vulnerable me listening - "How did I grow up? You understand now, right? What was my father like? You understand now, right?" Yes, rock is, and exceeds, suffering, but not only. The early stages of Chinese rock were hypersensitive, suspicious, sarcastic, and intense about moments of life's meaninglessness, truly embodying the tragic nobility of rock. Zhang Chu's "Lonely People Are Despicable" is a rock single that captures your heart from the title alone, brimming with authentic rock sentiment. Whether discarded by love, oneself, or the times, can you not feel loneliness? But no! You must not feel lonely. Because: "It's a season of love / The air is filled with couples' scent / Lonely people are despicable / It's a season of love / Everyone should smile at each other / Hugging and kissing, that's enough..." Because of loneliness, people desire love and physical affection. Moreover, you must learn the city's ways: "Cities should have flowers / Even if they are picked, flowers should grow again..." I wish to explain these two "shoulds": The first "should" is correct; cities naturally should have flowers. The second "should" exposes the unpleasantness of the first. Zhang Chu feels some inexplicable discomfort, some retaliatory attitude, some confidence and arrogance. What's wrong with Zhang Chu? Originally, it was a beautiful season. But emotions shift abruptly, criticizing the city, the flowers, and the pervasive hugging and kissing? Actually, Zhang Chu uses reverse thinking to express the ultimate loneliness of a perfectionist. "Just at this moment, you have no ideas / Just at this moment, you still enjoy daydreaming / Just at this moment, I still have some ideas / I want to find someone to share dreams with / I say I love you, and you're satisfied / You hug me, and I feel very peaceful / You say this city is dirty, and I think you're pretty thoughtful / You say our love is immortal / I look at you, and I believe..." Zhang Chu, how is your pain so similar to mine? Are you me? Or am I you? - I look at you, and I believe! Your "Love" has become my love parable, unfortunately, I realized my connection to you a bit late. Some say that Zhang Chu's perpetual loneliness most authentically interprets the illusions of the era we live in, and I agree. Perhaps, only Zhang Chu can think, sing, and cultivate vast fields of lonely flowers with his heart's blood. If you think further, what problems can loneliness and non-loneliness solve? Life doesn't shift according to your "should." "Life blooms like flowers / We cannot let ourselves wither / Without choice, we must fall in love / Love like flowers is scattered by the wind / Scattered by the wind, scattered by the wind..." Constantly in conflict, "lonely people imagine themselves as beautiful as flowers..." It's like traveling from the heart to the grave, reaching another kind of beauty! Zhang Chu consistently carries progressive dreams, consistently opposes shame while singing about it.

4. Punk? He Yong

On stage, a strong beam of light from the lower-left hits He Yong, almost shattering his sailor shirt. The microphone flies toward him like a spacecraft, demanding his voice. His beloved guitar hangs low on his hips, the canvas strap seemingly cutting into his sturdy shoulders. His left hand presses the strings, his right hand momentarily paused from strumming. That seems to be a brief pause in the strumming, I think He Yong must be singing, "No hope / No hope / No hope..." He Yong's mouth opens squarely, like his square face. His hair is neither too long nor too short, shiny black outshining the night - this is a photograph from 1994 at the Hong Kong Coliseum "Rock the Chinese Music Power" concert that I particularly like. He Yong, immortalized on paper, once shook the entire Hong Kong! However, everything has become the past. But regarding He Yong, I can't think this way at all; He Yong shouldn't be in the past. The He Yong who shouldn't be in the past is forever etched in a place. This place is not time or space, nor is it memory; it is "transmission." Transmission - is a place! He Yong exists within transmission. Back in the day, He Yong was only fifteen years old. In the late 80s, he joined the Mayday Band. In 1990, he founded the Newsboy Band, with music leaning towards punk style. During these three years, countless hopes and ideas sprouted prematurely in He Yong's life branches. Young He Yong, from wanting to sing something, to actually singing it out, completed his evolution as a rock singer overnight. Perhaps this was a mistake regarding He Yong's later circumstances. But it cannot be denied that He Yong is a genius with an innate sense of rock. His relationship with the world can only be sustained through rock. Otherwise, he is nothing, worse than nothing. He Yong only exists as a rock singer, and he is immeasurable. These four words, "immeasurable," resemble a poetic crown, finding different reasons for each outstanding Chinese rock singer. But on He Yong's head, I believe it is a necessity! Because, apart from rock, He Yong is still rock. He once said, "In terms of music, I can do well, but in life, emotions, and other aspects, I cannot take care of myself, even now." He Yong is confined within his rock realm, where the immeasurable is buried deep in the depths of his life, with possibilities of eruption and destruction. In June 1994, He Yong's album "Garbage Dump" was released. The design of the "Garbage Dump" sleeve still makes my eyes light up today. Especially the back cover - He Yong lying diagonally, naked from the waist up, his right arm naturally resting below, his left arm extending straight upwards. An intention, an effort to reach for something just out of reach, makes me feel an indescribable, inexhaustible emotion. Still, that face unable to hide its youthful and handsome features, young like a sophomore in high school. Yet, I see the suppression, indignation, and sorrow behind that face. The background behind him is swirling color blocks, which can be storms, waves, flames. No! It's a garbage dump. "The world we live in / Is a garbage dump / People are like insects / Here we fight for food / Eating is conscience / Excreting is all thought..." Ten years ago, He Yong sang this, prophetically singing out the negative realities of modern and postmodern societies. A fable-like "Garbage Dump," stirring, awakening, rebellious, sharp, fearless... all coming from a teenager. Among the songs in the "Garbage Dump" album, the ones most reflective of He Yong's early life are "Bumps on the Head" and "Beautiful Girl." I also like "African Dream," which seems less direct in expressing emotions, but clearly shows He Yong's struggle and search. After the tropical drum beats rain heavily, he sings confusedly, "I want to go far away to Africa to see the sky and trees there / To personally hear the African drum beats and the real confession of songs..." All of this deeply moves me. Truly, those immeasurable things exist in such seemingly simple words. Returning from afar to beneath the Drum Tower, he looks up at it as if seeing it for the first time. The Drum Tower is taller, older, and incomprehensible. Let me think, why do we still need to ponder so many things? "My home is inside the Second Ring Road / My home is on this side of the Drum Tower / My home is inside this courtyard / My home, my home, my home is on this Earth..." He Yong passes by the Drum Tower from childhood to adulthood, yet forgets it from childhood to adulthood. He suddenly remembers it, just like suddenly remembering another self! Unexpectedly, another self is born in the act of "remembering."
