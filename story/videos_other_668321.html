Leather goods industry development encounters obstacles, domestic high-end brands are scarce_80

by qazwsx852 on 2012-02-17 16:52:08

The development of the leather goods industry has encountered obstacles, with a lack of high-end brands domestically. The three tiers of the leather goods market are formed due to the natural conditions, economic differences, and varying consumer perspectives in different regions, generally divided into the high-end brand leather goods market, the popular trend market, and the affordable leather goods market.

High-end brands are concentrated overseas: The high-end brand leather goods market mainly operates premium products known for their selective materials, exquisite craftsmanship, and expensive prices. These products are primarily found in the super malls and brand specialty stores of major cities in China, with the majority of consumers being successful individuals from various industries. High-end leather goods mainly consist of European and American, Hong Kong and Taiwan brands that entered the domestic large shopping malls during the 1980s to 1990s, as well as Chinese brands that have developed domestically. Foreign brands include Louis Vuitton (France), Pierre Cardin, Cartier, Saint Laurent (YVESSAINTLAURENT), Chanel (CHANEL), etc. These high-end brands are concentrated abroad, with virtually no domestic brand able to compete on the same level.

Domestic focus on affordable leather goods: The popular trend market caters to mid-range and upper-mid-range genuine leather product consumers and is a vast and most potential-filled space, yet also one of the most fiercely competitive. Its operating venues mainly include several large professional leather goods wholesale markets nationwide, medium and small-sized wholesale markets in various cities, retail locations in local malls and brand specialty stores, and a portion of products exported through foreign trade companies to many countries and regions overseas.

Affordable product markets cater to the needs of lower-income consumers both domestically and internationally, focusing on fabric, synthetic fiber, and artificial leather products. This is the earliest rising domestic market, with the largest number of products and the most exports. Hebei Baigou and Zhejiang Luqiao are the largest distribution centers for affordable leather goods in China, with other large leather goods wholesale markets also having some dealers. The manufacturing process and quality of affordable leather goods are relatively inferior to genuine leather products but offer a variety of materials, extensive design options, and affordable prices. Their products are widespread in towns and cities across China and are sold overseas.

Affordable leather goods mainly come from Hebei, Zhejiang, Liaoning, Sichuan, and Shiling Town in Huadu District, Guangzhou. Among them, Huadu Shiling's products, being closest to the source of advanced technology and relatively more accessible information, enjoy advantages in novel designs, diverse and unique materials, and relatively stable quality. Therefore, they are favored by many domestic and international leather goods dealers.

Lack of high-end leather goods care domestically: More consumers choose to purchase luxury brands overseas, but due to the distance from the origin, many maintenance issues for these luxury leather goods are challenging. In response to this demand, some merchants have started targeting the luxury leather goods care industry. However, issues such as low industry standards, disputes over rights protection, and other problems frequently arise in this field.

"Buying a bag and having to send it to Hong Kong for regular maintenance is too costly, and it’s hard to find reliable leather goods care shops locally." Xiao Lin, who works at an accounting firm, is a "bag enthusiast," owning more than thirty luxury bags. Several larger luxury brand specialty store managers in the city stated that bags can undergo simple cleaning and maintenance in-store. However, processes like coloring, washing, and renewal requiring specialized chemicals and tools are not provided by these luxury stores. "We can help customers send their bags back to the origin, but these costs must be borne by the customer," a staff member said. Since they are only salespeople and their brand does not have a repair or maintenance department established in mainland China, they primarily focus on sales, leaving after-sales issues to be resolved by contacting the original factory.

What happens if accidents occur during the maintenance of luxury leather goods? How are responsibilities between merchants and consumers defined? How should stores compensate for damages or losses to leather goods? As this is an emerging industry, there are still legal gaps regarding regulations. These issues make many domestic consumers wary of luxury item maintenance.

Due to the relatively short development time of the domestic leather goods industry, there are still some issues during its growth. The overall design level of the leather goods market is relatively low, with a scarcity of specialized technical and design talents. Few people receive formal education or professional training, resulting in a significant gap between art academy graduates' practical working abilities and those of professionals grown within enterprises. Small enterprises with fifty to sixty employees dominate the domestic landscape. Many operators lack long-term vision, have limited patience in nurturing talented professionals, and heavily rely on copying popular styles from renowned brands rather than effectively utilizing designers to leverage their product advantages.
