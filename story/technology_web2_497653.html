East Airlines Finance: Bernanke hints at possibility of QE3, crude oil continues to climb slightly - Forex Trading

by nordfx0808 on 2011-09-30 14:49:20

Source: Asia Forex Net

Author: Dongfang Airlines Finance

Recently, Chairman of the Federal Reserve, Ben Bernanke made his first public statement after unveiling measures to lower long-term interest rates. Although he did not explicitly mention the current economic situation or specific monetary policies, he hinted that if the U.S. inflation rate or inflation expectations were to drop significantly, the U.S. might need to further loosen its monetary policy through unconventional measures. Bernanke has consistently been creating liquidity and has always hoped that this liquidity would be maintained. With the U.S. implementing $400 billion worth of medium- and short-term treasury bonds, such a large amount means that the Fed's room for maneuvering similar measures again is already limited. To further expand liquidity in the market, the Fed may have to increase the size of its balance sheet, which is to say, launching QE3.

With the U.S. economic data showing good performance, it has offset the market's concerns about what measures EU countries will take next to resolve the sovereign debt crisis, causing crude oil prices to continue their slight climb. According to data from the New York Mercantile Exchange, the November-delivery light sweet crude oil futures price increased by 93 cents, closing at $82.14 per barrel, a rise of 1.2%, with an intra-day high touching $83.98 per barrel. As of now in this month, the New York crude oil futures price has fallen by 7.5%, likely to record the highest quarterly fall since the fourth quarter of 2008. So far this year, the New York crude oil futures price has dropped by 10%. On the London ICE European Futures Exchange, the November-delivery North Sea Brent crude oil futures price rose by 15 cents, closing at $103.96 per barrel. Calculated by closing price, the difference between the North Sea Brent crude oil futures price and the New York light sweet crude oil futures price was $21.82 per barrel, which reached a historical high of $26.87 per barrel on September 6.

According to a report published today by the Energy Information Administration (EIA) under the U.S. Department of Energy, as of the week ending September 23, U.S. natural gas inventories increased by 111 billion cubic feet compared to the previous week, exceeding analysts' expectations. A Platts market research company survey showed that analysts expected the U.S. natural gas inventories for that week to increase by 99 billion to 103 billion cubic feet.

Bernanke's speech this time suggested the possibility of introducing a third round of quantitative easing. Combined with the huge $400 billion medium- and short-term treasury bond plan implying that the Fed's room for maneuvering similar measures again is already small, this has further intensified the possibility of QE3, while crude oil may still remain volatile.
