No. 35 ITES: Information Technology Enabled Services

No. 36 Back office

No. 37 Many reasons why India has flourished through software where East Asia has concentrated more on hardware – but some of India’s advantages include:

- Low-cost labor

- Language (time difference from major markets; while the Western world sleeps, India can work)

- First-rate Engineering colleges

- Mathematics knowledge of the West

No. 38 Bottom line

- India has a vast pool of English-speaking graduates who work for 20% of the wages of foreign counterparts.

No. 39 These advantages have served the China software offshore Outsourcing industry – now serving ITES… China software offshore Outsourcing; Offshoot to the software sector – often nothing to do with software..the growth of the Internet in Asia Reliant on technology built in the last economic/technological wave – broadband internet - ITES cost-free to transfer data from Asia to America.

No. 40 Companies outsource because they need to save money..

- Talent shortage (the West is not producing enough engineers)

- Increased pressure for speed to market

- Increase efficiency, pressure to remain operational at least virtually 24 hours a day

No. 41 Companies like Amex, GE, and British Airways began experimenting with India as an offshore base in the mid-‘90s.

- Setting up captive bases

No. 42 These early experiments began a huge wave around the year 2000.

- Late 1990’s was a huge boom in India’s software sector as Western companies employed scores of Indian engineers to fix the Y2K problem – after Y2K all these engineers had nothing to do.

- Indian IT industry needed a new project.

- Fortunately for India, this coincided with the popping of the Internet bubble in the West which led to increased pressure to outsource – Recent downturn in the West big push to offshore outsourcing.

No. 43 When I went to India in 2001 to research my book, I expected that people would be down because of the downturn in the global economy (the bursting of the IT sector) – this is what I found in Silicon Valley that year – Instead, people in India were extremely upbeat and energized.

- Took advantage of demand - Quickly mobilized marketing campaign and entrepreneurial activity to create an entire industry.

No. 44 Can be argued that India really made a crucial innovation in the globalization of white-collar work – went to great lengths to prove to Western companies that this could be done.

- Satyam’s Little India experiment

No. 45 Probably the most common form of outsourcing to India are the call centers.

- Large centers for credit card enquiries, billings, and other customer service.

No. 46 One of the most interesting and controversial aspects of call centers is the way in which workers are trained to act American (or British etc).

- Accent training, cultural training.. Take an American-sounding name.. Computer set up with the local weather, sports scores, etc… can chat with customer about how the weather affected the local baseball game.

No. 47 Call centers are most common now, but many non-voice services are being outsourced.

- From simple data entry to high-end R&D, credit card processes, HR, finance and accounting, website development, market research, translation, transcription, reading and interpreting X-rays and CAT scans.

No. 48 Outsourcing holds a great promise for India…

- Could do for India what manufacturing has done for South East Asia.

- Huge boost to the nation's economy – potentially much greater than software.

- Hire any competent graduate, not just engineers.. Like many of China’s factories employ women up to 50% employees.

No. 49 One very important factor: India has the largest group of educated unemployed women in the world.

- Call centers have had to do a lot of work to create environments and convince people that it was okay for women to work (especially late at night).

- Lobby to change labor laws.

- Any industry that can employ this group radically alter the economic and social make-up of the country.
