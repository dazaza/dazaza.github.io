After divorce

by wenazjlove on 2011-09-13 19:23:46

After the divorce, it's my turn to be my husband's third party () (1)

Recently, I read "After the Divorce, It's My Turn to Be My Husband's Third Party" and had many thoughts. I will post them on my space soon to share with everyone!

I met my husband when he was about to graduate from university. We fell in love and got married, and we were always very happy. Our life was not luxurious, but it was still complete and happy. (However, at that time, we didn't have a baby yet. The plan was to have a baby when I was 29 years old, which was decided by all family members including my in-laws.) My husband is excellent, three years older than me (although now I often think of many things and feel hatred towards him). He is 178 cm tall, with distinct facial features, just looking a bit thin. He belongs to the type I like. I am the kind of petite woman, with good looks and body proportions. I can also be elegant in formal settings and handle household chores well. Classmates, friends, and relatives have always said that we are a perfect match and will surely be happy after marriage. We truly loved each other. The romance during our school days was relatively genuine and pure, unaffected by any material factors. In such a situation, we naturally came together and established a family. From marital life to every small matter, we were very "XX". Perhaps because everything went too smoothly and was too ordinary, it seemed mediocre.

Therefore, my husband might be seeking excitement outside. (I only found out later) If it weren't for a text message from an unfamiliar number, I might have remained unaware. The content of the message was: Sister, you don't know me, and perhaps you're still unaware of my existence, but for the sake of my love, I must let you know that I exist. When I saw this, I was confused and thought maybe someone sent it to the wrong number. Without giving it much thought, I ignored the message. However, a few minutes later, another message came with a photo. This time, I was stunned, feeling as if my mind went blank, muttering "impossible, impossible." The photo turned out to be of my husband and a young woman kissing while embracing each other... Since it was a side view, I couldn't clearly see my husband's face, but I clearly recognized the woman's challenging gaze. I felt pain in my heart, a tangled pain spreading throughout my body...

At this point, I didn't know what to do. I didn't know whether I should immediately call her and ask who she was and why she did this, nor did I know whether I should call my husband and demand he come home to explain. Thus, I hesitated in the office for a long time, forcing myself to remain calm and stabilize, trying to think of the most effective measures. Perhaps due to reading too many stories online, various ideas popped into my head. In short, I didn't want this incident to harm my originally happy life or damage my family. Finally, I decided to play it cool, ignoring the bad woman first and seeing what she would do next. As for my husband, I would deal with him similarly, not making a fuss, observing his situation, and trying to uncover the truth. Maybe knowing oneself and one's enemy would be useful.

Finally, it was time to go home from work. I returned home earlier than usual, just like any other day. Looking at everything at home, it was still so warm. Seeing the large wedding photo hanging on the wall, I suddenly felt very ironic. The gentle look in my husband's eyes, my happy and satisfied expression, contrasted strongly with the photo sent via text message in my mind. Tears involuntarily streamed down my face. Once again, I forced myself to stay calm and strong, telling myself to act as if nothing had happened. I opened the fridge to prepare dinner and saw the soup sent by my mother-in-law. I felt very uneasy inside. Although our family didn't live together, we lived happily. My in-laws treated me like their own daughter. Knowing I had had an abortion before, they worried that we were busy with work and couldn't carefully take care of our diet to restore health. They would send us food every few days. Sometimes, when they came, they would help me tidy up the house briefly. I treated them like my own parents, showing filial piety and respect. I felt lucky to have met such elders. Even though such a blow appeared, at that moment, I was still firmly determined that no matter what happened between my husband and me in the future, I would not conflict with the elderly. After all, humans are emotional beings, and we should know how to repay kindness. I think this idea helped me a lot in my later plans. Heaven has eyes...

Four dishes and a soup, just waiting for him to return. However, it was already past 11 pm, and there was no phone call or text message from him. My husband hadn't returned either. In the past, I would definitely worry if something had happened to him or if he had forgotten to contact me after drinking too much at a social gathering. But now, a premonition told me it wasn't that simple. My previous thoughts were too naive and trusting. So, I picked up the phone and dialed the numbers of some acquaintances I knew, asking casually if my husband had eaten with them recently. They all said no, and I suggested we arrange a meal together some other day. After hanging up, my heart was racing. Calming down, I called my mother-in-law and told her the soup was delicious. I also mentioned that I would take her shopping when I had time (my mother-in-law and I both liked shopping, but she usually wouldn't buy recklessly since she was good at managing the household budget. I usually noted down what she liked and bought it for her later). She hurriedly said there was no need since the clothes she bought last time hadn't been worn yet. As an old woman, she didn't need to waste money on more clothes. She even advised me to buy more for myself since I was beautiful and should dress up while young to look better. In the past, I would say that since I was married and had such a good husband, there was no need to spend extra money on clothing. But now, I simply said okay, then added "we'll shop together..."

At this moment, I heard the door opening; it was my husband returning. I bid goodnight to my mother-in-law and turned around to see him. His face was full of satisfaction, as if he had been drinking but wasn't drunk. He walked directly to me and gently said, "You're still awake so late? Are you waiting for me?" Suppressing my anger, I replied, "Yes, you didn't tell me you wouldn't come back for dinner, and I lost my appetite alone." But he said he hadn't eaten either. I suggested we eat together. I reheated the dishes and sat down across from him. He might have noticed something was off in my eyes and asked if something was wrong. I said no, but perhaps my conflicted gaze betrayed me. I asked him what he had been busy with lately. He said the usual things. I then asked why he came home so late today. He said there was a sudden event. I said, "This Saturday, please accompany me and Mom to go shopping," to which he replied, "You women go yourselves; I'm too lazy to shop." I said okay and left the room. When I returned to the bedroom after tidying up the kitchen, I saw him lying in bed. Watching his back, tears streamed down my face. I softly asked if he was asleep, but the only response was heavy breathing. I thought he must be tired, but definitely not because of work. I understand my man; he only sleeps so quickly after having sex. I seemed to hear the sound of my heart breaking.

I secretly took the phone he had placed on the bedside table and went to the bathroom. I checked the messages and call records. Surprisingly, for several consecutive days, there were multiple calls from the same unknown number. I wrote down the number. The mailbox was empty, probably deleted. Normally, there wouldn't be no messages at all. Comparing it with the number that sent the message to my phone, it turned out to be the same number. After putting the phone back, I sat dumbfounded in the living room. I had always trusted my husband and never peeked into his bag or phone, but at this moment, I felt no guilt. I stayed awake all night, thinking about many things from the past and many possibilities. Finally, my woman's intuition told me that there must be something between my husband and that woman in the afternoon, or perhaps my husband had taken a mistress (since our jobs were good, I didn't like controlling his finances, and we managed our money separately). But I didn't know what to do next. I didn't want to tell anyone, as I felt ashamed, even though I was the one being hurt. I never dreamed that a third party would appear in our lives.
