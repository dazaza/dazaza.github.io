In the dual impact of a global economic slowdown and market funds gradually entering the market, the post-year trend of gold has been bullish. Recently, foreign banks such as Goldman Sachs and UBS have successively released research reports, upgrading their expected annual returns for gold and firmly supporting a bullish stance on gold.

Bullish performance

On February 23, despite most commodity varieties declining, Shanghai Gold (363, 4.39, 1.23%) still rose against the trend. The main contract of gold 1206 increased by 4.41 basis points, with a rise of 1.23%, reaching a new high in nearly three months.

Meanwhile, US gold also continued to rise for four consecutive days, directly facing the major threshold of 1800 points. From the trend, it was evident that there was a clear upward momentum.

Relevant analysts from Ping An Futures, when interviewed by *The Economic Information Daily*, stated that recently, gold futures have gathered numerous positive factors, with strong buying power. [From an international perspective, Iran has ceased oil exports to the UK and France, causing a significant rise in international oil prices, which in turn boosted gold prices; the Eurozone finance ministers' meeting will approve the second tranche of bailout loans for Greece, leading to a general rise in risk assets, also pushing up gold prices. Domestically, on February 24, the central bank reduced the reserve requirement ratio for deposit-taking financial institutions by 0.5 percentage points, giving the market more room for speculation about a loose monetary policy.]

The analyst indicated that in the short term, gold would remain a strong asset, recommending investors to buy low.

Bullish on Gold

Gold's strong performance in the market has triggered the attention of foreign banks. In fact, at the beginning of this year, foreign banks such as Goldman Sachs and UBS had already successively issued reports on commodities like gold and oil. In these reports, the promotion of gold and oil was very apparent.

However, after a wave of market activity, foreign banks are even more optimistic about the future performance of gold. On February 23, Peter Helbiger, Managing Director of UBS Group and Global Commodities & Raw Materials Strategist, in an exclusive interview with *The Economic Information Daily*, stated that the price of gold this year would reach $2025 per ounce.

[The economies of Europe and the United States are not particularly good, so gold will become a very important currency. Additionally, the European Central Bank is about to launch its second phase of the LTRO plan, injecting one trillion euros into European banks. This will be an excellent catalyst, further promoting the development of the European economy to reach our expected goals.] said Peter Helbiger.

Moreover, regarding the consumption of gold, Peter Helbiger believed it would also be an important factor driving the rise in gold prices. [In the next five years, China’s jewelry market is expected to grow by 40%. This means that over the next five years, China’s economic growth model may change, shifting from past reliance on heavy industries and fixed-asset investment to a consumer-oriented economic development model, such as the demand for gold, which fully reflects this point.] said Peter Helbiger.

[The trend of gold prices is closely related to economic conditions. We believe that in 2013, the price of gold may fall back to $1725 per ounce.] Peter Helbiger believes that possibly next year, the global economy will experience a recovery process, providing investors with more investment channels, making them less likely to choose gold investments.
