Pedestrians and bicycles still violate regulations despite warnings (picture)

by cwzyysz148 on 2011-08-29 17:37:36

Electric motorcycles travel in the wrong direction on Guping Road and enter the motor vehicle lane. On Baihuang Road, students do not use the zebra crossing.

By Hou Xichen, Zhang Wei, Huang Shan, and Bao Hua of N-News

According to reports, yesterday journalists went out on the streets for interviews and found fewer violations by motor vehicles, but there were still frequent occurrences of pedestrians running red lights and bicycles traveling in the wrong direction, especially during traffic rush hours. Many people take shortcuts or try to save a few minutes, leading to dangerous situations on the road.

Pedestrians "in a hurry" run red lights

Yesterday evening during the peak traffic hour after work, the reporter drove to several main roads in Fuzhou for investigation. Around 5 PM at the intersection of Bayiqi Road and Daoshan Road, after the red light lit up on the pedestrian zebra crossing, the reporter heard a sharp sound of emergency braking. A young man was seen jogging through the traffic, almost getting hit by a car, causing the surrounding pedestrians to worry about him.

This man, however, paid no attention and continued to cross the road amidst the traffic flow.

Subsequently, the reporter saw many pedestrians seizing every opportunity to grab the road at multiple intersections such as Tate Road and Rendao Road. Although traffic assistants stopped quite a few pedestrians who wanted to run the red light, many still acted on their own accord, crossing the road when the assistants were not paying attention, forcing many cars to slow down to avoid them.

"There are too many pedestrians running red lights; they just don't want to wait a few more minutes or even seconds," a traffic assistant said helplessly. The beginning and end of the red light are concentrated times for pedestrians to illegally cross the road, "which is also the time when there are the most and fastest-moving vehicles on the road, making it the easiest time for traffic accidents to occur."

Besides running red lights, many pedestrians also cross the road without using the zebra crossing and jump over road barriers.

Bicycles enter the motor vehicle lane

At 6 PM yesterday, there were more and more vehicles on the road. On Guping Road, an electric bicycle not only rode onto the motor vehicle lane but also traveled in the wrong direction, forcing passing cars to sharply change direction to avoid it.

Traveling along Hualin Road, within half an hour, the reporter saw more than ten bicycles and electric bicycles riding onto the motor vehicle lane, with a significant number traveling in the wrong direction.

"Suddenly, I saw a bicycle dart out from the adjacent lane, blocked from view by the car in front," Mr. Yang, who works on Pingdong Road, said. Yesterday at noon, there was such a dangerous situation at the intersection of Hualin Road and Wusi Road, but fortunately, he braked in time, "the front of the car was just close to the other party's wheel. When driving, this is the scariest thing. If everyone followed their designated lanes, there wouldn't be so many traffic accidents." During the street investigation, the reporter also noticed some buses not stopping according to the designated lanes. At the Douchi bus stop on Baihuang Road, some buses stop one lane away from the platform.

"Passengers are easily hit by bicycles behind them when they get off," passengers reported.

Left no-go, right no-left-turn, vehicles entering and exiting Hongxing Industrial Zone are stuck

Mr. Chen works at the Hongxing Industrial Zone on the north side of North Ring Central Road. A few days ago, checking his violation record online, he found that he had violated traffic rules four times in three days, losing all 12 points and, according to regulations, should be fined 600 yuan.

After carefully checking, all four violations occurred at the entrance of his workplace, leaving Mr. Chen confused.

Yesterday afternoon, the reporter drove to the entrance of the Hongxing Industrial Zone. The industrial zone is on the roadside of North Ring Central Road heading eastward, separated from the motor vehicle lane by a green belt, about two hundred meters from Jinjishan Tunnel. Mr. Chen said that many drivers in the company have received fines, mostly due to unknowingly violating no-entry rules.

The reporter noticed that along North Ring Central Road heading east, when turning into Hongxing Industrial Zone, there is a "No Entry" sign at the intersection. "There are hundreds of truck entries and exits daily in the industrial zone. Is this how you expect us to continue working?" Mr. Chen and his colleagues told the reporter that this sign was erected just last week.

The way into the industrial zone is blocked, and the way out cannot be used either. Following truck driver Master Zheng out of the industrial zone, upon reaching the intersection, a prominent "No Left Turn" sign stood there. "This is a must-pass route. This way, it's like we're being 'sealed off.'" Master Zheng could not help but laugh bitterly. He told the reporter that his 12 points have already been deducted, and he dared not go to handle it, "Apply for reconsideration, it's too time-consuming."

(Source: Strait Evening News)
