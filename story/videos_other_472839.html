Six Gods shower cream, similar memories of different eras

by logitech888 on 2011-08-08 22:40:18

Summer has arrived again, with mosquitoes rampant and heat rashes running wild. On my desk, there is definitely a bottle of Huaxing water, ready for our unique battle. That faint fragrance always reminds me unintentionally of the relevant childhood memories, as if I can still feel the warmth and gentleness of my mother's big hands from back in the day. The events from ten or twenty years ago seem like they were just yesterday. This is Liushen Huaxing Water.

To this day, I still seek out the familiar scent of Liushen Huaxing Water. Every summer, I make sure to stock up on a bottle beforehand; otherwise, I cannot comfortably get through the season. In the supermarket, you will be pleasantly surprised to find that every summer, while many brands of Huaxing water come and go, it remains steadfastly waiting for you to take it home. I have also noticed that Liushen Huaxing Water has improved its packaging from the original glass bottle to today's spray Huaxing water, attracting more young people with its clear green hue, which seems to stir the heartstrings even more.

Starting with my fondness for Liushen Huaxing Water, this summer, I immediately fell in love with their newly launched herbal soap and body wash. Especially the Aiye soap and body wash from Liushen, the words "Aiye" evoke memories of washing in Aiye baths and eating Ai cakes when we were children.

During the Dragon Boat Festival, the children at home would be pulled over by their grandmothers and mothers for an Aiye bath. While bathing, they would mutter words about staying healthy and scaring away snakes, insects, and rodents. It wasn't until I grew up that I learned Aiye has been a long-standing remedy for warding off disease, recorded in ancient medical texts such as the "Huangdi Neijing" and "Bencao Gangmu." In the face of chemical additives ravaging our health, Liushen's new Aiye essential oil bath series and Aiye soap have caught the attention of consumers.

While lingering at the daily chemical product counters of various supermarkets this summer, I discovered that Liushen had launched a new body wash: Liushen Ice Clear Moisturizing Body Wash. Just from the name, I could guess the secret of this body wash - both refreshing and moisturizing.

In most of our minds, refreshment and moisturization are two functions that go in opposite directions. Bath products on the market either emphasize being refreshing or focus on moisturizing. This body wash that combines both features immediately piqued my interest. Especially since it has its own unique formula label - Qinglu Cool Moisturizing Formula, originating from an ancient prescription in "Bencao Gangmu."

The wood lotus leaf, or what she refers to as the Qinglu leaf, gives one an idea of the effect of using this body wash just by looking at the wood lotus flower. There’s also lily, white solomonseal, and others. Whether they are Chinese herbs or flowers and grasses, they are kings among flowers and saints among grasses. Particularly the lily, which brings to mind the memory of my mother cooking mung bean lily soup every summer when I was a child, saying it could cool down and reduce heat. Every summer, I think of my mother's mung bean lily soup.

Weakly asking, could it be that the ancients were inspired by ancient life to create the Qinglu Cool Moisturizing Formula? Haha.

Change also means constancy. I believe that many years from now, this Liushen Ice Clear Moisturizing Body Wash will become an unforgettable memory for the next generation.
