Which brand of radiation protection clothing is good for pregnant women, and is the radiation protection clothing useful?

by hanhan2niu on 2012-02-19 15:20:00

Recently, many expectant mothers have been asking: Are anti-radiation suits for pregnant women effective? How do these suits protect against radiation? To answer these questions, the editor specially interviewed Professor Zhang, an expert in electromagnetic radiation. According to Professor Zhang, anti-radiation suits for pregnant women work by using metal fibers within the fabric to form a loop that generates induced currents. These induced currents then produce a reverse electromagnetic field which shields against radiation. Currently, all anti-radiation suits available on the market are made based on this principle, using fabrics containing conductive metal fibers or conductive silver fibers. The metal fibers refer to stainless steel metal fibers, while silver fibers are a type of composite fiber created by infusing nano-silver ions into nylon fibers. Both types of fibers possess good conductivity and thus can shield against electromagnetic waves.

So, how can one evaluate the effectiveness of an anti-radiation suit? Professor Zhang explained that the degree of radiation protection mainly depends on the content of metal or silver fibers. The higher the content, the better the anti-radiation effect. However, if the content is too high, the fabric will lack a pleasant texture and affect comfort when worn. Therefore, anti-radiation fabrics generally contain 50-100% silver fibers or 20-30% metal fibers, with 30% being the optimal content for the highest level of anti-radiation protection.

Through the expert's explanation, many expectant mothers now understand the principle behind anti-radiation suits and can make their own judgments about their effectiveness. Expectant mothers should ideally prepare an anti-radiation suit before pregnancy. However, with so many brands of anti-radiation suits available on the market, how does one choose? The editor reminds pregnant women to avoid wearing second-hand anti-radiation suits, although doing so won't cause harm; the shielding effect may not be ideal, reaching only 50%-80%.
