Gold trading differs from stock trading. When people trade gold, they often focus solely on the price while ignoring the upward and downward trends of gold prices. When gold prices rise, the prices become increasingly expensive, and the higher they go, the less willing people are to buy. When gold prices fall, the prices become increasingly low, and the lower they go, the cheaper it seems. Therefore, during actual transactions, traders often forget the adage of following the trend and instead become counter-trend traders. In an upward trend of gold prices, there is only one wrong buying point, which is when the price reaches its peak. Like rising from the floor to the ceiling, it cannot rise further. Except for this point, any other point is correct for buying. In a downward trend of gold prices, there is only one wrong selling point, which is when the price has already reached its lowest point, like falling to the floor where it cannot go lower. Apart from this point, any other point is correct for selling.
