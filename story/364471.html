Christmas customs in different countries around the world

by baizhao2010 on 2010-09-14 15:36:49

Merry Christmas

Name: Ashu, Occupation: International Student

Ashu has been in China for almost two years. He introduced to us: "Thailand is a country with considerable inclusiveness. In our country, you can see the convergence and integration of Eastern and Western cultures while maintaining its own distinct characteristics."

Ashu continued: "There are many festivals in Thailand, such as the Ploughing Festival, Buddha's Birthday, Magha Puja, Loy Krathong, and countless others.

 

Simultaneously, like most Asian countries, Western holidays such as Christmas and Valentine's Day are widely accepted and loved by the youth. On such days, young people get very excited and start planning early each year, coming up with different ideas, determined to enjoy the festivities fully."

Diet Keywords: Thai Cuisine

Ashu said: During Christmas in Thailand, turkey, cakes, and desserts are, of course, indispensable. However, traditional Thai cuisine seems more popular because foreigners visiting our country want to taste the unique delicacies of Thailand. After all, Western food can be found anywhere, so on Christmas tables, Thai dishes are also highly favored within the country.

 

Christmas in Thailand

Thailand is a tourist destination, with visitors from all over the world arriving every day. Therefore, they have long become accustomed to and accepted holidays from other countries. All the young people gather together to celebrate joyfully.

 

Christmas in Finland

Finnish Christmas falls around December when violets bloom across the mountains and fields, their purple flowers contrasting against the white landscape, creating a stunning sight of purplish-red hues. The Purple Christmas brings immense joy.

Christmas in South Korea

South Korea is a country that greatly values tradition and has numerous traditional festivals that older generations hold dear, but the younger generation seems more enthusiastic about these Western holidays.

Name: Kim Mi-Jeong, Occupation: International Student

Lively Christmas

Miss Kim said: "On this day, young people will gather together. They carefully decorate the gathering place to highlight a romantic atmosphere, order various Western-style cakes, turkeys, and desserts, then drink, chat, sing, and dance, making it very lively!"

 

South Koreans have a strong interest in Western pastries, which are essential foods during Christmas.

Diet Keywords: Cake

Miss Kim feels that Christmas in South Korea is actually quite similar to that in China, but her favorite food is definitely cake, and it’s also an essential food for all her friends during Christmas.

Christmas in America

Americans emphasize family arrangements during Christmas, setting up Christmas trees, filling socks with gifts, enjoying turkey-based feasts, and holding family dances.

Bulgarian Christmas

According to the Bulgarian calendar, Christmas is a time when souls wander and evil spirits run rampant. Thus, people use fire to drive away evil spirits. On Christmas Eve, every household lights a fire that burns until dawn and must not be extinguished, or it will bring misfortune. Men fetch Christmas wood to light fires indoors and loudly recite: "Christmas has arrived; cattle, sheep, pigs, horses fill the barns, wheat sprouts cover the ridges, everyone walks under good fortune." Those waiting inside respond in unison: "Amen!"

 

 Singaporean Christmas

Singapore, though predominantly populated by descendants of Chinese immigrants, also has many immigrants from other countries. The result of cultural fusion is that Christmas has become a New Year celebration enjoyed by everyone.

Name: Wang Ping, Occupation: International Student

Shopping Christmas

Miss Wang is generous and friendly, with excellent Chinese language skills and a talkative nature. She becomes very excited when talking about her hometown, like a child. "In Singapore, people regard Christmas as a day to play and have fun with people from all over the world. Some large shopping malls stay open until midnight on Christmas Eve to facilitate people buying Christmas gifts. Along the streets, Christmas lights and Christmas trees stand tall, with colorful decorations brighter than the stars in the sky."

Diet Keywords: Turkey

Singapore's Christmas continues the Western tradition of Christmas dining habits, so turkey has become a necessary dish for Singaporeans during Christmas. At the same time, ham and Christmas pudding are also common dishes, and some families prepare several delicious local Singaporean dishes for the elderly.

French Christmas

In the center of France in the Ségurès region, it snows heavily every year around Christmas, covering everything in pristine white, refreshing the spirit. In Western eyes, a white Christmas is auspicious. In France, the manger is the most distinctive Christmas symbol because it is believed that Jesus was born near a manger. After singing Christmas carols praising Jesus, people must indulge in merry drinking, with champagne and brandy being traditional French Christmas drinks.

Australian Christmas

Australia is one of the countries in the Southern Hemisphere. By the end of December, when Western European countries are celebrating Christmas in the biting cold, Australia is experiencing the sweltering heat of midsummer. Therefore, Christmas in Australia sees shirtless, sweaty young men and women in miniskirts mingling with the meticulously decorated winter scenes, snow-covered Christmas trees, and Santa Claus in red cotton coats in store windows, creating a unique holiday scene in Australia. This stark contrast between scorching heat and freezing cold is probably unique among Western countries. The best Christmas gift parents can give their children is a small surfboard. Surfing at Christmas is a major feature of Australia. On Christmas Eve, people carry beverages into the forest for a "Barbecue" picnic. They cook sausages, beef, and fresh fish on iron plates placed over open fires made from dry branches stacked on stone-built outdoor stoves. After eating and drinking their fill, they dance the "Disco" or "Kangaroo Dance," reveling until late at night. Those who are drunk simply lie down on the grass, snoring loudly as they welcome the arrival of Santa Claus.

Polish Christmas

Polish Christmas is celebrated annually on December 25th and 26th. Every household decorates Christmas trees, and Christmas ornaments are sold on every street. During the festival, relatives and friends exchange greetings and gifts to show friendship. Grass is placed under the tablecloth during dinner to symbolize the birth of Jesus. The dinner is rich but does not include meat. After 12 o'clock at night, the whole family goes to church for worship. Generally, people do not visit others' homes during Christmas, except in cases of special invitations.

 

 British and German Christmas

The British and Germans alike drink beer and eat roasted goose during Christmas. They prefer to travel during the Christmas holidays.

Swiss Christmas

Four weeks before Christmas, the Swiss ignite four giant candles, placing them in a wreath decorated with tree branches. Each week, one candle is lit, and when the fourth candle is lit, Christmas arrives.
