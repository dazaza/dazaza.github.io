When the heart wanders, where does the body go?

by huiming on 2012-02-02 23:04:26

Where should the soul go, and where should the body go?

I originally planned to record this trip in my own space. Now I don't have any of that intention. The whole person feels like being suspended in the air, without sorrow or joy, without thought, without goal, without direction. I don't know whether this is a process or an outcome. I am very tired, very weary, unhappy, but cannot find the door to happiness.

I once thought that this life would be like this. Living peacefully, there is one self living in the world, and another self residing in the heart. But unexpectedly, on the same day, the self in the world and the self in the heart were both sentenced to life imprisonment - unable to die, and unable to live.

Living under the sun, yet feeling so lonely inside. I can't get out of this vicious circle. I feel too insecure. The people I trusted have become strangers, and I am unwilling to trust strangers. I am just living alone, and I just desire to be cherished by someone for a lifetime - carefully placed, properly preserved, attentively saved, sparing me from fear, sparing me from pain, sparing me from wandering, sparing me from having no place to rely on. I just need someone who truly understands me, someone who will cherish each other until old age, living a simple and pleasant life. That's all.

The me in the world has lost all hope, and is no longer willing to make any effort. It's as if I see those I know as strangers. It's not that I want revenge, it's just that I am too disappointed, and have no hope at all. How can you expect any emotion from a dead-hearted person?

The me in my heart, because of facing reality every day without seeing an end, no longer holds any illusions. Suddenly realizing facts that have long been true, but I just didn't want to believe them. I deceived myself, telling myself that it was love. But if it was real love, how could it have come to such a state? I should have awakened earlier, but I just didn't want to face it.

In a moment, both body and heart are dead. You say, what strength or emotion do I still have left? In fact, I really want to find someone to talk to, to cry to. But in the end, it's just a dialogue with myself. There is no listener, and the tears flow only in my heart. I am just imagining that if I can leave, I can be reborn. Forgetting the joys and sorrows of the past life, forgetting the good and bad people from the past life, forgetting all gains and losses, forgetting everything, and then being reborn anew, living a new life in a strange city.

If this life is ultimately destined to be full of hardships and wanderings, then why not try to be happy as much as possible? Since there is already no place to settle down and rely on, why endure so many difficulties and loneliness? Living alone may be lonely, but perhaps it can bring more ease and peace. You might think I am selfish, but I really don't want to bear it anymore. But when will I be able to break free from this predicament, and when will I be reborn?
