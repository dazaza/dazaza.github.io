Mobile QQ intensifies internal competition with WeChat and will launch the second game

by anonymous on 2013-08-20 14:50:32

According to news on August 19, Tencent's mobile QQ and WeChat simultaneously launched their game centers on August 5 with the same mobile game "Tiantian Ai Xiaochu". Within ten days, the registered users reached 40 million, and the daily active users exceeded 25 million. Recently, since August 17, the mobile QQ has shown a splash screen countdown upon login. According to insiders, mobile QQ plans to launch its second game "Tiantian Lianmeng" on August 20 to continue the success of "Tiantian Ai Xiaochu".

"Tiantian Ai Xiaochu" is a game that was launched simultaneously on the mobile QQ and WeChat game centers. Users can choose to share their scores and rankings with QQ or WeChat friends. Thanks to Tencent's strong and tight social network, this game quickly became a major trend in a short period.

In last week's Tencent second-quarter financial report, Tencent President Martin Lau revealed for the first time that within ten days of its release, "Tiantian Ai Xiaochu" had 40 million registered users and 25 million daily active users.

Since August 17, when logging into mobile QQ, a splash screen countdown appears showing the numbers 3, 2, and 1 respectively, with the message "In three days, look forward to something happening." According to insiders, the mobile QQ game center plans to launch its second game on August 20 to continue the success of "Tiantian Ai Xiaochu".

Notably, Lau emphasized during the earnings call that "the first mobile game was actually simultaneously released on both mobile QQ and the new version of WeChat." Some commentators noted that what Lau implied was that the success of "Tiantian Ai Xiaochu" was not just due to WeChat, but rather it was driven by Tencent's overall mobile gaming platform.

In fact, from the promotional tone of mobile QQ and WeChat in this quarter, the status of mobile QQ seems higher than WeChat. Meanwhile, according to insiders, the mobile QQ team at Tencent is currently facing enormous pressure from WeChat, and the competition between the two teams within the company is gradually intensifying. This might be the main reason why the second game "Tiantian Lianmeng" chose mobile QQ for its first launch.

Data from the financial report shows that by the end of the second quarter, the monthly active accounts of QQ on smartphones increased by over 200% year-on-year to 477.6 million, while the combined monthly active accounts of WeChat and WeChat International increased by 176.8% year-on-year to 235.8 million. Comparing these figures reveals that the larger user base of QQ on smartphones grew faster year-on-year than WeChat and WeChat International.

Lau stated that there is definitely overlap between QQ and WeChat, but they also cover different user groups. QQ offers some cross-PC and mobile user experiences that WeChat does not have. Over time, these two products will develop distinct features to meet their respective user groups, and it is expected that many users will use both products simultaneously in the future.

In fact, Tencent's mobile gaming platform has already exposed several games. In addition to self-developed games such as "Tiantian Paoku", "Tiantian Feiche", "Rhythm Master", "Happy Fight Landlord" that participated in internal testing, games like "Fruit Ninja", "Temple Run 2", "Moon Wolf", "Magic Tower", "Plants vs Zombies 2" will also enter the testing phase as the first batch of signed products and will subsequently land on the mobile QQ game center.

For Tencent, although WeChat may be the greatest hope for the future, mobile QQ has irreplaceability and is the focus of monetization in the short term. For mobile QQ, apart from creating active user data, games will inevitably take on more responsibilities for monetization.
