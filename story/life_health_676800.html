**What are the harms of cervical inflammation?**

Generally speaking, increased vaginal discharge is the most common, sometimes even the only symptom of acute cervical inflammation, often presenting as purulent. Since cervical inflammation often coexists with urethritis, cystitis, or acute vaginitis, acute endometritis, etc., other symptoms of cervical inflammation may be masked, such as varying degrees of lower abdominal and sacral pain, and bladder irritation symptoms. In cases of acute gonococcal cervicitis, there may be varying degrees of fever and increased white blood cells. Regarding the harms of cervical inflammation, women should have a correct understanding. Generally speaking, simple cervical inflammation does not pose a significant threat to health, but the symptoms such as increased vaginal discharge, back pain, and pelvic heaviness caused by cervical inflammation can affect mood. From a cancer prevention perspective, cervical inflammation is closely related to cervical cancer.

Specifically, the harms of cervical inflammation mainly include the following points:

1. **Affecting the quality of sexual life**

Severe cervical inflammation can affect the quality of sexual life, causing women to experience pain and discomfort during intercourse, leading to aversion towards sexual activity, thereby disrupting marital relations and affecting the relationship between couples.

2. **Likely to cause miscarriage**

Cervical inflammation is also a cause of miscarriage. Chronic cervical inflammation can lead to habitual miscarriages because it causes tissue changes, reducing elasticity, making labor difficult, and thus leading to miscarriage.

3. **Causing infertility**

About 20-25% of patients with excessive vaginal discharge are due to cervical inflammation. If the inflammation causes thick, purulent vaginal discharge, it can hinder the passage of sperm through the cervical canal, easily leading to female infertility.

4. **Inducing cervical cancer**

According to statistics, women with cervical inflammation have a cervical cancer incidence rate ten times higher than those without cervical inflammation. Long-term untreated or persistent cervical inflammation is considered a factor in the development of cervical cancer.
