Beautiful Legends of China's Top Ten Famous Teas

by hanhai1314 on 2007-06-12 11:20:30

[Longjing Tea]

Legend has it that when Emperor Qianlong went on a tour to the south of the Yangtze River, he came to the area below the Lion Peak Mountain in Hangzhou and watched the village girls picking tea leaves as a sign of caring about people's livelihood. On this day, Emperor Qianlong saw several village girls picking tea under more than ten lush tea bushes, and with a smile, he started to pick them too. After picking for a while, suddenly the eunuch reported: "The Empress Dowager is ill, please return to the capital immediately." Hearing that the Empress Dowager was sick, Emperor Qianlong casually put a handful of tea leaves into his bag and hurried back to the capital without delay. In fact, the Empress Dowager only ate too much rare delicacies, her liver fire rose temporarily, her eyes were red and swollen, and her stomach felt uncomfortable, but there was no serious illness. When she saw her son coming, she just smelled a whiff of fragrance and asked what good things he had brought. The emperor also found it strange, where did the fragrance come from? He touched it by hand, ah, it turned out to be a handful of tea leaves from the foot of Lion Peak Mountain in Hangzhou. A few days later, they had dried and the strong aroma was emanating from them. The Empress Dowager wanted to taste the flavor of the tea, so the maid brewed the tea and served it to the Empress Dowager. Indeed, the fragrance wafted to her nose. After drinking a sip, her eyes became comfortable instantly. After finishing the tea, her redness and swelling disappeared, and her stomach didn't feel bloated anymore. The Empress Dowager happily said, "The tea from Longjing in Hangzhou is truly a miraculous medicine." Seeing the Empress Dowager so happy, Emperor Qianlong immediately gave the order to seal the eighteen tea trees in front of the Hu Gong Temple at the foot of Lion Peak Mountain in Hangzhou as imperial tea. Every year, the newly picked tea would be specially offered to the Empress Dowager. To this day, the eighteen imperial tea trees in front of the Hu Gong Temple in Longjing Village, Hangzhou are still preserved. Many tourists visiting Hangzhou even make a special trip to investigate and take commemorative photos.

Longjing tea (one of China's Top Ten Famous Teas) and Hupao Spring have always been known as the "Hangzhou Twin Wonders." How did the Hupao Spring come about? It is said that long ago there were two brothers named Dahu and Erhu. The two brothers were extraordinarily strong. One year, the two came to Hangzhou and wanted to settle down in the small temple where they lived now. The monk told them that water was scarce here, and they had to climb over several ridges to fetch water. The brothers said, "As long as we can live here, fetching water is our job," so the monk took them in. One summer, due to drought and lack of rain, the small stream also dried up, making the water supply even more difficult. One day, the two brothers remembered the wandering "Tongzi Spring" on Mount Heng in Nanyue. If they could bring the Tongzi Spring to Hangzhou, it would be great. The two brothers decided to go to Mount Heng to move the Tongzi Spring. After a long journey, they collapsed at the foot of Mount Heng. A violent storm broke out. After the wind stopped and the rain subsided, they woke up and saw a child holding a willow branch standing in front of them. This was the little immortal who managed the "Tongzi Spring." After hearing their story, the little immortal pointed with the willow branch, and water sprinkled on their bodies. Instantly, the two brothers turned into two colorful tigers, and the child leapt onto the tiger's back. The tiger roared to the sky and carried the "Tongzi Spring" straight back to Hangzhou. The old monk and the villagers dreamed a dream at night. They dreamed that Dahu and Erhu turned into two fierce tigers, moved the "Tongzi Spring" to Hangzhou, and there would be spring water at dawn. The next day, the sky was filled with countless rays of light, and the two tigers descended from the sky. The fierce tiger clawed at the ground in the bamboo garden beside the temple, and soon dug a deep pit. Suddenly, a violent storm broke out. After the rain stopped, a gushing clear spring emerged from the deep pit. Everyone understood, it must be the spring water that Dahu and Erhu brought for them. To commemorate Dahu and Erhu, they named the spring "Hutiger Spring." Later, for convenience, it was called "Hupao Spring." Using the Hupao Spring water to brew Longjing tea results in an excellent color, aroma, and taste. Nowadays, in the Hupao Tea House, you can taste this exquisite drink of the "Twin Wonders."
