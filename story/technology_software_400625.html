In 2011, physical goods purchased online gradually resumed taking orders...

by anonymous on 2011-04-01 13:14:54

Dear Valued Buyers,

First of all, thank you for your continuous support of our store! We originally planned to resume online shopping services on the tenth day of the first lunar month. However, due to repeated requests from buyers to place orders, we have decided to start accepting orders today ahead of schedule. As for the shipping date, it will depend on the seller's arrangements and the recovery of express delivery services in different regions. Please understand that for urgent items, please inform us in advance. We strongly advise you to purchase with caution. Our store guarantees to place an order with the seller immediately after receiving the deposit and actively follow up on the delivery status. Thank you for your cooperation!

Regarding fees: Given that new users frequently ask about related fee issues, I would like to reiterate some points here. Since we started online shopping, our store has always charged a minimum fee of 2.0 yuan per order. For amounts exceeding this, we charge 6% of the total shopping price. For example, if the order total is 100.0 yuan, we would charge 100X6%=6.0 yuan as a transaction fee. For single orders totaling 1200.0 yuan (including 1200.0 yuan) or less, we charge a maximum of 30.0 yuan per order. For amounts exceeding 1200.0 yuan, we still charge 6%. For instance, if the order total is 1280.0 yuan, the transaction fee would be 30.0 + 80X6%=34.80 yuan.

Regarding the issue of fees, I would like to say a few words: If we consider it from the consumer's perspective, everyone naturally wants the lowest possible fees and the best possible service. But think about it: you want the horse to run but don't want to feed it grass; you want to eat well but don't want to spend money – dreaming of perfect scenarios? What I want to say is, personally speaking, my usual consumption level isn't high either. The fee standard I set has been carefully considered and deemed acceptable by someone with my consumption level. Our store has consistently maintained equal charging standards regardless of your consumption level, ensuring openness and transparency. Each buyer receives detailed explanations of the fees on their receipt. If you happen to get a receipt from our store with differing fee standards, you can bring it back to us for clarification. I hereby commit: if it is found that our store has falsified these charges, we will accept any penalty from the buyer.

If you insist on comparing this fee standard with others, saying someone else charges less for similar services, then let’s be straightforward: you can directly cooperate with the cheaper alternative, or even block me – we won’t force anyone. Some people say I lack patience, and I admit I indeed have little patience when dealing with such discussions. I can only guarantee that I have absolute responsibility in handling every transaction! Regarding the minimum fee per order, Taobao sets the standard at 5.0 yuan per order, and for transactions exceeding millions, it charges 50.0 yuan per transaction. Initially, I did consider adopting Taobao's standard. However, considering the reality, many of our buyers are students: first, they have no income source and rely on parents for money, though many families are doing well financially; second, choosing online shopping is mainly for convenience and affordability, especially affordability. If the transaction fee is set too high, plus shipping costs, and the total ends up being close to market prices, it will significantly reduce buyers' interest in online shopping; third, the consumption level in our area is not high, it’s more rural. Therefore, before truly implementing the 5.0 yuan minimum transaction fee, I decided to set it at 2.0 yuan per order instead. Additionally, regarding the 6%, some sellers were incredulous when I mentioned my 6% charge, saying that several purchasing agents they knew (in Shanghai and Zhejiang regions) generally charge 10%-15%. Honestly, following that standard would scare off customers! So far, our store has maintained its original fee standard with no short-term fluctuations planned. Please don't hesitate unnecessarily...

About chatting formalities: Earlier, I mentioned thanking everyone for supporting our store. Due to your support, our store has grown from a few friends on one QQ number to over 1,400 friends across three QQ numbers. During busy times, I often face multiple chat windows simultaneously. Therefore, when consulting about purchases, please avoid questions like "Hello, are you there?" and go straight to the point with concise expressions. It is particularly important to note that when shopping, please follow the correct format, clearly noting everything in text before sending it to our store. Guangzhou VIP Information Technology Co., Ltd., simply put, if you see a product you like, please copy the product's URL and paste it into the QQ chat window. Then, after a space, specify the details of the item you wish to purchase: indicate the size if applicable, the color if applicable, and finally the quantity. All these details are essential. For single items without color or size options, just note the quantity. Thank you for your cooperation!

Screenshots, shaking windows, video/audio chats: At any time, for any matter, please do not communicate with me using these methods. There is nothing that cannot be expressed through text, right? First, about screenshots, because of the numerous open chat windows and slow internet, the images you send often fail to load. Even if they do load sometimes, it's difficult to retrieve them later when verifying past conversation records, which could lead to disputes. If special products require images, you can send files instead, which will result in clearer outcomes than screenshots. Next, shaking windows – I don't know how many friends I've blocked due to shaking windows. Truly, I find this method of communication very annoying! Some friends shake the window just for fun, leaving me helpless!! I don't know whether Tencent or Taobao "invented" this bad trick! Alibaba also supports this feature, calling it "screen shaking." I want to ask those who like to shake: if you're busy chatting or operating something on your computer, typing a string of words fluently, and suddenly a window shakes interrupting you, causing you to repeat your actions, sometimes even leading to operational errors, sending messages intended for someone else to another person entirely, wouldn't you be upset?! Once or twice might be fine, but if this happens repeatedly, I really find it extremely irritating!!! Because of this irritation, I myself never use the window-shaking method to urge others for replies. Is your house on fire? Unless it's an emergency, why are you shaking? Call 119 for fires – that's common sense, understood? Lastly, video and voice chats – please note that my computer does not have video or audio equipment installed, so please do not send me videos or voice messages. If you intend to send something for me to see, forgive me for asking: are you planning to perform a striptease for me? Please send it between 2-3 AM...
