IFM Proximity Sensors

　　Inductive Sensors

　　Currently, inductive sensors are widely used in the industrial field. Compared to mechanical switches, inductive sensors have perfect prerequisites: contact-free and wear-free operation, high switching frequency, and precision. Additionally, they are resistant to vibration, dust, and moisture. Inductive sensors can detect all metals without contact.

　　Magnetic Sensors

　　In control technology, magnetic sensors use a contact-free and wear-free working method for position detection, which can be applied in all areas where inductive sensors are limited. Since magnetic fields can penetrate all non-magnetized materials, magnetic sensors can identify objects such as non-ferromagnetic materials, stainless steel, aluminum, plastic, or wooden walls.

　　Capacitive Sensors

　　Capacitive sensors can detect any object in a contact-free manner. Compared to inductive sensors that only detect metals, capacitive sensors can also detect non-metallic materials. Typical application fields include the wood industry, paper industry, glass manufacturing, plastics manufacturing, food industry, and chemical industry.

　　Infrared/Red Light Sensors

　　In all areas where it is necessary to safely and contactlessly identify the precise position of an object, photoelectric sensors should be used. Regardless of the material of the object being detected, this product can function properly. Compared to proximity sensors, photoelectric sensors have a larger detection range.

　　Through-beam Sensors

This system consists of two separate components: a transmitter and a receiver. The transmitter emits light in the infrared or red wavelength range. The receiver identifies the arriving beam and immediately converts any interruption caused by an object within the detection range into a switching signal.

　　Retro-reflective Sensors

The transmitter and receiver are housed in one casing and the emitted beam is reflected back to the receiver via a reflector mirror. When an object appears in the beam path and interrupts it, a switching action occurs. Retro-reflective sensors without polarization filters work with infrared light, while systems with polarization filters operate with visible red light.

　　Diffuse Reflective Sensors

Used for direct object detection. Like retro-reflective sensors, diffuse reflective sensors house both the transmitter and receiver in one casing. The transmitter emits light, which is reflected by the object being detected and identified by the receiver. This system does not evaluate beam interruptions but rather the light reflected back from the object. It is important that the switching function is adjusted according to the light-on/dark-on mode. The emitted light from this system is also pulsed.

　　Fork and Angle Photoelectric Sensors

Similar to through-beam sensors, these consist of facing transmitters and receivers. Both components are fixed in fork-shaped or angular configurations and do not need to be aligned face-to-face.

　　Fork and Angle Photoelectric Sensors trigger a switch signal when an object enters the detection area and interrupts the light beam. These sensors use particularly fine beams, suitable for detecting small objects.

　　Infrared Sensors

Detect heat radiation in the µm range and convert it into electronic switch signals. The sensor works as an infrared receiver. Thus, the object itself acts as the sensor's transmitter. The spectrum of the emitted infrared rays directly depends on the object's temperature. Compared to temperature sensors (such as PT100), the advantage of this sensor is that there is no mechanical contact between the object and the sensor.

IFM Flow Sensors

　　Flow Sensors, Flow Sensor Units, and Flow Meters

　　Sensors operating according to calorimetric and ultrasonic principles are especially suitable for monitoring gas and liquid media.

　　IFM offers various sensor models to meet different applications and requirements. All systems are carefully designed so that signals can be directly transmitted to the control system for further processing.

　　Flow sensor units provide an analog output signal and display flow changes on-site via an LED bar.

　　Airflow sensors are specifically designed to monitor airflow in air conditioning equipment and for cooling switch cabinets. DC and AC specifications are suitable for other application areas.

　　Sensors are made from different materials, such as stainless steel, ceramics, and titanium, with plug-in connectors or cable connections, specifically designed for connection to individual process displays.

　　DC and AC specification control displays evaluate the signals from flow sensors and issue two-position output signals for further processing. They feature wire break protection and temperature monitoring.

　　Flow measurement systems based on calorimetric principles monitor the consumption of compressed air and gases and can also identify minor leaks. Ultrasonic flow meters detect water usage and flow.

　　Both systems offer computing functions with various output capabilities.

IFM Pressure Sensors

　　Pressure Sensors and Pressure Sensor Units

　　IFM provides a wide variety of electronic pressure and vacuum sensors to meet the application requirements of different industrial fields. Depending on the scope of use and application requirements, IFM offers sensors with ceramic capacitive sensing elements and sensors with thick-film DMS stainless steel sensing elements. Various output functions and appropriately designed housings ensure perfect matching for different processes.

　　Sensors with ceramic sensing elements have appropriate support in pipe connections and can withstand loads with rated pressures ranging from 1 bar to 600 bar. These sensors are particularly durable, resistant to mechanical influences, and can operate completely wear-free. Therefore, they have long-term stability and can ensure reliable operation for more than 100 million pressure cycles.

　　The stainless steel sensing element of the sensor has a measuring element with thick-film technology. The product has an integral housing and is very durable. The sealing performance of the stainless steel sensing element is excellent, ensuring high safety. Depending on the measurement range of the product, IFM's pressure sensors with stainless steel sensing elements have burst pressures ranging from 3 to 30 times and strong impact and vibration resistance characteristics, ensuring maximum safe operation performance.

　　The accuracy of sensors with piezoresistive silicon sensing elements is as accurate as those with ceramic sensing elements. Due to its perfect special structure, even without connecting a second sensor, this sensor can still monitor differential pressure.

　　In addition, IFM's diverse range of pressure sensors provides particularly reliable system solutions. Special pressure sensors are suitable for more application areas. With the help of PC-based communication and programming software solutions, IFM pressure sensors can be used more conveniently.

IFM Temperature Sensors

　　Temperature Sensors and Temperature Sensor Units

　　Temperature regulation and monitoring are primary tasks in automation and process technology, such as in pipeline process equipment, where the correct temperature plays a decisive role in the quality and economic efficiency of the process. To prevent dangerous situations, temperature must be controlled in many applications.

　　Therefore, not only compact temperature sensors and temperature sensor units can be used, but also cable sensors and rod-type sensor units that can be connected to external control displays. Additionally, in some industrial applications, non-contact infrared sensors must be used.

　　Unlike other sensors, temperature sensors come in a rich variety of models. Different temperature ranges, immersion depths, environmental conditions, and media require different types of temperature sensors.

　　Whether it is a rod-type sensor unit (TT, TM) or a cable sensor (TS), they are suitable for solid, liquid, or gaseous media in pipes or tanks in industrial and sanitary applications: IFM Electronics provides suitable temperature sensors for all industrial applications.
