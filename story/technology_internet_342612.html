Creating a low-carbon network environment, dual-line hosting solves high-carbon access

by now on 2010-04-09 08:58:57

After the Qingming Festival holiday, "digital" tomb sweeping has drawn great attention from all circles. When people begin to commemorate their ancestors on the Internet, it becomes more clear that the influence of the network has permeated all aspects of life. The traditional Chinese tomb sweeping combined with the avant-garde Internet era, making it not hard for people to see that this is just a small aspect of the performance of the low-carbon economy on the Internet.

The Internet is a grand platform for information exchange across industries. The rapid development of e-commerce further uncovers the economic exchange capabilities of the Internet, and the rise of the Internet of Things also brings people more expectations. However, inevitably, when people seek various kinds of help from the Internet, the information feedback from the Internet is becoming increasingly "high-carbon."

With the rising trend of individuals and enterprises building websites, all sorts of websites are competing for prominence on the Internet. By the end of 2009, the number of websites in China had reached 3 million, and this figure continues to rise. However, the eight-second rule of the Internet has caused headaches. Website access being obstructed leads to customer loss, which directly results in economic losses - something every website manager would rather avoid. The "high-carbon" state of the eight-second rule has been a major problem troubling website managers.

For this reason, I visited one of the top five domestic virtual hosting service providers, Times Interconnect (www.now.cn/vhost), and learned that the main cause of this "high-carbon" state in network access obstruction is economic loss. The reasons for website access obstruction mainly fall into two categories: line stability and space stability.

Firstly, China has always had issues with poor North-South access. Therefore, choosing the right line is the most important part of website construction. Although selecting lines by region is the most direct and effective method, it may affect future development. Most website managers hope for a better solution. In response to market demand, Times Interconnect has specially launched a set of three dual-line hosts, covering three space specifications, meeting the different needs of personal and enterprise website builders. With dual telecommunications and Unicom lines, smart judgments are made during line selection, saving access time. Supporting GCDN website accelerators benefits enterprise website acceleration engines, removes obstacles to North-South interconnection development, increases website access speed, and attracts more potential users. Additionally, there are current discounts, with not only better prices but also a COM top-level domain name for an extra dollar, helping website builders better complete their website construction and conserve resources.

Secondly, choosing a stable space is another key consideration for every website builder. This includes hardware configuration, data center network environment, and the professionalism of maintenance teams. A good environment can provide sufficient assurance for space maintenance. It's worth noting that 7x24-hour technical support is also indispensable. This service allows website managers to receive timely help when encountering technical problems, avoiding losses due to delays, and creating a good website operation environment.

The "high-carbon" state of the eight-second rule is merely one aspect of the incomplete utilization of Internet resources, and using a stable dual-line host can effectively solve this problem. Low-carbon has not only become the first proposal of the CPPCC during this year's two sessions but is also a major theme direction for the transformation of social and economic lifestyles today. The Internet still has great development prospects. To create an environmentally friendly, low-carbon network environment, efforts from all industries are still needed.
