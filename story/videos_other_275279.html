Financial management includes two very important aspects: one is how to use the money you already have to make more money, and the second is how to spend less money but get more done. Therefore, knowing how to spend money is also a part of financial management. I have always been committed to being a civilian economist, so I pay close attention to ordinary people's lives and enjoy living like an ordinary person. I drive a small car, but I also ride a bicycle; I buy tall buildings from small state-owned enterprises, but I also rent houses to live in; I like spending money, but I also like thinking about how to spend it more scientifically. Interestingly, in recent years, I have almost not spent a single cent on air tickets, yet I have traveled by plane to many places I wanted to go across the country, including Chongqing to Shanghai air tickets, visiting many famous mountains and rivers, meeting many old classmates and friends, and getting to know a large number of social celebrities. The following example can tell everyone how I traveled around the country for free by plane. The price of China Civil Aviation air tickets fluctuates every day according to market conditions. Different routes at different times often have different discount rates. So as long as you can flexibly arrange your time, you can save a lot of money on air tickets. On March 21, 2006, Professor Mao Yushi and I went to Yinchuan for a lecture. At that time, the air ticket from Changsha to Yinchuan was only discounted by 90%, costing 1600 yuan, and there were only flights on the 1st, 3rd, and 5th, meaning that we had to go to Yinchuan on the 19th. However, the air ticket from Changsha to Xi'an on the 19th was 50% off, costing only 640 yuan, and the air ticket from Xi'an to Yinchuan on the 20th was 40% off, costing 340 yuan. And I had long wanted to visit my two old classmates in Xi'an, so I chose to fly from Changsha to Xi'an on the 19th, played with my old classmates in Xi'an for a day, and then transferred to Yinchuan. This way, I not only saved a day's time, but it was also equivalent to specially flying to Xi'an to visit my old classmates, and I saved 620 yuan (1600-640-340=620 yuan) on air tickets. In August 2006, I had to go to Guangzhou to provide consulting services for a company. The air ticket from Beijing to Guangzhou was only 90% off, costing 1670 yuan, while the air ticket from Beijing to Shanghai was 2.5% off, costing 410 yuan, and the air ticket from Shanghai to Guangzhou was 40% off, costing 640 yuan. Before this, I had long wanted to visit Professor Zhang Wuchang in Shanghai. So I chose to visit Professor Zhang Wuchang in Shanghai first, and then transfer from Shanghai to Guangzhou. This way, it was also equivalent to specially flying to Shanghai to visit Professor Zhang, and I saved 620 yuan (1670-410-640=620 yuan) on air tickets. Even more interestingly, in May 2007, Professor Mao Yushi and I went to Zhenjiang, Hunan for a lecture. At that time, I was worried about the tightness of air tickets during the "May Day" Golden Week, so I booked connecting air tickets in advance from Beijing to Changsha and then to Huaihua Zhenjiang. At that time, the air ticket from Beijing to Changsha was 60% off, costing 860 yuan, and the air ticket from Changsha to Zhenjiang was full price, costing 650 yuan. Unexpectedly, three days after booking, the civil aviation booking office called to say that the flight from Changsha to Zhenjiang would be delayed by 2 hours, and passengers had two choices: either agree to the delayed departure or refund the ticket at the original price. Although I knew that there was only one flight per day from Changsha to Zhenjiang, so there was no other choice, I knew that the booking price of civil aviation changes every day, so I did not immediately choose to agree to the delayed departure. Soon, I learned that if I booked the ticket on the same day from Beijing to Changsha, it would be 80% off, costing 1100 yuan, and from Changsha to Zhenjiang, it would be 50% off, costing 360 yuan. So I chose to keep the air ticket from Beijing to Changsha, refund the air ticket from Changsha to Zhenjiang, and then rebooked the same flight from Changsha to Zhenjiang. This way, I saved (650-360)x2 people = 580 yuan. All my family lives in Loudi, Hunan. I came to Beijing purely because I regard joining the Tianze Economic Research Institute as a valuable and very happy career, so I have to return to Hunan for a week every month. But as long as I am not in a hurry, as long as I haven't bought a ticket to Changsha at a discount of less than 50%, as long as there is a ticket to another city I really want to go to at a discount of less than 40%, I will choose to transit through that city I really want to go to, and then travel or visit friends there. Because of using this trick of taking flights, in recent years, regarding Chongqing air tickets, I have almost traveled everywhere I wanted to go across the country without spending a penny on air tickets.
