Hysteroscopic Treatment for Ovarian Cysts:

If the ovarian cyst is located under the uterine mucosa, medically referred to as a submucosal ovarian cyst, this type of cyst is suitable for removal via hysteroscopic electrocautery. Hysteroscopic surgery uses a long, thin medical endoscope that enters the uterine cavity through the natural channel of the cervical opening to perform the surgery. This method does not damage the abdominal wall, features minimal trauma, short surgical time, minimal bleeding, and rapid postoperative recovery.

Laparoscopic Treatment for Uterine Fibroids:

This surgical technique requires only a few 3-millimeter incisions on the abdomen. Advantages include minimal trauma, quick recovery, ability to walk the same day, full recovery within 3-5 days, no impact on physiological functions, and more. Laparoscopic technology not only aids in diagnosis and treatment but also separates adhesions, helps with conception, ensures surgical safety, and prevents recurrence. It is widely adopted in international gynecological surgeries. This procedure is not only for infertile women seeking solutions due to childlessness but is also recommended for women who already have children if the fibroid is large (diameter greater than 6cm), causing excessive menstrual flow, ineffective drug conservative treatment, or presenting compressive symptoms and rapid fibroid growth.
