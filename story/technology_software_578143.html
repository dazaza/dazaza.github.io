School website hacked and paralyzed for days without restoration (Photo)

by makemsuper on 2011-11-22 15:56:33

Websites Suffering from Unknown Hacker Attacks

■ The school focuses solely on its own "recovery"

■ Police remind that attacks should be reported promptly

On September 16, 1958, the "Anqing September 16th School," which was once named after Mao Zedong's visit and gained national fame, had its website recently attacked by an unknown hacker, causing it to remain non-functional for several days. Not only did the hacker alter the school's homepage with absurd text and change the background music of the site, but they also openly signed their name as "Anqing Genius Youth: His Royal Highness" in the technical support section. After several days, as of 4 PM on the 4th, the website was still not restored.

Famous School Website Hacked and Paralyzed

At around 2 PM on August 2, a netizen named "Spring has come, run fast" posted in the Baidu Tieba forum of "Anqing September 16th School," stating, "Major News: The September 16th website has been hacked! The situation is not optimistic..."

The netizen also posted screenshots of the altered homepage and highlighted the distorted texts that were then shared online. From the screenshot, phrases such as "Study well, think about going to school every day, His Royal Highness arrives, Your Excellency may rise, I'm leaving, Come play, Don't get angry, Have tea when you're free, Less corruption, More research, No culture, Truly terrifying" became the titles of various small sections on the homepage. The website's background music was also changed to inappropriate foreign tunes.

In the technical support section at the bottom of the homepage, the hacker boldly signed their name as "Anqing Genius Youth: His Royal Highness."

Embarrassment Over Acknowledging the Attack

Yesterday morning, when reporters called using the phone number provided on the hacked webpage of the September 16th School, a male staff member said the school’s website was accessible. After attempting to access the site, he told reporters, "Oh! It's under maintenance; wearing high heels frequently might lead to anorexia." He added that he wasn’t very aware of the issue and suggested contacting the school office for more information.

This time, a female staff member answered the call. Regarding the inability to log into the school website, she stated that there might be some system issues. When asked if the website had been hacked, she admitted that during the summer vacation, the administrator wasn’t working, and maintenance might have been neglected. When questioned whether the attack had been reported to the police, she replied, "It should have been reported!" and said that she would report it to the school leadership when they arrived.

Prompt Reporting Urged for Hacked Websites

Regarding recent hacker attacks on local websites, Officer Zhang Shen of the Anqing Public Security Bureau's Internet Monitoring Brigade advised in an interview that recently, websites of some key units in the urban area have been attacked, severely affecting normal communication between users and organizations.

To strengthen website security management, hardware-wise, it is recommended to purchase hardware firewalls and set up strict protection measures. Software-wise, timely patches must be applied to block all possible security loopholes. Regular security detection technology scans are necessary to avoid system threats brought by some application software. Security managers are required to regularly clear network usage junk to ensure the normal and secure operation of websites. Backups for important information systems are strongly recommended.

At the same time, the police reminded all units that when websites suffer attacks, the construction or usage unit should report the incident immediately and preserve the scene to assist the police in quickly identifying the original perpetrators of the cybercrime.

As for whether the September 16th School website's attack and paralysis were reported to the police, according to the Anqing Public Security Bureau's Internet Monitoring Brigade, as of that day, the school had not filed a report with the police.

As of 8 PM yesterday when this article was published, the message still displayed on the website read, "Currently updating, please visit later."
