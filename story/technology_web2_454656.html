With the continuous development of society and multimedia technology, modern educational multimedia courseware has gained widespread application. Using multiple media information such as graphics, images, text, sound, and animation to stimulate students' senses, facing lively letters applications, music, showcasing the clever fusion of knowledge formation, enriching educational methods, cultivating students' thinking skills, comprehensively enhancing the high quality of students. In our institute, many teachers still use this advanced teaching method in education and teaching to improve teaching quality. The college has made a large investment in multimedia teaching equipment and facilities, increasing the number of multimedia classrooms to meet the needs of modern multimedia teaching. The creation of multimedia courseware is inseparable from guidance in aspects such as modern educational thought, educational theory, learning theory, and instructional design theory. Different courses, teaching content, and teaching objectives result in different ways and forms for students to learn. Teachers need to grasp the design principles of multimedia courseware well, choosing different teaching theories based on course settings, teaching content, and teaching objectives. Sufficiently advanced teaching theory and complete multimedia courseware allow learners to immerse themselves in a specially pre-built environment, learning knowledge and skills; utilizing the significant advantages of multimedia courseware in teaching fully realizes teaching objectives. I present one opinion regarding the design principles of multimedia courseware.

The behaviorist learning theory in teaching and learning guides the principle of courseware design. Behavior theory believes that cause and response to stimuli, in a specific environment with special sound, light, graphics, images, and other multimedia effects, stimulate students. When students make clear responses during learning, it is achieved. It emphasizes: 1. Learning is the link between stimuli and responses. What kind of stimuli will result in what kind of response. (2) The learning process is a gradual process, understanding the whole part of events. 3. To strengthen the key to successful learning, learning should focus on knowledge and skills, emphasizing the study of external behavior. According to behaviorist learning theory, courseware design follows dividing much of the teaching content into small units, gradually forming questions and answers. When students want to enter the next unit's learning steps of a series of teaching content teaching objectives, they must complete the operation of the current machine. If students make mistakes, the courseware provides students with some hints or directly tells them the right answer, thereby completing teaching tasks and objectives. The use of such courseware in the teaching process is shown as follows: under the guidance of multimedia courseware design behavior learning theory, the following principles should be grasped: 1. Given goals. Courseware design targets educational teaching, allowing students to indicate behavior. Teaching goals and learning practice are closely linked, forming the directory part of multimedia courseware. (2) Continuous one side. In courseware construction, rich dishes and review work positions ensure students' revision and side test design library, strengthening student learning and promoting memory. 3 Step-by-step principle: designing a small unit of knowledge in an orderly series from shallow to deep, implementing step by step, ensuring the change in difficulty between knowledge in multimedia courseware content is small, keeping students' high learning interest, and smoothly completing learning goals. Teaching through this method, such as theoretical courses, designs courseware: mathematics. Generally using graphics, images, sound, animation, and other multimedia information representations. Such courseware only passively allows students to receive knowledge, neglecting the initiative to understand the process, so there are great limitations in courseware design. However, due to simple production, quickness, and ease in satisfying people's recognition of rules, now this way creates or multiple multimedia courseware for teachers.

Second, Constructivist Theory in Teaching and Courseware Design Principles. Constructivist teaching theory emphasizes the main role of students, understanding that students are the subject of information processing. Students acquire knowledge through interaction with the environment itself constructed, not filled by John. It emphasizes: 1. Learning is the process of knowledge construction. Knowledge comes from the interaction between students and the environment. Cultivating students to acquire knowledge through an institution of knowledge units, forming new knowledge through the knowledge chain. (2) Learning is an active process of constructing knowledge structure. Active students, through actively providing teachers with new knowledge, when the teacher controls new knowledge, thus conducting comprehensive knowledge chain activities, allowing students to continuously accumulate knowledge and property, maintaining an open knowledge structure unit with the knowledge chain. To mobilize students' learning enthusiasm. 3, There must be a rich learning scenario. The teacher's learning environment is conducive to students building the meaning of content, helping students apply knowledge to solve practical life problems. In the design of multimedia courseware under the guidance of constructivist learning theory, the following principles should be well grasped: 1. To play the main role of students. In courseware design, particular attention should be paid to designing the learning environment, using heuristic methods, creating knowledge methods to construct the meaning of learning problems, and verifying various hypotheses through multimedia experiences. This requires cultivating students' knowledge and problem-solving abilities more innovatively through multimedia systems, achieving quality education through courseware examples. (2) Play the leading role of teachers. In multimedia courseware design, multimedia technology is used to create a quiet environment, providing access to knowledge and books. Using computer network platforms allows students free access to the ocean of knowledge. Helping students build knowledge structures of current knowledge units. Constructivist theory in courseware production among teachers is widely used in guiding and creating rich teaching methods: commonly used modes include situational teaching, problem-based learning, and computer-supported cooperative learning courseware. With people-oriented, quality-oriented today's home education importance, such courseware shows strong teaching ability, fully cultivating students' innovation awareness and innovation capabilities, adapting to modern social needs.
