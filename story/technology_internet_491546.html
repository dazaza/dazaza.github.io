On December 7, market news reported that Alibaba had successfully acquired the alibank.cn domain name recently. Subsequently, a reporter from the Economic Reference News checked through the WHOIS domain query system and found that the current owner information of the alibank.cn domain, registered in 2007, showed as Alibaba. Some analysts pointed out that this acquisition of a new domain by Alibaba might indicate substantial progress on "Ali Bank."

Zhao Nige, head of the public relations department at Alibaba, introduced to the Economic Reference News that speculations about the progress of "Ali Bank" were not accurate. Regarding the use of the new domain, Zhao Nige explained that due to Alibaba's development of small loans for SMEs, they might adopt a completely new domain in the future, and alibank.cn, along with previously registered alibank.net and alibank.org, could all be used for related businesses.

"If 'Ali Bank' is successfully approved, it will become a landmark event in China's financial sector, positively promoting the entry of private capital into industries such as finance, and effectively breaking monopolies, allowing sectors like finance and telecommunications to grow more healthily," said Liang Jia, an analyst at CitiBank London, in an interview with the Economic Reference News. He also mentioned that the "Several Opinions of the State Council on Encouraging and Guiding the Healthy Development of Private Investment" has clearly encouraged private capital to enter the financial sector, so there is hope for "Ali Bank" to be quickly approved, truly breaking the barriers to entry in monopolistic industries.

Analysts stated that Alibaba's plan to establish a commercial bank may be based on two considerations. First, it can solve the high proportion of Yahoo's shareholding in Alipay. Due to national policies limiting foreign ownership in payment companies, and Yahoo's unwillingness to reduce its stake in Alipay (Yahoo holds a higher proportion of shares in Alipay via Alibaba), setting up a commercial bank and introducing new investors can dilute Yahoo's equity, thus better resolving the equity disputes between Alibaba and Yahoo. Second, it paves the way for Alipay to obtain an electronic payment license. According to the People's Bank of China's "Management Measures for Non-financial Institution Payment Services," no non-financial institution or individual may engage in or disguisedly engage in payment services without PBOC approval. If Alibaba can establish a commercial bank, then Alipay's "legitimacy" will be secured.

Guo Tianyong, a Ph.D. in Economics from Central University of Finance and Economics, believed that Alibaba was already very mature in online trading and e-commerce, laying a certain foundation for its future move into banking. On the other hand, Alibaba has an extremely rich customer base, which would be a natural advantage if it were to engage in banking in the future.

However, Yi Xianrong, a researcher at the Institute of Finance of the Chinese Academy of Social Sciences, thought that "Ali Bank" was basically impossible to be approved. He pointed out that from the perspective of equity structure, private capital applications to establish banks are unlikely to be approved. The financial industry operates under a prudent supervision principle, facing numerous regulatory requirements, making it difficult for private capital to meet current standards. Despite frequent news in recent years about private capital establishing banks, only Minsheng Bank has been approved, and Minsheng Bank was established under the leadership of the All-China Federation of Industry and Commerce, still carrying a certain degree of government coloration.
