Four balance theories for daily liver protection

by king0r8k on 2010-07-01 15:21:33

Liver diseases include hepatitis, cirrhosis, and liver cancer. The course of these diseases is generally long, with treatment lasting for several months to even years. Even after stabilization and recovery following treatment, there is a tendency for relapse. Therefore, how to properly care for oneself plays an extremely important role in the prognosis and rehabilitation of these diseases. Besides undergoing effective treatment, it is also necessary to closely coordinate mental health, diet, daily living habits, work, exercise, marriage, and sexual life, to achieve the purpose of supporting treatment, consolidating therapeutic effects, and preventing recurrence.

1. Regulation and Balance of Exercise

During the acute phase of hepatitis, chronic active hepatitis, severe hepatitis, and decompensated severe cirrhosis, liver function is significantly impaired and patients lack the strength for physical activity; rest during this period should primarily consist of bed rest. Additionally, in cases of cirrhosis complicated by serious infections (such as bacterial peritonitis), esophageal varices and gastric fundus vein rupture bleeding, or liver cancer patients, bed rest should be maintained, avoiding large activities and strenuous exercise. In the recovery phase of hepatitis, improvement phase of cirrhosis ascites, or stable phase of liver cancer, appropriate exercise can be carried out, mainly walking, but participation in large activities and strenuous exercise is still not possible.

When the liver function of hepatitis patients has basically recovered, the cirrhosis has stabilized or entered a quiescent phase, or when liver cancer shows significant improvement, more intense activities can be participated in. However, attention should be paid to balancing work and rest, avoiding heavy physical labor and strenuous exercise (such as running) to prevent overexertion and fatigue that may lead to disease relapse.

2. Regulation and Balance of Mental Health

Maintaining emotional stability, reducing anger, avoiding excessive mental stimulation, preventing extreme emotional fluctuations, correctly addressing the disease, maintaining an optimistic mood, establishing strong willpower and belief in victory are all crucial. Overcoming feelings of pessimism and despair can play a certain role in enhancing immune function, improving therapeutic effects, and promoting disease recovery. Especially for patients with chronic active hepatitis, cirrhosis, and liver cancer, apart from self-regulating their mental state, close cooperation from medical staff, family members, and friends is also needed to boost the patient's morale and actively cooperate with treatment, which is a key method.

3. Regulation and Balance of Daily Living Habits

Liver patients have delicate resistance, especially those with chronic hepatitis and cirrhosis, who have low immune functions and are prone to various infections such as respiratory, digestive, urinary system, and appendicitis infections. Infections can worsen the condition, leading to deterioration or even death. Attention should be paid to regulating daily living habits to prevent various infections, ensuring an orderly routine, maintaining regularity in life, paying attention to keeping warm in winter and preventing heatstroke in summer, avoiding overeating, and avoiding overexertion during exercise. It is also important to ensure fresh indoor air and personal hygiene.

4. Regulation and Balance of Marriage and Sexual Life

Marriage and sexual life are important aspects of life. For liver disease patients, when to get married and how to manage sexual life are extremely important. Hepatitis patients should marry only after being cured of hepatitis. HBV carriers with positive HBsAg or HBeAg but normal liver function can marry, and the partner can receive vaccinations to enhance preventive measures. Cirrhosis ascites patients and liver cancer patients, in principle, cannot marry, but those who have been rehabilitated after treatment and remained stable for many years can marry and have children. During the acute phase of hepatitis, or with liver function damage, chronic active hepatitis, active cirrhosis, large amounts of ascites due to cirrhosis, complications of cirrhosis, hepatorenal syndrome, portal hypertension with a tendency to bleed, post-esophageal variceal bleeding, or during the development phase of liver cancer, sexual life should be prohibited.
