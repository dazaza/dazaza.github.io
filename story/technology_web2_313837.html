Inter Milan Suddenly Announces a Flash Transfer Agreement! Toni to Join in Winter with Half the Salary

According to reports from Sina Sports, shortly after rumors emerged about Inter Milan's intention to invite Italian striker Luca Toni, currently playing for Bayern Munich in the Bundesliga, back to Serie A, Football Market web news exclusively revealed on Wednesday that Toni has already reached an agreement with Inter Milan. He might join Inter Milan in January, and his requested salary would be around 2.5 million euros. Football Market stated that it is still unclear whether Toni will join Inter Milan on loan or as a permanent transfer, and the economic terms agreed upon between Inter Milan and Bayern Munich regarding this transfer are also unknown.

It is reported that there are still some economic treatment-related details that need to be determined between Toni and Inter Milan. It is rumored that Toni's agent will come to Milan for negotiations next Monday. Currently, Toni's net salary after tax at Bayern Munich is approximately 4.8 million euros. If the above rumors are confirmed, it would mean that Toni is returning to his homeland after many years. Now, due to continuous ideological differences with team coach Louis van Gaal, Toni has gradually been marginalized and lost his starting position.

Actually, even before the start of the 2006 World Cup, Inter Milan had intended to invite then-popular Fiorentina striker Toni to join. Although it was not ultimately confirmed, it was generally believed that Inter Milan offered a very tempting 25 million euro bid to Fiorentina at the time. However, despite the threat of relegation due to football scandals, the price did not match the status of Toni, who had become the best scorer in Europe. Subsequently, after surviving the relegation crisis, under the firm attitude of club president Della Valle, Fiorentina kept Toni for another year before letting him go. In 2007, Toni transferred from Fiorentina to Bayern Munich in the Bundesliga, with an annual salary of 6 million euros, but his transfer fee was much lower than the rumored price offered by Inter Milan a year earlier, only 11 million euros. At Bayern Munich, Toni once shone brilliantly, but now he has completely gone downhill.

Now, Toni has once again set his sights on Serie A, hoping to start anew at all costs. Due to his differences with Bayern Munich's coach Van Gaal, he has gradually been squeezed out of the starting lineup and finds it difficult to continue his journey in the Bundesliga. For Toni, returning to Serie A is not just a reluctant choice; several clubs, including Napoli, Roma, and even Inter Milan, are quite interested in inviting him to join. Due to the upcoming Africa Cup of Nations, Inter Milan's forward Samuel Eto'o will be absent from club games in January, so the Nerazzurri are now looking for a capable replacement to fill Eto'o's position at that time. However, according to All Market, Toni is not a good choice. All Market believes that unless Inter Milan cannot acquire any players more likely to strengthen the team, they really shouldn't sign Toni. Given Toni's current age and form, it would be hard for him to compete with Diego Milito or Mario Balotelli for a starting position, and he might even become a burden to the team as soon as he joins. All Market believes that Toni and Inter Milan should have joined forces in the summer of 2006, but now things have changed, and it's too late to rekindle their past relationship.

However, during an interview with Sky TV, FIFA-registered agent Fabio Parisi expressed great optimism about Luca Toni joining Inter Milan when commenting on the club's impending transfer actions in January. He said: "The rumor of Cavani joining is undoubtedly the most attractive, but since he is still under contract, I doubt Zapparini would be willing to sell him. Moreover, the Uruguayan's price might be high, and I don't think Palermo would let him go. As for the possibilities of Pandev and Toni joining, the situation differs from Cavani. Since these two players are already on the fringes of their respective teams, it is easier for Inter Milan to bring them in. To sign Pandev, we need to wait for the arbitration committee's decision. In terms of timing, I don't know if we can complete this deal in January. Once the Macedonian successfully terminates his contract with Lazio, he will become a target for most European teams, not just Italian clubs."

Therefore, according to Fabio Parisi, Inter Milan's easiest option is to sign Toni. Parisi said: "Now, Toni has completely broken ties with Van Gaal. To maintain his position in the national team and his world champion title won in Germany, he wants to leave Bayern and find a new club. I think he can secure free transfer rights at a low cost. I believe that Inter Milan could offer him appropriate conditions by extending the contract term. Roma is also looking for a striker, and someone like Toni should be very appealing. However, unless it's a zero-cost transfer and signing, Toni's salary is still relatively high compared to their club's standards."
