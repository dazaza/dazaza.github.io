Corporate operations are enhanced by website establishment - 3 essential knowledge points for building a corporate website. Corporate website building is gradually becoming a trend in the wave of informatization. Establishing its own website benefits a company by enhancing its image, enabling it to have network communication capabilities, using the vast outlet of the internet to promote the company and its products, and also allowing for e-commerce functions.

So, what knowledge is needed for website construction? Today, IDC Review has compiled some basic knowledge for everyone's reference.

First, types of corporate websites:

1. Basic Information Type: Mainly targeting customers, industry professionals, or general browsers, focusing on introducing the company's basic information and helping establish the corporate image; can also appropriately provide news or knowledge information within the industry.

2. E-commerce Type: Mainly targeting suppliers, customers, or the consumer group of the company's products (services), providing services or transactions directly related to the company's business scope or services for business purposes; such websites can be considered at the core stage of e-commerce transformation. Due to differences in industry characteristics and the depth and breadth of corporate investment, their level of e-commerce may range from relatively basic service support and product listings to more advanced online payment stages.

3. Multimedia Advertising Type: Primarily targeting customers or the consumer group of the company's products (services), mainly promoting the company's core brand image or main products (services). This type resembles a print or television advertisement in terms of goals and actual presentation techniques, thus naming this type of website as "multimedia advertising" is more appropriate.

4. Product Display Type: Mainly targeting demanders, showcasing detailed information about the company's products and demonstrating the company's strength. Providing comprehensive introductions regarding product prices, production, and specifics. The primary purpose of this type of corporate site is to present the most direct and effective way to showcase its products. While emphasizing brand and image, attention should also be given to product introductions.

Second, choosing an appropriate website construction service provider:

1. Strong graphic design capabilities. Website content should be rich and professional, with page designs reflecting the company's business philosophy and strength, thereby enhancing competitive advantages in the industry.

2. Fast access speed, stable service, and supporting interconnectivity. According to surveys, people usually tolerate waiting for webpage loading for only 7 seconds. Many visitors may turn to other websites due to slow webpage opening speeds, so the browsing speed of the website is quite important.

3. Preferably choose professional website builders with strong capabilities, good reputation, and reliable after-sales services. Professional web companies can provide websites that meet specific needs. It's better to choose quality over solely pursuing low prices for the sake of corporate benefits, requiring careful consideration in making the choice.

Third, matters to attend to after corporate website construction:

1. Domain ownership issues. Domain ownership problems need to be negotiated with the website construction service provider. Some unethical providers may register domains under their own names, and if the company wishes to transfer virtual space, they might simply shut down the website. Therefore, the company should confirm with the service provider whether the domain username and password can belong to the company itself.

2. If renting a virtual host, the space belongs to the enterprise itself. The company needs to reclaim: 1. FTP username; 2. FTP password; 3. IP address; 4. Control panel username; 5. Control panel password; 6. Control panel address. If the service provider does not plan to provide the source code, these details will not be disclosed.

3. If the website program has a management backend, then the management address, username, and password must be requested from the website construction service provider.

Conclusion: If a company neglects its own website construction issues and merely makes exploratory operations with less information and technical content, such a website cannot bring added value to the company's operations. Therefore, companies need to continuously update and enrich their websites to play a bigger role.
