The call for the introduction of standards related to smart home is becoming increasingly apparent. As more and more functions of smart home products are integrated, it is necessary to establish a comprehensive management platform consisting of various systems such as security, network services, remote control, and home automation. This platform will integrate and uniformly manage the systems of various devices such as appliances, alarms, and intercoms. According to Zhao Hong, the market director of Xiamen Dinaic Electronic Technology Co., Ltd.: "Currently, each manufacturer has different targeted products, markets, sales models, and channels. In the future development of the smart home market, including smart shoe cabinets, close cooperation between home appliance manufacturers, system integrators, network operators, and security manufacturers is needed to integrate the advantages of each manufacturer, only then can the entire smart home market be expanded."

Currently, although the functions of smart home products are becoming increasingly powerful, there is a lack of unified industry standards and communication protocols between systems! Issues such as manufacturers operating independently, complex system operations, product instability, and poor coordination capabilities are factors that restrict the development of the smart home market. The key to integrating smart home systems with other systems lies in the unification of product communication protocols. Since each manufacturer produces according to different interface standards and protocols, interconnectivity between products is relatively difficult. During the process of system integration, technical problems can gradually be resolved. Currently, the only issue that manufacturers cannot solve is the market application problem because of the absence of standardization, which makes the market prone to various issues. As the saying goes, "Without rules, nothing can be accomplished." According to Qiu Yupeng, the marketing manager of the switch and socket business unit of ABB China's Low Voltage Products Business Department: "There are many manufacturers in the smart home market, but few brands can cover all aspects of smart homes. Moreover, an increasing number of users require manufacturers to provide them with overall solutions. For smart home systems, a universal, open, stable, and standardized system bus is crucial for the completeness of the solution, product stability, and functional scalability."
