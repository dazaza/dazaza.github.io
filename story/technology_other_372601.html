Rare Snow Disaster in Inner Mongolia

Xing'an League, November 27 - According to recent reports, severe snowfall occurred in pastoral areas such as科尔沁Right Front Banner in the northern part of Xing'an League in Inner Mongolia, affecting livestock production. After the disaster happened, the Ministry of Agriculture immediately contacted the disaster area to understand the situation and anti-disaster work, requiring local animal husbandry departments to take effective measures and make every effort to maintain the normal life and production order of farmers and herders. On November 26, a working group from the Ministry of Agriculture rushed to the disaster area, went deep into the front line of the disaster area such as Wulanmaodu Su Mu, understood the disaster situation, consoled farmers and herdsmen, guided disaster resistance and self-help, actively coordinated with the local government to clear snow on the main roads, restored the adjustment and transportation of forage, and urgently arranged 1 million yuan of local fiscal disaster relief funds.

It is reported that as early as September this year (2010), the Ministry of Agriculture issued a "Notice on Strengthening Forage Reserves and Doing a Good Job in Preventing and Reducing Disasters in Grassland Animal Husbandry", requiring increasing winter forage reserves for livestock in grassland pastoral areas, improving disaster prevention and resistance plans, and preventing potential dangers. It is understood that the growth of forage grass in the disaster-stricken pastoral areas was generally good this year, and the stored grass could at least last more than 2 months. According to the understanding of the working group of the Ministry of Agriculture, the roads in the disaster area have been basically unblocked, the emotions of farmers and herdsmen are stable, their lives are guaranteed, and livestock production has basically returned to normal. According to the deployment of the Ministry of Agriculture, Inner Mongolia's animal husbandry department has strengthened disaster prevention and resistance preparations in response to the actual local conditions, encouraging and guiding herdsmen to increase forage reserves.
