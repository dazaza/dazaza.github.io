The Ultimate Dual Cultivation Spirit's PK

by lk0gameg on 2009-07-18 14:33:49

I am Luan Shi VS Tian Yun, a Dance of Fans over the江山wei, level 151. Currently, many people are criticizing dual cultivation Youhun characters, saying things like "those who start dual cultivation after level 150 must have water in their brains" or that "dual cultivating Youhun after level 150 is garbage."

Everyone needs to understand one thing: After level 150, dual cultivation Youhun is no longer just about summoning; it transforms into a high-level Earth Immortal with Youhun! This is because our Curse skill has already reached level 7. Skills like Soul Devour, Devour, and Enhanced Revival also have at least 1 point invested. Everyone knows that these skills depend on Spirit, so dual cultivation Youhun players should try to put +10 Spirit stones on all your necklaces and rings!

Let me talk about my equipment. I mainly use 130+ gear all upgraded to 11 stars, but my staff is a 150 11-star. I wear blood clothes with 28,000 red health points, and after full status buffs, I have 1,500 physical defense and 7,400 magic defense. My pet's magic attack is 10,500. After merging, my physical attack reaches 16,000! Regarding spending money, I've invested quite a bit, but I've wasted a lot, plus I was stolen from once and lost everything. Actually, this set of equipment was re-obtained after being stolen at level 136, costing me 1,500. Buying points on Taobao is much cheaper, so I saved quite a bit! Of course, most of the equipment has been remodeled. If not remodeled, this set would probably only cost 1,100. However, remodeling for 400 wouldn't be enough. I spent all my salary from two months of Conquer Online work, plus some extra money earned by herding camels, to remodel everything piece by piece. First, I remodeled the weapon, rings, and necklaces because attack is the top priority. I don't want to chase the Sun upgrades anymore. Plus, since we've learned Curse, having Sun upgrades isn't that necessary!

One big weakness of dual cultivation Youhun criticized by others is the low HP of the summoned pet. For example, they say things like "your pet dies in two hits, so what's the point of fighting?" Of course, I often encounter situations where my pet is killed in two hits, but my newly summoned pet without any enhancements still has an attack power of 9,200 after adding Immortal status. Even without any status, its attack is still 8,000!

The reason is simple: After level 150, weapons do not need higher star levels, which significantly increases the attack power of summoned pets. Additionally, with the increase in Curse level and Spirit, summoning Youhun becomes akin to bringing a high-level Earth Immortal with a pet. Although we don't have Return Light, Curse and Soul Devour actually depend on Spirit effects. That means Curse level 7 doesn't just reduce 40% defense; with high Spirit, it reduces even more! I tested with a friend, and after cursing him, his magic defense dropped from 6,000 to 1,500! With my Spirit at 1,400, my Curse reduces the opponent's defense by more than 45%. I also got a small fan that adds +1 Curse, making my actual Curse level 8. Due to high Spirit, when I use Soul Devour, the opponent loses 1,400 red per second, totaling 7,000 red in 5 seconds. All of this ignores the opponent's defense!

When facing warriors above level 155, I rarely need to merge in one-on-one battles. I just go Invincible, Curse, and Soul Devour. Even if my pet is killed, my newly summoned pet still has an attack of 8,000. After the opponent is cursed, their defense can be as low as 5,000, assuming they're wearing full Sun gear and magic clothes at level 155. They'd be foolish not to run!

When encountering high-level wizards who instantly kill my pet, it's normal given the current high wizard attacks. I go Invincible, Curse, Soul Devour, Soul Absorb, then summon a new pet and have time to merge. The newly summoned pet, even without any status, has a merged attack of 11,000 and 45,000+ health. But the opponent's red health won't last long either. Nowadays, everyone wears casual clothes with Dragon's Might, so killing them takes at most two hits.
