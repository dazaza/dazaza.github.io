On the day I interviewed at Jingdezhen No. 2 Middle School, a regular football match was being held on the playground. The movements were fluid, and the shots were precise, showing that the children were fully committed. They ran all over the field, and each successful defense, tackle, or shot brought pure smiles to their faces. They were a group of teenagers who loved football.

"Training starts at 8 am, with a lot of content: running warm-ups, basic ball control movements, shuttle runs, passing and receiving... During the morning training session, no one complained about being tired or bitter, and no one was absent," said Lei Xilong, the head coach of the provincial youth football training team, while guiding the students and introducing them.

Every morning at 8 o'clock, more than 40 students from Class 17, Grade 7 at Jingdezhen No. 2 Middle School would start their training on time. "Integration of physical education and academics" is an attempt by the provincial football association, the Jingdezhen Education Bureau, and the Sports Bureau in this school. Jingdezhen No. 2 Middle School is the first youth football training school in our province, as well as the first provincial football training base established based on a campus. By opening football specialty classes and forming a campus football team, the school adopts a flexible and scientific educational method for training, achieving commendable results: they performed admirably in competitions held in Beijing and Shanghai, attracting attention from people in the football world. In 2008, five students were selected for the national youth hope training team.

Because they lived far from home, more than 20 students from the special football class at Jingdezhen No. 2 Middle School stayed in two rented apartments. Their daily lives were taken care of by a retired physical education teacher. The growth path of these young players under the age of 13 is not limited to professional football training; cultural knowledge enhancement is indispensable. To cultivate football talents, the school has specially compiled textbooks to improve the comprehensive knowledge of the young players.

Last week, I came to Jingdezhen for the second time for an interview, shooting the Fuliang County Youth Girls' Football Team, which consists of more than 20 elementary school students from Fuliang No. 1 Primary School and No. 2 Primary School. On the football field, the children were practicing earnestly. Whether it was shuttle runs, passing and receiving balls, or resistance training, they were continuously improving. If one round wasn't done well, they would practice again until their footwork became more solid. A pair of twin sisters especially caught the eye, fighting under the scorching sun, competing... sweat splashing on the football field.

Mentioning youth girls' football in Fuliang County, almost everyone knows about it. In 2009, a group of pure Fuliang girls represented the Beijing team in volleyball matches, winning multiple championships in the national U-13 girls' football competition. Recently, 38 Fuliang girls are training at the Beijing Daxing Youth Football Training Base. Recently, Fuliang girls Fan Yuqiu, Li Yingqing, and Fang Yun were selected for the national women's football (U-14) training team, preparing to participate in the AFC U-14 Women's Football Championship in Vietnam in June.

"Our success in youth football cannot be separated from the support and care of various levels of departments. The provincial football association has coordinated to solve issues such as venue construction and competition funds for us; In 2005, we proposed the 'Football Vitality Project', first focusing on cultivating a batch of excellent football coaches, currently having 9 full-time coaches; In primary schools, we promote普及and conduct regular training and competitions, promoting campus football. Participating in various football competitions nationwide and throughout the province, the Jingdezhen representative team has won 12 championships," Coach Lei Xilong, who also serves as the vice chairman of the city football association, talked in detail about the youth football he personally built up.

Xiao Tai-xi, member of the Chinese National Youth Team, participated in the Dutch Ajax Cup International Youth Football Competition.

Ding Shi-jun and Luo Yi-min, members of the Chinese Youth Football Hope Team, topped the charts at the German Youth Football International Invitational Tournament (U-13) and played all 7 games as key players.

Yang Xin, player of Guangzhou Evergrande's second tier in the Chinese Super League, participated in the trial training of the British Chelsea Football Team, and is currently representing Guangzhou in the City Games.

It is reported that the Shaanxi Bafa Club recruited 16 young players from Jingdezhen for their reserve team, Changchun Yatai Club selected 2 players, and the Shanghai Dongya team of the China League One took away 5 players. Currently, more than 20 young players from Jingdezhen have been selected for national teams or attracted by CSL and China League One teams.

Chinese football needs to start from the children. Today's young footballers will be tomorrow's football stars. We hope their dreams come true.
