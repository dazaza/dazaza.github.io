Nanjing's real estate market sees a price-cutting trend with the maximum reduction approaching 13% (Part I)

by comeccoo81 on 2010-05-12 15:32:52

Nanjing's Jiangbei real estate market suddenly saw a wave of price cuts yesterday. With its super-sized developments consistently leading in both development and sales volume in Nanjing, the Jiangbei real estate market suddenly made a splash yesterday! Following the Xuri Shangcheng development’s launch of a fixed-price offer for over 300 units at 4500 yuan/square meter, Suning Properties also rolled out a fixed-price strategy for its three Qiaobei developments: the Tianruncheng development set a fixed price of 4088 yuan/square meter for 350 units, which is about 600 yuan/square meter cheaper than before.

The continuous discounting and promotions by the Qiaobei developments raise questions: does their collective price drop indicate that Jiangbei housing prices can no longer hold? Is this a sign of an overall decline in Nanjing's housing prices?

[Price Plunge] Three more developments offer fixed-price units

The super-large Tianruncheng development, which shocked with its fixed-price offer of 4088 yuan/square meter, has 350 special-priced units distributed across the development. These 350 special-priced units account for 16% of the total available units for sale.

Interestingly, two other developments in the same Qiaobei area of the Jiangbei sector also adopted fixed pricing: Venetian Water City offered 150 units at a uniform price of 4480 yuan/square meter, while Tianhua Lvgu offered 50 units at a uniform price of 4188 yuan/square meter. However, it was learned that these two developments have strict restrictions on the floors of the units, such as targeting only the first and top floors or specific floors like the first to sixth floors of high-rise buildings and the first and sixth floors of low-rise buildings.

In total, the 550 special-priced units represent what proportion of the available units for sale? According to data from Nanjing Online Real Estate, these three developments currently have a total of 3360 available units, meaning the 550 special-priced units account for more than 16% of the total available units.

An industry insider stated that for a major player like Suning in Qiaobei, 550 units spread across three developments is just a drop in the bucket. However, considering the current total number of available units, 16% is still a significant proportion.

Sold to specific groups? Sales offices say anyone can buy

Interestingly, Suning Properties' official stance yesterday was unified: the 550 units from the three developments were specially selected to express social gratitude and care, intended for provincial and municipal model workers, advanced workers, military personnel, and others who have contributed to society.

However, when reporters asked the sales staff of these three developments yesterday, they all said there were no such restrictions, and buyers could purchase normally.

Why is the developer being so secretive? An industry insider speculated that the developer might be concerned that previous buyers who feel they bought at a loss might demand refunds or returns.

[Public Reaction] Both those who want to buy and those who don't have reasons

Citizen Mr. Shi expressed that if the fixed-price discounts are only for the first and top floors, it doesn't mean much. However, he noticed that some fixed-price offers aren't exclusively for the first and top floors; for instance, development C offers fixed pricing for the first to sixth floors of high-rises, selling fourth and fifth floor units at 4480 yuan/square meter, which is worth considering.

Nevertheless, Mr. Shi also believed that compared to the prices he had been following, these fixed-price units are several hundred yuan cheaper per square meter, and with hundreds of units involved, the scope of the price cut is quite broad. He decided to visit the developments over the weekend.

Other citizens, however, are indifferent to the price drops in Jiangbei developments. Ms. Lu said that during her recent bus trip to view properties in Jiangbei, she was stuck in traffic on the bridge for over two hours. By the time she reached the development site, she had lost all enthusiasm. She feels that living there with such traffic conditions would be too troublesome. Despite the significant price reduction, she remains cautious and will not consider buying in Jiangbei for now.

[Industry Analysis] The dream of Qiaobei housing prices reaching five thousand shattered

Developments in Jiangbei last year aimed to break the five-thousand-yuan barrier, but now it seems they've returned to their original state. Shi Dong, Vice President of Nanjing Wangshang Real Estate Research Institution, analyzed that from an average transaction price of less than 3500 yuan/square meter in February last year to struggling to maintain at 4500 yuan/square meter now, breaking through 5000 yuan/square meter has clearly failed.

This can also be seen from the comparison of sales volumes in the same period: in February this year, the sales volume of developments in Pukou dropped by 74% month-on-month, putting pressure on developers.

According to forecasts by Wangshang Real Estate Research Institution, this year, Jiangbei will see the new listing of 3.313 million square meters of commercial residential properties (slightly higher than the 3.21 million square meters in 2007), including 2.865 million square meters of newly approved listings, ranking first among all sectors in Nanjing. This year, the additional supply of residential properties from the 45 developments on sale in the Jiangbei sector will reach 2.8 million square meters, and the concentrated, large-scale supply of commercial residential properties will inevitably make the competition in the Qiaobei real estate market even fiercer.
