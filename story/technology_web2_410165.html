I often think about what causes my daily worries. Patients who need pillow massage are mostly urban white-collar workers who have to work with their heads down for a long time. After much deliberation, I have found the correct answer to my own worries - I do not know what my future direction will be, and I am uncertain about how my future will unfold.

I often believe that the reason why a person worries about the future is due to either not knowing what state or position they will be in the future, or because of this uncertainty, they also do not know how to act in their daily lives. However, reality often forces us to clarify some future states or positions. Another reason is naturally knowing what the future will bring but not knowing how to achieve it.

Reality often pressures us to quickly provide answers, but we often find it difficult to obtain these answers or the steps to realize them. For example, as we are about to graduate, reality demands that we quickly decide on our future jobs, where we will work, and what direction we will develop towards. However, it is often difficult for us to truly obtain or make decisions that satisfy us when graduation approaches.

Why do people worry about their daily lives? Issues related to personal development largely contribute to this concern, leading to anxiety and worry in daily life. Reality pressures us, yet under this pressure, it is hard for us to get clear answers.

Sometimes, I often ponder whether each day of our lives is an independent unit, seemingly disconnected from yesterday's actions. But are they really disconnected? I think it's impossible since today is a continuation of yesterday. Naturally, what was done yesterday forms the foundation for today's actions. Unfortunately, I seem to gradually lose this awareness, feeling like I only exist in the present, without living for the future or reflecting on the past. This contrasts sharply with the mindset I had when writing the blog post "We Live Both in the Present and in the Future!"

Why am I like this? The reason lies in not having truly found my direction. Although I do have a development plan now, this plan itself does not excite me enough to take action consistently. Without motivation, it leads to procrastination (three days of fishing, two days of drying nets), making me feel lost and changeable due to an unfixed goal.

Because the goal is unclear, we often change ourselves. Sometimes, I find it silly that success is actually quite simple - make a plan and implement it well. But instead, we often dwell on goals and steps (especially the goal). Is this goal something I like? Can I achieve it? What will achieving this goal bring me? Is this goal suitable for me? Does it align with my life?... Even if you set a goal, you might still think: How can I achieve this goal? Is what I'm doing today right? Will what I do tomorrow help? What if I fail?...

Sometimes, I feel that our troubles and worries about the future are self-imposed. Since we don't know what will happen, why not make a plan to know what will happen? If we don't know how to achieve it, why not do something within our capabilities? It's regrettable that we often create obstacles for our minds and actions. Isn't that too sad?
