This autumn has an extra sadness, and more with a touch of eeriness. Lu Ling was blankly staring out the window, not realizing that the lights around the campus had already gone out. Only then did Lu Ling move her heavy body towards the bedside. These past two days, she was indeed exhausted, even fearing the nightfall. She quietly lay on the bed, eyes wide open, staring at the ceiling. The dormitory was deathly quiet, her roommates were all asleep, perhaps already in sweet dreams.

Chapter One: A Strange Dream

Dreams, at the thought of this, Lu Ling couldn't help but shiver. In the past, Lu Ling loved dreaming, welcoming any scene that came her way. But ever since that day when she attended the school reunion, every night as soon as she fell asleep, she would have a strange dream. She found herself walking in a damp and dark tunnel, surrounded by pitch blackness, unable to see her hand in front of her face. Lu Ling kept walking downwards, but it only got darker and darker without an end in sight. Fear started creeping into Lu Ling's heart. Suddenly, from afar, came the sound of high heels, getting closer and closer. This made Lu Ling, who was already scared, even more terrified. She began to panic and ran frantically forward, afraid that the sound of the high heels would catch up with her. So she ran recklessly into the abyss of darkness, yet no matter how far she ran, the sound echoed in her ears...

After an unknown amount of time, she was awakened by a cacophony of sounds. Slowly opening her eyes, she felt her whole body ache, her sweat soaking through her clothes as if she had just finished exercising.

Lan saw her condition and lovingly asked, "Lu Ling, are you alright? Are you sick? You always wake up earlier than us, but today we woke up and you didn’t move, so I came to check on you. When I saw you drenched in sweat, I woke you up."

"Big Sister Lan, thank you for your concern, I'm fine," Lu Ling said weakly.

Lan still wasn't completely reassured after hearing this. "Then rest for the day, I'll take leave for you."

Lu Ling nodded feebly.

The three girls in the dormitory all went out. They had always gotten along well. Yao Meng was someone Lu Ling met on her first day at this university while on the road, unexpectedly they ended up in the same department and became roommates. Yao Meng was not only pretty but also a rare talent. The other one was Ying Xue, don't be deceived by her beautiful name, she was rather cold, and although they didn't know each other before, they had become almost good friends now.

Lu Ling thought about how full her life had been in this half semester at university, which was enough to make her forget the unhappy memories of the past. But now, she was haunted by a nightmare, making her lose her luster, appearing much older, and emitting an aura of death.

At this moment, the mobile phone on the bedside table rang, snapping Lu Ling back from her thoughts. She was startled as if anything that made a sound had become sensitive to her. Slowly picking up the phone, she saw it was Xiao Li calling. She sighed in relief and pressed the answer button.

"Hello, Xiao Li, is there something?"

"Hehe, Lu Ling, I didn't see you at school today. Is everything okay?"

"Oh, I'm fine, just a bit tired and want to rest for a day."

"Oh, is it serious? If not, I can come pick you up and take you to see a doctor," Xiao Li asked anxiously.

"No, no, it's not serious. I'll rest for a bit and I'll be fine."

"Oh, okay then. Let me know if there's anything, rest well, I won't disturb you anymore."

"Alright."

Hanging up the phone and looking at the time, it was still early. Lu Ling decided to take a nap during the day since she hadn't slept well for several nights...

Time passed quickly, Lu Ling didn't know how long she had slept. She vaguely heard the sound of high heels. Suddenly, Lu Ling opened her eyes wide, it was already dark around her. She slowly sat up in bed, wondering if she was dreaming again. But this was clearly her own dormitory, could it be that she slept all day? Impossible, Big Sister Lan and the others haven't returned yet. Even if they did, they would have called her to eat together. What's going on here? As Lu Ling was guessing wildly, the door handle turned, it was them returning. Lu Ling felt a wave of joy, preparing to open the door, but suddenly realized she couldn't move at all, her brain no longer under her control. Lu Ling started to panic, but the footsteps outside were getting closer and closer, they were high heels! Beads of sweat the size of beans rolled down Lu Ling's forehead, over her nose tip, her eyes fixed on the door.

Some fragile things that are hard to express, will slip away in lonely nights, there are too many words that can only be said to oneself,... The ringing of the mobile phone continued, breaking the eerie night sky. Lu Ling found that her hands could move, quickly grabbing the phone on the bedside table and checking it. It was a number she didn't recognize, but she mustered up the courage to press the answer button.

Lu Ling asked tremblingly, "Hello, may I ask who you're looking for?"

"..." There was silence on the other end of the phone, no one answered her. Lu Ling became even more frightened, tightly gripping the blanket with one hand, like holding onto a lifeline. Lu Ling shouted, "Who are you? What do you want? No matter if you're human or ghost, I will never surrender!" There was still no response from the other end of the phone, ignoring a heart-wrenching laugh, shaking Lu Ling's ears as if they were about to burst. Instinctively, Lu Ling hung up the call, but it continued ringing, getting softer and softer, making her feel even more chilled, as if her laughter carried crying, making it hard to listen. Lu Ling threw the phone on the ground, wrapping herself in the blanket to block out the laughter. The phone broke into pieces upon impact, and the sound disappeared.

At this moment, Luo Lan and the others who had just finished class walked outside the door and heard the noise inside, rushing in immediately. They saw the mess inside, the phone lying on the ground, Yao Meng picking up the broken pieces, Ying Xue standing by the door with her arms crossed, coldly watching everything. Lan rushed over to support the trembling Lu Ling and asked worriedly, "What happened to you, Lu Ling? Why are you like this? Tell us what's wrong, okay?"

Upon hearing Lan's voice, Lu Ling calmed down somewhat, starting to look at Lan with blurry eyes, "How could this happen, how could this happen?" Lu Ling's originally calm heart was now racing, unable to distinguish between dreams and reality, fainting in a flash of darkness.

Lan and the others immediately took Lu Ling to the hospital. After coming out, Lan softly asked the doctor, "Doctor Li, please tell me what's wrong with Lu Ling?"

Doctor Li hesitated, "She fainted due to excessive shock, leading to weakened brain nerves and mental breakdown. There's nothing serious, but the patient cannot remain in this state for long, otherwise, she might develop depression."

What? The three people looked at each other in shock, unable to believe their ears. How could this be? Lan recalled Lu Ling's strange behavior, which was indeed a bit frightening. However, no matter what, they needed to wake Lu Ling up and ask her what happened.

When the three saw the doctor off and prepared to go in and see Lu Ling, a hurried man's voice came from afar, "Nurse, which ward is Lu Ling in?" Yao Meng recognized the voice, "Ah," Yao Meng excitedly exclaimed, everyone's attention turning to her, Xiao Li included. He peeked around the corner and saw the little girl Yao Meng, "Little Yao Meng, what are you shouting for? Where's Lu Ling?"

"I didn't mean to, why are you shouting?" Yao Meng complained innocently.

Lan called Xiao Li into the ward, Xiao Li seeing Lu Ling who was still unconscious, her complexion as pale as ashes, her lips slightly dry, his face filled with pain.

Yao Meng seeing Xiao Li's tender affection for Lu Ling, felt a pang in her heart, but she could only watch helplessly. Since she met Lu Ling, she often saw this boy visiting her, thinking he was her boyfriend. Later, she learned that they were very good friends, only then did Yao Meng start to like him. But because Yao Meng was a proud and beautiful girl, no matter what, she wouldn't confess her feelings.

Chapter Two: Entering the Dream

Lan looked at the time, it was already past six o'clock. She decided to stay behind and accompany Lu Ling, so that when Lu Ling woke up, she could take care of her. She patted Xiao Li on the shoulder and said, "Don't worry, I'll take care of her, go ahead with your business." Xiao Li looked at Lan and said, "Alright, if she wakes up, be sure to let me know. I'll go handle some matters, and when I'm done, I'll relieve you." Lan nodded, Ying Xue turned and walked out the door, Yao Meng followed suit.

Lan went out to eat some food, bought some fruits, and quietly sat by Lu Ling's bedside. Looking at this playful girl who called her Big Sister Lan as soon as she entered university, she was now finally calm. If it weren't for this big illness, she wouldn't have caught up with Lu Ling and these deadbeats... Lan thought more and more until she couldn't help but fall asleep. A cold breeze brushed past, waking Lan up. She looked out the window, the night was strangely dark, making her shiver. Lan walked towards the window and opened it a crack. She felt strange, remembering that the window had always been closed, how could it open on its own? Maybe she remembered wrong. Just as she closed the window, she smelled the scent of cherry blossoms drifting in from somewhere. Lan was even more surprised, she didn't expect there to be cherry blossom trees in the hospital in autumn, she decided to take a look tomorrow...

Ding Ding Ding... A cell phone ringtone sounded, Lan didn't expect to receive a message at this hour. Picking it up, she saw an unfamiliar number. Opening it, a video popped up. The video was pitch black, showing nothing, but she could feel the screen moving. Suddenly, from the phone came the sound of high heels, getting louder and louder, as if right next to her ear. At this moment, a person appeared on the phone. Lan stared intently at the phone screen, ah, wasn't that herself? How could this be? She instinctively glanced around, everything was dark, not knowing when the lights went out, leaving only the pale light emitted by the phone. Suddenly, from the hospital corridor came the 'thud thud thud' sound of high heels, gradually getting closer. Lan's heart raced to her throat, wanting to scream but unable to utter a sound. Cold sweat streamed down her cheeks, soaking her collar. Her fearful eyes stared at the phone, the black screen began to ooze crimson liquid, spreading gradually. Lan reflexively threw the phone away, producing a loud 'bang'.

Xiao Li arrived at this moment, hearing the commotion coming from Lu Ling's ward, he rushed over. When he pushed the door open, the light bulb emitted a dim glow, revealing the shattered phone on the messy floor.

Seeing Xiao Li enter, Lan sat down weakly, her legs powerless, her head buried in her hands, trembling uncontrollably.

Xiao Li glanced at Lu Ling sleeping peacefully, putting his mind at ease. He squatted down and asked Lan, "Lan, what's wrong? What happened?"

Lan lifted her terrified eyes and after a long pause, said one word, "Ghost."

Xiao Li felt a chill run down his spine at her words, thinking about the late hours in the hospital.

After comforting her a few times, Xiao Li went to get some sleeping pills from the doctor, letting her take them and sleep. Xiao Li looked at his watch, it was 12:30, having been up all night, his eyes were already too heavy to keep open.

Chapter Three: Disappearance of Lan

Hehe, this is my first attempt, it might not be good, please give me lots of advice. Please point out the flaws, thank you.

The next morning, Lan woke up at ten o'clock. She felt a slight headache, rubbing her temples. Suddenly, she remembered everything that happened last night, causing her fear to rise again. Perhaps she was too tired, having stayed up late for days to pass the final exams. Now, with Lu Ling being ill, it made her even more anxious. She found it ironic.

At this moment, Xiao Li also woke up, noticing her lost in thought, he asked, "Lan, are you alright?"

Lan just noticed him then, smiling and shaking her head.

"Well, I'll stay here and watch over her, you should go home and rest. I'll let you know when she wakes up."

"Alright, Xiao Li, thanks for your trouble," Lan said as she got up to tidy up.

Lan returned to school around noon, just in time to meet Yao Meng and Ying Xue finishing their classes. They ate lunch together. Ying Xue asked, "Lan, has Lu Ling woken up?"

Lan was initially shocked by this usually cool person asking actively, then slowly shook her head. Ying Xue was also skeptical. Yao Meng interjected, "It couldn't possibly be supernatural, could it?"

This comment sent a chill down Lan's spine, though it was autumn, the sun was still shining brightly. Ying Xue gave her a disdainful glance, and Yao Meng realized her mistake and shut up.

After lunch, Lan went to the library to read books, while the other two went back to catch up on sleep. The library wasn't too crowded. Lan spent a long time selecting books from the shelves, picking up several history books and classics. Suddenly, her gaze landed on a book titled 'Disappearing Yesterday' in the corner of the shelf, making her melancholy. She remembered when she was 18 years old, she and her sister had always gotten along well. It was because she saw this book on her sister's desk and was curious to read it. But just as she picked it up, her sister saw it and unexpectedly got angry and scolded her. Lan felt very wronged. Her father happened to see this and reprimanded her sister a few words. Her sister got angry and said some hurtful things, and her father hit her with a slap. Her sister ran out and never came back.

She wondered how her sister was doing now. Lan didn't want to think about those painful memories anymore, now she just wanted to study well and find her in the future.

So, she found a seat and quietly flipped through the books. She didn't know how much time had passed when Lan felt her eyes getting tired and looked up. Fewer and fewer people were around, and it was only 9:00, yet the library was unusually empty. She thought about staying a little longer, but her stomach started growling, so she had no choice but to head back. She stood up and walked out, outside drizzling lightly, not too heavy, the drops lightly hitting her face with a chill. Wrapping herself tighter in her thin coat, she trudged heavily back to school.

Returning to the dormitory, it was roughly 10:30, and her roommates were all asleep. Quietly climbing into bed, waves of fatigue washed over her. She didn't even bother to check her wet hair and changed clothes before diving into the icy quilt. But it was still better than being outside.

Lan vaguely saw herself standing in endless darkness, seeing nothing around her. Feeling empty in the vast night, she was the only one, seeming so small and helpless. Lan was a bit afraid, tightly hugging herself. She lacked the courage to reach into the boundless abyss to search for anything else. Moving her feet, she felt the ground was very wet. Wearing a pair of canvas shoes, she slightly felt her shoes being soaked by some liquid and slowly rising. Lan only felt a strong smell of blood mixed with decay assaulting her nostrils, making her feel nauseous, even unable to breathe. Instinctively, Lan jumped once, the liquid making a muffled sound splashing on her body and hands. She didn't know where her courage came from as she put her hand to her nose, it was that smell. Where am I? No, I don't want to die here. "Help!" This voice was so soft that even she couldn't hear it. What to do? Lan panicked, the liquid seemed to have no end and was crazily rising, as if it was going to swallow her alive. Lan could only watch helplessly as she was submerged in this disgusting thing. When the liquid reached her neck, Lan felt unbearable discomfort, as if there were thousands of hands below trying to tear her body apart. Lan was even more terrified, struggling to breathe. Just as she was about to suffocate, a shrill bell woke her up from her dream. She sat up in bed, gasping for breath, sweat soaking through her clothes, sticking thinly to her body. Realizing it was just a terrible dream, Lan sighed deeply and grabbed her phone.

Thank goodness the bell rang, I wonder who would send me a message at this hour of the night, but it definitely saved my life, I must thank her properly. Thinking this, Lan opened her phone, recognizing a familiar number but seeing only a large blood-red 'die' character. Lan was so scared she dropped her phone and cried loudly, seemingly no one heard her cries as everyone was sound asleep. Lan wiped her red tear-streaked eyes, finding the surroundings eerily quiet. She looked towards Ying Xue, wanting to wake her up for comfort.
