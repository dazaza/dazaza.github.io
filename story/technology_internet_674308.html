Jiangxi Yongxiu County issues a red-headed document to solicit votes for officials' awards

by king2z9o on 2012-02-24 11:40:43

On July 27, 2011, the Publicity Department of the Yongxiu County Committee in Jiangxi Province issued an "Urgent Notice" to organize votes for Xiong Maodong, the Education Bureau Director participating in the second National Education Reform and Innovation Award.

Recently, a famous column on the Hunan Rednet website titled [People's Voices] featured a post titled "The Director of the Education Bureau in Dongkou County spent more than 500,000 yuan of public funds to buy personal reputation!" The whistleblower claimed:

"The Director of the Education Bureau in Dongkou County, in order to fraudulently obtain the 'National Education Reform and Innovation Award', held multiple meetings and issued documents forcing over 6,000 teachers and staff in the county to each cast at least 30 votes online for him. Each vote came with a private subsidy ranging from 2 to 5 yuan. As a result, an unprecedented wave of frenzied and ridiculous online voting for Zeng XXZeng emerged within the education front of Dongkou County. Teachers collected all available ID cards from their relatives and friends to vote for Zeng XXZeng! They jokingly asked each other upon meeting: 'How many votes have you cast for Director Zeng?'"

Not only in Dongkou County, Hunan Province, but also according to investigations by the China Youth Daily, it was discovered that during the process of the National Education Reform and Innovation Award - an unofficial award organized by media outlets - there were multiple instances of candidates or candidate organizations organizing votes. In some cases, local governments even issued official documents to enforce voting quotas, which were sometimes included in unit evaluations.

Red-headed Document Initiating Voting

[Currently, Xiong Maodong ranks third in our county. Please give high priority to this matter and assign dedicated personnel to organize voting in the next four days. Among them, online voting requires no less than 200 votes per township and no less than 20 votes per county-level unit.]

On July 27, 2011, the Publicity Department of the Yongxiu County Committee in Jiangxi Province issued an official document titled "Urgent Notice on Organizing Votes for Candidate Xiong Maodong for the National Education Reform and Innovation Management Award," making the aforementioned request. After interviewing the Publicity Department of the Yongxiu County Committee, this official document disappeared from the Yongxiu County Government website.

Xiong Maodong is the Director of the Education Bureau in Yongxiu County, Jiangxi Province, and he participated in the second National Education Reform and Innovation Award.

According to the China Education News Network, the National Education Reform and Innovation Award evaluation activity is a biennial large-scale evaluation activity jointly hosted by the China Education Newspaper and the China Education News Network. Its aim is "to vigorously promote the spirit of innovation and pioneering times, and widely showcase the resolute reform and daring innovation of the times."

The second National Education Reform and Innovation Award evaluation activity started in May 2011. According to the introduction, this session set up four awards: Education Reform and Innovation Award, Education Reform and Innovation Management Award, Education Reform and Innovation Principal Award, and Education Reform and Innovation Teacher Award, with excellent awards for each of the four categories. The organizers stated: "The most important condition for participation is the implementation of innovative practices and achieving good results, generating widespread influence in the education sector and society."

Xiong Maodong's application materials showed that his innovative achievements lay in promoting quality education and focusing on teaching model innovations, etc., and "for many years, there has not been a single campus safety liability incident across the county."

On July 21, 2011, after going through the selection phase, the award entered the voting stage. It was reported that the organizers selected 210 candidate objects from 1,205 applicants and individuals, including 60 candidate units for the Innovation Practice Award, 30 candidates for the Innovation Management Award, 80 candidates for the Innovation Principal Award, and 40 candidates for the Innovation Teacher Award.

The organizers provided three voting methods: online voting, SMS voting, and mail-in voting. Online voting required filling in an ID number; SMS voting cost 1 yuan per message with a limit of 20 votes per number per day. The voting deadline was August 21, 2011.

On July 27, 2011, the urgent red-headed document requesting votes for Xiong Maodong was sent to various townships (towns, fields, enterprise groups) party committees, all departments of the county committee, and party organizations of county-level and resident units in Yongxiu County. The document stated, "Our county's Education Bureau Director Xiong Maodong, after being recommended by the Provincial Education Department and announced by the organizing committee, successfully made it to the list, becoming the sole candidate for the Innovative Educational Management Award in Jiujiang City."

This notice was also posted on the [Yongxiu People Forum]. It triggered heated discussions among netizens. Netizen [A Ben] said, "Fake ID cards can be used too. Anyway, each ID number can vote once a day. I estimate I've voted no less than 300 times. Among them, I made up a lot of ID numbers and names." [A Ben] also wrote: "Later, I used all the teachers' IDs at the school. Director Xiong led for only a few days, then fell behind others. He was ranked second yesterday, but today he is sixth."

However, some netizens commented on this post saying, "Yes, I said it would be easy to get votes this way." "I wonder which department 'urgently' initiated this?" "It might backfire if announced online like this!"

Data from the China Education News Network showed that Xiong Maodong eventually ranked fourth with over 1.12 million votes. Data from the Yongxiu County Government website showed that the total population of Yongxiu County was 380,000.

Teachers Voting Allegedly Eligible for Subsidies

In the famous [People's Voices] column on Rednet, several follow-up posts appeared under the thread "The Director of the Education Bureau in Dongkou County used over 500,000 yuan of public funds to buy personal reputation!"

One follow-up post read: "From July to August last year, the principal of each school informed every teacher that they must vote for the director every day! The phone costs incurred will be reimbursed by each school in the second semester of last year! Each teacher received one hundred yuan!" Another follow-up post read: "Our principal assigned the voting as a task at the meeting, and all teachers who sent text messages received money."

But another follow-up post read: "With so many units under the Education Bureau, it cannot be ruled out that some regular directors or arrangements were made, and phone information fees were reimbursed, but it should be a minority. Our school did not reimburse such expenses."

Understanding revealed that the Director of the Education Bureau in Dongkou County, Hunan Province, is named Zeng Guangzeng.

An article seen on the Dongkou County Education Bureau website showed that on the morning of August 13, 2011, the Youlan Town Central School convened a town-wide primary and secondary school duty meeting, carefully arranging the Second National Education Reform and Innovation Management Award evaluation activity. The meeting required each school to organize teachers to carefully read relevant materials, "and provided a detailed introduction to the advanced deeds of Director Zeng Guangzeng of the Dongkou County Education Bureau, the only person in our province to obtain the qualification for this award. It required all faculty members to vote for the excellent candidate in their minds based on specific circumstances. At the meeting, everyone expressed: We must implement the spirit of the higher-level documents, carefully organize all personnel in the school to actively vote through SMS, network, and mailing methods, and vote for the candidate we are satisfied with."

An article titled "Our County Teachers Busy Voting for the Education Director" on the Dongkou Education Network stated, "Teacher Da from Youlan Central Primary School, in order to use his home computer to cast more votes for Director Zeng, asked for the ID numbers of all family members and collected a large amount of colleagues' names, ID information, using one-person operation to vote for multiple people method, managing to cast dozens of votes every day. Teacher Wang Aiqun from Shijiang Central Primary School, while traveling in Yunnan, did not forget to vote. Unable to conveniently use online voting, she cast more than twenty votes for Director Zeng via mobile phone text messages."

Data from the China Education News Network showed that Zeng Guangzeng ultimately received over 1.13 million votes, ranking third among the 30 candidates.

Besides the above situation, searching through the list of candidates and candidate units revealed that calling for votes was far from uncommon. For example, on July 25, 2011, the Wujin District Education Bureau of Changzhou City, Jiangsu Province, issued a notice requiring "organizing all teachers to participate in the voting activity and mobilizing the general public through various means. Schools should report the total number of votes to the district education bureau office after August 22." This bureau participated in the Education Reform and Innovation Award evaluation.

On August 10, 2011, the Education Supervision Office of Sanyuan County People's Government in Shaanxi Province issued a notice requiring "after the voting ends on August 21, each school must report the number of voters to the county education supervision office, which will be used as an important indicator for annual task evaluation."

Among the notices from various places, the China Youth Daily only saw the Putuo District Education Bureau Office in Zhoushan City, Zhejiang Province indicating "voluntary principle, organizing learning, publicity, and evaluation voting activities." The bureau ultimately only received 521 votes, but among the candidate units for the Education Reform and Innovation Award, the People's Government of Wulian County, Shandong Province ranked tenth with 279,000 votes.

Calls were made successively to units such as the Wujin District Education Bureau, but the responsible personnel all claimed "not clear" or "do not know" about the matter.
