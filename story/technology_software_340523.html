Gathering to obstruct the construction of Caotang Base in the Hi-Tech Zone: 23 people sentenced

by udolaw45 on 2010-04-06 10:33:09

On the afternoon of April 1, Hu County People's Court in Shaanxi Province made a public verdict on two cases involving 23 criminals who had gathered to obstruct the construction of Caotang Science and Technology Base in the Hi-Tech Zone. Their actions had severely impacted Hu County’s investment environment and peaceful development. The criminals were sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment ranging from 4 years to 6 months or detention for the crime of gathering to disrupt social order.

On October 21, 2009, defendants Lei Jianwei, Zhou Duhong, Guan Conglai, etc., in order to monopolize the earthwork project of the water supply and drainage engineering of part of the section of the No.8 South-North Road of the Caotang Base contracted by Xi'an Municipal Road and Bridge Construction Co., Ltd., gathered seven people including Chen Baobao in Xi'an and took taxis to the construction site. They surrounded the loader that was under construction by the project department, used threats and other means to force the driver to drive the loader out of the construction site, causing the project to be suspended for one day with losses amounting to about 10,000 yuan.

In August 2009, Hu County Construction Company's Road and Bridge Branch undertook the riverfront road beside the new Li Jiayan Village in Caotang Town. Defendants Zhang Ruimin and two others used the excuse that "the construction of the new Li Jiayan Village destroyed the original irrigation channel, demanding the restoration of the original irrigation channel" as a pretext. They organized people nine times to block the construction and extort money, with more than 130 participants. On August 26, the criminals forcibly dismantled the temporary workers' shed of the construction team and looted the materials for building the shed. On September 17, the Road and Bridge Branch was forced to withdraw from the construction site, resulting in direct economic losses of more than 200,000 yuan.

From the end of 2008 to March 2009, Zhang Jianchun and Zhang Ruimin from Caotangying Village in Caotang Town deliberately spread rumors, inciting dozens of villagers from Groups Seven and Eight of Caotangying Village to gather multiple times to obstruct the high-voltage power transmission line installation project undertaken by Xi'an Zhongyuan Electric Power Installation Co., Ltd. passing through their village. Despite the land compensation having been paid, they demanded higher "compensation." Due to the construction schedule, after coordination, Xi'an Zhongyuan Electric Power Installation Co., Ltd. reached an "agreement" with the four defendants including Zhang Jianchun, additionally compensating 24,000 yuan for the land occupation fee and 25 yuan per person for the misemployment fee due to blocking the construction, totaling 10,775 yuan.

Based on the facts ascertained, Hu County Court sentenced the main criminal Zhang Jianchun and Zhang Ruimin to 4 years in prison, and Lei Jianwei to 3 years in prison. The other 20 people were respectively sentenced to 2 years and 6 months in prison or detention.

After the verdict, among the 23 criminals involved in the two cases, except for two who expressed appeals, the rest all indicated acceptance of the judgment and would not appeal.

Legal provisions: [Criminal Law] Article 290, Paragraph 1: For those who gather to disrupt social order seriously, causing work, production, business operations, teaching, and scientific research to be unable to proceed, creating serious losses, the ringleaders shall be sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment of three to seven years; other active participants shall be sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment of less than three years, detention, surveillance, or deprivation of political rights.

[Definition]: The crime of gathering to disrupt social order refers to the act of gathering to disrupt the normal activities of Party and government agencies, enterprises, institutions, and mass organizations, causing serious consequences, making work, production, business operations, teaching, and scientific research impossible, thereby causing serious losses to the state and society.

[Criminal Elements]:

(1) Object Element

This crime infringes upon social order. Here, the social order referred to is not the broad general social order but rather the social order within a specific scope. Specifically, it refers to the working order of state organs and people's organizations, the production and business operation order of enterprise units, and the teaching and scientific research order of institutional units. The objects infringed upon are state organs, enterprise units, and people's organizations.

(2) Objective Element

The objective aspect of this crime is manifested in disrupting the normal activities of enterprise units and social groups through a gathering method, making their work, production, business operations, and teaching and scientific research impossible, causing serious losses.

So-called gathering refers to assembling many people to commit criminal acts, usually involving the assembly of more than three people, with key figures who play roles in organizing, planning, and commanding, active participants who actively implement criminal activities, work particularly hard, and have relatively serious circumstances, and during the process of committing crimes, there may also be misled masses, threatened general offenders, onlookers, troublemakers, and the assembly of more than three people refers to including the leaders and active participants in the number of more than three people. If one or two people cause a disturbance attracting onlookers and commotion, it does not constitute this crime. The methods used by the leaders to gather people can be diverse, such as incitement, bribery, provocation, instigation, etc. The leaders may incite and plan behind the scenes without personally implementing specific disruptive behaviors.

The main ways in which the perpetrator disrupts social order include: gathering to storm the premises of enterprise units and social groups; making loud noises and disturbances in front of or inside the premises of enterprise units and social groups; sealing off gates and passages to prevent staff from entering; surrounding, insulting, and assaulting staff members; damaging property and equipment; forcibly occupying workplaces, business premises, production sites, etc.; forcibly cutting off power and water supplies, etc. In the course of implementing this crime, if the perpetrator assaults staff members or damages public or private property constituting a crime, multiple crimes should be punished concurrently.

As long as the behavior of the perpetrator gathering to disrupt social order is serious, causing the work, production, business operations, and teaching and scientific research of enterprise units and social groups to be impossible, resulting in serious losses, it constitutes this crime. According to the first paragraph of this article, seriousness is one of the elements constituting this crime. Seriousness refers to the fact that due to the perpetrator's gathering and disruption, the normal activities of enterprise units and social groups cannot proceed, resulting in serious losses. Making work, production, business operations, and teaching and scientific research impossible and causing serious losses must occur simultaneously. The former is the direct manifestation of the social harm caused by the perpetrator's disruptive behavior, while the latter is the actual harm. Although the perpetrator's behavior makes work, production, business operations, and teaching and scientific research impossible, if no serious loss has occurred, it will not be treated as a crime, and the public security organ will handle it according to relevant provisions of the Public Security Administration Punishment Regulations. Serious losses refer to severe losses in various aspects such as tangible material and intangible intellectual achievements, social interests, and political interests. Material losses include both property damage and potential interest losses caused by the cessation of production and business operations due to criminal behavior. Potential interests should be limited to those that meet sufficient conditions and could be smoothly realized if not interfered by criminal behavior. The severity of material losses is measured by the amount of losses caused. Intangible intellectual achievements, social interests, and political interest losses refer to the inability of social organizations aimed at social and political interests and other social organizations not directly engaged in production and business activities, such as political parties, trade unions, women's federations, schools, and research institutions, to work and the resulting immeasurable losses. The severity of these losses can generally be determined by considering factors such as the means used in the disruption, the duration of the disruption, the importance of the delayed work matters due to the disruption, and whether the losses can be remedied. Generally speaking, disruptions using severe means, causing the relevant units to be paralyzed for a longer time, and delaying important social or political interest-related work matters can be considered serious. Gathering a particularly large number of people, surrounding and assaulting multiple staff members, damaging certain properties; occupying office premises, blocking passages for a considerable period of time, refusing to leave, causing relevant units to be paralyzed for a long time; due to the disruption, teaching plans cannot be completed, affecting the studies of multiple students; major scientific research projects cannot continue or result in significant losses; causing large meetings of political parties and mass organizations (such as party congresses, youth congresses, etc.) to be unable to proceed as scheduled or be interrupted; disrupting other crucial social interest-related matters (such as preventing epidemic prevention plans from being implemented) etc. Due to the perpetrator's disruption, the inability of relevant units to normally carry out work causes serious damage to third-party interests; although the damage results are not directly caused by the perpetrator, they belong to the losses caused by the perpetrator's disruption of social order to social interests and should also be considered as one of the criteria for measuring whether the perpetrator's behavior is serious. For example, due to the perpetrator's gathering to disrupt the work order of medical units, critically ill patients cannot receive timely treatment leading to death or disability, even though the perpetrator's behavior and the death or disability of the patient do not have a direct causal relationship, the perpetrator's behavior and the inability of the medical unit to conduct work have a direct causal relationship, therefore, the perpetrator's behavior and the death or disability of the critically ill patient have an indirect causal relationship and should be considered as the harmful result of the perpetrator's behavior.

(3) Subject Element

The subject of this crime is a general subject. However, not everyone who gathers to disrupt social order can constitute this crime. Only the key figures and other active participants who disrupt social order can constitute this crime. Key figures refer to criminal individuals who play roles in organizing, planning, and commanding in the crime of disrupting social order. Other active participants refer to criminal individuals who play major roles in criminal activities other than key figures. General participants can only be held administratively responsible and cannot become subjects of this crime.

(4) Subjective Element

The subjective element of this crime can only be intentional. The perpetrator often attempts to create incidents through such disruptive activities, exerting pressure on agencies, units, and groups to achieve some unreasonable demands or vent dissatisfaction. Since this crime is a collective crime, the disruptive activities must be based on the common intent of numerous perpetrators. This common intent does not require the connection between the intents of the perpetrators to be very tight. As long as the perpetrators clearly understand that they and others are engaging in activities that disrupt the work order of national agencies, enterprises, institutions, and people's groups, it suffices, and it is not necessary for the criminal purposes or motives of each perpetrator to be exactly the same.

From the perspective of the consequences of the disruption, if the agency, group, or unit's work suffers serious consequences and creates a bad influence, it is considered serious. From the standpoint of the means used, violent means are more serious than non-violent means.

[How to correctly identify the crime of gathering to disrupt social order in judicial practice?]

(1) The boundary between this crime and general violations of public security management that disrupt social order.

Both may appear in the same form, disrupting the order of state agencies, groups, and institutions, causing work, production, business, teaching, and scientific research to be unable to proceed normally. The main difference is whether the situation is serious and whether it causes serious losses to the state and society. If it has not yet caused serious losses, it is a general violation of public security management and should be punished accordingly.

Due to bureaucratic leadership, improper handling of issues involving the interests of the masses, or shortcomings and mistakes in work, it leads to mass disturbances, student unrest, or strikes, requiring deep and meticulous ideological and political work. It should be distinguished from the crime of gathering to disrupt social order. For those who take advantage of student unrest or strikes, deliberately distort the party's policies, incite the masses, make unreasonable demands, disrupt the normal social order, and meet the provisions of this article, they constitute this crime.

(2) The boundary between this crime and the crime of hindering official duties.

(a) The former targets state agencies, enterprises, institutions, and people's groups; the latter targets specific state functionaries.

(b) The former involves gathering; the latter can involve a single individual.

(c) The former is not limited to using violence or threats; the latter uses violence or threats.

(3) The boundary between this crime and the crime of gathering to assault state agencies.

Gathering to assault state agencies is originally one type of disrupting social order. Given the importance of maintaining the normal activities of state agencies for social stability, the law separately defines it as a crime. The criminal object of the two crimes is different. The object of this crime is the normal activity order of enterprises, institutions, and social groups. The object of the crime of gathering to assault state agencies is the normal activities of state agencies; the criminal object of the two crimes is different. The object of the crime of gathering to assault state agencies is limited to various levels and types of state agencies, while the object of this crime is state agencies outside of other enterprises, institutions, and social groups.

(4) The boundary between this crime and the crimes of gathering to disrupt public place order and traffic order.

This crime and the above two crimes have very similar subjects and objective aspects. The main difference is that the above two crimes occur in places such as stations, docks, civil aviation stations, malls, parks, etc., destroying public place order; this crime occurs at the locations of enterprises, institutions, and social groups, destroying the work, production, teaching, and scientific research orders of these units. The perpetrators of the above two crimes must have the circumstance of resisting and obstructing national public security management personnel from performing their duties according to law, and the circumstances are serious, while this crime does not need to have this. In practice, since some enterprises, institutions, and social groups are located in or near stations, docks, civil aviation stations, parks, and other public places, when the perpetrators gather to disrupt social order, it may lead to the consequence of public place order being disrupted or traffic order being disrupted; it may also happen that when the perpetrators gather to commit the above two crimes, it leads to these enterprises, institutions, and social groups being unable to work, causing serious losses. In practice, distinctions can be made starting from the purpose of the crime. Generally speaking, the purpose of the perpetrators of this crime is directly targeted at specific enterprises, institutions, and social groups, while the perpetrators of the above two crimes do not aim to disrupt the work order of specific units. For the former case, this crime should be applied, and the consequence of causing public place order and traffic order chaos should be considered as one of the factors to measure whether the circumstances are serious. For the latter case, if the perpetrator subjectively constitutes indirect intent and objectively causes serious losses, it should be handled as an absorption crime and this crime should be applied; if the perpetrator subjectively belongs to negligence regarding the consequence of causing enterprises, institutions, and social groups to be unable to work and causing serious losses, it does not constitute this crime, but this harmful consequence should be considered as a factor in sentencing.
