IDC: Android reaches new milestones, WP strives to catch up, BlackBerry struggles to reverse its fortunes

by anonymous on 2013-11-17 19:46:00

From a market share perspective, Android's dominant position has become even more solid.

According to the latest report from IDC, in Q3 of this year, global shipments of Android smartphones reached as high as 210 million units, with its market share breaking through 80% for the first time, reaching a new milestone of 81%. It is also worth noting that 39.9% of Android smartphone shipments came from Samsung, while other manufacturers' figures were all single-digit percentages.

WP (Windows Phone) also delivered an impressive performance, with smartphone shipments reaching 9.5 million units in Q3, up by 156% compared to 3.7 million units last year during the same period, increasing its market share to 3.6%.

IDC’s research manager of the Mobile Phone team, Ramon Llamas, mentioned that the outstanding performances of Android and WP (despite the significant difference in their shares) can be attributed to one key factor—price. Both platforms have provided sufficiently affordable products for mass consumers.

**Android**

Many Android manufacturers have introduced a large number of low-cost devices to the market to boost their market share, a strategy particularly common in emerging markets. Huawei and Lenovo from China had smartphone shipments of 12.5 million units and 12.3 million units respectively in Q3, ranking third and fourth globally. The low-cost smartphones from these two companies remain a crucial driving force behind their remarkable achievements.

The strong sales of low-cost Android smartphones have also helped China become one of the fastest-growing markets for global smartphones. The Chinese market accounted for more than one-third of total shipments in Q3.

**WP**

On the WP side, Lumia smartphone sales reached 8.8 million units in Q3, making Nokia the undisputed leader in the WP camp. However, it should be noted that this success was largely due to the success of the low-end model, the Lumia 520. Therefore, the strong sales of low-cost models directly led to the sharp increase in overall WP shipments.

**iOS**

Apple's iOS phone market share continued to decline, with a share of 12.9% in Q3, lower than 14.4% last year and 13.2% in the previous quarter. The direct cause was that the growth rate of iOS phone shipments was lower than the overall market performance.

In September this year, Apple launched the iPhone 5s and iPhone 5c. While the former has remained popular, the performance of the iPhone 5c has been less satisfactory. Moreover, due to its unexpectedly high price, the iPhone 5c failed to take on the responsibility of expanding its share in emerging markets.

**BlackBerry**

BlackBerry smartphones continue to show signs of decline, with shipments falling by 41.6% year-on-year in Q3, and its market share dropping to 1.7%, with BB10 being partly responsible.

BlackBerry has faced numerous negative news this year—layoffs, sluggish sales, falling stock prices, and plans for sale. However, the company ultimately did not sell itself but instead chose bond financing and replaced its leadership. It will be interesting to see where new CEO John Chen will lead the company.

**Average Selling Price of Smartphones**

IDC introduced changes in the average selling price of smartphones. In Q3 this year, the average selling price of smartphones fell to $317, a year-on-year decrease of 12.5%.

At the same time, there are increasingly more phablets (5 to 7 inches) in the market. Compared to small-screen phones, phablets usually require stronger batteries and other costlier components, so their prices are generally higher than those of small-screen phones. The average selling price of phablets in Q3 was as high as $443, far exceeding the overall market average of $317, but it fell by 22.8% compared to $573 last year.

Ryan Reith, project director at IDC, said: "Almost every successful Android manufacturer has launched phablets, and Nokia has also recently released such products (Lumia 1320, Lumia 1520). In Q3, phablet shipments already accounted for 21% of the smartphone market, whereas this figure was only 3% last year during the same period." In Reith's view, the absence in the phablet market might be an important reason why Apple failed to increase its market share.

Recently, Bloomberg cited insider information reporting that Apple plans to release two iPhones with curved screens next year, with screen sizes of 4.7 inches and 5.5 inches respectively. Many readers believe that it is highly likely that Apple will launch phablets next year, while the possibility of launching curved iPhones is slim.
