Summary of Key News This Week (2011/09/03)

by xdash on 2011-09-23 11:32:28

Overview: This week, 360 and China Mobile successively launched their own cross-platform Kik-like messaging products. Tencent's WeChat for Android added support for cloud-based contact backup. The giant Baidu's cloud service, which has been in preparation for nearly a year, is also about to go online. The overall trend is that cross-platform synchronization applications are increasingly being valued by manufacturers, even reaching a strategic level, entering a white-hot stage of substantial competition. Industry Data

@Sync Control: #Data Sharing# [China's 3G Users Have Reached 87.2 Million People, Penetration Rate Less Than 10%] According to data from the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology, as of the end of July this year, China's cumulative number of mobile phone users reached 930 million accounts, of which 3G users totaled 87.199 million accounts. In July, the net increase in telephone users nationwide was 8.61 million accounts, with the total number reaching 1.219 billion accounts. Fixed-line telephone users decreased by 687,000 accounts, while mobile phone users increased by 9.297 million accounts. (Original Weibo: http://weibo.com/1668709244/xlPw9sTRf)

@Sync Control: #Industry Dynamics# [Open Source Cloud Sync Service Company Funambol Raises $3 Million Investment] According to documents from the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission, open source cloud sync service company Funambol has raised $3 million in investment from three companies: HIG, Pacven Walden, and Nexit Infocom. Funambol's last round of financing occurred in June 2008, when HIG and Walden International invested $12.5 million. http://t.cn/ausZ0F (Original Weibo: http://weibo.com/1668709244/xlTdDpCwe)
