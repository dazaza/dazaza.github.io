English 　　Resident Identity Card of the People’s Republic of China [Edit this section] One of the Origins of China's ID Cards 　　The earliest ID card system in China should be the "Resident Certificate System" established by the Ningxia Provincial Government of the Republic of China in 1936. 　　Ningxia Hui Autonomous Region (historically known as Ningxia Province), located in the northwestern border, was economically and culturally backward in its early years, but it became the birthplace of China's ID card system for historical reasons. [Edit this section] Historical Reasons 　　Ningxia Province was established on January 1, 1929, bordering Gansu, Shaanxi, Suiyuan, and Mongolia. In 1933, Ma Hongkui was appointed as the Chairman of the Ningxia Provincial Government by the central government. To strengthen his rule, Ma Hongkui began a province-wide census and established the Baojia system in 1934. In May 1936, the Central Red Army started the Western Expedition, liberating areas such as Yanchi, Yuwang, and Tongxin in Ningxia. To resist the Red Army, Ningxia developed various anti-communist and anti-Red Army strategies, one of which was issuing "Resident Certificates." Residents aged 15 and above, both male and female, were required to wear these certificates. [Edit this section] Resident Certificate —— The Earliest ID Card 　　The Ningxia "Resident Certificate" was made of white cloth, measuring 7 centimeters in length and 3 centimeters in width. It included details such as name, age, place of origin, occupation, height, appearance, characteristics, and the shape of fingerprints. The "Resident Certificate" was required to be sewn inside the upper garment for easy inspection. Although this "Resident Certificate" differed from later national ID cards, it already contained all the elements of an ID card and can be considered the earliest prototype of China's ID card system. 　　However, the implementation of the "Resident Certificate" system in Ningxia Province was not smooth. The main reason was that the cloth-based resident certificate, being sewn onto clothing, quickly became blurred due to washing and wear and tear. As a result, this system only lasted for over a year before becoming defunct. 　　In the summer of 1940, Ma Hongkui ordered the issuance of "Ningxia Province National ID Cards" based on the census and resident certificates. For this purpose, the "Ningxia Province National ID Card Issuance and Inspection Law" was also formulated. Along with the issuance of "ID Cards," "Residence Permits for Non-native Residents" and "Travel Permits" were also issued, aimed at "preventing traitors, spies, traitorous parties, and bandits." In reality, the establishment of the ID card system in Ningxia Province was mainly to blockade our Shaanxi-Gansu-Ningxia Border Region base and further strengthen control over the people of Ningxia. Since the invention of the national ID card, it became much easier for Ma Hongkui to conscript soldiers. After male laborers were drafted, their ID cards were immediately confiscated by the military. If any conscripts fled, the households providing the conscripts would be held accountable. Fugitives without ID cards would find it impossible to move around in Ningxia: either they would be captured or flee to other provinces. The implementation of the national ID card system played a special role in Ma Hongkui's governance, control of the people, prevention of desertion, and apprehension of deserters.
