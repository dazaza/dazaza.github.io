Before engaging in network marketing activities for different products and markets, it is necessary to have an early prediction of the product's market entry and effectiveness. Below is SEO Tribe's explanation of the two most critical factors for enterprises before conducting network marketing work:

1. Improvement-oriented network marketing planning

Network marketing has been carried out before, but as the internet develops rapidly and update frequencies increase, the existing network marketing mechanism can no longer satisfy public tastes, affecting multiple aspects. Negative product news directly impacts the popularity of the product in the market, the emergence of new manufacturers for the same product intensifies corporate competition in the network market, and overcrowded promotion phases in network marketing prevent the product from gaining consumer trust. All these previously implemented network marketing behaviors will become obsolete, requiring new solutions to improve the current plan. This is the premise for the appearance of improvement-oriented network marketing planning.

For improvement-oriented network marketing planning, focus on the constrained factors on the original basis, not only keeping up with the pace of the network market but also observing the various methods competitors adopt in network marketing. For issues encountered by a company during its development, it must combine with the network market, integrating the network with the market for development to secure a place in the large network market.

2. Entrepreneurship-oriented network marketing planning

Network marketing and planning are more inclined towards many entrepreneurs, thus forming what we call entrepreneurship-oriented planning. For the emergence of new things, there must be a mental preparation. Any e-commerce personnel and enterprise managers must have this entrepreneurial planning awareness.

The content that entrepreneurship-oriented planning should include: project initiation, project prediction, project pre-implementation, unforeseen factor prediction, project launch, project evaluation, project improvement, specific implementation content, finally turning to improvement-oriented planning.

In project prediction, a network market and offline market survey for the project must be conducted to ensure the smooth progress of network marketing and timely resolve any issues discovered. Among them, unforeseen factors are very important and require considering the various problems encountered by similar or other products after entering the network market, thereby ensuring the comprehensiveness of entrepreneurship-oriented planning and ensuring the success of the entire network marketing plan.

3. Auxiliary-type network marketing planning

Using various network means, such as SEO technology, to strengthen a company's network marketing efforts to achieve better results. Planning done in conjunction with various technologies and means is called auxiliary-type network marketing planning. This type of planning requires companies to coordinate cooperation between departments, optimize combinations, and enhance marketing effects in the new network market.

Basic principles of network marketing planning

1. Systematic principle

Network marketing is a systematic enterprise operation activity using the network as a tool. It manages the information flow, commerce flow, manufacturing flow, logistics, capital flow, and service flow under the network environment. Therefore, network marketing planning is a complex system engineering task. Planners must guide the integration and optimization of various elements of the company's network marketing activities, ensuring that all 'six flows' are present and complement each other.

2. Innovation principle

The network greatly facilitates customers comparing the utility and value brought by products and services from different enterprises. In the increasingly personalized consumption demand environment of network marketing, through innovation, creating product and service characteristics that match customer's personalized demands is the key to increasing utility and value. Special contributions bring special returns. Innovation brings uniqueness, which not only means being different but also represents additional value. During the process of network marketing planning, it is necessary to deeply understand the network marketing environment, especially customer needs and competitor movements, striving to create welcomed product and service characteristics that increase customer value and utility.

3. Operability principle

The first result of network marketing planning is the formation of a network marketing plan. The plan must be operable; otherwise, it is worthless. This operability manifests in the plan where planners deploy, elaborate, and arrange in detail based on the company's network marketing goals and environmental conditions regarding what, when, where, who, and how the company will act in future network marketing activities. That is to say, the network marketing plan is a series of specific, clear, direct, and interconnected action plans. Once implemented, every department and employee in the company clearly understands their objectives, tasks, responsibilities, and methods to complete tasks, knowing how to collaborate with other departments or employees.

4. Economic principle

Network marketing planning must center on economic benefits. Not only does network marketing planning consume certain resources itself, but it also changes the allocation state and utilization efficiency of the company's operating resources through the implementation of the plan. The economic benefit of network marketing planning is the ratio between the economic gains brought by the plan and the cost of planning and plan implementation. Successful network marketing planning should achieve the greatest economic gains under fixed costs for planning and plan implementation, or spend the least planning and plan implementation costs to achieve target economic gains.

5. Synergy principle

Network marketing planning should involve the application of various marketing tools rather than isolated use of methods. Resources like forums, blogs, communities, online media, etc., must be used in synergy to truly achieve the effects of network marketing.

Types of network marketing planning

Currently, network marketing planning for Chinese enterprises can roughly be divided into three levels:

1. Information application layer planning

This is the simplest and most basic level. At this level, enterprises mainly use the Internet to release information and fully utilize network advantages to communicate bidirectionally with the outside world. In this application layer, there is no high requirement for information technology from the enterprise, just basic usage. For example: communicating and exchanging with consumers via email, regularly sending various product information emails, product recommendation emails, electronic publications, etc., to strengthen contact with customers; establishing a corporate homepage and placing some introductions about the enterprise and its products and services, supplemented by beautiful graphics, for visitors to browse; accessing the Internet through dedicated data lines.

2. Tactical marketing layer planning

Enterprises mainly carry out the following tasks:

(1) Network marketing research. Using online surveys via the Internet can easily complete large amounts of complex research work, fully meeting various statistical data requirements and improving the quality of marketing research. Since it uses electronic questionnaires, it significantly reduces data input work and shortens research time.

(2) Online sales. This is one of the most attractive places in current network marketing. Thousands of enterprises set up camp online, selling a wide variety of products. In reality, these enterprises might simply consist of one computer, without factories, employees, or office buildings. They are "virtual giants" online yet so real. Online sales are separated from the physical flow processes of traditional commercial sales, representing a marketing method of the information age.

(3) Marketing tactical systems. Mainly include subsystems for managing inventory, subsystems for promoting products and linking websites, and subsystems for responding to user opinions and feedback information. Decision-makers use online analysis tools to conduct various decision-making activities.

3. Strategic marketing layer planning

This level is built on the foundation of tactical marketing, integrating the entire enterprise's marketing organization, marketing plans, and marketing concepts into the network. Relying on the network to set guidelines, develop strategic deployments, implement strategic shifts, and form strategic alliances among other strategic decisions.

Five key elements for successful network marketing planning services

1. Listening to customers

The basic starting point of network marketing planning services is to meet customer needs. One common feature of their site designs is convenience for customer use, allowing customers to directly provide feedback to the enterprise. Customers can tell the enterprise whether a product meets market demands or request specific improvements. Many enterprises find that direct customer feedback systems stimulate the best ideas from staff and promote quality enhancement. Suppliers, retailers, and customers should be important participants in the entire marketing process, forming an interactive system.

2. Gradual progress

Treat each service and product as a multi-step, gradual process rather than something achieved all at once. This requires continuous daily improvements to the site, such as changing graphics, repairing broken links, and correcting spelling errors. Starting with these small matters makes the site better and better. As a result, customers will appreciate the continuous efforts made by the enterprise.

3. Closely monitoring customer changes

One initial reason for enterprises setting up sites was to reduce phone services. But now, customers may still call regarding technical details or more challenging issues handled by the technical service department. After absorbing information from the network site, customers' demands for services have changed significantly compared to before — their knowledge base about products and information needs have greatly increased. Companies must adapt to this growing demand, continuously accumulating and enhancing their own knowledge.

4. Flexibility

Network media allow enterprises to continuously refine and expand their content, expanding step by step rather than achieving perfection all at once, providing great flexibility.

5. Emergency support plans and systems

Include the development, delivery, and training departments in the network customer service support group. If they do not know how network services are utilized, they cannot help customers through network tools. Additionally, consider the possibility of catastrophic events: if 10,000 customers use the site daily to seek help, but one day the system suddenly malfunctions, what would you do? All enterprises must ensure there is an emergency support system, supporting online database analysis systems or other problem-solving methods.
