Doctor Liu Hongchen's Profile More Doctors: Liu Hongchen, male, Chief Physician, Professor, Head of the Dentistry Department, doctoral supervisor. He is currently a member of the Academic Degree Committee of the State Council, standing director and deputy secretary-general of the Chinese Stomatological Association, vice president of the Beijing Stomatological Association, deputy director of the Military Stomatological Professional Committee, deputy director of the National Oral Restoration Professional Committee, deputy director of the Geriatric Stomatological Professional Committee, deputy director of the Temporomandibular Joint Disease and Occlusion Professional Committee, vice president of the Oral Computer Application Society, vice president of the International Dental Computer Application Society China Branch, member of the International College of Dentists, editor-in-chief of the "Chinese Journal of Geriatric Stomatology" and the "Journal of Oral and Maxillofacial Restoration", editorial board member or invited editorial board member of more than 10 journals including the "Chinese Journal of Stomatology". He has presided over or completed 12 national and military research projects, including the National Natural Science Foundation, and has supervised 5 post-doctoral fellows, 11 doctoral students, and more than 20 master's students. Professor Liu Hongchen combined research on edentulous jaws and temporomandibular joints, discovering the movement patterns of the mandible and temporomandibular joint after complete edentulism. Internationally, he was the first to propose the concept and hypothesis of edentulous position and indirect occlusion. His research proposed that the vertical distance at centric occlusion is not a single point but an acceptable physiological range, which is important for understanding oral physiological functions and guiding the restoration of tooth loss and the treatment of temporomandibular joint disorders. Based on theoretical research, he pioneered research on temporomandibular joint disorders in edentulous patients in China, summarizing the incidence of temporomandibular joint disorders in edentulous patients, especially unilateral edentulous patients, and proposing diagnostic criteria and treatment characteristics. The treatment methods he used have achieved good results in clinical applications. In view of the oral characteristics of the elderly, he pioneered the use of full-arch fixed dentures with retained roots in elderly patients both domestically and internationally, achieving excellent results. Since 1996, through online appointment registration, he led his research team to pioneer research on oral function and brain function as well as the impact and feedback of oral diseases on the central nervous system. Research results have discovered the influence of chewing movements and taste on specific functional areas of the brain, explored the effects of occlusal trauma and orofacial pain and other oral diseases on the central nervous system and its transmission pathways, and conducted extensive research from the depth of molecular biology. Many of these discoveries fill the gaps in the study of oral function and brain function. He has published more than 160 papers in domestic and international academic journals, given special reports more than 20 times at domestic and international academic conferences, and authored 10 books, including editing and publishing "Geriatric Stomatology", "Journal of Oral and Maxillofacial Restoration", "Dictionary of Specialized Terms in Stomatology", and "Oral Difficult Restorations". He won the third prize of National Science and Technology Progress Award once, the second prize of Army Science and Technology Progress Award six times, the third prize four times; the second prize of Army Medical Achievement Award once, the third prize three times; the second prize of Army Teaching Achievement Award once. He received commendations twice and was awarded the title of Outstanding Returned Overseas Students by the Ministry of Education and the Ministry of Personnel. In 1996, he was rated as a Scientific New Star of the General Logistics Department, and in 2000, he was rated as a Scientific Silver Star of the General Logistics Department. He undertook the oral medical care tasks for the leaders of the Party and the state and the members of the Central Military Commission, and was commended many times by the Central Health Office and the Health Office of the PLA.
