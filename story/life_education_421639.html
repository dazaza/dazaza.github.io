**Scoring for the Writing Section Follows the "Divide by Six" Principle**

Below is a table with many evaluation criteria:

**SAT Essay Scoring Criteria**

| Score | Writing Perspective or Argument | Organization of Examples or Evidence | Sentence Structure | Vocabulary Level | Grammar and Usage | Overall Effect |

|-------|----------------------------------|--------------------------------------|--------------------|-------------------|-------------------|------------------|

| 6 | Clear, persuasive, and independent perspective | Well-organized with strong examples | Rich and engaging sentences | Very mature and appropriate word choice | Almost no grammar errors or usage issues | Excellent |

| 5 | Basically clear and coherent | Well-organized with appropriate examples | Some sentence variation | Fairly rich vocabulary | Few serious errors | Good |

| 4 | Reasonably clear and coherent | Adequately organized with some evidence | Moderate sentence variation | Sufficient vocabulary for test requirements | Some technical problems | Competent |

| 3 | Somewhat unclear and incoherent | Incomplete development and sparse examples | Little sentence variation | Inappropriate or incorrect word choice | Some small errors | Average |

| 2 | Unclear and incoherent | Lacks argument or organization | Sentence structure errors | Very limited vocabulary | Several major errors | Below Average |

| 1 | Extremely unclear and incoherent | No argument, evidence, or support | Severe sentence structure errors | Severely limited vocabulary | Many critical errors | Poor |
