Because there was a young friend celebrating his/her birthday, Guo Yong personally lit the candles on the cake he brought. Guo Yong is helping a child practice pronunciation. One child seemed a little melancholy, so Guo Yong took him to the activity room. Due to living far away, after finishing the morning's public welfare activities, Guo Yong returned home at dusk.

On September 28, 2008, Guo Yong initiated the establishment of the "Beijing Charity Alliance". After much difficulty, the alliance has now grown into a large charity organization with more than ten thousand registered volunteers. The scope of their activities has expanded from disability assistance work to include services such as caring for the elderly, traffic management, and supporting migrant workers.

In October this year, Guo Yong also launched the disabled assistance hotline "With Love, No Obstacles". He and the volunteers manually carry wheelchairs, having already helped over 500 wheelchair-bound disabled people who have difficulty traveling to fulfill their wishes of visiting scenic spots like the Forbidden City, the Great Wall, and the CCTV Tower.

Thus, every weekend thereafter, Guo Yong rushed to the deaf children rehabilitation center, spending time with these kids, playing with them, and teaching them pronunciation. Because the school is in Yanqing, he had to get up at 5 o'clock in the morning each time, spending more than 7 hours on transportation round trip, only returning to the dormitory late at night. "The children can't hear sounds, but they know you're kind and treat you like family, looking forward to your arrival every week, which makes me feel especially warm."

Last year, due to his outstanding performance in the field of public welfare, Guo Yong was awarded the Beijing Urban Contribution Award. However, behind the brilliant facade lies the countless efforts of Guo Yong. Starting from high school, Guo Yong worked part-time jobs everywhere. By the time he reached university, he had taught himself layout design and stayed busy until deep into the night. To maintain the operation of the charity alliance, over the past three years, he has spent nearly 50,000 yuan out of pocket while simultaneously repaying over 50,000 yuan of personal debt. He laughed humbly: "Although there are many things to do, I usually just sleep less to save time for doing things."

When Guo Yong was just one year old, his parents died in a car accident, and a year later his grandfather passed away due to illness. When he was 12, the mainstay of the family, his uncle, died on duty; when he was 18, his last relative, his grandmother, also left. "Though experiences are different, seeing these deaf-mute children still gives me a feeling of empathy, always reminding me of my own childhood," said Guo Yong.

Most of the volunteers in the alliance are socially-minded individuals, with the oldest being well into their eighties. Mr. Zhang, a 38-year-old civil engineer, has participated in the deaf children rehabilitation activities four times in the past year. He admires Guo Yong greatly: "This young man is excellent! He has changed my view of post-90s, and I thank him for providing this platform for public welfare."

Guo Yong is from Linfen, Shanxi. During high school, he participated in numerous public welfare activities. In September 2008, he entered a certain science and technology vocational college in Beijing. Not long after entering the university campus, he went to the Haidian Deaf Children Rehabilitation Center to participate in public welfare activities, learning that the 80-plus deaf children here came from all over the country, often only seeing their parents once or twice a year, which made him sigh deeply.

Every weekend, under the organization of the "Beijing Charity Alliance", a group of volunteers regularly comes to the Haidian Disabled Federation Deaf Children Rehabilitation Center to accompany the children through a joyful day. When sophomore student Xiao Zhang learned that the chairman of the alliance, Guo Yong, was the same age as her, she couldn't help but be shocked. But what she didn't know was that when Guo Yong, born in 1990, founded the alliance alone three years ago, he carried the pain of losing his loved ones one after another and a debt of over 100,000 yuan. And today, tens of thousands of volunteers have united around him.

Shao Shao, who is in his senior year, hopes that after graduation, he can establish a disabled service center, "to continue engaging in public welfare and doing what I want to do is the greatest happiness."
