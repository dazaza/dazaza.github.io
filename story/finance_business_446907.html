"Avatar" Forces Domestic Films to Compete Among Themselves

2009-2010 New Year Film List

Every year during the New Year season, this year's films are especially numerous, and the period is particularly long. Starting from November 20th, the 2009 mainland New Year season began. This industry-recognized golden period will continue until February 20th, 2010, lasting for 93 days, making it the longest New Year season since the formal introduction of such periods in mainland China in 1997. During these 93 days, more than 50 films will be released one after another, practically a new film every two days, including Zhang Yimou's "A Simple Noodle Story" and Peter Chan's "Bodyguards and Assassins." Gao Jun, vice president of Beijing Xin Yinglian Theater Line, boldly claimed: The box office for the 2010 New Year season reaching 2 billion yuan is no problem! Unlike previous years' New Year seasons, this year sees a group of Hollywood films joining the competition, with blockbusters like "2012" and "Avatar" becoming strong rivals for domestic films vying for box office success. Qiu Qing, head of the Southern New Cinema Line of China Film, believed that although domestic films still hold a relative advantage in terms of quantity, from the perspective of market and box office, domestic films might lose to Hollywood films this New Year season.

Party concerned Zhu Yanping expressed concern about the impact of Hollywood films. He also revealed that the release date of "Crescent" was adjusted twice, moved forward from December 29th to December 10th, just to avoid "Avatar." He further stated that apart from "Crescent," films like "A Simple Noodle Story," "Storm II," and others had to adjust their schedules to avoid "Avatar."

Unable to compete with "2012," domestic films can only fight among themselves. Good narrative films like "My Tang Dynasty Brothers" and "Mars Without Incident" and "Panda Kung Fu" were released almost simultaneously. Speaking of "2012," the producer could only sigh, finally saying that compared to "Mars Without Incident" and "Panda Kung Fu," he still has confidence.

Generally speaking, from November to early December is a relatively slow time for the mainland movie market. Some medium and small budget films also choose to be released during this period, hoping to become market hotspots through buzz or special genres, thereby becoming box office dark horses. Unexpectedly, the sudden appearance of "2012" shattered all these plans. For instance, "Mars Without Incident," which focuses on parent-child themes, invited a batch of stars like Huang Lei, Xie Na, and He Jiong to support, using science fiction as its shell while telling a family comedy. This kind of film subject matter is light-hearted and easily becomes a choice for audiences without specific targets. However, with "2012" around, things are different. Liu Yiwei originally thought that "Mars Without Incident" could achieve a box office of 25 million to 30 million yuan. As he said, upon hearing that it would be released in the same period as "2012," I was stunned. He estimated that the box office would shrink significantly.

During the screening period of "Avatar," there will be at least a month when large-scale domestic productions are not released. Films like "Brotherhood of Blades," "Flower Field Story 2010," "Future Police," "The Little Soldier," and "Su Qier" will only be released at the beginning of February.

Gao Jun, vice president of Xin Ying Lian Pictures, expressed: "Avatar" is the most heavyweight Hollywood blockbuster in the past five years, bringing enormous box office impact.

"2012" Creates a Heap of Cannon Fodder

"Avatar" is a grand production prepared by American director James Cameron for 20 years. As the most successful commercial director, Cameron's credibility ensures that he surpasses Spielberg or any other Hollywood director. His works like the "Terminator" series and "Titanic" have never suffered defeats; it makes perfect sense that "Avatar" exudes an intimidating aura.

Even though "Avatar" was released in mainland China on January 2nd, a month later than in the U.S., its power cannot be underestimated. Films like "A Simple Noodle Story," "Bodyguards and Assassins," "Storm II," and "Crescent," which involve significant investments, carefully chose mid-December for their releases. During this period, the momentum of "2012" had already begun to weaken, and "Avatar" had yet to unleash its full force, providing at least two to three weeks for them to perform. Although there were a few Hollywood films released during this time, they were not worth worrying about.
