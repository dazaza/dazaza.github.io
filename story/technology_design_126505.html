Soy sauce incident-

by harvon on 2009-02-25 15:35:38

I don't know if you've heard of the "buying soy sauce" incident. If not, I'm afraid you're out of touch, haha, just kidding.

The "buying soy sauce" incident has recently become extremely popular on the internet. Just type in the phrase "buying soy sauce incident" on Baidu and all sorts of things will come up, like "buying soy sauce non-violent non-cooperation" or "the truth behind buying soy sauce". Even after my explanation, I reckon you still don't quite get what it means. So let me tell you about this "buying soy sauce" incident.

It originally came from Guangzhou Television. A reporter from the broadcasting station interviewed a citizen and asked for his opinion on the very explicit and violent "sex photo scandal". This citizen replied: "It's none of my business, I'm here to buy soy sauce." Thus, the phrase "buying soy sauce" spread and various PS versions and adaptations became wildly popular for a time. As a result, the term "soy sauce man" became a laughing matter on the internet, even spawning other internet slang such as "soy sauce tribe".

This is also when the phrase "buying soy sauce" became popular during the Carrefour boycott incident.

People liked to say then: "Do you want to buy soy sauce? If you don't want to, you don't have to."

Another reason is that soy sauce used to be sold loose and households generally sent their children to run errands, which was also a way to train them.

Generally, it refers to children growing up to the point where they can go buy soy sauce by themselves. It usually metaphorically represents an expected outcome or the passage of time, or refers to oneself being old or no longer young. For example: "When I was in college, my middle school classmate got married. He said to me: 'When you get married, my child will be able to buy soy sauce.'"

The so-called "soy sauce party" declaration derived from the "buying soy sauce" incident:

Buying soy sauce is an attitude, the so-called silent majority, the so-called "non-violent non-cooperation";

Buying soy sauce is a lifestyle, it's neither simply passing by, nor merely sightseeing;
