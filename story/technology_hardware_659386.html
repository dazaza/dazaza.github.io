With summer vacation here, many people want to take advantage of the holiday to deal with scars left from childhood. Scar repair has thus become a focus this summer. However, even small scars near joints are not so easy to remove. We would like to share some experiences in scar treatment. Not all scars can be removed, and experts from the Han Family Scar Repair Center have listed several conditions under which scars can be repaired. Generally, scar treatment can be divided into three stages: preventing deformation, reconstructing active functional areas, and reconstructing passive functional areas.

Preventing Deformation: Wound contraction is part of the healing physiological mechanism.

Method to prevent abnormal continuous wound contraction: Reduce tissue deficiency. Correct diagnosis of tissue deficiency is the most important part of reconstruction work - first release then supplement.

Wound reconstruction methods:

Suturing - Excision followed by suturing and Z-plasty

Skin grafting - Split-thickness, full-thickness, composite layer

Selection of donor site: Proximity principle

Flap reconstruction: Flaps, muscle flaps, microvascular flaps, water balloons (tissue expanders).

Suggestions: Tissue expanders can be used for local patchy scars, requiring two surgeries spaced about two and a half months apart.

Types of scars: Wide scars, hypertrophic scars, pigmentation issues (scars on limbs due to poor blood circulation and venous return problems, besides the scars themselves, also tend to have pigmentation changes), hypertrophic scars or keloids.

Scar concerns: Itchiness, pain or infection, and scar contraction leading to functional impairment.

Delayed wound healing - Unstable skin

Wound treatment: Artificial skin coverage and fixation / burn ointment

Just healed wounds → anti-inflammatory ointment / lanolin oil for 4-5 days.

Sunscreen, antipruritic drugs...

Suggestions:

1. For excessive scar hyperplasia, consider local steroid injections to improve the appearance of scars.

2. Indented scars can consider autologous fat injection.

3. Raised flap-treated scars can consider liposuction.

4. For well-healed wounds, early correct use of cosmetic glue and silicone sheets can prevent scar formation.

5. "Pressure therapy" refers to using various materials that provide pressure (elastic pressure garments, silicone pads, compression sleeves...).

6. Avoid scratching postoperative wounds again, as it will certainly lead to scarring.

Suggestions for elastic pressure garment use: Pressure garment wear time: 2 weeks after wound healing ~ within 2 months

Ideal pressure: 15~25mmHg, 22~23 hours/day, (every 2~3 months)

Low but sustained pressure → better final results!

High pressure → faster scar maturation

If pressure marks remain 30 minutes after removing the pressure garment, it indicates too tight, completely remove only for bathing, moisturizing the skin, exercising.

Stop wearing pressure garments: When the wound turns white, flat, and soft.

Silicone sheets (silicone, gel pads): Silicone: Composite containing silicon elements

Composition: Liquid, gel, solid

Usage time: 12~24 hours/day

Advantages: Reduces pain, prevents skin graft contraction, softens scars, reduces scar hyperplasia.

Disadvantages: Allergic reactions causing skin irritation → stop usage

Conclusion: Proper wound handling after injury during suturing, including debridement management and maintaining wound moisture, along with absolute rest, can help reduce scar formation. For laser scar removal, the most effective method is for linear wounds, which can be performed around 6 weeks after good wound healing.
