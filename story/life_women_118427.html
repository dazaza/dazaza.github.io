A Seven-Year Pain Caused by Injection Breast Augmentation: A Personal Confession

A Guangzhou resident underwent cosmetic surgery in pursuit of beauty, enduring not only the pain but also complications from the injected substance "Omeida," necessitating another surgery to remove it. Following the suspension of Omeida production, the victim took the cosmetic and plastic surgery institution to court, gradually revealing a seven-year "beauty black hole"...

The Incident Began with Breast Augmentation

Chen Baijiao, a white-collar worker at a Guangzhou advertising company, is slender and petite.

In the autumn of 2002, nearing the age of 26, she noticed during a shopping trip that the same clothes looked beautiful on her friend but lacked definition on her, leaving her feeling regretful.

Under the temptation of advertisements for cosmetic and plastic surgery institutions, Chen Baijiao entertained the idea of breast augmentation. During the 2002 National Day holiday, she saw an advertisement promoting "no incision, no surgery, artificial fat, natural safety, realistic texture, easily removable, imported materials, affordable price." She was intrigued. Chen Baijiao approached this cosmetic and plastic surgery institution where staff introduced her to injection-based breast augmentation using a hydrophilic polyacrylamide gel, known as Implanon gel or "Omeida." The staff assured her that "Omeida" was an approved imported material and very safe. With these assurances, Chen Baijiao readily paid the surgical fee. After a difficult recovery period, her figure showed noticeable improvement, restoring her lost confidence.

Post-Operative Nightmare

However, soon after, the "after-effects" Chen Baijiao had worried about before the surgery came to fruition. One afternoon, she experienced sudden chest pain, causing her alarm. A month later, symptoms such as swelling, pain, and lumps at the surgical site appeared successively. Frightened, she rushed to the institution that performed her surgery, only for the staff to perform a cursory examination and say, "This is normal; just massage more yourself."

Afterward, despite following the staff's advice and persisting with massages, Chen Baijiao saw no results, and her symptoms worsened.

Second Surgery

In 2007, Lu Qianyu, who had been suffering from recurring conditions, received shocking news: the State Food and Drug Administration issued a notice titled "Suspension of Production, Sales, and Use of Polyacrylamide Hydrogel (for Injection)," stating that its "safety cannot be guaranteed when used," requiring a "complete halt in production, sales, and use of polyacrylamide hydrogel (for injection)."

Chen Baijiao immediately underwent a physical examination and was informed that "Omeida" had caused adverse reactions within her body and must be removed immediately. Two days later, she underwent surgery to remove the injected substances from both breasts. After removal, Lu Qianyu felt as if she had been emptied out, beginning to doubt whether her body could return to its original health.

Lu Qianyu was merely one of hundreds of thousands of victims. It was estimated that from 1997, when "Omeida" was approved for production until its suspension, approximately 300,000 people nationwide had implants. The main adverse reactions after implantation of polyacrylamide hydrogel included inflammation, infection, hardening, lump formation, hardness, deformation, displacement, and residue.

Legal Action for Rights Protection

After much internal struggle, Lu Qianyu, whose breast augmentation surgery failed, decided to seek justice. In February this year, she took the cosmetic and plastic surgery institution that performed the surgery to court, arguing that under the influence of their advertisements, she underwent the breast augmentation procedure. The filler material was later banned by the state, forcing her to have it removed, which still caused immense pain and could lead to serious consequences in the future. She demanded compensation for various losses totaling over 40,000 yuan.

The defendant's cosmetic and plastic surgery institution argued that both the institution and the operating doctor possessed medical beautification qualifications. Using gel injection for breast augmentation did not violate the medical beautification regulations at the time. The plaintiff's current suffering could be resolved through insurance claims, and they claimed no fault, refusing compensation.

After reviewing the case, the trial court ruled that the beautification institution, knowing the high risk of complications from using Implanon gel for breast augmentation, still falsely claimed the surgery was "undoubtedly safe," violating the operator's obligation of integrity and infringing on the consumer's right to know. Their medical actions were found to have flaws. Considering the inherent complexity and risks of medical procedures, the court determined that the defendant should compensate the plaintiff, Lu Qianyu, with economic losses amounting to 15,000 yuan.

Director Tan of the Guangdong General Hospital's Cosmetic and Plastic Surgery Center emphasized that patients considering injection-based breast augmentation must seek treatment at large, reputable public tertiary hospitals to avoid tragic mastectomies.
