Relatives of the deceased in a traffic accident case discover the person who took the blame while chatting with netizens

by w9pzjf58625 on 2012-03-07 16:43:48

Our newspaper, Weinan News (reporter: Zhengshan Li) - Just before the trial was set to begin, relatives of the deceased in the traffic accident learned from a netizen during a chat that the person who had been arrested was not the actual perpetrator, but someone who had taken the blame for them.

At around 8 p.m. last night, the suspected hit-and-run offender Tian Li was escorted back to Weinan by police officers from Weinan after being apprehended in Guangxi. After hitting and killing two pedestrians, he fled the scene and paid 50,000 yuan for someone else to take the blame.

A couple was killed on their way home:

"My two children died tragically; my son-in-law was thrown thirty to forty meters away," said Bing She Wang, aged 56, from Wuli Pu in the Duqiao Street Office of Linwei District in Weinan City, still unable to contain his emotions when recalling the tragedy that occurred a year ago.

At about 11 p.m. on November 6, 2010, Ping Wang and her husband Jianfeng Gao were visiting their parents. As they left their house and prepared to hail a ride home on Qianjin Road in Weinan City, a silver-gray Jetta suddenly sped towards them. Both were struck down and died on the spot.

The following morning, Bing She Wang received a call from the traffic management department informing him that a young man had turned himself in at the police station, claiming to be the driver responsible for the accident. After the traffic police led the self-confessed Penghui Li (born in 1986) to identify the scene, it was confirmed that this Li was the perpetrator of the traffic accident escape. The accident determination report from the Linwei Brigade of the Weinan Public Security Bureau Traffic Police Detachment showed that Penghui Li was driving without a license and bore full responsibility.

Unexpected truth revealed through online chat:

The deaths of Jianfeng Gao and Ping Wang left behind a five-year-old child. The two families were deeply plunged into the grief of losing their loved ones. "We can only wait for the law enforcement departments to resolve this according to legal procedures," said Bing She Wang. However, an unexpected turn of events occurred after he received the court summons notifying him of the impending trial date on May 17th this year.

On May 25th, while chatting online, Long Wang, Bing She Wang's 23-year-old son, encountered a netizen who claimed that a friend of theirs had taken the blame for someone else and was currently detained in a holding cell, soon to be sentenced. The person they were substituting for was not only free but also untrustworthy, having not paid even a single cent.

Long Wang, having lost his sister and brother-in-law, naturally became very alert and asked the netizen for more details. Unexpectedly, the netizen mentioned an accident that was directly related to his sister and brother-in-law.

The money never arrived, and the substitute began to complain:

The trial was scheduled for May 31st. On May 26th, the family of Ping Wang provided information about the substitute to the Linwei District People's Procuratorate. Subsequently, a special task force consisting of the court, procuratorate, traffic police detachment, and criminal investigation detachment interrogated Penghui Li, who was about to stand trial.

Zhanhong Zhang, captain of the Linwei District Criminal Investigation Team, introduced yesterday that Penghui Li confessed that on the night of the incident, Tian Li (born in July 1991) drove to the Hi-Tech Zone, parked the car, and then met with Juan Wang (Tian Li's collaborator in transporting sand and Penghui Li's employer), Penghui Li, Tian Li's cousin Weifeng Li, and two others at a hotel. They agreed that Juan Wang would act as a guarantor, Penghui Li would take the blame for Tian Li, and Tian Li would pay Penghui Li a one-time fee of 50,000 yuan, along with an additional monthly stipend of 3,000 yuan for Penghui Li's family. After listening to Tian Li recount the details of the accident, Penghui Li turned himself in to the police station the next day. However, the money promised by Tian Li never materialized, and due to Tian Li's failure to handle the post-accident compensation promptly, Penghui Li faced the possibility of a heavy sentence and thus complained to friends who visited him in detention.

After verifying the content of Long Wang's online chat, the police initiated an arrest warrant for Tian Li. With the help of his father Anqi Li and cousin Weifeng Li, Tian Li managed to escape.

On July 25th, Anqi Li, Weifeng Li, and Penghui Li, suspected of harboring a criminal, were legally arrested. Juan Wang, who was pregnant, was released on bail.

Upon investigation, the police discovered that Tian Li, suspected of causing a traffic accident, was hiding in Nanning, Guangxi, working under a pseudonym at a hotel. On October 30th, the Linwei police captured Tian Li on the streets of Nanning. At around 8 p.m. last night, the Weinan police escorted Tian Li back to Weinan.
