Grapefruit: Make good use of big data, but do more than just period management

by anonymous on 2013-09-08 19:34:18

With the arrival of the big data era, mobile internet projects related to personal health have suddenly become a popular entrepreneurial direction. And since young women pay much more attention to health than men, many entrepreneurs have set their target audience in the health field as women.

In this category of applications, those related to the female menstrual cycle are particularly hot. Chen Fangyi, an 85s entrepreneur from Xiamen, also chose to re-entrepreneur in this field. The application they launched, called Grapefruit, has already surpassed earlier entrants like Dayima and PT in terms of growth rate.

"However, we are fundamentally different from Dayima and others. From the outside, it may seem like a menstrual software, but in essence, it is not. Essentially, we are an analytical software. The most important thing we do is data upload, then recording and analysis followed by suggestions - these two aspects haven't been done by others," said Chen Fangyi.

In fact, the reason why Chen Fangyi named his application Grapefruit is because he didn't just want to manage the female menstrual cycle, but hoped that his application could help with overall female health.

But whether managing the menstrual cycle or daily physical health, Grapefruit's method is the same: reducing the cost for users to input data, then using big data for analysis, and providing relevant suggestions.

Core: Data acquisition and analysis

Chen Fangyi recently participated in an inspection tour to Israel, visiting many Israeli internet companies. One company left a deep impression on him.

This company focuses on patient recovery after surgery, combining body recovery with games to make some originally boring physical recovery exercises very interesting. For example, arm fracture recovery requires continuous arm movement, so this company combines this movement with a game similar to whack-a-mole, allowing the rehabilitator to complete related recovery training while playing the game.

"The biggest inspiration for us is how to reduce the cost for users to enter information," said Chen Fangyi. As an application with the core entry point of female menstrual cycle management, obtaining data related to the female menstrual cycle is the most basic work. Without data, everything would be impossible to discuss.

Currently, however, users still need to manually enter their height, weight, and menstrual data, which increases the user's usage cost. Grapefruit's approach is to give users certain incentives, thereby motivating them to actively fill out the data.

Chen Fangyi also frankly admitted that they still haven't done well in this area, and he is considering whether there is a way to obtain data without requiring manual input from users. "We will definitely do hardware," he said. Smart hardware might currently be the best direct method to acquire data.

After obtaining the data, analysis becomes key.

Since all of Grapefruit's data is stored in the cloud, it can longitudinally analyze individual user historical data and horizontally analyze specific population data to compare with a particular user, helping users understand themselves better from two dimensions.

However, one important problem Grapefruit faces is that it is difficult to make very precise analysis and suggestions based solely on a user's initial month of data. Most users need to continuously input data for two months to experience the value Grapefruit brings to them.

This is different from ordinary applications. Generally, ordinary applications give users a strong sense of novelty after downloading, but as long-term use continues, fatigue may set in. However, Grapefruit requires long-term use; the more data accumulated, the greater the value provided to users.

Another issue is that not all women's menstrual cycles will experience abnormalities. Many women have very regular menstrual cycles and do not need specific applications to better get through these uncomfortable three to five days. In response, Grapefruit provides them with more functions.

Chen Fangyi stated that although the menstrual cycle may not require help, through the operation of Grapefruit, he discovered that all women care about issues such as skin, weight loss, and breast enhancement. "For normal conditions, we will tell users that you are healthy, making users happy. At the same time, we provide some health-related advice, which is what they are concerned about."

Providing health and exercise advice also allows Grapefruit to solve the problem of user usage frequency. Although menstrual cycle management applications have fixed usage frequencies, there is a certain interval period. Providing daily suggestions and reminders can maintain a higher user usage frequency.

Chen Fangyi: Always on the path of entrepreneurship

Grapefruit is Chen Fangyi's latest project, and before that, his main focus was on Return Network, a website that allows users to receive return rewards while shopping. Return Network and Grapefruit are independent companies, but both belong to Xiamen Inspiration Ark Group. Another business under Inspiration Ark is developers for Tencent's open platform.

Currently, Inspiration Ark can generate good income from Return Network and Tencent platforms, so there is no financial pressure for Grapefruit.

However, Return Network and platform developers also have their disadvantages, such as being highly constrained by platform policy changes. After Taobao and Tmall adjusted policies for guide-to-purchase and rebate websites last year, Return Network had to make corresponding adjustments.

Additionally, Chen Fangyi once worked on a project mimicking the American IFTTT. IFTTT provides a mechanism that allows users to connect different internet services together, forming mutually triggering behaviors, thus creating some interesting applications.

Upon seeing IFTTT, Chen Fangyi immediately created a domestic version called IfCloud, gaining 10,000 users on the first day and quickly surpassing 100,000 users. However, after that, the number of users hardly grew. A month later, he closed the project. "Later, I realized that only we ourselves would use this, others wouldn't," he said.

Interestingly, Grapefruit is exactly the opposite of IFTTT. This is an application that Chen Fangyi himself cannot use. Regarding Grapefruit's future development, Chen Fangyi expressed that community mechanisms will be added after reaching millions of users, while ensuring the privacy of the community, as women's physiological states are suitable only for sharing among pure female groups.
