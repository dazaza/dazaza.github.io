The story of water and fish

by zxcdm331dl on 2011-08-27 11:03:30

The fish said, "You cannot see the tears in my eyes because I am in the water."

The water said, "I can feel your tears because you are in my heart."

These two lines of dialogue are very classic, almost everyone knows them, but few people know the full story. The fish was a mischievous child from a young age; she was never quiet like other children. She liked to dart around in the water, first doing a 50-meter sprint, then making an emergency stop or a sharp turn. Every time, the water would always smile and watch the fish. Sometimes, the fish would encounter some frustrating things, but at these times, the gentle water would always quietly listen and comfort the fish. During the day, the water would gently toss the fish up, letting her leap out of the water to see the outside world, and then catch her steadily. At night, the water became the warmest cradle; he would always sway gently, soothing the fish to sleep. On summer nights, the water would always drag the fish to the surface.

As the fish grew up, she found something in her heart that made her worry - it was the water. One day, the fish finally mustered up the courage to tell the water that she liked him, but the water remained silent. "Why don't you say anything?" the fish asked. The water continued to be silent, just shaking his head lightly. The mother said the fish could not love the water. This is the law of nature, just like how zebras only love zebras, leopards only love leopards; stripes can only love stripes, spots can only love spots, and spots can never love stripes.

The fish didn't understand, if stripes really fell in love with spots, flying birds really fell in love with fish, and the fish really fell in love with the water, then what should be done? The fish didn't understand, she bubbled and told the water, "I love you!" The water fell silent again. The fish didn't say anything more, just lying quietly in the arms of the water.

After a long while, the fish's words broke the silence, "You cannot see the tears in my eyes because I am in the water." The water said, "I can feel your tears because you are in my heart." The fish anxiously asked, "Then why don't you love me?" But the water could only say, "I cannot love you, I have no fixed place, often drifting everywhere, being with me will be very hard for you."

The fish firmly said, "I'm not afraid, I want to be with you forever!" However, the water couldn't escape its fate of drifting. It flowed into a big river, and the fish stayed by its side all the time. They embraced each other as they bypassed hidden reefs and dangerous waves, flowing through rivers and lakes, leaping down waterfalls, and flowing into a small stream. Along the way, the water would gently toss the fish up, catching her again and again, playing around. The water flow got warmer and warmer, and finally, it was almost drying up!

"Great, we can finally settle down," the fish cheered. "No, the water level is too shallow, it's too dangerous. While there's still a way out, you should swim back quickly!" the water nervously said. "No, no matter what, I will not leave you!" the fish resolutely said. To reduce the evaporation of water, during the day, the fish lay quietly in the arms of the water, without any movement. At night, when all the stars fell into the water, the fish began to play, swallowing the stars one by one, then spitting them out, swallowing them again, and spitting them out again, endlessly entertained. In June, the fiery sun shone on the water surface. Despite their various efforts, the water was evaporating bit by bit. The fish's back gradually emerged from the water, and the water worked hard to create ripples, moistening her back so that the sun wouldn't burn her. But this accelerated the evaporation of the water even more. Finally, the last drop of water left the fish. The fish lay on the cracked land, barely alive. When the fish's heart completed its last beat, a tear slid down her cheek. Suddenly, a lightning bolt streaked across the sky, followed by several thunderclaps, and then torrential rain poured down. The fish returned to the embrace of the water, who called out to the fish, but the fish never woke up again. The water carried the fish and ran like the wind, filled with sorrow, its heartbreaking cries audible to anyone.

The water carried the fish, running forcefully, and flowed to the side of a withered little tree. The water seeped into the soil, burying the fish's body in the soil. The water softly said to the fish's decayed corpse, "We don't need to keep flowing everywhere anymore, I've found your home, from now on, you have me within you, and I have you within me..."

Years passed, new green buds appeared on top of the tree, and there was a drop of water on them, shining brightly under the sunlight. That was the tear shed by the fish...

The fish said, "You cannot see the tears in my eyes because I am in the water."

The water said, "I can feel your tears because you are in my heart."

The fish said to the water: "I've been crying all along, but you'll never know, because I am in the water."

The water said: "I know, because you're always in my heart."

"I am not a fish, and you are not water, can you see my lonely tears?"

"Perhaps, because these are the tears of a lonely lover."

The fish said to the water: "I will never leave you, because without you, I cannot survive."

The water said: "I know, but what if your heart isn't here?"

"I am not a fish, and you are not water, I won't leave you because I love you, but is there room for me in your heart?"

The fish said to the water: "I am lonely because I can only stay in the water."

The water said: "I know, because my heart carries your loneliness."

"I am not a fish, and you are not water, I am lonely because I miss you, but can you, far away, feel it?"

The water said to the fish: "If there were no fish, what would remain in the water?"

The fish said: "If there were no you, how could there be me?"

"I am not a fish, and you are not water, without your love, I can still live well, but living well doesn't mean I can forget you."

The fish said to the water: "Being unable to go out and see the outside world throughout my life is my greatest regret."

The water said: "Being unable to get rid of this thought throughout my life is my biggest failure."

"I am not a fish, and you are not water, now I just want a lifetime commitment, but can you afford it?"

The fish said to the water: "In your life, which number fish am I?"

The water said: "You are not the first fish in the water, but you are the first in my heart."

"I am not a fish, and you are not water, we are not each other's firsts in life, but do you know? You are the first person I want to marry..."

The fish said to the water: "Do you believe in love at first sight?"

The water said: "When I realized you were a fish, I knew you would swim into my heart."

"I am not a fish, and you are not water, I thought my feelings for you wouldn't last long because it was love at first sight, but I was wrong. Feelings are like wine, the longer they are sealed, the more fragrant and lasting they become."

The fish said to the water: "Why is it always me asking and you answering?"

The water said: "Because I like to let you understand my heart in my answers."

"I am not a fish, and you are not water, why do you always make me wait? Don't you know, waiting = losing confidence = giving up?"

"If I were a fish, and you were water, wouldn't that be great! Water always knows what the fish thinks, because the fish is in the heart of the water."

"But I am not a fish, and you are not water, you will never know my love, because maybe I am not even in your heart!"

"If I were a fish, and you were water, could I swim into your heart?"
