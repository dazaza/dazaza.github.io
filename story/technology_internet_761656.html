Microsoft Should Learn from Apple's Hardware Lessons: Upgrading the Surface is Imminent

by geekzhang on 2013-05-05 21:38:18

iPad Mini Fixes Three iPad Problems at Once (Image credit: Tencent Technology)May 5, Beijing time - Foreign tech site CNET published an article by Brooke Crothers stating that from the perspective of market acceptance of hardware products, Apple rarely experiences failure. Even when there is a possibility of weak market demand, Apple is very adept at resolving hardware issues. This is something Microsoft could learn a lot from.Below is the full content of the article:Apple is highly skilled at addressing oversights in product design. Can Microsoft quickly learn to do the same? For example, the Retina display-equipped iPad clearly violated Apple's design philosophy: Apple products should be thinner, lighter, and cooler. However, Apple took only six months to swiftly correct these three problems with the iPad Mini. Not only is the iPad Mini thinner and lighter, but it is also cheaper, rapidly gaining popularity worldwide.Now, as Microsoft is also engaged in tablet manufacturing, can the company respond quickly? So far, the Microsoft Surface tablet cannot be considered a success. According to surveys released by multiple market research institutions including IDC, consumers generally perceive the Surface Pro as large, heavy, and expensive.However, Microsoft initially categorized the Surface as a PC during the design phase. The Surface RT has been criticized for poor performance and operating system issues, and like the Surface Pro, it faces challenges in the transition to smaller tablets.This week, authoritative market research firm NPD DisplaySearch revealed to the tech site CNET that Microsoft will launch a 7.5-inch tablet. Sources also indicated that the price of this tablet might be $400 or less. Nevertheless, DisplaySearch pointed out that it would be difficult for this tablet to be released in the near term.This raises the question: I believe that the sales momentum of the Surface Pro and RT may continue to slow down over the next few months. Moreover, I feel that future products, including Acer's 8-inch Iconia tablet, will find it hard to fill the market gap.Of course, we cannot overlook Android tablets. I speculate that hardware manufacturers such as Asus and HP will increasingly rely on the Android system to produce cool and affordable products.Microsoft seems eager to emulate Apple's dual focus on software and hardware and anticipates the success of Windows 8 tablets. But the question is, can Microsoft replicate Apple's successful hardware model? Rapid upgrades to the Surface tablet will be inevitable.But the problem is: Time waits for no one.
