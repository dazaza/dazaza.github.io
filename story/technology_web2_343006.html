For a breakup, where would you choose to have it?

by j1990910 on 2010-04-10 13:46:11

First of all, I would certainly not break up beside the toilet to avoid catching bad luck; nor would I choose a place often hit by sandstorms and dust storms, to prevent myself from relapsing into old feelings because I am too soft-hearted and afraid that she might get something in her eyes. In such a scenario, I might impulsively, concernedly hug her in my warm arms, unfortunately leading to the failure of our breakup. Moreover, I wouldn't let the two of us stand for a long time in the active area of the campus pretending to be profound, cool, and eye-catching, which would only add more excitement to others' jokes.

The above parallel sentence tells us that, in this era where searching for "broken-hearted people" on Baidu yields more results than "people in love," breaking up absolutely requires attention to location. Unfortunately, with Google gone, we can only unconditionally rely on one-sided search data displays. It can be certain that choosing an inappropriate place to break up may lead to the failure of the breakup. Failure is a minor issue as it is the mother of success; however, if a lot of awkward situations arise during the breakup, one might be looked down upon by the other party. This may still be a minor issue since both will eventually become "the most familiar strangers." But if one is looked down upon by future rivals due to this, it is no longer a trivial matter. Or, if the environment's sorrowful atmosphere is overly exaggerated, it could lead to severe inner wounds, taking at least half a month to recover or even resulting in years of depression.

In short, breaking up is not an easy thing to resolve. Choosing the right place for a good ending is just as difficult as having a good start. However, for those who truly no longer have feelings, it doesn't matter where the breakup occurs. The above five points are for reference only. If there are any similarities, consider it my copying of you. Anyway, after breaking up, one must never harm oneself, making oneself seem like a wounded tiger licking its wounds in a cave. This is meaningless. One should calm down and think about how to recover and start anew. Alternatively, keep oneself busy, as being busy is undoubtedly the best way to forget pain.
