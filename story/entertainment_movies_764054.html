A brand new work from the producers of "Through Washington" - "The Beauty of Washington," this is a video that takes you on a tour of the beautiful scenery of Washington and the Evergreen State. Filmed both on land and in the air, it is entirely in high-definition video for television. This film captures the colorful cityscape, landscapes, as well as entertainment activities, culture, and celebratory scenes. All these make Washington unique!

Throughout the city of Washington, there are hundreds of memorial buildings, monuments, and statues, most of which are related to various presidents. In the western part of the city, on the east bank of the Potomac River, there are memorials for three historical presidents: The Washington Monument overlooks the Potomac River Park. It's a white marble obelisk standing at 169 meters tall, offering panoramic views of the entire city. Along the river lies the famous Japanese cherry blossom grove; west of the monument is the Lincoln Memorial, an ancient Greek-style building with 36 white marble columns outside, symbolizing the 36 states that made up the nation at the time; inside stands the statue of Lincoln sitting; south of the monument is the Jefferson Presidential Memorial, with his equestrian statue outside. In 1971, the John F. Kennedy Center was completed, including a modern theater, concert hall, opera house, symphony hall, and ballet theater. Named in honor of America's first president, George Washington, it houses important departments of U.S. national agencies, such as the White House, Congress, the State Department, and the Department of Defense, making it the highest command center and the hub of political and cultural activities in the United States. East of the Capitol is the Supreme Court Building, and nearby, the Library of Congress is the second largest library in the world after Moscow's Lenin Library. The adjacent Shakespeare Library is renowned for its collection of Shakespeare’s works and research documents. The Pentagon, where the Department of Defense is located, stands on the southwest bank of the Potomac River. Washington D.C. has a rectangular layout covering an area of 178 square kilometers. With the Capitol as the center, it is divided into four districts: Southeast, Northeast, Northwest, and Southwest. Streets running north-south are named with Arabic numerals, while those running east-west are named with English letters. Thirteen diagonal avenues are named after the original thirteen states of the United States. The Northwest District is the heart of Washington, housing the most important government buildings, monuments, and museums. North of Pennsylvania Avenue is the main commercial area, and the northern extension of Massachusetts Avenue is the embassy district.
