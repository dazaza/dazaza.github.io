Yang Wei and Ye Shengnan fell in love during the Cultural Revolution, their union bearing heavy historical significance. Their small family underwent various social trends, beneficial or otherwise, amidst the great tide of reform and opening-up. Yang Wei found that there was a huge difference between him and his wife's worldviews and values, and they ultimately chose to split up.

Epidemic Marital Change Part One

Due to having spareribs, braised pork, and burnt bread as protection within the rebel organization, after this incident happened, the rebel organization didn't do much to me. They just expelled me from the organization. I became a free spirit, idling at home all day, while my father was locked up in a cowshed. When I delivered food to my father, I complained about how he had sold me out for no reason, betraying and harming me. My father chuckled and said, "You students will calm down after a while. If you don't leave that organization, it will have internal strife, and if things go badly, there might even be armed conflict. All of you are children of commoners; none of you should hurt each other. What's so valuable about being a commander? Let someone else take the position. It's like Kong Rong giving up the bigger pear or Lei Feng prioritizing others."

I gently caressed her. My body was in deep calm after intense exercise, but my mood was in great turmoil. From this moment on, we were already one, intertwined with each other, inseparable. I lovingly held her onto my body, feeling her weight, enjoying her softness, breathing in her scent... She covered me, her fingers softly stroking my cheeks, lips, chin, and neck... Then she calmly kissed my mouth, we mutually sucked on each other's lips. My blood began to concentrate again, my affection coalescing into hardness, yearning to once again merge with her. She slid off me: "Do you want me again? Come on."

Epidemic Marital Change Part Three

Her behavior repulsed me, and I lost the normal desire a man should feel towards a woman. I knew that perhaps our relationship was truly over. But I still lacked the courage to say those words to her. I didn't know what would happen once I really uttered the two words "divorce." Uncertain matters easily make people hesitate and become timid. If she brought it up, I think I would calmly accept it. The temperature in the room made me feel cold without the blanket covering me, but she felt no cold. On her naked body, she only wore a tiny pair of triangular underwear. I rolled up the blanket, moved to Dandan's room, it was really cold, so I quickly wrapped myself in the blanket, curling up to warm myself.

Epidemic Marital Change Part Four

Parents love hearing others praise their children. Ye Shengnan genuinely flattered me in front of my parents, making them extremely happy. My father kept drinking toast after toast, and my mother didn't strictly stop him as usual. As for me, I naturally became even more unrestrained with the free-flowing good wine and good dishes. That day, both my father and I were slightly tipsy but not drunk. Drinking to this extent was just right. My legs felt light, my head swayed, yet my mind was exceptionally clear. My entire body was in a state of complete relaxation, lazily soft from the inside out, feeling comfortable. The world also seemed more pleasing than usual.

Epidemic Marital Change Part Five

On the third day after returning home, I accompanied Ye Shengnan to the neighborhood office to handle our remarriage procedures. Ye Shengnan sat in a wheelchair, and I pushed her. It wasn't until we registered our marriage that we suddenly realized we were already over fifty years old. On the way home, Ye Shengnan tightly hugged the bag in her arms, which contained our marriage registration certificate. I looked at her back. She quietly sat in the wheelchair, her long hair flowing over her shoulders like a cascading waterfall. Her delicate ears were bare of earrings, and the fine fuzz around her temples gleamed like golden silk under the sunlight. At this very moment, the journey of my marriage with Ye Shengnan, from falling in love, getting married, having children, divorcing, illegally cohabiting (this was my mother's definition, fashionable terms call it cohabitation without the 'illegal' part), and then remarrying, whether it was the cold disappointment or the warm happiness, whether it was the bitter fragments or the sweet wanderings, were all paths we walked together, all bright days we jointly owned. These thoughts leaping in my mind were like melancholy flowers sprinkled all over my heart. Suddenly, a wave of warmth flooded my heart, and I couldn't help stopping my steps. I cupped her face from behind, and she turned around, smiling at me with tears welling up in her eyes. I saw happiness in her smile and tasted sweetness in her tears.
