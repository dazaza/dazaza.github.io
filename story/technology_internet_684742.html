Application of Pilz safety light curtains

by maipaishiye on 2012-03-01 10:14:54

The PSENop2/4 series safety light curtains and grids, among other photoelectric protection devices, are used to safeguard hazardous points and areas in production processes that require effective intervention. The PSEN series safety light grids comply with common international standards and are designed for protection levels 2 and 4. The relevant standards include:

- EN 61496-1:1997 Machine safety: Photoelectric protective equipment. Part 1: General requirements and tests.

- prEN 61496-2:1997 Machine safety: Photoelectric protective equipment. Part 2: Special requirements for the use of photoelectric protective equipment.

These devices consist of transmitters and receivers that define the height and width of the protected area using infrared beams. Both units have control and evaluation logic; electrical connections are made through M12 connectors located at the bottom of the grid. The transmitter and receiver are optically synchronized, so they do not need a direct connection. Infrared beams are controlled and monitored by microprocessors, and operational status is displayed via LEDs. Two yellow LEDs facilitate simple alignment of the grid during installation. If an object or operator's body interrupts the beams emitted by the transmitter, the OSSD output immediately switches from high to low level, shutting down motion in the hazardous area.

### I. How to Choose Safety Equipment

When selecting safety light grids, three typical characteristics must be considered:

- **Resolution**: Based on the part of the body that needs protection.

The resolution (R) of the grid refers to the smallest dimension of an object that can darken at least one beam forming the detection zone.

As shown in Figure 1, resolution is determined by geometric characteristics such as diameter and center distance; it is independent of external factors and operating conditions of the grid.

The resolution can be calculated using the formula: R = I + d.

- **Protected Area Height**

It is important to distinguish between [detection zone height] and [protected area height].

- Detection zone height is the distance from the top edge of the first beam to the bottom edge of the last beam.

- Protected area height refers to the effective protection area where objects larger than or equal to the grid's resolution safely darken the beams.

- **Safety Distance**

The safety distance of the safety device should ensure that the operator cannot reach the hazardous area until the movable dangerous machinery has come to a complete stop (as shown in Figure 3).

According to EN999, 775, and 294 standards, the distance is composed of four factors:

1. ESPE response time

2. Machine overrun time

3. ESPE resolution

4. Approach speed of the object to be detected

The formula for calculating the safety distance is: S = K(t1 + t2) + C

Where:

- S = Minimum safe distance between the protected area and the hazardous area (mm)

- K = Speed at which the object approaches the hazardous area (mm/s)

- t1 = ESPE response time

- t2 = Machine overrun time

- d = Resolution of the safety device

- C = 8(d - 14) for safety devices with resolution ≤ 40mm

- = 850 mm for safety devices with resolution > 40mm

Note: The value of K is:

- 2000 mm/s if the calculated distance ≤ 500 mm,

- 1600 mm/s if the calculated distance > 500 mm.

If there is a possibility of entering the hazardous area from above or below (as shown in Figure 3) and the device's resolution is > 40mm, the top beam must be placed at a height of 900mm (H2), starting from the reference plane (or the bottom of the machine), and the bottom beam must be placed at a height of 300mm (H1).

The grid must be installed horizontally (as shown in Figure 4). The distance between the hazardous area and the furthest beam is calculated using the following formula: S = 1600 mm/s(t1+t2) + 1200 – 0.4H

Where:

- S = Minimum safe distance between the protected area and the hazardous area

- t1 = ESPE response time

- t2 = Machine overrun time

- H = Height of the beam above the floor, which must be at least 1000mm.

### II. Typical Application Areas

The PSEN op 2/4 series safety light grids can be applied in all automated control areas where access to hazardous zones must be controlled.

Particularly used to prevent dangerous movements of machinery parts:

- Automatic machines

- Packaging, handling, and storage machines

- Textile, woodworking, and ceramic processing machines

- Automatic or semi-automatic assembly lines
