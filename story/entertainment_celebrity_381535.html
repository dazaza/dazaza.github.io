On February 17, the 15th day of the first lunar month, which is the traditional Chinese festival known as Lantern Festival, Hunan TV's annual traditional evening called "Yuanxiao Xilei" will once again bring exciting and entertaining programs to audiences nationwide. It is undoubtedly one of the most concerning topics for countless Mango fans in recent times. Today, a reporter learned from the director's team that this year's 2011 Lantern Festival Gala will continue to bring joyous elements, inviting celebrities and masters to present wonderful crosstalk and skit segments, while also giving out generous gifts such as red rabbits and yellow rabbits. Additionally, there will be dazzling stars presenting carefully prepared song and dance performances on this festive night.

On the seventh day of the first lunar month, the majority of people start bustling about for their happiness in the new year. However, the festive atmosphere of the New Year in 2011 continues to ferment with joy because, according to old traditions, the Lantern Festival marks the end of the Spring Festival celebrations. Today, a reporter learned from Hunan TV that as an important part of the Mango Channel's New Year family entertainment season, the annual "Lantern Festival Gala" will present all-new highlights in 2011. Particularly exciting for Mango drama fans is the appearance of popular screen couples like Feng Shaofeng and Yang Mi, the male and female leads from the hit drama series "Palace," and Li Chen and Qin Lan, the male and female leads from the romantic love story "Love in Lushan 2010." They will grace the gala with sweet and romantic musical performances. Yang Mi and Feng Shaofeng duet on "Tonight I Want to Marry You."

Feng Shaofeng and Yang Mi became acquainted through the drama "Palace," which skyrocketed them to fame. The two young actors' popularity soared rapidly, and rumors of their secret romance have been circulating widely, earning them plenty of attention and discussion. According to the gala's director, Feng Shaofeng and Yang Mi will make their first joint performance on Hunan TV's 2011 Lantern Festival Gala by singing "Today I Want to Marry You." Is there any special reason behind this unique arrangement? The director neither confirms nor denies it. What sparks will the two create then? Whether they will reveal their relationship as lovers to the world remains one of the biggest highlights of the evening.

Additionally, Li Chen, who gained immense popularity through his portrayal of Wu Zhe in "Soldiers Sortie" and further cemented his status as a top-tier actor in mainland China with works like "Aftershock," "My Battalion My Brother," and "Shrimp Ball Story," starred alongside the stunning beauty Qin Lan in the TV series "Love in Lushan 2010." Their on-screen chemistry left a deep impression on viewers. As a uniquely meaningful hometown, Qin Lan returned to Hunan TV with Li Chen to appear at the 2011 Lantern Festival Gala. What kind of performances they will bring is worth everyone's anticipation.

Does Mango intend to pair Wei Chen and Jiang Kai Tong?

In addition to the aforementioned well-known couple pairs, this year's Lantern Festival Gala features another particularly eye-catching duo: Wei Chen, who has successfully transformed into a semi-mature trendsetter, and the oxygen girl Jiang Kai Tong, who gave an excellent performance in "Accidentally Fell in Love."

According to industry insiders, these two young artists under Mango met through work and have a good relationship privately. Recently, Jiang Kai Tong even appeared as a guest on Wei Chen's variety show "Powerful Sunday," enjoying themselves immensely. There are reports that Mango Pictures, which has been promoting Jiang Kai Tong, plans to produce a youth idol drama with an urban love theme specifically tailored for her and Wei Chen. This appearance together at the 2011 Lantern Festival Gala is intended to promote and build momentum for them in advance. Is the rumor true? How will things progress? The truth will soon be revealed.
