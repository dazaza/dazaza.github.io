Alibaba Announces the Privatization of B2B Listed Company: Share Price HK$13.5

Tech News, February 21 evening - Alibaba Group announced today that it would make a privatization offer to the listed company, Alibaba B2B. The final buy-back price will be HK$13.5 per share, which is in line with the offering price at the end of 2007 when the company went public. This price represents a 60.4% premium over the average closing price of the last 60 trading days before the suspension on February 9. If the privatization is successful, it is expected to cost around HK$19 billion.

The privatization offer agreement announced a buy-back price of HK$13.5 per share, representing a 60.4% premium over the average closing price of the most recent 60 trading days prior to the suspension on February 9, and a 55.3% premium over the average closing price of the last 10 trading days.

Alibaba Group stated in an announcement on the Hong Kong Stock Exchange that the buy-back price of HK$13.5 per share will not be increased further. According to relevant regulations of the Hong Kong Stock Exchange, Alibaba will have no authority to increase the buy-back price after issuing this announcement.

The announcement states that Alibaba shareholders will receive the plan document at an appropriate time and will be invited to vote on the offer proposal. Meanwhile, Alibaba Group also pointed out in the announcement that it intends to raise funds for the privatization through new external financing and existing cash on hand.

A key factor driving Alibaba Group's decision to privatize its listed subsidiary (B2B) is to give small shareholders an opportunity to realize their investment returns during Alibaba's strategic transformation and upgrade period. In the joint announcement from Alibaba Group and Alibaba, it was pointed out that the strategic transformation might lead to slower revenue growth and affect profit expectations in the medium to short term.

Alibaba's early business model focused on attracting manufacturers, trading companies, and wholesalers to sell products via its online trading market, quickly increasing the number of paying members to achieve maximum revenue growth. Starting last year, the company made significant adjustments, shifting its focus from increasing membership numbers to enhancing the user experience for buyers on the trading platform. This could slow down the growth rate of paying members to some extent. Alibaba has mentioned this major shift in previously disclosed information and reminded investors that although they are confident in the long-term prospects of this strategy, it might impact the company's financial performance in the medium to short term.

Alibaba Group Chairman Jack Ma said, "Privatizing Alibaba allows us to avoid the pressure of having a listed subsidiary and enables us to make long-term plans that are most beneficial to our customers. The privatization offer also provides our shareholders with an attractive opportunity to realize their investments without having to wait a long time for the company to complete its transformation."

Since going public, the Hang Seng Index has cumulatively fallen by more than 27% (as of the close of trading on February 20, 2012).

An independent director committee composed of independent non-executive directors of Alibaba has been established and is evaluating this proposal. They have appointed an independent financial advisor to provide recommendations to independent shareholders regarding this offer. The recommendation of the independent director committee will be specified in the plan document, which will be distributed to all shareholders as soon as practicable.

Upon the effectiveness of the plan, all plan shares will be cancelled, and Alibaba will immediately apply to the Hong Kong Stock Exchange for the delisting of shares upon the effectiveness of the plan.

The joint announcement also shows that Alibaba Group is in discussions with Yahoo! about the possibility of restructuring the shares held by Yahoo!, but no agreements have been reached or there is certainty about reaching an agreement. The privatization plan is not contingent on the completion of any potential Yahoo! deal, and the Yahoo! deal is not contingent on the completion of the privatization plan.
