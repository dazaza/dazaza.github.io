Source: Guangzhou Window Auto, Auto Channel Editor: Black Cat. Contributions and reservations are welcome. Update time: March 31, 2011.

China's first online car purchase flagship store is here. According to insiders from Geely, the official Global Hawk Tmall flagship store will open in early April. The first "online exclusive car," the dual-color Panda, will also be launched simultaneously. What makes this online exclusive car different? Who will become China's first online purchaser? All the suspense will be revealed on the day the flagship store opens.

First Attempt: Selling 300 Pandas in 1 Minute

In December 2010, the Global Hawk brand had already tried selling cars online as a prelude to the opening of the flagship store. In just one minute, 300 Pandas were sold out, surpassing the record previously set by Smart. Even with products priced in the tens of thousands, online purchases can still go crazy. This pre-opening promotion has filled Geely and Tmall with confidence in online car sales. Liu Jinliang, Vice President of Geely Group and General Manager of the Sales Company, stated that Geely will specifically design online exclusive cars based on the consumption characteristics of internet users. These online exclusive models can be test-driven, maintained, and repaired at 4S stores, enjoying the same after-sales service and consumer protection as offline purchases, but they can only be sold online.

What Makes the First Online Exclusive Car, the Dual-Color Panda, Different?

In the official Global Hawk flagship store, we see a blue-and-white Panda car. As China's first online exclusive car, the dual-color Panda continues the fashionable appearance and five-star safety features of the Panda while attempting to lead trends with color.

The C-NCAP five-star safety rating makes the Panda the safest A00-class small car in the country. Its classic biomimetic design gives the Panda a high回头 rate. So, what makes the upcoming dual-color Panda different? Based on the information released so far by Global Hawk, the use of color and fashion elements is another characteristic of this online exclusive car. Geely attempts to "color" tempt consumers. Beyond this, what other trump cards does the dual-color Panda have? We can only wait for the launch day to uncover the mystery.

Who Will Be China's First Online Purchaser?

While we are still guessing what makes the dual-color Panda unique, the activity "Who is China's first online car purchaser?" is also underway with great enthusiasm. It is understood that consumers need only pay a deposit of 1888 yuan before March 28, then post tweets on Taobao or Sina Weibo in the specified format to obtain participation eligibility.

The top five participants whose tweets are forwarded the most will simultaneously bid for the designated item at the event site. The first successful bidder will receive a certificate signed personally by Li Shufu and Jack Ma titled "China's First Online Car Purchaser." Moreover, all five participants will qualify for an overseas trip for two, with the first participant receiving a Greece trip for two worth 30,000 yuan.

Will the opening of the official Global Hawk flagship store become the ignition point for China's automobile industry to collectively embrace the internet? Can Geely lead Chinese automotive marketing into a new era? We shall wait and see.
