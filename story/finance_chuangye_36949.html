Geography -

Maotai Town is surrounded by plateaus over 1,000 meters in altitude. Here, the terrain drops steeply by more than 300 meters to form a river valley, flanked by mountains in a nearly windless embrace, making it one of the rarest places for brewing wine. Moreover, Maotai Town has a very special geological structure: experts have determined that the purple sandstone and conglomerate formed 70 million years ago, evolving into a surface layer of purple soil with moderate acidity and alkalinity, good water permeability, dissolving multiple beneficial trace elements while filtering out pure, sterile, and clear spring water. The conclusion of soil experts from the Chinese Academy of Sciences after their on-site investigation was — Maotai’s purple calcareous soil is rare nationwide, forming an important basis for the production of Maotai Town’s sauce-flavored liquor.

Water Source -

Additionally, brewing cannot be done without water, which is one of the main components of finished liquor. Good water quality not only improves the quality of the liquor but also imparts a special flavor to it. Maotai Town is located by the Chishui River, known as the "River of Fine Wines" due to the numerous wineries around it producing many renowned brands. This water system contains over 170 types of clean, unpolluted trace elements and minerals and has been designated as a national ecological protection zone, prohibiting factories that could pollute the area.

Climate -

More uniquely, the humid and sultry climate of southern Guizhou has formed a distinctive microbial community. These microorganisms reproduce on the yeast cakes and raw materials, along with their complex biological metabolic mechanisms, making the flavor profile of Maotai Town's sauce-flavored liquor more intricate and harmonious. This is something other places cannot replicate. In practice, even if factories are established outside Maotai Town in Guizhou using the exact technology and processes of Maotai Town's sauce-flavored liquor, they cannot produce authentic Maotai Town sauce-flavored liquor.

Process -

Any pure bottle of sauce-flavored Maotai Town liquor takes at least five years from production to market release. Its brewing process is entirely natural, strictly adhering to the schedule of making yeast cakes during the Dragon Boat Festival and adding ingredients during the Double Ninth Festival. It undergoes nine rounds of steaming, eight rounds of adding yeast, eight rounds of stacking and fermenting in pits, and seven rounds of extracting liquor, lasting a full year. Afterward, it is aged in cellars for at least three more years. Skilled brewers carefully craft it, repeatedly adjusting it, using advanced distillation techniques and equipment to refine it before it is sold on the market. The time from production to market release is at least five years. The complexity of the brewing process is something no other liquor can achieve, making it the most outstanding and unique aspect of Maotai Town's sauce-flavored liquor.

2. Brand沉淀showcasing prestige

■ In 135 BC, Han envoy Tang Meng presented Goujiang to Emperor Wu, who praised it as "deliciously sweet," thus Maotai Town's sauce-flavored liquor became famous worldwide.

■ In 1704, Qing scholar Zheng Zhen encountered Maotai Town's sauce-flavored liquor, writing after drinking it, "the best wine in Guizhou."

■ In 1915, Maotai Town's representative, "Maotai Liquor," won gold at the "Panama International Exposition" after throwing a bottle of Maotai to assert national pride, alongside British whiskey and French brandy, being hailed as one of the world's three major distilled fine wines.

■ In 1996, in the commemorative event marking the 80th anniversary of the Panama International Exposition, "International Wine Appraisal Conference," Maotai Town's representative "Maotai Liquor" again won the special gold medal in first place, being praised as an 80-year-old golden medal.

■ In May 2006, Maotai Liquor brewed in Maotai Town was included in the first batch of national intangible cultural heritage lists.

■ From ancient times to the present, Maotai Liquor brewed in Maotai Town has been used countless times in significant national events, establishing and consolidating Maotai Town's position in China's liquor industry, and was officially recognized as "China's Wine Capital" in July 2004.

3. Origin of Sauce Aroma Liquor

Sauce aroma liquor originates from the Maotai Town process, hence it is also called "Maotai aroma type," with "Guiyang Maotai Liquor" as its main representative. Various aromatic substances in this type of liquor have higher content and a wide variety, resulting in rich aromas composed of high-boiling-point acidic substances and low-boiling-point alcohols.
