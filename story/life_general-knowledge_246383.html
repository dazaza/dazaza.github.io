Choose wooden floors carefully to avoid falling into nine major pitfalls

by longersing on 2009-10-11 18:35:45

The home decoration of modern people is increasingly inclined to choose wood products, including wooden crafts and decorations, as well as floors used for decoration. However, there are usually some difficulties in choosing laminate flooring and solid wood flooring. The article "Nine Pitfalls to Avoid When Selecting Wood Flooring" is designed to help you avoid these pitfalls.

Consumer Pitfall One - Pursuing thicker panels when selecting flooring

The thickness of the top layer of a three-layer solid wood composite floor should ideally be between 3-4 mm. Therefore, choosing an appropriate panel thickness is the best strategy, rather than excessively pursuing panel thickness.

Consumer Pitfall Two - Separation between sales and installation parties

If the sales party and the installation party are not from the same unit, once problems occur with the flooring, both parties may blame each other, causing consumer distress and regret. Therefore, it's best if the sales and installation parties are from the same entity.

Consumer Pitfall Three - Overly critical of color differences, pursuing consistent texture

The top layer of a solid wood composite floor is made from natural wood. Trees differ in planting location, sunlight exposure, temperature, and humidity, resulting in varying wood colors. Additionally, boards cut from the same wood can also vary in color and texture due to the cutting position. Generally, sapwood is lighter in color, while heartwood is darker, so solid wood composite floors objectively exhibit color differences and uneven patterns, which is a natural phenomenon.

Therefore, when selecting solid wood composite floors, as long as there are no obvious color differences, there's no need to overly demand uniformity in color.

Consumer Pitfall Four - Adding plywood (commonly known as large core board) under the joist method for better foot feel

Some consumers excessively pursue foot comfort by adding plywood under the joists. In reality, the quality of plywood varies greatly, and its quality directly affects the installation quality of solid wood composite floors. If you must add plywood, make sure to select products from well-known large enterprises.

It is recommended to use the commonly adopted floating installation method.

Consumer Pitfall Five - Excessively pursuing expensive wood species

There are dozens of wood species available for solid wood composite floors on the market, which can be overwhelming. Different tree species have varying prices, properties, and materials, and more expensive species do not necessarily perform better. Consumers should purchase based on their living environment, decoration style, personal preferences, and financial capabilities.

Consumer Pitfall Six - Focusing only on purchasing, neglecting installation

There are various methods for installing three-layer solid wood composite floors, such as direct adhesive method, joist method, and floating method, among which the floating method is the most common. Regardless of the chosen method, it's essential to select a good construction team. Adhere to the principle of six non-installations: (1) Do not install if walls are wet or leaking, and the ground is not dry or level; (2) Do not install during mixed construction; (3) Do not install if substandard auxiliary materials are used; (4) Do not install if the schedule is too tight to implement procedures; (5) Do not install if product quality issues are detected; (6) Do not install if absolute flatness and no color difference are required.

Consumer Pitfall Seven - Neglecting maintenance, leading to human-induced problems

Mainly due to improper use and maintenance by consumers, such as washing the floor with water, leaks from heating pipes onto the floor, or long-term disuse after installation.

Consumer Pitfall Eight - Misunderstanding solid wood composite flooring as laminated flooring, believing it performs worse than solid wood flooring

In fact, the structure of a three-layer solid wood composite floor is reasonable, possessing the advantages of solid wood flooring while offering better stability and resistance to underfloor heating.

Consumer Pitfall Nine - Misunderstanding that solid wood composite flooring has high formaldehyde content and is not environmentally friendly

In fact, as long as the formaldehyde release of solid wood composite flooring meets national standards, it can be used safely without posing harm to human health.
