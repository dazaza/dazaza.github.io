Lu Xingyu's Donated School Bus

Lu Xingyu (Executive Chairwoman and Secretary General of the China-Africa Hope Project, Secretary General of the Global Chinese Future Leaders Club)

Reason: Donated a school bus to Wuan Primary School in Nuojiang Town, Tongjiang County.

"I'm not afraid of what others say, if you have the ability, come and show off too"; "Only losers have so much envy and jealousy"... These sharp and confident words come from Lu Xingyu - the 85's girl who is cursed as "Lu Meimei" online. Yesterday, under the witness of many media outlets, Lu Xingyu donated a 250,000 yuan "Safe School Bus" to Wuan Primary School in Nuojiang Town, Tongjiang County, Sichuan Province.

In an exclusive interview with Chengdu Evening News in Tongjiang, Lu Xingyu strongly responded to netizens' doubts about her "showing off charity" and "high-profile charity".

First Impression:

Carrying a Black Assistant and an LV Bag

Valuing Cleanliness of Bedding

Lu Xingyu arrived at Guangyuan from Beijing at 3 PM on June 6th. This time she only brought one black assistant, who helped her put her handbag into the car. The lower right corner of the handbag was printed with a dark floral LV logo. At this moment, Lu Xingyu looked plain-faced, with her hair tied back, like a neighbor's girl.

The next day during the donation, she changed to a semi-furry black small handbag, without carrying that large LV bag. She shopped on Taobao at: http://shop36036899.taobao.com She wore light makeup, a pair of famous brand shoes with panda heads on the tongues, "I like pandas."

After entering the hotel, Chengdu Evening News noticed that Lu Xingyu valued cleanliness. She carefully checked the bedding, found a black mark on one pillow, and asked the attendant to replace it.

At 9:50 PM last night, when Lu Xingyu gave staff members local specialties of Tongjiang silver ear fungus, she had already changed into a white high-end suit. She said that changing clothes for dinner was her habit, presenting a mature and gentle image of a well-bred lady.

Exclusive Interview with Lu Xingyu:

Response to the Question of High-Profile Charity after the "Weibo Incident":

"We Have No Legal or Moral Issues to Clarify"

Chengdu Evening News: Where does the money for donating school buses come from?

Lu Xingyu: My father supports me.

Chengdu Evening News: How do you supervise the donated school buses later? Some netizens mentioned whether the gasoline problem will be solved?

Lu Xingyu: Supervision is carried out by the local education bureau and traffic management department. We have communicated with these two departments, they will strictly supervise. As for gasoline, the school and the education bureau will solve it, I just donate the car.

Chengdu Evening News: For this donation of school buses, there are still people who doubt it, saying that you're doing this for show and fame. What do you think?

Lu Xingyu: Only losers always envy and hate others. As long as it can mobilize more people to participate in charity, I think it's worth it. I'm not afraid of what others say. If you have the ability, come and show off too.

Chengdu Evening News: A kind of crisis PR awareness is, cold handling, waiting for things to calm down slowly. But after the Weibo incident, why did you and your father respond so actively instead?

Lu Xingyu: We have no legal or moral issues, we must clarify.

Chengdu Evening News: Some netizens say you're "showing off charity", thinking that "doing charity doesn't need to be shown off", feeling that you're doing good deeds quite high-profilely now.

Lu Xingyu: Am I high-profile? Is media exposure our high-profile? As long as it mobilizes more people to participate in charity, it should be encouraged.

Chengdu Evening News: Have you reflected on why, until now, whatever you do or say is questioned?

Lu Xingyu: It's related to social reasons, people's psychology has deep-rooted inferiority. I think the criticism is not aimed at me personally, but at the matter of charity, I'm just one of the points. I think it doesn't matter, why punish yourself with others' mistakes, walk your own path and let others talk.

Chengdu Evening News: Who pays your regular salary? Is it paid from the 10% management fee of the donations?

Lu Xingyu: No, the 10% management fee of the donations is managed by the Youth Foundation. My salary is paid by my father's company, which is Tianjiu Roshang Group.

Chengdu Evening News: Do you drive to work usually? What kind of car do you drive?

Lu Xingyu: Sometimes the driver sends me, sometimes I drive myself. The car I drive is not convenient to disclose, it's for safety considerations, and I don't want to show off.

Chengdu Evening News: Do you still need to disguise yourself when going out now?

Lu Xingyu: Only in that month last year when the incident came out, later it wasn't necessary. I don't look like a fairy descending from heaven, I don't look aggressive, I look very kind, now it's not necessary anymore.

Behind the "High-Profile Charity"

"Staff Member" Calls Our Newspaper:

Lu Xingyu Comes to Sichuan to Donate a Car, Come and Cover!

Lu Xingyu's trip to donate the school bus was relatively high-profile - multiple media outlets were invited to report on the scene. Around four o'clock in the afternoon, three or four days before the event, the Education Weekly Department of our newspaper received a landline call starting with 010. A person claiming to be a network staff member proactively informed us of Lu Xingyu's upcoming donation activity itinerary, asking if we could send a reporter to cover it.

According to the Weibo staff, after the Gansu school bus incident on November 16, 2011, Weibo immediately launched the "Safe School Bus" initiative campaign. Soon after, Lu Xingyu posted a message on Weibo, willing to donate a school bus in response to the "Safe School Bus" campaign. "She wanted to join herself, so we contacted her. She had such thoughts, it's also a good thing for the children, we wouldn't refuse."

This time, multiple media outlets were present to report. Was contacting media coverage proposed by Lu Xingyu herself or Weibo's idea? The staff member said that it was the result of communication between both parties, "After all, it's a good thing, and it's positive for her."

On this trip, apart from the 250,000 yuan for the school bus donation, did Lu Xingyu pay any additional activity fees to Weibo? The staff member said no, all accommodation, food, and transportation were covered by Weibo, "Lu Xingyu and her assistant's air tickets to Sichuan were provided by us." The journalist continued to ask if Weibo was the pusher behind Lu Xingyu, the news publicity team. The other party replied: "We have so many people we can contact, she is so controversial, why would we push her? We can only show that she is our user, respecting all her personal actions. Weibo is not her behind-the-scenes team."
