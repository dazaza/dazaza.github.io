There are two interesting parts in this New Year film. The first is the insert song "Shiretoko Travel Sentiment," originally sung by Japanese singer Kato Tokiko. It once swept Japan. Why did Director Feng use a Japanese song in the movie?

The second is near the end of the movie, when Mr. Wu drives away. At that time, I worried that such intense filming might lead to a car accident or something, but the car slowed down more and more, eventually stopping completely. Mr. Wu cried silently...

At this point in the movie, Mr. Wu became the secondary protagonist in the film's expression. A movie that satirizes worldly affairs and admires love suddenly reflects on the friendship of middle-aged men. Is this a branch or the main trunk? How can a Chinese person singing a Japanese song move Chinese audiences? It's the melody of the song. To be precise, Chinese people don't know what "Shiretoko" means, nor can they understand the lyrics, but the melody moves people. The melody isn't just the tune; it also includes the emotion and scene.

"Want not to laugh, get anesthesia." This is Director Feng's evaluation of "If You Are the One." Indeed, it's a New Year film filled with laughter. Humor permeates many aspects of humanity within it. On the surface, it seems very funny, even laugh-out-loud funny, but what lies behind the laughter? That's warmth.

Although the movie contains many jokes, they exist solely to tell a warm story — how Ge You wins over the beautiful Shu Qi and makes her stop clinging to a hopeless romance. In the latter half of the movie, the pace slows down. Qin Fen, played by Ge You, and Xiao Xiao, played by Shu Qi, embark on a romantic journey in Hokkaido. At the end of the journey, Qin Fen says, "I've fallen in love with you." Xiao Xiao leans on Qin Fen's shoulder. Two lonely souls lean on each other, making the moment very touching.

I've never considered Director Feng to be adept at playing with camera lenses, but this movie has definitely changed that old image. The cinematography in the movie becomes increasingly cinematic, increasingly beautiful and captivating.

It's truly inspiring, truly brave, and also truly heart-wrenching. There are many leftover men and women like Old Barry waiting for this movie to fulfill our romantic hopes for love.
