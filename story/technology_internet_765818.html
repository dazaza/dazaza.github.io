Let's go on a trip with Google Street View this weekend.

by anonymous on 2013-08-26 13:58:47

The weekend is coming up. Do you have any plans to go out after a tiring week?

However, it's summer now, either with scorching heat or typhoons, so it's not that easy to go out. For those who decide to stay home in front of the computer, you can also take a tour through Google Street View. Here we've compiled some interesting Google Street View locations recently covered by iFanr and other media. You can click in when you're free or need a break from work. (Note: Links in the text will directly lead to relevant Google Street Views.)

The collision of technology and magic, Harry Potter's Diagon Alley

As one of the most popular movie series in history, "Harry Potter" has shown us a magical world beyond the dull Muggle world. Unfortunately, the movie series has come to an end.

Fortunately, Google Street View collaborated with the film company Warner Bros. to bring the iconic scene of Diagon Alley from the movie to fans. It took Warner Bros. three months to build this famous movie set, while creating nearly 20,000 small items displayed in the shop windows of Diagon Alley took six months.

Inception-style ruins, Japan's Hashima Ghost Island

Hashima Island is an island affiliated with Nagasaki City in Japan, covering only 6.3 hectares. Due to its resemblance to a battleship, it is also known as "Battleship Island." In 1890, the Mitsubishi conglomerate bought Hashima Island to mine its abundant coal resources. To facilitate workers' lives, Mitsubishi built a large number of reinforced concrete buildings on the island, including residential buildings, schools, hospitals, cinemas, and other living facilities, forming a miniature city. At its peak population, it reached 5,267 people.

Later, Japan's main energy consumption shifted from coal to oil, and the island's coal mine closed in 1974, with all residents evacuating Hashima Island. This ghost town resembles the ruins in "Inception," but more like a scene from "Skyfall" in the James Bond series.

Finding Nemo underwater, experiencing Hawaiian charm

Google launched the Underwater Street View project last September. Currently, Google's Underwater Street View covers six areas, including Oahu and Maui in the Hawaiian Islands, as well as the surrounding seas of the Great Barrier Reef.

In addition to conducting scientific research and obtaining data, the Underwater Street View aims to raise environmental awareness. Nowadays, the marine environment is deteriorating, and issues such as rising sea levels and ocean pollution urgently need to be addressed.

Visitors on the Iceberg, exploring Mount Everest

When you open Google Maps and type Kala Pattar located west of Mount Everest in Nepal into the search bar, you'll find that there is a place supporting Google Street View amidst these snow-capped mountains. Opening the street view here feels like being on a sacred mountain over 5,000 meters above sea level. Snow-covered gravel is accompanied by an abandoned hiking pole, and colorful prayer flags flutter on distant mountaintops.

This street view offers an opportunity for those who admire Mount Everest but cannot climb it to explore.

Failed to step in the snow, cherry blossoms near the Akakura Mountains

Last winter, many marketing accounts on Weibo talked about a strange "Snow Corridor" in Russia and exaggerated its height. Later, I checked and found that this "Snow Corridor" was not in Russia but around the Akakura Mountains in Japan. While writing this article, I thought if Google Street View could reach here, so I searched for a long time on Google Maps but didn't find the "Snow Corridor" street view. However, as compensation, I accidentally found a beautiful street view with blooming cherry blossoms, which was also very lovely.

Let's go to the zoo together

Google Street View has also entered zoos, not just in the U.S., but also at the Chengdu Research Base of Giant Panda Breeding in China. However, Google Street View is not a live broadcast. You won't see the cute pandas eating bamboo dynamically here.

Compared to browsing the zoo on your computer, I would recommend visiting a real-life zoo with family and friends.

On weekends, watching a movie at home is also nice. I recommend the zoo-related movie "We Bought a Zoo," featuring young girls, mature women, and uncles. If you haven't watched it, you can relax this weekend.

Additionally, during the image capture process for Google Street View, some bizarre, eerie, or funny pictures often appear. For this reason, the Mashable website has collected over twenty interesting or quirky street view images, which you can check out when you have time.

Happy weekend.
