Intraday Characteristics: This week, the complex and varied news has caused the stock index to exhibit characteristics of wide fluctuations. Due to the sequential release of economic data, the lower-than-expected newly added credit volume in March and the first quarter GDP growth rate that reached the upper limit of expectations have all created certain disturbances to the upward movement of the stock index. On Friday, the steady launch of the stock index futures was offset by the new round of regulation on real estate by the management level, causing the stock index to decline and adjust. Ultimately, the Shanghai Composite Index closed this week at 3130.3 points, down 15.05 points, with a decrease of 0.48%, and a trading volume of 747.4 billion yuan; the Shenzhen Component Index closed at 12416.86 points, down 138.26 points, with a decrease of 1.10%, and a trading volume of 603.5 billion yuan. Due to the wide fluctuations in the stock index, the competition between bullish and bearish forces became increasingly intense, resulting in a significant year-on-year increase in the trading volume of both markets.

Observation of Market Conditions: This week, the real estate sector, which continued to be hit hard by policy regulations, once again led the declines, falling more than 4% for the entire week, dragging down the building materials and home appliance sectors. However, due to the continuous rebound in international commodity prices and oil prices, the non-ferrous metal and coal sectors further strengthened, especially gold stocks, which showed extremely strong trends. In a volatile market, defensive stocks gained favor from funds, and sectors such as food and beverages, agriculture, forestry, animal husbandry, fisheries, medicine, and transportation exhibited relatively strong performance. Meanwhile, technology stocks, which had performed strongly earlier, faced selling pressure from profit-taking under the backdrop of the launch of stock index futures, resulting in larger individual stock adjustments. On Friday, due to the impact of negative policies on the real estate sector, heavyweight blue-chip stocks showed sluggish performance, causing the stock index to briefly dip to 3121 points. Additionally, with the realization of stock index futures, futures companies and futures-related concept stocks collectively adjusted. Outlook for the Future Market: Overall, with the release of economic data, the expectation for policy regulation continues to strengthen, increasing the likelihood of raising the reserve requirement ratio or interest rates again in the second quarter. At the same time, the pressure for RMB appreciation is also growing. Under the backdrop where stimulus policies cannot be completely withdrawn, the situation of abundant liquidity will continue to be maintained. The current price-to-earnings ratio of CSI 300 at 19.68 undoubtedly increases the weight towards liquidity tilting towards the stock market. Moreover, the introduction of stock index futures and margin trading has improved the trading system of A-shares through its short-selling mechanism. Although the trading currently remains relatively inactive, given the high activity of stock index futures as a trading variety, its impact on the stock market still deserves continued attention.

Therefore, in the short term, the balance of power between bullish and bearish forces will tend to stabilize, and the market is likely to enter a period of narrow fluctuations, maintaining within the range of 3100 to 3200 points. It is recommended to continue paying attention to the performance opportunities of heavyweight blue-chip stocks, especially second and third-line blue-chip stocks, and to keep an eye on defensive varieties such as food and beverage, medical biology.
