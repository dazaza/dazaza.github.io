LG Expected to Launch G Pad Tablet Next Month, Aiming for Monthly Sales of 100,000 Units

by anonymous on 2013-08-19 15:18:03

Beijing, August 18th - According to foreign media reports, sources have revealed that LG will release its tablet, the G Pad, at the upcoming IFA electronics show in Berlin next month, with a sales target of 100,000 units per month.

Techblog.gr cited insiders as saying that the LG G Pad tablet will feature an 8.3-inch display with a resolution of 1920 x 1200 pixels, supporting full HD playback, and will be equipped with a quad-core Snapdragon 800 processor and 2GB of RAM.

Reports indicate that LG's tablet is expected to be officially unveiled at the IFA electronics show in Berlin from September 6th to 11th, continuing the G series product naming convention. There is currently no clear information about the operating system for the G Pad tablet; however, it is highly likely that this tablet will run on the Android system, similar to the G2 smartphone released earlier this month. In fact, LG has always been a member of the Android camp.

The Korea Herald cited sources stating that based on the company's production plans, LG has set a sales target of 100,000 units per month for this tablet. However, LG has the capability to double this number.

In 2011, LG launched a tablet called the Optimus Pad. However, this tablet performed poorly in the market and struggled in competition with Apple's iPad and Samsung's Galaxy Tab.

According to the latest data published by Strategy Analytics, although Apple's iPad is still considered the leading brand in the industry, Android tablets now account for 67% of all tablet shipments, meaning that LG is in a favorable position in the new round of competition.

Despite the challenges in achieving success with its tablets, LG can draw encouragement from its smartphone business. IDC data shows that LG sold a record-breaking 12.1 million smartphones in the second quarter of 2013, ranking third among all brands by shipment volume in that quarter. (kirk)
