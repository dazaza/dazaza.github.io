Precautions for using magnetic drills with hollow drill bits

by pp70gaugna on 2012-03-07 16:56:24

Definition of Magnetic Drill

A magnetic drill, also known as a magnet base drill, magnetic iron drill, suction iron drill, magnetic drill, hole opener, magnetic electric drill, steel plate drill, core extraction drill, and annular drill, among others. Magnetic drills have high drilling efficiency, can ensure processing accuracy, are easy to operate, and are safe and convenient to use. They are mainly used in situations where hand drills and drilling machines are not suitable for drilling operations. Compared with regular electric drills, magnetic drills can significantly reduce working pressure, improve work efficiency, and save labor.

Magnetic Drill Working Principle

When powered on, the magnetic drill generates thousands of kilograms of magnetic suction force perpendicular to the bottom surface of the magnetic base through electromagnetic effects, firmly adsorbing it onto the surface of steel plates or structural components for processing, serving to fix the machine. The stronger the magnetic suction force, the more stable the body of the magnetic drill will be during operation, resulting in higher drilling accuracy. By turning on the motor start switch, the rotor inside the machine motor rotates at high speed, driving the rotation of the drive shaft through a gear set, which in turn drives the drill bit to rotate and cut holes at the required drilling location. Compared to flame cutting holes, the holes drilled by magnetic drills have higher precision, belong to physical cutting, do not cause chemical reactions, and do not deform the material.

Precautions when using hollow drill bits:

1. Before starting to drill, make sure that the tool is fully installed without any looseness or clamping issues.

2. When using a magnetic base drill, ensure there are no metal shavings under the magnetic block, the adsorption surface is flat, and the machine does not wobble or fail to fully adsorb.

3. Throughout the entire process from the start of drilling until completion, cooling should be maintained sufficiently and not interrupted. Brief cooling can easily cause chipping of the tool.

4. At the beginning of drilling, the feed should be slow and steady. After cutting 1-2 mm, the feed speed can be increased. When retracting the tool, appropriately slow down the feed speed, and maintain uniform feeding during the cutting process.

5. When drilling hard alloy steel plates, the reasonable cutting edge linear speed should be above 40 meters per minute, with a minimum of no less than 30 meters. Meanwhile, hard alloys are high hardness materials; care should be taken during storage and use to prevent blade damage, and avoid impact during use.

6. The sharp edges of new tools may chip due to impact. It is recommended to be particularly careful with the first few dozen holes when using a new tool, and feed slowly to prevent chipping. After the blade naturally dulls, normal use can continue. This method can significantly extend the life of the tool.

7. In case of machine stalling during the drilling process, first cut off the power supply, manually reverse the tool slightly to disengage the cutting edge from the cutting area, then lift the motor and remove the tool. After checking for abnormalities, operations can resume. If too many chips are wrapped around the tool body, the tool can be retracted and removed with a hook.

8. The center pin is used for holding the core material. During use, there should be no noticeable vibration. If the center pin bends, it should be replaced promptly. When the core material does not pop out after drilling, gently tap the bottom of the core material to assist in its ejection. If it still cannot be ejected, remove the tool and use the small hole in the handle to gently strike the core material to extract it. Safety should be noted during the core extraction process, and care should be taken not to damage the blade.

9. When drilling deep holes, if resistance is encountered, the tool can be retracted multiple times to assist in chip removal. The entire drilling process must ensure safety and attention to protection.
