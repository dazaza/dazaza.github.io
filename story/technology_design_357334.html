Common faults and countermeasures of special motors for instrument valves

Common faults of special motors for instrument valves:

Low protection rating: The special motor for instrument valves used in electric devices is a fully enclosed squirrel cage structure, short-time intermittent duty (10 min), self-cooling. According to the requirements of GB 4942.1-85, the minimum protection rating is IP44, and the maximum protection rating is IP68. Different working conditions and environments of instrument valves require different protection ratings. A low protection rating will cause the motor cavity to become damp or have dust and other foreign objects invade, causing the motor insulation resistance to decrease and leading to damage.

Improper packaging, transportation, and storage: The packaging of electric devices for instrument valves should have measures to prevent rain, moisture, and dust, and the packaging should be sturdy and reliable. During transportation, there should be measures to prevent rain, and after the product arrives on-site, it should be stored in a well-ventilated and dry place, not outdoors. Debugging or maintenance should not be carried out during rainy weather. After debugging, all fasteners should be tightened to ensure that all electrical parts are sealed tightly and reliably.

Improper selection and adjustment of travel position control for electric devices of instrument valves: There are two important parameters for electric devices of instrument valves. ①The opening and closing torque value should include the actual working torque value of the instrument valve plus the torque value of the instrument valve itself. The size of the opening and closing torque affects the use of the instrument valve; too much force can easily damage it, and too little force can easily cause leakage. In terms of selection and matching, there should be sufficient margin (generally required to be 1.1 to 1.3 times greater than the actual operating torque value of the instrument valve). ②Travel position control is related to the size of the instrument valve, the length of the opening and closing time, and the structural form. The motor matched with the electric device of the instrument valve is a special motor, with a short-time duty cycle, lasting 10-15 minutes. If high load occurs within the short-time duty period, it will cause the motor to overheat, reducing the insulation grade and causing damage.

Mismatch between the electric device of the instrument valve and the motor model: The selected motor model does not match the requirements of the electric device, and there is not enough corresponding margin for the actual output torque. Overloaded operation can easily cause motor damage.

Failure of electrical protection and poor quality of electrical components: The microswitch inside the electric device of the instrument valve is a key component of the control mechanism. When the instrument valve exceeds the travel position, the microswitch cuts off the power supply and provides overload protection. Most electric device manufacturers connect the travel and torque protection in series. When over-travel or over-torque occurs, the microswitch promptly cuts off the control power supply of the AC contactor coil, thus cutting off the main circuit of the motor.

Improper debugging, installation, and use: ①Electric devices for instrument valves are divided into horizontal and vertical installations. In vertical installation, the tail end of the motor is below. When the sealing of the rubber part of the wiring sleeve fails, the lubricating grease in the cavity enters the motor cavity through the cable outlet, easily causing a short circuit in the winding and burning out the motor. ②According to JB 8528-1997 regulations, the handwheel rotation direction of the electric device should be consistent with the output shaft rotation direction, clockwise for closing the valve, counterclockwise for opening the valve. If the actual rotation direction of the motor does not conform to the specifications, then any two phases of the three-phase power supply need to be swapped (just switch them). Otherwise, due to phase reversal, protective measures will fail and cause motor damage. ③Since the special motor for instrument valves operates under high load for a short time, if the continuous debugging time is too long exceeding the rated time, it will lead to motor overheating and damage.

Mismatch between the electric device of the instrument valve and the instrument valve: Multi-turn electric devices for instrument valves are divided into torque type and thrust type. The torque type mostly uses three-claw connection or key connection, and the electric device of the instrument valve does not bear axial thrust. The thrust type mostly uses threaded connection and can withstand significant axial thrust. To prevent interference between the electric device of the instrument valve and the instrument valve, there must be an appropriate gap between the upper part of the mating surface of the instrument valve and the electric device of the instrument valve. Ensure that the worm gear on the output shaft does not move axially, ensuring good meshing of the worm gear center plane, guaranteeing that the transmitted torque reaches the rated output torque value.

Motor internal temperature control protection failure: A temperature control switch is installed inside the motor. When the instrument valve motor is overloaded or runs for a long time, the motor temperature rises. When it exceeds the set temperature value, the temperature control switch immediately cuts off the control circuit to protect the motor.

Establish a periodic operation inspection system: After the electric device of the instrument valve is installed and debugged, a complete maintenance system should be established. For electric devices of instrument valves that are not used for a long time, regularly detect the insulation resistance value of the stator winding of the motor.
