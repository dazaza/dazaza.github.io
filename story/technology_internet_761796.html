Sina Weibo Through the Eyes of a Twitter Engineer

by geekzhang on 2013-05-10 15:02:05

In the eyes of Twitter engineers, there are four main differences between Sina Weibo and Twitter:

1. Twitter advocates the principle of simplicity, while Sina Weibo promotes richness. Sina Weibo seems like a company led by multiple product managers simultaneously.

2. Sina Weibo is like a micro-forum, encouraging discussions and retweets; Twitter does not encourage discussion.

3. Sina Weibo's system design encourages social interaction and showing off, whereas Twitter focuses on news information itself.

4. Twitter has found a precise advertising monetization model and is already profitable. Sina Weibo has yet to find its monetization method.

The following is the full text of the author's article:

No matter how the industry sings the decline of Sina Weibo, at least Alibaba still recognizes this mobile internet ticket in Sina's hands. Sina Weibo was valued by Alibaba at approximately $3.256 billion, and with a rough calculation based on 500 million registered users, this equates to about 40 RMB per user, even higher than Facebook's valuation of $5 per user (about 32 RMB) at the time of its IPO. Sina Weibo, which has won users but lost money, forms a stark contrast with its American counterpart, Twitter; the latter also has about 500 million registered users but has long been profitable through its advertising business.

The author, seeing the recent series of newsworthy events where Twitter has taken center stage (such as the Boston bombing news starting on Twitter), couldn't help but ponder why Sina Weibo has drifted further and further away from its mentor. With this question in mind, the author visited Twitter headquarters and interviewed several Chinese engineers, hoping to hear their thoughts on the differences between Sina Weibo and Twitter.

Twitter's simplicity vs. Sina Weibo's richness

An engineer at Twitter told me that he sees Sina Weibo as a company led by multiple product managers simultaneously. All kinds of new features keep rolling out non-stop (micro-publications, micro-connections, micro-disks, micro-videos, etc.). Sina has greatly enriched Weibo: short posts, long posts, instant chats, reading news, participating in hot topic discussions, making friends, socializing, watching public intellectuals show off, etc. In the eyes of Twitter engineers, Sina Weibo's overall design principle is not very clear: it has gone through many redesigns, with hundreds of functions all over the place, and one wonders how many product managers are trying hard to cram their ideas into the platform.

In contrast, Twitter promotes the principle of simplicity ("Simplify" is one of the company's ten core values). This can be seen from Twitter's interface design. Unlike Sina Weibo, which has various functional recommendations on both sides, Twitter does not focus on additional features but hopes to direct everyone's attention to the tweets in the center, which is where the essence of the information lies. The interviewee told me that Twitter strictly controls its design, nothing can be added casually, and more features do not necessarily mean better. From its birth to now, Twitter has consistently followed one product logic, firmly positioning itself as a mobile-end information broadcasting platform. This blue bird was born with a simple mission: to satisfy people's desire to express and share, telling the world "What are you doing?" through fragmented information. Twitter knows what it wants and what it doesn't want.

Twitter's stubbornness, Sina Weibo's flexibility

A Twitter engineer joked, "I use Twitter to read the tweets themselves (whether they're from my circle of friends or CNN), but I use Sina Weibo mainly for comments and retweets." These interviewees unanimously mentioned Sina Weibo's micro-forum gene; Weibo is seen as a platform that particularly encourages comments and retweets. Posts with little news value, such as "Raise your hand if you have a dog at home," can receive tens of thousands of retweets and comments on Sina Weibo, which is almost unimaginable on Twitter unless the poster is Lady Gaga.

In fact, we rarely see any tweets on Twitter aimed at initiating discussions (such as "Prove you've read the Four Great Classical Novels with one sentence"). Whether it's Lady Gaga, an ordinary person, or Samsung Mobile (Samsung Mobile's verified account), the tweets they post all contain substantive information (or links to such information). They can be descriptions of mood, expressions of status, exclamations, announcements, knowledge, advertisements, etc., but absolutely not "Raise your hand if you have a dog at home."

Regarding Sina Weibo's micro-forum gene, I summarize its most significant differences from Twitter as follows:

1) Twitter's Retweet (retweet) defaults to no additional information, making it a one-click operation. This is quite different from Sina Weibo's retweet function. When we click "retweet" on Sina Weibo, the "//@某某" format immediately appears, encouraging you to add your own comments or attitudes before the "//".

2) Replying to a tweet on Sina Weibo is similar to Twitter's Reply. However, Sina Weibo adds a reply+retweet function; simply check the retweet option when replying, and your response will become an independent tweet in the format of "我回复的东东//@某某".

3) Whether replying or retweeting, Sina Weibo always includes the original text and context, whereas on Twitter, we must click on the information itself to see its context. The former helps everyone understand the origin and development of a post within two or three seconds, allowing quick participation in discussions, while the latter allows you to focus more on a specific information chain without being distracted by unrelated content (often, discussions around a particular comment can divert the original discussion).

4) Each Sina Weibo post directly displays images, the number of comments, retweets, and even views (stimulating people's desire to retweet); Twitter does not do so. While browsing tweets, we only see neatly arranged text, and only by clicking on the tweet itself can we obtain more detailed information such as images and the number of retweets.

Sina plays social games, Twitter focuses on news

In the eyes of Twitter engineers, Sina Weibo often serves as a discussion platform for everyone. Sina cannot let go of its portal gene and artificially adds some topic summaries and guidance in the Weibo platform, such as popular Weibos and trending lists. Seizing on our national tendency to spectate and follow trends, Sina Weibo guides everyone to participate in discussions and get involved through easily operable functions like retweeting+commenting. Sina's various efforts have achieved its micro-forum gene, gathering extremely high popularity for the platform, but of course, it has also caused some trouble.

An interviewee believed that it is Sina Weibo's system design that makes social pretentious behavior easier to benefit from on this platform. Sina Weibo's design strongly encourages retweeting behavior, putting a lot of effort into drawing attention to a single Weibo post, but not spending much thought on organizing information or guiding people to view the information itself in a more objective way. As a result, people tend to superficially participate in performance-type discussions without substantial meaning; back-and-forth exchanges consume emotions rather than truths. Sina Weibo unknowingly fosters a group of large accounts that speak ambiguously, filled with emotional show-off texts and unfortunately becomes a marketing platform for speculators who disregard user experience.

Unlike Sina Weibo, Twitter is even deliberately reducing its social attributes, striving to direct everyone's attention to the information itself (not clicking on a tweet means you won't see pictures, effectively preventing situations where images are used just to attract attention; the number of times a tweet is retweeted is not directly displayed either, not directly encouraging competition or leading users to look for entertainment). Moreover, compared to Sina Weibo, Twitter has put more effort into optimizing its information search function, striving to present the objective development of an event. The Boston bombing case fully demonstrated Twitter's news nature; the first tweet related to the explosion occurred only 20 seconds after the incident, and the first photo of the suspect appeared on Twitter first.

Twitter's profit mechanism, Sina Weibo's monetization expectations

According to industry analysts/experts' estimates, Twitter has started to make profits through advertising and recently secured a multi-year contract with SMG Advertising Company (representing Procter & Gamble, Coca-Cola, Walmart, etc.).

Twitter engineers stated, "Our ads are tweets. Twitter will not display various banners (those flashy ones) on the interface like Sina Weibo does because it harms user experience." Using a perfect and precise ad placement system, Twitter selectively recommends ads that users are most likely interested in based on known user information. In short, user experience is the most important; we strive to ensure that users do not feel interrupted by ads or forced content insertion when accepting ads. Advertisers pay Twitter based on clicks. If an ad's click-through rate remains low, Twitter's algorithm will correspondingly reduce the frequency of its appearance or increase its placement cost. Twitter has created such a mechanism to effectively encourage advertisers to design high-quality ads, increasing click-through rates without affecting user experience, creating a virtuous cycle.

Compared to Twitter's successful business model, Sina Weibo, the leader in news portals, has inadvertently operated topics and gossip on Weibo, fattening up grassroots big accounts and giving away all advertising opportunities to these gossip celebrities. This has made Sina Weibo financially strapped in terms of advertising profits and even forced it to sacrifice user experience to compete with big accounts for food.

Now, apart from advertising, Sina Weibo's other monetization possibilities are still tied to Alibaba. Sina Weibo resembles a fallen goddess beloved by all. Alibaba's investment in Sina Weibo at this time might reverse its monetization predicament. Whether Sina Weibo's monetization strategy diverges further from Twitter may no longer be so important. But ultimately, we can only wait and see.
