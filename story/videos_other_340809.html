[Description] With the dust of telecommunications restructuring finally settling, the competitive landscape of China's telecommunications market has become clear from the fog - a tripartite confrontation. The telecommunications restructuring has brought about two results: full-service operation and the launch of 3G.

In the 2G and 2.5G eras, the potential of value-added services has gradually emerged, much like coral reefs originally hidden underwater that have surfaced due to their continuous growth. The part that has surfaced is gradually becoming larger and higher. In countries where value-added services have developed best, such as South Korea and Japan, the proportion of revenue from operators' value-added services can even reach 40-45%. In China, well-developed operators like China Mobile have reached around 30%.

For 3G, the biggest change it brings is value-added services - there is no essential difference between 3G voice services and 2G/2.5G. It can be said that if there cannot be a breakthrough development in value-added services, it would be hard to describe the development of 3G as "successful."

So, facing 3G, which businesses should operators focus on developing? From the development experience abroad and the characteristics of 3G, value-added services should expand into two key areas in the future: mobile e-commerce and content services. The core of mobile e-commerce is to use the convenience of having a mobile phone at hand to help customers quickly find the products they need - users obtain product information rather than the product itself through their mobile phones, such as mobile ticket booking and mobile food ordering services. Content services, on the other hand, present more entertainment features, with products being carried by mobile phones - users obtain the product itself through their mobile phones, such as mobile newspapers, mobile music, and mobile games.

Comparatively speaking, mobile gaming will be the largest market in content services after mobile music, and "games everywhere" will be an important label for mobile content services. However, the current issue is: what kind of business model should mobile gaming adopt (charge customers? charge merchants? charge for value-added services?)? What should be the position of telecom operators in the value chain (build a platform? develop games?)? How should telecom operators operate mobile gaming?

Based on years of research and practical operational experience in telecommunications operations and value-added services, combined with the characteristics of mobile gaming and the experiences of overseas operators, 3-info attempts to outline the blueprint for mobile gaming response strategies and answer the above key questions one by one.

This report is one of a series of reports on 3G operations and product strategies - the "Development Strategy Report for Mobile Gaming Facing 3G." If you are interested, you can visit our website or contact us directly to learn more about other reports.
