[Data] Music master -- Kenji Kawai

by jiee on 2007-05-24 18:14:19

Producer of animated music. Born in 1957, he studied atomic force at the Department of Engineering of Tokai University, but dropped out midway because he was not interested in it. After entering the Sangme Conservatory of Music, he dropped out again and formed a rock band called Muse. After the band broke up, he began to compose music for some companies. During this period, Kenji Kawai also designed stage music for the famous voice actor SAN (Japanese) Yuo Ji, and began to work on animation music. < BR> < BR> His first musical animation was the OVA COSMOS PINK SHOCK. In 1987, he became famous for his role in the music production of the live-action film Red Glasses, which was supervised by Mamoru Oshii. Since then, he has been involved in the music production of a series of Oshii's works, such as Mobile Police, Ghost in the Shell, and Long Live The Ancestors, and as these works have become a sensation in Japan and abroad, he has also become an internationally renowned industry figure. In addition, Kenji Kawai has also produced scores and songs for several other anime works. From the early "Chaotic Horse 1/2", "Together for a moment", to the later "Bloodsucker Mixi", "Genius Baby", such as Kawai Kenji can be familiar with the soundtrack work of various types of animation music producers, in the Japanese animation industry is also quite rare. < BR> < BR> Note: Recently, domestic films have also begun to invite Kenji Kawai to compose music, such as Seven Swords, Ink Attack, there are music composed by Kawai.
