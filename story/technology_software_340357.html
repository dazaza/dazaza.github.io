But they all have a "noble status"

by nealge5 on 2010-04-05 20:55:46

Currently in the provincial capital beverage market, the "tripod" situation of carbonated beverages, fruit juice drinks, and tea drinks has long been broken. Health drinks represented by the newly emerged vitamin drinks in the recent two years have become the new main force. However, since the moment Vitamin C drinks became popular, they have been filled with consumers' doubts about price and nutritional components. But amid all the controversies, Vitamin C drinks quickly took over the beverage market. Now, it has been two years since the debut of Vitamin C drinks. In early March 2010, when reporters revisited the beverage market, they found that many Vitamin C products produced in July 2009 were still on sale in the fruit juice section, forming a stark contrast with the beverages produced after 2010 that had gone through a hot selling period during the Spring Festival. This situation continued until mid-March, showing that consumers no longer bought Vitamin C drinks to replenish their Vitamin C.

[Market Review]

In 2008, the beverage market saw the emergence of a "dark horse"

"Vitamin C drink" stood out

At the beginning of 2008, when various beverages were still fiercely competing for the market, a lemon-flavored beverage quietly launched under the concept of replenishing Vitamin C. Although this beverage was not promoted by handsome men or beautiful women at the time and was priced 50% higher than other beverages, it became famous overnight with its slogan of "each bottle contains the equivalent amount of Vitamin C as five and a half fresh lemons", sweeping a large number of young consumers.

At that time, major supermarkets in the provincial capital unanimously opened special sales sections for it, giving it special treatment. Of course, its sales did not disappoint the distributors at all, almost surpassing all fruit juice drinks to become the top seller.

When a tempting cake is placed before them, merchants will surely rush to grab it. As expected, following the first product that pioneered the new category of Vitamin C drinks in the Chinese market, various beverage companies also followed suit. "HELLOC", "Lemon ME"... In 2009, the shelves in the provincial capital's beverage counters were filled with a large number of drinks featuring Vitamin C as their selling point. Their prices were also aligned with the first product, selling at 30%-50% higher than regular fruit juices.

It was understood that these Vitamin C replenishing drinks were different from traditional fruit juice drinks. Previously, the concentration of popular fruit juice drinks on the market was mostly below 10%, while the concentration of these "Vitamin C drinks" reached above 12%. Besides having a stronger sweet and sour taste, these fruit juices also carried the "health" tag, successfully entering the functional beverage series, with consistently high sales, becoming a dark horse in the beverage market.

Slower Sales of Vitamin C?

Products from 2009 are still being sold

Facing the nearly two-year-long hot sales from 2008 and 2009, in March 2010, when reporters revisited the "Vitamin C drink" market, they found that the sales of Vitamin C drinks on the shelves in the provincial capital had significantly dropped. Products produced in the second half of 2009 were everywhere, and the scene of few consumers showing interest seemed to suggest that these once-popular drinks had fallen into a "low valley".

During the interviews, reporters visited major supermarkets and community supermarkets in the provincial capital and found that most of the "Vitamin C drinks" on display in early March were products made in the second half of 2009. The earliest production date was July 2009, and products produced in October and November 2009 were also common. Only one brand had products made in January 2010. However, facing these "old" products, reporters did not find any significant discount sales.

The investigation revealed that the situation where the production dates of Vitamin C drinks remained stagnant continued until mid-March. It was only then that large supermarkets in the provincial capital gradually updated their Vitamin C drink inventory. The leading year in the production dates of various brands of Vitamin C drinks changed from 2009 to 2010. However, even today, some small supermarkets in the provincial capital still sell Vitamin C drinks with production dates from 2009.

In contrast to Vitamin C drinks, other beverage products like carbonated drinks and fruit juices experienced a sales peak in February 2010 during the Spring Festival. Currently, the production dates of products on the market have largely been replaced with those from after 2010. Compared to this, the update in production of Vitamin C drinks appears to be slower.

Children and young people make up the main market

Relevant experts: consumer views are gradually returning to rationality

From the launch of the first Vitamin C drink to now, there are already no fewer than five brands of Vitamin C drinks available on the market. Although these products come from different manufacturers, they all have an "aristocratic identity". For example, a regular 445ml or 450ml Vitamin C drink sells for more than 4 yuan, which is about 1 yuan higher than other pure fruit juice drinks and nearly 50% higher than regular carbonated drinks.

It was learned that the majority of Vitamin C drink buyers are young people. According to supermarket sales staff, the sales peak of this type of beverage was initiated by young white-collar workers because its sweet and sour taste is favored by young women, and Vitamin C has whitening effects. Many people buy 3-4 bottles at a time. Statistics show that in 2009, Vitamin C drinks occupied 20% of the fruit juice market, with young people and children making up more than 60% of total sales.

During the interviews, reporters found that since the launch of Vitamin C drinks, their high pricing and nutritional components have been continuously questioned. "We cannot outright deny that Vitamin C drinks cannot replenish Vitamin C in the human body, but the amount of Vitamin C contained in Vitamin C drinks themselves is not much. Moreover, during the manufacturing process and sterilization process, some Vitamin C is lost, so we cannot solely rely on drinks to replenish the daily required amount of Vitamin C." Nutritionist Mr. Liu stated that therefore, to replenish Vitamin C, we should primarily obtain it from fresh fruits and vegetables.

Regarding the current cooling trend in the market sales of Vitamin C drinks, marketing expert Mr. Cui analyzed that since Nongfu Spring fired the first "C" shot, the beverage has heavily relied on its concept marketing strategy. Also, due to its rapid popularity, this type of beverage quickly approached market saturation. Coupled with increasing doubts, consumers' consumption views have gradually shifted from blind enthusiasm to rationality. However, Mr. Cui pointed out that this does not mean that Vitamin C drinks have faced "elimination" within less than two years of entering the market; rather, it highlights the immense competitiveness in the beverage market. Various beverage companies will continue to innovate, and consumers will directly benefit.
