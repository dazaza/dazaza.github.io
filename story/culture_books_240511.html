"Follow Her Journey" - An intense and absurd coming-of-age story.

by jqrxass on 2009-09-20 13:29:58

1991, in the summer at Dai City. Lu Xiaolu, 18 years old, was studying at a technical school and was about to start an internship at a factory. During that summer vacation, he met Yu Xiaoqi, the daughter of his Chinese language teacher. Yu Xiaoqi attended an art technical school and was set to go to Shanghai to study comics. As Lu Xiaolu pursued her, he uncovered a secret that could not be confronted. This secret, like the core of a nut, stubbornly remained within him. Consequently, fights underwent a qualitative change for Lu Xiaolu, escalating layer by layer until the final moment, transforming unconscious violence into conscious revenge... Soon after, the Chinese language teacher passed away due to illness, Yu Xiaoqi eventually went to Shenzhen, and Lu Xiaolu continued wandering around in Dai City.

Journey of Following Her Part One

Due to insufficient classrooms, eight classes had to rotate for lessons. The specific method was: six classes would have academic courses while the other two would have physical education, running on the streets, then rotating afterward. We had to carry our backpacks while running, which was extremely conspicuous. About a hundred students running with backpacks on the street included those wearing high heels, those in bell-bottom pants, long-haired boys, and girls with buzz cuts. To look cool, we all ran with our hands in our pockets, cigarettes dangling from our mouths, cursing along the way, and even stealing things. When the crowd saw us coming, they would panic and make way, and vendors would scatter like rats. To be honest, we were absolutely more arrogant back then than today's urban management officers.

Journey of Following Her Part Two

I never expected that because I loved sleeping in class, I caught his attention. This old man's mind was a bit off; he always hoped to find literary talent among the technical school students, and now I understand what it means to seek fish by climbing a tree. One day he called me to the office, flipped through my composition book, and said, "Your compositions are pretty good, showing great literary potential. Why don't you become the Chinese class representative?" At the time, our class representative was a girl who got expelled for having an abortion, and I inexplicably replaced her. Unfortunately, soon after, it was the end-of-term exams, and there wouldn't be any more Chinese classes in the second academic year. My only experience as a class representative lasted a month before it ended.

Journey of Following Her Part Three

I'm not interested in handsome guys, so I continued asking about the girl with the watermelon knife. Yu Xiaoqi said that Zeng Yuan was her good friend, and they had been roommates for over a year. Her father, that tycoon, was the boss of the famous "Hongyun Restaurant" in Dai City. I knew Hongyun Restaurant; it was located on Liberation Road in the center of Dai City. It was essentially a den of thieves, full of old hooligans. These old hooligans would have breakfast at Hongyun Restaurant every morning, take a bath at the bathhouse at noon, sleep in the afternoon, and come out at night to return to Hongyun Restaurant. They lived like gods.

Journey of Following Her Part Four

I forgot to tell her one thing—Dai Chuan has already started working. His dad found him a job at a neighborhood factory producing candied fruit, where they specifically placed disabled people such as cripples, deaf people, dwarfs, and as for mentally challenged Dai Chuan, he was the first. Their family was thrilled; even an idiot could go to work! Although the pay wasn't much, he spent even less! An idiot working equates to utilizing waste, and this sense of accomplishment is stronger than invention or creation. His dad even wrote him a resume, stating that he was physically imposing and had a steady character. Recently, Dai Chuan has been carrying a black imitation leather bag to work every day, looking quite serious about it.
