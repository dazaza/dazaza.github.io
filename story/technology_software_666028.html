The ex-wife of "Big Worm" only likes to take photos. After four consecutive years of missing the playoffs, the Rockets began their push in the second half of the season. Yao Ming led the Rockets with 16 points, 6 rebounds, and 4 blocks, as six players scored in double digits in a 107-81 rout over the Wizards. After the game, the Rockets players all appeared elated.

Even so, the Blazers did not make a firm decision. They recently offered Wallace a contract worth $60 million, which he flatly rejected. As a free agent at the end of this season, he is hoping for better offers. At this point, the Blazers can only watch helplessly as Wallace leaves, and are forced to trade him.

The Wizards' crushing defeat extended their losing streak against the Rockets to nine games, as they have not won in Houston since December 6, 2001. "It's not the end of the world," said head coach Eddie Jordan. "Our bench played well, but our starters did not show leadership. Their performance tonight was very poor."

Hearing the coach's criticism, Francis was quite humble: "The increase in assists shows that we passed the ball well, creating many open shots for the team."

The Hawks made trades with an eye on the future.

Wallace is one of the most famous "troublemakers" in the NBA. Earlier this season, he harshly criticized Commissioner Stern, saying that black players were enriching white managers, causing a big stir before he was forced to apologize. Last season, after threatening a referee in a game, he was suspended for seven games, the longest suspension ever due to verbal misconduct. In the 2000-01 season, he received 41 technical fouls, setting an NBA record.

When rumors were rampant, Wallace had expressed his stance: "I don't care about current trade rumors! As long as someone needs CTC, they (rumors) will be around me. Do you know what CTC is? It means cutting paychecks." He said this after a game against the Hawks last November 8th.

With the Rockets' current record at 31-22, tied with the Grizzlies for sixth place in the Western Conference, they have a good chance of making the playoffs. However, what impressed Van Gundy the most tonight was the Rockets' 34 assists, "We passed the ball well tonight, creating many good scoring opportunities. Steve created a lot of chances for the whole team."

"The coach gave us a lot of warnings after yesterday's practice," said Francis, who scored 11 points and had 7 assists but was still happy. "He wants us to treat the remaining 30 regular-season games as playoff games, so we can focus on playing and eventually reach the level we hope to achieve."

After the Hawks' trade, their general manager Billy Knight said, "That's the ugliest side of business." You can't say he's being insincere, because the Hawks' moves have both style and deeper meaning.

"It saves us a lot of money," Knight said when talking about the upcoming salary cap space. "This gives us flexibility to get a major star in the summer. We now wield a huge sword."

However, Wallace has been in excellent form in the past five weeks. Since recovering from his sprained left ankle, his mood has improved significantly. His best friend Stoudemire said, "He seems happier, maybe because he feels that no matter whether the team trades him or not, he must show his strength." After hearing the rumors that Wallace would be traded, several teammates stood up for him. Anderson said, "We need him, he's a great asset..." Stoudemire said, "Given his current form, we cannot trade him, it's like the Timberwolves trading KG, the Spurs trading Duncan..."

Wallace's salary this season is $16.98 million, and Person's is $7.7 million. Both contracts expire at the end of the season, meaning the Hawks will have around $24 million in salary cap space next season, providing a good lure for big-name free agents. Abdur-Rahim and Radmanovic's contracts expire in the 2004-05 season; this year, the former earns $13.5 million, and the latter earns $10.16 million. Trading them away is equivalent to starting the rebuilding plan early, as their records have not been satisfactory.

Human emotions cannot match reality. After the trade was completed, Trail Blazers General Manager John Nash said, "We have willing trade partners, and this is our best option right now. If a player is ready to be traded, then let's do it." Head Coach Nate McMillan felt indifferent about the trade, "In the long run, it will make the team better. We need to make the players work together, and when you have such players, it's not easy," he said. The players he referred to were Abdur-Rahim and Radmanovic.

Abdur-Rahim is from Georgia, so playing in Atlanta is like being home. However, during his two and a half seasons with the Hawks, the team's record was only 86 wins and 131 losses. "From a hometown perspective, that (losing) is the worst thing," he said. "I didn't expect it, I wanted to help Atlanta win, but I couldn't achieve it." Without winning games, there's no audience, and finances are tight. Changing head coaches next season won't help, so the Hawks now have to think about their players.

Reported by Shen Zhiyu, late on the 9th, in mid-air over the Atlantic Ocean, Abdur-Rahim was still basking in his joy after grabbing 27 points and 10 rebounds in a game against the Mavericks, helping the Hawks beat the Mavericks for the first time in six years. Suddenly, a thunderclap: he had to pack his bags and go to Portland.

Every rumor hides some truth. Although local media may know something, they dare not write it down recklessly. So the written rumors are often based on some facts. Portland has always been rumored to be getting rid of "Rasheed the Roarer", and now it has become a reality. Abdur-Rahim was the main character swapped for him, along with others including the Hawks' Radmanovic, Dan Dickau, and the Blazers' Wesley Person.

Another reason for the Blazers' move is that young player Randolph has become a force to be reckoned with, becoming an indisputable starting power forward and reaching All-Star level. Wallace might even have to switch to small forward. Two tigers cannot coexist on one mountain, so the Blazers used the same tactic as the Cavaliers sending away Dajuan Wagner – one team can only have one big man.
