Developers are accused of collecting advance fees in disguise by selling discount cards for presale properties

by hj1718004 on 2011-09-28 19:45:50

Pre-sale housing estates lure customers with "10,000 yuan deposit for 100,000 yuan discount" gimmicks; Beijing police solve the largest counterfeit alcohol case in three years, seizing nearly 10,000 bottles of fake liquor.

The pre-sale price is not set yet, but discounts are already being offered through down payment cards. Industry insiders reveal that these are actually disguised intention deposits. Beware of traps that lure customers with low prices and then open at high prices.

“10,000 yuan deposit for 100,000 yuan discount,” “50,000 yuan deposit for 230,000 yuan discount”... Recently, some pre-sale housing projects in Beijing have been advertising such deals to attract home buyers. Simply by depositing money and getting a card, you can enjoy discounts when purchasing a house, which sounds very attractive indeed.

However, industry insiders point out that these so-called down payment cards may seem beneficial, but they are actually disguised intention deposits, full of pitfalls.

### Invention

Price not set, but discounts already announced: Deposit 10,000 yuan to get 100,000 yuan off.

"We're running a promotion now. Deposit 10,000 yuan in cash, and it will offset 100,000 yuan from your house purchase. It's equivalent to a 94% discount, how cost-effective!" Yesterday, at a pre-sale housing project in Daxing District, as soon as I entered, I was bombarded with discount promotions by the staff. The project is expected to officially open for sale in mid-June, even though the price has not been determined yet, the discounts have already been announced early.

When I questioned this discount method, salesperson Ms. Li said that although the opening price has not been finally confirmed, it is "almost certain." "The selling price might be around 19,000 yuan per square meter. If you get the platinum card, after the discount, it will be about 18,000 yuan, saving you more than just a little bit," Ms. Li said.

Subsequently, I visited another pre-sale housing project also located in Daxing District. This self-proclaimed high-end community offers a "50,000 yuan deposit for 230,000 yuan discount," but the housing price has also not been determined yet.

### Follow-up Visit

At least 20+ housing projects in Beijing offer similar card registration discounts.

When I searched online using the term "10,000 yuan deposit for 100,000 yuan discount," various property promotion messages immediately appeared. I roughly counted, and in Beijing alone, there are at least 20+ housing projects where registering for a down payment card allows you to enjoy discounts, including large-scale discounts like "100,000 yuan deposit for 490,000 yuan discount."

Mr. Zhang, who had previously registered for a down payment card, told me that he was "fooled" by the staff when he bought a property in Tongzhou last year.

"At that time, it hadn't officially opened yet, but they said if I deposited 5,000 yuan, it would offset 20,000 yuan from my house purchase. I thought it was a good deal, so I got a down payment card," Mr. Zhang said. Unexpectedly, when the project opened, the price was much higher than expected, and those supposed discounts were eaten up by the price increase.

Ms. Yang, who was similarly tricked by the down payment card, introduced her experience. When she purchased a property in Fangshan, under the recommendation of the housing project staff, she obtained a discount card. "They said the average opening price would be around 14,800 yuan per square meter, and getting the card would give additional discounts. I thought it was a great deal. But unexpectedly, the actual opening price was 15,200 yuan per square meter, and even after the discount, the price was still higher than 14,800 yuan per square meter."

### Revelation

Lure customers with low prices, open at high prices - essentially an "upgraded version" of intention deposits.

Salespeople keep mum about the opening price, only giving a price range while enthusiastically persuading potential buyers that registering for a card will give them discounts. A staff member from the Real Estate Industry Association described this tactic as a "brilliant move that achieves three goals at once."

First, by registering for a card and paying a deposit, developers can collect part of the payment upfront, alleviating financial strain. Second, registering for a card helps lock in potential buyers; even if they regret their decision later, it’s difficult to cancel the card, forcing them to wait patiently until the official opening to select a unit. Most importantly, developers can determine the final selling price based on the number of people showing interest.

An unnamed person in charge of a currently sold-out housing project spoke more frankly: "All kinds of down payment cards are essentially the old intention deposits."

Specifically, by offering card registration discounts, developers can estimate the approximate number of interested clients, and the amount paid for the card becomes a significant factor in determining the housing price. "The higher the amount you pay for the card, the higher the price they might set when the project opens," the individual said. Essentially, down payment cards are used to lure customers with low prices and then open at high prices.

### Official Statement

Beijing Municipal Commission of Housing and Urban-Rural Development: Will penalize non-compliant developers.

Regarding the act of collecting disguised intention deposits before the price is finalized, this practice has already been explicitly prohibited in the "Notice on Improving Issues Related to the Pre-sale System of Commercial Housing" implemented last year by the Ministry of Housing and Urban-Rural Development.

In response, a reporter interviewed the Beijing Municipal Commission of Housing and Urban-Rural Development this morning. They stated that they are aware of the current situation where developers use down payment cards to collect disguised intention deposits, and they are currently conducting a comprehensive investigation. Non-compliant developers will definitely be punished, and specific penalties will be announced after the investigation results are released.

Written by reporter Yang Yunuo.
