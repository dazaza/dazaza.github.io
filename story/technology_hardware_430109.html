Look, there is a pedestrian ahead! Liu Yishou

At around 14:20, when Lü Kai and his colleagues were patrolling near the 468KM mark of the Fuyin Expressway, they noticed a middle-aged woman walking on the highway. Liu Yishou

The weather was quite hot that day, but the middle-aged woman was dressed warmly, carrying a large bag in her hand, walking briskly along the side of the highway. The scene before him made Lü Kai feel something was not right, so he got out of the car to ask about the situation. Liu Yishou

Hello, I am a traffic policeman from Jiangxi Province. Pedestrians are prohibited on highways, please cooperate and leave immediately, thank you! Lü Kai told the woman that walking on the highway was very dangerous and persuaded her to leave quickly. Liu Yishou

The middle-aged woman ignored him and continued walking forward. Lü Kai had no choice but to stop the woman, bring her to a safe place, and then question her. During their conversation, Lü Kai discovered that she was not from Jiangxi, and initially judged that she might be a lost person. Liu Yishou

The woman started by saying she was from a place called Xiaowangzhuang in Hebei Province. Lü Kai then contacted the on-duty police officer at the station to search for a village named Xiaowangzhuang, communicating by phone with the local police station. However, the police station responded that there was no one named Song Limei there. Liu Yishou

In order to obtain accurate information, Lü Kai patiently communicated with her. In subsequent conversations, the woman's thoughts seemed rather chaotic. Sometimes she claimed she was from Hunan Province, other times she said she was from Hubei Province, mentioning more than ten places. Liu Yishou

Just as Lü Kai was at a loss, he surprisingly found that the woman could write many characters. Lü Kai asked her to write down her name, and she neatly wrote down "Song Limei." However, through the population information network, many people were found with this name. Because it was hard to determine which province she was from, it was difficult to find accurate information, so Lü Kai decided to take her back to the station first. Liu Yishou

By now, the sun had already set. Lü Kai had been talking with the woman for more than three hours, not knowing that a long-lasting rescue operation was just beginning. Liu Yishou

Lü Kai's sincere help finally moved her. After eight hours, just as everything seemed about to fall into a deadlock, things took a turn for the better, and the identity of the middle-aged woman was finally revealed. Liu Yishou

In the office of the Third Brigade of the Directly Subordinate Third Detachment of Jiangxi Traffic Police Corps, the police officers brought the middle-aged woman a steaming hot dinner.

She's called Song Limei?!! Liu Yishou

Just as Lü Kai was following up on this information and searching for clues, an unexpected situation occurred. The woman changed her statement, claiming she was called Deng Xiumei. She then wrote down more than 20 names related to her. Lü Kai tried to find information about her parents, but nothing came up online, and no useful information was extracted. Liu Yishou

By now, it was getting late. Looking at the confused eyes of the auntie, Lü Kai felt uneasy inside. According to his previous experience helping others who couldn't even write a single word, Lü Kai managed to help them find their way home. But this auntie had some educational background, yet why couldn't he find any valuable information? The situation became more complicated, leaving a deep question mark in Lü Kai's mind. Liu Yishou

The middle-aged woman stared at the police officers with a depressed look and fiddled with the pen in her hand. Liu Yishou

Auntie, we're here to help you. Do you want to go home? Lü Kai guessed that the middle-aged woman might have suffered a severe shock and had a resistant attitude towards strangers. Liu Yishou

Perhaps moved by Lü Kai's sincerity, she nodded, indicating her strong desire to return home. Liu Yishou

Lü Kai cleared his thoughts and decided to start from her ability to write. He asked her to write down her home address, and she cooperatively wrote down "Xianfeng Commune." Based on this, Lü Kai speculated that she might have been lost for many years. Liu Yishou then searched online and found that the Xianfeng Commune was under the jurisdiction of Xiangtan County in Hunan Province, and had been renamed many years ago. To further confirm the accuracy of the information, Lü Kai asked her which school she graduated from, and she wrote down "Xiangtan No. 9 Middle School" and "Baoyu Primary School." After checking, it turned out that Xiangtan No. 9 Middle School was indeed in Xiangtan County. Liu Yishou

This result left Lü Kai feeling relieved, confirming that her home address was likely around Xiangtan County in Hunan Province. However, when Lü Kai searched for "Song Limei" in Xiangtan County on the public security population information network, he still couldn't find her, leading to another deadlock. Liu Yishou

Lü Kai thought calmly, reorganized every piece of information written by the middle-aged woman, and analyzed that she was likely from Hunan. Liu Yishou

By this time, the clock pointed to 10 PM, and Lü Kai decided to start from the people around her. Liu Yishou

What was the name of your production team leader during the Xianfeng Commune era? Liu Yishou

The middle-aged woman wrote down "Zhao Qingxiang." Lü Kai quickly looked it up online, and the information showed that he was a villager from Shuijiazhuang Village, Jiangshe Town, Yuhu District, Xiangtan County, Hunan Province. The information was accurate! Lü Kai felt he was onto something, so he asked her what Zhao Qingxiang's younger brother's name was, and she quickly wrote down "Zhao Qixiang" on the paper. Liu Yishou

It matched!

Following this, Lü Kai verified her neighbors online, and all these people were from the same village, matching what she had written. Liu Yishou

After 8 hours of effort, the mysterious identity of the middle-aged woman was finally uncovered. Song Limei, born in 1952, was from Shuijiazhuang Village, Jiangshe Town, Yuhu District, Xiangtan County, Hunan Province. Due to suffering a major shock, she had left home and wandered for over 20 years. Liu Yishou

Because it was too late, Lü Kai arranged for her to stay at the Fuzhou Social Assistance Station for rest. Liu Yishou

Going home, she waited for this day for over 20 years. Liu Yishou

On the afternoon of May 7th, after contacting the local police station in Xiangtan, Hunan, Lü Kai quickly got in touch with the middle-aged woman's son and younger brother. Through her family, it was confirmed that she was indeed Song Limei, born in 1952, 59 years old, and because she had been missing for over 20 years, her household registration had been canceled. Upon hearing news of his mother, Pan Liyang was momentarily speechless with excitement and expressed that he would drive to Fuzhou immediately to reunite with his mother after being separated for over 20 years. At around 11:50 PM, Song Limei's family arrived in Fuzhou City. Under the guidance of Policeman Lü Kai, they went to the Fuzhou Assistance Station. Song Zhengnan immediately recognized his sister and couldn't help but shed tears. When Pan Liyang saw his mother, he choked up and kept telling her stories from his childhood. Liu Yishou

According to Song Zhengnan, Song Limei had left home after suffering a major shock, and this absence lasted for 20 years. The family thought she was already deceased. Today, being able to see her sister again, he was deeply moved! Liu Yishou

Song Limei is still alive, and everyone in her hometown knows this news now. They all feel amazed and are looking forward to Song Limei returning home after being separated for 20 years.
