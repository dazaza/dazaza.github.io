Driver sentenced to suspended term for compensation of 370,000 yuan after door collision killed a pedestrian(www.dnfaa.com)

by dehua900 on 2011-05-08 17:10:42

At around 12 o'clock on June 10 this year, Zhao drove his own "Buick" sedan and took his wife to work. When the car traveled from west to east to the motor vehicle lane in front of a residential area in Fangshan District, Zhao saw a store on the north side of the road and suddenly wanted to buy a pack of cigarettes. Therefore, he parked the car on the south side of the road, opened the front left door of the car, and prepared to get out of the car.

After reviewing the case, Fangshan Court believed that Zhao, as a driver, obstructed the passage of other vehicles when temporarily parking and opening the car door, failed to ensure safety, and caused a major traffic accident resulting in one death. His behavior has constituted the crime of causing a traffic accident. Due to Zhao's voluntary surrender, good attitude towards confession, and compensation for the economic losses of the victim's family, he was given a lighter punishment. In the end, Zhao was sentenced to six months imprisonment with a one-year reprieve for committing the crime of causing a traffic accident.

"Opening the car door not only requires checking the rearview mirror, but also looking back once. This is necessary!" said Mr. Yang Jicheng, a coach at Yuanda Driving School.

"There are too many accidents caused by opening the car door. As a coach for many years, I always emphasize this point when teaching students," said Coach Yang.

"When sending family members to work or school during the morning and evening rush hours, drivers must check the rearview mirror first, then look back once to see if there are cyclists or electric bicycle riders behind, and also remind passengers in the same car to pay attention when getting out of the car," said Coach Yang.

Just at that moment, Li was riding an electric bike heading from west to east. The right guard panel of the bicycle scraped against the left door of the car, and Li fell off the bike. Zhao and his wife saw someone injured, so they quickly called the police and an ambulance, and sent Li to the hospital. After unsuccessful rescue, Li died on June 11.

Not checking the rearview mirror when opening the car door will result in deduction of points in the exam.

When learning to drive, do driving school coaches remind trainees to pay attention to the situation behind when opening the car door?

The Fangshan Traffic Brigade determined that Zhao, the motor vehicle driver, bore full responsibility. Afterwards, Zhao reached a one-time compensation agreement with Li's relatives. In addition to paying for the deceased's medical expenses, Zhao compensated Li's relatives with 370,000 yuan in one lump sum.

Mr. Yang said that during the examination, if the trainee does not check the rearview mirror or look back before opening the car door to get out, points will be deducted.

Reported by our reporter Zhang Weina: While driving, due to certain reasons, Zhao parked his car on the side of the road, unexpectedly colliding with an electric bicycle coming from behind when opening the car door. The cyclist was injured and later died.

It is reported today that driver Zhao was sentenced to six months imprisonment with a one-year reprieve by Fangshan Court for committing the crime of causing a traffic accident.
