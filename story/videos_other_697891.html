Hazards of genital warts in women

by givz9587 on 2012-03-08 20:07:44

Hazards of female genital warts: Disease progression: The impact of female genital warts on the individual is very serious. Due to the more secretive nature of the female genitalia, it is not easily detected after infection with genital warts. Therefore, the clinical symptoms of female patients with genital warts are often more severe, making treatment more difficult. At the same time, the female genital area is very warm and humid, which greatly promotes the growth and reproduction of the genital wart virus. Therefore, the disease progresses very quickly, and the probability of malignancy is also relatively high. Clinical data shows that the occurrence of cervical cancer in women is closely related to the virus of genital warts. The virus of genital warts can be found in the pathological smears of almost 98% of female cervical cancer patients.

Hazards to mother and baby:

1. During pregnancy, the influence of estrogen and progesterone in the body causes the local environment to be more conducive to the growth and reproduction of the genital wart virus. Therefore, the onset and progression of the disease occur more rapidly, and the recurrence rate is also higher. During this period, the virus of genital warts can infect the fetus through the placenta directly. The fetus can also be infected by swallowing amniotic fluid contaminated with the virus. Moreover, during childbirth, when passing through the mother's vagina, the fetus will also be infected, posing a serious hazard.

2. Studies have reported that genital warts in pregnant women can cause premature rupture of membranes, leading to chorioamnionitis, which may result in stillbirths.

3. Experts compared the characteristics of genital warts in pregnant women and non-pregnant women, believing that besides sharing common features with non-pregnant women, there are some differences:

①. The course of genital warts is short, and the lesions develop rapidly. Most patients can significantly feel the enlargement of the lesion and the appearance of new lesions;

②. The lesions of genital warts are most commonly seen in the external genitalia, followed by the cervix. The proportion of multi-site concurrent damage is relatively high, with 25.8% occurring in more than two sites, which is 10-15% higher than in non-pregnant women;

③. Typical lesions of genital warts are more common, and the shape of genital warts during pregnancy mostly presents as cauliflower-like or rooster comb-like, often fusing into patches.
