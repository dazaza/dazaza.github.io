**Precautions:**

1. Difficult/easy to deploy.

- The steel lock determines the force required to deploy the baton while in the contracted state. You can adjust the steel lock using pliers (preferably needle-nose pliers) to flatten or widen the steel sheet, thereby adjusting the deployment force. The stability of the locked position after deployment also depends on the deployment force, which can be adjusted according to personal preference.

2. The top section locks too tightly, making it hard to retract.

- Drop the baton vertically onto a flat, hard surface (like concrete) several times to successfully retract it. If the top section locking is too tight and inconvenient, you can apply some pencil powder to the trumpet-shaped opening of the top section using a 2B pencil to reduce the locking tension.
