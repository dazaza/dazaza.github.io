Article Title: Why must Sanjing Oral Liquid advertisements say "Blue bottle"? - Discussing the focusing principle of brand communication

Often, in TV advertisements for Sanjing Oral Liquid, there is a scene like this: A woman bursts into the house like she's been injected with adrenaline and shouts loudly, "Mom, I bought Sanjing Oral Liquid for him!" Her mother looks at her with an expression full of class struggle and asks, "Sanjing Oral Liquid? Is it the blue bottle?" The woman proudly says, "Of course! Sanjing Oral Liquid, they're all blue bottles!"

Why does the advertisement for Sanjing Oral Liquid always have to say "blue bottle"?

In fact, the main function of Sanjing Oral Liquid is "calcium supplementation and zinc supplementation." "Calcium supplementation and zinc supplementation" should be the core of its brand communication, but how much relevance does the well-known "blue bottle" have with the core value of Sanjing Oral Liquid's brand? The huge expenditure on advertising has deeply ingrained the concept of "blue bottle" in people's minds, but how meaningful is it for the accumulation of Sanjing Oral Liquid's brand equity? Has part of Sanjing Oral Liquid's advertising budget been wasted?

This phenomenon cannot help but make us reflect on an issue: How can brand communication focus, and how can every penny of brand communication be spent effectively?

A global trade giant once complained worriedly: I know half of my advertising budget is wasted! But I don't know where it's wasted.

In today's era of diversified information, people are surrounded by vast seas of information, and thousands of brand messages confront them every day. However, most of these messages are fleeting like clouds and water.

The waste of brand construction costs has become the biggest waste for companies, also limiting the cultivation and growth of corporate brands.

How can we ensure that every penny of brand communication is spent effectively? This requires us to focus effectively and become a marksman who never misses, only pulling the trigger when aiming at the enemy's vital spot.

I. The meaning of brand focus

Why does a laser have such great penetrating power? Because it can focus all the light on one point. Brand building following the focusing principle will allow a company's limited resources to exert maximum energy.

Nobel laureate in economics, Hurter, once said: "With the development of the information age, what is valuable is not information but your attention." In the era of eyeball economy, attention is wealth; if eyeballs are effectively attracted, it equals being halfway successful. Brand competition ultimately boils down to the competition for consumers' attention. To attract consumers' attention, brands need to concentrate their efforts and convey the same message, which requires brand focus, focus, and more focus!

For example, after entering the Chinese market, Safeguard spent over a decade repeatedly focusing on conveying one message—"antibacterial"—which allowed the Safeguard brand to deeply resonate with people, becoming the dominant player in the soap market.

Head & Shoulders consistently emphasized "dandruff removal," Rejoice continuously promoted "smoothness," Liushen continually conveyed "cooling and herbal essence, best used in summer," Wanglaoji repeatedly stressed "detoxification"...

However, many of our companies often neglect focus in brand building and instead favor dispersion.

For instance, Sanjing Oral Liquid should have concentrated all its limited advertising budget on conveying the message of "calcium supplementation and zinc supplementation." Instead, the massive spread of "blue bottle" information is essentially wasting ammunition and has no significance for brand building.

In fact, this kind of dispersed brand communication seen in Sanjing Oral Liquid is not uncommon.

At Shanghai Jahwa, the brand manager for Meijiajing was changed six times in eight years. The previous one was emphasizing "youth without wrinkles," while the next one launched "CQ condensation hydration skin." The result of this dispersion is that Meijiajing's brand personality has become increasingly diluted.

Moutai's cultural essence originally was the "national liquor's" nobility, authority, and weightiness. In recent years, Moutai has loudly promoted "National Liquor Moutai, drink for health," highlighting the health benefits of Moutai wine, almost becoming the "spokesperson" for health wine. It has also played the "humanistic" card, "technology" card, "green" card, etc.

Since 1990, the Li-Ning brand has evolved from the initial "the hope of China's new generation" to "leave excellence to oneself," "I move, I exist," "the beauty of sports shared by the world," "excellence stems from nature," and now "everything is possible." The core value of the Li-Ning brand has never focused on one point, leaving it somewhat unclear.
