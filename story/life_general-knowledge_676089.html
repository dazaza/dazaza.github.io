9 Essential Tips for Daily Life to Alleviate Bodily Strain -- Common Living Knowledge

Health stems from the details, so it's important to start with small actions to prevent illness and avoid letting minor issues become major problems. Forbes Magazine in the U.S. introduced nine methods that can effectively alleviate physical strain and create a healthy, renewed lifestyle.

1. Use the correct posture when retrieving luggage.

Experts from Northwestern University’s Feinberg School of Medicine point out that people often rush to retrieve their luggage from overhead compartments when disembarking from a plane. Due to incorrect posture, this often leads to prolonged back pain. Experts suggest not rushing, standing with feet apart, facing the direction of the luggage compartment without twisting or bending your body, and utilizing leg muscles as much as possible. Additionally, when carrying backpacks, heavier items should be placed in the middle of the bag to ensure balanced weight distribution.

2. Wear bras that fit properly.

Bras that do not fit correctly can affect breast shape and cause neck pain, shoulder and back soreness, chest tightness, dizziness, nausea, and numbness in the upper limbs. Some women don't even know what size suits them, while others are unaware that they need to change bra sizes according to bodily changes. Experts recommend that women replace their bras at least once a year.

3. Buy a pair of well-fitting shoes.

Experts from the American Podiatric Medical Association indicate that the best way to reduce foot strain is to wear well-fitted shoes. Shoes should be bought in the afternoon because feet tend to swell slightly at this time, making them larger than usual. Additionally, you should buy shoes that feel comfortable immediately, rather than those that require a break-in period. Experts suggest alternating shoes daily to relieve foot pain and women should also switch between shoes with different heel heights.

4. Wear earplugs to protect hearing.

Experts from Columbia University’s Mailman School of Public Health state that people who are frequently exposed to urban noise not only experience decreased hearing but also elevated blood pressure and adrenaline levels. To avoid noise stimulation, covering ears doesn't work, and using headphones or MP3 players at high volumes worsens the situation by exposing individuals to two noise sources simultaneously. Experts suggest temporarily wearing earplugs to protect hearing.

5. Balance exercise with rest.

Experts from the Cooper Institute for Human Performance and Nutritional Research emphasize that balancing exercise with adequate rest is crucial. Failing to give the body proper rest during fitness activities can lead to injuries. Furthermore, physical fatigue can cause mental exhaustion. Experts suggest reducing exercise intensity or trying other forms of movement to allow the body to relax appropriately. For example, regular runners could try cycling or swimming. Alternatively, take a day off and read a book as a form of rest.

6. Create a comfortable working environment.

Experts highlight that sitting posture during work has a significant impact on the body. Many jobs involve computer use, which requires adjusting the positions of eyes, hands, and the computer screen. Ensure that the mouse, keyboard, and chair are all oriented towards the monitor, and that the mouse and keyboard are within easy reach. Additionally, find the optimal visual distance between your eyes and the display and adjust the text size accordingly.

7. Find a suitable chair.

To relieve seated fatigue, adjust your position each time you sit down to make yourself more comfortable. Be mindful that the chair should not exert any pressure on your thighs. When driving, adjust your sitting posture every 20 minutes. Experts suggest that if adjustments still leave you uncomfortable, it may be time to consider changing chairs.

8. Eat more fruits and vegetables.

The latest issue of the "Journal of Nutrition" indicates that eating less fast food and more fruits and vegetables not only improves mood but also protects the heart. Experts recommend drinking pure fruit juice daily and switching flavors weekly to intake different nutrients. At lunch and dinner, it's best to add a serving of vegetables or salad.

9. Provide nutrition for your hair.

If protein intake is insufficient, hair may appear dry, split, or damaged. Hair experts suggest supplementing your hair with 2.5-3 ounces (equivalent to 70-85 grams) of protein daily, such as consuming more eggs and drinking milk.
