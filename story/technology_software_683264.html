Man uses fake shopping coupons to buy more than 300,000 yuan worth of gold

by cfe2g8bgyi on 2012-02-29 13:35:32

Five times of easy consumption over 300,000 yuan. After the second print, Zhang picked out 9,000 fake coupons with higher simulation degree from the 40,000 shopping coupons and took them back to Suzhou. Afterwards, he carved a steel stamp according to the real coupons and stamped it on the fake coupons.

In the following month, Zhang used the fake coupons four more times at the People's Mall and the New Suzhou Department Store. Each time, he bought gold worth about 60,000 yuan. "Every time I used around 600 fake coupons. Sometimes the counter staff would be suspicious when they saw a thick stack of fake coupons, but I told them that my unit used them to buy gifts. I didn't expect that I could get away with it," Zhang said. In order not to attract the attention of the mall staff, he alternated using the fake coupons between the People's Mall and the New Suzhou Department Store. In five times, he bought over 1,000 grams of gold bars and gold jewelry, with a total value of over 300,000 yuan.

Zhang, who worked in Suzhou, used the fake shopping coupons he had printed and easily consumed five times at the Suzhou People's Mall and the New Suzhou Department Store. He used over 3,000 fake shopping coupons to buy gold worth over 300,000 yuan. When he boldly came to the New Suzhou Department Store for the sixth time and took out more than 600 shopping coupons to consume, he was caught on the spot by the cashier.

Subsequently, Zhang selected gold necklaces, bracelets, and gold bars worth more than 67,700 yuan and went to the checkout counter to pay with the fake coupons. What Zhang did not expect was that the cashier took out a machine to identify the authenticity of the shopping coupons, identified Zhang's fake coupons, and then the mall staff took Zhang to the office and immediately reported the case to the police. Zhang was then taken to the Guanqian Police Station of the Pingjiang Branch of the Suzhou Public Security Bureau. Under the questioning of the police, Zhang confessed to his fraudulent behavior of using fake coupons. The police found 5,082 remaining fake shopping coupons at Zhang's home.

On March 5, 2011, Zhang went to the Suzhou People's Mall with the fake shopping coupons and bought gold bars and gold jewelry worth more than 50,000 yuan.

After succeeding, Zhang immediately sold the gold bars and gold jewelry at a low price and got more than 200,000 yuan. With this money, Zhang went gambling and quickly squandered more than 100,000 yuan.

The cost of each fake coupon was very low. As Zhang became increasingly bold, he eventually got caught.

The reporter of Kuaibao, Chen Hongjiang and Wang Yijingran wrote and photographed the article about the fake shopping coupons seized by the police.

When talking about his criminal behavior, Zhang regretted it very much. He said that he committed the crime due to侥幸 psychology and ultimately fell because of his own "greed." Currently, Zhang has been arrested by the Suzhou Pingjiang Procuratorate on suspicion of fraud.

Zhang is 27 years old. After getting married, he came to Suzhou alone. He lost more than 10,000 yuan doing business in Kunshan. On February 13 this year, Zhang spent money to buy a 100-yuan Suzhou People's Mall shopping coupon, brought it to a printing factory in Shenzhen, and spent 8,000 yuan to print 40,000 fake coupons. "The first batch of printed coupons had a very low simulation degree, so I added 1,500 yuan and asked the printing factory to reprint them."

On April 9, Zhang came to the New Suzhou Department Store again with 677 fake coupons, risking being caught, and chose a counter where he had previously used the fake coupons to make purchases.
