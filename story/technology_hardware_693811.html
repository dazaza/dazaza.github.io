Male infant suffered from a strange skin disease and could not be diagnosed. Netizens posted on Weibo for help.

by o6nihao8h on 2012-03-06 18:40:16

At the beginning of October, Ms. Chen brought her child to the Zhujiang Hospital. At that time, the child's body was covered with grain-like rashes and red blisters, occasionally accompanied by fever. On October 6th, the child’s heart rate became abnormal and was admitted into the pediatric intensive care unit. "Currently, the baby's skin is bright red, peeling off in patches. After shedding one layer cleanly, another layer comes off after a few days. The attending doctor still does not know what kind of skin disease it is, and the hospital's dermatology department also cannot determine the cause."

Multiple hospitals unable to diagnose the cause

On August 3rd this year, the male infant Li Haoran was born in Lizhi Village, Taiping Town, Luoding City, in a rural family. He weighed more than 6 jin (approximately 3 kilograms) at birth, dark and plump, very adorable. "He was very active when he was born; I didn’t expect him to become like this now." Yesterday morning, in the pediatric ward of Zhujiang Hospital, the child's mother, Ms. Chen, couldn't help but shed tears when talking about her child.

Information Times News (Reporter Xiong Xulan): "In Luoding, there is a two-and-a-half-month-old baby who has a strange skin disease. His whole body is red with blisters, and his skin is peeling off in large patches. He also frequently has high fevers. Despite visiting all the hospitals in Guangzhou, he hasn’t been cured. Now we hope to use the power of Weibo (http://t.sina.com.cn) to find doctors who can help this unfortunate baby! Thank you!" The day before yesterday, an urgent post on Sina Weibo drew significant attention from netizens. According to the Weibo clues, reporters found the child's parents yesterday. It is understood that this baby is currently hospitalized in the pediatric ICU of Zhujiang Hospital in Guangzhou, and the doctors have still not been able to confirm the cause of the illness. In their anxiety, the parents decided to try every possible method to find medical experts.

Passionate netizens posted a Weibo appeal for help

Ms. Chen said she had tried posting appeals on some forums, but initially, there was no response. The day before yesterday, after a netizen posted an appeal on Weibo, she received many condolences from netizens. Many netizens today forwarded this appeal post on Weibo.

Ms. Chen recalled that the child started getting sick 20 days after birth. "First, he had diarrhea, which lasted for two or three days. His bottom was always red, then his neck turned red, and later even his eyes had a bit of redness." Ms. Chen took the child to see a doctor who diagnosed it as eczema, but after using the medication, there was no improvement. So they went to the Luoding Health Center and Guigui Hospital, but the condition did not improve. Instead, the child's skin began peeling off, with red rashes appearing on his face, legs, and back, forming large patches.

The reporter contacted the Weibo user @Cyu3 who posted the appeal. She claimed that she didn't know the baby's mother personally but discovered the mother's hospital diary online, feeling sorry for the baby, so she posted on Weibo hoping to help them.

To treat the child, the originally not wealthy couple had already spent all their savings and borrowed a large sum from relatives and friends. "After seeking medical help for over a month, we've already spent tens of thousands of yuan. Now, every day in the hospital costs two or three thousand yuan, and inviting expert consultations costs one or two thousand yuan. We really don't know what to do," Ms. Chen said. She and her husband are currently staying at her sister's house in Guangzhou, relying on donations from friends to get through this period.

During their time seeking medical help in Guangzhou, Ms. Chen and her husband also consulted Zhongshan First Hospital, Zhongshan Second Hospital, the Provincial Traditional Chinese Medicine Hospital, and the City Skin Disease Prevention and Treatment Institute, but they still couldn't find a definitive cause. The day before yesterday, they sent an invitation letter through Zhujiang Hospital to Zhongshan Second Hospital, asking relevant experts to come for consultation. "We hope the disease can be identified as soon as possible, and treatment can begin promptly."

Developed a strange illness 20 days after birth

It is understood that starting in the afternoon of the day before yesterday, many people continuously called and sent messages to the child's parents. "Some just offered words of concern, while others gave advice for us to consider, such as suggesting we find traditional Chinese medicine practitioners or recommending other hospitals. Anyway, we will note down any useful information," Ms. Chen said. She expressed gratitude to these netizens and also hoped the child's illness could be identified and treated as soon as possible. "No matter what, I won't give up on him. I believe there must be a doctor who can cure him."

On September 19th, Ms. Chen's family came to Guangzhou for medical help. However, neither the Children's Hospital nor the Dongsheng Hospital could figure out the strange illness of the baby. The diagnoses from different hospitals varied: some said it was Candida or other fungal infections, some suggested psoriasis, and others thought it might be eczema.
