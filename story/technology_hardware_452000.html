Netizens' design of a mini overpass to relieve traffic congestion sparks heated debate (picture)

by jyq2011668 on 2011-06-26 09:50:02

College Student Designs Mini Overpass, Diagram by Yang Fangfang

In the face of increasingly severe traffic congestion, college student Li Xu, majoring in transportation, spent two years designing a "mini overpass." In his design, vehicles at crossroads do not need to stop, and it also conserves space. This design has sparked heated discussions among netizens. Some netizens believe that this plan could help solve Beijing's traffic congestion problems. Li Xu also stated that his plan suits Beijing. However, some experts pointed out that if implemented, it might exacerbate congestion. The "mini overpass" has also drawn attention to Beijing's existing overpasses. Some citizens have pointed out design issues with certain overpasses that lead to traffic jams.

According to reports, recently, the design diagram of the "mini overpass" appeared online, drawing numerous questions from netizens. "Can Beijing's overpasses use this design?" "This plan has no traffic lights, allowing all vehicles to go straight, turn, or make U-turns."

Netizen Amy0311 believed that existing overpasses, such as Beitaipingzhuang Bridge, allow mainline vehicles to pass without traffic lights for straight travel, but turning vehicles need to wait for the light. The design of the "mini overpass" can solve this problem.

The release of the design diagram sparked criticism of Beijing's existing overpasses among netizens. Netizen Ttyin said that the Beichen Overpass lacks a connecting ramp from north to east linking to the eastern direction of the Fourth Ring Road. The Wanquanhe Bridge at the intersection of the Third and Fourth Ring Roads lacks a connecting ramp from south to west linking to the westbound direction of the Fourth Ring Road, causing westbound vehicles on the Fourth Ring Road to have to make a U-turn under the Yihetan Road overpass and detour. Some netizens also pointed out that many overpasses at intersections with the Fifth Ring Road either cannot exit the Fifth Ring or cannot enter it. Netizens indicated that these issues lead to congestion around the overpasses.

Netizen Shaozi pointed out that there are design issues with the Shuangjing Bridge: the concentration of bus stops, subway stations, and shopping centers leads to chaotic interactions between vehicles exiting the ring road, bicycles, and pedestrians, making it very disorganized. There is hope that the "mini overpass" plan can help alleviate traffic congestion at the Shuangjing Bridge.

Designer

Beijing's Wide and Flat Roads Suit This Plan

Regarding whether this plan is suitable for Beijing, designer Li Xu expressed optimism. He said that this plan is particularly suitable for Beijing because Beijing has many intersections, and the roads are relatively wide and stable.

Li Xu said that making left turns on Chang'an Street is a big challenge, and traffic congestion on Chang'an Street is becoming increasingly severe. However, Li Xu's plan has been questioned by some citizens. After reviewing the plan, citizen Mr. Li thought that its design merely consolidates the outwardly expanded left-turn loop into the internal main road. This design would suddenly reduce the number of lanes on two main roads. At intersections where vehicles need to slow down, this could cause significant congestion.

Currently, Li Xu has submitted materials to apply for a patent for the "mini overpass."

Expert

This Design Would Exacerbate Beijing's Congestion

The day before yesterday, Ma Lin, deputy director of the Urban Traffic Engineering Technology Center of the Ministry of Housing and Urban-Rural Development, said that the design proposal by university students was commendable. However, the "mini overpass" lacks feasibility and is unsuitable for Beijing. If adopted, Beijing would experience even worse congestion.

Ma Lin believed that the proposal lacks an engineering technical foundation. If strict adherence to some design standards for overpasses, such as slope and turning ramps, were considered and applied to specific dimensions, it would no longer be "mini." Instead, it would be quite massive.

Ma Lin said that this design does not take into account traffic flow issues. According to this plan, the speed of vehicles during turns would inevitably decrease. For a city like Beijing with enormous traffic volume, any reduction in speed would exacerbate congestion. Moreover, the design has a steep slope, making driving more difficult in rainy or snowy weather. Additionally, this plan has blind spots regarding visibility; the distance between the four up-ramps, down-ramps, left-turn lanes, and U-turn lanes of the overpass is too small, the turning radius and clearance height under the bridge are insufficient, and the overall indicators are low. This could easily cause visual interference and accidents.

Dialogue

Father's Business Loss Due to Traffic Jams Inspired the Design

At 22 years old, Henan native Li Xu spent two years designing the "mini overpass" and applied for a patent. He believes his plan is superior to traditional overpasses and could alleviate Beijing's traffic congestion. The day before yesterday, our reporter had a dialogue with Li Xu.

New京 Newspaper: What was the initial motivation for designing the "mini overpass"?

Li Xu: My father came to Beijing to do business in 2007 and lost a big deal due to traffic jams. By then, Beijing already started experiencing traffic jams. My father thought that many overpasses in Beijing were poorly designed, like the Xizhimen Overpass being too complicated. I received strong support from my father when I designed the "mini overpass." My father is interested in drawing diagrams, and some of the work was completed by him.

New京 Newspaper: The "mini overpass" has attracted a lot of attention from netizens. What do you think about it?

Li Xu: I didn't expect it to attract so much attention, which shows how serious the traffic congestion issue is and that there are still imperfections in the existing overpasses. Netizens have personal experiences, which is why they pay attention.

New京 Newspaper: Many netizens criticized your "mini overpass" for not considering pedestrians, calling it a "fatal flaw." How do you address the interaction between vehicles and pedestrians and the issue of pedestrian crossings?

Li Xu: That is indeed a shortcoming, and I have considered this issue. It could possibly be solved by building sky bridges or underground passages.

New京 Newspaper: Netizens mentioned many flaws in the "mini overpass," such as having many additional exits on the bridge, which could easily cause congestion and be difficult to correct once a wrong turn is made. Experts also believe your plan lacks feasibility. What do you think about this?

Li Xu: The design diagram is not set in stone when it comes to application. It needs to be adapted according to actual conditions, including traffic volume, etc., tailored to local conditions. The opinions of experts have been very helpful, such as the blind spots in visibility and the interaction between people and vehicles, which were areas I did not consider thoroughly before.

Will the "Plan" Alleviate Congestion at Shuangjing Bridge?

Designer Li Xu believes that the "mini overpass" is suitable for Beijing. Some citizens suggested using this plan to "diagnose" congested points like the Shuangjing Bridge to see if it could solve the congestion.

Li Xu stated that although Beijing's road characteristics suit his plan, he cannot immediately give a definitive answer regarding its application at specific locations. A survey of local traffic volume, road characteristics, and surrounding environment is necessary. "We can find time to conduct software simulation tests first."

[On-site]

Multiple Congestion Points Surround the Overpass

Yesterday afternoon, reporters conducted a field investigation of the Shuangjing Bridge. In the northeast corner of the Shuangjing Bridge, vehicles coming from Liangguang Road just past the bridge encounter multiple bus stops, including nearly 20 bus lines such as routes 28, 37, and 57. Because the buses stop too close to the bridge, the crowd boarding and alighting mixes with cyclists, creating a congestion point. On the northwest corner of the bridge, there is a large shopping center. Some vehicles park randomly on the roadside, occupying bike lanes, forcing cyclists onto motor vehicle lanes, exacerbating congestion.

On the west side of the Shuangjing Bridge, heading south from Guomao Bridge, if one needs to head towards Liangguang Road, they must leave the main road before reaching the Shuangjing Bridge. However, vehicles leaving the main road interact with vehicles on the auxiliary road, bicycles, and pedestrians. Also nearby are bus stops and subway stations, leading to mixed flows of people and vehicles, creating another congestion point. The owner of a nearby newsstand said that during peak hours, the area often becomes gridlocked, with minor accidents happening almost every day. Even a 200-meter stretch of road may require half an hour of waiting in a car.

Additionally, from under the Shuangjing Bridge heading east to the intersection of Guang渠 Road and Xidawang Road, this 3-kilometer stretch has four traffic lights, slowing down vehicle speeds and causing queues.

Ms. Zhang, a resident, believed that if Li Xu's design were applied here, traveling from Liangguang Road to Guomao in any direction would eliminate traffic lights, making left turns very convenient. Through multiple entrances and exits on the overpass, the traffic volume could be dispersed, maintaining speed without significant reductions, naturally eliminating congestion points.

[Commentary]

To Solve Traffic Congestion, One Must Address Mixed Vehicle and Pedestrian Flows

After reviewing Li Xu's plan, Zheng Xueshin, a civilian designer who has studied overpasses for over a decade, believed that the design does not adequately solve the problem of vehicle flow intersections. Therefore, it is unsuitable for places like the Shuangjing Bridge with high traffic volumes.

Zheng Xueshin believed that the biggest problem with traditional overpass designs lies in vehicle flow intersections and mixed vehicle-pedestrian traffic. The congestion points at the Shuangjing Bridge are formed similarly. Where vehicle flows intersect, road capacity decreases. Furthermore, motor vehicles and bicycles sharing parallel lanes mix during turns, causing chaos. Li Xu's design does not offer solutions to these issues.

[Gathering Suggestions]

Please Share Your Ideas on Alleviating Traffic Congestion at Overpasses

Since the construction of the first overpass, the Zhenxingmen Bridge, in 1974, Beijing currently has nearly 200 overpasses, yet they fail to significantly alleviate increasingly severe traffic congestion. Multiple citizens believe that some overpasses prioritize form over function, with designs becoming increasingly complex, limiting passage. Some citizens also believe that overpasses themselves have become congestion points.

Which overpasses do you think suffer from unreasonable design leading to congestion? Which overpasses have caused you to take unnecessary detours?
