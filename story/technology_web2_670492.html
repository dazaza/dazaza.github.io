Face. To be honest, I like a clean and fresh face because I believe in the saying from ancient times that one's appearance reflects their heart. A face is not just about looking ugly or beautiful; it carries significant emotional characteristics and is an expression of human emotion. People’s appearances vary in countless ways. Good-looking individuals often receive encouragement, appreciation, acceptance, and tolerance, while those considered unattractive may face strikes, negation, sarcasm, and harshness. Plain-looking people tend to be neglected. You see, in today's society, interpersonal relationships and job advantages often favor handsome people, leaving those overlooked with almost twisted souls.

Besides the development of modern beauty industries and cosmetics, I think the key to enhancing one's face lies in nurturing the heart. Presumably, eating well, living healthily, reading books, and enjoying music are not things you should neglect. Only by doing so can you quietly and completely refine your face, filtering out greed, fear, anger, jealousy, and other bad moods. What remains is compassion, joy, gratitude, and other positive psychological suggestions. Over time, you'll find your eyes becoming clearer, and desires or plunder will no longer easily assault you. The personality exuding from within will bring you extraordinary grace. Even if you grow old to eighty years, you will still be benevolent, kind, courteous, and accessible. Conversely, if you eat poorly, sleep restlessly, and harbor hatred, your eyes will turn pale yellow, start drooping, and fill with blood. Hatred will inevitably show up on your face.

Looks might be ugly due to genetics given by our parents, but what truly changes is our state of mind and inner world, which reflect through our looks and beauty. When you're filled with hate and troubles, your face becomes tense, even ferocious. When you open your heart wide, your face appears calm and festive. Friends often say that kindness is familiar to me, and this is why. Eternal scenes are light, love, and hate, all illusions. The detached idealism I pursue still needs continuous practice.

Remember, you can be ugly, but your eyes must laugh. You can be plain, but your face must be fresh. You can be beautiful, but don't let vanity leave your lips.
