Manchester United's acknowledged weakest link makes opponents despair; a defense talent with zero transfer fee becomes famous in one game.

by qinglai888 on 2009-12-16 13:10:14

Manchester United's acknowledged weakest link has become a source of despair for opponents. A versatile defender with zero transfer cost has made a name in one game.

By Sina Sports -- With the high-density twice-a-week matches, along with an injury-plagued defense line, Ferguson had no choice but to use some less-experienced players to temporarily fill the gaps. The Belgian Riki Dier became an indispensable figure on Manchester United's defensive line at this moment. Just over 21 years old, Dier should have said that his previous career development was not smooth, but under Ferguson, using a very cliché phrase: Dier was reborn.

Interestingly, Dier's first appearance this season was in the League Cup against Wolves; today again against Wolves, and this time in the higher-level league as a starter. The recent domino-style injuries among center-backs indeed created the current opportunity for Dier. But this is just on the surface. Ferguson used him first as a right-back and then as a center-back today, daring to examine a player with only two league experiences in such a way itself shows Ferguson's considerable confidence in his abilities.

Dier played with great authority in this match. The Belgian dared to face tough challenges head-on. To suppress Manchester United, Wolves planned to rely on strong tackling, but you must be tougher than them. Dier made 6 tackles throughout the game, ranking first among both teams, succeeding 3 times, and completed 4 successful clearances, ranking first in Manchester United. This indicates that the opponent deliberately targeted his side, but Wolves did not gain any opportunities, even consolatory ones.

Passing the defensive test was just one aspect. As a right-back, Dier must push forward to assist in attack. Perhaps the Belgian didn't have many highlight moments in this area, but he successfully crossed from the right flank in the first half, with the ball reaching the front of the goal, being cleared by the opposing defender in the ensuing melee. For Dier, who played his first league game for Manchester United this season, you can't ask for too much. Additionally, when he switched to center-back at the 60th minute, it seemed more like the demeanor of a seasoned general. His decisive and clean handling of the ball, and his ease in dealing with several opponents over 1.9 meters tall despite his 1.86-meter height, earned him specific praise from Ferguson after the game. He said: "Dier performed very well when he moved to center-back, showing great potential. He is a natural-born center-back."

Including today's game, Dier has played a total of 5 games for Manchester United, 3 in the League Cup and 2 in the league, with his last league game being against Hull City in the final game of the previous season. Although five games may seem insufficient to draw conclusions, Dier has already played left-back, right-back, and both sides of center-back, making him a standard all-around backfield player. Considering the future, Dier has every chance to become a legendary figure at Manchester United, or at least an indispensable versatile player like O'Shea who plays multiple positions. In January 2009, when he joined Manchester United, it didn't cost them anything because Manchester United agreed to pay his former club Stoke City based on his performance, which Stoke also accepted at the time. Despite only passing a year, and Stoke potentially making a small profit, Manchester United is clearly the sole beneficiary. Stoke's limited strength is evident as they let go of a player who could have been a significant addition to their team.

It's hard to imagine if Dier's ultimate professional goal was merely joining Stoke City and if Manchester United scouts hadn't noticed him, when would he have emerged? Transferring from Antwerp to Stoke City in 2007 was seen by many Belgians as a good opportunity, but being loaned out to Wrexham further dampened his professional aspirations, making staying in Antwerp to play in the Belgian Second Division a better option. The keen eyes of Manchester United's scouts came first, followed by Ferguson's discerning talent identification, giving Riki Dier a real opportunity for growth.

We must marvel at Ferguson's recognition of young players' abilities. The initial successes with Macheda, the Da Silva brothers, and Obertan, among others, are key, especially given how inexpensive their acquisitions were. Obertan was the most expensive at only £3 million, and Dier is the latest success. The 16-year-old Paul Pogba brought in from Le Havre in August might be the next surprise. Recently, he helped Manchester United's U18 team secure four consecutive wins, scoring 3 goals in 4 games, including a goal against Liverpool's U18 team. Not long ago, he scored within the first minute of a game, helping Manchester United's U18 team advance smoothly to the fourth round of the Youth FA Cup. On the 14th, during his first appearance for Manchester United's reserve team against Bolton, he scored first and provided an assist. Born in 1993, this player's growth speed is astonishingly fast, and at this rate, a call-up to the first team could come at any time. Ferguson's emphasis on young players, particularly his unique and precise eye for talent selection, lays a solid foundation for Manchester United's future orderly development.
