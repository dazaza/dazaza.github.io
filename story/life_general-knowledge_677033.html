by huiming on 2012-02-26 19:38:50

It turns out that forgetting a person takes a lifetime.

Suddenly I understood, why there is always fickleness, why there are so many people living in a drunken stupor. Those who have loved must never believe they can forget the once heart-rending experiences, or laugh them off with nonchalance, because you are only hiding a feeling even deeper.

Until one day when you finally cannot control yourself anymore, it may be in a bar, or sitting alone at home during a lonely season like snow, suddenly you think of that person, your emotions no longer suppressed, tears uncontrollably falling down.

From when it started, to when it ended; from when hands were held, to when there was an embrace but tears fell? You cannot control these things, but you can try. When you cannot obtain someone, all you can do is not forget.

After how many sleepless nights of loneliness, you start to feel peace in your yearning heart, start to feel the world is still blue, start to feel you are strong enough to live alone. Regretting why you were so obsessed with someone initially? And someone who lives in a different world than you, life is short, why bother?

But, but, until one day, standing on a street corner, a familiar figure passes by, all those wonderful moments come rushing back instantly, all your self-protection mechanisms are not as invincible as you imagined, and they crumble instantly. You finally realize, time has not erased your sorrowful memories after all, tears have already fallen, and unknowingly everything you thought to be true has been shattered in an instant.

In a fleeting life, how many faces have we forgotten, the one face we haven't forgotten is yours. This time, we truly and completely understand, it takes a lifetime to forget a person.
