Title: Successful Joint Effort by Triangle Public and Firefighters in Extinguishing the Blaze

Xinkuai Daily Report

By reporter Zhang Enjie and correspondent Gu Lingkai - On the 27th at around 18:00, the professional fire station of Zhongshan's Triangle Town received an emergency call from the 119 command center stating that on the Zhongshan to Guangzhou section of the Jingzhu Expressway near the Triangle area, a large bus with a Yue A license plate had suddenly caught fire. The flames were fierce, and there was imminent danger of the fuel tank exploding! Upon receiving the alarm, the station promptly dispatched two fire trucks and 12 firefighters to the scene.

At 18:09 upon arrival, the firefighters saw a passenger bus stopped by the roadside, its upper portion engulfed in roaring flames. The entire vehicle was already surrounded by a sea of fire, emitting thick plumes of smoke. After investigation, it was confirmed that no passengers were on board, and the driver had managed to leave the burning bus in time, preventing any casualties. The firefighting personnel immediately assisted the traffic department in cordoning off the area and commenced extinguishing the blaze.

Due to the lack of water sources on the highway at the time, amidst the urgent need for water, the commander of the Triangle team coordinated with the command center to secure reinforcements from the public's professional firefighting team. By 18:46, the fire was completely extinguished.

According to the driver of the burning bus, the coach was being towed from Zhuhai to Guangzhou for repairs by a Guangzhou-based vehicle maintenance center. While passing through the Triangle section of the Jingzhu Expressway, he noticed smoke emanating from the rear of the towed bus via the rearview mirror. He quickly pulled over to the emergency lane and found that the bus was on fire. As there were no firefighting equipment available on the vehicle, all they could do was watch as the flames continued to grow uncontrollably, eventually leading to a major fire.
