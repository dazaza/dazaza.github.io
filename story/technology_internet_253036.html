Developing overseas business, focusing on international emails

by now on 2009-10-28 15:27:30

After the economic globalization, our country advocates the "go global" policy. Expanding overseas operations has become an important development path for many enterprises, and sending emails has become their main way of cooperating with overseas customers. With enterprises cooperating more frequently and closely with overseas partners, sending overseas emails has become increasingly important for enterprise development. Any obstruction or failure in email sending may cause immeasurable losses to enterprises.

However, the phenomenon of mainland email accounts having their emails to overseas recipients returned or lost happens quite often, which has caused alarm among many enterprises. An enterprise email also represents the image of the enterprise; if such situations occur frequently, it will inevitably damage the credibility of a company. More frighteningly, it may cause losses to the enterprise and even affect its development. Therefore, the issue of returned or lost overseas emails has drawn much attention from senior management in enterprises, and email developers have been trying to find ways to solve this problem.

According to relevant technical personnel from Times Interconnect, there are several reasons why overseas emails are returned or lost:

1. Self-built enterprise servers: For this situation, there is no solution available. The only option is to consider outsourcing or continue enduring the problem.

2. Non-professional email service providers: They usually do not invest in overseas servers for processing, or if they do, they are weak in both capability and technology (such as no encryption, no smart DNS), making them unable to effectively solve the problem of overseas forwarding.

3. Network congestion or too many hops between domestic and international networks, leading to transmission failure. Errors in the international gateway between domestic and foreign networks can also lead to inability to send and receive emails normally.

In response to these issues, Times Interconnect (www.now.cn/email) has developed a set of solutions through its own forwarding technology. It places encrypted email transmission on overseas SMTP servers, and then delivers the emails via overseas forwarding servers. The entire delivery process combines encryption, smart DNS, and other techniques to ensure that enterprise emails are safely delivered into the hands of overseas customers, thereby guaranteeing the smooth flow of enterprise emails and allowing the overseas business of enterprises to proceed smoothly.

It has been reported that after the financial storm, the overseas market is expected to recover in the fourth quarter. In the future, as the global economy stabilizes, overseas orders are gradually increasing. Enterprises with overseas operations usually rely heavily on emails to generate business. If their own enterprise email lacks reliability, they will be at a disadvantage right from the start. To ensure smooth sailing for overseas operations, enterprises should focus on the technical support of their enterprise email systems to avoid serious issues like returned or lost emails during sending and receiving.
