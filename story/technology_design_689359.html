Enlightenment from Watching NBA

by au63fvecy11 on 2012-03-04 02:12:03

All Chinese who watch NBA, I think you must pay attention to Yao Ming and Jeremy Lin, especially the recently rising Jeremy Lin. So why can they achieve such success? What are the key factors for their success?

I am not a loyal fan of NBA, especially after working, I only occasionally pay attention to the game results. For entertainment, I would like to share some shallow thoughts with everyone.

The success of Yao Ming can be simply归纳to the following three points:

1. Talent:

Close to 2.3 meters tall, Yao Ming's such先天conditions are probably the greatest wealth bestowed by heaven. There are indeed many people in the world with such physical conditions, and no one can change this condition. For those who like to wait for manna from heaven, they can only envy, jealousy, and hate.

2. The right choice:

If you don't choose wisely and blindly reach too high, it will be difficult to change the result. If Yao Ming insisted on playing field hockey, he would definitely not have achieved today's success. Yao Ming chose a basketball career closely related to his own advantages, allowing his potential to be fully released, and ultimately achieved Yao Ming.

3. Personal quality:

First of all, Yao Ming is a great speaker and speaks English. He can deal with the media and fans at ease, and his answers to questions are always appropriate. Yao Ming off the court is humorous, friendly, and knows how to keep himself safe. He actively participates in charity work, almost a perfect combination of Chinese and American spirits, showing professional spirit and dual success as a person.

Personal talent, hard work, and the dazzling stage of the NBA, when encountering this great globalization era, Yao Ming's success became an inevitable result of time and place.

On February 10, 2012, Jeremy Lin scored a career-high 38 points and handed out seven assists, leading the Knicks to a 92-85 victory over the Los Angeles Lakers. Lin's score surpassed Kobe Bryant of the Lakers, who scored 34 points. Another bright new star appeared on the NBA court!

I think Jeremy Lin's success can also be interpreted in three points:

1. Good at seizing opportunities:

After several lockouts in the NBA, Lin finally waited for this day: On February 3, 2012, after the Knicks squandered their lead in the fourth quarter against the Boston Celtics, head coach Mike D'Antoni decided to give Jeremy Lin a chance to play because the two main guards were injured and unable to play. D'Antoni said, "He was lucky because we played so poorly." On February 4, in a 99-92 victory over the New Jersey Nets, Jeremy Lin scored 25 points, grabbed five rebounds, and handed out seven assists - all career highs (During halftime, teammate Carmelo Anthony suggested to head coach Mike D'Antoni that Jeremy Lin should get more playing time in the second half. After the game, D'Antoni said that Jeremy Lin had the intelligence (mentality) of a point guard, and "has his own rhythm and reasons for knowing what to do."), thus Jeremy Lin's life took a new turn. Opportunities are important for one's success, but the ability to seize them comes from relentless accumulation and effort off the court.

2. Confidence:

From July 21, 2010, when Jeremy Lin signed with the Golden State Warriors as a free agent to December 10, 2011, Lin was sent down three times, and the fourth time Lin received the result of being cut by the Warriors; staying with the Houston Rockets for only 12 days, Jeremy Lin was cut again. Such life tests would undoubtedly be hell for ordinary people. Only experience can make people grow, and sitting through hell will surely bring about rebirth. As his high school coach Peter DiPombo said, "He has a lot of confidence in himself." During the lockout and injury periods, Jeremy Lin never stopped training.

3. Persistent effort:

"Hard work pays off" Having talent in some areas is something we all desire, but after all, not everyone has talent. More success comes from one's persistent efforts. In high school, Jeremy Lin demonstrated excellent basketball qualities: fearless in scrapping inside and selfless in ball control outside (At Harvard, his shooting percentage accounted for only 19.7% of the team). He developed a precise shooting technique (with a 61.3% shooting accuracy at Harvard).

By imitating, are there any points worth our reference? My personal understanding is:

1. Opportunities are created and grasped by oneself.

2. Talent is accumulated through relentless efforts later in life.

3. Confidence is the result of experience.

4. Quality and cultivation need to be gradually cultivated to obtain.

Finally, I want to say, choose what you like or what your heart desires, but also understand that the things we like often involve factors we dislike. Listening to Zhao Benshan's skits a thousand times makes you want to throw up, but perseverance is the only way to achieve everything.
