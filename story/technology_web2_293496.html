Against the backdrop of the global economy emerging from a recession, the electronics industry is expected to enter a phase of full recovery in 2010. In an optimistic scenario, the overall performance of the industry could approach or reach the peak levels of the previous boom cycle. We recommend investing in companies that are involved in new industrial sectors and have potential for asset integration.

Key Investment Points:

After experiencing a low point in late 2008 and early 2009, the global electronics industry has seen a continuous rise in capacity utilization, with terminal demand improving quarter by quarter and shipments recovering better than expected. The improvement in supply and demand is likely to make this round of recovery faster than the previous cycle, and the industry may only take about two years from the bottom to surpass the previous peak. Due to the benefits of international production capacity transfer and the stimulation of domestic demand, the recovery of China's electronics industry will outperform the global average.

Although capital expenditures in the industry in 2010 will increase significantly, they are still 30% lower compared to the scale of expenditures in 2008. With the improvement of the real economy and the arrival of a new round of terminal demand update cycles, terminal demand in 2010 is expected to exceed previous peaks. Currently, the inventory-to-sales ratio of manufacturers and channel distributors is at a reasonable level. Moderate capacity expansion and relatively low inventory levels are conducive to price recovery.

In 2010, external demand for China's electronics industry is expected to resume growth, though the extent of growth still needs to be continuously observed. Domestic demand will continue to grow under the impetus of national policy support and the recovery of 3C product demand. In terms of policy-driven areas, the communications market is expected to remain strong, and the demand for 3G terminals may see significant growth. The home appliance market is expected to continue growing, with orders having further development space.

The uncertainties for industry investment in 2010 come from two aspects: one is the sustainability of the global economic recovery and the resulting industry fluctuations; the second is the impact of the high performance base in the second half of 2009 on 2010. Based on this, there is a possibility that the year-on-year growth rate of earnings may slow down in the second half of next year. Overall, we believe the industry as a whole is in the upward phase of a major boom cycle, and it may reach the peak of this round of the boom cycle in 2011. At the same time, based on the sustainability of economic recovery, there is a possibility of volatility in industry prosperity in 2010.

We give the electronics industry an overweight rating and are optimistic about the investment opportunities in the electronics sector at the beginning of 2010. We particularly focus on investment varieties that align with the country’s low-carbon industry development direction and possess technological innovation capabilities and profit growth space. At the same time, we suggest paying attention to restructuring opportunities in military industries, central enterprise consolidations, and restructurings driven by the elimination of outdated production capacities.
