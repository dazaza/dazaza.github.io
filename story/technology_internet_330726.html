Hainan Poisonous Yardlong Beans and Greek Strikes - A Lesson in Management from GreenTree Inn

by alexsgxu on 2010-02-28 17:46:04

First, the toxic problem of long beans in Hainan Island. Only a small part of the long beans are toxic, but even if it's just a very small part, it has caused people to dare not buy or eat the long beans from Hainan Island. Vegetables, if they cannot be sold in time, will quickly rot in the field.

Many law-abiding and hardworking farmers growing long beans have suffered great losses. We can't distinguish which ones are toxic and which ones are not, so as customers, we can't tell them apart and end up punishing everyone. Is this reasonable? From the consumer's perspective, we have no choice. In fact, most of the farmers growing long beans are very kind, upright, and hardworking. Their long beans, grown with their hard sweat, are now rotting in the fields. Their families' livelihoods are not guaranteed, and they may not even have food to eat. Why? Because of a few bad apples, one fly has spoiled the whole pot of soup. This is very unfair to our hardworking farmers.

Of course, we also need to study from another angle: why did these toxic pesticides flow into the hands of our farmers? Were some ordinary people deceived? If so, we can solve the problem at its source and prevent such pesticides from falling into the hands of ordinary people. The manufacturers selling these toxic pesticides should also be dealt with promptly.

This issue gives us an important insight into management. To protect everyone's interests, everyone must unite, abide by laws and regulations, and serve customers well. If someone deliberately offends some customers, then those customers will never come back to your hotel. The entire team's income will decrease, the team may not be able to pay wages, and the company or team may go bankrupt. Everyone will have to find new jobs. Now

many jobs may still be relatively easy to find, but what if they're not? Will everyone be unemployed?

When encountering such problems, we must solve them from two aspects. First, many employees want to do their work well from the heart, but on one hand, they lack good working methods, and on the other hand, they fail to recognize their own issues. If our leaders can use new ways to train our employees well, similar problems can be avoided.

Of course, there are a few people who have serious problems. They cannot be cultivated, have formed habits of stealing chickens and dogs, and are lazy. If such situations occur, we must learn to remove these troublemakers from the team. The entire team, especially managers, must have clear eyesight, be able to discover these problems in time, and persuade these backward individuals who lead to team failure to leave in time. We absolutely cannot tolerate them destroying the fruits of other employees' hard labor, the team's integrity, service quality, and future. I believe that if this principle is explained clearly to the entire team, everyone will strongly support your actions. You will definitely be a fair leader who protects the entire team's interests.

There are also some department leaders who, for various reasons, whether it's because they don't know how to manage, or their behavior isn't exemplary, or to avoid trouble, or due to other personal relationships, fail to discover such backward employees, or simply ignore them. This further encourages their bad habits and conduct. Other employees, seeing this, will certainly feel it's unfair. This is indeed the least respectful and fairest management behavior towards other hardworking employees. It is a very irresponsible management behavior. Over time, good employees will choose to gradually leave, and the remaining ones will become increasingly disruptive, making the team more chaotic and dealing a fatal blow to the team. Therefore, as a leader, you must think big (place the entire team's interests in your heart), don't think small, protect the majority's interests, and don't sacrifice the entire team's interests due to one person's violation.

The second issue is the strike problem in Greece. Greek civil servants are now striking on the streets, paralyzing many tasks that taxpayers pay the government to do. They oppose national cost-saving measures, tightening belts, and reducing deficits (these are important conditions for Greece to gain trust and assistance loans from other countries). Don't you think these people are very detestable? These beneficiaries consume most of the country's income, have drained their country's wealth, and their country is about to collapse and go bankrupt. At this critical moment, everyone needs to tighten their belts and get through the difficulties together. Isn't this reasonable?

When your motherland can no longer feed you or satisfy your desires, what should a citizen do, kill the motherland? Like a group of children, they've drained their mother's milk, and she's barely alive. But these people still refuse to restrain themselves, take less, and let her recover? I believe a mother's milk is limited, and a country's wealth and income depend on all taxpayers' contributions. The total amount is also limited and cannot be wasted or consumed without limit. We all rely on this mother, so why can't we consider her more?

When a country or collective faces a crisis, we must all work together to help our own country and organization get through the difficulties. So shouldn't these people's behaviors be despised?
