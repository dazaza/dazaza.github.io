How Nokia Attracts Developers with Microsoft?

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 19:19:14

On November 3, Beijing time, according to foreign media reports, after Microsoft acquired Nokia's device department, the market has high expectations for the cooperation between these two companies. For Nokia, which is on its last legs, the WP platform has become its lifeline. How to use Microsoft's platform and technical advantages will be the key to Nokia's success or failure. The following is a summary of the article:

Last week, the photo-sharing application Instagram announced that it would enter the WP platform. The "introduction" of this application made up for the biggest flaw in Microsoft's mobile platform. Previously, Microsoft had already acquired Nokia's device department, and the latter equipped its smartphones with the coolest cameras available today. However, if the most popular photo application today is not on Microsoft's mobile platform, it would indeed be embarrassing.

Microsoft and Nokia stated that they would deepen their cooperation with each other to make up for the shortcomings of the WP app store. However, the two companies still need to persuade app developers to develop apps for the resource-scarce WP platform. Therefore, the two companies need to provide some convincing strategies.

Nokia's Global Vice President Bryan Biniak said: "App developers who refuse to develop apps for WP will miss out on the 'rich meat' that Microsoft and Nokia bring them."

Nokia currently has 100,000 employees worldwide, and after the completion of the acquisition transaction between Microsoft and Nokia, 32,000 Nokia employees will also join the Microsoft team. Hundreds of thousands of Microsoft employees will bring a large number of airline and hotel reservations. Biniak believes that there is room for improvement in the lack of travel applications in the WP app store. "If our employees, our company, deposit money into your bank, if our employees take your plane and stay in your hotel, you should have an application software in our app store. We have rich meat here, come on." Biniak said.

In addition to the spending of Microsoft employees, Microsoft will also invest hundreds of millions of dollars each year on marketing promotions. Biniak hopes to see this money spent on companies that develop apps for WP smartphones.

Extraordinary times call for extraordinary measures. One of the main reasons why app developers are lukewarm about the WP platform is that Android and iOS dominate the smartphone market.

However, Nokia and Microsoft continue to increase their market share. Nokia sold 8.8 million Lumia smartphones last quarter, a 20% increase from the previous quarter. Although ranked third in shipments, there is still a significant gap compared to the first and second place. According to data provided by IDC, the market share of Windows smartphones was only 3.7% in terms of shipments in the second quarter of this year.

In order to reverse this situation, Microsoft spared no expense. The company said it would pay WP app developers.

However, Biniak believes that this "pleading" is an effective way to attract app developers to the WP platform. These apps already exist on other platforms, so users will follow.

Biniak said that in order to distinguish itself from competitors, Windows needs to start doing something different. For this, he said that WP developers can not only develop apps for WP smartphones but also for Xbox game consoles and PCs. In addition, developers can use Nokia Maps and music services.

Biniak insisted on the commitment to pre-install these apps on WP devices. He said that the activation rate of pre-installed apps can reach as high as 86%.

Nokia is also beginning to see the fruits of its labor. Last week, DreamWorks exclusively launched an app called "Dragons Adventure" on Nokia's new tablet computer. This app uses HERE data from Nokia Maps service to provide map navigation guidance for gamers.

Things did not happen overnight. However, Biniak said that the increase in Nokia smartphone sales indicates that the company is moving in the right direction. He optimistically stated that the scale of Nokia's user base will not be a problem in the future, partly because Windows users currently spend more time on apps than Android users. In addition, Nokia will also leverage Microsoft's technological advantages on its smartphones.

"We are trying to create new highlights for our devices," Biniak said, "Yes, Instagram exists on the iOS platform; Yes, the Android platform also has Instagram; now, this app has entered Nokia Lumia. But it will perform better on Lumia." (Kirk)
