Poverty Mouth Little 8's Love Song of Dongli Lake

by e82794927 on 2009-08-27 10:01:40

This drama tells a romantic story in a rare pink humor with the flavor of cinnamon and clove, about a chatterbox girl who has just graduated and a poor PhD student from Peking University. The language is extremely humorous, vivid and full of wisdom of life. Even the gray behaviors such as being talkative, stingy and haggling seem very funny and lovely. We can feel an optimistic and upward motivation in the burst of laughter. This story has no trace of urban restlessness, no petty bourgeoisie and no violence. The girl in the drama is not pretty and has some petty meanness in her bones. She is stingy, loves to be sentimental and yearns for love. This down-to-earth but unusual happiness is right beside you and me. Apart from telling a touching love story, this drama also gives profound enlightenment on what kind of love view the youth should establish. After graduating from university, Beijing girl Badingding found herself in the first setback of her life. She thought she was the material for a strong person, but in fact, she was an unemployed young woman who was not liked by the neighborhood committee. However, a notification that fell from the sky changed everything. She thought she would start a new life in unfamiliar Dongli Lake, but encountered a series of troubles. That seemingly smart and capable female white-collar worker always seemed to be hiding something. And there was a roommates Bai Lin who was always sneaky and weird, torturing herself. The person she had regarded as a natural enemy since childhood, the cousin who always talked back, actually became her colleague. Of course, these were not a big deal. The worst thing was the arranged blind date object, Fox Doctor, arranged by her mother. Although Xiao vowed never to have anything to do with this person, fate always made them meet unexpectedly. When Xiao was troubled, a prince riding a white horse, not driving a BMW, appeared in front of Xiao. Did life change from then on? Is fate really as beautiful as depicted in fairy tales? In fact, life is like a box of filled chocolates. You will never know what it tastes like until you open the wrapper.
