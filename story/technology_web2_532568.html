W1 Panasonic washing machine produces a continuous "click, click" sound while draining - Repair case i3

by gccn76xh on 2011-11-03 21:41:19

Source: Beijing Panasonic Washing Machine Repair Phone http://blog.tianya.cn/blogger/blog_main.asp?BlogID=3444727.

Fault Analysis: After connecting the power supply, it was found that the washing function was normal. When draining, the electromagnetic coil of the drain valve made a "click, click" sound. Upon removing the electromagnet for inspection, it was discovered that one of the coils had an open circuit.

The control pulse output by the microprocessor 14021WFW passes through transistor BG3 to R15, triggering the bidirectional thyristor TR3 to conduct. As a result, the alternating current (AC) voltage of 220V is rectified by a bridge and applied to the electromagnetic coil of the drain valve. Initially, switch K is closed, causing a short circuit in coil B. A large current flows through coil A, generating a magnetic field that moves the core of the electromagnet, thereby opening the drain valve. Once the core reaches its position, it triggers switch K to open, at which point coils B and A are connected in series, reducing the current to a smaller value. Due to the many turns of wire in the series connection, this small current is still sufficient to maintain the open state of the drain valve. Normally, the resistance of coil A is 120Ω, while the combined resistance of coils A+B is 3300Ω. At the start of drainage, K is closed. After the microprocessor 14021WFW outputs the drainage control pulse, there is current flowing through coil A, causing the core of the electromagnet to move and open the drain valve. Once the core reaches its position, K opens. Due to the open circuit in coil B, the current in coil A disappears. Under the action of the spring inside the drain valve, the core is pulled out, closing the drain valve. The switch K then automatically closes again, allowing current to flow through coil A once more, reopening the drain valve. This forms a periodic cycle of opening and closing, resulting in the rapid "click, click" sound.

Panasonic washing machine emits continuous and urgent "click, click" sounds during drainage.

Fault Inspection and Repair: Replace the solenoid valve, test the machine after turning it on, and confirm that the washing machine operates normally, thus resolving the fault.
