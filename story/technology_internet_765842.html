A small survey: the mobile phone world of young people

by anonymous on 2013-08-26 14:09:20

Tim Green, editor of Mobile-ent, is on vacation in a remote area of Wales. Without access to his usual network, he can't keep up with major tech news at all times. Thus, he decided to interview a few young people to see what their mobile habits are like. These conversations may not be very representative, but overall they are quite interesting. The three interviewees are: Lois (16 years old), Marnie (14 years old), and Lucy (12 years old).

I absolutely hate BlackBerry

The iPhone is the choice for most young people. Marnie and her friends all use iPhones, and Lois says that out of her more than 70 friends, only four use BlackBerrys, while the rest use iPhones. Lucy uses a BlackBerry, but she says she really hates it. When asked why she dislikes BlackBerry, she said, "It keeps crashing, the camera is terrible. It's not even that old — less than a year."

In fact, they used to use BlackBerrys too, but starting from last Christmas, everyone began switching phones. Now, everyone whose contract has expired wants an iPhone. BlackBerry's new phones have been completely ignored by young people. Lois said, "I've never seen anyone use it. Interestingly, two years ago, everyone knew when BlackBerry released a new phone, and they all wanted one. Now, no one cares."

Lois still misses the full keyboard and BBM (BlackBerry Messenger). He said there is nothing on the iPhone that can replace BBM. When he heard that BBM would be coming to the iPhone, he was very happy and said he had to try it, but he didn't understand why BlackBerry would do that, "That's one of the reasons people like BlackBerry."

Girls don’t use Android

Although the Android system has conquered the world, these young people say they don’t know what it is. Lucy said, "Are you talking about HTC and Sony? Those big and bulky phones?" So Tim Green explained a bit, then Lois said that some boys have Android phones, but none of the girls do. Girl Marnie said she doesn't know anyone who uses an Android phone.

Nokia phones are just backups

Lucy said she knows a boy who uses a Nokia Lumia, but "he has no other choice." Lois said many boys use cheap Nokia phones because they don’t care much about their phones, whereas girls are different, "If you see a girl using an old Nokia phone, it's probably because her main phone broke or was confiscated or something like that."

Using phones is wasting time

There’s always information on smartphones to check. There are always new messages to reply to, or new things on YouTube to watch. Lois said sometimes he can't take it anymore, so he throws the phone aside and does something else. Marnie said that at least it's a form of socializing, "Unlike wasting time on Minecraft, which is aimless and sad."

Lucy said she isn't addicted to her phone, but she is addicted to her iPad. "The school gave us iPads, and we just use them for Instagram, SnapChat, and YouTube. Everyone is always connected to the internet. They must think iPads will help us study, but it's completely counterproductive."

No one buys ad-free paid versions

Lucy said her mother doesn't even know how to set up an iTunes account; she just buys stuff with gift cards. Lois has his own account, but he hardly buys anything. He hasn't bought any music either, "I used to use Limewire, now I record YouTube." Marnie said that since the Music Tube app came out, no one goes to iTunes to buy music. As for paid apps, she said, "Sometimes the paid version blocks ads. I don't know who would pay for that. You can just ignore the ads."
