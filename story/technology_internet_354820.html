Will Vanke's price cut trigger a domino effect in the real estate market?

by cscj010181 on 2010-05-10 13:27:18

Vanke, the fish that stirs the mud in the real estate market by lowering prices, after first making waves in the Pearl River Delta at the end of last year, has now also made a move in the Yangtze River Delta: it will offer a 95% discount on about 10 housing developments in Shanghai during the Lantern Festival of the Year of the Rat, with concentrated promotional sales. If the signs of a turning point in housing prices become increasingly evident, those who act accordingly first will definitely be the beneficiaries. In the situation where housing prices far exceed the actual purchasing power level of the people, under the condition of increased effective supply, developers acting against the trend will only invite stricter regulatory measures. The domino effect is very likely to appear under the combined influence of internal and external forces, and when that happens, those who realize it the latest will be the most damaged.

When Wang Shi put forward his theory of the turning point, he was denied or even ridiculed by his peers. But Wang Shi still systematically unfurled the banner of price reduction. On December 9th last year, Vanke's Golden Kangyuan on Guangzhou's Kangwang Road officially opened for sale, and all units were sold out in less than two hours. The reason lies in Vanke's opening price of an average of 13,000 yuan per square meter, much lower than the previously disclosed price of over 20,000 yuan per square meter. During this year's Lantern Festival, many of Vanke's housing developments in Shanghai, such as Baoma Garden, Four Seasons Flower City, Holiday Scenery, etc., offered a 5% discount. In the Shanghai real estate market, which has always been characterized by rising prices, multiple housing developments under one real estate company offering unified discounts is quite rare.

Unlike before, Vanke's price reduction actions have elicited responses from its peers. Zhang Yuliang, the boss of Shanghai Greenland, the leader of this Shanghai real estate company, closely followed Vanke and sold the Xianxianfang project under his company at a 10% discount. In Guangzhou, commercial properties priced below affordable housing have successively entered the market, directly leading to the abandonment of purchases of more than 200 sets of affordable housing. Moreover, in January this year, the average price of newly built residential housing in the ten districts of Guangzhou was 9,766 yuan per square meter, lower than Guangzhou's housing price in October last year (11,574 yuan per square meter). The price reduction phenomenon not only means that Wang Shi has allies that make the turning point in the real estate market increasingly clear, but also means that more and more real estate developers are beginning to reassess the trend of housing prices and make rational choices to act accordingly. This may trigger a domino effect.

It should be pointed out that in the domino effect, the energy released when a tile falls is cumulative rather than evenly transmitted. When a tile falls, its gravitational potential energy is converted into kinetic energy and transferred to the second tile. The second tile transfers the sum of the kinetic energy transferred from the first tile and the kinetic energy converted from its own gravitational potential energy during the falling process to the third tile. Thus, each tile falls with greater kinetic energy than the previous one, so their speeds get faster and faster, meaning the energy they release sequentially gets larger and larger, thereby producing a cumulative effect. Physicist Whitehead of the University of British Columbia points out that according to the reasoning that a falling tile can knock down a tile 1.5 times its volume, the energy released when the 13th tile falls is more than 2 billion times that of the first tile. If a set of tiles is made based on this principle, a skyscraper could be pushed down with just a finger's force.

We might as well analyze the trend of housing prices based on the domino effect. If the signs of a turning point in housing prices become increasingly evident, it is certain that those who act first will be strong, i.e., those who act accordingly first will benefit earliest. First, they sell houses at relatively higher prices. When the wave of price reductions truly begins, the selling price of later reducers will be lower. Second, early reducers face the least competition, have the best house sales, and the fastest capital inflow, and the improvement in efficiency is equivalent to indirectly increasing unit capital gains, while the opposite is true for later reducers. Besides, there is also the enhancement of corporate image brought by acting accordingly. Those forced to reduce prices due to funding pressures, downward price pressures, and sales pressures may fall into a passive situation. Especially when the wave of price reductions triggers people's clear expectations of future downward pressure on housing prices, it is very easy to form a domino effect. In fact, in the housing price decline cycles of many countries, when housing prices initially show signs of decline, the depth and speed of decline after the formation of the decline channel are far less than what occurs later. This principle is the same as the large increase in housing prices in the upward channel.

It should be recognized that an important basis for Wang Shi's insistence on the turning point theory lies in the change in demand, which includes two scenarios:

One is the decrease in actual demand capacity caused by housing prices far exceeding residents' incomes. The essence of housing is a consumer good, and the price of consumer goods is determined by residents' actual purchasing power. The price trend of any commodity that deviates or severely deviates from residents' actual purchasing power levels cannot continue indefinitely; the other is the reduction in demand for commercial housing caused by the increase in the supply of affordable housing. As Wang Shi said: the future welfare security system will be divided into two layers, one for low-income groups, and the other to attract talent and enhance city competitiveness, including incorporating high-tech talents into the urban welfare security system. Wang Shi's speculation on social security affordable housing is quite forward-looking. In fact, after the挤out and hindrance effects of high housing prices on high-tech talents, incorporating high-tech talents and other talents significant for enhancing city competitiveness into the affordable housing system has become an inevitable trend. The reduction in demand for commercial housing supplied by the market will inevitably affect the market's expectation of future housing price trends.
