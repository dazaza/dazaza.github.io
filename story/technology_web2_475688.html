"Interview" Yi Waimai: The takeout expert on your phone

by ybcz0519 on 2011-08-12 21:42:45

Ewaimai is a geo-location based food delivery reservation service. Through this application, users can conveniently check the nearby restaurants that deliver food. Users can also place their food orders via their mobile phones and complete the order directly on their phone.

Main functions:

- Find meals: Provides nearby food delivery options based on your location, allowing you to easily find good food wherever you are.

- Place orders: Browse through your favorite food delivery shops, select your dishes, confirm and submit your order.

- Favorites: Save the food merchants you frequently order from.

- Track: Follow the status of your order; after approval, wait for the food to be delivered.

A simple trial of this app shows that ordering food is relatively straightforward, requiring just three steps: check nearby food delivery merchants, place an order; confirm the order; input detailed delivery address. In terms of model, Ewaimai allows direct food ordering via the app instead of the traditional method of ordering by phone, which should involve merchants using dedicated food delivery reservation services. If food delivery merchant resources could be integrated through some kind of system, such food delivery services would be very valuable!

We have always been paying attention to food ordering services. Previously, we summarized some domestic food ordering services. Although there is a large demand for food delivery reservations, there are several issues with food delivery services that need to be resolved. One is how to timely obtain information about changes in food prices; the second is how to track the delivery status of food orders. For this reason, we conducted a special interview with the Ewaimai team [Huayi Technology (Etouch), www.etouch.cn]. Let's take a look at their understanding of food delivery services together.

Web20Share: Ordering food via mobile phones, in terms of demand, should be a very promising market. Regarding food ordering, we have previously paid attention to some food ordering websites. Many food ordering websites only provide the telephone number for placing orders. A big problem is the inability to promptly obtain information about changes in food prices. How do you handle this issue?

Ewaimai Team: We have a comprehensive system where merchants can log into our system to complete all operations, including menu updates, service times, and orders. We can even control down to the minute. As for orders, they actually go through our system. After the user submits the order, it flows in two ways: one is directly to our backend, where we handle it, and the other is directly to the merchant's end, where they handle it through the backend. Additionally, we have developed a tablet computer system, which is now ready for use. All functions have been completed. Merchants without computers can use our 3G tablet computers to process information. Currently, we have developed Android Pads.

Web20Share: Would such a model require more offline business development teams in the early stages to expand the merchant base?

Ewaimai Team: Business-wise, we need to invest more effort in developing merchants in the early stages. Once the platform's influence grows, we believe more merchants will be willing to actively join and use our services.

Web20Share: Regarding food delivery reservations, many users are very concerned about the delivery time indicator. However, this indicator is indeed difficult to control in practice. How do you handle this?

Ewaimai Team: Logistics are solved by the sellers themselves, and we do not interfere. However, we ensure that all our merchants have delivery capabilities. Our system analyzes data such as merchant response speed and processing efficiency, which can serve as a certain constraint on merchants.

Web20share: How do you consider the credit system for food delivery services?

Ewaimai Team: After receiving the food, users have a "confirm receipt" function in our software. At this point, users can review the service, speed, and taste.

Web20Share: Regarding "confirm receipt," unlike Taobao where payments are made online, food delivery involves offline payments. Therefore, the motivation to "confirm receipt" after receiving the item might not be strong. How do you incentivize users to "confirm receipt"?

Ewaimai Team: Our system has considered a points system. Currently, our product has only two versions, and we will enhance user incentives in the future. This area is currently relatively weak. Additionally, we have organized many activities with local merchants in Nanjing, providing some motivation.

Web20Share: What are the future plans for the product?

Ewaimai Team: Currently, we hope to make the product perfect and develop the user base first. In the future, we will also add more practical food delivery reservation-related features, such as integrating discount coupons for meal ordering, and even considering direct mobile payment methods in the future.
