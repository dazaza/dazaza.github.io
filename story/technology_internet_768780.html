91 Wireless CEO Talks About Being Acquired by Baidu for the First Time: Facing Different Challenges

by anonymous on 2013-11-22 11:51:10

At an event held by the American tech blog TechCrunch in Shanghai, Hu Zemin, CEO of 91 Wireless, revealed that he will be shifting his focus to angel investing, providing guidance to entrepreneurs.

This was Hu Zemin's first public appearance since Baidu acquired the company for $1.9 billion in July this year. He also shared various thoughts on joining Baidu.

Prior to being acquired by Baidu, 91 Wireless had already been a leading enterprise in China’s Android application industry. However, Hu Zemin stated that with the continuous increase in smartphone penetration in China, competition is expected to intensify further.

“I wrestled with the decision of whether or not to sell the company. When I discussed it with other companies, they were all shocked by my idea,” Hu Zemin said, “But I indeed found the competition in the mobile internet industry becoming increasingly fierce. 91 Wireless used to be a subsidiary of NetDragon, and we performed well. But I believed that due to synergistic effects, we could collaborate with Baidu. The $1.9 billion is not the end; we still aim to grow larger.”

Hu Zemin expressed that he appreciates Baidu’s culture which emphasizes "simplicity and reliability" in their products.

Critics have argued that the $1.9 billion valuation was too high. In response, Hu Zemin said: “I cannot say whether the price was too high or too low. We just needed someone who could see how 91 Wireless would transform China’s internet. I think we might collaborate with either Baidu or Alibaba, as these giants control the strategic landscape of China’s internet.”

When Hu Zemin joined 91 Wireless in 2008, the company was merely a small subsidiary of NetDragon with only 10 employees. He became the CEO the following year. Despite 91 Wireless's rapid growth, it also faced growing pains. At one point, one-third of their employees were poached by larger companies. Hu Zemin mentioned that he once felt so anxious about the debts owed by 91 Wireless that he almost smashed his own car.

He said: “I thought, if I die, it wouldn't be anyone else's fault but mine. I had to find a way out of depression. It's a mindset. No matter what kind of business you start, there will always be tough times, and you must find ways to overcome them. You can't let difficulties crush you; instead, you should view them as a test to enhance your capabilities.”

Despite some setbacks faced by 91 Wireless, Hu Zemin said that his team remained optimistic. After joining Baidu, he has become busier than before. “At 91 Wireless, as long as I saw daily growth, I was satisfied. I was obsessed with our market share. But at Baidu, I face different challenges from other large enterprises, and Baidu also has very high goals,” he said.
