Nanjing female investigator Wen Xiurong's weibo on women's anti-sexual harassment skills has been forwarded more than 10,000 times

by edivista on 2011-12-29 10:48:44

Click to enter Nanjing policewoman Wen Xiurong's Weibo

Since she opened her Weibo account on May 12 this year, the female detective of Nanjing has attracted much attention from netizens by posting less than 400 Weibos. Recently, her video teaching women anti-wolf skills has been forwarded more than a thousand times by netizens. On the 18th, the reporter connected with the blogger of "Nanjing Female Detective" Weibo, Wen Xiurong. She frankly said that in face of wolves, escape is the best strategy, and the anti-wolf skills are emergency moves used when there is no other choice. (Click to enter Nanjing policewoman Wen Xiurong's Weibo) Female police officer demonstrates anti-wolf skills in person. Recently, Wen Xiurong, a female detective at the Yanzi矶 Police Station of the Qixia Branch of the Nanjing Public Security Bureau and the blogger of "Nanjing Female Detective", posted a video on Weibo teaching women self-defense skills. In the video, she and her male colleagues cooperate to demonstrate the techniques for dealing with various possible situations where women may encounter wolves on the road. In the video, whether it is being grabbed by the collar when encountering a wolf face-to-face; being hugged from behind by a wolf; or having your hair pulled or tangled during resistance, Officer Wen has special moves for each situation.

After this video was posted on Weibo, it has been forwarded more than a thousand times by netizens. Netizen comments: Powerful, such teaching is very practical, women should learn. While netizens quickly forward it, Wen Xiurong also reminds that if the offender is only involved in a simple property crime, it is suggested that women do not resist or struggle excessively to avoid provoking the offender and causing unnecessary personal injury. You can appropriately give some money or items to the offender, then after the offender leaves, go to a safe place and call 110 to report the incident in time. Wen Xiurong said that as long as you master the key movements, dealing with wolves will definitely be effective. She hopes that citizens practice more. However, she suggests that you use this move to subdue the enemy only when you cannot escape. Once you get a chance to break free, leave quickly and do not continue to confront the offender. The most popular Weibo post was forwarded tens of thousands of times. This time, the Wen Xiurong who promoted the anti-wolf skill video, her most famous Weibo was a warm reminder not to flaunt happiness, which was forwarded more than 43,400 times by netizens and retweeted by celebrities like Yao Chen and Ma Yuli. CCTV also made a special report on it.

Always see parents proudly using pictures of their children as Weibo avatars. Inside the Weibo posts, they often mention their child's name, kindergarten or school name, and pictures often reveal the neighborhood where they live. In fact, it's easy to compile the child's name, home address, school, appearance, and the work of the parents. I hope parents realize that these are important privacy concerns for children and weigh carefully before posting. On October 31st, the female detective of Nanjing posted this Weibo, resonating with many parents. Why did she think of posting such a Weibo? Originally, Wen Xiurong herself is a mother of a two-and-a-half-year-old child. When browsing Weibo, she found that many friends like to upload pictures of their children to Weibo and even mention information about their children's kindergartens or schools in Weibo. This makes it easy for people with ill intentions to take advantage of the situation. Wen Xiurong said that considering this, she issued the Weibo warning against flaunting happiness, giving young parents a heads-up. A responsible person from the Qixia Public Security Bureau told the reporter that Wen Xiurong is an 80s-born native of Hainan. She graduated from the Nanjing Forest Police College Special Police major in 2004 and was assigned to the Nanjing Municipal Public Security Bureau Special Police Detachment after graduation. In 2008, she was transferred to the Yanzi矶 Police Station of the Qixia Branch, and she is a backbone detective in the station. Because of her strong skills, Wen Xiurong once served as a bodyguard for important guests visiting Nanjing along with her teammates.
