Preparation before going under the knife for a skin wrinkle removal surgery

by 865512 on 2008-12-16 10:53:50

If you want to undergo wrinkle removal surgery -- This type of cosmetic surgical procedure is relatively complex in the field of aesthetic surgery. Aside from the doctor, the patient also needs to prepare adequately and take good care of themselves post-surgery in order to achieve satisfactory results. If you're considering wrinkle removal surgery -- then let's discuss what the patient should do themselves. Hopefully, this will be helpful to all beauty-loving friends.

Firstly, one should have a correct attitude towards wrinkles. The appearance of wrinkles is a natural physiological process that everyone will experience. Wrinkle removal plastic surgery can only eliminate wrinkles and make oneself appear younger but cannot alter the natural aging process of the skin. It is not a one-time permanent solution. One's expectations for the surgical outcome should not be too high.

If you decide to undergo surgery, it is essential to choose a qualified cosmetic surgeon with rich experience and proper training. You must not blindly seek medical advice and should clearly communicate your views and requirements to the doctor. Consultation about the surgery situation should be carried out to align your ideas with the doctor as much as possible and reach a mutual agreement.

This will enhance your confidence and trust in the doctor, which contributes to the success of the surgery.

Before deciding on surgery, you should make a general assessment of your health condition. For example, whether you have hypertension, heart disease, diabetes, if your liver and kidney functions are normal, if your lungs are normal, if you have any infectious diseases, blood disorders, or a history of allergies. Are you prone to hypertrophic scars? Do you have any trauma or surgical history in the area where the surgery will be performed? And how well you can endure the surgery, etc. If you have any of the aforementioned diseases, you should first treat the disease and inform the plastic surgeon so that they can determine whether the surgery can proceed to avoid unnecessary accidents.

Before the surgery, adjust your mindset and mental state properly. Pay attention to the cleanliness and hygiene of your face and scalp to prevent hair folliculitis and other skin infections or dermatological conditions. Three days before the surgery, wash your hair thoroughly and soak it in a 1:5000 chlorhexidine solution twice daily for 10 minutes each time. Males should shave their heads completely; females who are willing may also shave their hair. Post-surgery, wigs can be worn to reduce the possibility of infection. To minimize the occurrence of bruising and hematoma, stop taking aspirin-like drugs four weeks before the surgery and discontinue vitamin E, salvia miltiorrhiza, and similar medications two weeks before the surgery. Women should calculate their menstrual cycle and postpone the surgery during menstruation or its surrounding periods.

Guangdong Armed Police Hospital Medical Aesthetic Center reminds you:

You should actively cooperate with the doctor according to their requirements without fear. Discuss anesthesia options based on your tolerance level with the doctor and build confidence. After the surgery, carefully follow the doctor’s instructions and treatments as required to prevent complications. Even if complications occur, there's no need to panic. Actively cooperate with the doctor and handle the situation promptly to ensure early recovery after the surgery.
