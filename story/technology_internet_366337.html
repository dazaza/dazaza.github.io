Baidu Tieba to Launch Social Games_Technology Era_Internet_Sina.com

by cacard on 2010-09-27 18:58:10

Reporters learned yesterday that Baidu Tieba would launch its self-developed social game at the beginning of next year. If operations go smoothly, Baidu will open up game interfaces and allow more developers to benefit from the Tieba platform.

Exploring Win-Win with Third Parties

Xun Shu, General Manager of Baidu Tieba Division, introduced yesterday that in the past year since adopting a divisional system, Baidu Tieba's page views had doubled, reaching a peak of 1 billion daily visits, surpassing some portal sites. Its goal, after being "the largest Chinese-language online community," was set as "China's second-largest SNS."

Regarding Baidu Tieba's current profitability and goals, Xun Shu did not provide a clear answer, only stating that currently Tieba's commercial model was mainly display ads, while future commercialization should focus on open platform applications. "We need to avoid inappropriate commercialization. There are 200,000 active Tiebas every day, and Baidu cannot maintain them all. Letting third parties operate some specific theme Tiebas, and achieving win-win with Baidu, should be the direction for the next step."

Before the establishment of Baidu Tieba Division, it positioned itself as the "largest Chinese-language online community." Xun Shu said that the next goal was to strengthen the SNS (social networking service) attributes of Tieba. Its SNS sub-product "i Tieba" has reached 60 million registered users in just 10 months since its launch, becoming China's fourth-largest SNS site, with the aim of ranking second within two years. "Of course, the first is Tencent's QZONE, although they never say it."

Competitive but Differentiated from Weibo

"Any service that captures user time is a competitor." Xun Shu defined the relationship between Weibo and Tieba like this. "However, Weibo is still mainly centered around individuals, with the core being self-expression; Tieba is centered around topics, with the core being the exchange among groups interested in the same topic. In terms of characteristics, the competition between these two types of products is not intense."

Xun Shu gave an example: there are a large number of administrative regional Tiebas in Baidu Tieba (such as Beijing Tieba which cooperates with Jingtan.com), usually the smaller the administrative region, the more popular its Tieba becomes, such as many county-level Tiebas. "These will provide opportunities for some local entrepreneurs, because there are many common local topics, and Tieba will become their potential market and communication platform."
