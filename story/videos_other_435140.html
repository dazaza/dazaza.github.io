Yogurt Fasting Method for a Clean Gut

The Origin of Fasting

Fasting has a long history in ancient China and India. It was a health preservation method adopted by many dedicated practitioners. The ancients believed that human survival depends on "qi", which can be converted from many substances, food being just one of them. Nowadays, people basically rely on food to sustain life. In ancient times, animals and humans only ate when they were hungry. Now, eating has become a habit. Many times, we cannot tell how much of what we eat is necessary for our body and how much is just for "satisfying our cravings".

Because of overeating, the balance of what our body needs becomes disrupted, placing a great burden on the internal organs. Excessive nutrients accumulate in the body, leading to obesity and toxin accumulation.

Therefore, one theory of fasting is: perhaps we need less than what we eat. On the other hand, humans gather energy through multiple channels such as air and water. A higher life form is never limited to food as the only source of energy. Developing several channels simultaneously is a training of life. Ancient sages believed that after short-term fasting, other abilities of humans might be developed. How amazing is that?

Yogurt Fasting Method

Yogurt is a favorite snack for many girls, and this time yogurt plays the main role in weight loss.

During the fasting period, breakfast, lunch, and dinner consist only of a fixed amount of yogurt. The yogurt must be of good quality and flavorless (such as sugar-free yogurt). If you can't stand it, you can add some vanilla essence or lemon juice to change the taste. The fasting period usually lasts for one or two days over the weekend.

Weight Loss Principle

The digestive system gets rest, and the internal environment gets cleaned up. Usually, because we eat too much, there is often a large amount of digestion material accumulated in the body. More and more people are troubled by constipation, which is also due to overeating. The digestive system works non-stop for 24 hours, becoming overly fatigued, and its excretory ability decreases accordingly.

The yogurt fasting method allows the digestive system to rest while cleaning up the stomach and intestinal environment disrupted by excessive and irregular eating habits. Additionally, it significantly improves constipation. Fasting also helps adjust and control the balance of the autonomic nervous system governing all bodily functions.

Modern people, due to irregular lifestyles and various pressures, disrupt the balance of the autonomic nervous system. If this balance is adjusted, metabolic function becomes more active, making it easier for fat to burn, thus naturally achieving weight loss effects. Compared with other fasting methods, fasting without solid food may feel uncomfortable. However, the "yogurt fasting method" gives a sense of "eating," so it is a relatively easy fasting method.

Suitable Population: Patients with hypertension, hyperlipidemia, early coronary heart disease, constipation patients, obese individuals, those who frequently catch colds, and those who cannot go without eating but find cooking troublesome and want to lose weight.

Unsuitable Population: Anemic, hypotensive, and hypoglycemic patients.

Key Points for Weight Loss

1. First, have an active and optimistic mental state, firmly believing that you will become healthier and slimmer through this fast.

2. During the yogurt diet, breakfast can only be fresh fruit. For lunch and dinner, consume 250 grams of low-fat yogurt. If you're hungry, you can eat low-calorie fruits or drink water. After the diet ends, do not immediately eat greasy foods; start with liquid foods like milk or porridge before gradually resuming normal eating.

3. During the fasting period, consume yogurt in moderation and not excessively.

4. During and around the fasting period, try to keep your life relaxed and ordinary. Avoid strenuous anaerobic exercise.

Expert Review

According to American research scientists, milk products contain abundant calcium, which has a weight-reducing effect. Long-term consumption of milk and dairy products makes people less prone to obesity compared to those who do not drink milk. To verify these results, American nutritionists conducted a test. The results showed that the group consuming yogurt long-term lost 5 kilograms, nearly twice the weight loss of the group not consuming yogurt. Moreover, the group consuming yogurt not only lost weight but also reduced two-thirds of their body fat.

From the perspective of calories, drinking low-fat yogurt amounts to only about 320 calories a day, far fewer than the approximately 2000 calories consumed by normal people daily, making it possible to lose weight. Additionally, yogurt has the effect of improving gut health, promoting bowel movements for those with constipation, thereby reducing weight.

However, some nutritionists have expressed concerns: in recent years, many new discoveries about yogurt have emerged, including lowering cholesterol and strengthening the immune system, but none include weight reduction. This method poses little risk in the short term but could lead to malnutrition in the long run. Furthermore, if you do not change your dietary habits and lifestyle, there is a risk of rebounding.

Blood Type Diet for Anti-Aging

Recently, a new weight-loss theory has gained popularity, drawing widespread attention from the medical community. It is the blood type diet advocated by Peter D'Adamo, a naturopathic doctor from Connecticut. Peter D'Adamo was selected as the Naturopathic Physician of the Year in 1990 by the American Association of Naturopathic Physicians. His proposed diet targets different blood types, providing specific diets that not only reduce weight but also slow aging and prevent cancer and heart disease.

Weight Loss Principle

According to Peter D'Adamo's theory, if blood of a different type enters the body, the immune system produces antibodies to resist foreign blood cells, causing them to cluster or "stick" together. This can lead to severe consequences such as organ failure or even death. Similarly, eating foods unsuitable for one's blood type can affect blood coagulation, especially due to external coagulants, leading to problems like heart disease and cancer caused by blood clotting. Currently, Peter's blood type diet is very popular in Asia. Editors have summarized research results from various countries. If interested, you might give it a try.

Type O

Origin: The oldest blood type. The ancestors of type O appeared around 40,000 BC, and approximately 100,000 years ago, most regions worldwide had populations with only type O blood.

Personality: Type O individuals are confident, strong-willed, energetic, but also moody and easily bored.

Diet: Intense exercise and animal protein-rich foods are essential for type O individuals. They can control their weight by eating lean meat, animal liver, seafood, and green leafy vegetables.

Grains, legumes, and certain vegetables like cabbage and potatoes have little effect on reducing the weight of type O individuals.

Exercise: Type O individuals should adopt concentrated and vigorous weight loss methods, such as brisk walking, running, swimming, etc., keeping muscle tissue acidic to effectively burn calories.

Type A

Origin: Type A blood appeared around 25,000 BC in Asia or the Middle East, emerging during humanity's adaptation to new environments. Type A blood is the second most common blood type.

Personality: Type A individuals adapt well to the environment, are meticulous, have endurance and a strong sense of responsibility, but suffer from excessive self-esteem and are easily hurt by others.

Diet: These individuals suit a vegetable-based diet. Soy protein is their best health food, helping reduce cardiovascular disease and cancer incidence while aiding beauty. Meat is the enemy for type A weight loss since type A individuals have less stomach acid and cannot digest meat, leading to fat accumulation. Controlling portion sizes and high-fat intake is most effective.

Exercise: Yoga or meditation.

Type B

Origin: Compared to type O and A, type B is a later blood type appearing around 10,000-15,000 BC. It is a hybrid of Caucasians and Mongoloids living in the Himalayas.

Personality: Type B individuals are indifferent to surroundings, lively, and independent.

Diet: Type B individuals can digest various rich foods. Fish, tofu, green leafy vegetables, and dairy products are their healthy foods.

The primary culprits for type B obesity are corn, buckwheat, lentils, peanuts, and sesame seeds. These foods contain components that lower type B metabolism, causing fatigue, swelling, and low blood sugar. Consuming these foods with wheat is prohibited. Noodles and chicken contain agglutinins that hinder type B metabolism. Type B individuals should eat small meals frequently.

Exercise: Type B individuals are active and stubborn. It's best to use inducement methods to enhance voluntary weight loss, such as placing pictures of slim celebrities indoors to motivate themselves. This method likely yields good results. Type B individuals suit regular exercises like brisk walking and swimming, and sports like tennis and fitness dancing that are not too intense and enjoyable with others.

Type AB

Origin: Type AB is the result of mating between type A Caucasians and type B Mongoloids. In blood type studies up to 1,000-1,200 years ago, type AB did not exist, being the latest and rarest blood type, accounting for about 5% of the population.

Personality: These individuals possess traits of both type A and B, with complex and changeable personalities, showing a significant tendency not to compromise. When pursuing a set goal for a while and finding little benefit, they quickly give up.

Diet: Physiologically, type AB individuals have less gastric acid and difficulty digesting meat. Obesity occurs because they only like to eat meat, converting it into fat. Therefore, the eating method for type AB should incorporate both type A and B methods, focusing primarily on tofu, fresh vegetables, and fruits, complemented by dairy products (yogurt) and small amounts of meat, along with some fresh fish and eggs. Eating smaller portions more frequently is recommended.

Exercise: Type AB individuals have weaker willpower, and long-term dieting tends to bore them. Short-term concentrated methods are also ineffective. Comparatively, medium-term dieting is more suitable. Thus, methods like stimulating acupoints that require little movement become the first choice. On the other hand, type AB individuals' constitutions resemble type A, so exercises like Tai Chi and yoga are suitable.

The blood type diet has already become popular in Asia, especially in Japan and South Korea, where it has further developed. Many people are actively trying this weight loss method.
