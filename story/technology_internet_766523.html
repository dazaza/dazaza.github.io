Among Baidu, 360, and Kuaibo, which one is better at hiding their copyright infringement?

by anonymous on 2013-09-18 21:43:25

360 Movie Directory will be officially launched today. With the release of this app, the mobile movie search apps have formed a tripartite confrontation.

However, after our use, we can only very pessimistically tell you that pirated video content has appeared in all three mobile video apps without exception.

Among these three, which one has the most severe piracy, and which one's piracy is more hidden? The following article will give you the answer.

Kuaibo:

Let's talk about the Kuaibo mobile product first. Among its functions, the most attractive one is the Kuaibo Radar sharing function. Through this function, you can see what content the nearby Kuaibo users are watching, and share with you what they have watched.

Through the Radar function, we searched for popular films such as "Fast & Furious 6" and "Pacific Rim". Of course, besides these popular films, you can also find some indecent video content that will not appear on mainstream websites through Kuaibo.

In summary, Kuaibo Movies is really a great tool to find movies, but don't think about copyright issues. We have almost never found any legal video content on Kuaibo. Because all the content comes from pirated sources, the clarity of the videos found through Kuaibo is inconsistent.

Hiddenness: 1

Evaluation: Boldly doing piracy

Baidu Player:

Actually, in terms of user experience, there is not much difference between Kuaibo and Baidu Player. However, since Baidu Player is backed by Baidu, it naturally appears to be more powerful.

By searching through Baidu Player, you can quickly find the HD version of the best-selling movie "The Great Gatsby" and watch it smoothly.

However, compared to Kuaibo's blatant piracy, we believe that Baidu Player may become more subtle in the future. In terms of the product, the Baidu Player on the IOS platform and Baidu Video have been merged into one product, which includes both legal content from video websites like iQIYI and Youku, and pirated content from small and medium-sized video websites.

We believe that this mix of legal and pirated content will not last long, and now it is more like a transition. Although Baidu Player has gained tens of millions of user installations in the past, this imitation of Kuaibo has caused a lot of controversy and harmed the Baidu brand. After the merger of Baidu Video and Baidu Player, it may mean that Baidu Player begins to abandon the imitation of Kuaibo and move pirated videos underground. Will this practice clean up Baidu Player? We can only wait and see.

Hiddenness: 4

Evaluation: Whitewashing itself through integration.

360 Movie Directory

Through testing, we found that the claim that iQIYI has blocked 360 Movie Directory is not true. At least in the current version, we have already found multiple playback sources from iQIYI through 360 Movie Directory. However, authoritative channels have revealed that iQIYI is discussing blocking 360 Movie Directory.

Strictly speaking, Baidu Player, Kuaibo, and 360 Movie Directory should be considered completely different types of products.

The first two products take pirated videos as their biggest selling point, while the content collected in 360 Movie Directory is mainly legal.

Of course, this does not mean that there is no pirated content in 360 Movie Directory, but rather that they cleverly control the proportion of pirated content.

In a test a week ago, we found the movie "Now You See Me" through 360 Movie Directory. The playback source was an unknown video website. After Youku purchased the copyright for this video, when we search for this film through 360 Movie Directory now, we can only find Youku as the legal playback source.

We made an incomplete statistic: 100% of the content in Kuaibo is pirated, after the merger of Baidu Player and Baidu Video, currently more than 70% of the content is still pirated, while the piracy ratio in 360 Movie Directory does not exceed 5%.

Summary

After writing a lot about the piracy situation of these three mobile apps, we can easily see that the positioning and target audience of these three products are different.

Kuaibo and Baidu Player do not directly provide pirated video content themselves, but instead develop a set of website building systems to let individual website operators build pirated websites. Kuaibo and Baidu Player then provide search results for these pirated video websites and share profits with them.

This form is very clever, completely avoiding legal litigation risks. But video websites are not fools, so Kuaibo and legal video websites are in a state of irreconcilable conflict, and Baidu? Also because of Baidu Player, the relationship with video websites has cracked, but because Baidu is too big, most legal video websites dare to be angry but dare not speak.

As for 360 Movie Directory? This product does not provide software products for webmasters to build pirated video websites, and at the same time, it has made some concessions and adjustments to win over sites like Youku who have been angry but dared not speak against Baidu for a long time.

But would video websites be willing to hand over mobile traffic to 360? On PC, video websites can only present their products in the form of web pages, so they are constrained by search engines, but with the advent of the mobile era, video websites are fully capable of creating a super App that has both traffic and content.

From this perspective, although 360 Movie Directory is very careful with pirated content, video websites may not necessarily support this product.
