Introduction

When mobile phones integrate various functions such as radios, MP3 players, cameras, camcorders, TVs, PDAs, etc., they have long surpassed their initial positioning as pure communication tools and transformed into an essential part of daily life. The rapid advancement in communication technology has further accelerated the arrival of the 3G era. Compared to the previous 2G, 3G moves the Internet onto mobile phones, allowing users to handle images, music, browse web pages, hold teleconferences, and engage in e-commerce on their phones. On the other hand, phone manufacturers seize the huge opportunities presented by 3G, continuously increasing investment in R&D, leading to the release of large-screen, full-keyboard smartphones that support WIFI and WAPI. Meanwhile, the emergence of knockoff phones has significantly reduced the average selling price of smartphones, greatly lowering the user threshold for high-performance phones, resulting in a sharp increase in the number of smartphones, providing a complete hardware foundation for users to access mobile Internet services.

According to data from the International Telecommunication Union, there were approximately 10 million minicomputers globally in 1980, over 200 million terminals connected to the Internet in 2000, more than 2 billion Internet users globally by the end of 2010, and over 5 billion mobile phone registrations. The rapid growth in the number of users in the mobile Internet era is an inevitable trend. The rapid increase in the number of users implies enormous potential, and at the same time, will create significant commercial value.

In the mobile Internet era, terminals become one of the key focuses of mobile Internet development. In response to the needs of mobile Internet development, the development of terminals in the mobile Internet era shows a clear trend: closely revolving around user needs, providing users with comprehensive services and experiences, i.e., user experience is king. In today’s just-opening mobile Internet era, user experience has gradually become the ultimate pursuit of terminal development. The iPhone launched by Apple has set a new standard for user experience, winning the favor of global mobile phone users. The application experience based on Android (Google's mobile operating system) has also reached its optimal level.

Based on 3G networks and smartphone platforms, the various needs of Internet users based on PCs are slowly shifting to mobile terminals. The trend of 3G encompasses the integration of desktop Internet and WAP content, and the development of 3G in China is making mobile phones increasingly surpass PCs. According to DCCI's "2010-2011 China Internet Market Data Report", the growth rate of mobile Internet in China is significantly faster than PC Internet. It is expected that by 2013, the scale of mobile Internet users in China will reach 721 million, exceeding the 716 million scale of PC Internet users, marking China's entry into the mobile Internet era. Faced with a market with a promising outlook, mobile Internet quickly gathers a batch of entrepreneurs to begin carving out territories.
