Born into a moderately wealthy family, somewhat of a homebody, a bit lazy, and even more lustful, with a bright future both in career and wealth, the returned overseas student professional woman—Su Jin became the only candidate for the Third Empress due to her promiscuity, leading to eternal regret.

But no matter how she thought about it, she couldn't figure out what benefits there were in being an empress in the 21st century?

Is it about being constantly chased by paparazzi, accidentally dying in a car crash, then having an ultra-luxury limited edition extended Rolls Royce buried with you as an offering? Or reaching middle age, suddenly becoming tired of palace life, getting divorced and then keeping a much younger pretty boy?

But then again: what's there to fear? Even if they divorce in the future, she could get substantial alimony. And with the title of ex-empress, countless tycoons would line up waiting for her hand in marriage. If she has a son, she will likely be later honored as Empress Dowager, so how is that a bad deal?

Faced with these temptations, would Su Jin abandon her principle of "taking her lust to the end" and willingly become an empress without freedom?
