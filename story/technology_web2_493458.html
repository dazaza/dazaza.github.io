Have gone through lonely days

by wenuzolove on 2011-09-22 17:19:38

It seems that people always grow up in self-dialectics.

Think about it: We are all about 25 years old. It's time to take on the responsibility of a family, but can we really shoulder it?

When you fall in love, just close your eyes and believe, holding onto his sleeve, even if you stumble, don't let go because there is always joy on this road, because there are always obstacles on this road. And most of the time, these obstacles do not come from your lover but from your own heart. How you treat them is what you get in return.

Love makes rational people sensitive and kind hearts selfish. It can also make tough natures become gentle and weak...

Who knows what unforgettable events await us around the next corner?

In our youth, encountering love often allows us to grow, endlessly pursuing it, causing harm through obsession, and experiencing fleeting moments of foolish happiness.

Perhaps it's just because we fear love becoming numb that we marry.

Walking on the road, talking about love.

Sometimes it brings joy and euphoria, sometimes disappointment, helplessness, and the urge to destroy.

You think two people can open up to each other completely, but you always talk about your own things, deepening your own memories separately.

The best years pass by like this. Finally, the impulse to get married arises: so-called love experiences end here. I love him, and I won't find anyone I love more in the future, so I will spend my life with the one I love. Or: I won't find someone better than me.

Maybe: Everything isn't so absolute.

Rather than knowing everything, happily lose your way in ignorance.

I don't know if I am resisting growth or if maturity comes too slowly. I always feel that many things remain distant and beautiful.

In the prime years for striving, don't place too much importance on love. Prioritizing career over love might be wise.

Everyone grows up overnight...

Later, we gather, scatter, love, hate. We experience pain and regret.

While still believing in love, when it's time to embrace, tightly hold on and never let go for the rest of your life.

If you no longer love, bravely walk away. The road is always full of excitement.

How many marry because they deeply love?

Some joys and sweetness you can only experience yourself, some pain and struggles you must endure alone.

There will always be confusion, unsure if those crazes, those desperate outbursts, those countless twists and turns, as well as those slight touches, unguarded harms, all-in bets, dream-like sweetness, dream-like confusion, and dream-like unpredictability, are indeed love.

As mentioned at the beginning, I am ignorant, but my ignorance has been criticized, so I don't fully indulge in enjoying love. Love is inherently blind; how many people can truly distinguish reality from illusion?

Perhaps it's just the fear of such hurtful love continuing to wound the heart that has been battered by past brave loves.

Hastily, the person who is always with you is yourself. When you doubt, the one giving bad advice, when you're in pain, the one adding salt to your wounds, when you decide, the one torturing you with crazy motives, and when you're quiet, the one you dislike or thank the most, is actually you.

Having experienced days of loneliness, I have finally grown to like my own ignorance. Being with them feels comfortable, like a blazing fire. At this moment, let the flames burn slowly without saying a word or commenting on anything. One must renew oneself in ignorance. - Marguerite Duras

Those who got married early almost without exception finished their studies and returned home to work. They began their small lives under the protection of both sets of parents. Those who ventured out locally mostly remained unresolved, waiting for some kind of success.

In 2011, many good sisters and classmates suddenly got married or were on the path to marriage, including that very strong, beautiful, independent classmate who often advised me to achieve something in my career, who quietly and secretly got married and was four months pregnant...

Nuan Nuan finally posted the wedding scene photos from Valentine's Day on February 14th. Page by page, looking at them made me tear up. I only had a few simple chats with Nuan Nuan online. Among the numerous lively photos, none were familiar, yet I could understand the mood behind every face: happy, sighing, worried, wishing, excited, longing, or carefree...

Perhaps I have become optimistic again.

Sometimes, looking back at those events, there is pure emotion, and also a faint sense of confusion: It ultimately feels like one person is in love.

Suddenly realizing that these past two years have been the most significant times in the lives of my peers. Getting married, having children, encountering love, or starting their own little lives. What once seemed distant has so quickly become a part of reality.
