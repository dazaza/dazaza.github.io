Gold futures real-time market conditions - many steel trading enterprise owners abscond, SHFE reminds to strengthen risk management

by yanlabav on 2012-03-05 09:48:18

Sina Finance News, March 2nd - In view of the recent phenomenon where some steel trading enterprises have experienced their owners "running away" due to broken capital chains and other issues, their risk bearing capacity has decreased. The Shanghai Futures Exchange reminded all futures companies today to strengthen the risk monitoring of steel trade enterprise customers, pay attention to their position and capital risk levels, and ensure the stable operation of the steel futures market. The notice from the Shanghai Futures Exchange mentioned that the media reported that due to broken capital chains and other problems, some steel trading enterprises have experienced their owners "running away", involving large amounts of money. Commercial banks have become aware of the potential risks of steel trade loans and started to tighten such loans. In this situation, the capital of steel trading enterprises may be in a tightening state, and the risk bearing capacity of some enterprises may significantly decrease. The Gold Steel Department of the Shanghai Futures Exchange reminded all futures companies to closely monitor the relevant situations of steel trading enterprise customers, pay attention to their position and capital risk levels, and timely monitor and prevent their potential risks. Recently, there have been reports of steel trading bosses running away in Shanghai and Wuxi. Commercial banks are gradually tightening loans to steel trading enterprises. The chairman of Yizhou Group in Wuxi, Li Guoqing, ran away with an amount involved reaching ten billion yuan. Huang Runming, the honorary president of the Shanghai Chamber of Commerce of Jianyang City, Fujian Province, with a background in steel trade, was sued due to disputes over private lending. The brothers Miao from Fujian's steel traders misappropriated bank loans to speculate in futures, involving amounts exceeding hundreds of millions of yuan. Analysts believe that low-price fluctuations, high inventory levels, and squeezed profit margins have led to the phenomenon of steel traders "running away". According to the latest data released by the China Iron and Steel Association, the loss-making scope of the main business of China's steel enterprises is expanding. The industry benefits sharply declined in the fourth quarter of last year, and the loss situation in the first month of this year was more serious than at the end of last year. Data shows that starting from the fourth quarter of 2011, the domestic steel industry's benefits have greatly declined. The total profit in October last year was 1.375 billion yuan, down more than 80% year-on-year; in November it was 1.222 billion yuan, down more than 84% year-on-year; in December it was 3.022 billion yuan, down more than 71% year-on-year. So far, the downward trend has not stopped. Market analysis shows that due to difficulties in reducing costs, raising prices, and increasing exports, the current domestic steel market is still in a process of slow demand recovery and inertial inventory increase, and steel prices will continue to adjust at low levels. (Ding Wen)
