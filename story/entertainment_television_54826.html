Plot introduction:

"The Liangzhou Case - The Black Robe Sect"

During the Wuzhou period of the Tang Dynasty, there were frequent border conflicts, continuous warfare, and the national treasury of the Great Zhou Dynasty was almost depleted. The morale of the border troops was unstable. At this time, Di Renjie was leading Li Yuanfang and the four military leaders on their way to Liangzhou. After solving a series of major cases in Youzhou, Bingzhou, Snake Spirit, Jiangzhou, etc., Di Renjie finally received permission from Empress Wu to return to his hometown of Bingzhou for rest. He wanted to take a detour on his way home to visit his old friend Zeng Tai, the governor of Liangzhou, and bid him farewell. Thinking of meeting with old friends, discussing successes and failures over poetry and wine, returning home far away from political disputes, and concentrating on farming and fishing in the Fen River, Di Renjie felt quite consoled. But Li Yuanfang frankly pointed out to him that he did not have the fate to enjoy such peace. Zeng Tai, the governor of Liangzhou, was a disciple of Di Renjie. He received an urgent report from the court saying that a major earthquake was about to occur in the northwest, and he was instructed to pay attention to disaster prevention. Zeng Tai immediately organized disaster prevention and earthquake resistance measures, and timely evacuated the civilians in the city. However, when the earthquake came, houses still collapsed, causing heavy losses. Thinking of the 3000 iron-armored soldiers escorting the military salary in the desert, Zeng Tai was extremely worried. If anything happened to them, it would be catastrophic. But when the rescue army found this group, all the people in the army were possessed by evil spirits, circling around and continuously singing and dancing wildly. Whoever stopped would die immediately. And the 30 million taels of silver they were escorting seemed to have disappeared like sand dunes swept away by a sandstorm. This shocking incident left Zeng Tai speechless and trembling. Upon the occurrence of the case, Zeng Tai took emergency measures. On one hand, he sealed off the state borders, checked all merchants and tourists, and no one could leave without approval. Any suspicious person would be immediately detained. On the other hand, he reported to the court and waited for further instructions. At this time, rumors spread in the city, army, and government offices that the case was caused by the great god of the Black Robe Sect. Given the superstition of the common people towards the great god of the Black Robe Sect, Zeng Tai required all officials to pay attention to public opinion, control the situation, and prevent civil unrest.

Di Renjie arrived in time. He criticized Zeng Tai's ignorance of the Black Robe Sect and immediately made an accurate judgment on the case. An extraordinary battle of investigation and counter-investigation, control and counter-control began, and the exciting battle to uncover the "God of Death" and "Hell Operation" unfolded layer by layer towards its climax. Eventually, the true Black Robe God appeared; she was the bandit leader Xiaotao and Wei'er. Xiaotao proudly told Di Renjie the entire plan of the so-called "Hell Operation" and revealed the full truth of the "God of Death" and "Hell Operation." This sounded somewhat terrifying and ominous. However, Di Renjie confidently smiled and told a "true story" of the Black Robe God, which stunned the bandit leaders.

Di Renjie started from the origin of the Black Robe Sect and detailed every aspect of their journey and situation, analyzing everything meticulously. At this moment, a deafening shout rang outside. Di Renjie informed the bandit leaders that it was General Fengyang of Liangzhou leading the iron cavalry into the city to capture the rebels of the Black Robe Sect. The bandit leaders were instantly shocked. Then, Di Renjie began recounting his miraculous experience of analyzing and unraveling everything. When he finally mentioned that the various states of mind of the bandit leaders Xiaotao and Wei'er, who thought the Black Robe Sect had succeeded, were actually part of his deception, Xiaotao fainted in despair. However, Wei'er held her up because Di Renjie's story was not yet finished. Di Renjie finally narrated the final battle, detailing the deployment of both sides' forces and the changes in the victory or defeat of the campaign, vividly and thrillingly. When Xiaotao and Wei'er realized their impending failure, Xiaotao pulled out her sword and tried to make a desperate attempt against Di Renjie. Her attack was easily deflected by Li Yuanfang. At this moment, Zhang Huan and Li Lang led their teams to surround the courtyard, capturing the Black Robe God Xiaotao and the bandit leader Wei'er. Di Renjie said life is precious, and only by respecting others' lives can one have the right to survive. Gods are compassionate and do not kill lives recklessly. The Black Robe God should understand this. Xiaotao said there was no fairness or compassion in this world, and only the victors had the right to judge success and failure. Di Renjie said that was why she would never become a victor.

At Xiaotao's final request, she and Wei'er, along with two other major bandits, committed suicide. They still clung to the idea of conquering Ganliang even as they died. Di Renjie helplessly shook his head. Zeng Tai asked Di Renjie why the secret passage of Cuihong Courtyard went straight to the residence of Lord Guiyi but not to the network of secret passages. Di Renjie surprisingly revealed an unknown emotional secret of the ancestors of the Black Robe Sect, which was a meaningful story.
