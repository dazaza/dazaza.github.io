Online shopping is a fashionable and convenient way of shopping. According to data from some survey companies, in recent years, the best-selling products of online shopping are mainly cosmetics, digital products, women's clothing, and accessories. These products sold in online stores are popular due to their low prices. Industry insiders said that with the improvement of people's convenience consumption and personalized consumption requirements, online stores will have a great impact on physical stores. According to Taobao's latest data in 2010, the annual transaction volume of the website in 2009 exceeded 200 billion yuan.

Physical Store: Online Stores Stealing Business

As the saying goes, "a bargain is always loved by three," female consumers have long been highly sensitive to price. The rapid popularization of online shopping has given them the tool to compare prices. The free and quick online shopping method also makes the public no longer favor physical stores. Physical store owners are calculating every day how to retain precious customers, and online store owners are doing their best to create higher browsing click rates. An invisible and protracted war is being fiercely played out around us.

"Yesterday, a girl came to the store to shop, picking and choosing for a long time, and finally concluded that 'your stuff is too expensive,' which really made me angry." Yesterday, a friend who runs a cosmetics store grabbed me and complained bitterly. In this friend's store, I saw this Meifubao whitening isolation sunscreen that was considered too expensive by customers, priced at 89 yuan. However, the search results on Taobao showed that the cheapest price for the same product was only 25 yuan, and the most expensive one was only 47.5 yuan. "At this price, even if there is no profit, it can't be sold!" my friend exclaimed. In the subsequent investigation, I also found that the price difference between the same cosmetics online and offline is common to be half.

Because of the low price, cosmetic online stores have caused a certain impact on the operation of physical stores. Many operators of cosmetic stores interviewed complained that the cost of physical stores is too high. If they lower the price, there will be no profit. But if they don't lower the price, they will lose customers, making business harder and harder to do.

In addition to cosmetic stores, the emergence of "copy number tribes" with online shopping has increasingly shown the impact and pressure on brand clothing physical stores. Faced with the high prices of hundreds or even thousands of yuan for various brands of clothing and shoes, smart consumers choose to try on clothes and shoes in specialty stores, secretly record the model numbers, and then compare and buy cheaper branded goods online. Physical stores become fitting rooms, leaving specialty store merchants very helpless, and these consumers are given the elegant name of "copy number tribes."

Online Store: No Rent, Naturally Low Price

Online stores can break through the limitations of time and space, and their consumer base can be spread all over the world. This point makes physical stores only able to sigh at the sight of the web. Compared with physical stores, the biggest advantage of online stores is that operating costs can be ignored. Opening a physical store, the rent of the storefront is a big expense. A friend who operates clothing in Hatton Square revealed to me that the monthly rent for a store of less than 10 square meters is about 1500 yuan. If you add water fees, electricity fees, and management fees, it won't be less than 2000 yuan per month. That is to say, regardless of whether you can sell goods, as long as you open a physical store, a considerable amount of money must be paid first.

With high costs, the price of physical stores naturally cannot come down. However, online stores do not require rent, and the cost is so low that it can be ignored, thus having astonishing price competitiveness. Frequent online shoppers know that for the same product, buying online is much cheaper than buying in physical stores. A housewife calculated for me: last year, because of online shopping, she saved nearly 5000 yuan. This figure is quite significant for an ordinary family.

Currently, many C2C websites in China do not charge rent to store owners. China's largest shopping website, Taobao, does not require renters to pay rent when opening a store. However, in May 2006, Taobao launched "Zhaocai Jinbao", which essentially aimed to implement a paid bidding ranking "value-added service." As a result, sellers who were already accustomed to a free environment strongly opposed this charging service, causing the service to be abandoned. In 2007, Taobao also launched "Wangpu", but this plugin only charges a few dozen yuan per month for usage.

Unlike Taobao, eBay's profit model involves charging users store rent, product listing fees, and transaction service fees. Although the monthly rent for regular stores keeps increasing, it remains within the range of dozens of yuan per month.

Although some websites may or may not charge rent for online stores, one thing is clear: compared to physical stores, the rent for online stores can almost be ignored.

Business Administration: No Online Store Registered

Operating online naturally constitutes a market. With markets and transactions comes a question: what kind of store is an online shop? Does it need to register for a business license like traditional stores? How should taxes be collected from these stores? In recent years, many industry insiders believe that issues such as licenses and taxation brought by online stores face an awkward situation of regulatory absence.

On April 2nd, the State Administration for Industry and Commerce published the "Interim Measures for the Administration of Online Commodity Transactions and Relevant Service Behaviors (Draft for Public Comments)" on its official website and formally solicited opinions from the public. In the "Interim Measures", I saw that online operators should register under their real names in the future, and those who meet the conditions should also register for industrial and commercial registration; existing business entities with business licenses should make their electronic version of the business license public.

However, according to the Registration Division of the Municipal Administration for Industry and Commerce, currently, none of the online store operators in our city have registered for industrial and commercial registration at the bureau. Deputy Director Tian Hong of the division stated that the "Interim Measures for the Administration of Online Commodity Transactions and Relevant Service Behaviors (Draft for Public Comments)" is just a draft and has not yet been officially implemented. Only after the formal introduction of the "Interim Measures for the Administration of Online Commodity Transactions and Relevant Service Behaviors" will Huangshi's specific management methods be introduced.

"Without a business license, without an office location. A network cable, a few computers, and a few people can start operations." The business administration department frankly admitted that the lack of a business license makes it difficult to supervise online shopping.

The "soft" registration of online stores creates a tax vacuum. Among the numerous online store owners I investigated, the number of people who admitted to paying taxes was zero. Despite some relatively "cautious" websites posting reminders, such as the service terms of the Yi趣 website indicating: "Users should pay taxes to relevant departments in accordance with national tax regulations." And on the service terms of Taobao, there are related prompts: "Users shall be responsible for paying all taxes incurred due to transactions, obtaining paid services, or accessing Taobao servers, as well as all hardware, software, service, and other expenses." However, all online store owners admitted that such clauses are "often ignored, and even if seen, there is no intention to pay."

Future Coexistence of Online Stores and Physical Stores

"The rapid rise of online stores and e-commerce meets the requirements of low-carbon economic development. Without physical stores and resource waste, it is a good way to develop the tertiary industry and a great enhancement to the traditional sales model. Online stores have low initial investment costs and small risks, making them suitable for laid-off workers and college students to start businesses."

Associate Professor Li Tao, Secretary of the General Branch of the Party Committee of the School of Economics and Management at Hubei Normal University, believes that online stores have no time or space restrictions, and customers are global, facing all groups. They break the previous information asymmetry of physical stores, reduce many intermediate links such as wholesalers and retailers, fully utilize modern logistics as support, adopt a direct sales model, offer low-priced goods, and strengthen consumer initiative. At the same time, this e-commerce model caters to the fast-paced lifestyle, is convenient and fast, and fits the modern consumption trend, which is an important reason for the rapid development of online stores.

Li Tao analyzed that although online stores develop rapidly and have an impact on physical stores, they cannot replace physical stores, and their integration is inevitable. Shopping in physical stores provides experiences such as browsing, on-site testing, and touching textures that online shopping cannot match. When shopping online, consumers initially see only beautiful pictures, some of which are even processed artistically, with insufficient demonstrations. Once consumers receive the goods and find them not meeting expectations, the psychological gap is large, and there may be wear and tear during transportation, easily leading to returns. Moreover, the habit of consumers believing in seeing rather than hearing is hard to change, and the safety of physical stores is relatively high, making their advantages obvious.

Li Tao indicated that individual online stores in our city are developing rapidly but are still in the early stage of development. The regional development of online stores is very unbalanced, and consumers' purchasing habits need to be guided and improved. Consumers are mostly young people with higher education levels, and the scale has not yet formed. The development space for online stores is large, but there needs to be a supportive phase. Before reaching a certain scale, the government can defer taxes and fees.
