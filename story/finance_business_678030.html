Android Phone Application: Chrome to Phone Program

by dream2012 on 2012-02-27 11:22:09

Cloud computing serves as the cornerstone of the Google empire, supporting various services offered by Google. Google is also systematically integrating some cloud computing services into the Android system. At this year's Google I/O 2010 conference, they introduced the Android Cloud to Device Messaging (C2DM) service. Recently, Google released the Chrome to Phone service last month, which allows users to send URLs being browsed on their PCs directly to their Android phones for opening. The Chrome to Phone service also supports sending Google Maps locations and YouTube videos to the phone, making it one of the coolest features currently available for Android phones. Although the Google Chrome to Phone service was launched for the Android 2.2 system and does not support versions below Android 2.2, it works with Google's own Chrome browser and Mozilla's Firefox browser on the PC side. Additionally, an Android phone needs to have the Chrome to Phone application installed and configured accordingly. We have listed detailed operation guides for both Chrome and Firefox browser users so that you can use this cool service on your Android phone and get a taste of Google’s mobile cloud computing.

* Android Phone Related Settings:

1. To use the Chrome to Phone service, you need to install the Chrome to Phone application on your Android phone. You can download and install it by searching for "Chrome to Phone" in the Android Market.

2. Open the Chrome to Phone application on your Android phone. The program will first prompt you to log in with your Google account, which should be the same one used on your PC browser.

3. After logging in, the Chrome to Phone application will provide two options: "Automatically launch Links" and "Let me launch links manually", corresponding to automatically opening links or manually opening them. Choose based on your preference.

* For Chrome Browser Users:

1. Google Chrome browser users should download and install the Google Chrome to Phone Extension.

2. After installing the Chrome to Phone extension, a phone icon will appear in the navigation bar. When browsing web pages, clicking this icon will send the link to your Android phone. On the first use, you will be prompted to log in with your Google account.

3. After sending the link from the browser, your Android phone will give an audio reminder and display a notification in the top notification bar. If you previously set the Chrome to Phone program on your phone to automatically open links, the Android phone will simultaneously give an audio prompt and automatically start the browser program to open the link.

* For Firefox Browser Users:

1. Firefox browser users should download and install the FoxToPhone add-on.

2. While browsing web pages with Firefox, right-click any webpage link or image and select the "Send this link/image to Android" option in the pop-up menu. On the first use, you will need to enter your Google username and password.

3. After sending the link from the Firefox browser, your phone will give an audio reminder and display a notification in the top notification bar. If you previously set the Chrome to Phone mobile application to automatically open links, the Android phone will automatically start the browser program to open the link.
