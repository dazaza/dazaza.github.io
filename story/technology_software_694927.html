Tens of thousands of Weibo users forwarded the message of the coffee shop, which promoted the romantic love.

by xue94fwsh on 2012-03-07 13:00:57

Messages in the guestbook Oriental Net, June 23 -- According to the report of "Morning Post", a man proposed to a woman who didn't want to say "yes" this year. The woman wrote down a message on the guestbook alone in a coffee shop: "I will marry you if you can find this message." Unexpectedly, the note was uploaded to Weibo by a customer and was forwarded by tens of thousands of netizens. The man who had no idea about it found the note eventually and the woman happily said that she would keep her promise. This is a real romantic story which happened in Shanghai recently. After being moved by the happiness of the couple, netizens have once again felt the power of information dissemination of Weibo. Internet matchmaker: ten thousand forwarding of the message in the coffee shop "find it and marry" Luo Jinghan, 30 years old, currently operates a western restaurant at Xintiandi in Shanghai. On Monday evening, she was waiting for her friends in a coffee shop on Xietu Road in Xuhui District and was attracted by a page of text in the guestbook. "Chen ..., I will marry you if you can find this message." The name signed on the paper was a woman with the surname Lu and the date was night of June 18. Miss Luo immediately took a picture of the text with her mobile phone and uploaded it to Sina Weibo "L Jinghan" (click here to view the Weibo), "the most touching words I have seen today, I hope that silly kid can see this message as soon as possible". She called on netizens to help forward it. There were other writings next to Ms Lu's words on the photo such as "take pictures with mobile phones" and "forward" etc. Miss Luo said that those were left by other customers, meaning taking photos and uploading them to Kaixin.com. To her surprise, only two days later, the Weibo has been forwarded tens of thousands of times. Miss Luo kept an eye on the increasing number of forwards all day long and was excited. Netizens "at" her on Weibo in a frequency of "per second". Is this a marketing plan thrown out by the coffee shop? In addition to sending a lot of blessings to the couple, some netizens also questioned the authenticity of the photo and guessed that it was a hype by the store. Yesterday afternoon, Miss Luo told reporters that she was not a "supporter". The reason why she wrote the name of the store in Weibo was hoping that the "main character" could find the note written by his girlfriend as soon as possible. Male protagonist: posted a Weibo to thank everyone, saying that he would sincerely welcome his bride Yesterday morning, Miss Luo received a very exciting message on Weibo. The man mentioned in the note eventually saw his girlfriend's message and voluntarily informed Miss Luo who "brought them together". It was reported that after being reminded by a friend, the man went to the coffee shop to take back the note around 9 pm the previous evening. He excitedly wrote on his newly registered Weibo: "I will sincerely welcome my bride." At the same time, he thanked netizens for their forwarding. The reporter called the coffee shop yesterday and Ms Wang, a waitress there, confirmed that the note had indeed been taken away, but did not pay attention to who did it. Since this was a personal behavior of the customers, the store could not interfere. Meanwhile, Ms Lu's Weibo also surfaced. Originally, she "thought (the note) would never be seen by anyone". A friend of hers left a message on Weibo: "get married quickly, I want to be your bridesmaid." After confirming the identity of the other party, Miss Luo told the reporter that she would invite the couple to a big meal. Being able to intersect with this strange relationship in a sea of people was itself a kind of fate. This was the most romantic thing she had ever encountered. Last night, the couple and the "matchmaker" had already got in touch with each other on Weibo. Female protagonist: "I will be an autumn bride" Yesterday afternoon, the reporter contacted Ms Lu who wrote the note. She was just sitting on the train returning to Shanghai after finishing a business trip. She said that her boyfriend was very low-key and felt a little awkward when seeing so many netizens paying attention to their relationship. "Proposal letter? Not at all." When hearing that someone misunderstood the note as her proposal to her boyfriend, Ms Lu said that she "would faint". She told the reporter that she and her boyfriend had been together for more than five years. Recently, he proposed to her but she didn't want to say "yes" this year. Later, when drinking coffee with friends and seeing the guestbook, she came up with this weird and cute idea. "If he can see it, then it is heaven's will that I should marry him and I won't delay (the wedding) anymore." She did not take the note seriously at first and considered it as a way to give herself peace of mind. Ms Lu said that since "heaven is helping him", she would keep her promise. "I will be an autumn bride," she said to the reporter yesterday. Not long ago, the sentence "I will never believe in love again" became a popular phrase online. A large number of netizens were keen on using this phrase to satirize various events. However, after witnessing this romantic story, many netizens changed their tune and said: "I believe in love again." Comments are welcomed. I want to comment.
