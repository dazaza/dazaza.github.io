Case Description: Disfigurement Gets Insurance Advance Payment

When the assistant general manager of Ningxia branch handed over a compensation of 92,000 yuan (an advance payment of 8,000 yuan had already been made) to Mr. Shen, his wife and children could not help but shed tears.

Mr. Shen is an ordinary taxi driver in Yinchuan who supports his family through hard work every day. On the morning of January 10 this year, misfortune struck him. A male passenger robbed him, splashed sulfuric acid on his face, and stabbed him several times. Mr. Shen and his family were at a loss due to this sudden event, and none of them remembered that they had previously insured with Xinhua Insurance. While Mr. Shen's family was seeking medical treatment for him everywhere, an agent of Xinhua Insurance saw the appeal in the newspaper and promptly informed the company that Mr. Shen was their customer.

According to normal claims procedures, Mr. Shen should have received compensation after the end of his treatment period, but because of the special circumstances of the client, Ningxia branch applied for an advance payment without the client reporting the case, which was approved by the headquarters. On January 18, the manager responsible for underwriting and claims went to Beijing Tongren Hospital to deliver an advance payment of 8,000 yuan and a voluntary donation of 4,000 yuan from employees, leaving Mr. Shen and his family stunned by the unexpected compensation. Although 8,000 yuan may not be much, for Mr. Shen, who suffered severe facial disfigurement, blindness in both eyes, and urgently needed treatment funds, it was like sending charcoal in the snow.

After Mr. Shen completed his treatment and returned to Yinchuan, he filed a claim with Ningxia branch on July 17. After evaluation, Mr. Shen was determined to have first-level disability. The company decided to compensate him with 100,000 yuan. After experiencing this incident, Mr. Shen's wife told reporters that she would use her personal experience to tell people about the important meaning and function of insurance, especially the advance payment system of Xinhua Insurance, which can provide timely assistance during difficult times.

Claims personnel delivered compensation: Sudden disaster encountered during out-of-province tourism, timely prepayment of insurance funds provided aid.

On August 28, a tour group departing from Xiangtan, Hunan to Yan'an encountered a major traffic accident within Shaanxi province, resulting in five deaths and multiple injuries. Relevant personnel from Xinhua Insurance Hunan branch quickly arrived at the scene and provided 80% of the insured amount as an advance payment for injured clients.

At the time of the accident, there were 31 passengers on the tourist bus, 26 of whom were employees of a certain group company in Xiangtan. They were organized by a local travel agency for an outing. Before the trip, the travel agency insured each tourist with a comprehensive travel accident insurance policy costing 7 yuan and covering 100,000 yuan through Xinhua Insurance Xiangtan Central Branch.

Within 24 hours of the accident, a four-person emergency claims team led by a vice president of Xinhua Life Insurance Hunan branch arrived at the scene. At Luochuan County People's Hospital where the injured were being treated, the claims personnel from Xinhua Insurance Hunan presented flowers and fruits to the clients one by one, handled relevant claims matters, and immediately pre-paid 80% of the accidental medical insurance money to the 19 injured clients, totaling tens of thousands of yuan, alleviating the urgent needs of the injured.

On the morning of September 7, 2005, ten days after the traffic accident, Xinhua Life Insurance Hunan branch held a "Luochuan Accident Claims Meeting for Xiangtan Tour Group" in Changsha, paying out 400,000 yuan in death benefits to the beneficiaries of four victims on-site.

Major Claims Cases:

On December 25, 2000, a major fire occurred at Dongdu Mall in Luoyang, resulting in the death of 309 people, 79 of whom were insured, including 75 insured with China Life Insurance. China Life promptly paid out 2.807 million yuan in insurance money.

On June 22, 2000, the "Rongjian No. 1" passenger ferry in Rongshan Town, Hejiang County, Sichuan Province capsized, causing 62 deaths and 69 missing. China Life promptly paid out 524,000 yuan in insurance money.

On November 3, 2003, a major fire and collapse occurred in Hengyang City, Hunan Province, resulting in the heroic sacrifice of 20 firefighters. Although more than 57,000 public security officers in the province were insured for accidental injury, the insured amount was only 40,000 yuan.

On June 11, 2005, a sudden heavy rainstorm hit Salan Town, Ning'an City, Heilongjiang Province, triggering a mudslide that resulted in the death of 107 students from the town's central primary school, 103 of whom were insured with China Life Insurance Company Limited. China Life Insurance Company Limited promptly paid out 1.06 million yuan in insurance money and organized a donation of 1 million yuan.

On August 13, 2007, the under-construction Diji Tuojiang Bridge in Fenghuang County, Hunan Province suddenly collapsed, resulting in 64 deaths and 23 injuries. China Life Insurance Company Limited paid out a total of 4 million yuan in insurance money.

In early 2008, rare low-temperature rain and snow ice disasters occurred in most areas of southern China, causing great difficulties to the lives and production of the affected people. All levels of companies under China Life acted swiftly, engaging in claims work. The entire system processed a total of 8,669 related cases, paying out 17.429 million yuan in compensation.

On February 28 and 29, 2008, Jiang Yuxin, an employee of Fucheng Power Company in Jiangxi, and Huang Kuai Sheng, an employee of Nancheng Power Company, unfortunately died on duty. China Life Insurance Company Limited immediately opened a "claims green channel," paying out 200,000 yuan each to the families of the two martyrs.

On February 4, 2008, China Life Insurance Company Limited promptly delivered the insurance money to the home of traffic police officer Kong Xiaoyan, who died on duty in the snowstorm disaster.

On March 7, 2008, the factory of Lixin Da Mu Ye Co., Ltd. in Harbin caught fire. Leaders and claims personnel from China Life Property Insurance Co., Ltd. rushed to the scene immediately, pre-paying compensation to the customers, greatly helping them restore production and reduce economic losses.

At 14:28 on May 12, 2008, a magnitude 8.0 earthquake struck Wenchuan County, Sichuan Province. This was the strongest and most extensive earthquake since the founding of New China. China Life paid out 378 million yuan and donated a cumulative total of 60 million yuan; adopting 624 orphans. The picture shows Beichuan County after the earthquake.

On the morning of May 14, 2008, President Yang Chao of China Life Insurance (Group) Corporation inspected the disaster situation at Fuxin No. 2 Primary School in severely affected Mianzhu County.

On the morning of May 16, 2008, President Yang Chao of China Life Insurance (Group) Corporation visited injured elementary school students at Chongqing Children's Hospital.

After the "5.12" Sichuan Wenchuan mega-earthquake, China Life mobilized hundreds of claims and sales elites from the entire system to assist companies in heavily affected areas with claims processing and post-disaster recovery work.

After the "5.12" Sichuan Wenchuan mega-earthquake, China Life Insurance Company Limited quickly provided personal accident insurance coverage of 200,000 yuan per person for 12 categories of rescue personnel, including PLA soldiers and medical staff, providing strong support for earthquake relief efforts.

China Life cumulatively paid out 6.74 million yuan in insurance money to injured and deceased rescue personnel participating in the "5.12" Sichuan Wenchuan earthquake relief, including 2.1 million yuan for the heroes of the "5.31" aircraft crash.

On June 16, 2008, a torrential rainstorm hit Shenzhen, flooding factories and residential areas and causing significant losses. China Life Property Insurance Co., Ltd. immediately rushed to the front line for investigation and assessment.

On September 24, 2008, the corporate property of Yunnan Yuntianhua United Business Co., Ltd. was hit by Typhoon Hagupit. China Life Property Insurance Co., Ltd. responded promptly to the client's report, organizing relevant personnel for on-site investigation and claims processing, minimizing the enterprise's losses to the greatest extent possible, receiving high praise from the client.
