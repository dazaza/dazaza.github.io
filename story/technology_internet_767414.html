Microsoft: Win8 users need to upgrade to Win8.1 before October 18, 2015

by anonymous on 2013-10-15 21:01:56

Microsoft naturally hopes that all Windows 8 PCs will be upgraded to Windows 8.1, and this goal is not difficult to achieve. After all, Windows 8.1 is a free upgrade for Windows 8 users, and it can significantly enhance the performance of Windows 8.

However, there's always a possibility of exceptions. What if you have reasons not to upgrade? If that’s the case, you should know that you still have two years to continue using Windows 8 without upgrading to Windows 8.1. According to Microsoft's official support page, "After the release of Windows 8.1, Windows 8 users will have two years to upgrade to Windows 8.1 in order to continue receiving support within the Windows 8 lifecycle."

In other words, Windows 8 (RTM version, SP0), which was released in October 2012, will reach the end of its lifecycle on October 18, 2015. Of course, this does not mean the end of life for Windows 8. According to Microsoft's official statement, the mainstream support for Windows 8 will expire on January 9, 2018, and extended support will expire on January 10, 2023.

But this is where the concept of SP1, SP2, etc., comes into play for Windows 8—though it's not entirely accurate to say so, because there will be no SP1 service pack for Windows 8. Instead, Windows 8.1 has taken its place, and future updates like Windows 8.2 or 8.5 may follow.

What will happen to Windows 8 systems that haven't been upgraded to 8.1 after October 18, 2015? Will they automatically restart with a black screen every hour?
