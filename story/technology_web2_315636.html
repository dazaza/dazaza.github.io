Leonardo, Milan, Manchester United, all born for the Champions League; my formation existed 110 years ago.

by qinglai888 on 2009-12-20 09:25:11

Leonardo, Milan, and United were all born for the Champions League. My formation existed 110 years ago.

Xinhua Sports - This Saturday, AC Milan will face Fiorentina away. During the Friday afternoon press conference, team coach Leonardo emphasized the importance of this last match before the Christmas break. At the same time, Leonardo also discussed the status of some players in the team and reviewed the draw results for the knockout stage of the UEFA Champions League.

Speaking about the recently announced draw results for the Round of 16 matches of the UEFA Champions League, Leonardo said: “Our opponent in the Round of 16 is Manchester United. Like Milan, they are also a traditional powerhouse in European competitions. Manchester United and Milan share the same goals, and I think we will be evenly matched. It's too early to speculate on the match situation since the Round of 16 doesn't start until February next year, which is still a long way off. Additionally, this season, there is quite a long interval between the home and away legs of the knockout stages of the UEFA Champions League, so many things need to be considered based on the situation at that time. Regardless, both Milan and Manchester United are naturally born competitors on the European stage. Looking back, every game has been thrilling.”

As for Saturday's match against Fiorentina, Leonardo said: “The last time AC Milan played in Florence was in the final game of last season, which was a very special moment. For me, it marked the beginning of my coaching career, and I never imagined what would happen after that. I remember clearly that the match day was filled with our emotions: Ancelotti's farewell to everyone in the locker room, his words, and the lump in our throats. AC Milan secured qualification for next season's UEFA Champions League... that was also Paolo Maldini and Kaká's last game, although the EU didn’t know yet that Ricci would leave. That was also the moment when Gattuso and Nesta returned to us. Tomorrow we will return to the Stadio Franchi in Florence, where we will face a team with distinct personality. Their style is clear, and their players are in good form, so they have plenty of players who can adapt to any possible tactics. For AC Milan, tomorrow's match will be an important test, even though I don't yet know what formation I will use because there are still many factors to consider. Ronaldinho has some physical issues, so I will make a decision at the last moment."

Leonardo continued: "The loss to Palermo will not affect the positive momentum the team is currently in. I don't think the result of one individual match can change the team's mental state. Although AC Milan lost the game, we did not play poorly. We created several chances."

Regarding the attack on President Berlusconi this week, Leonardo said: "What happened to President Berlusconi was unpleasant. We are not just talking about the chairman of AC Milan, but also the Prime Minister of Italy. The entire team sent him greetings, and at this moment, we stand by him."

Discussing the unique formation he is currently using, Leonardo said: "The formation I am using now was designed 110 years ago. Nowadays, almost all teams play this way; even Palermo pushes many players forward during games, like Cagliari, Inter Milan, and Fiorentina. In such cases, we cannot place the responsibility of being the team's lungs solely on two midfielders (Ambrosini and Pirlo). They cannot be the only ones running in the game. Everyone must take responsibility for their own tasks, and if each player can do this, the formation can withstand scrutiny."

Subsequently, Leonardo introduced the situations of certain players in the team, saying: "Huntelaar has less space compared to other players, and he paid the price for joining at a delicate moment. This season, he hasn't had many opportunities to play, but in Catania, he scored two goals, which made him feel better. Players like Borriello and Inzaghi are ahead of him and are in better form, but despite these factors, I believe he can become an important member of our team, and he won't leave. If we exclude the two months he was forced out due to injury, Storari spent six important months at Milan, and Marco successfully integrated himself into the team. Regardless, the ranking of goalkeepers is judged based on the current condition of all four, and it will never remain static. As for Gattuso, I don't think I had any influence on his decision to renew his contract with Milan. I wasn't surprised by his choice because I have been communicating with him, and I knew everything already. Anyway, it’s normal for players to occasionally have thoughts and emotions. We are talking about a player who has been at Milan for 11 years. When I was a player, I also got injured, and in such situations, a player has time to think and ponder many things in their mind. I believe he went through this moment and made the right decision. He can play in the match against Fiorentina; he has recovered, even though he hasn't played for a long time."

Regarding the January transfer market, Leonardo said: "We know we won't enter the transfer market, so I will strive to achieve excellent results with the current roster in a very optimistic mood."

Regarding the possibility of Flamini playing as a right-back, Leonardo stated: "I won't arrange for Flamini to play as a right-back because he doesn't feel comfortable doing so. Of course, if there is a need, he will play, but he prefers another position. Therefore, we found other options for this position."

As for his future, Leonardo expressed: "When I end my coaching career, it will be the time I end my journey with Milan. After I stop being a head coach, I won't go back to club management. My office at AC Milan's headquarters is still there, but it's empty. I've made my choice; when I'm no longer a coach, it will be the end of my cooperation with Milan."
