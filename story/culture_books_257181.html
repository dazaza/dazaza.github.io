"The Revolution of Familiar Years" - A "Platinum-style" Way of Life

by jqrxass on 2009-11-07 21:00:39

The way one lives the last ten to twenty years of their life is the key to determining whether they have lived a fulfilling life. For this reason, when we reach around fifty years old, we must seriously consider how we will live the "post-fifty" phase of our lives. Unfortunately, most Japanese people believe that at an advanced age, one should act like an elder—living peacefully and staying indoors. They simply equate aging with retreating. However, that's not true. Aging means having more freedom to enjoy life.

When traveling in Europe and America, I often see many older people who are stylish, suave, and happy. Japanese elders shouldn't feel inferior; they should step out of their homes and enjoy their free time.

To achieve this, we must first change the traditional titles for seniors, such as "elderly" or "silver-haired." Some might think: even if the title changes, the essence remains the same. But that's not entirely true—when the title changes, so does the mindset. When we change, others naturally follow suit.

Mature Years Revolution - Part 1

What is the motto of the Platinum Generation? I thought of the pledge of the Platinum Generation: We, unaffected by societal norms, full of curiosity, pursue what we love in life; we don't hesitate to praise others, and never forget to appreciate ourselves, elegant and carefree with a touch of "naughtiness!" This is the starting point of life, and I want to regard it as my motto. In a sense, this is a rebellion against the traditional image of the elderly. If one merely grows old and fades away, it's something everyone can do, but how boring it is. Instead, let's be someone who doesn't conform to their age.

Mature Years Revolution - Part 2

I think this isn't an idea from an elderly operator. It must have incorporated ideas from many people. For instance: to change the overly luxurious impression of hotels that make ordinary people hesitant to visit. Another example: to attract families with children and single women. Even those who decide to stay temporarily while passing by should find comfort for every family member—it's probably achieved with these goals in mind. In fact, men find it difficult to understand the thoughts and hopes of single women or travelers with family. Men usually leave the hotel after checking in to explore the surroundings and enjoy the evening scenery. But it's different for single women. If a woman can enjoy her stay without leaving the hotel in an unfamiliar place, she will definitely prefer it.

Mature Years Revolution - Part 3

Especially when praising the other person, there's no need to stare into their eyes. Try it—you won't find much beauty by staring directly at your aged wife. Sometimes, not looking at all can be wonderful.

Suppose on a Sunday morning, the husband wakes up early to read the newspaper. Just then, the wife gets up—her face upon waking, you can imagine. At this moment, without looking up, he continues reading the newspaper: "Good morning, dear, you look beautiful today"—what's important is to speak first; what you say isn't as important.
