        Snoring during sleep is considered normal by many people, at most it is regarded as an annoying noise, or even as a sign of a sound sleep. Few people associate snoring with physiological metabolism and disease. (Ju Yutang Anti-Snoring Device) In fact, severe snoring poses a danger of being on the "edge of life and death". Once you understand it, you will be "fearful in retrospect".

        Snoring is also known as sleep apnea syndrome. Snoring is a disease! (Ju Yutang Anti-Snoring Device) Modern medical research has proven: Snoring is a signal of a narrowed respiratory tract and breathing difficulty. If severe, it can cause damage to multiple organs throughout the body.

        Sleep apnea syndrome involves repeated episodes of breathing cessation throughout the entire sleep process. There is loud snoring during deep inhalation, followed by breath-holding (cessation of airflow) for several seconds or even tens of seconds, then forceful exhalation, and sometimes sighing sounds. This cycle repeats itself dozens of times each night. During the breath-holding phase, although contractions and expansions of the chest and abdominal walls are visible, due to airway blockage, fresh air cannot enter the lungs, causing oxygen deprivation in the body, medically referred to as obstructive sleep apnea syndrome. Because of repeated breath-holding, normal sleep patterns are disrupted. Meanwhile, due to severe oxygen deficiency, over time symptoms such as daytime drowsiness, headaches, weakness, memory loss, and delayed reactions may appear, causing serious negative impacts on physical and mental health.
