Reliving the desperate struggle on the Korean Peninsula sixty years ago: The Korean War

by jqrxass on 2009-09-22 13:56:23

The Korean War was a war filled with realism: for sixty years, the "war" between North Korea and the United States has never ended, and more political forces have been involved. The game of modern politics seems to have been arranged sixty years ago.

The Korean War was a special war: it is full of the characteristics of modern war, both a life-and-death struggle in international politics, and a demonstration of the power of human spirit.

The Korean War was a war full of mysteries: as the number one military superpower and the most developed economic power, almost no one doubted that this country and its army would surely win in the war; China, a country that was just born in 1950, was in ruins, with everything to be rebuilt, suffering from material shortages, backward equipment, and few heavy weapons. However, China fought for dignity and peace.

About "The Korean War"

June 25th this year marks the 59th anniversary of the Chinese People's Volunteer Army entering Korea, and the "Korean War Armistice Agreement" signed in 1953 was achieved at the cost of over a million young lives. Tracing its origin, we cannot help but return to that war in the early 1950s. The Korean War, which broke out over fifty years ago, was the war with the most participating countries and the highest number of casualties after the end of World War II.

Chapter One: Defeating the Ambitious Wolf of American Imperialism

The difficult historical choice finally came before the leaders of New China standing on the Tiananmen Gate Tower on October 1st.

As early as the day after the US military implemented the Inchon landing, Kim Il-sung sent Minister of Interior Pak Il-yu to rush to Antung in China to inform the military leaders of the Thirteenth Corps of the Chinese People's Liberation Army who were already gathered there about the serious situation faced by the Korean People's Army. For the situation of the Korean People's Army after the Inchon landing, the positions of various units, combat capabilities, and response measures, etc., Pak Il-yu was already unable to explain clearly. He only knew that the troops were retreating northward now, roads and railways had been destroyed, and the enemy was advancing rapidly towards the north.

Pak Il-yu conveyed Kim Il-sung's request to China: requesting that China send troops to provide assistance.

This was the first time that North Korea officially made this request.

Chapter Two: Yunshan - The First Hand-to-Hand Combat Between Chinese and American Soldiers

The Battle of Yunshan was the first successful example where the Chinese People's Volunteer Army severely defeated the US military despite having inferior equipment. This battle eliminated most of the 8th Regiment of the US 1st Cavalry Division and part of the 12th Regiment of South Korea's 1st Division, killing a total of 2,046 enemies, including 1,840 Americans, capturing four aircraft, shooting down one enemy plane, destroying and capturing 28 tanks, capturing 116 vehicles, 190 artillery pieces of various kinds, and a large number of guns and ammunition.

After the Korean War ended, the Battle of Yunshan was included as a model battle case in the book "Introduction to Operational Theory" by Japan's Ground Self-Defense Force Officers School. The book said: "For the Chinese army, the Yunshan campaign was the first engagement with the US military. Despite not fully understanding the tactical characteristics and combat capabilities of the US military, they still achieved complete success, mainly because they faithfully implemented Mao Zedong's ten military principles, concentrating an absolute advantage of troops against isolated and scattered US forces, and actively bravely implementing nighttime bayonet combat."

Chapter Three: Long Live the 38th Army!

Mao Zedong did not know that at the moment he sent this telegram to Korea, an event occurred that would make him sad for the rest of his life.

On the morning of the 25th, US planes flew over the location of the volunteer army command post, and a napalm bomb landed on the roof of the command house, instantly setting it on fire. Because the volunteer army command post had already been bombed by US planes the day before, under the firm insistence of Hong Xuezhi and others, Peng Dehuai and his entourage went to hide on the mountain that morning. However, Mao Anying and several other staff members did not go up the mountain to hide. The high-temperature napalm bomb burned the house to ashes within a few minutes. When the US planes left and Peng Dehuai came down from the mountain, he saw Mao Anying's charred body.
