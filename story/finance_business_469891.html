Fragrant delicacies, produced by Ningbo Fragrant Drift Food Co., Ltd. The level of piracy is relatively large. Firstly, the company name "Fragrant Drift Food" and secondly, the product name "Fragrant Drift" combined with "Youleme", pirated as "Fragrant Beautiful". It failed because of the names; a pirate named Pirate could deceive quite a few people, but two pirate names together are superfluous. At first glance, Fragrant Delicacies seems like a pirate name, and this only gives a sense of riding on the coattails of famous brands. But who would have guessed that the tea cup clearly writes "Produced by Ningbo Fragrant Drift Company." The merchants' actions aim to change the company name into a pirate one so you believe it's genuinely produced by the Fragrant Drift company.

Just from the pirate names, I can predict that this enterprise won't grow big, and the brand will never become well-known.

Despite its strong capabilities and appropriate marketing strategies, it doesn’t look like the work of pirates at all.

There are many counterfeit products nationwide, but "Fragrant Delicacies" makes me feel particularly painful and regretful. After investigation, I found that this company has been in the food industry for 20 years and entered the milk tea field in 2007. There are still gaps and segments in the milk tea market, so there's no need to come up with a pirate name. If done properly, there's hope to surpass Fragrant Drift and Youleme, or at least occupy a secondary brand position without issue.

Regardless of advertising slogans like these pirates, they still shout out slogans different from others.

In the city of Cixi, Zhejiang Province where I am located, small business owners abound, and their business minds are highly developed. They skillfully create "pirate" products, even becoming a significant "tradition." The scope of piracy is extensive, ranging from machinery equipment, refrigerators, washing machines, to cables, sockets, screws, and more. Regardless of the circumstances, if your goods make money, they dare to replicate them regardless of technical expertise or capability. The distance between piracy and counterfeiting is as close as the distance between piracy and a brand. In my view, piracy should always be condemned. First, it deceives consumers, and second, it shows irresponsibility from companies lacking long-term plans for their growth. If ten years ago, the general public lacked brand awareness and piracy could fool many, now in this era of strong brand recognition, it's truly not worth it.

Many young people who love milk tea might encounter such a situation: when buying milk tea at the supermarket, whether it’s Fragrant Drift or Youleme, or even Xiangyue Milk Tea, they grab a few bottles. Just as they're about to drink, they find it's "Fragrant Beautiful"? I've encountered such situations too, and then discovered that finding "Fragrant Delicacies" is also a big mistake, and not just in one place. Pirates are mistaken by other pirates.

I can't help but ask, why do capable companies resort to piracy? Is creating a new brand really worse than using a pirate name?
