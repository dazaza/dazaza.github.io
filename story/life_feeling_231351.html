Hate is always easier to let go than love. Sad space diary

by lwf225 on 2009-08-31 18:18:22

The days in school passed in a daze, almost half a month already. I spent my nights like they were days, lying in bed during the day unable to wake up, and always procrastinating until dawn before climbing into bed.

After taking a comfortable bath one day, I saw my dark circles and bags under my eyes in the steamed-up mirror, and suddenly realized how terrible my life had been during the vacation.

But even though I knew I was living a boring and bad life, I felt powerless to change it or live differently.

This is my current state, so I can only let this kind of life continue with me. I think when it's time for it to stop, it will naturally pause.

I silently believe that day is not far away because my vacation is almost over.

Time is hard to pass but also goes by quickly, these two extremes coexist. I choose to go with the flow. I said, after walking forward for several months, it's time to stop and look down. I don't want to blindly rush forward without purpose, that state is like a headless fly flapping its wings and flying around randomly in the air. That's foolish behavior.

What I want is to clear my thoughts, know what I want, and understand how to take the next step, where to go, and who to go with. Settling the heart is a very important thing. Filtering out dissatisfaction and complaints in my heart is also letting myself go. Looking at this unsatisfactory world purely and embracing it, I think this is something only I can help myself accomplish.

Have you ever, even just once, sat on the floor of your room in the afternoon, alone, calmly thinking about something, perhaps asking yourself questions, or maybe praying in your heart. I believe everyone needs such an afternoon, after a busy week, to quietly review oneself, see the world clearly, understand principles, and then continue on.

The meaning of life lies here, continuously recognizing and restarting.

On the seventh day, the sun was bright and the weather was pleasant. So I squinted and smiled up at the sky. I love this kind of weather, everything is bright, without any trace of darkness, all things can be laid out in the sunlight. I look forward to the season when all things are nourished, I believe it's not far away.

Driving to Shoufu Temple for worship, enjoying the scenery along the way, buying incense from street vendors, praying and seeking help.

Every time I passed a Buddha statue, I knelt in front of the Buddha, bowed my head, closed my eyes, and prayed earnestly for myself and my family. I hope the Buddha hears.

As long as you pray sincerely, the Buddha will help you achieve your wishes. I believe this statement.

So, I prayed and prayed.

After watching "Suki da," another Japanese movie. I still love Japanese movies, the ones I recognize always express meaning through a single lens or a subtle action rather than dialogue, which I enjoy. The gray sky in the lens, the lazy white clouds, and the emerald green grass growing along the riverbank. He sits on the grass by the riverbank playing the guitar, softly humming. But every time he gets to that part, he suddenly stops.

"Suki da" didn't say anything, but I understood everything.

I cower like a coward, deliberately avoiding those lovely children. They are kind, fragile, sensitive, and emotional. One by one, they update their space with some longing and reluctance. This makes me vulnerable. I can only quietly observe, yet each word makes my eyes red, leaving sentimental words behind.

Dear 308, we won't separate, we won't abandon, we'll last forever.

That night of drunkenness, I felt all kinds of pain. All sadness was silent, yet it still hit us with the heaviest blow. Crying while singing "Wife", shouting "Love You Till Death" with all my might.

At that moment, I think we no longer have regrets, everyone understands the unspoken sacrifices and memories in our hearts.

Also, Xiaona, Xiaohua, Xiaobai, Xiaolan, Liao Po, and a certain star. We will all be better.

In the days after growing up, life becomes richer, and there is an increasing desire for childishness.

Continuing at the end of the words, thank you to the man who has always been by my side.

When I got up and stretched on the balcony, the sun was pure and beautiful, the wind made the trees and flowers dance. I loved you a little more than yesterday.
