You haven't eaten the bean curd brain shake for many years.

by anonymous on 2012-02-10 09:49:31

I am a person from a large family with a poor premise. My parents' relationship was strained even before I was born, and as far back as I can remember, their feelings towards each other remained unchanged. Because of their relationship, I grew up not daring to believe in love and even fearing marriage. At that time, I never thought about getting married or starting a family. Later, because of constant arguments with my father for almost two years, I felt like an extra person in the family. I didn't receive any affection from him, and our relationship became increasingly strained.

At this point, an idea emerged in my mind: to get married or find someone to marry me so that my father might stop arguing with me. Not long after, I firmly decided to get married, ignoring my family's opposition. After I got married, my father indeed stopped all arguments with me, and our relationship improved significantly.

However, my impulsive marriage brought me misfortune in my own marriage. The fear of arguments and fights, which I had always considered terrifying events, eventually happened to me. Initially, the marriage wasn't too bad; there was more than a year of relative calm. But after a year, things deteriorated, and I realized I was living the same life as my parents, which I had never imagined happening to me. Therefore, I wanted to end this oppressive and fearful life. I am a decisive person, and if I encounter an environment unsuitable for me, I will immediately leave it. Thus, I chose to live alone.

Living alone is also difficult. During these years of wandering alone, I encountered unexpected events that made me stronger. These experiences, combined with my brief marriage, taught me self-reliance. I no longer depended on men for security, which I had never received from my family either. So, I handled everything by myself.

One day, a friend introduced me to a man. Out of curiosity, I agreed to meet him. After a short phone call, we exchanged photos and started chatting on WeChat. He seemed like a real man, full of masculinity. Although we were hundreds of kilometers apart, we chatted every day, sometimes for one to two hours. From our conversations, I learned he was kind-hearted, optimistic, and easily satisfied. He came from a happy and harmonious big family, something I admired deeply. Despite his past marriage, he showed no sadness, only optimism.

Our chats made me more open-minded, and I felt happier than I had in years. Unlike other men who might want something from me, he was genuine and down-to-earth. Gradually, I liked him and even fell in love with him. His enthusiasm was hard to resist.

We planned to meet in Yan'an, where he worked. I took the train, bringing him two scarves I had knitted for him. On the train, I was both excited and nervous. When I finally arrived in Yan'an, I saw him at the station. He was just as reliable and handsome as in his photos. That night, we drove to Zhidan County, where he lived. Along the way, despite the rain, he held my hand, making me feel incredibly warm inside.

We stayed in a hotel in Zhidan County. Despite being tired, seeing him erased all fatigue. He hugged me to sleep, and I felt a sense of security I had never experienced before. The next morning, he kissed my forehead and called me "wife," which made me feel sweet and happy.

The following days, he took me around Zhidan County, showing me local delicacies and scenic spots like Chairman Mao's cave dwellings. His thoughtful care made me feel warm and cherished. We then went to Yan'an, where we continued our romantic journey, holding hands and enjoying the city's nightlife.

On the last day, I returned to Taiyuan with reluctance. Since then, we have kept in touch through phone calls and WeChat. Each day, he would ask if I had eaten, drunk water, or taken medicine when I was sick. His continuous care made me feel loved and secure.

Encountering him changed me a lot. After my divorce, I never planned to remarry due to the emotional damage caused by my failed marriage. However, he gave me a sense of security and rekindled my hope for love and family. He made me realize I could still be a woman worthy of affection and dependence.

Before meeting him, I thought I would never fall in love again and had lost faith in family and love. He awakened my heart and made me believe in love once more. Through him, I learned what true love feels like.

Living this way now makes me feel like a woman should. I am grateful to fate and my friend for introducing me to such a treasure. I will cherish this hard-earned happiness and build a warm, loving home with him. Meeting you has been my greatest blessing.
