by fenqu on 2009-09-07 12:46:56

How Accounting Websites Roll Their Money-Making Snowball Bigger

According to Alexa's latest ranking, Caakee ranks first among domestic accounting websites. However, behind this "first" is still a cash-strapped company. After three years of establishment, it only started generating revenue by the end of last year; the company consists of six people who serve 400,000 customers. Nevertheless, compared to other accounting websites, Caakee has already developed a relatively clear profit model, and its income is continuously increasing.

Facing constantly failing and emerging accounting websites, how can one reduce failure and achieve success? This issue of the Entrepreneurship Edition interviewed Tian Keshan, one of the founders of Caakee, hoping to glean some insights from his entrepreneurial journey.

Soft Articles + Activities to Increase Awareness

In February 2006, with the intention of making things easier for himself, network technician Mu Mingxing developed an accounting program. With the idea that "joy shared is joy doubled," the first version of Caakee successfully launched online. However, until the end of 2006, Caakee had fewer than 100 users. Around this time, several similar websites began to emerge, eyeing the market hungrily.

In the spring of 2007, Mu Mingxing, actively seeking partners, met Tian Keshan. What kind of sparks would fly when Tian Keshan, skilled in online marketing, met Mu Mingxing, solid in technology? As soon as Tian Keshan joined Caakee, he made a bold vow: if they were going to do it, they would make it the best website in the industry. How would they do it? The top priority was to attract more members; once the website traffic increased, it could attract advertisers. Meanwhile, Tian Keshan was also actively seeking venture capital, but soon the financial crisis arrived.

With no funds coming in, but Caakee needing momentum, Tian Keshan began tirelessly posting on forums day and night, using soft articles for online marketing. Due to the massive number of articles he published, Tian Keshan was humorously referred to as the "King of Soft Articles" by netizens.

The environment of skyrocketing prices in China in 2007 and the 2008 financial tsunami were opportunities for all accounting websites. An increasing number of people realized they didn't have enough money and needed to start keeping accounts. Seeing so many people contemplating how to tighten their wallets, Tian Keshan, with his keen market sense, immediately launched the "Hundred Yuan Week" campaign, encouraging members to keep accounts with specific goals. Powerful accounting functions combined with pervasive promotional campaigns allowed Caakee to take the lead in 2008. Currently, the number of registered members at Caakee has reached over 400,000, with an average of over 2,000 new registered members daily, and up to 30,000 in a single day.

From Part-time to Full-time Corporate Operations

In China, online accounting websites are divided into two development models: one focuses on online accounting software, which belongs to the SAAS model, emphasizing products and services that solve customers' financial needs; the other is community websites connected by accounting, which belong to the WEB2.0 model.

"Although Caakee was initially positioned as an online accounting software, later many competitors launched WEB2.0 accounting websites, characterized as community-based sites connected by accounting. These websites quickly surpassed Caakee, causing us to doubt whether our chosen direction was correct or if we should also transform Caakee into a WEB2.0 site?" Tian Keshan said. Ultimately, after discussion, they decided to stick to their original strategic direction and continue positioning themselves as an online accounting software. "Although many members complained about the unappealing interface, due to limited team resources, based on strategic considerations, we chose to enhance functional improvements to help members manage their accounts and finances well." Tian Keshan explained.

As Caakee slowly grew and the accounting market began to heat up, the closure of several peers raised significant doubts about the security of online accounting. "Since most accounting websites are run part-time by individuals, both time and funding are difficult to guarantee. When work isn't busy, they can keep up; when it is, especially if there are major life changes, it inevitably affects the website. At such times, closing down becomes unavoidable." After much contemplation, Mu Mingxing believed that to eliminate user concerns about security, the key lies in two points: solving the stability of the team and finding a sustainable development path, primarily addressing profitability issues. Coincidentally, in early 2008, Alibaba approached Caakee with a collaboration proposal. Thus, Mu Mingxing seized the opportunity to register a company, build a team, and embark on a formalized operational path.

Advertising and Value-added Services as Sources of Revenue

Solving team issues alone couldn't completely alleviate difficulties; fundamentally, profitability problems needed to be addressed. While WEB2.0 websites generally burned money, Caakee mainly relied on ten thousand yuan borrowed from friends to sustain itself, amidst uncertain economic conditions. By the end of 2008, Caakee focused on launching advertising sales business, achieving profitability through revenue sharing with advertisers.

According to Tian Keshan, currently, the majority of advertisements come from store owners on Taobao selling product ads, along with some B2C websites. Prices vary depending on the ad space, with two settlement methods: monthly payments or pay-per-performance. Besides advertising revenue, starting from New Year's Day, all Caakee members using advanced features, such as sub-user permission allocation, credit card management and repayment, online mobile phone recharges, and online credit card repayments, must pay an annual fee of 50 yuan, payable via Tenpay, Alipay, or bank transfers. "The current business status is pretty good, with dozens of paying members daily," Tian Keshan stated, with May being the best month, yielding nearly 50,000 yuan in monthly profits.

Anticipating Venture Capital to Solve Funding Issues

Tian Keshan said, "Currently, the biggest bottleneck is funding; Caakee generates hundreds of thousands of data entries daily, consuming server resources significantly." Insufficient server resources have been troubling them for quite some time. Tian Keshan recalled that last year, during periods of high traffic, the website became inaccessible and extremely slow. "At the time, we never expected that the number of registered members would suddenly exceed 2,000, dozens of times more than usual." Upon investigation, it was found that the servers lacked a distributed setup, unable to withstand peak loads, resulting in crashes.

This is also a common flaw among many accounting websites—low technical levels and insufficient server resources, ultimately stemming from money issues. Insufficient funds mean they cannot afford better servers, hire more advanced programmers, or bear large human resource costs. "If only providing online accounting products and services to the existing 400,000 registered users, Caakee is fully capable of developing. However, to provide online accounting products and services to 200 million Chinese internet users, more capital is required to drive growth. Financing is mainly needed for R&D, market promotion, hardware, and other aspects." Tian Keshan eagerly awaits the favor of venture capitalists.

Currently, Caakee's primary profit model lies in advertising and advanced feature charges. In the long term, there are also prospects for data mining and wireless value-added services (combined with 3G phones). The website is considering expanding some functionalities, such as collaborating with banks and preparing to launch the "mobile accounting" feature. "From an overall industry perspective, it is still in a relatively initial stage, with unclear profit models. In the early stages of development, relying on one or two people can勉强maintain operations, but once scaled up, operating costs will soar dramatically. In such a situation, whoever finds a better profit model will prevail." Mu Mingxing anticipates that domestic accounting websites will enter a "reshuffling period" in the near future.

Entrepreneurial Data

Startup Capital: 100,000 yuan raised from friends

Revenue Model: Advertising + Advanced Feature Charges + Data Mining

Expenditure: 30,000 yuan per month, including salaries, rent, daily office expenses, and marketing costs

Profit: Monthly Profit 50,000 yuan

Workaholic: Turning on Air Conditioning Is Too Luxurious

In the summer of 2008, to promote Caakee, the main creators worked until 2 a.m. every night. Without money for advertisements, they could only post on forums for promotion, only to have their posts deleted immediately. Despite being banned by moderators, they persisted daily, even sacrificing weekends. If competitors worked until midnight, Caakee worked until 1 a.m.; if competitors worked until 1 a.m., Caakee worked until 2 a.m.

Recalling last summer, Tian Keshan said, "At the time, we had no money and rented a small room, which was particularly hot in the summer. Sitting in front of the computer, I was drenched in sweat and really wanted to cool off in the living room. But a voice inside kept telling me to persevere, believing that only by enduring harsh conditions could we succeed." In that room, Tian Keshan vividly remembered working late into the night, focusing all his energy and thoughts on work, forgetting the sweltering heat outside, then lying down on a burning bamboo mat, sweating profusely.

Although now they finally have a decent office environment and better computers, it's still hot. The electric fan continues to perform its duties, and although there's an air conditioner in the living room, they dare not turn it on because it consumes a lot of electricity, resulting in substantial electricity bills. "For grassroots entrepreneurs like us, we don't have much money, so we save wherever possible. We've already endured harsh conditions before, so how can we indulge in more luxury now?" Tian Keshan feels content.

Review of Domestic Online Accounting Websites

1. Caakee Website (www.caakee.com) ranks 34,281st overall on Alexa.

Its uniqueness lies in the "data export" function, allowing users to download their daily accounting records in EXCEL format onto their hard drives for backup purposes. Additionally, it includes a "Business Center" section with contents like "Accounts Receivable," "Accounts Payable," "Inventory," "Customer Management," and "Project Management," enabling general individual entrepreneurs to handle simple shop finances in the early stages.

2. Zhangben Website (www.zhangben.com) ranks 42,073rd overall on Alexa.

It features a simple, stylish, and practical interface. The search function is a standout feature of Zhangben, along with navigation and friend-finding capabilities, helping you easily find friends with similar consumption interests.

3. Qianbao Website (www.qianbao.com) ranks 86,690th overall on Alexa.

Claimed to be the earliest and largest online accounting website on Earth. It offers a couple's accounting function, convenient for two people to keep accounts together, without affecting either party's ability to account separately even after breaking up.

4. China Accountant Website (www.jizhangla.com) ranks 95,489th overall on Alexa.

It features a simple interface, easy to use, and includes a bank card function.

5. Mymoney Online Accounting Website (www.qian168.com) ranks 96,863rd overall on Alexa.

It employs an accounting engine from the banking industry, professional and stable, supporting up to 26 commonly used currencies.

(Order: Based on rankings on Alexa as of June 9, 2009.)
