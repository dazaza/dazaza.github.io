Gynecological experts from Xi'an Sankin Hospital have summarized the following reasons:

1. A common reason is non-standard use of medication and incomplete treatment. Generally, the treatment of vaginitis requires a certain course of treatment, and the improvement of symptoms such as itching is often very obvious after taking medicine for one or two days.

2. Some patients think they have already recovered and stop taking medicine on their own, ignoring the doctor's advice to continue taking medicine for a few more days. Some patients also don't pay much attention to follow-up examinations after taking medicine. In fact, vaginitis must be followed up to determine whether it has been cured.

3. The male partner has not been treated in time. If the male partner is not treated, there may be repeated cross-infections.

4. Taking large doses of antibiotics or having diabetes can increase the chance of developing vaginal candidiasis. If antibiotics are not stopped or blood sugar is not well controlled, it can cause women to relapse repeatedly.

5. Many women do not take proper preventive measures when they experience discomfort such as vulvar itching. They do not use a weakly acidic female care solution with PH4 for daily cleaning, allowing harmful bacteria to grow and worsen the problem.

6. If there is a history of unclean sexual activity or if the sexual partner continues to carry the pathogen, the patient is likely to get re-infected repeatedly.

7. Some bad hygiene habits, such as washing underwear with other clothes or drying underwear in dark, poorly ventilated places, can also cause vaginitis to recur.
