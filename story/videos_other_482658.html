Understanding Human Nature (Part I)

by ds5xkf6h on 2011-08-28 20:20:02

1) Human nature is evil: You should embrace the concepts of "human nature is evil" and "people are selfish" when you manage others; otherwise, you will definitely suffer a great loss. The reason why Western countries have sound legal systems is that they believe in "human nature is evil". Therefore, they don't believe that the president won't be corrupt, so there are impeachment mechanisms and various laws to restrict the "evil" side of human nature. However, China believes in the inherent goodness of human nature and trusts that the president or chairman is as pure and flawless as Lin Daiyu. But if someone like Hitler or Chen Shui-bian one day disguises himself and climbs to the highest position, our future generations will have a hard time. Recently, I often download "America's Funniest Home Videos" from the Internet to watch. Last night, I saw twin babies who were not yet one year old start throwing bottles at each other to compete for milk and their mother's affection, which shows that "human nature is evil" or "people are selfish" has a certain scientific basis. If two dying people get a steamed bun, and one person gives it up selflessly to the other, I believe there are only two reasons: one is "great love" makes him transcend his nature, and the other is that this person has been completely brainwashed. How likely is such selfless behavior to occur worldwide? Therefore, I believe in "human nature is evil". People over forty should not make five kinds of friends. However, through self-reflection and the constraints of law and morality, people can control their nature, just like everyone wants to live a good life, but most people won't steal or rob because of moral and legal constraints.

2) People have a natural tendency to beautify themselves: People naturally beautify everything about themselves and are particularly willing to compare their strengths with others' weaknesses. Thus, they compare appearances, heights, wealth, clothes, IQs, houses, cars, wives, and children. Even in areas where they obviously fall short, they surpass others in self-beautification and self-imagination.

3) When people face strangers or new things, they often make conclusions within 6 seconds: The first impression usually forms in just 6 seconds, and the rest of the time is spent collecting evidence from all aspects to prove their judgment. Since people have a tendency to beautify themselves, after they form a first impression of someone, they will actively look for evidence to support their judgment. For example, if they feel that someone looks rustic and tasteless at first glance, they will then criticize this person from aspects such as clothing and speech, and feel proud of their accurate judgment.

4) People cannot be changed: When you try hard to change someone, you will find how difficult it is to change a person. No wonder the ancients said, "It's easy to change the landscape, but hard to change one's nature." Sometimes a person may appear to have changed on the surface, but often it's just for show. Change only occurs under one circumstance: when a person encounters a major impact. For example, you always advise your relatives to quit drinking, and they superficially respond to you saying they've quit, but in reality, they may not have quit at all. Until one day they become severely disabled in a car accident after driving drunk, at which point there is a 60% chance they will truly quit drinking. Another example is when you advise a friend to exercise regularly and avoid junk food. They may seem to agree with your suggestion, but in reality, they won't change unless one day they receive an early cancer diagnosis.

5) It's easy to judge others, but hard to examine oneself: Picking out others' mistakes and finding faults in them is quite easy, while achieving perfection oneself is very difficult. People naturally prefer to do easier tasks.

6) People inherently like to be praised, psychological treatment and drug treatment are temporarily not applicable to infants, and dislike being criticized: Praising subordinates can make them work more positively and correct deficiencies more easily, while constant criticism can lead to rebelliousness, making them oppose you in everything or find various excuses to defend themselves, which is not conducive to improving work. Educating children probably works the same way.

7) One's position determines one's thoughts: As the saying goes, "If you're not in a position, you don't concern yourself with governance," "If it doesn't concern you, stay aloof." People are only enthusiastic about their own affairs and are indifferent to others' matters. Even if they pretend to care, it's often fake compassion, just to display their superiority.

8) There are only three types of people in the world: those who create problems, those who discover problems, and those who solve problems.

9) People like to talk about others behind their backs: Emperor Kangxi established the secret memorial system, allowing reports to fly everywhere. Those without the qualifications to submit reports could only gossip about others behind their backs to satisfy their verbal cravings. As the saying goes, "Who isn't talked about behind their back, and who doesn't talk about others behind their back?"

10) The mindset of unsuccessful people: I want to succeed. The mindset of successful people: I must succeed!
