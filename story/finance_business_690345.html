Futures - Many steel trade enterprise owners have fled, SHFE reminds to strengthen risk management

by yanlabav on 2012-03-05 09:47:18

Sina Finance News - March 2nd afternoon edition: In light of the recent phenomena where some steel trading enterprises have experienced owners "running away" due to broken capital chains and a decline in risk bearing capacity, the Shanghai Futures Exchange reminded all futures companies today to strengthen risk monitoring for steel trade enterprise customers, pay attention to their position holding and financial risk levels, ensuring the smooth operation of the steel futures market.

The reminder letter from the Shanghai Futures Exchange stated that according to media reports, due to issues like broken capital chains, some steel trading enterprises have experienced phenomena such as owners "running away", involving large amounts of money. Commercial banks have already realized the potential risks of loans to steel trade enterprises and have started tightening these types of loans. In this situation, the funds of steel trade enterprises may be in a tightened state, with the risk bearing capacity of some enterprises possibly significantly declining.

The Gold Steel Department of the Shanghai Futures Exchange thus reminded all futures companies to closely monitor the relevant situations of steel trading enterprise customers, paying attention to their position holding and financial risk levels, timely monitoring and preventing their potential risks.

Recently, there have been news about steel trade bosses running away in Shanghai and Wuxi, with commercial banks gradually tightening loans to steel trade enterprises. The run-away incident involving Li Guoqing, chairman of Yizhou Group in Wuxi, involved an amount reaching one billion yuan. Huang Runming, who has a background in steel trade and is the honorary president of the Shanghai Chamber of Commerce of Jianyang City, Fujian Province, was sued over private lending disputes. Also, the Miao brothers, Fujian steel traders, misappropriated bank loans to speculate in futures, involving amounts exceeding hundreds of millions yuan. Analysts believe that low price oscillations, high inventory volumes, and squeezed profit margins have led to the phenomenon of steel traders "running away".

According to the latest data released by the China Iron and Steel Association, the loss-making scope of China's steel industry is expanding. Industry benefits sharply declined in the last quarter of last year, and the loss situation in the first month of this year was more severe than at the end of last year.

Data shows that starting from the fourth quarter of 2011, domestic steel industry benefits have drastically declined. Total profits were 1.375 billion yuan in October, down by over 80% year-on-year; 1.222 billion yuan in November, down by over 84% year-on-year; and 3.022 billion yuan in December, down by over 71% year-on-year. So far, the downward trend has not stopped. Market analysis indicates that due to difficulties in reducing costs, increasing prices, and boosting exports, the current domestic steel market is still in a process of slowly recovering demand and habitually increasing inventory, and steel prices will continue to consolidate at low levels. (Ding Wen)
