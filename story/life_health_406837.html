Hemorrhoids are one of the main diseases that affect people's lives, so they should be treated as early as possible. Sometimes, patients with hemorrhoids experience constant bleeding, which can be embarrassing. What should be done if hemorrhoids always bleed? Experts from the Department of Proctology at Zhengzhou Anus and Rectum Hospital explain: Hematochezia (passing blood during bowel movements) is one of the main symptoms of hemorrhoids. In the early stages of the disease, there may only be a small amount of fresh blood after defecation, but as the hemorrhoid enlarges, the amount of bleeding will increase. Long-term repeated hematochezia can cause chronic anemia due to blood loss, leading to symptoms such as dizziness, weakness, palpitations, and a sallow complexion, seriously affecting health.

Anemia caused by blood loss from hemorrhoids generally develops slowly and may not present symptoms or have mild symptoms in the early stages. When the anemia is more severe or progresses rapidly, symptoms such as pallor, fatigue, lack of appetite, palpitations, increased heart rate, shortness of breath after physical activity, and edema may appear. Some patients may experience neurological symptoms such as irritability, excitement, and restlessness, which some believe are due to a lack of intracellular iron-containing enzymes. All these symptoms can disappear after correcting the anemia and treating the hemorrhoids. Therefore, if you discover you have hemorrhoids, you should seek treatment as soon as possible to avoid complicating the treatment process.

What should be done if hemorrhoids always bleed?

First, a proctoscope examination should be performed to determine whether medication or instrument-based treatment is needed. It’s important to keep bowel movements smooth, aiming for once a day without dryness. Eat more vegetables and fruits, drink plenty of water, exercise regularly, and promote gastrointestinal motility to prevent constipation. Also, be mindful not to consume too many spicy or stimulating foods, avoid smoking and alcohol, to prevent worsening of the condition. Avoid standing or sitting for long periods.

Although hemorrhoids are a type of anal disease with a high recurrence rate, this does not mean surgery is unnecessary. Some severe cases of hemorrhoids are difficult to treat, and only surgery can achieve clinical cure. With proper postoperative care, recurrence can be prevented. Minimally invasive surgery is recommended for treatment. HCPT minimally invasive surgery is considered the gold standard for treating hemorrhoids.

The main advantage of HCPT minimally invasive surgery is that the operation takes about 20-30 minutes, causing little pain and minimal bleeding, with quick recovery. Patients can resume normal bowel movements within 24 hours post-surgery without pain and without serious long-term complications such as anal stenosis or fecal incontinence. There is no need for hospitalization, and it does not affect daily life.

Advantages of HCPT minimally invasive surgery:

First, the anus is dilated, and a circular suture is placed on the rectal mucosa approximately 4 cm above the dentate line (the boundary between the rectum and the anal canal). Then, the HCPT stapler is inserted into the anus, and after ligating the suture, the stapler is triggered, cutting off the prolapsed mucosal band. The entire process takes less than half an hour. Since the rectal mucosa above the dentate line is innervated by visceral nerves, there is almost no postoperative pain. Additionally, since the surgery removes the prolapsed rectal mucosa and blocks the terminal branches of the rectal arteries and veins, it eliminates the root cause of hemorrhoids, preventing recurrence. For some complex types of hemorrhoids, such as mixed hemorrhoids, circumferential hemorrhoids, severe prolapse, and rectal prolapse, HCPT minimally invasive surgery shows unique therapeutic advantages.

What should be done if hemorrhoids always bleed? HCPT uses minimally invasive methods: utilizing controllable microwave technology to coagulate the tissue proteins at the base of the hemorrhoid, causing the diseased area to necrose and fall off. Advantages: Minimal postoperative pain, rarely requiring painkillers, short recovery time, accelerated recovery due to suturing of the incision, reduced use of antibiotics, and fewer side effects. This is currently the preferred method for hemorrhoid surgery.

The above is an explanation of what to do if hemorrhoids always bleed. Experts from Zhengzhou Anus and Rectum Hospital warmly remind: If you suffer from hemorrhoids, anal fistulas, anal fissures, or other proctological diseases, be sure to seek treatment at a regular specialized hospital!
