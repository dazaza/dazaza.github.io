Skin ulceration caused by tattooing, beauty shop compensates five thousand yuan

by lesile7hl0 on 2010-04-01 19:20:05

A young man became infected after getting a tattoo at a beauty shop and incurred over 8000 yuan in medical expenses. Since his claim for compensation was unsuccessful, the young man took the beauty shop to court. On February 25th, the People's Court of Tanghe County, Henan Province, ruled that the beauty shop should compensate the young man with various fees amounting to 5441 yuan.

In order to make his image appear more "cool," one day in June 2007, Xiao Liu went to the beauty studio run by defendant Wen and spent 50 yuan to get a rose tattoo on the back of his right hand. Unexpectedly, a week later, the back of Xiao Liu's right hand began to itch, followed by redness, swelling, pain, skin ulcers, and purulent secretions. Upon seeing this situation, Wen took Xiao Liu to a hospital in Zaozhuang City for treatment, costing 2000 yuan in medical expenses (of which Wen paid 1000 yuan), but Xiao Liu's condition did not improve. On March 28th, 2008, after diagnosis by the Nanyang Central Hospital, Xiao Liu's condition was identified as skin soft tissue deficiency and ulceration on the back of his right hand. Xiao Liu was hospitalized for 21 days, during which he underwent a skin graft surgery, costing a total of 7472.49 yuan in medical expenses. After being discharged, Xiao Liu demanded that Wen compensate him for all losses amounting to 30721 yuan, but was refused.

The People's Court of Tanghe County reviewed the case and believed that if a citizen's body is harmed, the corresponding fees should be compensated; if the victim is also at fault for the occurrence of the damage, the civil liability of the infringer can be mitigated. The plaintiff developed skin soft tissue deficiency and ulceration after getting a tattoo at the defendant's beauty studio. Since the plaintiff's skin ulcer damage may not be entirely due to reasons beyond his control, it is appropriate for both parties to bear 50% of the loss caused to the plaintiff. The court thus legally ruled that the defendant should compensate the plaintiff for medical expenses, lost wages, and other various fees totaling 5441 yuan.
