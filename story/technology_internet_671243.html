When should high school seniors apply for studying abroad at the University of Quebec?

by abgjlp615 on 2012-02-21 17:17:55

For senior high school students or college seniors who wish to study abroad at the University of Quebec in Canada, they should seize the best application time in order to smoothly enter their ideal university. Generally, it takes about six months to a year to complete an application for the University of Quebec in Canada. Experts from the Quebec Study Abroad Network provide a detailed introduction:

Firstly, due to limited spots, the application deadlines for universities and Quebec visas are often half a year or even a year in advance.

Secondly, the preparation of study-abroad guarantee funds for the Quebec visa needs to start a year ahead.

Thirdly, applying to study in Quebec, Canada involves many steps and takes time.

1. Limited Positions and Application Deadlines

The University of Quebec in Canada is mainly public, funded by government investment, primarily serving students from Quebec, Canada. It prioritizes meeting the educational needs of Canadian Quebec students (including immigrants and international students already in Canada), and only then satisfies the needs of international students. Therefore, positions are limited, and the application deadline is generally half a year or even a year in advance. Some popular schools may run out of spots quickly even if the application deadline hasn't changed due to the high number of applicants.

2. Preparation of Study-Abroad Guarantee Funds

The embassy's visa process places significant emphasis on the total amount and history of the study-abroad guarantee funds. Ideally, there should be a 12-month financial history, which many high-income families in China cannot meet as their income is mainly used for investments rather than fixed deposits, and they lack awareness of retaining relevant deposit records. Therefore, it is necessary to plan finances a year in advance so that when the embassy investigates the history of the guarantee funds, the process will be more efficient. This point is particularly important.

3. Many Steps and Time Required for Applying to Study in Quebec, Canada

It takes time to research which university or school in Quebec, Canada to apply to, determine the school and major direction, prepare for language exams and achieve qualified scores, strengthen language skills, prepare application materials for schools and embassies, create school materials and embassy visa materials, supplement materials according to embassy and school requirements and submit new materials, wait for schools to review and issue acceptance letters, wait for embassies to process and issue visas, and prepare for pre-departure matters. Under normal circumstances, this entire process takes about half a year. Without sufficient time, the success rate of studying in Canada will be significantly reduced. Conversely, if there is ample time, applicants have a better chance of entering good schools and obtaining embassy visas.

A special reminder: Graduate school applications are recommended to start one and a half years in advance because the procedures are more complicated and the application period starts earlier. Generally, fall semester graduate admissions are determined by spring of the same year, with the application process starting in the previous year.

Study abroad experts advise parents and students to start the application process a year in advance, especially current senior high school candidates. If planning to study in Canada, do not wait until after the college entrance exam results are out or until language test scores are available to rush the application process. By then, it may be difficult to enter your ideal university in Quebec, Canada at the desired time.
