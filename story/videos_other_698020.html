A Record of Bicycling Through Luoyang

by vkk8om6i13 on 2012-03-08 23:02:03

Cycling Journey in Luoyang

Preface: The true meaning of travel is not about where you're going or what scenery you're seeing, but the process of enjoying the sights that others may not consider as scenery with a new perspective.

At 8 o'clock on April 22nd, we set off from Henan Industrial University. It was still gloomy then, as if it hadn't woken up yet, or perhaps it was reluctant to see us go. However, we decisively started our journey without hesitation! I love traveling, and I believe nothing makes people happier than traveling.

Cycling on the road, humming out-of-tune songs, feeling very relaxed, carefree, and comfortable... The excitement in my heart can hardly be expressed in words! When we reached Hulao Pass, we followed the Mang Mountain route. This was the hardest part of our journey. The road was winding and bumpy with many ups and downs. Later, everyone got tired, and every time we went uphill, we had to get off the bike and push it up before riding down again. The feeling of sliding down the slope was excellent because you didn't have to exert any effort, and your speed would increase until you reached the bottom. It was even more comfortable to feel the roar of the wind beside your ears, which made your face cool instantly and relieved much of the fatigue. Perhaps this was all psychological comfort!

Later, whenever we saw a downhill road, everyone would shout excitedly, "Ah! Downhill! Let's put in more effort!" At this moment, there was no better motivation than this! On the way up and down the slopes, we were no longer afraid of going uphill because if there was an uphill, there must be a downhill. After our arduous climb, we would surely enjoy the fun of descending. This was absolutely certain! When I saw an uphill, I would tell myself that there must be a downhill ahead, and for the sake of the downhill, I must climb up! Downhill, this was hope; for the sake of the downhill, this was energy! Life is no different. There is the difficulty of climbing uphill, as well as the speed and passion of descending downhill! As long as you are willing to climb, you will experience this joy!

We arrived in Gongyi at around two-thirty in the afternoon. By the time we reached Gongyi, our journey was halfway done! In Gongyi, we saw the confluence of the Yellow River and Luo River. Although the scene wasn't very spectacular, it still calmed my heart. I don't know why, but I have a natural affinity for water. Water has always been a spiritual thing to me. Now it's still the dry season, so there isn't much river water, and the river surface isn't wide, but the water here is clean and tranquil. Standing on the Heluo Bridge, looking at the quietly flowing confluence of rivers gave me a feeling like being connected throughout my body, as if the Ren and Du meridians were opened, making me feel very comfortable! The ripples shimmered, slightly moving forward without an end, only a distant horizon...

We found a small restaurant in Gongyi to eat something. After six hours of cycling, everyone was tired and hungry. Once seated, some of us had a bottle of beer each, cold and refreshing. We chatted and laughed together without any sense of fatigue. At this moment, I remembered the time when I was teaching in Guizhou, and we all went on home visits together. We walked many mountain roads every day, eating only steamed buns and sleeping rough, but none of us felt tired, and no one complained. All we experienced was happiness and joy, without any conflict or complexity, just the simple happiness of having a place to rest when we were tired...

Among my comrades, there was one who impressed me the most, a girl named Cui Fangfang. She also cycled with us to Luoyang, covering nearly 400 kilometers over three days and persevering through it all. Her perseverance was truly admirable. Later, whenever someone mentioned her, Dongge would point to the sky and say, "She came from there!" Truly deserving admiration!

After finishing our meal, without waiting for the food to digest, we set off again! In the following journey, we sped up. Our average speed in the morning was about 15 km/h, and in the afternoon, it increased to 20 km/h. Despite such an increase in speed, everyone managed to keep up, and I admired them all. I clearly remember Yangyang saying, "I have no strength left. I'm relying solely on my willpower to keep cycling and can't stop!" At this point, everyone feared stopping because at this level of exhaustion, if we stopped, we wouldn't know if we could stand up again. When we saw the sign indicating "Luoyang 14 km away," we were all delighted. We were finally almost there, and involuntarily, we sped up again because it was getting dark, and we needed to reach the city of Luoyang before nightfall. With such enthusiasm, no one felt tired. We were close to our destination, close to success, and only needed to persevere a little longer. Would you not persist? Could you not persist? Following the National Highway 310, we entered the ancient capital of Luoyang. On the way, we saw the ancient temple - the White Horse Temple. Unfortunately, due to time constraints, we couldn't visit it.

This was my first time coming to the famous ancient capital introduced in books, and my first time cycling here. My heart was filled with excitement! When we reached the Luoyang Bridge, I parted ways with everyone to meet my classmate (Zhou Xiaoshuai). After cycling for more than an hour in the city, I finally reached his school - Henan University of Science and Technology. It was already 9:47 PM! Xiaoshuai was surprised and joked, "Let me see how you walk after getting off the bike, haha..." Indeed, not only was I very tired, but also because I had been sitting on a bicycle all day, my buttocks were painfully sore, and the muscles in my thighs were stiff. Every movement caused pain, and my walking was unnatural. But I didn't care; I was still proud. This pride came from deep within, a self-acknowledgment. After washing up and resting in his dormitory until 10 PM, we went to eat. At his school's snack street, we ordered a few dishes and bought a bottle of white wine. Despite being very tired, I didn't eat much because my stomach suddenly hurt, probably due to the fatigue. Finally, unable to eat, I drank a little wine and returned. Fortunately, Xiaoshuai had some stomach medicine. I took six tablets and lay down. Drowsily, I sent greetings to a few friends in Zhengzhou and fell asleep.

When I woke up, it was already the next morning. After lunch at 11:00 AM, Xiaoshuai and I set off by bike to tour the urban area of Luoyang! It was during the Luoyang Peony Festival, so we decided to see the world-famous peonies of Luoyang first! According to legend, after Wu Zetian became empress, she was very pleased. One winter, she visited the back garden and saw withered branches swaying and weeds growing wildly. So she got angry and wrote a poem: "Tomorrow morning, I will visit the upper garden, hurry to inform spring. All flowers bloom overnight, do not wait for the morning breeze." The anger of the empress had significant consequences, and even the flower gods were afraid, so overnight, all the peach, plum, and crabapple blossoms bloomed. Only the peonies had character and refused to defy nature. The empress was furious and expelled all the peonies from Chang'an, exiling them to Luoyang. Thus, Luoyang gained unexpectedly. Legends are just legends. Peonies bloom not only for them; one land nurtures its people, and one land nurtures its flowers. This is also related to Luoyang's unique geographical location!

The two of us entered the Wangcheng Park. At first glance, everywhere was filled with peonies, truly living up to their reputation of being the "most beautiful flower"! The peony flowers were large and vibrant, radiating elegance amidst their brilliance. Perhaps this is the origin of their title as the "flower of wealth and honor"! There were many varieties of peonies here, too many for me to remember. It was my first time seeing these peerless beauties, and I didn't know how to describe their beauty, especially when these beautiful flowers swayed gently in the wind, slowly moving like graceful maidens, half-covered and shy... However, I particularly liked the white ones and a kind called Hai Tang Huang. These two types of peonies seemed pure and sincere to me, beautiful without being overly delicate, elegant without being ostentatious, making one feel comfortable, unlike other peonies that were dazzling and hard to accept.

After seeing the peonies, we cycled along the Longmen Avenue straight to the Longmen Grottoes, this Buddhist art sanctuary that had captivated my imagination. Whenever I read about Luoyang, I fantasized about being in the Longmen Grottoes, admiring these artistic treasures! The caves carved into the cliffs and the Buddha statues inside represented a belief, a belief in life and Buddhism, allowing life to continue, a belief drifting in the wind... There were many visitors at the Longmen Grottoes, crowded shoulder to shoulder. I thought that such noise couldn't allow one to appreciate the tranquility and the heart-touching power of Buddhism! No, no! Ultimately, I chose not to enter. I am waiting for the off-season to visit properly and experience the mysterious power of Buddhism! After returning from the Longmen Grottoes, we went to the Guanlin Temple, where Cao Cao buried Guan Yu. Here lies a man known for loyalty and righteousness, whose demeanor, spirit, and deeds are still worthy of our admiration! He - I don't need to elaborate, everyone knows him - the Peach Garden Oath, warming wine and slaying Hua Xiong, scraping bone to heal wounds, crossing five passes and slaying six generals, a thousand-mile solo ride, etc., he, the hero of turbulent times, the hero of the world...

After paying respects to Guan Yu, we ate some food and directly headed to the music fountain square! The fountain show began promptly at eight o'clock. As the music played, a song from Qinghai-Tibet Plateau, the fountain moved rhythmically with the music, like the spectrum of a music player, sometimes rising, sometimes falling, sometimes rotating, sometimes intertwining, and sometimes colored lights danced on the fountain, so rhythmic and dynamic. Watching it made me laugh uncontrollably, deeply moved. In this vast world, wonders abound! At this moment, I felt this was the most romantic and wonderful moment in my heart. I felt like a child, unable to say anything other than "good." So let me be silent and quietly enjoy this beautiful moment...

To facilitate gathering and returning early the next morning, after the music fountain ended, I went to find my beloved fellow cyclists! Our journeys were so similar! Peony Festival - Longmen Grottoes - Music Fountain! Seeing everyone's joy, I completely forgot that we had to fight for another dozen or so hours the next day. Sharing happiness truly allows one to forget any fatigue or unpleasantness, and now I believe this! After taking a cold shower in the evening, although it was cold, I still felt refreshed after washing. I didn't catch a cold the next morning, and I really thank heaven!

On the morning of April 24th, we ate something casually and set off. We took a wrong turn along the way according to the directions given, and ended up at the entrance of Lian-Ho Expressway, unfortunately unable to enter, so we had to return and find National Highway 310 again! My friend Lao Shu and I rode faster, reaching a top speed of over 40 km/h with an average of 26-27 km/h. The comrades behind couldn't keep up. We followed the road signs of National Highway 310, but when we reached Gongyi, we found the signs were incorrect. National Highway 310 goes straight to Zhengzhou, but the sign pointed to Provincial Road 237. We asked people nearby, who said to turn right! We slid down the road, only to find out we had taken the wrong path. We wasted a lot of time in this small mountain hollow because the mountain roads were difficult to navigate, all uphill, almost exhausting us. Luckily, we eventually found National Highway 310, but it was already past five in the afternoon. Time was running out, and it was getting dark. Without streetlights on the national highway, our journey became much more dangerous! When we reached Xingyang, an unfortunate accident occurred - the rear tire of the bike I was riding went flat. There were no bike repair shops along the way, but the bike couldn't be ridden anymore! So, I rode one bike carrying Zhang Haifeng while he held onto the bike I couldn't ride anymore, and we continued forward. Because I was carrying someone, the speed slowed down. I carried Zhang Haifeng for about an hour until I was extremely tired. Then Dazui took over for a while. The two of us alternated carrying until we reached the intersection of West Fourth Ring Road and National Highway 310 in Zhengzhou, where Zhang Haifeng took a taxi back! We continued forward. When we saw Lotus Street, we no longer cared about being tired. Each of us was like a wild horse, accelerating crazily, wanting to see who would reach the school first... It was like going home, an urgent feeling! That night, we returned to the dormitory at around 10:30 PM, just before lights-out, and there were no meals available. We probably didn't have the energy to eat anyway! We went to the supermarket and bought a bottle of Jingo wine, a bottle of beer, a bottle of Red Bull, a box of milk, and a pack of cigarettes. We sat on the balcony of the dormitory, quietly smoking and drinking silently. At this moment, my whole body had no strength left. After drinking some alcohol, my head felt dizzy, as if entering a dreamlike state, everything was blurry! Then, in this blur, I fell asleep, and without realizing it, dawn arrived!

On Monday evening, all eleven of us gathered to celebrate our successful return! For me, this was not only an extraordinary experience, but more importantly, I met these new comrades, all of whom were the pride of Henan Industrial University! Life should be enjoyed to the fullest, don't let the golden goblet face the moon empty, live for today and drink for today, don't worry about right and wrong at the door! Cheers to our comrades!

Postscript: Recently, I've been very happy because I've avoided things and events I dislike and pursued the travel I love! I gradually realized that my inner passionate love belongs to travel. Travel can make people far away from complexity and worldly affairs, and this process is incredibly joyful! I once set my life goal: wandering life, drifting life! Although I can't do it many times, I told myself that I must have the courage to pursue it!
