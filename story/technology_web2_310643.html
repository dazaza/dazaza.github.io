Manchester United's acknowledged weakest link has left opponents despairing; a versatile defender with zero transfer fee has become famous in one game.

by qinglai888 on 2009-12-16 12:31:29

Manchester United's acknowledged weakest link has become a source of despair for opponents. A versatile defender with no transfer fee has made a name in one match. In the high-density week of two matches, plus the injury-ridden defensive line, Ferguson had to use some less experienced players to temporarily fill the gaps. The Belgian Riki Dierckx became an indispensable figure in Manchester United's defense at this moment. Just over 21 years old, Dierckx's previous career development was not smooth, but under Ferguson, as a common saying goes: Dierckx has been reborn.

To mention this season, Dierckx's first appearance was in the League Cup against Wolves; today again against Wolves, and this time in a higher-level league, and as a starter. The recent domino-style injuries in the center-back line indeed created today's opportunity for Dierckx. But that's just the surface. Ferguson played him first as a right-back and then as a center-back today, daring to examine a player with only two league experiences in such a way in the league itself, which already indicates Ferguson's considerable confidence in his abilities.

In this match, Dierckx played with considerable authority. The Belgian dared to confront head-on, knowing that the Wolves team planned to suppress Manchester United through strong tackling. You must be even tougher than them. Dierckx completed six tackles throughout the game, ranking first among both teams, succeeding three times, and also making four successful clearances, ranking first in Manchester United. This shows that the opposing side deliberately attacked him, but Wolves did not gain any opportunities, even consolatory ones.

Passing the defensive test is just one aspect. After all, playing as a right-back, Dierckx must push forward to assist. Perhaps in this area, the Belgian does not have many highlights, but in the first half, he successfully broke down the right flank once, driving the ball straight towards the goal, and it was cleared by the opposing defenders in the ensuing scramble. For Dierckx, who played his first league match for Manchester United this season, you cannot ask for too much. Additionally, he changed to play center-back at the 60th minute, showing more generalship, handling the ball decisively and cleanly, and effortlessly dealing with several players over 1.9 meters tall with his 1.86-meter height. Ferguson specifically praised Dierckx after the match when he was playing as a center-back, saying, "Dierckx performed very well when he played center-back, showing great potential. He is a natural-born center-back."

Including today's match, Dierckx has played a total of five games for Manchester United, three in the League Cup and two in the league, where the last game of the previous season against Hull City was his first league appearance. Although five games may not seem significant, Dierckx has already played left-back, right-back, and both sides of center-back, a standard versatile defender in the backfield. Considering the future, Dierckx has a complete chance to become a legendary figure in Manchester United, at least an indispensable universal player like O'Shea, who can play any position. When he joined Manchester United in January 2009, he didn't cost the club anything because Manchester United agreed to pay his former club Stoke City based on his performance. It seems Stoke underestimated his potential at the time. Despite only a year passing, and Stoke making a small profit from him, Manchester United is clearly the sole beneficiary. Stoke's strength is evident there, yet they let go of a player who could have been a crucial addition to their team.

It's hard to imagine if Dierckx had set entering Stoke City as his highest career goal, and if Manchester United scouts hadn't noticed him, when he would have emerged. Transferring from Antwerp to Stoke City in 2007 was seen by many Belgians as a good opportunity, but being loaned out to Wrexham by Stoke City suppressed his professional aspirations greatly. It might have been better to stay in Antwerp and play in the Belgian Second Division. The keen eyes of Manchester United scouts came first, followed by Ferguson's discerning talent recognition, allowing Riki Dierckx to seize a truly developmental opportunity.

We must marvel at Ferguson's recognition of young players' abilities. The use of Macheda, the Da Silva brothers, and Obertan has already achieved initial success, and the key lies in how cheaply they were introduced. Obertan was the most expensive, costing no more than £3 million, and Dierckx is the latest achievement. And Paul Pogba, who was brought in from Le Havre in August at the age of 16, might be the next surprise. Recently, he helped Manchester United's U18 team achieve a four-match winning streak, scoring 3 goals in 4 matches, including a goal against Liverpool's U18 team. He scored within the first minute of a match recently, helping Manchester United's U18 team smoothly advance to the fourth round of the Youth FA Cup. Furthermore, in his debut for Manchester United's reserve team against Bolton on the 14th, he scored first and assisted another goal. Born in 1993, this player's growth rate is astonishingly fast, and at this rate, a call-up to the first team will come at any time. Ferguson's emphasis on young players, especially his unique and precise judgment during selection, has laid a solid foundation for Manchester United's future orderly development.
