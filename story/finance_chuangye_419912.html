On Contemporary Education: How to Balance Success and Happiness?

by dianxin8 on 2011-04-22 18:09:47

Each of us has a different boundary for private resistance to success! And there is only one secret to reaching success, which is to learn to pay the price that ordinary people cannot afford! When comparing the differences between happiness and success, it's not hard to find they have one common point: they both reach a sense of satisfaction. Therefore, a person who lives happily is considered successful in our eyes compared to those who live unhappily. And when a person achieves success, it satisfies his desires, thus bringing a sense of happiness. The question is, can this sense of satisfaction be sustained? We believe that whether satisfaction can be sustained is an important factor distinguishing happiness from success.

Happiness and success may seem to have no conflict; some people achieve success and thus gain happiness because their lifelong pursuit is to have a successful life. The path to success is full of ups and downs. In the course of human development, there is neither perpetual smooth sailing nor eternal wandering.

What is happiness? What is success? According to Baidu, the basic definition of happiness is: the state when psychological desires are satisfied. It is a prolonged sense of satisfaction with life and a feeling of great interest in living, naturally hoping for long-term happiness. Another interpretation of happiness: happiness cannot be separated from four elements: wealth, emotions, health, and revenge. When these four factors are harmoniously balanced and unified, one is at the peak of happiness! The interpretation of success is: success is achieving the set goal. The secret of success is actually a kind of belief, which can be described as a positive belief. It is a confident state and a sense of satisfaction after each individual reaches their own goal!

If mentors focus on developing the talents of the mentees from the beginning, it undoubtedly aligns with the interests of the mentees. As they say, interest is the best teacher. Therefore, mentees are likely to achieve success in areas they are interested in. Being fully engaged in work they are passionate about first satisfies their interest needs. In this aspect, life can be said to have no regrets. Achieving satisfaction naturally brings a sense of success. Take Han Han for example, even though he is a controversial talent and eccentric genius, at least he has achieved success in literature. Even if his literary works are limited to narrative reporting, it cannot be denied that he has his own thoughts and insights. A good literary work often becomes a classic due to its unique insights in thought content. Thus, even without a college diploma, Han Han has lived life on his own terms. He engages in racing and expresses his views through writing, all things he loves. He lives happily and, to a certain extent, is a model of success. How many people of Han Han's age have achieved what he has?

Happiness and success, these two terms, have both points of contention and intersection. If we can respect the development of the mentee's talents while promoting detailed development, this is undoubtedly the best way to resolve the contradictions between the two. If the development of talent is lost, then the overall happiness of the mentee's life will likely be lost, making it meaningless to talk about the happiness of success afterward.

In a counseling class, we watched the extremely Chinese-style parenting methods of the Tiger Mom towards her children. Regarding such parenting methods, we neither completely oppose nor fully agree. Such methods deprived the Tiger Mom's two daughters of the simplicity and joy of childhood but also gave them the secrets to life success. They now live happily. Mentees when young do not truly understand what they want. Under parental guidance, they begin to follow their own life paths. In today’s harsh employment pressure, to adapt to societal needs, parents must guide their children towards societal requirements. This is the best approach for the child, otherwise, the child would only be eliminated in societal development, and such a life would not be a happy one either. Regardless, the ultimate intention of parents is always for the well-being of their children. So, should children be educated to be happy or successful? This is a very worthy research topic. Educators should teach without discrimination and tailor education to individual aptitudes to achieve the best results in education.

Regarding the cultivation of children by parents in the educational process, should children be cultivated into happy individuals or successful ones? This involves the issue of detailed development versus talent development in educational science. If, in the educational process, we follow China's educational advocacy of cultivating individuals with moral, intellectual, physical, aesthetic, and labor development, but expect the student to excel in every subject, this is indeed impossible. There are very few such talents in the entire society. Such education might turn the student into an ordinary talent who merely follows the educator's steps, losing their creativity and vitality. This neglects the development of the student's talents. In such education, the student cannot develop according to their own interests from the start, so they will not feel happiness from the beginning. Therefore, this progress that sacrifices personal happiness may temporarily reach the goals set by the educator, but these goals cannot sustain development because the student's talents are almost entirely lost during the educational process. Their self-development space will appear very narrow when they grow up. Thus, such an educational method will lead the student from initial unhappiness to temporary happiness upon reaching the secret of success, only to return to unhappiness later. This is the state presented when cultivating students into successful individuals.
