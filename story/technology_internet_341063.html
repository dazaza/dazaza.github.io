T-shirt Fabric Selection:

Commonly used fabrics for T-shirt and cultural shirt production include: pure cotton, polyester-cotton, spandex, polyester, nylon, etc.

1. Pure Cotton: A commonly used fabric, with several types of cotton available, including finely spun ones. Advantages include good moisture absorption and environmental friendliness. Disadvantages include easy deformation and wrinkling. General characteristics of pure cotton fabric: Good texture, comfortable wearing experience, eco-friendly, weight generally between 160g and 280g. Too thin would be too transparent, too thick would be stifling. Generally, choosing between 180g to 240g is best. Common counts are 21-count and sometimes 32-count. The count refers to the fineness of the cotton fiber yarn; the higher the count, the softer it is. The yarn is divided into regular yarn and fine yarn. Regular yarn has a slightly rougher surface. Fine yarn has a cleaner surface and a softer feel.

2. Polyester-Cotton: A mix of cotton and polyester, another common fabric. Typically, polyester-cotton consists of 65% cotton and 35% polyester. It overcomes the disadvantage of pure cotton being prone to wrinkles and deformation while maintaining excellent moisture absorption, even better than pure cotton. However, comfort level is slightly less than pure cotton.

3. Nylon-Cotton: A mix of cotton and nylon, possessing all the advantages of cotton with added elasticity and a silk-like feel, providing comfort when worn.

4. Spandex-Cotton: A mix of cotton and spandex, an elastic fabric that is generally more form-fitting, showing off the figure without feeling restrictive due to its elasticity.

Supplement: During warmer autumn days, cotton cardigans can still be worn, but for thicker clothing, pure cotton is recommended as cotton cardigans are generally thinner. In colder weather, wearing pure polyester can easily cause static electricity, which can be annoying, so pure cotton is definitely the preferred choice for cold weather. Cotton-polyester blends usually have different outer and lining materials, where the lining made of polyester can cause static electricity.

5. Modal: A new type of environmentally friendly material, relatively rare, with much stronger water absorption and sweat-wicking properties compared to cotton, very good. The disadvantage is that it tends to deform easily, currently rarely used. If blended with polyester or spandex, it performs well.

6. 100% Polyester: Suitable for wearing during exercise, commonly used functional fabric by ADIDAS and NIKE. It can promptly evaporate sweat, avoiding the heavy feeling of sweating in pure cotton, suitable for sports. Athletes typically wear polyester rather than cotton.
