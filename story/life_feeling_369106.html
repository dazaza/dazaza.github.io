Why does Hai Zao betray while Mao Doudou won't?

The other day I finished watching "The Wonderful Era of Daughter-in-law". Since the leading actress in both dramas is Hai Qing, I can't help but associate it with "Snail House". Similar to both dramas are the segments involving third parties or mistresses, but they end up with completely different endings.

In "Snail House", the third party Song Siming is rich and of high status, and of course he has a wife and children. In "The Wonderful Era of Daughter-in-law", the third party Li Ruqiu is also rich, single, and is a typical wealthy bachelor. Plus, he is Mao Doudou's first love, which gives him an unparalleled advantage in terms of emotions. Therefore, from all angles, as a third party, Li Ruqiu in "The Wonderful Era" seems to be superior, but Mao Doudou does not rush towards Li Ruqiu like Hai Zao does in a fiery passion. Instead, she very firmly lives her ordinary, trivial, and even annoying little life.

Of course, there are the moral qualities of Hai Zao and Mao Doudou, but the second parties Xiao Bei and Yu Wei are also indirect causes for these different endings.

We can compare the various differences between Yu Wei and Xiao Bei.

Why does Hai Zao betray while Mao Doudou won't?

In their attitudes towards money, Yu Wei and Xiao Bei are completely different.

Many people say that the trigger for Hai Zao to fall into Song Siming's arms is Xiao Bei's unwillingness to take out the accumulated 60,000 yuan to repay the high-interest loan borrowed by her sister Hai Ping due to buying a house. Xiao Bei's view is that Hai Ping and her husband do not have the ability to buy a house in this city, but borrow money to buy a house for vanity, so he will not pay for Hai Ping's vanity. Some people say that Song Siming does not really love Hai Zao; he just spends money on her. There is a good saying: A man who is willing to spend money on you may not necessarily love you, but a man who really loves you will definitely be willing to spend money on you.

From the 60,000 yuan, we see Xiao Bei's selfishness. His love is only expressed in sweet words that do not cost money, and in affection that does not involve expenses. Once it comes to real cash, the love he usually talks about, which can "sacrifice everything" and "climb knife mountains and dive into fire seas," becomes calm. No matter how many "revolutionary family histories" Hai Zao says, or even begs bitterly, he remains exceptionally rational and indifferent.

Why does Hai Zao betray while Mao Doudou won't?

Let's take a look at Yu Wei in "The Wonderful Era". After his brother-in-law Mao Feng gets into such a big mess, when everyone else is criticizing Mao Feng, he is constantly comforting everyone. At the same time, he makes the painful decision to transfer his photography studio. This studio is Yu Wei's career, something he has built with great effort and contains his blood and sweat. Then he takes out the 280,000 yuan from transferring the studio to pay off Mao Feng's debts, avoiding the exchange of his father-in-law's large house for a smaller one. If Yu Wei did not do this, no one could say he was wrong because all this has nothing to do with Yu Wei. But Yu Wei did it, and Mao Doudou feels more respect and gratitude towards Yu Wei besides love, thinking he is a man worth entrusting her life to. Facing such a deeply emotional man, how could a woman leave?

What Hai Zao and Mao Doudou face is not a simple financial crisis, but difficulties in life. When you face difficulties, do you expect the man beside you to be like Xiao Bei, standing aside calmly observing and analyzing rationally, or like Yu Wei, rushing forward to protect you from wind and rain?

A person's love only becomes apparent when it undergoes tests. Life does not need smooth-talking sweet words; what it needs is heartfelt care and cherishing.

Why does Hai Zao betray while Mao Doudou won't?

Yu Wei is more considerate towards his girlfriend's family. When problems arise, Xiao Bei's attitude is that Hai Ping is Hai Ping, and it has nothing to do with their lives, each living their own lives. Yu Wei is different. When Mao Doudou prepares for Mao Feng's wedding, Yu Wei decorates his home anew, hanging large wedding photos on the wall, preparing a new house for them. He himself will live in the six-square-meter small space in the studio. Yu Wei thinks that his wife's family matters are his own family matters. No matter what happens, Yu Wei will resolve major issues into minor ones and minor ones into nothing, allowing Doudou to live simply.

Why does Hai Zao betray while Mao Doudou won't?

In their attitudes towards rivals, Yu Wei appears more confident and manlier.

After Xiao Bei discovers Hai Zao's betrayal, he runs in the rain and stays out all night. After deciding to forgive Hai Zao and start anew, he continuously tortures her both physically and psychologically. While torturing her, he is also torturing himself, trapped in this vicious circle and unable to escape.

Yu Wei is different. After arguing with Mao Doudou, he goes downstairs to pick her up and unexpectedly finds Doudou getting into her rival Li Ruqiu's car. Yu Wei does not choose to indulge in wild thoughts alone, nor does he run wildly in the rain at night. On the contrary, he quietly does housework at his mother-in-law's house. Even when facing this strong rival directly, Yu Wei shows the side of a confident man. After Li Ruqiu boasts about his wealth and past, Yu Wei tells him that we enjoy our own life pleasures, going to work together, going to the market together, cooking together... When she is sick, I stay by her bedside all the time. Can you do that? Through our own efforts, today we save money to buy a wheel, tomorrow we save money to buy a steering wheel, and then we can buy a QQ car. The happiness I give Doudou, you can never give her in your whole life!

Yu Wei's confidence earns him many points, and his confidence and generosity soothe Doudou's heart full of scars.

Why does Hai Zao betray while Mao Doudou won't?

Compared to Yu Wei, Xiao Bei is just a child who hasn't grown up.

Because Yu Wei's parents divorced when he was young, he is not a child who grew up in favorable circumstances. Originally, he has a mother who has been neurotic for a long time, and later his sister encounters misfortunes in life. Because he has experienced hardships, Yu Wei understands feelings better and knows the essence of life better. It can be said that for a boy, difficulties are truly a treasure. What Yu Wei has experienced is something Xiao Bei, as an only child in the family and always in favorable circumstances, cannot understand. Faced with problems, everyone has different perspectives. Xiao Bei's experiences determine how he handles problems. Of course, Xiao Bei is not wrong, but living with someone like Yu Wei is more comfortable and pleasant.

Xiao Bei is a child who hasn't grown up, while Yu Wei is a mature man who bears much responsibility.

Xiao Bei and Yu Wei are not right or wrong, but they change the lives of the women around them.

If your man is like Yu Wei, which woman would betray?
