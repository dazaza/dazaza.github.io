Opportunities Emerge as Some Stay Strong Amid Plunge

by huaxun399 on 2010-04-19 18:00:56

The entire domestic A-share market experienced the biggest crash since last September today. The decline of Shanghai Composite Index reached as high as 4.79%, while the decline of Shenzhen Component Index affected more by the real estate sector reached even higher at 6.22%. However, in this panic-driven crash, pharmaceutical stocks rose against the trend and its increase once approached 2% during trading. Although it witnessed a certain degree of volatile decline at the close, its decline was only 1.06% at the close. Compared with many other sectors whose declines exceeded 5%, it still demonstrated relatively strong volatile consolidation trends. So, does this strength of pharmaceutical stocks imply that once the market stabilizes and begins short-term rebound, they will definitely soar and thereby bring good investment opportunities to investors? Below we will conduct a brief analysis on this.

From the current fundamentals of the pharmaceutical industry, we can say that its development prospects are very good. First, judging from the annual reports already announced in the pharmaceutical stock sector, the performance of listed companies has been better than the industry due to the introduction and implementation of the healthcare reform plan last year. Second, it is expected that the operation data of the pharmaceutical manufacturing industry in the first quarter of this year will exceed expectations. The growth rate of the industry scale and profit will significantly accelerate compared with the same period last year. Revenue, operating profit, and total profit will all increase significantly year-on-year and may exceed market expectations. Among them, the recovery of the raw material drug sub-industry conforms to expectations, but the pre-tax profit growth rate of the Chinese patent medicine and chemical preparation sub-industries exceeds expectations.

Finally, looking ahead to the future, we believe: (1) The continuous development of the essential drugs market hinges on the financial investment of local governments; (2) Essential medicines will gradually be included in the medical insurance reimbursement scope. In the long term, the trend of scale expansion is inevitable, but the current policy is still in the adjustment stage, which requires time; (3) The essential drug system may have different impacts on different sales channels. Enterprises will gradually adjust their marketing systems, and the final degree of benefit will depend on enterprises' understanding, adaptation, and adjustment capabilities regarding policies. Ultimately, differentiation is unavoidable. (4) The integration enters the harvest period, with three state-owned enterprises making their appearances. Sinopharm, CRH, and Shanghai Pharmaceutical Group have become large pharmaceutical groups of great significance in the industry through mergers and acquisitions. Future major integrations in the industry may also revolve around these three large enterprises.

As a rapidly developing sunrise industry, the pharmaceutical industry, under the background of China's aging population, increasing national policy support and financial input, and accelerating industry integration, will achieve faster development in the next few years. Although the current PE ratio of the pharmaceutical sector for 2010 is 31 times, the premium rate relative to all A-shares reaches 63%, higher than the historical average premium rate of 51%, indicating that the sector valuation is not low. However, the pharmaceutical industry has the potential for accelerated growth, and there may be more phased individual stock opportunities.

From the technical trend of the entire pharmaceutical sector, since the successful bottoming out on March 16 this year, the pharmaceutical sector has shown a sustained upward trend with volatility. In the past week, it has maintained a strong consolidation trend amid the market's volatility adjustments. Although the fundamentals and technical aspects of the pharmaceutical sector both show medium-term positive trends, and its excellent Q1 report expectation may drive this sector to continue to strengthen, from its 5-minute K-line chart, it shows a certain short-term weakening trend. If the medium-term weakening trend of the market holds true, then it may also face some catch-up decline possibilities. Therefore, prudent investors can mainly observe the trend of this sector tomorrow, while aggressive investors can appropriately pay attention to the possible short-term low-absorption opportunities that may appear in the leading varieties and potential supplementary rise varieties in the sector during tomorrow's low-volatility consolidation. Specific stocks to watch include Jinling Pharmaceutical, Xinhua Pharmaceutical, Tonghua Jinma, and Kehua Biotech.
