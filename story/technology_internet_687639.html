Common harms caused by external hemorrhoids include:

1. Bloating: Since external hemorrhoids are ball-like growths outside the anus that block it, gas cannot escape due to insufficient pressure, causing it to be trapped inside. Long-term retention of waste gases in the body can lead to abdominal bloating or even affect the normal contraction of the heart due to increased vascular pressure, potentially resulting in symptoms like dizziness, brain swelling, and weakness.

2. Anemia: Although external hemorrhoids are less likely to bleed, bleeding is still possible if they are scratched or inflamed. Long-term bloody stools can lead to anemia.

3. Anal itching and gynecological diseases: The external hemorrhoid mass secretes more substances outside the anus, leading to anal itching or anal eczema. For female patients with external hemorrhoids, there is a greater risk of developing other gynecological diseases, posing a significant threat to women's health.

4. Anal fissures and constipation: Due to pain during defecation, some patients endure discomfort rather than relieving themselves, creating a vicious cycle that eventually leads to constipation, anal fissures, or even colorectal tumors.

Experts warn that failure to treat external hemorrhoids promptly can result in the aforementioned harms. Therefore, patients should visit a reputable anorectal hospital as soon as possible and not miss the optimal treatment window.
