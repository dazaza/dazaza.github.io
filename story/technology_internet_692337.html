The security team leader has been involved in more than 10 thefts, with a total value of over 600,000 yuan

by uelu6c6b on 2012-03-06 08:56:29

The security captain who stole company property for over a year is finally caught. Photo by Liu Xiaoqing.

At No. 6 Renmin East Road in Kunming, in a skyscraper with dozens of floors, there are tens of companies. Since November 2010, continuous cases of theft have occurred within the building, but no prying marks were found at the scene. Some people reported it to the police, while others suspected their colleagues of taking things without permission and didn't want to affect their relationship with them, so they kept it to themselves. Unexpectedly, similar incidents happened repeatedly. In order to find the black hand, community police officer Yang Xudong from Lishutou Police Station of Panlong Branch of Kunming Public Security Bureau made a suggestion to some companies within the building. Soon, the black hand was locked down - it turned out to be Li Mou, the security captain of the building's property management office. In more than a year, he committed dozens of thefts, stealing cash, shopping cards, mobile phones, computers, and other goods worth over 600,000 yuan.

Security Captain rummaging through the company

At the beginning of this year, during the "three visits and three evaluations" work, community police officer Yang Xudong from Lishutou Police Station of Panlong Branch learned about the repeated thefts in the building on Renmin East Road and analyzed the situation with the relevant company managers.

It turns out that since the end of November 2010, the properties of many companies and employees in the building had disappeared without a trace. Suspecting acquaintances of taking things without permission and considering colleague relationships, many victims did not report the case to the police. Yang Xudong suggested to some company managers: install surveillance cameras in hidden places on some floors and install sound-controlled lighting next to the surveillance cameras. At the same time, Yang Xudong also reported to the police station.

After the suggestion was adopted, results came quickly. On the night of January 31st, a man opened the iron door and entered a company. He turned on his flashlight and started rummaging through drawers when suddenly the roof light turned on. The man looked fearfully at the light and quickly fled the office.

On February 6th, when the company staff reviewed the surveillance footage, they discovered this scene and reported it to the Lishutou Police Station. After identification, the rummager turned out to be Li Mou, the security captain of the building's property management office. At around 6 PM on February 10th, the police arrested Li Mou in the building.

Li Mou began committing crimes as soon as he became the security captain

Li Mou, 29 years old, is from Zhenxiong, Zhaotong, and lives in a rented house in Rixin Village. He is married. In early 2009, he joined the building's property management office as an ordinary security guard. Due to his good performance, he became the security captain after working as a security guard for more than a year. People who know him feel that he is very low-key. Unexpectedly, he turned out to be a big thief.

Through investigation, the police found many water tickets, shopping cards, and other stolen goods in his residence and discovered that he had several ten thousand yuan in deposits and 300,000 yuan in funds.

On the afternoon of March 2nd, when the police escorted him to the building for crime scene recognition, the secrets behind Li Mou's "low-key" behavior were revealed: ever since Li Mou became the security captain, he frequently stole property using his work convenience and promptly sold the stolen goods.

One evening in February last year, he forcibly pushed open the glass door of the floor, entered a company office in the building, found the warehouse key in the drawer, and then opened the warehouse and stole 24 Motorola TM810 mobile phones worth nearly 200,000 yuan. At the same time, he created a false impression by putting the empty boxes back in place. One evening in March of the same year, he used the same method to enter the company and stole 20 Samsung mobile phones worth nearly 100,000 yuan. After stealing the phones, he sold them all to the second-hand market for a few hundred yuan each.

Police officers from Lishutou Police Station introduced that during the more than a year Li Mou served as the security captain, he committed dozens of crimes, stealing shopping cards, cash, vouchers, mobile phones, computers, and other items from multiple companies in the building, with a total value involved of over 600,000 yuan. Currently, the police have verified 15 cases, and the suspect has been criminally detained on suspicion of theft.
