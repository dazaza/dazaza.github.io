The centipede is also known as the Sky Dragon, Hundred-footed Bug, or Lesser Spine Centipede, etc. Common centipedes come in three varieties: red-headed, green-headed, and black-headed. The red-headed centipede has a back that is red-black in color, an abdomen that is light red, and legs that are light orange-red or yellow. The green-headed centipede has a blue back and legs, with a light blue abdomen; it is smaller in size, about half the length of the red-headed centipede. The black-headed centipede has a black back and legs, with a pale yellow abdomen, and is even smaller in size. Among these three types, the red-headed centipede is considered the best due to its larger size, higher yield, gentle temperament, strong adaptability, and fast growth, making it suitable for artificial breeding.

1) Temperature required for breeding centipedes:

The optimal temperature for centipede growth and reproduction is between 20°C and 32°C. Below 10°C, their activity slows down, they move less, and eat less. Below 5°C, they enter hibernation.

2) Relative humidity of the air in the breeding den is generally around 70%, while the humidity of the bedding soil inside the den should be between 30% and 35%.

3) Centipede breeding density:

Generally, about 600 adult centipedes are placed per square meter, around 1800 medium-sized centipedes, and approximately 3000 small centipedes. Centipedes of different sizes must be bred separately to prevent bullying of the weak by the strong, large ones preying on smaller ones, and mutual killing.

4) Feeding time for centipedes:

Feeding usually begins around 6 PM because centipedes are nocturnal creatures. They come out at night to feed, primarily between 9 PM and 3 AM. Food is provided every two days, and the feed must be sufficient to avoid competition and cannibalism due to lack of food. Although centipedes have a wide and varied diet, they require fresh food and will not eat rotten food. In one feeding session, they can consume up to 1/5 to 3/5 of their body weight. After eating their fill, they can survive without food for ten days to half a month.

5) How to provide water to centipedes:

Centipedes have the ability to endure hunger and can survive without food for 1 to 2 months. However, they cannot live without water, and their demand for water is relatively high. During hot weather, drinking water should be supplied promptly. This can be done by wetting corn cobs or sponges and placing them on bricks or tiles in the den, or by soaking clean cloth strips in water and draping them over bricks or tiles. Drinking utensils can also be placed, but care must be taken to prevent centipedes from falling into them. Small stones can be placed inside the drinking utensils to keep the water level balanced. Water must be changed regularly to ensure its freshness.

6) What are the feeds for centipedes:

Centipedes prefer fresh animal-based feeds and feed on soft-bodied, juicy small insects such as crickets, Tenebrio molitor, locusts, spiders, mole crickets, grasshoppers, earthworms, flies, maggots, sparrows, rodents, frogs, and other carnivorous animals, as well as pig blood, chicken blood, tender grass shoots, and watermelon rinds, etc. The prospect of centipede breeding in Neijiang.
