The path covered with roses is bathed in moonlight.

by lili1 on 2012-01-03 00:39:24

The road covered with roses flows with moonlight.

I believe in fate so much. Four years ago, we met. You were very pure back then, and I started to believe in love at first sight from that time on. You were not outstanding, nor a prince charming, but I was deeply infatuated with you.

Back then, I didn't have too many thoughts. I just quietly watched you, paid attention to you. As partners, we were jokingly referred to as "a suitable pair". I stopped believing in the Cinderella fairy tale long, long ago. I knew I had no right to expect perfect love. But I liked you so much, really.

A year later, I realized it was difficult to get along with you. You didn't talk much, kept a low profile, an incredibly low profile. No one got close to you. Until she appeared, your story instantly became a public secret. At that time, I dared not have any thoughts, because she was so perfect. But you still looked like you didn't care about anything. You said it was already over. When I touched your eyes, I couldn't read anything, but there was a heart-wrenching pain. I thought this would make me give up.

Two years later, a girl in my dormitory started pursuing you. She often talked about you and her when she came back to the dormitory. Even if I didn't know whether these things were true or not, I began to consciously or unconsciously avoid you. Once, when she was joking with you, I looked back at you two. You stared at me, I was speechless, turned around, and continued to do my own thing. That day, I seemed to cry. I always felt that there was no possibility between us.

Until our last year as classmates, I was completely disheartened. However, you lit my hope at this time. You voluntarily proposed to sit next to me, helped me with my homework, sat with me in the classroom during breaks, and told me many stories, your past stories... Your sudden kindness made me feel at a loss, but I forgot that such kindness would leave one day, I forgot that I should be prepared for losing before having...

You left four months later. When you were leaving, I kept staring at you while you packed your books. You didn't look back at me until you left the classroom. Maybe there was no possibility between us, maybe we should end...

Two months later, you contacted me. You added my QQ number and sent me a text message. Later, I found out that the first person you added on QQ was me, and the first text message you sent after buying a new phone was to me. I went on a trip to Shanghai. At that time, you were still studying at school. You secretly sent me text messages during class. I told you to focus on your studies, and you agreed. Every evening, your text message would arrive at 8:35 pm. Later, I found out that you finished self-study at 8:35 pm... Those ten days, you indulged me in every way.

After coming back, we prepared to receive our grades. You kept asking if the results were out. I told you not to rush, and I would immediately tell you once the results were announced. Later, the results were out, but they were quite disappointing. I told you that I didn't perform well, don't ask anything, I wanted to think alone, and then I turned off my phone. The next day, my best friend found me and asked what happened. I looked blankly, I didn't do anything special. My best friend said accusingly: You little fool, did you turn off your phone yesterday? Didn't you know how worried he was? He sent text messages everywhere asking us what happened, whether you didn't perform well or not, he really, really likes you. I fell silent. Is that true? Does he really like me?

Do you remember? You said you would always be with me. You said I was someone you wouldn't bully, let alone allow others to bully. Now I understand, those so-called promises are so powerless. Yes, you told me, you like me, very much, from the day we met. I thought I would like you for a long time, but I never expected that after just a year, the you who loved me disappeared. This time, I was truly heartbroken. I realized that falling in love and then separating hurts more, breaking something beautiful is even crueler. But it only took me a year to dilute three years of our feelings...

Yes, I have to admit, time is the cruelest enemy. Perhaps this isn't called love, but it was indeed a very sincere feeling for me.
