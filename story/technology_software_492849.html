I have a group of young friends around me.

by sacxiaoyanl on 2011-09-21 18:24:05

Each spring and autumn has its own happiness. Those things considered as excessive indulgence by adults may just be small happiness of a certain age.

I think the most fortunate thing in life is that when you make a mistake, even if you are found out, the other party chooses not to point it out. Yet, you feel guilty and relieved at the same time for not being discovered. I think next time you probably won't make the same mistake because such fear and tension really don't feel good.

I have a group of young friends around me who always come home very late after playing outside. Their entertainment is no more than singing in KTV, chatting in bubble tea shops, or playing cards in card rooms. Suddenly, I remember myself from a few years ago. I also often indulged in these simple joys.

But I hesitate to say anything because I know these beauties are fleeting. One day, when their lives get back on track, they won't have the time and energy to play outside; because I know if someone goes home first, he will seem particularly out of place. If someone is distanced from the current lifestyle and doesn't stay out so late, he will gradually distance himself from his friends around him.

Every person in love always thinks the person they encounter is the most special, and they think they are the ones most loved by them. Actually, it's not like that at all. One day, when you fall in love again, hearing familiar words and experiencing romantic things once again, you realize that those special things were just some self-perceived beauties.

Seeing this group of friends around me, I want to tell them that coming home too late is unsafe and family members will worry.

After hanging up the phone, I secretly rejoiced that I hadn't been discovered by my mom. Many years later, when we mentioned this incident, we burst into laughter.

I often complain to my friends that in the past, staying up all night talking on the phone was very happy, but now we get tired while talking; In the past, we could talk for a whole day and night, but now we gradually run out of topics.

Many years later, I suddenly miss a certain night when I analyzed with my best friend until three in the morning due to her breakup. To avoid being discovered by my mom, I secretly called her while hiding under the covers. I remember her crying on the other end of the phone, and I could only comfort her in a low voice.

But deep down inside, we always complain about certain people and certain things. In fact, those hurts and misunderstandings happened during our naive ages. We can use another perspective to tolerate the mistakes of younger people, so why can't we forgive the past?

The plots in movies and TV dramas, those touching languages that resonate deeply within us. One day, when we comment with the eyes of a veteran or an outsider, we find that we judge only right and wrong. The naivety and excitement of the past have long been erased by time.

We look at those younger than us and feel they are still young and inexperienced.

My family had strict rules, requiring me to be home no later than 10:30 pm. So, I always leave with a smile and apology when everyone is having the most fun.

There is a lyric that touched me deeply: "I don't know, I am not willing to give up this moment because of the uncertainty of my future." But I prefer another sentence: "Maybe I will regret in the future, but now you are the most important."

I really like a sentence: "If you knew my past, you would forgive my present." So why can't we forgive others' past?
