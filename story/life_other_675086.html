How to Drink Without Getting Drunk - Methods for Not Getting Drunk When Drinking - Ten Tricks to Keep You Sober After Thousands of Cups

Many businesspeople cannot avoid frequent social engagements. During meals, to liven up the atmosphere, they often have to grit their teeth and drink a few glasses even if they don't want to. How can one avoid getting drunk?

◆ Nutritionist's Ten Ingenious Tips

1. Before drinking, have a glass of milk or eat a few slices of bread. Avoid drinking on an empty stomach to prevent irritation of the stomach lining.

2. If you expect to drink heavily, take Vitamin B supplements in advance until your social engagements decrease to protect your liver. You can also consciously consume more foods rich in Vitamin B, such as animal livers, beef, lamb, egg yolks, vegetables, oats, and other whole grains, to increase the level of Vitamin B in your body.

3. When drinking spirits, drink plenty of water to help flush alcohol out of your system through urination. When drinking beer, go to the restroom frequently. When drinking strong liquor, it's best to add ice cubes.

4. Avoid drinking too quickly or aggressively; sip slowly to allow your body time to break down the ethanol.

5. Eat more animal livers like pork liver while drinking to enhance your body's ability to detoxify ethanol.

6. Eat more green leafy vegetables, which contain antioxidants and vitamins that protect the liver.

7. Eat more soy products, as their lecithin has a protective effect on the liver.

8. It’s better not to drink when feeling tired.

9. Do not drink after taking medication, especially after taking sleeping pills, sedatives, or cold medicine.

10. Avoid drinking carbonated beverages like cola or soda while drinking alcohol, as they can speed up the absorption of alcohol by the body.

◆ Common Misconceptions About Sobering Up

1. Strong tea can sober you up

There is no scientific basis for the popular belief that drinking strong tea can sober you up. While the catechins in tea do have some liver-protective effects, the caffeine in strong tea can cause blood vessels to constrict and raise blood pressure, potentially worsening headaches. Therefore, after being drunk, you can drink some light tea, but it's best not to drink strong tea.

2. Showering after drinking

Taking a hot shower after drinking can accelerate blood circulation, increasing energy consumption and possibly leading to low blood sugar. People with hypertension or cardiovascular diseases may be prone to strokes after drinking and should rest first before considering a shower.

◆ Effective Ways to Sober Up

1. Eating fruit

If someone drinks too much against their will, they can eat some fruit or drink some fruit juice afterward, as the acidic components in fruits and juices can neutralize alcohol.

2. Eating steamed buns

Many people skip meals after drinking, which is even more harmful. They should eat easily digestible starchy foods like steamed buns or noodles. Steamed buns convert into glucose, aiding in supplying blood to the body and boosting energy. Since they undergo fermentation, they also have a neutralizing effect on stomach acid, making the body feel better immediately after eating them.
