Speech at the Spring Festival Travel Rush Volunteers Symposium_377

by bstookuy0 on 2011-10-19 08:22:06

Respected leaders, teachers, and colleagues:

Hello everyone! I am very pleased to be here today as a representative of young volunteers to share with all of you a few insights I have gained since the start of our Spring Festival travel rush work. Since January 30th, the first day of the Spring Festival travel season, under the close care of various leaders from the Railway Bureau's Youth League Committee and with the strong support of the on-duty comrades at the station, I have learned a great deal through participating in railway volunteer services. I have come to understand many principles and have gained a deeper awareness of my future work, study, and life. Now, please allow me to take this opportunity to report some of my feelings during this period.

Firstly, I feel that serving the Spring Festival travel rush as a volunteer is an immense honor and glory. This is because it is the first time I have stepped out of campus and truly immersed myself in the ranks of those serving the people. My previous social practices or holiday activities were all carried out under the arrangements and organizations of the school, without directly contributing to society or making any direct contribution to its development or the happiness of the masses. What makes this volunteer work different from before is that I am now part of the team working for a harmonious and safe Spring Festival travel season, directly contributing my own strength. As a citizen of Quancheng, I am delighted and proud to finally be able to fulfill my duty for my hometown. I can proudly say that on the service post at Jinan Station, there is my silent dedication and selfless struggle, my hardworking spirit and love for my job, and my strictness and efficiency in my work achievements! For me, if passengers can leave Jinan Station and Jinan City with a good impression due to our work, that is our greatest joy. Similarly, through our service, we ensure that passengers from all over the country, whether they are on business trips, visiting relatives and friends, or vacationing, can safely and smoothly board their trains and embark on their journeys—this is the responsibility of every young volunteer in Quancheng.

Secondly, I feel that serving the Spring Festival travel rush as a volunteer means starting with caution and carrying a heavy responsibility throughout. Through several days of service work, I have more intuitively understood that the successful completion of any task requires constant improvement and cannot tolerate any carelessness. Our work of attending to elevators and assisting elderly and children may seem simple and not require technical support, but it greatly tests one's meticulousness and patience. As a volunteer, one must always prioritize passenger safety. Although the three-hour work period may seem short, every second requires high concentration, and vigilance must never be relaxed. On one hand, we must constantly remind passengers to pay attention to safety, being patient and sincere; on the other hand, we must continuously observe if there are any special passengers who need assistance, staying alert and observant. It is clear today that "urgently addressing the needs of passengers, fulfilling their requirements, solving their difficulties, and eliminating their concerns" is not just empty talk—it requires us to possess a strong sense of responsibility and service consciousness. Being a good server is not just about wearing the uniform; only by fully embracing the role of a server can we truly do our work well and meticulously. In the upcoming work, I will continue to summarize experiences, overcome shortcomings, improve, and enhance my abilities, leaving a better impression on passengers and taking my work achievements to the next level.

Thirdly, I feel that serving the Spring Festival travel rush as a volunteer means bearing significant responsibilities and pursuing noble missions. Compared to the railway workers and leaders present here, we university students are inexperienced, young, and have yet to put forth enough effort. Through our research, visits, and learning from various departments, sections, and workshops within the railway system, I have gained a more comprehensive, systematic, and profound understanding of how railway work is conducted. At the same time, I have become more acutely aware of the arduousness, greatness, and nobility of this cause. For the great rejuvenation of the railway industry and the smooth progress of railway work, frontline railway workers have silently contributed so much that if we hadn't visited them, perhaps no one could clearly articulate it. I still vividly remember a statement made by Master Kong from our internal mechanical section. He said that the vast majority of employees in the line section rarely get holidays or complete rest times. They often rise at two o'clock in the morning, braving the cold to carry out construction, putting down their bowls halfway through meals to rush to the work site, working overtime, day and night, year after year. But who knows, who understands all this? When we sit comfortably in train carriages, who considers the sweat, hard work, and tears that railway workers have poured into ensuring passenger safety and comfort? At this moment, I hold deep admiration and gratitude for every railway worker present here, feelings that words cannot fully express. Therefore, I can only say that I will learn from you, learn from every comrade fighting on the frontlines of the locomotive, learn from your selfless enthusiasm, and learn from your spirit of dedication. I will let this enthusiasm and spirit guide my future work and studies, striving to do better.

"Spring silkworms spin silk until death, and willingly serve as oxen for the young." Although our work hours are strictly defined, and the Spring Festival travel rush will eventually end, the journey of serving the people has no final destination! I want to let the experience, knowledge, and wealth I have gained during these sixty days of the Spring Festival travel rush accompany me for the rest of my life, enabling me to continue serving the people well when I take up the position of a teacher, dedicating myself wholeheartedly and tirelessly striving for the cause of a harmonious and beautiful modernization!

On the occasion of the arrival of the Spring Festival, I humbly offer a poem to every silent contributor among the railway workers and to my fellow young comrades present here for mutual encouragement:

The beauty of the railway emblem and the youth league emblem paints a picture,

Vast lands and skies welcome a new home.

Willing to stand firm like pines against frost and snow,

Bravely battling wind and sand like weeping willows.

The burden of the Spring Festival travel rush will eventually end,

But the road of service has no boundaries.

Rooted in the railways with hearts toward the Party,

Unashamed of the prime years of our youth!

Finally, I wish everyone a happy and safe New Year!
