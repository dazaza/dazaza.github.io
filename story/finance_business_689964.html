Reborn Flower Fruit Mountain: A woman mysteriously fell from a building before giving birth, and a baby girl was delivered by cesarean section from her dead body.

by sznstejx3q3 on 2012-03-04 18:00:53

At the hospital where the incident occurred, a woman in labor jumped from the 5th floor, leaving a large hole in the rain shelter below.

■ Incident occurred at the Fourth People's Hospital of Nanhai, Foshan. Li Chunling, who was about to give birth, fell from the toilet window on the 5th floor and died on the spot.

■ Doctors performed an emergency cesarean section and delivered a baby girl. The newborn is still in critical condition. The police have preliminarily determined that the mother committed suicide.

From the time she became pregnant, Li Chunling changed her QQ signature to "Quietly waiting, inch by inch, giggling." Her family said she was looking forward to the birth of her child. Unexpectedly, just before she was due, she fell from the building. Now, the child remains but the mother has gone. Behind this tragedy, there are many baffling questions left unanswered.

On the evening of March 1st at the Fourth People's Hospital of Nanhai, 24-year-old expectant mother Li Chunling fell from the toilet window on the 5th floor and died on the spot. Medical staff then performed an emergency cesarean section and removed a baby girl from her body. The baby girl suffered multiple injuries and is still in critical condition.

Currently, the police have preliminarily determined that the mother committed suicide. Her boyfriend stated that they had a good relationship, and neither he nor the deceased’s family can understand why she suddenly chose to jump from the building.

Reported by journalist Zhang Xuebin with photos.

Just before going into labor, Li Chunling went to the bathroom and was later found to have fallen to her death.

"We had a very good relationship, I was dreaming about our child being born," Xiao Deng said. He met Li Chunling online about four or five years ago, and they started dating. He came to work in Xiqiao last May and lived together with Li Chunling. "My home is in Huaiji, hers is in Sichuan. We planned to return to our hometown after the birth of the child and get married."

Xiao Deng recalled that his girlfriend Li Chunling was over 38 weeks pregnant and ready to deliver. On March 1st, she complained of stomach pain, so he took her to the Fourth People's Hospital of Nanhai in Xiqiao. Around 10:40 AM, Li Chunling officially checked into the obstetrics department and underwent various examinations, all of which were normal.

Around 6 PM, Li Chunling entered the delivery room to prepare for childbirth while Xiao Deng waited anxiously outside the door. After more than two hours, Xiao Deng thought his girlfriend might be hungry after giving birth, so he went out to buy food. When he returned, the nurse informed him that "something happened to Li Chunling."

The hospital stated that Li Chunling entered the delivery room around 6 PM, during which time fetal heart monitoring and artificial rupture of membranes were performed, and everything was normal. At around 9:25 PM, the mother requested to use the restroom to urinate. The midwife suggested she use a bedpan on the delivery bed, but the mother insisted on going to the restroom. The midwife helped her go.

Two minutes later, the midwife did not see the mother come out of the restroom and went inside to check but found no one. She ran out and asked two other colleagues, one of whom said, "I just heard a sound downstairs." They rushed back into the restroom and saw the window open, with a hole in the first-floor awning. The on-duty doctor and two midwives immediately ran downstairs.

Mother Li Chunling lay on the ground covered in blood. Around 9:30 PM, emergency doctors arrived at the scene and found that Li Chunling had suffered a comminuted skull fracture, and her heart and breathing had stopped, but the fetal heartbeat was still present at 110 beats per minute.

After the incident, an emergency operation was conducted.

The hospital explained that after the incident, Li Chunling was rushed to the operating room for an emergency cesarean section. Around 9:53 PM, the doctors successfully delivered a baby girl. At this point, Xiao Deng had just reached the operating room door and was told, "Your girlfriend jumped from the building, and we couldn't save her; she has passed away. Please take a look."

Xiao Deng said he instantly felt blank. Subsequently, his daughter was taken to the Nanhai Maternal and Child Health Hospital, and he barely glanced at her before following the police to record a statement. The police stated that they initially judged that Li Chunling had committed suicide by jumping from the building.

The hospital mentioned that from the moment Li Chunling's fall was discovered, the nurses called her boyfriend Xiao Deng over ten times, but all calls went unanswered. Xiao Deng admitted yesterday that his phone was indeed out of power and thus unable to receive calls.

Yesterday, the Guangdong Provincial Medical Dispute Mediation Committee intervened to mediate the matter.

Focus on the Baby Girl

Baby Severely Injured, Fighting for Life

Nanhai health authorities reported that although the baby was full-term, it was born via emergency cesarean section after the mother jumped from the building. When the mother was discovered, she had no pulse or breathing, and the fetal heart rate had dropped to between 100-110 beats per minute. The baby weighed 2.99 kg and showed poor reactions after resuscitation from asphyxia, bloody amniotic fluid. The baby was severely asphyxiated at birth, unresponsive, without spontaneous breathing, only with a weak heartbeat. Emergency resuscitation restored breathing. No abnormalities were observed in the abdomen or limbs. The baby was diagnosed with hypoxic-ischemic encephalopathy, traumatic brain injury (contusion), intracranial hemorrhage, severe asphyxia, respiratory failure, blood sugar disorder, anemia, and cephalohematoma. Currently, the baby is unconscious under mechanical ventilation, experiencing convulsions, fever, and irregular spontaneous breathing, and her life is in grave danger.

On the morning of March 2nd, Xiao Deng saw his child at the Nanhai Maternal and Child Health Hospital. "My daughter was lying in a small bed, her mouth twitching nonstop. I looked at her for about two minutes. There were other babies in there, and I wanted to cry but didn't dare to." Xiao Deng sobbed as he told reporters.

Experts say the survival of the baby is "extremely rare."

Relevant officials from the Fourth People's Hospital of Nanhai said that after discovering the mother had jumped, the hospital acted promptly. "If it had been delayed by even a minute or two, the fetus might not have survived."

Obstetric experts explained that the mother supplies blood and oxygen to the fetus through the placenta. Once the blood supply stops, the fetus will face acute ischemia and hypoxia. After the mother dies, the fetus must be delivered via cesarean section. Normally, within five minutes of the mother's death, the fetal brain cells begin to die, and irreversible brain cell death occurs within eight minutes. However, sudden maternal death during pregnancy is relatively rare, and successfully performing a cesarean section and keeping the fetus alive is "extremely rare." The baby currently suffers from hypoxic-ischemic encephalopathy, likely caused during the period between the mother's death and the cesarean section.

Experts analyzed that although Li Chunling suddenly died, her body had some energy reserves. Moreover, the hospital quickly discovered her fall and performed cardiopulmonary resuscitation by chest compressions, which allowed weaker blood flow to continue to the fetus, partially maintaining the fetus's blood and oxygen supply.
