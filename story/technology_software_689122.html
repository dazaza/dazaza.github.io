by anonymous on 2012-03-03 17:59:18

I have a dream, one day, driving a jeep, with a camera, running all over the mountains. Chasing nature, chasing sunrise and sunset, chasing flowers blooming and falling. I really yearn for that feeling of returning to nature. Really, I long for it so much....... I like to look at this wonderful world from a small perspective. So I am very interested in macro photography. Through the lens, we can see many things we often see but don't notice clearly. It's really amazing. This is a robber fly, there are many coarse bristles in front of the compound eyes of the robber fly, their function is to protect the eyes from being hurt. It is reported that their prey range is relatively wide, almost any flying insects are their food. After catching the prey, they inject digestive fluid into the body of the prey, dissolve the prey into liquid and then absorb it slowly. This is an adult male jumping spider photographed by Thomas in the Red Bud Valley Nature Reserve in Catoosa City, Oklahoma, USA. This is a close-up shot of the head of a damselfly, and it was also his first time shooting a damselfly. Damselflies are slender insects, very similar to dragonflies. At present, damselflies and dragonflies in the Mediterranean region are facing a survival crisis, the fresh water resources in this region have seriously reduced, and have been polluted to a certain extent. Thomas photographed a female horsefly landing on a toothpick box, his shooting angle was exactly right in front of the head of this horsefly. This is a yellow grasshopper photographed by Thomas. Initially, Thomas did not clearly find this grasshopper, its body color is very close to the surrounding environment. This female jumping spider is at least three-quarters of an inch long, one of the larger jumping spiders. Unfortunately, this female jumping spider quickly jumped away after being photographed and disappeared from Thomas' sight. This is a bee with a wet head landing on a flower, he said: "I don't think it's dead, maybe it cannot fly because of the cold dampness, landing on the flower to dry its wings." This is a ladybug landing on a plant leaf, there is a small insect in front of it. Ladybugs are brightly colored beneficial insects, usually with red, black or yellow spots on their bodies, their heads are all small. Ladybugs are the natural enemies of aphids growing on crops, they are very beneficial to the growth of crops. This is a mantis 1.5 inches long, when Thomas was taking pictures, it was chewing its own front claw for some unknown reason. This is a white-faced fly, this species is more representative in the fly family, its face is white. The body length of this miniature rhinoceros beetle is about 4-5 mm, it was resting on a flower when Thomas took the picture, they are a kind of beetle. This is a green collector bee, very small, only 10 mm long. It is reported that green collector bees are significantly different from most bee species, they do not live in hives, but live separately outside the group, mainly living in the soil, female bees are responsible for building nests, and laying eggs on pollen balls. In the picture, this green collector bee is covered with yellow pollen spots. This is a striped lynx spider, its scientific name is "Oxyopes salticus", there are some striped patterns on its shell. He pointed out that it is difficult to photograph this male striped lynx spider, it is harder to find than females. This is a close-up image of the head of a katydid, as the katydid enters adulthood, the bright colors on its body gradually fade. A male striped horsefly, it was perched on a white ceiling at the time, he pointed out that the legs of the striped horsefly have strange moving characteristics, in short, it is very different from other fly species. This is a tiger-patterned blind spider, its scientific name is "Nephrotoma ferruginea", the behavior of blind spiders is quite special, usually their behavior is unpredictable. This is a blind spider photographed by Thomas in the state park of Arkansas, although it has 8 legs (one leg is hidden in the picture), it does not belong to the spider species. This is a photo taken by Thomas of the eyes of a female jumping spider, its eyes show colorful colors.
