The bride-to-be was killed in a car accident on the third day of the Chinese New Year. Her fiancé then sent out a Weibo post to gather clues.

by xue94fwsh on 2012-02-24 12:05:30

JeromeZheng: On such a festive day, I reluctantly repost this Weibo. Faced with such an unbearable lightness of life, please cherish every day you live. As of last night when the reporter filed the story, this Weibo had been forwarded over 7,000 times, with more than a thousand comments. Many netizens left candles in their posts to express condolences. One netizen commented, "A girl born in 1986 really left this world on the third day of the lunar year! All friends born in 1986 who see this Weibo, please don't be stingy with your blessings, and pray for Yajun to reach another world." "Even though we didn't know each other, there is an inexplicable sadness. Peace is a blessing; cherish every person who loves you."

In her Weibo, Yajun mentioned her husband many times, calling him "Old Zhang". On January 24th, the day before the incident, she wrote in her Weibo post: "Such a man... got up early to clean the cat house at his aunt's place... then filled up the gas tank to pick me and my dad to go back to our hometown, chatted with the unfamiliar aunts and uncles in the countryside, helped grandma serve soup and dishes during dinner, helped brush off the dirt on dad's clothes, drank water himself when thirsty, played with his phone when bored... Such a man, without any complaints, no worries about not fitting in that I was concerned about, no complaints about the dirty and cold countryside, this is the man I chose! Worth praising!"

Mr. Zhang said that due to his minor injuries, he has returned to Nanjing to handle the aftermath. Yajun's parents are currently very disheartened as she was their only daughter.

Yesterday, the reporter contacted Mr. Zhang via telephone, who confirmed that the deceased in the accident was his newlywed wife, whose real name was Li Yajun, aged 26.

"Is there a photo of your new house renovation? Is it still under renovation?" "Yes!" The voice on the other end choked up, "It's almost done, at Taipingmen." He said they had known each other for 10 years and were classmates at Nanjing No. 34 Middle School, and later in high school they were in the same class. They registered their marriage on November 11, 2011. "We chose this date to represent forever, wholeheartedly!"

Li Yajun wrote on Weibo: "After arriving home, mom told me that an American student from East China Normal University found the breakfast stall run by my parents and ordered 60 breakfasts to give away to strangers, also as a small charity. He communicated with Baba (dad) in neatly written Chinese characters and paid the full amount. Mom said we need to do a good job with this task and not waste the goodwill of the foreign student! The plan will start on Monday (September 19), please follow @summer Yajun for updates, everyone please help spread the word."

The reason for posting this Weibo, Mr. Zhang explained, was because after the car accident, he couldn't remember anything. It wasn't until about 15 minutes later that he woke up. Mr. Zhang said that his first sight was of the airbag deployed, with his wife still unconscious in the passenger seat. He noticed that the large truck had moved its position and stopped on the emergency shoulder.

Yesterday, a Weibo post from Sina caught widespread attention from netizens: "Around 6 p.m. on January 25, my wife and I were on the Shanghai-Nanjing Expressway heading towards Nanjing, about two kilometers from the Zhenjiang exit, when a car accident occurred. My Toyota collided with a large truck, and my wife passed away that evening. Due to my loss of consciousness at the time, I have no photos from the scene. Please help forward this post and provide any clues. Thank you. My car number is Su A-FT656, and the truck's license plate is Hu A-C8562..."

This accident is truly heart-wrenching. On Weibo, Yajun mentioned that on October 2 last year, she posted a picture taken on the highway, writing, "Slippery roads in rainy weather, drive carefully!" She also recorded how her husband "Old Zhang" encountered an accident and proactively stopped the vehicle involved to ensure the injured were taken care of.

According to Mr. Zhang's introduction, during the last day of winter break, his wife graduated from a junior college but always set high standards for herself and had just obtained her bachelor's degree.

Reporter's Notes

During the conversation with Mr. Zhang, the reporter mentioned having browsed through his wife's Weibo, which almost documented every detail of the girl's life in the past year.

Mr. Zhang said that on January 24, they returned from Nanjing to Yajun's hometown Yangzhou to attend the wedding of one of her cousins. That night, they traveled from Yangzhou to Changzhou, and on the 25th, after visiting the Dinosaur Park, they left Changzhou at around 4:50 PM. Around 5:40 PM, they rear-ended a van-type truck ahead of them.

Mr. Zhang stated that Yajun's family situation was not good. Originally from Yangzhou, they later moved to Nanjing. Both parents were laid off and relied on selling breakfast for a living, working very hard. Therefore, Yajun was very filial to her parents.

In the car accident, the husband driving hit a large truck and was injured, while she succumbed to her severe injuries and passed away. Yesterday, the husband used her account to send out the 1987th Weibo post, hoping those who knew about the accident could provide clues.

"I have basically no memory of the entire accident scenario," Mr. Zhang said, "Maybe due to the shock." He admitted that his speed at the time was 100 km/h, which should not have been speeding. He acknowledged that he bore some responsibility in the accident, but the traffic police were still investigating and had not yet issued an accident determination report. He also hadn't seen the opposing driver.

She also shared chat records with "Old Zhang": "Yajun: Go away. Old Zhang: I've come back, wife." Then she said, "Old Zhang tricked me into marrying him like this."

An ambitious and grateful girl

On Weibo, the reporter unintentionally discovered a connection between Li Yajun and the Express Newspaper. Li Yajun once posted a half-page report from the Express Newspaper dated September 20 last year, titled "American Guy Buys 60 Pancakes to Have Them Given Away," subtitled "The pancake vendor couple decided to give away 5 to 10 pancakes daily to poor students and sanitation workers within half a month." She was the daughter of the pancake vendor couple.

On January 9, Yajun posted her admission ticket for the Jiangsu Province Adult Bachelor Degree English Exam on Weibo and said, "Passed the Jiangsu Province Adult Bachelor Degree English Exam!! Thanks to all dear ones' support..."
