Yuancheng Express: Freight passengerization, full-route fast direct service

by yuanchenwl1989 on 2012-02-27 09:24:59

"Yuancheng New High-Speed Railway" is a high-end intercity express product introduced to meet the growing logistics demands of highways in large and medium-sized cities. Unlike traditional intercity express services, Yuancheng New High-Speed Railway has abandoned the old method of highway less-than-truckload (LTL) transportation which waits until a full truckload is assembled before shipping. Instead, it draws on the concept of five fixed train services from railway EMU (Electric Multiple Unit) trains and implements the "five fixed" principles: fixed points, fixed routes, fixed trips, fixed times, and fixed prices, providing station-to-station and door-to-door services; vehicles achieve full highway travel and GPS full-process positioning tracking to fully ensure cargo safety. In addition, it provides value-added services such as cargo delivery, warehousing, collection of payment on behalf of customers, insurance, packaging, etc.

The "Yuancheng New High-Speed Railway" operates like an EMU train, with punctual and fast direct services, effectively improving operational efficiency and bringing great convenience to customers. Yuancheng New High-Speed Railway adopts a "freight passenger-like" transport model, breaking through the traditional LTL road transport methods in the domestic logistics industry. As one of the top 100 logistics enterprises nationwide, Yuancheng Group was the first to propose the service commitment of five fixed train services in the national logistics industry: "fixed points, fixed lines, fixed trips, fixed times, fixed prices", realizing road express from station-to-station and door-to-door, effectively improving operational efficiency.

The already opened 24 Yuancheng New High-Speed Railways have achieved comprehensive profitability. By the end of 2010, Yuancheng Group will successively open 50 new high-speed railways. To provide customers with more convenient services, Yuancheng Group is comprehensively expanding its terminal network construction. It is expected that by 2011, the scale of terminal business outlets will reach 2000, achieving nationwide coverage of nearly 300 large and medium-sized cities for direct road logistics.
