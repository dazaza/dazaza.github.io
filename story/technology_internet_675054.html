Beware of the Pitfalls of Popular Beauty Rules

by ib9b8x3m8m1 on 2012-02-24 17:33:05

Books published by female celebrities easily become a "beauty bible" in the hearts of many women, and the various "beauty secrets" introduced in these books are treasures for sisters to learn from and imitate to become more beautiful. However, are all the "beauty secrets" introduced in these books scientifically reasonable and safe? Some methods still need to be approached with caution.

Misconception 1: Toothpaste has a cooling and detoxifying effect; applying toothpaste can eliminate annoying pimples.

Analysis: In fact, this is a misconception, and hospitals often treat patients who suffer from skin allergies due to randomly applying toothpaste. Although some toothpastes contain antibacterial components, the oral environment and the skin environment are different. Applying toothpaste meant for oral use on the skin may cause side effects. For example, whitening ingredients in some toothpastes often contain hydrogen peroxide, which if used improperly, can burn the skin or cause allergies. It is recommended that if you want to treat acne, choose specialized products for acne treatment or consult a formal hospital. Currently, red and blue light therapy can be used to treat acne, while fractional laser treatments can be used for sunken acne scars.

Misconception 2: Drinking milk makes your skin whiter, eating soy sauce makes it darker.

Analysis: The claim that food color changes skin color lacks scientific basis. Our skin tone is influenced equally by genetic factors and external factors such as UV exposure, premenstrual phase, pregnancy, etc., all of which can alter the condition of the skin. Among them, UV exposure and melanin deposition have a closer relationship with skin darkening. However, frequent consumption of foods high in metals, such as seafood, combined with prolonged sun exposure, can easily lead to pigmentation. This is why residents in coastal cities tend to be darker. If individuals desire a fairer complexion, they might consider intravenous whitening injections, which work differently from physical sun protection (sunscreen, umbrellas) by inhibiting melanin production at the physiological and biochemical levels. With regular injections over a period of time, the skin will gradually become whiter and more delicate.

Misconception 3: Not washing your face in the morning helps maintain the moisturizing effect formed by night creams.

Analysis: After a night's metabolism, the skin secretes sweat, oil, and other substances. Dust, mites, and other contaminants from sheets and pillowcases may also affect the skin. Therefore, morning cleansing steps are essential, otherwise, pores can easily become clogged, leading to blackheads and acne. If people are troubled by issues like enlarged pores, blackheads, and oily skin, they might consider "Black Doll Baby" treatment for improvement. Named after the carbon powder applied before treatment, it is a process of gradual improvement. After five treatments in a course, the skin will appear finer and tighter.

Misconception 4: To nourish the skin, you must apply a mask every day.

Analysis: Unless you can ensure that the mask you use contains no chemicals, daily use will only burden the skin. Actually, the most important aspects of skincare are cleaning, moisturizing, and sun protection. During autumn and winter, moisturizing is emphasized, and simply applying ordinary moisturizers daily, such as Vaseline or glycerin, suffices. However, the use of Vaseline and glycerin varies depending on the environment. Vaseline is a moisture-retaining agent that forms a protective layer on the skin to prevent moisture evaporation, making it suitable for dry environments. Glycerin, on the other hand, absorbs moisture from the air, helping the skin retain more moisture in humid environments. However, glycerin becomes ineffective in dry environments like air-conditioned rooms.
