Experts remind that it's better to eat less of these 5 kinds of fruits in spring.

by anonymous on 2012-03-09 09:24:36

Bananas are nutrient-rich tropical fruits. They have a sweet taste and cold properties, and they help to moisten the intestines and clear heat. Their ripening season is every summer.

1. Pineapple

Problem: Some people experience nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, diarrhea, numbness of the lips, and skin itching after eating pineapple, which are symptoms of an allergic reaction.

Reason: This is due to the presence of glycosides and bromelain in pineapples. To prevent allergic reactions, after cutting the pineapple, it should be soaked in light salt water for more than ten minutes to destroy the bromelain before consumption.

Nutritionist: Pineapple is a tropical fruit rich in dietary fiber and bromelain, which is very beneficial for preventing constipation and indigestion. It's a great choice for summer fruits. As the weather gradually warms up, we see fewer vendors selling pineapples. Many vendors use fiberglass containers filled with saltwater to soak the pineapples, and each piece of pineapple has a chopstick inserted into it. Here’s a reminder: consider how long it has been since the saltwater in the fiberglass container was changed? Are the chopsticks new? Not only does this not meet hygiene standards, but it can also cause secondary contamination.

2. Bananas

Problem: To make the banana peel look tender and yellow, some unscrupulous vendors use excessive sulfur dioxide to ripen them. However, the flesh remains hard and not sweet at all. Sulfur dioxide is harmful to the human body.

Tip: The correct method should be warming and fumigating to ripen them.

Nutritionist: Bananas are nutrient-rich tropical fruits. They have a sweet taste and cold properties, and they help to moisten the intestines and clear heat. Every summer is their ripening season.

3. Grapes

Problem: Some unscrupulous vendors and farmers dilute the ripening agent ethylene with water, then soak the unripe green grapes in the diluted solution. After a day or two, the green grapes turn purple.

Nutritionist: Every August and September is the grape ripening season. Grapes have a sweet and slightly sour taste, neutral properties, and possess the effects of replenishing blood and qi, strengthening tendons and bones, and promoting urination. Their sugar content is relatively high, so diabetics should remember to eat sparingly.

4. Strawberries

Benefit: According to traditional Chinese medicine, strawberries have the effect of reducing internal heat, relieving summer heat, and clearing heat. In spring, people often have a strong liver fire, and eating strawberries can suppress it. Additionally, strawberries are best eaten after meals because they contain large amounts of pectin and fiber, which can promote gastrointestinal peristalsis, aid digestion, improve constipation, and prevent hemorrhoids and colon cancer.

Problem: Strawberries that are hollow in the middle, have irregular shapes, and are excessively large are generally due to excessive hormone use, except for a few high-yield new varieties. After using ripening agents or other hormonal drugs, the strawberry growth cycle shortens, and the color becomes more vibrant, but the inherent fragrance of strawberries is diluted, and they lack fresh flavor when eaten.

Nutritionist: Strawberries are very attractive fruits with a high vitamin C content. Eating fresh strawberries is a good choice. The natural ripening period for strawberries is late May each year.

5. Sugarcane

Problem: Most sugarcane in spring is stored from autumn. After being stored through winter, it is extremely prone to spoilage, with white fluff or moss-like substances appearing at the end of the sugarcane. When cut open, there will be red thread-like substances on the cross-section.

Hazard: Some people do not carefully select sugarcane when buying it. After cutting it open, they mistakenly think the red thread-like substance is normal, resulting in sugarcane poisoning after consumption.

Nutritionist: Sugarcane is a great "fire-clearing" food, with a sweet taste and cold properties, possessing the effects of clearing heat and generating body fluids, lowering qi and moisturizing dryness, harmonizing the stomach, and reversing qi. The sugarcane ripening season is in November. Due to improper storage, sugarcane may mold. Unripe sugarcane may also mold after being cut down.
