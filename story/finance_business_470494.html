Social networks fuel spread of smartphones in India

by tuoqing1188 on 2011-08-04 09:44:34

Introduction: Foreign media reported today that smartphones are quickly gaining popularity in India. In some relatively backward areas, and even those without tap water, people have started to use smartphones.

The following is the full text of the article:

Facebook as a Key Driver

In a shack in northern Mumbai with no running water, Darshana Verma is boiling tea on a small stove. On a nearby stool, her 18-year-old son is sending messages to his Facebook friends via a Nokia smartphone.

Verma spent half of her monthly income of $300 to buy Samsung and Nokia phones for her son. She said, "This is the age of the Internet. Facebook is there, and everything happens there. They make friends, and maybe they can even find jobs there."

According to Nielsen data, with the development of affordable internet phones, India may become the country with the second-largest number of Facebook users next year after the United States. With the growing popularity of Google's social network, companies like Pepsi are also increasing their online advertising efforts in India to reach 352 million internet users under the age of 15.

Tarun Abhichandani, head of business at WPP’s IMRB International group, said: "India has a young population, and social networks attract them to use the internet."

According to data from socialbakers.com, the number of active Facebook accounts in India increased by 85% this year, reaching 32 million. This ranks third globally, behind the U.S.'s 153 million and Indonesia's 39.2 million. Gartner estimates that by 2014, India's mobile phone sales will exceed 206 million units, up from 175.9 million last year.

Advertisers Leveraging Facebook

Pepsi and MTV, owned by Viacom, are using Facebook for marketing in India. The Facebook pages dedicated to India for both companies have received 1.4 million and 2.9 million "Likes," respectively. A Nielsen report stated: "Indian users want big brands to communicate with them through social media." Sixty percent of social media users in the Indian market are willing to engage with major brands.

According to IMRB data, in the year ending March of this year, online advertising revenue in India grew by 26%, reaching 9.9 billion rupees (approximately $223 million). Social network advertising grew by 65%. Abhichandani said, "The shift of the advertising industry towards online advertising has just begun. The number of internet users in India is rapidly increasing and will continue to grow for some time. Advertisers have realized this."

Facebook opened an office in Hyderabad, southern India, in September last year to assist users, advertisers, and developers. The company is trying to improve its services by collaborating with mobile partners and building good relationships with Indian developers and entrepreneurs.

Development of Other Social Networks

Twitter is also growing rapidly in India. Some well-known figures, such as Bollywood star Shahrukh Khan and corporate tycoon Anand Mahindra, use Twitter. Tata Consultancy Services, India's largest software exporter, recently released its quarterly financial report via a Twitter message.

LinkedIn has 10 million users in India, making it the country with the second-highest number of LinkedIn users after the U.S. Facebook currently faces competition from Google+. According to comScore data, as of July 24, Google+ had 6.44 million visitors in the U.S. and 3.62 million visitors in India.

Google is testing mobile applications in the U.S. and India to help users send status updates via SMS because most Indian mobile users do not have internet browsers. Facebook currently offers similar services in India.

RIM stated that its growth in emerging markets like India and Indonesia is mainly driven by social networking applications, including Facebook. Huawei currently sells a phone with a "Facebook button."

Paul Scanlan, Vice President of Solutions and Marketing for Huawei in the South Pacific region, said, "You click the button, and all your social networks will be integrated together. You don't need to log in everywhere. If the phone doesn't have a Facebook button, you won't be able to sell 10 million phones."

The decrease in smartphone prices has also attracted many Indian users. Currently, internet-enabled phones in the Indian market can be as low as $23. For Verma, who has never learned how to use a computer, she saved money for ten months to buy a phone for her daughter. She said, "I don't understand Facebook and the Internet, but they should. It's worth it." (Weijin)
