"The First Class" is a large-scale activity titled "Knowledge Protects Life" hosted by CCTV's Economic Channel. It is a major public welfare event with life awareness education as its theme, advocating emergency risk avoidance education for primary and secondary school students every semester. The emergency knowledge lecture hall in Beteck Technology provides four lessons' worth of materials. This class consists of four parts: "Potential," "Teamwork," "Persistence," and "Life." Eight Olympic champions including Chen Xiexia, Zhang Xiangxiang, and Liu Chunhong, along with members of the Chinese national basketball team such as Yao Ming, Yi Jianlian, and Wang Zhizhi will join children from disaster areas to learn through interaction and storytelling, closely combining Olympic spirit with disaster resistance spirit to make emergency risk avoidance education deeply resonate.

What kind of class is "The First Class?"

We applaud the special program on safety education for primary and secondary schools, "The First Class," co-produced by the Ministry of Education and CCTV.

Firstly, the theme of "The First Class" is excellent. With the theme of "Knowledge Protects Life," it allows 220 million primary and secondary school students across the country to learn emergency risk avoidance in their "first class." Not only does this reflect the high regard of the Party and the State for the safety of primary and secondary school students, but more importantly, with the theme of "Knowledge Protects Life," learning emergency risk avoidance in the "first class" marks the beginning of humanistic education and quality education, which helps strengthen the safety awareness of teachers and students and improve the ability and quality of primary and secondary school students to protect themselves. As Ye Zhiping, principal of Sangzao Middle School in An County, Sichuan Province, known as the "most awesome principal ever" by netizens, said in his message for "The First Class": "On May 12th, when the earthquake occurred, all 2,300 teachers and students in our school were evacuated to a safe place in just one minute and thirty-six seconds. This miracle was due to the evacuation drills we organized for students every semester since 2005. I advocate that at the start of each semester, give students a lesson on emergency risk avoidance. Because the value of life is above everything else. In the first class, knowledge protects life."

Secondly, the content of "The First Class" is excellent. Under the theme of "Knowledge Protects Life," through four classes—"Potential," "Persistence," "Teamwork," and "Life"—children are taught to recognize the value of life and master practical escape techniques. In the course, pianist Lang Lang tells a story about two children successfully escaping from a fire using their knowledge; literature professor Yu Dan explains from the "Shuowen Jiezi" (a classical Chinese dictionary) about lightning and thunder, telling children what they should pay attention to during thunderstorms; astronaut Yang Liwei tells about the persistence of astronauts during training, introducing what we should persist in doing when floods come to correctly escape... These contents recorded in the special TV program "The First Class" are not only urgent and necessary lessons for a wide range of primary and secondary school students but also more conducive to letting them learn common sense and skills of emergency risk avoidance.

Thirdly, the format of "The First Class" is excellent. The biggest feature of this "The First Class" in terms of format lies in the pre-production of a special program on safety education for primary and secondary schools. It will be broadcast on CCTV's Economic Channel (CCTV-2) from 18:55 to 20:55 on September 1st, inviting 220 million primary and secondary school students across the country to watch. Schools can record it and play it within the campus. Such a TV teaching format, compared to the traditional teaching formats organized by schools in the past, has better concentrated time and visualized teaching effects. Not only can teachers, students, and parents watch and receive education, but the entire population can also watch and be educated.

Fourthly, the methods of "The First Class" are excellent. Besides being hosted by popular figures like Xiao Ya Wang, "The First Class" also features renowned pianist Lang Lang, literary professor Yu Dan, and space hero Yang Liwei who personally teach safety knowledge. Additionally, famous Olympic stars and championship teams have been invited as guests and classroom teachers: the entire men's basketball team including Yao Ming, Yi Jianlian, and Wang Zhizhi; Olympic weightlifting champions Chen Xiexia, Liu Chunhong, Long Qingquan; Olympic sailing champion Yin Jian; former Olympic champions Luo Xuejuan, Gao Min, Yang Ling, Tian Liang, etc., have all come. Heroic youth Lin Hao from the earthquake rescue and Lin Miaoke, who sang "Singing Our Motherland" at the opening ceremony, will act as young class leaders in the program. Through such interactive and entertaining forms, the Olympic spirit and disaster-resistance spirit are closely combined, making emergency risk avoidance education deeply resonate. Such teaching methods are not only lively and interesting, enhancing the appeal of teaching, but also more conducive to helping children understand how to recognize life, respect life, and protect life in such lively and interactive ways.

Fifthly, the organization of "The First Class" is excellent. From the reports, we know that although the Ministry of Education notified local educational administrative departments and primary and secondary schools to do a good job in safety education at the start of school, the notification did not stop there. To provide educational resources for primary and secondary schools, the Ministry of Education jointly held the large-scale public welfare activity "Knowledge Protects Life" with CCTV and produced a special program on safety education for primary and secondary schools—"The First Class," requiring local primary and secondary schools to inform students and parents to actively watch it. Boarding schools can organize students to watch it together. The meticulous organization, solid work, and active actions of the Ministry of Education and CCTV are highly commendable.

National primary and secondary schools conduct theme class meetings on "Education of Love"

"The First Class" themed public welfare action is jointly held by CCTV and the Ministry of Education. Around August 20th, 2009, the Ministry of Education will issue a special document notifying primary and secondary schools across the country to organize teachers, students, and parents to watch the themed evening gala together on the evening of September 1st; and advocates that each class hold a themed class meeting on "Education of Love" once a year, to fill in this important lesson of "Education of Love" for primary and secondary school students across the country.

Last year's "Ten Major Rules for Life Protection" launched by "The First Class" received recognition and promotion throughout society, effectively promoting the popularization of safety education. This year, the themed evening gala will also launch "Words of Love" in simple language and rhymes to help teenagers understand the essence of love, cultivate the ability to love, and grow up healthily in an environment of loving education.

Prominent figures and celebrities unite to deliver this lesson well

Last year's "The First Class" received support from well-known social figures such as Peng Liyuan, "Flying Hero" Yang Liwei, renowned piano artist Lang Lang, the Chinese men's basketball team led by Yao Ming, the Olympic champion weightlifting team led by Ma Wenguang, the Olympic champion sailing board team, Liu Huan, Song Zuying, and cultural scholar Yu Dan, achieving good social results. This year, "The First Class" continues this tradition and invites well-known social figures such as Li Zhaoxing, Fan Zeng, Yu Dan, Yang Liwei, Deng Yaping, and Yao Ming to participate, using their personal experiences of serving the country and patriotic sentiments to deliver a solid patriotic education lesson to the children.

Background of the 2009 "The First Class: I Love You, China!" initiative

On September 1, 2008, CCTV and the Ministry of Education jointly launched the large-scale public welfare action "The First Class: Knowledge Protects Life." The program, with themes of life awareness education and emergency risk avoidance education, combined Olympic spirit with earthquake resistance spirit. Through interactions between Olympic champions and children, it made learning fun, highlighting people-oriented values, respect for life, and protection of life. After the program aired, it achieved good social results. On August 26, 2008, the Ministry of Education issued a special document notifying schools across the country to organize students and parents to watch the program together, allowing 220 million primary and secondary school students nationwide to take this class together. Due to strong social feedback, "The First Class" was rebroadcast on the first channel in October last year, and the economic channel also rebroadcast it multiple times, making "The First Class" a well-known brand.

In 2009, it is the 60th anniversary of the founding of our country. The Publicity Department of the Central Committee proposed to carry out mass patriotic education activities in depth. Minister Liu Yunshan particularly emphasized that carrying out patriotic education activities should be an important part of strengthening ideological and moral construction for minors and ideological and political education for college students, running through all aspects of classroom teaching and social practice. Adhering to the principle of "three close-to," using more forms that the masses enjoy, opening more channels that the masses can easily accept, and building more platforms that the masses are willing to participate in. A new round of patriotic education fever is also surging in various places. Against this backdrop, in 2009, "The First Class" will take the promotion of the spirit of patriotism as its theme, delivering a solid patriotic education class to primary and secondary school students across the country.
