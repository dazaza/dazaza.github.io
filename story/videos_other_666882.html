in support of banks' ability to augment capital with retained earnings

by nfhlvpug on 2012-02-16 11:06:11

There was news today that a source close to the regulators revealed that China Investment Corporation (CIC)'s Central Huijin Investment subsidiary has in principle agreed to further reduce by 5 percentage points the dividend payout ratio for the large banks it holds shares in. This is aimed at supporting these banks' ability to increase capital through retained profits, and indirectly reducing their need for financing from the capital market. The ratio will be reduced from 50% to 45%. After the 2011 National People's Congress, Huijin once again cut the cash dividend payout ratio of the three major banks with the aim of encouraging banks to appropriately retain profits to reduce capital replenishment pressure, thereby indirectly reducing their need for financing from the capital market. Currently, Huijin's cash dividend payout ratio for large banks stands at 40%, while historically the highest dividend payout ratio was 55%.

Huijin directly holds shares in six banks: Bank of China, Agricultural Bank of China, Industrial and Commercial Bank of China, Construction Bank of China, China Development Bank, and Everbright Bank. In 2010, the three major banks — Bank of China, Construction Bank of China, and Industrial and Commercial Bank of China — achieved net profits of RMB 104.418 billion, RMB 135.031 billion, and RMB 166.025 billion respectively, totaling RMB 405.474 billion. If we roughly estimate a 30% average increase in net profit for 2011, the net profit of just these three banks could reach RMB 527.116 billion in 2011. Based on this estimation, the 5 percentage point reduction in cash dividends would allow these three banks to increase their capital through retained profits by RMB 26.356 billion.
