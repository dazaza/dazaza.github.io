School requires students to be fined 1 yuan for scoring 1 mark less and rewards those who score high marks

by yyuyuu618 on 2012-02-25 14:34:25

Students' examination scores are below the standard. For every mark below, a fine of 1 yuan will be imposed to reward high-scoring students. On December 20, a parent of a student in Huoqiu County, Liu An City, surnamed Li and using a pseudonym, reported to our newspaper that his child is in the ninth grade and studying has already been very arduous. Instead of being cared for after performing poorly on exams, the child was asked by the teacher to pay money to "reward" classmates who performed well. The parent said that they could understand the teacher's painstaking efforts, but as a parent, they cannot accept it. A person in charge of the junior high school section of Huoqiu County Agricultural Machinery School admitted that this indeed happened, stating that the teacher’s method was inappropriate and has been stopped. Currently, the local education bureau has expressed that they will intervene in the investigation.

Report: Children who perform poorly on exams have to pay fines

According to Li's introduction, his child attends the ninth grade at the junior high school section of Huoqiu County Agricultural Machinery School. More than a month ago, the homeroom teacher, Mr. Chen, required students who performed poorly on exams to contribute money to reward classmates who performed well.

Li said that students in the ninth grade already endure tough studies and face immense pressure. Some students who performed poorly were even fined dozens of yuan. Alas, knowing the web, children who perform poorly already hesitate to tell their parents, let alone about the fines. They can only borrow from others; if they can't borrow, some even steal money from home. "Such fines began in the seventh grade and have continued for three years now."

Investigation: One student was fined up to 24 yuan

On December 20, the reporter went to the junior high school section of the county agricultural machinery school for an investigation. Cheng Xiaoguo (a pseudonym), a student in class nine(3), told the reporter: "For a single subject exam with a total score of 150 points, if the students' scores are below 105 points (i.e., 70% of the total score), Homeroom Teacher Chen will impose a fine of one yuan for each point below. The collected fines are used to reward students who perform better. During the last monthly exam, I was also fined a few yuan. A classmate in the class was fined up to 24 yuan."

Cheng also introduced that when he was in the seventh grade, he was fined 8 yuan. In the eighth grade, the teacher calculated based on rankings, and those who dropped five places compared to the previous exam had to be fined 5 yuan. "So far, I've been fined nearly 20 yuan."

The student's parent also told the reporter that when the child was fined 8 yuan in the seventh grade, they cried and ran home.

Subsequently, the reporter visited the classroom of class nine(3). Wang Mingnan (a pseudonym) of the class told the reporter that because he performed poorly in English, he was fined more than ten yuan, but the math exam rewarded him with 5 yuan, "My 'cost' hasn't been recovered yet."

Response: The teacher's good intentions but inappropriate methods

The principal of the school, Li Lintao, explained: "The issues reflected by the parents and students are true. The teacher's intention was certainly to motivate the students' enthusiasm for learning. The collected fines are held by the class monitor, rewarding those who performed well and temporarily keeping the money of those who performed poorly, which will be returned if they perform well next time."

He introduced that this method improved the average score of the entire class significantly. "Later, three students from class nine(3) wrote a letter to the 'Principal's Mailbox' about this matter, so I let their class vote on whether to continue or not. At the time, students ranked in the lower-middle range were quite worried, and only a minority supported continuing. Finally, after the first monthly exam, I stopped it and had the class monitor return the money to the fined students."

Progress: The educational authority will intervene in the investigation

Before publishing, the reporter informed the Huoqiu County Education Bureau of this situation.

The director of the county education bureau, Liu Yuhong, stated that such phenomena are absolutely unacceptable, and he will arrange relevant personnel to intervene in the investigation.
