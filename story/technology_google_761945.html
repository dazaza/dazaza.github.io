Google Game Platform is About to Launch: Chinese Developers Have Different Attitudes

by geekzhang on 2013-05-16 17:21:25

At the opening of the 2013 Google I/O (Google Global Developer Conference), Google announced the release of its gaming platform named Google Play Games, which was a long-awaited feature by many game developers. In the early hours of May 16 in Beijing, this answer would be revealed.We are in the gaming business, so we most look forward to their gaming platform. On May 15, Zhang Rui, CEO of Zhaomeng Chuangyou who was in San Francisco awaiting the opening of Google I/O, told reporters that he hoped to gain more users on this platform after its launch.Before the conference opened, some foreign websites had already leaked information about the Google Play Games platform. From the disclosed information so far, this is a platform similar to Apple's Game Center, allowing users to interact with friends through games.The specific functions may include: synchronized game saving, inviting friends, achievement rankings, etc. Of course, the specific details will be revealed after the release. Setting aside the specific details, what does the official launch of Google's gaming platform mean for developers?This is definitely a good thing for developers. Sun Jian, CEO of Xiaao Games, told reporters that due to the openness of Google's Android system, many third parties have launched their own gaming platforms on it, but it has been difficult to unify. This is also a problem for users.It can be foreseen that after the launch of Google's gaming platform, similar third-party tools will be further impacted because Apple and Microsoft already have their own game centers. Developers will tend to choose the platforms of the three major operating system manufacturers.However, there are also calls for developers to remain calm about this. The impact of Game Center on Apple is just so-so. Xiao Yongquan, CEO of Dangle Network, said that Google should have launched this function two or three years ago; now it is merely following the trend.The Late Game CenterIn the Android ecosystem, gaming applications have always been the hottest and easiest way to generate revenue, with countless developers investing in them.Although there are a lot of gaming applications on the Android platform, there is a lack of a unified platform. Separating the app market from the gaming platform is a trend. Xiao Yongquan believed that both Apple and Microsoft launched such services quite early.As early as September 2010, Apple officially released iOS 4.1, one of the important updates being the addition of Game Center. Apple terminal users could play games through Game Center and engage in simple social interactions with friends, such as inviting friends to play together and checking leaderboards, etc.The benefit for developers lies in enhancing user stickiness. Especially before mobile online games became mainstream, single-player games were the mainstay on iOS. The biggest drawback of single-player games is that users easily lose interest, but adding social attributes can extend the lifecycle of the game.For example, the achievement rankings in Game Center encourage some players to keep investing in order to boast among their friends. Additionally, the invitation recommendation feature of Game Center is much loved by developers. Because it is a good channel for good games to spread through word-of-mouth and gain users outside of ranking manipulation.Sun Jian, CEO of Xiaao Games, commented that Game Center plays a significant role in single-player games. Now, after making an iOS game, they generally ensure to add Game Center functionality.Two months after Apple launched Game Center, Microsoft also rolled out a service called Game Hub. It is largely similar to Game Center, capable of connecting games across various Microsoft platforms, enabling online multiplayer matches, inviting players, and score rankings, etc.For Android games, this is also a market demand. Sun Jian believed that previously, this demand was met by some third-party platforms, such as OpenFeint, etc. Many developers have been urging Google to provide this service officially.Now it seems that Google is finally going to follow this trend. Xiao Yongquan also hopes that Android game developers remain calm about this because this is not a new feature, and for the Chinese market, due to the difficulty of landing the official Google Play store domestically, it is hard to judge how much impact the gaming platform can bring.Solving the Chaotic Domestic MarketThe Chinese market has its particularities. In the short term, the influence of Google's gaming platform on the domestic market is limited. Xiao Yongquan believes that for overseas game developers, the release of the gaming platform might bring more opportunities. Because abroad, the primary download channel for Android apps is the official Google Play store.For overseas promotion, we definitely lead with Google Play and AdMob. Sun Jian told reporters that afterward, they could develop some local channels, such as local carriers, etc.In the Chinese market, due to policy reasons, Google Play has not yet landed domestically, so there are various third-party Android markets within the country. It is difficult for any one platform to cover all users. Moreover, the quality of applications varies greatly across different download channels, and many counterfeit or malicious applications are mixed in.Regarding the differences between the Chinese and overseas markets, Wang Junyu, co-founder of Wandoujia, deeply understands this. We focus purely on tools abroad, like the management features inside the PC client. Our English version only covers this area. Wang Junyu said that although there are other app stores abroad, they cannot compete on the same level as Google Play.But domestically, the core function of Wandoujia currently is application whitelisting, which means helping users find genuine applications without malicious plugins from the multitude of cluttered apps.Our backend has collected over 3 million different application installation files, but we only release 540,000 of them, less than one-fifth of the total, for users to download. Wang Junyu said, because we filter out fake applications or those containing malicious software and ad plugins.From this ratio, it can be seen how chaotic the domestic Android app market is. Among all Android apps, games are the hardest hit by piracy.Sun Jian believes that the biggest challenge facing domestic Android game developers is the fragmentation of distribution channels and piracy. Although fragmented channels have advantages and disadvantages, the inability to control piracy seriously affects the enthusiasm of developers.Compared to single-player games, pirating online games is relatively harder and less frequent. Industry insiders analyzed that if the social features of the gaming platform can solve the piracy problems of domestic Android games, perhaps this is the greatest role that Google's gaming platform can play.
