Shanghai Mobile Launches Driving Assistant Service for Timely Violation Records - News Center - Sina Tech

by qiyouchax05 on 2011-05-15 08:22:47

A few days ago, I had dinner with several friends who own cars. During the meal, we talked about the "electronic eye" issue. The original intention of those automatic probes is naturally to regulate drivers' behavior, recording those who speed or violate prohibitory signs as a warning to others. "Now think about it, if there were no electronic eyes to restrain us, we might drive even more recklessly," said Old Zhang after sipping a mouthful of tea.

However, troubles soon followed. When driving out, one could be caught on camera for a violation without even realizing it. Unlike real police officers, drivers often don't know they've been caught until they leave Shanghai or during their annual inspection, by which time they have to pay a late fee. Last year, there was a news report in another province about a ten thousand yuan late fee for traffic violations, causing panic among car owners who now frequently check their violation records online. But everyone is busy, and after checking a few times, they forget to regularly go online to look, so the problem persists.

However, Xiao Wang didn't think much of it: "Apply for Shanghai Mobile's Driving Treasure service. For just two yuan a month, you can receive your violation records via text message in a timely manner, and never worry about late fees again."

The Driving Treasure that Xiao Wang mentioned is a text messaging service provided to Shanghai Mobile customers for vehicle traffic police information inquiries, mainly focusing on traffic violation information. It sends traffic police information (electronic eye violations, ground violations, etc.), demerit points information, vehicle inspection information, driver physical examination information, vehicle insurance expiration information, and driver's license expiration and renewal information to subscribed users. It basically covers all the issues that car owners need to worry about, allowing them to easily check and customize their information on their mobile phones, enhancing the enjoyment of driving.

"However, I must remind everyone to still abide by traffic laws. Without any violation records, everyone would be happy," I said as a closing remark after a satisfying meal, "And definitely do not drive under the influence of alcohol!"
