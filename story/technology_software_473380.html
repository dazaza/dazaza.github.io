Abstract: With the deep development of the market economy, market competition has become increasingly intense, and the important role of procurement management in enterprise management has gradually become more prominent. Material procurement is a sensitive, complex process that is prone to issues. This article explains the significant role of material procurement management in enterprise management and proposes methods such as establishing and improving regulations, scientifically and reasonably selecting material procurement methods, and building an enterprise supply chain management system to improve the company's material procurement management.

Keywords: Material Procurement; Enterprise Management; Bidding Procurement; Procurement Management

Introduction

Chinese enterprises, especially the numerous small and medium-sized enterprises, face increasingly fierce international competition, with product production cycles gradually shortening and consumer product needs becoming more diversified. Procurement refers to the overall activity of purchasing items necessary for the company's product production and normal operations, as well as facilities that must be equipped. It is a key focus of cost control for companies. Material procurement management is an effective means for enterprises to reduce material costs, enhance market competitiveness, and increase profit levels, making it an important part of the enterprise management process. To adapt to the evolving market situation, material procurement management should be strengthened.

I. Issues in Traditional Procurement

Material procurement and management are not well-established. Currently, material management, procurement management, and supplier management are handled by one functional department, lacking necessary supervision and control mechanisms. Since most procurement operations and negotiations with suppliers are conducted via telephone or the internet, procurement information and supplier information are monopolized by individual business personnel without being shared. The execution performance depends on individuals, and changes in personnel positions significantly impact business operations. From the procurement situations of some enterprises, the existing problems cannot be ignored and are mainly manifested as follows:

Inadequate, unstandardized, and imperfect systems. System construction is a long-term process. Before enterprise procurement is implemented, corresponding systems and measures should be formulated, but there remains a considerable gap from achieving scientific standardization.

Arbitrariness exists in the operation process, failing to strictly follow bidding procurement procedures, leading to non-transparent, unfair, and inequitable actions. This inevitably results in behind-the-scenes operations, favoritism, and even transforming "dispersed corruption" into "concentrated corruption."

Unclear responsibilities exist between the procurement supervisory body and the procurement execution body, with relationships yet to be fully smoothed out, easily resulting in low efficiency.

The overall quality of procurement personnel needs improvement. The comprehensive quality of procurement personnel includes moral integrity, knowledge, ability, and physical fitness in four aspects. With the increasingly fierce development of the market economy, procurement personnel are required to enhance their ideological and moral qualities, strengthen their business capabilities, and master professional knowledge about the products they procure. Strengthening vocational education for procurement work positions is particularly important. However, currently, many enterprises focus only on efficiency while neglecting preventive vocational education for procurement work positions. Most adopt economic means to reward and punish procurement personnel, causing them to gradually lower their self-requirements.

II. Necessity of Strengthening Procurement Management

Increasingly fierce competition and the need to reduce product costs. There are two basic ways to reduce costs: one is to reduce consumption during the production process, and the other is to reduce procurement prices during the procurement process. Traditional cost management primarily addresses reducing production consumption, where cost-saving potential in the production process is limited. In contrast, due to the ever-changing market, the procurement process offers infinite possibilities for cost reduction.

The need for standardizing material procurement management in enterprises. As the market economy develops, procurement activities have become more challenging. Enterprises must compare and select materials based on quality, price, delivery method, credit, and other factors, necessitating a real strengthening of procurement management. Additionally, various promotional tactics, rebates, kickbacks, commissions, and other methods used to sell products can lead to personal gain and corruption if strict procurement management systems are not established to regulate and supervise these processes, causing serious damage to enterprises.

This is the fundamental responsibility of the human resources department.
