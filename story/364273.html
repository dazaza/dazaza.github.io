Yu Dan's quotes

by rentouma on 2010-09-11 11:30:28

1. Time didn't wait for me, it was you who forgot to take me away. The fireflies in my left hand are unforgettable, while in my right hand is a decade of long meditation.

2. Everyone is a king, domineering in their own world. You don't have to listen to me, but you shouldn't make me listen to you either.

3. There are only so many spots around a person and only so much you can give. In this small circle, when some people come in, others inevitably have to leave.

4. A person always has to walk on unfamiliar roads, see unfamiliar scenery, and listen to unfamiliar songs. Then, at an inattentive moment, you will find that the things you tried hard to forget are really forgotten.

5. Not every effort yields harvest, but every harvest requires effort. This is an unfair irreversible proposition.

6. Memory is like water poured into the palm, whether you spread it out or hold it tightly, it will eventually flow clean between your fingers.

7. I forgot which year, which month, and which day I carved a face smiling with sorrow, gazing at me on a wall.

8. We smile and say we stay in the original place of time, but in fact, we have already been silently carried away by the torrent.

9. Some people will always be etched in memory, even if you forget their voice, their laughter, their face, but whenever you think of them, that feeling will never change.

10. Those who once said they would never separate are now scattered to the ends of the earth.

11. You will never see me at my loneliest, because I am loneliest when you are not by my side.

12. Who is who's passerby, who is who's life wheel, the dust of the previous life, the wind of this life, an endless soul of sorrow. Ultimately, no one belongs to anyone.

13. This city has no legend of grass growing and orioles flying, it forever lives in reality, rapid drumbeats, hurried figures, numb eyes, false smiles, and I am being assimilated.

14. Time did not teach me anything, but it taught me not to easily believe in myths.

15. Departure makes things simple, people kind, like children, we start anew.

16. A wounded beast can run to a cave and hide, lick its wounds, endure alone, but once comforted, it cannot bear it.

17. Wounds are like me, stubborn children, refusing to heal because inside is a warm humid place suitable for anything to grow.

18. Because I know you're a child prone to worry, so I entrust the line to you yet dare not fly too far.

19. One day I will quietly walk away from your side without making any sound. I missed a lot, I am always sad alone.

20. I look at you smiling, silent, smug, lost, then I follow your happiness and sadness, but I stand in the present while you remain stuck in the past.

21. If we were all kids, we could stay in the original place of time, sitting together listening to stories that never age as we slowly grow old.

22. When you truly love something, you will find how fragile and powerless language is. Words and feelings always have a gap.

23. Forgetting is our unchangeable fate. Everything is like misaligned drawings; the past cannot return, slowly extending, bit by bit diverging. Perhaps, the things that diverged, we should really forget.

24. What is happiness? It's covering up your own sadness and smiling at everyone.

25. I don't like talking but talk the most every day, I don't like laughing but laugh nonstop. Everyone around me says my life is happy, so I also think I am really happy. But why do I suddenly become silent among a group of friends, why do I feel sad seeing a similar back in the crowd, why do I forget to speak when I see autumn trees shedding leaves wildly, why do I forget my original direction when I see the warm yellow streetlights as dusk falls...

26. You give me a drop of tears, and I see your entire ocean.

27. Your smile once can make me happy for days; but seeing you cry once makes me sad for years.

28. Lonely people always carefully remember everyone who appears in their lives, so I keep thinking of you on every starry night where I count my loneliness.

29. Hiding at a certain time, missing the palm prints of a certain period; hiding at a certain place, missing someone who stands on the path I came from and also the path I'm going to, making me miss.

30. Holding my hand, closing your eyes, you won't get lost.

31. If one day we are not together anymore, we should still be like we are together.

32. Children who are involved with words are never happy, their happiness is like playful children wandering until dawn, wandering until dawn but still unwilling to return.

33. Will the love carved on the back of the chair be like flowers on the cement ground, blooming endlessly in a forest without wind?

34. Aim for ideals, not fantasies; passion, not pretense. Be content with what you have.

35. From the moment the butterfly emerges from the cocoon, it's the pain of tearing off a layer of skin, thorough and heart-wrenching. Many butterflies die from the pain at the moment they break free from the cocoon.

36. We keep searching, searching for the ending we both share.

37. At that very moment, I felt as if I heard the sound of the whole world collapsing.

38. I've been brave for too long, deciding to live just for you.

39. I've stood for too long, spoken for too long, and I'm tired myself. Why can't you still understand? I've written too much, too long, and I'm tired myself. Why can't you still comprehend?

40. The noise at the airport stops soon, each person here has their own direction, rushing to take off, rushing to land, taking others' stories, leaving their own memories behind.

41. My sunset-like sadness is like the melancholy birds, melancholy birds flying turn into my sunset-like sadness.

42. Even if the whole world leaves you, I'll still be by your side. Let's go crazy together in hell.

43. My tears flowed down, watering the soft grass below, not knowing next year, whether it will bloom with memories and sorrows.

44. The flying birds in the sky, are you lonelier than me, or am I sadder than you? The remaining time, will you accompany me? So neither of us feels lonely, nor will I feel sad...

45. If waiting could bring about miracles, I'd rather wait, even for a year, or perhaps a lifetime!

46. We are like the needles on the surface, constantly turning, one side turns, one side watches time rush away, but powerless.

47. I always gaze at those sunrises and moonsets, homeless sorrows.

48. The sound of the cello is like a river, the left bank is the memories I cannot forget, the right bank is the brilliant youth I should hold tightly, flowing in between is the faint sadness year after year.

49. I don't know, when facing death, looking at the vast sky can be so desolate, the mournful cries of snow geese, slanting across the sky, I see your face floating in the cobalt blue sky, and I smiled because I saw you, happy like a child who never grows up.

50. Friends always shield you from the wind and rain, if you endure snowstorms far away and I am powerless to help, I will pray, letting those winds and snows fall on me.

51. If memories are as hard as steel, should I smile or cry? If steel corrodes like memories, is this a joyful city or a ruin?

52. The clamor and brightness of the world, the mundane happiness and joy, like a clear stream, in the wind, before my eyes, flows gently by, warmth surges like a spring, I have no extravagant hopes, I just want you to be happy, not sad.

53. If I could be with you, I'd rather let all the stars in the sky fade, because your eyes are the brightest light in my life.

54. "I always thought mountains were the story of rivers, clouds the story of winds, you my story, but I didn't know if I am your story."

55. Matches spoke wisely, saying men are animals driven by lower instincts. Those men who promised you happiness for the rest of your life actually meant happiness for your lower parts.

56. Holding your hand, wherever we are, I feel like we're running towards paradise.

57. Memories are like decaying leaves, those fresh green ones are long buried in the front segment of the timeline, only the pervasive smell of decay lingers at the end of the timeline.

58. Among the sounds of oars and lanterns, the sky is still cold, the water still cold. Dreamlike string music softly sings, beyond the tower, beyond the mountain. People outside the tower and mountain haven't returned, haven't returned.

The returning geese look back, having passed the River of Oblivion. The zither player's tears soak his robe. Snowflakes drift down on shoulders, filling the shoulders. Cold flute music echoes, window shadows remain incomplete. Amidst the misty waters and paddle sounds, where is Jiangnan?

59. They used to be ambiguous, when exactly were they ambiguous?

60. Happiness and joy are so similar, but is happiness the same as joy?
