NOKIA officially released N85 and N79 on August 27th, with moderate prices.

by clickme on 2008-08-26 11:39:07

On August 24th, a website with its domain name in date format quietly appeared. The website's URL was http://www.080827.com, and it only featured an animation in Flash format. The abstract images displayed the interface diagrams of two Nokia "All in One" all-in-one models - the N85 and N79. Looking back at similar moves by BlackBerry when they launched the BlackBerry Bold and HTC when they launched the Touch Diamond, it is not difficult to see that Nokia's mysterious models will be revealed on the date 20080827.

The N85, though considered an upgrade of the N81, is seen by the NOKIA Mini Station as a "scaled-down version" of the N96. It focuses on the audio-visual field, fully supporting the N-Gage gaming platform and coming equipped with an 80GB capacity MicroSDHC memory card. Most notably, the N85 is the first to use an AMOLED display screen. This new type of screen not only uses far less power than liquid crystal screens but also has astonishing display effects!

In this release, the N79 can only be considered a secondary to the N85~ Unfortunately, Nokia's TOUCH era has yet to officially begin, and the 5800 TUBE will still be released at the end of 2008.

The N85 is priced at $450, approximately £358 or 3100 RMB; the Nokia N79 is priced at $350 (£279), or 2400 RMB.
