Practical tips for quick weight loss

by xiaomyan2004 on 2012-03-05 23:13:28

I used to be overweight as well, but I enjoy reading, including books about weight loss. So, over time, I accumulated a fair amount of knowledge about losing weight. Are there any easy ways for us to lose weight? The women in my office have always wanted to slim down, but they often get discouraged. I always tell them that they can lose weight. Back then, I weighed over 100 pounds and wore size 31 pants, but now look at me! Losing weight always comes at a cost. They always say I'm lying to them, that it's easy for me to talk now that I'm so thin. Out of frustration, I decided to prove it by looking through my old photo albums when I went home for the National Day holiday. Sure enough, it worked. If it weren't for encouraging them, I wouldn't have wanted to show them my uglier pictures.

Actually, my weight loss secret is very simple, and it doesn't cost much money! Two words: "eat less"! There's no need to spend a lot of money on weight loss products, because not eating is absolutely impossible, as you won't have any energy.

Before college, I never really felt the effects of being overweight, but after starting university, I suddenly gained weight. Solid evidence includes the fact that someone of my height, just over 150 cm, had to wear size 31 pants (do you know what size 31 means?). During that period, the most frustrating thing was buying clothes, especially pants, which all had to be several sizes larger, and then I would cut off a large portion of the legs. Shirts also had to be larger sizes. Every shopping trip left me feeling very insecure. At my heaviest, I took a picture with a classmate (unfortunately, I don't have that picture anymore, which is quite regrettable). Later, when I saw that photo again, I was shocked at how fat I used to be, with my features squeezed into the middle of my face by those two big cheeks. Oh, what days were those!

So I started trying to lose weight, but I never succeeded. I tried cucumber, fruit, and vegetable diets, eating only these things and avoiding carbohydrates entirely. After persisting for a month, I found that my weight hadn't changed at all, and I was craving meat like crazy. Finally, I gave up during a classmate's birthday celebration. I also tried the three-day apple diet, the seven-day grape diet, binging one day and then fasting for several days (all these methods were quick-fix celebrity diets I read about in magazines), but none of them worked. Therefore, I believe you should never trust those rapid weight loss methods; they're simply impossible. Of course, I can't make definitive claims about situations where longing leads to "a person becoming thinner than chrysanthemums."

During the winter break of my third year in college, I decided to lose weight. It was a huge challenge for me. Since I hadn't been home for half a year, and it was the New Year with plenty of delicious food, I still insisted on eating only slightly more than half a bowl of rice per meal. If I ate various snacks, I would eat even less, maintaining a balanced food intake. To avoid my mother nagging, I forced myself to finish two meals, but only a little bit. After returning to school after the winter break, I only ate one or two taels of rice and a small amount of vegetables per meal (eating half and dumping the rest). Don't be stingy; be resolute for the sake of losing weight. Dinner must be finished before six o'clock, and absolutely no eating after six (at least three hours before sleeping, though water is allowed). Remember this!

The daytime was okay, because after breakfast, I could look forward to lunch, and after lunch, I could look forward to dinner. But the long nights were hard to endure. This depends on everyone's self-discipline. Back then, I kept comforting myself, "Fat is burning," burning fat helps with weight loss. After eating, I stood for 30 minutes (walking is even better) to prevent abdominal fat accumulation. I persisted throughout the spring. The north is relatively cold, and even in spring, I was still wearing thick thermal clothing, so no one said I was thinner, and I couldn't determine when I actually lost weight. In May, when the weather warmed up, one day while I was washing clothes in the washroom, a classmate from the next dormitory said, "Ma Ya, you seem to have lost weight." "Really?" I was pleasantly surprised. Throughout the summer, I kept hearing people say I had lost weight, and I no longer had to buy clothes that didn't fit my height. Everything was S or XS, and I was very happy!

That's how I lost weight, weighing around 86-88 pounds. After starting work, although I occasionally returned to 94-95 pounds in winter, I have consistently weighed 80 pounds this year. At this point, I feel the need to eat more and replenish. Girls shouldn't be too thin; legs thin like bamboo poles scare me. That state is too terrifying and too tragic. So when I was too thin, I quickly ate more to replenish. A close friend has always weighed around 70 pounds, she is a few centimeters taller than me. Recently, she has been drinking milk before bed, and in two months, she broke the 80-pound barrier, changing her years-long status quo. She was very happy.

Here, I want to emphasize that one shouldn't blindly pursue being thin. I suggest that while reducing food intake, it's best to strengthen exercise as well, so you won't end up like me, being extremely afraid of the cold! Thin people lack vitality!

Of course, you can selectively use some side-effect-free weight loss pills to lose weight, but you must combine them with exercise and absolutely cannot be lazy. Only then can you truly achieve weight loss results and showcase your charm with an attractive figure, ready to flaunt fashionable clothing.
