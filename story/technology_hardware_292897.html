(6) Before You Kiss Me —— The Rich Playboy Falls in Love with Me, a Young Widow

Manager Liang has taken on another major task: planning a "biography" for the famous entrepreneur Mr. Yang Guofeng. Although it's called a "biography," it's actually written by a ghostwriter, with only the entrepreneur's name on it. Entrepreneurs are usually too busy making money every second to have time and energy for writing. However, rich people often like to pretend to be refined, taking pride in publishing autobiographies, specialized books, and blogs.

As usual, Manager Liang handed the planning over to me and instructed me to finish it carefully because this was a big deal. It was rumored that Mr. Yang Guofeng's company had rejected countless proposals already. If we didn't want to become one of the "dead bodies," we would have to try our best!

With just a few words from her, I ended up staying up all night.

In the evening, I was working overtime in front of the computer while Su Chang was doing his homework under the lamp. After a while, he came to me with his exercise book: "Mom, sign here."

I was surprised: "Does your homework require a parent's signature?"

Su Chang nodded seriously.

Su Chang is indeed a handsome boy, with double eyelids, large round eyes, slightly curly hair, and fair skin. Like all boys, he is mischievous and energetic, but perhaps because he knows his family situation is different from others, he is more mature than ordinary children of the same age.

Looking at his exercise book, even though the handwriting was crooked, all the answers were correct. Su Chang is a child with strong learning ability.

"Will the teacher still grade your work after I sign it?"

Su Chang shook his head: "The teacher said that if the parents see it and sign it, that's enough. The teacher only checks the signatures."

I frowned, understanding that the teachers at this school were being lazy, shifting responsibility to the parents. How could they do this?

Su Chang goes to a nearby elementary school with average teaching quality. In fact, I've thought about sending him to a nearby famous private school, but the tuition fee of ten thousand yuan per academic year makes me hesitate.

When Uncle Su passed away, he left me only this mortgage-free small house and a savings account of thirty thousand yuan. Over these years, I've lived frugally, trying to save some tuition fees for Su Chang's further education. But now it seems that I might need to use those savings earlier. I've also attended university and deeply understand the importance of early quality education. Private schools offer more comprehensive education, including not only academic learning but also special talent training and emotional intelligence cultivation. From the perspective of future development, it is more suitable for Su Chang's growth.

After signing my name on the exercise book, I told Su Chang: "Your handwriting is terrible. When you grow up and become a boss, if your signature on the contract looks like this, others will laugh at you. Go practice your handwriting."

Pouting, Su Chang replied: "Moms always type on computers, and bosses definitely do too."

I was momentarily stunned, then laughed. This little guy has quite an unusual observational ability, but I still need to educate him: "Are you not listening to Mom again?"

He obediently held his exercise book and continued practicing writing.

Smiling, I watched him. Time flies so fast. The first time I saw him, he was only two years old, unable to walk steadily, always wanting to be carried by adults. But now, he is already a six-year-old elementary school student.

Sigh, can I see from him that I am gradually getting older?
