Chinese men's table tennis team bravely won the 17th team championship, being the king of both World Table Tennis Championships and Olympic Games.

by icf168 on 2008-08-18 23:06:34

On the evening of August 18, 2008, the Peking University Gymnasium witnessed the birth of the second table tennis gold medal at the Beijing Olympics. The Chinese men's table tennis team defeated the German men's team 3-0 to bravely win the men's team championship. This was also the first ever men's team gold medal in Olympic table tennis history.

Table tennis is known as China's national ball. The Chinese men's table tennis team won the first men's team gold medal in Olympic history, continuing the glorious history of Chinese table tennis and once again proving to the world that the Chinese table tennis team is worthy of being the king of the world table tennis arena.

The men's team competition at this Olympic Games consisted of one doubles match and four singles matches. The Chinese men's table tennis team has consistently demonstrated strong abilities in both singles and doubles matches in various major competitions.

A Comprehensive Strong Team in the World Table Tennis Championships

Since the 26th World Table Tennis Championships held in Beijing in 1961, where the older generation of Chinese table tennis athletes won the men's team championship, the Chinese men's team has lifted the Swaythling Cup 16 times, the most among all countries. At the 49th World Table Tennis Championships held in Guangzhou, China, in 2008, the Chinese men's team led by Ma Lin, Wang Liqin, and Wang Hao won the cup again, achieving the second four consecutive championships in the history of the Chinese men's team.

The individual singles ability of Chinese male table tennis players is equally beyond doubt. In 1959, Rong Guotuan won the men's singles championship at the 25th World Table Tennis Championships in Dortmund, Germany, earning China its first world championship title. In 2007, in Zagreb, Croatia, Wang Liqin, known as "Dali," won the men's singles World Championship for the 14th time for China, becoming the second Chinese player after Zhuang Zedong to win the World Table Tennis Championship three times.

China's men's doubles also boasts a brilliant history. In 1963, in Prague, Czechoslovakia, Zhang Xielin and Wang Zhiliang won China's first men's doubles championship at the World Table Tennis Championships. At the 2007 World Table Tennis Championships in Zagreb, Croatia, Ma Lin/Chen Qi and Wang Liqin/Wang Hao swept the men's doubles gold and silver medals, achieving China's eighth consecutive men's doubles championship since 1993.

The Biggest Winner in Olympic History

Since table tennis entered the Olympics in 1988, up to today, the Chinese men's table tennis team has won 8 out of the 11 gold medals produced so far, along with 4 silver medals and 3 bronze medals. At the Atlanta and Sydney Olympic Games, the Chinese team won all four gold medals.

At the 1988 Seoul Olympics, table tennis became an official Olympic event for the first time. Although Chinese table tennis was in a low ebb at the time, South Korea's Ryu Nam-Kweon took home the men's singles gold medal. However, Wei Qingguang and Chen Longcan bravely won the men's doubles championship.

At the 1992 Barcelona Olympics, Wang Tao and Lü Lin defeated the famous German pair of Rosskopf/Fetschelar in the final, retaining their men's doubles crown. At that time, Sweden's "table tennis evergreen" Jan-Ove Waldner was at his peak, defeating France's Guy Drout in the final to win the championship, while Ma Wenhe added a bronze medal for China.

At the 1996 Atlanta Olympics, Chinese table tennis completely emerged from its low point. Liu Guoliang, the current head coach of the Chinese men's table tennis team, and Wang Tao met in the final, finishing first and second respectively. Then Liu Guoliang partnered with Kong Linghui to defeat the defending champions Wang Tao/Lü Lin in the final, winning another gold medal. In Atlanta, the Chinese team historically achieved the remarkable feat of a single country sweeping all the table tennis gold medals.

In 2000 in Sydney, the Chinese table tennis team repeated the magic of Atlanta, winning all four gold medals again. Kong Linghui defeated Swedish veteran Jan-Ove Waldner 2-1 in the men's singles final, and his emotional kiss of the national flag on his chest after winning touched countless fans across China. Kong Linghui also achieved the grand slam of the World Table Tennis Championships, World Cup, and Olympics in individual singles. Another grand slam winner, Liu Guoliang, won the bronze medal in that edition. However, they failed to retain the men's doubles crown in the final, as the Wang Lichun/Yan Sen combination defeated them, winning the gold medal.

At the 2004 Athens Olympics, Ma Lin/Chen Qi defeated Li Jing/Gao Lizhe from Hong Kong, China, to win the gold medal, defending China's dominance in men's doubles at the Olympics and achieving five consecutive championships in men's doubles. However, in Athens, young Wang Hao could not withstand the immense psychological pressure and lost 2-4 to South Korean player Ryu Seung-min under fierce attacks, finishing as the runner-up. This was also his first loss to Ryu Seung-min.

After losing the men's singles championship in Athens, the Chinese men's team aimed to sweep both gold medals at home. Today, the Chinese men's table tennis team won the first men's team gold medal in Olympic history, and will continue to make the strongest push for the men's singles gold medal.

Today's victory in the men's team also gave Wang Liqin and Ma Lin their second personal Olympic gold medals, tying the highest number of individual gold medals in the history of the world men's table tennis. Whichever of the two wins the upcoming men's singles championship will surpass the two grand slam winners Liu Guoliang and Kong Linghui, becoming the top scorer in the history of men's table tennis at the Olympics.
