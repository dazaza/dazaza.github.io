4-Year-Old Child Falls from the 9th Floor and is Caught by an Electric Scooter, Suffering Only Minor Bruises

by aiipii12987 on 2012-02-27 09:33:17

Henan Business Daily reporter Donghua Yang / photo, illustration / Yifu Fang. The little guy was fine, with only minor abrasions on his face and body; the electric bike owner repeatedly called it lucky: "The bike was there as if to save this child."

This incident is quite alarming when you think about it. Shangbao reminds you again: do not leave children home alone, and preferably add protection to the window sill.

The child fell from the 9th floor, with only slight abrasions on his face and body. For his parents, it's hard to summarize with "luck" or "pitiful," while for all the witnesses, "this is almost a wonder."

Henan Business Daily reporter Xiaoshan Song, intern Liu Ying

Event

The first witness:

No one at home, 4-year-old child falls from the 9th floor

At around 10 am on February 19, Ms. Wang, a resident of No. 9 Courtyard of Jinshui Public Security Bureau in Zhengzhou City, had just returned from the clinic, walking to Building No. 2 when she heard a child crying.

Soon, in the parking lot under Unit 3 of the building, she found a child dressed in a blue cotton jacket, clothes hanging on the handlebars of an electric bike, the whole person dangling in front of the car. With the child's cries, his body was also constantly twisting, more frighteningly, there was a lot of blood on the child's face, and his eyes were swollen.

"Is someone harming the child?" Ms. Wang said this was her first reaction, so she immediately picked up the child from the bike and wrapped him in her down coat.

Holding him, Ms. Wang boldly asked where the child lived and who his parents were. Unexpectedly, the child was very conscious and answered everything. Following the direction pointed by the child, Ms. Wang looked up and saw that above the electric bike, a window on the 9th floor was wide open.

"Fell from the 9th floor?" Ms. Wang couldn't believe it, but the property management staff soon verified that the child indeed lived on the 9th floor. Worried about internal injuries, she immediately laid the child flat on the ground and called 110 and 120 for help. A few minutes later, the ambulance took the child away. Before getting into the ambulance, the child kept twisting his body and crying: "I don't want to go to the hospital, ah..."

Half an hour after returning home, Ms. Wang's hands were still trembling. The child fell from the 9th floor and was unharmed, "this is simply a miracle."

The second witness:

A life-saving vehicle, the electric bike supported him

There are such feelings not just for Ms. Wang, but also for Mr. Zhou, who works on the second floor of Unit 3 in Building No. 2, who feels like everything was destined.

Before the Spring Festival, he usually drove to work, but he parked a green electric bike downstairs, so he could ride it out for shorter distances during peak hours. However, in fact, the bike had never been moved. "It seemed like it was placed there just to save this child."

That day, after hearing a "thud", Mr. Zhou didn't pay much attention. Soon, the noise outside became louder, and when he went out, he saw more than ten people surrounding a woman and a child, with his own electric bike in the middle. The basket of the bike was on the ground, and the front wheel cover was shattered, with a screw about the size of a little finger squeezed out.

"This child is too lucky, falling from the 9th floor and having nothing happen." Someone said. It was then that Mr. Zhou realized that this unused electric bike saved a life without him knowing. He guessed that it might be the basket that blocked the child's body, and the clothes were caught on the handlebars, preventing him from directly falling to the ground.

At the scene, reporters saw three vehicles directly below the 9th-floor window, surrounded by small patches of open space, with Mr. Zhou's electric bike in the middle. If the child had fallen slightly to the left, he would have hit the frame of an overturned bicycle; if he had fallen slightly to the right, he would have landed on another bicycle, with some irregular protrusions on the basket; or if he had fallen slightly further, landing on the tiled ground nearby, the consequences would have been unimaginable.

Outcome:

The child only had facial abrasions and minor bruises on his body

Subsequently, reporters rushed to the Henan Provincial Traditional Chinese Medicine Hospital. Xiao Xiangxiang (a pseudonym), who fell from the 9th floor, was lying on the bed, crying: "My nose itches, Mommy scratch it..." There was a large bruise under his left eye, and a cut about three to four centimeters long on his nose.

According to Xiangxiang's family, on the day of the incident, both of Xiangxiang's parents were working their first day after the New Year, so he was being taken care of by his aunt. After waking up, his aunt wanted to make him a bowl of noodles, so she went downstairs to buy tomatoes. She didn't expect that leaving for just a moment resulted in the accident.

Fortunately, after a CT scan, no major problems were found in Xiangxiang's brain or internal organs. Currently discovered injuries were merely facial abrasions and soft tissue damage. Additionally, although the electric bike cushioned the fall, it still caused a slight bruising to the lungs. The only concern was that his left eye couldn't open.

Yesterday afternoon, reporters learned from his family that Xiangxiang's left eye had already opened, and the pupil reacted normally, so there was no major problem.

"The condition is stable, with no serious issues," said Dr. Hu, the attending physician.

Doubt:

Falling at a speed of 25 meters per second, the child only had abrasions and bruises

Why was he so lucky?

In everyday life, serious injuries or even death caused by falling from high buildings are not uncommon, but why did the 4-year-old Xiangxiang escape unscathed? Ren Zhanhong, a physics teacher at Zhengzhou No. 1 High School, gave us a physics-based answer according to our description.

Calculating based on each floor being 2.5 meters high, the child fell from the 9th floor, a height of 22.5 meters. Subtracting the 0.5-meter height of the electric bike, the child's free-fall distance was 22 meters. According to the free-fall motion theorem, S=1/2gt^2, v=gt (S is the distance, g=9.8, t is time, v is velocity), the final calculation shows that the child's speed upon hitting the electric bike was approximately 25 meters per second.

"At such a speed, the child falling from a great height without sustaining serious injury is truly a miracle," said Teacher Ren. During such a high-speed fall, the wind blew the child's clothes, forming a small parachute. When falling, the child first pressed against the basket of the electric bike, reducing the impact force, and the clothes also got caught on the handlebars, increasing the area of force distribution.

Meanwhile, according to the momentum theorem, a rough calculation was made. Normally, the child weighed 150N when falling onto the electric bike. If the weight had fallen slightly forward or backward, it might have caused the bike to flip over, crushing the child and causing more severe injuries.

All of this did not happen, possibly due to the child maintaining a head-up, feet-down position during the fall. Also, thanks to Mr. Zhou's positioning of the vehicle, this is not something pure physics can explain. If a reason must be given, then it's the two words "luck".

Reminder:

Do not leave children home alone

On December 1, 2010, the newly revised "Regulations on the Protection of Minors in Henan Province" was officially implemented, which mentioned that minors under the age of seven should not be left alone at home for long periods. Therefore, if you need to go out, it is best to entrust your child to someone else for care or take them with you.

After the incident of Xiangxiang falling from the building, his family expressed that they would install protective nets at home as soon as possible. It is understood that in most newly built communities, to maintain overall aesthetics, installing burglar-proof windows is prohibited. So how can we ensure safety without affecting aesthetics?

Reporters found online that invisible balcony protective anti-theft nets can meet both requirements. Each steel wire is spaced 5 cm apart, with a load-bearing capacity of over 100 kg. It is understood that the price of these anti-theft nets ranges from 140 to 180 yuan per square meter.
