Healthy Living Habits - Changing Unhealthy Lifestyle

In fact, habit is a tendency of human behavior. Habit is stable and even an automated behavior. A good health-preserving habit is a behavior pattern that suits oneself and is scientific. Many friends in life neglect good habits and often have some bad habits, which cause harm to physical health.

There are 8 misconceptions in living habits:

First, eating only when hungry. Many friends in life cannot eat on time, or do not eat until they feel hungry, with the reason being "not hungry." Some friends often eat after finishing their work, which is unscientific. Food stays in the stomach for only 4-5 hours, and by the time we feel hungry, the stomach has already emptied. At this point, the gastric mucosa will be "self-digested" by gastric juice, leading to gastritis or peptic ulcers, and can weaken the body's ability to fight disease.

Second, drinking only when thirsty. Some friends do not have the habit of drinking water regularly and only drink when they feel thirsty. In fact, when we feel thirsty, the body is already severely dehydrated. At this point, replenishing water is too late. Water is the source of life and more important than food in the metabolic process of the human body. Physiologists tell us that each adult needs about 1500-2000 milliliters of water daily. Drinking a glass of water after getting up in the morning or an hour before meals is very beneficial to the body. Water can not only clean the gastrointestinal tract but also help digestion and stimulate appetite. Surveys show that people who drink water regularly have significantly lower incidence rates of constipation and kidney stones compared to those who don't drink water regularly. Drinking water also plays a positive role in preventing diseases like myocardial infarction and stroke.

Third, resting only when tired. Resting only when tired is a tradition passed down from our ancestors, and many friends follow this practice throughout their lives. These friends believe that one should rest only when feeling tired. In reality, by the time one feels tired, the body is already quite fatigued. Excessive fatigue can easily lead to accumulated illnesses, reducing the body's immune capabilities and allowing diseases to take advantage. Whether it's mental or physical labor, after continuous work for a period of time, one should take appropriate breaks.

Fourth, sleeping only when drowsy. Some friends think they should only sleep when they feel drowsy. In fact, drowsiness is a sign of significant fatigue in the brain, and one should not wait until then to sleep. The good habit of going to bed on time can not only protect the brain but also make it easier to fall asleep, improving sleep quality and reducing the occurrence of insomnia. About one-third of a person's life is spent sleeping. Sleep is a physiological process of human metabolism. Ensuring regular bedtime and guaranteeing at least 7 hours of sleep daily can maintain the normal operation of the sleep biological clock.

Fifth, relieving oneself only when urgent. Many friends only go to the toilet when they feel a strong urge to urinate or defecate. Some even hold it in despite the urge. This is extremely detrimental to health. When waste stays in the body for a long time, it can easily lead to constipation or excessive bladder fullness. Toxic substances in feces and urine may be reabsorbed by the body, easily causing "self-poisoning." Therefore, forming the habit of relieving oneself on time can reduce the chances of developing hemorrhoids, constipation, and colon cancer.

Sixth, losing weight only when overweight. With the improvement in living standards and changes in lifestyle, there are more and more people with a tendency towards obesity. Many experts believe that the main causes of obesity are overeating, excessive nutrition, and lack of exercise. After becoming obese, many friends spend a lot of money and time on weight loss, which is very uneconomical. In fact, any current weight-loss method is quite limited. Because post-facto weight loss is not as effective as preventive measures beforehand, we can completely prevent it before our weight exceeds the standard. For example, controlling and adjusting diet, preventing binge eating, and strengthening physical exercise.

Seventh, treating illness only when sick. "Preparation ensures success; lack of preparation leads to failure," and health medicine also regards prevention as the first line of defense. By the time a disease has taken hold, it has already caused harm to the body, and treatment at this point may involve significant costs. In fact, diseases always give signals before they form, and what we often refer to as sub-health state is such a signal. Once one enters a sub-health state, attention should be paid. Moreover, many diseases are difficult to detect early based solely on self-perception. Only through regular health check-ups at the hospital can early detection and treatment occur. Quite a number of friends consider themselves healthy until they visit the hospital and discover issues.
