Kunming Vehicle Owner Upgrades Truck into RV

Yesterday morning, a Weibo user posted that in recent days, a famous local forum in Kunming had a very cool post. The post claimed that a certain car owner in Kunming, in response to high housing prices, firmly refused to become a mortgage slave, and independently started transforming a van into a warm and fully-equipped RV. This Weibo user also posted a link with pictures and text showcasing the interior decoration photos of the RV transformed from a truck.

"Though small, it has everything," from the pictures posted by netizens, it is not difficult to find that the configuration of this modified RV is perfect, with all kinds of living supplies. Inside the vehicle, furniture, bunk beds, kitchen, and bathroom are all available, along with an LCD display. Inside the vehicle, you can cook, shower, sleep, watch TV, and surf the internet.

Subsequently, the reporter found the original post on this forum and discovered that the poster referred to themselves as a true 80s generation RV enthusiast who has always liked RVs since childhood and has always wanted to own one. To achieve this dream, they spent four months modifying a small truck into an RV with a usable area of 8.5 square meters. After completing the modification, they felt the RV was very practical and shared it online with other RV enthusiasts.

Regarding the decoration and configuration inside the vehicle, many RV enthusiasts asked about the cost of the modified vehicle. The poster responded: "This vehicle costs less than 130,000 yuan, suitable for 80s generation working outside who cannot afford to buy a house. They can travel when they have time, and during the Spring Festival, they don't have to squeeze onto trains; they can drive home for the New Year, which is very convenient."

"130,000 yuan is not enough to buy a house in Zijun Village in Kunming; this is undoubtedly a good way to solve the problem of high housing prices." Some netizens commented after seeing the pictures. Many netizens also praised the technical skills and abilities of the car owner, praising this RV as "much cheaper than buying an expensive house," and considering it "true economical and practical housing."

Experience

The RV, "Though small, it has everything"

Is there really such an amazing RV in Kunming? Yesterday morning, based on the information provided by netizens, the reporter met this vehicle on the side of the second ring road, where the car owner, Mr. Feng, was surfing the internet. If it weren't for the small satellite dish revealing the secret, it would be hard to notice that this was a modified RV.

After being modified, the space inside the vehicle is somewhat narrow. However, the reporter experienced that not only can the TV be watched inside the vehicle, but by inserting a 3G network card, fast internet access is possible, and all daily necessities are available.

The modifications start from the cargo compartment. The car owner hung a gas water heater at the right front position adjacent to the car hood. Next to the water heater on the right side of the cargo compartment, there is a small window with an exhaust fan installed. Entering the cargo compartment reveals that the entire floor is made of solid wood flooring and bamboo products. Opposite the cargo compartment door is a tall shoe rack, which not only holds shoes but also several potted plants.

To the right of the shoe rack is the kitchen, equipped with a sink and cabinets, where transformers and inverters are stored. Next to the kitchen is a shower room where you can take a shower and use the toilet. In this space of less than 1 square meter, there is also a small washing machine, and the water source comes from a 400-liter large water tank under the cargo compartment base.

To the left of the shoe rack is the place for internet and dining. Behind the shoe rack is a large wardrobe occupying most of the cargo compartment space. Next to the wardrobe is a storage cabinet containing the computer host and power socket. Below the cabinet hangs a 38-inch LCD display, which can receive TV signals via an antenna. Diagonally opposite the TV is a 118-liter refrigerator. Near the tail of the cargo compartment is a bunk bed made of solid wood with sliding glass edges. The owner said this is the bedroom's floor-to-ceiling window. On the wall beside the bed, there is also a foldable and compressible small table for eating and office use.

If the interior modifications are for convenience, then the exterior modifications ensure the operation of all household appliances inside. To prevent rust, the car owner wrapped a layer of oilcloth around the outside of the cargo compartment. On the roof, the car owner placed a solar panel, ensuring that the electricity needs inside the vehicle are met as long as there is sunlight. Additionally, there is a red generator at the rear end of the cargo compartment capable of self-generating electricity.

"There are four power generation facilities on the vehicle, in addition to solar panels and generators, there is also a set of batteries providing power," Mr. Feng said. If the parking location has an external power source, you can directly plug in the external power source to provide electricity for the RV and charge the battery simultaneously, which is very convenient.

Owner

Modifying the vehicle is just a personal preference

"I have always wanted to own my own RV, and there are many people back home who modify vehicles," said Mr. Feng yesterday afternoon. This truck version RV took him more than 4 months to handcraft entirely.

Mr. Feng is originally from Heilongjiang and came to Yunnan at the age of 18. Later, he got married and settled down in Kunming, buying a house near the second ring road. Now, he has a 4-year-old son attending kindergarten. Mr. Feng said he only attended technical school but has always enjoyed handmade crafts. He once modified his bicycle and motorcycle according to his own ideas. In Kunming, Mr. Feng also has a stable job. Because he wasn't busy recently, in February this year, he bought this small truck and started modifying it.

"Originally planned to spend 140,000 yuan, but actually only spent less than 130,000 yuan, compared to today's housing prices, it is indeed very cost-effective. With this amount of money, you can't even buy a kitchen within the second ring road in Kunming now," Mr. Feng said. The new car plus registration only cost more than 80,000 yuan, and the rest was material costs, with the most expensive being electrical appliances and generators. There was no much design involved in the modification; he referenced some methods introduced on online forums and his own ideas before starting construction. The floor and household appliances were purchased in Kunming, while the inverter for conversion and stainless steel cabinets were bought locally.

After the vehicle was modified, to experience the functions of the vehicle, Mr. Feng drove with his wife and children for a trip to western Yunnan. "In Dali Harbor, many tourists who were originally going to take a cruise saw our car and didn't go anymore, they were busy taking pictures with us," Mr. Feng said. At the time, he and his wife were making breakfast on the car, which caught the attention of tourists who requested to visit the inside of the car. And in Kunming, if he suddenly drove the car to an unfamiliar neighborhood, it would also attract onlookers. In the past month, Mr. Feng and his family of three have almost lived and eaten on the car, only returning home three or four times.

Regarding his beloved car, Mr. Feng said that within the national scope, it is not yet the most amazing. He once saw on the Internet a modified RV of the same model from another province, which could expand when parked, reaching 17.5 square meters, doubling the space of his vehicle.

"I am not a mortgage slave, modifying this vehicle is purely out of interest." Regarding the comments from netizens, Mr. Feng said he doesn't quite understand them. His internet skills are not very good, and the pictures online were posted by his elder brother far away in his hometown. Originally, he just wanted to exchange experiences with netizens after the modification, but unexpectedly it attracted media interviews.

"First, I will experience it for a period of time, if later there are netizens who need it, I want to sell it and modify another one myself." Mr. Feng said that the process of modifying this vehicle is not complicated, and anyone with basic professional knowledge can handle it. If the policy allows, he wants to make modifying vehicles a career. (Reported by Li Jiajian, Gao E, Zhang Meng)
