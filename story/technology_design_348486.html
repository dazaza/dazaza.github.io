Google's first quarter net profit was 1.96 billion US dollars, increased by 38% year-on-year

by tfseo8888 on 2010-04-19 20:09:12

Sina Technology News, in the afternoon of April 15th (Eastern Time), or early morning of April 16th (Beijing Time), Google released its first quarter financial report for fiscal year 2010 ending March 31. The report showed that Google's revenue for the first quarter was $6.77 billion, an increase of 23% compared to the same period last year; according to U.S. Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP), Google's net profit for the first quarter was $1.96 billion, compared to $1.42 billion during the same period last year, representing a growth of 38%.

Key performance indicators:

- Google's revenue for the first quarter was $6.77 billion, an increase of 23% year-over-year. Google calculates total revenue under GAAP without deducting traffic acquisition costs (TAC). Google's TAC for the first quarter was $1.71 billion, accounting for 26% of advertising revenue.

- Under GAAP, Google's operating profit for the first quarter was $2.49 billion, accounting for 37% of revenue. Under GAAP, Google's operating profit for the same period last year was $1.88 billion, accounting for 34% of revenue. Excluding GAAP, Google's operating profit for the first quarter was $2.78 billion, accounting for 41% of revenue. Excluding GAAP, Google's operating profit for the same period last year was $2.16 billion, accounting for 39% of revenue.

- Under GAAP, Google's net profit for the first quarter was $1.96 billion, compared to $1.42 billion for the same period last year. Excluding GAAP, Google's net profit for the first quarter was $2.18 billion, compared to $1.64 billion for the same period last year.

- Under GAAP, Google's earnings per share (EPS) for the first quarter was $6.06, compared to $4.49 for the same period last year. Excluding GAAP, Google's EPS for the first quarter was $6.76, compared to $5.16 for the same period last year.

- Excluding GAAP, Google's operating profit, net profit, and EPS do not include stock-based compensation expenses. Google's stock-based compensation expense for the first quarter was $291 million, compared to $277 million for the same period last year. Excluding GAAP, Google's operating profit, net profit, and EPS do not include tax benefits related to stock-based compensation. Google's tax benefits related to stock-based compensation for the first quarter was $65 million, compared to $64 million for the same period last year.

Financial Analysis:

Google's revenue for the first quarter was $6.67 billion, an increase of 23% from $5.51 billion for the same period last year. Google calculates total revenue under GAAP without deducting traffic acquisition costs.

Revenue from Google's own websites for the first quarter was $4.44 billion, accounting for 66% of total revenue, an increase of 20% from $3.69 billion for the same period last year.

Network revenue, which is the revenue generated by Google's partner websites through the AdSense program, was $2.04 billion for the first quarter, accounting for 30% of total revenue, an increase of 24% from $1.64 billion for the same period last year.

International revenue, which is the revenue generated outside the United States, was $3.58 billion for the first quarter, accounting for 53% of total revenue, consistent with the same period last year. If exchange rates were the same as the previous quarter (excluding gains related to foreign exchange risk management programs), Google's revenue would have increased by $112 million; if exchange rates were the same as the same period last year (excluding gains related to foreign exchange risk management programs), Google's revenue would have decreased by $242 million.

Revenue from the UK for the first quarter was $842 million, accounting for 13% of total revenue, consistent with the same period last year.

Revenue benefited from the foreign exchange risk management program was $10 million for the first quarter, compared to $154 million for the same period last year.

Total paid clicks (including Google sites and AdSense partner sites) increased by approximately 15% year-over-year and by approximately 5% quarter-over-quarter for the first quarter.

Traffic acquisition cost (TAC), which is the revenue shared with partners, was $1.71 billion for the first quarter, compared to $1.44 billion for the same period last year. TAC as a percentage of advertising revenue was approximately 26%, down from 27% for the same period last year.

Of Google's TAC expenses for the first quarter, $1.45 billion will eventually be paid to AdSense partners, and $265 million will eventually be paid to specific distribution partners.

Other revenue costs for the first quarter were $741 million, accounting for 11% of revenue. Other revenue costs for the same period last year were $666 million, accounting for 12% of revenue. Other revenue costs mainly include data center operation expenses, credit card processing expenses, and content acquisition costs.

Operating expenses for the first quarter were $1.84 billion, accounting for 27% of revenue. Operating expenses for the same period last year were $1.52 billion, accounting for 28% of revenue. Operating expenses for the first quarter included $774 million in employee-related and equipment expenses, while operating expenses for the previous quarter included $890 million in employee-related and equipment expenses.

Stock-based compensation expenses for the first quarter were $291 million, compared to $277 million for the previous quarter.

Google expects the expenses associated with options granted to employees before April 1, 2010, to be approximately $1.2 billion for 2008, excluding expenses related to employee stock awards granted after March 31 and non-employee stock awards that have been or may be granted.

Under GAAP, Google's operating profit for the first quarter was $2.49 billion, accounting for 37% of revenue. Under GAAP, Google's operating profit for the same period last year was $1.88 billion, accounting for 34% of revenue. Excluding GAAP, Google's operating profit for the first quarter was $2.78 billion, accounting for 41% of revenue. Excluding GAAP, Google's operating profit for the previous quarter was $2.16 billion, accounting for 39% of revenue.

Interest income and other income for the first quarter were $18 million, compared to $6 million for the same period last year.

Under GAAP, Google's net profit for the first quarter was $1.96 billion, compared to $1.42 billion for the same period last year. Excluding GAAP, Google's net profit for the first quarter was $2.18 billion, compared to $1.64 billion for the same period last year. Under GAAP, Google's EPS for the first quarter was $6.06, compared to $4.49 for the same period last year. Excluding GAAP, Google's EPS for the first quarter was $6.76, compared to $5.16 for the same period last year.

Google's effective tax rate for the first quarter was 22%.

Net cash provided by operating activities for the first quarter was $2.58 billion, compared to $2.25 billion for the same period last year; capital expenditures for the first quarter were $239 million, mainly consisting of IT infrastructure investments, including data centers, servers, and network equipment; free cash flow for the first quarter was $2.35 billion.

Google expects to continue significant capital expenditure activities in the future.

As of March 31, 2010, Google held a total of $26.5 billion in cash, cash equivalents, and marketable securities.

As of March 31, 2010, Google employed 20,621 full-time employees worldwide, compared to 19,835 as of December 31, 2009.
