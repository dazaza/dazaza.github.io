"Queen Seondeok" to be aired in Japan, expected to lead the Asian "Korean Wave" again

"Queen Seondeok"

Beijing Time, September 4th afternoon report, according to South Korean media reports, MBC's Monday-Tuesday drama "Queen Seondeok" will be aired in Japan, becoming another "Korean Wave" drama sweeping Asia after "Dae Jang Geum".

Starting from October, "Queen Seondeok" will be broadcast in Japan. On September 4th, an MBC official revealed that Fuji TV in Japan would start broadcasting "Queen Seondeok" at 9 PM on October 29th, with one episode per week through a satellite channel. Before the official broadcast, Fuji TV specially went to Korea last month to produce a special episode of "Queen Seondeok", interviewing actors and doing on-site sketches.

After the completion of the drama shooting, the Japanese promotional activities of the actors are still ongoing. Following "Dae Jang Geum", "Queen Seondeok" is expected to become another Korean Wave drama.

Beijing Time, September 2nd morning report, according to South Korean media reports, MBC TV station's 48th anniversary drama "Queen Seondeok" has broken the 40% viewership mark for five consecutive times, becoming a true national drama in South Korea.

According to the statistics released by the South Korean viewership survey company TNS Media on the morning of September 2nd, the viewership rate of the "Queen Seondeok" aired on the evening of September 1st reached 42.1%, maintaining over 40% viewership for five consecutive broadcasts since it hit 42% on August 25th.

The "Folk Tales of Korea" aired at the same time as "Queen Seondeok" had a viewership rate of 6% on the evening of September 1st, slightly up from the previous week's 5.3%. The viewership rate of SBS TV station's "Drean" also saw a slight increase compared to the previous week but remained at 4.6%, failing to break the 5% mark.

As the currently hottest drama in South Korea, "Queen Seondeok" has been loved by South Korean audiences. MBC TV station is currently negotiating with the production company of the drama, and there is a possibility of extending the airing of "Queen Seondeok" by 12 episodes based on the original plan.

"Queen Seondeok" wins for five consecutive weeks

"Queen Seondeok": A brilliant battle of wits

Korean dramas have always excelled in romantic love stories, even in some so-called serious historical dramas, love is often a key plotline. From this perspective, "Queen Seondeok" is completely refreshing - the absolute trust between the male and female protagonists replaces lingering love, and the story of the queen's growth and struggle is captivatingly portrayed. This week, Queen Seondeok Deman faced her opponent Misil for the first time. Facing the highly intelligent Misil, Deman's strategy proved superior in her debut. She cleverly used Misil's confidence and strengths, causing her to outsmart herself and fall into the trap unwittingly. The thrilling battle of wits within the drama captivates the audience, while the rising viewership outside is exhilarating. The originally planned 50-episode "Queen Seondeok" is now almost certain to be extended by 12 episodes, airing until the end of 2009.

"Please Excuse Me" relies on Yoon Eun-hye to boost ratings

"Please Excuse Me": Increasing attention and entering the best phase

With nearly a year of hype before its premiere and the star power of Yoon Eun-hye, "Please Excuse Me" has maintained high attention. Although the premiere achieved a successful start the previous week, the lackluster story raised concerns about its future prospects. The turning point occurred in the fourth episode this week, as the bland storyline became intriguing, and the characters gradually became more three-dimensional. The male lead, Yoon Sang-hyun, who was somewhat criticized for his age, impressed the audience with his realistic acting. However, "Please Excuse Me" still cannot fully reassure drama fans due to the scriptwriting duo responsible for this drama. Despite their significant reputation, their skills have been frequently questioned. Their recent work, "Whenever Night Falls," is a drama with occasional brilliant details but deviates from the intended planning overall. This week, "Please Excuse Me" remains the viewing champion on Wednesdays and Thursdays. As long as the storyline doesn't disappoint, it is expected to maintain its dominant position.

"Style" turns controversial

"Style": Homosexuality appears, shocking the audience

The ending of the tenth episode of "Style," which emphasizes form over content, delivered a heavy shock to the audience. The second male lead (played by Lee Yong-woo) suddenly affectionately leaned towards the first male lead (played by Ryu Si-won), who had been perceived by the audience as his rival, without much warning, leaving the audience stunned. Homosexual content in Korean dramas is not unprecedented, and there were slight hints in the character introductions and earlier plots of "Style." However, unlike "Coffee Prince" or "Wind Painter," which touched upon the topic by playing around the edges with "female disguised as male," this bold attempt without any pretense shocked the audience, inevitably sparking controversy. The plot of "Style," which focuses on fashion, is not as engaging as initially anticipated. To stimulate the audience's interest, the shocking ending should not be viewed merely as a gimmick to boost ratings but rather as an attempt in terms of subject matter. Korean dramas have not stopped experimenting with various contents in recent years, and the results remain to be seen whether they are good or bad.

"Love You Ten Thousand Times" has a unique theme

"Love You Ten Thousand Times": Smooth premiere

Recently lagging behind in the ratings war, SBS TV has aired dramas this week that are more "shocking" than ever. While "Style" aimed at "homosexuality," the newly premiered "Love You Ten Thousand Times" boldly took "surrogate motherhood" as its selling point, almost competing to see who can stir up more topics. "Love You Ten Thousand Times" tells the story of a girl who unknowingly falls in love with her child's uncle after accepting a proposal for surrogate motherhood, leading to various entanglements. It stars Lee Soo-kyung, Ryu Jin, and others. Such a melodramatic content naturally easily arouses the audience's desire to watch, allowing "Love You Ten Thousand Times" to premiere smoothly.

"Friends, Our Legend": A well-crafted masterpiece

Directed by renowned film director Kwak Kyung-taek and starring Hyun Bin, Kim Min-jun, and others, the TV version of "Friends, Our Legend" concluded this week. Despite poor ratings, it remains a fine piece of work. Whether in terms of the completeness of the story, the cinematic visuals, or the actors' outstanding performances, it is undoubtedly well-crafted. The ending of "Friends, Our Legend" differs slightly from the movie version, adding content about Kim Min-jun avenging Hyun Bin, making the story clearer. The poor ratings of this drama are related to the broadcasting time slot and the heavy theme, and are also not unrelated to the dialects used throughout the series.
