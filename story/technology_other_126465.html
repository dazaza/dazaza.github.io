How to Spot a Fraudster in Business

by xlight on 2009-02-25 14:25:29

In today's society, as a businessman, apart from maintaining personal integrity, one has no power to completely change the untrustworthy business environment. Therefore, one needs to enhance their ability to prevent being deceived. As Mao Zedong said, always ask why about everything, constantly summarize lessons learned, and you may uncover some swindlers' true faces.

1. Do not engage in businesses that can easily make a fortune.

As the saying goes, money is hard to earn, just like eating feces is hard. There are indeed very few businesses that can make big money effortlessly. Often, there are great risks or even traps behind them. The stock market is the same; those who lose all their capital are often those chasing short-term profits by buying high and selling low.

2. Do not trust businesses that sound too good to be true.

There are no perfect things in the world, no wars without defeat, and no businesses without loss. You must not believe anyone who says a business has no risk at all. As the saying goes, a person with no flaws is not worth befriending. Anyone who seems to have no flaws should not be trusted as a friend. He is either a schemer or a smooth talker. Most businesses that seem to have no risks are often fraudulent.

3. Avoid overly enthusiastic businesspeople.

Business is like diplomacy. As the saying goes, there are no real friends in diplomacy, only interests and advantages. People's various performances in society are all for the pursuit of profit. Moderate enthusiasm is a necessary courtesy in business interactions, but excessive enthusiasm usually implies an excessive expectation or purpose. When encountering such individuals or situations, remind yourself why this is happening.

4. Do not be greedy for extra sweetheads or small benefits.

In the business field, if someone deliberately gives you small benefits or a little sweetness at the beginning, it is often to lure you into bigger deals later on. Sacrificing small profits for larger ones can lead to losses. As the saying goes, "If you cannot bear small annoyances, your grand plans will be disrupted." Those who see small profits and get excited will not achieve great success in business and may fall into a scammer’s trap. If you do not promptly summarize lessons learned, you will still be deceived when encountering similar situations.

5. Do not let go of the eagle until you see the rabbit.

Generally speaking, listen to what they say and observe their actions. Trust others while also questioning them. For relationships that haven’t been developed over a long period or units that haven't been examined, insist on delivering goods after receiving payment, clearing both goods and money, and not extending credit even if it means losing potential sales. Especially beware of those who boast loudly and recklessly, as they require extra caution.

6. Overturn the tree, scoop out the crow - stay calm and composed.

There is a general sense of impatience in today's business world. Everyone is rushing to "seize opportunities". In reality, genuine business opportunities cannot be broken up easily, and impossible businesses will scatter upon involvement. Meanwhile, frequently engaging in business affairs requires adhering to the principle of "a gentleman loves wealth but acquires it in a righteous way." Also, remember the saying, "Do not have the heart to harm others, but do not lack the heart to guard against them." Because even very reliable people may resort to deception when they reach their end.
