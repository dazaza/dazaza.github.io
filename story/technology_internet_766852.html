User Experience Showdown: iOS 7 Outshines Android/WP8

by anonymous on 2013-10-01 22:17:20

Technical advisory firm Pfeiffer conducted a mobile operating system user experience study, designed to collect and compare quantifiable user experiences between Apple iOS 6, the newly redesigned iOS 7, Microsoft’s Windows Phone 8, the Android system used in Samsung smartphones, and BlackBerry 10.

According to Pfeiffer, the testing methodology attempted to eliminate as many subjective factors as possible, such as brand loyalty and personal feelings. The tests focused solely on comparing experiences that affect the everyday lives of ordinary non-technical users. Pfeiffer identified four key points: cognitive load of the operating systems, efficiency and integration, customization options, and user experience friction.

Cognitive load is a psychological concept representing the multidimensional structure of the load placed on a learner's cognitive system when performing specific tasks. For example, dialing a phone number requiring 11 keyboard clicks indicates a cognitive load of 11. To calculate the cognitive load for each operating system, Pfeiffer researchers counted the user interaction elements of pre-installed applications and the base system after setup. The chart below shows the results of the cognitive load evaluation; shorter bars indicate better performance, with Apple iOS 6 ranking first, followed by iOS 7. Due to the addition of the Control Center interface in iOS 7, the cognitive load on users has increased. In this evaluation, the Samsung Android system was far behind. Additionally, Microsoft and BlackBerry's mobile operating systems also lagged behind iOS 7. Android has consistently been criticized for the complexity of its user interface.

The second test evaluated efficiency and integration, where Pfeiffer analyzed access to key settings, notification integration, multitasking, and camera functions. In this test, iOS 7 and the Android system tied for first place. Researchers believed that the app-switching interface in iOS 7 was excellent, while the customizable notifications and shortcuts in the Android system were also very mature, offering many choices.

The Samsung Android system ranked first in the customization options evaluation, with iOS 7 slightly behind but still stronger than Microsoft Windows Phone 8 and BlackBerry 10. A problem with the Microsoft system is the inability to change background images or manage application folders.

Finally, there was the user experience friction evaluation, where the iOS system emerged as the biggest winner. Pfeiffer stated, "Experience friction occurs when the device fails to perform what you want it to do, or lacks certain expected features." The Control Center remains the primary reason why iOS 7 lags behind iOS 6. The survey found that although this feature is convenient and easy to use, it can sometimes be accidentally activated, causing frustration.

In summary, the survey found that iOS 7 is more humanized compared to other mobile operating systems, and Apple has indeed brought iOS into the modern smartphone era. Although many users are not fond of the design of iOS 7, it indeed provides a better and smoother experience compared to other systems.
