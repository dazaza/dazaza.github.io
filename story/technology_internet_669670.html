Nowadays, the normal wedding banquets of newlyweds are held in restaurants or hotels. As for which place to hold the wedding, the following points can be considered: 1. The preferred wedding form by both parties' parents. If both parties' parents say that either is fine, the newlyweds should inform their parents before making a decision. Only after they really have no objections should you make the final decision. 2. Economic ability. Before deciding where to set up the banquet, economic ability must be taken into account. Because the red envelopes given by relatives and friends attending the wedding banquet generally follow a market rate. Regardless of where your wedding banquet is held, the amount of red envelopes received will not differ much. Therefore, if your economic conditions allow, you could choose a more upscale hotel to hold the wedding. Weddings should also be within one's means. 3. Weather factors. Before choosing, prospective newlyweds should also consider the weather conditions on the wedding day. If it is a scorching summer day and you choose an outdoor wedding, the bride's makeup may unintentionally turn into a "cat face" and guests may sweat profusely and harbor resentment! After understanding the above information, the next step is to evaluate the various hardware conditions of the banquet venue. Generally speaking, there are five points to pay special attention to when conducting inspections: 1. The lobby - first estimate how many tables need to be booked, then check if the chosen place meets your requirements, if there is enough space to accommodate the required number of tables, and if there is space for 2-3 extra tables in case more tables are needed. Most importantly, when signing the agreement, the name of the hall and the required number of tables should be clearly noted, and the breach of contract compensation responsibility should be strictly agreed upon. 2. Beverages - higher-end wedding venues usually provide alcohol, soda, or juice. However, regarding alcohol, it might be limited per table. In cases where you bring your own beverages, attention should be paid to the quality of the drinks, whether leftover wine or spirits can be refunded. Moreover, if there are promotional gifts for certain spirits, this should be clearly understood during reservation. 3. Venue arrangement - whether the wedding banquet venue can meet all your wedding requirements, including entertainment programs. Ensure that the venue you choose has the necessary facilities. 4. Location - try to find a place that is convenient for parking or accessible by public transportation. Otherwise, your guests may end up receiving a parking ticket while coming to celebrate your wedding, which would be undesirable! 5. Cost-effectiveness - even among hotels or restaurants of the same level, the quotations for wedding banquets can vary greatly. Before making a final decision, it's best to compare prices and the quality of dishes. For example, whether the "roast pig" is whole or a platter, whether the "steamed grouper" is tiger grouper or regular green grouper, these differences can result in significant price variations.
