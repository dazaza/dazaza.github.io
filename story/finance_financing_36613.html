Three major engines may create a red Monday

by jisen888 on 2007-11-26 09:43:03

Three engines may create a red Monday

Adjustment pressure has been fully released

Regarding the wide fluctuations in the A-share market last week, industry insiders believed that the core factors were two-fold: first, the internationalization trend of the A-share market made it increasingly affected by the fluctuations of global capital markets. Unfortunately, the surrounding markets have been continuously weak recently, which has curbed the enthusiasm of the bulls to go long. Second, high valuations have made the A-share market unstable at high levels; thus, any slight wind of information similar to policy regulation expectations or instability in surrounding markets causes the bull funds to overreact, leading to adjustment trends.

However, these two major pieces of information improved significantly on last Friday. The surrounding markets have quietly stabilized, with the Hang Seng Index in Hong Kong rebounding by 536.17 points, a rise of 2.06%. After continuous adjustments, the dynamic P/E ratio of the A-share market for 2007 quickly dropped to around 35 times. Considering the earnings growth for 2008 and the profit growth exceeding expectations brought about by tax reforms, the market's valuation has gradually become reasonable. The adjustment pressure on the index has gradually been released, and the market will face an upward opportunity brought about by the gathering of bullish momentum.

Three pieces of information drive the gathering of bullish momentum

From the trading floor perspective, the gathering of bullish momentum in the market is mainly reflected in three optimistic pieces of information: first, the RMB exchange rate broke through the important psychological barrier of 7.40, hitting a new high since the currency reform at 7.3992. The appreciation of the RMB is one of the foundations of this round of super bull market. There is a view that as long as the RMB continues to appreciate, the bull market will not end. Therefore, the exchange rate setting a new high since the currency reform will boost the confidence of the bulls to hold shares and their enthusiasm to go long.

Second, there are signs that PetroChina (stock code, stock bar) has stopped falling and stabilized. Although from the daily K-line chart of PetroChina, it is still in a downward channel, but the 5-day moving average shows clear signs of new buying interest appearing around 34 yuan. Especially with the continuous high enthusiasm of fund marketing, it indicates that subsequent funds are relatively abundant. As PetroChina holds a strategic advantageous position in the future index futures, it naturally will attract active allocation from institutional funds. Therefore, PetroChina is likely to build a short-term bottom around 34 yuan, pulling the index into a rebound rally.

Third, new bullish varieties have appeared in the market. Recent positive changes in industry fundamentals are driving the formation of new hot spots in the A-share market. For example, the positive changes in aerospace military industry policies have made the aerospace military sector repeatedly strong recently. Also, the continuous rise in the price of long steel products in steel stocks has also made steel stocks such as Sansteel Minlight, Laiwu Steel, and Bayi Steel repeatedly strong, becoming another engine force for the index rebound. In addition, the small and medium-sized board benefits from the expectation of high bonus shares in the annual report, which has repeatedly risen in recent days, with trading volumes repeatedly expanding, indicating that funds took advantage of the main board adjustment last week to find alternative routes and intervene in the small and medium-sized board, promoting the gathering of bullish momentum.

A red Monday can be expected

For this reason, I believe that under the impetus of the stabilization of surrounding markets, the new high of the RMB exchange rate, and the positive information from the trading floor, the index will welcome a rebound rally this week. On Monday, it is more likely to see a red Monday trend driven by the stabilization of PetroChina and the push of many strong stocks such as aerospace military stocks.

The question now is whether the red Monday trend can bring about a red week trend characteristic. I believe the key lies in the coordination of two aspects of information: first, trading volume. The increase in trading volume at the close of last Friday laid the groundwork for the appearance of a red Monday. Similarly, whether a red Monday can appear hinges on whether trading volume can increase on Monday of this week. The Shanghai market must see at least 7 billion yuan in trading volume to confirm the start of a rebound rally; otherwise, it would merely be a rebound during a decline. Second, whether the heavyweight stocks can fully stabilize.

Therefore, in actual operations, we should track two sectors: first, the heavyweight stock sector, which is the key signal determining whether the market will experience a short-term rebound or a mid-term rebound rally; second, individual stocks with positive industry changes or obvious signs of increased trading volume on the K-line chart, which could be the preferred choice in a short-term rebound rally, such as steel stocks like Sansteel Minlight and Laiwu Steel, small and medium-sized board stocks like Hongda Textile and Beiwai Communication, and stocks with relatively good fundamentals and low prices like Baoshang Group, Sanhuan Shares, and Longjian Shares.
