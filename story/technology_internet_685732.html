Why should you invest in gold [http://blog.sina.com.cn/s/blog_9227cb0e0100udg0.html]? According to the National Bureau of Statistics, the Consumer Price Index (CPI), which is the most important indicator of inflation, increased by 5.9% throughout 2008. Generally speaking, when the CPI increase exceeds 5%, it indicates serious inflation.

Inflation is the archenemy of wealth, especially for those who have large bank deposits and bonds, as its eroding effect becomes more evident. Therefore, many people resort to investment to counteract inflationary pressures. However, most people only focus on the stock market, often ignoring the importance of "diversified investment," leading to heavy losses for many investors during the 1997 Asian financial crisis, the 2000 dot-com bubble burst, and the recent financial tsunami. The key to "diversified investment" is to allocate funds across various assets with low correlation between their returns, thereby reducing risks. In other words, "do not put all your eggs in one basket." Currently, the main asset categories for "diversified investment" include stocks, bonds, and money market instruments.

The king of currencies—gold—should be your best choice for strategic investment.

The value of investing in gold:

The investment value of gold mainly manifests in two aspects: long-term preservation of value and asset diversification.

(1) Long-term preservation of value

There are only over 150,000 tons of global gold reserves, increasing at a slow annual rate of 2%. However, the enthusiasm for gold investment has rapidly heated up, with demand surging by 51% alone in the second quarter of this year. Rarity makes things precious, and the tight supply-demand relationship in the gold market determines its function of long-term preservation of value against inflation. Over the past four centuries, compared to the significant devaluation of global currencies, the purchasing power of gold has only increased. The famous economist Keynes pointed out that gold, as the last line of defense and emergency reserve, cannot yet be replaced by anything else. This highlights gold's ability to effectively resist the effects of inflation-induced currency value changes and price increases.

(2) Asset diversification

International gold prices are primarily determined by the US dollar, oil prices, and international geopolitical factors, with the dollar having an influence exceeding 50%, moving inversely to gold prices. Under the US's persistent quantitative easing monetary policy and expectations of dollar depreciation, more professional investors are buying gold from an asset allocation perspective to hedge against the risk of dollar exchange rate declines. A major bull market in gold has already arrived, and after breaking its historical high before the financial tsunami, international gold prices continue to rise strongly. This is the perfect time for you to choose gold products as a stable investment to optimize your financial portfolio.
