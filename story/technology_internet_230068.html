Half-topic Speech Annotations:

Steps for preparing a half-topic speech:

1. Receive the scope defined by the "Premier League" competition committee.

2. Propose your own title based on the given scope.

3. Write the speech according to your chosen title and the time requirement of the competition.

4. After completing the speech, memorize it and practice delivering it without notes repeatedly.

5. Deliver the speech without notes on the day of the competition.

Principles for Half-topic Speeches:

1. Autonomy: Propose your own title and write the speech yourself; plagiarism is prohibited.

2. Depth: Choose a topic you are familiar with to enhance the depth of your speech content.

3. Innovation: Select a unique topic that will impress the judges.

Steps for Scenario Dialogues:

1. Carefully analyze the question: Based on the scenario title and description, combine it with reality to analyze the content of the question.

2. Preparation requirements: Each scenario involves a dialogue between two people. According to the preparation requirements, analyze the characteristics of both roles and prepare as much conversational content as possible from both perspectives.

3. Examination arrangement: On the exam day, candidates will draw a scenario and complete the dialogue with another candidate based on the role assigned by the examiner.

Principles for Scenario Dialogues:

1. Rich Content: Since there is no prior rehearsal time for both parties, candidates should prepare as much content related to the topic as possible.

2. Novel Content: Original and reasonable content can improve the impression in the examiner's mind.

3. Acting Ability: Besides language itself, acting ability can affect the score.

Round Two Exam Questions:

(For Junior High and Senior High School Students)

1. Half-topic Speech Title: Campus

Scenario Dialogue Title: Father-Son Network Talk

One Sunday afternoon, I was surfing the Internet when my father suddenly appeared beside me. Lately, going online has become the focal point of arguments between my father and me. It seems I won't be able to avoid this today...

Preparation Requirements: One side plays the father, the other side plays the child. Discuss the pros and cons of students using the Internet.

2. Half-topic Speech Title: Olympics

Scenario Dialogue Title: "Overseas Returnee" vs. "Local"

My sister, who just returned from overseas study with a master's degree, invited me and classmate Wang Fang to dinner. During the meal, she expressed feelings of being a stranger and feeling pressured by age despite her academic achievements and fluency in foreign languages in the competitive Beijing job market.

Preparation Requirements: One side plays me, the other side plays classmate Wang Fang. The focus is whether overseas study is better or domestic development is better.

3. Half-topic Speech Title: Pollution

Scenario Dialogue Title: To Show "Sympathy" or Be "Indifferent"

It was already 8:30 PM, and the sparse crowd in the subway was walking listlessly. My friend Li Hua and I were among them. Not long after boarding, a middle-aged couple dressed in tattered clothes, looking serious, began telling passengers about their misfortunes while extending their hands for alms. Without hesitation, I gave them some money, which unexpectedly sparked an argument between Li Hua and me.

Preparation Requirements: One side plays me, the other side plays Li Hua. Focus: Whether to show sympathy or indifference towards beggars.

4. Half-topic Speech Title: Hero

Scenario Dialogue Title: Ongoing Selection

Li Hua: Do you know "Super Girl"? Have you watched "I Am a Good Boy" and "Happy Boys"?

Wang Fang: No!

Li Hua: Wow!!!!!!!!!

Preparation Requirements: One side plays Li Hua, the other side plays Wang Fang. Focus: Whether students should pay too much attention to talent shows during their school years.

5. Half-topic Speech Title: Internet

Scenario Dialogue Title: "School Uniform" - Saying 'I Love You' Is Not Easy

Monday is my (school uniform) happiest day because we have the flag-raising ceremony, and all the students wear me, making the campus look very neat. However, as soon as the dismissal bell rings, some students hastily crumple me up and throw me aside. I always painfully think, if you don't like me, why did you bring me into this world?

Preparation Requirements: One side believes students should wear uniforms; the other side believes they shouldn't.

6. Half-topic Speech Title: Travel

Scenario Dialogue Title: True or False "Monkey King"

Li Hua: Hey, buddy! Want games, software, VCDs? Cheap! Come take a look. Everything's here!

Wang Ming: Really? Can they be used?

Li Hua: Don't worry! They're exactly the same as the original ones! If they can't be used, I'll exchange them for you.

Preparation Requirements: One side agrees with piracy; the other side opposes it.

7. Half-topic Speech Title: Transportation

Scenario Dialogue Title: Teacher, Are You Ready?

Li Hua: I heard our homeroom teacher is changing.

Wang Ming: Is that true? Great!!!

Li Hua: Sigh! If only we could choose ourselves.

Preparation Requirements: (Both sides debate what they expect from teachers) One side plays a student; the other side plays the principal.

8. Half-topic Speech Title: Ambition

Scenario Dialogue Title: To Go "Fast" or Eat "Fast Food"

"Fat Tiger" stood outside McDonald's dragging "Big Bear," hoping he would treat him to a meal. Looking at Fat Tiger's big belly, Big Bear started persuading Fat Tiger not to eat this kind of "junk food."

Preparation Requirements: One side plays Fat Tiger, the other side plays Big Bear. Focus: Pros and cons of fast food.

9. Half-topic Speech Title: Health

Scenario Dialogue Title: Girls, I'm Dizzy

Chinese teacher: I really feel old; I can't understand the essays students write anymore. I want to apply for retirement.

English teacher: Yes, I feel the same way! Let's retire together.

Student A: Recently, a girl and I said goodbye.

Student B: +U, let's meet.

Preparation Requirements: One side believes that network slang should not be overused in life and study, while the other side believes it can be used and considers it a trend.
