STMicroelectronics (ST) has exceeded the limit of LED camera flash.

by ledlamp8 on 2012-03-06 22:17:37

China, February 13, 2012 - The world’s leading semiconductor supplier STMicroelectronics across multiple electronic applications (STMicroelectronics, referred to as ST; NYSE: STM), has developed a compact digital camera solution that meets user requirements for improving the built-in flash output power. It also supports more advanced user controls and smartphone integration.

STMicroelectronics' new chip, the STCF04, is a dual-mode camera flash controller with built-in flash / flashlight functions. This controller can boost the maximum power of LED flash modules from a few watts today to over 40W, addressing brightness levels comparable to outdoor security floodlights.

The new product offers enhanced brightness control features for both flash and flashlight modes. In flash mode, it provides 8 user-selectable brightness levels, while in flashlight mode, it offers 12 user-selectable brightness levels, along with a light sensor input. The controller chip includes safety features such as a built-in timer for flash and torch modes, a temperature sensor, and short-circuit protection.

The STCF04, designed with STMicroelectronics’ unique architecture, integrates a super capacitor, high-current MOSFET switching discrete devices, and multi-branch high-power white LED arrays. This makes it an ideal choice for high-brightness and emergency flash solutions in mobile phone cameras. The key advantage of the STCF04 over similar products lies in its power MOSFET discrete device design, as other brands' controllers only integrate a single power MOSFET.

The new product is designed to replace traditional high-power xenon flashes with LED flashes, meeting the demands of today’s high-pixel camera sensors which require higher light intensity. Additionally, it offers the advantages of compact design and simplicity.

STMicroelectronics has already begun providing STCF04 samples to two major partners, Murata (MURATA) and Osram (OSRAM). Murata Manufacturing Co., a market leader in super capacitor manufacturing, and Osram, one of the world’s leading suppliers of smartphone flash modules, have started showcasing their latest products using this chip.

Key Features of STCF04:

- Maximum flash power > 40W

- Flashlight mode current (torch current): 1 x to 320mA

- 100mA red LED address privacy indicator light output

- 12 adjustable options via I2C bus

- Temperature sensing, short circuit shutdown, overvoltage protection, and flash/torch mode timer safety features

- STCF04 TFBGA package 3 x 3mm, 25-ball, available for sampling

Volume production is expected to begin in the first quarter of 2012.
