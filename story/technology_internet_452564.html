The development time of China's radio and television is not long. China's first radio station was built in 1923 by foreigners. The People's Radio of China was founded in December 1940, with its predecessor being the Yan'an Xinhua Radio Station. Beijing Television (on May 1, 1978, Beijing Television officially changed to CCTV) was China's first television station, which started trial broadcasts on May 1, 1958, marking the birth of China's television industry. Beijing was the only program broadcast four times a week, and Beijing only had a few dozen TV sets to receive the programs. However, with the rapid development of radio and television, by the end of 2002, there were 360 TV stations and 1300 radio stations. There were 2058 projects for TV sets, and the TV population coverage rate reached 94.54%. With the rapid development of radio and television, there is a demand for radio and host talents, thus attracting many students' active applications. With an increasing number of candidates, many colleges and universities have rushed to offer broadcasting and hosting programs. According to the statistics published on the Ministry of Education website, in 2001, the Ministry of Education approved seven universities to establish broadcasting and hosting programs, 11 in 2002, nine in 2003, two in 2004, 14 in 2005, 12 in 2006, 16 in 2007, 18 in 2008, and 12 in 2009. Only nine out of the 101 universities approved by the Ministry of Education have set up broadcasting and hosting programs, showing the remarkable expansion speed. The schools offering these programs are not only universities, but also art schools, technical colleges, and engineering colleges with a passion for the project. However, among these universities, very few develop human resources for social radio hosts, creating a strange and contradictory phenomenon. Major courses include voice broadcasting, creation foundation of literary and artistic works for broadcasting, radio hosts, television recording studio, broadcasting school studies, introduction to journalism, news editing, master thesis writing, and production of radio and television programs.
