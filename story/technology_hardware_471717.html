#### Additional Requirements and Explanations:

1. **Challenges and Breakthroughs: Potential Prospects for the Tourism Industry**

- From the 1980s to the present, despite the turbulent global economy, the world tourism industry has demonstrated increasing adaptability and gradually matured. Entering the 21st century, the global tourism industry continues to show robust development momentum, reflecting its vitality worldwide.

- However, tourism is a highly sensitive economic industry. Factors such as international political changes, economic fluctuations, natural disasters, epidemics, and unexpected events can significantly impact the growth of the global tourism industry. The financial crisis, stock market crashes, credit tightening, and investor confidence collapse have left many developed countries and emerging markets struggling, posing a risk of stagnation or even recession for the global economy. This global crisis has brought enormous challenges to the world tourism industry, affecting tourism revenue, corporate investment, and consumer spending.

- As part of the global economy, tourism is one of the most open, sensitive, and fluid sectors. Despite the strong impact of the economic crisis, modern tourism has shown remarkable resilience and an ability to recover quickly. We believe that, facing various challenges, the future of global tourism will continue to grow and develop while addressing crises.
