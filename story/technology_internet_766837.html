Up to 3200 yuan off iPhone trade-in: A complete guide

by anonymous on 2013-10-01 22:07:21

Even companies that were once glorious may start to decline when they reach a certain bottleneck, and may even fall drastically, such as Nokia, such as Motorola. Both were once the biggest winners in the mobile phone market, but now? They are both being acquired.

Of course, the main characters of this article are not these two companies, but rather Apple Inc., currently the company with the highest market value. Since the death of Steve Jobs, Apple under Tim Cook seems to be gradually losing the unique "character" it once had: less innovation, poor confidentiality, and product highlights seem to be getting worse with each generation.

A while ago, Apple launched the iPhone 5s/5c. These two products have almost no particular highlights, basically just hardware upgrades + changing the shell and material, which all belong to normal routine upgrades. No wonder after their launch, Apple's stock price also plummeted.

Perhaps, out of admiration for Steve Jobs, some Apple fans still bought the two products, but significantly fewer than before. It seems that Apple is unable to reverse its fate. Recently, Apple implemented a "trade-in" strategy in the U.S., trading old iPhones for the iPhone 5s/5c. Although it is unclear how the response has been, this seems to prove that Apple has fallen from its pedestal in the hearts of users.

The implementation of this strategy in the U.S. seems to have little value for the Chinese people, but four domestic companies have already started the "trade-old-for-new" activity, could this be considered a China-first? However, regardless, making users spend money to buy an iPhone 5s/5c might not sit well with some people, but if they can trade in their old iPhone for a new one, there will surely be many users rushing to grab the deal. This article aims to bring you a trade-in guide, hoping to provide correct guidance to our dear readers.

China Unicom Trade-In: Up to 3200 RMB credit

On September 20th, the iPhone 5s/5c went on sale in Beijing, and Beijing Unicom announced that it would simultaneously launch a trade-in event. The iPhone 5 can be credited up to 3200 RMB. This means that old users who intend to upgrade can trade in their old devices at a discounted price for a new iPhone.

According to reports, Beijing Unicom conducted an iPhone trade-in event in 20 designated service halls. The iPhone4/4s/5 can all be traded in for discounts towards the purchase of an iPhone 5s/5c, but only for contract phones. The iPhone 4 can be credited up to 1300 RMB, the iPhone 4s up to 2000 RMB, and the iPhone 5 up to 3200 RMB. When users sign a contract for the new iPhone 5s or 5c, they only need to pay the difference between the contract price and the old phone's value.

This event allows users holding mainland China versions of the iPhone4/4s/5 to participate. Currently, this activity is only available at designated Unicom service halls. Users wishing to learn more can call the Unicom customer service hotline at 10010 for inquiries.

China Telecom Trade-In: Popular models can all be credited

Although the U.S. has not implemented the "trade-in" activity for China, the two major domestic telecom operators, China Unicom and China Telecom, have taken the lead in launching this activity. Compared to China Unicom, China Telecom offers a wider variety of models for trade-in.

Beijing Telecom does not only offer trade-ins for the iPhone 5; the event also includes popular phones like the Samsung S4 and HTC ONE, all of which can be credited towards the purchase of a new phone. Among them, the Samsung S4 can be credited up to 3300 RMB for the iPhone5s/5c, the iPhone 5 up to 3150 RMB, the NOTE 2 up to 2900 RMB, the iPhone 4s up to 2550 RMB, and the HTC ONE up to 2400 RMB. Non-China Telecom users can also participate, and by signing a contract, they can enjoy the discount.

However, this activity is currently only available at own-operated service halls, and partner service halls do not yet conduct this event.

Yihaodian Trade-In: All iPhones can be credited

Compared to China Telecom's policy of only repurchasing iPhones, Yihaodian offers more brands for trade-ins. Currently, users can already see the trade-in entry point on the product pages of mobile phones.

Regarding Yihaodian's mobile phone trade-in, I did a simple test using a white iPhone 5 as an example. A 90% new machine can be credited up to about 3656 RMB, while a 64GB machine can be credited up to over 4000 RMB.

Currently, the repurchase amount from Yihaodian cannot be cashed out but can only be used as payment for purchasing a new phone. Perhaps the merchant will introduce a more flexible policy in the future.

Hongtu Sanbao Trade-In: Old tablets can also be credited

As one of the important channel partners of Apple in China, Hongtu Sanbao has also joined the "trade-in" event. However, unlike the aforementioned three companies, Hongtu Sanbao also accepts old tablet computers for credits.

This event is named "Old Want Change," and it will be implemented in designated Hongtu Sanbao stores in Nanjing, Yangzhou, Suzhou, Wuxi, Changshu, Nantong, Xuzhou, Yancheng, Shanghai, Zhoushan, Beijing, Hefei, Jinan, Fuzhou, etc. At that time, users can trade in their old phones and tablets for the iPhone 5s, iPhone 5c, Samsung Note 3, and other smartphones. On-site evaluation of the old device can credit up to 3000 RMB.

Summary:

The above are the four major domestic companies implementing the iPhone "trade-in" event, along with related trade-in guides. Here we remind everyone to remember to bring all original packaging, accessories, manuals, invoices, and other miscellaneous documents before trading in, so as to get a better price. Even with the best events, merchants always have ways to deal with customers or lower prices, so bringing all the necessary documents is essential to prevent any potential losses. Otherwise, suffering silently may be the only option.
