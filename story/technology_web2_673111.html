On the eve of World Cancer Day, Seeking Medical Network

by lokes350 on 2012-02-23 13:53:24

International Online Report: According to the UN website, ahead of World Cancer Day on February 4th, the World Health Organization (WHO) pointed out that cancer is one of the main causes of death globally, currently claiming over 7 million lives each year, and this number is expected to rise rapidly. People should pay attention to preventing risk factors that may lead to cancer, such as smoking, drinking, and unhealthy diets.

According to WHO estimates, with the global population aging, the number of deaths from cancer is expected to continue rising, potentially exceeding 13.1 million by 2030.

On February 1st, Andreas Ullrich, an official from WHO's Department of Noncommunicable Diseases and Health Promotion, stated that the threat of cancer and related prevention measures have not yet received sufficient attention. World Cancer Day provides an opportunity to highlight this issue and corresponding solutions—well-established, evidence-based methods for prevention, early detection, and treatment.

WHO indicated that many cancer-related deaths can be avoided. By adopting a healthy lifestyle, reducing risk factors such as tobacco use, alcohol consumption, unhealthy diets, and lack of physical activity, as well as implementing vaccination programs against hepatitis B virus and human papillomavirus infections that cause cancer, more than 30% of cancers can be prevented. Some cancers can also be detected and cured at an early stage. Even in advanced stages of cancer, good palliative care can alleviate patients' suffering.

WHO data shows that tobacco use is the most significant carcinogenic risk factor, responsible for 22% of all cancer deaths globally and 71% of lung cancer deaths. In middle- and low-income countries, up to 20% of deaths are caused by carcinogenic infections such as hepatitis B and C viruses and human papillomavirus.
