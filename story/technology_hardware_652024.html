At the briefing, regarding the purchase of school buses in Guangnan County, the county government office stated that due to financial difficulties, no school bus was provided for any school in Guangnan County. It is understood that there are currently 433 official vehicles in Guangnan County.

According to reports from "Urban Times," yesterday, Guangnan County in Yunnan Province held a press briefing on the "12-24" accident. The head of the traffic police department in Guangnan County preliminarily determined that the cause of the accident was improper operation by the driver, and the accident was classified as a non-road traffic accident.

It is understood that none of the microcars pulling passengers in the entire Shuguang Township have obtained operating qualifications. The farthest distance between local schools and students' homes is 20 kilometers, so these unlicensed vehicles became their only choice.

Yesterday afternoon, Guangnan County held a briefing on this matter. Director Ye of Guangnan County stated that after preliminary investigation, the driver of the accident vehicle, Wen Minggang, who died on the spot, obtained his driver's license in October this year. The registration time of the accident vehicle was November 17 this year, with the license plate number Yun HB3410. The vehicle's registered purpose was "for personal use," and it had gone through relevant insurance procedures but did not have operating qualifications. Therefore, it could not be used for passenger transport operations. After on-site inspection by the traffic police department, the vehicle flipped into a 77.95-meter-deep ditch at Dàyázi (the accident site) due to improper operation while climbing uphill. Since the road where the accident occurred did not meet the relevant standards for roads and was a self-built road by the villagers, even legally qualified vehicles could not operate passenger services on such roads. Therefore, this accident was categorized as a non-road traffic accident. The accident vehicle lacked operating qualifications and had not obtained any operating procedures.
