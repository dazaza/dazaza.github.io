The domain name will not be devalued, and SEO is never easy.

by jdwo on 2007-05-03 11:48:51

Before the opening of the Search Engine Strategy Conference Xiamen Station, Inway Marketing Portal (www.timev.com), the official Chinese website of the conference, conducted a series of interviews with the speakers. Here is the first one:

Rand Fishkin (Randfish): Search engine expert and founder of the world-renowned SEOMoz company

Yingwei Ni: Vice President of Xiamen Shi Yi Hao Wei Technology Co., Ltd. and online marketing expert

Xiaohua Chen (Shitou): General Editor of Inway Marketing Portal and well-known domestic search engine marketing expert

Below is the transcript of this interview:

Inway: Thank you for accepting our interview, Shitou and I. First, I would like to ask what motivated you to come to China and participate in the search conference? What are your expectations for this trip to China regarding search or China itself?

Rand: I wanted to come to China last year to participate in the first search conference, but I couldn't make time. This year, I knew I had to come to China. After all, Americans are often culturally isolated and frequently ignore events happening in other parts of the world, whether politically, regionally, or socially. I believe this trend should be changed, and as someone who believes this change must occur, it is my responsibility to see as much of the world as possible.

China is gradually becoming an important political and economic force in the world. From a purely business perspective, ignoring the size, influence, and importance of the Chinese market would result in irreparable losses. Although we currently do not conduct direct business with Chinese companies and customers, I hope this trip can build a bridge to better understand the Chinese market and allow the Chinese market to recognize the value of SEOmoz.

During this trip, I plan to visit several cities: Xi'an, Xiamen, Beijing, Shanghai, and Hong Kong. I hope to understand China from multiple different angles.

To be honest, I don't know what to expect, but anything is possible. Although I don't have very clear goals, I believe that I will meet some important people, establish long-term friendships, and derive business opportunities from this trip. This is a brand-new experience for me. What excites me even more is that my grandfather will come to China with me. He is an engineer who has been to China many times. To travel with him makes me feel incredibly happy.

Inway: Thank you for your answer, Rand. Now, we can talk about the topics of search and the search conference. Shitou, please start.

Shitou: Hello, Rand. You have a lot of fans in China, and this should be your first time interacting with Chinese readers. How does it feel?

Rand: I am very excited to participate in this dialogue about Chinese search engines. I hope to learn more from every Chinese person I meet.

Shitou: Many Chinese SEOs find that many companies use the guise of consulting services to actually understand what SEO is or obtain some free plans and suggestions. I wonder if companies providing SEO services in the United States also frequently encounter similar situations?

Rand: The same thing happens in the U.S. as well, so many (SEM/SEO) companies charge thousands of dollars for their plans and quotes.

Shitou: The price reduction of the .cn domain to 1 RMB makes the cost of domains almost negligible. Some people think this will lead to rampant spam. Some even worry that if .cn domains become filled with junk, search engines like Google might lower the overall ranking of .cn domains. What do you think about this view?

Rand: I don't believe that will happen. Although the reduced domain prices will decrease the cost of producing spam, search engines won't simply devalue a top-level domain because it contains too much spam. They will directly specify stricter algorithms or create processes similar to "Sandbox" to delay the time new sites acquire rankings.

Shitou: Do you think SEO is a simple task? How can one become a qualified SEO?

Rand: Absolutely not an easy task! SEO requires you to continuously focus on many details in the market. Just observing changes and updates in search engines and external traffic factors is already a terrifying challenge.

Shitou: There are currently a large number of SEO beginners in China who are very interested in SEO. Can you give them some advice on learning methods? Preferably with some secret tips explained.

Rand: There are some English beginner resources (including SEOmoz's own SEO Beginner's Guide) that I often recommend to friends in North America and Europe. But I haven't noticed any materials in the Chinese market that can help SEO beginners quickly familiarize themselves with their learning objects.

Shitou: Your blog often talks about link bait. Can you recommend a few great examples of link bait? Preferably ones you've been involved in.

Rand: I'm happy to recommend some cases we've been involved in:

http://www.seomoz.org/blog/21-tactics-to-increase-blog-traffic - ranked first for the keyword "increase blog traffic"

http://www.drivl.com/posts/view/738 - ranked second for the keyword "college mascots".

http://www.seomoz.org/ip2loc - ranked first for the keyword "IP Location Lookup".

http://www.seomoz.org/article/social-media-marketing-tactics - ranked second for the keyword "social media marketing".

Shitou: Do you think there are differences between SEO for Web 2.0 sites and traditional sites? For example, when implementing SEO for sites like Digg.com or YouTube, what aspects should be paid attention to?

Rand: I believe all SEO revolves around two elements: search engine friendliness and keyword research.

Shitou: In China, many people, including the media and even search engine manufacturers, may have biases against SEO. They think it's meaningless or even equate it directly with spam. I imagine this view exists in the U.S. as well. How do you respond when faced with such doubts?

Rand: In the early days, we needed to prove with results: once a company sees they are getting target traffic, and these traffics eventually convert into customers, they will realize the true value of SEO. I think this is why so many SEO enthusiasts first got involved in SEO because of running their own sites. I believe China will go through the same process.

Shitou: Let's talk about SEOmoz. We're curious, what keeps you consistently updating and providing high-quality content?

Rand: To be a company that provides high-value services, I must ensure I stay at the forefront of the industry. Blogging helps achieve this goal while documenting our work and methods. Knowledge sharing is an excellent marketing tool. The knowledge we share attracts a large number of companies asking about related services daily.

Shitou: Can you briefly introduce the theme of your speech at SES? Regarding the Chinese search engine and search engine marketing market, what content are you most interested in learning through the SES conference?

Rand: I am very interested in understanding the status of the Chinese search engine marketing market. For example, how do users use search engines? Which search engine is more popular? How do the ranking algorithms and traffic composition of search engines differ from the European and American markets?

Shitou: Have any of your clients gained business opportunities from China?

Rand: We served a company selling perfumes globally. They have many sellers in China, mainly in Hong Kong, but also in other cities.

Shitou: Can you describe the goal of SEOmoz in a sentence or two?

Rand: SEOmoz is dedicated to comprehensively providing industry-leading resources for online marketers and technical developers.

Inway: Let's talk about leisure. It's your first visit to China, what food in China are you most looking forward to trying?

Rand: I've watched a lot of Japanese versions of Iron Chef (a cooking show), and I really like some of the Chinese dishes mentioned in it. I want to try real Sichuan cuisine, which is spicy, fragrant, and sweet.
