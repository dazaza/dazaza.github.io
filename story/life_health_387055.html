Experts interpret the hazards of hemorrhoids: If left untreated for a long time, hemorrhoids can lead to serious consequences:

1. Some may induce cardiovascular and cerebrovascular diseases, especially in elderly patients.

2. For pregnant women, it may affect the normal development of the fetus, leading to slow development, low weight, or even premature birth or death.

3. Depression is more likely to occur since hemorrhoids are located in private areas, many patients feel embarrassed to let doctors see the affected area, leading to delayed treatment. The longer the delay, the more severe the condition becomes, resulting in psychological burdens.

4. Gynecological inflammation: In young women, hemorrhoids can cause perianal abscesses and infections that spread to the vaginal opening, causing other gynecological infections, affecting their quality of life.

5. Anemia: Due to blood loss from hemorrhoids, iron-deficiency anemia occurs. Early symptoms include fatigue in women. When anemia is severe or progresses rapidly, symptoms like pale complexion, fatigue, lack of appetite, palpitations, increased heart rate, shortness of breath, and swelling after physical activity may occur.

6. Rectal cancer: Because the location of hemorrhoids and rectal cancer is similar, over 90% of rectal cancer cases are initially misdiagnosed as hemorrhoids. Clinical evidence shows that among patients seeking treatment for hemorrhoids, 1% to 3% are eventually diagnosed with rectal cancer.
