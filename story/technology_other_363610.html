That's the qualitative difference.

Looking back at how Wikipedia next introduces the controversy in the scientific community over the Duman method (as a Wiki, what do you call an "encyclopedia" if you don't (dare/willingly) introduce the controversy, display and encourage the controversy), the Duman method mainly has the following flaws:

1. recapitulation theory, its theoretical basis, has been abandoned by the natural sciences.

2. There is no evidence that passive movements in young children, such as forced crawling, can affect neural tissue.

3. When a child's voluntary action is prevented by the IAHP (according to its theory) from doing so, it may harm the child.

4. Master Duman's only published scientific paper contains many, many methodological errors and exaggerated descriptions. His study did not have a control group, so comparisons could not be made. When other independent third-party scientists have tried to compare these results with children who have not been treated with IAHP methods, the results have been singularly unimpressive. In short, do a comparative trial to prove that Master Dorman's clinical trial results are not effective.

5. It is cruel for participants to place their hopes on a course that is completely impossible to carry them.

An assistant professor at Yale University summed up the Duman method as follows: "The Duman model technique is based on a bankrupt, completely abandoned theory that has also failed to test under controlled conditions. This process wastes parents' time, energy, feelings and money."

The American Academy of Pediatrics questioned the IAHP's procedures for treating children with brain injuries as early as 1968, and repeated them in 1999, 2002, and 2005. This treatment procedure is based on a simplified and outdated theory of brain development, all the current information does not prove the effectiveness of this treatment, and there are no guarantees for the use of this method. The demands and expectations placed on the family are so high that they strain the family's financial resources in a way that strains parent-child relationships and sibling relationships."
