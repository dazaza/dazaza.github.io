Experience of opening a store on Taobao...... The difference between online stores and physical stores

by anonymous on 2011-03-25 14:19:09

On Taobao, it's difficult to find unique or rare items in the major categories for you to sell. In that case, why not look for unique products within those major categories? And if you can't even find unique products, then look for sources outside of official channels. This is not encouraging you to sell inferior or counterfeit goods; such behavior must be strictly prohibited. Compared to business, credibility is more important (do you think Taobao's review system is just for show?). What I mean by sources outside of official channels refers to things like end-of-line stock or exports. The quality is actually identical to the genuine product, but due to differences in packaging or channels, they might be much cheaper than in specialty stores. Actually, quite a few people are already doing this now. Because this way, there's a market. China hasn't yet reached the economic level where everyone can spend several thousand yuan per month on luxury goods. Therefore, what to sell is a question worth studying. Society today is about niche markets, and this is a trend that needs to be seized.

Unlike physical stores, online stores don't have tangible displays or products for customers to touch, so credibility is the most fundamental guarantee. In this regard, the importance of an online store's credibility surpasses that of a physical store. Do you agree? Intangible things require respect for the rules of the game, and these rules are market rules. Those who don't follow them will be eliminated. Taobao's credibility mechanism is a very powerful thing. So don't think that selling inferior goods will give you small advantages; it will only lead to your downfall. Believe it or not? Therefore, attracting attention and increasing popularity is essential. If you only do this, however, it will only increase your store's browsing volume without any other effects. But merely increasing browsing doesn't bring in profits, does it? Look at the newspapers and TV now. Ubiquitous advertisements may arouse your desire to shop, but what ultimately drives you to consume is still the service quality, the product quality, and the after-sales service. Right? Agree? Raise your hand :) Alright, let's keep talking, unstoppable.

Quality issues are directly related to trust issues, because in online stores, you can't touch the products. Okay, I might be willing to take a risk on something costing 50 or 100 yuan, but what about something costing 1000 or 5000 yuan? Ask yourself. Therefore, you need to have a clear understanding and confidence in the quality of your own goods. This confidence and understanding should not be self-promoting.

Nowadays, many sellers on Taobao use drop shipping. Sellers sometimes haven't even seen the products they're selling, but they still upload pictures and descriptions, thinking that as long as someone buys, they're good. I believe that this approach cannot lead to successful store management. Maybe the drop-shipping products you choose are great, in which case congratulations, you've hit the jackpot. But what if they're not? You wouldn't know, since you don't understand them yourself. Look at Taobao. Many product pictures are copied, and below the same pictures, the qualities vary. Therefore, I suggest that everyone should purchase some inventory, no matter how small. This investment might not be large; perhaps 1000 or 2000 yuan would allow you to have a sufficient understanding of your sources. Isn't that right? This cost cannot be spared. For example, I buy a set of anything I really like. Why? Consider the perspective of a consumer, and practice empathy. It's important and critical. There are many goods, and it's impossible to stock all of them. Stocking up carries risks, but I think stocking up with 1000 or 2000 yuan worth of goods should be fine. A meal with friends costs 100-200 yuan, and saving on two packs of cigarettes could cover it, isn't that right? To succeed in a certain industry, you must make sacrifices. Therefore, this is an attitude issue. What do you think?

Regardless of whether it's a physical store or an online store, repeat customers are the favorite type of customers for sellers. Besides taking care of business, the existence of repeat customers proves that your management is effective, your service quality is good, and there are people who recognize you. Compared to sales volume, being recognized feels better, just like in "Naruto" where it says: "A person's existence is constantly striving for others' recognition." I support this statement. Being recognized is indeed a driving force in life. Secondly, the economic benefits brought by repeat customers are also significant. They can attract potential other customers and serve as your living advertisements. Remember when a certain economist said on TV: "Faced with ubiquitous advertisements, people nowadays prefer word-of-mouth recommendations." Isn't that right? When your friend recommends something for you to buy, you're likely to feel interested. To get more repeat customers, the first step is service. Be nice, consider the buyer more during after-sales services. If there's a problem with something that costs dozens, hundreds, or thousands of yuan, anyone would feel uncomfortable, right?

Taobao's Consumer Protection Program has a threshold, though it's not high, it seems unreachable for beginners. So do we have ways ourselves? If you have confidence in the products you sell, you can actually promise a 14-day return policy. Don't worry about unreasonable troublemakers from individual buyers; they are the minority. Most transactions are made to obtain desired items. Moreover, check carefully when shipping out, and remind buyers when they receive the goods. This can avoid many problems. Taobao's complaint mechanism is also aimed at dealing with malicious transactions, and there are many means. That is to say, don't let a few people affect your business strategy; you should have a long-term vision.

In fact, running an online store is not easier or less stressful than running a physical store. It requires arduous efforts. Of course, if you're just playing around, the requirements are different. People who want to start a business on Taobao and gain rewards must take it seriously. Every day, newbies flood in, and every day, frustrated people exit. Here, the bittersweet experiences are witnessed. How can we say that the internet is virtual? Everything is real. It's lively. Taobao is truly changing our lives. Perhaps it's just a platform or a stepping stone, but it's definitely a good place to cultivate. It witnesses our growth together. These real feelings may have errors, but at least they express the sentiments of new sellers. Even if they can't represent the majority, they can at least represent oneself. Before leaving, let me advertise a bit. Today, it's considered opening for business. I hope I can persist, persist, and persist. Friends are welcome to correct me. Making friends comes first, business second. Guangzhou VIP Information Technology Co., Ltd.
