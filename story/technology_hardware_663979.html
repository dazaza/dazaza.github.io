The winter transfer situation of Croatian team Dinamo Zagreb has been receiving significant attention from local media. According to reports, two midfielders recently came close to leaving the club: 31-year-old Jelko Leko and 26-year-old Nikola Pokrivac. Among them, Leko is the player who was recently reported by Chinese media as the "new foreign signing targeted by Liaoning FC."

However, according to Croatian media, both players ultimately decided to reject lucrative offers from China. Reports indicate that the Chinese club’s offer matched their current salaries at Dinamo with a two-year contract, extendable to three years based on performance. The local media did not disclose the specific name of the Chinese team.

Local analysts suggest that Jelko Leko's decision to stay with Dinamo is understandable, as he is closer to being part of the first-team lineup under new coach Zoran Čačić, who is expected to give him more playing time in the upcoming season. Additionally, Leko has had a successful career and recently became a father, which likely influenced his desire to remain close to home rather than move to distant China.

For Pokrivac, the media opines that rejecting the Chinese offer might have been a mistake, as he is not among new coach Čačić's top choices. By moving to China, he could have secured a good salary and guaranteed playing time.

The media further speculates that if these two Dinamo players had accepted the Chinese club's offer, Dinamo would not have made a substantial profit but would have allowed them to leave in accordance with contract terms, gladly saving over ten million euros for the upcoming season.
