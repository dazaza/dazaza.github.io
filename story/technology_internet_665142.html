China becomes cooperation country of 2012 Hannover Industrial Fair

by testchamber4 on 2012-02-14 13:20:59

China becomes the partner country of the 2012 Hannover Industrial Fair.

The China Council for the Promotion of International Trade and Deutsche Messe AG officially signed a cooperation agreement in Beijing on the 14th, confirming that China will be the partner country of the 2012 Hannover Industrial Fair. The China Council for the Promotion of International Trade is directly entrusted by the Chinese government to participate in the organization of the partner country.

According to the introduction, on June 28, Minister of Industry and Information Technology of China, Miao Wei, and German Federal Minister of Economics, Dr. Philipp Rösler, jointly signed a memorandum of understanding regarding the 2012 Hannover Industrial Fair in Berlin. The governments of both countries are confident that through China being the partner country of the 2012 Hannover Industrial Fair, it will strengthen and consolidate the opportunity for further development of bilateral economic and trade relations. The Ministry of Industry and Information Technology of China and the German Federal Ministry of Economics and Technology will support Deutsche Messe AG politically, while at the same time urging the China Council for the Promotion of International Trade to implement this project. The signing of the memorandum took place at the Federal Chancellery under the framework of Sino-German intergovernmental consultations.

The Hannover Industrial Fair is the world's most influential technical event. Next year's exhibition will be grandly held in Hannover, Germany from April 23-27, 2012, covering eight thematic exhibitions: industrial automation, energy, mobile technology, digital factory, industrial supply, coil technology, industrial green technology, and research and technology. Chinese enterprises participating in next year's exhibition will not only cover all exhibition themes of the Hannover Industrial Fair but will also participate in the forums and conferences held concurrently. In the 2011 Hannover Industrial Fair, more than 500 Chinese enterprises participated, and this number will significantly increase next year.
