3.3 Guide Shopping Websites

Inland online stores are abundant with a wide variety of products, making it difficult for consumers to find the specific product websites they have in mind. In this scenario, the search engines on guide shopping websites can assist consumers in finding suitable product sites. The search engine is a selection tool where consumers can set filtering conditions such as price, function, brand, and specifications, allowing the website to screen and narrow down the products that meet customer requirements. For example, Aibei.com and Congmingdian in inland areas list products from various regional retailers and provide functional matching services, enabling consumers to shop easily.

Guide shopping websites offer the selling prices of the same product across different merchants or regions. Consumers can also set their requirements for products (such as price, brand, merchant, region), and the website will match the data of online stores that meet consumer conditions, along with user reviews of products and merchants. After comparing, consumers can then link to the selected merchant's online store to make a purchase.

Guide shopping websites provide extensive shopping information to consumers through product information, price details, shopping searches, and comparison functions. They also help merchants match high-quality potential customers. To ensure that the enterprise's products are successfully selected by the search engine, product suppliers need to describe the functions and features of the products in detail and accurately grasp the search terms used by consumers to establish appropriate keywords.

4. Comparison between Independent Online Stores and Third-Party Trading Platforms

4.1 Autonomous Space

Third-party trading platforms already have a fixed customer base and browsing rate, allowing businesses just starting online sales to quickly reach potential consumers. However, at the same time, there are more products and suppliers on third-party trading platforms, making it hard to highlight individual suppliers or brands.

Some companies hoping to highlight their own brand image, especially those that have already established a fixed customer base, choose to set up independent online stores. Some Hong Kong brands that are well-known in inland areas, such as Giordano, Sasa, and Chow Sang Sang, have adopted the independent website model to develop online sales channels in inland areas. Sasa opened its first cosmetics specialty store in Shanghai in 2005, but Sasa’s online store was established as early as 2000, allowing customers from inland areas and around the world to conveniently select beauty products. From 2008-2009, Sasa’s sales accounted for about 4.8% of total revenue, an increase of 66% compared to 2007-2008.

Some brands have only sold through independent online stores since their inception, such as the inland clothing brand Vancl. Founded in October 2007, Vancl has been selling its own brand VANCL men's and women's clothing on an independent online store under the concept of "Internet Fashion Lifestyle". It gradually expanded its product range to include children's clothing, shoes, and home goods. Despite not having physical stores or other operational methods on third-party platforms, VANCL's revenue increased from RMB 1.12 million in 2007 to RMB 6 billion in 2009, accounting for approximately 28.4% of the B2C clothing market share in inland areas, ranking first.

To meet the needs of some sellers who want to enhance their brand image and have an independent store, some third-party trading platforms like Taobao Mall allow individual brands such as Uniqlo, Levi’s, Belle, etc., to open exclusive zones/flagship stores within their websites. However, third-party platform websites are systematized, so even if they have their own sales sections, the websites still have designated layouts and will standardize the buying and selling terms between sellers and consumers, affecting the autonomous space and development strategies of enterprises. For example, sellers on Taobao Mall must provide a "Taobao Mall 7-day no-reason return/exchange" after-sales service.

4.2 Registration and Management

The procedures for setting up an online store on a third-party platform are simple, requiring only a business license and tax registration certificate in inland areas. Additionally, if the enterprise is a brand manufacturer or factory, it needs to apply for a China trademark certificate (trademark acceptance notice); if the enterprise is an agent, it needs a brand authorization letter. However, enterprises opening independent online stores as sales channels need to apply to national telecommunications management institutions or the State Council Information Industry Department.

Third-party platform websites not only provide basic services such as web page layout, payment systems, integrity security systems, and web management, but also have the responsibility to delete suspected plagiarized or counterfeit products or companies from the website to protect the patents of registered merchants and maintain copyright interests. Generally speaking, the threshold for setting up an online store on a third-party platform is lower, requiring fewer human resources, technology, and funds. However, independent online stores require enterprises to be responsible for daily operations and system maintenance, as well as electronic payment security issues, which require a certain amount of human resources, placing a heavier burden on small and medium-sized enterprises.

Most third-party platforms have designated independent electronic payment providers, and enterprises need to separately apply for accounts and pay deposit fees to the electronic payment provider. For example, Taobao Mall uses Alipay as the payment platform, and Alipay charges enterprises RMB 10,000 as a deposit. Subsequently, all transactions between enterprises and consumers will be handled by the independent electronic payment provider.

5. Security Issues in Electronic Payments

Network transaction security and confidentiality are one of the obstacles to the development of internet sales because consumers cannot directly pay sellers (except for direct transfers to the seller's bank account). More than 70% of consumers choose electronic payments when shopping online. The Ministry of Commerce's "Opinions on Promoting the Standardized Development of E-commerce" aims to regulate electronic payment behavior and ensure the safety of capital flows; and to guide enterprises to adopt effective technical and management measures to ensure the security of payment passwords and financial data.

5.1 Third-Party Payment Platforms

To address the issue of the safety of customer capital flows, most online commodity trading platforms adopt third-party payment methods. For example, Taobao uses Alipay's platform, and eBay uses Anfutong's platform. Both websites require consumers to deposit cash into Alipay or Anfutong accounts when purchasing goods; Alipay/Anfutong then notifies the seller to ship the goods. When the transaction is completed and the consumer confirms that the received goods match what was purchased online, they notify Alipay/Anfutong to pay the seller. However, third-party payment methods also pose some problems because during the transaction process, a capital retention platform is formed. How to ensure the safety of its cash requires supervision from regulatory authorities.

5.2 Anti-Fraud System

Currently, most consumers use credit cards or debit cards issued by banks for online electronic payments. However, due to the short duration of online transactions, banks find it difficult to detect problematic credit cards (stolen, lost, fake cards) and may incur losses. Some third-party payment platforms provide an "anti-fraud system" to protect sellers and banks. Before issuing transaction instructions to the bank, the third-party payment platform compares the credit card data with the bank's customer database, assesses the risk, and confirms the transaction with the bank. For example, if the same credit card number makes online purchases in different regions or from different IP addresses within a few minutes, it is likely to involve a fake card. If the risk rating is high, the third-party payment platform will terminate the transaction to protect the interests of the bank and the seller.

5.3 Guofubao Electronic Payment Platform

To meet enterprise needs and the development of e-commerce, in 2007, the International E-commerce Center of the Ministry of Commerce and Guofutong Company launched the state-owned background "Guofubao" electronic payment platform. Besides providing B2B (e.g., China Garment Network and China Electromechanical Trade Network) and B2C (e.g., Zhengjia Network and Joyo.com) payment platforms, it is also applied to G2C government fee collection and public utility payments. Guofubao is the only nationally guaranteed trading platform, with a national-level security certification system ensuring payment and fund safety, reducing consumer concerns and protecting sellers.

6. Methods of Goods Delivery

The biggest difficulty in goods delivery is the dispersion of customers and varying distances, increasing shipping costs while requiring additional manpower to individually package and ship goods. On the other hand, time is also an important factor to consider because consumers are accustomed to the traditional "buy now, get now" shopping mode, so goods must be delivered to consumers as soon as possible. Currently, the goods delivery models for B2C online transaction websites mainly include the following three types.

6.1 National Postal Service

Sellers can use China Post Group Corporation and its provincial postal subsidiaries to send goods to the address provided by consumers. Regular mail delivery is slower, so sellers can choose express delivery, though it is more expensive. However, sellers should be aware of the uneven quality of regional postal services, which may lead to consumers failing to receive goods within the expected time frame, damaged packaging, or damaged goods, affecting the company's reputation.

6.2 Website Self-built Delivery Model

Some large-scale enterprises and major B2C websites (such as dangdang.com, Joyo.com, and JD.com) have self-built logistics delivery modes, setting up warehousing centers and delivery points in areas with dense internet users, delivering goods based on consumer shopping lists and personal information. This delivery model allows for quick delivery of goods to consumers, but increases logistics costs, making it difficult for sellers to pass on these costs to consumers, thereby reducing profits. It is also challenging to accurately determine the layout of B2C website delivery points, the number of staff, and inventory levels. Some websites have multiple outlets allowing consumers to pick up goods in person, such as JD.com's "in-store pickup," where consumers can pay in cash, by check, or via POS machine after online shopping.

6.3 Third-Party Logistics Delivery Model

Since the volume and size of deliveries for B2C are relatively small, even though courier fees are high (they can exceed 20% of gross profit), many sellers are willing to adopt them because of the fast delivery times (same-city delivery can be completed within 24 hours). However, sellers still face risks such as lost goods, product damage during transit, and leakage of customer data. If goods are lost, even if the seller presents receipts, the courier company will only compensate at 3 to 5 times the freight cost. Regardless of the delivery model chosen, the existing delivery system in inland areas has yet to catch up with the development of B2C business, and shipping and courier fees often constitute a significant proportion of the operating costs of online stores.

7. Store Promotion and Competition

7.1 Building Store Reputation

Online marketing has low barriers to entry, resulting in a large number of competitors. Some online stores may remain unnoticed for months. How can customers find Hong Kong merchants' online stores amidst the vast internet? Mainland consumers value word-of-mouth, with nearly half of netizens hearing about or being introduced to shopping websites by friends and family, and 30% learning about them through the internet.

Most websites have buyer ratings where consumers who have purchased products leave feedback on their transaction experience with the company, evaluating both the product and the seller, helping to build the company's reputation. For example, eBay has a Seller Service Rating System (DSR), where after completing a transaction, buyers can rate the seller's service quality in four categories: differences between product descriptions and actual items, communication quality and response speed, reasonableness of item delivery time, and reasonableness of freight and handling fees. This allows companies to directly understand their service performance and helps other consumers select sellers with higher service quality ratings for a better shopping experience.

7.2 Utilizing Third-Party Platform "Recommendation Positions"

Recommendation positions are like online advertisements, obtained by sellers through purchase. These recommendation positions are usually located in prominent places on the website, displaying products in text or photo form. Of course, the fees for recommendation positions vary depending on the website and location, allowing merchants to choose according to budget and needs. Since recommendation positions are paid, online stores cannot place all products in these positions, but instead strategically place the most distinctive products to attract buyers to view other product information on the store.

The category of products also affects the effectiveness of recommendation positions. Products with standard specifications and functionalities (such as computer accessories, vitamins) tend to use recommendation positions less frequently because the product characteristics may be less influential compared to other factors (such as price, after-sales service) that affect consumer purchasing desires. On the contrary, consumer products (such as jewelry, watches) differ in style, material, and design and need to keep up with seasons and trends, so recommendation positions effectively highlight the merchant's products.

7.3 Promotion of Independent Online Stores

Independent online stores can register their company URLs on major search engines (such as www.google.cn, www.yahoo.cn, www.baidu.com, etc.). If you want your customers to find your company website quickly, you can buy "keyword ads", "friend links", "promotion links", etc., from search engine service providers to appear in better positions in search results, attracting consumer attention.

Another method is through "Search Engine Optimization" (SEO), utilizing search rules to improve the ranking of the company's website on relevant search engines. When searchers input keywords into a search engine, the search engine compares the content of websites and presents these contents quickly and as completely as possible to the searcher. Websites processed by SEO are more likely to be accepted by search engines and ranked higher.

7.4 Enhancing Product Competitiveness

Although data shows that clothing is the most commonly purchased item by mainland internet users, the quality, style, and price of products are also important factors for success. One example is a Hong Kong trading company engaged in import and export that uses a third-party platform to open an online store in mainland China, selling trendy Korean clothing targeting working women in Shanghai. Due to the large number of similar online stores, products imported from Korea are more expensive and lack competitiveness. Moreover, fashion trends change quickly, and styles become outdated rapidly. Selling clothing also requires providing different sizes and colors, necessitating substantial capital for inventory preparation. Due to intense competition, the online store closed after one year. Therefore, Hong Kong merchants should thoroughly understand the actual situation of online sales, the unique features and selling points of their products before opening an online store.

8. Government Regulation of Internet Sales

Since online shopping is still a relatively new channel, mainland China has not yet established a complete set of laws and regulations specifically for online sales. In 2008, the Ministry of Commerce published draft standards for "E-commerce Business Models" and "Norms for Online Shopping Services" aiming to standardize online commodity sales and service behaviors. According to the Ministry of Commerce's 2007 "Guidelines for Online Transactions (Interim)", online sale of goods or services extends traditional business operations onto the internet and should be detailed according to existing laws such as the "General Principles of Civil Law", "Company Law", "Electronic Signature Law", and other commercial operation laws. Many regional governments have also formulated regional e-commerce regulations, such as the "Shanghai Regulations for Promoting the Development of E-commerce" and Beijing's "Opinions on Promoting the Development of Online Retail".

8.1 Business Registration

According to the "Guidelines for Online Transactions (Interim)", opening an online store is akin to operating a physical store, and enterprises need to register with the State Administration for Industry and Commerce. Enterprises already registered in mainland China that operate physical stores do not need to re-register if the scope of the online store is consistent with the physical store. If the enterprise does not have a physical store in mainland China, it needs to submit the required application documents to the provincial commerce department of the foreign investment commercial enterprise's registration location.

If an enterprise only sells on the internet through third-party platforms or through online distributors, it only needs to register for commercial registration in mainland China. However, if the enterprise sets up an independent online sales platform (i.e., directly providing internet information services - ICP), according to the 2000 State Council Order "Administrative Measures for Internet Information Services", it should apply to the provincial/autonomous region/municipality telecommunications management institution or the State Council Information Industry Department for an "Internet Information Services Value-Added Telecommunications Business Operating License". The operating license number should be displayed in the middle of the homepage footer. Additionally, according to the 2005 "Administrative Measures for Internet IP Address Filing", the independent IP address (with or without a domain name) of the website must also be registered with the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology. Websites providing news, publishing, education, medical care, pharmaceuticals and medical devices, culture, radio, film, and television program internet information services should undergo pre-approval procedures.

8.2 Taxation Issues

According to the 2002 "Detailed Rules for the Implementation of the Tax Collection and Administration Law of the People's Republic of China": "Taxpayers engaged in production and business operations shall, within 30 days from the date of obtaining their business licenses, declare and register for tax purposes with the competent tax authority at the place of production, business operations, or where the tax obligation arises, truthfully filling out the tax registration form and providing relevant certificates and materials as required by the tax authority." The main taxes applicable to foreign-invested enterprises selling goods in China include corporate income tax, value-added tax, consumption tax, and business tax for agency services. If the taxpayer is a producer or business merchant, the taxes include value-added tax, corporate income tax, and business tax. However, internet sales also include some digitalizable products, such as books, magazines, audiovisual products, and computer software. These types of transactions do not require crossing traditional borders or national boundaries, so customs duties and domestic taxes cannot be levied. In fact, no country can currently fully levy all taxes on online trade.

9. Summary

There is no doubt that online sales will be an important growth pole in China's consumer market and a new channel for Hong Kong merchants to explore domestic sales. Online sales not only free themselves from geographical and store size restrictions but also sell goods to consumers nationwide 24 hours a day. The development of different types of third-party sales platforms provides small and medium-sized enterprises with opportunities for domestic sales that have low thresholds, simple procedures, and relatively low costs.

With many market opportunities come much competition. Facing fierce competition, Hong Kong merchants must break through among numerous suppliers and products. They must understand the actual situation of mainland online sales, find market space, and use service platforms effectively to highlight product features and advantages, attracting buyers. Considering the current characteristics of mainland online shoppers, Hong Kong merchants can consider the following when starting online sales:

Consumers often prefer to personally experience the size and quality of products, especially personalized items like clothing and home decor, before purchasing online to ensure the product suits them. Therefore, to effectively expand the market, consideration can be given to the complementary development of physical stores and online stores, with corresponding arrangements made for product structure and pricing.

Mainland internet users are accustomed to browsing different websites and using the site's internal search tools to look for products before making purchases online. To increase click-through rates and transaction opportunities, titles should be utilized as much as possible, along with concise explanations of product features and ingredient details, and accurate annotations in product photos/pictures to facilitate buyer selection. Simultaneously, to increase the chances of search engines selecting products, Hong Kong merchants should clearly describe the functions and features of products and accurately grasp consumer search terms to establish appropriate keywords.

Mainland consumers love fashion. Hong Kong merchants, who primarily engage in foreign trade production with unique product designs and assured quality, should be popular among consumers but must adapt appropriately to the tastes of the mainland market. Moreover, the mainland market offers a wide variety of products, and consumers focus on cost-performance ratios. To increase competitiveness for online sales, Hong Kong merchants must consider rapid product updates, diverse design options, complete size and color availability, and competitive pricing.

Some internet users leave credit ratings on the seller's website after online purchases, which serve as important references for other consumers when shopping. Therefore, Hong Kong merchants should pay attention to building credibility online. Apart from the products themselves, the selling process and after-sales service are also crucial. To maintain good communication with customers, most online stores have online customer service. If real-time responses are not possible, replies are generally provided within 24 hours; otherwise, potential customers may be lost.

Additionally, goods should be delivered within the specified timeframe, without excuses such as stock shortages, mailing delays, or other reasons. Especially since the biggest difference between online stores and physical stores is that consumers cannot immediately enjoy the product after payment. If consumers receive the product later than the expected period, it significantly reduces their interest in the product and impression of the seller, making it difficult to attract repeat purchases. At the same time, sellers should list the product's after-sales service details on the online store, clearly explaining situations where consumers can return or exchange products, and which party bears the transportation costs, to avoid future disputes.

Currently, there are many choices for third-party online shopping platforms in mainland China. Some large comprehensive malls provide warehouse storage, logistics delivery, electronic payment services, and some website distributors even offer customer management and information feedback value-added services. Hong Kong merchants can select suitable websites as partners based on their own requirements and development strategies to explore mainland online sales.
