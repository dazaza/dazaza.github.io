Continuation of the story about a man struggling to register his household after being released from prison for 31 years, with his wife's request to the police station for registration being rejected.

by bi1s8099ha on 2012-02-15 11:14:56

■ Follow-up on "Beijing Man Being 'Blacklisted' for 31 Years After Release from Prison"

Report by our correspondent Chen Bo: Beijing man Wang Zhanyou had his household registration (hukou) restored only in 2008 after being released from prison in 1977. However, his wife and three children's application to relocate their hukou was rejected. Wang’s wife and three children sued the Lugouqiao Police Station for inaction, demanding that they be granted Beijing hukou (reported by our newspaper on December 7).

Yesterday, the Fengtai Court ruled that Wang’s wife did not submit an application, and her lawsuit was dismissed.

After nearly 30 years of marriage without a proper hukou

Wang Zhanyou was imprisoned twice in 1969 and 1993, being released in 1977 and 1996 respectively. During this period, he married Shi Qingyun and had three children with her.

Recently, Shi Qingyun and her four children filed a lawsuit against the Lugouqiao Police Station in Fengtai District, accusing them of “inaction” and “refusal to approve relocation of hukou to Beijing.”

According to the complaint, based on the "Regulations on Household Registration," when Wang Zhanyou was first released and returned to Beijing, the defendant refused to restore his hukou because he lacked a release certificate. During this time, Wang got married and had children.

In 1996, Wang was released again and obtained a release certificate, but his hukou was still not restored. According to Beijing regulations at the time, spouses and children could apply to relocate or settle in Beijing. However, the defendant rejected the application citing "historical issues." Even after Wang's hukou was restored in 2008, the plaintiffs' applications for hukou relocation were not approved.

Shi Qingyun and her four children requested the court to order the police station to grant them hukou.

The court found no evidence that the plaintiffs had submitted an application

During the trial, the Lugouqiao Police Station stated that after Wang Zhanyou's hukou was restored in 2008, Shi Qingyun and her children applied for relocation based on spousal dependency. Since Wang concealed his marital status during the restoration process, the legality of his hukou needed further confirmation. Thus, Shi and her children's application could not be processed temporarily. Additionally, Shi herself never visited the police station to file a written application, and there were also suspicions of over-quota births, which disqualified them from relocating their hukou to Beijing.

The Fengtai Court held that when the plaintiffs Shi Qingyun et al. applied for hukou processing, neither she nor her representatives appeared in person, nor did they provide proper authorization documents. Therefore, the court could not confirm that the plaintiffs had indeed filed an application. Hence, the claim that the police station acted improperly was not established, and the plaintiffs’ demands should be dismissed.

■ Follow-up Interview

Defendants: The police station did not require "personal presence"

After the trial, Wang Zhanyou's son, Wang Wei, said that the court ruled that his mother Shi Qingyun did not personally file the application. However, since his father's hukou was restored in 2008, they had repeatedly approached the local police officers to submit their applications. The officers never informed them that Shi Qingyun must appear in person or provide a power of attorney or other materials. Later, the police station even refused to accept any application materials, stating that they were too busy. "The police station did not fulfill its duty to inform us properly," Wang Wei said.

■ Changes in Wang Zhanyou's Household Registration

- March 1954: Wang Zhanyou was born in Wulidian Village, Lugouqiao Township.

- 1969: Wang Zhanyou was imprisoned for rape, and his hukou was canceled.

- July 1977: After serving his sentence, Wang was forced to remain employed at the labor camp.

- October 1982: Wang Zhanyou married Shi Qingyun and had one son and two daughters. Shi Qingyun's hukou was canceled, leaving all three family members without hukou.

- 1988: Wang brought his family back to live in Wulidian Village. Due to the lack of a release certificate, his hukou application was not approved.

- May 1993: Wang Zhanyou was imprisoned again for hooliganism.

- 1996: Wang Zhanyou was released and applied for hukou restoration with his release certificate but was denied.

- March 2008: Wang Zhanyou's Beijing hukou was restored, with his marital status registered as "unmarried."
