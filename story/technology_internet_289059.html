Zang Tianshuo Confirmed to File Appeal, Focus on Whether He "Instructed" - Sohu News

The case of Zang Tianshuo's appeal has seen new developments. It was reported yesterday that Zang Tianshuo has confirmed the filing of an appeal and also changed his defense lawyer. The new appointed lawyer is Xu Lanting, a renowned criminal defense lawyer in China. Currently, Xu Lanting has already met with Zang Tianshuo at the detention center. Xu Lanting said: "For the representation project, whether it will be a defense of innocence or mitigation of punishment has not yet been determined."

Hiring Experienced Lawyer, Original Lawyer Exits Voluntarily

The court sentenced Zang Tianshuo to six years in prison for organizing a brawl. According to informed sources, although Zang Tianshuo calmly accepted the verdict in court, after returning to the detention center, he looked very heavy-hearted, appeared extremely disheartened, spoke little, and looked even more haggard the next day.

Similarly burdened was Zang Tianshuo's wife. Although she did not attend the trial on the day of the verdict, she learned the result immediately afterward. She believed the court's judgment was too severe and expressed her intention to appeal. Subsequently, during Zang Tianshuo's meeting with his lawyer in the detention center, he explicitly stated his intention to appeal.

At the same time as filing the appeal, Zang Tianshuo also requested increased legal defense efforts, specifically hiring a more experienced lawyer in criminal defense cases, believing this would increase the likelihood of a successful appeal. Consequently, Zang Tianshuo's wife, through relevant channels, found the famous criminal defense lawyer Xu Lanting and invited him to join the team, forming a new defense lineup with two lawyers. However, this combination was not approved by the original lawyer Luo.

Last night, Luo, who previously served as Zang Tianshuo's defense lawyer, told reporters that he had formally exited the second-instance defense of Zang Tianshuo's case. Regarding the reasons, Luo said that Xu Lanting's professional ability and level are fully capable of handling the second-instance defense work, so his continued participation would be a waste of resources. Moreover, potential disagreements in defense strategies between them could lead to internal consumption, which would not benefit the case's defense.

Met with Zang Tianshuo, Defense Category Still Undetermined

"Already determined to defend him, but whether it will be a defense of innocence or mitigation has not yet been decided." Yesterday, Xu Lanting told reporters that currently, all the case files of Zang Tianshuo have not yet been obtained, and the entire case details are not yet fully understood, so it is impossible to decide what kind of defense strategy to adopt. Regarding Luo’s decision to exit the second-instance defense, Xu Lanting stated that he hoped Luo would continue to cooperate with him, as Luo is well-versed in the overall case details, especially some key evidence.

Regarding any dissatisfaction from Zang Tianshuo and his family towards Luo's first-instance defense, Xu Lanting said that based on his current understanding, there has been no expression of dissatisfaction from Zang Tianshuo and his family. Luo voluntarily requested to exit the defense.

It was introduced that hiring Xu Lanting was the idea of Zang Tianshuo's wife, and this idea quickly received Zang Tianshuo's approval. Currently, both Xu Lanting and Luo have met with Zang Tianshuo at the detention center and consulted him about Luo's exit from the second-instance defense. Zang Tianshuo has agreed to Luo's exit.

Last night, Luo told reporters that he had completed the handover procedures with Xu Lanting and officially exited the defense of Zang Tianshuo's case.

Confirmed Appeal, Whether “Instructed” Becomes the Focus

"The case is relatively simple; the key lies in how the related evidence should be recognized." Yesterday, Xu Lanting said that whether Zang Tianshuo is guilty hinges on whether he gave instructions. The main evidence determining whether he gave instructions comes from the conversation content between Zang Tianshuo and Lu Changchun in the bar, as well as whether Zang confessed after being arrested. If these pieces of evidence do not indicate that Zang gave instructions, then he is innocent; if he did give instructions, then the content of his instructions must be examined to see if it warrants a six-year sentence. Xu Lanting pointed out that the Langfang brawl incident occurred over five years ago, and many statements may be vague.

Regarding Zang's sentencing, Luo also believed it was too severe. Luo said that from Zang's arrest until now, he has never confessed to instructing Lu Changchun to fight others, nor has he flipped his confession in court as some media claimed. He only mentioned asking Lu Changchun to go to the bar to maintain order and prevent any incidents. How it turned into a mass brawl at the Langfang train station is something Zang was unaware of. He only learned about it afterward. However, these favorable confessions were not recognized.

According to reports, Zang Tianshuo will file an appeal application with the Second Intermediate People's Court soon, and the Second Intermediate People's Court will transfer this case to the Beijing Higher People's Court for review.

Biography

Xu Lanting (born 1963) has handled many major criminal, civil, and economic cases since practicing law for over a decade. Among these, there are more than 100 criminal cases, specializing in criminal defense.

He has served as the principal defendant's defense attorney in significant criminal cases such as the "Shenyang Mu Ma Major Case," the "Liu Xiaqing Company Tax Case," the "Zhang Brothers Organized Crime Case," the "Biyuxi's Wife Bribery Case," the "Former Minister of Land and Resources Tian Fengshan Bribery Case," the "Former Deputy Governor of Agricultural Development Bank Hu Chushou Bribery Case," the "National Food and Drug Administration Corruption Nest Case," and the "Shanghai Social Security Fund Case." Xu Lanting has successfully handled numerous cases transitioning from guilt to innocence and from death penalty to life imprisonment.

Xu Lanting also serves as legal counsel for several enterprises and public institutions and has received honors such as "China's Top Ten Criminal Defense Lawyers" and "China's Top Ten Influential Figures in the Legal Profession."
