SMS Gate: Another wave of deceptive messages is spreading

by m8008 on 2008-04-19 09:15:40

The echoes of the spam text message disturbance have not yet subsided, and in recent days, many friends have received a deceptive text message. This morning, a colleague's father called to ask if the colleague was indeed involved in this matter. We told him it was a scam. Afterward, I felt compelled to give my family a call, only to find out that my father had also received the same message but did not believe it because he knows we always call home if there is something important.

Here’s the content of the message:

"Dad, Mom, today while I was out playing, I lost my phone and wallet. I'm currently using a classmate's phone. I urgently need money, so please quickly transfer 600 yuan to (name, bank account number XXXXXXXXXXXXXXX). The account belongs to my classmate."

After looking at it, we all agreed this was clearly a deceptive message, and we discussed the following points:

1. This message exploits parents' love for their children.

2. If you try to reply via text or call, the phone line is unavailable.

If this classmate really lost their phone and money, given how convenient communication is nowadays, with cheap phone fees costing only 0.15 yuan per long-distance call, wouldn't they just call home instead?

Of course, I believe most people would not fall for such a scam. It can only deceive a few individuals. For families with students studying away from home, receiving such a text might prompt some parents to immediately return the call. Due to various reasons, the call may not go through, and neither will calls to the child's phone. With no other way to contact the child and unable to immediately verify the situation at school, some parents end up transferring the money.

The purpose of writing this is to once again remind everyone. I hope students studying away from home frequently call home and specifically discuss this issue with their families to avoid falling victim to scams.
