Unique Australia Tour 212

by qianljfsx on 2012-02-27 14:47:32

Don't assume that visiting Sydney and the Gold Coast means you know Australia. Don't assume that, in this era of flight, geographical distance can still limit your steps. Currently, Western and Southern Australia have become the latest tourist routes for travel agencies. Southwest Australia is not as far as you think. Here, the west is blue. Here, the south is wild. Here, life is pure.

■ When the desert meets the sea

The mention of winter in the west usually brings to mind desolation and沧桑. But upon arriving in Western Australia, one realizes that the winters here can be so mild and pure. Blue is the dominant color here. There are also deserts in the west, equally vast, but can you imagine what it would be like when the desert meets the sea? Driving across the continent of Western Australia, the most refreshing sight is the continuous sky and sea, blue enough to confuse, and next to this blue is the silver desert. The happy driver will take you on a ROCK ROLL in the desert - crazy dives and climbs between the dunes by the sea, no less thrilling than a roller coaster ride, with the blue of the sea and the white of the sand switching instantly, creating a truly unforgettable visual stimulation.

To experience the grandeur of the west, visit the Pinnacles Desert. It looks similar to the Stone Forest in Yunnan, but the stone pillars are not as tall; its colors resemble the Loess Plateau, but the area is not as large. But why is it described as one of the most beautiful natural landscapes in the world, and the most visited place by tourists in Western Australia? You wouldn't know unless you see it: it's because of the deadly blue sky of Western Australia, plus the silence and vastness of the sea, desert, and grassland, which create a unique flavor for the Pinnacle Desert, mixing grandeur with a touch of mystery, and silence containing a kind of power.

In this blue-toned west, you can experience true romance at any time, such as feeding black swans by the blue Swan River in the bright city of Perth; or enjoying Italian coffee with a flock of seagulls in Fremantle, the small port town created by Captain Cook, waiting for the most wonderful sunset at sea in the world.

■ Date with sea lions at Kangaroo Island

Sea lions, seals? Many people in Beijing can only learn about them from TV. Have you ever thought of really taking a stroll with sea lions on the beach? It's very simple: go to South Australia, go to Kangaroo Island.

The south of Australia is completely different from the gentle south of Asia, replaced by wildness and ruggedness. Unbelievable? Let's spend a wild weekend on Kangaroo Island together.

Here, you may encounter kangaroos and wild birds anywhere. To see koalas, just look into the wild forest (search carefully with your own eyes, there will be endless surprises! - but don't shout too loud, see how soundly they sleep!). Here, you can go to Seal Bay, the home of the second largest colony of sea lions in Australia, and interact with a group of sea lions at the closest distance (about 6 meters) - it's not zero distance, and you can't make loud noises, because they are purely wild and will become extremely fierce if they feel threatened!

Standing on the steep shore where Remarkable Rocks are located, I suggest you calm down and listen to the sound of huge waves crashing against ancient rocks. On top of the giant reefs, the sky-piercing strange rocks testify to the wonders of nature; the wild wind blows past your ears, as if telling incomprehensible stories - all signs tell you: here, this is the edge of the world.

■ The World's Greatest Studio

Wayne, who works as a tour guide and driver on Kangaroo Island, is 44 years old but has never left his hometown. His gaze towards the seals is like looking at his own children. He points to the sea, sky, and rocks before him and says calmly: "This is my studio, I have the best job in the world."

Wayne is really enviable. Can you feel the purity in his heart? Because he has never doubted the place he lives and the way he lives.

In the German village of South Australia, all shops on a small street are private boutiques, selling almost all handmade products by the shop owners. An elder even loves making pottery so much that he makes and sells it simultaneously. Walking into his shop is like walking into his garden, everywhere exuding the warmth of home and the owner's mood.

Perhaps the perfect social security system allows people not to struggle for survival, allowing them to live as they like. People who have been to Sydney all think that life there is very leisurely, but Sydneysiders yearn more for the lifestyle of people in Southwest Australia: simpler, purer.

A Beijinger who has lived in South Australia for 11 years said that when he first arrived, he couldn't stand it: there were hardly any people on the streets at 7 or 8 PM, and most families went to bed around 9 PM. The liveliest time was only in the early morning at 5 or 6 o'clock, when you could see people exercising, walking, running, cycling, and playing ball games everywhere - completely different from his previous lifestyle in Beijing. After feeling bored for a while, he started finding things to do, learning from his neighbors to manage the front and back yards and lawns himself. Since everything needed for a home was readily available here, he gradually became addicted, calmed down, and then realized that life could be very pure.

Wise Decision:

Three reasons to stopover in Lion City

■ Easier

From Beijing to Australia, it takes 10 hours just to get to Sydney (add 2 hours of time difference, it feels like 12 hours!), let alone another nearly 4-hour flight to Adelaide in South Australia. The result of a long journey is that even the most beautiful scenery loses its charm.

If you're smart, consider flying from Beijing via Singapore to South Australia. This way, you can scientifically divide the long journey into two segments: over 5 hours from Beijing to Singapore, rest your feet, regain your energy, then take a 4-hour flight directly to Adelaide in South Australia. Counting it up, you save nearly 5 hours of flying compared to going through Sydney!

■ Cheaper

Singapore is one of the world's most convenient and fast transportation hubs, especially for China. There are many flights every day to West and South Australia, and the ticket prices are more economical than taking domestic flights within Australia.

■ More Interesting

Singapore has excellent reception conditions and has become the center of tourism in the Asia-Pacific region. Compared to the natural beauty of Western and Southern Australia, Singapore appears more urbanized, with bustling shopping malls full of world-famous brands, perfect for crazy shopping. At the same time, including this unique Asian-Southeastern charm as a highlight in your Southwest Australia itinerary undoubtedly adds contrast, layers, and makes the trip richer and more interesting.
