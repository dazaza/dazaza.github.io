Yesmywine Reveals: Lie 1 - Cured Meat and Sausages as Accompaniments for Alcohol

During a feast, it is absolutely essential not to drink on an empty stomach. If you can't eat something beforehand to fill your stomach, then it's best to sip alcohol while eating dishes. At the same time, one must be cautious about using salted fish, sausages, or cured meats with alcohol because such preserved foods contain large amounts of pigments and nitrosamines, which react with alcohol, not only damaging the liver but also harming the oral cavity and esophagus lining, potentially even inducing cancer.

To minimize the damage of alcohol to the stomach and liver and reduce the occurrence of fatty liver disease, it's best to eat something before drinking. For instance, having a glass of milk or eating some eggs and meat, because these high-protein foods can combine with alcohol in the stomach, producing reactions that reduce alcohol absorption.

Yesmywine Reveals: Lie 2 - Inducing Vomiting by Scratching Throat When Drunk

In daily social gatherings, many people resort to the trick of going to the restroom and scratching their throat to induce vomiting after drinking too much, feeling better afterward, and even continuing to drink. However, experts point out that this is a dangerous action.

Scratching the throat to induce vomiting should only be done when awake or under the supervision of medical personnel because intoxicated individuals are often unconscious and can easily inhale vomit, causing suffocation, and even endangering life. Furthermore, violent vomiting increases intra-abdominal pressure, which not only easily causes gastric bleeding but can also lead to the reflux of duodenal contents, triggering acute pancreatitis and other emergencies.

Yesmywine Reveals: Lie 3 - Liquor Harms the Body While Red Wine Nourishes It

Many believe that drinking liquor harms the body, whereas drinking wine benefits health, so drinking more doesn't hurt. In reality, whether it's red wine or liquor, the key still lies in controlling the amount consumed.

Experts point out that men should consume less than 140 grams of alcohol per week, and women less than 70 grams. Exceeding this amount poses a risk of developing alcoholic liver disease. 140 grams of alcohol equates to 3-4 taels of 50-degree liquor, meaning adult males should not exceed 3-4 taels of 50-degree liquor weekly, and red wine should be limited to 1-2 taels daily.

Yesmywine Reveals: Lie 4 - Suddenly Quitting Alcohol Can Harm the Body

Many are advised by doctors to quit drinking due to health issues, but a large number fail to do so successfully, even citing sudden quitting as harmful to justify their continued indulgence in fine wines.

Experts note that those who think suddenly quitting alcohol harms the body are actually referring to withdrawal symptoms. For those dependent on alcohol, abruptly quitting may result in tremors, palpitations, seizures, vomiting, and other withdrawal symptoms. However, at this point, it's more important to quit drinking rather than believing a small amount will alleviate symptoms. There are appropriate medications available clinically to effectively control withdrawal symptoms.

Yesmywine Reveals: Lie 5 - Smoking and Drinking Go Hand in Hand

Some believe that a cigarette and a glass of wine bring immense joy. Especially when drinking gets exciting, and someone offers a cigarette nearby, even those without the habit might accept and light up, saying, "It's rare to be so happy today."

However, smoking while drinking harms both the liver and lungs. Nicotine in cigarettes weakens the effects of alcohol on the body, akin to being anesthetized, leading to unconsciously increasing alcohol consumption.

Yesmywine Reveals: Lie 6 - Strong Tea and Coffee Help Sober Up

Some believe strong tea or coffee helps sober up after drinking, but this is a misconception.

Drinking strong tea after alcohol, caffeine in tea quickly promotes diuresis, pushing acetaldehyde (a substance highly stimulating to the kidneys) prematurely into the kidneys, damaging them. Coffee's main component is caffeine, which stimulates the central nervous system and muscles; drinking coffee after alcohol makes the brain transition from extreme inhibition to extreme excitement, stimulating blood vessel expansion and accelerating blood circulation, significantly increasing cardiovascular burden, causing far more harm to the body than just drinking alcohol alone, possibly even triggering hypertension.

Yesmywine Reveals: Lie 7 - Mixing Alcohol with Beverages is Fashionable

Nowadays, mixing alcohol with beverages has become a fashionable way of drinking. Red wine with Sprite, whiskey with ice black tea, beer with cola... countless combinations exist. Since beverage-mixed alcohol has a lower concentration, it feels like drinking a beverage, thus many are fond of it.

But experts warn that carbonated beverages used to mix alcohol release carbon dioxide in the stomach, forcing alcohol to enter the small intestine quickly. The small intestine absorbs alcohol faster than the stomach, thus increasing harm.

Additionally, mixing drinks with alcohol may seem to dilute the alcohol, but it actually encourages people to drink more. Initially, drinkers feel like they're drinking a beverage and consume heavily. By the time they realize the effects of alcohol, they've already had too much.

Yesmywine Reveals: Lie 8 - Red Face Means Less Drunkenness

People who turn red after drinking are less likely to get drunk, a common excuse used at banquets to persuade others to drink. However, in reality, drunkenness has little relation to facial color.

Some believe turning red as if becoming Guan Yu is beneficial, thinking it indicates good blood circulation and swift alcohol breakdown, making one less prone to drunkenness. But experts state that drinking capacity isn't greatly related to facial expression, varying from person to person. In fact, many believe that people whose faces turn red don't get drunk easily because they are usually less persuaded to drink, hence consuming less. After drinking, they feel drowsy, sleep for 15-30 minutes, and look refreshed again, whereas those with pale faces often don't know their limits and drink excessively in high spirits.

Yesmywine Reveals: Lie 9 - Deep Emotions Require Quick Gulping

Some prefer quick drinking, frequently urging everyone to drink deeply if emotions are deep, sip lightly if shallow, or take one shot.

Actually, drinking speed should be slow rather than fast. Drinking quickly raises blood ethanol concentration rapidly, quickly leading to intoxication. Gradual drinking gives the body ample time to metabolize ethanol, reducing ethanol production, and making it harder to get drunk.

Yesmywine Reveals: Lie 10 - High-Alcohol Content Equals Better Kick

In everyday life, some always feel low-alcohol content drinks are mixed with pure water and lack strength, while high-alcohol content drinks, mostly grain-brewed, don't cause headaches after intoxication and feel more potent.

Yesmywine reveals: In fact, the higher the alcohol content, the more alcohol it contains. Since over 90% of alcohol is metabolized through the liver, excessive alcohol adds detoxification burdens to the liver. The higher the alcohol content, the greater the intake, the more severe the damage to the liver. Additionally, various enzymes and vitamins are required for liver metabolism of alcohol. The higher the alcohol content, the more enzymes and vitamins the body consumes.
