So he thought, rather than keeping the cow, he could sell it and buy a few sheep. He could slaughter one sheep to eat, and the rest could give birth to baby sheep, which when grown up, could be sold for more money.

His plan went smoothly, but after eating one sheep, the baby sheep took longer to be born than expected. Life became difficult again, and he couldn't resist eating another sheep. The poor man thought that this way would not work, so he decided to sell the sheep and buy some chickens instead. Chickens lay eggs faster, and life could improve immediately.

Again, his plan went as planned, but life didn't get better. When difficulties arose, he couldn't resist slaughtering chickens. Finally, when only one chicken was left, his dream of getting rich collapsed. He thought that since there was no hope of getting rich, he might as well sell the chicken, buy a pot of wine, and get drunk to forget his sorrows.

Spring came, and the rich man excitedly brought seeds, only to find the poor man lying drunk on the ground, with his house still as poor as ever. The rich man turned around and left, leaving the poor man to continue living in poverty.
