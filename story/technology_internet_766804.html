New Heights for the Series: Summary of Foreign Media Hands-on with Surface Pro 2

by anonymous on 2013-10-01 21:50:44

Following up on the previous hands-on summary of the Surface 2, let's now take a look at what changes have been made to the higher-end Surface Pro 2. Foreign media (The Verge, CNET, Engadget) have already conducted their first-hand trials.

Unlike the Surface 2, foreign media reports that when holding the Surface Pro 2, it is difficult to notice any significant changes in appearance; it still maintains the same all-black body as the previous generation, which may be related to its more business-oriented positioning. However, upon closer inspection, one can notice that the Microsoft logo on the back of the Surface Pro 2 has been changed to a simpler "Surface" wordmark.

After trying it out, Engadget expressed that the magnesium alloy body of the Surface Pro 2 gives a very sturdy feel, overall fitting its "Pro" positioning.

The internal structure of the Surface Pro 2 is entirely that of a complete PC, upgraded from the previous generation's Ivy Bridge to the latest Haswell architecture, equipped with a Core i5 1.6 GHz dual-core processor. Graphics performance has improved by 50%, and battery life has increased by 75%, meeting the requirement for all-day use (officially claimed to be approximately 9 hours).

According to the hands-on experience of foreign media, the Surface Pro 2 does not generate much heat during operation.

The Power Cover has a considerable thickness.

The Surface Pro specifications come in two versions: 4GB RAM models with 64/128GB storage, and 8GB RAM models with 256/512GB storage. During the launch event, Microsoft claimed that its performance surpasses 95% of the current laptops on the market.

The Surface Pro 2 shares many improvements from the Surface 2, such as a more accurate color 1080P screen (a CNET reporter on-site stated that the improved screen is "immediately noticeable"), a two-stage rear stand providing more support angles, making it more suitable for use on the lap.

Of course, as a full-function PC, compared to its ARM-based sibling, the thickness of the Surface Pro 2 has correspondingly increased to 13.5mm, weighing 907g. However, this is basically not much different from the previous generation. From the current hands-on situation, foreign media generally believe that although the weight of around 900g is not extremely heavy, for a tablet, it certainly isn't light either.

In terms of the system, the Surface Pro 2 will come pre-installed with the Windows 8.1 system to be released next month, starting at $899. Users purchasing this tablet PC will receive a year of free international long-distance calls provided by Skype, as well as 2 years of 200GB SkyDrive cloud storage space.

Notably, in addition to the improved Type Cover and Touch Cover mentioned in the Surface 2 article, Microsoft has also prepared two powerful accessories for the Surface Pro 2: the Power Cover and a dedicated docking station offering more ports.

As the name suggests, the Power Cover is a Type Cover with an integrated battery. Besides being an external keyboard, when combined with the Surface Pro 2, it can extend the battery life to 2.5 times that of the previous generation model.

The professional docking station turns the Surface Pro 2 into a professional-level computer, equipped with three USB 2.0 ports, one USB 3.0 port, audio output/input, Ethernet port, and mini Display video output port. According to official introductions, the dedicated docking station allows the Surface Pro 2 to output a resolution of up to 3840 x 2160.
