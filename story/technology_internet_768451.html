Twitter IPO makes 1,600 millionaires, boosting local real estate

by anonymous on 2013-11-17 18:27:40

If you're looking to buy property in the San Francisco Bay Area, then act fast. Twitter's IPO has already added over 1,600 new millionaires to this region.

Financial intelligence company PrivCo provided data indicating that Twitter's successful IPO means a large number of employees will become millionaires. PrivCo CEO Sam Hamadeh stated that while Twitter co-founders Ev Williams and Jack Dorsey join the "billionaire club" and CEO Dick Costolo's net worth exceeds $300 million, many regular Twitter employees will also suddenly become millionaires.

Hamadeh said, "This group will have significant influence, affecting real estate prices, new capital injections into innovative enterprises, and California's budget among other issues." Hamadeh estimates that although Twitter employees cannot sell their company shares for six months during the "lock-up period," the cashing out after the "lock-up period" ends will generate $479 million in income tax revenue for California.

Palo Alto real estate broker Ken DeLeon said that the impact of Twitter's IPO on the local real estate market is similar to Facebook's last year. DeLeon said, "Silicon Valley housing prices are supply-constrained. If liquidity is too strong, it naturally leads to rising house prices." DeLeon predicts that by the end of next year, San Francisco home prices will maintain double-digit percentage growth. Moreover, due to the reduction in vacant homes, house prices in other nearby areas will also rise.

DeLeon said, "It's like a concentric circle starting from Palo Alto. Considering numerous companies preparing for IPOs next year, East Bay and San Jose home prices in San Francisco will reach levels seen during the peak of 2007 next year."

Venture capitalist Venky Ganesan stated that Twitter's successful IPO will encourage more Silicon Valley companies to prepare for listing. In Ganesan's view, the more innovative companies go public, the more they can feed back into venture capital firms for further investments. For example, early-stage investor Benchmark Capital in Twitter will reap a $1.3 billion return on investment. Additionally, KPCB, which invested in Twitter at the end of 2011, may see its holdings increase to over $1 billion.

However, credit research and ratings company Rapid Ratings warned that Twitter's IPO might signal the formation of a new internet bubble, as the company has yet to turn a profit and its expenses continue to rise. Based on Friday's closing price, Twitter's market value exceeds $23 billion, rivaling its profitable competitor LinkedIn.

News website Quartz pointed out that due to strong demand for Twitter stock, besides the planned issuance of 70 million shares, Twitter's underwriters also exercised an over-allotment option for 10.5 million shares. This means that Twitter raised nearly $2.1 billion through its initial public offering.
