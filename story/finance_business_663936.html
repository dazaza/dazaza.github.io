In recent years, Midea Group has surpassed the old rankings in the industry, pursuing scale as the top priority. Although it has become a large-scale enterprise with annual revenue of hundreds of billions, it still lags behind its competitor Gree in some aspects. Recently, the massive layoffs at Midea Group have caused great impact, and some media even believe that the winter of the home appliance industry is approaching. In contrast, Gree's sales this year will reach 80 billion yuan, with profits reaching 5 billion yuan, maintaining an annual growth rate of 30% to 40%, which can be called "spring is coming".

"The most important thing for a company is flexibility. You need to know when the company should change. This road may initially be a golden path, but if you continue to walk on it, it will become a dead end. Past successes often turn into traps." The words spoken by He Xiangjian seem to confirm the current situation of Midea Group.

In the past 20 years, Midea's average annual growth rate was 40%. In the past 10 years, Midea grew from a billion to tens of billions, increasing tenfold. In 2010, the entire group's sales reached 110 billion yuan, becoming the second largest enterprise in China's home appliance industry after Haier. Despite his advanced age, He Xiangjian remains ambitious: "Rebuild another Midea within five years, and achieve sales of 200 billion yuan by 2015." He said confidently. At this time, He Xiangjian seemed to forget the failed Yunnan bus acquisition case in 2004 - that failure made him return to rationality and caution, no longer engaging in large-scale cross-sectoral and unrelated diversified acquisitions. Today, however, He Xiangjian once again gives people the impression of being conservative and restless.

An unnamed insider told reporters: "The domestic market is already very competitive, and foreign companies are also far-sighted. It is not suitable to adopt a divisional management model. One might say that in the People's Republic of China, the divisional system is synonymous with decentralized management."

At this point, home appliance companies experiencing layoff news are not just Midea. Industry insiders believe that there are many reasons for this, among which the sluggish macroeconomic environment is one of the reasons for the layoffs in the home appliance industry. Due to the continuous expansion of the global financial crisis and the continued deterioration of the economic situation, it has had a significant impact on China's product exports, leading to a contraction in the domestic market.

The most affected by this round of layoffs is Midea Daily Appliances Group, which includes various traditional home appliance products such as fans, rice cookers, and foot baths. Previously, the expansion speed was quite exaggerated, indicating that Midea Group's slimming transformation will, to a certain extent, break the original industrial structure of the enterprise.

Industry experts believe that on the one hand, Midea excessively pursues scale and the position of industry leader, causing problems such as bloated institutions and inefficiency; on the other hand, the divisional system implemented by Midea Group leads to parochialism and lays the seeds for management slackness.

Midea Group's large-scale layoffs mainly target its marketing subsidiaries and sales personnel, revealing the long-standing issue of Midea being a marketing-driven company.

Previously, Midea's goals were to expand scale, ensure profitability, and adjust structure. Now, they have changed to ensuring profitability and adjusting structure. Midea Group's voluntary layoffs and structural adjustments are seen by outside experts as a correct decision: "Midea needs to slow down," which will help Midea enter a virtuous cycle of development.

Starting from August this year, the leaders of Midea repeatedly emphasized transformation and upgrading in internal meetings, shifting from prioritizing scale growth to simultaneous development of scale and profit. In early December 2012, Midea Daily Appliances Group underwent reforms, reducing the originally 60+ wholly-owned sales subsidiaries to 11. The remaining institutions were reorganized into 50 branches. A person familiar with Midea explained that the marketing institutions of Midea Daily Appliances in various places were divided into wholly-owned sales companies and management-type marketing business centers, the latter also known as branches. After the changes, the management type replaced the wholly-owned type to become the mainstream of local marketing institutions. Some smaller sales subsidiaries streamlined 20-30 people, while larger ones streamlined 40-50 people.

This means that under the changes in external circumstances, the home appliance industry has ushered in a new round of reshuffling. Gree has taken advantage of this reshuffling due to several reasons: First, Gree focuses on a single product, having a very clear and rational understanding of the relationship between scale and profit; Second, Gree understands the importance of innovation, advocating industrial spirit, emphasizing making high-quality products, and achieving breakthroughs in product research and development and innovation; Third, although Gree builds its own sales channels, it mainly adopts a dealer operation model. Gree only has more than 100 salespeople nationwide, significantly saving on sales costs.

"Over the past two years, Midea Group has been striving for channel flattening, believing that this would reduce intermediate环节 costs, enhance the autonomy and initiative of dealers, and strengthen group control capabilities. However, the reality now shows that things have not gone as planned. Not only has it led to excessive staffing, insufficient tasks for employees, and high operating costs typical of large enterprises, workplace corruption is also unsurprising."家电 industry observer Liang Zhenpeng told reporters.

In the red home appliance sector, Haier, Midea, and Gree have formed a tripartite confrontation. Unlike Gree's specialization in air conditioners, Midea has chosen a diversified path. In 2003, Midea began large-scale diversification efforts. Since then, Midea has been like a child picking shells by the sea, continuously adding beautiful shells to its collection, becoming a common practice. One of its most classic merger cases is the 2003 acquisition of Hualing Group, incorporating Hualing Group's refrigerators and air conditioning businesses into Midea Electric (000527.SZ). In 2008, Midea acquired the well-known household brand Dadi E. To date, Midea has ventured into over 40 categories of home appliances, including air conditioners, refrigerators, rice cookers, and footbaths, making it a prime example of diversified product varieties.

After large-scale layoffs and structural adjustments, Midea Group's subsequent business profits will increase somewhat, which also means that its high-speed expansion has come to an end, and its annual sales growth rate of over 20% will be difficult to achieve.

Wang Xiaoran, who was laid off from Midea Group, found it hard to believe that three months before the layoffs, the company was still hiring on a large scale. This deeply revealed to her that although Midea Group gave itself a "cruel game," the emotions behind the layoffs were more about helplessness and urgency.

November each year is usually the busiest month for large enterprises to recruit fresh graduates. For many years, Midea Group has demonstrated its highly expansionary pace through recruitment. However, in November 2011, an unexpected event occurred: Midea Group was carrying out large-scale layoffs.

Insiders familiar with Midea and He Xiangjian revealed that in recent years, He Xiangjian has not directly participated in the daily management and operations of the company, but he requires his empowered professional managers to achieve annual performance growth rates, some as high as 70%. Professional managers resort to all kinds of methods to meet these rigid targets, even hiring excessive numbers of sales personnel and employing a sea of tactics to conduct aggressive marketing.

He Xiangjian of Midea Group once emphasized, "My goal is very clear, to make Midea bigger and stronger, to run a business, and running a business means making money, earning profits."

At the beginning of the year, during his inspection tour in the east, He Xiangjian surprisingly discovered that even regional sales managers had secretaries, drivers, and assistants. It is evident that the degree of institutional bloat cannot be underestimated.

He Xiangjian's goal of making Midea bigger and stronger was achieved step by step through mergers and marketing drive. However, Midea now faces a bipolar dilemma in its marketing channel architecture. Originally, Midea mainly relied on agents and distributors for distribution, which obviously involved the problem of layer-by-layer profit extraction from Midea products. Therefore, Midea changed its layered retail agency sales model to branch company unified management. For example, the cooling appliances group has more than 60 sales branch companies across the country. These companies were established using a joint venture model with local distributors, with Midea Group dispatching general managers and concentrating management authority in the group. The year-end performance of each sales branch is also determined by Midea Group.

As early as 1997, Midea Group implemented the divisional system reform, distinguishing divisions based on products. Divisions integrate R&D, procurement, production, sales, and service. The group became an investment, monitoring, and service center, forming an internal authorization model of "centralized power with reason, decentralized power in order, authorized according to regulations, and power used in moderation", promoting a new round of rapid growth for the enterprise. However, it also sowed the seeds for lax management. Due to the imperfect management system of Midea Group itself, central control ability has not yet formed and corporate culture lacks influence. Before implementing the divisional system, the group only managed sales and profits, and each division had relative autonomy in new product development and internal management.

Regarding the following questions, we contacted Zhou Heng, the brand manager of Midea Household Appliances Group. Zhou Heng stated that the group's departmental leaders had already communicated with the media regarding the layoffs, so he could not express his views. The person he referred to, Fang Hongbo, the president of Midea Electrical Appliances, responded that there was no "organized, targeted layoffs". However, Fang added that Midea Group does not rule out making corresponding adjustments to cope with the upcoming situation.

Earthquake

Under the pressure from the dominance of Suning and Gome in the home appliance retail chains and the low-profit encroachment of JD.com, as a manufacturer, Midea Group started building its own specialty stores in late 2009 to try to preserve higher profit margins. Currently, Midea has over 10,000 specialty stores, accounting for approximately 1/6 of the total 60,000 national home appliance sales outlets. Among home appliance manufacturers, none have stepped up channel construction as aggressively as Midea, including Gree, Haier, Skyworth, TCL, etc., all of whom are actively laying out their networks. However, unlike Gree, which leaves the management entirely to dealers and distributors with only around 100 actual salespeople nationwide, Midea's specialty stores require substantial additional labor costs and related expenses.

Changes in China's home appliance development policies have also had an impact, including the termination of air conditioner energy efficiency subsidy policies, the end of rural home appliance promotion policies, and the implementation of real estate control policies, all of which have exacerbated the downturn in the home appliance industry. However, industry experts believe that Midea Group's mass layoffs are not solely due to external factors but are more importantly caused by internal issues.
