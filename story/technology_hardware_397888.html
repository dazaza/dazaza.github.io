A 9.0-magnitude earthquake occurred 130 kilometers east of Sendai Port on the main island of Honshu in Japan, which then triggered a tsunami and hydrogen explosion of nuclear reactors. Japan and its neighboring countries were covered in the shadow of nuclear leakage and radiation, interwoven with panic and rumors. The disaster caused by this earthquake not only affected the people in the quake-stricken areas of Japan but also dealt a heavy blow to the gaming industry. However, the Japanese gaming industry still set an example. Multiple online games announced suspension of services to cope with the situation of power tension, and video games were either delayed or stopped from being released. The performance of players was also very touching. Some players gave up buying game consoles for donations. In addition, many games involved themes related to earthquakes and nuclear radiation. While playing games, we should also bear in mind the warnings of disasters. "Final Fantasy XIV" and other multiple online games announced suspension of services.

Due to the impact of the Great East Japan Earthquake, Japan's power supply is currently under tight conditions. Following Square Enix's announcement that "Final Fantasy XI" and "Final Fantasy XIV" would suspend services for about a week, Konami and Tecmo Koei also decided to respectively suspend the services of their operated online games such as "Metal Gear Solid Online", "Uncharted Waters Online", "Nobunaga's Ambition Online", and "Dynasty Warriors Online" for a week. French media analysis: Earthquake affects Asian stock market

According to French media reports: The strong earthquake and subsequent nuclear power plant crisis that occurred in Japan on March 11 have led to a comprehensive decline in the Asian stock market. Reports said that the Bank of Japan continues to increase capital injection to mitigate the negative impact of the earthquake on the economy. The Japanese government stated that the sharp drop in stocks caused by the earthquake was inevitable, but Japan's economic strength and abundant funds can ensure post-disaster reconstruction, and they believe the market will gradually stabilize. Multiple Japanese video games announce delays or stop selling

As several online games announced suspension of services to support power supply in the disaster area, video game developers also set examples, delaying the release or even stopping the sale of multiple games. For instance, new works of "Yakuza", "Dead or Alive Dimensions", "Monster Hunter Festa", "BlazBlue", "MotorStorm: Apocalypse", etc., were indefinitely postponed. The PS3 game "Fatal Frame IV" was directly discontinued and met the fate of being withdrawn from the market. Rumors say that the tsunami triggered by the earthquake washed away 120,000 PS3 units

The tsunami and other disasters triggered by the Japanese earthquake have caused damage to many enterprises. According to Bloomberg, Sony has ceased operations at six factories in the northeast region of Japan. All employees at the affected factories have been evacuated, and Sony is still assessing the impact of the earthquake and power outages on the company. It is currently unclear how much the loss will be. It is rumored that the massive tsunami triggered by the Japanese earthquake has washed away 120,000 PS3s and 5,600 Xbox 360s, just temporarily confirmed numbers. Earthquake DS 72 Hours

This game can be considered a textbook; the relief within 72 hours after an earthquake is a crucial time segment that determines the life or death of all trapped individuals.

How to calmly wait for rescue within these 72 hours, or escape and save yourself under possible conditions, is the main content brought to you by "Earthquake DS 72 Hours". The game will provide detailed explanations through 36 major items and 720 scenarios such as "the cause of the earthquake", "what to do when an earthquake occurs", "when an earthquake happens at home", "when an earthquake happens during class", "when an earthquake happens at the office", etc. It also includes more than 400 questions and 3 modes for players to test their "disaster prevention ability" when dealing with earthquakes.

Nuclear War Crisis

This is a sci-fi themed online multiplayer strategy game, with the theme of global nuclear war. The game simulates the terrifying nuclear war through holographic maps, where players act as commanders and must fight for their homeland.

The game capacity is very small, only dozens of MB, without dazzling graphics. What you face is a large global map. You determine the battlefield situation through radar and deploy troops for defense and attack.

Perhaps, politically speaking, the deaths in war are no different from what is shown in the game. You cannot see the scenes of death and injury, nor the ruins filled with smoke.

Absolutely Desperate City

The game mainly focuses on the background of a major earthquake, where players personally operate the protagonist to escape from the dangerous earthquake environment. Upon its release, it gained praise for its unique theme and content. Subsequently, sequels like "Absolutely Desperate City 2: Frozen Memories" for PS2 and "Absolutely Desperate City 3: Broken City and Her Song" for PSP were launched, gaining recognition from players. Fallout: New Vegas

The game tells the story of the Mojave Wasteland.

Continuing the story of the Fallout trilogy, the game reveals more behind-the-scenes stories. Interestingly, the story mainly takes place in New Vegas, formerly known as Las Vegas before the war. This once vibrant city has become a green oasis of human culture after the nuclear war.

Setting aside the gameplay experience the game itself brings to players, the stories behind Fallout are richer. The intertwining of politics, humanity, and morality is vividly displayed in the game. Why did the nuclear war happen? Why is the wasteland world like this? Where will humanity go? These are issues the game strives to explore.

Stalker: Shadow of Chernobyl

Different from actual history, the game is set in the Chernobyl nuclear power plant in 2006. An accident made the surrounding twenty square kilometers a dead zone, where mutant creatures and mysterious forces began to slaughter survivors. To prevent these mutated monsters from spreading to other areas, STALKER (Stalkers) fully armed themselves to carry out this dangerous mission. While exploring the radiation zone, they discovered unknown secrets, making the entire event more complicated.

The consequences of Chernobyl were severe, with more than eight tons of highly radioactive material leaking. Dust spread with the wind, causing many regions in Russia, Belarus, and Ukraine to suffer from nuclear radiation contamination. Thirty-one people died on the spot, and thousands of others suffered fatal or serious illnesses due to the long-term effects of radioactive materials.
