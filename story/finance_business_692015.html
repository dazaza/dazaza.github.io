A woman gave directions to a stranger, but was extorted 300 yuan by the man

by qq8826368 on 2012-03-05 21:00:36

Xinhua News Agency, reported by journalist Bin Xu. A kind-hearted citizen offered directions to a stranger only to be blackmailed in return. "I've only ever heard of drivers being blackmailed, but I didn't expect that doing a good deed would also lead to me being taken advantage of by scammers." On the 24th, Miss Zhou (a pseudonym), a citizen of Nanchang, was increasingly aggrieved after being extorted for 300 yuan.

Miss Zhou stated that at around 4 PM on the afternoon of the 19th, while she was preparing to take a bus home from the bus stop at the Provincial Chinese Medicine Hospital, a man approached her asking if there were any buses going to Yaohu. He claimed he had come to Nanchang to find a friend, but his wallet had been stolen, and he hoped to borrow a few yuan for the bus fare. "Because I have experienced the awkward situation of having my wallet stolen, I gave the man two yuan. At this point, the man noticed the fruit I was carrying and expressed a hope that I could give him some. Out of kindness, I gave him a banana, and thus the scam began," said Miss Zhou. After eating half a banana, the man clutched his stomach and fell to the ground, shouting that he had been poisoned and was in pain. Then, three middle-aged men appeared, surrounding her and asking what had happened, demanding that she send the man to the hospital.

"At that time, I thought since I don't know this person, why should I send him to the hospital, so I prepared to leave. But one of the men grabbed me and wouldn't let me go, saying that with a life on the line, I couldn't just walk away. I was scared out of my wits. Another man then said that if I didn't send him to the hospital, I had to give them money so they could send him instead," said Miss Zhou. Due to fear, in the end, she gave them 300 yuan and immediately left.
