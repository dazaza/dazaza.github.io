Symptoms of cervical spondylosis are not exclusive to the elderly.

by xiaozhou on 2008-12-17 10:36:53

In today's rapidly developing economy, many things that used to belong only to the elderly have now changed. Many young people think that cervical spondylosis is a "patent" of middle-aged and elderly people, but now the proportion of middle-aged and young people suffering from cervical spondylosis is increasing year by year, and even obvious symptoms of cervical spondylosis have appeared during adolescence. The reason for this is that maintaining the same posture for a long time and external force invasion are the "culprits" that induce spinal diseases. Due to the complex and changeable symptoms of cervical spondylosis, it has become one of the more difficult global common and frequent diseases. Orthopedic experts have found in spinal disease research that those who have the habit of "crossing their legs" or who work with their heads tilted or at a desk for a long time mostly have lateral bending or displacement of the transverse processes of the third, fourth, and fifth cervical vertebrae. "If the upper beam is not straight, the lower beam will be crooked", this displacement can also cause chest, waist, and pelvis displacement. Therefore, patients with cervical displacement must carefully check the entire spine, pelvis, hip joints, and knee joint changes, and after a clear diagnosis, simultaneous adjustment treatment should be carried out. The symptoms of cervical spondylosis are complex, so the symptoms of cervical spondylosis vary from person to person.

Experts say that the symptoms of cervical spondylosis are manifested in five types:

1. Radiculopathy type cervical spondylosis symptoms: nerve roots are compressed, resulting in numbness, weakness, and pain. Patients feel discomfort, numbness, and pain in both hands, weakened grip strength, sometimes even feeling powerless to hold a cup. In severe cases, the pain lasts all night, making it hard to sleep.

2. Vertebral artery type cervical spondylosis symptoms: when passing through the vertebral artery canal, due to the lesion of the cervical vertebrae, life expulsion changes, causing compression of the vertebral artery, leading to insufficient blood supply to the brain, resulting in dizziness, mild head confusion, severe dizziness, and serious cases experience a spinning sensation.

3. Sympathetic nerve type cervical spondylosis symptoms: clinical manifestations are relatively complex, common ones include headache, occipital pain, or blurred vision, photophobia, tearing, etc., and even elevated blood pressure, arrhythmia, palpitations, sometimes sweating all over.

4. Myelopathy type cervical spondylosis symptoms: patients feel weak in both feet or hands, walking feels like stepping on a sponge, deep one step shallow one step, many women will experience dizziness, which also belongs to this type.

5. Mixed type cervical spondylosis symptoms: the above two or more types of symptoms appear simultaneously, combined together.
