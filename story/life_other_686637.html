"Ten Major Traffic Bad Habits" Special Rectification Campaign Launched

On the first day of the rectification campaign, 361 cases of "forceful overtaking and lane-cutting" traffic bad habits were eliminated.

(Yang Zhiyong) In the previous stage of the "Ten Major Traffic Bad Habits" solicitation activity jointly organized by our newspaper and the traffic police department, citizens voted to select the "Ten Major Motor Vehicle Traffic Bad Habits" and the "Ten Major Non-Motorized Vehicles, Pedestrians, and Passengers" traffic bad habits. In response to the strongly reflected traffic bad habits by the public, in order to purify the urban traffic environment, Harbin Public Security Traffic Management Bureau decided that starting from the 28th, a special rectification campaign for the "Ten Major Traffic Bad Habits" would be carried out throughout the city. On the first day of the rectification, the traffic police department severely cracked down on the "forceful overtaking and lane-cutting" traffic bad habits ranked first among the "Ten Major Motor Vehicle Traffic Bad Habits", eliminating 361 related traffic violations such as failing to wait in line according to regulations, lane-cutting and aggressive driving, and violating overtaking rules.

At 8:40 am on the 28th, reporters went on the road with the duty traffic police to participate in the rectification action together. Around 8:40 am, the patrol police car drove to Fushun Street, 100 meters away from the Ansheng Street intersection, where other vehicles were queuing up for passage. A gray Songhua River minibus with the license plate Hei AZ1372 crossed the solid center line and drove into the wrong lane, cutting in front of the queue. At the same time, a Toyota sedan with the license plate Hei A49D99 also cut through the queue, crossed the solid center line, and drove into the wrong lane. After driving 20 meters, it drove onto the sidewalk and parked in front of a shop. The illegal acts of these two vehicles were captured by the duty traffic police on the spot. At 10:10 am, when the patrol police car passed through the channel under Hai Cheng Bridge on Ji Hong Street, all motor vehicles were queued up in two columns for orderly passage. A red Jetta car with the license plate Hei A51241 occupied the emergency lane to overtake the queue from the right side. At 11:37 am, when the patrol police car passed through the underground tunnel on Hongjun Street, a black Great Wall SUV with the license plate Hei AUD089 crossed the solid line and overtook the police car from the right side, which was caught on camera by the reporter for illegal overtaking and crossing the line. All the illegal information of the above vehicles will be recorded in the vehicle file by the traffic police department.

At 9:35 am, the reporter arrived at the intersection of Zhongshan Road, Zhongxuan Street, and Heping Road. On Zhongshan Road, a black sedan that had already entered the straight lane suddenly turned on the turn signal, weaving through two straight lanes and merging into the left-turn lane. This lane-changing behavior caused the queues in the two straight lanes at the intersection to stop. Duty traffic police Jiang Liguang stopped the car. The driver Lu Qingquan said, "I just remembered I needed to make a left turn when I reached the intersection, and indeed affected others." At around 10:40 am, similarly at this intersection, a black Passat sedan with the license plate Hei LM1930 was stopped by the traffic police after entering the straight lane on Heping Road and forcibly changing lanes to the left-turn lane. The driver Cao Jianzhong said, "I've been driving for only two months and am still not very skilled. I couldn't merge into the left-turn lane earlier. After entering the guiding line, I noticed there was an opening in the left-turn lane, so I rushed in." At 10:54 am, a No. 28 bus with the license plate Hei AF1355 was intercepted by the police for not waiting in line as required and weaving through the queue. The driver Liu Hong stated that the team leader had told him not to change lanes like this at intersections, but today he thought the traffic police hadn't noticed. The driver Zhu Changhai of the Hyundai sedan with the license plate Hei ANE216 was penalized by the traffic police for lane-cutting and aggressive driving. He said, "I used to drive this way frequently, never expecting such severe punishment. I'll have to be more careful next time." After the Nissan sedan with the license plate Hei ABP843 was stopped, the driver Yang Xinghai said that he often drives between Beijing and Harbin. Beijing strictly enforces penalties for lane-cutting while driving and frequently uses electronic traffic police for monitoring. He didn't expect Harbin to start managing this issue too. For the above-mentioned traffic violations, the police fined each violator 200 yuan and deducted 2 points.

According to the duty traffic police who introduced the situation to the reporter, the traffic violations corresponding to the "forceful overtaking and lane-cutting" traffic bad habits fall into two categories with eight items. One category is characterized by weaving through queues on both sides, borrowing lanes to overtake and aggressively competing for road space. The phenomenon of forcefully overtaking and lane-cutting at intersections is quite common. It usually happens after entering the guiding lane at an intersection and then trying to merge into another lane's queue, significantly affecting the order of vehicles passing through the intersection. The other category is characterized by violating overtaking regulations. These traffic bad habits occur both on sections of roads and at intersections. On road sections, some drivers overtake on bends or overtake from the right side of the motor vehicle; some even cross double solid lines to overtake in reverse direction. In the collection of the "Ten Major Traffic Bad Habits of Motor Vehicles," this type of traffic bad habit ranks first, fully demonstrating the level of public aversion to it. Aggressive overtaking and lane-cutting give the impression that these drivers lack quality and rule awareness, only considering their own convenience without regard for others' feelings. Forcefully cutting in front of other vehicles disrupts social fairness and has a negative guiding role, encouraging those who feel they are "losing out" to join the ranks of breaking the rules. This traffic bad habit is also highly likely to cause minor traffic accidents and frequently leads to traffic congestion. Once an accident occurs, the vehicles involved in forceful overtaking and lane-cutting will bear full responsibility for the accident. To effectively eliminate the "forceful overtaking and lane-cutting" traffic bad habits, the traffic police department will this time use both non-on-site and on-site methods for enforcement, obtaining evidence through law enforcement recorders, electronic traffic police, cameras, and other means, recording the illegal information in the vehicle file.
