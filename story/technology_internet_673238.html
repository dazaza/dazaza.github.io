What should patients with diabetic foot [http://www.tangzk.com/] eat?

(1) Carbohydrates: For patients with diabetic foot, ensure a daily intake of 200-350 grams of staple food, such as rice and noodles, which are rich in carbohydrates and are indispensable nutrients for diabetic foot patients.

(2) Proteins: The protein intake in the diet of diabetic foot patients should be higher than that of ordinary people. For example, a patient weighing 60 kilograms should consume 90-120 grams of protein daily to meet their needs.

(3) Fats: The fat intake in the diet of patients should be limited to, for example, a patient weighing 60 kilograms should consume less than 36 grams of fat daily.

(4) High-fiber diet: Daily fiber content should account for about 20% of the total. Commonly used in life are bran, wheat bran, corn husk, beet shavings, and algae plants made into cakes and other fiber foods, which are healthy foods for diabetic foot patients to prevent various complications.

(5) Can eat small amounts of fruit, the principle is [exchange grain for fruit]. For example, reduce staple food (25 grams of raw rice or 60-70 grams of cooked rice) daily and eat a certain amount of fruit (choose one): 75 grams of fresh jujubes, 100 grams of bananas, 100 grams of hawthorn, 125 grams of persimmons, 125 grams of fresh lychees, 150 grams of apples, 150 grams of kiwis, 175 grams of peaches, 200 grams of pears, 200 grams of pineapples, 200 grams of tangerines, 200 grams of grapes, 200 grams of cherries, 250 grams of apricots, 250 grams of Hami melons, 300 grams of strawberries, 350 grams of watermelons. Most of the sugar in these fruits is fructose, which is sweeter than sucrose but absorbed slower than glucose, raising blood sugar levels more gradually, while supplementing dietary fiber and vitamins, which is a beneficial supplement to dietary control.

What should patients with diabetic foot [http://www.tangzk.com/] not eat?

Diabetics and diabetic foot patients must strictly limit the intake of white sugar, brown sugar, honey, jam, sweet pastries, chocolate, sugary drinks, and sweet fruit juices because these foods contain a lot of glucose and sucrose, with high calories, which significantly increase blood sugar after absorption. There have been cases where diabetic patients consumed large amounts of cola or ice cream due to thirst, leading to diabetic ketoacidosis or hyperosmolar coma, endangering their lives.
