[Section Edited] [Plot Summary]

Michael Scofield finds himself in a hopeless situation — his brother Lincoln Burrows has been sentenced for murder and placed in the death row of Fox River State Penitentiary. Despite all evidence pointing to Lincoln as the murderer, Michael is convinced of his brother's innocence. With Lincoln's execution date drawing near and no other options available, Michael stages a bank robbery, gets arrested, and ends up in the same prison as Lincoln. As an architectural engineer who participated in the prison’s renovation, Michael designs a complete escape plan, entering the prison with the sole purpose of freeing Lincoln and clearing his name. Lincoln's former girlfriend, Veronica Donovan, serves as the defense lawyer in the murder case; she believes Lincoln was framed. During her continued investigation, Veronica discovers that this is far more than just a simple murder case — the victim's death involves the interests of many powerful figures, including the shadowy presence of the Vice President emerging from behind the scenes. As the investigation deepens, danger also approaches Veronica; witnesses are mysteriously killed one after another, Lincoln's family also encounters misfortune, and the mysterious person pursuing Veronica turns out to be a secret FBI agent. Meanwhile, Michael struggles in the prison to prepare the escape plan, encountering unexpected people and situations on the path to freedom.

Imprisonment as a company trap?

The mysterious company that has consistently persecuted the Scofield brothers extends its influence to Panama. Before Michael was sent to the Panamanian prison, the old man behind the company repeatedly mentioned the word "Sona" during conversations with his subordinates. Based on this, fans boldly speculate that Michael being imprisoned was actually a trap set by the company. This prison houses people of interest to the company, and they intend to use Michael's expertise in escaping to help free someone else. In return, Michael could obtain what he urgently needs —— a pardon from the U.S. government.
