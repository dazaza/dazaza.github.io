Salpingitis Brings About Bad Pregnancies

In our clinical work, we have found that the incidence of ectopic pregnancies has increased several times over the past few years. Approximately one out of every 50 pregnancies turns out to be an ectopic pregnancy. The biggest reason for this outcome is precisely the multiple terminations of unintended pregnancies. Director Zhang Xianting, a gynecological expert at Nanyang Ren'ai Hospital [http://www.nyrayy.com/], introduced that an ectopic pregnancy refers to the implantation of the fertilized egg at a location outside the uterus, also known as an extrauterine pregnancy, with the most common type being a tubal pregnancy.

The Best Painless Abortion Hospital

After discovering an unintended pregnancy, some women who plan to undergo surgery may already have gynecological infections but are eager to proceed with the operation without receiving effective treatment. As a result, during the surgery, there is a possibility of bacteria spreading upward, leading to inflammation and infection within the uterine cavity. The most common condition is salpingitis. Chronic salpingitis, when recurring, can cause the fallopian tube mucosa to crack and adhere together, narrowing the tube's diameter. This obstructs the normal movement of the fertilized egg, preventing it from reaching the uterine cavity on schedule and causing it to implant inside the fallopian tube, leading to an ectopic pregnancy.

Therefore, gynecological experts from the best painless abortion hospital in Nanyang, Nanyang Ren'ai Hospital, suggest that women who discover infections while intending to terminate an unintended pregnancy should actively receive treatment to eliminate the inflammation before proceeding with the surgery. Post-surgery, they should pay special attention to maintaining external genital hygiene, frequently washing and changing sanitary products, avoiding tub baths for half a month, strengthening nutrition, and enhancing immunity. This way, they can stay away from inflammatory troubles.
