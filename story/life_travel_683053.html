Some issues you can't miss about Huangshan tourism

by jj0505 on 2012-02-29 11:30:15

■ Accommodation Issues:

When traveling independently to Mount Huangshan, many people take the train. On the train, there are always people who specialize in "recruiting" customers, making a living by receiving kickbacks from hotels. Since you have not been to Huangshan and are unfamiliar with the local situation, it is easy to be deceived by their professional scamming techniques. Especially during national holidays such as Spring Festival, Labor Day, and National Day, two-star and above hotels in Huangshan are fully booked 15 days before the holiday, with the full room fees paid in advance. These "room scalpers" usually do not have access to good rooms, and if they do, they will certainly charge you exorbitantly.

■ Travel Preparation: Pack spare clothes, rain gear (the weather on Mount Huangshan changes unpredictably, and it tends to be cooler in the morning and evening), common medications, ID cards, and other necessary documents.

■ Transportation: There are direct trains and civil aviation flights to Mount Huangshan. If your train does not go directly to Huangshan City (Tunxi), we recommend taking a bus to reach Huangshan City (Tunxi). The bus station is only 300 meters away from the train station. Then, transfer to a luxury minibus for about 2 hours to reach the Mount Huangshan Scenic Area. The fare is 13 yuan per person, and buses depart every 20 minutes.

■ Safety Concerns: When climbing the mountain, wear flat rubber-soled shoes, cloth shoes, or hiking shoes. Do not wear high-heeled leather shoes. Although every step on Mount Huangshan offers breathtaking scenery, it is unsafe to look at the views while walking. Therefore, remember the saying: "Do not walk while viewing the scenery; do not view the scenery while walking." Taking photos on Mount Huangshan differs from taking photos in a park. In a park, both the photographer and the subject can easily adjust angles by stepping back a few steps. However, on Mount Huangshan, stepping back could lead to dangerous cliffs. Always check behind you before moving backward to avoid falling injuries, which can be easily avoided with attention. The paths on Mount Huangshan are steep and perilous. People with hypertension or heart disease should carry essential emergency medication and should not climb alone or venture into dangerous areas.

■ Guide Services: Mount Huangshan is a masterpiece of natural beauty combined with human culture. It incorporates the best features of famous mountains—Mount Tai’s grandeur, Mount Hua’s precipitousness, Mount Heng’s clouds, Mount Lu’s waterfalls, Mount Yandang’s ingenious rocks, and Mount Emei’s elegance. Every peak on Mount Huangshan has stones, every stone has pines, and every pine is unique. It is renowned worldwide for its four wonders: unique pines, bizarre rocks, sea of clouds, and hot springs. With two lakes, three waterfalls, sixteen springs, and twenty-four streams, all add luster to its beauty. Do you know the fascinating legends associated with these wonders?... Stories of celebrities, strange tales, interesting anecdotes, ancient Yue culture... We highly recommend booking a guide to make your trip to Mount Huangshan more enjoyable, pleasant, and filled with fun.

■ Communication Issues: The scenic area is within the communication network range, but China Unicom's signal is relatively weak. Additionally, there are IC card phones in each scenic spot, so it is advisable to bring an IC card. For any issues, please contact us promptly. There are also telephone fee payment points available for your convenience.

Cash Issues: There are branches of Bank of China, China Construction Bank, Industrial and Commercial Bank of China, and Agricultural Bank of China within the Mount Huangshan Scenic Area, so there is no need to carry too much cash.
