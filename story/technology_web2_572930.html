Mo Card, put all membership cards into your phone.

by ybcz0519 on 2011-11-20 19:10:27

I particularly like the concept of Mokard. I am already tired of going to some chain business and then having a salesperson recommend a membership card to me. I really don't want to take those small advantages because usually, you only get one benefit and never use that card again. There are also countless membership cards at home...

Here are some introductions:

1. Apply to become a merchant's mobile member anytime and anywhere without filling out an application form. The "shopping mall" function of Mokard allows you to view more than 700 brands in 11 major categories nationwide. Nearly 6,000 merchants across the country recruit mobile members through Mokard, no need to repeatedly fill out information, and enjoy various membership benefits at any time.

2. Personalized customization, selectively pay attention to merchant event information. This software emphasizes the personalization and lifestyle of each user. Users can set the Mokard function according to different merchant categories, regions, their preferences, etc. According to the settings and preferences of different users, Mokard will broadcast the real-time activity information of the merchants they are interested in to different users at any time and targetedly.

Mokard link: www.mokard.com

I haven't actually tried whether the IKEA card issued by Mokard can be accepted by IKEA cashiers. There are several key issues with this model: how many large chain enterprises will open their membership systems? Do they have the motivation to use the system provided by Mokard for promotions? Currently, it seems that there are limited types of coupons.

O2O is undoubtedly the most promising mobile internet business model. Last week, when I was chatting with a VC, they said they were most optimistic about people who have strong resources offline, rather than many people doing O2O now who are only strong online. I can't completely agree. I think O2O largely depends on the maturity and operation mode of the offline chain enterprises you want to cover. For example, if you want to do O2O for mall discounts, the problem you face is that some market promotion is handled by the mall marketing department, while others are handled by individual stores. Each department has different motivations and budgets for market promotion.

O2O also faces a problem: which comes first, the chicken or the egg. An interesting answer encountered is: chicks and eggs come first...
