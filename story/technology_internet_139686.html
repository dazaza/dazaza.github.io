The ten websites that the secretary often visits

by paomianqq on 2009-04-13 22:56:06

1. Ctrip Travel Network (www.ctrip.com)

Business trips are a regular part of corporate life, and an essential task for business trips is booking flights and hotels. As the personal secretary to the boss, the responsibility of booking tickets and hotels naturally falls on the secretary. If it's a department secretary, there are also many colleagues in the department who need the secretary's help with bookings. Therefore, Ctrip Travel Network, which helps secretaries quickly complete this important task, has almost become a must-visit website for secretaries.

Visit frequency: ★★★★★

2. My Secretary Network (www.mymishu.com)

As an industry website for secretaries, My Secretary Network is also one of the websites that secretaries frequently visit. This site provides many templates for work summaries, reports, official document writing, speech materials, and various other types of documents. Whether in government offices or enterprises, all kinds of materials are available, making it a place where secretaries often go to learn and find materials.

Visit frequency: ★★★★☆

3. Fanease Network (www.fanease.com)

Lunch is a headache for many office white-collar workers. Regular employees either bring their own meals or order takeout, but many companies provide free lunches, such as NetEase. Some companies (or departments) collectively order meals from fast-food restaurants, like Xunlei. In these companies that order meals collectively, the task of ordering usually falls on the shoulders of the secretary. Handling several, dozens, or even over a hundred fast food orders alone is not an easy task for the secretary. Fanease Network solves this problem for them. Fanease not only provides information about many fast-food restaurants but also allows for group-buying management. If there isn't a nearby menu available, users can upload their own menus.

Visit frequency: ★★★☆☆

4. QQ Zone (http://qzone.qq.com)

QQ Zone is a personal blog, with nearly as many users as QQ itself, around 300 million, including our beloved secretaries. They vent about their boss's bad mood, express their feelings for the day, upload a few cute photos of themselves. They love decorating their spaces beautifully and enjoy showing off in front of their QQ friends. Moreover, how to decorate their space is also a good topic of conversation during their leisure time.

Visit frequency: ★★★☆☆

5. Taobao (www.taobao.com)

Secretaries generally sit in front of computers all day and have more free time. Some secretaries, to make some extra money, usually open a shop on Taobao to sell small accessories and such. Of course, shopping for bargains on Taobao has become a habit for many internet users, and for secretaries who have access to the internet and plenty of free time, they will naturally often go there to look for cosmetics with a good price-to-performance ratio.

Visit frequency: ★★★☆☆

6. Mini Games (www.xiaoyouxi.com)

Similarly, because secretaries spend their days facing computers, have internet access, and have more free time, they need something to pass the time, but it shouldn't require too much effort. Mini Games perfectly meets this need. The site features educational and relaxing Flash mini-games that are simple to operate and don't require installation, making them very suitable for women to play. Some games even have special "boss keys" that allow quick screen switching at critical moments without affecting game progress. This site is loved by many secretaries.

Visit frequency: ★★★☆☆

7. China Entertainment Network (www.67.com)

Entertainment gossip is a shared interest among women, and some women can describe celebrity news as if reciting a family treasure list. China Entertainment Network is a dedicated site for reporting celebrity gossip, naturally becoming one of the websites that female secretaries frequently visit. However, due to the abundance of entertainment gossip sites, such as major portals, Mop, and Tianya Entertainment Gossip, it causes some diversion among secretaries, so the visit frequency can only be rated two stars.

Visit frequency: ★★☆☆☆

8. Rayli Women's Network (www.rayli.com.cn)

As China's largest urban women's portal site, it is also one of the websites that secretaries often visit. Clothing style, beauty care, emotional family, health cuisine... These are topics that women are most passionate about, and secretaries, being women, naturally share this characteristic. However, there are also many websites dedicated to women, such as Sohu Women, which cause some diversion, so the visit frequency is only two stars.

Visit frequency: ★★☆☆☆

9. Tianya Community (www.tianya.cn)

As the birthplace of controversies, Tianya Community is also one of the websites that secretaries frequently visit. Sections like Tianya Web Stories, Emotional World, Fashion Information, and True Me on Tianya are places where secretaries like to browse. Different people and personalities lead to slight differences in the sections visited. However, they generally just lurk without posting comments unless they encounter posts they are particularly interested in, in which case they might reply with a few words.

Visit frequency: ★☆☆☆☆

10. Xiaonei Network (www.xiaonei.com)

For secretaries who graduated in recent years, they usually used Xiaonei Network while in university. As the largest real-name SNS community in China, it retains a lot of friend information for its users, and secretaries also often go there to reconnect with old classmates, sharing the joys and sorrows of work or chatting about daily trivialities. However, as time since graduation extends, relationships with classmates gradually grow distant, and visits to Xiaonei decrease accordingly.

Visit frequency: ★☆☆☆☆
