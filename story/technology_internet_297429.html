Wang Xin, Wang Bo and four other individuals face public prosecution; anti-gambling efforts to continue in the new Chinese Super League season

by kersfe3f23 on 2009-12-07 08:41:31

Wang Xin, Wang Bo and five others are facing public prosecution as the anti-gambling campaign in Chinese football continues into the new season of the Chinese Super League (CSL).

The anti-gambling operation in football has been carried out with great intensity. Many people have wondered what impact this action would have on Chinese football and whether the CSL would be suspended like Malaysia's league was years ago. Based on information released recently, it is clear that the CSL will definitely take place next year, and the anti-gambling storm will continue until the start of the season.

The CSL Will Definitely Not Be Suspended

Will the CSL be paused? This question was raised by many during the early stages of the anti-gambling storm, but now it seems such concerns were unnecessary. Previously, Vice President of the Football Association Yang Yimin stated in Wuhan: "Suspend for a year? I haven't heard about that. The league just ended not long ago, and next year's schedule is being planned and formulated. The exact start date hasn't been determined yet."

In previous years, the FA wouldn't consider the schedule for the following year at this time, but when they really need to think about it, they'll find it to be a tricky issue. The number of teams participating in next year's league is still unknown. How to penalize Guangzhou Pharmaceutical, which is already confirmed to be involved in match-fixing, and how many teams will be excluded from the CSL due to its entry criteria are also unclear.

Despite these uncertainties, the fact remains that the 2010 CSL will definitely kick off on schedule. "The league will definitely not stop. Everyone now realizes that the league is the foundation of national football. If the league stops, then Chinese football will have no way out. Moreover, four teams have AFC Champions League tasks. If the league doesn't happen, what will they do?" According to insiders, the national team has heavy tasks next year, and the league is the best training ground for national players to maintain their form.

After the CSL Awards Ceremony on the 4th, people became even more convinced of one fact - the CSL will definitely take place as scheduled, and the purpose of the anti-gambling campaign is precisely to make the CSL healthier and more orderly.

Before the New Season, the Anti-Gambling Campaign Will Continue

The related investigation into the Guangyao and Shanxi Land Rover case has basically concluded. Next, the case will face conclusion of investigation and transfer to the procuratorate for public prosecution. At the time, Wang Xin, the leader of the Shanxi team, General Manager Wang Bo, Assistant Coach Ding Zhe, intermediaries You Ke-wei and Zhong Jianguo, and then-Vice President of Guangyao Yang Xu jointly planned and participated in this match-fixing case, and the six will face legal judgment. Next week, the public security department will announce new developments in the case, and the newly disclosed suspects will also be transferred to the procuratorate for public prosecution.

This anti-gambling storm has lasted for over a year, especially in the last two months, with frequent climaxes. So how long will this storm last? It is well known that match-fixing is not done by one person; if someone is involved, they will likely implicate others. Like this time, Wang Xin was caught, initially because he was wanted by Singaporean authorities, but subsequently led to the exposure of Wang Bo, Ding Zhe, and You Ke-wei. "This is a long-term and arduous struggle. At least before next year's league starts, this storm will continue." However, after the league starts, there will be certain technical difficulties in continuing the anti-gambling campaign. "If the league starts and someone involved gets taken away directly from the field, then things will get really serious!" Nevertheless, everyone believes that due to this anti-gambling storm, next year's league will definitely be cleaner. "Anyone who dares to take risks at this time will only bring trouble upon themselves!" Moreover, the anti-gambling campaign will continue in the future. The public security department will definitely continue to increase supervision over Chinese football, making sure that gambling and match-fixing are completely eradicated from Chinese football!
