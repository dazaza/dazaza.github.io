Amazon Cloud Computing Enters the Large Enterprise Market: Cooperation is Crucial

by geekzhang on 2013-05-16 17:20:56

Introduction: Reuters website published an article today stating that Amazon is gradually becoming a technology provider for global corporations. However, in this process, Amazon has found that it needs third-party consulting and software companies to help.The following is the full text of the article:The Challenge of the Large Enterprise MarketSince 2006, Amazon has been vigorously developing remote computing and remote storage IT services. In recent years, Amazon has collaborated with thousands of consulting and software companies, such as CapGemini SA and BMC Software, to attract global enterprise customers to use its cloud computing services.Amazon Web Services (AWS) has become very popular among startups. These startups need stable and cheap network services to accommodate their growth. However, large enterprises and government departments represent bigger business opportunities. Bernstein Research estimates that these enterprise customers spend over $300 billion annually on data centers.In the past five years, such market opportunities have driven Amazon's stock price up by more than three times. Some investors believe that Amazon's cloud computing service will become an integral part of businesses operating on the internet.The issue is that large enterprises have higher requirements compared to startups. They need better service guarantees, tighter tracking of costs, and special features developed to comply with regulatory requirements. Therefore, in trying to challenge corporate market giants like Oracle and IBM, and face competition from Microsoft and Google, Amazon's central strategy is: relying on intermediaries who provide solutions based on AWS services.Matt McIlwain, partner at Madrona Venture Group, said: "Enterprises need a complete meal, but Amazon only provides some ingredients, requiring users to cook themselves. Enterprises are willing to pay for the complete meal, and Amazon has realized this. This is why they established a partner network."Madrona is an investment firm led by Tom Alberg, a member of Amazon's board of directors, which has invested in several startups such as 2ndWatch, Apptio, and Skytap. These startups help other businesses use AWS and other cloud computing services. Terry Wise, global head of commercial development at AWS, stated that currently there are over 4,000 members in AWS's partner network, compared to fewer than 1,000 in the first quarter of 2012.Gartner research director Kyle Hilgendorf said: "If AWS did not have such a partner ecosystem, they would face difficulties in dealing with enterprises. I always receive calls from companies saying they want to use AWS. But they expect AWS to implement specific features. They usually look for AWS partners to solve these problems."A study conducted this year by Hilgendorf and other Gartner analysts showed that among companies fully embracing public cloud computing services, AWS meets the needs of 71% of them. Cloudability helps large companies like Adobe track cloud computing service expenditures. The company's CEO Matt Ellis said: "Partners can better understand enterprise customers and resolve existing issues."More Reliable ServiceAWS lacks in one area: service level agreements (SLAs), which specify the uptime of IT services. If the service is interrupted, the service provider must compensate the customer. And if the service frequently encounters problems, it risks being abandoned by customers.Consulting companies working with AWS say that AWS's SLAs do not always meet the needs of large enterprises. For example, according to calculations by Gartner and CapGemini, AWS's EC2 compute service SLA specifies no more than 22 minutes of downtime per month. However, Joe Coyle, CTO of CapGemini, pointed out that many enterprises require no more than 5 minutes of monthly downtime.To address this issue, CapGemini bundles AWS servers from different regions together. When one data center goes down, other data centers in the network continue to provide services. Coyle said: "We create new contracts for our clients. If we don't ask, clients may not even know they're using AWS." CapGemini currently helps nine companies use AWS services in this manner, including Greyhound Lines, an intercity bus operator.Other companies, such as the startup 2ndWatch, are also developing their own ways to reduce AWS downtime. AWS partners can also help enterprises retain their current systems and make them compatible with AWS. This has become a well-known strategy called AWS+1.AWS business leader Andy Jassy recently said that enterprises can now use both AWS and their own data centers simultaneously. Software companies like BMC and CA Inc. can help enterprises manage them through a unified control panel. The risk of this approach is that enterprises can easily switch to other cloud computing services. Indeed, many important customers have already formed long-term business relationships with Amazon's competitors.For instance, RightScale, a long-time AWS partner, helps enterprises run applications on multiple cloud platforms, including those from Microsoft, Google, HP, and Rackspace. RightScale's customers include Samsung SDS America under Samsung Electronics. Besides AWS, the company also uses Rackspace's OpenStack cloud platform. RightScale CEO Michael Crandell said: "Enterprises like cloud computing, but they don't want to rely solely on AWS."
