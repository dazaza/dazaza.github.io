People who have been to Bern, the fourth largest city in Switzerland, generally have two impressions of the people in this quiet city: orderly and punctual. However, on Monday, the fans around the hotel where the Argentine team stayed and near the Swiss Stadium overturned this impression, all because three-time Ballon d'Or winner Messi came. That day, 300 fans blocked the hotel entrance to welcome Messi, and the staff had to call the police for reinforcements to maintain order; later, 500 fans went to the Swiss Stadium, the Argentine training site, just to catch a glimpse of the young king.

Messi participated in this training session as the second captain (the first captain is Mascherano). As one of the team leaders, "Little Flea's" ability to deal with the media is not as dazzling as his ball skills, but he speaks plainly, although his words are bland, they are sincere. After arriving and resting briefly, Messi participated in the live talk show program of Radio Laredo, expressing his views on many issues. The key points include: willing to exchange his Ballon d'Or for the World Cup; Argentina's national team can replicate Barcelona's style; it is already very difficult for Barcelona to catch up with Real Madrid in La Liga...

At present, Messi's status in world football has been raised to the level of being mentioned alongside Pele and Maradona. The old coach of Switzerland's national team, Hitzfeld, said, "In my view, Pele, Maradona, and Cruyff rank in the top three, and Messi is fourth. However, Messi is still young, so this isn't the final ranking." The host threw this question to Messi, and after hearing it, the young king himself felt a bit embarrassed, repeatedly saying, "No, no, although many people say I am the best in the world, I don't think so. Of course, hearing this makes me feel satisfied, but when I started playing football, my dream was just to play in the top league and become a professional player. I never thought I would be like this today, so I am very content. As for the future, my personal goal is to maintain my level and improve myself as much as possible."

At the Ballon d'Or award ceremony, Brazilian King Pele, as a guest, presented the trophy to Messi and whispered a few sentences in his ear. What did he say? This is a question that many people are interested in, and Messi's answer was really embarrassing. He recalled, "Actually, I don't even remember what Pele said to me, because at the time I was so excited that all I could think about was finding my dad, mom, and family, wondering where they were." Compared with Pele and Maradona, Messi still lacks a World Cup trophy, and naturally acquiring it has become Messi's biggest dream. "Of course, I want to win the World Cup. Compared to group awards, individual honors are insignificant. If I can, I would trade my Ballon d'Or for the World Cup. If I could win the World Cup in Brazil, it would be such a beautiful thing, and I even dream about it like that," Messi said.

To achieve something in Brazil, Argentina's first priority is to establish its style, and Messi's suggestion is to learn from Barcelona. "It's hard to compare clubs with national teams, but I think Argentina can still learn some things from Barcelona." What specifically should be learned? "Learn Barcelona's attitude towards the ball, the use of players each fulfilling their duties, and the style of immediately counter-pressing after losing the ball. But I'm not the coach of the national team, as a player, I need to follow all the coach's instructions and execute them as perfectly as possible."

Currently, in La Liga, Barcelona is already 10 points behind Real Madrid. On this issue, Messi has an objective view, "Realistically speaking, it will be very difficult for Barcelona to catch up with Real Madrid. The number of points we are behind is a bit too much, and Real Madrid has also proven how excellent and powerful they are. However, we won't give up either. We will fight until the last moment, and I still hope to get closer."

Mourinho and Pepe? "We know Mourinho's coaching style, so there's no need to pay too much attention to him. Instead, we should focus more on Real Madrid because they are currently very strong, and the players are very capable. Pepe? I never said that thing, that's all. Let bygones be bygones, I have nothing else to say about Pepe, and I don't like talking behind other people's backs." (Liu Hui)
