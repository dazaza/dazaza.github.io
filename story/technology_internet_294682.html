by dnfmeimei on 2009-12-06 01:02:46

China Radio International, Beijing, December 5th (reporter Ye Feng) According to the report of "CNR News" at 19:18, since winter began, Beijing has experienced multiple heavy fog weather conditions, which have affected transportation in the city. This morning, the Beijing Traffic Management Bureau announced some alternative routes for highways that are prone to be closed due to fog.

For the Beijing section of the Jing Shen Expressway, if temporary traffic control measures are implemented at the Bailu Toll Station, vehicles can detour via National Highway 102. That is, by taking the Jing Tong Expressway and the Jing Ha Expressway to reach Sanhe City, Hebei Province, and then get on National Highway 102. For the Beijing-Tianjin-Tanggu Expressway, if a diversion measure is taken at Dayangfang Bridge, vehicles can detour via National Highway 104. That is, by getting on National Highway 104 from Yongdingmen Bridge on the Second Ring Road South, Muxiyuan Bridge on the Third Ring Road South, Dahongmen Bridge on the Fourth Ring Road South, Demao Bridge on the Fifth Ring Road South, or Nandahongmen Bridge on the Sixth Ring Road South.

If the entire line of the Beijing-Tianjin Expressway is closed, vehicles can take the Jing Tong Expressway and then get on National Highway 103. If the entire line of the Jing Shi Expressway is closed, vehicles can detour via National Highway 107, getting on the auxiliary road of the Jing Shi Expressway from Liuliqiao on the Third Ring Road West or Yeguangzhuang Bridge on the Fourth Ring Road West, and reaching Dujiakan Roundabout. Alternatively, vehicles can take Yan Stone Road from Yamenkou Bridge and then head south along the Fifth Ring Road West, exiting at the Jing Zhou Road exit to reach Dujiakan Roundabout or enter National Highway 107 heading south towards Fangshan District.

If the outbound direction of the Jing Cheng Expressway is closed, vehicles can detour via National Highway 101, getting on Jing Shun Road from Sanyuan Bridge on the Third Ring Road East, Siyuan Bridge on the Fourth Ring Road East, Wuyuan Bridge on the Fifth Ring Road East, or Beigao Bridge on the Airport Expressway.
