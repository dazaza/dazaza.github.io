Nourishing life in spring

1. Mental Adjustment:

Traditional Chinese health preservation practitioners believe that the liver corresponds to wood in the five elements, and is associated with spring. It governs growth and development, prefers smoothness, and dislikes depression. To make the liver energy adapt to the natural energy of spring, it's crucial to first adjust emotional states. Humans experience seven different emotional changes: joy, anger, worry, thoughtfulness, sadness, fear, and surprise. Under normal circumstances, these seven emotions will not cause disease. However, excessive contemplation or unresolved worries can affect the liver's regulation, the rise of yang energy, leading to dysfunction of internal organs, and consequently diseases arise. Statistics show that the incidence rate of mental illness in spring is higher than other seasons, and patients with liver disease or a history of hypertension often worsen. Therefore, it's especially important to focus on mental adjustment in spring, maintaining an open mind and optimistic mood to ensure smooth liver energy and harmonious blood circulation, achieving the purpose of preventing and treating diseases for health.

Practicing liver-nourishing techniques regularly to achieve the effects of relieving liver fire and balancing yin and yang. Such as yoga, qigong, Tai Chi, etc.

2. Exercise Health Preservation:

In spring, one should follow nature’s rhythm and engage in more physical activities. This is an essential health preservation method. Persisting in sports exercises in spring enhances human immunity, making it less likely to get sick. Choose suitable exercise projects based on individual physical condition.

3. Dietary Nourishment:

In spring, one should eat more foods that tonify yang energy. Scallions, garlic, and leeks are good foods for nourishing yang, especially leeks. Although available year-round, they taste best in spring. Leeks have a warming nature, which most benefits human yang energy. Additionally, in spring, one should eat less sour and more sweet foods. Doing so replenishes the spleen and stomach qi. Jujubes have a sweet and neutral nature, suitable for spring consumption, being excellent for nourishing blood vessels and strengthening the spleen and stomach.

4. Disease Prevention Health Preservation:

1. Dressing "should be warm in spring." As spring transitions from cold to warmth, but cold air still lingers, it's better to wear more clothes, even if it's a bit warm.

2. Sleep "should start early." As winter passes into spring, temperatures begin to rise, and the air gradually becomes humid. At this time, the skin and brain receive increased blood supply, sweat gland secretion also increases, producing a calming and sleep-inducing effect on the body, easily causing drowsiness, prompting some elderly people to like sleeping in. Sleeping in is harmful to health. Long lying damages qi, so spring should see early rising. To wake up early, one must go to bed appropriately earlier.

3. Diet "should be warm." In spring, diet should be warm, avoid raw and cold foods, consume more noodles, which helps support yang energy, making it flourish. Greedily eating cold food in spring can easily harm the stomach, leading to gastrointestinal diseases in summer.

4. Morning exercise "should produce little sweat." In spring, human yang energy tends to vent more easily, at this time, it's necessary to protect the yang energy within the body, avoiding excessive consumption. Spring is suitable for small amounts of activity; too much exercise leads to excessive sweating.

Five Principles of Spring Health Preservation:

Spring is the beginning of the four seasons, marking the renewal of all things. As spring returns to the earth, yang energy rises, ice and snow melt, birds sing, flowers bloom, and nature presents a vibrant scene of prosperity. The ancients valued following the timing for nurturing, adhering to principles. Therefore, in spring, health preservation, whether in terms of diet and daily routine, or in spirit and exercise, must align with the characteristics of spring yang energy rising and all things sprouting, focusing on the word "life".

Living Adjustment: With the warming of spring, human yang energy gradually moves outward. At this time, the skin expands, peripheral blood supply increases, sweat gland secretion also increases, and the load on various organs grows. Meanwhile, the central nervous system experiences a calming and sleep-inducing effect, making limbs feel tired. Therefore, there is a saying of "sleeping through the dawn sounds, everywhere hearing bird songs." However, sleeping in is not beneficial for yang energy rising. How to adapt to this climate change? In terms of living habits, one should sleep late and rise early, walk leisurely outdoors, integrate with nature, and respond to the growing energy of spring.

Spring weather changes frequently, with sudden warmth followed by cold. During this time, the human skin surface is loose, reducing resistance to external pathogens. Therefore, in spring, one should not hastily remove cotton clothing, and the elderly, due to their weak muscles, should be especially cautious when removing winter clothes.

Dietary Cultivation: Seasonal dietary cultivation usually adjusts according to the five flavors of food and seasonal differences. In spring, yang energy rises, and human metabolism also becomes vigorous. The diet should select pungent, sweet, or slightly warm items, avoiding overly sour, spicy, or hot foods, to prevent liver yang energy from rising excessively and harming the spleen, which is detrimental to health. Sour flavor enters the liver, its nature being contractive, which is not conducive to yang energy rising and liver qi regulating. Eating too much sour food can make liver qi overly strong, suppressing the digestive function of the spleen and stomach. Hence, "The Theory of Life Preservation" says: "During spring, food should reduce sourness and increase sweetness to nourish the spleen."

Spring diet should avoid raw and greasy foods, consuming vitamin B-rich foods and fresh vegetables. Modern medical research believes that overeating and lack of vitamin B are among the reasons for feeling sleepy in spring.

Spiritual Cultivation: Human spiritual activities must conform to the seasonal climate changes to maintain inner peace. So-called "following the four seasons and expressing emotions." The time when the human body is most affected by seasons is during transitional periods, especially the spring between winter and spring. Some people cannot adapt to the climatic changes of spring. According to relevant medical records, the number of people visiting psychiatric hospitals increases in spring, and the suicide rate is also high. Traditional Chinese medicine believes that spring belongs to wood, heaven and earth's qi grow, all things flourish, emotions should be internally guarded, anger must be strictly avoided, and depression must be particularly prohibited. One should have a broad mind, be open and optimistic, and possess a "birth without killing, giving without taking, rewarding without punishing" mental state. Thought patterns should be relaxed and natural, full of vitality, to make emotions broad. This way, many crimes and mental illnesses can be avoided.

Exercise Protection: In the cold winter and spring, human metabolism is low, storing essence to transform into qi, with varying degrees of yang energy decline. Therefore, after entering spring, one should strengthen movement to adapt to the characteristics of yang energy rising. One can run, walk, fly kites... in the fresh air of nature, allowing the body to renew itself, stretch muscles and bones, laying a solid physical foundation for work and study throughout the year. Practice has proven that those who exercise in spring have strong disease resistance, agile thinking, do not easily get tired, and have high efficiency in handling affairs.

Health Care and Disease Prevention: In early spring, as the weather warms, pathogenic microorganisms such as bacteria and viruses grow and multiply. Therefore, infectious diseases like flu, measles, meningitis, scarlet fever, pneumonia, etc., occur frequently. Moreover, in spring, flowers bloom, and pollen spreads with the wind, making allergic asthma patients prone to attacks. Prevention methods include: 1. Health exercises to enhance disease resistance; 2. Maintain hygiene, eliminate pests and disease sources; 3. Keep indoor air fresh, open windows frequently for ventilation. Second, indoor disinfection, use 5 ml of vinegar per cubic meter, add water twice as much, close the windows, heat and fumigate twice a week.

Several principles for seniors' spring health preservation:

Although spring is not as hot as summer and warmer than winter, it alternates between cold and warm, unstable, especially for the elderly, making spring health preservation very important. What are the principles of spring health preservation?

To help seniors spend a warm and comfortable spring safely, here are five recommendations:

1. Prevent and treat diseases: In spring, as temperatures rise, it favors the growth and reproduction of bacteria, viruses, and other microorganisms. Due to unpredictable weather changes, seniors' health is threatened. Therefore, seniors need to enhance their self-prevention awareness, achieving early prevention when healthy and early treatment when ill.

It's essential to prevent diseases, especially strokes. Simultaneously, manage existing conditions well, don't forget your illnesses, doctor's advice, or timely medication.

2. Moderate Exercise: Spring is the perfect season for seniors to leave home and stay fit. Regular outdoor exercise allows breathing fresh air, purging internal toxins, enhancing heart and lung functions, killing bacteria on the skin, and boosting the immune system.

Seniors exercising should first keep it moderate, avoiding excessive exertion. Secondly, combine dynamic and static exercises, maintaining energy without over-fatigue. Thirdly, choose suitable exercises based on personal conditions, avoiding difficult, large-scale, or risky movements. Fourthly, pay attention to keeping warm, as spring weather changes unpredictably, and seniors need extra warmth, especially around UASB anaerobic reactors.

3. Scientific Diet: In spring, life thrives, and the body's demand for nutrients increases. Ensuring seniors have sufficient heat energy is crucial. For this, seniors need good dietary habits and scientific methods. Specifically, pay attention to:

Ensuring enough heat while appropriately reducing food intake;

Balanced diet, not favoring fine or coarse foods, eating more millet, corn, sweet potatoes, etc.;

Protein should be "refined," fat minimal, eating high-protein foods like beans, fish, poultry eggs, lean meat, etc.;

Control salt intake, avoid greasy, sour, and cold foods, eat more vegetables and fruits.

4. Cold Protection: In spring, it's warm yet chilly, with fluctuating temperatures. If dressed too lightly or inadequately protected, it's easy to catch a cold.

Especially for seniors whose physiological functions decline, adapting to climate changes is harder. Clothing should be adjusted according to personal body conditions and weather changes, being loose, light, and snug against the body for warmth.

5. Living Habits Beneficial to the Body: As winter turns to spring, seniors' regulatory centers and bodily functions remain sluggish. At this time, seniors should:

Keep indoor air fresh, ventilating daily;

Maintain dry living quarters, washing and sun-drying bedding regularly;

Ensure harmonious interior colors and peaceful ambiance, keeping appropriate flowers;

Soak feet in warm water before bed, massaging both feet.

Additionally, seniors may feel drowsy in spring, a physiological phenomenon that needn't be feared, as it will naturally disappear once adjusted. However, seniors should avoid "oversleeping," as excessive sleep slows metabolism, disrupts blood circulation, and hinders toxin removal.
