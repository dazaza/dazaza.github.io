Maradona: If I were white, I would have been like Messi

by qinglai888 on 2009-12-14 10:46:34

Maradona and Juventus fans kill Inter Milan's miracle: if he were white, he would be like Messi.

Sina Sports -- Compared to the Meazza Stadium 3 days ago, Balotelli was relatively calm in Bergamo. However, even so, Balotelli’s status change in Mourinho's mind after starting consecutively twice within three days for Inter Milan is quite obvious.

This isn't the first time this season that Balotelli has started consecutively; in the 8th and 9th rounds of the league, he also started consecutively and performed well. However, these two different consecutive starts naturally have different values. With the 2-0 victory over Rubin Kazan in the midweek, Balotelli scored one goal and provided one assist, single-handedly sending Inter Milan into the round of 16. For Inter Milan, this 19-year-old Italian teenager is no longer just a "beneficial supplement"; he is a player who can make a difference.

The blue stadium of Atalanta is undoubtedly a place worth remembering for Balotelli. In April 2008, he completed his first goal in Serie A here and assisted Vieira in scoring another goal. This time returning here, Mourinho placed him and Eto'o on both wings, somewhat limiting Balotelli's effectiveness. However, the brilliance of a genius can be covered up, but it can never be extinguished. Just at the start of the game, Balotelli caused a foul by Gori with his personal breakthrough. Then in the 3rd minute, his cross pass after moving wide helped Maicon complete a shot. The most spectacular performance came in the 30th minute when he dribbled past a defender. Sneijder made a direct pass, Balotelli controlled the ball, turned around smoothly with a right-footed feint, leaving Padoin behind him almost instantly.

Sneijder was sent off with a red card in the 64th minute. To maintain balance in the formation, Mourinho immediately substituted Balotelli with Stanislovic, ensuring balance in the midfield and defense. Balotelli obviously wasn't prepared for this substitution and left the field unhappily under the persuasion of Materazzi and the urging of referee Rizzoli.

Balotelli's so-called "character issues" appeared again. Actually, for a 19-year-old child, such emotions are completely understandable. But in Lippi's view, this is the reason why Balotelli cannot enter his Italian national team: "I need to keep the atmosphere of the Italian team peaceful, please respect my choice." In Lippi's selection, Pepe and Quagliarella are more qualified than Balotelli to be selected for the Italian team because they can make the "atmosphere of the Italian team peaceful." Such an attitude is indeed a strange phenomenon in the Italian team. The standard for promoting new players is not ability but character. Isn't one of Lippi's jobs to integrate players with personality into the team? He avoids such issues. Of course, Lippi led the Italian team to win the 2006 World Cup championship, he is an authority, and no one can refute him. He is the Pope of Italian football; you can pray to him, but he will give you nothing in return.

Juventus legend Altafini said that if Lippi does not call up Balotelli, then he might make a big mistake. Altafini's reasoning is: "Balotelli has all the conditions to join the national team: he has technique, talent, and stands out for his personality."

But Altafini ignored one issue: Balotelli is black. Extreme Juventus fans say: "Italians are not black." They spread this rhetoric even when facing Bordeaux or Bayern Munich. A sentence can be found to compare Balotelli's difficulties: Moggi said: "If Melo were Italian, then he would only be worth 6 million." If Balotelli were white, would Lippi still reject him so decisively? Marchisio's goal in the Derby d'Italia was called "having Messi's skills" by the Turin Sport. Fortunately, Juventus didn't get past Bayern, otherwise, Marchisio, who provided one assist, would have been blown up to somewhere else. What Marchisio achieved, Balotelli had already done earlier. In the 2008-09 Champions League round of 16 away against Manchester United, Balotelli challenged O'Shea six times in the first 45 minutes, causing the Irishman to commit five fouls. If he were white, would Lippi still turn a blind eye?

Since Weah in 1995, the Ballon d'Or has never been awarded to a black player again, although Ronaldinho has never expressed his opinion on his skin color, and Ronaldo said in 2009 that he was black. However, even if Balotelli has the personal talent to rival Messi, he still finds it hard to shake off the prejudice brought by his skin color in Italy. Only a very few people outside the mainstream center dare to criticize the issue of prejudice. Former Perugia boss Cechi-Gori said: "Lippi is an arrogant lucky guy, he insists on using Lerrotaile, but never gives Materazzi another chance." Balotelli and Materazzi have the same experience, but Materazzi is still luckier than Balotelli. (Giotto)
