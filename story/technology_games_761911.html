The Unspoken Rules of the App Store Exposed Again: Games Bypass Reviews to Make Money疯狂

by geekzhang on 2013-05-15 10:35:45

By Gu XiaoboAfter a week of frenzied chart manipulation (related report: "Chart Manipulators Go All In, Chinese App Store Rankings Get Bloodbath"), the rankings in the Chinese App Store have returned to their usual calm. The major manipulators are once again accepting orders.The industry has become accustomed to this. Due to Apple's obvious channel monopoly advantage, chart manipulation has become an unfortunate necessity for many developers. However, chart manipulation is not the only hidden rule of the App Store.Games Self-Clone and Easily Get Listed on App StoreTech NetEase observed today’s free chart of the App Store and found a simulation business game called "Big Tycoon" that was highly suspicious. From 4 PM to 5 PM on May 6th, within just one hour, it climbed from rank 296 to rank 5, and by the evening of May 12th, it ranked sixth. Moreover, all 3000 reviews were five-star positive comments, with content being simply "fun" or "good".However, this wasn't the key point. The reporter found this application familiar, then discovered through a search that this app was very similar to another game called "Tycoon Legend." The pictures, content summaries, and even update records in the details were exactly the same. The two apps were submitted by two individual accounts.Subsequently, the reporter found another game called "Business Life" on the App Store that was quite similar to these two games, also submitted by an individual account. The reporter searched and found the developer of this game, Zhuhai Qirun Network Technology, whose official Weibo account replied that these three games were all developed by their company.At the same time, these three games appeared in the TOP50 of the best-selling ranking on the App Store and simultaneously in the TOP20 list of simulation business games. Games like "Immortal Path" and "Forgotten Immortal," which rank in the top teens on the best-seller list, currently have monthly revenue exceeding 20 million yuan on iOS. Occupying three seats in the TOP20, the income from these games can be imagined.Similar applications with changed names and icons can be found in abundance on the App Store, including "Sailing Domination - Arena Edition" and "Sailing Domination for iPhone," "Magic Cards" and "Fantasy Magic Cards." A game called "Basketball Manager" was submitted to the App Store four times under different accounts.The App Store is known for its strict review process, and last August even introduced new policies to reject applications with similar icons. So why can identical games be smoothly submitted?Developer: Change the Name and Submit as Many as You WantA game developer named Liu Yang revealed the reasons behind this to Tech NetEase.Liu Yang said that the App Store only cares whether the content meets its regulations and does not care whether it has been submitted before. To repeatedly submit, just change the name or add a suffix such as "iPhone Edition," "Free Edition," or "Deluxe Edition." Apple will consider them as different versions and allow submission. If that doesn't work, just change the name. As Liu Yang put it, you can submit as many as you want.Upon reviewing the "App Store Review Guidelines" published by Apple, it explains under 2.11: Programs that duplicate existing programs on the App Store may be rejected, especially if there are numerous instances.The word "may" could be an elastic policy by Apple for different platform versions, but it has been exploited by some developers, becoming a means to make money and manipulate users.The reporter downloaded and tested a few of these games and found that the servers of these games were interconnected, allowing login with the same account. On platforms like Weibo and Baidu Tieba, there were also users questioning this issue.The benefits of doing so are clear. By pushing up three applications, more users can be attracted to play. In fact, all three games lead to the same server, where user volume and in-app purchase transactions are shared among the three games, making it naturally faster to earn money than relying on a single game.Another developer who wished to remain anonymous stated that the reason Apple is unwilling to deal with these applications is not only because some versions are hard to distinguish, but also because Apple earns a 30% commission from games, and naturally wants them to earn more.Some of these games are self-repeated developments by developers, while others are for the sake of channelers and agents' share. Zhu Shunyan, COO of UCWeb, said that currently, all major channels on the Apple platform lead to the corresponding pages on the App Store. Users download and play the games, and developers and channelers cannot distinguish which users come from them, so billing is difficult. Currently, the UC9Game gaming platform focuses mainly on Android games.Apple is actually the instigator. If Apple had a good way to solve this problem, we would also be willing to try the iOS platform, said Zhu Shunyan.When Tech NetEase discussed this issue with some industry insiders, more people expressed that they had become accustomed to it. Nowadays, the competition among mobile game channels is extremely fierce, and there are many developers. If you don’t occupy the position, someone else will, said Zhang Minghao, analyst at Matrix Partners.On the free charts and category charts of the Chinese App Store, many games have already occupied the rankings through self-cloning and chart manipulation. The hidden rules of mobile game chart manipulation on the iOS platform have been disrupted. With limited position resources, the vicious competition among developers, channelers, and chart manipulation companies becomes increasingly severe.On the iOS platform, developers' revenue must first give 30% to Apple, then split 50-50 with the channelers, and subtract various expenses, leaving very little profit. Song Xiaofei, CEO of MoYou Century, told Tech NetEase that currently, mobile games require significant investment in research, operation, and promotion. After deducting all costs, if a mobile game's monthly revenue doesn't reach 20 million yuan, it’s hard to break even.Whether from a capital perspective or from the developers' standpoint, the current activity level of the mobile game industry far exceeds that of PC games and web games. However, excessive competition has led to various malicious developments, reaching a point where it seems normal. For the gaming industry, returning to product-driven development can bring longer-lasting vitality. Perhaps the industry is waiting for a reshuffle, or perhaps it's already happening.
