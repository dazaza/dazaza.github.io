1. When boiling cracked eggs, the egg white will seep through the crack into the water; if you boil the cracked egg in salt water, the egg white won't leak out.

2. When boiling eggs, adding a bit of salt and vinegar prevents the shell from cracking easily; if the shell cracks, boiling it in salt water prevents the egg white from leaking out. When steaming leftover rice from the previous day, adding a little salt water to the water can remove any strange odors.

3. Vegetable leaves often have some small pests that are somewhat troublesome to wash off. If you soak the vegetables in salt water first before washing, they become much cleaner.

4. When killing a duck, adding a spoonful of salt to the hot water used for scalding the feathers can prevent the duck skin from being damaged.

5. For bitter melon, radishes, or other vegetables with a bitter or astringent taste, after slicing them, add a small amount of salt, let the juice drain, and then cook them to reduce the bitter and astringent flavors.

6. Washing fresh fish in salt water can remove the muddy odor, making the flavor more fresh and delicious.

7. For freshly caught fish that you don't plan to eat immediately, rub the inside and outside of the fish with salt, wait an hour, and then fry it in the pan as usual to retain its freshness.

8. Before frying fish or other foods, sprinkle a bit of salt in the pan to prevent the oil from splattering outside the pan.

9. Adding a bit of salt when making steamed buns makes them soft and full of air pockets.

10. To keep vinegar from forming a film over time, add a little salt to the vinegar.

11. For new glassware, boiling it in salt water before use makes it less likely to crack when exposed to cold temperatures.

12. Bathing in water with a small amount of salt added can help treat allergic dermatitis.

13. Salting eggplant slices for ten minutes before stir-frying saves oil and improves the taste.

14. When blanching green vegetables, adding a bit of salt to the water, leaving the lid off, and quickly cooling them afterward helps maintain their vibrant green color.

15. Boiling new bowls and plates in salt water makes them less prone to breaking.

16. As summer approaches, for fruits and vegetables eaten raw, soaking them in lightly salted water for 20 minutes serves as an effective disinfectant. Remember, it's lightly salted water, not enough to pickle.

17. When making sweet dishes, adding just a tiny bit of salt (not too much) enhances the sweetness.
