Those, you don't remember.

by mimi456 on 2012-01-28 22:03:33

Those, you don't remember.

First, I don't love the sun.

It seems that I've said it many times, but you still forget. You forget so thoroughly.

You forgot. So every morning you pull open my dark green curtains, letting the sunlight destroy the silence in the room, and let it rudely intrude into my peaceful mind. I'm dissatisfied, but you say I'm full of shadows. In fact, you know I don't love it. But, you forgot.

You forgot. So you would ask me to walk aimlessly on boring streets under the scorching sun with you. Repeatedly walking through the same places, repeatedly asking where we're going. Or you yourself always dislike moving around, dislike turning around to bully you after you bully me. In fact, you know I don't love it. But, you forgot.

You forgot. So after logging online, you would require me to go to a nearby Internet cafe just for video chatting to see how I am recently, ignoring the pain brought by colds and exposure to the sun. In fact, you know I don't love it. But, you forgot.

You forgot. So five minutes after the power outage, you timely wake up the lamp. Let that pale light drive away all the quietness, bring me back to the hustle and bustle of reality. I will curse you, curse that when you drive away my darkness next time, you will be accidentally electrocuted. In fact, you know I don't love it. But, you forgot.

Fortunately. You didn't forget. You always sit by my side when I turn off the light and stay quiet alone, yea. This is what I love.

Second, I don't love noise.

This isn't an outcome I can control, but if possible, I would vent my dissatisfaction.

You forgot. So you always nag endlessly beside me. I know you are good for me, but you exceed my acceptance limit and achieve the opposite effect. Finally, you insist on saying that I don't want to listen. In fact, you know I don't love it, but you forgot and got used to it.

You forgot. So after I make some mistakes, you always repeat that sentence - I ruin everything of yours. I admit it, and I have been thinking whether we can calm down and negotiate in silence, instead of entangling each other with much talk. In fact, you know I don't love it, but you forgot and got angry.

You forgot. So after guests come, you would require me to go to the living room and greet them one by one, asking whether I remember their names, then listening to their chatter endlessly. In fact, you know I don't love it, but you forgot and became feudal.

Third, I don't love suspicion.

Ha. Is it because I myself don't trust others enough, or do I not have the qualification to be trusted?

You forgot. So after I interact with her, you always send messages suspecting whether something abnormal has happened between her and me as you think. I know that you never trusted me during this year and more, giving me only suspicion, and I have nothing worth suspecting. In fact, you know I don't love it, but you forgot.

You forgot. So every time I return from taking leave, you give me a distrustful sneer, asking what's wrong with me. I didn't skip class, I didn't sneak out to surf the net, why should your suspicion fall on me? In fact, you know I don't love it, but you forgot.

Fourth, I don't love overdoing.

You forgot. So every year in teacher evaluation, you always ask us to change CD items to AB, shamelessly saying this is tolerance. Who doesn't know that this is just a sugar-coated poison wrapped in tolerance. You play this single-player game, rather than for the school, it's overdoing and wasting paper. Of course, you can discard justice, but the school needs seriousness. In fact, you know I don't love it, but you forgot.

You forgot. So you always twist errors into a heavy stone, throwing it at me heavily. In fact, you know I don't love it, but you forgot.

You forgot. But I still remember.
