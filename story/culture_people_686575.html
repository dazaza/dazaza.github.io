【Qing Dynasty Culture】Yongzheng's Governance Philosophy

Since the founding of the Qing Dynasty, Yongzheng Emperor has been known as the strictest ruler. Therefore, Yongzheng is famous for his severity. Under his rule, not only corrupt officials but also mediocre and inactive people find it hard to survive.

There was a minister named Wang Yunjin who, after returning home from court one day, felt bored and invited a few friends over to play a game of leaf cards at his house. In no time, all his friends arrived as scheduled, and they started playing together. After a few rounds, they suddenly realized that one card was missing. They searched everywhere but couldn't find it. Since the game couldn't continue, Wang Yunjin called his servants to clean up the game and prepare some wine and dishes. He then drank with his friends. The next day, Wang Yunjin went to court as usual. After the court session ended, Emperor Yongzheng called Wang Yunjin and asked him: "What did you do yesterday after the court session?" Wang Yunjin truthfully answered. Seeing that he was honest, Yongzheng didn't say anything more. He took out a paper card from his sleeve and handed it to Wang Yunjin, saying: "Is this it? I'll give it back to you. Go finish your game." Wang Yunjin took it and found that it was indeed the one lost the previous day.

Yongzheng said that if an official doesn't work or can't work, regardless of how good their character is, they are nothing more than a "wooden puppet" decoration, unable to play a role in governing the country and ensuring the peace of the people. Yongzheng once divided law-abiding officials without faults into two categories: one being obedient but mediocre and incapable, and the other having talent and insight but inevitably often having different opinions. Between these two types, Yongzheng's attitude was very clear: remove mediocrities and use talents.

Wang Guodong, the governor of Hunan, was although clean and cautious in office, but achieved nothing in the region, so he was replaced by Yongzheng. Chang Sanle, the magistrate of Wugiao in Zhili, was clean and orderly, with no major mistakes, but also no significant achievements. For this reason, Li Weijun, the governor of Zhili, wanted to transfer Chang Sanle from his position as county magistrate, but the Ministry of Personnel refused to approve it because Chang Sanle had no serious misconduct. When Yongzheng learned about this matter, he clearly pointed out: Chang Sanle was indeed derelict in duty and should be dismissed from his post. Furthermore, Wang Xijie, the magistrate of Cao County in Shandong, failed to resolve more than twenty cases of murder and theft within over a year after taking office, and did not capture a single suspect. Although there was no evidence of embezzlement or malfeasance, Yongzheng believed that not doing work itself was a dereliction of duty and a crime. As a result, Wang Xijie not only lost his official hat but also spent five years in prison.
