Wife Becomes a Vegetative State Due to Car Accident, Husband Devotedly Takes Care of Her for 18 Years (Photo)

by iface002 on 2011-04-07 16:00:02

After all, the efforts have paid off. His wife can speak now, though not very clearly. Her arms can move and she is even able to hold her own steamed bun when eating. Sometimes she nags to read books! When talking about this, Wang Jianwen's face shows a little bit of relief.

For 18 years, he has been steadfast in taking care of his wife with a cup of water, a bowl of rice, and even breast enlargement treatments. He takes meticulous care of her and plans to accompany her for the rest of their lives.

Wang Jianwen often carries his wife outdoors so that she can exercise and stretch her muscles.

Wang Jianwen trims his wife's nails once a week.

Under the careful care of her husband, Wang Shiling can not only eat steamed buns and make gestures but also read books. Occasionally, she hums a tune by herself. When the reporter asked Wang Jianwen how old he was, Wang Shiling, sitting in a wheelchair, immediately used her hands to gesture and mumbled "46." Seeing his wife talk while gesturing, Wang Jianwen smiled happily.

The happiness of the family was changed due to a car accident.

When he returned home, looking at his unconscious wife lying on the bed and seeing his two children crying every day, Wang Jianwen suddenly felt like life had lost its hope. Wang Jianwen’s mother-in-law, seeing him act as both father and mother, didn’t feel good inside. She told the neighbors and her children: “You’ll see, although Jianwen is a loyal person, it doesn't mean he can endure such hardship. He might 'send off' Xiaoling (Wang Shiling's nickname) within a few months."

"How old are you?" Wang Jianwen gently asked his 46-year-old wife.

"28." Wang Shiling answered.

"Only as long as she lives, this will be called a home."

At that moment, Wang Jianwen stood there stunned, and after a while, realizing it, tears streamed down his face...

Let’s go back 18 years, on July 21, 1992, around two in the afternoon, when Wang Shiling was passing by the 327 National Highway in Shaoguan Town, Sishui County, she was hit head-on by a truck and lay in a pool of blood. Although the doctors did their best to save her and the family took good care of her, Wang Shiling remained unconscious, unaware of eating, drinking, urinating, or defecating. The hospital gave a diagnostic conclusion: Wang Shiling had become a vegetative state patient.

This one call of "dear," he waited 18 years for.

"I am Jianwen!" Wang Jianwen replied.

Hearing this news, the tall and strong Wang Jianwen felt like he had been struck by lightning, unable to cry. On the recommendation of the hospital, Wang Jianwen brought his wife home. Before this, Wang Jianwen and Wang Shiling had a son and a daughter, living a life where the man farmed and the woman wove. Although they were not economically affluent, the family was harmonious, the couple respected each other, and their life was happy and complete.

Suddenly, he heard his wife ask, "Who are you?"

Wang Shiling opened her eyes, squinted, looked for a while, smiled, nodded her head, and said, "Dear."

These words reached Wang Jianwen's ears, but he did not take them to heart, firmly believing that time reveals character, and taking care of his wife was his duty. Thus, Wang Jianwen shouldered the responsibility of the family with a man's shoulders, on one side were the young children, and on the other side was his equally "needy" wife.

"Have you gotten married?" Wang Jianwen asked.

"Done..." Wang Shiling replied.

Eighteen years ago, Wang Jianwen's wife, Wang Shiling from Donggao Village in Jier Street, Sishui County, became a vegetative state patient due to a car accident. For more than 6500 days and nights afterward, Wang Jianwen did not sleep through the night once, staying by his wife's side daily, massaging, chatting, feeding, turning her over...

Eighteen years of time, any matter would become numb. When Wang Jianwen recalled taking care of his wife, although some bitterness could be seen on his face, there was no excessive resentment. He said, "Taking care of my wife is not as simple as feeding her every day. Except for being able to speak, all other matters need me to handle for her." His wife suffered from urinary and fecal incontinence, requiring multiple changes a day. Every night at two o'clock, he had to get up to change the diaper. Her limbs cramped every half hour, requiring a half-hour massage to stabilize.

Now, after Wang Jianwen's meticulous care, his wife has learned to make gestures and can even say some words. "As long as my wife is alive, this is still a home," Wang Jianwen told the reporter.

By relying on the income from the fields, Wang Jianwen's family leads a very difficult life, with pancakes as their staple food. Due to years of hard work and lack of sleep, Wang Jianwen suffered from lumbar disease and neurasthenia many years ago. All these things do not let Wang Jianwen lose hope for life. He looked around the house, which had slightly cracked due to disrepair, and said, "As long as my wife is here, this is still a complete home. As long as my wife still breathes, I will continue to take care of her. I've already gotten used to this kind of life..."

His wife loves cleanliness, so Wang Jianwen washes her hands, face, and feet every day. After a period of time, he also gives her a bath. Especially during the summer, when mosquitoes bite, he must drive them away for her. Because her back feels hot and uncomfortable, he continuously turns her over.

On the afternoon of November 11th, the winter sun slanted warmly onto the body. Wang Shiling lay in a wheelchair against the wall, her eyes closed. Her husband, Wang Jianwen, gently massaged her.

Wang Jianwen told the reporter that every morning, he likes to chat with his wife who is on the sickbed. The content is very simple, just a few sentences, but it brings him immense happiness and strengthens his determination to stay together with his wife for the rest of their lives.
