Safety knowledge for the proper use of lithium batteries

Before we use lithium batteries as a power source, there are many things worth understanding. Nothing is more important than safety. We should treat all kinds of batteries carefully because they are full of energy when fully charged.

Lithium batteries have the highest energy density and many unique properties that require us to consider more special safety issues. It is very important to understand these safety issues before discussing other aspects of lithium batteries.

In general, if the lithium battery charger can accurately calculate and control the number of lithium battery cells (referring to how many individual small lithium batteries make up a large lithium battery), it can meet the charging needs of lithium-ion batteries (Li-ion) and polymer lithium batteries (Li-Poly). Remember never to use chargers designed for nickel-cadmium (Ni-Cd) and nickel-metal hydride (Ni-MH) batteries to charge lithium batteries! That would be extremely dangerous!!! Charging lithium batteries is the most dangerous part of using them, so pay extreme attention to the charging process. Correct charging voltage is very important! If the charger is used improperly, it may cause overcharging of lithium batteries leading to explosions! There have been several fire incidents recently caused by lithium batteries, so please take responsibility when charging lithium batteries.

The following are some guidelines for charging and using lithium batteries:

1. Use a verified lithium battery charger that is safe to use.

2. Ensure that you have correctly set the voltage or the number of battery cells on the lithium battery charger. If you don't know how to set it, buy a lithium battery charger that you know how to set up, or don't use lithium batteries to avoid explosions during charging.

3. Always have someone present when charging lithium batteries. Do not charge lithium batteries when no one is around. Houses and cars have been burned down due to this reason, so remember this.

4. Prepare an appropriate platform to place your charger and lithium batteries during charging to ensure that if an explosion or fire occurs, it will not cause serious damage and danger. Leave fire prevention space and do firefighting work.

5. Do not charge with a current exceeding the battery's tolerance, otherwise, explosions and fires are highly likely to occur.

6. If swelling occurs during charging, do not puncture it while it is still hot. Place the lithium battery in saltwater, cool it down, carefully peel off the outer skin, then put the battery pack back into the saltwater, and only after that can you safely dispose of the problematic lithium battery in the trash.

7. If the lithium battery suffers severe impact, even if it looks undamaged, still place the lithium battery in a safe place for at least 20 minutes or more to ensure it is really undamaged and not at risk of explosion. In the aircraft model community, there have been several fire cases where after a lithium-powered plane crashes, the owner throws the wreckage in the car, and seemingly undamaged but actually internally damaged lithium batteries explode in the car, burning the entire vehicle.

8. Charge lithium batteries in a well-ventilated area because if lithium batteries really rupture and explode, hazardous smoke and substances will leak from the battery.

9. Fully understand the dangers of lithium batteries and do not think "this won't happen to me." Otherwise, the next person rescuing family members from a burning house or car might be you. I take this very seriously.
