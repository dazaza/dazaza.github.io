Imitation wood furniture has always been a favored home furnishing for the middle-income group. Due to its low price and high practicality, it is gradually becoming the mainstream of consumption in major cities. Some premium furniture pieces, although made from imitation wood, can still be quite expensive. Here, we will teach you how to select a good set of imitation wood furniture.

Four Key Points to Consider When Buying Imitation Wood Furniture

It is understood that in the current furniture market, wooden furniture has become one of the larger categories of products due to its novel design, bright colors, and clear wood grain. In addition, with relatively moderate pricing, wooden furniture is increasingly favored by consumers. Experts suggest that understanding and mastering the technical requirements of wooden furniture and distinguishing between quality grades is an effective method for correctly selecting wooden furniture.

1. Focus on Surface Quality

In imitation wood furniture, artificial board furniture uses materials such as particleboard and medium-density fiberboard as the base material, which are then covered with melamine, PVC, thin wooden veneers, or paper imitating wood grain patterns. The imitation wood grain patterns are clear and natural, smooth and flat, providing a good visual effect and feel. When purchasing furniture, mainly check if there are any defects on the surface of the materials used, such as scratches, indentations, bubbles, delamination, peeling, or glue marks.

2. Focus on Manufacturing Quality

Wooden furniture is made by cutting shaped panels, decorating them with edge banding, and assembling components. To assess the quality of artificial board furniture, focus on the cutting quality, edge and surface decoration quality, and the quality of the panel ends. Panels must meet specific requirements for straightness, perpendicularity, and angular alignment. Generally speaking, precise cutting technology within 0.01 mm per meter results in smooth edges and accurate angles, preventing tilting of the panels when assembled into furniture. Edge and surface decoration should have uniform glue application, secure bonding, smooth and even trimming, and visible areas like sideboards, door panels, and drawer fronts should be properly sealed. High-quality decoration ensures no noticeable adhesive marks along the edges. Assembly quality focuses on the precision of drilled holes and joints, ensuring parts fit tightly together. Connections between planes and ends should have no gaps and withstand movement without loosening. Door and drawer gaps should not exceed 1.5mm to 2.0mm, and opening mechanisms should be smooth and effortless.

3. Focus on Metal and Plastic Components

Wooden furniture often uses metal and plastic components as fasteners, so their quality also affects the overall quality of the furniture. Metal components should be smooth, well-plated, free of rust or burrs, and precisely machined. Plastic components should be aesthetically pleasing, colorful, durable, and elastic in critical areas, avoiding excessive thinness. Moving parts should operate smoothly, and spring-loaded components should maintain appropriate tension, ensuring furniture operates steadily and quietly without friction.

4. Focus on Main Dimensions

Currently, the market primarily offers complete bedroom sets and office furniture. Additionally, multifunctional audio-visual cabinets are available. National standards specify functional dimensions for these types of furniture. For example, wardrobe hanging spaces should be at least 530mm deep, the distance from the top of the hanging rod for long garments to the bottom panel should be at least 1400mm, desks should be 680mm to 760mm high, and the net height from the bottom of the middle drawer to the floor in writing desks should be at least 580mm. Bookshelf layer spacing should be at least 230mm to 310mm. These dimensions are crucial to consider during purchase, as undersized furniture may cause inconvenience and affect usability. For instance, insufficient depth in wardrobes can prevent proper clothing storage and lead to doors not closing properly.

How to Identify Different Types of Wood

For most consumers, recognizing all solid wood types might not be possible, but remembering the following points can help:

Check the end grain for circular patterns or arc-shaped rows of pores (vessels). Look for layered lines, whether straight or curved, between the wood grains. If these patterns exist, it indicates the piece is likely made from plywood (veneer), as real solid wood, except for rosewood and camphorwood, generally lacks intricate patterns.

Smell: Pure wood usually has a distinct smell that remains even after prolonged storage or finishing. Many woods retain a faint aroma, such as pine resin, cedar's mild fragrance, cedar's spicy scent, fir's unique smell, camphorwood's strong camphor aroma, basswood's slight oily odor, redwood's intense spicy aroma, walnut's mild pungency, and many domestic woods lack distinctive scents while artificial boards have strong chemical odors, making them easy to identify.

Tap: Solid wood produces a crisp sound when tapped lightly, whereas artificial boards produce a dull sound, and boxed wood items emit a drum-like sound.

Weigh: Woods vary in weight across three levels. Lightweight examples include dried Paulownia wood, mid-weight includes pine, basswood, and beechwood. In Northern China, mid-density woods are referred to as soft mixed woods (excluding pine). Heavyweight woods include redwood, rosewood, ebony, oak, ash, birch, and imported Lauan wood, collectively known as hard mixed woods. All mentioned wood furniture tends to be lighter than particleboard and medium-density fiberboard furniture.
