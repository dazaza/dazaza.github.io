"Two Wives" tells the story of a man who lost his memory due to a traffic accident and the series of events that happen between him, his ex-wife, and his current fiancée.

Soo Ae plays Han Ji-sook, an unmarried mother who independently runs a high-end wine shop and a flower shop. Ji-sook's character lies between calmness and passion. While studying in the U.S., she fell in love with her boyfriend and had a child. However, she returned to Korea alone with her child without marrying her boyfriend. Despite her family introducing her to many men, these men all proposed that if they were to divorce in the future, she would have to pay a high amount of alimony. Already thoroughly disappointed in men and deciding to live alone, Ji-sook meets romantic writer Kang Cheol-soo (Kim Ho-jun) and falls in love with this money-agnostic writer. Cheol-soo, living as a full-time writer, is a poor writer who does not even have a representative work and earns money by working part-time as a lecturer at the university and writing miscellaneous articles. Naturally, the financial power of the household is controlled by his wife, Yoon Young-hi. After being married to his wife for ten years and feeling that his wife only cares about money, Cheol-soo gradually distances himself from his wife. Cheol-soo participates in the "borrowed husband" activity on the Internet for novel material collection and gets acquainted with unmarried mother Ji-sook, developing feelings for her. After divorcing his wife, Cheol-soo starts a new romance with Ji-sook and plans to marry her. Unexpectedly, Cheol-soo encounters a traffic accident before the wedding. He suffers severe injuries, falls into a coma for a month, and wakes up with amnesia. The amnesiac Cheol-soo can only remember his ex-wife Yoon Young-hi (Kim Ji-young), but forgets his current girlfriend Han Ji-sook. The sudden car accident places the three of them in an awkward and complex situation. Yoon Young-hi's irresponsible younger brother Yoon Nam-jun (Andy) is a loafer who does nothing productive.
