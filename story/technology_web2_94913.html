LenovoData (Lenovo Online Data Center) is a network storage and sharing service launched by Lenovo Labs. It provides up to 5GB of storage space (initially 1GB, then increases by 20MB per day until it reaches 5GB), with a single file limit of 100MB and no format restrictions. It offers both web-based and client-based management. Recently, LenovoData underwent a major redesign, attracting more users with its convenient, detailed, comprehensive, and massive features. Having been using it for over half a year, I have formed some opinions on its characteristics, advantages, and shortcomings, which I am sharing here for your reference.

① **Personal Storage**: For personal storage purposes, LenovoData offers two upload methods — batch uploads via the web interface (Figure 1) and uploads through the client application (Figure 2, the green tool, download link). Both methods are relatively simple and convenient to use, and the upload speed is fairly stable. Correspondingly, there are also two download options — direct downloads from the web interface (does not support multi-threaded downloads via download tools) and downloads through the client application.
