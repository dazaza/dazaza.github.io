Volume One: "Time-honored Chinese Figure Painting"

Figure painting is the earliest established discipline among the three major disciplines of traditional Chinese painting, marking the independence and maturity of Chinese painting art. Respected as the "Painting Saint," Wu Daozi was a master of figure painting. Figure painting includes diverse content and broad subjects, divided into historical paintings, genre paintings, portraits, and female figure paintings. Techniques include meticulous brushwork with color, white-line drawing, simplified strokes, splashed ink, and meticulous brushwork. Masters like Gu Kaizhi, Zhang Sengyou, Yan Liben, Wu Daozi, Li Gonglin, Tang Yin, Qiu Ying, and Chen Hongshou have left behind masterpieces unique to their times, reflecting the social appearance and customs of various periods. Besides their unique artistic value, these works also possess unparalleled historical and sociological document values difficult to achieve by other painting disciplines.

Volume Three: "Time-honored Chinese Flower-and-Bird Painting"

Flower-and-bird painting is the genre most adept at "expressing aspirations through objects" and conveying emotions through objects among the three major painting genres of ancient Chinese painting. Content not only includes flowers, birds, but also livestock, fish, algae, trees, bamboo rocks, and even fruits and vegetables. Styles range from meticulously detailed realistic flower-and-bird paintings to lively freehand expressionist flower-and-bird paintings. The themes of plum, orchid, bamboo, and chrysanthemum, known as the Four Gentlemen, are especially popular among literati and common people alike. From Emperor Huizong Zhao Ji to the "Eight Eccentrics of Yangzhou," Chinese flower-and-bird painting, with its most colorful painters, schools, and styles, and its most complex and varied techniques, has earned the widest international acclaim for traditional Chinese painting, and is deeply loved by domestic and international art galleries, museums, various collecting institutions, and private collectors.

Volume Four: "Time-honored Chinese Landscape Painting"

Landscape painting is the genre that best represents Chinese philosophical thought and Chinese painting aesthetics among the three major painting genres of ancient Chinese painting. Throughout the development process of over two thousand years from the Wei-Jin Southern and Northern Dynasties to the Yuan, Ming, and Qing Dynasties, there have been various painting methods such as green-blue landscape, ink-wash landscape, and literati small scenes. Renowned masters have emerged, including Zhan Ziqian of the Sui Dynasty, Li Sixun of the Tang Dynasty, Jing Hao, Guan Tong, Dong Yuan, Ju Ran of the Five Dynasties; Li Cheng, Fan Kuan of the Northern Song; the four Southern Song masters Li Tang, Liu Songnian, Ma Yuan, Xia Gui; the four Yuan masters Huang Gongwang, Wu Zhen, Ni Zan, Wang Meng; Shen Zhou, Wen Zhengming, Tang Yin, Qiu Ying of the Ming Dynasty, and Dong Qichang of the Qing Dynasty, creating countless masterpieces that have had an extremely profound impact on later generations of the art world.
