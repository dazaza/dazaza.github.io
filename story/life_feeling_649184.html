The Love of Stones

In her sophomore year, "Corn" fell in love with "Soybean," who was Corn's teacher - an oil painting instructor who liked to paint fruits and women. He had a shaved head, a small mustache, sometimes wore a fisherman's hat, rarely smiled, and even seemed a bit cold. But all of this, to Corn, was a kind of charm. Corn, who thought herself aloof, finally knocked on his door one moonlit night: "Teacher, I'm so cold..."

Without saying a word, Soybean embraced her and brought her in, then there was a gentle sound of the door closing. Their age difference was 10 years, which wasn't a problem. The real challenge was that at the end of the 1980s, "teacher-student romance" was not allowed, so they could only meet secretly, in the early hours of the morning, much like the meetings between ghost maidens and scholars in "Strange Tales from a Chinese Studio." Well, it was very exciting, and Corn liked this kind of secretive romance. Choosing what kind of boyfriend is actually choosing a certain way of life; to put it bluntly, it shows your personal taste. Corn has always been self-confident. In her view, although she was not the class beauty, she was definitely the most unique flower. Therefore, in class or in the studio, she didn't miss any opportunity to have "eye contact" with Soybean. Indeed, her figure and lively, enthusiastic personality were unmatched. Soybean was an introverted man, he liked this gunpowder-like girl, and he usually paid special attention to this female student. The eyes of the classmates were sharp, everyone knew that he liked Corn, but what made Corn gnash her teeth with hatred was that this cold-blooded man never directly confessed to her, not even giving her a hint of a different look. At most, he would stay behind Corn for a few more minutes, watching her paint attentively. Every time, Corn would feel a faint fragrance emanating from her body, and the teacher's breathing behind her would become increasingly coarse. This was an enticing yet alarming signal. After receiving this signal multiple times, Corn finally made the extremely bold move of knocking on his door under the moonlight that night. Thinking back to that night, Corn still feels frightened now. It turns out that she could be as brave as Liu Hulan, the female hero, just that she faced the killer of love, a male teacher named "Soybean."

Their midnight romance was extraordinarily beautiful. Soybean gradually relaxed, the wildness hidden in his chest finally erupted, and Corn's boundless tenderness also unfolded mistily under his exploration.

At this moment, a fierce knock on the door shattered their dream. Leaders from the art department and school patrol team members each held a flashlight, standing solemnly at Soybean's door like a monument... The next day, Corn was publicly criticized, and Teacher Soybean was required to write a self-examination statement and make a profound reflection, otherwise, he might be expelled.

Corn was extremely afraid because her father had been beaten to death in a previous "movement." At the same time, she felt wronged. She and Soybean had never done anything too out of line. They believed that the "last line of defense" should be completed on their wedding night. Their love was only in the "prelude" part. They were still browsing through the first page of love innocently. They painted each other's nudes and used each other as models, but they really both maintained their inner passion. However, the relevant leaders did not believe them. A young man and woman locked in the same room in the middle of the night, if no "incident" happened, even ghosts wouldn't believe it.

This was a "miscarriage of justice," concerning a pure midnight romance. At this point, Corn decided to drop out of school. Getting into university was very difficult at the time, but Corn wanted to protect this love, to protect Soybean's career. She applied to the relevant leaders to withdraw from school. This way, she would no longer be a college student, more accurately, she would no longer be Soybean's student. Then, they would no longer be in a teacher-student relationship. Thus, they could come out from the underground and openly love each other. Changing roles, they could still happily share different kinds of breaths. Unexpectedly, the relevant departments readily agreed to Corn's request. Soybean opposed Corn making this "unnecessary sacrifice," but he couldn't stop her. Finally, they were together again. Corn rented a bungalow outside and took on some private tutoring jobs. Life must go on, and she continued to pretend to be a college student in that city because she couldn't tell her mother the bad news about being expelled.

This incident ended satisfactorily. However, their love soon faced a crisis. Public love, for Soybean, lost its fighting spirit. He no longer held Corn and talked about his vast knowledge. Instead, every time Corn came to see him excitedly, he would light a cigarette and sit aside gloomily smoking, almost silent. Initially, Corn thought he was disheartened by this "incident," but half a month later, Corn keenly sensed an ominous atmosphere. He no longer loved her, and he even stopped kissing her. Even when he did open his lips, they were icy cold. Corn couldn't bear it anymore. That night, she grabbed his shoulders with both hands, looked into his eyes, and asked, "Tell me, don't lie, do you no longer have feelings for me?" Soybean nodded. Corn slowly let go of her hands, tears streaming down her face. One year and six months of love sprinting ended like this, as if it was all a dream, the first half a beautiful dream, the second half a nightmare. Waking up, she realized she had nothing.

Corn walked until dawn in the cold wind of midnight, heartbroken, and then self-reproachful. Why did she make so many sacrifices? But in the end, the person she loved became a thief, a thief of emotions who stole her heart and then discarded it carelessly.

The best medicine for healing this wound could only be time.

Corn understood that she couldn't fall apart, she had to stand up. So, she worked hard, arranging every day tightly without a gap, doing various jobs: tutoring, advertising design, drawing portraits for passersby, designing T-shirts, even knitting sweaters... Once while selling cultural shirts, a female college student brought a stone over and asked Corn to help her add a few brushstrokes with a pen, turning it into a small gift because her boyfriend was born in the Year of the Tiger. She hoped Corn could draw the stone to look "tigerish."

This incident greatly moved Corn. Yes, why didn't she open a gift shop specifically for couples in love? Each gift would be different, the raw materials basically being small stones, then using her painting skills to add lines and colors on the stones, wouldn't that create a vivid and unique love gift? And then putting it in a gift box, each stone would truly be able to sing!

Beautiful stones, singing songs of love. Say do and act. After preparing for a month, Corn's "The Love of Stones" boutique finally opened, and business was good, as was her mood. The scale wasn't large, but she put a lot of effort into it. Corn used this beautiful work to heal the old wounds that hurt her inside, while also earning money and beautifying her own life, utilizing her artistic talent. This was great.

Corn is already 35 years old and remains single. She has created tens of thousands of love gifts, given tens of thousands of cold stones temperature and spirit, and sent blessings to tens of thousands of people. Each stone was personally picked by her from the riverbed in the wild, natural, unpolluted, each with its own characteristics. If these silent stones were painted with a heart, then they would become precious. Looking at them was like reading countless love stories, and she would unconsciously smile.

Often smiling, this is Corn's current life. In her store, there is a signature shelf composed of "female" and "son" forming the character "good." She said, if men and women are always "good" together, how wonderful would that be!

If fate allows, she does not rule out the return of love, including from the painter called Soybean. Even at the age of 100, Corn will not refuse love, of course, on the premise that her teeth haven't fallen out, because she likes to use her teeth to bite and check whether the ring given by her boyfriend is the purest and truest gold.
