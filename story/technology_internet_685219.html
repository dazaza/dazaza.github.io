What are the treatment pitfalls of alopecia areata?

Experts from Shanxi Huimin Hospital point out: Alopecia areata is an autoimmune disease that commonly affects hair, characterized by sudden onset, rapid progression, high cure rate, and high recurrence rate. It seriously affects people's appearance and causes great distress to patients' lives and work, especially among young people. People usually seek quick solutions for treating alopecia areata, which often leads to some common treatment misconceptions. So, what are the common pitfalls in treating alopecia areata?

Experts indicate that alopecia areata is known as "oil wind" in traditional Chinese medicine and colloquially referred to as "ghost haircut." The causes of this condition are highly complex, often related to daily living habits, mental stress, diet, genetics, and other factors. Patients may fall into several common misconceptions when it comes to treating alopecia areata:

**Misconception 1:** Hair loss is painless and itchy-free, so there’s no need to visit a hospital; buying "anti-hair loss shampoo" or external "anti-hair loss" medication will suffice.

**Correct Treatment:** Hair loss is an external manifestation of internal secretion imbalance. No single shampoo or external medication can adjust the internal secretion disorder, so using them alone is ineffective.

**Misconception 2:** Hair loss does not require treatment and will get better on its own after some time.

**Correct Treatment:** Most diseases have a certain degree of natural immunity in a few individuals, and hair loss is similar. About 5% of patients with alopecia areata can recover without treatment. This explains why some people recover without treatment, while others might use a little vitamin or external medication and see improvement.

**Misconception 3:** Hair loss is caused by excessive scalp oil, so controlling scalp oiliness will prevent hair loss.

**Correct Treatment:** Youth is the period of most vigorous metabolism, during which scalp oil secretion and overall body oil secretion are both active. Hair loss results from internal secretion imbalance and has nothing to do with the amount of scalp oil. On the contrary, after severe follicle atrophy, blood circulation stagnates, metabolic levels drop, and the hair becomes dry and brittle, leading to less scalp oil.

**Misconception 4:** Relying on folk remedies, single prescriptions, or secret formulas.

**Correct Treatment:** Traditional Chinese medicine treats hair loss based on differential diagnosis and treatment, where each syndrome type requires a different prescription. Blindly following others' methods is not recommended.

**Misconception 5:** Not seeking medical advice and simply imitating others' prescriptions and treatments.

**Correct Treatment:** Although the symptoms of hair loss are similar for everyone, individual constitutions differ. This is why traditional Chinese medicine divides hair loss into various syndrome types. Medications that work for others may not necessarily be suitable for you.

**Misconception 6:** Hair loss is due to kidney deficiency, and replenishing the kidneys is enough.

**Correct Treatment:** Kidney deficiency-induced hair loss is just one type of syndrome in traditional Chinese medicine classification. If the condition does not fit this syndrome type, blindly replenishing the kidneys could worsen hair loss and affect overall health.
