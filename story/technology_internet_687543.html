Society calls for grassroots workplace elites

by abcekitv on 2012-03-02 16:30:47

Not long ago, a professional suit brand [http://www.8th.com] made a big fuss about an event to collect images of young workplace elites as spokespeople. What was originally an ordinary corporate action has, because of the results of the collection, caused people to have more societal level thoughts.

The original intention of Mr. Zhang, the person in charge of the event's organizing party Urban District No. 8 [http://www.8th.com], was to take six digits worth of prize money and collect a group of relatively representative young workplace elites from society to be their brand spokespeople. Originally, he thought this would be a very simple matter, but the ending turned out to be very disappointing. Mr. Zhang helplessly said: [Whether recommended by an economic company or personally registered, almost all of the elites can be found to have the shadow of being an official's child or rich man's child. It seems that we cannot find any real grassroots young elites.] Mr. Zhang said: [We didn't even say that we had to find China's Zuckerberg, Bill Gates, or today's Hu Shi, Liu Bannong. We just wanted to find some relatively outstanding young professionals in various industries, so why aren't there any?]

A small corporate collection activity reflects a very serious social problem currently in our country. What is it that makes it so hard for our current society to find true young grassroots workplace elites?

Professor Hu of the Chinese Academy of Social Sciences said in an interview: [Before liberation, there were a group of people like Liang Shuming, Liu Bannong, and Liu Wendian. Many came from humble beginnings, but at a young age became very important figures in Chinese society at the time. On the contrary, now it has become difficult for grassroots youth to rise up. The biggest problem facing young people in Chinese society today is unequal opportunities and information asymmetry. The resources needed for development, such as funds, connections, skills, knowledge, and even appearance, are mostly in the hands of the group at the top of the pyramid. The movement between social classes is difficult. Sometimes grassroots levels put in a lot of effort, but in the end the opportunity falls into the hands of the rich and powerful, forming such a cycle.]
