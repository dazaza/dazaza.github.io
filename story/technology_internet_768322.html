Baidu includes its own search results: is it a BUG or high-end SEO?

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 20:20:14

The situation where Baidu indexes its own search results has actually been around for a long time. I remember it appeared last year, and someone even wrote an article about this issue. However, after all this time, the problem still hasn't been resolved. I don't know if Baidu hasn't noticed it or what, but recently, this issue has surfaced again, and it's being exploited for external link building. Today, let's take a look at the true nature of this seemingly high-end and sophisticated SEO operation.

The Situation Where Baidu Indexes Its Own Search Results

High-End and Sophisticated SEO?

This method of manipulating external links similar to Baidu's search results is known by very few people and appears to be extremely powerful. This phenomenon doesn't require much explanation: Baidu indexed its own search results, but the URLs are different from those returned during our normal use of Baidu searches.

Normally, the URL search address returned is "www.baidu.com/s?wd=", while the search result URL in this case is "www.baidu.com/BAIDU?WD=". The latter has been used by many people to increase external links by changing the parameters to their own website URLs and keywords, mimicking a Baidu search to generate external links, leading many to believe that these external links from Baidu searches are high-quality.

After testing, by modifying the parameters and submitting the URLs through Baidu Webmaster Tools' URL submission tool, Baidu quickly indexes them, and the searched keywords rank high.

In fact, when people were widely building external links in the past, many have done this—using Baidu's own platforms to build external links, such as Baidu Zhidao, Baidu Tieba, Baidu Wenku, and Baidu Baike. These platforms, being Baidu’s own products, naturally have certain advantages. Regardless of how effective it is, or whether it is effective at all, people just do it first, resulting in these platforms being overrun by SEO efforts. Consequently, Baidu had to adjust these platforms, such as redirecting outbound links, adding nofollow attributes for link control, and imposing permission restrictions on link additions in Baidu Baike.

Is it Still a Baidu Bug?

To understand Baidu's indexing status for these types of URLs, you can use the site query command "site:www.baidu.com inURL:BAIDU?WD". The query results show that many such URLs have been indexed, and most are occupied by unconventional content. It can basically be judged as a Baidu bug, and due to the fast indexing speed, many SEO professionals exploit it for external link building.

This vulnerability should have been cracked programmatically, as no entry point for generating such URLs has been discovered. Perhaps there are specific tools targeting this bug to create links resembling Baidu searches, thereby achieving the effect of increasing external links.

This phenomenon has already been publicly raised on the Baidu Webmaster Platform, and the official Weibo account of Baidu Webmaster Platform has also been notified. I believe that Baidu has noticed this issue, but it remains unknown when this vulnerability will be fixed.
