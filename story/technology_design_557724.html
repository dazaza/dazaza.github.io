Part of the consumers united in the form of group buying to carry out pricing negotiation with commodity sellers collectively.

The development of group-buying activities in our country is very rapid. The increasingly developing large online consumer population has created a healthy market for online group-buying service enterprises. The famous Chinese economist, Chen Yu, proposed the theory of consumption capitalization which was hailed as the "Full Wealth Theory" and "Economics for the Poor", marking the beginning of innovation in market economy theory. It means that enterprises should regard consumers' consumption as an investment in the enterprise, and at certain time intervals, return a certain proportion of the enterprise's profits to consumers. Online group-buying service enterprises have well met this theory. Through the group buying by online buyers, manufacturers or distributors reduce marketing channel costs and share part of the benefits with group-buying consumers, allowing them to share in the gains. The online group-buying market in our country will inevitably become gradually prosperous and become one of the important ways for consumers to obtain the best customer transfer total value.

The Development Origins of Online Group Buying

Group buying is uniting all "unit divisions" with actual needs and intentions to form a larger virtual "single purchasing body". Due to the huge quantity of goods purchased, it forms a procurement advantage over suppliers (sellers), forcing the supplier to close deals at lower price discounts or even favorable service treatments. Specifically speaking, group buying adheres to the principle of "strength in numbers" to obtain the corresponding lowest market price. Moreover, if there are problems with the goods after purchase, the group-buying party can play a "collective rights protection" role, greatly enhancing the weak position of consumers in the market. For the large-scale orders formed by group buying, they are like a huge piece of cream cake, deeply attracting every supplier, making them "unable to quit". Even if they lose a bit of price discount, they will certainly try their best to compete.

The development of the group buying movement has roughly gone through two stages: the initial stage of group buying and the advanced stage of group buying. The initial stage of group buying is also called the traditional group buying era. The most significant feature of this stage is that the main participants in group buying are organizations such as enterprises, institutions, and governments. They often form a relatively large purchasing unit themselves and under normal circumstances or during holidays, purchase production raw materials, machinery equipment, office supplies, and employee benefits etc., under the collective name. Due to the huge quantity of purchases, they gain the right to negotiate with relevant suppliers and use this right to obtain a lower "negotiated discount", thereby realizing their scale purchasing benefits and achieving the goal of obtaining the greatest benefit with the least cost. Since 2000, with the in-depth development of the Internet in our country, a new type of group buying form carried by the network, namely online group buying, has been increasingly developed and growing. Group buying has become a relatively novel and fashionable consumption model, and the group buying movement in our country has entered a new stage.

In the development process of group buying activities, some individuals and enterprises have spotted business opportunities in the group buying movement. Relying on the group-buying website platform, they act as intermediaries between consumers and merchants, providing group-buying services for individual consumers. Based on membership, all group-buying members can enjoy product group-buying discounts, but these price discounts are only available to business partners with whom they have established cooperative relationships. These "group-buying type" service enterprises that rely on the network and mainly provide group-buying services have formed their own unique industry processes and profit models, becoming the "main force" guiding the masses to consume in group buying; another level of intermediary operation worth noting is that more and more media are gathering readers through advertisements to group buy products. The goal of media organizing group buying is not the goods themselves but the advertising from merchants. However, strong media can stand on the side of consumers and help them secure certain discounts, thus promoting the development of online group buying. This refers to online group buying centered around professional websites, such as the Groupon-style online group buying model, rather than the traditional group buying model where there is grouping but no actual buying.

When consumers select goods, whether the goods can bring them the maximum customer transfer value is an important basis for decision-making. In a market economy environment, for the vast majority of consumers, the asymmetry of product information causes the balance of transactions to always tilt towards the sellers. To gain a transactional advantage and create more customer transfer value for themselves, some consumers unite to collectively bargain with product sellers in the form of group buying, thus gaining the tilting advantage in the market. This is the earliest origin of consumption-type group buying. Since the beginning of the 21st century, with the rapid development of the domestic Internet and e-commerce market, network-based consumption-type "group buying" activities have been more widely promoted and popularized. Especially, the emergence of a large number of B2C, C2C group-buying websites has made online group-buying of goods a very popular consumption fashion among young people today.
