Introduction:

In order to find the Titanic which sank in the Atlantic Ocean in 1912 and the precious treasure on board - the invaluable "Heart of the Ocean", treasure hunter Brock retrieved a rusty safe from the wreckage, only to find a well-preserved sketch inside - a young woman wearing a diamond necklace. This news caught the attention of a centenarian old woman who was very excited and immediately arrived at Brock's salvage ship by helicopter. It turned out that her name was Rose Dawson, the very woman in the portrait.

Looking at the portrait, memories came flooding back to the elderly woman: On April 12, 1912, the Titanic, ready for its maiden voyage, was docked at the port. The wharf was crowded with people eager to see the largest and most luxurious ship ever built. Rose, a beautiful and elegant aristocratic young lady, boarded the first-class cabin with her mother and fiancé - the son of a steel tycoon, Cal Hockley. Meanwhile, the other protagonist of the film - the young vagabond artist Jack Dawson won a third-class ticket through gambling and shouted "We are the luckiest..." as he boarded the giant ship at the last moment. The Titanic set sail, with calm seas under an early spring sky stretching endlessly. Jack and his companions stood at the bow looking ahead, cheering loudly, thrilled as if the world belonged to them.

Rose lived in the social circle of the upper class, already tired of the trivial conversations of the nobility, feeling like a bird in a cage. She frowned and went to the deck to gaze into the distance, relieving her sorrow. Jack, upon seeing Rose, was deeply attracted by her grace. As night fell after another evening of revelry, Rose felt utterly helpless about her future and marriage; she rushed to the deck, attempting to jump into the sea to end her life. Jack discovered her in time and managed to convince her at a critical moment with his sincerity and unique humor. Although Cal was reluctant, he had to invite Jack to dine in the first-class cabin to thank him for saving his wife-to-be. Cal intended to belittle Jack, but unexpectedly, Jack's candid words at the dinner table earned applause from everyone. From then on, Jack and Rose began to know each other and understand each other, and Rose confided in Jack: "I feel like this is all my life will be, as if I've already lived a lifetime. Countless parties, balls, yacht races, polo matches, and the only people I can meet have narrow minds and empty hearts. I feel like I'm standing on the edge of a cliff, with no one pulling me back, no one caring, and no one paying attention."

After discovering the interactions between Jack and Rose, Cal became very displeased. He gave Rose a priceless diamond necklace called "Heart of the Ocean" and confessed his feelings in an attempt to win her heart. However, in Rose's eyes, Cal was just a complete snob, and she didn't want to marry him from the bottom of her heart. But under the pressure of her mother and fiancé, Rose had to deliberately avoid Jack's feelings, yet precisely because of this, Rose realized that she had fallen deeply in love with Jack.

From knowing each other to falling in love, although it was only a few short days, Rose and Jack could no longer be apart. In the bedroom, Rose put on the "Heart of the Ocean," and Jack drew a portrait that would leave her unforgettable. Rose decided to ignore the pressures of family and propriety and live with Jack after the Titanic docked. Happiness seemed within reach for this couple.

Bruce Ismay, the manager of the White Star Line, in order to make the Titanic set a record for crossing the Atlantic, disregarded the potential threat of icebergs and hinted to Captain Smith to increase speed. On the night of the 14th, the sea surface was unusually calm, and the Titanic continued to travel at full speed. The lookout tower spotted an iceberg directly ahead and immediately notified the control room and the chief mate, but the massive ship was unable to avoid it in time. The ship's right side was cut open by the iceberg, and six compartments were flooded. The Titanic, reputed to be unsinkable, would sink within two hours. At this point, there were not enough lifeboats for half the passengers, and panic spread across the ship.

Unexpectedly, Jack was falsely accused of stealing diamonds by Hockley and was locked up in the lower cabin. Unaware of the truth, Rose waited with others on the deck for the lifeboats, but she ultimately believed Jack was innocent. Regardless of everything, Rose returned to the empty cabin to search for Jack and managed to rescue him with a life-saving axe at a crucial moment. They reached the deck, and Rose, persuaded by Jack, got onto the lifeboat. As the lifeboat slowly descended, Rose looked dazed, suddenly abandoned what might have been her last chance to escape and jumped back onto the Titanic. The couple embraced tightly.

The "Dream Ship" Titanic began to slowly sink, and a tragedy unfolded. The dark ocean and sky merged into one, mercilessly swallowing the desperate passengers. Jack and Rose ran to the stern of the ship, climbed over the railing (the place where their love began) and held on until the Titanic sank. After struggling out of the huge whirlpool, Jack pushed Rose onto a floating piece of wood and immersed himself in the icy seawater.

Hours later, the rescue boat returned and picked up the barely alive Rose, while Jack was mercilessly swallowed by the frozen sea. Rose kept the promise she made to Jack and bravely lived on.

Eighty-four years later, Rose returned to the place where the Titanic sank and threw the "Heart of the Ocean" into the sea to inform Jack's spirit...

Costing more than $200 million, "Titanic" was the most expensive movie at the time, and also a masterpiece showcasing the development level of contemporary computer technology. The luxurious liner and grand scenes all reflect Cameron's ambition. However, the love story in the film is comical and unconvincing despite Leonardo's charm.

Cameron's lavish film almost has every square millimeter on the screen made possible by dollars. The unprecedented box office success of this film makes it a symbol of Hollywood at the end of the 20th century (perhaps like "Gone with the Wind" back then), whether it shows the complete victory of the bourgeois vulgar values represented by Hollywood or the last brilliance before decay (not long after "Gone with the Wind" appeared, Hollywood began to decline). Anyway, James Cameron has reached the pinnacle of world commercial cinema and thus left his mark in history.

[Awards]

This film won 11 major Oscars in 1998, including Best Picture, Best Director, Best Sound Effects, and Best Cinematography.

[Box Office Performance]

The global box office revenue of the film was $1.8354 billion, with North American revenue reaching $600 million, ranking first in both global and North American history, and its record remains unmatched to this day.
