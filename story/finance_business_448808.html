Car owner gets injured while pursuing robbers to protect justice, and then gets slapped by his wife

by zxyasdn20 on 2011-06-20 10:53:26

The scene of the incident and the accident vehicle. Mr. Ling looked very distressed.

Complaining about his lack of consideration for consequences, his wife slapped him, and his mother refused to recognize him.

Zhanjiang News (reported by Guan Jiayu, with information provided by Lin Mingcong) At around 6 PM on the evening of the previous day, a heart-stirring scene occurred on Guangyan Road in Wuchuan City: A private car owner witnessed a "motorcycle snatch robbery" ahead and bravely chased the bandits in his private car. As a result, when the bandits tried to turn back to escape, they collided with the private car and were captured severely injured.

Subsequently, the car lost control and collided with a small minivan, injuring a pedestrian, and the vehicle was almost completely written off. Even worse, he had his wife and son with him at the time, both of whom were injured and taken to the hospital.

Act of Bravery:

Witnessing a motorcycle robbery, he pursued courageously

Yesterday, I met Mr. Ling, the car owner, at the Liberation Police Station in Wuchuan. Mr. Ling is from Tangwei Street in Wuchuan, 32 years old, and an individual entrepreneur.

"My son and wife are in the hospital, my mom said she doesn't recognize me anymore, and my dad said in the hospital that he wants to beat me..." The eyes of this tough man immediately reddened...

The incident occurred at around 6:20 PM on the evening of the previous day when Mr. Ling was driving his family to a dentist's clinic for his son's dental check-up. On the way, Mr. Ling witnessed a motorcycle snatching scene about 10 meters away: two suspects riding a motorcycle reached out their hands toward the neck of a man driving a motorcycle, attempting to snatch his gold necklace. The man tightly held onto the suspect's hand and shouted "robbery." The two motorcycles struggled forward for a short distance.

Upon seeing this, Mr. Ling decisively accelerated his car towards the bandits without hesitation. At this point, the robbed man let go of the suspect's hand because there was also a woman and a child on his motorcycle. The suspect failed to snatch the gold chain and quickly fled.

Mr. Ling continued driving while chasing the bandits and honking his horn, signaling the suspects to stop. After chasing for about 200 meters, desperate to escape, as Mr. Ling's car approached, the suspect attempted to turn and maneuver past the car but ended up colliding heavily with it. Both suspects fell off the motorcycle. The driver suffered head injuries and was seriously injured, unable to get up, while the passenger got up and immediately ran towards the residential area.

"Causing" Big Trouble:

Loss of Control Injures Elderly, Wife and Son Injured

Mr. Ling's car also encountered major trouble. After being hit, the car lost control, rushed onto the roadside greenbelt, uprooted trees, and then moved forward, colliding with a parked minivan. It then injured a man in his sixties before finally stopping after traveling a certain distance.

At this moment, what concerned him most was the condition of his wife and son in the car. His son, sitting in the front passenger seat, suffered facial, eye, and back-of-the-head injuries. Fire broke out in the car at this time, burning his hair. His wife, sitting in the back, was heavily pregnant and struck her chest against the front seat, causing chest injuries.

Just as Mr. Ling pulled his wife and son out of the car, "slap, slap," came two sounds as his grief-stricken wife slapped him twice.

Later, the seriously injured suspect was loaded onto an ambulance. Mr. Ling's wife and son, as well as the injured elderly man, were subsequently sent to the hospital for treatment.

"I can determine that this is an act of bravery," said Li Rongxin, director of the Liberation Police Station last night, "His actions in chasing and capturing the bandits are very worthy of learning and promoting."

Dialogue with Mr. Ling

"I believe evil cannot overcome justice"

Reporter: Do your family members know about this? What's their reaction?

Mr. Ling: (eyes moistening) My parents know. My mom said she no longer recognizes me, and my dad wanted to beat me in the hospital, saying I don't consider the consequences of my actions...

Reporter: Do you regret tonight's events?

Mr. Ling: (pausing silently) No regrets. Although the losses are significant, I do not regret it; our society needs this kind of spirit.

Reporter: If you encounter such situations again in the future, will you still intervene?

Mr. Ling: I believe evil cannot overcome justice. Although there were some twists and turns this time, if I encounter similar situations in the future, I will still charge forward without hesitation.
