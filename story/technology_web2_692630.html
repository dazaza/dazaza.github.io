Vendors in the domestic smart home field are mainly divided into three categories.

by gdshstyle on 2012-03-06 10:45:36

DIY in the smart home field is restricted domestically

Currently, domestic manufacturers in the smart home field are mainly divided into three categories: The first category consists of traditional intercom manufacturers such as Fujian Guanlin, Videosecurity, Legrand, and Anjubao. These manufacturers mainly provide an intelligent integrated platform and integrate many subsystems such as security alarm, home appliance control, multimedia audio and video control management on this platform; The second category consists of traditional home appliance manufacturers such as Haier, TCL, and Changhong. These manufacturers mainly provide information networked appliances; The third category consists of manufacturers focusing on modules and interfaces for lighting and curtain control, such as Suobo and Bochuang. These manufacturers mainly cooperate with the first two categories of manufacturers and provide various smart switches and module interfaces for them. With more and more product suppliers joining the smart home industry, such as access control, alarm, intercom, home appliances, etc., the markets targeted by the products provided by various manufacturers are different, causing a situation where almost all manufacturers in the smart home market are fighting alone!

Relatively speaking, the development of the smart home industry abroad started earlier, and the brands are more mature. Generally, they are mainly based on wired systems combining strong and weak electricity disinfection shoe cabinets. In addition, foreign users can to some extent do it themselves, debugging and installing some products according to their actual needs for personalized design. The advantage of such operations is that the user's cost is reduced, and the number of optional solutions increases. The actual situation for domestic users is that few users will purchase and install products themselves; whereas it is different abroad, for example, in the United States, users can directly buy smart home products in supermarkets, such as lamp control devices and smart alarm devices, and users can DIY.

Domestic manufacturers rarely set up specialty stores directly or sell products to end users through supermarkets, department stores, etc. Usually, in smart home project applications, real estate developers and system integrators provide residents with an automated integrated control platform. After the platform is built, residents can purchase smart home systems according to their own needs and expand or further improve the functions of the smart home on the already built platform. The design, installation, and other operation links are usually handled by system integrators. At present, domestic smart home products have not yet fully reached the foolproof mode of operation, and cannot be used directly like traditional household appliances. They involve debugging, installation, and other operation links, which have a certain degree of professionalism and complexity, and are mostly carried out by professional companies, such as strong and weak electricity system integrators, according to the actual needs of residents for scheme design, debugging, and installation.

However, since manufacturers do not directly participate in the design, installation, and other operation links of projects, if relying on system integrators for installation, their technical level may not be able to complete the entire project, and may not meet the expected results of users. Or when accompanying the refined decoration houses of real estate developers, unified procurement, installation, and debugging are carried out, the advantage lies in the reduction of average costs, but residents cannot choose product styles, function types, etc. according to their own needs.

There are many reasons why domestic residents are restricted from doing it themselves, one of which is cost issues. For example, hiring a decoration company in China is cheaper compared to abroad, and the cost for residents to do it themselves is not necessarily cheaper. Another reason is the living environment issue. There are more people in China and the living environment is relatively dense, mostly consisting of residential buildings, unlike abroad where most are single households or villas, and each household generally has a basement where tools and construction can be placed. Therefore, compared to abroad, DIY in smart home applications is not popular among Chinese residents, and most installations are still carried out through real estate projects. Smart shoe cabinets. Currently, the main application of domestic smart home products in the market is communication with real estate developers, depending on the positioning of the real estate developers' own properties, the key lies in whether the real estate developers are willing to invest funds in the smart home sector.

Real estate developers often install a complete set of smart home systems in houses to increase the value of the house in order to boost sales. Abroad, smart homes are an "individual concept" and can be called home equipment control or lighting control, etc., and are not an overall concept, whereas in China, smart home manufacturers or real estate developers promote them as an "overall concept".
