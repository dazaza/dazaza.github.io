If you want to eat "Ding Family Pork", you will have to wait a bit longer.

by zjsr2010 on 2010-06-29 12:00:46

Recently, the Ministry of Commerce, together with the National Development and Reform Commission, the Ministry of Finance and other ministries, purchased frozen pork to stabilize domestic live hog market prices. This is the fourth time since April this year that the ministries have jointly supported live hog market prices. The weight of pork prices in the CPI reaches 4%.

According to the monitoring of the national agricultural by-products and agricultural material price situation system of the Xinhua News Agency, the domestic live hog market has shown a steady recovery trend, with the weekly average acquisition price of live hogs and the weekly average ex-factory price of fresh pork rising slightly for three consecutive weeks.

Guosen Securities Li Chen revealed on Weibo: "The inventory of old sows in China has fallen below 60 million heads, and it is expected that pork prices will start to rise from September this year."

Accumulation of various information seems to point to one thing: now is the right time for pig farming. Last year, Ding Lei announced his high-profile entry into the pig farming industry. How is the progress? Will big capital quietly intervene?

Ding Lei's Pig Farming: Known Hardships

Last year, Ding Lei made a high-profile announcement about "pig farming". A relevant person in charge of NetEase said at the end of the year that the "Ding family pigs" would be ready for release in 2010. Soon after, Guo Guangchang, the boss of Fosun Group, invested 160 million yuan in Jiangxi pig farming company Guohong Group.

Currently, NetEase is tight-lipped about "pig farming". A relevant person in charge stated: "This project is ongoing, and the reported release times and quantities may not be accurate."

After chatting for a while, this person in charge began to lament the difficulties of pig farming: "After conducting surveys in many places, we found that pig farmers in China are truly suffering. Corn for pig feed relies on imports, and when suppliers control prices, they rise. Domestic pig farming is still too scattered, lacks economies of scale, and has no say."

This person in charge stated that NetEase's pig farming is not an "investment", but rather hopes to explore new farming methods through experimentation to break the current "pig cycle".

The traditional price cycle of "one year of loss, one year of profit, one year of stability" in the pig farming industry has been broken in recent years. The market has fallen into a cruel cycle of "high prices - mass production - falling prices - culling breeding sows - high prices". Beyond this cycle, there are more uncontrollable factors for farmers such as logistics and feed.

Pig Market Poised for Rebound

Yesterday, the owner of a ham factory in Dongyang listed his purchase prices to reporters: pork prices were around 11.8 yuan per pound in 2007; the highest was about 10 yuan in 2008; the highest was about 9 yuan in 2009; currently, it is 7 yuan per pound. From the supply side, China's pork production was 42.878 million tons in 2007, 46.2 million tons in 2008, and 48.89 million tons in 2009. The result of large quantities and low prices is that farmers slaughtered breeding sows to reduce losses.

Industry insiders predict "the market is about to rebound," and many companies have already taken action. COFCO Meat Investment Co., Ltd., established in March 2009, quickly built large-scale pig farms with over one million heads in Tianjin, Jiangsu, and Wuhan, with a total planned investment of 15 billion yuan. In mid-June, COFCO announced the official completion of its pig farming bases in Tianjin and Dongtai, Jiangsu.

Jiangxi Guohong Group, invested in last year by Fosun Group, also indicated that it will increase its self-produced pigs from 120,000 heads in 2009 to 200,000 heads in 2010.

Industry insiders pointed out that pork prices are expected to rebound in the second half of the year, and COFCO's newly launched pig farming projects can just catch the rebound in pork prices and complement its grain and oil business, forming a synergistic advantage.

Large Pig Farmers Cautiously Optimistic

Although there are many positive signs, large pig farmers remain cautiously optimistic.

"Although the recent purchase price has slightly increased, the fundamentals of the industry have not significantly improved," said Lin Hong (a pseudonym), who has been raising pigs for more than 30 years. He has persisted for several months – the "bear market" for live hog purchase prices started before the Spring Festival.

During these past few months, he tried every method, such as "price-locked transactions" for corn. "I signed a supply contract with the corn supplier at 1,900 yuan/ton at the beginning of the year, and many farmers thought this price was too high at the time. Later, the price of corn kept rising, and now it exceeds 2,100 yuan/ton." As the most important feed for pig farming, the price of corn basically determines the cost of farming.

Lin Hong predicted that pork prices will have room for further increases from July to September this year, but after the concentrated release of live hogs in October, the situation becomes uncertain again.

A relevant person in charge of the provincial pig farming association told reporters that the "pig market" might recover in the second half of this year, but risks should still be reminded.

"Pig farming is not like stock trading. If you lose money, you cannot cut your losses as easily as selling stocks. Either you slaughter all the pigs or continue to invest more, or extend the release cycle, waiting for the market to recover. This requires a lot of funds and carries significant risks."
