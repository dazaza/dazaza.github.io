We can understand the expert's response below.

Experts from the gynecology department at Zhengzhou Anti-Aircraft Hospital pointed out that generally, if your period is early or late by up to a week, it is considered normal. If you are certain that you are not pregnant, then the absence of your period for two months could be considered menstrual irregularity. Menstrual irregularities refer to a series of women's health issues where the menstrual cycle, duration, color, amount, and quality deviate from the norm. These include conditions such as early menstruation, delayed menstruation, irregular periods, excessive bleeding, scanty menstruation, prolonged periods, and uterine bleeding disorders.

Why hasn't the period come for two months? There are two main causes of menstrual irregularities:

1. **Neuroendocrine dysfunction**: This mainly involves instability or defects in the hypothalamus-pituitary-ovarian axis, leading to menstrual disorders.

2. **Organic lesions or medication-induced**: This includes local inflammation, tumors, developmental abnormalities, malnutrition in the reproductive organs; intracranial diseases; other endocrine dysfunctions such as thyroid, adrenal cortex dysfunction, diabetes; liver diseases; blood disorders; use of psychiatric medications; endocrine agents; or the use of intrauterine devices for contraception, all of which may lead to menstrual irregularities. Clinically, it is necessary to determine whether the menstrual irregularity is due to neuroendocrine dysfunction or organic reasons.
