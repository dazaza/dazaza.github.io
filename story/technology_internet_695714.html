■ Xinhua News Agency (Multi-dimensional Steel Structure Xianghe Company Lin Kai) The magnetic drill is a commonly used tool in the steel structure workshop, small and convenient. Because of this, employees often underestimate its dangers. Here we analyze the risk sources of using magnetic drills:

1. Safety regulations clearly stipulate that gloves should not be worn when operating rotating equipment. However, our employees often think that magnetic drills have a slow speed, small size, and low danger, so it should be fine. In fact, it can cause significant harm to operators.

2. The hex screws on the drill bit are prone to damage. The screws we replace are longer and can easily catch on sleeves, causing accidents.

3. Iron filings can injure people. When the magnetic drill is working, the iron filings produced should not be cleaned by hand but with tools.

4. Electrical threats. When the magnetic drill suddenly loses power, the drill itself loses suction, and the drill bit continues to rotate, which can easily cause injury. Therefore, check if the wiring is secure before operation and do not use the magnetic drill before being notified of a power outage.
