Base on Shenzhen and play the Asia United Carbon Trade card

by ditan360 on 2010-07-29 17:33:10

http://www.ditan360.com 2010-07-29 10:58 Yangcheng Evening News

Yitan News Network - Low carbon became a popular keyword at the Sixth World Chinese Forum held yesterday. Senior System Analyst at the Chicago Mercantile Exchange in the United States, Yang Chunlai, suggested that if Guangdong Province wishes to gain the establishment rights for a Carbon Emission Rights Futures Exchange, it can only do so by basing itself in Shenzhen and playing the "Asian Joint Trading" card.

Yang Chunlai pointed out that if China wants to conduct carbon emission rights futures trading, the first choice might be Shanghai, as Shanghai already has two futures exchanges with the most active futures trading. It began exploratory research into carbon emission rights futures trading as early as 2006. As China's financial and trade center, Shanghai is undoubtedly the preferred city for establishing a climate exchange in China.

The second choice might be Tianjin. In 2008, the Tianjin Property Rights Exchange and the Chicago Climate Exchange (CCX) jointly established the Tianjin Emissions Trading Exchange. As one of the shareholders, the Chicago Climate Exchange leverages its position, professional capabilities, and global platform in the international emissions market to provide the Tianjin Emissions Trading Exchange with rich experience, including concepts, methods, market system construction, comprehensive solutions, and mature trading systems.

Yang Chunlai believes that if Guangdong Province wants to gain the establishment rights for a carbon emission rights futures exchange, it can only base itself in Shenzhen and play the "Asian Joint Trading" card.

He analyzed that on November 17, 2009, representatives from the Shenzhen International High-Tech Property Rights Exchange, the Shenzhen International Energy and Environment Technology Promotion Center, and RESET (Hong Kong) Company jointly signed a memorandum of cooperation to establish the "Asian Emissions Trading Exchange" in Shenzhen. The Asian Emissions Trading Exchange, following the establishment of similar institutions in Beijing, Shanghai, and Tianjin, is an emissions trading venue set up in the Shenzhen Special Economic Zone, serving South China and even Southeast Asia with international, market-oriented, and specialized characteristics. It plans to establish a regional emissions trading platform. If this exchange could cooperate in some way with the Shenzhen Stock Exchange, utilizing the latter’s mature trading technology and leveraging its special connections with Hong Kong and Southeast Asia by playing the "Asian Joint Trading" card, it might gain the establishment rights for a carbon emission rights futures exchange and ultimately become the Asian pricing center for the carbon emissions market.
