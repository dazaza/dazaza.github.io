Baidu Corporate Development History

by zhtd2008 on 2007-11-15 16:43:10

Baidu Inc (baidu.com) was founded at the end of 1999 in Silicon Valley, USA, by Mr. Robin Li and Mr. Eric Xu, who had years of successful experience in Silicon Valley, USA. In January 2000, Baidu established its wholly-owned subsidiary in China - Baidu Network Technology (Beijing) Co., Ltd., followed by the establishment of Shenzhen branch in October of the same year, and the Shanghai office in June 2001.

The name "Baidu" was inspired by a line from a classical Chinese poem, "Having searched for him among thousands", symbolizing the company's confidence in its own technology. Another layer of meaning is to break through the Western saying that "doing something to 99 degrees is doing it to the end." Baidu aims to strive for 100 degrees, reaching the ultimate level.

Baidu differs from ordinary Internet companies. It does not focus on providing Internet content or being a portal site, but rather is a technology-oriented company that independently masters and provides core Internet technologies. In today's rapidly developing Chinese Internet economy, Baidu combines advanced global network technology, Chinese language characteristics, and the current state of development of China's Internet economy to develop an infrastructure platform for information retrieval and delivery in China's Internet, and uses the most advanced business model to directly provide high-value technical services to the entire Chinese Internet, making it one of China's best Internet technology providers.

Since entering the Chinese Internet market, Baidu has been committed to developing truly scalable core Internet technologies that meet the habits of Chinese people. Baidu's goal is to improve the technological components of China's Internet and help it develop faster. For this purpose, Baidu not only brings products that solve basic Internet problems (information search and delivery), but also introduces Silicon Valley-style corporate culture and new concepts of Internet application. Currently, Baidu has three product lines: apart from the widely recognized Chinese search engine for portal sites, there are highly targeted search products for enterprises - Baidu Wangshitong, and website acceleration technology. The emergence of Baidu has raised the banner of national technology in China's Internet, breaking the original situation where China's Internet relied on foreign network technology.

Based on its core technology "Hyperlink Analysis," Baidu's search service experience has won the love of a large number of users; Hyperlink Analysis evaluates the quality of linked websites by analyzing the number of linking websites, ensuring that the more popular the content is with users, the higher it ranks in Baidu searches. Baidu President Robin Li is the sole holder of the Hyperlink Analysis patent, which is now widely adopted by major search engines around the world.

Baidu has the world's largest database of Chinese web pages, currently indexing over 2 billion Chinese web pages, with the number growing at a rate of millions per day. At the same time, Baidu's servers distributed across China can directly return the searched information from the nearest server to local users, allowing users to enjoy extremely fast search transmission speeds.

Baidu processes over hundreds of millions of search requests daily from more than 138 countries, with over 70,000 users setting Baidu as their homepage every day. Users can find the latest and most comprehensive Chinese information through Baidu's search engine. Since 2004, the phrase "If you have a question, Baidu it" has become popular in China, making Baidu synonymous with searching.

Baidu also provides software, bidding ranking, and related advertising services to various enterprises, offering them a marketing platform to reach potential consumers, and providing massive information retrieval and management solutions for large enterprises and government institutions. Baidu's main business model is Pay-for-Performance (P4P), a pay-per-effect online promotion method, which offers excellent development opportunities for small and medium-sized enterprises to conduct online marketing, but has also sparked some controversies; some believe that this service may affect user experience.
