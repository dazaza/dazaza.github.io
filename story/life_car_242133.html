Private transactions of used cars have risks, and it is recommended to choose a regular used car market.

by starlwx on 2009-09-24 11:13:50

When people have obtained their driver's licenses but are facing financial difficulties, they often turn to the second-hand car market to select a vehicle, hoping to purchase a suitable used car. However, there are many issues present in second-hand car transactions.

Phenomenon: Private transactions can be harmful

A few days ago, Mr. Wang reported that at the beginning of this year, he went to the second-hand car market looking for a cheaper used car. After walking around for a bit, a man called him aside and said he had a cheap second-hand car for sale. Mr. Wang followed him to a place not far from the second-hand car market where three cars were parked. This man claimed all the cars were his and that he wanted to sell them cheaply without paying the transaction fee... After getting someone who knew about cars to check the condition of the vehicles, Mr. Wang eventually bought a used Santana for 5000 yuan. According to Mr. Wang, the man had said the car would be under warranty for 10,000 kilometers, but after only 5,000 kilometers, the engine broke down. When Mr. Wang tried to contact the seller, he found the person was nowhere to be found and couldn't be reached by phone.

WeSellMyCar.com discovered that such improper transactions frequently occur around second-hand car markets. These sellers have no connection with the second-hand car market and conduct transactions with buyers entirely on an individual basis, leaving little room for any guarantees. Such individuals are commonly referred to as "car dealers." Not only that, in the second-hand car market, practices such as arbitrary valuation, false price inflation, and passing off inferior goods as good ones are common, significantly reducing the credibility of second-hand car market transactions.

Call: Private transactions should be eliminated as soon as possible

In real-life second-hand car transactions, there are indeed many owners like Mr. Wang who are "victims." Staff members at second-hand car markets stated that although the current second-hand car transaction fees have increased somewhat, the relevant procedures and formalities are legitimate and provide security for traders. Unlike other consumer goods, cars are relatively expensive, making after-sales service even more important, necessitating proper channels for transactions and purchases.

WeSellMyCar.com learned that second-hand car transactions cannot be taken lightly, as there are quite a few steps involved. Besides checking the exterior, testing the performance, and conducting road tests, changing relevant certificates is also crucial. For example, vehicle registration certificates, vehicle driving certificates, owner's ID, unit code certificates, vehicle purchase additional tax, and vehicle and vessel usage tax, etc. In addition, signing a vehicle transaction contract must be done clearly.

WeSellMyCar reminds consumers that when purchasing a second-hand car, one should seek out a reputable company rather than private sellers on the street. Because, in terms of basic rights protection, users engaging in transactions with reputable companies are assured security. Moreover, if any issues arise post-transaction, once evidence is confirmed, the second-hand car market will implement a pre-compensation mechanism, compensating users for their losses first, then resolving matters between the market and the old car company through negotiation or legal means, thus avoiding user losses.
