English experts from Yingtong English point out that the age of six is the golden period for people to learn English.

by ac73p77m73r6 on 2012-03-08 21:39:25

It might be said that, to a certain extent, all children are born linguists. If the critical period for learning English is seized and training is intensified, it will certainly benefit them for life. As an industry leader, Ying Tong English has launched an English written language course for teenagers, maximizing the needs of young people learning English. From the physiological and psychological development characteristics of humans, the younger the age, the more advantage there is in quickly absorbing language. The mystery behind this remains unexplained by anyone so far, but with future scientific advancements, humanity will eventually uncover the true nature behind language learning. However, based on adult experiences in learning their native language, humans indeed possess an innate ability to learn and accept languages without formal instruction. Particularly astonishing is how infants have high sensitivity to sounds, and the regulation of vocal cords, tongue, and lips' motor nerves have great plasticity, making it easier to form pure pronunciation. As age increases, this ability rapidly declines, making the study of English increasingly difficult. Ying Tong English experts further explained the scientific principles involved. People's mastery of language is closely linked to the development of cognitive abilities, which can be described as a synchronous relationship. Numerous psychological experiments on children indicate that in the development of cognitive abilities in infants, the first to develop rapidly and mature is imaginative thinking ability. The characteristics of imaginative thinking are concreteness, completeness, and leaps. When infants see a certain character shape, they do not perceive it as some kind of symbolic representation like adults; instead, they imagine it as mountains, sun, toys, etc. Under repeated stimulation, this special "image" leaves a deep impression in the infant's brain. This impression is long-term and can even be retained in the brain for life. Then, what is the best age for a person to learn a foreign language, especially English, the world's universal language? Experts from Ying Tong English, a professional institution dedicated to English training for Chinese nationals, clearly pointed out that before the age of six is the best time for learning a foreign language throughout one's life.
