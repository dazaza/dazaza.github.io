Regarding the Title Computer Examination

Respected Chairman Wang Zhenzhong of the Jiangsu Writers Association and Director of the Provincial Personnel Department,

My name is Rong Ping. I am 52 years old, a member of the Jiangsu Writers Association, and an art critic. On June 17th at 10 in the morning, I participated in the intermediate title computer examination at the Yangzhou Education College Examination Room 3. My admission ticket number was 0700232070617001014, and my score was 13 points. I would like to report to Chairman Wang and the Director of the Provincial Personnel Department about the suspicion of corruption regarding the computer examination: the test questions clearly have signs of corruption, for the following three reasons:

1. The test questions do not help improve the quality of technical personnel. Learning computers mainly involves Chinese input, computer writing, sending and receiving emails, and searching for advanced knowledge online. None of these were included in the test.

2. The simulation test papers are just gimmicks. For example, adding tables requires keyboard operation, and the mouse cannot be used. With only a junior high school education and no knowledge of English, it is essentially designed so that one cannot pass.

3. The exam focuses on testing the ability to play slideshows. May I ask Chairman Wang and the Director of the Provincial Personnel Department, is improving the scientific literacy of writers, doctors, nurses, and teachers really about testing their ability to play slideshows on a computer? In essence, such test questions are designed to prevent honest people from passing, teaching them to engage in corrupt practices, such as giving money or gifts to officials of the personnel department, or cheating in exams. If this happens, not only will the social atmosphere worsen, but also doctors, teachers, nurses, and writers will stop focusing on their own professional fields and instead focus on learning how to play slideshows on a computer for promotions. It may seem trivial for writers, but consider nurses - who will have the heart to research skills like administering scalp needles to children when promotions depend on playing slideshows? A medical accident could be disastrous. In the exam I took, only one question was useful for writers - converting a section of text into traditional characters. I couldn't solve it because I didn't know where it was located in the format during the exam. After asking a young person outside the exam room, I learned it was under tools. Later, when I returned to Gaoyou and asked a female director at the Personnel Bureau, I found out it was only available in Word 2003.

The Provincial Personnel Director might think that we writers just love to complain and blame our superiors for our inability with computers. I graduated from junior high school in 1970, worked as a team leader and deputy platoon leader in a farm brigade, later served as a clerk and deputy company-level technician in the agency, then worked as a worker at Gaoyou No.1 Middle School, guarding doors and printing lecture notes for ten years. Through self-study, I published nearly two hundred literary reviews in domestic newspapers and one academic paper in a world-class foreign newspaper. Since 1999, due to strict control by national propaganda departments over the publication of literary reviews, I temporarily stopped writing. In 2000, with the help of a kind female college student, I learned how to use a computer, including the Wubi input method, and also learned how to write using a computer. I even compiled a small dictionary called "Wubi Input Method Dictionary," which includes knowledge of correctly using recognition codes. I also trained a Wubi typist for the school voluntarily. As someone who can use a computer solidly and correctly, I only scored 13 points on the exam. May I ask the Director of the Provincial Personnel Department, isn't it absurd to charge money for the computer exam and still fail those who know how to use computers? There have been rumors of proxy exams and cheating in the title computer exam. I believe that people with a conscience will make the right judgment. This letter was written on my computer. If you don't believe me, you can ask the director, section chief, or discipline inspection committee secretary of the Gaoyou Personnel Bureau to verify the "Wubi Character Type Small Dictionary" (awaiting publication, manuscript) that I compiled. If any part of what I said is false, I am willing to accept criminal punishment.

Respected Chairman Wang, the Jiangsu Writers Association has repeatedly taught writers to follow the party's instructions. I personally follow the party's guidance, firmly obey the leadership of General Secretary Hu, and support the Scientific Development Concept. Now that I only scored 13 points on the computer exam, I wish to apply for the third level of national writer this year. Please instruct whether I can apply. I must clarify that it is impossible for a middle-aged person like me, with only a junior high school education, to take the same computer exam as young people in their twenties and thirties using English keyboard operations. I kindly request Chairman Wang's instruction on this matter.

Please forward this letter to the Director of the Jiangsu Provincial Personnel Department after reading.

Sincerely,

Rong Ping (Jiangsu Writers Association Membership Number 00571)

Date of Writing: August 3, 2007
