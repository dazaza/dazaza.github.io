Gynecological Consultation: Unveiling the Secrets of a Hong Kong Intern Doctor's Suicide by Disinfectant

by fuukee0019 on 2011-04-28 11:00:04

The incident occurred at 11:43 AM yesterday. A 65-year-old cleaner surnamed Zhong, working at the Tung Wah Hospital on Po Ren Street in Sheung Wan, smelled a strong disinfectant odor coming from one of the rooms while she was working in the doctors' dormitory. After knocking on the door and receiving no response, she pushed the door open out of suspicion and was shocked to find Dr. Leung sitting on a chair with his upper body leaning over the bed beside him, semi-unconscious. She immediately informed her superior and called the police.

The police and paramedics arrived on the scene after receiving the report and sent the victim to Mary Hospital for emergency treatment, where he had to be admitted to the intensive care unit. The police found a bottle of disinfectant that had been mostly consumed and some other medications at the scene but no suicide note.

There has been much recent discussion about the insufficient staffing of public hospital medical personnel. Yesterday's attempted suicide by an intern doctor has led some to question whether it might be related to high work stress. However, sources from the hospital said that Leung's suicide attempt this time was not related to work but likely due to personal issues. As there was no suicide note, the reasons for his despair will need further investigation by the police.

In response to the intern doctor's suicide attempt, the Hospital Authority stated that the Western Hospital Cluster takes this incident very seriously. The hospital management and the head of the surgery department have visited the intern doctor and explained the incident to his family, offering condolences and all appropriate assistance and follow-up. Tung Wah Hospital has also briefed its employees about the incident and provided emotional support to colleagues in need.

A spokesperson said that the intern doctor was transferred from Na Duk Sau Hospital in Tai Po to the surgery department at Mary Hospital at the beginning of this month for a three-month internship. According to standard practice, interns in the surgery department at Mary Hospital first undergo a six-week internship at Tung Wah Hospital before continuing their internship at Mary Hospital.

According to the shift schedule, the intern doctor needed to work 10 shifts in April, which is approximately once every three days, consistent with the usual number of shifts for interns. The doctor had just taken a day off two days ago (on the 26th) and attended the internal meeting held by the surgery department at Mary Hospital as usual at 7:30 AM yesterday morning. He returned to Tung Wah Hospital around 9 AM. The intern doctor usually performed well at work and was known for being quiet.
