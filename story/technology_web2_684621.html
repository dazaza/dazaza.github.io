Teen Walks 340km to Save Mother: Mom Luo to Undergo Tumor Removal Surgery Today

by lpoihjkmkk on 2012-03-01 09:24:12

Xiaoluo has been fond of fixing things since he was a child. When the alarm clock or mobile phone at home broke, his family would give them to him for repair, but the results got worse and worse.

"Dad once told me that repairing cars could earn more money," Xiaoluo said earnestly.

"I only have one semester left before I graduate from junior high school," said Xiaoluo's sister Luo Qianping. "I also want to continue studying." More than a month after dropping out of school automatically, Xiaoluo finally gave a definitive answer.

The bag given by the old woman contained a can of adult milk powder and a baby bottle, prepared for Luo's mother, as well as an envelope with money for Xiaoluo's schooling. The elderly woman encouraged Xiaoluo to be strong, "I also had cancer in the past and underwent chemotherapy, but now I'm slowly getting better."

In addition to reporting on Xiaoluo's filial piety, the New Express also specially opened a love hotline for him. Yesterday, the hotline was constantly ringing, and reporters received hundreds of text messages and calls. Some readers called to inquire as early as 8 a.m., and the farthest donations came from Heilongjiang, Inner Mongolia, and Xinjiang.

As of 8:30 p.m. last night, the public welfare account announced by the hospital had received 160,176.66 yuan in love donations for Luo Rujiao. However, Ms. Liu, a reader from Beijing, expressed her concern, "I'm afraid the love will be desecrated." Many enthusiastic readers called to suggest establishing an independent personal account or a more reliable donation channel for Xiaoluo, so they could provide long-term assistance in the future.

Yesterday, just the reporter's phone alone received more than a hundred messages and calls from all over the country concerning Xiaoluo and Luo's mother. A Shanghai girl who did not want to leave her name was deeply moved by Xiaoluo's filial piety after walking 340 kilometers to save his mother. After calling the reporter, when the reporter handed the phone to Xiaoluo nearby, she consoled the girl: "...Sister, I'm fine, mom is also good." After hearing this, the girl cried even harder.

Many office workers learned about Xiaoluo's deeds through the internet and called during working hours to inquire. Ms. Zhang from Beijing was one of them. "Doesn't he have a sister working as a nanny? If she needs a job in the future, she can come to my house, I will definitely pay her more than outside, and I can also support her to learn what she wants."

There are many readers who promise to sponsor Xiaoluo and his sister's education. Yesterday afternoon, when the reporter conveyed these messages one by one to the two siblings of the Luo family, the children kept saying "thank you".

At the age of 13, Luo Weike walked 340 kilometers to Guangzhou to shine shoes and raise money to treat his seriously ill mother. His story attracted the attention of enthusiastic people, and after being detailed reported by our newspaper yesterday (see A8 edition on June 10), his filial piety touched more enthusiastic people. At around 2 p.m. yesterday afternoon, a white-haired grandmother walked into the ward where Xiaoluo's mother was staying, carrying a bag. Seeing Xiaoluo, the old woman's voice choked up. She said to Xiaoluo: "No matter what, you must be strong! It's too hard for such a young person!"

The grandmother hugged Xiaoluo tightly, tears streaming down her face. The grandmother was in her 70s and took three buses in the hot weather from Yuexiu District to the hospital, "just wanting to quickly hand the things to the child".

In response to helping Luo's mother, the New Express initiated a "Let Xiaoluo return to class" assistance plan yesterday. Many enthusiastic readers who called expressed their wish to help Xiaoluo and even his sister with their studies.

"I am willing to sponsor Xiaoluo's schooling until he graduates from high school," Mr. Lin, a reader from Guangzhou, left a message. "I am willing to bear all the costs of Luo Weike's future studies," Ms. Shi, a reader from Shenzhen, replied.

"He is a very responsible little boy, he is very filial to his elders, which is rare," said Mr. Zhang, a reader from Inner Mongolia after verifying the relevant information. He said he would soon transfer 1500 yuan to the love account.

A kind-hearted grandmother told Xiaoluo to be strong no matter what.

"Sister, don't cry..." Xiaoluo held the reporter's mobile phone, comforting a 22-year-old girl from Shanghai who had already sobbed uncontrollably on the phone.

Many readers who were moved like this but had never met him personally donated money to Xiaoluo at the bank during their working hours to make sure their love reached him as soon as possible.
