Real Madrid Exposed: Eight Players Marked for Winter Sale - A Mixture of Truth and Falsehoods

Sina Sports - Recently, a Catalan media outlet revealed that Real Madrid is preparing for a major overhaul during the winter break. Some players who are unable to fit into Manuel Pellegrini's plans have become surplus to requirements, merely collecting their salaries without contributing on the field. As a businessman, Florentino Perez must consider costs and profits; hence, these players are set to be cleared out.

The report listed eight names: reserve goalkeeper Jerzy Dudek, backup left-back Rafael van der Vaart, unused defensive midfielder Fernando Gago, consistently excluded striker Ruud van Nistelrooy, reserve defensive midfielder Mohamed Diarra, substitute central defender Ezequiel Garay, Real Madrid vice-captain Guti, and backup attacking midfielder Wesley Sneijder. The report also stated that Real Madrid hopes to raise 40 million euros by selling these eight players. However, judging from the prices listed in the report, it seems this news should only be taken as one person's opinion, as some prices clearly do not align with market trends or the actual value of the players. One might say these prices are rather "amateur."

For example, they priced Garay at only 5 million euros. Where in today's European transfer market can you find a potential central defender for just 5 million euros? Moreover, Van der Vaart was marked at merely 1.5 million euros. Such a fee would be more suitable for renting a player. If anyone were to offer such a price for Van der Vaart, unless the entire Real Madrid management had lost their minds, the quote would undoubtedly be thrown straight into the trash.

Looking at the names, it is true that these players mostly struggle to secure playing time, with some frequently excluded from the matchday squad. Regardless of whether Florentino places commercial value as his top priority, respecting basic team-building principles remains crucial. Any team needs both starters and substitutes. If Real Madrid sells all eight of its substitutes, how could the team maintain balance? With the challenging schedule in the second half of the season, how could Real Madrid ensure sufficient depth on its bench? For the sake of an alleged 40 million euros, is Real Madrid willing to become a team that relies solely on one lineup to play all matches? Just thinking twice about it makes it clear that listing eight players for clearance during the winter break is far-fetched. After all, this is the winter break, not the summer transfer window.

Real Madrid executives Jorge Valdano and Emilio Butragueño have repeatedly emphasized that the club's principle this winter break is neither to sell nor buy players. Of course, if a player insists on leaving and the interested club offers a reasonable bid, Real Madrid might consider it. However, Real Madrid will not approve all transfer requests, and so far, it has not received any satisfactory offers.

Jerzy Dudek knew from the day he joined Real Madrid that he would be a backup. The Pole has never complained about his limited playing time. This summer, Real Madrid decided to keep Dudek, and he accepted the club’s new contract. Why would he suddenly think of leaving during the winter break, and why would Real Madrid release a backup goalkeeper for free mid-season?

Rafael van der Vaart leaving is also unlikely. He still gets some playing opportunities at Real Madrid, and the team's left flank requires backup strength. Currently, no buyer has offered a reasonable price for him, and Van der Vaart himself has expressed his intention to succeed at Real Madrid.

Mohamed Diarra, after recovering from injury, has indeed become a substitute but has no complaints. He now gets some playing time off the bench and believes that with effort, his playing time will increase. Recently, his agent also clarified his position: “Mohamed will participate in the Africa Cup of Nations in January, and at least during the winter break, he will not consider a transfer.”

As for Garay, he is the least likely to leave Real Madrid. On one hand, Real Madrid sees him as a promising asset, and on the other, the team's defense must ensure backup strength. After all, Pepe and Albiol cannot play every single game. Real Madrid absolutely will not sell Garay for a mere 5 million euros. Metzelder has a slightly higher chance of leaving compared to Garay. The German is currently dissatisfied with not playing enough games, as he worries that not playing at Real Madrid will affect his chances of participating in the World Cup. It is reported that Zenit St. Petersburg wants to sign Metzelder, but Real Madrid is unlikely to approve the transfer. The reason is the same as above—Real Madrid needs two backup center backs in addition to the two starting center backs. Unless a replacement is found during the winter break, Real Madrid will not easily accept Zenit's offer.

Wesley Sneijder did not even think of leaving during the most difficult times, and now that his situation has improved, he certainly will not leave. Moreover, his wife Silvie wishes to stay in Madrid for breast cancer treatment. After Kaka's injury, he became a regular starter. In a recent interview, Sneijder explicitly stated that he would not leave Real Madrid: “At the beginning of the season, the situation was indeed very tough, but now I feel happy and satisfied. My playing time is increasing, and I hope to become an important member of the team. In recent matches, I've been playing in key positions, and I'm gradually adapting to the rhythm. I hope to stay at Real Madrid next season and become an important part of the team. Additionally, I hope the team wins both La Liga and the Champions League this season.”

Guti has no motivation to leave Madrid. We have already mentioned that he does not want to leave the environment of Madrid, and his career is nearing its end. He does not wish to wander elsewhere. At present, the ones most likely to leave Real Madrid are Gago and Van Nistelrooy. Both players want to participate in the World Cup and are currently not getting enough playing time. Their agents are actively helping them find buyers. However, suitable buyers have yet to be found. No one is willing to pay the 15 million euros transfer fee for Gago, and Van Nistelrooy's request for a free transfer has not been approved by Real Madrid. They are continuing their search, and Real Madrid, seeing their eagerness to leave and having sufficient depth in their positions, reluctantly agreed to let them go, provided the interested clubs make reasonable offers.

Italian agent Giuseppe Bonucci stated that Van Nistelrooy joining an Italian team is unlikely. In fact, based on the information he obtained, Real Madrid's principle during the winter break is not to let any player leave. One of Real Madrid's bigwigs, Emilio Butragueño, also revealed that Real Madrid will not take any actions during the winter break: “The winter transfer window is short and comes with risks. We have no plans to buy or sell.” (Sergio)
