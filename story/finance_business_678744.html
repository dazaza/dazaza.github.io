What are the basic facts about Hongik University?

Korean Study Abroad Expert: Hongik University is located in Seoul, the capital of South Korea, and was founded in 1946 under the original name of "Hongmun Daehakgwang." It was renamed "Hongik Daehakgwang" in 1948 and finally settled on its current name, Hongik University, in 1949. In 1971, Hongik University developed into a comprehensive university with the art department being the most famous. It is a four-year private university that includes seven junior colleges, eleven departments (24 majors, 29 subjects), a graduate school, and 11 special colleges. To promote the growing economic and cultural exchanges between China and Korea, specialized Korean language courses were established. The International Language Education Institute has an excellent team of domestic and international lecturers, complete teaching facilities, and dedicated instructors to guide students' studies and further education. Additionally, a "one-to-one" system is implemented where Korean students directly interact with Chinese students. Various Korean cultural experience activities are organized so that students can directly experience Korean culture. Students not only receive systematic language education during their studies but also learn about Korea's advanced culture, enabling them to freely adapt to a globalized society and become knowledgeable, versatile talents.

What are the educational goals and principles of Hongik University?

Korean Study Abroad Expert: Hongik University's educational goal is to build a bridge between academia and industry for the benefit of all humanity. "Hongik University" translates to "benefiting humanity" in English. The university is a place where students prepare for their future careers. To achieve this goal, students should first learn independent thinking, free from external temptations, which is the first educational goal of the university. The second educational goal is to cultivate the innovative spirit of students. Innovation is crucial throughout one's life, so Hongik University spares no effort in nurturing students' innovative spirit, allowing them to learn and develop in a diversified society, absorb knowledge and others' experiences, think independently, and serve society with an innovative spirit and professional knowledge, jointly benefiting humanity.

Is it true that Hongik University's art and design rank first in Asia and are world-class?

Korean Study Abroad Expert: Yes, it is true. Hongik University's reputation in fine arts and design education is outstanding, widely supported and favored by various circles. Its industrial design, visual design, and other professional fields rank among the top in Korea and even Asia. Strong majors include industrial design, visual design, advertising design, multimedia design, animation production, and film and video studies. In 2004, it was rated as an "excellent specialized university" by the Korean Ministry of Education, and from 2005 onwards, it received support for three consecutive years for specialized industries. It also signed agreements with Samsung Electronics to "cultivate applicable talents and jointly utilize information networks," establishing an international industry-university cooperation plan and signing an industry-university cooperation agreement with Seoul Metro.

In 2005, Hongik University built the "Hongik Art Building" on its Woo-Ji campus, becoming a top art education university in Korea.

Hongik University's art and design rank well nationwide. Its College of Art and Design includes five majors:

- School of Design & Media

- Product Design Major

This major focuses on cultivating industrial product design talent. Courses mainly focus on furniture design and product design, particularly strengthening students' abilities in product design, material analysis, manufacturing techniques, and product production, enabling them to independently complete works.

- Advertising Communication Design Major

Founded in 1989, it is currently the only major in Korea's advertising design field. Its educational goal is to cultivate designers who are both professional and creative. Courses are divided into theoretical and practical research sections, focusing on cultivating students' self-expression and creativity, allowing them to play active roles in various settings such as advertising companies and design production.

- Digital Media Major

In the rapidly changing era of informatization, whether in education, entertainment, communication, or mass media, digital media plays an indispensable role. Therefore, digital media holds an increasingly important position in today's society. The establishment of this major aims to cultivate students with creativity, specialized technical skills, and management abilities, hoping they will become outstanding individuals in industries such as multimedia and the internet after graduation.

- Animation Major

Animation has become one of the important forms of cultural expression in the 21st century. Combining art with high technology can expand the field of animation. Under the current lack of human resources, we actively educate students in relevant technologies from traditional to digital media, along with professional knowledge and scholars' practical experience, applying the concept of industry-academia cooperation in commerce and industry.

- Film, Video & Moving Image Major

Our goal is to cultivate creative talents in the visual field. This major requires a wide range of skills and professional qualities. Its scope covers photography, directing, audiovisual media, visual art and design, screenwriting, sound and music creation, sales and financial management, computer and network skills. To ensure students' full understanding of visual information, we provide many common courses related to advertising, multimedia design, and animation.

Park Chun-ja is a famous painter, and I heard she graduated from Hongik University's College of Fine Arts.

Korean Study Abroad Expert: That's correct. Park Chun-ja, alias Ji-yu, female, Korean ethnicity, born in Yanji, Jilin. She specializes in Chinese painting. In 1985, she graduated from the Department of Fine Arts at Central Nationalities University and attended the assistant instructor training class at the Department of Chinese Painting at the Central Academy of Fine Arts; in 2000, she obtained her master's degree in Oriental Painting from the College of Fine Arts at Hongik University. She is currently a lecturer at the Department of Arts and Crafts at Beijing Institute of Fashion Technology. Her work "Gazing" won the bronze award at the Zhonghua Cup Grand Prix, and "Erosion" won the silver award at the China Ethnic Hundred Flowers Art Exhibition. Her works are collected by the National Art Museum of China. She has published "The Collection of Park Chun-ja's Paintings." Park Chun-ja started painting from a young age, excelling in detailed color painting of figures, mainly depicting the lives of people in China's ethnic minority regions. Her works use traditional detailed painting methods for flat treatment and unified yet varied colors to achieve simple and ultimate effects in the images. Moreover, Park Chun-ja places great emphasis on exploring and utilizing the form and materials of the image, resulting in aesthetic realms and style changes close to the characteristics of different eras, earning high praise and recognition in contemporary Chinese painting circles.
