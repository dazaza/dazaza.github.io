Who is the person you can talk to anytime, and after seeing this, there's a feeling that makes you want to cry... I remember an episode of "Artistic Life" where Zhu Jun asked actor Wang Zhiwen, who had remained single for a long time: Why haven't you married at 40? Wang Zhiwen said: Haven't met the right one. Zhu Jun then asked, "What kind of girl are you looking for?" Wang Zhiwen thought for a moment and seriously replied, "I just want someone I can chat with anytime." "Isn't that easy?" Zhu Jun laughed. "Not easy," Wang Zhiwen said, "For example, if you suddenly think of something in the middle of the night, you call her, and she says, 'What time is it? I'm so tired, let's talk tomorrow.' Immediately, you lose interest. Some words, some moments, for some people, once you think about it, you don't even want to say it anymore. Finding someone you want to talk to, someone you can talk to, isn't easy."

Yes, I often feel the deep, indescribable meaning in these words. It's really hard to find someone you can talk to anytime.

Perhaps you have good social skills, know many people, and have good relationships with many people. But even your family members who live with you every day, or even your intimate partner, you might not necessarily be able to talk to them whenever you want, say whatever you want, without ever worrying about being rude, feeling guilty, or being cold-shouldered and scolded.

In the vast sea of humanity, with countless names in your contact list and familiar faces by the thousands, sometimes when you open your phone and flip through name after name, how many people can you feel comfortable and at ease with, able to disturb, able to freely express yourself anytime?

Sometimes, we would rather repeat our thoughts a thousand times in our hearts than reveal even a word to those around us. Troubles and frustrations, moods and situations, others who have not experienced them firsthand naturally cannot empathize. Those who understand might offer sincere comforting words, while those who are indifferent will only utter a few stock phrases, making you immediately regret revealing your innermost feelings.

During the day, we heavily armor ourselves, deeply hiding our true selves. Even those closest to us may have concerns, and even those who know us best may have doubts. We are like a group of porcupines, covered in spines, huddling together for warmth but keeping a distance for self-protection. Finding someone you can talk to anytime is difficult. Finding someone who always tells the truth is even harder.

Occasionally, a gurgling spring flows from within us, and we genuinely and passionately reveal our sincerity and warmth, exchanging glances and warming hearts. But soon, we laugh at our own maturity, as hearts remain distant, unable to walk on the same path.

We are becoming less and less genuine, finding it harder to discover authenticity, and increasingly afraid to express our true selves. Our hearts, our once transparent and authentic hearts, now, where can we find them?

Another example comes from the TV series "The Kangxi Dynasty." Among his thousands of concubines, Emperor Kangxi loved Consort Rong most. When he visited Consort Rong, what he loved to say most was, "I want to talk to you." Then, he would confide in her about state affairs. Later, due to circumstances, he had no choice but to depose Consort Rong. Whenever he felt melancholic, he would walk to Consort Rong's palace. However, with the person gone and the palace empty, even as the great emperor of a thousand generations, he had no one to talk to.

These two "successful individuals" had the same simple requirement for their loved ones — just someone to talk to. Carefully reflecting, it is indeed so: no matter how great your achievements, how grand your deeds, you are still a person, a normal person with emotions and desires, hoping to have someone close to you, someone who understands your thoughts and feelings deeply, someone with whom you can communicate and connect. This way, you won't feel lonely or isolated.

I once read this passage: "Marry someone you love to talk to. When you grow old, you'll find that liking to chat is a person's greatest advantage." At the time, I thought it was a sentiment of a little woman. Now, I see that it applies not only to women but also to men.

So, marry someone you love to chat with. The world is too big, too complex, changing too fast. Holding the hand of someone you can talk to anytime, anywhere, means you possess a happiness that even Emperor Kangxi didn't have.

When reading this article, I suddenly felt that I had a clear definition of love. Like Wang Zhiwen, it seems I've always been searching for someone I can talk to anytime, and I also experience the feeling of flipping through my phone contacts and not finding anyone I can casually converse with.

Love should be about speaking freely, relying on each other; it's about feeling connected even in solitude, knowing the journey is long and arduous, yet still being grateful to walk it together...

I don't know if it's because I've grown up, started following the principle of "being humble in conduct and bold in action," and doing everything quietly.

Life before us is like a huge funnel. When young, we meet many people, have much to say, and do so without reservation. One day, we might talk endlessly with one friend, and the next day, another, even fabricating stories that both could discuss with great interest. But as we grow older, we gradually realize that fewer and fewer people listen to us or talk with us. Sometimes, this becomes a luxurious desire. At this point, we might only have one fixed close friend who listens to us when we're lonely, or perhaps none at all.

This kind of loneliness has existed throughout history. Even Lu Xun, upon meeting Qu Qiubai, sighed, "To find one true friend in life is enough, and we should cherish each other as such."

Maybe, on one day, when we find that true friend turns out to be of the opposite sex, love arrives.
