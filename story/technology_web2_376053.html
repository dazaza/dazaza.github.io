Fast Enough: A "Personal Cloud Storage" service based on multi-terminal support (with beta testing experience invitation)

by daodao on 2010-12-27 16:06:28

Gokuai is a "personal cloud storage" service provided to Internet users based on multi-terminal support. It allows for quick access, storage, transfer, backup, and sharing of various media files through online management functions. Gokuai is a service akin to Dropbox but may even go further in offering "personal cloud storage." It provides numerous features such as online network drives, online backup, online playback, file transfer, and sharing. Compared to previously introduced products like Data Bank, Everbox, and Kangaaroo, Gokuai has three differentiated competitive advantages:

1. "Offline Download" can download BT files, HTTP direct address files, and store them on the cloud (PT download supported). Once prepared on the cloud, they can be quickly downloaded onto your machine.

2. "HD Player Push" can send video files from the cloud directly to an HD player at home (requires a network-enabled HD player that supports the Gokuai network push protocol; currently supported hardware includes the Tianmin DMP550 set-top box).

3. "Cloud Transcoding" can transcode video files on the cloud into MP4 formats suitable for various mobile terminals. Currently, it supports 480*320 resolution formats compatible with general smartphones, and will later include formats tailored for high-resolution screens like the iPhone 4 and iPad.

In just half a year, similar products have emerged one after another (such as Shanda’s Everbox, Huawei's Data Bank, Sina's Weidisk, Kingsoft's KuaiPan, Xunlei's Suishenpan, and Kuaiche's Kangaaroo). The rush by both giants and startups clearly indicates the market trend, affirming that personal users' demand for data control and migration will shape the unique development prospects of China's cloud storage market. Faced with such unevenly differentiated products, standing out requires more than just competing on services and operations. Personally, I lean towards enhancing product-level differentiation. Due to some objective reasons, I cannot fully experience all its highlights, but many product innovations proposed by the Gokuai team are worth learning from and emulating.
