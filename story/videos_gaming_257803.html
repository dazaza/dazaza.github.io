Why does the "Golden Feather Award" reject Warcraft?

by nuannuanleng on 2009-11-09 23:07:27

The results of China's prestigious 2009 game selection competition, the "Golden Phoenix Award," have recently been announced across various categories. Unexpectedly, *World of Warcraft* did not win the award for Best Overseas Game. While it might not matter much to players whether *World of Warcraft* won this particular award or not, we still can't help but wonder: why?

**List of Winners in the Online Game Category for the Golden Phoenix Award**

After all, *World of Warcraft* is no small, subpar production. If we were to rank by merit, even at its lowest, it shouldn't have been completely absent from the entire Golden Phoenix Award selection process.

There’s no trace of *World of Warcraft* on the weekly rankings of the Golden Phoenix Award.

Instead, *Tian Long Ba Bu*, which received multiple awards from the "Three Ministries," consistently ranks first every time.

However, after carefully examining the organizers of the Golden Phoenix Award and Chinajoy, this question was quickly answered.

**Who are the organizers of the China International Digital Interactive Entertainment Exhibition (Chinajoy)?**

The China International Digital Interactive Entertainment Exhibition is jointly organized by the General Administration of Press and Publication of the People's Republic of China, the Ministry of Science and Technology of the People's Republic of China, the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology of the People's Republic of China, the General Administration of Sport of the People's Republic of China, the China Council for the Promotion of International Trade, the National Copyright Administration of the People's Republic of China, and the Shanghai Municipal Government.

So, when we see the close ties between a certain department and the Golden Phoenix Award, do we really need to ask why *World of Warcraft* was absent from the entire Golden Phoenix Award selection?
