Man suffering from terminal illness hides his condition from his wife and forces her to get divorce for lack of money for treatment (Photo)(www.dnfaa.com)

by dehua900 on 2011-05-08 17:09:42

In recent days, many enthusiastic citizens and netizens have visited and taken care of Liu Lijun, bringing him money and goods, encouraging him to bravely face the illness. Moreover, one hospital has agreed to provide medical assistance for Liu Lijun.

Narration

He Jinhong borrowed more than 2000 yuan, forcing Liu Lijun to be hospitalized in Xinqu Hospital in Baoding City. However, after being hospitalized for 13 days, Liu Lijun refused to stay any longer. After being discharged from the hospital, He Jinhong planned to live together with Liu Lijun to take care of him more conveniently. But Liu Lijun rejected her offer. "It's already hard enough for Jinhong to raise her daughter, how can she support me as well?" To save money, Liu Lijun rented a small room of about ten square meters, with one bed, one table, and a monthly rent of 80 yuan. But Liu Lijun said he still wanted to find a cheaper place.

Recollection

The Envious Happiness

Wife Learns the Truth and Returns to His Side

Wife's Greatest Wish is to Remarry Him

"When I saw it was her, I felt somewhat at a loss. I looked into his eyes for several seconds and then understood that he had deliberately quarreled with me and forced our divorce because he fell seriously ill and didn't want us two to suffer from his poverty. I hugged him and cried uncontrollably, and he also cried like a child. I was a bit angry with him for concealing such a serious disease for so long and even forcing our divorce. I also felt guilty for leaving him when he needed me most," He Jinhong choked up while saying this.

Yesterday morning, in a narrow rented room in Xiaochecun Village on the west suburb of Baoding City, Liu Lijun, suffering from late-stage hepatic ascites, lay on the bed, repeatedly flipping through old photos with He Jinhong. Although they were no longer husband and wife, the tragic love story of this working couple moved the city where they lived.

In 1996, while working in Xushui County, Baoding, Liu Lijun, who had nothing but debts, won the heart of a local girl. Despite her family's opposition, the girl married him. This girl was He Jinhong. At that time, Liu Lijun worked as a kitchen helper, and He Jinhong was a waitress.

Sudden Illness

An unexpected illness plunged him into pain and struggle

Forcing his wife to file for divorce, waiting for death alone

Later, Liu Lijun started drinking excessively on purpose and would intentionally pick fights with his wife after drinking, leading to endless arguments. "During that period, he seemed like a completely different person, unreasonable and arguing with me." What He Jinhong couldn't bear most was that Liu Lijun began to make things difficult for their daughter. Once during an argument, he even chased his wife and daughter with a shovel. "At that moment, I could no longer endure it. If I had done something wrong, it would be understandable, but he beat his wife and scolded his daughter without reason. Is he still a man?" He Jinhong said that she really started regretting marrying Liu Lijun and left home with her daughter, filing for divorce.

In August this year, a former neighbor found He Jinhong and told her that recently Liu Lijun's belly had swollen like he was carrying a basketball, possibly suffering from some serious illness. "I was shocked at the time, thinking that there must be something Li Jun was hiding from me," He Jinhong said. She hurriedly found Liu Lijun to ask for details, but was stunned when she entered the door. In just a few months, Liu Lijun had become emaciated, almost unrecognizable.

However, by April this year, Liu Lijun's symptoms of leg weakness became increasingly severe. Sometimes, he walked as if drunk, and carrying cement and sand became more and more strenuous. Sensing something was not right, Liu Lijun secretly went to the hospital for an ultrasound examination without telling his wife. The diagnosis revealed that he suffered from severe liver disease, which led to cirrhosis and abnormal enlargement of the spleen. This disease is very difficult to cure, and the initial treatment costs alone would require 20,000 yuan. "Let alone 20,000 yuan, even 2,000 yuan I couldn't afford," Liu Lijun said. Although both he and his wife worked hard to earn money, during this period, his parents passed away due to illness, his wife suffered from serious illnesses twice, and he himself underwent surgery for pleural effusion. Except for living expenses, all the money they earned was spent on medical treatments.

After learning the truth, He Jinhong returned to the side of the critically ill Liu Lijun.

After marriage, Liu Lijun took great care of He Jinhong, becoming a model husband. The next year, the birth of their daughter brought more laughter to the family. "He learned culinary skills and cooked particularly delicious meals. In our household, I never had to cook three meals a day; he always chose what I liked to cook. When I worked night shifts, other colleagues would just eat something simple, but Lijun, no matter how tired he was, always prepared good food for me to take along, and my female colleagues envied me greatly. In 2007, when I underwent a full anesthesia operation for gallstones, he stayed by my side for three days and nights without closing his eyes. That October, I got facial paralysis due to wind exposure, and he, thinking I had cerebral thrombosis, cried loudly while carrying me and holding our daughter, saying 'If your mom isn't here, this family will be over.' These are things my daughter told me later." He Jinhong said that she always believed her original judgment was correct.

Still wants to donate his body

After divorcing his wife, Liu Lijun's condition worsened. Initially, he could still scrape together some money to buy cheap herbal medicine and painkillers to endure. Later, unable to work anymore, maintaining basic living became a problem, let alone treating his illness. He began quietly waiting for death, taking out old photos taken with his wife and daughter, repeatedly touching and scrutinizing them when he could no longer endure the pain.

This touching story, often seen in TV dramas, actually happened around us.

Giving back to society

In June this year, Liu Lijun calmly signed the divorce agreement with his wife. "When they left, my heart was extremely painful, but I couldn't show it on my face, fearing that it would expose the truth. For a moment, I really wanted to reveal the truth and call them back. But no, in this life, I failed to give them the happy life they deserved. It's bad enough that I couldn't provide it, now I'm dragging them down, am I still a man?" Speaking emotionally, Liu Lijun was already tearing up.

Not wealthy

But having

In 2008, to earn more money, Liu Lijun quit his job at the restaurant and took on the physically demanding task of carrying cement and sand, which nobody else wanted to do. "Although it's exhausting, you can get paid immediately, unlike in restaurants where wages are often delayed." Liu Lijun said that by the end of 2008, he started feeling unwell - losing his appetite, feeling nauseous at oily foods, especially experiencing weakening strength in his legs, making him wobble when walking, falling off his bike suddenly, and experiencing rapid vision decline. Initially, Liu Lijun thought it was due to overwork and didn't pay much attention.

Holding the diagnosis results, Liu Lijun was caught in a painful struggle: getting this disease meant not only losing his ability to work and earn money, but also facing enormous medical expenses. In the future, lying in bed immobile, he would need his wife to take care of him, cutting off the family's income source entirely. If he borrowed money for treatment, with his wife's meager salary, how long would it take to repay? Without economic income, wouldn't his daughter be forced to drop out of school? The whole family would be dragged down by him, and the child's future would be ruined because of him. "Rather than letting them suffer, it's better to end my life directly," Liu Lijun said. After a fierce internal struggle, he hid the hospital's diagnosis report and concealed the truth from his wife.

Feeling his condition worsening, Liu Lijun submitted an application to the Baoding Red Cross Society on December 8th, volunteering to donate his corneas and body. Recently, when he learned that Mo Fei, a special guest host of Baoding Radio Station, urgently needed a kidney transplant due to chronic uremia, he requested to donate one of his kidneys.

He Jinhong could only spare time to take care of Liu Lijun, ensuring that she and her daughter ate at least one meal a day with him in his small room, returning to her own rented place at night. "I wish I could spend more time taking care of him, but I have to work to earn money for his treatment," He Jinhong said. To earn more money, she had been working consecutive night and early shifts for over a month, feeling quite exhausted. But no matter how hard or tired she was, she would continue. Now, her greatest wish is for Lijun's condition to improve quickly, allowing them to remarry happily. Even if life remains poor and bitter, she doesn't care.

Written/Photographed by Reporter Wang Yang

Subsequently, Liu Lijun made a painful decision: to avoid dragging his wife and daughter down, he would force his loving wife to divorce him, then wait for death alone. "If I proposed the divorce first, she would surely suspect something, possibly exposing the truth. So I have to let her propose the divorce, making her feel that leaving me is the right choice. Even if she hates me to the bone, I think it's worth it."
