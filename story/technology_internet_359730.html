Yushu Rescue Psoriasis Hospital Gives Love Again

by king0r8k on 2010-07-02 10:40:48

According to the latest reports, two earthquakes occurred in Yushu County of Qinghai Province on the morning of April 14th, 2010. The highest magnitude was 7.1 and the epicenter was near the county town. By 5:00 pm on April 17th, the earthquake had caused the death of 1484 people, 312 missing, and more than 12088 injured. Director Xu Depu, a senior dermatologist at Beijing Jingke Yinkang Hospital (www.npx110.com), introduced to me that epidemic prevention is the most important after the Qinghai earthquake. Yushu County is located in a high-altitude, relatively cold area with a lack of water. After the spring drought, field mice will come out and local epidemics may break out. It is quite important to prevent and control skin diseases. At present, the hospital is preparing to hold a meeting to discuss how to help the disaster-stricken masses of Yushu, especially the wounded and sick, to safely get through the initial period after the earthquake, as well as how to provide them with medicines for the prevention and treatment of skin diseases and simple preventive knowledge. "Contributing our bit of love for our compatriots in Yushu is the responsibility of Chinese people and also the common wish of all medical staff in our hospital," said Director Xu Depu.

It is reported that as early as early April, Beijing Jingke Yinkang Hospital responded to the call of the health management department in Beijing and continued to carry out the "Ten-Thousand-Mile Medical Quality Journey" activity. On one hand, the hospital combined with reality, strictly managed medical quality, ensured medical safety, provided humanized medical services, won patient trust, adhered to the principle of social benefits, and built an honest hospital. On the other hand, the hospital further conducted convenience service activities for psoriasis diagnosis and treatment in residential communities, with the aim of "sharing breath, sharing fate, and connecting hearts" between doctors and patients. They also opened a hotline at 010-63620999 to promote the construction of strengthened exchanges, harmonious development, and common progress between the hospital and the public, creating a good situation of unity and harmony between doctors and patients in the entire city. Since its establishment, the hospital has taken serving the public and building the most satisfactory hospital for the people as its founding purpose. It has received support and praise from a number of old leaders in national health departments and has been affirmed by more than twenty well-known media outlets in Beijing and across the country. The hospital is also the first large-scale, standardized, scientific, authoritative, and highly qualified expert team specializing in psoriasis in the capital.

Following the Sichuan earthquake, the Yushu earthquake once again touched the hearts of all Chinese people, including those at Beijing Jingke Yinkang Hospital. From national leaders to ordinary citizens, and our beloved rescue officers, everyone is contributing their strength during this critical moment. President Hu Jintao specially went to Yushu to inquire about the injuries of the affected people, guided the earthquake relief work on-site, and expressed that "we must cherish every life, focus on treating the injured, arrange for the lives of the affected people, ensure they have food to eat, clean water to drink, shelter, and enough warm clothing, accelerate the construction of infrastructure to create conditions for earthquake relief and post-disaster reconstruction, care for children, and quickly create conditions so they can return to school, even if it's a temporary classroom." Behind these many determinations, we see hope for the revival of the disaster-stricken areas after the earthquake. The extensive medical teams rushing to the front lines of the earthquake have received high praise from all the people in the country, who call them "the cutest white angels."

**Introduction to Yushu County:**

Yushu County is located in the eastern part of the Yushu Tibetan Autonomous Prefecture in Qinghai Province, bordering the Tibet Autonomous Region to the southeast and Sichuan Province to the northeast. The total land area is 15,700 square kilometers. The average altitude within the county is 4493.4 meters. The county governs seven townships and two towns, with 62 village committees and 262 agricultural and pastoral production cooperatives. By the end of 2005, the total population of the county was 89,309. The county seat, Jiegu Town, has historically been a key transportation hub and commercial center connecting Qinghai, Tibet, and Sichuan provinces. Through long-term frequent trade activities and extensive cultural exchanges, it has formed a unique socio-economic, cultural, and natural landscape of the river source region. The hardworking, intelligent, and honest Yushu Tibetan people have lived and multiplied on this land for over four thousand years, creating brilliant civilizations and forming rich cultural deposits and humanistic landscapes.
