Unknown giant bird with 3-meter wingspan and fierce eyes discovered at Mount Songshan in Henan (Picture)

In the Songshan area, there are 43 species of birds in 14 families. However, people cannot match the "Bird King" with any of them.

The villagers of Wangzhuang Village at the foot of Shaoshi Mountain have encountered a rare event these days - a large bird, almost as tall as half an adult, strolls on the hill behind the village every day. Moreover, there are flocks of smaller birds surrounding it like fans, becoming the most beautiful scenery of early spring in Songshan Mountain. Tourists visiting the Sanhuangzhai scenic spot of Shaoshi Mountain also exclaimed that they had broadened their horizons.

Evening News reporter Jianlong Yuan and correspondent Chaohui Zhang provided the text and photos.

From afar, it looked like a bird's nest, but up close, it turned out to be a big bird.

Yesterday, upon hearing the news, I went to Wangzhuang Village. Villager Chunyou Wang said that on the morning of March 14th, he was planting trees on the slope of Shaoshi Mountain with several friends when he noticed many small birds circling around a locust tree about 10 meters away. There was a dark lump on the tree which moved slightly upon closer inspection. Chunyou Wang found this strange and called his friends to look up. His friend glanced up indifferently: "It's nothing, just a bird's nest." "Impossible, www.caidiyuan.com, how can a bird's nest be so big and still move?" Curious, Chunyou Wang pulled his friend closer to the tree and discovered a huge bird perched on the tree!

What surprised Chunyou Wang and others even more was that the big bird wasn't afraid of people. The small birds around it were scared off by the arrival of people and flew away in all directions, but this big bird only looked down at the crowd below, then shifted its gaze to the distance, showing no intention of flying away.

Its head was bigger than a bowl, its eyes fierce, scaring away the old shepherd.

"This thing's head is almost as big as a bowl, its claws larger than an adult's palm, its eyes very fierce, looking at it makes you scared," said Xinjun Guo, the 60-year-old sheep herder of Wangzhuang Village, who still felt uneasy when talking about his encounter with the strange bird.

On the afternoon of March 15th, while herding sheep on the hillside, Guo accidentally spotted a "black-clad person" sitting on a large rock not far from the sheep flock. Thinking it was a tourist visiting the Sanhuangzhai scenic spot of Shaoshi Mountain, Guo warmly greeted the "black-clad person", but received no response.

Feeling awkward, Guo thought the guest might have encountered something and walked over to check it out. As he approached the "black-clad person", he realized it was not a tourist at all, but a huge strange bird! The big bird was staring straight at the sheep flock. "I'm 60 years old, and I've never seen such a big bird before. It looks like it wants to attack my sheep."

Guo observed carefully and noticed that under the bird's wings, there was a patch of red, its wings hanging down, creating a striking red-black contrast. Standing there, it resembled an ancient general draped in a cloak, dignified and imposing. Guo cautiously herded the sheep away from the strange bird, but unexpectedly, the bird calmly jumped down from the rock and leisurely followed the sheep for a while. Guo was filled with unease, both startled and fearful, breaking out in cold sweat.

"Hundreds of Birds Paying Homage to the Phoenix" - "Bird King" Unrecognized

Chunyou Wang said that when standing, the big bird was almost as tall as half a person, dignified and imposing, its eyes gleaming fiercely, exuding an air of killing intent, just like a general observing the enemy and deploying troops; when walking in the grass, it seemed leisurely and composed, graceful and unhurried. What was most surprising was that whenever this big bird appeared, there were always flocks of smaller birds like crows, magpies, and sparrows around it, either hovering near it on the grass or chirping loudly on the branches, creating a lively scene, just like a group of "fans" surrounding their beloved star. This big bird seemed well-versed in the world, not minding at all, appearing calm and composed, enjoying the attention. Occasionally, when it got impatient, the big bird suddenly spread its wings and soared into the sky. Behind it, flocks of black crows and countless magpies took off in alarm, chirping loudly and following closely behind.

Chunyou Wang said that when the big bird spread its wings, they were nearly 3 meters long, resembling the great eagle from "The Legend of the Condor Heroes," and it truly looked like the legendary "Bird King"!

After several days of observation, Chunyou Wang found that the "Bird King" usually roosted at night on a large paulownia tree about 50 meters away from his pig farm, so he placed some pig offal under the tree. "These past few days, I put some every day, and it ate them."

Chunyou Wang led me to the place where the "Bird King" dined. "This is its droppings," pointing to the white substance on the ground resembling lime water. He said that there were many pheasants in Shaoshi Mountain, and the types of birds were numerous, but he had never encountered such a big bird in his nearly 50 years of life. "In recent years, the vegetation of Shaoshi Mountain has become lush, and the ecological environment has greatly improved compared to before. Perhaps because of this reason, such a big bird has appeared." Chunyou Wang said that neither he nor his fellow villagers knew the name of this big bird.

The appearance of the "Bird King" attracted the "Black Hands" of poachers.

On the morning of yesterday, while interviewing, I found six traps made of fine steel wire near the paulownia tree where the "Bird King" rested. Locals also call them "chus."

Chunyou Wang angrily said, "It seems someone wants to harm the 'Bird King.'"

Upon hearing the news, Officer Lili Zhao from the Shaolin Police Station rushed to the scene. After a careful investigation, she dismantled all six traps one by one. "Although we don't yet know the origin of this bird and can't name it, protecting wild animals is the responsibility of each of us," said Lili Zhao. She reminded Chunyou Wang and the villagers of Wangzhuang that if anyone was found harming the "Bird King," they should stop it promptly and report it to the police.

It is understood that in ancient times, Dengfeng and the Songshan area had sparse human settlements, with lush forests and abundant vegetation. The populations and numbers of wild animals were plentiful, including 14 families and 43 species of birds. Among the birds were swans, crows, cicadas, thrushes, owls, mountain eagles, seated mountain hawks, woodpeckers, cuckoos, and orioles. However, people could not match the "Bird King" with any of them.
