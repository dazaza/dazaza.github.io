"The Brutal Life of Single Middle-aged Men"

by jqrxass on 2009-10-17 14:21:51

Lao Zhou has been hustling in the business world for twenty years, and now finds himself a single middle-aged man. Looking back, those who haven't already been half-buried in yellow earth have fared differently: some of his former classmates and business partners have become American citizens, others county party secretaries. In general, most people floundering at sea don’t shine as brightly as those standing on shore.

Certain events - like closing a shockingly large deal, befriending "nobles" within the system, having less-than-stellar colleagues approach him for cooperation, or his cohabiting partner sharing insights with him about every blind date - have caused Lao Zhou to waver between following principles and being indifferent. Many things that Lao Zhou initially couldn't understand, he gradually became accustomed to: for all matters under heaven, anything can become habitual given time.

After a round of hustling, Lao Zhou discovered that there were people crowding ahead and pushing from behind. Moving forward would cost him half his life; retreating would cost him the rest. Lao Zhou knows how to calculate gains and losses, so he quickly stopped daydreaming and continued on his path. But now, Lao Zhou faces yet another round of involuntariness...

Sincere but absurd friendships, stunningly tragic "last shots," bizarre urban风情s, and effortlessly profound life reflections unfold. Various middle-aged men take turns performing their lives of helplessness.

**Single Man Part One**

In the scuffles, more than ten years passed. During this period, his businesses have been varied, generally transitioning from brilliance into decline, from boss to employee. Despite this, his current boss remains polite in front of him, privately always addressing him as "Fourth Brother," and he can also call his boss by name directly. But a boss is still a boss, something not subject to personal will. The boss may treat you with respect, but you cannot fail to consider yourself an outsider. As for the superficial harmonious atmosphere, it's merely both parties being wise enough to grasp the nuances perfectly.

**Single Man Part Two**

Shen Hong stood waiting outside the shop door, making Lao Zhou even more embarrassed. Heart for heart, he isn't an official or a wealthy landowner, so why should she be so humble? Even if there are some feelings involved, it’s normal pursuit, plainly speaking, she still looks up to him. With this shift in mindset, he unexpectedly developed more affection for Shen Hong. The feeling between people is truly a strange thing, or perhaps, because he has received too little real warmth in recent years, any bit of kindness towards him makes him feel immensely grateful.

**Single Man Part Three**

"Everyone sit down." Lao Ma called out, snapping Lao Zhou out of his reverie. The reason he had idle thoughts before was partly due to Li Yun, who was with Jun, being somewhat dull. Although she looked fairly plain and elegant, her clothing was too careless, giving off a just-come-down-from-a-renovation-site vibe, suggesting little potential for development. So he decided to treat them both as drinking companions, drink freely and casually, which made the situation more relaxed.
