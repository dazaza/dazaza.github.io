Balmer's Last Shareholder Letter Exposed: Pushing for a Unified Microsoft

by anonymous on 2013-10-10 18:28:32

Microsoft CEO Steve Ballmer disclosed in a letter to shareholders last year that Microsoft would transform from a software manufacturer into a "hardware and service company." In his final letter to shareholders this year, he stated that "the transformation is still in its early stages, but Microsoft's future products are worth looking forward to, and the best days for Microsoft are yet to come."

Ballmer will step down within the next year. In his final letter to shareholders, he referred to himself as the "deeply loving CEO of the company," which inevitably reminded people of his emotional farewell speech not long ago during his last company meeting.

In the letter, Ballmer expressed that Microsoft was still in the "early stages" of transforming into a family device and service company. After launching Windows 8, tablets, Xbox, phones, and other products, Ballmer said he was proud of the achievements the company had made over the past year. Looking to the future, he viewed "high-value activities" as the goal for Microsoft's products. These "high-value activities" include connecting with family members, meeting with colleagues in different settings, etc., and Microsoft and its partners will launch a series of products and services targeting these "high-value activities" in the future.

The commitments made by Ballmer in the letter align with the "One Microsoft" vision proposed by Ballmer this year. Microsoft is currently undergoing a large-scale reorganization, and the company will soon need to integrate Nokia's devices and services department. Ballmer stated: "This is a landmark event in our transformation process; Microsoft and Nokia together will become the best mobile device manufacturer." Microsoft still has many issues to address regarding the transition involving Nokia, and the company has remained silent since announcing the acquisition deal.

"When I look to the future, I am very optimistic about Microsoft's future products," Ballmer said. He referred to these products including new Windows 8.1 devices, Surface 2 tablets, Xbox One, new Windows Phones, and some enterprise services. Ballmer concluded in the email: "Working at Microsoft is an exciting experience - we have changed the world and achieved unprecedented success - and I know the best days are still ahead."

Below is the full text of the letter:

To our shareholders, customers, partners, and employees:

For me, this is a unique email, the last shareholder email written as the CEO of the company I deeply love. We have always believed that technology will unleash human potential, and this is why I have worked every day with full passion for more than 30 years.

In all aspects, the fiscal year 2013 was a crucial year for Microsoft.

In last year's letter, I announced our fundamental transformation into a device and service company. This transformation has influenced how we operate the company, how we develop new experiences, and how we market products to consumers and businesses.

In the past year, we took bold first steps in our transformation, successfully achieving our goals while increasing revenue by 6% to $77.8 billion. Additionally, through dividends and stock buybacks, we returned $12.3 billion to shareholders, an increase of 15%. Despite record revenue growth, profit figures reflect our investments and the challenges brought by this large-scale transformation.

Based on this background, I will summarize our current situation and future development because it will explain why I am so enthusiastic about future opportunities.

Our strategy: high-value activities delivered by a series of devices and services

We are still in the early stages of our transformation, but we have made strong progress in the past year, launching devices and services that people love and businesses need. We launched Windows 8; achieved a consistent user experience on PCs, tablets, phones, and Xbox; and made significant progress in areas such as Windows Server, Windows Azure, Microsoft Dynamics, and Office 365. We are proud of what we've achieved this year and will continue to release better devices and services at a faster pace.

To strengthen innovation, capabilities, efficiency, and speed emphasized further in our strategy, we announced the execution of the "One Microsoft" strategy in July 2013. We announced that Microsoft's future focus will be on developing a series of devices and services for individuals and enterprises to help global users engage in high-value activities at home, work, and on the go.

In the future, our focus on high-value activities will bring exciting innovations and new growth areas. So what are high-value activities? Consider the most important daily experiences for people, including communicating with family members, searching for term papers, engaging in entertainment, and expressing their ideas. In the enterprise market, high-value activities include holding employee meetings synchronously across multiple locations, analyzing large amounts of data and information, and interacting with consumers.

Microsoft will assist in achieving these high-value activities with a series of devices, including those from Microsoft and its partners, as well as our services.

In the go-to-market process, we will commercialize high-value activities by emphasizing devices and enterprise services. In this model, consumer services such as Bing and Skype will differentiate our devices and serve as entry points for enterprise services, generating part of our revenue through subscriptions and advertising. Enterprise services will remain a strong, high-growth area with significant opportunities, where companies of all sizes seek Microsoft's help to develop cloud computing, manage an increasing number of devices, explore big data, and embrace new social capabilities.

Execution and acceleration

In the past year, we took bold steps in executing our strategy.

First, we are building the new organizational structure announced in July. Teams are collaborating in new and effective ways. The key changes we've made may seem simple but are very powerful: instead of organizing teams around individual products, we organize them by functions such as engineering, sales, marketing, and finance. This ensures that we execute a unified strategy, work as one team, and share common goals.

Second, in September, we announced the acquisition of Nokia's devices and services business, including Nokia's smartphone and regular phone businesses, award-winning engineering and design teams, global manufacturing and assembly plants, and related operational, sales, marketing, and technical support teams. This is a landmark event in our transformation, combining the best mobile devices from Microsoft and Nokia. It will accelerate the development of Windows Phone, strengthen the overall device ecosystem, and create greater opportunities.

Third, in September, we also announced a new department reporting framework. We closely integrated five new reporting departments around our current business priorities, namely providing innovative devices and services to both enterprise and consumer clients. This framework will help us understand valuable information about the progress of key business transformations, driving long-term development.

Regarding the future, I am very optimistic about what Microsoft can achieve. We are accelerating, working with partners to launch Windows 8.1 PCs and tablets, Surface 2, Xbox One, and new phones; developing enterprise services including Windows Server, Windows Azure, Microsoft Dynamics, and Office 365; and innovating in new high-value activities.

Future development

Based on the decisions we have made this year, the strategy we are executing, the organization structure we have designed, the world-class talent we possess, and the devices and services we have developed, we are in a good position to achieve growth in the long-term future and launch world-changing technologies.

Over the past decade, we have achieved incredible results, exceeding $200 billion in operating profits. Not only as a CEO but also as an investor who values my Microsoft shares, I feel optimistic.

Working at Microsoft has been an exciting experience; we have changed the world and achieved record-breaking success. I know the best days are still ahead.

Thank you for your support.

Steve Ballmer

September 27, 2013
