Office English

>> Common Short Sentences Used by Foreigners

★Chickens Come Home to Roost. Evil will be punished.

[Usage]

When saying someone gets what they deserve, besides using "you deserve," you can also use "Chickens come home to roost."

[Dialogue]

A: I heard David is now serving time in jail.

B: Really? Chickens came home to roost.

[Translation]

A: I heard David is in jail.

B: Is that so? Truly, evil has its own reward.

[Learn More]

To say someone reaps what they sow, you can also say:

1) It serves him right.

2) He reaped what he had sowed.

★Act Your Age. Be mature.

[Usage]

If someone behaves immaturely despite their age, you can tell them "Act your age." Meaning, their actions should match their age, i.e., "You should behave more maturely."

[Dialogue]

A: I think we can do it this way.

B: Act your age. Think twice before you do it.

[Translation]

A: I think we can do it this way.

B: Be mature. Think twice before acting.

[Learn More]

To evaluate someone's immaturity, you can say:

1) You are so immature.

2) You shouldn't act like a child.

>> Common Spoken Phrase "Stand Up"

1) Indicates "facts prove to be true":

Upon reflection, what he said doesn't really stand up.

2) Indicates "stand someone up":

Mary got stood up by the blind date.

3) Indicates "stand up, rise":

Stand up please!

4) Indicates "durable, wear-resistant":

This sort of shoes stands up well.

5) Slang: Indicates "end a relationship, break an engagement":

Why did you stand up just one week before your wedding?

6) With "for": Indicates "support someone's view":

We should stand up for what we believe in.
