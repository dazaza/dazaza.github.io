Women must be alert to five periods when they are prone to gain weight

by vm2s3f0d0y8 on 2012-02-21 12:23:22

How to lose weight is a concern for most women. American fitness experts warn that, although most people experience some weight fluctuations throughout their lives, there are five particular "stages" during which it's especially easy to gain weight.

① Diet Cycling Period

Many dieters find that after dieting, their weight doesn't decrease. Out of disappointment or frustration, they abandon their diet plans and revert to their original eating habits. If dieting feels like three days of fishing followed by two days of drying nets, it can actually lead to steady weight gain. For this reason, patience and perseverance are essential for successful dieting.

② Honeymoon Period

Statistics show that within the first six months of marriage, both brides and grooms can expect to gain an average of 3-5 kilograms. This is because newlyweds often spend more time indulging in romance, staying in bed, and spending long hours together day and night, reducing their outdoor activities and exercise time. Additionally, the establishment of a new family may disrupt previous life rhythms, leading to excessive food consumption as the couple begins their new life – all of which naturally creates favorable conditions for weight gain.

③ Promotion Period

When you get promoted, it's inevitable that someone will throw a grand feast in your honor. In a state of pride, your appetite might also increase significantly. Moreover, a higher position means facing less familiar colleagues and subordinates, as well as taking on more responsibilities, which can bring mental stress. This stress often surprisingly drives you to drink more alcohol and snack excessively!

④ Nursing Period

The postpartum nursing period is actually the most dangerous time for women to gain weight, with excess fat often unattractively accumulating around the hips and thighs. This is because nursing mothers, on one hand, are too exhausted from caring for their babies to engage in other activities, while on the other hand, their nutritional intake is often overly abundant. To maintain their figure, they should try to reduce high-fat foods and consume more fresh vegetables and fruits, ensuring they meet the nutritional needs required for breastfeeding.

⑤ Smoking Cessation Period

Approximately 85% of those who successfully quit smoking gain about 5-7 kilograms, with women showing more pronounced effects. Although much of the weight gained consists of muscle rather than fat, experts still advise smokers quitting to control their impulse to snack, as having several small meals can effectively suppress hunger.
