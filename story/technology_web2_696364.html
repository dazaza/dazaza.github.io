(Sohu Sports, Ding Wu) The English media claim that Guardiola is Chelsea's top target for their managerial selection, and that Abramovich has even made contact with Pep. When asked about this matter, Guardiola responded sarcastically: "Yes, I am in contact with Chelsea every day. Boas is a fantastic coach, but unfortunately in the football world, everything is determined by results."

"I don't know what talking about referees will bring us as benefits; when we say these things, we represent the club, not ourselves. I have always believed that the most important people in sports are the players – only they can solve problems when they arise. So I always hope that the players focus on their own affairs because that is the most important thing. Even if we play with 10 men, we can still play well and win." Guardiola continued, "If our words have offended anyone, we apologize, including to the referees. The players are all adults; they can take responsibility for themselves, like Pique. I won't impose too many restrictions on them."

The Spanish Football Federation decided to hold the Copa del Rey final at Atletico Madrid's Vicente Calderon Stadium. Both Barcelona and Athletic Bilbao originally hoped to play at Real Madrid's Bernabeu Stadium, but were firmly rejected by Real Madrid. "The reason Athletic Bilbao fans cannot enter the Bernabeu lies with us; if Barcelona hadn't reached the final, it would have likely been held at the Bernabeu." Guardiola subtly criticized the narrow-mindedness of Real Madrid's management.

Last week, Alexis Sanchez played the full game in a Chile national team friendly match in the United States. He then came off injured as a substitute against Sporting Gijon last weekend. As Alexis Sanchez left the field, Guardiola shouted at him to "use his head more," as Pep believed he didn't need to give his all in such an inconsequential national team game. During a press conference, Guardiola was asked about this again: "I like players who want to play every single game; Alexis wants to satisfy everyone. But he crossed the entire Atlantic Ocean, and he didn't need to play the full game in that kind of match. He had just recovered from a muscle injury, and he should have learned to protect himself. I believe he has learned his lesson from this."

At 3:45 AM Beijing Time on March 8, the second leg of the round of 16 in the 2011/12 UEFA Champions League will feature a key match. At Camp Nou, defending champions Barcelona will host Bundesliga powerhouse Bayer Leverkusen. In the pre-match press conference, Barcelona manager Guardiola used a sarcastic tone to state that he stays in contact with Chelsea every day. Guardiola also pointed out that the reason Real Madrid does not want to host the Copa del Rey final at the Bernabeu is simply because Barcelona has advanced to the final. Regarding the conflict between Pique and the referees, Guardiola firmly defended his trusted protégé. For more La Liga news > >

When asked about tomorrow's game, Guardiola did not think Barcelona had already secured victory: "The Manchester clubs were the strongest, but they have both been eliminated, which shows the difficulty and beauty of the Champions League. In previous rounds of 16 matches, we didn't win the first leg, so this situation will be much better. However, Leverkusen still has a chance, and they will go all out. I hope we can control 70% of the possession. What concerns me most is the speed of the opponent; we must control the ball well and prevent them from playing fast."

Yesterday, Barcelona's spokesperson Freixa publicly criticized the referees, demanding that they provide specific criteria for their complaints against Pique. When asked about this, Guardiola firmly supported Pique: "I completely agree with Freixa's views. We haven't yet decided whether we agree with punishing Pique or not; we just want to know the basis for punishing Pique, and we want to know the specific standards. These matters are not my responsibility; the club's management is handling them, and I'm not a lawyer."
