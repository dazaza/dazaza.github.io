Financial stocks rise, next week's market still lingers in consolidation＿Stocks

by xl0958870 on 2009-12-05 02:24:57

Suddenly, the market doesn't know what kind of thinking is at work, so the market experienced a strange fluctuation of sharp rises and falls in the afternoon. However, regardless of everything, we found that the macro environment hasn't changed, and the market's style transition cannot be completed in just one day, so under such consolidation and capital inflow, the stock index will still be in a process of adjustment next week. After the large-cap stocks have seen consecutive volume expansion, a significant rise has become unrealistic. The only thing left to do is further rest and prepare for new momentum.

After two days of consolidation, the theme stocks will inevitably trigger a technical rebound. Therefore, the market eliminates the potential risks of upward pressure and the surging volume from last week during the adjustment. Although we are not afraid of the market adjustment, it is also quite difficult for a new short-term direction to appear immediately.

Our judgment on the market trend for next week remains that the adjustment will continue, while we remain firmly optimistic in the medium term.

Recently, overseas investment banks have all released their investment reports for the coming year. We discovered that hot money from overseas intends to further enter China, so we cannot solely focus on the reduction of credit and liquidity and their impact on the market. This point requires our deep reflection. We must also consider the hotspots where this hot money is willing to invest. From the sectors they favor, such as securities firms, healthcare reform, new energy, and banks, we find two patterns: First, the basis for the market rally is the launch of stock index futures and margin trading, which reinforces the foundation for the rise of the market; Second, it prompts us to re-examine our economic structure. Stocks related to the low-carbon economy present opportunities for stage gains. Especially those recommended by securities firms, these bring more opportunities due to the increasing number of new listings.

What we need to study is not just buying into whatever they say is good, but understanding the opportunities within and the starting point of their thinking. Regardless of anything, we should correctly understand that thematic investment and value investment need to go hand-in-hand, without tilting too far to one side. Overseas investment banks are bullish on the first half of next year, and many domestic securities firms share the same view. Is the market going to act contrary again? Considering the year-on-year performance and the temporary non-withdrawal of economic stimulus policies, the first quarter may be the best investment period, but caution should gradually increase in the second quarter. We give a rational reminder here not to be fully invested.

The increase in high-priced stocks does not indicate a complete style conversion.

Just like overnight spring breeze bringing thousands of pear blossoms, this description fits perfectly with the current situation of high-priced stocks in the market. Before June this year, there was only one true high-priced stock over 100 yuan, which was Kweichow Maotai. While CSSC Yangzijiang Shipbuilding made two attempts to break above 90 yuan, it eventually returned to its original form. Recently, with the resumption of IPOs and the emergence of the Growth Enterprise Market (GEM), many newly listed companies with high issue prices and high P/E ratios, along with some GEM stocks, have easily surpassed the 100-yuan mark. There are now six stocks priced over 100 yuan, namely Kweichow Maotai, RSTY, Redsun Pharm, Yanghe Shares, Orient Landscape, and Salubris. Additionally, there are several stocks priced over 80 yuan, including old high-priced stocks like CSSC Yangzijiang Shipbuilding and Shandong Gold, as well as newly listed stocks like Jifeng Agriculture Machinery, Robot, and Dinghan Technology. Moreover, there are nearly 100 stocks priced over 40 yuan.

Historically, high dividend-paying stocks can be identified by examining the amount of capital reserve and undistributed profits. Thus, high-priced stocks that have not undergone bonus issues or rights issues are most likely to offer high dividends. However, not all high-priced stocks will necessarily provide high dividends. To effectively screen out the most likely candidates for high dividends among the emerging high-priced stocks, one must also pay attention to the company’s growth prospects and national support policies.

Therefore, the main driver for these high-priced stocks is the end-of-year bonus issues and rights issues. Hence, there isn't enough force to switch completely to blue-chip stocks at the end of the year. Investors should note this and not blindly believe that all blue-chip stocks will soar significantly.

However, it is certain that small-cap stocks offer much more opportunities than large-cap stocks. So, under the condition of an incomplete style conversion - although today the large-cap stocks gained strength, bank stocks also surged, but this is due to the oversold situation leading to a capital game - considering the special nature of the year-end, it might instead be worthwhile to study the opportunities for bonus issues and rights issues in small-cap and mid-cap stocks.

The initiation of the Yellow River Delta holds multiple meanings for the market.

The Yellow River Delta sector is not merely a short-term concept. With the recent approval by the State Council of the "Development Plan for the High-efficiency Ecological Economic Zone in the Yellow River Delta," up to now, the State Council has approved nine regional economic development plans elevated to the national strategy level this year. This number equals the total of the past three to four years, with unprecedented speed and intensity.

The continuous release of regional economic development plans by the central government mainly reflects the continuous deepening of our country's economic development. In the past, the country divided regional economies into three major zones: East, Central, and West. The 17th National Congress proposed the concept of main functional areas. This time, it has been further subdivided according to the characteristics of different places, drawing internal economic links according to intrinsic economic relationships. This plays a very important role in further coordinating the development of China's regional economies, issuing policies for corresponding economic zones, thereby enabling the comparative advantages of various places to be utilized.

According to the already approved regional economic development plans, the following economic regions are included: Yangtze River Delta, Pearl River Delta, Beibu Gulf, Bohai Rim, Strait West Coast, Northeast Three Provinces, Central and Western Regions, plus the recently approved Yellow River Delta. The new regional economic map of China is gradually taking shape. China's economic development will shift from the previous outward-oriented growth structure to a dual development of external demand and internal demand, fully consistent with the country's decision to expand domestic demand. Next year, the country's credit policy will further support infrastructure construction in the Bohai Rim region, central and western regions, and second-tier cities from a regional economic perspective.

Regional rotation gives the market the foundation to develop deeper – based on past experience, the country's policy orientation often triggers the overall outbreak of geographically-based bull stocks. The linkage between regional sectors often propels the market's deeper development. Since the beginning of this year, the market has systematically speculated on regional concepts such as Haixi, Hainan, Changji Tu, and Shenzhen according to national policies. We believe that under the backdrop of the imminent announcement of the "Development Plan for the High-efficiency Ecological Economic Zone in the Yellow River Delta," a new round of regional speculation is quietly unfolding. Some of these are conceptual speculations, while others are stocks with solid performance.

This plan will accelerate the industrial upgrading of relevant companies. The plan states that according to relevant management methods, the region will establish a Yellow River Delta Industry (Equity) Investment Fund. At the same time, the region will also seek to set up a rural commercial bank. Previously, the Shandong provincial government had issued an opinion, proposing that the Shandong provincial government and large provincial enterprises, as well as relevant cities (counties, districts) and large enterprises within the region, jointly fund the establishment of the Yellow River Delta Development Fund. On this basis, financial capital, industrial investment capital, market liquidity funds, and foreign strategic investments will be absorbed to gradually expand the scale, striving to establish the Yellow River Delta Industry Investment Fund to provide financial support for key projects, key enterprises, and promising small and medium-sized enterprises.

This move undoubtedly brings good news to enterprises within the region. Currently, Shandong Haihua has created a new model for seawater utilization, achieving zero emissions and forming an ecological economic effect. Since the new economic map of China has been established and the market has the foundation to develop deeper, research and holding in this area can be increased.

In summary:

Affected by yesterday's decline in the Dow Jones Index, following yesterday's weak trend, the Shanghai and Shenzhen stock indices opened lower today. Subsequently, with strong policy expectations, the market style gradually shifted, with banks, insurance, and securities firms rising strongly, causing the index to surge rapidly. The theme stocks retreated, and capital rotation and stock differentiation began to emerge.

In terms of trading volume, the transaction volume of the Shanghai and Shenzhen markets significantly increased compared to yesterday, indicating possible large-scale capital inflows into blue-chip stocks. We believe that after the Shanghai Composite Index filled the gap from the previous day and then rose steadily along the 10-day and 20-day moving averages, it indicates that the market bottom has appeared and is waiting for the effective release of capital aggregation effects. From the trend channel line since September 29, the index is expected to re-enter the accelerated recovery channel after confirming the retracement.

Considering the special moment at the end of the year, the possibility of a complete style shift is small, so the adjustment may continue until next week. However, we remain firmly optimistic about the medium-term outlook for the first quarter next year, aiming for 3800 points, and gradually implementing the layout in the three-pronged practical approach.
