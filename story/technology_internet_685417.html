Magic's brother returns 300,000 yuan of lost cash to a female passenger

by sznstejx3q3 on 2012-03-01 15:10:41

Mr. Wu, the Honest Finder of Lost Money

For Mr. Wu Shunsheng, a taxi driver in Hangzhou, 300,000 yuan is an enormous sum of money. He said it would take him at least five years of not eating or drinking to earn that much.

However, when the box that had lain in his car's trunk for nearly two hours was opened and 300,000 yuan in cash appeared before his eyes, Mr. Wu was both surprised and relieved — finally, he could return it intact.

The incident began when a careless female passenger left 300,000 yuan in cash in Mr. Wu's taxi.

The day before yesterday afternoon, when the reporter found Mr. Wu at his home, and asked him if he was tempted by the sight of the 300,000 yuan, unexpectedly, Mr. Wu smiled and said, "I've even encountered over a million yuan left in my car before!"

At around 11:40 AM, at Hangzhou's Kaiyue Grand Hotel where Mr. Wu often waits in line for customers, after waiting for an hour, a woman got into his car. She said she was going to Dongfang Jinzuo. He asked her, isn't it on Pinghai Road? Why take a taxi for such a short distance? The polite female passenger replied that she didn't want to walk. Even for such a short trip, it was still business, so Mr. Wu started driving towards Dongfang Jinzuo.

Upon arrival, the female passenger got out of the car. Mr. Wu didn't notice anything unusual and quickly moved on to the next fare.

Mr. Wu soon picked up another customer heading to Fuxing Road.

Just as he finished this fare and while still driving, someone waved to him. As soon as Mr. Wu stopped the car, he was told that there was a call from his company saying, "Is there a box in your trunk? A guest who got in at Kaiyue has left a box behind." Hearing this, Mr. Wu indeed recalled something — a service staff had opened his trunk at Kaiyue, which is why he hadn't seen the female passenger's luggage.

Upon checking the trunk, he indeed found a small box lying there. He immediately instructed the passenger bound for Xiaoshan to place their luggage on the back seat instead, then informed the company that the lady should contact him, and he would return the item.

Soon after, his phone rang again. The female passenger told him that the box contained keys to her house, and without them, she couldn't get in. She kindly requested Mr. Wu to urgently deliver the box.

The Return of 300,000 Yuan

At 2 PM, at the entrance of Dongfang Jinzuo, Mr. Wu met the previous female passenger. In front of the passenger, he took out the box.

At this moment, the female passenger opened the box. It was only then that Mr. Wu discovered that it was full of cash.

Of course, not a single cent was touched. According to their prior agreement, the guest paid Mr. Wu for the fare from Xiaoshan back to the city.

This event was also confirmed by the female passenger, Ms. Jiang. She said that next, she planned to write a commendation letter to Mr. Wu's company.

Such Incidents Happen Frequently

When asked by the reporter if seeing 300,000 yuan didn't tempt him, Mr. Wu said that earning this amount would take him at least five years of not eating or drinking, but he really never thought about keeping it, even after knowing it contained money.

He laughed and said that he'd been driving a taxi for nearly 20 years and had encountered many similar situations. Finding lost phones was already commonplace. The most remarkable incident happened around 2002 when he dropped off two Japanese guests at Xiaoshan Airport. Glancing at the backseat, he noticed — there was a bag lying there! Opening the bag, it was full of US dollars, estimated to be worth over a million yuan in RMB!

Regardless of all else, Mr. Wu turned around and rushed back to Xiaoshan Airport. In the terminal building, he eventually found the two passengers anxiously pacing around.

According to the Qianjiang Evening News
