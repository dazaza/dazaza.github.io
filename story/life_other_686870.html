Accident Scene

Firefighters rescue at the scene

Firefighters spare no efforts to rescue the trapped

The fire department successfully rescued the trapped

At 14:46 on February 29, Anning Brigade of Kunming Fire Department in Yunnan received an order from the command center that a 14-car pile-up accident occurred at K38 section of Kun-Chu Expressway. There were multiple people trapped. The brigade was required to rush to the scene for rescue immediately after receiving the order. Anning Detachment quickly dispatched 3 vehicles and 19 people to the accident site.

At 15:04, the rescue team arrived at the scene. After inquiry with the traffic police, it was found that there were 14 cars involved in the chain collision. Five people had already been sent to the hospital by Kun-Chu Traffic Police Team for treatment. Three people were still trapped in their vehicles. Two of them were trapped in a Mitsubishi SUV, while one person was trapped in a Chery SUV. Some vehicles had slight oil leakage.

At 15:06, after carefully investigating the scene, Chief Ran Guangyu of the scene command quickly mobilized Tida Professional Firefighting Team and divided the rescuers into three groups. The first group was responsible for rescuing the trapped people in the Chery car, the second group was responsible for rescuing the trapped people in the Mitsubishi car, and the third group was responsible for evacuating the onlookers and further investigating whether there were any other trapped people and the oil leakage situation of the collided vehicles. Meanwhile, the water tanker and dry powder foam combined vehicle were ready to prevent oil fires.

At 15:07, the three groups of rescuers began their rescue work simultaneously. The first group used rescue tools such as hydraulic pliers, hydraulic jacks, crowbars, toothless saws, and fire axes to dismantle the Chery car. Due to the severe deformation of the car body, it was difficult to find a better force point, which brought great difficulty to the rescuers. The rescuers quickly used the toothless saw to cut open the car door and found that the trapped driver's abdomen was tightly pressed by the steering wheel, and his right foot was stuck between the driver's seat and the driving platform, unable to move. Deputy Commander Lou Jingming quickly ordered the rescuers to use the hydraulic jack to expand the space of the driving platform and slowly move the trapped person's right foot. At 15:50, one of the trapped persons in the Chery car was rescued and carried onto the 120 emergency vehicle for treatment.

The second group of rescuers was responsible for rescuing the two trapped persons in the Mitsubishi car. According to the detailed investigation by the rescuers on the scene, the Mitsubishi car was sandwiched between two large trucks, with severe deformation of the car body. The distance between the vehicles was about 1 meter wide, only allowing one person to pass through. Due to the mutual embedding of the three vehicles, traction could easily cause secondary injuries to the trapped persons. Both of the two trapped persons in the car were seriously injured. If delayed, both would be in danger of losing their lives. The rescue team quickly used crowbars and hydraulic pliers to dismantle the car body. To create more favorable rescue conditions, one rescuer quickly cleared the debris inside the car and encouraged the two trapped persons. At 15:24, due to excessive blood loss, the trapped person in the passenger seat was unconscious. At this moment, the rescuers used all their strength to use hydraulic shears and hydraulic spreaders to cut and expand the passenger seat area. The hydraulic spreader gradually expanded the space in the driver's cabin, creating an operation space to facilitate the rescuers' work. After 11 minutes of intense rescue, the trapped man in the passenger seat was rescued and handed over to the 120 emergency team for on-site treatment, but was confirmed dead on the spot. Subsequently, the rescuers began to rescue the second trapped person (the driver) in the Mitsubishi car. The commander ordered concentrated efforts to use dismantling equipment to dismantle the driver's cabin. Time passed second by second, and the space under the operating platform in the driver's cabin gradually expanded. After multiple expansions of the driver's platform, the second trapped person (the driver) was rescued at 16:14.

During the tense rescue process of the other two groups, the third group of rescuers also carefully protected the scene, preventing possible sources of sparks, such as power sources and fire sources. They also evacuated the onlookers and used a multi-functional water gun to rinse the oil stains on the road surface to eliminate the risk of ignition, protecting the trapped persons at the scene and the rescuers.
