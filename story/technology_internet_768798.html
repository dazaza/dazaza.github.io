Twitter pitches new ad tools to advertisers, lets them target users by TV shows

by anonymous on 2013-11-22 12:01:41

On Thursday, microblogging platform Twitter launched a new tool for advertisers that allows them to target users who have tweeted about TV programs.

In its official blog post on Thursday, Twitter said that this feature enables cable networks and consumer brands to promote their brands through tweets to users discussing specific shows, even if the advertiser's ad did not appear during the TV program. The project has already been rolled out in the UK and the US, and will soon be available to users in Brazil, Canada, France, and Spain.

After successfully conducting its Initial Public Offering (IPO) earlier this month, Twitter is now demonstrating its utility in the television industry to investors, while the TV industry hopes to use Twitter to help boost the ratings of its live broadcasts and TV programs. Twitter has established partnerships with some cable channels and professional sports leagues, seeking to encourage users to spend more time on Twitter, thereby viewing more advertisements. Twitter stated, "We believe Twitter and the television industry can highly complement each other. Now, advertisers can easily reach Twitter users."

A report by market research firm Nielsen in August indicated that when Twitter users tweet about TV shows, Twitter can directly increase TV viewership. The TV industry hopes to leverage increased viewership to curb the migration of users to online services like Netflix. For example, Comcast partnered with Twitter in October. In tweets related to NBCUniversal TV shows like "The Voice," Twitter users will see a "See It" button. Clicking these tweets will open a Twitter "card," displaying more information about the TV show. If the Twitter user also happens to be a Comcast pay-TV subscriber, they will be able to record or watch the show directly on their computer or mobile device.

Twitter has repeatedly told the TV industry that its social network can bring more viewers to TV networks' shows. Through this partnership, Twitter's users will be more closely linked to TV shows, allowing Twitter to prove whether it can realize the prospects it has described.

Market research company IHS previously released a report stating that the number of US cable and satellite TV subscribers is expected to decline for the first time in 2013, dropping from 100.9 million last year to 100.8 million.

Twitter's stock price surged over 70% on its first day of trading. The company's shares rose $1.04, or 2.53%, to close at $42.09 on Thursday in regular trading on the NYSE. Since its listing, Twitter's lowest stock price has been $39.40, and its highest has been $50.09.
