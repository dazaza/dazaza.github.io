Nanjing Certificate: Two brothers' three children were abducted at the same time, and after 17 years of searching, one child is finally coming home.

by jeerly94w on 2012-02-16 09:54:32

Golden Guizhou Online News: Every family searching for their loved ones has a heartbreaking story. Yesterday, the hearts of Xiao Lifu and his brother, who lost three children 17 years ago, were finally filled with a bit of sweetness. Thanks to a report published in our newspaper in 2009, they successfully found one of the three missing children - Xiao Yusong.

Busy with business, the three children disappeared right beside them.

Yesterday afternoon, in the office of Xinhua Printing Factory, I met Xiao Lifu, who had struggled on the road of seeking relatives for a full 17 years.

"The children went missing on August 26, 1994." Recalling the missing children, Xiao Lifu still couldn't believe that moment was real. He said that at the time, he and his wife were selling goods near the Guiyang Hotel fountain. His two sons (Xiao Yusong, 6 years old, Xiao Yuqing, 5 years old) and his younger brother Xiao Ligui's son (Xiao YuKun, 6 years old) were playing beside the stall. Because they needed to take care of their business, they didn't pay attention to the children playing nearby.

Around 11 PM, when the couple prepared to close the stall and go home, they were shocked - the three lively children had vanished. Immediately, the couple stopped what they were doing and searched everywhere, but there was no news. Realizing that the children had been abducted, his wife Zhang Jiqin cried all night.

Afterwards, in order to find the three missing children, the couple called on friends and relatives and searched for an entire month around Guiyang, but found nothing.

Unable to find the children around Guiyang, the couple then went to Dalian, Fujian, Guangzhou, Zhejiang, and other places to search. "Going to these places didn't yield any clues, it was just out of desperation," Xiao Lifu was like fishing for a needle in the ocean, trying his best to search.

"The longest time I spent was in Dalian, about a month." Xiao Lifu said, since the family wasn't very wealthy, during that month, he often went without food for three or four days to save every penny, allowing him to travel further and have more hope of finding his son.

Thus, Xiao Lifu and his wife searched for 15 whole years, still without any news. At this point, Xiao Lifu's leader, Ms. Teng, heard about his experience of looking for his son and suggested seeking help from the media. Then, on June 25, 2009, a report titled "Two Brothers' Three Sons Abducted Simultaneously" was published in our newspaper.

Subsequently, many kind-hearted citizens provided leads to Xiao Lifu and his wife, even though they ultimately couldn't be used, it gave the couple a glimmer of hope. In order to expand the scope of the search, Xiao Lifu specifically saved up to buy a second-hand computer and asked his daughter to post the newspaper article, along with their home address and contact information, on the "Baby Come Home" website.

Good things come to those who wait. On the afternoon of the 16th, Xiao Lifu's daughter received a call from a stranger. The voice on the other end belonged to a young man who, after multiple confirmations, revealed his intentions and requested a video call.

"After opening the video, the young man didn’t ask too many questions, but first told us everything he remembered. He said that the house he lived in as a child was a two-story building, in front of which there was a high slope. There was also a madman and a scavenger elder by the door. Not far away, there was a primary school. His grandmother’s house had many trees, and once, he fell into the toilet..." Xiao Lifu's daughter said that her father was sitting beside her during the video call, and afterward, he burst into tears on the spot.

It turned out that the high slope mentioned by the young man was Yinxian Alley in Fushui North Road, and the nearby primary school was Yu Ying Primary School. As for falling into the toilet, it happened when he accidentally fell into a latrine while going to the countryside with his father.

Even though he felt assured inside, Xiao Lifu still arranged another video call with the young man the next day and invited friends, relatives, and neighbors for the video call. After the video ended, everyone said that the young man looked exactly like Xiao Lifu when he was young. "Finally, we've found him!" One neighbor even cried with excitement.

In the video, the young man said that over the past dozen years, he had been searching online for his biological father. Last year, he even checked the Guiyang Public Security Bureau's Missing Children Registration, but still found nothing. A few days ago, his cousin recommended that he browse the "Baby Come Home" website. Unexpectedly, he saw the article "Two Brothers' Three Sons Abducted Simultaneously" and the photos on it. Wasn't Xiao Yusong mentioned in the article another name he remembered? With some unease, he showed the photo on the internet to his adoptive father, and only after his confirmation did he make the call to verify, unexpectedly finding the father he had been searching for so long.

In the video, the man repeatedly said: "Dad, when I return to Guiyang on the 21st, let's immediately do a paternity test to set everyone's mind at ease." Hearing his son's words, Xiao Lifu kept saying: "No need, no need, my child, it's good enough that you can come back home..."
