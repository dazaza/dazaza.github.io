The Person Who Once Cherished Me

To the one who once said they liked me so much, the one who said life was dark and colorless without me.

Where are you now, and what are you doing? I don't know.

The one who once said they could no longer bear to be far away from me, what are you doing now?

I don't know.

I don't even know when it started that I couldn't find you anymore. You were always so busy, telling me how tired your work made you!

I had a day and a half off on weekends, happily going to have lunch with you. I spent the entire afternoon until nightfall by your side, sitting next to you while you played mahjong, inhaling secondhand smoke, enduring hunger until I forgot about it, simply because I had little rest time and wanted to stay with you a bit longer.

When looking for rental information, I only considered places near your factory. I just hoped to spend more time with you after work, hoping to bring you a bit more warmth, hoping that my presence could alleviate some of your fatigue and exhaustion.

But I can't find you.

You didn't want me to rent a place outside, as it was expensive and unsafe, so you applied for a room in the factory for me. I was grateful and happy because it would bring us closer. But after a week, I still hadn't moved in. On weekends, I got up at nine in the morning and carried things from the fifth floor to the first floor, back and forth, until three in the afternoon. I was hungry and exhausted, lying in bed not wanting to get up. After eating dinner, I didn't blame you at all.

I complained that you always played mahjong until late at night. You confidently told me that this was your only form of entertainment. You said working was tiring, and asked me if it wasn't okay to entertain yourself. You questioned whether I even wanted to take away your only form of entertainment. I had nothing to say. Every time I passed by after work, I saw several people gathered around a table playing mahjong happily. When I opened the door, the room was filled with smoke as if a fire had just been extinguished. Still, I sat obediently by your side, inhaling secondhand smoke, patiently waiting on an empty stomach until I left the room with a headache, but your entertainment activity still hadn't ended. I remember you used to play basketball, but now it seems you've stopped, maybe because of the cold weather.

Of course, sometimes you stayed in the house instead of entertaining outside. But even then, your eyes never left your phone. I've told you countless times that staring at the phone all day is bad for your eyes. I've repeatedly said, "Stop looking and spend some time with me, talk to me, or just be with me, even if there's nothing to say." I even cried over this before, but what good did it do!

You didn't like eating noodles, so I stopped making them, even letting them spoil while I cooked rice instead. You didn't like drinking congee, so I only made it once (that was when my stomach hurt and I vomited, leaving my stomach empty). After being busy all day cooking, I asked you to help me wash the dishes, but you refused, angrily saying we shouldn't cook in the future and that washing dishes should be my responsibility.

Like today, I asked if you wanted me to come home early or bring something to eat back, because I hoped to return early and spend more time with you. But what did you say? "Whatever, I'm out. I'll be back late. Playing mahjong again." So, I went to the supermarket. Although I was angry and felt wronged, I still brought a bottle of red wine when I came out because you said it tasted good.

Walking on the road, I wondered if there was water at home, unsure if you had fetched any. Since you've been resting these past few days, I assumed you would have fetched water. The first thing I checked when I got home was the thermos, and indeed, you hadn't fetched a drop of water.

A kiwi has been sitting there for so long because I've been looking for an opportunity to share it with you, digging into it together. Until it softened, we never had the chance to eat it.
