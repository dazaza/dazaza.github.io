China's largest joint venture refining and petrochemical project is born

by jdhgsa078 on 2011-08-17 15:44:26

Guangdong Province has always been a large consumer of refined oil products in China, but there has always been a shortage in the supply of these products. Simultaneously, Guangdong is also a major consumption hub for chemical products such as polyethylene and polypropylene, and it faces the largest deficit in these products, significantly impacting the national economy and daily lives of the general public. The Sinochem炼化 project, however, is well positioned to ease this contradiction. Moreover, while Guangdong currently possesses considerable refining capacity, only a few refineries are capable of producing Euro IV standard gasoline. After the implementation of new emission standards in Guangzhou and Shenzhen, even the locally produced gasoline and diesel cannot be used in the Pearl River Delta region. The Sinochem炼化 project will become China's first refinery and petrochemical plant adhering to the Euro IV standard. Once completed, it will meet the demand for refined oil products in the Guangzhou area, alleviate the supply-demand imbalance of oil and petrochemical products in the Pearl River Delta and surrounding regions, and adjust the industrial structure and layout of China’s petrochemical industry.

As the largest joint venture project in China, the Sinochem炼化 project fosters mutually beneficial cooperation between China and Kuwait, a major oil-producing country, thereby promoting friendly relations. The implementation of this project will add a reliable crude oil supply channel for China, enhancing the security of our petroleum supply and strengthening national energy security.

According to available information, Zhanjiang is planning grandly around this project. Petrochemical projects have a significant impact on economic growth. Deputy Director Zhong Shengbao of the Zhanjiang Development and Reform Bureau stated that Japan and Singapore’s early economic take-offs were largely due to petrochemical projects. In 1973, Japan's GDP was still relatively low, but by 1976 after the launch of petrochemical projects, the GDP doubled, and again doubled after 1979. After the Sinochem炼化 project commences operations, it will increase the total industrial output value by 65 billion yuan and add 5.8 billion yuan in local tax revenue. This figure refers solely to the refinery; it does not include downstream links.

However, concerns about the project have persisted.

Firstly, environmental concerns remain predominant. Although the project has passed the environmental assessment (market conditions: http://www.gtobal.com/info/t_963.html), villagers on Donghai Island, Zhanjiang, where the project will be located, still harbor worries. It is reported that within a 5-kilometer radius outside the factory boundary, there are 14,382 households with approximately 68,360 people; within a 1.3-kilometer radius of the health protection zone, there are 782 households with about 3,693 people. Some residents of Zhanjiang have expressed their sentiments: Zhanjiang boasts clear blue seas and skies, an unparalleled environment bestowed by nature. If the petrochemical project moves in, there is a risk that all this—blue skies, white clouds, and clear seas—could disappear.

Additionally, following the 7·23 accident, the public has generally shown skepticism towards large-scale projects pursued at all costs. Industry insiders have warned that although this massive refining project has reached advanced levels in terms of equipment, if management standards do not keep pace, any accidents could be more difficult to handle. Experts further point out that in Guangdong alone, there were 8 petrochemical safety accidents from 2006 to 2007, with the more tragic lesson being the 1993 QingShuiHe explosion in Shenzhen. Therefore, while pursuing large projects, high-standard management must also be ensured.
