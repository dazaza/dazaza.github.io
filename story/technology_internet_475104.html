Russian woman plays naked with white whales in minus degree sea water (Picture)

by iface011 on 2011-08-12 11:35:30

In order to prevent the belugas from swimming too far away, experimenters cordoned off part of the sea area so that Natalia could catch up with the belugas without swimming too far.

According to reports on June 16th by British media, Natalia Avseenko, a two-time world champion in women's free diving from Russia, followed the advice of marine biologists. She stripped naked and swam and played with two belugas in cold water below zero degrees Celsius, truly "returning to nature."

Belugas are giants in nature and are known for their faces which can express emotions similar to humans. Apart from those in aquariums, there are currently about 100,000 belugas living in the ocean worldwide.

Natalia Avseenko, experienced as a two-time world champion in women's free diving, recently at the age of 36, carried out a bold and "beautiful" experiment in the White Sea near the Arctic Circle in northwest Russia. She was completely naked while dancing and playing with belugas underwater in the freezing water. A Russian photographer captured these scenes. Reports suggest that Avseenko seemed like a beautiful "Beluga Princess" in the water.

Marine experts suggested that since belugas are very sensitive to man-made materials such as diving suits and do not like to touch them, Natalia would be better off going into the water completely naked for a more "intimate" skin contact if she wanted to get closer to the belugas.

It is reported that the water temperature in the local sea area is around -1.5 degrees Celsius. Natalia, who stripped naked, would obviously be "shivering with cold." Generally speaking, an ordinary person staying in water below zero degrees for 5 minutes has a risk of death. However, Natalia, with rich diving experience, not only is a professional yoga practitioner but also has trained herself to hold her breath underwater for long periods. She can stay in water below zero degrees for about 10 minutes and 40 seconds.
