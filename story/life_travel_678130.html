Spring Huangshan Travel Strategy

by jj0505 on 2012-02-27 11:54:13

I. Distinguish the Three "Mount Huang" Concepts

After the establishment of Huangshan City, due to historical reasons, three basically identical place names emerged: Huangshan City, Huangshan District, and Mount Huang Scenic Area. The predecessor of Huangshan City was Huizhou Region in Anhui Province, established as Huangshan City in 1987, with its municipal government located in Tunxi, which is the political, economic, and cultural center of Huangshan and serves as the gateway for southern entry into Mount Huang. Huangshan District was originally Taiping County, located at the northern foot of the Mount Huang Scenic Area, serving as the north gate of Mount Huang. The Mount Huang Scenic Area is what truly represents Mount Huang for us tourists.

Therefore, tourists should clarify these three places before visiting Mount Huang and choose their destination and where to disembark.

The Mount Huang we are about to climb, namely the Mount Huang Scenic Area, is located in Tangkou Town, while Tunxi is the urban area of Mount Huang, 69 kilometers away from the Mount Huang Scenic Area, approximately a one-hour and ten-minute drive.

Note: Some vehicles first arrive at Tangkou and then proceed to Tunxi; be sure not to take the wrong route.

II. Mount Huang's Gates

Generally speaking, Mount Huang has two gates: the South Gate and the North Gate. The South Gate refers to Tangkou, where you can board buses from the transfer center up the front mountain to Ciguang Pavilion and the back mountain to Yungu Temple. The North Gate refers to Taiping in Huangshan District, where you can take the cable car or hike up the mountain from Songguan Temple.

III. Mount Huang Admission Tickets

Mount Huang admission tickets have two price categories: off-season and regular season. The off-season runs from December 1 to February 28 of the following year, while the regular season runs from March 1 to November 1. Off-season ticket prices are 150 yuan per person, 115 yuan per person for students, military personnel, and those over 60 years old, and free for those over 70. Regular season prices are 230 yuan per person, 115 yuan per person for students, military personnel, and those over 60 years old, and free for those over 70. Golden Week falls within the peak tourist season for Mount Huang, during which ticket prices do not increase.

IV. Special Precautions for Mountain Climbing and Sightseeing

1. While sightseeing, walking and looking around simultaneously divides your attention and increases the risk of slipping. Therefore, visitors should remember the principle of "look at the scenery without walking, walk without looking at the scenery," "don't miss the grand views, don't linger on small ones." Attached: Best scenic spots and winter travel tips.

2. The steep and perilous trails of Mount Huang make it advisable to wear rubber or cloth shoes. With many stone steps and hazardous sections, climbers should lean forward when ascending, especially when descending. It's recommended to carry light gear. Before departure, extra luggage can be stored at the small item storage areas of hotels and guesthouses at the base of the mountain. Due to strong winds, summer thunderstorms may occur. During thunderstorms, climbing is not advised, nor should you touch metal cables or seek shelter under trees, and carrying an umbrella is also discouraged. For winter visits, wearing down jackets and windproof hats is recommended. Roads are slippery, so wearing hiking shoes is advisable, and adding straw shoes would be even better. Additionally, don't forget to bring a walking stick and gloves.

3. Temperatures on Mount Huang are approximately 10 degrees lower than on the plains, with strong solar radiation. Appropriate sun-protective clothing and hats should be prepared. If planning to stay overnight on the summit during the peak season, finding available accommodations might be challenging. When there are too many tourists, even renting coats could be difficult. At such times, raincoats become crucial as they offer better wind resistance than cotton clothes but are still much better than ordinary sweaters or jackets. Moreover, the Huangshan region receives abundant rainfall, with precipitation occurring seven or eight days out of ten in summer. On the peaks, due to strong winds and steep paths, using umbrellas is prohibited, making raincoats indispensable!

4. Medical Care: Those with high blood pressure or heart conditions should carry necessary emergency medications and avoid traveling alone, especially in hazardous areas. Elderly and young travelers should be accompanied by family members. There are clinics at Beihai Hotel and Yupinglou. If experiencing difficulty descending the mountain, stretchers are available to transport patients to the Huangshan Convalescent Hospital Clinic behind the Huangshan Hotel at the foot of the mountain.

V. Introduction to Mount Huang Scenic Area

1. The Mount Huang Scenic Area is divided into five smaller scenic areas: Hot Springs Scenic Area, Yuping Scenic Area, YunGu Scenic Area, Beihai Scenic Area, and Xihai Scenic Area.

Hot Springs Scenic Area

This is usually the starting point of Mount Huang tourism because most vehicles heading up the mountain stop here. This area is home to many famous hot springs and numerous hotels and restaurants within the scenic area. The temperature of the Huangshan hot spring river remains around 42°C all year round, clear and sweet, suitable for both bathing and drinking.

Yuping Scenic Area

Lianhua Peak and Tiantu Peak are the highlights of Mount Huang tourism. Most of the extraordinary peaks and landscapes of Mount Huang are concentrated here. Tiantu Peak, Yuping Peak, and Lianhua Peak are the three most exquisite peaks. Additionally, "One-Line Sky," "Penglai Three Islands," and "Hundred Steps Cloudy Stairs" can be found here, along with the famous Welcoming Pine.

YunGu Scenic Area

Composed of famous pools and strange rocks, the YunGu Scenic Area is particularly known for its pools. Among the three major pools of Mount Huang, YunGu Scenic Area includes two: Jiulong Pool and Hundred Feet Spring, leaving only Renzi Pool to the Hot Springs Scenic Area. Standing at the viewing platform, you can see waterfalls cascading like white silk ribbons, impressive during the rainy season.

Beihai Scenic Area

This area features peaks, pines, and seas of clouds. From Guangming Peak, you can view the sea of clouds. Paiyun Pavilion offers the best panoramic views of the unique rock formations of Mount Huang. Qingliang Pavilion, known as the first terrace of Mount Huang, and Shixin Peak, where "if you haven't reached Shixin Peak, you haven't seen the Huangshan pines," are must-see attractions. Near the Beihai Scenic Area are concentrated viewing hotels. If you have ample time to visit Mount Huang, consider staying here.

Xihai Scenic Area

The sea of clouds in Xihai is exceptionally beautiful and one of the must-see sights of Mount Huang. The most charming and deep part of the Xihai Grand Canyon, also called the Dreamlike Scenic Area, is located here. From Paiyun Pavilion, you can look up at the peaks of Xihai and down into the Xihai Grand Canyon. It's not only the best spot to appreciate the unique rock formations of Mount Huang but also a renowned place to watch the sunset over Xihai.

2. Major Attractions of Mount Huang

Three Wonders and Four Absolutes

Mount Huang is characterized by "no mountain without peaks, no peak without rocks, no rock without pines, and no pine without uniqueness," renowned worldwide for its "three wonders": peculiar pines, strange rocks, and seas of clouds. Adding hot springs, it is collectively referred to as the "Four Absolutes."

Peculiar Pines: Ranked first among the four absolutes, Huangshan pines are an independent species of pine, mostly growing on cliffs, graceful and distinctive. The famous Welcoming Pine located in front of Yupinglou is well-known. Additionally, at the back mountain's Shixin Peak, you can see many peculiar Huangshan pines. It is said, "If you haven't reached Shixin Peak, you haven't seen the Huangshan pines."

Strange Rocks: The peaks of Mount Huang are steep and majestic, with dense clusters of bizarre rocks scattered throughout the mountains, intricate and mysterious. The rocks of Mount Huang have been weathered for a long time, some curved, straight, circular, or square, others fractured horizontally or vertically. The thousands of peaks and valleys form a harmonious landscape, naturally enhancing the beauty of Mount Huang significantly.

Related Scenic Spots: Flying Stone, Squirrel Leaping Over Tiantu (located at Tiantu Peak), Golden Rooster Crowing at Tianmen, Drunken Stone, Dream Pen Blooming Flowers, etc.

Seas of Clouds: Marvelously resembling seas yet not seas, non-seas yet similar to seas. When appearing, they seem like surging white waves submerging deep valleys and gorges, exposing only the peak tips like islands floating in the sea. In an instant, they surge and splash, with waves crashing against the shore. Every year from November to May is the ideal season to view the seas of clouds in Mount Huang, especially after snow or rain when the skies suddenly clear. The sunrise and sunset formed during these times create colorful seas of clouds, known as "Colorful Seas of Clouds," which are the most spectacular. In summer, the seas of clouds in Mount Huang are relatively less frequent.

Best Viewing Locations: To view the sea of clouds, Wenshu Platform at Yupinglou for the Front Sea, Qingliang Platform atop Lion Peak for the Back Sea, Bai'e Ridge at Donghai Gate for the East Sea, Paiyun Pavilion for the West Sea, and Guangming Peak for the Sky Sea are the most suitable.

Mount Huang Unique Scenery

Besides the three wonders and four absolutes, Mount Huang boasts countless other unique sceneries that captivate visitors.

No wonder Xu Xiake exclaimed, "After returning from the Five Great Mountains, one doesn't look at other mountains; after returning from Mount Huang, one doesn't look at the Five Great Mountains."

Mount Huang Sunrise: With its towering terrain, surrounded by thousands of peaks and often shrouded in mist, Mount Huang offers magnificent sunrise views. The sunrise on Mount Huang is grand and spectacular. The most ideal locations for viewing the sunrise are Qingliang Platform in the Beihai area and Lion Peak, followed by Guangming Peak and Yupinglou.

Sunrise Times: Spring - 5:30 to 6:00 AM; Summer - 4:40 to 5:20 AM; Autumn - 4:50 to 5:20 AM; Winter - 5:30 to 6:00 AM.

Mount Huang Evening Glow: The Xihai area of Mount Huang often features seas of clouds, presenting unique scenery. As evening approaches, the red sun descends, casting beautiful rosy hues over the peaks and clouds. As the sunlight changes, the colors reflected on the peaks also change, from crimson red to vermilion red, pale red to dark red. When the red sun nears the horizon, the western sky turns a bright red, with the fiery sun and layered peaks mutually reflecting each other, creating a grand spectacle known as the "Sea of Glow."

The best locations for viewing the Xihai evening glow are Paiyun Pavilion and Danxia Peak.

Mount Huang Glories: Around the sun or moon in cloud layers, inner violet and outer red colored halos appear, known as Solar Glory or Lunar Glory, anciently referred to as "Strange Light." These beautiful halos are formed when sunlight or moonlight diffracts through fine water droplets or ice crystals in clouds, commonly appearing on altocumulus, cirrocumulus clouds, and sometimes on stratocumulus clouds. The size of the halo is inversely proportional to the size of the water droplets or ice crystals.

Mount Huang Buddha's Light: When standing facing the sun on a peak top, with clouds and fog ahead and clear skies behind, a colorful halo appears on the cloud and fog screen, clearly surrounding your own head image. As you move, the shadow moves; as you stand still, the shadow stops, akin to a "Buddha Image." When the sunlight is strong, the halo presents seven colors, red on the outside and purple on the inside, dazzling and splendid. Sometimes, multiple Buddha's Light halos appear consecutively, quite extraordinary.

Mount Huang Buddha's Light occurs on average 42 times a year, about 2 to 5 times a month. The golden season for Buddha's Light appearances is typically before 9:00 AM on mornings after rain and after 5:00 PM on evenings after rain. It is more commonly seen at Yupinglou, Tiantu Peak, and Baiyun New Path, generally above an altitude of 1600 meters.

Snow Scenes of Mount Huang: The winter season of Mount Huang is relatively long, entering winter around late October. December to February of the following year is the peak snow season and the best time for winter tourism. Snow scenes can be observed as early as late October. Even without snowfall, beautiful "frost-covered trees" (also known as frost hanging) can be seen, draping trees, rocks, and houses in a crystalline white layer, whiter and more transparent than snow, hence more elegant and full of charm.

Best Locations: Yupinglou, Beihai, Songgu, YunGu, and Hot Springs.

Self-guided Tour Tips for Mount Huang

Route Planning:

Mount Huang is extremely beautiful, so if you plan to visit, it's essential to carefully plan your itinerary. One to two days are absolutely insufficient. Often, people complain about Mount Huang not being good, but this is usually due to poor trip planning. Also, accommodation on the mountain can be quite expensive, so it's advisable to book online or via phone in advance to rest well and enjoy the trip fully. Regarding safety, the public security on the mountain is excellent, but always pay attention to foot safety, adhering to the saying "look at the scenery without walking, walk without looking at the scenery."

Two-day Itinerary Reference for Ascending Mount Huang

Day 1: YunGu Temple → Bai'e Ridge → Shixin Peak → Lion Peak → Mengbi Shenghua → Paiyun Pavilion → Flying Stone → Guangming Peak

Day 2: Tianhai → Aoyu Peak → Lianhua Peak → Yupinglou → Tiantu Peak → Ciguang Pavilion

First Day: In the morning, take a vehicle from Tangkou Town to YunGu Temple, reaching Bai'e Ridge via YunGu Temple either by cable car or on foot. Proceed to the Shixin Peak Scenic Area (1 km). Shixin Peak is the world of Huangshan pines, where you can admire famous pines like Wulong, Longzhao, Heihu, and Lianli. Continue to the Beihai Scenic Area (1.5 km), the core area of the Huangshan Scenic Area, featuring concentrated and diverse wonders. Beihai Hotel is located beside Lion Peak at an altitude of 1630 meters, serving as the reception center for the back mountain. At Beihai Dawn Pavilion, you can enjoy the beautiful scenery of Shixin Peak and Shisun Peak. In Beihai Sanhua Wu, you can admire "Mengbi Shenghua." Proceed to Paiyun Pavilion in the Xihai Scenic Area (0.5 km). The Xihai Scenic Area is an excellent location for viewing the sea of clouds and sunset. From Paiyun Pavilion, you can overlook the peaks of Xihai and the evening glow. The Flying Stone stands tall on the peak. Behind Paiyun Pavilion is Danxia Peak at an altitude of 1712 meters, offering a grand view of the sunrise above the clouds, as well as the Flying Peak and Jiulong Peak. Proceed to the Flying Stone (0.5 km). From the Flying Stone to Guangming Peak (1 km). Guangming Peak is the second highest peak of Mount Huang at an altitude of 1860 meters, with a broad and flat top, making it one of the best places to view the sea of clouds. You can overlook the East Sea, South Sea, West Sea, North Sea, and Sky Sea, with the five seas' clouds visible all at once. Return to Beihai and stay overnight at Beihai Hotel or Paiyun Building.

Second Day: Depart from Beihai, proceed via Guangming Peak to the Tianhai Scenic Area (1 km), here to watch the sunrise. The Tianhai Scenic Area is located north of Aoyu Peak, west of Liandan Peak, and south of Guangming Peak, forming a relatively open and expansive high-altitude valley. Descend 800 steps to reach the bottom of Lotus Valley, then ascend to the Yupinglou Scenic Area (2.5 km). Centered around Yupinglou, with Lotus Peak and Tiantu Peak as the main attractions, the Yuping Scenic Area is formed. Yupinglou is close to Yuping Peak at an altitude of 1668 meters, situated between Lotus and Tiantu Peaks. From Wenshu Platform, you can overlook the surrounding "Squirrel Leaping Over Tiantu," "Peacock Playing with Lotus," and other rock landscapes. The Welcome Pine and Farewell Pine are planted in front of Lion Rock and Elephant Rock, respectively. Upon reaching Yupinglou, you need to decide whether to descend by cable car or on foot. Taking the cable car will bypass Tiantu Peak, so decide based on your physical condition and whether you want to ascend Tiantu Peak. If you ascend Tiantu Peak, go on foot; if you're exhausted, take the cable car. From the Yupinglou Scenic Area to Tiantu Peak (1.5 km), Half Mountain Temple (1 km), immediately after Lima Peak, and finally reach Ciguang Pavilion (2.5 km) (total 5 km). Originally built as Buyun Pavilion by Taoist Cheng Xuanyang, later rebuilt as Fahai Chan Monastery by Pumen upon his arrival, it was personally named "Protect the Country Ciguang Temple" by the emperor, expanding greatly and flourishing for over a hundred years. Take a vehicle from Ciguang Pavilion back to Tunxi, stay overnight in Tunxi or return.

Small Tips:

1. You can bring your own tent and camp on the mountain. The lodges on the mountain are relatively unclean and expensive.

2. You can also choose to ascend from Ciguang Pavilion (front mountain) and descend from the back mountain. The front mountain is steeper, making climbing more tiring, but the scenery on the front mountain is more beautiful compared to the back mountain.

One-day Itinerary Reference for Ascending Mount Huang

Depart from the Tangkou Transfer Center at 5:30 AM, take the cable car up the mountain from YunGu Temple on the back mountain, visit Bai'e Ridge, Shixin Peak, Mengbi Shenghua, Xihai Scenic Area, Paiyun Pavilion, enter the Xihai Grand Canyon, after 3~4 hours, proceed from Buxian Bridge, via Baiyun Hotel, Tianhai, Aoyu Peak, One-Line Sky, Hundred Steps Cloudy Stairs, Happiness Avenue, Yupinglou, take photos with the Welcome Pine, then descend on foot or by cable car from Yupinglou to Ciguang Pavilion, and transfer to Tangkou by shuttle bus.

Reference Routes Around Mount Huang

Besides ascending Mount Huang, some nearby ancient villages are worth visiting, showcasing typical Huizhou-style architecture. For example, the archways in ancient villages of She County, wood carvings in ancient villages of Jixi County, residential buildings in ancient villages of Yi County, and ancestral temples in ancient villages of Jing County, all very distinctive. Choose one direction based on personal preference as part of your Mount Huang tour, ensuring a rewarding experience. Typically, a day is sufficient for a round trip. If you prefer a more leisurely pace, choose a local inn with character, spend a night, experience the carved beds in old houses, and reflect thoughtfully on the beauty of life.
