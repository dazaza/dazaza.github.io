The Chinese Red Cross Society has reported Guo Meimei to the public security organs for fabricating facts and disrupting public order. The public security organs have already filed a case and are conducting relevant work according to law.

CCTV focuses on the Guo Meimei incident: The Red Cross needs to prove its innocence

In response to the heatedly discussed Guo Meimei incident on the Internet and in the media recently, the Chinese Red Cross Society issued two statements through the official website of the Chinese Red Cross on June 22 and 24, and reported the incident to the public security organs on June 24, deciding to initiate legal procedures to protect the legitimate rights and interests and good reputation of the Red Cross. Wang Wei, Executive Vice President of the Chinese Red Cross Society, introduced that one aspect of reporting the Guo Meimei incident to the public security department is the legitimate action of the Red Cross to protect its own proper rights and interests, and the other aspect is to ask the law enforcement department to clarify the facts through investigation and evidence collection, give the Red Cross a fair judgment, and let illegal personnel bear corresponding legal responsibilities. Currently, the police have started related work, and the headquarters of the Chinese Red Cross Society will announce the progress of the work to the public at an appropriate time.

Wang Wei also stated that some photos, Weibo posts, family relationship claims, etc., circulating online about the Chinese Red Cross Society and related individuals, after verification, have no factual basis whatsoever and are purely irresponsible rumors. Vice President of the Chinese Red Cross Society, Guo Changjiang, does not have a daughter named Guo Meimei or a son named Guo Zihao. Guo Meimei also declared through Weibo that she does not know Guo Changjiang, and there is no familial relationship. Guo Changjiang has never used Weibo or a blog, nor has he registered an account named Guo Changjiang RC on Weibo. A set of photos circulating online about Guo Changjiang wearing watches is completely fabricated and baseless.

"I have never seen Guo Changjiang wear a Patek Philippe watch, and as far as I know, he does not own a Patek Philippe watch. Our association has always opposed those who attempt to gain attention by creating false information, and we hope all sectors of society can view such issues with a calm attitude and not be taken advantage of," said Wang Wei.

Wang Wei also pointed out that the Chinese Red Cross Society does not have an institution called the Red Cross Chamber of Commerce, nor has it established a position of commercial general manager, and there is no person named Guo Meimei. The Chinese Commercial System Red Cross was applied for by the China National Federation of Supply and Marketing Cooperatives based on Article 8 of the "Red Cross Society Law of the People's Republic of China" and was approved and established by the headquarters of the Chinese Red Cross Society as an industry Red Cross. It itself does not engage in commercial activities. Their main work is to spread humanitarianism and the spirit of the Red Cross within the commercial system, carry out publicity, rescue, and first aid training work for the Red Cross, and there is no issue of donation distribution with the Chinese Red Cross Society. Its staff members consist of volunteers from units such as the China National Federation of Supply and Marketing Cooperatives. These personnel's salaries, bonuses, and subsidies are all paid by their original units, and they have not received any remuneration from the Red Cross Society. The Chinese Commercial System Red Cross has positions such as president, vice president, and secretary-general, but there has never been a title of general manager. Meanwhile, according to the situation provided by the Chinese Commercial System Red Cross, neither Guo Meimei nor Guo Meiling participated in its organization or its various activities.

According to Wang Rupeng, Secretary-General of the Chinese Red Cross Society, the Chinese Red Cross Donation Information Management System being developed by the headquarters of the Chinese Red Cross Society will be launched to the public soon. This system will enable online inquiries regarding the receipt, management, and use of donations through a network platform. It is understood that the information management system will first release the usage status of donations received by the Chinese Red Cross Society for the Yushu earthquake.
