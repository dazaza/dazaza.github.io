Car Beauty - Maintenance Knowledge

DIY Maintenance

As long as you properly maintain the "five oils" - gasoline, engine oil, transmission fluid, power steering fluid, and brake fluid - along with the "three waters" - radiator water, windshield washer fluid, and battery water - and add basic maintenance for the "five" tires, you can effectively reduce the likelihood of car troubles.

Many people have a mistaken notion that car maintenance is solely the responsibility of repair shops; they think that as long as they pay for the service, the shop will take care of their car. However, even if you are a conscientious owner who regularly schedules maintenance checks every 5,000 kilometers or six months, there is still a gap between visits during which many basic maintenance tasks and inspections need to be done. Generally, we refer to the maintenance tasks we can do ourselves as "Level One Maintenance," which mainly involves checking and replenishing the "five oils," "three waters," and the "five" tires.

Five Oils Section

Gasoline

How to Check:

Simply check the fuel gauge frequently to see if it has reached empty.

Note:

Never let your fuel gauge reach empty before refueling. Modern cars use electric fuel pumps and electronic fuel sensors that must remain submerged in gasoline for cooling. Additionally, running low on fuel may affect the oxygen sensor in the exhaust system, and these parts are not cheap. Therefore, avoid waiting until the last minute to refuel.

Engine Oil

How to Check:

Every few days, pull out the dipstick, clean it, reinsert it, then pull it out again to check if the oil level is between the upper and lower limits.

Note:

In general, oil consumption is not severe enough to require attention between two maintenance cycles. However, in summer due to the heat, oil consumption increases slightly. If you find impurities or a gasoline smell in the oil, or if the exhaust smoke appears light blue during routine checks, this indicates an engine problem and potential abnormal wear. Visit the repair shop immediately.

Transmission Fluid

How to Check:

When you change the engine oil, you can also check the transmission fluid. Since the inspection process is more complicated, you can ask the dealership's service department to handle it. For accurate measurement, the car must be in neutral (P), idling, parked on a flat surface, and the fluid must reach its working temperature of 80℃-93℃.

Note:

Too much or too little fluid can damage the transmission. Excessive fluid may increase resistance during operation or cause the transmission fluid to foam and degrade, leading to abnormal wear. When checking the transmission fluid, ensure precise measurements.

Power Steering Fluid

How to Check:

Once the engine cools down, wipe the filler cap and reservoir top clean. Then unscrew the power steering fluid cap and clean the dipstick with a lint-free cloth before reinstalling and locking it back in place. Finally, open it again to check the fluid level.

Note:

When adding or replacing the fluid, always use the correct power steering fluid. Using inappropriate fluid can damage the steering system.

Brake Fluid

How to Check:

Just open the hood and check the brake fluid reservoir's level. As long as it is not below the minimum level, it is fine.

Note:

Do not add brake fluid without checking the level, as overfilling could cause the fluid to spray onto the hot engine, leading to corrosion or even fire. If bubbles appear in the brake fluid or it becomes dirty, it means the fluid has degraded. Replace it promptly to prevent a drop in boiling point and bubble formation, which could impair braking performance and pose significant risks.

Three Waters Section

Radiator Water

Some people like to add antifreeze concentrate to the radiator water. The main purpose of antifreeze is to prevent freezing, lubricate, and protect against corrosion. Its principle works by mixing the antifreeze with water to form a solution that lowers the freezing point, preventing ice formation. Additionally, its boiling point is relatively higher, enhancing engine cooling in summer while reducing water evaporation.

Note:

When the engine and radiator are overheated, removing the radiator cap may cause steam and antifreeze to spray out. Do not open the cap under such conditions.

Windshield Washer Fluid

It’s best to add some windshield cleaner concentrate. Simply using water doesn't provide effective cleaning.

Note:

- Follow manufacturer instructions when using concentrated cleaner.

- Do not mix ready-to-use cleaner with water, as small particles in water can clog the system. Water can freeze and damage the reservoir and other system components.

- In cold weather, only fill the cleaner to leave space for expansion when frozen, avoiding reservoir damage.

Battery Water

How to Check:

Although some batteries are maintenance-free and don't require water addition, in hot summer regions, some batteries still need water. When the battery water is insufficient, simply distilled water is sufficient. Battery water evaporates faster in summer, so frequent checks are necessary to prevent damage and extend battery life.

Note:

- Avoid using matches near the battery to prevent explosion. Use a flashlight for illumination if needed.

- Regularly check the battery water level if your vehicle does not use a maintenance-free battery. Add water carefully if needed.

Tires Section

Tires should be checked every 6000 to 8000 kilometers for abnormal wear, tire pressure, and front wheel alignment.

Improper use and maintenance of tires can lead to danger:

- Overloading may cause excessive friction and blowouts, so pay attention to the maximum load capacity of the tires.

- Low tire pressure causes wear on the tire shoulders, and the sidewalls are prone to cracking.

- High tire pressure results in wear on the central tread.

- Worn-out tires can lead to accidents. Tread patterns may become worn flat from prolonged use and should be replaced promptly.

Tire Pressure

There is a standard tire pressure label on the driver's side door jamb. This refers to the pressure when the tires are cool, i.e., after parking for at least three hours or driving less than 1.6 km. Cool tires provide the most accurate pressure readings.

How to Check Tire Pressure:

Check the tire pressure at least once a month. A quality tire pressure gauge must be used as visual inspection alone cannot determine if the pressure is normal.

If abnormal tire wear is noticed or the car drifts to one side, a four-wheel alignment should be performed first. If vibrations occur on smooth roads, the wheels may need balancing.

Spare Tire

Among the five tires, regular attention should be given to the spare tire's pressure. Many people overlook the condition of the spare tire and discover it is also flat when trying to replace a damaged tire.
