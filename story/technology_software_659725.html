Currently, the expected annual yield of stable-income wealth management products issued by various banks is about 2.7% for US dollar-denominated products, around 1.3% for euro-denominated products, and approximately 5.4% for Australian dollar-denominated products. If investors anticipate that the RMB will appreciate by 3%-5% within the next year, it implies that except for high-interest currencies like the Australian dollar, the yields of foreign currency wealth management products in US dollars and euros will generally struggle to outpace RMB appreciation, making them less worthwhile investments.

How should ordinary people respond?

Excerpt four from the popular financial book "What Should the Renminbi Do?"

Zhao Xiao, Professor at University of Science and Technology Beijing

The government's response strategy

From the perspective of business operations and industry selection in securities investment, under the expectation of RMB appreciation, export-oriented industries face the risk of losing their price advantage, but some industries will benefit from the appreciation of their own currency.

Overall, purchasing imported goods, traveling abroad, and studying overseas do not constitute our entire lives. The current appreciation is relatively small and has a minimal impact on the personal finance of ordinary people. It is not advisable to advocate extravagant spending due to "money illusion."

Firstly, industries that own RMB assets will be pursued by capital, such as real estate, finance (including banks, securities, insurance), and commercial enterprises with property resources. For these sectors, RMB appreciation means an increase in asset value and economic strength. RMB appreciation will lead to international financial capital investing in RMB, meaning that RMB will become an object of purchase for people to preserve and increase the value of their wealth. This is known as the "gold effect" internationally, which will reduce the country's fiscal revenue. At the same time, RMB appreciation facilitates the repayment of foreign debt; originally, we might need $200 billion to repay external debts, but after RMB appreciation, we may need fewer billions of dollars. RMB appreciation also raises the cost for foreigners buying properties in mainland China. If external demand decreases, it will undoubtedly ease the so-called supply-demand imbalance and cool down the overheated domestic real estate market, positively impacting the reduction of housing prices.

Secondly, industries with higher foreign exchange liabilities, mainly including aviation and commerce. In the case of RMB appreciation against the US dollar, their debt repayment capacity increases accordingly. Airlines typically have large amounts of foreign currency liabilities, mostly in US dollars. RMB appreciation is equivalent to reducing debt burdens, directly benefiting airlines. Due to the relative depreciation of the US dollar, the prices of aviation fuel and maintenance decrease relatively. Since variable costs account for about 50% of the main operating costs, every 1% appreciation of RMB will increase the gross margin by 0.5 percentage points.

For the government, three key tasks should be focused on. Firstly, long-term institutional reforms and short-term policy adjustments should be appropriately combined, channeling funds back into the real economy. Then, through the development of the real economy, guide the flow of capital market funds to achieve the function of truly nurturing the real industry. Meanwhile, actively support the funding of basic industries and high-tech industries, using a combination of marketization and administrative legal means to protect national industrial security. Currently, in the face of many adverse effects of RMB appreciation, pure economic liberalization and rapid RMB appreciation would bring significant impacts to the Chinese economy and lay the groundwork for a financial crisis. The hard-earned money of the Chinese people could instantly become the fruit of international capital.

For ordinary people, RMB appreciation directly affects their financial behavior. First, it directly influences consumption behavior for certain products. A stronger RMB makes foreign goods or services cheaper, allowing imports like cars, high-end clothing, cosmetics, etc., to be purchased at lower prices. Once the price transmission is in place, many people may turn prior expectations of waiting consumption into actual consumption. For instance, overseas travel. On one hand, there is greater enthusiasm among Chinese citizens for traveling abroad, as more foreign exchange can be obtained with RMB, encouraging more active consumption overseas. On the other hand, foreign destinations will welcome more Chinese tourists. Another example is studying abroad. Many parents choose to send their children overseas for education, but the high tuition fees and living expenses have always been a headache for many families. With the threshold lowered, more parents can fulfill their children's dreams of studying abroad. However, this still depends on the destination countries, as the expected RMB appreciation may trigger a new round of regional currency appreciation, such as other Asian currencies potentially appreciating as well. If all currencies appreciate simultaneously, there may be no change, so each situation should be treated differently.

Additionally, RMB-denominated wealth management products and foreign exchange-denominated wealth management products are affected. Ordinary people will have higher expectations for returns on foreign exchange wealth management products, such as an additional 2% increase based on existing expected returns. This poses greater product pressure on current commercial banks. Currently, RMB-heavy products like RMB-denominated wealth management products and money market funds will gain more popularity, and high-quality RMB trust wealth management products will be highly sought after. Commercial banks will innovate more RMB-denominated wealth management products, appropriately increasing the issuance of stable products to meet market demands. For families with foreign exchange assets, the situation becomes relatively complex, requiring cautious differentiation. Typically, when we refer to RMB appreciation, it is usually against the US dollar, but against other currencies, the space for RMB appreciation may not be very large. Therefore, we need to comprehensively consider the pros and cons before making decisions. For example, for families with foreign exchange wealth management products, these products often have a longer freeze period. Observing rather than acting hastily is a more suitable choice. Blindly converting foreign exchange assets can also result in losses, balancing gains and losses.

Secondly, continue to maintain moderately strict capital account supervision, controlling the scale and speed of hot money inflows, breaking the expectation of RMB appreciation, and stabilizing the RMB exchange rate. Those advocating expanding the floating range of the RMB exchange rate fail to consider clearly that prematurely enlarging the floating range of the RMB not only accelerates its appreciation but also leads to excessive appreciation, causing mass bankruptcies of export companies. After all, it’s easier for the old to leave than for the new to come. Japan's 20-year economic stagnation is a painful lesson to avoid repeating.

Secondly, it affects savings behavior. In fact, during the expectation of RMB appreciation, some customers have started to handle USD exchange, converting foreign exchange into RMB for further wealth management. This will directly affect changes in foreign exchange savings. To offset this change moderately, the People's Bank of China raised the upper limit of small-denomination deposit interest rates for USD and HKD in banks. The upper limit of one-year deposit interest rates for USD and HKD was increased by 0.5 percentage points, adjusted to 1.625% and 1.5%, respectively. After the adjustment, the interest rate levels compared to the one-year fixed RMB interest rate of 2.25% narrowed the gap to 0.625 and 0.75 percentage points, but there is still a certain difference. Some customers who expect continued RMB appreciation may adjust their foreign exchange savings positions, converting foreign exchange savings into RMB savings. Considering that RMB is not yet freely convertible, there is a certain cost to exchanging currency. Appropriately retaining some foreign exchange on hand can be convenient for future overseas travel, study, or business, depending on individual circumstances. Additionally, you can consider converting to other currencies expected to appreciate against the USD.

Thirdly, it affects investment and wealth management behavior. The most direct impact is on the stock market. For the A-share market, appreciation is good news. Appreciation attracts outside funds to buy stocks in beneficiary industries, so RMB appreciation is usually accompanied by a strengthening of the securities market. Families should prioritize allocating securities assets while reducing bond holdings in their asset allocation. However, increasing securities assets does not mean encouraging everyone to trade stocks, as retail investors lacking professional experience and information bear great risks in the stock market. Working-class families should determine their risk tolerance, such as through investing in equity funds and other more stable wealth management tools to increase their securities assets. This way, they can share in the benefits of RMB appreciation and a favorable stock market while avoiding many non-systematic risks.

Thirdly, industries where the proportion of imported raw materials or parts is relatively high. From an industry perspective, papermaking, steel, automobiles, petrochemicals, synthetic fibers, plastics, rubber, logs, wool, copper ore, and non-ferrous metals are products whose imports are sensitive to exchange rates. Although the total demand for these raw materials and energy products has a certain rigidity, exchange rate appreciation will reduce the import costs of these industries' main raw materials, leading to an increase in import volumes and thus enhancing the profitability of the industry. Overall, RMB appreciation has a positive impact on imports. Given that China's industrialization process is entering the heavy chemical industrialization era, characterized by massive resource and energy consumption, appreciation may save foreign exchange for imports.

Under the shadow of currency wars, on the one hand, the RMB has appreciated rapidly against the USD recently; on the other hand, domestic inflationary pressures have increased, and the "negative interest rate" situation will continue. In the context of RMB "internal devaluation and external appreciation," how can enterprises and investors better hedge risks? How can ordinary people prevent the shrinkage of their assets? And what responsibilities should the government exercise?

Enterprise operations and securities investment industries

Thirdly, solving the RMB exchange rate issue necessarily requires reforming the distribution system and addressing low wage issues. A migrant worker who stays away from home for years earns barely enough to support two people and cannot afford the cost of sending his child to elementary school. Is such a wage level conducive to the long-term stability and development of the nation and society? The mechanical low-wage production model inevitably causes trade imbalances and prompts the appreciation of the domestic currency. Therefore, legislating to raise workers' wages is a necessary measure. Only by establishing and improving the social security system, solving the concerns of the people regarding pensions and medical care, and building a long-term mechanism for wage increases to genuinely improve the income of ordinary workers, can consumers have optimistic expectations for the future, releasing genuine consumer demand, thereby fundamentally alleviating the pressure of RMB appreciation. China is currently in an economic transformation period, and government functions must also adapt accordingly. The government needs to focus on what it should do, and establishing and improving the social security system is the top priority, relating to the overall healthy and long-term development of China's economy.

It is worth noting that under the current continuous RMB appreciation, export enterprises can no longer rely solely on the past low-cost model for aggressive expansion but should maintain an appropriate scale because products originally sold to Europe and America that could earn 10 yuan per piece now only earn 5 yuan. Coupled with rising labor wages and domestic price hikes, enterprises should prepare for tough times ahead. They should not blindly accept foreign orders regardless of costs but instead carefully invest funds in the domestic market and improve product quality.
