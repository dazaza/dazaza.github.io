Panda celebrity reborn after more than 10 days of critical rescue treatment for severe illness (picture)

by yjankuib37 on 2011-11-03 14:01:25

Baosi Survives and Returns to Normal Feeding

By reporter Chen Huijuan / Text, intern reporter Liu Xing / Photo

"Here it comes, here it comes! Baosi is walking towards us!" Yesterday, outside the exhibition hall of the famous female panda Baosi at Fuzhou Panda World, dozens of journalists and tourists surrounded the transparent glass wall. All eyes and cameras were focused on Baosi. She, like a centenarian, was shakily approaching us. When the caretaker fed her favorite bamboo leaves and apples bit by bit, she acted mischievously and coquettishly like a child.

On June 1st at noon, Baosi suddenly developed enteritis complicated with pancreatitis. After more than ten days of rescue efforts by experts from the Giant Panda Research Center and Nanjing Military Region Fuzhou General Hospital, she has survived again. This is her third rebirth since she turned 30 (a panda's 30 years is equivalent to over a hundred human years).

Famous Doctors' Diagnosis Gave Baosi New Life

Upon learning that Baosi was sick, Director Chen of the Fuzhou Giant Panda Research Center immediately sought help from renowned doctors at Fuzhou General Hospital. Administering anesthetic drugs, drawing blood, testing... Baosi’s white blood cell count had doubled, diagnosing her with enteritis and liver function problems. There was also the possibility of "heart failure," which could lead to death at any time.

The next day, the rescue team found that Baosi's blood albumin levels were decreasing, and hemoglobin dropped by 2 grams per day, alarming everyone who urgently searched for new solutions. At this point, Director Chen recalled a trial conducted 13 years ago on other pandas. It was a groundbreaking achievement obtained through risks, and this time it was applied to Baosi's rescue, playing a crucial role.

During the rescue, it was discovered that Baosi might have pancreatitis.

However, the indicator for diagnosing pancreatitis in pandas remains a blank both domestically and internationally. Despite concerns about misdiagnosis endangering Baosi's life, the rescue plan for enteritis complicated with pancreatitis was initiated, relieving Baosi's intestinal paralysis and pain.

On June 6th, after several days of heavy rain, the sky cleared up, and Baosi improved significantly, able to wobble to the door. Through more than ten days of efforts, Baosi has survived once again!

Fuzhou Panda World specially filmed the first day's rescue work and the 24-hour care provided by the caretakers for Baosi during the rescue process. Readers can log onto Dongkuaibao website (www.dnkb.com.cn) to see how this internationally renowned panda star overcame the third major hurdle in her life.

Domestic and International Tourists Pray for Baosi's Quick Recovery

Baosi's third rebirth delighted everyone.

Baosi's first adventure occurred on February 14, 1984, when she was still in her hometown Sichuan. At that time, due to the large-scale flowering of bamboo in Baoxing County, Sichuan, she fell into an icy river at minus thirty degrees Celsius while starving, but fortunately was rescued.

On July 23, 2002, Baosi, staying at the Guling Villa in Fuzhou, experienced a nosebleed due to her blood pressure being twice as high as normal, causing her to lose consciousness for a week, but she was successfully saved. And this time, Baosi's illness was the most severe, yet she created another miracle of life.

Yesterday, Mr. Chen, a tourist from Malaysia who specifically came to Fuzhou to see the pandas, learned about Baosi's story and gave a thumbs-up repeatedly: "Impressive! I hope she recovers soon." A tour guide surnamed Zhang from a local travel agency in Fuzhou donated 3333 yuan as a gesture of goodwill according to Fuzhou's auspicious numbers after hearing about Baosi's recovery from serious illness; several foreign tourists visiting from the United States also extended their kindness.

"Baosi, you must live healthily." This is the heartfelt blessing from everyone who knows Baosi.

Anecdotes

Caretaker "Mom" Never Gave Up on Baosi

Her name is Fei Ning, Baosi's caretaker "mom." Since the age of 18, for 20 years, she has treated Baosi as her own daughter, taking meticulous care of her.

On June 1st, when Baosi collapsed, Fei Ning was the first to discover it. During Baosi's illness, she tracked her condition 24 hours a day, feeding her meals and medicine six or seven times, squatting for hours on end, so exhausted that she couldn't even straighten her back. She also cleaned the area, disinfecting the panda house twice daily with chemicals and flames. Baosi liked quietness, so she set her phone to silent mode and didn't dare let her family call her unexpectedly.

Baosi takes nearly twenty types of medication per meal, and only Fei Ning can coax her into eating them. The medicine needs to be administered half an hour before meals, ground into powder, mixed with rice soup. During meals, the remaining medicine is mixed into her food. To help Baosi recover quickly, Fei Ning and a few other caretakers personally picked tender bamboo leaves, cut them into fine threads, and mixed them with carrot threads to feed her.
