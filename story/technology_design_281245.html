Chelsea's 100 million pound bid for Tsubasa: A 250,000 pounds weekly salary to create a new "working emperor"

It is no longer a secret that Chelsea wants to spark a transfer storm in the winter. In January, during the Africa Cup of Nations, Chelsea will lose Didier Drogba and Kalou. Therefore, strengthening the forward line is the top priority. The son-in-law of Maradona, Sergio Aguero, is the first target of Roman Abramovich. According to previous reports from the British media, Chelsea has already offered a 50 million pound bid for Aguero. ESPN today reported that Chelsea would offer a total package worth 100 million pounds to convince Atletico Madrid. There are possibilities that Aguero could report to Stamford Bridge as early as January.

ESPN revealed the details of this 100 million pound offer. If the Argentine striker joins Chelsea, he will become the new "working emperor" of the Premier League. Aguero can earn about 250,000 pounds per week. 40 million pounds will be used as Aguero's transfer fee, with an additional 10 million pounds as a floating clause. This means that Aguero's transfer fee will reach 50 million pounds. Besides, Chelsea will make enough concessions in terms of bonuses and image rights, which amounts to about 50 million pounds. Altogether, it adds up to 100 million pounds.

If this deal succeeds, Aguero will definitely break Robinho's record of 32.5 million pounds for the highest transfer fee in English football history. Moreover, the Argentine striker will become the highest-paid player in the world. Previously, Aguero told Sky Sports that he was willing to join a world-class club like Chelsea. After witnessing Aguero's performance in the match where Atletico Madrid drew 2-2 with Chelsea, Ancelotti looked forward to the new combination of Drogba and Aguero.

Despite Aguero's huge demand for salary, Chelsea knows they must meet the player's wage requirements. Regarding Aguero's potential transfer to Chelsea, an informed source revealed: "There is still much work to be done, although things are developing positively, you have to admit, there is still a 50-50 chance."

Just in terms of transfer fees, Manchester City is the only club that can compete with Chelsea. However, according to insider information: "This player does not want to go to Manchester City, just like Kaka refused before. They do not yet have the prestige and level of a big club." For this reason, Atletico Madrid may have to face only one bidder - Chelsea, so their insistence on a 50 million pound transfer fee may loosen. An informed source disclosed: "Money is still the key, but negotiations will continue, everyone needs to try to find solutions."
