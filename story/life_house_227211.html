Shanghai装修: Please be aware of the common pitfalls in children's room renovation

by longersing on 2009-08-22 20:38:30

Alternative, unique, novel, personalized - many Shanghai decoration companies have successively launched their main characteristic children's rooms to meet consumer needs. However, many Shanghai renovators blindly pursue fashion and often ignore the pollution caused by children's room decoration. What are the misconceptions in children's room decoration? Let's listen to what experienced netizens have to say.

Shanghai Renovation Misconception One: Less paint equals no pollution

Case: Ms. He heard from a friend that renovation can cause pollution, especially paint pollution. Therefore, when decorating her child's room with built-in wardrobes, she required the workers to use less paint, thinking this would basically eliminate pollution. However, after three months of renovation, there was still a strong smell of paint, making it impossible to move in. Helplessly, Ms. He asked a home renovation supervisor for inspection and learned that it is not true that using less paint means less pollution; the pollution mainly depends on the type of paint used. The workers at Ms. He's house used oil-based paint, which is polluting even if used in small amounts.

Liu Zhi suggests: Many renovators only partially understand the sources of pollution, believing that using less material means less or even no pollution. This is actually a serious misconception in renovation. For example, to save costs, some renovators hire workers to build wardrobes, unknowingly bringing pollution into the home. Because many workers, for profit and effect, will use oil-based paints with severe pollution. Oil-based paints mainly consist of nitrocellulose lacquer and polyester lacquer, requiring large amounts of organic solvents during dilution, which linger indoors long-term and harm health. Due to their high hardness and good film fullness, they are often used by unscrupulous workers or decoration companies. Therefore, if you must use paint for wardrobe construction, strictly require the workers to use water-based paint.

Shanghai Renovation Misconception Two: Frequent ventilation eliminates pollution

Case: Ms. Liu's son is two years old. In the later stages of decoration, when choosing furniture, all the furniture in the children's room was pure solid wood, while adult rooms used ordinary panel furniture. She believed that since the child mostly plays, rests, and studies in the children's room, there would be no pollution.

Zhang Zhiqiang suggests: There are many consumers like Ms. Liu who believe that as long as the main materials and furniture in the children's room are good and the adults' room uses regular furniture with frequent ventilation, there won't be any pollution. In fact, ventilation can only alleviate pollution when it's not too severe. Formaldehyde release lasts 5 to 15 years, and simple ventilation cannot thoroughly treat pollution. Moreover, formaldehyde, benzene, and other pollutants, if severe, can spread throughout the space in winter. Additionally, when purchasing solid wood furniture, attention should be paid because sometimes the main frame is solid wood, but the backboard, lining board, and bottom board may not be. If these boards are of poor quality, pollution can still exist.

Shanghai Renovation Misconception Three: Foam mats are safe

Case: After finishing her renovation, Ms. Kang spent a lot of time on accessories for her child's room. To ensure her child's safety, she wrapped the entire floor and sharp corners of furniture in the children's room with foam mats.

Xiang He suggests: Nowadays, using foam mats in children's rooms is common. Many consumers believe that foam mats do not pollute children. This is incorrect, as many low-grade, inferior foam mats contain formaldehyde and other harmful gases. Although the amount of formaldehyde they contain may not significantly harm adults, it can harm children who play on them frequently. If you must use foam mats on the floor, it's best to purchase them from large malls.
