Online shopping: Beware of the traps behind low prices

by anonymous on 2011-04-01 13:26:28

Recently, with the continuous rise in prices, to increase revenue and cut expenditure, housewives have carefully planned their budgets and come up with various strategies to deal with the consumption pressure brought by rising prices. The younger generation, on the other hand, has turned to online shopping to meet their consumption needs. For a time, the online shopping trend for clothing, shoes, accessories, even home appliances and furniture has been wave after wave. Online shopping, due to its convenience and relatively cheaper advantages, has become an important means of fashionable consumption, and "flash sales" have become the ultimate skill of major consumer websites.

Matters needing attention when shopping online

1. Beware of illegal transaction behaviors on the Internet. Because currently online transactions require special permission from the state to engage in business activities, consumers should be able to identify them when shopping online and first pay attention to the legality of the transaction itself.

2. Pay more attention to the "blacklist" before online shopping. Currently, multiple websites such as Taobao have established third-party trading platforms, online transaction guarantee centers, and other third-party guarantee platforms. Based on consumer complaints and reports, they have formed poor credit records or "blacklists" that infringe on consumer rights and are updated in a timely manner. Consumers can avoid being deceived by paying attention to "blacklist" information when shopping online.

3. Beware of low-price traps. Online shopping is relatively cheaper than traditional real-life shopping, but low prices are limited. Extremely low prices may hide traps. Unscrupulous individuals often take advantage of consumers' psychology of "greed for cheap goods" by offering free gifts, "flash sales," and other low-price tactics to attract attention and trap consumers through "phishing" or requiring advance payment.

4. Identify the seller's qualifications. Understand whether the seller's transaction payment method includes third-party payments or cash-on-delivery; legitimate websites generally have free customer service numbers; the various certification marks displayed at the bottom of the seller's website are also key points to pay attention to. Generally, the certification marks of legal and legitimate websites are approved by relevant institutions and linked to the corresponding third-party certification pages, and the certification is within the validity period.

5. Choose third-party guaranteed payment or cash-on-delivery for transaction payments. A safe and reasonable payment method is an important means to ensure the safety of consumer funds. Generally, regular and legal websites will provide consumers with third-party guaranteed payment and cash-on-delivery payment methods. If the seller requires the consumer to pay in advance during the transaction, it usually involves fraud and illegal activities, and consumers should choose carefully.

6. Learn and master basic safe online shopping knowledge. Many online shopping consumers fall victim to scams mainly because they lack understanding of online shopping and basic online shopping safety knowledge, cannot distinguish between low-price temptations and phishing scams, and end up making decisions detrimental to themselves. Therefore, consumers must consult people with online shopping experience, read anti-fraud knowledge provided by netizens or third-party guarantee institutions, and master basic online shopping safety knowledge before shopping online.

Online Shopping Becomes New "Battlefield"

With the selling points of being cheap, limited quantity, trendy, stimulating, and providing a sense of accomplishment, online flash sales have now become a growing new form of fashionable consumption. From individual shops on Taobao to large online malls like Dangdang.com, they have all become the main battlegrounds for enthusiastic flash sale hunters.

Xiaoyu runs a store on Beimen Street. The shop is not big, but despite its small size, it offers everything: bags, boots, accessories, clothes. At first glance, it looks like a general store. Despite the variety, the business is surprisingly good. "Many of the popular items in my store are flash-bought from Taobao, Dangdang, and other online stores. Since I buy them cheaply, I sell them cheaply too," Xiaoyu explained. Apart from looking after the store, her main job is to collect all sorts of flash sale information online. "Don't think flash sales don't require much brainpower; doing well isn't easy. Early birds catch the worms. You need to do your homework, such as studying how many items are limited in the flash sale, how good the reviews are, which ones are easy to resell, and you need to be faster than others to grab the hot items."

Xiaoyu said that today's consumers are very smart, knowing that the prices in reality are too high, so they directly turn to online shopping. "On one hand, it saves the trouble of walking around until your legs hurt, and on the other hand, there are more choices online, and the prices are cheaper, suitable for this era of rising prices." It is understood that due to the recent significant increase in prices, many consumers have become more cautious about spending and shifted their focus to online shopping.

Many shopping websites have seized the opportunity to launch discount promotions and notify "members" in a timely manner via text messages and emails. This marketing strategy is favored by many young people. In most cases, consumers first select clothes in specific stores, and if they find them suitable after trying them on, they purchase them directly online. "Sometimes online shopping can save half the money." Xiaoyu once saw a piece of the latest style clothing in a Da Yi Yan specialty store, priced at 498 yuan after discounts. Although it was expensive, she liked it very much and finally bought it painfully. However, after searching online, she found that the lowest price for this piece of clothing was 312 yuan, "saving nearly 200 yuan!" Since then, Xiaoyu has learned the trick of "trying on first, then buying online." "You should check out several online stores, especially those indicating flash sales, and compare prices and pay attention to buyers' comments before placing an order; this way, it's safer."

In addition to general merchandise, many best-selling books also participate in flash sales or discount promotions. Consumers still use the same trick: they spot a book in the bookstore and then buy it online, making it even more cost-effective! However, the downside is that these flash sale items are mostly limited-time and limited-quantity releases. While this allows some "flash sale experts" like Xiaoyu to show off their skills, for latecomers, grabbing the treasures won't be that easy.

Problems with Signing First, Inspecting Later

It is understood that with the rise of online shopping, ordinary consumers gradually recognize and use courier services. The domestic courier market has expanded nearly twofold in just a few years. Data shows that 35% of China's express delivery business volume comes from Asia's largest personal online trading platform - Taobao. Many courier companies have rapidly developed their businesses due to partnerships with Taobao. Currently, courier stations have been set up in university campuses, communities, and other places to facilitate consumers sending and receiving mail.

Xiao Jie, who works for Shentong Express Qingyuan branch, is a "regular" at a certain unit. "I almost go to that office every day; I've almost become the dedicated courier for that unit," Xiao Jie joked. Due to being responsible for delivering logistics goods along Jiang'er Road, he has become the dedicated deliveryman for some "online shopping enthusiasts" in this area. "Especially one girl from a unit, she almost receives goods every day, so we have默契 (a tacit understanding); when I arrive at the office, I walk straight to her, sign the receipt, and deliver the goods without much ado." According to him, he has worked at Shentong Express for almost two years. With the increase in the number of online shoppers, his workload has significantly increased over the past year, forcing him to adjust his living schedule. "When I first started, I could sleep in, but now it's impossible. There are too many items to deliver each day, and sometimes the delivery locations are far away, so I have to get up early and work hard."

On one side, the logistics industry is developing rapidly, and delivery personnel are busy, while on the other side, online shoppers are somewhat dissatisfied. Why? "Because theoretically, it's supposed to be inspecting the goods before signing, but in practice, there's no chance for you to inspect the goods; you sign first and then they leave, and you have no choice but to accept the goods." Xiao Cao, who works at an advertising company, questioned why, if the goods have quality issues, he would have to personally send them back to the logistics company? Why not inspect and confirm the goods before signing?

In response, an employee of a logistics courier company stated that if the goods have quality problems, it's an issue between the seller and the buyer, and it has nothing to do with the third-party logistics company. So regardless of the quality, they only handle receiving and delivering goods and do not participate in other matters. That is to say, no matter the quality of the goods, the logistics company is only responsible for delivery, so there's no need to let the recipient inspect the goods. "After all, we are not under the direct management of the seller and are not directly responsible for the quality of the goods." And this is where online shoppers worry about the quality not being guaranteed and the hassle of needing to return goods.

Beware of Online Shopping Traps

Xiao Cao once saw a "1-yuan flash sale for a certain domestic brand model air conditioner" on a website. Within less than a minute of refreshing the webpage, he found that more than 400 people had already booked this model of the air conditioner. But soon after, he couldn't access the website anymore. Later, the website explained that there was a technical issue in the backend, leading to incorrect information that misled consumers. In the end, the website did not honor its promise but instead deleted the "1-yuan flash sale for a certain air conditioner" information and replaced it with the words "free shipping at low prices, limited purchases." "For consumers, this is purely a disguised fraudulent behavior," Xiao Cao believed. No matter what, the website indeed provided information about the 1-yuan flash sale, but "possibly seeing too many people placing orders, they withdrew the flash sale citing a technical error, manipulating customers at will," Xiao Cao guessed angrily.

Citizen Xiao Huang also helplessly said that he once bought shoes online, ordering a red canvas shoe in size 38, but received a purple product in size 37. After negotiating with the seller for an exchange, Guangzhou VIPshop Information Technology Co., Ltd., the seller agreed but required Xiao Huang to bear the one-way shipping fee. "This way, the price ends up being almost the same as the market price, so why bother with online shopping? In the end, I could only accept it." In response, online shopping expert Xiao Cao reminded consumers to set an upper limit when shopping. Below this price, they can choose online shopping, above which they'd better "see it to believe it, touch it to confirm," but they should also consider whether the specific type of goods is suitable for online purchase. "After all, the internet is still a virtual market, and the margin of error is relatively high, so one must be prepared mentally when shopping."

Additionally, "choosing merchants for online shopping is also important," Xiao Cao said. It's best to choose sellers with higher positive reviews and multiple quality indicators. "Although there is also the possibility of fake reviews, sellers with multiple quality indicators are relatively reliable and trustworthy."
