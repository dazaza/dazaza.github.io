Wang Chuan of Xiaomi: Change the TV just like iPhone

by anonymous on 2013-09-24 20:59:06

Wang Chuan is the project leader of Xiaomi TV, and he is a fan of Apple.

In 2007, Apple changed the world; the iPhone redefined smartphones. Before the birth of the iPhone, physical keys were standard on phones, and touch screens, which were rare at that time, required styluses for assistance. After the iPhone's debut, physical keyboards disappeared, and fingers became the best medium for touch control. This was the biggest astonishment among the public regarding the iPhone — Steve Jobs cleverly captured people’s attention during that presentation.

Wang Chuan's amazement at the iPhone goes beyond its appearance. He was shocked by the App Store, which was born in 2008, and realized it contained so many possibilities. "Your phone and my phone are different not because the phones themselves are different, but because the apps installed inside them are different."

In the logic of the iPhone, ARM SoC is the technical platform, iOS is the system control center, and telephone and text messaging are just one 'App'. This attitude towards 'phone' was unimaginable in the smartphone worldview before the iPhone. A phone equaled a telephone, making dialing always the top priority. Call quality and hardware craftsmanship were the enduring standards for measuring a good phone. This included smartphones.

A similar understanding has replayed over the past two years with the concept of smart TVs. Smart TVs equal TVs, watching live broadcasts remains the top priority, and live streaming and LCD panels are still the criteria for evaluating the quality of smart TVs. Content outside of live broadcasts, like the touchscreens on Windows Mobile smartphones that required auxiliary touch devices, is difficult to use and no one wants to make a radical change.

Wang Chuan is contemplating such a change. Back in 1998, he realized that under the influence of Moore's Law, computers would become smaller and eventually be embedded into all devices, with each device becoming a powerful computer. Undoubtedly, Xiaomi TV is a computer embedded with powerful computing capabilities, presented in the form of a TV. Unfortunately, it took more than a decade to wait.

Wang Chuan said that it will take a few more years for the outside world to truly understand Xiaomi, due to this clear product philosophy, carefully planned before action. Lei Jun repeatedly emphasized that before founding Xiaomi, he had a clear understanding that "the mobile phone is the future center of the world," also adhering to a consistent product philosophy. However, due to the controversial nature of his marketing tactics, the essence of the product has been overlooked.

The essence of Xiaomi TV is a computer. "TV" is an App, "Internet content" is an App, and at the same level are games, music, maps, education, and other Apps. This is the most fundamental difference between Xiaomi TV and other TVs — it is not a TV, but rather a computing device disguised as a TV. It shares the same product logic as the iPhone: the underlying layer is Qualcomm's quad-core chip, MIUI TV Edition is the scheduling center, and TV and Internet content are a "App".

This time, Wang Chuan followed in Apple's footsteps. Only he doesn't have the arrogance of Jobs, instead showing the traditional humility of Chinese people: "I know that TV will be reinvented, no one is ready yet, I think we have a chance to do it, and contribute to the industry."

But making such a "contribution" is extremely difficult. From the results, Wang Chuan provided a practical reference design for smart TVs through practice. To initiate a revolution at the bottom layer interaction, Xiaomi TV almost failed several times, and the final product release date was extended by nearly half a year (originally expected to be released in April this year), missing a better release window, ultimately joining the September competition.

What is referred to as a reference design is the blueprint that chip manufacturers show to partner companies to demonstrate the capabilities of the chips. Intel, Qualcomm, and MTK all provide reference designs to device manufacturers. Among them, MTK mostly provides total solutions (complete solutions), so MTK core phones are relatively cheap —— there is almost no R&D cost. In the TV field, MSTAR, acquired by MTK in June 2012, is the provider of smart TV total solutions, using MSTAR chips almost eliminates R&D costs; of course, this also leads to "thousands of machines with one chip", making it difficult for manufacturers to differentiate and compete in chip configurations.

Xiaomi TV uses Qualcomm Snapdragon MPQ 8064 chip, similar to the APQ 8064 chip used in LeTV TV launched in May, but with a slightly higher processor. However, the user experience of the two products is almost completely different. The reason is that Xiaomi TV redesigned the system interaction logic, taking SoC chip as the system base, building Android OS (MIUI TV Edition) on this basis, and then building the application layer on top. This transformation of interaction logic made the Xiaomi TV team "spend at least ten times more effort."

To understand the interaction revolution brought by Xiaomi TV, you need to understand what the previous TV interaction logic was. For ordinary smart TVs, “TV” is the most basic thing (like the call function before the iPhone), it is a ready-made mature product. Under the premise of not changing the “TV”, connect a set of smart TV systems via HDMI, RF, or USB ports on the TV, thereby calling it a smart TV.

The revolution carried out by Xiaomi TV is: after obtaining Qualcomm's chip, they redesigned the circuit board, developed the underlying driver, and optimized the software design. "The Qualcomm reference design we received was also a traditional smart TV solution, where the bottom layer is TV, and various input sources introduce smart content," Wang Chuan said, "this architecture was completely redone by us. We chose a path that no one has taken before, thus spending a lot of effort on adjustment and optimization."

In Wang Chuan's view, as a computing device, TV should have rich application scenarios (including "TV"), these applications need to be coordinated by the OS. Instead of handing them over to an external interface attached to "TV" to handle —— even though it might be more stable on the original TV basis. If you want to make the smart TV truly "smart", it needs a smart "core" —— Android OS. Letting Android coordinate various Apps is more efficient than an external system.

Starting from scratch is worth it. Just like how Symbian could never be suitable for touch screens, if smart TVs hope to become truly intelligent and distinguish between TVs based on Apps, the logic of the OS layer must be smoothed out. "MIUI TV Edition may not be the best interaction, after Apple releases its TV product next September, everyone might feel that Apple got it right," Wang Chuan said, "but MIUI is the best Android system, adjusting the interaction logic from the bottom up, preparing for future expansion."

The adjustments made by Wang Chuan at the bottom layer can, in turn, serve as a reference design for smart TVs. This reference design, if widely referenced, can be considered either a plagiarism or a tribute. Wang Chuan has the mindset to accept the latter, and confidence in it: from the "cross" shaped interaction design of "Duokan for Apple TV" to the flat design of Xiaomi Box, all have been copied. After Xiaomi TV ships, you'll see if people copy it then.

However, before Qualcomm changes its reference design, due to the limited self-research and development capabilities of traditional TV manufacturers and other internet enterprises misunderstanding the key points, even if this plagiarism unfolds as scheduled, it will only be surface-level UI borrowing. The product philosophy rooted in Wang Chuan's user experience taste isn't necessarily something everyone can learn. And he has been immersed in this field for more than a decade —— as early as when he was working on KTV song selection systems at Leishi, he knew how to create an "interaction system that provides the richest functions with the simplest experience." This is precisely Wang Chuan's greatest attraction to Lei Jun.
