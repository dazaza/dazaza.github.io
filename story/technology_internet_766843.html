Curiosity Rover Discovers Ancient Riverbed on Mars: Water Existed Billions of Years Ago

by anonymous on 2013-10-01 22:15:49

Since landing in Gale Crater in August 2012, NASA's Curiosity rover has made several major discoveries suggesting that ancient Mars once had a wet environment. The image shows Curiosity investigating an area named Hottah, which may have been part of an ancient Martian riverbed. At the European Planetary Science Conference, scientists gathered to present evidence of Mars' damp past. Between September 8 and 13, researchers detailed the findings from Curiosity. Melissa Rice, a researcher from the California Institute of Technology, believes that Curiosity has revealed a habitable Mars, with instruments capable of exploring the planet’s atmosphere and surface environment.

The study of Martian rocks by the rover indicates that ancient Mars could have supported microbial life. Not long after Mars formed, there was an early habitable environment. At least two major stages of wet environments have occurred in Mars’ evolutionary history. During its drilling into Martian rocks, Curiosity discovered clay minerals, indicating significant changes in the Martian environment, with flowing water shaping the riverbeds we see now. Not only has Curiosity found evidence of a wet Martian environment, but the Spirit and Opportunity rovers, which landed on Mars in 2004, also uncovered many signs of water on ancient Mars. However, the earliest "water" on Mars was likely acidic. Scientists at the Planetary Science Institute believe this information is very similar to liquid water environments on Earth, where similar phenomena occur. They speculate that Martian lakes were ideal places for microbial survival and reproduction.

Curiosity's ChemCam instrument investigates Martian rocks using laser pulses to detect their internal composition. Near Yellowknife Bay, it found traces resembling Earth’s riverbeds. The results show that turbulent water flows gradually shaped the early Martian riverbeds. Scientists from Imperial College London believe these phenomena clearly indicate the existence of riverbeds on ancient Mars, preserved for billions of years.

Curiosity is now beginning to investigate Mount Sharp in Gale Crater, which stands about 5000 meters high. Scientists believe microbial traces may exist within its rock layers. Recently, scientists announced that no microbial life was found in the soil beneath Curiosity's landing site because no methane or other substances related to biological metabolism were detected.
