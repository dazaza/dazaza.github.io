The process of buying and selling stocks is as follows: When buying or selling stocks, you must specify whether you want to buy or sell, the name (or code) of the stock, quantity, and price. Moreover, this entrustment is only valid on the day it is placed. The content of the entrustment includes the abbreviation (code) of the stock you wish to trade, the quantity, and the price at which you want to buy or sell the stock. The abbreviation of the stock usually consists of three to four Chinese characters, while the stock code is six digits. The stock code and abbreviation must be consistent when entrusting a trade. Meanwhile, there are certain regulations regarding the quantity of stocks traded: the quantity of stocks to be purchased must be a whole multiple of 100, whereas the quantity of stocks to be sold does not have to be a whole multiple of 100. There are four ways to place an order: counter submission, telephone automatic entrustment, computer automatic entrustment, and remote terminal entrustment.
