Do you remember the 1100 with the flashlight? A look back at Nokia's classic phones.

by anonymous on 2013-09-04 15:02:50

Do you remember the 1100 that could light up your way home? Or the 3210 that kept you company during boring afternoons? And the "fat six" that introduced you to smartphones... Over the past few decades, Nokia has brought us countless products that deserve a place in the history of mobile phones. But today, Microsoft announced the acquisition of Nokia's phone business, signaling the end of an era. Let's take a look back at Nokia's classic products over the years.

The Nokia 1100 was released in October 2003 and has sold a cumulative total of 200 million units worldwide, making it the best-selling phone in the world.

The Nokia 3210 ranks second in global sales, launched in 1999, selling 160 million units.

The 3250 features a unique twist design, stylish and novel, with a dedicated music key for controlling music playback as a music phone.

The 3310 is a classic representative of Nokia's early low-end phones, popular for its superior performance and simplicity.

The Nokia 5110 is an economical phone, an external antenna straight-bar model produced in 1998, now discontinued.

The Nokia 6108 is a new type of Nokia phone launched in June 2003, mainly featuring handwriting input functionality, cleverly designed on the back cover of the numeric keypad.

The Nokia 6600 was launched in October 2003 by Nokia, using the Symbian 7.0S S60 2.0 operating system based on GSM networks.

The Nokia 6630 is a classic Nokia S60 smartphone, equipped with a 1.3-megapixel camera.

The 7610's appearance resembles a leaf to some and a knife to others, with an unprecedented oblique symmetrical structure different from the usual "Nokia curve."

In 2002, the Nokia 7650 alone created five firsts: the world's first color screen phone, the first Symbian system smartphone, the first built-in camera phone, the first slider phone, and the first five-way joystick phone.

The Nokia 8250 is a straight-bar phone launched by Nokia in 2001.

The Nokia 8310 adopts the traditional straight-bar shape of the Nokia series, with a mirror finish on the front panel dividing the screen and keys, allowing for interchangeable front and back covers.

Gamers can play N-Gage games on the N-Gage QD, access the "N-Gage Arena" via mobile networks to connect with the global mobile gaming community, and participate in close-range multiplayer mobile games through Bluetooth wireless technology.

The Nokia N73 features a straight-bar design with a 2.4-inch 260k-color TFT screen with a resolution of 240x320 pixels, using the Symbian OS 9.1 operating system, and an integrated 3.2-megapixel Carl Zeiss optical lens.

The Nokia N95 uses a dual-slide design for easy mode switching, with a slide keyboard on top and touch-sensitive operations for music and video playback functions.

The Nokia E71 is a business smartphone launched by Nokia in November 2008, using the Symbian 9.2 S60 3.1 (S60 Third Edition FP) operating system.

The X7 is Nokia's first phone to use the Symbian Anna system, featuring a 680MHz processor, 256MB RAM + 1GB ROM body memory, a 4.0-inch 640x360 pixel AMOLED capacitive touchscreen supporting multi-touch.

In 2012 at MWC, Nokia unveiled the 808 PureView, a phone with a stunning 41-megapixel Carl Zeiss camera, which became Nokia's last Symbian phone.

In November 2011, Nokia released the Nokia N9. The N9 used the MeeGo1.2 Harmattan operating system, becoming the only and final MeeGo phone.

The Lumia 800 is Nokia's first Windows Phone smartphone in the Lumia series, inspired by the polycarbonate unibody design of the Nokia N9, adding three Windows Phone required buttons on the front.

The Lumia 920 is a smartphone co-released by Nokia and Microsoft, running the WP8 operating system, powered by a Qualcomm Snapdragon S4 dual-core processor, integrating second-generation PureView photography technology, and possessing multiple advanced technologies.

The Nokia Lumia 1020 features a 41-megapixel backlit camera, the main improvement of the device, which Nokia calls the most advanced backlit sensor to date.
