Water is an essential element for every season, and many skin issues start due to dehydration. Currently, it's the transition from spring to summer, and different skin types require different methods of hydration. For oily skin, signs of dehydration include excessive oiliness, especially around the nose area, enlarged pores. The remedy strategy focuses on thorough cleansing and hydration for oily skin. Choose products that are refreshing and oil-free but still provide high hydration. Use water-based oil-control emulsions, moisturizing gels, mineral water or toners, followed by a moisturizing gel. Products containing tea tree oil can remove excess oils from the skin surface, providing anti-inflammatory, antibacterial effects, and treating acne.

For oily skin, here’s a rice-water facial method:

Steps:

1. Stir the rice water: Take 3 tablespoons of rice, put them in half a basin of water, and stir for 2 minutes.

2. Wash face with rice water: After the water cools down, use it to wash your face. Soak a cotton pad in the water and wipe from top to bottom repeatedly, which helps to shrink pores. It’s suitable for daily use.

For lemon hydration balance toner method for oily skin:

Steps:

1. Peel a lemon: Take one lemon, peel it and cut it into pieces.

2. Mix lemon water: Put all pieces into a juicer, squeeze until it becomes juice, then mix with distilled water at a 1:1 ratio. Do not apply directly to the face.

For normal skin, having a balanced skin type is a blessing, but if not properly cared for, it may show signs of being too dry or too oily.

The care strategy suggests using products close to the skin's pH level. Many chemical products contain hydrating and moisturizing components, so just using an appropriate degree of mineral water is sufficient. Avoid using too nourishing night creams before bedtime, as oily products can mix with the oil on your skin and cause pimples.

For dry/sensitive skin, dehydration shows as dryness, roughness, easy flaking during seasonal changes, sensitivity, small rashes, and fine lines around the eyes.

Care strategy involves using moisturizing lotions with hyaluronic acid and plant extracts after washing your face. Dry skin lacks moisture and easily develops tiny cracks, so while hydrating, it's important to replenish oil content. Use non-greasy moisturizing products that are highly hydrating without being oily.

For dry skin, here’s a green tea facial method:

Green tea has excellent antioxidant properties and is a star ingredient in many cosmetic products, enhancing skin resistance, delaying skin aging, and nourishing the skin. When choosing green tea, pay attention to selecting tea leaves rich in antioxidants and removing tea stems, as tea stems are less effective than tea leaves.

Steps:

1. Take green tea: Grab a handful of green tea leaves, and note that green tea can only be used once, so the amount doesn't need to be large.

2. Brew green tea: Place in half a basin of water, stir for 3 minutes, fully steep the tea, wait until the water temperature approaches skin temperature, then use a cotton pad to directly wash your face. This method can be performed daily.

For dry skin cucumber beauty water hydration method:

Ingredients: 1 cucumber, 3 tablespoons of honey, half a cup of purified water.

Steps:

1. Extract cucumber water: Wash a small cucumber, cut it into slices, and place it in a juicer.

2. Apply cucumber beauty water: Wipe from top to bottom, repeat wiping until the cotton pad dries. Don't worry about cucumber coloring affecting the skin because when using a cotton pad, the color has already been absorbed by the cotton pad, and only the water reaches the skin.

In conclusion, regardless of the season, maintaining proper hydration is crucial for healthy skin. Whether you have oily, normal, or dry/sensitive skin, there are specific methods and products tailored to each skin type to keep it hydrated and glowing.
