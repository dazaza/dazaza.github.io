However, head coach Shi Zhihao was quite modest when looking forward to tomorrow's championship match against Singapore.

On the evening of April 24th, the Table Tennis Asian Championships concluded two semi-final matches in Macau. The Singapore women's team narrowly defeated Japan 3-2 to advance to the final first, followed by China cleanly defeating South Korea 3-0. Guo Yan, Ding Ning, and Liu Shiwen respectively defeated their respective opponents in a completely one-sided match.

"Today's semi-final against the South Korean team can be said that we were well-prepared. We have been preparing for the South Korean team during the closed training session. From today's match, the overall tactics were basically under the control of our players. The effect of our entire closed training session, from today's match, is still pretty good." Shi Zhihao was satisfied with his disciples' performance.

When talking about tomorrow's final opponent, the Singapore team, Shi Zhihao showed sufficient respect: "The Singapore team is in good form. In such a tense situation today, they ultimately defeated the Japanese team 3-2. After passing this tough battle, their morale must be high."

After being upset by Singapore in the Moscow World Table Tennis Championships, the Chinese women's table tennis team has defeated their opponents multiple times in major competitions such as the Asian Games and the World Cup, proving with facts that they are still worthy of being the dominant force in today's table tennis world. However, Shi Zhihao humbly stated that the Asian Championships mainly served the World Championships. It is evident that he does not want to put unnecessary pressure on his players: "This Asian Championship is mainly for pre-tournament practice. It is part of our preparation for the World Championships group match during the closed training session. Everyone will probe each other's current status." Shi Zhihao also humbly said, "From our perspective, we must first place ourselves in the right position because they are the world champions after all."
