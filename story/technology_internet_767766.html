Apple Event Summary: OS X is now free, and the Retina iPad mini has finally arrived.

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 13:28:43

Apple held its third event since 2013 (WWDC, iPhone, iPad) at the芳草地 Art Center, also marking the last major product launch in the tech world this year. Apple unveiled the iPad Air (iPad 5), the iPad mini, and began offering OS X Mavericks as a free update. During the event, the MacBook lineup was refreshed, with Retina display-equipped models becoming lighter and thinner. Additionally, new purchasers of iOS or Mac devices will receive the iLife and iWork suites for free.

The iPad was the centerpiece of this event. As more people favor tablets, these devices are surpassing laptops to become what more users prefer in the future. The latest iPad updates have once again widened the gap between Apple and its competitors. Two new iPads were introduced: the iPad Air and the iPad mini 2.

The iPad Air (iPad 5) is 135 grams lighter than its predecessor.

The new generation iPad, originally predicted to be named iPad 5, was instead called the iPad Air, emphasizing Apple's desire to highlight significant improvements in size. The iPad Air is 7.5mm thick compared to 9.4mm for the iPad 4 and 8.6mm for the second-generation Nexus 7. Weighing 469 grams, it is one-third lighter than the previous generation.

Performance-wise, the iPad Air is no slouch, with the following specifications:

- 9.7-inch Retina display

- A7 processor, M7 coprocessor

- 5-megapixel iSight camera

- Dual microphones

- Weight: 469 grams (Wi-Fi version), 478 grams (cellular version)

- Thickness: 7.5mm

- Battery life: 10 hours

On Apple’s Chinese website, the Wi-Fi version of the iPad Air starts at 3588 RMB, which is 100 RMB cheaper than the previous iPad launch price. The cellular data version has not yet had its price updated.

The new iPad mini finally features a Retina display, starting at 2888 RMB.

The iPad mini 2 is another highlight of the event. As smaller tablets gain popularity, the importance of the iPad mini line even surpasses that of the standard iPad. The arrival of Retina on the iPad mini comes at the cost of an increased thickness by 0.3mm (Wi-Fi version) and an additional weight of 23 grams. Specific parameters include:

- 7.9-inch Retina resolution display

- A7 processor, M7 coprocessor

- 5-megapixel iSight camera

- Weight: 331 grams (Wi-Fi version), 341 grams (cellular data version)

- Thickness: 7.5mm

- Battery life: 10 hours

On Apple’s Chinese website, the Retina-equipped iPad mini starts at 2888 RMB, with no pricing yet available for the cellular data version.

Both products will launch in China first. Pre-orders for the iPad Air begin on November 1, while the iPad mini 2 is scheduled for November, though exact shipping dates have not been announced.

Apple’s official website also adjusted the market distribution of the entire iPad lineup, stopping sales of the iPad 3 and iPad 4. Now four products are offered: the iPad Air, iPad 2, iPad mini 2, and iPad mini. Their prices are 3588 RMB, 2888 RMB, 2888 RMB, and 2098 RMB respectively, covering a broad price range similar to the iPhone lineup.

Perhaps the absence of fingerprint recognition and champagne gold disappoints you, but in comparison, the iPad remains the best tablet on the market.

1 million apps, 60 billion downloads

At the start of the event, Tim Cook presented a set of updated figures as usual.

According to Tim Cook, over 200 million iOS devices have upgraded to iOS 7, accounting for 64% of the total.

App Store statistics were also updated: the App Store now has 1 million apps (475,000 iPad apps), with a total of 60 billion downloads. Additionally, Apple has shared $13 billion in revenue with developers.

OS X Mavericks begins rolling out today – for free!

Craig Federighi, Apple’s senior vice president, introduced the new OS X Mavericks.

OS X Mavericks underwent numerous software optimizations, resulting in an extra hour of battery life when browsing web pages on the same 2013 MacBook Air model – extending total usage time to over 13 hours.

OS X Mavericks uses a "memory compression" (Compressed Memory) technology that can cache more data than its actual capacity on a single memory unit. For instance, 6GB of data can be stored on a 4GB memory unit. OS X Mavericks will support OpenCL GPU acceleration, speeding up performance by 1.8 times.

Craig Federighi also introduced some OS X Mavericks applications we saw at WWDC in June. For example, direct replies in notifications, Mac version of Apple Maps, and the new Safari browser.

The biggest surprise is that OS X Mavericks, the latest version of the OS X system, is now free! Apple maintained its style by criticizing Windows 8 Pro's $199 price tag while showcasing its uniqueness.

MacBook upgrades to Haswell, Retina-equipped models are lighter and thinner

The MacBook Air lineup was updated at WWDC in June. At that time, the MBA achieved a 12-hour battery life (13-inch version) through CPU downclocking.

Today, the MacBook and MacPro lines were also updated. As expected, both the 13-inch and 15-inch MacBook models were upgraded to processors above Haswell level.

The 13-inch MacBook version features an Intel Haswell processor with a battery life of 9 hours. It comes equipped with a Retina display, yet it is 25% thinner at only 1.8 cm, weighing 1.57 kg. It supports 802.11ac Wi-Fi (three times faster) and Thunderbolt 2 (twice as fast). The starting price for the 13-inch MacBook version is $1299, $200 cheaper than the previous generation.

The 15-inch MacBook version uses an Intel Crystalwell processor, configured with a 2.4GHz i5 processor, 4GB RAM, and a 128GB SSD hard drive, featuring an Intel Iris Pro integrated graphics card. If necessary, an nVIDIA GeForce GT 750M discrete graphics card can be added. The 15-inch MacBook is also 1.8 cm thick, 25% thinner and over 20% lighter than the previous generation, with a battery life of 8 hours and a weight of 2.02 kg. The starting price for the 15-inch MacBook is $1999, also $200 cheaper than the previous generation.

Mac Pro, massive performance boost / starting at 21888 RMB

The standout feature of the new Mac Pro is its performance boost, achieving a 70% increase compared to the previous generation Mac Pro.

The new Mac Pro uses the latest Xeon E5 4/6/8/12-core processor with up to 30MB L3 cache. It features AMD professional dual workstation graphics cards with up to 12GB VRAM. It leads the industry with 1866MHz DDR3 high-speed memory, equipped with PCI-E interface SSD and dual workstation-dedicated graphics cards, supporting 4K display output, with stunning hardware improvements.

In terms of ports, the Mac Pro offers 4 USB3.0 ports, 6 Thunderbolt 2 ports, dual Ethernet ports, 1 HDMI port, 1 audio input port, and 1 audio output port. Apple officially priced the entry-level quad-core version of the new Mac Pro at $2999, with an expected release in December.

Apple continues the free trend: iLife and iWork suites are now free

Apple has already made OS X Mavericks free, and next came the high-priced iWork and iLife suites. However, the condition is that you must purchase a new iOS or Mac device to get the software for free – existing owners can upgrade for free naturally.

The iLife suite includes Garageband, iMovie, and iPhoto. Among them, music production software Garageband adds a "drummer" function that can produce various drum sounds. iMovie has been "completely" redesigned for iOS 7, adding an iMovie Theater function that collects videos you've watched in iMovie. iPhoto adds "albums," allowing you to create photo albums that can be printed and placed on your table.

The iWork suite includes Pages, Numbers, and Keynote, with the iOS version previously priced at 68 RMB per app, now also becoming free with the purchase of hardware. This delayed free offer was due to iWork being thoroughly optimized for iOS 7.

iWork also adds a collaboration feature. After completing work in Keynote, you can generate a link to share with friends, allowing them to modify it together via the link, similar to Google Docs' collaboration but applied to Keynote files.

It is evident that Apple is fully embracing the free trend, with software increasingly becoming a decoration or enhancement for Apple's hardware. Apple's business model relies on generating high profits from hardware sales, and any means beneficial to hardware sales will be employed by Tim Cook. Today, it's OS X, iWork, and iLife that are free; who knows what else might be free tomorrow? Of course, we welcome this enthusiastically.
