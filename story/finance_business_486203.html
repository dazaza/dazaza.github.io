Will toothpaste with triclosan cause cancer?

by jdhgsa078 on 2011-09-07 10:39:38

Recently, a bombshell news appeared on Weibo: triclosan in toothpaste may cause cancer. Colgate Total and some Crest toothpaste are reported to contain triclosan. News about the possible carcinogenicity of triclosan in toothpaste have been seen in the press at home and abroad.

In no time, the triclosan incident caused panic among many consumers. Toothpaste is an essential household item, and brushing teeth is something almost everyone must do every day. If it really causes cancer, then we would be exposed to these things every day, which would be very dangerous. Therefore, consumers call for relevant departments to clarify the issue in time so that people can feel reassured!

The reporter visited several large supermarkets such as Tesco and Carrefour and found that the ingredient list of Colgate and Crest products exposed in 2005 did not contain triclosan. However, in the ingredient descriptions of Liubijian Super Strong All-in-One, Fresh and Cool Perfume, Yunnan Baiyao Jin Kou Jian, Kangchiling 3 Superior倍Protective Toothpaste, Zhonghua All-in-One + Gum Protection, and Zhonghua All-in-One + Whitening, there was an item called triclosan, related to food market conditions.

What exactly is triclosan? The reporter first consulted experts in biochemistry, organic chemistry, and applied chemistry, all of whom said they had never heard of it. Associate Professor Wang Yuanling from the Department of Organic Chemistry at University of Jinan said: This should be a brand name, not a chemical component.

Observer Yu Xindie of Jitongbao (http://www.gtobal.com/) stated that the scientific name of triclosan is dichlorobenzyloxychlorophenol, which is a broad-spectrum antimicrobial agent widely used in high-efficiency medicated soap, sanitary soap, sanitary wash, armpit odor removal, foot odor spray, disinfectant hand wash, wound disinfection spray, medical device disinfectant, sanitary facial cleanser/paste, air freshener, and refrigerator deodorizer. It is also used in the treatment of hygienic textiles and plastic anti-corrosion processing. Although the national standard for toothpaste implemented in February 2009 prohibits or strictly limits nearly 1500 ingredients, triclosan is included in the category of allowed preservatives, with a limit of no more than 0.3%.

In fact, as early as 2005, there were many reports about Colgate toothpaste receiving a red card warning for cancer, and the mentioned carcinogen was triclosan. In addition to Colgate, dozens of other washing and chemical products also contain this substance. Up to now, the old issue has been revisited because the US Food and Drug Administration recently provided the latest assessment progress on the safety of triclosan.

So does triclosan really cause cancer? Observer Yu Xindie of Jitongbao indicated that currently, experts in the industry have conducted such experiments where the subjects used toothpaste containing triclosan and regular chlorinated tap water, and the brushing time exceeded the expected time of ordinary people. The results showed that the formed chloroform content did not reach a measurable range.

Professor Hu Deyu, director of the Preventive Dentistry Department at West China Stomatology Hospital of Sichuan University, who has repeatedly been commissioned by the Ministry of Health to evaluate mouthwash products, stated that up to now, he has not received any notice from the Ministry of Health regarding the safety assessment of triclosan. Currently, there is no research result indicating that triclosan has carcinogenic properties.
