The protagonist, Kyoko Mogami, is a girl who doesn't dress up and is busy with multiple part-time jobs every day. She secretly admires her childhood friend, Fuuga Shou, who is now an ultra-popular top singer. A huge secret is... they live together! One day, using the convenience of work, Kyoko sneaks into his company to find her beloved Shou, only to discover the shocking truth that she's merely regarded as a maid. Her rationality begins to unravel... Kyoko shouts at Shou, "I will take revenge!" and gets carried out by the security guard. Faced with Kyoko’s tearful anger, Shou tells her, "If you want to take revenge, enter the entertainment industry!" Determined to change, Kyoko decides to abandon her past wealth and join the entertainment circle!!

Jun Chistuki uses "entering the entertainment industry for revenge" as the beginning of a manga. The story is drawn hilariously and inspiringly, making it impossible to stop reading, eagerly awaiting the next chapter. This work showcases more refined art styles than the previous work *Tokyo Crazy Paradise* (formerly translated as *Tokyo Angel Bodyguards* - published by Dalian), along with different character designs.

Kyoko, the heroine, in no way resembles the typical shoujo manga protagonist—she is neither a hero nor a gentle and charming beauty. Kyoko is a simple and straightforward girl, with all her emotions clearly displayed on her face. Her expressions are rich, whether it's her pleading puppy-like eyes, her vacant expression, or her resolute gaze when serious, which makes her even more charming. Besides the energetic Kyoko, there's the gentlemanly adult Toga Ren, the youthful and cool Fuuga Shou, and characters like the unforgettable President Hojo, the kind Director Ogata, and the talented actress Sora Kanzaki... Each character in the story has distinct personalities, standing out uniquely, whether main or supporting roles, each having their own popularity. It's a lively and vibrant work, and as Kyoko gradually gains recognition in the entertainment world, it becomes even more anticipated to one day see Kyoko make a name for herself.

The subtitle of the book, "~Glorious Challenge~", not only signifies Kyoko's glamorous transformation and challenge to the entertainment industry, challenging Fuuga Shou, challenging Toga Ren, but also implies challenging herself! Due to Fuuga Shou's devilish heart and sharp tongue, Kyoko steps into the entertainment industry, originally for revenge, but she actually falls in love with acting, discovering her talent. Once on stage, she seems transformed, radiating an awe-inspiring presence. In the story, it's not just Kyoko; as the plot unfolds episode by episode, the seemingly perfect Toga Ren is actually clueless about love, Director Ogata bears the burden of his father's fame, Fuuga faces underhanded tactics in the competitive arts world, or Sora Kanzaki's strange family... Each character has their own challenges, facing pain, helplessness, or growth while being humorous and inspiring.
