On February 1, the CSRC website pre-disclosed the draft prospectuses of three companies: Shanxi Tian'en Technology, MZ Civilisation, and Wuxi Huadong Machinery. These three companies became the first to "test the waters" under the new CSRC issuance review rules.

According to the new rules, the time for IPO companies to pre-disclose their prospectuses has been advanced to around 30 days before the issuance review meeting. This means that the period during which IPO companies are subject to public supervision and scrutiny will be approximately 25 days longer than before.

The new rules have attracted significant public attention and positive responses. In just ten days, The Jin Securities received phone calls from investors raising questions, mainly focused on issues related to Tian'en Technology's high growth in performance relying on government orders, major customers also being suppliers, and shareholders previously occupying company funds.

The 30-day "life-and-death" test

On February 1 this year, the China Securities Regulatory Commission (CSRC) announced that starting from that day, the pre-disclosure system for proposed listed companies would be officially implemented. The time for proposed listed companies to pre-disclose their draft prospectuses would be advanced from five days before the issuance review meeting to 30 days before.

Under the new rules, the review process for initial public offerings is divided into ten key stages: acceptance, feedback meeting, interview meeting, inquiry verification, pre-disclosure, preliminary review meeting, issuance review meeting, file sealing, post-meeting matter review, and approval issuance.

"The pre-disclosure time under the new rules has been advanced to after the implementation of feedback opinions and before the preliminary review meeting," a sponsor representative in Beijing told The Jin Securities reporter. "This means that public concerns raised could very likely appear at the preliminary review meeting."

According to The Jin Securities, during the IPO review process, the Issuance Department of the CSRC will conduct a pre-review of the company’s application materials during the preliminary review stage, raise doubts, request verifications from intermediaries, and form a preliminary review report to be submitted to the issuance review meeting.

For proposed listed companies like Tian'en Technology, the 30 days between pre-disclosure and the issuance review meeting could become the critical period for testing their "life and death."

The aforementioned sponsor frankly stated, "The CSRC's pre-disclosure system has been in place since 2005. Since its implementation, many companies have been rejected by the issuance review committee due to issues discovered by investors and media before the meetings."

Government orders contribute to sixty percent gross profit

Notably, the new rules of the CSRC have sparked enthusiastic public response. Within just ten days, The Jin Securities reporter received numerous tips and questions from investors.

An investor specializing in IPOs, Mr. Zhu, pointed out, "Shanxi Tian'en operates in the photovoltaic industry. Looking at the entire photovoltaic industrial chain, the company's main businesses, such as monocrystalline silicon wafers, solar panel components, and photovoltaic system applications, all occupy highly competitive and less profitable mid-to-lower value chain positions."

"Last year, the photovoltaic industry faced overcapacity, but Tian'en Technology achieved high growth through government orders." Mr. Zhu expressed concern, "Such performance growth that defies the industry cycle may be difficult to sustain."

Data shows that in the first three quarters of 2011, Tian'en Technology realized a net profit of 62.49 million yuan, while the company's net profit for the entire year of 2010 was only 32.27 million yuan.

The Jin Securities reporter noticed that it was precisely due to special "support" from local governments that Shanxi Tian'en was able to achieve explosive growth in 2011.

According to the prospectus, in the first three quarters of 2011, among the top five customers for the company's photovoltaic system applications, the Yingxian Public Utilities Bureau contributed sales revenue of 44.0171 million yuan, and the Shuozhou City Shucheng District Jinsha Huihe Park Management Office contributed sales revenue of 37.7778 million yuan. Additionally, sales to the Shuocheng District Huihe Construction Management Office amounted to 9.1324 million yuan, and the Shuozhou City Real Estate Administration contributed sales revenue of 3.8462 million yuan.

Based on the 43.73% gross margin of the photovoltaic system application, last year in the first three quarters alone, government orders contributed 41.43 million yuan in gross profit, accounting for about sixty percent of the total profit.

More notably, large clients in the millions—such as the Yingxian Public Utilities Bureau and the Shuozhou City Shucheng District Jinsha Huihe Park Management Office—are new customers for the company in 2011 and had never appeared on the company's major customer list before.

Three major clients are also suppliers

Related-party transactions also trouble Tian'en Technology.

According to the prospectus, during the reporting period, the company engaged in both sales and procurement with the same client, such as Langfang Songgong, Zhejiang Xiangrikui, and Hengdian Dongci.

The Jin Securities reporter noted that Langfang Songgong Semiconductor Material Co., Ltd., Xiangrikui, and Hengdian Dongci were respectively the largest suppliers of Tian'en Technology in 2008, 2009, and 2010, with procurement amounts accounting for 28.46%, 18.11%, and 16.58% of the respective years' totals.

At the same time, these three companies also appeared on the list of the company's top five customers, with sales amounts accounting for 31.56%, 12.80%, and 32.26% of the respective years' totals.

A certified public accountant raised doubts, stating that if the raw materials purchased and the products sold belong to the same purpose, there is a possibility of consignment processing. In such cases, the company can only collect processing fees and cannot simultaneously increase both the company's income and costs.

However, Tian'en Technology claimed that its purchase and sales business with Langfang Songgong, Xiangrikui, and Hengdian Dongci differs significantly from consignment processing and does not fall under agency business. Both parties' contracts do not specify consignment processing terms or processing fee settlement methods, and the price risks of purchases and sales are borne by each party independently.

Moreover, shareholder fund occupation has also drawn attention.

As the controlling shareholder and actual controller of the company, Qin Haibin holds 70.614 million shares of Tian'en Technology, accounting for 63.63% of the company's total issued capital before the offering.

According to the prospectus disclosure, Qin Haibin borrowed funds from the company in 2008, mainly for personal property purchase and renovation. By the end of 2008, the balance of funds occupied by Qin Haibin reached 14.4575 million yuan, while the net profit realized by Tian'en Technology that year was only 8.9376 million yuan.

Will public doubts affect the issuance review process of the proposed listed company? The Jin Securities reporter will continue to follow up.
