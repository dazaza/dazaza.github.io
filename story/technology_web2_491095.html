**TCL, Beijing TCL TV after-sales service hotline for 9425 model TVs. After turning on the TV, a 2 cm wide raster appears on the left side of the screen. After about 5 minutes, the raster expands to 2 cm from the right side and breaks, with jagged edges at the break point. The width is unstable, varying in size.**

**Solution: After replacing C434, the raster returns to normal.**

**Technical data for Beijing TCL TV repair hotline.**

**Analysis and Repair: When connecting a TV signal, the picture and sound in the area with the raster are normal, and the break widens to 4-5 cm. Analyzing the cause of the fault:**

1. **There are thermally unstable components in the horizontal scanning circuit.**

2. **There are faulty components in the horizontal blanking channel.**

3. **High-voltage arcing at the CRT socket.**

4. **Poor horizontal synchronization signal.**

After the above analysis, the voltage at the collector of the output transistor D1547 was measured as 150V (normal), then the voltages at the collectors of the three video amplification transistors were measured as 180V without significant change, and the acceleration electrode voltage of 450V was also normal, indicating that the horizontal scanning circuit is normal. Replacing the CRT socket proved ineffective. In this model, the vertical and horizontal blanking signal channels are divided into two paths: one vertical blanking signal is amplified by Q406 and passed through D408, D409, D410 to the bases of the three video amplification transistors Q501, Q502, Q503; another horizontal blanking signal passes from the collector of the output transistor D1547 through C416, D411, C434, ZD401 (15V) to the emitters of the three video amplification transistors and pin 6 of IC304 (TDA3504).

When setting the multimeter to the 50V DC range, the voltage across ZD401 was found to be very small. Changing to the 10V DC range for measuring ZD401, the fault disappeared. Checking ZD401 and D411 both showed normal operation. The capacitance value of C434 was measured as 0.02uF (223/2KV). After disconnecting C434, the raster returned to normal, confirming that C434 was performing poorly.
