A Bigger Google+, A Smaller Android

by geekzhang on 2013-05-16 17:38:50

Unlike the comprehensive development of software and hardware last year, in this year's Google I/O Conference keynote speech that lasted for 3 hours, neither Android 4.3 nor Android 5.0 made an appearance. Instead, a series of Google’s own products were on full display, including the newly born Google Play Game service, Music All Access music service, as well as traditional products like Google Maps, Google Now, and Google+.Although a series of products, including game services, are still rooted in the Android platform, we can see that the ambitious Google is increasingly dissatisfied with tying its products to its own platform. In fact, apart from Music All Access, all products in the Google application suite have already achieved cross-platform functionality. The launch of the Google Play Game service might have been expected, but surprisingly, it supports both the iOS platform and the Web platform simultaneously.In the face of competition from iOS, the cross-platform nature of Google services demonstrates even greater openness. Although from the outside, the absence of hardware products may seem somewhat lacking in surprises, but for Google, a series of core application services are the foundation of hardware products.As for Android, it is merely one platform in Google's grand strategy.In the era of mobile internet, social relationships have clearly become the core of all services. The reason why Page has consistently promoted Google+ lies in the value of social networks to Google's product line. Whether it is games, music, or search, only by integrating social relationships can true user stickiness and intelligence be generated.Integration can be considered a keyword in Google's efforts to build an application suite. The unification of Google Hangouts is the best example. Previously, from the desktop platform to the mobile platform, including Google Talk, Google Voice, message services on the Android platform, and Google+ Hangouts, Google's communication applications were once fragmented. This consolidation of communication applications under Hangouts precisely reflects Google's emphasis on Google+.In fact, a series of applications created by Google can all be associated with Google+. The Google Play Game service can interact with friends on Google+, and the updated Google Maps has integrated users' Google+ information, allowing users to see locations visited by their friends when performing map searches.There are countless similar examples.In the past, we could say that the node connecting users to a series of Google services was the Gmail account, just as QQ numbers connect users to Tencent services. But now, the focus of this integration and unification lies with Google+. Google+ has become the core platform and hub that runs through Google's application suite, despite being relatively new.The title image comes from theverge.
