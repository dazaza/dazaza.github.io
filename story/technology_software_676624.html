Drunk man drove into the railway and forced two trains to stop (Photo)

by rogerzeng265 on 2012-02-26 12:00:47

The picture shows the Audi trapped on the railway, unable to move. The picture shows that Xia's breath alcohol content was 92.8 mg/ml.

A 25-year-old man named Xia almost caused a major disaster two nights ago: after driving under the influence of alcohol, he drove his car onto the railway and traveled over a hundred meters until the tires were worn out and the car chassis got stuck on the rail. His recklessness forced two trains to stop, causing the railway authorities to urgently suspend the use of this section of the railway.

The railway where the Audi intruded is located at the intersection near the Binjiangyuan residential area along the Yangtze River in Hankou, leading to the Jiang'an Bridge Engineering Section of Wuhan Railway Bureau. According to Mr. Yu, the section manager of the railway authority, they discovered the incident around 9:30 PM last night and immediately reported it to the Jiangxi Police Station of the Wuhan Railway police. They then urgently shut down the railway where the incident occurred.

This railway is used for cargo transportation and is quite busy. After the incident, two sets of cargo transport locomotives were urgently stopped. Because the car was stuck too tightly, the railway authorities could not handle the situation immediately, so the Audi spent the night on the railway.

The driver claims he was misled by GPS

In the morning yesterday, Wuhan railway police, the driver and his family were coordinating at the scene on how to tow the car away, and someone was removing the license plate (Hubei R59888).

When the journalist took photos, a young man with red eyes, claiming to be the driver, came forward to block him. He said it was just a minor accident; he was originally planning to drive from Sanyang Road to Liberation Park Road but, unfamiliar with the route, was guided onto the railway by the car’s GPS.

The journalist then asked him if he had driven under the influence of alcohol, why his eyes were red and bloodshot, and why he did not turn back even after driving nearly a hundred meters on the railway? He did not give a clear answer.

From the observation at the scene, the Audi's tires were severely worn, and there were obvious tire friction marks on both rails. A patrolling worker speculated that the Audi might have initially driven between the two parallel tracks. Later, upon seeing a cargo container on one track, it moved to the right track until its chassis got stuck on the rail.

It was learned that the driver surnamed Xia holds an ID card from Tianmen City and is not yet 25 years old. This Audi is registered under Xia's father's name.

Not removed until the next day

Around 10 o'clock in the morning, a tow truck arrived at the scene and prepared to tow the Audi away. However, after checking the scene, the tow truck driver found that the Audi's chassis was too low, making it difficult to drive more than a hundred meters on the rail; even if it managed to get onto the rail, it would be hard to tow the car on the ballast-filled embankment.

Helpless, the railway police had to abandon the towing plan. After much deliberation, the railway department decided to use a locomotive to drag the Audi off the tracks. Around 10:40 AM, the locomotive was called in and successfully towed the Audi away.

The reporter observed at the scene that the railway section Xia intruded into was close to the Binjiangyuan residential area along the Yangtze River in Hankou, with an open entrance about seven or eight meters wide separating it from the municipal road.

Residents of Binjiangyuan told the reporter that although local residents rarely use this entrance, they still hope that the railway department will seal it off to prevent children from accidentally entering.

Difficulties in penalizing Xia puzzle the police

The reporter learned that at 11 PM last night, the traffic police of Jiang'an tested Xia's breath alcohol content, which was 92.8 mg/ml, indicating drunk driving. After the breath test was completed, Xia was handed over to the Wuhan Railway police for handling. The traffic police of Jiang'an revealed that since the accident ultimately affected the railway and its nature exceeded that of an ordinary traffic accident, the final penalty should be determined by the Wuhan Railway police.

Yesterday, the reporter contacted the Wuhan Railway police, who stated that since Xia did not cause direct losses to the railway department, the penalty should still be handled by the traffic police according to drunk driving regulations.

For this reason, the penalties for Xia left both parties in a dilemma. On the one hand, Xia has not undergone a blood test since being handed over, and only the breath test results cannot serve as criminal evidence; on the other hand, his condition before being handed over had already met some criteria for criminal charges.

Currently, the Wuhan Railway police are negotiating with the Jiang'an Traffic Brigade regarding the penalty decision for this incident. If it were a general drunk driving accident, the party involved would at least lose 12 points, be fined 2,000 yuan, have their driver's license suspended, and receive a criminal penalty based on relevant blood test results.

●Volunteer Army once did it

During the Korean War, when faced with the destruction of supply roads and the inability of trains to transport goods due to their large size, logistics soldiers attempted to let cars drive on the tracks to deliver supplies to the front line. However, at that time, the tires were removed, and the wheelbase was adjusted, making the driving difficulty extremely high.

●Hollywood blockbuster selling point

For example, in Hollywood blockbusters like "Transporter 3" and "xXx: State of the Union", such scenes were used as selling points for the movie and repeatedly appeared in trailers. This sufficiently illustrates how difficult it is for regular cars to travel on railroad tracks.
