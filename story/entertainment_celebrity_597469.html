Yan Lianke's microblogging live broadcast of his house being forcibly demolished draws netizens' strong support

by edivista on 2011-12-01 10:41:22

Click to enter Yan Lianke's Weibo

On November 30, writer Yan Lianke sent out an urgent letter on Sina Weibo, telling the story of how his residential area began to be forcibly demolished that day. Once posted, netizens quickly followed the issue on Weibo and many expressed their support for Yan Lianke.

According to Yan Lianke's own account, three years ago, he bought a house in the World Famous Garden neighborhood in Huaxiang, Fengtai District, Beijing. In July this year, he was officially notified that due to the southward extension of the Wanshou Road in Beijing, including him, 39 households were required to relocate. Yan Lianke said that residents initially actively cooperated with the project. The relocation office told the residents that regardless of the size or value of each house, all would be compensated 500,000 yuan, with an additional 700,000 yuan reward for those who complied. This one-size-fits-all approach caused disagreements and conflicts between the residents and the local government and demolition personnel.

In August, the relocation office no longer mentioned the reward, changing the compensation standard per household to 1.6 million yuan. Yan Lianke asked the demolition party three questions: As a citizen whose home is being forcibly demolished, I want to see relevant documents about the road expansion project, I want to know how much land has been requisitioned for the road construction within the community, and how many households truly need to be relocated? Secondly, I hope to know the relevant compensation regulations and standards; Thirdly, why is every household receiving 1.6 million yuan instead of being compensated based on the area of the property? He said that the answers he received were "it's decided by higher-ups" and "confidential". During this period, the residents made multiple appeals to the city and district governments, hiring lawyers to resolve the matter through legal channels but without success.

However, the development of events made Yan Lianke feel even more absurd and illogical. According to his introduction, despite the neighborhood having a history of six years, on November 24, the Huaxiang government of Fengtai District suddenly issued notices to all the affected households, stating that enforcement officers discovered houses with unknown occupants (unclear who was living there) and unknown addresses (no civil registration address) on September 23 this year, thus declaring the neighborhood as illegal structures and scheduled for forced demolition at 8 a.m. on November 30.

Yan Lianke's Weibo post includes an urgent letter to the General Secretary and Premier: (click on the image to enter Yan Lianke's Weibo)

This morning, when I arrived at the neighborhood, I saw crowds of people and groups of security personnel in uniform, along with vehicles blocking the roads. The residents marked for demolition almost all hung banners pledging to defend their homes with their lives and blood! In the letter, Yan Lianke described the scene of forced demolition that he witnessed firsthand. He said: No one knows what will happen during the forced demolition, and no local official has come to negotiate or communicate with them. It seems that bloody casualties could occur at any time in this game of cat and mouse, either today or tomorrow.

Yan Lianke said that the purpose of writing this letter is for this matter to be resolved rationally and humanely, roughly according to the law, and to end as soon as possible. He hopes that through this incident, fewer absurd demolition tragedies will occur, and more dignity, informed rights, security, and happiness will be given to the people, so that such disheartening events will decrease, and decrease further.
