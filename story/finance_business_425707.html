Pickled Vegetable Superhero Series 12 - Pickled Vegetables and the Witch

Pickled Vegetable Superhero Series 4 - Pickled Vegetables as a Monk

Category: Fairy Tale Added Time: February 8, 2011, 1:46:25 PM Source: admin Clicks: 2178

Since that time they fought Pickled Vegetables, the spiders had been keeping an eye on him. Every now and then, they would make Pickled Vegetables bring them some good food from home. If he didn't bring anything, they would intercept him on his way to school and beat him up. Afraid that Ma Mabo would worry about him, Pickled Vegetables didn't tell her. Instead, he secretly took all the good food from home and gave it to the spiders while secretly practicing martial arts.

When the spiders bullied him, the ants saw the benefit the spiders received and also came to threaten Pickled Vegetables, demanding that he show them respect and give them something too. Extremely angry, Pickled Vegetables, who had never hit anyone before, suddenly lashed out one day. He released all the anger he had built up against the spiders onto the ants, breaking the ant's leg.

Seeing the trouble he had caused, Pickled Vegetables decided not to go to school or return home. He went straight to the Shaolin Temple, thinking that no matter what, he needed to learn some skills. This was no longer just about being successful or not.

Pickled Vegetables arrived at the Shaolin Temple and knocked on the temple door. A little monk came out, made a bow, and asked: "Which temple are you from, master?" This question confused Pickled Vegetables. He touched his bald head and said: "I'm from school. I want to become a monk. I haven't been to any temple." The little monk asked again: "Then why are you bald?" Pickled Vegetables replied, "This is how I was born. Now I understand. This must be arranged by Buddha," said the little monk, leading Pickled Vegetables inside.

The master looked at Pickled Vegetables and said: "You don't need to shave your head, it's quite convenient. Plus, with all these bumps, you're perfect for practicing the Iron Head technique," and thus accepted him.

Pickled Vegetables trained very hard. After starting the Iron Head technique, he treated his head like a ball or even like a spider's head, colliding forcefully. As a result, several trees in the Shaolin Temple were broken by him.

One day, someone's cow ran to the front of the Shaolin Temple and ate all the green vegetables planted there. Pickled Vegetables was furious and charged at the cow with his head. Unexpectedly, the cow did not back down and also charged towards him. They started butting heads, going back and forth for a few rounds. Suddenly, Pickled Vegetables exerted all his strength and pushed the cow onto a tree branch. Sitting on the branch, the cow mooed and cried: "Master Pickled Vegetables, please let me down. I will never steal vegetables from the Shaolin Temple again."

Seeing that Pickled Vegetables had reached perfection in the Iron Head technique, the master said to Pickled Vegetables: "Pickled Vegetables, you have completed your training. You can go home now." So Pickled Vegetables returned home.

Upon returning, Pickled Vegetables found his family poorer than ever. Ma Mabo had aged significantly. It turned out that after he left, the ants came to their house demanding compensation. Ma Mabo had given away all their property to pay them off.

Heartbroken for his mother, Pickled Vegetables worked tirelessly to earn money. Gradually, the family's situation improved. Because everyone knew Pickled Vegetables had skills, no one dared to bully him anymore, and many people sought his help. The spiders, hearing about Pickled Vegetables' new abilities, feared retaliation and specially invited him for a drink, wanting to be friends.

Seeing her son become a capable person, Ma Mabo was very happy.
