The composition of the dehumidifier's moisture-proof box and the application of dehumidification equipment

The dehumidifier in the moisture-proof box is composed of a front shell, a rear shell, a perforated box and perforated cover, two ventilation control plates, a tension spring, and an expandable memory metal wire. The front shell has a convex shell with a ceramic heater, with front baffles and front ventilation holes on the top and bottom. The rear shell also has upper and lower rear baffles and rear ventilation holes, and ventilation control plates with front and rear door panels are assembled up and down. The perforated box contains restorable desiccants. Through the set actions of the tension spring, expandable memory metal wire, and ceramic heater, the front and rear door panels of the ventilation control plate open and close the front and rear ventilation holes as per the settings to accurately control the absorption of moisture inside the box and expel the moisture.

The drainage structure includes a front panel installed at the front of the case with an air outlet. Inside the case, there is a condensate collector board equipped with a heat exchanger and a fan driven by a motor, which has a drain outlet. A compressor is located at the front part of the base under the condensate collector board, while a movable water tank for accumulating condensate water is placed at the rear part of the base. An inverted siphon tube is installed on the drain outlet of the condensate collector board, with one end introduced into the water tank under the condensate collector board. When the water tank is removed for cleaning of the condensate water inside, it will not cause the condensate water to continue flowing onto the base of the dehumidifier, so there is no need to constantly wipe the accumulated water on the base, thus avoiding rusting of the dehumidifier components.

Such weather is most annoying, with dampness everywhere. Not only does it make people feel uncomfortable, but worse still, furniture and clothes are prone to mold, and washed clothes won't dry. In order to keep the indoor environment as dry and comfortable as possible, residents have started using drying and dehumidifying equipment. Before the return of dampness at the end of the cold period and the beginning of warmth, heating devices such as radiators and electric ovens can be used to increase the indoor temperature to equal or slightly higher than the outdoor temperature. Efforts should be made to isolate the intrusion of warm humid airflows. Close attention should be paid to weather changes; once the wind direction shifts from north to south, windows and doors should be promptly closed, and cabinet doors inside the house should also be tightly shut to reduce the entry of warm humid outside air. Place some moisture-absorbing substances indoors. Relatively economical and ideal is quicklime (lump lime), which absorbs water vapor from the air when it dissolves and releases heat, having a warming effect on the room. Use equipment for dehumidification. Air conditioners with dehumidifying functions should be turned on immediately; where conditions permit, a dehumidifier (machine) can be used to filter the indoor air, performing dehydration, while continuing to heat the indoor space.
