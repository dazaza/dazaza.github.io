A Brief Discussion on the Development of the Internet and SEO Trend in 2012

by qingian007 on 2012-02-27 10:38:58

Trend One: [The Arrival of HTML5]

A year or two ago, HTML5 seemed like a vague concept with few people paying close attention to it. However, in the second half of 2011, the growth trend of HTML5 became increasingly clear. Using various semantic tags in HTML5 for learning how to guide search engine optimization does not seem to affect SEO rankings at this point since HTML5 itself is still immature and not yet widely adopted. But when it matures, all search engines will surely take action.

Trend Two: Security and Trust Crisis

At the end of 2011, the Internet experienced a security and trust crisis with over 6 million user registration data leaks from CSDN and multiple websites being hacked. This sparked doubts about the development of the Internet and its safety — Is the Internet reliable? Following this was the involvement of the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology (MIIT). Though seemingly unrelated to SEO trends, is it really so? Perhaps one day, website security checks will become one of the criteria for ranking measurement. In fact, this has already begun to show in 2011.

Trend Three: The Impact of Mobile Internet

In recent years, mobile internet has become the fastest-growing, most promising, and most fascinating business globally. SEO optimization will undoubtedly be part of its trajectory. Whether you believe it or not, although SEO in China currently seems to be thriving, there are still many shortcomings. For instance, in the use of mobile internet, we simply leave it to the search engines. To give an example, as a site owner, have you ever considered whether your current optimization methods are suitable for displaying on WAP sites? Search engine development is driven by user experience, and perhaps the presentation in mobile internet will also be part of their plans. Of course, this is just a personal speculation; don't criticize if you dislike it. Maybe 2012 is still too early.

Trend Four: Original Content Produces Better Results

Originality is not only reflected in the original form of websites; if that were the case, it would be too simple. It also includes the protection offered by search engines to corporate websites and products, which can be considered a form of originality. In future developments, possibly in 2012, pseudo-original content and fake products might be the focus of search engine crackdowns, as seen in Baidu's Sun Storm in 2011.

Trend Five: Changes in External Link Weight

In 2011, chain links that had been foolproof were repeatedly attacked, and numerous sites selling PR were shut down. Site owners accustomed to traditional optimization methods will find that in 2012, not only do the number of external links need to increase, but quality must also be improved. This is the basic standard for search engines to maintain harmony on the Internet.

In the past year of 2011, many events touched both the Internet and SEO: the deepening of MIIT filing leading to large-scale migration of website servers between domestic and international locations; the emergence of Baidu's Sun Storm eliminating junk sites; the rumors surrounding Google's second PR update; and even the burgeoning HTML5. These events inevitably raise questions about the direction of changes in China's Internet development in 2012. Although I am not an Internet analyst, I would like to share my personal views and predictions regarding the trends in China's Internet and SEO, as a year-end summary.
