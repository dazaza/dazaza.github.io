Working Principle of Magnetic Base Drill

The magnetic base drill, also known as a magnetic drill, magnet drill, or iron suction drill, is a type of hand-held electric drill. Compared to ordinary hand-held drills, the magnetic base drill has advantages such as high precision, good perpendicularity, high safety, and no reactive force. It is mainly used for drilling holes in metal structures, such as stainless steel, aluminum plates, steel plates, H-beams, angle steels, and other profiles. It is suitable for large structural components and workpieces that require large holes, making it an indispensable specialized equipment in industries like shipbuilding, bridge engineering, oil platforms, power plant maintenance, power company construction, and railway construction.

Working Principle of Magnetic Drills

The main structure of a magnetic base drill consists of four parts: motor, magnetic base, gearbox, and control circuit. In terms of speed adjustment, magnetic base drills are divided into stepped speed regulation and stepless speed regulation. The advantage of stepless speed regulation is that the speed can be changed without stopping the machine.

When powered on, the magnetic base switch is turned on through electromagnetic effect to generate thousands of kilograms of magnetic force, allowing it to adhere to steel plates and structural components, serving to fix the machine. The stronger the magnetic base's adsorption ability, the more stable the machine body will be during operation, resulting in higher drilling accuracy. When the machine switch is turned on, the rotor part rotates at high speed and drives the transmission shaft rotation via the gear group, which in turn drives the drill bit movement. Drilling holes are performed by the drill bit at the required location. Compared with flame cutting, the drilled holes have higher precision, belong to physical cutting, do not cause chemical reactions, and do not deform the material. Besides the traditional twist drill available on the market, there are also special hollow drill bits designed specifically for magnetic drills. These drill bits use the principle of milling cutters, offering high efficiency and good accuracy.

Traditional twist drills can only drill up to 32mm in diameter, while using core drills can achieve diameters of 50mm-100mm.

Hollow magnetic drills have a drilling speed 3-10 times faster than regular twist magnetic drills, and they require much less operating force because there are no issues with drill bit concentricity and vibration, thus achieving higher precision. However, hollow drill magnetic drills can only drill through holes. Hollow drill bit drilling is a highly efficient drilling method, faster, quieter, and more accurate than twist drill bits. It requires very little power to drill large holes in steel plates of varying thicknesses quickly and without pre-drilling or changing drill bits, making it 40% more efficient than solid twist drill bits.
