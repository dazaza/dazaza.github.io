1. Use egg white to process spring roll wrappers

When we fry spring rolls at home, the spring roll wrappers often break apart while frying. What should we do? There is actually a way to make frying spring rolls easier! After placing the filling on the spring roll wrapper and wrapping it up, dip the spring roll in egg white before putting it in the pot. This will not only make the wrapper stick better but also improve the texture of the fried spring rolls! Adding an extra step of dipping in egg white really makes frying spring rolls much easier!

2. Broken potato chips have special uses

When you open a bag of potato chips ready to indulge, you find that the chips are all broken. Should you throw them away or force yourself to eat them? Actually, broken potato chips have special uses, so you don't have to throw them away or eat them reluctantly! You can crush these potato chip pieces further and mix them with breadcrumbs, which can then be used as a coating for frying things! With this potato chip coating, the fried food will not only have the flavor of potato chips but also become more crispy and delicious!

3. Cleaning and maintenance method for light-colored leather items

Light-colored leather items give a refreshing feeling, but even a small stain is quite noticeable. How do we clean light-colored leather items? If you notice stains on summer's most popular light-colored leather items, you can use a toothbrush dipped in toothpaste or diluted cleaner (ten times diluted) to brush the stained areas. If it's leather, you can replace the toothbrush with a cotton swab. This way, dirt can be easily removed! If you're unsure whether the leather item will fade, you can test it by brushing an inconspicuous area or the bottom of the item. If it doesn't fade, you can safely clean the stained area!

4. Correct steps for washing dishes

After eating, facing a mountain of pots and pans, what steps do you take when washing them? Do you directly use dish soap with a sponge? Or do you dilute it with water first and then use the sponge to scrub? In fact, neither of these methods is entirely correct! The correct way to wash dishes doesn't require using a lot of dish soap; the key lies in how it's used! To clean the dishes thoroughly, first rinse off any residue or oil from the dishes with water or use napkins/old newspapers to wipe them. Then immerse the dishes in water with diluted dish soap, use the sponge to scrub the dirty areas clean. Finally, rinse off the foam with clean water, and they'll be sparkling clean!

5. Tricks for drying quilts

With summer gone, the big sun outside tempts us to take out our thick quilts for a good airing before the cold winter arrives.

When drying quilts, you can cover them with a layer of black large plastic bags or a large piece of black cloth. Since black has heat-absorbing properties, it can quickly absorb sunlight, allowing the quilt to fully enjoy the warmth of the sun. Moreover, under the intense high temperature gathered by the black material, it can kill dust mites in the quilt, achieving sterilization and disinfection effects.

6. Clean your sunglasses

Summer is over, and the sunglasses that accompanied us throughout the season can now be put away. However, before storing them, don't forget to give your sunglasses a good wash so they can hibernate properly.

To clean the sunglasses, prepare a basin of warm water, add some soapy water, soak the sunglasses in the soapy water, then gently scrub them with a soft cloth. Finally, rinse them with clean water, dry them, and place them in a ventilated area to air dry before storing them. Sunglasses come into contact with a lot of sweat when worn on the face, so for the longevity of the sunglasses, it's best to clean them thoroughly!
