by qinglai888 on 2009-12-20 09:25:49

Beckham's return stirs up nostalgic memories: Is he Manchester United's greatest No. 7?

According to Sina Sports, Manchester United drew AC Milan in the Champions League Round of 16, meaning former Red Devil David Beckham will return to Old Trafford.

Although he won't be able to represent AC Milan until after the New Year, the draw on the 18th has already created endless anticipation for this clash between the Red Devils and the Rossoneri. In Beckham, people suddenly realized that some memories can never be erased.

In August 1996, Beckham scored a famous goal from the halfway line in Manchester United's Premier League opening match against Wimbledon. This goal became his signature piece, and the radiant smile of the then blonde-haired Beckham remains etched in everyone's heart. In the summer of 1997, after Eric Cantona retired, Beckham was given Manchester United's No. 7 jersey. A year later, wearing the No. 7, Beckham led Manchester United to the historic treble of the Premier League, FA Cup, and Champions League titles.

However, after that, Beckham's time at Manchester United began to become less smooth. In February 2003, after being eliminated by Arsenal in the FA Cup, Ferguson kicked a boot in the dressing room which hit Beckham's eyebrow, leaving him with a scar. That summer, Beckham transferred to Spanish giants Real Madrid.

In fact, before the draw results were revealed, Beckham had already expressed his desire to face Manchester United. Days ago, Beckham said in an interview with La Gazzetta dello Sport: "I really want to face Manchester United. Since I left Manchester United in 2003, I've never played at Old Trafford. Seven years have passed, and I hope that happens. Me playing against Manchester United is a beautiful idea, isn't it?"

In fact, Beckham wasn't the first player to turn against Manchester United. Fans may remember Dutch defender Jaap Stam. After leaving Manchester United after three and a half years in 2001, Stam faced Manchester United again in 2005 while representing AC Milan. Coincidentally, it was also in the Champions League Round of 16, and Stam's performance on the field could only be described as "crazy." Ultimately, AC Milan defeated Manchester United 1-0 in both home and away legs, advancing to the quarterfinals. Two months later, will Beckham be as spirited in front of Manchester United like Stam was? Perhaps only during the game will we find the answer.

However, Beckham is the first No. 7 of Manchester United to turn against them. In Ferguson's view, Manchester United's No. 7 jersey "has special significance; players who wear this number are at the core of Manchester United and are great players." Those who have worn this number include George Best, Cantona, Bryan Robson, Bobby Charlton, Mark Hughes, Denis Law, and Cristiano Ronaldo, all luminous names. Now, this number belongs to Owen.

Each No. 7 of Manchester United has its own history. Bobby Charlton is globally recognized as the top star of English football, having led Manchester United to quickly rise again after the Munich air disaster; George Best is seen by many as a genius, having once won the Ballon d'Or; Denis Law once enjoyed the reputation of being Manchester United's king, and in Britain, many football institutions rank him ahead of Charlton and Best; Bryan Robson once led Manchester United as captain to win the FA Cup three times and secured their first league title in 26 years, establishing the brilliance of the "Class of '92"; Cantona is even more renowned as Manchester United's "King."

Compared to these legendary figures, Beckham is no less remarkable. After Beckham left Manchester United, Ferguson said: "I must admit, Beckham made a huge contribution to Manchester United. He is a legend of Manchester United, fully comparable to all the legends in Manchester United's history. Beckham's name will always be remembered by Manchester United and always familiar to Manchester United fans." And Best believed: "Beckham knows what to do on the field. He is an excellent player, but unlike ordinary players, he has higher star power as a celebrity."
