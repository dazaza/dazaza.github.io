Barcelona is not mature enough, and if they underestimate their opponents, they might get eliminated in the Champions League.

by kersfe3f23 on 2009-12-07 16:58:50

Barcelona is not mature enough and could be eliminated from the Champions League if they underestimate their opponents. For a championship team, it's difficult to use different tactics based on the type of opponent. But one thing is certain: all games are worth 3 points, and all opponents will be particularly motivated when facing Barcelona. In his latest column, Cruyff wrote that in the away game against Deportivo La Coruna, Barcelona made an appropriate response, but their performance was not convincing enough. Here is the full translation:

When a team has won many honors, players naturally lose interest in some games. This is where the current Barcelona needs to improve. Such problems won't occur in matches against Inter Milan or the "Clash of the Century" against Real Madrid. In last Saturday's away game against Deportivo La Coruna, Barcelona did not have this issue either. However, before that, Barcelona made mistakes in Pamplona and Bilbao, even though they didn't lose the games. And in the match against Xerez, although Barcelona eventually won, it was a tough victory.

In sports, no matter what kind of sport you're involved in, winning in games where you're expected to win easily is the most difficult. Especially in a long season, if you can win those less attractive games, it will be a great advantage for you. Here I give an example, although he participates in individual competitive sports, Edwin Moses is an athlete I have always admired. This famous American track and field athlete always used to win races over a long period and repeatedly broke world records. His performances sometimes had a showmanship quality, as he would speed up when his opponents accelerated and slow down when they decelerated. In short, Moses always controlled the situation, always holding something back to seal the deal at crucial moments. For many years, Moses was the benchmark figure in the 400m hurdles, but he was very good at controlling his own pace, knowing how to win in the easiest way possible. This mindset, this ability to self-control psychologically, is exactly what the current Barcelona lacks.

This is not a criticism of Barcelona, but a reality that Barcelona must face. I admit, overcoming this point is very difficult, especially in a team sport. To cultivate such a mature mindset takes time and experience. The current team has been playing together for less than a year and a half. Since last season, many people have hoped that Barcelona would defeat all their opponents with large margins - and in many games, they indeed did so. Personally, I believe what matters is not often sweeping opponents with big scores, but that the team gradually matures. I hope they can take just the right approach to win games, like Moses.

I hope that Barcelona can control games even when they don't play well, and not create too many difficulties for themselves on certain fields or against certain opponents, maintaining physical fitness appropriately but keeping psychological peak condition consistently, just like Moses. In fact, Barcelona already has the ability to achieve this; the remaining challenge is how to flexibly apply it, continuing their performance with minimal physical exertion.

The result of the game against Inter Milan will determine Barcelona's fate in the Champions League. If Barcelona loses, they will be eliminated. Thus, they will go all out. Facing Real Madrid, Barcelona achieved their goal satisfactorily. This is because, when you face strong opponents, you try to prove in the game that you are stronger than them, and the opponent will also spare no effort, putting all their strength into one battle. But in front of Xerez, Barcelona's performance only proved that the level between the two sides was almost equal, with no gap between the top team and the bottom team. In that game, Barcelona conceded goals frequently, disrupting the team's overall positioning. All of this happened because Barcelona's pace was too slow: passing, control, running, and receiving were all too slow. Certainly, Barcelona eventually secured 3 points, but they paid a heavy and unnecessary price. Their original intention was to reduce movement, but they expended unprecedented energy in the game due to incorrect timing and manner of movement. The root cause of this problem lies not in the body, but in the players' psychology.

So, how to solve this problem? The answer is both simple and complex. First, where does the team concede the most goals? It's where the players move slowly. Therefore, in the future, Barcelona's players should play more simply and quickly, with more forceful passes. If your speed increases, your actions naturally become faster because you must concentrate; if you think slowly and act slowly, your vigilance decreases, your control ability worsens, and the opponent is more likely to steal the ball from under your feet. Once the opponent steals the ball successfully, your position becomes chaotic, and you lose control of the game situation.

For Barcelona, adjusting their style based on the opponent may be something they don't want to do. But the opponents are different; once they face Barcelona, they will give it their all. Facing Deportivo La Coruna, Barcelona needs to know not that they are at the top of the league table, but that they haven't won at Riazor for many years. Many teams will be like Deportivo La Coruna, going beyond the norm to stop Barcelona when facing them. Fortunately, this time Barcelona played smartly: although they only sealed the victory in the second half, they could have ended the game in the first half. This was a good experience for Barcelona, and they should play this way against similar opponents.

From the above perspective, Wednesday's away game against Dynamo Kyiv might be a problem. The Champions League is a high-level competition, and Dynamo Kyiv is a well-known team. Usually, in such situations, Barcelona will surely focus on playing well. But this time it's a bit different; Barcelona still risks elimination. As Barcelona is highly likely to advance as the group leader, the players' mentality in this game deserves attention. If they wait for the opponent's attack with侥幸psychology侥幸, they will fall into trouble. At this moment, the risk Barcelona faces is not only the result of the game but also the health of the players, as they are more prone to injury when distracted. Without a doubt, Dynamo Kyiv will, like other opponents, put all their efforts into battling Barcelona, so Barcelona should play intelligently and move like Moses. (KKGonzalez)
