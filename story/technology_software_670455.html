Recently, a community-based product relying on Baidu Tieba has been launched.

by fanli5uv7b639 on 2012-02-20 21:39:56

Game platforms trial operation of the first batch of mini-games. Attached is a screenshot of Baidu Tieba Mini-game Platform. Baidu's "Box Computing" tends to favor open-source sites. It is understood that Baidu Tieba Mini-game Platform has currently launched 25 Flash mini-games, each embedded in the game-related sections of Tieba. The top three games are "Bunny Jump Bell", "Gold Miner", and "Flying Ninja Cat". Besides Flash games, it also recommends five web games such as "Ming Dynasty Era", "Tantan Cannon", and "Ten Years One Sword".

Due to the extremely high activity level of Baidu Tieba, Baidu Mini-games immediately gained a large number of basic players after its launch. According to reports, the cumulative popularity of Baidu Tieba Mini-games has reached 752,030,690 people. Notably, games like "Punch the Beans" and "Gold Miner" from the Tieba have become officially recommended by Baidu's "Box Computing".

What is "Box Computing"? Box Computing is a new technical concept proposed by Robin Li, Chairman and CEO of Baidu, at the "Baidu Technology Innovation Conference" on August 18, 2009. Before the "Box Plan", search was always a transit station; users would be redirected to relevant pages through search. Users could access mini-game platforms such as 4399, 7K7K, 3366, 2144, and Dawanguo through Baidu search. After the "Box Plan", Baidu has become a "mini-game destination".

Through the latest data, it is learned that Baidu's search engine market share has reached 75.5%, making Baidu the expressway. After Baidu's "Box Plan", the inclusion principle tends to favor open-source sites, and individual developers can also join Baidu's Open Platform, becoming a channel for monetizing their works. When searching for related mini-games, they can be played directly on Baidu's homepage. Some industry insiders have expressed concerns about this.

"Box Computing" amidst "doubts" and "applause"

After the launch of this plan, relevant industry insiders commented that Baidu's Open Platform "search and use instantly" is equivalent to a "high-speed road", but it is also a traffic black hole. Currently, many websites have not cooperated with Baidu, and even cooperative websites worry that users will stay on Baidu. In response to these concerns, Baidu CEO Robin Li explained at the "2010 Baidu World Conference" that "Box Computing" does not pose a threat to developers but actually lowers the threshold for developers. Developers do not need their own servers or websites; they only need their content to submit it to this platform for sharing.

Regarding Baidu's application open platform of "Box Computing", there have been numerous doubts. Relevant industry insiders stated that for users, this is a pretty good direction for improvement, but for the industry chain, it carries some monopolistic implications. Another strong opposing voice claimed that Baidu's "Box Computing" breaks the moral bottom line of search engines, as it only provides an "excellent" application, which kills the entire Internet's ecological network, placing it at the center of the interests vortex between Chinese users and small and medium-sized developers.

Regarding the profit model, other industry insiders indicated that the term "excellent" is quoted because it is an upgrade to its post-Fengchao bidding profit model, representing a strategic expansion of Baidu's ambition not to be just a search engine. Other small search engines can try differentiated searches.

Cai Wensheng, chairman of 4399, also shared his views on Baidu's open platform: one is to do what you should and refrain from what you shouldn't; two is that helping webmasters make money is the hard truth. A year ago, Box Computing was still a conceptual thing, but today's Box Computing has already formed a scale application, and he believes more and more websites and enterprises will join Box Computing, achieving win-win with Baidu!

Innovation inevitably brings numerous obstacles and touches the interests of many merchants. Whose cheese did Baidu's "Box Computing" move? Is it innovation behind or monopoly? In response to doubts and approvals, what exactly is boxed inside can perhaps be answered by Cai Wensheng's words: helping webmasters make money is the hard truth. (Wang Pei)

Postscript: "Box Computing" encourages innovation, making it very convenient for users to search for related resources, showing legal and authentic results at the top. This move aims to encourage authenticity and innovation. Developers without websites or servers can directly upload their works to the Baidu platform while enjoying profits. However, due to the "excellent" application, non-cooperative websites will inevitably be excluded. Cooperation with the Baidu platform is undoubtedly a "high-speed road," but for those who don't cooperate, the loss is significant. After "Box Computing", Baidu is no longer just a search engine; users can "search and use instantly" without needing to jump to other pages again. They can play games directly on the Baidu page.
