Shenyang's "elevator law" is expected to be introduced by the end of the year, focusing on elevator safety.

by qiuman8013 on 2012-02-14 17:22:25

Since August 30th of last year, a series of reports focusing on high-rise elevators have been continuously published, winning the support of many deputies to the People's Congress and members of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference (CPPCC), and also drawing high attention from the leaders of the Shenyang Quality and Technical Supervision Bureau. On January 5th, Sui Li, Director of the Shenyang Quality and Technical Supervision Bureau, stated in an exclusive interview with our reporter that under the appeal of *Shanghai Mitsubishi Elevator*, *Shenyang Evening News*, the Shenyang Quality and Technical Supervision Bureau, and multiple deputies to the People's Congress and CPPCC members, legislation for elevators in Shenyang has officially been listed as a project by the Shenyang Municipal Government Legal Affairs Office. The Shenyang Quality and Technical Supervision Bureau has already drafted the "Shenyang Elevator Safety Management Measures" (hereinafter referred to as the "Measures").

Elevator Legislation Officially Listed in Shenyang

Sui Li indicated that elevators have become indispensable tools for carrying goods and passengers in people's production and daily lives. Currently, there are more than 25,000 registered elevators in use in Shenyang. With the continuous development and prosperity of urban construction, the number of elevators will continue to increase. However, elevator safety accidents occur from time to time, making elevator safety a generally concerning issue.

"As the main regulatory department for elevator safety, our Quality and Technical Supervision Bureau has been jointly planning and promoting local legislation for elevator safety supervision in Shenyang with relevant media. In this regard, I would like to especially thank the *Shenyang Evening News* for its efforts and outstanding work," said Sui Li. She pointed out that under the high attention of the leadership of the Shenyang Municipal Committee and Municipal Government, and with the appeals from all sectors of society, the local legislation for elevator safety supervision in Shenyang has now passed the project initiation stage and has been listed as a legislative project for the Shenyang Municipal Government in 2012.

The "Measures" Focuses on "Elevator Safety"

Currently, there are mainly four issues regarding elevator safety in Shenyang: first, the configuration standards for elevators, particularly in residential buildings, are too low; second, elevator management is inadequate, and maintenance awareness is poor; third, the elevator maintenance market is not standardized, and the quality of repairs cannot be guaranteed; fourth, it is difficult to reach a consensus on elevator renovation and updates, and funds are hard to secure.

Although regulations such as the "Regulations on the Safety Supervision of Special Equipment" and safety technical specifications have made provisions for the safety management of elevators, further standardization and improvement are still needed for some key factors affecting elevator safety.

Sui Li told reporters that in response to these problems, when drafting the "Shenyang Elevator Safety Management Measures," the focus was placed on solving five aspects of elevator safety issues, especially residential elevator safety issues, including clarifying the responsible entities for elevator safety, establishing a joint governance mechanism for elevator safety supervision, managing the "entry" and "exit" of elevators, ensuring related elevator fees, and strengthening emergency response work for elevators.

The "Elevator Law" is Expected to be Issued by the End of the Year

Sui Li revealed that the "Measures" plans to include several links such as the manufacture, installation, transformation, repair, sale, use, maintenance, inspection, testing, and safety technology evaluation of elevators into the adjustment and standardization scope, achieving seamless coverage. It will clarify the safety primary responsibilities of production and operation units and elevator user units.

The "Measures" also stipulates the exit mechanism for elevators. It specifies that residential elevators used for over fifteen years or those with serious accident hazards, which after safety technology assessment are confirmed to no longer guarantee safety and have no value for renovation or repair, should be scrapped. Regarding the public's general concern about elevator costs at different times, the "Measures" clarifies three aspects: one is the daily maintenance cost of elevators; two is the cost for elevator repairs, updates, and renovations; three is the purpose of advertising revenue fees.

It is understood that this elevator safety management method, which concerns the personal and property safety of the public, is expected to be issued according to the legislative procedures by the end of this year.
