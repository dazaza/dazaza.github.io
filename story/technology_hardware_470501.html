Huawei Terminal Strengthens Cloud Services, Aiming to Reposition Brand in European Market

by tuoqing1188 on 2011-08-04 09:48:12

Huawei's first "cloud phone" - Vision: Foresight

Sina Tech News, August 4th early morning - It's hard to imagine and predict that Huawei, usually low-key, would suddenly be associated with the Italian Super Cup match. But it happened.

Following Huawei's official announcement on July 6th that they will exclusively sponsor the 2011 Italian Super Cup Beijing match, on August 3rd, Huawei Terminal released two terminal products based on cloud services in Beijing, including a phone named Vision (Foresight) and a tablet called MediaPad. Huawei Terminal also launched its new brand concept of "Let's Simply Share".

"These two models will both be available in the market in the fourth quarter of this year, but we cannot disclose the price at present," said Xu Xinquan, Chief Marketing Officer of Huawei Terminal.

People have noticed that Huawei Terminal has changed the image of Huawei as a mere equipment manufacturer, attempting to appear younger, more vibrant, and closer to human needs. "Huawei Terminal is transitioning to a new model, creating a B2B + B2C = B2P (people) model," Xu Xinquan said.

Xu Xinquan stated that according to Maslow's hierarchy of human needs, user behavior was studied. The "cloud service" platform launched by Huawei Terminal focuses on users, with service capabilities ranging from basic needs such as "backup, storage, synchronization", to intermediate needs like "entertainment, games, navigation", and then to advanced needs like "social networking". Xu Xinquan revealed that the platform can provide each user with 160G of storage capacity.

Why make a cloud phone?

Wan Biao, CEO of Huawei Terminal, believed that Huawei Terminal had its own advantages. "In terms of cloud, Huawei Terminal comes from the network, originating from the pipeline. Ultimately, the differentiation between 'cloud' and 'end' will occur within the pipeline. From 'cloud' to 'end', it must go through the 'pipe'. Huawei has the foundation and capability of the 'pipe', and we have a strong understanding of the 'pipe'. Due to our deep understanding of the 'pipe', we can achieve optimal synergy between 'end' and 'cloud'."

Xu Xinquan said that starting from the Vision (Foresight) phone, many phones in the second half of the year will support cloud services, and the number of cloud service phones next year will double.

From Huawei Terminal's performance, in the first half of 2011, Huawei Terminal achieved global sales revenue of $4.2 billion, an increase of 64% year-on-year; global shipments reached 72 million units, an increase of nearly 40% year-on-year. Huawei's Android smartphone shipment volume ranks among the top five globally.

"Looking at the global scope, the Chinese market contributes the most to our performance. Followed by the United States, Europe, Japan, and Latin America. Huawei's sales growth in all regions globally has exceeded 50%," Wan Biao said.

Approximately 65% of Huawei Terminal's sales come from operator channels. For a long time, Huawei Terminal has been mainly focused on ODM and primarily used operator brands. However, this time, from the words of Wan Biao and Xu Xinquan, there is a desire to reshape Huawei's brand in Europe: "While deepening cooperation with operators, we will increase efforts to establish Huawei's brand in Europe."

This may explain why Huawei Terminal chose to sponsor the 2011 Italian Super Cup Beijing match.

Wan Biao said that going forward, Huawei will focus on product, channel, and brand aspects in the European market, such as launching high-end models, expanding the high-end market, and increasing efforts to build Huawei's brand in Europe.

Huawei Terminal has even greater ambitions, "Based on shipment volume, we hope to rank among the world's top three mobile phone brands by 2015," said Wan Biao. (Xiuqian)
