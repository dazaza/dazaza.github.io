Why are the prices of dental implants so high?

by 454kq on 2011-05-30 11:30:06

Among all methods of tooth restoration, the price of dental implants is the most expensive. However, this has not greatly affected people's enthusiasm. Regarding the price of dental implants, it is actually subject to many factors that make it more expensive than other methods. So what are the main factors affecting the price of dental implants?

How exactly are dental implants placed?

Dental implants, also known as artificial dental implants, are not real natural teeth. Dental implants use a medical method where pure titanium metal, which is highly compatible with human bone, is designed and manufactured into cylindrical shapes or other forms similar to tooth roots. These are then implanted into the alveolar bone of the missing tooth area through a minor surgical procedure. After 1-3 months, when the artificial tooth root tightly integrates with the alveolar bone, a porcelain crown is then made on the artificial tooth root. Due to its non-destructive nature, dental implants have been recognized by the field of oral medicine as the preferred method for restoring missing teeth.

The main influencing factors affecting the price of dental implants include:

1) Many types of materials for dental implants: The manufacturing process of implant bodies is complex but extremely precise. Currently, famous implant systems in the world offer a variety of implant bodies and accessories that can meet different restoration designs, but their cost is extremely high.

2) High-tech clinical application requires a solid medical environment such as clinical treatment equipment, X-ray examination equipment, technician equipment, and some unique and relatively expensive restoration materials needed during the tooth-making process.

3) Dental implants are completed by specially trained implant doctors and technicians, requiring a significant investment of manpower. The doctor's technical skills also involve substantial costs.

The advantages of dental implants are also the main factors affecting the price of dental implants:

1. Strong functionality: They can restore tooth function very well, with chewing functions far superior to other traditional dentures.

2. Aesthetics: Crowns can be made according to the patient's face shape, the shape and color of other teeth, achieving the best overall coordination and aesthetic effects.

3. Good retention: Without using traditional denture clasps or crowns, the artificial tooth root tightly integrates with the alveolar bone, firmly rooting in the mouth like real teeth, providing strong retention and stability.

4. No grinding of teeth: Restoration relies on the artificial tooth root itself, without needing to grind down adjacent healthy teeth, causing no harm to the teeth.

5. Comfort and convenience: No baseplates or clasps required for removable dentures, eliminating foreign body sensation, making them very comfortable and convenient. This also helps maintain oral cleanliness and hygiene.
