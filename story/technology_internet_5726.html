Solidot announces an auction starting at 100,000 yuan on Ebay

by www9blog on 2006-09-04 15:48:45

"If any organization or individual is optimistic about Solidot's prospects and willing to take over the site, continuing to maintain this space for geeks, I am willing to transfer the website. Personally, I am also willing to spend half an hour to an hour each day helping with maintenance. I believe other team members will continue to participate as well. The minimum transfer price is RMB 100k (after tax), which includes a 100M database, the complete set of code, all expenses including compensation for team members, and the transfer fees for the two domains (Solidot.org/.com). You can contact via email at admin@solidot.org, but if your offer is below this amount, it would be a waste of time to send an email. In that case, I’d rather let these data rest on the hard drive for a while."

In other words, Solidot is preparing to be sold, with a base price of RMB 100k. If there isn't an appropriate offer, Solidot still has room to maneuver since the announcement was only for a "temporary shutdown." "After this shutdown, it may return in the future, but if it does come back online, it will definitely be when there is time and preparation." In fact, given Solidot's current brand value, finding an investor or selling it for RMB 100k wouldn't be too difficult. Alternatively, similar to how Kiko was auctioned on eBay before, Solidot could consider being auctioned on platforms like eBay or Taobao. Even if it eventually shuts down, it should at least go out with a bang.
