Your balance and proper timing in your golf swing will likely help increase your club head speed. This is achieved by turning away from the ball during your backswing and creating a tight "coil" in your abdomen. Then, by cocking your wrists to approximately a 90-degree angle at the top of your backswing. After shifting your weight during the backswing, you should properly transfer your weight to your front side (if you're right-handed) during the downswing. This is done in the following manner: driving your right knee toward your left, start rotating your body (allow your lower body to initiate the downswing to maximize the torsion in your abdomen), letting your hands "fall" into the swing slot, your upper body begins to catch up with your lower body which is releasing a lot of power and club head speed, and finally, un-cock your wrists to generate the club head around the arc of the golf swing into the back of the ball.
