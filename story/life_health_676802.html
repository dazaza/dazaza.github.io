The main reasons for delayed menstruation in women are two-fold: one is pregnancy, and the other is gynecological inflammation. Will pelvic inflammatory disease lead to delayed menstruation in women?

Experts from Xi'an Gynecology Hospital point out that the harm of PID is relatively significant; therefore, PID can affect women's menstruation. However, the impact of PID on women's menstruation mainly depends on the severity of PID. Different degrees of PID have different effects on menstruation. For example, when suffering from chronic endometritis, due to long-term inflammation stimulation, it affects the shedding, regeneration, and repair of the uterine lining, as well as the contraction of uterine vessels, leading to irregular menstrual cycles, excessive menstrual flow, prolonged menstruation or irregular vaginal bleeding. Chronic pelvic inflammatory disease includes chronic endometritis, chronic salpingo-oophoritis, chronic pelvic peritonitis, and chronic pelvic connective tissue inflammation. Whether several types of inflammation coexist or exist independently, they can all cause menstrual disorders.

The harm of pelvic inflammatory disease is significant. In addition to affecting women's menstrual irregularities, there are many other harms. Chronic pelvic inflammatory disease often involves bilateral salpingitis. Over time, this leads to adhesion and blockage of the fallopian tubes, narrowing or closure of the lumen, making it easy for fertilized eggs to fail to implant in the uterine cavity and form ectopic pregnancies. Sometimes, the occurrence of pelvic inflammatory disease often involves both fallopian tubes, causing lumen adhesion or even complete obstruction, leading to obstacles in the passage of ova, sperm, or fertilized eggs, resulting in infertility. Pelvic inflammatory disease can also lead to negative emotions in women such as anxiety, irritability, depression, and even sexual aversion or sexual indifference, seriously affecting marital life and disrupting marital harmony. What harm does pelvic inflammatory disease cause to women?

If a woman experiences delayed menstruation, if it only happens occasionally, this is a normal phenomenon, and women don't need to worry. However, if it happens frequently, a check-up at the hospital should be done as soon as possible. Delayed menstruation sometimes manifests as pelvic congestion during the menstrual period, which can cause aching and heaviness in the lower back and sacrum. About half of young women experience dysmenorrhea during their periods, and usually, it is accompanied by headaches, low blood pressure, pallor, cold sweats, and infertility. Therefore, women should go to the hospital for a check-up as soon as possible to determine whether pelvic inflammatory disease is causing delayed menstruation.
