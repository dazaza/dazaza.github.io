Those who reflect on themselves are strong, and those who are independent are tough.

by hunanshida on 2012-02-16 10:50:26

Teachers of the self-study examination of Hunan Normal University found that many Chinese people have a very interesting superstitious psychology and behavior. That is, whenever they encounter difficulties that they think cannot be resolved, or they strongly wish to get something, they are always unwilling to find problems in themselves so as to seek the path to success, but often turn to superstition, burning incense in temples, worshiping gods wherever they encounter them. Some Western scholars believe that the Chinese are a very strange nation with very strange religious beliefs.

In fact, the appearance of superstitious thoughts has a deep relationship with fatalism. This theory holds that all human destinies are arranged by heaven, no matter how you resist, you will never escape the control of fate. Therefore, some parents naively believe that burning incense in temples and praying for the protection of the Buddha will help their children succeed in high school entrance exams or university entrance exams. Such an idea, as a pure emotion, may not be wrong, but it is a big mistake to use it to guide and educate children.

The teachers of the self-study examination of Hunan Normal University think that the right approach should be that no matter what happens, one must first calm down and strictly analyze the causes of the problem from all aspects. For example, if a child is not doing well in studies or health, we need to analyze the situation or consult the opinions of relevant experts to understand the real reasons. Then, according to the cause of the problem, take targeted measures. In fact, as long as the cause is accurately identified, solving the problem is not difficult.

The teachers of Hunan Normal University's self-study examination website believe that naturally, there will be some difficulties in solving these problems, and one might temporarily lose direction. These things don't matter. As long as you firmly believe in your own strength, believe in your own mind, also believe in your own child, and believe in the wisdom of science, then one day, you will surely find the root of the problem. There is nothing difficult in the world, as long as you are willing to work hard. No matter what, as long as you put in the effort, there is nothing that cannot be achieved—unless you yourself raise your hands in surrender, bow to difficulties, and kneel to fate.

"Believe and it exists; disbelieve and it doesn't." This phrase often becomes the "catchphrase" of those who promote superstition and indeed confuses countless people. However, if you look back and think carefully, you will find another truth hidden behind this phrase: if you do not believe in superstition, then you will be confident, and the source of confidence will be forever young and will never dry up.
