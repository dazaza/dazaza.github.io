15 Teenagers Randomly Assault Strangers on the Street, Claiming It's a Game

by uelu6c6b on 2012-02-23 08:56:29

It is often said that youth tends to be reckless, but recklessness that leads to the harm of innocent passers-by is bound to provoke indignation. Recently, the Xuanwu police in Nanjing acted swiftly, arresting 15 members of a gang within just 24 hours who were responsible for the "beating of passerby A." All the suspects were born in the 90s, aged between 16 and 18. In search of excitement, they invented this game called "Beating Passerby A," randomly selecting pedestrians and suddenly surrounding them to deliver a severe beating. From 10 PM on February 17th to 6 AM the next morning, they attacked three innocent passers-by in succession, leaving the victims injured.

Out-of-town examinees beaten while shopping

At around 10:30 PM on February 17th, the Xinjiekou Police Station of the Xuanwu Public Security Bureau received a report of an assault on Zhujiang Road. Upon arriving at the scene, the officers found a young man lying in a pool of blood, while another was being supported by his companion. The officers then sent the injured young man to the hospital for treatment and questioned him about what had happened. It turned out that these three young men were candidates taking the art entrance examination in Nanjing. The one lying on the ground was named Li Ming, the one with lighter injuries was Wu Gang, and the other was Yang Yun. Around 10 PM, while strolling on Zhujiang Road and nearing Taiping North Road, they were suddenly surrounded by seven or eight young people. One of them abruptly asked them, "Why are you looking at us?" Confused by the question, Li Ming and his companions were about to answer when a baton flew towards them, striking Li Ming and knocking him down. Others then surrounded them and started punching and kicking.

Seeing the situation turn dire, Yang Yun ran away immediately, while Wu Gang instinctively tried to stop them from beating Li Ming, only to receive a severe beating himself. Pedestrians on the road, seeing the attackers wielding batons and other dangerous weapons, dared not intervene. After two or three minutes, the group stopped their assault, threatening the onlookers not to meddle in their affairs. Subsequently, they left in an orderly line. Wu Gang noted that the attackers were roughly their age, likely high school or university students, all quite young.

Out-of-towner also brutally assaulted

As the Xinjiekou Police Station officers began investigating this incident, another case of an out-of-towner being beaten for no apparent reason occurred at around 5:30 AM on February 18th on Taiping North Road. That night, Gu Wei, who was in Nanjing on business, returned to the hotel on Taiping North Road after a gathering with friends. As soon as he got out of the taxi, he was knocked down and severely beaten. Taken by surprise, Gu Wei could only instinctively protect his vital parts. However, the attackers seemed deranged, progressing from punching and kicking to whipping him with a belt, and eventually grabbing a bicycle to smash it against Gu Wei's head. Gu Wei recalled vaguely seeing three or four young men attacking him, with a few girls watching nearby.

After two or three minutes, the attackers left. Gu Wei called the police, and the officers conducted an on-site investigation, retrieving surveillance footage from a nearby camera. The video showed that six people—three males and three females—were involved in the attack. The three women watched without participating, while the three men were actively engaged in the beating. According to the victim's identification, the bicycle used in the attack was brought over from the opposite side of the road.

Case solved within 24 hours; 15 suspects arrested

Within one night, two street violence cases occurred. The attackers did not seek any financial gain or take anything else, simply leaving after the beatings. The victims did not reside in Nanjing and had no grudges with anyone there, which puzzled the officers. Due to the severity of the case, Hu Shinin, the Chief of the Xuanwu Public Security Bureau, immediately organized a joint investigation team comprising the Criminal Investigation Brigade and the Xinjiekou Police Station, deploying more than 50 police forces to work on these two cases. After Li Ming and others identified the assailants, it was determined that those who beat Gu Wei were not the same group.

Through on-site investigations and interviews, the police quickly ascertained the physical characteristics of the two groups of suspects. During the process of linking the cases, the police made a significant discovery. At around midnight on February 18th, another cyclist was unjustly beaten by a group of young people, with unclear motives. After the victims' identification, the police confirmed that it was the same group that beat Li Ming, with an additional member. Captain Ding Xu of the Criminal Investigation Brigade of the Xuanwu Public Security Bureau stated that the officers were determined to capture them and ask why they attacked people. By evening on February 18th, 15 criminal suspects were apprehended, less than 24 hours after the first incident.

Gang members claim they beat people for fun and thrill

Upon investigation, all 15 suspects were born in the 90s, aged between 17 and 18, with the youngest being only 16 years old. These individuals were technical school or vocational school students who usually hung out in bars, hair salons, and similar places.

What infuriated the officers was that when the suspects confessed their motives, they claimed they had no particular reason for their actions, purely treating it as a game. Moreover, they even gave the game a name: "Beating Passerby A." Starting last year, they began playing this violent game, with no fixed target, location, or time. Whenever they gathered, if someone suggested playing "Beating Passerby A," they would start searching for targets on the streets. The selection of targets was random—if they decided to attack the tenth person passing by, that person would be unlucky. Before each game, they would play rock-paper-scissors to determine who would throw the first punch.

The suspects admitted that the inspiration for this game came from a trivial post online. After trying it once or twice, they found it stimulating and exhilarating, gradually becoming addicted to the game. The tools used for the attacks evolved from bare hands to purchasing batons and carrying wooden sticks. Some suspects initially hesitated and didn't dare to hit, but under the encouragement of their peers, they grew bolder. Currently, the 15 suspects have been criminally detained on suspicion of disturbing public order. Many parents were shocked upon learning of their children's behavior, expressing disbelief that their kids could do such things. The police remind parents and schools to pay more attention to their children, communicate more with them, and provide proper guidance for their actions.

(All names except for the police officers are pseudonyms)
