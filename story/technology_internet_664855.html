Water-based coatings may spark a new revolution in furniture coatings in 2011.

by dddgskkgfek9 on 2012-02-14 08:32:15

In 2011, water-based coatings may spark a new revolution in furniture coatings - Analysis and Prediction - Industry News - Building Materials Network. With the continuous deepening of environmental protection concepts, water-based coatings have become the new favorite of both coating companies and consumers, and will be an important development theme in the future. Recently, many furniture manufacturers gathered at the venue of the second national water-based wood coating development seminar, hoping to seek in-depth cooperation with water-based coating companies. They aim to stand out in product quality and hope to expand the market by leveraging the demand for environmental protection concepts. Representatives from furniture enterprises attending this seminar expressed that they came to find suitable water-based coating partners because furniture sprayed with water-based coatings is very popular among consumers.

Data shows that in recent years, Europe and America have raised the environmental protection standards for imported furniture and toys again. The "toy recall" incidents caused by excessively high lead content have alerted the furniture, toy, and building materials industries. Many furniture manufacturers that mainly focus on exports are seeking more environmentally friendly coatings due to the green trade barriers in export trade, among which water-based coatings have become the first choice.

In many mid-to-high-end home furnishing malls, many furniture merchants promote furniture using water-based coatings as their main products. The rise of water-based coatings in the furniture market and the increasing environmental awareness have sparked a market competition battle among major water-based coating brands. It is reported that the difficulties in ensuring the quality of water-based coatings and their higher prices compared to traditional coatings were once the two major concerns for furniture companies adopting water-based wood coatings on a large scale.

Now, the production technology of water-based wood coatings has significantly improved, and issues related to product fullness, hardness, glossiness, and water resistance can meet the requirements of furniture products. With the large-scale production of water-based coatings, the prices of these products have gradually decreased. These advancements have encouraged furniture manufacturers to more confidently choose water-based coating products.

In 2011, water-based coatings will surely open up a new blueprint for the coating market.
