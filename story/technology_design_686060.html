The Desperate Father's Last Resort: Sick Baby, Uninvited Guest, Kneeling for Help

On the evening of January 9th, a chilling night in Wanyuan City, a journalist was writing at home when they heard an urgent knock on the door. Opening it, they were startled to see a disheveled man kneeling at the entrance, pleading, "Comrade reporter, you must save my son—he’s only two years old." The journalist quickly helped him up and invited him inside, but as soon as he entered, the man knelt again with a thud. "If you don't promise to help me, I will kneel here until you do." Then, he opened a cloth bag he was carrying and took out a pack of cigarettes from a certain brand: "Take this first; I'll repay you when I earn more money in the future..."

Exhausted Savings: Mother Paralyzed, Son Afflicted with Strange Illness

The man told the journalist that his name was Wu Lihua, a villager from Huishi Gou Village in Liu Huang Township of Wanyuan City. In 2004, his mother fell seriously ill and has been bedridden ever since. To pay for her medical treatment, he worked as a coal miner in Henan and Shanxi provinces. However, his annual salary of three or four ten-thousand yuan was far from enough to cover the medical expenses, plunging his family deep into debt.

Misfortune never comes singly. On December 6th, 2011, Wu Lihua's two-year-old son, Wu Hongtu, who was being cared for by his mother-in-law, suddenly developed a high fever. After one day of treatment at the Xindian Township Health Center in Wanyuan City, his condition worsened, prompting transfers to Wanyuan Central Hospital and Dazhou Second People's Hospital for further examination and treatment, but no cause could be found. On December 9th, upon hearing the news, Wu Lihua rushed back from afar to Dazhou and brought his son to Chongqing Medical University's Affiliated Children's Hospital for diagnosis. A few days later, his son was diagnosed with "Hemophagocytic Syndrome."

According to Baidu Encyclopedia, hemophagocytic syndrome is described as follows: "Phagocytes in human blood are a double-edged sword. On one hand, they act like cleaners, swallowing invading bacteria and aging cells in the body, removing harmful substances and protecting the body. On the other hand, if they become overly active, they cannot distinguish between friend and foe, swallowing useful cells in the body indiscriminately. In severe cases, they can erode human tissues and organs bit by bit, causing a series of damages, leading to serious consequences such as massive bleeding points, liver failure, and respiratory failure." The diagnosis certificate issued by Chongqing Medical University Children's Hospital showed that the treatment cycle for this disease is long, and the cost is relatively high.

"From the onset of the child's illness until now, we have already spent over 80,000 yuan on medical expenses. We currently need nearly 2,000 yuan per day, and we truly can't go on anymore," Wu Lihua told the journalist. Relatives and friends have already contributed what they can, and even the coal mine workers have helped to some extent, but now there isn't a single cent left to raise. "We are almost desperate," Wu Lihua said.

Cold Reception from Authorities: Kneeling for Journalist's Help

Wu Lihua had sought help from civil affairs departments but met with cold responses from both Liu Huang Township Civil Affairs Office and Wanyuan City Civil Affairs Bureau. Out of desperation, he turned to the media. After much inquiry, he located the journalist's home.

After hearing his story, the journalist immediately called Qin Deshun, the Party Secretary of Liu Huang Township in Wanyuan City. Qin Deshun said that he was already aware of Wu Lihua's plight, deeply sympathizing with him, and had communicated the situation to Wanyuan City Health Bureau, which might offer some assistance.

Li Min, Director of Wanyuan City Health Bureau, told the journalist over the phone that the bureau would make its utmost effort within policy limits to assist young Wu Hongtu, but compared to the treatment costs, this would merely be a drop in the bucket. He hoped that caring individuals from all sectors of society would participate in the rescue effort.

Visiting the Patient: Moved to Tears by the Child's Condition

On the evening of January 10th, after the journalist disclosed Wu Hongtu's situation in some public welfare QQ groups, members of the Dazhou Love Association in Chongqing, including netizen "Danbo", decided to visit the patient. On the evening of January 12th, he and another kind-hearted person, Hu Mei, went to Chongqing Medical University Children's Hospital. "The child's condition is very serious; his lips are ulcerated, and he can't say a word. We couldn't help but cry after just one glance," Hu Mei recounted.

"Currently, the child's grandmother and mother are taking care of him at the hospital. They stay by his bedside around the clock, resting alternately by leaning against the walls of the ward when tired," Hu Mei told the journalist. "Most urgently, they are about to fall behind on payments. The hospital allows them to owe fees for only one day at most, which is already an exception."

Journalist's Notes:

That evening, the journalist accepted the cigarettes given by Wu Lihua and paid according to market price.

In recent years, our "Angel No. 1" rescue campaign successfully helped people like Pu Zhigang, the "filial brother" who dug coal to save his mother, and Fu Huaqing, the "most beautiful blind girl," out of their difficulties. Doing similar news reports for a long time, we occasionally feel a bit weary. However, how could anyone bear to refuse someone like Wu Lihua?

Before publication, "Danbo" called to say that in these past few days, he had organized some caring individuals to visit the hospital, successively donating over 2,000 yuan in cash to little Wu Hongtu. "It may not be much money, but perhaps it can give them hope and let them feel a little warmth." Yes, we firmly believe that love is hope and warmth, accompanying the Wu Lihua family into the spring of the Dragon Year.
