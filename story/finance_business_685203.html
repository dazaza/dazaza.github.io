Chen Lingfu: Based on the future of the country and nation, speed up the revitalization of preschool education

by l8cdds09338 on 2012-03-01 14:05:49

Standing on the Future of the Country and Nationality, Accelerating the Revitalization of Preschool Education

By Chen Lingfu, Member of the National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference (CPPCC), Chairman of the Jiangsu Provincial Committee of the China Democratic Promotion Association (CDPA), and Vice Chairman of the Jiangsu Provincial Committee of the CPPCC

Children are the future of the nation and nationality. Providing quality early childhood education for children is to build wealth for our country and reserve talent for the Chinese nation. For a long time, compared with developed countries or even some developing countries, preschool education in our country has been very backward. This backwardness can be specifically manifested in four aspects:

Firstly, the proportion of children who can receive specialized preschool education is extremely low. The enrollment rate of kindergartens nationwide is less than 40%, which means that more than half of the eligible children are excluded from preschool educational institutions, especially in western provinces. In some regions, the kindergarten enrollment rate is even less than 5%. "Difficulties in entering kindergarten" and "high costs of kindergarten" have become one of the most concerning issues among the public.

Secondly, government investment is seriously insufficient. By the end of 2010, the government's investment in preschool education in our country accounted for only 1.3% of the total national educational funding. This not only falls far short of the high proportion of 10% in developed countries but also lags significantly behind the world average of 3.8%, including African countries.

Thirdly, the overall level of teachers is generally low. Taking Jiangsu Province as an example, fewer than 10% of kindergarten teachers graduated from qualified early childhood education programs, and less than one-third of kindergartens meet the standard of "two teachers and one caregiver." Worse still, there are some individual teachers who lack love, responsibility, and professional qualifications. Not only do these individuals fail to provide good education and care for children, but they may also cause harm to the physical and mental health of children. Over the past year, incidents such as "slapping children," "stabbing children with needles," and "burning children with irons" involving kindergarten teachers harming children have caused extremely negative social impacts.

Fourthly, the teaching standards in kindergartens vary greatly, and children cannot fairly access early education. High-quality, low-cost public kindergartens mainly serve privileged groups, while high-quality, high-cost private kindergartens primarily serve high-income groups. Low-cost kindergartens lack sufficient guarantees, and illegal kindergartens pose serious safety hazards. Meanwhile, many private kindergartens pursue profits and attract parents by focusing solely on hardware facilities. While they may appear attractive, they fail to provide scientific and appropriate early childhood education.

The aforementioned phenomena have already attracted the high attention of central leaders and governments at all levels. Documents and measures aimed at improving preschool education in our country, represented by the "Ten Measures of the State," have been successively issued. However, we believe that if we truly want to revitalize preschool education in our country, we must effectively solve the following four problems:

1. Further enhance the status of preschool education in the development of people's livelihoods. Existing documents, including the "Five Measures of the State" and the "Ten Measures of the State," focus more on solving the worrying problems listed earlier. Although this approach is commendable, it is still far from enough considering the important value of preschool education. We must genuinely regard preschool education as a major people's livelihood issue closely related to "building national wealth" and "reserving talent for the nation."

There are two reasons for this: From a global perspective, studies show that the investment and return ratio in the preschool stage is over 1:17, making it the educational sector with the highest return rate in social development. Therefore, it directly relates to the prosperity of the country and the well-being of its citizens. Regarding our country's situation, the single-child family model makes preschool education a focal point of public concern. Providing quality preschool education is the most popular and people-oriented government action, directly relating to the construction and development of a harmonious society.

2. Further increase financial investment and reasonably plan and use preschool education funds. The government must clarify the public welfare attributes of preschool education and take on the responsibility of developing preschool education. It should establish a kindergarten system characterized by "government-led, social participation, public-based, and private supplementary." Governments at all levels must establish special fiscal funds for preschool education, specify the proportion of preschool education funds in local educational development budgets, and establish per-student public funding systems to maximize investment amounts. At the same time, high-quality private kindergartens should also be encouraged.

3. Emphasize the training of preschool teachers and improve the quality of kindergarten teachers. The preschool stage is the most rapid developmental period in a person’s life. Compared with primary and secondary school students, preschool children have many unique characteristics in their physical and mental development, so preschool teachers must be specially trained personnel to be competent. The proposal in the "Ten Measures of the State" that "excess teachers from primary and secondary schools can be transferred to preschool education after qualified training" should only serve as an emergency measure. In the long term, we should attach great importance to the construction of preschool teacher colleges, increase the proportion of teachers graduating from early childhood education programs and obtaining kindergarten teacher qualifications, and enhance the quality of kindergarten teachers in our country. A minimum wage standard for kindergarten teachers should be established in reference to the performance-based wage standards for primary school teachers, building and stabilizing a high-quality team of kindergarten teachers.

4. Clarify the positioning and management of government-run kindergartens and strictly carry out quality assessment work. The government needs to sponsor and develop safeguard-oriented kindergartens to provide inclusive preschool education that meets the basic needs of people's livelihoods to the greatest extent possible. For private kindergartens, governments at all levels should adopt corresponding policies to encourage their positive and constructive development.
