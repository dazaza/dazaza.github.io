Facebook Tests Outside Mobile Ad Network Again

by anonymous on 2013-10-02 10:55:46

Facebook today confirmed that it has once again started testing an external mobile ad network. This network may use Facebook's data to serve ads on mobile apps outside of Facebook.

Facebook had previously tested such an ad network, but strangely abandoned it in December last year. Twitter recently also began seeking revenue through a mobile ad network outside of Twitter.

If the test is successful, this kind of network could provide Facebook with a new significant source of income. Currently, the company’s quarterly mobile ad revenue is approximately $656 million. However, that revenue all comes from Facebook’s internal network — its own website and mobile app.

That is still a considerable amount; Facebook's mobile ad business is second in scale only to Google's. In third place is Millennial Media, which generates quarterly revenue of $57 million.

A Facebook spokesperson sent the following statement to Business Insider:

“We are currently conducting a second test for showing ads beyond our Facebook mobile app and mobile website. Our goal is to improve the accuracy and relevance of the ads people see. Because we're in the early stages of testing, we don't have other details to share at this time.”

Currently, Facebook primarily sells ads in two ways: by having clients pay directly to target ads based on Facebook’s audience data; or through the ad exchange FBX, which uses cookies from non-Facebook sites to track visitors and deliver targeted ads. Both types of ads appear in the news feed on Facebook’s site and app.

In Facebook's new mobile ad network test, the ads triggered by its targeting data will appear on other companies' mobile websites and apps. This could allow Facebook ads to reach across the entire mobile web and app ecosystem.

Insiders reveal that the network's targeting options will include standard Facebook audience categories, such as age, gender, and interests.

Facebook's new move inevitably invites comparisons with Twitter. Twitter recently spent $350 million acquiring MoPub, which many believe was done because MoPub operates an ad exchange and network outside of Twitter. It is expected that Twitter will use its user data to serve precisely targeted ads to them on other apps and sites.
