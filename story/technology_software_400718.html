Online shopping surpasses 4 trillion yuan in 2010

by anonymous on 2011-04-01 13:30:57

Perhaps, no one would have expected that an industry could develop at an average annual growth rate of 40% for several consecutive years. Not only does it make consumers feel cost-effective, fast and trendy, but also makes sellers count money until they laugh, provides another channel for manufacturers to sell their products, and solves a large number of employment positions for the society.

The name of this industry is e-commerce, commonly known as online shopping. In the year 2010 that is about to pass, the development momentum of online shopping in China can only be described by the four words "irresistible". The latest monitoring data released by iResearch Consulting Group shows that the number of online shopping orders in China reached a record-breaking 380 million in the third quarter, an increase of about 50% compared to the same period last year. Among them, the number of C2C and B2C online shopping orders were 330 million and 57 million respectively. Yang Xueshan, Vice Minister of the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology, said that the transaction volume of China's e-commerce is expected to exceed 4 trillion yuan in 2010, and the amount of online shopping will exceed 400 billion yuan.

213.3 billion yuan: Crazy transaction volume

According to statistics from the Internet consulting agency Analysys Mason, the transaction scale of China's online retail market reached 213.3 billion yuan in the first half of 2010, approaching the full-year transaction volume of 258.6 billion yuan released by the Ministry of Commerce in 2009. However, for the huge Chinese market, the transaction volume of tens of billions of yuan is just the tip of the iceberg. In the first half of this year, the proportion of China's online shopping transaction volume in the total retail sales of consumer goods was close to 3%, while as early as 2007, the proportion of the US online shopping market in its retail market was 3.72%, the UK was 4.5%, and South Korea was as high as 8.65%.

As the data changes, online shopping is increasingly showing trends. First, "Internet goods" are accelerating brandization. Internet goods have become the focus of consumption in the online shopping market, including Ge Rui'er, De'er and other foreign trade-to-domestic sales internet goods, as well as a batch of internet goods brands such as Mai Baobao and Zero Number Men that are completely based on network development; second, "mainstream brands" are becoming online sales. The international financial crisis has accelerated the online sales process of brand manufacturers, and a batch of "star internet goods" have emerged, including international well-known brands such as Lenovo, Procter & Gamble, Uniqlo, Dell, as well as domestic famous brands such as Li Ning; third, "mainstream consumption" is becoming online shopping, and the quantity and amount of daily necessities sales have increased exponentially.

7.1 times: Group buying rises in China

Since the beginning of 2010, the search index related to group buying in China has shown a sharp increase trend. From January to May, the average daily search volume was 65,258 times, while from June to October, the average daily search volume soared to 529,696 times, increasing by 7.1 times.

With the success of the GROUPON model, the domestic group-buying website market has witnessed a dramatic rise in 2010, growing rapidly like mushrooms after rain. According to the "2010 China Online Group Buying Survey Report", by the end of August 2010, the number of domestic online group-buying companies with initial scale had reached 1,215, excluding the 256 group-buying websites that had not yet started or had gone bankrupt.

"This is the best personal entrepreneurship opportunity in the history of China's Internet," said Huang Yu, former COO of China's group-buying website F Team. Almost every day, four or five new group-buying websites are established, and nearly a thousand start-up websites with similar business models have flooded into this niche e-commerce field. On some group-buying navigation website pages, there are listed these strange and unfamiliar names of group-buying websites such as Aijia Group, Jia You Group, Aibang Group, Wowo Group, Nuomi Group, Fan Group, Keketuan, TuanKu...

According to the team size of each group-buying website, which ranges from 15 to 20 people, even calculated conservatively with 1,000 group-buying websites, nearly 20,000 IT entrepreneurs are gathered here, accumulating tens of millions of yuan of initial investment funds, excluding venture capital funds entering this field. Just in May and June, several websites including 5151 Group-Buying Network, Cool Group Network, Lashou Network, and Ayatuan received nearly 2 billion yuan of venture capital investments from Wan Jia Venture Capital, GSR Ventures, Zhejiang Business Venture Capital, and even Shanxi coal boss groups.

13 million families: Online stores drive employment

The "2010 (First Half) E-commerce Market Data Monitoring Report" released by the China E-commerce Research Center shows that by June this year, the number of individual online stores has reached 12 million families, and the growth rate is rapid, with the number of online stores expected to reach 13 million families by the end of this year. Currently, the number of B2C merchants and products in our country is increasing at a rate of 30% per year, and the number of newly added B2C enterprises this year will reach around 10,000.

Taking Taobao.com as an example. According to calculations by the global information agency IDC, one person opening a store on Taobao to achieve employment can drive 2.85 related industrial employment opportunities. By April 30 this year, Taobao.com had created 3.021 million job opportunities for the entire e-commerce industry chain, meaning that 1.06 million people have achieved employment through opening stores on Taobao.com. While at the end of 2008, Taobao.com created only 570,000 direct and sufficient employment opportunities.

In addition to driving individual employment, online shopping has also led to the emergence of "villages," such as "Taobao Villages." In Qingyanliu Village, Yiwu, Zhejiang Province, originally with only 1,486 villagers, now accommodates more than 8,000 people, operating 1,800 Taobao online stores. Last year, the online transaction volume reached 800 million yuan, and this year it is expected to exceed 2 billion yuan, making it the "first Taobao Village" in China.

There are many such "villages" in China. In some rural areas of Zhejiang, Jiangsu, Hebei, and other places, farmers almost every household live by opening stores on Taobao, becoming genuine online merchants. Such "Taobao Villages" are increasing, and the economic effects they generate are surprising. Yiwu's Qingyanliu Village is the most extreme case. It is reported that nearly one-tenth of the "Golden Crown" shops on Taobao with over 500,000 transactions are located here.

205 smart cars: The era of bulk consumer goods arrives

As early as the beginning of this year, statistics from iResearch Market Consulting showed that the transaction scale of China's B2C home appliance online shopping market in 2009 was 2.12 billion yuan, an increase of 177.0% compared to 2008. With the continuous maturity of online shopping conditions and the professionalization, scaling, and informatization of the logistics industry, the era of online shopping for large items has arrived.

In January 2010, Suning Appliance launched its "Suning Purchase" e-commerce platform. On June 23, TCL Group announced the launch of its new official website mall; a week later, Galanz officially launched its B2C direct sales website. "Small commodity online shopping has paved the way for the development of the entire online shopping industry. As small item online shopping matures, the development trend of the online market becomes increasingly obvious. For online retailers, transitioning from small items to large items online shopping is an inevitable trend," said Shi Wanwen, Senior Vice President of TCL Group.

Data also supports this trend. During the peak season, in August, Taobao Electrical City announced its performance for half a month: 12 brands, 14 trading days, a daily average transaction volume increase of 39%, a daily average transaction volume of 7.44 million yuan, twice breaking through 10 million yuan, and a total transaction volume exceeding 100 million yuan in 14 days.

Even more astonishing events are happening. At 10 a.m. on September 9, the Mercedes-Benz group buying on Taobao JuHuSuan went live as scheduled, and within just 3 hours and 28 minutes, all 205 Mercedes-Benz smart cars were snapped up.

Annual income of 100,000 yuan: Sniping is a technical skill

The term "sniping" in online transactions refers to a type of online bidding method where popular goods are placed online and immediately snatched up within seconds, sometimes even faster. Most goods participating in sniping are presented at unbelievably low prices, so naturally, they "disappear" within a few seconds.

Such online sniping activities are increasingly becoming a promotional tool for merchants or platforms, and this has given rise to a new profession: professional sniping teams. These sniping teams generally come in two types: one is proxy sniping, i.e., auction teams; the other is self-sniping, where goods are sniped and then resold immediately. There are more proxy sniping teams, and their charges are usually based on 5% of the value of the goods or according to the success rate. It is reported that professional sniping teams have at least a 40% success rate, and some sniping teams earn quite substantial annual incomes, with some self-sniping teams earning around 100,000 yuan annually.

Although the threshold to enter a sniping team is low, requiring a good computer and a fast enough internet connection, the key is still having "sniping software." According to experts in the security industry, sniping software is actually similar to game cheats, simulating manual operations for placing orders on online stores like Taobao and Paipai through programming. By setting the sniping time, click frequency, and order information in advance, the entire sniping process can be completed in the shortest possible time, definitely faster than manual operation. Similar technology was first used in online games and is now relatively mature.

Size matters: Traditional malls turn comprehensive

Nowadays, the concept of online shopping among consumers has completed its cultivation phase. A large number of consumers have already gotten used to shopping online. Even items like glasses, digital products, and daily chemical products have deeply rooted themselves on the internet. Is there anything that cannot be bought online? It is precisely due to the explosive growth of massive demand that the scale effect and vast business opportunities in this field have been increasingly noticed by more and more enterprises and merchants, prompting traditional B2C malls to begin transforming into comprehensive malls.

Redbaby, originally a baby and maternal product seller, strives to transform itself into a department store that includes cosmetics, home furnishings, and health products; JD.com, which started with 3C digital products, is now also venturing into department store home furnishings; Vancl, besides its original shirts, has also introduced canvas shoes, jeans, and other products.

31,600: College students are the main force among sellers

The Taobao Employment Index shows that, as of April 30 this year, among the 1.06 million professional sellers, there are more than 31,600 people aged 18-22, accounting for 8.1%. This age group corresponds to college students, and a considerable number of college students choose to make a living by opening a Taobao store after graduation because their Taobao store business is stable during school.

According to the data from the "Statistical Report on the Development Status of China's Internet (July 2010)," by June 2010, the scale of internet users in our country reached 420 million, among whom post-80s and post-90s account for 58%.

(According to Xin Kuai Bao)
