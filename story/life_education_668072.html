Nurture children to become part of the sports population

by hudazikao on 2012-02-17 14:03:56

Teachers from the self-study examination office of Hunan University noticed a research finding. The author, an individual who didn't enjoy exercising during childhood, would find it difficult to develop exercise habits when they grow up, which will obviously have adverse effects on the quality of life. I feel ashamed that my daughter hasn't developed the habit of exercising, and this is related to the failure of her father. Because in terms of children's sports, fathers bear especially important responsibilities. I sincerely hope that all fathers take me as a warning and start educating their children with sports. According to experts, individuals who exercise at least three times a week, with each session lasting no less than 30 minutes, are considered part of the sports population. As a father, one should lead by example and cultivate children into members of the sports population.

Teachers from Hunan University's continuing education program believe that wise parents always place sports first in the process of their children's growth. The great thinker Mr. Lu Xun did just that. He believed that "wild cattle become domestic cattle, wild boars become domestic pigs, wolves become dogs, the wild nature disappears, but it only makes enemies happy and does no good for themselves."

Teachers from Hunan University's self-study examination website think that in the process of children's growth, physical education should be the premise and foundation of all education. How do parents value their children's physical exercise and choose suitable sports for them? The educational advice from Hunan University's self-study examination teachers is:

1. If you find your child not very sociable and not accustomed to interacting with peers: You can suggest that your child choose collective projects such as football, basketball, volleyball, and relay races for exercise.

2. If you find your child often hesitates and indecisive: Then frequently take your child to participate in activities such as table tennis, badminton, tennis, boxing, hurdles, long jump, high jump, fencing, etc., these projects play a significant role in cultivating decisiveness. If you find your child has an irritable temperament and prone to impulsivity: It's best to take your child to participate in activities that require control, such as playing chess, practicing Tai Chi, slow cycling, shooting, etc., these activities help stabilize emotions.

3. If you find your child lacks confidence: You can take your child to participate in activities like skipping rope, push-ups, doing radio calisthenics, etc. After persisting in exercise for a period of time, you will find that your child's confidence has increased.

4. If you find your child cannot perform normally and tends to get nervous easily: Take your child to more public and intense sports competitions to train their ability to calmly and composedly handle competitions.

5. If you find your child likes to show off and be overconfident: You can choose some more difficult and complex technical projects for training, such as diving, gymnastics, marathon, etc., or find a few opponents for your child, so that they understand the principle of 'there’s always someone better out there.'
