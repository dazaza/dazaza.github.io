FOR Phelps

"Because Phelps' image is too consistent with our brand image, so the entire company decision process took less than 30 minutes!" On January 11th, when Yu Hongjiang, the vice general manager of FAW Mazda Sales Company, proudly announced that the Mazda6 Riui would officially sign swimming champion Michael Phelps, who won eight golds at the Beijing Olympics, as their image endorser, the bold and extravagant move drew much attention. This was the first time a domestic car brand had hired an international sports superstar for endorsement, and also the first time Phelps endorsed a car brand globally.

What shocked everyone was that just over twenty days later, foreign media exploded with news of Phelps' marijuana scandal in Hunan recruitment advertisements. As criticism and disappointment towards this "flying fish" who once received hero-like treatment poured in, the domestically produced car Mazda6 Riui, which was dubbed the "land flying fish" but hadn't yet taken off, found itself in an awkward situation: Would the originally planned April launch date still go ahead? Should the originally planned advertisements featuring Phelps be replaced?

FOR Obama

When the sports hero from the distant United States, Phelps, was making waves in China's automotive market, another political hero from the same country, currently enjoying no less popularity, was also driving Chinese car dealers crazy.

After Obama officially swore in as the 44th President of the United States on January 20th, his "tank-level" Cadillac "Number One" began to receive extensive exposure at an extremely high rate. "Obama will endorse the younger Cadillac," "All secrets revealed about Obama's new ride," "Cadillac in Obama and Hillary's hearts"... In recent days, Zhejiang Cadillac dealers were planning to bring all stories of the new president and Cadillac into the showroom. The wall space dedicated to the "presidential zone," which stood two meters tall, was specially developed. As the manufacturer of the presidential limousine, Cadillac hoped to capitalize on the super-popularity of the first African-American president to gain more attention.

Mazda6 Riui caught in an awkward position due to Phelps

Cadillac capitalizes on Obama’s popularity

Leveraging celebrity effects to sell cars is no longer a novel marketing strategy. However, under the large economic downturn, how effective is it to spend big money inviting celebrities? Moreover, the unexpected negative news brought by wildly popular celebrities must be handled appropriately. These cases of opportunity accompanied by risk have made car manufacturers reconsider risk assessments and balance between investment and output.

Spending 20 million yuan to hire "Phelps" as an endorser

It is rumored that FAW Mazda spent 20 million RMB to hire "Phelps" as their image endorser. Yu Hongjiang, Vice General Manager of FAW Mazda Sales Company, when discussing the reasons for choosing Phelps to endorse the Mazda6 Riui, said: "Although the current market situation is not very good, FAW Mazda has maintained an upward trend in recent years, including the generally unfavorable year of 2008, we still achieved nearly 30% growth compared to 2007, and this 30% growth was achieved without any new product launches." Clearly, the manufacturer originally intended to leverage Phelps' popularity in 2009 to make a splash with the new car, but instead, they encountered a "bolt from the blue". Just as the news of Phelps smoking marijuana spread rapidly online along with the striking photo of "Phelps" holding a glass pipe smoking marijuana, the news of "reputation damage" and "decreased value" also left FAW Mazda scrambling to respond.

To this day, the factory has not made a clear statement externally. Industry insiders believe that FAW Mazda is likely observing the reaction of Chinese consumers.

Liu Yijun, General Manager of Zhejiang Ruida Automobile Co., Ltd., Hangzhou FAW Mazda exclusive dealership, stated that last year the Mazda6, listed among the top ten best-selling sedans in Zhejiang, has always been a popular mid-range car brand among Zhejiang consumers due to its excellent handling and safety, enjoying high market recognition. The new Mazda6 Riui, with its fashionable and personalized styling as well as upgraded safety performance, attracted a group of consumers visiting the showroom and inquiring since the beginning of the year. "From the perspective of the consumers we've interacted with, Phelps' scandal has not negatively impacted the launch of the new model. Instead, due to the high exposure rate, it has brought some promotional effect. Drug involvement is a personal behavior unrelated to the quality of the car itself. Since it was an unexpected event, consumers have shown understanding and sympathy towards FAW Mazda." Regarding actual advertising and sales strategies, Hangzhou dealerships have not received any notice from the factory.

Over 60% of netizens believe

Mazda should not continue cooperation with Phelps

For many automobile companies that did not invite stars as endorsers this year, the "marijuana scandal" has drawn significant attention, with more people regarding it as a warning. Ma Lin Kui, General Manager of Zhejiang Baixue Long Automobile Co., Ltd., believes: "Although cars are not as much benefited by star influence in sales as cosmetics and fashion products, most car buyers focus more on the practicality of cars, but car sales will definitely be affected by negative news about celebrities. Scandals are certainly something manufacturers do not wish to happen. It is even harder to say that scandals can have a promotional effect."

A recent survey conducted by China Auto Website showed that 56.8% of people believed that the "marijuana scandal" destroyed Phelps' image and expressed that they could not forgive such behavior; 68% agreed that the exposure of Phelps' drug use photos would affect the endorsed Mazda6 Riui; 66.2% believed that FAW Mazda should not continue cooperation with Phelps.

"FAW Mazda hiring Phelps cannot be considered a wrong choice, as unforeseen events can happen to anyone." Liu Ning, Marketing Director of Zhejiang Hecheng Audi Automobile Co., Ltd., believes that automobile brand promotion is a process of integrated communication, and celebrity endorsements are only one aspect. Manufacturers can mitigate or downplay the already existing negative impact through other channels.

Fortunately, the advertisement featuring Phelps endorsing the Mazda6 Riui has not been fully launched yet, giving the manufacturer time to adjust their strategy. Some industry insiders believe that after the scandal, FAW Mazda can achieve high-frequency exposure for the new car launch without spending a cent, leveraging Phelps' own influence. Others believe that FAW Mazda might be waiting for Phelps to participate in public welfare activities to restore his image and compensate for losses before adjusting their marketing strategy. Perhaps this situation could turn out to be a blessing in disguise.

Limited role of celebrity endorsements in boosting sales

There is no denying that FAW Mazda's decision to spend a large sum inviting top-tier celebrities to endorse under poor economic conditions has long been seen as a risky move, prompting more car manufacturers to begin evaluating costs and outputs in detail during lean market marketing.

Within the Guangqi Toyota brand family, the Camry (parameters/configuration library), which has never invited any celebrities for endorsement, has consistently ranked among the top three best-selling models in Zhejiang for many years, while the Yaris (parameters/configuration library), which invited popular singers Jay Chou and Karen Mok as endorsers, might attract only 10% of the fans of the invited celebrities, which would already make the car dealers happy. "The choice of celebrities mainly depends on the characteristics of the target customer group of the promoted car. Not everyone will buy into the celebrity's appeal. But using the celebrity's fame to increase the new car's visibility at the time of its launch has a certain effect, though driving sales involves the car's performance and quality. How much influence the celebrity has is hard to quantify," said Zhou Xinyan, manager of the marketing department of Zhejiang Guangtong Automobile.

In early 2007, actress Maggie Cheung obtained the latest Audi TT 3.2 quattro and became its endorser, and she was rated as the hottest endorser of 2007 on the internet. This successful endorsement indeed made many consumers obsessed with Maggie Cheung's temperament fall in love with the Audi TT. Even today, Maggie Cheung, with her enduring popularity and unique charm, remains the irreplaceable choice for Audi's brand image endorsement. Regarding this, Liu Ning believes that people's evaluation of celebrities endorsing cars cannot be overly short-sighted. "We choose celebrities whose temperament matches the product features. Their role is to enhance the brand image and gradually permeate into the sales field, which is a gradual process. Brand building also requires a phase," said Liu Ning.
