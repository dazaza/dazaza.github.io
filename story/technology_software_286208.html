Because at that time we were deeply aware that unity is strength, so everyone must unite in a new unit or group. Suddenly letting you drift out, establish a database called MirWorldDB, including your physical and mental sense of belonging, this will be quite uncomfortable. After the fear comes委屈——I originally loyally fought for the cause of the party and people, how am I the rightist against socialism? Of course, it doesn't make sense. And at the time, colleagues and friends who played well with you, once they hear you're a rightist, in their thinking, they'll think this person is really a rightist, and think ——originally he was a wolf in sheep's clothing. People close to me all say I'm a rightist, so I will self-reflect, where did I do wrong. One reflection leads to reflecting on the roots of one's origin, originally my badness was the influence of my family.

It wouldn't lead to hatred for the family, because the family represents specific people, representing parents, but you will dig into the ideological roots from your origin ——originally my family is an object that needs revolution, so I unconsciously oppose the revolution... It's all such a set of thought logic, so everything is sincere self-reflection.

After drifting and doubt, ultimately it is self-doubt, from self-doubt to self-denial, leading to sincere transformation. Coming to Fangmen, this is the key point to understand our generation of rightists from a ideological perspective. At the time, the pressure of public opinion, or rather the guiding pressure, political atmosphere pressure, made you feel burdened, thus sincerely believing oneself to be wrong, and therefore sincerely laboring for reform. You might not think that Huang Miaozi, Wu Zuguang, etc., went to Beidahuang with a sense of grievance. They truly determined to create a new life for themselves.

Labor reform? Labor reform means losing everything, even the right to see a doctor, it's a day when all human survival conditions are completely lost. Twenty years of labor reform, for me, there is only one life goal —— as long as you can survive, find a way to live. In fact, how to survive is the biggest theme for all Chinese people during those ten years. In the planned economy era, everything had to be supplied by coupons, the poverty of people's lives is evident. Not eating enough, hard for dull people to endure, smarter people have it a bit easier. For someone like me, the paste allocated could be eaten, naturally surviving. I described it in my novels ——going to beat the paste, then placing the clean shovel head at the furnace opening, stirring the feces flour into a paste-like flow, with a "sizzling" sound pouring a handful onto the scorching hot shovel. The Loess Plateau uses flat shovels, resembling a frying pan, the feces flour paste spreads evenly towards the edges, bubbles appear momentarily before disappearing, within less than a minute it fries into a pancake.

Nowadays, I never eat fruit, because I ate too much fruits and vegetables back then. There wasn't a distinction between good and bad taste, anything edible was fine, raw cucumbers, vegetable leaves, unripe carrots... all used as cold dishes; ...we even ate rats, I couldn't eat them, probably due to some education restrictions, thinking it's too dirty.

During the labor reform, I escaped once, I'm just a restless person, but upon coming out, I found the vast countryside worse than the labor reform farm, for the sake of food for survival, I returned. Later, I saw research reports from the Chinese Academy of Sciences saying ——before the 1978 reform and opening up, 70% of China's resources were wasted, waste meant ineffective labor, and destructive labor.

In fact, it was then that I truly formed my faith in Marxism, I became a fundamental Marxist. My constant reading of "Capital" told me that what China practiced at the time was not Marxism, but exactly the military communism and feudal communism criticized in "The Declaration".

Now I am a firm Marxist, I know China inevitably has to take the path of market economy. Marxism said we must go through such a historical stage to greatly improve productivity and material wealth. This period we are in now requires a long span of 50 to 100 years. Market economy is our inevitable path. Back then wanting to step into communism all at once, how possible, that's the military communism Marx talked about.

From 1959 to 1961, these three years we call the "three difficult years", because of this difficult period, there was the 1962 conference of seven thousand people, at this conference the eight-character guideline of "adjustment, consolidation, enrichment, improvement" was formulated. At the time, China had a brief production recovery period, a spring for literature and art, similar to "Notes from Three Villages", the movie "Early Spring in February", etc., which emerged during this time.
