Ranking of Personality by Blood Type

The people who calm down most easily are:

1. O type: Impatient, small things can irritate them. They have a combative side in their nature and may explode with the excuse of "I just want to vent."

2. B type: They seem to have a good temper, but in fact, they are very straightforward. When they lose their temper, their usual sensibility disappears instantly, and people around them often don't know why they're angry.

3. AB type: The usually calm AB types rarely get upset. However, when they do, it's quite frightening because they control their emotions well, and anger usually subsides quickly.

4. A type: The patient A types tend to explode only when they truly can't hold it in anymore. So once they get angry, the consequences are often unimaginable.

The people who observe others' feelings least are:

1. B type: Free-spirited B types, stubborn and self-centered, say what comes to mind without considering others' feelings.

2. O type: When focused on something interesting, they forget about everything else around them.

3. A type: The A types, who care deeply about others' opinions, cannot tolerate criticism and will try hard to understand the situation around them to avoid mistakes.

4. AB type: With sharp minds, they can quickly analyze and adapt to situations, making appropriate responses.

The people who worry the most are:

1. A type: A types worry about everything, big or small. Even if others assure them repeatedly that there is no problem, they still worry instinctively about how things are progressing.

2. O type: Although they appear free-spirited, they worry unexpectedly much. But they also have strong action ability; worrying leads them to take actions to eliminate concerns actively.

3. AB type: As individualists, AB types think others' affairs should be left alone. Even if they show concern, it won't last long.

4. B type: Basically, they don't know the meaning of worry. Even if everyone else is extremely worried, they remain carefree, saying things like "There's definitely no problem."

The people who listen to advice least are:

1. B type: No matter how much you emphasize that it's for their own good, or even if a boss yells at them, they stick to their own worldview and don't value others' opinions.

2. O type: O types dislike listening to long speeches. If the advice lacks substantial content, they might not know what to do, even interrupting the conversation and irritating the other party.

3. A type: A types humbly listen to others' opinions. Even if they don't like it inside, they won't show it. However, their stubborn personalities mean they rarely follow advice.

4. AB type: For useful content, they gladly accept it, carefully analyzing which parts suit them, making the other party feel they've done something beneficial.

The people who are best at organizing are:

1. A type: Extremely fond of cleanliness, tidying up has become a habit. After using something, they unconsciously return it to its original place.

2. AB type: Wanting a high-quality life, they pay attention to even furniture arrangements, enjoying simple and plain lives. They immediately discard unused items without regret.

3. O type: Believing cleaning and organizing should be done periodically, they stuff all small items into drawers, not wanting others to see them cluttered.

4. B type: In short, they are not good at housekeeping. Free-spirited B types think life continues without cleaning and never put effort into this aspect.

The people who fall into unrequited love for the longest time are:

1. A type: A types are both fearful and passive in love. They silently like someone for a long time but dare not act on it.

2. AB type: AB types use various methods, direct or indirect, to overcome unrequited love. Their period of unrequited love usually doesn't exceed half a year.

3. B type: Curious and seeking new experiences, they don't hold onto feelings for long. Even if it starts as unrequited love, they take action to move it forward.

4. O type: Impatient O types rich in action won't tolerate long-term unrequited love, always hoping for progress.

The people with the most opportunities to meet others are:

1. AB type: Having many friends, they can meet different people in various settings. Understanding how to seize opportunities, they continuously make new friends.

2. O type: Action-oriented, they have many chances to make friends. Cheerful O types are everyone's joy and easily attract the opposite sex.

3. A type: Needing love at any time, they can't stand being single and will seek new friends. However, A types are shy and find it hard to be close to strangers.

4. B type: Seemingly active, but actually passive, they wait for others to approach or rely on introductions from friends.

The people who can remain friends after a breakup are:

1. AB type: Not too attached to past friends, they switch emotions naturally to friendship after breaking up.

2. A type: Even after breaking up, they can't forget the other person. If they hope for reconciliation, temporarily being friends might help.

3. B type: If they were dumped, they don't want to see the other person again. However, if they initiated the breakup, they might continue the friendship if the other person wishes so.

4. O type: Emotionally rich and clear about likes and dislikes, O types, after enduring so much pain to break up, wouldn't consider becoming friends again.

The people who are easily approached by strangers are:

1. B type: Drawing attention, friendly, they radiate a magnetic field that makes others approach them unconsciously, making them more attractive than other blood types.

2. AB type: During conversations, they regain confidence and sometimes intentionally give the other party a chance, increasing the likelihood of being approached.

3. A type: Although not easily compromising with the opposite sex, their grooming and appearance are outstanding, so they are still approached.

4. O type: Looking unapproachable, they create pressure for those who might approach them. If someone does, they might get a cold stare, so there's basically no result.

The people who express themselves boldly are:

1. O type: Waiting passively isn't their style. They prefer taking the initiative to express themselves, regardless of who the other person is, following their own thoughts.

2. A type: Lacking courage to approach, they still hesitate before finally mustering the courage to confess.

3. B type: Very popular, they don't need to think hard about how to express themselves. The probability of expressing themselves or being expressed to is about equal.

4. AB type: Quite traditional in this aspect, even if they like someone, they won't express it directly but instead try to get the other person to say it first.

The people who make the fewest mistakes at work are:

1. AB type: Strong sense of balance, broad vision, they objectively approach problems from multiple angles, handling difficult tasks flexibly and competently.

2. O type: Not as capable as AB types, but with strong physical stamina and concentration, they often complete work satisfactorily.

3. B type: They do make mistakes, but solve problems in unique ways due to their instinctive and flexible nature.

4. A type: Not understanding work methods and bringing personal factors into work, even experienced ones may make mistakes.

The people who prioritize work over love are:

1. O type: Typical career-first individuals, they usually schedule work first and then check if there's time for dates. If questioned by their partner, they may get angry.

2. A type: Their serious nature doesn't allow neglecting work, so they must complete their duties perfectly before considering dating.

3. AB type: Love is more important than work. If work causes issues in love, they may even consider changing jobs unless absolutely necessary.

4. B type: Compared to the company, their feelings always come first, so naturally, love outweighs work.
