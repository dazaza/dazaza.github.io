by king5t11 on 2012-03-01 20:21:51

【Wine Culture [http://www.hongjiul.com]】It can be said that on the road to making wine, my spouse and I have struggled all the way. Last year, we had the experience of failure (the sugar added later turned the wine into vinegar, which wasn't a waste either). During this year, we asked some friends and looked up a lot of information online. A few days ago, we tried making a small amount, so we also summarized a set of our own experiences.

However, if you ask me if this method will definitely work? I wouldn't dare to say that, but it won't be wasted. If it doesn't turn out as wine, it will become vinegar.

36 kilograms of Kyoho grapes, fully ripe, not a single one was slightly green.

The showerhead is set to the smallest hole and maximum water flow, producing a powerful stream of water that can wash off the dust from the grapes. Some people suggest soaking them in salt water for about ten minutes. If you're making a small amount, you can do this. Since I have too much, I just rinsed them directly.

After drying, it was already dark. The grapes were placed into the container, then uniformly crushed. You can crush while placing them, but I think this way is faster. Mainly because last year when we made it, Old Mr. Li was responsible for crushing them. Watching him crush with satisfaction, this year I also wanted to try. At the beginning, it was indeed very satisfying, using a lot of strength. He looked at me and said, "This fat guy is good, huh? Very strong."

After crushing, add rock sugar. For 36 kilograms of grapes, prepare 11 kilograms of rock sugar. Generally, it's a ratio of 10 parts grapes to 3 parts rock sugar.

When sealing, it's best to place two layers of gauze, otherwise after fermentation, there will be a layer of white film on top. I bought medical gauze directly from the pharmacy, with two pieces inside. The size is also appropriate.

&；nbsp；

Two layers of gauze, two layers of plastic bags, tied well. The preliminary work is done, now we wait.

Old Mr. Li said, "Let's make it again next year." Then he said himself, "Oh, that's right, I said the same thing last year." So we sighed, saying that we should make appropriate things at different times, so life feels more worthwhile. Just like when we were kids, eating mooncakes during the Mid-Autumn Festival, eating zongzi during the Dragon Boat Festival, and eating tangyuan during the Lantern Festival, each festival was something to look forward to. Now that life is better, these traditional foods can be eaten anytime, so people often lose their anticipation, leading to a lack of interest in festivals. Perhaps when we lament the changes of today compared to yesterday, can we also consider these matters from another perspective? Like how every year, only during this period can we buy seasonal grapes, and the two of us can make sweet red wine together...
