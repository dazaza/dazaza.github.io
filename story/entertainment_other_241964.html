Emotional diary: Accompany you through the last two hundred meters

by lwf225 on 2009-09-23 17:20:15

A man and a woman were returning from delivering goods to a distant place. They sat in the trailer of an extended semi-truck, with the man focused on driving in the driver's seat, while the woman dozed off on a nearby seat.

Suddenly, the man nudged the woman and said, "Wake up!" The woman opened her eyes, confused as she looked at the man. The man asked, "Do you remember when we came here and saw a very deep ditch by the side of the road?" The woman, still half-asleep, asked, "Where?" The man replied, "It's just ahead, about six hundred meters away. When we get there, I'll shout 'jump,' and you jump down. Remember, do it quickly, jump into the ditch, and don't worry about anything else!"

Only then did the woman realize something was wrong.

She noticed that the man was drenched in sweat, anxious and restless. He had already pressed the brakes to the bottom, but the truck was still rushing forward madly. It was a long and steep downhill road, and the truck was gaining terrifying acceleration, like a stone falling into an abyss. This was an abandoned highway, and they had traveled this road many times before. They were familiar with the road and everything around it. They knew that eight hundred meters ahead, there was a rural market. On market days, the market would always be crowded with many nearby residents. And they also knew that today happened to be a market day. This meant that they couldn't drive carefully into the market and continue as they had done before. There were no alternative roads, and on both sides of the narrow highway were cliff-like steep walls. So their truck could only rush towards those unsuspecting people. The man kept honking the horn, but the noise in the market was too loud, and no one heard, paid attention, or cared.

The man continuously released the brake, then stepped hard on it again, releasing it again, and stepping on it again. But it was useless; the brakes had completely failed.

They knew that two hundred meters away from the market, there was a recessed gap in the mountain wall on one side of the road. That gap was on the driver's side. If the truck continued at its current speed, then ramming the truck into that gap might prevent them from colliding with innocent villagers in the market. However, such an outcome would inevitably result in the destruction of the car and the death of both of them.

The woman tightly grabbed the man's hand. The man said, "We're almost at that ditch. When I say 'jump,' you jump." The woman asked, "What about you?" The man said, "I'll jump too." After speaking, the man opened one side of the car door and opened the other side for the woman.

They both saw the ditch, but the ditch was on the woman's side, seemingly rushing towards them. The man shouted, "One, two, three, jump!" Then the car rushed past.

Neither of them jumped!!!!!

The man became anxious and asked, "Why didn't you jump?"

The man clenched his fists in despair and smashed the steering wheel. His face was distorted with anger and sadness.

The truck continued to rush forward...

When the man decided to crash into the gap, he wanted to try one last time. At that moment, he suddenly heard the sound of the car tires rubbing against the ground. Overjoyed, the man slammed on the brakes again and never let go.

The truck stopped, stopping just a step away from the crowd. The man was drenched in sweat, taking deep breaths, while the woman hugged him and sobbed uncontrollably.

The man embraced the woman and said, "Do you know how foolish you were? If we had crashed, we would have surely died!"

Wiping her tears, the woman said, "I know. So I couldn't jump. I had to stay with you and finish the last two hundred meters of our lives together!"
