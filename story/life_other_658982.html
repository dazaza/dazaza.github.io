Confession of the Soul

I hate pain, but I never refuse it. I hate loneliness, but I never reject being alone. I hate hypocrisy, yet I don't only understand truthfulness. I hate melancholy, but I don't only know happiness.

I want to give up, but I can't. I want to possess, but I can't have. I want to forget, but I will not forget. I want to party, but I won't indulge.

Some say, to be a person, one needs a bit of spirit. Others say, to be a person, one needs a sense of responsibility. Some others say, to be a person, one must learn to let go and keep. And still others say, God is fair to everyone...

All these are excuses that cannot stand on their own. When did we start becoming so hypocritical?

Freud believed that when we are most at a loss, we can still find answers in the riddles of our dreams, but he didn't realize that illusions do not foresee reality; reality is merely where we expect illusions to exist.

Life is about折腾 (struggling/endeavoring). I don't know which least cultured person said the most profound sentence.

Zhuangzi said life should have persistence, survival should be adaptable. But I see that all those who claim to live freely are actually competing to put on shackles they cannot escape from. When survival becomes the sole meaning of life, life itself becomes the most hypocritical excuse.

With such a good night, just sleeping would be a real waste. So some people drink excessively, letting alcohol freely penetrate the blood-brain barrier to destroy brain cells, just to numb themselves. Others wear a skin they themselves don't recognize, wandering through all possible venues within the fortress of free speech, talking nonsense with abandon, just to avoid themselves. Still others change their avatars constantly, roaming like ghosts in the virtual world, indulging recklessly, debasing themselves. Even more unknown are people like me, working anxiously during the day, and staying quiet as mice passing through streets on New Year's Eve in 2008 when night falls.

Most people who've seen Disney's classic cartoon "Tom and Jerry" probably all like the smart cat that always wins, bringing us endless laughter. Rats are the spirits of the night. Now, worldly people have put down their bricks, stools, weapons, afraid to scare off this suddenly popular auspicious symbol.

All is silent, long live the rats. I am willing to be a rat at night.

Today's society is full of restlessness and confusion. People work hard, beyond basic needs, pursuing various material desires. When materials can enrich life, or even lead to extravagance, the heart often withers. Isn't it true? In real life, many people, after satisfying their physical appetites, instead lock away their innate intelligence, feeling generally empty, anxious, suffering greatly, unable to express, unable to vent, leading to spiritual decadence. This results in excessive luxury, wasting time, living drunk and dreaming dead, treating life as a game. Day after day, year after year, daily life moves forward passively and numbly without conscious awareness, lacking true personal freedom. Because standards of good and evil, right and wrong, are socially determined. So people today often feel that although they possess the rich material life dreamed of by ancestors, they also lose the most precious spiritual self. This is the tragedy of modern humanity.

Bacon said: "Books are the spiritual legacy of the previous generation, a ship of thought navigating the waves of the times, carefully delivering precious spiritual wealth to generation after generation." Therefore, only in the quiet of the night, spreading open a book, studying diligently, conversing with history, exchanging with ancient sages, breathing with nature, can we expand our hearts, inspire our intelligence, guide us into a freer, more transcendent world. I always believe that only good books can place life at its true height - the high ground of truth, goodness, and beauty.

The darkness outside my window has swallowed my room. With the lights off, the only brightness comes from the still operating computer screen and the blue glow of the mouse by my hand. I should prefer recording my feelings with real pen and paper, enjoying the sensation of my little finger and ring finger gliding over the page. But the lit lamp reminds me of all sorts of daytime affairs, and the pen replays everything routine in my mind. So, I can only choose, as now, to use this faint light, leaving marks with both hands.

What illuminates the dark isn't necessarily a lamp. What records thoughts isn't necessarily a pen. What plays music isn't necessarily an instrument. What interprets happiness isn't necessarily a smile.

Even the brightest lamps have darkness they cannot drive away. Even the smoothest pens have moods they cannot sketch. Even the greatest Chopin cannot play my sorrow. Even the most intoxicating happiness is merely a moment of smiling while crying.

So, just like when God gets bored, He floods the entire world, when we truly have no way to escape the things that bind us, one method is to extinguish the light.

Turn off all the lights, dissolve yourself in the night, see nothing of yourself, see nothing around you. The flowing darkness washes away the grime attached to our minds, not removing them, but moving them to places we ourselves hardly notice, then we gain the courage to continue walking. The journey is long, but humans cannot live forever. Those wounds sealed away by different means will eventually exceed their limits, bursting irreversibly, whether physically or spiritually.

The Buddha says, one flower is a world, one tree is a Bodhi. Almost every instant sees the destruction of a world. These instants strung together form time. We have always tried to avoid what we call the end, not realizing that in fact, we have always lived within the end.

This is our fate, our Zen.
