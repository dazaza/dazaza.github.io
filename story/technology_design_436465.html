However, historically, the weather has been abnormal across most regions of the country, especially in the northern areas where it tends to be colder. In contrast, the southern regions do not show such significant changes. If the seasonal period arrives but the corresponding weather does not, it seems even more necessary for people to enhance their health preservation methods to adapt to the changing climate.

1. Characteristics of the Spring Equinox:

The "Spring Equinox" occurs annually on March 21st or 20th. It is the fourth of the twenty-four solar terms, with almost equal day and night durations. According to the "Seventy-Two Hou Collection," it states: "Fen refers to balance, occurring at the midpoint of the ninetieth day, hence called Fen." Records in the "Yin Yang Exits High and Low" chapter of the "Luxuriant Dew of the Spring and Autumn Annals" state: "The Spring Equinox represents the balance of Yin and Yang, thus day and night are equal and cold and heat are balanced." These explanations indicate that after the Spring Equinox, the weather is in a state of relative balance between Yin and Yang, as well as day and night. Temperatures should continue to rise, and strong convective weather increases. Therefore, attention should be paid to preventing lightning and strong convective weather disasters during this season. The weather in the north fluctuates greatly, with temperature differences within a week ranging from 6 to 10 degrees Celsius, illustrating the climatic characteristics of the "Spring Equinox." A folk proverb says: "During the Spring and Autumn Equinoxes, health preservation is important, day and night are equally divided; after eating the Spring Equinox meal, each day grows longer by a line."

2. Health Preservation During the Spring Equinox:

The Spring Equinox divides day and night, as well as cold and heat. People should focus on maintaining the balance of Yin and Yang in their bodies through mental, dietary, and living habits, as well as the use of medication. From the Beginning of Spring to the Qingming Festival, it is the budding period for plants. Human blood is also at its peak during this time, and hormone levels are relatively high, making it easy to develop non-infectious diseases such as hypertension, large fluctuations in blood pressure, menstrual disorders, and allergic diseases. Therefore, dietary adjustment during this season is very important. It should be chosen based on individual circumstances, with the general principle being to avoid overly hot or cold diets and maintain a balance between cold and heat. For example, when eating cold foods, seafood like fish and shrimp can be paired with warming ingredients like scallions, ginger, and alcohol to disperse the cold. When consuming yang-enhancing foods like leeks and garlic, they can be paired with yin-nourishing ingredients like eggs to achieve a balance of Yin and Yang. Regular routines should be maintained, keeping a cheerful mood. Appropriate exercise should be kept up, along with regular sleep and meals, to achieve a balance of Yin and Yang. Suitable dishes for the Spring Equinox include White-Braised Eel, Du Zhong Kidney, and Garlic-Braised Eggplant, which have functions of replenishing deficiencies, lowering blood pressure, and cooling the blood to stop bleeding. During this season, one should avoid eating extremely cold or hot foods, such as excessive consumption of seafood products and mutton or beef without paying attention to proper cooking combinations.

In summary, during the Spring Equinox, day and night are of equal length, and cold and heat are balanced. People's lives should follow the principles of diet, living habits, and temperature regulation, maintaining a balance of Yin and Yang.
