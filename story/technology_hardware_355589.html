Abstract: Foreign trade development letters are very important for expanding foreign trade business volume and developing new customers. They extend an olive branch hoping to establish a friendly cooperative relationship, which may bring in a continuous flow of money. 1. What is a Foreign Trade Development Letter?

Simply put, a development letter is the first email or letter you write to a potential customer. A foreign trade development letter is the first letter you write to your potential customer with the aim of developing this potential customer, establishing business cooperation, gaining orders, and expanding business. The foreign trade development letter extends an olive branch hoping to establish a friendly cooperative relationship, which may bring in a continuous flow of money. What is sent out is hope, and what is expected is a reply. The most worthy of study is the development letter delivered via email, which is a fast, cost-effective, comprehensive, and less likely to cause misunderstandings method of effective communication.

Before analyzing and discussing the factors affecting the reply rate of foreign trade development letters, let's talk about "love letters" that you are familiar with and often talk about, because there are many similarities between foreign trade development letters and the first love letter.

Let's still start with love letters. Who would you write your love letter to?

Now talking about foreign trade development letters, if the other party can be judged as your potential customer, then you must have some understanding of them through certain channels. If you have no understanding at all, then it's a typical case of Person B.

Of course, this understanding comes at a cost, mainly in terms of time and energy, as well as channel investment costs. To obtain benefits based on cost input, you need to strive to improve the reply rate of your development letters. Since there are costs involved, they must be controlled to pursue maximum profit, so it is necessary to mention the channels.

The main channels through which we foreign trade professionals understand our customers include: exhibitions, paid B2B websites, free B2B websites, "posting product information and letting customers find you actively", search engines, government agencies, yellow pages, foreign-related BBS or communities, the website of the other company...

In a buyer's market, there is demand, purchase intention, market research, suitable products, quality, price, service, and it is easier to make a deal. To evaluate the quality of potential customers, one should analyze factors such as whether the product is suitable, purchase intention, quality, price, service, combined with possible transaction volume and potential profit size.

Generally speaking, potential customers obtained from exhibitions have the highest quality. They already have some understanding of your products, and their channel investment is also quite substantial, indicating strong purchase intention and large quantities, making them key targets for development.

Paid B2B websites and free B2B websites generally provide the latest purchasing information, but each case needs specific analysis and comprehensive evaluation. Based on the experience of the author and many foreign trade netizens and friends using these websites, purchasing information on B2B websites should be treated cautiously. Some are fraudulent, some are market research, some are advertising, of course, some are genuinely interested in doing business. Therefore, it is necessary to combine your own experience, products, and the regions covered by the B2B website, the country and region of the purchaser, and the details provided in the purchasing information for analysis. Of course, your first concern should still be whether the product is suitable. If it is, then take a closer look.

"Posting product information and letting customers find you actively", this is actually hard to talk about. Where have you posted your information? How do you introduce your company or product in the information? Because when the other party contacts you, you may know nothing about them. Therefore, I advocate taking the initiative, knowing yourself and your enemy leads to victory in a hundred battles.

Government agencies, company directories, the customer information collected here may not be detailed enough, possibly having three "non-existents": no contact person's name, no email, no product subdivision. Therefore, it is best to combine other network tools for deeper understanding.

3.2 How to Send Foreign Trade Development Letters?

When we write love letters, we work hard to write them, of course hoping that "the person in our heart" will open and read them carefully. Some small tricks I used in the past include: 1. Getting the close friend of the person you admire to pass it on, hehe, how to win them over, I have my own tricks, Buddha said: wonderful, cannot be expressed in words; Laozi said: the way that can be told is not the eternal way. 2. Observing closely the living habits of the person you admire, placing it in items they frequently use rather than outside, avoiding others opening it, otherwise it would not only embarrass yourself but also involve that person. 3. Making the medium beautiful, such as using colorful scented stationery...

Foreign trade development letters are the same. The purpose of writing them is of course hoping that potential customers can receive and open them. This requires you to be familiar with and use the transmission methods well. 1. Let old friends or old customers recommend, note, don't cause "jealousy" phenomena, once the vinegar jar is overturned, you will be in big trouble, and may even lose old friends or old customers. Therefore, it is essential to ensure that establishing a new friendly cooperative relationship with potential customers will not cause harmful impact on existing old friends or old customers, but instead bring "benefits". What kind of "benefits"? Hehe, think more, and try to "win them over". 2. Observe and understand potential customers, send to places they frequently pay attention to. 3. Make the medium beautiful. For the most commonly used means of transmitting foreign trade development letters, which is email, a good sending email address and a carefully considered email title are crucial for potential customers to open your email. 3.3 How to Write Foreign Trade Development Letters?

By now, if your love letter has been opened, congratulations, you have taken the first step of a long journey! But if your letter is full of misspellings, coarse language, and empty words, oh! There is no hope. Remember there is a saying "tasteless words, detestable appearance", be sure not to let "the person in your heart" classify your love letter under such evaluation.

For foreign trade development letters, it is the same. When your letter is opened, your intentions can be understood:

Greeting: Each person likes hearing their name the most, so it's best not to use "Dear Sir/Madam" if possible, try to use the recipient's name; try not to let the customer see at a glance that you are mass mailing, putting the customer's email among a bunch of recipient addresses.

Content: It should be concise yet meaningful; both format and tone should be appropriate; since the writing of the content of the development letter varies depending on the understanding, analysis, and evaluation of potential customers, and there are many articles summarizing the methods, this article will not elaborate further.
