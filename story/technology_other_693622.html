Introduction to Fly Ash, Its Characteristics, and Applications

by hx515317 on 2012-03-06 17:06:40

Fly ash, also known as pulverized fuel ash, is the dust collected from the chimneys of thermal power stations. It is a mixed material with pozzolanic properties. Its main components are oxides of silicon, aluminum, iron, calcium, and magnesium, which possess potential chemical activity. This means that fly ash alone does not exhibit hydraulic activity when mixed with water. However, under certain conditions, it can react with water to form gel-like substances similar to cement gel, which have a certain degree of strength.

Due to the fine nature of coal powder and its transformation into glass beads during high-temperature processes, fly ash particles are mostly spherical. Fine ash captured from the flue gas after coal combustion is called fly ash, which is the primary solid waste discharged by coal-fired power plants.

Fly Ash Combustion Process

Coal powder burns in a suspended state within the furnace.

Most combustible materials in the coal can be completely burned inside the furnace, while the non-combustible components (mainly ash) are largely mixed in the high-temperature flue gas. These non-combustible components partially melt due to high temperatures and, because of surface tension, form numerous small spherical particles.

Under the suction effect of the induced draft fan at the rear of the boiler, the flue gas containing large amounts of ash flows towards the furnace tail. As the temperature of the flue gas decreases, some of the molten fine particles undergo rapid cooling to form glassy states, thereby possessing high potential activity. Before the induced draft fan discharges the flue gas into the atmosphere, these small spherical particles pass through a dust collector where they are separated and collected as fly ash.

Uses of Fly Ash

Fly ash is one of the industrial waste residues with the largest current discharge volumes in China. At present, the annual slag discharge in our country has reached 30 million tons. With the development of the electric power industry, the amount of fly ash discharged from coal-fired power plants increases year by year. If large amounts of fly ash are left untreated, they will cause dust pollution in the atmosphere; if discharged into water systems, they can lead to river siltation, and the toxic chemicals contained within can pose hazards to humans and living organisms. Therefore, the treatment and utilization of fly ash have drawn widespread attention.

Uses of Fly Ash:

National Standard Level One: The modern concrete technology using high-quality fly ash and high-efficiency water reducers for producing high-grade concrete is rapidly developing nationwide.

National Standard Level Two: High-quality fly ash is especially suitable for preparing pumped concrete, mass concrete, anti-seepage structural concrete, sulfate-resistant concrete, soft water erosion-resistant concrete, underground and underwater engineering concrete, grouting concrete, and roller-compacted concrete.

National Standard Level Three: Fly ash concrete has advantages such as good workability, strong pumpability, improved finishability, enhanced impact resistance, and increased frost resistance.

Advantages of Using Fly Ash:

Adding fly ash in concrete saves a large amount of cement and fine aggregates; reduces water consumption; improves the workability of concrete mixtures; enhances the pumpability of concrete; reduces concrete creep; lowers hydration heat and thermal expansion; improves the impermeability of concrete; and enhances the finishability of concrete.
