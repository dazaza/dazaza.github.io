The Battle for Fabregas Begins: Real Madrid Prepares a 50 Million Euro Check

As is widely known, Real Madrid has shown great interest in Arsenal's midfield star Cesc Fabregas. The question remains, when will they formally submit an offer to the Gunners? According to the latest report from Marca, Florentino Perez has already prepared a 50 million euro check, launching the battle for Fabregas six months ahead of Barcelona.

After testing their new-season lineup in both La Liga and the Champions League, it became evident that apart from some defensive vulnerabilities, Real Madrid also needs improvement in midfield. Specifically, the combination of Kaka and Xabi Alonso has yet to achieve the desired synergy. To address this issue, Real Madrid’s sports department spent several months searching for reinforcements in midfield.

Liverpool's Steven Gerrard was once on Madrid's radar but, at 29 years old, he appears "too old" compared to younger stars like Kaka and Cristiano Ronaldo, especially given his injury-plagued season, which led to him being removed from consideration. French midfielder Yoann Gourcuff was another option, but his inconsistent performances at both club and international levels have raised concerns. Moreover, after reviewing talents from Germany, Brazil, and Argentina, Real Madrid concluded that aside from Fabregas, it would be difficult to find a more mature and versatile midfielder.

Based on these considerations, Real Madrid has resolved to sign Fabregas next summer. Fabregas has decided to leave Arsenal on June 30th, 2011, with Arsene Wenger's approval. Additionally, Arsenal has set an initial price tag of no less than 50 million euros for the Spanish player.

Marca suggests that under the competition between Real Madrid and Barcelona, 50 million euros might just be the starting point for Fabregas' transfer fee. However, if Real Madrid can reach an agreement with the player first, this could potentially be his final price.

Barcelona is currently playing the emotional card, trying to lure Fabregas back to his hometown club. While Barcelona has its advantages, it also faces certain disadvantages. The Blaugrana already boasts a strong midfield comprising Busquets, Xavi, Iniesta, and Keita, meaning there may not be immediate opportunities for Fabregas upon joining. Furthermore, former Barcelona vice-president Sandro Rosell's efforts to bring Fabregas back are largely driven by his presidential campaign aspirations, factors that Fabregas must consider carefully.

On the other hand, if Fabregas were to join Real Madrid, the situation would differ significantly. Currently, Real Madrid only has two holding midfielders in Xabi Alonso and Lassana Diarra. If Fabregas joins, Madrid could offer him a guaranteed starting position. Although there could be positional conflicts with Kaka, these can be resolved through tactical adjustments and role changes.

In conclusion, Real Madrid has made up its mind to acquire Fabregas. Now it all comes down to the player's decision. Marca indicates that all signs point to Real Madrid officially contacting Arsenal in January 2011 to launch a full-scale bid for Fabregas, making a decisive move against Barcelona in this high-stakes competition.
