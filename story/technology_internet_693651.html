Nostalgia...

by joyce8899 on 2012-03-06 17:18:02

Dear reader, allow me to translate the following passage with a poetic tone while staying true to its original meaning:

To this day, my stubbornness was merely for waiting for your fleeting appearance, those sleepless days were just to witness your beautiful face, and that quiet wait was only to see how the past disappeared after the rain. —— Preface

Why does melancholy always seep through my words? Why do the romances in my writings always end tragically? Why do the protagonists in my stories have the same ending in real life? I don't know what kind of feeling this is, I only know that my writing is always filled with sadness. Perhaps I've grown accustomed to recording life's sorrowful events with sad words, and the happy days around me are always unrelated to me. Even if there are happy days, they are always brief. I've never thought about recording these fleeting years in words.

The unbearable yesterday hides beneath the clear sky, concealing our painful parting. The sky over Zunyi is also clear, as if our past has left no trace. The happy times of yesterday have been exiled, constantly flowing away, and then disappearing with time. Now we are no longer young, the memories of the years only exist in an illusory dream. But in reality, we have passed the age of dreaming, even if we have far-fetched ideas, they can only be buried in our hearts.

Youth easily fades, gently knocking on our hearts in the memory of the years, then playing out endless joys and sorrows, and finally leaving unwillingly.

The oaths and lies of the past have dried up with the passing years. To this day, I have gone through the hardest times of my college years, cold and lonely, pitifully miserable, turning my daily sorrowful words into memories of the past. And my sorrow is all intertwined with the memories of the years.

I didn't cherish happy moments well, I never thought we would end this way. I always assumed such days were bound to exist, I never knew how to cherish them until they ended, and I realized my regret. These times will never come back. Now I deeply regret it, but unfortunately, everything has become wind, and the past has scattered with the wind.

Perhaps there is too much sorrow in life, too much sadness that we cannot remember the happy times, always treating each day with an indifferent attitude. In the end, the outcome is that our future days are full of sadness, always overshadowed by gloomy clouds.

It's not because of loneliness that I think of you, but because of thinking of you that I feel lonely. For three months, lonely days have haunted me in this unfamiliar city, becoming my nightmare, entwining into a sorrowful past. Perhaps because the past was too sad, I hope to live quietly, and because of tranquility, I fell in love with words, liking to spend 18 hours out of 24 alone in the dormitory. Quietly reading a book, brewing a cup of tea letting the aroma swirl, or in the afternoon, liking to quietly hold a book by the Clearwater Lake silently appreciating, or finding an uninhabited corner letting loneliness surround me.

Even living like this, even living in this way, the sadness buried in my heart hasn't diminished, but rather become stronger. Perhaps as people say, lonely people remain lonely no matter how they spend their time. Then now I can describe myself with this sentence: sorrowful people can't escape the torment of sorrow, perhaps it really describes people like me!

Now it's the time when thousands of lights shine brightly. The night has begun to envelop the earth, the neon lights in the streets are flashing continuously. I don't know where I should be, standing alone in this unfamiliar street. The brightness of the neon lights exaggerates my loneliness to the extreme, magnifying my sadness infinitely, allowing tears to invade at once.

In this world, the past is like clouds, once they pass, they are no longer the same. Today, I can only use sorrowful words to continue our story, turning my reckless youth into a sealed memory of growth.

I couldn't capture your beauty, only because you were like a fleeting flower in my life, rushing away. A meteor streaked across the sky, I didn't have time to make a wish before it vanished. Memories can only be hidden deep in my heart, and you don't know what kind of mood I am in now.

Perhaps many years later, we will be strangers, perhaps many years later we will forget our wonderful years, perhaps many years later we will forget our sadness, none of this matters anymore. Perhaps the beautiful past can only be cherished in memory, unable to be replayed.

Do you still remember? We leaned on each other at the beach, silently making wishes together, carving our beautiful wishes at the edge of the sea. Hoping to hold your hand and grow old together, but all this was shattered by my departure, and now I have no way to understand you.

The past is always the past, because it was too beautifully tragic, it makes people nostalgic, because it was too sad, it makes people sorrowful. Because of nostalgia, so we promise that when time turns our hair white, when our teeth are all gone, will we lie on wicker chairs and silently reminisce about our youthful past? Silently reminiscing about our happy times? Maybe when I grow old, my memory will have faded, maybe I can no longer move my frail body to write down the memories of the years, imprinting your memory stroke by stroke on A4 paper, but now I am still indulging in the sweetness of our past times, even though it's in the past, even though it's full of sorrow, I haven't stopped. Longing is like a demon, binding my thoughts tightly to memories related to you.

I always cover my sadness with a smile, numbing my thoughts with words, collecting sorrowful clouds from the sky in a bag, not letting them increase with time. When I open the bag, I find it empty, thinking my pain has completely disappeared, but when I look up, my sky is full of sadness, pressing down on me so I can't breathe.

I always like to use my eyes to record mottled colors, like to use my eyes to preserve beautiful scenes, always looking into each other's eyes, in the moment of parting, eyes falling with infinite sadness. The past is like clouds, when the last tear falls, I quietly put you in my worldly memory, and you delete me from your memory.

I plant a forest for you in the desert of the world, using your tears to draw a beautiful Lop Nur, supporting green oases in my small world, from then on filling my heart completely with you.

We are accustomed to the ups and downs of the mundane world, used to seeking survival gaps in the secular world. Sometimes, like silkworms, we wrap ourselves in thick cocoons, confining ourselves in the love and hatred of the world, unable to extricate ourselves, unable to be free, only able to bind ourselves like silkworms.

Whose silhouette does the small flower shadow intoxicate? Whose sorrow does the small flower dry? Butterflies chase flowers, flowers chase butterflies, this is an unchanging rule since ancient times, but the flowering season has passed, butterfly shadows flutter, flowers have turned to mud, full of loneliness. Who poetically embellished the butterfly's shadow, who dried the flowers, why are the remaining stories always romantic and melancholic? Since the ending is already known, why start, leaving behind is all sorrow, perhaps truly, the past is just a bright sorrow of youth.

The storms of youth might just be a shadow in our mundane world, fading away after we pass, leaving behind only unrealistic dreams. Let those pasts become our memories, in the slanting sun of the afternoon, leaning on a rattan chair, smiling watching the flowers bloom and fall in front of the courtyard, poetically writing a beautiful life, after the clouds pass, a beautiful beginning awaits.
