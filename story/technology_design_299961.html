Armed police shot dead a suspect on the spot, netizens questioned the necessity of shooting.

by 8hbo226e2j on 2009-12-09 12:43:54

Core Tip: The other day, a villain with a knife sneaked into Yanglou Village, Taiping Township, Xiang County, Henan Province. After being discovered and surrounded by the villagers, the police rushed to the scene. The villain attacked the policemen and the crowd with bricks, and was then shot dead by the police. Some netizens thought that the suspect only held a brick and was surrounded in a resident's courtyard without holding any hostages, which would not cause greater harm. The police could completely gather high-pressure water guns and other equipment to subdue him.

The suspect on higher ground attacked the policeman and the crowd with bricks (screenshot from video)

Sichuan Online, December 8th - On November 26th, a suspected thief who looked crazy was surrounded in a yard in Taiping Township, Xiang County, Henan Province. After blocking the gate, the suspect resisted arrest with his advantageous position. He attacked the police and the crowd with bricks and other weapons from higher ground, shouting that he would kill all the villagers. The captain of the Special Patrol Police Team of the County Public Security Bureau was hit on the head and fell unconscious immediately. Two other policemen and a villager were also injured. After the police fired warning shots, the suspect continued to attack with bricks. After several hours of confrontation with the police, the suspect was shot dead by a "sharpshooter".

Many local villagers deeply hated the suspect's ferocious behavior. Afterward, they sent brocades to the Public Security Bureau. However, some netizens questioned the necessity for the police to shoot dead the "brick-wielding thug".

After the strange man was trapped, he resisted arrest and attacked the police

On the morning of November 26th, Chen Xinggao from Yulou Village, Taiping Township, Shangqiu City, was building a house for someone. Around 11:30, someone shouted at him: "A thief has entered your eldest son's home. Go and take a look quickly!"

Chen Xinggao, 58 years old, lives alone with his wife. Last year, his house collapsed, just as his eldest son and daughter-in-law went to Xinjiang to work. He and his wife then took care of their eldest son's home.

When Chen Xinggao rode his bicycle to his eldest son's home, there were already many people around. He asked his neighbor, Chen Qiujian, what happened. Chen Qiujian told him that around 11 o'clock in the morning, while sweeping the yard, suddenly the door was kicked open, and a nervous-looking person came in. Chen Qiujian didn't know him, so he asked what he was doing. This person didn't respond. Seeing this suspicious behavior, Chen Qiujian picked up a shovel and drove him away, shouting loudly. The nearby villagers immediately came over. With no way out, this person had to jump over the two-meter-high wall and jumped into the courtyard of Chen Xinggao's eldest son. Just then, Chen Xinggao's wife was also outside, and the gate was locked.

Subsequently, the gate was blocked by the suspect with a farm tricycle and a thick wooden stake.

Chen Xinggao saw that the suspect was about 30 years old, wearing a ragged coat, with a hat on his head, looking crazy. He held red bricks, knives, and other dangerous weapons, walking back and forth on the bungalow and tile roof. Whoever got close, he threw bricks at them. The suspect also moved the corn cobs under the tile roof corridor into the yard and set them on fire, filling the yard with smoke.

Seeing this, Chen Xinggao became extremely anxious. He feared that if the suspect lost control, he might burn down the four brick-earth structured tile houses. Chen Xinggao hurriedly found the village cadres, who said that the police station staff were trying to deal with it.

The director of Taiping Township Police Station, Wei Dongfeng, had someone bring a ladder. He climbed up along the east wall of the tile roof. The suspect, on higher ground, noticed and chased Wei Dongfeng down with an iron fork, pulling the ladder onto the roof.

Later, Li Can, a member of the Party Committee of Xiang County Public Security Bureau and the captain of the Special Patrol Police Team, also led the police to the scene. Li Can decided to lead the police in a strong assault, but before the police could approach the wall, the thug started throwing bricks and tiles violently. The police shouted at the suspect through the wall, but the suspect continued to resist and shouted that he would kill everyone in the village after getting out!

The special patrol team captain suffered a head injury and fell unconscious

November 26th happened to be the local market day, and villagers from nearby areas came here to shop.

That morning, Yang Zhenying from Yanglou Group, Yulou Village, also came here to shop. Close to noon, Yang Zhenying heard that there was a thief in the village who was trapped in a courtyard, so he hurried to the scene.

At this time, there were people everywhere around Chen Xinggao's house, and the police warned everyone not to get close.

At this moment, the police were talking to the suspect, asking him to drop his weapon and surrender. However, the suspect kept throwing bricks around, preventing the villagers and the police from getting close.

Yang Zhenying, standing near the tile roof, suddenly heard someone shout: "Run fast! He is throwing bricks!" Yang Zhenying was just about to run when a brick hit his right shoulder. He felt pain in his right shoulder and discomfort in his heart, so he was helped by others to the village health center for medication and intravenous infusion. He still cannot work now.

Li Can sent someone to find a ladder, deciding to flank from behind the bungalow.

When Li Can prepared to climb the ladder, the suspect heard the noise and threw four bricks downward one after another. One brick hit Li Can's head, causing him to fall unconscious immediately. He was sent to the county People's Hospital, where brain surgery doctors diagnosed him with a depressed skull fracture.

Director Wei Dongfeng of the Taiping Township Police Station said that after Li Can was hit, he quickly dragged Li Can out. When halfway through, two more bricks fell down. One hit his foot, and the other injured Officer Zhang Junquan.

Subsequently, Director Jia Hongliang of the Xiang County Public Security Bureau also arrived at the scene. As Jia Hongliang prepared to talk to the suspect, the suspect continuously threw bricks.

Time passed second by second, and the suspect became restless, walking back and forth on the house with a brick in hand.

Jia Hongliang reported to Xu Dagang, Vice Mayor of Shangqiu City and Director of the Municipal Public Security Bureau. Xu Dagang required decisive measures based on the situation on site. Around 4 p.m., to avoid further casualties among the participating officers, Jia Hongliang decided to fire a warning shot at the thug.

"The sharpshooter" killed the suspect

After the police fired a warning shot, the atmosphere on the scene became tense immediately.

Ding Chaofan, director of the Command Department of the Xiang County Public Security Bureau, is about 40 years old. He was a shooting team member during his time at the police academy and participated in provincial public security system shooting competitions multiple times. He received personal second-class merit twice and third-class merit three times, and was named a "sharpshooter" by the Provincial Public Security Department in 2002.

Ding Chaofan, in an interview with the media, said that he first fired a warning shot, but the thug did not listen and instead threw bricks at him. He quickly used a poplar tree as cover. He heard "bang, bang," as two bricks hit the poplar tree above his head. Then he heard "whoosh," as a brick flew past his ear. In the instant the thug threw the fourth brick, he quickly opened fire.

He said: "I also wanted to try my best to save the suspect's life, but he continued to resist. If I didn't decisively kill him, it would cause new casualties, so I had to shoot."

Regarding the killing process, previous media reports stated: "One shot killed the suspect."

However, some witnesses at the scene said they saw the police fire three shots.

So, how many shots were fired at the time? The reporter went to the Public Security Bureau for verification.

In the afternoon of December 4th, the reporter came to the Xiang County Public Security Bureau and explained the purpose to the gatekeeper, wanting to interview Captain Li Can of the Special Patrol Police Team, who was injured. After seeing the reporter's credentials, he reported to the on-duty leader. After he returned, he told the reporter: "Director Ding from the Command Department will come soon, wait here for a moment." Later, after the gatekeeper went out and returned, he told the reporter: "Director Ding has something and won't come. You can directly go to the county People's Hospital to find Captain Li Can, who is hospitalized there." At the neurosurgery department of the county People's Hospital, a doctor told the reporter that Li Can had been discharged. In the morning of December 6th, the reporter dialed the mobile phone of Director Wei Dongfeng of the Taiping Township Police Station, but no one answered.

The suspect's identity has not yet been determined

On December 5th, the reporter saw in the courtyard where the suspect was killed that it was still in disarray, with traces of the suspect burning corn cobs still visible.

Most of the villagers, including Yang Zhenying and Chen Xinggao, believed that the suspect appeared crazy on the surface, but in reality, it was an act to confuse everyone.

Their basis was that the suspect was both alert and cruel. During the several hours of standoff, the suspect occupied an advantageous position between the bungalow and the tile roof. Standing there, the entire courtyard was within his sight. This position also allowed him to observe the movements behind the bungalow and the tile roof. As soon as there was movement outside the courtyard, he immediately ran over and threw bricks. After Ding Chaofan fired a warning shot, the suspect threw three bricks at him hiding behind a poplar tree in Chen Qiujian's courtyard, with two hitting the tree. Some villagers even suspected that the suspect knew martial arts; otherwise, how could he throw so accurately?

The reporter saw two marks on the poplar tree where it had been hit. If it had hit a person, the injuries would definitely have been serious.

For this, some villagers also had different opinions. Yang Jun (a pseudonym), who was present at the scene, told the reporter that the suspect's mind might have problems. "Otherwise, why would he confront the police when he didn't steal anything?" What made Yang Jun most puzzled was that after the police fired warning shots at the suspect, according to normal thinking, he should have stopped resisting, but instead of avoiding, he stood there and continued to throw bricks in resistance, which did not conform to normal thinking.

The reporter hasn't learned more information about the suspect from the Xiang County police. A villager said that judging by his accent, he seemed to be from Anhui Province.

Controversy: Is it necessary to "shoot on the spot"?

The bloodstains left by Li Can angered many villagers, who thought the suspect was too cruel. Subsequently, more than 20 cadres and villagers from Yulou Village hired a band, playing music all the way, and presented a banner to Director Jia Hongliang of the Xiang County Public Security Bureau.

Videos of the Xiang County police shooting the "brick-wielding thug" circulated online, sparking heated debates among netizens.

Some netizens believed that decisive measures should be taken to shoot dead such vicious thugs, which could reduce casualties among the police and the public.

But some netizens questioned the necessity of "shooting on the spot". Some netizens believed that the suspect holding a brick was surrounded in a resident's courtyard without holding any hostages, posing no greater danger. The police could completely gather high-pressure water guns and other equipment to subdue the suspect.

Lawyer Li Huayang from Henan Huahao Law Firm believed that according to the situation at the time, it was inappropriate for the police to shoot the suspect dead, and injuring him would suffice.

He said that according to Article 4 of China's Regulations on the Use of Police Equipment and Weapons by the People's Police, the use of police equipment and weapons by the People's Police shall be based on the principle of stopping criminal acts, minimizing personnel casualties and property losses as much as possible. Article 9 of the Regulations clearly stipulates the use of firearms, such as murder, hostage-taking, etc., seriously endangering personal safety, or criminals carrying guns, explosives, highly toxic substances and other dangerous items resisting arrest and fleeing, etc., under these circumstances, the People's Police may use weapons.

Li Huayang said that at the time, the thug was trapped in the courtyard and merely used bricks as his final resistance, which did not reach the level of danger requiring immediate death. Negotiation experts could communicate with him, or the police could capture him safely. Moreover, the person executing the task at the time was a "sharpshooter", so hitting the non-vital parts of the suspect would not be difficult.
