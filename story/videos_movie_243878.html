According to "Records of the Grand Historian - The Life of Confucius", Confucius's ancestors were originally descendants of the Yin-Shang dynasty. After the Zhou Dynasty defeated the Shang Dynasty, King Cheng of Zhou enfeoffed Weizi Qi, the illegitimate brother of King Zhou of Shang and a loyal minister of the Shang Dynasty, in Song. The capital was established in Shangqiu (now around Shangqiu, Henan). After Weizi Qi died, his younger brother Weizhong ascended the throne, and Weizhong became the ancestor of Confucius. The sixth-generation ancestor of Confucius was named Kong Fu Jia, who was a doctor in the state of Song and served as the Grand Marshal. He was killed in a palace coup. Since then, his descendants began to use Kong as their surname. His great-grandfather Kong Fangshu fled from Song to Lu to avoid internal turmoil in Song. From then on, the Kong family settled in Zhouyi and became people of Lu. Confucius's father was Shuliang He (Shuliang was his courtesy name, He was his given name, pronounced 'he'), and his mother was Yan Zhengzai. Shuliang He was a famous warrior in Lu at that time, with outstanding character and having established military merits twice, becoming famous for single-handedly holding up the suspended gate to let the troops retreat into the city. He once served as the doctor of Zhouyi. Shuliang He first married Shi's wife, they had nine daughters but no son. Then he took a concubine, they had a son named Bo Ni, also called Meng Pi. Meng Pi had a foot ailment, according to the etiquette of the time, it was inappropriate to continue the family line, so he had another child with the young woman Yan Zhengzai, which was Confucius.

In 551 BC (the 22nd year of Duke Xiang of Lu), Confucius was born in Changping Township of Zouyi in the State of Lu. Because his parents prayed for a son on Mount Niqiu, he was named Qiu and styled Zhongni. When Confucius was three years old, Shuliang He passed away, and Confucius and his mother were not accepted by Shi's family (Shuliang He's legal wife), so Confucius' mother Yan Zhengzai had to take Confucius and Meng Pi to move to Que Li in Qufu, living a difficult life. When Confucius was 17 years old, his mother Yan Zhengzai passed away, and he mourned for three years.

At the age of nineteen, Confucius married the daughter of an official from the Song family. A year later, the daughter of the official gave birth to a son. Duke Zhao of Lu sent someone to congratulate with a carp, Confucius felt very honored, so he named his son Li and styled him Boyu. In the tenth year of Duke Ai of Lu (485 BC), Confucius's wife, the daughter of the official, passed away.
