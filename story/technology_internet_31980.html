Don't Let Google Kill You | Ge Li Zhi

by onehedge on 2007-10-22 18:54:30

Nowadays, the services I use most often are Google Search, Google Reader, Gmail, Google Photos, and Gtalk. In the past, I frequently used Google Blogger, but after it became inaccessible (due to certain restrictions), I switched to using Wordpress for blogging. Occasionally, I also play around with Google Earth, or use Google Maps and Google News. When I list these services, you might think it’s nothing out of the ordinary. However, almost every time I open my Firefox browser, Google Search is set as the homepage. Then I open Gmail to check and reply to emails, followed by a long session on Google Reader.

Even though I continuously unsubscribe from some feeds, before long there are always plenty of new articles waiting to be read. So most of the time, I don’t bother checking Baidu News or other portal sites; instead, I dive into Google Reader. Of course, as soon as my computer starts up, I open Gtalk (even though I haven’t set it to auto-launch, I still click on its small icon to open it). I use Gtalk to communicate with some internet friends—most of them are bloggers or people involved in internet-related technologies.

Once I enter Google Reader, I often get absorbed in reading the articles inside. If there’s something I don’t fully understand or want to learn more about, I’ll turn to Google Search to find additional articles to read. So, the real culprit behind consuming most of my time is Google Reader.
