In a village that exists alone, there is a dilapidated two-story building. The floor, corroded by age, has exposed spots of small holes, which are precisely the windows for peeping. Upstairs lives a vigorous boy who sees a beautiful but lonely woman downstairs. Her husband, a security guard, is away day and night, and when he returns, he uses her as a tool for releasing his desires. The peeping boy silently falls in love with this desolate woman. A peculiar plot is that the woman never has direct contact with her husband during their sexual encounters. This drives the boy, who eventually gets the key to the downstairs door, to imitate the man and have relations with the woman. When the secret is exposed, the woman is finally moved by the boy's naivety and sincerity, and love ignites between them like flames.

The film contains numerous scenes of voyeurism, masturbation, and various sexual positions, making it inevitably rated as unsuitable for children. However, the faintly visible political background in "Love's Release" gives the film a sense of aspiration, despite its immaturity. The story of extramarital affairs in "Love's Release" takes place against the historical backdrop of the 1980 Gwangju Massacre, where hundreds of civilians striving for democracy were gunned down. The three main characters involved in the affair all have distinct social identities: the male protagonist is a wanted student activist, the woman is the daughter of a professor arrested during the Gwangju incident, and her husband is the police officer who forcibly took possession of her when he stormed into her home. The film concludes with the policeman discovering the affair, killing the escapee, and then committing suicide. The woman gives birth to the baby in her womb and then commits suicide by train. The identity of the characters is enough to make the audience imagine a lot, and the abandoned infant may well represent the politically bewildered younger generation of today's South Korea.

When the abandoned infant grows up, he receives a ballet shoe from his mother's teenage years. The boy embraces the milky white ballet shoe, standing beside the speeding train, and amidst the swaying, he vaguely sees his mother, transformed like a butterfly, wearing a white dress and dancing gracefully before him.
