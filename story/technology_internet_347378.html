--== If You're Going to Marry, Marry a Man Who's Willing to Carry You Upstairs -- Touching Edition ==--

by swgb7501a on 2010-04-17 21:18:55

She was a white-collar worker in the city, and he was a baggage carrier. After graduating from high school, the two of them followed completely different trajectories in their youth. Yet, they still maintained their relationship as lovers. Just maintained.

During the day, she drank authentic Nestle coffee at the company; after work, she ate the cheap popsicles he bought her. At noon, she enjoyed the refined meals at the company; in the evening, he took her to a dingy restaurant to eat not-so-authentic Lanzhou noodles.

She thought that her life was too uncoordinated. This kind of romance seemed destined for a certain outcome from the very beginning. Every day, he would pick her up and send her to the elevator entrance of the white-collar apartment where she lived, saying goodnight before rushing off.

One day, she suddenly wanted to act coquettish and said, "Carry me upstairs." He looked at the elevator which was working fine, then turned around and said, "Alright." He didn't ask for any reason.

He carried her, starting from the first floor and slowly climbed upward.

When they reached halfway, he got tired and said, "Can we take a break?" She suddenly became interested and playfully refused.

So he really didn’t take a break and continued climbing all the way to the 13th floor where her apartment was located.

She asked him if he was tired, and he said, "Tired, even more tired than carrying bags." She knew he was telling the truth and felt a bit moved.

But they still broke up. Sometimes, mere emotion is not enough to sustain love.

The city didn't lack for one baggage carrier, so he returned to the countryside. Occasionally, he would call her and tell her about the greenhouses he was planting now and how he had earned some money. She listened indifferently. By then, she already had a new boyfriend, someone who was well-matched with her background.

Then one day, he called again and said he had saved up five thousand yuan, which was enough to get married in the countryside.

She discovered that, suddenly, her eyes were moistening.

Her new boyfriend also picked her up every day after work and sent her to the elevator, politely saying goodnight before leaving.

One day, she said, "Carry me upstairs." Her boyfriend agreed. The elevator was stopped on the first floor, and her boyfriend quickly picked her up and rushed into the elevator. She leaned on her boyfriend's back, rising together with the elevator, but her heart sank rapidly. Her boyfriend chuckled, seemingly quite satisfied with his little humorous trick.

That day, she didn't accept her boyfriend's customary farewell kiss.

She called him and asked if he had spent those five thousand yuan yet, and then she found herself crying uncontrollably. He said he had spent it. She threw down the phone, feeling at that moment like she was losing her entire world.

A few days later, she saw him at the elevator entrance. In his hand was a ring, a very expensive one. He waved the ring and said, "Five thousand yuan." She laughed. Then she started crying, sobbing uncontrollably.

She said, "Carry me upstairs?" He said, "Alright." Then he carried her, step by step climbing the stairs. On the way, he got tired, but remained silent, continuing until the 13th floor.

At this point, she thought, if a man is willing to carry a woman up the longest staircase without asking for any reason, then what reason does this woman have to reject him?

And so, their story continued...
