Those unforgettable pasts, are they still the same?

by lili1 on 2012-01-03 00:34:57

Those unforgettable pasts, are they still fine after all these years?

Day after day, the busy life of going out early and coming home late, not knowing why I live, sadness will arise at some unpredictable moment, spreading little by little in my heart. Walking on the streets of the town, under the dazzling neon lights, my shadow is right under my feet. Stopping between the reflection and the body, several passers-by glance sideways as they pass by me. I smell the familiar fragrance of a well-known brand of soap from a child's body. Turning around, I see that pair of bright eyes, pure and clear, untouched by the world, which makes me suddenly feel moved.

During the waiting time at the bus stop, I saw the woman who was wearing red flower patterned clothes and was mentally ill a year ago. Now she looks completely different. Her clothes are ragged and dirty, her hair is messy, and she walks heavily on the side of the road, occasionally smiling foolishly to passers-by. Speculations about the reasons for her madness have spread throughout the streets and alleys. I only remember that day many years ago when she kindly sold her goods with two children at the crossroads, quietly smiling. The two images in my memory can never be superimposed, but they were picked up without any defense, like the thickness of a layer of sugar cake, measuring her past and present.

After days of leisure, life becomes quiet in a busy scene. After much thought, I took the bus to the small town where my friend had taken me before. During the long journey, we passed through vast green fields. At that time, your words echoed in my ears, gradually becoming clearer. The long walk is a kind of memory, thread by thread, like the scarf I tried countless times to weave into completeness, interlocking ring by ring, spreading boundlessly in my heart.

The small town I once visited is a place full of spiritual charm. People passing by all smiled at me with an irresistible warmth that silently penetrated my heart. Walking on the stone roads, I felt the presence of you who had been here. At the beginning of this year, you brought me here, climbing mountains and crossing rivers to reach this secluded small town. You said it was called Huozhen, where you were born, with a calm demeanor. You laughed and told stories of your childhood memories, the integration of mountains and water, the happy and satisfying process, made me indulged in this small town with your happy memories.

Quietly walking through this section of the road, I began to miss peacefully. Youth, will eventually come to an end. I am used to my unchanging silent and bland appearance, not overly happy, nor overly sad. During the day, busy and alone, eating alone, at night, quietly reading and writing alone, the days pass simply. However, when dusk approaches, the heart feels empty. I am accustomed to entering the fruit shop, buying food that I like at first sight, enjoying the warmth of owning them, at least making me not completely nothing.

It's the autumnal equinox now, I take out the purplish grapes from the refrigerator to fill my stomach. The honey juice is thick and sweet, the cold taste melts in my lips, a slight coolness, a faint chill. With the increasing age, I like the light original flavor of food. Life is like a scroll of paintings unfolding, the warmth and coldness known to oneself.

Shallow time has left some traces that cannot be polished away. There is beauty, but it is too short-lived, like sudden brilliance, consumed bit by bit in the depths of sorrow. And finally, what can be left is only the deep and shallow images in the memories. Waiting for the time to come, year after year doing one thing, forgetting.

In the afternoon, I met a friend I haven't seen for many years on the bus. His appearance hasn't changed much, I recognized him instantly and called his name softly. After talking about many people and things, it turns out that we, who live in the same city, have never had such an unexpected encounter before. We were mostly silent, but when we said goodbye, looking at his eyes filled with happiness, I saw my own heart full of panic hidden deep inside. Under the street lamp with drizzling rain, I shivered and walked towards the depth.

At 12:10 AM, a song started and ended with the tapping of fingers. In the early morning, I like to sing softly to myself.

Those unforgettable pasts, are they still fine after all these years?
