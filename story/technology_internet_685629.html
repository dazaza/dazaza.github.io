Social security and expenditure allocation become the most concerned topics of the two sessions

by abcurtbt on 2012-03-01 15:56:39

Guangzhou, March 1 (Changhong, Luo Xu) - Just before the convening of the 2012 National People's Congress and Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference sessions, the People's Network and the Political Culture Department of the People's Daily conducted a survey on the [Top Ten Hot Topics of the 2012 Two Sessions]. The online survey ran from February 7 to February 29. By 24:00 on February 29, more than 1.55 million people participated in the online survey, including 190,000 mobile phone users. The survey is now complete, but netizens continue to leave messages.

The survey covered 20 hot issues, including housing price regulation, social security, medical reform, food safety, environmental problems, traffic problems, price issues, income distribution, small and micro enterprises, agriculture-related issues, social morality, social governance, grassroots democracy, public expenses, institutional reform, cultural system reform, educational bias, legal system construction, online governance, and anti-corruption.

The survey results showed that the top ten hot issues selected by netizens were [social security], [income distribution], [medical reform], [social governance], [educational bias], [agriculture-related issues], anti-corruption, price problems, food safety, and housing price control adjustments. Among them, [social security] received 250,000 votes, [income distribution] received 180,000 votes, and [medical reform] received 100,000 votes.

The People's Network 2012 Two Sessions survey included a [Top Ten Hot Issues] survey where participants selected ten out of twenty options, as well as separate surveys for each of the twenty candidate hot issues. Nearly 490,000 netizens participated in the [Top Ten Hot Issues] survey.

[Social Security] has ranked first among netizens' concerns for three consecutive years.

In the online survey of the Top Ten Hot Issues of the Two Sessions, social security ranked first with 250,000 votes, reflecting the urgency and broad scope of further improving and perfecting the social security system in China. In the sub-item survey, more than 90% of netizens believed that pension increases should be raised. In the survey [Are you satisfied with the increase in pensions for retired corporate employees?], 92% of netizens hoped to increase the rate and speed of pension increases. Regarding improvements in the medical insurance system, 27% of netizens hoped to expand reimbursable diseases and drug types, 42% believed personal reimbursement amounts should be increased, and 14% hoped for inter-regional connectivity to achieve异地reimbursement. Regarding whether elastic retirement systems are supported, 58% of netizens supported it, while 42% opposed it.

Reviewing recent People's Network Two Sessions surveys, in the 2011 Two Sessions survey, social security ranked first with 71,090 votes; in the 2010 Two Sessions survey, old-age insurance ranked first with 104,304 votes; in the 2009 Two Sessions survey, social insurance ranked eighth; and in 2008, old-age insurance also entered the top ten of the Two Sessions survey.

Analyzing netizen comments, there was a high call to abolish the "two-track" retirement system. Netizens indicated that retired corporate employees and laid-off workers indeed faced great difficulties, especially those who had been laid off, bought out, or unemployed elderly workers who struggled to pay continuously rising old-age insurance premiums and medical insurance premiums. Some netizens pointed out that despite seven consecutive years of pension increases for retired corporate employees, their pensions were still only one-third of civil servants under the same conditions. Other netizens directly criticized the "two-track" system as the greatest injustice in today's society.

The demand for fair treatment in pensions has sparked public skepticism about the "two-track" system. Netizens noted that although the state raises pensions for retired corporate employees every year, compared to continuously rising pensions for civil servants and public institution retirees, the large pension gap remains difficult to bridge. Netizens hope that the state listens to the people's demands and formulates a fair and reasonable retirement policy, ensuring that farmers do not lose the land they rely on for survival. They look forward to the state improving the retirement pension system to reflect fairness. Experts believe that reforming the "two-track" system requires consensus and institutional reform at the national ministry level. Local governments currently make adjustments according to overall national arrangements, which are limited in effectiveness. Ultimately resolving the gap depends on action at the national level. Solving this issue will require time and cannot be achieved overnight. Experts believe that the state should focus on promoting social equity and reducing pension gaps.

[Income Distribution] ranks second with increasing attention over the years.

Income distribution has been a widely watched issue in recent years, ranking second in this online survey, with 180,000 people participating in the survey.

The survey showed that among netizens participating in the sub-item survey, 81% believed current income disparities are large and wealth inequality is severe; 36% hoped individual income tax adjustments would keep pace with per capita income growth and further raise the tax threshold; 76% believed the current minimum wage line is generally too low. Regarding how to reduce income disparities, 21% of netizens believed wages should be raised and a normal wage increase mechanism established; 21% believed corruption should be thoroughly investigated; 20% believed higher-income individuals should be taxed more heavily, with these three measures receiving the highest number of votes. Additionally, 62% of netizens believed civil servant salaries should not be increased.

Reviewing recent Two Sessions surveys, [income distribution] has consistently ranked in the top five. In 2011, [personal income] ranked fourth with 24,814 votes, and [wealth disparity], related to income distribution, ranked fifth. It can be said that attention to income distribution issues has been increasing annually.

Netizens generally reported low wages for frontline workers, significant income disparities between industries, and inability to keep up with CPI. Netizens suggested raising the proportion of national income allocated to the first distribution, establishing a wage negotiation system, and increasing wage-based income for ordinary workers. Wages for monopolistic industries such as state-owned enterprises should be restricted, fiscal transfer payments should be increased, social security coverage and subsidy standards should be improved, and a subsidy increase mechanism linked to the market should be established to ensure the basic living standards of low-income groups.

Professor Wang Yuke of the National Academy of Governance analyzed that currently, progress in China's income distribution reform has not been as rapid as expected, largely due to obstruction by vested interest groups. China already has three major vested interest groups: the aristocratic interest group represented by corrupt officials, the monopoly interest group represented by monopolistic enterprises, and the real estate-resource interest group represented by the real estate and resource industries.

Li Peilin, an academician of the Chinese Academy of Social Sciences and director of the Institute of Sociology, believes that China is in a period of social transformation, and straightening out the income distribution order and establishing a fair and reasonable distribution system is the direction to move forward. Determined efforts must be made to touch existing interest patterns, resolve distribution issues for long-term stability, and satisfy all social classes.

[Medical Reform] became the third most concerned topic, with increased attention over the past three years.

By the end of the survey, [medical reform] received 100,000 votes, ranking third.

Reviewing recent Two Sessions surveys, in the 2011 Two Sessions survey, medical reform ranked sixth with 18,889 votes; in the 2010 Two Sessions survey, it ranked sixth with 29,084 votes; and in the 2009 Two Sessions survey, medical issues ranked third. Attention to medical issues has increased.

Among the 100,000 netizens who participated, 40% expressed the hope of increasing the proportion of personal reimbursement amounts, and many netizens believed that "seeing a doctor away from home" was the main cause of difficulty in accessing healthcare. Netizens called for the prompt implementation of inter-regional medical insurance reimbursement and hoped for a nationwide medical insurance card.

Currently, China's medical insurance has not yet achieved national coordination. In recent years, with the continuous expansion of the population covered by medical insurance and the steady increase in mobile populations, the number of urban employees, urban residents, and new rural cooperative medical care patients seeking medical treatment away from home has been increasing annually. Additionally, hospitals with abundant medical resources are mostly concentrated in large and medium-sized cities, making issues such as seeking medical treatment away from home and fee reimbursement increasingly prominent. Retirees who rely on local employed children for support face significant problems when hospitalized outside their home region and need prior approval from their original residence’s medical insurance system to receive reimbursement. Netizens believe that the issue of inter-regional settlement and reimbursement of medical insurance should be given high attention. They urge the prompt implementation of inter-regional medical insurance reimbursement and hope for the early realization of a nationwide medical insurance card.

Some experts pointed out that fundamentally solving the problem of universal use of medical insurance cards is a real need for the people. They suggest implementing a nationwide medical insurance card, integrating all qualified hospitals into designated hospitals nationwide, establishing a national personal health record, achieving electronic management, and networking information between national medical institutions so that citizens’ health records follow them wherever they go, and their medical insurance cards are universally accepted like “UnionPay,” allowing them to seek medical care and reimbursement anywhere in the country with their ID or medical insurance card.

Currently, provinces such as Shandong and Guangdong have successively completed real-time settlement of medical insurance across regions within the province. Employees, retirees, and urban residents within Shandong Province who meet the criteria for referral or inter-regional placement can enjoy immediate settlement at designated connected hospitals, paying only their portion of the costs, with the remaining amount settled directly between the medical insurance agency of their insured location and the designated hospital.

Changes in rankings based on participation in sub-item surveys [Anti-Corruption] ranks third

From the voting participation in the sub-item surveys, [social security] and [income distribution] remain the two most highly regarded issues. Respectively, 323,077 and 161,305 people participated in the sub-item surveys. However, different from the rankings of the top ten hot issues, [anti-corruption], [social governance], [agriculture-related issues], and [housing price control] are issues with high participation in the sub-item surveys.

In the [anti-corruption] sub-item survey, which saw participation from 97,044 people, in response to the question [What do you think is key to curbing corruption?], 39.3% of voters chose [deepen institutional reforms and eradicate the soil where corruption grows], while 29.3% chose [promote government and official information transparency and play the role of multi-subject comprehensive supervision].

In the [social governance] sub-item survey, which saw participation from 96,723 people, in response to the question [How do you evaluate the role of new media such as the internet in social governance?], 68.6% of participants believed [it increases information volume and transparency, which is very beneficial].

In the [agriculture-related issues] sub-item survey, which saw participation from 96,607 people, in response to the question [Among the following issues related to farmers, agriculture, and rural areas, which ones are you most concerned about?], the top three were [empty-nest elders, left-behind women and children in rural areas], [corruption among rural cadres], and [forced requisition and demolition].

In the [housing price control] sub-item survey, which saw participation from 69,214 people, in response to the question [What do you think of the state's real estate control policies in 2011?], 82.5% of voters chose [not strong enough]. In response to the question [Do you hope the purchase restriction order continues?], 85.5% of voters chose [hope it continues, let the house prices drop further].
