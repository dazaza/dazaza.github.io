How to choose an extension trainer?

by boe868 on 2012-03-05 17:33:30

I. Intelligent - A good coach must possess sufficient theoretical knowledge and practical experience. The responsibilities of an outdoor training coach include teaching, mentoring, and answering questions. If the coach only has a cup of water, he can't give a student a glass of water. If the coach is an empty cup, how can he add water for the students? Therefore, a coach needs to have years of solid accumulation. Additionally, each company the coach targets is different, so every course must be developed and adjusted according to the needs of the enterprise. This also requires the coach to have profound knowledge, practical experience, and extraordinary thinking, summarizing, and induction abilities.

II. Articulate - No matter how experienced the coach is or how good the training content is, it all needs to be expressed through speech. Regardless of how capable the coach may be, if their verbal expression cannot satisfy the students, they are not a good coach.

III. Physically Fit - Especially with strong legs. A coach's daily training session lasts 6-10 hours, and there are often high-altitude, outdoor, or even two-day or three-day consecutive trainings. Such long durations would be impossible to endure without a strong physique. Physical fitness is the capital for outdoor training.

IV. Professional Appearance - A coach should have the demeanor of a coach and a good training "stage presence." As a trainer, one should set an example in terms of appearance, making sure not to repel the students. Especially in outdoor training, clothing should be appropriate for the occasion. The coach's body language should be vivid but not excessive. Coaches who are physically fit, tall, and imposing with a confident aura are more likely to be popular among students.

V. Empathetic - An outdoor training coach must observe and think carefully. When formulating the training process, the actual needs of the enterprise must be considered carefully. Face-to-face communication with the company regarding the outdoor training should take place, considering the company's needs more thoroughly. During the training, the coach should always "pay attention" to the reactions of the students below. By observing their eyes and expressions, the coach can gauge the students' level of participation. If the students appear tired, the coach should immediately play a small game or tell a humorous story to boost their spirits. If the students appear confused, the coach should re-explain the content of the outdoor training so that the students understand and participate.
