Orange LAVA and ZTE announced the launch of Intel architecture phones within the year

2012-02-28 01:46:48 Source: NetEase Technology Report 193 people participated Mobile phone news Forward to Weibo (1)

NetEase Technology News, February 28, Intel's steps towards the mobile phone chip market are gradually accelerating. At the 2012 World Communications Conference, Intel CEO Paul Otellini announced three new partners: European operator Orange, Indian mobile phone manufacturer LAVA, and Chinese mobile phone manufacturer ZTE Communications. These three companies will each launch smartphones based on Intel chips in their respective markets this year.

At the beginning of this year at CES, Intel announced that Lenovo and Motorola would release phones with Intel chips, adding the above three partners, Intel will launch smartphones based on its own chips in at least three markets: Europe, India, and China this year, with the current system being Android.

In addition to these partners, Intel also announced cooperation with Visa, both parties providing a foundation for mobile phone security, allowing mobile phone manufacturers to quickly adopt this technology to enhance the security of mobile payments on mobile phones.

Paul Otellini said that in smartphone applications, only 10% of the uses are for making calls, while the remaining 90% of the time it functions more like a data computing device. Intel's experience in computing chips can help itself in the mobile phone chip market.

Orange will be the first in Europe to launch an Android phone based on the Intel architecture. This pre-exposed Santa Clara has quite powerful performance, with a standby time of 14 days, capable of shooting HD videos with an 8 million pixel camera, and capturing 10 photos per second.

Yves Maitre, Senior Vice President of Mobile Multimedia and Devices at Orange, stated on-site that Intel-chip phones will provide users with very good data processing speed and experience, and this phone will be sold first in the UK and France by late summer this year.

Indian manufacturer LAVA and domestic Chinese manufacturer ZTE Communications will both launch smartphones based on Intel chips within the year, first selling in the local market.

Lava co-founder and company director Vishal Sehgal announced the launch of the Lava XOLO smartphone, which will be available in major retail stores in the second quarter of this year.

ZTE Vice President He Shiyou said that the new phone will be launched within this year, and besides smartphones, ZTE will also cooperate with Intel in other aspects.
