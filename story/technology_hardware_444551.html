34 Small Common Sense Knowledge That Women Should Understand In Their Lives

by dress7h7q on 2011-06-11 16:29:57

1. No matter which part of the shoe rubs your feet, you can apply a little liquor to the area of the shoe that is causing friction, and it will stop rubbing.

2. If there are stains or dirt on your leather bag, you can use cotton dipped in mentholated oil to wipe it.

3. After washing your face, dip your fingers in some fine salt and gently rub the sides of your nose, then rinse with clear water. Blackheads and pimples will be cleared, and pores will shrink.

4. When going out, always carry a small dry battery in your bag. If your skirt has static electricity, rub the positive pole of the battery on the skirt a few times to remove the static.

5. If there is minor skin damage or burns, applying a little toothpaste can immediately stop bleeding and relieve pain, and also prevent infection.

6. Jeans fade after being worn for a long time. You can soak newly bought jeans in concentrated salt water for 12 hours, then wash with clear water. They won't fade when washed later.

7. Tofu can be used for beauty purposes. Every morning after getting up, take a piece of tofu, place it in the palm of your hand, and use it to rub your face for a few minutes. Persisting for a month will make your facial skin become white, tender, and moisturized.

8. Zipper maintenance: Don't pull the zipper too quickly or forcefully; don't tighten it too much. Keep it dry and avoid contact with acidic or alkaline substances. If the zipper feels rough, apply a little wax and gently pull it a few times.

9. White undershirts may develop black spots after being worn for a long time. Take 2 ounces of fresh ginger, crush it, and boil it in a pot with one pound of water. After it cools slightly, pour it into a laundry basin, soak the white undershirt for ten minutes, then rub it a few times. The black spots will disappear.

10. Trick to remove wrinkles from ties. Wrinkled ties can become smooth and beautiful without using an iron. Simply roll the tie around a beer bottle. The next day, the original wrinkles will be gone.

11. When very thirsty and there's no cool boiled water at home, you can put a cup of hot water in cold water to soak, then sprinkle a handful of salt in the cold water. This will speed up the cooling of the boiled water.

12. During travel, if caught in thunderstorm, never take shelter under large rocks, cliffs, or at cave entrances. Current passing through these places can produce electric arcs that injure those seeking shelter. If the cave is deep, you can hide inside.

13. Eating lamb helps maintain a fit body shape. Lamb is an ideal source of carnitine, a substance similar to amino acids that helps cells "burn" off excess fat in the body.

14. For rough skin, mix vinegar and glycerin in a 5:1 ratio and apply to the face daily. Persistent use will make the skin finer and softer. Adding a tablespoon of vinegar to your washing water and washing your face with it also has beautifying effects.

15. Drying clothes tip: Clothes should not be exposed directly to sunlight but dried in a cool, well-ventilated place until half-dry, then placed under weaker sunlight to finish drying, protecting the color and lifespan of the clothing.

16. Drying clothes tip: Clothes should not be wrung out too much but dried while still damp. Smooth out the collar, cuffs, etc., by hand to keep the clothes flat and wrinkle-free when dried.

17. Two methods to prevent fabric fading: Add an appropriate amount of vinegar when washing dark cotton fabrics to prevent fading and maintain a new shine; Soak newly bought colored cloth in salt water for ten minutes before the first wash to prevent fading.

18. When there is a wound with bleeding, sprinkle some sugar on the wound. Sugar reduces the moisture in the wound area, inhibiting bacterial growth and helping the wound heal.

19. When applying makeup, first place a slightly wet makeup sponge in the refrigerator. After a few minutes, pat the cool sponge on your powdered skin. Your skin will feel especially refreshed, and makeup will look particularly fresh.

20. Trick for drawing eyeliner: To draw delicate eyeliner, fix your elbow on the table, then place a small mirror flat and let both eyes look down at the mirror. You can confidently draw your eyeliner.

21. Experts suggest eating more red-colored foods like red peppers, carrots, tomatoes, onions, and hawthorn in winter to prevent colds. Drinking a glass of yogurt and a bowl of chicken soup daily also helps prevent colds.

22. Scientific towel disinfection method: Boil the towel in boiling water for about 10 minutes, then wash with soap, thoroughly rinse with clean water, fold the towel neatly, and heat it in the microwave for five minutes.

23. Nine smile-enhancing foods: Celery, cheese, green tea, onions, mushrooms, mustard, sugar-free gum, mint, and water. Regular consumption of these foods can eliminate bad breath and prevent cavities.

24. Excessive dandruff can be improved through diet. Eat more alkaline foods such as fruits, vegetables, honey, etc.; consume more vitamin B2 and B6-rich foods like animal liver, kidney, heart, milk, egg yolk, wheat germ, etc.

25. Pillows should have a certain elasticity, but excessive elasticity is not good either. Continuous external elastic force on the head can easily cause muscle fatigue and injury. Spring pillows and air pillows cannot be considered healthy pillows.

26. Beware of "low-temperature scalds" in winter. The water temperature in the hot water bag should not be too hot, and the heated surface should not directly touch the skin. Diabetics, those with delayed peripheral sensation, and infants should preferably not use hot water bags for warmth.

27. Strategies to reduce computer harm: Take a ten-minute break after working continuously for one hour. Ensure suitable indoor lighting and maintain ventilation and freshness. Pay attention to correct operating posture. Keep your skin clean.

28. Old white sweaters gradually turn black. After cleaning the sweater, freeze it in the refrigerator for one hour, then take it out to dry, and it will be as white as new.

29. Diamond maintenance: Do not pile diamond jewelry together to avoid scratching between prongs. Remove diamond jewelry during heavy or intense activities. Send it to a jeweler for professional cleaning every six months.

30. Method for airing quilts: Quilts made of synthetic fabric as lining or cover should not be exposed to direct sunlight to prevent chemical fibers from being damaged by high temperatures. Cover the quilt with a layer of cloth while airing to prevent direct sunlight exposure.

31. Don't stop wearing rabbit hair sweaters just because they shed hair. Place them in a plastic bag and refrigerate for 3-4 days to prevent shedding.

32. Silk scarf tricks. If your hairstyle done at a beauty salon gets messed up after waking up, don't worry. Lay a smooth silk scarf on your pillow before sleeping, and it won't mess up your hair, keeping your beautiful hairstyle intact.

33. Stay vigilant against colds in hot weather. Sweating consumes a lot of energy, reducing resistance and making people susceptible to colds. Drink more plain water, in small amounts multiple times, ideally 300-500 ml each time.

34. In hot weather, many people suffer from foot odor. Try these two methods: Place a little tea in your shoes when wearing them; Fill a basin with warm water, add a little tea, and soak your feet for ten minutes to eliminate odors!
