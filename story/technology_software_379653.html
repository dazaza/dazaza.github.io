Step-by-step Guide to Synchronizing and Backing Up Your Phone Contacts

by xdash on 2011-01-24 10:25:20

(Author of this article: @Li Shuo, Original link: http://lishuo.me/archives/75)

As the year-end approaches, there has been an alarming increase in the number of people losing their phones recently. Losing a phone is not a big deal, but if your contact list is lost as well, that could be troublesome and may even result in potential or immeasurable losses. To prevent such situations, it is advisable to spend a few minutes backing up your contact list while you still have your phone. This way, even if your phone gets lost, you can recover your original contact information within three seconds, minimizing any loss.

Below, I will guide you step-by-step on how to back up your phone contacts using four methods:

Method One: Backup using QQ Sync Assistant

It seems like everyone has a QQ number, so the first recommended backup method is Tencent's QQ Sync Assistant. QQ Sync Assistant supports various phones such as iPhones, Androids, Nokias, and more. You only need to download one software for convenient backups. When you switch to a new phone, simply install QQ Sync Assistant on the new phone, and you can download the contacts from your old phone.

QQ Sync Assistant Download Address: http://mobile.qq.com/pim/

After downloading and installing the sync assistant according to your phone model, choose the backup method. You can back up to the cloud or to a storage card. It is suggested to perform dual backups just in case.

Method Two: Synchronize your phone contact list with Google Contacts through Google Sync

Let’s discuss the advantages of using Google Sync to back up your contacts: no need to download software, no data cables required; Google is now and will continue to be the most stable and secure data storage service provider; Google Sync can be used across different phone models without being restricted to a specific operating system or brand.

Here, we will use the commonly used Nokia phone as an example to demonstrate how to synchronize your contact list via Google Sync:

1. Open the phone's tools - settings - connection function - data transfer - synchronization, options - new synchronization scenario mode.

2. Name the synchronization scenario mode as "Google sync".

3. Connection settings.

Server version: 1.2; Server ID: Google (the letter G must be capitalized); Data carrier method: Internet; Access point: default connection; Host address: https://m.google.com/syncml (note the 's' after http); Port: 443; Username: enter your Gmail address xxx@gmail.com; Password: enter your Gmail password; Synchronization type: two-way.

4. Contact list synchronization settings.

Remote database for the contact list: contacts (currently, Google only supports contact list synchronization).

5. After creating the new synchronization scenario mode, save it and start the synchronization directly. This way, your Google contacts will contain the contacts from your phone.

Method Three: Use the built-in contact list synchronization feature of your phone brand

Nokia phones come with PC suites, which allow you to connect your phone to a computer with the PC suite installed to export the contact list in vCard format and store it on your computer. You can quickly restore it when switching to a new phone.

Android operating system phones come with Google Contacts, which are updated in real-time, making it very easy!

BlackBerry and Meizu also have built-in contact list synchronization features, which I won’t elaborate on further.

Method Four: Use third-party software

Currently, some good third-party contact list software include:

That concludes the tutorial. I hope everyone chooses at least two methods to back up their contact lists and does not put all their business cards on one service provider!
