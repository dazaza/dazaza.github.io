Qin Yu Tong's notebook is now open for posting!

by anonymous on 2011-04-01 14:18:15

Today is Qin Yutong's 5th year, 8th month and 8th day. I suddenly realize how much he has grown. Now that I don't have to work, I stay at home every day taking care of my husband and teaching our child, spending more time with him than before. No, I should say that he is now the center of my life, and I revolve around him every day. In the past, I was busy with work and had no time to take care of him, so I could only listen to his grandparents recount the details of his life. But now, I have so much free time that I might as well use it to record his interesting stories, silly things, and all sorts of messy events.

Leave them for him to recall and laugh about when he grows up a little more, haha~ From now on, I will start a new post every month, even if I can't update daily, I will try to leave as many footprints as possible!

More than half of March has already passed, so I won't start a new post. Let's just make do for now!

I don't remember the things that happened a long time ago, so let me first record some of the events and witty remarks of young Qin Yutong from the last two days!

Scenario 1

Time: 4:50 PM on March 21st

Location: On the way home from kindergarten

Characters: Mom, Qin Yutong (Tiantian), a strange grandpa and a child

Event: While walking home with Tiantian, it was drizzling outside. We were sharing an umbrella, talking about some things that happened in kindergarten today. Suddenly, a sharp bicycle bell scared Tiantian. Reflexively, he looked back and then pointed to a grandfather riding a bicycle through the rain to pick up his granddaughter. He said to me, "Mom, look, there's a grandpa." Before I could respond, he shook his head and said, "In this weather, at such an old age, having to come pick up children, it must be tough!" I smiled, this kid, when did he start feeling empathy? "Tiantian, is it easy for mom to come pick you up every day?" "Mom, you're not that old." "But, what about this kind of weather?" After thinking for a while, he finally replied, "Anyway, I just think it's easy for you!"

Speechless...

Scenario 2

Time: 8:15 AM on Tuesday, March 22nd

Location: Kindergarten

Characters: Mom, Qin Yutong, Homeroom Teacher Ms. Liu

Event: After changing shoes at the kindergarten, Qin Yutong suddenly remembered that he had forgotten to bring his homework from last night. So, he quietly negotiated with me to ask Ms. Liu, his homeroom teacher, which I refused. Then, he mustered the courage to walk up to Ms. Liu himself. Below is their conversation.

Qin Yutong: "Ms. Liu, I forgot to bring my homework."

Ms. Liu: "What?! You forgot? Yesterday, one student didn't bring it, and I already said it's not allowed."

Qin Yutong lowered his head and remained silent...

Ms. Liu: "What should we do? I already said yesterday that today you cannot forget your homework."

Qin Yutong still didn't speak, glancing at me.

Ms. Liu: "Why are you looking at your mom? You're growing up, you need to take care of your own things. It's your responsibility to remember to bring your homework, not your mom's. Now, since you've forgotten, think about what to do and tell the teacher."

I was secretly impressed by the homeroom teacher's educational method. At the previous kindergarten, when Tiantian forgot his homework, those teachers never said anything. This morning, I was busy and forgot, and I refused to help him because I wanted him to learn from his mistakes and not think that everything should be done by mom and dad. One must learn to take responsibility for their own actions.

A minute passed, and Qin Yutong suddenly looked up, "Ms. Liu, can mom bring my homework when she comes to pick me up this afternoon?"

I guess Ms. Liu didn't expect the child to actually come up with a solution rather than just pushing the responsibility onto his mom. She smiled slightly, then sternly said to Qin Yutong, "Alright, but this is the only time. This kind of thing absolutely cannot happen again."

At this point, Qin Yutong sighed in relief and happily ran off to play with other kids.

That evening, after returning home, he asked me to write a note on a sticky note: "Tiantian, remember to bring your homework in the morning!" Then, he stuck the note on the door...

Location: Home

"Hmm, how do you know?"

"Our teacher mentioned it in class."

"Do you know why they are fighting?"

"Yes, it's because... because oil!" (Does this kid even know what oil is?)

"Why are they fighting over oil?"

"You don't know? Because Libya has a lot of oil, and other places don't, so they all want to grab it—it's very expensive."

"Who are 'they'? Which countries?"

"Britain, America, and anyway, a lot..."

"Is China grabbing too?"

"China doesn't need to grab; China also has a lot of oil!"...

Is this something a child under six years old should know? Actually, I feel embarrassed to admit, all I know is that Libya is at war, but as for the reasons and which countries are involved in the conflict, I have no idea at all. Haha, I'm ashamed...

This is just someone who doesn't care and stays aloof—me!

Scenario 4

Time: 4:45 PM on Wednesday, February 23rd

Location: Kindergarten, Home

Characters: Mom, Qin Yutong, Homeroom Teacher Ms. Liu

Event: Today, I picked up Qin Yutong a bit late. When I arrived at the kindergarten, only a few children were left reading books. Ms. Liu saw me, found an excuse to send Qin Yutong away, then quietly told me that during her class in the afternoon, she noticed that the pinyin letters written in the morning had been erased by half by some unknown child. She got angry and asked everyone to report who did it. Initially, no one spoke. Finally, pressured, a child reported that Qin Yutong did it. She asked Qin Yutong if it was true, and the child immediately teared up, crying loudly and repeatedly claiming it wasn't his fault. Ms. Liu said that Qin Yutong is a new child in the kindergarten, unlike other children who have known her for three years, so she understands them very well. She didn't want to blame him based on one child's word, but if it was indeed him, he should have the courage to take responsibility. She asked me to investigate the truth from another angle once we returned home.

After arriving home, I played with Qin Yutong for a while, deliberately choosing a relaxed atmosphere before starting my inquiry.

Mom: "Tiantian, did you have fun at kindergarten today?"

Tiantian: "Yeah, I made a new friend. His name is Dong Zhuoyan, he's a boy, I call him Doggyhead."

Mom: "Great, you made a new friend. But why do you call him Doggyhead? That's very impolite!"

Tiantian: "Because he looks as cute as a dog!"

Mom: "I heard...you cried in kindergarten today!"

Tiantian glanced at me, "No, I didn't!"

Mom: "Now that you're growing up, you don't tell mom the truth anymore!"

Tiantian hesitated for a long time, still unwilling to speak.

Mom: "Actually, mom knows what happened today. It's not serious. The teacher didn't mean to criticize you; she just hopes you'll be honest. Mom won't criticize Tiantian either, but I hope Tiantian can tell mom the truth!"

Tiantian: "It wasn't me. I didn't see who did it."

Mom: "Tiantian, come on, look into mom's eyes. Mom believes in you."

This time, Tiantian confidently replied, "Mom, it really wasn't me. I didn't go near the blackboard all day."

Mom: "So, that child must have mistaken. Why did you cry then? If you didn't do anything wrong, you could have just told Ms. Liu."

Tiantian: "The teacher...was too scary, I was afraid!"

Little man, yet so timid!

Scenario 5

Time: 7:30 PM on Wednesday, February 23rd

Location: Home

Characters: Mom, Qin Yutong

Event: Accompanying Tiantian doing homework for a while, I still don't understand why these children in kindergarten have to learn not only addition and subtraction but also multiplication and division. Do they really understand?

Addition and subtraction are simple, Tiantian has learned them before, so we finished that book in just two days. But the multiplication and division exercises we currently have, we've only managed a few pages after N days, because Tiantian hasn't learned them before. I have to teach him while he does the exercises. Division is slightly easier since the teacher reviewed it this semester, but multiplication is like alien text to children. They recognize it, but it doesn't recognize them. For us, a problem that seems extremely simple, I've explained seven or eight times, but Tiantian still doesn't understand, looking blankly at me. Maybe the explanation method isn't right. Sigh~ Clearly, I don't have the talent to be a teacher.

Mom: "Tiantian, do you understand this question?"

Tiantian: "Yes."

Mom: "Do you really understand? If you don't, just say so. Mom will explain until you understand, without getting angry. But, you can't pretend to understand when you don't. If you do, mom will get very angry!"

Tiantian: "I don't understand, mom, why is it so hard?"

Mom: "It's not hard. Maybe mom didn't explain it well!"

Tiantian: "Hmm, maybe. I always forget. Ah~ Getting old, memory isn't good. But sometimes you knock my head, did you knock me dumb?!"

Mom and Tiantian burst out laughing...

Scenario 6

Time: 7:00 PM on Thursday, March 24th

Location: Home

Characters: Mom, Qin Yutong, Milkman

Event: Yesterday morning, after drinking milk, Qin Yutong accidentally broke the milk bottle. His first reaction was to apologize to me, saying sorry. I guess he was afraid I would scold him. Since he admitted his mistake, I didn't have much to say, so I let it go. On the way to kindergarten, I muttered that the milk bottle was broken, and tomorrow we wouldn't be able to return it to the milkman. I didn't know what to do. Later that evening while doing homework, he suddenly asked me, "Mom, you figured out what to do about the milk bottle I broke?" "No," I replied, quite surprised that he was still thinking about this matter. "Then let's write a note to Uncle Milkman and tell him," I suggested. "Tell him what?" I asked intentionally. Qin Yutong answered seriously, "Ask if he needs compensation." Thus, following his dictation, I wrote a small note and placed it in the milk box: "Today's milk bottle was accidentally broken by a child. Sorry. If compensation is needed, please leave a message."

This morning, as soon as Tiantian started dressing, he urged me to check the milk box. Sure enough, there was a reply inside: "Company deducts 0.9 yuan from me." Tiantian paused, curiously asking me, "Mom, the milk bottle was broken by me, so why does it deduct his money?" After I confirmed yes, he stayed quiet for a long time. I thought the matter was settled and decided to put a yuan coin in later that evening, as we couldn't let others bear the cost of our mistake. However, just as we were about to start our homework that evening, Qin Yutong suddenly brought up the issue again.

Tiantian: "Mom, why did that person say the company will deduct his money?"

Mom: "Because he delivers fresh milk every day, and the company doesn't know whether the milk bottle was broken by him or by us. Therefore, whoever breaks it, he has to compensate."

Tiantian: "Then isn't he very pitiful?"

Mom: "Yes, he earns only a few cents per bottle of milk, and if he doesn't earn any money, he still has to pay, it's a loss."

Tiantian: "But the milk bottle was broken by me."

Mom: "Then what should we do? What does Tiantian want to do?"

Tiantian: "Mom, let's compensate him."

Ah? I didn't expect the child to have this thought. I was momentarily speechless. Seeing that I didn't respond, Tiantian thought I didn't agree and hurriedly ran back to his room. Soon, he came out with a fabric drawer. I knew it contained some pocket money given by his grandparents when we were in Zhuzhou. He kept it with him after moving to Nanchang and occasionally showed it off jokingly when we asked him to treat us. "Mom, don't worry about compensating. I'll use my money. The milk bottle was broken by Tiantian." Hearing this, I felt very happy inside. A yuan coin is a trivial matter, whether to compensate or not is also a minor issue, but his ability to bravely take responsibility for his mistakes and actively propose solutions made me feel very comforted. I nodded, and he immediately ran to the study, where he dictated a sentence to me: "The milk bottle was accidentally broken by us. It should be compensated by us. The child asks me to apologize on his behalf!"

After finishing, he and I together placed the money and the note in the milk box and smiled contentedly.

While I was making the bed, Tiantian stood beside me saying it looked nice and wondered why it wasn't put on his bed. I laughed and told him it was for the big bed, not suitable for the small one. He thought for a while and stopped speaking.

In the evening, after bathing him, he read a book and finally decided it was time to sleep. At this moment, he began to act. He ran into our room and said to his dad, "Dad, I want to sleep with you." I don't know what happened next because I went to take a shower, but I know my husband definitely didn't agree, otherwise, when I came out after showering, I wouldn't have faced a sad Qin Yutong. I was puzzled because since coming to Nanchang, the child has been sleeping alone. Even when we were both at home, he never asked to sleep with me. Why did he make such a request today? "Why do you want to sleep with us? You're a brave little warrior and should sleep alone." "But I want to sleep in the new bed. It's so pretty, I want to try it to see if it's comfortable." Oh, I see..."Not allowed. I changed the bedsheet, but the bed itself hasn't changed, so it's still the same." "Can't I just sleep there for a bit?" "No." After refusing, I went to sleep.
