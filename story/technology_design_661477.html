Galliani: Milan Derby will not decide the championship, Balotelli needs love

by mdhopidy on 2012-02-09 09:21:10

Finally, Galliani talked about the problematic youth Balotelli, and the Manchester City player was once again in the media spotlight due to the dart incident. "How to solve his current problems? Mario needs care. Is Milan ready to embrace him with love? There is still time, we will consider it in the future. Love is a good thing for anyone." The vice president of Milan was right, of course, this also includes Pato and Barbara. Like Moratti's statement, Galliani believed that the weekend game would not have a decisive impact on the championship. "Of course there will be some tension, because it's a derby. We've had too many encounters since President Berlusconi took over, but it has been many years since the situation where both Milan teams are competing for the league championship, so it will be more tense than before," said the vice president of Milan. "Will Saturday's derby decide the champion? Not quite. Only if there is a difference of 3 points or less in the last round can we say that. This is an important game, but it is not decisive." Galliani did not forget to point out the threats from Napoli and Udinese. "There are still 8 rounds left in the league, including Napoli and Udinese, who still have the chance to catch up. Anything can happen, didn't we lose points in Bari and Palermo after winning against Napoli and Juventus? Losing the derby but winning the championship? I wouldn't sign up for that choice." Before the derby, Milan Vice President Galliani gave an interview to the media, sharing his views on the topics of interest to the media, the weekend derby will be different compared to the past, but it will not decide the league championship. The vice president of Milan also talked about players like Ibrahimovic, Ganso, and Balotelli. The vice president of Milan also mentioned Ibrahimovic and Ganso, as the Swede was ineligible for this derby due to suspension, while Ganso's agent had already appeared in Milan, and according to 'Milan Sport', they will negotiate with the two Milan clubs. "Would we welcome a reduction in Ibrahimovic's ban from 3 games to 2? I won't comment. Ibrahimovic is our key player, not only for his contributions in attack, but also for his role in defending corners," said Galliani. "Ganso? I don't want to talk about this topic. Will there be negotiations? I don't know..."
