On June 25, 2009, the first breakout match of the national finals of "Happy Girl" lasted until the early morning. Among the 18 contestants, Liu Xijun, the queen of popularity from the Guangzhou singing region, along with talented singers Yu Kewei and Li Xiaoyun, advanced into the top 10 first. Zeng Yike, the controversial contestant who angered Bao Xiaobo due to her off-key performance in previous rounds, once again became the focus of the judges' debate.

During the interview, it was reported that Zeng Yike, the new controversial figure of the 2009 "Happy Girl," became the focal point of the entire evening. She continued to perform her original songs while playing the guitar. During the competition, there were constant issues; not only did she continue to have pitch problems, but she also forgot lyrics, laughed awkwardly, and had microphone angle issues. The judges were clearly divided into two camps regarding her advancement. Judges like Bi Xiaoshi, music director of Ocean Butterflies Music, and executives from Hong Kong and Taiwan record companies such as Emperor Entertainment Group did not approve of her "sheep voice" and cast their votes for Huang Ying or Liu Xijun, who were competing against her.

Mainland judges who supported Zeng Yike highly regarded her original music and fresh stage presence. Representatives of the Zeng Yike support group, such as Shen Lihui and Song Ke, praised her by saying things like "This is the freshest creation I've heard in recent years" and "You are the only singer who moved me today," encouraging Zeng Yike to keep going. Even Gao Xiaosong, who previously stated on his blog that Zeng Yike was unsuitable for the spotlight, changed his stance, first sarcastically saying "Occasionally a line touches your heart, but occasionally another line goes all the way to India," but ultimately gave her the evaluation of "touching hearts."

In the end, Zeng Yike lost by one vote to Liu Xijun and became a candidate for elimination, shattering the earlier rumor that "she would be eliminated in the next round." The remaining 12 contestants, including Zeng Yike, will compete for the final 7 spots in the top 10 during the second match held on the evening of July 3 (the current top 10 has been determined).

On July 3, 2009, Zeng Yike, known as the "Sheep Angel," performed her original work "Leo." The judges grouped Huang Ying and Zeng Yike together for a head-to-head competition. After voting by the 11 professional judges, Zeng Yike defeated Huang Ying and became the eighth member of the national top 10. Her naive singing style and fresh, pure lyrics made Zeng Yike the most controversial contestant in this year's "Happy Girl." This match also received praise from afar by composer Xiao Ke, who wrote "Welcome to Beijing." However, Wu Qixian still expressed dissatisfaction with her vocal skills, and Zeng Yike had to accept the challenge of being placed on standby.

On August 1, in the third match of "Happy Girl," Zeng Yike, who defeated Huang Ying by one vote in the first round PK, could not escape the second round. In a double duel with her good friend Pan Chen and losing, despite showing great resilience towards various public opinions, Zeng Yike couldn't help but burst into tears on the spot. Due to too much controversy, Zeng Yike's defeat in the PK led to half the audience cheering, while many others felt sorrowful. For the departure of this controversial figure, the entire venue almost immediately erupted in commotion. Seemingly having endured too much criticism, facing explanations from He Jiong and the judges, Zeng Yike quickly said: "I love Teacher Shen, I love these four teachers, I don't hate Teacher Bao. You can't stop watching because I'm leaving; you must pay attention to their growth, and I will also pay attention to their growth."

Such is this girl who is devoted to music and never gives up, proving her music time and again in her own way.
