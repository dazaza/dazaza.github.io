Yesterday, Fengtai Court held the second concentrated announcement of sentences for the "71-person card distribution prostitution ring" (on January 18, sentences had been announced for 33 card distributors).

Du, the female ringleader, and her husband Cui were sentenced to 14 years and 13 years in prison respectively for organizing prostitution, and were fined. The other 32 accomplices were sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment ranging from 9 months to 7 years for assisting in organizing prostitution, and were also fined. Four minors were handled separately.

In court, Cui and others claimed they did not know the law well, but felt the sentence was too severe and would appeal. It is reported that this is currently the largest card distribution prostitution gang that has been sentenced in China.

Card distribution prostitution lasted for 4 years

Yesterday morning, 34 defendants (22 men and 12 women) were led into the courtroom one by one by court police. The oldest was 52 years old, and the youngest was 19 years old.

The judge read out the judgment, stating that since 2007, Cui and his wife Du gradually monopolized the card distribution prostitution activities around the Asian Games Village area in Chaoyang District. Under their instructions, card distributors handed out prostitution cards to people coming to Beijing at the entrances of various hotels. After customers contacted the card distributors through the prostitution cards and agreed on the price and location of prostitution, Du and Cui arranged for the prostitutes they managed to provide sexual services. After receiving the money from the customers, Du and Cui deducted a commission and then distributed it to the prostitutes and card distributors. Wan, Zeng, and 33 others either engaged in card distribution prostitution for several years or made prostitution cards for profit.

The court's first-instance judgment was that Du and Cui committed the crime of organizing prostitution and were sentenced to 14 years and 13 years in prison respectively, while simultaneously being deprived of political rights for three years and fined 100,000 yuan. The other 32 people were sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment ranging from 9 months to 7 years for assisting in organizing prostitution, and were also fined. Four accomplices who assisted in organizing prostitution were handled separately due to being minors.

Female ringleader complained about the heavy sentence

"I definitely made a mistake, but it shouldn't be this severe. I will appeal," said Du, the 28-year-old female ringleader, crying after hearing the verdict. "I just introduced some prostitutes, how can it be heavier than many robbery cases?"

At the same time, multiple defendants, including Cui, also stated that although they didn't fully understand the law, they believed the punishment was too severe and would consider appealing. Deputy Chief Zhang Yalin of the Criminal Division of Fengtai Court explained that the sentencing for the 71 people mainly depended on the role they played and the profits they made in the joint crime. For example, Hu employed eight people specifically to distribute prostitution cards and was sentenced to six years in prison; Zhou was sentenced to four years for printing the cards. Additionally, Wan, Zeng, Yang, and five others who had been administratively punished more than twice received harsher penalties and were sentenced to five to seven years in prison.

It was understood that all the involved prostitutes were administratively detained and were not held criminally responsible.

■ Inside Story

Prostitutes paid "fees" and queued up by number to "work"

According to the defendants' confessions, there were three groups distributing cards in the Asian Games Village area, with the Hubei group being the largest, followed by the Northeast group and the Shaanxi group. Du and her husband maintained single-line contact with the prostitutes and card distributors. Every night, the card distributors gathered around various hotels, handing out cards to guests or inserting them into hotel rooms. After a customer called, the card distributor negotiated the price of prostitution based on the class of the hotel where the customer stayed, ranging from 2000 yuan to 500 yuan. After receiving the call from the card distributor, Du and her husband arranged for a dedicated unlicensed taxi driver to pick up and drop off the prostitutes. To meet customer needs, Du specially prepared a book of fake invoices to provide to customers.

Law enforcement personnel revealed that Cui also had foreign prostitutes under him, and the youngest prostitute was underage. For fairness, every night when working, the prostitutes queued up by number to "work," each serving two to three customers per night.

■ Dialogue

"Society's atmosphere is like this, I shouldn't be the typical case"

Du, a 28-year-old woman from Shenyang with only junior high school education, controlled a prostitution gang of nearly 70 people.

Reporter: How did you get into this business?

Du: After graduating from junior high school, I stayed in my hometown until 2007 when I came to Beijing and met Cui who lived next door. Later, I got involved in this business.

Reporter: How do you manage the card distributors and prostitutes?

Du: It's not really management. These people usually work in this field, I just connect them together. As for the prices of prostitution and the commission ratio, these are industry standards.

Reporter: How many prostitutes do you have under you?

Du: Not fixed, there were five during the period when I was caught. Each person served two to three customers a day, sometimes four to five times.

Reporter: How much profit do you make daily?

Du: On good days, I could earn over 5000 yuan a day, and on bad days, only 500 yuan.

Reporter: According to the case file, you earned more than 100,000 yuan in four months from February to June 2011.

Du: Living expenses in Beijing are so expensive, I didn't earn much money. I only took a hundred yuan each time, making about ten thousand to twenty thousand yuan a year.

Reporter: Do you think being sentenced to 14 years is too heavy?

Du: I just introduced a few prostitutes, and got 14 years? There are so many prostitutes in society, why choose me as an example? I know it's wrong, but the crime isn't that serious. The social atmosphere is like this, prostitution has existed throughout history. I don't know the law well, always thinking it's not a big deal.

[Legal Explanation]

Organizing prostitution can result in the death penalty

Regarding Du's belief that her sentence was too heavy, Zhang Yalin explained that organized prostitution has always been a key target of crackdowns in China. The starting sentence for organizing prostitution is five years, with those committing serious offenses receiving more than ten years. Those committing particularly serious offenses may face life imprisonment or even the death penalty.

The sentencing for Du and her husband mainly considered four factors: the long duration of the crime, lasting four years from 2007 to last year; having over ten prostitutes under them; nearly seventy card distributors under them, with a large number of distributed cards; and significant profits, with each prostitute paying 1000 yuan monthly as management fees and hundreds of yuan for each act of prostitution, accumulating a large amount of profit.

■ Case

Easy money from card distribution leads to involving elderly mother

Yang, a 35-year-old man from Hubei, has been distributing cards for four years. Because it was profitable, he not only brought his sister and her husband, his uncle and aunt, but also his 57-year-old mother, and hired two fellow villagers.

In this announcement of sentences, except for Yang's mother who was sentenced to probation due to minor crimes, the other six were sentenced to actual imprisonment, with Yang being sentenced to seven years.

Yang said that in 2008, while working in the Asian Games Village, he met fellow villagers distributing prostitution cards and joined the business. Because it was profitable, relatives from his family also followed, including his mother. "I originally wanted my mother to come to Beijing to help look after my child. Seeing that distributing cards was profitable, my mother also helped me distribute some. Who would have thought she would be implicated?" Yang said.

Yang had been administratively detained four times before. He said, "I always thought distributing prostitution cards wasn't a big deal. Every time I was caught, I was released after detention. Who would have thought this time I would be sentenced to seven years?"

"Now regretting seems too late. My daughter is only ten months old. Thinking about going to prison for this crime, I don't know how I will explain it to her in the future," Yang admitted frankly.

[Case Analysis]

A card distributor saved nearly 60,000 yuan in three months

Presiding Judge Zhang Yalin introduced that this gang alone had more than 20 couples. "Relatives committing crimes have several convenient conditions: avoiding outsiders participating in the flow of ill-gotten gains; facilitating division of labor where some distribute cards and others negotiate prices over the phone; relatives won't fight among themselves, reducing exposure."

Zhang Yalin introduced that the reason for these family-style crimes was partly due to misunderstanding the law, assuming that if caught, they would only be detained, and partly due to the huge profits from card distribution leading to following trends. "The police retrieved the bank transaction records of one card distributor, who deposited 59,000 yuan into his account in just three months."
