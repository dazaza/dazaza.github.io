One person's marriage is not real.

by da2j4g9z5o9 on 2012-02-24 17:56:15

Confidant: Qiu Ping, female, 24 years old, salesperson

Qiu Ping is the weakest mother I've ever seen - at 160 centimeters tall and weighing only 40 kilograms, her small face seems to have only a pair of large eyes left. Her daughter is not yet one year old. Her emaciation is due to exhaustion.

Because of pregnancy, Qiu Ping hastily married Chang Song. After marriage, she discovered that he had a mistress, and she began a life akin to living widowhood - her husband was often absent, ignoring her completely. In those days when tears washed her face daily, she increasingly felt that this marriage was like a trap, as if she was merely a tool for reproduction.

The Uninvited Guest in the Honeymoon

I used to be a teacher in my hometown, but later I came to Wuhan alone to work. Colleagues kept introducing me to friends on video websites, but I always wanted to learn something while young, so I refused repeatedly.

Last year, a colleague kindly said to me, "It's not easy for a girl to be alone outside. Having a boyfriend can provide mutual care. I have a good candidate." This sentence touched my heart, and I didn't refuse. The colleague introduced his situation: he was a bus driver, divorced once because his ex-wife couldn't bear children, but his family conditions were decent. I thought being divorced wasn't a big deal, so I agreed to start dating to see if it would work out.

He was Chang Song. During our first meeting, he said that these past few years, he had been lonely, always wanting to find an honest and responsible person to live with, but due to his divorce history, he was very self-conscious and hadn't found anyone. I said, "It's not your fault. As long as you take responsibility for the family, it's fine." He immediately made a promise.

At the beginning of our relationship, he treated me well, picking me up and dropping me off every day. When he learned that I shared a rented apartment, he suggested I move in with him. He lived alone in a three-bedroom apartment. So, I moved in. Not long after, I became pregnant, and we hastily got married.

After moving in together, I noticed that many of his bills featured a name called "Wang Min," and the first name in his phonebook was also "Wang Min." I was suspicious, but since he didn't explain who she was, I didn't ask much.

On the second day after our wedding, while we were at home, someone suddenly knocked on the door. When I opened it, I felt something was wrong. There were two women; the leading one looked about 40 years old, her face cold and expressionless. She didn't even greet me, walking straight into the bedroom and sitting on our marital bed before lighting a cigarette. I was puzzled. Everyone else comes to congratulate with smiles, but why was she like this? It was as if we owed her a few hundred dollars. I poured them two cups of water. Chang Song seemed embarrassed too, trying to make conversation with the other woman, but she remained silent, smoking with a tight face. The atmosphere in the house was unpleasant, as if a sudden cold snap had hit the warm spring.

After sitting for a while, the two women left. I asked Chang Song who the woman was, and he said she was a former colleague from his bus company. I said, "It's not just a colleague, is she named Wang Min?" Under my persistent questioning, he finally admitted to having an eight-year relationship with her. I was both shocked and angry. He knelt down in front of me, promising never to contact her again, saying he had already divorced once and couldn't let his parents suffer again. I forgave him.

When the colleague introduced Chang Song to me, they only mentioned he was a bus driver. It wasn't until after our marriage that I learned he hadn't worked for several years.

A Marriage Alone

After Wang Min's visit, he changed. Every day, he slept until 11 a.m., ate a little, then went out and didn't return until 3 or 4 a.m. the next morning. I asked him where he had been, and he either said he was helping a friend or looking for work. However, I never saw him bring home a single penny. His parents gave me 400 yuan each month for living expenses. My heart started to worry: how would he support the family if he continued to hang around outside all day?

Moreover, he never introduced me to his friends. Sometimes when he didn't come home at night, his phone was dead, but he preferred to use a public phone rather than borrow a friend's phone to call me. Later, after careful consideration, I guessed the reason: he was afraid I would call his friends to check on his whereabouts.

My pregnancy reaction was particularly severe, vomiting everything I ate, making it impossible to go to work. Considering it was still early in our marriage, friends advised me to give up the child. I hesitated. After all, getting married isn't a small matter. I decided to seriously talk to him. I said, "I want someone who will treat me well." He patted his chest and promised that following him would surely bring happiness.

In reality, he became even worse, leaving me alone at home every day. When I said a few words to him, he got angry. Because I was worried about affecting the child, I always yielded to him whenever he lost his temper. His anger could be heard throughout the building. His mother's health was poor, and I bore all the troubles alone, not daring to tell his parents. One day, when I stood up from the sofa, I suddenly fainted. When I woke up, I couldn't get up for a long time because of my big belly. I was alone at home, calling heaven and earth for help, but no one responded. I lay on the ground for a long time before finally struggling to get up by holding onto the edge of the sofa. Fortunately, my sister-in-law called to ask about my condition that day, and I couldn't help but cry. My sister-in-law was furious, coming over to confront his parents, hoping they could control him. When Chang Song returned, not only did he show no remorse, but he exploded, accusing me of tattling. In front of my sister-in-law, he broke all the chairs in the house.

By late October, I was vomiting severely and eventually coughing up blood. That day, his mother was at my place and panicked, calling him to come back quickly. When he returned, I was still vomiting, but he just coldly watched, unmoving, even making sarcastic remarks: "Your body is so weak! I've never seen anyone pregnant like you!" My heart was chilled to the bone. Later, unable to endure, I decided to go to the clinic for an IV. I had no strength, but he walked ahead without even helping me cross the street.

My daughter was born in February. That evening, before 8 p.m., he said he had things to do and left, leaving my sister alone with me. To strengthen nutrition, I ate something every night during the last period of pregnancy. Since my sister wasn't familiar with the environment, at around 10 p.m., I had to descend the stairs myself, heavily pregnant, to buy food.

When I heard my daughter's first cry, my tears fell, and the doctor thought I was crying out of joy. Chang Song, upon hearing it was a girl, immediately pulled a long face. People in the same ward said, "Your husband treats you so poorly. You seem like such a gentle person, how did you end up with such a husband?" Unfortunately, I had to swallow my tears.

Secrets in the Drawer

Since I became pregnant, he hasn't touched me. After giving birth, he said I needed to wait 100 days, and after 100 days, he claimed he had a cold and was afraid of infecting the baby. We've been sleeping separately ever since.

Pregnancy was my affair alone, and after my daughter was born, it was still my affair alone. My weight after childbirth was 50 kilograms, but within two months, I had slimmed down to 40 kilograms. Friends who visited me, seeing this situation, all said, "I don't know what you're doing, living as a virtual widow. Just divorce him." Everyone, including my parents, believed that he married me just to have a child. But I thought, bearing this child was too difficult, and the child was too young. Let's just leave it at that. Perhaps when the child grows up, he will gradually improve.

However, despite my repeated tolerance and concession, life remained unbearable.

His mobile phone was something I was never allowed to touch, even taking it into the bathroom while showering. This made me suspicious. He was most enthusiastic when he heard I was going back to my parents' home. He would immediately call to arrange transportation, even paying for a taxi if necessary, to send me back as quickly as possible. Once, my parents caught a cold and asked me to visit a few days later, but he said, "What if they catch a cold? Anyway, you are the one taking care of the baby." Yet, he still sent me back the next day. If he didn't have another woman outside, would he act like this?

There was a drawer in the house with a lock, and only he had the key. He carried the key with him all the time, even hiding it under his pillow at night. One summer day, he wore a set of clothes without pockets, and I knew the key must be at home. As soon as he left, I started searching everywhere. Finally, I found the key in the pocket of one of his winter outfits. Opening the drawer, I saw a thick package wrapped in newspaper. Unwrapping it, I found numerous photos of him and Wang Min, their intimacy chilling me to the bone. At that moment, I thought about divorce.

That day, I didn't plan to reveal this. In the evening, his younger brother came to discuss some matters. Later, when our daughter was ready to sleep, I bathed her. Since he was home, I asked for his help. While handing the towel, I accidentally dropped it on the baby's face, and he instantly lost his temper, kicking the basin far away, splashing water everywhere. I defended myself a bit, and he tried to hit me. His younger brother struggled to hold him back, resulting in bruises all over his arms. He cursed at me, telling me to leave. I also got angry and immediately started packing my clothes. His younger brother immediately called their mother over. All my resentment surged to my head, and I said, "Today, I finally found out why he treats me so badly!" I revealed the matter of the photos. He still stubbornly claimed it was something from the past, but he clearly looked deflated. That day, under our insistence, he burned the photos.

But that wasn't the end. Later, I found Wang Min's work ID in his wallet. He said she lent it to him so he could ride the bus for free. I asked, "Why among so many colleagues, only she lent it to you? Are you two still in contact?" He couldn't answer. I finally resolved to divorce.

I found a job and moved out on August 24th.

We went to the Civil Affairs Bureau to handle the divorce agreement. A staff member there said we needed the household registration book. He said, "I'll arrange a car for you to go home and get it now?" He seemed eager. Since the child was less than one year old, we couldn't complete the divorce process at that time.

Later, I met the girlfriend of one of his friends. Upon learning about our situation, she shook her head and said, "His previous wife used to complain to me all the time." Curious, I asked, "Isn't she infertile?" She smirked and said, "Just listen to what they say!" Only then did I learn that Wang Min had posted their合影 at his previous wife's workplace, forcing her to divorce.

Qiu Ping sighed, her expression somewhat bewildered, and said, "In less than a year and a half, I've experienced so much. It feels like a nightmare, and in the end, it was all for nothing." After a moment of silence, she added, "What pains me most is my daughter. I want to keep her with me, but I can't afford to raise her alone. But staying with such an irresponsible father, will she have a happy life? What can I do?"

Handwritten Note: People Shouldn't Be Too Selfish

After listening to Qiu Ping's story, my first feeling was: Isn't this almost the Chinese version of the story of Prince Charles and Diana? Prince Charles married Diana to cover up a relationship that the royal family wouldn't acknowledge, but after marriage, he continued his affair with Camilla, leaving Diana to live alone. Despite Charles being a prince, the British people sympathized more with Princess Diana.

Perhaps, at the beginning of the marriage, Chang Song intended to sever ties with Wang Min through marriage, but the events that unfolded indicated that he failed to do so, instead escalating the situation and turning the marriage into a cover for his extramarital affair, leaving his wife in a sexless marriage. Qiu Ping is a healthy woman with normal emotional and physiological needs. Chang Song's actions are quite selfish and cruel, and he deserves condemnation.
