Online store experience

by anonymous on 2011-03-25 14:10:16

1. As a seller on Taobao, due to the nature of online transactions, personal integrity becomes extremely important. Always remember: do business as a person first. Don't make promises lightly, and once you've made a promise, you must try your best to fulfill it. For example, when a customer asks if a product is in stock, we should confirm it thoroughly instead of waiting for the customer to place an order and pay only to find out that the item is out of stock. Or, when a customer asks about the product's information, but we don't have samples on hand, making it difficult to explain to the customer. This can be fatal or at least quite disadvantageous for the seller.

2. Once issues like the ones mentioned above occur, it’s crucial to contact the customer immediately after confirming the facts. First, admit the mistake to let the customer know that you are a responsible seller who takes responsibility. Then, seek the customer's advice and avoid arguing with them out of frustration. This kind of mindset is not conducive to doing business. Arguing isn’t the goal; both parties want to solve the problem. So, don't make things too tense.

3. If you encounter rude buyers, it’s better to listen more and talk less. Imagine if we were the customer, we would also have a lot of complaints bottled up inside. This is understandable. Therefore, when communicating with customers, we should listen more and talk less. After listening, provide the customer with a satisfactory response based on their concerns and the root cause of the conflict, which will help to calm down their anger or dissatisfaction.

4. Once a solution has been agreed upon with the customer, as a seller, you should actively resolve it without waiting for the customer to follow up. Don’t put yourself in a passive position, otherwise, you will end up losing out. Where possible, compensate the customer even if it means some financial loss. Because everyone knows, acquiring a new customer is much harder than maintaining a loyal old one!

5. In daily store operations, always think from the customer's perspective. By anticipating what the customer wants, you turn potential passivity into proactivity. From the customer's point of view, you become a trustworthy seller. I hope this apology letter can reach more Taobao sellers so they can learn from it and avoid or minimize such mistakes in their store operations, showcasing a vibrant and responsible store to everyone.

Here, I mainly discuss two insights regarding communication with customers: principles of communication and methods of communication.

1. Communication must be timely.

In online store operations, since sellers and buyers do not communicate face-to-face, it is important to actively respond to questions raised by buyers promptly. Do not drag your feet or keep customers waiting, because there may be many other stores selling similar products at competitive prices. Therefore, never keep customers waiting, build trust with them. When a buyer sends a message and does not receive a reply, they might feel frustrated. Since buyers aren't always online and may not have time to wait for your reply, it’s advisable to leave contact details and explanations when stepping away from the computer, avoiding making the buyer feel neglected.

Given how valuable everyone's time is, when serving customers, prioritize saving their time by providing quick services and considering their needs. Look at product displays, procurement, variety, various services, and feedback from the customer's perspective. Try to meet their requirements as much as possible. Of course, unreasonable requests can be ignored, but during the communication process, remain polite and friendly. A good attitude is essential for successful business transactions, and even when faced with unreasonable demands, avoid arguments. Always maintain rational communication.

2. Integrity is the foundation of all business.

Smile first, listen next, praise appropriately, and let your character back you up. Many merchants firmly believe in this saying. Regardless of the industry or profits, integrity should always come first. Even with identical products and prices, different communication styles can lead to different choices by buyers. Sometimes, one party may have better products, yet buyers still choose the other side due to better communication. Both in the sales process and long-term sales benefits, integrity is the cornerstone of success, the very first step in any transaction. Always consider things from the customer's perspective, understand what they need, communicate sincerely, and establish trust. Answer questions earnestly. A good seller must first sell themselves before they can sell their products, gaining customer trust so that they shop confidently.

3. Pay attention to polite language.

Online businesses are service-oriented industries. As sellers, respecting others is crucial whether they buy or not, maintaining a positive image of our store. This time they may not buy, but next time they might. Therefore, use consultative language in communication. For example: "Do you think this works?" or "Is this suitable?" etc. When customers perceive us as polite and cultured, they are more willing to continue the conversation. Politeness is the first key to retaining customers, and no one dislikes polite sellers. On a side note, when dealing with female customers, don’t guess their age, as it’s a sensitive topic.

4. Possess professional product knowledge and offer expert opinions.

Customers always want to buy quality products at reasonable prices. As store owners, we must be very familiar with our products, adept at introducing their advantages, and answering customer inquiries fluently and accurately. Avoid vague or irrelevant answers, understanding your product is the basis of selling well.

5. Clearly state logistics details for customer reassurance and better service.

Customers come from all over the country and world. We cannot be familiar with every logistics situation. After confirming the address with the buyer, also confirm whether the courier you use can deliver there. This way, you save yourself potential trouble and save the customer unnecessary waiting time. A little extra effort brings greater peace of mind.

6. Communicate with different types of customers.

Online shoppers vary greatly, and a single communication method won’t work for everyone. Use appropriate methods depending on the type of buyer. Below are some types of customers:

(1) Uninformed Type. These customers lack true understanding of the product, so they ask the most questions. We need to patiently answer these questions, providing them with comprehensive product information, highlighting the advantages of our products, building trust, increasing their liking for our store, and boosting the likelihood of a sale.

(2) Expert Type. These customers are highly knowledgeable about the product, decisive, and already know what they want. When communicating with them, get straight to the point, don’t exaggerate, focus on practical aspects, and show them that we are straightforward, locking in their trust and increasing the chances of a sale.

(3) Semi-Informed Type

These customers are somewhat challenging, being subjective and believing they know a lot about the product, including cost price and materials. They present themselves as experts in front of us, often saying things like “You can't fool me,” demanding honesty. For these buyers, control your emotions, don’t argue, listen patiently to their views, and then answer their questions patiently. Keep your tone gentle to ensure they see you as a kindred spirit, facilitating easier communication and increasing the chances of a sale.

7. Confirm after completing a transaction.

After successfully concluding a deal with a buyer, it’s not wise to ignore them. Contacting them post-sale avoids losing customers due to neglect. You can use emails or instant messages via platforms like Wangwang. After the buyer confirms payment, send a few concise words or pleasant emojis. Although it takes a bit of time, thorough service leaves a good impression, encouraging repeat visits to your store.

Communication Skills

For a store to operate well, it relies not just on price and quality advantages but also on the seller's service attitude, especially when competing products have similar prices. Service attitude is mostly conveyed through language! When we sit in front of our computers eagerly awaiting customers, how do we better capture their interest? Here, using some clever phrases can help retain them!

● Language Communication Skills

⑴ During communication with customers, word choice is critical. Customers should be addressed as "you" (formal) rather than "you" (informal). The difference between the formal and informal forms lies in the heart behind them, significantly affecting the outcome. I believe most sellers are aware of this, yet many overlook or fail to notice it. Replace all informal "you" with formal "you," ensuring all transactions occur in a respectful and harmonious atmosphere.

Of course, "you" (formal) isn't the only option. Friendly terms like "beautiful lady," "handsome guy," or "dear" can also be used, but moderation is key. Overusing them might make customers feel uncomfortable or even repelled. For instance, "Dear handsome/beautiful..." might be acceptable to some, but not everyone is accustomed to it. If I heard such a phrase upon my first visit to a store, I'd likely feel... a chill running down my spine!

⑵ Respond promptly to buyer inquiries. Don't let customers wait too long, or they might go elsewhere. This requires sellers to be very familiar with their products, mastering the performance, advantages, and attributes of what they sell. However, don't respond too quickly either—maintain a balanced pace. Remember, as a buyer, when I asked about a product at a store, the seller responded so fast it was overwhelming, leaving me speechless with all the chat window filled before I could finish asking. Does this demonstrate fast typing skills? No, it just felt uncomfortable, and I didn't buy anything. The seller could only silently cry!

⑶ When a customer contacts you via Wangwang, be enthusiastic and smiley, even though they can't see you. Wangwang provides many emoticons that sellers should utilize fully. Avoid cold or rigid responses, even if the customer irritates you, unless you're not interested in doing business with them!

Clever language and lively expressions make buyers happy and leave a great impression!

⑷ Fully understand your product, skillfully introduce its advantages, and calmly address its disadvantages. When customers repeatedly inquire about product performance, answer patiently and in detail, avoiding superficial responses.

⑸ Sellers must be honest, truthfully introducing products and services without lying, concealing inherent defects, or exaggerating functions for the sake of a transaction.

⑹ Eliminate customer concerns. Many people worry about online shopping, such as the seller's credibility, product quality, and after-sales service. Sellers should use language techniques to alleviate or reduce these concerns.

For example, if a customer questions product quality due to the seller's low credibility, the seller can respond accordingly.

For hesitant buyers, sellers should proactively persuade them.

If the seller has good credibility and the customer doubts the product quality, the seller can use facts to convince them.

⑺ Maintain continuous and ongoing conversations with buyers, which facilitates closing deals. Long silences can scatter the buyer's thoughts, possibly leading to lost opportunities.

⑻ Appropriately complimenting the other party makes buyers feel pleased, unconsciously falling for the "gentle palm - bone-melting soft palm," shortening the distance between both sides and making communication smoother.

⑼ During communication, when the seller disagrees with the buyer's viewpoint, avoid directly saying "no," which carries strong opposition. Instead, sellers should navigate around this, learning to pivot, with the key being the words "but." First, affirm the buyer's opinion, then gently express disagreement. This shows understanding while winning trust, achieving two goals at once. Never confront directly!

To better grasp the use of "but," here are some safer responses summarized by experienced sellers:

① Yes, but...

② I completely understand your point, and agree with you, but...

③ I really like your idea, but...

④ What you say makes sense, but...

⑤ I understand lower prices would be better, but...

Regardless of the situation, flexible application ensures steady handling!

⑽ Before deciding to purchase an item, buyers may negotiate prices with sellers. Bargaining usually falls into two categories: the price exceeds the buyer's psychological limit, or it's habitual bargaining.

If the item meets the buyer's approval but exceeds their budget, ask the buyer about their desired price range and recommend appropriately priced items from your store.

Most buyers tend to bargain after locking onto a product. Besides wanting a good deal, they enjoy the satisfaction of successful negotiation. Therefore, sellers should leave room for bargaining when setting prices. If prices are set too low, buyers won't experience the satisfaction of negotiating, reducing the likelihood of a sale.

For straightforward buyers, a couple of rounds of bargaining suffice, otherwise, they might feel the seller isn't genuine. New sellers can emphasize "the price is already very low, now reputation is more important than profit, I'm sure you understand."

When dealing with price-sensitive buyers, sellers should prepare mentally, ideally letting the buyer state their desired price first, then reaching a mutually acceptable price.

⑾ After purchasing an item, buyers often worry about shipping times and product quality. Timely inform the buyer: "Thank you for your trust and support. I will ship your item promptly and notify you of the tracking number."

Of course, don't feel disheartened if a customer doesn't buy. Maintain a proper attitude, politely bid farewell like a physical store clerk would, saying "Welcome to visit again," leaving a good impression. Keep a normal and peaceful mindset, even if the sale doesn't happen, aim to let the customer recognize the seller's character, allowing the online store to operate sustainably.

⑿ After a buyer purchases an item, don't assume everything is fine. Post-sale service is equally important. Actively provide the shipping tracking number to the buyer without waiting for them to ask, remember this point!

⒀ Before the buyer confirms receipt of the goods, sellers should maintain communication to lay the groundwork for future evaluations. Thank the buyer via Wangwang messages or email, giving them a delivery reminder (Note: not the "confirm receipt" prompt on the store page). Enthusiastic service will subtly leave a lasting impression on the buyer.

⒁ Don't forget customers, nor let them forget you. Completing one transaction marks the beginning of the next. Regularly revisit completed customers, sending holiday greetings or informing them about product maintenance and pairing tips, giving reasons for follow-ups.

● Taboos in Communication

Avoid certain phrases that could lead to misunderstandings or biases, preventing awkward situations. Otherwise, the losses fall on the seller.

Don't discuss topics involving the other party's privacy, such as "Why don't you buy this piece of clothing? Is it because of your figure...". The rest is left to your imagination!

Don't rush the other party, such as "Please decide quickly" or "Please reply immediately," especially for new sellers. Everyone knows "even hanging requires a breath" right? Conducting a transaction is not easy, so sellers often hope for quick closure, but they must maintain a calm attitude, not urging the other party, which could irritate or even doubt them. I once encountered a seller who kept pushing me despite my intention to buy, until I hesitated due to a phone call. The seller kept pressing: "Are you buying or not?" "Hurry up, limited quantity, don't miss the chance!" Dizzy... I ended up doubting and passing on the purchase. Isn't this a loss of business and wasted time and energy? So, please be mindful of this!

Don't speak in anger. If after prolonged communication, the buyer still nitpicks and hesitates, don't rush to frustration. Be patient and avoid angry remarks like "I've given you the lowest price, buy it or not, it's up to you." — "Then I won't buy it!" Right?

Don't speak in self-centered ways. Using such language might let buyers understand your point of view, but excessive use loses persuasiveness, like "My opinion is..." "In my view..." "If I were you..." But if the "I" is replaced with "You," a subtle change drastically improves effectiveness. Agree?

Don't use extreme language. Phrases like "Absolutely," "Definitely," "Absolutely no problem," etc., sound exaggerated and boastful even if correct. Avoid using such vocabulary!

Don't shift topics. If the buyer's question is about A, don't voluntarily switch to B, causing unnecessary trouble!

Believe that following the above points can leave a good impression on every customer and increase their goodwill towards you, establishing a good seller image for yourself! Among the customers I’ve served, whether they bought or not, their evaluations often mention: the seller/owner is very nice and friendly, sincere in attitude, and warm and responsible. This gives you an opportunity for future cooperation, right?
