A Major Life Decision

I’ve only been in Wuhan for over a month, yet I have already gone through a series of major life events such as renting a place, getting married, and finding a job.

Two months ago, I never thought this would be my future life.

That day was a Saturday, and it was a sunny day in Shanghai. Xiao Wan looked panicked: "Something happened, I have to go home." He was my and Pi Pi's roommate, a 32-year-old handsome guy from Wuhan.

"What happened now?" I was lazily listening to my MP3 while leaning on Pi Pi.

"My mom is sick; she is my only family member, I must go back."

Xiao Wan's parents divorced when he was young, and his mother raised him alone. His mom was disabled, with difficulty moving her limbs, but still managed to raise Xiao Wan to be healthy and strong.

Pi Pi said, "Can't you just hire a caregiver for your mom?"

"You're talking out of your ass, some things can't be bought with money." In a rush, I blurted out a sentence in Hunan dialect. My hometown is a small city in Hunan, and I've been drifting outside for nearly ten years. For survival, I changed identities like a chameleon. Even my boyfriend Pi Pi doesn’t know that I once worked as a maid, beer salesperson, or mall cashier. Even during our relationship, I was merely working as a purchaser in a foreign supermarket, not a financial director of a multinational company.

Xiao Wan thanked me for speaking up for him: "Pai Pai understands me." "She may understand you, but she’s still my wife," Pi Pi teased him. I quickly changed the subject: "Xiao Wan, how long will you be gone this time?" "Maybe I'll come back after one or two months, or maybe I won't come back at all."

Suddenly, I felt a sense of desolation as if losing something important. Besides Pi Pi, there wasn't much else for me to miss in Shanghai. And even Pi Pi couldn’t give me a sense of security.

Without thinking, I blurted out, "Xiao Wan, let me go home with you." I knew Xiao Wan liked me.

This was a serious decision in my life, but it was made in an instant.

The Three of Us in Shanghai

Pi Pi was a doctoral student at a university in Shanghai. Two years ago, he found me because of a post I made online about co-living with someone of the opposite gender. I became his live-in girlfriend under false pretenses. Xiao Wan was a colleague of a fellow townsman of Pi Pi’s, who moved in with us after struggling to find a place after changing jobs.

Nowadays, Pi Pi's requirements for co-living with someone of the opposite gender are "Bachelor's degree or above, Master's preferred." I lied to him, saying I graduated with a bachelor's degree, and he didn't ask to see my diploma. Soon, he started pursuing me. At first, I thought it was because I was beautiful, but later I learned that Pi Pi had just gone through a breakup, and I filled his emotional void.

My parents and sisters all thought I hit the jackpot by finding a PhD boyfriend in Shanghai. They don't know that I live my life like acting in a play, always fearing the day Pi Pi discovers my true identity.

Xiao Wan was a pastry chef, often making various pastries for us. He once said that his greatest life wish was to marry a wife. When he said that, his eyes were shining brightly at me. Pi Pi saw right through it and chased after him, threatening to beat him: "You know, a friend's wife is off-limits." "Legally speaking, Pai Pai is still unmarried. If you can pursue her, why can't I?" The two men chased each other around the house, laughing and joking, while I laughed so hard I almost cried.

I never expected that Xiao Wan would show up at the entrance of my supermarket with his bread waiting for me after work. I was stunned. Xiao Wan smiled kindly: "Don't worry, I won't tell Pi Pi. I just want you to taste my bread first, then we can go home together and watch videos." It seemed that this guy had been tracking me for more than a day or two.
