Tutorial for rooting Android 2.2 devices

by dream2012 on 2012-02-23 11:12:19

Many Android phone users have encountered problems when trying to gain root access after upgrading to the official Android 2.2 system. Various rooting tutorials and methods available online are not compatible with Android 2.2. To obtain root access, one must first downgrade the system to Android 2.1 or an earlier version before performing the rooting process. This method is not only cumbersome but also prone to issues, causing many users to hesitate. However, this problem has been resolved with the release of Unrevoked3. Unrevoked is a well-known Android one-click root tool known for its simplicity and minimal risk. Starting from version 3.2, Unrevoked supports one-click rooting for phones that have already upgraded to the official Android 2.2, including models like the Sprint EVO 4G, Droid Incredible, HTC Desire (GSM and CDMA), HTC Aria, Droid Eris, and HTC Wildfire. Below is a tutorial on how to use Unrevoked3 to obtain root access for Android 2.2 devices:

### Installation Instructions:

1. **Install HBOOT driver**: Before using Unrevoked, you need to install the Windows HBOOT driver. If you already have HTC Sync installed on your computer, uninstall it first and restart your computer. Follow these steps:

2. **Download unrEVOked modified USB driver**: Download the unrEVOked modified USB driver file and extract it to your computer. Remember the location where you extracted the driver.

3. **Enter HBoot mode**: Turn off your phone and press the power button and volume down button simultaneously to enter HBoot mode.

4. **Connect your phone to the computer via USB**: Connect your phone to the computer using a USB cable. You will see a notification in the lower-right corner of your Windows screen about HBOOT USB connection and driver installation. Ignore it.

5. **Open Device Manager**: Right-click "My Computer" (or "Computer" for Windows 7 users) on your desktop, select "Manage," and then click "Device Manager."

6. **Locate unknown device**: Under "Other Devices" in the Device Manager, you will see an unknown device labeled "Android 1.0."

7. **Update driver**: Right-click "Android 1.0" and select "Update Driver." Choose "Browse my computer for driver software" and then click "Browse" to locate the directory where you extracted the Windows HBOOT driver. Click "Install." If you receive any warning messages during the installation, click "OK" to proceed.

8. **Verify driver installation**: Once the driver is installed, the "Other Devices" section in the Device Manager will show "Android Phone" with a corresponding "Android Bootloader Interface" entry, indicating successful driver installation.

### Rooting Process:

1. **Access Unrevoked website**: Open the [Unrevoked](http://unrevoked.com/) website and select your phone model.

2. **Confirm your Android model**: After selecting your phone model, confirm the details on the next page. If everything is correct, click the "unrevoked3" link to go to the download page.

3. **Download Unrevoked program**: On the download page, click the "Download for Windows" link to download the Windows program (reflash_package.exe).

4. **Run the program**: Run the reflash_package.exe program. For Windows 7 users, right-click the application and select "Run as Administrator."

5. **Enable USB debugging**: Follow the instructions on the program interface to enable USB debugging on your Android phone. To do this, go to Settings > Applications > Development and check the "USB debugging" option. The program also reminds you to uninstall HTC Sync, so double-check this step.

6. **Connect your phone via USB**: Connect your phone to the computer via USB to start the final rooting process. After connecting, the Unrevoked program will display a prompt informing you that the rooting operation is about to begin, along with some associated risks. Click "OK" to proceed.

7. **Automated rooting process**: The Unrevoked program will now perform the rooting process. During this time, your phone will reboot twice and display some prompts. The entire process is automatic, so avoid interacting with your phone to prevent failure. The process typically takes around 2 minutes to complete.

8. **Completion**: Once the process is done, the Unrevoked program will display "Done," and your phone will automatically reboot into the system.

9. **Verify root access**: If everything went smoothly, your phone should now have root access. You can verify this by checking if the "Superuser Permissions" app appears on your phone. Now, you can search the Market for root-only apps or start experimenting with custom ROMs. Enjoy!
