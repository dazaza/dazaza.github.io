Qingfeng City, a place that makes martial artists pale when mentioned. No one knows where this city is, but the people who come out from this city are not only stunningly beautiful but also skilled in music, chess, calligraphy, painting, fortune-telling, astrology, and more. Even more chilling is their martial arts prowess. Someone once witnessed the skills of Mudan, the hall master of Peony Hall, but they were never able to speak again. When questioned by martial artists, those in the know only showed expressions of terror, with their hair standing on end. Others have seen the enchanting lightness of Yuebing, the hall master of Flying Flower Hall. In just the blink of an eye, as flower scents wafted through, her figure would disappear.

If these were just rumors, then the thousand-face transformation skills of Yuxin, the hall master of Charm Hall, would be enough to make everyone tremble with fear. No one knows if a close friend or a beloved beauty might suddenly stab you and then laughingly tell you, "I am a demon, a demon in the rain."

Thus, such an organization in the martial world inspires no less than absolute respect, though this respect is laced with inner fear.

(1)

On the tenth day of the tenth lunar month, late autumn, slightly cool. The maple leaves were red like blood, spinning and falling onto the roof of the tavern. Ziyeh sat by the street side in the tavern, lazily leaning against the wall, one leg stretched out resting on the bench, carelessly watching the bustling crowd on the street. He always had that same laid-back appearance.

This city seemed tranquil, no different from any other. Yet, the world often works this way: the quieter the water, the more turbulent the hidden currents beneath. Ziyeh knew that here was the main base of Qingfeng Sect.

On the table, two pots of huadiao wine had already been emptied, yet the person he awaited had not arrived. Ziyeh was growing impatient, shifting his back away from the wall, straightening his waist, bending his numb leg off the bench, and changing his sitting posture. Now, he could see anyone entering or leaving the tavern door.

He knew that the person he awaited would never break their promise. Through many years of interaction, they had developed an unspoken understanding. Despite her coldness and aloofness that deterred others, it was only so for others.

As the third pot of huadiao wine neared its end, Ziyeh smiled gently. His smile was because of a woman who appeared at the tavern door, adorned with vermilion hairpins and silver ornaments, her snow-white robes trailing on the ground, her skin as smooth as congealed fat, white like snow. Although it was still deep autumn, the desolate chill she emitted made all the tavern's patrons involuntarily shiver. Truly, she lived up to her name—Xueluo, the one he had been waiting for this trip.

No one saw how she arrived; at this moment, she silently appeared like a snowflake, lightly landing by Ziyeh's wine table.

"You've come." Ziyeh's gentle smile was as calm as the weather in March, just like many years ago.

Seeing Ziyeh's familiar smile again, Xueluo's heart suddenly ached, memories of those tender and intimate times vividly returning. Xueluo clearly remembered the day he left, when the sky was filled with falling snow. From that day on, she changed her name to Xueluo.

Yes, she, formerly known as Shuluo, and Ziyeh loved each other deeply. But due to societal norms and family traditions, they were forced apart. Now, Ziyeh was a father, while she remained alone, biding her time.

"You've grown thinner." Xueluo opened her lips, her voice melodious like a nightingale.

"Is that so? But I don't feel it." Ziyeh retracted his smile, his face now filled with frost. Yes, if it weren't for his master, the former head of Qingfeng Sect, and also Xueluo's father, who opposed them, they should have become a celestial couple.

"Speak then, why did you urgently summon me?" Ziyeh's tone was still very stiff.

Tears welled up in Xueluo's eyes but she managed to hold them back. She knew that over the years, Ziyeh had not been happy, even somewhat dispirited. But what could she do? Her father had said that she was destined with a curse, and if she married, her husband would surely meet an untimely death. However, could she tell Ziyeh this? No, she couldn't. She could only keep this secret to herself.

"A few of the Demoness' transformation manuals have been stolen." Xueluo slowly sat down.

"What does that have to do with me?" Ziyeh's expression remained devoid of emotion.

"Of course it has something to do with you." On the windowsill, a shadow laughed and looked at Ziyeh and Xueluo. Clad in bright red attire, her delicate green embroidered shoes swayed freely in the autumn wind.

"Mudan, can't you come down and talk?" Xueluo's lips curved into a faint smile.

All martial artists in the jianghu knew that within Qingfeng Sect, there were two most dazzling flowers—one as cold as ice, the other as fiery as fire. These were Mudan and Xueluo.

So Ziyeh also knew this.

But Ziyeh often wondered how such a cold and hot pair could get along so harmoniously.

"First, the stolen transformation manuals are the fruits of your master's labor. Second, this book records some secrets about Luo'er. What they are, I will naturally tell you after you find those manuals." After saying this, Mudan playfully blinked at Xueluo.

Xueluo lowered her long lashes, appearing graceful.

Ziyeh glanced at Mudan, then turned to look at Xueluo. Heaving a heavy sigh, it seemed that he would have to take on this mission.

2

The sixteenth day of the tenth lunar month. The setting sun cast its glow, and the full trees of withered yellow leaves trembled in the wind. Autumn was nearing its end.

Within the main base, the usually unseen leader, Ximen Chuixue, paced back and forth anxiously. The deputy leader, Zhulin Qingke, sat solemnly in a corner. They knew that if the secrets of Qingfeng Sect were discovered by the martial world, then Qingfeng Sect would surely face a calamity.

Everyone in Qingfeng Sect knew that the so-called transformation manuals were not as simple as they seemed. They contained all the sect's martial arts secrets and confidential documents.

Among the stolen manuals were not only the secrets of the Holy Maiden Xueluo, but also the sword techniques of the Ximen family. Back in the day, the old ancestor Chuixue wielded a sword that roamed the martial world for decades without ever encountering an equal. Now, alas! Thinking about this, Ximen Chuixue couldn't help but let out a long sigh.

"Has the hall master of Dianhua Hall, An Zhilan Ting, arrived?" Zhulin Qingke's raspy voice broke the tense silence.

"Long ago." As the sound faded, the person appeared.

An Zhilan Ting's Dianhua Hall was responsible for daily inspections of all branches of Qingfeng Sect. With urgent news calling for immediate recall, it was clear that something significant had occurred. Thus, An Zhilan Ting had returned without rest several days prior.

"How is Ziyeh's investigation progressing?" Zhulin Qingke asked again in his raspy voice, like bamboo being peeled.

"It seems he hasn't done much, just wandering around every day, walking, eating, and sleeping."

"Are Mudan and Xueluo always by his side?"

"Yes, they haven't stepped away even half a step." An Zhilan Ting's response was both playful and sharp.

"Hmm, understood. You may retire early."

"Understood." A fragrant breeze passed, and An Zhilan Ting's elegant figure was already gone.

"Chuixue, can we really trust Ziyeh?" Zhulin Qingke turned back.

"Perhaps. He was the former leader's prized disciple, and only left Qingfeng Sect in anger because of his relationship with Xueluo. Now that this involves Xueluo, how could he not give his all? Besides, Ziyeh's tracking skills are unparalleled under heaven. To my knowledge, no one has ever escaped him." Ximen Chuixue gazed at the increasingly darkening twilight, feeling myriad emotions.

"Hmm." In the darkness, Zhulin Qingke's eyebrows twitched slightly.

Night fell, enveloping Qingfeng Sect in darkness.

In the southeast corner room, a shadow darted out, disappearing in a few swift leaps. If the enchanting lightness of Yuebing, the hall master of Feihua Hall, was considered a top-level expert, then this person's lightness skill would be ranked as a top-tier expert.

Indeed, this shadow was Ziyeh. After several days of covert investigation, he had uncovered many suspicious points. First, Meihall was heavily guarded; not only could ordinary people not enter, but even a fly would find it hard to pass through its multiple lines of defense. Secondly, the thief specifically targeted certain books containing Qingfeng Sect's secrets, which would require insider knowledge to achieve such precision. Therefore, these few days, he could only investigate at night to avoid alarming the suspects prematurely.

3

The twentieth day of the tenth lunar month. Morning light pierced through the autumn mist. Chrysanthemums bloomed gracefully.

"Truly beautiful!" Ziyeh marveled at Qingfeng Sect enveloped in mist. How long had it been since he last came here? Five years? Ten? He didn't dare to calculate.

"Hehe! Then don't return to your Peach Blossom City. Stay and accompany Luo'er." On the pavilion eaves, Mudan's delicate green embroidered shoes swung. She always appeared unexpectedly.

"Mudan, what have you said about me?" Xueluo glanced sideways at Ziyeh, her face flushed with embarrassment as she turned away.

Ziyeh suddenly felt a pang in his heart, a slight ache in his nose. It wasn't that he didn't want to, but some things were beyond his control.

"Can you perform Peach Branding?" Mudan persisted relentlessly.

"Hmm."

"Great! Hehe! Luo'er is saved."

"How?" Ziyeh was confused.

"Sigh! Destiny!" Mudan sighed deeply before continuing: "Have you ever heard of the legend of the Holy Maiden of Qingfeng Sect?"

"No."

In the depths of Mudan's gaze, there was a misty haze: "It is said that every fifty years, a Holy Maiden is born into Qingfeng Sect. They are fated with a curse, and if they marry, they will surely bring misfortune upon their husbands. Each Holy Maiden bears a plum blossom brand on their right shoulder. Among the four Holy Maidens Qingfeng Sect has seen, two who defied the curse and married died suddenly within three days. The following two chose to live alone till the end of their lives. However, it is recorded that only the Heart Sutra of Peach Branding can lift the curse. Unfortunately, the art of Peach Branding has been lost, and all efforts to find it have been fruitless. Hence, the former leader forbade Luo'er from marrying you, truly out of necessity."

"Who would have thought that after you left, you learned Peach Branding. Isn't that destiny?" Mudan's tone shifted, her smile blossoming, charming and captivating.

Ziyeh was stunned. He never imagined that everything back then was actually for his own good. His angry departure led him to destroy Qingfeng Sect's martial arts, leading him into a valley where he accidentally learned Peach Branding—a skill that had been lost for generations.

In an instant, all resentment, grievances, and doubts dissipated like the morning mist. Some things were not as simple as they seemed. Ziyeh's eyes became slightly moist.

"Luo'er, you've suffered injustice." Looking at the tearful Xueluo, Ziyeh was filled with remorse. He recalled his harsh attitude towards her over the years.

"We know that you will eventually discover that the transformation manuals were stolen by us, but why were Chuixue's sword technique manuals also lost?" Xueluo wiped away her tears, stepping forward to change the subject.

"Yes! There is a traitor within Qingfeng Sect, taking advantage of the situation to frame us. In a hurry, Xueluo thought of asking you to investigate." Mudan spoke quickly.

Watching the gradually dimming courtyard of Qingfeng Sect, the pale yellow chrysanthemums swayed gently in the morning breeze. Ziyeh's lips curled slightly upwards, his brows furrowed, and a figure emerged in his mind.

4

The twenty-fifth day of the tenth lunar month. Drizzling autumn rain battered the courtyard, tearing the chrysanthemums into tatters.

Qingfeng Sect was filled with the various hall masters, all whispering among themselves. Only the two leaders maintained grave expressions, silent.

Amidst the fallen flowers, Ziyeh and Xueluo approached slowly.

"Ziyeh, according to Mudan, have you uncovered the truth?" Chief Leader Ximen Chuixue's tone lacked the usual gravity.

The entire hall was astonished, except for Deputy Leader Zhulin Qingke, whose demeanor betrayed unease.

"Yes, the truth is clear." Ziyeh retrieved the Holy Maiden Scripture from his bosom.

"How is there only one copy?" The various charming hall masters were momentarily dumbfounded, then suddenly erupted in clamor.

"Indeed, it is this Holy Maiden Scripture that triggered the simultaneous theft of the other volumes." Ziyeh's gaze swept across the hall, fixing on Zhulin Qingke, who feigned calmness.

"Regarding the legend of the Holy Maiden of Qingfeng Sect, surely the various hall and chief leaders understand better than I do!" Ziyeh continued calmly: "This scripture is useless to others, but for the Holy Maiden of Qingfeng Sect, it is a treasure. Therefore, Xueluo stole this book to free herself from her cursed fate."

"Hmm... That is understandable." Ximen Chuixue nodded slightly.

"What about the other volumes?" Yuxin, who had been silent, finally spoke.

"Good question. This matter requires an explanation from your deputy leader, Zhulin Qingke." Ziyeh smiled, his gaze sharp like an arrow, directly targeting the deputy leader Zhulin Qingke.

Under the collective gaze, Zhulin Qingke's complexion shifted from greenish to ghostly white. He knew that a fleeting greed had plunged him into an abyss of eternal regret.

"Let me explain." Two pairs of feet—one in light green embroidered shoes and another in pink—appeared before the assembly. It was Mudan and An Zhilan Ting. In Mudan's hand were the stolen volumes, including the Holy Maiden Scripture held by Ziyeh, matching exactly the number of stolen books: "That night, Xueluo and I quietly infiltrated the secret chamber of Meihall and stole the Holy Maiden Scripture. Unexpectedly, we were spotted by Zhulin Qingke on patrol. Seizing the opportunity, he went further, stealing the sword techniques of Chuixue that he had long coveted, along with some transformation manuals containing Qingfeng Sect's secrets."

"Later, I informed Ziyeh that only three people could enter Meihall—the chief leader, the deputy leader, and the hall master Yuxin. Coincidentally, that night, it was supposed to be Deputy Leader Zhulin Qingke on patrol. So Ziyeh deduced that it must have been the deputy leader who committed the theft himself." Mudan paused to clear her throat: "With the permission of the chief leader, An Zhilan Ting and I entered the deputy leader's bedroom and found these transformation manuals."

As soon as Mudan finished speaking, Zhulin Qingke sprang up from his seat, dissipating like a wisp of smoke toward the door.

However, faster figures blocked his path—Ziyeh, Xueluo, and An Zhilan Ting from Dianhua Hall.

"Let him go!" Ximen Chuixue's voice was filled with resignation. After all, having interacted for many years, how could he bring himself to enforce the sect rules?

5

The eleventh month, winter arrived. The first snowflakes drifted gently down. Plum blossoms exuded fragrance, swaying gracefully outside the study window.

The plum blossom on Xueluo's right shoulder was gradually fading.

"Luo'er, congratulations! You are finally free from the constraints of fate." Mudan remained as brash as ever.

Xueluo's eyes carried a blush, her powdered cheeks turning red: "Where is Ziyeh?"

"Oh! Haven't seen him for several days. Could he have left?"

Tears streamed down Xueluo's eyes. He had indeed left. Even if she was no longer the Holy Maiden, what could it matter if the person she loved was not by her side? All worldly affairs passed like a melancholy dream.

Outside Qingfeng City, on the snowy path leading to Peach Blossom City, a trail of footprints extended solitarily toward the horizon...
