What are the methods for making Hunan Wugang preserved vegetables?

by hwwkfgby on 2012-03-06 11:50:59

If you want a simple way to make Hunan Wugang cold dishes, you can go to the supermarket to buy seasonings for making cold dishes. Add chili peppers, five-spice powder, sugar, etc., according to your personal taste. To preserve the brine pot well, you should first wash the ingredients to be braised. Pig stomachs and large intestines are the hardest to wash. You can use a small amount of dry flour to remove dirt, then rinse with clean water, and put them in the brine pot to braise. There are many ways to make Hunan Wugang cold dishes [http://www.zglcjyw.com/].

After the cold dishes have cooled, a layer of sesame oil can be applied on the surface to prevent the surface of the cold dishes from drying out and changing color. For older-textured cold dishes, they can be immersed in the brine pot after braising, and taken out as needed.

For storing brine, earthenware containers should be used. The floating oil on the surface of the brine should be in appropriate amounts, and any excess should be removed. In summer, the brine should be boiled twice a day, placed in a container, and stored in a fixed location that is shaded, ventilated, dry, and not easily subject to collisions. The boiled and cooled brine can also be poured into ceramic containers, sealed with cling film, and stored in the refrigerator.

Example of making cold dishes —— Braised Pig Tongue:

Place the pig tongue in a pot with water and boil for 15 minutes. Remove it and use a knife to scrape off the white skin on the pig tongue; place water and ginger in the pot, add the pig tongue and simmer for 40 minutes, then take out the pig tongue and set aside; bring the brine pot to a boil, add the pig tongue, and simmer slowly for about 30 minutes before removing; let it cool, slice it, and it's ready to eat.
