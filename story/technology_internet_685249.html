Man uses blank retirement certificates to defraud 42 people of over 1.2 million yuan

by xue94fwsh on 2012-03-01 14:29:00

Wu Jun used the embezzled money to buy private housing, furniture, clothes, repair his parents' graves, and lend to others. The rest of hundreds of thousands of yuan were spent on gambling and extravagance. Prosecutors introduced that before the case broke out, the two victims worried about being defrauded and demanded their money back. Wu Jun returned 63,000 yuan. Chongqing Morning Post reporter learned from Fengdu County Procuratorate yesterday that Wu Jun would spend the next 12 Spring Festivals in prison.

Wu Jun from Fengdu who worked at the Social Security Bureau discovered a way to make money. By filling out blank retirement certificates for fraud, he was detained for stealing cars multiple times and only gained 7.7 yuan. In nearly two years, he indeed made a fortune, defrauding 42 people of over 1.2 million yuan. However, he faced imprisonment.

Around March 2010, after Wu Jun started working at the Fengdu County Human Resources and Social Security Bureau administrative service center window, he found that he could take advantage of the loose management of the unit and his own work convenience to obtain blank retirement certificates. Without going through the legal approval process, he privately filled out these blank retirement certificates and stamped them with the steel seal and official seal of the Fengdu County Human Resources and Social Security Bureau, thus generating the idea of using these fake retirement certificates to defraud others of their money.

Prosecutors introduced that in order to prevent others from discovering that the retirement certificates he handled were invalid and that pensions could not actually be collected, Wu Jun regularly deposited 1,150 to 1,180 yuan into the bank accounts of the individuals he had falsely processed retirements for until the case broke out. A total of 165,930 yuan was deposited.

Chongqing Morning Post reporter Feng Chao shares: welcome to comment and review. Two years of indulgence exchanged for a 12-year sentence.

Wu Jun knew this was illegal, but he thought about it and believed that since he had already gotten involved, most of the money he had defrauded had been used up. He could only continue to defraud more money under the pretense of processing retirement certificates to maintain his scam. If one day there was insufficient funds and he couldn't transfer money to them, or if the truth eventually came out, all he could do was go to jail.

Since he was indeed a staff member of the Human Resources and Social Security Bureau and worked at the administrative service center, they trusted him even more, leading to the defrauding of so many people's money.

Yesterday, Fengdu County Procuratorate introduced that Wu Jun took advantage of his work position to issue fake retirement certificates, defrauding a total of 1.204 million yuan from 42 people. After Fengdu County Procuratorate prosecuted, the county court sentenced him to twelve years in prison for fraud and fined him 100,000 yuan.

To prevent the scam from being exposed, he paid nearly two years of retirement wages to these 42 people.

Wu Jun said that the 42 people he handled did not meet national regulations and could not process formal retirements or enjoy the benefits provided by the state. They neither had relevant archives nor met retirement conditions. He collected money for issuing fake retirement certificates and distributed it monthly under the guise of "retirement wages" to gain the trust of those seeking retirement certificates and avoid suspicion of his fraudulent behavior.

From April 2010 to July 2011, Wu Jun deceived Qin Moumou, Liao Moumou, Luo Moumou, and 42 others, issuing fake retirement certificates without legal approval procedures and defrauding Qin Moumou and others of money under the pretext of needing to pay supplementary pension insurance fees, totaling 1.204 million yuan.

For those who officially processed retirement certificates, their retirement wages were determined based on their years of service and the amount of social security contributions, generally ranging from 700 to 2,000 yuan, along with other related benefits from the state.

After deceiving the victims, Wu Jun avoided his fraud being uncovered by continuing to implement the scam. He regularly transferred money to the bank accounts provided by those with fake retirement certificates, referred to as "retirement wages."

After the case broke out, Wu Jun turned himself in and returned 297,600 yuan of ill-gotten gains to his workplace and the public security bureau.

The money he defrauded was squandered entirely.

According to prosecutors, according to national regulations, only those who meet the requirements for formal retirement can apply for retirement certificates.
