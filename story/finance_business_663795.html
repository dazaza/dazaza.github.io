Russia Claims China's Navy Strengthens Its Activities in the Pacific to Challenge American Hegemony

by anonymous on 2012-02-12 16:34:55

Russia Claims China's Navy Strengthens Activity in the Pacific to Challenge US Hegemony - http://www.sina.com.cn - February 10, 2012, 16:28 - Global Times Weibo Reference Image: Chinese Navy Fleet.

According to "The Voice of Russia" on February 7, Chinese naval ships are entering and exiting the western Pacific region for military exercises, responding to Tokyo's concerns about the increasing activity of the People's Liberation Army Navy in this area.

As reported by Japanese mass media, a Chinese naval formation consisting of four warships (frigates) passed through the waters between Japan's Okinawa Island and Miyako Island. The Chinese Ministry of National Defense has issued a statement regarding this matter, stating that the fleet's movement is to prepare for China's annual routine military exercise. In response to Japan's concerns, Beijing stated that China has the right to freely pass through these waters. The Chinese leadership believes that this is entirely in accordance with international law and international practice.

However, observers point out that these exercises conducted by China may lead to deteriorating relations between China and Japan due to old territorial disputes such as floor controversies. A few days ago, Japan protested against China's unilateral announcement of its intention to conduct oil exploration on oil fields in the East China Sea. On the other hand, China's Foreign Ministry condemned the Japanese government's plan to unilaterally name several disputed islands near the floor.

Recently, Tokyo has repeatedly emphasized concerns about China's rapidly growing military strength without fully understanding Beijing's geopolitical demands. In 2011, Japan's Defense Ministry predicted in its annual defense report that China would attempt regular naval operations in waters near Japan, including the East China Sea, South China Sea, and nearby Pacific waters. Leonid Ivanshov, Director of the Geopolitical Problems Institute, said in an interview with "The Voice of Russia" that China's increasingly frequent naval activities indeed indicate that China plans to expand its naval activities in the sea areas claimed by Tokyo. This is especially because the region contains abundant hydrocarbons and fishery resources, and China will rely on military strength to support its claims.

On the other hand, the situation is quite clear. In recent years, the United States has gradually strengthened its naval presence and actions around China. The U.S. established a military base in Australia and conducted strategic patrols. The U.S. also controls the Malacca Strait, which is very important to China, and they are trying to befriend India as a counterbalance to China.

To respond to China's power display and demand for dominance in the Asia-Pacific region (i.e., the western Pacific region), Ivanshov indicated that Beijing repeatedly stresses its dissatisfaction with Washington's interference in territorial disputes in the South China Sea. Recently, Washington has accelerated strengthening security ties with countries that pose threats to China's strategic interests, including India, Vietnam, Indonesia, the Philippines, and Singapore. Therefore, the current exercises are not only related to territorial disputes over the floor but also represent China's response to the U.S.'s frequent activities in the Pacific region. In the past two years, the Chinese Navy has conducted nearly 50 military exercises. Beijing views its increasing military activities in the Pacific region as a way to enhance its influence as a superpower in the region and challenge American hegemony. (By Sen Nian) ★
