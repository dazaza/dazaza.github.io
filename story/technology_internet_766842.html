Gates responds to returning as Microsoft CEO: I won't do it full-time again

by anonymous on 2013-10-01 22:15:39

As Steve Ballmer (Steve Ballmer) is about to step down as Microsoft's CEO, rumors in the industry about Microsoft's new CEO are rife. There are even rumors that Bill Gates (Bill Gates) may come out of retirement again to take over this position. However, judging from Gates' recent responses to the media on this matter, the possibility of Gates resuming the role of Microsoft's CEO is almost zero.

On Tuesday, the US tech blog Business Insider reported that in August this year, Steve Ballmer announced he would resign as Microsoft's CEO within a year, a piece of news that shocked the world. Many people therefore believed that Bill Gates might replace Ballmer and once again become the company’s CEO.

Of course, based on the current information available, it seems unlikely that Gates will take over as Microsoft's CEO again, as he has been busy with his foundation work, and Gates appears to have no intention of taking over this position. Nevertheless, rumors in the industry about this issue continue to circulate. In response, Gates stated that he might be more involved in Microsoft's hiring process for the new CEO, but the likelihood of him returning to Microsoft to resume the CEO role is extremely slim.

Today, BI directly interviewed Gates on this issue. However, Gates did not firmly answer "no" when responding to this question. Gates' response was: "No, I served as Microsoft's CEO for a period of time. Now, as the chairman of Microsoft's board, I will partially participate in helping Microsoft find a new CEO." From this statement, Gates appears to indicate that he will not serve as Microsoft's CEO again, though his denial isn't entirely emphatic. Nonetheless, BI still believes that, based on this response, Gates will not return as Microsoft's CEO. If before this interview, the possibility of Gates replacing Ballmer was 0%, then now the possibility is at most 1%.

Below is BI's interview and Gates' main response:

BI: You have always been passionate about global health and poverty issues, but is it possible that you would abandon your current foundation work to become Microsoft's CEO again?

Gates: No, I served as Microsoft's CEO for some time. Now, I am the chairman of Microsoft's board, and I will participate in part of the relevant work. My full-time job is still with the foundation; I will not be fully dedicated to the position of Microsoft's CEO.
