Without doubt, the reverse group-buying model of JuShou.com can maximally meet users' personalized group-buying demands.

by beetgk2958 on 2012-02-22 14:19:28

Group buying websites, such as LuKe.com, have recently suffered frequent setbacks. Negative news such as layoffs, station closures, and funding breaks often appear in the media. In this situation, what will be the future of the group-buying business model? Currently, many group-buying entrepreneurs are exploring new paths, among which reverse group-buying is undoubtedly a major highlight. There are already quite a few group-buying websites both domestically and internationally that are attempting this model.

JuShou.com is a newly launched group-buying website in China. However, unlike traditional group-buying models, on this website, users initiate group-buying activities. After initiating a group buy through simple steps, users then select truly popular products through specific voting and interactive rules, forming a group with sufficient bargaining power. Subsequently, the website communicates with suppliers to organize group-buy transactions. Typically, users can only choose from a limited variety of existing products for group-buying on traditional websites. The reverse group-buying model of JuShou.com undoubtedly maximizes the satisfaction of users' personalized group-buying needs. At the same time, product prices on JuShou.com are not fixed; they implement a tiered pricing system. As the number of buyers increases, the group-buy price decreases accordingly. This incentivizes users to invite more friends to purchase so that the price can drop even further, thereby creating a virtuous cycle. When the activity ends, merchants supply goods at the final group-buy price, allowing users to enjoy the most discounted product prices.

Ringleadr not only allows users to easily initiate and share local group-buy information but also gives local merchants the opportunity to approve, reject, or adjust the transaction according to their own standards once the information is posted, shared, and reserved. Moreover, after users initiate a group-buy, Ringleadr encourages them to share links via social media to attract enough people to join the group-buying team. After accumulating a certain number of potential users, Ringleadr submits the group-buy plan to merchants for review, with the review and modification cycle usually within 15 days. Additionally, Ringleadr adds a ranking function and launches a mobile app, using LBS and other mobile internet technologies to enhance interaction between merchants, businesses, and users.

Loopt and Ringleadr have promoted the popularity of reverse group-buying but also have shortcomings such as long cycles. SoBiz10, as a later entrant into reverse group-buying, aims to improve the reverse group-buying experience by shortening waiting times. SoBiz10's working method is: users gather 10 friends to decide what they want to group-buy from the merchant. Then the user posts it on SoBiz10, and the corresponding merchant receives emails, text messages, and voicemails, notifying them that someone hopes to group-buy. Merchants have 48 hours to decide whether to accept, reject, or negotiate. If the merchant accepts, the user directly receives an email containing the coupon and pays the merchant through SoBiz10's secure payment system.

As pioneers of reverse group-buying, Loopt and Ringleadr launched the reverse group-buying service U-Deals. The core value of U-Deals lies in making users replace group-buying websites and become the main characters in group-buying. Its model is: users can initiate group-buys on smartphones, meaning publishing the group-buy services they want, and inviting friends to participate through channels like Facebook, Twitter, Email, etc.; when the number of participants reaches the standard, Loopt contacts the merchant to negotiate group-buy matters. This demand-based matching approach has quickly made Loopt popular.

Initially, LuKe.com was a mobile social platform based on geographical location, providing real-time sharing of lifestyle service information. With the gradual rise of group-buying services provided in a reverse auction model, LuKe.com also began to try entering the reverse group-buying field and launched "I Want to Group-Buy." Similar to foreign reverse group-buying models, "I Want to Group-Buy" is also location-based, where users themselves propose group-buy demands and applications. The difference lies in that after completing the information collection, LuKe.com does not participate in subsequent services such as negotiating with merchants itself but outsources these group-buy demands to websites like Meituan and Lashou, which have more experience and strength in traditional group-buy execution.
