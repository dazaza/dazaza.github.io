The judge said that Internet bars should purchase qualified video platforms to prevent infringement.

by erdf9wft on 2012-03-02 14:15:26

"We all feel wronged." The manager of the Strategic Internet Bar, Mr. Wei, was the first to stand up and voice his dissatisfaction. Mr. Wei said that nowadays, the vast majority of internet bar owners have purchased film platforms, and are also willing to pay a certain fee for it. However, the movies on the platform do not include the copyright for all films, and internet bars still face lawsuits from some rights holders. "To whom exactly should we pay the copyright fees in order to be exempt from infringement liability?" Mr. Wei raised his doubts, "Have Wangshang Company and Shiqu Company promoted film copyrights in Hefei? Are these two companies maliciously enforcing their rights?"

According to information obtained from the seminar, since 2008, Beijing Wangshang Cultural Communication Co., Ltd. (hereinafter referred to as "Wangshang Company") and Shiqu Company have embarked on the path of suing internet cafes for rights protection. Over the past three years, these two companies have almost sued all the internet cafes in Hefei, with some cafes even being sued multiple times. Such infringement cases generally have little suspense, with courts usually ruling in favor of the copyright owners. Initially, after the judgment, internet cafes would generally accept the verdict and willingly compensate the copyright owners. However, as the concepts of internet cafe operators have been updated, most internet cafes have also purchased film platforms to play movies, but they are still repeatedly sued for compensation, which makes them feel very wronged.

The owners of internet cafes also feel wronged, not knowing how to avoid infringement.

In this regard, the representative of Shiqu Company said, "We don't have a film platform, so you may not be willing to pay us compensation; however, our company owns the copyright for some films, and your internet cafe plays them without permission, which constitutes an infringement."

"If internet cafes purchase qualified film platforms and conduct reasonable reviews of the platforms, they can be exempt from liability," said Ma Jian. "It would be best if the industry authorities and trade associations take the lead in establishing a pre-review mechanism for recommending film platforms for internet cafes, which would reduce or even eliminate the purchase of some illegal film platforms."

Due to unauthorized playback of movies such as "If You Are the One" in the internet cafe, at the beginning of this year, Beijing Shiqu Era Technology Co., Ltd. (hereinafter referred to as "Shiqu Company") sued Anhui Strategic Master E-Sports Club (hereinafter referred to as "Strategic Internet Cafe") in the High-tech Court, claiming 36,000 yuan in economic losses. Eventually, the court determined that the internet cafe infringed and ordered it to compensate 24,000 yuan. Dissatisfied with the first-instance judgment, Strategic Internet Cafe recently appealed to the Intermediate People's Court of Hefei.

Most internet cafes lose in movie playback infringement cases.

Knowing that infringement will be punished, why do internet cafe operators repeatedly break the law knowingly? At the seminar, judges, internet cafe owners, and copyright parties discussed this issue.

How to resolve the "infringement risk"? Court experts offer suggestions.

So, how to solve the problem of internet cafes being frequently sued for infringement? In response to the concerns of internet cafe operators, Ma Jian, the presiding judge of the Intellectual Property Tribunal of the High-tech Court, proposed some suggestions.

In November 2010, the Supreme Court issued the "Notice on Properly Handling Cases Involving Copyright Disputes Related to Internet Cafes." Article 4 stipulates that if internet cafe operators can prove that the involved films were legally obtained from qualified film providers, they will not bear civil liability for compensating losses.

Gao Zhiting, reporter Zhang Yuxue

Caijing Morning Post: Internet cafe operators say, "Most internet cafes are willing to pay for copyrights, but they want to buy but cannot buy, nor do they know how to buy to avoid infringement, and after broadcasting, they are asked for high compensation. It's not that we are unwilling to compensate, but we just feel wronged."

Rights protection party says: "We don't have a film platform for you to buy, so of course you don't want to pay; but our company has the right to broadcast, and indeed, you infringe."

Mr. Wei's lawyer also expressed his opinion: "If an internet cafe is sued for infringing on one movie, the court may rule to compensate thousands of yuan, or even tens of thousands of yuan. As an internet cafe, within a year, it may provide hundreds or even thousands of movies for playback, so the risk of infringement is very high, and subjectively, the internet cafe does not wish to infringe."

On April 26, it is the 11th World Intellectual Property Day. On April 21, in order to solve the problem of internet cafes being frequently sued, the High-tech Court of Hefei specially held a seminar titled "Strengthen Intellectual Property Protection, Promote Network Culture Development." During the seminar, the judge gave the suggestion: "Internet cafe operators should purchase qualified film platforms."
