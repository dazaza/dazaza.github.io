Matters that Zuo Li You franchisees need to pay attention to:

Many customers often regard chain franchising as a shortcut to success, especially when many franchise headquarters promote the idea of guaranteed profits, making people believe that they can easily realize their dream of becoming a small boss without much effort. However, while there are successful franchisees, there are also quite a few who fail. Below are some self-assessment preparations and taboos provided by Zuo Li You Silver Jewelry experts for those considering franchising.

Franchise Preparation:

● Self-assess whether you are suitable for franchising and if you are willing to comply with various regulations from the franchise headquarters.

● Evaluate your financial situation and discuss investment amounts with an investment advisor.

● Gain insight into future trends and combine your interests and industry recognition to choose an appropriate business category.

● Conduct market research before joining, such as investigating the potential for long-term development and customer affordability in silver jewelry specialty stores.

● Look for brands with years of experience and a certain scale of chain stores for better operational security.

● Have face-to-face discussions with the headquarters to understand their operational strength and corporate character.

● Understand the support system provided by the headquarters before opening the store, as well as the content of operational guidance after opening.

● Be fully prepared and have the mindset to work harder than others; this is what makes a qualified franchisee.

Franchise Taboos:

● Thinking that franchising guarantees profit and that the dream of being a big boss will immediately come true.

● Lacking basic knowledge of franchising, not realizing how much capital is needed or how many restrictions exist.

● Not possessing actual operational capabilities and hoping to rely on franchising to make up for personal deficiencies.

● Handing over all matters to the headquarters for management, not bothering to think about anything yourself.

● Spending every day trying to raise money to repay debts incurred during franchising, leaving no focus on the business.

● Realizing after signing the contract that the contract terms differ from initial expectations, leading to disputes with the headquarters.

● Joining a franchise system with high technical difficulty but lacking the patience and perseverance to continue.

● Refusing to accept guidance from the headquarters, refusing to execute orders, and refusing to cooperate with plans.
