Introduction to the Subplots of "Still a Soldier"

Episode 1:

One day in the mid-1980s, just before his transfer to civilian life, Feng Xiaobing hurriedly came to Nanjiang City to find one of his old comrades-in-arms, Zhou Lingyun, the plant manager of the Sino-foreign joint venture Hongxing Pharmaceutical Factory. Zhou Lingyun thought it was about arranging work for the old comrade-in-arms Chen Zhijiang, but unexpectedly Feng Xiaobing asked Zhou Lingyun not to accept Chen Zhijiang, which made Zhou Lingyun very difficult.

Chen Zhijiang, Zhou Lingyun, and He Ningsheng are comrades-in-arms who have survived life-and-death battles together. After Chen Zhijiang's transfer to civilian life, he proposed to work at Zhou Lingyun's Hongxing Pharmaceutical Factory. The old political instructor He Ningsheng accompanied him to Hongxing Pharmaceutical Factory, only to find that the Japanese representative Fujiwara agreed to consider allowing Chen Zhijiang to enter the factory as deputy plant manager.

The city military transfer office arranged for Chen Zhijiang to be a deputy director in an agency due to his disability. However, feeling idle in the agency, Chen Zhijiang felt extremely restless. One day, while changing medicine at the hospital, he met a farmer patient who couldn't afford the medicine, which touched Chen Zhijiang again, prompting him to resign from public office. Chen Zhijiang then became the deputy plant manager at Zhou Lingyun's Hongxing Pharmaceutical Factory. Despite the care from his old comrade-in-arms Zhou Lingyun, Chen Zhijiang insisted on engaging in sales to understand the pharmaceutical market.

After two years of practical work experience, Chen Zhijiang found that the Shan Zhu Root Granules produced by Hongxing Factory did not sell well, so he proposed developing new drugs. Zhou Lingyun told him that developing new drugs requires significant investment and carries greater risks, something a small factory like Hongxing cannot afford. At present, maintaining the status quo and paying employees' salaries is enough. He advised Chen Zhijiang not to have unrealistic thoughts, but Chen Zhijiang still believed that relying solely on the current Shan Zhu Root Granules would make it hard to have a market prospect.

Zhou Lingyun and Chen Zhijiang had divergences in their factory management philosophies. After entering the factory, Chen Zhijiang realized his lack of knowledge and persisted in attending university courses for further study in traditional Chinese medicine. At this time, there was an outbreak of hepatitis A in coastal areas, urgently needing Shan Zhu Root Granules. Overnight, they became highly sought-after goods. The existing inventory of over 120 tons of Shan Zhu Root in Hongxing Pharmaceutical Factory quickly found its way to the market, with favorable prospects. Zhou Lingyun was quite pleased.

Just then, the Japanese representative Fujiwara, who was on vacation back in Japan, suddenly returned and requested Hongxing Factory to stop domestic sales, use all the Shan Zhu Root in the factory for export, and increase the export price. Zhou Lingyun immediately agreed.

Episode 2:

To seize the opportunity to earn more money for the factory, Zhou Lingyun signed a contract with Fujiwara to increase the export of 120 tons of Shan Zhu Root, stipulating that no Shan Zhu Root could be shipped out domestically and should wait for foreign export. Since it was unplanned export, Zhou Lingyun found Feng Xiaobing, who worked in the Foreign Trade Bureau. Feng Xiaobing quickly helped him complete the customs export procedures. Upon hearing that Zhou Lingyun had completed the export procedures so quickly, Fujiwara notified the Japanese headquarters to immediately transfer all the payment into Hongxing Pharmaceutical Factory's account.

While running sales and understanding the pharmaceutical market situation outside, Chen Zhijiang accidentally heard that hepatitis A was prevalent in coastal areas, making the Shan Zhu Root Granules produced by Hongxing Factory highly sought-after goods, with insufficient supply.

He Ningsheng's son, He Mingcong, who also studied pharmacy, came to Hongxing Factory with Dr. Bai from Pujian Hospital, hoping to obtain some Shan Zhu Root Granules through his relationship with He Mingcong, but was rejected by Zhou Lingyun.

Upon learning that Hongxing Factory would use all its stock medicines for export, Chen Zhijiang was very confused. While Shan Zhu Root was scarce domestically, a batch of high-priced imported Shan Zhu Root Granules appeared on the domestic market, causing skyrocketing prices and complaints from patients and ordinary people. Thus, Chen Zhijiang rushed back to Hongxing Factory and made an unexpected decision without consulting Plant Manager Zhou Lingyun. He sent all the stock Shan Zhu Root to the coastal epidemic area and exposed Fujiwara's behavior of repackaging the Shan Zhu Root Granules produced by Hongxing Factory and re-importing them to China, profiting from China's national difficulties.

Due to Chen Zhijiang sending the medicine to the coastal epidemic area, Hongxing Pharmaceutical Factory breached the export contract during its execution. When Fujiwara learned about this, he got angry and threatened to withdraw capital. Upon hearing that Chen Zhijiang had acted on his own initiative and sent away the Shan Zhu Root, both Zhou Lingyun and Feng Xiaobing had intense conflicts with Chen Zhijiang. In anger, Chen Zhijiang resigned. Due to affecting the export, the Japanese representative Fujiwara insisted that Chen Zhijiang personally take responsibility and demanded a fine of 100,000 yuan.

Episode 3:

Knowing that he couldn't escape paying the fine, Chen Zhijiang planned to use the family's meager savings as the penalty, but Feng Xiaobing opposed it. Feng Xiaobing blamed Chen Zhijiang for messing things up, while Chen Zhijiang believed he had done nothing wrong because he delivered the medicine to the patients in the epidemic area. To retain Chen Zhijiang, Zhou Lingyun gathered 100,000 yuan and handed it over to Chen Zhijiang, asking him to personally deliver the money to Fujiwara under his personal name to ease the conflict between the two.

However, Chen Zhijiang secretly took out the family's only 100,000 yuan severance pay and handed it over to Fujiwara. But Chen Zhijiang required Fujiwara to write a receipt in front of him, exposing the despicable actions of Japanese businessmen in the receipt. Although Chen Zhijiang paid the fine, the conflict with Fujiwara not only failed to ease but instead became even sharper. Zhang Chengli, the plant manager of Hongtu Pharmaceutical Factory, heavily indebted due to poor management, asked planner Hu Yue for an idea. Hu Yue gave him the creative idea of "creating a new drug" without changing the essence.

Although Zhou Lingyun tried to retain Chen Zhijiang multiple times, Chen Zhijiang ultimately chose to resign. But after truly resigning, Chen Zhijiang again felt aimless. Feng Xiaobing asked Chen Zhijiang about his next plan, and Chen Zhijiang said he wanted to be a boss and start a pharmaceutical factory. Feng Xiaobing believed it was impossible for him to start a pharmaceutical factory since he had neither money nor connections. One day, Chen Zhijiang encountered a discharged soldier named Song Xiaojie by the canal. Song Xiaojie's entrepreneurship and success inspired Chen Zhijiang.

Chen Zhijiang decided to start from scratch like Song Xiaojie. But how to take the first step? Wandering on the snack street, he saw an old grandma selling dumplings and remembered that he used to make dumplings in the army. Therefore, he decided to start by setting up a street stall to sell dumplings, paving his own entrepreneurial path.

Episode 4:

Chen Zhijiang decided to start everything from scratch. While attending lectures at the medical college, he sold dumplings on the street. His dumplings were called "Veteran Dumplings," attracting many discharged and transferred veterans. Feng Xiaobing and Zhou Lingyun opposed Chen Zhijiang setting up a street stall. Zhou Lingyun persuaded Chen Zhijiang to return to the factory, but unexpectedly, Chen Zhijiang set up his dumpling stall right in front of Hongxing Pharmaceutical Factory, causing frequent conflicts between Chen Zhijiang and Zhou Lingyun, making Feng Xiaobing feel uncomfortable being caught in the middle.

After the epidemic was lifted, the sales of Shan Zhu Root Granules plummeted significantly, leaving Hongxing Pharmaceutical Factory struggling. Zhang Chengli, the plant manager of Hongtu Pharmaceutical Factory, proposed collaborating with Zhou Lingyun to produce new drugs, which were essentially just renaming and changing dosage forms of so-called new drugs. Unexpectedly, both factories lost money.

Feng Xiaobing strongly persuaded Chen Zhijiang to abandon the dumpling stall and look for a new job in the city. Feng Xiaobing asked Zhou Lingyun to help find a way for Chen Zhijiang, but Chen Zhijiang was determined to start his own pharmaceutical factory. Chen Zhijiang and Zhou Lingyun's former political instructor, He Ningsheng, was already the regimental political commissar. Facing the army's downsizing, He Ningsheng proposed demobilization and returning to civilian work, despite opposition from his wife. However, his postgraduate son, He Mingcong, fully understood and supported him.

After conducting research on the Chinese pharmaceutical market, Fujiwara reminded Zhou Lingyun that if the Sino-foreign joint venture Hongxing Factory didn't launch any real new drugs, Hongxing Factory would eventually collapse. Meanwhile, Fujiwara informed Zhou Lingyun that if valuable secret formulas or folk remedies could be found, the Japanese headquarters would fully support him in developing new drugs. Zhou Lingyun realized that finding secret formulas and developing modern traditional Chinese medicine was the only way out for Hongxing Factory. Chen Zhijiang was also seeking ways to develop his factory. Under the guidance of Professor Lu from the Medical College, Chen Zhijiang was inspired by the works of the ancient Chinese medical sage Zhang Zhongjing and determined to explore the treasure trove of traditional Chinese medicine and develop modern traditional Chinese medicine as the direction for his factory.

To help Chen Zhijiang find a new job, Feng Xiaobing approached leaders at various levels of the municipal party committee and government. With the care of the party secretary, the municipal government decided to reassign Chen Zhijiang as a state cadre to work in the Beijing office of the municipal government.

Episode 5:

Zhang Xiaodong deliberately led Liang Jun to Chen Zhijiang's dumpling stall in front of Hongxing Pharmaceutical Factory to invite Liang Jun for dinner, while Zhou Lingyun wanted to drive Chen Zhijiang away. Chen Zhijiang learned that Hongxing Pharmaceutical Factory was not operating well. Zhang Chengli introduced a girlfriend to Zhou Lingyun, but was strangely rejected by Zhou Lingyun, leaving Zhang Chengli very puzzled.

Feng Xiaobing informed Zhou Lingyun about Chen Zhijiang's reassignment to a new job and hoped Zhou Lingyun could go with him to find Chen Zhijiang, but Zhou Lingyun said Feng Xiaobing should go himself.

Excitedly, Feng Xiaobing brought this good news to Chen Zhijiang, but unexpectedly, Chen Zhijiang flatly refused, making Feng Xiaobing very sad. Feng Xiaobing left with her daughter, Chen Yuanfan. After demobilization from the army, He Ningsheng decided to find Chen Zhijiang in Nanjiang City to start a business together. His son, He Mingcong, was already a graduate student in traditional Chinese medicine and came to Nanjiang with his father. He Ningsheng told Chen Zhijiang that this time he was demobilized, not transferred to civilian life. He didn't want to rely on national care but intended to work together with Chen Zhijiang to achieve something.

Fujiwara handed over a list of Chinese medical masters to Zhou Lingyun, hoping to obtain secret formulas or folk remedies from one of these masters. Fujiwara clearly told Zhou Lingyun that if Hongxing Pharmaceutical Factory could get the secret formula of a famous old Chinese doctor, the Japanese side would invest again in Hongxing Factory and deepen cooperation.

Chen Zhijiang, He Ningsheng, and He Mingcong studied the factory establishment plan together. Chen Zhijiang proposed that they must develop new drugs. He Mingcong's mentor was precisely the master of traditional Chinese medicine Gu Shihang, who possessed a precious secret formula called Xin Shu Yin. Chen Zhijiang was determined to get this secret formula. Deciding to start his own enterprise, Feng Xiaobing was very worried about this.
