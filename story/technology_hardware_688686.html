Budgeting Tips for Smart Home System Prices

For homeowners preparing to renovate their homes and purchase smart home system products, how to budget the cost is their most concerning and frequently considered issue! So, how exactly should one budget the price? Below, we will discuss this in detail.

1. How Much Does a Smart Home System Cost?

From a more common perspective, installing a smart home system in a three-bedroom, two-living room apartment generally costs about 20,000 RMB for basic lighting control (the cheaper option), around 50,000-80,000 RMB for all regular functions and economical options, and approximately 100,000-200,000 RMB for imported high-end systems. For villas, due to an increased number of control points, the cost for basic lighting control starts at about 30,000-40,000 RMB, with all regular functions and economical options ranging from 80,000-100,000 RMB, while imported high-end systems can range from 150,000-500,000 RMB.

Of course, these prices vary based on the strength of the smart home control system's functions, differing technologies, brands, and whether they are imported or domestically produced. If you want a relatively reliable, stable, economical system with all standard smart home functions, the general installation price for an apartment would be between 50,000-80,000 RMB, while for a villa, it would be around 100,000-150,000 RMB. These configurations should have practical significance, with fairly comprehensive functions. The following example uses apartment configuration; villa installations would increase point quantities by approximately 40%. Generally included are:

1. Wireless wall switches, wireless dimming switches (about 15-20 channels);

2. Wireless smart sockets, wireless infrared repeaters (usually around 2 for air conditioners, 2 for TVs, and 3-4 for appliance power sources);

3. Wireless electric curtain control (mainly single or double layer curtains for the living room and master bedroom);

4. Home background music system: including background music functionality in four zones, where each room can choose different sound sources (MP3, FM radio, CD, DVD, etc.), with independent free switching and volume adjustment for each zone;

5. Smart home video system: capable of achieving 4-channel, 8-channel, or 16-channel video surveillance, allowing smartphone users to check the home situation anytime and anywhere.

2. Why Do Smart Home Systems Have Different Prices for the Same Area?

Even though the apartments or villas may have the same area, why do smart home system prices differ? This is a question often asked by users planning to install such systems.

The price of a smart home system does not directly relate to the size of the house but is mainly determined by several core issues:

1. The amount of smart home functions? If the accompanying smart home functions are comprehensive, the total cost will naturally be higher; if the functions are relatively ordinary, the cost will be lower. The most conventional smart home functions include: smart home security systems, intelligent lighting control systems, remote and timer-controlled electrical appliances, electric curtain control systems, home background music systems, smart home video systems, etc.

2. For the same function, choosing products from different manufacturers (different domestic brands vs international brands) or using different technologies (zigbee, wifi, 315 MHz, 433 MHz, Bluetooth) can result in significant price differences.

3. On the basis of the same smart home configuration and similar smart home products, the key lies in the number of control points. For instance, controlling just one light versus controlling all the lights in the house would result in vastly different prices because controlling one light might require only one sensor, whereas controlling an entire house could require at least 10-20 sensors.

3. The Core Application of Smart Home Systems Lies in Reasonable Design

There are no perfect smart home products, only perfect smart home system designs. To achieve smart home functionality in a house, it’s not enough to look at products from just one manufacturer. It's crucial to select an excellent smart home system design company to create a professional and comprehensive system design and plan. Typically, residential smart home renovation design involves the following key steps:

1. Personal Dream Phase: Conception and feasibility of your ideas for the smart home system.

2. Smart Home System Design Phase: Find an outstanding smart home design company that can help design a professional and comprehensive residential smart design and plan, meeting your personalized home intelligence needs while recommending and designing the most suitable smart home system solutions based on different house sizes, family member characteristics, and price tolerance levels.

3. Smart Home System Technology Selection: Zigbee is based on IEEE 802.15. However, IEEE only handles the low-level MAC layer and physical layer protocols, so the Zigbee Alliance extended IEEE, standardizing its network layer protocol and API. Zigbee is an emerging short-range, low-speed, low-power wireless network technology primarily used for close-range wireless connections. It has features like low complexity, low power consumption, low speed, low cost, self-organizing networks, high reliability, and long-distance coverage. It is mainly suitable for automatic control and remote control applications, which can be embedded into various devices. In short, ZigBee is an affordable, low-power, self-organizing short-range wireless communication technology.

4. Smart Home Product Installation Phase: When it's time to install lights, appliances, and curtains, prepare accordingly beforehand. Then, according to project progress, gradually install relevant smart home products.

5. Smart Home Product Debugging Phase: According to the installation progress of related smart home devices, the smart home system needs to be debugged in phases and subsystems, finally followed by overall system debugging.

6. Smart Home Delivery and Usage Phase - After debugging, allow the homeowner to trial use for about a week. If any personalized adjustments are needed, make appropriate modifications. During delivery, provide some training on the most common operations and settings!

7. Smart Home After-sales Service Phase: Within the specified after-sales service period, if there are any product quality or system faults, the smart home system service company will typically provide on-site service within 12-24 hours.

With the development of technology and social progress, smart homes are now starting to enter the daily lives of ordinary people. Homeowners with certain economic means who are preparing to renovate generally consider smart renovations, which involve the design and installation of smart home systems. Smart homes indeed enhance the technological level of modern life, improve the quality of life, increase comfort, safety, and convenience, as well as achieve environmental protection and energy saving goals! A complete smart home system should include seven major sub-systems: smart home security system, smart home control system, smart home entertainment system, smart home comfort system, smart home energy-saving system, smart home ecological system, and smart home appliance system. Among them, the smart home security system is one of the most basic smart home sub-systems and also the most commonly selected smart home function by end-users.
