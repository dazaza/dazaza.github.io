Need to pay extra money to get the film of wedding photos?

by qjywi521 on 2011-11-26 12:14:52

Regarding the issue of giving away all the negatives in the wedding photography industry, merchants and consumers in various regions across the country are really having endless disputes. In fact, the market conditions in each region are indeed different. Some give away all the negatives, while others do not. Whether or not to give away all the negatives is currently undetermined by clear regulations, so it is up to each merchant's marketing strategy to decide whether or not to give away all the negatives. Wenbo from Qingmu Vision of Changsha Wedding Photography reminds consumers that regarding the after-sales consumption in the photography industry, one just needs to ask clearly before placing the order, and there is really no need to have disputes afterwards. Let's take a look at the dispute that occurred. Recently, my sister and her boyfriend went to a studio to take wedding photos. Previously, they had roughly discussed it in the store, but during the photo shoot, the studio not only said that adding an undergarment would cost extra money, but after taking the photos, they were only willing to provide some sample photos, saying that if they wanted extra sample photos, they would have to pay an additional 400 yuan, which ended up ruining their day.

That morning, my sister and her boyfriend arrived at the studio very early. Before changing clothes, the photographer said that adding an undergarment would cost an extra 100 yuan. Since the studio's clothing does not match regular undergarments, and since this was a must-do event for their wedding, my sister had no choice but to pay. That day's shooting was very tiring. When shooting outdoor scenes, they ran to several places, and my sister felt that the clothes were too tight, and she had to touch up her makeup frequently. She also caught a breeze outside and could only hastily eat a boxed lunch at noon. During the afternoon shoot, her face hurt from smiling too much, and she felt soreness in her waist and back. After a busy day, starting from around 8:30 in the morning until 7:30 in the evening, they finished taking nearly a hundred photos.

A few days later, when she went to select the photos, the staff of the studio said that apart from the 25 photos she wanted, if she wanted the rest of the photos, she would have to pay an additional 400 yuan. My sister tried to bargain it down to 300 yuan, but after talking for a long time, the person still did not agree. In anger, she decided she didn't want them anymore, and the person then said, "Just give me 280 yuan and take them."

It cannot be denied that the studio staff worked hard for the photoshoot, but since they paid for the photoshoot, why should they have to pay extra money?

As an industry professional, Wenbo answers this question to make consumers understand: It's like going out to eat, you choose a 100-yuan set meal, but the set meal does not include napkins, so if you want napkins, you have to pay extra. Very simple, right? The difference lies in whether or not you were told before choosing the set meal that the napkins would cost extra~ So, all in all, it's because things weren't clarified before placing the order, and the responsibility for this issue lies with the merchant. Consumers generally get married for the first time and take wedding photos, so how can they know everything clearly? Don't you agree?
