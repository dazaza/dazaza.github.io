Recommend! 6 Chinese herbal tea drinks for quick relief from colds

by fuweiwo17 on 2011-10-23 17:14:53

As autumn deepens and the temperature difference grows larger, many people are prone to catching a cold during this seasonal transition. Traditional Chinese medicine practitioners indicate that as the weather cools down, the yang energy in the human body also gradually weakens, making it particularly easy to catch a cold. Experts recommend four herbal teas, such as those with ingredients like astragalus (huangqi), windproof (fangfeng), longan, radix scrophulariae (shashen), and chrysanthemum, which can replenish qi and nourish blood, preventing colds or alleviating symptoms. Additionally, pressing the Zhongtan point or Taichong point can relieve coughing and sore throat associated with cold symptoms.

The preparation method for all these teas is the same: divide all the materials into three portions. Each time, take one portion and add 250c.c of boiling water. Steep for about 5 minutes and drink after meals. You can consume 1-3 portions per day. If you frequently experience soft stools or diarrhea, add 3 qian of kudzu root; if you are prone to constipation, add 3 qian of Cassia seeds.

1. Astragalus Windproof Tea

Suitable for: Those with weak qi and blood, who often feel cold, have headaches, nasal congestion, sneezing, runny nose, and body aches after catching a cold.

Ingredients: Astragalus 3 qian, windproof 2 qian, longan 1 liang, radix scrophulariae 5 qian, chrysanthemum 1.5 qian

Effects: Astragalus replenishes deficiency and enhances resistance; windproof has antibacterial and analgesic properties; longan replenishes blood and boosts immunity; radix scrophulariae clears the lungs, nourishes the liver, and removes heat due to deficiency; chrysanthemum dispels wind-cold.

2. Ginseng Mint Tea

Suitable for: Those with lung qi deficiency, who often experience coughing or prolonged coughing after catching a cold.

Ingredients: Oriental ginseng 1.5 qian, Ophiopogon japonicus 5 qian, mint 2 qian, yam 5 qian, apricot kernel 2 qian

Effects: Oriental ginseng replenishes qi and benefits the lungs, boosting vitality; Ophiopogon japonicus cools and moistens the lungs; mint can dissipate heat, soothe the liver, and detoxify; yam benefits the spleen and kidneys, enhancing immunity; apricot kernels can soothe the throat and dissolve phlegm.

3. Codonopsis Kudzu Root Tea

Suitable for: Those with spleen-stomach qi deficiency, who often experience nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal distension, intestinal rumbling, and abdominal pain after catching a cold.

Ingredients: Codonopsis 3 qian, Perilla 1.5 qian, Kudzu root 3 qian, seedless red dates 5 qian, osmanthus 1.5 qian

Effects: Codonopsis generates fluids and nourishes qi and blood; Perilla has the effect of dispersing cold and stopping diarrhea; Kudzu root cools and moistens the lungs and has the effect of stopping diarrhea; red dates benefit qi and nourish blood; osmanthus strengthens the stomach.
