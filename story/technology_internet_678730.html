### Case: OEM Private Labeling

1. **[Apparel Industry]** Miss Lin from Hangzhou: Operated a clothing wholesale business in Hangzhou’s Sijiqing Market and Beijing's Dahongmen Market. Due to the lack of her own brand, she had always relied on sourcing products from other factories. In 2001, through Ruifeng Accounting Firm, she registered [XX International Apparel Co., Ltd.] in Hong Kong [http://www.rf.hk/company/hongkong/] and simultaneously registered trademarks in both Hong Kong and mainland China. Under the name of the Hong Kong company [http://www.rf.hk/company/hongkong/], she outsourced production with related factories for private labeling. The packaging and labels of the garments were marked with the name of the international apparel company, giving the clothing its own brand identity and moving towards higher-end development. Now, Miss Lin’s family has invested in Hangzhou, introducing advanced equipment to establish a large-scale garment production line.

2. **[Furniture Industry]** Mr. Wang from Guangdong: Founded a furniture production factory in Shunde, Guangdong Province, investing substantial funds in advanced production equipment. Although his product craftsmanship was excellent, in the fiercely competitive home furnishings market, consumers favored international brands. After careful consideration, he learned that Ruifeng Accounting Firm [http://www.rf.hk/] could assist clients in registering an Italian company [http://www.rf.hk/company/italiana/]. He specially visited Ruifeng's Hong Kong headquarters [http://www.rf.hk/] to consult about relevant matters. Our commercial advisors at Ruifeng explained the situation to Mr. Wang—registering a company in Italy would be costly and involve complex maintenance procedures in the future. We suggested registering an Italian company [http://www.rf.hk/company/italiana/] in Hong Kong instead, while simultaneously registering trademarks in both Italy and mainland China [http://www.rf.hk/trademark/]. The furniture instruction manuals and labels bear the name of the Italian company [http://www.rf.hk/company/offshore/ltaly.html] and the trademark registration [http://www.rf.hk/trademark/] TM. Now, Mr. Wang's home furnishings have truly become popular in the Chinese market.

3. **[Kitchen Appliances]** Mr. Zhang from Guangdong: Engaged in the kitchen cabinet equipment manufacturing industry in Zhongshan, Guangdong Province. His products passed national safety quality standards, but the market was difficult to expand. At an industry product exhibition, he noticed that some competitors had inferior product quality, safety standards, and production processes compared to his, yet they received more orders. In one accidental business deal, a client revealed the secret—Mr. Zhang should register a German company [http://www.rf.hk/company/germany/] or a Japanese company [http://www.rf.hk/company/offshore/japan.html] and adopt the OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturer) model. Realizing this, Mr. Zhang immediately searched online and found Ruifeng Accounting Firm in Hong Kong, gaining a comprehensive understanding of the company registration process [http://www.rf.hk/company/] and corporate brand image planning methods. Subsequently, he registered [German XX Appliances (Hong Kong) Co., Ltd.] and [Japanese XX Kitchen Appliances Corporation] in Hong Kong. Mr. Zhang said, "With these two companies, our brand image improved, our market share increased, and our product prices rose. I never realized how much Chinese people prefer foreign brands."

### Case: International Trade

1. **[Tax Optimization]** Mr. Wang from Fujian: Primarily engaged in project engineering investment bidding. Due to business expansion needs, he wanted to establish a reputable group company in mainland China. However, due to the inability to secure tens of millions of yuan in registered capital and the fact that his desired company name was already taken by others, he later learned through friends to establish the group company in the Cayman Islands [http://www.rf.hk/company/cayman/introduction.html]. He also established holding subsidiaries in Hong Kong, Samoa [http://www.rf.hk/company/samoa/13235.html], the Bahamas, and mainland China, becoming a true multinational group. After meticulous planning by accountants, the company achieved reasonable tax arrangements.

2. **[Import and Export]** Miss Du from Hefei: Long-term engagement in international import trade. Once, a French chemical raw material supplier refused to sign a contract with her unless she entrusted an overseas intermediary company to sign the contract and issue a letter of credit [http://www.rf.hk/exp/xinyongzheng/], citing the high credibility and reliable payment of the overseas intermediary company. Since this was a long-term business relationship, Miss Du had no choice but to establish a company in Delaware, USA [http://www.rf.hk/company/usa/], opening an offshore account for the Delaware company at a foreign bank in Shanghai (the account possessed all settlement functions required in international trade). This way, she established a long-term good cooperative relationship with the client.

3. **[Trade Settlement]** Mr. Jin from Tianjin: Engaged in international textile product export trade. He had always exported through intermediaries but was concerned about business secrets and the safety of settlements. To ensure long-term stability and avoid exchange rate differences, he wanted to register an overseas offshore company [http://www.rf.hk/company/offshore/], independently export, and directly settle payments. The prerequisite was that the offshore company did not require complicated financial reporting procedures. With the help of Ruifeng professionals, Mr. Jin registered a BVI offshore company [http://www.rf.hk/company/bvi/bvijr.html] in the British Virgin Islands (this company does not need to provide annual financial reports to the government) and opened an offshore account at a foreign bank in Beijing. This fully met Mr. Jin's offshore trade strategy and requirements, allowing for greater international development.

4. **[Export Rebate [http://www.rf.hk/exp/ckts/]]** Mr. Lin from Xiamen: Long-term engagement in stone import and export trade. Since Xiamen had canceled the export rebate for stones, trade profits plummeted. Through online research, he learned about Ruifeng Accounting Firm and successfully registered [XXXX Stone Import and Export Co., Ltd.], which not only exempted him from the export rebate issue but also allowed him to retain maximum profits. Additionally, the speed of receiving overseas payments significantly improved.

5. **[Payment Settlement]** Mr. Chen from Shenzhen: Supplied electronic components to Hong Kong manufacturers. Most of these Hong Kong companies had factories in Shenzhen, Dongguan, and Zhuhai. Sometimes, the Hong Kong manufacturers paid with Hong Kong bank checks [http://www.rf.hk/bank/hongkongbank.html]. Mr. Chen always struggled with safe and direct collection of payments. Later, after registering a company in Hong Kong and opening a bank account there, he not only solved the long-standing problem of difficult collections but also gained the trust of long-term cooperation from clients.

### Case: Exhibition and Conferences

1. **[Exhibition and Conferences]** Mr. Dong from Beijing: Ran an exhibition company specializing in organizing meetings, exhibitions, and public relations activities. Due to fierce market competition and the lack of official institutional partnerships, the company was consistently operating at a loss. When Mr. Dong was struggling with the company's development and operations, he learned that it was convenient to apply for association or society registration in Hong Kong. He quickly applied to register three associations in Hong Kong (the Hong Kong Government [http://www.rf.hk/hkzn/hkgov/] registry allows names like "China XX Industry Association"). Subsequently, under the name of the association, he invited and organized enterprises to participate in exhibition and conference activities, gaining the trust of participating companies.

### Case: Cultural Education

1. **[Publishing and Distribution]** Miss Qu from Beijing: Engaged in cultural publishing. However, registering a publishing house in mainland China was a headache for her. ### Case: Business Secretary

1. **[Business Secretary]** Mr. Ma from Guangzhou: Had many businesses in Hong Kong but couldn't frequently visit Hong Kong to handle them. Some Hong Kong-related businesses caused him many difficulties until he registered a Hong Kong company through Ruifeng. Ruifeng's business secretary solved all his problems, such as providing a company address, phone, fax, and dedicated staff to answer calls and collect checks. They also provided professional services to design inquiry forms, quotation forms, invoices, delivery notes, and pickup slips, allowing him to do business in mainland China under the name of the Hong Kong company without any worries.

2. **[Phone Forwarding]** Mr. Ren from Hangzhou: After registering a Hong Kong company, many customers directly called Hong Kong to inquire about business issues. However, Mr. Ren himself was unable to personally go to Hong Kong to handle business. To ensure every deal went smoothly, Mr. Ren came up with a perfect solution—forwarding the Hong Kong phone [http://www.rf.hk/hkzn/hkphone/] to be answered in mainland China. This solved both the company image problem and ensured no business call opportunities were missed.
