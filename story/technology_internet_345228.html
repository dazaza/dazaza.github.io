Top Ten Reasons for the Reduction of Baidu Indexed Pages in Beijing Website Construction

by bjwanzjs on 2010-04-14 10:58:36

The problem of Baidu收录 (Baidu inclusion) reduction has been encountered by many webmasters, and it seems to be more serious recently. Lately, I've seen many friends saying that their Beijing website construction experiences inexplicable reductions in Baidu收录, some haven't found the cause yet, and there's no proper solution. The reduction in Baidu收录 is also referred to as "Baidu plucking feathers" or "Baidu cutting meat." How to solve the problem of Baidu收录 reduction? Keep reading. (The above verbose language is for Baidu, letting Baidu know what this article is about, come and grab this post quickly.)

If it's a new site and there's a sudden decrease, there's actually nothing to worry about, because Baidu and Google are different. Although Google has loose restrictions, it is stable and has certain requirements for pages, with content updates gradually increasing. On the other hand, Baidu likes to include new sites, including all pages, then slowly organizing them.

There are too many possible specific reasons, approximately dozens. Below are the common causes:

(1) Website cheating.

Reason: For example, keyword stuffing, invisible text, etc. If such situations occur, don't celebrate if Baidu includes you, because during updates, it will slowly eliminate these. Cross-linking between secondary domains and blogs.

Solution: This one doesn’t need much explanation, just quickly modify your pages! Also, do not overuse secondary domains. Blog cross-linking is now useless; leave one or two links inside, remove the rest.

(2) Website standards.

Reason: Too many titles and keywords(KeyWords). Some webmasters like to put all search-related keywords in, but even if you add ten thousand, it’s useless. Also, unreasonable descriptions(De***ion), especially for new websites where this absolutely matters, so set it up well.

Solution: Three or four keywords in the title are enough; too many and Baidu won’t like it. It’s recommended to set it as Page Theme - Special Topic Name - Website Name. As for keywords, adding them is fine, but at least the page should have some related content. Setting the description is simple, just ensure smooth language, summarize the page, and having the keyword appear two or three times is sufficient.

(3) Website quality.

Reason: Almost all content is collected from elsewhere, and it's very popular articles. Baidu may suddenly include thousands of your pages, but after inclusion, within a certain period, it will re-check. If your content has no value, it will be discarded.

Solution: After collecting, make slight modifications before publishing. Don’t be lazy; browse the article simply, change paragraphs or part of the content, ensuring there's something unique compared to others. More original content is best, and leaving copyright information on the page helps. When re-posting, you can also leave —— Article organized by XXX website http://XXXXXX

(4) Website connections.

Reason: The website lacks external links, or external links are gradually decreasing. Of course, Baidu's attention to your site will also lower, slowly reducing the included content. The quality of the links is very important. Don't use junk links! Additionally, check your pages; if there are links pointing to banned sites, Baidu will consider you an accomplice.

Solution: Check the website's external links, exchange if there are few, or post interesting threads on large forums, leaving links. The more replies, the better the effect. If there are links within the site pointing to banned sites, delete them as soon as possible.

(5) Website redesign.

Reason: The website was submitted to Baidu without being properly modified, making frequent major changes and updates, today one way, tomorrow another. Categories and titles keep changing. Sometimes test content or content unrelated to the website appears.

Solution: Position well, stick to what kind of site you want to do. You can add new categories and content. Old content should not be recklessly deleted. If you're changing hosting, it's best to switch early. Ensure the previous hosting content continues to exist for a period, just in case.

(6) Website hosting.

Reason: Unstable website hosting, often unable to open the website. If Baidu fails to update twice or more and cannot capture reliable information, you will definitely be cleared from the database because Baidu assumes your site has closed or the relevant pages no longer exist. Another reason could be multiple cheating sites on your IP address, leading to misfortune.

Solution: Be careful when buying hosting, find reputable IDCs. Don't just go for cheap options; constant problems are unbearable not only for Baidu but also for regular users. After all, your site won't attract much attention from Baidu. Additionally, check the sites on the virtual host IP address when purchasing hosting, along with their inclusion status and the number of banned sites.

(7) Website security.

Reason: If malicious code is added to your site or horses are intentionally hung, Baidu can analyze it and reduce your trustworthiness. Also, if small hackers use tools to intrude, modify, or delete most pages, it leads to reduced inclusion. One of my websites once had reduced inclusion due to hacker intrusion, so website security is very important.

Solution: Regular backups, timely resolution of issues. Generally, Baidu updates frequently at night. Not many personal sites update daily. If anything abnormal occurs, handle it immediately, which should prevent further issues.

(8) Website optimization.

Reason: Having some knowledge of SEO, you might deliberately modify the site, overly optimizing it, although there’s no obvious cheating, but it fundamentally has no value. Even though you can get traffic through Baidu, the website is made to provide browsing to others. If your return rate is too low, it's still not good. Baidu does emphasize user experience after all.

Solution: Don’t over-optimize; the essence of SEO is to serve search engines, not to deceive. Everything should be done in moderation. For example, bolding a keyword once lets Baidu know it’s important, but bolding all related keywords makes Baidu think differently.

(9) Website misfortune.

Reason: Maybe you’re unlucky, encountering a major Baidu update or algorithm change, resulting in many sites experiencing inclusion reduction or being penalized. It just happens that your site catches Baidu's attention.

Solution: There’s not much you can do. Just wait for Baidu to re-include your site. If you're an SEO expert, you can quickly figure out the latest algorithm and share it with everyone.

(10) Website closure.

Reason: Your site has already closed, but Baidu still has a lot of related inclusion information. This is more common with forum types. Included content keeps decreasing.
