On the evening of February 27, the national team departed from the Qingyuan training base to Guangzhou, checking into the JianGuo Hotel to prepare for the last match of the top 20 qualifiers. However, it is worth noting that the team's pre-match field adaptation training was scheduled for 10:30 AM, which is one of the new changes brought by Camacho: most future trainings will be held in the morning.

The weather in Guangzhou has suddenly changed these days, with daytime temperatures dropping to around 10 degrees Celsius. The national team's base at the Qingyuan training center, located in the Guangdong mountains, not only has lower temperatures but also cold rain. Under such conditions, the national team’s final training session in Qingyuan could only focus on restorative slow jogging, stretching, and passing drills. Coach Camacho was concerned about players getting injured during physical confrontation under these weather conditions. Currently, what comforts Camacho is that there are no injured players in the national team, and all players are available for the match. Only a few national team members from Evergrande (Weibo) need to recover their stamina after participating in the Super Cup.

After finishing their last training session in Qingyuan in the evening, the national team headed to Guangzhou and checked into the JianGuo Hotel, located not far from the Tianhe Sports Center. At 10:30 AM on the 28th, the national team will conduct its field adaptation training at the competition venue, the Guangzhou University Town Sports Center. Scheduling the pre-match field training in the morning is unprecedented for the national team. It is understood that this is one of the new changes brought by Camacho recently: most future trainings will be arranged in the morning.

In fact, since Camacho took office in Kunming, he often scheduled training sessions in the morning. According to European teams' training schedules, most trainings take place in the morning, allowing for good rest in the afternoon and evening. Therefore, Camacho decided that most of the main training sessions would be scheduled in the morning, hoping that the players would gradually adapt to this arrangement.

Since the field adaptation training is scheduled in the morning, the national team's pre-match press conference has been moved up to 10 AM. However, the Jordanian team still follows the game time for their field adaptation, scheduling their pre-match press conference at 3:30 PM and their training at the same time as the match, 4 PM. Such arrangements have made things difficult for journalists covering the event, as it takes nearly an hour by car from the university town to the city center, and there is a long interval between the two teams' press conferences. This means either staying in the university town at noon or making two trips back and forth within the city.
