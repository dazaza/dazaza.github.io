Madi Kochavi: Urban flood warning is an international topic

by ditan360 on 2010-06-17 14:38:35

Low Carbon Network exclusive - In May 1672, England and France jointly declared war on the Netherlands, launching attacks from land and sea respectively. On June 15th, unable to withstand the French army's assault, the Netherlands was forced to open its sluice gates, breach its sea dikes, and flood its capital Amsterdam itself to prevent it from being occupied by French troops.

Nowadays, in Amsterdam, known as the "Venice of the North," an invisible "sea dike" has been built to counteract seawater and flood intrusion, ensuring the safety of the land below sea level.

"This invisible 'sea dike' is the urban management and crisis response system." On the 13th, Mati Kochavi, Chairman of the Board of AGT International Group, said to reporters.

Mati Kochavi had just accompanied members of the Dutch royal family, Pieter Christiaan, President of the Dutch Custodia Group, on a visit to the Shanghai Expo, where they showcased innovative solutions for this urban management and crisis response system at the Dutch Pavilion.

Mati Kochavi also announced on the same day that the US-based AGT International Group would enter the Chinese market for urban management, water management safety, and disaster prevention, introducing advanced technology through pilot demonstrations.

Helping China build a warning system

"In recent years, China's urban flood prevention work has made substantial progress, but compared to international advanced levels, there are still some gaps." On the 12th, Mati Kochavi was told this by Hu Sisi, Vice Minister of Water Resources of China during his visit.

During their meeting, Hu Sisi further informed Mati Kochavi that the overall level of flood warning in Chinese cities is not yet high, and there is an urgent need to strengthen urban hydrological construction, establish a monitoring, prediction, and warning system for urban torrential rain and floods, providing scientific decision support for urban flood management. "We hope that AGT International Group, as a global leader in urban flood monitoring, forecasting, and warning, can provide more advanced urban flood prevention concepts and technical solutions for Chinese cities."

This news greatly invigorated Mati Kochavi.

"China is undergoing earth-shaking reforms and changes, and we hope to participate in this process, using our high-end technology and capabilities to help China grow together." Mati Kochavi said during a live interview with People's Daily Online on the 13th.

He told reporters that the "urban management and crisis response system" was developed by teams of experts from the Netherlands and other countries. "More than 60% of the Netherlands' land lies below sea level, constantly threatened and troubled by floods, making its natural topography an ideal testing ground for this technology." Urban flood warnings remain an international challenge.

According to information provided by the journalist, this system includes three parts:

An information center. Responsible for real-time monitoring of every corner of the city and collecting relevant information; An information processing center. Integrating research teams, expert teams, and intelligent computers to analyze data and promptly propose solutions; A command center. Implementing and applying these solutions.

"Specifically, this system helps city managers predict what will happen." He said that before events occur, evaluations are conducted, allowing people to clearly and accurately identify what kind of threat will occur where, and if this threat occurs, when it will happen. This enables managers to better handle emergencies.

Currently, all basic urban service management such as transportation, energy, water, food supply, healthcare, and transport usually require different institutions to supervise and manage them. Mati Kochavi believes that under this complex network structure, AGT Group's urban safety emergency response system could be more effective.

This system collects information through tens of thousands of monitors and sensors placed throughout the city. Mati Kochavi informed reporters that after successfully promoting and applying this system in the Netherlands, currently, AGT Group is fully developing the world's largest urban safety management project in the Middle East. This project will integrate over 10,000 high-tech sensors, unified standard command and control systems, mature analytical capabilities, and the most advanced data systems.

Conducting demonstration projects

At the Shanghai Expo, AGT Group specially launched flood safety emergency response solutions and water management modules for the Chinese market. These solution modules connect cities into a whole, timely collecting flood hazard information and issuing warnings before flood disasters occur.

"China has large rivers like the Yangtze River and Yellow River, with increasing populations living and working along their banks. How to effectively monitor and warn against floods has become an urgent issue to solve." Mati Kochavi said.

In the first half of this year, several southern provinces in China experienced continuous heavy rains, resulting in severe urban flooding in many cities. On May 8th, a super-heavy downpour almost submerged the entire city of Guangzhou, affecting or damaging 1409 vehicles. On June 15th, Nanning in Guangxi Autonomous Region was hit by heavy rain, with some sections of the city experiencing severe flooding exceeding one meter deep, causing temporary traffic disruption.

"Technically, it's impossible to forecast extremely heavy rainfall more than 24 hours in advance, which makes defense and response difficult." According to Liu Min, Director of the Wuhan Regional Climate Center, the current accuracy rate for predicting heavy rain within 24 hours nationwide is only 15%. In terms of urban safety emergency monitoring systems, most cities are in a "blind" state.

However, in some provincial capitals, preliminary urban safety monitoring networks have been established, focusing on urban transportation and neighborhood security surveillance. Mati Kochavi told reporters that the existing cameras, surveillance systems, and corresponding infrastructure in large cities actually present an advantage, potentially reducing the investment costs for implementing urban management and crisis response systems.

"We plan to select a city in China as a pilot city to carry out demonstration projects." Mati Kochavi said that with the help of the China Mayors Association, AGT Group has already discussed with multiple mayors in China.

"The final choice of which city will be the pilot city has not been determined yet. But we will meet with the mayors for a second time soon." Mati Kochavi said that AGT Group is preparing to set up its own branch in China and make large-scale investments. "I believe that the technology and solutions developed in China will also be promoted to other countries in the near future."
