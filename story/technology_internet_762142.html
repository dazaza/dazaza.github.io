Agent revelation: how does 360 compete with Baidu?

by anonymous on 2013-07-24 13:58:18

After causing a stir online, 360 Search ultimately still needs to focus on monetization. As such, establishing a search marketing system has become one of the main tasks for 360 this year. Under the immense pressure from Baidu's extensive nationwide channels, how is 360, as a latecomer, able to break into the market?

The reporter interviewed some local agents of 360 Search, uncovering the expansion situation of 360's search marketing system. We learned that 360's rapidly growing market share made the agents smell an opportunity to make money, and Baidu's tightened agency rights in recent years indirectly contributed to the agents switching sides. Both the number of 360's current agents and order volume have seen significant increases compared to the beginning of the year.

The specific details are as follows:

1. In fact, the agency rights for 360 Search are highly sought after by the agents. So far, 360 Search agents cover 16 provinces and cities, totaling 27 agencies, whereas four months ago, this number was only 13. Moreover, many local agents have already started developing sub-agencies. The agency rights for 360 Search are quite popular locally. "It's like during the early development phase of Baidu; many agents back then reaped substantial benefits. Therefore, when 360 entered the search market, they were all eager to secure the agency rights as soon as possible," said an agent from Henan province to Sina Tech. They obtained the 360 agency rights last month.

2. The development of 360 agents also benefited from the gap created by Baidu reclaiming its agency rights. The aforementioned Henan agent stated, "After so many years of development, Baidu has reclaimed many local agency rights and established branch companies or provincial chief agents." 360 has not yet set up branch companies in various locations and still mainly relies on agents. Among these agents, some have previously represented Baidu ads, others have represented Sogou or Sou ads, and some even represented 3721 ads. However, currently, they have shifted their focus to 360, with most agents establishing independent companies specifically for representing 360 ads.

3. Low prices are an important advantage for 360 Search ads locally. An agent from Wuhan believed that the current advantages of 360 Search ads are: less competition, lower prices, and higher conversion rates. "Some keyword ad prices for 360 Search are less than one-fifth of Baidu's," he said. With the reputation of being the "second largest search engine" and the prior market education provided by Baidu, this agent claims that promotion isn't too difficult. Nevertheless, many clients are still observing, as they "also want to see if 360 is truly willing to dedicate itself to improving the search engine."

4. In just half a year, the order volume for 360 Search ads has grown rapidly. In March, we reported that 360 Search completed approximately 1000 orders in Beijing over three months. A salesperson from Tiandiyizai, a 360 agent in Beijing, told us that now they can exceed 600 orders per month alone. There are two agents for 360 Search in Beijing: Tiandiyizai and Jutao Culture. The latter entered later and is relatively smaller in scale. Just last month, Henan SanbaiLiu, which just obtained the agency rights, has already completed more than 1000 orders.

5. The average transaction price per customer is around ten thousand yuan, which is not low. The minimum account opening fee for 360 Search ads is 4600 yuan, including a 600 yuan service fee and 4000 yuan prepaid deposit. According to the Wuhan agent, the current average transaction price per customer is about ten thousand yuan. For comparison, Baidu's average revenue per customer last quarter was 14,500 yuan.

6. 360 Search ads have covered all industries. Currently, 360 Search ads have covered all industries except healthcare. Recently, they also opened up previously restricted industries such as health supplements and adult products. Notably, 360 currently only accepts corporate clients and does not accept individual clients, and each company can only place ads for one website (Baidu can accept individual clients). Multiple agents mentioned they are developing offline customers in education and consulting sectors.

But can 360 threaten Baidu? No.

Although 360's agency system has initially taken shape, its competition with Baidu is still at a very preliminary stage. When talking to a Baidu agent, he bluntly stated: "Currently, 360 does not pose a threat to Baidu."

From the feedback of various 360 agents, the current competition is not yet intense. "360 Search is now more about giving customers a new market and a new choice," said an agent. Many customers will place ads on both platforms.

However, this is definitely a phased situation. 360's search volume is still small and requires more subsequent investment. Nonetheless, the monetization of search ads is undoubtedly the most imaginative income growth point for 360 going forward. When 360's client base truly grows and prices rise, there may be another inevitable battle between 360 and Baidu.
