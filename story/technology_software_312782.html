Two Arsenal starters confirmed out against Liverpool as Wenger refuses Cruyff's call for Fabregas

by qinglai888 on 2009-12-17 17:54:49

Arsenal vs Liverpool: Two Confirmed Absentees; Wenger Again Rejects Cruyff's Call for Fabregas

Sina Sports - The away match against Burnley this Wednesday is not expected to be too challenging for Arsenal, with the sole aim being to secure three points and reclaim their position above Villa. However, Wenger's team has encountered more injuries. In Sunday's match against Liverpool, mainstay Denilson suffered a back injury, while Traore injured his groin. Arsenal's official website reported that both players have been confirmed unavailable for the match against Burnley.

There is also some good news, as Eboue, who missed the Liverpool game due to a groin strain, has returned to the squad and can bolster the right flank options. Wenger said: "There was too little recovery time after Sunday's game, so we've picked up a few more injuries. Denilson is definitely out of contention, possibly available for Hull on Saturday, but we must check thoroughly to ensure his back is fully recovered before he plays. However, Eboue has returned to the team."

After defeating Liverpool, Wenger was in high spirits, with his team completely overcoming the shadow of their heavy defeat by Chelsea. Discussing the championship prospects this season, Wenger stated that the 09-10 Premier League title race is the most open (in terms of the number of teams involved) since he arrived at Arsenal. Wenger, speaking in an interview with the official website, said: "No team has been able to maintain a dominant lead early in the season. Instead, four teams are closely entangled, and I believe there are six, seven, or even eight teams capable of surprising anyone on any given weekend. Clearly, this is the most open championship race I've seen in my 13 years as coach, and I feel many teams have a chance to win the league title."

Respect from other teams does not equate to pessimism about his own team. For Arsenal, Wenger always has a special confidence. He said: "I was deeply convinced from the start of the season that this would be our year, and I said the same after the Chelsea game. The reason I say this is because: look at us as a whole, how well we've improved, how strong our team spirit and quality are, and how thoroughly prepared we are for each match like it's a battle. From September to May next year, all of this will continue to improve."

For Arsenal to maintain its championship momentum, the importance of Cesc Fabregas is undeniable. With Van Persie injured, Fabregas is almost the sole guiding light for Arsenal. Wenger naturally wants to ensure Fabregas can play as well as possible in every key match. Some external requests can be refused if necessary, such as the friendly match between Catalonia and Argentina scheduled for next month on the 22nd. Catalonia coach Johan Cruyff requested that Fabregas play in this match at Camp Nou, but Wenger firmly rejected it.

Fabregas takes pride in his Catalan heritage and admires Cruyff, but Wenger disregards these sentiments. He has the right to reject such requests because Catalonia is not recognized as an official FIFA national team. However, the 22-year-old Fabregas feels physically fine, and since there are no double headers next week, he believes he has time to visit Spain. He said: "I'll play against Burnley on Wednesday and then Hull City three days later in the league, but after that, there will be nearly a week without Premier League games, so I can return to Spain on the 22nd to play for Catalonia."

However, Wenger can only focus on his own team. There is a crucial match against Villa next weekend, where the other three big clubs have all lost to Villa, raising questions about Arsenal's ability to perform. Van Persie was injured during international duty for Holland, and Liverpool clearly refused to allow Mascherano to represent Argentina in the match against Catalonia. Wenger, who has always disliked the complications of international matches, naturally would not agree for Fabregas to play in a match that doesn't count as an A-level international. Wenger said: "We must respect the hierarchy of priorities, and the priority is the club itself."

Besides this match, Spain has another highly anticipated friendly next week, which is the Spanish All-Stars versus the African All-Stars. This match is called the Second African Charity Match, jointly initiated by UNICEF and the Kanoute Foundation, and will take place at the Bernabeu. Originally, Arsenal's five players were included in the African team list: Song, Nasri, Diaby, Sagna, and Eboue. Clearly, besides some native-born players, those with African heritage were also counted. Wenger wouldn't agree with such arrangements either. Therefore, in the final list, there isn't a single Arsenal player, in fact, not even one current Premier League player. The closest connection to the Premier League, aside from the founder of the Kanoute Foundation, former West Ham and Tottenham forward and current Sevilla player Mali's Frederic Kanoute, might just be former Manchester United striker Manucho, who recently joined Valladolid.
