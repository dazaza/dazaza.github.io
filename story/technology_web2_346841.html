Randomly listening to songs from the QQ Music playlist

by longyixun on 2010-04-16 16:51:50

Today, I casually flipped through the music on QQ Music and wanted to change the collection in my space. Originally, I had no clear purpose because I rarely listen to songs normally, and I can't remember the names of the songs or singers that I've heard. Listening randomly to the songs in QQ Music's random playlist, I found it a bit boring. Most newly released songs seem to be crudely produced for profit; some melodies are so bizarre that they disrupt one's train of thought.

Suddenly, I remembered some songs I listened to during college, especially those from 1995 and 1996. At that time, I listened to some foreign language songs, but strangely, I don't have much impression of Chinese songs from that period. Perhaps it was because campus radio played them too often. Instead, the cassette tapes of foreign songs I bought remain vivid in my memory. These tapes were called "Music Heaven." After searching on Baidu, I discovered there are still some loyal nostalgic fans of MH (referring to Music Heaven, not Men's Health). There was a post on Tianya in 2004 where a group of people reminisced about the past. It turns out that MH was a beacon in the hearts of many people back then. It was probably the earliest domestic audio magazine dedicated to introducing Western pop music. I always lagged behind trends, but surprisingly, I kept up with fashion when I entered college by buying several early issues of MH and later its supplement, "The Rhythm of the Sun," which is why others find it strange that someone who doesn’t listen to songs knows a few Japanese songs.

In the university years without internet or much television, I relied on an 80-yuan Jinghua Walkman and these cassettes to pass the time. I liked listening to English songs for two reasons: first, I was influenced by the cassettes my sister bought when I was in junior high school; second, I didn't need to memorize the lyrics since I didn't have the ability to sing along. While listening to the songs, I could read books without being distracted. The "Classic Golden Hits of American and British Pop" only became popular later, and songs like "Yesterday Once More" felt outdated compared to MH.

I listened repeatedly to the first issue of MH. For ten yuan, you got a cassette tape and a simple magazine that introduced popular music and provided lyrics. I most clearly remember the last song of that issue, whose lyrics weren't printed in the magazine. It was mentioned that if you could transcribe the lyrics correctly and send them to the magazine, you would receive the second issue of MH as a gift. Though I knew I couldn't do it, I still listened to that song many times. To this day, I only remember that it was a very beautiful song. Driven by curiosity, I searched for the track lists of various issues on Baidu and eventually found the song again on QQ Music: "If You Leave Me Now..." When I heard the first line, my eyes welled up with tears. Scenes from my freshman year in 1995 came flooding back— the atmosphere in the dormitory, the narrow corridors, the ping-pong table downstairs, the old classrooms, and all those people and events. They suddenly hit me with an overwhelming sense of nostalgia. Youth—it feels so distant yet vividly clear...

Below is the list of tracks from the first 15 issues of MH, which may evoke similar feelings in others... MH has now ceased publication, reportedly because it gradually leaned towards underground music, which wasn't widely accepted. I stopped paying attention just before I graduated due to the increasing heavy metal and electronic music. Nevertheless, I am grateful for the memories MH gave me in that era.
