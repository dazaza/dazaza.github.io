Twitter Posts $64.6 Million Loss in Q3, Revenue Doubles

by anonymous on 2013-10-19 11:55:41

The S-1 amendment document that Twitter submitted to the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) on Tuesday revealed that this company's revenue in the third quarter grew more than doubled year-on-year.

The document showed that Twitter's third-quarter revenue reached $168.6 million, up 105% from $82.3 million in the same period last year; net loss was $64.6 million, lower than the $21.6 million net loss of the same period last year. In addition, the number of Twitter users continues to rise. As of the end of September this year, the average monthly active user base had reached 232 million, higher than 215 million as of the end of June.

Sources disclosed that Twitter is currently seeking to raise over $1 billion through its initial public offering (IPO). However, Twitter has not yet determined when it will conduct its IPO. A report by CNBC said that Twitter would start its IPO roadshow on October 25 and expects to announce the issue price after the securities market closes on November 14, with plans to list on November 15.

Twitter's S-1 amendment document also showed that the company has finalized its listing on the New York Stock Exchange (NYSE), with the stock code "TWTR". For Nasdaq, Twitter's choice of NYSE is undoubtedly a heavy blow. During Facebook's listing on Nasdaq last year, technical glitches at the Nasdaq Stock Exchange caused delays in trading Facebook's shares.

Regarding Twitter's decision to list on the NYSE, the NYSE expressed, "This is a decisive victory for the NYSE. We appreciate Twitter's trust in our platform and look forward to working with them."
