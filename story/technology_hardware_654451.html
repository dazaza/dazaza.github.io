III. Comparison between Collagen Tripeptides and Ordinary Collagen Peptides:

Collagen in the human body is lost every day, so it is necessary to supplement an appropriate amount of collagen to maintain the youthful state of the skin. However, large molecular collagens (composed of 100 to 1000 amino acids) or polypeptides must be decomposed into individual amino acids before absorption, then randomly synthesized into collagen required by bones, blood vessels, tendons, etc., "distributed" to the skin. Only a very small part of it can be absorbed even with long-term and large amounts of supplementation, making it difficult to feel the "beautifying" effect on the skin. What the skin needs is actually "collagen tripeptides," which consist of three amino acids. This form of collagen is faster and easier to absorb than regular collagen and can directly be used to synthesize fresh collagen. However, digestive enzymes in the human body cannot break down regular collagen into "collagen tripeptides." The acquisition of high-purity collagen tripeptides significantly enhances the synthesis ability of new collagen in the skin, greatly improving the "beautifying efficiency." Both collagen tripeptides and collagen peptides have moisturizing and beautifying effects. Since collagen tripeptides contain both small molecular components and larger molecular components, they not only have the same skincare effects as ordinary collagen peptides but are also more stable, effective, and obvious.

IV. Q&A about Collagen Tripeptides:

1. How to distinguish between first-generation, second-generation, and third-generation collagen products?

Answer: First, explain what third-generation collagen is: Third-generation collagen is commonly known as collagen tripeptides or tripeptide collagen. A typical collagen molecule consists of 3,000 amino acids (3,000 molecular weight), which is difficult for the body to absorb and utilize, let alone be directly absorbed by the skin.

Tripeptide collagen cuts the 3,000 amino acids into the smallest collagen molecular structure, i.e., a combination of three amino acids. Tripeptide collagen has superior absorption properties compared to regular collagen and can directly penetrate into the dermis layer, providing better moisturization effects.

1) Large molecular collagen has a molecular weight of about 300,000 Dalton, collagen peptides have a molecular weight of 1000-3000 Dalton and above, while collagen tripeptides have a molecular weight of 280 Dalton.

2) In 100ml of cold water at 4°C, dissolve 50g of sample collagen: After 30 minutes, it is not completely dissolved, and the solution is turbid; CTP collagen tripeptides: Completely dissolved after 9 minutes, with clear solution. Collagen tripeptides have smaller molecular weights, better solubility, and stronger stability.

3) The absorption pore size of human skin and small intestine is less than 1000 Dalton. Large molecular collagen cannot be absorbed, collagen peptides can be partially absorbed, but collagen tripeptides can be 100% absorbed. Therefore, their main difference lies in the size of the molecular weight. Collagen tripeptides are the basic structure of human skin, composed of three amino acids. The size of the molecular weight determines the absorption and effectiveness of the product. Collagen tripeptides with a molecular weight of 280 Dalton represent the most perfect collagen structure.

2. What is collagen tripeptide? What is CTP collagen tripeptide? What's the difference?

Answer: Collagen tripeptide (collagen tripeptide, abbreviated as CTP) is a sequence composed of three amino acids arranged as "glycine (G)-proline (P)-X (other amino acids)." It is the smallest unit that gives collagen biological activity. Its structure can be simply represented as GLY-X-Y, with a molecular weight of 280D. Collagen tripeptide and CTP collagen peptide or CTP collagen tripeptide refer to the same product, just expressed differently.

3. What are the advantages of collagen tripeptides compared to the previous two generations of products?

Answer: Similar to question 1, the main advantages include smaller molecular weight, better absorption, and higher utilization rate.

4. What are the technical advantages of collagen tripeptides?

Answer: Collagen tripeptides are obtained through globally patented composite enzyme gradient enzymatic hydrolysis technology and peptide separation technology. After this technology purifies collagen tripeptides, they have lower molecular weights and higher absorption and utilization rates.

5. What is the relationship between collagen protein, collagen peptides, collagen oligopeptides, and collagen tripeptides?

Answer: Collagen protein - collagen peptides - collagen oligopeptides - collagen tripeptides are arranged in order of decreasing molecular weight.
