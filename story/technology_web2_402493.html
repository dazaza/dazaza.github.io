ZT How many things in life are relatively indispensable

by vnsdct474 on 2011-04-02 14:03:59

No matter how upset you are, don't forget to smile; no matter how anxious you are, be careful with your tone of voice;

No matter how bitter it is, don't forget to keep it up; if you're tired, take care of yourself.

Be modest in your behavior and you will become more mature step by step; be aggressive in your work and you will become better and better.

When you succeed, don't forget the past; when you fail, don't forget that there is a future.

For what you hope to get, try your best; for what you have no hope to get, don't mind, then no matter whether you win or lose, your posture will be ugly.

Life is not a one-way road. If one road is blocked, you can turn around.

Tears and sweat have similar chemical compositions, but the former can only win you sympathy, while the latter can help you win victories.

Change is a required course in life, and becoming mature is an optional course.

Take tempering as the foundation, and learn health; take progress as the foundation, and learn knowledge;

Take moral improvement as the foundation, and learn how to be a person; take adaptation as the foundation, and learn survival.

The four basic principles of life: know how to choose, learn to give up, endure loneliness, and withstand temptation.

When everyone is low-key, you can be high-profile, but don't go off key.

Learn to forget is a skill in life, and learn to smile is an art of life.

What is a good job: one does not affect daily life, two does not affect family reunions, three can support the family.

Laziness is like rust, which consumes the body more than labor.

The best way to make dreams come true is to wake up.

Fools have no worries, wise men often enjoy happiness. It's not because they possess everything they love, but because they love everything they possess.

There are several things in life that you must absolutely not lose: the power of self-control, a calm mind, hope, and confidence!

1. Society is full of unfair phenomena. Don't think about reforming it; you can only adapt to it first. (Because you can't control it).

2. The world doesn't care about your self-esteem; people only see your achievements. Before you have achievements, don't overemphasize self-esteem too much. (Because the more you emphasize self-esteem, the more disadvantageous it is for you).

3. You just graduated from high school, usually you won't become a CEO, until you actually get the CEO position. (Until this point, people won't care that you only graduated from high school).

4. When you find yourself in a man-made predicament, don't complain; you can only silently learn from the experience. (You need to quietly rally and rise again).

5. You should understand: before you were born, your parents weren't as dull as they are now. You should consider that this is the great price they paid to raise you. (You must always be grateful and filial to them, that's the hard truth).

6. In school, where you rank isn't that important, but in society, it is different. No matter where you go, you will be ranked and classified. (Society and companies often rank people, it's common, so you need to have the courage to compete).

7. Schools have holidays, but in a company, it's different. You almost can't rest, and rarely can you relax during holidays. (Otherwise, you'll fall behind right from the start of your career, and may even stay behind forever).

8. In school, teachers will help you learn, but in a company, they won't. If you think the teachers at school demand a lot from you, that's because you haven't started working in a company yet. Because if the company isn't strict with you, you'll be unemployed. (You must clearly realize: companies are stricter on you than schools).

9. People all love watching TV dramas, but you shouldn't watch them, that's not your life. If you work in a company, you won't have time to watch TV dramas. (I advise you not to watch them, otherwise, if you get hooked on TV series and enjoy them thoroughly, you'll lose the qualification for success).

10. Never criticize others behind their backs, especially don't criticize your boss for being ignorant, harsh, and incompetent. (Because such a mindset will lead you down a bumpy and difficult path of growth).
