by anonymous on 2011-06-07 17:38:35

The money earned from selling waste bottles, I all put them in the Anglo-Chinese dictionary at home. When it reaches 550 yuan, I will take it out for my father to use, enough to buy a bottle of albumin... Little Chunhan indeed showed his extraordinary way of life as a teenager because he has a father who is seriously ill and needs more than 3000 yuan per month to sustain his life.

Han Aijun said that her son had always been sensible. The clothes and shoes on him were given by others, and he never complained. He grew up eating steamed buns or fried rice for breakfast. One time, Chunhan even asked her what fried dough sticks taste like.

The son insisted on replying: if we have more money for dad, he can be treated for a longer period.

He pouted to the reporter and said: It's cold, so I picked too few. Last summer vacation, I didn't eat for a whole day and collected over 90 bottles...

The young man doesn't know the taste of fried dough sticks, but the walls of his house are full of certificates of merit.

Chunhan said he never felt ashamed when classmates saw him picking up plastic bottles. His classmates also never laughed at him. A classmate surnamed Fang even helped him pick up bottles to sell.

Xu Chunhan did not disappoint his mother's expectations and kept excellent grades. On the wall of their home, there were many certificates of merit such as "Good Student", "Good Child", and "Second Prize in Writing Competition". Han Aijun was proud of her son and also approved of her son picking up bottles every day.

After finishing homework at night, Chunhan would help his father massage his legs and have a heart-to-heart talk with his father.

Little Chunhan massaged his father's legs who was lying on the bed.

He started picking up bottles in the fourth grade. His mother, Han Aijun, talked about this matter with some sadness: My husband and I only wanted him to study well and didn't want him to know about our family's difficulties. But... Our family is poor, and the three of us sleep on one bed. One night, while my husband and I were discussing his illness, he overheard it!

When he sees an old grandmother pushing a wheelchair for an old grandfather, he would proactively offer help until they reach home. If the grandmother gives him a coin, he would accept it; if she doesn't give anything, he wouldn't say much.

Every noon and evening after school, this young man's figure would appear in the neighborhood, Wu Guang Gate entrance, and Zhongshan Park. He lowers his head and focuses on picking up plastic bottles discarded by passersby.

Yesterday at noon, 11-year-old little Chunhan went home after school, took out four flattened plastic bottles from his schoolbag, threw them on the balcony, and frowned.

Even so, Han Aijun absolutely did not want her son to pick up bottles and still hoped that he would concentrate on studying. She said that her son's previous dream was to become a police officer, but now it had changed. "I want to be a manager," Xu Chunhan said because being a manager could earn a lot of money to treat his father.

The Libei Community of Bao Feng Street in Kou District where Chunhan lives, secured a 3500 yuan relief fund for this family and also sent clothes and shoes to little Chunhan. These, relative to the Xu family's monthly medical expenses of more than 3000 yuan and a debt of over 100,000 yuan, although couldn't solve the fundamental problem, were enough to make Han Aijun feel grateful.

One night, while sleeping, he heard his parents talking and began silently picking up bottles.

Xu Chunhan, a sixth-grade student at Wuhan Stadium Primary School, comes from a difficult family. His father, Xu Hesheng, is 45 years old, was laid off early, and six years ago was diagnosed with liver cirrhosis; his mother, Han Aijun, is 41 years old and has been unemployed, relying on低保 (low-income assistance) to survive.

Soon, Xu Hesheng was found to have ascites due to liver disease, and Han Aijun had to quit her part-time job to take care of her husband. One day, she discovered that her son was returning home late from school and his grades were dropping. At around 9:30 pm, she finally found her son and learned the truth: "I want to help mom collect bottles to sell for money to treat dad." "No matter how hard it is, I can't let you collect bottles and delay your studies..." Han Aijun hugged her son and cried.
