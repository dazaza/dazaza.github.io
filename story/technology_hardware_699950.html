Chapter Three: Don't Get Angry Easily When Doing Things "No, it's impossible, absolutely impossible!" "This thing is going to kill me, Qin Yuan Chun... I'm really going to be killed by him!" "I will never forgive him!" We all are familiar with this kind of anger. Don't get angry easily when doing things. A little bit of anger becomes "rage" when it gets bigger. This is a momentary emotional impulse. When facing things that go against our wishes or hearing unpleasant words, if we can't calmly deal with them in a rational and correct way, and don't use reasonable methods to handle them accurately and appropriately, such as arguing with the other party, calling to scold the other party, immediately finding someone to complain, warning or threatening the other party, using fists to solve problems violently, even throwing things, banging our heads against the wall, kicking tables or dogs, cursing, shouting, being extremely angry, etc., these are the most brutal and simple expressions of anger. Anger reflects the lack of rationality. This is a bad personality trait, and the more stupid and rude people are, the easier they get angry. When anger reaches its limit, it is easiest to lead to the loss of reason, saying things that should not be said, and doing things that should not be done. Therefore, we find that those who often apologize after the fact are mostly those who love to lose their temper and get furious for no reason. Anger and rage are punishing oneself for others' mistakes, and what gets hurt can only be one's own physical and mental health. When a person is angry and emotionally agitated, the entire sympathetic nervous system is activated, causing pupil dilation, increased heart rate, rapid breathing, arterial contraction, increased adrenal secretion, etc., and some people may even grit their teeth and shake all over... In such a situation, it's easy to act on impulse, resulting in harm to oneself and others, causing irreparable regrets. Acting on personal anger alone is just the bravery of a common man, and in the end, encountering misfortune and adversity, who can you blame? However, wise people can clearly recognize the harm of losing one's temper, and even when in adverse circumstances, they don't easily get angry, but face things calmly and peacefully.
