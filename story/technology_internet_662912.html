Natural Rubber Fundamentals: Although China has seen a significant increase in inventory, globally the fundamentals of natural rubber are gradually presenting good opportunities. On the supply side, the gradual decline in natural rubber prices has drawn high attention from producing countries. Producing nations have repeatedly issued statements and held meetings to boost rubber prices, and have also implemented some measures such as reducing market supply and setting minimum prices. On the demand side, with the recovery of the global auto market, especially the vigorous development of the automotive industry in developing countries like Mexico, Indonesia, Thailand, South Africa, and Russia, these countries' automobile industries are still in a period of rapid growth. The automobile industries of traditional automotive powerhouses, the United States and Japan, are also gradually recovering. Although China's growth rate has slowed down, due to its large base, the demand pull for natural rubber remains relatively fast.

Japan's automobile industry has basically recovered to pre-earthquake levels: In June, the domestic production of Nissan and Toyota automobiles has largely returned to normal, reaching 95% and 90% respectively compared to before the earthquake. Previously, Toyota expected to only recover to 70% production by November, while Honda is expected to return to pre-earthquake levels in August.

In general, the global automotive industry is still on the path to growth, and the demand for natural rubber has not decreased. Currently, global inventories of natural rubber are not as high as in China but are instead at relatively normal levels.

In 2010, calculated based on the cost for rubber farmers, the rigid cost of one ton of rubber was around 12,000 yuan/ton. Adding transportation and processing costs, the manufacturer's cost was near 13,500 yuan, which also includes equipment, electricity, and labor expenses.

The Natural Rubber Futures Market in 2010: "China's natural rubber futures market is now just like a casino." Shen Jinrong introduced that the highest open interest in natural rubber futures reached close to 200,000 lots in a day, with the highest trading volume nearing 2 million lots in a day, and 1.5 million lots being a common state. "The trading volume in a single day exceeds China's consumption for two years. There is too much hot money in China's rubber futures trading market!" Globally, the annual consumption of natural rubber is approximately 9 million tons, with China consuming about 3 million tons, accounting for roughly one-third of global consumption. Thus, the global market for natural rubber is not very large, so if the government releases about 100,000 tons of national reserves, it can play a role in stabilizing the entire spot market price of natural rubber.

On January 13, 2011, the closing price of the main contract 1105 of Shanghai natural rubber futures was 38,220 yuan per ton, once again approaching the historical high of 39,800 yuan/ton set in November of the previous year. On February 9, 2011, the Shanghai rubber futures surged to a historical high of 43,500 yuan (43,800 yuan/ton on February 10), whereas in June 2010, the price of Shanghai rubber futures was only around 20,000 yuan.
