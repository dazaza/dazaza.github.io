Beckham 3.0

by hhhiii83805 on 2011-08-27 11:03:27

If you are a staunch Beckham fan, in the past few days on British "Gossip Girl" website and various tabloids, you must have seen the pictures of Beckham's family of five taken last Saturday at Heathrow Airport by diligent reporters, with the headline "Leaving London". The family has long been used to facing the lens; Beckham was holding his four-year-old Cruz, Posh Beckham was leading her second son Romeo who was wearing big sunglasses, and their eldest Brooklyn, already ten years old, was walking proudly as if he had seen it all before. Attentive editors tirelessly introduced the outfits of the three children, as well as the black dress designed by a designer that Posh Beckham was wearing.

The destination of the family was Los Angeles on the other side of the ocean; this was their second long-distance flight within 24 hours. Previously, after a 12-hour flight, they had just returned from a seaside vacation in Panama. Reporters witnessed them entering and exiting a private island villa costing £2000 per night, celebrating their tenth wedding anniversary. Such "flying around", is all because according to the contract, Beckham needs to return to the United States from Europe to continue playing for the LA Galaxy team, which is his third year in Major League Soccer (MLS), so his imminent return has been referred to by American media as "Beckham 3.0".

On Thursday, Beckham will join the LA Galaxy at the Giants Stadium to play against the New York Red Bulls, which will be the first official match of "Beckham 3.0". The MLS official website's FLASH animation vividly promotes the theme of this game: "Beckham is back! A few tickets left!" Prior to this, the Galaxy team had already struggled through 17 rounds in this year's league, currently ranking fifth in the west with a record of 5 wins, 9 draws, and 3 losses, making it extremely difficult to enter the playoffs this year. Remember when the Galaxy team welcomed Beckham, one of the most brilliant stars in history, they were full of excitement hoping to shine across the entire United States. Who would have thought that for two consecutive seasons, the Galaxy team remained a second-tier team, struggling in the regular season. After being injured, Beckham was left on the bench and then returned to Europe in January on loan to play for AC Milan. According to the contract, in October this year, Beckham will once again cross the ocean, leave the Galaxy team, and return to Milan to make his final effort for his super dream of participating in the World Cup for the fourth time consecutively. Capello has only one suggestion for this former superstar who could possibly become an outsider of the England national team: if you want to go to South Africa, you need to return to the European continent first.

There are rumors that once Beckham leaves the Galaxy team, he won't return to the MLS anymore but will choose a European club to end his professional football career. "Body in Galaxy, heart in Europe", naturally the Galaxy team feels humiliated, and the relationship between Beckham and the Galaxy team players upon his parachuting-style return is generally not optimistic. Even MLS executives believe that "conflict" is inevitable.

Such a tense atmosphere is not merely due to Beckham's disloyal behavior of serving two masters, because there are also American internationals who use the time difference between MLS and European leagues to shuttle between the two continents. The reason is actually quite simple: it's because of a book called "The Beckham Experiment: How the World's Most Famous Athlete Tried to Conquer America?" This 304-page book was written by Grant Wahl, a famous football journalist for Sports Illustrated in the US. Its release date was carefully planned to be the day after Beckham returned to training, two days before the official launch of "Beckham 3.0". On July 6, Sports Illustrated specially excerpted the most shocking chapter in advance, revealing many stories of Beckham's failures during the past two years in the Galaxy team. After reading, most readers will remember two keywords - "miser" and "bad captain".

Grant Wahl is somewhat well-known in American soccer circles, having reported on the previous versions of Beckham thanks to his identity with Sports Illustrated. In 2007 when Beckham first arrived in the American soccer scene, he even became the cover figure for Sports Illustrated while wearing the Galaxy's white uniform. The publisher had been asking this football journalist to write a book about Beckham's first year in the US. Due to some twists of fate, Wahl didn't start writing immediately, later following his wife to South Africa for seven months, and finally completed the book this year. As an experienced journalist, Wahl contacted Beckham's agency - "19 Entertainment" - hoping to get the opportunity to interview face-to-face this highest-paid star in the history of American professional football. The agency neither confirmed nor denied, only hinting that the net profit from the last authorized autobiography was $1 million, implying that there would be no talk without money. Helpless, Wahl turned his attention to Beckham's teammates in the Galaxy team, among whom Donovan, the biggest name, poured out his dissatisfaction with Beckham without reservation.

Donovan, the top scorer in MLS, captain of the US national team, and the number one star in the Galaxy team before Beckham's arrival, whose wife Bianca is a major star in CBS's popular TV series, can be considered an American version of Beckham. According to Donovan, when Beckham first arrived at the Galaxy team, the club hinted that he should hand over the captain's armband. After careful consideration, he voluntarily surrendered the sacred captaincy. Originally thinking it was the plan of the high-ups of AEG Group, the parent company of the club, he later found out it was actually orchestrated by "19 Entertainment". Later, "19 Entertainment" went beyond its role, bypassing the management, directly interfering with internal club matters, bringing in Guillerme who was far from in form to take charge, causing the Galaxy team to fall into deep trouble.

In 2007, Beckham played very little due to injury. In 2008, his form improved, but in the crucial last regular-season game, Beckham refused to return to the US after participating in the national team games abroad due to accumulated yellow cards, thus missing the Galaxy's life-and-death battle, resulting in a disastrous defeat for the Galaxy, blocking them from the playoffs. Donovan and other teammates were extremely angry, believing that a captain earning $6.5 million had no excuse not to be with his team, which was the greatest disloyalty to the Galaxy team. Therefore, there was a saying in the team specifically referring to Beckham as "a good player, a bad captain".

Now I haven't had the chance to read this book yet, and I don't have much interest in caring about the trivial matters behind a superstar, including what Donovan disclosed, such as when Beckham invited his teammates for dinner as the senior member of the team but insisted on splitting the bill. At that time, Beckham, whose annual income was close to $50 million, was sitting with many teammates whose annual salaries were less than $20,000, living blue-collar lives in one of the cities with the highest cost of living in the US, and even needed to work a second job besides playing football. In Donovan's view, a rich man eating with workers and being so ungenerous made him an unforgivable "miser".

Faced with Donovan's direct attack, Beckham was prepared and accused the other party of being "unprofessional", demanding a private conversation to clarify things.
