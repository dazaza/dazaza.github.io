Sony has released the full-frame mirrorless A7/A7r cameras - featuring a 36 million pixel sensor.

by geekzhang on 2013-10-05 11:21:27

According to the latest SAR report, Sony will release the NEX-7T and two full-frame NEX cameras with different positioning in the middle of this month. The latter models have abandoned the traditional "NEX" or "AXXXX" naming convention, instead opting for A7 and A7r. Don't be misled by the model names; though they start with "A," they are fully E-mount mirrorless cameras. Furthermore, the relationship between them is not simply about the presence or absence of an anti-aliasing filter.

It is reported that the high-end version, the A7r, features a 36-megapixel sensor, while the lower-tier A7 uses a slightly lower-resolution but more mature 24-megapixel sensor. Perhaps due to the relatively lower resolution, the A7 has a faster continuous shooting speed than the A7r. In addition to resolution differences, another major distinction lies in autofocus speed, as the A7r employs a different focusing technology from the A7 (possibly phase detection? or sensor shift?).

Another pleasant surprise is that the A7 and A7r look almost identical, both adopting a DSLR-like design. Their size is only slightly larger than the RX1, with an appearance similar to Olympus OM-D cameras. In terms of operation, they may retain some NEX elements, such as the grip, dual control dials on the right side, and built-in electronic viewfinder (center-mounted).
