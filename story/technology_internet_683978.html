◇ Ask about emotions: When is life the most beautiful?

Loss is more painful than gain

In life, we always have some possessions that we never get; some wishes that cannot be fulfilled. For example, sometimes I wish I could be like other people's children, asking my mother to buy me a beautiful doll; or like the neighbor's partner who has a piano at home... Even if it's just longing for a deep hug from my parents; or waking up in the morning and seeing my father or mother by my side... Those big or small wishes, which once seemed so hard to achieve, then became the knots or secrets in our hearts. In fact, after growing up, we find out: losing is more painful than not getting, because what we can't get is always a beautiful illusion, even the pain is not real. But, what we once had and then lost will make you feel the real pain and regret.

What has reality changed about us?

Regardless of whether you're from the 60s, 70s, 80s, or 90s generation, every era of people has their own understanding of society and life. When [migrant workers], [light immigrants], [rich second generation], [official second generation] are all around us, have we ever asked ourselves: [What has reality changed about us?] Is it yesterday's honesty, simplicity, kindness, struggle, and persistence; or have we learned to compare, be vain, speculative, and restless? Only when one is calm can they find peace, stability, wisdom, enlightenment, and gain. Calm down and think, how much have you changed over these years? The human spirit is fragile and needs constant encouragement and comfort. Often self-encouraging and self-praising will make the spirit extremely happy. Learning to free the spirit means creating a warm harbor for yourself, often going inside to give your busy and tired spirit a massage, ensuring that all parts of the spirit are frequently maintained and cared for.

◇ The confusion that makes us lose our former beauty

{Conclusion}

Life is like this, gains and losses are unpredictable, misfortune and blessing depend on each other. All things that pass by count as scenery; a relationship, an event, whatever occupies our memory, all are happiness. The lost past doesn't need to be overly cherished, but you must learn to find out what can be lost and what cannot be lost from the loss. Happiness for us: the process of pursuing happiness is the most important! In pursuit and struggle, we cannot forget why we are rushing while we are on the way. Therefore, when we go far, we must know how to look back. You will find that failure makes people strong, parting makes people cherish, pain clarifies people. No matter how good something is, you cannot possess it for too long, what you get is temporary; the important thing is that we must learn not to follow the trend in our struggles, only then can we stand up from past losses, cherish every bit of past beauty, and tomorrow will have new hope.
