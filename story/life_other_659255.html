Flowing in the water, flowers have their blooming seasons.

A cup of turbid wine, I shed thousands of tears for you. The shattered starry sky is like a dream, the black night like eyes, and the drifting clouds like broken words that sever emotional wounds. Quietly fading clouds and evening waves, wanderers at the ends of the earth recall the past and lament autumn as everything goes with the wind. Beneath the bridge, red flowers fall like pitying rain, their appearance also weathered by time. Broken intentions and severed ties hasten aging, white hair like snow. Reflections borrow from flowing water to go westward, ripples arise one after another, desolate like smoke. Looking at the sky outside the window, only empty clouds guard a piece of blue sky. Besides this, it feels empty as if only the soul is there breathing lightly, leaving an indescribable silence. Drifting clouds are like broken words that sever emotional wounds, only the proud heart remains.

In the season of June, youth's atmosphere lingers in suspension. But when standing by the bridge looking far away, the fragmented reflections sunk in the water seem to never dissipate, tirelessly striving to restore their original form. Suddenly, there's a feeling of being in reality yet wandering in a dream-like water world, seeking an answer whose right or wrong is unknown. Flowers have their blooming seasons, following shadows and chasing waves towards the west where the sun sets, fallen flowers have feelings.

Flowers bloom like rain and fall all over the city with the wind, falling without knowing what they hurt. Picking one at random, I am left with a hand full of fragrance. Although the beautiful and delicate body of the flower cannot be retained, its image can be etched in the heart as an eternal memory. Not cherishing beauty is rare, only that serene flower is like water under the starry sky, whispers left as tomorrow's promise. A gentle breeze sends off the boat which carries passengers, a long road of blooming seasons, flowing water is not without intention, fallen flowers also have feelings, staying behind with a sky full of stars.
