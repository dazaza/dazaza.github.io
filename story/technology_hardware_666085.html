Displacement rust preventive oil

Some displacement rust preventive oils use sulfonate salts with strong adsorption as the main rust inhibitor. They can displace moisture and sweat attached to the metal surface, preventing corrosion caused by human sweat. At the same time, they adsorb themselves onto the metal surface and form a strong protective film, preventing the intrusion of external corrosive media. Therefore, they are widely used for rust prevention between processes and surface pretreatment before long-term rust prevention. Many displacement rust preventive oils can also be directly used for sealed rust prevention. When using, petroleum solvents such as kerosene or gasoline can be used for dilution. Therefore, some varieties of these rust preventive oils also fall into the category of solvent-diluted rust preventive oils. During use, attention should be paid to fire prevention and ventilation issues due to solvent evaporation.

According to the domestic standards for displacement rust preventive oils, these oils are divided into grades 1, 2, 3, and 4. Among them, grade 1, 2, and 3 oils are mainly used for packaging and sealing of various metal products and parts, and have a certain ability to displace human sweat. Grade 1 oil is used for ferrous metals. Grade 2 oil is used for ferrous metals and copper. Grade 3 oil is used for ferrous metals and non-ferrous metals. After being diluted with petroleum solvents, grade 1, 2, and 3 oils can be used as process-sealed oils. Grade 4 oil is a sweat-washing oil, used for cleaning and rust prevention between processes.
