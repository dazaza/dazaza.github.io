Press conference of the Environmental Protection Department

by wujizhui on 2011-08-12 06:42:13

[Deputy Director of the News Bureau of the Information Office, Host Hua Qing] Ladies and gentlemen, good morning! Thank you all for attending this press conference held by the State Council Information Office. Today, we have invited Mr. Li Ganjie, Vice Minister of the National Environmental Protection Department, to meet with everyone and introduce the environmental conditions in China as well as answer any questions that interest you. Also present at today's press conference are Mr. Zhao Hualin, Director of the Pollution Prevention and Control Department, and Mr. Cheng Lifu, Director of the Environmental Impact Assessment Department. Now, please allow Mr. Li, the Deputy Minister, to introduce the situation first. [2011-06-03 10:00:00]

[Vice Minister of Environmental Protection, Li Ganjie] Ladies and gentlemen, good morning! I am very glad to meet with friends from the press community. The main purpose of today's press conference is to announce the national environmental status for the year 2010. Relevant materials have already been distributed to all of you, and for more details, please refer to the "Bulletin on the Status of China’s Environment for 2010." Below, I will briefly introduce the relevant situation.

[Li Ganjie] In 2010, some of our environmental quality indicators continued to improve. The annual average concentration of permanganate index at national controlled sections of surface water across the country was 4.9 mg/L, a decrease of 3.9% compared to the previous year and a drop of 31.9% compared to 2005. The annual average concentration of sulfur dioxide in urban air across the country was 0.034 mg/m³, reaching the secondary standard of the national ambient air quality standards, a decrease of 2.8% compared to the previous year and a drop of 19.0% compared to 2005. However, the overall environmental situation remains extremely severe, facing many difficulties and challenges.

[Li Ganjie] First, pollution of surface water nationwide remains serious. The seven major river systems—Yangtze River, Yellow River, Pearl River, Songhua River, Huai River, Hai River, and Liao River—are generally lightly polluted. Among the 409 national controlled sections of the 204 rivers, the proportions of sections with water quality classified as Class I–III, Class IV–V, and worse than Class V were 59.9%, 23.7%, and 16.4%, respectively. The Yangtze River and Pearl River have generally good water quality, while the Songhua River and Huai River are lightly polluted, the Yellow River and Liao River are moderately polluted, and the Hai River is heavily polluted. Eutrophication issues in lakes (reservoirs) remain prominent; among the 26 lakes (reservoirs) monitored for their nutrient status, 42.3% are in a state of eutrophication.

[Li Ganjie] Second, the overall quality of coastal waters near land is lightly polluted. The proportion of seawater classified as Class I and II is 62.7%, Class III is 14.1%, and Class IV and worse than Class IV is 23.2%. Among the four sea areas, the coastal waters near land in the Yellow Sea and South China Sea have good water quality, the coastal waters near land in the Bohai Sea have poor water quality, and the coastal waters near land in the East China Sea have very poor water quality. Compared to the previous year, the proportion of Class I and II seawater in Jiaozhou Bay increased by 25.0%, whereas the proportion of Class I and II seawater in Bohai Bay, Yangtze River estuary, and Pearl River estuary decreased by over 20.0%.

[Li Ganjie] Third, the overall air quality in cities nationwide is generally good, but pollution in some cities remains severe; the distribution of acid rain regions has remained stable, but acid rain pollution remains severe. Among the 471 county-level and above cities conducting environmental air quality monitoring, 3.6% of the cities met the primary standard, 79.2% met the secondary standard, 15.5% met the tertiary standard, and 1.7% did not meet even the tertiary standard. For the 113 key environmental protection cities, the proportion that met the secondary standard was 73.5%, an increase of 6.2 percentage points compared to the previous year. Among the 494 cities (counties) conducting acid rain monitoring, 249 cities experienced acid rain, accounting for 50.4%, and 107 cities had severe or relatively severe acid rain (annual average pH value <5.0), accounting for 21.6%, basically unchanged from the previous year.

[Li Ganjie] Fourth, the acoustic environment quality in cities nationwide is generally good. Nationwide, 73.7% of cities have good or relatively good regional acoustic environment quality, and among the key environmental protection cities, 72.5% have good or relatively good regional acoustic environment quality. Nationwide, 97.3% of cities have good or relatively good road traffic acoustic environment quality. The daytime compliance rate for various functional zones in cities nationwide is 88.4%, and the nighttime compliance rate is 72.8%.

[Li Ganjie] Fifth, the overall radiation environment quality nationwide is good. The level of environmental ionizing radiation remains stable, and the environmental ionizing radiation levels around nuclear facilities and nuclear technology utilization projects have not seen significant changes overall. The overall situation of environmental electromagnetic radiation levels is good, and the environmental electromagnetic radiation levels around electromagnetic radiation facilities have not seen significant changes overall.

[Li Ganjie] Sixth, some ecological system functions in the country have improved, but major ecological environment problems remain prominent. The trend of biodiversity decline has not yet been effectively curbed, and genetic resources continue to be lost.

[Li Ganjie] Seventh, rural environmental issues are becoming increasingly apparent, agricultural source pollutant emissions are large, the situation in some local areas has improved, but the overall situation remains very severe. In 2010, the environmental protection system made solid progress in its work and completed various tasks satisfactorily.

[Li Ganjie] First, the task of reducing major pollutants was exceeded. Emissions of sulfur dioxide and chemical oxygen demand dropped by 14.29% and 12.45%, respectively, compared to 2005, both exceeding the reduction targets. Second, the comprehensive role of environmental protection in optimizing economic development has become increasingly evident. We completed strategic environmental assessments for key industrial development in five major regions, including the Bohai Rim. We continuously deepened project environmental impact assessments, refusing to accept, approve, delay approval, or return the environmental impact assessment reports of 59 projects that did not meet requirements, involving a total investment of 90.4 billion yuan, setting an insurmountable "firewall" for high-energy-consuming, high-polluting, and resource-intensive projects, low-level repeated construction, and overcapacity projects.

[Li Ganjie] Third, efforts in pollution prevention and control in key basins and regions have continuously intensified. We deeply advanced allowing rivers and lakes to rest and recuperate, assessing the implementation of key basin plans. We established and improved new mechanisms for regional pollution joint prevention and control, successfully completing the air quality assurance tasks for the Shanghai World Expo and Guangzhou Asian Games.

[Li Ganjie] Fourth, we focused on resolving prominent environmental issues that harm public health. Water safety assurance work has been steadily advancing, and comprehensive management of heavy metal pollution has been fully launched. In 2010, for the first time, a special fund of 1.5 billion yuan was allocated for heavy metal pollution prevention and control, supporting comprehensive management in key prevention and control areas, demonstration, and promotion of new technologies. Environmental law enforcement supervision has continuously strengthened, and sudden environmental incidents have been properly handled.

[Li Ganjie] Fifth, rural environmental protection and natural ecological conservation work have continued to strengthen. We actively carried out pilot demonstrations for contiguous rural environmental remediation and organized activities for the International Year of Biodiversity, improving the work system for ecological construction demonstration zones.

[Li Ganjie] Sixth, basic and strategic environmental protection projects have yielded abundant results. We successfully completed the macro-strategic research on China's environmental protection, forming a series of important outcomes, including comprehensive and specialized strategic research reports. The major science and technology special project for water body pollution control and treatment has entered a critical stage.

[Li Ganjie] Seventh, policy and legal affairs, environmental protection planning, scientific and technological monitoring, international cooperation, and environmental publicity and education have steadily progressed. Environmental regulations have been continuously improved, and the role of environmental economic policies has become increasingly evident. The mid-term evaluation of the execution of the "Eleventh Five-Year Plan" for environmental protection has been successfully completed, further reinforcing scientific support, accelerating the advancement of environmental monitoring work, and achieving noticeable results in international environmental cooperation and environmental publicity and education.

[Li Ganjie] Eighth, the level of nuclear and radiation safety supervision has improved. The safety status of nuclear energy and nuclear technology utilization is good, radioactive pollution prevention and control work is steadily progressing, and the quality of the national radiation environment remains good. Ninth, environmental capacity building has been further strengthened. Positive progress has been made in environmental capacity building, and breakthroughs have been achieved in institutional and team building within the environmental protection system.

[Li Ganjie] This concludes my brief introduction to the situation, and now my two colleagues and I are willing to answer your questions.
