International manufacturers accelerate the layout of LED lighting market (1)

by dddgskkgfek9 on 2012-02-14 09:14:52

International manufacturers from various countries are accelerating their layout in the LED lighting market – Building Materials Knowledge - Industry News - Building Materials Network. With the increasing demand for LED lighting, international manufacturers are gradually strengthening their market layouts. Japan's Citizen Holdings subsidiary, Citizen Electronics, plans to integrate its production bases in mainland China and will increase the annual production capacity of lighting white LEDs by 20% during the 2010 fiscal year (April 2010 to March 2011). Meanwhile, the U.S. manufacturer GE will launch a 9-watt bulb using Cree chips, www.qjgx.net, which is expected to be available at the end of 2010, nhl-jerseys-trade.com.

Foreign media reports indicate that Citizen plans to consolidate its current LED production bases in Shanghai and Suzhou into Jiangmen City, Guangdong Province. In addition to the existing factory, Citizen has leased a new factory with an area of 20,000 square meters and will introduce an integrated production line for white light LEDs. Citizen Electronics is the world leader in mobile phone LEDs. Due to the rapid replacement of incandescent bulbs by LED lighting, they plan to increase production and maintain a global market share of more than 10% for lighting LEDs by 2011.

If the increased production costs in Japan (Fukushima and Yamanashi Prefectures) are combined to calculate the expanded production capacity, Citizen’s total investment in expanding production capacity in 2010 will be 1.5 billion yen. In the future, the annual production capacity of white light LEDs (mobile phone use + lighting use) can reach up to 7.2 billion units, rc24h.net.

In addition, GE also plans to launch a 9-watt LED bulb product, paraguayfootball.co.uk, which will have the brightness equivalent to a 40-watt incandescent bulb, with a luminous flux reaching 450 lumens. Energy savings can reach 77%, adopting the XLamp XP LED from the major LED manufacturer Cree. GE stated, blog.colombiafootball.co.uk, that the product is expected to hit the market by the end of the year or early 2011, with a retail price likely between $40 to $50.
