How Long Can E-Commerce "Burn Money to Gain Popularity" Last?

by jdhgsa078 on 2011-08-31 16:35:24

This year, it seems that the group-buying industry has been infected with a wave of layoffs. Following Wotuan and Gaopeng's layoff controversies, recent news of Vancl laying off 5% of its workforce has once again ignited heated discussions in the industry about the "cold winter" facing e-commerce. What is driving such actions from this large manufacturer, which already ranks in the top four in terms of revenue scale in the B2C sector?

Due to marketing costs far exceeding sales for many e-commerce companies, as early as July this year, JD.com CEO Richard Liu had predicted the coming of a cold winter. Liu believed that from the end of this year to the first half of next year, the winter for e-commerce will arrive, and its arrival will be nearly a year earlier than expected.

Regarding Vancl's layoffs, external speculation suggests it may be related to cost-cutting measures and preparation for an IPO, but Vancl has not provided any public response. Currently, Vancl ranks in the top four in terms of revenue scale in China's B2C sector. At the beginning of 2011, Vancl CEO Chen Nian stated that they would invest heavily, spending 1 billion yuan on market promotion, with a sales target of 10 billion yuan for 2011.

As a leading enterprise in China's e-commerce sector, the signal sent by Vancl's layoffs, as well as how long the burn-money-first-and-raise-capital-later sprint model can last, has sparked reflection within the industry.

In this regard, some e-commerce experts say that the e-commerce industry, which was once characterized by reckless spending, is now entering a new phase where only a few companies with traditional resources and expertise in online marketing will survive.

Leke.com founder Liu Bing also expressed on Weibo: If the role of e-commerce in the Internet era is to destroy value through low prices and discounts, then this model is not one that creates value and goes against the trend of wealth creation in human social development. E-commerce must find a way to create wealth and add value to products to truly be valuable!

Pai Dai Wang analyst Chengdong Li expressed on Weibo: From a game theory perspective, do you think the mutual destruction among e-commerce companies through price competition is reasonable? Regardless of whether you believe it or not, I know that group-buying is doomed.

Meituan.com executive Huiwen Wang also stated that burning money for attention is the Achilles heel for group-buying websites from the second half of this year to the first half of next year. Once a group-buying website runs out of money, the only choice left is to abscond with the funds.

Most industry insiders believe that from the initial peak to the current wave of layoffs, the Pandora's box of China's e-commerce has been quietly opened.
