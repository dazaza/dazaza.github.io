The scientific research team at the Great Wall Station in the Antarctic all use QQ and QQMail!

by bjseeprof on 2009-12-07 11:34:04

At 2 p.m. on February 22, our group arrived at the Chilean Air Force airport in Punta according to plan and waited for the plane flying from Santiago, the capital of Chile, which would take us to Antarctica. Due to sudden change in weather from sunny to stormy rain, we had to wait there. After waiting for three hours, we finally boarded the Hercules transport aircraft of the Chilean Air Force. The plane flew from Punta and after two hours and a half flight, it landed at the Chilean Air Force airport on King George Island in Antarctica at about 7 p.m. This island is also the location of China's Great Wall Station! Director Pei of the Great Wall Station and assistant Xiao Li drove specially to the airport to welcome our group. After driving for more than ten minutes, we arrived at the Great Wall Station. All scientific researchers and staff of the Great Wall Station, about thirty people, came out to the gate to warmly welcome us with gongs and drums, and they held up a huge welcome banner. Everyone was very excited and shook hands and greeted each other one by one. The scale of the Great Wall Station is larger than I imagined, with more than ten buildings of various sizes, including large equipment buildings for various communications, meteorology, logistics, etc. We were arranged to stay in single rooms in the living building of the station. The living building is a two-story building. On the first floor is the only canteen of the Great Wall Station and some comprehensive function units such as the China Post Office, and on the second floor are single dormitory rooms. After a short rest, the director invited our group to have dinner together in the VIP room of the canteen. The VIP room displays all kinds of souvenirs given to the Great Wall Station from home and abroad. As soon as we entered the room, we saw the banner and Great Wall tapestry used during the Spring Festival Gala to greet the whole nation. The walls are almost full of various exhibits, but I did not find any with penguin images. Just this time, I specially represented Tencent to give a crystal QQ penguin to the Great Wall Station, which should be a very unique souvenir!

All the staff of the station dine in the large dining hall outside the VIP room. This large hall is about twenty square meters, with two rows of tables and chairs that can accommodate thirty people dining at the same time. This place is actually also everyone's activity room, with TVs and some entertainment items inside. Since all food materials must be brought in from outside Antarctica, fresh vegetables and fruits are very scarce here. The main vegetables are Chinese cabbage and carrots, and there is no fruit supply. You can imagine how hard life is here. Usually, everyone lives like in a communist collective, and the director also queues up with everyone else to get food. But everyone's spirit is very good, helping each other and being intimate without gaps. After dinner, I specially went to the large hall to see everyone. The first thing I noticed was a laptop on the distant dining table. Coming closer, I saw a scientific researcher using QQ! I was very excited and asked him what he mainly uses QQ for? He said he communicates a lot with colleagues and friends in the country, and transferring files from Antarctica to the country is very fast, but from the country to Antarctica is much slower. He also specially added, "All our scientific researchers here use QQ!" Indeed, on the other side, I found the chief mechanic of the station also using QQ Mail on his laptop.

The Great Wall Station in Antarctica just celebrated its 25th anniversary last week on February 20, but internet access was only opened a month ago! Before the internet was available, it was almost impossible for the scientific researchers here to contact the outside world. The only way was through satellite phones or mobile phone text messages, which were not only expensive but also monotonous. Now that the internet has been opened, it has brought about earth-shaking changes in daily communication and contact between the staff here and the outside world! This time, Tencent specially prepared 100 consecutive QQ numbers to give to the Great Wall Station, hoping to bring some warmth and happiness to these respectable and lovely scientific researchers and staff!
