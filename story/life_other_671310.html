Masako Fujino

Note: This story is based on the content of "My Appointment with a Zombie - Masako Chapter", with significant improvements in sentence application and embellishment. The story is purely fictional. It tells a touching love story, mainly conceived from the perspective of humans and ghosts, focusing on love rather than horror. Please enjoy.

Chapter One: Preface

Who in the dream forcibly changed my originally wonderful dream, invading my privacy like pulling silk? Yet I had no conscious response or resistance. If all this were a dream, I wish you would be by my side when I wake up. In the dream, there was a faint image of a woman in a kimono. Her face was unclear, as if seen through heavy fog with low visibility, even within just a few meters. In another part of the dream, there was a man strolling around but seemingly avoiding the Japanese woman in the kimono intentionally or unintentionally. That man was me, but I didn't know the woman in the kimono, nor had we likely ever met.

Chapter Two: Rumors

Once upon a time, there was a legend that if you logged into the famous "Masako's webpage" for seven consecutive days and kept playing "Rock, Paper, Scissors," you would die precisely at twelve o'clock on the seventh day. The scariest part of this legend was about a woman in a kimono. This woman had a decent figure, but unless you played "Rock, Paper, Scissors," you couldn't see her entire appearance. The only way was to win. People who logged into Masako's webpage would gradually appear listless, their faces filled with gloom, exuding an unsettling aura. Anyone approaching the victim would involuntarily hit or curse them, perhaps unable to endure the evil energy surrounding the victim, though not out of malice. Just like when you feel uncomfortable because something unpleasant appears in your living space, hitting and cursing becomes the first unavoidable choice. Moreover, some people whispered that Masako lived in the computer's resource world, so those who logged into Masako's webpage generally had little chance of escape. Even if you got tired, Masako would control your nerves and consciousness, making you continue until death. Currently, this legend has become one of the most terrifying and widely spread legends in Japan. Despite this, some are willing to try, others doubt its authenticity, while still others, driven by curiosity, play for a few days or moments, only to get trapped. So don't be overly curious, lest you find it hard to break free. Don't joke with your life. Stay away as much as possible, and be careful not to log into Masako's webpage, or else someone might die on the seventh day—possibly you.

Chapter Three: Prelude

My name is Masako Fujino. From the beginning of the story, my gender was set opposite to male, meaning female, and my age was fixed at nineteen. I was born in 1970, a typical post-70s individual, yet my experiences never resembled those of the post-80s generation. When I was a child, my parents passed away early, leaving my grandparents to take care of me. However, they also left when I was about to turn nineteen, as if cursed. Fortunately, I graduated from a top university in Tokyo, which was enough to ensure my survival in this city full of material desires. At this moment, I found a job, though not prestigious, it was enough to support myself. Perhaps, destiny had already decided that on the day I found work, my family would perish. But this was fate, and I had to accept it.

Chapter Four: Encounter

In 1999, rumored to be the day of the world's end, real life did not unfold as predicted. However, in that year, I entered a small private company in Tokyo as an employee. The salary was not high, but it was sufficient to sustain my livelihood.

After working in the new company for a considerable period, I discovered the despicable nature of the leadership. The boss intended to humiliate me, though I managed to evade his advances multiple times, I remained on edge, unsure when something might happen. As long as an issue wasn't fully resolved, nothing could be said. Eventually, what I feared came true. Despite being cautious, I accidentally fell into a rather clumsy trap and was thus persecuted by the leader. Feeling disheartened, I believed I had no place in this world, finding it unbearably cold. This made me recall my family, causing my heart to ache. Suddenly, I felt that everyone walking on the street was disgraceful and despicable, all due to the misfortunes I had endured. On the morning of the second day, amidst a chilling breeze, numerous pedestrians walked numbly without anyone trying to stop my horrifying thoughts. This became my last glimpse of human ugliness before becoming a spirit.

Chapter Five: The Man

Women need men, and men need women too. A statement that seems correct but doesn't apply to me. Due to past humiliations by men, I avoided any interaction with them. Similar to the saying "once bitten, twice shy," except for me, the snake represents men who spout sweet nothings. Working alongside them felt suffocating due to their overwhelming foulness. Later, I deceitfully avoided every man until he appeared.

Chapter Six: Weaving the Web

Computers are incredibly fascinating, not because of their powerful functions, but because they have many network resources, which are the best habitats for ghosts. For me, it was the same. After my death, with the help of a person named Crow, I constructed my own network world and created a webpage called "Masako's webpage." Crow was a highly accomplished monk from four hundred years ago. Due to the failure of a plan four centuries earlier, he intended to restart four hundred years later. Or perhaps, Crow spent four hundred years searching for a person, a vengeful spirit who died tragically. Thus, I agreed to help Crow absorb the souls of three thousand men to assist him in fulfilling his four-hundred-year-old wish. Consequently, two individuals with differing needs reached an agreement—not quite right, it should be one zombie and one female ghost, neither of whom were human.

Thus, a web filled with revenge was woven. The main characters were often men, meaning men were the primary targets of attack, but women wouldn't be harmed. The grand plan to collect three thousand souls began.

From May 1999 to the end of July, I had already collected the souls of 2,921 men, leaving only seventy more. Would this grand plan succeed? Please read the next chapter.

Chapter Seven: ICQ

My name is Jin Zhengzhong, the forty-first generation inheritor of the Ma family's demon-slaying lineage, and my master is Ma Xiaoling, the fortieth generation inheritor of the Ma family. My master appears as tough as iron but has a kind heart inside. Therefore, I admire her greatly, admiring Ma Xiaoling. My job, along with my master's, is to slay demons. If someone can afford the price, we will act, eliminating monsters without questioning their good or evil, righteousness or wickedness.

On the eve of the National Day holiday, after my master accepted a big business deal—a demon-slaying task near the Yidong Group's Four Countries Building complex—she gave me some instructions and then went to the UK for five days. Perhaps today's task was too exhausting for me, so my master also wanted to relax, leading to subsequent events.

I heard that now it's popular to use an instant messaging software called ICQ. I planned to spend a few days chatting with friends worldwide using ICQ. On the night I bought the computer, after applying for an account and filling out complete information (though somewhat exaggerated), while waiting for netizens from around the world to add me, I fell asleep... When I woke up again, I saw a message on the computer screen from an unfamiliar ICQ number requesting to add me as a friend. Out of boredom, I agreed. When I opened her profile, I found she hadn't left any information except her name, which showed as "Fujino Masako." Seeing this name, I felt quite ordinary since I knew almost nothing about Masako's webpage and didn't realize it existed on ICQ.

She sent me a message: "Are you lonely? If you're lonely, check out my webpage." After smoothly entering, a simple game called "Rock, Paper, Scissors" appeared, accompanied by a covered picture, which I assumed was Masako's photo. Wanting to know the contents of the picture, I aimlessly played "Rock, Paper, Scissors," a game that should have been very simple. Surprisingly, I won on the first try and saw her eyes, which instantly struck me as mesmerizing and beautiful. I saw nothing else during the next few hours. Driven by excitement, I continued playing until the third day, finally seeing her full appearance—a Japanese woman in a kimono. I was startled, feeling somewhat suspicious, so I decided to quit. However, on the sixth night, when I tried to end everything, I unexpectedly found myself controlled by Masako. The game continued until midnight on the sixth day, leaving only one day until the seventh day.

Being controlled by Masako happened during a video call, a face-to-face conversation mediated by the computer. At this moment, my master happened to return from the UK, but my vacation still had one day left—the seventh day. In the morning, I swapped to a laptop, preparing for the surprise that night. I planned a special program for recognizing this week-long experience.

Time flew by quickly, and at 8:30 PM on the seventh day, Masako appeared on the screen as beautiful as ever, still wearing her kimono. Initially, I was in a mountain-top hotel, but due to excessive noise, I moved to the summit, the closest place to view the stars; previously, in the mountain-top hotel, I said a series of words: "The legendary butter pineapple bun (what's special about this butter pineapple bun is that I've never seen such a heartfelt one. If I told others that this butter pineapple bun was brought back by a murderous female ghost who worried I, Jin Zhengzhong, would be hungry, I think no one would believe it. They aren't doubting you, but they doubt me, doubting that I could receive pure and genuine love from a woman. Heaven has been too good to me. If someone asked me how heavy this butter pineapple bun is, I would say even ten hands couldn't lift it. If this butter pineapple bun had feelings, I believe the butter would drip everywhere because it's shedding tears of happiness for me.)"

With such words spoken, soon it was dusk on the seventh day. I told Masako, "Whenever I was unhappy, I used to come here to watch the stars. But today is the last time I'll come here to watch the stars because now I have you, so there's no need, and I won't be unhappy anymore." Some time passed, and it was midnight on the seventh day. Masako intended to kill me while I slept, but in the end, she didn't, because she truly fell in love with me, and love dissolved hatred.

Later, my master, Tian You, Uncle Qiu, and Fusheng wanted to deal with Masako, but were resisted and impacted by Zhengzhong, so they couldn't destroy her. Eventually, Zhengzhong was taken by Masako into the cyber resource world, the previously woven world. At this point, Masako wanted to be with Zhengzhong forever, while Zhengzhong wanted to escape. When Zhengzhong's master was about to destroy Masako, her words moved Zhengzhong. Thus, Zhengzhong bid farewell to his master and others, deciding to stay with Masako forever in the cyber world. The story ended here, without any extra descriptions.

Postscript: Love is always foolish, making people abandon all unsuitability to be together. From strangers to acquaintances, from ignorance to understanding, from strangers to intimates, from willingness to familiarity, all these transformations occur because of the emergence of love. In this story, Masako and Zhengzhong are the same. For love, they were willing to forsake everything they had worked hard to gain, worthy of praise. In love, there is no worthiness or unworthiness; it's only about whether there is love, mutual understanding, and supporting love. If I were in their situation, I would surely do the same. After all, love knows no age or other conditions.
