Plot Summary:

"A Heart of Iron and Blood":

The Taoist master Qiu Chuji from the Quanzhen Sect was being pursued by Jin soldiers when he was saved by two sworn brothers, Guo Xiaotian and Yang Tiexin. Unfortunately, this act of kindness led to the massacre of both families. The Prince of the Jin Empire was captivated by the beauty of Yang's wife, Bao Xiruo, and devised a plan to kill both families, deceiving Xiruo into marrying him. Xiaotian fought bravely but perished, while Tiexin disappeared. Guo's wife eventually fled to Mongolia and gave birth to her posthumous son, Guo Jing. Although Xiruo married into the royal family, she never forgot her old companion and soon gave birth to Yang Kang.

Master Qiu heard the tragic news and vowed to protect his friend's orphaned child. However, due to a misunderstanding, he ended up in a fierce battle with the Seven Freaks of Jiangnan. They made an agreement to find the descendants of Guo and Yang, teach them martial arts, and after eighteen years, they would hold a competition in Jiangnan to determine who was superior. Time flew by, and the date for the competition approached as the Seven Freaks and Guo Jing descended upon Jiangnan.

By then, under the guidance of the Seven Freaks, Guo Jing had mastered various martial arts skills. Due to his integrity and bravery, he was favored by Temujin, who bestowed upon him the title of Golden Knife Prince. Meanwhile, Yang Kang, born in the princely residence, grew up spoiled and was secretly taught martial arts by Master Qiu. Upon arriving in Central Plains, Guo Jing fortuitously met Huang Rong, and their fates became intertwined as they embarked on adventures together in the jianghu.

After the destruction of his home, Yang Tiexin went into hiding and adopted a foster daughter, Mu Nianci, with whom he wandered aimlessly searching for his wife. However, due to a marriage proposal through martial arts competition, a complicated relationship developed between Yang Kang and Nianci, leading to further entanglements between Guo, Huang, Yang, and Mu.

"The Evil and the Good":

Guo Jing and Huang Rong traveled together in Jiangnan and met Hong Qigong, the Northern Beggar. Fond of fine cuisine, Hong Qigong was delighted by Rong’s cooking and thus imparted the Eighteen Dragon Subduing Palms to Jing.

Hua Zheng and Tuo Lei, siblings from Mongolia, came to Central Plains and reunited with Jing. Hua Zheng reminded Jing of their betrothal. Though Jing did not want to break his promise, he also shared deep feelings with Rong, caught between the two women, feeling increasingly conflicted and unable to make a decision.

In anger, Rong returned to Peach Blossom Island. It was only then that Jing realized how deeply he loved Rong and despite great hardships, rushed to the island to reunite with her. However, he encountered numerous obstacles orchestrated by Rong's father, the Eastern Heretic Huang Yaoshi. By chance, Jing befriended Zhou Botong, who was trapped on the island, and learned the Two Hands Combating Technique and the Nine Yin Manual from him, advancing his martial arts to new heights.

Just as Jing reconciled with Rong and Hong Qigong proposed their marriage to Huang Yaoshi, the Western Venom Ouyang Feng arrived with his nephew to propose marriage as well, leading to conflict. Huang Yaoshi decided to settle the matter through martial arts competition, resulting in intense battles. Jing narrowly won, but due to some nonsensical remarks by Zhou Botong, Huang Yaoshi misunderstood and expelled Jing from Peach Blossom Island. After many twists and turns, the lovers were finally reunited and continued their adventures together in the jianghu.

Yang Kang and Wan Yan Lie arrived in Central Plains, plotting to steal the Wu Mu Relic Book to invade the Song Dynasty. Jing and Rong learned of their plan and swore to protect their homeland. While protecting the relic book, Jing was severely injured by Ouyang Feng and nearly lost his life. With the help of Huang Rong, he managed to escape danger. They retreated to a secret room to recuperate using the Nine Yin Manual, greatly improving Jing's martial arts skills. During this time, they repeatedly encountered formidable enemies but always managed to save each other, strengthening their bond.

"The Sword Discussion on Mount Hua":

Huang Rong accepted the position of the leader of the Beggars' Sect entrusted by Hong Qigong and set out with Guo Jing to Yuezhou to hold the meeting of the Beggars' Sect. Yang Kang had long coveted the position of the leader of the Beggars' Sect and took advantage of Rong's enthronement day, appearing with Hong Qigong's staff and claiming to be the true heir, inciting the sect members to attack Jing and Rong. Fortunately, Rong was quick-witted and exposed Kang's lack of knowledge of the staff techniques in front of everyone, revealing the truth, causing Kang to flee in disgrace.

Jing and Rong relentlessly chased after Kang and accidentally entered the Iron Palm Sect. Rong was severely injured by the sect leader. In despair, they were guided by Zhou Botong to seek medical help from the One Lamp Master. Despite the risk to his own life, the compassionate One Lamp Master spent three days and nights treating Rong. Unexpectedly, this almost cost him his life. After Rong recovered, they bid farewell to the One Lamp Master and returned to Peach Blossom Island, only to discover the corpses of the Five Freaks of Jiangnan on the island. Filled with grief and anger, Jing accused Huang Yaoshi of killing his mentors and set a date for a duel at the Misty Rain Tower before leaving Rong heartbroken. On the appointed day, Jing arrived with the sole survivor Ke Zhennan and the Seven Masters of Quanzhen, while Huang Yaoshi also brought Rong to the meeting. As they were fiercely battling, Kang suddenly led troops to attack.

In the chaos, Rong rescued Ke and hid in the Iron Staff Temple. Soon, Ouyang Feng arrived with Kang, and from their conversation, Rong and Ke discovered that they were the real culprits behind the murder of the Five Freaks. Meanwhile, the once-in-several-years grand event of the martial arts world, the "Sword Discussion Meeting," was held again on Mount Hua. The Eastern Heretic, Western Venom, Southern Emperor, Northern Beggar, Elder Zhou Botong, and other experts gathered to determine who was truly the supreme master.

Review:

This is the seventh TV drama starring Zhou Xingchi, and also the most famous extra role, "Song Soldier B." There's no need to elaborate on the main cast; Wong Mei Ling tragically passed away, but the lively Rong will live forever, Huang Rihua may age, but the honest Brother Jing remains young. Add to that the suave Miu Qiaowei and the skilled Yang Pann Pann, a group of today's top actors who were extras back then, forever unmatched. The 1983 version of "The Legend of the Condor Heroes" is an eternal classic that has influenced an entire generation and will continue to do so.

Zhou Xingchi appeared twice in the first episode of the first part "A Heart of Iron and Blood." Once, he was chasing Qiu Chuji on the street, encountering Guo Xiaotian and Yang Tiexin, exchanging a few lines, and extorting a few taels of silver. The second time, he was raiding the homes of the Guo and Yang families, looting and stealing until he was killed by Guo and Yang. The actor portraying Yang Tiexin was Adman Tsui, who later made a cameo appearance in "Shaolin Soccer." Perhaps it was during this collaboration that the elder Tsui developed a fondness for Zhou Xingchi, though it's unclear if this sparked any special connection.

In the seventh episode, Zhou Xingchi made another appearance, playing a prisoner sent by Yang Kang to Mei Chaofeng for training, meeting a tragic end under the Nine Yin White Bone Claw technique.

Zhou Xingchi mentioned at Shantou University that he once suggested to the director whether he could raise his hand to block before dying, but the director completely ignored him. This director, who didn't listen, was Du Qi-feng, who later collaborated with Zhou Xingchi on "The Magistrate" and "The Buddhist Monk Ji Gong." It's unknown whether Director Du regrets not accepting the suggestion from this small extra back then.
