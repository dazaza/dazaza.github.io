On June 26, after a devastating downpour hit Malong County, the Provincial Party Committee and the provincial government gave high priority to flood control and disaster relief work. Provincial Party Secretary Bai Enpei, and Deputy Secretary of the Provincial Party Committee and Governor Qin Guangrong made important instructions requiring strengthened leadership, proactive command, ensuring that disaster victims were properly accommodated, had food to eat, shelter to live in, and injured people received timely treatment; effective measures must be taken from multiple fronts, river channels must be cleared, flood discharge efforts accelerated, normal order of production and life in disaster areas restored as soon as possible, and prevention of new disasters ensured. Yesterday morning, Qin Guangrong went to inspect the disaster situation in Malong and directed flood control and rescue operations, visiting and consoling disaster victims and participating rescue armed police officers. Deputy Secretary of the Provincial Party Committee Li Jiheng, Vice Governors Kong Chuzhu, Cao Jianfang, and other leaders shox si accompanied him during the inspection and condolences. The government will consider initiating assistance measures. After overnight drainage, the accumulated water in Malong city almost completely receded yesterday, and residents gradually returned home to clean up their houses ravaged by floods, actively preparing for the restoration of production and life order.

Governor Qin and his team first visited the hospital to see 165 injured individuals due to the disaster. Most were injured while escaping, either cut or bruised, with injuries not being serious. Many lightly injured individuals received outpatient treatment and have already returned home.

Nine patients are hospitalized, five of whom underwent surgery due to more serious injuries. One fatality in the flood was a 67-year-old elder who passed away due to a heart attack triggered by the flood.

On the third floor of the inpatient department at Malong County People’s Hospital, Governor Qin and his team successively visited five injured individuals, asking about their injuries, family conditions, and comforting them to get well-treated.

Upon seeing Governor Qin coming to console him, Feng Angyou, who runs a pharmacy on Huancheng West Road, couldn't help but express his concerns: "My pharmacy, which I've struggled to start for two years, has lost nearly 300,000 yuan, it's hard to recover!"

"The government will consider initiating assistance measures. After recovery, go back and work hard to expand your business!" Governor Qin's comfort and encouragement gave Feng Angyou great hope.

Quickly let the disaster victims eat hot meals

In Xiaohai Village, Yuewang Township, more than ten kilometers from the Malong county town, 400 households were affected to varying degrees. In the village, collapsed adobe houses can be seen everywhere. Governor Qin climbed onto the rubble of Xu Guangxiang's house and asked about his losses, encouraging him to believe: with the government's help, post-disaster reconstruction would quickly take shape.

"Is that area over there a reservoir?" Standing on the rubble, Governor Qin pointed to a vast expanse of "water" surrounded by farmland and asked Xu Guangxiang.

"It's not a reservoir, it's all paddy fields. The crops on the mountain suffered little damage, but the rice and tobacco plants below were severely damaged!" After hearing this, Governor Qin frowned deeply and looked very solemn.

"There was a severe drought earlier, and after the drought, there was heavy flooding. This is a general rule. For this reason, during the drought, the Provincial Party Committee and the provincial government made arrangements for flood control, but this time the single-point heavy rain in Malong was too large and concentrated. But wherever the difficulties are greatest, there are always the figures of the soldiers!" In front of Xu Guangxiang's house, Governor Qin required the participating armed police officers of the Yunnan Corps to continue playing the style of not fearing hardship and capable of fighting, continuing to make helping disaster victims save property and self-help production the main task of current rescue efforts.

At the disaster victim resettlement point in Xiaohai Village, seeing tents already erected, quilts and other supplies provided, everything orderly, Governor Qin appeared very pleased. Seeing disaster victims, he asked when the tents were set up, whether there was enough food, and reminded the accompanying Qujing City and Malong County leaders: the resettlement of the masses must be done well and carefully. "We cannot be satisfied with just letting the masses eat dry food, we must also let them eat hot meals as soon as possible."

Seeing staff at the epidemic prevention room at the resettlement point busy giving medicine and taking blood pressure for disaster victims, Governor Qin requested that health and epidemic prevention work must be carried out well, ensuring no spread of epidemics after the disaster.

Ensure disaster victims move into new homes before the Spring Festival

At the field office meeting held after the inspection and condolences ended, Governor Qin highly praised the organization and advancement of Malong's flood control and rescue work. He required that continuing to help the masses save property and quickly restore self-help production capabilities should remain the primary task of current flood control and rescue efforts; communication and power departments should increase repair efforts, restoring normal production and life order in cities as soon as possible; based on accelerating disaster verification, for rural post-disaster reconstruction, it must be combined with socialist new rural construction, scientifically planned, ensuring disaster victims move into new homes before the Spring Festival. For urban resident families whose production and living conditions are extremely difficult due to the disaster, the government must study appropriate assistance measures. Here, he mentioned Feng Angyou, whom he met three hours ago at the hospital. "That pharmacist, 300,000 yuan worth of medicines gone, without proper assistance, resuming operation indeed isn't easy!"

Governor Qin also encouraged local cadres to have confidence in post-disaster reconstruction. "Although natural disasters cause enormous destruction, many examples of post-disaster reconstruction in cities show that successful reconstruction can elevate the development of a city to a higher level. Lijiang is a typical example." Governor Qin said that currently the provincial government is actively seeking central support for Malong's post-disaster reconstruction funds, and once the funds arrive, they will be promptly disbursed to the disaster area.

As of 7 PM last night, key units such as schools and hospitals in Malong city have resumed normal power supply, some units and residential areas' power restoration inspections are still ongoing, and the city has restored normal water supply. Many shops have resumed business, various living materials are adequately supplied, and no price hikes have been found. The weather in Malong county town on that day was sunny with partly cloudy skies, favorable for various tasks to proceed, but restoring normalcy in the county town still requires several days, and disinfection and epidemic prevention work is about to fully commence. Malong initiates daily reporting system for epidemic prevention

Chief reporter Zhu Xunhang At 11 AM yesterday, the floodwaters in Malong county town had not yet completely receded, and Ma Long County CDC Director Chen Jianlin's mobile phone kept ringing incessantly. According to requirements from the local government, the disease control department began deploying post-disaster health and epidemic prevention work yesterday.

"After a flood disaster, diseases like dysentery and typhoid fever are prone to occur, and could easily develop into major epidemics after the disaster." Chen Jianlin introduced that during the disaster process, floodwaters carried much filth to every corner of the affected areas. As the saying goes, 'Disease enters through the mouth.' Disaster-affected residents drinking unclean water sources or eating contaminated food easily lead to the proliferation of various intestinal diseases.

After cleaning up the mud and debris brought by the floodwaters, epidemic prevention personnel will carry out environmental disinfection in the affected areas, focusing on effectively disinfecting the water sources in the disaster area, and exterminating mosquitoes and flies. According to deployment, the county CDC mainly takes charge of urban epidemic prevention, while epidemic prevention personnel from eight township epidemic prevention stations and sixty-four village epidemic prevention points will implement epidemic prevention work near their respective disaster-stricken areas.

To ensure no major epidemic occurs after the disaster, the CDC initiated the three-level epidemic prevention emergency response plan at the county, township, and village levels, restored the 24-hour duty system and the daily one-report system for epidemics. "If two or three cases of the same disease are found anywhere, it must be immediately reported level by level to the epidemic prevention department. Even if no epidemic occurs, daily one-reporting must persist," Chen Jianlin said. Once the needed disinfection drugs and equipment are in place, the county CDC will supervise the implementation of disinfection and epidemic prevention work in each affected township and surrounding townships.

Early yesterday morning, responsible persons from Qujing City CDC also rushed to Malong, guiding the local CDC department to comprehensively carry out environmental disinfection, monitoring of water supply points, and infectious disease epidemic monitoring work. Meanwhile, 500 kilograms of bleaching powder and 20 boxes of disinfection spirit tablets were transported to the Malong County CDC. The Qujing City CDC department will also allocate 20 tons of bleaching powder max90 shoes, 80 boxes of mosquito-killing spirits, six electric sprayers, and sixty hand-operated backpack sprayers among other disinfection drugs and equipment to support the Malong disaster area.
