by mimi456 on 2011-12-31 00:48:01

Reversed Time

Once again, I flipped through my college diaries and notebooks, gradually bringing those distant memories closer. I am glad that I never lost them. Now, as I flip through them again, I touch the slightly yellowed pages, knowing well that they are filled with years of leisurely time.

Back then, I was a simple and introverted child, not good at expressing myself and somewhat stubborn and reckless. I liked conversing with myself using a pen rather than engaging with the various bustling clubs and associations in college. I got used to and enjoyed the happiness of being alone. There were naive feelings and clumsy expressions, and it was also during that time that I, this small self, began to taste some desolate loneliness.

In general, back then I was a purely adorable child who didn't like to express myself, sensitive and reserved, and even carried a bit of inferiority. As I first ventured into life, I resembled a bird about to take flight, examining this world with curious yet fearful eyes. I rejoiced in growth, harvesting simple and beautiful joys; similarly, I grieved over setbacks, constantly adjusting my direction. My young heart sometimes couldn't bear it all, so writing gave me a window for dialogue or release.

Even now, I still cherish these moments when I write things down on paper. Compared to typing on a keyboard, which feels cold and devoid of warmth, I prefer using a pen. Sometimes, I simply list items without grammar, sentence structure, punctuation, or sometimes just a few simple words. Such records are like seeds carelessly left by a negligent gardener along the long passage of time, sprouting and flourishing under the sunlight and caress of spring wind...

Thinking back to those days, it's already 5-6 years ago. College was like a vast lake, and we, bursting forth from its gates, ran tirelessly without realizing that we had scattered far and wide across the north, south, east, and west. Those once so familiar names, voices, faces—when one day you try hard to recall but can't remember a classmate who once vowed undying loyalty until the seas dried up and the rocks crumbled, your heart aches, and tears uncontrollably fill the corners of your eyes.

In these past few years, several classmates have forever left us. When we lament our fates' twists and turns, shouldn't we deeply reflect on ourselves? For so many years, we've been striving, wanting to stand out, trying hard to prove ourselves, yet forgetting something important along the way. Where has that self gone who was happy for days because of a teacher's praise, who was moved by a classmate's support and simple greetings, who felt profound emotions upon seeing a tender bud or a fallen leaf?

Nowadays, we still hold beautiful hopes for the future, just like when we first stepped into the campus of our university. However, when you're alone, lonely, missing someone, reminiscing, that university we all walked through together, those times we spent side by side, will give you warmth and support.

This spring, have the magnolias in the campus bloomed? Do you remember distinguishing between white magnolias and broad-leaved magnolias? And the Forsythia and winter jasmine, the mountain peach and peach blossoms. The most beautiful mornings in our memory are the clear chirps of birds in the mountains, the fresh and refreshing winds among the trees, and the warm and gentle sunshine. In this dusty spring, in the clusters of buildings in cities, do you still remember?

For those classmates now in the workplace, is there a hope and fear that others might understand your longing, just like when I turn the pages of my old books, where each thin page carries heavy memories of time? When we stop our busy feet and look back at the times we've walked through, picking up the scattered calendars on the ground, let's try to reclaim some of our former selves.

We turn off our phones, close our computers, close our eyes, gently press our foreheads, and bring our memories back to that distant university, that quiet and peaceful small city beneath the mountain. Those shaded paths lined with robust plane trees, those undulating streets, those ancient and dignified buildings, that fresh and tranquil air, those kind and familiar faces... Don't they bring you a long-lost joy and emotion from deep within?

Unintentionally, this page of memories almost turned into a complaint-filled monologue. Possessing such beautiful memories is a generous gift from heaven. I simply hope that those classmates not around, those friends who have drifted away, and those countless people scattered across the world, are all safe and happy!
