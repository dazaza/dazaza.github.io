Gang defrauded more than 10 million yuan through illegal publication of journals

by ege4f9hkkk on 2012-03-02 14:14:33

At present, this case is still under further trial, and the illicit gains are being fully recovered. The various illegal tools and counterfeit publications seized by the police were photographed by correspondent Zhenhan Zhang.

At 6 PM on February 16th, with the active assistance of the Provincial Cultural Market Inspection Corps and Qiongshan District Detachment, at the rented residence of Guo (from Changjiang) and his wife Fu (from Wenchang) in Ding'an Neighborhood, Mo Ke Village, Longkun South Road, Haikou police found another warehouse for illegal publications, seizing 7934 copies of illegal magazines in 16 categories related to education, medicine, and logistics, as well as receipts, invoices, and other evidence. They dismantled a criminal gang that had been engaging in fraud since 2004, using internet mailboxes and fake publication notices to solicit page fees.

The provincial "Anti-Pornography and Illegal Publications" office reminds citizens not to easily trust acceptance notices for academic papers sent by some medical or educational journals. If you receive such a notice, you can contact the Department of Newspaper and Periodical Publication Management of the General Administration of Press and Publication, telephone: 010-65212787 010-65212785. Informants may also report to local press and publication administrative departments or the "Anti-Pornography" office.

After police interrogation, the suspects involved, led by Guo and Fu, admitted to renting apartments in Jibao Community and Jin Niu New Village in Haikou as their base of operations. By querying personal information of teachers and medical workers across the country via the Internet, they used self-compiled and printed newspapers and journals such as the "Chinese Education Research Journal" and the "Chinese Medical Forum" to send out paper acceptance notices to teachers and medical workers across the country, defrauding them of page fees. Members of the fraud gang had clear divisions of labor. Some were responsible for mailing paper acceptance notices and withdrawing remittances; others collected information on medical personnel from the Internet. Both bases had dedicated staff for management.

Hainan Daily, Haikou, February 22nd (reporters Ya Jing Wu and Yan Zhen Wang) Under the coordination of the provincial "Anti-Pornography and Illegal Publications" office, after more than four months of meticulous investigation, the Haikou police successfully cracked a major case involving the counterfeiting of national educational journals, arresting nine suspects. It is conservatively estimated that the amount involved in the case exceeds ten million yuan, and over twenty thousand illegal publications were seized. It was revealed that the number of victims exceeded 20,000, mostly teachers and doctors who were deceived into paying money to publish academic papers for professional title evaluation.

According to relevant officials from the provincial "Anti-Pornography and Illegal Publications" office, based on previous investigation results, under the unified command of the Public Security Bureau leadership, multiple police units including the Criminal Investigation Brigade, Cyber Police Brigade, and Public Security Brigade worked together, deploying more than 40 police forces. At 9 AM on February 16th, they successfully cracked the key supervised case of counterfeiting "Chinese Educational Research Journal" and other educational journals at Jibao Community and Jin Niu New Village in Haikou. They arrested nine suspects, mainly Guo and Fu, seizing two small cars, one electric bike, 93 forged seals, 12 computers, 20 types of magazines including the "Chinese Educational Research Journal", four types of newspapers including the "Chinese Medical Forum" with over ten thousand copies, more than 60 bank cards, as well as remittance slips, invoices, and other evidence, with an especially large amount involved. Upon checking the computer records, there were over 20,000 victims.
