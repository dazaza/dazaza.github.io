In-depth Analysis of Car Noise

A systematic soundproofing project is completed through three steps: sealing, vibration reduction, and sound absorption. The door frame, car chassis, trunk, hood, and roof are all areas prone to air friction noise. Moreover, the majority of the noise enters the cabin through the car's suspension system, chassis, trunk, and doors. Therefore, the most basic soundproofing work should not overlook the sealing of the doors and trunk. For engine noise, if the engine itself does not have excellent smoothness and quiet performance, and you do not desire the acceleration roar that car enthusiasts crave, it is recommended to attach a layer of Soundproof Treasure - a professional automotive soundproofing and noise reduction material under the hood. This material can absorb and consume a large amount of engine noise, suppress the vibration of the hood, and block heat from the engine with an efficiency rate of 97%. It also protects the paint surface from damage caused by prolonged exposure to high temperatures. Car owners need to note that all soundproofing materials must meet relevant fire safety standards to ensure vehicle safety and reduce vehicle weight.

The wind cutting sound at high speeds and the creaking of the four wheels are the sounds we usually hear in the driving cabin. To effectively reduce these noises, it cannot be achieved through the mechanical parts themselves, but dedicated body sealing strips can be used to soften the edges of the body to reduce wind noise. We should also understand the principles and patterns of sound propagation in different media. Sound travels faster in water than in air and with less distortion because water is much less compressible than air. Sound cannot propagate in a vacuum. Since the medium is crucial for sound, to fully understand sound, we must grasp the characteristics of the medium. Therefore, choosing the right soundproofing and sound-absorbing materials is the most important issue in automotive soundproofing projects.

The transmission of sound depends on the pushing between air molecules. On the other hand, the frequency of sound has a direct relationship with the oscillation rate of the sound source. The structure of a car and the thickness of its metal panels are greatly related. Noise occurs because the frequency of the sound changes irregularly, making the vibrations received by the auditory system chaotic. As a result, the brain becomes tired and repelled, causing the listener to feel discomfort and unpleasantness.

Therefore, eliminating car noise first requires doing a good job of sealing the vehicle's cabin. This principle is simple: even if the walls between two rooms are thick, as long as there is a small hole, the sound from the adjacent room will be heard clearly. One can imagine how many openings exist in our car cabins' firewall, door seals, trunk, and floor!

Properly sealing the vehicle merely effectively prevents external noise from entering. However, during the journey, all components of the vehicle are constantly vibrating, and where there is vibration, there is noise. Thus, completing the sealing work is precisely the beginning of vibration reduction and soundproofing work. Tightly attaching a layer of high-damping, high-sound-absorbing, fireproof, and waterproof material - the Soundproof Treasure Vibration Reduction and Sound Absorption Board on all parts of the car body can truly enhance your vehicle's comfort performance.

Isn't a driving environment where outside sounds cannot penetrate and internal noise is not generated exactly what us car enthusiasts strive for as the ultimate goal?
