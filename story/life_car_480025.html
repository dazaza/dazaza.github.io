Several Methods for Car Beauty and Maintenance in Summer!

It is now the height of summer, and the maintenance and inspection of your beloved car are extremely necessary. Currently, a large portion of car owners know very little about car maintenance. While enjoying the various pleasures that a car brings, don't forget to do some essential work and take good care of your beloved car so that it doesn't "lose its temper" during the "dog days". At the same time, avoid traffic accidents caused by car malfunctions. Here are a few suggestions from the Chinease Auto Beauty and Maintenance experts:

Prevent Poor Lubrication

In summer, the high temperature causes lubricating oil to thin out easily, reducing its antioxidant properties, leading to degradation, which may even cause bearing seizure failures. Therefore, you should frequently check the quantity and quality of the lubricating oil and replace it as needed. Also, lubricants from different brands should not be mixed. It is recommended that car owners use high-quality lubricant additives, such as Chinapep Anti-Wear King, using them regularly can ensure the engine never requires major repairs.

Prevent Over-Rich Fuel Mixture

High summer temperatures make gasoline flow more easily. Additionally, heat expansion increases the fuel flow, and gasoline evaporates easily, leading to an overly rich fuel mixture. Therefore, the heat hole should be adjusted smaller, the position of the accelerator device connected to the throttle lever should be adjusted appropriately, and the fuel level should be lowered to reduce the fuel supply.

Prevent Vapor Lock

In summer, gasoline engine fuel systems are prone to vapor lock due to rising temperatures, causing insufficient or interrupted fuel supply. To prevent vapor lock, asbestos pads can be used to separate the gasoline pump from the exhaust pipe, or wet cloths can be used to wrap around the gasoline pump to cool it down.

Prevent Evaporation

Under high temperatures, the evaporation of oil and water increases. Ensure the gas cap is tightly closed to prevent oil leaks from the fuel line; regularly check the water level in the radiator, the oil level in the crankcase, the brake fluid level in the master cylinder, and the electrolyte level in the battery. If they do not meet specifications, they should be replenished or adjusted promptly.

Prevent Overheating

When the temperature is high, the cooling rate slows down, and the high temperature of the car's radiator often affects the normal power output of the engine, and may even cause the engine to malfunction. To prevent the engine from overheating, note that the fan belt should not be contaminated with oil to prevent slipping, and the belt should be kept at the right tension. During long-distance travel, take appropriate breaks, preferably in shaded areas, and open the engine hood to ventilate and cool down.

Prevent Tire Blowouts

When driving under high-temperature conditions, due to the high external temperature, tire cooling is slower, and the air pressure also correspondingly increases, making blowouts more likely. Therefore, when driving in high-temperature conditions, pay attention to the temperature and air pressure of the tires, check them frequently, and ensure the specified air pressure standard. If a lack of air is detected, replenish it immediately; absolutely do not drive on underinflated tires. If the car's tires are too hot, do not cool them down by splashing cold water, as this will cause cracks due to uneven contraction between the tread and the sidewall rubber layers. Do not immediately step on the brakes after a blowout.

Prevent Spontaneous Combustion and Explosion

With higher summer temperatures, the failure rate of cars greatly increases, and incidents of "spontaneous combustion" and "explosion" are often reported in the media. Besides some cases being due to design faults in certain models, improper operation or existing hidden dangers are also the main reasons for car spontaneous combustion. Therefore, regular checks of the car should be done, focusing on checking the oil and electrical lines to prevent spontaneous combustion.

A few small tips for summer car maintenance:

Appropriately reduce the fuel supply.

Timely add coolant.

Pay attention to whether the circuit is overheated.
