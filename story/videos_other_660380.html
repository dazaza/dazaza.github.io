Female driver mistook accelerator for brake, causing three consecutive collisions; knowing she was at fault, she locked her windows and refused to exit the car.

by gh5kj0 on 2012-02-07 16:40:47

Mistaking the gas pedal for the brake caused a three-car collision (Photo)

In an accident, all three vehicles sustained varying degrees of damage. At around 1:30 PM yesterday, at the intersection of Dong Erhuan Road and Fengyang East Road, a young woman mistook the gas pedal for the brake while stopping her car at a red light, resulting in a three-car chain collision. Fortunately, there were no casualties.

At the scene, reporters observed that among the three cars, some had their hoods damaged, others had their rear ends deeply dented, and still others were deformed from the impact, leaving the area in disarray. The drivers of the two cars that were rear-ended were quite helpless, complaining endlessly in the cold wind, while the female driver, aware of her fault, remained inside with her windows rolled up, ignoring the repeated questioning from the two drivers.

The drivers of the rear-ended cars quickly called the police, who subsequently arrived at the scene to conduct an investigation and handle the situation. It was learned that this female driver had not held her license for very long, and due to an operational error, she caused a three-car chain collision, taking full responsibility for the accident.

"Brake" mistaken for "gas," has recently become the root cause of numerous tragedies. Last year in Guangdong alone, this mistake led to multiple major traffic accidents. Was the "right foot" of the drivers placed correctly during driving? The results of the survey are alarming: more than three in ten people do not know how to correctly use their right foot to control the brake and gas pedals. Only three in ten have developed the habit of using their right foot correctly, while the remaining four in ten, although aware of the correct method, act quite casually. During the survey, it was found that with automatic transmission vehicles dominating new car sales, the number of people improperly using their left foot for braking continues to rise.

Experts point out that if the right foot is not always ready to apply the brakes during driving, the vehicle could become a bomb waiting to explode amidst the flow of traffic or pedestrians. For the sake of their own safety and that of others on the road, it is urgent for drivers to develop the habit of keeping their "right foot" ready to brake at all times.
