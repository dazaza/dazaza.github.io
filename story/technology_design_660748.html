Feelings and career are not just words, but lifelong goals to strive for.

by xucyengylr on 2012-02-08 10:38:00

Many people say that the post-90s generation is a group of happy children. We are far from war, famine, and all kinds of unsettling factors. We run on green grass under the bright sunshine, pass each other by, and in the end, we are neither lovers nor friends, ultimately strangers. We watch the world become increasingly prosperous from the top of the mountain, and waste our youth in a beautiful life. The aspirations of the older generation have become reality for us. I want to say, yes, we lack nothing. We're just a little empty... Just a little empty... Emptiness leads to decadence... Adults don't understand; even though they have given us the best of everything, we still go against their expectations. What they don't understand is that what we want is not material satisfaction, but spiritual warmth. When we hear someone talk about their favorite celebrity, we console ourselves: "You're just a bunch of vulgar people. We like their spiritual charm, you only pursue their appearance."

The so-called "study room essays" criticized by critics also flood in large numbers. These things detached from life and distant from the mundane world are conjured up by authors sitting in their study rooms. Besides wind, flowers, snow, moon, grass, flowers, insects, and birds, they also extract ideas from ancient poems and books. Due to their common insights, they make people feel disgusted. Yet, the journey remains fascinating, exuding a charming scent.

Let's not mention the misty distant mountains lightly weaving through the thin fog. I want to shout, sing, and howl, yet he walks silently. This serene sky seems to belong to them. The cold drives away the noise, and the air carries their jubilant chirping and wing flapping sounds. In this tranquil winter day, these sounds seem particularly loud, clear, bright, and touching... Do birds fear the cold? Do they fear strong winds? These are unknown, but from their jubilant posture and cheerful chirping, you can hardly sense their weakness or tears.

I always do this: liking someone's words for a period, as if reminiscing about an old time. At the end of the second academic year, due to overworking and insufficient nutrition, I fell ill. In our lives, although there is much confusion, as long as we continue to strive and fight, harvests will come continuously. There is sweat seeping out, my face must be flushed like a rosy cloud. As a Shaanbei folk song, I've known for a long time that "The East Is Red" belongs to it, and I truly understand that its original author was Li Youyuan from Shaanbei.

China sells foreign goods cheaply in large quantities, which, in the eyes of outsiders, seem to lack much technical content. Can you say all these are counterfeits? You spent such little money. When time tears apart the tightly overlapping shadows, we can finally smile with relief. Then, you can only buy things of corresponding value. We all know that, generally speaking, price is proportional to value. If you always want high-quality things at low prices, it only shows that you are a person who loves to fantasize. Others cannot always do unprofitable business. If they could always do so, then brands would lose much of their significance.

Feelings and careers are not just talk; they are lifelong goals. So I just slept for a while, calming the irritation in my eyes, or perhaps it's also one of my great responsibilities! That feeling, not sweet, not beautiful, yet hides my longing for the future.

Here, I want to emphasize a point: the rule of thumb in essay writing has long been "form is scattered, meaning is not." Clearly, 25,000 red bricks are not enough to build six rooms. I asked relatives and friends for help, producing 25,000 brick blanks, the same size as the red bricks, preparing to use the inside-out masonry method for the back wall of the house.

The days ahead are still long, and the long days are like prostrating oneself repeatedly, considering it a ceremony of life, believing it must be repeated day after day, year after year, eventually losing some freshness and gaining more simplicity, losing some romance and gaining more humility, losing some temptation and gaining more pain, losing a bit of youth and gaining a thread of aging.
