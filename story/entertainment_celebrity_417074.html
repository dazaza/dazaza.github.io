Immediately enter the Weibo hot discussion Luo Le Lin

(Weibo information network) Who is Luo Le Lin? I guess many audiences still can't recall his appearance when hearing his name. But because he died continuously for 5 times in the TV episodes aired on April 6~7, setting a record in Hong Kong television history. The media from six countries including Europe, America and Australia also widely reported it the other day, making Luo Le Lin famous again in Hong Kong. Reporters contacted with the wireless yesterday, hoping to interview Luo Le Lin, only to be told that there were too many media wanting to interview him, so we need to arrange the interviews in Hong Kong first. He laughed and said in the interview with Hong Kong media: It's really incredible! Six countries in Europe and America are competing to report.

In last Wednesday and Thursday, the characters played by Luo Le Lin coincidentally died one after another in five TV plays broadcasted by the wireless, namely "Bu Yi Shen Xiang", "Hong Wu Thirty-Two", "Female Fist" and replayed "Jie Da Hui Xi" (costume version, fashion version) and "Number Seven Police Station". Unexpectedly, after the Hong Kong media reported, it received more attention from foreign media. Yesterday, the websites of the British newspaper "Daily Telegraph", American AllVoices, Italian AGINewsOn, Hungarian BorsodOnline, Australian ABC.NET and the German version of Agence France-Presse all extensively reported the news of Luo Le Lin's death for 5 times, making Luo Le Lin instantly famous internationally. However, careful netizens found that Luo Le Lin also coincidentally died in the replay of the martial arts drama "Wu Jin Xue Jian" at 2 p.m. last Friday afternoon. So the exact number is that he died for 6 times within 42 hours. Luo Le Lin said he felt very magical, I am always a very low-key person, don't know how to go online and can't use computers! But the whole world says (dying 5 times in 24 hours) is impossible, even if the TV station deliberately arranges it, it can't be arranged. Still difficult to escape death in new dramas

Luo Le Lin, aged 63, said that he has no taboos when shooting, and does not mind shooting scenes of death. Whenever he shoots death scenes, the wireless will give him red envelopes according to tradition, praying for good luck. He has died over a hundred times since he entered the industry, sometimes receiving red envelopes of at least one dollar or fifty cents. Last night, he was shooting "Forensic Heroes III" at the General Au Television City of the wireless, indicating that this role might still be difficult to escape death. He has not yet collected all the scripts, but he heard that it seemed like he had to die. But the later broadcasted "Why Sir Is Sir" is a comedy, so he doesn't have to die. Luo Le Lin does not think dying in the drama is particularly special, but because the wireless dramas are shot and aired very intensively, and the supporting actors used repeatedly are those few people, so it is common to see the same actor in several different TV dramas on the same day. But this situation is very rare abroad, no wonder it has become an international curiosity.

The first generation of Yang Guo

Luo Le Lin, aged 63, joined Asia Television in 1970 and starred in the drama "The Return of the Condor Heroes" in 1976 with Li Tongming, becoming the first generation of Yang Guo. Luo Le Lin later joined ATV (formerly known as Rediffusion Television), and transferred to the wireless in 1990, shooting many popular TV dramas. His representative works include playing the mentally ill stock god Ye Tian in "A Time of Change". Luo Le Lin has two daughters with his ex-wife, remarried to actress Chen Baoyi (the cousin of movie star Chen Baozhu) in 1982, and they have a daughter.

Year after year of supporting roles, finally made it after 20 years

Lai Yaoxiang asked in "Gown Hero": How many decades are there in life? This expressed his early feelings as a supporting actor. Although the wireless is the largest TV station in Hong Kong, the supporting actors come and go, not more than a dozen people, so they appear in nearly ten TV dramas a year. In recent years, a supporting actor becomes famous every year, and the shortest time is probably Mai Changqing who became famous last year, who also waited for 20 years to become famous overnight.

Mai Changqing in 2010

After 23 years in the industry, Mai Changqing graduated from the wireless training class and was once the host of the children's program "Lightning Fax Machine". He has been playing second and third-line roles. Before starring in "Righteous Sea Affection", the deepest role he left on the audience was playing the Sand Monk in the wireless version of "Journey to the West". Because of playing the villain Liang Fanbu in "Righteous Sea Affection", he was called Brother Fanbu on the street, allowing him to taste the feeling of being famous, and won the best supporting actor award at the 2010 Wireless Awards Ceremony.

Chen Honglie in 2009

Born in Shanghai, Chen Honglie was a famous villain in Mandarin films in the 1960s and 1970s. He entered the wireless three times. In 2008 and 2009, he respectively played Yan Yingming in "Colleagues Three Intimacies" and Da Yansheng in "Bida Zijiren", making his popularity soar; but on November 24, 2009 afternoon, Chen Honglie suddenly died while shooting "Bida Zijiren", at the age of 66. Because he passed away during filming, it was disclosed that the wireless working hours were too long and the salary was too low, attracting widespread attention. The wireless even opened a special section at the awards ceremony to mourn him.

Yuen Ngai Hung in 2008

His real identity is a taxi driver, and the guest role he played is also a taxi driver. Discovered by staff in 1988, Yuen Ngai Hung has appeared in more than 20 wireless dramas, each time not exceeding 30 seconds of screen time. For 20 years, he played a taxi driver in multiple TV dramas such as "No Room for Crime", "Virtuous Doctor", and "Love Without Boundaries", even the posture of appearing on screen was the same. Due to finishing shooting "Pearl Glow" in 2008, he became popular online and was crowned as the wireless exclusive taxi boss.

Lin Jia Hua in 2007

Joined the first Junior Police Training School in 1974 and won the Silver Rooster Award. He also hosted the new program "Junior Police Bulletin" at the time. He was highly appreciated by the ATV producer McKeown, thus entering the TV circle to develop, shooting many TV dramas and serving as a host. Later, he immigrated to Toronto with his family in 1988, and returned to Hong Kong in 2006 to continue his acting career. He filmed the movie "Dog Bite Dog" and guest-starred in "Police Recruits" in 2007, receiving good reviews, and last year he also competed for the best supporting actor award at the Wireless Awards Ceremony.
