Love is supply, hate is consumption.

by wangquan1986 on 2012-03-03 16:39:54

Love is a supply, hate is a consumption. I once hated a person with all my heart, that kind of deep-seated disgust and hatred made me suspicious of all the beauty in life. I could not imagine there being such an intolerable, venomous and vulgar person in this world, nor could I imagine that I would encounter such a unreasonable and unyielding person. At that time, I exhausted all my strength to deal with this demon-like woman. For many days, I abandoned my cultivation, elegance, intellect, calmness, and composure, turning all the real disgust and resentment in my heart into equally sharp words, like a sword piercing towards her, wanting to give her a heavy blow.

She was actually a person unrelated to me, we had never met, just two strangers in the vast sea of the internet. It wasn't really a deep grudge between us, it was just because of her overly harsh and acidic words, and her cursing about the health and future of my child, which hurt me so much that it provoked me to retaliate. It was a period of chaos and darkness, we spent all our time and energy on endless confrontations, not knowing how to forgive, understand, empathize, or pardon. That period was so dark that I didn't want to look back, so dim that I wanted to erase it from my life. I really didn't want such a disgraceful history in my otherwise peaceful memories.

But after the passage of time, memories have been erased by the river of time. When we both calmed down, I calmly reflected and found that those verbal battles were so immature and funny, and also so pitiful and inappropriate. Between people, it's already a great fortune to have an intersection point. In this country with 1.3 billion people, each person is like a grain of sand in the vast ocean. There are many missed opportunities and encounters, if we can luckily meet and get to know each other, it is such a rare edge. However, many people discard such rare edges in meaningless arguments and comparisons, which cannot be said to be a regret and a defect of human nature.

When two acquaintances meet, certain events will make us like or dislike a person, and let others like or dislike us. We are not RMB, we cannot make everyone truly like and appreciate us. But if two people with grievances, even if they cannot turn swords into plowshares, but understand the principle of "a step back makes the horizon wider, a moment of patience makes the storm subside", I think there won't be too many stains left behind, and when looking back, even oneself may dislike the irrational, unrestrained self at that time?

Nowadays, every time I recall the foul words of that woman back then, I still feel as if I swallowed a fly, so nauseating and uncomfortable. But when I recall the words I returned back then, although none of my words contained dirty words, I quite despise myself. How could I waste so much talk on a person not worth arguing with, low-quality person, dirtying my mouth? Every time I think about it, I regret it endlessly. Arguing with such a person doesn't exactly represent that I am not a graceful and tranquil person either? If I were calm and composed enough, I wouldn't lose my elegance for those unfounded insults, lowering my personal charm.

Hate is indeed a double-edged sword, while hurting others, the rebound force also shocks one's own means. Our hearts are like gardens, if planted with colorful, various kinds of flowers inside, then there will be less desolation and more beautiful scenery. In springtime, butterflies flutter gracefully, bees buzz around, flowers bloom together, purple and red everywhere, what a delightful sight. The phrase "give roses to others, and the fragrance remains on your hands" well interprets the phrase "love is a supply". It can purify our hearts, making us fragrant ourselves while giving to others; and "do not do to others what you do not want done to yourself" does it not also show "hate is a consumption"?

I remember not long ago in a chat, during the time when I was entangled with that woman I disliked, I often vented my frustrations in the mood section of QQ space. Sister Su Xin Ru Lan saw it and gave me a piece of advice, like a beacon, guiding the direction of my heart, allowing me to escape from the unbearable hesitation and suppression. She told me that she once read such a passage in a book, which can be used to remind oneself, please me to always remember and follow: if you admire me, please sit quietly and wait, I will make you more admiring; if you envy me, please stay away, because I will make you more envious; if you think I am out of the ordinary, I am; if you think I am affected, I am; if you think I am arrogant, I don't argue; if you think I am vulgar, I smile lightly. Your jealousy, anger, and insult, if I don't accept them, they still belong to you.

That passage woke me up like a dream. I suddenly realized the shame and embarrassment of my previous actions, wishing I could find a hole to hide in. Always claiming to be a "heart as pure as orchid, person as light as chrysanthemum" woman, but did not realize how ugly I was during that period, mistakenly thinking I had retaliated and felt secretly relieved. Originally, I didn't know I had uglified myself. After that, I treated all her attacks and insults as a breeze passing by my ears.

Yes, go ahead and love, love more. Love will make us braver, own more, harvest more, understand more. Love is a supply, hate is a consumption. Stay away from the battlefield of life full of smoke, in this complicated world, find a quiet place for your heart, settle down all the unwillingness, hatred, and anger, return yourself a clear and wonderful life. Life only has a few decades, if we encounter each other within our lifetime, no matter what happens, put down the attachment, stubbornness, and hatred in our hearts, learn to tolerate, forgive. Forgiving others is also forgiving yourself.

Loving will make one's heart become soft, happy, warm, noble, dignified, confident, and beautiful; hating will make one's heart become dirty, tired, filthy, and grotesque. Initially, human nature is good. Ordinary people all have originally kind and pure hearts, liking to appreciate beautiful things. But in this big dye vat of society, we are influenced by all kinds of people, we may inadvertently be dyed in various colors. But I sincerely believe that in everyone's heart, there is a corner hidden, that corner is related to kindness, beauty, and tranquility. That is the most genuine self.
