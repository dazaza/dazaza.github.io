Facebook Launches Premium Advertising Service: Reaching 98% of Brand Fans

by bingo123 on 2012-03-08 17:12:41

Facebook Places High Hopes on Reach Generator

Sina Tech News, March 5th - At last week's marketing conference, Facebook launched its new advertising product, the Reach Generator. Industry insiders believe this to be a significant innovation in Facebook’s advertising market strategy, offering global advertisers a new option beyond cost-per-click (CPC) and cost-per-thousand-impressions (CPM). It also allows Facebook to more effectively utilize its ad space.

The Reach Generator enables advertisers to pay a fixed price based on the number of fans on their Facebook pages. In return, Facebook will display content from these pages to 75% of the fans monthly or 50% weekly. This content will appear as ads in the fans' sidebar, news feed, and logout page. Previously, a single piece of content could only reach 16% of the fans, making this new tool undoubtedly enhance the coverage of page content.

Compared to previous ad formats, the Reach Generator is priced higher and may only suit the wealthiest global brands. Facebook claims that this new ad product can bring three times the return on investment with a fan reach of up to 98%. This will help brand advertisers leverage existing investments to develop their fan base but offers little assistance in attracting new fans.

Since Facebook only provides the Reach Generator through its paid ad account team, it might lead to a decrease in revenue from Facebook's ad application programming interface (API), but it could also help Facebook expand its advertising business. As long as there is ad space available, Facebook can display ads from the Reach Generator, helping Facebook fully utilize ad spaces that were previously hard to sell.

Through the Reach Generator, the tasks of ad targeting and effectiveness measurement shift from brand advertisers to Facebook, allowing advertisers to avoid showing too many ads to the same fan while not neglecting other fans. Industry insiders believe that in advertisers' nationwide or global large-scale marketing campaigns, the Reach Generator could become an important component.

Facebook also announced the launch of the Timeline feature on brand pages, which will help brands better express themselves. The function allowing fans to send direct messages to brand pages opens new customer service channels. With the enhancement of brand page functions and potential, the value of third-party brand page management solutions becomes even more apparent.

According to Facebook, content from the pages can become ads appearing on both the web and mobile versions of the news feed, the web sidebar, and Facebook's logout page. Facebook aims to make ads valuable content, providing users with interactive experiences and mixing them with regular content on the site without causing disruptions. The added ad placements can increase Facebook's ad revenue and create a new advertising marketing strategy. However, some users may not want to see ads everywhere on the Facebook site.

To complement the new ad product, Facebook also introduced real-time tracking of ad performance. Within 5 to 10 minutes after posting page content, advertisers can view data such as impressions, reach, user likes, clicks, video views, and negative feedback. Previously, Facebook provided such data two days later. This will allow advertisers to clearly understand what content interests users, enabling them to promote user-interested content more or further purchase Facebook's "Sponsored Stories" ads. Real-time monitoring will improve the quality of branded content on Facebook and attract advertisers to allocate more of their ad budgets to Facebook. (Viking)
