Bullets don't have eyes, and the U.S. military has killed civilians again.

by nicezone on 2008-03-16 23:57:05

On the 12th, the US military forces stationed in Afghanistan launched a cross-border air strike against Pakistan, killing two women and two children in the country. On the 13th, Pakistan strongly protested to the coalition forces stationed in Afghanistan. That evening, as many as 2,000 people demonstrated at the scene of the incident.

"The coalition forces stationed in Afghanistan, led by the US military which assisted in overthrowing the Taliban regime at the end of 2001, confirmed on the 13th that they had carried out a 'precision-guided' air strike on Pakistani territory. However, they claimed that the target of this attack was a gathering place for armed militants located in the town of Luwara Mandi in North Waziristan, a border area, which is one of the breeding grounds for 'Al-Qaeda' and Taliban armed militants. The Pakistan-Afghanistan border is 2,500 kilometers long, and cross-border incidents frequently occur. US missiles have crossed the border before, killing senior Al-Qaeda commander Abdul Rashid Baloch who was stationed in North Waziristan. Pakistan has also deployed about 90,000 soldiers in the area to capture armed militants.

Although Pakistani President Pervez Musharraf is a close ally of the United States in the war on terror, cross-border military intervention by foreign forces remains a sensitive issue. Earlier this year, Musharraf stated that if a foreign country were to carry out military actions without Pakistan's permission, it would be considered an act of invasion......"
