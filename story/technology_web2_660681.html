BHP Billiton Locks on 4 to 5 Acquisition Targets, a New Round of M&A Wave to Rise in Global Mining Industry

by fdjrkkgfed3 on 2012-02-08 09:31:52

BHP Billiton Locks on 4 to 5 Acquisition Targets, a New Round of M&A Wave Expected in Global Mining Industry – Corporate Dynamics - Industry News - Building Materials Network

According to Reuters, the Chief Commercial Officer of BHP Billiton, the world's largest mining company, Alberto Calderon, stated that the company currently holds approximately $18 billion in cash and plans to use part of this amount, along with some loans, to acquire large competitors. Calderon’s statements likely indicate that a new wave of mergers and acquisitions will sweep through the global mining industry.

Calderon mentioned that BHP Billiton has already identified 4 to 5 potential acquisition targets, all of which are major companies in the mining, oil, and natural gas industries. It is reported that BHP Billiton will take action within the next 12 months. Calderon said: "We don’t feel pressured to rush into an acquisition. However, I can say that we have done a lot of preliminary work for this."

Calderon also noted that BHP Billiton has reserved $10 billion for the current fiscal year, which will be used for the development of new projects and the expansion of existing ones. Additionally, BHP Billiton has accumulated an unspecified amount of funds through its operations over recent years, specifically designated for acquisition activities.

Calderon emphasized that BHP Billiton currently has ample financial resources, and due to its low debt level, it can further expand its capital scale through borrowing. Calderon said: "After this economic crisis ends, our financial strength will play a role." Calderon refused to disclose specific acquisition targets but pointed out that BHP Billiton plans to expand in certain areas, including iron ore, copper, coal, oil, and fertilizer raw materials. Calderon stated: "These are all industries we are focusing on. Whether through internal growth or mergers and acquisitions, we will seek further development in these industries."

Analysts believe that Freeport-McMoRan Copper & Gold, headquartered in the U.S. and the world's largest publicly traded copper company (referred to as Freeport), could be one of BHP Billiton’s targets. The company's copper assets have low costs, and its gold business is also significant. Currently, Freeport’s total market value is $29 billion. Analysts believe that if BHP Billiton decides to acquire Freeport, its offer will certainly be lower than its previous bid for Rio Tinto, but the amount will still be substantial. Currently, Freeport officials have refused to comment on this matter.

Analysts also think that PotashCorp. in Canada could be another acquisition target for BHP Billiton. This company is a supplier of fertilizer raw materials and has been seeking opportunities to open up new markets. The company's spokesperson indicated that they have heard rumors about BHP Billiton's intention to acquire them but declined to comment on the matter. Other potential targets speculated by analysts include Anglo American PLC and Xstrata PLC, though neither company has responded to these speculations.

Analysts believe that Vale, Brazil's iron ore giant, will not become a target for BHP Billiton because it is partly owned by the Brazilian government. BHP Billiton has previously expressed interest in acquiring businesses around its existing operations or in easily penetrable markets. Analysts thus believe that parts of Russia and Africa have become target markets for BHP Billiton. In comparison, companies in Australia, the U.S., Canada, Chile, Indonesia, and Colombia are more likely to become acquisition targets for BHP Billiton.
