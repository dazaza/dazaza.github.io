How to register for the registration of public ownership and collective ownership enterprises

by violet0807 on 2008-08-28 16:33:50

Application Methods

Applicants can submit applications not only directly at the business registration office but also through mail, fax, electronic data interchange, and email in non-fixed formats.

Applicants submitting administrative license applications in non-fixed formats should use the application form provided by the Beijing Administration for Industry and Commerce.

Applicants submitting administrative license applications in non-fixed formats should simultaneously provide detailed contact information including telephone numbers, mailing addresses, email addresses, and authorization documents of the applicant or a legally authorized agent.

Applicants submitting administrative license applications via letter to the administrative licensing department of the administration for industry and commerce should include original application documents and certificates. Signatures and seals on application documents must be genuine and valid.

Concepts of State-Owned Enterprises and Collectively-Owned Enterprises:

State-Owned Enterprise: A company where means of production are owned collectively by all the people, which operates independently according to law, bears its own profits and losses, maintains independent accounting, and aims for profit.

Collectively-Owned Enterprise: An enterprise where property is owned collectively by the public, laborers work together, distribution according to work predominates with appropriate dividends as supplements, and a certain amount of public accumulation is extracted.

Conditions that State-Owned Enterprises and Collectively-Owned Enterprises Should Meet:

(1) The investment subject of a state-owned enterprise must be a public entity - a legal person of a state-owned enterprise, social organization legal person, or institutional legal person.

The legitimate qualification certificate of the superior sponsoring unit (investor): If the superior sponsoring unit (investor) is an enterprise, it should issue a copy of the "Business License" stamped with the official seal of this enterprise; if it's an institution, it should issue a copy of the "Institutional Legal Person Certificate" stamped with the official seal of this unit; if it's a social organization, it should issue a copy of the "Social Organization Legal Person Registration Certificate" stamped with the official seal of this unit. If the union is the superior sponsoring unit, it should submit approval documents from the district or county-level or above union agreeing to the establishment.

Please note that the following units do not have investment qualifications or their investment capabilities are restricted:

1. Enterprises rated B in annual inspection cannot add branches or invest in establishing state-owned or collectively-owned enterprises.

2. Market entities locked into the "Warning Information System" of the Beijing Credit Information System will have their investment qualifications restricted during the lock-in period.

3. Party and government agencies, judicial administrative departments, and social organizations sponsored by party and government agencies in this city cannot invest in establishing state-owned (collective) enterprises.

Party and government agencies' affiliated institutions with administrative management and law enforcement supervision functions, as well as logistics and security economic entities operated by various departments of the party and government agencies in this city, cannot invest in establishing state-owned (collective) enterprises.

4. Accounting firms, auditing firms, asset appraisal institutions, and law firms cannot act as investment subjects to invest in other industries to establish state-owned or collectively-owned enterprises.

5. Foundations cannot invest in establishing state-owned or collectively-owned enterprises.

6. Limited liability companies, joint-stock companies, and share-cooperation system enterprises cannot invest in state-owned or collectively-owned enterprises.

7. Unions can invest in setting up state-owned or collectively-owned enterprises after being approved by unions at or above the district or county level.

8. Domestic enterprises (excluding investment category enterprises) adopting the method of paying registered capital (funds) in installments should fully pay the registered capital (funds) before they can invest in setting up state-owned or collectively-owned enterprises.

9. Enterprises registered with non-monetary contributions that have not completed the property transfer procedures cannot make external investments or set up branches.

Special Attention: Qualification issues for legal representatives and branch managers.

Persons locked into the "Warning Information System" of the Beijing Credit Information System cannot serve as legal representatives or branch managers during the lock-in period.
