When to eat snacks without gaining weight

What time to eat snacks without gaining weight

Under normal circumstances, people can meet their physiological needs for nutrients through three meals a day, so there is no need to eat snacks. However, in certain special situations, eating some snacks can be beneficial to health. Below are a few examples to illustrate this:

1. The elderly: Eating an appropriate amount of snacks can be beneficial to health. Why? Because the digestive system function of the elderly decreases, such as reduced gastric juice secretion and decreased secretion of various digestive enzymes in the digestive tract, leading to a certain degree of reduction in digestive and absorption functions. In this case, if they still have only three meals a day, it cannot meet the elderly's nutritional needs. Due to reduced digestive function, eating too much per meal can be hard to digest and absorb, putting more burden on the gastrointestinal tract, often causing symptoms of indigestion. If each meal is eaten until seven or eight-tenths full, and snacks that are easy to digest and rich in nutrition are consumed when hungry between meals, both the normal nutritional needs of the elderly can be ensured, and the gastrointestinal tract won't be overly burdened.

2. School-age children: Children in this age group are in the period of acquiring knowledge and growing physically. However, for various reasons, breakfast is often rushed, or due to its simplicity and poor nutrition, or monotonous taste affecting children's appetite, resulting in eating less at breakfast and rushing off to school, feeling hungry before lunch, directly affecting learning outcomes in the third and fourth classes. In this situation, if a snack is eaten around 10 a.m., this contradiction can be satisfactorily resolved. Developed Western countries have already taken measures to address the issue of snacks during class breaks for elementary school students. Chinese nutrition experts have also repeatedly called for the adoption of snacks during breaks in schools with conditions to do so, and small-scale pilot programs have been conducted, yielding very satisfactory results. Not only has student learning improved, but physical fitness has also increased.

3. Pregnant women: Due to special circumstances, pregnant women require more nutrients than the general population of the same age. However, because the fetus compresses the digestive system in late pregnancy, causing a strong sense of fullness after eating, which affects food intake. And the nutritional needs during this period are quite large; insufficient nutrition can directly harm the fetus and the mother. At this time, the method of eating snacks can be adopted, commonly known as the "small frequent meals" approach to solve the problem.

4. Certain patients, primarily diabetic patients: Eating too much at one time can cause rapid and sustained elevation of blood sugar, which is detrimental to the condition. Dividing the food from three meals into six or seven smaller meals can resolve the issue of sustained rapid increase in blood sugar that diabetics should avoid. Of course, snacks eaten between meals are also very particular; it's not about eating whatever you want. Another example is gastrointestinal diseases like gastritis, where patients' digestive abilities are weaker. Eating a large amount of food at once can increase the burden on the gastrointestinal tract, not only making digestion and absorption less ideal, but sometimes even worsening the condition. Therefore, main meals should not be eaten to fullness, and snacks should be consumed between meals.

The above lists several cases where eating snacks is beneficial to health. However, what kind of snacks to eat is also a matter of some knowledge.

Firstly, one should not always eat processed foods with low nutritional value that only provide energy, such as cookies. Instead, one should vary the choices and eat nutrient-rich foods like boiled eggs, beef jerky, milk, etc. Of course, these foods require a certain amount of time and place for preparation, and can only be adopted under permitted conditions. We should have a concept: first, we cannot generally oppose eating snacks; second, eating snacks should focus on nutrition, not just satisfying hunger; third, eating snacks must be in moderation, not affecting the next main meal and disrupting regular eating habits, thereby affecting the normal pattern of digestion and absorption.
