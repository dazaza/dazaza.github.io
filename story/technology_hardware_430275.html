The Super Pickle with Twelve Pickles and the Witch

Deep in the Beishan Mountains, there indeed lived an old witch. Due to her habit of never brushing her teeth, her breath was extremely foul. Her servants couldn't endure her bad breath; some fell ill from the stench while others simply fled. With no one left to serve her, the old witch had to live alone in a deep cave located in the heart of the mountain. The area around her cave was barren as all the plants and trees had been killed by her foul breath.

One day, a peddler selling pears passed by her cave. Just as the witch emerged, she saw the peddler and called out for some pears. The peddler, upon smelling her breath, immediately felt nauseous. When she asked about the price, the peddler fainted from the odor, and the pears turned black and rotten within moments.

Another time, the Land God wanted to discuss something with the witch but was overwhelmed by her foul breath. Eventually, he had water brought in to make her rinse her mouth for a long time before they could converse.

Pickle, one of the super pickles, wandered in the mountains for three days and waited two more before finally meeting the witch. Despite having heard about her foul breath, Picked still felt the urge to vomit when he met her. Thinking of Rose, he endured his discomfort and greeted the witch politely: "Grandma, I've heard of your immense powers. I have come specially to seek your advice." The witch replied that if he wished to consult her, he would first need to serve her for three months. Feeling lonely in her cave, the witch was delighted at this prospect. However, Picked nearly fainted at the thought of enduring three months of her breath. Yet, thinking of how he needed her help to become a superhero and win Rose's hand, he agreed without hesitation.

To survive the three months, Picked devised a plan and wore a mask. The witch noticed and accused him of being disrespectful. He quickly explained that it was not because of her breath but because he was too ugly and feared making her uncomfortable. This pleased the witch, who rewarded him with a moldy pancake. After eating it, he pretended to be happy but ran to the restroom to throw up.

After the grueling three months, the witch, appreciating Picked's dedication, asked what he desired. Picked requested wings that could fly in the sky and swim underwater. The witch informed him that he needed swan wings, which must be removed within three minutes of the swan's death. She could sew these wings onto his body, though it would be excruciatingly painful. For six months afterward, he would need to continuously flap his wings, each movement causing unbearable pain. If he failed to endure, both the wings and he would die. Faced with such risks, Picked hesitated after hearing the conditions.

Returning home, Picked visited Rose. Seeing her beauty and noticing rivals like winds and bees around her, he realized he must act fast. Determined, he returned to the witch, who demanded another three months of service. Once again, Picked endured the ordeal.

Next, Picked found a dying swan and managed to remove its wings within the required timeframe. After sewing them onto his body, Picked transformed into a grotesque creature, neither bird nor vegetable. Every day, he endured excruciating pain. Through this torment, he gained profound insights into life, love, and existence. More importantly, he developed the ability to understand the language of all animals.

After six months, Picked emerged with new wings and a matured mind. No longer the naive youth, he became a wise and experienced individual. On the day his bandages were removed, Picked soared into the sky, experiencing freedom and transcendence. What once seemed impossible — a pickle flying in the sky — had become reality.

Continuing his journey, Picked ventured into the sea, learning survival skills from sharks and whales, mastering the art of navigating ocean waves with his fin-like wings.

Through trials and tribulations, Picked not only transformed physically but also spiritually, becoming a symbol of surpassing one's limits and achieving greatness.
