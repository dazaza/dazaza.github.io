Why are cosmetics cheaper when bought online compared to in-store counters?

by anonymous on 2011-04-01 12:47:45

Why is it cheaper to buy cosmetics online than from counters?

Many buyers who have shopped on Taobao ask this question: why do the same products have such a large price difference between online and counter prices? A product that costs thousands of yuan at the counter sells for only three or four hundred yuan online, which is about 30-40% of the genuine product's price, and can still be inspected at the counter. Sellers claim that their products are absolutely sold in mainland counters, so where do these cheap but authentic full-price items come from?

In the skincare and cosmetics industry, there are some "unspoken rules" that may explain this phenomenon.

Recently, an incident involving a cosmetics counter manager at a well-known department store in Shanghai has shed some light on the matter. Over the course of three or four years, the manager, along with other beauty consultants (commonly referred to as BA, short for "Beauty Advancer") at the counter, had been embezzling various free samples that were supposed to be given to customers. They then sold these samples and genuine products through both the counter and online channels, earning a substantial amount of money (the exact amount is unclear due to hearsay).

After doing this for several years, they were eventually caught due to a customer complaint about the counter, leading to a thorough investigation by the cosmetics company. As a result, the counter manager and all the BAs were exposed. In order to save face, the company made the BAs return all the embezzled funds, erased the past incidents, but handed over the most culpable manager to the judicial authorities for legal punishment. The BA was ultimately sentenced to prison.

Online cosmetic and skincare products sell for less than the purchase price

Physical store owner: Online selling prices are lower than the purchase price

Anny has been running a physical cosmetics store in Hangzhou. During the usual purchasing season, she would directly go to Hong Kong, where SASA offers very cheap authentic goods, especially for brands like Lancome, Clinique, and DIOR, with discounts ranging from 40-70%. Larger purchases receive even greater discounts. However, recently, she discovered that some items on Taobao sell for less than her purchase price.

For example, Lancome launched a new Pure Vision Essence in the second half of the year, with a counter price of 1880 yuan, but it was listed online for 738 yuan, and could be further negotiated down to around 600 yuan, which is almost less than 30% of the original price. "I tried buying a few items, and when I compared them at the counter, whether it was the outer packaging, batch number, or production date, they were exactly the same. When I opened them, they were indistinguishable from the genuine products," Anny said. Since she also sells Lancome products, if these items were counterfeit, they would definitely not have the CIQ label or Chinese label required for sales in mainland China, and the batch numbers would also differ. Therefore, these products are generally considered to be from mainland counters.

When communicating with a seller named "Angel," the seller claimed that all the items came from Lancome counters and accepted various forms of "counter inspection." If the items were found to be counterfeit, a full refund would be given. The facts proved that the seller had no negative reviews, and two buyers who shopped at the store revealed that they had checked the items at the counter, confirming them to be genuine, making the seller trustworthy.

Counter ladies expose the "black curtain"

The reporter contacted a former counter lady named Sasha, who revealed that in the cosmetics industry, it is common for BAs to embezzle free samples, and most of the time, all the BAs at a counter work together to commit these acts, with clear division of labor. From obtaining samples to selling them for cash, different BAs handle different parts of the process.

Method one: Embezzlement of gift samples

Most women shopping at malls know that when you spend a certain amount, you will be given some product samples, but there is no standard. Many regulars on beauty forums have experienced this: the number of samples given depends on the mood of the BA. If you encounter a good BA, you might get many samples; if not, you might only get a poor sample. Even in the same mall and the same counter, spending 1000 yuan might yield different samples. Every season change, cosmetics companies prepare many samples to distribute to old customers, but some old customers often don't receive sample gifts, and some customers trying to get samples with cut-out coupons from brochures are often told they are out on the first day. All these situations are manipulated by the BAs.

Cosmetics brand suppliers generally don't care how many samples the counter uses as long as the sales figures are high. BAs can request any number of samples, but the company does not monitor the destination of these samples. As a result, these samples end up in the hands of BAs, who resell them. Sasha mentioned that at a previous counter she worked at, the manager kept six sets of samples on display while pocketing the rest.

Method two: Withholding member gifts

BAs have the convenience of embezzling samples, but how do they obtain genuine products? It's actually quite simple because genuine products are also given as member gifts, which can be redeemed with points.

Sasha explained that many customers purchasing gifts at the counter are unaware of the membership points redemption system. Especially at the end of the year, many companies giving gifts to clients or men giving gifts to girlfriends don't care about membership card points. There are many tricks involved here. When Sasha worked at the counter, the BAs would create many membership cards under the names of their relatives or friends, accumulating all customer purchases onto their own cards. Sometimes, they could accumulate thousands or even tens of thousands of points in a day, allowing them to redeem a significant number of member gifts—genuine products—to be sold through other channels.

Method three: Trial products being "switched"

Every so often, the brand supplier provides the counter with a batch of trial products. These products are the same size as the genuine ones and can be freely tested by customers. Some counters often face "out of stock" situations with trial products, and this happens not just at one counter but at many. The BAs would tell you: "Sorry, we've run out of this trial product." However, the trial products weren't used up by customers but were switched by the BAs through other means.

Method four: Personal use products repackaged and re-entered into the market

An online post by a woman described her experience at a counter where the BA asked for the product packaging, promising to give her an extra sample if she returned the outer packaging. What did the BA need these empty packages for?

It turns out that every quarter, the cosmetics company distributes personal-use products to their BAs. Some companies provide a lot of goods, sometimes worth 4000-5000 yuan, which are genuine products but often "naked"—without outer packaging—or with damaged packaging. These products without complete packaging cannot be sold at high prices online. Once they have outer packaging, the selling price can be much higher. These outer packages are precisely what the BAs use to package their personal-use products, enabling better sales.
