by xjyzhenai on 2009-07-08 09:23:32

AirPlay is a small but powerful music player, with a size of only 736KB. Despite its compact size, it offers the following features:

# Less than 1MB in size, green software with no installation required

# Native reading of compressed files (RAR/ZIP)

# Native seamless playback support (effective in compressed state)

# Pure Music technology for sound restoration to deliver pure and high-quality audio

# Self-developed Zion architecture for graphics and audio engine

# Lyrics support, album picture display, and preview functionality

# Support for multiple formats, including lossless audio compatibility (with embedded CUE support)

# Support for various playlist formats, directory lists, and smart playlists

# Global shortcut keys and multimedia keyboard support

On June 17, 2009, a new version Close Beta 2, Zion was released.

Seamless playback, also known as Gapless, refers to the seamless playback method of digital audiovisual formats. It allows live music or continuous tracks (for CDs) to play continuously without interruption, with no silence between tracks.

Currently, Foobar2000 can achieve similar effects through plugins, and Apple iTunes can perform seamless playback for specific formats. However, AirPlay can realize seamless playback across media formats while supporting compressed files.

PureMusic technology is a unique sound precision restoration algorithm exclusive to the AirPlay media player. Unlike other decorative sound effect technologies, the PureMusic engine uses intelligent analysis techniques and efficient real-time calculations to process all audio information played back through AirPlay for restorative sound effects. The upgraded Zion architecture enhances the PureMusic technology, enabling better restoration of lossy music. AirPlay is developed based on the self-developed IIEP graphics and multimedia engine, achieving crystal-clear interface effects under both XP and Vista. Additionally, AirPlay's refined and aesthetically pleasing details have been appreciated by many users, and we look forward to you discovering more.

Of course, the IIEP engine does more than just provide an interface. AirPlay can open password-free ZIP or RAR compressed package files and automatically decompress them in the background. This can be done without installing any ZIP or RAR decompression programs as AirPlay already includes this functionality. Moreover, images and cue files within the compressed packages can also be utilized by AirPlay, providing a usage experience identical to that of uncompressed files.
