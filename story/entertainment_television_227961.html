Introduction to the plot of "Beijing War and Peace":

In the autumn of 1948, the battle in Northeast was intense. Chiang then secretly ordered him to suddenly attack Xibaipo. Fu's mobilization of troops alerted the underground party in Beijing. The Central Committee of the Party immediately deployed a pocket formation to meet the enemy. Mao Zedong's ingenious strategy successfully thwarted this conspiracy with an empty city tactic. The leakage of information caused suspicion among the Military Intelligence Bureau, who conducted investigations everywhere, secretly pointing the finger at Fu Zuoyi.

One day, Yan Youwen, the news director of Fu's unit, welcomed a reporter from the "Ta Kung Pao" into the "suppression headquarters". She was an undercover party member named Fu Dongju. In order to win Fu Zuoyi over for peace talks and achieve the peaceful liberation of Beijing, the North China Urban Work Department of the Chinese Communist Party agreed to her request to return to work in Beijing. Father and daughter met, each with their own concerns, but unable to speak openly, only carefully probing. Until one day, Fu Dongju discovered her father secretly reading Mao Zedong's "On the Coalition Government", realizing that her father had already been considering the issue of war and peace in Beijing. Fu Dongju's appearance immediately drew the attention of the Military Intelligence Bureau agents, who began tracking her like dogs, hoping to find a breakthrough through her. However, under the secret protection of Yan Youwen, Fu Dongju shook off the pursuit and connected with the party organization. At this time, the economy in the Nationalist-controlled areas collapsed, political corruption was rampant, and morale was low. The Pingjin region was filled with the clouds of war, prices soared, and people were anxious. To cooperate with the actions of the People's Liberation Army, the underground party in Beijing actively launched student movements and labor movements, and took advantage of every opportunity and method to participate in the work of persuading Fu Zuoyi's forces to stage a peaceful uprising. Meanwhile, various forces also infiltrated Fu Zuoyi. The American side indicated that it would directly provide military aid to him. Fu was keenly aware, carefully responding, but had his own plans in mind.
