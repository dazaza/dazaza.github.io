Good deeds will be rewarded with good.

by xiaoxiao67 on 2012-03-02 15:38:15

Good deeds will be rewarded with good results.

When we were on the boat, a young woman and a boy of about five years old were sitting next to us. Children get along naturally, so the two quickly started playing together. My wife wanted to chat with her but noticed that she had a gloomy face, so she decided not to disturb her.

The wind picked up, making the boat rock more violently. The young woman began to vomit and turned yellow in complexion; it was clear that she suffered from seasickness.

The boy stopped his noise and went over to ask, "Auntie, what's wrong with you?"

My wife took out some seasick medicine and handed it to her, saying, "Take one pill. It works wonders. You'll feel better soon."

The woman said thanks but did not take the medicine. She held her stomach and continued to endure the pain.

"Or you can suck on a piece of ginger," my wife handed over a slice of ginger.

This time, she accepted it, looked at it, put it in her mouth, then closed her eyes to rest.

As soon as we got off the ship, everyone was immediately captivated by the beauty of Mount Putuo. There were many monks and nuns here, and my wife, who believed in Buddhism, kept greeting them: "Amitabha."

At the Puji Temple, my wife and many other Buddhist tourists bowed down to worship the statue of Guanyin.

"Sister, who is that?" the little boy asked curiously.

"That's the Bodhisattva Guanyin. Don't you know?" our daughter proudly answered, then made a funny face at me.

"Why do they bow to her?"

"Because the Bodhisattva Guanyin can bless people with peace and happiness."

"Why don't you bow to her?"

"I don't believe in Buddhism. Besides, my dad says: As long as there is Buddha in your heart, do more good deeds and no bad deeds, it doesn't matter whether you worship Buddha or not."

"Auntie, is what she said correct?" the boy asked the woman beside him.

The woman blushed and said yes, then tried to pull the boy away.

"I want to play with sister, play with sister!" the boy shouted, so the woman let him stay.

The children chattered endlessly when they were together. The woman also started talking with my wife.

When we arrived at the Heart Stone, a tour guide was introducing: "Everyone, just touch this Heart Stone, and everything will go well for you. But there is a prerequisite: you must have a kind heart."

For a moment, many people went to touch the stone, and many took a lot of pictures. I noticed that the woman was staring at the stone from afar. I walked up to her and said, "Why don't you touch it? For good luck."

She smiled at me, then returned to her gloomy expression, saying, "I don't believe in this."

"The sister is right, nothing happens if you don't do anything," my wife joined in.

"But a kind heart should still be cherished," I said.

"That's true. Good deeds will be rewarded with good results, evil deeds with evil consequences," my wife agreed this time.

After a few minutes, the woman looked at me and suddenly said, "Big brother, may I borrow your pen?"

I handed her the pen and paper.

We continued wandering in this sea and sky Buddhist paradise. When we reached the Reluctant-to-Depart Guanyin Temple, the woman suddenly called us over.

She cried and said to my wife, "Big brother, Big sister, I know you are good people. I beg you to return him to his parents. Here is his address and home phone number."

"You're not his aunt?" my wife asked in confusion.

"No, I am their nanny. I've worked for them for three years. Everything was fine until a few days ago, when that beast, his father, came home drunk. While the female owner was not at home, he actually...he actually violated me..."

At this point, the woman was already in tears. She continued, "I couldn't just let it pass. I wanted revenge, so I tricked this boy out, intending to kill him, making his family end their lineage. But after three years, I couldn't bring myself to do it. When I didn't know what to do, I happened to encounter a cruise to Mount Putuo, so I boarded. I planned to seize the opportunity to throw him into the sea or leave him on the mountain. Along the way, your kindness moved me and made me abandon my evil thoughts. Big brother, Big sister, I hope good people will have good rewards." After finishing, she knelt down and bowed to us, then quickly disappeared among the tourists.

I dialed the boy's father's mobile phone and soon heard an anxious voice. Upon learning that the child was with us, I only heard him say, "How much money do you want? Just tell me, and I will pay you accordingly."

"I don't want a penny," I loudly replied.

In order to wait for them, we didn't continue sightseeing, but my wife and I both felt that the purpose of coming here had been achieved.
