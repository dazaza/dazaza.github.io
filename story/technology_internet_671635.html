29-Year-Old Man Swallows 400 Estrogen Pills to Enlarge Breasts, Wishing to Be a Woman

by aiipii12987 on 2012-02-22 11:14:08

Regardless of whether it's a red light or a green light ahead, A Huan remains committed to walking the path as a woman. A is still biologically a man, 29 years old this year, but since the age of 6, he has been fantasizing about becoming a woman. On the night of Qixi Festival, he secretly left his mother who was in the late stages of cancer and traveled alone from Wuhan to Hefei, saying that he wanted to spend some time with his "sisters" here. If possible, he would choose to have breast augmentation surgery in Hefei. On August 7th, A Huan swallowed 400 estrogen pills, "I can only feel like a woman through medication." He told reporters that he didn't want to further distress his sick mother, but he will continue on this path to become a woman.

A 29-year-old man swallows 400 estrogen pills.

"Is this the Morning Post hotline? My friend came from Wuhan to Hefei, bought 3 bottles of ethinyl estradiol at 15:18, each bottle containing 100 pills, and also brought a bottle of soy isoflavone estrogen (100 pills). After buying the medicine, he immediately took it with mineral water and is now on the grass opposite the Walmart supermarket on Changjiang East Road. Can you come and check on him?"

On the afternoon of August 7th, after receiving a call from a reader, the reporter immediately rushed to the scene and saw a man and a woman chatting by the grass. The man was about 1.75 meters tall, overweight, with heavy body hair, while the woman looked relatively delicate but had somewhat strange behavior.

It was later learned that the man's name is A Huan, 29 years old, graduated from a university in Wuhan in 2005. The woman's name is Hai Na, a man who has undergone gender reassignment surgery. The reason she named herself Hai Na is to express her wish to regain her daughterhood. According to her, she and A Huan are "sisterly" related.

Upon seeing the reporter, encouraged by Hai Na, A Huan directly said, "I want to be a woman. I hope everyone will not discriminate against our TS (transsexual) community."

A Huan introduced himself, saying that since childhood, he followed his mother shopping and felt particularly close to women's clothing in stores. His mother, a middle school teacher, always praised girls for being sensible and continuously encouraged him to play with girls and be a well-behaved child who doesn’t fight. From an early memory, A Huan increasingly inclined towards being a girl. He often thought that he should have been born as a daughter, just a joke played by heaven during his creation.

Due to his gender orientation, A Huan frequently encountered setbacks in job hunting after graduation. His passion and expertise in the cosmetics industry were often rejected either because he was male or due to his feminine tendencies. In 2006, A Huan’s mother was diagnosed with breast cancer (his parents divorced in 2002, and he lived with his mother), and the family situation became difficult. Unable to escape the psychological predicament caused by his gender issues, he persisted in wanting to be a woman.

■ Dialogue

Experience: Wearing women's clothes, classmates ignored me

Reporter: When did you realize you liked being a girl?

A Huan: At 6 years old. When I went shopping with my mom, I saw girls' children's clothing and found them especially beautiful and familiar. I thought they should be worn by me. But my mom didn't buy them for me, which was the first time I had a concept of gender.

Reporter: When was the first time you wore women's clothes?

A Huan: At 7 years old. I secretly wore my mom's skirt, remember it was light red, and I felt particularly free. My mom usually liked tying my hair with red silk ribbons, regretting that she couldn't have a daughter. Sometimes she tied my hair into pigtails, which I really liked. I was afraid my classmates would undo my braids, so I always wore a hat. Later, I often wore women's clothes at home or when meeting TS friends.

Reporter: When was the first time you wore women's clothes in public?

A Huan: During my sophomore internship. I wore women's clothes on the road. Women's jeans, short white top, women's bag, shoulder-length hair. On the road, except for the team leader, all the classmates ignored me.

Reporter: How were your relationships with classmates during school?

A Huan: In middle school, I was often bullied by male classmates, saying I liked playing with girls and disgraced the boys. The most memorable incident was when more than half of the male students surrounded me and raised their table tennis paddles, saying "We'll chop you to death!" I was very scared. Eventually, the female classmates protected me and escorted me home.

Explanation: Taking medication to experience being a woman

Reporter: When did you start thinking about gender reassignment surgery?

A Huan: At 19 years old.

Reporter: Can your family accept it?

A Huan: At 22 years old, I told my mom about my idea. She only had one son, initially could not accept it, and always cried. But she also knew it couldn't be changed and has now accepted it. However, other relatives still cannot accept it.

Reporter: When did you come to Hefei? Why did you choose Hefei?

A Huan: I arrived in Hefei on the evening of Qixi Festival because I was particularly troubled. I have a TS sister in Hefei, so I came to relax.

Reporter: Why do you take so much estrogen?

A Huan: Because I don't have money for breast augmentation surgery, I can only feel like a woman through taking medication, especially the pain in the breasts, which is also a kind of emotional release. To be a woman, to let society accept us, I will continue without hesitation.

Emotion: Had a girlfriend before, but no feelings for women

Reporter: What work have you done before?

A Huan: I used to sell sanitary napkins, intermittently for 5 years. But I am good at makeup and really want to work in the cosmetics industry, but they all refused me because I am not a woman. I feel my feminine intuition is particularly good, I am naturally a woman.

Reporter: Are you of age, hasn't anyone introduced you to a girlfriend at home?

A Huan: Definitely, others introduced me to a few, but I had no feelings for them. Then I told them my desire to be a woman, and we naturally parted ways.

Hope: To have a family and children as a woman

Reporter: What difficulties do you currently face?

A Huan: First, I don't want my mother to know that I am still committed to being a woman, because my mother is in the late stages of cancer, and I fear she won't be able to handle the shock; second, I cannot choose my way of life according to my own ideas, and my father opposes it, "Once I become a woman, my father will immediately cut ties with me."

Reporter: What do you hope society can do for you?

A Huan: I want to call for more people to pay attention to the TS group, because we are a vulnerable group in this society. We hope to get help and care, hope not to be discriminated against by society, and hope to gain recognition. Do you know, some sisters really want to go home during the New Year, but they can't because once their families know about their situation, their parents will cut ties with them.

The reporter feared that he might feel unwell after taking the medication and kept urging him to go to the hospital quickly, but A Huan insisted that taking so much medication was just to experience the feeling of being a woman. Helplessly, the reporter could only ask A Huan's friend Hai Na to take good care of A Huan.
