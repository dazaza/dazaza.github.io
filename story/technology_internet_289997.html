Not Much Change - Static Evaluation of the 2010 Lexus GS300

by 0pni077j3y on 2009-12-03 10:50:42

Although this GS is called the 2010 model, we didn't see any major changes. There were only minor detail changes in appearance; everything else basically remained the same as the old model. The craftsmanship is still delicate and the sheet metal part is also quite good.

NetEase Auto, November 26th report - Many brands have launched new or updated models this year, and the variety that has caught our attention is not small. Although we are already at the end of the year, there are still manufacturers who want to catch up with this wave of updates, and Lexus is one of them. What kind of changes will there be in the updates of models under this luxury brand?

The 2010 model with little change - Static evaluation of the styling and craftsmanship of the 2010 Lexus GS300. The body shape still continues the consistent style of Lexus, exactly the same as the old model. It's hard to find any difference between the new GS300 and the old one from the overall appearance. The body size is still the same, and the lines are the same as the old model, which might make some people feel a bit disappointed.

The headlights are designed in a large and small shape, reminiscent of the Mercedes-Benz E-Class, with a very strong sense of quality.

The taillights are relatively regular, with two round light groups in a square, which can also be considered as an element of stability with a touch of dynamism. Of course, the handling of the light group is still the same as before. However, the shape of the headlights easily reminds us of the new Mercedes-Benz E-Class. It turns out that such design originates from there. The taillight section consists of square and round combinations, which are also quite common in the market.

The vertical intake grille is a new family emblem of Lexus, and the chrome decoration on the body details embodies a sense of luxury. Such densely arranged vertical intake grilles can be said to be the new family emblem of Lexus, very eye-catching, and this point has been well preserved on the new GS300.
