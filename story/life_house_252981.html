What are the potential hazards in kitchen renovation?

by longersing on 2009-10-28 14:45:59

What are the potential hazards in kitchen renovation? These factors, such as preventing dampness, fire, dirt accumulation, and ventilation failure, etc., should be considered before the renovation to avoid complaints after the renovation. A reasonable design structure can help avoid these hidden dangers after the kitchen renovation.

The kitchen is where people wash vegetables and cook, so it not only needs to be moisture-proof and fireproof but also needs to solve the problem of cleaning oil stains. Therefore, when selecting materials for kitchen renovation, one must put in a lot of effort. Experts have provided three aspects of expertise for this purpose.

Flooring material: Slip-resistant tiles or full-through tiles are the best

Nowadays, people are very particular about the choice of materials during renovation. Some people use granite, marble, and other natural stones in the kitchen to achieve uniformity with the indoor flooring. However, experts point out that although these stones are durable and aesthetically pleasing, natural stones are not waterproof. Long-term exposure to water can deepen the color of the stone, causing discoloration. Moreover, if the floor gets wet over a large area, it becomes slippery, increasing the risk of falls. Therefore, it is recommended to use less or no natural stone in the humid kitchen environment.

In addition, although solid wood floors and laminate floors continue to improve in technology, their biggest weakness is still their susceptibility to water damage and deformation due to moisture. Currently, the most commonly used materials in kitchens are anti-slip tiles or full-through tiles, which are both economical and practical. Experts remind us to fully consider the moisture-proof function when selecting materials for kitchen renovation.

Wall material: Easy-to-clean tiles are popular

Experts point out that kitchen walls should be made of materials that are easy to clean and resistant to grease. They should also be fireproof and heat-resistant. Currently, available options in building material markets include fireproof plastic wallpaper and treated fireproof boards, but the most popular choice remains colorful tiles that can liven up the kitchen's visual appeal. The unique physical stability of tiles, their ability to withstand high temperatures, and ease of cleaning are reasons why they remain the primary material for kitchen walls.

Ceiling material: Consider using panels

Experts point out that regardless of the type of ceiling material chosen, it must be fireproof and non-deformable. Currently, the main ceiling materials available for kitchens in the building material market are plastic panels and aluminum panels. Among them, plastic panels are inexpensive but offer fewer color options. Aluminum panels are very beautiful, commonly available in square and long strip shapes, with a wide range of spray-painted colors to choose from, though they are more expensive. Additionally, experts particularly remind that if recessed lights are used, a layer should be added when embedding the light into the ceiling to prevent heat generated by the light from causing issues.
