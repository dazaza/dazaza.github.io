Principle of Operation of Jaw Crushers:

Jaw crushers use electric motors as power sources. Through motor pulleys, V-belts, and groove wheels, the eccentric shaft is driven, causing the movable jaw to move reciprocally along a predetermined trajectory, thus crushing the material inside the crushing cavity formed by fixed jaw plates, movable jaw plates, and side guard plates, and discharging the crushed material through the lower discharge opening.

Structure Composition of Jaw Crushers:

The structure mainly consists of frame, eccentric shaft, large belt wheel, flywheel, moving jaw, side guard plate, toggle plate, toggle seat behind, clearance adjustment screw rod, reset spring, fixed jaw plate, and movable jaw plate, among which the toggle plate also serves as a safety function. This series of jaw crushers adopts a curved motion compression type. The motor drives the belt and pulley, through the eccentric shaft causing the moving jaw to move up and down. When the moving jaw rises, the angle between the toggle plate and the moving jaw increases, pushing the moving jaw closer to the fixed jaw, simultaneously crushing the material by squeezing, grinding, and pulverizing it. When the moving jaw descends, the angle between the toggle plate and the moving jaw decreases, allowing the moving jaw to leave the fixed jaw under the action of tie rods and springs. At this point, the already crushed material is discharged from the lower outlet of the crushing cavity. With the continuous rotation of the motor, the jaw crusher performs periodic crushing and discharging, achieving batch production.
