Eight Points to Note for Home Bar Decoration Design

by longersing on 2009-10-07 21:53:50

There are eight points to note in the design and decoration of a home bar, such as choosing appliances, electrical and drainage design, sink design, wine cabinet design, and countertop material design, etc., all of which require attention to many aspects:

1. When setting up a bar inside, it is necessary to consider the bar as part of the complete space, not just as a piece of furniture. Good design can integrate the bar into the space. There are no specific rules for the location of the bar. Designers usually recommend using some odd spaces. If the bar is integrated into the space as the main body, then the flow line direction must be carefully considered. Good design has guidance, making living more comfortable.

2. The position will also affect the circuit and water supply and drainage design, especially when the corner is far from the pipe room or drain pipe, drainage becomes a big problem. Drainage pipes need a certain tilt angle. If the bar is close to the outdoors, the drainage pipe can be connected to the outside, with a separate pipeline for drainage; if the pipeline needs to be connected to the pipe room but the tilt angle is insufficient, and the pipe must be installed in the ceiling or wall, construction will be more complicated, and costs will increase accordingly.

3. If you want to use high-power appliances like induction cookers in the bar, it's best to design a separate circuit to prevent circuit breakers from tripping.

4. Bars built in corners require at least 90cm of operating space. Bar heights come in two sizes: single-layer bars are about 110cm, while double-layer bars are 80cm and 105cm. The difference between the two layers should be at least 25cm so that items can be placed on the inner layer.

5. The depth of the countertop must be determined according to the function of the bar. The width of the countertop needed for drinking beverages and dining is different. If there are seats in front of the counter, the countertop must protrude from the bar itself, so the countertop depth should be at least 40-60cm. This width makes it easier to store items below the bar.

6. For bars with sinks, pay attention when purchasing the sink. It’s best to choose a flat-bottomed sink to prevent cups from tipping over or breaking. The depth of the sink should be at least 20cm to avoid splashing water everywhere.

7. Wine cabinet design should focus on convenience of use. Each level should be at least 30-40cm high. The part for placing wine bottles should be designed to be slanted so that the wine can cover the cork, allowing the wine to be stored longer. The depth of the cabinet should not be too deep, as it would be inconvenient to grab a glass if you have to reach over other objects.

8. The countertop should preferably be made of wear-resistant materials, veneer is not suitable. Bars with sinks should also be water-resistant; if the bar uses appliances, fire-resistant materials are the best, such as artificial stone, melamine laminate, natural stone, etc., which are all ideal materials.
