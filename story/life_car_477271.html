Refresh Your Car for Summer: A Comprehensive Guide to Car Beauty and Maintenance

As the seasons change, the characteristics of summer - hot weather and frequent thunderstorms - become increasingly apparent. Car maintenance for summer has also become a recent theme and hotspot in services offered by 4S stores. Due to weather conditions, some systems or devices that are used frequently or under heavy load, such as the air conditioning system, lubrication system, and tires, are prone to failure. At the same time, increased rainfall and strong sunlight can accelerate the aging of the car's paint and components. Therefore, in addition to regular maintenance, it is advisable to add some special care to prevent these issues from developing.

For this reason, China Auto Beauty Recruitment Network (www.qcmrrc.com) recommends a summer car maintenance guide to provide advice on summer car care, especially for new car owners.

1. Create a Cool Cabin

In summer, air conditioning is one of the essential features of a car. Summer is also a high-risk period for car air conditioning failures. If your car's air conditioning suddenly stops working under the scorching sun, you might have to endure a "free sauna." Therefore, car owners who neglect regular maintenance should pay attention to maintaining their vehicle’s air conditioning system.

2. Inspect the Air Conditioning System

Some car air conditioners, after being unused for nearly half a year, may have leaked all their refrigerant and need replenishment. When you visit a repair shop, don't forget to remind the technician to clean the evaporator and air conditioning filter. Their blockage will affect cooling efficiency and cause unpleasant odors during use. Additionally, checking whether the air conditioning cooling effect matches the factory specifications is an essential step in maintaining the air conditioner, which is also a crucial part of diagnosing if the air conditioner is functioning properly.

Moreover, it's best to give the entire air conditioning system a thorough cleaning in summer. There are many types of cleaning agents available on the market for cleaning air conditioning ducts. Car owners can buy them and clean the system themselves. Alternatively, those who prefer not to do it themselves can go to a 4S store or car decoration shop where they can use high-concentration ozone water to sterilize the car air conditioning system.

3. Learn to Use the Air Conditioning Correctly

1. Start with the external circulation mode. Cars parked under the blazing sun in summer can reach internal temperatures of sixty to seventy degrees Celsius. In such cases, open the car doors and windows first to ventilate using fans. After two or three minutes of airing out the heat, start the car without rushing to close the windows. Begin with the external circulation mode of the air conditioning until the temperature inside and outside the cabin becomes similar. Then close the windows and switch to internal circulation. Although it may be slightly warmer at the beginning, this method avoids overloading the engine upon startup and allows the air conditioning compressor to quickly enter its optimal operating state, making it the best choice.

2. Direct the cold air upwards. The direction of the air conditioning outlet is also important. According to the physical principle of air circulation, cold air sinks while hot air rises, creating convection. Therefore, directing the cold air upwards allows for better airflow between hot and cold air in the car, speeding up the cooling process. Also, avoid setting the air conditioning temperature too low and the wind speed too high. For automatic climate control systems, the temperature difference between the inside and outside of the car should not exceed 10°C. For non-constant temperature systems, set the air conditioning temperature to the lowest and the wind speed to the highest initially, then adjust the temperature higher and reduce the wind speed when you feel cool enough.

3. Turn off the air conditioning before reaching your destination. The unpleasant odor from the air conditioning is often caused by mold breeding in a dark and damp environment formed by condensation water in the air conditioning pipes. If you turn off the air conditioning immediately upon stopping the car, the condensation water may accumulate in the air conditioning pipes, forming a source of pollution. Therefore, it is generally recommended to turn off the air conditioning three to five minutes before reaching your destination or to turn off the air conditioning power upon arrival, keeping the fan running while increasing the airflow appropriately. This way, the temperature inside the air conditioning pipes can rise before parking, reducing the production of condensation water and maintaining the relative dryness of the air conditioning system, thus eliminating the space for mold to survive.
