New technology application refreshes the new game experience - Age1983

by hexiaoyao on 2008-12-22 23:46:15

The successful development and application of a new technology or material may not be a big deal for the scientific community. However, if within a period of time, a series of electronic technologies, especially those that can be applied to computers, emerge, the effect produced will be significantly different. And for the gaming industry, the emergence of some technologies and materials that seem unrelated to games may, to some extent, help games achieve impossible dreams. Let's take a look at a few conceptual-level new technologies and materials launched by scientists this year. Although they are still at the conceptual level, in the future, they may have an extraordinary impact on our gaming world.

Double-sided display: making games sharable

At some point, we might miss the scenes from our childhood when we and our buddies played Contra and Double Dragon with a red-and-white game console. Back then, it was truly a case of brotherhood and teamwork. With the popularity of personal computers, such opportunities for two people to play games on one computer have become fewer and fewer. Even though sometimes we still use keyboards or controllers to play games like Samurai Shodown on emulators, it doesn't quite feel right. Playing online games in virtual worlds may still reflect team spirit, even if sitting side by side in an internet cafe, but the feeling of sharing games is still hard to find.

However, this dream has once again become possible thanks to the successful advancement of 3D technology. 3D display technology is gradually becoming more mature, and people are paying close attention to it. While this technology continues to develop, another display technology - double-sided display technology - has also made significant progress. On October 21st, LG showcased its latest LCD monitor, which features the ability to support double-sided displays. This LCD monitor has a screen size of 15 inches and a resolution of 512x384. Compared to regular 15-inch LCD monitors, this resolution is not high. Through special cutting and technology, the same image content will be simultaneously displayed on both sides of the monitor. Double-sided display LCD monitors are very suitable for users in special industries.

It's easy to imagine that after the large-scale application of such double-sided display LCD monitors, we can play games while sitting face-to-face with friends, using the same computer to duel and fight. Even if playing alone, friends can sit quietly across from you, admiring your gaming skills. The reality of shared gaming is no longer just a childhood memory.

"Paper" battery: making laptops true entertainment tools

Nowadays, notebook computers are sold at prices as low as vegetables. Laptops that used to cost tens of thousands of yuan can now be taken home for as low as 2999 yuan. Truly, what was once reserved for the wealthy is now accessible to ordinary households. However, while the price has dropped, many Chinese computer users who use their computers as gaming machines are facing new problems. The convenience and compactness of laptops come with certain limitations. Not only is CPU heat dissipation an old issue, but the battery is also a headache for many gamers. Sometimes when traveling, you want to play a game on the road, but the battery doesn't cooperate. Gaming consumes a lot of power, and before you know it, the fully charged battery runs out and automatically shuts down, ruining your gaming mood. The problem is that achieving breakthroughs in battery technology to make them more durable and long-lasting with greater capacity seems difficult. Traditional batteries consist of three elements: electrolyte composed of positive and negative ions, two electrodes made of different materials, and a separator that allows positive and negative ions to move in opposite directions. Many scientists have tried to create smaller, more flexible batteries, but without significant success. A major reason is the difficulty in combining these elements into thinner materials.

This situation is about to change. Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute (RPI) in New York State announced the development of a double-layer capacitor and lithium battery composed of cellulose and carbon nanotubes. Scientists dissolve cellulose used in papermaking in saltwater solutions, add carbon nanotubes, and dry the mixture. This produces a paper-like film, white on one side and black on the other due to the carbon nanotubes. The research team then soaks the cellulose in lithium hexafluorophosphate solution and covers the white side of the film with metallic lithium. A new type of paper battery is born, where carbon nanotubes and metallic lithium represent the two electrodes, the solution provides the electrolyte, and the cellulose acts similarly to the separator.

This battery generates electricity in the same way as traditional lithium-ion batteries used in many electronics today. However, all components of this battery can be combined into a lightweight, flexible sheet of paper. These components are only dozens of micrometers thick, equivalent to the thickness of a human hair, and can bend like paper. Researchers at RPI believe that "batteries could be manufactured in the future using roll-to-roll printing technology." This battery has a static capacitance of 22F/g, a maximum operating voltage of 2.3V, and an energy density of approximately 13Wh/kg, reaching the level of existing double-layer capacitors. The weight per unit area is only 15mg/cm², extremely light, and since [Bmin][Cl] contains no water, the working temperature range of this component is very wide, from -77°C to +150°C. Although this component cannot function as a capacitor when the temperature drops to -196°C, it can fully recover its function when the temperature rises. RPI has confirmed that this component can maintain good characteristics after repeated charging and discharging more than 100 times. If this battery is successfully used in laptops, it will achieve the largest capacity with the smallest volume and lightest weight, surpassing the widely used lithium batteries in laptops today. After all, technically speaking, the maximum voltage of lithium batteries during charging is 3.6V, discharging at a fixed output power of 10mA/g. The battery capacity can reach up to 430mAh/g during the first charge, but drops below 110mAh/g after the second charge. It's easy to imagine that with such powerful power assurance, even if gaming consumes a lot of power, you can play continuously for nearly ten hours. Meanwhile, the prototype of this battery uses inexpensive materials, which will greatly reduce the cost of purchasing laptop batteries.

Memristor: Instant-on PCs let gaming happen without waiting

Almost everyone who plays games has inevitably encountered the following situation: playing happily, suddenly the computer crashes. Those playing single-player games will definitely regret not saving the game and have to start over from the last save point. Online gamers might not worry about this, but the problem is that the process of restarting after a crash can be long, and the result may be that you were in the middle of a battle with friends or fighting the ultimate boss. After all, this is the moment when the computer load is the highest, and the possibility of crashing increases several times. Once crashed, when you log back in, everything might be over.

On May 1st, researchers at HP Labs published a paper in the British journal Nature claiming that they had confirmed the existence of the fourth fundamental element in the circuit world—the memristor, abbreviated as Memristor—and successfully designed a working physical model of the memristor. This discovery paves the way for creating non-volatile storage devices, instant-on PCs, more energy-efficient computers, and analog computers that handle and connect information in ways similar to the human brain. In the future, it may even significantly impact the development of electronic science by greatly increasing the functional density that transistors can achieve.
