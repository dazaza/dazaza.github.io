by lieencq09 on 2011-09-18 10:42:56

Lin Rongda, born in 1989 and residing in Huadu District's Xinhua Street, has always been very concerned about social issues. In September 2010, while traveling to Lianjiang on a bus from the Huadu Bus Terminal, he noticed that the issued invoice lacked a financial seal or an invoice-specific stamp. Upon this discovery, Lin Rongda reported the matter to the Huadu District Local Taxation Bureau. In November 2010, the Huadu District Local Taxation Bureau issued Lin Rongda a document titled "Written Notice of Tax Violation Reporting Case Review Situation" along with a reward notification for reporting tax violations.

This written notice stated that the Huadu District Local Taxation Bureau had lawfully investigated the reported invoice issue, confirming it to be true. Consequently, a fine of 500 yuan was issued to the Huadu Bus Terminal. After several leaders signed off, the Taxation Bureau decided to award Lin Rongda a reporting bonus of 5 yuan. However, Lin Rongda expressed that his expenses for taking a taxi to report the issue at the Taxation Bureau and sending registered letters far exceeded 5 yuan. Dissatisfied with the 5-yuan reward, Lin Rongda took the Huadu Local Taxation Bureau to court.

On July 7 of the same year, the first-instance Huadu District Court ruled that after receiving Lin Rongda's report, the Huadu District Local Taxation Bureau promptly investigated the reported enterprise and informed him in writing of the investigation results. After verifying the truth of the report, based on the extent of contribution, following legal regulations and procedural approvals, the Taxation Bureau rewarded Lin Rongda, which the court supported. The court did not support Lin Rongda's request to revoke the reward notice and reissue a response, as well as compensation for his losses, due to lack of factual and legal basis.

Unsatisfied with the verdict, Lin Rongda appealed to the Guangzhou Intermediate People's Court. Yesterday afternoon, after waiting for Lin Rongda for half an hour without his appearance, the judge decided to proceed with the trial in absentia. During the trial, the Huadu District Local Taxation Bureau hoped the court would uphold the first-instance judgment. After reviewing the written materials, the judge announced a recess, with a decision to be announced later.

According to reports by Dong Liu and intern He Chao of the Yangcheng Evening News, Lin Rongda, who resides in Huadu District, reported to the Huadu District Local Taxation Bureau that the bus station he traveled from had issued invoices lacking financial seals or invoice-specific stamps. Dissatisfied with the 5-yuan reporting reward, he filed a lawsuit against the Taxation Bureau. After losing the case, the second-instance hearing was held at the Guangzhou Intermediate People's Court on August 31.
