The silver-white metal palace, with exaggerated lines and constantly flashing colorful lights, all indicated that this place belonged to a world of high-tech civilization. The vast space could accommodate tens of thousands of people. Various types of robots were busily at work. The forms of these machines were diverse: some were mechanized, others human-like, and still others resembled various biological entities. This suggested one thing: the level of technology in this world had reached an unimaginable height. Despite being so advanced, there was a sense that something was missing—life! The entire room was filled with all sorts of robots, but there were no humans!

After a long while, a golden, round toy with small wings flew out, followed by a chubby little boy dressed in a silver robe. When the little boy appeared, all the robots in the hall had their eyes flashing with respectful light, and they only continued their work after realizing that the boy was ignoring them.

"Little Jin, don't run around too much, wait for me," said the little boy who clearly adored his lively toy. His arrival also brought more vitality into the grand hall, dispersing its rigidity and sterility. It must be admitted, although everything here was splendid and advanced, it was overly rigid. Everything was made of shiny metal whose texture was indistinguishable. A few green plants as decorations would surely make the environment more comfortable.

"Master, come catch me. If you catch me, Little Jin will sing you a song," laughed the little robot, flapping its wings. Its big mouth split open, almost as if its head was going to split in half, but it was undeniably cute, making people want... to slap it playfully.

However, this time, Little Jin's master did not joyfully chase him as he usually did, but instead stood in silence for a moment, which puzzled Little Jin. Even though it might seem insignificant, Little Jin was far more advanced than the other working robots. He was the latest product of the Maya Empire, specifically designed to serve the great prince, which was considered the highest honor for a robot. In this dimension, Maya technology had already reached its peak, invincible across the stars.

In this dimension, the Maya Empire was as revered as God, with countless star systems bowing under the might of the Maya army. Space-time jumping technology rendered spatial distances meaningless. Neither spiritual civilizations, technological civilizations, nor other alien civilizations could withstand the onslaught of the Maya army. However, a thousand years ago, the Maya Empire halted its forward march because the emperor discovered an ultimate secret at the source of Maya civilization on Earth—the secret to truly becoming a god.

And recently, rumors had it that this plan had been realized. Indeed, at the current level of Maya technology, once conceived, nothing was impossible, the only requirement being time.

The adorable little prince silently stared at Little Jin, then suddenly grabbed him before he could escape. The force used by the master was something no robot could resist. The little prince looked at Little Jin in his hand, his eyes flickering with a moment of hesitation, but he still tightly held onto his favorite toy as he walked towards a well-like object next to the grand hall.

The prince peered into the well, which was like a mirror reflecting the normal situation inside the grand hall. At that moment, clear footsteps could be heard from outside the hall. Clenching his teeth, the little prince threw Little Jin into the mirror-like surface before he could shout. "Boo..." The mirror didn’t break but rippled like water, and Little Jin disappeared without a trace.

At that moment, a dignified middle-aged man wearing a crown stood angrily in front of the little prince. Beside him was a woman, whose beauty was beyond description, surpassing any language's ability to depict her. You couldn't find any flaws on her, and even if you did dislike something about her, she would adjust perfectly under your perception.

"Kart, do you know what you are doing!" The middle-aged man's angry voice echoed throughout the grand hall. All the robots working there were frightened and fell prostrate on the ground; several even exploded with sparks.

"Kart, what you're doing is wrong. The Parallel Mirror connects to other lateral universes, worlds completely different from ours. If Little Jin escapes the judgment of the universe's parallel laws, he will bring an unbalanced development factor to that universe!"

The little prince showed no fear, but smiled slightly. "Father, Mother, you've already said that even we cannot resist the parallel laws of the universe. Little Jin has merely a theoretical chance in trillions of quarks."

The anger of the emperor seemed not to have subsided, but the empress beside him pulled him back. "Your Majesty, today is a grand day. Let us not blame our son. My dear, today our Maya civilization will reach its final glory. Soon, we will become true gods, the sole beings of this universe!"

Speaking of becoming gods, the king's expression finally softened. "We will become light. The universe will be us, and we will be the universe, inseparable forever!"

"Let's go, my beloved child, let us become light!" The king and empress each took the prince's hand and walked towards the entrance of the grand hall. The massive doors opened, revealing blinding light illuminating the entire world. In sight was a floating grand altar, from which rose milky white light. Beneath the altar stretched an endless mechanical army!

That light wasn't from the sun, but from these countless mechas, any one of which possessed the terrifying power to conquer a planet. And it was precisely these forces that made the Maya Empire invincible!

The entire Earth was covered with densely packed robots... not a single living creature, just robots. Their eyes unanimously gazed at the lone altar and their sole king! Outside Earth, the entire solar system was filled with dense clusters of giant interstellar warships. All soldiers aboard stood solemnly, looking at the altar on the screen where the king, queen, and prince appeared... all of whom were robots.

Interstellar warships extended endlessly from the solar system, their end still unseen. These were the invincible fleets of Maya civilization, against which no civilization could stand. On this day, all soldiers and vassal states of Maya civilization would witness a glorious moment—they would see their great king become a god, become light, and eternally illuminate this universe!

Gazing at the sacred altar, the king raised his hand. Immediately, all soldiers raised their weapons, shouting in unison—"Long live the king!" This voice quickly spread across the entire universe. In this dimension, Maya civilization was already unmatched!

But the Maya civilization now consisted of only three individuals. Now they were ready to achieve the ultimate goal of their ancestors—to become gods, and merge with the universe.

This was a solemn and magnificent moment. Countless robots draped in pure white robes began reciting scriptures, the great history of Maya civilization. And at this moment, all citizens would bear witness together.

The three slowly floated towards the altar, their bodies free from gravitational constraints. Soon, they arrived at the entrance of the altar. Before them burned a fierce white flame, where the future danced. This was the ultimate power of Maya civilization. Only by merging with the universe could they achieve their ultimate destination. The king, queen, and prince quietly glanced at each other, solemnly placing their hands on their heads, and gently removed them.

"At this glorious moment, let us face the world with truth!" said the king's head seriously. After speaking, his hand slowly tore off his hair, peeled off his skin, revealing a semi-circular transparent vessel containing a red brain.

After the king completed this act, the queen and prince also accomplished this return to authenticity. Their bodies respectfully lifted their brains and slowly pushed them forward. Three brains carrying the last imprint of Maya civilization flew towards the center of the flames.

Silence enveloped the world, not a sound remained. As if time and space had stopped. But in the next moment, the towering flames violently compressed, instantly disappearing. Then a beam of white light shot up into the sky, spreading into space. Everything was fused into the light. Breaking through the limits of time and space, everything became light. Following this was a thunderous explosion. The solar system vanished from then on, and the existence of Maya civilization would forever become history. A faint sadness seemed to drift in the universe.

In the early days of Maya civilization, there was a great king named Nietzsche, who said he was the sun… The universe isn't three-dimensional, nor seven-dimensional, but infinitely dimensional, originating from a single point. The derived universes are parallel universes, sharing a common origin but leading to different results. For example, the same Earth from the moment of its birth possessed countless possibilities for development. Each possibility formed a universe. On a certain Earth, dinosaurs went extinct, and a mammalian species called humans ruled the world. But in parallel other universes, dinosaurs might not have gone extinct and continued to survive.

Each possibility could spawn an existence. Parallel universes may be similar or entirely different. Perhaps in some universe spaces, life never existed on Earth, while in others, Earth developed to the ultimate stage of civilization. Similarities also exist, such as humans ruling Earth, but the first emperor unifying China wasn't Ying Zheng, Einstein didn't invent the theory of relativity, and his father did. Even finer similarities could occur.

All of this constitutes parallel universes. Between parallel universes exist cosmic laws that cannot be crossed. Even when Maya civilization developed to such heights, they could not violate these rules. Any object daring to challenge would be destroyed during the crossing. When this conclusion was reached, Maya civilization lost its purpose for survival.

But as the prince said, when technological power reaches its peak, this law isn't entirely unbreakable, just with a probability close to zero. Moreover, even if one managed to cross by chance, whether living or non-living entities would lose over 80% of their functions. According to the mainframe's calculations of Maya civilization, this was utterly meaningless. The unclear status of so-called becoming light led to the annihilation of Maya civilization, akin to the emperor who once wanted to become the sun. Simultaneously, this triggered a stellar explosion, a chain reaction of energy that pushed the entire plane to the brink of destruction. An incalculable energy storm simultaneously affected the channels of parallel space.

A tiny golden dot disappeared on the verge of destruction. When it reappeared, a blue planet lay before it...
