"Cherry" red tears, mother's emaciation, conveying feelings in Liaoyang's black soil. The director of "Cherry", Wang Zhenhong, persistently focuses his lens on this vast expanse of black soil in Liaoyang. In 2007, the TV series "Warm Spring" was produced, and in 2012, "Cherry" was directed by him as a testament to his work. Looking at Wang Zhenhong's creations, there is a clear and defined main creative theme: praising the truth, goodness, and beauty of the world, calling for the return of humanity, and bringing countless tears to the audience. Let's take a look at why Liaoyang's black soil attracts Wang Zhenhong.

Liaoyang, the filming location of "Cherry"

Liaoyang, the filming location of "Cherry", has many famous historical and cultural landscapes and numerous picturesque scenic areas. The historical and cultural landscapes of Liaoyang mainly include murals from tombs of the Han-Wei period, Tang Dynasty Goguryeo Yanzhou City (White Rock City), Eight Treasures Glazed Well, Liao Dynasty White Pagoda, Ming Dynasty Qingfeng Temple, ancient city wall ruins; Qing Dynasty Tokyo City, Tokyo Mausoleum, Cao Zhenyan's famous stele, and the Cao Xueqin Memorial Hall, etc. The natural scenery of Liaoyang includes the picturesque Tang River Tourist Scenic Area, the beautiful Hehu Valley Natural Scenic Area, the Shidong Valley Forest Park with its deep valleys and lush trees, and the Binwo Reservoir Tourist Area with its rippling waters.

Gra(shen)wo Reservoir, the filming location of "Cherry" in Liaoyang

The Gra(shen)wo Reservoir, located in the eastern part of Liaoyang City, is surrounded by mountains in the eastern mountainous area of Liaoyang. It is situated between Benxi and Liaoyang cities along the middle section of the Taizi River main stream, formed by the confluence of three major rivers: Taizi River, Xi River, and Lan River. The Gra(shen)wo Reservoir is one of the large reservoirs in Liaoning Province and also the only reinforced concrete gravity dam in Liaoning Province. Built in 1973, it serves multiple functions including flood control, irrigation, and power generation.

The majestic and spectacular dam of the Gra(shen)wo Reservoir in Liaoyang, with its vast and boundless water surface, is nestled among the surrounding mountains. The undulating hills, lush greenery, and serene waters create a picturesque landscape. The reservoir resembles a precious mirror embedded among thousands of peaks and cliffs. The Gra(shen)wo Reservoir on the Taizi River main stream shines brightly like the Taizi River, which nurtures the culture of Liaodong.

Taizi River Scenic Area, the filming location of "Cherry" in Liaoyang

The Taizi River Scenic Area, located on the east bank of the Taizi River in Liaoyang City, stretches 3,260 meters from the Beishao Gate Bridge in the north to the Man Water Pipe Bridge in the south. To the west, it connects with the urban area, while to the east, it overlooks the Hedong Village. The average width from west to east is 750 meters, covering a total area of 240 hectares. Construction began in 1991 and opened to visitors in 1992. On the basis of preserving the original vegetation, 4,542 trees of 33 varieties and 7,412 flowers of 14 varieties were planted, along with 3 hectares of lawn. A 350 square meter square and 3,400 meters of garden paths were constructed. Inside, there are facilities such as a 40,000 square meter natural bathing area, 60-meter flower trellis, 530-meter fence, and 1,855 square meters of brick pathways. There are also various pavilions, small architectural features, sculptures, and other attractions. Additionally, there are 13 scenic spots such as the Derivation Rainbow Bridge, Dragon King Night Crossing Ruins, and water-based yachts, making it an excellent place for sightseeing, rest, swimming, and cooling off.

White Pagoda Park, the filming location of "Cherry" in Liaoyang

The White Pagoda Park in Liaoyang was built in 1908, covering an area of 8 hectares, located on the north side of Zhonghua Street Section One in front of the station. The park has four tourist areas: ancient relics tours, flower appreciation, children's amusement, and animal viewing. The White Pagoda, built during the Liao Dynasty, is the tallest pagoda in Northeast China, ranking among the six tallest pagodas in China. In 1988, it was designated as a national key cultural relic protection unit by the State Council.

Wazi Valley Ecological Tourism Area, the filming location of "Cherry"

Wazi Valley Village is located at the southwest end of the Gongchangling District, being an administrative village under the Tanghe Town jurisdiction. Covering a total area of 15,000 mu, it includes 12,000 mu of forested land. Situated 5 km from the Xituo Highway, 27 km from Liaoyang, and 38 km from Anshan, the transportation is convenient. Located upstream of the Tanghe Reservoir, it is surrounded by green mountains and winding blue waters, with ten thousand mu of fragrant flowers, fruits, and vegetables. The fields and villagers fishing by the water complement each other beautifully. The dense forests and good vegetation form a beautiful natural environment, offering a tranquil retreat from the bustling city, akin to a secluded paradise within the Gongchangling District.
