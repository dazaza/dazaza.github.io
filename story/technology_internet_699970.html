For job hunting self-introduction, please remember the "3P" principle.

by jintaimqr on 2012-03-09 17:15:59

Interviews [http://www.jt077.cn/index.php?m=job] are a necessary part of job hunting [http://www.jt077.cn/index.php?m=job]. As the saying goes - everything is difficult at the beginning, and many interviewers start the interview with the question: [Please give a brief self-introduction]. In a short interview lasting only about ten minutes, the time given to the candidate for self-introduction is usually around 1-3 minutes. How can the candidate "show" themselves in such a short period? And what issues should be paid attention to?

Question: [All basic information is already written in the resume, so why do we need to repeat it orally?]

Facing the self-introduction, many people may have the above doubts. In fact, through the self-introduction, the interviewer can take the opportunity to evaluate the candidate's language expression ability and adaptability; the candidate can also actively recommend themselves to the interviewer, showcase their confidence, and reveal their talents. These are things that cannot be conveyed by a plain black-and-white resume. In practice, to resolve the above doubts, candidates can work on several aspects:

Avoid [reciting] style self-introductions

The content of the self-introduction can be prepared and practiced beforehand, but when speaking, the candidate should avoid using the written language from the resume and instead use flexible spoken language to reorganize the information, while supplementing some content not reflected in the resume. Avoid introducing yourself in a reciting or reading tone, otherwise, the interviewer may interpret this as artificial or even fake. Additionally, candidates should learn to communicate with the interviewer through eye contact. Wandering eyes may leave an impression of "lack of focus", and always looking down may seem unconfident, so candidates should let the interviewer see more of your eyes. Of course, staring at the interviewer all the time is also unnatural behavior.

Case Study:

Graduate Xiao Zhang was very talkative and had excellent eloquence. For the self-introduction during the interview, he thought it would be no problem, so he didn't prepare. After graduation, Xiao Zhang combined his interests and applied for a planning position at a large online game company. During the self-introduction, he started talking about his experience with online games and continued to discuss the trends in China's online gaming industry. However, due to straying too far off-topic, the interviewer had to bring the topic back several times. Eventually, the self-introduction could only be "cut short", and the interview ended hastily.

Reasonable allocation of self-introduction time

As mentioned earlier, self-introductions generally last only 1-3 minutes, so candidates need to allocate their time reasonably. The conventional arrangement is: the first part introduces personal basic information, the middle part focuses on work experience or social practice experience, and the last part looks forward to one's career aspirations. If the self-introduction is required to be completed within 1 minute, the candidate needs to emphasize the most important point. In practice, some candidates try to reveal all their experiences in a short time, while others finish their self-introduction with just a few words. Both approaches are unwise.
