Guess the gift package: Innovative E-commerce 2.0 model

by ybcz0519 on 2010-10-04 05:05:11

What is Guess Gift Package? "Guess Gift Package," a new term that became popular starting from 2010, refers to "a kind of gift package that requires guessing." The popularity of "Guess Gift Package" originated from a university in Shanghai. Students always liked exchanging gifts during holidays, and within the campus where gift packages were flying around everywhere, it was becoming increasingly difficult to give a gift that would make someone feel surprised. On the eve of Christmas in 09, students were once again troubled by the issue of giving gifts. A student club came up with a way that could save everyone time and effort: they prepared a batch of gift packages that only indicated the price but did not reveal the contents. They guaranteed that the gifts inside the packages would be suitable for each student. The students just had to choose the price range, and they could send out the gift packages. They called these packages "Guess Gift Packages." "Guess Gift Package" no longer required each gift-giver to think about what to give, but instead left it to a team to think on behalf of everyone. Collective wisdom ensured that the creativity and sincerity of the gift packages were equally emphasized, making everything simple with "Guess Gift Package." (Via) The Guess Gift Package website is essentially moving this offline Guess Gift Package service onto the internet, upgrading the pre-determined "Guess Gift Package" to one custom-made according to user needs while still maintaining its mystery. The "Guess Gift Package website" provides services for planning, purchasing, designing, packaging, and delivering the gift packages. Guess Gift Package is equivalent to a new type of e-commerce model that combines gift planning (consultation) + e-commerce. Service Model: After users register, they can customize gifts. First, they need to choose whether to send the gift on a specified date, then select the price of the gift. Currently, prices range from 99 yuan to 5999 yuan, and users can choose a price tier. Next, they need to choose the recipient (age range and relationship), the reason for giving the gift (such as birthdays, housewarmings, etc.), provide a description of the reason, and fill in their own phone number and name. Then, they need to input the recipient's relevant information and can set whether to include a card with the gift (choosing between machine-printed or handwritten). Afterwards, payment can be initiated, currently supporting Alipay, Tenpay, and online banking payments. Once the order is completed, the Guess Gift Package website will have gift planners handle the gift selection, procurement, and delivery. The person receiving the gift can showcase the received gift through the website. Additionally, users can also use this website to request gifts from friends. Guess Gift Package also provides corporate services, offering personalized group-buying of such gifts. Model Analysis: By using gift planning/consultation as the core, it attracts users and builds a bridge between users and gift suppliers, which has a similar effect to Groupon group-buying sites. Compared to regular gift e-commerce websites, Guess Gift Package adds a gift planning step, eliminating many troubles for users when selecting gifts. It actually offers more of a consulting service. In the process of buying gifts, there are two steps: one is the gift consultation service (what to buy), and the other is purchasing the gift (where to buy). Often, users care more about what to buy rather than where to buy. Clearly, Guess Gift Package provides this consulting service, not only solving the problem of what to buy but also adding a lot of suspense to the gift-giving process. This service undoubtedly offers the best way to create differentiation in e-commerce. Of course, some may question whether the gifts selected by the website will definitely satisfy users. I believe this satisfaction is relative and can be improved through telephone communication, meaning resolving issues through online orders and discussing gift concepts (or the recipient's taste, etc.) via phone calls. Registration Invitation: Similar to previously popular group-buying websites, after registration, users can obtain a "trial version Guess Gift Package" through invitations. Business Model: Charge for gift planning fees and collaborate with gift service suppliers to charge per transaction. Watsonxu says: There are currently many e-commerce websites in China, and the development of many e-commerce websites is also doing well. An increasing number of vertical e-commerce websites are emerging. The appearance of Guess Gift Package indicates that besides pursuing verticalization in e-commerce, it is also possible to take a differentiated path by providing more personalized value-added services, namely shopping consultation/planning services. Gift shopping consultation/planning is a demand, and in many fields, there is also a demand for shopping planning and consultation. The previous group-buying trend led a new direction in e-commerce. I believe that this mode of increasing more humanized value-added services on pure e-commerce services will attract more and more people's attention!
