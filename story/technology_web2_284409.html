Cleaning companies have moved from providing comprehensive services a few years ago to offering specialized industry services.

The cleaning industry is developing so rapidly that its prospects seem very bright. However, in mainland China, in cities like Beijing and Qingdao... how many domestic cleaning companies can lead the pack? How many domestic cleaning companies can expand beyond Beijing to other regions or even other countries? Let’s look at multinational corporations: as long as they enter China, their businesses flourish nationwide, and their bidding prices are often several times higher than those of domestic cleaning companies.

Yet, building owners still choose these multinational companies. Is it because these owners favor foreign entities? I’ve discussed and analyzed this with many industry insiders. From the perspective of multinational companies, they possess substantial capital, long-term investment goals, and a few foreign faces. But they lack local advantages! Domestic enterprises don’t lack capital either, and they also have long-term investment goals. So what advantages do these multinational companies have? After years of exploration and analysis, we found that these multinational companies have a set of standardized systems for process management that support their operations, ensuring service quality for clients, thus enabling rapid development.

So, what is the operational management standardization system for cleaning companies in Qingdao? How do we establish a standardized system for operational processes? Below, I will introduce some content related to the standardized system for the operational processes of cleaning companies:

Standardization of operational processes for cleaning companies refers to achieving optimal order within a certain scope for project operations, formulating common and reusable operational procedures for actual or potential problems, which is called standardization. It includes the processes of formulating, issuing, implementing, and reviewing standards.

The value and significance of standardizing operational management for cleaning companies lie in improving internal management and effectively controlling operational costs, enhancing the work efficiency of employees, improving the quality of services provided by cleaning companies, and promoting the rapid development of cleaning companies.

Basic principles for establishing standardized operational processes for cleaning companies usually include unification, simplification, coordination, and optimization principles.

The principle of unification ensures necessary operational procedures, quality assurance, and employee operational efficiency for the operation of cleaning company projects, determining consistency in project management suitable for a specific period and conditions.

The principle of simplification ensures that cleaning company operations economically meet project needs by screening and refining the structure, style, specifications, or other performance aspects of standardization, eliminating unnecessary, low-efficiency, and replaceable links, keeping the overall composition concise and reasonable, and maximizing functional work efficiency.

The principle of coordination ensures that the overall function of the cleaning company's operational standardization system reaches its best, facilitating publication, implementation, and review, and generating practical results. This must be achieved by effectively coordinating relationships between relevant factors inside and outside the system, determining the conditions required for the establishment and implementation of the standardization system to be consistent, adaptable, or balanced.

The principle of optimization involves selecting, designing, or adjusting the constituent elements and relationships of a standard system according to specific goals under certain constraints, achieving the most ideal effect. Such a standardization principle is called the optimization principle.

Execution and review of standardized operational processes: after formulating the standardized system for operational process management, the company should establish a dedicated organization and method to announce it to relevant departments and employees. After implementation, regular reviews should be conducted, and finally, improvements should be made based on the established principles of operational process standardization, followed by re-publication and execution.

This is the basic factor in formulating the standardized system for operational processes of cleaning companies. It is evident that establishing a management system for operational process standardization is imperative. It is also the foundation for the rapid development of domestic cleaning companies.
