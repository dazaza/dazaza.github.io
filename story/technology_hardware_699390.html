The crime of illegal public deposit collection should be abolished

NPC deputy Guo Guangchang suggested

The crime of illegal public deposit collection should be abolished

Shenzhen Evening News reported that Guo Guangchang, a national NPC deputy and chairman of Fosun Group, said that private enterprises played an important role in improving the income level of low- and middle-income groups, especially in expanding the middle-income group. Guo Guangchang said that to promote the development of the private economy, it was suggested that the financial sector should be open to private capital, the crime of illegal public deposit collection should be abolished; more private banks like Minsheng Bank should be cultivated to reduce the financing burden on small and medium-sized enterprises; the loan scale of small loan companies should be expanded, and creditworthy small loan companies should be supported to take the road of banking; direct financing should be liberalized, and equity investment enterprises should be actively encouraged.

Guo Guangchang also suggested reducing corporate taxes, such as abolishing the cultural undertakings construction fee; reducing personal tax burdens, studying individual income tax collection methods based on families, stimulating consumption, and making economic development more vibrant.

Online shopping needs legislation

It is understood that currently more than 30 countries and regions around the world have formulated comprehensive e-commerce laws. Tax increase is faster than Liu Xiang

Tax reduction is slower than snails

When we add taxes, the speed is like Liu Xiang; when we reduce taxes, the speed is like a snail. China's tax revenue has been growing at a speed far higher than GDP growth for many years. Tax revenue grew by 24.8% last year compared to the previous year, while the GDP growth rate was only about 9.2% during the same period. This has far exceeded the normal range.

So far, the number of local governments or departments that have disclosed details of government budgets is still very small. Ordinary taxpayers have no way of knowing how tax money is allocated and where it is used, let alone tax supervision. Meanwhile, what people often see is that the average annual expenditure on official vehicles across the country is hundreds of billions of yuan, official dining, overseas trips, training, private villas for officials, violation construction of grand buildings, face-saving projects, etc., which are banned but continue to occur. This has greatly hurt people's understanding and support for public budgets and the tax system.

National Committee member of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference, audit partner of PricewaterhouseCoopers Zhongtian Accounting Firm Zhang Guojun
