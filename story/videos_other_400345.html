[Online Shopping Guru] Online Shopping Skills + The Losses I've Experienced, Please Avoid Them (Repost)

by anonymous on 2011-04-01 12:22:48

The pitfalls I've fallen into, I hope you can avoid.

After wandering around Taobao for several years, I've unwittingly become a Platinum VIP and also obtained a Yellow Diamond. However, from the registration at the university library, I have been exploring step by step with my passion for online shopping. Therefore, when I now loudly talk to others about my Taobao experience, I also want to share the detours and small losses I've encountered, which might serve as a reminder and be helpful to everyone. That way, I won't have wasted my time typing these words on the screen O(∩_∩)O~

One, Don't blindly follow trends

This is the pain I feel every time I open my wardrobe. But I have never thrown away "her," just to remind myself that suffering the loss of blindly following trends once is enough!!

It was not long after graduation, with some idle money in my card, I started browsing aimlessly on Taobao. I suddenly saw an advertisement on the side of the page for a set with over six thousand transactions. What kind of beautiful clothes could have such high sales volume? Back then, that number was really significant because ladies all know how awkward it is to bump into someone wearing the same outfit. The fact that so many people bought this set must mean something. When I opened the page, wow, more than two hundred yuan for a four-piece set: a wool coat, a floral skirt, a matching scarf, and a black undershirt! The model looked absolutely radiant in them! No, I was moved too. A four-piece set was so cheap, no wonder everyone was rushing to buy it. Buy!

Looking at the reviews, they were all quite good, so I immediately placed an order and started waiting for the clothes. After waiting and waiting, the clothes finally arrived. Excitedly opening the package, my heart sank by more than half. Without even mentioning how synthetic the fabric of the coat was, the skirt was just a tube, and overly long, without any "shape." The undershirt didn't even fit over my head, and I don't have a big head either! Risking tearing the clothes, I squeezed my head through, but it felt like I was wearing a "straitjacket," extremely uncomfortable. As for the scarf, it's not even worth mentioning! Thus, the four-piece set that once made me so excited became a permanent fixture in my wardrobe.

Two, Don't overestimate yourself

I am just over one meter sixty-seven in high heels, and my weight has never exceeded a hundred catties, so I considered myself a slim girl. So when boot-cut pants became all the rage, I also picked a pair that was cheap and good-looking, really cheap, only 29 yuan, a stylish gray color. Seeing others buy size S, I figured my body should be similar. Besides, T-shirts generally come in size S, right? Facts proved that T-shirts and boot-cut pants are incomparable. The pants I received were indeed of good quality as described in the reviews, but the boot-cut pants hugged the hips tightly, making them uncomfortable to wear. Should I return and exchange them? I had already discussed the size with the seller, and choosing the wrong size was my responsibility. With a round-trip shipping fee of 20 yuan, it would significantly increase the value of these pants. So, I decided to let it go! Thus, my wardrobe gained another lesson. Also, girls from the north should note that most clothing factories are in the south, especially certain small brands, which design sizes according to the petite southern girls. Girls with larger frames must carefully measure their dimensions, Guangzhou Vipshop Information Technology Co., Ltd.

Three, Fake Rayli magazine styles, I hate you without compromise

This has actually been repeatedly exposed, and there are plenty of posts denouncing and accusing such items, but I was part of the first batch who fell victim before benefiting from the fake-busting posts. The truth about the black tiered skirt is that it's just a rag, no, not even absorbent as a rag. It's too terrible to behold. Let's not talk about it anymore. Just remember, cheap magazine-style or Rayli-style clothes shouldn't be bought. This "lesson" may be buried deep in the darkest corner of my wardrobe, but whenever I dig it out, I must reflect on my actions and mourn the wasted RMB!

Four, Big stores have deep waters, enter with caution

Many websites or groups recommend excellent Taobao stores. Especially in the earlier days, with fewer advertisements, everyone promoted each other out of enthusiasm and experience. The good stores that emerged back then have mostly grown large. These stores have common features: customer service is friendly and热情towards buyers; pictures are meticulously processed, with plenty of detailed images, such as clothing main labels, wash labels, stitching diagrams, button diagrams, nothing is left out; descriptions are thorough, allowing customers to make informed purchases.

Nowadays, these stores usually have several crowns or even gold crowns. With their credibility, they don't need advertisements to attract customers. They already have loyal repeat customers and a steady stream of new consumers. Such stores also inevitably have sophisticated pages, and even the names of their products differ from the norm, unsearchable elsewhere on Taobao. I think this is the smartest move by merchants—creating unique names makes it hard for customers searching for identical items to find them easily. They assume exclusivity, but that’s not necessarily true. Moreover, while the store provides detailed images, they often pixelate the product logos, partially hiding them. This saves you the trouble of searching for the brand. Thus, their items can be tens or even hundreds of yuan more expensive than others, and you wouldn’t know! Look at the pages and pages of purchase counts; clearly, people don’t know! By revealing this, I want everyone to know that if you take the time to search for items based on basic characteristics, you can save a lot of money. If you don’t believe me, give it a try!

Five, Impulse is the devil

That's right, shopping impulses will make your RMB grow wings and flutter away, leaving not a feather behind!

My biggest hobby during holidays spent at home is browsing Taobao. There's a saying that holds true: the desire to buy arises from browsing. Looking at this and wanting to buy, seeing that and thinking it's not bad, when you encounter love at first sight and happen to have money in your card, your mouse instinctively moves to "Buy Now." Especially with clothes, if you don’t wear them immediately (or the weather isn’t suitable, or there's no occasion, or you lack complementary outfits), after a while, you may not feel the same affection. And after a bit longer, they may end up in the "cold palace." A fleeting passion robs them of their inherent value. Simply put, it's a waste of money. Talking to a friend about buying clothes in physical stores, her experience is to first imagine whether the item can match with something else in your wardrobe. If not, turn around and walk away. I think this applies equally to online stores. Unless you decide to immediately purchase a full set, it's better to think twice.

I have a windbreaker I love dearly, silver-gray with butterflies scattered all over it. Because the weather wasn't suitable, it was shelved, and I still haven't worn it outside. I regret spending the money on it and have decided that if I like something, I'll wait a week. If I still feel the same after reconsideration, I'll go ahead and buy it. If I'm unsure, I can wait a little longer. If the stock is limited, I'll keep checking regularly. Even if I miss out, it just means the karmic connection wasn't strong enough. Remember, Taobao has so many great things waiting for us, right?

Finally, I hope everyone can save money and buy what they truly desire~~~
