What English should children aged 9-10 learn?

by thatseed123 on 2011-04-26 21:03:07

Student Age: 9-10 years old

1. Student Characteristics:

This is the middle stage of primary school learning, where children's knowledge becomes increasingly abundant and they have also mastered more learning methods. They are becoming more inclined towards independent thinking and no longer blindly worship "authority." Before following instructions or taking action, they will ask themselves, "Why should I do this?" This is a great time to cultivate and train their thinking abilities, and both parents and teachers should provide them with more inspiration and guidance, encouraging them to use their brains and think deeply. We should truly regard them as mature and sensible "big kids" rather than continue to handle everything for them, which could hinder their development.

2. Expert Guidance:

(1) Expand Knowledge Base: Learn English while using English to learn other subjects.

English is inherently a tool. The ultimate goal of learning English isn't the language itself. Knowing an additional language is like having another pair of eyes; through it, children can discover more interesting knowledge from different aspects, thereby increasing their fondness for English. Parents and teachers should not consider this as "neglecting one's main task," but instead actively help the child use English as a key to open the door to the vast world.

(2) Two Aspects of Enhancing English Skills:

① Relatively Fluent Communication Ability:

Vocabulary and pronunciation remain important components of English learning, but the focus of parents and teachers shouldn’t be limited to these areas. More attention should be paid to whether the child has started using simple sentence patterns to naturally and fluently express themselves in communication. This is an essential prerequisite and foundation for further English learning.

② Basic Reading Comprehension Ability:

English learning encompasses many different aspects: vocabulary, sentence patterns, grammar, etc. Some people try to increase their vocabulary by looking up dictionaries or memorizing vocabulary books, but the results are not ideal; others read numerous grammar books, familiar with various grammar items and concepts, yet when put into practice, they still make countless mistakes. The reason is only one: they treat language, something derived from real-life situations, as lifeless symbols on paper without truly understanding its application method. Knowledge learned in this way is "dormant" and will eventually be forgotten. However, reading is an excellent way to "awaken" English knowledge. In meaningful and contextualized reading processes, vocabulary and grammar come alive. As reading volume increases, English knowledge naturally grows. Therefore, from the early stages of children’s English learning, there should be a planned cultivation of this ability. Good reading habits and methods will benefit the child for life.

(3) Stick to Your Choice, Ensure Systematic and Complete Knowledge Acquisition

“Hearing that Zhang San’s child at * * School had * * class and it was especially good, already passing the * * exam! Should my child give it a try too?”

“Heard that Li Si’s child studied * * textbook and his grades improved instantly. Should I stop my child’s current lessons and switch to that textbook?”

With so many types of textbooks and courses available, can you maintain your position and resist temptation?

Currently, the level of English teaching in China is improving day by day, and there are increasingly more kinds of textbooks. Whether it’s courses or textbooks, there are quite a few good ones worth choosing. However, any scientific textbook inherently has a complete knowledge system and a systematic grading structure. If chapters are taken out of context or changed midway, there will inevitably be fragmentation and loss of the knowledge system. Any responsible and effective course training system should follow the basic principles of English learning and not take exams as the sole purpose, blindly following trends or swallowing things whole without digestion. As long as you choose a scientifically sound textbook teaching system and help your child persist in studying and work hard, significant achievements will definitely be made. Frequent changes in textbooks and jumping between different courses often directly lead to poor learning outcomes and confusion about subsequent learning directions.

[Expert Tips]

If parents want their students to learn English well, they must create a good family language environment for them. For example, frequently listening to English recordings together, guiding students to showcase the English knowledge they’ve learned, and trying to communicate with them in English whenever possible. Students have strong language imitation abilities, and if they are often placed in an English-speaking environment, their English learning will yield twice the result with half the effort. If parents have a certain level of English proficiency, please set aside some time each day to chat with the student in English. If parents lack such conditions, they can also take advantage of family gatherings or even friend visits to encourage the student to boldly engage in English communication activities.
