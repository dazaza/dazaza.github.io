How to Choose Breast Implants

by 865512 on 2008-12-19 10:40:08

Modern silicone gel breast implants, which have been in use since the 1960s, have been highly satisfactory to plastic and cosmetic surgeons. The Medical Plastic Surgery and Beauty Center of Guangzhou Armed Police Hospital uses implants produced with the latest technology from the United States and the United Kingdom, as well as new technology developed through China-Korea cooperation. These implants are made from a soft, jelly-like medical-grade silicone gel that is high-density, highly tear-resistant, and of high strength. They possess an excellent anti-leakage isolation layer function. The bonding method uses a secondary vulcanization process, ensuring the product fully complies with standards.

### I. Surgical Indications:

- Poor breast development or atrophy of the mammary glands

- Asymmetry of both breasts

- Mild breast ptosis

### II. Preoperative Preparation:

1. Routine physical examination to check for any abnormal masses within the breast.

2. Routine preoperative photography (frontal view of both breasts, oblique lateral view).

3. Selection of implant size:

- Large-sized implants: 220–300 mL, suitable for individuals around 170 cm tall.

- Medium-sized implants: 180–200 mL, suitable for individuals around 165 cm tall.

- Small-sized implants: 150–180 mL, suitable for individuals around 160 cm tall.

### III. Surgical Method Options:

1. **Axillary Fold Incision:** Breast augmentation via a small incision in the armpit has been practiced domestically for nearly 20 years. Due to its hidden incision, it provides an aesthetically pleasing appearance and natural feel, making it popular among beautification seekers. Although the incision is farther from the implant insertion site, using the axillary fold incision results in an aesthetically pleasing shape and natural feel post-implantation. Both parties are satisfied, and there have been no instances of implant displacement or breast hardening. Completing surgery on both sides takes only about 30–45 minutes.

2. **Inframammary Fold Incision:** The inner side of the incision lies along the vertical line of the nipple. This incision is closer, involves less dissection, and offers better exposure, making surgical operations convenient. However, the incision position is less concealed, and postoperative scars are not very noticeable.

3. **Areolar Incision:** Located at the junction between the lower edge of the areola and the skin. The incision is situated between the dark brown areola and normal skin, disguised by nodular sebaceous glands in the areola. Therefore, post-surgical scars are not very noticeable. However, implanting larger breast implants is more difficult, and there is a higher risk of damaging milk ducts.

4. **Placement Position of Breast Implants:**

A. **Behind the Breast Gland Gap:** Located in the loose connective tissue gap between the deep layer of the chest line fascia surrounding the base of the breast and the superficial chest fascia of the pectoralis major muscle. Initially, breast implants were placed in this gap. The surgery is simple, causes minimal damage, and the breast's position, external morphology, and tactile sensation are all very natural. The aesthetic appearance of the breast is ideal. However, the incidence of fibrous contraction is relatively high.

B. **Behind the Pectoralis Major Muscle Gap:** Located between the pectoralis major and minor muscles. The muscle cavity formed by these two muscles serves as a good bed for the breast implant. Placing the implant in this gap can reduce the chance of fibrous contraction.

5. **Recovery Time:** Recovery time is brief; post-surgery hospitalization may not be necessary. Normally, one can resume daily activities the next day. The doctor will guide you to avoid heavy pressure and activities that might increase pulse rate and blood pressure for the first 2–4 weeks.

### IV. Standards for Beautiful Breasts:

From an aesthetic perspective:

1. Full, symmetrical, fine skin, supple and elastic.

2. Erect shape, semi-spherical.

3. Breast size within the average range, with an average breast height of 3.5 cm, an average radius of 18.2 cm, and an average breast volume of 310 ml.

4. Breasts should be located between the 2nd and 6th ribs on the chest. The nipples should protrude slightly outward and be positioned at the level of the 4th–5th ribs.
