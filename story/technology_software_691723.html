Best program for humanities majors to study in Quebec, Canada?

by dingyi947279 on 2012-03-05 17:25:40

There are many liberal arts majors in China who, if they choose to study abroad, can opt for the University of Quebec in Canada, which offers affordable tuition and high-quality education. Studying at the University of Quebec is cost-effective, requiring only 70,000-80,000 RMB annually. It helps students break through their limits with bilingual English and French instruction, enhancing future employability. Some programs offer paid internships, providing good job security. Many programs have government scholarships to alleviate financial burdens. The immigration policy is relaxed and comprehensive, making it easy to change status after completing studies. So what preparations should students make before studying in Quebec?

Canadian universities generally start in September each year, though some also accept students in January and May. Considering university admissions policies, program selection, and new student adaptation, September is the best time to start. To take advantage of this golden opportunity, both students and parents need to prepare well in advance.

Firstly, students planning to study in Quebec should be reminded that attending university in Quebec will not be easier than in China. It's essential to lay a solid foundation before enrolling in a renowned university or pursuing a quality major. Laying a foundation means achieving good results in Chinese high school courses. We don't require students to strive for top exam scores, but they should at least demonstrate they are not falling behind. A high school score of 70-80% is the basic preparation students should aim for. With the relaxation of the overseas education market, it's no longer difficult for students in grades 10 and 11 to study in Quebec. Additionally, studying high school or pre-university in Quebec does not require TOEFL or IELTS scores, making the process very convenient. Here, we sincerely recommend that students applying to schools in Quebec should prepare fully in advance and start early on the application and visa material preparation.

Secondly, parents planning to send their children to study in Canada should prepare financially in advance. Many people mistakenly believe that having money alone is enough for studying abroad, but this concept is not entirely correct. To study at the University of Quebec, one must not only prove financial capability but also clearly explain the deposit history, demonstrating sufficient economic strength to cover all expenses during their child's studies in Canada. It’s advisable to seek advice from friends who already have children studying abroad or professional overseas education agencies.

Lastly, students and parents should pay attention to the application timeline. Those hoping to directly enter undergraduate programs or pursue language/preparatory courses must adhere to application deadlines to secure admission into an ideal university either this September or next September.
