Post-80s Hangzhou lady starts an online store with over 200,000 yuan in cash, should the business focus on wholesale or retail, and how to build a team?

by tonzhuanyin0y on 2012-03-01 01:34:18

I am a post-80s girl, and have successively worked as cashier in an advertising company, advertisement salesperson, real estate salesgirl. Later, I started my own business. Speaking of entrepreneurship, it was actually just opening a small children's clothing store. I registered on Taobao in 2010 and seriously tinkered with it for a while. Tasks such as photographing, processing, uploading pictures, and web page production were all done by me slowly figuring them out, but the results were minimal. Later, because the physical store was very busy during the peak season, I was responsible for purchasing goods and sales, from going to the market to buy goods at six or seven in the morning until around ten o'clock at night, every day I was running around the small shop, so I no longer took care of Taobao.

It could be said that my physical store was relatively successful. A small shop of more than six square meters opened in July, which is the off-season. During the off-season, it sold two or three hundred yuan worth of goods per day. As we entered autumn and winter, the turnover reached over 500,000 yuan in four months. I was quite satisfied personally.

But I still kept thinking about Taobao, thinking about Alibaba. E-commerce is an inevitable development trend.

However, that previous experience on Taobao made me realize that going solo wouldn't make much of a difference. One thing that often happened back then was: a piece of clothing had just been uploaded, and it was sold out in the physical store. After several times, I became very discouraged. Moreover, tasks like photographing and uploading pictures were also very time-consuming and energy-draining. Besides, I'm not professional at these things. Furthermore, I think even if I were professional, if I wanted to do well on Taobao, one person would still not be enough.

My sourcing channels are varied: some from Ali, some from Guangzhou, some from the local market, and some from Taobao. Once, I purchased more than 300 puffy skirts from a children's clothing store on Taobao over the course of two months. That children's clothing store was only a small retail seller with two diamonds, but since my local market didn't have this product, I found her store. Several experiences of online sourcing gave me the idea of doing wholesale on Ali or Taobao. However, wholesale requires a larger investment, and I've always been unsure how much capital genuine online wholesalers invest.

What I want to say is that I am not an impetuous girl, looking at this mountain and then that mountain. Years of work experience have taught me that success must be gradual, and one must not rush into action upon seeing others succeed. But I wonder if it's because I consider too much, so I haven't taken a step yet.

I would like to ask Mr. Jack Ma and the experts on the forum a few questions:

1. If Taobao is operated in a team-based way, at my current primary stage, how many people should I hire? What are the job types of these people? How much is the approximate initial investment?

2. Should a small model be hired to take photos for the online store, or should the items be photographed flatly? Because I see that many clothing stores on Taobao don't use models to take photos, but the entire store looks beautiful, and their business is also very good;

3. To do wholesale on Ali, how much capital is roughly required?

4. My available funds are about twenty thousand US dollars, and I am familiar with the children's clothing markets in Guangzhou and Hangzhou, but not familiar with factories. In my current situation, should I focus mainly on wholesale or retail?

I sincerely hope to receive replies from everyone. I am really confused right now. If there is an opportunity, I mean if there is, having an experienced old seller guide me would be such a lucky thing.
