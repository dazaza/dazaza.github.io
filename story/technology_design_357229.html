In less than a month on Taobao, I'm about to reach 3 diamonds!

by buocrf937 on 2010-05-31 22:59:16

My small store has been open for less than a month, and I've already gained 3 diamonds in positive feedback. It feels so good! Although I haven't made much money yet, every day I give out smiles and earn trust. But seeing more and more new and returning customers every day makes my heart happy. In just this short month, I've accumulated a lot of experience and lessons. Here are some tips for new sellers:

1. When doing virtual goods sales, you must be patient. First, be patient with yourself and your store because at the beginning, you won't make money. Be prepared to lose money while building up your reputation. Second, be patient with your customers. I'm sure everyone has encountered very inexperienced customers who ask all sorts of questions and don't know much but still want to try buying something. We must be patient and try our best to capture these customers, making a good impression on them even if they have limited online shopping experience.

2. When doing virtual goods sales, you must be careful. For example, when recharging someone's phone, you must ensure the accuracy of the phone number and the amount. Recharging too much or too little is not acceptable. Virtual goods sales have very low profit margins, so even a small mistake can be disastrous.

3. When doing virtual goods sales, you must be professional. My store uses an automatic recharge platform and several card platforms. Nowadays, most virtual goods sellers use automatic recharge software, and manual submissions are rare except for some automatically shipped card codes. To be professional, it’s better to use an automatic recharge platform. This will greatly relieve your pressure and reduce workload, from the original copy-paste recharges to now having computers fully automate order grabbing, shipping, recharging, and evaluation. Here, I want to mention Jieyitong Platform. I’m not saying this platform is particularly good; all platform agency prices are similar, and purchase prices are also similar. Relatively speaking, the stability and feature recharges (slow recharges) of Jieyitong are relatively good. Of course, there are also bad points, like sometimes contacting customer service for checking orders takes a long time to get a response.

4. When doing virtual goods sales, you must have a process. I used the English word "process," which you can understand as a flow or procedure. I think that doing anything should have a plan, follow certain rules, and have a process to follow. You can’t just rush into things without thinking, or it will surely end up in chaos. Doing virtual recharges can also refer to this. For example, if you receive a slow recharge order and have already submitted it to the platform for recharging, but suddenly the customer says the slow recharge is too slow and doesn't want it anymore and wants a fast recharge instead, we are helpless in such situations. We can only explain as much as possible. Customers have their own thoughts, but we also have our principles. Once submitted to the platform for recharging, it cannot be canceled, whether successful or failed, we must wait for the system to return the status. Also, some slow recharges may take up to 48 hours to return the status. It's not something that can be returned immediately just because the customer wants to. Some customers do not understand, and some complaints, disputes, and negative reviews are unavoidable. Later, we came up with a good method. The method is simple: for those who choose slow recharges or other special recharges, we must confirm with them first before recharging. Although this wastes time, it makes the customer feel your service is excellent and avoids unnecessary trouble.

That's all for now. I'll share more later.
