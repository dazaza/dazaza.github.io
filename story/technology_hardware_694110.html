Hehehei down jacket: Fishermen fish out missile boosters while fishing (Photo)

by ulfs3491 on 2012-03-06 22:12:27

Everyone was talking, some said it was from a wind turbine, some said it was an entire aircraft, some said it was a rocket component, and some even said it was from a flying saucer. The police immediately went forward to check, and found that the unidentified object had been salvaged from the nearby sea area by fishermen from Dongcun Village on Daqin Island. It was cylindrical in shape, about 4 meters long, with a diameter of about 2 meters, hollow inside, with cable lines at the top, and the color of the object was light blue. There were words like "support position" and "-2" printed on it.

Since they could not determine whether the object was harmful or not, in order to ensure the safety of the people, the police immediately dispersed and persuaded the surrounding residents to leave, and reported the situation to the relevant departments. After contacting the island's garrison army, it was preliminarily identified as a missile launch booster. Currently, the station has properly stored it, waiting for further identification and handling by the relevant departments.

At the beginning of January, when the officers of the Qindao Border Defense Police Station in Changdao County were patrolling to the dock of Dongcun Village on Daqin Island, they found many people watching a large cylindrical object. A fisherman said that this cylindrical object was salvaged by a fisherman who was fishing at sea. As no one on the boat recognized this thing, they brought it ashore. As soon as it landed, the surrounding residents came over to watch.

Our hotline 6610123 news (correspondent Yang Peijun, reporter Yuan Feifei) Originally, it was supposed to be fishing, but after casting the net, no fish was caught, instead, a cylindrical unknown object was netted. After confirmation by experts contacted by the Changdao police, the object turned out to be a missile booster. Currently, the booster has been properly stored by the relevant department.
