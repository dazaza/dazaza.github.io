Untitled

by anonymous on 2011-04-01 11:32:45

I helplessly watched my beloved C510 fall into the bottomless black hole of the women's toilet on the 12th floor of Development Building in Guangzhou VIPshop Information Technology Co., Ltd., and I was a little stunned for a moment! I wanted to retrieve it, at least to take one last look at it, but how could I possibly do that? It was simply impossible. Foolishly standing outside the restroom, I looked at a girl washing her hands outside with a very pitiful expression, asking her what I should do, and her answer was: this toilet has already "eaten" many phones. She asked me what kind of phone it was, but I didn't respond. To her, she only wanted to know the value of the phone, but for me who had lost my beloved phone, all I felt at this moment was sorrow and desolation!

Packing away the earbuds that I kept in my bag every day, and unplugging the charger that I almost used daily from the socket, it's time to get rid of this habit that has lasted for more than two years! But, I am very unaccustomed to being without them, I am very unaccustomed! The phone wasn't particularly valuable, but it was the most useful and suitable phone I've ever used! It carried so many memories, it stored all my numbers for over two years, carefully selected pictures, classic videos I had filmed, and now they're all gone! The phone's most distinctive feature was its camera function, and I took a lot of photos with it. Now, opening my computer and looking at the photos in each folder, seeing how it recorded my hardships and sweetness, I feel an inexplicable sadness! It's like losing a good friend! Although I had this feeling half a year ago when it went to the "hospital", at that time I told my friends that if it couldn't be used anymore, I would buy one exactly like it! Unfortunately, this model is not available domestically, only in Hong Kong! Yan reminded me not to buy a phone online, and thinking about it, that's true. Well, well, let's choose a Nokia with a good cost-performance ratio then.

Friends all say that the old must go for the new to come, but I'm someone who easily develops feelings for things, whether they are people or objects, as long as they have accompanied me, as long as we understand each other, as long as the time is long enough, the farewell moment becomes a period of time, and I must spend time healing. I like to anthropomorphize things: actually, it did give me advance warning that it was going to leave. A few days ago, I said to someone that I seemed to rarely use it to take photos recently, maybe it was my fault, during that period I was too busy, I neglected it, so it thought I didn't value it anymore, and chose to leave. Hmm, leaving isn't necessarily bad, it seems like it should be a full stop in a small phase of my life. All the numbers are gone, some can be easily retrieved, some might never be retrievable again - well, this is also a re-screening of my contact list, choosing to keep those who should stay, and letting go of those who should leave! Can we understand it this way: everything has a cycle of coming and going, people are like this, things are also like this? Without it, life cannot continue normally, so soon another white phone replaced it (of course, I always believe that some things are irreplaceable). Alright, I don't need to be attached anymore, even if I am, it has already said goodbye to me. Goodbye C510, thank you for accompanying me for more than two years, and leaving me with so many memories... Your owner's normal life continues.
