Application examples of various types of crushing stations

by hx515317 on 2012-02-13 15:48:22

Applications of Mobile Crushing Stations

(1) Yangjiabu Limestone Mine, operated by Zhejiang Tarmac (Hunan) Mining Co., Ltd., primarily supplies high-quality limestone aggregates for asphalt operations in East China with an annual mining capacity of 3 million tons. The mine utilizes the LT140 mobile crushing station equipped with a C140 jaw crusher featuring a feed opening of 1400mm x 1070mm.

(2) China Water Resources and Hydropower Construction Group uses the LT1415 mobile crushing station, which houses an NP1415 impact crusher with a feed opening size of 1540mm x 1320mm. Additionally, the company pairs the LT1415 mobile crushing station with the ST358 mobile screening station for joint operations. For the Longtan Hydropower Station, one of China's large hydropower projects, Metso mineral machinery equipment was adopted in 2003: two sets of LT140E primary mobile crushing stations and two sets of LL12 mobile conveying systems. Furthermore, four NP1520 impact crushers and two NP1315 impact crushers were used for medium and fine crushing stages. This project marked the first use of self-propelled mobile crushing stations in China's hydropower engineering.

(3) MaeMoh Open-pit Mine in Thailand is the largest lignite open-pit mine in the country with a production capacity of 5000t/h. The mine features four double-roll crushers with roller diameters of 2m and roller lengths of 215m, each having a total installed power of 2x450kW. The crusher feeding bin has a volume of 300m³ and is connected to a plate conveyor that is 1215m long and 214m wide, driven by a motor with a power of 280kW. Crushed material is transported within the mine via a belt conveyor that is 20m long and 2m wide. The crusher can reduce large blocks measuring 115m x 112m x 112m down to 0.35cm. The crushed material is then conveyed through continuously operating belts. The mobile crusher is equipped with tracks for easy movement along each open-pit working line. Currently, seven mobile crushers from Krupp are in operation at this mine.

Application of Semi-mobile Crushing Stations

(1) Qidashan Iron Mine of Ansteel commenced operations of its semi-mobile crushing station in October 1997 with a working height of 13m. It is used to crush and transport ore to the TunjunTai cylindrical ore bin. In November 2002, when the quarry descended to -45m, relocation began on November 28th, taking 21 days to travel 115km through three bends using TR850 type tracked haulers capable of climbing slopes up to 12% with a minimum turning radius of 18m. The heaviest relocated component weighed 800t with a height of 24m. In March 2006, the second relocation took place, followed by the third relocation in February 2008, lasting 8 hours with a vertical drop of 42m over a distance of 800m. The semi-mobile crushing station includes a heavy-duty plate feeder with a receiving hopper, a crushing unit including a control tower, and discharge belt components. The crusher is a hydraulic cone crusher model 60x89 produced by Metso with a crushing capacity of 7118t/h. The control tower is 21m tall.

(2) Shougang Shui Chang Iron Mine's semi-continuous mining process consists of three belt transportation systems. One is the ore transportation system: truck → semi-mobile crushing station → belt conveyor → beneficiation crushing process. The other two are rock transportation systems, namely the western belt dumping system and the eastern belt dumping system. The western semi-mobile crushing station is located at the 117m level on the western edge of the quarry and officially entered production in May 1998. It consists of one 60x89 type gyratory crushing station and three fixed belt conveyors, as well as one relocatable belt conveyor. The designed annual production capacity reaches 16 million tons, with an actual maximum capacity of 23 million tons. The eastern semi-mobile crushing station is located at the -20m level on the eastern side of the quarry and went into operation in March 2006. It comprises a ThyssenKrupp KB63-75 type hydraulic gyratory semi-mobile crushing station, four fixed belt conveyors, one relocatable belt conveyor, and a spoil plow produced by Voestalpine. The designed annual production capacity is 20 million tons.

The ore semi-mobile crushing station consists of one ThyssenKrupp KB63-75 type hydraulic gyratory semi-mobile crushing station, two fixed belt conveyors, and an electrical control system. It was built and commissioned in August 2006 with a design capacity of 3000t/h. The tipping platform is located at the -20m level.

(3) The planned Yujia Village Iron Mine of Tai Steel will adopt a semi-mobile crushing station equipped with a 63x89 type gyratory crusher with a throughput of 4444t/h. It consists of two modules, featuring a feeding device where trucks unload directly into the crusher from above. Additionally, two semi-mobile rock crushers with belt disposal systems are arranged on the upper and lower benches of the quarry.

(4) Baiyinhua No. 2 Open-pit Mine. In May 2009, Hongxing Machinery's new semi-mobile crushing station was successfully installed and commissioned at Baiyinhua No. 2 Open-pit Mine of Zhongdian Investment Mengdong Energy Group. The equipment employs Hongxing Machinery's CR600 type double-toothed roll screening crusher with a crushing capacity of 2200t/h. The open-pit mine adopts a semi-continuous production process with a designed annual output of 15 million tons.

Application of Semi-fixed Crushing Stations

(1) E'kou Iron Mine aims to maximize the utilization of mineral resources by temporarily stockpiling ore with high dilution rates and difficult grinding and beneficiation processes exposed during the stripping of the upper part of the northwest area of the quarry at the 1852 level of the southwest area quarry. In the second quarter of 2009, a semi-fixed crushing station was constructed at the 1900 level of the Shirengu dump site to crush and dry-select the stored ore. The semi-fixed crushing station consists of three parts: a steel-concrete structure ore bin (including retaining walls and belt feeders), a 750x1060 type jaw crusher for breaking large lumps, and a magnetic drum dry selection system. The jaw crusher is mounted on a concrete platform with a designed capacity of 150t/h. The reason E'kou Iron Mine chose a semi-fixed crushing station is due to the high dilution rate of the ore at this location, reaching 60%, resulting in a significant amount of waste rock. To minimize the haulage distance of waste rock, the crushing station was set up in the existing waste rock dump site without a solid foundation, necessitating the construction of a reinforced concrete foundation. Issues with this system include high initial investment, long construction period, and non-reusable foundation structures.

(2) Dagushan Iron Mine of Ansteel, the ore transportation system consists of truck-shovel train inverted loading railway transport ore semi-fixed crushing station belt transport system Dagushan beneficiation plant, with a designed capacity of 6 million t/a and an actual production capacity of 4.5 million t/a. The rock transportation system currently uses a truck-rock semi-fixed crushing station belt conveyor transport system spoil plow, with a designed capacity of 13 million t/a. The semi-fixed crushing station consists of feed equipment for the crusher and belt conveyor, with a buffer hopper between them. Both occupy a height of 30m. The crusher is installed on a concrete foundation. When moving the unit to a new position, it needs to be disassembled, transported by separate vehicles for each independent component, and reassembled at the new location.

Application of Fixed Crushing Stations

(1) The crushing system of E'kou Iron Mine follows a three-stage closed-circuit process. The coarse crushing uses a PX1200/180 gyratory crusher located at a fixed position at the 1653m elevation outside the quarry. It is considered a permanent crushing station in the design, hence its foundation is a robust reinforced concrete structure. Every year during system maintenance, worn components such as upper and lower rings, mantle, and liners are replaced. It has been in service for 33 years and is expected to serve another 30 years.

(2) Nanfen Open-pit Iron Mine of Benxi Steel extracts ore blocks ranging from 1200mm to 0mm, which are hauled by 80t electric locomotives via the 120t track set up by the company’s metering and control department into the Nanfen beneficiation plant for coarse crushing, directly dumped into the PX1400/170 gyratory crusher. The crusher product size ranges from 320mm to 0mm, entering the intermediate crushing ore bin, then transported via a heavy-duty plate feeder and belt conveyor to the 2100 standard type spring cone crusher. After crushing, the product size ranges from 60mm to 0mm, entering the fine crushing ore bin, then screened by a 1500mm x 4000mm self-centering vibrating screen for pre-screening. The undersize product size ranges from 12mm to 0mm, entering the grinding and beneficiation workshop, while the oversize product enters the 1650mm short-head type spring cone crusher for further crushing. The above crushing system represents a typical fixed crushing station configuration.

Through research and literature review, the following classification points for crushing stations are summarized:

(1) Mobile crushing stations are self-driven, with typical walking methods including wheeled, tracked, and stepping types. In terms of layout position, they are generally located at the quarry working face and need to flexibly move with the advancement of the mining work face. Some materials classify mobile crushing stations as either self-propelled or relocatable. The author considers self-propelled to be true mobile crushing stations, while relocatable ones should be categorized as semi-mobile or semi-fixed depending on the situation.

(2) Semi-mobile crushing stations do not have their own walking function and require specialized tools for relocation either as a whole or in parts without needing to connect to the ground. Sled-type crushing stations also fall into this category. In terms of layout position, they need to be arranged on the working bench or non-working bench, migrating to a suitable lower position as the bench descends and the truck haulage distance increases.

(3) Semi-fixed crushing stations have noticeable connections to the ground, requiring crushers to be installed on effective concrete foundations or steel structures. In terms of layout location, they need to be placed on fixed benches. However, due to terrain and geological conditions, some examples require constructing relatively sturdy foundations on the working bench to place the crusher, which is also classified as a semi-fixed crushing station.

(4) Fixed crushing stations are the least controversial in classification, with robust foundations, used for extended periods outside the quarry, with relatively fixed haulage distances, making adjustments unsuitable.
