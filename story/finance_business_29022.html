"Fuyanjie" Becomes the First Unregistered Well-Known Trademark Recognized by Judiciary

Event: On March 9, 2006, the Intermediate People's Court of Yichun City, Jiangxi Province, while hearing a civil case where Jiangxi Kangmei Pharmaceutical and Health Products Co., Ltd., a subsidiary under Jiangxi Renhe Group, sued Jienuo Pharmaceutical Factory of Xianju County, Zhejiang Province, for infringing on the "Fuyanjie" trademark rights and the unique packaging and decoration of well-known products, made a final ruling based on relevant laws and combined with substantial evidence provided by the plaintiff. The court determined that the "Fuyanjie" trademark was a well-known Chinese trademark and offered legal protection accordingly. As such, "Fuyanjie" became the first case since the amendment of China's Trademark Law and the implementation of the dual-track administrative and judicial recognition system for well-known trademarks, where an unregistered trademark was judicially recognized as a well-known Chinese trademark.

Commentary: In regard to the first judicial recognition of an unregistered trademark as a well-known trademark in China, many people have long believed that Mengniu's "Sour Milk" trademark was the first unregistered well-known trademark recognized by the Chinese judiciary, including many media outlets which reported it as such. However, in fact, "Fuyanjie" is the first case of an unregistered well-known trademark being judicially recognized in China. From the judgment documents of the two cases concerning unregistered well-known trademarks, "Fuyanjie" was recognized on March 9, 2006, and thereafter, the defendant did not file an appeal within the statutory time limit, causing the judgment to take effect. On the other hand, "Sour Milk" was first recognized as a well-known Chinese trademark on April 13, 2006, but the defendant appealed to the Higher People's Court of Inner Mongolia within the statutory period. Subsequently, dairy giants such as Yili, Sanlu, and Bright also jointly raised objections to the Higher People's Court of Inner Mongolia. On November 30, 2006, the Higher People's Court of Inner Mongolia made a final ruling, dismissing the appeal and upholding the original verdict. This means that the effective time of the judicial recognition of "Sour Milk" as a well-known trademark was November 2006, eight months later than "Fuyanjie". Therefore, "Fuyanjie" is the first unregistered well-known trademark judicially recognized after the promulgation of the new Trademark Law in China, which holds significant importance in the history of well-known trademark protection in China.
