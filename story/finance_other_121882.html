Development and Future of China's Conference Hotels

by longersing on 2009-01-30 12:33:42

Several Suggestions for Conference Hotels

Conference hotels play an important role in the development of international conferences in China, and to date remain the main venue for hosting such events. However, whether in terms of hardware配套设施 or software management, China's conference hotels still have a gap compared to more mature countries internationally. Continuous improvements are necessary to adapt to the needs of international conferences.

1. Conference Hotels Are the Main Venues for Hosting Conferences

Conference hotels primarily refer to those capable of independently hosting conferences. Some industry professionals even believe that direct revenue from hosting conferences should account for at least half of the main operating income of conference hotels. In the 30-year history of international conferences in China, conference hotels have played a significant role. The first conference center in China — Beijing International Conference Center — only started operations in October 1990 after the Asian Games. Prior to this, all international conferences were held in conference hotels. Even today, despite many cities in China having built international conference centers, the status of conference hotels in the development of the conference industry has not weakened but rather strengthened. According to ICCA’s survey report on the selection of conference venues, 40.9% of organizers choose hotels, which increased by 10.5% over 10 years; 36.9% choose conference centers, which dropped by 4.8% over the same period. This indicates that hotels hold a certain advantage in the number of conferences they host, and will continue to play an important role in the development of international conferences.

2. Conference Hotels Should Focus on Domestic Conferences

According to UIA statistics, there are currently over 50,000 international organizations worldwide. These organizations have very high requirements for the choice of host country, city, and hotel for their conferences, with conditions as strict as those for sports events. Annual meetings of these international organizations typically occur every 3 to 5 years, similar to the Olympics, rotating between Europe, Asia, and America. It often takes decades for each continent to host one edition, and even within Asia, countries like Japan, South Korea, and Singapore have competitive strengths. Therefore, the number of international conferences held in China each year is not very high. Furthermore, the successful international conferences hosted in China are mainly concentrated in cities like Beijing and Shanghai, as well as some key tourist cities such as Guilin. The development of conferences and the extension of the hotel industry chain in Guilin have been driven by its tourism. Thus, other regions no longer possess the market advantages for developing international conferences.

3. Hardware Facilities Must Be Adequate for Hosting International Conferences

Most conference hotels do not neglect the construction of the main conference hall, decorated lavishly with complete equipment. Many hotels are also equipped with multi-channel simultaneous interpretation systems to meet the needs of international conferences. Although these are essential hardware conditions for hosting international conferences, they are far from sufficient. Sometimes, the inability to select a particular hotel for an international conference is due to inadequate supporting hardware facilities.

(1) Provision of a Certain Number of Breakout Rooms

The trend for international conferences, especially scientific ones, is that each participant is not only a listener but also a speaker, allowing better face-to-face exchange. Currently, some large international conferences in Europe and America have even eliminated plenary sessions, opting instead for parallel breakout sessions from start to finish to ensure every participant gets a chance to speak. However, many conference hotels in China lack dedicated breakout rooms and use small restaurants instead, significantly reducing the quality of the exchange environment.

(2) Lack of Sufficient Registration Area

Registration is the first activity attendees encounter during a conference, and their primary requirement is speed. Therefore, the registration area must be spacious enough, with clear signage and provision of telephones and internet cables. Many so-called conference hotels lack ideal registration areas, causing difficulties for conference organizers. For example, during an international conference organized in a famous conference hotel in Beijing, the organizers had to change the registration location three times in three days due to the lack of a suitable site.

(3) Poster Boards Do Not Meet International Standards

International conferences not only arrange oral presentations but also extensively use poster presentations for exchange. Hotels need to consider this function, both in terms of space and poster boards. Internationally, poster boards are usually horizontal, allowing representatives to reuse the same posters when traveling to different countries. However, China traditionally uses vertical display boards, creating much inconvenience for participants.

(4) Expensive E-Commerce Prices

E-commerce services for international conferences have become a popular trend. In the U.S., even mid-to-low-end hotels offer free wireless internet in the lobby. Currently, China lags behind in this aspect, not only lacking free internet access but also charging exorbitant fees. For instance, renting a 1-megabit internet line at the Beijing International Conference Center costs at least 5,000 yuan per day. Many hotels also have extremely slow internet speeds in their business centers and guest rooms, sometimes even slower than wireless connections.

(5) Exhibition Space and Other Public Areas

ICCA reports indicate a trend in the new century where more and more international conferences include exhibitions to enhance exchange effects. Hotels need to consider reserving exhibition space. Despite having good meeting facilities, Friendship Hotel and Kunlun Hotel in Beijing cannot host many conferences due to the lack of exhibition space. Additionally, tea breaks, information desks, and storage for personal belongings require public areas.

(6) No Need for Specialized Meeting Equipment

Equipment costs account for a very small proportion of international conference expenses, and the equipment used is relatively simple. However, due to rapid technological updates (e.g., laptops upgrading from "Pentium III" to "Pentium VI" in just a few years), if hotels provide equipment, the cost of updating would be substantial, requiring significant manpower for on-site management. Providing meeting equipment and services often results in losses. A few years ago, a conference hotel in Beijing banned outside equipment to increase revenue, exhausting its engineering department without providing professional services, leading to customer dissatisfaction and eventually abandoning the practice.

4. Emphasis on Software Construction for Hosting International Conferences

Compared to hardware facilities, software construction for conference hotels is more complex and challenging because it involves organizational structure.

(1) Proper Setup of Conference Reception Institutions

Currently, most hotels' sales departments mainly provide accommodation services, symbolically helping coordinate work with other departments within the hotel, but specific details still need to be negotiated with relevant departments. Some hotels have multiple external sales departments, such as corporate client service departments, sales departments, travel departments, and reservation departments, with numerous titles. Since these departments can all receive bookings externally, they often quote different room rates for the same international conference, causing confusion for organizers and even negative international reactions. The form of the conference reception institution in conference hotels also affects overall sales. When rewarding employees based on performance, "land grabbing" phenomena may occur. Conference hotels should change from "Sales Marketing" to "Marketing Developing," studying and analyzing markets, formulating development and sales policies. Market developers no longer manage meetings on-site but establish a convention and exhibition management department for execution and operation. As early as the early 1990s, China Grand Hotel established such an organization, where on-site services during conferences are handled by the conference management division, not the market development department.

(2) Application of E-Commerce and New Technologies

Currently, hotels use computer systems to manage guest rooms, but none manage conferences via computer systems, even large conference hotels hosting thousands of events annually still use manual management methods. Due to sales personnel's unfamiliarity with the conference management process, contracts signed with clients often result in issues like overlapping conference schedules or insufficient time for pre-conference registration, leading to chaotic management. Manual management, with incomplete data, frequently results in "dramatic reductions" in conference sizes. In the internet era, building conference websites is crucial. Many conference hotels' web pages cannot be opened, and the content on some hotel websites is rarely updated. Conference needs should be met on the website, including detailed information. For instance, a map of dining spots around the Asian Games Village was made by a PCO, not any internal hardware institution. Some conference hotels have beautifully designed Chinese websites with ample information, but their English websites contain much less content.

(3) Formulation of a Reasonable Pricing System

Pricing is a common issue when hosting international conferences. Some hotels' prices seem disorganized, lacking a systematic approach, with strong arbitrariness, mainly reflected in room rates and catering fees. For example, international conferences require room rates to be announced one year in advance. Hotels usually follow a "high price for distant dates, low price for near dates" policy, offering higher room rates for conferences scheduled months ahead. However, as the conference approaches and the hotel's occupancy rate does not meet expectations, lower prices may appear on the hotel's website, causing price discrepancies, confusion, and even negative international impressions. Regarding catering prices, internationally, simple and quick foods like sandwiches and soft-packaged beverages are popular, keeping prices low. However, in Beijing, the minimum lunch price at star-rated hotels starts at 80 yuan. Additionally, due to the characteristics of international conference catering activities, there are specific patterns, such as varying attendance numbers at receptions by up to 30%. Most hotels strictly limit adjustments to the number of people attending catering activities, often resulting in ordering too much food and causing waste. As advocates of green Olympics and green meetings, hotel managers should actively cooperate with volunteer organizations to manage catering activities.

(4) Diversified Operations and Comprehensive Services

Administrative reforms in China have led to fewer staff in many conference organizing institutions. To improve efficiency, outsourcing conference services is inevitable. Besides providing hardware services, conference hotels should also enhance software service awareness, offering comprehensive services and diversified operations to increase profit points. For example, assisting conference organizers in arranging cultural activities, providing notebooks and pens with the hotel's logo, or even providing vehicle permits and banquet invitations, as done by the booking department of the Great Hall of the People in Beijing. The key issue here is not to overcharge for services. For instance, a well-known conference center in Beijing charged 30,000 yuan for a background board used in the main conference hall, whereas the market price was 10,000 yuan. Under pressure, they adjusted the price, attracting more business. Statistics show that if half of the conferences use the venue for background boards and banners, annual revenue could reach nearly ten million yuan.

(5) Hotel Managers Need to Understand Conferences

Conferences, especially international ones, have their own operational rules and management processes at different stages. Decision-making leaders and sales departments of conference hotels need to understand these processes and rules to provide satisfactory services to conference organizers and maximize hotel profits. Some conference hotels now get involved from the bidding stage, but this is also the stage with the highest investment risk. After years of effort and significant human and financial resources, failure by just a few votes can lead to total loss of investment. For example, a hotel in Beijing failed to assist in successfully bidding for an international conference, mainly because the series of conferences had been held in Beijing in 2000, making it impossible to hold another one in such a short cycle. A PCO refused to participate in the bidding process, avoiding tens of thousands of yuan in losses.

(6) Specialized Contracts for Hosting Conferences

Many conference hotels still copy contracts used for tour groups when signing agreements with conference organizers. Hosting a conference is much more complex than hosting a tour group, involving several departments such as sales, reservations, catering, engineering, and security. Without detailed provisions, problems often arise during execution, leading to losses for the hotel. For instance, a high-level international conference banquet at a hotel resulted in dozens of foreign attendees suffering from diarrhea the next day, prompting the organizing unit to refuse payment. Therefore, conference hotels should develop standard contract templates for hosting conferences and widely solicit opinions from local conference companies.
