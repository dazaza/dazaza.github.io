What is Premenstrual Syndrome?

What is Premenstrual Syndrome [http://www.0351nfm.com/yjjk/jqzhz/index.html]? Experts from Shanxi Huimin Hospital introduce: Premenstrual Syndrome refers to various symptoms that occur during the menstrual period or before and after menstruation, such as lower abdominal pain, often accompanied by nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea. In severe cases, symptoms such as pallor, cold hands and feet, and profuse sweating may appear, recurring with the menstrual cycle. Premenstrual Syndrome is more common in unmarried or non-pregnant women, often easing or disappearing after childbirth.

Symptoms of premenstrual syndrome usually start a week before menstruation, gradually worsening until the last 2-3 days before menstruation, then disappearing after menstruation. Some patients take longer for their symptoms to subside, gradually easing and only completely disappearing 3-4 days after menstruation begins.

Menstruation [http://www.0351nfm.com/yjjk/index.htm], is a "good friend" for mature women, generally causing no discomfort. However, a few women may experience irritability, insomnia, edema, abdominal pain, back pain, and other general reactions besides possible symptoms of premenstrual syndrome.

Menstrual rash: Itching of the skin, appearance of herpes, red spots, or purpura occurs 2-3 days before each menstrual period. Rashes mostly occur on the face, chest, back, and limbs. As menstruation ends, the rashes and itching symptoms disappear without treatment.

Premenstrual toothache: Within two weeks before menstruation, eating cold dishes or drinking cold drinks can cause sudden severe toothaches that quickly stop. This is because the weight of the dental pulp and periodontal mucosal vessels causes expansion and congestion before the menstrual cycle. When the dentin is stimulated by cold, it results in intermittent pain. This condition is not related to dental disease and does not require treatment.

Menstrual headache [http://www.0351nfm.com/yjjk/yjtc/index.html]: Modern medical research has confirmed that the imbalance of estrogen and progesterone secretion in women during menstruation is the fundamental cause of menstrual headaches.

Oral herpes during menstruation: This is related to the reduced resistance of the body during menstruation and the activity of latent herpes viruses within the body. It commonly occurs 1-2 days before menstruation and lasts 2-3 days into the period, mostly affecting the lips but also appearing around the eyelids, nostrils, and edges of the labia minora. Mulberry juice can be used for application, and scratching should be avoided to prevent secondary infections.

Premenstrual nasal congestion: Nasal congestion occurs every time menstruation appears, due to the physiological connection between the nasal mucosa and female reproductive organs.

Hemoptysis during menstruation: Some women may experience hemoptysis 3-4 days before menstruation or during menstruation, which resolves without medication after menstruation ends. Treatment for this menstrual disorder can involve intramuscular injection of progesterone under a doctor's guidance.

Meniere's disease during menstruation: This condition is more common in middle-aged women. Sudden dizziness, tinnitus, and visual rotation with nausea and vomiting may occur during menstruation. Generally, bed rest is sufficient; in severe cases, appropriate sedatives and diuretics can be used.

Heart arrhythmia during menstruation [http://www.0351nfm.com/yjjk/yjbt/index.html]: Premature beats, tachycardia, bradycardia, and paroxysmal supraventricular tachycardia are common. Rest is advised when symptoms occur, and sedatives can be taken according to a doctor's instructions.

Experts at Shanxi Huimin Hospital remind: Mild premenstrual syndrome can be relieved through self-regulation, while severe cases require hospital visits for diagnosis and treatment under a doctor's guidance.
