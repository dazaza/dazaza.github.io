Odd! Simultaneous conception inside and outside the uterus

Information Times News (Reporter: Li Nan Nan, Communicators: Peng Wen Bin, Lin Hui Fang) — For an expectant mother, becoming pregnant with twins is a kind of luck. However, for Xiao Ling (a pseudonym), a 25-year-old mother from Guangzhou, this was not a good thing, because one of her twins was in the uterus, while the other was outside the uterus. This type of compound pregnancy, involving both intrauterine and ectopic pregnancies, has an incidence rate of only three in ten thousand. Recently, the Guangdong Maternal and Child Health Care Hospital successfully performed surgery on Xiao Ling, removing the ectopic fetus while preserving the intrauterine fetus.

### Removing the Ectopic Pregnancy While Preserving the Intrauterine Pregnancy

Xiao Ling, 25 years old, currently works at a medical institution in Guangzhou. A month ago, she suddenly experienced severe lower abdominal pain when she was already three months pregnant. She was rushed to the hospital two hours later. An ultrasound revealed a large amount of fluid accumulation in the pelvic cavity. The attending doctor decided to immediately perform exploratory laparotomy, during which it was discovered that besides the fetus in the uterus, her left fallopian tube was also significantly enlarged with a rupture—a typical case of compound intrauterine and ectopic pregnancy.

"Simply put, there was simultaneous conception both inside and outside the uterus," explained Luo Xi Ping, Director of Gynecology at the Guangdong Maternal and Child Health Care Hospital. With the widespread application of assisted reproductive technologies such as artificial insemination, women often produce multiple eggs after medication. However, natural conception resulting in one fetus being intrauterine and the other ectopic is extremely rare. "The incidence rate is only three in ten thousand. Reviewing domestic literature, most cases are individual reports, and they are often misdiagnosed or overlooked." Subsequently, doctors urgently operated on Xiao Ling, removing the ectopic fetus while successfully preserving the normal intrauterine fetus.

### Experts: B-mode Ultrasound Can Exclude Dangers at Six Weeks of Pregnancy

It was reported that although Xiao Ling conceived naturally, after becoming pregnant, she only underwent a single urine test and did not conduct prenatal examinations or any other tests. In response, Luo Xi Ping reminded pregnant women that once they discover they are pregnant, they must undergo routine pre-pregnancy examinations. Generally, an ultrasound can be done by the sixth week of pregnancy, and following the doctor's guidance, proper prenatal care should be ensured. If any abnormal pregnancy conditions occur during pregnancy, immediate medical attention is necessary.

And if a compound pregnancy is confirmed, can the intrauterine baby be preserved? Experts stated that it cannot be guaranteed. Doctors will comprehensively consider factors such as the size of the mass and the amount of bleeding within the abdominal cavity, combined with the patient’s fertility requirements, to decide whether surgical treatment or drug-conservative treatment should be adopted.
