Reflections on "Beijing Love Story"

by qingshou27 on 2012-02-10 10:05:10

Beijing, this is a city filled with various opportunities, challenges, temptations, and audacity. Yearning for Beijing, envisioning this mother nurturing China's take-off and prosperity, aspiring to be the fashionable gentleman with braids. The red walls and green tiles of the quadrangle courtyards, the grandeur of the Forbidden City, the deep-rooted customs of the Mao'er Hutong, the Beijing opera hummed by the elderly bird-walking gentlemen in theaters... These things that seem worlds apart from the metropolis filled with towering skyscrapers are precisely what constitute the soul of Beijing. The accumulation of history makes the bustling city less detestable, and thus, we begin to become obsessed with it - Beijing.

There should be no one who doesn't like Beijing because of its diversified development. Everyone can always find their "old place" in Beijing, belonging to you, belonging to you and him, belonging to you and them - the old places where love, friendship, and pain are remembered, written on the sticky notes on the wall, contained in the glass of wine, inscribed in the lyrics of the youth memory book, left in the images of playing and teasing in the former schoolyard... Old places, those people and events of the past, and those years when we chased after boys and girls, those years when we squandered our youth, North Love, being loved. Everyone has a different scale to measure happiness. Some put Burberry and Chanel on the side of happiness, some put CEO and a house in Beijing's Sanyuan Bridge, Lin Xia puts the madman, the madman puts Shen Bing, Wu Di puts friends and family, and Wu Wei puts business opportunities, money, and position. The three words "Beijing, love, story", seven people, or even more, through which you can see idols, love, life, choose what you want.

The ending wasn't as wonderfully satisfying as I had imagined.

Looking at each person inside, evolving step by step in the city of Beijing. Their laughter or tears, victories or failures, gains or losses, all made me involuntarily reminisce about the past.

In them, I seemed to see my own reflection, or even the shadows of people around me. It felt so familiar, making me laugh and cry with them, stumbling along with them.

When the people in the story have all grown up and matured, that's the end of this story. As Lin Xia summarized in the final episode, it leaves a long-lasting silence, a mix of emotions like a mirror reflecting oneself and the stories around. Sighs, hidden injuries, expectations, confusion, mostly due to the helplessness of reality. Many things we face, many things we experience, and many things we haven't faced yet but still exist. We keep repeating yesterday, today, and tomorrow. With all our might, we rush forward, helplessly pondering. The irresistible forces of society, everyone's values, but one thing is the same - as reflected in the story - we are constantly growing. The impatience of society makes our steps too hurried, forgetting to slow down and cherish what we already have. Family, love, friendship, and everything else, how tired our hearts are, have we ever tightly embraced them? We stubbornly maintain what we want, obstinately believing that's the right way, not knowing until everything is gone that the scenery by the roadside was also quite nice. Until the end of life, we realize that we have been on the road all along, never stopping to appreciate. Thus, we regret that life is too short, originally, we were wrong all along, lost in the journey of life. Society witnesses our story, witnessing the falls of people like Xiao Meng, the success of people like Fei Si; the loss of Wu Wei, the possession of Wu Di, and more importantly, the unique growth of our friends like Cheng Feng.

Lin Xia - Who's Summer

Lin Xia makes a scene threatening to jump off a building right from the start, for that crazy guy who deceives women without remorse. Lin Xia is really good, too good for me to ever be like her - capricious, passionate, kind, and stubborn. She likes the crazy guy so much that she can guard herself for years for him, who flirts with countless women. But the crazy guy doesn't like Lin Xia, wrong, he does like her, just doesn't love her.

When Lin Xia sees the crazy guy hugging Shen Bing, I thought she would slap him hard, or at least glare at them. But she didn't, she crouched down and hid. Why did she hide? Because she likes the crazy guy. At that moment, I think this girl loves too humbly, a heart that cannot be moved by any amount of persistence, tied to another cold heart.

Fei Si liked Lin Xia at first, but later married Da Hua. Shao Huayang liked Lin Xia, but in the end, Lin Xia didn't end up with the big medicine bottle.

When the woman in front said she was Mrs. Shao Huayang, Lin Xia's originally cruel smile froze at the corner of her mouth. Although Mrs. Shao doesn't have much time left, she came to ask Lin Xia to take care of her husband and children. But Lin Xia feels sorry for her, her sadness hides in the corners of her eyes, she keeps saying sorry, and she dares not look at the frail woman in front of her. I want to hug her and say, Lin Xia, don't cry.

Such a good Lin Xia, she loves the crazy guy, loves Shao Huayang, but once they have other ties, she is always the one to let go. Such a good Lin Xia, who brings laughter to everyone, but cannot bring summer to her own summer. I suddenly remember, originally, all their joys and sorrows, all their partings and reunions, all their love and hate happened when summer had been gone for so long.

She gradually parted ways with what she once possessed. From the very beginning, I deeply liked this pure and generous girl. Her straightforwardness sometimes makes me adore her. Her persistence and dedication ultimately make her lose her initial happiness, bringing changes she never expected before. She says, "I love you, and it doesn't matter whether you do or not." Shen Bing asks Lin Xia why she is sure the crazy guy is her true goal, and she answers, "I believe in feelings, what if the feelings change?" She remains chaste for a man, in this day and age, isn't such love touching? She says, "Just thinking about not being able to be together forever makes me reluctant: realizing those hopes are all so disappointing." However, everyone has the right to choose their own way of life. At least, such a choice is very good.

Yang Zixi - Spring Does Not Blame

Lin Xia didn't jump off the building, but Wu Di, who came to persuade her, fell instead, because of Yang Zixi, who had been dating him for four years but sought a richer partner. He fell accidentally, and in this day and age, if a man truly suffers from unrequited love, I would surely beat my chest and lament to the sky. Yang Zixi is played by Yang Mi, heaven knows how much we miss the Guo Xiang-era Yang Mi. This TV series made me like Zhang Xin Yi, like Tong Li Ya, and start to not dislike Yang Mi.

The story of Wu Di and Yang Zixi is common in real life. They met in school and parted in society, due to work, environment, or blood-related status and parental orders. In general, the reason for the breakup can be summed up in one word - money. "I don't feel the same anymore" is all nonsense. Either this girl is especially kind-hearted and doesn't want to hurt your fragile heart, or she's too lazy to talk to you, using this universal excuse as her escape tool. Yang Zixi is particularly honest, she clearly tells Wu Di that she chose Andy because he was rich. What could Wu Di say to that? Analyze the pros and cons and persuade her to come back, or swear eternal vows to earn a certain amount of money within a few years? What kind of girl is Yang Zixi, Wu Di understands better than us viewers.

What kind of girl Yang Zixi is, I didn't understand at first, until she talked about the pair of shoes she thought could talk to her when she was little. I realized then that Yang Zixi worships not wealth, but a dream.

All girls have a dream of owning a flower shop, Yang Zixi does, and so do I.

Wu Di ends up with Yang Zixi after she takes off her Chanel, they hold hands, but whether they can successfully grow old together, we must ask who?

Yang Zixi and Lin Xia both scream over branded shoes in the magazine, but Lin Xia's obsession with shoes stays in her screams, while Yang Zixi pays a lot for them. Such a person would write: "The best husband is the one who lets his wife indulge in luxury, the best lover is the one who comes whenever called and surprises me every day." I don't hate gold-diggers, choosing to rely on others is your own choice, I despise and disdain those who decide to give up halfway. If you've chosen, don't look back.

Is this her fault? It could be said it is, or it could be said it isn't. Loneliness in childhood plus a beautiful face that has never lacked suitors, relying on material things for security, opening a flower shop is her dream, so she chose such a life. Actually, she also wants to be someone's wife, wants to be a loyal wife. Some people always give up the simplest happiness to pursue dreams, do you think it's worth it?

Wu Mei - Quiet Autumn Comes Late

Originally, Yang Zixi could immediately return to Wu Di's side after her enlightenment, but at that time, Wu Di already had an extraordinary and charming presence - Wu Mei. What a dazzling name, even before meeting her, one could imagine the charm and youth hidden in her eyebrows and eyes. So, it's no wonder little Wu fell under her spell.

I actually admire the creators of this TV series, who managed to portray two brothers competing for the same woman in such a way that it doesn't feel disgusting. Big Sister Wu's romance is divided into two distinct parts: one is her first love, Wu Wei, who was once poor but now successful, and the other is Wu Di, our steadfast friend dedicated to his ideals. Wu Di may not be young, but compared to Big Sister Wu, he's still a naive college student. Big Sister Wu was Wu Wei's ex-girlfriend, and when they struggled for their dreams in America, Wu Di was probably somewhere flirting with girls.

I didn't expect Wu Wei's feelings for Big Sister Wu to be so deep, every word he spoke made this iron lady in the business world want to cry. Wu Wei's cancer brought her back to that distant world. In that world, she wasn't General Manager Wu, and he wasn't General Manager Wu either. She cried with him all night, and he fought for her his entire not-so-long life.

Wu Mei said, "Don't think women are weaker than men, women's psychological endurance is much stronger. Women can cry, can compromise; men can't."

"Life is based on happiness, love is based on mutual benefit." Wu Mei could be considered the Yang Zixi of the past. Initially, I thought she was at least lucky, but by the end, when the deceased's diary was opened after many years, tears streamed down my face, and she returned to a foreign land, "returned."

She needed company every night, not out of affection but loneliness, she feared aging, she feared losing her youth. Originally, even the strongest women have the weakest sides, which is something I misunderstood for a long time.

Everyone harbors deep feelings for someone, love arises unknowingly, and grows deeper. Many times, you wait for someone, and they never come. But sometimes, that person, that love, comes unexpectedly, catching you off guard.

Shen Bing - Turns Out You Are That Warm Sun

From the moment Shen Bing appeared, Cheng Feng, the suave rogue, fell in love with her. But Shen Bing was Shi Xiaomeng's girlfriend, and Shi Xiaomeng was Cheng Feng's best buddy. An ancient saying goes, "Do not deceive your friend's wife." Of course, Cheng Feng wouldn't be so despicable and desperate as to directly pounce on his brother-in-law's wife. The scene where he helped Shen Bing find a job in kindergarten was so adorable, it made me laugh. Who is Cheng Feng? A well-known playboy in the capital, it's not surprising that he pleases girls, but his single-minded pursuit of one girl makes you think this young man is ready to settle down. I've always enjoyed watching the transformation of a playboy into a faithful man. He Zhen Nan from "Cui Rao Zhu Wei," Joe from "The Legend of the Condor Heroes," and the wealthy and romantic male protagonists in urban romance novels, each transforms into a reformed prodigal son upon meeting the female lead.

Later, Shen Bing asked Cheng Feng when he started liking her, and Cheng Feng said it was at first sight. Not only Cheng Feng fell in love at first sight, but I also fell in love with her at first glance. At the bustling train station, he shouted loudly, "Shen Bing!" She stood behind him, tapped his shoulder, and said, "I'm Shen Bing, Ice's Bing." What a lively and transparent young lady! Her features were beautifully drawn, her eyes bright as stars, her white teeth gleaming with a gentle smile, she truly looked like Little Dragon Maiden stepping out of the ancient tomb. I used to think Tong Liya was very beautiful, then I lost interest in her, but now I genuinely find her beautiful, and the phrase "beauty beyond compare" seems cliché for her. At that moment, I thought Shi Xiaomeng was truly fortunate.

All childhood sweethearts are hard to reconcile unless one of them changes their heart midway. Of course, it's possible that it's not a change of heart, but rather the most pitiful four words - "no choice." Thinking about it empathetically, we should understand Shi Xiaomeng. He came from the remotest countryside, determined to stay in the big city, wanting to give his beloved the greatest happiness, but that happiness requires money. Again, it's money, okay, money is the root of all evil. I believe, at the moment he pushed Shen Bing away, he must have thought, someday, he would stand before her again, then, rocking in a chair, slowly grow old with her. Unfortunately, his "girl" fell in love with someone else.

The ending of Shi Xiaomeng and Yang Zixi wasn't due to their own faults. If it were someone else, they might have made different choices. If it were a different reality, they might have made different outcomes. Unfortunately, at a specific time, with specific experiences, specific people led to a specific ending. When you finally see such a beautiful girl working as a waitress in a high-end hotel, do you ever think about her past wearing Prada? Pursuing materialism blinded them, or perhaps gave them a correct worldview. Say what you will.

The madman abandoned wealth, and Shi Xiaomeng counting his riches didn't necessarily make the madman any more refined. One was a rich second generation, the other a poor second generation. Twenty years of accumulation originally shaped two completely different people. The madman came from wealth, so it was easier for him to abandon wealth for some obsessions. Xiaomeng couldn't do that. Years of feeling inferior, fate, oppression, tormented him, who was not originally very strong. Do you think he doesn't love Shen Bing? Not at all, he loves her just as much as the madman. This is the difference between the rich and the poor. Twenty years of accumulation changed too much, even destiny. Onlookers can't understand him, try it yourself?

Born into privilege, they don't cherish it, what they struggle for themselves, they cling to. Asking such a person to abandon wealth is like asking them to die. Therefore, you'll find that most major corrupt officials come from poverty, real aristocrats have no desire for money.

Shi Xiaomeng is pitiable. His lack of perseverance certainly has its reasons. Material desires run rampant, confusion prevails. Life itself is unfair, there's only choice, unfairness.

Wu Di is the person I admire most, the sole reason being his authenticity. In a city full of splendor and fame, as a marketing department employee who has seen countless scenes of frivolity, maintaining authenticity is not easy. Obsessively yearning for his ex-girlfriend who hurt him twice, appearing when she needs him, quietly withdrawing when she doesn't. All because he lives too genuinely. He says he admires the madman's carefree attitude, truly being able to let go of everything, but the only thing he can let go of in this lifetime is chopsticks.

The madman rides alone for thousands of miles for a woman, thanks to a strong backing and a handsome appearance. Money clears the way, invincible. Morality, emotion, perspectives, these have all distorted this person, but he is strong enough. Being confused isn't scary, what's scary is having no rules. His final ability to let go of wealth and power, though not as difficult as Shi Xiaomeng's, is still extremely hard. The inevitable path for a playboy's growth includes family misfortunes, including corporate bankruptcy and the death of loved ones, etc. Suddenly, Prince Cheng falls from the paradise of material wealth to the ground, he begins to awaken and take responsibility, naturally, this is also due to Shen Bing's influence. Shen Bing is actually a very "motherly" character, refusing to bow to worldly possessions, believing only in her own heart. Cheng Feng did so much for Shen Bing, her heart isn't made of iron, slowly, the sun rises.

Some say this drama is like a reflection of current societal conditions, so the director isn't trying to present a perfect or tragic moving story. Instead, it's meant to make us see some things. As for what those things are, I don't know, because I haven't lost my youth, I haven't matured but am gradually heading towards maturity.

I guess I'll have to accept the changes of things remaining but people changing, face the departure of friends and relatives, watch the clear skies above turn gray. But I believe, even the darkest skies will have a moment of blue. This story is less about choosing love and more about choosing a lifestyle. There's also Lao Cheng, Wu Wei, Wu Wei's assistant Hua Yong, Shao Huayang, Fei Si, sub-health, Hu Rongqiang... Each deserves deep contemplation, and I believe everyone seeing them will gain insights, I firmly believe.

I've been thinking, what should we truly pursue? Wealth and material goods are external possessions, isn't love too? Wu Di sacrificed so much for Yang Zixi, but she still left. Family, friendship, love, which is better? Has anything changed? Time continues to flow, what flows is ourselves. In the passage of time, everyone controls their actions, because these and many other things aren't controllable by everyone. Life crushes everyone, perhaps there's no winner or loser in life, maybe it's supposed to be this way.

The conclusion of the story is almost a case of things remaining but people changing, everyone has completely transformed due to fate, personality, experience, and emotions.

I like the final lens treatment in this TV series, everything freezes and stops. Outside, Lin Xia's phone drops, the text message hasn't been sent; Fei Si smokes gloomily, the smoke resembling a large cotton ball; Da Hua drinks water looking up; Wu Di hands a cup to Xiao Xi, who still wears the wind chime Wu Di bought her. Inside, Cheng Feng holds Shen Bing's hand, continuously holding Shen Bing's hand, in the last moment of 24 hours, everything freezes. It seems, we can still hope for Shen Bing to wake up.

I don't know why, but as the last moment of the lens approaches, time quickly counts down to zero, I suddenly feel a wave of despair and sadness for the main characters. But except for the characters in the story, no one knows the true ending?
