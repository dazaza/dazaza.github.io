BEIJING, February 23 - Although the WTA tour is now dominated by players like Victoria Azarenka, Petra Kvitova, and Caroline Wozniacki from the post-90s generation, Ana Ivanovic, who won the French Open title and reached world number one as early as 2008, is confident in her ability to return to the center stage. Nigel Sears, the coach leading her resurgence, believes that it was precisely this premature success at age 20 that caused Ivanovic to subsequently lose her way and competitiveness.

Ivanovic, who dropped to 64th in the rankings two years ago, is now gradually returning to her peak form. After reaching the fourth round in Australia and narrowly losing to Kvitova, her performance this week in Dubai has been equally impressive. Particularly noteworthy was her 6-1, 7-5 victory over 2010 French Open champion Francesca Schiavone in the first round, which seemed to evoke memories of her past glory.

"I am sure I can return to my peak and lift another Grand Slam trophy," Ivanovic said in an interview. "It's precisely because of this belief that I'm still here and training diligently. If I had no confidence in returning to my peak, I wouldn't still be striving in the tennis world."

Ivanovic's quiet resurgence owes much to the help of Nigel Sears. This renowned coach, who started coaching Ivanovic last July, has instilled great confidence in her for a return to the top. "I think he understands not only my characteristics as a player but also me outside of the court," Ivanovic praised her coach, continuing: "I think this is the most crucial point for our successful cooperation. He remains calm during my matches, and sometimes I can't help but want to shout at him: let me feel your passion. But I think this aspect complements me well because I am a perfectionist and may get overly excited at times. While it's not easy to remain calm in such situations, he can bring me peace from the sidelines."

Nigel Sears, praised by his protégé, maintains a cool head. He believes that although Ivanovic is on the right path, the outside world should not expect her to suddenly become the dominant force in tennis overnight. "Ana is gradually returning to the level she wants, but such things cannot be rushed. She cannot suddenly start playing tennis good enough to win a Grand Slam," Sears said in an interview. "I am satisfied with her performance over the past few months. Reaching the fourth round at the Australian Open is an acceptable result, and her championship win in Bali showcased her best level. However, it must be clear that returning to the peak is a long process and requires more patience."

Looking back, Sears believes that this premature success disrupted Ivanovic's growth trajectory. "In retrospect, I believe Ana did indeed win the Grand Slam too early. Because she had already reached such heights, it became confusing for her to make further progress afterward," Sears concluded, "This is an extremely challenging task, but now I see many exciting signs in Ana. She is on the right path, setting off again toward her dream."
