Golden Mouth Host - Chen Luyu

by longersing on 2009-05-23 14:45:26

Name: Chen Luyu

Place of Origin: Zhoukou, Henan

Education: Grade 88, International News Major, Foreign Languages Department, Beijing Broadcasting Institute (Graduated in 1993)

Zodiac Sign: Gemini

Motto: Go all out and do your best

Height: 163cm

Work Experience:

"Music Unlimited" on the Chinese channel of Phoenix Television

"Hong Kong's Return to China Century Report - 60 Hours Non-stop Broadcast"

"Phoenix Morning Express"

"Live commentary for Princess Diana's Funeral"

"One Point Talks About Both Sides of the Straits and Three Regions"

"VIP Guest Room"

"Ruyu Has an Appointment: Tell Your Story"

In 2000, she was selected as the "Best Female Host of the Year 2000" by audience voting hosted by New Weekly.

Chen Luyu, from Beijing, majored in international news and often interviews political figures, entrepreneurs, and social focal points in fluent English. Starting from hosting the music program "Music Unlimited", then "Phoenix Morning Express", "One Point Talks About Both Sides of the Straits and Three Regions", "VIP Guest Room", and the interview show tailored for her "Ruyu Has an Appointment: Tell Your Story", Luyu successfully created a warm and approachable image with her human touch, making the "Phoenix Morning Express" she hosts very popular among audiences.

In "Ruyu Has an Appointment: Tell Your Story", she has interviewed famous people including Liao Jingwen, the current director of the Xu Beihong Memorial Hall in Beijing, Wang Junxia, the Olympic long-distance running gold medalist, renowned poet Wang Guozhen, international superstar Jackie Chan, basketball star Yao Ming, former Secretary for Security of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region Regina Ip, and youth idol Nicholas Tse. This program was selected as the second most favorite program by viewers in the "Top Ten Favorite Programs of Phoenix Television in 2002" on the Phoenix Network.

Additionally, in major news live broadcasts such as "Hong Kong's Return", "Millennium Journey", "Princess Diana's Funeral", "US Presidential Election", and events like 9/11 and the Iraq War, she fully demonstrated her talent in international news. In 2000, she was elected as the Best Female Host of the "China TV Chart 2000". In 2001, she was elected as the "Best Female Host of News Programs".

On March 5, 2007, an article titled "Chen Luyu: China's 'Oprah'" appeared on the CNN website page. The article generously bestowed the title of "China's Oprah" upon the famous host of Phoenix Television, Chen Luyu, and gave full attention and affirmation to her distinctive Chinese-style "talk show" program "Ruyu Has an Appointment" that she has persisted with for five years. However, despite this, Luyu does not dare to claim success, only referring to herself as a "host who has achieved some small results", because "achievements and success are too grand."
