Express companies sign peak season service commitment letters: suspension of operations requires reporting and approval

by tuoqing1188 on 2011-08-04 09:39:22

Our reporter Zhang Yan and intern reporter Zhang Li reported that yesterday, the China Express Association announced that it had organized its members to sign a service commitment letter to ensure smooth delivery services during peak seasons. Enterprises that signed the letter must formulate specific measures regarding manpower, equipment, transportation, collection and delivery, and overtime pay during the peak season to ensure that no parcels are accumulated, delayed or damaged at any of the branch companies (collection and delivery points). Moreover, a reserve fund system will be established to ensure sufficient funds for the peak season delivery services.

Shao Zhonglin of the China Express Association, in an interview with Beijing Times, introduced that all major express enterprises with nationwide networks have signed this commitment letter. This move is aimed at ensuring people can use express services during peak seasons, especially during the Spring Festival. It was reported that last year around the Spring Festival, due to reasons such as warehouse overload and employees returning home for the holiday, many express companies temporarily ceased operations, causing products purchased by people to be stranded in transit for a long time.

According to this commitment letter, after signing, these enterprises must establish a "Leadership Group for Ensuring Express Services During Peak Seasons" with the legal representative of the company serving as the group leader. They must also formulate specific measures regarding manpower, equipment, transportation, collection, delivery, and employee overtime pay during the peak season.

In addition, express enterprises are required to legally establish a reporting system for the suspension or cessation of service outlets. If any service outlet, processing center, or network needs to suspend or cease operations due to special reasons, they must promptly report to the local postal regulatory authorities and industry associations. After obtaining approval, they may suspend or cease operations but must properly handle the衔接and processing of parcels beforehand. Except for reasons beyond their control, they cannot accumulate or delay parcels for any reason.
