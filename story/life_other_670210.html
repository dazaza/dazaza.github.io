Letting go is also a kind of beauty.

I let you go, and gave you the freedom you wanted, just to make you happy. But don't say I don't love you. In fact, it's precisely because I love you that I choose to let you go.

- Preface

Yesterday, I received a message from a friend asking me to keep him company, to talk with him. Seeing the message, I knew something must have happened; otherwise, he wouldn't have called me out. Because my friends all know: I've been living a peaceful and happy life recently. If it's not absolutely necessary, no one would come to disturb me.

After school, I came to the coffee shop as agreed. Entering the coffee shop, I looked around and, as expected, I saw him by the window. He still retained his long-standing habit - a seat by the window, a steaming cup of coffee, gazing aimlessly into the distance alone. I walked over quietly and sat down opposite him.

Just as I was about to speak, I took a good look at him and swallowed my words. Because he looked so haggard and tired, a state I had never seen before.

I stared at him for a long time before slowly speaking, "What's wrong? How could you end up in such a mess?" He didn't answer, but instead picked up his coffee and sipped it lightly, then raised his head and said nonchalantly, "Nothing, I'm divorced, I just wanted to let you know..."

"What? Why?" Before he could finish, I interrupted him urgently because I couldn't believe he would get divorced so easily, and I clearly knew how much he loved his wife. After hearing my shout, he smiled bitterly, "Do you think I would joke about something like this?"

I was speechless, because this was indeed not a funny joke.

However, I still found it strange. Based on my understanding of their marriage, they shouldn't have reached the point of divorce.

Silent silence. I sat quietly across from him, waiting for him to explain everything. Because I thought he called me out to listen to his story. (In the eyes of my friends, I've always been a good listener.)

So any friend with troubles likes to confide in me.) After a long while, he spoke softly, "Do you know? Actually, our marital happiness was just a show for others. In front of you all, we always acted extremely loving. But when you turned away, we were like strangers, sleeping apart." I didn't understand, so I just listened silently.

He picked up his coffee cup again and sipped it lightly before continuing, "She always complained that I wasn't competent enough, saying I lacked ambition..."

"Hey, I think every woman says this to her husband. Like me, I often say this to my husband, but it shouldn't lead to divorce, right?" I asked aloud.

"Hmm, indeed, if it was only that, I wouldn't have divorced so easily," he replied to my question solemnly.

"But..." As he said this, he couldn't help but tightly hug his head with both arms, burying it deep within them. I knew he was crying. Although he was trying hard to suppress himself, I could still feel his pain through his voice and broken sentences. After a long pause, he raised his head and said, "Sorry, I lost control."

"Between friends, there's no need to be polite. Speak up. Today, I'll be an excellent listener. Tell me everything, that way, you might feel better. Of course, if you want to." I slowly said sitting across from him.

"Do you know? You've always had the quality to make people feel at ease. That's why we friends like to confide in you when we have problems," he said. I nodded slightly but didn't reply. Regarding this, I was very clear. Perhaps because I prefer quietness, I enjoy listening to people quietly tell their stories the most.

"A few days ago was our wedding anniversary, do you remember?" he asked me. I thought for a moment and remembered, it was indeed true. He had even mentioned inviting us friends to celebrate for them. But later, for some unknown reason, he didn't call us, and I gradually forgot about it.

"Originally, that day, I planned to invite all of you friends over, and we would have a great party together. But she said she wanted us two to quietly celebrate our wedding anniversary, because she felt too much noise was tiring. Nowadays, she rarely makes demands on me, so when she said it, I felt very happy. Because I thought she was finally willing to open her heart to me, which was a good sign. At least, she wouldn't ignore me anymore. So, I didn't invite you over." When he said these words, I could feel his inner joy. His eyes suddenly sparkled, unfortunately, only for a moment, then returned to dimness.

"That day was the happiest day for me in recent years because she personally cooked a full table of delicious meals, and they were all my favorites. She actually remembered what I liked to eat, which made me really happy. There was also red wine that day. Oh, we haven't quietly faced each other for so long, gently and tenderly pouring wine for each other, then silently drinking together..."

As he spoke, he suddenly stopped, his eyes misty, as if he was savoring the memory for a long time. Watching him like this, I felt sour and bitter in my heart. Is this really the person I know - that mature, steady, wise, and rational man?

I don't know how long it took, but he slowly continued, "That day, I was so happy because I felt her tenderness and warmth of home again..." After finishing, he chuckled silently, "Unfortunately, I didn't expect that it would be our farewell dinner."

"After dinner, I was still immersed in happiness, feeling deeply moved inside. I sat on the sofa, watching her tidy up the table quietly, looking at her busy figure, my heart was warm, so warm. This has been a memory far away from me for a long time. It should be a very ordinary thing, but for me, it was so hard to achieve.

I was deeply moved, so I quietly walked behind her, stretched out my arms, wanting to embrace her tightly, wanting to pass my happiness and warmth to her. But she almost instinctively dodged my embrace, then said awkwardly, 'What are you doing? Can't you see I'm busy?' After saying that, she ignored me and went on tidying up the table herself. I felt disappointed and returned to the sofa."

I sat quietly across from him, unconsciously stirring my coffee with my hand, listening to his story silently.

"I don't know how much time passed before she finally finished tidying up. Then, she slowly walked to the side of the sofa and sat next to me. She was silent, just staring at me for a long time, so long that I thought she wouldn't say anything. But suddenly, she spoke, 'Are you tired?' I didn't understand her meaning, so I looked up at her. But she quickly turned away, unwilling to meet my eyes. Then, she continued, 'I feel tired...so tired...

I know I shouldn't say this, but...we...should get divorced. Let each other go, give each other freedom, and let us find our own happiness, okay?' After that, she cried silently. Do you know how I felt at that moment? First, like being struck by lightning, my mind went blank; then, completely clear, I understood everything clearly; finally, piercing pain, cutting through the heart, reaching the bone.

But I also knew that I had no choice left - giving her freedom and making her happy was the only thing I could do. So, the next day, which was the second day of our wedding anniversary, we peacefully went to handle the divorce.

Then, I was free, and she was free. How about that? Have you ever seen anyone break up so efficiently and peacefully?" Finally, he even made a joke. But I clearly saw tears welling up in his eyes.

Facing such a painful and extremely suppressed him, I really didn't know what to say. So I jokingly said, "Let's go! I'll drink with you. Drinking coffee all the time is so boring." After saying that, I got up and walked out, and he didn't want to sink back into pain either. So he also got up and followed me out.

When we walked out of the coffee shop, it was already dark outside. Pedestrians hurried along the road, each carrying their own worries, indulging in sorrow or joy under a starless night sky. The audio-video store by the roadside played Amu's hoarse singing:

"If the paradise of two people is like a warm wall,

Imprisoning your dreams, is happiness like an iron window?

If you yearn for the sky and desire wings, let you fly.

If romance becomes a burden, I'd rather choose to return to solitude for you,

If entanglement turns into chains, discard the vows...

If our commitment costs you everything, let true love take you away...

A kind of love is called letting go, ending love forever..."

A kind of love is called letting go, for love to end eternity...
