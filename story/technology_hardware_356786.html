Towards Success ---- Matters needing attention during the college entrance examination

I. Safety Requirements

1. Diet and Safety

Pay attention to the intake of protein and carbohydrate-rich foods, and make sure to eat full meals for breakfast, lunch, and dinner, especially breakfast and lunch since the morning exam lasts until 11:30 AM. You can prepare some nutritious food for yourself and have a little before heading to the examination room. During the college entrance examination period, you can also eat more vegetables and fruits because they contain a large amount of dietary fiber and vitamins that can promote intestinal peristalsis, fight fatigue, and enhance immunity. At the same time, it is important to ensure the diversity of your diet. During the college entrance examination while having typhoid fever, particular attention should be paid to food safety. Do not buy food from roadside stalls, avoid eating raw and cold food, and do not consume frozen food such as popsicles and ice cream. During the examination period, under no circumstances should you eat leftover food to prevent food poisoning due to food spoilage in hot weather. Fruits should be washed with boiling water or disinfected before eating them raw. Always keep a box of "Cipro" on hand for any emergency needs.

2. Transportation and Safety

Walk attentively and do not listen to music or chat with friends while walking on the road. During the examination period, try to give yourself ample time to get to the test site.

3. Psychological Safety

Be mentally prepared for any possible accidents. Maintain self-confidence, and overcome any negative feelings during the exam using confidence. Timely communicate with teachers. Do not exchange good or bad feelings with classmates; instead, encourage each other by saying supportive words. The college entrance examination is just one exam, so ignore the focus on the outcome and success or failure, and put worries and desires behind. Do not estimate scores after the exam. Just focus on whether you have basically performed at your level.

Experts' research on successful national college entrance examination participants shows that:

The first factor is the candidate's psychological state during the exam.

The second factor is the candidate's psychological state before the exam.

The third factor is learning methods and learning foundation.

The rule of success in the college entrance examination can be stated as follows: the mastery level of knowledge is the basis for success in the college entrance examination, and the adjustment status of the candidate's psychological state is an important factor in the performance of the college entrance examination level.

It is crucial to adjust one’s mindset and establish self-confidence. Confidence, good mood, entering the state, and fully playing one's role can be said to be the four great treasures of winning in the college entrance examination. To enhance self-confidence and ensure mental security during the college entrance examination period, candidates may consider making efforts in the following areas:

① Set appropriate college entrance examination goals. Expectations should be moderate. Too high expectations, which cannot be reached despite efforts, will hurt confidence; too low expectations will prevent you from entering the right state. Generally speaking, the average score of two mock exams indicates the probable score in the college entrance examination. In the days leading up to the exam, our only goal is: to perform normally and demonstrate our own level.

② Do not compare with others. People tend to blindly compare themselves with others, thinking they must catch up with someone else. In fact, everyone has their own situation. A girl once said that there were several girls in her class who scored higher than her, and she was不服气 (determined not to accept defeat), but the harder she tried to catch up with them, the further she fell behind. Actually, as long as each person does their best in the exam, they have achieved their purpose, and there is no need for comparison. Of course, taking it as motivation is also very good.

③ Maintain a good mood. A good mood is a guarantee for success in the college entrance examination. Mood changes can cause physiological changes, affecting the performance in the college entrance examination. When encountering negative emotions, adjust promptly.

The process of taking the college entrance examination is actually the simultaneous release of knowledge energy and psychological energy. The psychological release process is the foundation and catalyst for the release of knowledge energy. Good psychological quality and healthy mental states can ensure super-normal performance of knowledge energy. Some students say that they performed exceptionally well in a certain exam, upon careful reflection, it was because their mindset was particularly good, and their mood was particularly good, thus their performance was also particularly good.

Gorky once said: "Talent is believing in oneself and believing in one's own strength."

In a certain sense, confidence is the ancestor of success, self-confidence is power, and confidence is a miracle. As long as you believe you can succeed, you will win success.

Facing the college entrance examination, besides having confidence, one must also have a certain amount of pressure. Moderate pressure can become motivation, but if the pressure is too great, exceeding one's psychological endurance, it will lead to psychological illness, affecting review and exams.

Everyone is both a psychological patient and a psychological doctor. Therefore, candidates should learn to regulate their psychology.

Treat the college entrance examination as an opportunity to "display one's talent," seize the opportunity, turn passivity into initiative, let smiles appear on our faces, let self-confidence accompany us, and let the brilliant sunshine illuminate our hearts. In this way, the pressure in our hearts will naturally be released. Senior three is a beautiful segment of life, dividing innocence from youth, harvesting academics as well as growth.

II. Exam Preparation

1. Knowledge Preparation

We have already completed this task quite well. Regarding middle school knowledge, you are now all-knowing.

2. Physical Preparation

Your body has always been in great shape. Even if some students have minor issues now, they will be fine by the time of the college entrance examination. During the exam period, we need to ensure that we eat enough at every meal and pay attention to our sleep. How to have a good night's rest? Follow these points:

Ensure sufficient sleep. Guarantee at least 30 minutes of nap every day and 7-8 hours of overall sleep. There are individual differences in sleep; short-sleepers can sleep less, but still need enough sleep. Enough sleep means feeling refreshed after waking up, not feeling drowsy during the day, and being energetic.

Sleep on schedule. This is the key to sleeping well. Most people who don't sleep well often fail to stick to a regular sleep schedule. Go to bed at the same time every day, wake up at the fixed time, strictly follow the routine, and don't change it arbitrarily. This way, the biological clock will "instruct" you to sleep and wake up every day, ensuring high-quality sleep. Maintain the adjusted school routine during the college entrance examination period:

Time Content Arrangement Morning 6:30-7:00 Wake Up 7:00 Breakfast, rest a bit after breakfast 7:50 Depart for the exam venue 8:45-11:30 Exam Afternoon 12:00 Lunch 12:30-1:30 Nap (strive for 30-60 minutes of sleep) 1:50-2:00 Depart for the exam venue 2:45-5:00 Exam 5:30 Dinner, arrange appropriate time after dinner to rest the brain Evening 7:30-8:30 Appropriate study, read books, etc. 9:15-9:40 Bedtime (strive to enter the sleep state before 10 PM)

Methods for dealing with insomnia: Have a correct understanding of insomnia. Occasional insomnia does not harm you much, and you should still adhere to a regular schedule, not go to bed early, and wake up on time in the morning. Secondly, face insomnia calmly. After occasional insomnia, do not have a "desire to sleep" mentality. Do not think that insomnia last night means you have insomnia syndrome and won't be able to sleep tonight. The correct approach is to relax physically and mentally, close your eyes to rest, and sleepiness will come unexpectedly.

To deal with pre-exam insomnia, you can try the following five strategies:

(1) Do not think about what might happen during the college entrance examination, do not set unrealistic requirements for yourself, and form optimistic emotions.

(2) Eat appropriately before sleeping. Candidates can drink a glass of milk or eat an apple before going to bed, which promotes sleep. However, note that you should not eat a large amount of watermelon or drink too many beverages before bedtime, as this increases nighttime urination frequency, affecting sleep quality.

(3) Soak your feet in hot water before sleeping and massage your head and feet. Soak your feet in a basin of hot water for 15 minutes, adding hot water when the water cools down; or comb your hair from front to back for 5-10 minutes, which helps sleep.

(4) Turn off the lights and lie flat on the bed, “sleeping the heart first, then the eyes.” Close your eyes naturally, breathe deeply repeatedly, and listen to some light music.

Even if you haven’t slept well before the exam, it won’t significantly affect the exam. The day before the college entrance examination, don’t go to bed too early or too late, just like usual. If you don’t sleep well at night, don’t worry. Sleep affects people's energy levels, but the greatest impact of poor sleep on the exam is not sleep itself but self-suggestion. According to years of tracking the pre-exam state of candidates, candidates often get inspiration and clear-headedness upon entering the exam room, allowing the exam to proceed smoothly and perform well. If you really didn’t sleep well, as long as you treat it with a normal attitude, it won’t have a big impact.

Regulation methods for insomnia:

First method: After getting into bed and turning off the light, lie down on your back, take a deep breath comfortably, and exhale slowly. On the second inhalation, silently count the number "1", and as you exhale, silently tell yourself "relax." Meanwhile, try imagining a blackboard in front of you, and write the numbers "1" and "relax" on it. On the next inhalation, silently count the number "2", and as you exhale, silently tell yourself "relax." Continue this process until you reach the number "20", each deep breath imagining hearing and seeing those numbers and the word "relax." The purpose of this is to repeatedly occupy your thoughts with words and images that do not excite your emotions. If your thoughts remain continuously listening and watching this series of monotonous words, then exam anxiety and tension will not easily invade your mind. In this process, you may sometimes wander off-topic. At this point, continue following the original steps persistently. Eventually, your thoughts will enter a relaxed state, and sleep will come.

Second method: After getting into bed and turning off the light, lie down on your back, first take 3-5 deep breaths, then imagine a dim white dot in the darkness and concentrate on controlling this imagined white dot to move in slow circular motions 50 times, then switch to drawing the trajectory of a star 50 times. If you feel little change in your mood, repeat the above thought movement procedures several times, then perform two more deep, slow breaths. Each time you perform a deep, slow breath, give yourself a suggestion: "I am already asleep." This can effectively induce sleep. The principle of this method is consistent with the principle of the first method.

During normal times and exam periods, you should cultivate the habit of taking naps. Naps generally last half an hour, preferably not exceeding half an hour. This allows the brain to rest and the mind to quickly become clear upon waking.

Third method: After lying down on the bed, do not force yourself to fall asleep, stay in a subconscious state. Do not overly focus on whether you are falling asleep, think about some relaxing things; tell yourself that you will definitely succeed, and there is no need to be anxious. Secondly, imagine yourself floating on water, tell yourself that you are currently very comfortable and will soon fall asleep. With positive psychological suggestions, your emotions will relax, making it easier to fall asleep.

If the symptoms of insomnia are severe, medication can be used under the guidance of a doctor. Generally, short-acting sedatives are preferred, and special attention must be paid to prevent residual sleepiness from affecting the next day's revision or exam. Candidates who have never used sedatives should not use them during the college entrance examination.

III. Examination Precautions

1. Schedule for the day before the exam.

① Stop all preparatory activities to allow the brain its most precious "pre-battle" quiet rest time, "conserving energy." It is important to understand this simple truth: "A bowstring stretched to its limit cannot shoot far." Only when the bowstring is tightened at the moment of shooting can it unleash immense power. If you are still uneasy, you can review familiar parts of the knowledge.

② Stop intense mental labor, let your body and mind relax and be in the best tranquil and pleasant state, listen to music, chat, do calisthenics, consciously forget the upcoming battle, and instead imagine the most wonderful things.

③ Go to bed on time and ensure adequate sleep that night. Go to bed at the usual time on June 6th, do not go to bed too early. After the morning exam on July 7th, rest for a while, have lunch. Do not immediately sleep after lunch, do some light exercise, listen to music, and take a nap for 30 to 40 minutes.

2. Things to do before entering the exam room

① Arrive at the exam room at an appropriate time. Arriving too early increases the possibility of encountering unexpected events, which could easily disrupt a good mindset. Arriving too late leaves insufficient time to calm down and adjust to the exam role, possibly resulting in unnecessary mistakes throughout the entire exam.

② Check the admission ticket and other exam tools. Ten minutes before leaving home, listen to a lively piece of light music, which can make you happy and activate your thinking. While enjoying the music, check if the admission ticket and stationery are all ready. Upon reaching outside the exam room, check the admission ticket again. Do not check the admission ticket on the way to ensure safety.

③ Walk slowly and avoid talking. On the way to the exam room, walk slowly to avoid accelerating your heartbeat and causing emotional tension. Do not wear headphones to listen to music to ensure safety.

④ Waiting outside the exam room. Before entering the exam room, do not engage in high-level discussions, as this could make you overly excited and then low-spirited once inside the exam room. Also, do not discuss revisions or questions with others, as this could ruin the "confident" feeling you had. Rest quietly outside the exam room, do not make noise, and do not read books. Seeing others reading should not make you envious or nervous; believe that you already know everything. If you have time, look at the scenery or listen to music.

⑤ Critical times may bring various unexpected events. First, be mentally prepared. If something unfortunate happens, do not take it too seriously. Tell yourself that whatever happens, it will pass. As long as you can participate in the exam, you can perform at your level and achieve good results. Protect your admission ticket. Avoid excessive stress in the days leading up to the exam, such as restlessness, anxiety, etc., and do not exhaust yourself by cramming in the final few days out of fear.

3. Examination Adjustments

(1) Regulation of Emotions in the Exam Room

① Smile and enter the exam room calmly, do not rush in. Candidates should use the time before the exam to adjust their emotions, stabilize their mental state, and boost their confidence.

② After entering the exam room, do not discuss problems with classmates, and do not play around. Let your spirit adapt to the exam environment and eliminate nervous emotions.

Method: Recall the solution methods of a question that you found interesting and did well during your revision, or silently memorize concepts in your mind to shift your attention away from irrelevant thoughts and towards the exam context. Accurate recall of problems can reinforce your self-confidence, accelerate the formation of a good mindset, and save answering time. For students with poorer grades, forget the gaps between yourself and others, stand on the same starting line with everyone, and build self-confidence. Self-identification and self-encouragement, constantly telling yourself "I am very capable."
