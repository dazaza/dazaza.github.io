The Birth of the Black Warrior

McTi Suen has always been full of business acumen since childhood and understands the principle that doing business leads to prosperity. Thus, he studied hard and completed his MBA at Stanford University in the United States through his efforts, and looked for opportunities there.

After graduation, Suen saw the potential of Ho Man Tin's company and took the initiative to recommend himself to Man Tin, frankly stating that he didn't want to be an employee but only a partner, willing even if he only had 0.01% of the shares. Seeing that Suen was different from others, Man Tin allowed Suen to join as a partner. Their relationship was both teacher and friend, working seamlessly together, specializing in acquiring companies and selling them off, becoming famous snipers in the American business world.

Later, Suen became the well-known Black Warrior in the business world, ruthless and merciless, gradually growing in power. Similarly, the divergence of opinions between Suen and Man Tin increased. For the benefit and development of the company, Suen decided to fully acquire Man Tin's company. Unable to bear the blow, Man Tin drove drunk, and Suen learned that Man Tin's car lost control and fell into the sea. After the incident, Man Tin's body could not be found, but Suen believed that Man Tin was dead and felt extremely guilty. Is the value of a deal really greater than friendship, life? Suen reflected on everything in the past and decided not to be the Black Warrior anymore, withdrawing from the business world. Suen learned from the newspaper that Man Tin's wife and children were living in difficult conditions in Hong Kong. Suen believed it was his responsibility and decided to return to Hong Kong to take care of Man Tin's family.
