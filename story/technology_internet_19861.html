Li Shanyou Secures $10 Million in Venture Capital, Reappears with Ku6 to Partner with Baidu

by xiaopang on 2007-05-20 09:28:08

On May 18th, the chairman and CEO of the Web2.0 video sharing site Ku6.com, Li Shanyou, announced that on May 17th, Ku6.com had reached a cooperative relationship with Baidu. Ku6.com will provide exclusive video service support for Baidu Tieba. Users of Baidu Tieba will be able to directly add video content through Ku6.com when posting in the forum, but they must become registered users of Ku6.com before doing so. Li Shanyou revealed that the cooperation with Baidu would "last at least two years." At the same time, Li Shanyou announced that Ku6.com had received millions of dollars in venture capital.

The style is closer to media

Ku6.com currently has about 20,000 video uploads per day, which is similar to the daily update volume of Sohu.com where Li Shanyou used to work. With such a large upload volume, how to reduce video duplication and improve the accuracy of advertising placement is a challenge. In response, Li Shanyou told Hexun IT that the proportion of original videos on domestic video sharing sites is on average 10%, while Ku6.com's is 15%. Relatively speaking, the repetition caused by reprints has already been relatively low, and the impact of repetition on content is not significant. The key is that the occupation of storage space has a great impact on cost control. Ku6.com solved this problem through technology from the beginning. The backend can identify duplicate uploaded videos and directly call up the same video uploaded for the first time when netizens watch it.

Li Shanyou stated that the homepage style of Ku6.com is closer to media, adopting a channel-based content classification method. Advertisers can classify and place ads according to channels. As for a more refined ad classification system, this is not only the technology that Ku6.com will fully develop, but also the direction that the entire video sharing industry strives for.

Not worried about being copied

Li Shanyou introduced that the core business model of Ku6.com includes "making money together", sharing advertising revenue with netizens based on click volume; "everyone does creative work", targeting the needs of advertisers, requiring video producers to embed customer product or brand elements into the video when soliciting video creativity. Li Shanyou told Hexun IT that doing video sharing "is not as easy as it sounds", and he is not worried about other companies, including Baidu, copying its business model.

Millions of dollars in venture capital

Ku6.com started development in 2006 and went online at the beginning of September. By the end of 2006, the initial investment of 2 million RMB had been recovered with a slight profit. Li Shanyou claimed that among many domestic video sharing websites, Ku6.com is the first video website to achieve scale income through its own capabilities, and has already earned tens of millions of advertising revenue. Ku6.com's advertising clients include Yili, Lenovo, Hisense, Aigo, Samsung, Motorola, Nokia, McDonald's, Disney and other companies.

Li Shanyou revealed that the American DFJ (Draper Fisher Jurvetson) Venture Capital Company and DT Capital China Investment Fund will invest tens of millions of dollars in Ku6.com. DFJ once invested in Baidu, K-net Mobile, and Focus Media in China, and DT Capital once invested in Wuxi Suntech.

On April 17, 2006, Li Shanyou resigned from Sohu. Previously, he served as Senior Vice President and Chief Editor of Sohu.com.
