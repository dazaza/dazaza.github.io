If Manchester City does not make it to the top four this season, Hughes will definitely be fired. Mourinho and Benitez are on the list to take over.

by kersfe3f23 on 2009-12-09 09:28:09

If Manchester City does not make it to the semi-finals this season, Hughes will definitely be sacked, with Mourinho and Benitez on the shortlist to take over. After defeating Chelsea, Manchester City has climbed to sixth place in the rankings despite having played one less round. With their strong performance in the League Cup, Manchester City's progress towards their goals is relatively smooth, but they are not entirely without problems. Their absolute starting left-back, Bridge, was injured by Belletti during the match against Chelsea. According to reports from the Daily Mail after the game, Bridge will be absent for at least three weeks due to a medial collateral ligament injury in his knee.

From the game footage, it was clear that although Bridge largely avoided Belletti's tackle, he still got hit on the knee. He immediately clutched his knee and fell to the ground, subsequently being carried off the field on a stretcher. Hughes expressed his hope after the game that his key player wouldn't be too seriously injured, and currently, resting for three weeks seems to be a fortunate outcome given the circumstances.

In this game, goalkeeper Given left a deep impression on everyone. Combined with the sympathetic scene that occurred with the Irish team not long ago during the World Cup qualifiers, many people's evaluations of this goalkeeper have risen significantly in recent days. Mirror columnist McDonnell even stated: "Given is not only Manchester City's best acquisition, but simply the best goalkeeper in the league!"

Below is the essence of McDonnell's article: “Among all the big-name players bought with large sums of money, there is one person who Mark Hughes has used most efficiently, and yet he was the cheapest acquisition. Shay Given, who cost only 6 million pounds, represents an extremely significant value. It can be said that his worth far exceeds that of Robinho, Tevez, Adebayor, Leskot, and Toure. Saving Lampard's penalty kick greatly helped Manchester City defeat Chelsea, and this was just the latest heroic act by the Irish goalkeeper.”

McDonnell compares Given to Schmeichel, saying: “Hughes admitted after the game that Given shares some similarities with his old teammate Schmeichel. They create a certain atmosphere on the field, and convey the message to the defensive line that with them guarding the goal, the last line of defense is secure. Ferguson once said that Schmeichel, in terms of points, was worth about 10 to 12 points per season, and Given is no different in this regard. When Manchester City's newly cobbled-together defensive line was still lost, the 33-year-old Given helped his team countless times. We must thank his performance; fairly speaking, he has already earned Manchester City 7 to 8 more points.”

McDonnell concludes: “In modern football matches, a reliable goalkeeper is truly a godsend, so Hughes was right to sign Given. As a goalkeeper, at 33 years old, he is precisely at the peak of his career. Few goalkeepers can control the will of penalty takers, but in the penalty duel with Lampard, Given achieved a victory in terms of mental willpower. Therefore, without a doubt, in terms of current form, he is the best goalkeeper in the Premier League.”

Finally, there is a rumor that might disturb Mark Hughes slightly. The People newspaper revealed that the Manchester City, equipped with vast amounts of money, is what the Middle Eastern consortium hopes to see achieve results like Chelsea did in the 04-05 season. At the very least, they want to enter the Champions League by the end of the season, and this minimum goal is also the premise for whether Hughes can continue in his coaching position. If this goal is not reached, his job security will immediately be at risk.

In fact, Manchester City's management already has a potential list of coaches, all of whom are non-British. Among them are Hiddink and Mourinho, who have already proven themselves capable in the Premier League, and even the current Reds coach, Benitez, is on the list, along with Rijkaard.

A strong entry into the semi-finals of the League Cup, defeating Chelsea in the league, and having already faced each of the four major clubs at least once this season (only losing to Manchester United in a game with an extended stoppage time) has temporarily secured Hughes' position, ensuring his job safety in the short term. Of course, achieving these goals is still insufficient. The previous seven consecutive draws in the league greatly angered the upper management, regardless of home or away games, as they cannot keep drawing with every team. As a result, they are currently only ranked sixth, still some distance from the top four positions that would grant them a spot in the Champions League. Manchester City needs to learn how to earn 3 points from weaker teams; merely defeating a few major clubs will not play a decisive role in the final standings, and Hughes' position will still be precarious.
