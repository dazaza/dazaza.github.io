Nanning Sugar Industry: Rising sugar prices boost company performance_ Stock Forum

by dx0966632 on 2009-12-03 22:38:18

Nanning Sugar Industry (000911) (enter the stock bar, new version of market conditions) surged strongly in the early session today, but eventually saw a narrowing of gains due to the drag of the overall market decline. By the close, it rose 6.49%, with a full-day turnover rate of 7.12%. The company is the largest state-owned listed company in China's sugar industry. The sugarcane industry to which the company belongs is a sector supported by the state, and it is also a pillar industry of Guangxi and Nanning. For a long time, it has enjoyed various preferential policies from the state. The comprehensive earnings per share forecast for the company from 2009-2011 are 0.38 yuan, 0.76 yuan, and 0.64 yuan respectively, corresponding to dynamic price-to-earnings ratios of 63, 32, and 37; currently, there are 24 analysts tracking, 2 giving "strong buy" ratings, 18 giving "buy" ratings, and 4 giving "hold" ratings, with a composite rating coefficient of 2.08.

The supply in the sugar market will still be tight in 2009-2010, and domestic sugar prices will remain in an upward cycle. It is expected that sugar production in the next two years will be around 12.5-13 million tons, basically flat compared to the previous season. In the third quarter of 2009, the company achieved operating revenue of 695 million yuan, up 25.57% year-on-year, operating profit of 43 million yuan, up 6852.34% year-on-year, net profit attributable to the parent company of 37 million yuan, achieving earnings per share of 0.13 yuan. The gross margin for the first three quarters was 18.81%, up 0.16 percentage points year-on-year, and up 2.1 percentage points compared to the first half of the year, with the company's profitability continuing to recover.

According to the quarterly report disclosure, the old sugar inventory nationwide for the 08/09 season is only about 3 million tons, which is not much pressure compared to normal years. If the start of the 09/10 season is delayed, there may be a slightly tighter supply-demand situation, and sugar prices in the fourth quarter will continue to rise slowly. The planting area in Guangxi for the 09/10 season remains unchanged, while other regions across the country continue to reduce output. Therefore, the variable in the new season's output lies in Guangxi's yield per unit area. Judging from the current growth trend, limited by the planting area, it is almost impossible for the national sugar output in the new season to increase significantly year-on-year. There is a supply-demand gap of over 1 million tons in the 08/09 season, and the gap in the new season will be close to this number.

For the 09/10 season, the company's sugarcane planting area remains basically unchanged at 1.08 million mu. Thus, the production of refined sugar in the new season mainly depends on the yield per unit area of sugarcane and the sugar content rate. According to reports from various professional websites, despite the sustained drought and less rain in southern provinces since July this year, the impact on the Nanning region has been relatively small, and the growth of sugarcane is slightly better than last year, with the sugar content rate likely to be slightly higher than last year. Shanxi Securities estimates that the company's sugar production in the 09/10 season could reach 600,000 tons, and sugar sales from 2009-2011 are expected to remain at the level of 600,000 tons.

Huatai Securities believes that sugar is comparable to "colored" varieties in agricultural products, but demand is more rigid than colored commodities. Since a reduction in production for the next season in China and even globally has become inevitable, the data on both supply and demand ends, as well as inventory data, are relatively transparent, and the growth of the sugar-making industry is certain. Given that the planting area in Guangxi may be lower than market expectations, the supply-demand relationship next year will be more favorable, maintaining a "recommend" rating.

Risk factors: (1) Risk of sharp declines in international market sugar prices; (2) Risk of reduced yields caused by natural disasters.
