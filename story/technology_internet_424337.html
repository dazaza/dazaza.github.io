College Entrance Exam Admission: Crossing the Middle and High School Exams Together with Your Child

by gaokao203 on 2011-05-04 14:59:45

On April 30, the 2011 pre-exam psychological adjustment seminar for the high school entrance exam and college entrance exam, hosted by the Education Weekly of our newspaper, was successfully held in the conference hall on the 20th floor of the Jinan Daily Building. Nearly 300 students and parents came to the venue and exchanged views with experts on issues such as reducing the pressure of the high school entrance exam and college entrance exam for children and how to adjust the mindset of their children.

This pre-exam psychological adjustment seminar for the high school entrance exam and college entrance exam was presented by Mr. Yin Yongsheng, a counselor of the Shandong Provincial Behavioral Science Society, an expert in pre-exam tutoring for the high school entrance exam and college entrance exam, and the chief mentor of the NLP Family Management Premium Course. Mr. Yin has been engaged in education for nearly 20 years and is skilled in training middle school students' learning methods, cultivating learning habits, improving learning efficiency, and igniting learning motivation. Based on his many years of teaching practice, he provided a detailed analysis for the students and parents of Jinan regarding the guidance of the final review for the high school entrance exam and the adjustment of students' mindsets.

This seminar mainly targeted candidates and parents who are about to face the high school entrance exam and college entrance exam, as well as second-year junior high school and second-year senior high school students and their parents who are about to enter the preparation stage. During the meeting, many parents reflected that their children experienced excessive pressure during the preparation process, couldn't find suitable learning methods, often got distracted in class, and were easily irritable, leaving the parents feeling helpless. Some parents even said they would be willing to spend any amount of money if it could solve their children's psychological problems and adjust their mindset. In response to these issues, Mr. Yin Yongsheng conducted an in-depth exchange with the parents and students of Quancheng.
