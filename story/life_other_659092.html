Unforgettable memories that are hard to erase

by lili1 on 2012-02-05 11:40:35

Hard-to-buy memories

As long as you have money, you can buy anything; without money, nothing is possible... This statement seems to make a lot of sense. Women have always believed this to be the truth and have used it as their standard for everything.

A woman found a boyfriend, a very wealthy one. She felt that she loved this person dearly and enjoyed being affectionate. In front of this man, she was almost completely unrestrained. The man also liked the woman's freedom and casualness. During their time together, the man spoiled the woman: when they went shopping and she got tired, he would carry her home on his back, not caring about the envious or disdainful looks from others; after returning home, he would proactively cook the dishes that the woman loved most; even washing clothes, the man wouldn't let the woman do it... The woman felt very happy, but her love for the man was very demanding: she didn't allow him to play cards, chat on QQ, or talk with other women... It seemed to stem from a strong sense of insecurity within herself. The woman hoped that the man only knew her, that he belonged solely to her. But in reality, living in society means living in a group, and no one can detach themselves from this group. The man felt very unhappy, feeling that he was tightly controlled by the woman and had no personal space or freedom. He decided to tell the woman: "Let's break up. I can hardly accept such constraints."

The woman clearly understood that this man no longer wanted to live with her. But deep down, she told herself: even if he had no money, she still loved him, still enjoyed being with him, and was very dependent on him. She was just afraid of losing him, so she tightly held onto the glass ball of love, never expecting that the glass ball of love would break because she gripped it too tightly. The woman said: "Alright, if you want freedom, I'll give it to you. What will you trade for it?" The man thought for a long time and said: "What do you need?" The woman said: "I need money, buy out our feelings with money!" The man said: "Alright then! Hopefully, this money will help you." After saying this, the man left.

And thus, love was bought out, but the woman's memories were deeply etched in her heart. Whenever she was lonely, she would recall the scenes of strolling with the man; whenever she missed the tobacco scent on the man's body, she would light a cigarette and greedily suck it like a child sucking its mother's milk; whenever she finished her business and had some leisure time, a faint sadness would appear on her face, leaving her unsure of what to do next... The man left behind money, but also all the memories. The woman bought out her feelings, but she couldn't buy out her memories.

In fact, in life, money is indispensable, but it's not omnipotent. Just like the woman's memories, they are irreplaceable. If at first, the woman hadn't chosen to buy out her feelings, but instead invested her own emotions and truly cherished her man, perhaps the man wouldn't have left, and the memories left behind wouldn't torment her so much...
