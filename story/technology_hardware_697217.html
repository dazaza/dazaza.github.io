by ledlamp8 on 2012-03-08 14:13:36

Sichuan Rainbow possesses the most complete domestic PMOLED production line, also has an AMOLED panel and PMOLED panel experimental line, and plans to build a 4.5-generation AMOLED production line before the end of 2013. In June this year, China’s OLED Industry Alliance was formally established, with Sichuan Rainbow becoming the first Union Co-Chair. In the domestic field of OLED display technology, Sichuan Rainbow has been at the forefront of the industry. Currently, domestic PMOLED display technology development has become more mature, but there are still some gaps in AMOLED mass production compared to Korean and Japanese companies. Korean and Japanese companies have focused on large-size OLED production line layout, while Rainbow, as said OLED lane layout, Sichuan Rainbow, aims to achieve two kinds of two-lane layout in the OLED industry. OLED display technology is regarded as the greatest potential for development of new flat panel display technology after LCD and PDP, and has gained attention from many companies due to its outstanding performance advantages. According to experts, OLED display technology based on drive mode can be divided into two categories: passive matrix driven OLED, also known as PMOLED, and actively driving OLED, also known as AMOLED. Sichuan Rainbow has laid out both technologies in the OLED industry. Sichuan Rainbow possesses the most complete domestic PMOLED production line, with an annual designed capacity of 12 million (1-inch screens), mainly meeting the demand for producing their own products. Since starting production in April 2010, the production line product yield has exceeded 90%. It is understood that Rainbow also has an AMOLED panel test line and a PMOLED experimental line. Currently, China’s PMOLED display technology has developed more maturely, and basically caught up with international advanced levels in terms of technology and industry. Sichuan Rainbow, along with Visionox, has achieved PMOLED mass production, and AMOLED display technology can be seen as providing a reference for AMOLED panel technology based on PMOLED's technological foundation. However, in the large-scale industrialization of AMOLED, domestic enterprises still have many shortcomings, and domestic manufacturers will place AMOLED technology R&D in the primary position. A relevant person in charge of Sichuan Rainbow told the China Electronics News in an interview that it is understood, Sichuan Rainbow is a joint venture company of Changhong Group and Chengdu Municipal Government. The official told reporters that the company's layout in the OLED industry is an inevitable transition for Changhong Group to move from high-end manufacturing assembly to core display manufacturing, to master the right to define electronic information products, and to seize the ownership of key resources in the industry chain, laying the foundation. For large sizes, domestic enterprises should not blindly follow but must fully consider their own funds, technology, talent actuality, not blindly obeying. Not long ago, Samsung Electronics announced that the 8th generation OLED production line would be put into use in May next year, with OLED panels applications expanding to large sizes. In addition, Korean and Japanese companies like Matsushita and LGD are planning to enter the field of large-size OLED panels. Regarding this, an official from the company said: In 2009, combined with their own actual situation, they developed a development path and product planning that meets their own needs, including the development and production of large-size OLED panels. Sichuan Rainbow has completed the development of 7.6-inch products, is developing around 10-inch products, and has planned R&D for 14-inch class and 20-inch-class products, aiming to develop AMOLED screen TV products from portable products. Currently, many companies are involved in the field of OLED panels, but basically concentrated in the small and medium size market, relatively lacking in the core technology of large-size AMOLED. For the construction of large-size AMOLED production lines, a person in charge of Rainbow told reporters that as early as the end of 2009, they proceeded to develop appropriate planning, taking into account the technical risks of the AMOLED mass production line, equipment risk factors. Rainbow has repeatedly demonstrated these. He said, in August of this year, Rainbow convened experts for the final round of technical feasibility studies, and plans to build a 4.5-generation AMOLED production line before the end of 2013. It is understood that the 4.5 generation AMOLED production line is currently the world’s most advanced OLED production line, with only a few companies in the world having such a production line and technology. There is a large gap between domestic enterprises and Japanese and Korean companies in key materials and equipment. Domestic OLED technology started almost the same time as Japanese and Korean companies, but the pace of development cannot keep up with the pace of Japanese and Korean companies. An official from the company told reporters that domestic enterprises have somewhat smaller gaps in panel technology grasp compared to foreign countries, but there is a big gap in key materials and equipment technology. For key materials and equipment, domestic raw material and equipment enterprises are closely cooperating with panel enterprises, but the core technology of these critical materials and equipment still lies in the hands of foreign enterprises. Except for some materials capable of production, energy-saving lamps, domestic enterprises basically do not have independent R&D and production capabilities. Looking at several enterprises in mainland China producing OLED panels, there is a serious lack of product support. According to the person in charge of Rainbow, most of the mass production materials used still rely on imports from Japan, South Korea, and Taiwan, China. Mainland Chinese enterprises can provide small quantities of products, but the overall product performance is far from the required continuous improvement in specific technical specifications, performance, reliability. He said that for the development of the OLED industry, mainland Chinese enterprises need to continuously strengthen low-temperature polysilicon TFT technology, large-area deposition technology, high reliability, packaging technology R&D and innovation to enhance core patent competitiveness. At the same time, enterprises should strengthen the construction of supporting industries. Key materials in the upstream equipment for capacity, mainland China is less competitive, especially in high-end manufacturing equipment and supporting capacity, more missing. In addition, a number of enterprises, including Rainbow, BOE, Cigna, Pegasus, Rainbow, etc., have indicated that the OLED industry becoming bigger and stronger cannot be done without active policy guidance and support. In June this year, under the support of relevant state departments, China’s OLED Industry Alliance was formally established. The Alliance is composed of 19 domestic enterprises with greater influence in the field of OLED screens, materials, equipment, machines, covering the entire OLED industrial chain, which will better promote the development of the OLED industry, localization of materials, equipment, and other aspects, and promote the industry chain getting more sophisticated.
