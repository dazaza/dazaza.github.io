"Marry Me" - The Thorny Emotional Journey of a Widow

by jqrxass on 2009-09-30 15:19:41

A single woman who has lost her husband quietly waits in loneliness and solitude, as the men in her life take their turns to appear on stage. A desperate abandoned wife struggles with life in different ways, whether looking back or moving forward, she is unwilling to escape from her original mindset. The young sisters who are caught up in emotional whirlpools throw themselves resolutely into an unknown field with all their spirit and life - that is, a man's family. The wings of love carry the heavy burden of the flesh, unable to bear the weight.

Fate connects their lives with men in unimaginable ways, and thus everything begins: honesty and deception, conscience and morality, love and family ties, entanglement and rupture...

When life appears in the form of trivial complexities, when indifference and obedience become the main ways to numb the nerves, when the aimless floating of the soul becomes the norm of life, when the persistence of love becomes a legend, and when selfless giving becomes a joke, who still believes that love is the only eternal thing that can exist in this world?

However, those passions and dreams that have been impacted, overturned, and destroyed actually continue to make noise deep in the heart, making one unable to be calm. Those hidden pains, despairs, helplessnesses, and those deep fears of the future that cannot be grasped, we must face them one by one instead of just passing by.

"Marry Me" Part One

Xiao Lin in the office was authoritative, naturally commanding respect without anger. Most of her hair was combed backward, gathered into a beautiful bun at the back, with no decoration on it, elegantly tied with a black ribbon, leaving no trace of being tied. But at her forehead, there were neatly cut bangs, jet black, sticking straight above her eyebrows. Her eyebrows slightly arched, making her eyes look sharp and cold. She liked to use the same cold light purple lipstick, lightly applying a layer, seemingly pursuing that kind of calm color.

"Marry Me" Part Two

In fact, An Qingqing felt very hopeless. During this period, she bought a lot of magazines, women's magazines, of course, and some women's readings, guides for urban women's emotional lives. These were about the living conditions and strategies of abandoned wives. An Qingqing spent all her free time studying these texts. Later, she sadly found that the magazines had collectively run out of ideas. She even once suspected that the editors were all morons, could they come up with any new tricks?

"Marry Me" Part Three

The sky became darker and darker, the air warm and humid, and snowflakes began to fall sporadically in the sky. People outside the window hurried home or rushed to the ward. Xiao Lin placed the narcissus flowers by the bedside, hung two lanterns by the window, and suddenly, the ward had a New Year's atmosphere. Mao Mao held Wang Fan's hand by the bed, telling him the stories and jokes he had once told.

After arranging all this, Xiao Lin started the normal care for Wang Fan again.

"Marry Me" Part Four

She recalled every detail of what happened bit by bit from start to end, from when she met Zhengwei, to chatting, to his inexplicable disappearance for a period, then reconnecting, then her coming to Hangzhou, and then meeting Xiao Lin. Everything, yes, just like Mao Mao said, that Zhengwei was Xiao Lin. This Zhengwei who left messages for herself every now and then was not the original Zhengwei but his wife, Xiao Lin. According to An Qingqing's account, the time Jiang Zhengwei passed away matched the time he disappeared.
