Algorithm: Baidu's latest search results favor its own products

by longersing on 2009-08-25 18:31:12

By Nangong Weiguo

Baidu, as the leading Chinese search engine, has always been committed to developing a search engine that meets the needs of ordinary people. While pursuing fairness and impartiality in search results, it has also been competing with Google and Yahoo in terms of algorithm accuracy for retrieval. This year, Baidu has been quite active, making several significant changes to its search function, which were clearly mentioned in our previous articles "Baidu Attempts to Become a Global Search Engine" and "Major Changes in Baidu's Search Engine Retrieval Methods Over the Past Two Months." Today, we will discuss Baidu's preference for its own products through the search results of a few entertainment celebrities.

One of the more popular entertainment events this year is Hunan TV's "Happy Girl," so let's search using some of the contestants' names to see how Baidu presents its search results.

In the search results, the first ranking is marked as "Promotion" from Baidu Entertainment. It's unclear why Baidu chooses to present its own product at the top in the form of "Promotion." Does this mean that Baidu lacks confidence in the natural ranking of this entertainment page? Therefore, it adopts an artificial promotion method to make it rank higher?

The second ranking is related news pages updated by Baidu earlier this year. This phenomenon only started this year; in previous years, Baidu would only display such information separately in Baidu News. Now, like Google, it includes relevant news in the regular web search results.

The third ranking is Baidu Baike (Baidu Encyclopedia), which being in the top three is unsurprising. In natural search results, Baidu Baike consistently ranks high for various keywords. The fourth result is Baidu Tieba (Baidu Forum). Unless Tieba is temporarily closed, it always highlights itself.

On the first visible page of our search results, we see four of Baidu's own products and one natural ranking. A year ago, we might have only found this phenomenon in Baidu's paid promotion advertisements. But now, it appears in normal name searches.

For the public seeking fair, accurate, and truthful search results, has Baidu considered providing 100% accurate information? Otherwise, forcing its own products into the search results will only increase users' aversion.

We found similar situations when searching for other entertainment personalities. We hope Baidu can provide more authentic, accurate, and fair search results to everyone because people use Baidu due to their trust in it, support for domestic search engines, and most importantly, the need to find the information they are looking for.
