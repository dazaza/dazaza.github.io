Baidu's entrepreneurial history "Believe in China"

Author: Liang Dong. "It's just an exam for Yangquan No.1 Middle School, how big of a deal is it!" Li Yanghong, born in Yangquan, Shanxi Province, did not think he had great ambitions as a child. Like all the children who were wild at heart, he was always busy every day, but it was all about "following the crowd". Li Yanghong's parents worked at Jindong Chemical Plant, and their family background was very ordinary. Li Yanghong has three sisters and one younger sister. When he was young, he often played games like throwing sandbags and skipping ropes with his sisters. His shyness and gentleness might be related to the love from his sisters.

Li Yanghong's father often took him to watch opera movies, which made him once obsessed with operas. His stage was small, just the bed at home. He often wrapped the bedsheet around his waist as a battle skirt, using a stick as a gun, playing on the bed. He constantly appeared on the "stage" alone. When the Shanxi Yangquan Jin Opera Troupe recruited students, he went and performed what he had practiced at home. Although he only learned a few moves from the movies, the teachers from the theater still saw a kind of talent in his movements and decided to admit him. If this growth plan had been persisted, people could have expected a Peking Opera elite to emerge from Yangquan, Shanxi. However, another assumption should not be overlooked: when the curtain opened, the first person to jump out and do somersaults might be a young Li Yanghong whose talent was seriously wasted.

Fortunately, Li Yanghong's interest in opera had already faded by then. In the planned economy era, everything depended on connections. As parents, they had no backdoor to knock on. At the dinner table, the father began to guide the children to make personal growth plans. The result of the planning was that there was only one path ahead - if you wanted to find a good job in the future, you had to go to university. Li Yanghong's eldest sister entered university in 1977, right after the resumption of the college entrance examination, drawing envy from neighbors. Compared to learning opera, attending school seemed more traditional and thus more attractive.

For Li Yanghong's study of opera, we can provide another interpretation: he actually liked the stage more than the opera itself. Only on the stage could he display his talents and influence more people through every move. This aligns with Baidu's current commitment to the concept of "using technology to change life." For a child, wanting to exert maximum influence was natural, but at that time, he hadn't found the appropriate way. After starting school, Li Yanghong attended the children's school of Jindong Chemical Plant in Yangquan, Shanxi. Back then, the teaching level of most state-owned units' children schools was average because there wasn't much pressure for higher education; older children could attend technical schools and then take over their parents' jobs. People didn't expect their children to enter provincial key high schools such as Yangquan No.1 Middle School.

Li Yanghong's studies were acceptable; his essays were often used as model essays by his teachers. Before the high school entrance exam, Li Yanghong became obsessed with chess. In the cool alleys, a group of people would gather around giving advice on moving pawns and knights. Later, his junior high school teacher told him that before his third year, he never studied and was always seen playing chess. To this, Li Yanghong was completely unaware. He thought of himself as a diligent and hardworking student, but strangely, others saw him as the type who didn’t study much.
