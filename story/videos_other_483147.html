by fdwhw8888 on 2011-08-30 10:29:05

Asia Television (ATV) has once again been embroiled in controversy, with suspicions arising over management's alleged interference in the news department's operations. Starting today, some staff members have been placed on leave. It is reported that senior management of ATV's news department is dissatisfied with recent actions by upper management, specifically regarding the designation of a financial program host as a "financial anchor." There are also concerns that management had intentions to air segments from an advertising-laden financial program during one of the news department’s financial shows. Yesterday, during an employee meeting, the senior news management confronted upper management, expressing their opposition to anyone using the news department programs for personal financial gain.

The latest conflict between the news department and management stems from the Sunday evening financial program "Stepping into Listed Companies" and its host, Yan Li. It is understood that "Stepping into Listed Companies" is sponsored by listed companies, making it akin to an advertising program. Yan Li, who was previously employed at a foreign exchange company, caught ATV's attention and was tasked with hosting this program, discussing topics related to financial derivatives.

What incited discontent among senior news department personnel was Yan Li’s lack of involvement in the day-to-day operations of the news department despite being granted the honorary title of "financial anchor" or "chief financial journalist" by upper management.

Adding fuel to the fire, Yan Li, who also hosts another financial program for the news department called "Financial Blog," had initially planned to edit highlights from "Stepping into Listed Companies" to be aired within "Financial Blog." This drew the attention of Deputy Director of News, Tam Wai-ni, who believed that content with advertising elements should not be broadcasted during news programs. Such actions could potentially violate the "Broadcasting Ordinance," prompting her to immediately halt the airing of these segments.

During yesterday’s employee meeting, Tam Wai-ni raised these issues with upper management, hoping to clearly demarcate news from advertisements. Director of the News Department, Leung Ka-wing, even proposed during the session, "News remains news, advertisements remain advertisements," indicating they would not oppose the company earning money through programming, but were against anyone profiting under the guise of the news department.

It is known that during discussions between the news department and management, tensions ran high as management continued to strongly support these practices, emphasizing the independence of the news department and assuring no interference in its operations. Others suggested that as long as it benefits the company financially, other issues need not be considered. Yan Li, seated in the audience as the central figure of the incident, spoke up, defending her role in helping the company earn money. Wang Zheng, sitting on stage as the "boss," attended the employee meeting but did not speak much.

An ATV spokesperson, when questioned, acknowledged that an employee meeting was held yesterday. The purpose was to allow management to provide updates on operational matters from the first half of the year and plans for the second half, aiming to increase managerial transparency. However, internal operational details were deemed inappropriate for public disclosure. Regarding the establishment of the "financial anchor" position, the spokesperson claimed to have no knowledge of it.

On August 23, 2011, amid the turmoil at ATV, Yan Li tearfully addressed a press conference. Facing repeated inquiries from ATV journalists, she eventually broke down in tears.

According to reports from the Sing Tao Daily, ATV management had planned to incorporate advertising-laden content into news programs, sparking dissatisfaction within the news department and escalating internal conflicts. Yan Li appeared at a press conference to clarify the situation, arguing that "Financial Blog" was an informational program and thus could include advertisements. However, she faced persistent questioning from ATV news department journalists, leading her to shed tears. She expressed her belief that if media institutions cannot survive, neither can the news departments, urging everyone not to further divide themselves.

Employees of the news department were reportedly dissatisfied with her explanations and the management's decision to replace the original program last night with an edited version of the press conference, omitting more sensitive questions and answers.

Previously, ATV management had planned to air excerpts from the "Stepping into Listed Companies" program, sponsored by listed companies, within "Financial Blog," sparking controversy between management and the news department. ATV chose to address the issue solely through a "Yan Li Press Conference," without the presence of management or senior news department officials.

At the press conference, Yan Li emphasized that since 2007, she has always understood "Financial Blog" to be an informational program rather than a news program. Despite changes in personnel and leadership under Director Leung Ka-wing, no one mentioned that the program was journalistic reporting. According to her understanding of the "Broadcasting Ordinance," informational programs can contain advertising elements. Many financial information programs similarly integrate commercial sponsorships, though she denied any intention to include content from "Stepping into Listed Companies" into "Financial Blog."

She also mentioned that after the September reform of "Financial Blog," she would take on triple roles as host, producer, and project director, leading several anchors to continue hosting the program, all with the "blessings" of Leung Ka-wing.

However, ATV news department journalists present at the press conference continuously challenged Yan Li’s statements. They pointed out that according to their knowledge, Leung Ka-wing never participated in the advertising-related work of "Stepping into Listed Companies" and never "blessed" her dual hosting roles. They further questioned why no senior news department officials accompanied her to clarify the matter. The journalists also asked whether Yan Li, in her new role as producer, would still maintain her status as a journalist and whether it was appropriate for her to continue soliciting advertisements in this capacity.

Faced with relentless questioning from her "colleagues," Yan Li couldn't help but shed tears at the end of the press conference. Emotionally charged, she argued that she too is a "media professional" and hoped the disputes could subside. She questioned, "If the media cannot survive, how can journalism survive? What I'm talking about now is the media industry; I don't want us to keep dividing between you and me."

It is reported that news department employees who closely followed the developments were generally dissatisfied with her explanations, especially given that news department financial journalists have consistently written scripts for "Financial Blog" and do not accept its classification as an "informational program."

Additionally, ATV unexpectedly replaced the scheduled entertainment talk show "Eva's Living Room" at 7 PM last night with an edited version of the "Yan Li Press Conference," removing incorrect or sensitive content, including scenes where Yan Li's eyes welled up with tears. Some employees felt that management deliberately avoided addressing significant issues.
