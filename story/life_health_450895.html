So, how long after becoming pregnant can it be detected?

Experts kindly remind: The question of how long after becoming pregnant it can be detected is actually quite simple. Women who are observant in their daily lives should know. Let's take a look at the expert's introduction.

The most common and earliest reaction to pregnancy is a delayed menstrual period. If your menstrual cycle is usually regular, you've had sexual intercourse without any contraception, and your period is delayed for more than 10 days without cause, then your first thought should be whether you are pregnant. Another situation: if your menstrual cycle isn't always regular, and this month's delay exceeds 10 days, it would be too early to determine pregnancy. You should look for other signs.

Firstly, if your menstrual period is prolonged and you experience symptoms such as dizziness, fatigue, cold intolerance, excessive sleepiness, loss of appetite, accompanied by varying degrees of nausea, food aversions, cravings for sour foods or dislike of greasy smells and certain odors, you should consider these as possible signs. It's important to note that some people may experience sudden changes in appetite or preferences. They may crave different foods at different times, lose interest in foods they usually like, or suddenly want foods they previously disliked. Severe cases might even lead to vomiting or breast tenderness, darkening of the nipples and areola with prickling pain, and frequent urination. At this point, you can roughly judge that you might be pregnant. You can buy a home pregnancy test and check yourself. Experts suggest that morning urine is generally more accurate, which means the first urine after waking up in the morning. Other times can also work, but it might not be as accurate.

In normal circumstances, if a woman pays attention, she can usually detect pregnancy around 18 days after intercourse or 7-10 days after a delayed period. After the sperm and egg combine to form a fertilized egg, it takes 6-8 days to implant into the uterine lining. Then, 7-10 days later, HCG secreted by the placenta enters the urine. The principle behind pregnancy tests is detecting HCG levels, or human chorionic gonadotropin levels. This hormone is produced by the placenta and appears in the urine a few days after pregnancy, but due to low amounts, it may not be easily detected at first. It becomes increasingly evident between 10-14 days. Adding these together, scientifically speaking, pregnancy can be detected in the urine around 18 days after intercourse.

Experts say that the time to detect pregnancy varies from person to person; some might detect it with just a one-day delay in their menstrual period.
