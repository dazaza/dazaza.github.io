Give Mom a Warm Home (Editor: SN021)

Just as life began to improve, fate seemed to play another joke on Yang Sen. In 2001, Yang Sen's body condition deteriorated. "One morning, when I got up to run, I found my whole body in pain, especially below the spine where it was stiff, and my legs felt like they were filled with lead. I could barely walk after just a few minutes," recalled Yang Sen, still shaken by the memory. He visited all the major and minor hospitals in Beijing but could not find the cause.

From then on, Yang Sen lived a dependent life with his "mom." In 1998, through a friend's introduction, Yang Sen became a security guard at a company in Changping. At that time, the company had strict leave policies, so he could only sneak back into the city to visit his mother every so often. "I would swap shifts with good colleagues to squeeze out some time to return, and my mother also understood very well," said Yang Sen. Life was tough, but Yang Sen was happy. For Yang Sen, who lost both parents at a young age and came to Beijing alone, "mom" let him experience long-lost maternal love and gave him a sense of home.

In November 2005, No. 25 Xinzhong Street where Grandma Zhi Wenping lived faced demolition. As an elderly person without family, the street arranged for her to go to a nursing home, but the old lady did not want to go. Yang Sen said, "Mom, don't worry, if we don't have a house, we'll rent one." Thereafter, Grandma Zhi Wenping moved with Yang Sen to rent a place on Dongsi Thirteen Lane. Afterward, they moved twice more. With an elderly mother needing care at home, Yang Sen would first inform any potential partner about this responsibility. In 2009, Yang Sen took out a loan to buy a 90+ square meter apartment in Tonghui Home near SiHui.

In August 1994, the Dongzhimen Street and the Sixth Brigade of the Beijing Armed Police Corps held a "Military and Civilian Family Bond" event. That year, the 78-year-old widowed elder Zhi Wenping formed an unbreakable bond with Yang Sen, who had lost both parents at a young age. From then on, whenever Yang Sen had free time, he would go to Grandma Zhi's house to help with chores and buy medicine. Three years passed quickly, and Yang Sen was about to be discharged from the army. After much deliberation, he decided to stay in Beijing and work. "To be honest, I couldn't leave the elder behind; her heart condition flared up frequently, and she needed someone nearby. Fortunately, I didn't have parents or other ties at home," said Yang Sen.

Watching her son gradually become thinner, Grandma Zhi Wenping sought folk remedies and prescriptions everywhere. Under the meticulous care of the elder, Yang Sen's health miraculously improved. Now, although he occasionally experiences leg pain, he is basically normal. Later, as Yang Sen's job stabilized, their lives began to improve slowly.

One winter, while working in Changping, Yang Sen returned to visit the elder. Late at night, the elder accidentally fell after bumping into the stove when getting up to use the restroom. Yang Sen immediately sent her to the hospital, where she was saved from danger. This incident deeply touched Yang Sen: his mother was getting older and needed his care nearby. Determinedly, Yang Sen resigned from his job in Changping and returned to be with the elder. Since then, to survive, Yang Sen has worked in cleaning, advertising, sales...

Now, in this warm home, Yang Sen's wife stays at home full-time to take care of grandma. Recently, the elder was hospitalized due to illness, and Yang Sen stayed by her side all the time. Regarding his unwavering commitment over 17 years, some people have expressed doubts, but Yang Sen told reporters that the elder had no real estate or savings. "I take care of the elder simply for the mother-son bond."

An Elderly Widow Becomes 'Mom'

Seventeen years ago, during a military-civilian gathering, Yang Sen, a soldier who lost his parents at a young age, met the elderly widow Zhi Wenping. After being discharged from the army, Yang Sen resolutely decided to stay in Beijing to work and take care of the elder. Despite having no blood relation, Yang Sen and Grandma Zhi Wenping have been living together day and night for 17 years, surpassing even the closest familial bonds. Recently, the 95-year-old elder was hospitalized, and Yang Sen stayed by her side without fail.
