Transportation Section - Travel Choices and Precautions

1. In the city, taxis cost a flat rate of 5 yuan, but trips out of town may cost between 7-10 yuan. To the airport, it's 20 yuan; or you can take a shuttle bus from the airport to the city for 10 yuan per person. If there are tourism promotions on the bus, just ignore them and tell them where you're getting off. Three rules and eight precautions for taking taxis:

Three Rules:

(1) Confirm the price: If you know you're within the city limits, have 5 yuan ready before getting in and pay the driver directly when you get out. For trips out of town, make sure to ask the price beforehand.

(2) Don't talk to strangers: You don't have to answer any questions the driver asks. If you need to ask something, do so after getting out and ask local residents, who are generally trustworthy and will answer your questions. As for taxi drivers, it's best not to ask them anything or engage in conversation.

(3) Do not leave any contact information: If the driver gives you a business card, throw it away at the next trash bin. Don't call the number on it.

Eight Precautions:

(1) Beware of female drivers: If you're walking down the street and a car follows you slowly while shouting "Cat多多哩" (cheap rides around the city), just keep walking and ignore them.

(2) Don't go to "fun" places: Some drivers might offer to take you to "fun" places, especially those with pretty Dai girls. If you go, you'll likely end up physically hurt, emotionally drained, and financially damaged.

(3) Be cautious of meeting someone from your hometown: The saying "When countrymen meet, one gets stabbed in the back" is no joke.

(4) Avoid taxis waiting outside hotels: These might be targeting you for a scam. It's better to hail a passing taxi. Also, don't let hotel bellhops call taxis for you; do it yourself.

(5) Don't engage in conversations about tours: And don't reveal your travel plans. Otherwise, you might fall into a trap.

(6) Be wary of extremely low prices for tours: Many people think it's foolish not to take advantage of a good deal, but in reality, they end up losing out. This makes the locals upset because they end up being blamed for the tourists' greed.

(7) Ignore offers for discounted tickets or cheap hotel bookings: If they were that capable, why would they be driving taxis? Who would believe that?

(8) Remember the normal fare from the airport to the city is 20 yuan: Keep this market price in mind. For those offering rides for 10 or even 5 yuan, consider this: the parking fee at the airport is already 5 yuan. If someone says they'll give you a free ride because their passenger didn't show up, politely decline by saying, "I don't accept unearned favors."

Why go through all this trouble explaining how to take a taxi? The issue with taxis in tourist cities across the country can be summed up in one sentence: Despite numerous exposures by CCTV, the situation with the day-trip cars near Beijing's train stations hasn't improved much. For other regions, we can only warn you to avoid trouble if possible.

Tourists traveling to Xishuangbanna often complain first about being scammed by joining a tour group and forced shopping, secondly about being cheated by taxi drivers. Here, we must say on behalf of the people of Xishuangbanna: if you don't follow the three rules and eight precautions and end up losing money, it's your own fault, so don't blame the people of Xishuangbanna!

2. Although we've said a lot about taxis, most tourists still end up taking taxis. If you use the buses from Xishuangbanna Tourism Car Company and refuse to shop, you'll have to pay an additional 50% of the car fare. If you exceed the time limit at a location, you'll also have to pay a "waiting fee." Their service and prices may not suit your needs, so you'll still rely on taxis. We advise against using private cars as they may not have full insurance. Some companies have fixed taxis for group tours that are strictly managed, focus on service, maintain their image, value their reputation, and aim for long-term business. However, don't compare prices with those claiming to take you to the botanical garden for 100 or even 80 yuan. History has proven and will continue to prove that trying to save money doesn't lead to good outcomes. There are bad people everywhere in the world, but also good ones. Don't fear every taxi you see; the three rules and eight precautions apply specifically to taxis you encounter at the airport, bus station, hotel entrance, or on the street.

3. Take the route bus: You can mostly take public buses to various scenic spots. The bus station is called "Banna Bus Station," but it's better to say "Flip Tire Factory Bus Station," as they are actually the same place. Currently, after adjustments, the bus to Manfeilong White Pagoda (which is basically abandoned and rarely visited) has been moved to the South Station, while others remain at the "Flip Tire Factory Bus Station." However, taking public transportation is tiring and time-consuming. After spending thousands on plane tickets, the last few hundred kilometers of your trip should be more enjoyable with a rented car. If you're traveling alone or with a small group, you can post online to find others to share the ride, which adds a unique charm. There's no need to waste time waiting for buses.

4. Renting a car with a driver is the best choice for leisure and relaxation in Xishuangbanna. Having control over the steering wheel means you decide where to go and where to stop. Especially for leisure vacations, you shouldn't sacrifice comfort for speed. For those vacationing at resorts or playing golf, taking a taxi might seem too low-class. Taking a bus might be something you haven't done in years.

Appendix: Transportation Guide from Jinghong City to Major Attractions in Xishuangbanna

- Botanical Garden: Jinghong — Menglong Town — Botanical Garden, 75KM, 1.5-hour drive, 18 yuan/person. Take a bus from Banna Bus Station (commonly known as Flip Tire Factory) or buses heading towards Mengla County, stopping at Menglong Town. The entrance is 1KM from Menglong Town.

- Dai Ethnic Park: Jinghong — Ganlanba, 28KM, 0.5-hour drive, 8 yuan/person. Buses are available from Banna Bus Station (commonly known as Flip Tire Factory) or the South Station.

- Mannaisangkang: Jinghong — Ganlanba — Mannaisangkang, 39KM, 12 yuan/person. Buses are available from Banna Bus Station (commonly known as Flip Tire Factory) or the South Station.

- Wild Elephant Valley: Jinghong — Wild Elephant Valley, 48KM, 15 yuan/person. Any bus from Banna Bus Station (commonly known as Flip Tire Factory) heading towards Pu'er stops at the 48KM mark.

- Primeval Forest Park: Jinghong — Primeval Forest Park, 8KM, 7 yuan/person. Any bus from Banna Bus Station (commonly known as Flip Tire Factory) heading towards Xiaomengyang stops at the 8KM mark.

- Mengyuan Fairyland: Jinghong — Mengxing Town — Mengyuan Fairyland, 115KM to Mengxing Town, 22 yuan/person. Take a bus from Banna Bus Station (commonly known as Flip Tire Factory) heading towards Mengla County to reach Mengxing Town, then another 2KM to the attraction.

- Wangtianshu: Jinghong — Mengla County — Wangtianshu. Take a bus from Banna Bus Station (commonly known as Flip Tire Factory) heading towards Mengla County, 195KM, 38 yuan/person. Then take a bus heading towards Mengban or Yao District and get off at the 14KM mark to reach the ticket office, 7 yuan/person.

The above serves as a reminder to ensure your visit to Xishuangbanna is both relaxing and comfortable. We hope you enjoy your trip thoroughly.
