Who can stay with you for a lifetime?

When she was two years old, she once had a high fever and fell into a coma. Her father held her through the night to take her to the hospital. On the way, she who had been in a coma for a whole day suddenly opened her eyes and clearly called out: "Dad!"

Her father often mentioned this incident to her later on. Those tiny details were carved into a landscape through her father's repeated recounting. Every time her father finished talking, he would sigh: "You tell me, you were so little back then, and you were in a coma for such a long time, how did you suddenly wake up?" At that moment, her father's eyes were filled with tenderness and affection. After he told the story many times, she became annoyed and retorted, but her father didn't care at all, just laughed happily and contentedly. Her arrogance and tyranny grew under her father's indulgence.

In fact, her father wasn't a man of good temper, he was irascible and easily angered. Often, just because of some trivial daily matters, he would have a big argument with her mother, every time it was earth-shaking. Her father was addicted to alcohol, every time he drank, he would get drunk, and after getting drunk, he would quarrel. Since she could remember, there were few warm and peaceful moments in their home. Inside and outside the house, the smell of gunpowder always lingered.

Her father's gentleness and love were only given to her. He rarely quarreled with her mother in front of her. If she happened to encounter their quarrels, no matter how fierce the arguments were, as long as she shouted: "Stop arguing!" her father who was full of anger would immediately lower his head and stop. Later on, whenever her parents argued, her brother would immediately call her because everyone knew: only she could subdue her father.

Her feelings towards her father were complicated. She once felt sorry for her mother and thought in her heart: when she looked for a boyfriend in the future, the first requirement would be gentle and tolerant, the second would be not addicted to smoking or drinking. She would never look for a man like her father: irascible, picky, petty, and making the family restless over small things.

However, as his daughter, she knew she was happy.

She thought this happiness would last a lifetime until one day, her father solemnly told her that from now on, she would live with him. Later, she learned that it was her mother who proposed the divorce. Her mother said that after so many years of fighting, she was tired of it. Her father resisted for a long time, eventually chose to compromise, and the only condition he put forward was that he must take her with him.

Although it was her mother who proposed the divorce, she still stubbornly blamed her father for it. From then on, she became a cold and proud child, refusing her father's care and moving to live in school. Her father came to find her at school, bringing a thermos full of braised pork ribs that she liked to eat. She didn't even look, lowered her head, and stuffed rice into her mouth forcefully, bite after bite, until tears welled up in her eyes. Her father sighed, asking her to go home, but she remained silent with a cold face. Her father raised his hand to touch her head, pityingly saying, "Look, in just a few days, you've become so thin." She "slapped" her hand with the book to block her father's hand, shouting hysterically: "Don't bother me!" Then she suddenly swept the table, the lunch box fell to the ground with a "clang", the reddish-brown pork ribs spilled all over the floor, the rich aroma filled the entire dormitory.

Her father's hand, awkwardly stopped in mid-air. According to his temper, if it were someone else, a slap might have already landed. She saw her father's facial muscles twitch violently several times, saying: "No matter what, dad will always love you!" When her father was about to leave, he looked back at her deeply. She watched her father walk away, the defense line she had held firmly collapsed, and she cried loudly alone in the quiet dormitory, looking at the ribs scattered all over the floor.

She was just a spoiled child by her father.

As soon as the autumn wind arose, after evening study session, the night wind already had a chill. As soon as she walked out of the classroom, she saw a shadow flickering by the window. Her heart tightened, she asked, who is there? The person immediately responded, "YaYa, don't be afraid, it's dad." Her father walked up to her, handed her a roll of something, and instructed her: "It's getting cool, you've always kicked off the quilt while sleeping since you were young, be careful not to freeze." She returned to the dormitory and opened the package. It was a new cotton quilt. Burying her head in it, she took a deep breath, it was full of the smell of sunshine. She knew that it must have been dried by her father all day, and then hurriedly delivered to her.

That day, she went home to get some things. When she pushed open the door, her father was curled up on the sofa, asleep, the TV was still on. Her father's hair had all turned gray, his complexion was haggard. In just a year's time, her spirited father had suddenly aged. She suddenly realized how lonely her father actually was. She stood there for a long time, took the quilt to cover her father. Her father woke up suddenly. Seeing her, he was a bit nervous and hurriedly tidied up the messy things on the sofa, then remembered something, put down the things in his hands, and incoherently said: "Have you eaten yet? Wait, I'll make your favorite braised pork ribs..." She originally wanted to say she wouldn't eat and would just take the things and leave. But seeing her father's expectant and nervous expression, she couldn't bear it and sat down. Her father was excited like a child, running into the kitchen like a streak, she heard her father drop the spoon on the ground and break a bowl. She went in and helped her father pick up the pieces. Her father sheepishly said to her: "My hands are too slippery..." Her eyes became moist, suddenly regretting: why should she hurt the people who loved her so much?

When she was in her third year of university, her father remarried. Her father called her, cautiously saying: "She's an elementary school teacher, retired, meticulous, and has a good temper... If you don't have time, you don't have to come back..." She was also dating a boyfriend at that time, knowing that some things depend on fate. She also knew in her heart how lonely her father had been these years. She remained silent for a long time on the phone before gently saying: "In the future, don't argue with others anymore." Her father kept agreeing: "Yeah, won't argue anymore, won't argue anymore."

During the summer vacation, she returned home with her boyfriend. There was new furniture in the house, and the flowers on the balcony were in full bloom. Her father was well-dressed and radiant. Facing the slightly plump woman, she shyly called: "Auntie." Auntie immediately got flustered, happily went to the kitchen to cook, coming out every once in a while to ask whether she preferred sweet or spicy food, and whether the taste should be light or heavy. She then directed her father, telling him to peel an onion here, wash vegetables there. She never expected that her irascible father would be obediently controlled like a child. Listening to her father and aunt laughing softly in the kitchen, the sound of oil pans, the smell of cooking smoke overflowing from the kitchen, her eyes became hot, this was truly the taste of home.

That night, after everyone went to sleep, her father came to her room and seriously said to her: "YaYa, this boy isn't suitable for you." Her stubbornness rose again: "How is he unsuitable? At least, he doesn't drink, his temper is much better than yours, and he never argues with me." Her father was a bit embarrassed but still persuaded her: "You haven't experienced much, people like him may not argue with you, but they record everything in their hearts."

She insisted on her own choice. In her second year of work, she got married. But her father's prophecy unfortunately came true. She inherited her father's quick temper, and when she lost her temper, quarrels were inevitable. He never argued with her, but his silence and persistence in not giving in made her even harder to bear. Cold war, separation, when her child was two years old, they divorced.

After the divorce, she lived alone with her child, suffered from insomnia, her hair fell out in large amounts, her work was unsatisfactory, and she suddenly aged a lot. Once, her child suddenly asked her: "Does Dad not want us anymore?" She held back her tears and said: "No matter what, Mom will always love you." As soon as the words came out, she was stunned. These words were once said to her by her father, but had she ever understood her father's feelings?

Her father said on the phone, if life was not good, come back. Let the aunt take care of the child, can't the old man support you? She remained silent, not speaking, tears falling drop by drop, thinking her father couldn't see.

The next day, her father suddenly came, without hesitation packed her things, picked up the child, and said, let's go home with grandpa.

Still her room, Auntie had cleaned it spotlessly. Her father liked to cook, three meals a day, changing the dishes for her. Her father was old and forgetful, often putting double portions of salt in the dishes. But she remembered everything clearly about her childhood. Her father told her child again about the time she had a fever as a child. Her father said: "It was your mom's one word 'dad' that captured grandpa's heart..." She listened beside them, suddenly remembering that poem: "Old age brings much forgetfulness, except for love."

In early spring, seeing her wearing dark clothes, her father insisted on going shopping for new clothes for her. He proudly opened his wallet to show her, inside was a bundle of new banknotes, her father's newly received pension. She smiled, took her father's arm, playfully said: "So this is what it feels like to lean on a rich guy!" Her father then acted like a gentleman, walking proudly with his chest puffed out, making both her and Auntie laugh uncontrollably.

Walking on the street, her father withdrew his arm, saying, you walk ahead, I'll follow behind. She laughed and asked, are you shy? Her father said, if you walk ahead, in case of any unexpected situation, I can remind you to dodge. She stopped, the sunlight shone from behind, and she suddenly realized, when did her father's back become bent? She remembered that her father used to be such a tall and strong man. But now, such an old man still walked behind her, reminding her of possible dangers...

She walked ahead, thinking, in this life, who else would wait for her like her father? Thinking of this, tears couldn't help but flow out. She didn't dare to wipe them, afraid that her father behind her would see. She just straightened her back and kept walking forward.
