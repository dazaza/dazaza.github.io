Seven teachers in Honghe, Yunnan ride horses down the mountain every week to buy vegetables for students

by hdj4vjfbd3 on 2012-03-07 01:43:39

One dish and one soup: the children's dinner is very simple. Photo by Huang Xingneng. In addition to teaching and nurturing, the seven teachers of Dachong Primary School in Wantang Township, Pingbian Miao Autonomous County, Honghe Hani and Yi Autonomous Prefecture, also have to go down the mountain on horseback to buy vegetables. Every Tuesday, Principal Chu Jianhua and several other teachers will ride horses rented from the villagers, spending four hours round trip to the town government location to buy enough vegetables for the 77 teachers and students to eat for a week.

Dinner is one dish and one soup.

Dachong Primary School in Wantang Township is two hours' drive from the county seat of Pingbian. Driving to the school, you first descend from an altitude of over 1400 meters in the county seat of Pingbian to the bottom of Mi Luo River at 350 meters, then ascend more than 1000 meters from the river bottom to Sha ZiTian Village. Along the way, you can see many tropical fruits such as bananas and lychees, but there are even more high mountains and deep valleys.

At 6:40 PM on February 29th, when the "Free Lunch Yunnan Tour" activity personnel arrived at the school, they happened to encounter the students eating dinner. Many students had already served themselves and were sitting in rows on the steps outside the classroom door, quietly eating their meals. Their meal was very simple: white rice, stir-fried potatoes, and vegetable soup. The reporter entered the kitchen and saw several types of vegetables on the rack: green peppers, cauliflower, Chinese cabbage, garlic, etc. "The vegetables are all bought from the market in the township every Tuesday," said Principal Chu Jianhua.

Weekly horse rides down the mountain to buy vegetables

Recalling the road conditions when coming here, the reporter found that from the township to the school, it was all winding mountain roads, and the distance was relatively far. "Is there a vehicle for transportation?" the reporter asked worriedly.

"That depends on whether we can hitch a ride or not. If not, we can only rent horses from the villagers, costing 30 yuan per horse. It takes an hour to go down the mountain and about three hours to climb back up," said Chu Jianhua. Meat is bought from the village at 13 yuan per catty and stored in the freezer.

Deputy Director Wang Fei of the Education Bureau of Pingbian County said that Wantang Township cannot grow vegetables, so vegetables are relatively expensive, and sometimes the restaurants in the township run out of vegetables on weekends.

Implementation of the 3-yuan nutrition fee means more meat

It was introduced that the school has grades one through four, with currently 70 students, 61 of whom live on campus; there are six formal teachers and one substitute teacher. Chu Jianhua told the reporter that the children's parents leave home early to work and return late in the evening, leaving no time to cook. To ensure that the nine day students do not go hungry, the school allows all the children to eat in the cafeteria.

Chu Jianhua introduced that impoverished boarding students used to receive a monthly living allowance of 75 yuan, which was still quite tight after expenses. Later, the primary school living allowance increased to 100 yuan per month, but not all students were eligible. "This living expense is not much, and eating meat every day is insufficient. At most, we can only eat meat three to four times a week."

With the implementation of the national 3-yuan nutritional lunch plan starting in March, Chu Jianhua felt delighted: "With these 3 yuan, the children's diet will be better than before, allowing for more meat to be added weekly, and occasionally some fruit can be purchased."

Too cold, so they have to get up at 6 o'clock

When the reporter entered a boys' dormitory, there were six wooden bunk beds inside. These beds are about twice as wide as regular bunk beds, resembling double beds. Each bed has two or three mattress pads and relatively thick blankets. Chu Jianhua said: "During this period, the students wake up before 6 o'clock every day because it is too cold to sleep."

The reporter recalled that when entering the school gate and shaking hands with the school teachers, each teacher's hands were frozen cold. No wonder the students at Baitu Primary School in Chake Town, Jianguo County, only need one mattress pad, while the students here need two or three layers of mattress pads.

(Reported by Yang Zhigao)
