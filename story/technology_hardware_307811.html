AC Milan: One player staying can only hold back his teammates; what's the use of a defender who only knows how to shoot?

by qinglai888 on 2009-12-14 22:55:13

AC Milan: One player staying can only hold back teammates; what's the use of a defender who only knows how to shoot?

Sina Sports reports: AC Milan's unbeaten run of 13 games in all competitions since October was ended by Palermo at home. In terms of attacking quality, the Rossoneri did not play poorly in this match. They had a possession rate of 57.5%, attempted 13 shots on goal, and Ronaldinho delivered several good passes. However, they failed to capitalize on their final shots. Compared to previous matches, Milan lacked some scoring luck this time around, and issues on the defensive end remained unresolved. This imbalance resulted in a defeat.

Thiago Silva's injury saw Favalli come into the starting lineup. The 37-year-old looked like the weakest link in the defense, but in reality, aside from being breached twice by Cavani, Favalli made no direct mistakes. He was at least better than Kaladze. The responsibility for both goals conceded is clear: In the 49th minute, Bresciano distributed the ball in front of the penalty area, and Miccoli cut into the box from the left wing. Zambrotta, wary of the opponent's speed, kept his distance, not daring to press aggressively. Miccoli easily deceived Zambrotta with a simple feint and comfortably aimed at the far post to score. Zambrotta's only contribution in this goal was obstructing Dida's view.

In the 63rd minute, Zambrotta actively overlapped down the right flank and crossed the ball, but Palermo cleared it out and launched a counterattack. Miccoli exploited the vacated space left by Zambrotta, racing through along the right touchline. Left-back Antonini hurried across the field to cover but was still a step too late as Miccoli elegantly maneuvered past him. By the time Pastore dribbled past defenders inside the box and took a shot that was blocked, followed by Bresciano scoring on the rebound, Zambrotta had still not returned to the penalty area! Pirlo, Ambrosini, and Nesta were all covering for him, while Zambrotta simply abandoned the pursuit, thus burdening his teammates.

To be fair, Zambrotta did have some bright moments offensively. In the 33rd minute, Seedorf distributed the ball, and Zambrotta charged forward to unleash a powerful shot that hit the side netting. In the 88th minute, Zambrotta struck a fierce shot from just outside the penalty area, forcing Palermo goalkeeper Sirigu into an impressive save that deflected the ball onto the crossbar. But AC Milan does not need a winger-style Zambrotta reminiscent of his days in Bari; they need a full-back primarily focused on defensive duties. In the 28th minute, during Palermo’s quick counterattack, Cavani broke through Favalli and sent a through pass to Miccoli on the left. It was Antonini who came over to cover, while Zambrotta was nowhere to be seen. Near the end of the game, Milan was hit by another counterattack, and Zambrotta seemed to have lost any motivation to track back. He only slowly returned to his own half when the opposition began slowing down after moving horizontally in midfield, taking a full 7-8 seconds to do so! If Palermo hadn’t substituted Miccoli early, Zambrotta's defensive zone would have been exposed many more times.

In fact, Leonardo had the chance to substitute Zambrotta, but when Pato came on, Leonardo chose to take off Abate instead. At the press conference, some journalists questioned: “Why was Abate substituted instead of Zambrotta?” Indeed, with Zambrotta on the pitch, his teammates were playing too tired.

Zambrotta’s active participation in offense to try and claw back the deficit demonstrated his desire to win and sense of responsibility. Throughout the game, he attempted seven crosses, equaling the combined total of Abate and Pato. However, under fatigued physical conditions, Zambrotta did not properly gauge the extent of his involvement in the attack. Knowing his body wouldn't allow him to recover in time defensively, he still frequently pushed too high up the pitch. Clearly, Zambrotta struggles to handle the task of dominating an entire flank corridor in a three-forward formation. Leonardo needs to change how he uses Zambrotta, leveraging his experience rather than exposing his physical limitations.

Abate and Antonini are two crucial players in alleviating the rotation problem among full-backs. But as Pato’s form has dipped, Abate has been repositioned by Leonardo as a substitute right winger. After the winter transfer window opens, the newly signed youngster Adja may not immediately serve as Pato's backup. Milan still needs to reinforce its full-back positions to increase bench depth; otherwise, Zambrotta's tragedy will replay itself.
